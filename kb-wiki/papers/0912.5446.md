# Work Function of Strongly Magnetized Neutron Star Crustal Matter and the Associated Magneto-Sphere

> Following an extremely interesting idea \cite{R1}, published long ago, the work function at the outer crust region of a strongly magnetized neutron star is obtained using relativistic version of Thomas-Fermi type model. In the present scenario, the work function becomes anisotropic; the longitudinal part is an increasing function of magnetic field strength, whereas the transverse part diverges. An approximate estimate of the electron density in the magnetosphere due to field emission and photo emission current, from the polar cap region are obtained.

# Work Function of Strongly Magnetized Neutron Star Crustal Matter and the Associated Magneto-Sphere

## Key claim
The work function for electron emission from the outer crust of strongly magnetized neutron stars is anisotropic: the longitudinal component increases with magnetic field strength as B^{1/2}, while the transverse component diverges to infinity regardless of the transverse dimension of the cylindrical atomic cells. The authors note that their results are "very much approximate in nature" and intended as a first treatment showing the lines along which more exact calculations could be carried out.

## Method
The authors employ a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model in cylindrical coordinates, assuming fully ionized atoms in the outer crust are replaced by Wigner-Seitz cells of approximately cylindrical shape, with the magnetic field aligned along the cylinder axes. They solve Poisson's equation for the electrostatic potential under the approximation that electrons occupy only the 0th Landau level (ultra-strong field limit, m_e = 0) and apply continuity conditions on electric field components at the material-vacuum interface, following the approach of Schieber (R1) originally developed for cold cathode emission in engineering contexts.

## Key numbers
- The longitudinal work function is given by W_f = (1/3)(2B/πB_c^(e))^{1/2} m_e e^3, where B_c^(e) ≈ 4.43 × 10^13 G is the critical field strength for relativistic Landau level population
- For low field values, the longitudinal work function is a few eV in magnitude, consistent with experimentally known values for laboratory metals
- The field emission electron number density is evaluated using a constant polar electric field E_0 = 10^12 V/m, and also using a magnetic-field-dependent E_0 from the literature
- The photo-electric current density J is calculated for incident photon energy E_γ = 1 GeV, shown for electron densities of 10^{-3}, 10^{-4}, and 10^{-5} times normal nuclear density

## Context
This work extends the idea of Schieber (R1), originally applied to cold cathode emission in engineering, to the astrophysical context of strongly magnetized neutron stars and magnetars. The anisotropic work function result is claimed as a first prediction, with the diverging transverse component also reported for the first time. The field emission density results are compared with published theoretical results from the pulsar magnetosphere literature (R2, R5). The work enables approximate estimates of magnetospheric electron density from field emission and photo-emission at the polar cap, though the authors emphasize the approximate nature of their treatment and suggest directions for more exact calculations.
