# Reference calculations for subthreshold $Ξ$ production

> We present a minimal statistical model designed for the description of rare-hadron multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions at energies below the threshold of the particle production in binary elementary collisions. Differences to more conventional canonical statistical model are explained. The minimal statistical model is applied to the description of multiplicity ratios involving $Ξ$ hyperons, which are measured by the HADES collaboration at GSI-SIS. It is argued that the HADES data cannot be reproduced by the model based on the statistical equilibrium and the strangeness conservation. The data remain underpredicted even when in-medium potentials acting on hadrons are taken into account. This hints to non-equilibrium production of the $Ξ$ hyperons and their continuous freeze-out.

# Reference calculations for subthreshold Ξ production

## Key claim

The minimal statistical model, which accounts for the volume scaling of rare strangeness production, cannot reproduce the HADES measurement of the Ξ/(Λ+Σ⁰)K⁺ multiplicity ratio in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 AGeV, even after including in-medium potentials for hadrons; this failure suggests that Ξ hyperons are produced out of chemical equilibrium and undergo continuous freeze-out rather than a single sudden decoupling.

## Method

The authors construct a minimal statistical model where the number of s s̅ pairs is determined from measured K⁺ multiplicities and the isospin asymmetry of the colliding system, and the s quarks are distributed among strange hadrons via thermal probabilities that depend on temperature, baryon chemical potential (fixed at freeze-out density 0.6ρ₀), and volume. The model averages over collision centralities by incorporating volume factors ⟨V⁴/³⟩, ⟨V⁵/³⟩, and ⟨V⟩, and then compares predicted multiplicity ratios (K⁻/K⁺, Λ+Σ⁰/K⁺, Σ/K⁺, and Ξ/[(Λ+Σ⁰)K⁺]) to the HADES data. In a second step, density-dependent scalar and vector potentials are added to the dispersion relations for nucleons, hyperons, and antikaons to test whether in-medium effects can resolve the discrepancy.

## Key numbers

- In Ar+KCl collisions at E_beam = 1.76 AGeV, the measured ratio M_Ξ / M_(Λ+Σ⁰) = (5.6 ± 1.2 +1.8 −1.7) × 10⁻³; the canonical statistical model predicts a value of order 10⁻⁴.
- The isospin asymmetry parameter for Ar+KCl is η ≈ 1.14.
- The measured K⁺ multiplicity is M_K⁺ = (2.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻².
- The measured ratio M_(Λ+Σ⁰) / M_K⁺ = 1.46 +0.49 −0.37.
- The measured ratio M_Ξ / (M_K⁺ M_(Λ+Σ⁰)) = 0.20 +0.16 −0.12.
- The freeze-out density is taken as 0.6 ρ₀, with ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- In-medium potentials used: vector potentials V_N = V_Δ = (3/2)V_Λ = (3/2)V_Σ = 3 V_Ξ = 130 MeV (ρ_B/ρ₀); scalar potential S_N = −190 MeV (ρ_B/ρ₀); hyperon potentials U_Λ = −27 MeV, U_Σ = 24 MeV, U_Ξ = −14 MeV; antikaon optical potentials U_K̅ = −75 or −150 MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature that simultaneously explains K⁻/K⁺, Λ/K⁺, and Σ/K⁺ ratios with in-medium potentials is approximately 70 MeV.
- For Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV, the predicted ratio M_Ξ / (M_(Λ+Σ⁰) M_K⁺) is expected to be about five times smaller than in Ar+KCl, because the Au+Au volume is roughly five times larger and the ratio scales as V⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the canonical statistical model of strangeness production by introducing a minimal model that accounts for the volume scaling of rare-strangeness production in subthreshold collisions, contrasting with the conventional approach that assumes chemical equilibrium. The HADES data (HADES Collaboration, 2015) for Ξ production in Ar+KCl at 1.76 AGeV and Au+Au at 1.23 AGeV cannot be described even with in-medium potentials, pointing to non-equilibrium production mechanisms. The authors propose that Ξ production may proceed via strangeness recombination reactions (e.g., K̅ + Λ → Ξ + π or Λ + Λ → Ξ + N), and they provide parametrized cross-sections for such reactions in a separate proceedings paper (Tomasik and Kolomeitsev, 2015). Planned kinetic calculations aim to validate these mechanisms.
