# Neutron pair transfer in sub-barrier capture process

> The sub-barrier capture reactions following the neutron pair transfer are proposed to be used for the indirect study of neutron-neutron correlation in the surface region of nucleus. The strong effect of the dineutron-like clusters transfer stemming from the surface of magic and non-magic nuclei $^{18}$O, $^{48}$Ca, $^{64}$Ni, $^{94,96}$Mo, $^{100,102,104}$Ru, $^{104,106,108}$Pd, and $^{112,114,116,118,120,124,132}$Sn is demonstrated. The dominance of two-neutron transfer channel at the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier is further supported by time-dependent mean-field approaches.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that sub-barrier capture (fusion) reactions with negative one-neutron transfer Q-values and positive two-neutron transfer Q-values can serve as an indirect probe of dineutron-like spatial correlations and surface-enhanced pairing in nuclei, under the condition that the two-step sequential transfer is energetically closed before capture.

## Method

The authors employ a quantum diffusion approach treating the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable, with fluctuation and dissipation effects modeling couplings to non-collective single-particle excitations and low-lying collective modes. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated using a double-folding formalism with Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the capture probability is obtained by integrating a propagator for an inverted oscillator that approximates the potential barrier. Additionally, Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations are used to extract one-, two-, and three-neutron transfer probabilities in heavy-ion scattering reactions for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca: $Q_{1n} = -1.6$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 2.6$ MeV
- For the reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{116}$Sn: $Q_{1n} = -1.2$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 2.8$ MeV
- For the reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{124}$Sn: $Q_{1n} = -0.1$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 5.4$ MeV
- For the reaction $^{58}$Ni + $^{64}$Ni: $Q_{1n} = -0.66$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 3.9$ MeV
- For the reaction $^{64}$Ni + $^{132}$Sn: $Q_{1n} = -1.21$ MeV, $Q_{2n} = 2.5$ MeV
- Coulomb barrier heights from mean-field theory: $B_0 = 116.41 \pm 0.07$ MeV ($^{40}$Ca + $^{116}$Sn), $114.69 \pm 0.04$ MeV ($^{40}$Ca + $^{124}$Sn), and $113.92 \pm 0.02$ MeV ($^{40}$Ca + $^{130}$Sn)
- The internal-excitation width used in the model: $\hbar\gamma = 15$ MeV
- After two-neutron transfer in $^{40}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca, the quadrupole deformation of $^{42}$Ca becomes $\beta_2 = 0.247$ (from $\beta_2 = 0$ for $^{40}$Ca)

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously developed by the same group (Sargsyan et al., 2012-2013) to explicitly demonstrate the role of dineutron-like clusters in sub-barrier capture, building on earlier studies of pair transfer in reactions such as ($p$,$t$) and ($t$,$p$) by Bohr and Nathan (1969) and von Oertzen (1997). The results support the surface enhancement of pairing predicted in theoretical studies by Matsuo (2005), Grasso (2005), and Lacroix (2009), and are consistent with experimental observations by Corradi (2000-2011) and Simenel (2011) showing that two-neutron transfer probabilities grow faster than one-neutron probabilities near the Coulomb barrier. The work enables future experiments on $^{40}$Ca + $^{116,124}$Sn and $^{40}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca to test the predicted dominance of pair transfer in sub-barrier capture.
