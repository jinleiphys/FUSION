# An alternative explanation for the $Θ(1540)$ pentaquark peak

> We have studied the $γd \to K^+ K^- n p$ reaction in which the LEPS collaboration found a signal in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass for the claimed $Θ(1540)$ pentaquark peak. Our study reveal that the procedure used at LEPS to reconstruct the $K^+n$ invariant mass generates an artificial strength in the $Θ(1540)$ region and that the LEPS collaboration underestimated the background in that region, such that the signal observed for the $Θ(1540)$ is compatible with a fluctuation of $2σ$ over the calculated background.

## Key claim

The peak observed by the LEPS collaboration in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass for the claimed $\Theta(1540)$ pentaquark is not necessarily a genuine resonance; instead, it can be explained as an artifact of the minimum momentum spectator approximation (MMSA) used to reconstruct the $K^+ n$ mass combined with an underestimated background, making the signal compatible with a $2\sigma$ statistical fluctuation.

## Method

The authors simulated the $\gamma d \to K^+ K^- n p$ reaction using a Monte Carlo model that included $\phi$ production on nucleons and $\Lambda(1520)$ production on the proton, with the LEPS detector geometry (kaon angles within 20 degrees of the incident photon) and the MMSA prescription to reconstruct nucleon momenta. They compared the $K^+ n$ invariant mass distribution obtained with the MMSA prescription to the distribution using the real momenta from the simulation, and then fitted the LEPS data with both a resonance-plus-background model and a fluctuation-plus-background model.

## Key numbers

- The LEPS best fit assumed a background of about 22 events per bin below the $\Theta(1540)$ peak, but the calculated background in the same region is around 36 events per bin.
- With the calculated background, the statistical significance of the $\Theta(1540)$ peak is about $2\sigma$, compared to the $5\sigma$ significance claimed by LEPS.
- The MMSA prescription produces an artificial broad peak in the $K^+ n$ spectrum that can be fit by two Gaussians: one peaking at 1520 MeV with a width of 80 MeV and another peaking at 1660 MeV with a width of 185 MeV.
- The $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom for both the resonance-plus-background fit and the fluctuation-plus-background fit is 1.2.

## Context

This work extends the debate over the $\Theta(1540)$ pentaquark, first predicted by Diakonov (1997) and reported by the LEPS collaboration (2003, 2009), but not confirmed by the CLAS collaboration (with ten times more statistics and complete kinematics). The authors provide an alternative explanation for the LEPS signal, showing that the MMSA reconstruction method and underestimated background can produce an artificial peak, and they note that a similar $2\sigma$ fluctuation was already observed in the $K^- p$ spectrum around 1650 MeV in the same LEPS experiment. This enables future experiments to re-evaluate the statistical significance of claimed pentaquark signals when using incomplete kinematic reconstruction.
