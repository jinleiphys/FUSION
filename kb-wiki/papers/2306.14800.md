# The Entanglement of Elastic and Inelastic Scattering

> The entanglement properties of systems in which elastic and inelastic reactions occur in projectile-target interactions is studied. A new measure of entanglement, the scattering entropy, based on the unitarity of the $S-$matrix (probability conservation), is suggested. Using simple models for both low- and high-energy interactions, the amount of entanglement is found to track with the strength of the inelastic interaction. The familiar example of the classical ``black disk", total absorption, model is found to correspond to maximum entanglement. An analysis of high-energy $pp$ scattering data shows that entanglement is near maximum for lab energies greater than about 1 GeV, showing that the total absorption model is a reasonable starting point for understanding the data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new measure of entanglement, the "scattering entropy," based on unitarity of the S-matrix, and argues that entanglement between projectile and target tracks with the strength of inelastic interactions; the claim is hedged by the use of simple models and the condition that the formalism applies when a single measurement of projectile energy can determine whether the target is excited.

## Method

The author develops a coupled-channels formalism using delta-shell interactions for low-energy s-wave scattering, solving for T-matrix elements and defining probabilities from the optical theorem. For high-energy scattering, the eikonal approximation is applied to a two-channel model with Gaussian potentials, and the scattering entropy is computed from the ratio of elastic to total cross sections. The formalism is then applied to experimental proton-proton scattering data from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- For the low-energy delta-shell model with parameters a=3.5 fm, V1=0.25 fm^{-1}, and Delta=0.1 fm^{-1}, the scattering entropy reaches maximum (S=1) when V12/V1=1 and k is sufficiently above threshold.
- For high-energy pp scattering, the total cross section of about 40 mb is reproduced using lambda_V of about 100 MeV and a Gaussian radius parameter of sqrt(2) fm.
- The entanglement entropy for pp scattering is near maximum for fixed-target laboratory momenta greater than about 2 GeV/c (kinetic energy about 1.3 GeV).
- The black disk model yields elastic and inelastic cross sections equal (sigma_el = sigma_inel = pi R^2, sigma_tot = 2 pi R^2), corresponding to maximum scattering entropy S=1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entanglement in nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleus scattering by Beane (2018), Liu (2022), and Miller (2023), and connects to the black disk model used in high-energy scattering by Kovchegov (2012) and the Blair model from Blair (1954). The results enable using scattering entropy as a diagnostic tool for understanding the dynamics of projectile-target interactions, particularly showing that the total absorption model is a reasonable starting point for high-energy pp scattering data.
