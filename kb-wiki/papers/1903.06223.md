# Monopole moments and nuclear compressibility

> The nuclear compressibility has a role in nuclear physics in several ways. Its relationship to the giant monopole is well known and has been subject of much theoretical work. Less well known is its affect on in nuclear structure, namely monopole transitions between low-lying states in the spectrum. Here I revisit the relationship between monopole and quadrupole moments in deformed nuclei. It has often been assumed without any microscopic justification that the nucleus can be treated as an incompressible fluid. An analytic formula is derived here for the resulting relationship between monopole and quadrupole moments. The formula is shown to be well satisfied within self-consistent mean-field theory calculated with several energy functionals. The formula can be tested when both monopole and quadrupole matrix elements of band-to-band transitions are known. Data is available for the decay of the first excited $K^π= 0^+$ band to the ground-state band in $^{156}$Gd, and the extracted matrix elements are found to be consistent with the incompressible fluid picture.

# Monopole moments and nuclear compressibility

## Key claim

The incompressible fluid assumption for deformed nuclei, often used without microscopic justification, is shown to be consistent with self-consistent mean-field calculations and with available experimental data for band-to-band transitions in ¹⁵⁶Gd, though the experimental uncertainties are too large to constitute a strong test of the relationship.

## Method

The author derives an analytic formula relating the mean square radius to the mass quadrupole moment under the assumption of irrotational, divergence-free flow for axially symmetric deformations. This formula is tested against self-consistent mean-field calculations using three different energy functionals (Gogny D1S, BCPM, and Skyrme SLy4) for the nucleus ¹⁵⁶Gd, and the predicted monopole matrix element is compared with experimental data from the decay of the first excited K^π = 0⁺ band to the ground-state band.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵⁶Gd, the intrinsic quadrupole moment of the ground-state band from self-consistent mean-field theory is about 800 fm², giving q ≈ 0.13 and F'(q) ≈ 0.22.
- The experimental band-to-band matrix element of the charge quadrupole operator ⟨g|Q̂²_ch|0⁺₂⟩ is reported as 30 e-fm².
- The predicted monopole matrix element from the incompressibility formula is ⟨g|r̂²_ch|0⁺₂⟩ = 6.6 e-fm².
- The experimental range for the monopole matrix element is 6–10 fm².

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of nuclear compressibility, previously focused on giant monopole resonances (Bortignon and collaborators, 1988; Varga, 2001; Colò, 2001), to low-lying monopole transitions in deformed nuclei, a topic reviewed by Garrett (2001). The incompressible fluid assumption for shape changes dates back to early nuclear physics (Carter, 1935) and was formalized by Reiner (1961) and Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The derived formula provides a microscopic justification for this assumption within self-consistent mean-field theory and can be tested further when more precise monopole measurements become available, potentially enabling new constraints on nuclear compressibility from low-energy structure.
