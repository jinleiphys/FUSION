# Vector Manifestation of the Chiral Symmetry

> We propose ``vector manifestation (VM)'' of the Wigner realization of the chiral symmetry in which the symmetry is restored at the critical point by the massless degenerate pion (and its flavor partners) and rho meson (and its flavor partners) as the chiral partner, in sharp contrast to the traditional manifestation á la linear sigma model where the symmetry is restored by the degenerate pion and scalar meson. The application to the chiral phase transition of the large Nf QCD is performed using the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian. Combined with the Wilsonian matching proposed recently, the VM determines the critical number of massless flavors Nf \simeq 5 without much ambiguity.

# Vector Manifestation of the Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim

The authors propose that at the chiral phase transition point, the Wigner realization of chiral symmetry is manifested by the pion and the rho meson becoming massless degenerate chiral partners, rather than the traditional linear sigma model picture of degenerate pion and scalar meson. This "vector manifestation" (VM) is argued to be the only consistent realization in QCD when the Wilsonian matching condition (which includes quadratic divergence and yields a non-zero pion decay constant at the cutoff scale) is imposed.

## Method

The authors apply the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) effective field theory, which includes both pion and rho meson fields, to the large N_f QCD chiral phase transition. Bare parameters of the HLS Lagrangian at the cutoff scale Lambda are determined by matching the axialvector and vector current correlators from the HLS to those from the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD, using the Wilsonian matching procedure that incorporates quadratic divergence. The critical number of massless flavors N_f^cr is then derived by requiring that the order parameter F_pi(0) vanishes at the critical point, using the renormalization-group equation for F_pi^2 in the vector limit where the HLS gauge coupling g goes to zero and the parameter a goes to 1.

## Key numbers

- Critical number of massless flavors: N_f^cr approximately 4.9, estimated using parameters for N_f=3: cutoff Lambda_3 = 1.2 GeV, Lambda_QCD = 0.35 GeV, alpha_s = 0.56, and gluon condensate <(alpha_s/pi) G_{munu} G^{munu}> = 0.012 GeV^4.
- A more precise estimate yields N_f^cr approximately 4.8, consistent with the above value.
- For N_f=3, the physical inputs used are F_pi(0) = 88 MeV and m_rho = 770 MeV.
- Near the critical point, the order parameter scales as F_pi^2(0)/Lambda_f^2 ~ epsilon, and the rho mass scales as m_rho^2/Lambda_f^2 ~ epsilon^{1+q}, where epsilon = 1/N_f - 1/N_f^cr and q >= 1, so m_rho approaches zero faster than F_pi.
- The rho-gamma mixing strength g_rho ~ epsilon^{1+q/2} and the rho-pi-pi coupling g_{rho pi pi} ~ epsilon^{q/2} both go to zero at the critical point.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Wilsonian matching approach (Harada and Yamawaki, 1999) which successfully predicted pion and rho properties for N_f=3, and earlier work (Harada and Yamawaki, 1999 letter) showing that the renormalization-group equation for F_pi^2 including quadratic divergence can drive the order parameter to zero for larger N_f. The VM is contrasted with the traditional linear sigma model manifestation and with Georgi's "vector realization" (Georgi, 1990), which the authors argue lacks the quadratic divergence and therefore incorrectly predicts F_pi(0) non-zero even at the critical point. The results enable predictions for the chiral phase transition in large N_f QCD that can be tested by lattice simulations (vanishing m_rho/F_pi(0) ratio) and may apply to finite temperature/density phase transitions (observable in dilepton spectra) and to technicolor models with light composite vector bosons.
