# Total cross section for p-d breakup below 30 MeV

> The total cross section for p-d breakup is studied in terms of the elastic S-matrix through the unitary condition. Calculations using the complex Kohn variational method along with the Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis are presented. The results have been restricted to energies below Ep=30 MeV where Coulomb effects are expected to be sizable and are compared to the existing data. Two different measurements have been found in the literature: 40 years ago, Gibbons and Macklin (1959); and 26 years ago, Carlosn et al. (1973). The calculations are found to be in reasonable agreement with these old data, though a discrepancy is observed near the deuteron breakup threshold. Moreover, a detailed analysis of the contributions to the observable from different partial waves has been presented. Unexpectedly, the main contribution for a wide range of energies has been detected in the J=3/2- state.

## Key claim

The total cross section for p-d breakup can be calculated from the elastic S-matrix via the unitary condition using the complex Kohn variational method with a Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis, and the results show reasonable agreement with old experimental data, though a discrepancy is observed near the deuteron breakup threshold.

## Method

The authors computed the p-d total breakup cross section from the elastic S-matrix elements using the unitary condition, which relates the missing flux in the elastic channel to the breakup cross section. The three-nucleon scattering wave function was expanded in a Pair Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis, and the elastic S-matrix elements were obtained via the complex Kohn variational principle. Calculations were performed with the Argonne v18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon interaction, and in some cases the Urbana three-nucleon force (3NF) was included to study sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy is B_d = 2.225 MeV.
- The deuteron breakup threshold is at incident nucleon energies below 3.337 MeV or incident deuteron energies below 6.675 MeV.
- At E_p = 3.5 MeV, the systematic uncertainty of the Gibbons and Macklin (1959) measurement is estimated to be 40%, decreasing to 5% at E_p = 5.5 MeV.
- At E_p = 4.9 MeV, the measured p-d breakup cross section is sigma_b(p-d) = 21.5 +/- 1.4 mb, compared to sigma_b(n-d) = 34 +/- 6 mb at the same energy (Holmberg, 1969), showing a Coulomb reduction of approximately 30%.
- The Carlson et al. (1973) measurements cover proton energies between 22 and 46 MeV with uncertainties between 8% and 5%.
- The accuracy of the PHH calculations is estimated to be better than 1%.
- At 3 MeV, calculations with AV18 and AV18+UR differ by 20% for the 2S_{1/2} phase-shift.
- The inclusion of the three-nucleon force (AV18+UR) reduces sigma_b by 3% to 4%.
- States with J > 11/2+ make a negligible contribution to sigma_b below 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PHH variational method, previously applied to elastic N-d scattering below the deuteron breakup threshold (Kievsky et al., 1997; Kievsky et al., 1999; Viviani et al., 2000), to calculate the total breakup cross section for p-d scattering above threshold. The results are compared to two old experimental datasets: Gibbons and Macklin (1959) below 5.6 MeV and Carlson et al. (1973) between 22 and 46 MeV. The paper identifies a gap in experimental data between 6 and 22 MeV and calls for new high-precision measurements, which would enable single-energy phase-shift analyses with complex phases and provide stringent tests of theoretical methods for the charged three-nucleon system.
