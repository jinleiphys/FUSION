# Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model

> The ratio of shear viscosity ($η$) to entropy density ($s$) for an equilibrated system is investigated in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions below 100$A$ MeV within the framework of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model . After the collision system almost reaches a local equilibration, the temperature, pressure and energy density are obtained from the phase space information and {$η/s$} is calculated using the Green-Kubo formulas. The results show that {$η$}/$s$ decreases with incident energy and tend towards a smaller value around 0.5, which is not so drastically different from the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider results in the present model.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) for an equilibrated nuclear system in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions below 100A MeV decreases with incident energy and tends toward a value around 0.5, but the BUU model shows no obvious minimum that would indicate a liquid-gas phase transition, possibly due to the model's lack of dynamical fluctuation and correlation.

## Method

The authors simulated head-on Au+Au collisions (b = 0 fm) using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with both soft (K = 200 MeV) and hard (K = 380 MeV) equations of state. After the system reached local equilibrium within a spherical volume of radius 5 fm, they extracted temperature, pressure, and energy density from phase space information, then calculated the shear viscosity using the Green-Kubo formula from stress tensor fluctuations and the entropy density via the Gibbs formula with a nucleon chemical potential of 20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- η/s decreases with incident energy and tends toward a value around **0.5** for beam energies above 70A MeV in Au+Au collisions.
- A turning point in the derivative of η/s versus beam energy appears around **E ∼ 65A MeV**.
- The soft EOS (compressibility K = **200 MeV**) and hard EOS (K = **380 MeV**) produce different η/s values at lower beam energies, with the hard EOS giving larger η/s.
- The relaxation time extracted from the correlation function decreases as incident energy increases, shown for the Au+Au system at **50A MeV**.
- Using zero chemical potential instead of μ_n = 20 MeV increases entropy by about **8%** and decreases η/s by about **8%**.
- All calculated η/s values are well above the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) lower bound of **1/4π**.

## Context

This work extends the study of η/s from ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC (where values near the KSS bound are found) to intermediate energies below 100A MeV, where liquid-gas phase transition is expected. The results are compared with earlier work by Shlomo on finite nuclei at low temperature and with RHIC results from Song and others. The authors note that the BUU model, being a one-body theory, cannot treat fragmentation or phase transition behavior, and suggest that future work using quantum molecular dynamics-type models (such as QMD or SMM) should be checked for a possible minimum of η/s around the liquid-gas phase transition point.
