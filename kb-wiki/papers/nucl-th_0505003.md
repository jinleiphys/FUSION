# Canonical Quantization of SU(3) Skyrme Model in a General Representation

> A complete canonical quantization of the SU(3) Skyrme model performed in the collective coordinate formalism in general irreducible representations. In the case of SU(3) the model differs qualitatively in different representations. The Wess-Zumino-Witten term vanishes in all self-adjoint representations in the collective coordinate method for separation of space and time variables. The canonical quantization generates representation dependent quantum mass corrections, which can stabilize the soliton solution. The standard symmetry breaking mass term, which in general leads to representation mixing, degenerates to the SU(2) form in all self-adjoint representations.

## Key claim

The paper performs a complete canonical quantization of the SU(3) Skyrme model in general irreducible representations, showing that the Wess-Zumino-Witten term vanishes in all self-adjoint representations and that the symmetry breaking mass term degenerates to the SU(2) form in those same representations, contrary to the behavior in non-self-adjoint representations.

## Method

The authors extend the collective coordinate formalism for canonical quantization of the Skyrme model from SU(2) to general irreducible representations \((\lambda,\mu)\) of SU(3), using Wigner D-matrices for the unitary field and working on the coset space SU(3)/U(1). They derive the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian density operators explicitly in terms of SU(3) generators, compute the representation-dependent quantum mass corrections from the canonical commutation relations, and evaluate the Wess-Zumino-Witten term's contribution via the cubic Casimir operator.

## Key numbers

- The quadratic Casimir operator eigenvalue for irrep \((\lambda,\mu)\) is \(C_2^{SU(3)}(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{1}{3}(\lambda^2 + \mu^2 + \lambda\mu + 3\lambda + 3\mu)\).
- The cubic Casimir operator eigenvalue is \(C_3^{SU(3)}(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{1}{9}(\lambda-\mu)(2\lambda+\mu+3)(2\mu+\lambda+3)\).
- The dimension of irrep \((\lambda,\mu)\) is \(\dim(\lambda,\mu) = \frac{1}{2}(\lambda+1)(\mu+1)(\lambda+\mu+2)\).
- The soliton moments of inertia are given as integrals: \(a_1(F) = \frac{1}{e^3 f_\pi} \frac{8\pi}{3} \int d\tilde{r} \tilde{r}^2 \sin^2 F [1 + F'^2 + \frac{1}{r^2}\sin^2 F]\) and \(a_{1/2}(F) = \frac{1}{e^3 f_\pi} 2\pi \int d\tilde{r} \tilde{r}^2 (1-\cos F)[1 + \frac{1}{4}F'^2 + \frac{1}{2\tilde{r}^2}\sin^2 F]\).
- The normalization factor for the baryon number is \(N = \frac{1}{4} \dim(\lambda,\mu) C_2^{SU(3)}(\lambda,\mu)\), chosen so that the smallest nontrivial baryon number equals unity.

## Context

This work extends the canonical quantization approach previously applied to SU(2) Skyrme models by Norvaisas, Acus, and collaborators (1997-1998) and to SU(3) in the fundamental representation by Fujii and collaborators (1987-1988). It generalizes the formalism to arbitrary irreducible representations of SU(3), revealing that the Wess-Zumino-Witten term vanishes in self-adjoint representations due to the vanishing cubic Casimir operator, and that the symmetry breaking mass term reduces to SU(2) form in those representations. This enables future phenomenological applications in hyperon and hypernuclear physics, as well as in Skyrme model descriptions of the quantum Hall effect and Bose-Einstein condensates.
