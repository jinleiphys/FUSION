# Competition of deformation and neutron-proton pairing in Gamow-Teller transitions for $^{56, 58}$Ni and $^{62,64}$Ni

> We investigated effects of neutron-proton ($np$) pairing correlations comprising isoscalar (IS) and isovector (IV) component on Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions of $^{56, 58,62,64}$Ni by taking into account nuclear deformation in a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field. Our GT calculations based on a deformed Bardeen-Cooper-Schriffer (DBCS) approach have been performed within a deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA) which explicitly includes the deformation as well as all kinds of pairing correlations not only at the DBCS but also at the DQRPA stage. Competition of these two effects, the deformation and the IS spin-triplet and IV spin-singlet component of the $np$ pairing, is shown to be significant for understanding the GT strength distributions of Ni isotopes.

## Key claim

The competition between nuclear deformation and neutron-proton (np) pairing correlations, specifically the isoscalar (IS) spin-triplet and isovector (IV) spin-singlet components, significantly affects Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions in Ni isotopes, but the relative importance of these two effects depends on the neutron excess: for the N=Z nucleus 56Ni the np pairing dominates, while for 58Ni the deformation effect is more important, and for the more neutron-rich 62,64Ni the trend digresses again.

## Method

The authors performed calculations using a deformed Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (DBCS) approach followed by a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (DQRPA), both based on a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field. The residual nucleon-nucleon interaction was derived from the Brueckner G-matrix based on the realistic Bonn CD potential, and all types of pairing correlations (proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton including both IS and IV components) were included at both the DBCS and DQRPA stages. The particle model space was exploited up to N=5 hbar-omega for the deformed basis and up to N=10 hbar-omega for the spherical basis.

## Key numbers

- For 56Ni, the empirical pairing gaps are Delta_p^emp = 2.077 MeV and Delta_n^emp = 2.150 MeV, with an empirical np pairing gap delta_np^emp = 1.107 MeV; the Ikeda sum rule for GT transitions is satisfied at 99.6% of 3(N-Z)
- For 58Ni, the empirical pairing gaps are Delta_p^emp = 1.669 MeV and Delta_n^emp = 1.349 MeV, with delta_np^emp = 0.233 MeV; the Ikeda sum rule is satisfied at 98.6%
- For 62Ni, the empirical pairing gaps are Delta_p^emp = 1.747 MeV and Delta_n^emp = 1.639 MeV, with delta_np^emp = 0.465 MeV; the Ikeda sum rule is satisfied at 99.1%
- For 64Ni, the empirical pairing gaps are Delta_p^emp = 1.747 MeV and Delta_n^emp = 1.602 MeV, with delta_np^emp = 0.454 MeV; the Ikeda sum rule is satisfied at 99.2%
- The experimental deformation parameters |beta_2^E2| are 0.173 for 56Ni, 0.182 for 58Ni, 0.197 for 62Ni, and 0.179 for 64Ni
- For the enhanced IS pairing test in 56Ni, the IS pairing factor was taken as 3.0 relative to the normal value

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DQRPA studies of sd-shell nuclei (Ha 2015, Ha 2016, Ha 2017) to pf-shell Ni isotopes, and addresses the interplay of deformation and np pairing that was not explicitly treated in earlier spherical QRPA calculations by Cheoun (1993) or in the self-consistent pn-QRPA by Nilsen (2004). The results enable comparison with experimental GT data from Sasano (2011) for 56Ni(p,n) and Fujita (2007) for 58Ni, and with the discussion by Bai (2014) on the role of IS np pairing in creating low-lying GT states in pf-shell N=Z+2 nuclei.
