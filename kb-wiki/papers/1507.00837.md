# Diversities in the properties of neutron stars at a fixed neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb nucleus

> We study the diversities in the properties of the neutron stars arising due to the different choices for the cross-coupling between various mesons which governs the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy in the extended relativistic mean-field(RMF) model. For this purpose, we obtain two different families of the extended RMF model corresponding to different non-linear cross-coupling term in the isovector part of the effective Lagrangian density. The lowest order contributions for the $δ$ mesons are also included. The different models within the same family are so obtained that they yield wide variation in the value of neutron-skin thickness in the $^{208}$Pb nucleus. These models are employed to compute the neutron star properties such as, core-crust transition density, radius and red shift at canonical mass ($1.4M_{\odot}$), tidal polarizability parameter, and threshold mass required for the enhanced cooling through direct Urca process. Most of the neutron star properties considered are significantly different(10\%-40\%) for the different families of models at a smaller neutron-skin thickness ($\sim 0.15$ fm) in the $^{208}$Pb nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that even when the neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb is fixed to a specific value (e.g., $\sim 0.15$ fm), different choices for the cross-coupling terms in the isovector part of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian, specifically $\sigma-\rho$ versus $\omega-\rho$ coupling, produce significantly different predictions (10%-40%) for neutron star properties such as radius, core-crust transition density, and threshold mass for direct Urca cooling.

## Method

Two families of extended RMF models were constructed, labeled $F_\rho$ (with $\sigma-\rho$ cross-coupling) and $F_{2\rho}$ (with $\omega-\rho$ cross-coupling), both including the lowest-order contributions from $\delta$ mesons. For each family, the coupling constants $g_\rho$, $g_\delta$, and either $\eta_\rho$ or $\eta_{2\rho}$ were varied to produce a wide range of neutron-skin thicknesses in $^{208}$Pb while keeping the isoscalar parameters fixed to the BKA22 model and maintaining agreement with binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei. The resulting equations of state were used to compute neutron star properties by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, with core-crust transition densities determined via relativistic random-phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: $M_{\rm max} = 1.95 - 2.02 M_\odot$ for the $F_\rho$ family; $M_{\rm max} = 1.91 - 1.98 M_\odot$ for the $F_{2\rho}$ family
- Radius for canonical mass ($1.4 M_\odot$): $R_{1.4} = 13.3 - 15.4$ km for $F_\rho$; $R_{1.4} = 12.3 - 14.9$ km for $F_{2\rho}$
- At fixed neutron-skin thickness $\Delta r_{\rm np} = 0.15$ fm in $^{208}$Pb, the maximum difference in $R_{1.4}$ between the two families is $\sim 1.0$ km, and the difference in red shift $Z_{1.4}$ is $\sim 0.02$
- Symmetry energy slope parameter $L$ ranges from 23.3 MeV (SET4, $F_{2\rho}$ with $\delta$ mesons) to 79.0 MeV (SET1, $F_\rho$ without $\delta$ mesons)
- Core-crust transition density $\rho_t$ is significantly larger for the $F_{2\rho}$ family at smaller $\Delta r_{\rm np}$ values

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Fattoyev (2012) and Erler (2013) that examined correlations between neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb and neutron star properties using TOV-min (Skyrme-type) and FSU (RMF) models. The present study systematically investigates how different choices for the isovector cross-coupling terms ($\sigma-\rho$ vs. $\omega-\rho$) and the inclusion of $\delta$ mesons affect neutron star properties at a fixed neutron-skin thickness, which had not been previously examined in detail. The results demonstrate that tighter correlations observed in earlier RMF studies may stem from limited freedom in the isovector part of the Lagrangian, and that future models aiming to simultaneously describe neutron-skin measurements and neutron star observations may need to include multiple cross-coupling terms and $\delta$ meson contributions.
