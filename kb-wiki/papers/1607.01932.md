# Probing Transverse Momentum Broadening via Dihadron and Hadron-jet Angular Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> Dijet, dihadron, hadron-jet angular correlations have been reckoned as important probes of the transverse momentum broadening effects in relativistic nuclear collisions. When a pair of high-energy jets created in hard collisions traverse the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, they become de-correlated due to the vacuum soft gluon radiation associated with the Sudakov logarithms and the medium-induced transverse momentum broadening. For the first time, we employ the systematical resummation formalism and establish a baseline calculation to describe the dihadron and hadron-jet angular correlation data in $pp$ and peripheral $AA$ collisions where the medium effect is negligible. We demonstrate that the medium-induced broadening $\langle p_\perp^2\rangle$ and the so-called jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ can be extracted from the angular de-correlations observed in $AA$ collisions. A global $χ^2$ analysis of dihadron and hadron-jet angular correlation data renders the best fit $\langle p_\perp^2 \rangle \sim 13~\textrm{GeV}^2$ for a quark jet at RHIC top energy. Further experimental and theoretical efforts along the direction of this work shall significantly advance the quantitative understanding of transverse momentum broadening and help us acquire unprecedented knowledge of jet quenching parameter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Probing Transverse Momentum Broadening via Dihadron and Hadron-jet Angular Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that the medium-induced transverse momentum broadening ⟨p⊥²⟩ and the jet quenching parameter q̂ can be extracted from angular de-correlations of dihadron and hadron-jet pairs in AA collisions, but only for events with smaller jet energies or in lower energy collisions; at LHC energies with high jet transverse momenta (above 120 GeV), the angular de-correlation is completely insensitive to medium-induced broadening due to overwhelming perturbative Sudakov contributions.

## Method

The authors employ a Sudakov resummation formalism that separates vacuum parton shower contributions from medium-induced broadening effects in auxiliary b-space, then convolute partonic cross sections with collinear fragmentation functions and Sudakov factors. They perform a global χ² analysis using the CERN MINUIT package on dihadron and hadron-jet angular correlation data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, focusing on the region |π−Δφ| < π/4 to suppress rare hard processes, and extract the medium-induced broadening by comparing pp baseline calculations to AA data.

## Key numbers

- Best fit medium-induced broadening ⟨p⊥²⟩ = 13⁺⁵₋₄ GeV² for a quark jet in central Au-Au collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC, from global χ² analysis of dihadron and hadron-jet angular correlation data
- Extracted jet quenching parameter q̂₀ = 3.9⁺¹·⁵₋₁·² GeV²/fm for a quark jet at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c at the center of the medium produced in central Au-Au collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC
- Non-perturbative Sudakov factor for dihadron production required multiplication by a factor of 4.8 relative to the Drell-Yan and DIS parameterization (g₁ = 0.212, g₂ = 0.84, Q₀² = 2.4 GeV², b_max = 1.5 GeV⁻¹)
- Non-perturbative Sudakov factor for hadron-jet production required multiplication by a factor of 1.3 relative to the same parameterization
- A 5-10% fractional energy loss on associated jets leads to increase of the central q̂ value by 15-35%

## Context

This work extends the Sudakov resummation framework previously applied to dijet angular correlations at the LHC by Mueller et al. (2016) and generalizes it to dihadron and hadron-jet correlations. It provides a new and complementary method to the JET Collaboration effort (Burke et al., 2013), which extracted q̂ from single hadron production data; the central value obtained here (3.9 GeV²/fm) is a few times larger than the JET Collaboration value of 1.2 GeV²/fm for a 10 GeV quark jet. The framework enables future quantitative studies of jet quenching parameter with more precise experimental data and can be generalized to forward rapidity dihadron production and gluon saturation effects, as well as RHIC beam energy scan measurements.
