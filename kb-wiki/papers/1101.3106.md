# Self-consistent description of nuclear photoabsorption cross sections

> Several approaches to photonuclear reactions, based on the time-dependent density-functional theory, have been developed recently. The standard linearization leads to the random-phase approximation (RPA) or the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA). We have developed a parallelized QRPA computer program for axially deformed nuclei. We also present a feasible approach to the (Q)RPA calculation, that is the finite amplitude method (FAM). We show results of photoabsorption cross sections for deformed nuclei using the QRPA and FAM calculations. Finally, the canonical-basis approach to the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method is presented, to demonstrate its feasibility and usefulness.

## Key claim

The paper presents three theoretical approaches (QRPA, FAM, and Cb-TDHFB) for calculating nuclear photoabsorption cross sections, claiming that the Cb-TDHFB method can reproduce QRPA results at the small-amplitude limit with significantly lower computational cost, though the QRPA and FAM calculations systematically underestimate the GDR peak energy by a few MeV for light nuclei.

## Method

The authors developed a parallelized QRPA computer code for axially deformed nuclei using Skyrme functionals, solving the HFB equation in cylindrical coordinates with a two-quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV. They also implemented the finite amplitude method (FAM), which avoids explicit linearization of the residual field by numerically computing the Hamiltonian variation with a small parameter η, using an iterative algorithm to solve the RPA equations. Finally, they formulated the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) method, which assumes diagonal pair potential in the canonical basis and evolves only the canonical states (number proportional to particle number) rather than all quasiparticle states.

## Key numbers

- Two-quasiparticle energy cutoff: 60 MeV; for the Kπ=0− excitation in 154Sm, this yields about 38,000 two-quasiparticle states.
- QRPA calculation for 154Sm requires roughly 1,000 CPU hours, while the Cb-TDHFB calculation in full 3D space requires roughly 50 CPU hours.
- For nuclei with A ≤ 40, observed dipole strength up to 30 MeV exhausts only 60–100% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum-rule value.
- The Cb-TDHFB calculation uses quasiparticle truncation at maximum quasiparticle energy Eqp = 200 MeV for 50Ca.
- The HFB calculation for 154Sm with SkM* parameters produces a prolate deformation of β = 0.31.

## Context

This work extends the standard RPA and QRPA approaches (Ring and Schuck, 1980) for deformed nuclei by introducing the FAM (Nakatsukasa et al., 2007) as a computationally efficient alternative to explicit matrix construction, and the Cb-TDHFB method (Ebata et al., 2010) as a feasible real-time approach applicable beyond linear response. The systematic QRPA calculations on Nd and Sm isotopes show excellent agreement with experimental data from spherical, transitional, to deformed nuclei, particularly for the evolution of the GDR width as a function of neutron number. The Cb-TDHFB method enables future studies of large-amplitude nuclear dynamics beyond the linear approximation, such as fission, where pairing correlations are crucial.
