# Structure of krypton isotopes within the interacting boson model derived from the Gogny energy density functional

> The evolution and coexistence of the nuclear shapes as well as the corresponding low-lying collective states and electromagnetic transition rates are investigated along the Krypton isotopic chain within the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM). The IBM Hamiltonian is determined through mean-field calculations based on the several parametrizations of the Gogny energy density functional and the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian. The mean-field energy surfaces, as functions of the axial $β$ and triaxial $γ$ quadrupole deformations, are mapped onto the expectation value of the interacting-boson Hamiltonian that explicitly includes the particle-hole excitations. The resulting boson Hamiltonian is then used to compute low-energy excitation spectra as well as E2 and E0 transition probabilities for $^{70-100}$Kr. Our results point to a number of examples of the prolate-oblate shape transitions and coexistence both on the neutron-deficient and neutron-rich sides. A reasonable agreement with the available experimental data is obtained for the considered nuclear properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian derived from Gogny energy density functional mean-field calculations can reproduce the prolate-oblate shape transitions and coexistence in krypton isotopes across a wide isotopic range, but the agreement with experimental data is described as "reasonable" rather than exact, and the method relies on the assumption that the interpretation of shape coexistence from the lead region also holds for krypton nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to obtain deformation energy surfaces as functions of axial β and triaxial γ quadrupole deformations for 70-100Kr. These microscopic energy surfaces were then mapped onto the expectation value of a configuration-mixing IBM Hamiltonian that includes up to three boson spaces corresponding to 0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h proton particle-hole excitations across the Z=28 shell gap. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized to compute low-energy excitation spectra and E2 and E0 transition probabilities, with the effective charges for electromagnetic operators fitted to experimental data for 76Kr.

## Key numbers

- The study covers the isotopic chain 70-100Kr, with neutron numbers ranging from N=34 to N=64
- For 74Kr, the Gogny-D1M energy surface shows two oblate minima at β=0.04 and β=0.15, plus a prolate minimum at β=0.48
- For 76Kr, a spherical global minimum is found (associated with the N=40 sub-shell closure) with a shallow oblate minimum around β=0.17
- For 96-100Kr on the neutron-rich side, a prolate local minimum appears at β≈0.4
- The ratio e0^d/e0^s = 1.4 was assumed for the E0 effective charges to obtain overall agreement with experimental ρ²(E0; 0⁺₂→0⁺₁) values around N=40
- The energy difference between neighboring contours in the deformation energy surface plots is 100 keV

## Context

This work extends the fermion-to-boson mapping procedure previously applied to Ru, Mo, Zr, Sr isotopes (Nomura 2016) and neutron-rich Ge and Se nuclei (Nomura 2017) to the krypton isotopic chain. The study addresses the open question of how shape transitions evolve in neutron-rich Kr isotopes beyond N≈60, where recent experiments (Albers 2012, 2013; Dudouet 2017; Flavigny 2017) have shown a more moderate onset of deformation compared to the rapid shape transition around N=60 in neighboring Sr and Zr nuclei. The method enables systematic spectroscopic predictions for regions where experimental data are scarce, complementing large-scale shell model calculations (Caurier 2005) that become computationally prohibitive for heavy open-shell nuclei.
