# Effects of hadronic rescattering on multistrange hadrons in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We study the effects of hadronic rescattering on hadron distributions in high-energy nuclear collisions by using an integrated dynamical approach. This approach is based on a hybrid model combining (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the quark gluon plasma (QGP), and a transport model for the hadron resonance gas. Since the hadron distributions are the result of the entire expansion history of the system, understanding the QGP properties requires investigating how rescattering during the hadronic stage affects the final distributions of hadrons. We include multistrange hadrons in our study, and quantify the effects of hadronic rescattering on their mean transverse momenta and elliptic flow. We find that multistrange hadrons scatter less during the hadronic stage than nonstrange particles, and thus their distributions reflect the properties of the system in an earlier stage than the distributions of nonstrange particles.

## Key claim

Multistrange hadrons (particularly φ mesons and Ω baryons) have smaller scattering cross-sections with pions than non-strange hadrons because they do not form resonances, and therefore their final distributions reflect the properties of the system at an earlier stage (immediately after hadronization) than the distributions of non-strange particles, making them cleaner probes of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors employed an integrated dynamical approach combining (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma stage with the JAM hadronic cascade model for the subsequent hadron resonance gas stage, using the s95p-v1.1 equation of state that connects lattice QCD results to a hadron resonance gas. Initial conditions were generated from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with a switching temperature of 155 MeV from hydrodynamics to the hadronic cascade, and the hadronic rescattering effects were quantified by comparing results with and without hadronic rescatterings activated in JAM.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: T_sw = 155 MeV, chosen to reproduce observed p_T spectra of pions, kaons, and protons at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamics: τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- φ meson lifetime: approximately 47 fm/c, larger than the typical system lifetime of approximately 10 fm/c
- Ratio of Σ^0 to Λ at switching temperature estimated as approximately 0.61 from thermal weights; after resonance decay and rescattering corrections, final ratio is around 0.3
- Model reproduces pion data up to p_T approximately 3 GeV/c in central (0-12%) Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Shor (1985), van Hecke (1998), Bass (2000), Cheng (2003), and Hirano (2008) on multistrange hadrons as probes of the quark-gluon plasma, using a more realistic equation of state (s95p-v1.1 from Huovinen, 2009) that includes all resonances in the JAM cascade model. The violation of mass ordering in v_2(p_T) predicted by Hirano (2008) and observed by the STAR Collaboration is investigated systematically, and the inclusion of Ξ and Ω baryons goes beyond earlier work that only considered hadrons up to the mass of Δ(1232). The results enable quantitative assessment of how much final observables retain information from the hydrodynamic stage, and suggest that finite but small viscosity may be needed to better match STAR elliptic flow data.
