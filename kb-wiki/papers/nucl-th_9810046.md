# Elastic Scattering of Pions From the Three-nucleon System

> We examine the scattering of charged pions from the trinucleon system at a pion energy of 180 MeV. The motivation for this study is the structure seen in the experimental angular distribution of back-angle scattering for pi+ 3He and pi- 3H but for neither pi- 3He nor pi+ 3H. We consider the addition of a double spin flip term to an optical model treatment and find that, though the contribution of this term is non-negligible at large angles for pi+ 3He and pi- 3H, it does not reproduce the structure seen in the experiment.

## Key claim

The double spin flip (DSF) term, added as a second-order correction to an optical model treatment of charged pion scattering from the trinucleon system at 180 MeV, gives a non-negligible contribution at large angles for pi+ 3He and pi- 3H, but it does not reproduce the distinctive dip and rise structure seen in the experimental angular distributions for those two channels.

## Method

The authors calculated the double spin flip amplitude in the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) as a second-order correction to the optical model of Gibbs and Gibson (1990). They used two independent evaluation techniques: one based on integration by parts yielding a local operator, and another using covariant components of the gradient operator in a spherical basis. The calculations used Faddeev densities for 3He and 3H, with the optical model parameters fixed at energy shift = 0 MeV, angle transform parameter = 1, rho^2 coefficient = 8.8 fm^4, and off-shell ranges (s and p) at 600 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Pion kinetic energy: T_pi = 180 MeV, near the peak of the P33 resonance for pi-nucleon scattering
- At this energy, the elastic scattering cross sections are in the ratio sigma(pi+ p)/sigma(pi+ n) approximately equal to sigma(pi- n)/sigma(pi- p) approximately equal to 9
- The dip in the cross section occurs at approximately 130 degrees lab (or 5.8 fm^-2 momentum transfer)
- The rho^2 coefficient in the optical model was fixed at 8.8 fm^4
- The off-shell range parameter alpha was taken as 600 MeV/c
- The delta-function correction to the DSF amplitude decreased the cross section by less than 4 percent

## Context

This work extends the optical model treatment of Gibbs and Gibson (1990) for pion scattering from the three-nucleon system, motivated by experimental data from Matthews et al. (1990s) showing a distinctive rise in back-angle scattering for pi+ 3He and pi- 3H but not for pi- 3He or pi+ 3H. The double spin flip mechanism was suggested by Scott and Gibbs as a possible explanation, analogous to multiple spin flip effects investigated by Franco (1970s) for pi-4He scattering in the Glauber approximation. The failure of the DSF correction to reproduce the experimental structure points toward the need for a re-examination of the basic scattering theory, possibly using the approach of Garcilazo (1990s) or a more detailed representation of the energy shift as a function of angular momentum as found by Kaufmann and Gibbs (1980s).
