# Extracting $σ_{πN}$ from pionic atoms

> We discuss a recent extraction of the $πN$ $σ$ term $σ_{πN}$ from a large-scale fit of pionic-atom strong-interaction data across the periodic table. The value thus derived, $σ_{πN}^{\rm FG}=57\pm 7$ MeV, is directly connected via the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner expression to the medium-renormalized $πN$ isovector scattering amplitude near threshold. It compares well with the value derived recently by the Bern-Bonn-Jülich group, $σ_{πN}^{\rm RS}=58\pm 5$ MeV, using the Roy-Steiner equations to control the extrapolation of the vanishingly small near threshold $πN$ isoscalar scattering amplitude to zero pion mass.

# Extracting σπN from pionic atoms

## Key claim

The πN σ term extracted from pionic-atom data, σπNFG = 57 ± 7 MeV, is derived from the in-medium renormalization of the πN isovector scattering amplitude b1 using its leading-order density dependence, and this value agrees with modern hadronic phenomenology results (58 ± 5 MeV from the Bern-Bonn-Jülich group) but disagrees with lower values from some lattice QCD calculations (e.g., 41.6 ± 3.8 MeV). The extraction relies on the model introduced by Weise (2000, 2001) and assumes that higher-order corrections to the leading density dependence largely cancel each other.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of the Ericson-Ericson optical potential to 98 data points of strong-interaction level shifts and widths in pionic atoms across the periodic table from Ne to U, including deeply bound states in Sn isotopes and 205Pb. The in-medium πN isovector s-wave amplitude b1 was related to σπN via the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner expression through the density dependence b1 = b1free (1 − σπN ρ / mπ2 fπ2)−1, and σπN was varied as a free parameter in four-parameter fits where the p-wave single-nucleon parameters c0 and c1 were fixed at their SAID threshold values and Re B0 and Re C0 were kept at zero.

## Key numbers

- σπNFG = 57 ± 7 MeV: the extracted value from pionic-atom fits, with a minimum χ2min = 167.1 reached at neutron-skin parameter γ ≈ 1.1 fm.
- σπNRS = 58 ± 5 MeV: the value derived by the Bern-Bonn-Jülich group using Roy-Steiner equations for comparison.
- b1LO = −79 × 10−3 mπ−1: the leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg chiral limit value for the isovector scattering amplitude, using fπ = 92.2 MeV.
- Two sets of free-space scattering lengths from Baru et al. (2011): (b0free, b1free) = (−0.9, −85.3) × 10−3 mπ−1 and (+7.9, −85.4) × 10−3 mπ−1, differing by whether charge-dependent effects are incorporated.
- γ ≈ 1.1 fm: the neutron-skin parameter at the χ2 minimum, consistent with other determinations for 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pionic-atom analyses (Friedman and Gal, 2007, 2014) where σπN was held fixed around 50 MeV, by now treating σπN as a free parameter. It directly connects to the approach of Weise (2000, 2001) and Kaiser, Weise, and collaborators (2003) for in-medium renormalization of the πN isovector amplitude. The result agrees with modern πN phenomenology from the Bern-Bonn-Jülich group (Hoferichter et al., 2015, 2016) and from Ruiz de Elvira et al. (2018), but contradicts lower lattice QCD values such as 41.6 ± 3.8 MeV from the JLQCD collaboration (2018). The method enables a third independent route to σπN that avoids the model dependence of the isoscalar amplitude b0, which is the dominant source of uncertainty in the Roy-Steiner approach.
