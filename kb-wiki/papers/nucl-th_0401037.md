# Enhancement of kinetic energy fluctuations due to expansion

> Global equilibrium fragmentation inside a freeze out constraining volume is a working hypothesis widely used in nuclear fragmentation statistical models. In the framework of classical Lennard Jones molecular dynamics, we study how the relaxation of the fixed volume constraint affects the posterior evolution of microscopic correlations, and how a non-confined fragmentation scenario is established. A study of the dynamical evolution of the relative kinetic energy fluctuations was also performed. We found that asymptotic measurements of such observable can be related to the number of decaying channels available to the system at fragmentation time.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for systems initially equilibrated inside a fixed constraining volume, removing the walls and allowing free expansion enhances relative kinetic energy fluctuations above the canonical expectation value, and that this enhancement reflects the number of decaying channels available at fragmentation time. The hedge is that this enhancement is observed only for certain initial densities and energies, and that the asymptotic fluctuations are mainly determined by total energy rather than initial density.

## Method

Classical molecular dynamics simulations of 147 particles interacting via a 6-12 Lennard-Jones potential were performed. Systems were first equilibrated inside a spherical confining wall at three different densities (ρ₁=0.026σ⁻³, ρ₂=0.10σ⁻³, ρ₃=0.6σ⁻³), then the walls were removed and the systems evolved to asymptotic stability. Fragment mass distributions were analyzed using the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm for asymptotic configurations and the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm (ECRA) for confined states, while the relative kinetic energy fluctuation A_K ≡ Nσ_K²/⟨K⟩² was tracked as a function of time.

## Key numbers

- Three initial densities studied: ρ₁=0.026σ⁻³, ρ₂=0.10σ⁻³, ρ₃=0.6σ⁻³, chosen from three different regions of the phase diagram
- Freeze-out time identified as τ_fo ∼ 40t₀ for ρ₃=0.6σ⁻³ systems
- Fragment formation time τ_ff ∼ τ_fo, meaning information is frozen once fragment surfaces develop
- Enhancement of A_K observed in the energy region 0 ≤ E ≤ 0.5 ε₀ for expanded systems, where fluctuations rose above the canonical expectation value
- Thermodynamic critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition: ρ_c ≈ 0.35σ⁻³, T_c ≈ 1.12 ε₀

## Context

This work extends previous studies of confined classical systems (Chernomoretz et al., 2003) that showed large relative kinetic fluctuations occur in confined systems when volumes are large enough. It contradicts the sharp-boundary assumption used in statistical models like MMMC and SMM, showing that expansion dynamics modifies fragment correlations and that freeze-out configurations from expansion are not equivalent to equilibrium freeze-out with sharp boundaries. The results enable future investigation of whether fragmentation patterns from expansion can be described by information theory with fluctuating volume and radial flow, as proposed by the flow model.
