# Properties of the first excited state of 9Be derived from (gamma,n) and (e,e') reactions

> Properties of the first excited state of the nucleus 9Be are discussed based on recent (e,e') and (gamma,n) experiments. The parameters of an R-matrix analysis of different data sets are consistent with a resonance rather than a virtual state predicted by some model calculations. The energy and the width of the resonance are deduced. Their values are rather similar for all data sets, and the energy proves to be negative. It is argued that the disagreement between the extracted B(E1) values may stem from different ways of integration of the resonance. If corrected, fair agreement between the (e,e') and one of the (gamma,n) data sets is found. A recent (gamma,n) experiment at the HIgS facility exhibits larger cross sections close to the neutron threshold which remain to be explained.

## Key claim

All recent experimental data on the first excited state of 9Be are consistent with a resonance rather than a virtual state, but the extracted resonance energy is negative (below the neutron separation threshold), which is unusual for a typical resonance.

## Method

The authors performed R-matrix analyses of (gamma,n) and (e,e') cross section data from several experiments (Darmstadt, Sumiyoshi et al., Arnold et al.) to extract the energy and width of the 1/2+ state. They fitted the photoabsorption cross section using a one-level R-matrix approximation that includes the correct pole contribution near threshold, and also modeled non-resonant contributions using an effective range approximation to test their influence on the extracted parameters. For the B(E1) strength, they compared two different integration methods: integration to infinity (which yields the R-matrix parameter C) versus integration over a finite energy range covering the resonance peak.

## Key numbers

- R-matrix parameters from Darmstadt data: E_R_bar = 83(6) keV, Gamma_R = 274(8) keV, giving E_res = -31(6) keV and Gamma = 153(4) keV
- R-matrix parameters from Sumiyoshi data: E_R_bar = 70(3) keV, Gamma_R = 225(12) keV, giving E_res = -21 keV and Gamma = 132 keV
- R-matrix parameters from Arnold data: E_R_bar = 66(2) keV, Gamma_R = 213(6) keV, giving E_res = -21 keV and Gamma = 124 keV
- B(E1) upward values from (gamma,n) data: 0.052(1) fm^2 (Sumiyoshi), 0.0535(35) fm^2 (Jap), 0.068(1) fm^2 (Arnold)
- B(E1) upward from (e,e') data using R-matrix fit: 0.057 fm^2
- Finite-range integration (to E_gamma = 2.2039 MeV) gives B(E1) upward = 0.034 fm^2 from Darmstadt (e,e') data and 0.033 fm^2 from Sumiyoshi (gamma,n) data
- The 8Be-n threshold S_n = 1.6654 MeV

## Context

This work resolves a controversy in the literature about whether the 1/2+ state of 9Be is a resonance or a virtual state, with earlier calculations by Koike (1990) and Arai (1995) predicting a virtual state while Furutani (1980) and Garrido (2004) found a resonant state. The analysis also reconciles apparent disagreements between B(E1) values from (e,e') experiments (Darmstadt, Bates) and (gamma,n) experiments (Sumiyoshi, Jap) by showing they arise from different integration conventions. The remaining discrepancy with the latest HIgS facility experiment (Arnold) is noted as unresolved, with a parallel drawn to similar discrepancies in the 48Ca M1 transition studied by Steffen (1983) and Tompkins (2011).
