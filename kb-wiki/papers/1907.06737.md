# Radiative Corrections to Neutron and Nuclear Beta Decays Revisited

> The universal radiative corrections common to neutron and super-allowed nuclear beta decays (also known as ``inner'' corrections) are revisited in light of a recent dispersion relation study that found $+2.467(22)\%$, i.e.~about $2.4σ$ larger than the previous evaluation. For comparison, we consider several alternative computational methods. All employ an updated perturbative QCD four-loop Bjorken sum rule (BjSR) defined QCD coupling supplemented with a nucleon form factor based Born amplitude to estimate axial-vector induced hadronic contributions. In addition, we now include hadronic contributions from low $Q^2$ loop effects based on duality considerations and vector meson resonance interpolators. Our primary result, $2.426(32)\%$ corresponds to an average of a Light Front Holomorphic QCD approach and a three resonance interpolator fit. It reduces the dispersion relation discrepancy to approximately $1.1σ$ and thereby provides a consistency check. Consequences of our new radiative correction estimate, along with that of the dispersion relation result, for CKM unitarity are discussed. The neutron lifetime-$g_A$ connection is updated and shown to suggest a shorter neutron lifetime $< 879$ s. We also find an improved bound on exotic, non-Standard Model, neutron decays or oscillations of the type conjectured as solutions to the neutron lifetime problem, $\text{BR}(n\to \text{exotics}) < 0.16 \%$.

## Key claim
The universal radiative correction to neutron and super-allowed nuclear beta decays is found to be Δ_V^R = 2.426(32)%, which reduces the discrepancy with the recent dispersion relation result (2.467(22)%) to approximately 1.1σ, rather than the 2.4σ tension claimed in the dispersion relation study.

## Method
The authors employ an updated perturbative QCD four-loop Bjorken sum rule (BjSR) defined QCD coupling, supplemented with a nucleon form factor based Born amplitude to estimate axial-vector induced hadronic contributions. They include hadronic contributions from low Q^2 loop effects based on duality considerations and vector meson resonance interpolators, using two complementary approaches: a Light Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) method and a three resonance interpolator fit, which are then averaged.

## Key numbers
- Primary result for universal radiative correction: Δ_V^R = 2.426(32)% (average of LFHQCD and three resonance interpolator approaches)
- Dispersion relation result: Δ_V^R = 2.467(22)% (Seng et al. 2018)
- 2006 evaluation: Δ_V^R = 2.361(38)% (Marciano and Sirlin 2006)
- Transition scale Q_0^2 = 1.10(10) GeV^2 used for matching perturbative and non-perturbative regimes
- Neutron lifetime bound on exotic decays: BR(n → exotics) < 0.16%
- Updated neutron lifetime prediction: τ_n < 879 s
- V_ud from AdS BjSR approach: 0.97391(18)
- V_ud from dispersion relation: 0.97370(14)

## Context
This work revisits the universal radiative corrections to neutron and super-allowed nuclear beta decays in light of a dispersion relation study by Seng, Gorchtein, Patel, and Ramsey-Musolf (2018) that found a 2.4σ larger correction than the previous evaluation by Marciano and Sirlin (2006). The new result provides a consistency check between different computational methods and has implications for CKM unitarity tests, where the dispersion relation result suggested a roughly 3.2σ violation. The analysis also updates the neutron lifetime-g_A connection and improves bounds on exotic neutron decays conjectured as solutions to the neutron lifetime problem.
