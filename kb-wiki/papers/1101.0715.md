# Universality of Unintegrated Gluon Distributions at small x

> We systematically study dijet production in various processes in the small-$x$ limit and establish an effective $k_t$-factorization for hard processes in a system with dilute probes scattering on a dense target. We find that the well-known Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution can be directly probed in the quark-antiquark jet correlation in deep inelastic scattering and the dipole gluon distribution can be directly measured in the direct photon-jet correlation in $pA$ collisions. In the large-$N_c$ limit, the unintegrated gluon distributions involved in other different dijet channels in pA collisions are shown to be related to two widely proposed ones: the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution and the dipole gluon distribution.

## Key claim

The paper establishes an effective transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization for dijet production in collisions of dilute probes on dense targets at small x, showing that the unintegrated gluon distributions involved are not universal but can be constructed from two universal building blocks: the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution and the dipole gluon distribution, with the large-Nc limit required to relate the distributions in more complicated dijet channels.

## Method

The authors systematically studied dijet production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and proton-nucleus (pA) collisions using both the TMD approach and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, focusing on the back-to-back correlation limit where the transverse momentum imbalance q_perp is much smaller than the individual jet momenta P_perp. They derived the operator definitions of the two unintegrated gluon distributions, computed the differential cross sections for each process, and demonstrated consistency between the two approaches in the correlation limit by applying the eikonal approximation and power counting methods.

## Key numbers

- The Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution scales as Q_s^2/q_perp^2 at large transverse momentum (q_perp much greater than Q_s) and as ln(Q_s^2/q_perp^2) at small transverse momentum
- The dipole gluon distribution scales as Q_s^2/q_perp^2 at large transverse momentum (q_perp much greater than Q_s) and as q_perp^2 at small transverse momentum
- The saturation scale Q_s^2 is defined as (g^2 N_c)/(4 pi) ln(1/(r_perp^2 lambda^2)) integral dx^- mu^2(x^-) where N_c=3 is the number of colors
- The differential cross section for dijet production in DIS involves the factor S_perp N_c alpha_em e_q^2/(4 pi^4) times an integral over transverse separation r_perp

## Context

This work extends the understanding of TMD factorization violations for dijet production in hadron-hadron collisions found by Rogers (2010) and Bomhof (2006) by providing a solution in the small-x limit for dilute probes on dense targets. It resolves the long-standing question of whether the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution is observable, showing it can be directly probed in quark-antiquark jet correlations in DIS, while the dipole gluon distribution can be measured in direct photon-jet correlations in pA collisions. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC, LHC, and the planned Electron-Ion Collider to distinguish between these two gluon distributions and test saturation physics.
