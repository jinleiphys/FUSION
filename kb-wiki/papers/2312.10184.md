# Lattice QCD studies of the $Δ$ baryon resonance and the $K_0^\ast(700)$ and $a_0(980)$ meson resonances: the role of exotic operators in determining the finite-volume spectrum

> Studies of the $Δ$ baryon resonance and the $K_0^\ast(700)$ and $a_0(980)$ meson resonances using $N_f=2+1$ lattice QCD for pion masses near 200 MeV are presented. The $s$-wave scattering lengths for both the $I=1/2$ $N π$ and $I=3/2$ $N π$ channels and properties of the $Δ$ resonance are identified from the finite-volume energy levels of the lattice simulation. The importance of a three-quark $Δ$-operator in the $Nπ$ system and tetraquark operators in the mesonic systems is investigated.

# Lattice QCD studies of the Δ baryon resonance and the K₀*(700) and a₀(980) meson resonances: the role of exotic operators in determining the finite-volume spectrum

## Key claim

Using Nf=2+1 lattice QCD with pion masses near 200 MeV, the authors demonstrate that including a local three-quark Δ operator in the Nπ system and tetraquark operators in the K₀*(700) and a₀(980) mesonic systems is crucial for reliably extracting the finite-volume energy spectra; without these operators, energy levels can be missed entirely and resonance behavior may not be observed, even when all standard single- and two-hadron operators are included.

## Method

The finite-volume energy spectra were computed from matrices of temporal correlation functions using the stochastic LapH method on CLS ensembles, with energies extracted via a single-pivot rotation method and two-exponential fits to diagonal elements. For the Δ resonance, the spectrum was obtained in the isoquartet nonstrange Nπ channel using Nπ operators with and without a local three-quark Δ operator. For the K₀*(700) and a₀(980) resonances, spectra were computed in the isodoublet strange and isotriplet nonstrange channels at zero total momentum using meson and meson-meson operators, with and without tetraquark operators; hundreds of tetraquark operator designs were screened on 25 configurations before selecting the best operators for high-statistics analysis. The Lüscher quantization condition was applied using a box-matrix formalism with simple K-matrix parametrizations to extract scattering information.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 200 MeV on the D200 ensemble with lattice spacing a = 0.0633(4)(6) fm, volume 64³ × 128, and 2000 measurements
- Pion mass in lattice units amπ = 0.06617(33), kaon mass amK = 0.15644(16), nucleon mass amN = 0.3148(23) on the D200 ensemble
- For the meson-meson analysis: temporal lattice spacing at = 0.033357(59) fm, anisotropy ξ = 3.451(11), volume 32³ × 256, 412 measurements
- For the κ (K₀*(700)) channel with tetraquark operator: fit parameters A₁ = 1.7(7), s₀ = 4.42(24), mKa₁ = 6.1(3.6), χ²/d.o.f. = 3.08/(6-3)
- For the κ channel without tetraquark operator: A₁ = -0.20(18), s₀ = 10(5), mKa₁ = -0.3(5), χ²/d.o.f. = 5.81/(5-3)
- For the a₀(980) channel with tetraquark operator: mKa₀ = 2.1(5), mKa₁ = -1(3), χ²/d.o.f. = 1.54/(3-2)
- For the a₀(980) channel without tetraquark operator: mKa₀ = -0.1(1.9), mKa₁ = -0.1(1.9), χ²/d.o.f. = 2.67/(3-2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Nπ scattering study (Bulava 2022) by examining the role of the three-quark Δ operator in more detail, and presents preliminary results from an ongoing study (Darvish 2019) of the K₀*(700) and a₀(980) resonances. The findings contradict earlier works (Brett 2018, Wilson 2019) that found no resonance behavior in these channels without tetraquark operators, and support suggestions from other groups (Prelovsek 2010, Alexandrou 2017) that tetraquark operators may be necessary. The results enable future quantitative studies using multiple total momentum channels to fully map out the resonance parameters of the K₀*(700) and a₀(980) using the Lüscher formalism with tetraquark operators.
