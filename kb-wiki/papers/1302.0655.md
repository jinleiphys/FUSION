# Possibility for $J/Ψ$ suppression in high multiplicity proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7\,$TeV

> We study $J/Ψ$ absorption in high multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}=7\,$TeV. We predict a modification of the $J/Ψ$-yield within the UrQMD transport approach, where explicit interactions of the $J/Ψ$ with the surrounding comovers and a prehadronic phase with adjusted cross sections and $J/Ψ$ melting is included. We present the analog of the nuclear modification factor in pp collisions at various charged particle multiplicities. It turns out that $J/Ψ$-Mesons may be suppressed towards higher particle multiplicities in pp collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors predict that J/Ψ mesons may be suppressed by up to 20-30% at very high charged particle multiplicities in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, but note that this is a "possibility" and that the magnitude of the input from Ferreiro (2012) was tuned to describe pp data without additional late stage effects, meaning slight parameter re-adjustment might improve data description.

## Method

The authors modified the UrQMD transport approach to include J/Ψ interactions with the medium across three temperature regimes: a melting regime above 325 MeV, a prehadronic stage between 155 MeV and 325 MeV, and a hadronic stage below 155 MeV. They mapped transition temperatures to scalar quark densities using the chiral equation of state, then used local scalar quark densities at the J/Ψ position to determine cross sections in each time step. The initial J/Ψ momentum distributions were fitted to LHC data from Aamodt (2011) and Khachatryan (2010), while the spatial distribution was obtained by smearing production points with a Gaussian of variance 0.88 fm.

## Key numbers

- J/Ψ melting onset temperature: 325 MeV, corresponding to quark density n_q = 12 fm^{-3}
- Phase transition temperature: 155 MeV, corresponding to quark density n_q = 0.6 fm^{-3}
- Charmonium decay proper time in hot medium: 1 fm/c
- Meson-J/Ψ elastic and inelastic cross section in prehadronic phase: 0.78 mb
- Baryon-J/Ψ dissociation cross section in hadronic phase: 4.18 mb
- Elastic cross section for charmonia in hadronic phase: 5 mb
- At very high charged particle multiplicities (dN_ch/dy > 70 at |y| < 1), J/Ψ suppression may reach 20-30%
- Average suppression in minimum bias collisions: approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends studies of collective effects in high multiplicity pp collisions by Vogel (2010) and Werner (2010, 2011), who found substantial medium influence at high multiplicities. It applies the J/Ψ suppression mechanisms previously studied in nucleus-nucleus collisions (comover absorption by Gavin (1988) and Debye screening by Matsui (1986)) to the new regime of high multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies. The results enable comparison of J/Ψ suppression across different collision systems and energies, suggesting that suppression may depend primarily on charged particle multiplicity rather than collision energy when recombination can be neglected.
