# Parton distributions with small-x resummation: evidence for BFKL dynamics in HERA data

> We present a determination of the parton distribution functions of the proton in which NLO and NNLO fixed-order calculations are supplemented by NLLx small-x resummation. Deep inelastic structure functions are computed consistently at NLO+NLLx or NNLO+NLLx, while for hadronic processes small-x resummation is included only in the PDF evolution, with kinematic cuts introduced to ensure the fitted data lie in a region where the fixed-order calculation of the hard cross-sections is reliable. In all other respects, the fits use the same methodology and are based on the same global dataset as the recent NNPDF3.1 analysis. We demonstrate that the inclusion of small-x resummation leads to a quantitative improvement in the perturbative description of the HERA inclusive and charm-production reduced cross-sections in the small x region. The impact of the resummation in our fits is greater at NNLO than at NLO, because fixed-order calculations have a perturbative instability at small x due to large logarithms that can be cured by resummation. We explore the phenomenological implications of PDF sets with small-x resummation for the longitudinal structure function $F_L$ at HERA, for parton luminosities and LHC benchmark cross-sections, for ultra-high energy neutrino-nucleus cross-sections, and for future high-energy lepton-proton colliders such as the LHeC.

## Key claim
The inclusion of NLLx small-x resummation in parton distribution function (PDF) fits leads to a quantitative improvement in the perturbative description of HERA inclusive and charm-production reduced cross-sections in the small-x region, with the effect being greater at NNLO than at NLO because fixed-order calculations have a perturbative instability at small x due to large logarithms that can be cured by resummation.

## Method
The authors performed global PDF determinations using the NNPDF3.1 methodology and dataset, supplementing NLO and NNLO fixed-order calculations with NLLx small-x resummation. Deep inelastic structure functions were computed consistently at NLO+NLLx or NNLO+NLLx, while for hadronic processes small-x resummation was included only in the PDF evolution, with kinematic cuts (H_cut values of 0.5, 0.6, and 0.7) introduced to ensure fitted data lie in a region where fixed-order calculations of hard cross-sections are reliable.

## Key numbers
- The total chi^2/N_dat for the global NNLO fit was 1.130, improving to 1.100 for the NNLO+NLLx fit (with H_cut=0.6), corresponding to a total Delta chi^2 of -121.
- For the HERA I+II inclusive neutral current data, the chi^2/N_dat improved from 1.17 (NNLO) to 1.11 (NNLO+NLLx), with Delta chi^2 of -62.
- For the HERA charm production cross-section (sigma_c^NC), the chi^2/N_dat improved dramatically from 2.33 (NNLO) to 1.14 (NNLO+NLLx), with Delta chi^2 of -56.
- For the DIS-only fits, the HERA I+II inclusive NC chi^2/N_dat improved from 1.16 (NNLO) to 1.12 (NNLO+NLLx), with Delta chi^2 of -47.
- For the DIS-only fits, the HERA charm production chi^2/N_dat improved from 2.45 (NNLO) to 1.24 (NNLO+NLLx), with Delta chi^2 of -57.
- The differences between NNLO and NNLO+NLLx predictions for the reduced cross-section are at most 10% and only for the smallest values of x.
- At Q^2 approximately 10 GeV^2, the NNLO+NLLx prediction for the longitudinal structure function F_L is more than a factor 2 larger than the NNLO result.

## Context
This work extends the NNPDF3.1 analysis (Ball et al., 2017) by incorporating small-x resummation, providing evidence for BFKL dynamics in HERA data. The diagnostic tools build upon methods first presented by Caola et al. (2010, 2009). The results enable improved predictions for the longitudinal structure function F_L at HERA, parton luminosities and LHC benchmark cross-sections, ultra-high energy neutrino-nucleus cross-sections, and future high-energy lepton-proton colliders such as the LHeC.
