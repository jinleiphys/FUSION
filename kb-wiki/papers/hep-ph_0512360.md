# Enhanced mu-e conversion in nuclei in the inverse seesaw model

> We investigate nuclear mu-e conversion in the framework of an effective Lagrangian arising from the inverse seesaw model of neutrino masses. We consider lepton flavour violation interactions that arise from short range (non-photonic) as well as long range (photonic) contributions. Upper bounds for the LFV parameters characterizing mu-e conversion are derived in the inverse seesaw model Lagrangian using the available limits on the mu-e conversion branching ratio, as well as the expected sensitivities of upcoming experiments. We comment on the relative importance of these two types of contributions and their relationship with the measured solar neutrino mixing angle theta_12 and the dependence on theta_13. Finally we show how the LFV mu-e conversion and the mu -> e gamma rates are strongly correlated in this model.

## Key claim

The inverse seesaw model can produce observable mu-e conversion rates even without supersymmetry, because the heavy neutrino mass scale M can be as low as the weak scale (around 100 GeV) while still explaining neutrino oscillation data, unlike the conventional seesaw where the heavy scale must be much higher.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective quark-level Lagrangian for mu-e conversion in the inverse seesaw model, including both photonic (long-range) and non-photonic (short-range) contributions from Z-boson exchange and box diagrams. They then translated this Lagrangian to the nucleon level using vector current form factors determined by conservation of vector current, and finally to the nuclear level using proton and neutron densities from DeJager (1987) and Gibbs (1987) with muon and electron wave functions obtained by solving the Dirac equation with artificial neural network techniques. The coherent mu-e conversion branching ratio was calculated for three target nuclei of experimental interest: Al-27, Ti-48, and Au-197.

## Key numbers

* Current experimental upper limit on mu-e conversion branching ratio for Au-197: R_mu_e^Au ≤ 5.0 × 10^{-13} at 90% C.L. (SINDRUM II experiment)
* Expected sensitivity for Al-27 target (MECO experiment): R_mu_e^Al ≤ 2 × 10^{-17}
* Expected sensitivity for Ti-48 target (PRISM experiment): R_mu_e^Ti ≤ 10^{-18}
* Typical inverse seesaw parameter relation: (m_nu / 0.1 eV) = (m_D / 100 GeV)^2 × (mu / 1 keV) × (M / 10^4 GeV)^{-2}
* Example model parameters used for mixing angle dependence plots: M = 100 GeV, mu = 10 eV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of lepton flavor violation in the inverse seesaw model, which was originally proposed by Mohapatra (1986) and developed by Bernabeu (1987) and Branco (1989). It differs from the conventional seesaw mechanism of Minkowski (1977) by allowing enhanced LFV rates without requiring a large mass scale or supersymmetry, unlike the supersymmetric inverse seesaw studied by Deppisch (2004). The results enable experimental programs at SINDRUM II, MECO, and PRISM to constrain the inverse seesaw parameter space, and show that mu-e conversion and mu → e gamma rates are strongly correlated in this model, indicating photonic diagram dominance.
