# Hydrodynamic attractor of noisy plasmas

> We provide a generalized formulation of fluctuating hydrodynamics for the far-from-equilibrium noisy medium. As an example, we consider a noisy plasma experiencing Bjorken expansion, for which the leading order evolution is captured by hydrodynamic attractor of classical hydrodynamics, while the quadratic couplings of fluctuations are solved effectively via a generalized version of the hydrodynamic kinetic equation. In the far-from-equilibrium plasma, backreaction of hydrodynamic fluctuations results in renormalization of transport properties, as well as long time tails, of high orders. In particular, corresponding to a renormalized hydrodynamic attractor, evolution in a noisy plasma towards equilibrium becomes non-monotonic.

## Key claim

The paper extends fluctuating hydrodynamics to far-from-equilibrium systems by using the classical hydrodynamic attractor as the background evolution, and shows that the backreaction of hydrodynamic fluctuations renormalizes transport properties and generates long-time tails, leading to a non-monotonic renormalized attractor that differs qualitatively from the monotonic classical attractor.

## Method

The authors formulate a hydrodynamic kinetic equation for equal-time two-point correlators of energy-momentum fluctuations on top of the Bjorken-expanding background described by the classical hydrodynamic attractor. They solve this equation analytically via a slow-roll approximation and numerically for various initial conditions, then compute the thermal corrections to the averaged energy-momentum tensor by integrating the two-point correlators over momentum space, separating cut-off dependent (renormalization) and finite (long-time tail) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The classical hydrodynamic attractor for Bjorken flow is given by \( g(w) = -\frac{1}{2} \left[\frac{22}{7} + w - \sqrt{\left(\frac{10}{21} + w \right)^2 + \frac{64}{45}}\right] \), where \( w = \tau/\tau_\pi \) is the inverse Knudsen number.
- The leading long-time tail coefficient for the energy density correction is \( f_0^{\tau\tau} = 0.45 \pm 0.1 \).
- The leading long-time tail coefficients for the longitudinal pressure correction are \( f_0^{\zeta\zeta} = 0.15032 \pm 0.00002 \) and \( f_1^{\zeta\zeta} = -0.53 \pm 0.05 \).
- The cut-off dependent correction to the energy density at order \( 1/w \) is \( T^{\tau\tau}_\Lambda = \frac{T\Lambda^3}{2\pi^2} - \frac{\Lambda T^3}{4\pi^2}\frac{C_\tau}{(C_\tau C_\eta)^2} \frac{35}{8w} \left(\frac{4}{3}+g(w)\right) \).
- The minimum of the renormalized attractor occurs parametrically at \( \sqrt{w} \sim 4\pi^2 f_0^{\zeta\zeta}/C_\tau \).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to fluctuating hydrodynamics (Kovtun 2014, Crossley 2015, Liu 2018) to far-from-equilibrium systems by building on the classical hydrodynamic attractor concept developed for Bjorken flow (Heller 2013, Romatschke 2017, Blaizot 2017, Kurkela 2019). It generalizes the hydrodynamic kinetic equation previously derived for near-equilibrium noisy fluids (Akamatsu 2016, 2017) to out-of-equilibrium plasmas, and enables future studies of nonlinear mode couplings, non-conformal corrections, and the interplay between initial-state fluctuations and hydrodynamic fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.
