# Review of heat and charge transport in strongly magnetized relativistic plasmas

> We review field-theoretic studies of charge transport in hot relativistic plasmas under strong magnetic fields and extend the analysis to thermal conductivity. The calculations rely on accurately determining the fermion damping rate. Using the Landau-level representation, these damping rates are computed exactly at leading order and incorporated into the Kubo formula to obtain the thermal and electrical conductivity tensors. Our analysis reveals that the mechanisms underlying longitudinal and transverse transport differ significantly. Strong magnetic fields markedly suppress transverse charge transport by confining particles within localized Landau orbits, allowing transport only through quantum transitions between these discrete states. In contrast, longitudinal charge transport is enhanced, as it primarily depends on the reduced scattering probability of particles moving along the direction of the magnetic field. The anisotropy of thermal conductivity is also nontrivial but less pronounced since its underlying transport mechanism is different. We also examine the modification of the Wiedemann--Franz law in strongly magnetized plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in strongly magnetized relativistic plasmas, the electrical and thermal conductivity tensors become highly anisotropic, but the anisotropy of thermal conductivity is less pronounced than that of charge transport because heat conduction is mediated by collective-mode dissipation rather than by particle motion across magnetic field lines, and the authors note that at vanishing chemical potential thermal conductivity is only meaningful in hydrodynamic frames other than the conventional Landau-Lifshitz frame.

## Method

The authors compute the fermion damping rate at leading order in the coupling using the Landau-level representation of Green's functions, considering one-to-two and two-to-one quantum transitions between Landau levels. They then incorporate these damping rates into the Kubo formula for the retarded current-current and energy-current correlation functions to obtain the full electrical and thermal conductivity tensors. The calculations are performed for a QED-like plasma with a single fermion species and extended to QCD by including quark flavors, colors, and appropriate charges.

## Key numbers

- Transverse electrical conductivity $\sigma_{\perp}$ is suppressed in the strong-field regime when $\sqrt{|eB|} \lesssim T/\alpha$, as partial contributions involve products of Lorentzians peaked at adjacent Landau levels with little overlap
- Longitudinal electrical conductivity $\sigma_{\parallel}$ scales as $1/\alpha$ in the weak-coupling limit, with contributions from each Landau level dominated by products of Lorentzians centered at the same energy
- The photon contribution to thermal conductivity is estimated as $\kappa_\gamma \simeq \frac{4\pi^2T^3}{45 \Gamma_\gamma} \sim \frac{T^4}{\alpha |eB|}$, which is negligible compared to the fermionic contribution in the strong magnetic field limit
- Numerical results for thermal conductivity are presented as dimensionless ratios $\tilde{\kappa}_{\perp}\equiv \kappa_{\perp}/T^2$ and $\tilde{\kappa}_{\parallel}\equiv \kappa_{\parallel}/T^2$ over the range $|eB|/T^2$ from approximately 1 to 200, with temperatures spanning from $20m$ to $85m$ (where $m\approx 0.511~\mbox{MeV}$ is the electron mass) and magnetic fields from $(20m)^2$ to $(226m)^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous first-principles investigations of electrical conductivity in magnetized hot relativistic plasmas (Ghosh 2024) to include thermal conductivity, building on field-theoretic techniques using Landau-level representations of Green's functions (Miransky 2015). The approach contrasts with earlier studies that focused on weak-field regimes (van Erkelens 1984, Pike 2016) or used kinetic theory with relaxation-time approximations (Kurian 2017, Das 2019), and differs from studies of electron transport in neutron star crusts (Potekhin 1999, Harutyunyan 2016) where dissipation is due to scattering from ions and phonons rather than quantum transitions between Landau levels. The results enable predictions for magnetic field evolution in neutron stars and magnetars, interpretation of heavy-ion collision data, and understanding of conditions in the early Universe.
