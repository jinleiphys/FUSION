# Homogeneous versus Spiral Phases of Hole-doped Antiferromagnets: A Systematic Effective Field Theory Investigation

> Using the low-energy effective field theory for magnons and holes -- the condensed matter analog of baryon chiral perturbation theory for pions and nucleons in QCD -- we study different phases of doped antiferromagnets. We systematically investigate configurations of the staggered magnetization that provide a constant background field for doped holes. The most general configuration of this type is either constant itself or it represents a spiral in the staggered magnetization. Depending on the values of the low-energy parameters, a homogeneous phase, a spiral phase, or an inhomogeneous phase is energetically favored. The reduction of the staggered magnetization upon doping is also investigated.

## Key claim

Using a low-energy effective field theory for magnons and holes (the condensed matter analog of baryon chiral perturbation theory), the authors systematically determine that the energetically favored phase of a doped antiferromagnet, homogeneous, spiral, or inhomogeneous, depends on the values of the low-energy parameters, with the spiral phase stabilized when the fermionic contribution to the energy density dominates over the magnon contribution.

## Method

The authors construct the most general time-independent configurations of the staggered magnetization that provide a constant background field for doped holes, proving that such configurations are either constant or represent a spiral. They then compute the fermionic energy density for these configurations by diagonalizing the single-particle Hamiltonian in momentum space, treating the 4-fermion contact interactions perturbatively, and compare the total energy density (magnon plus fermion) to determine which phase is favored for different values of the low-energy parameters.

## Key numbers

- The energy density of the undoped antiferromagnet in the \(t\)-\(J\) model was determined as \(\epsilon_0 = -0.6693(1) J/a^2\), where \(J\) is the exchange coupling and \(a\) is the lattice spacing.
- The condition for stability against phase separation in the \(t\)-\(J\) model is \(M < -\epsilon_0 a^2 = 0.6693(1) J\), where \(M\) is the hole rest mass.
- The effective mass for hole pockets is \(M_\text{eff} = M' M'' / \sqrt{{M''}^2 - {M'}^2}\), where \(M'\) and \(M''\) are kinetic masses.
- The fermion density for a filled pocket is \(n^f_\pm = (1/2\pi) M_\text{eff} T^f_\pm\), and the kinetic energy density is \(t^f_\pm = (1/4\pi) M_\text{eff} {T^f_\pm}^2\), where \(T^f_\pm\) is the kinetic energy at the Fermi surface.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously developed by the same authors (Kae05, Bru06) for magnons and holes in antiferromagnets, which is the condensed matter analog of baryon chiral perturbation theory in QCD. The systematic investigation of spiral phases complements earlier calculations in microscopic \(t\)-\(J\)-like models by Shraiman and Siggia (1988), Kane et al. (1990), and others, providing a model-independent and systematically controlled derivation. The results enable future studies of whether spirals with constant composite vector fields may be unstable against developing inhomogeneities, such as the double spiral configuration discussed in the paper.
