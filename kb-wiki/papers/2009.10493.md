# Medium effects on the electrical and Hall conductivities of a hot and magnetized pion gas

> The electrical and Hall conductivities in a uniform magnetic field are evaluated for an interacting pion gas using the kinetic theory approach within the ambit of relaxation time approximation (RTA). The in-medium cross sections vis-a-vis the relaxation time for $ππ$ scattering are obtained using a one-loop modified thermal propagator for the exchanged $ρ$ and $σ$ mesons using thermal field theoretic techniques. For higher values of the magnetic field, a monotonic increase of the electrical conductivity with the temperature is observed. However, for a given temperature the conductivity is found to decrease steadily with magnetic field. The Hall conductivity, at lower values of the magnetic field, is found to decrease with temperature more rapidly than the electrical conductivity, whereas at higher values of the magnetic field, a linear increase is seen. Use of the in-medium scattering cross-section is found to produce a significant effect on the temperature dependence of both electrical and Hall conductivities compared to the case where vacuum cross-section is used.

## Key claim
The electrical and Hall conductivities of a hot pion gas in a uniform magnetic field are evaluated using kinetic theory with relaxation time approximation, where the in-medium cross sections for pion-pion scattering are computed using one-loop thermal propagators for exchanged rho and sigma mesons. The authors find that using in-medium cross sections produces a significant effect on the temperature dependence of both conductivities compared to using vacuum cross sections, but the calculation assumes a weak magnetic field and ignores Landau quantization.

## Method
The Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation was solved for charged pions in a uniform magnetic field to obtain the conductivity tensor. The relaxation time was computed from the isospin-averaged total cross section for 2-to-2 pion-pion scattering, where the invariant amplitudes were calculated using one-loop thermal self-energies for the exchanged rho and sigma mesons via the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory. The momentum-dependent relaxation time was then averaged over the pion momentum distribution and used to evaluate the electrical conductivity (sigma_0), Hall conductivity (sigma_1), and the longitudinal conductivity component (sigma_2) as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- The electrical conductivity sigma_0/T at low magnetic field (omega_c tau << 1) scales approximately as 1/T^2, while at high magnetic field (omega_c tau >> 1) it scales as T^4.
- The Hall conductivity sigma_1/T at low magnetic field scales approximately as 1/T^5, while at high magnetic field it shows a linear increase with temperature.
- The average relaxation time was fitted to a polynomial form sum_{i=0}^3 a_i (m/T)^i (1/T^3) with a_0 = 0.0145 fm-GeV^3, a_1 = -0.0109 fm-GeV^3, a_2 = 0.0058 fm-GeV^3, and a_3 = 0.0026 fm-GeV^3.
- The coupling constants used were g_{rho pi pi} = 6.05 and g_{sigma pi pi} = 2.5, obtained from experimental decay widths of rho and sigma mesons.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of electrical conductivity in hadron resonance gas by Das et al. (2019) and Dash et al. (2020), which used constant or free-parameter relaxation times, by incorporating finite-temperature effects in the scattering cross sections. The study is motivated by the need for accurate transport coefficients in magnetohydrodynamic descriptions of heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic fields of order 10^18 Gauss (comparable to m_pi^2) are produced. The results enable more realistic modeling of the time evolution of the magnetic field in the hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions and complement calculations of conductivities in quark-gluon plasma by Hattori et al. (2016) and Feng et al. (2017).
