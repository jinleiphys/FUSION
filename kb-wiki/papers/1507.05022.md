# Tetraquarks from the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We present a numerical solution of the four-quark Bethe-Salpeter equation for a scalar tetraquark. We find that the four-body equation dynamically generates pseudoscalar poles in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The sensitivity to the pion poles leads to a light isoscalar tetraquark mass M ~ 400 MeV, which is comparable to that of the sigma/f0(500). The masses of its multiplet partners kappa and a0/f0 follow a similar pattern, thereby providing support for the tetraquark interpretation of the light scalar nonet.

## Key claim

The four-body Bethe-Salpeter equation dynamically generates pseudoscalar poles that drive the scalar tetraquark mass down to approximately 400 MeV, but this result depends on the momentum dependence of the amplitudes and is only obtained when the doublet momentum variables are included; without them the mass would be around 1400 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous four-quark Bethe-Salpeter equation for a scalar tetraquark using a rainbow-ladder kernel consistent with QCD's chiral symmetry, with dressed quark propagators from the Dyson-Schwinger equation. The tetraquark amplitude was decomposed into 16 s-wave Dirac-Lorentz tensors and two color structures, and the nine momentum variables were arranged into irreducible multiplets of the permutation group S4 to assess their importance. The mass was extracted by tracking the largest eigenvalue of the kernel as a function of total momentum squared, finding the point where λ(P² = -M²) = 1.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar tetraquark mass M_σ ~ 380 MeV at the physical u/d quark mass
- Multiplet partner mass M_κ ~ 700 MeV
- Multiplet partner mass M_{a0/f0} ~ 920 MeV
- Without the doublet momentum dependence, the eigenvalue crosses λ=1 at M ~ 1400 MeV (essentially four times the constituent-quark mass)
- With only the symmetric singlet momentum dependence, the mass would be ~ 1400 MeV; adding the doublet reduces it to M ~ 400-500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the two-body meson-meson/diquark-antidiquark approximation of Heupel (2012) by solving the full four-body Bethe-Salpeter equation, confirming that the system is dominated by pion poles rather than diquarks. The results support the tetraquark interpretation of the light scalar nonet (σ/f0(500), κ(800), a0/f0(980)) proposed earlier, but indicate these states are predominantly meson molecules rather than diquark-antidiquark states. This enables future calculations that can include three- and four-body irreducible interactions and extend beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation.
