# Rapidly rotating hot nuclear and hypernuclear compact stars: integral parameters and universal relations

> In this work, we investigate hot, isentropic compact stars in the limiting cases of static and maximally rotating configurations, focusing on how variations in the symmetry energy of the equation of state derived from covariant density functional theory affect stellar properties. We consider both nucleonic and hyperonic matter with systematically varied symmetry energy slopes, fixed entropies per baryon $s / k_B=1$ and 3, and electron fractions $Y_e=0.1$ and $Y_e=0.4$, representative of conditions in binary neutron star mergers and proto-neutron stars. We compute and analyze mass--radius and moment--of--inertia--mass relations, as well as the dependence of the Keplerian (mass-shedding) frequency on mass, angular momentum, and the ratio of kinetic to gravitational energy. Furthermore, we show that several universal relations between global properties remain valid across both nucleonic and hyperonic equations of state with varying symmetry energy, both in the static and Keplerian limit, and for various combinations of the fixed entropy and electron fraction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that several universal relations between global properties of compact stars remain valid across both nucleonic and hyperonic equations of state with systematically varied symmetry energy slopes, even for hot, isentropic configurations at fixed entropy per baryon and electron fraction, but the universality between hot supramassive Keplerian and cold static maximum masses breaks down due to uncertainties from the unknown entropy of the hot remnant.

## Method

The authors used the RNS code to solve the coupled Einstein field equations and equations of hydrostatic equilibrium for stationary, axisymmetric configurations, inputting isentropic equations of state at fixed electron fractions derived from covariant density functional theory. They considered three values of the symmetry energy slope parameter L_sym = 30, 50, and 70 MeV at fixed skewness Q_sat = 400 MeV, for both nucleonic and hyperonic matter, with fixed entropies per baryon s/k_B = 1 and 3 and electron fractions Y_e = 0.1 and 0.4. They computed mass-radius relations, moment-of-inertia-mass relations, and the dependence of Keplerian frequency on mass, angular momentum, and the ratio of kinetic to gravitational energy for both static and maximally rotating configurations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study (Tsiopelas 2024, TSO24) of static and rapidly rotating compact objects to finite temperatures and entropies, using the same set of covariant density functional equations of state with systematic variations in symmetry energy slope. It builds upon earlier investigations of universal relations for rapidly rotating neutron stars (Doneva 2013, Pappas 2014, Cipolletta 2015, Breu 2016), for stars containing hyperons (Li 2023), and for hot proto-neutron stars and postmerger remnants (Martinon 2014, Marques 2017, Khadkikar 2021). The results enable more physically consistent modeling of hot, rapidly rotating compact objects formed in core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, and provide a framework for confronting upcoming multimessenger data, particularly for inferring the maximum Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff mass from gravitational wave events like GW170817.
