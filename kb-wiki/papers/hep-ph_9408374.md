# Pion Observables and QCD

> The nonperturbative, Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to solving QCD provides a straightforward, microscopic description of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement. It is an ideal tool for the study of pion observables. This is illustrated via its application to the calculation of: the $π$-$π$ scattering lengths $a_0^0$, $a_0^2$, $a_1^1$, $a_2^0$, $a_2^2$ and associated partial wave amplitudes; the $π^0\rightarrow γγ$ decay width; and the charged pion form factor, $F_π(q^2)$.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach, applied at tree-level with a parametrized quark propagator that implicitly encodes the unknown infrared gluon propagator, reproduces pion observables at the 10% level, with the chiral-limit result for the π⁰→γγ decay width emerging exactly independent of the details of the quark propagator.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in the rainbow approximation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation, using a parametrized quark propagator (Eqs. SSM and SVM) that is an entire function in the complex p² plane. The six parameters of this propagator are fixed by a global best-fit to fπ, ⟨q̄q⟩, mπ, the dimensionless π-π scattering lengths a₀⁰, a₀², a₁¹, a₂⁰, and a least-squares fit to the charged pion form factor Fπ(q²) on the spacelike domain [0,4] GeV². Tree-level (minimal dressed-quark loop) calculations are then performed for π-π scattering, π⁰→γγ decay, and Fπ(q²).

## Key numbers

- fπ = 0.0839 GeV (calculated) vs. 0.0931 ± 0.001 GeV (experiment)
- -⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} at 1 GeV² = 0.211 (calculated) vs. 0.220 ± 0.01 (experiment)
- mπ = 0.127 GeV (calculated) vs. 0.138 GeV (experiment)
- rπ = 0.596 fm (calculated) vs. 0.663 ± 0.006 fm (experiment)
- gπ⁰γγ = 0.497 (dimensionless, calculated) vs. 0.504 ± 0.019 (experiment)
- a₀⁰ = 0.174 (calculated) vs. 0.21 ± 0.02 (experiment)
- a₀² = -0.0496 (calculated) vs. -0.040 ± 0.003 (experiment)
- a₁¹ = 0.0307 (calculated) vs. 0.038 ± 0.002 (experiment)
- a₂⁰ = 0.00161 (calculated) vs. 0.0017 ± 0.0003 (experiment)
- a₂² = -0.000251 (calculated, no experimental value given)
- Mass scale D = 0.133 GeV² from fitting procedure
- Λ_QCD = 0.20 GeV used in the calculation

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to QCD, which is reviewed in Roberts (1994, Ref. DSErev), by demonstrating its practical application to a comprehensive set of pion observables. The calculations are tree-level in the sense of minimal dressed-quark loops, and the 10% agreement with experiment is consistent with the estimate from Holdom, Rhem, and Maksymowicz (Ref. HRM) and Alkofer, Bender, and Roberts (Ref. ABR) that nonpointlike π-loop contributions provide <15% corrections. The approach enables the inference of low-energy constants of Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) from the underlying QCD parameters, with the tree-level DSE results for ℓ̄₁, ℓ̄₂, and ℓ̄₆ compared to the 1-point-pion-loop ChPT values of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984).
