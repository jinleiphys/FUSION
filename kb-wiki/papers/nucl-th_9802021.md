# Transport theory with self-consistent confinement related to the lattice data

> The space-time development of a quark-gluon plasma is calculated from a Vlasov equation for the distribution function of quasiparticles with medium dependent masses. At each space-time point the masses are calculated selfconsistently from a gap equation, whose form is determined by the requirement that in thermal equilibrium and for a range of temperatures the energy density of the quasi-particle system is identical to the one from lattice calculations . The numerical solutions of the Vlasov equation display confinement. Relations to effective theories like that by Friedberg Lee and Nambu Jona-Lasinio are established.

# Transport theory with self-consistent confinement related to the lattice data

## Key claim

The Vlasov equation for quasiparticles with medium-dependent masses, where the masses are determined self-consistently from a gap equation fitted to lattice QCD energy density data, produces effective confinement of partons in a deconfined fireball without requiring explicit hadronization dynamics. The confinement is only effective (not absolute), because the vacuum mass is taken as finite (~9.4 T_c) rather than infinite, which suffices to trap partons at the temperatures studied.

## Method

The authors extract the temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass m(T) by requiring that the energy density of a single-species Boltzmann gas of massive partons matches the lattice QCD energy density for four-flavor QCD. They solve a first-order differential equation derived from this matching condition, then use the resulting mass function in a gap equation to obtain the potential energy density V(m). For the nonequilibrium case, they solve the Vlasov equation for the phase-space distribution function together with the same gap equation, using a test-particle method with spherically symmetric initial conditions for a fireball at T=1.3 T_c.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant extracted from the model is B^{1/4} = 240 MeV for case (a) and B^{1/4} = 300 MeV for case (b), using T_c = 180 MeV for four-flavor QCD.
- The vacuum parton mass used in the nonequilibrium calculation is approximately 9.4 T_c, which effectively confines partons with initial momentum smaller than ~9.4 T_c.
- The cutoff for the chiral condensate calculation is set to Λ = 430 MeV, chosen to reproduce the zero-temperature chiral condensate value of 2 × (250 MeV)^4 for four-flavor QCD.
- The initial fireball temperature is T = 1.3 T_c = 180 MeV.
- The parton mass in case (a) is m(T) ≈ 2.1 T_c right above T_c and m(T) ≈ 1.1 T for large T.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle approach of Gorenstein and collaborators (1995) and Levai and Heinz (1998) by using lattice QCD data directly to determine the mass function, rather than assuming a specific effective Lagrangian. It relates to the Friedberg-Lee model (1977) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (1961), but differs in that the potential V(m) is extracted from lattice data rather than postulated. The observed collective oscillations of the fireball resemble those seen in Vlasov evolution of the nucleon in the Friedberg-Lee model by the Giessen group (1996). The approach enables future studies of hadronization mechanisms, including possible fragmentation of the fireball or hadronization via parton collisions, which cannot be addressed within the spherically symmetric mean-field treatment used here.
