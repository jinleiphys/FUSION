# Computation of products of phase space factors and nuclear matrix elements for the Double Beta Decay

> The nuclear matrix elements (NME) and phase space factors (PSF) entering the half-life formulas of the double-beta decay (DBD) process are two key quantities whose accurate computation still represents a challenge. In this paper we propose a new approach of calculating them, namely to compute directly their product as an unique formula. This procedure allows a more coherent treatment of the nuclear approximations and input parameters appearing in both quantities and avoids possible confusion in interpreting the DBD data due to different individual expressions adopted for PSF and NME (and consequently their reporting in different units) by different authors. Our calculations are performed for both two neutrino ($2νββ$) and neutrinoless ($0νββ$) decay modes, and for five nuclei of most experimental interest. Further, using the most recent experimental limits for $0νββ$ decay half-lives, we provide new constraints on the light mass neutrino parameter. Finally, by separating in the half-lives formulas the factor representing the axial-vector constant to the forth, we advance suggestions on how to reduce the errors introduced in calculation by the uncertain value of this constant by exploiting the DBD data from different isotopes and/or decay modes.

# Computation of products of phase space factors and nuclear matrix elements for the Double Beta Decay

## Key claim
The paper proposes that computing the product of phase space factors (PSF) and nuclear matrix elements (NME) as a single formula, rather than separately, yields more coherent treatment of common input parameters (such as the axial-vector constant g_A, nuclear radius, and average intermediate-state energy) and removes confusion from different units used by different authors; however, the approach is validated only for five specific nuclei and the authors acknowledge that the effective g_A values for the 0νββ mode remain unknown, so the reported P^0ν values are computed using the free nucleon value of 1.273.

## Method
The authors built numerical codes that combine their previous separate PSF and NME codes into a single product calculation. For the PSF part, exact Dirac electron wave functions were solved with a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density in the daughter nucleus, including finite nuclear size and electron screening effects. For the NME part, the double Gamow-Teller transitions were computed using shell-model effective interactions (GXPF1A for ⁴⁸Ca, JUN45 for ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se, gcn50:82 for ¹³⁰Te and ¹³⁶Xe), with neutrino potentials including finite nucleon size, short-range correlations, and higher-order current corrections. The products P^2ν and P^0ν were then used to reproduce measured 2νββ half-lives and to derive new constraints on the light neutrino mass parameter from the most recent experimental 0νββ half-life limits.

## Key numbers
- For 2νββ decay, the product P^2ν values (in yr⁻¹) are: ⁴⁸Ca: 123.81 × 10⁻²¹; ⁷⁶Ge: 5.16 × 10⁻²¹; ⁸²Se: 186.62 × 10⁻²¹; ¹³⁰Te: 25.26 × 10⁻²¹; ¹³⁶Xe: 20.30 × 10⁻²¹.
- The effective g_A values (g^2ν_A,eff) used to reproduce experimental half-lives are: ⁴⁸Ca: 0.65; ⁷⁶Ge: 0.56; ⁸²Se: 0.49; ¹³⁰Te: 0.47; ¹³⁶Xe: 0.39.
- For 0νββ decay (light Majorana neutrino exchange), the product P^0ν values (in yr⁻¹, computed with g_A = 1.273) are: ⁴⁸Ca: 7.30 × 10⁻¹⁵; ⁷⁶Ge: 9.95 × 10⁻¹⁵; ⁸²Se: 34.45 × 10⁻¹⁵; ¹³⁰Te: 71.45 × 10⁻¹⁵; ¹³⁶Xe: 71.01 × 10⁻¹⁵.
- Upper limits on the light neutrino mass parameter ⟨m_ν⟩ (in eV) derived from experimental half-life limits: ⁴⁸Ca: < 26.49; ⁷⁶Ge: < 0.29; ⁸²Se: < 2.87; ¹³⁰Te: < 0.59; ¹³⁶Xe: < 0.28.
- The percentage difference between g^2ν_A,eff values from half-life reproduction and those from charge-exchange reaction data (g^2ν_A,exp) ranges from 6.60% (⁷⁶Ge) to 18.33% (⁸²Se).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous separate calculations of NME (Stoica and collaborators, 2007) and PSF (Stoica and collaborators, 2013-2015) by unifying them into a single product computation, addressing the inconsistency noted in the literature where different groups (e.g., using pnQRPA, Shell Model, IBA2, PHFB, or GCM with EDF) report NME and PSF in different units with different input parameter choices. The approach enables more reliable constraints on the light neutrino mass parameter using the most recent experimental limits from GERDA (2018) for ⁷⁶Ge and EXO (2018) for ¹³⁶Xe. The paper also suggests that by taking ratios of half-lives from different isotopes or decay modes, one can reduce the uncertainty from the poorly known axial-vector constant g_A, following suggestions in the literature about experimental determination of this parameter for specific isotopes (Simkovic, 2018).
