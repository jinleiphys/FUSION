# Temperature dependence of single-particle properties in nuclear matter

> The single-nucleon potential in hot nuclear matter is investigated in the framework of the Brueckner theory by adopting the realistic Argonne V18 or Nijmegen 93 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented by a microscopic three-body force. The rearrangement contribution to the single-particle potential induced by the ground state correlations is calculated in terms of the hole-line expansion of the mass operator and provides a significant repulsive contribution in the low-momentum region around and below the Fermi surface. Increasing temperature leads to a reduction of the effect, while increasing density makes it become stronger. The three-body force suppresses somewhat the ground state correlations due to its strong short-range repulsion, increasing with density. Inclusion of the three-body force contribution results in a quite different temperature dependence of the single-particle potential at high enough densities as compared to that adopting the pure two-body force. The effects of three-body force and ground state correlations on the nucleon effective mass are also discussed.

## Key claim

The ground-state correlations (rearrangement term) provide a significant repulsive contribution to the single-particle potential in nuclear matter, mainly within the Fermi sphere, but this effect is reduced by increasing temperature and enhanced by increasing density; the three-body force suppresses these correlations due to its strong short-range repulsion, and at high enough densities it reverses the temperature dependence of the single-particle potential compared to the pure two-body force case.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne V18 or Nijmegen 93 two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions supplemented by a microscopic meson-exchange three-body force (TBF). The mass operator was expanded up to the second order in the hole-line expansion, with the second-order term (rearrangement contribution) calculated from the G-matrix to account for ground-state correlations; the TBF was reduced to a temperature-dependent effective two-body force via averaging over the third nucleon degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and normal density (0.17 fm⁻³), the repulsive ground-state correlations raise the depth of the single-particle potential at k=0 from the BHF value of approximately -85 MeV to approximately -60 MeV.
- At T=0 with the V18 potential, the TBF reduces the rearrangement contribution V₂(k=0) from 30 MeV to 24 MeV (a reduction of about 6 MeV); at T=20 MeV, the reduction is from 13 MeV to 10 MeV (about 3 MeV).
- The TBF reduces the saturation density from 0.26 fm⁻³ to 0.19 fm⁻³, raises the energy per particle from -18 MeV to -15 MeV, and lowers the compression modulus from 230 MeV to 210 MeV.
- The effective mass at the Fermi surface (m*_F/m) is approximately 1.02 with TBF included and 1.08 without TBF, compared to the BHF value of about 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finite-temperature BHF calculations with microscopic TBF (Zuo 2004) by including the second-order rearrangement term from the hole-line expansion of the mass operator, following the formalism of Jeukenne (1976) and Mahaux (1991). It confirms earlier zero-temperature findings by Baldo (1988a, 1988b) and Zuo (1999) that ground-state correlations are crucial for restoring the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem and improving agreement with the empirical optical model potential. The results enable more realistic single-particle potentials for use in transport-model simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and the comparison with in-medium T-matrix approaches (Schnell, Czerski 2002, Dewulf 2003, Frick) shows qualitative agreement, indicating that the rearrangement term captures most of the hole-hole correlations.
