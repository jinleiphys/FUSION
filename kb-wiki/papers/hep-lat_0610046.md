# Color superconductivity in ultra-dense quark matter

> At ultra-high density, matter is expected to form a degenerate Fermi gas of quarks in which there is a condensate of Cooper pairs of quarks near the Fermi surface. This phenomenon is called color superconductivity. In these proceedings I review the underlying physics of color superconductivity and our current understanding of the possible phases of real-world quark matter. Then I consider how lattice gauge theorists would proceed to investigate the phase structure of dense quark matter if it were possible to perform the path integral numerically, i.e. if the sign problem had been solved.

## Key claim

Color superconductivity is expected to occur in ultra-dense quark matter via Cooper pairing of quarks near the Fermi surface, but the actual phases realized in nature depend on the competition between pairing gaps (Δ ~ 10–100 MeV) and the stress from strange quark mass and neutrality conditions, which split Fermi momenta by approximately M_s²/(4μ).

## Method

The paper reviews the BCS mechanism applied to QCD, where quarks of different colors and flavors form Cooper pairs that break local color symmetry. The analysis uses Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model calculations and Ginzburg-Landau approximations to compare free energies of competing phases (CFL, 2SC, gapless phases, LOFF crystalline phases, and single-flavor pairing) under constraints of color and electrical neutrality and weak equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The gap parameter in the CFL phase is Δ₀ ~ 10–100 MeV.
- The splitting between Fermi momenta due to strange quark mass is M_s²/(4μ), rising to tens of MeV at densities relevant for neutron star cores.
- The criterion for CFL or 2SC pairing to occur is Δ > M_s²/(4μ).
- The strange quark mass is ~ 90 MeV; up and down quark masses are ≲ 5 MeV.
- Critical temperatures for color superconducting phases are expected to be of order 10 MeV or higher.
- Single-flavor pairing phases have critical temperatures as large as a few MeV, more typically in the eV to many keV range.
- A 20 fm lattice corresponds to T = 10 MeV; current lattice sizes are limited to less than 5 fm, corresponding to T ≥ 40 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the understanding of color superconductivity from earlier work by Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (BCS) applied to QCD, and builds on prior NJL model calculations by Ruster (2005) and others. It addresses the open question of which phases occur at intermediate densities relevant for neutron star cores, where the CFL phase is stressed by the strange quark mass. The paper also considers how lattice QCD could investigate dense quark matter if the sign problem were solved, noting that phases with critical temperatures below about 10 MeV would remain difficult to study on current lattices.
