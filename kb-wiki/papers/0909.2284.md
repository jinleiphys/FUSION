# On Analytic Solutions of (1+3)D Relativistic Ideal Hydrodynamic Equations

> In this paper, we find various analytic (1+3)D solutions to relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations based on embedding of known low-dimensional scaling solutions. We first study a class of flows with 2D Hubble Embedding, for which a single ordinary differential equation for the remaining velocity field can be derived. Using this equation, all solutions with transverse 2D Hubble embedding and power law ansatz for the remaining longitudinal velocity field will be found. Going beyond the power law ansatz, we further find a few solutions with transverse 2D Hubble embedding and nontrivial longitudinal velocity field. Finally we investigate general scaling flows with each component of the velocity fields scaling independently, for which we also find all possible solutions. Possible physical relevance of these solutions will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic method for constructing analytic (1+3)D solutions to relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations by embedding known lower-dimensional scaling solutions (specifically 2D Hubble flow) into higher dimensions, with the hedge that the solutions are limited to specific ansatz forms (power law, scaling variable dependence) and particular equations of state.

## Method

The authors first impose a 2D Hubble embedding on the transverse velocity field (setting \(v_x = x/t\), \(v_y = y/t\)) and derive a single second-order partial differential equation (the constraint equation) for the remaining longitudinal velocity field \(\bar v_z(\tau_\perp, z)\) in curved coordinates \((\tau_\perp, \eta_\perp, \phi_\perp, z)\). They then solve this constraint equation under two approaches: (1) a power law ansatz \(\bar v_z = A \tau_\perp^B z^C\), enumerating all possible exponent combinations; (2) a scaling ansatz where \(\bar v_z = f(z/\tau_\perp)\) and pressure \(p = \tau_\perp^{-a/(1-\nu)} g(z/\tau_\perp)\), reducing the problem to ordinary differential equations for \(f\) and \(g\). Finally, they investigate general scaling flows with velocity components of the form \(v_i = \alpha_i (x_i/t)\) (no sum) and solve the resulting system of equations for the pressure field.

## Key numbers

*   Power law solutions yield four sets: (i) \(A=0\) (2D Hubble); (ii) \(A=1, B=-1, C=1\) (3D Hubble); (iii) \(A=1, B=1, C=-1\) (anti-Hubble longitudinal flow); (iv) \(A=-3, B=-1, C=1\) with \(\nu = 1/2\) (special EOS).
*   Solution II (linear polynomial ansatz) gives \(a = 3\kappa^2/(1+\kappa^2)\) and \(f = [(1+\kappa^2)\xi \pm \sqrt{(1+\kappa^2)^2\xi^2 - 4\kappa^2}]/(2\kappa^2)\), with \(\kappa^2 = (1-\nu)/\nu\).
*   Solution III (nonlinear ansatz) gives \(f = 1/(2-\xi)\) with \(a=3\) and \(\kappa=1\) (i.e., \(\nu = 1/2\)).
*   Solution V gives \(f = (1 \pm \sqrt{5-4\xi})/[2(\xi-1)]\) with \(a=3\) and \(\kappa=2\) (i.e., \(\nu = 1/5\)).
*   Solution VII gives a cubic-root expression for \(f\) with \(a=3\) and \(\kappa=\sqrt{6}\) (i.e., \(\nu = 1/7\)).
*   General scaling solutions include the special case \(\alpha_z = -3\) with \(\nu = 1/2\) (from 2D Hubble embedding) and \(\alpha_{x,y} = -1\) with \(\nu = 1/2\) (from 1D Hubble embedding in prior work).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liao and Koch (2009), who developed a method for solving (1+3)D relativistic ideal hydrodynamics by embedding longitudinal and transverse radial flows and finding all power-law solutions for the transverse velocity. The present paper generalizes that method to 2D Hubble embedding and finds new nontrivial longitudinal flow profiles beyond power laws, as well as exhaustively classifying all general scaling solutions with independent anisotropic scaling constants. These analytic solutions are relevant for modeling the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly for describing non-boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and transverse flow patterns beyond the simple Hwa-Bjorken (1974, 1983) and 3D Hubble scenarios.
