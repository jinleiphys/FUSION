# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group Approach to the Nuclear Many-Body Problem

> We present a pedagogical discussion of Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) methods, in particular the In-Medium SRG (IMSRG) approach for solving the nuclear many-body problem. These methods use continuous unitary transformations to evolve the nuclear Hamiltonian to a desired shape. The IMSRG, in particular, is used to decouple the ground state from all excitations and solve the many-body Schrödinger equation. We discuss the IMSRG formalism as well as its numerical implementation, and use the method to study the pairing model and infinite neutron matter. We compare our results with those of Coupled cluster theory, Configuration-Interaction Monte Carlo, and the Self-Consistent Green's Function approach. The chapter concludes with an expanded overview of current research directions, and a look ahead at upcoming developments.

## Key claim

The In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (IMSRG) uses continuous unitary transformations to decouple the ground state from all excitations, thereby solving the many-body Schrodinger equation on the operator level without constructing the Hamiltonian matrix in a factorially growing basis of configurations; the method can be viewed as a re-organization of the many-body perturbation series in which correlations are absorbed into an RG-improved Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors derive and implement SRG flow equations for the nuclear Hamiltonian, using normal-ordering techniques to control the size of induced interaction terms. They apply the IMSRG in the IMSRG(2) truncation scheme to study the pairing model and to compute the neutron matter equation-of-state, comparing results with Coupled Cluster theory, Configuration-Interaction Monte Carlo, and the Self-Consistent Green's Function approach. The implementation is demonstrated through a symmetry-unconstrained Python code example.

* The flow equation for the toy model yields tan(theta(s)/2) = tan(theta(0)/2) e^{-4 Omega^2 s}, where theta parameterizes the evolving Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the SRG methodology from free-space evolution of nuclear interactions (Bogner 2003, Bogner 2007, Bogner 2010, Furnstahl 2013) to in-medium applications, building on the initial IMSRG formulation by Tsukiyama (2011) and Hergert (2013, 2016). The IMSRG is related to Coupled Cluster theory (Chapter 8 of the volume) and continuous unitary transformation methods used in solid-state physics (Heidbrink 2002, Drescher 2011, Krull 2012, Fauseweh 2013, Krones 2015). The approach enables the use of RG-improved Hamiltonians as input for the nuclear shell model and equation-of-motion methods, and the Magnus formulation of the IMSRG (Section 5.1) provides a path to efficient computation of observables and approximate IMSRG(3) implementations.
