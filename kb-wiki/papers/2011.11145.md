# On the energy budget of the transition of a neutron star into the third family branch

> Transition of a compact star into the third family for an equation of state (EoS) featuring mass twins is considered. The energy released at a baryon number conserving transition for static compact stars configurations is computed for two sets of models for comparison. The EoS of choice is the density dependent functional DD2 EoS with excluded model correction for hadronic matter which suffers a phase transition into deconfined quark matter described by a constant speed of sound approach. The two sets of EoS models feature different compact star mass onsets that maximize the energy and radius difference at the transition while simultaneously fulfilling state-of-the-art constraints from multi-messenger astronomy and empirical nuclear data. It is found that the maximal energy budget at the transitions falls in the range of $10^{49}$ - $10^{52}$ ergs.

## Key claim

The maximal energy released during a baryon-number-conserving transition of a static neutron star into the third family branch (mass twins scenario) falls in the range of 10^49 to 10^52 ergs, but this budget depends strongly on the stiffness of the hadronic equation of state and the mass onset for quark deconfinement, with opposite tendencies compared to earlier work by the same author.

## Method

The author constructs two sets of hybrid equations of state (EoS) using the density-dependent functional DD2 with excluded volume corrections for hadronic matter, combined with a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) model for deconfined quark matter (c_QM = 1). A Maxwell construction enforces equal pressures at the phase transition. For each EoS set, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to generate static compact star sequences, and the energy released in the transition from the last stable hadronic configuration to the hybrid configuration (at fixed baryon number) is computed for varying mass onsets M_onset.

## Key numbers

* Maximal energy budget at the transition falls in the range of 10^49 to 10^52 ergs (abstract).
* Set 1 (DD2p80) features mass onsets M_onset from 0.6 to 1.35 solar masses, with transition pressures p_trans ranging from 8.651 to 23.836 MeV/fm^3 and energy density discontinuities Delta epsilon from 282.868 to 354.782 MeV/fm^3 (Table 1).
* Set 2 (DD2p15) features mass onsets M_onset from 0.6 to 2.0 solar masses, with transition pressures p_trans from 10.805 to 99.817 MeV/fm^3 and energy density discontinuities Delta epsilon from 166.842 to 339.108 MeV/fm^3 (Table 1).
* For set 2, the parameter gamma = Delta epsilon / epsilon_trans is fixed at 0.75; for set 1, gamma is varied to maximize radius difference between twin configurations while satisfying constraints from Annala et al. (2017) and Bauswein et al. (2017).
* The available energy values for set 1 and set 2 can differ by two orders of magnitude for some model parameters, with set 1 displaying the highest values (Section 4).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations on the energetics of the neutron-star-to-third-family transition (Alvarez-Castillo 2015, Alvarez-Castillo 2016) but finds an opposite tendency: here both the energy release and radius difference decrease with increasing mass onset, whereas earlier work reported the opposite trend. The study uses state-of-the-art constraints from multi-messenger astronomy (GW170817 tidal deformabilities from Abbott et al. 2018, NICER mass-radius measurements from Miller et al. 2019 and Miller et al. 2021, and lower bounds on maximum mass from Rezzolla et al. 2018 and Most et al. 2020). The results enable estimates of the imprint of such transitions on binary system eccentricities (Alvarez-Castillo 2019) and inform future multi-messenger observations of compact star mergers and cosmic ray studies.
