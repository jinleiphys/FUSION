# Impact of statistical uncertainties on the composition of the outer crust of a neutron star

> By means of Monte Carlo methods, we perform a full error analysis on the Duflo-Zucker mass model. In particular, we study the presence of correlations in the residuals to obtain a more realistic estimate of the error bars on the predicted binding energies. To further reduce the discrepancies between model prediction and experimental data we also apply a Multilayer Perceptron Neural Network. We show that the root mean square of the model further reduces of roughly 40\%. We then use the resulting models to predict the composition of the outer crust of a non accreting neutron star. We provide a first estimate of the impact of error propagation on the resulting equation of state of the system.

## Key claim
The Duflo-Zucker mass model (DZ10) combined with a Multilayer Perceptron Neural Network reduces the root mean square deviation from experimental binding energies by roughly 40%, but the resulting outer crust composition of a non-accreting neutron star depends on whether statistical uncertainties from the mass model are propagated, with the transition regions between magic numbers N=50 and N=82 becoming probabilistic rather than sharp.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo error analysis on the 10-parameter Duflo-Zucker mass model (DZ10) using Block Bootstrap to account for correlations in the residuals, obtaining type-2 error estimates. They then trained a Multilayer Perceptron Neural Network with two hidden layers (45 and 84 nodes) on the DZ10 residuals to create a combined DZ10+NN model. Using both models, they applied the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) method to determine the sequence of nuclei minimising the baryonic chemical potential at each pressure, thereby predicting the outer crust composition and equation of state of a non-accreting neutron star.

## Key numbers
- Root mean square deviation of DZ10 model: 0.572 keV (from fit to 2293 nuclei with A>=14 from AME2016)
- Root mean square deviation of DZ10+NN model: 0.344 keV (a reduction of roughly 40% relative to DZ10 alone)
- For nuclei with 20 <= Z <= 50 (relevant for neutron star crust), only 27.9% of measured nuclei fall within 1-sigma error bars using type-2 (Block Bootstrap) errors, compared to 11.5% using type-1 (Hessian) errors
- Block size l=8 used for 2-dimensional Block Bootstrap, with N_boot=5000 Monte Carlo samplings
- Outer crust sequence using DZ10 includes nuclei up to N=82, Z=36 at maximum pressure 4.86 x 10^-4 MeV fm^-3 and baryonic density 2.39 x 10^-1 fm^-3
- DZ10+NN sequence includes an additional nucleus (Z=30, N=50) at pressure 2.67 x 10^-5 MeV fm^-3 and density 2.19 x 10^-2 fm^-3, and omits 118Kr at the outer-inner crust transition
- 80Zr has a 60% probability of appearing in the outer crust when error bars are propagated via 10^4 Monte Carlo mass tables

## Context
This work extends the methodology of error analysis in nuclear mass models (Dobaczewski 2014, Schunck 2015, McDonnell 2015) and applies it to the Duflo-Zucker model, which is widely used in astrophysical calculations. It builds on the BPS formalism (Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland 1971) for outer crust composition and prior studies of neutron star crusts (Ruster 2006, Pearson 2011, Sharma 2015). The use of neural networks to improve mass predictions follows earlier work by Utama (2017, 2018) and Niu (2018). The results enable future comparisons of neutron star equations of state from different mass models with quantified statistical uncertainties, and can guide experimental measurements of key neutron-rich nuclei in the N=50 and N=82 regions.
