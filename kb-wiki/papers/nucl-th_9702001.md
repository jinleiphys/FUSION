# Meson-nucleon vertex corrections to the vacuum polarization in the cutoff field theory and tensor coupling

> At zero-density, meson-nucleon vertex corrections to vacuum polarization are studied in the $σ-ω$ model with the cutoff. It is shown that the properties of the vertex corrections to vacuum polarization are somewhat different from those described by the ordinary renormalization procedures when the cutoff is small (< 5 GeV). The low-energy effective Lagrangian is constructed in the framework of the renormalization group method. The weak tensor and derivative couplings of meson-nucleon interactions may be needed in the low-energy effective theory of mesons and nucleons.

## Key claim

The properties of meson-nucleon vertex corrections to vacuum polarization in the sigma-omega model with a cutoff are somewhat different from those described by ordinary renormalization procedures when the cutoff is small (less than 5 GeV), and weak tensor and derivative couplings of meson-nucleon interactions may be needed in the low-energy effective theory.

## Method

The authors computed one-loop meson-nucleon vertex corrections to vacuum polarization at zero density in the sigma-omega model, using a Pauli-Villars regulator to introduce a finite cutoff Lambda. They evaluated the scalar, vector, and tensor vertex functions for on-shell nucleons, then reformulated the cutoff field theory using renormalization group methods to construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian expanded in powers of -q^2/M^2.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, sigma-meson mass m_s = 550 MeV, omega-meson mass m_v = 783 MeV
- Couplings g_s = 8.81907 and g_v = 10.0998, which reproduce binding energy a_1 = 15.75 MeV at normal density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Vertex functions F_s, F_v, and F_t at QM = 0.5 GeV were computed for cutoff values Lambda = 1 GeV and 2 GeV, and compared to the Lambda = infinity limit
- At Lambda = 20 GeV, the vertex functions are almost the same as in the Lambda = infinity limit
- The tensor coupling constant kappa_{t,0} is approximately 0.4 when Lambda = 2 GeV and Lambda' = 1 GeV
- The couplings kappa_{s,1} and kappa_{v,1} are small, and kappa_{t,1} is very small

## Context

This work extends the cutoff field theory approach of Cohen (1990) for relativistic Hartree calculations to the study of vertex corrections, and builds on the authors' previous work (Kouno et al., 1996) on nuclear matter properties using cutoff field theory and renormalization group methods. The results are compared to the dimensional regularization results of Chin (1977) and Serot and Walecka (1986). The paper enables construction of low-energy effective Lagrangians for quantum hadrodynamics with phenomenologically determined tensor and derivative couplings, which may be useful in nuclear Schwinger-Dyson formalism.
