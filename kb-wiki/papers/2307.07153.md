# Nuclear Level Density and $γ$-ray Strength Function of $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$ and the impact on the i-process

> Proton-$γ$ coincidences from $(\mathrm{d},\mathrm{p})$ reactions between a $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}$ beam and a deuterated polyethylene target have been analyzed with the inverse-Oslo method to find the nuclear level density (NLD) and $γ$-ray strength function ($γ$SF) of $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$. The $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}(n,γ)$ capture cross section has been calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach model in TALYS using the measured NLD and $γ$SF as constraints. The results confirm that the $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}(n,γ)$ reaction acts as a bottleneck when relying on one-zone nucleosynthesis calculations. However, the impact of this reaction is strongly dampened in multi-zone models of low-metallicity AGB stars experiencing i-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}(n,\gamma)$ reaction acts as a bottleneck in one-zone nucleosynthesis calculations, but this impact is strongly dampened in multi-zone models of low-metallicity AGB stars experiencing i-process nucleosynthesis, meaning the reaction's importance depends critically on the dimensionality of the stellar model.

## Method

Proton-gamma coincidences from $(\mathrm{d},\mathrm{p})$ reactions between a $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}$ beam at 4.47 MeV/u and a deuterated polyethylene target were analyzed using the inverse-Oslo method to extract the nuclear level density (NLD) and gamma-ray strength function ($\gamma$SF) of $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$. The $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}(n,\gamma)$ capture cross section was then calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach model in TALYS with the measured NLD and $\gamma$SF as constraints. Due to the absence of neutron resonance data for normalization, a Bayesian analysis was performed using three NLD models (Back-Shifted Fermi Gas, Constant Temperature, and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) and four $\gamma$SF models (Simplified Modified Lorentzian and Gogny-HFB+QRPA) to constrain the normalization parameters.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 4.47(1) MeV/u for the $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}$ beam
- Beam intensity: approximately $3.5 \times 10^6$ particles per second
- Target thickness: approximately 670 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ deuterated polyethylene (99% deuterium enrichment)
- Total beam-on-target time: approximately 140 hours
- Background-subtracted proton-gamma coincidences: $3.2 \times 10^5$ with LaBr$_3$:Ce detectors and $1.1 \times 10^6$ with Miniball clusters
- Excitation energy resolution: FWHM approximately 1.1 MeV
- Neutron separation energy $S_n$ for $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$: 5.8 MeV
- Excitation energy range for NLD and $\gamma$SF extraction: 3.5 to 5.6 MeV
- Minimum gamma-ray energy considered: 1.5 MeV
- Maximum final excitation energy probed: 4.1 MeV
- Estimated axial deformation of $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$: $\beta_2 \approx 0.05$

## Context

This work extends the inverse-Oslo method previously demonstrated for $^{87}\mathrm{Kr}$ by Ingeberg (2020) to the neutron-rich nucleus $^{67}\mathrm{Ni}$, providing the first experimental NLD and $\gamma$SF for this isotope. The study directly addresses the bottleneck role of $^{66}\mathrm{Ni}(n,\gamma)$ identified in the weak i-process by McKay (2020), showing that while the reaction is critical in one-zone models, its impact is reduced in multi-zone AGB star models. The results enable more reliable neutron capture rate calculations for the i-process and demonstrate the feasibility of the inverse-Oslo method for radioactive beams at facilities like HIE-ISOLDE.
