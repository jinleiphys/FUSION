# The $S_{11}- N$(1535) and $-N$(1650) Resonances in Meson-Baryon Unitarized Coupled Channel Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The $s-$wave meson-baryon scattering is analyzed for the strangeness S=0 sector in a Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating Chiral Symmetry. Four channels have been considered: $πN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$, $K Σ$. The needed two particle irreducible matrix amplitude is taken from lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory in a relativistic formalism and low energy constants are fitted to the elastic $πN $ phase-shifts and the $π^- p \to ηn$ and $π^- p \to K^0 Λ$ cross section data. The position of the complex poles in the second Riemann sheet of the scattering amplitude determine masses and widths of the $S_{11}-$ $N$(1535) and $-N$(1650) resonances, in reasonable agreement with experiment. A good overall description of data, from $πN$ threshold up to 2 GeV, is achieved keeping in mind that the two pion production channel has not been included.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter coupled channel formalism incorporating lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory, with 12 fitted low-energy constants, can describe elastic πN phase shifts and inelastic cross sections from threshold to 2 GeV, but the explicit omission of the three-body ππN channel means the description is necessarily incomplete, particularly for inelasticities above 1406.4 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=0, isospin T=1/2 sector, coupling four channels (πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ). The two-particle irreducible amplitude was taken from lowest-order relativistic Chiral Perturbation Theory, and 12 renormalization constants (low-energy constants) were fitted to elastic πN phase shifts and the π⁻p → ηn and π⁻p → K⁰Λ cross section data. The complex poles of the scattering amplitude in the second Riemann sheet were then extracted to determine resonance masses and widths.

## Key numbers

- The fit used 281 phase shift and inelasticity data points from πN scattering, covering center-of-mass energies from 1077.84 MeV to 1946.52 MeV.
- For CM energies below 1406.4 MeV, where η > 0.99, phase shifts were assigned a 3% relative error added in quadrature with a 1° systematic absolute error.
- For CM energies above 1406.4 MeV, phase shifts were assigned a 3% relative error added in quadrature with a 15° systematic absolute error to account for the omitted ππN channel.
- The pion weak decay constant was taken as fπ = 93.2 MeV, with fη = fK = 1.3 fπ.
- Physical masses used: mπ = 139.57 MeV, mη = 547.45 MeV, mK = 497.67 MeV, MN = 938.27 MeV (proton) or 939.57 MeV (neutron), MΛ = 1115.68 MeV, MΣ = 1192.55 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to pion-pion scattering (EJ99) and the πN P33 partial wave (EJ01) into the S11 channel, incorporating chiral symmetry constraints without phenomenological form factors. It contrasts with the K-matrix approach of Vrana, Dytman, and Lee (2000), which includes the ππN channel through effective two-body channels but does not incorporate chiral symmetry, and with the Schrödinger coupled channel treatment of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995), which included hadronic form factors. The results enable future inclusion of the three-body ππN channel and matching to higher-order Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations.
