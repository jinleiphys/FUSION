# Reply to "Comment on `Breakdown of the expansion of finite-size corrections to the hydrogen Lamb shift in moments of charge distribution'"

> To comply with the critique of the Comment [J. Arrington, arXiv:1602.01461], we consider another modification of the proton electric form factor, which resolves the "proton-radius puzzle". The proposed modification satisfies all the consistency criteria put forward in the Comment, and yet has a similar impact on the puzzle as that of the original paper. Contrary to the concluding statement of the Comment, it is not difficult to find an ad hoc modification of the form factor at low $Q$ that resolves the discrepancy and is consistent with analyticity constraints. We emphasize once again that we do not consider such an ad hoc modification of the proton form factor to be a solution of the puzzle until a physical mechanism for it is found.

## Key claim
The authors present a modified proton electric form factor that resolves the proton-radius puzzle while satisfying the analyticity constraints that their earlier modification violated, but they emphasize that this remains an ad hoc modification and not a solution until a physical mechanism is found.

## Method
The authors decompose the proton electric form factor into a smooth part (taken from the Arrington and Sick chain-fraction fit to electron-proton scattering data) and a nonsmooth part parameterized as a rational function with poles only on the negative Q² axis. The two free parameters of the nonsmooth part are fitted to reproduce the empirical finite-size contributions to the Lamb shift in both electronic hydrogen and muonic hydrogen simultaneously.

## Key numbers
- The position parameter Q₀ is set to 1.6 MeV, placing the modification at the muonic hydrogen inverse Bohr radius scale.
- The fitted parameters are A = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ MeV² and ε = 0.143 MeV.
- The magnitude of the modification relative to the smooth form factor is |G̃_E / Ḡ_E| < 3 × 10⁻⁶ for any positive Q².
- The ratio of the modification to the deviation of the form factor from unity is |G̃_E / (Ḡ_E − 1)| < 0.57.
- The modified form factor yields a Lamb shift contribution of −0.6200 neV for electronic hydrogen and −3650 μeV for muonic hydrogen, matching the empirical values.
- The nonsmooth part contributes a third moment ⟨r³⟩_E of (8.539)³ fm³, compared to (1.052)³ fm³ from the smooth part.

## Context
This reply addresses the critique by Arrington (2016) of the authors' earlier work (Hagelstein and Pascalutsa, 2015), which had proposed a form factor modification violating analyticity constraints. The new modification satisfies all consistency criteria raised in the Comment while still resolving the proton-radius puzzle. The work enables further investigation into whether a physical mechanism can be found for such low-Q² modifications of the proton form factor, as discussed in the review by Hagelstein and Pascalutsa (2015).
