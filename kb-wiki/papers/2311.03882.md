# Odd nuclei and quasiparticle excitations within the Barcelona Catania Paris Madrid energy density functional

> An extension of the Barcelona Catania Paris Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional is proposed to deal with odd-mass systems as well as multiquasiparticle excitations. The extension is based on the assumption that the equal filling approximation (EFA) is a valid alternative to the traditional full blocking procedure of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. The assumption is supported by the excellent agreement between full blocking and EFA calculations obtained with different parametrizations of the Gogny interaction. The EFA augmented BCPM functional is used to compute low energy excitation spectra of selected nuclei in different regions of the nuclear chart, and high-$K$ isomers in $^{178}$Hf. We show that BCPM predictions are in good agreement with Gogny D1M results and experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the equal filling approximation (EFA) is a valid alternative to the full blocking procedure of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method for extending the Barcelona Catania Paris Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional to odd-mass nuclei and multiquasiparticle excitations, based on the assumption that the absence of time-odd fields in BCPM does not compromise accuracy when using EFA.

## Method

The authors implemented the EFA within the BCPM functional framework, where the density matrix and pairing tensor are averaged over time-reversed blocked quasiparticle states to preserve time-reversal symmetry. They first validated the equivalence between EFA and full blocking by comparing results obtained with the Gogny D1M and D1S interactions for selected nuclei across different mass regions. They then performed BCPM-EFA calculations for low-energy excitation spectra of odd-mass nuclei in five isotopic chains (rutherfordium, plutonium, dysprosium, lanthanum, and krypton) and for high-K isomers in 178Hf, preserving axial symmetry so that K (angular momentum projection) remains a good quantum number.

## Key numbers

- For 253-259Rf isotopes, all levels have prolate quadrupole deformation with beta2 approximately 0.28 and negligible octupole deformation, with one-to-one correspondence between EFA and full blocking excitation energies
- Plutonium isotopes have quadrupole deformation beta2 approximately 0.27 in both full blocking and EFA cases
- 239Pu shows some levels with octupole deformation as large as beta3 = 0.1 in full blocking calculations
- Dysprosium isotopes show deformation ranging from beta2 approximately 0.20 in 155Dy up to beta2 approximately 0.30 in 161Dy
- 135La exhibits shape coexistence with minima at beta2 approximately 0.10 on the prolate side and beta2 approximately -0.10 on the oblate side
- 137-139La are spherical, while 141La has beta2 approximately 0.06

## Context

This work extends the BCPM functional, previously limited to even-even nuclei (Baldo 2013), to odd-mass systems and multiquasiparticle excitations by adopting the EFA approach validated for Skyrme forces by Schunck (2010). The authors demonstrate that the EFA-full blocking equivalence also holds for Gogny forces (D1M and D1S parametrizations), enabling BCPM-EFA calculations that can be compared against Gogny D1M results and experimental data. This extension enables future large-scale calculations of odd-mass nuclei properties relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis and neutron star physics, building on prior BCPM applications by Giuliani (2013, 2018, 2020) and Sharma (2015).
