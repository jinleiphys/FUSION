# Nuclear inputs of key iron isotopes for core-collapse modeling and simulation

> From the modeling and simulation results of presupernova evolution of massive stars, it was found that isotopes of iron, $^{54,55,56}$Fe, play a significant role inside the stellar cores, primarily decreasing the electron-to-baryon ratio ($Y_{e}$) mainly via electron capture processes thereby reducing the pressure support. The neutrinos produced, as a result of these capture processes, are transparent to the stellar matter and assist in cooling the core thereby reducing the entropy. The structure of the presupernova star is altered both by the changes in $Y_{e}$ and the entropy of the core material. Here we present the microscopic calculation of Gamow-Teller strength distributions for isotopes of iron. The calculation is also compared with other theoretical models and experimental data. Presented also are stellar electron capture rates and associated neutrino cooling rates, due to isotopes of iron, in a form suitable for simulation and modeling codes. It is hoped that the nuclear inputs presented here should assist core-collapse simulators in the process of fine-tuning of the $Y_{e}$ parameter during various phases of presupernova evolution of massive stars. A reliable and accurate time evolution of this parameter is a possible key to generate a successful explosion in modeling of core-collapse supernovae.

# Nuclear inputs of key iron isotopes for core-collapse modeling and simulation

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model provides Gamow-Teller strength distributions, stellar electron capture rates, and neutrino cooling rates for ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe that are suitable for incorporation into core-collapse supernova simulation codes, with the caveat that during oxygen and silicon burning phases the reported electron capture rates on ⁵⁴Fe are around three times larger than those calculated by large-scale shell model (LSSM), and the neutrino cooling rates up to four times larger, which may assist in fine-tuning the time evolution of the electron-to-baryon ratio (Yₑ).

## Method
The proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model, originally developed by Halbleib and Sorensen (1967) and extended to nuclear excited states by Muto and collaborators (1992), was used to calculate ground and excited state Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distribution functions for ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe. A quenching factor of 0.6 was applied to the calculated GT strengths, and experimentally adopted deformation parameters from Raman et al. (1987) were used for ⁵⁴Fe and ⁵⁶Fe. Stellar electron capture rates and neutrino cooling rates (including contributions from both electron capture and positron decay) were computed as functions of stellar temperature, density, and Fermi energy on a fine-grid scale suitable for interpolation in simulation codes.

## Key numbers
- For ⁵⁴Fe: pn-QRPA calculated GT₊ centroid at 3.6 MeV (compared to measured centroid at 3.6 MeV); total B(GT₊) = 1.7 (measured total B(GT₊) = 1.5); GT₋ centroid at 5.0 MeV (measured centroid at 5.0 MeV); total B(GT₋) = 5.8 (measured total B(GT₋) = 5.5).
- For ⁵⁵Fe: pn-QRPA calculated GT₊ centroid at 6.2 MeV; total B(GT₊) = 2.0; GT₋ centroid at 5.5 MeV; total B(GT₋) = 5.6.
- For ⁵⁶Fe: pn-QRPA calculated GT₊ centroid at 3.5 MeV (measured centroid at 3.5 MeV); total B(GT₊) = 2.2 (measured total B(GT₊) = 2.0); GT₋ total B(GT₋) = 9.9 (measured ∑B(GT₋) = 9.9 ± 2.4 from Rapaport et al. 1983).
- At stellar density 10⁷ g cm⁻³ and T₉ = 1, the pn-QRPA electron capture rate on ⁵⁴Fe is around 3 orders of magnitude smaller than the previous pn-QRPA calculation from 2004.
- At T₉ = 1 and density 10⁷ g cm⁻³, the TQRPA electron capture rates on ⁵⁶Fe are suppressed by 7 orders of magnitude compared to pn-QRPA and LSSM rates.
- During oxygen and silicon burning phases, pn-QRPA electron capture rates on ⁵⁴Fe are around 3 times larger than LSSM rates; neutrino cooling rates on ⁵⁴Fe are up to 4 times larger than LSSM rates.

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations by Nabi (2009, 2011, 2011a) and provides updated weak interaction rates for ⁵⁴Fe, ⁵⁵Fe, and ⁵⁶Fe using improved model parameters (particle-hole GT strength parameter χ = 0.15 MeV, particle-particle GT strength parameter κ = 0.07 MeV) and experimentally adopted deformations from Raman et al. (1987). The results are compared with large-scale shell model calculations by Langanke and collaborators (2000), the earlier FFN rates by Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1982), and the thermofield dynamics QRPA formalism by Dzhioev and collaborators (2010). The calculated rates are intended to assist core-collapse simulators in fine-tuning the time evolution of Yₑ, which may be key to generating successful explosions in supernova models. The authors note that significant progress is expected from next-generation radioactive ion-beam facilities (FAIR in Germany, FRIB in USA, and FRIB in Japan) when measured GT strength distributions for more nuclei, including unstable isotopes, become available.
