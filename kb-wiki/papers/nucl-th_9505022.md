# A METHOD OF CALCULATING THE JOST FUNCTION FOR ANALYTIC POTENTIALS

> A combination of the variable-constant and complex coordinate rotation methods is used to solve the two-body Schrödinger equation. The latter is replaced by a system of linear first-order differential equations, which enables one to perform direct calculation of the Jost function for all complex momenta of physical interest, including the spectral points corresponding to bound and resonance states. Explicit forms of the equations, appropriate for central, noncentral, and Coulomb-tailed potentials are given. Within the proposed method, the scattering, bound, virtual, and resonance state problems can be treated in a unified way. The effectiveness of the method is demonstrated by a numerical example.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method that combines the variable-constant approach with complex coordinate rotation to solve the two-body Schrodinger equation, enabling direct calculation of the Jost function for all complex momenta of physical interest including bound, virtual, and resonance states, provided the potential is analytic and vanishes rapidly enough along the rotated ray.

## Method

The authors replace the radial Schrodinger equation with a system of linear first-order differential equations for functions related to the Jost solutions, then apply a complex rotation of the radial variable (r → x exp(iθ)) to extend the method onto the unphysical sheet of the complex momentum plane. The coupled differential equations are solved numerically from x=0 to a sufficiently large x_max, with boundary conditions imposed at the origin using the Riccati-Bessel functions as zero-order approximations. Explicit forms of the equations are given for central short-range potentials, noncentral spin-dependent potentials, and potentials with Coulomb tails.

## Key numbers

- For the triplet NN-interaction exponential potential (v=155.17 MeV, R=0.76 fm, ħ²/(2μ)=41.47 MeV·fm²), the Jost function zero lies at k = i 0.23640 fm⁻¹, corresponding to the deuteron binding energy.
- For the singlet NN-interaction exponential potential (v=104.2 MeV, R=0.73 fm), the Jost function zero lies on the negative imaginary axis, corresponding to the virtual singlet deuteron.
- The complex rotation angle θ must be greater than the resonance angle φ defined by k₀ = |k₀| exp(-iφ) to make the resonance state wave function square integrable.
- The potential must satisfy lim_{r→0} r² V(r) = 0 and lim_{r→∞} r^{1+ε} V(r) = 0 with ε > 0 for the method to apply.

## Context

This work extends the variable-phase approach of Rakityansky (prior work) from bound and scattering states (upper half of complex momentum plane) to include resonance states on the unphysical sheet. The method combines the variable-constant method with the complex coordinate rotation (complex-scaling) technique used in atomic and molecular physics by Kukulin and others, enabling a unified treatment of scattering, bound, virtual, and resonance states. The approach enables direct calculation of the Jost function and Jost matrix for central, noncentral, and Coulomb-tailed potentials without requiring the full solution of the scattering problem first.
