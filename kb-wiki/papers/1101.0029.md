# Heavy quarks and the collective properties of hot QCD

> After reviewing the evidence that QCD matter at ultrarelativistic energies behaves as a very good fluid, we describe the connection of QCD fluidity to heavy quark observables. We review the way in which heavy quark spectra can place tighter limits on the viscosity of QCD matter. Finally, we show that correlations between flow observables and the event-by-event charm quark abundance ("flavoring") can shed light on the system's equation of state.

## Key claim

Heavy quark observables can probe the equation of state of QCD matter, specifically through correlations between event-by-event charm quark abundance ("flavoring") and flow observables, but this effect is only significant in the deconfined, strongly coupled phase and requires that heavy quarks are thermally (though not chemically) equilibrated with the medium.

## Method

The authors compute the speed of sound in a deconfined QCD medium with a small admixture of charm quarks by adding a Polyakov loop density term to the free energy density, using lattice QCD results for the Polyakov loop expectation value. They then estimate the event-by-event variation of the dimensionless charm-to-entropy ratio at LHC energies using perturbative QCD cross sections and logarithmic multiplicity scaling, and connect changes in the speed of sound to changes in average transverse momentum via hydrodynamic expansion relations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of heavy quark thermalization time to light quark thermalization time scales as $\tau_{heavy}/\tau_{light} \sim M/T$, where $M$ is the heavy quark mass and $T$ is the temperature.
- The estimated average dimensionless charm-to-entropy ratio at the LHC is $\tilde{\rho} \sim 0.05$.
- The expected charm pair abundance at the LHC is $\sim 10^{1-2}$ pairs, parametrically smaller than the total multiplicity of $\sim 10^4$.
- In the confined phase, the speed of sound of a mixture scales as $c_s^2 \sim 1/3 + \mathcal{O}(\tilde{\rho} \, T/M_{meson})$, parametrically smaller than the deconfined phase contribution of $\mathcal{O}(\tilde{\rho})$.

## Context

This work extends the finding that QCD matter at RHIC behaves as a near-perfect fluid (Heinz, Shuryak, Huovinen) by proposing heavy quarks as a more sensitive probe of the equation of state than the flow observables previously used. The authors note that standard hydrodynamic probes are insensitive to the equation of state (Shuryak, Huovinen) and that probes like HBT radii show no phase transition structure (Heinz, Pratt). The paper enables experimental searches for correlations between charm abundance and average transverse momentum at the LHC, which could distinguish between a deconfined strongly coupled QGP and a weakly coupled or confined system.
