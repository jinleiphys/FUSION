# In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group for Open-Shell Nuclei

> We present a nonperturbative derivation of effective valence-shell Hamiltonians in the framework of the recently developed in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG). As a first application, we calculate the spectra of $p$- and $sd$-shell nuclei, $^6$Li and $^{18}$O, based on evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions. For $^6$Li, the spectrum is in very good agreement with ab-initio results. For $^{18}$O, the IM-SRG provides a new method for the shell model to systematically go beyond effective interaction techniques based on diagrammatic expansions.

## Key claim

The in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) can be generalized to derive effective valence-shell Hamiltonians for open-shell nuclei nonperturbatively, with the condition that the truncation to normal-ordered two-body operators [IM-SRG(2)] is a very good approximation, as indicated by the weak generator dependence of the results.

## Method

The IM-SRG flow equations are solved in a Hartree-Fock (HF) basis truncated to \(e_{\rm max}={\rm max}(2n+l)\) single-particle excitations, keeping up to normal-ordered two-body operators [IM-SRG(2)]. The generator \(\eta(s) = [H^{\rm d}(s),H(s)]\) is constructed from the diagonal part of the Hamiltonian to drive off-diagonal couplings to zero, with two specific choices for the off-diagonal set: \(H_1^{\rm od}\) (containing \(f_{ph}, f_{qv}, \Gamma_{pp'hh'}, \Gamma_{pp'(vh\,{\rm or}\,hv)}, \Gamma_{(pq\,{\rm or}\,qp)vv'}\) plus conjugates) and \(H_2^{\rm od}\) (which additionally drives the one-body part to diagonal). The resulting effective valence-shell Hamiltonian \(H_{\rm eff} \equiv P H_{1,2}^{\rm d}(\infty) P - E_0^{A_c}\) is then diagonalized in the valence space to obtain energies relative to the core ground state.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of \(^6\)Li: converges to \(-32.7(3)\) MeV from IM-SRG(2), compared to extrapolated NCSM value \(-32.0(2)\) MeV (both using SRG-evolved N\(^3\)LO NN potential at \(\lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- For \(^6\)Li, calculations use harmonic-oscillator parameter \(\hbar\omega = 24\) MeV and HF basis; the \(\hbar\omega\) dependence is very weak in the HF basis for large \(e_{\rm max}\).
- For \(^{18}\)O, IM-SRG(2) calculations use an \(e_{\rm max}=8\) space and \(\hbar\omega = 24\) MeV in the HF basis, with single-particle energies from the USDb interaction for the diagonalization in the \(sd\)-shell.
- The IM-SRG scaling is polynomial: \(\sim N_h^4 N_p^2\) with the number of hole and particle orbits.

## Context

This work extends the IM-SRG method, previously developed for closed-shell nuclei (Tsukiyama 2011, Tsukiyama PhD thesis), to open-shell systems with valence nucleons. For \(^6\)Li, the results agree well with No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations (Ericson 2008). For \(^{18}\)O, the IM-SRG provides a nonperturbative alternative to conventional diagrammatic \(Q\)-box expansions (Morten 2005) used to derive effective shell-model interactions, and goes beyond perturbative approaches by including many higher-order terms. The method enables future extensions to larger valence spaces, effective operators, and inclusion of three-nucleon forces.
