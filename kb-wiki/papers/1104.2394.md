# Equation of state in the PNJL model with the entanglement interaction

> The equation of state and the phase diagram in two-flavor QCD are investigated by the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with an entanglement vertex between the chiral condensate and the Polyakov-loop. The entanglement-PNJL (EPNJL) model reproduces LQCD data at zero and finite chemical potential better than the PNJL model. Hadronic degrees of freedom are taken into account by the free-hadron-gas (FHG) model with the volume-exclusion effect due to the hadron generation. The EPNJL+FHG model improves agreement of the EPNJL model with LQCD data particularly at small temperature. The quarkyonic phase survives, even if the correlation between the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop is strong and hadron degrees of freedom are taken into account. However, the location of the quarkyonic phase is sensitive to the strength of the volume exclusion.

## Key claim

The entanglement-PNJL (EPNJL) model, which introduces a Polyakov-loop-dependent four-quark vertex, reproduces lattice QCD data on the equation of state and phase diagram better than the standard PNJL model, but the quarkyonic phase (a chirally-symmetric confined phase with finite quark density) survives only when the chiral transition temperature is lower than the deconfinement transition temperature, which occurs for chemical potentials above approximately 0.15 GeV.

## Method

The authors extended the standard two-flavor PNJL model by making the scalar four-quark coupling constant depend on the Polyakov loop as G_s(Phi) = G_s [1 - alpha_1 Phi Phi* - alpha_2 (Phi^3 + Phi*^3)], with parameters alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.2 determined from lattice QCD data at zero and imaginary chemical potential. They then added hadronic degrees of freedom through a free-hadron-gas (FHG) model including pions, sigma mesons, protons, and neutrons, incorporating a volume-exclusion effect with hadron radii of 0.5 fm or 0.8 fm. The thermodynamic potential was computed in the mean-field approximation with a three-dimensional momentum cutoff regularization, and the phase diagram was mapped by solving the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate.

## Key numbers

* The pseudocritical temperature for the deconfinement transition at zero chemical potential is T_Phi = 173 MeV, used as the reference scale for the model.
* The chiral transition temperature in the standard PNJL model is T_sigma = 216 MeV, giving a difference Delta = |T_sigma - T_Phi|/T_Phi of approximately 20 percent.
* The entanglement parameters are alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.2, determined from lattice QCD data at zero and imaginary chemical potential.
* The hadron masses used in the FHG model are m_pi = 139 MeV for pion, m_sigma = 680 MeV for sigma meson, and m_p = m_n = 940 MeV for proton and neutron.
* The volume-exclusion radii considered are 0.8 fm (charge radius of nucleon) and 0.5 fm (repulsive core radius in nuclear force).
* The chiral and deconfinement crossovers coincide for mu less than approximately 0.15 GeV, but diverge for larger chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model framework developed by Meisinger, Fukushima, Ratti, Rossner, Schaefer, Kouno, and others by introducing an entanglement vertex between the chiral condensate and Polyakov loop, following the earlier EPNJL proposal by Sakai et al. (2010). The results contradict the simpler PNJL model's prediction of a sizable difference between chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures at zero chemical potential, and instead support the lattice QCD finding of coincident crossovers. The inclusion of hadronic degrees of freedom via the FHG model with volume exclusion, following the approach of Rischke and Steinheimer, enables better agreement with lattice QCD data at low temperature and clarifies the role of the quarkyonic phase originally proposed by McLerran and Pisarski in the large-N_c limit. The work enables more reliable predictions of the QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential, where lattice QCD suffers from the sign problem.
