# NN correlations and final-state interactions in (e,e'NN) reactions

> After a brief overview of relevant studies on one-nucleon knockout showing the importance of quantitatively understanding the origin of the quenched spectroscopic factors extracted from data, attention is focussed on two-nucleon emission as a suitable tool to investigate nucleon-nucleon correlations inside complex nuclei. In particular, direct (e,e$'$pp) and (e,e$'$pn) reactions are discussed, and the role of final-state interactions is studied. The influence of the mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons is shown to depend on the kinematics and on the type of the considered reaction.

## Key claim
The apparent $Q^2$ dependence of spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e$'$p) data, jumping from values around 0.6–0.7 at low $Q^2$ to unity at $Q^2 \ge 1$ GeV$^2$, can be resolved by a consistent treatment of initial and final states with the same energy-dependent Hamiltonian, but the remaining quantitative discrepancy between calculated and observed spectroscopic factors is still tied to nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical framework for exclusive two-nucleon knockout reactions (e,e$'$pp) and (e,e$'$pn) on complex nuclei, using the hadron current formalism in the one-photon-exchange approximation. They compare four treatments of final-state interactions: plane-wave (PW), distorted-wave with an optical potential (DW), plane-wave plus mutual nucleon-nucleon interaction (PW-NN), and distorted-wave plus mutual NN interaction (DW-NN). Calculations are performed for $^{16}$O targets under superparallel kinematics at Mainz and compared to NIKHEF kinematics.

## Key numbers
- For $^{16}$O p-shell holes, Green-function calculations give $S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.890$ and $S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.914$, while low-energy (e,e$'$p) experiments yield $S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.644$ and $S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.537$.
- At higher $Q^2$, extracted spectroscopic factors are $S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.73$ and $S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.71$ (Udias 1999) and $S_{p_{1/2}} = 0.72$ and $S_{p_{3/2}} = 0.67$ (Kelly 1999).
- Reanalysis of $^{12}$C(e,e$'$p) data gives s- and p-shell strength reduced by factor $0.57 \pm 0.02$ for $Q^2 \le 0.3$ GeV$^2$, while for $Q^2 \ge 1$ GeV$^2$ the strength approximates the independent-particle shell model value.
- In superparallel kinematics at Mainz ($E_0 = 855$ MeV, $\theta_e = 18^\circ$, $\omega = 215$ MeV, $q = 316$ MeV/$c$), NN-FSI enhances the (e,e$'$pp) cross section by roughly an order of magnitude at $p_{\rm B} \simeq 300$ MeV/$c$.
- For (e,e$'$pn) in the same kinematics, predicted cross sections are about one order of magnitude larger than for (e,e$'$pp).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of one-nucleon knockout (e.g., the Oxford review, Polls 1995) that revealed quenched spectroscopic factors, and builds on two-nucleon emission calculations by Boffi, Giusti, and Pacati (1993) and Geurts (1996). The authors address the open problem of disentangling short-range correlations, tensor correlations, and two-body currents in (e,e$'$pp) and (e,e$'$pn) reactions. The results enable future experiments at Mainz and NIKHEF to interpret data with improved final-state interaction treatments, and the full three-body problem of the final state is stated to be in progress.
