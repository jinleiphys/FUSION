# Isospin kaon anomaly and its consequences

> Isospin symmetry is well fulfilled in the QCD vacuum, as evidenced by small mass differences of isospin partners and suppressed isospin-violating decays. Recently, the NA61/SHINE collaboration reported an unexpectedly large isospin-violating charged-to-neutral kaon ratio in Ar-Sc heavy-ion collisions (HIC).Using a quark recombination approach, we introduce a function of kaon multiplicities that reduces to unity in the isospin-symmetric limit independently of the scattering energy and type of nuclei. Using this quantity, we show that nucleus-nucleus collisions violate isospin sizably (at the $6.4σ$--level), while proton-proton data on kaon multiplicities do not. We predict other isospin-violating enhancements in HIC, such as the proton-to-neutron ratio $p/n \sim 1.2$ and the hyperon ratio $Σ^{+}/Σ^{-}\sim1.4$. Finally, we extend the approach to antiquarks in the initial state, useful for e.g. pion-nucleus scattering reactions.

# Isospin kaon anomaly and its consequences

## Key claim

Using a modified ratio of kaon multiplicities that reduces to unity in the isospin-symmetric limit independently of scattering energy and nuclear type, the authors show that nucleus-nucleus collisions violate isospin at the 6.4 sigma level, while proton-proton data on kaon multiplicities do not. The isospin breaking is attributed to finite-density effects in nuclear collisions rather than to a fundamental violation of isospin symmetry in QCD.

## Method

The authors employ a quark recombination model in which hadron multiplicities are proportional to the probabilities of picking constituent quarks and antiquarks from an ensemble that includes both preexisting quarks from the colliding nuclei and quark-antiquark pairs created from the QCD vacuum. They define a modified ratio of kaon multiplicities, denoted as R_tilde_K, which equals unity when isospin is conserved (alpha = beta, where alpha and beta are the numbers of u-ubar and d-dbar vacuum pairs) for any collision energy and any nuclear charge-to-mass ratio. The null hypothesis of isospin conservation is tested via chi-squared analysis of world data on kaon production in both proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- The weighted average of R_tilde_K from nucleus-nucleus collisions is 1.185 ± 0.029, incompatible with unity at the 6.47 sigma level.
- The chi-squared value for the null hypothesis (R_tilde_K = 1) in nucleus-nucleus collisions is chi^2(y=1) = 50.3 for 15 degrees of freedom, corresponding to a worse-fit probability of 1.1 × 10^{-5}.
- For proton-proton collisions, the weighted average of R_tilde_K is 1.030 ± 0.038, compatible with unity; the chi-squared for the null hypothesis is chi^2(y=1) = 6.270 for 9 degrees of freedom, with a worse-fit probability of 0.71.
- The weighted average of the simpler ratio R_K from nucleus-nucleus collisions is 1.152 ± 0.027.
- The NA61/SHINE measurement of R_K in Ar-Sc collisions is 1.184 ± 0.061.
- The predicted proton-to-neutron ratio in heavy-ion collisions is p/n ~ 1.185 ± 0.029.
- The predicted hyperon ratio is Sigma^+/Sigma^- ~ r^2 = 1.404 ± 0.068.
- The predicted Delta^++/Delta^- ratio is r^3 = 1.664 ± 0.120.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the kaon anomaly reported by the NA61/SHINE collaboration (2023), which found an unexpectedly large charged-to-neutral kaon ratio in Ar-Sc collisions that could not be reproduced by the hadron resonance gas model (HRG) or the UrQMD approach. The authors build on the quark recombination formalism previously used by Stepaniak (2023) and Bonesini (2001), and they note that the UrQMD approach was recently updated by Reichert (2025) to require three times more u-ubar than d-dbar production to describe the data. The paper provides predictions for other isospin-violating enhancements in heavy-ion collisions, including proton/neutron and hyperon ratios, and extends the formalism to reactions involving initial-state antiquarks such as pion-nucleus scattering, where the NA61/SHINE collaboration (2022) found R_K ~ 1.2 in pi- C collisions.
