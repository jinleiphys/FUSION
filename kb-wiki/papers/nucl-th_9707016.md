# Nuclear Sizes and the Isotope Shift

> Darwin-Foldy nuclear-size corrections in electronic atoms and nuclear radii are discussed from the nuclear-physics perspective. Interpretation of precise isotope-shift measurements is formalism dependent, and care must be exercised in interpreting these results and those obtained from relativistic electron scattering from nuclei. We strongly advocate that the entire nuclear-charge operator be used in calculating nuclear-size corrections in atoms, rather than relegating portions of it to the non-radiative recoil corrections. A preliminary examination of the intrinsic deuteron radius obtained from isotope-shift measurements suggests the presence of small meson-exchange currents (exotic binding contributions of relativistic order) in the nuclear charge operator, which contribute approximately 1/2%.

# Nuclear Sizes and the Isotope Shift

## Key claim

The interpretation of precise isotope-shift measurements is formalism-dependent, and the authors strongly advocate that the entire nuclear-charge operator be used in calculating nuclear-size corrections in atoms rather than relegating portions of it to non-radiative recoil corrections. A preliminary examination of the intrinsic deuteron radius obtained from isotope-shift measurements suggests the presence of small meson-exchange currents contributing approximately 0.5%, though this result carries caveats about unitary equivalence parameters that modify the nuclear potential and charge operator.

## Method

The authors review the Darwin-Foldy correction and its treatment across electron scattering and atomic physics, comparing the covariant form factor conventions (Dirac/Pauli form factors, Sachs form factors, and the invariant charge form factor) for the proton, deuteron, triton, helium-3, and helium-4. They then perform a nonrelativistic impulse-approximation calculation of the deuteron matter radius using first-generation and second-generation nucleon-nucleon potentials, corrected by the zero-range approximation, and compare this theoretical baseline to the experimental deuteron point-nucleon radius extracted from the 2S-1S deuterium-hydrogen isotope shift measured in Garching and Paris.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of Darwin-Foldy corrections in nuclear charge distributions, building on earlier treatments by Friar (1970s) and the Sachs form factor formalism. The authors address the controversy between Pachucki and Karshenboim (1995) and Khriplovich, Milstein, and Sen'kov (1996) regarding whether the Darwin-Foldy term should be included for the deuteron in atomic calculations. The analysis uses second-generation nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen, Argonne V18, Reid Soft Core 93) to provide an improved baseline for the deuteron matter radius, and compares with the experimental isotope-shift measurements from Garching and Paris. The results enable future relativistic treatments of deuteron dynamics at "second-generation" quality to resolve the remaining 0.004 fm discrepancy between the experimental point-nucleon radius and the theoretical estimate including meson-exchange currents.
