# Jet Tomography of Au+Au Reactions Including Multi-gluon Fluctuations

> Jet tomography is the analysis of the attenuation pattern of high transverse momentum hadrons to determine certain line integral transforms of the density profile of the QCD matter produced in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. In this letter, we calculate the distortion of jet tomography due to multi-gluon fluctuations within the GLV radiative energy loss formalism. We find that fluctuations of the average gluon number, <N^g> ~ 3 for RHIC initial conditions, reduce the attenuation of pions by approximately a factor Z ~ 0.4-0.5. Therefore the plasma density inferred from jet tomography without fluctuations must be enhanced by a factor 1/Z ~ 2.

## Key claim

Multi-gluon fluctuations within the GLV radiative energy loss formalism reduce the attenuation of high transverse momentum pions by a factor Z ~ 0.4-0.5, meaning that the plasma density inferred from jet tomography without including fluctuations must be enhanced by a factor 1/Z ~ 2.

## Method

The authors computed the induced gluon radiation spectrum up to third order in opacity expansion for a static plasma with an effective opacity L/λ_g = 5, using a color-screened Yukawa potential with screening mass μ ≈ 0.5 GeV. They then constructed the probability distribution P(ε,E) of the total fractional energy loss ε via a Poisson expansion that accounts for multi-gluon fluctuations, and applied this distribution to modify the pQCD calculation of π⁰ production in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Average gluon number ⟨N^g⟩ ~ 3 for RHIC initial conditions
- Fluctuation renormalization factor Z ≈ 0.4-0.5, requiring density enhancement by 1/Z ~ 2
- Effective static opacity L/λ_g = 5 used to fit PHENIX data
- Transport coefficient μ²/λ_g ≈ 0.25 GeV²/fm
- Induced fractional energy loss varies from 0.5 at E ~ 5 GeV down to ~0.3 at 20 GeV
- Estimated initial gluon rapidity density dN^g/dy ~ 800 ± 100
- Estimated initial gluon density ρ_g ~ 20/fm³ ~ 100 ρ_A

## Context

This work extends the GLV opacity expansion formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 2001) for jet energy loss by including multi-gluon fluctuations, which were neglected in earlier jet tomography analyses (Gyulassy, Vitev, Wang 2001; Levai et al. 2001). The results show that the Poisson-distributed fluctuations significantly reduce the quenching effect compared to mean energy loss approximations, and the authors find that their effective transport coefficient is four times smaller than estimates in Baier et al. (2001). This enables more reliable extraction of initial gluon densities from RHIC data and provides a framework for interpreting upcoming high-statistics data at p_T ~ 10-20 GeV.
