# Soft photon production from real-time dynamics of jet fragmentation

> Soft photons produced in heavy ion collisions are an important tool for probing the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. It is therefore crucial to understand the background - soft photons produced in elementary collisions. Low theorem states that soft photon production in hadron collisions is dominated by Bremsstrahlung off charged initial and final state hadrons. Surprisingly, almost every experiment observed an enhancement (by a factor of $2÷5$) above Low theorem's prediction. This is the longstanding puzzle of "anomalous soft photon production." The phenomenon is not observed in processes with leptonic final states, which suggests that the mechanism is due to nonperturbative QCD evolution. We study this phenomenon using an exactly soluble, massless, Abelian model in $1+1$ dimensions which shares with QCD many important properties: confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, axial anomaly and $θ$-vacuum. We then apply this model to the soft photon production in the fragmentation of jets produced in $Z^0$ decays and find a qualitative agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the longstanding puzzle of "anomalous soft photon production" (an enhancement by a factor of 2 to 5 above Low theorem predictions) can be explained by nonperturbative QCD dynamics during jet fragmentation, modeled using an exactly soluble Abelian model in 1+1 dimensions. The model achieves only qualitative agreement with DELPHI data and requires an adjustable parameter (effective width gamma) to describe the observed enhancement.

## Method

The authors model jet fragmentation using massless QED in 1+1 dimensions (the Schwinger model), which shares properties with QCD including confinement and chiral symmetry breaking. They introduce a di-jet via an external current representing a quark-antiquark pair moving back-to-back, solve the equations of motion via bosonization, compute the induced electromagnetic current, and evaluate the photon Bremsstrahlung spectrum using the standard formula involving the Fourier transform of the total current. They incorporate flavor-dependent quark charges weighted by Z0 decay branching fractions and introduce a Gaussian distribution for string tension fluctuations and a finite width for the scalar propagator to obtain realistic results.

## Key numbers

- The observed enhancement factor is 2 to 5 above Low theorem predictions
- The DELPHI data measured photons with transverse momenta p_perp < 80 MeV and total energies within 0.2 < E_gamma < 1 GeV
- The model uses a scalar mass m = 0.6 GeV
- The string tension fluctuation uses <kappa^2> = 0.9 GeV/fm, suggested by lattice studies and Regge phenomenology
- The effective width gamma values range from gamma ~ 8 x 10^-4 GeV (for eta meson) to gamma ~ 8 x 10^-2 GeV (for omega meson)
- The fitted value of gamma used in the calculation is gamma ~ 0.003 GeV
- The Z0 decay branching fractions used are (Gamma_uu + Gamma_cc)/Gamma_hadron = 0.331 and (Gamma_dd + Gamma_ss + Gamma_bb)/Gamma_hadron = 0.669

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Loshaj (2011) and Kharzeev (2012) applying the Schwinger model to jet fragmentation, and directly addresses the anomalous soft photon puzzle observed by experiments including Chliapnikov (1984), Botterweck (1991), Banerjee (1992), Belogianni (1997, 2002), and the DELPHI Collaboration (2005, 2010). The model enables a mechanism where coherent oscillations of axial and vector currents during jet fragmentation produce photons, and the authors note that understanding this background is crucial for using soft photons to probe the quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions.
