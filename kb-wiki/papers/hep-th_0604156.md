# Nonequilibrium dynamics in the O(N) model to next-to-next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion

> Nonequilibrium dynamics in quantum field theory has been studied extensively using truncations of the 2PI effective action. Both 1/N and loop expansions beyond leading order show remarkable improvement when compared to mean-field approximations. However, in truncations used so far, only the leading-order parts of the self energy responsible for memory loss, damping and equilibration are included, which makes it difficult to discuss convergence systematically. For that reason we derive the real and causal evolution equations for an O(N) model to next-to-next-to-leading order in the 2PI-1/N expansion. Due to the appearance of internal vertices the resulting equations appear intractable for a full-fledged 3+1 dimensional field theory. Instead, we solve the closely related three-loop approximation in the auxiliary-field formalism numerically in 0+1 dimensions (quantum mechanics) and compare to previous approximations and the exact numerical solution of the Schroedinger equation.

## Key claim

The authors derive the real-time causal evolution equations for the O(N) model to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in the 2PI-1/N expansion, but note that the resulting equations appear intractable for a full 3+1 dimensional field theory due to internal vertices requiring nested memory integrals; they therefore solve only the closely related three-loop approximation in the auxiliary-field formalism numerically in 0+1 dimensions (quantum mechanics).

## Method

The authors use the 2PI effective action in the auxiliary-field formalism to derive statistical and spectral self energies at N$^2$LO in the 1/N expansion for the symmetric phase of the O(N) model, including diagrams with internal vertices for the first time. They then specialize to 0+1 dimensions (quantum mechanics) and solve the evolution equations for the statistical and spectral two-point functions numerically using a leap-frog algorithm with time step dt=0.01, comparing results to the NLO approximation and to the exact numerical solution of the Schrodinger equation for Gaussian initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- For a run until t=20 (2000 timesteps) with dt=0.01, the entire memory kernel fits on a 1GB CPU; using dt=0.001 leads to indistinguishable results.
- A run including the first N$^2$LO diagram takes about 2 days on a single 3GHz machine, with CPU time growing as the number of timesteps to the 4th power.
- A run at NLO is roughly 400 times faster than the N$^2$LO run; including the second N$^2$LO diagram is expected to give at least another factor of 400.
- The authors set m=1 throughout and consider N=8, lambda=1 with initial parameters eta=5, xi=0.4, sigma=1 for comparison with the exact Schrodinger solution.

## Context

This work extends the 2PI-1/N expansion beyond the next-to-leading order (NLO) treatments previously studied by Berges (2001), Mihaila (2000), Aarts (2002), and others, where only leading-order parts of the self energy responsible for memory loss, damping and equilibration were included. The N$^2$LO results enable a quantitative comparison between NLO and N$^2$LO dynamics to test effective convergence of the 1/N expansion, though the authors note that full 3+1 dimensional field theory implementation remains numerically challenging.
