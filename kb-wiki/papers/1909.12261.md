# Catching a glimpse of the parton structure of the bound proton

> A new generation of experiments is expected to shed light on the elusive parton structure of the bound proton. One of the most promising directions is incoherent deeply virtual Compton scattering, which can provide a tomographic view of the bound proton. The first measurement has been recently performed, using $^4$He targets at Jefferson Lab. In the work presented here, a rigorous Impulse Approximation analysis of this process is proposed. As ingredients, state-of-the-art models of the nuclear spectral function and of the parton structure of the struck proton, together with novel scattering amplitudes expressions for a bound moving nucleon, have been used. A good overall agreement with the data is obtained, in particular at high values of the photon virtuality. The observed big difference between results for the bound proton and those for the free one turns out to be due in small part to modifications of the parton structure, and rather it should be related to kinematical nuclear effects. The analysis demonstrates that the comparison of the results of this approach, based on a conventional description, with future precise data, has the potential to expose exotic quark and gluon effects in nuclei.

## Key claim

The observed large difference between beam-spin asymmetries for the bound proton in $^4$He and the free proton is due primarily to kinematical nuclear effects rather than modifications of the parton structure itself, as demonstrated by an impulse approximation analysis that reproduces the data well especially at high photon virtuality.

## Method

The authors performed an impulse approximation (IA) analysis of incoherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off $^4$He, assuming the process proceeds through one quark in one nucleon. They used state-of-the-art models of the nuclear spectral function (based on the Av18 NN interaction including UIX three-body forces) and the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model for the proton generalized parton distributions (GPDs), together with novel scattering amplitude expressions for a bound moving nucleon. The beam-spin asymmetry (BSA) was calculated as a convolution of the nuclear spectral function with the bound-proton DVCS and Bethe-Heitler cross sections, and compared to recent Jefferson Lab EG6/CLAS data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $A_{LU}^{Incoh} / A_{LU}^p$ shows an effect as large as **25 percent** as a function of $x_B$, but this is attributed to kinematical nuclear effects rather than parton structure modification.
- The EMC-like ratio $R_{EMC-like}$ (Eq. 19 in the text) is found to be **close to one**, indicating negligible modification of the bound proton's parton structure in the IA framework.
- The calculation reproduces data well at high $Q^2$ values, with disagreement only at the lowest $Q^2$ bins (below approximately **4 GeV$^2$**, the stated validity limit of the GK model).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful IA analysis of coherent DVCS off $^4$He (Fucini 2018) to the incoherent channel, following the first experimental separation of coherent and incoherent DVCS off $^4$He by the EG6/CLAS collaboration (Hattawy 2017, 2018). The analysis provides a conventional baseline calculation that can be compared with future precise data to expose possible exotic quark and gluon effects in nuclei, addressing the need for realistic benchmark calculations raised by the experimental collaborations. The approach contrasts with earlier estimates by Guzey (2003) and Liuti (2005) that corresponded to different kinematical regions.
