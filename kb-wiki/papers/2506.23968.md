# Finite Gaussian assistance protocols and a conic metric for extremizing spacelike vacuum entanglement

> In a pure Gaussian tripartition, a range of entanglement between two parties ($AB$) can be purified through classical communication of Gaussian measurements performed within the third ($C$). To begin, this work introduces a direct method to calculate a hierarchic series of projective $C$ measurements for the removal of any $AB$ Gaussian noise, circumventing divergences in prior protocols. Next, a multimode conic framework is developed for pursuing the maximum (Gaussian entanglement of assistance, GEOA) or minimum (Gaussian entanglement of formation, GEOF) pure entanglement that may be revealed or required between $AB$. Within this framework, a geometric necessary and sufficient entanglement condition emerges as a doubly-enclosed conic volume, defining a novel distance metric for conic optimization. Extremizing this distance for spacelike vacuum entanglement in the massless and massive free scalar fields yields (1) the highest known lower bound to GEOA, the first that decays slower than the two-point correlation functions and (2) the lowest known upper bound to GEOF, the first that decays exponentially mirroring the mixed $AB$ negativity. Furthermore, combination of the above with a generalization of previous partially-transposed noise filtering techniques allows calculation of a single $C$ measurement that maximizes the purified $AB$ entanglement. Beyond expectation that these behaviors of spacelike GEOA and GEOF persist in interacting theories, the present measurement and optimization techniques are applicable to physical many-body Gaussian states beyond quantum fields.

## Key claim

This work introduces a new geometric framework of double-cone volumes (DCVs) with an inter-DCV distance metric that provides a necessary and sufficient entanglement condition for multimode Gaussian states, enabling the calculation of tighter bounds on Gaussian entanglement of assistance (GEOA) and Gaussian entanglement of formation (GEOF) than previously achieved, though the bounds are not proven to be exact due to violations of symplectic orthogonality among local components (SOL) in the relevant subspaces.

## Method

The authors develop a direct method for calculating projective measurements in a third party C that remove Gaussian noise from an AB bipartition, circumventing divergences that arise in prior Gaussian POVM formulations by working with explicit physical resources (auxiliary modes, unitary transformations from bilinear Hamiltonians, and homodyne detection). They then construct a multimode conic framework for symmetric, phi-pi-uncorrelated Gaussian states where the separability condition reduces to the existence of a non-zero intersection volume between two DCVs, quantified by a Frobenius norm distance metric xi; extremizing this distance yields lower bounds on GEOA and upper bounds on GEOF for spacelike vacuum entanglement in free scalar fields.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of spacelike vacuum entanglement in free scalar fields (scalar1dextra, NKnegativitysphere, NKentsphere, gao2024detectingspacelikevacuumentanglement) and the Gaussian entanglement of formation framework of WolfGEOF, while improving upon the lower and upper bounds for GEOA and GEOF respectively that were previously estimated in NKvolumemeasure and gao2024partialtransposeguided. The new conic distance metric and projective measurement formalism enable calculation of explicit underlying pure states whose entanglement scaling with vacuum separation is parametrically tighter than prior estimates, with the GEOA lower bound achieving asymptotically constant (massive) or double-logarithmic (massless) decay reminiscent of the AKLT state, and the GEOF upper bound achieving exponential decay mirroring the region-region logarithmic negativity.
