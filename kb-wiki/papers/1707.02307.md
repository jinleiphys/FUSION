# Are minimal conditions for collectivity met in e+e- collisions?

> Signatures of collective behavior have been measured in highly relativistic p+p collisions, as well as in p+A, d+A, and 3He+A collisions. Numerous particle correlation measurements in these systems have been successfully described by calculations based on viscous hydrodynamic and transport models. These observations raise the question of the minimum necessary conditions for a system to exhibit collectivity. Recently, numerous scientists have raised the question of whether the quarks and gluons generated in e+e- collisions may satisfy these minimum conditions. In this paper we explore possible signatures of collectivity, or lack thereof, in e+e- collisions utilizing A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) framework which comprises melted color strings, parton scattering, hadronization, and hadron re-scattering.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT framework with a single color string to model e+e- collisions at the Z boson mass, the authors find no observable collectivity signal; however, a simple extension to two color strings predicts finite long-range two-particle correlations and a strong v2 with respect to the initial parton geometry, implying that a minimum of two strings may be sufficient to generate collectivity signals in small collision systems.

## Method

The authors modified the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model version 2.26 to initialize every event with a single color string representing e+e- annihilation at the Z boson mass (91.18 GeV/c2), with the receding quark and antiquark moving along the longitudinal axis. The model then performs string melting into partons, parton-parton scattering with a cross section of 3.7 mb, hadronization via spatial quark coalescence, and final-state hadronic rescattering. For comparison, they also ran events with all final-state interactions turned off, and extended the study to a two-string configuration with strings separated by 0.5 fm in the transverse plane, each at half the Z boson mass.

## Key numbers

- Mean charged particle multiplicity in e+e- collisions at 91 GeV is approximately 21, and the authors tuned the AMPT Lund parameter PARJ(41)=2.5 to achieve a mean initial parton multiplicity of approximately 43 and mean charged particle multiplicity of approximately 21.
- Only 17.9% of all partons from single string melting undergo one or more partonic scatterings; in the two-string case, 40.3% of partons suffer one or more scatterings, similar to the percentage in central d+Au collisions at 19.6-200 GeV.
- The net effect of interactions on v2 in the single string case is less than a 1% effect; in the two-string case, the net v2 is much larger and similar in magnitude and pT trend to that observed in p+p and light ion-nucleus collisions.
- For the two-string configuration at total energies from 184 GeV down to 4.5 GeV, the percentage of partons with at least one scattering ranges from 40.4% at 184 GeV to 11.3% at 4.5 GeV, with dNch/deta (|eta|<2) ranging from 11.6 to 3.7.
- The ridge feature in two-string correlations is visible down to 45 GeV, with a hint at 30 GeV, and disappears at lower energies; the v2 at pT=0.75 GeV/c relative to the true geometry shows little energy dependence until below approximately 10 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of collectivity in small hadronic collision systems (p+p, p+A, d+A, 3He+A) to the case of e+e- collisions, motivated by observations that viscous hydrodynamic calculations describe data from systems as small as p+p (Weller 2017) and that the agreement may reflect non-hydrodynamic modes damping away rather than equilibration (Romatschke 2016, 2017). The study uses the AMPT framework, which has been successful in describing particle spectra, yields, and azimuthal correlations in systems from p+A to A+A at RHIC and LHC (Bzdak 2014, Koop 2015, Adare 2015, Ma 2016). The results suggest that future experiments at a possible Electron-Ion Collider could explore whether color dipole scattering with a nucleus might generate similar conditions, and whether higher-order QCD effects could produce collectivity in e+e- collisions.
