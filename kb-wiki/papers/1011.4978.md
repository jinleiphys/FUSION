# Validation and Verification of MCNP6 Against Intermediate and High-Energy Experimental Data and Results by Other Codes

> MCNP6, the latest and most advanced LANL transport code representing a recent merger of MCNP5 and MCNPX, has been Validated and Verified (V&V) against a variety of intermediate and high-energy experimental data and against results by different versions of MCNPX and other codes. In the present work, we V&V MCNP6 using mainly the latest modifications of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) and of the Los Alamos version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (LAQGSM) event generators CEM03.02 and LAQGSM03.03. We found that MCNP6 describes reasonably well various reactions induced by particles and nuclei at incident energies from 18 MeV to about 1 TeV per nucleon measured on thin and thick targets and agrees very well with similar results obtained with MCNPX and calculations by CEM03.02, LAQGSM03.01 (03.03), INCL4 + ABLA, and Bertini INC + Dresner evaporation, EPAX, ABRABLA, HIPSE, and AMD, used as stand alone codes. Most of several computational bugs and more serious physics problems observed in MCNP6/X during our V&V have been fixed; we continue our work to solve all the known problems before MCNP6 is distributed to the public.

## Key claim

MCNP6, the merged successor of MCNP5 and MCNPX, describes reasonably well various reactions induced by particles and nuclei at incident energies from 18 MeV to about 1 TeV per nucleon, but the paper explicitly notes that most of several computational bugs and more serious physics problems observed during validation and verification have been fixed, with work continuing to solve all known problems before public distribution.

## Method

The authors validated and verified MCNP6 by comparing its predictions, using the CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators, against experimental data for thin and thick target reactions spanning incident energies from 18 MeV to 400 GeV. They also compared MCNP6 results against calculations from stand-alone versions of CEM03.03, LAQGSM03.03, and other codes including INCL4+ABLA, Bertini INC+Dresner evaporation, EPAX, ABRABLA, HIPSE, and AMD. Specific bugs and physics problems identified during the V&V process were documented and corrected in successive code versions.

## Context

This work extends the validation of the MCNP6 code, which merges MCNP5 (validated for low-energy neutron, photon, and electron transport) with MCNPX (less extensively tested at high energies). The paper specifically tests the new CEM03.03 and LAQGSM03.03 event generators within MCNP6, comparing against experimental data and against results from stand-alone codes such as INCL4+ABLA, Bertini INC+Dresner evaporation, EPAX, ABRABLA, HIPSE, and AMD. The V&V enables future use of MCNP6 for applications including positron emission tomography (the ^18O(p,n)^18F reaction), NASA space shielding, cancer treatment with heavy ions, FRIB (Facility for Rare Isotope Beams) simulations, and studies of cumulative particle production in ultrarelativistic reactions.
