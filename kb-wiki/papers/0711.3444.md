# Dilepton Radiation at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron

> A quantitative evaluation of dilepton sources in heavy-ion reactions is performed taking into account both thermal and non-thermal production mechanisms. The hadronic thermal emission rate is based on an electromagnetic current-correlation function with a low-mass region (LMR, M \lsim 1 GeV) dominated by vector mesons (ρ, ω, ϕ) and an intermediate-mass region (IMR, 1 GeV \le M \le 3 GeV) characterized by (the onset of) a multi-meson continuum. A convolution of the emission rates over a thermal fireball expansion results in good agreement with experiment in the low-mass spectra, confirming the predicted broadening of the ρmeson in hadronic matter in connection with the prevalence of baryon-induced medium effects. The absolute magnitude of the LMR excess is mostly controlled by the fireball lifetime, which in turn leads to a consistent explanation of the dilepton excess in the IMR in terms of thermal radiation. The analysis of experimental transverse-momentum (q_T) spectra reveals discrepancies with thermal emission for q_T \gsim 1 GeV in noncentral In-In collisions, which we address by extending our calculations by: (i) a refined treatment of ρdecays at thermal freezeout, (ii) primordially produced ρ's subject to energy-loss, (iii) Drell-Yan annihilation, and (iv) thermal radiation from t-channel meson exchange processes. We investigate the sensitivity of dilepton spectra to the critical temperature and hadro-chemical freezeout of the fireball. The ρbroadening in the LMR turns out to be robust, while in the IMR Quark-Gluon Plasma radiation is moderate unless the critical temperature is rather low.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a thermal fireball model with in-medium ρ-meson broadening (driven primarily by baryon-induced effects) quantitatively describes the NA60 dilepton invariant-mass spectra in central In-In collisions, but that discrepancies in transverse-momentum spectra for q_T > 1 GeV in noncentral collisions require additional non-thermal sources (primordial ρ's with energy loss, Drell-Yan, and t-channel meson exchange) to achieve agreement.

## Method

The authors compute thermal dilepton emission rates from a hadronic electromagnetic current-correlation function, using in-medium vector-meson spectral functions (ρ, ω, φ) from hadronic many-body theory at finite temperature and baryon density, plus a multi-meson continuum for the intermediate-mass region (1–3 GeV). These rates are convolved over a thermal fireball expansion model that includes a hadro-chemical freezeout stage and a critical temperature for the transition to quark-gluon plasma. Non-thermal sources are added: ρ decays at thermal freezeout, primordial ρ mesons subject to energy loss (scaled from high-p_T pion data), Drell-Yan annihilation extrapolated to low mass, and t-channel meson exchange contributions to the thermal rate.

## Key numbers

- Fireball lifetime adjusted downward by 30% relative to earlier predictions to match the absolute yield of the NA60 low-mass excess.
- In-medium ρ width at SPS conditions averages 350–400 MeV (almost three times the vacuum width of ~150 MeV).
- In-medium ω width in the hadronic phase is ~100 MeV; in-medium φ width is ~80 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the ρ spectral function has (m_ρ, Γ_ρ) ≈ (820, 450) MeV at vanishing three-momentum.
- The ω → πγ radiative decay width computed from the model is 0.72 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.76 ± 0.03 MeV.
- The QGP contribution to the thermal dilepton yield in the IMR is ~30% (and ~10–20% in the LMR) for a standard critical temperature, but becomes moderate unless T_c is rather low.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier fireball-model calculations (van Hees and Rapp, 2006) that described NA60 invariant-mass spectra from threshold to ~1.4 GeV, by now analyzing transverse-momentum spectra and incorporating non-thermal sources at high q_T. It builds on hadronic many-body calculations of in-medium ρ spectral functions (Rapp, 1999; Urban, 1998) that had previously been tested against CERES/NA45 data and nuclear photoabsorption data. The results enable a consistent description of both mass and q_T spectra at SPS energies, and provide a baseline for future studies of dilepton emission at lower beam energies or at RHIC/LHC.
