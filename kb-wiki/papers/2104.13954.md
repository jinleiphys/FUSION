# A bound on the nucleon Druck-term from chiral EFT in curved space-time and mechanical stability conditions

> Using dispersive representations of the nucleon gravitational form factors, the results for their absorptive parts from chiral effective field theory in curved space-time, and the mechanical stability conditions, we obtain a model independent inequality for the value of the gravitational $D(t)$ form factor at zero momentum transfer (Druck-term). In particular, the obtained inequality leads to a conservative bound on the Druck-term in the chiral limit $D \leq -0.95(9)$. This bound implies the restriction on the low-energy constant $c_8$ of the effective chiral action for nucleons and pions in the presence of an external gravitational field, $c_8\leq -1.1(1)$ GeV$^{-1}$. For the physical pion mass we obtain a model independent bound $D\leq -0.20(2)$.

# A bound on the nucleon Druck-term from chiral EFT in curved space-time and mechanical stability conditions

## Key claim

Using dispersive representations of the nucleon gravitational form factors, chiral effective field theory results for their absorptive parts in curved space-time, and mechanical stability conditions, the authors derive a model-independent inequality for the gravitational D(t) form factor at zero momentum transfer (Druck-term). The bound is conservative and depends on a distance parameter R0, chosen as 2 fm for the final estimate, with the chiral expansion expected to converge for R0 around 1.5-2 fm based on model calculations in the Skyrme model.

## Method

The authors combine dispersion relations for the nucleon gravitational D(t) form factor with the imaginary part of D(t) computed to fourth order in chiral effective field theory in curved space-time, using the low-energy constants c1, c2, c3 extracted from pion-nucleon scattering data by Yao et al. (2016). The mechanical stability condition (2/3 s(r) + p(r) >= 0) is translated into a monotonicity condition on the Fourier-transformed form factor, which together with the dispersion relation yields an inequality for the Druck-term that can be evaluated at large distances where chiral EFT is applicable. The inequality is evaluated analytically in the chiral limit and numerically for physical pion masses, with the parameter R0 chosen to ensure convergence of the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

- Bound on the Druck-term in the chiral limit: D <= -0.95(9) (for R0 = 2 fm)
- Bound on the low-energy constant c8: c8 <= -1.1(1) GeV^{-1} (for R0 = 2 fm)
- Bound on the Druck-term at physical pion mass: D <= -0.20(2) (for R0 = 2 fm)
- Bound on c8 for R0 = 1.5 fm: -c8 >= 1.64(18) GeV^{-1}
- Bound on c8 for R0 = 2.5 fm: -c8 >= 0.78(6) GeV^{-1}
- Bound on c8 for R0 = 3 fm: -c8 >= 0.61(4) GeV^{-1}
- Bound on D at physical pion mass for R0 = 1.5 fm: -D >= 0.50(6)
- Bound on D at physical pion mass for R0 = 2.5 fm: -D >= 0.081(9)
- Bound on D at physical pion mass for R0 = 3 fm: -D >= 0.035(3)
- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit: m = 0.8828 +/- 0.004 GeV
- Pion decay constant: F = 0.092 GeV
- Nucleon axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.27
- Low-energy constants from Yao et al. (2016): c1 = -1.22(2)(2) GeV^{-1}, c2 = 3.58(3)(6) GeV^{-1}, c3 = -6.04(2)(9) GeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the stability bound D < 0 from Polyakov and Schweitzer (2018), Perevalova et al. (2016), and Lorce et al. (2019) by incorporating chiral EFT information on the imaginary part of the gravitational form factor from Alharazin et al. (2020). The results provide a quantitative lower bound on the absolute value of the Druck-term, complementing experimental extractions from deeply virtual Compton scattering data by Burkert et al. (2021) and lattice QCD calculations by Shanahan and Detmold (2019). The chiral expansion of the D(t) form factor's imaginary part enables future dispersive analyses of nucleon gravitational form factors using Roy-Steiner equations, analogous to the work of Hoferichter et al. (2012, 2016) for scalar and electromagnetic form factors.
