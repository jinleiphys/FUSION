# Production of phi-mesons on nuclear targets in the Quark-Gluon String model

> We consider the experimental data on phi-meson production on nuclear targets, and we find that they present unusually small shadow corrections for the inclusive density in the midrapidity region. We also give a quantitatively consistent description of both the initial energy dependence and the A-dependence of the produced phi-mesons, obtained in the frame of the Quark-Gluon String Model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that phi-meson production on nuclear targets exhibits unusually small shadow (inelastic screening) corrections for the inclusive density in the midrapidity region, and that the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) can provide a quantitatively consistent description of both the energy dependence and the A-dependence of phi-meson production without introducing additional parameters beyond those used for proton-proton collisions.

## Method

The authors apply the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), which treats high-energy hadron-nucleon, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus interactions via the exchange of one or several Pomerons, with all elastic and inelastic processes resulting from cutting through or between Pomerons. The inclusive spectrum of secondary hadrons is determined by the convolution of diquark, valence quark, and sea quark distributions in the incident particles with fragmentation functions of quarks and diquarks into the secondary hadron. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the authors use a rigid target approximation where the beam of independent nucleons of the projectile interacts with the target nucleus, and they account for percolation effects by introducing a maximal number of Pomerons, n_max, that can be emitted by one nucleon.

## Key numbers

*   For pA collisions at sqrt(s) = 41.6 GeV, the experimental cross sections d(sigma)/dy (|y| <= 0.5) are: p+C = 1.74 +/- 0.15 mb (QGSM: 1.5 mb); p+Ti = 6.85 +/- 0.7 mb (QGSM: 7.1 mb); p+W = 23.5 +/- 2.1 mb (QGSM: 19.1 mb).
*   For central nucleus-nucleus collisions, the experimental inclusive densities dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) are: Pb+Pb (0-5%) at sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV: 2.35 +/- 0.15 (QGSM: 2.764); Au+Au (0-20%) at sqrt(s)=62.4 GeV: 3.52 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.45 (QGSM: 3.36); Au+Au (0-11%) at sqrt(s)=130 GeV: 5.73 +/- 0.37 +/- 0.57 (QGSM: 6.15); Au+Au (0-5%) at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 7.95 +/- 0.11 +/- 0.73 (QGSM: 7.57).
*   For central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, the experimental dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) is 13.8 +/- 0.5 +/- 1.7 +/- 0.1 (QGSM: 13.57), with the calculation without inelastic screening giving approximately 20.5, indicating an inelastic screening effect of about 1.5.
*   The QGSM predicts dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 3.98 for minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV and 0.16 for minimum bias p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.0 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QGSM application to phi-meson production in pi p and pp collisions (Arakelyan et al., 2015) to nuclear targets, providing a consistent description of the small shadow correction effects observed experimentally. The paper contrasts phi-meson production with that of pions and kaons, where significant saturation effects from inelastic screening appear starting at RHIC energies (as discussed by Capella et al., 2008), and explains that phi-mesons are produced from cut Pomerons with relatively small transverse radii, making overlapping and fusion significant only at very high energies. This work enables predictions for phi-meson production at LHC energies and suggests that charm and beauty secondary particles, produced from Pomerons with even smaller transverse radii, should exhibit even weaker saturation effects.
