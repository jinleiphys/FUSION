# Parametric correlations in energy density functionals

> Parametric correlations are studied in several classes of covariant density functional theories (CDFTs) using a statistical analysis in a large parameter hyperspace. In the present manuscript, we investigate such correlations for two specific types of models, namely, for models with density dependent meson exchange and for point coupling models. Combined with the results obtained previously in Ref. [1] for a non-linear meson exchange model, these results indicate that parametric correlations exist in all major classes of CDFTs when the functionals are fitted to the ground state properties of finite nuclei and to nuclear matter properties. In particular, for the density dependence in the isoscalar channel only one parameter is really independent. Accounting for these facts potentially allows one to reduce the number of free parameters considerably.

## Key claim

Parametric correlations exist in all major classes of covariant density functional theories (CDFTs) when the functionals are fitted to ground state properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter properties; for the density dependence in the isoscalar channel only one parameter is truly independent, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific fitting protocols used (ground state and nuclear matter properties only) and may not hold for protocols containing other types of data.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical analysis in a large parameter hyperspace for two classes of CDFTs: density-dependent meson exchange (DDME-X) and point coupling (PC-X) models. They randomly generated parametrizations in the N-dimensional parameter hyperspace and accepted those satisfying a chi-squared condition (Delta chi-squared_max = 3.0), then examined two-dimensional projections of the parameter distributions for linear correlations. They also developed an alternative simplex-based minimization method that searches for local minima to reveal parametric correlations more efficiently.

## Key numbers

- For the DDME-X functional (8 parameters), linear correlations reduce the number of independent parameters from 8 to 6.
- For the PC-X functional (9 parameters), linear correlations reduce the number of independent parameters from 9 to 6.
- For the NL5 (non-linear meson exchange) functional studied previously, parametric correlations leave only 5 independent parameters.
- In the DDME-X functional, parameters vary from the optimal values by at most 0.5% for m_sigma, 0.6% for g_sigma, 1% for g_omega, 2.5% for g_rho, 10% for a_rho, and 30% for c_sigma, b_sigma, and c_omega.
- Linear correlation equations for DDME-X: f(b_sigma) = 1.1396 f(c_sigma) - 0.14191; f(c_omega) = 1.083 f(c_sigma) - 0.08655.
- Linear correlation equations for PC-X: f(alpha_v) = 1.4203 f(alpha_s) - 0.42178; f(delta_v) = 0.08221 f(delta_s) + 0.96062; f(gamma_v) = -5.5582 f(gamma_s) + 6.6311.

## Context

This work extends the parametric correlation study of Taninah et al. (2019) on non-linear meson exchange (NLME) functionals to the two other major classes of CDFTs: density-dependent meson exchange and point coupling models. The Zagreb group (Niksic and Vretenar, 2016; Niksic, Imbrišak, and Vretenar, 2017) previously attempted parameter reduction in point-coupling models using the manifold boundary approximation method, but that approach was limited to the vicinity of the global minimum. The present results suggest that many current CDFTs are over-parametrized, with only 5-6 independent parameters definable from ground state and nuclear matter data, which could reduce statistical uncertainties and increase predictive power. The findings also connect to efforts for microscopic derivation of energy density functionals from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory.
