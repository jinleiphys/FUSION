# Antiferromagnetic spin phase transition in nuclear matter with effective Gogny interaction

> The possibility of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic phase transitions in symmetric nuclear matter is analyzed within the framework of a Fermi liquid theory with the effective Gogny interaction. It is shown that at some critical density nuclear matter with D1S effective force undergoes a phase transition to the antiferromagnetic spin state (the opposite direction of neutron and proton spins). The self--consistent equations of spin polarized nuclear matter with D1S force have no solutions, corresponding to the ferromagnetic spin ordering (the same direction of neutron and proton spins) and, hence, the ferromagnetic transition does not appear. The dependence of antiferromagnetic spin polarization parameter as a function of density is found at zero temperature.

## Key claim

Symmetric nuclear matter with the D1S Gogny effective force undergoes a phase transition to an antiferromagnetic spin state (opposite direction of neutron and proton spins) at a critical density, while no ferromagnetic spin ordering (same direction of neutron and proton spins) appears; this result contrasts with calculations using Skyrme effective forces, which predict ferromagnetic transitions in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employed a Fermi liquid theory framework with the effective Gogny interaction (D1S parametrization) to study spin-polarized states in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature. They derived and solved self-consistent equations for the ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin order parameters directly, rather than calculating magnetic susceptibility, and determined the density dependence of the order parameters and the thermodynamic stability of the various phases.

## Key numbers

- The antiferromagnetic spin order parameter arises at density approximately 3.8 times the nuclear matter saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}) for the D1S potential.
- A totally antiferromagnetically polarized state (Delta rho_{uparrowdownarrow} / rho = 1) is formed at approximately 4.3 rho_0.
- The neutron and proton spin polarization parameters for the antiferromagnetic state satisfy Pi_n = -Pi_p = Delta rho_{uparrowdownarrow} / rho.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in nuclear matter using Skyrme effective forces (e.g., SkM*, SGII, SLy4, SLy5) and contrasts with microscopic calculations using realistic NN potentials (e.g., Nijmegen II, Reid93, Argonne v18) that find no evidence of spin instability. The results enable further investigation of spin-related phenomena in neutron star interiors and highlight the need for constraints on spin-dependent interaction amplitudes at high densities, potentially from neutron star magnetic field decay data.
