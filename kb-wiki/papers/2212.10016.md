# The isentropic equation of state of (2+1)-flavor QCD: An update based on high precision Taylor expansion and Padé-resummed expansion at finite chemical potentials

> The HotQCD Collaboration performed Taylor expansion calculations in 2017 for the pressure, energy density, and entropy density at non-zero chemical potentials up to the $6^{th}$ order. Since then, they have significantly improved the statistics for lattices with temporal extents of $N_τ=8$ and $12$, and have also included results for $N_τ=16$ that were not previously available. They have also calculated the $8^{th}$-order expansion coefficients for $N_τ=8$. These calculations showed that the Taylor series expansion for the pressure is accurate up to $μ_B / T \leq 2.5$. In this study, we use the high-statistics results on Taylor expansion coefficients, calculated with HISQ fermions and extrapolated to the continuum limit, to determine the QCD equation of state under conditions relevant for hot and dense matter produced in heavy ion collisions. We also calculate the energy density and pressure along lines of constant entropy per net baryon number.

## Key claim
The Taylor series expansion for the QCD equation of state is accurate up to mu_B/T less than or equal to 2.5, with Padé-resummed expansions extending reliability to mu_B/T between 2 and 2.5 at low temperatures and to mu_B/T greater than 3 at temperatures above 200 MeV, though "wiggles" appear in energy and entropy densities at the highest chemical potentials.

## Method
The HotQCD collaboration performed high-statistics lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action on N_tau = 8, 12, and 16 lattices, including approximately 1.5 million configurations on 32^3 x 8 lattices at the pseudo-critical temperature of 156.5 MeV. They computed Taylor expansion coefficients for pressure, energy density, and entropy density up to eighth order in baryon chemical potential for a strangeness-neutral medium with electric charge to baryon-number density ratio n_Q/n_B = 0.4. They also constructed [2,4] Padé approximants for energy and entropy densities and determined lines of constant entropy per net baryon number (s/n_B) by solving for mu_B/T as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers
- Taylor series expansion for pressure is accurate up to mu_B/T less than or equal to 2.5
- Approximately 1.5 million configurations on 32^3 x 8 lattices at the pseudo-critical temperature of 156.5(1.5) MeV
- For s/n_B = 400 at T_pc = 155 MeV: pressure = 55(7) MeV/fm^3, energy density = 355(42) MeV/fm^3, entropy density = 512(60) MeV/fm^2
- For s/n_B = 50 at T_pc = 150 MeV: pressure = 47(6) MeV/fm^3, energy density = 301(35) MeV/fm^3, entropy density = 446(52) MeV/fm^2
- For s/n_B = 30 at T_pc = 145 MeV: pressure = 48(9) MeV/fm^3, energy density = 336(83) MeV/fm^3, entropy density = 525(107) MeV/fm^2
- The EoS for fixed s/n_B is applicable to beam energies 7.7 GeV less than or equal to sqrt(s_NN) less than or equal to 200 GeV (BES-II at RHIC collider mode)
- Deviation from ideal gas curves is approximately 20% for all s/n_B values at the highest temperature

## Context
This work updates the HotQCD Collaboration's 2017 Taylor expansion calculations (Bazavov 2017) with significantly improved statistics for N_tau = 8 and 12 lattices, new N_tau = 16 results, and eighth-order expansion coefficients for N_tau = 8. It extends the earlier continuum-extrapolated equation of state at zero chemical potential (HotQCD 2014) to finite baryon chemical potential using Padé resummation techniques recently proposed by Bollweg (2022). The results provide the QCD equation of state along isentropic trajectories relevant for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at RHIC BES-II energies and for neutron star merger simulations at low temperature and high chemical potentials.
