# Study of $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn Fusion with Density Constrained TDHF Formalism

> We study fusion reactions of the $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn system using the recently introduced density constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock formalism. In this formalism the fusion barriers are directly obtained from TDHF dynamics. In addition, we incorporate the entrance channel alignment of the deformed (oblate) $^{64}$Ni nucleus due to dynamical Coulomb excitation. We discuss the influence of particle transfer and other dynamical effects on the fusion cross sections. Calculated cross sections are in very good agreement with data and other calculations.

## Key claim

The density constrained TDHF formalism, which contains no adjustable parameters, produces fusion cross sections for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn system that agree very well with measured data and with state-of-the-art coupled-channel calculations, except for the lowest energy data point at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=142.6$ MeV where all existing theories underestimate the cross section by a few orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn system using the Skyrme SLy5 effective interaction, then extracted the ion-ion interaction potential by applying a density constraint to the instantaneous TDHF density at each separation distance. They incorporated the entrance channel alignment of the deformed oblate $^{64}$Ni nucleus by performing a semiclassical Coulomb excitation calculation to determine the orientation probability distribution, then averaging fusion cross sections over orientation angles. Fusion cross sections were calculated using the incoming wave boundary condition method with the resulting dynamical barriers.

## Key numbers

- The barrier height for $\beta=0^{\circ}$ orientation (symmetry axis aligned with collision axis) is 155.8 MeV, with the barrier peak located at approximately $R_B=12.1$ fm
- The lowest barrier, for $\beta=90^{\circ}$ orientation, has a height of 150.1 MeV and peaks around $R_B=13$ fm
- Experimental fusion cross sections were measured in the energy range 142 MeV $\le E_{\mathrm{c.m.}} \le$ 195 MeV
- Fission is negligible for $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}\le$ 160 MeV, so evaporation residue cross sections were taken as fusion cross sections
- At a separation of 12 fm (approximately the top of the potential barrier), about two neutrons are transferred; at 11 fm, as many as six neutrons are transferred
- The $^{64}$Ni nucleus has $E_{2+}=1.346$ MeV, $E_{4+}=2.610$ MeV, and $M(E2, 0+ \rightarrow 2+) = -27.0\ e$ fm$^2$ (oblate deformation)
- The Fourier transform of isovector dipole oscillations shows a 9 MeV peak; isoscalar quadrupole peaks appear at 4 MeV and 7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the density constrained TDHF method introduced by Umar and Oberacker (2006) to provide a complete calculation of fusion cross sections for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{132}$Sn system, where a large sub-barrier fusion enhancement was observed by Liang et al. (2003, 2005). The results are compared with coupled-channel calculations by Liang et al. (2007) that included inelastic excitations and two-neutron transfer. The paper demonstrates that TDHF dynamics naturally incorporate neck formation, particle exchange, internal excitations, and deformation effects that are treated phenomenologically in coupled-channel approaches, and it enables future microscopic studies of fusion involving neutron-rich and deformed nuclei where the definition of sub-barrier fusion becomes ambiguous.
