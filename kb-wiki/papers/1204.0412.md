# Spectrum of the excited $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ baryons in a relativistic chiral quark model

> The spectrum of the SU(2) flavor baryons is studied in the frame of a relativistic chiral quark potential model based on the one-pion and one-gluon exchange mechanisms. It is argued that the N* and Delta* resonances strongly coupled to the pi-N channel are identified with the orbital configurations $(1S_{1/2})^2(nlj)$ with a single valence quark in the excited state (nlj). With the obtained selection rules based on the "chiral constraint", we show that it is possible to construct a schematic periodic table of baryon resonances, consistent with the experimental data and yielding no "missing resonances". A new original method for the treatment of the center of mass problem is suggested, which is based on the separation of the three-quark Dirac Hamiltonian into the parts, corresponding to the Jacobi coordinates. The numerical estimations for the energy positions of the Nucleon and Delta baryons (up to and including F-wave resonances), obtained within the field-theoretical framework by using time ordered perturbation theory, yield an overall good description of the experimental data at the level of the relativized CQM of S. Capstick and W. Roberts without any fitting parameters. The Delta(1232) is well reproduced. However, N g. s. and most of the radially excited baryon resonances (including Roper) are overestimated. Contrary, the first band of the orbitally excited baryon resonances with a negative parity are underestimated. At the same time, the second band of the orbitally excited Delta* states with the negative parity are mostly overestimated, while the N* states are close to the experimental boxes. The positive parity baryon resonances with J=5/2, 7/2 are close to the experimental data. At higher energies, where the experimental data are poor, we can extend our model schematically and predict an existence of seven N* and four Delta* new states with larger spin values.

## Key claim

The paper argues that all excited nucleon (N*) and delta (Δ*) baryon resonances that appear in πN scattering data can be identified with the orbital configuration (1S₁/₂)²(nlj) containing a single excited valence quark, and that this identification yields no "missing resonances" when combined with selection rules from the chiral constraint. However, the model overestimates the nucleon ground state and most radially excited resonances (including the Roper), while underestimating the first band of orbitally excited negative-parity resonances.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation with a Cornell-type potential (linear scalar confinement plus Coulomb-like vector term) for single quark states, then construct three-quark baryon states with the configuration (1S₁/₂)²(nlj). Second-order perturbative corrections from one-pion and one-gluon exchange are calculated using time-ordered perturbation theory within a field-theoretical framework. A new method for center-of-mass correction is developed by separating the three-quark Dirac Hamiltonian into parts corresponding to Jacobi coordinates, using effective potentials for the scalar diquark and the single excited quark.

## Key numbers

- The only free parameter of the model (the mass term m in the confinement potential) was previously fitted by Th. Gutsche to reproduce the axial charge of the nucleon; all other parameters are fixed from lattice QCD studies (Kawa11, Green03)
- The pion decay constant is fπ = 93 MeV
- The Coulomb-like vector potential strength is α = π/12, defined from lattice QCD (Green03)
- The linear confinement strength parameter c is defined from lattice study (Kawa11)
- The ground state Δ(1232) is well reproduced by the model
- The model predicts seven new N* states and four new Δ* states with larger spin values at higher energies where experimental data are poor

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral quark model previously developed by Tursunov (2005, 2009, 2010) for lower excitation spectra of nucleon and delta, and tested earlier by Oset (1984) for nucleon charge form factors and by Gutsche (1987, 1989) for nucleon properties. The model is compared to the relativized constituent quark model of Capstick and Roberts (1986, 2000) and achieves comparable description without fitting parameters. The approach contrasts with other constituent quark model variants based on Goldstone-boson exchange (Glozman 1998), one-gluon exchange (Capstick 1986, 2000), or instanton-induced exchange (Metlitski 2001). The results can be reproduced in any chiral quark model describing baryons as three valence quarks with Dirac two-component structure surrounded by a pion cloud, as required by chiral symmetry (Gell-Mann 1960).
