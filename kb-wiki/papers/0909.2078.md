# The sum rule for the structure functions of the deuteron from the current algebra on the null-plane

> The fixed-mass sum rules for the deuteron target have been derived by using the connected matrix element of the current anti-commutation relation on the null-plane. From these sum rules we obtain the relation between the pseudo-scalar meson deuteron total cross sections and the structure functions of the deuteron. We show that the nuclear effect on the mean hypercharge of the sea quark of the proton can be studied by this relation. Further we obtain the relation among the Born term, the resonances and the non-resonant background in the small $Q^2$ region, and as a new aspect of the spin 1 target, explicit relation of the tensor structure function $b_2$ at small or moderate $Q^2$ to that at large $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The paper derives fixed-mass sum rules for the deuteron target from the current anti-commutation relation on the null-plane, showing that these sum rules can probe the nuclear effect on the mean hypercharge of the sea quark of the proton in the deuteron, and that they relate the tensor structure function \(b_2\) at small or moderate \(Q^2\) to that at large \(Q^2\), provided the regularization of divergent integrals is handled via the method of subtracting the Pomeron pole singularity.

## Method

The author uses the Deser-Gilbert-Sudarshan (DGS) representation to abstract the current anti-commutation relation on the null-plane for the deuteron target, then integrates the good-good component (\(J^+ J^+\)) of the hadronic tensor over \(q^-\) to obtain sum rules. The sum rules are regularized by first deriving them in the non-forward direction, subtracting the Pomeron pole singularity, and then continuing to the forward direction at \(t=0\). The resulting relations connect the deuteron structure functions to pseudo-scalar meson–deuteron total cross sections via PCAC, and to the elastic form factors of the deuteron.

## Key numbers

* The soft Pomeron intercept is taken as \(\alpha_P(0) = 1 + b\) with \(b = 0.0808\).
* The HERMES experiment reports \(\int_{0.002}^{0.85} \frac{dx}{x} b_2^{ed}(x, Q^2 = 5\ \text{GeV}^2) > 0\).
* The cutoff invariant mass \(W_c\) defines \(x_c(Q^2) = Q^2 / 2\nu_c(Q^2)\) with \(\nu_c(Q^2) = (W_c^2 - m_d^2 + Q^2)/2\).
* The pion and kaon decay constants \(f_\pi\) and \(f_K\) appear in the sum rules, with thresholds \(\nu_0^\pi = m_\pi m_d\) and \(\nu_0^K = m_K m_d\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous application of null-plane current anti-commutation sum rules to nucleon targets (Koretune, 1993, 1995, 1997, 1998) by applying the same formalism to the deuteron as a spin-1 nuclear target. It builds on the DGS representation method (Deser, Gilbert, and Sudarshan, 1959) and the earlier null-plane current algebra (Koretune, 1980). The derived sum rules enable the study of nuclear effects on sea quark distributions, particularly the mean hypercharge of the sea quark of the proton in the deuteron, and provide a relation between the tensor structure function \(b_2\) at low and high \(Q^2\) that can be tested against HERMES data.
