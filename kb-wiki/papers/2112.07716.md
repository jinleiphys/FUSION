# Dark matter component in hadronic models with short-range correlations

> A relativistic mean field hadronic model with a dark matter (DM) particle coupled to nucleons including short-range correlations (SRC) is applied to study neutron stars (NS). The lightest neutralino is chosen as the dark particle candidate, which interacts with nucleons by the exchange of Higgs bosons. A detailed thermodynamical analysis shows that the contribution of the DM fermions to the energy density of the matter composed by these particles and nucleons is completely dominated by the DM kinetic terms. The model reproduces satisfactorily the constraints on the mass-radius diagram obtained from the analysis of the combined data from the NICER mission, LIGO and Virgo collaborations, and mass measurements from radio observations. We show that the SRC balance the reduction of the neutron star mass due to the DM component, and because of that the model is able to present more massive NS. We also present a study of the effect, in the NS mass-radius profiles, of the uncertainties in some bulk parameters related to the hadronic sector. We find that it is possible to generate parametrizations, with DM content, compatible with the recent astrophysical constraints and with the uncertainty in the symmetry energy slope obtained from the results reported by the updated Lead Radius EXperiment (PREX-2).

## Key claim

The paper shows that including short-range correlations (SRC) in a relativistic mean-field hadronic model coupled to dark matter can balance the mass reduction that dark matter alone would cause in neutron stars, allowing the model to produce neutron star masses compatible with recent observational constraints. The dark matter contribution to the energy density is completely dominated by the kinetic terms of the dark fermions, making the specific values of the Higgs-dark matter and Higgs-nucleon couplings essentially irrelevant to the thermodynamics.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic model with the lightest neutralino as the dark matter candidate, coupled to nucleons via Higgs boson exchange, and incorporated short-range correlations by replacing the Fermi gas momentum distribution with a high-momentum tail (HMT) distribution proportional to \(1/k^4\). They solved the field equations in the mean-field approximation, computed the equation of state for charge-neutral \(\beta\)-equilibrium stellar matter including electrons and muons, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius profiles. The hadronic sector used the FSU2R parametrization, with coupling constants refitted to maintain identical bulk properties when SRC were included.

## Key numbers

* Maximum neutron star mass of the FSU2R model (without SRC or DM): \(2.05 M_\odot\) with radius \(11.6\) km.
* Dark matter particle (lightest neutralino) mass: \(M_\chi = 200\) GeV.
* Higgs boson mass: \(m_h = 125\) GeV.
* Higgs vacuum expectation value: \(v = 246\) GeV.
* Higgs-nucleon coupling parameter: \(f = 0.3\) (central value from Cline (2013)).
* Dark matter coupling parameter: \(y = 0.01\), producing a spin-independent scattering cross-section around \(10^{-47}\) cm\(^2\).
* Bulk parameters of the reference FSU2R model: saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(B_0 = -16.27\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_0 = 237.7\) MeV, effective nucleon mass ratio \(m^* = 0.593\), symmetry energy \(J = 30.7\) MeV, symmetry energy slope \(L_0 = 46.9\) MeV.
* Range for symmetry energy slope from PREX-2: \(L_0 = (106 \pm 37)\) MeV.
* Range for incompressibility variation: \(220\) MeV \(\leq K_0 \leq 260\) MeV.
* Neutron star mass constraint from Cromartie (2019) for PSR J0740+6620: \(M = 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\) at 68.3% credible level.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars using RMF models (e.g., RMFDM2, RMFDM3, RMFDM4, RMFDM8, RMFDM9) by incorporating short-range correlations, which were previously shown to stiffen the equation of state in hadronic models by Cai and Lucas. The SRC phenomenology is motivated by electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab (Subedi 2008) that reveal correlated nucleon pairs with high relative momentum. The results enable simultaneous compatibility with NICER mission data for PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, gravitational wave constraints from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817, GW190425), and the PREX-2 uncertainty range for the symmetry energy slope, showing that dark matter and SRC can coexist in neutron star models without violating observational bounds.
