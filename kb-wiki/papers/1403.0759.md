# Efimov states in Li-Cs mixtures within a minimal model

> We use a minimal zero-range model for describing the bound state spectrum of three-body states consisting of two Cesium and one Lithium atom. Using a broad Feshbach resonance model for the two-body interactions, we show that recent experimental data can be described surprisingly well for particular values of the three-body parameter that governs the short-range behavior of the atomic potentials and is outside the scope of the zero-range model. Studying the spectrum as a function of the three-body parameter suggests that the lowest state seen in experiment could be influenced by finite range corrections. We also consider the question of Fermi degeneracy and corresponding Pauli blocking of the Lithium atoms on the Efimov states.

# Efimov states in Li-Cs mixtures within a minimal model

## Key claim
A minimal zero-range model with a constant three-body parameter can reproduce experimental Efimov resonance positions in Li-Cs mixtures surprisingly well, but only if the three-body parameter is fixed to one experimental data point; a single constant value cannot simultaneously match all three observed resonances, with the variation in the required three-body parameter being about 25%.

## Method
The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using a zero-range momentum-space method regularized by the Danilov method, generalized to non-identical masses. Two-body interactions are modeled using a simple Feshbach resonance parameterization for the scattering length as a function of magnetic field, with fixed parameters for the Cs-Cs subsystem and two parameter sets for the Li-Cs subsystems taken from experimental papers. The three-body parameter κ is treated as a constant input, and the magnetic field positions where Efimov trimers cross the three-atom continuum are computed as a function of κ.

## Key numbers
- The three-body parameter values required to match each experimental resonance lie in the range κ = 0.0095 a₀⁻¹ to κ = 0.013 a₀⁻¹, a variation of about 25%.
- The Efimov scaling factor for the Li-Cs system is approximately 4.8 (e^{π/s₀} ∼ 4.8).
- For the second excited Efimov state, the product κ²a₋² = -29.17 (in units where m_r = m_Li).
- Using the experimental value a₋⁰ ∼ -265.14 a₀, the product κ⁰a₋⁰ = -25.69, about 12% lower than for the second excited state.
- The estimated finite-range correction needed to explain the ground state deviation is r_e ∼ 22-66 a₀; the van der Waals length of Li-Cs is r_vdW = 45 a₀.
- The three-body parameter κ ∼ 0.01 a₀⁻¹ translates to a length L = κ⁻¹ = 100 a₀, nearly equal to the Cs-Cs van der Waals length of 101 a₀.
- For Li densities from 10⁶ cm⁻³ to 10¹² cm⁻³, virtually no changes in the three-body thresholds for Efimov trimer formation are found.

## Context
This work extends the study of Efimov states in highly mass-imbalanced systems, specifically the Li-Cs mixtures recently investigated experimentally by the Chicago group (Tung 2014) and the Heidelberg group (Pires 2014). The authors compare their minimal zero-range model to these experimental data and find good agreement for the two states closest to the Feshbach resonance, while the deepest bound state shows deviations attributed to finite-range corrections, consistent with earlier work on equal-mass systems (Sorensen 2013). The results suggest that the three-body parameter may be related to the van der Waals length, as proposed in recent theoretical works (Naidon 2011, Chin 2011, Wang 2012). The study also considers the effect of Fermi degeneracy in the Lithium component via Pauli blocking, finding negligible influence on Efimov thresholds for experimentally relevant densities, which contrasts with expectations from systems with smaller mass imbalance.
