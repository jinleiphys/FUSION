# Quick Guides for Use of the CompOSE Data Base

> We present a combination of two quick guides aimed at summarizing relevant information about the CompOSE nuclear equation of state repository. The first is aimed at nuclear physicists and describes how to provide standard equation of state tables. The second quick guide is meant for users and describes the basic procedures to obtain customized tables with equation of state data. Several examples are included to help providers and users to understand and benefit from the CompOSE database.

# Quick Guides for Use of the CompOSE Data Base

## Key claim
This paper provides two practical quick guides for the CompOSE nuclear equation of state repository: one aimed at nuclear physicists who wish to contribute standard equation of state tables, and another for end-users who need to obtain customized tables with equation of state data. The guides are intended to simplify the process of both providing and retrieving data, but they do not introduce new physical results or models.

## Method
The first quick guide describes the required format and metadata for submitting equation of state tables to the CompOSE repository, including the necessary thermodynamic quantities and particle compositions. The second guide explains the web-based interface and command-line tools that users can employ to select specific physical conditions (e.g., temperature, baryon density, proton fraction) and download tailored equation of state data. Several worked examples are included for both providers and users to illustrate the submission and retrieval procedures.

## Context
This work extends the documentation for the CompOSE database, which was previously described in earlier CompOSE collaboration papers (Typel et al., 2015; Oertel et al., 2017). The guides enable the broader nuclear-physics and astrophysics community to more easily contribute and access standardized equation of state tables, facilitating comparisons between different theoretical models and their application in simulations of neutron stars, supernovae, and neutron-star mergers. Future work could involve expanding the repository to include additional microphysical quantities or extending the interface to support more complex queries.
