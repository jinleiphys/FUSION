# Two-Body Bound States in Light-Front Dynamics

> We present the main features of the explicitly covariant Light-Front Dynamics formalism and a summary of our recent works on this topic. They concern the bound states of two scalar particles in the Wick-Cutkosky model and of two fermions interacting via the usual OBEP ladder kernels.

## Key claim

The paper presents the explicitly covariant Light-Front Dynamics (ECLFD) formalism and its application to two-body bound states, claiming that relativistic effects in binding energies can be sizeable (up to 100% at coupling alpha=0.3) even when the average momentum squared is only about 2% of the squared mass, and that for massive exchange there is no nonrelativistic limit even at zero binding energy.

## Method

The authors solved the ECLFD integral equation for two scalar particles in the Wick-Cutkosky model (scalar exchange) and for two fermions interacting via one-boson-exchange-potential (OBEP) kernels (scalar, pseudoscalar, pseudovector, vector couplings), both in the ladder approximation with Fock space truncated to two- and three-body components. For nonzero angular momentum states, they constructed eigenstates of a kinematical angular momentum operator and imposed the angular condition to restore rotational invariance. The scalar calculations were performed in the center-of-mass frame with omega=(1,hat n) and compared to nonrelativistic Schrodinger, Klein-Gordon, and Bethe-Salpeter results.

## Key numbers

- At coupling alpha=0.3 in the Wick-Cutkosky model (mu=0), relativistic effects account for a 100% effect in the binding energy, while <k^2>/m^2 is approximately 2%.
- For the scalar deuteron model (MT potential), the LFD binding energy shifts from B=2.23 MeV to B=0.96 MeV when using the same nonrelativistic parameters; adjusting lambda_A from 3.18 to 3.37 (or lambda_R from 7.29 to 6.60) recovers the physical B, a 10% change in coupling constant.
- For mu=0 at alpha=0.5, LFD binding energy is B=0.0267 (in units where hbar=c=m=1) compared to nonrelativistic B=0.0625; after rescaling the NR coupling to match B, squared wavefunctions differ by about 5% at k=0 and a factor of 3 at k=2.
- For mu=0 at alpha=5 (highly relativistic case, B=0.84), squared wavefunctions at k=0 differ by approximately 70% between LFD and nonrelativistic results.
- Relative differences in coupling constant between LFD and Bethe-Salpeter for fixed binding energy are limited to 10% for B=m (alpha >= 5) and decrease with increasing exchange mass mu.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ECLFD calculations of deuteron structure functions and polarization observables (Carbonell and Karmanov, 1995-1999) to a systematic study of bound-state solutions in the ladder approximation. It compares with the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Wick and Cutkosky, 1954) and finds close agreement despite differences in the ladder kernels, as also noted by Bakker. The results enable future application to "genuinely relativistic systems" (binding energy comparable to mass) and to systems probed in relativistic regimes (e.g., deuteron at high Q^2), and the scalar deuteron model illustrates the difficulty of determining coupling constants in strong-interaction physics even for systems considered nonrelativistic.
