# Theory of Heavy Flavor in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Heavy-quark interactions in the Quark-Gluon Plasma are analyzed in terms of a selfconsistent Brueckner scheme using a thermodynamic $T$-matrix based on a potential model. The interrelations between quarkonium correlators, spectral functions and zero-modes, and open heavy-flavor transport and susceptibilities are elaborated. Independent constraints from thermal lattice QCD can be used to improve predictions for heavy-quark phenomenology in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
Heavy-quark interactions in the Quark-Gluon Plasma can be described by a selfconsistent Brueckner scheme using a thermodynamic T-matrix based on a potential model, but the approach is limited by ambiguities in whether the free energy or internal energy from lattice QCD should be used as the input potential, with the two representing limiting cases that bracket the uncertainty.

## Method
The author develops a thermodynamic T-matrix approach in ladder approximation for both heavy quarkonium (Q-Qbar) and heavy-light (Q-q) interactions, using a potential constructed from a field-theoretic ansatz with Coulomb and confining propagators fitted to lattice QCD free energy data. The selfconsistent Brueckner scheme iterates between the heavy-light T-matrix, the heavy-quark selfenergy, and the heavy-quark propagator, which then enters the two-particle propagator for the T-matrix. Heavy-quark transport coefficients are computed from the T-matrix via a Fokker-Planck equation, and the results are compared to thermal lattice QCD data on euclidean correlators, susceptibilities, and free/internal energies.

## Key numbers
- The string tension in vacuum is sigma ≈ 1 GeV/fm.
- The ground-state binding energy of J/psi in vacuum is E_B ≈ 0.6 GeV, collapsing to ~0.05 GeV if the string term is switched off.
- At 2T_c ≈ 350 MeV, the heavy-quark free energy still levels off at a positive value indicative of nonperturbative effects.
- The T-matrix calculation using the internal energy potential yields charm-quark widths of Γ_Q ≈ 0.1-0.2 GeV for on-shell charm quarks.
- The ground-state (eta_c, J/psi) melting temperature is found to be around 1.5 T_c when width effects are included, compared to ~2 T_c without width effects.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient D_s for charm quarks from the U-potential is a factor of 3-5 smaller than perturbative QCD.
- The in-medium heavy-quark mass correction Δm_Q from the free energy is about 0.3 GeV smaller than from the internal energy.

## Context
This work extends the potential-based T-matrix approach previously applied to electromagnetic plasmas (Redmer, 1997) and to heavy quarks in QCD (Mannarelli, 2005; Cabrera, 2006; Riek, 2010), and aims to unify descriptions of quarkonium and open heavy-flavor transport. The approach enables direct comparison with thermal lattice QCD computations (Aarts, 2007; Petreczky, 2009) and can be implemented into phenomenological models of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, such as Langevin simulations and rate equations. Open questions include proper potential definition, the role of correlations near T_c, and nonperturbative Q-gluon interactions.
