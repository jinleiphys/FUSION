# Light-Front Hamiltonian Approach to the Bound-State Problem in Quantum Electrodynamics

> This dissertation presents the first theoretical investigation of the Lamb shift in a light-front hamiltonian approach: the dominant part of the splitting between the 2S(1/2) and 2P(1/2) energy levels in hydrogen is calculated. Also presented for the first time is an analytic calculation in a light-front hamiltonian approach of the singlet-triplet spin splitting in the ground state of positronium through fourth order in the fine-structure constant.

## Key claim

This dissertation presents the first theoretical investigation of the Lamb shift in a light-front Hamiltonian approach, calculating the dominant part of the splitting between the 2S(1/2) and 2P(1/2) energy levels in hydrogen, and also presents the first analytic calculation in a light-front Hamiltonian approach of the singlet-triplet spin splitting in the ground state of positronium through fourth order in the fine-structure constant. The results are derived under specific conditions: a nonrelativistic limit where typical electron momentum is much less than the electron mass, and a final cutoff satisfying m alpha^2 much less than the cutoff much less than m alpha.

## Method

The author starts from a canonical QED Hamiltonian on the light front and sets up a general formalism for deriving an effective Hamiltonian to a prescribed order in the fine-structure constant alpha, renormalized by requiring it to satisfy coupling coherence. Bound-state perturbation theory (BSPT) is then used to compute the low-lying spectrum in a consistent set of approximations to a prescribed order in alpha and alpha ln(1/alpha). The general formulas are applied explicitly to the positronium and hydrogen systems, with renormalization carried out through order e^2 and a nonrelativistic limit taken.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order binding energy for positronium is given by B_N = - (1/4) (m alpha^2 / n^2), where n is the principal quantum number.
* The difference in the b_4 coefficient between triplet and singlet states of positronium ground state is b_4(triplet) - b_4(singlet) = 7/3.
* The resulting singlet-triplet spin splitting in the ground state of positronium is B_triplet - B_singlet = (7/6) alpha^2 Ryd + O(alpha^5), where Ryd is the Rydberg energy (13.60 eV).
* When physical values of the fine-structure constant (1/137.0) and Rydberg energy (13.60 eV) are applied, the result agrees with experiment to one-half of a percent.

## Context

This work extends the light-front Hamiltonian approach to bound-state problems in QED, building on the foundational work of Dirac (1949) on light-front dynamics and the renormalization group methods of Wilson and collaborators (1994). The calculation of the positronium singlet-triplet splitting through order alpha^4 reproduces the well-known result first obtained by Pirenne (1947) and Berestetski and Landau (1949), while the Lamb shift calculation for hydrogen follows the original approach of Bethe (1947). This work enables future applications of light-front Hamiltonian methods to more complex bound-state problems in quantum electrodynamics and potentially quantum chromodynamics.
