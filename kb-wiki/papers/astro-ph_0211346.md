# Hadronization of the Quark Universe

> We explore quantitatively the conditions in which matter (protons, neutrons) formed in the Early Universe during a period which spanned 10--50mus. We obtain all chemical potentials implied by the present day baryon-to-photon ratio. The baryochemical potential reaches its minimal value at the beginning of hadronization of the quark Universe, where mu_b=1.1 \pm 0.25 eV. In the mixed hadron-quark phase, a significant hadron sector electric charge distillation is found.

## Key claim

The baryochemical potential at the beginning of hadronization of the quark Universe reaches a minimal value of μ_b = 1.1 ± 0.25 eV, with the dominant uncertainty arising from the 6% uncertainty in the hadronization temperature (T_h = 170 ± 10 MeV) rather than from the WMAP baryon-to-photon ratio uncertainty.

## Method

The authors solved three coupled, non-linear equations for the independent chemical potentials (μ_d, μ_e, μ_ν) using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, imposing charge neutrality, net lepton number equal to net baryon number, and constant entropy-per-baryon (S/B = 1.3 ± 0.1 × 10^10). They employed partition functions for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic gas (HG) phases, using QGP equations of state with a bag constant B = 258 MeV fm^{-3} and summing all hadrons with mass below 2 GeV from the Particle Data Group (1998) with finite volume corrections. During the mixed phase, they parameterized the total partition function as a linear combination of the two phases using a fraction f_HG representing the volume fraction occupied by the HG phase.

## Key numbers

- Baryochemical potential at beginning of hadronization: μ_b = 1.1 ± 0.25 eV
- Hadronization temperature: T_h = 170 ± 10 MeV
- Present day baryon-to-photon ratio from WMAP: η = 6.1^{+0.3}_{-0.2} × 10^{-10}
- Entropy per baryon: S/B = 1.3 ± 0.1 × 10^{10}
- Ratio of baryon number density in QGP to HG during phase transformation: n_B^{QGP} / n_B^{HG} ≈ 3
- Energy fraction in baryons and antibaryons dropped from ~10% at 40 μs to ~10^{-7} at one second
- Phase transformation duration estimate: τ_h = 10 μs
- Nucleon freeze-out temperature: T ≈ 35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the standard big-bang model of baryon-antibaryon annihilation described by Kolb (1990) by applying equations of state developed from high-energy nuclear collision studies by Letessier (2002). The authors use the WMAP result from Spergel (2003) for the baryon-to-photon ratio. The charge distillation effect found during the QGP-HG phase transformation extends prior work on strangeness separation and strangelet formation by Greiner (1987, 1991), and the authors note this could affect baryon-antibaryon asymmetry as discussed by Witten (1984) and generate primordial magnetic fields as explored by Cheng (1994). The results enable detailed study of hadron and lepton abundances during the epoch from hadronization temperature down to nucleosynthesis temperature.
