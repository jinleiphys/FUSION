# Momentum distribution and correlation of two-nucleon relative motion in $^6$He and $^6$Li

> The momentum distribution of relative motion between two nucleons gives information on the correlation in nuclei. The momentum distribution is calculated for both $^{6}$He and $^6$Li which are described in a three-body model of $α$+$N$+$N$. The ground state solution for the three-body Hamiltonian is obtained accurately using correlated basis functions. The momentum distribution depends on the potential model for the $N$-$N$ interaction. With use of a realistic potential, the $^6$He momentum distribution exhibits a dip around 2 fm$^{-1}$ characteristic of $S$-wave motion. In contrast to this, the $^6$Li momentum distribution is very similar to that of the deuteron; no dip appears because it is filled with the $D$-wave component arising from the tensor force.

## Key claim

The momentum distribution of the two-nucleon relative motion in $^6$He and $^6$Li, calculated using a realistic $N$-$N$ potential (G3RS), reveals a dip around 2 fm$^{-1}$ for $^6$He that is characteristic of $S$-wave motion, whereas $^6$Li shows no such dip because the $D$-wave component arising from the tensor force fills in that region; this difference is not captured by effective potentials like the Minnesota potential, which lack a tensor component.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body $\alpha$+$N$+$N$ Hamiltonian for the ground states of $^6$He and $^6$Li using a variational method with explicitly correlated Gaussian basis functions, employing the stochastic variational method (SVM) to select basis parameters. The $N$-$N$ interaction was modeled with either the realistic G3RS potential (containing central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) or the effective Minnesota (MN) potential (central only, no tensor), while the $N$-$\alpha$ interaction used the parity-dependent KKNN potential. The momentum distribution for the relative motion of the two valence nucleons was obtained via the Wigner phase-space distribution function, computed from the density matrix in the T-type Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- $^6$He ground state energy with G3RS: $-$0.460 MeV (underbound by 400–500 keV relative to experiment $-$0.975 MeV).
- $^6$Li ground state energy with G3RS: $-$3.31 MeV (underbound by ~400 keV relative to experiment $-$3.70 MeV).
- $^6$He charge radius with G3RS: $\sqrt{r_c^2} = 2.07$ fm (experiment: 2.054(14) fm).
- $^6$Li charge radius with G3RS: $\sqrt{r_c^2} = 2.52$ fm (experiment: 2.55(4) fm or 2.540(30) fm).
- $^6$Li matter radius with G3RS: $\sqrt{r_m^2} = 2.39$ fm (experimental value deduced from charge radius: 2.42(4) fm).
- $^6$He matter radius with G3RS: $\sqrt{r_m^2} = 2.56$ fm (experimental range from interaction cross section: 2.48(3) fm).
- $^6$Li $D$-state probability (channel $(LS)=(21)$) with G3RS: 5.0%; with MN: 1.0%.
- $^6$He $D$-state probability (channel $(LS)=(11)$) with G3RS: 12.5%; with MN: 15.3%.
- Deuteron $D$-state probability with G3RS: 4.8%.
- The dip in the $^6$He momentum distribution appears around 2 fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends prior three-body calculations of $^6$He and $^6$Li (e.g., Funada et al. and Csoto et al., which included the tensor force) by specifically computing the momentum distribution of the two-nucleon relative motion, which had not been addressed in those studies. The results are directly relevant to interpreting ongoing experiments at RIKEN that use one-nucleon exchange reactions ($^6$He$(p,dn)\alpha$ and $^6$Li$(p,dp)\alpha$) to probe spatial correlations in these $A=6$ nuclei, following the intensity interferometry technique of Marques et al. The finding that the tensor force qualitatively changes the momentum distribution around 2 fm$^{-1}$ enables future experimental discrimination between $S$-wave and $D$-wave contributions in two-nucleon correlations.
