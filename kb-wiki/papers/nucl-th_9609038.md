# The Nuclear-Matter Response in the Quark String-flip Model

> Nuclear matter is modeled directly in terms of its constituent quarks. A many-body string-flip potential is used that confines quarks within hadrons, enables the hadrons to separate without generating van der Waals forces, and is symmetric in all quark coordinates. We present variational Monte Carlo results for the ground-state properties of large, three-dimensional systems. A phase transition from nuclear to quark matter is observed which is characterized by a dramatic rearrangement of strings. We report on exact calculations of the dynamic response of many-quark systems in one spatial dimension. At low density and small momentum transfers the response is substantially larger than that of a free Fermi gas of quarks; this suggests that there is a coherent response from all the quarks inside the hadron. This coherence, however, is incomplete, as the response is suppressed relative to that of a free Fermi gas of nucleons due to the internal quark substructure of the hadron.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a nuclear- to quark-matter phase transition, characterized by a dramatic rearrangement of strings, is observed in a three-dimensional string-flip model; however, this result is limited to a very small system of only 8 hadrons (24 quarks), and the cluster probabilities that define the transition are acknowledged to be model dependent.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter directly in terms of constituent quarks using a many-body string-flip potential that confines quarks within hadrons, enables hadron separation without van der Waals forces, and is symmetric in all quark coordinates. Ground-state properties of three-dimensional systems are computed using variational Monte Carlo with simulated annealing for the quark-assignment problem, while exact calculations of the dynamic response are performed for many-quark systems in one spatial dimension using a different version of the string-flip model.

## Key numbers

- For the three-dimensional system, calculations were performed with a total of N=96 quarks (32 hadrons) using the pairing model, and with A=8 hadrons (24 quarks) for the full three-color model.
- The Coulomb sum was computed at a density of ρ=0.25, where the confinement scale is considerably smaller than the average inter-hadron separation.
- The variational wave function reproduces the exact wave function of isolated clusters in the limit of λ→1/√3 and the Fermi-gas result in the limit of λ→0.
- The 3-quark cluster probability drops from dominating the low-density nuclear phase to about one third in the quark-matter phase.

## Context

This work extends the string-flip model developed by Lenz (1986) and earlier studies by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (1991, 1992) and Frichter, Piekarewicz, and collaborators (1994). It addresses the long-standing problem of modeling systems with quarks confined inside color-neutral hadrons at low density but free quarks at high density, which was previously identified as generating unphysical van der Waals forces in pairwise confining models (Greenberg and Lipkin, 1981). The results enable future studies of quark giant resonances via maximum-entropy methods, inclusion of spin-isospin degrees of freedom, and extension to strange matter transitions.
