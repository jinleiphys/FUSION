# Degree to which CVC is Established Through Beta Decay Alone

> Precision studies of $0^{+} \rightarrow 0^{+}$ superallowed $β$ decays provide the necessary data to test stringently the CVC hypothesis. They will also provide a value for the weak vector coupling constant and the $V_{ud}$ quark mixing element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix. The determination of this element is crucial for the test of the unitarity of the CKM matrix, and the search for physics beyond the Standard Model. This paper reviews the current status of $0^{+} \rightarrow 0^{+}$ $β$-decay data and their implications, as well as some relevant data from the $β$ decay of $^{19}$Ne and the neutron.

## Key claim

The Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis is established through nuclear beta decay at the 4 × 10⁻⁴ level based on the constancy of corrected Ft values from nine superallowed 0⁺ → 0⁺ emitters, but the resulting test of CKM matrix unitarity shows a discrepancy of more than two standard deviations from unity, leaving the unitarity question unresolved.

## Method

The authors compiled precision experimental data on Q_EC values, half-lives, and branching ratios for nine superallowed 0⁺ → 0⁺ beta emitters (¹⁰C, ¹⁴O, ²⁶ᵐAl, ³⁴Cl, ³⁸ᵐK, ⁴²Sc, ⁴⁶V, ⁵⁰Mn, ⁵⁴Co). They applied calculated charge-dependent corrections (δ_C) and nuclear-structure dependent radiative corrections (δ_R) to obtain corrected Ft values, then tested the constancy of these Ft values as a test of CVC. The average Ft value was used to extract the weak vector coupling constant G'_V and the V_ud element of the CKM matrix, which was combined with V_us and V_ub from the Particle Data Group (1996) to test CKM unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Average corrected Ft value: 3072.3 ± 1.0 s, with reduced chi-square of 1.20
- CVC hypothesis accuracy level: 4 × 10⁻⁴ from constancy of nine Ft values
- Weak vector coupling constant: G'_V/(ħc)³ = (1.1496 ± 0.0004) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- V_ud = 0.9740 ± 0.0005
- CKM unitarity sum: |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² = 0.9972 ± 0.0013
- Nuclear-structure independent radiative correction Δ_R = (2.40 ± 0.08)%
- Q_EC values for nine emitters range from 1907.77(9) keV (¹⁰C) to 8242.56(28) keV (⁵⁴Co)
- Half-lives range from 193.270(63) ms (⁵⁴Co) to 70603(18) ms (¹⁴O)
- Individual Ft values for the nine emitters range from 3069.4(31) s (³⁸ᵐK) to 3077.3(24) s (⁴²Sc)

## Context

This paper reviews and updates the status of superallowed 0⁺ → 0⁺ beta decay data following the framework established by Hardy and Towner (1990) and extends the analysis to include the most recent measurements from the Chalk River group and the Auckland group. The work builds on earlier calculations of charge-dependent corrections by Towner (1995), Ormand and Brown (1995), and Sagawa, Suzuki, and Giai (1996), and compares results with complementary data from ¹⁹Ne decay (Johnson et al., 1996) and neutron decay. The persistent unitarity deficit motivates searches for physics beyond the Standard Model, such as extra Z bosons or right-handed currents, and highlights the need for improved neutron decay measurements as a complementary test.
