# Universality in quasinormal modes of neutron stars with quark-hadron crossover

> We examine the gravitational wave frequencies of the fundamental ($f$-) and 1st pressure ($p_1$-) modes excited in the neutron star models constructed with the quark-hadron crossover (QHC) type equations of state (EOS). We find that the $f$-mode frequencies with QHC EOS basically are smaller and the $p_1$-mode frequencies with QHC EOS are larger than those with hadronic EOS, focusing on the neutron star model with a fixed mass. We also find that the universality in the $f$-mode frequencies multiplied by the stellar mass as a function of the stellar compactness or as a function of the dimensionless tidal deformability, which is derived with various hadronic EOSs, can keep even with QHC EOS. That is, using these universal relations, one cannot distinguish QHC EOS from hadronic EOSs. Instead, using the relations one can extract the stellar radii whose evolution from low to high mass neutron stars can differentiate QHC from hadronic EOSs. On the other hand, we find that the $p_1$-mode frequencies multiplied by the stellar mass with QHC EOS significantly deviate in a certain mass range from the corresponding empirical relations derived with various hadronic EOSs, with which one may distinguish QHC EOS from hadronic EOSs.

## Key claim

The universal relations for f-mode gravitational wave frequencies (multiplied by stellar mass) derived from purely hadronic equations of state hold even for neutron stars with quark-hadron crossover (QHC) equations of state, meaning these relations alone cannot distinguish QHC from hadronic models; however, the p1-mode frequencies with QHC EOS show deviations up to ~20% from hadronic empirical relations in a certain mass range, potentially allowing discrimination.

## Method

The authors constructed non-rotating, spherically symmetric neutron star models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using four specific QHC21T equations of state (AT, BT, CT, DT) and the purely hadronic Togashi EOS as reference. They solved the linearized Einstein equations with appropriate boundary conditions (regularity at center, vanishing Lagrangian pressure perturbation at surface, outgoing waves at infinity) to compute the complex quasinormal mode frequencies for the fundamental (f-) and first pressure (p1-) modes, adopting the zero-damping approximation where the imaginary part is set to zero.

## Key numbers

- The f-mode frequencies with QHC21T EOSs are smaller than those with Togashi EOS for fixed stellar mass
- The p1-mode frequencies with QHC21T can become larger than those with Togashi EOS within a certain mass range (~1.7-2.1 solar masses)
- The relative deviation of p1-mode frequencies from hadronic empirical relations reaches up to ~20% for QHC21T models, compared to at most 10% for hadronic EOSs
- The universal f-mode relations (f_f * M_1.4 vs. M/R and vs. dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda) hold for QHC EOSs with deviations only slightly larger than for Togashi EOS
- The canonical neutron star with 1.4 solar masses and 12 km radius has compactness M/R = 0.17
- The saturation density n_0 = 0.160 fm^{-3} corresponds to rho_0 = 2.68 x 10^{14} g/cm^3 for Togashi EOS
- The 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius constraint from GW170817 is R_1.4 <= 13.6 km

## Context

This work extends studies of universal relations in neutron star quasinormal modes (e.g., Sotani 2020, Sotani 2021) from purely hadronic equations of state to quark-hadron crossover models (QHC19, QHC21). It builds on the QHC21T EOS family developed by Kojo (2021) and contrasts with first-order phase transition models (Gibbs equilibrium mixed phases) which tend to produce softer EOSs. The results enable using f-mode universal relations to extract stellar radii whose mass evolution can differentiate QHC from hadronic scenarios, while p1-mode deviations offer a potential direct discriminant for future high-frequency gravitational wave detectors.
