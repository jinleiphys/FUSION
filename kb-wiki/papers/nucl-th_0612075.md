# 11Li beta decay into 9Li and deuteron within a three-body model

> The beta decay process of the 11Li halo nucleus into 9Li and d is studied in a three-body model. The 11Li nucleus is described as a 9Li+n+n system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh. Various 9Li+d potentials are compared involving a forbidden state, a physical bound state, and a resonance near 0.25 MeV in the s-wave. With an added surface absorption, they are compatible with elastic scattering data. The transition probability per time unit is quite sensitive to the location of the resonance. For a fixed resonance location, it does not depend much on the potential choice at variance with the 6He delayed deuteron decay. The calculated transition probability per time unit is larger than the experimental value but the difference can be explained by a slightly higher resonance location and/or by absorption from the 9Li+d final channel.

## Key claim

The calculated transition probability per time unit for the beta decay of 11Li into 9Li and deuteron overestimates the experimental value by a factor larger than 3, but this difference can be explained by a slightly higher resonance location and/or by absorption from the 9Li+d final channel.

## Method

The 11Li nucleus is described as a 9Li+n+n three-body system in hyperspherical coordinates on a Lagrange mesh, with effective 9Li+n and n+n interactions. The final 9Li+d state is modeled as a deuteron ground-state wave function multiplied by a scattering wave function derived from various 9Li+d potentials, some of which include a forbidden state, a physical bound state, and a resonance near 0.25 MeV in the s-wave. The transition probability per time and energy units is calculated from the Gamow-Teller matrix element using the ingoing scattering wave function formalism.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of 11Li is 300 ± 19 keV (atomic mass evaluation) or 376 ± 5 keV (recent remeasurement), leading to Q values of 2.71 ± 0.02 MeV and 2.63 MeV respectively.
- The experimental branching ratio for the beta delayed deuteron decay is (1.5 ± 0.2) × 10^(-4), corresponding to a transition probability per second of (12 ± 2) × 10^(-3) s^(-1).
- For potential Va with S2n = 0.307 MeV and no absorption, the calculated total transition probability per second is 38.1 × 10^(-3) s^(-1) (E>0 cutoff), over three times the experimental value.
- With surface absorption strength W0 = 5 MeV, the calculated transition probability per second for potential Va drops to 4.4 × 10^(-3) s^(-1) (E>0 cutoff).
- The resonance in the s-wave 9Li+d potential is located at 0.25 ± 0.15 MeV above threshold, corresponding to an excitation energy of 18.15 ± 0.15 MeV in 11Be.
- The deuteron binding energy obtained with the Minnesota interaction is -2.202 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model approach previously applied to the 6He beta delayed deuteron decay (Tursunov, Baye, Descouvemont 2006) to the 11Li halo nucleus. Unlike the 6He case, where a strong cancellation in the Gamow-Teller matrix element makes the result highly sensitive to the potential choice, the 11Li decay shows no such cancellation and is only weakly dependent on the potential for a fixed resonance location. The study enables improved interpretation of ongoing experiments (Raabe 2006) and highlights the need for more precise determination of the resonance energy and low-energy elastic scattering data for the 9Li+d system.
