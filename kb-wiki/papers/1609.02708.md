# Hyperon star in a modified quark meson coupling model

> We determine the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter with the inclusion of hyperons in a self-consistent manner by using a Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model (MQMC) where the confining interaction for quarks inside a baryon is represented by a phenomenological average potential in an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. The hadron-hadron interaction in nuclear matter is then realized by introducing additional quark couplings to $σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$ mesons through mean-field approximations. The effect of a nonlinear $ω$-$ρ$ term on the equation of state is studied. The hyperon couplings are fixed from the optical potential values and the mass-radius curve is determined satisfying the maximum mass constraint of $2$~M$_{\odot}$ for neutron stars, as determined in recent measurements of the pulsar PSR J0348+0432. We also observe that there is no significant advantage of introducing the nonlinear $ω$-$ρ$ term in the context of obtaining the star mass constraint in the present set of parametrizations.

## Key claim

The Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model (MQMC) can produce a hyperonic neutron star equation of state stiff enough to satisfy the 2 M$_{\odot}$ maximum mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432, but the authors find that introducing a nonlinear $\omega$-$\rho$ coupling term provides no significant advantage for meeting this mass constraint within their chosen parametrizations.

## Method

The authors model baryons as three quarks confined by an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic potential, then introduce additional quark couplings to $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons through mean-field approximations to realize hadron-hadron interactions in nuclear matter. They include the full baryon octet (nucleons, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) under $\beta$-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, fixing hyperon couplings from empirical optical potential values ($U_\Lambda = -28$ MeV, $U_\Sigma = 30$ MeV, $U_\Xi = -18$ MeV with variations). The equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass constraint of 2.01 $\pm$ 0.04 M$_{\odot}$ from pulsar PSR J0348+0432 is used as the benchmark
- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ with binding energy $B_0 = -15.7$ MeV and symmetry energy $J = 32.0$ MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility $K = 292$ MeV for quark mass $m_q = 150$ MeV, and $K = 246$ MeV for $m_q = 80$ MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy $L = 86.39$ MeV for $m_q = 150$ MeV with $\Lambda_\nu = 0$, increasing to $L = 99.95$ MeV for $\Lambda_\nu = 0.1$
- Hyperon potentials fixed at $U_\Lambda = -28$ MeV, $U_\Sigma = 30$ MeV, and $U_\Xi = -18$ MeV (with variations to $U_\Xi = -10$ MeV and $U_\Xi = 0$ MeV)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio $M_N^*/M_N = 0.87$ at saturation density for $m_q = 150$ MeV
- Hyperons appear at baryon density $\rho_B = 0.49$ fm$^{-3}$ corresponding to pressure $P \geq 86.2$ MeV/fm$^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends the Modified Quark Meson Coupling Model previously applied to symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Mishra, Sahoo, Panda, Barik, and Frederico to include hyperonic degrees of freedom for neutron star applications. The model addresses the challenge identified by Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991) and others that hyperon softening typically reduces maximum neutron star masses below 2 M$_{\odot}$, contrasting with the recent high-mass measurements of pulsars PSR J0348+0432 by Antoniadis (2013) and PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest (2010). The results enable further studies of how quark-level descriptions of baryon structure can produce sufficiently stiff equations of state for hyperonic stars, while suggesting that nonlinear $\omega$-$\rho$ couplings are not essential for meeting the 2 M$_{\odot}$ constraint in this framework.
