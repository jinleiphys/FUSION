# Locating the special point of hybrid neutron stars

> The special point is a feature unique to models of hybrid neutron stars. It represents a location on their mass--radius sequences that is insensitive to the phase transition density. We consider hybrid neutron stars with a core of deconfined quark matter that obeys a constant--sound--speed (CSS) equation of state model and provide a fit formula for the coordinates of the special point as functions of the squared sound speed ($c_s^2$) and pressure scale ($A$) parameters. Using the special point mass as a proxy for the maximum mass of the hybrid stars we derive limits for the CSS model parameters based on the recent NICER constraint on mass and radius of pulsar PSR J0740+6620, $0.36 < {c^2_s}_{\rm min} < 0.43$ and $80<A [{\rm MeV/fm}^3]<160$. The upper limit for the maximum mass of hybrid stars depends on the upper limit for $c_s^2$ so that choosing $c^2_{s,max} = 0.6$ results in $M_{\rm max}<2.7~M_\odot$, within the mass range of GW190814.

## Key claim

The special point of hybrid neutron stars, a location on the mass-radius diagram insensitive to the phase transition density, can be used to translate the NICER mass-radius constraint for PSR J0740+6620 into limits on the constant-sound-speed (CSS) quark matter model parameters, under the assumption that the special point mass approximates the maximum mass of hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid neutron star equations of state using a Maxwell-type first-order phase transition from a hadronic relativistic density-functional model (DD2 parametrization with excluded volume) to a constant-sound-speed (CSS) quark matter model. They performed an extensive scan of the CSS parameter space (squared sound speed \(c_s^2\) and pressure scale \(A\)) to map the location of the special point in the mass-radius plane, then derived a fit formula for the special point coordinates as functions of these parameters. By inverting this fit, they translated the NICER mass-radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 into constraints on the CSS model parameters.

## Key numbers

* The NICER constraint on PSR J0740+6620 yields limits on the CSS model parameters: \(0.36 < {c^2_s}_{\rm min} < 0.43\) and \(80 < A [{\rm MeV/fm}^3] < 160\).
* Choosing an upper limit of \(c^2_{s,max} = 0.6\) gives an upper limit for the maximum mass of hybrid stars of \(M_{\rm max} < 2.7~M_\odot\), within the mass range of GW190814.
* The special point mass is typically no less than \(M_{\rm max} - M_{\rm SP} = 0.2~M_\odot\) below the maximum mass.
* For \(A = 80\) MeV/fm\(^3\), the allowed range for the squared sound speed is \(0.36 \leq c^2_s \leq 0.43\) when assuming PSR J0740+6620 is the heaviest possible neutron star.
* For \(A = 80\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and assuming \(M_{\rm max} > M_{J0740}\), the upper limit on the squared sound speed becomes \(c^2_{s,max} = 0.8\) with a lower limit of \(c^2_{s,min} = 0.36\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the hybrid star special point by Yudin (2014) and Cierniak (2020, 2021), which showed that the special point is independent of the underlying hadronic equation of state and phase transition type. The authors apply the special point concept to translate the recent NICER mass-radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (Miller 2021, Riley 2021) into direct constraints on the CSS quark matter model parameters, enabling future comparisons with color-superconducting nonlocal NJL model calculations (Antic 2021) and further observational constraints from gravitational wave events like GW170817 and GW190814.
