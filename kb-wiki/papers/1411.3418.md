# Emergent Soft Monopole Modes in Weakly-Bound Deformed Nuclei

> Based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solutions in large deformed coordinate spaces, the finite amplitude method for quasiparticle random phase approximation (FAM-QRPA) has been implemented, providing a suitable approach to probe collective excitations of weakly-bound nuclei embedded in the continuum. The monopole excitation modes in Magnesium isotopes up to the neutron drip line have been studied with the FAM-QRPA framework on both the coordinate-space and harmonic oscillator basis methods. Enhanced soft monopole strengths and collectivity as a result of weak-binding effects have been unambiguously demonstrated.

## Key claim

Enhanced soft monopole strengths and collectivity in weakly-bound deformed nuclei emerge due to two distinct mechanisms: a less collective mode around 4 MeV generated by near-threshold non-resonant continuum states associated with pairing halos, and a more collective second mode around 9 MeV linked to a pygmy monopole resonance; however, the collectivity and existence of these modes depend sensitively on the pairing interaction (surface versus mixed pairing) and the binding energy (Fermi energy) of the specific isotope.

## Method

The authors implemented the finite amplitude method for quasiparticle random phase approximation (FAM-QRPA) based on Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) solutions in large axially symmetric coordinate spaces using B-spline techniques, enabling treatment of continuum couplings in deformed weakly-bound nuclei. The HFB equations were solved with the hfbax code using Skyrme parameterizations SLy4 and SkM* for the particle-hole channel and density-dependent surface pairing for the particle-particle channel, with quasiparticle wavefunctions represented on a 2D Gauss-Legendre lattice. The FAM-QRPA equations were solved iteratively using a hybrid MPI+OpenMP parallel scheme with Broyden iteration, applying an imaginary frequency of 0.5 MeV for smoothing resonances.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths: V0 = 500 MeV fm^3 for SLy4 and V0 = 450 MeV fm^3 for SkM*, with a pairing window of 60 MeV
- Maximum mesh spacing: 0.6 fm; order of B-splines: 12
- Coordinate-space box size: 27 fm for weakly-bound nuclei (compared to 24 fm for 100Zr and 18 fm for convergence tests)
- Quasiparticle energy cutoff: 80 MeV for 100Zr, 95 MeV for convergence in 40Mg
- FAM-QRPA matrix size: 24,000 by 24,000 with 27 fm box (compared to typical QRPA matrix of 160,000 by 160,000 with 20 fm box)
- Fermi energy of 42Mg: lambda_n = -0.22 MeV (SLy4) versus lambda_n = -1.19 MeV (SkM*); 44Mg: lambda_n = -0.57 MeV (SkM*)
- Low-energy monopole peaks appear around 4 MeV in Mg isotopes; second resonance around 9 MeV in 42Mg (SLy4)
- Two giant monopole resonances around 15 MeV and 23 MeV due to deformation splitting

## Context

This work extends previous HFB studies of deformed halos in weakly-bound Ne and Mg isotopes by Pei et al. (2013) and earlier QRPA calculations by Yoshida (2009) that used mixed pairing, by implementing a fully self-consistent continuum QRPA approach in deformed coordinate spaces. The results contradict the expectation from Hamamoto that low-energy monopole modes would be non-collective, and instead support the prediction of enhanced collectivity due to weak-binding effects by Yoshida (2009). The approach enables future studies of multipole excitations in weakly-bound deformed nuclei and their implications for astrophysical nucleosynthesis, particularly for facilities such as FRIB.
