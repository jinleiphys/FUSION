# $Δ(1232)$ Isobar Excitations in Nuclear Many-Body Systems derived from various NN Interactions

> $Δ$ isobar components in the nuclear many-body wave function are investigated for the deuteron, light nuclei (${}^{16}O$) and infinite nuclear matter within the framework of the coupled-cluster theory. The predictions derived for various realistic models of the baryon-baryon interaction are compared to each other. These include local ($V28$) and non-local meson exchange potentials (Bonn$_{2000}$) but also a model recently derived by the Salamanca group accounting for quark degrees of freedom. The characteristic differences which are obtained for the $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ correlation functions are related to the approximation made in deriving the matrix elements for the baryon-baryon interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the predicted probabilities and spatial distributions of Δ(1232) isobar components in nuclear wave functions depend strongly on whether the baryon-baryon interaction is treated with a local or non-local representation of the one-pion-exchange transition potential, with the local approximation (V28) producing longer-range ΔΔ components while the non-local approaches (Bonn2000, Salamanca quark model) yield stronger short-range components.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel problem for the deuteron in a spherical box with 6 coupled partial waves (NN ³S₁-³D₁ plus four ΔΔ channels) using exact diagonalization in momentum space. For ¹⁶O and infinite nuclear matter, they employed the coupled-cluster (exponential S) approach generalized to include isobar degrees of freedom, solving the integral equations for S₂ amplitudes in momentum space with a Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹ for nuclear matter and harmonic oscillator states (ħω = 14 MeV) for ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy contributions from isobar components: -5.21 MeV (V28), -6.75 MeV (Bonn2000), -1.59 MeV (Salamanca CQC)
- Total ΔΔ probability in deuteron: 0.524% (V28), 0.611% (Bonn2000), 0.241% (Salamanca CQC)
- Largest ΔΔ partial wave probability in deuteron is ⁷D₁: 0.417% (V28), 0.361% (Bonn2000), 0.124% (Salamanca CQC)
- ³S₁ ΔΔ probability in deuteron: 0.043% (V28), 0.223% (Bonn2000), 0.107% (Salamanca CQC)
- Nuclear matter Fermi momentum used: k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹
- Harmonic oscillator parameter for ¹⁶O: ħω = 14 MeV
- Spherical box radius for deuteron calculation: R_box = 20 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isobar configurations in nuclear systems by Hajduk (three-nucleon problem) and Manz (nuclear matter) by comparing three different baryon-baryon interaction models within a unified many-body framework. The paper contrasts the local Argonne V28 potential with the non-local Bonn2000 meson-exchange model and the Salamanca Chiral Quark Cluster model, building on earlier observations by the authors about differences between local and non-local approximations. The results enable future experimental tests of isobar components through photoproduction of pions and exclusive (e,e'NN) reactions, as well as theoretical refinement of transition potentials.
