# Unraveling the neutron skin thickness through jet charge in deep inelastic scattering

> The neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich nuclei has traditionally been measured using elastic fixed target electron-nucleus scattering since the 1970s. In this paper, we propose a novel probe of the neutron skin thickness through deep inelastic scattering in electron-ion collisions, leveraging the intrinsic correlation between final-state jet charge distribution and initial-state partonic distributions in nucleons. Specifically, we demonstrate the sensitivity of jet charge distribution to the neutron skin thickness in $e$+Pb collisions with varying centralities, and in isobar collisions of $e$+Ru and $e$+Zr. We predict a strong suppression of positive jet charge distribution and an enhancement for negative jet charge distribution in peripheral electron-ion collisions, revealing the neutron skin effect. This proposal can also be extended to photon and Z-boson tagged jet charge distribution in proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, providing an alternative access to neutron skin thickness.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the jet charge distribution in deep inelastic electron-ion collisions is sensitive to the neutron skin thickness of the target nucleus, with the effect most pronounced in peripheral collisions; the claim is conditional on the use of centrality-dependent nuclear modification factors and double ratios to cancel uncertainties from nuclear parton distribution functions and final-state effects.

## Method

The authors simulate electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider using PYTHIA 8.3 with CT14nlo and EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution functions, implementing impact-parameter-dependent nuclear parton distribution functions based on a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution for protons and neutrons. They compute the jet charge distribution using the anti-kT jet algorithm with radius R=1.0, selecting events with Q² ≥ 10 GeV², transverse momentum pT ≥ 10 GeV, and rapidity |η| ≤ 2 for both electrons and jets. The centrality-dependent nuclear modification factor R^C and a double ratio of positive to negative jet charge regions are calculated for e+Pb collisions at √s = 105 GeV with varying neutron skin thickness parameters.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb, three neutron skin thickness values are used: ΔR_np = 0 fm (vanishing skin), ΔR_np = 0.15 ± 0.03 fm (from coherent π photoproduction), and ΔR_np = 0.28 ± 0.071 fm (from PREX-II experiment)
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for ²⁰⁸Pb proton distribution are fixed at c_p = 6.680 fm and a_p = 0.448 fm, giving proton rms radius R_p = 5.436 fm
- For ⁹⁶Ru, the neutron skin thickness is ΔR_np = 0.03 fm; for ⁹⁶Zr, ΔR_np = 0.16 fm
- The double ratio ℝ shows up to 30% deviation from unity in the most peripheral 90-100% e+Pb collisions for the PREX-II neutron skin thickness
- For ⁹⁶Zr, the double ratio shows a 17% change in peripheral (80-100%) collisions compared to ⁹⁶Ru
- The jet charge parameter κ = 0.3 is used for jet flavor identification

## Context

This work extends the traditional approach of measuring neutron skin thickness through elastic fixed-target electron-nucleus scattering, which has been used since the 1970s (Frois 1977), and parity-violating electron scattering as done by the PREX-II collaboration (2021). It builds on recent proposals to extract neutron skin from relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Giacalone 2023) and from hard probes such as W+/W- ratios (Paukkunen 2015) and charged particle ratios in isobar collisions (van der Schee 2023). The method enables the Electron-Ion Collider to probe nuclear structure at the partonic level using perturbative QCD, and can be extended to photon and Z-boson tagged jet charge distributions in proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider.
