# Excitation function of initial temperature of heavy flavor quarkonium emission source in high energy collisions

> The transverse momentum spectra of $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and $Υ(nS, n=1,2,3)$ produced in proton-proton ($p$+$p$), proton-antiproton ($p$+$\bar{p}$), proton-lead ($p$+Pb), gold-gold (Au+Au), and lead-lead (Pb+Pb) collisions over a wide energy range are analyzed by the (two-component) Erlang distribution, the Hagedorn function (the inverse power-law), and the Tsallis-Levy function. The initial temperature is obtained from the color string percolation model due to the fit by the (two-component) Erlang distribution in the framework of multisource thermal model. The excitation functions of some parameters such as the mean transverse momentum and initial temperature increase from dozens of GeV to above 10 TeV. The mean transverse momentum and initial temperature decrease (increase slightly or do not change obviously) with the increase of rapidity (centrality). Meanwhile, the mean transverse momentum of $Υ(nS, n=1,2,3)$ is larger than that of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$, and the initial temperature for $Υ(nS, n=1,2,3)$ emission is higher than that for $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ emission, which shows a mass-dependent behavior.

## Key claim

The initial temperature of the heavy flavor quarkonium emission source, extracted from transverse momentum spectra using the color string percolation model, increases with collision energy from 39 GeV to 13 TeV and shows a mass-dependent behavior where bottomonium states have higher initial temperatures than charmonium states, though the authors note that the extracted temperature values carry a systematic uncertainty of 8% depending on which fitting function is used.

## Method

The transverse momentum spectra of J/ψ, ψ(2S), and Υ(nS) from p+p, p+p̅, p+Pb, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions were fitted using three functions: the (two-component) Erlang distribution from the multisource thermal model, the Hagedorn function (inverse power-law), and the Tsallis-Levy function. The initial temperature was then calculated from the root-mean-square transverse momentum via the color string percolation model formula T_i = √(⟨p_T²⟩/2), assuming a single-string configuration with color suppression factor F(ξ)=1.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range analyzed: 39 GeV to 13 TeV (center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)
- Systematic uncertainty on extracted ⟨p_T⟩ and T_i across the three fitting functions: 8%
- Initial temperature and mean transverse momentum increase with collision energy from 39 GeV to 13 TeV
- Mean transverse momentum of Υ(nS) is larger than that of J/ψ and ψ(2S); initial temperature for Υ(nS) emission is higher than for J/ψ and ψ(2S)
- Mean transverse momentum and initial temperature decrease with increasing rapidity from mid-rapidity to forward rapidity
- Mean transverse momentum and initial temperature increase slightly with event centrality from peripheral to central collisions

## Context

This work extends the application of the color string percolation model (originally developed for heavy-ion collisions) to small systems such as p+p and p+p̅ collisions, treating them as having sufficient thermalization to define a temperature. The analysis uses data from STAR (2009, 2014), CDF (2002, 2007), ALICE (2011), LHCb (2013, 2015, 2017, 2018), ATLAS (2014, 2017), and CMS (2012, 2015, 2017) collaborations. The results enable systematic comparison of quarkonium excitation functions across different collision systems and energies, and the mass-dependent temperature behavior is interpreted as a reflection of formation time differences in hydrodynamic evolution, where more massive particles exit the system earlier with smaller radial flow velocities.
