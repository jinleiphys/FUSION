# Shell-structure and asymmetry effects in level densities

> Level density $ρ(E,N,Z)$ is derived for a nuclear system with a given energy $E$, neutron $N$, and proton $Z$ particle numbers, within the semiclassical extended Thomas-Fermi and periodic-orbit theory beyond the Fermi-gas saddle-point method. We obtain $~~ρ\propto I_ν(S)/S^ν$,~~ where $I_ν(S)$ is the modified Bessel function of the entropy $S$, and $ν$ is related to the number of integrals of motion, except for the energy $E$. For small shell structure contribution one obtains within the micro-macroscopic approximation (MMA) the value of $ν=2$ for $ρ(E,N,Z)$. In the opposite case of much larger shell structure contributions one finds a larger value of $ν=3$. The MMA level density $ρ$ reaches the well-known Fermi gas asymptote for large excitation energies, and the finite micro-canonical limit for low excitation energies. Fitting the MMA $ρ(E,N,Z)$ to experimental data on a long isotope chain for low excitation energies, due mainly to the shell effects, one obtains results for the inverse level density parameter $K$, which differs significantly from that of neutron resonances.

## Key claim

The paper derives a micro-macroscopic approximation (MMA) for the nuclear level density ρ(E,N,Z) that interpolates between the Fermi-gas asymptote at high excitation energies and a finite micro-canonical limit at low excitation energies, with the key condition that the exponent ν in the Bessel-function representation takes the value ν=2 for small shell-structure contributions but increases to ν=3 when shell-structure contributions are much larger.

## Method

The authors start from the inverse Laplace transformation of the partition function within the grand-canonical ensemble, using the extended Thomas-Fermi approach for the smooth component and periodic-orbit theory for the oscillating shell corrections. They expand the Jacobian determinant from the saddle-point integration over neutron and proton chemical potentials to linear order in the shell-structure parameter ξ, then perform the inverse Laplace transformation over β beyond the standard saddle-point method to obtain a closed-form expression in terms of modified Bessel functions of the entropy S. The resulting MMA level density is fitted to experimental data on long isotope chains at low excitation energies to extract the inverse level density parameter K.

## Key numbers

- The level density parameter a decomposes as a = a_n + a_p, where each component a_τ = π² g_τ/6, with g_τ the single-particle level density for neutrons (τ=n) or protons (τ=p)
- The shell-correction parameter ξ* at the saddle point is approximately ξ* ≈ (2π⁴ U)/(3a A^{1/3}) ℰ_sh, where ℰ_sh is the relative shell correction energy; for A≈200, λ≈40 MeV, and ℰ_sh≈2.0, ξ* ranges from ~0.1 to ~10 for temperatures T≈0.1–1 MeV (excitation energies U≈0.2–20 MeV for K=10 MeV)
- The condition for neglecting higher-order 1/β⁴ terms is U ≪ √(90/7) (a λ²)/(2π⁴ A^{2/3}), which numerically gives U ≪ λ≈40 MeV for typical parameters A=200, a≈20 MeV⁻¹, K≈10 MeV
- The smooth chemical potential is approximately constant at λ_τ ≈ ħ²k_F²/2m ≈ 40 MeV, with Fermi momentum k_F≈1.37 fm⁻¹ and nuclear matter density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the MMA approach previously developed for one-component nucleon systems (Magner et al., NPA; Magner et al., PRC) to neutron-proton asymmetric nuclei, building on the Strutinsky shell correction method (Strutinsky, 1967; Brack et al., 1972) and the periodic-orbit theory (Stratmann and Magner, 1976; Brack and Bhaduri, 2003). The results enable fitting the inverse level density parameter K from low-energy experimental data on long isotope chains, where the extracted K differs significantly from values obtained from neutron resonance data, and provide a framework for studying shell and asymmetry effects in level densities below the neutron resonance region.
