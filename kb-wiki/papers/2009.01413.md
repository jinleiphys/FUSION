# Utility of antiproton-nucleus scattering for probing nuclear surface density distributions

> Antiproton-nucleon ($\bar{p}N$) total cross sections are typically 3-4 times larger than the $NN$ ones at incident energies from a few hundreds to thousands MeV. We investigate antiproton-nucleus scattering as it could work as a probe of the nuclear structure giving the sensitivity differently from a proton probe. High-energy antiproton-nucleus reactions are reasonably described by the Glauber model with a minimal profile function that reproduces the $\bar{p}N$ and $\bar{p}$-$^{12}$C cross section data. In contrast to the proton-nucleus scattering, we find that the complete absorption occurs even beyond the nuclear radius due to the large $\bar{p}N$ elementary cross sections, which shows stronger sensitivity to the nuclear density distribution in the tail region. This sensitivity is quantified in the total reaction cross sections with various density profiles for future measurement including neutron-rich unstable nuclei.

# Utility of antiproton-nucleus scattering for probing nuclear surface density distributions

## Key claim

Antiproton-nucleus scattering at 100–1000 MeV probes nuclear density distributions down to approximately 1/100 of the central density, about one order of magnitude deeper into the tail than proton scattering, but this sensitivity is conditional on using a finite-range profile function with slope parameters β ≈ 0.5–1.3 fm²; the zero-range approximation fails entirely.

## Method

The authors employed the Glauber model in the optical-limit approximation (OLA) to calculate total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections for antiproton-nucleus (p̄A) and proton-nucleus (pA) scattering. They parametrized the antinucleon-nucleon (N̄N) profile function using a Gaussian finite-range form, fixing the slope parameter β_N̄N by fitting to p̄-¹²C total reaction cross section data from three experimental datasets (Abrams 1971, Aihara 1981, Nakamura 1984). The validity of the resulting profile function was tested against elastic scattering differential cross section data for ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca at 180 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton-nucleon total cross sections are approximately 4 times larger than NN total cross sections at 100 MeV and approximately 3 times larger at 1000 MeV.
- The slope parameter β_N̄N required to describe p̄-¹²C data ranges from 0.5 to 1.3 fm², compared to 0.1–0.7 fm² for NN profile functions.
- For p̄-¹²C at 180 MeV, total reaction cross sections increase from 366 mb (β=0.0 fm²) to 430 mb (β=0.4 fm²) to 487 mb (β=0.8 fm²); for p-¹²C the corresponding values are 206, 225, and 246 mb.
- In p̄-⁴⁰Ca scattering at 180 MeV, the reaction probability reaches 0.5 at ρ_m/ρ_0 = 0.02 (corresponding to 5.5 fm radius), whereas for p-⁴⁰Ca the half-probability occurs at ρ_m/ρ_0 = 0.2 (4.2 fm radius).
- The ratio σ_R(p̄A)/σ_R(pA) peaks at around 300 MeV incident energy.
- The black-sphere empirical relation holds within approximately 10% for A=40–250 with diffuseness parameters a=0.4–0.6 fm across all incident energies studied.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model analysis of hadron-nucleus scattering previously applied to proton probes by Horiuchi (2014, 2016) and Hatakeyama (2018), who showed that energy-dependent NN cross sections can separately extract proton and neutron radii. The authors demonstrate that antiprotons offer complementary sensitivity to the extreme nuclear surface, particularly for probing halo structures such as the candidate two-neutron halo nucleus ³¹F studied theoretically by Michel (2020) and Masui (2020). The results enable future experimental programs like the PUMA proposal (low-energy antiproton beams for exotic nuclei) and suggest that combined p̄A and pA total reaction cross section measurements could determine nuclear surface diffuseness parameters, especially for neutron-rich unstable nuclei where recent radius measurements by Tanaka (2020) show unexpected increases in calcium isotopes.
