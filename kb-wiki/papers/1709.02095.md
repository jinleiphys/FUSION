# Thermodynamics and relativistic kinetic theory for q-generalized Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac systems

> The thermodynamics and covariant kinetic theory have been elaborately investigated in a non-extensive environment considering the non-extensive generalization of Bose-Einstein (BE) and Fermi-Dirac (FD) statistics. Starting with Tsallis' entropy formula, the fundamental principles of thermostatistics have been established for a grand canonical system having q-generalized BE/FD degrees of freedom. The many particle kinetic theory has been set up in terms of the relativistic transport equation with q-generalized Uehling-Uhlenbeck collision term. The conservation laws have been realized in terms of appropriate moments of the transport equation. The thermodynamic quantities have been obtained in weak non-extensive environment for a massive pion-nucleon and a massless quark-gluon system with non-zero baryon chemical potential. In order to get an estimate of the impact of non-extensivity on the system dynamics, the q-modified Debye mass and hence the q-modified effective coupling have been estimated for a quark-gluon system.

## Key claim

The paper develops a complete relativistic kinetic theory and thermostatistical formalism for systems obeying q-generalized Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics, but the explicit numerical results for thermodynamic quantities and effective coupling are obtained only in the weak non-extensive limit (small deviations from q=1, neglecting terms quadratic in (q-1)).

## Method

The author starts from Tsallis' entropy formula and uses Lagrange multiplier extremization to derive q-generalized grand canonical probability distributions and single-particle distribution functions for bosons and fermions. A relativistic transport equation is then constructed with a q-generalized Uehling-Uhlenbeck collision term that includes quantum statistical factors (Bose enhancement and Pauli blocking), and conservation laws are derived from moments of this transport equation. Thermodynamic quantities (number density, energy density, pressure, entropy density) are computed by expanding the q-generalized distribution functions in a power series in (q-1) and performing momentum integrals, keeping only terms linear in (q-1). The q-modified Debye mass and effective QCD coupling are estimated for a massless quark-gluon system using the same perturbative expansion.

## Key numbers

- The q-generalized single-particle distribution function for bosons and fermions is given by \( f_q = 1 / [1 - (1-q)(e/T - \mu/T)]^{1/(q-1)} \mp 1 \), which reduces to standard BE/FD distributions when q → 1.
- The identity \( \{f_q^k\}^q = \mp \frac{\partial}{\partial y_k} \left\{ \frac{(1 \pm f_q^k)^{q-1}}{(q-1)} \right\} \) is derived, where \( y_k = (p_k \cdot u_q - \mu_{kq})/T_q \).
- The expansion of \( \{f_q^k\}^q \) to first order in (q-1) yields: \( \{f_q^k\}^q = \sum_{l=1}^\infty (\pm)^{l-1} e^{l\tilde\mu_k} e^{-l\tau_k} + (q-1) \sum_{l=1}^\infty (\pm)^{l-1} e^{l\tilde\mu_k} e^{-l\tau_k} \left\{ \frac{l}{2}\tilde{\mu_k}^2 + \tilde{\mu_k} - l\tilde{\mu_k}\tau_k - \tau_k + \frac{l}{2}\tau_k^2 \right\} \), where \( \tau_k = p_k \cdot u_q / T_q \) and \( \tilde\mu_k = \mu_k/T \).
- The q-generalized Boltzmann distribution function is \( \{f_q^k\}_B = \{1 + (q-1)(\tau_k - \tilde\mu_k)\} \), and its q-th power expands to \( \{f_q^k\}_B^q = e^{\tilde\mu_k} e^{-\tau_k} + (q-1)[ \frac{1}{2}\tilde{\mu_k}^2 + \tilde{\mu_k} - \tilde{\mu_k}\tau_k - \tau_k + \frac{1}{2}\tau_k^2 ] \).

## Context

This work extends prior non-extensive statistical mechanics approaches (Tsallis, 1988; Tsallis, 2009) and relativistic kinetic theory (de Groot, 1980) by incorporating quantum statistical effects (Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac statistics) that were missing in earlier q-generalized Boltzmann treatments (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000; Biro and Molnar, 2012). The formalism enables future calculations of transport coefficients and hydrodynamic evolution for strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where long-range correlations and memory effects are significant. The work also provides a foundation for computing q-modified Debye screening and effective coupling in hot QCD matter, extending studies of non-extensive effects on the equation of state (Lavagno, 2002; Gervino, 2012) and phase transitions (Lavagno, 2008).
