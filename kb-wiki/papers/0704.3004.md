# Four-Quark Condensates in Nucleon QCD Sum Rules

> The in-medium behavior of the nucleon spectral density including self-energies is revisited within the framework of QCD sum rules. Special emphasis is given to the density dependence of four-quark condensates. A complete catalog of four-quark condensates is presented and relations among them are derived. Generic differences of such four-quark condensates occurring in QCD sum rules for light baryons and light vector mesons are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the density dependence of four-quark condensates in nucleon QCD sum rules is poorly known and usually approximated by factorization, but a complete catalog of these condensates reveals that their structure differs generically from those appearing in light vector meson sum rules, which may explain the apparent contradiction between weak density dependence inferred from chiral effective field theory for nucleons and significant density dependence deduced from omega meson data.

## Method

The authors perform an operator product expansion (OPE) for the nucleon current-current correlator in cold nuclear matter, decomposing the correlator into scalar, vector, and quark-projected invariant functions. They derive the full set of sum rule equations including all condensates up to mass dimension 5 and explicitly write the four-quark condensate contributions, which are usually given only in the factorization approximation. A complete classification of independent four-quark condensates is presented using projection onto Dirac, Lorentz, and color structures under parity and time reversal invariance, with relations among them derived from Fierz transformations and the equivalence of two representations of the nucleon interpolating field.

## Key numbers

* The coefficients c_i for the mixing parameter t in the interpolating field are: c_1 = 7t^2 - 2t - 5, c_2 = -t^2 + 1, c_3 = 2t^2 - t - 1, c_4 = 5t^2 + 2t + 5, c_5 = 7t^2 + 10t + 7.
* The Borel-transformed sum rule equations contain terms up to Borel^6 (coefficient B_0 = c_4/(256 pi^4) E_2) and down to 1/Borel^2 (coefficient B_4).
* The continuum threshold factors are defined as E_0 = [1 - exp(-s_0/M^2)], E_1 = [1 - (1 + s_0/M^2) exp(-s_0/M^2)], E_2 = [1 - (1 + s_0/M^2 + s_0^2/(2 M^4)) exp(-s_0/M^2)], where s_0 = omega_+^2 - q_vec^2.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon QCD sum rule framework of Furnstahl (1992), Jin (1992, 1993), and Cohen (1994) for nucleons inside cold nuclear matter, and is also discussed in Henley (1993) and continuously explored by Drukarev and Sadovnikova (1988-2006). The authors previously found evidence for significant density dependence of four-quark condensates in omega meson sum rules (Thomas, 2005), based on the CB-TAPS collaboration measurement of omega spectral shift (Trnka, 2005). The present catalog enables direct comparison of four-quark condensate structures between baryon and vector meson sum rules, and provides the basis for testing whether the weak density dependence suggested by chiral effective field theory (Gross-Boelting, 1998) for nucleons is consistent with the explicit four-quark condensate contributions.
