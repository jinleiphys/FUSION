# Efimov Physics in small bosonic clusters

> We study small clusters of bosons, $A=2,3,4,5,6$, characterized by a resonant interaction. Firstly, we use a soft-gaussian interaction that reproduces the values of the dimer binding energy and the atom-atom scattering length obtained with LM2M2 potential, a widely used $^4$He-$^4$He interaction. We change the intensity of the potential to explore the clusters' spectra in different regions with large positive and large negative values of the two-body scattering length and we report the clusters' energies on Efimov plot, which makes the scale invariance explicit. Secondly, we repeat our calculation adding a repulsive three-body force to reproduce the trimer binding energy. In all the region explored, we have found that these systems present two states, one deep and one shallow close to the $A-1$ threshold, and scale invariance has been investigated for these states. The calculations are performed by means of Hyperspherical Harmonics basis set.

## Key claim

The authors show that for bosonic clusters with A=4, 5, and 6, each Efimov state in the three-body system produces two bound states (one deep and one shallow) across a wide range of scattering lengths, but this pattern depends on the inclusion of a repulsive three-body force and the excited state disappears for A=5 and A=6 at negative scattering lengths when such a force is added.

## Method

The authors used a soft-gaussian two-body potential tuned to reproduce the dimer binding energy and atom-atom scattering length of the LM2M2 helium-helium interaction, then varied the potential intensity to explore regions with large positive and negative scattering lengths. They solved the bound-state problem for A=3 using the modified LM2M2 potential, then fixed a hypercentral three-body force to reproduce the trimer energy, and diagonalized the Hamiltonian for A=3-6 using the Hyperspherical Harmonics basis set.

## Key numbers

- The LM2M2 helium-helium scattering length is a=189.41 a.u., with natural length ℓ=10.2 a.u.
- The two-body gaussian potential range is R0=10 a.u.
- The scaling factor for three identical bosons is λ = e^(π/s0) with s0=1.00624, giving λ ≈ 22.69; the plots are scaled to shrink the scale factor to √λ ≈ 4.8
- The modified LM2M2 potential strength λ was varied from λ=0.883 (where a = a^0_- = -43.84 a.u.) up to λ=1.1 (corresponding to a=44.79 a.u.)
- The unitary limit occurs at λ ≈ 0.9743
- The mass parameter used is ħ²/m = 43.281307 (a.u.)² K

## Context

This work extends previous analysis of A=4-6 bosonic spectra by Gattobigio et al. (2011) to the (a⁻¹,k) plane, and confirms predictions for A=4 tetramers attached to a trimer by Platter (2004), von Stecher (2009), and Deltuva (2011). The results enable further investigation of universal Efimov physics in larger bosonic clusters and the role of three-body forces in determining the number of bound states.
