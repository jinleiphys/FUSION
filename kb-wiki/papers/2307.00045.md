# Simple Hamiltonian for Quantum Simulation of Strongly Coupled 2+1D SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory on a Honeycomb Lattice

> We find a simple spin Hamiltonian to describe physical states of $2+1$ dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a honeycomb lattice with a truncation of the electric field representation at $j_{\rm max}=\frac{1}{2}$. The simple spin Hamiltonian only contains local products of Pauli matrices, even though Gauss's law has been completely integrated out.

# Simple Hamiltonian for Quantum Simulation of Strongly Coupled 2+1D SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory on a Honeycomb Lattice

## Key claim
The authors construct a simple 2D spin Hamiltonian that exactly describes 2+1D SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a honeycomb lattice, but only under the truncation of the electric field representation at j_max = 1/2. Gauss's law is completely integrated out while the resulting interactions remain local, which the abstract presents as a general feature but the full text clarifies applies only to this specific truncation and lattice geometry.

## Method
The Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for 2+1D SU(2) gauge theory is formulated on a honeycomb lattice, where each vertex connects exactly three links. The electric field Hilbert space is truncated to representations j = 0 and j = 1/2, yielding four possible vertex states satisfying Gauss's law. The physical Hilbert space is mapped bijectively onto a 2D spin model with N spins for N plaquettes, where plaquette operators become products of Pauli matrices. The resulting spin Hamiltonian contains only local products of Pauli matrices with coefficients determined by Wigner 6j-symbols evaluated at the truncated representations.

## Key numbers
- The electric energy coefficient for the spin Hamiltonian is h_+ = (27√3/8) a g^2, where a is the lattice spacing and g is the gauge coupling
- The nearest-neighbor interaction coefficient is h_{++} = (9√3/8) a g^2
- The magnetic field coefficient is h_x = 4√3/(9 a g^2)
- For periodic boundary conditions, the Ising coupling becomes J = -9√3 a g^2 / 32
- The dimension of the truncated Hilbert space for N plaquettes is 2^N
- The nonvanishing reduced matrix elements between vertex states are: ⟨j_A|M_V|j_0⟩ = ⟨j_0|M_V|j_A⟩ = ⟨j_B|M_V|j_C⟩ = -i, and ⟨j_C|M_V|j_B⟩ = i/2

## Context
This work extends the plaquette chain study of Yao (2023) to two spatial dimensions, providing a 2D generalization of the approach that integrates out Gauss's law while preserving locality. It contrasts with prior approaches that introduce gauge violation penalty terms (Halimeh 2019, 2021), use dynamical decoupling (Kasper 2020), or employ symmetry protection (Tran 2020), all of which have limitations the authors discuss. The resulting spin Hamiltonian enables quantum simulation on hardware without all-to-all connectivity and allows classical exact diagonalization for testing the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) in a non-Abelian gauge theory, which the authors plan to pursue in future work.
