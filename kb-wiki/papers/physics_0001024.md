# Scattering of positronium by H, He, Ne, and Ar

> The low-energy scattering of ortho positronium (Ps) by H, He, Ne, and Ar atoms has been investigated in the coupled-channel framework by using a recently proposed time-reversal-symmetric nonlocal electron-exchange model potential with a single parameter $C$. For H and He we use a three-Ps-state coupled-channel model and for Ar and Ne we use a static-exchange model. The sensitivity of the results is studied with respect to the parameter $C$. Present low-energy cross sections for He, Ne and Ar are in good agreement with experiment.

# Scattering of positronium by H, He, Ne, and Ar

## Key claim
The symmetric nonlocal electron-exchange model potential with a single parameter C, when tuned to C ≈ 0.785–0.85 rather than the originally suggested C = 1, reproduces accurate low-energy cross sections for He, Ne, and Ar and simultaneously yields the correct Ps-H resonance and binding energies. The abstract's claim of "good agreement with experiment" depends on this adjustment of C away from unity.

## Method
The authors solve momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger scattering equations for ortho-positronium (Ps) scattering using a coupled-channel framework with a time-reversal-symmetric nonlocal electron-exchange model potential containing a single adjustable parameter C. For H and He, a three-Ps-state model (Ps 1s, 2s, 2p) is employed; for Ne and Ar, a static-exchange model is used. The target atoms are described by exact wave functions (H) or Hartree-Fock wave functions (He, Ne, Ar), and the equations are solved by matrix inversion after partial-wave projection.

## Key numbers
- Ps-H singlet S-wave resonance position: 4.01 eV for C = 0.785, matching the accurate variational prediction of 4.01 eV (Ho, year not given).
- Ps-H binding energy: 0.99–1.02 eV for C = 0.785, compared to the accurate prediction of 1.0598–1.067 eV (Frolov and Smith, year not given).
- Ps-H resonance energy for C = 1.0: 4.715 eV; binding energy: 0.165 eV.
- Ps-H resonance energy for C = 0.9: 4.470 eV; binding energy: 0.445 eV.
- Ps-He scattering length (three-Ps-state model, C = 0.85): 0.90 a₀ (from Table I, k = 0 entry).
- Ps-Ne scattering length (static-exchange, C = 0.85): 1.41 a₀.
- Ps-Ar scattering length (static-exchange, C = 0.85): 1.65 a₀.
- S-wave phase shifts for Ps-He (three-Ps-state) at k = 0.8 au: −0.445 radians (Table I).
- P-wave phase shift for Ps-Ar at k = 0.8 au: −3.17 × 10⁻¹ radians (Table II).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier development of a nonlocal electron-exchange model potential for Ps scattering (Biswas and Adhikari, 1998; 1999) by applying the symmetric form to He, Ne, and Ar, and by systematically studying the sensitivity to the parameter C. Previous theoretical studies on Ps-He (e.g., by other groups cited as references 4a and 4 in the paper) produced results in strong disagreement with experiment. The present results agree with the recent measurements of Skalsey et al. (1998) and with unpublished work of G. Peach. The success of this simple single-parameter model across systems ranging from two electrons (Ps-H) to 19 electrons (Ps-Ar) suggests it can be reliably applied to further Ps scattering problems, including electron-impact scattering as demonstrated in the authors' prior work.
