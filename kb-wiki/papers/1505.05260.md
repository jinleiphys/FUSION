# Nuclear dynamics induced by antiprotons

> Reaction dynamics in collisions of antiprotons on nuclei is investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics model. The reaction channels of elastic scattering, annihilation, charge exchange and inelastic collisions of antiprotons on nucleons have been included in the model. Dynamics on particle production, in particular pions, kaons, antikaons and hyperons, is investigated in collisions of $\overline{p}$ on $^{12}$C, $^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ca and $^{181}$Ta from a low to high incident momenta. It is found that the annihilations of $\overline{p}$ on nucleons are of importance on the dynamics of particle production in phase space. Hyperons are mainly produced via meson induced reactions on nucleons and strangeness exchange collisions, which lead to the delayed emission in antiproton-nucleus collisions.

# Nuclear dynamics induced by antiprotons

## Key claim
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) model, extended to include antiproton-nucleon annihilation, charge exchange, elastic and inelastic channels, shows that hyperons in antiproton-nucleus collisions are produced predominantly via secondary meson-nucleon collisions and strangeness exchange reactions rather than direct antiproton-nucleon annihilation, leading to delayed hyperon emission; this mechanism dominates at incident momenta below the threshold for direct antihyperon-hyperon pair production (1.439 GeV/c).

## Method
The LQMD transport model was used to simulate antiproton collisions on 12C, 20Ne, 40Ca, and 181Ta targets across a range of incident momenta. The model includes hadron-hadron collisions, resonance decays, and mean-field potentials for baryons, antibaryons, and mesons, with antiproton-nucleon cross sections parametrized from experimental data and annihilation treated via a statistical model with SU(3) symmetry. Particle production (pions, kaons, antikaons, hyperons) was tracked temporally and in phase space to identify the dominant reaction channels.

## Key numbers
- The threshold momentum for direct antiproton-nucleon annihilation into antihyperon-hyperon pairs (p + N → Λ̅Λ) is 1.439 GeV/c.
- At saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) and zero momentum, the optical potentials are: -32 MeV for Λ and Σ, -16 MeV for Ξ, -164 MeV for antinucleons (N̅), -436 MeV for anti-Lambda (Λ̅), and -218 MeV for anti-Xi (Ξ̅).
- The kaon-nucleon potential is repulsive at +27.8 MeV and the antikaon-nucleon potential is attractive at -100.3 MeV at saturation density in isospin-symmetric matter.
- The effective mass ratio at normal baryon density is m*K/mK = 1.056 for kaons and m*K̅/mK̅ = 0.797 for antikaons.
- The antinucleon optical potential at zero momentum and normal density is -164 MeV, obtained with a scaling factor ξ = 0.25 applied to the relativistic mean-field NL3 parameter set.

## Context
This work extends the LQMD transport model (Feng 2014) to antiproton-nucleus collisions, building on earlier intranuclear cascade models (Cugnon 1982, Cugnon 1989) and kinetic approaches (Koch 1987) as well as the Giessen BUU (GiBUU) transport model (Gaitanos 2012, Larionov 2012). The results enable future extraction of the hyperon-nucleon potential from comparison with experimental kinetic energy or momentum spectra of hyperons, and provide a framework for studying hypernucleus formation in antiproton-induced reactions.
