# Bulk viscosity from Urca processes: $npeμ$ matter in the neutrino-trapped regime

> In this work, we extend our previous study of the bulk viscosity of hot and dense $npe$ matter induced by the Urca processes in the neutrino trapped regime to $npeμ$ matter by adding the muonic Urca processes as well as the purely leptonic electroweak processes involving electron-muon transition. The nuclear matter is modeled in a relativistic density functional approach with two different parametrizations which predict neutrino dominated matter (DDME2 model) and antineutrino dominated matter (NL3 model) at temperatures for which neutrinos/antineutrinos are trapped. In the case of neutrino-dominated matter, the main equilibration mechanism is lepton capture, whereas in the case of antineutrino-dominated matter this is due to neutron decay. We find that the equilibration rates of Urca processes are higher than that of the pure leptonic processes, which implies that the Urca-process-driven bulk viscosity can be computed with the leptonic reactions assumed to be frozen. We find that the bulk viscosity decreases with temperature as $ζ\sim T^{-2}$ at moderate temperatures. At high temperatures this scaling breaks down by sharp drops of the bulk viscosity close to the temperature where the proton fraction is density-independent and the matter becomes scale-invariant. This occurs also when the matter undergoes a transition from the antineutrino-dominated regime to the neutrino-dominated regime where the bulk viscosity attains a local maximum. We also estimate the bulk viscous dissipation timescales and find that these are in the range $\gtrsim$ 1 s for temperatures above the neutrino trapping temperature. These timescales would be relevant only for long-lived objects formed in binary neutron star mergers and hot proto-neutron stars formed in core-collapse supernovas.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of hot, dense $npe\mu$ matter in the neutrino-trapped regime is dominated by Urca processes (both electronic and muonic) because their equilibration rates are higher than those of purely leptonic electroweak processes; however, the bulk viscosity scaling $\zeta\sim T^{-2}$ at moderate temperatures breaks down at high temperatures near the point where the proton fraction becomes density-independent and the matter becomes scale-invariant, and also when the matter transitions from antineutrino-dominated to neutrino-dominated regimes.

## Method

The authors extended their previous relativistic density functional study of $npe$ matter to $npe\mu$ matter by including muonic Urca processes (neutron decay with muon emission and muon capture) and purely leptonic electroweak processes (muon decay, neutrino scattering, and antineutrino scattering). They used two parametrizations of the covariant density functional Lagrangian, DDME2 (density-dependent couplings, predicting neutrino-dominated matter) and NL3 (density-independent couplings with nonlinear scalar self-interactions, predicting antineutrino-dominated matter), to compute the weak interaction rates and bulk viscosity in the neutrino-trapped regime for baryon densities $0.5n_0 \leq n_B \leq 5n_0$ and temperatures from $T_{\rm tr}\simeq 5$ MeV up to about 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density $n_0 \simeq 0.152$ fm$^{-3}$ defines the density range studied ($0.5n_0 \leq n_B \leq 5n_0$).
* Neutrino trapping temperature $T_{\rm tr} \simeq 5$ MeV is the lower bound of the temperature range studied.
* Bulk viscosity scales as $\zeta \sim T^{-2}$ at moderate temperatures.
* Bulk viscous dissipation timescales are $\gtrsim 1$ s for temperatures above the neutrino trapping temperature.
* Lepton fractions used are $Y_{Le} = Y_{L\mu} = 0.1$ for binary neutron star mergers and $Y_{Le} = Y_{L\mu} = 0.4$ for proto-neutron stars and supernovae.
* Electron mass $m_{e^-} = 0.51$ MeV, muon mass $m_{\mu^-} = 105.7$ MeV, and neutrino masses $m_{\nu_e} = m_{\nu_\mu} = 0$.
* Fermi coupling constant $G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$, Cabibbo angle $\cos\theta_c = 0.974$, axial-vector coupling $g_A = 1.26$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of bulk viscosity in $npe$ matter (Alford 2019) to include muons, which appear significantly above nuclear saturation density and introduce new Urca processes and purely leptonic electroweak reactions. The results are relevant for understanding damping of oscillations in binary neutron star merger remnants and hot proto-neutron stars formed in core-collapse supernovae, where the neutrino-trapped regime applies. The study enables future work on bulk viscous damping in long-lived postmerger objects and complements earlier low-temperature studies of $npe$ and $npe\mu$ compositions (Sawyer 1979, Haensel 1992, Alford 2010) by focusing on the finite-temperature, neutrino-trapped regime with nonzero lepton chemical potentials.
