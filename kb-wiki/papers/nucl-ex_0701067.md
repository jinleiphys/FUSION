# Proton-proton physics in ALICE

> The ALICE experiment has several unique features which makes it an important contributor to proton-proton physics at the LHC, in addition to its specific design goal of studying the physics of strongly interacting matter in heavy-ion collisions. The unique capabilities include its low transverse momentum (\pT) acceptance, excellent vertexing, particle identification over a broad \pT range and jet reconstruction. In this report, a brief review of ALICE capabilities is given for studying bulk properties of produced particles which characterize the underlying events, and the physics of heavy-flavour, quarkonia, photons, di-leptons and jets.

# Proton-proton physics in ALICE

## Key claim

The ALICE experiment, despite being designed primarily for heavy-ion collisions, has unique capabilities that make it an important contributor to proton-proton physics at the LHC, particularly in the low transverse momentum region, but this paper is a review of planned capabilities and expected performance rather than a report of actual measurements.

## Method

The paper reviews the ALICE detector subsystems (Inner Tracking System, Time Projection Chamber, Time-Of-Flight, Transition Radiation Detector, PHOS calorimeter, EMCAL, forward muon arm, and trigger detectors) and describes their expected performance for proton-proton physics based on detailed simulations. It surveys planned measurements across multiple physics topics including pseudorapidity and multiplicity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, strangeness production, baryon number transport, heavy flavour and quarkonia production, jet reconstruction, and photon physics.

## Key numbers

- ALICE low transverse momentum acceptance reaches down to 100 MeV/c depending on particle type
- Momentum resolution ranges from 100 MeV/c to approximately 5% at 100 GeV/c
- Minimum bias trigger efficiency is approximately 98%
- PHOS calorimeter covers photon energies from 0.5 to 80 GeV with energy resolution of 2% above 3 GeV
- Forward muon arm covers pseudorapidity range 2.5 to 4 in eta
- Central barrel covers pseudorapidity of plus or minus 0.9 units
- Total proton-proton cross section at 14 TeV is 102 mb, with 76 mb for non-diffractive inelastic processes, 12 mb for single diffractive processes, and 1 mb for bottom-quark production
- LHC center-of-mass energy for proton-proton collisions is 14 TeV, with lead-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV

## Context

This paper extends the planning and simulation work for ALICE proton-proton physics described in the ALICE Physics Performance Reports (aliceppr1, aliceppr2) and the Les Houches physics workshop (2005). It describes how ALICE measurements will complement those of dedicated proton-proton experiments at the LHC, and enables future studies of non-perturbative QCD phenomena at low Bjorken-x values, multi-parton interactions, and parton saturation effects that were previously inaccessible at lower energy colliders such as the Tevatron and CERN SPS.
