# Threshold expansion of Feynman diagrams within a configuration space technique

> The near threshold expansion of generalized sunset-type (water melon) diagrams with arbitrary masses is constructed by using a configuration space technique. We present analytical expressions for the expansion of the spectral density near threshold and compare it with the exact expression obtained earlier using the method of the Hankel transform. We formulate a generalized threshold expansion with partial resummation of the small mass corrections for the strongly asymmetric case where one particle in the intermediate state is much lighter than the others.

# Threshold expansion of Feynman diagrams within a configuration space technique

## Key claim

The configuration space technique reduces the near-threshold expansion of generalized sunset-type (water melon) diagrams with arbitrary masses to purely algebraic operations and simple Gamma-function integrals, but the expansion is valid only up to a chosen order N, beyond which the subtracted term $\Pi^{di}(p)$ may contribute to the spectral density.

## Method

The authors start from the configuration-space representation of the polarization function $\Pi(x)$ as a product of massive scalar propagators (McDonald functions). They split the Fourier transform of $\Pi(x)$ into two parts using the decomposition of the Bessel function $J_\lambda$ into Hankel functions $H_\lambda^\pm$, where only the $H_\lambda^-$ part carries the non-analytic threshold behaviour. They replace both the polarization function and the Hankel kernel by their asymptotic series expansions at large $x$, reducing the integral to a sum of terms of the form $x^{-a}e^{-\Delta x}$ times a power series, which are integrated analytically using Euler's Gamma function. The discontinuity of the resulting expression across the cut on the negative $\Delta$ axis gives the spectral density near threshold.

## Key numbers

* For the equal-mass sunset diagram in $D=4$ dimensions, the spectral density expansion is $\tilde\rho(E) = \frac{E^2}{384\pi^3\sqrt 3} \left\{1 - \frac12\eta + \frac7{16}\eta^2 - \frac38\eta^3 + \frac{39}{128}\eta^4 - \frac{57}{256}\eta^5 + \frac{129}{1024}\eta^6 - \frac3{256}\eta^7 - \frac{4047}{32768}\eta^8 + \frac{18603}{65536}\eta^9 - \frac{248829}{524288}\eta^{10} + O(\eta^{11})\right\}$, where $\eta = E/M$ and $M=3m$.
* For the equal-mass sunset in $D=2$ dimensions, the exact spectral density is $\tilde\rho(E) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2(2m+E)\sqrt{(4m+E)^2-4m^2}} K(k^2)$ with $k^2 = \frac{((2m+E)^2-4m^2)(4m+E)^2}{((4m+E)^2-4m^2)(2m+E)^2}$ and $M=3m$.
* The exponent $a$ controlling the large-$x$ behaviour is $a = (n-1)(\lambda+1/2)$ for $n$ internal lines in $D=2\lambda+2$ dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the configuration-space method for water melon diagrams developed by the same authors in Groote and Pivovarov (1999) (hep-ph/9903310), which gave exact integral representations for the spectral density. The threshold expansion reproduces the coefficients obtained by Smirnov (1999) using the momentum-space technique of region separation from Beneke (1998). The method enables systematic threshold expansions for any number of internal lines with arbitrary masses, including the strongly asymmetric case where one mass is much lighter than the others, which is difficult to treat in momentum space. The technique can be generalized to other simple topologies of $n$-loop diagrams.
