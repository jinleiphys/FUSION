# Comment on the paper Eur. Phys. J. A (2019) 55:150

> The conclusions of the study published as Eur. Phys. J. A (2019) 55:150 questioning the adequacy of the recently proposed Gogny D1M* interaction for finite-nuclei calculations using harmonic oscillator (HO) basis are revised. The existence of an instability in finite nuclei for D1M* when coordinate-space methods are used to solve the HF equations (as shown in Eur. Phys. J. A(2019) 55:150) is independently confirmed using a computer code based on a quasi-local approximation (QLA) to the HF energy density with finite-range forces. We confirm that the most affected quantity in the coordinate-space calculation is the spatial density at the origin. Our study reveals that some findings concerning these instabilities are not easy to reconcile with the arguments used in Eur. Phys. J. A (2019) 55:150. For instance, some nuclei such as $^4$He and $^{40}$Ca, which diverge in HF mesh-point calculations performed with D1M*, become perfectly stable when Coulomb force is switched off. We have also found instabilities in some nuclei when the D1M interaction is used. Finally, a connection between the occupancy of $s$-orbitals near the Fermi level and the appearance of instabilities is observed. Several convergence and stability studies are performed with HO basis of different sizes and oscillator parameters to demonstrate the robustness of the D1M* results for finite nuclei when the HO basis is used.

## Key claim

The authors independently confirm that the Gogny D1M* interaction exhibits finite-size instabilities in coordinate-space HF calculations, but argue that the instability criterion based on nuclear-matter critical density (proposed in Martini et al., 2019) is insufficient, because the D1M interaction also shows instabilities in some nuclei despite having a critical density above the threshold, and because switching off the Coulomb force stabilizes nuclei like ⁴He and ⁴⁰Ca even though their central densities remain nearly unchanged.

## Method

The authors performed spherical HF calculations on a spatial mesh using a quasi-local approximation (QLA) to the HF energy density with finite-range Gogny forces, which makes the energy density functional local and the HF equations similar to those of Skyrme forces. They compared these results with full coordinate-space calculations using the FINRES₄ code and with harmonic oscillator (HO) basis calculations using the HFBaxial code, varying the number of HO shells from 11 to 20 and adjusting oscillator lengths. They also tested the effect of switching off the Coulomb interaction and examined the role of s-orbital occupancy near the Fermi level.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for D1M* is about 1.2 ρ₀ ≈ 0.20 fm⁻³ for a momentum transfer of about 2.5 fm⁻¹ (from Martini et al., 2019).
- The critical density for D1M is about 1.35 ρ₀ (from Martini et al., 2019).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with D1M, the HO-basis binding energy is 1636.08 MeV, the QLA result is 1637.96 MeV (0.11% deviation), and the FINRES₄ result is 1639.31 MeV (0.20% deviation).
- For ¹⁶O with D1M*, the HO-basis binding energy is 128.32 MeV, the QLA result is 127.29 MeV (0.81% deviation), and the FINRES₄ result is 128.58 MeV (0.21% deviation).
- For ¹⁰⁰Sn with D1M*, the HO-basis binding energy is 827.98 MeV, the QLA result is 824.71 MeV (0.40% deviation), and the FINRES₄ result is 829.08 MeV (0.13% deviation).
- For ¹⁷⁶Sn with D1M*, the HO-basis binding energy is 1146.15 MeV, the QLA result is 1146.26 MeV (0.01% deviation), and the FINRES₄ result is 1147.51 MeV (0.12% deviation).
- HF calculations with D1M* for N=Z nuclei from ²He to ¹²⁶Sn without Coulomb were stable after about 10000 iterations with a mixing factor of 0.9.

## Context

This Comment responds to Martini et al. (2019), which claimed that the D1M* and D1N Gogny interactions suffer from spurious finite-size instabilities detected via RPA nuclear-matter response functions and coordinate-space HF calculations. The present work extends the analysis by showing that the D1M interaction (from Goriely et al., 2009) also produces instabilities in some nuclei despite having a critical density above the proposed threshold, and that the instability correlates with the occupancy of s-orbitals near the Fermi level rather than solely with central density. The authors argue that the HO basis acts as a UV regulator, enabling consistent finite-nuclei calculations with D1M* across a wide range of basis sizes, and call for further investigation into the RPA in finite nuclei with finite-range interactions, as suggested by Pastore (2015).
