# Generalized parton distributions of few body systems

> The relevance of measuring Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) for few nucleon systems is illustrated. An approach which permits to calculate the GPDs of hadrons made of composite constituents by proper convolutions is described. The application of the method to the nucleon target, assumed to be made of composite constituents is reviewed. Calculations of GPDs for few nucleon systems are summarized, with special emphasis to the $^3$He target.

# Generalized parton distributions of few body systems

## Key claim
Measuring Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) for few nucleon systems, particularly $^3$He, can reveal the interplay of nucleon and parton degrees of freedom at short light-like distances, but the realistic calculation yields results that differ significantly from simple parameterizations of nuclear GPDs, especially at larger values of the skewedness $\xi$ and momentum transfer $\Delta^2$.

## Method
The author reviews an Impulse Approximation (IA) framework in which GPDs of hadrons made of composite constituents are calculated as convolutions between the light-cone non-diagonal momentum distribution of the hadron and the GPD of the constituent. For the $^3$He target, a realistic non-diagonal spectral function is calculated using AV18 wave functions, and the nucleon GPD input is modeled via the Double Distribution representation with the $\Delta^2$-dependence given by the quark contribution to the nucleon form factor.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear effects in $^3$He GPDs are as large as 15% at most for $x_3 \leq 0.7$.
- The hadronic scale used for the model is $\mu_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$, with NLO QCD evolution applied up to $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ for the unpolarized case.
- Results for $H_q^3$ are shown at $\Delta^2 = -0.15$ GeV$^2$ and $\Delta^2 = -0.25$ GeV$^2$, with skewedness values $\xi_3 = 3\xi$ in the range relevant to coherent DVCS off $^3$He.
- The formalism is valid for $\Delta^2 \ll Q^2, M^2$ and small $\xi^2$, with terms of order $O(\xi^2)$ neglected.

## Context
This work extends the study of GPDs from the nucleon target, previously modeled using the Isgur and Karl model for constituent quarks (Isgur and Karl, 1980s) and the double distribution representation (Radyushkin, 1990s), to few-body nuclear systems. The deuteron target was studied earlier by Cano and others (2000s), who found the coherent DVCS cross section comparable to that of the proton. The $^3$He target is particularly valuable because it serves as an effective polarized free neutron target (Friar, 1990s) and allows realistic calculations that can distinguish conventional nuclear effects from exotic ones. The results enable future calculations of helicity-dependent GPDs and cross-section estimates for DVCS off $^3$He.
