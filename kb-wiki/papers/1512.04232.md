# Collective and Single-particle Motion in Beyond Mean Field Approaches

> We present a novel nuclear energy density functional method to calculate spectroscopic properties of atomic nuclei. Intrinsic nuclear quadrupole deformations and rotational frequencies are considered simultaneously as the degrees of freedom within a symmetry conserving configuration mixing framework. The present method allows the study of nuclear states with collective and single-particle character. We calculate the fascinating structure of the semi-magic 44S nucleus as a first application of the method, obtaining an excellent quantitative agreement both with the available experimental data and with state-of-the-art shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The authors present a symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) method that includes both the cranking frequency and an extended triaxial quadrupole deformation range, which they claim largely solves the spectrum-stretching problem of previous beyond-mean-field approaches and introduces single-particle effects through pair alignment. The method achieves "excellent quantitative agreement" with experimental data and shell-model calculations for the specific case of ⁴⁴S, though the authors note the computational cost increases by at least two orders of magnitude.

## Method

The method combines the generator coordinate method (GCM) with particle-number and angular-momentum projection, using the quadrupole deformations (β, γ) and the cranking frequency ħω as generator coordinates. The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions are determined by minimizing an energy functional that includes constraints on quadrupole moments and the cranking term -ωĴₓ, with the Gogny D1S interaction used in a configuration space of eight harmonic oscillator shells. The triaxial deformation range is extended to -60° ≤ γ ≤ 120° (beyond the usual 0° ≤ γ ≤ 60°) to account for time-reversal symmetry breaking introduced by the cranking term.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁴S, three values of the angular frequency are used: ħω = 0.0, 0.75, and 1.25 MeV
- 70 points in the (β, γ) plane are taken for each ħω value, with 0 ≤ β ≤ 0.7 and -60° ≤ γ ≤ 120°
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moments of band heads: Q_spec(2⁺₁) = -14.4 e·fm², Q_spec(2⁺₂) = 6.5 e·fm², Q_spec(4⁺₂) = 26.9 e·fm², Q_spec(2⁺₃) = -13.8 e·fm²
- B(E2) transition probabilities from the present work: B(E2; 6⁺₁ → 4⁺₁) = 153 e²fm⁴, B(E2; 4⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) = 125 e²fm⁴, B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 87 e²fm⁴
- The 4⁺₂ state decays with B(E2) = 1.4 e²fm⁴ to the 2⁺₁ state and B(E2) = 20 e²fm⁴ to the 2⁺₂ state
- Estimated lifetime of the 4⁺₂ state: 84 ps (compared to experimental value of about 50 ps)
- For the 6⁺₂ state, the K_X = 6 component amounts to 76% in the |I K_X⟩ basis

## Context

This work extends previous SCCM calculations that included only quadrupole deformations with angular-momentum projection after variation (PAV), which suffered from a stretched spectrum as noted by the authors' earlier work (Rodríguez and Egido, 2011) and by other studies (PRL 99 062501, 2007). The method improves upon cranked HF and HFB plus projection approaches (e.g., PRC 76 044304, 2007; HHR; ETY) and recent GCM calculations (Borrajo et al., 2015) that considered only the collective sextant 0° ≤ γ ≤ 60°. The results for ⁴⁴S are compared with state-of-the-art shell-model calculations from the Madrid-Strasbourg collaboration (PRC 83 061305, 2011; PRC 85 024311, 2012) using the SDPF-U interaction and from the Tokyo group (Otsuka et al., 2015) using the SDPF-MU interaction, as well as with the authors' own earlier work without the cranking frequency (Rodríguez and Egido, 2011). The approach enables the study of aligned configurations and high-K bands previously inaccessible in density-functional-based beyond-mean-field methods.
