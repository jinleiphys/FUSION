# Bremsstrahlung from Electrons and Positrons in Peripheral Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the spectrum of the bremsstrahlung photons coming from the electrons and positrons, which are produced in the strong electromagnetic fields present in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions. We compare different approaches, making use of the exact pair production cross section in heavy ion collisions as well as the double equivalent photon approximation.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung from the produced electron-positron pairs, rather than from the heavy ions themselves, is the dominant mechanism for photon emission in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions; the double equivalent photon approximation (DEPA) can reproduce the exact pair-production-based calculation only if the cutoff parameter R is adjusted to about 100 fm, which is larger than the conventionally used Compton wavelength of the electron.

## Method

The authors calculated the soft bremsstrahlung cross section from electron-positron pairs produced in peripheral heavy ion collisions using three methods: (1) the infrared (IR) approximation applied to the exact lowest-order pair production cross section in the external field approximation, with a restriction condition based on the invariant mass of the lepton pair; (2) the double equivalent photon approximation (DEPA), folding the exact QED cross section for gamma+gamma -> e+e-+gamma with the equivalent photon spectra of the two ions; and (3) the IR approximation applied to the gamma+gamma -> e+e- subprocess within the DEPA framework. The calculations were performed for RHIC (Au-Au, gamma=100) and LHC (Pb-Pb, gamma=3400) conditions.

## Key numbers

* The cross section for electron-positron pair production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is of the order of 100 kbarn for RHIC and LHC.
* The majority of the leptons are produced with an invariant mass of the order of 2–5 m_e.
* For RHIC conditions (Au-Au, gamma=100), the DEPA calculations with the IR approximation (method 2 and 3) agree within a factor of two; without the restriction condition they would differ by more than a factor of 100 at 3 MeV and 90 degrees.
* The cutoff parameter R was adjusted to approximately 100 fm to achieve fair agreement between the DEPA and exact pair production methods.
* For the photon-photon center-of-mass frame comparison, the bremsstrahlung photon energy was chosen as omega = 1 MeV.
* The equivalent photon spectrum for heavy ions is approximately n(omega) = (2 Z^2 alpha / pi) ln(gamma / (omega R)), and for large gamma simplifies to n(omega) = (2 Z^2 alpha / pi) ln(gamma).
* At RHIC, gamma = 100; at LEP, gamma = 200,000 (E_el = 100 GeV); at LHC, gamma = 3400.
* The analytic formula for high energies (K_perp = omega sin(theta) >> m_e) gives dsigma/dOmega domega proportional to 1/K_perp^4; for low energies (omega << m_e) the cross section has a 1/omega dependence and scales as 1/(m_e^2).

## Context

This work extends the study of bremsstrahlung in peripheral heavy ion collisions beyond the previously considered emission from the heavy ions themselves, which was found to be small by Bertulani and Baur (1988) for real photons and by Meier, Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1998) for virtual photons. The authors compare their results to the analytic expressions of Fadin and Kuraev (1973) for e+e- colliders, finding that the heavy-ion case requires a modified equivalent photon spectrum due to the lower gamma values. The calculated low-energy bremsstrahlung photons constitute a potential background for detectors at relativistic heavy ion colliders and could also interfere with proposed measurements of soft bremsstrahlung from central collisions suggested by Jeon, Koch, and Srivastava (1998).
