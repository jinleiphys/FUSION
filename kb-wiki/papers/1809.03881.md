# Energy dependent kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity in high energy collisions

> Transverse momentum spectra of negative and positive pions produced at mid-(pseudo)rapidity in inelastic or non-single-diffractive proton-proton collisions and in central nucleus-nucleus collisions over an energy range from a few GeV to above 10 TeV are analyzed by a (two-component) blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics and with Tsallis statistics respectively. The model results are in similarly well agreement with the experimental data measured by a few productive collaborations who work at the Heavy Ion Synchrotron (SIS), Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS), Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), and Large Hadron Collider (LHC), respectively. The energy dependent kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity are obtained and analyzed. Both the quantities have quick increase from the SIS to SPS, and slight increase or approximate invariability from the top RHIC to LHC. Around the energy bridge from the SPS to RHIC, the considered quantities in proton-proton collisions obtained by the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics show more complex energy dependent behavior comparing with the results in other three cases.

# Energy dependent kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity in high energy collisions

## Key claim
The kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity extracted from pion transverse momentum spectra show a quick increase from SIS to SPS energies, then a slight increase or approximate invariability from top RHIC to LHC energies; however, in proton-proton collisions analyzed with the blast-wave model using Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, the energy dependence shows a more complex behavior around the SPS-to-RHIC transition region, including a hill near 10 GeV, a drop at dozens of GeV, and a subsequent increase.

## Method
The authors fitted transverse momentum spectra of negative and positive pions produced at mid-(pseudo)rapidity using a two-component blast-wave model with either Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics or Tsallis statistics. The first component (soft process, low pT region) provided the kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 and transverse flow velocity beta_T. Data from inelastic or non-single-diffractive proton-proton collisions and central nucleus-nucleus collisions were analyzed across energies from the SIS (few GeV) to LHC (above 10 TeV), using measurements from the FOPI, NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, and CMS Collaborations.

## Key numbers
- In proton-proton collisions with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (case I), T1 values range from 108 +/- 5 MeV at sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV (NA61/SHINE, pi-) to 110 +/- 5 MeV at sqrt(s)=13000 GeV (CMS, pi+); beta_T1 ranges from 0.30 +/- 0.02 c to 0.37 +/- 0.02 c over the same energy range.
- In central nucleus-nucleus collisions with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (case III), T1 values range from 43 +/- 2 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=2.24 GeV (FOPI Au-Au, pi-) to 132 +/- 6 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (STAR Au-Au, pi-); beta_T1 ranges from 0.27 +/- 0.01 c to 0.39 +/- 0.02 c.
- In proton-proton collisions with Tsallis statistics (case II), T1 values range from 80 +/- 4 MeV at sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV (NA61/SHINE, pi-) to 86 +/- 4 MeV at sqrt(s)=13000 GeV (CMS, pi+); beta_T1 ranges from 0.18 +/- 0.01 c to 0.28 +/- 0.02 c; the entropy index q ranges from 1.032 +/- 0.001 to 1.055 +/- 0.007.
- In central nucleus-nucleus collisions with Tsallis statistics (case IV), T1 values range from 27 +/- 1 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=2.24 GeV (FOPI Au-Au, pi-) to 87 +/- 4 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=2760 GeV (ALICE Pb-Pb, pi-); beta_T1 ranges from 0.16 +/- 0.01 c to 0.35 +/- 0.02 c; q ranges from 1.042 +/- 0.012 to 1.083 +/- 0.014.
- At approximately 11 GeV, the kinetic freeze-out temperature in proton-proton collisions is T0 approximately 115 MeV and in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is T0 approximately 120 MeV, obtained from the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave model.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on energy-dependent freeze-out parameters (Author references 12-20) which showed contradictory trends (increase, decrease, or invariance) from RHIC to LHC energies. The analysis covers a wider energy range from SIS to LHC than most prior work, and systematically compares four cases: proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions with both Boltzmann-Gibbs and Tsallis statistics. The special behavior near 10-11 GeV in proton-proton collisions with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics is linked to the energy where the highest net baryon density occurs and where a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated final states takes place, as noted by Cleymans (reference 45). The results enable systematic comparisons of freeze-out parameters across collision systems and statistical treatments, and identify the SPS-to-RHIC energy bridge as a region requiring further theoretical attention.
