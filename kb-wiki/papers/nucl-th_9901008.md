# Three-body Faddeev Calculation for 11Li with Separable Potentials

> The halo nucleus $^{11}$Li is treated as a three-body system consisting of an inert core of $^{9}$Li plus two valence neutrons. The Faddeev equations are solved using separable potentials to describe the two-body interactions, corresponding in the n-$^{9}$Li subsystem to a p$_{1/2}$ resonance plus a virtual s-wave state. The experimental $^{11}$Li energy is taken as input and the $^{9}$Li transverse momentum distribution in $^{11}$Li is studied.

# Three-body Faddeev Calculation for 11Li with Separable Potentials

## Key claim

The halo nucleus 11Li can be described as a three-body system (9Li core plus two valence neutrons) using separable potentials in the Faddeev formalism, but only if the effective neutron-9Li potential differs appreciably from the usual single-particle potential, specifically, the parameter β0 must take values not in accordance with the harmonic oscillator prescription β0 = 1/√(mω).

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Faddeev equations for the 11Li three-body system using separable potentials for the n-9Li and neutron-neutron interactions. The n-9Li interaction was modeled with separable potentials in the s1/2, p3/2, and p1/2 partial waves, including a p1/2 resonance and a virtual s-wave state, with Pauli blocking of the occupied 1s1/2 and 1p3/2 states handled via the Kukulin projection method. The neutron-neutron interaction was taken as a separable potential acting in both the 1S0 and 3P1 channels with Yamaguchi form factors. The experimental 11Li two-neutron separation energy was taken as input, and the transverse momentum distribution of the 9Li fragment was calculated from the resulting three-body wave function.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy S2n = 0.293 MeV (calculation I), compared to experimental value 0.294 ± 0.030 MeV from Tanihata (1996)
- Half width at half maximum (HWHM) of the 9Li transverse momentum distribution: 82 MeV/c (calculation I), 40 MeV/c (calculation II), 35 MeV/c (calculations III and IV)
- n-9Li p1/2 resonance energy Er = 0.42 ± 0.05 MeV with width Γ = 0.15 ± 0.07 MeV from Bohlen et al. (1993); calculated widths ranged from 0.12 to 0.20 MeV
- n-9Li s1/2 virtual state at εv = -40 keV with scattering length as1/2 = -20 fm (calculations I-III) or -40 fm (calculation IV)
- n-n 1S0 scattering length a = -17 fm and effective range r = 2.84 fm; 3P1 scattering length a = 2.2 fm³ and effective range r = -8.0 fm⁻¹
- Fractional admixture of n-n states in 11Li wave function for best-fit case (III): 1S0 = 0.54, 3P1 = 0.41, 1D2 = 0.03, 3F3 = 0.02

## Context

This work extends the three-body cluster model approach to 11Li previously pursued by Johannsen, Jensen, and Hansen (1990), Zhukov et al. (1991), Bang and Thompson (1992), and Thompson and Zhukov (1994), who used local potentials for the n-9Li interaction. The authors' earlier separable potential formalism, effective for 18O, 18F, and 16O(d,p) stripping (Ueta, Miyake, and Mizukami 1983; Bund and Ueta 1995), is here applied to the Borromean halo nucleus 11Li. The results enable future work using local n-9Li potentials to test whether the required deviation from standard single-particle potentials indicates that a more detailed description beyond the inert-core approximation is needed.
