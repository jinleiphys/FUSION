# (Non)-Renormalization of the Chiral Vortical Effect Coefficient

> We show using diagramtic arguments that in some (but not all) cases, the temperature dependent part of the chiral vortical effect coefficient is independent of the coupling constant. An interpretation of this result in terms of quantization in the effective 3 dimensional Chern-Simons theory is also given. In the language of 3D dimensionally reduced theory, the value of the chiral vortical coefficient is related to the formula $\sum_{n=1}^\infty n=-1/12$. We also show that in the presence of dynamical gauge fields, the CVE coefficient is not protected from renormalization, even in the large $N$ limit.

# (Non)-Renormalization of the Chiral Vortical Effect Coefficient

## Key claim

The temperature-dependent part of the chiral vortical effect (CVE) coefficient is independent of the coupling constant for Yukawa-type interactions, but this non-renormalization does **not** hold when dynamical gauge fields are present, even in the large N limit.

## Method

The authors use diagrammatic arguments analogous to the Coleman-Hill theorem for Chern-Simons terms in 3D, analyzing Euclidean (Matsubara) Green's functions at zero frequency to extract the CVE coefficient from the retarded two-point function between the axial current and momentum density. For the gauge field case, they split diagrams into fermionic and gauge energy-momentum tensor classes, replacing the anomalous triangle subdiagram with an effective vertex to compute the leading correction at two-loop order in the large N expansion.

## Key numbers

- For a single right-handed fermion at zero coupling, the CVE coefficient is C1 = 1/12
- The leading correction to the CVE coefficient from dynamical gauge fields in the large N limit is σ^V_Correction = (g^2 C(r) d(G))/(48π^2) T^2, where C(r) is the quadratic Casimir and d(G) is the dimension of the adjoint representation
- The quantization condition from large gauge transformations on a 3-torus gives κ ∈ (e/2) Z, where e is the electric charge
- The contribution from each Matsubara mode m to the CVE coefficient is σ^V_A,m = |m| T

## Context

This work extends the holographic findings of Landsteiner et al. (2008, 2011) that the CVE coefficient at strong coupling coincides with its zero-coupling value, providing a perturbative proof for Yukawa interactions. It contradicts the expectation of universal non-renormalization by showing that dynamical gauge fields produce radiative corrections, unlike the case of scalar couplings. The connection to 3D Chern-Simons theory and the quantization argument builds on the dimensional reduction approach of Banerjee et al. (2012). The result enables further investigation into whether global anomalies, as discussed by Witten (1983, 1985), might fix the CVE coefficient despite the failure of perturbative non-renormalization.
