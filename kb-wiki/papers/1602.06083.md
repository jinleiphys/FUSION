# Charge-changing transitions and capture strengths of pf-shell nuclei with $T_{z} = -2$ at proton drip-line

> Charge-changing transitions, commonly referred to as Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions, and electron capture/$β^{+}$-decay strengths for pf-shell nuclei with $T_{z} = - 2$ at proton drip-line have been calculated using the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model. The total GT$_{+}$ strength values and electron capture/$β^{+}$-decay rates are needed for the study of the late stages of the stellar evolution. The pn-QRPA theory is used for a microscopic calculation of GT strength distribution functions and associated stellar electron capture/$β^{+}$-decay rates of proton-rich pf-shell nuclei with $T_{z} = - 2$ in the mass range 46 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 56 at proton drip-line. Standard quenching factor of 0.74, usually implemented in the shell model calculation, has been incorporated for the comparison with experimental data (wherever available). The calculated GT strength of the two proton-rich nuclei, $^{52}$Ni and $^{56}$Zn are compared with experimental data of corresponding mirror nuclei. It has been found that the pn-QRPA results are in good agreement with the experimental data as well as shell model result. It is noted that the total GT strength increases linearly with the increase of mass number. The electron capture/$β^{+}$-decay rates for proton-rich nuclei are calculated on a temperature and density scale relevant to presupernova evolution of massive stars. The $β^{+}$ decay half-lives are compared with measured and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model produces Gamow-Teller strength distributions and electron capture/beta-plus decay rates for proton-rich pf-shell nuclei with Tz = -2 that are in reasonable agreement with shell model calculations and available experimental data, but the agreement is qualified by the use of a standard quenching factor of 0.74 and the comparison is made against mirror-nuclei data where direct experimental data for the proton-rich nuclei themselves are unavailable.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a 7-hbar-omega model space to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the isospin raising direction for five proton drip-line nuclei (46Mn, 48Fe, 50Co, 52Ni, 56Zn) in the mass range 46 ≤ A ≤ 56. Single-particle energies and wave functions were computed using the Nilsson model with nuclear deformations, pairing was treated in the BCS approximation, and both particle-hole and particle-particle GT residual interactions were included with separable forms characterized by interaction constants kappa and chi. The effective axial-vector to vector coupling ratio was taken as 0.74 times the bare value of -1.257, and electron capture and beta-plus decay rates were calculated on a temperature and density grid relevant to presupernova stellar evolution.

## Key numbers

- Total unquenched GT strength for 46Mn: 12.45 (pn-QRPA) compared to 12.89 (shell model); quenched strength: 6.82 (pn-QRPA) compared to 6.98 (shell model)
- Total unquenched GT strength for 48Fe: 12.62 (pn-QRPA); quenched strength: 6.91 (pn-QRPA) compared to 7.18 (shell model)
- Ground state mass excess of 48Fe: (-18160 ± 70) keV
- Isobaric analog state energies: 25.06 MeV (46Mn), 19.38 MeV (48Fe), 25.75 MeV (50Co), 19.99 MeV (52Ni), 22.09 MeV (56Zn)
- Q-values for electron capture: 17.10 MeV (46Mn), 11.16 MeV (48Fe), 17.28 MeV (50Co), 11.27 MeV (52Ni), 12.87 MeV (56Zn)
- The pn-QRPA result for total GT strength in the A = 56 system agrees with the upper limit of the total strength observed in (p,n) charge-exchange reactions on the mirror nucleus 56Fe

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA approach previously applied by Nabi and collaborators (1999, 2009, 2012, 2013) to calculate weak interaction rates for proton-rich nuclei at the proton drip-line, a region made experimentally accessible by advances at radioactive ion beam facilities such as GANIL. The results are compared with shell model calculations by Caurier and collaborators (1998) using the KB3 interaction, and with the earlier parameterized approach of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980-1985) which the authors note placed GT centroids at incorrect excitation energies. The calculated rates are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulation codes and rp-process nucleosynthesis studies up to mass number 110 as called for by Pruet and collaborators (2003).
