# Pion decay in magnetic fields

> The leptonic decay of the charged pion in the presence of background magnetic fields is investigated using quenched Wilson fermions. It is demonstrated that the magnetic field opens up a new channel for this decay. The magnetic field-dependence of the decay constants for both the ordinary and the new channel is determined. Using these inputs from QCD, we calculate the total decay rate perturbatively.

# Pion decay in magnetic fields

## Key claim
The magnetic field opens a new decay channel for charged pions via an additional decay constant f' that has been ignored in prior literature; the total decay rate is enhanced by up to two orders of magnitude for muonic decay at fields around eB ≈ 0.3 GeV², but this result relies on the lowest Landau-level approximation which is only valid for eB ≫ m_ℓ².

## Method
The authors performed a tree-level perturbative calculation of the weak decay π⁻ → ℓ⁻ ν̄_ℓ in a background magnetic field using the lowest Landau-level approximation for the outgoing charged lepton, valid when eB ≫ m_ℓ². They determined the two independent decay constants f_π and f'_π non-perturbatively using quenched Wilson fermion lattice simulations on three lattice spacings (0.124 fm, 0.093 fm, 0.062 fm) with pion masses ranging from 415 MeV to 770 MeV and magnetic fields 0 ≤ eB < 1.5 GeV². The correlators C_PP, C_AP, and C_VP were simultaneously fitted to extract the pion mass and decay constants.

## Key numbers
- At B=0, the charged pion decay constant is f_π ≈ 130 MeV (vacuum value with physical quark masses)
- The linear coefficient for the new decay constant is f'_π/f_π ≈ 0.8 GeV⁻² at low magnetic fields
- For eB ≈ 0.3 GeV², the electronic decay rate enhances by a factor ~20 relative to Γ(0)
- For eB ≈ 0.3 GeV², the muonic decay rate enhances by more than two orders of magnitude relative to Γ(0)
- The ratio Γ(π→e ν̄_e)/Γ(π→μ ν̄_μ) = m_e²/m_μ² ≈ 2.27 × 10⁻⁵ for eB ≫ m_μ², independent of magnetic field
- The B=0 pion masses studied were 415 MeV, 628 MeV, and 770 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.124 fm (12³×36), 0.093 fm (16³×48), 0.062 fm (24³×72)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of charged pion masses in magnetic fields using quenched Wilson fermions (Bali 2017) and quenched overlap quarks (Luschevskaya 2014), as well as chiral perturbation theory predictions for f_π(B) (Andersen 2012). The new channel and decay constant f'_π had been ignored in all previous literature. The results enable predictions for pion decay rates in magnetars and heavy-ion collision scenarios, and the ratio Γ(e)/Γ(μ) being independent of B provides a robust testable prediction irrespective of lattice uncertainties.
