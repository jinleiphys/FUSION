# The Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule reexamined

> We review the status of a Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule for the photoproduction of pions from nucleons, indicating how well this sum rule is satisfied using a recent analysis of pion photoproduction data. Our results are discussed in light of a recent paper comparing the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and spin-dependent polarizability sum rules.

## Key claim

The Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti (FFR) sum rule for the isovector anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon is reasonably well satisfied by recent single-pion photoproduction data, but the isoscalar component yields only about 25% of its predicted value in one analysis and remains less certain, with the sum rule not being exact due to its reliance on the Goldberger-Treiman relation (PCAC).

## Method

The authors evaluated the FFR sum rule integral, which relates nucleon anomalous magnetic moments to an integral over the invariant amplitude \(A_1\) for single-pion photoproduction, using two multipole analyses: a 1993 analysis and a more recent analysis extended to 2 GeV and improved through critical examination of the entire photoproduction database. The isovector and isoscalar combinations of the integral were computed from charge-channel data for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 p\) and \(\gamma n \to \pi^0 n\), with the latter inferred from measurements in other charge channels.

## Key numbers

- For the isovector anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa^V\), the integral gave a value of 1.96, close to the predicted value (exact agreement not expected).
- For the isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa^S\), the 1993 analysis gave only about 25% of the predicted value; the more recent analysis changed \(\kappa^S\) from \(-0.015\) to \(-0.069\), compared to the predicted value \((\kappa_p + \kappa_n)/2 \approx -0.06\).
- The analysis was extended to 2 GeV to regularize results at the end-point energy of 1.8 GeV.

## Context

This work reexamines a sum rule from Fubini, Furlan, and Rossetti (1960s) in light of renewed interest in chiral perturbation theory and extended current algebra, following a reexamination of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule by Chang and Liang. The results are compared to a recent paper by Sandorfi et al. (1995) that compared the GDH and spin-dependent polarizability sum rules; the isoscalar FFR integral provides a test of single-pion production input to the GDH sum rule, and the findings weaken arguments requiring large discrepancies in single-pion multipoles to explain the GDH discrepancy.
