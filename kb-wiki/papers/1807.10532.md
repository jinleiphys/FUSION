# Analysis of the MINERvA antineutrino double-differential cross sections within the SuSAv2 model including meson-exchange currents

> We compare the results of the SuSAv2 model including meson-exchange currents (MEC) with the recent measurement of the quasielastic-like double differential antineutrino cross section on hydrocarbon (CH) performed by the MINERvA Collaboration. The relativistic nature of the model makes it suitable to describe these data, which correspond to a mean beam energy of 3.5 GeV. The standard SuSAv2 model predictions agree well with the data without needing any additional or tuned parameter. The role of longitudinal MEC is non-negligible and improves the agreement with the data. We also consider the impact of different treatments of the $Δ$-resonance propagator in the two-body currents on the data comparison.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2 model including meson-exchange currents (MEC) provides a good description of the MINERvA antineutrino double-differential cross section data on hydrocarbon without any additional or tuned parameters, but the agreement is quantified as slightly worse than the tuned GENIE model (chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.79 versus 1.58).

## Method

The authors compared the SuSAv2 model, which incorporates relativistic mean field (RMF) dynamics with a smooth transition to relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) at high momentum transfer, to the MINERvA measurement of the quasielastic-like double differential antineutrino cross section on hydrocarbon (CH) at a mean beam energy of 3.5 GeV. The model includes two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) excitations from meson exchange currents treated within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, and the authors examined the effect of using either the real part only or the full complex Delta-resonance propagator in the two-body currents. The theoretical predictions were compared to both the "CCQE" and "QE-like" data sets from the MINERvA collaboration, with the muon kinematics restricted to the experimental acceptance (muon scattering angle less than 20 degrees, muon longitudinal momentum between 1.5 and 15 GeV, transverse momentum below 1.5 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Mean beam energy of the MINERvA antineutrino flux: 3.5 GeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for SuSAv2-MEC compared to MINERvA CCQE data: 1.79 for 58 degrees of freedom
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for MINERvA-tuned GENIE compared to the same data: 1.58 for 58 degrees of freedom
- Difference between QE-like and CCQE data sets varies between approximately 5% and 15% depending on kinematics
- Longitudinal MEC contribution to the total cross section: approximately 5-10%
- Longitudinal MEC contribution to the 2p2h channel: approximately 30-35%
- Using the full complex Delta propagator instead of the real part only changes the 2p2h cross sections by approximately 30-40%
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for SuSAv2-MEC with full Delta propagator: 2.30
- Axial mass used in the dipole axial nucleon form factor: 1.032 GeV
- Muon tracking kinetic energy threshold for protons: 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2 model comparisons from previous analyses of MiniBooNE and T2K data (with mean energies around 0.7-0.8 GeV) to the higher energy range (1.5-15 GeV) of the MINERvA experiment, which is relevant for future facilities like DUNE. The results are compared to the MINERvA-tuned GENIE (MnvGENIE) model from Rodrigues (2015) and Gran (2018), which incorporates RPA screening from Nieves (2004) and an empirically enhanced 2p2h model from Nieves (2011). The paper demonstrates that the SuSAv2 model, based on RMF predictions, naturally places additional cross section strength in the dip region without empirical tuning, unlike the MnvGENIE approach. The findings enable implementation of the SuSAv2-MEC model into Monte Carlo event generators such as GENIE, NEUT, and NuWro for use in current and future neutrino oscillation experiments.
