# Assessing the foundation of the Trojan Horse Method

> We discuss the foundation of the Trojan Horse Method (THM) within the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory. We demonstrate that the direct part of the INEB cross section, which is of two-step character, becomes, in the DWBA limit of the three-body theory with appropriate approximations and redefinitions, similar in structure to the one-step THM cross section. We also discuss the connection of the THM to the Surrogate Method (SM), which is a genuine two-step process.

## Key claim

The Trojan Horse Method (THM) cross section, which is usually treated as a one-step direct process, can be derived from the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) theory where the direct part of the INEB cross section is inherently a two-step process; under the DWBA limit with appropriate approximations and redefinitions, this two-step process "collapses" into a one-step form equivalent to the THM, but only for the direct (non-compound) component of the reaction.

## Method

The authors start from the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup (INEB) cross section of the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) theory, expressed in terms of a source function and the imaginary part of the optical potential for the interacting fragment \(x\). They separate the imaginary part of the optical potential into direct (\(W_x^D\)) and compound nucleus (\(W_x^{CN}\)) components, then isolate the direct contribution to the INEB cross section. Using the post-form IAV source function and the Hussein-McVoy (HM) source function, they show that the direct part of the IAV cross section (a two-step process involving a Green's function propagator) is formally equal to the HM direct cross section (a one-step process). By further approximating the modified elastic S-matrix of the spectator fragment \(b\) as the momentum-space internal wave function \(\phi(\mathbf{k}_b)\), they recover the standard THM cross section formula.

## Key numbers

* The INEB cross section is given by \(d^{2}\sigma^{\rm INEB}_b/(dE_{b}d\Omega_{b}) = \hat{\sigma}_{R}^{x} \rho_{b}(E_b)\), where \(\rho_{b}(E_b) = \mu_{b}k_{b}/[(2\pi)^{3}\hbar^{3}]\) is the density of states of the spectator fragment \(b\).
* The reaction cross section \(\hat{\sigma}_{R}^{x}\) is expressed as \(\hat{\sigma}_{R}^{x} = - (k_x/E_x) \langle\hat{\rho}_{x}(\mathbf{r}_x)|W_{x}(\mathbf{r}_x)|\hat{\rho}_{x}(\mathbf{r}_x)\rangle\), where \(W_x\) is the imaginary part of the optical potential for fragment \(x\).
* The direct part of the INEB cross section for a specific final channel \(y+B\) is \(d^{4}\sigma^{\rm INEB, (D)}_{(b, y)}/(dE_{b}d\Omega_{b}dE_{y}d\Omega_{y}) = \pi\rho_{b}(E_b)\rho_{y}(E_y)(k_x/E_x) |\langle\chi_{y}^{(-)}(\mathbf{k}_{y})|V_{(x,y)}|\hat{\rho}_{x}^{(+){\rm IAV}}\rangle|^2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation of the Trojan Horse Method (THM), originally developed by Baur (1986) and collaborators, by placing it within the Inclusive Non-Elastic Breakup theory of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985) and Hussein and McVoy (1985). The authors also connect the THM to the Surrogate Method (SM), noting that the SM is a genuine two-step process involving compound nucleus formation, whereas the THM's direct component collapses to a one-step form. This analysis enables future improvements to the THM by allowing approximate treatment of the principal part of the Green's function propagator that is omitted in the standard one-step formulation, potentially refining cross section extractions for nuclear astrophysics applications.
