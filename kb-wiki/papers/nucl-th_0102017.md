# Extraction of nuclear matter properties from nuclear masses by a model of equation of state

> The extraction of nuclear matter properties from measured nuclear masses is investigated in the energy density functional formalism of nuclei. It is shown that the volume energy $a_1$ and the nuclear incompressibility $K_0$ depend essentially on $μ_n N+\barμ_p Z-2E_N$, whereas the symmetry energy $J$ and the density symmetry coefficient $L$ as well as symmetry incompressibility $K_s$ depend essentially on $μ_n-\barμ_p$, where $\barμ_p=μ_p-\partial E_C/\partial Z$, $μ_n$ and $μ_p$ are the neutron and proton chemical potentials respectively, $E_N$ the nuclear energy, and $E_C$ the Coulomb energy. The obtained symmetry energy is $J=28.5MeV$, while other coefficients are uncertain within ranges depending on the model of nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nuclear matter properties can be extracted from measured nuclear masses using an energy density functional formalism, but the nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) cannot be determined from nuclear masses alone without additional assumptions on the model of the equation of state; the symmetry energy \(J\) is obtained as \(28.5\,\mathrm{MeV}\) with almost no model dependence, while \(K_0\) and the symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) exhibit strong model dependence.

## Method

The authors use an energy density functional with a nuclear equation of state parameterized by a power-law form in \(\rho/\rho_0\) with exponent \(\gamma\). They separate the data fit into two independent steps: first, the symmetry energy \(J\), density symmetry coefficient \(L\), and symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) are extracted from \(\mu_n - \overline{\mu}_p\) data; second, the volume energy \(a_1\) and nuclear incompressibility \(K_0\) are extracted from \(\mu_n N + \overline{\mu}_p Z - 2E_N\) data. The nucleon density is taken as a two-parameter Fermi distribution, and the chemical potentials are obtained from nuclear mass differences using 1654 measured masses, yielding 1140 data points for each of the two key combinations.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy \(J = 28.5\,\mathrm{MeV}\) from the model fit (almost independent of \(\gamma\)).
- From the leptodermous expansion fit to 1140 data points: \(J = 28.16\,\mathrm{MeV}\), \(L = 70.69\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- From the leptodermous expansion fit to 222 nuclei with \(|\delta| < 0.1\): volume energy \(a_1 = 15.29\,\mathrm{MeV}\), nuclear incompressibility \(K_0 = 225.7\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- As \(\gamma\) increases from 3.1 to 25: \(J\) increases from \(28.50\) to \(28.52\,\mathrm{MeV}\); \(a_1\) increases from \(15.86\) to \(17.47\,\mathrm{MeV}\); \(L\) decreases from \(61.76\) to \(50.97\,\mathrm{MeV}\); \(K_0\) increases from \(199.2\) to \(673.9\,\mathrm{MeV}\); \(K_s\) decreases from \(-119.7\) to \(-716.4\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the mass-formula fit with \(\gamma=5\): \(a_1\) varies from \(16.028\) to \(16.042\,\mathrm{MeV}\) as \(\gamma\) goes from 3.1 to 25; \(K_0\) increases monotonically from \(181\) to \(647\,\mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nayak and Satpathy, who used the generalized Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem to extract nuclear matter properties from masses, by separating the fit into two independent steps based on different combinations of chemical potentials. The results are compared with earlier droplet-model extractions by Myers and Swiatecki (1996) giving \(K_0 = 234\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and by Groote (1976) giving \(K_0 = 306\,\mathrm{MeV}\), as well as with breathing-mode extractions by Treiner (1981) giving \(K_0 = 210\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and Sharma (1988) giving \(K_0 \approx 300\,\mathrm{MeV}\). The paper demonstrates that nuclear masses alone cannot determine \(K_0\) without additional constraints from giant monopole resonance energies or a specific choice of the equation-of-state model.
