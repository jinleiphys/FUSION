# Benchmarking nuclear energy density functionals with new mass data

> Nuclear masses play a crucial role in both nuclear physics and astrophysics, driving sustained efforts toward their precise experimental determination and reliable theoretical prediction. In this work, we compile the newly measured masses for 296 nuclides from 40 references published between 2021 and 2024, subsequent to the release of the latest Atomic Mass Evaluation. These data are used to benchmark the performance of several relativistic and non-relativistic density functionals, including PC-PK1, TMA, SLy4, SV-min, UNEDF1, and the recently proposed PC-L3R. Results for PC-PK1 and PC-L3R are obtained using the state-of-the-art deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc), while the others are adopted from existing literature. It is found that the DRHBc calculations with PC-PK1 and PC-L3R achieve an accuracy better than 1.5 MeV, outperforming the other functionals, which all exhibit root-mean-square deviations exceeding 2 MeV. The odd-even effects and isospin dependence in these theoretical descriptions are examined. The PC-PK1 and PC-L3R descriptions are qualitatively similar, both exhibiting robust isospin dependence along isotopic chains. Finally, a quantitative comparison between the PC-PK1 and PC-L3R results is presented, with their largest discrepancies analyzed in terms of potential energy curves from constrained DRHBc calculations.

# Benchmarking nuclear energy density functionals with new mass data

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 and PC-L3R density functionals achieves root-mean-square deviations better than 1.5 MeV against 296 newly measured nuclear masses, outperforming other relativistic and non-relativistic functionals that all exceed 2 MeV RMS deviation; however, this accuracy is demonstrated only for this specific dataset and the PC-L3R result for even-even nuclei is an exception to the sub-1.5-MeV claim.

## Method

The authors compiled 296 newly measured nuclear masses from 40 references published between 2021 and 2024, subsequent to the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 (AME2020). They performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) calculations with the point-coupling density functionals PC-PK1 and PC-L3R, using the same numerical parameters as the DRHBc mass table project (pairing strength -325 MeV fm^3, saturation density 0.152 fm^-3, energy cutoff 300 MeV, angular momentum cutoff 23/2 hbar). Results for the functionals TMA, SLy4, SV-min, and UNEDF1 were taken from existing literature for comparison.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of DRHBc+PC-PK1 against the 296 new masses: 1.35 MeV
- RMS deviation of DRHBc+PC-L3R against the 296 new masses: better than 1.5 MeV (with the exception of even-even nuclei)
- RMS deviation of TMA: 2.04 MeV; SLy4: 3.95 MeV; SV-min: 2.37 MeV; UNEDF1: 2.21 MeV
- RMS deviation between the new experimental data and AME2020 experimental data: 0.0984 MeV; after including AME2020 empirical values: 0.1178 MeV
- Among 296 new masses, 247 in AME2020 are consistent with deviations smaller than 0.15 MeV
- For 23 nuclides, central values of new masses deviate from AME2020 by more than 200 keV
- For 6 nuclides (28S, 67Fe, 71Kr, 75Sr, 96Ag, 153Pm), central values differ by more than 200 keV with non-overlapping uncertainty ranges where both new and AME2020 masses are experimental
- For 111Mo, the quadrupole deformation difference between PC-PK1 and PC-L3R reaches 0.193, and the RMS matter radius difference reaches -0.031 fm

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table project (Zhang 2022, Guo 2024) by testing its predictions against the most recent experimental data, and benchmarks the newly proposed PC-L3R functional (Liu 2023) for deformed nuclei. The DRHBc approach with PC-PK1 had previously achieved approximately 1.5 MeV RMS deviation against AME2020 for even-Z nuclei, and this study confirms similar performance on an independent dataset while showing that PC-L3R yields qualitatively similar results. The findings motivate completing the full DRHBc mass table including odd-Z nuclei and pursuing large-scale DRHBc+PC-L3R calculations, as these functionals are the only relativistic density functionals that reproduce experimental masses with RMS deviations below 8 MeV even in spherical calculations (Xia 2018, Liu 2024).
