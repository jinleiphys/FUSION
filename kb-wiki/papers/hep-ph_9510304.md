# The Spin Structure of the Nucleon in the Asymptotic Limit

> In analogy to the Altarelli-Parisi equation for the quark and gluon helicity contributions to the nucleon spin, we derive an evolution equation for the quark and gluon orbital angular momenta. The solution of the combined equations yields the asymptotic fractions of the nucleon spin carried by quarks and gluons: $3n_f/(16+3n_f)$ and $16/(16+3n_f)$, respectively, where $n_f$ is the number of active quark flavors. These are identical to the well-known asymptotic partitions of the nucleon momentum between quark and gluon contributions. We show that the axial-anomaly contribution to the quark helicity is cancelled by a similar contribution to the quark orbital angular momentum, making the total quark contribution to the nucleon spin anomaly-free.

# The Spin Structure of the Nucleon in the Asymptotic Limit

## Key claim
The asymptotic fractions of the nucleon spin carried by quarks and gluons are identical to the well-known asymptotic partitions of the nucleon momentum, but only in the leading-log approximation and at asymptotically large momentum scales where sub-leading terms can be neglected.

## Method
The authors derive evolution equations for quark and gluon orbital angular momenta in QCD, analogous to the Altarelli-Parisi (AP) equations for helicity contributions. They calculate splitting functions for orbital angular momentum by considering parton splitting processes in light-front coordinates and light-front gauge, including both homogeneous terms (self-generation of orbital angular momentum) and inhomogeneous terms (orbital angular momentum generated from parton helicity). The combined evolution equations for helicity and orbital angular momentum are solved to obtain the asymptotic spin composition.

## Key numbers
- Asymptotic fraction of nucleon spin carried by quarks: 3n_f/(16+3n_f), where n_f is the number of active quark flavors
- Asymptotic fraction of nucleon spin carried by gluons: 16/(16+3n_f)
- Quark orbital angular momentum estimate at Q^2 ~ 3 GeV^2: L_q = 0.05 to 0.15, based on experimentally measured Delta Sigma
- The gluon helicity splitting matrix element from a quark: (3/2)C_F, where C_F = 4/3
- The gluon helicity net gain probability per splitting: beta_0/2 = (11 - 2n_f/3)/2

## Context
This work extends the Altarelli-Parisi formalism (Altarelli and Parisi, 1978) for helicity evolution to include orbital angular momentum, building on earlier work by Ratcliffe (1987) who first studied the inhomogeneous term. The authors show that the asymptotic partition of nucleon spin follows the same pattern as the momentum partition found by Gross and Wilczek (1974). The paper also addresses the axial anomaly cancellation between quark helicity and quark orbital angular momentum, connecting to the work of Carlitz, Collins, and Mueller (1988) and Altarelli and Ross (1988). This enables future measurements of gluon helicity (e.g., at RHIC experiments) to be interpreted within a consistent evolution framework, and provides a theoretical basis for understanding the scale dependence of the nucleon spin decomposition.
