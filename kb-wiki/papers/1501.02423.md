# Study of dynamical charge fluctuations in the hadronic medium

> The dynamical charge fluctuations have been studied in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions by using hadronic model simulations, such as UrQMD and HIJING. The evolution of fluctuations has been calculated at different time steps during the collision as well as different observation window in pseudorapidity (\DelEta). The final state effects on the fluctuations have been investigated by varying $\bigtriangleupη$~ and the time steps with the aim of obtaining an optimum observation window for capturing maximum fluctuations. It is found that $\bigtriangleupη$~ between 2.0 and 3.5 gives the best coverage for the fluctuations studies. The results of these model calculations for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$~=~7.7 to 200~GeV and for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are presented and compared with available experimental data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The paper finds that an optimal pseudorapidity window of Δη between 2.0 and 3.5 captures the maximum dynamical charge fluctuations from the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, with the caveat that this optimum is determined from hadronic model simulations (UrQMD and HIJING) rather than from experimental data directly, and that the optimum window may be somewhat lower for lower collision energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model and the HIJING (Heavy Ion Jet INteraction Generator) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. They calculated the dynamical net-charge fluctuation measure ν+-,dyn (corrected for global charge conservation) at different time steps during the hadronic evolution (5 fm/c, 30 fm/c, and 100 fm/c) and for pseudorapidity windows Δη ranging from 0.2 to 10.0, using a unit bin method to avoid dependence on central bin width.

## Key numbers

- The optimal pseudorapidity window for capturing maximum charge fluctuations is Δη between 2.0 and 3.5 for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- The value of the net-charge fluctuation measure D remains constant for Δη from 2.0 to 3.5, then decreases to near zero at Δη = 10.0.
- For a pion gas, the value of D is approximately 2.8; for a hadron resonance gas (HRG), D is also around 2.8; for a QGP, D is estimated to be 1–1.5.
- The dynamical fluctuation measure ν+-,dyn remains negative at all collision energies studied, indicating finite correlation between positive and negative particles.
- At LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV), the value of D for Δη = 1.0 in central Pb+Pb collisions falls below the HRG limit, and at Δη = 1.6 the fluctuations decrease further, with D values lying between the HRG and QGP limits.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of charge fluctuations as a signature of QGP formation, particularly the predictions by Jeon and Koch (1999) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2000) that QGP fluctuations should be significantly smaller than those from a hadron gas. The paper also builds on experimental measurements from the STAR collaboration at RHIC (Adams et al., 2003) and the ALICE collaboration at LHC (ALICE Collaboration, 2014), which showed reduced charge fluctuations at LHC energies. The study enables better design of experimental acceptance windows for future fluctuation measurements at RHIC Beam Energy Scan and LHC energies, and provides a baseline from purely hadronic models against which QGP signatures can be compared.
