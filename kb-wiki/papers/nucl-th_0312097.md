# Quark distributions in nuclear matter and the EMC effect

> Quark light cone momentum distributions in nuclear matter and the structure function of a bound nucleon are investigated in the framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. This framework describes the nucleon as a relativistic quark-diquark state, and the nuclear matter equation of state by using the mean field approximation. The scalar and vector mean fields in the nuclear medium couple to the quarks in the nucleon and their effect on the spin independent nuclear structure function is investigated in detail. Special emphasis is placed on the important effect of the vector mean field and on a formulation which guarantees the validity of the number and momentum sum rules from the outset.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the vector mean field in nuclear matter produces a shift in the quark light-cone momentum distribution that is essential for simultaneously satisfying both the baryon number and momentum sum rules, and that this shift is a key ingredient in explaining the EMC effect beyond what Fermi motion and binding effects at the nucleon level alone can account for.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective chiral quark theory, describing the nucleon as a relativistic quark-diquark bound state via the static approximation to the Faddeev equation, and nuclear matter via the mean field approximation. Quark light-cone momentum distributions in nuclear matter are evaluated using a convolution formalism that separates the quark distribution in the nucleon from the nucleon distribution in nuclear matter, with the vector field effect incorporated through a local gauge transformation. The proper time regularization scheme is employed to eliminate unphysical thresholds and achieve saturation of nuclear matter binding energy.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon mass: M_N0 = 940 MeV (used as reference for Bjorken variable definition)
- The nucleon LC momentum distribution in nuclear matter f_N/A(y_A) has support in the region 1 - p_F/epsilon_F < y_A < 1 + p_F/epsilon_F, where p_F is the Fermi momentum and epsilon_F is the Fermi energy
- The quark distribution in the nucleon f_q/N(z) has support in the region V_-/p_- < z < 1 - 2V_-/p_-, where V_- is the minus component of the vector potential
- The quark distribution in nuclear matter f_q/A(x_A) has support in the region V_0/epsilon_F < x_A < (E_F + p_F + V_0)/epsilon_F

## Context

This work extends the earlier NJL model studies of nuclear matter saturation by Bentz and Thomas (2001) and the quark-diquark description of the nucleon by Mineo et al. (2000), and is similar in spirit to the earlier investigations of the EMC effect by Thomas (1983) using the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon and collaborators. The framework enables a systematic study of how nuclear mean fields, particularly the vector field, modify quark distributions in a manner consistent with sum rules, and provides a foundation for future incorporation of pion cloud effects that were included in the free nucleon structure function calculations by Mineo et al. (2000).
