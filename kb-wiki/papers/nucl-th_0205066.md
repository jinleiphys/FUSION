# Penetration of a high energy QQbar bound state through SU(N) color background

> Attenuation of the singlet quark--anti-quark bound state passing through a random external SU(N) color field at high energy is studied within the eikonal framework as a model for the absorption of a quarkonium by nuclear target. For a thick target of length L the penetration probability is suppressed non-exponentially and behaves asymptotically $\propto 1/LN^2$ with 1/N^2 indicating the equi-partitioning in the color space. An approximate rational expression for the penetration of the small Gaussian bound state is found which allows the explicit L and N dependences, and shows the attenuation is in the powers of L although the approach to the asymptote is rather slow.

## Key claim

The penetration probability of a high-energy color-singlet quark–anti-quark bound state through a random external SU(N) color field is suppressed non-exponentially, behaving asymptotically as ∝ 1/(L N²) for a thick target of length L, with the 1/N² factor indicating equipartitioning in color space; however, the approach to this asymptotic form is rather slow.

## Method

The author uses the eikonal approximation for quark and anti-quark propagation through a random external SU(N) color field, modeling the nuclear target as a series of uncorrelated zones of length l. The target average is performed by expanding the eikonal amplitude to O(g²) in each zone, following the approach of Kovner and Wiedemann (2001). The survival probability is computed from the averaged eikonal factors expressed in the color irreducible basis of singlet and adjoint states, and an approximate rational expression is derived for a small Gaussian bound state.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic survival probability for a thick target: S ∝ 1/(L N²), with explicit form S ≈ (2/N²)(L_in/L) for large L
- For a Gaussian bound state, the analytic approximation S(L/L_in) = (2/N²)×1/(1+L/L_in) + ((N²-2)/N²)×1/(1+L/(2L_in))² reproduces numerical results well for N=3–10 and L/L_in=0–10
- For SU(2), the asymptotic form acquires an extra factor of 2 compared to SU(N≥3)
- For U(1), the survival probability is S = 1/(1+L/L_in)
- The inelastic cross section for a thin target gives 1/L_in = σ^in n_0 = 2 C_F ⟨v⟩ l⁻¹, where C_F = (N²-1)/(2N)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous SU(2) study (Fujii and Matsui, 2002) to general SU(N), showing explicitly the 1/(N²L) suppression of the penetration probability. The phenomenon is essentially the same as the "superpenetration" effect conjectured for ultra-relativistic positronium passing through a metallic foil (Lubkin and Pinsky, 1981; Nemenov, 1981; Baryshevsky, 1996). The results enable future studies of quarkonium absorption in nuclear targets, particularly for J/ψ production in heavy ion collisions, and suggest that the survival probability cannot have exponential suppression at very high energies.
