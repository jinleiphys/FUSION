# Relativistic Faddeev 3D Equations for Three-Body Bound States Without Two-Body $t-$Matrices

> This paper explores a novel revision of the Faddeev equation for three-body (3B) bound states, as initially proposed in Ref. \cite{golak2013three}. This innovative approach, referred to as \tmatrixfree in this paper, directly incorporates two-body (2B) interactions and completely avoids the 2B transition matrices. We extend this formalism to relativistic 3B bound states using a three-dimensional (3D) approach without using partial wave decomposition. To validate the proposed formulation, we perform a numerical study using spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon and Yamaguchi interactions. Our results demonstrate that the relativistic \tmatrixfree Faddeev equation, which directly implements boosted interactions, accurately reproduces the 3B mass eigenvalues obtained from the conventional form of Faddeev equation, referred to as \tmatrixdependent in this paper, with boosted 2B $t-$matrices. Moreover, the proposed formulation provides a simpler alternative to the standard approach, avoiding the computational complexity of calculating boosted 2B $t-$matrices and leading to significant computational time savings.

## Key claim

The paper extends a nonrelativistic "t-matrix-free" formulation of the Faddeev equation to relativistic three-body bound states, showing that directly using boosted two-body interactions (rather than boosted two-body t-matrices) reproduces the same mass eigenvalues with a relative difference of less than 0.01%, but only for bound-state calculations; the authors explicitly note that for scattering calculations, the t-matrix-dependent approach remains necessary.

## Method

The authors reformulate the relativistic Faddeev integral equation in a three-dimensional momentum representation without partial-wave decomposition, replacing the boosted two-body t-matrices with boosted two-body potentials obtained by solving a quadratic integral equation from the nonrelativistic potential. They solve the resulting 3D integral equation using a Lanczos iterative technique for two spin-independent potentials (Malfliet-Tjon V and Yamaguchi model IV), and compare the binding energies and computational times against the conventional t-matrix-dependent approach (solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation or the first-resolvent method).

## Key numbers

- Nonrelativistic binding energy for MT-V potential: -7.7360 MeV (t-matrix-free) vs. -7.7368 MeV (t-matrix-dependent), a difference of about 0.8 keV
- Relativistic binding energy for MT-V potential: -7.5847 MeV (t-matrix-free) vs. -7.5848 MeV (t-matrix-dependent), a difference of about 0.1 keV
- Nonrelativistic binding energy for Yamaguchi-IV potential: -8.5088 MeV (t-matrix-free) vs. -8.5089 MeV (t-matrix-dependent), a difference of about 0.1 keV
- Relativistic binding energy for Yamaguchi-IV potential: -8.3051 MeV (t-matrix-free) vs. -8.3052 MeV (t-matrix-dependent), a difference of about 0.1 keV
- Relative percentage difference between 3B binding energy and expectation value of the 3B Hamiltonian: 0.022% or less for all cases
- At 110 mesh points in the nonrelativistic case, the t-matrix-free method required about 7 times less CPU time than the t-matrix-LS method
- At 110 mesh points in the relativistic case, the t-matrix-LS method required approximately 1.8 times more computational time than the t-matrix-free method

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic t-matrix-free Faddeev formulation proposed by Golak et al. (2013) to the relativistic regime, following the relativistic three-body framework developed by Hadizadeh et al. (2014, 2020) and the boosted-potential approach of Kamada and Gloeckle (2007). The method avoids solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-body subsystem energies at each step of the three-body energy search, which was required in the conventional t-matrix-dependent approach (Elster et al., 1999; Hadizadeh et al., 2014). The authors note that this simplification is reliable only for bound states and that scattering calculations still require the t-matrix-dependent formulation.
