# Momentum anisotropies in the quark coalescence model

> Based on the quark coalescence model, we derive relations among the momentum anisotropies of mesons and baryons in relativistic heavy ion collisions from a given, but arbitrary azimuthal distribution for the partons. Besides the familiar even Fourier coefficients such as the elliptic flow, we also pay attention to odd Fourier coefficients such as the directed flow, which has been observed at finite rapidity even at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytic relations between the Fourier coefficients of parton azimuthal distributions and those of mesons and baryons formed via naive quark coalescence, showing that the observed large ratio of hadron fourth-order anisotropy to the square of second-order anisotropy (approximately 1.2) requires a partonic ratio of about 2, which is larger than the value of about 1 seen in the more realistic AMPT model.

## Method

The authors start from a Fourier expansion of the parton transverse momentum azimuthal distribution and apply the naive quark coalescence model, where mesons form from two partons each at half the meson momentum and baryons form from three partons each at one-third the baryon momentum, all traveling in the same direction. They compute the hadron Fourier coefficients by squaring (for mesons) and cubing (for baryons) the parton distribution function, then expand the resulting expressions to obtain analytic formulas for the hadron anisotropy coefficients \(V_n\) and \(\tilde V_n\) in terms of the parton coefficients \(v_n\). The analysis is carried out both for midrapidity (where odd coefficients vanish) and for forward rapidity or non-identical collisions (where odd coefficients are present).

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(V_4/V_2^2\) for mesons at midrapidity is approximately \(1/4 + (1/2)(v_4/v_2^2)\); for baryons the ratio \(\tilde V_4/\tilde V_2^2\) is approximately \(1/3 + (1/3)(v_4/v_2^2)\).
- To reproduce the experimentally observed hadron ratio of about 1.2, the partonic ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) must be approximately 2.
- For a maximum parton elliptic anisotropy \(v_2\) of about 10%, the meson \(V_4\) generated purely from \(v_2\) (without partonic \(v_4\)) is about 1%.
- The ratio \(\tilde V_3/(\tilde V_1 \tilde V_2)\) for baryons at forward rapidity is approximately \(2/3 + (1/3)(v_3/(v_1 v_2))\); if \(v_3 = 0\) this ratio is about 0.7.
- The relation between baryon and meson ratios at midrapidity is \(\tilde V_4/\tilde V_2^2 (3p_T) \approx (2/3) V_4/V_2^2 (2p_T) + 1/6\).

## Context

This work extends the naive quark coalescence model of Molnar and Voloshin (2003) and Lin and Ko (2002) by deriving general relations for all Fourier harmonics, including odd coefficients relevant at forward rapidity. It provides a theoretical basis for the scaling relation \(v_n(p_T) \sim 1.2 v_2^{n/2}(p_T)\) observed by the STAR Collaboration (2003) and complements the more realistic coalescence model of Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) and Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003) that includes quark momentum distributions inside hadrons. The results enable experimental tests of the coalescence picture through identified-particle measurements of the ratio of fourth-order to squared second-order anisotropy, and allow extraction of partonic anisotropy coefficients from hadron data.
