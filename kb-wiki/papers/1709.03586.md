# A quasiparticle equation of state with a phenomenological critical point

> We propose a hybrid parameterization of a quasiparticle equation of state, where a critical point is implemented phenomenologically. In this approach, a quasiparticle model with finite chemical potential is used to describe the quark-gluon plasma phase by fitting to the lattice quantum chromodynamics data at high temperature. On the other hand, the hadronic resonance gas model with excluded volume correction is employed for the hadronic phase. An interpolation scheme is implemented so that the phase transition is a smooth crossover when the chemical potential is smaller than a critical value, or otherwise approximately of first order according to Ehrenfest's classification. Also, the thermodynamic consistency is guaranteed for the equation of state related to both the quasiparticle model and the implementation of the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid equation of state that implements a phenomenological critical point at finite baryon chemical potential, where the phase transition is a smooth crossover for chemical potentials below a critical value and approximately first order above it, with thermodynamic consistency guaranteed.

## Method

A quasiparticle model with finite chemical potential, following Bannur's approach (2006-2007), is fitted to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. (2012, 2013) to describe the quark-gluon plasma phase at high temperature. A hadronic resonance gas model with excluded volume correction from Rischke (1991) and Hama et al. (2004) is used for the hadronic phase at low temperature. An interpolation scheme from Hama et al. (2005) is applied to smoothly connect the two phases, with a critical chemical potential of 0.3 GeV and a Gaussian suppression factor that ensures the transition becomes first-order-like above the critical point.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential: mu_c = 0.3 GeV, the value below which the phase transition is a smooth crossover and above which it becomes approximately first order.
- Quark masses: strange quark current mass m_s0 = 0.150 GeV; up and down quark current masses m_u0,d0 = 5.33 x 10^{-4} GeV (derived as m_s0/28.15).
- Reference point for zero chemical potential: T_0 = 0.175 GeV, p_0/T_0^4 = 1.08.
- Scale parameter Lambda_T = 0.135 GeV for zero chemical potential and Lambda_T = 0.130 GeV for finite chemical potential.
- Interpolation parameter delta_0 = 5.90 x 10^{-10} GeV^8, with Gaussian suppression constant c = 10^3 GeV^{-2}.
- Overall normalization factor of 1.06 applied to account for unknown corrections to effective degrees of freedom, following Schneider (2001) and Ivanov (2004).

## Context

This work extends the parameterization of Huovinen and Petreczky (2009), which combined hadron resonance gas with lattice QCD data only at zero baryon chemical potential, by introducing finite chemical potential and a phenomenological critical point. The quasiparticle model follows the thermodynamically consistent approach of Bannur (2006-2007), and the interpolation scheme is adopted from Hama et al. (2005). The resulting equation of state is intended for use in hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the RHIC beam energy scan program aimed at studying the QCD phase boundary and searching for the critical point.
