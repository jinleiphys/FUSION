# Global Analysis of Leptophilic Z' Bosons

> New neutral heavy gauge bosons ($Z^\prime$) are predicted within many extensions of the Standard Model. While in case they couple to quarks the LHC bounds are very stringent, leptophilic $Z^\prime$ bosons (even with sizable couplings) can be much lighter and therefore lead to interesting quantum effects in precision observables (like $(g-2)_μ$) and generate flavour violating decays of charged leptons. In particular, $\ell\to\ell^\primeν\barν$ decays, anomalous magnetic moments of charged leptons, $\ell\to\ell^\primeγ$ and $\ell\to3\ell^\prime$ decays place stringent limits on leptophilic $Z^\prime$ bosons. Furthermore, in case of mixing $Z^\prime$ with the SM $Z$, $Z$ pole observables are affected. In light of these many observables we perform a global fit to leptophilic $Z^\prime$ models with the main goal of finding the bounds for the $Z^\prime$ couplings to leptons. To this end we consider a number of scenarios for these couplings. While in generic scenarios correlations are weak, this changes once additional constraints on the couplings are imposed. In particular, if one considers an $L_μ-L_τ$ symmetry broken only by left-handed rotations, or considers the case of $τ-μ$ couplings only. In the latter setup, on can explain the $(g-2)_μ$ anomaly and the hint for lepton flavour universality violation in $τ\toμν\barν/τ\to eν\barν$ without violating bounds from electroweak precision observables.

## Key claim

Leptophilic Z' bosons, which couple only to leptons and not to quarks, can be much lighter than Z' bosons that couple to quarks and can therefore produce observable quantum effects in precision observables; in the specific scenario with tau-mu couplings only, the model can simultaneously explain the (g-2)_mu anomaly and the hint for lepton flavour universality violation in tau->mu nu nubar / tau->e nu nubar without violating bounds from electroweak precision observables.

## Method

The authors extend the Standard Model by adding a heavy neutral gauge boson Z'_0 with mass above the electroweak symmetry breaking scale, including kinetic terms, mass terms, and interactions with Standard Model fields via left- and right-handed couplings g^{L,R}_{ij} to charged leptons and neutrinos. They perform a global fit using the publicly available HEPfit code, considering constraints from lepton flavour violating decays (ell->ell'gamma, ell->3ell'), anomalous magnetic moments, lepton flavour universality violation in ell->ell' nu nubar decays, Z pole observables, neutrino trident production, and LEP searches for contact interactions across multiple coupling scenarios.

- The (g-2)_mu anomaly has a tension of 4.2 sigma compared to the Standard Model prediction, with Delta a_mu = 251(59) x 10^{-11}
- The experimental ratio A[tau->mu nu nubar]/A[mu->e nu nubar] = 1.0029 +/- 0.0014
- The experimental ratio A[tau->mu nu nubar]/A[tau->e nu nubar] = 1.0018 +/- 0.0014
- The experimental ratio A[tau->e nu nubar]/A[mu->e nu nubar] = 1.0010 +/- 0.0014

- Br[mu->e gamma] <= 4.2 x 10^{-13} at 90% C.L.

- Br[tau->e gamma] <= 3.3 x 10^{-8} at 90% C.L.

- Delta a_e (Cs) = -0.88(36) x 10^{-12}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Z' bosons in the context of gauged abelian flavour symmetries such as L_mu - L_tau (He, Foot, 1990-1991) and builds on analyses of lepton flavour violation and anomalous magnetic moments (Altmannshofer 2014, Crivellin 2015, Heeck 2011). The global fit using HEPfit (de Blas 2019) enables a comprehensive assessment of leptophilic Z' parameter space, and the results can guide future searches at experiments such as BELLE II, Mu3e, COMET, and Mu2e, as well as inform interpretations of the Cabibbo angle anomaly and b->s l+l- anomalies.
