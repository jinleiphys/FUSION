# The scientific legacy of J. P. Elliott

> James Philip Elliott, one of the towering figures of nuclear physics of the second half of the twentieth century, died on the 21st of October 2008. Obituaries appeared in the British press but relatively little attention was paid by the international scientific community to Elliott's contributions. Given their importance, in particular for theoretical nuclear physics, it seems appropriate to reflect here, two years after his death, on the scientific legacy left by Phil Elliott.

# The scientific legacy of J. P. Elliott

## Key claim

Elliott's SU(3) model bridged the gap between the spherical shell model and the collective model by showing that valence nucleons confined to a single harmonic-oscillator shell can display rotational behavior, but the scheme presupposes Wigner's supermultiplet classification and hence LS coupling, which restricts direct applications to the beginning of the sd shell and does not accommodate spin-orbit coupling in heavier nuclei.

## Method

The paper is a retrospective review of J. P. Elliott's career, tracing his work from PhD calculations of two-body coefficients of fractional parentage for p-shell nuclei through his development of the SU(3) model, his derivation of the Sussex interaction from nucleon-nucleon phase shifts using Talmi-Moshinsky brackets, and his later contributions to the interacting boson model (IBM) including the isospin-invariant IBM-3 and IBM-4 versions. The author describes the algebraic substructure of U(6) for the sd shell of the harmonic oscillator, showing that SU(3) appears as a subalgebra and that a single irreducible representation of SU(3) corresponds to one or several intrinsic states from which rotational band states can be projected.

## Key numbers

- Elliott's two SU(3) papers (1958a, 1958b) received close to 2000 citations.
- Mixed-symmetry states in IBM-2 were predicted to occur in the energy range of 2–3 MeV.
- The Sussex interaction was applied to the triton and alpha particle using a symmetry technique that reduced a very large shell-model calculation to one that could be comfortably carried out at that time (no specific matrix dimension given).
- The OAI mapping associates the seniority quantum number v in fermion space with 2N_d, twice the number of d bosons.

## Context

This tribute extends the recognition of Elliott's work beyond the obituaries that appeared in the British press, placing his SU(3) model in the context of the parallel development of the spherical shell model (Mayer, 1949; Haxel, Jensen and Suess, 1949) and the collective model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1953). The SU(3) model preceded similar ideas in high-energy physics such as the eightfold way (Ne'eman, 1961; Gell-Mann, 1962). Later extensions discussed include pseudo SU(3) (Hecht and Adler, 1969; Arima et al., 1969), quasi SU(3) (Zuker et al., 1995), and the symplectic algebra Sp(6) (Rosensteel and Rowe, 1977). The paper enables future work on a deformed mapping between fermion and boson spaces using SU(3) as the connecting symmetry, as outlined in Elliott and Evans (1999).
