# Kaonic nuclei excited by the $(K^-,N)$ reaction

> We show that kaonic nuclei can be produced by the $(K^-,p)$ and $(K^-,n)$ reactions. The reactions are shown to have cross sections experimentally measurable. The observation of the kaonic nuclei gives a kaon-nucleus potential which answers the question on the existence of kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter especially neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that kaonic nuclei can be produced via the $(K^-,p)$ and $(K^-,n)$ reactions with experimentally measurable cross sections, but the calculation is explicitly described as "rather crude" and based on a harmonic oscillator potential approximation that is "good enough for the present purpose" of estimating gross structure and order-of-magnitude cross sections.

## Method

The author calculates energies and widths of kaonic nuclei using a Schrodinger equation with a harmonic oscillator potential, taking the potential depth as -200 MeV from kaonic atom data. The cross section for kaonic nucleus formation via the $(K^-,N)$ reaction is evaluated using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA), where the differential cross section is given by the two-body laboratory cross section multiplied by an effective nucleon number. The calculation considers natural parity stretched states with transferred angular momentum $L = \ell_N + \ell_K$, and uses eikonal absorption to account for distortion of incoming and outgoing waves.

## Key numbers

- The $1s$ state for kaonic $^{28}_K$Si appears at around -140 MeV bound, with $\hbar \omega_K$ roughly 40 MeV
- Momentum transfer for well-bound states (binding energy 100-150 MeV) is $q = 0.3$ to $0.4$ GeV/c
- Incident kaon momentum chosen is 1 GeV/c, where the $K^-p \rightarrow K^- p$ reaction has a distinct peak
- The CM cross section for the elementary reaction at 1 GeV/c is taken as approximately 10 mb/sr
- Calculated laboratory differential cross sections for the $1s$ state via $(K^-,p)$ at $P_K=1$ GeV/c: for $^{12}$C target, 0.72 to 3.3 mb/sr; for $^{28}$Si target, 0.24 to 1.3 mb/sr
- The width of the deeply bound state is around 10 MeV, with the next excited state ($1p$) expected 40 MeV higher

## Context

This work extends the study of kaonic atoms, where recent analysis of kaonic X-ray data by Batty, Friedman, and Gal concluded the $K^-$ optical potential is strongly attractive with depth around -200 MeV, though earlier studies gave much shallower potentials of -80 to -90 MeV. The paper proposes the $(K^-,N)$ reaction as a new method to directly observe deeply bound kaonic nuclei, which would provide decisive information on whether kaon condensation occurs in neutron stars. This approach differs from other strangeness transfer reactions like $(K^-,\pi)$, $(\pi^\pm,K^+)$, and $(\gamma,K^+)$ that primarily produce hyperons, and is analogous to the $(d,^3\text{He})$ reaction used by Yamazaki to observe deeply bound $\pi^-$ atoms.
