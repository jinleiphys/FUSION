# pi- and rho-mesons, and their diquark partners, from a contact interaction

> We present a unified Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of static and electromagnetic properties of pseudoscalar and vector mesons, and scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations, based upon a vector-vector contact-interaction. A basic motivation for this study is the need to document a comparison between the electromagnetic form factors of mesons and those diquarks which play a material role in nucleon structure. This is an important step toward a unified description of meson and baryon form factors based on a single interaction. A notable result, therefore, is the large degree of similarity between related meson and diquark form factors. The simplicity of the interaction enables computation of the form factors at arbitrarily-large spacelike-Q^2, which enables us to expose a zero in the rho-meson electric form factor at z_Q^ρ~ Sqrt[6] m_ρ. Notably, r_ρ*z_Q^ρ~ r_D*z_Q^D, where r_ρ, r_D are, respectively, the electric radii of the rho-meson and deuteron.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple contact interaction (momentum-independent vector-vector interaction) in rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations produces a large degree of similarity between electromagnetic form factors of mesons and their corresponding diquark correlations, but this similarity is contingent on the truncation scheme and the accidental vanishing of the rho-meson's F_rho amplitude in rainbow-ladder truncation.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving regularized contact interaction (vector-vector current-current interaction mediated by a momentum-independent boson propagator) within the rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. They compute static properties (masses, decay constants, condensates) and electromagnetic form factors (elastic for pi-meson, rho-meson, scalar and axial-vector diquarks) by solving the gap equation, Bethe-Salpeter equations, and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex, ensuring Ward-Takahashi identities are preserved through a specific regularization scheme with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius: r_pi = 0.45 fm (compared to experimental 0.672 +/- 0.008 fm)
- Rho-meson electric charge radius: r_rho^E = 0.56 fm
- Rho-meson magnetic radius: r_rho^M = 0.51 fm
- Rho-meson magnetic moment: mu_rho = 2.11 (in natural units)
- Rho-meson quadrupole moment: Q_rho = -0.85
- Zero in rho-meson electric form factor at Q^2 = 5.0 GeV^2 (corresponding to z_Q^rho approximately sqrt(6) m_rho)
- Scalar diquark radius: r_{0+} = 0.49 fm
- Axial-vector diquark electric radius: r_{1+}^E = 0.55 fm
- Axial-vector diquark magnetic moment: mu_{1+} = 2.13
- Axial-vector diquark quadrupole moment: Q_{1+} = -0.81
- Chiral limit values: M^0 = 0.358 GeV, f_pi^0 = 0.100 GeV, m_rho^0 = 0.919 GeV, f_rho^0 = 0.130 GeV, kappa_pi^{1/3} = 0.241 GeV
- With m=0.007 GeV: M = 0.368 GeV, m_pi = 0.140 GeV, m_rho = 0.928 GeV, f_pi = 0.101 GeV, f_rho = 0.129 GeV

## Context

This work extends the contact-interaction Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously developed by Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2010) and Roberts et al. (2010, 2011) to simultaneously describe meson and diquark electromagnetic properties. It provides a systematic comparison with QCD-based DSE studies by Maris and Tandy (1999, 2000) and Bhagwat et al. (2006), and with phenomenological studies by Burden et al. (1995) and Hawes et al. (1998). The results enable future unified descriptions of meson and baryon form factors based on a single interaction, as pursued by Eichmann et al. (2008) and Cloet et al. (2008), and provide benchmarks for identifying signals of the pointwise behavior of the light-quark interaction in experiments at modern facilities.
