# Chiral symmetry and open-charm mesons

> Pseudo-scalar and scalar $D$ mesons are considered within the QCD sum rule approach. We present an analysis of the mass splitting of the pseudo-scalar D - D-bar mesons and the relation to QCD condensates. Weinberg type sum rules are derived for chiral partners which highlight the role of the chiral condensate.

# Chiral symmetry and open-charm mesons

## Key claim

The mass splitting between pseudo-scalar D and anti-D mesons is robustly related to QCD condensates via QCD sum rules, but the centroid mass shift is fragile under variation of continuum threshold parameters; the actual value of the mass splitting depends sensitively on the sign of the poorly known mixed quark-gluon condensate ⟨q† g σ G q⟩.

## Method

The authors employ QCD sum rules with Borel-transformed current-current correlators for pseudo-scalar D mesons, evaluating the operator product expansion up to mass dimension 5 in the rest frame of nuclear matter and in the limit of a light d quark mass. They define spectral moments S_n(M²) and derive quantities Δm and m that encode combined mass-width properties, then perform a numerical analysis using condensate values from prior work with the exception of the odd mixed quark-gluon condensate, whose sign is varied to assess its importance. Weinberg-type sum rules are also derived for the difference between pseudo-scalar and scalar chiral partners.

## Key numbers

- The mixed quark-gluon condensate is varied with the value ⟨q† g σ G q⟩ = -0.33 GeV² · n (where n is baryon density relative to normal nuclear density n₀).
- The continuum threshold parameter is set as s₀² = (6 + ξ n/n₀) GeV², with ξ = 1, 0, and -1 considered.
- The relation 1/12 ⟨q† g σ G q⟩ - ⟨q† D₀² q⟩ ≈ 0.031 GeV² is used to relate two condensates.
- For the Weinberg-type sum rules, the chiral condensate appears with coefficient -2 m_c for the i=1 moment, where m_c is the charm-quark pole mass.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule analysis of open-charm mesons initiated in Hilger (2008) and follows the suggestion in Erice (2008) that open charm mesons are suitable hadrons depending strongly on the chiral condensate. The derived Weinberg-type sum rules for chiral partners highlight how dropping in-medium chiral condensates cause degeneracy of spectral moments, which is relevant for interpreting data from the HADES collaboration and for the planned CBM and PANDA experiments at FAIR. The approach contrasts with earlier conjectures by Brown and Rho (1991) and Hatsuda and Lee (1992) regarding direct relations between dilepton emissivity and chiral symmetry.
