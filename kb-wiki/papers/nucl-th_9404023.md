# Two-Loop Calculations with Vertex Corrections in the Walecka Model

> Two-loop corrections with scalar and vector form factors are calculated for nuclear matter in the Walecka model. The on-shell form factors are derived from vertex corrections within the framework of the model and are highly damped at large spacelike momenta. The two-loop corrections are evaluated first by using the one-loop parameters and mean fields and then by refitting the total energy/baryon to empirical nuclear matter saturation properties. The modified two-loop corrections are significantly smaller than those computed with bare vertices. Contributions from the anomalous isoscalar form factor of the nucleon are included for the first time. The effects of the implicit density dependence of the form factors, which arise from the shift in the baryon mass, are also considered. Finally, necessary extensions of these calculations are discussed.

## Key claim

Two-loop corrections to nuclear matter energy in the Walecka model are significantly reduced when on-shell scalar and vector form factors derived from vertex corrections within the model itself are used, compared to calculations with bare vertices; however, the results depend on an on-shell approximation for the vertex functions and the implicit density dependence of the form factors through the effective nucleon mass, and definitive statements about nuclear matter saturation require extending the calculations to include off-shell vertex functions.

## Method

The authors applied the Freedman-McLerran formalism to nuclear matter in the Walecka model, truncating the thermodynamic potential at the two-loop level with one dressed vertex. On-shell scalar and vector form factors were derived from vertex corrections within the model, using the leading logarithmic infrared behavior at large spacelike momentum transfer from summed vector ladder and crossed ladder diagrams, with smooth interpolation to the lowest-order vertex correction at small momentum transfer. The two-loop corrections to the energy density were computed first using one-loop parameters and mean fields, then by refitting the total energy per baryon to empirical nuclear matter saturation properties, including for the first time contributions from the anomalous isoscalar form factor of the nucleon and the implicit density dependence arising from the shift in the baryon mass.

## Key numbers

- The two-loop corrections with dressed vertices are "significantly smaller" than those computed with bare vertices (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1989), which had shown large vacuum corrections indicating the loop expansion was not convergent or asymptotic.
- The contribution from the anomalous vector form factor is "small compared to those from the vector charge and scalar vertices, but not negligible."
- The effects of the M* dependence and uncertainty in the form factor interpolations are "modest compared to the overall size of the two-loop corrections, but are nevertheless significant on the scale of the nuclear matter binding energy."
- The scalar density of baryons is denoted by ρ_s, with the spin-isospin degeneracy γ = 4 for nuclear matter and γ = 2 for neutron matter.

## Context

This work extends the method of Allendes and Serot (1992) for the on-shell vector form factor to the scalar-baryon vertex, applying the resulting form factors to two-loop calculations in the Walecka model. It contrasts with earlier two-loop calculations by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1989) that found large vacuum corrections with bare vertices, and with approaches using ad hoc form factors by Prakash, Ellis, and Knoll (1991) and Friedrich (1992), which introduced nonlocal couplings without a model-internal derivation of the vertex functions. The results enable future calculations with off-shell vertex functions and studies of medium-modified vertices at finite density, which are necessary before definitive statements about nuclear matter saturation can be made.
