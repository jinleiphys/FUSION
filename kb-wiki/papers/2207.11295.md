# Measurement of the Parity-Odd Angular Distribution of Gamma Rays From Polarized Neutron Capture on $^{35}$Cl

> We report a measurement of two energy-weighted gamma cascade angular distributions from polarized slow neutron capture on the ${}^{35}$Cl nucleus, one parity-odd correlation proportional to $\vec{s_{n}} \cdot \vec{k_γ}$ and one parity-even correlation proportional to $\vec{s_{n}} \cdot \vec{k_{n}} \times \vec{k_γ}$. A parity violating asymmetry can appear in this reaction due to the weak nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction which mixes opposite parity S and P-wave levels in the excited compound $^{36}$Cl nucleus formed upon slow neutron capture. If parity-violating (PV) and parity-conserving (PC) terms both exist, the measured differential cross section can be related to them via $\frac{dσ}{dΩ}\propto1+A_{γ, PV}\cosθ+A_{γ,PC}\sinθ$. The PV and PC asymmetries for energy-weighted gamma cascade angular distributions for polarized slow neutron capture on $^{35}$Cl averaged over the neutron energies from 2.27~meV to 9.53~meV were measured to be $A_{γ,PV}=(-23.9\pm0.7)\times 10^{-6}$ and $A_{γ,PC}=(0.1\pm0.7)\times 10^{-6}$. These results are consistent with previous experimental results. Systematic errors were quantified and shown to be small compared to the statistical error. These asymmetries in the angular distributions of the gamma rays emitted from the capture of polarized neutrons in $^{35}$Cl were used to verify the operation and data analysis procedures for the NPDGamma experiment which measured the parity-odd asymmetry in the angular distribution of gammas from polarized slow neutron capture on protons.

## Key claim

The parity-violating (PV) and parity-conserving (PC) asymmetries for energy-weighted gamma cascade angular distributions from polarized slow neutron capture on 35Cl were measured to be consistent with previous experimental results, but the primary motivation was to verify the operation and data analysis procedures for the NPDGamma experiment rather than to extract new weak interaction physics from chlorine itself.

## Method

Polarized slow neutrons from the Spallation Neutron Source were captured on liquid carbon tetrachloride targets (first in an aluminum container, then in a teflon container), and the resulting gamma rays were detected by an array of 48 CsI current-mode detectors arranged around the target with approximately 3pi acceptance. The parity-odd asymmetry proportional to the dot product of neutron spin and gamma momentum, and the parity-even asymmetry proportional to the triple product of neutron spin, neutron momentum, and gamma momentum, were extracted from energy-weighted gamma cascade angular distributions using an 8-step spin sequence to suppress detector gain drifts. Data were taken in two configurations, with the final physics asymmetries extracted only from the second configuration using a teflon target to minimize background from neutron capture on the container.

## Key numbers

- PV asymmetry: A_gamma,PV = (-23.9 +/- 0.7) x 10^(-6), averaged over neutron energies from 2.27 meV to 9.53 meV
- PC asymmetry: A_gamma,PC = (0.1 +/- 0.7) x 10^(-6), averaged over the same neutron energy range
- Previous world average for 35Cl PV asymmetry: (-23.9 +/- 1.36) x 10^(-6), from measurements by Vesna et al. (1982), NPDGamma LANL (2004), and ILL (1985)
- Neutron wavelength selection: 1.93 to 5.6 Angstrom for configuration 1, 2.93 to 6.0 Angstrom for configuration 2

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of parity violation in 35Cl by Vesna et al. (1982), NPDGamma LANL (2004), and ILL (1985), achieving improved statistical precision. The large parity-odd asymmetry in chlorine (almost three orders of magnitude larger than in hydrogen) arises from mixing of a J-pi = 2- p-wave level at +398 eV and a J-pi = 2+ sub-threshold s-wave resonance at -130 eV in the n+35Cl system. The measurement served as a calibration and verification tool for the NPDGamma experiment, which measured the parity-odd asymmetry in polarized neutron capture on protons to extract the weak pion-nucleon coupling constant h^1_pi.
