# Constraining neutron star properties and dark matter admixture with the NITR-I equation of state: Insights from observations and universal relations

> A recent observational study has constrained the maximum mass of neutron stars (NSs), with particular attention to PSR J0952-0607 and the compact star remnant HESS J1731-347, especially in the low-mass regime. Building on our earlier work, which developed the NITR energy density functional (EDF) to reproduce the mass limit of PSR J0952-0607 but did not satisfy other observational constraints, this study introduces a refined EDF named ``NITR-I". NITR-I successfully reconciles the PSR J0952-0607 mass limit with observational data, including radius measurements from NICER+XMM and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817, demonstrating its robustness. The low-mass constraint associated with HESS J1731-347 indicates diverse NS compositions. Since NITR-I alone cannot satisfy this constraint, we explore the role of dark matter (DM) within NSs to bridge the gap. Incorporating DM, particularly at specific Fermi momentum values, enables the model to address this constraint. We further analyze the influence of DM on various NS properties, such as tidal deformability and non-radial $f$-mode oscillations, across multiple relativistic mean-field models. The presence of DM suggests a reduction in tidal deformability and shifts in oscillation frequencies, potentially offering detectable signatures in gravitational wave observations from neutron star mergers. Additionally, we investigate universal relations (URs) for DM-admixed NSs, focusing on correlations such as compactness versus tidal deformability and $f$-mode frequency versus tidal deformability. Canonical values for these properties are estimated using GW170817 data, offering further insights into the structure and composition of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The NITR-I energy density functional successfully reproduces the maximum mass of PSR J0952-0607, the canonical radius from NICER+XMM, and the tidal deformability from GW170817 simultaneously, but it cannot satisfy the low-mass constraint of HESS J1731-347 unless dark matter with a specific Fermi momentum is included.

## Method

The authors constructed a new relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameter set called NITR-I by calibrating coupling constants and nuclear matter properties to experimental binding energies and charge radii of eight nuclei using simulated annealing. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations with a unified core-crust equation of state to compute neutron star masses, radii, tidal deformabilities, and non-radial f-mode oscillation frequencies within the relativistic Cowling approximation. Dark matter was incorporated as a single-fluid component of non-annihilating weakly interacting massive particles (neutralinos) coupled to nucleons via Higgs exchange, with the dark matter Fermi momentum varied between 0 and 0.04 GeV.

## Key numbers

* NITR-I maximum mass: 2.34 solar masses, with corresponding radius 11.46 km
* NITR-I canonical (1.4 solar mass) radius: 12.71 km; canonical tidal deformability: 526.96
* For NITR-I equation of state, f-mode frequency at 1.4 solar masses is 2.15 kHz with no dark matter (k_f^DM = 0 GeV) and increases to 2.32 kHz with k_f^DM = 0.03 GeV
* At maximum mass (2.35 solar masses for NITR-I), f-mode frequency is 2.51 kHz with no dark matter and 2.61 kHz with k_f^DM = 0.03 GeV
* Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.151 fm^-3; saturation energy: -16.337 MeV; incompressibility: 199.018 MeV; symmetry energy: 30.937 MeV; slope parameter: 61.826 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier NITR model (Routaray 2023), which reproduced the PSR J0952-0607 mass limit but failed other observational constraints. The NITR-I model improves agreement with NICER+XMM radius measurements (Miller 2021) and GW170817 tidal deformability constraints (Abbott 2017, 2018). The dark matter admixture approach follows previous studies of HESS J1731-347, including the two-fluid analysis by Sagun (2023) suggesting the object could be a dark-matter-admixed neutron star. The universal relations for compactness versus tidal deformability and f-mode frequency versus tidal deformability extend prior work by Maselli (2013) and Yagi (2013, 2015), and the f-mode frequency versus average density fits are compared with earlier relations by Andersson and Kokkotas (1996, 1998), Pradhan and Chatterjee (2021), and Das (2021).
