# Towards a three dimensional solution for 3N bound states with 3NFs

> After a brief discussion about the necessity of using the 3D approach, we present the non PW formalism for 3N bound state with the inclusion of 3N force (3NF). As an example the evaluation of 3NF matrix elements, which appear in the obtained coupled three dimensional integral equations, for $2π$-exchange Tucson-Melbourne 3NF show how would be this formalism efficient and less cumbersome in comparison with the PW formalism.

## Key claim
The paper presents a three-dimensional (3D) formalism for solving the three-nucleon (3N) bound state problem that includes three-nucleon forces (3NFs), claiming this approach is "efficient and less cumbersome" than the traditional partial-wave (PW) formalism because it avoids the involved angular momentum algebra required for permutations, transformations, and especially for 3NFs. The authors explicitly state this is a "non PW formalism" and that the number of equations is energy independent, but they do not present any numerical results or convergence tests in this paper.

## Method
The authors formulate the Faddeev equation for the 3N bound state including a 3NF in momentum space using a 3D basis of Jacobi momentum vectors **p** and **q** with explicit spin and isospin quantum numbers, replacing discrete angular momentum quantum numbers with continuous angle variables. They derive coupled three-dimensional integral equations for the Faddeev amplitude, and as an example evaluate the matrix elements of the 2π-exchange Tucson-Melbourne (TM) 3NF by splitting it into A-, B-, C-, and D-terms and inserting complete sets of states in different 3N chains (types 1, 2, and 3) with coordinate transformations between them.

## Key numbers
- The TM 3NF is expressed with momentum transfers **Q** and **Q'**, pion mass m_π, and form factors F(Q²) = (Λ² - m_π²)/(Λ² + Q²)² with cutoff parameter Λ (the specific value of Λ is not given in the text).
- For the A-, B-, and C-terms of the 3NF, the exponent N in the matrix element |**q'** - **q''**|^N takes values 1, 2, and 3 respectively.
- For the D-term of the 3NF, the exponent M in the matrix element takes values 0 (first and second parts), 1 (third part), and 2 (fourth and fifth parts).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous 3D approach to the 3N bound state with only NN interactions (Bayegan 2008) by including 3NFs, specifically the Tucson-Melbourne 2π-exchange 3NF (Coon 1979). The formalism is presented as an alternative to traditional PW calculations that require summing many angular momentum components to achieve convergence, and it enables future calculations with realistic 2N and 3N forces without the complexity of angular momentum algebra. The paper is a conference proceedings contribution (APFB08) that presents the formalism but does not report numerical results or binding energies.
