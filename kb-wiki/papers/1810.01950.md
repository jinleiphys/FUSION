# New Results on Three-Nucleon Short-Range Correlations

> The recent progress in studies of two-nucleon~(2N) short-range correlations~(SRCs) are reviewed with the main emphasize given to the observation of the strong dominance of proton-neutron~(pn) SRCs in nuclei as compared to proton-proton and neutron-neutron SRCs. Based on the pn SRC dominance a specific prediction is made for the dynamical structure of 3N- SRCs for inclusive $A(e,e^\prime)X$ reactions, according to which the 3N- SRCs are generated through the sequence of two short range $pn$ interactions. This allowed us to predict that the height of the plateau in the ratios of inclusive eA cross sections to that of $^3He$ in the 3N-SRC region is related to the height of the plateau in the 2N-SRC region by a quadratic relation. The analysis of the available experimental data supports validity of such a relation.

# New Results on Three-Nucleon Short-Range Correlations

## Key claim
Based on the observed dominance of proton-neutron (pn) short-range correlations (SRCs) in two-nucleon systems, the paper predicts that three-nucleon SRCs in inclusive A(e,e')X reactions are generated through a sequence of two short-range pn interactions, leading to a quadratic relation between the plateau heights in the 3N-SRC and 2N-SRC regions. The analysis of available experimental data supports this relation, though the author notes that the accuracy of data is not sufficient for verification of 3N-SRC plateaus for all considered nuclei.

## Method
The author uses light-cone nuclear spectral functions to describe the kinematics of three-nucleon SRCs in inclusive electron-nucleus scattering, deriving the light-cone momentum fraction alpha_3N for the interacting nucleon. The analysis identifies a threshold value of alpha_3N = 1.6 above which 3N-SRCs are expected to dominate, and evaluates the ratio R_3 of per-nucleon cross sections for nucleus A relative to 3He in the region alpha_3N > 1.6. The prediction R_3 ≈ R_2^2 is tested against experimental data from Fomin (2011) for several nuclei including 4He, 9Be, 12C, 64Cu, and 197Au.

## Key numbers
- The threshold for 3N-SRC dominance is alpha_3N = 1.6, corresponding to a longitudinal momentum of the struck nucleon p_z ≥ ~700 MeV/c.
- For Q^2 ≥ 2.5-3 GeV^2, there exists a finite kinematic range with alpha_3N ≥ 1.6 where 3N-SRC dominance is expected; for Q^2 ≥ 5 GeV^2, the onset becomes practically insensitive to the recoil mass of the spectator system.
- The ratio R_3 for 4He to 3He at Q^2 ≈ 2.7 GeV^2 shows a plateau in the 2N-SRC region (1.3 ≤ alpha_3N ≤ 1.5) and an indication of a plateau in the 3N-SRC region (1.6 < alpha_3N < 1.8) consistent with R_3 = R_2^2.
- The prediction R_3 ≈ (a_2(A,Z)/a_2(3He))^2 shows reasonable agreement for A = 4, 9, 12, 64, and 197 when comparing weighted averages of R_3 at alpha_3N > 1.6 with R_2^2 values from Fomin (2011).

## Context
This work extends the experimental observation of strong pn dominance in 2N-SRCs from experiments at Brookhaven National Laboratory (eip1, eip2) and Jefferson Lab (eip3, eip4), and the theoretical understanding that this arises from the tensor component of the NN interaction at distances ≤ 1 fm (eheppn2, Schiavilla 2006). The prediction of a quadratic relation between 2N- and 3N-SRC plateaus builds on the light-cone formalism of Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and the analysis of three-nucleon systems in eheppn2. The results enable future experiments at Q^2 ≥ 5 GeV^2 to unambiguously verify 3N-SRCs and extract the a_3(A,Z) parameters characterizing 3N-SRC probabilities, which could provide input for understanding super-dense nuclear matter dynamics.
