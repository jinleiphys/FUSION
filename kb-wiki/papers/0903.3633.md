# Confinement-induced Efimov resonances in Fermi-Fermi mixtures

> A Fermi-Fermi mixture of 40K and 6Li does not exhibit the Efimov effect in a free space, but the Efimov effect can be induced by confining only 40K in one dimension. Here the Efimov's three-body parameter is controlled by the confinement length. We show that the three-body recombination rate in such a system in the dilute limit has a characteristic logarithmic-periodic dependence on the effective scattering length with the scaling factor 22.0 and can be expressed by formulas similar to those for identical bosons in three dimensions. The ultracold mixture of 40K and 6Li in the 1D-3D mixed dimensions is thus a promising candidate to observe the Efimov physics in fermions.

# Confinement-induced Efimov resonances in Fermi-Fermi mixtures

## Key claim

A Fermi-Fermi mixture of 40K and 6Li, which does not exhibit the Efimov effect in free space, can be made to exhibit Efimov trimers by confining only the 40K atoms in one dimension, with the three-body parameter controlled by the confinement length rather than by short-range physics; the claim is conditional on the system being in the 1D-3D mixed dimensions and on the dilute limit where the effective scattering length is small relative to densities.

## Method

The authors employ an effective field theory approach to describe two-species fermions in 1D-3D mixed dimensions, where 40K atoms are confined to a one-dimensional line and 6Li atoms move in three dimensions. They solve the integral equation for the odd-parity three-body scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame, using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for qualitative insight and a full numerical solution of the integral equation for quantitative results. The three-body recombination rate constant is computed via the optical theorem from the forward three-body scattering amplitude in the dilute limit.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor for the logarithmic-periodic dependence of the three-body recombination rate on the effective scattering length is 22.0 (dimensionless), corresponding to \(e^{\pi/s_0}\) with \(s_0 = 1.02\) for the 1D-3D mixed dimensions.
- The Efimov parameter is \(\kappa_* \approx 1.91 / l\), where \(l\) is the confinement length.
- For \(a_\text{eff} > 0\), the dimensionless quantity \(m_A \alpha / (\bar{p}_z^2 a_\text{eff}^4)\) oscillates between zero at \(a_\text{eff} \kappa_* = 0.404 \, e^{n\pi/s_0}\) and a maximal value of 1.93 at \(a_\text{eff} \kappa_* = 1.89 \, e^{n\pi/s_0}\).
- For \(a_\text{eff} < 0\), resonant peaks in the three-body recombination rate occur at \(a_\text{eff} \kappa_* = -1.89 \, e^{n\pi/s_0}\).
- The critical effective scattering lengths where Efimov trimers merge into thresholds are \(a_\text{eff} \kappa_* = 0.0199 \, e^{n\pi/s_0}\) (atom-dimer threshold) and \(a_\text{eff} \kappa_* = -1.89 \, e^{n\pi/s_0}\) (three-atom threshold).
- The fitting parameters for the recombination rate formulas are \(b_+ = 284\), \(c_+ = 2.48\) for \(a_\text{eff} > 0\), and \(b_- = 143\), \(c_- = 0.528\) for \(a_\text{eff} < 0\).
- The predicted positions of three-body resonances in terms of the confinement length are \(a_\text{eff} / l \approx -0.989 \times (22.0)^n\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction by Nishida and Tan (2008) that confining 40K in one or two dimensions with 6Li in three dimensions can induce the Efimov effect, which is absent in free-space Fermi-Fermi mixtures. It provides quantitative predictions for experimental observation via three-body recombination, analogous to the successful detection of Efimov trimers in bosonic systems such as 133Cs (Kraemer 2006), 87Rb-41K mixtures (Barontini 2009), and three-component 6Li Fermi gases (Ottenstein 2008; Huckans 2009). The results enable experimental searches for Efimov physics in fermionic mixtures using species-selective confinement, with the key advantage that the Efimov parameter is tunable via the external confinement potential rather than being fixed by short-range physics.
