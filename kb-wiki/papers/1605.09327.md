# Non- Markovian Quantum Stochastic Equation For Two Coupled Oscillators

> The system of nonlinear Langevin equations was obtained by using Hamiltonian's operator of two coupling quantum oscillators which are interacting with heat bath. By using the analytical solution of these equations, the analytical expressions for transport coefficients was found. Generalized Langevin equations and fluctuation-dissipation relations are derived for the case of a nonlinear non-Markovian noise. The explicit expressions for the time-dependent friction and diffusion coefficients are presented for the case of linear couplings in the coordinate between the collective two coupled harmonic oscillators and heat bath.

## Key claim

The paper derives a system of nonlinear, non-Markovian quantum Langevin equations for two coupled harmonic oscillators interacting with a heat bath, and obtains explicit analytical expressions for time-dependent friction and diffusion coefficients. The key hedge is that the analytical solution is possible only when the functionals in the equations are replaced by their mean values considered to be weakly varying in time and the renormalized potential is approximated by a harmonic (or inverted) oscillator.

## Method

The authors start from a microscopic Hamiltonian for two collective coordinates (R and beta) with conjugate momenta, coupled linearly to a bath of harmonic oscillators. They derive Heisenberg equations of motion, eliminate the bath variables to obtain nonlinear integro-differential stochastic equations, and solve these using Laplace transforms and the residue theorem, assuming a specific Lorentzian spectral density for the bath. The resulting time-dependent transport coefficients (friction, stiffness, and diffusion) are extracted from the first and second moments of the collective variables.

## Key numbers

- The spectral density is chosen as \( D(\omega)|\Gamma(\omega)|^{2}/(\hbar^{2}\omega) = (1/\pi) \gamma^{2}/(\gamma^{2}+\omega^{2}) \), where \( \gamma^{-1} \) is the memory time of the dissipation.
- The dissipative kernels take the exponential form \( K_{1}(t) = \lambda_{1}\gamma e^{-\gamma|t|} \), \( K_{4}(t) = \lambda_{2}\gamma e^{-\gamma|t|} \), and \( K_{2}(t)=K_{3}(t)=\lambda_{1}^{1/2}\lambda_{2}^{1/2}\gamma e^{-\gamma|t|} \).
- The renormalization shifts are \( \bigtriangleup_{1}=\lambda_{1}\gamma \), \( \bigtriangleup_{4}=\lambda_{2}\gamma \), and \( \bigtriangleup_{2}=\bigtriangleup_{3}=\lambda_{1}^{1/2}\lambda_{2}^{1/2}\gamma \).
- The characteristic equation for the roots \( s_i \) (six roots) is given by \( g_{R\beta}^{2}(s_i+\gamma)^{2}/(m_1 m_2) - ((s_i+\gamma)(s_i^{2}+\eta\omega_1^{2}) - \lambda_1\gamma^{2}/m_1)((s_i+\gamma)(s_i^{2}+\omega_2^{2}) - \lambda_2\gamma^{2}/m_2) = 0 \).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of non-Markovian quantum Langevin equations previously developed for single oscillators (e.g., by Katia and references therein, and by MyAllDM, MyAllQDA, MyAllNT) to the case of two coupled oscillators. It generalizes the approach of Leggett (1987) and Weiss (1999) by not introducing a counter-term in the Hamiltonian, leading to renormalized stiffnesses. The derived time-dependent transport coefficients enable the study of dissipative quantum non-Markovian systems beyond the weak-coupling or high-temperature limits, with potential applications to the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions.
