# The Cronin Effect, Quantum Evolution and the Color Glass Condensate

> We show that the numerical solution of the classical SU(3) Yang-Mills equations of motion in the McLerran-Venugopalan model for gluon production in central heavy ion collisions leads to a suppresion at low $p_t$ and an enhancement at the intermediate $p_t$ region as compared to peripheral heavy ion and pp collisions at the same energy. Our results are compared to previous, Color Glass Condensate inspired calculations of gluon production in heavy ion collisions. We revisit the predictions of the Color Glass Condensate model for $pA$ ($dA$) collisions in Leading Order and show that quantum evolution--in particular the phenomenon of geometric scaling and change of anomalous dimensions--preserves the Cronin enhancement of $pA$ cross section (when normalized to the leading twist term) in the Leading Order approximation even though the $p_t$ spectrum can change. We comment on the case when gluon radiation is included.

## Key claim
The classical SU(3) Yang-Mills equations in the McLerran-Venugopalan model produce a Cronin enhancement (suppression at low p_t and enhancement at intermediate p_t) in central heavy ion collisions, and quantum evolution via geometric scaling and anomalous dimension changes preserves this Cronin enhancement in pA collisions at Leading Order when normalized to the leading twist term, even though the p_t spectrum can change.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the classical SU(3) Yang-Mills equations of motion on a 2-dimensional lattice (256×256 for SU(3), 512×512 for SU(2)) with boost invariance, using the McLerran-Venugopalan model with Gaussian-distributed color charges characterized by a saturation scale Λ_s. They computed gluon number distributions in transverse Coulomb gauge for Au-Au collisions at b=0 fm (central) and b=11 fm (peripheral), and for p-p collisions with Λ_s0=0.2 GeV. For pA collisions, they analyzed the dipole scattering cross section and its sum rule under Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution.

## Key numbers
- Λ_s0 = 2 GeV for the color charge squared per unit transverse area in the center of each nucleus; the average over the entire nucleus is ~1.4 GeV
- For p-p collisions, Λ_s0 = 0.2 GeV was used as the small saturation scale
- The gluon liberation coefficient f_N from numerical results is 0.3-0.5, compared to analytical factorized results that are 3-4 times larger
- For mid-rapidity high p_t processes at RHIC, the effective x range is ~0.05-0.1 for p_t ~5-10 GeV

## Context
This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) by demonstrating that its classical Yang-Mills solutions produce the Cronin effect (Cronin 1975) in AA collisions, contrary to earlier saturation-inspired models by Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2002) and Kharzeev, Levin, and Tuchin (2003) that missed this enhancement due to using k_t-factorized approximations. The pA analysis revisits earlier work by Jalilian-Marian, Kovchegov, and collaborators and shows that quantum evolution via the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation preserves the Cronin enhancement through a sum rule. This enables future work to include both quantum evolution and the Cronin effect in numerical simulations to quantify parton energy loss contributions in heavy ion collisions.
