# Pairing Dynamics of Polar States in a Quenched p-wave Superfluid Fermi Gas

> We study the pairing dynamics of polar states in a single species p-wave superfluid Fermi gas following a sudden change of the interaction strength. The anisotropy of pair interaction together with the presence of the centrifugal barrier results in profoundly different pairing dynamics compared to the s-wave case. Depending on the direction of quenches, quench to the BCS regime results in a large oscillatory depletion of momentum occupation inside the Fermi sea or a large filling of momentum occupation. We elucidate a crucial role of the resonant state supported by the centrifugal barrier in the pairing dynamics.

## Key claim

The pairing dynamics of polar states in a p-wave superfluid Fermi gas following a sudden change of interaction strength is qualitatively different from the s-wave case due to the anisotropy of pair interaction and the centrifugal barrier; the resonant state supported by the centrifugal barrier plays a crucial role, and the dynamics depends on the direction of the quench (forward or backward) and whether the final state lies in the BCS or BEC regime.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations within a mean-field approximation for a single-species polarized Fermi gas with a separable p-wave interaction potential of the form V₁(k,k') = -4πg Γ₀*(k)Γ₀(k'), where Γ₀(k) ∝ k k₀/(k²+k₀²) Y₁,₀(ˆk) with Y₁,₀(ˆk) ∝ cosθ. The initial ground state at a given scattering volume a_p is suddenly quenched to a final value, and the time evolution of the pairing field Δ₀(t), momentum occupation |v_k|², and pair amplitude u*_k v_k is computed in three-dimensional momentum space.

## Key numbers

- For a forward quench from 1/k_F³a_p = -20 to -10 (BCS to BCS), the hole-burning oscillation frequency is ω ≈ 0.12 ε_F, which is much smaller than the resonant state energy: ω/E_r ≈ 0.12.
- For a forward quench from 1/k_F³a_p = -20 to -10, the hole-burning point occurs at k = k_h satisfying k_h²/2M ≈ E_r/2 ≈ 0.5 ε_F.
- For a backward quench from 1/k_F³a_p = -10 to -20 (BCS to BCS), the particle-peak occurs at k = k_p satisfying k_p²/2M ≈ E_r/2 ≈ ε_F.
- For a backward quench from 1/k_F³a_p = +10 to -15 (BEC to BCS), the particle-peak occurs at k = k_p satisfying k_p²/2M ≈ E_r/2 ≈ 0.8 ε_F.
- The asymptotic value of the pairing amplitude |Δ₀| in the BEC limit is Δ_BEC = 8 ε_F (0.5 k₀²/9 ε_F)^{1/4} ≈ 29.2 ε_F.
- The quench time interval is δt ≈ 0.01/ε_F, with ε_F = k_F²/2M and k_F = (6π² N/V)^{1/3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pairing dynamics in s-wave Fermi gases (Barankov 2004, Andreev 2004, Yuzbashyan 2006, Gurarie 2009, Bulgac 2009) and theoretical studies of axial (p_x + ip_y) states in two dimensions (Foster 2013, Dong 2015, Dzero 2015) to three-dimensional polar (p_x) states of p-wave superfluid Fermi gases. The results clarify the crucial role of the resonant state supported by the centrifugal barrier in the BCS regime, which had not been identified in prior work, and the distinctive hole-burning and particle-peak dynamics in momentum occupation could potentially be observed in time-of-flight experiments.
