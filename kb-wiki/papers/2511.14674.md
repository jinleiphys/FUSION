# Deviations from the Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation due to threshold states

> Recent studies have completed the A=16 isospin quintets for states with spin/parity Jπ =0+ and 2+. The dependence of their masses as a function of isospin projection shows evidence for deviations from quadratic behavior indicating isospin violation beyond the expectation from two- body forces. The deviation is most pronounced for the 2+ states. Predictions from the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) allow us to explain that this isospin violation is associated with a modification of the nuclear structure due to the open-quantum-system nature of the proton- rich members of the quintet. In particular, the 0+ and 2+ states in 16Ne and the 2+ state in 16F are threshold resonances located just above a proton-decay threshold where s-wave coupling to the continuum is expected. The measured deviations of these threshold states from the quadratic behavior of the remaining members of the multiplets makes it possible to obtain information on the magnitude and the energy dependence of the continuum-coupling energy correction. Continuum coupling is also indicated for the ground state of 8C, but this time through p-wave coupling.

# Deviations from the Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation due to Threshold States

## Key claim
The observed deviations from quadratic behavior in the Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation (IMME) for the A=16 and A=8 isospin quintets can be explained by continuum-coupling effects in threshold resonances, rather than by isospin mixing alone. The effect is most pronounced for the 2+ states in A=16, where the 2+ state in 16Ne sits near the optimal energy for s-wave continuum coupling.

## Method
The authors completed the A=16 isospin quintets for Jπ=0+ and 2+ states using invariant-mass spectroscopy following proton knockout from a 17Ne secondary beam, with reanalyzed calibrations based on known p+14N resonances in 15O. For 16Ne states produced from neutron knockout, events with the heavy residue recoiling perpendicular to the beam axis were selected to reduce background and improve resolution. The continuum-coupling correlation energy Ecorr was calculated using the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) with Zuker-Buck-McGrory and Cohen-Kurath effective Hamiltonians, and a Wigner contact force with coupling strengths of 510 MeV fm3 for A=16 and 650 MeV fm3 for A=8.

## Key numbers
- Cubic term d = +4.0(22) keV for the 0+ quintet and d = +8.9(31) keV for the 2+ quintet in A=16 (the latter a 2.9-sigma deviation)
- Mass excess of 16Ne 0+ state: 24.047(10) MeV; 16Ne 2+ state: 25.766(12) MeV
- Mass excess of 16F 0+ state: 20.821(11) MeV; 16F 2+ state: 22.588(26) MeV
- Mass excess of 8C ground state: 35.067(13) MeV (weighted average of AME2020 and a newer measurement)
- Optimal energy for maximum continuum-coupling correlation: Ep = 0.58 MeV for A=16, Ep = 0.32 MeV for A=8
- SMEC spectroscopic factors: S_s1/2 = 0.41 in 0+ resonances and S_s1/2 = 0.35 in 2+ resonances of 16Ne and 16F; S_p3/2 = 0.75 in 8C

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin symmetry breaking beyond the traditional focus on isospin mixing with nearby lower-T states, which had been used to explain nonzero cubic terms in the A=9 quartet (Brodeur 2012, Mukwevho 2018) and A=32 quintet (Signoracci 2011). The paper provides the first quantitative explanation of IMME deviations through continuum coupling, building on earlier theoretical work by Grigorenko (2002, 2015) and Li (2025) on threshold effects in 16Ne, and on the SMEC framework developed by Okolowicz (2012, 2013). The results enable future studies to extract the magnitude and energy dependence of continuum-coupling energy corrections from measured deviations in threshold states, with the effect expected to be restricted to p- and sd-shell nuclei due to increasing Coulomb barrier in heavier systems.
