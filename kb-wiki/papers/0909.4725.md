# Classical strongly coupled QGP: VI. Structure Factors

> We show that the classical and strongly coupled QGP (cQGP) is characterized by a multiple of structure factors that obey generalized Orstein-Zernicke equations. We use the canonical partition function and its associated density functional to derive analytical equations for the density and charge monopole structure factors for arbitrary values of $Γ=V/K$, the ratio of the mean potential to Coulomb energy. The results are compared with SU(2) molecular dynamics simulations.

## Key claim

The classical strongly coupled QGP (cQGP) is characterized by multiple structure factors that obey generalized Ornstein-Zernicke equations; the analytical results for the two lowest structure factors (density and charge monopole) are derived for arbitrary values of the Coulomb coupling parameter Gamma, but the analytical density structure factor differs from molecular dynamics simulations at small momenta because the sound mode drops out of the Debye-Huckel colored potential used in the charging process.

## Method

The authors used the canonical partition function and its associated density functional to derive analytical equations for the density and charge monopole structure factors for arbitrary Gamma. They employed the Debye charging procedure with a linearized Poisson-Boltzmann equation and a Debye-Huckel-hole approximation to obtain the structure factors. The analytical results were compared with SU(2) molecular dynamics simulations using the Verlet algorithm for 108 particles in a periodic box, with a scalar core potential of the form V_core = (1/n)(1/r^n) with n=9 to ensure classical stability at short distances.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb coupling parameter is defined as Gamma = (g^2/(4pi)) * (beta * C_2 / a_WS), where C_2 is the quadratic Casimir and a_WS is the Wigner-Seitz radius (4pi a_WS^3/3 = 1/n)
- The Wigner-Seitz radius a_WS = 0.866 lambda for the simulation parameters with fixed volume V/(N lambda^3) = 2.72
- Radial distribution functions were computed for Gamma = 2.2, 6.6, and 12.8
- Static structure factors S_00(q) and S_01(q) were computed for Gamma = 2.2, 6.6, and 12.8
- The density at close packing is n_cp = 1/lambda^3, with lambda setting the unit of length scale

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous equilibrium analysis of bulk thermodynamics for the classical strongly coupled QGP (Cho and Zahed, 2009) to static structure factors. The classical model of colored plasma was originally suggested by Gelman, Shuryak, and Zahed (2006) and consists of massive quarks and gluons interacting via classical colored Coulomb interactions. The results enable assessment of transport parameters at strong coupling and can be extended to higher color SU(N>2) groups and multiple species including gluons, quarks, and antiquarks.
