# Uncertainty evaluation of peak energy of giant dipole resonance propagated from uncertainties of Skyrme parameters

> We evaluate uncertainty of peak energy of giant dipole resonance (GDR), propagated from uncertainty of parameters of Skyrme interaction. The Monte Carlo calculation of the random phase approximation using randomized Skyrme parameters is performed. Under the condition that the correlations between each of the Skyrme parameters is considered, the GDR peak energy has the uncertainty of $\sim$ 1 MeV irrespective of nuclear mass and is strongly correlated with the Skyrme parameters, in present calculations. This serves a guide for a new parametrization of effective interactions.

# Uncertainty evaluation of peak energy of giant dipole resonance propagated from uncertainties of Skyrme parameters

## Key claim

Under the condition that correlations between Skyrme parameters are explicitly included, the uncertainty of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) peak energy propagated from Skyrme parameter uncertainties is approximately 1 MeV regardless of nuclear mass, and the GDR peak energy shows strong correlations with the Skyrme parameters. If parameter correlations are neglected, the uncertainty becomes much larger and the correlations vanish.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo calculations by generating 1000 random samples of the SLy5-min Skyrme parameter set, using the known covariance matrix to enforce correlations between parameters. For each randomized parameter set, they solved the random phase approximation (RPA) equations for spherical nuclei using the skyrme_rpa code in coordinate representation with a 25 fm sphere radius and 0.1 fm mesh spacing, extracting the GDR peak energy from the dipole strength distribution smeared with a width of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca with correlated SLy5-min parameters: mean GDR peak energy 17.9 ± 1.5 MeV
- For ¹⁶O with correlated SLy5-min parameters: mean GDR peak energy 19.10 ± 1.21 MeV
- For ⁹⁰Zr with correlated SLy5-min parameters: mean GDR peak energy 15.70 ± 1.08 MeV
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with correlated SLy5-min parameters: mean GDR peak energy 12.93 ± 1.19 MeV
- Pearson correlation coefficients between GDR peak energy and parameters t₀, t₁, t₂, t₃ are 0.8 or -0.8 for ⁴⁰Ca
- With uncorrelated parameters, the GDR peak energies scatter over 5-50 MeV and Pearson coefficients are less than 0.1
- With the SAMi interaction (smaller parameter uncertainties), the GDR peak energy uncertainty is approximately 100 keV

## Context

This work extends covariance analysis studies of nuclear density functional theory parameters, such as those by Fattoyev (2011), Reinhard (2013), and Kortelainen (2013), by explicitly propagating parameter uncertainties to excited-state observables using Monte Carlo methods. The finding that GDR peak energies are strongly correlated with Skyrme parameters when correlations are included provides a pathway for improving effective interaction parametrizations by tuning parameters to reproduce GDR data, which has been difficult because previous studies did not clearly establish these relationships. The author indicates that uncertainty evaluations for other observables are forthcoming.
