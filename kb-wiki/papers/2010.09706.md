# Mass-dependent sequential freeze-out of hadrons

> Fluctuation measurements of hadron yields at heavy-ion collisions can reproduce the phase transition parameters of QCD matter. The fluctuation results produce accurate parameters near zero baryonic chemical potential($μ_B$) being very sensitive in that region. In this work, using the Hadron Resonance Gas Model(HRG), we determine the freeze-out temperatures of the hadrons using individual fluctuation results and find that sequential freeze-out of hadrons during the QCD phase transition near zero $μ_B$ is dependent on the mass of produced hadronic species. Using recently published results of net-$Λ$ fluctuations and some other fluctuation results, we study the phase diagram at low baryonic chemical potential and center-of-mass energies ranging from 19.6 - 200 GeV/$c^2$ and find significant, quantitative evidence to support our claim.

## Key claim

Using individual fluctuation measurements of hadron yields in the Hadron Resonance Gas Model (HRG), the freeze-out temperature of hadrons during the QCD phase transition near zero baryonic chemical potential depends on the mass of the hadronic species, with heavier hadrons freezing out at higher temperatures than lighter ones.

## Method

The author performed a chi-squared minimization to fit HRG model predictions for the ratio of second-order to first-order susceptibilities (sigma-squared over mean) to experimental cumulant ratios for net-proton, net-kaon, and net-lambda fluctuations measured by the STAR collaboration at center-of-mass energies from 19.6 to 200 GeV. The fit determined freeze-out parameters (temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, charge chemical potential mu_Q, and strangeness chemical potential mu_S) for each hadron species individually, with constraints enforcing zero net strangeness and a net charge to net baryon ratio of 0.4, and including resonance decay effects.

## Key numbers

- For net-kaon at 19.6 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 144.1 +/- 0.6 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 311.4 +/- 2.2 MeV
- For net-proton at 19.6 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 154.9 +/- 0.1 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 205.7 +/- 0.3 MeV
- For net-lambda at 19.6 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 156.7 +/- 0.9 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 212 +/- 2.5 MeV
- For net-kaon at 200 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 138 +/- 67.7 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 62 +/- 723.3 MeV
- For net-proton at 200 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 153.6 +/- 5.2 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 25.9 +/- 0.8 MeV
- For net-lambda at 200 GeV: freeze-out temperature T_ch = 153 +/- 1.2 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B,ch = 25 +/- 0.5 MeV
- The chi-squared error values from the fits ranged from 10^-2 to 10^-5

## Context

This work extends the approach of using fluctuation observables to determine freeze-out parameters, previously applied to net-proton and net-kaon fluctuations by Adamczyk et al. (2013, 2017) and net-charge moments by Adamczyk et al. (2014), by incorporating recently published net-lambda fluctuations from Adam et al. (2020). The results support a mass-dependent sequential freeze-out picture, in agreement with a previous study by Adamczyk et al. (2017) which found that excluding heavier hadrons from yield fits lowered the freeze-out temperature by 10-20 MeV, but contradict the flavor-dependent sequential freeze-out proposed by Bellwied et al. (2018) and Bluhm et al. (2018) where strange hadrons like kaon freeze out at higher temperatures than non-strange hadrons like proton.
