# Effective Theory for Neutron-Deuteron Scattering: Energy Dependence

> We report on results of the effective theory method applied to neutron-deuteron scattering. We extend previous results in the $J=3/2$ channel to non-zero energies and find very good agreement with experiment without any parameter fitting.

## Key claim

The effective field theory (EFT) approach, without explicit pions, reproduces neutron-deuteron scattering phase shifts in the \(J=3/2\) channel up to the deuteron break-up threshold with "very good agreement with experiment" and without any parameter fitting, but only when terms up to order \((r_{0t}/a_t)^2\) and \((p r_{0t})^2\) are included; the leading-order calculation (setting the effective range \(r_0=0\)) deviates significantly from data.

## Method

The authors constructed an EFT of nucleons and a dibaryon field (resumming contact interactions) to describe \(^3S_1\) two-nucleon interactions, fixing the dibaryon parameters \(g\) and \(\Delta\) from the effective range expansion using the triplet scattering length \(a=5.42\) fm and effective range \(r_0=1.75\) fm. They then solved the three-body integral equation for neutron-deuteron scattering in the \(J=3/2\) channel, projected to s-wave, by summing an infinite set of diagrams (Fig. 2) to order \((r_0/a)^2\). The resulting \(K\)-matrix equation was solved numerically to extract \(k \cot \delta\) for energies from zero up to the deuteron break-up point.

## Key numbers

* Triplet scattering length: \(a = 5.42\) fm; triplet effective range: \(r_0 = 1.75\) fm (from Nijmegen phase-shift analysis, used as input).
* Dibaryon coupling: \(g^2 = 1.6 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-1}\); dibaryon mass parameter: \(\Delta = 8.7\) MeV (derived from \(a\) and \(r_0\)).
* Theoretical quartet scattering length: \(^4a_{\text{th}} = 6.33 \pm 0.10\) fm; experimental value: \(^4a = 6.35 \pm 0.02\) fm (from Dilg).
* The calculation is accurate up to corrections of order \((r_0/a)^3\) and \((k r_0)^3\) relative to leading order.

## Context

This work extends the zero-energy EFT result for the quartet scattering length reported in Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998) to finite energies, showing that the same framework, without explicit pions, works up to deuteron break-up. It contrasts with potential-model calculations (e.g., Jim) which do not produce a pole in \(k \cot \delta\) near \(0.05\) fm\(^{-2}\) that appears in the EFT result; the authors suggest this pole may be smoothed by higher-order terms. The approach enables systematic, improvable calculations of three-nucleon observables and can be extended by including p-wave interactions or matching to a pionful EFT for higher energies.
