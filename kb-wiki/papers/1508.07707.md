# Construction of KbarN potential and structure of Lambda(1405) based on chiral unitary approach

> Based on chiral unitary approach, we construct the realistic KbarN local potential, which is useful for the quantitative calculation of Kbar-nuclei. Since the resonance pole structure of the KbarN system seems important for the Kbar-nuclei and the spacial structure of Lambda(1405), we establish the construction procedure of the local potential paying attention to the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane. Furthermore, for the quantitative study of the Kbar-nuclei, we consider the constraint from the recent experimental data measured by SIDDHARTA, which significantly reduces the uncertainty of the KbarN amplitude. With this new local potential, we estimate the spacial structure of Lambda(1405) and obtain the result indicating the meson-baryon molecular state of Lambda(1405).

## Key claim

The authors construct a single-channel local \(\bar{K}N\) potential that reproduces the chiral unitary coupled-channel scattering amplitude not only on the real energy axis but also in the complex energy plane, enabling quantitative study of \(\bar{K}\)-nuclei and the spatial structure of \(\Lambda(1405)\); the key qualifier is that the potential must be calibrated using the precise SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data and a new construction procedure that adds a complex energy-dependent correction and a high-order polynomial parameterization.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral unitary approach, which yields a coupled-channel scattering amplitude for the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) systems. They perform a Feshbach projection to obtain an effective single-channel \(\bar{K}N\) interaction, then construct a local potential \(U(r,E)\) with a Gaussian spatial form factor \(g(r)\) of range parameter \(b\). To improve reproduction of the amplitude in the complex energy plane, they add a complex energy-dependent correction \(\Delta V(E)\) parameterized as a polynomial (up to tenth order) over an extended energy range, and determine the parameters to match the original chiral amplitude at the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold and around the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance poles.

## Key numbers

* The average deviation \(\Delta F_{\rm real}\) on the real axis is reduced from \(1.4\times10^{-1}\) (previous potential) to \(4.8\times10^{-3}\) (Potential I) and further to \(4.0\times10^{-4}\) (Potential II).
* The \(\bar{K}N\) pole position from Potential II is \(1428-17i\) MeV, matching the original chiral value \(1428-17i\) MeV; the \(\pi\Sigma\) pole is \(1400-77i\) MeV, matching the original \(1400-76i\) MeV.
* For the SIDDHARTA potential \((I=0)\), the pole positions are \(1424-26i\) MeV and \(1381-81i\) MeV, matching the original chiral poles within 1 MeV accuracy.
* The root mean squared \(\bar{K}N\) distance from the SIDDHARTA potential \((I=0)\) wave function is 1.44 fm.

## Context

This work extends the local potential construction method of Hyodo (2007) by incorporating the precise SIDDHARTA data (Bazzi 2011, 2012) and by improving the reproduction of the scattering amplitude in the complex energy plane, which is essential for describing the two-pole structure of \(\Lambda(1405)\). The resulting potentials enable quantitative few-body calculations of \(\bar{K}\)-nuclei and confirm the meson-baryon molecular nature of \(\Lambda(1405)\) with a \(\bar{K}N\) distance of 1.44 fm, larger than the charged radii of the proton (0.85 fm) and \(K^-\) (0.55 fm).
