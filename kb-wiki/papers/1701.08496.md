# First Results with HIJING++ in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> First calculated results with the new HIJING++ are presented for identified hadron production in high-energy heavy ion collisions. The recently developed HIJING++ version is based on the latest version of PYTHIA8 and contains all the nuclear effects has been included in the HIJING2.552, which will be improved by a new version of the shadowing parametrization and jet quenching module. Here, we summarize the major changes of the new program code beside the comparison between experimental data for some specific high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The new HIJING++ event generator, based on PYTHIA8 and incorporating nuclear effects from HIJING2.552, produces identified hadron spectra that agree with experimental data in proton-proton collisions across a wide energy range, but shows deviations in the intermediate transverse momentum region (2–7 GeV/c) in proton-lead collisions where high-energy nuclear effects are active, indicating that further fine-tuning of these effects is required.

## Method

The authors rewrote the FORTRAN-based HIJING Monte Carlo event generator in C++, embedding a new `Hijing` class within the PYTHIA8 namespace and using its XML configuration and PDF handling extended for impact-parameter-dependent nuclear shadowing. They generated 10 million events for proton-proton collisions at five center-of-mass energies (200 GeV, 900 GeV, 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV, and 7 TeV) and 5 million minimum-bias events for proton-lead collisions at 5.02 TeV, calculating charge-averaged yields of pions, kaons, and protons within pseudorapidity ranges of |η|<0.8 (pp) and −0.5<η<0 (pPb).

## Key numbers

* Proton-proton collisions: charge-averaged pion yields calculated at center-of-mass energies of 200 GeV, 900 GeV, 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV, and 7 TeV, using 10 million events per energy in the range |η|<0.8.
* Proton-lead collisions at √sNN=5.02 TeV: charge-averaged yields of pions, kaons, and protons calculated using 5 million minimum-bias events in the range −0.5<η<0.
* In pPb collisions, the theoretical curves deviate from experimental data in the intermediate transverse momentum region 2 GeV/c < pT < 7 GeV/c, where high-energy nuclear effects are active.

## Context

This work extends the original HIJING model developed by Gyulassy and Wang, which was written in FORTRAN and based on PYTHIA version 5, ARIADNE, and CERNLIB PDFLIB. The new C++ version enables compatibility with modern experimental frameworks such as ALICE's AliRoot and allows future inclusion of scale-dependent nuclear shadowing via HOPPET evolution and jet quenching models such as the Gyulassy–Lévai–Vitev (GLV) formalism. The results enable systematic tuning of nuclear effects against high-precision RHIC and LHC data for the next generation of heavy-ion collision simulations.
