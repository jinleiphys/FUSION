# Gauge invariant reduction to the light-front

> The problem of constructing gauge invariant currents in terms of light-cone bound-state wave functions is solved by utilising the gauging of equations method. In particular, it is shown how to construct perturbative expansions of the electromagnetic current in the light-cone formalism, such that current conservation is satisfied at each order of the perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper solves the problem of constructing gauge-invariant electromagnetic currents in terms of light-front bound-state wave functions by showing that the light-front vertex function can be obtained directly from the light-front potential through a gauging procedure, enabling a perturbative expansion where current conservation is satisfied at each order.

## Method

The authors apply the "gauging of equations" method to the light-front reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two scalar particles. They define the gauging of a two-time quantity as first gauging the corresponding four-dimensional quantity and then equating the light-front times in the initial and final states. Using this definition, they gauge the light-front potential expansion to obtain the corresponding current operator, and demonstrate that the Ward-Takahashi identity holds for each order of the perturbation series. They then construct explicit expressions for the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) currents and verify current conservation and charge conservation at each order.

## Key numbers

- The light-front potential expansion: V = V_1 + V_2 + ... where V_1 = \tG_0^{-1} \e{G_0 K_1 G_0} \tG_0^{-1} (one-particle exchange) and V_2 contains crossed two-particle exchange and subtraction terms.
- The LO current: J_1^\mu = \bPhi (\tGamma_0^\mu + V_1^\mu) \Phi, where \Phi satisfies (\tG_0^{-1} - V_1)\Phi = 0.
- The NLO current correction: \delta J^\mu = \bPhi \delta \tGamma_1^\mu \Phi + \bPhi \Delta^\mu \Phi + \bPhi \tGamma_1^\mu \delta\Phi + \delta\bPhi \tGamma_1^\mu \Phi, where \Delta is the NLO potential correction.
- Charge conservation condition: J^\mu(P,P) = 2(e_1 + e_2)P^\mu for a two-particle bound state.

## Context

This work extends the theory of gauge-invariant currents previously developed for the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter approach and its three-dimensional spectator reduction (Gross and Riska, 1987; Gross and Van Orden, 1992) to the light-front formalism. It addresses the practical limitation that earlier light-front formulations (Garsevanishvili et al., 1974; Tiburzi, 2002) required infinite series expansions even for simple interactions. The method enables calculations of meson cloud effects on electromagnetic form factors, which are known to be important (Miller, 2002), and can be applied to models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Birse, 1990). The approach differs from the equal-time quasipotential method (Wallace and Mandelzweig, 1989; Phillips and Wallace, 1991) by providing wave functions with kinematical boost transformations while maintaining gauge invariance.
