# Nodal Lines in the Cranked HFB Overlap kernels

> Norm overlap kernels of the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states are studied in the context of angular momentum projection. In particular, the geometrical distribution of nodal lines, i.e., one dimensional structures where the overlap kernels possess null value, is investigated in the three dimensional space defined by the Euler angles. It is important to know the distribution of these nodal lines when one attempts to determine the phase of norm overlap kernels.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nodal lines (one-dimensional structures where the norm overlap kernel is zero) exist in the Euler-angle space for cranked HFB states at high spin, and that these nodal lines cause the failure of the standard sign-determination method for the norm overlap kernel based on continuity of the argument, leading to negative probabilities in angular momentum projection for odd-spin components.

## Method

Principal-axis cranked HFB calculations were performed for the nucleus ¹⁷⁰Dy using a pairing-plus-Q·Q force with standard parameters, with constraints on angular momentum and particle numbers. The norm overlap kernels were computed using the Onishi formula, and the sign ambiguity was resolved by a new optimization method that uses the third-order derivative of the norm overlap kernel (a complex quantity) to check continuity of both the norm and argument, rather than relying solely on the argument's continuity. Nodal line positions were determined by linear interpolation of the norm kernel on each face of the discretized Euler-angle mesh, solving for where both real and imaginary parts vanish.

## Key numbers

- Nodal lines first appear at angular momentum \(J = 12\hbar\) and are present up to \(J = 20\hbar\); no nodal lines are found for \(0 \le J \le 10\hbar\)
- For \(J = 14\hbar\), the old sign-determination method yields negative probabilities for odd-\(I\) components on the order of \(10^{-3}\)
- The new method restores positive definiteness up to \(I = 31\hbar\) for odd-\(I\) components and up to \(I = 34\hbar\) for even-\(I\) components
- The total probability sum \(P = \sum_{I<35\hbar} W^I\) is 0.998 at \(J = 12\hbar\), 0.991 at \(J = 14\hbar\), 0.981 at \(J = 16\hbar\), 0.965 at \(J = 18\hbar\), and 0.939 at \(J = 20\hbar\)
- The appropriate mesh size estimated from the uncertainty principle is \(\Delta\Omega \simeq 2\hbar/J\), giving \(\Delta\Omega \simeq 6^\circ\) for \(J = 20\hbar\)
- The integration validity limit for the mesh used (\(\Delta\alpha = \Delta\gamma = 5^\circ\), \(\Delta\beta = 1^\circ\)) is \(I \simeq 32\hbar\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous angular momentum projection calculations for high-spin states in ¹⁷⁸W (Oi et al., 2002) and ¹⁸²Os (Oi et al., 2000), where difficulties with negative probabilities were encountered. It addresses a known problem with the sign-determination method of Hara, Hayashi, and Ring (1982), which assumes continuity of the norm overlap kernel's argument and fails near nodal lines. The results enable more accurate angular momentum projection for cranked HFB states at high spin, particularly for studying odd-spin states such as high-\(K\) bands, and suggest that finer mesh sizes are needed for \(J > 20\hbar\).
