# Constraining the initial temperature and shear viscosity in a hybrid hydrodynamic model of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions using pion spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic radii

> A new framework for evaluating hydrodynamic models of relativistic heavy ion collisions has been developed. This framework, a Comprehesive Heavy Ion Model Evaluation and Reporting Algorithm (CHIMERA) has been implemented by augmenting UVH 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic model with eccentricity fluctuations, pre-equilibrium flow, and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamic (UrQMD) hadronic cascade. A range of initial temperatures and shear viscosity to entropy ratios were evaluated for four initial profiles, $N_{part}$ and $N_{coll}$ scaling with and without pre-equilibrium flow. The model results were compared to pion spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic radii from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions for the 0--20% centrality range.Two sets of initial density profiles, $N_{part}$ scaling with pre-equilibrium flow and $N_{coll}$ scaling without were shown to provide a consistent description of all three measurements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two specific sets of initial density profiles, participant scaling with pre-equilibrium flow and binary collision scaling without pre-equilibrium flow, can provide a consistent description of pion spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic radii simultaneously, but this conclusion is hedged by the restriction to a single centrality bin (0–20%) and a limited set of Glauber initial conditions.

## Method

The authors developed the CHIMERA framework by augmenting the VH2 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic code with eccentricity fluctuations from a Glauber Monte Carlo, pre-equilibrium flow following Vredevoogd and Pratt (2009), and the UrQMD hadronic cascade. They generated a grid of initial central temperatures and shear viscosity to entropy ratios for four initial profiles (participant and binary collision scaling, each with and without pre-equilibrium flow) and compared model results to published STAR and PHENIX data for pions at 200 GeV Au+Au collisions in the 0–20% centrality range using a chi-squared statistic that incorporates systematic errors.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature range for participant scaling without pre-equilibrium flow: 280–325 MeV for spectra, 260–380 MeV for v2 and femtoscopy
- Initial central temperature range for binary collision scaling without pre-equilibrium flow: 330–370 MeV for spectra, 300–420 MeV for v2 and femtoscopy
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio sampled in steps of 0.08 from 0.0001 to 0.48
- Fixed impact parameter of 4.4 fm, corresponding to mean Npart = 276 for 0–20% centrality
- Switching temperature Tsw = 165 MeV for transition from hydrodynamics to UrQMD cascade
- Minimum chi-squared for participant scaling spectra without pre-equilibrium flow occurred at Tcent = 0.310 GeV and eta/s = 0.08, with chi-squared per degree of freedom values of 9.27 (PHENIX) and 0.60 (STAR)
- Minimum chi-squared for participant scaling spectra with pre-equilibrium flow occurred at lower Tcent = 0.300–0.305 GeV
- For binary collision scaling without pre-equilibrium flow, minimum chi-squared occurred at Tcent = 0.345 GeV with eta/s = 0.08, giving chi-squared per degree of freedom values of 2.77 (PHENIX) and 1.75 (STAR)

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamic modeling approach of Luzum and Romatschke (2008) and Song (2011) by adding a systematic framework for uncertainty quantification across multiple observables simultaneously. It enables future comprehensive parameter extraction for the quark-gluon plasma by demonstrating that spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic radii can jointly constrain initial temperature and shear viscosity, though the authors note that a full determination will require extending the analysis to more initial condition profiles, including IP-Glasma (Schenke 2012) and KLN scaling (Kharzeev 2005), and to additional centrality bins and collision energies.
