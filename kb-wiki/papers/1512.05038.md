# Fluctuations of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions: An introduction

> Bulk fluctuations of conserved charges measured by event-by-event analysis in relativistic heavy ion collisions are observables which are believed to carry significant amount of information on the hot medium created by the collisions. Active studies have been done recently experimentally, theoretically, and on the lattice. In particular, non-Gaussianity of the fluctuations has acquired much attention recently. In this review, we give a pedagogical introduction to these issues, and survey recent developments in this field of research. Starting from the definition of cumulants, basic concepts in fluctuation physics, such as thermal fluctuations in statistical mechanics and time evolution of fluctuations in diffusive systems, are described. Phenomena which are expected to occur in finite temperature and/or density QCD matter and their measurement by event-by-event analyses are also elucidated.

# Fluctuations of conserved charges in relativistic heavy ion collisions: An introduction

## Key claim

This pedagogical review argues that cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations, particularly non-Gaussian cumulants up to fourth order, carry significant information about the hot QCD medium created in heavy ion collisions, but their measurement becomes statistically more difficult as the system volume increases, with the error of generalized susceptibilities growing as \(V^{(n-2)/2}\) for nth-order cumulants.

## Method

The paper provides a pedagogical introduction to fluctuation physics by first defining cumulants and their generating functions, then deriving relations between cumulants of superposed probability distributions. It analyzes specific distribution functions (binomial, Poisson, Skellam, Gauss) relevant to heavy ion collisions, and derives the volume scaling of statistical errors for cumulant measurements using the extensive property of cumulants in grand canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers

- For the Poisson distribution, all cumulants satisfy \(\langle m^n \rangle_{\rm c} = \lambda\) for any \(n \ge 1\)
- For the Skellam distribution, cumulants are given by \(\langle m^n \rangle_{\rm c} = \lambda_1 + (-1)^n \lambda_2\)
- For the Gauss distribution, cumulants satisfy \(\langle x^n \rangle_{\rm c} = 0\) for \(n \ge 3\)
- The skewness of the Poisson distribution is \(S = \lambda^{-1/2}\) and the kurtosis is \(\kappa = \lambda^{-1}\)
- The statistical error of the density \(\rho = N/V\) scales as \((\Delta \rho)^2 = \chi_2 / (V N_{\rm stat})\)
- The error of the second-order cumulant \(\chi_2\) scales as \((\Delta \chi_2)^2 = 2\chi_2^2 / N_{\rm stat} + \mathcal{O}(V^{-1})\)
- The error of the nth-order generalized susceptibility scales as \(\Delta \chi_n \sim \sqrt{\chi_2^n V^{n-2} / N_{\rm stat}}\) for large volume \(V\) and statistics \(N_{\rm stat}\)

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical foundation laid by earlier work on fluctuation observables in heavy ion collisions, including the superposition formalism developed by Kitazawa (2012, 2015). It provides the mathematical framework for interpreting experimental measurements of net-baryon, net-charge, and net-strangeness fluctuations, and enables quantitative understanding of why non-Gaussian cumulants up to fourth order have been observed in heavy ion collisions despite the general difficulty of measuring higher-order cumulants in large systems. The paper explicitly references the error analysis of Luo (2011) and the statistical error discussions by Morita (2013, 2012, 2014).
