# Signature of the N=126 shell closure in dwell times of alpha-particle tunneling

> Characteristic quantities such as the penetration and preformation probabilities, assault frequency and tunneling times in the tunneling description of alpha decay of heavy nuclei are explored to reveal their sensitivity to neutron numbers in the vicinity of the magic neutron number $N$ = 126. Using realistic nuclear potentials, the sensitivity of these quantities to the parameters of the theoretical approach is also tested. An investigation of the region from $N=116$ to $N=132$ in Po nuclei reveals that the tunneling $α$ particle spends the least amount of time with an $N=126$ magic daughter nucleus. The shell closure at $N=126$ seems to affect the behaviour of the dwell times of the tunneling alpha particles and this occurs through the influence of the $Q$-values involved.

## Key claim

The transmission dwell time of an alpha particle tunneling in alpha decay reaches a minimum when the daughter nucleus has the magic neutron number N=126, but this effect is mediated by the Q-values of the decay rather than being a direct signature of the shell closure itself; the assault frequency, by contrast, is too sensitive to the choice of the semiclassical parameter G to serve as a reliable indicator.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha-decay characteristic quantities for Po isotopes with parent neutron numbers from N=116 to N=132 using a standard semiclassical JWKB approach. The nuclear potential was constructed from a double folding model with realistic M3Y nucleon-nucleon interactions and a Woods-Saxon density distribution for the daughter nucleus, with the potential strength fixed by the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition. They computed the assault frequency, penetration probability, preformation probability, and transmission dwell time, and compared their behavior as functions of neutron number.

## Key numbers

- The transmission dwell time (τ_theory = ln2 / (ν P)) reaches a minimum at parent neutron number N=128, which corresponds to daughter N=126, for Po isotopes in the range N=116 to N=132.
- The assault frequency ν shows a minimum at N=126 when using G_< = 22 for N ≤ 126 and G_> = 24 for N > 126, but this minimum shifts to N=128-130 with other G choices.
- The preformation probability P_α displays a local minimum at N=126 for even N, with a magnitude similar to that in XuRen (2013) but differing from Paper1 by one order of magnitude.
- Experimental half-lives show a local maximum at N=125 and a minimum at N=128, with a steep slope from N=126 to N=127 spanning orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the study of alpha-decay characteristics near shell closures, following earlier investigations by Paper1 (2015) that used a toy model to study assault frequencies, and by XuRen (2013) who found that shell model effects become important near N=126. The authors also build on their own previous work (Kelkar and Nowakowski, 2015) relating tunneling times to nuclear half-lives. The results suggest that transmission dwell time, rather than assault frequency, could serve as a tool for identifying shell closures in other mass regions, though the different behavior of neutron versus proton magic numbers (e.g., no analogous dip at Z=84 for decay to Z=82) remains an open question requiring further investigation.
