# Constraining the 0ν2β matrix elements by nuclear structure observables

> The discovery that neutrinos have finite rest mass has led to renewed interest in neutrinoless double beta decay. The development of large-scale experiments to search for neutrinoless double beta decay has increased the probability of a credible observation of the process in the near future. The reliability of calculations of the associated nuclear matrix elements is likely soon to become a critical issue. In this paper experimental techniques that access properties of the ground-state wave functions of double beta decay candidates, the occupancies of valence single- particle orbitals and pairing correlations, are summarized and the experimental data for candidate nuclei are reviewed. The results are discussed in relation to questions concerning which aspects of nuclear structure may play an important role in determining the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while theoretical calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements are critical for extracting neutrino mass from future observations, the reliability of these calculations can be constrained by measuring experimentally accessible nuclear structure observables, specifically valence nucleon occupancies and pairing correlations, in the parent and daughter nuclei, though the quantitative impact of these observables on the matrix elements remains uncertain.

## Method
The authors review experimental measurements of single-nucleon transfer reactions (d,p; p,d; ³He,d; d,³He; α,³He; ³He,α) to determine valence single-particle orbital occupancies in candidate double beta decay nuclei, using a normalization procedure that requires the sum of measured occupancies and vacancies to equal the orbital degeneracy (2j+1). They also discuss two-nucleon transfer reactions (p,t; t,p; ³He,n) as probes of pairing correlations, where enhanced ground-state-to-ground-state cross sections indicate BCS-like pairing, while significant population of excited 0⁺ states signals departures from simple BCS behavior due to shell gaps or shape changes.

## Key numbers
- The upper limit on neutrino rest mass is currently 0.28 eV/c².
- Double beta decay with two neutrinos (2ν2β) has observed lifetimes in the range of 10¹⁹⁻²⁵ years.
- Neutrinoless double beta decay (0ν2β) is expected to have lifetimes greater than 10²⁵ years.
- For the ⁷⁶Ge/⁷⁶Se system, the normalization factors obtained from the sum-rule procedure were 0.53±0.02 for ℓ=1 and 0.54±0.06 for ℓ=3 neutron transfers.
- For the Ni isotopes, comparable normalization values were obtained: 0.57±0.02 for ℓ=1 and 0.52±0.03 for ℓ=3.
- The proton occupancies from the normalization procedure showed consistency at the level of around 0.2 nucleons across four targets (⁷⁴,⁷⁶Ge and ⁷⁶,⁷⁸Se).
- Adjusting single-particle energies in QRPA calculations after the transfer-reaction measurements changed the calculated decay rate by about a factor of two.
- For medium-mass nuclei, the cross section for ground-state-to-ground-state pair transfer is estimated to be enhanced by a factor of around 20–30 compared to population of non-collective 0⁺ excited states.

## Context
This review extends the experimental program initiated by the authors and their collaborators (Geser et al., 2010; Geser et al., 2011) to measure valence occupancies in double beta decay candidates, complementing earlier theoretical work using QRPA (Vogel, 2000) and shell-model (Poves, 2000) approaches. The measurements on ⁷⁶Ge/⁷⁶Se showed that prior QRPA calculations did not reproduce the measured orbital occupancies, and adjusting the single-particle energies improved agreement while changing the calculated decay rate by a factor of two. The work enables future comparisons between experimental nuclear structure observables and theoretical predictions, potentially allowing systematic constraints on matrix element calculations before a definitive observation of neutrinoless double beta decay is made.
