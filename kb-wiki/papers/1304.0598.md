# (d,3He) reaction on odd-neutron nuclear target for the formation of deeply bound pionic atoms

> We consider the pionic atom spectroscopy by the (d,3He) reaction on an odd-neutron nuclear target in this article, which has not been investigated so far. In the (d,3He) reaction on the odd-neutron nuclear target, we can observe the pionic states in the even-neutron nucleus with spin-parity 0+. We expect that this pionic state does not have the additional shifts due to the effects of the residual interaction between neutron-hole and pionic states. For the even-neutron nuclear target cases, we may have to take into account the residual interaction effects to deduce the binding energies of the pionic states precisely from the high precision experimental data since the final pionic states are the pion-particle plus neutron-hole states. Thus, in addition to widening the domain of the pionic atom spectroscopy in nuclear chart, the present study of the (d,3He) reaction on the odd-neutron target is considered to be important to deduce extremely precise information on the binding energies of the observed pionic states, and to know the pion properties and aspects of the symmetries of the strong interaction at finite density. We modify the formula of the even-even nuclear target case to study the pionic atom formation spectra on the even-odd nuclear target and show the numerical results of 117Sn target case. This experiment will be performed at RIBF/RIKEN in near future.

## Key claim

The authors propose that using an odd-neutron nuclear target (such as 117Sn) in the (d,3He) reaction allows observation of pionic states in the even-even daughter nucleus with spin-parity 0+, which should be free from additional energy shifts caused by residual interactions between the neutron-hole and pionic states that complicate the analysis of data from even-even targets.

## Method

The authors modify the effective number approach previously developed for even-even nuclear targets to treat the odd-neutron target case, assuming the 117Sn ground state has an extra neutron in the s1/2 orbit coupled to a 0+ even-even core. They calculate the cross section for pionic atom formation by summing contributions from two types of final states: those where the extra s1/2 neutron is removed leaving the even-even core in its ground state, and those where a neutron is picked up from other orbits leaving a neutron-hole in the core. The effective numbers are evaluated using the Eikonal approximation for distorted waves, and the realistic ground-state configurations are incorporated using experimental spectroscopic strengths from 117Sn(d,t)116Sn data to determine occupation probabilities and relative excitation strengths.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor FO for the 3s1/2 neutron orbit in 117Sn is 0.47, for 2d3/2 is 0.40, for 2d5/2 is 0.39, for 1g7/2 is 0.80, and for 1h11/2 is 0.11, determined from experimental data.
- The incident deuteron kinetic energy is fixed at Td = 500 MeV for all calculations.
- The instrumental energy resolution is assumed to be 300 keV FWHM in the calculated spectra.
- The reaction Q-value includes the constant Mn + Md - MHe = 6.787 MeV.
- The relative strength FR for the 0+ ground state of 116Sn from the s1/2 neutron hole is 0.68, with excited 0+ states at 1.757 MeV (FR=0.13), 2.027 MeV (FR=0.11), and 2.545 MeV (FR=0.09).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical and experimental studies of deeply bound pionic atoms formed via the (d,3He) reaction, which until now have concentrated exclusively on even-even nuclear targets (Yamazaki 1996, Gilg 2000, Itahashi 2000, Geissel 2002, Suzuki 2004, Itoh 2005). The authors note that experimental errors in the latest data from Suzuki (2004) are approaching the magnitude of estimated level shifts due to residual interactions calculated by Kaneyasu (2005) and Nose (2005), making the odd-neutron target approach important for extracting precise binding energies. The proposed experiment will be performed at RIBF/RIKEN in the near future, and the results could enable more accurate determination of the density dependence of the chiral condensate beyond the linear density approximation, as discussed by Ikeno (2011) and Goda (2012).
