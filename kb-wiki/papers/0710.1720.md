# Meson mass spectrum at finite temperature and density in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD for color SU(3)

> We investigate the meson mass spectrum in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD with one species of staggered fermion for the SU(N_c) color gauge group, including N_c=3. We analytically derive meson masses as functions of temperature and chemical potential via chiral condensates. We show that meson masses quickly decrease to zero when the chemical potential or the temperature approaches to the critical value.

# Meson mass spectrum at finite temperature and density in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD for color SU(3)

## Key claim
Meson masses (except the pion) decrease quickly to zero when the chemical potential or temperature approaches the critical value, but the behavior depends on the order of the chiral phase transition: at temperatures below the tri-critical point the drop is discontinuous (first-order transition), while at higher temperatures it is smooth (second-order transition).

## Method
The authors work in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD (omitting the pure gauge plaquette terms) with one species of staggered fermion, using a 1/d expansion and a mean-field approximation. They perform the path integral over spatial link variables, introduce the chiral condensate via bosonization, and evaluate the temporal quark hopping determinant analytically using the Vandermonde determinant technique combined with a recursion formula. Meson masses are obtained from the pole of the propagator for fluctuations of the chiral condensate, derived from the second-order variation of the effective action.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: $T_c(\mu=0) = d(N_c+1)(N_c+2)/[6(N_c+3)]$ (in lattice units, with $d=3$ spatial dimensions and $N_c=3$)
- Critical chemical potential at zero temperature: $\mu_c(T=0) \simeq 0.55$ (in lattice units)
- Tri-critical point temperature: $T_{\text{tcp}} = [\sqrt{225N_c^2 + 20d^2(3N_c^2+6N_c-4)} - 15N_c]/(20d)$ (in lattice units)
- For small current quark mass $m_0 \sim 0$, the pion mass satisfies $M_\pi \simeq 2\sqrt{\bar{\sigma} m_0}$, the PCAC relation
- Taste quantum number $\kappa$ takes values $\{-3, -1, 1, 3\}$ for $d=3$, corresponding to $\pi$, $\rho$, $a_1$, and $\delta$ mesons respectively

## Context
This work extends previous analytical studies of the effective potential in strong coupling lattice QCD at finite temperature and density by Nishida (2003) and by the same authors (Kawamoto, Miura, Ohnishi, 2005), which had derived the effective potential including baryon propagation effects but had not addressed meson masses. While hadron masses had been studied at zero temperature by Damgaard, Kawamoto, and Shigemoto (1985) and by Kluberg-Stern et al. (1982), this is the first derivation of meson masses as explicit functions of both temperature and chemical potential in the strong coupling limit. The results enable comparison with lattice Monte Carlo simulations in the strong coupling region and provide a general picture of meson behavior near the chiral phase transition that may qualitatively resemble real QCD.
