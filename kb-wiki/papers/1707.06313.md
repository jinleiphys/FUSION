# Rigorous constraints on the matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor

> The structure of the matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor play an important role in determining the properties of the form factors $A(q^{2})$, $B(q^{2})$ and $C(q^{2})$ which appear in the Lorentz covariant decomposition of the matrix elements. In this paper we apply a rigorous frame-independent distributional-matching approach to the matrix elements of the Poincaré generators in order to derive constraints on these form factors as $q \rightarrow 0$. In contrast to the literature, we explicitly demonstrate that the vanishing of the anomalous gravitomagnetic moment $B(0)$ and the condition $A(0)=1$ are independent of one another, and that these constraints are not related to the specific properties or conservation of the individual Poincaré generators themselves, but are in fact a consequence of the physical on-shell requirement of the states in the matrix elements and the manner in which these states transform under Poincaré transformations.

## Key claim

The paper rigorously demonstrates that the vanishing of the anomalous gravitomagnetic moment \(B(0)=0\) and the condition \(A(0)=1\) are independent constraints, contrary to the literature where they are often derived together from a single sum rule; these constraints arise not from conservation of the Poincaré generators themselves but from the physical on-shell requirement of the states and their transformation properties under Poincaré transformations.

## Method

The authors apply a distributional-matching approach within an axiomatic quantum field theory framework, treating hadronic states as distribution-valued states supported on the positive mass shell. They equate the matrix elements of the Poincaré generators \(P^{\mu}\) and \(J^{i}\) (and also \(K^{i}\)), expressed both via the standard action on momentum eigenstates and via the Lorentz-covariant decomposition of the energy-momentum tensor form factors \(A(q^{2})\), \(B(q^{2})\), and \(C(q^{2})\), and then match the resulting distributional expressions in the limit \(q \rightarrow 0\) using test-function smearing and distributional identities.

## Key numbers

- \(A(0) = 1\): derived from the constraint \(A(q^{2})\delta^{4}(q) = \delta^{4}(q)\) obtained from matching the \(P^{\mu}\) and \(J^{i}\) matrix elements.
- \(B(0) = 0\): derived from the constraint \(B(q^{2})\delta^{4}(q) = 0\) obtained from the \(J^{i}\) matrix element.
- \(\partial^{k}A(0) = 0\): derived from the constraint \(\partial^{k}\delta^{4}(q) = \partial^{k}[A(q^{2})\delta^{4}(q)]\), which formally corresponds to the vanishing of the derivative of \(A\) at \(q=0\).

## Context

This work extends the axiomatic QFT approach to energy-momentum tensor form factors, addressing inconsistencies in the derivation of spin sum rules found in earlier work by Jaffe and Manohar (1990), Ji (1997, 1998), and Bakker, Leader, and Trueman (2004). The distributional-matching method resolves issues with boundary terms and the handling of non-normalisable states that have plagued previous analyses. The results enable a model-independent, frame-independent understanding of the low-energy constraints on \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\), and the approach can be generalised to other currents (e.g., electromagnetic or axial form factors) and to arbitrary spin states.
