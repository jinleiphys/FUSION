# Chiral Spirals from Noncontinuous Chiral Symmetry: The Gross-Neveu model results

> It is shown that the inhomogeneous chiral condensate in the Gross-Neveu (GN) model takes the chiral spiral form, even though the thermodynamic functional depends only on the chiral scalar density. It is the inhomogeneity of the chiral scalar condensate that drives the spatial modulations of the pseudoscalar one. The result has broader implications once we start to think of fundamental theories behind the effective models. In particular, some effective interactions---which may be omitted for descriptions of the homogeneous phases---can be dynamically enhanced due to the spatial modulations of the large mean fields. Implications for the four-dimensional counterparts of the GN model are discussed. In a quark matter context, proper forms of the effective models for the inhomogeneous phases are speculated, through considerations on the Fermi-Dirac sea coupling.

## Key claim

The inhomogeneous chiral condensate in the Gross-Neveu (GN) model takes the chiral spiral form, even though the thermodynamic functional depends only on the chiral scalar density; the spatial modulation of the chiral scalar condensate drives the spatial modulation of the pseudoscalar one, but the amplitudes of the two densities are unequal because the net contribution to the chiral scalar density comes from the Dirac sea while that for the chiral pseudoscalar density comes from the Fermi sea.

## Method

The author revisits the analytically known inhomogeneous mean-field solution for the GN model in the large-N limit, using the Thies ansatz for the scalar mean field M(x) expressed in terms of Jacobi elliptic functions with variational parameters A and lambda. Fermion eigenstates are computed explicitly from the Lame form of the Dirac equation, and expectation values of the scalar and pseudoscalar densities are evaluated using the density of states and the known wavefunctions, with the Fermi surface placed at the gapped points to minimize energy.

## Key numbers

- The energy gap between the first and second positive energy branches is given by omega_F^2 = lambda_1 (where lambda_1 = 1 - lambda) for the first branch and omega_F'^2 = 1 for the second branch.
- The quarter period of the elliptic functions is the complete elliptic integral of the first kind K(lambda), which approaches pi/2 as lambda -> 0 and (1/2) ln(16/lambda_1) as lambda -> 1.
- The complete elliptic integral of the second kind E(lambda) satisfies lambda_1 <= E/K <= 1, with E(lambda) -> pi/2 as lambda -> 0 and E(lambda) -> 1 as lambda -> 1.
- The quasimomentum at the gap is Q = pi/(2K) = p_F/A (at eta=0), which determines A as a function of lambda.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in the Gross-Neveu model, previously studied by Thies (2003), by explicitly computing the pseudoscalar density to reveal the chiral spiral structure. The result contrasts with the QCD_2 and NJL_2 models, where the chiral spiral is manifest in the thermodynamic functional, and provides a basis for elevating the analysis to the NJL_4 model for quark matter applications, where tensor-type four-Fermi interactions may become dynamically enhanced in inhomogeneous phases.
