# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry in the shell model basis

> The proxy-SU(3) symmetry has been proposed for spin-orbit like nuclear shells using the asymptotic deformed oscillator basis for the single particle orbitals, in which the restoration of the symmetry of the harmonic oscillator shells is achieved by a change of the number of quanta in the z-direction by one unit for the intruder parity orbitals. The same definition suffices within the cartesian basis of the Elliott SU(3) model. Through a mapping of the cartesian Elliott basis onto the spherical shell model basis, we translate the proxy-SU(3) approximation into spherical coordinates, proving, that in the spherical shell model basis the proxy-SU(3) approximation corresponds to the replacement of the intruder parity orbitals by their de Shalit--Goldhaber partners. Furthermore it is shown, that the proxy-SU(3) approximation in the cartesian Elliott basis is equivalent to a unitary transformation in the z-coordinate, leaving the x-y plane intact, a result which in the asymptotic deformed oscillator coordinates implies, that the z-projections of angular momenta and spin remain unchanged. The present work offers a microscopic justification of the proxy-SU(3) approximation and in addition paves the way, for taking advantage of the proxy-SU(3) symmetry in shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) approximation, previously defined in the asymptotic deformed oscillator basis and the cartesian Elliott basis, is shown to be equivalent in the spherical shell model basis to replacing intruder parity orbitals by their de Shalit–Goldhaber partners, which differ by one quantum in the z-direction and have identical structure in the x-y plane. This provides a microscopic justification for the proxy-SU(3) scheme and enables its use in shell model calculations.

## Method

The authors map the cartesian Elliott basis onto the spherical shell model basis using a unitary transformation matrix R and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients C, then demonstrate that the proxy-SU(3) replacement rule (ΔK[ΔNΔn_zΔΛ] = 0[110] in Nilsson notation) translates to the replacement of intruder orbitals (1g^{9/2}, 1h^{11/2}, 1i^{13/2}) by their de Shalit–Goldhaber partners (1f^{7/2}, 1g^{9/2}, 1h^{11/2}) with the same m_j in the spherical shell model. They further show that the proxy-SU(3) approximation in the cartesian Elliott basis is equivalent to a unitary transformation in the z-coordinate that leaves the x-y plane intact, and that the operator T = a_z ⟨a_z^† a_z⟩^{-1/2} acting on intruder states with j_max = N + 1/2 and |m_j| ≤ j_max − 1 maps them to their de Shalit–Goldhaber partners.

## Key numbers

* The intruder orbitals 1g^{9/2}, 1h^{11/2}, 1i^{13/2} are replaced by 1f^{7/2}, 1g^{9/2}, 1h^{11/2} respectively, with the same m_j, to restore SU(3) symmetry in shells between magic numbers 28-50, 50-82, 82-126.
* After replacement, the spin-orbit like shells among magic numbers 6-14, 14-28, 28-50, 50-82, 82-126, 126-184 become 6-12, 14-26, 28-48, 50-80, 82-124, 126-182 shells.
* For the 1d^{5/2}_{3/2} orbital, the expectation value ⟨a_z^† a_z⟩ = 4/5, giving normalization constant C = √5/2, so that T|1d^{5/2}_{3/2}⟩ = |1p^{3/2}_{3/2}⟩.
* The transformation matrix R for N=1 has dimension 6×6; for N=2 it is 12×12; for N=3 it is 20×20 (tables provided for N=1 and N=2).

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2017) from the Nilsson model framework to the spherical shell model basis, providing a microscopic justification. It contrasts with the pseudo-SU(3) symmetry (Arima, 1969; Hecht and Adler, 1969) and the quasi-SU(3) scheme (Zuker et al., 1995), and shows that the proxy-SU(3) replacement corresponds to a unitary transformation in the z-coordinate, analogous to but distinct from the unitary transformation used in pseudo-SU(3). The results enable the use of SU(3) computational tools for shell model calculations in heavy nuclei away from closed shells, where full microscopic calculations remain prohibitive.
