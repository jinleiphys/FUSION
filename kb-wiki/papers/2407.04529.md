# Spectroscopy of deeply bound orbitals in neutron-rich Ca isotopes

> The calcium isotopes are an ideal system to investigate the evolution of shell structure and magic numbers. Although the properties of surface nucleons in calcium have been well studied, probing the structure of deeply bound nucleons remains a challenge. Here, we report on the first measurement of unbound states in $^{53}$Ca and $^{55}$Ca, populated from \ts{54,56}Ca($p,pn$) reactions at a beam energy of around 216 MeV/nucleon at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotopes Beam Factory. The resonance properties, partial cross sections, and momentum distributions of these unbound states were analyzed. Orbital angular momentum $l$ assignments were extracted from momentum distributions based on calculations using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) reaction model. The resonances at excitation energies of 5516(41)\,keV in $^{53}$Ca and 6000(250)\,keV in $^{55}$Ca indicate a significant $l$\, =\,3 component, providing the first experimental evidence for the $ν0f_{7/2}$ single-particle strength of unbound hole states in the neutron-rich Ca isotopes. The observed excitation energies and cross-sections point towards extremely localized and well separated strength distributions, with some fragmentation for the $ν0f_{7/2}$ orbital in $^{55}$Ca. These results are in good agreement with predictions from shell-model calculations using the effective GXPF1Bs interaction and \textit{ab initio} calculations and diverge markedly from the experimental distributions in the nickel isotones at $Z=28$.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of unbound states in 53Ca and 55Ca, providing experimental evidence for the ν0f7/2 single-particle strength of deeply bound hole states in neutron-rich calcium isotopes. The results show that the 0f7/2 neutron-hole states maintain a nearly pure single-hole character at high excitation energies, with good agreement with shell-model and ab initio calculations, but the abstract's claim of "extremely localized and well separated strength distributions" is qualified by the observation of some fragmentation for the ν0f7/2 orbital in 55Ca.

## Method

Unbound states in 53Ca and 55Ca were populated via one-neutron quasi-free (p,pn) scattering reactions on 54Ca and 56Ca beams at approximately 216 MeV/nucleon at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory. The reaction residues and emitted neutrons were detected using the SAMURAI spectrometer, NeuLAND demonstrator, and NEBULA arrays, while de-excitation gamma rays were measured with the DALI2+ array; invariant-mass reconstruction of the unbound systems was performed, and orbital angular momentum l assignments were extracted from momentum distributions using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) reaction model.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the resonance in 53Ca: 5516(41) keV, with a significant l = 3 component indicating ν0f7/2 single-particle strength.
- Excitation energy of the resonance in 55Ca: 6000(250) keV, with a significant l = 3 component indicating ν0f7/2 single-particle strength.
- Beam energy for the (p,pn) reactions: approximately 216 MeV/nucleon.
- Neutron detection efficiency: εn ≈ 37% and εnn ≈ 12% for decay energies at 2 MeV.
- Gamma-ray photopeak efficiency for a 2-MeV gamma ray: 23% with add-back; energy resolution after Doppler correction: 75 keV (σ).
- New gamma-ray transitions in 52Ca: 553(9) keV and 950(17) keV, leading to tentative level placements at 4543(10) and 4100(17) keV.
- Spectroscopic factor for the ν0f7/2 hole state from neutron-knockout of 52Ca: C2S = 6.6(10) (from prior work cited in the paper).

## Context

This work extends experimental knowledge of bound states in 53,55Ca from earlier one-neutron knockout studies by Chen (2019) and Koiwai (2022), and from a beta-decay investigation by Perrot (2006). The results contrast with the nearly 50% reduction in spectroscopic strength observed for the 0f7/2 neutron hole state in 49Ca from 50Ca knockout by Crawford (2017) and Macchiavelli (2022), and with the reduced l = 3 strength in neutron-rich scandium isotopes reported by Schwertel (2012). The findings enable systematic tracking of the ν0f7/2 single-particle strength evolution across the calcium isotopic chain up to 56Ca, providing crucial tests for shell-model calculations using the GXPF1Bs interaction and ab initio VS-IMSRG approaches.
