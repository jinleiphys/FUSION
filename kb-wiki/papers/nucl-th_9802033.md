# Dynamics of kaons in nuclear matter

> We consider $K^-$ nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering in isospin symmetric nuclear matter. It is found that the proper description of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance structure in nuclear matter requires a self-consistent approach. Then the $Λ(1405) $ resonance mass remains basically unchanged, however, the resonance acquires an increased decay width as nuclear matter is compressed. The elastic and inelastic scattering amplitudes show important medium modifications close to the kaon nucleon threshold. We also construct the $K^-$-nuclear optical potential appropriate for $K^-$ atoms. Our microscopic approach predicts a strong attractive non-local interaction strength.

# Dynamics of kaons in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The Lambda(1405) resonance mass remains basically unchanged in nuclear matter when a self-consistent treatment of the kaon and Lambda(1405) states is used, but the resonance acquires an increased decay width as nuclear matter is compressed; this self-consistent result contradicts earlier Pauli-blocked calculations that predicted a repulsive mass shift of the resonance.

## Method
The author constructs a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for K- nucleon scattering in isospin symmetric nuclear matter, using an effective Lagrangian with local four-point interactions that couple the K- N, pi Sigma, and pi Lambda channels. The in-medium kaon self-energy is evaluated in the nucleon gas approximation, with self-consistency achieved by requiring that the in-medium kaon propagator used in the loop integrals depends on the same self-energy it generates. The coupling strengths are fitted to reproduce empirical free-space scattering amplitudes from Kaiser, and range parameters are constrained by SU(3) symmetry.

## Key numbers
- Free-space K- N scattering lengths from the fit: a^{(I=0)}_{K-N} = (-1.76 + i 0.60) fm and a^{(I=1)}_{K-N} = (0.35 + i 0.69) fm
- At Fermi momentum k_F = 300 MeV, self-consistent calculation gives K- mass shift Delta m_{K-} = -140 MeV and width Gamma_{K-} = 35 MeV, compared to Delta m_{K-} = -141 MeV and Gamma_{K-} = 31 MeV from the free-space amplitude, and Delta m_{K-} = -123 MeV and Gamma_{K-} = 29 MeV from the Pauli-blocked amplitude
- The effective scattering length a_eff(k_F) changes sign from repulsive to attractive as density increases; at k_F = 150 MeV the effective non-local slope parameter b = (0.13 + i 0.13) fm^3
- The leading-order density expansion for the K+ mass shift at saturation density (k_F = 265 MeV) gives 28 MeV from the leading term and 35 MeV including the subleading correction, a 20% increase

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of kaon propagation in nuclear matter by Koch, Waas, and collaborators, who used a Pauli-blocked approach that predicted a repulsive shift of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The self-consistent treatment shows that the attractive feedback from the decreased K- mass counteracts the Pauli blocking, keeping the resonance mass nearly unchanged. The results are compared to the phenomenological optical potential of Mizoguchi et al. for kaonic atoms, and the author notes that the predicted density-dependent effective scattering length alone cannot explain kaonic atom data, but the combination with the predicted non-local interaction strength may provide a good description when combined with a small p-wave term from the Y(1385) resonance. The work enables improved calculations of K- nuclear optical potentials for kaonic atoms and has implications for pion and kaon production in heavy-ion collisions as noted by Dover.
