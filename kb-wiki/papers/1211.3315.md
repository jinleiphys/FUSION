# Toward the Ab-initio Description of Medium Mass Nuclei

> As ab-initio calculations of atomic nuclei enter the A=40-100 mass range, a great challenge is how to approach the vast majority of open-shell (degenerate) isotopes. We add realistic three-nucleon interactions to the state of the art many-body Green's function theory of closed-shells, and find that physics of neutron driplines is reproduced with very good quality. Further, we introduce the Gorkov formalism to extend ab-initio theory to semi-magic, fully open-shell, isotopes. Proof-of-principle calculations for Ca-44 and Ni-74 confirm that this approach is indeed feasible. Combining these two advances (open-shells and three-nucleon interactions) requires longer, technical, work but it is otherwise within reach.

## Key claim

Adding realistic three-nucleon interactions to the state-of-the-art many-body Green's function theory of closed-shell nuclei reproduces neutron dripline physics with very good quality, and introducing the Gorkov formalism extends ab-initio theory to semi-magic, fully open-shell isotopes, though combining both advances (open-shells and three-nucleon interactions) requires longer technical work.

## Method

The authors employed Dyson self-consistent Green's function (Dyson-SCGF) theory with the Faddeev Tamm-Dancoff (FTDA/ADC(3)) approximation for closed-shell nuclei, using chiral N^3LO two-nucleon and N^2LO three-nucleon interactions evolved via free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG) to a cutoff of 1.88 fm^-1. For open-shell isotopes, they introduced Gorkov self-consistent Green's function (Gorkov-SCGF) theory up to second order in the self-energy, which breaks particle number symmetry to handle intrinsic degeneracies of open-shell systems. Three-nucleon forces were incorporated through density-dependent interactions obtained by contracting with fully correlated propagators rather than a mean-field reference state.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of oxygen isotopes from SRG-evolved NN+3N interactions (cutoff lambda=1.88 fm^-1) reproduce experimental values throughout the isotopic chain and locate the neutron dripline correctly
- Matter radius of ^16O: 2.38 fm with full 2+3NF (experimental charge radius 2.718 +/- 0.210 fm)
- Matter radius of ^44Ca: 2.94 fm with full 2+3NF (experimental charge radius 3.520 +/- 0.005 fm)
- For ^44Ca with SRG lambda=2.0 fm^-1 interaction: neutron Fermi energy shifted to -8.69 MeV with full 3NF (close to experiment)
- Neutron shell gap between 0f_{7/2} and 0d_{3/2} in ^44Ca reduced from 12.9 MeV (2NF only) to 7.2 MeV (full 3NF); centroid gap of 9.3 MeV agrees with data-driven predictions
- Gorkov-SCGF calculations for ^74Ni show good convergence properties with model space using 2N SRG interactions with cutoff lambda=2.0 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends ab-initio nuclear structure calculations from the A~60 mass range (previously accessed by coupled-cluster (Jansen 2011), in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama 2011), and Dyson-SCGF (DiB 2004, FTDA)) into the A=40-100 range by incorporating three-nucleon forces and the Gorkov formalism for open-shell systems. It builds on recent developments showing three-nucleon forces determine crucial features of exotic isotopes (Otsuka 2010, Holt 2012, Hage 2012) and on the Gorkov extension of SCGF proposed in earlier work (papI, G.PRL). The results enable first-principle calculations along full isotopic chains of medium-mass nuclei, though a correct microscopical extension to include missing three-nucleon forces in the Gorkov approach remains to be developed.
