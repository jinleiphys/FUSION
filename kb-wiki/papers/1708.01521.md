# The symmetry energy $γ$ parameter of relativistic mean-field models

> The relativistic mean-field models tested in previous works against nuclear matter experimental values, critical parameters and macroscopic stellar properties are revisited and used in the evaluation of the symmetry energy $γ$ parameter obtained in three different ways. We have checked that independent of the choice made to calculate the $γ$ values, a trend of linear correlation is observed between $γ$ and the symmetry energy ($\mathcal{S}_0$) and a more clear linear relationship is established between $γ$ and the slope of the symmetry energy ($L_0$). These results directly contribute to the arising of other linear correlations between $γ$ and the neutron star radii of $R_{1.0}$ and $R_{1.4}$, in agreement with recent findings. Finally, we have found that short-range correlations induce two specific parametrizations, namely, IU-FSU and DD-ME$δ$, simultaneouslycompatible with the neutron star mass constraint of $1.93 < M_{max}/M_\odot < 2.05$ and with the overlap band for the $L_0\times\mathcal{S}_0$ region, to present $γ$ in the range of $γ=0.25\pm0.05$.

## Key claim
The gamma parameter of the symmetry energy, defined from the potential part as S^pot(rho) proportional to (rho/rho_0)^gamma, shows linear correlations with the symmetry energy S_0 and a clearer linear relationship with its slope L_0, independent of the method used to calculate gamma. Only two parametrizations (IU-FSU and DD-MEδ) simultaneously satisfy the neutron star mass constraint of 1.93 ≤ M_max/M_sun ≤ 2.05 and the overlap band for the L_0 × S_0 region, yielding gamma = 0.25 ± 0.05 when short-range correlations are included.

## Method
The authors revisited 35 relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations previously approved against nuclear matter constraints, and calculated the symmetry energy gamma parameter using three different definitions of the kinetic and potential parts of the symmetry energy. For cases 1 and 2, they separated the kinetic term either as the complete kinetic term (including effective mass dependence) or as a "free" kinetic term (using bare nucleon mass), while case 3 incorporated short-range correlations (SRC) from Ref. gamma025 into the kinetic part. Only parametrizations with a deviation Δ(rho) ≤ 15% between the exact potential symmetry energy and the power-law approximation over 1 ≤ rho/rho_0 ≤ 4 were retained for gamma analysis.

## Key numbers
- Gamma values from case 1 (complete kinetic term) ranged from 0.05 (FSUGold4) to 0.76 (BSR20), with only 7 parametrizations falling within the experimental range of gamma = 0.72 ± 0.19 from Ref. gamma072.
- For case 3 (with SRC), the two parametrizations IU-FSU and DD-MEδ simultaneously satisfying the neutron star mass constraint of 1.93 ≤ M_max/M_sun ≤ 2.05 and the L_0 × S_0 overlap band gave gamma = 0.25 ± 0.05.
- The symmetry energy S_0 values for the CRMF models ranged from approximately 30.97 MeV (BSR15) to 34.54 MeV (BSR20), with L_0 values from 51.74 MeV (FSUGold4) to 88.03 MeV (BSR20) in case 1.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of 263 RMF models from Dutra et al. (2014) and Dutra et al. (2016), which tested models against nuclear matter constraints and stellar properties, downselecting to 35 consistent parametrizations. The gamma parameter correlations with S_0 and L_0, and with neutron star radii R_1.0 and R_1.4, agree with recent findings from Steiner et al. (2009, 2010). The results enable further constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities, particularly by comparing with the experimental gamma ranges from the ASY-EOS experiment (gamma = 0.72 ± 0.19) and from SRC-inclusive analyses (gamma = 0.25 ± 0.05).
