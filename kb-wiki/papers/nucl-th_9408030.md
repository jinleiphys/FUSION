# Two-Body Correlations in Pionic Systems

> In close analogy to fermionic many-body theory the truncation of the bosonic BBGKY density matrix hierarchy on the two-body level leads to a coupled set of nonlinear equations of motion for the one-body density matrix and the two-body correlation function. These equations provide a nonperturbative description of the nonequilibrium time evolution of particle number conserving bosonic many-body systems including the dynamical resummation of parquet-like diagrams. Within this framework we study the properties of a pionic system as a function of temperature and density with focus on two-body quantities. For each temperature we find a related pion density for which the relative strength of the two-body correlation function assumes a maximum and the pionic system is far from the mean-field limit. Since these correlated phases up to $T$=200 MeV only appear at rather low pion density, the hot and dense pion gas as generated in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions should be well described within mean-field theory; i.e. the HBT analysis of pion sources from $π-π$ correlations should remain valid even in the case of strongly interacting pions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a nonperturbative bosonic correlation dynamics (NCBCD) that extends the fermionic NQCD method to bosonic systems, and they find that for pionic systems the two-body correlation function is maximized only at very low pion densities for temperatures up to 200 MeV, implying that the hot dense pion gas produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions should be well described by mean-field theory and that HBT analyses remain valid even for strongly interacting pions.

## Method

The authors truncate the bosonic BBGKY density matrix hierarchy at the two-body level by neglecting the three-body correlation function (c3), obtaining coupled nonlinear equations of motion for the one-body density matrix and the two-body correlation function. They then apply this NCBCD framework to a pionic system using a Hamiltonian with a two-body interaction mediated by sigma-meson exchange, solving for correlated near-equilibrium states as a function of temperature and density.

## Key numbers

* For each temperature up to T=200 MeV, the relative strength of the two-body correlation function assumes a maximum at a specific pion density, with these correlated phases appearing only at rather low pion density.
* The study covers temperatures up to T=200 MeV, above which the correlated phases are not found at any density considered.

## Context

This work extends the fermionic NQCD method (previously applied to nuclear many-body problems by the same group in references cited as [1]-[9]) to bosonic systems, specifically the pion gas relevant to ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results enable validation of the standard HBT analysis approach (used in works cited as [13]-[22]) for pion source imaging, showing that mean-field theory suffices for the hot dense pion gas regime even when pions interact strongly.
