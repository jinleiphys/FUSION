# Searching for squeezed particle-antiparticle correlations in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Squeezed correlations of particle-antiparticle pairs were predicted to exist if the hadron masses were modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions. Although well-established theoretically, they have not yet been observed experimentally. We suggest here a clear method to search for such signal, by analyzing the squeezed correlation functions in terms of measurable quantities. We illustrate this suggestion for simulated phi-phi pairs at RHIC energies.

# Searching for squeezed particle-antiparticle correlations in high energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Squeezed back-to-back correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs, predicted to exist if hadron masses are modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions, have not yet been observed experimentally; the authors propose a method to search for this signal by analyzing squeezed correlation functions in terms of measurable quantities (pair average momentum and relative momentum), and illustrate it for simulated phi-phi pairs at RHIC energies, noting that the signal strength depends critically on the unknown time emission distribution.

## Method
The authors simulate phi meson momenta using a non-relativistic model with a Bogolioubov-Valatin transformation linking in-medium quasi-particles to free observable particles, assuming a linear mass shift (m_* = m ± δm). They generate momenta with simple geometrical selection cuts mimicking PHENIX experimental conditions, then compute the squeezed correlation function C_s(2K_{12}, q_{12}) as a function of pair average momentum K_{12} and relative momentum q_{12}, including effects of radial flow and finite emission time via a Lorentzian time distribution factor.

## Key numbers
- The maximal squeezed correlation intensity for phi-phi pairs occurs at m_* ≈ 1 GeV, corresponding to roughly a 2% reduction from the asymptotic phi mass (m = 1.02 GeV)
- The finite emission time factor |F_s(Δt)|² = [1 + (ω₁ + ω₂)² Δt²]⁻¹ with Δt = 2 fm/c reduces the squeezed correlation signal by almost three orders of magnitude compared to instantaneous emission (Δt = 0)
- The squeezed correlation signal is expected to be significant for |2K| roughly between 0 and 100 MeV/c (depending on system radius R) and |q| between 500 and 1500-2000 MeV/c
- Three parameter sets for transverse mass distributions are compared: (T_fo = 109 MeV, β_T = 0.77), (T_fo = 157 MeV, β_T = 0.4), and (T_fo = 140 MeV, β_T = 0.5); the authors fix T = 140 MeV for their analysis
- System radii of R = 7 fm and R = 3 fm are compared, showing narrower (broader) correlation curves in 2|K| for larger (smaller) radii

## Context
This work extends theoretical predictions of squeezed correlations first established by Asakawa, Csörgő, and Gyulassy (1999) for bosons and later by Padula, Krein, Csörgő, and Hama (2001) for fermions, which showed that hadronic mass modification in hot and dense media could produce novel particle-antiparticle correlations via a Bogolioubov-Valatin transformation. The authors build on their own earlier studies of finite-size systems with radial flow (Padula, Krein, Csörgő, Hama, and Chinellato, 2005) and preliminary results on phi-phi squeezed correlations (Padula, Socolowski, Chinellato, and Nakamura, 2008), but shift focus from the theoretical maximum at exactly back-to-back momenta to a practical experimental search using pair average and relative momentum variables. The method enables experimental searches at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that a Levy-type time emission distribution (as fitted by the PHENIX Collaboration to Bose-Einstein correlations) could further reduce signal strength, particularly for heavier particles like phi mesons.
