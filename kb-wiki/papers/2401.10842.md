# Unified neutron star equations of state calibrated to nuclear properties

> Recently, in Malik23, a dataset of several EoS for purely nucleonic stellar matter based on a non-linear RMF model prescription, and constrained to properties of nuclear matter, to state-of-the-art chiral effective field theory calculations for low-density neutron matter, and to astrophysical data, were proposed. In this work, twenty one unified neutron star EoS were chosen from that dataset, in such a way that a large range of values of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation is covered. Several quantities are calculated and discussed, such as the the proton fraction and the direct Urca behavior, the density dependence of the speed of sound and the trace anomaly, the crust-core transition properties, the compatibility with astrophysical observations, and the neutron matter properties from $χ$EFT calculations and pQCD constraints. We construct unified EoS, where the outer crust is given by the BSk22 functional, and the inner crust is calculated from a CLD approximation. The core is purely nucleonic, made of protons, neutrons, electrons and muons, under charge neutrality and in $β-$equilibrium conditions. The correlation of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation with the crust-core transition density and proton fraction is analysed, and equations that translate these relations are proposed. Moreover, the spectral representation for all the EOS according to the format proposed in Lindblom10 is given, which is a convenient representation to study quasi-periodic oscillations with realistic EOS. It is shown that several of these EoS have in the center of the most massive NS a speed of sound squared of the order of $\lesssim 0.5$. Most of the EoS predict a maximum central density of the order of about 6 times the nuclear saturation density. Three of the EoS satisfy all of the constraints imposed. All these EoS will be made available in the CompOSE platform.

## Key claim

The paper presents 21 unified neutron star equations of state (EoS) selected from a larger dataset to cover a wide range of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation (22 to 70 MeV), but only three of these EoS satisfy all imposed constraints from nuclear properties, chiral effective field theory calculations, and astrophysical observations.

## Method

The authors construct unified EoS using a nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for the purely nucleonic core (protons, neutrons, electrons, and muons under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium), with the outer crust taken from the BSk22 functional and the inner crust calculated from a compressible liquid drop (CLD) approximation. The 21 EoS are selected from a prior Bayesian inference study (Malik 2023) to sample a broad range of the symmetry energy slope at saturation, and their properties are evaluated against constraints from chiral effective field theory neutron matter calculations, perturbative QCD, and astrophysical observations including mass-radius measurements and tidal deformability from GW170817.

## Key numbers

- The 21 EoS cover a range of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation from 22 MeV to 70 MeV
- The incompressibility of nuclear matter K0 has a median of 233 MeV, ranging from 190 MeV to 290 MeV
- The crust-core transition density rho_t has a median of 0.0944 fm^-3, ranging from 0.067 fm^-3 to 0.127 fm^-3
- The binding energy per nucleon epsilon0 has a median of -16.16 MeV, ranging from -16.40 MeV to -15.72 MeV
- The nuclear saturation density rho0 has a median of 0.155 fm^-3, ranging from 0.144 fm^-3 to 0.165 fm^-3
- Several EoS have a speed of sound squared of order less than or equal to 0.5 in the center of the most massive neutron star
- Most EoS predict a maximum central density of about 6 times the nuclear saturation density
- Three EoS (EoS 8, 18, and 19) satisfy all imposed constraints

## Context

This work extends the dataset of purely nucleonic stellar matter EoS from Malik (2023) by constructing unified EoS that include a consistent treatment of the inner crust via a CLD approximation, rather than using polytropic crust approximations as in earlier works such as Carriere (2002) or Malik (2022). The study enables systematic investigation of correlations between the slope of the symmetry energy and crust-core transition properties, and provides spectral representations following Lindblom (2010) for studying quasi-periodic oscillations. The EoS are made available on the CompOSE platform for use by the broader community.
