# Mixing of quasiparticle excitations and gamma-vibrations in transitional nuclei

> Evidence of strong coupling of quasiparticle excitations with gamma-vibration is shown to occur in transitional nuclei. High-spin band structures in [166,168,170,172]Er are studied by employing the recently developed multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell model approach. It is demonstrated that a low-lying K=3 band observed in these nuclei, the nature of which has remained unresolved, originates from the angular-momentum projection of triaxially deformed two-quasiparticle (qp) configurations. Further, it is predicted that the structure of this band depends critically on the shell filling: in [166]Er the lowest K=3 2-qp band is formed from proton configuration, in [168]Er the K=3 neutron and proton 2-qp bands are almost degenerate, and for [170]Er and [172]Er the neutron K=3 2-qp band becomes favored and can cross the gamma-vibrational band at high rotational frequencies. We consider that these are few examples in even-even nuclei, where the three basic modes of rotational, vibrational, and quasi-particle excitations co-exist close to the yrast line.

## Key claim
The low-lying K=3 bands observed in 166,168,170,172Er, whose nature had remained unresolved, originate from angular-momentum projection of triaxially deformed two-quasiparticle configurations, with the band's structure depending critically on shell filling. The authors claim that these bands represent examples where the three basic modes of rotational, vibrational, and quasiparticle excitations coexist close to the yrast line, but note that the correct description critically depends on the choice of basis deformation.

## Method
The authors employed the multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, which performs three-dimensional angular-momentum projection from triaxially deformed 0-quasiparticle (qp), 2-proton, 2-neutron, and 4-qp configurations. The projected bands were then mixed through diagonalization of a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian with fixed deformation parameters (axial deformation ε and triaxial deformation ε') listed in Table I. The calculations used a Nilsson mean-field with three major shells for each nucleon type and standard monopole pairing strengths G1=20.12 and G2=13.13.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters used: ε = 0.325 (166Er), 0.321 (168Er), 0.319 (170Er), 0.314 (172Er); ε' = 0.126 (166Er), 0.125 (168Er), 0.110 (170Er), 0.110 (172Er)
- Calculated unperturbed band heads of γ- and γγ-bands (relative to ground state): 0.7033 MeV and 2.0643 MeV for 166Er; 0.7009 MeV and 1.9651 MeV for 168Er; 0.8659 MeV and 2.0730 MeV for 170Er; 0.8659 MeV and 2.073 MeV for 172Er
- In 170Er, the K=3 band crosses the γ-band at spin I=12 and becomes the first excited band above this spin
- Calculated yrast-band B(E2) values for 166Er: 245.02 w.u. for (2,0)→(0,0), 351.63 w.u. for (4,0)→(2,0), 390.38 w.u. for (6,0)→(4,0)
- Inter-band B(E2) from K=3 to γ band in 170Er: 105.16 w.u. for (12,3)→(10,2), 40.04 w.u. for (14,3)→(12,2)
- Inter-band B(E2) from K=3 to γ band in 172Er: 110.24 w.u. for (14,3)→(12,2), 84.16 w.u. for (16,3)→(14,2)

## Context
This work extends the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach previously developed by the same group (Sheikh 1999, Sun 2000, Jin 2001, Yang 2002) and its generalization to include quasiparticle excitations (Bhat 2008, Jehangir 2009, 2010). It addresses the unresolved structure of K=3 bands in Er isotopes that had been observed experimentally (Caprio 2000, Borggreen 1995, Erdman 1991, Vigezzi 1994) but not explained. The results enable future experimental confirmation of predicted systematic appearance of two K=3 bands with proton and neutron structures, and the authors plan to adopt a mapping procedure (Alhassid 2006, 2008) to microscopically determine interaction strength parameters from realistic density functional approaches.
