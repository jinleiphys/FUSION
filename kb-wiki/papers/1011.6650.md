# A covariant study of tensor mesons

> We investigate tensor mesons as quark-antiquark bound states in a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation. As a first concrete step we report results for masses of J^{PC}=2^{++} mesons from the chiral limit up to bottomonium and sketch a comparison to experimental data. All covariant structures of the fermion-antifermion system are taken into account and their roles and importance discussed in two different bases. We also present the general construction principle for covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of mesons with any spin and find eight covariant structures for any J>0.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter study of tensor mesons that is consistent with the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, finding that the masses of $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ states agree with experimental data significantly better than axial-vector mesons do in the same rainbow-ladder truncation, suggesting tensor mesons are simpler and less sensitive to details of the quark-gluon vertex.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean momentum space using rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, with a phenomenologically motivated effective interaction that preserves the one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD. They construct all eight covariant structures for the $2^{++}$ Bethe-Salpeter amplitude explicitly, using both the original basis from Smith (1969) and an orthonormal basis generated via Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization, and generalize the construction principle to mesons of any spin $J>0$.

## Key numbers

- For the $s\bar{s}$ $2^{++}$ state with $\omega=0.4$ GeV, the full mass is 1.448 GeV; omitting covariant 6 from the basis of Eq. (8) changes the mass to 1.287 GeV (a decrease of 0.161 GeV)
- For the $u/d$ $2^{++}$ state at $\omega=0.5$ GeV, the extrapolation uncertainty is $\pm 75$ MeV
- The effective interaction uses parameters $m_t=0.5$ GeV, $\Lambda_\mathrm{QCD}^{N_f=4}=0.234$ GeV, $N_f=4$, and $\gamma_m=12/(33-2N_f)$
- The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied at the per-mill level in the calculations
- Omitting the contribution from $N^{\mu\nu}$ yields a mass 7% too low compared to the full result

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger-Bethe-Salpeter study of mesons by Krassnigg (2009) to tensor mesons, providing the first covariant treatment of $2^{++}$ states consistent with chiral symmetry. The construction principle for arbitrary-spin Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes generalizes the work of Smith (1969), who gave the tensor covariants only implicitly. The results enable future investigations of Regge trajectories in the covariant DSE-BSE approach and open the possibility to study states with $J^{PC}=2^{-+}$, $3^{--}$, and their radial excitations.
