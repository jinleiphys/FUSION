# Physical region for three-neutrino mixing angles

> We derive a set of symmetry relations for the three-neutrino mixing angles, including the MSW matter effect. Though interesting in their own right, these relations are used to choose the physical region of the mixing angles such that oscillations are parameterized completely and uniquely. We propose that the preferred way of setting the bounds on the mixing angles should be $θ_{12} \in [0,π/2]$, $θ_{13} \in [-π/2,π/2]$, $θ_{23}\in [0,π/2]$, and $δ\in [0,π)$. No CP violation then results simply from setting $δ=0$. In the presence of the MSW effect, this choice of bounds is a new result. Since the size of the asymmetry about $θ_{13} = 0$ is dependent on the details of the data analysis and is a part of the results of the analysis, we argue that the negative values of $θ_{13}$ should not be ignored.

# Physical region for three-neutrino mixing angles

## Key claim

The authors propose that the physical bounds for three-neutrino mixing angles should be chosen as θ₁₂ ∈ [0, π/2], θ₁₃ ∈ [−π/2, π/2], θ₂₃ ∈ [0, π/2], and δ ∈ [0, π), which is a new result in the presence of the MSW matter effect; they argue that negative values of θ₁₃ should not be ignored because the size of the asymmetry about θ₁₃ = 0 depends on the details of data analysis.

## Method

The authors derive symmetry relations for the three-neutrino mixing matrix using an algebraic formalism based on the invariance of the trace of operators under unitary conjugation, extending their previous vacuum treatment (Latimer and Ernst, 2004) to include the MSW matter effect. They express the mixing matrix in terms of exponentiated SO(3) generators Uⱼ(θ) and a CP phase operator Sδ, then apply conjugations by Uⱼ(π) to the full oscillation probability expression to identify equivalence classes of parameter quadruples (θ₂₃, θ₁₃, θ₁₂, δ) that yield identical oscillation probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Proposed bounds for CP-violating case: θ₁₂ ∈ [0, π/2], θ₁₃ ∈ [−π/2, π/2], θ₂₃ ∈ [0, π/2], δ ∈ [0, π)
- For no CP violation: θ₁₂ ∈ [0, π/2], θ₁₃ ∈ [−π/2, π/2], θ₂₃ ∈ [0, π/2], δ = 0
- Example regular hierarchy parameters: Δ₂₁ = 7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², Δ₃₂ = 3 × 10⁻³ eV², θ₁₂ = 0.60, θ₁₃ = 0.10, θ₂₃ = 0.80
- Example inverted hierarchy nearly-equivalent parameters: Δ₂₁ = 3 × 10⁻³ eV², Δ₃₂ = 7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², θ₁₂ = 1.45, θ₁₃ = −0.60, θ₂₃ = 0.70

## Context

This work extends the algebraic formulation of vacuum neutrino oscillations developed by the same authors (Latimer and Ernst, 2004) to include the MSW matter effect, and agrees with the bounds previously considered by Gluza (2000) while arriving at additional conclusions. The proposed choice of bounds with negative θ₁₃ is presented as an alternative to the conventional CKM-like bounds (θⱼₖ ∈ [0, π/2] with δ ∈ [0, 2π)) used in quark mixing and neutrino analyses, and enables a mathematically complete and unique parameterization of three-neutrino oscillations that naturally handles the mass hierarchy question without reordering mass eigenstates.
