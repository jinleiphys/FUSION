# Dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon collisions around 1 GeV/nucleon: a theoretical update

> We present a fully relativistic and gauge invariant framework for calculating the cross sections of dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon (NN) collisions which is based on the meson-exchange approximation for the NN scattering amplitudes. Predictions of our model are compared with those of other covariant models that have been used to describe this reaction. We discuss the comparison of our calculations with the old DLS and the recent HADES data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "DLS puzzle" – the long-standing discrepancy between measured dilepton yields in nucleus-nucleus collisions and transport model calculations – has now shifted to a proper understanding of the elementary nucleon-nucleon (NN) dilepton cross sections, because the HADES collaboration has confirmed the old DLS data. The authors show that their own model (SM2) produces NN bremsstrahlung cross sections that are 2–4 times lower than those of the KK model (Kaptari & Kämpfer), and that the SM2 results agree with HADES pp data while the KK model overestimates them, implying that the larger KK cross sections should not be used in transport models.

## Method

The authors use a fully relativistic, gauge-invariant meson-exchange model for NN scattering amplitudes, employing a pseudoscalar (PS) coupling for the nucleon-nucleon-pion vertex to avoid ambiguities from contact terms that arise with pseudovector (PV) coupling. They compare their results (SM2 model) with those of the KK model (which uses PV coupling) and the dJM model (based on Paris potential T-matrices), for both pp and pn bremsstrahlung and Δ-isobar contributions to dilepton production at beam energies around 1–2 GeV. For the quasi-free np case at HADES energies, they additionally include Fermi smearing of the center-of-mass energy using the Argonne V18 potential and contributions from η meson and Δ(1600) Dalitz decays.

## Key numbers

- SM2 pp bremsstrahlung cross sections are lower than KK model cross sections by factors of 2–4 for M < 0.6 GeV at beam energies of 1.04 and 2.09 GeV.
- For pn collisions, SM2 cross sections are a factor of 3–4 lower than KK model results.
- The Δ-isobar contribution in the KK model is larger than SM2 by factors of ~2 at smaller M values at 1.04 GeV beam energy.
- In quasi-free np collisions at 1.25 GeV/nucleon, the yield in the invariant mass region 0.15–0.35 GeV is about 9 times higher than in pp collisions.
- The ratio of quasi-free np to pp cross sections reaches almost a value of 100 at M = 0.5 GeV.
- The π-exchange process dominates the pn cross section and interferes destructively with the ρ-exchange term at 1.25 GeV beam energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SM1 model (Shyam 2003) and its updated SM2 version (Shyam 2009), and directly contrasts with the KK model (Kaptari & Kämpfer 2006, 2009) which uses pseudovector coupling and produces larger cross sections. The paper also compares with the dJM model (de Jong 1996) based on the Paris potential. The results are relevant for interpreting DLS data (Porter 1997) and recent HADES data (Agakishiev 2009), and aim to resolve the "DLS puzzle" that has persisted since the late 1990s. The authors note that even after including Fermi smearing and η and Δ(1600) Dalitz decays, the absolute cross sections around M = 0.5 GeV in quasi-free np collisions remain unexplained, indicating a new puzzle requiring further theoretical work.
