# Effective Hamiltonians for Complexes of Unstable Particles

> Effective Hamiltonians governing the time evolution in a subspace of unstable states can be found using more or less accurate approximations. A convenient tool for deriving them is the evolution equation for a subspace of state space sometime called the Krolikowski-Rzewuski (KR) equation. KR equation results from the Schrödinger equation for the total system under considerations. We will discuss properties of approximate effective Hamiltonians derived using KR equation for $n$--particle, two particle and for one particle subspaces. In a general case these affective Hamiltonians depend on time $t$. We show that at times much longer than times at which the exponential decay take place the real part of the exact effective Hamiltonian for the one particle subsystem (that is the instantaneous energy) tends to the minimal energy of the total system when $t \rightarrow \infty$ whereas the imaginary part of this effective Hamiltonian tends to the zero as $t\rightarrow \infty$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the exact effective Hamiltonian for a one-particle unstable subsystem depends on time, and at times much longer than the exponential decay regime, its real part (instantaneous energy) tends to the minimal energy of the total system as \(t \rightarrow \infty\), while its imaginary part tends to zero as \(t \rightarrow \infty\). This asymptotic behavior is a condition that the abstract glosses over: it applies only at times \(t \gg t_{as}\), where \(t_{as}\) is the crossover time when nonexponential deviations begin to dominate.

## Method

The author derives approximate effective Hamiltonians for \(n\)-particle, two-particle, and one-particle subspaces using the Krolikowski-Rzewuski (KR) equation, which follows from the Schrödinger equation for the total system. For the one-particle case, the exact effective Hamiltonian \(h(t)\) is expressed in terms of the survival amplitude \(a(t)\) as \(h(t) = i (1/a(t)) (\partial a(t)/\partial t)\). The asymptotic form of \(h(t)\) for large \(t\) is obtained by analyzing the Fourier transform of the energy density \(\omega(E)\) under the assumption that the spectrum of the total Hamiltonian \(H\) is bounded from below, \([E_{\text{min}}, +\infty)\).

## Key numbers

- The survival probability \({\cal P}(t) = |a(t)|^2\) at the crossover time \(t_{as}\) satisfies \({\cal P}(t_{as}) \simeq \exp[-\gamma^0_{\phi} t_{as}]\), where \(\gamma^0_{\phi}\) is the decay width.
- The asymptotic expansion of the effective Hamiltonian for \(t \rightarrow \infty\) is \(h(t) \simeq E_{\text{min}} + (-i/t) c_1 + (-i/t)^2 c_2 + \ldots\), with real coefficients \(c_i\).
- Numerical calculations in the paper use the ratio \(E_{\alpha}^{0} / \gamma_{\alpha}^{0} = 25\) for the one-particle case, where \(E_{\alpha}^{0}\) is the energy and \(\gamma_{\alpha}^{0}\) is the decay width at canonical decay times.

## Context

This work extends the Weisskopf-Wigner (WW) and Lee-Oehme-Yang (LOY) approximations, which describe unstable particle evolution accurately only for times up to the transition time \(t_{as}\), beyond which nonexponential decay dominates. The paper builds on the Paley-Wiener theorem (Khalfin, 1958) showing that decay laws cannot be purely exponential at long times, and on experimental confirmation of late-time deviations from exponential decay (Rothe, 2012). The derived time-dependent effective Hamiltonians enable analysis of very long time properties of unstable particle complexes, with potential applications in cosmology and astrophysics where large numbers of unstable particles may survive to late times.
