# Fluctuations and correlations of quark spin in hot and dense QCD matter

> In this work, we examine the impact of QCD phase transitions on the quark spin fluctuations and correlations. We propose the quark-antiquark correlation, which relates to the vector meson spin alignment and the $Λ-\barΛ$ correlation, can be used as a novel probe of the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram. Using the Nambu-Jona-Lanisio model, we qualitatively study the properties of quark-antiquark spin correlations. Our findings reveal a peak structure near the CEP of the chiral phase transition, which may serve as an experimental signature of the CEP and account for the non-monotonic behavior of $ϕ$ meson alignment at low collision energies observed recently in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quark-antiquark spin correlations and fluctuations are enhanced near the QCD critical end point (CEP), and that this enhancement can produce observable signatures in vector meson spin alignment and hyperon-anti-hyperon correlations, potentially explaining the non-monotonic behavior of phi meson spin alignment at low collision energies. The claim is explicitly qualified as a qualitative study using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and the authors note that the phase boundary obtained in their NJL model is not in quantitative agreement with QCD results.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in a rotating frame with constant angular velocity Omega = 10 MeV to compute the thermodynamic potential and its derivatives. They define a quark-antiquark spin correlation as the ratio of spin cumulants to number cumulants, and calculate it by taking derivatives of the partition function with respect to rotation parameters Omega_q and Omega_qbar that act separately on quarks and antiquarks. The calculations are performed at r=0 (the rotation axis) where orbital angular momentum vanishes, allowing the spin contribution to be isolated, with additional calculations at r not equal to 0 included in supplemental materials.

## Key numbers

- The CEP in the NJL model is located at T_CEP = 82.1 MeV and mu_CEP = 322 MeV
- The angular velocity is fixed at Omega = 10 MeV, described as a typical value for fluid vorticity in experiment
- The NJL model parameters used: Lambda = 587.9 MeV, m_0 = 5.6 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 2.44, N_c = 3, N_f = 2
- The estimated order of the overall contribution from critical fluctuations to delta_rho_00^Omega is about 10^{-2}
- The quark chemical potential is related to collision energy via mu = 2.477/(1 + 0.343*sqrt(s))

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy ion collisions, which has been observed experimentally by STAR (2017, 2022), by connecting spin fluctuations to QCD phase transitions for the first time. It builds on the framework of baryon number fluctuations near the CEP (Stephanov, 2011) and recent STAR results on net-proton cumulant ratios showing a 3.1 sigma non-monotonic energy dependence (STAR, 2021). The paper proposes that quark-antiquark spin correlations, measurable through phi meson spin alignment or hyperon-anti-hyperon correlations, can serve as a novel experimental signature of the CEP alongside net baryon number fluctuations.
