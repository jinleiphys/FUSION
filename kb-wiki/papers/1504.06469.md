# A compression algorithm for the combination of PDF sets

> The current PDF4LHC recommendation to estimate uncertainties due to parton distribution functions (PDFs) in theoretical predictions for LHC processes involves the combination of separate predictions computed using PDF sets from different groups, each of which comprises a relatively large number of either Hessian eigenvectors or Monte Carlo (MC) replicas. While many fixed-order and parton shower programs allow the evaluation of PDF uncertainties for a single PDF set at no additional CPU cost, this feature is not universal, and moreover the a posteriori combination of the predictions using at least three different PDF sets is still required. In this work, we present a strategy for the statistical combination of individual PDF sets, based on the MC representation of Hessian sets, followed by a compression algorithm for the reduction of the number of MC replicas. We illustrate our strategy with the combination and compression of the recent NNPDF3.0, CT14 and MMHT14 NNLO PDF sets. The resulting Compressed Monte Carlo PDF (CMC-PDF) sets are validated at the level of parton luminosities and LHC inclusive cross-sections and differential distributions. We determine that around 100 replicas provide an adequate representation of the probability distribution for the original combined PDF set, suitable for general applications to LHC phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors present a compression algorithm that reduces the combined Monte Carlo representation of three global PDF sets (NNPDF3.0, CT14, and MMHT14) from 900 replicas down to around 100 replicas, while preserving the statistical features of the original combined probability distribution for LHC phenomenology applications.

## Method

The authors first convert Hessian PDF sets (CT14 and MMHT14) into Monte Carlo representations using the Watt-Thorne method, then combine 300 replicas from each of the three NNLO sets (NNPDF3.0, CT14, MMHT14) to form a 900-replica prior (MC900). They apply a compression algorithm that minimizes an error function (ERF) to select a subset of replicas that best reproduces the statistical properties of the prior, producing Compressed Monte Carlo PDF (CMC-PDF) sets.

## Key numbers

* The combined prior MC900 contains 900 replicas (300 from each of NNPDF3.0, CT14, and MMHT14 NNLO sets) at a common value of alpha_s(M_Z)=0.118.
* The compression reduces the number of replicas to around 100, providing an adequate representation of the probability distribution for the original combined PDF set.
* The comparison of PDFs is performed at a typical LHC scale of Q=100 GeV.
* LHC benchmark cross-sections are computed at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo combination method originally proposed by Forte (2010) and developed by Forte and Watt (2012-2013), providing a practical compression algorithm to reduce the computational burden of using the full 900-replica combined set. It provides an alternative to the Meta-PDF method of Gao (2013) and the envelope method of the PDF4LHC recommendation (Alekhin 2011, Botje 2011), enabling a single combined PDF set with well-defined statistical interpretation for LHC phenomenology.
