# Lattice Boltzmann study of the one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion with anisotropic initial conditions

> A numerical algorithm for the implementation of anisotropic distributions in the frame of the relativistic Boltzmann equation is presented. The implementation relies on the expansion of the Romatschke-Strickland distribution with respect to orthogonal polynomials, which is evolved using the lattice Boltzmann algorithm. The validation of our proposed scheme is performed in the context of the one-dimensional boost invariant expansion (Bjorken flow) at various values of the ratio $η/ s$ of the shear viscosity to the entropy density. This study is limited to the case of massless particles obeying Maxwell-Jüttner statistics.

## Key claim

The lattice Boltzmann algorithm can be extended to handle anisotropic initial conditions for the one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion (Bjorken flow) by expanding the Romatschke-Strickland distribution in orthogonal polynomials, but the study is limited to massless particles obeying Maxwell-Juttner statistics and the accuracy depends on the truncation order of the Legendre polynomial expansion.

## Method

The authors discretize momentum space using Gauss-Laguerre quadrature of order 2 for the momentum magnitude and Gauss-Legendre quadrature of order Q_xi for the angular variable xi = p^eta_M / p^tau. The Romatschke-Strickland distribution is expanded in Laguerre and Legendre polynomials with truncation order N_Omega = 6, and the relativistic Boltzmann equation with the Anderson-Witting collision term is evolved using a third-order Runge-Kutta algorithm with time step delta tau = 10^-3. The algorithm is validated against first-order and second-order hydrodynamics, analytic free-streaming solutions, and the semi-analytic solution of Florkowski (2013).

## Key numbers

- The reference parameters are set to c * tau_0 = 0.25 fm and k_B * T_0 = 600 MeV.
- The relaxation time parameter tau_A-W;0 is given by tau_A-W;0 = (5 hbar * eta/s) / (tau_0 * k_B * T_0) approximately 0.523426 * (0.25 fm / c * tau_0) * (600 MeV / k_B * T_0) * 4 pi * eta/s.
- The initial anisotropy parameter xi_0^H1 corresponding to the first-order hydrodynamics initial condition is approximately 0.11 for 4 pi eta/s = 0.1, 0.76 for 4 pi eta/s = 0.5, 2.67 for 4 pi eta/s = 1, and 48.4 for 4 pi eta/s = 1.7.
- The time step used in simulations is delta tau = 10^-3.
- The truncation order N_Omega = 6 is used for the Legendre expansion of the Romatschke-Strickland distribution.
- For the free-streaming case, Q_xi = 40 is used, and small discrepancies at xi_0 = 10 become unnoticeable when N_Omega = 8.

## Context

This work extends the lattice Boltzmann algorithm of Ambrus (2018) from isotropic to anisotropic initial conditions for the Bjorken flow, using the Romatschke-Strickland form from Romatschke (2003). The results are validated against the semi-analytic solution of Florkowski (2013) and compared with first-order and second-order hydrodynamics. The authors find that first-order hydrodynamics remains in good agreement with numerical results even at 4 pi eta/s = 1.7, where second-order formulations become inaccurate, and they plan to compare with the hydrodynamic attractor solution proposed by Romatschke (2017) in future work.
