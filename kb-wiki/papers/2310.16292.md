# Dyson-Schwinger equations towards cold-dense QCD matter with improved truncations

> We take the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach of QCD to study the phase transition and the equation of state of cold dense matter. Besides the bare vertex and Gauss gluon model, we take into account an improved truncation scheme, the CLRQ vertex and infrared-constant gluon model. For the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking solution of the DSE, we require that the emergence of quark number density to be at the chemical potential for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition to take place, by incorporating a chemical potential dependent modification factor to the gluon model. The result shows that our modified scheme can not only describe the phase transition of the cold dense matter well but also the deduced equation of state of the matter can describe the recent astronomical observations consistently.

## Key claim

The authors show that standard Dyson-Schwinger equation truncations (bare vertex with Gauss gluon, bare vertex with infrared-constant gluon, and CLRQ vertex with infrared-constant gluon) fail to reproduce the correct nuclear liquid-gas phase transition chemical potential and show no convergence; only after introducing a chemical-potential-dependent modification factor to the gluon coupling strength can the scheme simultaneously describe the phase transition and yield an equation of state consistent with neutron star observations.

## Method

The quark gap equation was solved at zero temperature and finite chemical potential using three truncation schemes: rainbow-ladder (RL) vertex with Gauss gluon model (DSE1), RL vertex with infrared-constant (IC) gluon model (DSE2), and CLRQ vertex with IC gluon model (DSE3). A chemical-potential-dependent damping factor \(h(\mu_q) = (1 + \mu_q^2 / \mu_{q,c}^2) \exp(-\beta \mu_q^2 / \omega^2)\) was applied to the coupling strength \(D\) to force the quark number density to emerge at the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition chemical potential \(\mu_{q,c} = 307.7\ \text{MeV}\). The quark number density, pressure, and equation of state were computed from the DSE solutions, and the hadron-quark phase transition was constructed using the relativistic mean field model for hadronic matter.

## Key numbers

* Critical quark chemical potential for emergence of quark number density: 0.566 GeV (DSE1), 0.630 GeV (DSE2), 0.512 GeV (DSE3) without modification; all three are forced to 0.3077 GeV after modification.
* Ratio of critical coupling to vacuum coupling at \(\mu_{q,c}\): \(D_c/D_0 = 0.716\) (DSE1), 0.460 (DSE2), 0.826 (DSE3).
* Damping factor parameters: \(\beta_1 = 2.714\) (DSE1), \(\beta_2 = 3.861\) (DSE2), \(\beta_3 = 2.337\) (DSE3).
* Left boundary of coexistence region for Wigner solution: \(\mu_q = 276\ \text{MeV}\) (DSE1), 227 MeV (DSE2), 270 MeV (DSE3).
* Pressure difference between Nambu and Wigner solutions at the left boundary: \(\Delta P = 4.19\ \text{GeV}^4\) (DSE1), 5.36 GeV\(^4\) (DSE2), 4.32 GeV\(^4\) (DSE3).

## Context

This work extends the DSE approach to cold dense QCD matter previously developed by Chen et al. (2011, 2012, 2015, 2016) and Bai et al. (2017, 2021), which used a damping factor with a free parameter to simulate asymptotic freedom at large chemical potential. The present paper improves upon those studies by implementing the CLRQ vertex and infrared-constant gluon model, which had been shown by Binosi et al. (2014) to bridge bottom-up and ab initio continuum QCD, and by Gao et al. (2016) for finite-temperature phase transitions. The results enable the construction of neutron star mass-radius relations that can be compared with observations from NICER and gravitational wave detections.
