# Observation of Partial U_A(1) Restoration from Two-Pion Bose-Einstein Correlations

> The effective intercept parameter of the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation function, lambda_*, is found to be sensitive to the partial restoration of U_A(1) symmetry in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. An increase in the yield of the eta' meson, proposed earlier as a signal of partial U_A(1) restoration, is shown to create a ``hole'' in the low p_t region of lambda_*. A comparison with NA44 data from central S+Pb collisions at 200 AGeV is made and implications for future heavy ion experiments are discussed.

## Key claim

The effective intercept parameter $\lambda_*$ of the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation function is sensitive to partial $U_A(1)$ restoration, because an increased yield of $\eta'$ mesons (proposed earlier as a signal of that restoration) creates a "hole" in the low $p_t$ region of $\lambda_*$; however, a qualitative analysis of NA44 S+Pb data suggests no visible sign of $U_A(1)$ restoration at SPS energies.

## Method

The authors used the core-halo model to separate pion emission into a resolvable core (direct pions and short-lived resonance decays) and an unresolvable halo (long-lived resonances including $\eta$, $\eta'$, $\omega$, $K^0_S$). They simulated the $\pi^+$ invariant $m_t$ distributions from core and halo using Jetset 7.4 for decays and $m_t$ distributions of the form $N(m_t) = C m_t^{\alpha} e^{-m_t/T_{eff}}$ with $T_{eff} = T_{fo} + m \langle u_t \rangle^2$, then computed $\lambda_*(m_t) = [N_{core}^{\pi^+}(m_t)/N_{total}^{\pi^+}(m_t)]^2$ for central S+Pb collisions at 200 AGeV. To model partial $U_A(1)$ restoration, they introduced an additional fraction of $\eta'$ mesons with a medium-modified mass $m_{\eta'}^*$ and a modified $p_t$ spectrum assuming transverse mass conservation at the phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- The average $p_t$ of pions from $\eta'$ decay (with $m_{\eta'} = 958$ MeV, $m_{\eta} = 547$ MeV, $m_{\pi^+} = 140$ MeV) is found to be $p_t \simeq 138$ MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature used is $T_{fo} = 140$ MeV.
- The effective mass $m_{\eta'}^* = 738$ MeV corresponds to an enhancement of $\eta'$ production by a factor of 3; $m_{\eta'}^* = 403$ MeV corresponds to a factor of 16; $m_{\eta'}^* = 176$ MeV corresponds to a factor of 50.
- The effective temperature of the hot and dense region used for the modified $\eta'$ spectrum is $T' = 200$ MeV.
- A mean transverse flow of $\langle u_t \rangle \approx 0.50$ is deduced from the NA44 S+Pb data.
- The higher-order Bose-Einstein symmetrization effect on $\lambda_*$ is estimated to be about a 1% decrease.

## Context

This work extends the core-halo model of Csorgo (1996) and Nickerson (1997) by connecting the effective intercept parameter $\lambda_*$ to the partial restoration of $U_A(1)$ symmetry, following the proposal by Kapusta (1996) and Wang (1996) that increased $\eta'$ production signals this restoration. The paper enables future experimental searches for $U_A(1)$ restoration at the CERN SPS (Pb+Pb), RHIC, and LHC using two-pion correlations, and provides a method to measure collective flow from the $m_t$ dependence of $\lambda_*$.
