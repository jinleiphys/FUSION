# Spectrum and electromagnetic transitions of bottomonium

> Stimulated by the exciting progress in the observation of new bottomonium states, we study the bottomonium spectrum. To calculate the mass spectrum, we adopt a nonrelativistic screened potential model. The radial Schrödinger equation is solved with the three-point difference central method, where the spin-dependent potentials are dealt with non-perturbatively. With this treatment, the corrections of the spin-dependent potentials to the wave functions can be included successfully. Furthermore, we calculate the electromagnetic transitions of the $nS$ ($n\leq 4$), $nP$ ($n\leq 3$), and $nD$ ($n\leq 2$) bottomonium states with a nonrelativistic electromagnetic transition operator widely applied to meson photoproduction reactions. Our predicted masses, hyperfine and fine splittings, electromagnetic transition widths and branching ratios of the bottomonium states are in good agreement with the available experimental data. Especially, the EM transitions of $Υ(3S)\to χ_{b1,2}(1P)γ$, which were not well understood in previous studies, can be reasonably explained by considering the corrections of the spin-dependent interactions to the wave functions. We also discuss the observations of the missing bottomonium states by using radiative transitions. Some important radiative decay chains involving the missing bottomonium states are suggested to be observed. We hope our study can provide some useful references to observe and measure the properties of bottomonium mesons in forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

Using a nonrelativistic screened potential model with nonperturbative treatment of spin-dependent potentials, the authors achieve good agreement with experimental bottomonium masses and electromagnetic transition widths, particularly explaining the previously poorly understood $\Upsilon(3S)\to \chi_{b1,2}(1P)\gamma$ transitions by including corrections of spin-dependent interactions to the wave functions.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation with a three-point difference central method, using a screened potential $V_s(r)=b(1-e^{-\mu r})/\mu$ instead of the linear potential $br$, and treat spin-dependent potentials (spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) nonperturbatively. They then calculate electromagnetic transition widths using a nonrelativistic electromagnetic transition operator that includes binding potential effects and higher multipole contributions, applied to $nS$ ($n\leq 4$), $nP$ ($n\leq 3$), and $nD$ ($n\leq 2$) bottomonium states.

## Key numbers

- Predicted $\Upsilon(1S)$ mass: 9460 MeV (experimental: 9460 MeV)
- Predicted $\eta_b(1S)$ mass: 9390 MeV (experimental: 9398 MeV)
- Hyperfine splitting $m(1^3S_1)-m(1^1S_0)$: 70 MeV (experimental: $62.3\pm 3.2$ MeV)
- Fine splitting $m(1^3P_2)-m(1^3P_1)$: 18 MeV (experimental: $19.43\pm 0.57$ MeV)
- $\Gamma[\Upsilon(1S)\rightarrow \eta_b(1S) \gamma] \simeq 10$ eV
- $\Gamma[\Upsilon(2S)\rightarrow \eta_b(2S)\gamma] \simeq 0.59$ eV
- $\Gamma[\Upsilon(3S)\rightarrow \eta_b(1S)\gamma] \simeq 71$ eV (experimental: $10\pm 2$ eV)
- $\Gamma[\eta_b(2S)\to h_b(1P)\gamma] \simeq 3.41$ keV
- Estimated total width $\chi_{b0}(1P)$: $1.56^{+0.59}_{-0.33}$ MeV
- Estimated total width $\chi_{b1}(1P)$: $94\pm 7$ keV
- Estimated total width $\chi_{b2}(1P)$: $166^{+12}_{-9}$ keV

## Context

This work extends the screened potential model approach of Chao (2009) and addresses discrepancies in electromagnetic transition predictions that existed between the relativized quark model of Godfrey (2015) and the nonrelativistic constituent quark model of Segovia (2016). The nonperturbative treatment of spin-dependent potentials distinguishes this work from previous perturbative approaches. The results provide predictions for missing bottomonium states and their radiative decay chains that can guide searches at LHC and Belle experiments.
