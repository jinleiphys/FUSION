# Temperature dependence of $η/s$: uncertainties from the equation of state

> We perform a global model-to-data comparison on Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $2.76$ TeV and $5.02$ TeV, using a 2+1D hydrodynamics model with the EKRT initial state and a shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $(η/s)(T)$ with a linear $T$ dependence. To quantify the amount of uncertainty due to the choice of the equation of state (EoS), we compare analysis results based on four different EoSs: the well known $s95p$ parametrisation, an updated parametrisation based on the same list of particles in hadron resonance gas, but using recent lattice results for the partonic part of the EoS, and two new parametrisations based on the Particle Data Group 2016 particle list and the recent lattice results. We find that the choice of the EoS does affect the favoured minimum value of $η/s$, although within the confidence limits of the analysis. On the other hand, our analysis hardly constrains the temperature dependence of $η/s$, no matter the EoS.

## Key claim

The choice of equation of state (EoS) does affect the favored minimum value of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (η/s), but the differences remain within the confidence limits of the analysis; the analysis hardly constrains the temperature dependence of η/s regardless of which EoS is used.

## Method

The authors performed a global Bayesian model-to-data comparison using a 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics model with the EKRT pQCD+saturation initial state and a linear parametrization of (η/s)(T). They compared four different equations of state: the s95p parametrization based on PDG 2005 hadron list and older lattice data; an updated s87r parametrization using the same hadron list with contemporary HISQ lattice data; and two new parametrizations (s88s16 and s83z16) based on the PDG 2016 hadron list with HISQ and stout lattice data respectively. The model parameters were constrained using charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow data from Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The proportionality constant Ksat peaks at 0.5 for all equations of state, in agreement with the value used in Niemi (2015).
- The median value of (η/s)min depends on the EoS, with the largest difference observed between s95p and s83z16 parametrizations.
- The 90% credible intervals for (η/s)min overlap even for the most different EoS parametrizations (s95p and s83z16).
- The analysis used 120 training points for each EoS, with 20 points removed for cross-validation and 100 used for conditioning the Gaussian process emulators.
- The freeze-out temperature was set to Tdec = 150 MeV for all calculations.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach of Bernhard (2016) and Bass (2017) and the earlier EKRT model study of Niemi (2015) by systematically comparing four different equations of state to quantify the uncertainty they introduce in extracted (η/s) values. The results show that earlier studies using the s95p parametrization (Huovinen, 2009) remain valid within overall uncertainties, but the new parametrizations based on PDG 2016 particle lists and contemporary lattice data systematically prefer larger (η/s)min values. The analysis enables future work to incorporate more observables and wider prior ranges to better constrain the temperature dependence of η/s.
