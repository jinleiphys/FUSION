# Kaon Condensates, Nuclear Symmetry Energy and Cooling of Neutron Stars

> The cooling of neutron stars by URCA processes in the kaon-condensed neutron star matter for various forms of nuclear symmetry energy is investigated. The kaon-nucleon interactions are described by a chiral lagrangian. Nuclear matter energy is parametrized in terms of the isoscalar contribution and the nuclear symmetry energy in the isovector sector. High density behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy plays an essential role in determining the composition of the kaon-condensed neutron star matter which in turn affects the cooling properties. We find that the symmetry energy which decreases at higher densities makes the kaon-condensed neutron star matter fully protonized. This effect inhibits strongly direct URCA processes resulting in slower cooling of neutron stars as only kaon-induced URCA cycles are present. In contrast, for increasing symmetry energy direct URCA processes are allowed in the almost whole density range where the kaon condensation exists.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy critically determines whether direct URCA cooling processes operate in kaon-condensed neutron star matter; contrary to the common expectation that high proton fractions enable fast cooling, a decreasing symmetry energy can suppress direct URCA cycles even when the proton fraction exceeds the usual threshold, leaving only slower kaon-induced URCA processes.

## Method

The authors model kaon-nucleon interactions using a chiral Lagrangian with parameters fitted to kaon-nucleon scattering and kaonic atom data, while the nucleon-nucleon part is taken from realistic potential models (UV14+UVII, AV14+UVII, UV14+TNI) and from relativistic mean field theory. They impose beta equilibrium and local charge neutrality to determine the composition of neutron star matter as a function of baryon density, then evaluate the triangle conditions for direct URCA and kaon-induced URCA processes and compute the total neutrino emissivity.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for kaon condensation with the RMF symmetry energy (E1) and strangeness parameter a3ms = -310 MeV: 2.4 n0 (where n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is saturation density)
- Critical density for kaon condensation with UV14+TNI interactions and a3ms = -310 MeV: 2.8 n0
- Critical density for kaon condensation with UV14+TNI interactions and a3ms = -134 MeV: no condensation occurs (entry marked "--")
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: Es(n0) = a4 = 30 MeV
- Threshold proton fraction for direct URCA in normal n,p,e,mu matter: between 0.11 and 0.15
- Kaon-induced URCA emissivity is about 10 times smaller than direct URCA emissivity when both are allowed

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Thorsson et al., 1994) by systematically comparing the effects of different nuclear symmetry energy behaviors from realistic potential models (Wiringa et al., 1988) and RMF theory. The authors show that the common assumption that high proton fractions guarantee fast cooling via direct URCA (Lattimer et al., 1991) does not hold in kaon-condensed matter, and that the uncertainty in high-density symmetry energy (Kutschera, 2000; Li, 2002) produces qualitatively different cooling scenarios. The results enable future work incorporating neutral kaons and hyperons, which the authors note will be addressed in a forthcoming paper.
