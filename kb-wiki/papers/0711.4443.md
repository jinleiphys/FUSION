# Nuclear effects in F_3 structure function of nucleon

> We study nuclear effects in the $F^A_3(x)$ structure function in the deep inelastic neutrino reactions on iron by using a relativistic framework to describe the nucleon spectral functions in the nucleus. The results for the ratio $R(x,Q^2)=\frac{F^A_3(x,Q^2)}{AF^N_3(x, Q^2)}$ and the Gross-Llewellyn Smith(GLS) integral $G(x,Q^2)=\int_x^1 dx F^A_3(x,Q^2)$ in nuclei are discussed and compared with the recent results available in literature from theoretical and phenomenological analyses of experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects on the \(F_3^A(x)\) structure function in iron are dominated by Fermi motion, binding energy, and off-mass-shell modifications of nucleons in the region \(0.1 < x < 1\), but they explicitly exclude the shadowing region (\(x < 0.1\)) and note that mesonic contributions (which produce anti-shadowing in \(F_2\)) vanish for \(F_3\), so their results apply only to the valence-quark-dominated regime.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic spectral function for nucleons in nuclear matter, calculated from the nucleon self-energy in an interacting Fermi sea via Lehmann's representation and nuclear many-body theory. They apply a local density approximation to translate the nuclear-matter results to finite \({}^{56}\)Fe nuclei, using a two-parameter Fermi density distribution. The nuclear structure function \(F_3^A(x, Q^2)\) is obtained by convoluting the free-nucleon structure function \(F_3^N(x_N, Q^2)\) (taken from MRST2004 NNLO parton distributions) with the hole spectral function, including target mass corrections and QCD evolution of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith integral.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{56}\)Fe, the average kinetic energy per nucleon is \(\langle T \rangle = 30\) MeV and the average total nucleon energy is \(\langle E \rangle = 48\) MeV, obtained after fixing the free parameter in the nucleon self-energy.
* The ratio \(R(x, Q^2) = F_3^A(x, Q^2) / [A F_3^N(x, Q^2)]\) shows suppression for \(x < 0.7\) and enhancement for \(x > 0.7\) at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\); the crossover point \(x_{\text{min}}\) decreases with increasing \(Q^2\).
* The strong coupling constant is calculated with \(\Lambda = 251\) MeV for \(n_f = 4\) and \(\Lambda = 178\) MeV for \(n_f = 5\), giving \(\alpha_S(M_Z) = 0.1153\).
* The nuclear density parameters for \({}^{56}\)Fe are \(c1 = 4.106\) fm and \(c2 = 0.519\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic spectral-function approach previously applied to charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering on nuclei by Marco et al. (2003) to the neutrino case, specifically the \(F_3\) structure function. It contrasts with the nuclear-model approach of Kulagin (2004) and the phenomenological analyses of Hirai et al. (2001) and the NuTeV collaboration (Tzanov et al., 2006), finding that nuclear effects in \(F_3\) differ from those in \(F_2\) (especially the absence of anti-shadowing enhancement at \(0.1 < x < 0.3\)). The results enable future studies of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule on nuclear targets and call for a comprehensive treatment of nuclear medium effects across all structure functions at small \(x\).
