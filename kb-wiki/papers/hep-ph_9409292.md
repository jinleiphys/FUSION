# Off-Shell Electromagnetic Form Fators of the Nucleon in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study the electromagnetic form factors of a nucleon in next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory (CPT) in the case where one of the nucleons is off its mass shell. We calculate the leading nonanalytic contributions to relevant measures for the off-shell dependence in the limited kinematical range allowed.

## Key claim

The off-shell dependence of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, specifically the change of the mean square charge radius with the off-shell mass parameter \(W\), is dominated by nonanalytic contributions from one-loop chiral perturbation theory; the isovector part diverges in the chiral limit and shows a reduction of the mean square radius for an off-shell nucleon, while the isoscalar part has a weaker dependence starting at order \(\mathcal{O}(1)\).

## Method

The authors calculate the half off-shell \(\gamma\)NN vertex in next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory (CPT), up to \(\mathcal{O}(E^3)\), using tree-level diagrams from the CPT Lagrangian up to \(\mathcal{O}(E^3)\) and one-loop diagrams up to \(\mathcal{O}(E)\). They focus on the leading nonanalytic (singular) contributions in the chiral limit (\(M_\pi \to 0\)) to the mixed derivative \(\partial^2 f_1^{++} / (\partial W \partial q^2)\) at the on-shell point \(W=M\) and \(q^2=0\), which quantifies the change of the nucleon's mean square radius with off-shell mass.

## Key numbers

- The isovector form factor derivative at \(W=M\) and \(q^2=0\) is given by \(6 \, \partial^2 f_1^{++, \text{V}} / (\partial W \partial q^2) = \frac{5}{32 \pi} \frac{g_A^2}{F^2 M} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} + \mathcal{O}(\ln x)\), where \(x = (M_\pi / M)^2\).
- Using the physical value \(x = 0.022\) and retaining only the leading term, the free nucleon mean square radius of \(0.6 \; \text{fm}^2\) (at \(W = 940 \; \text{MeV}\)) is reduced by about \(0.1 \; \text{fm}^2\) when extrapolated linearly to \(W = 890 \; \text{MeV}\).
- The isoscalar form factor derivative at the same point is of order \(\mathcal{O}(1)\), indicating no singular off-shell dependence.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic model calculations of off-shell nucleon form factors by Naus and Tiemeijer (1990) and others, using the systematic framework of chiral perturbation theory to isolate the leading nonanalytic contributions. The results provide a qualitative prediction for the off-shell dependence of the nucleon's electromagnetic radius, which is relevant for interpreting two-step processes such as Compton scattering on a free nucleon. The calculation also highlights that off-shell properties are not fixed but depend on kinematical parameters, and it sets the stage for including analytic contributions and higher-order terms in future work.
