# NLO inclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction at large $P_T$ at HERA and the EIC

> We study inclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction at NLO at large $P_T$ at HERA and the EIC. Our computation includes NLO QCD leading-$P_T$ corrections, QED contributions via an off-shell photon as well as those from $J/ψ$+charm channels. For the latter, we employ the variable-flavour-number scheme. Our results are found to agree with the latest HERA data by H1 and provide, for the first time, a reliable estimate of the EIC reach for such a measurement. Finally, we demonstrate the observability of $J/ψ$+charm production and the sensitivy to probe the non-perturbative charm content of the proton at high $x$, also known as intrinsic charm, at the EIC.

## Key claim
The Colour-Singlet Model at NLO (order ααₛ³) can describe the latest HERA inclusive J/ψ photoproduction data, provided that previously overlooked QED contributions and J/ψ+charm channels are included; the agreement is enhanced when subtracting expected b→J/ψ feed-down from the data.

## Method
The authors computed NLO QCD leading-PT corrections using the NLO* approximation, which imposes a democratic lower cut on the invariant mass of every pair of massless partons, and validated it against a full NLO calculation by Butenschoen and Kniehl (2011). They included LO QED contributions via an off-shell photon and J/ψ+charm production using a LO Variable Flavour Number Scheme (VFNS) that merges 3-flavour and 4-flavour scheme calculations. Predictions were generated with HELAC-Onia using CT14NLO proton PDFs, with scale uncertainties from independent variation of μF and μR in [1/2:2] μ0 (μ0 = mT) and charm mass uncertainty from mc = 1.5 ± 0.1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- At HERA (√sₑₚ = 319 GeV, Q² < 2.5 GeV², 60 GeV < Wγp < 240 GeV), the NLO* QCD + LO QED + LO VFNS sum agrees with H1 data for PT > 1 GeV.
- At the EIC with √sₑₚ = 45 GeV (Ee = 5 GeV, Ep = 100 GeV), the yield is measurable up to PT ≈ 11 GeV at ℒ = 100 fb⁻¹.
- At the EIC with √sₑₚ = 140 GeV (Ee = 18 GeV, Ep = 275 GeV), the yield is measurable up to PT ≈ 18 GeV.
- For J/ψ+charm production at √sₑₚ = 45 GeV, expected event counts are O(500, 50, 5) for ℒ = (100, 10, 1) fb⁻¹ with 10% charm detection efficiency.
- For J/ψ+charm production at √sₑₚ = 140 GeV, expected event counts are O(10³) at ℒ = 100 fb⁻¹.
- The NLO* approximation with √sᵢⱼᵐⁱⁿ = 2 mc (mc = 1.5 GeV) reproduces the complete NLO calculation by Butenschoen and Kniehl (2011).

## Context
This work extends the Colour-Singlet Model analysis of inclusive J/ψ photoproduction at large PT, which was seldom studied at HERA (H1 and ZEUS collaborations, 1996-2012), by adding QED contributions and J/ψ+charm channels previously overlooked. It provides the first reliable estimate of the EIC reach for such measurements, demonstrating that the EIC can probe the non-perturbative charm content of the proton (intrinsic charm, Brodsky 1980) via J/ψ+charm production, with a valence-like BHPS peak visible at √sₑₚ = 45 GeV. The analysis can be extended to future colliders such as the LHeC and FCC-eh.
