# $Λ$ hyperonic effect on the magnesium dripline

> Neutron dripline calculations for both magnesium nuclei and magnesium + $Λ$ hypernuclei have been carried out in a microscopic framework using a chiral effective model. The results are compared with two other relativistic mean field models, SPL-40 and NL3. All three models describe the $Λ$ separation energy of known hypernuclei adequately. The extrapolation to the driplines for moderately heavy hypernuclei are found to be strongly model-dependent.

# Λ hyperonic effect on the magnesium dripline

## Key claim
The addition of a single Λ hyperon can shift the neutron dripline of magnesium isotopes by up to eight neutrons, but the extrapolated position of the hypernuclear dripline is strongly model-dependent across the three relativistic mean-field models tested (TS2, SPL-40, and NL3), even though all three describe known Λ separation energies adequately.

## Method
The authors performed deformed relativistic mean-field calculations for even-even magnesium isotopes and magnesium-plus-Λ hypernuclei using a newly parameterized chiral SU(3) effective model (TS2). They compared the results with two other relativistic mean-field models, SPL-40 and NL3, by computing two-neutron separation energies and identifying the dripline as the point where the binding energy of the next isotope becomes higher than that of the last bound nucleus.

## Key numbers
- TS2 model predicts the magnesium dripline at ⁴²Mg; adding a Λ hyperon shifts the dripline to ⁵⁰ΛMg (eight additional neutrons).
- SPL-40 model predicts the magnesium dripline at ⁵²Mg and the hypernuclear dripline at ⁴⁶ΛMg (six fewer neutrons with the Λ).
- NL3 model predicts the magnesium dripline at ⁴⁶Mg and the hypernuclear dripline also at ⁴⁶ΛMg (no shift in neutron number).
- The TS2 model reproduces known Λ separation energies within 0.5 to 1 MeV of experimental values, e.g., ⁴ΛH: experiment 2.04 MeV, TS2 2.179 MeV; ²⁰⁸ΛPb: experiment 26.5 MeV, TS2 25.432 MeV.
- The TS2 model yields a neutron star maximum mass of 2.05 M⊙ at a radius of 12 km, with a compressibility κ = 275.13 MeV and hyperon potentials at saturation density of Λ = -29.2 MeV, Σ⁺ = 5.6 MeV, Ξ⁰ = -19.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous deformed nuclear structure calculations using the Frankfurt chiral model (Papazoglou 1998, Schramm 2004) and compares with the established relativistic mean-field parameter sets SPL-40 (Sharma 1993) and NL3 (Lalazissis 1997). The results highlight that while all three models describe known low-mass hypernuclear separation energies adequately, extrapolations to the neutron dripline for hypernuclei are highly model-dependent, with SPL-40 even predicting a destabilizing effect of the Λ. The study motivates experimental searches for neutron-rich hypernuclei at facilities such as JPARC, JLab, GSI (HypHI), and NICA, and shows that hypernuclear binding can substantially modify the limits of nuclear stability.
