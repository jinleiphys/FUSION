# Constraints on the phase transition and nuclear symmetry parameters from PSR $\mathrm{J}0740+6620$ and multimessenger data of other neutron stars

> Recently, the radius of neutron star (NS) PSR J0740+6620 was measured by Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) and an updated measurement of neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb ($R_{\rm skin}^{208}$) was reported by the PREX-II experiment. These new measurements can help us better understand the unknown equation of state (EOS) of dense matter. In this work, we adopt a hybrid parameterization method, which incorporates the nuclear empirical parameterization and some widely used phenomenological parameterizations, to analyze the results of nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations. With the joint Bayesian analysis of GW170817, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0740+6620, the parameters that characterize the ultradense matter EOS are constrained. We find that the slope parameter $L$ is approximately constrained to $70_{-18}^{+21}$ MeV, which predicts $R_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.204^{+0.030}_{-0.026}\,{\rm fm}$ by using the universal relation between $R_{\rm skin}^{208}$ and $L$. The bulk properties of canonical $1.4\,M_\odot$ NS (e.g., $R_{1.4}$ and $Λ_{1.4}$) as well as the pressure ($P_{2ρ_{\rm sat}}$) at two times the nuclear saturation density are well constrained by the data; i.e., $R_{1.4}$, $Λ_{1.4}$, and $P_{2ρ_{\rm sat}}$ are approximately constrained to $12.3\pm0.7$ km, $330_{-100}^{+140}$, and $4.1_{-1.2}^{+1.5}\times10^{34}\,{\rm dyn\,cm^{-2}}$, respectively. Besides, we find that the Bayes evidences of the hybrid star and normal NS assumptions are comparable, which indicates that current observation data are compatible with quarkyonic matter existing in the core of massive star. Finally, in the case of normal NS assumption, we obtain a constraint for the maximum mass of nonrotating NS $M_{\rm TOV}=2.30^{+0.30}_{-0.18}$ $M_\odot$. All of the uncertainties reported above are for 68.3% credible levels.

## Key claim

The paper finds that current multimessenger data (GW170817, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620) are compatible with both normal neutron star models and hybrid stars containing quarkyonic matter in the core, with comparable Bayes evidences; the slope parameter L is constrained to approximately 70 MeV, which predicts a neutron skin thickness for 208Pb that is smaller than the PREX-II measurement but slightly mitigates the tension between that measurement and other nuclear experiments.

## Method

The authors adopt a hybrid parameterization method that combines a parabolic expansion-based nuclear empirical parameterization (valid up to about 1.85 times nuclear saturation density) with three widely used phenomenological parameterizations: piecewise polytrope, causal spectral representation, and constant-speed-of-sound parametrization. They perform a joint Bayesian analysis using the Bilby and dynesty packages, incorporating data from the gravitational wave event GW170817, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and construct separate models with and without a phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter L constrained to 69 (+20, -17) MeV for the phase transition model and 71 (+21, -18) MeV for the no-phase-transition model (68.3% credible level)
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb predicted as R_skin^208 = 0.204 (+0.030, -0.026) fm using the universal relation between R_skin^208 and L (68.3% credible level)
- Radius of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star R_1.4 = 12.29 (+0.66, -0.64) km for phase transition model and 12.30 (+0.69, -0.67) km for no-phase-transition model (68.3% credible level)
- Tidal deformability of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star Lambda_1.4 = 334 (+138, -94) for both models (68.3% credible level)
- Pressure at two times nuclear saturation density P_2rho_sat = 4.1 (+1.5, -1.2) x 10^34 dyn cm^-2 (68.3% credible level)
- Maximum mass of nonrotating neutron star M_TOV = 2.30 (+0.30, -0.18) solar masses for the no-phase-transition model (68.3% credible level)
- Bayes factor between phase transition and no-phase-transition models of B^PT_NPT = 0.9

## Context

This work extends the hybrid parameterization method previously developed by Tang et al. (2021) by incorporating nuclear empirical parameterization around saturation density, similar to models used by Steiner et al. (2010) and Biswas et al. (2021). The results are consistent with those of Biswas et al. (2021), Essick et al. (2021), and Li et al. (2021) regarding the neutron skin thickness prediction. The constrained maximum mass favors the results of Fan et al. (2020) and Shao et al. (2020), and the authors conclude that a binary black hole merger scenario for GW190814 is more plausible given current observational and theoretical knowledge of the neutron star population and its equation of state.
