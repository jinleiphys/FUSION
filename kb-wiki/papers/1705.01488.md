# Quasi-PDFs, momentum distributions and pseudo-PDFs

> We show that quasi-PDFs may be treated as hybrids of PDFs and primordial rest-frame momentum distributions of partons. This results in a complicated convolution nature of quasi-PDFs that necessitates using large $p_3 \sim 3$ GeV momenta to get reasonably close to the PDF limit. As an alternative approach, we propose to use pseudo-PDFs $P(x, z_3^2)$ that generalize the light-front PDFs onto spacelike intervals and are related to Ioffe-time distributions $M (ν, z_3^2)$, the functions of the Ioffe time $ν= p_3 z_3$ and the distance parameter $z_3^2$ with respect to which it displays perturbative evolution for small $z_3$. In this form, one may divide out the $z_3^2$ dependence coming from the primordial rest-frame distribution and from the problematic factor due to lattice renormalization of the gauge link. The $ν$-dependence remains intact and determines the shape of PDFs.

## Key claim

Quasi-PDFs are hybrids of PDFs and primordial rest-frame momentum distributions of partons, resulting in a complicated convolution structure that necessitates using large momenta \(p_3 \gtrsim 3\) GeV to approach the PDF limit; as an alternative, pseudo-PDFs allow one to divide out the \(z_3^2\) dependence from the primordial distribution and from lattice renormalization of the gauge link, leaving the \(\nu\)-dependence intact to determine the PDF shape.

## Method

The author derives the relation between quasi-PDFs and transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) from Lorentz invariance, showing that quasi-PDFs are given by a convolution integral over the TMD's \(x\) and \(\kappa^2\) arguments. He then introduces pseudo-PDFs \(\mathcal{P}(x, z_3^2)\) as Fourier transforms of Ioffe-time distributions \(\mathcal{M}(\nu, z_3^2)\) with respect to the Ioffe time \(\nu = p_3 z_3\), and proposes forming the reduced distribution \(\mathfrak{M}(\nu, z_3^2) = \mathcal{M}(\nu, z_3^2) / \mathcal{M}(0, z_3^2)\) to cancel the \(z_3^2\)-dependent factor from the gauge-link renormalization and the primordial rest-frame distribution.

## Key numbers

- Quasi-PDFs require \(p_3 \gtrsim 3\) GeV to get reasonably close to the PDF limit (based on \(\Lambda \sim \langle k_\perp \rangle \sim 300\) MeV and \(P \sim 10\Lambda\)).
- In the factorized Gaussian model, the rest-frame distribution is \(r_G(k_3) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi} \Lambda} e^{-k_3^2/\Lambda^2}\).
- The maximal Ioffe-time values reached in existing lattice calculations range from \(3\pi\) (Lin 2014) to \(5\pi\) (Alexandrou 2015) and \(6\pi\) (Orginos 2017).
- The residual \(z_3\)-dependence for small \(z_3 \leq 4a\) may be explained by perturbative evolution with \(\alpha_s/\pi = 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF method proposed by Ji (2013) by revealing its hybrid convolution nature and the need for large momenta, and it introduces pseudo-PDFs as an alternative approach for lattice QCD extraction of PDFs. The pseudo-PDF formalism builds on the Ioffe-time distributions of Braun (1994) and the author's earlier work on virtuality distribution functions (Radyushkin 2014, 2015). The proposed ratio method addresses renormalization difficulties noted by Ishikawa (2016) and Chen (2016), and was subsequently tested in a lattice calculation by Orginos (2017) that confirmed the cancellation of the gauge-link factor and mild residual \(z_3^2\)-dependence.
