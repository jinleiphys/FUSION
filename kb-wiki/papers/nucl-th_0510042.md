# A High Energy Nuclear Database Proposal

> We propose to develop a high-energy heavy-ion experimental database and make it accessible to the scientific community through an on-line interace. This database will be searchable and cross-indexed with relevant publications, including published detector descriptions. Since this database will be a community resource, it requires the high-energy nuclear physics community's financial and manpower support. This database should eventually contain all published data from the Bevalac, AGS and SPS to RHIC and LHC energies, proton-proton to nucleus-nucleus collisions as well as other relevant systems and all measured observables. Such a database would have tremendous scientific payoff as it makes systematic studies easier and allows simpler benchmarking of theoretical models to a broad range of old and new experiments. Furthermore, there is a growing need for compilations of high-energy nuclear data for applications including stockpile stewardship, technology development for intertial confinement fusion and target and source development for upcoming facilities such as the Next Linear Collider. To enhance the utility of this database, we propose periodically performing evaluations of the data and summarizing the results in topical reviews.

# A High Energy Nuclear Database Proposal

## Key claim
The authors propose creating a community-driven, web-accessible database for high-energy heavy-ion experimental data, but note that due to budget cuts in FY05-06, it is unlikely to be funded before FY07, making the proposal contingent on future financial and manpower support from the high-energy nuclear physics community.

## Method
The authors outline a plan to build a searchable MySQL relational database accessed through JSP or PHP dynamic web pages, storing data in XML format for long-term readability. Data would be collected by having experimental collaborations submit both published and auxiliary supporting data, piggybacking on journal peer-review processes. The database would store cross sections, particle yields, single-particle spectra, multi-particle spectra, flow, and correlation observables, and would be cross-indexed with publications and detector descriptions.

## Key numbers
- The database is proposed to cover energies from Bevalac, AGS, and SPS up to RHIC and LHC energies, spanning proton-proton to nucleus-nucleus collisions.
- The project was submitted as a white paper to DOE-OS Heavy-Ion and Nuclear Theory programs and circulated in the STAR and PHENIX collaborations.
- Due to budget cuts in FY05-06, funding is not expected before FY07.

## Context
This proposal extends the concept of existing nuclear data compilations such as the Evaluated Nuclear Data File (ENDF/B) and EXchange FORmat (EXFOR) databases, as well as the HEPDATA website for high-energy particle physics, but addresses the gap in high-energy heavy-ion data archiving. The authors note that while experiments like PHENIX post data on collaboration websites, this leads to a proliferation of formats and risks data loss when experiments end. The database would enable systematic studies, theory benchmarking, and applications including stockpile stewardship, inertial confinement fusion, and secondary beam source development for facilities like the Next Linear Collider.
