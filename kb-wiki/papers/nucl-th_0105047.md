# Anomalous rotational-alignment in N=Z nuclei and residual neutron-proton interaction

> Recent experiments have demonstrated that the rotational-alignment for the $N=Z$ nuclei in the mass-80 region is considerably delayed as compared to the neighboring $N \ne Z$ nuclei. We investigate whether this observation can be understood by a known component of nuclear residual interactions. It is shown that the quadrupole-pairing interaction, which explains many of the delays known in rare-earth nuclei, does not produce the substantial delay observed for these $N=Z$ nuclei. However, the residual neutron-proton interaction which is conjectured to be relevant for $N=Z$ nuclei is shown to be quite important in explaining the new experimental data.

## Key claim

The observed delay in rotational-alignment for N=Z nuclei in the mass-80 region cannot be explained by the quadrupole-pairing interaction that accounts for similar delays in rare-earth nuclei, but can be reproduced by increasing the strength of the neutron-proton quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction in the projected shell model, although this modification offsets the good agreement previously obtained for neighboring N≠Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the projected shell model (PSM) with three major shells (N=2,3,4) for both neutrons and protons, including 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle states built from deformed Nilsson single-particle states. They systematically varied the strength of the neutron-proton quadrupole-quadrupole interaction term (χνπ) by multiplying factors α=0.8, 1.0, 1.1, 1.2, and 1.3, while keeping the like-particle quadrupole-quadrupole strengths self-consistently determined by deformation, and compared the calculated moments of inertia for 72Kr, 76Sr, and 80Zr with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 72Kr, increasing the n-p interaction strength by factor α=1.3 (from the standard value given by χνπ = (χνν χππ)^(1/2)) produces a delayed crossing frequency and smooth backbending that matches the experimental data, where the experimental moment of inertia shows a slight upbend around ħω = 0.8 MeV.
- Varying the quadrupole pairing strength γ from 0.16 to 0.28 for 72Kr produced a delay in crossing frequency that was too small to explain the data, with a sharp backbend remaining for all γ values.
- The monopole pairing strength was taken as G_M = [18.0 - 14.5(N-Z)/A]/A for neutrons and G_M = 14.5/A for protons, with quadrupole pairing strength G_Q = γ G_M where γ = 0.16 as the standard value.

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model calculations of Sun et al. (2001) for Kr, Sr, and Zr isotopes, which had successfully described 74Kr and 76Kr but failed to reproduce the delayed alignment in the N=Z nucleus 72Kr observed by Fischer et al. (2001) and Kelsall et al. (2001). The study contradicts earlier suggestions that quadrupole pairing, which explained delays in rare-earth nuclei (Sun et al. 1994, Sun and Frauendorf 1996), could account for the N=Z delay, and instead supports schematic single-j shell model predictions (Frauendorf 1999, Sheikh et al. 1999, Wyss et al. 2000) that neutron-proton residual interactions are responsible. The results enable future work to explicitly include a neutron-proton pairing term in the Hamiltonian to obtain a quantitative renormalization of the interaction for N=Z nuclei.
