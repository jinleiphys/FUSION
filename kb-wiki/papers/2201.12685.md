# The Interplay of Femtoscopic and Charge-Balance Correlations

> Correlations driven by the constraints of local charge conservation have been shown to provide insight into the chemical evolution and diffusivity of the high-temperature matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Two-particle correlations driven by final-state interactions have allowed the extraction of critical femtoscopic space-time information about the expansion and dissolution of the same collisions. Whereas correlations from final-state interactions mainly appear at small relative momenta, a few tens of MeV/$c$, charge-balance correlations extend over a range of hundreds of MeV/$c$. In nearly all previous analyses, this separation of scales is used to focus solely on one class or the other. The purpose of this study is to quantitatively understand the degree to which correlations from final-state interactions distort the interpretation of charge-balance correlations and vice versa.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively demonstrates that femtoscopic correlations from final-state interactions (FSI) and charge-balance correlations (BFs) can significantly distort one another, contrary to the common assumption in previous analyses that the separation of momentum scales (tens vs. hundreds of MeV/c) allows them to be treated independently.

## Method

The authors use a blast-wave model with parameters for chemical freeze-out temperature (150 MeV), kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow, and Gaussian spatial correlation widths to generate pairs of hadrons. They calculate charge-balance functions from a two-step Monte Carlo procedure: first from resonance decays, then from correlated pairs weighted by a charge-susceptibility kernel derived from a non-interacting hadron gas. Femtoscopic correlations are incorporated by weighting each pair with the squared two-particle wave function accounting for symmetrization, strong interactions, and Coulomb interactions, with screening of the Coulomb interaction by balancing charges included via a modified weighting scheme.

## Key numbers

- Femtoscopic correlations from symmetrization largely vanish for relative momentum |q| > 50 MeV/c
- The two-proton correlation function has a peak at q ≈ 22 MeV/c
- Coulomb contribution to the BF scales roughly as (dN/dy)^(2/3) with multiplicity, potentially becoming non-negligible even when the correlation function contribution is below 0.1%
- The blast-wave model uses a chemical freeze-out temperature Tc = 150 MeV, and the STAR acceptance is modeled with Y_max = 0.9
- The Gaussian spatial correlation widths are parameterized by σ_R (transverse) and σ_η (longitudinal), with the transverse size parameter R and breakup time τ_f affecting femtoscopic measurements

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of charge-balance correlations developed by Pratt and collaborators (Pratt 2015, Pratt 2012, Pratt 2019) and femtoscopic analyses dating back to Koonin (1977) and Lisa (2005). It directly addresses the assumption made in nearly all previous experimental analyses by STAR (Westfall 2004, STAR 2015, Wang 2011) and ALICE (ALICE 2021, Pan 2018) that the two classes of correlations can be studied independently due to their different momentum scales. The results enable future simultaneous fits of both charge-balance and femtoscopic data to extract diffusivity and chemical evolution parameters, and improve upon the crude screening estimate for a pion gas given in Pratt (2003).
