# Impact of nuclear structure from shell model calculations on nuclear responses to WIMP elastic scattering for $^{19}$F and $^{nat}$Xe targets

> Non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) is one approach used for describing the interaction of WIMPs with ordinary matter. Among other factors, these interactions are expected to be affected by the structure of the atomic nuclei in the target. The sensitivity of the nuclear response components of the WIMP-nucleus scattering amplitude is investigated using shell model calculations for $^{19}$F and $^{nat}$Xe. Resulting integrated nuclear response values are shown to be sensitive to some specifics of the nuclear structure calculations.

# Impact of nuclear structure from shell model calculations on nuclear responses to WIMP elastic scattering for $^{19}$F and $^{nat}$Xe targets

## Key claim
Integrated nuclear response values for WIMP-nucleus elastic scattering, computed using the NREFT formalism, show sensitivity to the choice of nuclear shell model interaction for both $^{19}$F and $^{nat}$Xe targets, with the subleading $\Phi''$ operator exhibiting differences of approximately 20% for $^{19}$F and 40% for Xe isotopes between different interactions. The abstract glosses over the condition that the largest fractional differences occur in subleading channels, while the dominant spin-independent $M$ operator remains nearly unchanged.

## Method
Nuclear shell model calculations were performed using NuShellX with alternative interactions compared to prior work: USDB for $^{19}$F (versus USD) and SN100PN for Xe isotopes (versus B&D). The nuclear form factors for six operator types ($M$, $\Sigma''$, $\Sigma'$, $\Delta$, $\Phi''$, $\tilde{\Phi}'$) were computed using a pre-developed Mathematica package, and integrated over momentum transfer $q$ from 0 to 100 MeV to obtain Integrated Form Factor (IFF) values for both proton and neutron channels.

## Key numbers
- For $^{19}$F: the largest factor difference in proton IFF values is 1.03 between USDB and USD interactions
- For $^{19}$F: the neutron $\Phi''$ channel shows approximately 20% difference between the two interactions
- For Xe isotopes: the neutron $\Phi''$ operator shows approximately 40% difference between SN100PN and B&D interactions
- IFF values are evaluated over the range $q = 0$ to 100 MeV using the integral $\int_{0}^{100\text{MeV}} \frac{q dq}{2} F^{(N, N)}_{X,X} (q^2)$, with results in units of $(\text{MeV})^2$

## Context
This work extends the NREFT formalism developed by Fitzpatrick et al. (2013) and Anand et al. (2014), who provided IFF values for a range of isotopes using specific shell model interactions (USD for $^{19}$F and B&D for Xe). By employing alternative interactions (USDB and SN100PN), the authors quantify theoretical uncertainties in nuclear response due to nuclear structure modelling. The results enable improved uncertainty estimates for direct dark matter detection experiments using fluorine and xenon targets, and motivate further nuclear structure calculations coupled with high-energy models linking to the NREFT formalism.
