# Longitudinal Scaling of Elliptic Flow in Landau Hydrodynamics

> This study presents generalization of the Landau hydrodynamic solution for multiparticle production applied to non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions. Obtained results shows longitudinal scaling of elliptic flow $v_2$ as a function of rapidity shifted by beam rapidity ($y-y_{beam}$) for different energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV and 200 GeV) and for different systems (Au-Au and Cu-Cu). It is argued, that the elliptic flow and its longitudinal scaling is due to the initial transverse energy density distribution and initial longitudinal thickness effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the longitudinal scaling of elliptic flow observed in non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions can be reproduced by a generalized Landau hydrodynamic solution that incorporates an azimuthally asymmetric initial transverse energy density distribution and an azimuthally dependent initial longitudinal thickness, although the model does not correspond well to experimental data due to the lack of realistic freeze-out dynamics and hadronic reinteractions.

## Method

The author generalizes Landau's 1+1-dimensional hydrodynamic solution for longitudinal expansion by coupling it to a separate transverse expansion solution that includes an angle-dependent pressure gradient, using the ideal gas equation of state (P=e/3). The initial conditions are parametrized using the Wounded Nucleon model based on Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions, which provides the azimuthally asymmetric energy density ratio f(R_phi) and the angle-dependent initial longitudinal thickness Delta(phi). The elliptic flow v_2 is then calculated from the entropy (proportional to particle number) at the freeze-out hypersurface, where the freeze-out time depends on both rapidity and azimuthal angle.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow v_2 is calculated for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 GeV and 200 GeV, and for Cu-Cu collisions at the same two energies.
- The impact parameter used is b=6 fm for Au-Au and b=3 fm for Cu-Cu.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used are R_A=1.12A^(1/3)-0.86A^(-1/3) fm, d=0.54 fm, and n_0=0.17 fm^(-3).
- The initial longitudinal thickness is given by Delta(phi)=kappa_phi R_A/gamma, where gamma=sqrt(s_NN)/(2 m_proton).

## Context

This work extends the original Landau hydrodynamic solution (Landau, 1953) for multiparticle production to non-central collisions by incorporating transverse asymmetries, building on recent modifications to the Landau rapidity distribution (Csernai, 2010; Wong, 2008). The model aims to explain the longitudinal scaling of elliptic flow observed in RHIC data, which is not reproduced by microscopic transport models such as AMPT (version 1.11) and UrQMD (version 2.3), but is captured by the Buda-Lund model based on analytic perfect fluid dynamics. The results enable further investigation of pure hydrodynamic effects in non-central collisions, though the author notes that more sophisticated initial state models and realistic freeze-out dynamics are needed for quantitative agreement with experimental data.
