# Effect of three-nucleon interaction in p-3He elastic scattering

> We present a detailed study of the effect of different three-nucleon interaction models in p-3He elastic scattering at low energies. In particular, two models have been considered: one derived from effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order and one derived from a more phenomenological point of view -- the so-called Illinois model. The four-nucleon scattering observables are calculated using the Kohn variational principle and the hyperspherical harmonics technique and the results are compared with available experimental data. We have found that the inclusion of either one of the other force model improves the agreement with the experimental data, in particular for the proton vector analyzing power.

## Key claim

Including either a chiral effective field theory three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-leading order or the phenomenological Illinois-7 model reduces the long-standing underprediction of the proton vector analyzing power in p-3He elastic scattering, but a discrepancy of about 8-10% at the peak remains.

## Method

The authors solved the four-nucleon scattering problem using the Kohn variational principle with a hyperspherical harmonics expansion of the wave function. They compared calculations using only nucleon-nucleon potentials (N3LO500, N3LO600, and AV18) with calculations that also included either a chiral N2LO three-nucleon force (with cutoff 500 or 600 MeV) or the phenomenological Illinois-7 three-nucleon force. Results were compared to experimental data at proton lab energies up to 6 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The 4He binding energy for the N3LO500/N2LO500* model is 28.36 MeV, for N3LO500/N2LO500 is 28.49 MeV, for N3LO600/N2LO600 is 28.64 MeV, and for AV18/IL7 is 28.44 MeV (experimental value is 28.30 MeV)
- Including three-nucleon forces reduces the underprediction of the proton analyzing power Ay to about 8-10% at the peak at Ep=5.54 MeV
- For p-d scattering at Ep=3 MeV, the underprediction of vector polarization observables remains about 18-20% after including three-nucleon forces
- The N2LO three-nucleon force parameters cD and cE are: 1.0 and -0.029 (N2LO500*), -0.12 and -0.196 (N2LO500), -0.26 and -0.846 (N2LO600)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the "Ay puzzle" in nucleon-deuteron scattering (Kie96, KH86, WGC88) to the four-nucleon system p-3He, where a similar discrepancy had been observed (Fon99, Vea01, HH03, Fisher06, DF07). The calculations build on a benchmark paper (Vea11) that showed close agreement between different few-body methods for p-3He phase shifts. The results enable future tests of higher-order chiral three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, which are needed to further reduce the remaining discrepancies.
