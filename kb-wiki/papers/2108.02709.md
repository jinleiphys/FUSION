# Two-particle scattering from finite-volume quantization conditions using the plane wave basis

> We propose an alternative approach to Lüscher's formula for extracting two-body scattering phase shifts from finite volume spectra with no reliance on the partial wave expansion. We use an effective-field-theory-based Hamiltonian method in the plane wave basis and decompose the corresponding matrix elements of operators into irreducible representations of the relevant point groups. The proposed approach allows one to benefit from the knowledge of the long-range interaction and avoids complications from partial wave mixing in a finite volume. We consider spin-singlet channels in the two-nucleon system and pion-pion scattering in the $ρ$-meson channel in the rest and moving frames to illustrate the method for non-relativistic and relativistic systems, respectively. For the two-nucleon system, the long-range interaction due to the one-pion exchange is found to make the single-channel Lüscher formula unreliable at the physical pion mass. For S-wave dominated states, the single-channel Lüscher method suffers from significant finite-volume artifacts for a $L=3$~fm box, but it works well for boxes with $L>5$~fm. However, for P-wave dominated states, significant partial wave mixing effects prevent the application of the single-channel Lüscher formula regardless of the box size (except for the near-threshold region). Using a toy model to generate synthetic data for finite-volume energies, we show that our effective-field-theory-based approach in the plane wave basis is capable of a reliable extraction of the phase shifts. For pion-pion scattering, we employ a phenomenological model to fit lattice QCD results at the physical pion mass. The extracted P-wave phase shifts are found to be in a good agreement with the experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors propose an alternative to Lüscher's formula for extracting two-body scattering phase shifts from finite-volume spectra that avoids the partial wave expansion, instead using an effective-field-theory-based Hamiltonian method in the plane wave basis; the approach is claimed to be particularly beneficial when long-range interactions are present, but the reliability of the method is demonstrated only for spin-singlet two-nucleon channels and pion-pion scattering in the rho-meson channel, with the caveat that for P-wave dominated states in the two-nucleon system, significant partial wave mixing effects prevent application of the single-channel Lüscher formula regardless of box size except near threshold.

## Method

The authors discretize the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (for non-relativistic systems) or the reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation (for relativistic systems) using a plane wave basis with discrete momenta in a finite cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. They decompose the matrix elements of operators into irreducible representations of the relevant point groups (O_h, D_4h, D_2h, D_3d) using projection operator techniques, then compute finite-volume energy levels by finding the poles of the T-matrix in the box via a determinant equation for each irrep. For the two-nucleon system, they employ a chiral effective field theory potential including one-pion exchange, while for pion-pion scattering they use a phenomenological model to fit lattice QCD results at the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- For S-wave dominated states in the two-nucleon system, the single-channel Lüscher method suffers from significant finite-volume artifacts for a box of size L = 3 fm, but works well for boxes with L > 5 fm.
- For the two-nucleon system at the physical pion mass, the long-range interaction due to one-pion exchange makes the single-channel Lüscher formula unreliable.
- For P-wave dominated states in the two-nucleon system, significant partial wave mixing effects prevent application of the single-channel Lüscher formula regardless of box size, except in the near-threshold region.
- For pion-pion scattering at the physical pion mass, the extracted P-wave phase shifts from the plane-wave-basis approach are found to be in good agreement with experimental results.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lee (2020) from a Fourier basis in coordinate space to a plane wave basis in momentum space, and generalizes the finite-volume quantization conditions of Lüscher (1985, 1986, 1990) to incorporate long-range interactions and partial wave mixing effects without relying on the partial wave expansion. The method addresses known limitations of the standard Lüscher approach identified by Bedaque (2006) and Sato (2007) regarding exponentially suppressed corrections, and by Luu (2011) and Doring (2012) regarding partial wave mixing; it enables more reliable extraction of scattering phase shifts from lattice QCD simulations in smaller volumes, particularly for systems with long-range interactions such as the two-nucleon system and exotic hadrons like X(3872) and Z_c(3900).
