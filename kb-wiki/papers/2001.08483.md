# Renormalizability of leading order covariant chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction

> In this work, we study the renormalization group invariance (RGI) of the recently proposed covariant power counting (PC) scheme in the case of nucleon-nucleon scattering [Chin.Phys. C42 (2018) 014103] at leading order (LO). We show that unlike the LO Weinberg case, RGI is satisfied in the $^3P_{0}$ channel, because a term of $pp'$ appears naturally in the covariant PC scheme at LO. Another interesting feature is that the $^1S_{0}$ and $^3P_{1}$ channels are correlated. Fixing the two relevant low energy constants by fitting to the $^1S_{0}$ phase shifts at $T_\mathrm{lab.}=10$ and $25$ MeV with a cutoff $Λ$ of $400-650$ MeV, the $^3P_{1}$ phase shifts can be described relatively well. In the limit of $Λ\rightarrow \infty$, the $^1S_0$ channel becomes cutoff independent, while RGI is lost in the $^3P_{1}$ channel, consistent with the Wigner bound and the previous observation that the $^{3}P_1$ channel better be treated perturbatively. As for the $^1P_{1}$ and $^3S_{1}$-$^3D_{1}$ channels, RGI is satisfied, similar to the Weinberg approach.

## Key claim

The covariant power counting scheme for chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions satisfies renormalization group invariance at leading order in the \(^3P_0\) channel, unlike the Weinberg scheme, because a \(pp'\) contact term appears naturally; however, the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_1\) channels are correlated, and renormalization group invariance in the \(^3P_1\) channel is lost in the infinite-cutoff limit, consistent with the Wigner bound.

## Method

The authors solved the Kadyshevsky equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering using a leading-order covariant chiral potential that includes five four-fermion contact terms and one-pion exchange, with the full Dirac spinor structure retained. They projected the potential into partial waves for total angular momentum \(J=0\) and \(1\), fitted the low-energy constants to Nijmegen phase shifts at specific laboratory energies, and studied the cutoff dependence of the phase shifts by varying the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) from 0.4 GeV to 10 GeV (or 400–650 MeV for the correlated \(^1S_0\)–\(^3P_1\) system). A separable regulator \(f(p,p') = \exp[(-p^{2n} - p'^{2n})/\Lambda^{2n}]\) with \(n=2\) was used.

## Key numbers

* The \(^3P_0\) contact potential is \(V_{3P0} = (-2\pi C_{3P0}/M^2) pp'\), and fitting \(C_{3P0}\) to the \(^3P_0\) phase shift at \(T_\mathrm{lab.}=10\) MeV yields cutoff-independent results up to \(\Lambda = 10\) GeV.
* The \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_1\) channels are correlated via \(\hat{C}_{1S0} = C_{1S0} - C_{3P1}\); fitting \(C_{1S0}\) and \(\hat{C}_{1S0}\) to \(^1S_0\) phase shifts at \(T_\mathrm{lab.}=10\) and \(25\) MeV with \(\Lambda = 400-650\) MeV predicts \(^3P_1\) phase shifts relatively well.
* The Wigner bound restricts the cutoff in the \(^1S_0\) channel to \(\Lambda \lesssim 650\) MeV, above which the scattering length and effective range cannot be fitted simultaneously.
* In the \(^3P_0\) channel with only one-pion exchange, the second limit cycle appears at \(\Lambda = 10.6\) GeV in the covariant scheme, compared to a smaller interval in the non-relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger approach.

## Context

This work extends the covariant power counting scheme proposed by Ren et al. (2018) for nucleon-nucleon scattering, which retains the full Dirac spinor structure, by testing its renormalization group invariance at leading order. It contrasts with the Weinberg power counting approach, where the \(^3P_0\) channel is not renormalization group invariant at leading order (Nogga, 2005). The results support the idea that the \(^3P_1\) channel should be treated perturbatively, as suggested by Yang (2009) and Wu (2018), and demonstrate that the covariant scheme can describe the \(^1S_0\) channel's unique features (e.g., the virtual bound state pole and zero amplitude at scattering momentum 340 MeV) as previously shown by Ren (2017). This work enables further studies of higher-order covariant chiral nuclear forces and their application to nuclear structure and reactions.
