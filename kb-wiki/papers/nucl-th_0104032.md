# The $庢NN$-system at low energy within a three-body approach

> The role of the $庢NN$-interaction is studied in the low energy regime in $庢$-deuteron reactions as well as in coherent and incoherent $庢$-photoproduction on the deuteron using a three-body model with separable two-body interactions. The three-body approach turns out to be quite essential in the most important lowest partial wave. Results are presented for differential and total cross sections as well as for the $庢$-meson spectrum. They differ significantly from those predicted by a simple rescattering model in which only first-order $庢N$- and $NN$-interactions in the final state are considered. The major features of the experimental data of $庢$-photoproduction in the near-threshold region are well reproduced.

## Key claim

The three-body approach is essential for describing the low-energy $\eta NN$ system, particularly in the lowest partial wave, and the major features of experimental $\eta$-photoproduction data near threshold are well reproduced, but the authors focus primarily on qualitative aspects because precise quantitative statements are difficult given the large uncertainties in the $\eta N$ scattering length (ranging from $a_{\eta N}=(0.27+0.22i)$ fm to $(0.88+0.27i)$ fm) and strong model dependence.

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev-type equations for the $\eta NN$ system using separable rank-one potentials for the $s$-wave $\eta N$ and $NN$ two-body interactions, with the $N^*(1535)$ resonance treated as an isobar coupled to both $\eta N$ and $\pi N$ channels. The formalism included antisymmetrization of the two nucleons, partial wave decomposition retaining $L=0,1,2$, and relativistic kinematics, with the resulting coupled integral equations solved numerically to obtain scattering and photoproduction amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- $\eta N$ scattering length used in the calculation: $a_{\eta N} = (0.75 + 0.27i)$ fm (from Wycech, consistent with Bat1)
- $NN$ scattering lengths: $a = 5.378$ fm for the $^3S_1$ state and $a = -23.690$ fm for the $^1S_0$ state, with $\beta_d = 1.4488$ fm$^{-1}$
- $N^*$ parameters: $g_{N^*}^{(\eta)} = 2.0$, $g_{N^*}^{(\pi)} = 1.1$, $\beta_{N^*}^{(\eta)} = 6.5$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta_{N^*}^{(\pi)} = 4.5$ fm$^{-1}$, $M_0 = 1622$ MeV
- The $\eta d \to \eta np$ break-up cross section is reduced by about 85% due to the $\eta NN$ interaction compared to the impulse approximation
- The first-order rescattering approximation overestimates the break-up cross section by about a factor of 2 compared to the full three-body calculation
- Using the cut-off parameters from Pena2 ($\beta_{N^*}^{(\eta)} = 13$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta_{N^*}^{(\pi)} = 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$) without pion exchange reduces the total $\eta d$ elastic cross section by about 30% near zero energy and by about 10% at $E_{\eta d} = 20$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body studies of the $\eta NN$ system (Fix and Arenhoevel, 2000) and addresses the unresolved question of whether a bound $\eta d$ state or resonance exists, which had been claimed by Shevchenko (1998) but not observed by Pena (1999) or Wycech (1999). The results contradict the strong damping of $\eta d$ attraction due to pion exchange reported by Pena (2000), finding instead that pion exchange effects are almost negligible. The three-body approach enables a more realistic treatment of $\eta$-photoproduction on the deuteron than the optical model used by Kamalov (1997), and provides a foundation for studying $\eta$-meson production on heavier nuclei near threshold.
