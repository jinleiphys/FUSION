# Three-hadron dynamics from lattice QCD

> Three-hadron spectroscopy is a key frontier in our understanding of the hadron spectrum. In recent years, significant formal and numerical advances have paved the way for studying three-hadron processes directly from lattice QCD, with outstanding applications including the Roper resonance and the doubly charmed tetraquark. This requires theoretical frameworks that relate finite-volume energies to infinite-volume three-particle scattering amplitudes. In this contribution, I discuss recent progress in formulating such frameworks for generic three-hadron systems, and present numerical results for three-meson systems at maximal isospin with physical quark masses, as well as our recent investigation of the three-body dynamics of the doubly charmed tetraquark, $T_{\rm cc}$.

# Three-hadron dynamics from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice QCD can now produce three-hadron scattering amplitudes at physical quark masses, resolving subleading three-body effects in certain systems; however, the three-body K matrix is a cutoff-dependent intermediate quantity whose parametrization must be assumed, and the formalism is strictly valid only below the first inelastic threshold.

## Method

The authors extract finite-volume energies from lattice QCD Euclidean correlation functions using a generalized eigenvalue problem with large operator sets, then simultaneously fit two- and three-body energy levels using the relativistic field-theoretic (RFT) three-particle quantization condition (QC3). The extracted K matrices are subsequently used as input to integral equations that yield cutoff-independent, unitary three-hadron scattering amplitudes. For the doubly charmed tetraquark, the D* meson is incorporated as a bound-state pole in the I=1/2 Dπ scattering amplitude within the three-body formalism.

## Key numbers

- Three-meson systems (3π+, 3K+, π+π+K+, K+K+π+) studied at physical quark masses using the E250 CLS ensemble, with additional ensembles at heavier quark masses ranging from 200-340 MeV
- For the 3π+ system, the threshold expansion coefficients K0 and K1 are extracted and compared to NLO ChPT predictions, showing qualitative agreement at the physical point
- The 3K+ scattering amplitude in the J^P=0- equilateral configuration is significantly larger than the 3π+ amplitude, consistent with chiral expectations
- For the Tcc analysis, the three-body K matrix is parametrized as Kdf,3 = KE (pπ - kπ)2, with mD^2 KE = 1.9 × 10^5 for the central curve matching the Padmanath (2022) finite-volume data
- The Tcc pole position in the complex plane is mapped as a function of KE, with values ranging from mD^2 KE / 10^5 = 1.0 to 3.0, showing two poles with non-zero imaginary parts at the best-fit value

## Context

This work extends the decade-long program of developing three-hadron finite-volume formalisms (Hansen and Sharpe 2014, 2015; Hammer et al. 2017; Mai and Doring 2017) to physical quark masses, building on earlier heavier-mass studies by Blanton et al. (2021) and Draper et al. (2023). The three-meson results provide benchmarks for Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions (Bijnens et al. 2021, 2022; Baeza-Ballesteros et al. 2023, 2024). The Tcc analysis introduces a three-body treatment that smoothly connects regimes where the D* meson is stable and unstable, addressing left-hand cut issues that complicate two-body approaches (Meng et al. 2023; Du et al. 2023). This enables future first-principles predictions of the Tcc pole position with controlled uncertainties, and opens the door to studying three-body resonances such as the Roper resonance and excited hyperons.
