# The Reality of the Fundamental Topological Structure in the QCD Vacuum

> Long-range order of a specific kind has recently been found directly in configurations dominating the regularized QCD path integral. In particular, a low-dimensional global structure was identified in typical space-time distributions of topological charge defined via the overlap Dirac matrix. The presence of the order has been concluded from the fact that the structure disappears after random permutation of position coordinates in measured densities. Here we complete the argument for the reality of this structure (namely the conjecture that its existence is a consequence of QCD dynamics and not an artifact of the overlap-based definition of lattice topological field) by showing that the structure ceases to exist after randomizing the space-time coordinates of the underlying gauge field. This implies that the long-range order present in the overlap-based topological density is indeed a manifestation of QCD vacuum, and that the notion of the fundamental structure (structure involving relevant features at all scales) is viable.

## Key claim
The long-range double-sheet structure observed in overlap-based topological charge density is a genuine consequence of QCD dynamics, not an artifact of the non-ultralocal overlap operator, because the structure disappears when the space-time coordinates of the underlying gauge field are randomly permuted.

## Method
The authors computed topological charge densities via the overlap Dirac operator on five independent \(8^4\) equilibrium configurations of Iwasaki gauge action at lattice spacing \(a = 0.165\) fm. They then randomized the gauge field by randomly permuting the space-time coordinates of the link variables and recomputed the topological densities on these randomized backgrounds. For both the original and randomized configurations, they identified all maximal connected sign-coherent regions built from elementary 3-d cubes connected via 2-d faces and measured the fraction of space-time occupied by each such region.

## Key numbers
- Five independent \(8^4\) lattice configurations of Iwasaki gauge action were used, at lattice spacing \(a = 0.165\) fm (determined from string tension).
- The overlap Dirac operator parameter was \(\rho = 1.368\) (\(\kappa = 0.19\)).
- In the original QCD configurations, the two largest sign-coherent 3-d structures (the double-sheet) together occupy about 70–80% of space-time, with a finite continuum limit.
- After gauge-field randomization (\(q^{QCD} \to q^{RU}\)), the double-sheet structure disappears, leaving only fragmented pieces, analogous to the result after random permutation of the topological density itself (\(q^{QCD} \to q^{R}\)) and to the result for infinitely strong coupling (\(\beta=0\)) configurations.

## Context
This work completes the argument begun in Horvath et al. (2003) and Horvath et al. (2005), which identified a low-dimensional global structure in overlap-based topological charge density on equilibrium QCD configurations. Those studies showed the structure disappears after random permutation of the density values; the present work shows it also disappears after randomizing the gauge field itself, ruling out the possibility that the structure is an artifact of the overlap operator's non-ultralocality. The result supports the viability of the "fundamental structure" concept, a structure involving features at all scales, and suggests that direct study of lattice QCD ensembles is a productive avenue for understanding the QCD vacuum, extending beyond purely low-energy approaches.
