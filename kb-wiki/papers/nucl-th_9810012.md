# Dimensional versus cut-off renormalization and the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> The role of dimensional regularization is discussed and compared with that of cut-off regularization in some quantum mechanical problems with ultraviolet divergence in two and three dimensions with special emphasis on the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Both types of renormalizations are performed for attractive divergent one- and two-term separable potentials, a divergent tensor potential, and the sum of a delta function and its derivatives. We allow energy-dependent couplings, and determine the form that these couplings should take if equivalence between the two regularization schemes is to be enforced. We also perform renormalization of an attractive separable potential superposed on an analytic divergent potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that equivalence between cut-off and dimensional regularization schemes in nonrelativistic quantum mechanical problems with ultraviolet divergence can be enforced only by allowing energy-dependent bare couplings, which the abstract does not explicitly state as a necessary condition.

## Method

The authors performed renormalization of several divergent potentials, including one-term and two-term separable potentials, a tensor potential, and a delta-function potential with derivatives, using both cut-off regularization (with a sharp momentum cutoff Λ) and dimensional regularization. They solved the partial-wave Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the K matrix in two and three dimensions, treating the bare couplings as energy-dependent functions to cancel divergences. For the numerical study, they added divergent potentials to an attractive separable potential modeling the S-wave spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon interaction and adjusted renormalized couplings to reproduce the experimental scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization techniques previously applied to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Adhikari and collaborators (1995) and by Cohen and collaborators (1996) using cut-off regularization, and by Lepage (1997) and others using dimensional regularization. The paper demonstrates that energy-dependent bare couplings are required to achieve equivalence between regularization schemes for potentials with L>0, and shows that divergent potentials added to finite-range separable potentials can be successfully renormalized to produce physically plausible low-energy results, enabling further development of effective field theory approaches to the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
