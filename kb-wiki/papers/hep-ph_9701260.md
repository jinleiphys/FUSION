# Baryon magnetic moments in chiral perturbation theory

> We consider the chiral expansion of the octet baryon magnetic moments in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory including all terms which are of order $q^4$. These terms are formally of quadratic order in the quark masses. We show that despite the large non-analytic quark mass corrections to the Coleman-Glashow relations at order $q^3$, including all analytic and non-analytic corrections at order $q^4$, which in total are of moderate size, allows for a fit to the measured magnetic moments due to the appearance of counter terms with free coupling constants of natural size. In this scheme, the $ΛΣ^0$ transition moment is predicted to be $μ_{ΛΣ^0} = (1.42 \pm 0.01) μ_N$.

## Key claim

The authors show that including all analytic and non-analytic corrections at order \(q^4\) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory allows a fit to the measured octet baryon magnetic moments with counter terms of natural size, despite the large non-analytic quark mass corrections at order \(q^3\) that had previously appeared to worsen the Coleman-Glashow relations. The hedge is that the predictive power is limited (only the \(\Lambda\Sigma^0\) transition moment is predicted), and the intention is primarily to demonstrate convergence of the chiral expansion rather than to produce many new predictions.

## Method

The authors performed a complete calculation of octet baryon magnetic moments in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at order \(q^4\), including one-loop graphs with insertions from the dimension-two meson-baryon Lagrangian, tree graphs from the dimension-four Lagrangian, and contributions from \(1/m\) fixed-coefficient terms and scalar symmetry-breaking insertions. Low-energy constants \(b_{9,10,11}\) were estimated via resonance saturation using decuplet, Roper octet, and vector meson exchange, while the five dimension-four counter terms \(\alpha_{1,2,3,4}\) and \(\beta_1\) were fitted to the measured magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: \(\mu_p = 2.79\,\mu_N\) at order \(q^4\), with contributions \(\mu_p^{(2)} = 4.31\), \(\mu_p^{(3)} = -2.20\), \(\mu_p^{(4)} = 0.69\) (relative sizes \(1 - 0.51 + 0.16\))
- Neutron magnetic moment: \(\mu_n = -1.91\,\mu_N\), with contributions \(\mu_n^{(2)} = -2.61\), \(\mu_n^{(3)} = 0.84\), \(\mu_n^{(4)} = -0.13\) (relative sizes \(1 - 0.32 + 0.05\))
- \(\Lambda\Sigma^0\) transition moment predicted: \(\mu_{\Lambda\Sigma^0} = (1.42 \pm 0.01)\,\mu_N\)
- Fitted dimension-two low-energy constants at order \(q^4\): \(b_6^D = 3.93\), \(b_6^F = 3.01\)
- Fitted dimension-four low-energy constants: \(\alpha_1 = -0.45\), \(\alpha_2 = -0.06\), \(\alpha_3 = -0.47\), \(\alpha_4 = 0.45\), \(\beta_1 = -0.74\)
- Resonance-saturated low-energy constants (in GeV\(^{-1}\)): \(b_9 = 0.81\), \(b_{10} = 0.95\), \(b_{11} = 0.36\) (for \(Z=0\), \(\mathcal{C}=1.5\))
- Decuplet and vector meson contributions to \(\mu_p^{(4)}\): \(\mu_p^{(4,\Delta)} = -0.42\), \(\mu_p^{(4,V)} = -0.27\), \(\mu_p^{(4,R)} = -0.04\)

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory treatment of octet baryon magnetic moments beyond the order \(q^3\) calculation of Jenkins, Manohar, et al. (1993), which had found that one-loop corrections worsened the tree-level Coleman-Glashow relations and required unphysically small axial couplings or explicit decuplet states. The authors show that including all order \(q^4\) terms (including previously neglected vector meson t-channel contributions and \(1/m\) fixed-coefficient insertions) restores convergence, with \(q^4\) corrections typically a factor two to three smaller than the \(q^3\) ones. This enables a consistent chiral perturbation theory description without resorting to the extended counting scheme of Jenkins et al. (1993) or the reordering based on linear flavor symmetry breaking proposed by Borasoy (1996).
