# The characteristics of thermalization of boost-invariant plasma from holography

> We report on the approach towards the hydrodynamic regime of boost-invariant N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma at strong coupling starting from various far-from-equilibrium states at tau=0. The results are obtained through numerical solution of Einstein's equations for the dual geometries, as described in detail in the companion article arXiv:1203.0755. Despite the very rich far-from-equilibrium evolution, we find surprising regularities in the form of clear correlations between initial entropy and total produced entropy, as well as between initial entropy and the temperature at thermalization, understood as the transition to a hydrodynamic description. For 29 different initial conditions that we consider, hydrodynamics turns out to be definitely applicable for proper times larger than 0.7 in units of inverse temperature at thermalization. We observe a sizable anisotropy in the energy-momentum tensor at thermalization, which is nevertheless entirely due to hydrodynamic effects. This suggests that effective thermalization in heavy ion collisions may occur significantly earlier than true thermalization.

## Key claim
Despite a wide variety of far-from-equilibrium initial states, the transition to hydrodynamic behavior in strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma shows surprising regularities: the initial entropy density (in units of effective temperature at tau=0) strongly correlates with both the total entropy produced and the effective temperature at thermalization. The authors find that hydrodynamics becomes applicable for proper times larger than 0.7 in units of inverse temperature at thermalization, and that a sizable pressure anisotropy at thermalization is entirely due to hydrodynamic effects, suggesting effective thermalization may occur significantly earlier than true thermalization.

## Method
The authors numerically solve 5-dimensional Einstein's equations in the AdS/CFT correspondence for boost-invariant Bjorken flow of strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma, starting from 29 different initial metric profiles on the hypersurface tau=0. They extract the plasma energy-momentum tensor from the asymptotics of the solution at the AdS boundary and compare the evolution to all-order viscous hydrodynamics up to third order, using a numerical criterion for thermalization based on the deviation of tau*dw/dtau from the third-order hydrodynamic expression (threshold of 0.5% deviation). Entropy is determined from the apparent horizon area element mapped to the boundary along ingoing radial null geodesics.

## Key numbers
- For 29 different initial conditions, hydrodynamics is definitely applicable for proper times larger than 0.7 in units of inverse temperature at thermalization (w = T_eff * tau > 0.7).
- The dimensionless parameter w at thermalization ranges between 0.37 and 0.67 for all considered initial states.
- The entropy production follows a fitted power law: s_n-eq^(f) - s_n-eq^(i) = 1.64 * (s_n-eq^(i))^1.58.
- The thermalization criterion uses a threshold of |tau*dw/dw / F_hydro^(3rd order)(w) - 1| < 0.005.
- For a selected profile, perfect agreement with hydrodynamics is observed for w > 0.63.

## Context
This work extends previous holographic studies of Bjorken flow by Beuf (2009) and Kovchegov (2007) that used power series expansions around tau=0 but had insufficient radius of convergence to analyze the transition to hydrodynamics. It differs from the numerical approach of Chesler (2009) which introduced a deformation of the 4-dimensional metric to pump energy into the vacuum, precluding analysis from tau=0. The results enable systematic characterization of how initial conditions affect thermalization and entropy production in strongly coupled plasma, and suggest that effective thermalization in heavy ion collisions may occur earlier than true thermalization, with implications for interpreting RHIC and LHC data.
