# N/Z dependence of balance energy throughout the colliding geometries

> We study the N/Z dependence of balance energy throughout the mass range for colliding geometry varying from central to peripheral ones. Our results indicate that balance energy decreases linearly with increase in N/Z ratio for all the masses throughout the colliding geometry range. Also, the N/Z dependence of balance energy is sensitive to symmetry energy.

# N/Z dependence of balance energy throughout the colliding geometries

## Key claim
Balance energy decreases linearly with increasing N/Z ratio for all masses across central to peripheral colliding geometries, but the strength of this linear dependence (the slope) is sensitive to the symmetry energy and varies strongly with system mass and impact parameter.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state and an isospin- and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section reduced to 80% of the free value. They simulated collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe isotopic series with N/Z ratios from 1.0 to 2.0 in steps of 0.2, at three impact parameter ranges (b/b_max = 0.2–0.4, 0.4–0.6, and 0.6–0.8), and extracted the balance energy from the directed transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- For the Ca isotopic series, the slope of balance energy versus N/Z is 33 MeV per unit N/Z at b/b_max = 0.2–0.4, 58 at b/b_max = 0.4–0.6, and 187 at b/b_max = 0.6–0.8.
- For the Ni series, slopes are 25, 41, and 115 MeV per unit N/Z for the same three impact parameter ranges.
- For the Zr series, slopes are 21, 27, and 67 MeV per unit N/Z.
- For the Xe series, slopes are 18, 19, and 39 MeV per unit N/Z.
- For the Sn series, slopes are 15, 18, and 36 MeV per unit N/Z.
- The slope for the Ca series increases by approximately 400% when moving from central (b/b_max = 0.2–0.4) to peripheral (b/b_max = 0.6–0.8) collisions.
- The slope for the Xe series increases by approximately 150% over the same change in colliding geometry.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Gautam and Puri, 2011) on the sensitivity of collective transverse in-plane flow to symmetry energy in the Fermi energy region, now focusing specifically on the N/Z dependence of balance energy. The results demonstrate that balance energy is a probe of the isospin-dependent part of the nuclear equation of state, and the strong variation of the N/Z slope with impact parameter and system mass provides constraints for future models of asymmetric nuclear matter. The study enables systematic comparisons with experimental data from radioactive beam facilities and with other proposed symmetry-energy observables such as isospin diffusion, isoscaling, and neutron-proton differential flow.
