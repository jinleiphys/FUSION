# Does $f_1(1420)$ have a double-peak structure?

> The one-pion exchange interaction between the kaon and the vector antikaon is investigated by solving the Schrodinger equation in the S-wave approximation. In addition to the $f_1(1285)$ particle, another bound state of $K \bar{K}^*$ is found, which is approximately 9 MeV below the threshold of $K \bar{K}^*$ and labeled $f_1(1378)$ for convenience in this manuscript. Under the outgoing wave condition, two resonance states of $K \bar{K}^*$ are produced by solving the Schrodinger equation with different coupling constants in the one-pion-exchange potential fixed with the binding energies of $K \bar{K}^*$ bound states $f_1(1285)$ and $f_1(1378)$ respectively. Both of the resonance states are located in the vicinity of 1400 MeV, and thus it is reasonable to assume that these two resonance states correspond to the $f_1(1420)$ particle in the review of the Particle Data Group although they arise from different couplings of the kaon and vector antikaon, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $f_1(1420)$ particle listed by the Particle Data Group may actually consist of two distinct resonance states of the $K \bar{K}^*$ system, arising from different coupling constants in the one-pion-exchange potential, rather than a single resonance; this double-peak structure is conditional on the existence of a second, previously unobserved bound state labeled $f_1(1378)$.

## Method

The one-pion-exchange interaction between a kaon and a vector antikaon is modeled with a Yukawa-type potential, and the radial Schrodinger equation is solved analytically in the S-wave approximation by transforming it into a Bessel equation. Bound states are obtained by requiring the wave function to vanish at infinity, which imposes zero-point conditions on Bessel functions; resonance states are obtained under the outgoing wave condition, which imposes zero-point conditions on Hankel functions of the second kind. The coupling constant in the potential is fixed separately using the binding energies of two different $K \bar{K}^*$ bound states: the known $f_1(1285)$ (binding energy 105 MeV) and a predicted shallow bound state $f_1(1378)$ (binding energy approximately 9 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The $f_1(1285)$ particle is treated as a $K \bar{K}^*$ bound state with a binding energy of **105 MeV** below the $K \bar{K}^*$ threshold.
- A second $K \bar{K}^*$ bound state, labeled $f_1(1378)$, is predicted approximately **9 MeV** below the $K \bar{K}^*$ threshold.
- With the coupling constant fixed from $f_1(1285)$, the resulting resonance state lies at **1417 - i18 MeV** on the complex energy plane (center-of-mass frame).
- With the coupling constant fixed from $f_1(1378)$, the resulting resonance state lies at **1425 - i41 MeV** on the complex energy plane.
- The coupling constant values are **g = 1.682** (from the $f_1(1285)$ zero point at $\alpha = 7.1831$) and **g = 0.9293** (from the $f_1(1378)$ zero point at $\alpha = 3.9696$).
- The PDG value for the $f_1(1420)$ mass is $1428.4^{+1.5}_{-1.3}$ MeV and width $56.7 \pm 3.3$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Sun, 2024) of the $K \bar{K}^*$ system using the same Schrodinger equation approach, which identified one bound state ($f_1(1285)$) and one resonance ($f_1(1420)$). It contrasts with the unitary coupled-channel approximation used by Roca (2005) and Sun (2014), where vector meson exchange is dominant and pion exchange is neglected. The prediction of a second bound state near threshold and two distinct resonances near 1400 MeV suggests that the $f_1(1420)$ may have a double-peak structure, which could be tested by future experimental searches for the $f_1(1378)$ state.
