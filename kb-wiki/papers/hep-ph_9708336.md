# On the Parity Degeneracy of Baryons

> The gross features of the observed baryon excitation spectrum below 2 GeV are well explained if the spectrum generating algebra of its intrinsic orbital angular momentum states is o(4)*su(2)_I. The spins of the resonances are obtained through the coupling of a Lorentz bi-spinor (1/2,0)+ (0,1/ 2) to a multiplet of the type (j,j) in its O(4)/O(3) reduction. The parities of the resonances follow from those of the O(3) members of the (j,j) multiplets. In this way relativistic SL(2,C) representations are constructed. For example, the first S11, P11, and D13 states with masses around 1500 MeV fit into the (1/2, 1/2)* [(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] representation. The observed parities of the resonances correspond to natural parities of the (1/2,1/2) states. The second P11, S11, D13- together with the first P13, F15, D15, and (a predicted) F17 -resonances, centered around 1700 MeV, are organized into the (3/2,3/2)*[(1/2,0)+(0,1/2)] representation. I argue that the members of the (3/2,3/2) multiplet carry unnatural parities and that in this region chiral symmetry is restored. In the N(939)- N(1650) transition the chiral symmetry mode is changed, and therefore, a chiral phase transition is predicted to take place.

# On the Parity Degeneracy of Baryons

## Key claim

The baryon excitation spectrum below 2 GeV is well explained if the spectrum generating algebra of its intrinsic orbital angular momentum states is o(4)⊗su(2)_I, but the parity degeneracy observed in certain resonance groups arises from the specific O(4)/O(3) reduction of (j,j) multiplets rather than from exact parity doublets; a chiral phase transition is predicted to occur in the N(939) to N(1650) transition, where the chiral symmetry mode changes from Nambu-Goldstone to Wigner-Weyl.

## Method

The author classifies non-strange baryon resonances below 2 GeV into SL(2,C) representations constructed by coupling a Lorentz bi-spinor (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) to O(4) multiplets of type (j,j), with j half-integer, and performing the O(4)/O(3) reduction to obtain the orbital angular momentum content and parities. The total angular momentum J is obtained by coupling the spin-1/2 state to the orbital angular momentum states l=0,...,2j from the (j,j) multiplet, with parities determined by whether the O(3) members carry natural parity (-1)^l or unnatural parity (-1)^(l+1). The relativistic equations of motion for the resonance families are identified with the projectors onto the corresponding SL(2,C) representations.

## Key numbers

- The first excited P11, S11, and D13 states have masses 1440 MeV, 1535 MeV, and 1520 MeV respectively, and fit into the (1/2,1/2)⊗[(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)] representation.
- The second P11, S11, and D13 states have masses 1710 MeV, 1650 MeV, and 1700 MeV respectively, and together with P13, F15, D15, and a predicted F17 resonance (masses between 1675 MeV and 1720 MeV) form the (3/2,3/2)⊗[(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)] representation.
- The maximal mass splitting within the first nucleon resonance family is about 90 MeV; within the second family it is about 70 MeV; for Delta resonances these splittings are 130 MeV and 50 MeV respectively.
- The nucleon-S11 transition at 1650 MeV is predicted to be a chiral phase transition; previous work (Hatsuda and Prakash, year not given) predicted initial density 3.1ρ0 and final density 6.4ρ0 for the chiral phase transition using the first S11 state.
- The third nucleon resonance family (P11 at 2100 MeV, S11 at 2090 MeV, D13 at 2080 MeV, and others from 1900 MeV to 2250 MeV) fits into the (5/2,5/2)⊗[(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)] representation, with mass splitting reaching 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic classifications of baryon parity doublets by Dashen (late 1960s) using SU(3)_f⊗Z and by Iachello (year not given) using geometric symmetry groups C3v and C∞v, but provides a relativistic SL(2,C) framework that yields equations of motion. The classification predicts a chiral phase transition in the N(939) to N(1650) transition, building on lattice QCD results for parity degeneracy above the critical temperature (DeTar, year not given) and on sigma-model calculations of the nucleon-S11 mass splitting (Hatsuda and Prakash, year not given). The work enables predictions of new resonances (F17 near 1.7 GeV, H1,11 above 2 GeV, and three new Delta states near 2.3 GeV) and identifies the Rarita-Schwinger field as describing the S11, P11, and D13 family as a whole rather than an isolated spin-3/2 state.
