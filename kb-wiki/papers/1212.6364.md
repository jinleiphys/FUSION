# Living on the edge of stability, the limits of the nuclear landscape

> A first-principles description of nuclear systems along the drip lines presents a substantial theoretical and computational challenge. In this paper, we discuss the nuclear theory roadmap, some of the key theoretical approaches, and present selected results with a focus on long isotopic chains. An important conclusion, which consistently emerges from these theoretical analyses, is that three-nucleon forces are crucial for both global nuclear properties and detailed nuclear structure, and that many-body correlations due to the coupling to the particle continuum are essential as one approaches particle drip lines. In the quest for a comprehensive nuclear theory, high performance computing plays a key role.

## Key claim

Three-nucleon forces (3NFs) are crucial for both global nuclear properties and detailed nuclear structure, and many-body correlations due to coupling to the particle continuum become essential as one approaches particle drip lines; however, these conclusions emerge from theoretical analyses that are still limited by basis truncation errors and the challenge of including continuum effects in ab initio frameworks.

## Method

The paper reviews and discusses several ab initio many-body methods applied to nuclear structure along isotopic chains, including the no-core shell model (NCSM), coupled-cluster theory, and the Gamow Shell Model (GSM). These methods are combined with realistic nuclear interactions derived from chiral effective field theory, with interactions renormalized via similarity transformations such as the Lee-Suzuki transformation, Vlow-k, and similarity renormalization group (SRG) approaches. The authors present selected results for light and medium-mass nuclei, focusing on long isotopic chains such as calcium and oxygen, and discuss how three-nucleon forces and continuum coupling affect binding energies, shell structure, and drip-line positions.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff for reproducing nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts up to ~300 MeV laboratory kinetic energy requires Λ ~ 500-700 MeV.
- In coupled-cluster theory at the singles and doubles level, the number of floating-point operations scales as n_o^2 n_u^4, where n_o and n_u are the numbers of hole and particle orbitals, respectively.
- For 6Li, NCSM calculations were performed in model spaces up to N_max = 16, giving 171 possible nlj orbitals and 2280 individual single-particle states, with a many-body space dimension d = 0.79 × 10^9.
- The ground state in NCSM is usually reached with ~10-20 Lanczos iterations.
- Lattice QCD calculations for NN systems require lattice spacings around b = 0.05 fm and lattices of order 96^3 × 193, with simulation cost growing as b^{-6}.

## Context

This paper reviews the theoretical roadmap for describing nuclear systems along the drip lines, extending prior work on ab initio methods such as NCSM (Navratil 2009), coupled-cluster (Hagen 2010a), and the Gamow Shell Model (GSM). It builds on chiral EFT interactions (Machleidt 2011, Epelbaum 2009) and discusses how three-nucleon forces are essential for understanding shell evolution in oxygen isotopes (Otsuka 2010, Hagen 2012a) and calcium isotopes (Holt 2012, Hagen 2012b). The work enables future studies of very neutron-rich nuclei at facilities like FRIB and connects to large-scale DFT calculations assessing the limits of the nuclear landscape (Witek 2012).
