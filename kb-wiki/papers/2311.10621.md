# Hadronization of Heavy Quarks

> Heavy-flavor hadrons produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are a sensitive probe for studying hadronization mechanisms of the quark-gluon-plasma. In this work, we survey how different transport models for the simulation of heavy-quark diffusion through a quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions implement hadronization and how this affects final-state observables. Utilizing the same input charm-quark distribution in all models at the hadronization transition, we find that the transverse-momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor of various charm hadron species has significant sensitivity to the hadronization scheme. In addition, the charm-hadron elliptic flow exhibits a nontrivial dependence on the elliptic flow of the hadronizing partonic medium.

## Key claim

Using identical charm-quark distributions at the hadronization transition across multiple transport models, the transverse-momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor for various charm hadron species shows significant sensitivity to the hadronization scheme, while the charm-hadron elliptic flow exhibits a nontrivial dependence on the elliptic flow of the hadronizing partonic medium.

## Method

The authors surveyed how eight different transport models (Catania, Duke, LBT, Nantes, PHSD, TAMU, Torino, LANL) implement hadronization of heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions, providing detailed descriptions of each model's coalescence probability functions, Wigner function parameters, light-quark distributions, quark masses, resonance state accounting, and fragmentation functions. They imposed identical charm-quark distributions at the hadronization hypersurface across all models to enable systematic comparison of hadronization effects on final-state observables.

## Context

This work extends the series of comparative studies of heavy-quark transport models initiated by Rapp (2018) on benchmark heavy-quark interactions, Cao (2018) on heavy-quark interactions with the QGP, and Xu (2018) on initial-production mechanisms and QGP expansion scenarios. The paper provides the first dedicated in-depth comparative study of hadronization processes across multiple heavy-flavor transport models, enabling improved understanding of how different hadronization implementations affect final-state observables and reducing uncertainties in scientific conclusions drawn from these models.
