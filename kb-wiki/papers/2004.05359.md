# First measurement of direct photoproduction of the $a_2(1320)^0$ meson on the proton

> We present the first measurement of the exclusive reaction $γp \rightarrow a_2(1320)^0 \, p$ in the photon energy range $3.5$-$5.5$ GeV and four-momentum transfer squared $0.2<-t<2.0$ GeV$^2$. Data were collected with the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility. The neutral $a_2$ resonance was detected by measuring the reaction $γp \rightarrow π^0 ηp$ and reconstructing the $π^0 η$ invariant mass. The differential cross section $dσ/dt$ was extracted at different beam energies in each $-t$ bin. The most prominent feature of the differential cross section is a dip at $-t\simeq 0.55$ GeV$^2$. This can be well described in the framework of Regge phenomenology, where the exchange degeneracy hypothesis predicts a zero in the reaction amplitude for this value of the four-momentum transfer.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first measurement of neutral a2(1320) meson photoproduction on the proton, with the differential cross section showing a dip at -t approximately 0.55 GeV^2 that can be described by Regge phenomenology under the exchange degeneracy hypothesis, though the measurement is limited to the photon energy range 3.5-5.5 GeV and four-momentum transfer squared 0.2 < -t < 2.0 GeV^2.

## Method
The exclusive reaction gamma p -> a2(1320)^0 p was measured using the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab by detecting the final state gamma p -> pi0 eta p, reconstructing the pi0 eta invariant mass, and extracting the a2 resonance contribution via a simultaneous chi-squared fit to the differential cross section d^2sigma/dt dM across kinematic bins. The a2 line shape was modeled with a Breit-Wigner function, the background as a decreasing exponential, and the detector acceptance was corrected using iterative Monte Carlo simulations matched to data distributions in -t and Gottfried-Jackson angles.

## Key numbers
- Photon beam energy range: 3.5 to 5.5 GeV
- Four-momentum transfer squared range: 0.2 < -t < 2.0 GeV^2
- Dip in differential cross section observed at -t approximately 0.55 GeV^2
- Fitted a2 mass: (1308 +/- 2) MeV, compared to PDG value (1312.2 +/- 2.8) MeV
- a2 width fixed to PDG value: (113.4 +/- 1.3) MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the fit: 64.3/53 = 1.21
- Photon flux during run: approximately 4 x 10^7 gamma/s
- Fraction of events with multiple tagged photons: 12.5%
- Trigger efficiency: average 80%
- Systematic uncertainties: photon flux 5.7%, trigger efficiency 2.8%, acceptance correction variable approximately 5%, sPlot variable approximately 4%, kinematic fit variable approximately 3%

## Context
This work provides the first measurement of neutral a2(1320) photoproduction, extending prior studies of charged a2 photoproduction at SLAC (Ballam 1969, PhysRevD.41.3317, PhysRevD.48.3045) and low-energy pi0 eta photoproduction measurements by GRAAL (Ajaka 2008), Crystal Ball/TAPS/A2 (Kashevarov 2009, 2010), and CB-ELSA (Horn 2007, 2008). The measurement enables future partial wave analysis of the pi0 eta channel, where variations of the P-D phase difference could signal exotic resonances as proposed by Rodas (2018) and Albrecht (2019).
