# Pion momentum distributions in the nucleon in chiral effective theory

> We compute the light-cone momentum distributions of pions in the nucleon in chiral effective theory using both pseudovector and pseudoscalar pion-nucleon couplings. For the pseudovector coupling we identify δ-function contributions associated with end-point singularities arising from the pion-nucleon rainbow diagrams, as well as from pion bubble and tadpole diagrams which are not present in the pseudoscalar model. Gauge invariance is demonstrated, to all orders in the pion mass, with the inclusion of Kroll-Ruderman couplings involving operator insertions at the πNN vertex. The results pave the way for phenomenological applications of pion cloud models that are manifestly consistent with the chiral symmetry properties of QCD.

## Key claim
The paper computes light-cone momentum distributions of pions in the nucleon using both pseudovector (PV) and pseudoscalar (PS) pion-nucleon couplings, and demonstrates that gauge invariance in the PV theory requires inclusion of delta-function contributions at zero light-cone momentum fraction (y=0) from rainbow, bubble, and tadpole diagrams, as well as Kroll-Ruderman terms. These delta-function contributions are absent in the PS model and may be artifacts of chiral perturbation theory that would soften into a peak near x=0 in full QCD.

## Method
The authors evaluated the isovector light-cone momentum distributions for pions and recoil nucleons by computing the relevant Feynman diagrams (rainbow, bubble, tadpole, and Kroll-Ruderman) from the chiral effective Lagrangian, using both PV and PS couplings. They integrated over the k- component of pion momentum using Cauchy's integral formula, performed k-perp integrations with an ultraviolet cutoff Lambda, and verified gauge invariance by checking the Ward-Takahashi identity relating wave function and vertex renormalization factors.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV; nucleon axial vector charge g_A = 1.267
- The leading nonanalytic term in the delta-function contribution from the pion rainbow diagram is: f^(delta)(y) = -[g_A^2 / (4 (4pi f_pi)^2)] m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2) delta(y) + terms involving Lambda
- The pion bubble contribution gives: f^(bub)(y) = [1 / (2 (4pi f_pi)^2)] m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2) delta(y) + terms involving Lambda
- The total delta-function coefficient in the combined pion distribution is proportional to (1 - g_A^2), vanishing when g_A = 1
- The wave function renormalization factor satisfies: Z_2^(-1) - 1 = 3 integral_0^1 dy (f^(on)(y) + f^(off)(y) - f^(delta)(y))

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Chen and Ji (2001, 2002) and Arndt and Savage (2002) who computed nonsinglet twist-2 operator matrix elements using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but only considered lowest-order moments or light-cone distributions without the full set of diagrams. It contrasts with the majority of earlier pion cloud calculations, from Drell, Levy and Yan (1970) and Sullivan (1972) through Thomas (1983) and the nonanalytic behavior studies by Thomas, Melnitchouk and Steffens (2000), which used PS coupling that is inconsistent with chiral symmetry without a scalar sigma field. The results enable future phenomenological applications of pion cloud models that are manifestly consistent with QCD chiral symmetry, and provide the foundation for an upcoming study by Hendricks et al. (2013) on the phenomenological consequences of the PV theory distributions.
