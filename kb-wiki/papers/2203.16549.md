# Exponential Approach to the Hydrodynamic Attractor in Yang-Mills Kinetic Theory

> We use principal component analysis to study the hydrodynamic attractor in Yang-Mills kinetic theory undergoing the Bjorken expansion with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions. The late time hydrodynamic attractor is characterized by a single principal component determining the overall energy scale. How it is reached is governed by the disappearance of single subleading principal component characterizing deviations of the pressure anisotropy, the screening mass and the scattering rate. We find that for wide range of couplings the approach to the hydrodynamic attractor at late times is well described by an exponential. Its decay rate dependence on the coupling turns out to translate into a simple dependence on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a wide range of couplings, the approach to the hydrodynamic attractor at late times in Yang-Mills kinetic theory is well described by an exponential, with the decay rate depending on the coupling in a way that translates into a simple dependence on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. This claim is hedged by the fact that the exponential behavior is observed for the variance of the pressure anisotropy across different initial conditions, not necessarily for individual trajectories, and that slight differences in the attractor curves persist at intermediate times \(\tilde{w} \lesssim 3\) before full convergence.

## Method

The authors performed 0+1 dimensional Bjorken-expanding simulations using the effective kinetic theory of pure glue QCD, with initial conditions from the Color Glass Condensate model parametrized by the ratio of nuclear saturation scales \(x\) and the longitudinal smearing width \(\sigma\). They applied principal component analysis to a set of four dimensionless observables (transverse pressure, longitudinal pressure, screening mass, and scattering rate) to study dimensionality reduction and the emergence of the hydrodynamic attractor. For the late-time analysis, they additionally initialized the system with Romatschke-Strickland type distributions at various initial times \(\tilde{w}_0\) and anisotropies \(\xi_0\) to isolate the exponential decay of non-hydrodynamic contributions.

## Key numbers

* The variance of \(P_L/(\epsilon/3)\) decays exponentially for \(\tilde{w} \gtrsim 0.5\) across coupling strengths \(\lambda = 0.1\) to \(20\).
* The initial conditions spanned ratios of saturation scales \(1 \leq x \leq 6\) and longitudinal smearing widths \(0.05 < \sigma < 0.50\).
* The coupling strengths studied were \(\lambda = 5, 10, 20\) for the CGC initial condition analysis and \(\lambda = 0.1 - 20\) for the Romatschke-Strickland late-time analysis.
* The initial proper time was set to \(\tau_0 = 1/Q\) for CGC initial conditions.
* The universal time scale is defined as \(\tilde{w} = \tau (\epsilon+P)/(4\pi\eta)\), where \(\eta\) is the shear viscosity.
* The first-order viscous hydrodynamics prediction is \((P_L/(\epsilon/3))_{\rm hydro} = 1 - 4/(3\pi\tilde{w})\).
* The conformal RTA kinetic theory predicts a decay rate of non-hydrodynamic contributions proportional to \(-(6/5)\pi \tilde{w}\) for individual realizations, with the variance decaying twice as fast.

## Context

This work extends the principal component analysis approach to hydrodynamic attractors introduced by Heller et al. (2020) from conformal Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) kinetic theory to the more realistic setting of pure glue QCD kinetic theory with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions. It builds on previous studies of hydrodynamic attractors in effectively 0+1 dimensional systems by Romatschke (2017), Strickland (2017), and others, and on the effective kinetic theory framework for pure glue QCD developed by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002). The results enable future work on generalizing the concept of hydrodynamic attractors to higher-dimensional systems and on understanding the analytic structure of non-hydrodynamic excitations in QCD kinetic theory, which has been explored by Kurkela et al. (2017) and Moore et al. (2018).
