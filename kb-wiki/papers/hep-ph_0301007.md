# Solution of coupled vertex and propagator Dyson-Schwinger equations in the scalar Munczek-Nemirovsky model

> In a scalar $ϕ^2χ$ model, we exactly solve the vertex and $ϕ$ propagator Dyson-Schwinger equations under the assumption of a spatially constant (Munczek-Nemirovsky) propagator for the $χ$ field. Various truncation schemes are also considered.

# Solution of coupled vertex and propagator Dyson-Schwinger equations in the scalar Munczek-Nemirovsky model

## Key claim

The exact solution of the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations for the vertex and propagator in the scalar Munczek-Nemirovsky model exists only for imaginary coupling (or negative correlation strength α < 0), despite the fact that various truncated versions of the equations admit solutions for real coupling; the central result is that there is no real solution to the untruncated model unless the coupling is imaginary, as shown by the divergence of the diagrammatic expansion of the vertex.

## Method

The authors consider a scalar φ²χ field theory in Euclidean space and impose the Munczek-Nemirovsky approximation, in which the χ propagator is taken as a constant in spacetime, g² D(p) = (2π)⁴ α δ⁴(p). This reduces all integral Dyson-Schwinger equations to algebraic or ordinary differential equations. The system is closed without truncation by functionally differentiating the self-energy with respect to the propagator to obtain the four-point scattering kernel, yielding a functional differential equation for the vertex Γ as a function of the propagator S. Two variants are considered: one with δD/δS = 0 and one with δD/δS determined from the χ propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- For α = 1 (positive coupling), the rainbow truncation yields the propagator S(x) = (x ± √(x² + 4α))/(2α), where x = p² + m².
- The coefficients f_i in the vertex expansion Γ = Σ f_i (α S²)^i follow the recursion f_i = δ_{i0} + Σ_{j=0}^{i-1} (2j+1) f_j f_{i-j-1} for δD/δS = 0, with first few values: 1, 1, 4, 27, 248, 2830, ...
- The asymptotic behavior of these coefficients is f_n ∼ (2n+1)!!/e, causing the vertex sum to diverge for α S² > 0.
- For α = -1 and δD/δS = 0, the full solution has a critical point at x_crit = 1.518 √|α|, beyond which the solution terminates.
- For α = -1 and δD/δS determined from Eq. (ddds), the solution exists for all x.

## Context

This work extends the Munczek-Nemirovsky model, originally introduced by Munczek and Nemirovsky (1983) as a simple infrared-enhanced kernel for quark Dyson-Schwinger equations in QCD, and previously applied in studies by Bender (1996, 2002) and others. The paper demonstrates that the common practice of truncating the Dyson-Schwinger hierarchy at the propagator level (rainbow truncation) or at the vertex level (ladder truncation) can produce solutions where none exist in the full untruncated theory, highlighting the importance of consistent truncation schemes. The results enable future extension to fermion-gauge boson theories with Dirac structure, where the existence problems encountered here are not expected to persist, and suggest that similar functional closure methods could be applied to models with less restrictive propagator forms such as Gaussian shapes.
