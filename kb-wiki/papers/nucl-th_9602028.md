# Delta production in $\bar{p}$d-annihilation at rest

> We study the $Δ$-excitation in $\overline{p}d$ annihilation at rest. The invariant spectra of the $π^+p$ and $π^-p$ systems selecting the protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c are analyzed. The calculations reproduces reasonably the experimental data.

# Delta production in $\bar{p}$d-annihilation at rest

## Key claim
The $\Delta^{++}$ resonance can be cleanly observed in the $\pi^+p$ invariant mass spectrum from $\bar{p}d$ annihilation at rest when protons with momenta above 400 MeV/c are selected, but the $\Delta^0$ resonance in the $\pi^-p$ system is too weak to be extracted from the strong combinatorial background under the same conditions.

## Method
The authors calculate the amplitude of a triangle diagram describing $\bar{p}N \rightarrow n\pi$ annihilation followed by $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$ scattering in the $\Delta$-isobar region. The annihilation probability is taken from a statistical model with pion multiplicity distributions, and the $\pi N$ scattering amplitude is computed using a resonance model with a $\pi N\Delta$ interaction Lagrangian, where the coupling constant and cut-off parameter are fitted to experimental $\pi^+p$ total cross sections. The deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential is adopted, and protons are selected with momenta above 400 MeV/c to match the experimental selection of the OBELIX collaboration.

## Key numbers
- The cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ in the form factor for the $\pi N\Delta$ vertex is fitted to 0.33 GeV.
- The coupling constant $g_{\pi N\Delta}^2/4\pi$ is determined as 0.83 (with the form factor included) or 0.38 (without the form factor), the latter comparable to the Bonn potential value of 0.35.
- The $\Delta^0$ excitation is most promisingly studied in the reaction $\bar{p}d \rightarrow p \pi^+ 2\pi^- \pi^0$, showing a narrow peak at $M(\pi^-p) \simeq 1.25$ GeV with a large ratio of the $\Delta^0$ amplitude to the combinatorial background.

## Context
This work extends earlier searches for $\Delta$ resonances in $\bar{p}d$ annihilation, where Kalogeropoulos et al. found no $\Delta$ influence in the $\pi N$ system, and Voronov and Kolybasov showed that the isobar structure is smeared out unless recoil nucleons are selected with momenta above 200 MeV/c. The authors use recent data from the OBELIX collaboration (Ableev et al.) and adopt the statistical model for annihilation from Hernandez et al. and the resonance model for $\pi N$ scattering from Tsushima et al. The results enable future experimental searches for the $\Delta^0$ resonance in specific final-state channels with reduced combinatorial background.
