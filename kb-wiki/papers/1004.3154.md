# Hyperfine mixing in electromagnetic decay of doubly heavy $bc$ baryons

> We investigate the role of hyperfine mixing in the electromagnetic decay of ground state doubly heavy $bc$ baryons. As in the case of a previous calculation on $b\to c$ semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons, we find large corrections to the electromagnetic decay widths due to this mixing. Contrary to the weak case just mentioned, we find here that one can not use electromagnetic width relations obtained in the infinite heavy quark mass limit to experimentally extract information on the admixtures in a model independent way.

## Key claim

Hyperfine mixing between $S_h=0$ and $S_h=1$ spin components of the heavy quark subsystem produces large corrections to electromagnetic decay widths of ground-state doubly heavy $bc$ baryons, but, unlike the previously studied semileptonic weak decays, the electromagnetic width relations derived in the infinite heavy quark mass limit cannot be used to experimentally extract the mixing admixtures in a model-independent way.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic quark model with explicit wavefunctions for the $b$, $c$, and light ($u$, $d$, $s$) quarks, evaluating the electromagnetic current matrix elements in the one-body approximation. They computed decay widths for $1/2 \to 1/2$ and $3/2 \to 1/2$ transitions using form-factor decompositions that satisfy current conservation, both for unmixed states with well-defined heavy-quark-subsystem spin $S_h$ and for the physical mixed states whose admixture coefficients were taken from their earlier work (Albertus et al., 2010). The calculation included contributions where the total spin of the heavy quark subsystem changes, which are forbidden in the infinite heavy quark mass limit.

- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^* \to \Xi_{bcu}^{(1)}\gamma) = 6.05 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^* \to \Xi_{bcd}^{(1)}\gamma) = 0.12 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^{(1)} \to \Xi_{bcu}^{(2)}\gamma) = 12.4 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^{(1)} \to \Xi_{bcd}^{(2)}\gamma) = 20.9 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Omega_{bc}^* \to \Omega_{bc}^{(1)}\gamma) = 0.31 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Omega_{bc}^{(1)} \to \Omega_{bc}^{(2)}\gamma) = 8.52 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^* \to \Xi_{bcu}^{(2)}\gamma) = 73.9 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^* \to \Xi_{bcd}^{(2)}\gamma) = 103 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- $\Gamma(\Omega_{bc}^* \to \Omega_{bc}^{(2)}\gamma) = 50.2 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV (full calculation, physical mixed state)
- For unmixed states: $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcu}^* \to \Xi_{bcu}\gamma) = 105 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV, $\Gamma(\Xi_{bcd}^* \to \Xi_{bcd}\gamma) = 50.5 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV, $\Gamma(\Omega_{bc}^* \to \Omega_{bc}\gamma) = 20.9 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of hyperfine mixing effects in $b \to c$ semileptonic decays of doubly heavy $bc$ baryons (Albertus et al., 2010) to the electromagnetic decay channel. It contradicts the expectation from Pervin et al. (2005) that heavy-quark spin symmetry relations might be useful for electromagnetic transitions, and it goes beyond the earlier calculation of electromagnetic decays of $bc$ baryons by Dai et al. (2000), which did not consider hyperfine mixing. The results show that experimental measurement of electromagnetic widths can reveal the presence of mixing, but, unlike the semileptonic case, cannot determine the mixing matrix without theoretical input; this motivates continued work on weak decays for model-independent extraction of mixing parameters.
