# RPA puzzle in C(12) weak decay processes

> We explain the origin of the difficulties that appear in a straightforward application of the QRPA in C(12), and we demonstrate that it is imperative to use the projected QRPA (PQRPA). Satisfactory results, not only for the weak processes among the ground states of the triad B(12),C(12),N(12), but also for the inclusive ones are obtained. We sketch as well a new formalism for the neutrino-nucleus interaction that furnishes very simple final formulae for the muon capture rate and neutrino induced cross sections.

## Key claim

The difficulties in applying the standard QRPA to weak decay processes in C(12) arise from a degeneracy of unperturbed quasiparticle energies caused by the BCS approximation in this N=Z nucleus; the problem is solved by using particle-number projected BCS (PBCS) within the projected QRPA (PQRPA), which yields satisfactory results without the rescaling factors needed in previous RPA approaches.

## Method

The authors performed PQRPA calculations for charge-exchange excitations in C(12), using a delta-force residual interaction within a configuration space spanning the 1s, 1p, 1d, 2s, 1f, and 2p shells. Single-particle energies were fixed from experimental energies of odd-mass nuclei C(11), B(11), C(13), and N(13) via the inverse-gap-equation method, and the particle-particle channel of the residual interaction was switched off entirely. Three different sets of particle-hole interaction parameters were tested, and results were compared with previous RPA, QRPA, and shell-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- The B(GT) value for beta decay from N(12) and B(12) to the C(12) ground state: experimental range 0.466–0.526; PQRPA calculation (III) gives 0.480
- Exclusive muon capture rate to the B(12) ground state: experimental 6.2 ± 0.3 × 10^3 s^-1; PQRPA (III) gives 6.5 × 10^3 s^-1
- Inclusive muon capture rate to all final states in B(12): experimental 38 ± 1 × 10^3 s^-1; PQRPA (III) gives 42 × 10^3 s^-1
- Exclusive flux-averaged electron neutrino cross section to N(12): experimental 9.1 ± 0.4 ± 0.9 × 10^-42 cm^2 (LSND 1997); PQRPA (III) gives 8.1 × 10^-42 cm^2
- Inclusive flux-averaged electron neutrino cross section: experimental 14.8 ± 0.7 ± 1.4 × 10^-42 cm^2 (LSND 1997); PQRPA (III) gives 17.4 × 10^-42 cm^2
- Exclusive flux-averaged muon neutrino cross section to N(12): experimental 0.66 ± 0.1 ± 0.1 × 10^-40 cm^2 (LSND 1997); PQRPA (III) gives 0.56 × 10^-40 cm^2
- Inclusive flux-averaged muon neutrino cross section: experimental 12.4 ± 0.3 ± 1.8 × 10^-40 cm^2 (LSND 1997); PQRPA (III) gives 13.4 × 10^-40 cm^2
- Energy of the 1+_1 state in N(12): experimental 17.34 MeV; PQRPA (III) gives 18.13 MeV

## Context

This work resolves the "RPA puzzle" identified by Kolbe (1994), Auerbach (1997), and Volpe (2000), where standard RPA and QRPA calculations overestimated exclusive weak decay rates in the C(12) triad by factors of 2–4, requiring ad hoc rescaling or partial occupancy of the p1/2 subshell. The authors show that particle-number projection within the PQRPA framework, previously developed by Krmpotic (1993), eliminates the spurious degeneracy of quasiparticle energies that plagued BCS-based approaches in this light N=Z nucleus. The results enable reliable calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino oscillation experiments with liquid scintillators, and the authors suggest the PQRPA approach should be investigated in other light N=Z and N approximately equal to Z nuclei.
