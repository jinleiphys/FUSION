# Continuous decoupling and freeze-out

> The decoupling and freeze-out of energetic nuclear collisions is analysed in terms of transparent semi-classical decoupling formulae. They provide a smooth transition and generalise frequently employed instantaneous freeze-out procedures. Simple relations between the damping width and the duration of the decoupling process are presented and the implications on various physical phenomena arising from the expansion and decay dynamics of the highly compressed hadronic matter generated in high energy nuclear collisions are discussed.

# Continuous decoupling and freeze-out

## Key claim

The paper argues that freeze-out in high-energy nuclear collisions is not an instantaneous process occurring on a sharp three-dimensional hypersurface, but rather a continuous decoupling that takes place over extended time intervals (typically 6–10 fm/c or more), with a robust "decoupling uncertainty relation" linking the damping width at peak emission to the decoupling duration.

## Method

The author derives semi-classical decoupling formulae from gradient-expanded Kadanoff-Baym equations, expressing the local emissivity as a product of the gain part of the collision term and an escape probability factor that depends on the optical depth along classical escape paths. The formalism is applied to a spherically expanding uniform fireball model, comparing chemical freeze-out (where inelastic processes dominate) with kinetic freeze-out (where elastic scattering dominates), using damping widths and collective expansion parameters to compute visibility probability distributions in time and temperature.

## Key numbers

- For a fireball with freeze-out radius R_dec ≈ 6 fm and collective velocity dR/dt = 0.5 fm/c, the decoupling peak occurs at t_max = 12 fm/c for both chemical and kinetic freeze-out.
- Damping widths at decoupling peak: Γ^chem_max = Γ^kin_max = 0.5 c/fm ≈ 100 MeV.
- Damping width values across the freeze-out process: Γ_i : Γ_max : Γ_f = 390 : 100 : 26 MeV (begin, peak, end).
- The damping width at peak and decoupling duration satisfy Γ(t_max) × Δt_dec ≈ e (the base of natural logarithms).
- The damping rate decreases by about a factor e^e ≈ 15 from the opaque interior to the outside.
- For 100 GeV Au + Au collisions: decoupling durations of 6–10 fm/c for phase transition, >5 fm/c for chemical freeze-out, >8 fm/c for kinetic freeze-out; corresponding volume growth factors >5, >4, and >6 respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier continuous freeze-out descriptions by the Brazilian group (Grassi 1994) and later efforts (Csernai 1997, Magas 1999, Molnar 2005) by providing micro-dynamical decoupling formulae based on classical kinetics, similar to the conceptual progress by Sinyukov (2002) and Akkelin (2008). The results contradict the extremely short decoupling durations inferred from earlier HBT interpretations (Adamova 2002), and instead provide a solution to the HBT puzzle by showing that long freeze-out durations (above 10 fm/c) are consistent with R_out/R_side close to unity as observed at CERN SPS and RHIC. The formalism enables reanalysis of nuclear collision data to map thermodynamic properties during freeze-out and to identify signatures of the equation of state or phase transition effects.
