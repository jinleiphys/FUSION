# Components of the dilepton continuum in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 2.76 $ TeV

> The dilepton invariant mass spectrum measured in heavy-ion collisions includes contributions from important QGP probes such as thermal radiation and the quarkonium ($J/ψ$, $ψ'$ and $Υ$) states. Dileptons coming from hard $q \bar q$ scattering, the Drell-Yan process, contribute in all mass regions. In heavy-ion colliders, such as the LHC, semileptonic decays of heavy flavor hadrons provide a substantial contribution to the dilepton continuum. The dilepton continuum can give quantitative information about heavy quark yields and their modification in the medium and thus it is important to know all different sources which populate the continuum. In the present study, we calculate $c \bar c$ and $b \bar b$ production and determine their contributions to the dilepton continuum in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}} = 2.76$ TeV with and without including heavy quark energy loss. We also calculate the rates for Drell-Yan and thermal dilepton production. The contributions to the continuum from these dilepton sources are studied in the kinematic ranges relevant for the LHC detectors. The relatively high $p_T$ cutoff for single leptons excludes most dileptons produced by the thermal medium. Heavy flavors are the dominant source of dilepton production in all the kinematic regimes except at forward rapidities where Drell-Yan start dominating in high mass range beyond 10 GeV/$c^2$.

## Key claim

Heavy flavor decays (charm and bottom) are the dominant source of dilepton production in all kinematic regimes for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, except at forward rapidities where Drell-Yan production becomes dominant for dilepton masses above 10 GeV/$c^2$, but only after heavy quark energy loss is included and with the caveat that the thermal dilepton contribution is essentially eliminated by typical single-lepton $p_T$ cuts.

## Method

The authors calculated next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD cross sections for $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$ production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV using CTEQ6M parton densities with EPS09 nuclear shadowing, then decayed the heavy quarks semileptonically to obtain correlated dilepton pairs. They also calculated Drell-Yan production to NLO and thermal dilepton production assuming a 1D Bjorken expansion with a first-order phase transition, and included heavy quark energy loss by modifying fragmentation functions to reproduce measured $R_{AA}$ values of $0.25-0.30$ for both charm and bottom.

## Key numbers

- Charm pair production cross section per nucleon: $\sigma_{\rm PbPb}^{c\bar{c}} = 1.76^{+2.32}_{-1.29}$ mb; bottom pair cross section: $\sigma_{\rm PbPb}^{b\bar{b}} = 89.3^{+42.7}_{-27.2}$ $\mu$b
- Number of $c\bar{c}$ pairs per Pb+Pb event: $N_{c\bar{c}} = 9.95^{+13.10}_{-7.30}$; number of $b\bar{b}$ pairs: $N_{b\bar{b}} = 0.50^{+0.25}_{-0.15}$
- Number of dimuon pairs per event from charm decays: $N_{\mu^+\mu^-}^{c\bar{c}} = 0.106^{+0.238}_{-0.078}$; from bottom decays: $N_{\mu^+\mu^-}^{b\bar{b}} = 0.0059^{+0.0029}_{-0.0017}$; from Drell-Yan ($1 \leq M \leq 100$ GeV): $N_{\mu^+\mu^-}^{DY} = 0.0004$
- Initial temperature extracted from charged particle multiplicity $dN_{\rm ch}/dy = 1600$: $T_i = 636$ MeV
- Critical temperature assumed: $T_c = 160$ MeV; initial time: $\tau_i = 0.1$ fm/$c$
- Total Pb+Pb cross section at 2.76 TeV: 7.65 b
- After energy loss, bottom quark distribution begins to dominate charm at $p_T \sim 10$ GeV/$c$ (instead of $\sim 20$ GeV/$c$ without energy loss)
- At forward rapidities ($2.5 \leq \eta^\mu \leq 4.0$), Drell-Yan exceeds heavy flavor decays for $M_{\mu^+\mu^-} > 10$ GeV/$c^2$
- With $p_T^\mu > 10$ GeV/$c$ and $|\eta^\mu| \leq 2.4$, Drell-Yan becomes the dominant continuum contribution

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Pb+Pb collisions at 5.5 TeV (Gavin et al., Lin et al., and Gallmeister et al.) to the actual LHC energy of 2.76 TeV, incorporating updated parton distribution functions, final detector acceptances, and heavy quark energy loss estimates consistent with LHC $R_{AA}$ measurements. The results provide a baseline for interpreting upcoming background-subtracted dilepton continuum measurements from CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE, enabling quantitative extraction of medium properties by comparing Pb+Pb yields to $pp$ collisions at the same energy.
