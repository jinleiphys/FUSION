# IRDFF-II: A New Neutron Metrology Library

> High quality nuclear data is the most fundamental underpinning for all neutron metrology applications. This paper describes the release of version II of the International Reactor Dosimetry and Fusion File (IRDFF-II) that contains a consistent set of nuclear data for fission and fusion neutron metrology applications up to 60~MeV neutron energy. The library is intended to support: a) applications in research reactors; b) safety and regulatory applications in the nuclear power generation in commercial fission reactors; and c) material damage studies in support of the research and development of advanced fusion concepts. The paper describes the contents of the library, documents the thorough verification process used in its preparation, and provides an extensive set of validation data gathered from a wide range of neutron benchmark fields. The new \mbox{IRDFF-II} library includes 119 metrology reactions, four cover material reactions to support self-shielding corrections, five metrology metrics used by the dosimetry community, and cumulative fission products yields for seven fission products in three different neutron energy regions. In support of characterizing the measurement of the residual nuclei from the dosimetry reactions and the fission product decay modes, the present document lists the recommended decay data, particle emission energies and probabilities for 68 activation products. It also includes neutron spectral characterization data for 29 neutron benchmark fields for the validation of the library contents. The IRDFF-II library and comprehensive documentation is available online at https://www-nds.iaea.org/IRDFF/. Evaluated cross sections can be compared with experimental data and other evaluations at https://www-nds.iaea.org/exfor/endf.htm. The new library is expected to become the international reference in neutron metrology for multiple applications.

# IRDFF-II: A New Neutron Metrology Library

## Key claim

The IRDFF-II library provides a consistent set of evaluated nuclear data for neutron metrology up to 60 MeV, but the validation results show that while most reactions achieve C/E ratios close to unity within uncertainties, several reactions (such as Sc45g-bare, Na23g-bare, Co59g-bare, and Ag109g-bare in the ACRR benchmark) exhibit C/E deviations exceeding 10%, indicating that not all evaluated cross sections are equally validated across all benchmark fields.

## Method

The authors compiled 119 metrology reactions, four cover material reactions, five dosimetry metrics, and cumulative fission product yields for seven fission products, extending the energy range to 60 MeV using TENDL libraries and least-squares evaluation methods based on Pade approximants. They performed verification through least-squares consistency analysis comparing calculated-to-experimental (C/E) reaction rate ratios across 29 neutron benchmark fields, including the ACRR central cavity and 252Cf spontaneous fission fields, with covariance information provided for each reaction.

## Key numbers

- The library contains 119 metrology reactions, four cover material reactions, five dosimetry metrics, and cumulative fission yields for seven fission products in three neutron energy regions
- Decay data, particle emission energies and probabilities are provided for 68 activation products
- Neutron spectral characterization data are included for 29 neutron benchmark fields for validation, with an additional six reference fields assessed but not used due to systematic discrepancies
- In the ACRR central cavity benchmark, the 58Ni(n,p) reaction shows C/E = 1.000 ± 4.53% with activation adjustment of 1.67% and spectrum uncertainty of 2.75%
- In the ACRR benchmark, 109Ag(n,g) bare shows C/E = 1.268 ± 16.04% with activation adjustment of 4.66% and spectrum uncertainty of 14.74%
- In the ACRR benchmark, 59Co(n,g) Cd-covered shows C/E = 1.305 ± 12.53% with activation adjustment of 4.26% and spectrum uncertainty of 11.53%
- In the 252Cf spontaneous fission benchmark, 197Au(n,g) shows C/E = 0.991 ± 1.79% with activation adjustment of -0.33% and spectrum uncertainty of 0.42%
- In the 252Cf benchmark, 235U(n,f) shows C/E = 1.013 ± 1.21% with activation adjustment of 1.94% and spectrum uncertainty of 0.07%
- In the 252Cf benchmark, 237Np(n,f) shows C/E = 1.000 ± 1.69% with activation adjustment of 0.66% and spectrum uncertainty of 0.23%
- In the 252Cf benchmark, 169Tm(n,3n) shows C/E = 0.795 ± 16.24% with activation adjustment of -0.33% and spectrum uncertainty of 14.75%

## Context

This work supersedes the previous IRDF-2002 and IRDFF-v1.05 libraries, extending the energy range to 60 MeV and adding new reactions such as 27Al(n,2n)26gAl, 28Si(n,p)28Al, and 209Bi(n,xn)(210-x)Bi to support specialized dosimetry applications including fusion material damage studies for IFMIF. The library benefits from the comprehensive TENDL libraries (TENDL-2015 and TENDL-2017) for extending evaluations to higher energies, and includes dosimetry metrics endorsed by national regulators and international standards organizations such as ASTM E693-17 for iron displacement-per-atom and ASTM E722-14 for silicon and GaAs displacement damage. The library enables consistent neutron metrology across research reactors, commercial fission reactor safety applications, and fusion material damage studies, with the expectation of becoming the international reference standard.
