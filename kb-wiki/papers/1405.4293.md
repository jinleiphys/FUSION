# Jet Shape Resummation Using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory

> The jet shape is a classic jet substructure observable that probes the average transverse energy profile inside a reconstructed jet. The studies of jet shapes in proton-proton collisions have served as precision tests of perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). They have also recently become the baseline for studying the in-medium modification of parton showers in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. The jet shape is a function of two angular parameters $R$ and $r$, which can be at hierarchical scales. Its calculation suffers from large logarithms of the ratio between the two scales, and these phase space logarithms can conveniently be resummed in the framework of soft-collinear effective theory (SCET). We find that, up to power corrections, the integral jet shape can be expressed in a factorized form which involves only the ratio between two jet energy functions. Resummation is performed at next-to-leading logarithmic order using renormalization-group evolution techniques. Comparisons to jet shape measurements at the LHC are presented to verify the dominant role of the collinear parton shower and to identify the kinematic region in which power-suppressed soft modes and non-perturbative effects may play a role.

## Key claim

The integral jet shape can be expressed, up to power corrections, in a factorized form involving only the ratio between two jet energy functions, with the soft radiation contribution being power-suppressed when a recoil-free jet axis is used. The resummation is performed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order, but the authors note that non-global logarithms affecting the α_s² ln² r/R terms are not included and would appear at NNLL.

## Method

The authors used soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to factorize the jet shape observable into hard, jet, and soft functions, with the jet shape reducing to a ratio of jet energy functions after cancellation of the hard and soft sectors. They calculated the one-loop jet energy functions for both quark and gluon jets reconstructed using the cone and anti-kT algorithms, then performed resummation of large logarithms ln(r/R) to NLL accuracy using renormalization-group evolution between two characteristic jet scales proportional to the angular parameters r and R.

## Key numbers

- The jet shape is defined as Ψ_J(r) = (sum of E_T within distance d < r) / (sum of E_T within distance d < R), with Ψ_J(R)=1 by definition
- For cone jets at O(α_s), the quark jet energy function contains a term (α_s C_F)/(2π) [1/2 ln²(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) - 3/2 ln(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) - 2 ln X ln(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) + ...]
- For cone jets at O(α_s), the gluon jet energy function contains a term (α_s)/(2π) [C_A/2 ln²(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) - (11/6 C_A - 2/3 T_F n_f) ln(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) - 2C_A ln X ln(ω² tan²(r/2)/μ²) + ...]
- Typical jet radii used at the LHC are R=0.5 to 0.7 for proton-proton collisions, and R=0.3 for heavy ion collisions at CMS

## Context

This work extends the jet shape calculations originally introduced by Ellis (1992) and later resummed using the modified leading logarithmic approximation by Seymour (1997), providing a systematically improvable effective field theory framework. The SCET-based approach contrasts with the perturbative QCD resummation framework of Li (2011, 2012) and enables future extension to heavy ion collisions using SCET with Glauber gluon interactions, which the authors state will be discussed in a forthcoming paper. The results serve as a precision baseline for studying in-medium modification of jet shapes in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, following the first CMS measurement of jet shape modification in Pb+Pb collisions by Chatrchyan (2013).
