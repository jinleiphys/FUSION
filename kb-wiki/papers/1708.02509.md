# Two-photon exchange correction to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen

> We reevaluate the Zemach, recoil and polarizability corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen expressing them through the low-energy proton structure constants and obtain the precise values of the Zemach radius and two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution. The uncertainty of TPE correction to S energy levels in muonic hydrogen of 105 ppm exceeds the ppm accuracy level of the forthcoming 1S hyperfine splitting measurements at PSI, J-PARC and RIKEN-RAL.

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen is reevaluated with an uncertainty of 105 ppm, which is two orders of magnitude larger than the ppm-level precision expected from forthcoming measurements at PSI, J-PARC, and RIKEN-RAL; the dominant uncertainty comes from the polarizability correction and the knowledge of the proton magnetic radius.

## Method
The Zemach, recoil, and polarizability corrections to the hyperfine splitting were separated and evaluated using a low-energy expansion in the region of small photon virtuality, expressing contributions through proton radii and moments of spin structure functions. The elastic proton form factors were taken from a global fit to unpolarized and polarization transfer world data (Bernauer 2013), and the spin-dependent inelastic structure functions g1 and g2 were taken from the JLab parametrization (Kuhn 2008, Griffioen, Sato 2016, Fersch 2017). The polarizability correction was also expressed directly in terms of measurable inclusive inelastic lepton-proton cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Total TPE correction to S-level hyperfine splitting in muonic hydrogen: -6122 ± 105 ppm (using muonic hydrogen charge radius and A1 magnetic radius)
- Total TPE correction: -6207 ± 127 ppm (using muonic hydrogen charge radius and the more conservative magnetic radius of Arrington 2015)
- Zemach correction: -7333 ± 48 ppm (muonic hydrogen charge radius, A1 magnetic radius)
- Recoil correction: 846.4 ± 6.2 ppm (muonic hydrogen charge radius, A1 magnetic radius)
- Polarizability correction: 364 ± 89 ppm
- Zemach radius: 1.0440 ± 0.0068 fm (muonic hydrogen charge radius, A1 magnetic radius)
- Zemach radius: 1.0557 ± 0.0120 fm (muonic hydrogen charge radius, Arrington 2015 magnetic radius)
- Electric charge radius from electron-proton scattering: 0.879 ± 0.008 fm (Bernauer 2013)
- Electric charge radius from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy: 0.84087 ± 0.00039 fm (Antognini 2013)
- Magnetic radius from A1 Collaboration: 0.799 ± 0.017 fm (Bernauer 2013)
- Magnetic radius from Arrington 2015: 0.844 ± 0.038 fm

## Context
This work extends dispersive estimates of the TPE correction by Carlson (2011) and Birse (2012) and the effective field theory approach of Peset (2016), providing updated values using the most recent form factor fits and spin structure function parametrizations. The results enable future 1S hyperfine splitting measurements at PSI, J-PARC, and RIKEN-RAL to constrain the low-Q^2 proton structure, particularly the combination of electric and magnetic radii and the derivative of the spin structure function moment I_1(0)'. The polarizability correction disagrees with the chiral perturbation theory result of Hagelstein (2017) due to large differences in the contribution from the first moment of g1.
