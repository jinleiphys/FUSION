# Chiral dynamics of the $Λ(1520)$ in coupled channels tested in the $K^-p\toππΛ$ reaction

> The $Λ(1520)$ resonance is generated dynamically in a unitary coupled channel framework with the $πΣ^*$ and $KΞ^*$ channels in s-wave and $πΣ$ and $\bar K N$ channels in d-wave. The dynamics of this resonance close to and above threshold is then tested through the reactions $K^-p\toππΛ$ and a good agreement with the experimentally measured cross section is observed.

## Key claim

The Λ(1520) resonance is dynamically generated from coupled-channel interactions involving πΣ* and KΞ* in s-wave and πΣ and K̅N in d-wave, but the model requires phenomenological d-wave transition potentials with several free parameters to reproduce the resonance shape, and the agreement with experimental cross sections is only "fairly well" close to and slightly above the resonance region.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation using a kernel matrix V that combines chiral Lagrangian terms for the s-wave πΣ* and KΞ* channels with phenomenological d-wave potentials for the πΣ and K̅N channels, where the d-wave vertices are proportional to the square of the incoming and outgoing momenta. The unknown parameters (five γ coefficients and two subtraction constants) are fitted to experimental partial-wave amplitudes for K̅N → K̅N and K̅N → πΣ in d-wave and isospin I=0 from Gopal (1976) and Alston-Garnjost (1977). The resulting unitarized amplitudes are then used to predict the cross sections for K⁻p → π⁰π⁰Λ and K⁻p → π⁺π⁻Λ.

## Key numbers

- Subtraction constants: a₀ = -1.8 for s-wave channels, a₂ = -8.1 for d-wave channels
- γ₁₃ = 0.98 × 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³, γ₁₄ = 1.1 × 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³ for the K̅N and πΣ couplings to s-wave channels
- γ₃₃ = -1.7 × 10⁻¹² MeV⁻⁵, γ₄₄ = -0.7 × 10⁻¹² MeV⁻⁵, γ₃₄ = -1.1 × 10⁻¹² MeV⁻⁵ for the d-wave diagonal and transition potentials
- Couplings of Λ(1520) to channels: g₁(πΣ*) = 0.91, g₂(KΞ*) = -0.29, g₃(K̅N) = -0.54, g₄(πΣ) = -0.45
- Pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV, with f = 1.15 fπ used in the chiral coefficients

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation of 3/2⁻ resonances from chiral unitary approaches by Sarkar (2004) and Lutz, which successfully produced resonances like N*(1520), Δ(1700), and Λ(1520) but gave a width much larger than the physical one. The novelty is the inclusion of the πΣ and K̅N channels in d-wave, which are essential to reproduce the Λ(1520) width since its dominant decays go through these channels. The predicted amplitudes involving πΣ* are tested against experimental data for K⁻p → ππΛ from Prakhov (2004) and Mast (1973), enabling future checks of the dynamical generation mechanism in other reactions where the πΣ* channel plays a role.
