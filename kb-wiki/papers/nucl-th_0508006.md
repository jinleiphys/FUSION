# Recursive renormalization of the singlet one-pion-exchange plus point-like interactions

> The subtracted kernel approach is shown to be a powerful method to be implemented recursively in scattering equations with regular plus point-like interactions. The advantages of the method allows one to recursively renormalize the potentials, with higher derivatives of the Dirac-delta, improving previous results. The applicability of the method is verified in the calculation of the $^1S_0$ nucleon-nucleon phase-shifts, when considering a potential with one-pion-exchange plus a contact interaction and its derivatives. The $^1S_0$ renormalization parameters are fitted to the data. The method can in principle be extended to any derivative order of the contact interaction, to higher partial waves and to coupled channels.

## Key claim

The subtracted kernel approach can be recursively applied to renormalize the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the \(^1S_0\) channel when the potential includes one-pion-exchange plus a contact interaction and its derivatives, but the fit to phase-shift data up to laboratory momentum \(\sim 300\) MeV/c requires at least a three-times subtracted scattering equation and the inclusion of derivative contact terms beyond the leading Dirac-delta.

## Method

The authors start from the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) plus a Dirac-delta interaction, for which a one-subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation suffices. They then recursively construct a three-fold subtracted scattering equation, introducing higher-order derivative contact terms (up to \(p'^2 p^2\)) in the driving term at the subtraction scale. The renormalized strengths \(\lambda_{\mathcal{R}00}\), \(\lambda_{\mathcal{R}10}\), and \(\lambda_{\mathcal{R}11}\) and the subtraction point \(\mu\) are fitted to reproduce the Nijmegen \(^1S_0\) phase-shift data and the singlet scattering length.

## Key numbers

* Singlet scattering length \(a_s = -23.739\) fm is used as the input constraint for fitting \(\lambda_{\mathcal{R}00}\).
* Best-fit subtraction point \(\mu = 214\) MeV/c, corresponding to a negative subtraction energy of approximately \(-50\) MeV.
* Effective range obtained from the fit is \(r_{0,s} = 2.73\) fm, compared to the Nijmegen value of 2.68 fm.
* Dimensionless renormalized strengths for the full \(\pi + \delta + \delta'\) interaction: \(\mu \lambda_{\mathcal{R}00} = -0.1465\), \(\mu^3 \lambda_{\mathcal{R}01} = 4.7124\), \(\mu^5 \lambda_{\mathcal{R}11} = 5.0265\), yielding \(a_s = -23.739\) fm.
* For comparison, the OPEP-only interaction gives \(a_s = -0.878\) fm; OPEP plus Dirac-delta gives \(a_s = -1.250\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the subtracted kernel renormalization method previously applied to the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel by Frederico (1999) to the \(^1S_0\) channel, where leading-order OPEP plus Dirac-delta alone proved insufficient. It contrasts with dimensional regularization approaches by Nieves (2000) and boundary condition regularization by Arriola (2000), offering a recursive subtraction scheme that can be systematically extended to higher derivative orders, higher partial waves, and coupled channels. The method enables future inclusion of two-pion-exchange interactions and application to three-body systems with contact interactions.
