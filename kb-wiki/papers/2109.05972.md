# Coalescence, the thermal model and multi-fragmentation: The energy and volume dependence of light nuclei production in heavy ion collisions

> We present results of a phase space coalescence approach within the UrQMD transport and -hybrid model for a very wide range of beam energies from SIS to LHC. The coalescence model is able to qualitatively describe the whole range of experimental data with a fixed set of parameters. Some systematic deviations are observed for very low beam energies where the role of feed down from heavier nuclei and multi-fragmentation becomes relevant. The coalescence results are mostly very close to the thermal model fits. However, both the coalescence approach as well as thermal fits are struggling to simultaneously describe the triton multiplicities measured with the STAR and ALICE experiment. The double ratio of $tp/d^2$, in the coalescence approach, is found to be essentially energy and centrality independent for collisions of heavy nuclei at beam energies of $\mathrm{E_{lab}}> 10 A$ GeV. On the other hand the clear scaling of the $d/p^2$ and $t/p^3$ ratios with the systems volume is broken for peripheral collisions, where a canonical treatment and finite size effects become more important.

## Key claim
The phase-space coalescence approach within the UrQMD transport and hybrid model can qualitatively describe light nuclei production across a very wide range of beam energies from SIS to LHC with a fixed set of parameters, but both the coalescence approach and thermal model fits struggle to simultaneously describe the triton multiplicities measured by the STAR and ALICE experiments.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD model in both default cascade mode and hybrid hydro-mode to simulate heavy ion collisions, extracting nucleon phase-space distributions at kinetic freeze-out. Light nuclei were formed via phase-space coalescence using a box profile in relative momentum and coordinate space, with coalescence parameters fixed to data from Au+Au collisions at 10.8 A GeV and Si+Pb collisions at 14.6 A GeV. For tritons, a two-step coalescence procedure was employed where a pre-deuteron first forms from a nucleon pair, then a third nucleon coalesces with that pair if the relative distance and momentum criteria are satisfied.

## Key numbers
- Coalescence parameters for deuterons: Δr_max = 3.575 fm, Δp_max = 0.285 GeV
- Coalescence parameters for tritons (Set I): Δr_max = 4.1 fm, Δp_max = 0.32 GeV
- Coalescence parameters for tritons (Set II): Δr_max = 4.3 fm, Δp_max = 0.35 GeV
- Spin-isospin correction factor for deuteron formation: 3/8
- Spin-isospin correction factor for triton formation: 1/12
- The double ratio tp/d² is essentially energy and centrality independent for collisions of heavy nuclei at beam energies of E_lab > 10 A GeV
- The transition from fluid to transport description in the hybrid model occurs on an iso-energy-density hypersurface at ε = 3ε₀, where ε₀ ≈ 145 MeV/fm³

## Context
This work extends the phase-space coalescence approach previously applied to deuterons and anti-deuterons by Sombun et al. (2018) to tritons and helium-3 production, and compares results with the thermal model baseline from Vovchenko et al. (2020) and the "hot coalescence" multifragmentation approach by Botvina et al. (2020). The study provides a baseline for understanding light nuclei production without explicit baryon correlations, enabling future investigations of how a phase transition or critical point might affect cluster yields, and demonstrates that volume scaling of d/p² and t/p³ ratios breaks for peripheral collisions where canonical treatment and finite size effects become important.
