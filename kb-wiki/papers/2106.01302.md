# Singular Value Decomposition and Similarity Renormalization Group Evolution of Nuclear Interactions

> One of the main challenges for ab initio nuclear many-body theory is the growth of computational and storage costs as calculations are extended to heavy, exotic, and structurally complex nuclei. Here, we investigate the factorization of nuclear interactions as a means to address this issue. We perform Singular Value Decompositions of nucleon-nucleon interactions in partial wave representation and study the dependence of the singular value spectrum on interaction characteristics like regularization scheme and resolution scales. We develop and implement the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of the factorized interaction, and demonstrate that this SVD-SRG approach accurately preserves two-nucleon observables. We find that low-resolution interactions allow the truncation of the SVD at low rank, and that a small number of relevant components is sufficient to capture the nuclear interaction and perform an accurate SRG evolution, while the Coulomb interaction requires special consideration. The rank is uniform across all partial waves, and almost independent of the basis choice in the tested cases. This suggests an interpretation of the relevant singular components as mere representations of a small set of abstract operators that can describe the interaction and its SRG flow. Following the traditional workflow for nuclear interactions, we discuss how the transformation between the center-of-mass and laboratory frames creates redundant copies of the partial wave components when implemented in matrix representation, and we discuss strategies for mitigation. Finally, we test the low-rank approximation to the SRG-evolved interactions in many-body calculations using the In-Medium SRG. By including nuclear radii in our analysis, we verify that the implementation of the SRG using the singular vectors of the interaction does not spoil the evolution of other observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon-nucleon interactions have a low-rank structure that can be exploited via Singular Value Decomposition, and that the Similarity Renormalization Group evolution can be performed directly on the SVD factors while accurately preserving two-nucleon observables, provided the Coulomb interaction is treated with special care.

## Method

The authors performed Singular Value Decompositions of nucleon-nucleon interactions in partial wave representation, studying the singular value spectra for different regularization schemes and resolution scales. They developed and implemented the SRG evolution of the factorized interaction by evolving the singular vectors via flow equations while keeping the singular values constant, with the kinetic energy contributions absorbed into a time-dependent shift. The low-rank approximations were tested in In-Medium SRG many-body calculations for energies and radii.

## Key numbers

- Singular value spectra for the chiral N3LO interaction (EM, cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV/c) show exponential decay, with a natural threshold around sigma_t approximately 10^{-3} fm^{-2} for the proton-proton 1S0 partial wave
- Two equidistant momentum meshes with q_Max = 7 fm^{-1} and spacings Delta q = 0.05 fm^{-1} and 0.1 fm^{-1} produce practically identical singular value spectra for sigma_i >= 10^{-10} fm^{-2}
- Reducing input precision from 10 digits to 6 digits after the decimal produces an elongated tail of unphysical singular values of order sigma_i approximately 10^{-6} fm^{-2}
- The rank is uniform across all partial waves and almost independent of the basis choice in the tested cases

## Context

This work initiates a program for principal component analysis of modern nuclear interactions, extending the use of factorization methods that have been successful in quantum chemistry (Hohenstein 2012, Parrish 2012, Schutski 2017). The approach addresses the computational and storage cost growth in ab initio nuclear many-body methods as calculations are extended to heavier nuclei, and sets the stage for applying the same SVD-SRG techniques to three-nucleon forces. The low-rank factorization could enable efficient rank-reduced calculations for medium-mass and heavy nuclei, following the lines of Tichai (2019).
