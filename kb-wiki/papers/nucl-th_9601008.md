# Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics of wave packets with stochastic incorporation of Vlasov equation

> On the basis of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) of wave packets for the quantum system, a novel model (called AMD-V) is constructed by the stochastic incorporation of the diffusion and the deformation of wave packets which is calculated by Vlasov equation without any restriction on the one-body distribution. In other words, the stochastic branching process in molecular dynamics is formulated so that the instantaneous time evolution of the averaged one-body distribution is essentially equivalent to the solution of Vlasov equation. Furthermore, as usual molecular dynamics, AMD-V keeps the many-body correlation and can naturally describe the fluctuation among many channels of the reaction. It is demonstrated that the newly introduced process of AMD-V has drastic effects in heavy ion collisions of 40Ca + 40Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon, especially on the fragmentation mechanism, and AMD-V reproduces the fragmentation data very well. Discussions are given on the interrelation among the frameworks of AMD, AMD-V and other microscopic models developed for the nuclear dynamics.

# Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics of wave packets with stochastic incorporation of Vlasov equation

## Key claim

The authors construct a novel model (AMD-V) by stochastically incorporating the diffusion and deformation of wave packets calculated from the Vlasov equation into antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), such that the instantaneous time evolution of the averaged one-body distribution is essentially equivalent to the Vlasov solution, while the model retains the many-body correlation and channel fluctuation that one-body transport models lack. The stochastic branching process is formulated under the restriction that only diffusing (not shrinking) components of the wave packet are treated, and the energy conservation is recovered by adjusting other degrees of freedom in the nucleus.

## Method

The authors start from the standard AMD framework, where the nuclear system is described by a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets with fixed width parameter ν = 0.16 fm⁻², and the centroids evolve via the time-dependent variational principle plus stochastic two-nucleon collisions. In AMD-V, for each nucleon during a short time step δt, the diffusion of the wave packet predicted by the Vlasov equation is implemented by giving a stochastic displacement ξ to the centroid with probability c = 4s²δt, where s² is derived from the positive eigenvalues of the second-moment rate tensor σ̇²_ab(t₀) computed via Monte Carlo integration. The displacement is applied to the relative coordinate within the nucleus containing that nucleon, and the total energy is restored by solving a constrained cooling/heating equation that keeps collective coordinates fixed. Displacements that lead to Pauli-forbidden states or impossible energy recovery are rejected.

## Key numbers

- Width parameter ν = 0.16 fm⁻² for the Gaussian wave packets.
- Incident energy: 35 MeV/nucleon for the ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca system.
- Impact parameter region investigated: b < 7 fm.
- Dynamical simulations continued until t = 300 fm/c.
- Experimental α particle multiplicity M_α = 5.2; proton multiplicity M_p = 6.2.
- Incident momenta of projectile and target correspond to p_z/A = ±128 MeV/c.
- Excitation energy limit for lighter daughter nucleus in statistical decay: E* < 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework of Ono and Horiuchi by addressing the problem that AMD cannot describe the splitting of wave packets, which is essential for processes like nucleon emission and multifragmentation. The authors had previously shown (Ono, 1995) that the nucleon emission problem could be solved by stochastic splitting based on momentum width at the nuclear surface. AMD-V generalizes this by incorporating the full Vlasov equation diffusion, removing the restriction on the one-body distribution. The paper contrasts AMD-V with time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) and Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) one-body transport models, which cannot treat channel fluctuation, and with fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) of Feldmeier, which treats wave packet widths as dynamical variables but does not allow independent propagation of branched components. The ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca system at 35 MeV/nucleon was previously analyzed by Hagel (1994) with quantum molecular dynamics (QMD), the statistical sequential decay model GEMINI, and the simultaneous multifragmentation model of Gross, none of which reproduced the α particle multiplicity. AMD-V enables consistent dynamical description of the whole reaction without requiring early truncation and connection to a statistical decay model.
