# Medium modifications of the nucleon--deuteron break--up cross section in the Faddeev approach

> The three--nucleon scattering problem in a nuclear medium is considered within the Faddeev technique. In particular the deuteron break--up cross section that governs the formation and the break--up reactions of deuterons (NNN <-> Nd) in a nuclear environment is calculated at finite temperatures and densities. A significant enhancement of the in--medium break--up cross section with increasing density has been found.

## Key claim

The deuteron break-up cross section in a nuclear medium is significantly enhanced with increasing density, with the maximum enhanced by one order of magnitude for the largest density considered, but this result is obtained using a separable representation of the nucleon-nucleon potential restricted to the dominant $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1-{^3D}_1$ channels, and the authors note that agreement with experimental data on free scattering can still be improved through using more realistic potentials and including higher partial waves.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations for nucleon-deuteron scattering, modified to include medium effects through a mean-field approximation with effective mass and Pauli blocking factors. The two-body interaction was represented by a separable potential parameterized by Phillips (1968) with parameters from Bruinsma and van Wageningen (1977), restricted to the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1-{^3D}_1$ channels. Medium effects were incorporated by replacing the nucleon mass with an effective mass $m^*$ and multiplying the form factors by the square root of the angular-averaged Pauli blocking factor $\langle Q \rangle^{1/2}$, with the break-up cross section calculated via the optical theorem.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass ratio decreases to $m^*/m = 0.97$ at temperature $T=10$ MeV for the Mott density of the deuteron $n = 8 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The Mott transition for deuteron dissociation occurs at density $n = 8 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The break-up cross section was calculated for densities $n = 0.1, 1, 3, 5, 7 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$ as a function of laboratory energy $E_{lab}$.
- The maximum enhancement of the break-up cross section is one order of magnitude for the largest density value considered ($7 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$).
- At 100 MeV laboratory energy, the deviation of the in-medium cross section from the free cross section is on the order of 10%.
- The free-space calculations of the dominant three-body transition matrix elements agree within approximately 5% with previous calculations by Doleschall (1973).

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS formalism for three-nucleon scattering, previously applied to vacuum problems by Glockle (1983) and others, to include nuclear medium effects relevant for heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies ($E/A \le 200$ MeV). The study addresses the regime where the impulse approximation used by Danielewicz and Bertsch (1991) for higher energies fails, and provides medium-modified cross sections that can be incorporated into kinetic approaches to cluster formation, such as those by Tsang et al. (1989), Xu et al. (1990), and Ropke and Schulz (1988), to improve descriptions of deuteron production in heavy-ion reactions.
