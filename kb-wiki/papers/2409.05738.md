# Exclusive vector-quarkonium photoproduction at NLO in alpha_s in collinear factorisation with evolution of the generalised parton distributions and high-energy resummation

> We perform the first complete one-loop study of exclusive photoproduction of vector quarkonia off protons in Collinear Factorisation (CF) including the scale evolution of the Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs). We confirm the perturbative instability of the cross section at high photon-proton-collision energies (W_gamma+p) at Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) in alpha_s and solve this issue by resumming higher-order QCD corrections, which are enhanced by a logarithm of the parton energies, using High-Energy Factorisation (HEF) in the Doubly-Logarithmic Approximation (DLA) matched to CF. Our NLO CF + DLA HEF results are in agreement with the latest HERA data, show a smaller sensitivity to the factorisation and renormalisation scales compared to Born-order results. Quark-induced channels via interference with gluon ones are found to contribute at most 20% of the cross section for W_gamma+p > 100 GeV. Our results also show that such exclusive cross sections cannot be accurately obtained from the square of usual Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs) and clearly illustrate the importance of quarkonium exclusive photoproduction to advance our understanding of the 3D content of the nucleon in terms of gluons. Our work provides an important step towards a correct interpretation of present and future experimental data collected at HERA, the EIC, the LHC and future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the NLO cross section for exclusive vector-quarkonium photoproduction in collinear factorisation is perturbatively unstable at high photon-proton collision energies due to large logarithms, and resolves this by resumming higher-order QCD corrections using the Doubly-Logarithmic Approximation (DLA) within High-Energy Factorisation (HEF) matched to the NLO collinear factorisation result.

## Method

The authors compute the exclusive photoproduction amplitude for J/psi and Upsilon at NLO in alpha_s within collinear factorisation, including the full leading-logarithmic scale evolution of generalised parton distributions (GPDs) using the Goloskokov-Kroll double-distribution model with CT18NLO PDFs as forward inputs. They then resum large logarithms of the parton energy (ln(1/xi)) that appear at small xi (high W_gamma+p) using the DLA within HEF, and match the resummed coefficient functions to the fixed-order NLO result via a subtractive matching prescription.

## Key numbers

- Quark-induced channels contribute at most 20% of the cross section for W_gamma+p > 100 GeV
- For J/psi, the radial wavefunction at the origin R_J/psi(0) = 1 GeV^(3/2); for Upsilon, R_Upsilon(0) = 3 GeV^(3/2)
- The heavy-quark mass m_Q is chosen as M_Q/2, with a two-loop running of alpha_s using alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118
- The GPD initial condition is set at mu_0 = 2 GeV
- The NLO coefficient functions at small xi scale as -i*pi*c*|x|/(2*xi) * (alpha_s/pi) * ln(m_Q^2/mu_F^2) times {C_A, 2C_F} for gluon and quark channels respectively

## Context

This work extends the NLO collinear factorisation study of exclusive quarkonium photoproduction by Ivanov et al. (2004) by including full GPD evolution and performing the first complete one-loop study with such evolution. It addresses the perturbative instability at high energies that was already observed in that earlier work, and solves it via high-energy resummation. The results enable reliable predictions for future experiments at the EIC, LHC ultra-peripheral collisions, and potential future facilities, and demonstrate that exclusive cross sections cannot be accurately obtained from the square of usual PDFs, highlighting the importance of these processes for understanding the 3D gluon structure of the nucleon.
