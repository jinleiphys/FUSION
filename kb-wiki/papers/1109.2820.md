# BCS and BEC p-wave pairing in Bose-Fermi gases

> The pairing of fermionic atoms in a mixture of atomic fermion and boson gases at zero temperature is investigated. The attractive interaction between fermions, that can be induced by density fluctuations of the bosonic background, can give rise to a superfluid phase in the Fermi component of the mixture. The atoms of both species are assumed to be in only one internal state, so that the pairing of fermions is effective only in odd-l channels. No assumption about the value of the ratio between the Fermi velocity and the sound velocity in the Bose gas is made in the derivation of the energy gap equation. The gap equation is solved without any particular "ansatz" for the pairing field or the effective interaction. The p-wave superfluidity is studied in detail. By increasing the strength and/or decreasing the range of the effective interaction a transition of the fermion pairing regime, from the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer state to a system of tightly bound couples can be realized. These composite bosons behave as a weakly-interacting Bose-Einstein condensate.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a Bose-Fermi mixture at zero temperature, the fermion pairing regime can transition from a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) state to a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) of tightly bound fermion pairs by increasing the strength or decreasing the range of the phonon-mediated effective interaction, without assuming any particular ratio between Fermi velocity and sound velocity in the Bose gas.

## Method

The authors derived a gap equation for p-wave pairing in the fermionic component of a Bose-Fermi mixture, using the induced interaction from one-phonon exchange between fermions via the bosonic background. They solved the gap equation numerically for a mixture of 87Rb bosons and 40K fermions, expressing results in terms of two dimensionless parameters: b (related to interaction strength) and c (related to interaction range). The static limit was taken for the pairing field, and only the l=1 component was retained after verifying that higher odd-l components were negligible.

## Key numbers

- For b=5 and c=2, the energy gap is very small, indicating the system is well inside the BCS phase.
- The peak of the gap function Δ₁(k) moves from the Fermi surface to values in the range k_F to 4k_F as interaction effectiveness increases.
- The difference μ*_F - E_F approaches 0.02 ε_F for c=0.35 (with b=5) and 0.06 ε_F for b=60 (with c=2).
- The root mean square radius of the dimers approaches approximately 0.2 r₀ for c=0.35 (with b=5) and approximately 0.3 r₀ for b=60 (with c=2), where r₀ is the average fermion spacing.
- The product a_d³ n_d (where a_d is the dimer scattering length and n_d is dimer density) approaches 1.7×10⁻³ for c=0.35 (with b=5) and 1.5×10⁻² for b=60 (with c=2).
- The ratio v_F/c_S lies in the range 1.2 to 9 for the parameter values studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Matera (2003), which assumed the sound velocity c_S was much smaller than the Fermi velocity v_F, and by Suzuki (2008), which assumed the opposite limit c_S much larger than v_F. The authors abandon both approximations to study the BCS-BEC transition region where neither limit holds. The results enable quantitative predictions for p-wave superfluidity in Bose-Fermi mixtures, particularly for the 87Rb-40K system, and suggest that the composite bosons in the BEC phase form a weakly interacting condensate with repulsive interactions, consistent with few-body studies by Jona-Lasinio (2008) and D'Incao (2008).
