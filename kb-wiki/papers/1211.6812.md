# Simulation of heavy ion collision using a time-dependent density functional theory including nuclear superfluidity

> We carried out a simulation of heavy ion collision using a time-dependent density functional theory. We call it the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) which can describe nuclear dynamics in three-dimensional coordinate space, treating nuclear pairing correlation. We simulate 20O+20O collision using the Cb-TDHFB with a contact-type pairing functional, and show the behavior of gap energy which is decreasing and vibrating while colliding.

# Simulation of heavy ion collision using a time-dependent density functional theory including nuclear superfluidity

## Key claim
The canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) can simulate heavy ion collisions in three-dimensional coordinate space while treating nuclear pairing correlation, and the gap energy decreases and vibrates during the collision. The abstract glosses over that this is demonstrated only for a single symmetric system (20O+20O) at one incident energy and one impact parameter, using a contact-type pairing functional with a BCS-like approximation rather than the full TDHFB.

## Method
The authors derived the Cb-TDHFB equations from the full TDHFB equations by representing them in the canonical basis (which diagonalizes the density matrix) with a BCS-like approximation for the pairing functional (pp, hh-channel). They prepared HF+BCS ground states for projectile and target at an initial distance of 12 fm with impact parameter b=0 fm, boosted the wave functions, and time-evolved the nuclear densities in a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate-space box of 30×30×50 fm with 1.0 fm mesh spacing, using the Skyrme SkM* effective interaction with a contact-type pairing functional.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy: E_int = 40 MeV for the 20O+20O collision, higher than the Coulomb barrier for this system.
- Initial distance between projectile and target: 12 fm with impact parameter b = 0 fm.
- Average gap energy of 20O (Z=8, N=12) before collision: 1.901 MeV.
- Number of canonical basis states used: 24 (1.2 times that of TDHF).
- Coordinate space box dimensions: x- and y-sides = 30 fm, z-side = 50 fm; mesh spacing Δx = Δy = Δz = 1.0 fm.
- Touching configuration occurs at t = 98.66 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach (KD77) by including pairing correlations, which TDHF cannot treat. It builds on the authors' earlier development of Cb-TDHFB (EN10), which was previously validated against quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) in linear response calculations. The decrease in gap energies during collision is similar to results from an older work (CM80). The method enables future studies of pairing effects in heavy ion collisions and the relation to excited states of the compound nucleus (40S), with planned parallelization using MPI for improved computational efficiency.
