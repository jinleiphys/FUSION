# S-wave kaon condensation in neutron-star matter within a chiral model framework with dynamical meson masses

> We investigate s-wave kaon condensation in dense matter and neutron stars within the updated Chiral Mean Field model with an improved meson description (mCMF), which incorporates dynamically generated in-medium meson masses arising from explicit chiral symmetry breaking and vector-meson self-interactions. In contrast to conventional relativistic mean-field descriptions with constant meson masses, the mCMF framework introduces a self-consistent feedback between the meson sector and the dense-matter equations of motion. The kaon dispersion relation is derived from the nonlinear SU(3) Lagrangian, including the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction and additional baryon-pseudoscalar couplings, and the onset of condensation is determined under conditions of charge neutrality and $β$ equilibrium. Our calculations include the full baryon octet together with electrons and muons at zero temperature. We analyze the impact of hyperons, muons, and kaon condensation on the equation of state, on neutron-star mass--radius relations, and neutron-star thermal evolution, and examine the sensitivity of the onset density and stellar properties to variations in the nucleon--kaon scattering length and to different model vector parameters and vector self-interactions. We find that $K^{-}$ condensation sets in between $n \sim (2-8)\, n_0$ (in units of nuclear saturation density) and leads to a moderate to strong softening (in one case, a slight stiffening of the equation of state), depending on the interplay of kaons and hyperons, while remaining compatible with current $2\,M_\odot$ and small-radius neutron-star observational constraints and producing distinguishable behavior in the neutron-star cooling. This work provides an improved and thermodynamically consistent framework for studying exotic degrees of freedom in neutron-star matter.

## Key claim

S-wave $K^{-}$ condensation in neutron-star matter sets in between approximately 2 and 8 times nuclear saturation density within the mCMF framework, but the onset density and the degree of softening or stiffening of the equation of state depend sensitively on the interplay between kaons and hyperons, on the nucleon-kaon scattering length, and on the choice of vector parameters and vector self-interactions.

## Method

The authors extended the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model to include dynamically generated in-medium meson masses (mCMF) arising from explicit chiral symmetry breaking and vector-meson self-interactions, then derived the kaon dispersion relation from a nonlinear SU(3) Lagrangian that includes the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, the first range term, and baryon-pseudoscalar derivative couplings characterized by parameters $d_1$ and $d_2$. They solved the mean-field equations self-consistently including the kaon condensate under conditions of charge neutrality and $\beta$ equilibrium at zero temperature, incorporating the full baryon octet together with electrons and muons, and used the resulting equation of state to compute neutron-star mass-radius relations and thermal evolution.

## Key numbers

- $K^{-}$ condensation onset occurs between $n \sim (2-8)\, n_0$ (in units of nuclear saturation density $n_0$).
- The kaon decay constant used is $f_K = 122$ MeV.
- The vacuum kaon mass is $m^2_{K}=498$ MeV.
- For the reference parameter set, the kaon-nucleon scattering lengths are $a_{KN}(I=0)=-0.09$ fm and $a_{KN}(I=1)=-0.31$ fm, yielding $d_1=2.30/m_K$ and $d_2=0.71/m_K$.
- The optical potential for $K^{-}$ in isospin-symmetric matter at saturation is $U_{K^-}=-39.27$ MeV for the reference $d_1,d_2$ and $U_{K^-}=-219.19$ MeV when using $9d_1,9d_2$.
- The model remains compatible with current $2\,M_\odot$ neutron-star observational constraints.

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Mean Field model of Cruz-Camacho (2024) and Kumar (2024) by incorporating s-wave kaon condensation with dynamically generated in-medium meson masses, going beyond earlier treatments that used constant meson masses in relativistic mean-field descriptions. It builds on the pioneering work of Kaplan and Nelson (1986, 1987) on kaon condensation and subsequent studies by Thorsson (1993), Glendenning (1997), and others that examined hyperon-kaon competition, but provides a thermodynamically consistent framework where the meson sector feeds back into the dense-matter equations of motion. The results enable future comparisons with neutron-star cooling observations to distinguish stars with kaon condensation in their interiors, following the initial investigation by Page (1990).
