# Symmetry energy for fragmentation in dynamical nuclear collisions

> We extract values for the free symmetry energy as a function of the fragment size (the proton number Z) from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations of calcium collisions. Simple statistical physics describe well the distribution of hot nuclei at breakup, provided the surface symmetry term in the free energy is much smaller at high excitation than in ground state nuclei. This result may reflect the condition of low density and finite temperature when these systems disassemble.

## Key claim

The free symmetry energy extracted from AMD simulations of calcium collisions shows almost no surface contribution, implying that the surface symmetry term is much smaller at high excitation than in ground state nuclei, which may reflect the low density and finite temperature conditions at breakup.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations of central collisions of 40Ca+40Ca, 48Ca+48Ca, 60Ca+60Ca, and 46Fe+46Fe at E/A=35 MeV, using the Gogny effective force. Fragment yields from the four reaction systems were combined using the isoscaling relation to extend the range of neutron and proton numbers, and the coefficient ζ(Z) in a quadratic fit to the yield function K(N,Z) was extracted as the ratio of the symmetry free energy to the temperature.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: E/A = 35 MeV for all four collision systems (40Ca+40Ca, 48Ca+48Ca, 60Ca+60Ca, 46Fe+46Fe)
- Number of simulated events: 1040 (40Ca+40Ca), 949 (48Ca+48Ca), 978 (60Ca+60Ca), and 1400 (46Fe+46Fe)
- Ground state symmetry energy coefficients from AMD binding energies for 7 ≤ A ≤ 40: c_v = 30.9 MeV and c_s = -35.2 MeV
- Temperature extracted from the same reaction systems: T ≈ 3.4 MeV
- Density extracted from the same reaction systems: ρ ≈ 0.08 fm⁻³
- Volume symmetry energy for infinite nuclear matter at saturation density with Gogny force: C_sym(ρ₀) = 30.7 MeV
- The surface-to-volume ratio k for the symmetry energy of ground state nuclei is k = -c_s/c_v = 1.14; the extracted ζ(Z) shows a reduced surface effect between k = 0 and k = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study (Ono et al., 2004) which showed that isoscaling holds in these simulations, and provides a method to extract the symmetry free energy from multifragmentation data. The result that the surface symmetry term is weakened at finite temperature and low density is relevant to understanding the inner crust of neutron stars (Pethick, Lattimer and Prakash) and supernova collapse (Bethe), where the asymmetry term stabilizes nuclear droplets in a dilute neutron gas. The extracted volume symmetry energy can be compared directly to infinite nuclear matter values, enabling constraints on the nuclear equation of state at subsaturation densities.
