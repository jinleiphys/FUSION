# Some new results for "jet" stopping in AdS/CFT

> We give a breezy, qualitative overview of some of our recent results on studying jet stopping in strongly-coupled plasmas using gauge-gravity duality. Previously, people have found that the maximum stopping distance in such plasmas scales with energy as E^{1/3}. We show that there is an important distinction between typical and maximum stopping distances. For the strongly-coupled excitations that we study, we find that the typical stopping distance scales with energy as E^{1/4}.

## Key claim

The authors show that for jet stopping in strongly-coupled plasmas described by AdS/CFT, there is an important distinction between the maximum stopping distance (which scales as E^{1/3}) and the typical stopping distance (which scales as E^{1/4}), with the typical stopping distance being much shorter than the maximum for most jets.

## Method

The authors formulate the jet stopping problem entirely in the field theory by adding a source term to the Lagrangian that couples a high-energy plane wave e^{i k·x} to a field-theory operator, localized by an envelope function Λ_L(x) of size L. They compute the resulting 3-point equilibrium correlator using gauge-gravity duality, which maps it to a 3-leaf tree diagram in AdS_5-Schwarzschild space with bulk-to-boundary propagators given by Heun functions. They then apply a high-momentum WKB approximation for the source propagators and a low-momentum approximation for the measurement propagator, enabling analytic integration over the bulk vertex position.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stopping distance scales as E^{1/3}
- Typical stopping distance scales as (EL)^{1/4}, where L is the size of the space-time region where the jet is created
- The typical stopping distance can be expressed as l_typical ~ (E^2/q^2)^{1/4} (1/T), where q^2 is the typical virtuality of the source
- In the intermediate region between (EL)^{1/4} and E^{1/3}, the probability distribution falls algebraically with power law (x^3)^{-9} for the specific source operator analyzed

## Context

This work extends previous AdS/CFT results on jet stopping (Gubser, HIM, Chesler et al.) that found only the maximum stopping distance scaling as E^{1/3}, by revealing the existence and scaling of the typical stopping distance. The authors contrast their strong-coupling results with the weak-coupling picture of Baier et al. (BDMPS) and Zakharov, where the stopping distance scales as E^{1/2}, and with the mixed-coupling approach of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann. The results enable phenomenological comparisons to test whether experiment can differentiate between E^{1/2}, E^{1/3}, or other scaling laws for jet stopping distances.
