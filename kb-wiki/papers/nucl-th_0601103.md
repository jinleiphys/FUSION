# Charged Current Neutrino Nucleus Interactions at Intermediate Energies

> We have developed a model to describe the interactions of neutrinos with nucleons and nuclei, focusing on the region of the quasielastic and Delta(1232) peaks. We describe neutrino nucleon collisions with a fully relativistic formalism which incorporates state-of-the-art parametrizations of the form factors for both the nucleon and the N-Delta transition. The model has then been extended to finite nuclei, taking into account nuclear effects such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking (both within the local density approximation), nuclear binding and final state interactions. The in-medium modification of the Delta resonance due to Pauli blocking and collisional broadening have also been included. Final state interactions are implemented by means of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) coupled-channel transport model. Results for charged current inclusive cross sections and exclusive channels as pion production and nucleon knockout are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The authors present a model for charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions at intermediate energies (up to about 1.5 GeV) that combines a fully relativistic description of elementary neutrino-nucleon scattering with a coupled-channel transport treatment of final-state interactions, but they note that the model does not yet include medium modifications of the form factors or the renormalization of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium, which have been shown to have a non-negligible impact on the cross section.

## Method

The elementary neutrino-nucleon cross sections for quasielastic scattering and Delta(1232) production are computed using a fully relativistic formalism with state-of-the-art parametrizations of nucleon and N-Delta transition form factors, including the BBA-2003 parametrization for vector form factors and dipole forms for axial form factors with M_A = 1.00 GeV (quasielastic) and M_A = 1.05 GeV (Delta production). These elementary cross sections are then embedded in a local Fermi gas description of the nucleus (Woods-Saxon density distribution for 56Fe) with density-dependent Fermi momenta, Pauli blocking, and a density- and momentum-dependent mean-field potential; the in-medium modification of the Delta resonance includes Pauli-blocked decay and collisional broadening. Final-state interactions of produced nucleons and Delta resonances are implemented using the semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) coupled-channel transport model, which has been previously tested against data from photon-, electron-, pion-, and heavy-ion-induced reactions.

## Key numbers

* Quasielastic scattering axial mass: M_A = 1.00 GeV, extracted from data by Budd et al. (2003), with an experimental accuracy of about 5%.
* Delta production axial mass: M_A = 1.05 GeV, with a 7% error as reported in ANL and BNL analyses.
* Delta(1232) mass: M_Delta = 1.232 GeV; free decay width: Gamma_0 = 0.118 GeV.
* Woods-Saxon parameters for 56Fe: r_0 = 4.22 fm, alpha = 0.477 fm, rho_0 = 0.158 fm^{-3}.
* Mean-field potential parameters: A = -29.3 MeV, B = 57.2 MeV, C = -63.5 MeV, tau = 1.76, Lambda = 2.13 fm^{-1}.
* Delta potential in medium: U_Delta = -30 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0, set to 2/3 of the nucleon potential.
* Axial vector constant: g_A = -1.267 from beta decay.
* C_5^A(0) = 1.2 from the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation, with a 5% uncertainty.
* C_3^V(0) = 1.95 with M_V = 0.84 GeV for the vector form factor of the N-Delta transition.

## Context

This work extends the Giessen BUU transport model, previously applied to photon-, electron-, pion-, and heavy-ion-induced reactions, to neutrino-nucleus scattering, providing a framework with no free nuclear parameters for the neutrino case. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the Rein and Sehgal (1980) model used in event generators (NUANCE, NEUGEN, NEUT) and with simpler treatments of final-state interactions like the eikonal approximation of Singh et al. (1998) or the random walk approximation of Adler (1974) and Paschos (2000). The model enables predictions for exclusive channels (pion production, nucleon knockout) and inclusive cross sections relevant for experiments like MINERvA and FINeSSE, though the authors note that future work should include medium renormalization of weak transition strengths as studied by Singh et al. (2005) and Nieves et al. (2004, 2005).
