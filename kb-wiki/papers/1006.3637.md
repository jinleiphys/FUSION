# Deformed shell model results for two neutrino positron double beta decay of $^{84}$Sr

> Half-lives $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ for two neutrino positron double beta decay modes $β^{+}$EC/ECEC are calculated for $^{84}$Sr, a nucleus of current experimental interest, within the framework of the deformed shell model based on Hartree-Fock states employing a modified Kuo interaction in ($^{2}p_{3/2}$, $^{1}f_{5/2}$, $^{2}p_{1/2}$, $^{1}g_{9/2}$) space. For a reasonable description of the spectra of $^{84}$Sr and $^{84}$Kr and to generate allowed GT strengths, the single particle energies of the proton and neutron $^1g_{9/2}$ orbitals, relative to the $^{2}p_{3/2}$ orbital energy, are chosen to be (3.5 MeV, 1.5 MeV) for both $^{84}$Sr and $^{84}$Rb and (1.5 MeV, 1.5 MeV) for $^{84}$Kr. With this, the calculated half-lives for the $β^+$EC and ECEC modes are $\sim 10^{26}$yr and $\sim 4 \times 10^{24}$yr respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first nuclear-structure calculations for two-neutrino positron double beta decay half-lives of $^{84}$Sr, but the results depend critically on adjusting the single-particle energies of the proton and neutron $^1g_{9/2}$ orbitals separately for the parent/daughter nuclei to reproduce observed spectroscopy, with the half-lives changing by a factor of about 2 depending on this choice.

## Method

The authors used the deformed shell model (DSM) based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states with angular momentum projection and band mixing, employing a modified Kuo effective interaction in the ($^{2}p_{3/2}$, $^{1}f_{5/2}$, $^{2}p_{1/2}$, $^{1}g_{9/2}$) model space with $^{56}$Ni as the inert core. For $^{84}$Sr and $^{84}$Rb, the single-particle energies of the proton and neutron $^1g_{9/2}$ orbitals were set to (3.5 MeV, 1.5 MeV) relative to the $^{2}p_{3/2}$ orbital energy, while for $^{84}$Kr they were set to (1.5 MeV, 1.5 MeV) to reproduce the observed spectroscopy. The nuclear transition matrix elements were calculated by summing over 24 $1^+$ states of the intermediate nucleus $^{84}$Rb, using wavefunctions from 30 intrinsic states for $^{84}$Sr and 11 for $^{84}$Kr.

## Key numbers

* Half-life for the $\beta^+$EC mode of $^{84}$Sr: $\sim 10^{26}$ yr
* Half-life for the ECEC mode of $^{84}$Sr: $\sim 4 \times 10^{24}$ yr
* Atomic mass difference for $^{84}$Sr decay: 1787 keV (this makes the $\beta^+\beta^+$ mode forbidden as the Q-value would be negative)
* When using the same single-particle energies (3.5, 1.5) MeV for $^{84}$Kr as for $^{84}$Sr and $^{84}$Rb, the half-lives reduce by a factor of approximately 2 compared to the values obtained with (1.5, 1.5) MeV for $^{84}$Kr

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DSM calculations for $2\nu\;e^+$DBD in $^{78}$Kr and $^{74}$Se (Sahu and Kota, 2009) to $^{84}$Sr, a nucleus of current experimental interest at the KPNU facility in Korea (Kim et al.). The results are the first nuclear-structure predictions for $^{84}$Sr positron double beta decay half-lives, with no other theoretical results available in the literature for comparison; the authors note that DSM predictions for $^{78}$Kr are close to those of QRPA and PHFB models. The calculations enable future experimental searches using SrCl$_2$ crystals and provide a foundation for ongoing development of $0\nu\;e^+$DBD half-life calculations within the DSM framework.
