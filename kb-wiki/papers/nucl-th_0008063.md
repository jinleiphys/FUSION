# $π-N$ from an Extended Effective Field Theory

> Third order chiral perturbation theory accounts for the $π-N$ scattering phase shift data out to energies slightly below the position of the $Δ$ resonance. The low energy constants are not accurately determined. Explicit inclusion of the $Δ$ field is favored.

## Key claim

Third-order chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) can account for π-N scattering phase shift data up to energies slightly below the Δ resonance position, but the low-energy constants are not accurately determined and unconstrained fits often produce unreasonable values for the sigma term. Explicit inclusion of the Δ field is favored over calculations that omit it.

## Method

The authors extended heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory by explicitly including the Δ isobar field alongside the nucleon and pion fields, treating the Δ-nucleon mass difference as of order Q. At order Q², a K-matrix approximation was used with four low-energy constants fitted to S- and P-wave π-N phase shift data; at order Q³, the number of low-energy constants increased by about a factor of three and loop diagrams were calculated, with unitarity handled either by summing leading-order complex Δ self-energy diagrams (Ellis and Tang) or by assuming the real part of the scattering amplitude as the K-matrix (Fettes and Meissner).

## Key numbers

- At order Q², the data can be fitted up to a center-of-mass pion kinetic energy of ε ∼ 50 MeV (pion laboratory momentum p ∼ 150 MeV).
- At order Q³, the fit extends further to ε ∼ 100 MeV (p ∼ 250 MeV), slightly below the Δ resonance position.
- The sigma term obtained with the Karlsruhe data is close to the canonical 45 MeV (Fettes and Meissner), whereas a larger figure ∼75 MeV is favored with the Virginia Polytechnic Institute data (Ellis and Tang).
- The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy is a few percent, in agreement with a determination from pionic hydrogen data and the GMO sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of π-N scattering by explicitly including the Δ isobar, building on the approaches of Ellis and Tang and Fettes and Meissner. The results show considerable improvement over an order Q³ calculation that excluded the Δ field (Fettes and Meissner, earlier work), particularly in the P33 channel, and are comparable to or slightly better than an order Q⁴ calculation with only π and N fields (Fettes and Meissner, later work). The convergence of the chiral series is much better when the Δ field is explicitly included, enabling more reliable descriptions of π-N scattering up to the delta resonance region.
