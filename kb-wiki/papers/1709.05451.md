# Nuclear mass parabola and its applications

> We propose a method to extract the properties of the isobaric mass parabola based on the total double $β$ decay energies of isobaric nuclei. Two important parameters of the mass parabola, the location of the most $β$-stable nuclei $Z_{A}$ and the curvature parameter $b_{A}$, are obtained for 251 A values based on the total double $β$ decay energies of nuclei compiled in AME2016 database.The advantage of this approach is that we can remove the pairing energy term $P_{A}$ caused by odd-even variation, and the mass excess $M(A,Z_{A})$ of the most stable nuclide for mass number $A$ in the performance process, which are used in the mass parabolic fitting method. The Coulomb energy coefficient $a_{c}=0.6910$ MeV is determined by the mass difference relation of mirror nuclei, and the symmetry energy coefficient is also studied by the relation $a_{\rm sym}(A)=0.25b_{A}Z_{A}$.

# Nuclear mass parabola and its applications

## Key claim
The authors propose that the two key parameters of the isobaric mass parabola, the location of the most beta-stable nucleus \(Z_A\) and the curvature parameter \(b_A\), can be extracted purely from total double beta decay energies, thereby removing the need to fit the pairing energy term and the mass excess of the most stable nuclide. The method is applied to 251 mass numbers using the AME2016 database, but the results rely on the assumption that shell corrections can be neglected in the double beta decay energy expression.

## Method
The authors use the linear relation \(Q_{2\beta} = -2b_A (Z - Z_A + 1)\) derived from the Bohr-Wheeler mass parabola, where \(Q_{2\beta}\) is the total double beta decay energy between nuclei \((A,Z)\) and \((A,Z+2)\). They perform least-squares fitting of this relation to experimental double beta decay energies from the AME2016 database for 251 mass numbers, obtaining \(Z_A\) and \(b_A\) without needing the pairing energy term \(P_A\) or the minimum mass excess \(M(A,Z_A)\). The Coulomb energy coefficient \(a_c\) is then determined from the mirror-nucleus mass difference relation \(b_c = 0.5 b_A (A - 2Z_A) - \Delta_{(^1H-n)}\), and the symmetry energy coefficient is obtained via \(a_{\rm sym}(A) = 0.25 b_A Z_A\).

## Key numbers
- Coulomb energy coefficient: \(a_c = 0.6910\) MeV, determined from the mass difference relation of mirror nuclei.
- For \(A=111\): \(Z_A = 47.7566\), \(b_A = 1.7955\) MeV.
- For \(A=144\): \(Z_A = 59.9768\), \(b_A = 1.5102\) MeV.
- Fitting the mirror-nucleus relation to 251 A values yields \(b_c = 0.691 A^{2/3} - 0.8724\) MeV with an rms deviation of 0.384 MeV.
- Symmetry energy coefficient fit (form 1): \(a_{\rm sym}(A) = 26.575 (1 - 0.99 A^{-1/3})\) with rms deviation 389 keV; \(S_0 = 26.575 \pm 0.271\) MeV, \(\kappa = 0.99 \pm 0.035\).
- Symmetry energy coefficient fit (form 2): \(a_{\rm sym}(A) = 30.102 / (1 + 2.091 A^{-1/3})\) with rms deviation 398 keV; \(S_0 = 30.102 \pm 0.741\) MeV, \(\kappa = 2.091 \pm 0.166\).
- Pairing energy term from beta decay fit: \(P_A = 11.50 \delta / A^{1/2}\), where \(\delta = 0\) for odd-A, +1 for even-even, -1 for odd-odd.

## Context
This work extends the isobaric mass parabola analysis of Bohr and Wheeler (1939), Dewdney (1963), and Li et al. (1981) by using the much larger and more precise AME2016 database (3437 nuclides) and by introducing double beta decay energies as a cleaner probe that eliminates the pairing and minimum-mass parameters. The extracted Coulomb and symmetry energy coefficients are consistent with prior determinations such as those by Danielewicz and Lee (2009) and Min Liu (2010). The results enable improved empirical mass formulas and provide a benchmark for theoretical nuclear mass models, with further work planned to compare against several theoretical mass tables.
