# Non-perturbative gluodynamics of high energy heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of low-x partons in the transverse plane of a high-energy nuclear collision is classical, and therefore admits a fully non--perturbative numerical treatment. We report results of a recent study estimating the initial energy density in the central region of a collision. Preliminary estimates of the number of gluons per unit rapidity, and the initial transverse momentum distribution of gluons, are also provided.

## Key claim

The dynamics of low-x partons in the transverse plane of a high-energy nuclear collision is classical and admits a fully non-perturbative numerical treatment, but the reported results for initial energy density, gluon number per unit rapidity, and transverse momentum distribution are preliminary estimates that depend on the dimensional parameter \(g^2\mu L\), with significant uncertainties in the mapping to specific collider experiments.

## Method

The McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) effective field theory was discretized on a lattice in the transverse plane with boost invariance and periodic boundary conditions assumed. The classical Yang-Mills equations were solved numerically for the gluon field configuration at late proper times, with initial conditions given by the Kovner-McLerran-Weigert (KMW) prescription. Averages over random color source realizations were performed, and the continuum limit was taken by removing lattice spacing dependence.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions, the standard deviation \(\mu\) of the color charge distribution is estimated as 0.5 GeV for RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV per nucleon) and 1 GeV for LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV per nucleon).
- The dimensionless parameter \(g^2\mu L\) is estimated as approximately 120 for RHIC and 240 for LHC, assuming \(g=2\) and \(L=11.6\) fm.
- The transverse-plane energy density per unit rapidity \(\varepsilon\tau/(g^2\mu)^3\) was computed for three values of \(g^2\mu L\): 5.66, 35.36, and 297, and fitted to the form \(\alpha+\beta\exp(-\gamma\tau)\).
- The perturbative gluon number distribution per unit rapidity scales as \(n_{k_\perp}\propto(1/\alpha_s)(\alpha_s\mu/k_\perp)^4\ln(k_\perp/(\alpha_s\mu))\) for \(k_\perp\gg\alpha_s\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the classical effective field theory approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) by providing a fully non-perturbative numerical treatment, going beyond the perturbative solution of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert which showed an infrared divergence in the gluon number distribution. The results enable quantitative predictions for initial energy densities and gluon multiplicities in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the parton density is high enough that final-state interactions and coherence effects cannot be neglected, in contrast to approaches based on pairwise parton scattering or classical cascade descriptions.
