# Heavy ion collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ of 62.4 GeV down to 4 GeV in the EPOS4 framework

> The EPOS4 project is an attempt to construct a realistic model for describing relativistic collisions of different systems, from proton-proton ($pp$) to nucleus-nucleus ($AA$), at energies from several TeV per nucleon down to several GeV. We argue that a parallel scattering formalism (as in EPOS4) is relevant for primary scatterings in AA collisions above 4 GeV, whereas sequential scattering (cascade) is appropriate below. We present briefly the basic elements of EPOS4, and then investigate heavy ion collisions from 62.4 GeV down to 4 GeV, to understand how physics changes with energy, studying in particular the disappearance of the fluid component at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a parallel scattering formalism (as implemented in EPOS4) is appropriate for primary scatterings in nucleus-nucleus collisions above 4 GeV per nucleon pair, while sequential scattering (cascade) is appropriate below that energy; however, the parallel scattering scenario is expected to be fully valid only above approximately 24 GeV, and the paper investigates where and how the model breaks down between 7.7 and 62.4 GeV.

## Method

The authors employ the EPOS4 framework, which combines S-matrix theory for parallel primary scatterings with perturbative QCD and saturation, a core-corona procedure to separate fluidized bulk matter from escaping hadrons, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with eta/s = 0.08, and microcanonical hadronization. They simulate AuAu collisions at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair from 62.4 GeV down to 4 GeV, comparing transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow to STAR data for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and hyperons (Lambda, Xi, Omega) as well as phi mesons across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Low energy threshold E_LE approximately 4 GeV, high energy threshold E_HE approximately 24 GeV, derived from formation time tau_form = 1 fm/c and nucleus radius R = 6.5 fm
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 1.5 fm/c for all collision energies except 7.7 GeV, where tau_0 = 2.0 fm/c is used
- Freeze-out energy density epsilon_FO = 0.57 GeV/fm^3
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.08 used in hydrodynamic evolution
- At 200 GeV, the relative weight of normal to soft Pomerons is roughly 1:1
- At 4 GeV, no fluid is created; at 7.7 GeV, energy density is slightly above epsilon_FO
- Core contribution increases from peripheral to central collisions, with maximum around 1-2 GeV/c in transverse momentum

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4 framework (Werner 2023) from high-energy PbPb at 5.02 TeV and AuAu at 200 GeV down to lower RHIC energies, testing the limits of the parallel scattering approach. It builds on earlier models for the 7.7-62.4 GeV range including the UrQMD cascade (Karpenko 2015), a decelerating string picture (Shen 2017, 2022), and a 3D Monte Carlo Glauber approach (Shen 2020), which succeeded in reproducing rapidity densities and integrated yields but had limited differential p_t spectra comparisons. The paper provides the most complete collection of model-data comparisons for gold-gold collisions in this energy domain, particularly for differential yields, and identifies where the parallel scattering scenario fails at the lowest energies (11.5 and 7.7 GeV), motivating future implementation of a partially parallel scattering scenario between 4 and 24 GeV.
