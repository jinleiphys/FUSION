# Compton scattering on the nucleon at intermediate energies and polarizabilities in a microscopic model

> A microscopic calculation of Compton scattering on the nucleon is presented which encompasses the lowest energies -- yielding nucleon polarizabilities -- and extends to energies of the order of 600 MeV. We have used the covariant "Dressed K-Matrix Model" obeying the symmetry properties which are appropriate in the different energy regimes. In particular, crossing symmetry, gauge invariance and unitarity are satisfied. The extent of violation of analyticity (causality) is used as an expansion parameter.

# Compton scattering on the nucleon at intermediate energies and polarizabilities in a microscopic model

## Key claim

The Dressed K-Matrix Model reproduces Compton scattering on the nucleon from low energies (yielding polarizabilities) up to approximately 600 MeV while satisfying crossing symmetry, gauge invariance, and unitarity, but the extent of violation of analyticity (causality) is used as an expansion parameter, meaning the model is systematically approximate in its treatment of analyticity.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant Dressed K-Matrix Model in which the kernel is built from tree-level diagrams with dressed vertices and propagators, where the dressing is determined by solving coupled integral equations that incorporate analyticity constraints at the level of one-particle reducible diagrams. The K-matrix formalism ensures unitarity and crossing symmetry, while gauge invariance is enforced via minimal substitution to generate contact terms. Parameters are fixed from pion-nucleon scattering and pion photoproduction data, with only two free parameters in the Compton scattering calculation (the gamma-gamma-sigma vertex) adjusted to reproduce backward Compton cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The model extends to photon energies up to approximately 600 MeV (second resonance region).
- Nucleon self-energy renormalization constants: wave function renormalization Z_2^N = 0.8, mass shift delta m = -77 MeV.
- Bare pion coupling constant f = 10.82 in the bare pi-NN form factor.
- Dressed gamma-NN vertex normalization: F_2^{+,s}(m^2) = -0.06 and F_2^{+,v}(m^2) = 1.85.
- Bare anomalous magnetic moments: kappa^s_B = 0.017, kappa^v_B = 1.78.
- Coupling constants from PDG decay widths: g_{rho^0 pi gamma} = 0.131, g_{rho^pm pi gamma} = 0.103, g_{omega pi gamma} = 0.313.
- Pion decay width into two photons: 7.37(1.5) eV.
- The near-vanishing 0-degree cross section at the pion production energy is correctly reproduced in the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends the Dressed K-Matrix Model previously developed by the same authors (Kondratyuk and Scholten, 1999-2001) for pion-nucleon scattering and pion photoproduction to Compton scattering. It contrasts with Bethe-Salpeter approaches (Salpeter, 1951) and their 3-dimensional reductions (Lahiff, 1999; Pearce, 1991; Gross, 1993; Pascalutsa, 1998) which violate crossing symmetry with standard tree-level kernels, and with traditional K-matrix models (Goudsmit, 1994; Scholten, 1996; Feuster, 1998; Korchin, 1998) that violate analyticity. The model enables systematic improvement by extending the dressing to two-particle reducible diagrams, which would require incorporating the "handbag" diagram's real part beyond the approximate "cusp" contact term already included.
