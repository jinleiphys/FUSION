# Sudakov Resummation in Small-x Saturation Formalism

> Through an explicit calculation of massive scalar particle (e.g., Higgs boson) production in high energy pA collisions up to one-loop order, we demonstrate that the Sudakov-type logarithms in hard processes in small-x saturation formalism can be systematically separated from the typical small-x logarithms. The generic feature of the Sudakov logarithms and all order resummation is derived. We further comment on the phenomenological implications and extension to other hard processes in the small-x calculations.

## Key claim

Through an explicit one-loop calculation of massive scalar particle (Higgs boson) production in high-energy pA collisions, the authors demonstrate that Sudakov-type double logarithms can be systematically separated from small-x logarithms, and that both can be resummed consistently within the saturation formalism.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for massive scalar particle production in pA collisions up to one-loop order using the small-x factorization formalism, with the Weizsacker-Williams (WW) unintegrated gluon distribution describing the nucleus. They perform a power expansion in k_perp/Q, separate the soft gluon radiation (which yields Sudakov logarithms) from collinear gluon contributions, and apply dimensional regularization to handle divergences. The all-order resummation is then derived following the Collins-Soper-Sterman procedure.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop result yields a Sudakov form factor with coefficients A^(1) = N_c and B^(1) = -beta_0 N_c, where beta_0 = 11/12 - N_f/18
- The resummed cross section contains the Sudakov factor exp[-S_sud] with S_sud = integral from C_1^2/r^2 to C_2^2 Q^2 of (d mu^2/mu^2)[A ln(C_2^2 Q^2/mu^2) + B]
- The one-loop correction includes a term (alpha_s/pi) N_c [beta_0 ln(Q^2 r_perp^2/c_0^2) - (1/2)(ln(Q^2 r_perp^2/c_0^2))^2 + pi^2/2], where c_0 = 2e^{-gamma_E}
- The calculation is valid in the kinematic region where center-of-mass energy S >> Q^2 and Q^2 >> k_perp^2

## Context

This work extends the transverse momentum resummation formalism of Collins (1984) to the small-x saturation framework, addressing a gap where no theoretical investigation of Sudakov logarithms in the saturation formalism had been performed. The results are consistent with the BK-JIMWLK evolution kernel for the WW gluon distribution calculated by Dominguez (2011). The formalism enables future calculations for other hard processes in pA collisions, including two-photon production, heavy quarkonium production, and dijet correlations, and may also apply to jet processes in hot/dense medium such as quark-gluon plasma.
