# Temperature Dependence of Electric and Magnetic Gluon Condensates

> The contribution of Lorentz non-scalar operators to finite temperature correlation functions is discussed. Using the local duality approach for the one-pion matrix element of a product of two vector currents, the temperature dependence of the average gluonic stress tensor is estimated in the chiral limit to be $\langle{\bf E}^2 +{\bf B}^2\rangle_{T}=\frac{π^2}{10}bT^4$. At a normalization point $μ=0.5$ GeV we obtain $b\approx 1.1$. Together with the known temperature dependence of the Lorentz scalar gluon condensate we are able to infer $\langle{\bf E}^2\rangle_T$ and $\langle{\bf B}^2\rangle_T$ separately in the low-temperature hadronic phase.

# Temperature Dependence of Electric and Magnetic Gluon Condensates

## Key claim
Using the local duality approach for the one-pion matrix element of a product of two vector currents, the temperature dependence of the average gluonic stress tensor is estimated in the chiral limit to be $\langle{\bf E}^2 +{\bf B}^2\rangle_{T}=\frac{\pi^2}{10}bT^4$, with the caveat that this result applies only in the low-temperature hadronic phase where the hadronic basis (rather than a quark-gluon basis) is appropriate and where temperatures are much less than the confinement scale.

## Method
The authors compute the Lorentz non-scalar contribution $C_2$ to the finite-temperature vector current correlator by expanding in the density of thermal pions, keeping only one-pion matrix elements. They evaluate the spin-2, twist-2 operator contributions via the operator product expansion, relating them to the gluon energy-momentum tensor matrix element parameterized by a constant $b$. The constant $b$ is extracted by fitting a local duality sum rule for the second moment of the pion deep inelastic structure function, saturating the hadronic side with $\rho$, $a_1$, $\omega$, and excited meson states using chiral Lagrangian parameters fitted to masses and widths.

## Key numbers
- At normalization point $\mu=0.5$ GeV, the gluon matrix element parameter $b\approx 1.1$ (specifically $b=1.06$ after renormalization group evolution from $b\simeq 1.14$ at $\mu\sim 1$ GeV).
- The temperature dependence of the average gluonic stress tensor is $\langle{\bf E}^2 +{\bf B}^2\rangle_{T}=\frac{\pi^2}{10}bT^4$, with the factor 3 accounting for three charged pion states.
- At $T=150$ MeV, the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates change from their $T=0$ values by only about 1\%.
- The zero-temperature condensates are $\langle{\bf B}^2\rangle_0=-\langle{\bf E}^2\rangle_0\simeq 2\times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^4$ at $\mu=0.5$ GeV.
- For very low temperatures $T\ll m_\pi$, the magnetic condensate behaves as $\langle{\bf B}^2\rangle_T = \langle{\bf B}^2\rangle_0 -0.033\,m_\pi^{5/2}T^{3/2}e^{-m_\pi/T}$ and the electric condensate as $\langle{\bf E}^2\rangle_T = \langle{\bf E}^2\rangle_0 +0.20\,m_\pi^{5/2}T^{3/2}e^{-m_\pi/T}$.

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature QCD sum rule program initiated by Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1986) and developed by Dey et al. (1990) and Eletsky (1991), who showed that at low temperatures the appropriate basis for thermal averages is hadronic states rather than quark-gluon states. The authors build on the chiral perturbation theory results of Leutwyler (1993) for the temperature dependence of the scalar quark condensate and the three-loop calculation of the gluon condensate via the trace anomaly. The separate determination of $\langle{\bf E}^2\rangle_T$ and $\langle{\bf B}^2\rangle_T$ enables future studies of how chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields behave differently as the QCD vacuum approaches the deconfinement transition, and provides input for finite-temperature QCD sum rule analyses of hadronic spectral functions.
