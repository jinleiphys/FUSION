# $Q^2$ evolution of structure functions in the nucleon and nuclei

> $Q^2$ evolution of structure functions in the nucleon and nuclei is investigated by using usual DGLAP equations and parton-recombination equations. Calculated results for proton's $F_2$ and for the ratio $F_2^{Ca}/F_2^D$ are compared with various experimental data. Furthermore, we study nuclear dependence of $Q^2$ evolution in tin and carbon nuclei: $\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C]/ \partial [ln Q^2]\ne 0$, which was found by NMC.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether parton-recombination (PR) effects, which become relevant when the longitudinal localization size of a parton exceeds the average nucleon separation in a nucleus (for x < 0.1), can be observed in the Q² evolution of nuclear structure functions; the author finds that while PR contributions to the ratio F₂^Ca/F₂^D are larger than next-to-leading-order (NLO) DGLAP effects, the nuclear dependence ∂[F₂^Sn/F₂^C]/∂[ln Q²] calculated with PR equations disagrees with preliminary NMC data, suggesting that large PR contributions can be ruled out but the mechanism itself is not necessarily invalidated because the parameter K_HT is not yet theoretically determined.

## Method

The author solves leading-order (LO) DGLAP, next-to-leading-order (NLO) DGLAP, and parton-recombination (PR) evolution equations using a brute-force numerical method with logarithmic-x steps to achieve accuracy down to x = 10⁻⁵. Initial parton distributions for the nucleon are taken from MRS(G) at 4 GeV², while nuclear parton distributions are obtained from a hybrid model with parton-recombination and Q² rescaling mechanisms at Q₀² = 0.8 GeV². The calculations are performed with Λ = 0.2 GeV and N_f = 3, using the author's FORTRAN program BF1.

## Key numbers

- Proton F₂ calculations are performed with initial distributions given at 4 GeV².
- Nuclear parton distributions are evolved from Q₀² = 0.8 GeV².
- The ratio F₂^Ca/F₂^D is calculated at x = 0.0085.
- The nuclear dependence ∂[F₂^Sn/F₂^C]/∂[ln Q²] is shown at Q² = 5 GeV².
- The parameter K_HT, which determines the magnitude of the higher-dimensional gluon distribution via xG_HT(x,Q₀²) = K_HT [xg(x,Q₀²)]², is set to K_HT = 1.68 from prior work (Miyamoto, 1996).
- The small-x region is resolved down to x = 10⁻⁵.

## Context

This work extends the study of Q² evolution of structure functions from the nucleon to nuclei, following the NMC (New Muon Collaboration) finding that ∂[F₂^Sn/F₂^C]/∂[ln Q²] ≠ 0, which was the first indication of nuclear effects in the Q² evolution of F₂. The author compares DGLAP and parton-recombination evolution, building on the hybrid model with parton-recombination and Q² rescaling mechanisms developed by Kumano and collaborators (1990s). The results enable future tests of the recombination mechanism using small-x data from a proposed HERA nuclear program, and the author notes that the constant K_HT must be evaluated theoretically to validate the PR evolution approach.
