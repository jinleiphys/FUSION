# Hadronization via Coalescence

> We review the quark coalescence model for hadronization in relativistic heavy ion collisions and show how it can explain the observed large baryon to meson ratio at intermediate transverse momentum and scaling of the elliptic flows of identified hadrons. We also show its predictions on higher-order anisotropic flows and discuss how quark coalescence applied to open- and hidden-charm mesons can give insight to charm quark interactions in the quark-gluon plasma and $J/Ψ$ production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The quark coalescence model can explain the observed large baryon-to-meson ratio at intermediate transverse momentum and the scaling of elliptic flows for identified hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, provided that the finite momentum distribution of quarks inside hadrons is taken into account, which leads to only a small breaking (about 5%) of the coalescence scaling relation.

## Method

The authors evaluate the hadron transverse momentum spectrum using an overlap integral of the product of quark phase-space distribution functions (comprising a thermal component below p0 ~ 2 GeV and a minijet component above) with the hadron Wigner distribution function, using a Monte Carlo method to avoid the collinear approximation. The quark elliptic flow is extracted from a fit to pion data, and resonance decay contributions are included to compute the elliptic flows of stable hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Thermal quark masses: m_u,d = 300 MeV, m_s = 475 MeV; temperature T = 170 MeV with radial flow velocity β = 0.5
- Fireball volume: 900 fm^3, with transverse radius 8.15 fm and longitudinal length 4.35 fm
- Transverse energy for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: about 700 GeV
- Radius parameter for Wigner function: Δ_p = 0.24 GeV for mesons, 0.35 GeV for baryons
- Scaling breaking from finite quark momentum distribution: about 5%
- For J/Ψ production, the recombination probability decreases by about a factor of 3 when using PYTHIA initial charm quark distributions compared to the thermal case
- Upper limit for D meson elliptic flow from jet fragmentation: around 5%

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence model, previously applied to chemical composition at SPS and RHIC (Alcor), to explain the baryon-to-pion ratio and elliptic flow scaling observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Voloshin, Hwa, Fries, Molnar). The results enable predictions for higher-order anisotropic flow v4 and for charmed hadron production, allowing discrimination between J/Ψ regeneration and suppression mechanisms, and between coalescence and jet fragmentation for D meson production.
