# Hydrodynamization and resummed viscous hydrodynamics

> In this contributed chapter, I review our current understanding of the applicability of hydrodynamics to modeling the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), focusing on the question of hydrodynamization/thermalization of the QGP and the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic framework. I discuss the existence of far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractors and methods for determining attractors within different hydrodynamical frameworks. I also discuss the determination of attractors from exact solutions to the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation and effective kinetic field theory applied to quantum chromodynamics. I then present comparisons of the kinetic attractors with the attractors obtained in standard second-viscous hydrodynamics frameworks and anisotropic hydrodynamics. I demonstrate that, due to the resummation of terms to all orders in the inverse Reynolds number, the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework can describe both the weak- and strong-interaction limits. I then review the phenomenological application of anisotropic hydrodynamics to relativistic heavy-ion collisions using both quasiparticle aHydro and second-order viscous aHydro. The phenomenological results indicate that aHydro provides a controlled extension of dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics to the early-time far-from-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions. This allows one to better describe the data and to extract the temperature dependence of transport coefficients at much higher temperatures than linearized second-order viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), through its resummation of terms to all orders in the inverse Reynolds number, can describe both the weak- and strong-interaction limits and provides a controlled extension of dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics to the early-time far-from-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions, whereas the abstract's claim of applicability is conditioned on the framework's ability to handle large pressure anisotropies that standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics cannot.

## Method

The author derives the hydrodynamic attractor equation for aHydro by combining the first-moment energy-momentum conservation equation with the second-moment equation from the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation (RTA), using a 0+1-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken flow ansatz with a single anisotropy parameter ξ. The resulting attractor equation is expressed in terms of the dimensionless variable w = τT and the amplitude φ(w) = τ ẇ/w, and is compared to the corresponding attractor equations from Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) and Denicol-Niemi-Molnar-Rischke (DNMR) second-order viscous hydrodynamics frameworks. The aHydro attractor equation is then expanded in powers of ξ (equivalently, the inverse Reynolds number) to second order to establish the connection to standard viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used for conversion to physical times is η/s = 0.2, with an initial temperature T₀ = 500 MeV at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c.
* The isotropization time scale for the QGP fireball center is τ_iso ≳ 4 fm/c.
* In the small-ξ expansion, the relation between the normalized shear stress π̄ = π/ε and ξ is π̄ = (8/45)ξ [1 − (13/21)ξ + O(ξ²)].
* The inverse Reynolds number is given by R_π⁻¹ = 3√(3/2) |π̄|.
* The aHydro attractor equation (Eq. 2.2.2 in the text) when expanded to second order in π yields the coefficient β_ππ = 38/21 for the DNMR framework, with τ_π = τ_eq = 5η/(sT).

## Context

This review extends the work on hydrodynamic attractors initiated by Heller and Spaliński (2015) and subsequent studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics in kinetic theory and holography. It contrasts the aHydro framework with standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics approaches such as MIS and DNMR, demonstrating that aHydro's all-orders resummation in the inverse Reynolds number allows it to describe the early-time, large-anisotropy regime where gradient expansions have zero radius of convergence. The work enables more reliable extraction of the temperature dependence of transport coefficients at higher temperatures than possible with linearized second-order viscous hydrodynamics, and provides a framework for phenomenological applications to relativistic heavy-ion collisions using both quasiparticle aHydro and second-order viscous aHydro.
