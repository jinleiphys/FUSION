# Phase structure of a two-fluid bosonic system

> The phase diagram of a two-fluid bosonic system is investigated. The proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) possesses a rich phase structure involving three control parameters and multiple order parameters. The surfaces of quantum phase transition between spherical, axially-symmetric deformed, and SU*(3) triaxial phases are determined, and the evolution of classical equilibrium properties across these transitions is investigated. Spectroscopic observables are considered in relation to the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) is determined for a two-fluid bosonic system, revealing surfaces of quantum phase transition between spherical, axially-symmetric deformed, and SU*(3) triaxial phases, with the analysis restricted to the classical limit and the case of equal boson numbers for the two fluids.

## Method

The classical limit of the IBM-2 Hamiltonian is obtained through the coherent state formalism, yielding an energy surface as a function of deformation parameters (βπ, γπ, βν, γν) and relative Euler angles. The global minimum of this energy surface is used to determine ground state equilibrium configurations, and phase transitions are classified by the Ehrenfest criterion based on discontinuities in derivatives of the energy with respect to control parameters. The analysis is first performed for an F-spin invariant Hamiltonian with three control parameters (ξ', χπ, χν), then extended to a more general Hamiltonian incorporating realistic quadrupole and Majorana interactions.

## Key numbers

- The second-order phase transition between spherical and deformed structure for χV=0 occurs at ξ' = 1/5 when χ=0, with the first-order transition curve given by ξ'_c(χ) = 1/(5 + (2/7)χ²)
- The boundary between axially symmetric and triaxial deformation in the SU(3)-SO(6)-SU*(3) plane is given by the equation 1 - βπ² - βν² - 2βπβν³ + 3βπ²βν² - 2βπ³βν + 2βπ³βν³ = 0
- Along the SU(3)-SU*(3) line with χπ = -√7/2, the transition to triaxiality occurs at χν ≈ 0.4035
- The equilibrium β values for prolate axial deformation are βρ = -χρ/√14 + [(χρ/√14)² + 1]^(1/2), and for oblate axial deformation βρ = -χρ/√14 - [(χρ/√14)² + 1]^(1/2)

## Context

This work extends the phase structure analysis of the one-fluid interacting boson model (IBM), which has three dynamical symmetries separated by first and second order phase transitions as studied by Dieperink (1980) and Feng (1981), to the two-fluid IBM-2 with its richer SUπ(6)⊗SUν(6) algebraic structure supporting four dynamical symmetries. The results provide a framework for interpreting spectroscopic observables in nuclei, particularly signatures of triaxial deformation relevant to current and next-generation radioactive beam facilities, and build upon preliminary results reported by Caprio (2004) and parallel numerical studies by Arias, Dukelsky, and García-Ramos (2004).
