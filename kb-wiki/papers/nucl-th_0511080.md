# Structure of exotic three-body systems

> The classification of large halos formed by two identical particles and a core is systematically addressed according to interparticle distances. The root-mean-square distances between the constituents are described by universal scaling functions obtained from a renormalized zero-range model. Applications for halo nuclei, $^{11}$Li and $^{14}$Be, and for atomic $^4$He$_3$ are briefly discussed. The generalization to four-body systems is proposed.

# Structure of exotic three-body systems

## Key claim
The paper systematically classifies large halo systems formed by two identical particles and a core according to interparticle distances, using universal scaling functions from a renormalized zero-range model, but the classification is qualitative and the model omits finite core size and antisymmetrization of the total nuclear wave function.

## Method
The authors solve regularized momentum-space Faddeev equations for three-body systems with zero-range s-wave interactions, using a subtraction procedure that introduces a three-body scale μ(3) to avoid the Thomas collapse. Root-mean-square interparticle distances are expressed as universal scaling functions of the ratios of two-body and three-body binding energies, and the classification scheme (Borromean, Tango, Samba, all-bound) is ordered by comparing the dimensionless products √⟨r²⟩|E₃| for each configuration. The approach is extended to four-body systems via Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with an additional four-body scale μ(4).

## Key numbers
- For the three-boson system at infinite scattering length, the trimer binding energy from the zero-range model is E₃ = −0.0093 μ²(3) (in units of ℏ = mα = 1).
- The threshold for the appearance of an excited Efimov trimer state is |E₃| = 6.9/a² (in units of ℏ = mα = 1).
- For the atomic ⁴He₃ trimer, the root-mean-square αα distance follows √⟨r²αα⟩ = C √[ℏ²/(mα(|E₃ − Eαα|))] with 0.6 < C < 1.
- For the four-boson system at infinite scattering length with equal three- and four-body scales (μ(4)/μ(3) = 1), the tetramer binding energy is E₄ = 5 E₃.
- For μ(4)/μ(3) = 20, the ratio between tetramer and trimer energies is about 78.

## Context
This work extends the classification scheme for weakly bound three-body systems proposed by Jensen (2003) and applies universal scaling functions developed by Amorim (1999) and Frederico (1995) to halo nuclei and atomic helium trimers. The results for neutron-neutron separation distances in ¹¹Li and ¹⁴Be are compared qualitatively with experimental data from Marques (2001). The generalization to four-body systems builds on the Tjon line relation for nuclear systems and on calculations by Platter (2004), showing that a four-body scale independent of the three-body scale is needed to determine tetramer binding energies.
