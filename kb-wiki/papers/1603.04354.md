# High-spin structure of $^{87}$Sr and $^{87}$Zr nuclei : shell model interpretation

> In the present work we report a comprehensive analysis of shell model results for high-spin states of $^{87}$Sr and $^{87}$Zr for recently available experimental data within the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space using JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions developed for this model space. In this work we have compared the energy levels, electromagnetic transition probabilities, quadrupole and magnetic moments with available experimental data. We have confirmed structure of high-spin states of these two nuclei which were tentatively assigned in the recent experimental work. In the case of $^{87}$Sr, for positive parity states up to $\sim$ 7.5 MeV, both interactions predict very good agreement with experimental data, while negative parity states are slightly suppressed in jj44b calculation.For the $^{87}$Zr nucleus, the jj44b interaction predicts higher energies for the negative parity states beyond $J \geq 27/2^{-}$. The configuration, which have one hole in $νg_{9/2}$ orbital, is responsible for generating the states in $^{87}$Sr. In the case of $^{87}$Zr, low-lying positive parity states come with the configuration having three holes in the $νg_{9/2}$, while the odd-parity states have configuration $ν(f_{5/2}^{-1}g_{9/2}^{-2})$.

# High-spin structure of $^{87}$Sr and $^{87}$Zr nuclei: shell model interpretation

## Key claim

Both the JUN45 and jj44b effective interactions reproduce the experimental high-spin energy levels of $^{87}$Sr and $^{87}$Zr well within the full $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space, but the jj44b interaction systematically predicts higher energies for negative parity states in $^{87}$Zr beyond spin $J \geq 27/2^{-}$, and negative parity states in $^{87}$Sr are slightly suppressed in the jj44b calculation.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the Antoine code with $^{56}$Ni as an inert core and the spherical orbits $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, and $0g_{9/2}$. Two effective interactions were employed: jj44b, fitted to 600 binding energies and excitation energies from nuclei with $Z = 28-30$ and $N = 48-50$ (rms deviation ~250 keV), and JUN45, based on the Bonn-C potential with empirical modifications for the $A = 63-69$ mass region. The calculated energy levels, electromagnetic transition probabilities, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments were compared with recently available experimental data for both nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For $^{87}$Sr: the JUN45 calculation predicts the $5/2^+_1$ level only 65 keV lower and the $7/2^+_1$ level only 3 keV higher than experiment; the $13/2^+_1$ level is predicted 163 keV lower (JUN45) and 178 keV lower (jj44b) than experiment.
- For $^{87}$Sr: the experimental $Q(9/2_1^+)$ quadrupole moment is +0.305(2) eb; JUN45 gives +0.349 eb and jj44b gives +0.415 eb.
- For $^{87}$Sr: the experimental magnetic moment $\mu(9/2_1^+)$ is -1.0928(7) $\mu_N$; JUN45 gives -1.0298 $\mu_N$ and jj44b gives -0.9796 $\mu_N$.
- For $^{87}$Zr: the $21/2_1^+$ and $25/2_1^+$ levels are only 22 keV and 79 keV higher than experiment, respectively, in the jj44b calculation.
- For $^{87}$Zr: the experimental $43/2^-$ level is at 10093 keV; JUN45 predicts 9716 keV and jj44b predicts 10527 keV.
- For $^{87}$Zr: the experimental $Q(9/2_1^+)$ quadrupole moment is +0.423(48) eb; JUN45 gives +0.341 eb and jj44b gives +0.649 eb.
- For $^{87}$Zr: the experimental magnetic moment $\mu(9/2_1^+)$ is -0.895(9) $\mu_N$; JUN45 gives -0.9401 $\mu_N$ and jj44b gives -0.7816 $\mu_N$.
- The $B(E2;5/2^+_1 \rightarrow 9/2^+_1)$ transition in $^{87}$Sr is 7.5(23) W.u. experimentally; JUN45 gives 10.19 W.u. and jj44b gives 12.27 W.u.
- The $B(E2;7/2^+_1 \rightarrow 9/2^+_1)$ transition in $^{87}$Zr is 3.25(14) W.u. experimentally; JUN45 gives 17.68 W.u. and jj44b gives 16.48 W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of high-spin states in the Sr-Zr region, including recent calculations for $^{88}$Zr, $^{89}$Zr, $^{85}$Sr, and $^{86}$Sr by the same group. The experimental high-spin data for $^{87}$Sr were populated up to 7.4 MeV excitation energy at spin 31/2$\hbar$ via the $^{82}$Se($^{9}$Be,4n)$^{87}$Sr reaction reported by Srivastava et al. (2016), while $^{87}$Zr data came from the $^{59}$Co($^{32}$S,3pn)$^{87}$Zr reaction establishing levels up to (37/2$^+$) and (43/2$^-$). The results enable testing of the two-body matrix elements for predicting high-spin states generated by aligning angular momenta from broken nucleon pairs, and confirm the dominant configurations involving holes in the $\nu g_{9/2}$ orbital for $^{87}$Sr and three holes in $\nu g_{9/2}$ for low-lying positive parity states of $^{87}$Zr.
