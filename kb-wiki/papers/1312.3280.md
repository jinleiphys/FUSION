# Neutron matter under strong magnetic fields: a comparison of models

> The equation of state of neutron matter is affected by the presence of a magnetic field due to the intrinsic magnetic moment of the neutron. Here we study the equilibrium configuration of this system for a wide range of densities, temperatures and magnetic fields. Special attention is paid to the behavior of the isothermal compressibility and the magnetic susceptibility. Our calculation is performed using both microscopic and phenomenological approaches of the neutron matter equation of state, namely the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock (BHF) approach using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, the effective Skyrme model in a Hartree--Fock description, and the Quantum Hadrodynamic formulation with a mean field approximation. All these approaches predict a change from completely spin polarized to partially polarized matter that leads to a continuous equation of state. The compressibility and the magnetic susceptibility show characteristic behaviors, which reflect that fact. Thermal effects tend to smear out the sharpness found for these quantities at T=0. In most cases a thermal increase of 10 MeV is enough to hide the signals of the change of polarization. The set of densities and magnetic field intensities for which the system changes it spin polarization is different for each model. However, there is an overall agreement between the three theoretical descriptions.

## Key claim

All three models predict a continuous equation of state across the transition from completely spin polarized to partially polarized neutron matter, but the specific densities and magnetic field intensities at which this transition occurs differ for each model, with thermal effects of ΔT=10 MeV sufficient to obscure the transition signals in most cases.

## Method

The authors computed the Helmholtz free-energy density of infinite homogeneous neutron matter under external magnetic fields using three approaches: the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with the Argonne V18 potential plus Urbana IX three-nucleon force, the Quantum Hadrodynamic (QHD) model in mean-field approximation using the FSU-Gold parametrization, and the Skyrme effective model with the SLy4 parametrization in Hartree-Fock approximation. For each model, they minimized the free-energy density with respect to the spin asymmetry density to find the physical state, then derived the isothermal compressibility and magnetic susceptibility from the free energy.

## Key numbers

- Threshold densities at T=0 and B=10^19 G: n_t/n_0 = 0.55 for Skyrme, 0.65 for BHF, and 0.85 for QHD
- Threshold field intensities at T=0 and n/n_0=0.2: B_t = 2.86 × 10^18 G for QHD, 4.60 × 10^18 G for Skyrme, and 5 × 10^18 G for BHF
- For B=10^19 G at T=0, the spin-averaged effective mass decreases to approximately one half of its vacuum value at n/n_0=1.5 for QHD and Skyrme, while BHF shows at most a 20-22% decrease
- At n/n_0=0.2 and T=0, the magnetic field effect on effective mass is small: ~1-2% increase for Skyrme, ~5% decrease for BHF, and nearly constant for QHD
- The compressibility plateau value at strong fields is K ~ 400 fm^4 for all models at T=0 and n/n_0=0.2
- Calculations were restricted to densities below 2.5 n_0, temperatures up to T=10 MeV, and magnetic fields up to B=10^19 G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized neutron matter by comparing microscopic (BHF) and phenomenological (QHD, Skyrme) approaches, building on earlier investigations of ferromagnetic transitions in neutron matter that gave contradictory results between Skyrme-based models (Vidaurre, Rios) and realistic interaction calculations (Fantoni, Vidana). The comparison is motivated by applications to neutron star structure and cooling, as well as heavy-ion collision physics where magnetic fields up to eB ~ m_π^2 have been predicted (Skokov). The results enable assessment of model-dependent uncertainties in the equation of state for magnetized neutron stars and identify the isothermal compressibility and magnetic susceptibility as sensitive probes of the spin polarization transition.
