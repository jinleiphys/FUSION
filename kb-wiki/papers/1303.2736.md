# Nuclei as Probes of Meson-Nucleon Interactions at High and Low Energy

> This dissertation explores two main topics: 1) Color Transparency and quasi-elastic knockout reactions involving pions and $Žü$ mesons; and 2) determination of the $J/Žł$-nucleon scattering amplitude and scattering length via $J/Žł$ electroproduction on the deuteron. It is shown that at the energies available at the COMPASS experiment at CERN, Color Transparency should be detectable in the reaction $ŽĆ+A\to ŽĆ+p+(A-1)^*$ (proton knockout). It is also shown that Color Transparency should be detectable in the electroproduction reaction $╬│^*+A\toŽü+p+(A-1)^*$ at small $Q^2$ (where $Q^2$ is the virtuality of the photon) but large $t$ (4-momentum transfer squared to the knocked out proton), which represents an as-yet unexplored kinematic region in the search for CT effects in electroproduction of vector mesons. Calculations are also presented for the reaction $╬│^*+D\to J/Žł+p+n$ at JLab energies in order to determine the feasibility of measuring the elastic $J/Žł$-nucleon scattering amplitude and/or scattering length. It is found that it may be possible to measure the $J/Žł$-nucleon scattering amplitude at lower energies than previous measurements, but the scattering length cannot be measured.

## Method

The author uses the Glauber model of high-energy hadron-nucleus scattering, modified to include Color Transparency effects via the quantum diffusion model, to calculate nuclear transparency T for pion-induced proton knockout and Žü meson electroproduction. For the J/Žł case, a diagrammatic model of ╬│*+DŌåÆJ/Žł+p+n is used to evaluate the feasibility of extracting the J/Žł-nucleon scattering amplitude and scattering length at JLab energies with a 12 GeV electron beam.

## Context

This work extends the search for Color Transparency effects beyond the earlier experiments at Brookhaven (Carroll 1988) on p+AŌåÆp+p+(A-1) and the SLAC experiments (Makins 1994, O'Neill 1995, Garrow 2002) on e+AŌåÆe+p+(A-1), which showed inconclusive or negative results. The only unambiguous evidence for Color Transparency came from the diffractive dissociation experiment (Aitala 2001). The calculations for the J/Žł-nucleon scattering length are motivated by a JLab proposal (2010) and the theoretical work of Savage (1992) and Brodsky (1997) suggesting possible J/Žł-nucleus bound states. The results enable future experimental searches at COMPASS for pion-induced Color Transparency and at JLab for J/Žł-nucleon scattering amplitude measurements at lower energies than previously accessible.
