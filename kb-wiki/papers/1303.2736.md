# Nuclei as Probes of Meson-Nucleon Interactions at High and Low Energy

> This dissertation explores two main topics: 1) Color Transparency and quasi-elastic knockout reactions involving pions and $ρ$ mesons; and 2) determination of the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering amplitude and scattering length via $J/ψ$ electroproduction on the deuteron. It is shown that at the energies available at the COMPASS experiment at CERN, Color Transparency should be detectable in the reaction $π+A\to π+p+(A-1)^*$ (proton knockout). It is also shown that Color Transparency should be detectable in the electroproduction reaction $γ^*+A\toρ+p+(A-1)^*$ at small $Q^2$ (where $Q^2$ is the virtuality of the photon) but large $t$ (4-momentum transfer squared to the knocked out proton), which represents an as-yet unexplored kinematic region in the search for CT effects in electroproduction of vector mesons. Calculations are also presented for the reaction $γ^*+D\to J/ψ+p+n$ at JLab energies in order to determine the feasibility of measuring the elastic $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering amplitude and/or scattering length. It is found that it may be possible to measure the $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering amplitude at lower energies than previous measurements, but the scattering length cannot be measured.

## Key claim

Color Transparency should be detectable in the reaction π+A→π+p+(A-1)* at the energies available at the COMPASS experiment at CERN (200 GeV incident pion momentum), and also in the electroproduction reaction γ*+A→ρ+p+(A-1)* at small Q² but large t, which represents an as-yet unexplored kinematic region; however, for the J/ψ-nucleon scattering length measurement via γ*+D→J/ψ+p+n at JLab energies, the scattering length cannot be measured, though the J/ψ-nucleon scattering amplitude may be measurable at lower energies than previous measurements.

## Method

The author uses the Glauber model of high-energy hadron-nucleus scattering, modified to include Color Transparency effects via the quantum diffusion model, to calculate nuclear transparency T for pion-induced proton knockout and ρ meson electroproduction. For the J/ψ case, a diagrammatic model of γ*+D→J/ψ+p+n is used to evaluate the feasibility of extracting the J/ψ-nucleon scattering amplitude and scattering length at JLab energies with a 12 GeV electron beam.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion momentum of 200 GeV is available at the COMPASS experiment at CERN for the reaction π+A→π+p+(A-1)*
- The J/ψ mass is 3.097 GeV, with a threshold photon energy for photoproduction on a single nucleon of 8.2 GeV, accessible with a 12 GeV electron beam at JLab
- For proton-nucleon total cross-section σ=40 mb and nuclear density ρ≈0.2 fm⁻³, the mean-free path is l≈1.25 fm
- The SLAC experiment (Makins 1994, O'Neill 1995, Garrow 2002) covered Q² from 1 to 8.1 GeV² for e+A→e+p+(A-1) and showed no indication of Color Transparency
- The diffractive dissociation experiment (Aitala 2001) found an A-dependence of the cross-section as A^1.55 for pions scattered from carbon and platinum nuclei

## Context

This work extends the search for Color Transparency effects beyond the earlier experiments at Brookhaven (Carroll 1988) on p+A→p+p+(A-1) and the SLAC experiments (Makins 1994, O'Neill 1995, Garrow 2002) on e+A→e+p+(A-1), which showed inconclusive or negative results. The only unambiguous evidence for Color Transparency came from the diffractive dissociation experiment (Aitala 2001). The calculations for the J/ψ-nucleon scattering length are motivated by a JLab proposal (2010) and the theoretical work of Savage (1992) and Brodsky (1997) suggesting possible J/ψ-nucleus bound states. The results enable future experimental searches at COMPASS for pion-induced Color Transparency and at JLab for J/ψ-nucleon scattering amplitude measurements at lower energies than previously accessible.
