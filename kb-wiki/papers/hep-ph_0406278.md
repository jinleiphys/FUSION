# Thermalization of gluons in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions by including three-body interactions in a parton cascade

> We develop a new 3+1 dimensional Monte Carlo cascade solving the kinetic on-shell Boltzmann equations for partons including the inelastic gg <-> ggg pQCD processes. The back reaction channel is treated -- for the first time -- fully consistently within this scheme. An extended stochastic method is used to solve the collision integral. The frame dependence and convergency are studied for a fixed tube with thermal initial conditions. The detailed numerical analysis shows that the stochastic method is fully covariant and that convergency is achieved more efficiently than within a standard geometrical formulation of the collision term, especially for high gluon interaction rates. The cascade is then applied to simulate parton evolution and to investigate thermalization of gluons for a central Au+Au collision at RHIC energy. For this study the initial conditions are assumed to be generated by independent minijets with p_T > p_0=2 GeV. With that choice it is demonstrated that overall kinetic equilibration is driven mainly by the inelastic processes and is achieved on a scale of 1 fm/c. The further evolution of the expanding gluonic matter in the central region then shows almost an ideal hydrodynamical behavior. In addition, full chemical equilibration of the gluons follows on a longer timescale of about 3 fm/c.

## Key claim

Kinetic equilibration of gluons in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is driven mainly by inelastic $gg \leftrightarrow ggg$ processes and is achieved on a scale of 1 fm/c, while full chemical equilibration follows on a longer timescale of about 3 fm/c, under the assumption that initial conditions are generated by independent minijets with $p_T > p_0 = 2$ GeV.

## Method

The authors developed a 3+1 dimensional Monte Carlo cascade that solves the kinetic on-shell Boltzmann equations for partons, including inelastic $gg \leftrightarrow ggg$ perturbative QCD processes with the back reaction channel treated fully consistently for the first time. An extended stochastic method was used to solve the collision integral, where collision probabilities are computed directly from transition rates rather than from a geometrical interpretation of cross sections. The cascade was applied to simulate parton evolution for a central Au+Au collision at RHIC energy, with initial conditions generated by independent minijets.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic equilibration of gluons achieved on a scale of **1 fm/c** for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy with minijet initial conditions ($p_T > p_0 = 2$ GeV)
- Full chemical equilibration of gluons follows on a longer timescale of about **3 fm/c**
- For a fixed tube with thermal initial conditions, the stochastic method showed convergency achieved more efficiently than within a standard geometrical formulation, especially for high gluon interaction rates
- In box calculations with isotropic collisions and constant cross section $\sigma_{22}=10$ mb, the saturation value of the collision rate was **8.3 fm$^{-1}$** at high density, corresponding to an average collision time difference $<\Delta t_c>/2 = 0.12$ fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier parton cascade models such as VNI by Geiger and Muller (1992), which included binary elastic scatterings and gluon radiation but treated off-shell partons, and MPC by Molnar and Gyulassy (2000), which included only elastic gluon interactions. The authors note that elastic $gg \leftrightarrow gg$ collisions cannot drive the system to kinetic equilibrium, as pointed out by Shuryak and Sinha (2001), and that the stochastic method enables consistent treatment of inelastic $2 \leftrightarrow 3$ processes that are essential for thermalization. This work enables quantitative studies of thermalization and hydrodynamical behavior in heavy ion collisions using pQCD-based transport simulations.
