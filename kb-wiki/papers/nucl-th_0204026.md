# Relationship of the 3P0 decay model to other strong decay models

> The 3P0 decay model is briefly reviewed. Possible improvements, partly motivated by the examination of a microscopic description of a quark - anti-quark pair creation, are considered. They can provide support for the one-body character of the model which, otherwise, is difficult to justify. To some extent, they point to a boost effect that most descriptions of processes involving a pair creation cannot account for.

# Relationship of the 3P0 Decay Model to Other Strong Decay Models

## Key claim
The $^3P_0$ decay model can be related to a microscopic two-body interaction between quarks, which provides a natural justification for its one-body character and yields a specific momentum-dependent factor $(E-2e_q)$ that determines both the strength and momentum dependence of the model, though this derivation is strictly valid only in the spin-less particle approximation and for strongly bound mesons.

## Method
The authors derive the decay amplitude for a hadron decaying into another hadron and a meson using a two-body interaction picture, then exploit the Schrödinger-type equation for the meson wave function to replace the interaction term with the energy factor $(E-2e_q)$. This transformation converts the two-body amplitude into a one-body form resembling the $^3P_0$ operator. For spin-1/2 particles, they identify additional spin-dependent terms beyond the standard $^3P_0$ vertex that cannot be absorbed into the model.

## Key numbers
- The replacement $\gamma \frac{\vec{\sigma} \cdot (\vec{p}-\vec{p}')}{2\sqrt{2\pi}} \rightarrow (E-2e_q) \bar{u}(\vec{p}) v(\vec{p}')$ yields a value of $2\sqrt{2\pi} \simeq 5$ for $\gamma$ when the meson is strongly bound ($E$ negligible) and $e_q$ is approximated by the quark mass.
- This value of $\gamma \approx 5$ compares well with values obtained from meson decays but is significantly smaller than values derived from baryon decays.

## Context
This work extends the $^3P_0$ decay model originally introduced by Micu (1969) and developed by Le Yaouanc et al. (1988), addressing its conceptual difficulty of vacuum pair creation without relation to other particles. The authors build on prior suggestions by Kokoski and Isgur (1987) regarding flux-tube breaking and by Ackleh et al. (1996) linking pair creation to quark interactions. The derived boost effect addresses limitations that previous relativized versions (Cano et al., 1997) and momentum-dependent strength modifications (Roberts, 1998) could not fully explain, and shows similarity to Bethe-Salpeter approaches by Kulshreshtha (1983).
