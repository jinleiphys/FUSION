# Study of the $K^+K^-$ final state interaction in proton-proton and electron-positron collisions

> The strength of the kaon--antikaon interaction is a crucial quantity for many physics topics. It is for example, an important parameter in the discussion on the nature of the scalar resonances $a_{0}(980)$ and $f_{0}(980)$, in particular for their interpretation as $K\bar{K}$ molecules. So far, one of the few possibilities to study this interaction is the kaon pair production in multi particle exit channels like $pp \to ppK^+K^-$. In this article we present the latest results of the $K^+K^-$ interaction preformed based on near threshold data gathered at the Cooler Synchrotron COSY. We discuss also shortly perspectives for a new measurement of the kaon--antikaon scattering length in the $e^+e^-$ collisions.

## Key claim

The strength of the kaon–antikaon interaction is estimated from a combined analysis of total and differential cross sections for the \(pp \to ppK^+K^-\) reaction, yielding a \(K^+K^-\) scattering length that is consistent with zero, indicating that the interaction between protons and the \(K^-\) meson is dominant in this system.

## Method

The analysis used a factorization ansatz for the final state interaction (FSI) in the \(ppK^+K^-\) system, with enhancement factors for the \(pp\), two \(pK^-\), and \(K^+K^-\) subsystems. The \(pp\) amplitude was parametrized using a phase shift with Coulomb correction, while the \(pK^-\) and \(K^+K^-\) interactions were described in the scattering length approximation. Experimental Goldhaber plot distributions at excess energies Q = 10 MeV and Q = 28 MeV from the COSY–11 experiment were combined with total cross section data near threshold in a \(\chi^2\) fit, treating the \(K^+K^-\) scattering length as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- \(|\mathrm{Re}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}})| = 0.0^{~+1.1_{stat}}_{~-0.0_{stat}}~\mathrm{fm}\)
- \(\mathrm{Im}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = 1.1^{~+0.6_{stat}~+0.9_{sys}}_{~-0.5_{stat}~-0.6_{sys}}~\mathrm{fm}\)
- \(\chi^2/ndof = 1.87\) for the fit
- Statistical uncertainties determined at the 70% Confidence Level
- The result is a factor five more precise than the previous estimate from PhysRevC

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the \(K^+K^-\) interaction using data from the COSY–11 and ANKE experiments at the COSY synchrotron, where a significant enhancement near the kinematical threshold was observed but not fully explained by \(pK^-\) FSI alone. The result is consistent with zero, suggesting that the \(K^+K^-\) interaction is weak in the \(ppK^+K^-\) system, and the paper proposes that a cleaner determination of the kaon–antikaon scattering length could be obtained from \(e^+e^- \to K^+K^-\gamma\) reactions at the KLOE–2 detector, continuing the studies of scalar resonances \(a_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) done by the KLOE collaboration.
