# Finite temperature sum rules in the vector channel at finite momentum

> Exact sum rules for the longitudinal and transverse part of the vector channel spectral functions at nonzero momentum are derived in the first part of the paper. The sum rules are formulated for the finite temperature spectral functions, from which the vacuum component has been subtracted, and represent a generalization of previous work in which sum rules were derived only for the zero-momentum limit. In the second part of the paper, we demonstrate how the sum rules can be used as constraints in spectral fits to lattice data at various temperatures, with the latest dynamical lattice QCD data at zero momentum.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact sum rules for the longitudinal and transverse components of the vector channel spectral function at finite spatial momentum, generalizing previous work that only considered the zero-momentum limit; the sum rules are formulated for the finite-temperature spectral function from which the vacuum component has been subtracted.

## Method

The authors use the residue theorem on a contour in the complex frequency plane, combined with the operator product expansion (OPE) to describe the ultraviolet behavior and hydrodynamics to describe the infrared behavior of the retarded Green function. They decompose the vector current correlator into transverse and longitudinal components at finite momentum, then derive sum rules by matching the asymptotic behaviors in the UV and IR regions. The sum rules are tested against perturbative calculations in the chiral and weak coupling limits, and are applied as constraints in spectral fits to lattice QCD data at zero momentum.

## Key numbers

- The transport peak contribution to sum rule 1 (transverse channel) at leading order is \( \Cem\Nc\chi \frac{2}{3} \), where \( \chi \equiv T^2/6 \)
- The continuum contribution to sum rule 1 (transverse channel) at leading order is \( -C_{\text{em}}\Nc \frac{1}{3} \left[ \frac{T^2}{3} - \frac{\vp^2}{2\pi^2}\ln\frac{T}{\mu} \right] \)
- The continuum contribution to sum rule 2 (transverse channel) at leading order is \( -C_{\text{em}}\Nc \frac{1}{45} T^2 \left[ 14\pi^2T^2 + \vp^2 \right] \)
- The UV tail contribution to sum rule 2 (transverse channel) at leading order is \( \Cem \Nc\Cf \frac{4\pi^2T^4}{9} \frac{2}{4\Cf+\Nf} \)
- The longitudinal channel sum rule 1 involves the diffusion pole: \( \rho_{00}(p) = \sigma\vp^2 \frac{\omega}{\omega^2+(D\vp^2)^2} \), which at \( |\vp|=0 \) reduces to \( \rho_{00}(\omega,\vzero) = \pi\frac{\sigma}{D}\omega\delta(\omega) \)

## Context

This work extends the zero-momentum sum rules derived in Gubler (2016) to finite spatial momentum, where the vector spectral function splits into transverse and longitudinal components with distinct structures including a diffusion pole in the longitudinal channel. The sum rules provide constraints that can improve lattice QCD analyses of spectral functions, which previously relied on methods such as the maximum entropy method (Jarrell 1996, Asakawa 2000), the Backus-Gilbert method (Backus 1968, 1970), or the Schlessinger point method (Schlessinger 1967). The results enable more reliable extraction of transport coefficients like electrical conductivity from lattice data.
