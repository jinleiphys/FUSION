# Pentaquark photoproduction

> We present results and suggestions on how to confirm the existence and resonant nature of the $Pc(4450)$ detected at LHCb through photoproduction experiments. We find that this narrow structure might have escaped detection in past experiments and use those to give a constraint for the upper limit of the branching ratio/coupling to the $J/ψp$ channel.

## Key claim

The narrow \(P_c(4450)\) pentaquark candidate detected by LHCb could be confirmed via photoproduction in the \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) reaction, but only if its branching ratio to \(J/\psi p\) is below 30% (for spin-parity \(3/2^-\)) or below 17% (for \(5/2^+\)) at 95% confidence level, as past experiments may have missed it due to insufficient sensitivity.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) combining a Breit-Wigner resonance for the \(P_c(4450)\) pentaquark and a nonresonant background described by an effective Pomeron exchange constrained by existing high-energy data. The electromagnetic vertex for the pentaquark was related to the strong vertex via vector meson dominance, assuming equal helicity couplings. They fit the available data for \(|t| \leq 1.5~\text{GeV}^2\) and propagated uncertainties using the bootstrap technique and Monte Carlo sampling of the pentaquark mass and width.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the \(P_c(4450) \to J/\psi p\) branching ratio: below 30% for \(J^P = 3/2^-\) and below 17% for \(J^P = 5/2^+\) at 95% confidence level.
- The \(P_c(4450)\) mass is 4450 MeV and its width is narrow, as reported by LHCb.
- The Pomeron trajectory parameters \(\alpha_0\) and \(\alpha'\) and the constants \(A\), \(b_0\), and \(t_0\) were fitted to data for \(|t| \leq 1.5~\text{GeV}^2\).
- The \(J/\psi\) decay constant used in vector meson dominance is \(f_\psi = 280~\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the LHCb pentaquark signals (LHCb, 2015) by proposing photoproduction as an independent confirmation channel, following the suggestion by Wang et al. (2015) to use \(J/\psi p\) photoproduction. The model provides predictions for the upcoming Jefferson Lab Hall C experiment (approved by PAC44) and enables future searches for other members of the hidden-charm pentaquark multiplet and studies of the pentaquark electromagnetic form factor.
