# Possible Enhancements of Collective Flow Anisotropy Induced by Uncertainty Relation for Fluid Element

> In the stochastic formulation of viscous hydrodynamics, the velocity of a fluid element fluctuates satisfying a similar relation to the quantum-mechanical uncertainty relation. Using a non-relativistic toy model, we show that the presence of such a velocity fluctuation increases the local anisotropy of the momentum distributions of produced hadrons, and thus the collective flow parameters such as $v_2$ is emphasized.

## Key claim

Using a non-relativistic toy model, the authors show that velocity fluctuations of fluid elements, which arise from a hydrodynamic uncertainty relation analogous to quantum mechanics, can increase the collective flow anisotropy \(v_2\) rather than homogenize it, contrary to the usual expectation that fluctuations reduce anisotropy.

## Method

The authors derive the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation from a stochastic variational method, where fluid element trajectories obey forward and backward stochastic differential equations with a noise intensity \(\nu\). They then modify the non-relativistic Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula by averaging over the Gaussian velocity fluctuation distribution, which introduces an anisotropic temperature index \(T^{i}_{AI} = T_{FO} + (\mu/2k_B) I^{i}\) for each spatial component. The 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics is solved numerically with a simple Gaussian initial density profile and a polytropic equation of state, and the elliptic flow \(v_2(p)\) is computed from the modified hadron spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity is set to \(\eta/(2\rho) = \hbar/(8\pi)\), corresponding to the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound with Reynolds number \(\mathrm{Re} \approx 8\) for typical scales of 1 fm and speed of light.
* Initial density profile has Gaussian widths \((R_x, R_y) = (4/3, 3)\) fm, with central density \(\rho_c = 7.9\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\).
* Maximum initial temperature is \(\sim 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\); freeze-out temperature is \(T_{FO} = 0.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
* Hadron mass is \(\mu_\pi = 0.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (order of pion mass).
* Three values of noise intensity are used: \(\nu = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6\) fm; \(v_2\) increases with \(\nu\) and is larger than the standard \(\nu=0\) case (dotted line) for all momenta.
* The anisotropic temperature index variation is on the order of a few percent at maximum at \(t=0.4\) fm for \(\nu=0.2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method formulation of viscous hydrodynamics developed by Koide, Kodama, and collaborators (Koide 2018, 2020, 2022; Goncalves 2022) to the freeze-out process in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with the standard Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure (Cooper 1974) where fluid elements follow deterministic streamlines. The result suggests that velocity fluctuations, normally expected to homogenize systems, can instead enhance elliptic flow anisotropy, and may help distinguish between different sources of higher-order flow harmonics such as \(v_3\) in future applications to more complex initial geometries.
