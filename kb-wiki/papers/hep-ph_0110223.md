# Kaon Condensation and Enforced Charge Neutrality of the Color-Flavor Locked Phase

> We investigate the influence of charged kaon condensation in hadronic sector on the formation of the electron-free charge neutral quark matter of equipartition of three light flavors, i.e., a phenomenon called ``enforced electrical neutrality" of the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase. We employ the chiral quark model which is assumed to be applicable, "bottom-up," toward chiral restoration while accessible to kaon condensation in the hadronic sector. Approaching "bottom-up" the high density regime is more appropriate in addressing the properties of compact stars than approaching "top-down" from asymptotic QCD. It turns out that the presence of hadronic kaon condensation makes the electron-free charge-neutral CFL phase energetically more favorable with a relatively small color-superconducting gap $Δ_0\sim 20$ MeV compared to $Δ_0\sim 70$ MeV required in the absence of such kaon condensation.

## Key claim

The presence of hadronic kaon condensation (driven by electrons) makes the electron-free, charge-neutral color-flavor locked (CFL) phase energetically more favorable with a relatively small color-superconducting gap of about 20 MeV, compared to the approximately 70 MeV gap required in the absence of such kaon condensation, under the assumption that the chiral quark model with Brown-Rho (BR) scaling is applicable "bottom-up" toward chiral restoration.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral quark model to study kaon condensation in hadronic matter, first without and then with strange quark matter (hyperons), using both a heavy-fermion approximation and a relativistic treatment. They incorporate Brown-Rho (BR) scaling to account for the density dependence of parameters such as the pion decay constant and constituent quark masses. The in-medium kaon mass is computed from the kaon self-energy (a one-loop diagram with quark loops), and the resulting chemical potential shift from kaon condensation is then applied to the condition for the stability of the CFL phase against a paired (BCS) state, following the formalism of Rajagopal and Wilczek (2001).

## Key numbers

*   Without strange matter and without BR scaling, the in-medium \(K^-\) mass drops to approximately 260 MeV at a baryon density of \(3\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear matter density).
*   With strange matter (equal numbers of u, d, s quarks, \(y=1\)) and with BR scaling, the in-medium kaon masses (\(m_{K^+}^* = m_{K^-}^*\)) become approximately 26 MeV at a baryon density of \(2.84\rho_0\).
*   In the presence of hadronic kaon condensation, the condition for the CFL phase to be the global minimum requires a color-superconducting gap \(\Delta_0 > 20\) MeV (for \(m_s=200\) MeV, \(\bar\mu=400\) MeV, \(\mu_e=150\) MeV, and a kaon condensation angle \(\theta_{min} \approx 75\) degrees).
*   Without kaon condensation, the same condition requires \(\Delta_0 > 50\) MeV for the same quark masses and chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon condensation in the hadronic phase, originally proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and refined by Brown, Kubodera, Rho, and Thorsson (1992), to its influence on the high-density CFL phase of quark matter. It contrasts with the "top-down" approach from asymptotic QCD (e.g., Schafer (2000) on CFL kaon condensation; Rajagopal and Wilczek (2001) on enforced charge neutrality) by using a "bottom-up" chiral quark model that connects to phenomenologically understood hadronic physics. The results suggest that hadronic kaon condensation can significantly lower the required superconducting gap for a charge-neutral CFL phase, making it more plausible in the interiors of compact stars, though the authors note that a full comparison of ground-state energies is needed.
