# $β$ decay and evolution of low-lying structure in Ge and As nuclei

> A simultaneous calculation for the shape evolution and the related spectroscopic properties of the low-lying states, and the $β$-decay properties in the even- and odd-mass Ge and As nuclei in the mass $A\approx70-80$ region, within the framework of the nuclear density functional theory and the particle-core coupling scheme, is presented. The constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using a universal energy density functional (EDF) and a pairing interaction determines the interacting-boson Hamiltonian for the even-even core nuclei, and the essential ingredients of the particle-boson interactions for the odd-nucleon systems, and of the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators. A rapid structural evolution from $γ$-soft oblate to prolate shapes, as well as the spherical-oblate shape coexistence around the neutron sub-shell closure $N=40$, is suggested to occur in the even-even Ge nuclei. The predicted low-energy spectra, electromagnetic transition rates, and $β$-decay $\log{ft}$ values are in a reasonable agreement with experiment. The predicted $\log{ft}$ values reflect the structures of the wave functions for the initial and final nuclei of $β$ decay, which are, to a large extent, determined by the microscopic input provided by the underlying EDF calculation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simultaneous calculation of shape evolution, low-lying spectroscopic properties, and β-decay properties for Ge and As nuclei in the A≈70–80 region, with the caveat that the predicted log ft values are in only "reasonable" (not exact) agreement with experiment and are largely determined by the microscopic input from the underlying energy density functional calculation.

## Method

The author performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework with the DD-PC1 functional and a separable pairing force to compute deformation energy surfaces for even-even 66–78Ge nuclei. These energy surfaces were then mapped onto an interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian for the even-even cores, and the particle-core coupling for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei was treated within the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2), with Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators constructed from the same mean-field input without additional phenomenological parameters.

## Key numbers

- The IBM-2 parameters for 66Ge include εd = 0.061 MeV, κ = −0.680 MeV, χν = −0.07, χπ = 0.30, and κ' = 0.015 MeV
- For 72Ge, the IBM-2 parameters are εd = 0.747 MeV, κ = −0.180 MeV, χν = 0.30, χπ = 0.30, and κ' = 0.050 MeV
- For 78Ge, the IBM-2 parameters are εd = 0.620 MeV, κ = −0.620 MeV, χν = −1.12, χπ = −0.87, and κ' = 0.000 MeV
- The boson effective charges used are eBν = eBπ = 0.0577 e b, chosen to reproduce the experimental B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) value for 72Ge
- The neutron and proton fermion effective charges are eFν = 0.5 e b and eFπ = 1.5 e b
- The strength parameters for the boson-fermion interaction in odd-N Ge nuclei are Γν = 0.3 MeV, Λν = 0.8 MeV, and Aν = −0.5 MeV
- The strength parameters for the residual neutron-proton interaction are vd = 0.8 MeV and vt = 0.02 MeV
- The neutron boson number Nν varies from 2 to 5 across 66–78Ge, while the proton boson number is fixed at Nπ = 2

## Context

This work extends the author's previous framework applied to β decays of odd-A and even-A Xe, Cs, Ba, and La nuclei in the A≈130 region (Nomura 2020) to the Ge-As region around N=40, which exhibits rapid shape evolution and shape coexistence. The results are consistent with earlier Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny-D1M EDF (Nomura 2017) for the same Ge nuclei, and the method enables future studies of double-β decay matrix elements for 76Ge, which is a candidate zero-neutrino double-β decay emitter.
