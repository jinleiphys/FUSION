# Longitudinal fluid response and pseudorapidity dependent flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the pseudorapidity dependent hydrodynamic response in heavy-ion collisions. A differential hydrodynamic relation is obtained for elliptic flow. Using event-by-event simulations of 3+1D MUSIC, with initial conditions generated via a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, the differential response relation is verified. Based on the response relation, we find that the two-point correlation of elliptic flow in pseudorapidity are separated into the fluid response and the two-point correlation of initial eccentricity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a differential, pseudorapidity-dependent linear response relation between elliptic flow \(V_2(\zeta)\) and initial eccentricity \(\E_2(\xi)\) in heavy-ion collisions, showing that the relation is non-local and that the increase in the two-point correlation length of elliptic flow compared to that of initial eccentricity is purely a fluid dynamic effect.

## Method

The authors generalized the standard linear response relation for elliptic flow to a pseudorapidity-dependent form, \(V_2(\zeta) = \int d\xi\, G(\zeta-\xi) \E_2(\xi)\), and expanded the response function in Fourier space as a gradient series. They performed event-by-event 3+1D MUSIC hydrodynamic simulations with initial conditions from the AMPT model for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV (30-40% centrality), using approximately 5000 events to extract the response coefficients \(G_n\) and about 1000 events to study two-point correlations.

## Key numbers

- The initial two-point correlation length of eccentricity is \(\bra (\Delta \xi)^2\ket = 12.81 \pm 0.08\).
- The two-point correlation length of elliptic flow \(\bra (\Delta \zeta)^2\ket\) is related to the initial correlation length by \(\bra (\Delta \zeta)^2\ket = \bra (\Delta \xi)^2\ket + 4G_2/G_0\).
- Hydrodynamic simulations used a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 0.08\) for the main verification, with additional simulations scanning \(\eta/s\) from 0.001 to 0.2.
- The absolute values of even-order response coefficients \(G_n\) increase exponentially, implying a finite radius of convergence \(k^*\) of the gradient expansion.

## Context

This work extends the well-established linear response relation between eccentricity and elliptic flow in relativistic hydrodynamics (Ollitrault, 1992; Qiu, 2011; Niemi, 2012; Noronha-Hostler, 2015) to include pseudorapidity dependence, which had been largely absent until recent studies (Li, 2019; Franco, 2019). The differential formulation differs from previous approaches (Bozek, 2015; Sakai, 2018) and is expected to be applicable to other flow harmonics when nonlinear response is not dominant. The results enable future studies of longitudinal flow correlations and the extraction of transport properties like \(\eta/s\) from pseudorapidity-dependent flow measurements.
