# High Tranverse Momentum Suppression in Au+Au Collisions

> Great interest has attached to recent Au+Au, E = 200 A GeV measurements at RHIC, obtained with the PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS and BRAHMS detectors. For central collisions and vanishing pseudo-rapidity all experiments indicate a considerable lowering in charged particle production at mid to large transverse momenta. In the PHENIX experiment similar behavior has been reported for pi0 spectra. In a recent work on the presumably simpler D+Au interaction, to be considered perhaps as a tune-up for Au+Au, we reported on a hadronic cascade mechanism which can explain the observed but somewhat reduced pT suppression at higher pseudo rapidity as well as the Cronin enhancement at mid rapidity. Here we report on the extension of this work to the more massive ion-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-transverse-momentum particles in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by a hadronic cascade mechanism, specifically, a two-stage process involving parallel initial nucleon-nucleon interactions followed by a final-state hadronic cascade, without requiring a quark-gluon plasma, provided that the final-state cascade begins early enough.

## Method

The authors used the LUCIFER Monte Carlo simulation code, which treats the collision in two stages. Stage I handles initial nucleon-nucleon interactions in parallel within geometrically defined groups, assigning transverse momentum via a collision-driven random walk and producing a "hot gas" of prehadrons (colorless quark-antiquark and three-quark objects with hadron-sized cross sections). Stage II is a final-state hadronic cascade where these prehadrons interact and decay into stable hadrons, with energy-momentum conservation enforced within each group. The high-transverse-momentum tails are included by fitting the NN input spectra to UA1 and PHENIX pp data at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at √s = 200 A GeV and η = 0, the simulated π⁰ transverse momentum spectrum shows suppression comparable to PHENIX data, with the production time τ_p set to either 2(2R_Au/Γ) or twice that value.
- The initial transverse energy densities at the start of stage II are ≤ 1.8 GeV/fm³ for the shortest initial time τ_p chosen.
- Approximately 90% of all final p⊥ yields occur for p⊥ ≤ 0.7 GeV/c.
- About 5% of the integrated meson spectrum comes from p⊥ ≥ 1 GeV.
- The prehadron mass distribution for non-strange mesonic prehadrons is centered at m₀ ≈ 500 MeV with width w = m₀/4; for strange prehadrons, m₀ ≈ 650 MeV.
- Prebaryons are restricted to a mass spectrum from m_N to 2 GeV.
- The meson-meson cross sections are scaled to 4/9 of the known NN cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier hadronic cascade analysis of D+Au collisions (Kahana and Kahana, 2004) to the more massive Au+Au system, where they previously explained the reduced pT suppression at higher pseudorapidity and the Cronin enhancement at midrapidity. The approach is closest in spirit to RQMD and work by Gallmeister, Greiner, and Xu (2004) as well as Cassing (2004), and it contrasts with purely partonic models (Boal, Eskola, Wang, Geiger, Bass) and gluon-saturation approaches. The results suggest that the observed suppression does not necessarily signal quark-gluon plasma formation, and that more definitive signs of QCD plasma must be sought, particularly given the large elliptical flow measurements that are most easily explained by hadronic cross sections.
