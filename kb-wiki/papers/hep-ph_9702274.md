# Thermal hadron production in pp and p{\bar p} collisions

> It is shown that the hadron production in high energy pp and p{\bar p} collisions, calculated by assuming that particles originate in hadron gas fireballs at thermal and partial chemical equilibrium, agrees very well with the data. The temperature of the hadron gas fireballs, determined by fitting hadron abundances, does not seem to depend on the centre of mass energy, having a nearly constant value of about 170 MeV. This value is in agreement with that obtained in e^+e^- collisions and supports a universal hadronization mechanism in all kinds of reactions consisting in a parton-hadron transition at critical values of temperature and pressure.

## Key claim
The paper claims that hadron production in high-energy pp and p-pbar collisions can be described by assuming particles originate from hadron gas fireballs at thermal and partial chemical equilibrium, with a temperature that remains nearly constant at about 170 MeV across different center-of-mass energies. The hedge is that this conclusion applies specifically to light-flavored hadrons, as heavy-flavored hadrons (charm and beauty) are produced primarily through perturbative QCD processes and show strong deviation from statistical equilibrium.

## Method
The authors developed a canonical statistical mechanics model where the final state of pp or p-pbar collisions consists of multiple fireballs, each with its own temperature, volume, and quantum numbers, subject to exact conservation of electric charge, baryon number, strangeness, charm, and beauty. They calculated average hadron multiplicities using a global partition function with a U(1)^5 symmetry group, incorporating a strangeness suppression factor gamma_s, and fitted the three free parameters (temperature T, global volume V, and gamma_s) to experimental data on hadron abundances at various center-of-mass energies. The model accounts for resonance decays and uses a Boltzmann approximation for all hadrons except pions, with a leading baryon effect correction for p-pbar collisions.

## Key numbers
- Temperature in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.4-27.6 GeV: T = 169.0 +/- 2.1 (+/- 3.4) MeV
- Temperature in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV: T = 170.2 +/- 11.8 MeV
- Strangeness suppression factor in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.4-27.6 GeV: gamma_s = 0.510 +/- 0.011 (+/- 0.025)
- Strangeness suppression factor in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV: gamma_s = 0.578 +/- 0.063
- Temperature in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 19.4-19.6 GeV: T = 190.8 +/- 27.4 MeV
- Temperature in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: T = 175.4 +/- 14.8 MeV
- Temperature in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 546 GeV: T = 181.7 +/- 17.7 MeV
- Global volume parameter VT^3 in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.4-27.6 GeV: 11.04 +/- 0.69 (+/- 1.4)
- Global volume parameter VT^3 in p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV: 43.2 +/- 11.8
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.4-27.6 GeV: 136.4/27

## Context
This work extends the thermodynamic model previously developed by Becattini for e+e- collisions (1995) to hadronic collisions, generalizing it by releasing earlier assumptions and including electric charge conservation. The approach builds on Hagedorn's original thermodynamic model from about thirty years prior and addresses the need for canonical (rather than grand-canonical) statistical mechanics due to the small size of collision systems. The finding of a nearly constant temperature around 170 MeV across different collision systems supports a universal hadronization mechanism involving a parton-hadron transition at critical temperature and pressure, and enables future analysis of transverse mass spectra and collective dynamical effects in hadronic collisions.
