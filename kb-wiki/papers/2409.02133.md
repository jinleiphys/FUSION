# Origins and impacts of dynamical diquark correlations -- A continuum Schwinger functional approach --

> This conference proceedings contribution emphasizes the emergent hadron mass paradigm, which accounts for the majority of the visible mass in the universe, beyond the Higgs boson mechanism. The study delves into the Landau gauge gluon propagator and the dynamical generation of gluon mass, as well as the dressed-quark propagator and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. It also tackles the baryon bound state problem through the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation, analyzing the composition and masses of octet and decuplet baryons. The document concludes with a discussion on the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and its first radial excitation, providing insights into the quark-diquark structure within baryons.

## Key claim

The emergent hadron mass (EHM) paradigm, rather than the Higgs boson mechanism, accounts for approximately 94% of the visible mass in the universe, with the Higgs boson contributing only about 1% directly to a nucleon's mass (9 MeV out of 940 MeV); however, the computed baryon masses from the quark-diquark Faddeev equation are uniformly larger than empirical values because they represent the dressed-quark core, with meson-baryon final-state interactions expected to reduce ground-state masses by roughly 0.23(6) GeV.

## Method

The study employs the continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) to solve the Dyson-Schwinger equations for the Landau gauge gluon propagator and dressed-quark propagator, incorporating dynamical gluon mass generation via the Schwinger mechanism and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB). The baryon bound state problem is addressed through the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation, which is transformed into a linear homogeneous matrix equation describing baryons as three-quark bound-states with soft, fully-interacting diquark correlations. The electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and its first radial excitation (Roper resonance) are computed using the resulting Faddeev amplitudes and a specified electromagnetic current that probes quarks and diquarks separately.

- Meson-baryon final-state interactions reduce ground-state octet and decuplet baryon masses and their first positive-parity excitations by roughly 0.23(6) GeV

## Context

This conference proceedings contribution extends the continuum Schwinger function method framework developed by Roberts (2021), Binosi (2022), Papavassiliou (2022), Ding (2022), Ferreira (2023), and Carman (2023) for emergent hadron mass. The work builds upon lattice-QCD simulations by Bogolubsky (2009), Bowman (2007), and Cucchieri (2009) that confirmed gluon mass generation, and upon the demonstration of the Schwinger mechanism by Aguilar (2008). The baryon spectrum calculations follow the approach of Chen (2019) for octet and decuplet baryons, and the electromagnetic form factor computations extend the work of Segovia (2013, 2014, 2015, 2016) and Chen (2018) and Cui (2020). The results enable future experimental tests of the dressed-quark core predictions using CLAS12 detector data anticipated out to Q^2 ~ 12 m_N^2 for Roper-resonance electro-production form factors, and provide a framework for distinguishing between competing theoretical hypotheses about the quark-quark interaction.
