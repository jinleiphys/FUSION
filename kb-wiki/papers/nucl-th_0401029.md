# Electromagnetic Form Factors in the hypercentral CQM

> We report on the recent results of the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM). The model contains a spin independent three-quark interaction which is inspired by Lattice QCD calculations and reproduces the average energy values of the SU(6) multiplets. The splittings are obtained with a SU(6)-breaking interaction, which can include also an isospin dependent term. Concerning Constituent Quark models, we have shown for the first time that the decreasing of the ratio of the elastic form factors of the proton is due to relativistic effects using relativistic corrections to the e.m. current and boosts. Now the elastic nucleon form factors have been recalculated, using a relativistic version of the hCQM and a relativistic quark current showing a very detailed reproduction of all the four form factor existing data over the complete range of 0-4 $GeV^2$. Futhermore, the model has been used for predictions concerning the electromagnetic transverse and longitudinal transition form factors giving a good description of the medium $Q^2$ behaviour. We show that the discrepancies in the reproduction of the helicity amplitudes at low $Q^2$ are due to pion loops. We have calculated the helicity amplitudes for all the 3 and 4 star resonances opening the possibility of application to the evaluation of cross sections.

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), when combined with a relativistic quark current and boosts, reproduces all four elastic nucleon form factors over the range 0-4 GeV², but only after introducing small constituent quark form factors to account for missing quark-antiquark degrees of freedom; the discrepancies in helicity amplitudes at low Q² are attributed specifically to pion loop contributions rather than to relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors used a hypercentral potential (linear plus Coulomb-like) for the three-quark interaction, fitted to the baryon spectrum with three free parameters, then calculated elastic nucleon form factors and helicity amplitudes for all 3- and 4-star resonances using a relativistic version of the hCQM with relativistic kinetic energy, boosts to the Breit frame, and a relativistic quark current. The helicity amplitudes were computed for transverse and longitudinal electromagnetic transitions, and chiral corrections from pion loops were added to address low-Q² discrepancies.

## Key numbers

- The hypercentral potential parameters are α = 1.61 fm⁻² and τ = 4.59, fitted to the baryon spectrum.
- The proton r.m.s. radius from the model is 0.48 fm.
- The elastic form factors are calculated over the complete Q² range of 0-4 GeV².
- For the Δ(1232) resonance, the pion cloud contributes about 50% to the transverse amplitudes A₁/₂ and A₃/₂ at low Q².
- The hyperfine interaction contributes only about 35% to the N-Δ mass difference, with the spin-isospin term contributing 50% and the isospin term 15%.
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q²)/G_M^p(Q²) deviates from unity by almost 50%, consistent with TJNAF data from Ped (year) and Gay (year).

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model previously developed by the same group (De Sanctis, Giannini, Santopinto, Vassallo) and builds on the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur and Karl) and the Capstick-Isgur model (Capstick and Isgur). The results enable calculations of cross sections for processes such as two-pion production at JLab (Ripani contribution) and demonstrate that chiral corrections from pion loops (Tiator contribution) are necessary to fully reproduce helicity amplitude data at low Q².
