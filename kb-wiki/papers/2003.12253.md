# Cross sections for 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering

> We study the processes $K\bar{K} \to ϕ$, $πD \to D^\ast$, $π\bar{D} \to \bar{D}^\ast$, and the production of $ψ(3770)$, $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$ mesons in collisions of charmed mesons or charmed strange mesons. The process of 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering involves a quark and an antiquark from the two initial mesons annihilating into a gluon and subsequently the gluon being absorbed by the spectator quark or antiquark. Transition amplitudes for the scattering process derive from the transition potential in conjunction with mesonic quark-antiquark wave functions and the relative-motion wave function of the two initial mesons. We derive these transition amplitudes in the partial wave expansion of the relative-motion wave function of the two initial mesons so that parity and total-angular-momentum conservation are maintained. We calculate flavor and spin matrix elements in accordance with the transition potential and unpolarized cross sections for the reactions using the transition amplitudes. Cross sections for the production of $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$ relate to nodes in their radial wave functions. We suggest the production of $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, and $ψ(4415)$ as probes of hadronic matter that results from the quark-gluon plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates unpolarized cross sections for 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering processes (including $K\bar{K} \to \phi$, $\pi D \to D^\ast$, and 21 reactions producing $\psi(3770)$, $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$) using a quark-antiquark annihilation mechanism, but the cross sections for $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$ production are related to nodes in their radial wave functions, and the authors suggest these specific production channels as probes of hadronic matter from quark-gluon plasma rather than claiming direct experimental measurement.

## Method

The authors derive transition amplitudes for 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering from four Feynman diagrams where a quark and an antiquark from the two initial mesons annihilate into a gluon which is then absorbed by a spectator quark or antiquark. They employ a partial wave expansion of the relative-motion wave function of the two initial mesons to enforce parity and total-angular-momentum conservation, then calculate flavor and spin matrix elements from the transition potentials and mesonic quark-antiquark wave functions in momentum space. Unpolarized cross sections are computed in the center-of-mass frame of the two initial mesons using the resulting transition amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering such as $\pi\pi \to \rho$ and $\pi K \to K^\ast$ by Xu et al. (2015) using the same quark-antiquark annihilation mechanism, and contrasts with elastic meson-meson scattering approaches like chiral perturbation theory, the inverse amplitude method (Hannah, 1999), and coupled-channel unitary approaches (Oller and Oset, 1997). The 21 reactions producing $\psi(3770)$, $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$ have not been studied theoretically before; the results enable future calculations of how these reactions contribute to $J/\psi$ production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, since these $\psi$ mesons may decay into $J/\psi$.
