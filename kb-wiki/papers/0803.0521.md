# Three-nucleon interactions: dynamics

> A discussion is presented of the dynamics underlying three-body nuclear forces, with emphasis on changes which occurred over several decades.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dynamics of three-nucleon forces have evolved through distinct conceptual phases, from classical electrodynamic analogies through early quantum field theory to modern chiral symmetry, and that the leading-order two-pion exchange three-nucleon potential (TPE-3NP) has a unique structure given by eq. (e.6) whose parameters are determined solely by empirical pion-nucleon subthreshold coefficients, not by chiral symmetry itself.

## Method

The author presents a historical and conceptual review of the theoretical development of three-nucleon forces, tracing the evolution from classical superposition principles through Primakoff and Holstein's 1939 quantum treatment, the pseudoscalar meson theory of the 1950s, the Fujita-Miyazawa off-shell pion-nucleon amplitude approach (1957), and finally the chiral symmetry framework including the linear sigma model and chiral perturbation theory. The paper compares the parameter values of different TPE-3NP potentials (Fujita-Miyazawa, Tucson-Melbourne, Brazil, TM') across five decades, showing how they relate to the same underlying structure.

## Key numbers

* Fujita-Miyazawa (1957) potential parameters: a = -0.27, b = -1.24, d = -0.31 (dimensionless units, pion mass m)
* Tucson-Melbourne (1979) potential parameters: a = 1.13, b = -2.58, c = -1.05, d = -0.75
* Brazil (1983) potential parameters: a = -1.05, b = -2.29, d = -0.77
* TM'-93 (2001) potential parameters: a = -0.74, b = -2.53, d = -0.72
* TM'-99 (2001) potential parameters: a = -1.12, b = -2.80, d = -0.75
* Pseudoscalar pion-nucleon scattering lengths from linear sigma model: (a_PS+, a_PS-) = (-1.84, 0.136) m^{-1}
* Experimental pion-nucleon scattering lengths: (a_exp+, a_exp-) = (-0.008, 0.092) m^{-1}
* Sigma-exchange contribution to scattering lengths: (a_s+, a_s-) = (1.83, 0) m^{-1}
* Chiral cancellation result: (a_PS+ + a_s+, a_PS- + a_s-) = (-0.01, 0.136) m^{-1}

## Context

This paper reviews the conceptual history of three-nucleon force dynamics, extending from Primakoff and Holstein (1939) through the Fujita-Miyazawa (1957) formulation that established the modern TPE-3NP framework, to the chiral symmetry era beginning with the linear sigma model of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960). The work contrasts the early pair-suppression approach of Brueckner and Watson (1953) with the later chiral symmetry resolution, and shows how the leading-order three-body force structure became unique through chiral perturbation theory as outlined by Weinberg (1992). The paper enables understanding of how next-to-leading order corrections, currently being calculated by Epelbaum, Meissner and collaborators (2007), will extend the framework beyond the leading-order TPE-3NP.
