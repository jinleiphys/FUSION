# Topological Defects in QCD at large Baryon Density and/or large $N_c$

> The main leitmotiv of the present talk is analysis of different topological objects such as strings and domain walls in QCD. As it is well known, the standard model in general (and QCD in particular) does not support any kind of such objects due to some simple topological arguments. However, the situation cardinally changes when one considers QCD in large $N_c$ limit or in the limit of large baryon density when the unique light $ﾎｷ'$ field emerges. I discuss $ﾎｷ'$- domain walls and $ﾎｷ'$ global strings which occur in both systems: in high density QCD and in QCD at large $N_c $. I also discuss the effects of quantum anomalies in the presence of topological objects in high-density QCD. The anomaly induced interactions lead to a number of interesting phenomena (such as the induced currents along the strings) which may have phenomenological consequences observable in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Topological defects such as $\eta'$ domain walls and $\eta'$ global strings exist in QCD under two specific regimes, large baryon density or large $N_c$, where the $\eta'$ field becomes light, contrary to the standard expectation that the Standard Model does not support such objects due to trivial homotopy groups.

## Method

The author constructs effective Lagrangians for the $\eta'$ Goldstone mode in two distinct limits: high-density QCD (color-flavor-locked phase) and large-$N_c$ QCD. In the high-density case, the instanton-induced potential is computed explicitly using the dilute instanton gas approximation, yielding a sine-Gordon model whose classical solutions include domain walls and strings. In the large-$N_c$ case, the same topological structure is argued from $1/N_c$ scaling of the $\eta'$ mass and the periodic $\theta$ dependence. The decay of domain walls via hole nucleation is analyzed semiclassically, and quantum anomalies are incorporated to derive induced currents on topological defects.

## Key numbers

- $\eta'$ mass in high-density QCD: $m = \sqrt{a/2} \, (\mu/f) \Delta$, where $a \sim (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/\mu)^{b}$ with $b = 11/3 N_c - 2/3 N_f$, $\mu$ is the chemical potential, $\Delta$ is the BCS gap, and $f \sim \mu$.
- Domain wall tension in high-density QCD: $\sigma = 8\sqrt{2a}\, u f \mu \Delta$, where $u$ is the velocity parameter.
- Domain wall decay rate in high-density QCD: $\Gamma \sim \exp\bigl(-\pi^4 u^3 f^4 / (3 a \mu^2 \Delta^2) \cdot \ln^3(1/\sqrt{a})\bigr)$, exponentially suppressed at high $\mu$.
- Domain wall tension in large-$N_c$ QCD: $\sigma = (2 N_c^2 / N_f) f_\pi^2 m_{\eta'} (1 - \cos(\pi/2 N_c)) \sim \sqrt{N_c}$.
- String tension in large-$N_c$ QCD: $\nu \sim N_c \log N_c$.
- Domain wall decay action in large-$N_c$ QCD: $S_0 \sim N_c^2 \log^3 N_c$, giving lifetime $\tau \sim \exp(N_c^2 \log^3 N_c)$.
- Axial current on a superfluid vortex in CFL phase: $J^A = \mu/(12\pi)$.
- Electromagnetic current on an axial vortex in CFL phase: $j^{\rm em} = e C_{\A\B\gamma} \mu/(2\pi)$, with $C_{\A\B\gamma} = \sum_i Q_i^5 Q_i$.
- Axial current on a magnetic flux in dense matter: $J^A = C_{\A\B\gamma} \mu/(8\pi)$.
- Magnetic moment per unit area of an axial domain wall in CFL phase: $e\mu/(6\pi)$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of topological defects in QCD from earlier studies of $\eta'$ strings and domain walls in high-density matter (Forbes and Zhitnitsky, 2000; Son and Stephanov, 2004) and in large-$N_c$ QCD (Forbes and Zhitnitsky, 2000). It also builds on the general framework of anomalies in dense matter (Kogut et al., 1999, 2000). The results enable phenomenological predictions for neutron star observables, including anomalous cooling via axial currents on vortices and magnetic fluxes, neutron star kicks, and the possible production of long-lived domain walls at RHIC (Shuryak, 2001). The anomaly-induced currents on vortices are reminiscent of Witten's superconducting strings (Witten, 1985).
