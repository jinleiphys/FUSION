# Effects of $ω$ meson self-coupling on the properties of finite nuclei and neutron stars

> The effects of $ω$ meson self-coupling (OMSC) on the properties of finite nuclei and neutron stars are investigated within the framework of effective field theory based relativistic mean-field (ERMF) model which includes the contributions from all possible mixed interactions between the scalar-isoscalar ($σ$), vector-isoscalar ($ω$) and vector-isovector ($ρ$) mesons upto the quartic order. For a realistic investigation, several parameter sets corresponding to different values of OMSC are generated by adjusting the remaining parameters of the ERMF model to fit the properties of the finite nuclei. Though, all these parameter sets give equally good fit to the properties of the finite nuclei, only moderate values of OMSC are favored from the "naturalness" point of view. The equation of state for the symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matters resulting from the parameter sets with the moderate values of OMSC are in close agreement with the ones obtained within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. For such parameter sets the limiting mass for the neutron stars composed of $β$-stable matter is $\sim 1.9M_\odot$. It is found that the direct Urca process can occur in the neutron stars with "canonical" mass of $1.4M_\odot$ only for the moderate and higher values of OMSC. Some other interesting properties for the neutron stars are also discussed.

# Effects of ω Meson Self-Coupling on the Properties of Finite Nuclei and Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The ω meson self-coupling (OMSC) parameter ζ significantly affects the high-density equation of state and neutron star properties, but only moderate values of ζ (∼0.03) are favored from the "naturalness" perspective when all mixed interaction terms up to quartic order are included and parameters are constrained by finite-nuclei data. The limiting neutron star mass varies between 2.3 M⊙ and 1.7 M⊙ for ζ from 0.0 to 0.06, but the authors note that this range is narrower than earlier work by Muller (1996) because the present study includes mixed interactions and constrains the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors employed an effective field theory based relativistic mean-field (ERMF) model including all possible self and mixed interactions between σ, ω, and ρ mesons up to quartic order. Seven parameter sets (FSUGZ00 through FSUGZ06) were generated for fixed values of the ω meson self-coupling ζ = 0.00, 0.01, ..., 0.06 by adjusting the remaining parameters to fit total binding energies and charge rms radii for closed-shell nuclei ranging from 16O to 208Pb, plus the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb, using a simulated annealing method for χ² minimization. The equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Limiting neutron star mass for moderate OMSC (ζ ∼ 0.03) is ∼1.9 M⊙
- Maximum mass varies from 2.3 M⊙ at ζ = 0.0 to 1.7 M⊙ at ζ = 0.06
- Radius for 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars decreases linearly from 13.2 km at ζ = 0.0 to 12.4 km at ζ = 0.06
- Radiation radius R∞ for canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars lies in the range 15.19 – 15.93 km
- Nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient K restricted to 220 – 240 MeV
- Rms errors in total binding energies for FSUGZ03 parameter set: 1.6 MeV; for NL3: 2.5 MeV; for FSUGold: 3.6 MeV
- Rms errors in charge rms radii: 0.03 fm (FSUGZ03), 0.02 fm (NL3), 0.03 fm (FSUGold)
- Direct Urca process can occur in 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars only for ζ ≳ 0.02
- Ratio of largest to smallest dimensionless parameters: 9.8 (FSUGZ00), 8.0 (FSUGZ03), 220.1 (FSUGZ06)

## Context

This work extends the ERMF studies of Muller (1996) by including all mixed interaction terms between σ, ω, and ρ mesons and by constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy through finite-nuclei data, particularly the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb. The results are compared with the FSUGold parametrization of Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005) and the NL3 set of Lalazissis (1997). The study enables more realistic predictions for neutron star maximum masses and cooling scenarios, as the occurrence of the direct Urca process depends sensitively on the OMSC parameter. The authors note that inclusion of hyperonic degrees of freedom would soften the equation of state further, with larger effects for smaller ζ values.
