# Higher partial waves in femtoscopy

> Femtoscopy is recently gaining more attention as a new approach complementary to scattering experiments for constraining hadron-hadron interactions. We discuss the effect of higher partial waves on the two-particle correlation function, which has been neglected in traditional formulae used in the femtoscopy analyses. We consider the partial-wave expansion of the wave function in the Koonin-Pratt formula to give the correction to the correlation function by a sum of the contributions from each partial wave. We also generalize the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula, which was originally derived for the s-wave interaction, for higher partial waves. We find a compact representation of the generalized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula given by the backward scattering amplitude $f(θ= π)$ and the Fourier-Laplace transform of the relative source function, which gives an insight into the structure of the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula and its relation to the optical theorem. We numerically demonstrate the significance of higher partial waves with resonances using the square potential well. Also, the generalized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula turned out to be broken for higher partial waves, which suggests the importance of the centrifugal force for the higher partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the standard Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula, when naively generalized to higher partial waves (l >= 1), fails to reproduce the exact Koonin-Pratt formula even qualitatively because the asymptotic wave function used in the LL formula misses the centrifugal-force suppression near the origin, which is essential for higher partial waves.

## Method

The authors perform a partial-wave expansion of the scattering wave function in the Koonin-Pratt formula to derive the correction to the two-particle correlation function as a sum over partial waves. They then generalize the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula by replacing the s-wave asymptotic wave function with the O(1/r) asymptotic form for each partial wave, and test this generalized formula against exact numerical solutions of the Schrodinger equation for a square potential well with various depths and source sizes.

## Key numbers

- The reduced mass used in numerical calculations is mu = 600 MeV, with potential width b = 1 fm and source size R = 2 fm for the baseline calculation.
- For a shallow potential (V0 = 50 MeV), the s-wave correction Delta C_0(q) crosses zero around q approximately 200 MeV, where higher partial waves can dominate.
- For deeper potentials, a p-wave (l=1) resonance peak appears at q approximately 90 MeV with V0 = 300 MeV, and a d-wave (l=2) resonance peak at q approximately 190 MeV with V0 = 600 MeV.
- The correction from each partial wave scales as q^(2l) in the limit q approaches 0, as shown by the log-log plot slopes matching 2l.
- The original LL paper concluded that the s-wave approximation works within 1% error for q less than or equal to 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the traditional femtoscopy formalism of Koonin (1977) and Pratt (1984) and the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula (1981), which assumed only s-wave interactions, to include contributions from all partial waves. The study is motivated by experimental observations such as the d-wave resonance Lambda(1520) peak at q approximately 240 MeV in K-p correlations from ALICE (2019) and peaks from higher partial waves in Lambda-K- correlations from ALICE (2023). The results enable systematic inclusion of resonant higher partial waves in femtoscopy analyses, though the generalized LL formula is shown to be inadequate for l >= 1, requiring instead the full Koonin-Pratt formula with spherical source for quantitative work.
