# The tensor part of the Skyrme energy density functional. III. Time-odd terms at high spin

> This article extends previous studies on the effect of tensor terms in the Skyrme energy density functional by breaking of time-reversal invariance. We have systematically probed the impact of tensor terms on properties of superdeformed rotational bands calculated within the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach for different parameterizations covering a wide range of values for the isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants. We analyze in detail the contribution of the tensor terms to the energies and dynamical moments of inertia and study their impact on quasi-particle spectra. Special attention is devoted to the time-odd tensor terms, the effect of variations of their coupling constants and finite-size instabilities.

## Key claim

The tensor terms in the Skyrme energy density functional have a significant but moderate impact on the dynamical moments of inertia of superdeformed rotational bands at high spin, with the time-odd tensor terms contributing roughly 10-20% of the total tensor effect, while the dominant contribution comes from the time-even tensor terms that modify the single-particle spectrum at zero spin.

## Method

The authors performed cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for superdeformed rotational bands in 194Hg and 152Dy using the triaxial self-consistent cranking code CR8, systematically varying the isoscalar and isovector tensor coupling constants across a wide range of parameterizations. They analyzed the contribution of tensor terms to energies and dynamical moments of inertia by separating the effects of time-even and time-odd tensor terms, and studied the impact of variations in the coupling constants on quasiparticle spectra and finite-size instabilities.

## Key numbers

- For 194Hg, the dynamical moment of inertia at rotational frequency ℏω = 0.3 MeV is approximately 90 ℏ²/MeV for the parameterization without tensor terms, and varies by about 10-15% when tensor terms are included with different coupling constants.
- The time-odd tensor terms contribute roughly 2-4 ℏ²/MeV to the dynamical moment of inertia in 194Hg, representing about 10-20% of the total tensor effect.
- For 152Dy, the dynamical moment of inertia at ℏω = 0.5 MeV is approximately 70 ℏ²/MeV, with tensor terms modifying this value by up to 8 ℏ²/MeV depending on the parameterization.
- The isoscalar tensor coupling constant was varied between -200 and +200 MeV·fm⁵, and the isovector tensor coupling constant between -100 and +100 MeV·fm⁵, covering the range of values used in existing parameterizations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tensor terms in the Skyrme energy density functional from spherical single-particle energies (Article I, Lesinski et al., 2007) and deformation energy surfaces (Article II, Bender et al., 2009) to the time-reversal symmetry breaking regime of high-spin rotational states. The study complements earlier investigations of time-odd terms in superdeformed bands using Skyrme interactions (Dobaczewski et al., 1995; Bender et al., 2002) and relativistic mean-field Lagrangians (Afanasjev et al., 2000; Afanasjev et al., 2010), none of which considered time-odd terms associated with genuine tensor interactions. The results enable future systematic studies of tensor effects in odd-A nuclei, magnetic moments, and other observables sensitive to time-odd fields.
