# Pi0 -> Gamma Gamma in Dense QCD

> QCD superconductors in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase support light excitations (generalized pions) in the form of particle-particle or hole-hole excitations. We analyze the generalized process $\tildeπ^0\to\tildeγ\tildeγ$ in the weak coupling limit and show that it is related to the recently suggested Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term. In dense QCD, the radiative decay of the generalized pion is constrained by geometry and vanishes at large density.

## Key claim

The radiative decay of the generalized pion in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD is related to the generalized Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term, but the decay amplitude vanishes at large density (asymptotically as 1/μ), contrary to a naive expectation that the decay rate would increase substantially in dense matter.

## Method

The authors performed a direct weak-coupling calculation of the triangle diagram for the process \(\tilde\pi^0 \to \tilde\gamma\tilde\gamma\) in the CFL phase using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for gapped quarks. They evaluated the leading-order contributions from particle-particle, hole-hole, and antiparticle excitations, integrating over the Fermi surface in the limit of large quark chemical potential μ, and compared the result to the prediction from the generalized WZW term proposed by Hong, Rho, and Zahed (2000).

## Key numbers

* The temporal pion decay constant in the CFL phase is \(F_T = \mu/\pi\).
* The gap function to leading logarithm accuracy is \(G_0 \approx (4\Lambda_\perp^6 / \pi m_E^5) \exp(-\sqrt{3}\pi / 2h_*)\), where \(\Lambda_\perp = 2\mu\) and \(m_E = \sqrt{N_F/(2\pi^2)} g\mu\).
* The integral \({\cal I}_1 = \int d^4q/(2\pi)^4 \, G^2(q)/\Delta^2(q) = i\mu^2/(8\pi^2)\), proportional to the square of the temporal pion decay constant.
* The resulting amplitude for \(\tilde\pi^0 \to \tilde\gamma\tilde\gamma\) is proportional to \(1/F_T = \pi/\mu\), so the decay vanishes as \(1/\mu\) at large density.
* The color-flavor anomaly in the CFL phase is reduced by a factor of 1/3 compared to the usual QCD vacuum anomaly, due to color-flavor locking in the triangle graph.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Goldstone modes in the CFL phase by Hong, Rho, and Zahed (2000) and the bound-state calculations of Rho, Wirzba, and Zahed (1999) and Rho, Schäfer, Wirzba, and Zahed (1999). It provides a direct weak-coupling verification that the generalized WZW term correctly describes the anomalous decay of the generalized pion, and shows that the decay vanishes at asymptotic densities rather than growing. The result enables further calculations of anomalous and nonanomalous emission rates in dense QCD, relevant for neutron star physics.
