# Equation of State of Hot Neutron Star Matter using Finite Range Simple Effective Interaction

> The equation of state of hot neutron star matter of n+p+e+$μ$ composition in $β$-equilibrium is studied for both neutrino-free isothermal and neutrino-trapped isentropic conditions, using the formalism where the thermal evolution is built upon its zero-temperature predictions in a self-consistent manner. The accuracy of the parabolic approximation, often used in the finite temperature calculation of hot neutron star matter, is verified by comparing with the results obtained from the exact evaluation in the neutrino-free neutron star matter. The equation of state of neutrino-trapped isentropic matter at low entropic condition, relevant to the core-collapsing supernovae, is formulated. In the isentropic matter, the particle fractions and equation of state have marginal variance as entropy per particle varies between 1 to 3 (in the unit of k$_B$), but the temperature profile shows marked variation. The isentropes are found to be much less sensitive to the nuclear matter incompressibility, but have a large dependence on the slope parameter L. The bulk properties of the neutron stars predicted by the isentropic equation of state for different entropy are calculated. A model calculation for the early stage evolution of the protoneutron star to neutron star configuration is also given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the parabolic approximation (PA) for the nuclear equation of state, which approximates the free symmetry energy as the difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, remains valid at finite temperature for hot neutron star matter, with the caveat that this validity is demonstrated specifically for the finite range simple effective interaction with Yukawa form factor (SEI-Y) and has not been explicitly verified for other interactions in the finite temperature domain.

## Method

The authors use the finite range simple effective interaction with Yukawa form factor (SEI-Y) to compute the equation of state of hot neutron star matter with n+p+e+mu composition in beta-equilibrium. They perform a self-consistent iterative evaluation of the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions for neutrons and protons at finite temperature, solving the coupled equations for single-particle energies and chemical potentials until convergence within 0.01 percent. The exact evaluation of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature is compared with the parabolic approximation, where the free symmetry energy is obtained from separate calculations of pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter. For neutrino-trapped isentropic conditions, the beta-equilibrium condition includes neutrino chemical potentials, and the equation of state is computed for fixed entropy per particle.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.16103 fm^-3, with energy per particle e(rho_0) = -16 MeV and incompressibility K = 253.6 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation E_s(rho_0) = 30 MeV, with slope parameter L = 75.0 MeV and symmetry incompressibility K_sym = -37.159 MeV
- Muon production threshold at rest mass energy m_mu c^2 = 105.658 MeV, with the threshold density shifting to lower values as temperature increases
- For isentropic matter, entropy per particle varies between 1 to 3 (in units of k_B), with marginal variance in particle fractions and equation of state but marked variation in temperature profile
- At T = 50 MeV, the parabolic approximation slightly overestimates particle fractions compared to exact calculations over the whole density range

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the finite range simple effective interaction (SEI) for cold and hot neutron star matter (Behera 2002, Behera 2009, Behera 2011, Routray 2023, Routray 2024) by performing the first explicit verification of the parabolic approximation at finite temperature, which had been assumed valid without verification in many prior models including those using MDI interactions (Moustakidis 2021) and relativistic approaches (Nicotra 2006, Burgio 2007). The results enable more reliable modeling of protoneutron star evolution and core-collapse supernova conditions, and the authors note that their SEI-Y predictions for particle fractions at T=0 show quantitatively good agreement with the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formulation of Burgio and Schulze (2010).
