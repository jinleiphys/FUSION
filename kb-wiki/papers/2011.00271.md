# A study of nuclear radii and neutron skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line

> We studied the charge radius ($r_{c}$), neutron radius ($r_n$), and neutron skin-thickness ($Δr=r_n-r_p$) over a chain of isotopes from C to Zr with the stable region to the neutron drip line. Theoretical calculations are done with axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) with effective nonlinear NL3 and NL3* interactions. The theoretically estimated values are compared with available experimental data and a reasonable agreement are noted. We additionally assessed the two-neutron separation energy ($S_{2n}$) to mark the drip line nuclei of the considered isotopic series. In the reference of $S_{2n}$, neutron magicity is also discussed. The calculated neutron radii are compared with empirical estimation made by $r=r_0N^{1/3}$ to examine the abnormal trend of the radius for neutron drip line nuclei. In view to guide the long tails, the density distribution for some skin candidates is analyzed. Finally, neutron skin thickness is observed for the whole considered isotopic series.

# A study of nuclear radii and neutron skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron drip line

## Key claim
The authors claim that axially deformed relativistic mean-field theory with NL3 and NL3* interactions can reasonably reproduce available experimental charge radii and binding energies across isotopic chains from carbon to zirconium, but the predicted two-neutron drip line positions are model-dependent and differ substantially from both experimental data and other theoretical approaches such as spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) and the finite range droplet model (FRDM).

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3 and NL3* effective interactions for even-even, even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd nuclei from carbon to zirconium isotopes. They solved the coupled Dirac and meson field equations in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 14 fermion and 14 boson shells, using constant-gap BCS pairing with gaps from the Madland-Nix formula and Pauli blocking for odd systems. From the resulting densities they computed charge radii, neutron radii, neutron skin thickness, binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, and quadrupole deformation parameters.

## Key numbers
- The two-neutron drip line nucleus for oxygen is predicted as $^{29}$O with $S_{2n}=0.394$ MeV (NL3) and $S_{2n}=0.499$ MeV (NL3*), whereas the experimentally observed drip nucleus is $^{26}$O.
- For carbon, the predicted drip line nucleus is $^{23}$C with $S_{2n}=2.009$ MeV (NL3) and $S_{2n}=1.954$ MeV (NL3*), compared to the RCHB prediction of $^{24}$C.
- The neutron skin thickness at the drip line for $^{23}$C is 0.934 fm (NL3) and 0.945 fm (NL3*); for $^{135}$Zr it is 0.820 fm (NL3) and 0.968 fm (NL3*).
- For $^{28}$O, the calculated charge radius is 2.832 fm (NL3) and 2.837 fm (NL3*), compared to 2.903 fm from RCHB.
- The charge radius for $^{135}$Zr is 4.643 fm from the continuum method, while the present RMF calculations give values near 4.097 fm and 4.096 fm for $^{95}$Fe.
- The saturation density for NL3 is 0.148 fm$^{-3}$ and for NL3* is 0.15 fm$^{-3}$; the symmetry energy at saturation is 37.4 MeV (NL3) and 38.68 MeV (NL3*), with slope parameters L of 118.65 MeV and 122.63 MeV respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous RMF studies of neutron-rich nuclei by Agbemava (2014) and the spherical RCHB calculations of Xia (2018) to include axially deformed calculations for all parity combinations (even-even, odd-even, even-odd, odd-odd) across the C-to-Zr isotopic chains. The results are compared with the spherical RCHB method of Xia (2018) using PC-PK1, the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM of Moller, and experimental data from Wang (2017). The predicted neutron drip lines differ significantly between models, highlighting that drip line predictions remain interaction- and model-dependent. The calculated neutron skin thicknesses and density distributions for skin candidates provide input for constraining the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is linked to neutron star structure.
