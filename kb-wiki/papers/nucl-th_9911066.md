# alpha-decay chains of Z=114, A=289 and Z=118, A=293 in the relativistic mean-field model

> A comparison of calculated and experimental Q_alpha values of superheavy even-even nuclei and a few selected odd-N nuclei is presented in the framework of the relativistic mean-field model with the parameterization NL-Z2. Blocking effects are found to be important for a proper description of Q_alpha of odd mass nuclei. The model gives a good overall description of the available experimental data. The mass and charge assignment of the recently measured decay chains from Dubna and Berkeley is in agreement with the predictions of the model. The analysis of the new data does not allow a final conclusion about the location of the expected island of spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei.

# alpha-decay chains of Z=114, A=289 and Z=118, A=293 in the relativistic mean-field model

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field model with parameterization NL-Z2 gives a good overall description of the measured Q_alpha values for the decay chains assigned to 289_114 and 293_118, but the available experimental data do not allow a final conclusion about the location of the expected island of spherical doubly-magic superheavy nuclei. The relatively small Q_alpha values and corresponding long half-lives are attributed to deformed Z=114 and N=174 shell closures rather than the vicinity of the potential doubly-magic 298_184_114 predicted by some other models.

## Method

The author performed axially symmetric relativistic mean-field calculations on a grid assuming reflection symmetry, using the NL-Z2 parameterization. Nuclei with odd mass number were treated in a self-consistent blocking approximation including time-odd contributions to the Dirac equation, with pairing correlations handled within the BCS scheme using a delta pairing force. Calculated Q_alpha values were compared with experimental data for the decay chains of 277_165_112 (from GSI), 289_175_114 (from Dubna), and 293_175_118 (from Berkeley), as well as with predictions from the FRDM+FY, YPE+WS, and SHF+SLy4 models.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty in calculated Q_alpha values from variation of pairing recipes is on the order of a few 100 keV, especially in odd nuclei.
- Ground-state-to-ground-state Q_alpha values of odd-N nuclei may deviate on the order of 500 keV from the systematics of even-even nuclei due to blocking effects.
- Corrections for spurious rotational and vibrational motions, which are neglected, might affect Q_alpha values in some cases to the order of 1 MeV.
- The deformed N=162 shell closure is already confirmed experimentally, and NL-Z2 reproduces the large jump in Q_alpha between 273_163_110 and 269_161_108 caused by this shell.
- Transition energies from the first excited state to the band head in odd-N nuclei in this region are below 50 keV.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of experimental superheavy element decay data with self-consistent mean-field models, following a detailed study by Cwiok et al. (1999) using the Skyrme interaction SLy4. The RMF with NL-Z2 predicts the next spherical doubly-magic nucleus at 292_172_120, in contrast to macroscopic-microscopic models like the Yukawa-plus-exponential model with Woods-Saxon potentials (Smolanczuk, 1997) or the finite-range droplet model with Folded-Yukawa potentials (Moller et al., 1997) which predict 298_184_114, and the SLy4 interaction which shifts the island to around 310_184_126. The analysis enables further discrimination among nuclear models in the superheavy region but cannot yet resolve the location of the island of stability.
