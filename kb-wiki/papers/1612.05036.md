# $p_t$-Angular power spectrum in ALICE events

> We study the particles emitted in the fireball following a Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision with the traditional angular analysis employed in cosmology and earth sciences, producing Mollweide plots of the p_t distribution of a few actual, publically released ALICE-collaboration events and calculating their angular power spectrum. With the limited statistics at hand, we do not find evidence for acoustic peaks but a decrease of C_l that is reminiscent of viscous attenuation, but subject to a strong effect from the rapidity acceptance which probably dominates (so we also subtract the m=0 component). As an exercise, we still extract a characteristic Silk damping length (proportional to the square root of the viscosity over entropy density ratio). The absence of acoustic-like peaks is also compatible with a crossover from the QGP to the hadron gas (because a surface tension at domain boundaries would effect a restoring force that could have driven acoustic oscillations). Presently an unexpected depression appears in the l=6 multipole strength, which should be revisited by the ALICE collaboration with full statistics to confirm or discard it.

## Key claim

The paper applies cosmological angular power spectrum analysis to transverse momentum ($p_t$) distributions in individual ALICE heavy-ion collision events, but with the limited statistics of 186 publically released events, it finds no evidence for acoustic peaks; the observed decrease of $C_l$ is reminiscent of viscous attenuation but is dominated by the rapidity acceptance effect, and an unexpected depression at $l=6$ requires full-statistics ALICE data to confirm or discard.

## Method

The authors performed spherical harmonic transforms of the normalized transverse momentum fluctuation function $(p_t - \langle p_t \rangle)/\langle p_t \rangle$ over the sphere, using the SHTOOLS software package. They produced Mollweide projections of individual and combined ALICE events from Pb-Pb collisions at 2.7 TeV, applied a quadrupole alignment procedure using the event-by-event $Q$-vector defined by $\sum p_{t,j}(\cos 2\phi_j, \sin 2\phi_j)$ to orient events, and subtracted the $m=0$ component to isolate the effect of the rapidity acceptance cut.

## Key numbers

- 186 publically released ALICE events from Pb-Pb collisions at 2.7 TeV were analyzed, containing 87623 charged particle tracks total
- The TPC rapidity coverage is limited to $\theta \in [44.25^\circ, 135.7^\circ]$ (approximately one unit of rapidity around the equatorial plane), with support bars every $20^\circ$ in azimuth spanning $2^\circ$
- The ITS covers rapidity in $(-1.95, 1.95)$, corresponding to latitude $(-73.8^\circ, 73.8^\circ)$
- Individual high-multiplicity events contain $O(10^3)$ particles, limiting the maximum multipole reach to $l = O(80-90)$ when combining all events
- The angular spectrum $C_l$ stabilizes at $O(10^{-4})$ after the rapid falloff from the rapidity acceptance cut
- The monopole $C_0$ for the unit function over the full sphere would be $4\pi \simeq 12.56$

## Context

This work extends the analogy between cosmological CMB angular analysis and relativistic heavy-ion collisions previously explored by Naselsky (2012), Basu (2014), Rafelski (2013), Dobado (2015), and Kuznetsova (2010). It applies the spherical harmonic transform method to actual ALICE public data rather than Monte Carlo simulations, following the two-particle $p_t$ correlation angular spectrum work of Mocsy and Sorensen (2010) from STAR data. The absence of acoustic peaks is interpreted as compatible with a crossover from QGP to hadron gas (since surface tension at domain boundaries would be needed for restoring force to drive oscillations), and the analysis enables future full-statistics studies by the ALICE collaboration to confirm or discard the $l=6$ depression and extract transport coefficients such as the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio via Silk damping length extraction.
