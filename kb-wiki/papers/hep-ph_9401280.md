# Relativistic Quark Model Based Description of Low Energy NN Scattering

> A description of the NN scattering is constructed starting from the $π, η, η'$ pseudoscalar-, the $ρ, ϕ, ω$ vector-, and the $\varepsilon(600), a_0, f_0(1400)$ scalar - meson-nucleon coupling constants, which we obtain within a relativistic quark model. Working within the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze quasipotential dynamics we thus describe the NN phase shifts in a relativistically invariant way. In this procedure we use the phenomenological form factor cutoff masses and the effective $\varepsilon$ and $ω$ meson-nucleon coupling constants, only. The comparison of our NN phase shifts to the both empirical data and the Bonn OBEP fit shows good agreement -- the ratio of the $χ^2$ for the present results to the $χ^2$ for Bonn OBEP description is 1.2.

## Key claim

The authors construct a one-boson exchange (OBE) model of nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering using meson-nucleon coupling constants predicted by a relativistic quark confinement model (QCM), but they must treat the effective epsilon and omega meson-nucleon coupling constants and all form factor cutoff masses as free phenomenological parameters to fit the NN phase shifts, meaning the QCM predictions alone are insufficient without additional tuning.

## Method

The authors use the Blankenbecler-Sugar-Logunov-Tavkhelidze quasipotential equation to describe NN scattering in a relativistically invariant way, constructing an OBE model (QRBA9) that includes pseudoscalar (pi, eta, eta'), vector (rho, phi, omega), and scalar (epsilon(600), a0, f0(1400)) meson exchanges. They calculate the meson-nucleon coupling constants within a relativistic quark confinement model (QCM) that parameterizes quark confinement through entire analytical functions, then optimize the effective epsilon and omega coupling constants and all form factor cutoff masses by fitting to NN phase shift data up to 450 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of chi-squared for the QRBA9 model to chi-squared for the Bonn OBEP(B) model is 1.23 (or 1.80 when including the coupled isobar channels for the 1D2 and 3F3 partial waves)
- QCM prediction for the piNN coupling constant g^2_pi/4pi is close to 13.65, which is the minimal value needed to describe the deuteron quadrupole moment in the Bonn OBEP
- QCM predicts kappa_rho = 3.66 for the ratio of Pauli to Dirac coupling constants of the rho meson, consistent with the empirical value of 3.7 from vector meson dominance
- QCM predicts form factor cutoff masses Lambda_pi = 0.88 GeV and Lambda_rho = 0.60 GeV, with a ratio of 1.477 compared to the QRBA9 model ratio of 1.494
- The mixing parameter epsilon_1 at 50 MeV is predicted to be just below 2.6 degrees, consistent with the Basel group value of 2.9 +/- 0.3 degrees and the KSS92 value of 2.2 +/- 0.5 degrees

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quark confinement model developed by Efimov, Ivanov, and Lyubovitskij (1990s) from static hadron properties and decay widths to the NN scattering problem, using the same QCM parameter set that successfully predicted baryon magnetic moments and weak coupling constants. The results are compared to the Bonn OBEP(B) model of Machleidt (1989) and the Nijmegen group analyses, with the QCM predictions for the piNN coupling constant falling between the newer lower values from Arndt (1991) and the traditional value of 14.28. The approach enables a microscopic interpretation of NN scattering that preserves relativistic invariance, in contrast to nonrelativistic quark models such as those by Oka and Yazaki (1981) or Shimizu (1989), though the effective epsilon and omega couplings still require phenomenological adjustment to account for correlated two-pion exchange and short-range repulsion.
