# Speed of sound peak in isospin QCD: a natural prediction of the Medium Separation Scheme

> We present predictions for the zero-temperature equation of state at finite isospin density using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model within the medium separation scheme (MSS) -- a scheme that explicitly disentangles medium effects from the ultraviolet divergent vacuum terms. Recent lattice QCD results reveal a nonmonotonic speed of sound ($c_s^2$) as a function of isospin chemical potential ($μ_I$), exhibiting explicit violation of the conformal bound $c_s^2 = 1/3$. These findings have attracted significant theoretical interest, as established models -- including the NJL model -- failed to anticipate this behavior prior to lattice simulations. Conventional NJL implementations yield unphysical artifacts, often attributed to regularization scale sensitivity stemming from nonrenormalizability. However, in this work, we demonstrate that the standard NJL framework combined with MSS quantitatively reproduces state-of-the-art lattice data for isospin QCD.

## Key claim

The standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, when combined with the medium separation scheme (MSS) that explicitly disentangles medium effects from ultraviolet divergent vacuum terms, quantitatively reproduces state-of-the-art lattice QCD data for the speed of sound in isospin-dense matter, including the nonmonotonic peak that conventional NJL implementations fail to capture due to regularization artifacts.

## Method

The authors compute the zero-temperature equation of state for two-flavor and three-flavor NJL models at finite isospin chemical potential in the mean-field approximation, introducing both a chiral condensate and a pion condensate. They apply the medium separation scheme (MSS), which separates the thermodynamic potential into vacuum divergent terms (regularized with a momentum cutoff) and finite medium contributions (integrated without restrictions), in contrast to the traditional regularization scheme (TRS) where the cutoff is applied directly to all momentum integrals including those containing medium-dependent dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound peak from lattice QCD in Brandt et al. (2023) is located at $\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 1.6$ with a maximum value $c_s^2 \approx 0.56$
- The speed of sound peak from lattice QCD in Abbott et al. (2023, 2025) is located at $\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 2.5$ with a similar maximum height
- The conformal bound is $c_s^2 = 1/3$, which the speed of sound exceeds in the peak region
- Pion condensation occurs when $\mu_I > m_\pi$, where $m_\pi$ is the pion mass

## Context

This work extends recent lattice QCD results from Brandt et al. (2023) and Abbott et al. (2023, 2025) that first revealed the nonmonotonic speed of sound peak in isospin-dense matter, contradicting earlier effective model predictions. The MSS approach had been previously developed and applied to other dense matter phenomenologies by Farias et al. (2006, 2016) and Duarte et al. (2019). The results enable reliable use of the NJL model for studying isospin-asymmetric matter relevant to neutron star physics, and provide a benchmark for other approaches such as the quark-meson model studied by Chiba and Kojo (2024) and the nonlocal NJL model by Carlomagno et al. (2024).
