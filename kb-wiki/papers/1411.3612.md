# Three-nucleon force at large distances: Insights from chiral effective field theory and the large-N_c expansion

> We confirm the claim of Ref. [D.R. Phillips, C. Schat, Phys. Rev. C88 (2013) 3, 034002] that 20 operators are sufficient to represent the most general local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force and derive explicit relations between the two sets of operators suggested in Refs. [D.R. Phillips, C. Schat, Phys. Rev. C88 (2013) 3, 034002] and [H. Krebs, A.M. Gasparyan, E. Epelbaum, Phys.Rev. C87 (2013) 5, 054007]. We use the set of 20 operators to discuss the chiral expansion of the long- and intermediate-range parts of the three-nucleon force up to next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the standard formulation without explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. We also address implications of the large-N_c expansion in QCD for the size of the various three-nucleon force contributions.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that 20 operators (not 22) are sufficient to generate the 80 independent structures of a local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force, correcting the earlier claim of 22 operators in Krebs et al. (2013) and reconciling it with the 80-structure result of Phillips and Schat (2013). The chiral expansion of the long- and intermediate-range three-nucleon force at N⁴LO appears to converge only at distances beyond about 2.5 fm, with N⁴LO corrections being considerably smaller than N³LO ones only at such large separations.

## Method

The authors derived explicit linear relations showing that 9 of the 89 operators from Krebs et al. (2013) are redundant, reducing the independent operator count to 80 generated by 20 basis operators. They then computed the structure functions for the 2π-exchange, 2π-1π-exchange, and ring topologies in coordinate space up to N⁴LO in chiral effective field theory without explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom, using the equilateral triangle configuration (r₁₂ = r₂₃ = r₃₁ ≡ r) for visualization. The large-N_c expansion in QCD was applied to estimate the relative sizes of different three-nucleon force contributions.

## Key numbers

- 20 operators generate the 80 independent structures of a local isospin-invariant three-nucleon force (reduced from the previous 22 operators and 89 structures in Krebs et al. 2013)
- At relative distance r = 2 fm, the strongest potentials ℱ₁₅, ℱ₁₆, and ℱ₁₇ have strengths of 440 keV, -450 keV, and -440 keV at N²LO, reducing to 170 keV, 14 keV, and -90 keV at N⁴LO
- Low-energy constants used: c₁ = -0.75 GeV⁻¹, c₂ = 3.49 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -4.77 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.34 GeV⁻¹, with pion mass M_π = 138 MeV, pion decay constant F_π = 92.4 MeV, and nucleon axial vector coupling g_A = 1.285
- Additional LECs: \bar e₁₄ = -1.52 GeV⁻³ and \bar e₁₇ = -0.37 GeV⁻³

## Context

This work reconciles the operator counting of Krebs et al. (2013) with that of Phillips and Schat (2013), who found 80 independent structures for a local three-nucleon force using large-N_c methods. The corrected operator basis and numerical results supersede the coordinate-space structure functions plotted in Figures 4-8 of Krebs et al. (2013), which were affected by both the operator redundancy and a numerical error in the Fourier transformation of the 2π-exchange contribution. The results enable future few- and many-body calculations using the complete N⁴LO three-nucleon force, with preliminary nucleon-deuteron scattering studies by Witala (2013) and Golak (2014) already underway.
