# Neutron star crustal properties from relativistic mean-field models and bulk parameters effects

> We calculate crustal properties of neutron stars, namely, mass ($M_{\rm crust}$), radius ($R_{\rm crust}$) and fraction of moment of inertia ($ΔI/I$) from parametrizations of hadronic relativistic mean-field (RMF) model consistent with symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter constraints, as well as some stellar boundaries. We verify which one are also in agreement with restrictions of $ΔI/I \geqslant 1.4\%$ and $ΔI/I \geqslant 7\%$ related to the glitching mechanism observed in pulsars, such as the Vela one. The latter constraint explains the glitches phenomenon when entrainment effects are taken into account. Our findings indicate that these parametrizations pass in the glitching limit for a neutron star mass range of $M\leqslant 1.82M_\odot$ ($ΔI/I \geqslant 1.4\%$), and $M\leqslant 1.16M_\odot$ ($ΔI/I \geqslant 7\%$). We also investigate the influence of nuclear matter bulk parameters on crustal properties and find that symmetry energy is the quantity that produces the higher variations on $M_{\rm crust}$, $R_{\rm crust}$, and~$ΔI/I$. Based on the results, we construct a particular RMF parametrization able to satisfy $ΔI/I \geqslant 7\%$ even at $M=1.4M_\odot$, the mass value used to fit data from the softer component of the Vela pulsar X-ray spectrum. The model also presents compatibility with observational data from PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, and MSP J0740+6620 pulsars, as well as, with data from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) mission.

## Key claim

The authors find that relativistic mean-field (RMF) parametrizations previously constrained by nuclear matter and stellar boundaries can satisfy the glitch-related moment-of-inertia fraction constraint of ΔI/I ≥ 7% only for neutron star masses M ≤ 1.16M⊙, but by tuning bulk parameters (particularly symmetry energy) they construct a new RMF parametrization that satisfies this stricter constraint even at M = 1.4M⊙, the mass used for the Vela pulsar’s softer X-ray component.

## Method

The authors used 11 RMF parametrizations previously selected for satisfying symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter constraints and stellar boundaries, and computed crustal mass (Mcrust), crustal radius (Rcrust), and crustal fraction of moment of inertia (ΔI/I) via the Zdunik approximation without a full crust equation of state. They then systematically varied the bulk parameters (incompressibility K0, effective mass m*, symmetry energy J, and symmetry energy slope L0) around a baseline RMF model to isolate each parameter’s effect on crustal properties, and finally constructed a new RMF parametrization by adjusting these bulk parameters to meet the ΔI/I ≥ 7% constraint at M = 1.4M⊙.

## Key numbers

- For the 11 selected RMF parametrizations, the ΔI/I ≥ 1.4% constraint is satisfied for neutron star masses M ≤ 1.82M⊙, with the G2* model defining this upper boundary.
- For the stricter ΔI/I ≥ 7% constraint (including entrainment effects), the allowed mass range is M ≤ 1.16M⊙, with the BSR12 model setting the upper limit.
- At M = 1.4M⊙, the crust contains approximately 1.4% (G2*) to 2.6% (BSR12) of the total neutron star mass, and the crust contributes 7.1% (G2*) to 9.2% (BSR12) of the total radius.
- The newly constructed RMF parametrization uses bulk parameters: ρ0 = 0.15 fm⁻³, B0 = -16.0 MeV, m* = 0.575, K0 = 260 MeV, J = 35 MeV, and L0 = 70 MeV.
- This new model is compatible with observational mass data: PSR J1614-2230 (M = 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙), PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙), and MSP J0740+6620 (M = 2.14⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀.₁₈ M⊙ at 95.4% credible level), and with NICER data (M = 1.44⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀.₁₄ M⊙ with R = 13.02⁺¹·²⁴₋₁.₀₆ km).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of RMF parametrizations constrained by nuclear matter properties (Dutra et al., 2014) and stellar boundaries (Lourenço et al., 2016) by testing them against glitch constraints from the Vela pulsar, specifically the ΔI/I ≥ 1.4% limit from Link et al. (1999) and the stricter ΔI/I ≥ 7% limit from Chamel (2012, 2013) that includes entrainment effects. The analysis of bulk parameter effects on crustal properties, showing symmetry energy J as the dominant driver, enables the construction of a new RMF parametrization that simultaneously satisfies the ΔI/I ≥ 7% glitch constraint at M = 1.4M⊙ and is compatible with mass-radius observations from pulsars PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, MSP J0740+6620, and NICER data.
