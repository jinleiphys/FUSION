# Properties of the predicted super-deformed band in ^{32}S

> Properties like the excitation energy with respect to the ground state, moments of inertia, B(E2) transition probabilities and stability against quadrupole fluctuations at low spin of the predicted superdeformed band of ^{32}S are studied with the Gogny force D1S using the angular momentum projected generator coordinate method for the axially symmetric quadrupole moment. The Self Consistent Cranking method is also used to describe the superdeformed rotational band. In addition, properties of some collective normal deformed states are discussed.

## Key claim

The predicted super-deformed (SD) band in \(^{32}S\) is stable against quadrupole fluctuations at low spin, despite the SD minimum in the mean-field energy surface being very shallow; the excitation energy of the SD band head is reduced to 8.87 MeV (8.56 MeV with a larger basis) when angular momentum projection and quadrupole mixing are included, compared to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) result of 9.85 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the Angular Momentum Projected Generator Coordinate Method (AMP-GCM) with the Gogny force D1S, taking the axial quadrupole moment as the generating coordinate and restricting to \(K=0\) configurations. Intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions were generated in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells (220 states), and the Hill-Wheeler equation was solved for each angular momentum \(I\). Self-Consistent Cranking (SCC) calculations were also performed for the SD band to estimate the effect of Projection Before Variation (PBV) on the moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the SD minimum in HFB: \(E^{HFB}_{x}(SD) = 9.85\) MeV (reduced to 9.54 MeV with an 18-shell basis).
- Excitation energy of the SD band head from AMP-GCM: \(E^{AMP-GCM}_{x}(SD) = 8.87\) MeV (8.56 MeV with the 18-shell basis correction).
- The SD band becomes Yrast at spin \(I = 8\hbar\), four units lower than the HFB predictions of Molique et al. (2000) and Tanaka et al. (2000).
- Average moment of inertia of the SD band: \(10.3\ \hbar^{2}\text{MeV}^{-1}\), corresponding to \(k \equiv \hbar^{2}/(2\mathcal{J}) = 48.5\) keV.
- Deformation parameters of the SD minimum: \(\beta_{2} = 0.73\), \(\beta_{4} = 0.33\), axis ratio \(z/x = 1.92\), proton mean square radius \(3.66\) fm.
- Ground state binding energy after Coulomb exchange and basis correction: \(-271.63\) MeV, compared to the experimental value of \(271.780\) MeV (Audi and Wapstra, 1995).
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment for the \(2^{+}_{1}\) state: \(-13.29\ \text{fm}^{2}\), compared to the experimental value of \(-14.9\ \text{fm}^{2}\) (Endt, 1990).

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of the SD configuration in \(^{32}S\) using Skyrme forces (Yamagami and Matsuyanagi, 1999; Molique et al., 2000) and the Gogny interaction (Tanaka et al., 2000), which had found a very shallow SD minimum raising doubts about its stability at low spin. By including angular momentum projection and quadrupole mixing via the AMP-GCM, the authors demonstrate that the SD band is stable and becomes Yrast at a lower spin than previously predicted. The results also connect the SD configuration to the \(^{16}O+^{16}O\) quasimolecular picture and the Harvey prescription, and the reasonable agreement with experimental data for normal-deformed states (Brenneisen et al., 1997) supports the reliability of the Gogny force for this nucleus. The work enables future studies of the SD band in \(^{32}S\) with more advanced methods, such as Projection Before Variation, and provides predictions for experimental searches.
