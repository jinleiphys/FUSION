# Causality and stability of magnetohydrodynamics for an ultrarelativistic locally neutral two-component gas

> We investigate the causality and stability of the relativistic theory of magnetohydrodynamics derived in Phys. Rev. D 109, 096021 (2024) to describe a locally neutral two-component plasma of massless particles. We show that this formalism is linearly causal and stable around global equilibrium, for any value of the magnetic field and discuss its qualitative differences to the traditional Israel-Stewart formalism in the linear regime. Finally, we compare this framework with the magnetohydrodynamic model used in the study of astrophysical plasmas, in which only the longitudinal component of the shear-stress tensor is considered. We discuss the domain of applicability of this type of framework in the context of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the second-order magnetohydrodynamic formalism derived in Phys. Rev. D 109, 096021 (2024) for a locally neutral two-component plasma of massless particles is linearly causal and stable around global equilibrium for any value of the magnetic field, but this conclusion is restricted to the linear regime and does not automatically extend to nonlinear perturbations.

## Method
The authors linearize the conservation laws and the equations of motion for the total and relative shear-stress tensors around a global equilibrium state, then decompose the perturbations in Fourier space using an orthonormal basis of 4-vectors aligned with and transverse to the magnetic field direction. They obtain dispersion relations for both longitudinal and transverse perturbations with respect to the magnetic field, and analyze the hydrodynamic and nonhydrodynamic modes to test causality via the asymptotic group velocity condition and stability via the sign of the imaginary part of the frequency.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field frequency parameter is defined as ω₀ = 2|q|B/(5T), where T is the plasma temperature.
- The dimensionless magnetic field strength parameter is defined as ℬ = B₀²/(ε₀ + P₀), where ε₀ is the background energy density and P₀ is the pressure.
- The shear-stress tensor equations contain transport coefficients Σ and Σ' that are positive definite functions of the cross-sections σ_T^{++} = σ_T^{--} = σ_T (same-species) and σ_T^{+-} = σ_T^{-+} (inter-species).
- The relative shear-stress tensor yields a trivial nonhydrodynamic mode at Ω = iΣ'_0.

## Context
This work extends the second-order magnetohydrodynamic framework developed by Kushwah et al. (2024) by verifying its causality and linear stability, which had remained unverified. It contrasts with the traditional Israel-Stewart formalism, showing that different components of the shear-stress tensor satisfy distinct evolution equations with magnetic-field-dependent transport coefficients, unlike the single Israel-Stewart-type equation. The analysis also compares with the simplified magnetohydrodynamic model of Chandra (2015) that retains only the longitudinal shear-stress component, and discusses the domain of applicability of such approximations in the context of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.
