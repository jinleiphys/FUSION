# Non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation from holography

> We derive a non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation for bosonic correlation functions from holography in the classical gravity approximation at strong coupling. This generalizes the familiar thermal fluctuation-dissipation relation in absence of external sources. This also holds universally for any non-equilibrium state which can be obtained from a stable thermal equilibrium state in perturbative derivative (hydrodynamic) and amplitude (non-hydrodynamic) expansions. Therefore, this can provide a strong experimental test for the applicability of the holographic framework. We discuss how it can be tested in heavy ion collisions. We also make a conjecture regarding multi-point holographic non-equilibrium Green's functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation for bosonic correlation functions from holography in the classical gravity approximation at strong coupling, which holds universally for any non-equilibrium state that can be obtained from a stable thermal equilibrium state via perturbative derivative (hydrodynamic) and amplitude (non-hydrodynamic) expansions, with the relation set exactly by the final temperature of thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The author uses holographic gauge/gravity duality at strong coupling and large N to study non-equilibrium spectral and statistical functions in states perturbatively connected to thermal equilibrium with no external sources. The methodology involves constructing gravity duals of non-equilibrium states using derivative and amplitude expansions, studying the dynamics of a scalar field dual to a bosonic order parameter, and mapping boundary conditions of non-equilibrium modes to a parametrization of non-equilibrium Green's functions as weighted sums of retarded and advanced Green's functions. The analysis shows that regularity at the horizon in combination with linear response theory determines all non-equilibrium propagators holographically, leading to the non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Key numbers

- The viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s equals 1/(4 pi) at strong coupling and large N, cited as a famous universal holographic result (though not a genuinely non-equilibrium result by the author's definition).
- The non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation takes the form G_K(omega, k, x, t) = -i (n_BE(omega) + 1/2) A(omega, k, x, t), where n_BE(omega) = 1/(exp(omega/T) - 1) is the Bose-Einstein distribution set by the final equilibrium temperature T.
- The thermal fluctuation-dissipation relation at equilibrium is given by G_K(k) = -i (n_BE(omega) + 1/2) A(k), with n_BE(omega) = 1/(exp(omega/T) - 1).

## Context

This work generalizes the familiar thermal fluctuation-dissipation relation (known from non-equilibrium quantum field theory and the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism of Schwinger (1961) and Keldysh (1965)) to non-equilibrium states in holography. It extends prior holographic results for non-equilibrium spectral functions obtained by Mukhopadhyay (2012) and builds on the holographic prescription for thermal retarded Green's functions proposed by Son and Starinets (2002). The result enables experimental tests of the holographic framework in heavy ion collisions, as the non-equilibrium fluctuation-dissipation relation provides a strong test for the applicability of holography at strong coupling.
