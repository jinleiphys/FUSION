# Isospin breaking in the pion-nucleon scattering lengths

> We analyze isospin breaking through quark mass differences and virtual photons in the pion-nucleon scattering lengths in all physical channels in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim
Isospin-breaking corrections to pion-nucleon scattering lengths are computed at first order in quark mass differences and virtual photons, using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory to order \(p^3\), with the caveat that the large uncertainties in the charged-pion elastic channels are dominated by poorly known low-energy constants \(f_1\) and \(c_1\).

## Method
The authors calculate the S-wave scattering lengths for all physical pion-nucleon channels at threshold, including strong isospin breaking from the quark mass difference \(m_d - m_u\) and electromagnetic corrections from virtual photons. They work in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory at order \(p^3\) (i.e., first order in isospin breaking, \(\mathcal{O}(e^2, m_d - m_u)\)), using Feynman diagrams with nucleon, pion, and photon lines, and subtract one-photon-reducible diagrams to handle threshold divergences. The isospin-breaking shifts are expressed in terms of low-energy constants (LECs) such as \(c_1\), \(f_1\), \(f_2\), \(d_5^r\), and electromagnetic LECs \(k_i\) and \(g_i^r\), with numerical values taken from prior analyses and resonance saturation estimates.

## Key numbers
- \(a^+ = (1.5 \pm 2.2) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\), \(a^- = (85.2 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (isospin-limit scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen and deuterium data).
- Triangle relation \(R = (1.5 \pm 1.1)\%\) (violation of isospin limit).
- \(\Delta a_{\pi^- p} = (-3.4^{+4.3}_{-6.5} + 5.0 i) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (isospin shift for \(\pi^- p \to \pi^- p\)).
- \(\Delta a_{\pi^+ p} = -5.3^{+4.3}_{-6.5} \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (isospin shift for \(\pi^+ p \to \pi^+ p\)).
- \(\Delta a_{\pi^- p}^{\rm cex} = (0.4 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (isospin shift for \(\pi^- p \to \pi^0 n\)).
- \(\Delta a_{\pi^0 p} = (-5.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (isospin shift for \(\pi^0 p \to \pi^0 p\)).
- \(\text{Im}\, a_{\pi^- p} = (4.77 \pm 0.15) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (imaginary part from \(\pi^0 n\) and \(\gamma n\) intermediate states).
- \(\text{Im}\, a_{\pi^- p}^{\rm cex} = (-0.16 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-3} \, M_\pi^{-1}\).
- Ratio of strong to electromagnetic imaginary parts: \(1.57 \pm 0.10\), consistent with the Panofsky ratio \(P = 1.546 \pm 0.009\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory analysis of isospin breaking in pion-nucleon scattering by Fettes, Meissner, and Steininger (1999) and the covariant calculation for \(\pi^- p\) by Gasser and Rusetsky (2002) to all physical channels, including virtual photons. It enables improved extraction of scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen and deuterium data, and provides predictions for neutral-pion scattering lengths accessible in threshold photoproduction experiments at HI\(\gamma\)S and MAMI. The results contradict a 5–7% deviation in the triangle relation reported by Gibbs (1995) and Matsinos (1997) from low-momentum data.
