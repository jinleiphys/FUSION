# Light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We calculate cross sections for diphoton production in (semi)exclusive $PbPb$ collisions, relevant for the LHC. The calculation is based on equivalent photon approximation in the impact parameter space. The cross sections for elementary $γγ\to γγ$ subprocess are calculated including two different mechanisms. We take into account box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops. In addition, we consider a vector-meson dominance (VDM-Regge) contribution with virtual intermediate hadronic (vector-like) excitations of the photons. We get much higher cross sections in $PbPb$ collisions than in earlier calculation from the literature. This opens a possibility to study the $γγ\to γγ$ (quasi)elastic scattering at the LHC. We present many interesting differential distributions which could be measured by the ALICE, CMS or ATLAS Collaborations at the LHC. We study whether a separation or identification of different components (boxes, VDM-Regge) is possible. We find that the cross section for elastic $γγ$ scattering could be measured in the heavy-ion collisions for subprocess energies smaller than $W_{γγ} \approx 15-20$ GeV.

# Light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

The cross section for elastic photon-photon scattering in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at the LHC is much larger than previously estimated (by a factor of about 8 compared to d'Enterria (2013)), and could be measured for subprocess energies below approximately 15–20 GeV, but only if the VDM-Regge component, which dominates at higher energies, can be separated from the box-diagram contribution.

## Method

The calculation uses the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space to compute diphoton production in (semi)exclusive PbPb collisions. The elementary γγ → γγ subprocess cross section includes two mechanisms: one-loop box diagrams with leptons and quarks in the loops (calculated using FormCalc and LoopTools), and a vector-meson dominance (VDM-Regge) contribution where photons fluctuate into virtual vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) that interact via Regge-exchange amplitudes parametrized from πN and NN total cross sections via Donnachie-Landshoff parametrizations. Nuclear cross sections are obtained by integrating the elementary cross section over photon fluxes with nuclear form factors (realistic charge density and monopole approximations) and an absorption factor that excludes overlapping nuclear collisions.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for box diagrams with Wγγ > 5 GeV: 306 nb (realistic form factor), 349 nb (monopole form factor)
- Total cross section for VDM-Regge with Wγγ > 5 GeV: 19 nb (realistic form factor), 22 nb (monopole form factor)
- Box contribution with Eγ > 3 GeV and |yγ| < 2.5: 183 nb (realistic form factor)
- Box contribution with ALICE cuts (pt,γ > 0.9 GeV, |yγ| < 0.7): 107 nb
- Box contribution with CMS cuts (pt,γ > 5.5 GeV, |yγ| < 2.5): 10 nb
- VDM-Regge contribution with pt,γ > 2 GeV: 7 × 10⁻⁹ nb (effectively zero)
- The VDM-Regge elementary cross section exceeds the box contribution for Wγγ > 30 GeV
- Expected measurable range: Wγγ up to 15–20 GeV with more than 1 count per GeV bin at 1 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity
- Slope parameter for VDM-Regge: B = 4 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends the initial estimate of elastic γγ scattering in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions by d'Enterria (2013), finding cross sections approximately 8 times larger. It introduces a VDM-Regge component not considered in previous calculations, based on the hadronic fluctuation of photons and Regge phenomenology previously applied to γγ → ρ⁰ρ⁰ by Klusek-Gawenda et al. (2009). The results enable feasibility studies for the ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS collaborations at the LHC, though the VDM-Regge component is found to be extremely difficult to identify experimentally due to its very forward/backward production (rapidities around ±5) and its suppression by transverse momentum cuts. Future work should include resonance contributions and background from AA → AA e⁺e⁻ with misidentified electrons.
