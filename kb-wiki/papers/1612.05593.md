# Studies of three-particle correlations and reaction-plane correlators from STAR

> We present STAR measurements of various harmonics of three-particle correlations in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The quantity $\langle\cos(mϕ_1+nϕ_2-(m+n)ϕ_3)\rangle$ is measured for inclusive charged particles for different harmonics $m$ and $n$ as a function of collision centrality, transverse momentum $p_T$ and relative pseudorapidity $Δη$. These observables provide detailed information on global event properties like correlations between event planes of different harmonics and are particularly sensitive to the expansion dynamics of the matter produced in the collisions. We compare our measurements to different viscous hydrodynamic models. We argue that these measurements probe the three-dimensional structure of the initial state and provide unique ways to constrain the transport parameters involved in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents STAR measurements of three-particle correlations in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, arguing that these observables are more sensitive to the temperature dependence of the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio \(\eta/s(T)\) than conventional flow harmonics, but the full constraint on \(\eta/s(T)\) requires complementary measurements at multiple RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, not just at 200 GeV.

## Method

The STAR collaboration measured the three-particle correlator \(C_{m,n,m+n} = \langle\langle \cos(m\phi_1 + n\phi_2 - (m+n)\phi_3)\rangle\rangle\) for inclusive charged particles in the pseudorapidity range \(|\eta|<1\) and transverse momentum \(p_T>0.2\) GeV/\(c\) using the Time Projection Chamber. The analysis used Q-vector algebra with track-by-track weights to correct for detector acceptance imperfections, applied momentum-dependent tracking efficiency corrections, and corrected for track-merging artifacts. Systematic uncertainties were estimated by varying datasets, tracking algorithms, efficiency estimates, z-vertex position, and track selection criteria, and by studying the \(\Delta\eta\) dependence to quantify short-range quantum and Coulomb correlations.

## Key numbers

- The correlators \(C_{1,1,2}\), \(C_{1,2,3}\), \(C_{2,2,4}\), and \(C_{2,3,5}\) were measured in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV Au+Au collisions.
- Centrality bins used: \(0-5\%\), \(5-10\%\), \(10-20\%\), ..., \(70-80\%\).
- Charged particles were selected with \(p_T > 0.2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 1\).
- The \(C_{1,2,3}\) correlator shows a strong dependence on \(\Delta\eta_{1,3}\) (between first and third order harmonics) but a very weak dependence on \(\Delta\eta_{1,2}\) (between first and second order harmonics).
- For \(70-80\%\) peripheral events, both \(C_{1,1,2}\) and \(C_{1,2,3}\) become negative at high \(p_{T,1}\), consistent with momentum conservation.
- For \(0-5\%\) central events, \(C_{1,1,2}\) and \(C_{1,2,3}\) show negative signal at small \(p_{T,1}\) and nearly zero or positive signal at large \(p_{T,1}\).
- The Glauber model predicts negative values for all correlators from initial state geometry alone, while data shows positive values for \(C_{2,2,4}\) and \(C_{2,3,5}\), indicating dominance of non-linear hydrodynamic response.

## Context

This work extends measurements of event plane and flow harmonic correlations previously performed at LHC by the ATLAS collaboration (2014) and ALICE collaboration (2016) to RHIC energies, where the smaller beam rapidity allows stronger variation of initial geometry over a smaller pseudorapidity window. The measurements enable constraining the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) through comparison to viscous hydrodynamic models including those by Teaney and Yan (2010, 2013), the EKRT model by Niemi et al. (2015), and MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions by Schenke et al. (2010, 2012). The results also provide a baseline for chiral magnetic effect searches, as the charge dependence of three-particle correlations can be used to search for CME signals as studied by Abelev et al. (2009) and Adamczyk et al. (2013).
