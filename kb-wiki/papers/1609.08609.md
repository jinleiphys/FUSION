# Comprehending particle production at RHIC and LHC energies using global measurements

> The centrality dependence of the charged-particle multiplicity densities ($dN_{ch}/dη$) and transverse energy densities ($dE_{T}/dη$) are investigated using the two-component Glauber approach for broad range of energies in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. A comprehensive study shows that the data is well described within the framework of two component model which includes the contribution of "soft processes" and "hard processes" for different centrality classes and energies. The data at two different energies are compared by means of the ratio of $dN_{ch}/dη$ (and $dE_{T}/dη$) to see the interplay of energy and $x$-scaling.

## Key claim

The two-component Glauber model, which separates particle production into "soft" (scaling with number of participants) and "hard" (scaling with number of collisions) processes, describes the centrality dependence of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse energy densities across a broad range of RHIC and LHC energies, but the authors note the model is "bit crude and simplistic" and that the extracted parameter values depend on constraining the nucleon-nucleon multiplicity to experimentally measured values rather than treating it as a free fit parameter.

## Method

The authors fit the two-component model expression dNch/dη = npp × ((1-x) × ⟨Npart⟩/2 + x × ⟨Ncoll⟩) to published PHENIX Au+Au data at RHIC energies (19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV) and ALICE Pb+Pb data at LHC energies (2.76, 5.02 TeV), using experimentally measured npp values from pp collisions where available and extrapolated values via a power law for RHIC beam energy scan energies. For transverse energy densities, the same x parameter from the multiplicity fits was used while treating the mean pp transverse energy ETpp as a free parameter. Single-component power-law fits dNch/dη ∝ ⟨Npart⟩^α and dNch/dη ∝ ⟨Ncoll⟩^β were also performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The hard-process fraction x increases from 0.056 ± 0.013 at √sNN = 19.6 GeV to 0.18 ± 0.004 at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- The power-law exponent α (from dNch/dη ∝ ⟨Npart⟩^α) increases from 0.95 ± 0.006 at 19.6 GeV to 1.27 ± 0.002 at 5.02 TeV
- The power-law exponent β (from dNch/dη ∝ ⟨Ncoll⟩^β) increases from 0.84 ± 0.006 at 19.6 GeV to 1.02 ± 0.002 at 5.02 TeV
- The pairwise ratio R200/19.6 of multiplicity per participant pair shows an increase of approximately 20% from most peripheral to most central collisions
- The product of energy ratio and x ratio for R200/19.6 equals 28.27, comparable to the product 28.39 for R5020/200

## Context

This work extends the two-component Glauber approach previously applied by the MIT group (2002) to a broader energy range from RHIC to LHC, improving the methodology by fixing npp to experimental values rather than treating it as a free parameter. The analysis enables systematic comparison of the relative contributions of hard and soft processes across collision energies and provides estimates of mean transverse energy in pp collisions at energies where no direct measurements exist. The pairwise ratio technique reveals that the scaling of multiplicity densities with energy involves interplay between the energy difference and the increasing hard-scattering fraction, while transverse energy ratios show simpler energy-difference scaling.
