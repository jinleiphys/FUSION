# $ψ(2S)$ versus $J/ψ$ suppression in proton-nucleus collisions from factorization violating soft color exchanges

> We argue that the large suppression of the $ψ(2S)$ inclusive cross-section relative to the $J/ψ$ inclusive cross-section in proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions can be attributed to factorization breaking effects in the formation of quarkonium. These factorization breaking effects arise from soft color exchanges between charm-anticharm pairs undergoing hadronization and comoving partons that are long-lived on time scales of quarkonium formation. We compute the short distance pair production of heavy quarks in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory and employ an improved Color Evaporation model (ICEM) to describe their hadronization into quarkonium at large distances. The combined CGC+ICEM model provides a quantitative description of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ data in proton-proton (p+p) collisions from both RHIC and the LHC. Factorization breaking effects in hadronization, due to additional parton comovers in the nucleus, are introduced heuristically by imposing a cutoff $Λ$, representing the momentum kick from soft color exchanges, in the ICEM model. Such soft exchanges have no perceptible effect on $J/ψ$ suppression in p+A collisions. In contrast, the interplay of the physics of these soft exchanges at large distances, with the physics of semi-hard rescattering at short distances, causes a significant additional suppression of $ψ(2S)$ yields relative to that of the $J/ψ$. A good fit of all RHIC and LHC $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ data, for transverse momenta $P_\perp\leq 5$ GeV in p+p and p+A collisions, is obtained for $Λ\sim 10$ MeV.

## Key claim

The large suppression of the ψ(2S) relative to the J/ψ in proton-nucleus collisions can be attributed to factorization breaking effects from soft color exchanges between charm-anticharm pairs and comoving partons, but only because the ψ(2S) mass is close to the open charm threshold, making it susceptible to breakup even with very soft momentum kicks of order Λ ∼ 10 MeV, while the J/ψ is unaffected.

## Method

The authors compute the short-distance production of charm-anticharm pairs using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, which resums semi-hard multiple scattering off the nuclear target. They then employ an improved Color Evaporation Model (ICEM) to describe hadronization into quarkonium at large distances, incorporating kinematic constraints relating the pair momentum to the produced quarkonium momentum. Factorization breaking effects in nuclei are introduced heuristically by imposing a cutoff Λ on the upper limit of the invariant mass integration in the ICEM, representing the average momentum kick from soft color exchanges with nuclear parton comovers.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter Λ ∼ 10 MeV provides a good fit to all RHIC and LHC J/ψ and ψ(2S) data for transverse momenta P⊥ ≤ 5 GeV in p+p and p+A collisions.
- The ψ(2S) suppression parameter RpAψ(2S) is smaller than 0.6 at the LHC for both forward and backward rapidities.
- The time scale for the charm-anticharm pair to traverse the nucleus is tt ∼ 0.05 fm for PHENIX and tt ∼ 0.002 fm for ALICE.
- The charm quark pair production time scale is tc > 0.07 fm for charm quarks.
- The initial saturation scale for the proton is Qs0,p2 = 0.2 GeV2, and for heavy nuclei such as Pb and Au it is varied as Qs0,A2 = (1.5–2.0) Qs0,p2.
- The quark mass is varied in the range m = (1.3–1.4) GeV in the uncertainty bands.
- The D meson mass threshold is 2 mD = 3.728 GeV, while mJ/ψ = 3.1 GeV and mψ(2S) = 3.686 GeV.
- For RHIC d+Au collisions at √sNN = 0.2 TeV/nucleon, the best fit uses Λ = 7.5 MeV, with variation in Λ = 5–10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CGC+ICEM framework previously applied to J/ψ production in p+p and p+A collisions (Ma 2014, Ma 2015) to also describe ψ(2S) production. It contrasts with explanations invoking hadronic comovers (Ferreiro 2014), hybrid charmonium mixing (Kisslinger 2014), thermal rate equations (Du 2015), or hot medium effects (Chen 2016), by proposing that parton-level soft color exchanges before hadronization are sufficient. The results enable predictions for ψ(2S) suppression at LHC √s = 8.16 TeV/nucleon and further tests of factorization breaking in quarkonium production.
