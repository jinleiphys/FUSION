# Hadronic Fluctuations and Correlations

> We will provide a review of some of the physics which can be addressed by studying fluctuations and correlations in heavy ion collisions. We will discuss Lattice QCD results on fluctuations and correlations and will put them into context with observables which have been measured in heavy-ion collisions. Special attention will be given to the QCD critical point and the first order co-existence region, and we will discuss how the measurement of fluctuations and correlations can help in an experimental search for non-trivial structures in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

This review argues that event-by-event fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) in heavy-ion collisions can reveal the properties of the quark-gluon plasma and possibly identify the QCD critical point, provided that experimental acceptance is sufficiently large to separate the relevant rapidity scales and that hadronic re-interactions do not erase the early-stage signal.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework linking fluctuations in a thermal system to susceptibilities computed from the grand-canonical partition function, and compares these to Lattice QCD results for diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities up to sixth order. It then discusses how ratios of cumulants (e.g., \(R_{4,2}^{(B)} = \chi_B^{(4)}/\chi_B^{(2)}\)) and correlation coefficients (e.g., \(C_{BS} = -3\langle BS\rangle/\langle S^2\rangle\)) can distinguish between a hadron gas and a quark-gluon plasma, and reviews experimental measurements of net-charge fluctuations at RHIC and SPS.

## Key numbers

- For a 2-flavor QGP, the ratio of net-charge fluctuations to entropy is \(\langle\delta Q_q^2\rangle/S_{\rm QGP} = 1/24\); for a pion gas it is \(\langle\delta Q_\pi^2\rangle/S_\pi = 1/6\), a factor of 4 difference.
- For a hadron gas at \(T = 170\ {\rm MeV}\) and \(\mu_B = 0\), the baryon-strangeness correlation coefficient is \(C_{BS} = 0.66\); for an ideal QGP it is \(C_{BS} = 1\).
- The ratio of fourth-order to second-order baryon number susceptibilities is \(R_{4,2}^B = 1\) in the hadron phase (baryon number \(|B|=1\)) and \(R_{4,2}^B = 1/9\) in the QGP (quark baryon number \(|B|=1/3\)).
- Lattice QCD results with almost physical light quark masses show second-order susceptibilities consistent with the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of free massless quarks right above the transition temperature \(T_c\).
- A maximum correlation length of \(\xi \sim 2.5\ {\rm fm}\) at the QCD critical point has been estimated by Berdnikov (1999), much smaller than typical system sizes.
- At top SPS energies (\(E_{\rm Beam}=160\ {\rm AGeV}\)), the total rapidity width is \(\Delta Y_{\rm total} \simeq 2\), comparable to the charge kick \(\Delta Y_{\rm kick} \geq 1.5\) from rho and Delta decays.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions developed by Jeon (2000) and Asakawa (2000), who first proposed net-charge fluctuations as a QGP signature, and by Shuryak (2000) on relaxation times of conserved charge fluctuations. It connects Lattice QCD results from Allton (2005), Gavai (2005), and Schmidt (2008) to experimental observables, and discusses how the correlation coefficient \(C_{BS}\) proposed by Koch (2005) can distinguish between a QGP and a hadron gas. The review enables experimental searches for the QCD critical point and first-order coexistence region at facilities such as RHIC and the future FAIR facility.
