# Hyperfine structure splitting of the ground states in the $pdμ, ptμ$ and $dtμ$ ions

> The hyperfine structure splittings of the ground states of the $pdμ, ptμ$ and $dtμ$ ions are determined with the use of highly accurate expectation values of the interparticle delta-functions obtained in recent computations. The corresponding hyperfine structure splittings, e.g., $Δ_{12} =$ 1.3400149$\cdot 10^7$ $MHz$ and $Δ_{23} =$ 3.3518984$\cdot 10^7$ $MHz$ for the $ptμ$ ion, can directly be measured in modern experiments.

# Hyperfine structure splitting of the ground states in the $pd\mu, pt\mu$ and $dt\mu$ ions

## Key claim

The hyperfine structure splittings of the ground $S(L=0)$ states in the $pd\mu$, $pt\mu$, and $dt\mu$ muonic molecular ions are determined using highly accurate expectation values of interparticle delta-functions from recent computations, with the claim that these splittings (e.g., $\Delta_{12} = 1.3400149 \cdot 10^7$ MHz and $\Delta_{23} = 3.3518984 \cdot 10^7$ MHz for the $pt\mu$ ion) can be directly measured in modern experiments. The abstract glosses over the fact that the results depend on the specific numerical values of the delta-function expectation values computed with trial wave functions using up to 3840 exponential basis functions, and that the conversion from atomic units to MHz uses a specific conversion factor of $6.57968392061 \cdot 10^9$ MHz/a.u.

## Method

The hyperfine structure splitting operator $(\Delta H)_{h.s.}$ for each three-body ion was expressed as a sum of three terms, each proportional to $\frac{2\pi}{3} \alpha^2$ times the expectation value of an interparticle delta-function $\langle \delta(\mathbf{r}_{ij}) \rangle$ times the product of the corresponding $g$-factors and spin operators. Highly accurate expectation values of all interparticle delta-functions for the ground $S(L=0)$ states of $pd\mu$, $pt\mu$, and $dt\mu$ (and the first excited $S(L=0)$ state of $dt\mu$) were computed using trial wave functions with 3300, 3500, 3700, and 3840 exponential basis functions in muon atomic units, then converted to regular atomic units and applied to the hyperfine splitting formulas to obtain numerical splittings in MHz.

## Key numbers

- For the $pt\mu$ ion ground state: $\Delta_{12} = 1.3400149 \cdot 10^7$ MHz (splitting between the $J=3/2$ group and the first $J=1/2$ group)
- For the $pt\mu$ ion ground state: $\Delta_{23} = 3.3518984 \cdot 10^7$ MHz (splitting between the two $J=1/2$ groups)
- Conversion factor used: $6.57968392061 \cdot 10^9$ MHz per atomic unit
- Fine structure constant used: $\alpha = 7.297352586 \cdot 10^{-3}$
- Muon mass used: $m_\mu = 206.768262 m_e$
- Proton mass used: $m_p = 1836.152701 m_e$
- Muon $g$-factor used: $g_\mu = -2.0023218396$
- Proton magnetic moment used: $\mathcal{M}_p = 2.792847386$ (in nuclear magnetons)
- Deuteron magnetic moment used: $\mathcal{M}_d = 0.857438230$ (in nuclear magnetons)
- Triton magnetic moment used: $\mathcal{M}_t = 2.9789624775$ (in nuclear magnetons)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior highly accurate numerical computations of delta-function expectation values for muonic molecular ions (Frolov and Ward, 2011) to determine hyperfine structure splittings that can be compared with experiment. The $pd\mu$, $pt\mu$, and $dt\mu$ ions are of interest in muon-catalyzed fusion research, and the hyperfine splittings of the $dt\mu$ ion (including its first excited $S(L=0)$ state, designated $(dt\mu)^*$) are relevant for understanding fusion reaction rates. The results enable experimental verification of the predicted hyperfine structure patterns, which differ between ions with deuteron spin 1 ($pd\mu$, $dt\mu$) and those with triton spin 1/2 ($pt\mu$).
