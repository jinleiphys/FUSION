# Container evolution for cluster structures in $^{16}{\rm O}$

> Background: $α+{^{12}{\rm C}}$ clustering in $^{16}{\rm O}$ has been of historical importance in nuclear clustering. In the last 15 years the $4α$ condensate state has been proposed as a new-type cluster state. Purpose: The aim is to reveal a dynamical process of the formation of different kinds of cluster states, in terms of a "container" aspect of clusters, in $^{16}{\rm O}$. Method: The so-called THSR wave function for the $4α$ clusters is extended to inclusion of two different containers occupied independently by the $^{12}{\rm C}$ $(3α)$ and $α$ clusters. Results: The five $J^π=0^+$ states with $4α$ tetrahedral shape, $α+{^{12}{\rm C}}$ cluster structures, and the $4α$ condensate character, are found to be represented, to good approximation, by single configurations of the extended THSR wave function with containers of appropriate shape and size. Conclusions: It is demonstrated in $^{16}{\rm O}$ that the dynamical evolution of cluster structures can be caused by size and shape evolution of a container occupied with clusters. The $α$ condensate with gaslike $4α$ configuration appears as a limit of the cluster formation.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the dynamical evolution of cluster structures in $^{16}{\rm O}$, from the ground state through various $\alpha+{^{12}{\rm C}}$ cluster states to the $4\alpha$ condensate, can be understood as a continuous evolution of the size and shape of a "container" confining the clusters, rather than requiring distinct geometric arrangements for each state.

## Method
The author extends the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function for $4\alpha$ clusters to include two independent containers: one for the ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ ($3\alpha$) cluster and one for the remaining $\alpha$ cluster. The wave function parameters $\beta_{1\perp},\beta_{1z},\beta_{2\perp},\beta_{2z}$ characterize the shape and size of these containers. The five lowest $J^\pi=0^+$ states are obtained by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation with an $r^2$-constraint method to eliminate spurious continuum components, using the F1 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with finite-range three-body force.

## Key numbers
- Ground state ($0_I^+$): r.m.s. charge radius $2.71(0.02)$ fm (experiment: $2.70$ fm)
- $0_{II}^+$ state: r.m.s. radius $3.2$ fm, monopole matrix element $M(E0)=5.9$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$ (experiment: $3.55(0.21)$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$)
- $0_{III}^+$ state: r.m.s. radius $3.3$ fm, $M(E0)=5.7$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$ (experiment: $4.03(0.09)$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$)
- $0_{IV}^+$ state: r.m.s. radius $4.9$ fm, $M(E0)=0.8$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$
- $0_{V}^+$ state: r.m.s. radius $4.9$ fm, $M(E0)=0.7$ $e\,{\rm fm}^2$
- Squared overlap of ground state with single eTHSR configuration: $0.98$ (container parameters: $B_{1\perp}=2.3$ fm, $B_{1z}=1.4$ fm, $B_{2\perp}=1.4$ fm, $B_{2z}=3.9$ fm)
- Squared overlap of $0_{II}^+$ state: $0.94$ (container parameters: $B_{1\perp}=2.9$ fm, $B_{1z}=2.9$ fm, $B_{2\perp}=5.2$ fm, $B_{2z}=5.3$ fm)
- Squared overlap of $0_{V}^+$ ($4\alpha$ condensate) state: $0.78$ (container parameters: $B_{1\perp}=7.6$ fm, $B_{1z}=3.0$ fm, $B_{2\perp}=7.6$ fm, $B_{2z}=2.9$ fm)
- For comparison, the Hoyle state in ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ has squared overlap $0.99$ with single THSR configuration (container: $B_{\perp}=8.0$ fm, $B_{z}=2.4$ fm)

## Context
This work extends the "container" picture previously applied to ${^{8}{\rm Be}}$, ${^{12}{\rm C}}$, ${^{16}{\rm O}}$, ${^{20}{\rm Ne}}$, and other nuclei (Funaki et al., various years; Zhou et al., 2013; Lyu et al., 2015, 2016). It contrasts with traditional localized cluster models like the Brink-Bloch wave function (Brink, 1966) and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 1995), which require geometric arrangements of clusters. The results enable a unified description of both gaslike ($4\alpha$ condensate) and non-gaslike ($\alpha+{^{12}{\rm C}}$) cluster states in $^{16}{\rm O}$, and suggest the container evolution concept can be extended to heavier nuclei to understand cluster formation from ground states to highly excited states.
