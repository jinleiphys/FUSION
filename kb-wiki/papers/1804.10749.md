# Isotopic Symmetry Breaking in the $η(1405)\to f_0 (980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$. Decay through a $K\bar K$ Loop Diagram and the Role of Anomalous Landau Thresholds

> Anomalous isotopic symmetry breaking in the $η(1405)\to f_0(980)π^0\toπ^+π^-π^0$ decay through a mechanism featuring anomalous Landau thresholds in the form of logarithmic triangle singularities, i.e., through the $η(1405)\to (K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0(980)π^0 \toπ^+π^- π^0$ transition, has been analyzed. It has been shown that this effect can be correctly quantified only by taking into account the nonzero $K^*$ width. Different scales of isotopic symmetry breaking associated with the $K^+-K^0$ mass difference are compared.

## Key claim

Anomalous isotopic symmetry breaking in the decay \(\eta(1405)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) can be explained by a mechanism involving logarithmic triangle singularities from a \(K^*\bar K\) loop, but this effect can be correctly quantified only by taking into account the nonzero width of the intermediate \(K^*\) meson; assuming a stable \(K^*\) overestimates the decay probability by a factor of 6–8.

## Method

The authors computed the amplitude for the \(\eta(1405)\to (K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)\to K\bar K\pi^0\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) transition using a triangle-loop diagram with charged and neutral kaon intermediate states. They treated isotopic symmetry breaking as arising solely from the \(K^+-K^0\) mass difference, set \(m_{K^{*+}}=m_{K^{*0}}=0.8955\) GeV, and incorporated the finite \(K^*\) width (\(\Gamma_{K^*}\approx 50\) MeV) via a Källén–Lehmann spectral representation that averages the amplitude over the resonant Breit-Wigner mass distribution. The resulting isospin-violating amplitude \(\bar{F}(s_1,s_2)=\bar{F}_+-\bar{F}_0\) was then used to compute the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) mass spectrum in the \(\eta(1405)\) decay.

## Key numbers

* The measured branching ratio from BESIII: \(BR(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405)\to\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\to\gamma\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0) = (1.50 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.11)\times 10^{-5}\).
* The ratio of branching ratios from BESIII: \(BR(\eta(1405)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0) / BR(\eta(1405)\to a^0_0(980)\pi^0\to\eta\pi^0\pi^0) = (17.9 \pm 4.2)\%\).
* The ratio of the \(J/\psi\) decay to \(\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\) versus \(\gamma K\bar K\pi\): \((0.53 \pm 0.13)\%\).
* The authors' estimate from a previous calculation (Achasov et al. 2015) with nonzero \(K^*\) width: \(BR(J/\psi\to\gamma\eta(1405)\to\gamma f_0(980)\pi^0\to\gamma\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0) \approx 1.12\times 10^{-5}\), in agreement with the BESIII measurement.
* The degree of isotopic symmetry breaking from the \(K^+-K^0\) mass difference in typical processes: \(\simeq (m_{K^0}-m_{K^+})/m_{K^0} \approx 1/126\).
* The degree of isotopic symmetry breaking from S-wave \(K\bar K\) production without anomalous thresholds: \(\simeq \sqrt{2(m_{K^0}-m_{K^+})/m_{K^0}} \approx 0.127\).
* The degree of isotopic symmetry breaking from the triangle-singularity mechanism: \(\simeq \left|\ln\left|\frac{\Gamma_{K^*}/2}{\sqrt{m^2_{K^0}-m^2_{K^+}+\Gamma^2_{K^*}/4}}\right|\right| \approx 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Achasov et al. (2015) by providing a detailed demonstration of how the nonzero \(K^*\) width smooths the logarithmic singularities and produces a narrow resonance structure in the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) mass spectrum between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds. It contradicts earlier attempts by Wu et al. (2012), Albaladejo et al. (2012), Wang et al. (2013), and Aceti et al. (2015) that treated the \(K^*\) as stable and thus overestimated the decay probability. The results enable future experimental comparisons with the BESIII data on the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) mass spectrum shape and demonstrate that the phase of the \(f_0(980)\) formation amplitude changes abruptly by \(90^\circ\) between the two kaon thresholds, analogous to the behavior seen in \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing.
