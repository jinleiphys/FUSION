# Three regimes/phases of QCD at high T, their symmetries and N_c scaling

> We review recent developments on the QCD phase diagram at small chemical potentials and increasing temperature. There are three regimes/phases in QCD which differ by symmetries, degrees of freedom and N_c scaling: the hadron gas below the chiral restoration temperature T_ch, the stringy fluid between T_ch and the deconfinement temperature T_d and the quark-gluon plasma above T_d.

# Three regimes/phases of QCD at high T, their symmetries and N_c scaling

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD at high temperature and small chemical potential exhibits three distinct regimes, hadron gas, stringy fluid, and quark-gluon plasma, separated by the chiral restoration temperature T_ch (~155 MeV) and the deconfinement temperature T_d (~300 MeV), with the intermediate "stringy fluid" regime being a confined but chirally symmetric phase where degrees of freedom are color-singlet quark-antiquark pairs bound by the chromoelectric field, not weakly interacting partons.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results on temporal and spatial correlation functions of mesonic excitations at temperatures above T_ch, analyzing the multiplet structure of correlators to identify emergent symmetries (SU(2)_CS chiral spin symmetry and its flavor extension SU(4)). The N_c scaling of thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy density) is derived theoretically for each regime, and lattice data on fluctuations of conserved charges (net u, d, s quark numbers) are examined as evidence for the intermediate regime.

## Key numbers

- T_ch ~ 155 MeV: chiral restoration temperature, below which the hadron gas regime exists
- T = 220 MeV: temperature at which lattice correlators show approximate SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries in full QCD (N_F=2, physical quark masses)
- T ~ 500 MeV: upper temperature up to which SU(2)_CS and SU(4) symmetries are observed in spatial correlators
- T_d ~ 300 MeV: deconfinement temperature, estimated from pure glue theory deconfinement phase transition, center vortices percolation, and Hagedorn temperature
- 3 T_ch: approximate upper extent of the stringy fluid regime (roughly 465 MeV)
- T ~ 600 MeV: temperature below which perturbative expansion in hot QCD fails (from Ref. GPP)
- N_c^0 scaling: hadron gas energy density, pressure, and entropy density scale as N_c^0
- N_c^1 scaling: stringy fluid energy density, pressure, and entropy density scale as N_c^1
- N_c^2 scaling: quark-gluon plasma energy density, pressure, and entropy density scale as N_c^2

## Context

This review extends the work of Glozman and collaborators (G1, GP, G2, G3) on chiral spin symmetry and SU(2N_F) symmetries in QCD, building on lattice observations by D1, D2, DGP1, DGP2 that these symmetries emerge upon truncation of near-zero Dirac modes. The three-regime picture contradicts the conventional two-phase (hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma) view and is supported by lattice studies of correlators by R1, R2, R3, Chiu, and by the failure of perturbative QCD below 600 MeV shown by GPP. The intermediate stringy fluid regime enables a new understanding of the QCD phase diagram where chiral restoration and deconfinement are separate crossovers, with implications for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and for large-N_c QCD where the three regimes become distinct phases with first-order transitions (CG1).
