# Longitudinal decorrelation measures of flow magnitude and event-plane angles in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> We discuss the forward-backward correlations of harmonic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC), applying standard multibin measures, as well as proposed here new measures. We illustrate the methods with hydrodynamic model simulations based on event-by-event initial conditions from the wounded quark model with asymmetric rapidity emission profiles. Within the model we examine independently the event-plane angle and the flow magnitude decorrelations. We find specific hierarchy between various flow decorrelation measures and confirm certain factorization relations. We find qualitative agreement of the model and the data from the ATLAS and CMS Collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes new decorrelation measures that separate the contributions of event-plane angle fluctuations and flow magnitude fluctuations, and demonstrates that within a 3+1D hydrodynamic model with wounded-quark initial conditions, the flow angle decorrelation is significantly larger than the flow magnitude decorrelation, with a strong anti-correlation between flow magnitude and torque angle.

## Method

The authors perform event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations with initial conditions from the wounded quark model using asymmetric rapidity emission profiles, followed by statistical hadronization with THERMINATOR. They compute standard three-bin correlators of the flow vector, as well as new measures that isolate the event-plane angle decorrelation and flow magnitude decorrelation separately, and also introduce weighted correlators using different powers of the flow magnitude at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Flow angle decorrelation of 10-20 degrees over a few units of pseudorapidity (stated qualitatively in text, no explicit numerical value given for specific pseudorapidity separation)
- For the independent source model: ⟨v_n^2⟩^{1/2}/⟨v_n⟩ = 2/√π ≈ 1.13
- For the independent source model: ⟨v_n^4⟩^{1/4}/⟨v_n⟩ = 16^{1/4}/√π ≈ 1.34
- For the independent source model: ⟨v_n^6⟩^{1/6}/⟨v_n⟩ = 96^{1/4}/√π ≈ 1.52
- Freeze-out temperature set at 150 MeV
- Reference bin taken as 4.4 < η_ref < 5
- Simulations generate 200-800 THERMINATOR events per hydrodynamic event depending on centrality

## Context

This work extends the torque effect concept proposed by Bozek (2010) and further developed by Huo (2013), Jia (2014), and others, where fluctuating event-plane angles arise from asymmetric longitudinal emission profiles of sources. The paper confirms factorization relations first proposed by the ATLAS Collaboration (2017) and provides new weighted correlators that can be tested experimentally. The model shows qualitative but not quantitative agreement with CMS (2015) and ATLAS (2017) data, with too much decorrelation in central collisions and too little in semi-peripheral collisions, indicating that no existing model uniformly describes flow decorrelation across all centralities and collision systems.
