# Net-Proton Number Cumulants in Strongly and Weakly Coupled QGP

> We provide a description of the QCD phase diagram across different energy regimes. Without any adjustable free parameters fitted to lattice or experimental data, we demonstrate that the net-proton number cumulant ratios from the Beam Energy Scan-I (BES-I) central ($0-5\%$) Au+Au collision experiments at RHIC, for center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s} \geq 39\,\text{GeV}$, are well described by the $\textit{dynamical soft-wall}$ AdS black hole model, indicating the formation of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) dominated by gluons in the large $N_c$ limit. We identify a critical point at $μ_B = 247\,\text{MeV}$ or $\sqrt{s}=16\,\text{GeV}$ in this model, where net-proton cumulant ratios show non-monotonic variation. However, for $\sqrt{s} < 39\,\text{GeV}$, the recent BES-II data does not exhibit the expected variation due to the critical point, suggesting instead a transition to a nearly-cold ($μ_B \gg T$) weakly coupled QGP (wQGP), for $\sqrt{s} < 7.7\,\text{GeV}$, dominated by valence quarks. This prediction can be tested by the upcoming STAR Fixed-Target experimental data.

# Net-Proton Number Cumulants in Strongly and Weakly Coupled QGP

## Key claim
Without any adjustable free parameters fitted to lattice or experimental data, the dynamical soft-wall AdS black hole model describes net-proton number cumulant ratios from BES-I central Au+Au collisions at RHIC for center-of-mass energies of 39 GeV and above, indicating strongly coupled QGP formation; however, for energies below 39 GeV, the BES-II data does not show the expected critical-point variation, suggesting a transition to a nearly-cold weakly coupled QGP for energies below 7.7 GeV, a prediction testable by upcoming STAR Fixed-Target data.

## Method
The author uses the dynamical soft-wall AdS black hole model, an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic dual to strongly coupled QGP, with the mass-scale parameter kappa fixed to 0.430 GeV from the Regge slope of meson spectra. Net-proton number cumulant ratios are computed from the free energy density of the AdS black hole via baryon-number susceptibilities, evaluated on a freeze-out line at fixed temperature 0.157 GeV with a parametrized chemical potential as a function of collision energy. For the weakly coupled regime, the free energy density is computed using perturbative QCD at next-to-next-to-leading order in the nearly-cold (mu much greater than T) limit, with a varying freeze-out temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Critical point identified at mu_B = 247 MeV or sqrt(s) = 16 GeV in the dynamical soft-wall model
- The mass-scale parameter kappa = 0.430 GeV is fixed from the Regge slope of meson spectra (Model-IA of Li (2013))
- The chiral condensate sigma^(1/3) = 0.180 GeV and quark mass m_q = 0.006 GeV in the dynamical soft-wall model
- Freeze-out temperature T_freeze-out = 0.157 GeV used for the strongly coupled QGP description
- The QCD running coupling uses Lambda_MSbar = 0.378 GeV
- The strongly coupled QGP description applies for sqrt(s) >= 39 GeV
- The weakly coupled QGP regime is predicted for sqrt(s) < 7.7 GeV
- The critical point from the fRG approach of Fu (2021) shifts to mu_c = 212 MeV after the convention substitution, close to the model's 247 MeV

## Context
This work extends holographic QCD approaches, specifically the dynamical soft-wall model of Ballon-Bayona (2020) and the original soft-wall model of Karch (2006), to describe the Beam Energy Scan data from STAR (2020) and Pandav (2024). It contrasts with hydrodynamics results from Vovchenko (2021) and hadron resonance gas models from Braun-Munzinger (2020), which describe the intermediate energy range (7.7 to 39 GeV) without a critical point. The prediction for a weakly coupled QGP at low energies can be tested by the upcoming STAR Fixed-Target experiments at RHIC, and is consistent with the single data point from HADES (2020).
