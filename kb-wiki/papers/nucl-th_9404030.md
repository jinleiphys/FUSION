# Near-threshold $K^{+}$ Production in Heavy-ion Collisions

> Within a hadronic transport model we study in detail contributions to kaon yields and momentum spectra from various baryon (resonance)-baryon (resonance) and $πN$ interactions in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies near the free-space kaon production threshold. It is found that the finite lifetime of baryon resonances affects significantly the shape of kaon spectra, and the high energy parts of the kaon spectra are dominated by kaons from $πN\rightarrow ΛK^{+}$ processes. $N^{*}(1440)$ resonances are found to contribute about 10\% to the kaon yield. Effects of boosting the Fermi momentum distributions of the two colliding nuclei into their center of mass frame, centrality of the reaction as well as the nuclear equation of state on kaon yields and spectra are also discussed. Model calculations on $K^{+}$, $π^{+}$ and $π^{-}$ spectra for the reaction of Au+Au at $E_{beam}/A= 1.0$ GeV are compared with the experimental data from the KaoS collaboration.

# Near-threshold $K^{+}$ Production in Heavy-ion Collisions

## Key claim
The finite lifetime of baryon resonances significantly affects the shape of kaon spectra (though total yields change by only 5–12%), and the $\pi N \rightarrow \Lambda K^{+}$ channel dominates the high-energy part of kaon spectra, contrary to earlier conclusions that baryon-baryon collisions dominate at all momenta. The nuclear equation of state can be constrained only if the initial Fermi momentum transformation is treated relativistically, as the choice of transformation changes yields comparably to changing the incompressibility from 200 MeV to 380 MeV.

## Method
A hadronic transport model solving coupled transport equations for nucleons, $\Delta(1232)$ and $N^{*}(1440)$ resonances, and pions was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at $E_{beam}/A = 1.0$ GeV. Kaons were calculated perturbatively using the Randrup-Ko parameterizations for baryon-baryon channels ($NN$, $N\Delta$, $\Delta\Delta$, $NN^{*}$, $\Delta N^{*}$, $N^{*}N^{*}$) and the Cugnon parameterization for $\pi N \rightarrow \Lambda K^{+}$, with comparisons between finite-lifetime and frozen-resonance approximations, and between relativistic and non-relativistic Fermi momentum boosts.

## Key numbers
- Total kaon production probability with finite resonance lifetimes and relativistic boost: $P_A = 0.0653$; with frozen-resonance approximation: $P_B = 0.0686$ (Au+Au at 1.0 GeV/nucleon, impact parameter 1.0 fm, soft EOS $K=200$ MeV).
- Total kaon production probability with non-relativistic momentum transformation: $P_C = 0.0453$ (finite lifetimes) and $P_D = 0.0406$ (infinite lifetimes), a decrease of about 40% compared to relativistic transformation.
- Total kaon production probability with stiff EOS ($K=380$ MeV) and relativistic boost: $P = 0.0360$, a reduction of about 45% from the soft EOS case.
- $N^{*}(1440)$ resonances contribute about 10–11% to the total kaon yield across beam energies 1–2 GeV/nucleon and impact parameters 1–9 fm.
- Maximum $N^{*}(1440)$ multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions increases from 7 to 17 as beam energy increases from 1 to 2 GeV/nucleon; maximum $\Delta(1232)$ multiplicity increases from 52 to 89 over the same range.
- Free-space kaon production threshold beam energy: 1.58 GeV; reduced to about 0.8 GeV after accounting for Fermi motion.
- Lorentz $\gamma$ factor for Au+Au at $E_{beam}/A = 1.0$ GeV: 2.1; non-relativistic transformation suppresses nucleon kinetic energy by about 35 MeV on average.

## Context
This work extends earlier transport model studies by Randrup and Ko (1980s) and Aichelin (1985) on subthreshold kaon production as a probe of the nuclear equation of state, and addresses discrepancies among models from the KaoS collaboration data (Senger, 1994). It contradicts the frozen-resonance approximation used by Cugnon (1987), Batko (1990), and Xiong (1990), who neglected $\pi N$ contributions, and shows that the relativistic versus non-relativistic Fermi momentum transformation, used differently by Huang (1993) and Aichelin (1993), changes yields as much as the EOS stiffness. The results enable future studies incorporating momentum-dependent mean fields and in-medium kaon effects, which the author notes could change yields by factors of 2–3.
