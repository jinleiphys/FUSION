# Rapidity Dependence of $J/ψ$ Production at RHIC and LHC

> The motion of charmonium in heavy ion collisions is described by a three dimensional transport equation with initial production and continuous regeneration in hot medium. The observation of apparently stronger $J/ψ$ suppression at forward rapidity compared to that at midrapidity, so called $J/ψ$ puzzle at RHIC, can well be explained by the competition between the two production mechanisms. At LHC, however, the rapidity dependence of the $J/ψ$ production is dominated by the regeneration process.

## Key claim

The observed stronger $J/\psi$ suppression at forward rapidity compared to midrapidity at RHIC (the "J/ψ puzzle") can be explained by the competition between initial production and continuous regeneration, under the assumption that the medium temperature is approximately the same at mid and forward rapidity because the measured forward rapidity still lies on the plateau of the light hadron rapidity distribution.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-dimensional transport equation for charmonium motion in the hot medium, using Lorentz covariant time and space rapidity coordinates, with loss and gain terms describing gluon dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance. They assumed charm quark thermalization, used transverse hydrodynamic equations at y=0 for both rapidity regions, and solved the transport equation analytically to separate contributions from initial production and continuous regeneration. The dissociation and regeneration processes were restricted to the QGP temperature region between the critical temperature Tc and the dissociation temperature Td for each charmonium state.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of forward to mid rapidity R_AA at RHIC for N_p > 150: approximately 0.6
- J/ψ dissociation temperature: T_d^{J/ψ} = 1.9 T_c, with T_c = 165 MeV
- J/ψ fractions from direct production, χ_c decay, and ψ' decay: 6:3:1
- At RHIC, dσ_NN^{c\bar c}/dη|_(η=0) = 120 μb, with the ratio (dσ_NN^{c\bar c}/dη|_(η=1.7))/(dσ_NN^{c\bar c}/dη|_(η=0)) = 1/3
- At LHC, dσ_NN^Ψ/dy_Ψ = 2 μb for J/ψ production in NN collisions
- At LHC, ⟨p_t^2⟩_NN = 12 (GeV/c)^2 for J/ψ production
- At RHIC, ⟨p_t^2⟩_NN = 4.14 (GeV/c)^2 for J/ψ production
- Maximum regeneration fraction at RHIC: around 50%

## Context

This work extends the transport model approach of Yan and Liu (2006) to three dimensions to address the rapidity dependence of J/ψ production, which had been measured by PHENIX (2007) and discussed in models by Kharzeev (2000), Andronic (2007), and Zhao (2008). The results explain the J/ψ puzzle at RHIC that could not be understood with only initial charmonium production, and predict that at LHC the regeneration process dominates the rapidity dependence, with the ratios of forward to mid rapidity R_AA and ⟨p_t^2⟩ approaching regeneration limits already in semi-central collisions.
