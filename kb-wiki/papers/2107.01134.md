# Constraints on the $Λ$-neutron interaction from charge symmetry breaking in the $\mathbf{^4_Λ\rm \bf He}$--$\mathbf{^4_Λ\rm \bf H}$ hypernuclei

> We utilize the experimentally known difference of the $Λ$ separation energies of the mirror hypernuclei ${^4_Λ\rm He}$ and ${^4_Λ\rm H}$ to constrain the $Λ$-neutron interaction. We include the leading charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction into our hyperon-nucleon interaction derived within chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order. In particular, we determine the strength of the two arising CSB contact terms by a fit to the differences of the separation energies of these hypernuclei in the $0^+$ and $1^+$ states, respectively. By construction, the resulting interaction describes all low energy hyperon-nucleon scattering data, the hypertriton and the CSB in ${^4_Λ\rm He}$-${^4_Λ\rm H}$ accurately. This allows us to provide first predictions for the $Λ$n scattering lengths, based solely on available hypernuclear data.

## Key claim

The charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) part of the Λ-neutron interaction can be constrained by fitting two contact terms to the experimentally measured differences of Λ separation energies in the 0⁺ and 1⁺ states of the mirror hypernuclei ⁴_ΛHe and ⁴_ΛH, under the assumption that three-body forces have only a minor influence on these splittings.

## Method

The authors added leading-order charge-symmetry breaking contributions, including Λ-Σ⁰ mixing, π⁰-η mixing, and kaon mass difference effects, to their existing hyperon-nucleon potentials NLO13 and NLO19 derived within chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order. They solved the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space for ⁴_ΛHe and ⁴_ΛH, fitting the strengths of two CSB contact terms (spin-singlet and spin-triplet) to reproduce the observed separation-energy differences ΔE(0⁺) and ΔE(1⁺). The resulting interactions were then used to predict Λ-neutron scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- ΔE(0⁺) = 233 ± 92 keV and ΔE(1⁺) = −83 ± 94 keV, the present experimental values used as the primary CSB scenario (CSB1)
- For NLO13(600) with CSB1: singlet Λn scattering length a_s^{Λn} = −3.291 fm, triplet Λn scattering length a_t^{Λn} = −1.487 fm
- For NLO19(600) with CSB1: singlet Λn scattering length a_s^{Λn} = −3.227 fm, triplet Λn scattering length a_t^{Λn} = −1.362 fm
- The CSB contact-term LECs are of order 10⁻³ to 10⁻² MeV⁻², consistent with power-counting expectations of V^{CSB} ~ (ε M_π² / Λ²) V_BB with ε ≈ 0.3
- The hypertriton binding energy remains unchanged within at most 30 keV across all CSB scenarios

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to hyperon-nucleon interactions (Haidenbauer 2013, Haidenbauer 2019) by adding explicit charge-symmetry breaking, addressing a long-standing puzzle first noted by Dalitz and von Hippel (1964). Earlier four-body calculations using Nijmegen coupled-channel potentials (Nogga 2001) could only explain a fraction of the observed CSB, while recent no-core shell model studies by Gal and Gazda (2015, 2016) proposed a different CSB mechanism based on the ΛN → ΣN transition potential. The present approach enables first predictions of Λ-neutron scattering lengths constrained solely by hypernuclear data, rather than by direct scattering measurements.
