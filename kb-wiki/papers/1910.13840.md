# Equivalence of the phenomenological Tsallis distribution to the transverse momentum distribution of $q$-dual statistics

> In the present work, we have found that the phenomenological Tsallis distribution (which nowadays is largely used to describe the transverse momentum distributions of hadrons measured in $pp$ collisions at high energies) is consistent with the basis of the statistical mechanics if it belongs to the $q$-dual nonextensive statistics instead of the Tsallis one. We have defined the $q$-dual statistics based on the $q$-dual entropy which was obtained from the Tsallis entropy under the multiplicative transformation of the entropic parameter $q\to 1/q$. We have found that the phenomenological Tsallis distribution is equivalent to the transverse momentum distribution of the $q$-dual statistics in the zeroth term approximation. Since the $q$-dual statistics is properly defined, it provides a correct link between the phenomenological Tsallis distribution and the second law of thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The phenomenological Tsallis distribution, widely used to fit hadron transverse momentum spectra in high-energy collisions, is thermodynamically consistent only if it is interpreted as the zeroth term approximation of the $q$-dual nonextensive statistics (based on the $q$-dual entropy obtained from the Tsallis entropy by the transformation $q \to 1/q$), rather than as a distribution of the standard Tsallis statistics.

## Method

The author defines the $q$-dual entropy by applying the multiplicative transformation $q \to 1/q$ to the Tsallis entropy, and formulates the grand canonical ensemble for this statistics using standard expectation values (consistent with probability normalization). The equilibrium probabilities are derived from the second law of thermodynamics via the Jaynes maximum entropy principle. The transverse momentum distribution for a relativistic ideal gas of massive particles is then computed in the zeroth term approximation (retaining only the $n=0$ term in the series expansion of the integral representation), and compared to the phenomenological Tsallis distribution.

## Key numbers

- The $q$-dual entropy is defined as $S = q \sum_i (p_i^{1/q} - p_i)/(q-1)$, which recovers the Boltzmann-Gibbs-Shannon entropy in the limit $q \to 1$.
- The equilibrium probability for the grand canonical ensemble of the $q$-dual statistics is $p_i = [1 + (1-q)(\Lambda - E_i + \mu N_i)/T]^{q/(1-q)}$.
- The phenomenological Tsallis distribution (Eq. 1) is exactly reproduced by the zeroth term approximation of the $q$-dual statistics for Maxwell-Boltzmann particles (Eq. 29): $d^2N/(dp_T dy) = [gV/(2\pi)^2] p_T m_T \cosh y \, [1 - (1-q)(m_T \cosh y - \mu)/T]^{q/(1-q)}$.
- The norm function $\Lambda$ in the zeroth term approximation is $\Lambda = 0$.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing issue with the phenomenological Tsallis distribution (Cleymans 2012, Cleymans 2012a), which was previously shown to correspond to the zeroth term approximation of the Tsallis-2 statistics (Parvan 2017, Parvan 2019). However, the Tsallis-2 statistics is improperly defined because its generalized expectation values $\langle A \rangle = \sum_i p_i^q A_i$ are inconsistent with the probability normalization condition $\sum_i p_i = 1$, violating the second law of thermodynamics. By introducing the $q$-dual statistics, the author provides a thermodynamically sound foundation for the phenomenological Tsallis distribution. The work also shows that the phenomenological Tsallis distributions for quantum (Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein) statistics do not match the zeroth term approximation of either the $q$-dual or Tsallis statistics, leaving those cases as open questions.
