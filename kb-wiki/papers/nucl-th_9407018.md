# The relativistic transport model description of subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions

> The relativistic transport model, in which the nucleon effective mass is connected to the scalar field while its energy is shifted by the vector potential, is extended to include the kaon degree of freedom. We further take into account the medium modification of the kaon mass due to the explicit chiral symmetry breaking. Both the propagation of kaons in the mean-field potential and the kaon-baryon elastic scattering are explicitly treated in our study. We find that the attractive kaon scalar mean-field potential in the dense matter leads to an enhanced kaon yield in heavy-ion collisions at energies of about 1 GeV/nucleon. The final-state kaon-baryon scattering is seen to affect significantly the kaon momentum spectra, leading to an enhanced yield of kaons with large momenta or at large laboratory angles. With a soft nuclear equation of state and including the attractive kaon scalar potential, the calculated kaon energy spectra agree with the data from the heavy-ion synchrotron at GSI.

## Key claim

The attractive kaon scalar mean-field potential in dense matter, combined with a soft nuclear equation of state, enhances subthreshold kaon production yields in heavy-ion collisions at about 1 GeV/nucleon, and the calculated kaon energy spectra agree with preliminary GSI data only when both the kaon scalar potential and final-state kaon-baryon rescattering are included.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic transport model (based on quantum hadrodynamics with scalar and vector mean fields) to include kaon degrees of freedom, treating kaon propagation in the mean-field potential and kaon-baryon elastic scattering explicitly. Kaon production from baryon-baryon interactions (nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-delta, delta-delta) was calculated perturbatively using in-medium masses for baryons, hyperons, and kaons, with the kaon in-medium mass determined from the chiral Lagrangian including explicit chiral symmetry breaking. The transport equation was solved by the pseudoparticle method, and kaon rescattering was included by artificially producing multiple kaons per event with production probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for kaon production in free nucleon-nucleon interactions is 1.56 GeV; the experiments studied are at 1 GeV/nucleon, which is subthreshold.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{KN}\) is in the range 300 MeV to 600 MeV; a conservative value of 350 MeV was used.
- The kaon decay constant \(f_K\) is approximately 93 MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon total cross section used for rescattering is about 10 mb.
- For a head-on Au+Au collision at 1 GeV/nucleon, a dense matter with baryon density 2-3\(\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)) is formed for about 15 fm/c.
- The kaon abundance in Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon reaches a maximum of about 0.4 at about 15 fm/c.
- The nucleon-delta interaction accounts for more than half of the total kaon yield; the sum of nucleon-delta and delta-delta contributions is about 75% of the total.
- With the soft equation of state (incompressibility 200 MeV), the kaon yield is about 15% larger than with the stiff equation of state (incompressibility 380 MeV).
- The kaon production cross section from nucleon-nucleon interactions in free space is parametrized as \(\sigma_{NN\rightarrow BYK^+} = 36 (p_{\rm max}/m_K)\) \(\mu\)b.
- The nucleon-delta kaon production cross section is approximately 3/4 of the nucleon-nucleon cross section; the delta-delta cross section is approximately 1/2 of the nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic transport model of Ko et al. (1987) to include kaon production, building on earlier studies of subthreshold kaon production by Aichelin and Ko (1985) and others that used non-relativistic transport models or cascade models. The paper addresses the sensitivity of kaon yields to the nuclear equation of state, first suggested by Aichelin and Ko (1985), and the medium modification of kaon properties from chiral Lagrangian studies by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and Brown et al. (1987, 1988). The results enable further investigation of kaon properties in dense matter and the nuclear matter incompressibility, particularly at incident energies around 600-800 MeV/nucleon where the sensitivity to the equation of state is expected to be larger.
