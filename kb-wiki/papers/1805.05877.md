# Coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering off $^4$He

> Coherent Deeply virtual Compton scattering off the $^4$He nucleus is studied in impulse approximation. A convolution formula for the nuclear Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) is derived in terms of the $^4$He non-diagonal spectral function and of the GPD of the struck nucleon. A model of the nuclear non diagonal spectral function, based on the momentum distribution corresponding to the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, is used in the actual calculation. Typical impulse approximation results are reproduced, in proper limits, for the nuclear form factor and for nuclear parton distributions. The nuclear generalized parton distribution and the Compton form factor are evaluated using, as nucleonic ingredient, a well known generalized parton distribution model. An overall very good agreement is found with the data recently published by the EG6 experiment at the Jefferson Laboratory (JLab). More refined nuclear calculations are addressed and will be necessary for the expected improved accuracy of the next generation of experiments at JLab, with the 12 GeV electron beam and high luminosity.

## Key claim

The impulse approximation (IA) framework, using a model of the non-diagonal spectral function based on the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction and the Goloskokov-Kroll nucleon GPD model, reproduces the EG6 experimental data for coherent DVCS off $^4$He without requiring exotic arguments such as dynamical off-shellness, though more refined nuclear calculations will be necessary for the expected improved accuracy of next-generation experiments at JLab with the 12 GeV electron beam.

## Method

A convolution formula for the nuclear Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) of $^4$He was derived in terms of the non-diagonal spectral function of the nucleus and the GPD of the struck nucleon, using the impulse approximation to the handbag diagram at leading twist. The nuclear non-diagonal spectral function was modeled using variational wave functions for the 4-body and 3-body systems obtained through the hyperspherical harmonics method with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction including UIX three-body forces, employing a closure approximation for the excited sector. The nucleonic GPD input was taken from the Goloskokov-Kroll model, which uses a Double Distributions representation and a Regge-inspired profile function for the t-dependence.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square radius of $^4$He obtained from the GPD calculation is $\sqrt{\langle r^2_{rms}\rangle} \simeq 1.80$ fm, compared with the experimental value of 1.671(14) fm from Ottermann (1985).
- The present approach predicts a vanishing DVCS cross section already for $\xi$ as small as 0.15, representing the width of the light-cone momentum distribution.
- The momentum sum rule is violated by a few percent in the instant-form calculation.
- The results in the Laboratory frame or the Breit frame differ at most by a few parts in one thousand for the observables shown.
- The GK model is supposed to work properly at $Q^2 > 4$ GeV$^2$, but was forced to validity at much lower $Q^2$ values in this analysis.

## Context

This work extends the impulse approximation analysis previously applied to $^3$He by Scopetta (2004, 2009) and Rinaldi (2012, 2014) to the $^4$He nucleus, which is spinless and deeply bound, making it a prototype for finite nuclei. The results are compared with the first successful separation of coherent and incoherent DVCS contributions from the EG6 experiment at JLab by Hattawy (2017), and the approach provides a benchmark for the upcoming ALERT collaboration program at JLab12 with the 12 GeV beam. The paper notes that earlier models proposed by Guzey (2003), Liuti (2005), and Belitsky (2008) could not fully describe the collected data, and that a realistic evaluation of the non-diagonal spectral function of $^4$He is needed for future precise measurements.
