# Finite Isospin Chiral Perturbation Theory in a Magnetic Field

> The phase diagram of finite isospin, zero temperature QCD with the pions coupled to photons in a uniform external magnetic field is explored in the low field, small isospin density regime for which chiral perturbation theory is a valid description. For realistic pion masses, the system behaves as a type-II superconductor: a uniform superconducting state is formed at low-enough magnetic fields, a vortex state for intermediate magnetic fields and finally a normal state for large magnetic fields. In each these phases (including the vortex phase), the $π^{0}$ remains uncondensed just as in the zero-external field problem. The critical magnetic field where the phase transition from the uniform superconducting state to a vortex state occurs was found numerically.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for realistic pion masses, finite-isospin QCD in a low magnetic field behaves as a type-II superconductor, with a uniform superconducting state at low fields, a vortex state at intermediate fields, and a normal state at high fields; however, this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the analysis is performed only at leading order in chiral perturbation theory and assumes the regime where all physical parameters (isospin chemical potential, pion mass, pion momenta, and sqrt(eH)) are much smaller than the typical hadronic scale of 4πfπ.

## Method

The authors use leading-order chiral perturbation theory (χPT) with an SU(2) matrix ansatz for the pion fields, coupling the pions to dynamical photons and a uniform external magnetic field via a covariant derivative that includes both the isospin chemical potential and the electromagnetic gauge field. They derive the effective Lagrangian and Gibbs free energy density, then analyze the superconducting type by comparing the photon mass (penetration depth) to the charged pion mass (coherence length). For the type-II regime, they numerically minimize the Gibbs free energy of a single vortex state with quantized flux (n=1) by discretizing the Hamiltonian and solving for the radial profiles of the gauge field and charged pion field, using the pion decay constant fπ to set the scale.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ ≈ 135 MeV and pion decay constant fπ ≈ 93 MeV are used as physical input parameters.
- The critical magnetic field for the transition from normal to uniform superconducting state (type-I scenario) is |Hc| = fπ(μI² - mπ²)/μI.
- Numerical solutions for the vortex state are obtained for dimensionless parameters m̃π = mπ/fπ = 1.5 (realistic pion mass) and μ̃I = μI/fπ up to 2.5, with e = 1 assumed.
- The numerical error in the critical field Hc1 is of order O(a²), where a is the cell size; the maximum radial integration length Δr̃ = 22 with N = 160 discretization points.

## Context

This work extends the finite-isospin chiral perturbation theory framework of Son and Stephanov (2001) by adding a uniform external magnetic field and dynamical photons, building on earlier lattice studies by Endrodi (2014) that found a uniform diamagnetic phase at finite isospin with magnetic fields. The paper enables future studies of the vortex lattice structure and the upper critical field Hc2 in finite-isospin QCD, and provides analytic insight into how QCD behaves in magnetic environments relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions, complementing lattice approaches that face a fermion sign problem when both isospin chemical potential and magnetic fields are present.
