# Hydrodynamic Evolution of Chirally Symmetric Nuclear Matter

> The equation of state of the linear sigma model in the mean field approximation is used as input in a relativistic hydrodynamical numerical routine. Longitudinal and transverse energy distributions are calculated and compared with those obtained from the QHD-I equation of state.

## Key claim
The linear sigma model in the mean field approximation, when used as input for relativistic hydrodynamics, produces longitudinal and transverse energy distributions that differ from those obtained with the QHD-I equation of state, specifically because the chiral symmetry constraint fixes the nucleon-scalar meson coupling and forces the effective nucleon mass to zero after the phase transition, leading to a faster transition and a gas of massless particles with larger pressure.

## Method
The equation of state derived from the linear sigma model Lagrangian (including nucleon, vector meson, scalar meson, and pion fields) in the mean field approximation was used as input in a relativistic hydrodynamical numerical routine. Longitudinal and transverse energy distributions were calculated and compared with those obtained from the QHD-I (Walecka model) equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon-scalar meson coupling is fixed by chiral symmetry to \( g = g_\pi \), in contrast to other effective models where it is a free parameter.
- The chiral gap equation always admits a solution with \( M^* = 0 \), which makes the phase transition happen faster and immediately after it nucleons become massless.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier application of QHD-I to relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Navarra and Ornik, 1994), where the QHD-I equation of state was found to be too hard, producing too much pressure and too little entropy. The linear sigma model approach introduces chiral symmetry constraints that fix the scalar coupling and force a massless nucleon phase, in contrast to QHD-I where the scalar coupling is a free parameter. This enables investigation of how chiral symmetry affects the thermodynamic properties of hot and dense nuclear matter in hydrodynamic evolution.
