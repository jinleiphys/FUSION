# Isospin Symmetry violation in mirror E1 transitions: Coherent contributions from the Giant Isovector Monopole Resonance in 67As - 67Se

> The assumption of an exact isospin symmetry would imply equal strengths for mirror E1 transitions (at least, in the long-wavelength limit). Actually, large violations of this symmetry rule have been indicated by a number of experimental results, the last of which is the 67As - 67Se doublet investigated at GAMMASPHERE. Here, we examine in detail various possible origins of the observed asymmetry. The coherent effect of Coulomb-induced mixing with the high-lying Giant Isovector Monopole Resonance is proposed as the most probable process to produce a large asymmetry in the E1 transitions, with comparatively small effect on the other properties of the parent and daughter levels.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the coherent effect of Coulomb-induced mixing with the high-lying Giant Isovector Monopole Resonance (GIVMR) is the most probable process to produce a large asymmetry in mirror E1 transitions, with comparatively small effect on other properties of the parent and daughter levels; this mechanism is presented as a semi-quantitative explanation rather than a fully proven one.

## Method

The authors derive the E1 transition amplitude from an intruder single-particle orbital to a normal-parity orbital using fractional parentage expansions and the Wigner-Eckart theorem, separating isovector and isoscalar contributions. They then evaluate several possible sources of isospin symmetry violation: higher-order terms beyond the Siegert theorem, Coulomb mixing between close-lying states, analogue-antianalogue mixing, and coherent contributions from the Giant Isovector Monopole Resonance. The calculations are benchmarked against the mirror pair 67As-67Se using Woods-Saxon radial wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- B(E1) for the 725 keV transition in 67As: (1.4 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁶ e² fm², corresponding to a reduced matrix element of (3.7 ± 0.5) × 10⁻³ e fm
- B(E1) for the 717 keV transition in 67Se: (0.4 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁶ e² fm², corresponding to a reduced matrix element of (2.0 ± 2.0) × 10⁻³ e fm
- B(E1) for the 319 keV transition in 67As: (8.3 ± 2.5) × 10⁻⁶ e² fm², corresponding to a reduced matrix element of (9.1 ± 1.3) × 10⁻³ e fm
- B(E1) for the 303 keV transition in 67Se: less than 1.4(9) × 10⁻⁶ e² fm², corresponding to a reduced matrix element less than 3.7(11) × 10⁻³ e fm
- Ratio of isoscalar to isovector single-particle matrix element from higher-order Siegert corrections: 1.90 × 10⁻⁵ (Eγ[MeV])² for the g9/2 → f7/2 transition in A=67
- Ratio of isoscalar to isovector single-particle matrix element from magnetic contributions: approximately 2 × 10⁻⁴ (Eγ[MeV]) for the g9/2 → f7/2 transition
- Analogue-antianalogue mixing coefficient α ≈ 0.071 for A=67, assuming ΔE ≈ 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin symmetry violation in mirror E1 transitions, following experimental observations of large asymmetries in several mirror pairs including the 67As-67Se doublet investigated at GAMMASPHERE (letter, 2012). The authors examine and rule out several simple mechanisms: higher-order terms beyond the Siegert theorem (following Friar and Fallieros), mixing between close-lying states, and analogue-antianalogue mixing (discussed by Pattabiraman et al., 2008). The proposed coherent contribution from the Giant Isovector Monopole Resonance provides a new framework for understanding these asymmetries and could enable semi-quantitative predictions for other mirror pairs without requiring full shell-model calculations in huge Hilbert spaces.
