# Covariant Density Functional Theory with Localized Exchange Terms

> A new density-dependent point-coupling covariant density functional PCF-PK1 is proposed, where the exchange terms of the four-fermion terms are local and are taken into account with the Fierz transformation. The coupling constants of the PCF-PK1 functional are determined by empirical saturation properties and ab initio equation of state and proton-neutron Dirac mass splittings for nuclear matter as well as the ground-state properties of selected spherical nuclei. The success of the PCF-PK1 is illustrated with properties of the infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei including the ground-state properties and the Gamow-Teller resonances. In particular, the PCFPK1 eliminates the spurious shell closures at Z = 58 and Z = 92, which exist commonly in many covariant density functionals without exchange terms. Moreover, the Gamow-Teller resonances are nicely reproduced without any adjustable parameters, and this demonstrates that a self-consistent description for the Gamow-Teller resonances can be achieved with the localized exchange terms in the PCF-PK1. ?

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new covariant density functional, PCF-PK1, that includes localized exchange terms via the Fierz transformation, and claims that this eliminates spurious shell closures at Z=58 and Z=92 that are common in functionals without exchange terms, while also reproducing Gamow-Teller resonances without adjustable parameters. The hedge is that the exchange terms are localized (approximated as superpositions of direct terms) rather than treated exactly as in the full relativistic Hartree-Fock approach.

## Method

The authors constructed a density-dependent point-coupling Lagrangian with four-fermion, derivative, and electromagnetic terms, and used the Fierz transformation to express the exchange (Fock) terms as superpositions of direct (Hartree) terms, yielding five independent coupling channels (S, tS, V, tV, T). The coupling constants were determined in a multi-step fitting procedure: first, the isoscalar channels (S, V) were fit to symmetric nuclear matter properties including saturation density, energy per nucleon, Dirac mass, compression modulus, and the ab initio equation of state from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998); second, the isovector-scalar channel (tS) was fit to proton-neutron Dirac mass splittings from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by van Dalen et al.; third, the isovector-vector channel (tV) was fit to the symmetry energy; finally, the remaining parameters (density dependence of tV, tensor coupling α_T, derivative term δ_S, and pairing strength G) were fit to ground-state properties of 60 spherical nuclei, including binding energies, charge radii, two-nucleon separation energies, and pairing gaps.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_sat: five values in the range 0.152 to 0.160 fm^{-3} were used as fitting targets
- Energy per nucleon at saturation E/A|_ρ_sat: nine values in the range -16.00 to -16.16 MeV were used as fitting targets
- Dirac mass M*_D/M: three values (0.58, 0.70, 0.80) were used as fitting targets
- Compression modulus K: fixed at 230 MeV
- Symmetry energy J: fixed at 33 MeV
- Binding energy fitting weight: 1.0 MeV; charge radius fitting weight: 0.01 fm; two-nucleon separation energy weight: 0.07 MeV; pairing gap weight: 0.05 MeV
- For spherical calculations: 30 major harmonic oscillator shells; for deformed calculations: 24 major shells
- Oscillator frequency: ℏω_0 = 70A^{-1/3} MeV
- For ^208Pb, increasing major shells from N_f=30 to N_f=32 changed binding energy by 0.0006%, charge radius by 0.0004%, and neutron skin thickness by 0.05%
- For ^240U, increasing major shells from N_f=24 to N_f=26 changed binding energy by 0.007%

## Context

This work extends the relativistic point-coupling model approach of Nikolaus et al. (1992) and Zhao et al. (2010) by incorporating localized exchange terms, building on the zero-range reduction and Fierz transformation method studied by Liang et al. (2012). It contrasts with the full relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach of Bouyssy et al. (1987) and Long et al. (2006), which includes nonlocal potentials that are computationally expensive, especially for deformed nuclei. The new functional enables self-consistent description of Gamow-Teller resonances without adjustable parameters and eliminates spurious shell closures, providing a practical alternative to RHF for systematic studies of finite nuclei across the nuclear chart.
