# Different space-time freeze-out picture -- an explanation of different $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization?

> Thermal vorticity in non-central Au+Au collisions at energies $7.7 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 62.4$ GeV is calculated within the UrQMD transport model. Tracing the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons back to their last interaction point we were able to obtain the temperature and the chemical potentials at the time of emission by fitting the extracted bulk characteristics of hot and dense medium to statistical model of ideal hadron gas. Then the polarization of both hyperons was calculated. The polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ increases with decreasing energy of nuclear collisions. The stronger polarization of $\barΛ$ is explained by the different space-time distributions of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ and by different freeze-out conditions of both hyperons.

## Key claim

The difference in global polarization between Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) hyperons in non-central Au+Au collisions at Beam Energy Scan energies can be explained by their different space-time freeze-out distributions and different freeze-out conditions with respect to the thermal vorticity field, without invoking anomalous mechanisms such as the chiral vortical effect.

## Method

The UrQMD transport model was used to simulate non-central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 62.4 GeV with impact parameters b = 6 fm and b = 9 fm. The space-time volume was subdivided into 1 fm³ cells, and for each cell the energy density, net baryon density, and net strangeness density were extracted as functions of time. These bulk characteristics were fitted to a statistical model of an ideal hadron gas to obtain the temperature T, baryon chemical potential μ_B, and strangeness chemical potential μ_S at each cell. The thermal vorticity tensor was then computed from the temperature and velocity fields, and the spin 4-vector of each Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) hyperon was calculated using the thermal vorticity at its last interaction point, with the global polarization obtained by projecting the averaged spin onto the direction of the global orbital angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Mean freeze-out time for Λ at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV: \(\langle t_{\Lambda}^{FO} \rangle = 21.3009\) fm/c; for \(\bar{Λ}\): \(\langle t_{\bar{Λ}}^{FO} \rangle = 19.7806\) fm/c, showing \(\bar{Λ}\) hyperons are emitted about 1 fm/c earlier on average.
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV, the maximum of the Λ distribution over the thermal vorticity component \(\varpi_{zx}\) is at \(\varpi_{zx}^\Lambda \simeq -0.016\), while for \(\bar{Λ}\) the maximum is at \(\varpi_{zx}^{\bar{Λ}} \simeq -0.005\).
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 62.4\) GeV, both hyperon species have their maxima at \(\varpi_{zx}^{\Lambda/\bar{Λ}} \simeq -0.0014\).
- Global polarization of both hyperons drops from 20-60% at very early times to about 0.5% at t = 10 fm/c.
- The correction to global Λ polarization from decays of Σ hyperons was found to be about 4% for Au+Au collisions with b = 6 fm at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV.
- For collisions at \(\sqrt{s} \geq 11.6\) GeV, the UrQMD results for polarization are very close to the STAR data; the model cannot match the magnitude of \(\bar{Λ}\) polarization measured at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV, where polarization in collisions with b = 9 fm increases to approximately 3%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly addressing the discrepancy between the similar polarizations predicted by kinetic and hydrodynamic models (e.g., Becattini (2015), Karpenko and Becattini (2017), Ivanov and Soldatov (2018)) and the larger \(\bar{Λ}\) polarization observed by the STAR Collaboration at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV. The authors argue that the difference arises naturally from the different space-time freeze-out conditions of Λ and \(\bar{Λ}\) within the thermal vorticity field, rather than requiring the anomalous mechanism related to the axial vortical effect proposed by Sorin and Teryaev (2017) or the interaction between hyperon spins and baryon current vorticity discussed by Csernai et al. (2019). The results enable further investigation of the sudden rise of \(\bar{Λ}\) polarization at low energies and suggest that more peripheral collisions or increased statistics may resolve the remaining discrepancy at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.7\) GeV.
