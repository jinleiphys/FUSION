# Kaons and antikaons in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The properties of kaons and antikaons and their modification in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated using a chiral SU(3) model. These isospin dependent medium effects are important for asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments. In the present work, the medium modifications of the energies of the kaons and antikaons, within the asymmetric nuclear matter, arise due to the interactions of kaons and antikaons with the nucleons and scalar mesons. The values of the parameters in the model are obtained by fitting the saturation properties of nuclear matter and kaon-nucleon scattering lengths. The pion-nucleon scattering lengths are also calculated within the chiral effective model and compared with earlier results from the literature. The density dependence of the isospin asymmetry is seen to be appreciable for the kaon and antikaon optical potentials. This can be particularly relevant for the future accelerator facility FAIR at GSI, where experiments using neutron rich beams are planned to be used in the study of compressed baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the optical potentials of kaons and antikaons in asymmetric nuclear matter show appreciable density dependence of isospin asymmetry effects, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific chiral SU(3) model parameters fitted to saturation properties of nuclear matter and kaon-nucleon scattering lengths, and only the real parts of the self energies are addressed.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) model in the mean-field approximation to calculate the in-medium energies of kaons and antikaons in asymmetric nuclear matter. The dispersion relations were derived from an interaction Lagrangian that includes the Weinberg-Tomozawa vectorial interaction, scalar meson exchange from explicit symmetry breaking, a range term from the kinetic term of pseudoscalar mesons, and additional contact terms with parameters d1 and d2 fitted to empirical KN scattering lengths for I=0 and I=1 channels. The scalar-isovector delta field and the vectorial interaction with nucleons provided the isospin asymmetry contributions.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged KN scattering length used as input is approximately -0.255 fm, with individual channel values a_KN(I=0) ≈ -0.31 fm and a_KN(I=1) ≈ -0.09 fm.
- The symmetry energy a_4 at saturation nuclear matter density (ρ0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}) is 31.7 MeV.
- The pion-nucleon scattering lengths calculated in the model are a_πN(I=3/2) = -0.1474 fm and a_πN(I=1/2) = 0.1823 fm, yielding isoscalar and isovector scattering lengths a_+ = -0.0266/m_π and a_- = 0.078/m_π.
- The πN and KN sigma terms are calculated as 44 MeV and 725 MeV respectively.
- The parameters d1 and d2 fitted from KN scattering lengths are 5.5/m_K and 0.66/m_K respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and antikaon properties in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter within the same chiral SU(3) model (Mishra et al., 2004) to include isospin asymmetry effects relevant for neutron-rich heavy ion collisions. The results are relevant for the FAIR facility at GSI, where experiments with neutron-rich beams are planned to study compressed baryonic matter. The paper also calculates pion-nucleon scattering lengths and compares them with results from pionic atom data (Beane, 2005), empirical π-p and π-d scattering lengths (Ericson, 2002), and pionic deuterium shift measurements by the PSI group (2005).
