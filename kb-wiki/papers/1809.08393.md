# Quantum and Coulomb repulsion effects on the bubble structures in $^{204, 206}$Hg

> The decreasing proton and charge densities from around 5.0 fm towards the center of $^{204, 206}$Hg are investigated by a covariant density functional theory at the beyond mean-field level.The charge-density difference between $^{208}$Pb and $^{204}$Hg is improved significantly and a central depression is still visible in the ground-state density of $^{204, 206}$Hg when the dynamic correlations associated with symmetry restoration and shape mixing are taken into account. For the $0_2^+$ and $2_1^+$ excited states of $^{204, 206}$Hg, their densities remain decreasing from 5.0 fm to around 2.0 fm, but become flat in the interior region. The results show that the bubble structure in $^{204, 206}$Hg within 2.0 fm is mainly attributed to the quantum effect, while that beyond 2.0 fm is formed by the Coulomb repulsion.

# Quantum and Coulomb repulsion effects on the bubble structures in $^{204, 206}$Hg

## Key claim

The bubble structure (central depression of proton and charge densities) in $^{204, 206}$Hg is robust under dynamic correlations from symmetry restoration and shape mixing, with the depression within 2.0 fm attributed mainly to quantum shell effects (vacancy of the proton $3s_{1/2}$ orbit) and that beyond 2.0 fm formed by Coulomb repulsion; however, the central depression in the $0_2^+$ and $2_1^+$ excited states becomes flat in the interior region while still decreasing from 5.0 fm to around 2.0 fm.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference energy density functional (MR-EDF) calculations based on a relativistic point-coupling energy functional (PC-PK1 force), using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with angular-momentum, neutron-number, and proton-number projection. Axially deformed mean-field states were generated from relativistic mean-field plus BCS calculations constrained on the mass quadrupole moment, and the wave functions of low-lying states were constructed as superpositions of these projected states. Proton and charge densities were computed in the laboratory frame, with charge densities obtained by convolving proton densities with a Gaussian form factor for the finite proton size.

## Key numbers

- Occupation probability of the proton $3s_{1/2}$ orbit in $^{204}$Hg and $^{206}$Hg is approximately 48% and 52% of that in $^{208}$Pb, respectively.
- For the GCM ground state of $^{206}$Hg: central proton density $\rho_{cent}^p = 0.051$ fm$^{-3}$, maximal proton density $\rho_{max}^p = 0.063$ fm$^{-3}$, depletion factor $F_{max}^p = 0.18$.
- For the GCM ground state of $^{206}$Hg: central charge density $\rho_{cent}^{ch} = 0.053$ fm$^{-3}$, maximal charge density $\rho_{max}^{ch} = 0.063$ fm$^{-3}$, depletion factor $F_{max}^{ch} = 0.16$.
- For the $0_2^+$ state in $^{204}$Hg: central proton density $\rho_{cent}^p = 0.057$ fm$^{-3}$, $F_{max}^p = 0.10$; for the $2_1^+$ state: $\rho_{cent}^p = 0.056$ fm$^{-3}$, $F_{max}^p = 0.12$.
- For the $0_2^+$ state in $^{206}$Hg: central proton density $\rho_{cent}^p = 0.056$ fm$^{-3}$, $F_{max}^p = 0.10$; for the $2_1^+$ state: $\rho_{cent}^p = 0.054$ fm$^{-3}$, $F_{max}^p = 0.14$.
- The mean quadrupole deformation parameter of the GCM ground state for $^{208}$Pb and $^{204, 206}$Hg is $\bar\beta_{01} \simeq 0.02$.
- The charge-density difference between $^{208}$Pb and $^{204}$Hg is improved significantly by dynamic correlations, though the central bump remains overestimated compared to experimental data from Euteneuer (1978) and Burghardt (1989).

## Context

This work extends beyond mean-field studies of bubble nuclei, following earlier MR-EDF calculations for $^{34}$Si and $^{46}$Ar by Yao (2012, 2013) and Wu (2014), which found that dynamic correlations can quench central depletion in lighter systems. The authors revisit the mercury isotopes $^{204, 206}$Hg, where previous mean-field calculations by Todd-Rutel (2004) predicted a proton hollow but spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Wang (2015) and Li (2016) did not support it. The results show that unlike $^{34}$Si, where bubble structure is unlikely in excited states (Yao 2013, Wu 2017), the semi-bubble structure in $^{204, 206}$Hg persists in both ground and low-lying excited states due to the additional Coulomb repulsion mechanism. This enables future experimental searches for bubble signatures in heavy nuclei via electron scattering form factors.
