# Separable Representation of Proton-Nucleus Optical Potentials

> Recently, a new approach for solving the three-body problem for (d,p) reactions in the Coulomb-distorted basis in momentum space was proposed. Important input quantities for such calculations are the scattering matrix elements for proton- and neutron-nucleus scattering. We present a generalization of the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler scheme in which a momentum space separable representation of proton-nucleus scattering matrix elements can be calculated in the Coulomb basis. The viability of this method is demonstrated by comparing S-matrix elements obtained for p$+^{48}$Ca and p$+^{208}$Pb for a phenomenological optical potential with corresponding coordinate space calculations.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) scheme to produce a separable representation of proton-nucleus scattering matrix elements in a Coulomb-distorted momentum-space basis, rather than the standard plane-wave basis. The method is demonstrated to be viable for phenomenological optical potentials, but the authors note that the short-range Coulomb potential causes the proton-nucleus form factor to fall off more slowly at high momenta than the neutron-nucleus form factor, requiring larger integration grids for equivalent accuracy.

## Method

The authors solve the momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the Coulomb basis using Coulomb wavefunctions, following the method of Elster (1993). They then extend the EST separable representation formalism to charged-particle scattering by replacing the neutron-nucleus half-shell t-matrix with the Coulomb-distorted nuclear half-shell t-matrix. The form factors are computed from the short-range part of the CH89 global optical potential (Woods-Saxon nuclear plus short-range Coulomb from a uniformly charged sphere) for p+48Ca and p+208Pb, and the resulting S-matrix elements are compared with direct coordinate-space calculations.

## Key numbers

- For p+48Ca elastic scattering at Elab = 38 MeV, the separable representation yields S-matrix elements for l=0 of (-0.0512 + 0.3765i), compared to the coordinate-space calculation of (-0.0523 + 0.3767i)
- For p+48Ca at 38 MeV, the separable representation yields S-matrix elements for l=10 of (0.9818 + 0.0248i), compared to the coordinate-space calculation of (0.9814 + 0.0253i)
- For p+208Pb at 10 MeV laboratory kinetic energy, the partial-wave series converges by l=12
- For p+48Ca, the form factors vanish at 3.5 fm^-1 for all angular momenta except l=0
- For p+208Pb, the form factors vanish at 3.5 fm^-1 for all angular momenta except l=0, 1, 2
- The short-range Coulomb potential effects on the form factor become negligible by l=6 for 48Ca and l=8 for 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the EST scheme (Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler, 1973) from plane-wave to Coulomb-distorted bases, building on the authors' previous generalization for neutron-nucleus optical potentials (Hlophe, 2013). It is motivated by the three-body theory for (d,p) reactions developed by Mukhamedzhanov (2012), which requires separable representations of proton-nucleus interactions in the Coulomb basis. The method enables Faddeev-AGS calculations for (d,p) reactions on heavier nuclei without the screening and renormalization procedure used by Deltuva (2005, 2009), which becomes technically difficult for high charges.
