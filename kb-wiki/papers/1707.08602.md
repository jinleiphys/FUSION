# Phenomenology of collinear photon emission from quark-gluon plasma in $AA$ collisions

> We study the role of running coupling and the effect of variation of the thermal quark mass on contribution of the collinear bremsstrahlung and annihilation to photon emission in $AA$ collisions in a scheme similar to that used in our previous jet quenching analyses. We find that for a scenario with the thermal quark mass $m_q\sim 50-100$ MeV contribution of the higher order collinear processes summed with the $2\to 2$ processes can explain a considerable part ($\sim 50$\%) of the experimental photon spectrum at $k_T\sim 2-3$ GeV for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV. But for $m_q=300$ MeV and for the thermal quark mass predicted by the HTL scheme the theoretical predictions underestimate considerably the experimental spectrum.

## Key claim
The paper finds that for a scenario with a very small thermal quark mass (m_q=50 MeV), the sum of higher-order collinear processes and leading-order 2→2 processes can explain approximately 50% of the experimental photon spectrum at transverse momentum k_T ~ 2-3 GeV in Au+Au collisions at √s=0.2 TeV, but only under the condition that the quark-gluon plasma evolution model is consistent with jet quenching data. For larger thermal quark masses (m_q=300 MeV or the HTL scheme prediction), the theoretical predictions underestimate the experimental spectrum considerably.

## Method
The author calculates the photon emission rate from collinear bremsstrahlung (q→γq) and annihilation (q\bar{q}→γ) processes in the quark-gluon plasma using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach, reducing the calculation to solving a two-dimensional Schrodinger equation. The analysis uses a phenomenological scheme with running QCD coupling (frozen at α_s^{fr}=0.5 at low momenta) and compares results to the pQCD hard thermal loop (HTL) scheme with static coupling α_s=0.3. The space-time integration over the plasma fireball is performed using the Bjorken model with proper time τ_0=0.5 fm and initial entropy density from the two-component wounded nucleon Glauber model.

## Key numbers
- For the phenomenological scenario with running coupling and m_q=50 MeV, the sum of collinear processes and 2→2 processes explains ~50% of the experimental photon yield from PHENIX at k_T ~ 2-3 GeV for Au+Au collisions at √s=0.2 TeV
- For the HTL scenario with static α_s=0.3, the theoretical curves for the sum of collinear and LO 2→2 processes underpredict the data by a factor of ~5-7 at k_T ≥ 1.5-2 GeV
- For the phenomenological scenario with m_q=50 MeV, the photon spectrum is bigger than that for the HTL scenario by a factor of ~2
- The photon spectrum for m_q=100 MeV is smaller than that for m_q=50 MeV by only ~20%
- For τ_0=0.25 fm (compared to the standard τ_0=0.5 fm), theoretical predictions increase by a factor of ~2 at k_T ~ 2-3 GeV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous jet quenching analyses (Zakharov 2008, 2012) that used running coupling and the LCPI approach to successfully describe nuclear modification factors R_AA at RHIC and LHC. It directly addresses the longstanding discrepancy between thermal photon predictions and PHENIX data (PHENIX 2010), where sophisticated viscous hydrodynamical models (Gale et al.) underestimate the photon spectrum by a factor of ~3. The paper contrasts its phenomenological approach with the AMY formalism (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe 2001) for fixed coupling, and discusses alternative mechanisms including glasma phase contributions (glasma_L, glasma_L2, glasma_W) and boundary bremsstrahlung (Snigirev) that could potentially resolve the remaining deficit at high k_T.
