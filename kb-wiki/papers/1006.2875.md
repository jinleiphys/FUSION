# Racah's method for general subalgebra chains: Coupling coefficients of SO(5) in canonical and physical bases

> It is shown that the method of infinitesimal generators ("Racah's method") can be broadly and systematically formulated as a method applicable to the calculation of reduced coupling coefficients for a generic subalgebra chain G>H, provided the reduced matrix elements of the generators of G and the recoupling coefficients of H are known. The calculation of SO(5)>SO(4) reduced coupling coefficients is considered as an example, and a procedure for transformation of reduced coupling coefficients between canonical and physical subalegebra chains is presented. The problem of calculating coupling coefficients for generic irreps of SO(5), reduced with respect to any of its subalgebra chains, is completely resolved by this approach.

## Key claim

Racah's method (the infinitesimal generator approach) can be systematically formulated as a method applicable to calculating reduced coupling coefficients for any subalgebra chain G > H, provided the reduced matrix elements of the generators of G and the recoupling coefficients of H are known, and the problem of calculating coupling coefficients for generic irreps of SO(5) reduced with respect to any of its subalgebra chains is completely resolved by this approach.

## Method

The authors formulate Racah's method as a linear homogeneous system of equations for reduced coupling coefficients. They consider the action of an infinitesimal generator of G, expressed as an irreducible tensor operator with respect to H, on coupled and uncoupled product states, then apply Racah's factorization lemma and the Wigner-Eckart theorem to eliminate all dependence on weights, yielding relations expressed entirely in terms of reduced coupling coefficients, reduced matrix elements of the generators, and recoupling coefficients (6-Lambda symbols) of H. The resulting linear system is solved by finding null vectors of the coefficient matrix via Euler row reduction, with orthonormalization by the Gram-Schmidt procedure when outer multiplicities are present. For SO(5) > SO(4), the known reduced matrix elements of SO(5) generators and known Wigner calculus of SO(4) are used as input.

## Key numbers

* The orthonormality condition for reduced coupling coefficients involves sums over branching multiplicity indices a1, a2 and irrep labels Lambda1, Lambda2, with delta functions delta_{(rho Gamma)(rho' Gamma')} and delta_{aa'} for the bra-sum and delta_{(a1 Lambda1)(a1' Lambda1')} delta_{(a2 Lambda2)(a2' Lambda2')} for the ket-sum.
* The normalization factor for coupling coefficients is given by scrN^2 = sum_i C_i^2, where the sum runs over entries sharing the same a Lambda, and the same value of scrN must be obtained regardless of the choice of a Lambda.
* When outer multiplicity D is present, the matrix A is expected to have rank N-D, yielding D linearly independent null vectors, and the inner product for orthonormalization is scrM_{rho' rho} = sum_i C_{rho' i} C_{rho i} over entries sharing the same a Lambda.
* For the special case H = SO(3), the Racah relation involves Jhat = (2J+1)^{1/2} and 6-j symbols, with a phase factor (-)^{J2+JT+J'} or (-)^{J1+J2'+J} depending on the term.

## Context

This work extends Racah's original method (Racah 1949) for calculating Clebsch-Gordan coefficients from a recurrence-based approach to a general linear algebraic formulation applicable to any subalgebra chain, including those with outer multiplicities. It builds upon prior calculations of SO(5) coupling coefficients by Hecht (1965, 1967), Hemenger (1970), and Han (1993), as well as the known Wigner calculus for SO(4) by Biedenharn (1961) and reduced matrix elements of SO(5) generators by Hecht (1965) and Kemmer (1968). The results enable calculations for the canonical SO(5) > SO(4) chain and, via unitary transformation, for the noncanonical chains relevant to proton-neutron pairing (isospin algebra SO(3) in chain II) and the Bohr collective model or interacting boson model (angular momentum SO(3) in chain III).
