# Gravo-thermal catastrophe in gravitational collapse and energy progenitor of Gamma-Ray Bursts

> We study the homologous collapse of stellar nuclear core, the virial theorem for hadron collisional relaxations, and photon productions from hadron collisions. We thus show the gravo-thermal dynamical process that transforms gravitational energy to photon energy. The process is energetically and entropically favourable. The total baryon number conservation, Euler equation for energy-momentum conservation and Poisson's equation for gravitational potential are adopted to describe homologous core collapses. The virial theorem determines the hadron collision energy gain from gravitational potential. The hadronic photon production rate determines the photon energy density. The time scales of macroscopic and microscopic processes are studied to verify approximations. As a result, we show the formation of opaque photon-pair spheres, whose total energy, size, temperature and number density, accounting for the main energetic features of Gamma-Ray Burst progenitors. We obtain the intrinsic correlations of these quantities. They depend only on the averaged thermal index of the stellar core. We discuss the possibility to confront them with observational data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that gravitational energy in a homologously collapsing stellar core can be converted into photon energy via a gravo-thermal dynamical process involving hadron collisional relaxation and subsequent hadronic photon production, forming an opaque photon-pair sphere that accounts for GRB progenitor energetics, but this result depends on the assumption that the macroscopic gravitational collapse timescale is much larger than the microscopic timescales of baryon relaxation, photon production, and thermalization, allowing an adiabatic approximation.

## Method

The authors adopt Newtonian hydrodynamics with continuity, Euler, and Poisson equations for a homologously collapsing stellar core, using a polytropic equation of state \(p = \kappa \rho^\gamma\) with mean thermal index \(\gamma\). They apply the virial theorem locally to relate baryon temperature to gravitational potential, and use hadronic photon production rates from heavy-ion collision literature (Kapusta 1991, Turbide 2004) to compute photon number and energy densities. The eigenvalue problem for homologous collapse profiles is solved numerically for \(\gamma < 4/3\), and the time scale hierarchy \(\tau_{\rm grav} \gg \tau_{\rm therm} > \tau_{\rm prod} > \tau_{\rm relax}\) is used to justify treating microscopic processes as instantaneous relative to the collapse.

## Key numbers

- Baryon collisional relaxation timescale: \(\tau_{\rm relax} \approx 5.21 \times 10^{-3} (n_0/n) (T/m)^{1/2} T^{-1} < T^{-1}\), where \(n_0 \approx 0.16 \,{\rm fm}^{-3} = 1.6 \times 10^{38} /{\rm cm}^3\) is nuclear saturation density and \(m \approx 1\) GeV is baryon mass.
- Photon production rate: \(R_\gamma \approx \frac{4}{3} \frac{\alpha \alpha_s}{\pi} T^4 \ln\left(1 + \frac{2.9}{4\pi \alpha_s}\right)\), with \(\alpha = 1/137\) and \(\alpha_s \approx 0.5\).
- Photon number density: \(n_\gamma \approx \frac{4}{3} \frac{\alpha \alpha_s}{\pi} T^3 \ln\left(1 + \frac{2.9}{4\pi \alpha_s}\right)\).
- Photon energy density: \(\rho_\gamma \approx \frac{8}{3} \frac{\alpha \alpha_s}{\pi} T^4 \ln\left(1 + \frac{2.9}{4\pi \alpha_s}\right)\).
- Photon temperature: \(T_\gamma \approx 0.21 T\).
- Minimal gravitational scale length: \(a_{\rm min} = 3.06 \times 10^{5} (3\gamma)^{-1/2} (\rho_c^{\rm max}/\rho_0)^{-1/2}\) cm, with \(\rho_c^{\rm max} = 10 \rho_0\) adopted for numerical illustration.
- Maximal core centre density: \(\rho_c^{\rm max} = (3\gamma \kappa)^{-1/(\gamma-1)}\), about 5–8 times nuclear saturation energy density \(\rho_0 = 2.4 \times 10^{35} \,{\rm ergs}/{\rm cm}^3\).
- Three numerical solutions found for eigenvalues \((\gamma, \lambda_m)\): (1.24, \(1.0 \times 10^{-4}\)), (1.23, \(8 \times 10^{-5}\)), (1.225, \(8 \times 10^{-6}\)), with outer radii \(r_s \approx 23, 36, 34\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the gravo-thermal catastrophe concept pioneered by Lynden-Bell and Wood (1967, 1968) for stellar systems, applying it to nuclear-density stellar cores with collisional rather than collisionless relaxation. It connects the virial theorem approach of Padmanabhan (1989) and Chavanis (2002, 2003) to hadronic photon production rates from heavy-ion collision studies (Kapusta 1991, Turbide 2004, Gale 2015). The results enable a possible explanation for GRB progenitor energetics, with intrinsic correlations of photon-pair sphere properties depending only on the averaged thermal index \(\gamma\), offering testable predictions against observational data.
