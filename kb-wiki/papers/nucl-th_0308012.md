# K+ nucleon scattering and exotic S=+1 Baryons

> The K+ nucleon elastic scattering process has been reexamined in light of recent measurements which have found a narrow exotic S=+1 resonance in their KN invariant mass distributions. We have analyzed the existing database in order to consider the effect of a narrow state on fits to K+ nucleon observables.

## Key claim

The authors reanalyze existing K⁺-nucleon elastic scattering data to test whether a narrow exotic S=+1 resonance (the Θ⁺, mass near 1540 MeV) can be accommodated, and find that widths beyond a few MeV are excluded, but a width of 1 MeV or less in the P₀₁ partial wave is possible because the Θ⁺ mass falls in a data gap, leaving the question unresolved.

## Method

The authors used a product S-matrix approach to add a narrow elastic KN resonance with an energy-independent width to the previous VPI group (1992) partial-wave analysis, which had fitted K⁺p and K⁺d data. They scanned a grid of mass (1520–1560 MeV) and width (1–10 MeV, and sub-MeV) values for S-, P-, and D-wave partial waves (S₀₁, S₁₁, P₀₁, P₀₃, P₁₁, P₁₃, D₀₃, D₀₅, D₁₃, D₁₅) and evaluated the change in χ² relative to the baseline fit.

## Key numbers

- The baseline fit for isoscalar waves from threshold to T_Lab = 1.1 GeV gave χ²/data = 3198/1746.
- The baseline fit for isovector waves from threshold to T_Lab = 2.65 GeV gave χ²/data = 4872/3663.
- Adding states with widths above 5 MeV resulted in an enormous increase in χ², often more than doubling the χ²/data.
- The reported Θ⁺ masses from experiments cluster around 1540 MeV, with widths less than 25 MeV; a chiral soliton prediction gave 1530 MeV with width less than 15 MeV.
- A conservative estimate from Nussinov (based on K⁺d total cross section) gave Γ_Θ⁺ < 6 MeV; the ITEP bubble chamber measurements gave Γ_Θ⁺ < 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the VPI group’s 1992 analysis of K⁺-nucleon scattering, which had found broad (≈100 MeV) isoscalar and isovector poles later removed from the Review of Particle Properties due to lack of experimental activity. The analysis is motivated by recent measurements from SPring-8, ITEP, Jefferson Lab, and ELSA reporting narrow peaks near 1540 MeV, in agreement with a chiral soliton prediction by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). The results confirm the width limit from Nussinov (2003) and show that a definitive conclusion on the Θ⁺ requires further measurements, especially in the data gap near 1545 MeV.
