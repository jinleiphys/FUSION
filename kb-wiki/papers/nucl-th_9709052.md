# Variational treatment of quenched QED using the worldline technique

> There has been growing interest in studying the behaviour of QED in its strong coupling limit. The reason for this is not only that it serves as a simple prototype gauge theory for testing nonperturbative calculational techniques which one then goes on to apply to QCD, but also because the theory exhibits interesting behaviour in its own right. In particular, it now appears to be quite well established that QED breaks chiral symmetry dynamically as long as the coupling is sufficiently large. In this contribution we describe the progress made in applying the polaron variational approach to quenched QED within the context of the worldline formulation of field theory. Although it is too early for quantitative results, qualitatively the approach seems promising: The method is formulated covariantly in Minkowski space-time, the Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied, the connection between the bare- and pole-masses of the electron is gauge independent, the known exact result for the infrared dependence of the on-shell renormalized propagator is reproduced and corrections to the variational approximation can be calculated systematically.

## Key claim

The polaron variational approach, applied for the first time to a gauge theory with spin-1/2 particles, reproduces known exact results (such as the infrared dependence of the on-shell renormalized propagator) and satisfies Ward-Takahashi identities, but it is too early for quantitative results; the method appears qualitatively promising.

## Method

The authors use the worldline formulation of field theory to represent Green functions in quenched QED as path integrals over bosonic coordinates \(x(t)\) and Grassmann-valued functions \(\zeta(t)\) for the electron spin. They then apply the Feynman-Jensen variational principle, replacing the exact effective action with a trial action quadratic in \(x(t)\) and identical to the free action in the fermionic degrees of freedom, and evaluate the electron propagator near its mass shell using on-shell renormalization and Pauli-Villars regularization.

## Key numbers

* The gauge-dependent infrared divergence in the residue \(Z_2\) is extracted analytically as \(F_{qq}^{I} = -i (\alpha / 4\pi) (1 - \xi) \log(\Lambda^2 / m_\gamma^2)\), where \(\alpha\) is the fine-structure constant, \(\xi\) the gauge-fixing parameter, \(\Lambda\) a Pauli-Villars cutoff, and \(m_\gamma\) the photon mass.
* The residue \(Z_2\) behaves as \(Z_2 \propto (\Lambda / m_\gamma)^{(\alpha / 2\pi)(3 - \xi)}\), reproducing the Bloch-Nordsieck result.
* The variational equation for the mass is given by \(\delta \{ M_{\rm phys}^2 [1 - (1-\lambda)^2] - 2\Omega[A,\lambda] + V[A,\lambda] \} = 0\), where \(\lambda = \lambda' / A_{00}\) and \(M_{\rm phys}\) is the physical electron mass.

## Context

This work extends the polaron variational technique previously applied to scalar field theory by the same authors (Schreiber, Alexandrou, Rosenfelder, 1997) to the gauge theory of QED with spin-1/2 fermions. It contrasts with traditional nonperturbative approaches such as Schwinger-Dyson, lattice, and renormalization group methods. The qualitative success, satisfying Ward-Takahashi identities, gauge-independent mass relation, and reproducing the infrared behavior of the propagator, enables future quantitative studies with more realistic trial actions, potentially including dynamical mass generation in strong-coupling QED.
