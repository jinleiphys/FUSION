# Effective interactions and shell model studies of heavy tin isotopes

> We calculate the low-lying spectra of heavy tin isotopes from A=120 to A=130 using the 2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2} shell to define the model space. An effective interaction has been derived using 132Sn as closed core employing perturbative many-body techniques. We start from a nucleon-nucleon potential derived from modern meson exchange models. This potential is in turn renormalized for the given medium, 132Sn, yielding the nuclear reaction matrix, which is then used in perturbation theory to obtain the shell model effective interaction.

## Key claim

The authors derive a microscopic effective two-body interaction for heavy tin isotopes (A=120-130) using perturbative many-body techniques starting from a modern meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, and show that shell model calculations using this interaction reproduce many low-lying states well, though with systematic overestimates of yrast state energies by 0.1-0.4 MeV and increasing deviations for negative-parity states toward midshell.

## Method

The authors start from the charge-dependent Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix using a harmonic-oscillator basis with oscillator energy 7.87 MeV for A=132, employing a double-partitioning scheme to construct the Pauli operator that accounts for the different closed shells for neutrons (N=82) and protons (Z=50). They then calculate an effective two-body interaction within the 2s1d0g7/20h11/2 model space using the Q-box method, including all non-folded diagrams through third order in G, with the effective interaction evaluated at a model space energy of -20 MeV and iterated fewer than 10 times until numerical stability. Shell model calculations are performed using the Lanczos algorithm to solve the eigenvalue problem for basis dimensions up to approximately 16 million.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic shell model approach to heavy tin isotopes, where previously only few microscopic calculations were available, such as the multistep shell model BCS formalism by Insolia et al. (1993) using matrix elements extrapolated from the lead mass region by Pomar et al. (1990). The authors compare their results with the generalized seniority model of Talmi (1977) and find that seniority is well conserved for 0+ and 2+ states but fragments for 4+ states toward midshell. The calculations enable future inclusion of effective three-body forces, deferred to a later work by the same authors (1997), and provide a foundation for studying collective features and the role of proton core excitations across the Z=50 gap, which have been observed by Fielding et al. (1977) and Bron et al. (1979) in lighter tin isotopes.
