# Calculation of Power Spectrum in the Little Bangs

> The power spectrum of fluctuations in the momentum distributions of particles have been estimated with optical Glauber and Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The evaluation procedure adopted in this work is analogous to the one used in the calculation of power spectrum in Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMBR). The power spectrum due to perturbations in the phase space distribution of the particles has also been evaluated. The perturbation in phase space has been evolved through the Boltzmann transport equation in an expanding quark gluon plasma (QGP) background. The expansion of the QGP has been treated within the purview of (3+1) dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics. We observe that the non-equlibrium effects introduced as perturbations in the phase space distributions can be traced from the enhancement of the power spectrum as well as through its variation with temperature which is distinctly different from the case of vanishing perturbation. A relation has been derived between the power spectrum and the flow harmonics.

## Key claim

The power spectrum of fluctuations in particle momentum distributions in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be calculated using a procedure analogous to that used for cosmic microwave background radiation (CMBR), and the non-equilibrium effects introduced as perturbations in the phase space distributions can be traced from the enhancement of the power spectrum as well as through its variation with temperature, which is distinctly different from the case of vanishing perturbation.

## Method

The authors solved (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations with initial conditions from both Optical Glauber (OG) and Monte-Carlo Glauber (MCG) models for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, using a QCD-based equation of state. They evolved perturbations in the phase space distribution through the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within the hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma background, with the relaxation time taken from hard thermal loop QCD calculations. The power spectrum of the particle momentum distributions was then evaluated by expanding the distributions in spherical harmonics at surfaces of constant temperature.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Initial energy density normalization: epsilon_0 = 7.7 GeV/fm^3
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at RHIC energy: sigma_NN = 42 mb
- Power spectrum evaluated at constant temperature surfaces: T = 350 MeV, 250 MeV, and 170 MeV (the latter being near the transition temperature T_c)
- Relaxation time relation used: tau_R^{-1}(x) = 1.1 alpha_s T, from HTL approximation
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Au nuclei: radius R = 6.37 fm, diffuseness delta = 0.535 fm

## Context

This work extends the analogy between heavy ion collisions ("Little Bangs") and cosmology (CMBR) previously explored by authors such as Mocsy and Sorensen (extracting power spectrum from transverse momentum correlations) and by the ALICE collaboration (estimating p_T fluctuations expanded in Laplace series). The study builds on prior work using HIJING event generation for flow emulation and on calculations of root-mean-square flow harmonics showing similarities with CMBR power spectra. The results enable connecting initial-state fluctuations from nucleon "lumpiness" to final-state observables through hydrodynamic evolution, and the derived relation between power spectrum and flow harmonics provides a new tool for characterizing the QGP.
