# Differential freezeout and pion interferometry at RHIC from covariant transport theory

> Puzzling discrepancies between recent pion interferometry data on Au+Au reactions at s^1/2 = 130 and 200 AGeV from RHIC and predictions based on ideal hydrodynamics are analyzed in terms of covariant parton transport theory. The discrepancies of out and longitudinal radii are significantly reduced when the finite opacity of the gluon plasma is taken into account.

## Key claim

The puzzling discrepancies between RHIC pion interferometry data and ideal hydrodynamic predictions for the out and longitudinal radii are significantly reduced when the finite opacity of the gluon plasma is taken into account via covariant parton transport theory, though the side radius remains underestimated and the momentum scale of the predicted \(R_{out}/R_{side}<1\) is not yet realistic due to simplified hadronization assumptions.

## Method

The authors solved the Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann transport equation for a massless gluon gas with elastic \(2\to 2\) interactions using the MPC parton cascade algorithm with particle subdivision to maintain Lorentz covariance. Central Au+Au collisions at RHIC were modeled with minijet initial conditions starting from a longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken tube at proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/\(c\), with locally isotropic momentum distributions and a thermal \(p_\perp\) distribution at \(T = 700\) MeV, and the freezeout distribution was defined as the spacetime coordinates of the last interaction point of test particles, with a \(1g \to 1\pi\) hadronization model applied.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon rapidity density: \(dN_g(\tau_0)/d\eta = 1050\) for central Au+Au collisions.
- Transport opacities studied: \(\chi = 0.60\), \(3.01\), and \(7.73\), corresponding to elastic cross sections \(\sigma_{el} = 0.6\), \(3\), and \(7.5\) mb respectively for \(dN_g/d\eta = 1050\).
- Initial proper time: \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/\(c\).
- Initial temperature for thermal \(p_\perp\) fit: \(T = 700\) MeV.
- For transport opacity \(\chi = 7.73\), the covariant algorithm gave corrections of about 1 fm for \(R_O\) and 2 fm for \(R_L\) at \(K_\perp \approx 0.2\) GeV compared to the naive noncovariant cascade.
- The covariant result showed \(R_O < R_S\) for \(K_\perp > 2\) GeV, while the naive cascade gave \(R_O/R_S > 1\) for all \(K_\perp\).
- The predicted \(R_S(K_\perp) \approx \text{const} \approx 3\) fm was independent of transport opacity.
- The largest increase in decoupling time for low-\(p_\perp\) partons was \(\tau/\tau_0 \sim R/\tau_0 \sim 50\).

## Context

This work extends the covariant parton transport approach previously used by the authors to study elliptic flow saturation (Molnar and Gyulassy, 2002) and addresses the failure of ideal Euler hydrodynamics (Heinz and Kolb, 2002) to reproduce RHIC pion interferometry data from STAR and PHENIX at \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) and 200 \(A\)GeV. The results contrast with hybrid hydrodynamic/transport models (Bass and Dumitru, 2000; Humanic, 2002) that also failed to reproduce the observed decreasing \(R_{out}/R_{side}\), and suggest that future work must include more realistic covariant hadronization models and hadronic final state interactions to fully explain the differential features of HBT correlations.
