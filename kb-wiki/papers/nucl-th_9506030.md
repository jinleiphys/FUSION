# The Pion-Nucleon Form Factor From QCD Sum Rules

> QCD sum rules are used to calculate the $q^2$ dependence of the $πNN$ coupling $g_{πNN} (q^2)$ in the spacelike region $0.5 \ {\mbox{GeV}}^2 \lesssim q^2 \lesssim 1.5\ {\mbox{GeV}}^2$. We study the Borel sum rule for the three point function of one pseudoscalar and two nucleon currents up to order four in the operator product expansion. The Borel transform is performed with respect to the nucleon momenta, whereas the momentum $q^2$ of the pseudoscalar vertex is kept fixed at spacelike values. The results can be well fitted using a monopole form with a cutoff mass of about $Λ_π\approx 800 \ {\mbox{MeV}}.

## Key claim

QCD sum rules can determine the momentum dependence of the pion-nucleon form factor in the spacelike region, yielding a monopole cutoff mass of about 800 MeV, but only for intermediate momentum transfers where the operator product expansion is valid and pion pole dominance holds (approximately 0.5 to 1.5 GeV^2).

## Method

The author studies the Borel sum rule for the three-point function of one pseudoscalar and two nucleon (Ioffe) currents, performing the operator product expansion up to order four (quark condensate diagrams). The Borel transform is applied with respect to the two nucleon momenta while keeping the pion momentum q^2 fixed at spacelike values, and the resulting sum rule is analyzed with an effective resonance mass to achieve Borel stability.

## Key numbers

* The monopole cutoff mass fitted to the calculated form factor is Lambda_pi = 800 MeV (0.85 M_N).
* The calculation is performed in the spacelike region 0.5 M_N^2 less than or approximately equal to q^2 less than or approximately equal to 1.5 M_N^2 (corresponding to 0.5 to 1.5 GeV^2).
* The effective resonance mass used for Borel stability is M_N*^2 = 6.0 M_N^2 (M_N* approximately 2.3 GeV).
* The Borel plateau extends over 0.8 M_N^2 less than or approximately equal to M^2 less than or approximately equal to 1.8 M_N^2.
* The experimental nucleon mass M_N = 940 MeV and the experimental pion-nucleon coupling g_piNN = 13.4 are used as inputs.
* The nucleon current overlap parameter is lambda_N^2 = 5.5 x 10^{-4} GeV^6 = 8.07 x 10^{-4} M_N^6, taken from Henley and Pasupathy (1993).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for the on-shell pion-nucleon coupling (Reinders, Rubinstein, and Yazaki, 1983; Reinders, 1984) to the off-shell momentum dependence of the form factor. The result (Lambda_pi = 800 MeV) supports the softer form factors (500-950 MeV) advocated by chiral soliton and quark models, and agrees closely with the quenched lattice QCD result of Liu et al. (1995) giving Lambda_pi = 750 MeV, while contradicting the harder form factors (Lambda_pi greater than or approximately equal to 1.3 GeV) used in many one-boson exchange potentials. The method enables a QCD-based determination of the pion-nucleon form factor without the model ambiguities of hadronic theories.
