# Structure of the Vacuum in Nuclear Matter - A Nonperturbative Approach

> We compute the vacuum polarisation correction to the binding energy of nuclear matter in the Walecka model using a nonperturbative approach. We first study such a contribution as arising from a ground state structure with baryon-antibaryon condensates. This yields the same results as obtained through the relativistic Hartree approximation of summing tadpole diagrams for the baryon propagator. Such a vacuum is then generalized to include quantum effects from meson fields through scalar-meson condensates. The method is applied to study properties of nuclear matter and leads to a softer equation of state giving a lower value of the incompressibility than would be reached without quantum effects. The density dependent effective sigma mass is also calculated including such vacuum polarisation effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including vacuum polarisation effects via a nonperturbative ground state with baryon-antibaryon and scalar-meson condensates yields a softer equation of state for nuclear matter than the no-sea approximation, but the specific numerical results depend on the choice of the positive quartic coupling constant λ_R and the renormalised sigma mass m_R, which are fitted to produce an incompressibility in the correct range.

## Method

The authors construct a trial ground state for nuclear matter using a squeezed coherent (Bogoliubov) transformation that includes baryon-antibaryon condensates, then generalise this state to also include sigma-meson condensates. The condensate functions are determined by minimising the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature, and the resulting divergent integrals are renormalised using counterterms. The method is applied to the Walecka model (QHD-I) with nucleons, scalar sigma mesons, and vector omega mesons, including a quartic sigma self-interaction term with positive coupling.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility K = 401 MeV for λ_R = 1.8 and K = 329 MeV for λ_R = 5, compared to the mean-field result of 545 MeV and the relativistic Hartree result of 450 MeV.
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation density (k_F = 1.42 fm⁻¹): M* = 0.752M for λ_R = 1.8 and M* = 0.815M for λ_R = 5, compared to 0.56M in the no-sea approximation and 0.72M in relativistic Hartree.
- Scalar potential U_S = -232.7 MeV and vector potential U_V = 163.4 MeV at saturation for λ_R = 1.8; U_S = -173.14 MeV and U_V = 107.74 MeV for λ_R = 5.
- Nucleon potential (U_S + U_V) = -69.3 MeV for λ_R = 1.8 and -65.4 MeV for λ_R = 5; antinucleon potential (U_S - U_V) = -396.1 MeV for λ_R = 1.8 and -280.9 MeV for λ_R = 5.
- Fitted couplings for m_R = 520 MeV: g_σ = 7.34 and g_ω = 8.21 for λ_R = 1.8; g_σ = 6.67 and g_ω = 7.08 for λ_R = 5.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approximation of Chin (1977) by including quantum corrections from the sigma field through a nonperturbative vacuum realignment, rather than through a perturbative loop expansion. The baryon-antibaryon condensate approach reproduces the relativistic Hartree results exactly. The method enables the inclusion of vacuum polarisation effects with a positive quartic sigma self-coupling, avoiding the instability problems of negative quartic couplings used in earlier work by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and others. The results are compared to the no-sea approximation of Walecka (1974) and to calculations by Fox et al. (1993) that included cubic and quartic sigma self-interactions.
