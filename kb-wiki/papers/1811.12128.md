# Exotic Baryons in Chiral Soliton Models

> We cautiously review the treatment of pentaquark exotic baryons in chiral soliton models. We consider two examples and argue that any consistent and self-contained description must go beyond the mean field approximation that only considers the classical soliton and the canonical quantization of its (would-be) zero modes via collective coordinates.

# Exotic Baryons in Chiral Soliton Models

## Key claim
Pentaquark exotic baryons are not artifacts of the collective coordinate quantization but are genuine resonances in chiral soliton models, provided the description goes beyond the mean field approximation to include both collective and harmonic fluctuations. Any consistent and self-contained description must incorporate these fluctuations, which automatically produce the necessary specifications (such as Yukawa couplings and flavor representation constraints) rather than requiring external adjustments.

## Method
The paper reviews two approaches within chiral soliton models: (1) the rotation-vibration-approach (RVA) for light-flavor pentaquarks, which simultaneously treats collective rotations and small-amplitude fluctuations in kaon-nucleon scattering, using Dirac constraints to avoid double counting; (2) an extension of the bound state approach for heavy baryons, where heavy meson fields (pseudoscalar and vector) are coupled to the chiral soliton and quantized via collective coordinates, with the bound state wave function selecting the appropriate SU(3) representation for the light diquark content. The collective coordinate Hamiltonian is diagonalized including flavor symmetry breaking via the Yabu-Ando method.

## Key numbers
- The mass difference between the Θ⁺ pentaquark and the nucleon in a flavor-symmetric world is 3/(2β²) for N_C=3, where β² is the strange moment of inertia (O(N_C)).
- For charmed exotic baryons, the predicted mass differences relative to Λ_c are: M(B_c) – M(Λ_c) ≈ 495 MeV, M("Ξ_c") – M(Λ_c) ≈ 530 MeV, M("Ω_c") – M(Λ_c) ≈ 670 MeV.
- The mass difference between the anti-sextet and anti-triplet representations for heavy antiquark pentaquarks is M(anti-sextet) – M(anti-triplet) = 2/β² + symmetry breaking ≈ 300 MeV.
- The resonance phase shift δ_E passes through π/2 exactly at the momentum given by the mass difference between the Θ⁺ and the nucleon predicted by the collective coordinate Hamiltonian, up to a small (few MeV) pole shift.

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model treatment of baryons (Adkins 1983) and the bound state approach to strangeness (Callan 1985) to exotic baryons, while critically examining mean field approximations used by Diakonov (1997), Ellis (2004), and Kim (2017). The analysis of the Θ⁺ resonance in kaon-nucleon scattering (Walliser 2005) demonstrates that the pentaquark is not an artifact of the rigid rotator approach. For heavy baryons, the approach extends the work of Schechter (1995) and Blanckenberg (2015) to include exotic states, showing that the anti-decapentaplet representation contains exotic heavy baryons with a light antiquark. The results enable future calculations of resonance widths and spectra that go beyond first-order perturbation theory in flavor symmetry breaking, challenging the truncation used by Kim (2017).
