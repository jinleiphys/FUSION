# Theory of kaon-nuclear systems

> The strong interaction between an antikaon and a nucleon is at the origin of various interesting phenomena in kaon-nuclear systems. In particular, the interaction in the isospin $I=0$ channel is sufficiently attractive to generate a quasi-bound state, the $Λ(1405)$ resonance, below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. Based on this picture, it may be expected that the $\bar{K}N$ interaction also generates quasi-bound states in kaon-nuclear systems, sometimes called kaonic nuclei. At the same time, the $\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound picture of the $Λ(1405)$ is also related to the discussion of hadronic molecules in hadron spectroscopy. Here an overview is presented of the theoretical studies developed for kaon-nucleon and kaon-nuclear systems. We start from the modern understanding of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. We then discuss the $\bar{K}N$ interaction and various aspects of few-body kaonic nuclei. Heavier kaon-nuclear systems are examined from the viewpoint of nuclear many-body physics, with focus on the properties of antikaons in nuclear matter. Related topics, such as the $K^{-}p$ momentum correlation functions in high-energy collisions and the studies of kaonic atoms, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\bar{K}N$ interaction in the isospin $I=0$ channel is sufficiently attractive to generate a quasi-bound state (the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance) below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, and that this interaction may also generate quasi-bound states in kaon-nuclear systems (kaonic nuclei), though the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a quasibound state imbedded in the $\pi\Sigma$ continuum has a large decay width of about 50 MeV, unlike the stable deuteron.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of chiral $SU(3)$ dynamics, where the coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering amplitude $T_{ij}$ is obtained by solving a Lippmann-Schwinger scattering equation with the interaction kernel $V_{ij}$ constructed to satisfy chiral symmetry constraints. The leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term, proportional to the meson energy divided by the square of the pseudoscalar meson decay constant ($E/f^2$), dominates the low-energy $\bar{K}N$ interaction, and the scattering equation is reduced to algebraic form using on-shell factorization with dimensional regularization to handle ultraviolet divergences. The analysis incorporates constraints from $K^{-}p$ scattering cross sections, threshold branching ratios, kaonic hydrogen measurements from the SIDDHARTA collaboration, and $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is located 27 MeV below the $K^-p$ threshold (threshold energy $M_p + m_{K^-} = 1432$ MeV)
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ has a decay width $\Gamma \simeq 50$ MeV
- The leading Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel at threshold is proportional to $-m_K/f^2$, with $f \simeq 0.1$ GeV and kaon mass $m_K \sim 0.5$ GeV
- The pseudoscalar meson decay constant values used are $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV and $f_K = 109$ MeV
- The NLO chiral dynamics analysis achieved $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f}=0.96$ with the SIDDHARTA constraint; even with only the Weinberg-Tomozawa term the description gives $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f}=1.12$
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ pole position from Ikeda et al. (2012) NLO analysis: $1424^{+7}_{-23} - i\,26^{+3}_{-14}$ MeV
- The $\Lambda(1380)$ pole position from Ikeda et al. (2012) NLO analysis: $1381^{+18}_{-6} - i\,81^{+19}_{-8}$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on kaon-nuclear systems that began with the prediction of the $\Lambda(1405)$ by Dalitz and Tuan (1959) and the proposal of deeply bound kaonic nuclei by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The paper synthesizes results from chiral $SU(3)$ dynamics studies (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 2000) and the precise kaonic hydrogen measurements by the SIDDHARTA collaboration (2011, 2012), which resolved earlier inconsistencies from the DEAR experiment. The two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ region, first pointed out by Oller (2000), is now confirmed by multiple approaches including the cloudy bag model (Fink 1989), meson-exchange model (Haidenbauer 2010), and dynamical coupled-channel model (Kamano 2014). The framework enables future studies of kaonic nuclei, antikaon properties in nuclear matter, and the interpretation of $K^{-}p$ momentum correlation functions from high-energy collisions.
