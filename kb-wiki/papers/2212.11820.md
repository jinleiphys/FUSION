# Unobservability of the topological charge in nonabelian gauge theory: Ward-Takahashi identity and phenomenological aspects

> We argue that the topological charge of nonabelian gauge theory is unphysical. To show this statement, we use the Adler-Bardeen theorem and the Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin symmetry which are warranted by the perturbative finiteness of the chiral anomaly, thus being free of Gribov ambiguity. In addition to the original argument using the unphysical gauge field component collinear to the spatial derivative of the gauge function, we show the unobservability of the topological charge using the Ward-Takahashi identity. We then present the consequences of this finding and show the consistency with many physical pictures and ideas that have been developed around the topology of nonabelian gauge theory. The most important ones are the resolution of the Strong CP problem, the unobservability of topological instantons, the physical relevance of the axial $U(1)$ symmetry, the independence of the vacuum energy on the vacuum angle, and the impossibility to realize the sphaleron induced baryogenesis and chiral magnetic effects. The axial $U(1)$ symmetry and the unphysical $θ$-term imply that the physical complex phase of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix is the sole source of CP violation in the standard model. The unphysical sphaleron also means that the lepton number is phenomenologically free from the baryon number, and their violations may be modeled separately. We also comment on the consistency with the results of lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The topological charge of nonabelian gauge theory is unphysical because it always involves a gauge field component proportional to the gradient of the gauge function, and this conclusion holds nonperturbatively due to the Adler-Bardeen theorem ensuring that the chiral anomaly and topological charge are perturbatively finite and thus free of Gribov ambiguity.

## Method

The author uses the BRST symmetry and Ward-Takahashi identity to construct a BRST quartet for the topological current, showing that the topological charge density operator \(F\tilde F\) belongs to an unphysical BRST quartet. The argument relies on the Adler-Bardeen theorem to guarantee that the topological charge is not renormalized by quantum corrections and that infinitesimal gauge fields always lie in the fundamental modular region where BRST symmetry is exact, avoiding Gribov copies. The author then derives a Ward-Takahashi identity showing that the cluster-decomposed topological charge density vanishes when summed over all topological sectors.

## Key numbers

- Neutron electric dipole moment experimental bound: \(|d_n| < 1.8 \times 10^{-26} e\) cm (Abel 2020), which under the assumption that \(\theta\) is the sole source of CP violation gives the constraint \(|\theta| < 10^{-10}\).
- The topological charge \(\int d^4 x \, F\tilde F = \Delta n\), where \(\Delta n\) is an integer given by the shift of the winding number between gauge fields at \(t \to -\infty\) and \(t \to +\infty\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous argument (Yamanaka 2022) that the topological charge is unphysical, and builds on the BRST quartet mechanism for the topological current originally developed by Kugo (1978, 1979) and Hata (1980). The paper directly challenges the instanton-based resolution of the \(U(1)_A\) problem proposed by 't Hooft (1976) and the \(\theta\)-vacuum formalism of Jackiw (1976) and Callan (1976). It provides an alternative resolution to the Strong CP problem without requiring axions (Peccei 1977, Weinberg 1978, Wilczek 1978) or massless quarks, and implies that sphaleron-induced baryogenesis and chiral magnetic effects are impossible. The work also claims consistency with lattice calculations and opens the possibility that the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix phase is the sole source of CP violation in the standard model.
