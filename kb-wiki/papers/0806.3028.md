# The Gottfried sum rule in an unquenched quark model

> We present an unquenched quark model for baryons in which the effects of quark-antiquark pairs (light flavors, u, d and s) are taken into account in an explicit form. The method is illustrated with an application to the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea.

## Key claim

The unquenched quark model, which includes contributions from \(u\bar{u}\), \(d\bar{d}\), and \(s\bar{s}\) pairs, yields a Gottfried integral of 0.185, in qualitative agreement with the NMC experimental value of \(0.2281 \pm 0.0065\), but the authors caution that this numerical result is preliminary because the dependence on model parameters and on different choices of baryon and meson wave functions has not yet been fully investigated.

## Method

The authors extend the flux-tube breaking model for baryons by adding quark-antiquark pair creation with \(^{3}P_0\) vacuum quantum numbers as a perturbation. They solve the permutation symmetry problem for identical quarks using group-theoretical techniques and construct an algorithm to generate a complete set of intermediate baryon and meson states in the harmonic oscillator basis, allowing the calculation of sea-quark contributions for any initial baryon, any flavor of the \(q\bar{q}\) pair, and any model with harmonic-oscillator wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Gottfried integral \(S_G = 0.185\) from the unquenched quark model, compared to the NMC experimental value \(0.2281 \pm 0.0065\) at \(Q^2 = 4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) over the range \(0.004 \leq x \leq 0.8\).
- The flavor asymmetry is expressed as \(N_{\bar{d}} - N_{\bar{u}} = \int_0^1 dx [\bar{d}(x) - \bar{u}(x)]\), and the model produces an excess of \(\bar{d}\) over \(\bar{u}\) quarks in the proton.

## Context

This work extends the earlier unquenched quark model of Geiger and Isgur (1997), which only included \(s\bar{s}\) loops in the proton, to also include \(u\bar{u}\) and \(d\bar{d}\) loops, directly addressing the challenge posed in that prior work. The result provides a mechanism for the violation of the Gottfried sum rule observed by the NMC collaboration (1997) and confirmed by HERMES and NuSea, and it complements the pion-cloud mechanism proposed by Thomas (1983) for generating flavor asymmetry in the nucleon sea.
