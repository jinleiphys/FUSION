# Constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state using heavy ion beams

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy in the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter (N/Z $>$ 1) is important for understanding the structure of systems as diverse as the atomic nuclei and neutron stars. Due to a proper lack of understanding of the basic nucleon-nucleon interaction for matters that are highly asymmetric and at non-normal nuclear density, this very important quantity has remained largely unconstrained. Recent studies using beams from the Cyclotron Institute of Texas A$&$M University, constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy, is presented. A dependence of the form E$_{sym}(ρ)$ = C($ρ/ρ_{o})^γ$, where C = 31.6 MeV and $γ$ = 0.69, is obtained from the dynamical and statistical model analysis. Their implications to both astrophysical and nuclear physics studies are discussed.

# Constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state using heavy ion beams

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy in asymmetric nuclear matter is constrained to a form E_sym(ρ) = 31.6 (ρ/ρ_o)^0.69 MeV, indicating a "stiff" (monotonically increasing) behavior, but this result is obtained specifically by comparing experimental isoscaling parameters with both dynamical and statistical multifragmentation models under the assumption that sequential decay effects are small.

## Method

The authors measured isotopic yield distributions from heavy-ion reactions including 40Ar, 40Ca + 58Fe, 58Ni and 58Ni, 58Fe + 58Ni, 58Fe at beam energies from 25 to 53 MeV/nucleon at the Cyclotron Institute of Texas A&M University and the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory at Michigan State University. They extracted the isoscaling parameter α from the ratio of fragment yields between neutron-rich and neutron-deficient systems, and compared these experimental values with predictions from the dynamical AMD model (using Gogny-AS interaction) and a statistical multifragmentation model to infer the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy parameterization: E_sym(ρ) = 31.6 (ρ/ρ_o)^0.69 MeV, with C = 31.6 MeV and γ = 0.69
- Nuclear matter compressibility K ~ 230 MeV
- Neutron skin thickness for 208Pb: R_n - R_p ~ 0.21 fm
- Predicted neutron star mass: 1.72 solar masses
- Predicted neutron star radius: R = 11-13 km for the "canonical" neutron star
- Direct URCA cooling predicted for neutron stars above 1.4 solar masses
- Beam energies studied: 25 MeV/nucleon to 53 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical predictions of symmetry energy density dependence from microscopic calculations (Fuchs, no year given) and provides experimental constraints that distinguish between "stiff" and "soft" forms. The results agree closely with independent constraints from the relativistic mean field study of giant resonances by Piekarewicz et al. (2005), the isospin diffusion analysis by Chen et al. (2005), and the mass formula constraints from Danielewicz (2004), while differing from the stiffer parameterization (γ = 1.05) previously used by Chen et al. (2005). The constrained symmetry energy enables predictions for neutron star structure and cooling mechanisms, and informs future experiments with neutron-rich radioactive beams.
