# Uncertainty estimation and anomaly detection in chiral effective field theory studies of key nuclear electroweak processes

> Chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) is a powerful tool for studying electroweak processes in nuclei. I discuss $χ$EFT calculations of three key nuclear electroweak processes: primordial deuterium production, proton-proton fusion, and magnetic dipole excitations of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$. This article showcases $χ$EFT's ability to quantify theory uncertainties at the appropriate level of rigor for addressing the different precision demands of these three processes.

# Uncertainty estimation and anomaly detection in chiral effective field theory studies of key nuclear electroweak processes

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) can quantify theory uncertainties at varying levels of rigor appropriate to the precision demands of different nuclear electroweak processes, but the uncertainty estimates are Bayesian credible intervals or sensitivity-based bounds rather than statistically rigorous frequentist errors, and the method used depends on whether the expansion coefficients are functions of a kinematic variable or single numerical values.

## Method
For the primordial deuterium production reaction (np→dγ), the χEFT expansion coefficients for the reaction rate were modeled as Gaussian Processes (GPs) to estimate truncation errors, with the GP model validated through diagnostic assessments including Mahalanobis distances and credible-interval diagnostics. For the proton-proton fusion reaction, the truncation error on the astrophysical S-factor at zero energy was estimated as (Q/Λ)^4 times the maximum of the absolute values of the leading expansion coefficients |c0|, |c2|, or |c3|. For the magnetic dipole excitation of ⁴⁸Ca, a conservative 15% uncertainty estimate was obtained by considering sensitivity to the χEFT interaction used, the precision of the coupled-cluster many-body method, and the size of leading two-body current effects.

## Key numbers
- For np→dγ: The commonly adopted uncertainty of 0.2% for the reaction cross section comes from a simple estimate by Rupak and Ando, and is arbitrarily assigned a statistical meaning of "1σ"; the χEFT truncation errors for orders k>2 are much smaller than the experimental uncertainties of existing data over the energy range [20 keV–200 keV].
- For pp fusion: The astrophysical S-factor Spp(E=0) was compared across four different χEFT interactions, with truncation error estimated as (Q/Λ)^4 times the maximum of |c0|, |c2|, or |c3|, where these coefficients are numerical values rather than functions.
- For ⁴⁸Ca M1 excitation: The B(M1) strength at Ex=10.23 MeV is reported as 4±0.3 μN² from an (e,e′) experiment and 6.8±0.5 μN² from a (γ,n) experiment; a conservative 15% uncertainty estimate was applied to the χEFT calculation, which favored the (γ,n) measurement.

## Context
This work extends prior χEFT uncertainty quantification methods developed by Furnstahl (2014, 2015) and Melendez (2019), applying them to three specific electroweak processes of astrophysical importance. For primordial deuterium production, it addresses a deficiency in big bang nucleosynthesis simulations that previously relied on a 0.2% uncertainty estimate from Rupak and Ando without rigorous statistical justification. For pp fusion, it updates earlier χEFT calculations by Marcucci (2013) and Acharya (2016) with a comprehensive comparison across multiple interactions and a simpler truncation error estimate. For ⁴⁸Ca, the calculation using coupled-cluster theory with two-body currents enables discrimination between conflicting experimental results from Steffen (1980) and Tompkins (2011), challenging the widely circulated claim from Birkhan (2016) that the M1 strength is strongly quenched analogous to Gamow-Teller strength.
