# Spin-thermal shear coupling in relativistic nuclear collisions

> The spin polarization measurements of particles emitted in heavy-ion collisions have opened the possibility for new phenomenological investigations of spin physics in relativistic fluids. The theoretical predictions of global polarization are in agreement with the data, but consistent discrepancies stand out for the local polarization. We show that the covariant theory of relativistic quantum fluids at local equilibrium implies an additional, non-dissipative contribution to the spin polarization vector, which is proportional to the thermal shear, which has been previously overlooked. This additional contribution, together with an improved approximation in the expansion of the local equilibrium density operator, restores the quantitative agreement between the theoretical predictions and the experimental data.

## Key claim

The covariant theory of relativistic quantum fluids at local equilibrium implies an additional, non-dissipative contribution to the spin polarization vector proportional to thermal shear, which was previously overlooked; together with an improved approximation (isothermal local equilibrium) that avoids Taylor-expanding the full temperature field, this restores quantitative agreement between theoretical predictions and experimental data for local polarization in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Method

The authors derive the mean spin vector of Λ hyperons from the Wigner function of a free Dirac field, using a local equilibrium statistical operator expanded to first order in derivatives of the four-temperature vector. They then specialize to the isothermal local equilibrium (ILE) approximation appropriate for high-energy collisions where chemical potentials are negligible, expanding only the fluid velocity rather than the full β field, and compute the spin polarization vector including both thermal vorticity and thermal shear contributions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of spin polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which previously used only thermal vorticity (Becattini, 2013) and could reproduce global polarization but failed for local polarization measurements (Adam, 2019; Niida, 2018). The inclusion of thermal shear coupling (Becattini, 2021; Liu, 2021) together with the isothermal local equilibrium approximation resolves the sign discrepancy in the longitudinal component of Λ polarization. Future work is needed to study decay contributions to spin polarization, dependence on viscosities and initial conditions, and predictions at other energies.
