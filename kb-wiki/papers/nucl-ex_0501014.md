# Light-ion production in the interaction of 96 MeV neutrons with oxygen

> Double-differential cross sections for light-ion (p, d, t, He-3 and alpha) production in oxygen, induced by 96 MeV neutrons are reported. Energy spectra are measured at eight laboratory angles from 20 degrees to 160 degrees in steps of 20 degrees. Procedures for data taking and data reduction are presented. Deduced energy-differential and production cross sections are reported. Experimental cross sections are compared to theoretical reaction model calculations and experimental data at lower neutron energies in the literature. The measured proton data agree reasonably well with the results of the model calculations, whereas the agreement for the other particles is less convincing. The measured production cross sections for protons, deuterons, tritons and alpha particles support the trends suggested by data at lower energies.

## Key claim

The paper reports double-differential cross sections for light-ion production in oxygen induced by 96 MeV neutrons, but the agreement between experimental data and theoretical model calculations is only reasonable for protons, while for deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles the agreement is less convincing, with the models often overpredicting or underpredicting the cross sections by factors of two or more.

## Method

Double-differential cross sections for protons, deuterons, tritons, helium-3, and alpha particles were measured at eight laboratory angles from 20 degrees to 160 degrees in 20-degree steps using the MEDLEY experimental setup at The Svedberg Laboratory, with a quasi-monoenergetic neutron beam of 95.6 MeV produced via the lithium-7(p,n)beryllium-7 reaction. A silicon dioxide target was used for oxygen, with silicon subtraction performed using a separate silicon wafer target, and absolute normalization was obtained from the hydrogen(n,p) scattering reaction using a polyethylene target. The experimental data were compared to calculations from the GNASH and TALYS nuclear reaction codes, which integrate direct, pre-equilibrium, and statistical Hauser-Feshbach models.

## Key numbers

- Incident neutron energy: 95.6 +/- 0.5 MeV peak energy with 1.6 MeV FWHM width, from 98.5 +/- 0.3 MeV protons on a lithium-7 target
- Neutron flux: about 5 x 10^4 neutrons/(s x cm^2) at the target location with 5 microampere beam intensity
- Low-energy cutoff for particle identification: about 3 MeV for hydrogen isotopes and about 8 MeV for helium isotopes; alpha-particle cutoff reduced to about 4 MeV
- Relative statistical uncertainties at 20 degrees: 8% for protons, 13% for deuterons, 20% for tritons, 15% for helium-3, and 12% for alpha particles
- Absolute cross section uncertainty: about 5%, dominated by uncertainties in np scattering angle and reference np cross sections
- Deuteron yield about a factor of 3 lower than proton yield; triton and helium-3 yields more than an order of magnitude weaker
- Alpha particle yield at backward angles about four times weaker than at 20 degrees
- Average neutron energy including tail neutrons: 94.0 MeV; about 13% of neutrons belong to the low-energy tail

## Context

This work extends previous experimental data on neutron-induced reactions in oxygen at lower incident neutron energies of 27, 40, and 60 MeV (Nee82, Sub86) and between 25 and 65 MeV (Ben98a, Ben98b, Ben98c), providing the first comprehensive dataset at 96 MeV for all light charged particles. The measurements enable testing of nuclear reaction models such as GNASH and TALYS for light nuclei, where statistical assumptions may break down, and are particularly important for applications in neutron radiation therapy dosimetry and high-altitude neutron dosimetry, since oxygen constitutes 65% by weight of average human tissue.
