# Chiral transport of neutrinos in supernovae

> The conventional neutrino transport theory for core-collapse supernovae misses one key property of neutrinos: the left-handedness. The chirality of neutrinos modifies the hydrodynamic behavior at the macroscopic scale and leads to topological transport phenomena. We argue that such transport phenomena should play important roles in the evolution of core-collapse supernovae, and, in particular, lead to a tendency toward the inverse energy cascade from small to larger scales, which may be relevant to the origin of the supernova explosion.

# Chiral transport of neutrinos in supernovae

## Key claim
The chirality of neutrinos, which is missed in conventional neutrino transport theory, modifies macroscopic hydrodynamic behavior in core-collapse supernovae and leads to a tendency toward inverse energy cascade from small to larger scales. This effect may be relevant to the origin of supernova explosions, but the author notes this is based on a simplified model of pure neutrino matter in the hydrodynamic regime, and realistic verification requires numerical simulations of neutrino radiation chiral hydrodynamics.

## Method
The author constructs a chiral kinetic theory for left-handed neutrinos by incorporating the Berry curvature into the Boltzmann equation, then derives chiral hydrodynamic equations for neutrino matter in the Landau frame. The scaling symmetry of these equations is analyzed in the inertial range, and the asymptotic behavior of the correlation length is computed to determine the direction of turbulent energy cascade.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino mean free path can be of order 1 cm when nuclear matter density reaches order 10^15 g/cm^3, much smaller than the typical core radius of order 100 km.
- Neutrino chemical potential at the core is of order 200 MeV under near beta equilibrium and charge neutrality.
- Maximal temperature of supernovae is roughly 10 MeV, much smaller than the neutrino chemical potential.
- The chiral vortical effect current for left-handed neutrinos is given by j = -(mu^2/(8pi^2) + T^2/24) omega, where mu is chemical potential, T is temperature, and omega is vorticity.
- The correlation length xi_v(t) grows as xi_v(t) = xi_v(t_s) (t/t_s), indicating inverse energy cascade.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior proposals (Yamamoto, 2015; Yamamoto, 2016) that microscopic parity violation from neutrino chirality must be reflected in macroscopic supernova evolution. It applies the chiral kinetic theory developed by Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), and others, and chiral hydrodynamics from Son (2009), which were originally constructed in nuclear and particle physics, to the astrophysical context of core-collapse supernovae. The conventional 3D neutrino transport simulations without chirality (Hanke, 2011) show direct energy cascade, whereas this work predicts that chirality reverses the cascade direction, potentially enabling large-scale coherent fluid motion favorable for explosion. The approach enables future numerical tests using neutrino radiation chiral hydrodynamics.
