# Extraction of the Specific Shear Viscosity of Hot Hadron Gas

> We extract the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ of nuclear matter for various temperatures and chemical potentials in the hadronic phase using data taken in high energy nuclear collisions. We use a blastwave parameterization of the final state of nuclear collisions, including non-equilibrium deformations of particle distributions due to shear stress in the Navier-Stokes approximation. We fit spectra and elliptic flow of identified hadrons for a variety of collision energies and impact parameters at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The systems analyzed cover a temperature range from about 110 to 140 MeV and vary in their chemical potentials for stable hadrons. We attempt to assign meaningful systematic uncertainties to our results. This work is complementary to efforts using viscous fluid dynamics to extract the specific shear viscosity of quark gluon plasma at higher temperatures. We put our work in context with existing theoretical calculations of the specific shear viscosity.

## Key claim

The authors extract the specific shear viscosity $\eta/s$ of hot hadron gas at kinetic freeze-out independently of fluid dynamic simulations, using a blastwave parameterization that includes Navier-Stokes viscous corrections, with the caveat that the extraction assumes a sharp freeze-out at a fixed temperature and a simplified flow field geometry.

## Method

The authors fit a viscous blastwave model, based on the Retiere-Lisa parameterization with added Navier-Stokes shear stress corrections, to identified hadron spectra and elliptic flow $v_2$ data for pions, kaons, and protons from ALICE (Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV) and PHENIX (Au+Au at 200 GeV) across multiple centrality bins. They use a Bayesian analysis with a Gaussian process emulator (MADAI package) to determine seven fit parameters including freeze-out temperature $T_{fo}$, transverse flow parameters, and $\eta/s$, while fixing chemical potentials from literature and constraining geometric parameters via a simple expansion model.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperatures extracted range from about 113 MeV (ALICE 10-20% centrality) to 134 MeV (PHENIX 40-60% centrality), covering a temperature range from about 110 to 140 MeV as stated in the abstract.
- For ALICE 30-40% centrality, the extracted specific shear viscosity is $\eta/s = 4.06/(4\pi)$ (approximately 0.323), with freeze-out temperature $T = 121.9$ MeV, transverse flow parameters $\alpha_0 = 0.830c$, $\alpha_2 = 0.0564c$, and system size ratio $R_y/R_x = 1.270$.
- The speed of sound squared $c_s^2$ used in the analysis ranges from 0.158 (ALICE 10-20%) to 0.178 (PHENIX 40-60% and MUSIC comparison).
- Chemical potentials for pions range from 53 to 70 MeV, for kaons from 22 to 100 MeV, and for protons from 138 to 245 MeV across the analyzed centrality bins.

## Context

This work extends earlier viscous blastwave studies by Teaney (2003) and Jaiswal (2015) by incorporating elliptic geometry and realistic flow fields, and is complementary to fluid dynamic extractions of $\eta/s$ for quark gluon plasma at higher temperatures (e.g., Romatschke 2007, Dusling 2007, Schenke 2011, Heinz 2013). The results enable independent constraints on hadronic $\eta/s$ that can reduce uncertainties in QGP viscosity extractions, and are compared with theoretical calculations from chiral perturbation theory and hadronic transport by Prakash (1993), Dobado (2003, 2008), Chen (2006), Demir (2008), and Rose (2017).
