# Strangeness and threshold of phase changes

> We explore entropy and strangeness as signature of QGP for top AGS energy and the energy scan at SPS. We find that the hadronization dynamics changes between 20 and 30 $A$ GeV projectile energy. The high energy results are consistent with QGP.

## Key claim

The hadronization dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions changes between 20 and 30 A GeV projectile energy, with low-energy results (AGS 11.6 and SPS 20 GeV) favoring a chemically undersaturated phase (γ_q < 1) while higher-energy results (30, 40, 80, 158 GeV) favor oversaturation (γ_q > 1), consistent with a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) source.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical hadronization model (SHM) analysis using the SHARE suite of programs, fitting particle yields from the NA49 energy scan at CERN-SPS and top AGS data. They allowed for chemical non-equilibrium by introducing phase-space occupancy parameters γ_q and γ_s for light and strange quarks, and evaluated the strangeness-to-entropy ratio s/S as a diagnostic for QGP formation. The fit quality was assessed via χ²/dof and confidence level profiles as functions of γ_q.

## Key numbers

- The hadronization temperature T drops from 157.8 ± 0.7 MeV at AGS 11.6 A GeV to 123.5 ± 3 MeV at SPS 30 A GeV, then rises to 136.4 ± 0.1 MeV at 80 and 158 A GeV.
- The light quark occupancy γ_q jumps from 0.335 ± 0.006 at 11.6 A GeV and 0.48 ± 0.05 at 20 A GeV to 1.66 ± 0.10 at 30 A GeV, remaining near 1.64 for 40, 80, and 158 A GeV.
- The strangeness occupancy γ_s increases from 0.190 ± 0.009 at 11.6 A GeV to 1.84 ± 0.32 at 30 A GeV, staying near 1.6 for higher energies.
- The energy density ε rises from 190.1 MeV/fm³ at 11.6 A GeV to 429.7 MeV/fm³ at 30 A GeV, reaching 549.9 MeV/fm³ at 80 A GeV.
- The strangeness-to-entropy ratio 100 × s/S increases from 0.788 at 11.6 A GeV to 2.22 at 158 A GeV.
- The energy cost per strangeness pair E_th/ s decreases from 19.25 GeV at 11.6 A GeV to 6.80 GeV at 158 A GeV.
- The ratio E_th/TS shifts from 0.96 at 11.6 A GeV (below unity) to 1.27 at 30 A GeV (above unity), indicating a transition to supercooled hadronization.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strangeness production as a QGP signature from earlier studies by Rafelski (1982) and the NA49 collaboration (Gazdzicki et al.), and builds on the SHARE framework developed by Torrieri et al. (2004). The finding of a sharp transition in hadronization dynamics between 20 and 30 A GeV contradicts the assumption of a smooth energy evolution and suggests that deconfinement may occur at lower energies than previously thought, with a constituent quark phase (quark masses ~340 MeV for u,d and ~500 MeV for s) possibly present at 11.6 and 20 A GeV. The results enable future searches for the onset of deconfinement in the energy range below 30 A GeV and provide constraints for lattice QCD calculations of the phase diagram at finite baryon density.
