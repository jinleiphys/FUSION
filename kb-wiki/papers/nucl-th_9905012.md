# One-particle exchange in the double folded potential in a semiclassical approximation

> The one-particle exchange in the double folded model is analyzed. To this aim the Extended Thomas-Fermi approach to the one-body density matrix is used. The nucleon- nucleon force with Yukawa, Gauss and Coulomb-type form factors are considered. The energy dependence of the exchange part of the double folded potential is investigated and a comparison of the present approach with former ones is carried out.

# One-particle exchange in the double folded potential in a semiclassical approximation

## Key claim

The Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation to the one-body density matrix, when used to compute the exchange part of the double folded potential, reproduces the exact quantal potential more accurately than the Slater or Campi-Bouyssy approximations at small to intermediate separations (D < 6 fm), but its asymptotic behavior at large separations (D > 6 fm) is worse unless a cutoff radius is introduced to remove the hbar^2 correction in the outer region.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the exchange part of the double folded nucleus-nucleus potential using the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation to the one-body density matrix up to hbar^2 order, for nucleon-nucleon forces with Gaussian (Gogny force), Yukawa (M3Y-type force), and Coulomb form factors. They compared these ETF-based potentials with those obtained using the Slater (Thomas-Fermi) and Campi-Bouyssy approximations, and with the exact quantal double folded potential for the 16O-16O system using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for the ground state densities.

## Key numbers

- The ETF approximation reproduces the quantal double folded potential to within 1% when a modified ETF density matrix (ETFM) with a cutoff radius is used, for the Gogny and BDM3Y1* forces.
- For the Coulomb force, the ETF approximation gives an error within 0.25% for separations 6 < D < 12 fm.
- The Coulomb barrier for the 16O-16O system is about 10 MeV.
- The ETF selfconsistent density for 16O gives a binding energy and root mean square radius closer to the quantal Hartree-Fock results than the Slater or Campi-Bouyssy selfconsistent densities (Table 1, not reproduced numerically here).
- For the Gogny force with a cutoff radius R_co = 5.5 fm and for the BDM3Y1* force with R_co = 5 fm, the ETFM approximation reproduces the exact quantal Coulomb barrier height and position (Tables 2 and 3, not reproduced numerically here).

## Context

This work extends the use of the Extended Thomas-Fermi density matrix, previously developed for isolated nuclei by Soubbotin and Vinas (1998), to the calculation of heavy-ion optical potentials in the double folded model. It improves upon the Slater (Thomas-Fermi) approximation and the Campi-Bouyssy approximation (Campi and Bouyssy, 1978), which are commonly used in double folded model calculations with M3Y forces (Satchler and Love, 1979; Kobos et al., 1984). The results enable more accurate microscopic calculations of the real part of the nucleus-nucleus potential, particularly at low energies where exchange effects are significant, and provide a benchmark for testing local density approximations to the one-body density matrix.
