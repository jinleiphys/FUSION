# Ab initio lattice study of neutron-alpha scattering with chiral forces at N3LO

> We present the first ab initio lattice calculation of neutron-alpha ($n$-$α$) scattering using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). Building on the high-fidelity chiral Hamiltonian introduced in Ref. [1], we compute scattering phase shifts in the $S$- and $P$-wave channels using the Lüscher finite-volume method. Our results demonstrate excellent agreement with empirical $R$-matrix phase shifts in the $^2S_{1/2}$ and $^2P_{3/2}$ channels, while revealing persistent discrepancies in the $^2P_{1/2}$ channel for neutron energies above 5 MeV. To systematically investigate these discrepancies, we construct and analyze a simplified neutron-alpha toy model, demonstrating that these discrepancies are not due to the use of the Lüscher finite-volume method. Additionally, we revisit our three-nucleon (3N) force fitting procedure, explicitly incorporating neutron-alpha scattering data through comprehensive Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling. This analysis confirms the stability of nuclear binding-energy predictions and highlights the need for further refinements in the lattice N3LO three-nucleon forces to fully describe neutron-alpha scattering in the challenging ${}^2P_{1/2}$ channel.

## Key claim

The first ab initio lattice calculation of neutron-alpha scattering using chiral interactions at N3LO shows excellent agreement with empirical phase shifts in the \(^2S_{1/2}\) and \(^2P_{3/2}\) channels, but reveals persistent discrepancies in the \(^2P_{1/2}\) channel for neutron energies above 5 MeV, which are not due to the Lüscher finite-volume method but instead indicate the need for further refinements in the lattice N3LO three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors used nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with a high-fidelity chiral Hamiltonian at N3LO, employing the wavefunction matching method to treat the full Hamiltonian perturbatively around a simpler non-perturbative Hamiltonian. Scattering phase shifts in the S- and P-wave channels were extracted from finite-volume energy spectra using the Lüscher finite-volume method on a periodic cubic lattice with spacing \(a = 1.32\) fm and temporal spacing \(a_t = 0.20\) fm. A simplified neutron-alpha toy model was constructed to test whether the Lüscher method caused the observed discrepancies, and a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling procedure was performed to revisit the three-nucleon force fitting with explicit inclusion of neutron-alpha scattering data.

## Key numbers

* The lattice spacing used is \(a = 1.32\) fm, corresponding to a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = \pi/a \approx 471\) MeV.
* The temporal lattice spacing is \(a_t = 0.20\) fm.
* The nucleon mass used in the kinetic energy operator is \(m_N = 938.92\) MeV.
* The pion mass is \(M_\pi = 134.98\) MeV, the pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92.2\) MeV, and the axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.287\).
* The low-energy constants for the two-pion exchange three-nucleon potentials are fixed from pion-nucleon scattering data: \(c_1 = -1.10(3)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -5.54(6)\) GeV\(^{-1}\), and \(c_4 = 4.17(4)\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The regulator parameter for the one-pion-exchange potential is \(\Lambda_\pi = 300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the high-fidelity N3LO lattice action developed by Elhatisari et al. (2022), which was previously constrained only to ground-state binding energies of selected light and medium-mass nuclei, into the scattering regime for the first time. It builds on prior ab initio studies of neutron-alpha scattering using the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) by Kravvaris et al. (2020) and quantum Monte Carlo techniques by Lynn et al. (2015), which demonstrated that three-nucleon forces are crucial for accurately reproducing the P-wave phase shifts and the spin-orbit splitting between the \(^5\)He resonances. The results enable future refinements of lattice N3LO three-nucleon forces by incorporating scattering data directly into the fitting procedure, and demonstrate that the Lüscher finite-volume method is not the source of the remaining discrepancies in the \(^2P_{1/2}\) channel.
