# Net-charge fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> We report the first measurement of the net-charge fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV, measured with the ALICE detector at the CERN Large Hadron Collider. The dynamical fluctuations per unit entropy are observed to decrease when going from peripheral to central collisions. An additional reduction in the amount of fluctuations is seen in comparison to the results from lower energies. We examine the dependence of fluctuations on the pseudorapidity interval, which may account for the dilution of fluctuations during the evolution of the system. We find that the fluctuations at LHC are smaller compared to the measurements at the Relativistic heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), and as such, closer to what has been theoretically predicted for the formation of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP).

## Key claim

The net-charge fluctuations per unit entropy (the D-measure) in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies are smaller than those measured at RHIC and are closer to theoretical predictions for a Quark-Gluon Plasma, but the measured values still lie between hadron gas and QGP predictions, and the signal may be diluted by diffusion effects during the system's evolution.

## Method

The ALICE collaboration measured the dynamical net-charge fluctuations ν_(+-,dyn) using charged particle tracks reconstructed with the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) in the pseudorapidity range |η| < 0.8 and transverse momentum range 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c. The ν_(+-,dyn) values were corrected for global charge conservation and finite acceptance effects to obtain ν^(corr)_(+-,dyn), from which the D-measure (charge fluctuations per entropy) was derived via the relation ⟨Nch⟩ ν^(corr)_(+-,dyn) ≈ D - 4. The analysis was performed for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and pp collisions at the same energy, as a function of collision centrality and pseudorapidity window size Δη.

## Key numbers

- For the most central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at Δη = 1: D = 2.6 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.15 (sys.)
- For the most central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at Δη = 1.6: D = 2.3 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.21 (sys.)
- Extrapolated asymptotic D value for 0-5% centrality: D = 2.24 ± 0.09 (stat.) ± 0.21 (sys.)
- Diffusion parameter σf from fitting D vs. Δη: 0.41 ± 0.05 (0-5%), 0.44 ± 0.05 (20-30%), 0.48 ± 0.07 (40-50% centrality)
- Average charged particle multiplicity ⟨Nch⟩ for Δη=1 in 0-5% central Pb-Pb: 1637 ± 61
- Average total charged particles ⟨Ntotal⟩ in 0-5% central Pb-Pb: 17165 ± 772
- Theoretical predictions for D: ≈4 for uncorrelated pion gas, ≈3 for hadron gas with resonances, ≈1.0-1.5 for QGP

## Context

This work extends the study of net-charge fluctuations from RHIC energies (measured by STAR in Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV, reported by Abelev et al. (2009)) to the higher LHC energy of 2.76 TeV. The results show a monotonic decrease of fluctuations with increasing beam energy, with LHC values falling below hadron gas predictions for the first time, suggesting a QGP origin. The measurement enables further theoretical work on dynamical models of charge fluctuation evolution and diffusion in the expanding medium, as proposed by Shuryak and Stephanov (2001) and Abdel-Aziz and Gavin (2004).
