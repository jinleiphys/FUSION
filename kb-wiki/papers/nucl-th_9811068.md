# Chiral Lagrangians and the transition amplitude for radiative muon capture

> The transition operator for the radiative capture of mesons mu minus by protons is constructed starting from a chiral Lagrangian of the N-pi-rho-a_1-omega system obtained within the approach of hidden local symmetries. The transition operator is gauge invariant and satisfies exactly the CVC and PCAC equations.

## Key claim

The transition amplitude for radiative muon capture (RMC) constructed from a chiral Lagrangian of the N-pi-rho-a1-omega system within hidden local symmetries (HLS) satisfies gauge invariance, CVC, and PCAC exactly, not just to the order linear in the photon and pion momenta as in the standard low-energy expansion technique.

## Method

The authors constructed the transition operator for RMC (muon minus on proton) using Feynman graphs derived from a chiral Lagrangian of the N-pi-rho-a1-omega system obtained via the hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach. The associated vector and axial currents were obtained by the Glashow-Gell-Mann method. The full transition amplitude was built as a sum of the muon radiation term and the radiative hadron amplitude, which itself consists of nucleon Born terms, mesonic (pion) amplitudes, and contact terms of the a1 meson range; the authors then verified that the combined amplitude satisfies the CVC and PCAC continuity equations exactly.

## Key numbers

- The weak induced pseudoscalar constant extracted from the TRIUMF RMC experiment is g_P(q^2 approx 0.88 m_mu^2) = (9.8 +/- 0.7 +/- 0.3) g_A(0).
- The PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar constant is g^{PCAC}_P(q^2) = (2 M m_mu)/(q^2 + m_pi^2) g_A(q^2), yielding g^{PCAC}_P approx 6.6 g_A for q^2 approx 0.88 m_mu^2.
- The value of q^2 in the hadron radiative part of the RMC amplitude can reach q^2 approx -m_mu^2 at the high end of the photon spectrum, leading to an enhancement by a factor of 3 in the amplitude due to the induced pseudoscalar part.
- The axial coupling constant used is g_A = -1.26.
- The mass relation m^2_{a1} = 2 m^2_rho holds in the symmetry scheme employed.

## Context

This work extends the standard low-energy theorem approach to RMC (as developed by Adler, Dothan, and others in the 1970s) by constructing an amplitude that satisfies CVC and PCAC exactly rather than only to linear order in k and q. The authors compare their amplitude to that of Christillin and Servadio (1977) and note that their correction terms linear in k and q differ from those obtained by the standard expansion technique (references AD through KL). The amplitude is intended to provide a more reliable framework for extracting the weak induced pseudoscalar constant g_P from RMC data, particularly given the discrepancy between the TRIUMF result (1998) and the PCAC prediction.
