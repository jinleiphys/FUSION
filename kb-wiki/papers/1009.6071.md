# Systematic study of multi-fragmentation in asymmetric colliding nuclei

> We present a complete systematically theoretical study of multifragmentation for asymmetric colliding nuclei for heavy-ion reactions in the energy range between 50 MeV/nucleon and 600 MeV/nucleon by using soft and hard equations of state. This study is performed within an isospindependent quantum molecular dynamics model. To see the effect of mass asymmetry, simulations are carried out in the absence of Coulomb interactions. Coulomb interactions enhances the production of fragments by about 20%. We envision an interesting outcome for large asymmetric colliding nuclei. Although nearly symmetric nuclei depict a well known trend for rising and falling with a peak around E= 100 MeV/nucleon, this trend, however, is completely missing for large asymmetric nuclei. Therefore, experiments are needed to verify this prediction.

## Key claim
For large asymmetric colliding nuclei, the well-known trend of intermediate-mass-fragment (IMF) multiplicity rising and falling with a peak around 100 MeV/nucleon is completely missing; instead, a flat plateau is obtained at much higher incident energies. The authors state that experiments are needed to verify this prediction.

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model for heavy-ion reactions across incident energies from 50 to 600 MeV/nucleon. They varied the mass asymmetry parameter eta (from -0.8 to 0.8) while keeping the total system mass fixed, and also varied projectile mass from 16 to 56 units. To isolate the effect of mass asymmetry from Coulomb effects, simulations were carried out both with and without Coulomb interactions, using both soft (compressibility 200 MeV) and hard (compressibility 380 MeV) equations of state.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb interactions enhance fragment production by about 20% across all asymmetries studied.
- The peak in IMF multiplicity for nearly symmetric nuclei occurs around E = 100 MeV/nucleon.
- Simulations were performed in the energy range between 50 MeV/nucleon and 600 MeV/nucleon.
- The total mass of the system was kept fixed at 140 units for the asymmetry parameter study.
- The hard and soft equations of state have compressibilities of 380 and 200 MeV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of multifragmentation that focused on symmetric colliding nuclei (e.g., Au+Au by the ALADIN and plastic ball/FOPI collaborations) to systematically explore asymmetric systems. The authors note that experimental studies by Betts (1981) measured fusion probabilities for different colliding pairs leading to the same compound nucleus, and that Liu (recent communication) studied isospin effects on multifragmentation. The prediction that large asymmetric nuclei lack the characteristic rise-and-fall trend in IMF multiplicity calls for experimental verification, potentially by the Michigan State University miniball or ALADIN groups.
