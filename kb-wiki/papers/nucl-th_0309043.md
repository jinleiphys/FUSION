# Gamow-Teller transitions and deformation in the proton-neutron random phase approximation

> We investigate reliability of Gamow-Teller transition strengths computed in the proton-neutron random phase approximation, comparing with exact results from diagonalization in full $0\hbarω$ shell-model spaces. By allowing the Hartree-Fock state to be deformed, we obtain good results for a wide variety of nuclides, even though we do not project onto good angular momentum. We suggest that deformation is as important or more so than pairing for Gamow-Teller transitions.

## Key claim

The proton-neutron random phase approximation (pnRPA) built on a deformed Hartree-Fock state yields Gamow-Teller transition strengths that agree well with exact shell-model diagonalization, even without projection onto good angular momentum; the authors suggest that deformation is at least as important as pairing for these transitions, contrary to the emphasis on pairing in prior spherical quasiparticle RPA (pnQRPA) calculations.

## Method

The authors performed proton-neutron random phase approximation (pnRPA) calculations starting from a Hartree-Fock mean-field state that was allowed unrestricted deformation (spherical, oblate, triaxial), without treating pairing explicitly. They compared the resulting Gamow-Teller strength distributions (total strength, centroid, and width) against exact results from full \(0\hbar\omega\) shell-model diagonalization in the \(sd\) shell (using the Wildenthal USD interaction) and the \(pf\) shell (using the Richter-Brown interaction), for even-even, odd-odd, and odd-A nuclides.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{24}\)Ne \(\beta^+\) transitions: shell-model total strength \(S_0 = 0.51\) vs. pnRPA \(S_0 = 0.61\); shell-model centroid \(\bar{E} = 19.73\) MeV vs. pnRPA \(\bar{E} = 18.37\) MeV.
* For \(^{24}\)Ne \(\beta^-\) transitions: shell-model total strength \(S_0 = 12.51\) vs. pnRPA \(S_0 = 12.61\); shell-model centroid \(\bar{E} = 3.82\) MeV vs. pnRPA \(\bar{E} = 4.26\) MeV.
* For \(^{28}\)Si \(\beta^+/\beta^-\) transitions: shell-model total strength \(S_0 = 3.89\) vs. pnRPA \(S_0 = 3.39\); shell-model centroid \(\bar{E} = 13.54\) MeV vs. pnRPA \(\bar{E} = 12.29\) MeV.
* For \(^{44}\)Ti \(\beta^+/\beta^-\) transitions: shell-model total strength \(S_0 = 0.61\) vs. pnRPA \(S_0 = 0.79\); shell-model centroid \(\bar{E} = 9.95\) MeV vs. pnRPA \(\bar{E} = 7.96\) MeV.
* For \(^{26}\)Al \(\beta^+/\beta^-\) transitions: shell-model total strength \(S_0 = 4.28\) vs. pnRPA \(S_0 = 4.28\); shell-model centroid \(\bar{E} = 11.86\) MeV vs. pnRPA \(\bar{E} = 10.37\) MeV.
* The Ikeda sum rule \(S_0(\beta^-) - S_0(\beta^+) = 3(N-Z)\) is fulfilled in both shell-model and pnRPA calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier tests of the quasiparticle RPA (pnQRPA) for Gamow-Teller transitions, which had shown poor agreement with exact shell-model results, typically overestimating total strength and underestimating the centroid (Lauritzen 1988; Caurier, Martinez-Pinedo, and Poves 1991; Zirnbauer and Brown 1993; Engel 1997). By allowing deformation in the mean-field state, the authors obtain significantly better results than the spherical pnQRPA calculations of Lauritzen (1988) and others, and they note that shell-model studies (Auerbach, Zamick, and Zheng 1993; Toivanen and Dang 1996) had already shown a correlation between deformation and Gamow-Teller strength quenching. The results enable future work on deformed pnQRPA with improved pairing treatment and extension to multi-shell spaces.
