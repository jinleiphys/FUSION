# Event-by-Event Fluctuations Clusterization and Entropy Production in AA Collisions at AGS and SPS Energies

> Event-by-event (ebe) fluctuations in mean pseudorapidity values of relativistic charged particles in full phase space is studied by analysing experimental data on $^{16}O-AgBr$ collisions at 14.5A, 60A, and 200A GeV/c and $^{32}S-AgBr$ collisions at 200A GeV/c. The findings are compared with the prediction of A Multi-Phase Transport(\amm) model and those obtained from the analysis of correlation free Monte-Carlo events. Fluctuations in mean pseudorapidity distributions are noticed to be in excess to that expected from the statistically independent particle emission. The observed dependence of the fluctuation strength measure parameter,$ϕ$ on the beam energy and number of participating target nucleons indicate that nucleus-nucleus collisions can not be treated as simple superposition of multiple nucleon-nucleon interactions. Presence of clusters or jet-like phenomena in multihadron final states are searched for on ebe basis by using the concept of Jaynes Shannon entropy. The findings indicate the presence of cluster like objects in the experimental data with their size and frequency increasing with increasing beam energy. These observations, in turn suggest that the clustering or jet-like algorithm adopted in the present study may be used as a tool for triggering different classes of events.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations in mean pseudorapidity at AGS and SPS energies exceed the baseline expected from statistically independent particle emission, and the fluctuation strength measure Φη increases almost linearly with the logarithm of beam energy for nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the pp data point at 200 GeV falls far below this linear trend, indicating that nucleus-nucleus collisions cannot be treated as a simple superposition of multiple nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data from 16O-AgBr collisions at 14.5A, 60A, and 200A GeV/c and 32S-AgBr collisions at 200A GeV/c, taken from the EMU01 collaboration experiments. They compared the results with predictions from the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model and with correlation-free Monte Carlo mixed events. The fluctuation strength measure Φη was calculated from the mean pseudorapidity distributions, and cluster-like structures were identified in η-φ space using a cone algorithm, with cluster properties characterized by the Jaynes-Shannon entropy.

## Key numbers

- 379 events of 16O-AgBr at 14.5A GeV/c, 422 events at 60A GeV/c, 223 events at 200A GeV/c, and 452 events of 32S-AgBr at 200A GeV/c were analyzed
- For r² < 1, approximately 55-70% of particles are produced through the formation of clusters with multiplicity ≥ 5
- The ratio of maximum cluster number to the value in the last r² bin (ρc) and the corresponding entropy ratio (ρs) increase with beam energy and system size for cluster size m = 5
- Φη values for pp collisions at 200 GeV are much smaller than those obtained for 16O-AgBr or 32S-AgBr collisions at the same energy

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions from SPS through RHIC energies, following the approach of Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) who defined the Φ fluctuation measure. The findings contradict the hypothesis of independent particle emission and support the presence of dynamical correlations and cluster formation, consistent with earlier observations by the CERES and PHENIX collaborations that transverse momentum and transverse energy fluctuations are larger than those in pseudorapidity. The cone algorithm for cluster identification, previously applied to pp collisions, is shown to be applicable to heavy-ion data and may serve as a tool for triggering different classes of events in future experiments.
