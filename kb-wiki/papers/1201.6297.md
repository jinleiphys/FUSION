# Some exact results for the particle number projected BCS approach of the isovector proton-neutron pairing

> The mean values of a many-body Hamiltonian including a proton-neutron pairing term and matrix elements of one-, two- and four-body operators within a basis of particle number projected BCS states, are analytically expressed in terms of a single function Q(N) depending on the number of particles, $N$. The function Q(N) is calculated using a recursion in $N$ in which the shells and the BCS angles are kept the same for any step of iteration. An illustrative example is numerically considered in a restricted single particle space. Some specific features for the standard BCS, the projection after variation approach as well as for the variation after projection formalism, are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytical expressions for the mean values of a many-body Hamiltonian with isovector proton-neutron pairing, and for matrix elements of one-, two-, and four-body operators, in a basis of particle number projected BCS states, using a single function Q(N) that depends only on the particle number N. The key condition is that the shells and BCS angles are kept fixed during the recursion in N, meaning the approach is not fully self-consistent unless the angles are re-optimized for each N in a variation-after-projection scheme.

## Method

The authors factorize the rotation operator representing the BCS transformation into a product of exponentials of the SU(2) quasispin generators J_+, J_0, and J_-, using complex parameters derived from the BCS angles and particle number projection angle. They then express the particle number projected BCS state as a contour integral, and reduce the normalization constant C_N to a function Q(N) defined by a contour integral over a product over shells of (cos^2(ρ_α/2) + ζ^2 sin^2(ρ_α/2))^(2j_α+1). By integration by parts and series expansion, they derive a recursion relation for Q(N) in terms of Q(N-2n) for n = 1 to N/2, with the boundary condition Q(0) given by the product of cos^2(ρ_α/2) factors. The recursion avoids direct evaluation of oscillatory contour integrals and grows only quadratically in computational cost with N.

## Key numbers

* The quasispin quantum number for a shell with total angular momentum j_α is J_α = (1/2)(2j_α + 1), equal to the semi-degeneracy of the shell.
* The function Q(N) satisfies Q(0) = ∏_α (cos^2(ρ_α/2))^(2j_α+1), where ρ_α is the BCS angle for shell α.
* The recursion relation is Q(N) = Σ_β Q^β(N) with Q^β(N) = (Ω_β/N) Σ_{n=1}^{N/2} (-1)^(n+1) tan^(2n)(ρ_β/2) Q(N-2n), where Ω_β = 2(2j_β + 1).
* Q(N) = 0 for N odd (N = 1 mod 2) and for negative integer N.

## Context

This work extends the particle number projection formalism originally developed for like-nucleon pairing (Bayman, 1960; Dietrich, Mang, and Pradal, 1964; Mang, Rasmussen, and Rho, 1966) to the isovector proton-neutron pairing case. It provides an analytical alternative to the saddle-point method used in earlier projection approaches, and avoids the combinatorial explosion of binomial expansions for large N. The recursion enables efficient numerical implementation for the projection-after-variation scheme, and the authors note that the same fixed BCS angles can be used within a variation-after-projection procedure where the angles are determined by minimizing the energy for fixed N.
