# Pion correlations in hydro-inspired models with resonances

> The effects of the freeze-out hypersurface and resonance decays on the pion correlation functions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are studied with help of the hydro-inspired models with single freeze-out. The heavy-ion Monte-Carlo generator THERMINATOR is used to generate hadronic events describing production of particles from a thermalized and expanding source. We find that the short-lived resonances increase the pionic HBT radii by about 1 fm. We also find that the pion HBT data from RHIC are fully compatible with the single freeze-out scenario provided a special choice of the freeze-out hypersurface is made.

# Pion correlations in hydro-inspired models with resonances

## Key claim

Pion HBT data from RHIC are fully compatible with the single freeze-out scenario, but only when a special choice of the freeze-out hypersurface is made where the transverse radius decreases with time; the short-lived resonances increase the pionic HBT radii by about 1 fm.

## Method

The authors used the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo event generator to simulate hadronic production from a thermalized, expanding source with single freeze-out. They compared four different freeze-out hypersurface parameterizations (Cracow model and blast-wave model with a = 0.5, 0, and -0.5), fitting the 3-dimensional two-particle correlation functions with Gaussian and Bowler-Sinyukov formulas to extract the HBT radii Rout, Rside, and Rlong as functions of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Short-lived resonances increase the pionic HBT radii by about 1 fm
- Model parameters for the best-fitting blast-wave model (a = -0.5): T = 165.6 MeV, μB = 28.5 MeV, τ = 8.55 fm, ρmax = 8.92 fm, v⊥ = 0.311 c
- Transverse momentum bins used: 0.15–0.25 GeV, 0.25–0.35 GeV, 0.35–0.45 GeV, and 0.45–0.60 GeV
- Bin size Δ = 5 MeV used in the Monte-Carlo summation
- Static Gaussian source for Coulomb corrections characterized by widths of 5 fm in all three directions
- 381 particle types with 1872 decay modes included in the resonance treatment

## Context

This work extends the single freeze-out model previously developed by Broniowski and Florkowski (2001, 2002) and implemented in THERMINATOR by Kisiel (2005). It contradicts earlier expectations about resonance effects on HBT radii, finding that strong decays increase radii by about 1 fm rather than decreasing them. The results enable further studies of non-Gaussian features in correlation functions, particularly long-range tails from ω meson decays, and provide a framework for incorporating afterburner effects to study rescattering on particle correlations.
