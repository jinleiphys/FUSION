# Density dependent magnetic field and the equation of state of hyperonic matter

> We are interested on the effects, caused by strong variable density dependent magnetic fields, on hyperonic matter, its symmetry energy, equations of state and mass-radius relations. The inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moment of the particles involved in a stellar system is performed, and some results are compared with the cases that do not take this correction under consideration. The Lagrangian density used follows the nonlinear Walecka model plus the leptons subjected to an external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a density-dependent magnetic field in the equation of state for hyperonic matter produces mass-radius relations that are not very different from those found in current literature, while generating a much less bizarre symmetry energy curve as the magnetic field decays with density.

## Method

The authors use the nonlinear Walecka model (GM1 parametrization) with leptons subjected to an external magnetic field, incorporating the anomalous magnetic moment of baryons. They compare two scenarios: a fixed magnetic field (FIX) and a variable density-dependent magnetic field (VAR) that decays according to a function B(n/n0) = B_surf + B0{1 - exp[-beta(n/n0)^gamma]}, with two decay parameter sets representing fast (gamma=4.00, beta=0.006) and slow (gamma=1.00, beta=0.550) decay. The total energy density and pressure include a squared magnetic field term [B(n/n0)]^2.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}
- Surface magnetic field: B_surf = 10^{15} G
- Maximum magnetic field: B0 = 3.1 x 10^{18} G
- Critical magnetic field: B_e^c = 4.414 x 10^{13} G
- Maximum mass for zero magnetic field: 1.97 M0 (solar masses) with radius 12.55 km
- Maximum mass for fixed field (B0 = 3.1 x 10^{18} G) with full anomalous magnetic moment corrections: 2.06 M0 with radius 11.69 km
- Maximum mass for variable field (B0 = 3.1 x 10^{18} G) with full anomalous magnetic moment corrections: 2.12 M0 with radius 12.94 km

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetized neutron stars by incorporating a density-dependent magnetic field, contrasting with the fixed-field approach used in earlier work by Broderick (2000) and following the nonlinear Walecka model framework of Glendenning (1985). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors where magnetic fields are expected to vary with density, and show that variable fields produce less extreme effects on the symmetry energy than fixed fields, while maintaining consistency with observed mass-radius relations.
