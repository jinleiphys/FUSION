# Charged lepton mixing and oscillations from neutrino mixing in the early Universe

> Charged lepton mixing as a consequence of neutrino mixing is studied for two generations $e,μ$ in the temperature regime $m_μ\ll T \ll M_W$ in the early Universe. We state the general criteria for charged lepton mixing, critically reexamine aspects of neutrino equilibration and provide arguments to suggest that neutrinos may equilibrate as mass eigenstates in the temperature regime \emph{prior} to flavor equalization. We assume this to be the case, and that neutrino mass eigenstates are in equilibrium with different chemical potentials. Charged lepton self-energies are obtained to leading order in the electromagnetic and weak interactions. The upper bounds on the neutrino asymmetry parameters from CMB and BBN without oscillations, combined with the fit to the solar and KamLAND data for the neutrino mixing angle, suggest that for the two generation case there is resonant \emph{charged lepton} mixing in the temperature range $T \sim 5 \mathrm{GeV}$. In this range the charged lepton oscillation frequency is of the same order as the electromagnetic damping rate.

## Key claim

The authors argue that if neutrinos in the early Universe equilibrate as mass eigenstates with different chemical potentials prior to flavor equalization (at temperatures above 30 MeV), then charged leptons (electrons and muons) can exhibit resonant mixing and oscillations in the temperature range near 5 GeV, a possibility that arises from the off-diagonal neutrino propagator in the flavor basis.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop charged lepton self-energy in the temperature regime m_μ ≪ T ≪ M_W, using the Fermi effective field theory with the weak interaction integrated out. They assume an equilibrium density matrix nearly diagonal in the neutrino mass basis with different chemical potentials for each mass eigenstate, then diagonalize the full charged lepton propagator including one-loop radiative corrections from electromagnetic and weak interactions to find the true propagating modes in the medium.

## Key numbers

- Temperature regime studied: m_μ ≪ T ≪ M_W, with resonant charged lepton mixing suggested at T ∼ 5 GeV
- Neutrino asymmetry bounds from BBN and CMB without oscillations: −0.01 ≤ ξ_e ≤ 0.22, |ξ_{μ,τ}| ≤ 2.6
- Flavor equilibration among neutrino chemical potentials begins at T ∼ 30 MeV, with complete equilibration at T ∼ 2 MeV
- Oscillation frequency in the resonant regime is of the same order as the electromagnetic damping rate
- For Dirac neutrinos in vacuum, transition probabilities for μ → e γ are suppressed by a factor m_a^4/M_W^4, yielding branching ratios B ≲ 10^{-41} given WMAP bound m_a < 1 eV

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino mixing in hot and dense media (Notzold, Dolgov, Raffelt) to the previously unexplored possibility of charged lepton mixing and oscillations. It builds on the kinetic approaches of Dolgov, Stodolsky, and Manohar for oscillating neutrinos, and on the work of Barbieri and Enqvist on matter effects in the early Universe. The results suggest that charged lepton oscillations could be observable in the early Universe if large neutrino asymmetries exist prior to flavor equalization, opening a new avenue for probing neutrino properties beyond the vacuum-suppressed processes studied by Lee, Petcov, and Bile.
