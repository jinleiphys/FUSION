# Important role of projectile excitation in $^{16}$O+$^{60}$Ni and $^{16}$O+$^{27}$Al scattering at intermediate energies

> The elastic scattering angular distribution of the $^{16}$O$+^{60}$Ni system at $260$ MeV was measured in the range of the Rutherford cross section down to $7$ orders of magnitude below. The cross sections of the lowest $2^{+}$ and $3^{-}$ inelastic states of the target were also measured over a several orders of magnitude range. Coupled channel (CC) calculations were performed and are shown to be compatible with the whole set of data only when including the excitation of the projectile and when the deformations of the imaginary part of the nuclear optical potential are taken into account. Similar results were obtained when the procedure is applied to the existing data on $^{16}$O$+^{27}$Al elastic and inelastic scattering at $100$ and $280$ MeV. An analysis in terms of Dynamical Polarization Potentials (DPP) indicate the major role of coupled channel effects in the overlapping surface region of the colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

Coupled-channel calculations that include the excitation of the projectile (the 3⁻ state of ¹⁶O) and the deformation of the imaginary part of the nuclear optical potential are necessary to reproduce elastic and inelastic scattering data for ¹⁶O+⁶⁰Ni at 260 MeV and ¹⁶O+²⁷Al at 100 and 280 MeV; without both ingredients, the phase of the Fresnel oscillations and the absolute cross sections at large angles are not correctly described.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic angular distributions were measured for ¹⁶O+⁶⁰Ni at 260 MeV using the MAGNEX spectrometer at INFN-LNS, covering cross sections down to 10⁻⁷ of Rutherford. Coupled-channel (CC) calculations were performed with the FRESCO code using the double-folding Sao Paulo potential (SPP) as the bare potential, with an imaginary normalization factor of 0.8. The coupling scheme included the 2⁺ (1.33 MeV) and 3⁻ (4.04 MeV) states of ⁶⁰Ni and the 3⁻ (6.13 MeV) state of ¹⁶O, with the imaginary part of the nuclear potential deformed using the same prescription as the real part. The same procedure was applied to existing data for ¹⁶O+²⁷Al at 100 and 280 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O+⁶⁰Ni at 260 MeV, elastic cross sections were measured down to 10⁻⁷ of the Rutherford cross section.
- The 2⁺ state of ⁶⁰Ni has excitation energy E* = 1.33 MeV and B(E2)↑ = 0.093 e²b².
- The 3⁻ state of ⁶⁰Ni has excitation energy E* = 4.04 MeV and B(E3)↑ = 0.021 e²b³.
- The 3⁻ state of ¹⁶O has excitation energy E* = 6.13 MeV and B(E3)↑ = 0.0015 e²b³.
- For ¹⁶O+²⁷Al at 100 MeV, a normalization factor of 0.45 for the imaginary part of the bare potential was required, compared to 0.8 at 280 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O+⁶⁰Ni at 42 MeV (close to the Coulomb barrier), a normalization factor of 0.02 for the imaginary part was used.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Linares et al. (previous work on ¹⁶O+²⁷Al at 280 MeV) by including projectile excitation and deformation of the imaginary potential, which were not considered in that earlier study. It also builds on the approach of Ohkubo et al. (work on ¹⁶O+¹²C at 330 MeV) where the 3⁻ state of ¹⁶O was found crucial for rainbow scattering. The results open the possibility to apply this coupled-channel framework to other projectile-target systems at intermediate energies, and to investigate the role of continuum coupling in weakly bound nuclei.
