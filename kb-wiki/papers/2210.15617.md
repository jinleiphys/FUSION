# Light nuclei production in pp and pA collisions in the Baryon Canonical Ensemble

> The increase in yields of light nuclei with charged particle multiplicity, as reported by the ALICE collaboration at CERN in p-p and p-Pb collisions at the LHC energy is investigated in the thermal hadron resonance gas model. The model is extended to account for exact baryon number conservation. The focus is on the production of protons, deuterons, $^3$He, and $^3_Λ$H. A very good description of proton and deuteron yields is obtained as a function of charged particle multiplicity in the mid-rapidity region using the same fixed temperature as in central Pb-Pb collisions. The yields of light nuclei $^3$He and $^3_Λ$H though qualitatively explained as a function of multiplicity, are lower than the model expectation. One of the possible reasons could be that for $^{3}$He and $^{3}_Λ$H, the chemical equilibrium is not yet reached at small multiplicities.

# Light nuclei production in pp and pA collisions in the Baryon Canonical Ensemble

## Key claim

The thermal hadron resonance gas model with exact baryon number conservation (baryon canonical ensemble) provides a good description of proton and deuteron yields as a function of charged particle multiplicity in pp and p-Pb collisions at LHC energies using the same fixed temperature as in central Pb-Pb collisions; however, the model overpredicts the yields of ³He and ³ΛH by a constant multiplicative factor of approximately 0.45, suggesting these multi-baryon states may not be in chemical equilibrium at low multiplicities.

## Method

The authors extended the THERMUS code to implement a baryon canonical ensemble (BCE) formulation of the hadron resonance gas model, enforcing exact baryon number conservation via a Kronecker delta in the partition function. They fixed the chemical freeze-out temperature at T = 156.5 MeV (the same value used in central Pb-Pb collisions) and determined two volume parameters: the acceptance volume V_A from charged particle yields and the baryon canonical correlation volume V_C by fitting pion, proton, and deuteron yields measured by ALICE across different multiplicity classes in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV and p-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature fixed at T = 156.5 MeV, matching the value from central Pb-Pb collisions and the chiral crossover temperature from lattice QCD
- Baryon canonical correlation volume parametrized as V_C ≈ 27.3 + 2.9 × (dN_ch/dη) (in appropriate volume units) for the multiplicity range of the data
- Acceptance volume parametrized as V_A ≈ 1.55 + 3.0 × (dN_ch/dη) (in appropriate volume units), valid for dN_ch/dη > 2
- Ratio of experimental yields over BCE model predictions for ³He and ³ΛH fitted as λ = 0.45 ± 0.03, constant within uncertainties across all multiplicities
- Strangeness suppression for ³ΛH is at the percentage level at measured multiplicities, increasing to approximately 15% at lower dN_ch/dη

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach previously applied to strange particle production in small systems by Cleymans (2020) and to light nuclei in central heavy-ion collisions by Andronic (2018). The BCE formulation follows the canonical ensemble method originally noted by Hagedorn (1971) for anti-³He production in low-energy p-pbar collisions. The results enable future tests of chemical equilibrium assumptions for heavier nuclei such as ⁴He and ³ΛH at low multiplicities, and suggest that the canonical suppression effect, where baryon yields are suppressed at low multiplicity with the suppression increasing with baryon number, is qualitatively reproduced but quantitatively insufficient for multi-baryon states with |B| = 3.
