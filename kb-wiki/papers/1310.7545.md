# Confinement, quark mass functions, and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in Minkowski space

> We formulate the covariant equations for quark-antiquark bound states in Minkowski space in the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory. The quark propagators are dressed with the same kernel that describes the interaction between different quarks. We show that these equations are charge-conjugation invariant, and that in the chiral limit of vanishing bare quark mass, a massless pseudoscalar bound state is produced in a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) mechanism, which is associated with the Goldstone boson of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. In this introductory paper, we test the formalism by using a simplified kernel consisting of a momentum-space delta-function with a vector Lorentz structure, to which one adds a mixed scalar and vector confining interaction. The scalar part of the confining interaction is not chirally invariant by itself, but decouples from the equations in the chiral limit and therefore allows the NJL mechanism to work. With this model we calculate the quark mass function, and we compare our Minkowski-space results to lattice QCD data obtained in Euclidean space. In a companion paper, we apply this formalism to a calculation of the pion form factor.

## Key claim

The authors formulate covariant equations for quark-antiquark bound states in Minkowski space using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) and demonstrate that, in the chiral limit of vanishing bare quark mass, a massless pseudoscalar bound state emerges via a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) mechanism, even when a scalar confining interaction is included, because the scalar part decouples from the equations in that limit.

## Method

The authors derive a charge-conjugation-invariant set of four coupled CST equations (the “four-channel” equations) from the Bethe-Salpeter equation by retaining only selected propagator poles and symmetrizing the contour integration. They then specialize to the pseudoscalar bound state, take the zero-mass limit, and show that the resulting equation for the vertex function coincides with the one-body CST-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy, establishing the Goldstone-boson nature of the massless pion. The quark mass function is computed using a simplified kernel consisting of a momentum-space delta-function with a vector Lorentz structure plus a mixed scalar and vector confining interaction, and the results are compared to lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers

- The dressed quark mass \(m\) is defined by the condition \(M(m^2) = m = m_0 + A_0 + m B_0\), where \(A_0 = A(m^2)\) and \(B_0 = B(m^2)\).
- The renormalization constant is given by \(Z_0^{-1} = (1 - B_0)(2m M' - 1)\), with \(M' \equiv dM(k^2)/dk^2|_{k^2=m^2}\).
- In the zero-mass limit, the vertex function reduces to \(\Gamma_0 \equiv G_0 \gamma^5 = (G_{10} - m^2 G_{30}) \gamma^5\).
- The finite result for the propagator combination in the chiral limit is \(\Theta(\alpha \hat k) \to \gamma^5 / [2m(1 - B_0)]\).

## Context

This work extends and improves upon earlier CST-based studies by Gross and Milana (1991, 1994) and Savkli and Gross (1999), collectively denoted GMS, by making the model self-consistent: the quark mass function is now calculated from the same kernel that enters the two-body bound-state equation, in line with traditional Dyson-Schwinger approaches. The formalism enables future calculations of meson properties in Minkowski space, including transition form factors in the timelike region, and allows investigation of the relative strengths of scalar and vector confining interactions, a flexibility not present in Euclidean-space Dyson-Schwinger studies by Roberts and collaborators (2011, 2012).
