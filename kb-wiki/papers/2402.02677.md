# Tunable-fidelity wave functions for the \textit{ab initio} description of scattering and reactions

> The no-core shell model (NCSM) is an \textit{ab initio} method that solves the nuclear many-body problem by expanding the many-particle wave function into a (typically) harmonic oscillator basis and minimizing the energy to obtain the expansion coefficients. Extensions of the NCSM, such as its coupling with microscopic-cluster basis states, further allow for an \textit{ab initio} treatment of light-ion nuclear reactions of interest for both astrophysics and nuclear technology applications. A downside of the method is the exponential scaling of the basis size with increasing number of nucleons and excitation quanta, which limits its applicability to mass $A\lesssim 16$ nuclei, except for variants where the basis is further down-selected via some truncation scheme. We consider a basis selection method for the NCSM that captures the essential degrees of freedom of the nuclear wave function leading to a favorable complexity scaling for calculations and enabling \textit{ab initio} reaction calculations in $sd$-shell nuclei. The particle configurations within the NCSM basis are ordered based on their contribution to the first moment of the Hamiltonian matrix that results from the projection onto the many-body basis. The truncation scheme then consists in retaining only the lowest-first-moment configurations, which typically contain only few many-body basis states (Slater determinants). We present calculations for $^7$Li and $n+^{12}$C scattering using nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory and softened using the similarity renormalization group method. The obtained energy levels invariably demonstrate exponential convergence with the size of the basis, and we find improved convergence in scattering calculations. To demonstrate the possibilities enabled by the approach, we also present a first calculation for the scattering of neutrons from $^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim
The configuration-truncation no-core shell model (CT-NCSM) selects basis states by ordering configurations according to their first moment (trace) of the Hamiltonian, transforming basis dimension into a quasi-continuous variable and enabling tunable-fidelity wave functions for *ab initio* reaction calculations in nuclei beyond oxygen, though the method does not substantially accelerate convergence of absolute energies compared to standard NCSM truncation.

## Method
The authors order particle configurations within the NCSM harmonic oscillator basis by their contribution to the first moment (centroid energy) of the Hamiltonian matrix projected onto the many-body basis, then retain only the lowest-first-moment configurations up to a desired total number of Slater determinants (N_SD). The resulting wave functions are used directly in NCSM/RGM and NCSMC calculations for scattering, with center-of-mass contamination controlled by adding a Lawson-type term β×(H_CM − 3/2) to the Hamiltonian. Calculations use the nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem (2003) evolved to an SRG resolution scale of λ=2 fm⁻¹ with oscillator parameter ħω=20 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For ⁷Li, the difference between energies obtained with N_SD=10⁸ versus N_SD=10⁷ is approximately 2%, and wave function overlap exceeds 98.6%.
- At roughly N_SD=3×10⁷, the change in binding energy compared to N_SD=10⁷ is about 1%, marking the point of diminishing returns.
- The full N_max=20 space for ⁷Li would contain roughly 7.5×10¹⁰ Slater determinants, a factor of 3 larger than currently accessible dimensions.
- At N_SD=4.3×10⁷, basis states reaching as high as N_max=18 are already included, a dimension similar to the full N_max=10 space.
- B(E2) values for ⁷Li transitions to the 3/2⁻ ground state range from 5.139 e²fm⁴ (1/2⁻₁ at 0.75×10⁶ dimension) to 7.360 e²fm⁴ (1/2⁻₁ at 100×10⁶ dimension).
- The number of center-of-mass quanta in final wave functions never grows above 0.002 for calculations with reasonably large basis size.

## Context
This work extends the configuration-truncation approach first introduced by Horoi (1994) for the large-scale shell model to the *ab initio* NCSM framework, combining advantages of static pre-selection methods (like the symmetry-adapted NCSM of Launey (2020)) and dynamic truncation (like the importance-truncated NCSM of Roth (2009)). It builds on prior NCSM/RGM calculations for n+¹²C and n+¹⁶O scattering by Navratil (2010) that used IT-NCSM wave functions. The method enables first *ab initio* reaction calculations for sd-shell nuclei such as n+²⁴Mg scattering, opening pathways to studies of nuclei with mass A>16 for nuclear astrophysics and technology applications.
