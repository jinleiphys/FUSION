# Learning Informed Prior Distributions with Normalizing Flows for Bayesian Analysis

> We investigate the use of normalizing flow (NF) models as flexible priors in Bayesian inference via Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling for iterative Bayesian calibration. Trained on posteriors from previous analyses, these models can be used as informative priors that capture non-trivial distributions and correlations in subsequent inference tasks. We compare different training strategies and loss functions, finding that training based on Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence and unsupervised learning consistently yield the most accurate reproductions of reference distributions. We apply such a sequential Bayesian workflow to a high-energy nuclear physics problem; MCMC with NF-based priors reproduces the results of one-shot joint inference well, provided the target distributions are unimodal. In cases with pronounced multi-modality or dataset tension, distortions may arise, underscoring the need for caution in multi-stage Bayesian inference. A comparison between the pocoMC MCMC sampler and the standard emcee sampler further demonstrates the importance of advanced and robust algorithms for exploring the posterior space. Overall, our results establish NF-based priors as a practical and efficient tool for sequential Bayesian inference in high-dimensional parameter spaces.

## Key claim

Normalizing flow models trained on posterior distributions from previous Bayesian analyses can serve as informative priors that accurately reproduce one-shot joint inference results in sequential Bayesian workflows, but only when the target distributions are unimodal; pronounced multi-modality or dataset tension can cause distortions that cannot be recovered in later stages.

## Method

The authors trained normalizing flow (Real NVP) models on MCMC posterior samples from a seven-parameter Bayesian analysis of diffractive J/psi production in gamma+p and gamma+Pb collisions, comparing supervised training with Jeffreys' divergence and KL divergence loss functions against unsupervised maximum-likelihood training. They then used the trained NF models as priors in sequential Bayesian inference with the pocoMC MCMC sampler, applying constraints from one collision system first and the other second, and compared the resulting posteriors to a one-shot joint inference with both datasets simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The best NF model for the gamma+p posterior achieved an average KL divergence of 0.025 (KL loss, batch size 5000, 6 layers, learning rate 1e-3)
- The best NF model for the gamma+Pb posterior achieved an average KL divergence of 0.052 (KL loss, batch size 1000, 12 layers, learning rate 1e-3)
- Sequential inference starting from gamma+p data reproduced the joint posterior with average KL divergence of approximately 0.1
- Sequential inference starting from gamma+Pb data failed to reproduce the joint posterior, yielding average KL divergence of 6.482
- The simplified test case splitting gamma+Pb into integrated and differential datasets achieved average KL divergences of 0.038 and 0.051 for the two ordering directions

## Context

This work extends the normalizing flow framework from Yamauchi (2023) by adding unsupervised learning capability and applying it to a realistic high-energy nuclear physics problem from Mantysaari (2025), who performed Bayesian analysis of diffractive J/psi production in gamma+p and gamma+Pb collisions using color glass condensate theory. The results demonstrate that NF-based priors can enable efficient sequential Bayesian inference in high-dimensional parameter spaces, but caution is needed when posterior distributions exhibit multi-modality or when different experimental datasets produce tension in the model parameters. The comparison between pocoMC and emcee samplers shows that advanced MCMC algorithms are essential for exploring complex posterior spaces in multi-stage inference.
