# Minimal Wigner-$SU(4)$ Interaction in Microscopic Cluster Models for $α$-Conjugate Nuclei

> We present a minimalist, symmetry-guided interaction for microscopic cluster models based on Wigner-$SU(4)$ symmetry. Retaining only an $SU(4)$-invariant two-body attraction and a local three-body repulsion, this framework is implemented via the generator coordinate method (GCM) to describe $α$--$α$ scattering phase shifts, the low-lying spectrum and transition properties of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, and the cluster spectrum of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$. We show that the long-standing structural tension between the $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ ground states can be mitigated within this restricted $SU(4)$ operator space without introducing additional phenomenological complexity. These results indicate that Wigner-$SU(4)$ symmetry provides an effective organizing principle for $Nα$ clustering, offering a more fundamental baseline for understanding complex cluster structures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a minimal, symmetry-guided interaction containing only an SU(4)-invariant two-body attraction and a local three-body repulsion can simultaneously describe α–α scattering, the low-lying spectrum of ¹²C, and the cluster spectrum of ¹⁶O, mitigating the long-standing structural tension between the ¹²C and ¹⁶O ground states without introducing additional phenomenological complexity.

## Method

The authors implement the generator coordinate method (GCM) with parity- and angular-momentum-projected Brink-Bloch basis states, constructing intrinsic wave functions from localized α clusters that are fully antisymmetrized. The microscopic Hamiltonian consists of kinetic energy, Coulomb, and a Wigner-SU(4)-invariant nuclear interaction comprising a two-body Gaussian attraction and a local three-body Gaussian repulsion, with low-energy constants C and D and a resolution scale Λ. Two parametrizations are determined: SU4A is fitted to the ground states of ⁴He and ¹²C, while SU4B is simultaneously fitted to the ground states of ¹²C and ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers

- SU4A parameters: Λ = 500 MeV, C = -316.2 MeV, D = 1187.4 MeV
- SU4B parameters: Λ = 400 MeV, C = -180.3 MeV, D = 415.5 MeV
- For ¹²C with SU4A: ground state energy E(0₁⁺) = -7.3 MeV (from 3α threshold), charge radius r_c(0₁⁺) = 2.53 fm, M(E0, 0₁⁺ → 0₂⁺) = 5.9 e fm²
- For ¹²C with SU4B: ground state energy E(0₁⁺) = -6.5 MeV, charge radius r_c(0₁⁺) = 2.63 fm, M(E0, 0₁⁺ → 0₂⁺) = 6.9 e fm²
- For ¹⁶O with SU4A: ground state energy E(0₁⁺) ≈ -19.1 MeV relative to 4α threshold (overbinding by about 4.6 MeV)
- For ¹⁶O with SU4B: ground state energy E(0₁⁺) ≈ -15.6 MeV relative to 4α threshold (residual deviation of about 1.2 MeV)
- For ²⁰Ne with SU4A: ground state energy E_gs = -161.80 MeV, charge radius r_c = 2.93 fm (experimental: -160.65 MeV, 3.0055 fm)
- For ²⁰Ne with SU4B: ground state energy E_gs = -157.56 MeV, charge radius r_c = 3.08 fm

## Context

This work extends the line of microscopic cluster model studies of Nα systems that began with the Volkov interaction (Volkov, 1965), which successfully describes ¹²C but fails for ¹⁶O. The authors address the persistent difficulty that improved interactions such as the Tohsaki-F1 force (Tohsaki, 1994) and extended Volkov-type interactions (Dufour, 2005) still struggle to simultaneously describe 3α and 4α systems. The minimal SU(4)-symmetric approach is motivated by the large-N_c limit of QCD (Kaplan, 1995; Kaplan, 1996) and recent ab initio nuclear lattice effective field theory results (Shen, 2022; Shen, 2024). The SU4B parametrization substantially overcomes the limitations of both traditional and recently improved interactions, potentially enabling future studies of the astrophysical ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction, fusion reactions, and searches for new highly excited cluster states.
