# Chiral nonperturvative approach to the isoscalar s-wave pion-pion interaction in a nuclear medium

> The s-wave isoscalar amplitude for pion-pion scattering in a nuclear medium is evaluated using a nonperturbative unitary coupled channels method and the standard chiral Lagrangians. The method has proved successful to describe the pion-pion properties in the scalar isoscalar channel up to 1.2 GeV giving rise to poles in the t matrix for the f0(980) and the sigma. The extension of the method to the nuclear medium implies not only the renormalization of the pions in the medium, but also the introduction of interaction terms related to contact terms in the pion-nucleon to pion-pion-nucleon interaction. Off shell effects are also shown to be important leading to cancellations which reduce the coupled channel integral equations to a set of algebraic equations. As the density increases we find a reduction of strength below the $σ$ region and a certain accumulation of strength at energies around pion threshold. Our results, based on chiral Lagrangians, provide similar results to those obtained with phenomenological models which impose minimal chiral constraints.

# Chiral nonperturbative approach to the isoscalar s-wave pion-pion interaction in a nuclear medium

## Key claim

The s-wave isoscalar pion-pion amplitude in nuclear matter, evaluated using a nonperturbative unitary coupled channels method with standard chiral Lagrangians, shows a reduction of strength below the sigma region and an accumulation of strength near pion threshold as density increases; however, the association of this accumulated strength to experimental enhancements in the (pi, 2pi) reaction is not straightforward because the calculation considers the pion-pion system at rest in nuclear matter, while the pion pair in experiment moves with some momentum.

## Method

The authors use a coupled channels Lippmann-Schwinger (Bethe-Salpeter) equation with pi pi and K Kbar states in the scalar isoscalar channel, taking the kernel from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian of Gasser and Leutwyler. In the nuclear medium, pion propagators are renormalized by allowing ph and Delta h excitations (with finite Delta width), and the pion selfenergy includes 2p2h contributions taken from pionic atom data. Off-shell effects from the chiral amplitudes are shown to cancel exactly against contact terms from the pi N -> pi pi N interaction, reducing the integral equations to algebraic equations where only the on-shell part of the potential is needed.

## Key numbers

- The Landau-Migdal parameter is taken as g' = 0.7.
- The 2p2h part of the pion selfenergy uses C0* = (0.105 + i 0.096) m_pi^{-6}.
- Results are shown for Fermi momenta k_F = 0, 100, 150, 200, and 250 MeV (figure 7).
- In the region close to the f0(980) dip, results including the 2p2h part are about 30% smaller than those omitting it.
- The method reproduces free pi pi scattering up to 1.2 GeV, generating poles for the sigma and f0(980) resonances.

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative chiral unitary approach of Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1997) to the nuclear medium, providing a cleaner link to chiral dynamics than earlier phenomenological models. It addresses ambiguities raised by Chanfray et al. (1996) regarding chiral constraints and off-shell extrapolations, and produces results similar to those of Schuck et al. (1996) and Aouissat et al. (1996) which imposed minimal chiral constraints. The method enables future studies of meson-meson interaction modifications in other channels and may help interpret experiments showing enhanced pi pi invariant mass distributions near threshold in the T=0 channel.
