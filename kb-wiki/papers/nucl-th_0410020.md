# Singlet NN Regularization using a Boundary Condition for OPE and TPE potentials

> Boundary conditions provide a simple and physically compelling Wilsonian renormalization method in coordinate space in the framework of effective field theory as applied to nucleon-nucleon interaction in the non perturbative regime, enabling to remove unphysical regularization cut-offs while keeping physical low energy threshold parameters such as the scattering length and effective range invariant. We illustrate how the method successfully works in the $^1S_0$ channel for the One Pion Exchange (OPE) and Two Pion Exchange (TPE) potentials

# Singlet NN Regularization using a Boundary Condition for OPE and TPE potentials

## Key claim

Boundary conditions provide a Wilsonian renormalization method in coordinate space for the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the non-perturbative regime, enabling removal of unphysical regularization cut-offs while keeping physical low-energy threshold parameters invariant. The method successfully works in the $^1S_0$ channel for One Pion Exchange (OPE) and Two Pion Exchange (TPE) potentials, but the agreement to data is good only up to the region $k \sim m_\pi$, which corresponds to the two-pion left cut.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation in the $^1S_0$ channel with a short-distance boundary condition expressed as an effective range expansion of a "short distance" phase shift, parameterized by short-distance scattering length $\alpha_S$ and effective range $r_S$. Instead of fitting these parameters by integrating from the origin outward, they integrate from infinity downward using known experimental threshold parameters ($\alpha$ and $r_0$), then take the limit $R_S \to 0$ to remove the short-distance cut-off completely. This procedure is applied to the theory without pions (effective range only), OPE, and TPE potentials.

## Key numbers

- Experimental scattering length $\alpha = -23.73$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 2.67$ fm are taken as input for all calculations.
- The short-distance radius $R_S$ is taken to zero in the limit $R_S \to 0$, removing the ultraviolet cut-off completely.
- The agreement to data from Stoks (1993) is good up to the region $k \sim m_\pi$, corresponding to the two-pion left cut.
- For the OPE potential, the renormalization constant $C_0 \to 0$, corresponding to an ultraviolet fixed point; for TPE, $C_0$ oscillates with a varying period corresponding to a limit cycle.

## Context

This work extends the boundary condition renormalization method previously developed by Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2003, 2004) to the $^1S_0$ channel for OPE and TPE potentials, where other coupled channels were also studied. It addresses the problem that practical calculations by Rentmeester (1999), Beane (2001), Entem (2003), and Epelbaum (2004) require finite cut-offs, violating renormalization group invariance as noted by Birse (1998). The method enables removal of the short-distance cut-off entirely, and establishes a relation to the sharp momentum cut-off method used in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.
