# Revisiting the $Ω(2012)$ as a hadronic molecule and its strong decays

> Recently, the Belle collaboration measured the ratios of the branching fractions of the newly observed $Ω(2012)$ excited state. They did not observe significant signals for the $Ω(2012) \to \bar{K} Ξ^*(1530) \to \bar{K} πΞ$ decay, and reported an upper limit for the ratio of the three body decay to the two body decay mode of $Ω(2012) \to \bar{K} Ξ$. In this work, we revisit the newly observed $Ω(2012)$ from the molecular perspective where this resonance appears to be a dynamically generated state with spin-parity $3/2^-$ from the coupled channels interactions of the $\bar{K} Ξ^*(1530)$ and $ηΩ$ in $s$-wave and $\bar{K} Ξ$ in $d$-wave. With the model parameters for the $d$-wave interaction, we show that the ratio of these decay fractions reported recently by the Belle collaboration can be easily accommodated.

## Key claim

The $\Omega(2012)$ resonance can be interpreted as a dynamically generated hadronic molecule with spin-parity $3/2^-$ from the coupled channels interactions of $\bar{K} \Xi^*(1530)$ and $\eta \Omega$ in $s$-wave and $\bar{K} \Xi$ in $d$-wave, and with appropriate model parameters for the $d$-wave interaction the experimentally reported upper limit for the ratio of the three-body decay to the two-body decay mode can be easily accommodated.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with tree-level transition amplitudes for three coupled channels: $\bar{K} \Xi^*(1530)$ (channel 1), $\eta \Omega$ (channel 2) in $s$-wave, and $\bar{K} \Xi$ (channel 3) in $d$-wave. The $d$-wave interactions are parameterized by two unknown constants $\alpha$ and $\beta$, and the loop functions are regularized with a cutoff $q_{\rm max}$. The pole position of the scattering amplitude on the second Riemann sheet is associated with the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance, and the coupling constants $g_{ii}$ are extracted from the residues at the pole. The partial decay widths for the two-body decay $\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K} \Xi$ and the three-body decay $\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K} \Xi^*(1530) \to \bar{K} \pi \Xi$ are then computed using these couplings, with the width of the $\Xi^*(1530)$ resonance taken into account via a convolution with its spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $\Omega(2012)$: $M = 2012.4 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.6$ MeV (Belle collaboration, 2018)
- Width of $\Omega(2012)$: $\Gamma = 6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} \pm 1.6$ MeV (Belle collaboration, 2018)
- Upper limit on the ratio $R = {\rm Br}[\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\pi\Xi]/{\rm Br}[\Omega(2012)\to \bar{K}\Xi] < 11.9\%$ at 90% confidence level (Belle collaboration, 2019)
- With cutoff $q_{\rm max} = 735$ MeV (Set I), the fitted parameters are $\alpha = (-6.6 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-8} {\rm MeV}^{-3}$ and $\beta = (16.5 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-8} {\rm MeV}^{-3}$, yielding a pole at $(2012.3 \pm 0.4, 8.3 \pm 0.6)$ MeV and $R = 11.88\%$
- With cutoff $q_{\rm max} = 900$ MeV (Set V), the fitted parameters are $\alpha = (-20.8 \pm 1.7) \times 10^{-8} {\rm MeV}^{-3}$ and $\beta = (17.5 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{-8} {\rm MeV}^{-3}$, yielding a pole at $(2012.4 \pm 0.5, 6.4 \pm 1.3)$ MeV and $R = 7.22\%$
- The sum of branching fractions for $\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\pi\Xi$ and $\Omega(2012) \to \bar{K}\Xi$ exceeds 95% for all parameter sets

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel molecular approach of Pavao et al. (2018), which predicted a ratio $R$ of about 90% that was much larger than the new Belle upper limit. The authors also contrast their results with the quark-model interpretations of Capstick and Isgur (1986), Loring et al. (2001), and Pervin and Close (2007), which predicted masses different from the observed 2012 MeV, and with the mixed-component molecular model of Gutsche et al. (2019), which found that a sizable $\eta \Omega$ component could suppress the $\bar{K}\pi\Xi$ decay mode. The results enable future experimental measurements of the $\Omega(2012)$ strong decays to further discriminate between the molecular and quark-model interpretations.
