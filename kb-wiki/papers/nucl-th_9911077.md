# Diffusion over a saddle with a Langevin equation

> The diffusion problem over a saddle is studied using a multi-dimensional Langevin equation. An analytical solution is derived for a quadratic potential and the probability to pass over the barrier deduced. A very simple solution is given for the one dimension problem and a general scheme is shown for higher dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytical solution for the probability of diffusing over a saddle point using a multi-dimensional Langevin equation, but only for the case where the potential is approximated by quadratic functions around the saddle and where all parameters (mass, friction, temperature) are assumed time-independent and constant in the vicinity of the saddle.

## Method

The authors start from a multi-dimensional Langevin equation with a quadratic potential and transform it into a canonical form by diagonalizing the mass and spring tensors. They then solve the resulting first-order differential equation system by diagonalizing the drift matrix, obtaining a Gaussian distribution function for the phase-space variables. The probability to pass over the barrier is computed by integrating the reduced distribution for the valley coordinate over the region beyond the saddle point.

## Key numbers

- Critical kinetic energy for half the particles to pass the barrier in 1-D: \(K_{c} = \left(\frac{\beta_1+\beta'_1}{2\omega_1}\right)^2 B\), where \(B=m_1 \omega_1^2q_{10}^2/2\) is the barrier height; in the overdamped limit (\(\beta_1 \gg 2\omega_1\)), \(K_{c} = (\beta_1/\omega_1)^2 B\)
- In the overdamped limit for nuclei with typical values \(\hbar\omega_1=1\) MeV and \(\beta_1=5 \times 10^{21}\) s\(^{-1}\), the critical kinetic energy satisfies \(K_c \simeq 10 B\)
- Diffusion time to reach the barrier top when the critical condition is exactly fulfilled: \(2/(\beta_1+\beta_1')\), which becomes \(1/\omega_1\) in the weak damping limit and \(1/\beta_1\) in the overdamped limit
- For large times (\(t\gg 1/\beta_1'\)), the argument of the error function for the passing probability tends to \(\frac{\beta_1+\beta_1'}{\sqrt{2(\beta_1^2+\beta_1\beta_1')}} \left[\sqrt{\frac{B}{T}}- \frac{2\omega_1}{\beta_1+\beta_1'} \sqrt{\frac{K}{T}} \right]\)

## Context

This work extends analytical solutions of the Langevin equation previously obtained for parabolic potential wells (Uhlhorn, Chandrasekhar) to the case of a potential barrier, using quadratic approximations around the saddle point. The authors note that numerical Langevin simulations for heavy-ion fusion have been used by several groups (Aritomo, Aguilar, Mazurek), but the very small cross-sections make such simulations computationally expensive; the analytical expressions derived here, though using crude approximations, are intended to extract general trends. The paper does not present realistic numerical calculations, stating that these will be kept for a future publication.
