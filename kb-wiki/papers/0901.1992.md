# Mesonic Excitations of QGP: Study with an Effective Model

> We study the correlations between quark-antiquark pairs in different quantum number channels in a deconfined plasma by using an effective model of QCD. Using the three flavour PNJL model, the finite temperature spectral functions for different mesonic states are studied at zero and nonzero quark chemical potentials. It is found that in the $η$ channel resonance structures survive above the chiral transition temperature $\tc$, while the kaonic states seem to get washed off just above $\tc$. The sensitivity of the structures to the anomaly term are carefully investigated.

## Key claim

Resonance-like structures in the pseudoscalar mesonic channels persist above the chiral transition temperature in the deconfined plasma, but this survival is channel-dependent: the $\eta$ channel retains resonance structures above $T_\chi$, while kaonic states are washed off just above $T_\chi$, and the presence of these structures is sensitive to the strength of the $U_A(1)$ anomaly term.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential and the constituent quark masses via gap equations. They then calculated the finite-temperature spectral functions and pole masses for pseudoscalar mesonic channels (pion, sigma, kaon, $\eta$, $\eta^\prime$) within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), summing ring diagrams to obtain the retarded correlation functions. The effect of the Polyakov loop was incorporated by modifying the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions, and the sensitivity to the anomaly term was tested by introducing explicit temperature-dependent forms for the $U_A(1)$ coupling $g_D(T)$.

## Key numbers

- Chiral transition temperature $T_\chi \sim 190$ MeV for the PNJL model at $\mu_q = 0$, compared to $T_\chi \sim 170$ MeV for the NJL model.
- Critical point in the PNJL model phase diagram found at $(\mu_c, T_c) = (314$ MeV, $92$ MeV).
- Parameters used: $\Lambda = 631.4$ MeV, $m_u = m_d = 5.5$ MeV, $m_s = 135.7$ MeV, $g_S \Lambda^2 = 3.67$, $g_D \Lambda^5 = 9.29$, reproducing $m_\pi = 138$ MeV, $f_\pi = 93$ MeV, $m_K = 495.7$ MeV, and $m_{\eta^\prime} = 957.5$ MeV.
- At $\mu_q = 150$ MeV, the transition temperature is $T_\chi \sim 170$ MeV.
- The Polyakov loop potential parameters include $T_0 = 190$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of mesonic correlations in the deconfined phase using the NJL model by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994), who found that narrow structures persist above the chiral transition. The authors incorporate the Polyakov loop to capture confinement effects, following the PNJL model framework developed by Fukushima (2004) and Ratti et al. (2006). The study also builds on two-flavor PNJL calculations of pion and sigma correlations by Hansen et al. (2007) and pseudoscalar meson masses by Costa et al. (2009). The results enable phenomenological predictions for the nature of excitations in the quark-gluon plasma at temperatures and densities relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR, and highlight the need for lattice studies of the $U_A(1)$ anomaly restoration above $T_\chi$.
