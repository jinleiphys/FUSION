# Collective clusterization effects in light heavy ion reactions

> The collective clusterization process, proposed for intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, 4$<$A$\le$28, 2$<$Z$\le$14) emitted from the hot and rotating compound nuclei formed in low energy reactions, is extended further to include also the emission of light particles (LPs, A$\le$4, Z$\le$2) from the fusion-evaporation residues. Both the LPs and IMFs are treated as the dynamical collective mass motion of preformed clusters through the barrier. Compared to IMFs, LPs are shown to have different characteristics, and the predictions of our, so-called, dynamical cluster-decay model are similar to those of the statistical fission model.

# Collective clusterization effects in light heavy ion reactions

## Key claim
The dynamical cluster-decay model (DCM), originally proposed for intermediate mass fragments (IMFs), is extended to also treat light particles (LPs) as arising from the same collective clusterization process, rather than from statistical evaporation. The model's predictions for IMFs are similar to those of the statistical fission model, but the DCM removes the unphysical discontinuity between LP and IMF yields present in the transition-state model.

## Method
The DCM treats both LPs (A≤4, Z≤2) and IMFs (4<A≤28, 2<Z≤14) as preformed clusters in a hot, rotating compound nucleus, using collective coordinates of mass asymmetry η and relative separation R. The decay cross-section is calculated via partial waves as σ = (π/k²) Σ (2ℓ+1) P₀ P, where P₀ is the preformation probability from solving the stationary Schrödinger equation in η, and P is the WKB penetrability through the barrier. The model is applied to the ³²S+²⁴Mg→⁵⁶Ni* system at Ec.m.=51.6 MeV and 60.5 MeV, with a single adjustable parameter ΔR(T) simulating the neck length.

## Key numbers
- For ⁵⁶Ni* decay at T=3.39 MeV, LPs contribute to preformation probability P₀ at smaller ℓ-values, while IMFs contribute at higher ℓ-values (>18 ħ)
- The measured σ_LP = 1050 ± 100 mb for ⁵⁶Ni* decay; the DCM overestimates this by a factor of approximately 5, a discrepancy that is removed if mass-one-particle (proton) is replaced with neutron
- The DCM, EHFM, and TSM calculations are compared with experimental data for ⁵⁶Ni* at Ec.m.=51.6 MeV for even-A, N=Z IMFs up to A₂≤22
- Average TKE is calculated and compared with experimental data at Ec.m.=51.6 MeV and 60.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the dynamical cluster-decay model of Gupta (2002) from ground-state cluster radioactivity to hot, rotating compound nuclei formed in low-energy reactions. It contrasts with the statistical Hauser-Feshbach approach (BUSCO code of Campo, 1991; EHFM of Matsuse, 1997) and the saddle-point transition-state model (TSM) of Sanders (1989, 1991), which treat LPs via statistical evaporation and IMFs via fission. The DCM incorporates structure information through preformation probabilities, which influence yields via resonance behavior observed in elastic and inelastic scattering (Beck, 2001). The model enables unified treatment of both LP and IMF emission without invoking separate statistical evaporation processes.
