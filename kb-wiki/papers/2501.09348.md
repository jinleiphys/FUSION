# Visualization of quantum interferences in heavy-ion elastic scattering

> We investigate various interference effects in elastic scattering of the $α+ {}^{40}\text{Ca}$ system at $E_{\rm lab}=29$ MeV. To this end, we use an optical potential model and decompose the scattering amplitude into four components, that is, the near-side and the far-side components, each of which is further decomposed into the barrier-wave and the internal-wave components. Each component contributes distinctively to the angular distributions, revealing unique quantum interference patterns. We apply the Fourier transform technique to visualize these interference effects. By analyzing the images at specific scattering angles, we identify the positions and intensities of peaks corresponding to each interference component. This analysis offers insight into structural features of the angular distribution which are not apparent from the differential cross sections alone.

## Key claim

The Fourier transform imaging technique can resolve and identify the distinct contributions of four decomposed scattering amplitude components (nearside-barrier, nearside-internal, farside-barrier, farside-internal) in α+⁴⁰Ca elastic scattering at 29 MeV, but the choice of angular width Δθ critically affects whether individual peaks are resolved or merged, with Δθ = 15° providing a practical balance at θ = 64°.

## Method

The authors employed an optical potential model (potential B from Delbar (1978)) for α+⁴⁰Ca elastic scattering at Elab = 29 MeV. They decomposed the scattering amplitude into near-side and far-side components using the Fuller (1975) method with Legendre functions of the second kind, and separately into barrier-wave and internal-wave components following the Brink and Takigawa (1977) approach as refined by Albinski (1982). These decompositions were combined to yield four components (nearside-barrier, nearside-internal, farside-barrier, farside-internal), and a Fourier transform technique inspired by Hashimoto (2023) was applied to each component to generate spatial images on a virtual screen at specific scattering angles.

## Key numbers

* The system studied is α+⁴⁰Ca at Elab = 29 MeV, using optical potential parameters: U = 173.14 MeV, Wv = 6.67 MeV, WD = 46.92 MeV, with geometric parameters d1 = 1.41 fm, b1 = 1.24 fm, d2 = 1.00 fm, d3 = 0.62 fm, b3 = 1.04 fm.
* At θ = 64° with Δθ = Δφ = 15°, three distinct peaks appear in the image: two on the negative X side (farside components) and one on the positive X side (nearside component).
* The barrier-wave-farside component corresponds to a larger negative impact parameter than the internal-wave-farside component at θ = 64°.
* At θ = 30°, a single prominent peak appears at +X from the barrier-near component; at θ = 90°, a single peak at -X arises from barrier-far and internal-far components; at θ = 150°, two peaks appear at -X (internal-far) and +X (internal-near).
* The angular widths tested were Δθ = 10°, 15°, and 30°; Δθ = 10° shows two primary peaks, Δθ = 15° reveals three distinct peaks, and Δθ = 30° resolves finer interference patterns.

## Context

This work extends the Fourier transform imaging technique previously applied by Hagino (2024) to ¹⁶O+¹⁶O elastic scattering, now applied to the α+⁴⁰Ca system which exhibits both nuclear rainbow effects (Khoa, 2007) and anomalous large-angle scattering (ALAS) as noted by Planeta (1979). The combined decomposition of near-side/far-side and barrier-wave/internal-wave components was first introduced by Michel (2001) for ¹⁶O+¹⁶O. This approach enables intuitive visualization of quantum interference patterns that are not apparent from differential cross sections alone, and the authors suggest future application to proton elastic scattering where quantum effects are more prominent.
