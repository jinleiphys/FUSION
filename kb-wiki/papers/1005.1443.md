# Isospin effects on the energy of vanishing flow in heavy-ion collisions

> Using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model we study the isospin effects on the disappearance of flow for the reactions of $^{58}Ni$ + $^{58}Ni$ and $^{58}Fe$ +$^{58}Fe$ as a function of impact parameter. We found good agreement between our calculations and experimentally measured energy of vanishing flow at all colliding geometries. Our calculations reproduce the experimental data within 5%(10%) at central (peripheral) geometries.

## Key claim
Using the IQMD model with a reduced in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section (σ = 0.8 σ_free) and a carefully chosen Gaussian width (0.6L), the authors reproduce the experimentally measured energy of vanishing flow for both ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁵⁸Fe+⁵⁸Fe within 5% at central geometries and within 10% at peripheral geometries, resolving a long-standing discrepancy between earlier IQMD calculations and data.

## Method
The authors performed IQMD simulations of 2500 events each for ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁵⁸Fe+⁵⁸Fe at incident energies from 50 to 150 MeV/nucleon in 10 MeV/nucleon steps, using a soft equation of state with momentum-dependent interactions (SMD) and a symmetry energy linear in density. The energy of vanishing flow was extracted from the directed transverse momentum ⟨p_x^dir⟩ as a function of incident energy for several reduced impact parameters, using a reduced cross section σ = 0.8 σ_free and a Gaussian width of 0.6L (L = 1.08 fm²) chosen for maximum nuclear stability.

## Key numbers
- The EVF for ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni and ⁵⁸Fe+⁵⁸Fe was calculated over an incident energy range from 50 to 150 MeV/nucleon.
- Theoretical EVF values agree with experimental data within 5% at central geometries and within 10% at peripheral geometries.
- Using the free cross section σ_free instead of the reduced cross section yields EVF values consistently about 25% lower than data at all colliding geometries.
- For the more neutron-rich system (⁵⁸Fe+⁵⁸Fe), neutron and proton EVF differ by 3 MeV at peripheral collisions, with the all-nucleon EVF lying between them.
- The IBUU calculations of Pak et al. (1997) show a percentage deviation of about 28% at central collisions and about 10% at peripheral collisions, with an average deviation of about 19%.
- The earlier IQMD calculations of Chen et al. (1998) show a percentage deviation of about 55% at all geometries, consistently over-predicting the data.

## Context
This work extends the experimental study of isospin effects on collective flow by Pak et al. (1997) and the theoretical IQMD study by Chen et al. (1998), which had shown large deviations from data attributed to the choice of Gaussian width. The authors demonstrate that using a system-dependent Gaussian width (0.6L) and a 20% reduced in-medium cross section resolves the discrepancy, reproducing the data across all impact parameters. This enables future studies of isospin effects in the Fermi energy domain using systems with larger N/Z ratios, where symmetry energy effects on neutron-proton flow differences may become observable.
