# Re-evaluation of the $^{22}$Ne($α,γ$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($α,n$)$^{25}$Mg reaction rates

> The competing $^{22}$Ne($α,γ$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($α,n$)$^{25}$Mg reactions control the production of neutrons for the weak $s$-process in massive and AGB stars. In both systems, the ratio between the corresponding reaction rates strongly impacts the total neutron budget and strongly influences the final nucleosynthesis. The $^{22}$Ne($α,γ$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($α,n$)$^{25}$Mg reaction rates was re-evaluated by using newly available information on $^{26}$Mg given by various recent experimental studies. Evaluations of The evaluated $^{22}$Ne($α,γ$)$^{26}$Mg reaction rate remains substantially similar to that of Longland {\it et al.} but, including recent results from Texas A\&M, the $^{22}$Ne($α,n$)$^{25}$Mg reaction rate is lower at a range of astrophysically important temperatures. Stellar models computed with NEWTON and MESA predict decreased production of the weak branch $s$-process due to the decreased efficiency of $^{22}$Ne as a neutron source. Using the new reaction rates in the MESA model results in $^{96}$Zr/$^{94}$Zr and $^{135}$Ba/$^{136}$Ba ratios in much better agreement with the measured ratios from presolar SiC grains.

## Key claim

The re-evaluated $^{22}$Ne($\alpha,\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg reaction rate remains substantially similar to that of Longland et al. (2012), but the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha,n$)$^{25}$Mg reaction rate is lower at a range of astrophysically important temperatures when including recent results from Texas A&M, leading to decreased neutron production for the weak $s$-process.

## Method

The authors compiled new nuclear data on $^{26}$Mg levels from multiple experimental techniques including neutron-induced reactions on $^{25}$Mg, alpha-particle inelastic scattering, proton and deuteron inelastic scattering, the $^{22}$Ne($^6$Li,$d$)$^{26}$Mg transfer reaction, and gamma-ray spectroscopy following fusion-evaporation reactions. They used the Monte-Carlo calculation code RatesMC, previously employed by Longland et al. (2012), to compute the reaction rates, adopting the Longland et al. nuclear-data evaluation as a starting point so that any changes in the rates result solely from changes in input nuclear data rather than methodology. Stellar models were then computed using the NEWTON and MESA codes to assess the impact on weak $s$-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- The $^{22}$Ne($\alpha,\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg resonance at $E_r = 706$ keV (center-of-mass) has a resonance strength $\omega\gamma = 0.046(12)$ meV, measured at TUNL with $E_{\alpha,\mathrm{lab}} = 835.2(30)$ keV.
- The $^{22}$Ne($\alpha,\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg resonance at $E_x = 11.084$ MeV ($E_r = 469$ keV) has an alpha-particle partial width $\Gamma_\alpha = 5.7(15) \times 10^{-11}$ eV from sub-Coulomb alpha-particle transfer.
- For the state at $E_x = 11.102$ MeV ($E_r = 487$ keV), the neutron partial width upper limit is $\Gamma_n < 2-4$ eV based on non-observation in neutron-scattering experiments.
- For the state at $E_x = 11.119$ MeV ($E_r = 504$ keV), the neutron partial width upper limit is $\Gamma_n < 6-8$ eV.
- Using the new reaction rates in the MESA model, the $^{96}$Zr/$^{94}$Zr and $^{135}$Ba/$^{136}$Ba ratios show much better agreement with measured ratios from presolar SiC grains.

## Context

This work extends the evaluation of Longland et al. (2012) by incorporating new experimental data from Talwar et al. (2016), Adsley et al. (2017), Massimi et al. (2017), Jayatissa et al. (2020), Ota et al. (2020), and Lotay et al. (2019). The previous evaluations showed differences of up to a factor of 500 in the astrophysically relevant temperature region. The new, lower $^{22}$Ne($\alpha,n$)$^{25}$Mg reaction rate reduces the efficiency of $^{22}$Ne as a neutron source, decreasing weak $s$-process production in massive and AGB stars, and resolves discrepancies between model predictions and presolar grain measurements for specific isotope ratios.
