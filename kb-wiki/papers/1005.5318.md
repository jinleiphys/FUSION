# A comparative study of model ingredients: fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions using quantum molecular dynamics model

> We aim to understand the role of NN cross-sections, equation of state as well as different model ingredients such as width of Gaussian, clusterisation range and different clusterisation algorithms in multifragmentation using quantum molecular dynamics model. We notice that all model ingredients have sizable effect on the fragment pattern.

# A comparative study of model ingredients: fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions using quantum molecular dynamics model

## Key claim

The effect of varying the nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross-section between 20 and 55 mb on multifragmentation is comparable in magnitude to the effect of changing other model ingredients such as the Gaussian width, clusterisation range, or clusterisation algorithm, making it impossible to pin down the NN cross-section strength from multifragmentation observables alone within the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model.

## Method

The authors performed QMD simulations of symmetric Au+Au collisions at 100, 400, 600, and 1000 MeV/nucleon over the full impact parameter range, and asymmetric reactions of Ne+Al, Ar+Sc, Kr+Nb, and Xe+La at energies between 20 and 150 MeV/nucleon. They compared the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) using different NN cross-sections (constant values of 20, 40, and 55 mb, plus the energy-dependent Cugnon cross-section), different Gaussian widths (4.33 and 8.66 fm²), different clusterisation cut-off distances (2.5 and 4 fm), different equations of state (soft and hard, with and without momentum dependence), and two clusterisation algorithms (minimum spanning tree and simulated annealing clusterisation algorithm).

## Key numbers

- IMF multiplicity in Au+Au at 100 MeV/nucleon is maximal for small impact parameters and decreases with increasing impact parameter; at higher energies (400, 600, 1000 MeV/nucleon) a rise-and-fall pattern is observed.
- For Au+Au at 600 MeV/nucleon, the SACA method with 20 mb cross-section yields results close to the MST method with 55 mb cross-section.
- Gaussian width varied between 4.33 fm² and 8.66 fm²; broader Gaussian (8.66 fm²) reduces IMF production because fragments become heavier than the IMF upper limit of 65 for Au+Au.
- Clusterisation cut-off distance varied between 2.5 fm and 4 fm; narrower cut-off (2.5 fm) produces fewer fragments.
- For asymmetric reactions at scaled impact parameter b̂ = 0.3, IMF multiplicity in Ne+Al decreases with beam energy; for Ar+Sc, similar trend above 55 MeV/nucleon; for Kr+Nb and Xe+La, IMF multiplicity increases with beam energy.

## Context

This work extends prior QMD studies by Puri et al. (1996) and Hartnack et al. (1998) that established the model framework and showed NN cross-section effects on reaction dynamics. It directly compares with experimental data from the ALADIN collaboration (Schuettauf et al., 1996) for Au+Au and the NSCL group (Llopart et al., 1995) for asymmetric systems. The key finding that model ingredients affect fragmentation as strongly as NN cross-sections limits the ability of multifragmentation observables to constrain the in-medium NN cross-section, in contrast to collective flow which has been advocated for that purpose by Sood et al. (2009).
