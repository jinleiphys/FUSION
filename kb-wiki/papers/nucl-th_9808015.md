# Relativistic corrections to the electric polarizability of the neutron

> We demonstrate in a solvable model the connection between the intrinsic electric polarizability $\barα$ and the value $α_{Sch}$ obtained from neutron-atom scattering.

# Relativistic corrections to the electric polarizability of the neutron

## Key claim
The intrinsic electric polarizability of the neutron (in the Compton scattering sense) is not the same as the coefficient extracted from neutron-atom scattering experiments; the two are related by the addition of a term μ²/m, and this relation is confirmed by an exact solution of the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Dirac equation in a constant electric field, provided the neutron rest frame is defined by a vanishing expectation value of the velocity operator rather than by vanishing canonical momentum.

## Method
The authors solved exactly the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Dirac equation for a neutron with magnetic moment μ and mass m in a constant external electric field E. They computed the energy eigenvalues and the expectation value of the velocity operator, then imposed the rest-frame condition ⟨mv⟩ = 0 (where v is the velocity operator containing non-commuting spin operators) to determine which terms in the energy survive. The calculation used eigenstates of the operator p·(σ × E) and took E along the x-axis.

## Key numbers
- The relation between the intrinsic electric polarizability and the scattering-derived value is: ᾱ = α_Sch + μ²/m, where μ is the neutron magnetic moment and m the neutron mass.
- In the neutron rest frame (⟨v⟩ = 0), all terms quadratic in μ² cancel exactly from the energy eigenvalues, leaving ε = -½ ᾱ E².
- The rest-frame condition is achieved when μE = p_⊥ and p₁ = 0, where p_⊥ is the magnitude of the transverse momentum component.

## Context
This work extends the formal argument of Bawin and Coon (1997) by providing an explicit solvable model that confirms the relation ᾱ = α_Sch + μ²/m. It contradicts the naive interpretation that the coefficient of -½ E² in the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Dirac equation (α_Sch) directly equals the intrinsic polarizability. The result is consistent with Foldy's exact solution for a neutral Dirac particle with anomalous magnetic moment, but clarifies that Foldy's solution was obtained in the frame p = 0, which is not the neutron rest frame. The finding implies that experimental neutron-atom scattering polarizabilities (α_Sch) must be increased by μ²/m to obtain the Compton-scattering polarizability ᾱ.
