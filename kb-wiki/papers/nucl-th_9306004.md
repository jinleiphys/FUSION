# Dilepton Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Reactions With and Without Hadronic Inelasticities

> We calculate elementary proton-proton and neutron-proton bremsstrahlung and their contribution to the $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution. At 4.9 GeV, the proton-proton contribution is larger than neutron-proton, but it is small compared to recent data. We then make a first calculation of bremsstrahlung in nucleon-nucleon reactions with multi-hadron final states. Again at 4.9 GeV, the many-body bremsstrahlung is larger than simple nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung by more than an order of magnitude in the low-mass region. When the bremsstrahlung contributions are summed with Dalitz decay of the $η$, radiative decay of the $Δ$ and from two-pion annihilation, the result matches recent high statistics proton-proton data from the Dilepton Spectrometer collaboration.

## Key claim
The authors show that many-body bremsstrahlung from nucleon-nucleon reactions with multi-hadron final states (one-, two-, and three-pion production) is the dominant source of low-mass dileptons at 4.9 GeV, not simple elastic nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung. The claim is conditional on the soft-photon approximation being valid primarily at low invariant masses, and the agreement with data is described as "satisfactory" but "not perfect."

## Method
The authors used a soft-photon approximation to calculate the electromagnetic weighting for dilepton production, separating the hadronic matrix element from the electrodynamic factor. They parametrized elastic nucleon-nucleon differential cross sections from experimental data at 4.9 GeV and approximated inelastic matrix elements (for one-, two-, and three-pion final states) by their dominant resonance structure through Delta-isobar formation and decay. The dilepton yields were acceptance-filtered using the DLS experimental filter, and contributions from Dalitz decay of the eta, radiative decay of the Delta, and two-pion annihilation were added to the bremsstrahlung sum.

## Key numbers
- At 4.9 GeV, the proton-proto bremsstrahlung contribution to the e+e- invariant mass distribution is larger than neutron-proton by a constant factor of approximately 1.5.
- The ratio of sigma(np+pp)/sigma(pp) for bremsstrahlung is 1.67.
- Elastic cross sections at 4.9 GeV: 11.9 mb for pp and 11.1 mb for np.
- One-pion production cross sections at 5.5 GeV/c: 10.8 mb (total), with pp -> np pi+ at 8.03 mb and pp -> pp pi0 at 2.77 mb.
- Two-pion production cross section at 5.5 GeV/c: 11.4 mb total, with each channel assumed at 2.85 mb.
- Three-pion production cross section at 5.5 GeV/c: 11.7 mb total, with each channel at 2.34 mb; the channel pp -> np pi+ pi+ pi- gives dn/dM ≈ 8.0e-5 μb/GeV at M=0.075 GeV and 4.0e-6 μb/GeV at M=0.500 GeV.
- Delta production cross section used: 5.0 mb with a radiative decay branching ratio of 0.6%.
- Eta production cross section used: 0.5 mb (upper limit).
- The single-pion channel pp -> np pi+ e+e- is larger than simple bremsstrahlung by nearly an order of magnitude at low invariant masses.

## Context
This work extends earlier dilepton calculations by Gale and Kapusta (1987) and Haglin (1991) that assumed np bremsstrahlung dominance, and contradicts the conventional non-relativistic wisdom that np bremsstrahlung dominates over pp. The authors show that at 4.9 GeV, pp bremsstrahlung is actually larger, and that many-body bremsstrahlung from inelastic channels (previously ignored) is the dominant low-mass source. This enables future interpretation of heavy-ion data from the DLS and HADES collaborations, requiring state-of-the-art many-body numerical simulations with multi-particle final-state channels and their electromagnetic weighting.
