# Meson deformation by magnetic fields in lattice QCD

> We study light meson properties in a magnetic field, focusing on a charged pion and a charged and polarized rho meson, in quenched lattice QCD. The gauge-invariant density-density correlators are calculated to investigate the deformation caused by the magnetic field. We find that these mesons acquire elongated shapes along the magnetic field. The magnitude of the deformation is about 10-20 % when the strength of the magnetic field is of the order of the squared unphysical pion mass.

## Key claim

The authors find that charged pions and charged, polarized rho mesons acquire elongated (prolate) shapes along the direction of an external magnetic field, with a deformation magnitude of about 10–20% when the magnetic field strength is of order the squared unphysical pion mass (mπ ≈ 0.49 GeV). The abstract's claim of "elongated shapes" is qualified by the use of quenched QCD (no dynamical quarks) and an unphysical pion mass roughly five times the physical value.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations with a plaquette gauge action at β = 5.9 (a ≈ 0.10 fm) and unimproved Wilson fermions with κ = 0.1583 on a 16³ × 32 lattice. A homogeneous magnetic field in the z-direction was introduced via U(1) link variables, and gauge-invariant density-density correlators were computed for the charged pion (π⁺ = d̄γ₅u) and the charged, polarized rho meson (ρ⁺ = d̄(γ₁ − iγ₂)/√2 u). The mean-square interquark distances in the transverse (x̄) and longitudinal (z̄) directions were extracted from the correlators to quantify deformation.

## Key numbers

- At eB = 0: pion transverse radius x̄_π = 0.400(1) fm, longitudinal radius z̄_π = 0.400(1) fm; rho meson x̄_ρ = 0.437(5) fm, z̄_ρ = 0.425(7) fm; pion mass m_π = 0.485(4) GeV; rho mass m_ρ = 0.822(28) GeV.
- At eB = 0.27 GeV²: pion x̄_π = 0.392(5) fm, z̄_π = 0.429(4) fm; rho x̄_ρ = 0.401(8) fm, z̄_ρ = 0.441(3) fm; deformation ratios z̄/x̄ ≈ 1.09 (pion) and 1.10 (rho).
- At eB = 0.54 GeV²: pion x̄_π = 0.359(29) fm, z̄_π = 0.450(4) fm; rho x̄_ρ = 0.399(6) fm, z̄_ρ = 0.453(1) fm; deformation ratios z̄/x̄ ≈ 1.25 (pion) and 1.14 (rho).
- The minimum unit of eB on this lattice is 6π/N_s²a² ≈ 0.27 GeV².
- The pion radius at eB = 0 is r_rms ≈ 0.35 fm, smaller than the experimental value of 0.67 fm from the pion form factor.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of hadron structure in magnetic fields, following the quenched simulations of Hidaka (2012) which used the same lattice parameters. The deformation analysis using density-density correlators was pioneered by Burkardt (1994) and applied to mesons at zero field by Alexandrou (2002, 2003). The results are relevant for understanding the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon discovered by Bali (2012) and Bruckmann (2013), and for constructing hadron resonance gas models in magnetic fields as proposed by Endrodi (2013) and Hattori (2015). Future work should extend the analysis to full QCD with physical quark masses, larger volumes, and other hadrons including nucleons and hyperons for applications to heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
