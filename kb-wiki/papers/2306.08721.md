# Higher Order Corrections to the Effective Field Theory of Low-energy Axions

> Dark matter (DM) can be composed of a collection of axions, or axion-like particles (ALPs), whose existence is due to the spontaneous breaking of the Peccei-Quinn $U(1)$ symmetry which is the most compelling solution of the strong $CP$-problem of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Axions must be spin-$0$ particles with very small masses and extremely weak interactions with themselves as well as with the particles that constitute the Standard Model. In general, the physics of axions is detailed by a quantum field theory of a real scalar field, $ϕ$. Nevertheless, it is more convenient to implement a non-relativistic effective field theory with a complex scalar field, $ψ$, to characterize the mentioned axions in the low-energy regime. A possible application of this equivalent description is to study the collapse of cold dark matter into more complex structures. There have been a few derivations of effective Lagrangians for the complex field $ψ$; resulting to be all equivalent after a nonlocal-space transformation between $ϕ$ and $ψ$ was found, and some other corrections were introduced. Our contribution herein is to further provide higher order corrections, in particular, we compute the effective field theory Lagrangian up to order $(ψ^\astψ)^5$, incorporating also the fast-oscillating field fluctuations into the dominant slowly-varying non-relativistic field.

## Key claim

The authors compute the effective field theory Lagrangian for non-relativistic axions up to order \((\psi^*\psi)^5\), incorporating the back-reaction of fast-oscillating field fluctuations on the dominant slowly-varying non-relativistic field, which the abstract notes must be treated with caution because non-linear self-couplings can induce such effects.

## Method

Starting from the relativistic Lagrangian of a real scalar field \(\phi\) with self-interaction terms up to \(\phi^{10}\), the authors apply a non-local canonical transformation (from Namjoo, 2017) to obtain a complex scalar field \(\psi\) suitable for the non-relativistic regime. They then expand \(\psi\) as an infinite series of harmonics and implement an iterative procedure to solve the equation of motion for each mode, systematically incorporating higher-order corrections from both the gradient expansion of the operator \(\mathcal{P} = \sqrt{1 - \nabla^2/m_a^2}\) and the self-interaction terms up to \(\bar{\lambda}_{10}\).

## Key numbers

* The effective field theory Lagrangian is computed up to order \((\psi^*\psi)^5\), corresponding to including self-interaction terms up to \(\bar{\lambda}_{10}\) in the relativistic potential.
* The quantum-loop suppression factor for the QCD axion in the post-inflation scenario is approximately \(m_a^2 / f_a^2 \simeq 10^{-48 \pm 4}\), based on allowed ranges \(10^9\,\text{GeV} \lesssim f_a \lesssim 10^{12}\,\text{GeV}\) and \(10^{-6}\,\text{eV} \lesssim m_a \lesssim 10^{-3}\,\text{eV}\).
* For the instanton potential, the dimensionless coupling constants are \(\lambda_{2n}=(-1)^{n+1}\), giving \(\lambda_4 = -1\) (attractive axion pair interactions).
* For the chiral potential, the 4-axion vertex coupling is \(\lambda_4 = - (1-z+z^2)/(1+z^2)\), where \(z = m_u/m_d\) is the ratio of up and down quark masses.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic effective field theory for axions originally developed by Braaten (2016) and Mukaida (2016), which were shown to be equivalent after a non-local space transformation by Namjoo (2017). The authors also build upon the method of Eby (2017) for improving the equations for a complex field with harmonic time dependence. The higher-order corrections computed here enable more accurate studies of localized Bose clumps and high-density axion configurations where the classical field approximation may break down, and where higher-order self-interactions become important.
