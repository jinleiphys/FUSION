# Realistic Interactions and Configuration Mixing in Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> In Fermionic Molecular Dynamics the occurrence of multifragmentation depends strongly on the intrinsic structure of the many-body state. Slater determinants with narrow single-particle states and a cluster substructure show multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions while those with broad wave functions, which resemble more a shell-model picture, deexcite by particle emission. Which of the two type of states occurs as the ground state minimum or as a local minimum in the energy depends on the effective interaction. Both may equally well reproduce binding energy and radii of nuclei. This ambiguity led us to reinvestigate the derivation of the effective interaction from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials by means of a unitary correlation operator which is much more suited for dynamical calculations than the G-matrix or the Jastrow method. First results of mixing many Slater determinants are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the occurrence of multifragmentation in Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) depends critically on whether the intrinsic many-body state has a cluster substructure or a shell-model-like structure, and that both types of states can reproduce ground-state binding energies and radii equally well, creating an ambiguity that must be resolved by deriving effective interactions from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors use FMD, where trial states are Slater determinants of Gaussian single-particle states with complex width parameters, and derive equations of motion from the time-dependent variational principle. To treat short-range repulsion in realistic interactions, they develop the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), which applies a state-independent unitary correlator to shift relative wave functions out of the repulsive core region, producing correlated operators that act between uncorrelated states. They also perform configuration mixing by constructing superpositions of randomly rotated FMD Slater determinants and solving a generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- For the Afnan-Tang S3M potential, FMD with UCOM reproduces binding energies per nucleon for 4He, 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca in agreement with Yakubovski, FHNC, and CBF results (exact numerical values not given in the truncated text)
- The clustered and spherical FMD ground states for 40Ca are separated by only 1 MeV in energy
- In configuration mixing for 12C, the ground state gains about 12 MeV of additional binding energy as the basis dimension increases
- 40Ca+40Ca reactions are studied at Elab = 35 AMeV with impact parameter b = 2.75 fm
- Excited 56Fe nuclei vaporize into individual nucleons at excitation energies between 11 and 12 AMeV

## Context

This work extends the FMD framework (Feldmeier 1990, 1995, 1997) by addressing the ambiguity in effective interactions that can produce either clustered or shell-model-like ground states with similar binding energies. The UCOM approach provides an alternative to the G-matrix and Jastrow methods that is more suitable for time-dependent dynamical calculations. The configuration mixing results for 12C demonstrate that superpositions of Slater determinants can recover parity and angular momentum symmetries and reveal rotational bands, enabling future studies of halo nuclei and quantum branching in reaction dynamics.
