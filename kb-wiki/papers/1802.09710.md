# Hot and Dense Homogeneous Nucleonic Matter Constrained by Observations, Experiment, and Theory

> We construct a new class of phenomenological equations of state for homogeneous matter for use in simulations of hot and dense matter in local thermodynamic equilibrium. We construct a functional form which respects experimental, observational and theoretical constraints on the nature of matter in various density and temperature regimes. Our equation of state matches (i) the virial coefficients expected from nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, (ii) experimental measurements of nuclear masses and charge radii, (iii) observations of neutron star radii, (iv) theory results on the equation of state of neutron matter near the saturation density, and (v) theory results on the evolution of the EOS at finite temperatures near the saturation density. Our analytical model allows one to compute the variation in the thermodynamic quantities based on the uncertainties in the nature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Finally, we perform a correction to ensure the equation of state is causal at all densities, temperatures, and electron fractions.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new phenomenological equation of state for homogeneous nucleonic matter that simultaneously satisfies constraints from five distinct physical regimes, but the model requires a speed-of-sound correction to ensure causality at all densities, temperatures, and electron fractions, and the third-order and higher virial coefficients are ignored because they are not well-known.

## Method

The free energy density is constructed by combining a virial expansion (using second-order virial coefficients from nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts) for low-density, high-temperature matter with a degenerate matter description built from Skyrme parameterizations fit to nuclear masses and charge radii, quantum Monte Carlo results for neutron matter, and neutron star observations. The zero-temperature symmetry energy interpolates between quantum Monte Carlo results near saturation density and a fit to neutron star EOSs at higher densities, with finite-temperature corrections from chiral effective field theory added separately. A speed-of-sound correction following Constantinou and Prakash (2017) is applied wherever the EOS becomes acausal.

## Key numbers

- The virial coefficient fit uses scattering length -18.9 fm and effective range 2.75 fm for neutron-neutron scattering, and scattering lengths -23.74 fm (¹S₀) and 5.418 fm (³S₁) for neutron-proton scattering
- The symmetry energy S is constrained between 29.5 and 36.1 MeV, and the slope L between 44 and 65 MeV
- The speed of sound parameter φ is defined at the maximum baryon density considered, n_B = 2 fm⁻³
- The interpolation function h uses γ = 20.0 fm³ and a transition at 3/2 of saturation density n₀
- The Skyrme parameters for finite-temperature corrections include t₀ = 5.06729 × 10³ fm² and t₃ = -1.94596 × 10⁵ fm²⁺³α with α = 1.44165 × 10⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and H. Shen et al. (1998) by constructing a phenomenological EOS that avoids extrapolating a single nuclear interaction model across all density and temperature regimes, instead matching constraints from each regime independently. The model enables uncertainty quantification in simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers by allowing variations in different density and temperature regimes without spoiling agreement elsewhere, and it provides a framework for future inclusion of nuclear statistical equilibrium and weak reaction rates.
