# Ab initio calculation of the $β$ decay spectrum of $^6$He

> We calculate the $β$ spectrum in the decay of $^6$He using Quantum Monte Carlo methods with nuclear interactions derived from chiral Effective Field Theory and consistent weak vector and axial currents. We work at second order in the multipole expansion, retaining terms suppressed by $\mathcal O(q^2/m_π^2)$, where $q$ denotes low-energy scales such as the reaction's $\mathcal Q$-value or the electron energy, and $m_π$ the pion mass. We go beyond the impulse approximation by including the effects of two-body vector and axial currents. We estimate the theoretical error on the spectrum by using four potential models in the Norfolk family of local two- and three-nucleon interactions, which have different cut-off, fit two-nucleon data up to different energies and use different observables to determine the couplings in the three-body force. We find the theoretical uncertainty on the $β$ spectrum, normalized by the total rate, to be well below the permille level, and to receive contributions of comparable size from first and second order corrections in the multipole expansion. We consider corrections to the $β$ decay spectrum induced by beyond-the-Standard Model charged-current interactions in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory, with and without sterile neutrinos, and discuss the sensitivity of the next generation of experiments to these interactions.

## Key claim

The theoretical uncertainty on the normalized beta spectrum of 6He is well below the permille level, with comparable contributions from first and second order corrections in the multipole expansion, enabling sensitivity to beyond-the-Standard-Model interactions at the 10 TeV mass scale.

## Method

The authors used Quantum Monte Carlo methods (Variational Monte Carlo and Green's Function Monte Carlo) with Norfolk chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon interactions and consistent weak vector and axial currents, including two-body currents beyond the impulse approximation. They worked at second order in the multipole expansion, retaining terms suppressed by O(q^2/m_pi^2), and estimated theoretical uncertainties by using four different Norfolk potential models with varying cutoffs and fitting procedures.

## Key numbers

- The Q-value of the 6He to 6Li transition is W0 = 4.016 MeV
- The theoretical uncertainty on the beta spectrum normalized by the total rate is well below the permille level (10^-3)
- The pion mass used for the expansion scale is m_pi+ = 139.57 MeV, corresponding to r_pi = 1.41382 fm
- The charge radius of 6Li is sqrt(<r^2_ch>) = 2.5890(390) fm
- The expansion parameter q*r_pi is less than approximately 0.03 for this decay
- The Fermi constant is GF = 1.166 x 10^-5 GeV^-2
- The CKM element V_ud = 0.97373(31)

## Context

This work extends the first ab initio calculation of the 6He beta decay spectrum by Schiavilla (2002) using Variational Monte Carlo with Argonne v18 and Urbana-IX interactions, and more recent no-core shell model calculations by Glick-Magid (2021). The use of chiral EFT interactions with consistent two-body currents and systematic uncertainty quantification enables predictions at the precision required for next-generation experiments aiming to constrain tensor currents at the 10 TeV mass scale through the Fierz interference term.
