# Constraining the symmetry energy with heavy-ion collisions and Bayesian analyses

> Efficiency corrected single ratios of neutron and proton spectra in central $^{112}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn and $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn collisions at 120 MeV/u are combined with double ratios to provide constraints on the density and momentum dependencies of the isovector mean-field potential. Bayesian analyses of these data reveal that the isoscalar and isovector nucleon effective masses, $m_s^* - m_v^*$ are strongly correlated. The linear correlation observed in $m_s^* - m_v^*$ yields a nearly independent constraint on the effective mass splitting $Δm_{np}^*= (m_n^*-m_p^*)/m_N = -0.05_{-0.09}^{+0.09}δ$. The correlated constraint on the standard symmetry energy, $S_0$ and the slope, $L$ at saturation density yields the values of symmetry energy $S(ρ_s)=16.8_{-1.2}^{+1.2}$ MeV at a sensitive density of $ρ_s/ρ_0 = 0.43_{-0.05}^{+0.05}$.

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis of efficiency-corrected single and double neutron/proton ratios from central Sn+Sn collisions at 120 MeV/u provides correlated constraints on the symmetry energy parameters S0 and L and on the effective mass splitting, but the results are model-dependent and the sensitivity to the effective mass splitting is relatively small for nucleon energies below 100 MeV in the center-of-mass frame.

## Method

Central collisions of 124Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn at 120 MeV/u were measured using the Large-Area Silicon Strip Array (LASSA) for light charged particles and the Large-Area Neutron Array (LANA) for neutrons. Coalescence-invariant neutron and proton spectra were constructed by combining free nucleons with those bound in light isotopes with A between 1 and 5, and efficiency corrections were applied for multiple scattering and nuclear reaction losses. The data were compared to Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) transport model calculations using a Bayesian Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis with a four-dimensional parameter space of S0, L, the isoscalar effective mass ms*, and the isovector effective mass mv*, trained on 49 parameter sets sampled via Latin hypercube.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass splitting: Δm*np = (m*n - m*p)/mN = -0.05+0.09-0.09 δ
- Symmetry energy at sensitive density: S(ρs) = 16.8+1.2-1.2 MeV at ρs/ρ0 = 0.43+0.05-0.05
- Isoscalar effective mass: m*s/mN = 0.67 ± 0.03
- Isovector effective mass: m*v/mN = 0.69 ± 0.04
- fI parameter: fI = 0.06 ± 0.06 (from combined single and double ratio analysis)
- Prior parameter ranges: S0 from 25.7 to 36 MeV, L from 32 to 120 MeV, m*s/mN from 0.6 to 1.0, m*v/mN from 0.6 to 1.2

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of double ratios from the same experiment (Coupland 2016) by including efficiency-corrected single ratios, which enables multi-parameter extraction of both density and momentum dependencies of the isovector mean-field potential. The results provide constraints on the symmetry energy at approximately half saturation density, bridging the density region probed by nuclear structure experiments (around 0.7 ρ0 from mass measurements by Kortelainen 2010 and Brown 2013, and isobaric analog states by Danielewicz 2017) and very-low-energy heavy-ion collisions (below 0.3 ρ0 from isospin diffusion by Tsang 2009 and dipole polarizability by Tamii 2011). The effective mass splitting result is consistent with the lower bound from elastic scattering analyses (Li 2015) but lower than values from statistical analyses of S0 and L compilations (Li 2013).
