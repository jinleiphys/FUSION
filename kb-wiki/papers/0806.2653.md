# Modification of the dihadron fragmentation function in nuclear matter

> The medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions in the deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus is studied within the framework of the higher-twist expansion in the collinear factorization formalism. It is demonstrated that the modification due to multiple parton scattering in the nuclear medium is similar to that of the single hadron fragmentation function. However, the conditional distribution of the associated hadron given by the ratio of dihadron to single hadron fragmentation function shows only slight modification. The final results depend modestly on the nuclear density distribution. Comparisons with the experimental results on two hadron correlations as obtained by the HERMES collaboration at DESY are presented.

## Key claim

The medium modification of dihadron fragmentation functions due to multiple parton scattering in a large nucleus is similar to that of single hadron fragmentation functions, but the conditional distribution of the associated hadron (the ratio of dihadron to single hadron fragmentation functions) shows only slight modification, with results depending modestly on the nuclear density distribution.

## Method

The authors study semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus within the framework of the higher-twist expansion in the collinear factorization formalism of perturbative QCD. They isolate the nuclear-enhanced twist-four contributions to the hadronic tensor arising from multiple final-state scattering of the struck quark off soft gluons in the nuclear medium, and demonstrate that the hard part at next-to-leading twist is identical to that in the single fragmentation case. The medium-modified dihadron fragmentation functions are then obtained by convoluting this hard part with the appropriate dihadron or product of single hadron fragmentation functions, analogous to the DGLAP evolution equations.

## Key numbers

* The hard scale is provided by the momentum transfer \(Q^2\) between the lepton and the nucleus, where the Bjorken variable is defined as \(x_B = Q^2 / 2p^+ q^-\).
* The nuclear enhancement of higher-twist contributions scales as the nuclear size \(A^{(1/3)}\), where \(A\) is the atomic mass number.
* The leading-order hard part for the quark-photon scattering is given by \(H^{(0)\mu\nu} = \pi \delta[(q + x_B p)^2] \, \text{tr}[\gamma^\nu \not{p} \gamma^\mu (\not{q} - x_B \not{p})]\).
* The quark-to-quark-gluon splitting function is \(P_{q \to qg}(z) = C_F \left( \frac{1+z^2}{1-z} \right)_+\), where \(C_F\) is the color factor.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism for medium modification of single hadron fragmentation functions developed by Guo and Wang (2000) to the case of dihadron fragmentation functions. It provides a theoretical framework to describe two-hadron correlations measured in DIS off nuclei by the HERMES collaboration at DESY, and enables the extraction of jet transport parameters that characterize the properties of dense nuclear matter. The formalism is analogous to the DGLAP evolution equations for dihadron fragmentation functions in vacuum previously studied by Majumder (2004).
