# The Quasi-Molecular Stage of Ternary Fission

> We developed a three-center phenomenological model,able to explain qualitatively the recently obtained experimental results concerning the quasimolecular stage of a light-particle accompanied fission process. It was derived from the liquid drop model under the assumption that the aligned configuration, with the emitted particle between the light and heavy fragment, is reached by increasing continuously the separation distance, while the radii of the heavy fragment and of the light particle are kept constant. In such a way,a new minimum of a short-lived molecular state appears in the deformation energy at a separation distance very close to the touching point. This minimum allows the existence of a short-lived quasi-molecular state, decaying into the three final fragments.The influence of the shell effects is discussed. The half-lives of some quasimolecular states which could be formed in the $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$C accompanied fission of $^{252}$Cf are roughly estimated to be the order of 1 ns, and 1 ms, respectively.

# The Quasi-Molecular Stage of Ternary Fission

## Key claim

A three-center phenomenological model derived from the liquid drop model can qualitatively explain the recently observed quasimolecular stage of light-particle accompanied fission, but only for a limited range of sizes of the three partners; the new minimum in the deformation energy that allows a short-lived quasi-molecular state appears only under the specific assumption that the aligned configuration is reached by increasing the separation distance while keeping the radii of the heavy fragment and light particle constant.

## Method

The authors developed a three-center phenomenological model based on the liquid drop model, using a shape parametrization with one deformation parameter (the separation distance R between heavy fragments) while keeping the radii of the heavy fragment and light particle constant. They calculated the deformation energy as a sum of surface and Coulomb terms, then added corrections for differences in charge density, experimental Q-values, and phenomenological shell corrections adapted from Myers and Swiatecki (1966). Half-lives of the quasi-molecular states were estimated using the WKB approximation for barrier penetrability, with the nuclear inertia approximated by the reduced mass.

## Key numbers

- The half-life of quasi-molecular states formed in 10Be accompanied fission of 252Cf is estimated to be of the order of 1 ns
- The half-life of quasi-molecular states formed in 12C accompanied fission of 252Cf is estimated to be of the order of 1 ms
- For the 10Be + 146Ba + 96Sr channel of 252Cf fission, the calculated action integral K = 22.98 and log T(s) = -9.86, with Qexp = 201.486 MeV
- For the 12C + 147La + 93Br channel of 252Cf fission, the calculated action integral K = 39.80 and log T(s) = -2.56, with Qexp = 196.268 MeV
- The first narrow minimum in shell correction energy appears at R = Rmin1b ≈ 2.6 fm, corresponding to magic numbers Z1=20, N1=28, Z2=82, N2=126
- The second shallower minimum appears at R = Rmin2b ≈ 7.2 fm
- The radius constant used is r0 = 1.2249 fm from the Myers-Swiatecki variant of the liquid drop model
- The 3.368 MeV gamma line of 10Be has a lifetime of 125 fs and is not Doppler-broadened, suggesting emission from 10Be at rest

## Context

This work extends the unified approach of cold fission, cluster radioactivities, and alpha-decay previously developed by Poenaru and collaborators (1995) to the ternary fission case. It provides a theoretical explanation for experimental observations by Ramayya et al. (1998) and Singh et al. (1996) of non-Doppler-broadened gamma-ray lines from 10Be and 12C in the cold ternary fission of 252Cf, which suggested trapping of the light particle in a nuclear molecular potential well. The model builds on the concept of quasi-molecular configurations of two nuclei first suggested by Bromley et al. (1960) for 12C+12C scattering resonances, and extends it to three-nuclei configurations. The work enables future microscopic three-center shell model calculations and suggests that the quasimolecular minimum is related to collective liquid-drop-like behavior rather than shell effects for light-particle accompanied fission, unlike the case of "true" ternary fission where no such liquid drop model effect was found.
