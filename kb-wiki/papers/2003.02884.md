# Improved opacity expansion for medium-induced parton splitting

> We revisit the calculation of the medium-induced gluon radiative spectrum and propose a novel expansion scheme that encompasses the two known analytic limits: i) the high frequency regime dominated by a single hard scattering that corresponds to the leading order in the standard opacity expansion, ii) the low frequency regime that is dominated by multiple soft scatterings. Our approach is based on expanding around the harmonic oscillator instead of vacuum in the leading logarithmic approximation. We compute the first two orders in this improved opacity expansion and show that they account for the aforementioned limits.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an improved opacity expansion for the medium-induced gluon radiative spectrum that, by expanding around the harmonic oscillator instead of vacuum in the leading logarithmic approximation, simultaneously reproduces the BDMPS-Z multiple-soft-scattering limit at low frequencies and the GLV single-hard-scattering limit at high frequencies, provided the separation scale \(Q^2\) is chosen such that \(\ln(Q^2/\mu^2) \gg \ln(1/(x_\perp^2 Q^2))\).

## Method

The author splits the in-medium scattering potential \(v(\mathbf{x},t)\) into a harmonic-oscillator part \(v_\text{HO}\) (containing the leading logarithmic contribution proportional to \(\ln(Q^2/\mu^2)\)) and a perturbative remainder \(v_\text{pert}\) (containing \(\ln(1/(\mathbf{x}^2 Q^2))\)). The gluon spectrum is then computed to next-to-leading order (NLO) by treating \(v_\text{pert}\) as a perturbation around the harmonic-oscillator Green's function, rather than expanding order by order in the number of scatterings (standard opacity expansion). The first term in the NLO result is the BDMPS-Z spectrum with a \(Q^2\)-dependent transport coefficient, and the second term accounts for the single-hard-scattering (GLV) regime.

## Key numbers

* The BDMPS-Z spectrum at low frequencies (\(\omega \ll \omega_c\)) scales as \(\omega \, dI_\text{BDMPS}/d\omega \simeq 2\bar\alpha \sqrt{\omega_c/(2\omega)}\), where \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2\).
* The GLV spectrum at high frequencies (\(\omega \gg \omega_c\)) scales as \(\omega \, dI_\text{GLV}/d\omega \simeq \bar\alpha (\pi/4) (\hat q_0 L^2/(2\omega))\), with \(\hat q_0 = 4\pi\alpha_s^2 C_R n\).
* The numerical example uses \(\hat q_0 = 0.1\) GeV\(^3\), \(L = 4\) fm, \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV, yielding \(\hat q \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(\omega_\text{BH} \simeq 0.08\) GeV, and \(\omega_c \equiv \hat q_0 L^2 = 40\) GeV.
* The separation scale is chosen as \(Q^2 \simeq \sqrt{\omega \hat q(Q^2)} \simeq \sqrt{\omega \hat q_0 \ln(\sqrt{\omega \hat q_0}/\mu^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard opacity expansion of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2000) and Wiedemann (2000), and the BDMPS-Z multiple-soft-scattering approximation (Baier et al., 1996; Zakharov, 1996), by providing a unified analytic formula that interpolates between both regimes. The approach is inspired by Molière scattering theory and enables more accurate phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, reducing theoretical uncertainties associated with using either the BDMPS or GLV approximations separately. The resulting spectrum can be implemented in Monte Carlo event generators for in-medium parton cascades.
