# Studies of quasiclassical approach applicability to true three-body decays

> Within the hyperspherical harmonics approach the three-body problem is reduced to a motion of one effective particle in a "strongly deformed" field, which is described in coupled-channel formalism. This method is especially suited to studies of phenomena characterized by genuine three-body dynamics, e.g. Borromean haloes and true three-body decays. The reduction of the hyperspherical equations set to a single-channel Schrödinger equation provides the basis for the use of the standard quasiclassical expression for calculations of widths for true three-body decays. We demonstrate that the quasiclassical approach by itself is quite precise in application to typical profiles of the three-body effective potentials. However, the reduction to single-channel formalism leads to significant overestimation of the two-proton width $Γ_{2p}$. This is demonstrated by the example of the $^{17}$Ne first excited $3/2^-$ state decay, questioning, however, the applicability of such an approximation in general.

## Key claim

The quasiclassical (Gamow-type) approximation itself is quite precise for typical three-body effective potential barriers, but the reduction of the full three-body problem to a single-channel formalism leads to a significant overestimation of the two-proton width, demonstrated for the $^{17}$Ne first excited $3/2^-$ state; this calls into question the general applicability of such a single-channel reduction for true three-body decays.

## Method

The authors performed systematic comparisons of quasiclassical (QC) width calculations with integral formula (IF) results for two-body test systems (dineutron+dineutron and $^{15}$F $\rightarrow$ $^{14}$O+$p$) to validate the QC method itself. They then applied the QC and IF methods to effective single-channel potentials obtained from both the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method and the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion (HAE) method for the $^{17}$Ne $3/2^-$ state, and compared these single-channel results to full dynamical three-body calculations from Grigorenko (2007).

## Key numbers

* For the $^{17}$Ne $3/2^-$ state, the QC width obtained from the best-converged HAE effective potential of Garrido (2008) is $6.3 \times 10^{-12}$ MeV, exceeding the experimental limit $\Gamma_{2p} < 8.8 \times 10^{-13}$ MeV from Sharov (2017) by an order of magnitude.
* For the same state, the ratio of the QC width to the full three-body width from Grigorenko (2007) grows from 10.8 for basis size $K_{\max}=12$ to 81.0 for $K_{\max}=48$, with an asymptotic extrapolation giving a ratio of 141.
* The IF and QC widths for the single-channel reduction of the HH potentials from Grigorenko (2007) asymptotically extrapolate to $1100 \times 10^{-15}$ MeV and $971 \times 10^{-15}$ MeV respectively, while the full three-body calculation asymptotically gives $6.90 \times 10^{-15}$ MeV.
* For the two-body test case $^{15}$F $\rightarrow$ $^{14}$O+$p$, the QC and IF results agree within a few percent to a few tens of percent for various angular momenta and diffusenesses.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing controversy over the $2p$ decay width of the $^{17}$Ne $3/2^-$ state, previously debated between Grigorenko (2003, 2007) and Garrido (2004b, 2008). It directly contradicts the single-channel quasiclassical approach used in a series of papers by Garrido (2004b, 2005, 2005a, 2008, 2010, 2011) and Hove (2016, 2017) for three-body decay widths. The findings suggest that all such single-channel quasiclassical calculations should be reexamined, and that full dynamical three-body calculations (as in Grigorenko 2007) are necessary for reliable width predictions.
