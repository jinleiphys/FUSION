# Oblate-prolate transition in odd-mass light mercury isotopes

> Anomalous isotope shifts in the chain of light Hg isotopes are investigated by using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the Skyrme SIII, SkI3 and SLy4 forces. The sharp increase in the mean-square radius of the odd mass $^{181-185}$Hg isotopes is well explained in terms of the transition from an oblate to a prolate shape in the ground state of these isotopes. We discuss the polarization energy of time-odd mean-field terms in relation to the blocked level by the odd neutron.

## Key claim

The sharp increase in the mean-square charge radius of odd-mass \(^{181-185}\)Hg isotopes is explained by a transition from an oblate to a prolate ground-state shape, driven primarily by the loss of pairing energy due to blocking by the odd neutron, with the time-odd mean-field terms providing a weak (few tenths) opposing contribution.

## Method

The authors performed axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using Skyrme effective interactions (SIII, SkI3, SLy4) and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force. Lipkin-Nogami corrections were applied to both mean-field and pairing energies to handle the pairing phase transition between oblate and prolate minima. For odd nuclei, the time-reversal symmetry was broken by blocking the lowest quasi-particle state, and the polarization energy from time-odd mean-field terms was computed separately from the blocking energy.

## Key numbers

- The blocking energy in the oblate minimum is larger by **~200 keV** (SIII) or **~200 keV** (SLy4) than in the prolate minimum for \(^{181-185}\)Hg.
- The polarization energy with full time-odd terms is attractive and less than **-50 keV** in the oblate minimum and repulsive and less than **50 keV** in the prolate minimum (SIII).
- When the \(C^s_t\) terms are omitted, the polarization energy becomes attractive and more than **-250 keV** in the oblate minimum and attractive around **-150 keV** in the prolate minimum (SIII).
- The oblate minimum in even Hg isotopes has deformation \(\beta_2 \sim -0.17\) (for \(^{200}\)Hg) and the prolate minimum has \(\beta_2 \sim 0.29\) (for \(^{184}\)Hg), from SIII-HF calculations.
- The basis space cutoff was \(N_0 = 18\) oscillator shells, with \(\bar{e}_{\rm max} = 60\) MeV for quasi-particle states.

## Context

This work extends the earlier mechanism proposed by Frauendorf and Pashkevich (1975), who used Strutinsky's shell correction method to predict the oblate-prolate transition in odd-mass Hg isotopes. It provides the first systematic mean-field treatment of both even and odd Hg isotopes using Skyrme HFB with blocking, going beyond earlier even-only calculations by Chasman (1973), Yamaji and Tanaka (1997), Rutz and Reinhard (2000), and Nazarewicz and Volpe (2002). The results enable future studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes and tests of time-odd mean-field terms in other mass regions.
