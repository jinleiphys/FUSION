# Repulsive properties of hadrons in lattice QCD data and neutron stars

> Second-order susceptibilities $χ^{11}_{ij}$ of baryon, electric, and strangeness, $B$, $Q$, and $S$, charges, are calculated in the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model and compared to available lattice QCD data. The susceptibilities are sensitive to the short range repulsive interactions between different hadron species, especially to the hardcore repulsion of hyperons. Decreasing the hyperons size, as compared to the size of the non-strange baryons, does improve significantly the agreement of the CMF model results with the Lattice QCD data. The electric charge-dependent susceptibilities are sensitive to the short range repulsive volume of mesons. The comparison with lattice QCD data suggests that strange baryons, non-strange mesons and strange mesons have significantly smaller excluded volumes than non-strange baryons. The CMF model with these modified hadron volumes allows for a mainly hadronic description of the QCD susceptibilities significantly above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature. This improved CMF model which is based on the lattice QCD data, has been used to study the properties of both cold QCD matter and neutron star matter. The phase structure in both cases is essentially unchanged, i.e. a chiral first-order phase transition occurs at low temperatures ($T_{\rm CP}\approx 17$ MeV), and hyperons survive deconfinement to higher densities than non-strange hadrons. The neutron star maximal mass remains close to 2.1$M_\odot$ and the mass-radius diagram is only modified slightly due to the appearance of hyperons and is in agreement with astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that lattice QCD second-order susceptibilities can be described by a hadronic model (Chiral Mean Field model) with species-dependent excluded volumes, but only if strange baryons, non-strange mesons, and strange mesons are assigned significantly smaller hard-core radii than non-strange baryons; the description holds "significantly above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature" but the chiral first-order phase transition at low temperature (T_CP ≈ 17 MeV) and the neutron star maximum mass (~2.1 solar masses) remain essentially unchanged.

## Method

Second-order conserved-charge susceptibilities χ^{11}_{ij} (baryon B, electric Q, strangeness S) were computed in the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, which couples a chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet baryon Lagrangian, a full hadron resonance gas, excluded-volume repulsive interactions, and a Polyakov-loop quark sector. The excluded-volume parameters for strange baryons (v_BS), non-strange mesons (v_M), and strange mesons (v_MS) were varied independently while the non-strange baryon volume was fixed at v_B = 1 fm^3, and the resulting susceptibilities were compared to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations. The best-fit parametrization was then used to compute the equation of state for isospin-symmetric matter, heavy-ion collision matter, and neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- Non-strange baryon excluded volume fixed at v_B = 1 fm^3, corresponding to a hard-core radius R ≈ 0.39 fm.
- Best-fit strange baryon excluded volume: v_BS = 1/4 fm^3.
- Best-fit non-strange meson excluded volume: v_M = 1/2 fm^3.
- Best-fit strange meson excluded volume: v_MS = 1/4 fm^3.
- Nuclear ground-state properties reproduced: saturation density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E_0/B ≈ -15.2 MeV, asymmetry energy S_0 ≈ 31.9 MeV, compressibility K_0 ≈ 267 MeV.
- Effective nucleon mass at nuclear saturation density: m*_N(ρ = ρ_0) = 816 MeV.
- Critical temperature of the chiral first-order phase transition: T_CP ≈ 17 MeV.
- Neutron star maximal mass remains close to 2.1 M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that used excluded-volume hadron resonance gas models to describe lattice QCD susceptibilities (Vovchenko 2017) and thermal model analyses of experimental hadron yields (Alba 2016). It also builds on the CMF model's previous applications to nuclear matter and neutron stars (Steinheimer 2011, Motornenko 2019). The results enable a mainly hadronic description of lattice QCD data up to high temperatures and provide a framework to study how hyperonic repulsion affects the QCD phase diagram and neutron star structure, with the key finding that hyperons can survive to higher densities than non-strange hadrons without altering the maximum neutron star mass.
