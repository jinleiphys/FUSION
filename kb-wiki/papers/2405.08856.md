# Chiral properties of the nucleon interpolating current and $θ$-dependent observables

> We revisit the chiral properties of nucleon interpolating currents, and show that of the two leading order currents $j_1$ and $j_2$, only two linear combinations $j_1\pm j_2$ transform covariantly under the anomalous $U(1)_A$ symmetry. As a result, calculations of quantities which vanish by symmetry in the chiral limit may produce unphysical results if carried out with different linear combinations of the currents. This includes observables such as electric dipole moments, induced by the QCD parameter $θ$, and the $θ$-dependence of the nucleon mass. For completeness, we also exhibit the leading order results for nucleon electric dipole moments ($d_{n,p}$) induced by $θ$, and the nucleon magnetic moments ($μ_{n,p}$), when calculated using QCD sum rules for both the covariant choices of the nucleon interpolating current. The results in each channel, conveniently expressed as the ratios, $d_{n,p}/μ_{n,p}$, are numerically consistent, and reflect the required physical dependence on $θ$.

## Key claim
Only two linear combinations of the leading-order nucleon interpolating currents, \(j_1 \pm j_2\), transform covariantly under the anomalous \(U(1)_A\) symmetry; calculations of \(\theta\)-dependent observables such as nucleon electric dipole moments using other current choices (e.g., the nonrelativistic \(\beta=0\) current) may produce unphysical results that do not vanish in the chiral limit as required by QCD.

## Method
The authors analyze the chiral transformation properties of the two lowest-dimension nucleon interpolating currents \(j_1\) and \(j_2\) under the anomalous \(U(1)_A\) symmetry. They compute nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions in an external electromagnetic field using the operator product expansion (OPE) in the presence of the QCD \(\theta\) parameter, and examine the \(\theta\)-dependence of chirality-flipping structures for general linear combinations \(j_1 + \beta j_2\). They then construct QCD sum rules for the nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) and magnetic dipole moment (MDM) using only the covariant choices \(\beta = \pm 1\), extracting the ratios \(d_{n,p}/\mu_{n,p}\).

## Key numbers
- The two covariant current combinations are \(j_1 + j_2\) and \(j_1 - j_2\) (i.e., \(\beta = \pm 1\)).
- For \(\beta = 0\) (the nonrelativistic current), the two-point correlator contains two distinct chiral phases \(e^{\pm i\theta_G \gamma_5/2}\), leading to an unphysical \(\theta^2\) contribution to the nucleon mass in the chiral limit; the spurious phase is proportional to \(\theta_G^2/49\) at leading order.
- After subtracting the chiral phase from the two-point function, the residual EDM correlator for general \(\beta\) is proportional to \((1-\beta)^2(1+\beta)/(7+5\beta)\), which vanishes only for \(\beta = \pm 1\).
- The magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum is parametrized as \(\langle \bar{q}\sigma_{\mu\nu}q\rangle_F = \chi_q F_{\mu\nu}\langle \bar{q}q\rangle = \tilde{\chi}_q F_{\mu\nu}\), with \(\tilde{\chi}_q\) defined for brevity.

## Context
This work extends earlier QCD sum rule calculations of the neutron EDM by Pospelov and collaborators (1999, 2005) and Hisano et al. (2012), which used the \(\beta = +1\) current, and by Ioffe (1983) for nucleon magnetic moments. It also addresses a subtlety noted in lattice QCD computations of the neutron EDM by Abramczyk et al. (2017) and Bhattacharya et al. (2021), who proposed subtracting a chiral phase from the three-point function; the present paper shows that this procedure only yields physical results when the covariant currents \(\beta = \pm 1\) are used. The results enable reliable extraction of \(\theta\)-dependent nucleon observables from QCD sum rules and lattice QCD by enforcing the correct chiral-limit behavior.
