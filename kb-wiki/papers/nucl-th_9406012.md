# Chaos in Axially Symmetric Potentials with Octupole Deformation

> Classical and quantum mechanical results are reported for the single particle motion in a harmonic oscillator potential which is characterized by a quadrupole deformation and an additional octupole deformation. The chaotic character of the motion is srongly dependent on the quadrupole deformation in that for a prolate deformation virtually no chaos is discernible while for the oblate case the motion shows strong chaos when the octupole term is turned on.

## Key claim
Classical and quantum single-particle motion in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator potential with both quadrupole and octupole deformation shows a strong dependence on the sign of the quadrupole deformation: for prolate deformation (b>1) virtually no chaos is discernible even with the octupole term, while for oblate deformation (b<1) the motion becomes strongly chaotic when the octupole term is turned on.

## Method
The authors studied classical single-particle motion by numerically integrating the equations of motion for a potential V(ρ,z) = (mω²/2)[ρ² + z²/b² + λ(2z³-3zρ²)/√(ρ²+z²)], using surfaces of section and Lyapunov exponents to quantify chaos. For the quantum mechanical treatment, they diagonalized the Hamiltonian H₀+λH₁ in a basis of asymptotic quantum numbers (n⊥, nz, Λ) using matrices up to 1600×1600 dimensions, analyzing nearest-neighbor distributions (NND) of energy levels. All results were obtained for zero temperature and zero z-component of angular momentum (pφ=0).

## Key numbers
- For b=2 (superdeformed prolate nucleus), the Lyapunov exponent is virtually zero for all λ < λc, with only some scattering of dots in surfaces of section when λ is very close to λc.
- For b≈0.58, the motion is maximally chaotic; at λ=2/3 λc, no structure is discernible in surfaces of section.
- For b=0.58, λc≈1.5, where valleys along both the ρ=0 and ρ=0.4z directions open simultaneously.
- For b=0.5, λc≈1.64 for the valley along ρ=0.4z, while the valley along the z-axis opens at a larger λ.
- For λ ≤ 0.9λc and 0.5 ≤ b ≤ 2, the variation of the lower end of the quantum spectrum was less than 1% for the first 300 levels obtained from 1600×1600 dimensional matrices.
- For the isotropic harmonic oscillator (b=1, λ=0), levels are 15 MeV apart.
- For the superdeformed prolate case (b=2), new shell structures emerge near λ=0.5λc and λ=0.65λc.

## Context
This work extends studies of chaotic motion in deformed potentials, particularly the analysis of Arvieu (1987) on quadrupole-deformed cavities and the investigation by Blocki (1993) on cavities with oscillating walls of higher-order multipoles. The oblate/octupole case had not been dealt with in Blocki (1993). The results agree with a prediction of octupole instability of super- and hyperdeformed nuclei by Abrosimov (1990) based on realistic calculations. The paper enables further investigation of whether stability against chaos can be associated with the observed prevalence of prolate over oblate nuclei, and suggests that prolate nuclei should behave elastically while oblate nuclei show more dissipative behavior.
