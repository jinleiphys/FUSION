# Strange hadron matter and SU(3) symmetry

> We calculate saturation curves for strange hadron matter using recently constructed baryon-baryon potentials which are constrained by SU(3) symmetry. All possible interaction channels within the baryon octet (consisting of $N$, $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Ξ$) are considered. It is found that a small $Λ$ fraction in nuclear matter slightly increases binding, but that larger fractions ($>10%$) rapidly cause a decrease. Charge-neutral ${N,Λ,Ξ}$ systems, with equal densities for nucleons and cascades, are only very weakly bound. The dependence of the binding energies on the strangeness per baryon, $f_s$, is predicted for various ${N,Λ,Ξ}$ and ${N,Λ,Σ,Ξ}$ systems. The implications of our results in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the core of a dense star are discussed. We also discuss the differences between our results and previous hadron matter calculations.

# Strange hadron matter and SU(3) symmetry

## Key claim

Using baryon-baryon potentials constrained by SU(3) symmetry, the authors find that adding a small fraction of Λ hyperons to nuclear matter slightly increases binding, but fractions above about 10% rapidly decrease binding; charge-neutral {N,Λ,Ξ} systems with equal nucleon and cascade densities are only very weakly bound. The results depend on the choice of NSC97 potential model (e or f) and on the use of the "gap" choice for single-particle energies rather than the "continuous" choice, which would give more binding.

## Method

The authors perform Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the NSC97 baryon-baryon potential models, which are fitted to NN and YN scattering data and extended via SU(3) symmetry to all baryon-octet channels (S=0 to S=-4). The Bethe-Goldstone equation is solved self-consistently for the G-matrix with coupled channels including ΛΛ, ΛΣ, ΣΣ, and ΞN interactions, using a nonrelativistic expansion and the "gap" (standard) choice for the single-particle potential, with a quadratic momentum-dependence approximation for the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Pure nuclear matter saturation: approximately 16 MeV binding per baryon at density around 0.28 fm⁻³ (NSC97 models, gap choice)
- Most stable {N,Λ} system: E/A_B = -13.2 MeV at ρ = 0.28 fm⁻³ with χ_Λ = 0.1 (NSC97e); E/A_B = -12.7 MeV at ρ = 0.27 fm⁻³ with χ_Λ = 0.05 (NSC97f)
- Pure Λ system: unbound for both NSC97e and NSC97f models
- Pure Ξ system: more bound than nucleon system, saturates at higher density
- {N,Λ,Ξ} systems: only bound for nucleon fractions larger than 40%; become unbound when χ_N ≲ 0.4
- Threshold energy for Λ+Λ → Ξ+N reaction: approximately 28 MeV
- Q-values for strong transitions: ΣN → ΛN about 78 MeV, ΣΣ → ΛΛ about 156 MeV, ΣΛ → ΞN about 50 MeV, ΣΞ → ΛΞ about 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Schulze et al. (1998), who considered only {N,Λ} systems using the Nijmegen soft-core YN potential and found a most stable system at E/A_B = -16.0 MeV, f_s ≈ 0.06, and ρ ≈ 0.23 fm⁻³. The present calculation improves upon that work by including all baryon-octet channels (ΛΛ, ΛΣ, ΣΣ, ΞN, ΞΛ, ΞΣ, ΞΞ) as required by SU(3) symmetry, and by using the newly constructed NSC97 potentials. The results contradict relativistic mean-field calculations by Schaffner et al. (1993, 1994) and Gal et al. (1995), which found stronger binding for {N,Λ,Ξ} systems; the authors attribute this difference to two-body correlations neglected in mean-field models. The work enables future investigations of three-body terms in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach and more precise predictions for strange matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and neutron star cores.
