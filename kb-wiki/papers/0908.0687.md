# Neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei

> All available theoretical estimates of neutrino-induced coherent pion production rely on the 'local approximation' for the Delta propagator. The validity of this approximation is scrutinized. It is found that the local approximation overestimates the neutrino-induced coherent pion production on nuclei significantly, by up to 100%.

# Neutrino-induced coherent pion production off nuclei

## Key claim

The local approximation for the Delta propagator, used in all previous theoretical estimates of neutrino-induced coherent pion production, overestimates the cross section significantly, by up to 100% in the neutrino energy regime relevant to present experiments such as MiniBooNE and T2K, and the error is larger at lower neutrino energies.

## Method

The authors computed coherent pion production off a carbon-12 target using a model where pions are produced dominantly via the Delta(1232) resonance, summing the hadronic current over all occupied single-particle states in a Walecka-type relativistic mean field model. They compared this full calculation, which retains the momentum-dependent Delta propagator inside the nuclear integral, against the local approximation in which the Delta momentum is fixed to a specific value so that the propagator factorizes out of the integral. Both calculations were performed in the plane wave approximation without pion final state interactions and without in-medium changes to the Delta spectral function, using the same nuclear structure model.

## Key numbers

- At neutrino energy 500 MeV, the local approximation overestimates the full calculation by a factor of approximately 1.7 at zero degrees in the angular distribution.
- At neutrino energy 1000 MeV, the local approximation gives a cross section for very forward angles that is about 20% larger than the full calculation.
- For the pion momentum distribution at 500 MeV (charged-current), the local approximation overestimates the full result by a factor of about 2.5 at the peak.
- The local approximation overestimates coherent pion production by up to 100% in the neutrino energy regime relevant to MiniBooNE and T2K.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach to coherent pion production by scrutinizing the local approximation used in all prior theoretical models, including both PCAC-based models and nuclear structure models such as those by Singh (2006), Alvarez-Ruso (2007), Amaro (2008), Berger (2008), and Paschos (2009). The results show that the local approximation fails significantly, and the authors note that Nakamura and collaborators have shown the same failure persists when pion final state interactions are included. This finding implies that experimental analyses of coherent pion production at MiniBooNE and T2K, which rely on models using the local approximation, may need revision.
