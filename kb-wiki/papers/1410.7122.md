# Subbarrier fusion of carbon isotopes: from resonance structure to fusion oscillations

> At energies below the Coulomb barrier, the fusion excitation function for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system shows prominent fine structures, whereas that for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system behaves more smoothly as a function of energy. We demonstrate that these different behaviors can be simultaneously reproduced using an optical potential in which the strength of the imaginary part is proportional to the level density of each compound nucleus. We also discuss the oscillatory behavior of fusion excitation function for these systems observed at energies above the Coulomb barrier from a view point of quantum mechanical systems with identical particles.

## Key claim

The different resonance behaviors of the fusion excitation functions for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C systems below the Coulomb barrier can be simultaneously reproduced using an optical potential whose imaginary part is proportional to the level density of each compound nucleus, supporting the conjecture of Jiang et al. (2013) that the properties of the compound nucleus $^{24}$Mg are responsible for the resonance structure.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C systems, including excitations of both projectile and target nuclei up to the first member of the ground state rotational band (including mutual excitation channels). The imaginary part of the optical potential was taken proportional to the level density of the compound nucleus, with the same overall strength $w_0$ for both systems, and the coupled-channels equations were solved with a variational method. For the above-barrier analysis of the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system, single-channel calculations with a parity-dependent potential were used to mock up the effect of elastic neutron transfer.

## Key numbers

- The modified astrophysical $S$-factor for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C shows resonance peaks below the Coulomb barrier, while the $S$-factor for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C behaves smoothly; the calculations qualitatively reproduce this system dependence.
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system above the barrier, the penetrability for $l=14$ was reduced by a factor of 2 and all higher partial waves set to zero to account for the observed decrease of fusion cross sections above 25 MeV.
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system, a parity-dependent potential with depth parameter $V=(1+(-1)^l\epsilon)V_0$ gave a good fit to experimental data with $\epsilon=-0.15$ (shallower potential for even partial waves).
- The oscillatory part of fusion cross sections is given by a formula showing that the oscillation amplitude decreases exponentially for heavy systems (large reduced mass $\mu$), making oscillations observable in light systems like $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jiang et al. (2013), who explained the average behavior of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion cross sections using level density arguments, by implementing the level-density-dependent imaginary potential into coupled-channels calculations following the method of Kondo (1978). The above-barrier fusion oscillations are interpreted as due to the addition of successive partial waves, as advocated by Poffe (1983) and Kabir (1988), with the identical-particle nature of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C enhancing the oscillations. The parity-dependent potential treatment for $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C follows the approach used by Kabir (1988) for the $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O system with alpha transfer, and the sign of the parity dependence is consistent with a rule from Baye (1977, 1986) based on the resonating group method. Future work should include additional channels such as the first 3$^-$ state, the Hoyle state, and the alpha transfer channel to improve agreement at deep subbarrier energies.
