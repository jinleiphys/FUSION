# Probing the antikaon potential by K$^-$A elastic scattering

> We extract the antikaon potential from differential data on elastic $K^-A$ scattering within Glauber theory at an antikaon momentum of 800 MeV/c. The $K^-N$ cross section at densities up to 0.1 $fm^3$ is found to be close to the value given in free space $K^-p$ and $K^-n$ interactions (averaged over the number of protons and neutrons) whereas the ratio $α$ of the real to imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude $f(0)$ differs from the ratio $α$ in free space indicating a dropping of $Re f(0)$ at finite nuclear density. The extrapolated $K^-$ potential at a momentum of 0.8 GeV/c amounts to $\approx $--28 MeV at density $ρ_0$ which is substantially less than the values extracted from kaonic atoms at zero momentum or heavy-ion data at 300 MeV/c${\leq}p_K{\leq}$600 MeV/c. We, furthermore, investigate the perspectives of scattering experiments on $^{12}C$ at low ($\approx$300 MeV/c) and high ($\approx$1.2 GeV/c) momenta.

## Key claim

The antikaon potential extracted from elastic K⁻A scattering at 800 MeV/c is substantially less attractive (≈ –28 MeV at normal nuclear density) than the values obtained from kaonic atoms at zero momentum or from heavy-ion data at 300–600 MeV/c, but the confidence level of this result is below 10%, leaving it as an indication rather than a final conclusion.

## Method

The authors analyzed differential cross section data for elastic K⁻ scattering on ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca at 800 MeV/c using Glauber theory with Coulomb corrections. They performed χ² minimization with the Minuit procedure to extract the in-medium K⁻N total cross section σ and the ratio α of the real to imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude f(0), then compared these to free-space values obtained from dispersion relations and experimental K⁻p and K⁻n data.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated K⁻ potential at momentum 0.8 GeV/c and density ρ₀: ≈ –28 MeV
- K⁻N total cross section extracted from ¹²C data: 37.97 ± 0.01 mb
- K⁻N total cross section extracted from ⁴⁰Ca data: 38.3 ± 0.2 mb
- Ratio α extracted from ¹²C data: 0.315 ± 0.001
- Ratio α extracted from ⁴⁰Ca data: 0.396 ± 0.003
- Free-space averaged α (for comparison): 0.608
- Free-space averaged σ (for comparison): 36.12 mb
- Maximum nuclear density probed by K⁻ elastic scattering on ¹²C: ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³ (compared to saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)
- Reduced χ²/N for fit to ¹²C data: 1.4; for ⁴⁰Ca data: 1.7
- Reduced χ²/N using free-space parameters for ¹²C: 5.1; for ⁴⁰Ca: 4.3

## Context

This work extends the debate on the momentum dependence of the antikaon potential, where kaonic atom analyses (Gal, 1990s) suggest a potential of ≈ –180 MeV at normal density for stopped kaons, while heavy-ion collision data (Li, Cassing, 1990s) indicate a potential of –80 to –120 MeV at momenta 300–600 MeV/c. The results at 800 MeV/c support the idea that the potential becomes less attractive with increasing momentum, and the authors propose that future elastic scattering experiments at low (≈300 MeV/c) and high (≈1.2 GeV/c) momenta could test whether the real part of the K⁻n forward amplitude changes sign and probe the in-medium modification of the Λ(1405) resonance.
