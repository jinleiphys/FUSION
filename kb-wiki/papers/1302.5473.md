# P-shell nuclei using Similarity Renormalization Group evolved three-nucleon interactions

> The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) is used to soften interactions for ab initio nuclear structure calculations by decoupling low- and high-energy Hamiltonian matrix elements. The substantial contribution of both initial and SRG-induced three-nucleon forces requires their consistent evolution in a three-particle basis space before applying them to larger nuclei. While in principle the evolved Hamiltonians are unitarily equivalent, in practice the need for basis truncation introduces deviations, which must be monitored. Here we present benchmark no-core full configuration calculations with SRG-evolved interactions in p-shell nuclei over a wide range of softening. These calculations are used to assess convergence properties, extrapolation techniques, and the dependence of energies, including four-body contributions, on the SRG resolution scale.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of nuclear Hamiltonians, when including both initial and induced three-nucleon forces, yields nearly converged ground-state energies for p-shell nuclei at low resolution scales (lambda around 1.5 to 1.0 inverse fm), but the omission of induced four-body forces causes residual deviations from unitary equivalence that must be monitored, particularly for larger nuclei and higher lambda values.

## Method

The authors performed no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations using SRG-evolved Hamiltonians derived from chiral effective field theory potentials at N3LO (500 MeV cutoff) for two-nucleon forces and N2LO for three-nucleon forces, with evolution carried out in Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator bases for A=2 and A=3 subsystems. Ground-state energies were computed for p-shell nuclei (6Li, 7Li, 7Be, 8Be, 10B, 12C) across a range of SRG resolution scales (lambda from 2.5 to 1.0 inverse fm) and harmonic oscillator basis sizes (Nmax from 2 to 8 or 10), with exponential extrapolation to infinite basis size using both empirical and EFT-motivated methods.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 4He at lambda = 1.0 inverse fm shows only about 300 keV deviation from unitary equivalence when including induced three-nucleon forces, compared to about 3 MeV variation for NN-only calculations
- Three-body evolution basis truncation (N_A3max = 40) is sufficient for convergence only at harmonic oscillator energies hw >= 18 MeV, with systematic underbinding below this value
- For 7Li, 10B, and 12C at lambda = 1.5 and 1.0 inverse fm, extrapolated ground-state energies are consistent across different hw values within small error bars, indicating near-convergence
- The variational minimum in hw systematically shifts to lower values as lambda decreases, with NN+NNN-induced calculations showing minima at lower hw than NN+NNN calculations

## Context

This work extends previous SRG studies of two-nucleon forces in p-shell nuclei (Bogner 2007) and three-nucleon force evolution in light nuclei (Jurgenson 2009, 2010) to a wider set of p-shell systems including 7Li, 7Be, 8Be, 10B, and 12C. The results enable systematic assessment of convergence properties and induced many-body force hierarchies for SRG-evolved interactions, and provide benchmarks for comparison with Importance Truncated No-Core Shell Model calculations (Roth 2011). The findings establish practical limits for SRG evolution in ab initio nuclear structure calculations and identify the need for consistent treatment of four-body forces at very low resolution scales.
