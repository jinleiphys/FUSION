# Nucleon Compton scattering in the Dyson-Schwinger approach

> We analyze the nucleon's Compton scattering amplitude in the Dyson-Schwinger/Faddeev approach. We calculate a subset of diagrams that implements the nonperturbative handbag contribution as well as all t-channel resonances. At the quark level, these ingredients are represented by the quark Compton vertex whose analytic properties we study in detail. We derive a general form for a fermion two-photon vertex that is consistent with its Ward-Takahashi identities and free of kinematic singularities, and we relate its transverse part to the onshell nucleon Compton amplitude. We solve an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark Compton vertex in rainbow-ladder truncation and implement it in the nucleon Compton scattering amplitude. The remaining ingredients are the dressed quark propagator and the nucleon's bound-state amplitude which are consistently solved from Dyson-Schwinger and covariant Faddeev equations. We verify numerically that the resulting quark Compton vertex and nucleon Compton amplitude both reproduce the pion-photon transition form factor when the pion pole in the t-channel is approached.

## Key claim
The paper presents a Dyson-Schwinger/Faddeev framework for the nucleon Compton scattering amplitude that implements the nonperturbative handbag contribution and all t-channel resonances, but the authors explicitly note that electromagnetic gauge invariance cannot be realized with the subset of diagrams they calculate, limiting information on low-energy behavior and generalized polarizabilities in these initial studies.

## Method
The authors solve an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark Compton vertex in rainbow-ladder truncation and implement it in the nucleon Compton scattering amplitude. The dressed quark propagator and nucleon bound-state amplitude are consistently solved from Dyson-Schwinger and covariant Faddeev equations. The calculation focuses exclusively on the handbag and t-channel structure, ignoring the three-body scattering matrix and cat's-ears-type diagrams.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon mass M is used as the scale for dimensionless variables, with the momentum transfer variable defined as t = Δ²/(4M²)
- The average photon momentum variable is defined as σ = Σ²/M² = tX, where X is a dimensionless radial variable
- The nucleon onshell condition gives P² = -M² - Δ²/4, with P·Δ = 0
- The forward scattering limit corresponds to t = 0

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev framework previously applied to baryon masses (Eichmann 2009), electromagnetic form factors (Eichmann 2011), and the general formalism for two-current couplings to the nucleon (Eichmann 2011) to the specific case of Compton scattering. The approach contrasts with effective field theory methods (Bernard 1993, Hemmert 1997, Pascalutsa 2002) and dispersion relation approaches (Drechsel 2002, Pasquini 2011) by working directly with quark and gluon degrees of freedom. The results enable future calculations that include the full three-body scattering matrix and cat's-ears diagrams to achieve electromagnetic gauge invariance and access nucleon polarizabilities.
