# Heavy-ion double-charge-exchange and its relation to neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We introduce the formalism to describe heavy-ion Double-Charge-Exchange (DCE) processes in the eikonal approximation. We focus on the low-momentum-transfer limit -- corresponding to the differential cross section at $θ=0^\circ$ -- and, for the first time, we show that it is possible to factorize the DCE cross-section in terms of reaction and nuclear parts. Whereas in the $θ\neq0^\circ$ case the nuclear part is a convolution of the beam and target nuclear matrix elements (NMEs), for $θ=0^\circ$ we demonstrate - for the first time- that the transition matrix elements can be written as the sum of Double-Gamow-Teller (DGT) and Double Fermi (DF) type parts, and that they can both be further factorized in terms of target and projectile NMEs. By making use of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) formalism, we also show that the DGT and total parts of the neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs are in linear correlation with DCE-DGT NMEs. This confirms the hypothesis of a linear correlation between them, as introduced in [Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ {\bf 120}, 142502 (2018)]. The possibility of a Two-Step Factorization (TSF) of the very forward differential DCE-cross-section and the emergence of DGT and DF types for the DCE nuclear matrix elements, combined with a linear correlation between DCE-DGT and 0$νββ$ NMEs, opens the possibility of placing an upper limit on neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs in terms of the DCE experimental data at $θ=0^\circ$.

# Heavy-ion double-charge-exchange and its relation to neutrinoless double-beta decay

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that the heavy-ion double-charge-exchange (DCE) differential cross-section at $\theta=0^\circ$ can be factorized into a reaction part and a nuclear part, where the nuclear part separates into Double-Gamow-Teller (DGT) and Double Fermi (DF) components that are each further factorized into target and projectile nuclear matrix elements. This factorization, combined with a linear correlation between DCE-DGT and neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$) nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) found using the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), opens the possibility of placing an upper limit on $0\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs from DCE experimental data, though the slope of this correlation depends on the choice of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_{\rm A}$.

## Method

The authors derive a DCE effective potential in the closure approximation, combining one-pion-exchange and zero-range contact interactions, and compute the DCE cross-section using the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) within the eikonal approximation. They focus on the low-momentum-transfer limit ($\theta=0^\circ$) where the contact interaction dominates, allowing factorization of the transition amplitude into DGT and DF matrix elements for projectile and target nuclei. Nuclear matrix elements are calculated using the generalized seniority approximation and the microscopic Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2) formalism for several candidate $0\nu\beta\beta$ nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Calculated $^{40}$Ca($^{18}$O, $^{18}$Ne)$^{40}$Ar DCE cross-section at $\theta=0^\circ$: 8.9 $\mu$b/sr, in agreement with experimental data range 8.0–10.5 $\mu$b/sr
- DCE-DGT projectile matrix element for $^{18}$O $\rightarrow^{18}$Ne: 0.60 fm$^{-1}$; DCE-DF projectile matrix element: 0.24 fm$^{-1}$
- DCE-DGT target matrix element for $^{40}$Ca $\rightarrow^{40}$Ar: 0.27 fm$^{-1}$; DCE-DF target matrix element: 0.11 fm$^{-1}$
- Ratio ${\mathcal M}_{\rm DCE}^{\rm P,DF}{\mathcal M}_{\rm DCE}^{\rm T,DF}/{\mathcal M}_{\rm DCE}^{\rm P,DGT}{\mathcal M}_{\rm DCE}^{\rm T,DGT}$ for $^{40}$Ca($^{18}$O, $^{18}$Ne)$^{40}$Ar: 0.15
- DCE-DGT target matrix elements for $0\nu\beta\beta$ candidate nuclei: $^{116}$Cd $\rightarrow$ $^{116}$Sn: 0.20 fm$^{-1}$; $^{82}$Se $\rightarrow$ $^{82}$Kr: 0.28 fm$^{-1}$; $^{128}$Te $\rightarrow$ $^{128}$Xe: 0.27 fm$^{-1}$; $^{76}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{76}$Se: 0.34 fm$^{-1}$
- DF contribution to the $^{40}$Ca($^{18}$O, $^{18}$Ne)$^{40}$Ar cross-section: 26% correction (6.6 $\mu$b/sr without DF vs 8.9 $\mu$b/sr with DF)
- Closure energies used: $\bar E_{\rm P}=3.38$ MeV (projectile), $\bar E_{\rm T}=5.28$ MeV (target), for mass numbers $A=40$ and $a=18$
- Contact interaction parameters: $c_{\rm GT} = 217$ MeV fm$^3$, $c_{\rm T} = 151$ MeV fm$^3$
- Pion coupling constant: $f_{\pi}^{2}/4\pi = 0.08$, pion mass $m_{\pi} = 145$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism of single-charge-exchange (SCE) reactions, where factorization of the cross-section into reaction and nuclear parts is well established (Taddeucci, Lenske 2018), to heavy-ion double-charge-exchange processes for the first time. The linear correlation hypothesis between DCE-DGT and $0\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs was introduced by Shimizu et al. (2018) in the context of Double-Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance studies. The paper builds on ongoing DCE experiments at RNCP Osaka, RIBF RIKEN, and the NUMEN collaboration at LNS-INFN (Cappuzzello 2018), and uses the IBM-2 calculations of Barea et al. (2013) for comparison of $0\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs. The results enable future extraction of $0\nu\beta\beta$ NME constraints from DCE experimental data at $\theta=0^\circ$, provided the effective value of $g_{\rm A}$ at the relevant energy scale (~100 MeV) can be better constrained through studies of $\beta$ decays, $2\nu\beta\beta$ decays, and muon capture.
