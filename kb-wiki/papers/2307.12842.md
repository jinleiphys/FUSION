# Possibility of generating the $^3_{Λ_c}$H in the quark-delocalization color-screening model

> We probe the existence of the $^3_{Λ_c}$H where the $NΛ_c$ potentials are derived from the quark-delocalization color-screening model (QDCSM). The $NΛ_c$ system is studied and the $NΛ_c$ scattering length so as the effective range are obtained in the QDCSM. We construct effective Gaussian-type $NΛ_c$ potentials which reproduce the $NΛ_c$ scattering data given by the QDCSM. By solving the $NNΛ_c$ three body Schrödinger equation with the Gaussian expansion method, we calculate the energies of the $^3_{Λ_c}$H with isospin $I=0$, $J^P=1/2^+$ and $I=0$, $J^P=3/2^+$ under different color screening parameter $μ$. The $J^P=1/2^+$ and $J^P=3/2^+$ states are both bound when the color screening parameter $μ$ is set to 1.0 or 1.2, where the $J^P=1/2^+$ state is bound by $0.08\sim0.85$ MeV and the $J^P=3/2^+$ state is bound by $0.15\sim1.31$ MeV with respect to the deuteron-$Λ_c$ threshold.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a charmed hypertriton ($^3_{\Lambda_c}$H) can exist as a bound state, but only when the color screening parameter $\mu$ of the quark-delocalization color-screening model is set to 1.0 or 1.2; no bound state is found for $\mu = 0.8$, and the binding energies are small (0.08–1.31 MeV relative to the deuteron-$\Lambda_c$ threshold), making the existence conditional on the model parameter.

## Method

The authors first derived $N\Lambda_c$ potentials from the quark-delocalization color-screening model (QDCSM) by normalizing coupled-channel effects (including $\Sigma_c$, $\Sigma_c^*$, and D-wave components) into S-wave $N\Lambda_c$ channels for $J^P=0^+$ and $J^P=1^+$. They then constructed effective Gaussian-type $N\Lambda_c$ potentials that reproduce the QDCSM scattering lengths and effective ranges. Using these effective potentials together with the AV8' realistic $NN$ interaction, they solved the $NN\Lambda_c$ three-body Schrödinger equation with the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to calculate binding energies for $J^P=1/2^+$ and $J^P=3/2^+$ states under different color screening parameters $\mu$.

## Key numbers

- $N\Lambda_c$ scattering length for $J^P=0^+$: $-4.88$ fm (for $\mu=1.0$), $-2.58$ fm (for $\mu=1.2$), $-1.57$ fm (for $\mu=0.8$)
- $N\Lambda_c$ scattering length for $J^P=1^+$: $-7.11$ fm (for $\mu=1.0$), $-2.88$ fm (for $\mu=1.2$), $-1.92$ fm (for $\mu=0.8$)
- $N\Lambda_c$ effective range for $J^P=0^+$: 3.62 fm (for $\mu=1.0$), 3.65 fm (for $\mu=1.2$), 5.06 fm (for $\mu=0.8$)
- $N\Lambda_c$ effective range for $J^P=1^+$: 3.15 fm (for $\mu=1.0$), 3.50 fm (for $\mu=1.2$), 3.70 fm (for $\mu=0.8$)
- $\Lambda_c$ separation energy for $J^P=1/2^+$ state: 0.85 MeV (for $\mu=1.0$), 0.08 MeV (for $\mu=1.2$)
- $\Lambda_c$ separation energy for $J^P=3/2^+$ state: 1.31 MeV (for $\mu=1.0$), 0.15 MeV (for $\mu=1.2$)
- Energy splitting between $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ states: 0.46 MeV (for $\mu=1.0$), 0.07 MeV (for $\mu=1.2$)
- The $d+\Lambda_c$ threshold is at $E = -2.24$ MeV relative to the $NN\Lambda_c$ three-body breakup threshold

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmed hypernuclei using the QDCSM, where earlier work by Huang et al. (2013) found an attractive $\Lambda_cN$ interaction but no $\Lambda_cN$ bound state and did not investigate the three-body system. The results are compared with two prior calculations: Maeda et al. (2015) found much larger binding energies (18.23–18.89 MeV) using one-meson exchange and quark cluster models, while Garcilazo et al. (2015) obtained binding energies of 0.14–0.75 MeV for the $J^P=3/2^+$ state using a chiral quark model, which is consistent with the present results. The paper enables future experimental searches for charmed hypernuclei at facilities such as GSI-FAIR, J-PARC, or HIAF, and suggests that the $\Lambda_c$ in the charmed hypertriton is more compact than the $\Lambda$ in the ordinary hypertriton despite similar binding energies.
