# On the critical examination of the "field-theoretical approach" to the neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei

> We briefly outline the models of the $n\bar{n}$ transition in nuclei and analyse a well known calculation based on diagram technique published in 1992 and repeated recently by V. Kopeliovich and I. Potashnikova.

# On the critical examination of the "field-theoretical approach" to the neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the diagram-technique model for neutron-antineutron (n\(\bar{n}\)) transitions in nuclei (model 1, published in 1992 and repeated by Kopeliovich and Potashnikova) is unsuitable for the problem because it does not reproduce n\(\bar{n}\) transitions in medium or vacuum, contains no infrared singularity, and introduces an artificial vertex for virtual nuclear decay that has no corresponding term in the interaction Hamiltonian. The alternative field-theoretical model (model 2) using the evolution operator on a finite time interval is presented as the correct formulation.

## Method

The author compares two models for n\(\bar{n}\) transitions in nuclei followed by annihilation. Model 1 uses diagram technique for direct reactions with a neutron propagator arising from a virtual decay vertex A → n + (A-1), formulated in momentum representation. Model 2 uses a field-theoretical approach where the initial state is an eigenfunction of the unperturbed Hamiltonian (plane wave for medium, bound state wave function for nuclei) and the matrix element of the evolution operator U(t,0) is calculated on a finite time interval, with the antineutron described by a Green function rather than a propagator.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on free-space n\(\bar{n}\) oscillation time from model 2 with bare antineutron propagator: \(\tau^b = 10^{16}\) yr, interpreted as an estimation from above.
- Lower limit on free-space n\(\bar{n}\) oscillation time from model 2 with dressed antineutron propagator: \(\tau^d = 1.2 \times 10^{9}\) s, interpreted as a conservative lower limit.
- For model 1 applied to deuteron, the process probability is \(W_1(t) = \Gamma_{d\rightarrow mesons} t\) with \(\Gamma_{d\rightarrow mesons} = \frac{\epsilon^2}{6E_n^2} \Gamma_{\bar{n}p}\), where \(E_n\) is the pole neutron binding energy and \(\Gamma_{\bar{n}p}\) is the \(\bar{n}p\) annihilation width.
- The range of uncertainty for the free-space oscillation time when antineutron self-energy \(\Sigma\) varies between 0 and 10 MeV is: \(10^{16}\) yr \(> \tau > 1.2 \times 10^{9}\) s.
- The lower limit \(\tau^d = 1.2 \times 10^{9}\) s exceeds the Grenoble reactor experiment limit by a factor of 14 and the potential model lower limit by a factor of 5.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous publications (Nazaruk, 1996-2008) proposing the field-theoretical model (model 2) for n\(\bar{n}\) transitions in medium and nuclei, and directly contradicts the diagram-technique calculation of Kopeliovich and Potashnikova (2011) which repeated the earlier model of Nazaruk (1992). The paper asserts that model 1 is inapplicable because it is infrared-free by construction (virtual particle propagators cannot produce infrared singularities) and cannot describe the coordinate-dependence or density-dependence essential for nuclear processes. The analysis enables future work by clarifying that the large uncertainty range (from \(1.2 \times 10^9\) s to \(10^{16}\) yr) for the free-space oscillation time requires further theoretical and experimental investigation.
