# Radiative pion capture by a nucleon

> The differential cross sections for $π^- p \to γn$ and $π^+ n \to γp$ are computed up to $O(p^3)$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). The expressions at $O(p)$ and $O(p^2)$ have no free parameters. There are three unknown parameters at $O(p^3)$, low energy constants of the HBChPT Lagrangian, which are determined by fitting to experimental data. Two acceptable fits are obtained, which can be separated by comparing with earlier dispersion relation calculations of the inverse process. Expressions for the multipoles, with emphasis on the p-wave multipoles, are obtained and evaluated at threshold. Generally the results obtained from the best of the two fits are in good agreement with the dispersion relation predictions.

# Radiative pion capture by a nucleon

## Key claim

The differential cross sections for charged-pion radiative capture on a nucleon can be computed up to O(p^3) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with three free parameters, but two distinct parameter sets (fits A and B) give equally good fits to the cross section data; only comparison with dispersion relation calculations of the inverse photoproduction process distinguishes them, strongly favoring fit A.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for the photoproduction direction (gamma N -> pi N) up to O(p^3) in HBChPT using tree-level and one-pion-loop diagrams, with the photon emitted from the initial nucleon, final nucleon, pion, or the pi-N-N vertex. The three unknown low-energy constants (LECs) b10, b21^r(mu), and b22^r(mu) are determined by least-squares fits to 35 experimental measurements: 11 differential cross sections for pi- p -> gamma n at T_pi = 9.88, 14.62, and 19.85 MeV from a recent TRIUMF experiment, 16 measurements at T_pi = 27.4 and 39.3 MeV from an earlier TRIUMF experiment, and 8 measurements of the inverse reaction gamma p -> n pi+ near threshold (T_pi ~ 3 MeV). The s- and p-wave multipoles are then extracted from the fitted amplitudes and evaluated at threshold.

## Key numbers

- Two acceptable fits (A and B) are obtained; fit A(35) uses all 35 data points and gives b10, b21^r, b22^r values that produce multipoles agreeing with dispersion theory, while fit B(35) is ruled out by comparison with dispersion relation results.
- The O(p) and O(p^2) calculations are parameter-free; O(p^3) terms are necessary for a good fit to the data.
- At threshold, the electric multipole E0+ shows good agreement between HBChPT and dispersion theory for both pi+ and pi- cases; the magnetic multipoles m1+ and m1- differ dramatically between fits A(35) and B(35) due to their dependence on b10.
- The multipole e1+ is constant at threshold, depending only on b19 which was fixed from muon capture.
- Four parameter-free relations among the eight observable multipoles exist at O(p^3); these combinations show good convergence and agree with dispersion relation predictions of Hanstein, Drechsel, and Tiator.
- The O(p^2) and O(p^3) contributions are comparable in magnitude at most angles, raising concern about convergence of the chiral expansion at O(p^3).

## Context

This work extends the HBChPT treatment of radiative pion capture beyond the s-wave threshold calculation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s) by explicitly computing the p-wave multipoles for the first time. It provides a comparison with the dispersion theoretical analysis of s- and p-waves by Hanstein, Drechsel, and Tiator (late 1990s), finding good agreement for the preferred fit A(35). The determined LECs (b10, b21^r, b22^r) are made available for future studies of other reactions, particularly radiative muon capture by a nucleon, where existing experimental data disagree with theoretical expectations. The work also suggests that extending the calculation to O(p^4) or including explicit Delta(1232) fields could improve convergence, especially for the magnetic multipoles.
