# Space- and time-like electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon

> Recent experimental data on space- and time-like form factors of the nucleon are analyzed in terms of a two-component model with a quark-like intrinsics structure and a meson cloud. A good overall agreement is found for all electromagnetic form factors with the exception of the neutron magnetic form factor in the time-like region.

## Key claim

A two-component model with a quark-like intrinsic structure and a meson cloud, modified from Iachello, Jackson and Lande (1973) by including an intrinsic component in the isovector Pauli form factor, achieves good overall agreement with all space- and time-like nucleon electromagnetic form factors except the neutron magnetic form factor in the time-like region, where a major disagreement remains.

## Method

The analysis uses a vector meson dominance (VMD) model in which the photon couples to the nucleon through an intrinsic three-quark structure and a meson cloud mediated by \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\varphi\) mesons. The isoscalar and isovector Dirac and Pauli form factors are parametrized with a dipole intrinsic form factor \(g(Q^2) = (1+\gamma Q^2)^{-2}\), and the \(\rho\) meson width is treated via the Frazer-Fulco prescription. The five coupling parameters \(\beta_\rho, \beta_\omega, \beta_\varphi, \alpha_\rho, \alpha_\varphi\) and the parameter \(\gamma\) are fitted to a combination of recoil polarization data from Jefferson Lab for the form factor ratios and Rosenbluth separation data from SLAC for the magnetic form factors. Time-like form factors are obtained by analytic continuation of the intrinsic structure with a phase \(\theta\) and of the \(\rho\) meson propagator.

## Key numbers

* Proton magnetic form factor ratio: \(R_p = \mu_p G_{E_p}/G_{M_p} \approx 1 - 0.13 (Q^2 - 0.29)\) from polarization transfer data (Jones, Gayou, 2000).
* Fitted parameters: \(\beta_\rho = 0.512\), \(\beta_\omega = 1.129\), \(\beta_\varphi = -0.263\), \(\alpha_\rho = 2.675\), \(\alpha_\varphi = -0.200\), \(\gamma = 0.515\) (GeV/c)\(^{-2}\).
* Intrinsic structure spatial extent: \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2} \simeq 0.49\) fm (compared to \(\simeq 0.34\) fm in Iachello, Jackson and Lande (1973)).
* Proton charge radius (calculated): \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{E_p} = 0.838\) fm (experiment: \(0.895 \pm 0.018\) fm from Sick).
* Neutron electric form factor slope (calculated): \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{E_n} = -0.100\) fm\(^2\) (experiment: \(-0.115 \pm 0.003\) fm\(^2\) from Kopecky).
* Phase for analytic continuation: \(\theta = 0.397\) rad \(\simeq 22.7^\circ\) (compared to \(\simeq 53^\circ\) in Wan (2000)).
* \(\rho\) meson width: \(\Gamma_\rho = 0.112\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the VMD model of Iachello, Jackson and Lande (1973) by adding an intrinsic component to the isovector Pauli form factor \(F_2^V\), motivated by relativistic constituent quark models (Frank, Salme) and perturbative QCD scaling laws (Belitsky, Brodsky). The model reproduces the linear drop in the proton form factor ratio seen in polarization transfer experiments (Jones, Gayou, 2000) and improves agreement with neutron space-like data compared to the 1973 parametrization, but fails to describe neutron time-like data, confirming inconsistencies noted by Wan (2000) and Hammer. The results enable future tests of time-like polarization observables (BCHH, TG) and motivate new measurements of neutron form factors at higher \(Q^2\) at Jefferson Lab and MIT-Bates.
