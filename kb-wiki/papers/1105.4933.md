# Auxiliary field formalism for dilute fermionic atom gases with tunable interactions

> We develop the auxiliary field formalism corresponding to a dilute system of spin-1/2 fermions. This theory represents the Fermi counterpart of the BEC theory developed recently by F. Cooper et al. [Phys. Rev. Lett. 105, 240402 (2010)] to describe a dilute gas of Bose particles. Assuming tunable interactions, this formalism is appropriate for the study of the crossover from the regime of Bardeen-Cooper-Schriffer (BCS) pairing to the regime of Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) in ultracold fermionic atom gases. We show that when applied to the Fermi case at zero temperature, the leading-order auxiliary field (LOAF) approximation gives the same equations as those obtained in the standard BCS variational picture. At finite temperature, LOAF leads to the theory discussed by by Sa de Melo, Randeria, and Engelbrecht [Phys. Rev. Lett. 71, 3202(1993); Phys. Rev. B 55, 15153(1997)]. As such, LOAF provides a unified framework to study the interacting Fermi gas. The mean-field results discussed here can be systematically improved upon by calculating the one-particle irreducible (1-PI) action corrections, order by order.

## Key claim

The leading-order auxiliary field (LOAF) approximation for dilute fermionic gases with tunable interactions reproduces the standard BCS variational picture at zero temperature and the finite-temperature theory of Sa de Melo, Randeria, and Engelbrecht (1993, 1997), but only for the specific case of equal spin-up and spin-down populations; the formalism is presented as a unified framework that can be systematically improved by calculating one-particle irreducible (1-PI) action corrections order by order.

## Method

The authors start from the grand canonical partition function for spin-1/2 fermions with a contact interaction and rewrite the Lagrangian using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary fields corresponding to the normal densities (two real fields) and anomalous densities (two complex fields). They then derive the effective action in terms of these auxiliary fields and the fermion fields, and evaluate it at the leading-order (LOAF) level, which corresponds to a saddle-point approximation. The resulting equations are solved for uniform systems in thermal equilibrium, with special attention to the case of equal spin populations.

## Key numbers

* The noninteracting Fermi energy is defined as epsilon_F = gamma k_F^2, where gamma = hbar^2/(2m) and k_F is the Fermi momentum related to total density by rho = k_F^3/(3 pi^2).
* The quantum Monte Carlo result for the ground-state energy ratio at unitarity is (epsilon/epsilon_0)_QMC = 0.40(01), from Carlson et al. (2008, 2005, 2004, 2003).
* The BCS variational value of the chemical potential to Fermi energy ratio at unitarity is (mu/epsilon_F)_BCS = 0.59, from Leggett (1980).
* The only independent dimensionless variable in the dilute regime is a k_F, where a is the s-wave scattering length, and the condition for diluteness is |a| >> r_0 and k_F r_0 << 1.

## Context

This work extends the auxiliary field formalism previously developed for dilute Bose gases by Cooper et al. (2010) to the fermionic case, providing a unified theoretical framework for the BCS-BEC crossover in ultracold fermionic atom gases. The zero-temperature LOAF results match the BCS variational approach of Leggett (1980), while the finite-temperature results correspond to the theory of Sa de Melo, Randeria, and Engelbrecht (1993, 1997). The formalism enables systematic improvements beyond mean-field by calculating 1-PI corrections, and is relevant to understanding the unitarity limit, which has implications for the equation of state of neutron matter as discussed by Heiselberg (2000).
