# A Three-Flavor Chiral Effective Model with Four Baryonic Multiplets within the Mirror Assignment

> In the case of three quark flavors, (pseudo)scalar diquarks transform as antiquarks under chiral transformations. We construct four spin-1/2 baryonic multiplets from left- and right-handed quarks as well as left- and right-handed diquarks. The fact that two of these multiplets transform in a "mirror" way allows for chirally invariant mass terms. We then embed these baryonic multiplets into the Lagrangian of the so-called extended Linear Sigma Model, which features (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector mesons, as well as glueballs. Reducing the Lagrangian to the two-flavor case, we obtain four doublets of nucleonic states. These mix to produce four experimentally observed states with definite parity: the positive-parity nucleon $N(939)$ and Roper resonance $N(1440)$, as well as the negative-parity resonances $N(1535)$ and $N(1650)$. We determine the parameters of the nucleonic part of the Lagrangian from a fit to masses and decay properties of the aforementioned states. Studying the limit of vanishing quark condensate, we conclude that $N(939)$ and $N(1535)$, as well as $N(1440)$ and $N(1650)$ form pairs of chiral partners.

## Key claim

The paper constructs four spin-1/2 baryonic multiplets from quarks and diquarks in a three-flavor chiral effective model, where two multiplets transform in a "mirror" way to allow chirally invariant mass terms; after reducing to two flavors, the four resulting nucleonic doublets mix to reproduce the observed positive-parity nucleon N(939) and Roper resonance N(1440) and the negative-parity resonances N(1535) and N(1650), with the claim that N(939) and N(1535) as well as N(1440) and N(1650) form pairs of chiral partners in the limit of vanishing quark condensate.

## Method

The authors constructed four baryonic 3x3 matrix fields from left- and right-handed quarks combined with left- and right-handed (pseudo)scalar diquarks, with two fields (N1, N2) transforming under the naive assignment and two fields (M1, M2) under the mirror assignment from the left. They embedded these baryonic multiplets into the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) Lagrangian for three flavors, which includes (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector mesons, then reduced the Lagrangian to the two-flavor case by setting strange quarks to zero while retaining the strange scalar condensate. The parameters of the nucleonic part of the Lagrangian were determined from a fit to experimental masses and decay properties of the four nucleonic states.

## Key numbers

- The four experimentally observed states reproduced by the model are: N(939) (positive parity), N(1440) (positive parity, Roper resonance), N(1535) (negative parity), and N(1650) (negative parity); masses are given in MeV.
- The scalar meson f0(1370) is identified as the chiral partner of the pion, while the light scalar f0(500) is stated to be a state made from at least four quarks.
- The model predicts that in the limit of vanishing quark condensate, N(939) and N(1535) form a chiral partner pair, and N(1440) and N(1650) form another chiral partner pair.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor mirror-assignment studies of Gallas et al. (2009, 2013) to three quark flavors, enabling future studies of scattering processes involving strange hadrons and the hyperon puzzle in compact stars. The quark-diquark construction for baryons follows the "good" diquark concept of Jaffe (1977) and the approach of the tetraquark mixing model, while the mesonic sector builds on the extended Linear Sigma Model developed by Denis et al. for two, three, and four flavors. The four-multiplet structure was anticipated in the outlook of Gallas et al. (2009).
