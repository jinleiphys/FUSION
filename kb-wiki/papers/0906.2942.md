# Temperature and angular momentum dependence of the quadrupole deformation in sd-shell

> Temperature and angular momentum dependence of the quadrupole deformation is studied in the middle of the sd-shell for 28Si and 27Si isotopes using the spherical shell model approach. The shell model calculations have been performed using the standard USD interaction and the canonical partition function constructed from the calculated eigen-solutions. It is shown that the extracted average quadrupole moments show a transitional behavior as a function of temperature and the inferred transitional temperature is shown to vary with angular-momentum. The quadrupole deformation of the individual eigen-states is also analyzed.

# Temperature and angular momentum dependence of the quadrupole deformation in sd-shell

## Key claim

The average quadrupole moments of 28Si and 27Si show a smeared transitional behavior from deformed to spherical shape as temperature increases, with the inferred transitional temperature varying with angular momentum; however, the results are questionable for temperatures above approximately 2.5 MeV due to the restricted sd-shell configuration space, as fp-shell population is expected at higher temperatures.

## Method

Spherical shell model calculations were performed for 28Si and 27Si using the standard USD interaction, with the canonical partition function constructed from the lowest one-thousand eigen-solutions obtained from diagonalization of the shell model Hamiltonian for each angular momentum. Statistical averages of the quadrupole moment were evaluated using effective charges e_p=1.5 and e_n=0.5, and the harmonic oscillator length parameter was calculated with ħω = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3}.

## Key numbers

- For 28Si at I=2, the average quadrupole moment has a constant value of about 20 efm² up to T=1.2 MeV, then drops and approaches zero at higher temperatures
- The inferred transitional temperature for 28Si at I=2 is T=2.5 MeV, determined as the midpoint between the drop onset at T=1 MeV and the constant behavior resuming at T=4 MeV
- For 28Si at I=4, the drop in quadrupole moment starts at T=0.8 MeV and the transitional point is inferred to be T=2.3 MeV
- For 27Si at I=5/2, the inferred transitional point is approximately T=2.25 MeV
- For 27Si at I=9/2, the transitional point is lowered to T=2 MeV
- For 27Si at I=13/2 and 17/2, the transition occurs at about T=2 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) studies that predicted phase transitions in lighter sd-shell nuclei, such as the HFB calculations for 24Mg by Miller (1991) which gave a transition temperature of T=2.1 MeV, and the finite-temperature HFB and Landau theory studies by Goodman (1984, 1994) and Alhassid (1989) that found critical temperatures up to about 1.85 MeV in rare-earth nuclei. The present canonical shell model approach incorporates fluctuations more accurately than grand-canonical mean-field methods, and the angular-momentum-resolved analysis explains why earlier shell model studies that averaged over all angular-momentum states (Cole, 1989; Goodman, 1989) did not observe a clear transitional behavior in the quadrupole moment. The authors note that shell model Monte Carlo methods (Lang, 1995) would be needed to include fp-shell configurations for reliable results at higher temperatures.
