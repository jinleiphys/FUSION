# Polyakov loop and correlator of Polyakov loops at next-to-next-to-leading order

> We study the Polyakov loop and the correlator of two Polyakov loops at finite temperature in the weak-coupling regime. We calculate the Polyakov loop at order g^4. The calculation of the correlator of two Polyakov loops is performed at distances shorter than the inverse of the temperature and for electric screening masses larger than the Coulomb potential. In this regime, it is accurate up to order g^6. We also evaluate the Polyakov-loop correlator in an effective field theory framework that takes advantage of the hierarchy of energy scales in the problem and makes explicit the bound-state dynamics. In the effective field theory framework, we show that the Polyakov-loop correlator is at leading order in the multipole expansion the sum of a colour-singlet and a colour-octet quark-antiquark correlator, which are gauge invariant, and compute the corresponding colour-singlet and colour-octet free energies.

## Key claim
The Polyakov loop is computed at order \(g^4\), and the Polyakov-loop correlator is computed up to order \(g^6\) for distances \(r\) much smaller than the inverse temperature (\(rT \ll 1\)) and for electric screening masses larger than the Coulomb potential; the result for the Polyakov loop at order \(g^4\) disagrees with the long-accepted result of Gava and Jengo (1981) due to an incorrect treatment of static modes at the Debye-mass scale.

## Method
The authors work in Euclidean space-time using the static gauge, where the Polyakov line simplifies to an exponential of the temporal gauge field. They compute the gluon self-energy at one-loop order, separating contributions from non-static modes (nonzero Matsubara frequencies), static modes (zero Matsubara frequency), and fermion loops, and then use these to evaluate the Polyakov loop expectation value via an expansion of the exponential up to order \(g^4\). For the Polyakov-loop correlator, they perform a perturbative expansion up to order \(g^6\) at short distances, and also rederive the correlator in an effective field theory framework (pNRQCD\(_\mathrm{HTL}\)) that separates the colour-singlet and colour-octet contributions.

## Key numbers
- Polyakov loop at order \(g^4\): \(\langle L_R\rangle = 1 + \frac{C_R \alpha_s}{2} \frac{m_D}{T} + \frac{C_R \alpha_s^2}{2} \left[ C_A \left( \ln \frac{m_D^2}{T^2} + \frac{1}{2} \right) - n_f \ln 2 \right] + \mathcal{O}(g^5)\), where \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{3} (C_A + n_f/2)\).
- Debye mass: \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{3} (C_A + n_f/2)\), with \(C_A = N = 3\).
- Leading-order Polyakov loop contribution from the Debye scale: \(\delta\langle L_R\rangle_{\mathrm{LO}\, m_D} = \frac{C_R \alpha_s}{2} \frac{m_D}{T}\).
- Order-\(g^6\) correction breaking Casimir scaling: \(\delta\langle L_R\rangle = \left(3 C_R^2 - \frac{C_R C_A}{2}\right) \frac{\alpha_s^2}{24} \left( \frac{m_D}{T} \right)^2\).

## Context
This work extends perturbative calculations of the Polyakov loop and its correlator beyond leading order, correcting the earlier result of Gava and Jengo (1981) for the Polyakov loop at order \(g^4\). The Polyakov-loop correlator is computed at NNLO for short distances, a regime not previously addressed beyond LO; the result agrees with the later dimensionally reduced effective field theory analysis of Burnier, Laine, and Vepsalainen (2009). The effective field theory framework (pNRQCD\(_\mathrm{HTL}\)) clarifies the relation between the Polyakov-loop correlator and the in-medium heavy-quark potential, enabling a rigorous definition of colour-singlet and colour-octet free energies.
