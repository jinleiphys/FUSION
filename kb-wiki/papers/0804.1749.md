# Thermodynamics of resonances and blurred particles

> Exact and approximate expressions for thermodynamic characteristics of heated matter, which consists of particles with finite mass-widths, are constructed. They are expressed in terms of Fermi/Bose distributions and spectral functions, rather than in terms of more complicated combinations between real and imaginary parts of the self-energies of different particle species. Therefore thermodynamically consistent approximate treatment of systems of particles with finite mass-widths can be performed, provided spectral functions of particle species are known. Approximation of the free resonance gas at low densities is studied. Simple ansatz for the energy dependence of the spectral function is suggested that allows to fulfill thermodynamical consistency conditions. On examples it is shown that a simple description of dense systems of interacting particle species can be constructed, provided some species can be treated in the quasiparticle approximation and others as particles with widths. The interaction affects quasiparticle contributions, whereas particles with widths can be treated as free. Example is considered of a hot gas of heavy fermions strongly interacting with light bosons, both species with zero chemical potentials. The density of blurred fermions is dramatically increased for high temperatures compared to the standard Boltzmann value. The system consists of boson quasiparticles (with effective masses) interacting with fermion -- antifermion blurs. In thermodynamical values interaction terms partially compensate each other. Thereby, in case of a very strong coupling between species thermodynamical quantities of the system, like the energy, pressure and entropy, prove to be such as for the quasi-ideal gas mixture of quasi-free fermion blurs and quasi-free bosons.

# Thermodynamics of resonances and blurred particles

## Key claim

The paper constructs exact and approximate thermodynamic expressions for heated matter containing particles with finite mass-widths, showing that thermodynamic quantities can be expressed solely in terms of Fermi/Bose distributions and spectral functions rather than more complicated combinations of real and imaginary parts of self-energies. The key hedge is that this thermodynamically consistent treatment works provided spectral functions of particle species are known, and that in the specific example of a hot gas of heavy fermions strongly interacting with light bosons at zero chemical potentials, the interaction terms partially compensate such that the system behaves as a quasi-ideal gas mixture of quasi-free fermion blurs and quasi-free bosons.

## Method

The author derives exact Noether expressions for particle 4-current, energy-momentum tensor, and entropy density for non-relativistic particles, relativistic bosons, and relativistic fermions, expressing all thermodynamic quantities in terms of spectral functions. A simple ansatz for the energy dependence of the spectral function is suggested that satisfies thermodynamic consistency conditions, with the width vanishing at threshold and including a form-factor to correct high-energy behavior. The method is applied to several interacting systems including non-relativistic particles with paired potential, bosonic phi^4 theory, two single-flavor fermions interacting via one-boson exchange, and a hot heavy fermion-light boson system.

## Key numbers

- The rho-meson width at maximum in vacuum is about 150 MeV and the Delta-resonance width is about 120 MeV, used as examples of broad resonances where quasiparticle approximation is rough.
- For the phenomenological spectral function ansatz, the cut-off factor Lambda is taken as approximately 0.7 to 1 GeV with power beta greater than alpha + 1/2 to ensure decrease of width at large energies.
- For the width parametrization, alpha = 1/2 for s-wave resonance and alpha = 3/2 for p-wave resonance, with the width vanishing at threshold requiring alpha > 0.
- The factor xi in the spectral function ansatz is approximately 1 + O(Gamma_0 / m*_f) for m*_f much greater than Gamma_0 and m*_f > s_th.
- The Delta-resonance width in vacuum reaches 115 MeV, cited as an example where the Delta is a broad resonance while nucleons and pions have much smaller widths at not too high temperature.

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym approach within a Phi-derivable framework developed by Ivanov, Knoll, and Voskresensky (2000-2005), who derived exact Noether expressions for particle 4-current and energy-momentum tensor for non-relativistic particles and relativistic bosons with non-derivative couplings. The paper addresses the challenge of constructing simplified but thermodynamically consistent descriptions of heated matter consisting of quasiparticles, resonances, and blurred particles, which is relevant for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies where resonances with masses up to 2 GeV contribute to fireball thermodynamics. The approach enables treatment of systems where some species can be treated as quasiparticles and others as particles with widths, with applications to systems such as Delta-N-pi where the Delta is a broad resonance but nucleons and pions can be treated in the quasiparticle approximation.
