# Test of modified BCS model at finite temperature

> A recently suggested modified BCS (MBCS) model has been studied at finite temperature. We show that this approach does not allow the existence of the normal (non-superfluid) phase at any finite temperature. Other MBCS predictions such as a negative pairing gap, pairing induced by heating in closed-shell nuclei, and ``superfluid -- super-superfluid'' phase transition are discussed also. The MBCS model is tested by comparing with exact solutions for the picket fence model. Here, severe violation of the internal symmetry of the problem is detected. The MBCS equations are found to be inconsistent. The limit of the MBCS applicability has been determined to be far below the ``superfluid -- normal'' phase transition of the conventional FT-BCS, where the model performs worse than the FT-BCS.

## Key claim

The modified BCS (MBCS) model, which was proposed to improve upon conventional finite-temperature BCS (FT-BCS) for finite nuclear systems, is shown to be fundamentally flawed: it does not allow a normal (non-superfluid) phase at any finite temperature, its equations are inconsistent, and it severely violates the particle-hole symmetry of the pairing problem, with its range of applicability limited to temperatures far below the conventional critical temperature where it performs worse than FT-BCS.

## Method

The authors solved the MBCS equations (Eqs. MBCS2) with an accuracy of 10^{-11} for neutrons in Ni isotopes (⁷⁶Ni, ⁸⁴Ni) and for neutrons and protons in ¹²⁰Sn, using both truncated and extended single-particle bases. They then compared MBCS predictions for the pairing gap, excitation energy, specific heat, and spectroscopic factors against exact solutions of the pairing Hamiltonian in the Picket Fence Model (PFM) with N=10 two-fold degenerate levels, 10 particles, and pairing strength G=0.4 MeV, as well as against conventional FT-BCS results.

## Key numbers

- In ⁷⁶Ni (neutrons, truncated basis), the MBCS pairing gap reaches zero at T ≈ 2.1 MeV and becomes negative at higher temperatures.
- In ⁸⁴Ni (neutrons, extended spectrum), the MBCS pairing gap shows a "superfluid – super-superfluid" phase transition at T ≈ 3.3 MeV, with discontinuities in the gap, chemical potential, and excitation energy.
- In ¹²⁰Sn (neutrons, complete basis), the MBCS pairing gap vanishes at T ≈ 5.5 MeV and becomes negative at higher temperatures.
- In ¹²⁰Sn (protons, closed-shell system), the MBCS gap starts from zero at T=0 and develops to -0.73 MeV at T=5 MeV.
- In the PFM (N=10, G=0.4 MeV), the MBCS "superfluid – super-superfluid" phase transition occurs at T ≈ 1.78 MeV.
- The conventional BCS critical temperature relation is given as T_c ≈ 0.57 · Δ_{T=0}.
- In the PFM with T_c = 0.5 MeV, convergence of MBCS results for the gap, chemical potential, and excitation energy is reached at N ≈ 10.

## Context

This work tests the MBCS model proposed by Dang and collaborators (DZ01, DA03a, DA03b, DA03c, DA03d), which was intended to overcome particle number fluctuations in conventional FT-BCS for finite nuclei. The authors demonstrate that MBCS produces unphysical predictions including negative pairing gaps, pairing induced by heating in closed-shell nuclei, and a "superfluid – super-superfluid" phase transition, while severely violating the particle-hole symmetry inherent to the pairing problem. The analysis shows that MBCS is thermodynamically inconsistent and performs worse than conventional FT-BCS even in its narrow range of applicability far below T_c, contradicting the claims of the original MBCS papers.
