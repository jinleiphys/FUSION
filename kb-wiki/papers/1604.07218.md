# Excitation functions of parameters in Erlang distribution, Schwinger mechanism, and Tsallis statistics in RHIC BES program

> Experimental results of the transverse momentum distributions of phi mesons and $Ω$ hyperons produced in gold-gold (Au-Au) collisions with different centrality intervals, measured by the STAR Collaboration at different energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV) in the beam energy scan (BES) program at the relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC), are approximately described by the single Erlang distribution and the two-component Schwinger mechanism. Meanwhile, the STAR experimental transverse momentum distributions of negatively charged particles, produced in Au-Au collisions at RHIC BES energies, are approximately described by the two-component Erlang distribution and the single Tsallis statistics. The excitation functions of free parameters are obtained from the fit to the experimental data. A weak softest point in the string tension in $Ω$ hyperon spectra is observed at 7.7 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum distributions of phi mesons, Omega hyperons, and negatively charged particles in Au-Au collisions at RHIC BES energies (7.7-39 GeV) can be approximately described by combinations of Erlang distributions, the Schwinger mechanism, and Tsallis statistics, with a weak softest point in the string tension observed in Omega hyperon spectra at 7.7 GeV, though the authors note this is a "weak" observation and the relation between softest points and the critical point of QGP phase transition remains an open question.

## Method

The authors fitted STAR Collaboration data for phi mesons and Omega hyperons using the single Erlang distribution (folding of n=3 exponential functions) and the two-component Schwinger mechanism (folding of two Gaussian functions with string tension parameters kappa_1 and kappa_2). For negatively charged particles, they used the two-component Erlang distribution (with n_1=3 and n_2=2) and the single Tsallis statistics (with temperature T and entropy index q). All fits were performed for five collision energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV) across multiple centrality intervals, with chi-squared per degree of freedom reported for each fit.

## Key numbers

- For phi mesons at 7.7 GeV in 0-10% centrality: two-component Schwinger mechanism gave kappa_1 = 0.967 ± 0.132 GeV/fm, kappa_2 = 2.635 ± 0.245 GeV/fm, chi^2/dof = 0.050; single Erlang gave ⟨p_t⟩ = 0.287 ± 0.057 GeV/c, chi^2/dof = 0.076
- For Omega hyperons at 39 GeV in 0-10% centrality: two-component Schwinger mechanism gave kappa_1 = 2.564 ± 0.324 GeV/fm, kappa_2 = 4.235 ± 0.325 GeV/fm, chi^2/dof = 0.066; single Erlang gave ⟨p_t⟩ = 0.331 ± 0.026 GeV/c, chi^2/dof = 0.152
- For negatively charged particles at 7.7 GeV in 0-5% centrality: single Tsallis statistics gave T = 0.152 ± 0.014 GeV, q = 1.024 ± 0.008, chi^2/dof = 1.442; two-component Erlang gave ⟨p_t⟩_1 = 0.189 ± 0.013 GeV/c, ⟨p_t⟩_2 = 0.240 ± 0.024 GeV/c, chi^2/dof = 0.886
- Chemical potentials used in Tsallis fits: 0.407 GeV at 7.7 GeV, 0.304 GeV at 11.5 GeV, 0.197 GeV at 19.6 GeV, 0.149 GeV at 27 GeV, and 0.107 GeV at 39 GeV
- A weak softest point in the string tension for Omega hyperon spectra was observed at 7.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of excitation functions in heavy-ion collisions, including analyses of K+/pi+ ratios (reported by various authors around 2014-2015), chemical freeze-out temperature, mean transverse mass minus rest mass, and negative pion rapidity distribution widths that showed softest points around 7-8 GeV. The authors also reference their own recent work (Gao and Liu, 2015-2016) on two-component Schwinger and Erlang distributions for J/psi and Upsilon production, and Levy distributions for identified particles. The results enable systematic comparison of different statistical descriptions (Erlang, Schwinger, Tsallis) for the same experimental data sets, and provide excitation functions of model parameters that can be used to search for the onset of quark deconfinement and the QGP critical point in future BES analyses.
