# A low energy theory for superfluid and solid matter and its application to the neutron star crust

> We formulate a low energy effective theory describing phases of matter that are both solid and superfluid. These systems simultaneously break translational symmetry and the phase symmetry associated with particle number. The symmetries restrict the combinations of terms that can appear in the effective action and the lowest order terms featuring equal number of derivatives and Goldstone fields are completely specified by the thermodynamic free energy, or equivalently by the long-wavelength limit of static correlation functions in the ground state. We show that the underlying interaction between particles that constitute the lattice and the superfluid gives rise to entrainment, and mixing between the Goldstone modes. As a concrete example we discuss the low energy theory for the inner crust of a neutron star, where a lattice of ionized nuclei coexists with a neutron superfluid.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a low-energy effective field theory for matter that is simultaneously solid and superfluid, showing that the lowest-order Lagrangian is completely determined by the thermodynamic free energy (or equivalently by long-wavelength static correlation functions), and that interactions between the lattice and superfluid components produce entrainment and mixing between the Goldstone modes.

## Method

The authors construct an effective action for the four Goldstone modes (three lattice phonons \(\xi^a\) and one superfluid phonon \(\phi\)) by promoting the global symmetries of translation and particle-number conservation to local symmetries through coupling to external gauge fields and a curved spacetime metric. They prove that the leading-order effective Lagrangian is fully specified by the thermodynamic pressure as a function of chemical potential and external fields, using a power-counting scheme where loop contributions are suppressed by four powers of momentum relative to tree-level terms. The formalism is then applied to the inner crust of a neutron star, where a lattice of neutron-rich nuclei coexists with a superfluid neutron background.

## Key numbers

* The mixing coefficient \(g_{\rm mix}\) in the effective Lagrangian couples superfluid and lattice dynamics through the term \(g_{\rm mix} f_\phi \sqrt{\rho} \, \partial_0\phi \partial_a\xi^a\).
* The weak interaction timescale is \(\tau_{\rm weak} \simeq 1/(G_F^2 n_B T^2)\), and the ratio \(\tau_{\rm EFT} / \tau_{\rm weak} \simeq G_F^2 n_B T \ll 1\) justifies treating neutrons and protons as separately conserved.
* The leading-order Lagrangian for the pure superfluid case is given by \({\cal L}_0(X) = P(Y \equiv \sqrt{X} - m_n)\), where \(X = g^{\mu\nu} D_\mu\phi D_\nu\phi\) and \(P\) is the thermodynamic pressure.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach for superfluids developed by Son and Wingate (2005) and Son (2002) to the case where both translational and U(1) symmetries are spontaneously broken, as occurs in the neutron star crust. The formalism enables calculation of the coupled phonon eigenmodes relevant for heat transport in the inner crust (Aguilera et al., 2008) and for interpreting quasi-periodic oscillations in magnetar flares (Strohmayer and Watts, 2006). The results also connect to earlier work on elastic properties of LOFF phases by Mannarelli et al. (2007) and to studies of entrainment in neutron star crusts by Carter et al. (2004, 2006) and Pethick et al. (2010).
