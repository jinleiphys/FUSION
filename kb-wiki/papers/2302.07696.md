# The effect of weak magnetic photon emission from quark-gluon plasma

> We propose a novel effect that accounts for the photon emission from a quark-gluon plasma in the presence of a weak external magnetic field. Although the weak magnetic photon emission from quark-gluon plasma only leads to a small correction to the photon production rate, the induced photon spectrum can be highly azimuthally anisotropic, as a consequence of the coupled effect of the magnetic field and the longitudinal dynamics in the background medium. With respect to a realistic medium evolution containing a tilted fireball configuration, the direct photon elliptic flow from experiments is reproduced. In comparison to the experimental data of direct photon elliptic flow, in heavy-ion collisions the magnitude of the magnetic field before 1 fm/c can be extracted. For the top energy of RHIC collisions, right after the pre-equilibrium evolution, $|eB|$ is found no larger than a few percent of the pion mass square.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a weak external magnetic field (|eB|/mπ² ≪ 1) in the hydrodynamic stage of quark-gluon plasma evolution, while producing only a small correction to the total photon production rate, can generate a large azimuthal anisotropy in the direct photon spectrum through coupling with the longitudinal dynamics (rapidity-odd dipolar moment) of the background medium.

## Method

The authors compute the weak magnetic field correction to the quark distribution function via kinetic theory at leading order in |eB|/T², then incorporate this correction into the 2→2 photon production rate from QGP. They perform 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC program with a tilted fireball initial condition that reproduces the measured directed flow v₁ of charged hadrons, treating the magnetic field as constant during the hydrodynamic evolution with a space-time rapidity profile from the Lienard-Wiechert potential.

## Key numbers

- Extracted time-averaged field strength at RHIC (√sNN = 0.2 TeV): |eB̄y| = 0.02 mπ² (0-20% centrality), 0.04 mπ² (20-40% centrality), 0.06 mπ² (40-60% centrality)
- Extracted time-averaged field strength at LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV): |eB̄y| = 0.04 mπ² (0-20% centrality), 0.1 mπ² (20-40% centrality)
- Weak magnetic photon emission yields v₂EB ≈ 0.6 from realistic hydrodynamic simulations
- The weak magnetic field correction increases direct photon yields by about 10% (20-40% centrality at RHIC)
- Initial hydrodynamic evolution time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c; crossover temperature T_c = 145 MeV
- Electrical conductivity used: σ_el/T ≈ 5.98 from pQCD

## Context

This work addresses the "direct photon puzzle", the discrepancy between hydrodynamic model predictions and experimental measurements of direct photon yields and elliptic flow at RHIC (PHENIX 2015) and LHC (ALICE 2018). It extends prior work on photon production in strong magnetic fields (Basar 2012, Bzdak 2012, Tuchin 2014) by focusing on the more realistic weak-field regime relevant to the hydrodynamic stage, where magnetohydrodynamics and Landau level effects are not required. The tilted fireball initial condition follows Chatterjee (2017), and the background photon calculations are taken from Gale (2021). The results enable extraction of the time-averaged magnetic field strength in QGP and suggest that the conductive properties of the medium slow magnetic field decay compared to vacuum expectations.
