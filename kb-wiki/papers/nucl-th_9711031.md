# Effect of $ŽüN$ channel in the $╬│N \to ŽĆŽĆN$ reactions

> A model for the two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon proposedearlier is modified to simultaneously explain the total cross sections and the invariant mass spectra. Using this model, we discuss the role of the $Žü$ meson in the $╬│N \to ŽĆŽĆN$ reaction.

## Key claim
The paper modifies a previously proposed model for two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon to simultaneously reproduce total cross sections and invariant mass spectra, finding that the ŽüN intermediate state plays an important role alongside the ŽĆ╬ö channel, but only after changing the sign of the ŽüNN* coupling constant from negative to positive and increasing the range parameter of the ŽüŽĆŽĆ form factor to resolve discrepancies with experimental invariant mass spectra.

## Method
The T-matrix for ╬│N ŌåÆ ŽĆŽĆN is constructed from six processes: ╬ö Kroll-Ruderman term, ╬ö pion-pole term, N*(1520) excitation terms (s-wave ŽĆ╬ö, d-wave ŽĆ╬ö, and ŽüN channels), and Žü Kroll-Ruderman term. Parameters such as coupling constants, range parameters, and bare masses are determined phenomenologically from ŽĆN scattering amplitudes, ╬│N ŌåÆ ŽĆN multipole amplitudes, branching fractions of N*(1520), and the Žü-meson width, with remaining signs and parameters fixed by fitting ╬│p ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ-p and ╬│p ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ0n cross sections.

## Context
This work extends earlier models by Oset (1994) and Laget (1994) for two-pion photoproduction, which could not explain neutral pion production channels (╬│p ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ0n and ╬│n ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ0p) using only the ŽĆ╬ö(1232) mechanism that dominates ╬│p ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ-p. The modified model enables direct comparison with experimental invariant mass spectra from Mainz (Pedroni, preliminary data) and demonstrates that extrapolation procedures for total cross sections in the ╬│n ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ0p channel are model-dependent, suggesting future comparisons should use acceptance-integrated theoretical results rather than extrapolated data.
