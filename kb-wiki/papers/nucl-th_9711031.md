# Effect of $ρN$ channel in the $γN \to ππN$ reactions

> A model for the two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon proposedearlier is modified to simultaneously explain the total cross sections and the invariant mass spectra. Using this model, we discuss the role of the $ρ$ meson in the $γN \to ππN$ reaction.

## Key claim
The paper modifies a previously proposed model for two-pion photoproduction on the nucleon to simultaneously reproduce total cross sections and invariant mass spectra, finding that the ρN intermediate state plays an important role alongside the πΔ channel, but only after changing the sign of the ρNN* coupling constant from negative to positive and increasing the range parameter of the ρππ form factor to resolve discrepancies with experimental invariant mass spectra.

## Method
The T-matrix for γN → ππN is constructed from six processes: Δ Kroll-Ruderman term, Δ pion-pole term, N*(1520) excitation terms (s-wave πΔ, d-wave πΔ, and ρN channels), and ρ Kroll-Ruderman term. Parameters such as coupling constants, range parameters, and bare masses are determined phenomenologically from πN scattering amplitudes, γN → πN multipole amplitudes, branching fractions of N*(1520), and the ρ-meson width, with remaining signs and parameters fixed by fitting γp → π+π-p and γp → π+π0n cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range studied: 450 to 800 MeV (Mainz Microtron MAMI experiments)
- Total cross sections calculated for γp → π+π-p, γp → π+π0n, γn → π-π0p, and γp → π0π0p reactions
- Invariant mass spectra calculated at 730 MeV for γn → π-π0p and at 750 MeV for γp → π+π-p
- Sign of fπΔN* > 0, fπΔN* < 0, and fρNN* < 0 in initial parameter set; modified to fρNN* > 0 in final parameter set
- Detector acceptance for γn → π-π0p measurement: ≤ 50%

## Context
This work extends earlier models by Oset (1994) and Laget (1994) for two-pion photoproduction, which could not explain neutral pion production channels (γp → π+π0n and γn → π-π0p) using only the πΔ(1232) mechanism that dominates γp → π+π-p. The modified model enables direct comparison with experimental invariant mass spectra from Mainz (Pedroni, preliminary data) and demonstrates that extrapolation procedures for total cross sections in the γn → π-π0p channel are model-dependent, suggesting future comparisons should use acceptance-integrated theoretical results rather than extrapolated data.
