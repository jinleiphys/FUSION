# 3N potentials in the Faddeev coordinate space approach to Nd scattering

> In the last decade, for studying 3$N$ bound states and $Nd$ scattering the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) and Urbana 3$N$ force derived from the chiral EFT have been applied. We plan to use the TM 3$N$ force for studying the $Nd$ scattering on the basis of the Faddeev equations in configuration space. In the given paper, we present our final formulas for components of the TM 3$N$ potential obtained in the coordinate space.

## Key claim

The authors present the final coordinate-space formulas for the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) three-nucleon potential components, specifically the two-pion-exchange part, for use in Faddeev equations for nucleon-deuteron scattering; they note that in this first attempt they consider only the simplest contribution to the TM potential, and that the full implementation of these formulas into actual scattering calculations remains a problem to be treated at the next step.

## Method

The authors start from the momentum-space expression of the TM-ππ three-nucleon potential and perform its Fourier transform to obtain a local coordinate-space representation. They then rewrite the potential in terms of Jacobi coordinates (x and y vectors) and decompose it into nine distinct operator structures (O₁ through O₉) for each of the three possible nucleon permutations. The matrix elements of these operators are expressed in the MGL (Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne) basis by first computing them in a factorized a-basis (where individual spin projections and orbital momenta are given) and then transforming via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, with the spatial parts expanded in partial waves using bipolar harmonics.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant: g² = 13.4
- Pion mass: μ = 138.0 MeV
- Cutoff parameter: Λ = 5.8μ
- TM99 parameters: μa = -1.12, μ³b = -2.80, μ³d = -0.75
- Form factor: F²_πNN(q) = (Λ² - μ²)² / (Λ² + q²)²

## Context

This work extends the application of the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force, previously used for three-nucleon bound states, to nucleon-deuteron scattering calculations within the Faddeev equations in configuration space. The TM potential is one of several phenomenological three-nucleon forces (alongside the Urbana IX, Brazilian, and Illinois potentials) that have been developed to address the underbinding of light nuclei noted by Nogga and Pieper and the analyzing-power puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering. The authors plan to use these formulas in future numerical calculations of nucleon-deuteron scattering observables.
