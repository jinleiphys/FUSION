# Expanded calculation of weak-interaction mediated neutrino cooling rates due to $^{56}$Ni in stellar matter

> Accurate estimate of the neutrino cooling rates is required in order to study the various stages of stellar evolution of massive stars. Neutrino losses from proto-neutron stars play a crucial role in deciding whether these stars would be crushed into black holes or explode as supernovae. Both pure leptonic and weak-interaction processes contribute to the neutrino energy losses in stellar matter. At low temperatures and densities, characteristic of the early phase of presupernova evolution, cooling through neutrinos produced via the weak-interaction is important. Proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory has recently being used for calculation of stellar weak-interaction rates of $fp$-shell nuclide with success. The lepton-to-baryon ratio ($Y_{e}$) during early phases of stellar evolution of massive stars changes substantially alone due to electron captures on $^{56}$Ni. The stellar matter is transparent to the neutrinos produced during the presupernova evolution of massive stars. These neutrinos escape the site and assist the stellar core in maintaining a lower entropy. Here I present the expanded calculation of weak-interaction mediated neutrino and antineutrino cooling rates due to $^{56}$Ni in stellar matter using the pn-QRPA theory. This detailed scale is appropriate for interpolation purposes and of greater utility for simulation codes. The calculated rates are compared against earlier calculations. During the relevant temperature and density regions of stellar matter the reported rates show little differences with the shell model rates and might contribute in fine-tuning of the lepton-to-baryon ratio during the presupernova phases of stellar evolution of massive stars.

## Key claim

The paper presents an expanded calculation of weak-interaction mediated neutrino and antineutrino cooling rates due to \(^{56}\)Ni using the pn-QRPA theory on a detailed temperature-density grid suitable for interpolation in simulation codes, finding that during the most relevant presupernova conditions (\(\rho Y_e \sim 10^7\) g cm\(^{-3}\), \(1 \leq T_9 \leq 5\)) the rates agree well with earlier shell model and FFN calculations, but at high temperatures and densities the pn-QRPA rates are smaller by up to an order of magnitude.

## Method

The author used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory to calculate neutrino and antineutrino cooling rates from four weak-interaction channels: electron capture, positron capture, electron emission, and positron emission on \(^{56}\)Ni. The rates were computed on an expanded grid of stellar temperatures (\(T_9\) from 1 to 10) and densities (\(\log(\rho Y_e)\) from 0.5 to 11 g cm\(^{-3}\)), incorporating experimental data where available and constructing excited states as phonon-correlated multi-quasi-particle states without assuming Brink's hypothesis. The total cooling rates were summed over all initial and final states until convergence, using Boltzmann occupation probabilities for parent excited states.

## Key numbers

- The pn-QRPA model calculated the centroid of the GT\(_+\) strength distribution for \(^{56}\)Ni at around 5.7 MeV, compared to the FFN value of 3.8 MeV and the large-scale shell model (LSSM) range of 2.5–3.0 MeV.
- At low densities (\(\rho Y_e = 10\) g cm\(^{-3}\)) and temperature \(T_9 = 1\), the pn-QRPA neutrino cooling rates are larger than both FFN and LSSM rates by up to a factor of 7.
- At high densities (\(\rho Y_e = 10^9\) g cm\(^{-3}\), \(1 \leq T_9 \leq 3\)), LSSM rates are larger than pn-QRPA rates by up to a factor of 5.
- The total GT strength calculated by LSSM is 10.1, compared to the pn-QRPA value of 8.9.
- At \(\rho Y_e = 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), FFN rates are enhanced by at most a factor of 3, and at \(\rho Y_e = 10^9\) g cm\(^{-3}\) by up to an order of magnitude relative to pn-QRPA rates.
- For \(Y_e\) around 0.5, \(^{56}\)Ni has a mass fraction of around 0.99; the rate of change of \(Y_e\) is roughly 25% due to electron capture on \(^{56}\)Ni alone (citing Aufderheide 1994).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of weak-interaction rates on \(^{56}\)Ni by Nabi and Rahman (2005, 2008) using the pn-QRPA theory, and provides a finer temperature-density grid than the pioneering FFN (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman 1982) and large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations by Langanke and collaborators (2000). The reported rates are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulation codes, where they may help fine-tune the lepton-to-baryon ratio (\(Y_e\)) during presupernova evolution, particularly given that Aufderheide (1994) ranked \(^{56}\)Ni as the third most important electron capture nucleus and Heger et al. (2001) identified it as one of the most important nuclides for capture in massive stars.
