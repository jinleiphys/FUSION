# Pomeron and Reggeon contributions to elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering in holographic QCD

> The total and differential cross sections of elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering are studied in a holographic QCD model, considering the Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges in the Regge regime. In our model setup, the Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges are described by the Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson propagators, respectively. How those contributions change with the energy is explicitly shown, focusing on the contribution ratios. The adjustable parameters included in the model are determined with the experimental data, and it is presented that the resulting total and differential cross sections are consistent with the data in a wide kinematic region.

# Pomeron and Reggeon contributions to elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering in holographic QCD

## Key claim
The holographic QCD model, using Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson propagators for Pomeron and Reggeon exchanges respectively, can describe elastic pp and p̅p scattering data across a wide kinematic range (5 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 1000 GeV for total cross sections; 10 GeV < √s ≤ 13 TeV and |t| < 0.45 GeV² for differential cross sections), but only after the Reggeon exchange contribution is included alongside the previously considered Pomeron exchange, and with the caveat that deviations appear at |t| ∼ 0.4 GeV² for TeV-scale energies.

## Method
The authors constructed scattering amplitudes by combining proton-glueball and proton-vector meson couplings (derived from bottom-up AdS/QCD) with Reggeized propagators obtained by comparing to bosonic open and closed string amplitudes in flat space. The Pomeron parameters (intercept α_g(0)=1.084, slope α'_g=0.368 GeV⁻², coupling λ_g=9.59 GeV⁻¹) were taken from prior work (Xie 2019), while three Reggeon parameters (intercept α_v(0), and two coupling constants λ_vpp and λ_vp̅p̅) were determined by fitting to PDG total cross section data for √s between 5 and 1000 GeV using the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers
- Pomeron intercept: α_g(0) = 1.084 (fixed from prior work)
- Pomeron slope: α'_g = 0.368 GeV⁻² (fixed from prior work)
- Pomeron-proton coupling: λ_g = 9.59 GeV⁻¹ (fixed from prior work)
- Reggeon intercept (best fit): α_v(0) = 0.444 ± 0.008
- Reggeon coupling for pp: λ_vpp = 7.742 ± 0.205
- Reggeon coupling for p̅p̅: λ_vp̅p̅ = 16.127 ± 0.295
- The model describes total cross section data in the range 5 ≤ √s ≤ 1000 GeV
- Differential cross section predictions cover 0.01 < |t| < 0.45 GeV² and 10 GeV < √s ≤ 13 TeV

## Context
This work extends the holographic QCD model of Xie (2019), which considered only Pomeron exchange and was limited to √s ≥ 546 GeV, by adding Reggeon exchange contributions to describe lower-energy data. The Reggeized propagator approach follows the string-inspired formalism of Domokos (2009, 2010) and Anderson (2016). The results are compared to the phenomenological fits of the COMPETE Collaboration (2002) and to the Donnachie and Landshoff (1983) model; the Reggeon intercept found here (0.444) is close to that of Donnachie and Landshoff, while the Pomeron slope differs somewhat. The model enables predictions for differential cross sections without additional free parameters once the total cross section fit is performed.
