# Neutrino-nucleus interactions

> Interactions of neutrinos with nuclei in the energy ranges relevant for the MiniBooNE, T2K, NO$ν$A, MINER$ν$A and MINOS experiments are discussed. It is stressed that any theoretical treatment must involve all the relevant reaction mechanisms: quasielastic scattering, pion production and DIS. In addition, also many-body interactions play a role. In this talk we show how a misidentification of the reaction mechanism can affect the energy reconstruction. We also discuss how the newly measured pion production cross sections, as reported recently by the MiniBooNE collaboration, can be related to the old cross sections obtained on elementary targets. The MiniBooNE data seem to be compatible only with the old BNL data. Even then crucial features of the nucleon-pion-Delta interaction are missing in the experimental pion kinetic energy spectra. We also discuss the meson production processes at the higher energies of the NO$ν$A, MINER$ν$A and MINOS experiments. Here final state interactions make it impossible to gain knowledge about the elementary reaction amplitudes. Finally, we briefly explore the problems due to inaccuracies in the energy reconstruction that LBL experiments face in their extraction of oscillation parameters.

## Key claim

Misidentification of reaction mechanisms in neutrino-nucleus interactions leads to systematic errors in reconstructed neutrino energy that can mimic the effects of a CP-violating phase, making extraction of oscillation parameters ambiguous even when using state-of-the-art transport models.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model, which solves Kadanoff-Baym transport equations in the Botermans-Malfliet approximation for off-shell transport, to simulate neutrino-nucleus interactions across the energy ranges of MiniBooNE, T2K, NOνA, MINERνA, and MINOS. They compared calculations using both ANL and BNL elementary pion production cross sections as input, and studied the migration between true and reconstructed neutrino energy for quasielastic-like events, pion production, and deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE quasielastic-like cross section data show a difference between measured and extracted values that is largest at lowest energies and decreases at highest energies, opposite to the expectation from stuck-pion events; this is attributed to energy reconstruction errors
- For MiniBooNE pion production, calculations after final state interactions using BNL elementary cross sections underestimate data; a 10% upward renormalization of the MiniBooNE flux (still within flux uncertainties) brings better agreement
- The ratio of antineutrino to neutrino cross sections on iron rises steeply below about 10 GeV, approaching the asymptotic value of about 0.5 only above about 25 GeV
- At 4 GeV neutrino energy, Δ and quasielastic scattering dominate only at Q² < 0.4 GeV²; deep inelastic scattering dominates at larger Q²
- Pion kinetic energy distributions for MINOS show a suppression around 0.2 GeV due to pion final state interactions, similar to the MiniBooNE case

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GiBUU-based studies (Lalakulich 2012) and addresses the discrepancy between MiniBooNE quasielastic-like data and true quasielastic cross sections that was attributed to 2p-2h excitations by Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011). The paper shows that energy reconstruction errors from misidentified reaction mechanisms affect oscillation parameter extraction, as first investigated by Meloni (2012), and that final state interactions in the shallow inelastic scattering regime make it impossible to extract elementary reaction amplitudes from pion or kaon measurements at MINERνA or NOνA.
