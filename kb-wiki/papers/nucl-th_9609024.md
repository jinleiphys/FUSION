# Charge Symmetry Breaking in the Valence Quark Distributions of the Nucleon

> Using a quark model, we study the effect of charge symmetry breaking on the valence quark distributions of the nucleon. The effect due to quark mass differences and the Coulomb interaction of the electrically charged quarks is calculated and, in contrast to recent claims, found to be small. In addition, we investigate the effect of charge symmetry breaking in the confining interaction, and in the perturbative evolution equations used to relate the quark model distributions to experiment. We find that both these effects are small, and that the strong charge symmetry breaking effect included in the scalar confining interactions may be distinguishable from that generated by quark mass differences.

## Key claim
Using the Los Alamos Model Potential (LAMP), the authors find that charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effects in valence quark distributions from quark mass differences and Coulomb interactions are small (at most a few percent), contradicting earlier claims of larger effects from diquark kinematics. They also show that CSB in the confining interaction may be distinguishable from quark-mass-generated CSB by its opposite sign in the valence distributions.

## Method
The authors calculate valence quark distributions at a low quark model scale (0.5 GeV²) by evaluating light-cone matrix elements in a Peierls-Yoccoz projected momentum eigenstate, using Dirac wavefunctions from the LAMP linear confining potential. They incorporate CSB by introducing quark masses (m_u = 4 MeV, m_d = 8 MeV), a mean-field Coulomb interaction between quarks, and shifts in the Dirac eigenvalue; they also model CSB in the confining interaction by altering the string tension parameter. The distributions are then evolved perturbatively to 10 GeV² using QCD evolution equations, including QED splitting effects from photon emission.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses used: m_u = 4 MeV, m_d = 8 MeV.
- For minority quarks, the CSB ratio from quark mass differences rises to +1.5% at large x.
- For the proton, quark eigenvalues shifted from 361.8 MeV (massless) to ω_u = 364.2 MeV and ω_d = 365.0 MeV; for the neutron, ω_u = 363.0 MeV and ω_d = 365.6 MeV.
- Eigenvalue shifts produce CSB ratios of 2–3% at large x.
- The Coulomb correction to the CSB ratio is much smaller than 1% for majority quarks.
- Evolution from 0.5 GeV² to 10 GeV² has only a small effect on CSB ratios, shifting curves to low x and slightly increasing magnitude.
- In Ref. 6, the diquark model gave a 5–10% CSB ratio for minority quarks in the range 0.5 < x < 0.7, roughly twice the size of the authors' result.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of charge symmetry breaking in nucleon structure, particularly addressing claims by Ref. 6 (diquark model) of large CSB effects and by Ref. 5 (Sather, model-independent approach) of smaller but still significant ratios. The authors use the LAMP quark model to provide an independent check and to investigate additional CSB sources (confining interaction, evolution) not considered in those works. The results enable future experimental searches for CSB in valence quark distributions, potentially distinguishing between CSB from quark mass differences and from mixing of strong and electromagnetic interactions, though the authors note that isolating the minority quark distribution via Drell-Yan processes will be challenging due to systematic errors.
