# Gluon and Ghost Dynamics from Lattice QCD

> The two point gluon and ghost correlation functions and the three gluon vertex are investigated, in the Landau gauge, using lattice simulations. For the two point functions, we discuss the approach to the continuum limit looking at the dependence on the lattice spacing and volume. The analytical structure of the propagators is also investigated by computing the corresponding spectral functions using an implementation of the Tikhonov regularisation to solve the integral equation. For the three point function we report results when the momentum of one of the gluon lines is set to zero and discuss its implications.

## Key claim

The gluon propagator is finite and non-vanishing at all momenta, and the ghost propagator behaves essentially as a massless particle; however, the lattice data show nontrivial dependence on the lattice spacing, and the approach to the continuum limit is not monotonic for the gluon propagator, with the coarsest lattice spacing providing a lower bound.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the Landau gauge, using the Wilson action at several values of the gauge coupling beta (5.7, 6.0, 6.3) and lattice volumes ranging from 44^4 to 128^4. They computed the gluon and ghost two-point propagators and the three-gluon vertex for the special kinematic configuration where one gluon momentum is set to zero. The spectral functions for the propagators were extracted by inverting the Kallen-Lehmann integral equation using Tikhonov regularization with the Morozov discrepancy principle to select the optimal regularization parameter.

## Key numbers

* The gluon propagator at zero momentum is finite and can be described by a Yukawa-like massive propagator with a running mass of about 2 Lambda_QCD at zero momentum.
* For the three-gluon vertex form factor Gamma(p^2), the zero crossing occurs in the interval 216 - 264 MeV, consistent with the Dyson-Schwinger prediction of approximately 200 MeV.
* The lattice spacing values used were a = 0.1838 fm (beta = 5.7), a = 0.1016 fm (beta = 6.0), and a = 0.0627 fm (beta = 6.3), all for a physical volume of approximately (8 fm)^4.
* The gluon propagator data are compatible with a refined Gribov-Zwanziger expression having a pair of complex conjugate poles at p^2 approximately -0.3 +/- i 0.5 GeV^2.
* The ghost propagator and one-loop renormalization group improved perturbation theory become compatible for momenta above approximately 800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of gluon and ghost propagators by Cucchieri (2007) and Bogolubsky (2009), which used larger lattice spacings (approximately 0.2 fm), by investigating the approach to the continuum limit with smaller lattice spacings and larger volumes. The spectral function analysis follows the Tikhonov regularization method introduced by Dudal (2014). The three-gluon vertex results confirm the sign change predicted by Dyson-Schwinger equation analyses (Binosi 2013) and are consistent with earlier 3D SU(2) simulations (Cucchieri 2008) and recent 4D SU(3) results (Athenodorou 2016). The work enables future studies of higher-order gluon correlation functions and more refined spectral function calculations with improved ultraviolet behavior.
