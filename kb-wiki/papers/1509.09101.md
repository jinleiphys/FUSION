# Radiative lifetime and energy of the low-energy isomeric level in $^{229}$Th

> We estimate the range of the radiative lifetime and energy of the anomalous, low-energy $3/2^+(7.8 \pm 0.5$ eV) state in the $^{229}$Th nucleus. Our phenomenological calculations are based on the available experimental data for the intensities of $M1$ and $E2$ transitions between excited levels of the $^{229}$Th nucleus in the $K^π[Nn_ZΛ]=5/2^+[633]$ and $3/2^+[631]$ rotational bands. We also discuss the influence of certain branching coefficients, which affect the currently accepted measured energy of the isomeric state. From this work, we establish a favored region where the transition lifetime and energy should lie at roughly the 90% confidence level. We also suggest new nuclear physics measurements, which would significantly reduce the ambiguity in the present data.

## Key claim

The radiative lifetime and energy of the low-energy isomeric state in $^{229}$Th are estimated using phenomenological calculations based on available experimental data, but the authors note that the spread in measured reduced transition probabilities leads to a significant range of possible values, and the currently accepted isomeric energy of $7.8 \pm 0.5$ eV depends on branching coefficients that show considerable variation across different experiments.

## Method

The authors used the Alaga rules to relate the reduced probability of the isomeric $M1$ transition $3/2^+3/2[631](7.8\ \text{eV}) \rightarrow 5/2^+5/2[633](0.0)$ to measured $M1$ transition probabilities between excited levels of the $5/2^+[633]$ and $3/2^+[631]$ rotational bands in $^{229}$Th, primarily the $9/2^+5/2[633](97.14\ \text{keV}) \rightarrow 7/2^+3/2[631](71.83\ \text{keV})$ transition. They also analyzed the influence of branching coefficients $b_{29}$ and $b_{42}$ on the determination of the isomeric energy from microcalorimeter measurements, and calculated internal conversion probabilities for the neutral Th atom using a code developed from Soldatov (1979) and Band (1979).

## Key numbers

- The currently accepted isomeric energy is $E_{is} = 7.8 \pm 0.5$ eV, updating a previous value of $7.6 \pm 0.5$ eV from Beck (2007).
- Reduced probabilities $B_{W.u.}(M1;3/2^+(7.8\ \text{eV}) \rightarrow 5/2^+(0.0))$ calculated from four experiments range from $(1.42 \pm 0.30) \times 10^{-2}$ (Gulda 2002) to $(4.55 \pm 0.48) \times 10^{-2}$ (Ruchowska 2006).
- The favored region for the radiative half-life as a function of transition energy $\omega$ is $0.46 \times 10^6\ \text{s}\ \text{eV}^3/\omega^3 \leq T_{1/2} \leq 1.5 \times 10^6\ \text{s}\ \text{eV}^3/\omega^3$ at roughly 95% confidence level, including the Birge ratio of 3.4.
- A more conservative region gives $0.31 \times 10^6\ \text{s}\ \text{eV}^3/\omega^3 \leq T_{1/2} \leq 2.1 \times 10^6\ \text{s}\ \text{eV}^3/\omega^3$.
- The characteristic lifetime of the isomer in a neutral Th atom due to internal conversion is approximately 10 $\mu$s.
- Branching ratios $b_{29}$ calculated from experimental data range from 1/3.1 (Ruchowska 2006) to 1/13 (Beck 2007), while $b_{42}$ ranges from 1/50 (Beck-R) to 1/439 (Gulda 2002).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the $^{229}$Th isomeric state energy and lifetime, which is critical for proposed applications including a nuclear clock (Peik 2003, Campbell 2012) and a gamma-ray laser (Tkalya 2011). The authors critically assess the branching coefficients used in the microcalorimeter measurements of Beck (2007, Beck-R) that established the currently accepted isomeric energy, showing that the spread in experimental data from Bemis (1988), Gulda (2002), Barci (2003), and Ruchowska (2006) leads to considerable uncertainty. The results enable better planning of experiments using $^{229}$Th in large-bandgap crystals such as LiCaAlF$_6$ (Rellergert 2010, Hehlen 2013) or CaF$_2$ (Dessovic 2014, Stellmer 2015), and in trapped ion approaches (Campbell 2009, Herrera-Sancho 2012), by providing a favored region for the isomeric lifetime and energy.
