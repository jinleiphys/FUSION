# New description of four-body breakup reaction

> We present a novel method of smoothing discrete breakup cross sections calculated by the method of continuum-discretized coupled-channels. The method based on the complex scaling method is tested with success for $^{58}$Ni($d$, $pn$) reaction at 80 MeV as an example of a three-body breakup reaction, and applied to $^{12}$C($^6$He, $nn^4$He) reaction at 229.8 MeV as a typical example of a four-body breakup reaction. The new method does not need to derive continuum states of the projectile in order to evaluate the breakup cross section as a smooth factor of the excitation energy of the projectile. Fast convergence of the breakup cross section with respect to extending the modelspace is confirmed. For the $^6$He breakup cross section, the resonant component is separated from the non-resonant one.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new method that combines continuum-discretized coupled channels (CDCC) with the complex scaling method (CSM) to obtain smooth breakup cross sections as a continuous function of excitation energy, without needing to explicitly calculate the continuum wave functions of the projectile. The method is claimed to be practical for four-body breakup reactions and, in principle, applicable to many-body breakup reactions, provided the diagonalization of the projectile Hamiltonian and scaled Hamiltonian is feasible.

## Method

The method starts from CDCC calculations that produce discrete T-matrix elements for pseudostates of the projectile. The differential breakup cross section is rewritten in terms of a generalized response function containing the propagator (ve - H_B - i eta)^{-1}, which is then evaluated using the complex scaling method with a negative scaling angle theta. The scaled propagator is expanded in L^2-type basis functions obtained by diagonalizing the scaled internal Hamiltonian H_B^theta, avoiding the need to compute the exact three-body continuum wave functions. The method was tested on the 58Ni(d, pn) reaction at 80 MeV and applied to the 12C(6He, nn4He) reaction at 229.8 MeV and to 12C(6He, nn4He) and 208Pb(6He, nn4He) reactions at 240 MeV/A.

## Key numbers

* For the 58Ni(d, pn) reaction at 80 MeV, the new method (open circles) yields the same differential breakup cross section d sigma/d epsilon as the smoothing factor method (solid line), validating the approach.
* For the 12C(6He, nn4He) reaction at 229.8 MeV, convergence of the breakup cross section with respect to extending the model space is obtained with basis set II, which uses up to 15 Gaussian basis functions with maximum ranges of 20.0 fm for both Jacobi coordinates.
* The net breakup cross section to 0+, 1-, and 2+ continua converges at theta = -14 degrees when theta decreases from -6 degrees to -18 degrees.
* For the 1- continuum in the 12C(6He, nn4He) reaction at 229.8 MeV, including Coulomb breakup processes enhances the breakup cross section by a factor of 2 compared to the result without Coulomb breakup.
* For the 208Pb(6He, nn4He) reaction at 240 MeV/A, the present method underestimates experimental data at excitation energies epsilon greater than approximately 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method for four-body breakup reactions, building on prior pseudostate discretization methods using Gaussian basis functions (Matsumoto 2003, 2004) and transformed harmonic oscillator basis functions (Moro 2007). The new method circumvents the difficulty of calculating the smoothing factor directly, which was previously attempted with direct numerical integration (LSCSM1) and with CSM (LSCSM2), but those approaches required complicated numerical calculations and showed insufficient convergence. The present formalism enables analysis of both nuclear and Coulomb breakup processes in the same framework, and the results are compared with experimental data from Aumann (1999) that were previously analyzed using four-body distorted-wave Born approximation (4BDW) and the eikonal approximation (4BEIK).
