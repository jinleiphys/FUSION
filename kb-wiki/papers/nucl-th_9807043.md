# Polarization Effects in Superdeformed Nuclei

> A detailed theoretical investigation of polarization effects in superdeformed nuclei is performed. In the pure harmonic oscillator potential it is shown that when one particle (or hole) with the mass single-particle quadrupole moment q_{nu} is added to a superdeformed core, the change of the electric quadrupole moment can be parameterized as q_{eff}=e(bq_{nu}+a), and analytical expressions are derived for the two parameters, $a$ and $b$. Simple numerical expressions for q_{eff}(q_ν}) are obtained in the more realistic modified oscillator model. It is also shown that quadrupole moments of nuclei with up to 10 particles removed from the superdeformed core of 152Dy can be well described by simply subtracting effective quadrupole moments of the active single-particle states from the quadrupole moment of the core. Tools are given for estimating the quadrupole moment for possible configurations in the superdeformed A 150-region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when a single particle (or hole) is added to a superdeformed core, the change in the electric quadrupole moment can be parameterized as \(q_{eff}=e(b q_{\nu}+a)\), with analytical expressions derived for the parameters \(a\) and \(b\) in the pure harmonic oscillator model; however, the authors note that this linear relation holds strictly only for selfconsistent gaps at \(k:1\) deformation and requires specific conditions on the quantum numbers of the added orbital.

## Method

The authors used the pure harmonic oscillator potential to derive analytical expressions for the effective quadrupole moment \(q_{eff}\) by minimizing the total energy of the proton-neutron system and calculating the deformation change when one particle is added. They then extended the analysis to the more realistic modified oscillator (Nilsson-Strutinsky) model, calculating quadrupole moments both microscopically (summing single-particle quadrupole moments at minimum-energy deformations) and macroscopically (from a homogeneously charged spheroid). The study focused on superdeformed nuclei in the \(A \sim 150\) region, particularly around the \(^{152}\)Dy core, and tested additivity of effective quadrupole moments for configurations with up to 10 particles removed.

## Key numbers

- For the \(g=40\) selfconsistent gap at superdeformation (\(k=2\)), the microscopic slope parameters are \((b^{mic})_p = 1.5\) and \((b^{mic})_n = 0.5\), while the macroscopic slope parameters converge to \((b^{mac})_p = (b^{mac})_n \approx 0.995\) using \(D=41\) MeV (or exactly 1.000 with \(D=41.2\) MeV).
- The asymptotic macroscopic \(a\)-parameters for superdeformation (\(k=2\)) are \((a^{mac})_p = -(0 \pm 0.423 \cdot 2^{-4/3} g^{1/3})\) and \((a^{mac})_n = -(0.914 \cdot 2^{-2/3} g^{2/3} \pm 0.423 \cdot 2^{-4/3} g^{1/3})\) in units of \(r_0^2 e\).
- The asymptotic microscopic \(a\)-parameters for superdeformation are \((a^{mic})_p = -(0.613 \cdot 2^{-2/3} g^{2/3} \pm 0.212 \cdot 2^{-4/3} g^{1/3})\) and \((a^{mic})_n = -(0.306 \cdot 2^{-2/3} g^{2/3} \pm 0.212 \cdot 2^{-4/3} g^{1/3})\) in units of \(\hbar^2 e/(Dm)\).
- The quadrupole moments of nuclei with up to 10 particles removed from the superdeformed core of \(^{152}\)Dy can be well described by subtracting effective quadrupole moments of the active single-particle states.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of polarization effects in deformed nuclei, notably the work of Mottelson (1958) on spherical nuclei, and provides a theoretical foundation for interpreting high-precision quadrupole moment measurements in superdeformed bands such as those by Savajols et al. (1996), Nisius et al. (1997), and Hackman et al. (1998). The paper builds on the Nilsson-Strutinsky cranking method used in those experimental comparisons and on the additivity tests for deformations by Ragnarsson (1991, 1993), and parallels selfconsistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by Satula et al. (1996) who extracted effective quadrupole moments from least square fits. The derived analytical expressions enable estimation of quadrupole moments for any configuration in the superdeformed \(A \sim 150\) region, facilitating configuration assignments for newly observed rotational bands.
