# Symmetric nuclear matter from the strong interaction

> We study the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature over a wide range of densities using two complementary theoretical approaches. At low densities up to twice nuclear saturation density, we compute the energy per particle based on modern nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory. For higher densities we derive for the first time constraints in a Fierz-complete setting directly based on quantum chromodynamics using functional renormalization group techniques. We find remarkable consistency of the results obtained from both approaches as they come together in density and the natural emergence of a maximum in the speed of sound $c_S$ at supranuclear densities with a value beyond the asymptotic $c_S^2 = 1/3$. The presence of a maximum appears tightly connected to the formation of a diquark gap.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter computed from chiral effective field theory at low densities and from a Fierz-complete functional renormalization group approach based on QCD at higher densities show remarkable consistency, and that a maximum in the speed of sound emerges naturally at supranuclear densities with a value exceeding the asymptotic conformal limit \(c_S^2 = 1/3\), though the exact density of this maximum cannot yet be determined conclusively.

## Method

At low densities up to twice nuclear saturation density, the energy per particle was computed using many-body perturbation theory with nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory up to N\(^3\)LO. At higher densities, the authors employed functional renormalization group techniques starting from the Euclidean QCD action with two massless quark flavors, analyzing the RG flow of a Fierz-complete basis of ten pointlike four-quark couplings to identify the most dominant channel (scalar-pseudoscalar at low densities, diquark at intermediate/high densities), then performing a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation at a transition scale \(\Lambda_0\) to compute the pressure from a quark-meson-diquark-model truncation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Transition scale \(\Lambda_0\) varied from 450 to 600 MeV to estimate uncertainties
- At \(n/n_0 \approx 5\), the diquark gap \(\Delta\) extracted from this study is approximately 140 to 230 MeV (depending on \(\Lambda_0\)), compared to approximately 70 to 160 MeV from Alford (1997)
- The speed of sound maximum exceeds the asymptotic value \(c_S^2 = 1/3\)
- The FRG results approach those from perturbative QCD calculations at high densities (\(n/n_0 > 75\))

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT calculations of Drischler (2017) and Hebeler (2010) to higher densities by connecting them with a first-principles QCD-based FRG approach, building on the Fierz-complete analysis of Braun (2018). The results are consistent with the expectation from perturbative QCD studies (Kurkela 2009, Gorda 2018) at high densities and show that the diquark channel becomes dominant at intermediate densities, supporting the two-flavor color-superconductor pairing scenario (Rapp 1997, Alford 1997). The framework enables future extensions to finite temperature and general proton fractions for astrophysical applications.
