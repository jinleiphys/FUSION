# Pion Valence-quark Parton Distribution Function

> Within the Dyson-Schwinger equation formulation of QCD, a rainbow ladder truncation is used to calculate the pion valence-quark distribution function(PDF). The gap equation is renormalized at a typical hadronic scale, of order 0.5GeV, which is also set as the default initial scale for the pion PDF. We implement a corrected leading-order expression for the PDF which ensures that the valence-quarks carry all of the pion's light-front momentum at the initial scale. The scaling behavior of the pion PDF at a typical partonic scale of order 5.2GeV is found to be $(1-x)^ν$, with $ν\simeq 1.6$, as $x$ approaches one.

## Key claim

Within the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework using a rainbow-ladder truncation, the pion valence-quark parton distribution function (PDF) at a typical partonic scale of 5.2 GeV behaves as (1-x)^ν with ν ≃ 1.6 as x approaches one, which is softer than the (1-x)^2 prediction from the QCD parton model and perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion using a rainbow-ladder truncation, renormalizing the gap equation at a hadronic scale ζ_H = 0.5 GeV. They compute the moments of the PDF using algebraic parametrizations of the dressed-quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, then reconstruct the x-dependence by expanding in Gegenbauer polynomials. The PDF at the initial scale is evolved to 5.2 GeV using next-to-leading-order DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

- The initial scale for the pion PDF is set at ζ_H = 0.5 GeV, where valence quarks carry all of the pion's light-front momentum.
- With an input current quark mass of 18.6 MeV, the calculation yields m_π = 0.14 GeV and f_π = 0.092 GeV.
- At the evolved scale ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV, the large-x behavior of the pion PDF is (1-x)^ν with ν ≃ 1.6.
- The Gegenbauer expansion parameters for the PDF at the hadronic scale are: α = 1.158, a_2 = -0.175, a_4 = 0.1, a_6 = -0.019, a_8 = -0.015, with j_max = 8 sufficient for convergence.

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE studies of the pion PDF by Hecht (2001) and Nguyen (2011) by implementing a corrected leading-order expression that ensures valence quarks carry all momentum at the initial scale, and by using a moment-based extraction method previously applied to parton distribution amplitudes by Chang (2013). The result ν ≃ 1.6 at 5.2 GeV differs from the (1-x)^2 prediction of the QCD parton model (Farrar, 1975) and perturbative QCD, and from the linear (1-x) behavior favored by Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models with translational invariant regularization and the Drell-Yan-West relation. The calculation enables future inclusion of sea quark and gluon contributions following the perspective of Chang (2014).
