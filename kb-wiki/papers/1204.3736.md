# Laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect

> Proton emission from nuclei via the nuclear photoeffect in the combined electromagnetic fields of a gamma-ray photon and an intense laser wave is studied. An S-matrix approach to the process is developed by utilizing methods known from the theory of nonperturbative laser-atom interactions. As a specific example, photo-proton ejection from halo nuclei is considered. We show that, due to the presence of the laser field, rich sideband structures arise in the photo-proton energy spectra. Their dependence on the parameters and relative orientation of the photon fields is discussed.

## Key claim

The presence of an intense laser field can substantially modify the energy spectrum of protons emitted via the nuclear photoeffect, producing rich sideband structures, but the total cross section of the photo-proton emission is not affected by the laser field.

## Method

The authors developed an S-matrix approach within the strong-field approximation (SFA), treating the initial bound halo proton as a stationary Yukawa-type state and the final ejected proton as a Volkov state in a circularly polarized laser field. The partial cross sections for each laser photon number n were derived analytically, with the total cross section obtained by summing over n and integrating numerically over proton emission angles. The method was applied specifically to the one-proton halo nucleus ⁸B.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of ⁸B: Eb = 137 keV
- Root-mean-square distance in the ⁷Be-proton system: Rrms ≈ 4.73 fm
- Incident γ-photon energy: ωγ = 3 MeV
- Total cross section for all laser intensities considered: σ = Σ σn ≈ 63.4 mb
- For ω0 = 2 keV at I = 2.5×10²⁴ W/cm², the coupling parameter αmax ≈ 2.4, indicating transition to nonperturbative coupling
- For ω0 = 100 eV at I = 6.25×10²¹ W/cm², αmax ≈ 238, with sidebands extending to |n| ≳ 240
- Photo-proton energy spectrum width of 40 keV achieved with ω0 = 100 eV at I = 6.25×10²¹ W/cm²
- Equivalent spectrum width achievable with optical laser ω0 = 2 eV at I = 2.5×10¹⁸ W/cm²
- Bethe-Peierls formula estimate of total cross section: about 20 mb

## Context

This work extends methods from laser-assisted atomic photoionization (Kalman; Glover) to nuclear physics, specifically to one-proton halo nuclei such as ⁸B (boron; halostate2; halostate). The theoretical framework parallels the strong-field approximation used in atomic physics for laser-assisted processes. The results enable experimental investigation of laser-assisted photonuclear reactions using existing or near-future laser facilities, such as FLASH (DESY) with XUV frequencies ω0 ~ 100 eV at peak intensities ~10¹⁷ W/cm², or the Linac Coherent Light Source (SLAC) with x-ray beams at ω0 ~ 1 keV and ~10¹⁸ W/cm².
