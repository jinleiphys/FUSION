# Distillation of scalar exchange by coherent hypernucleus production in antiproton-nucleus collisions

> The total and angular differential cross sections of the coherent process $\bar p + {}^AZ \to {}^A_Λ(Z-1) + \barΛ$ are evaluated at the beam momenta $1.5\div20$ GeV/c within the meson exchange model with bound proton and $Λ$-hyperon wave functions. It is shown that the shape of the beam momentum dependence of the hypernucleus production cross sections with various discrete $Λ$ states is strongly sensitive to the presence of the scalar $κ$-meson exchange in the $\bar p p \to \barΛΛ$ amplitude. This can be used as a clean test of the exchange by scalar $πK$ correlation in coherent $\bar p A$ reactions.

## Key claim

The beam momentum dependence of coherent hypernucleus production cross sections in antiproton-nucleus collisions is strongly sensitive to the presence of scalar κ-meson exchange in the elementary \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) amplitude, and this sensitivity is enhanced because pseudoscalar K exchange is suppressed for bound \(\Lambda\) states, allowing a clean test of scalar \(\pi K\) correlation exchange.

## Method

The authors evaluate total and angular differential cross sections for the coherent process \(\bar p + {}^AZ \to {}^A_\Lambda(Z-1) + \bar\Lambda\) using a meson exchange model with \(K\), \(K^*\), and \(\kappa\) exchanges, employing bound proton and \(\Lambda\) wave functions from a relativistic mean-field Dirac equation. The elementary \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) amplitude is adjusted to experimental data with two parameter sets (Set 1 without \(\kappa\), Set 2 with \(\kappa\)), and the nuclear reaction is treated in the impulse approximation with eikonal corrections for initial and final state absorption of \(\bar p\) and \(\bar\Lambda\). Calculations are performed for the specific reaction \(\bar p + {}^{40}\mathrm{Ar} \to {}^{40}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Cl} + \bar\Lambda\) with \(\Lambda\) occupying discrete \(1s_{1/2}\), \(1p_{3/2}\), and \(1d_{5/2}\) states.

## Key numbers

- The \(\kappa\) meson mass used in calculations is \(m_\kappa = 682\) MeV.
- The \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) cross section peaks at \(p_{\mathrm{lab}} \simeq 2\) GeV/c; in Set 2 the peak is saturated mostly by \(\kappa\) exchange, while in Set 1 it is saturated by \(K\) exchange.
- At \(p_{\mathrm{lab}} = 2\) GeV/c, the angle-integrated \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) cross sections for Set 1 and Set 2 differ by only \(\sim 15\%\), but the hypernuclear production cross sections differ by almost one order of magnitude.
- The absorption of \(\bar p\) and \(\bar\Lambda\) reduces the hypernucleus production cross section at forward angles by about two orders of magnitude relative to the impulse approximation.
- The beam momentum range studied is \(1.5 \div 20\) GeV/c; the \(\kappa\) exchange dominates at moderate beam momenta \(\sim 1.5 \div 3\) GeV/c, producing a characteristic shoulder or maximum in the \(p_{\mathrm{lab}}\)-dependence of the hypernucleus production cross section, most pronounced for the \(1d_{5/2}\) \(\Lambda\) state.

## Context

This work extends the study of strange meson exchanges in \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) reactions to coherent hypernucleus production, building on prior meson exchange models for the elementary process (Cheoun 1996, Tsushima 1998, Han 1999) and on relativistic mean-field descriptions of hypernuclei (Keil 1999). The authors show that nuclear binding filters out pseudoscalar K exchange, which is suppressed for bound \(\Lambda\) states, unlike in free-space \(\bar p p \to \bar\Lambda\Lambda\) where K exchange dominates at low momenta. This enables a clean test of scalar \(\kappa\) exchange, which is a candidate member of the SU(3) scalar meson octet and represents correlated \(\pi K\) exchange. The proposed measurements could be performed at the planned \(\bar{\mathrm{P}}\)ANDA experiment at FAIR and at J-PARC, and the method could be extended to charmed hypernuclei production via scalar \(D_0^*(2400)\) exchange, as explored theoretically by Shyam (2016).
