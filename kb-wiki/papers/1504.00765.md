# Double bremsstrahlung from high-energy electron in the atomic field

> The differential cross section of double bremsstrahlung from high-energy electron in the electric field of heavy atoms is derived. The results are obtained with the exact account of the atomic field by means of the quasiclassical approximation to the wave functions and the Green's function in the external field. It is shown that the Coulomb corrections to the differential cross section (the difference between the exact result and the result obtained in the leading Born approximation) correspond to small momentum transfers. The Coulomb corrections to the differential cross section of double bremsstrahlung are accumulated in the factor, which coincides with the corresponding factor in the differential cross section of single bremsstrahlung. At small momentum transfer, this factor is very sensitive to the parameters of screening while the Coulomb corrections to the spectrum have the universal form.

## Key claim
The differential cross section of double bremsstrahlung from high-energy electrons in the atomic field is derived exactly in the parameter η = Zα (i.e., including Coulomb corrections to all orders), but only within the leading quasiclassical approximation; the Coulomb corrections are shown to factorize into the same factor as in single bremsstrahlung, and this factorization is violated by the first quasiclassical correction.

## Method
The authors used the quasiclassical approximation to the Dirac equation wave functions and Green's function in an external spherically symmetric atomic potential, including screening. They calculated the matrix element for double bremsstrahlung (e⁻Z → γ₁γ₂e⁻Z) in the leading quasiclassical approximation, separating the Born term from the Coulomb corrections, and integrated over the momentum transfer Δ⊥ to obtain the spectrum. The angular dependence of the cross section was evaluated numerically for various kinematic parameters.

## Key numbers
- The Coulomb correction factor f(η) = Re ψ(1 + iη) − ψ(1) appears in the integrated Coulomb correction cross section, Eq. (intR).
- The Born cross section integrated over Δ⊥ contains a logarithmic factor ln(1/β) where β = max{1/(mr_scr), m(ω₁+ω₂)/(ε_p ε_q)}.
- The function F(x) for the soft-photon limit (ω₂ → 0) has asymptotic behavior F(x) ≈ (4/3)x² ln(1/x) for x ≪ 1 and F(x) ≈ ln(1/(1−x)) for 1−x ≪ 1, where x = ω₁/ε_p.
- Numerical results for the dimensionless quantity S are shown for δ₁ = pθ_{k₁}/m = 0.2, 1, 2 and δ₂ = pθ_{k₂}/m = 0.5, 1, 2 at ω₁/ε_p = 0.2, ω₂/ε_p = 0.4.

## Context
This work extends the quasiclassical treatment of high-energy electromagnetic processes in atomic fields, following previous studies on single bremsstrahlung (Olsen et al., 1957; Lee et al., 2005) and pair photoproduction accompanied by bremsstrahlung (Krachkov et al., 2014). It provides the first exact-in-η result for double bremsstrahlung, which is a background process for precision experiments and electromagnetic shower calculations in detectors. The factorization of the Coulomb corrections into the same impact factor as single bremsstrahlung enables a recipe for calculating multiple bremsstrahlung amplitudes, extending the impact-factor approach of Cheng and Wu (1970) to small momentum transfer.
