# The classical field created in early stages of high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We show that a special choice of light-cone gauge can greatly simplify the calculation of the classical color field created in the initial stages of nucleus-nucleus collisions. Within this gauge, we can in particular construct explicitly the conserved color current and calculate exactly the gauge field immediately after the collision. This field is used as a boundary condition in an iterative solution of the Yang-Mills equations in the forward light-cone. In leading order, which corresponds to a linearization of the Yang-Mills equation in the forward light-cone, we obtain a simple formula for the spectrum of gluons produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions. This formula reproduces exactly the known formula for proton-nucleus collisions, where $k_t-$factorization is recovered, while the latter property apparently breaks down in the case of nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The authors show that a specific choice of light-cone gauge (the axial gauge \(A^+=0\)) greatly simplifies the analytic calculation of the classical color field in nucleus-nucleus collisions, allowing an explicit construction of the conserved color current and an exact determination of the gauge field immediately after the collision; however, the resulting leading-order gluon spectrum does not exhibit \(k_t\)-factorization for nucleus-nucleus collisions, whereas it exactly reproduces the known factorized formula for proton-nucleus collisions.

## Method

The authors work in the light-cone gauge \(A^+=0\) and exploit the residual gauge freedom to choose the field of nucleus A as a longitudinal solution (non-zero \(A^-\)) and the field of nucleus B as a transverse solution (non-zero \(A^i\)). They construct the conserved color current explicitly in terms of the sources \(\rho_A\) and \(\rho_B\) and Wilson lines \(U\) and \(V\). The gauge field on the light-cone (immediately after the collision) is solved exactly, and then an iterative solution of the Yang-Mills equations in the forward light-cone is developed, with the leading order corresponding to linearization of the equations around the non-interacting background fields.

## Key numbers

* The color charge densities \(\rho_A\) and \(\rho_B\) diverge as \(1/\epsilon\) when the longitudinal extent \(\epsilon \to 0\) (Section 2.3).
* The transverse components of the field remain finite as \(\epsilon \to 0\) because the divergence \(\sim 1/\epsilon\) of \(\Phi_A\) is eliminated by the \(y^+\) integration \(\sim \epsilon\) (Section 2.3).
* The free retarded propagator is given by \(G_0(x-y) = \theta(x^+-y^+)\theta(x^--y^-)\delta((x-y)^2)\) (Section 2.4).

## Context

This work extends the analytic treatment of classical color fields from proton-nucleus collisions (where \(k_t\)-factorization holds, as shown in earlier work by Kovchegov and Mueller, and by Blaizot, Gelis, and Venugopalan) to the more complex case of nucleus-nucleus collisions. It provides a gauge choice that eliminates spurious pure gauge fields in the forward light-cone, simplifying calculations that previously required numerical studies (e.g., by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan, and by Lappi) or approximate analytic treatments (e.g., by Kovchegov for AA collisions, and by Balitsky using a symmetric expansion in commutators of Wilson lines). The results enable further analytic progress in computing gluon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions and clarify the breakdown of \(k_t\)-factorization in that setting.
