# B(E1) Strengths from Coulomb Excitation of 11Be

> The $B$(E1;$1/2^+\to1/2^-$) strength for $^{11}$Be has been extracted from intermediate energy Coulomb excitation measurements, over a range of beam energies using a new reaction model, the extended continuum discretized coupled channels (XCDCC) method. In addition, a measurement of the excitation cross section for $^{11}$Be+$^{208}$Pb at 38.6 MeV/nucleon is reported. The $B$(E1) strength of 0.105(12) e$^2$fm$^2$ derived from this measurement is consistent with those made previously at 60 and 64 MeV/nucleon, i n contrast to an anomalously low result obtained at 43 MeV/nucleon. By coupling a multi-configuration description of the projectile structure with realistic reaction theory, the XCDCC model provides for the first time a fully quantum mechanical description of Coulomb excitation. The XCDCC calculations reveal that the excitation process involves significant contributions from nuclear, continuum, and higher-order effects. An analysis of the present and two earlier intermediate energy measurements yields a combined B(E1) strength of 0.105(7) e$^2$fm$^2$. This value is in good agreement with the value deduced independently from the lifetime of the $1/2^-$ state in $^{11}$Be, and has a comparable p recision.

## Key claim

The B(E1) strength for the 1/2+ to 1/2- transition in 11Be extracted from Coulomb excitation at 38.6 MeV/nucleon is 0.105(12) e2fm2, consistent with previous measurements at 60 and 64 MeV/nucleon and with the lifetime value, thereby resolving an anomalously low result previously obtained at 43 MeV/nucleon that may have been affected by a beam intensity normalization problem.

## Method

A secondary beam of 11Be at 38.6 MeV/nucleon was incident on a 552 mg/cm2 Pb target at GANIL; gamma rays in coincidence with 11Be ions were detected using four NaI detectors. The excitation cross section was extracted from the Doppler-corrected gamma-ray spectrum and compared to predictions from the extended continuum discretized coupled channels (XCDCC) method, which couples a particle-rotor description of 11Be (treating 10Be as a deformed rotor with its 2+ state) with a fully quantum mechanical reaction calculation including nuclear, continuum, and dynamical core excitation effects.

## Key numbers

- Measured cross section for 11Be+208Pb at 38.6 MeV/nucleon: 416(44) mb
- B(E1) strength extracted from this measurement: 0.105(12) e2fm2
- Combined B(E1) from three Pb-target Coulomb excitation measurements: 0.105(7) e2fm2
- Lifetime measurement B(E1) from Millener et al. (1983): 0.116(12) e2fm2
- B(E1) from re-analysis of the anomalous 43 MeV/nucleon GANIL measurement: 0.048(9) e2fm2
- B(E1) from re-analysis of MSU measurement on Pb: 0.101(16) e2fm2
- B(E1) from re-analysis of RIKEN measurement at 64 MeV/nucleon: 0.106(11) e2fm2
- Gamma-ray energy of the 1/2- to 1/2+ transition: 320 keV

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing anomaly in which the Coulomb excitation cross section for 11Be on Pb at 43 MeV/nucleon (Anne et al., 1995) was approximately 40% of that expected from Alder-Winther theory, while measurements at higher energies (Fauerbach et al., 1997 at MSU; Nakamura et al., 1997 at RIKEN) agreed with lifetime measurements. The XCDCC method, developed by Summers et al. (2006), provides the first fully quantum mechanical description of Coulomb excitation that simultaneously accounts for nuclear, continuum, and higher-order effects, enabling reliable extraction of B(E1) strengths from intermediate-energy Coulomb excitation with accuracy comparable to lifetime measurements.
