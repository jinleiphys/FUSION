# (2+1)-flavor QCD Thermodynamics from the Gradient Flow

> Recently, we proposed a novel method to define and calculate the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) in lattice gauge theory on the basis of the Yang-Mills gradient flow [1]. In this proceedings, we summarize the basic idea and technical steps to obtain the bulk thermodynamic quantities in lattice gauge theory using this method for the quenched and $(2+1)$-flavor QCD. The revised results of integration measure (trace anomaly) and entropy density of the quenched QCD with corrected coefficients are shown. Furthermore, we also show the flow time dependence of the parts of EMT including the dynamical fermions. This work is based on a joint-collaboration between FlowQCD and WHOT QCD.

# (2+1)-flavor QCD Thermodynamics from the Gradient Flow

## Key claim

The gradient flow method can define and calculate the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) on the lattice for both quenched and (2+1)-flavor QCD, but the fermionic contributions require careful handling of renormalization factors and the results for full QCD remain preliminary with only 11 gauge configurations.

## Method

The authors generate gauge configurations on a space-time lattice, then solve the Yang-Mills gradient flow for gauge fields and the adjoint fermion flow for quark fields to obtain flowed fields at positive flow time t. They construct the flowed operators U_{\mu\nu}(t,x) and E(t,x) for the gauge part, and t_{\mu\nu}(t) and s(t) for the fermion part, then perform an extrapolation toward (a,t) = (0,0) within a fiducial window where a << sqrt(8t) << R, with R being an infrared cutoff scale. The correctly-normalized conserved EMT is obtained from the small flow-time expansion using perturbative coefficients in the MS-bar scheme.

## Key numbers

- For quenched QCD at T = 1.65 T_c with N_tau = 8: statistical error 2.5% and systematic error 0.11% for Delta/T^4; statistical error 0.83% and systematic error 4.4% for s/T^3 at sqrt(8t)T = 0.40
- Quenched QCD simulations used N_s^3 x N_tau = 32^3 x (6, 8, 10) lattices with beta values ranging from 5.89 to 6.56, corresponding to T/T_c = 0.99, 1.24, and 1.65
- The fiducial window lower limit is sqrt(8t_min) = 2a and upper limit is sqrt(8t_max) = 1/(2T) = N_tau a/2
- For (2+1)-flavor QCD: beta = 1.973, c_SW = 1.669, kappa_{u,d} = 0.1361, kappa_s = 0.1354 on 32^3 x 8 lattices at estimated temperature 280 MeV
- Only 11 gauge configurations were used for the full QCD results, with 100 Monte Carlo trajectories between measurements

## Context

This work extends the gradient flow method for EMT proposed by Asakawa (2013) from quenched QCD to (2+1)-flavor QCD with dynamical fermions. The quenched results are compared with the integral method results of Boyd (1996), Okamoto (1999), and Borsanyi (2012), showing consistency within statistical errors. The full QCD results are preliminary and require more configurations for precise determination, but the method shows promise for direct measurement of bulk thermodynamic quantities including the trace anomaly and entropy density.
