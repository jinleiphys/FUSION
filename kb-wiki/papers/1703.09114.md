# Cumulants of event-by-event net-strangeness distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$=7.7-200 GeV from UrQMD model

> Fluctuations of conserved quantities, such as baryon, electric charge and strangeness number, are sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions to search for the QCD phase transition and critical point. In this paper, we performed a systematical analysis on the various cumulants and cumulant ratios of event-by-event net-strangeness distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV from UrQMD model. We performed a systematical study on the contributions from various strange baryons and mesons to the net-strangeness fluctuations. The results demonstrate that the cumulants and cumulant ratios of net-strangeness distributions extracted from different strange particles show very different centrality and energy dependence behavior. By comparing with the net-kaon fluctuations, we found that the strange baryons play an important role in the fluctuations of net-strangeness. This study can provide useful baselines to study the QCD phase transition and search for the QCD critical point by using the fluctuations of net-strangeness in heavy-ion collisions experiment. It can help us to understand non-critical physics contributions to the fluctuations of net-strangeness.

## Key claim
The UrQMD model calculations show that net-kaon fluctuations are not a perfect proxy for net-strangeness fluctuations, because multi-strange baryons contribute significantly to the higher-order cumulants and cause the kurtosis-times-variance product to exceed unity, especially at lower collision energies.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 2.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. They calculated the first four cumulants (C1 through C4) and the cumulant ratios kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma of the event-by-event net-strangeness distribution, considering four different sets of strange hadrons: (1) only charged kaons, (2) kaons plus Lambda baryons, (3) kaons plus Lambda, Sigma, Xi, and Omega baryons, and (4) charged and neutral kaons plus all those baryons. The centrality dependence was studied using participant number bins, and the pseudo-rapidity acceptance dependence was also examined.

## Key numbers
- The fraction of total strangeness carried by charged kaons (K+ and K-) at mid-rapidity in 0-5% central collisions is about 45% at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, and decreases monotonically with decreasing collision energy.
- The even-order cumulants C2 and C4 of net-strangeness increase monotonically from peripheral to central collisions at all seven energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV).
- For the case including only kaons and Lambda baryons, the kappa*sigma^2 values are consistent with unity within errors across centralities and energies.
- When multi-strange baryons (Xi, Omega) are included, the kappa*sigma^2 values rise above unity, reaching values greater than 1.0.
- The odd-order cumulants C1 and C3 of net-strangeness from the full set (K+K0+Lambda+Sigma+Xi+Omega) are close to zero at all energies, while the net-kaon C1 and C3 decrease with increasing collision energy.
- The S*sigma values for net-kaon distributions decrease with increasing collision energy, while those from the full strange-particle set are negative at lower energies.

## Context
This work extends the study of conserved-quantity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which are sensitive to the QCD phase transition and critical point as predicted by Stephanov (2004) and Fodor (2004). It directly compares with the STAR experiment's measurements of net-kaon cumulants reported by Xu (2016) and Thader (2016), showing that net-kaon fluctuations alone miss contributions from strange baryons. The results provide a hadronic baseline from the UrQMD model (Bleicher, 1999) that contains no critical-point physics, enabling future experimental searches for non-monotonic signals by distinguishing non-critical from critical contributions.
