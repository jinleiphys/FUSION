# Virtual states of light non-Borromean halo nuclei

> It is shown that the three-body non-Borromean halo nuclei like ^{12}Be, ^{18}C, ^{20}C have p-wave virtual states with energy of about 1.6 times the corresponding neutron-core binding energy. We use a renormalizable model that guarantees the general validity of our results in the context of short range interactions.

## Key claim

The authors show that the non-Borromean halo nuclei \({}^{12}\mathrm{Be}\), \({}^{18}\mathrm{C}\), and \({}^{20}\mathrm{C}\) possess a \(p\)-wave virtual state (a "pygmy dipole state") whose energy is approximately 1.6 times the corresponding neutron-core binding energy, but this result depends on the assumption that the \(n\)-\(n\) pairing is inoperative in the \(p\)-wave state and that the zero-range model is analytically continued to the second energy sheet through the elastic cut.

## Method

The authors use a renormalizable zero-range three-body model with \(s\)-wave interactions between the two identical neutrons and between each neutron and the core, solving the coupled Faddeev-type integral equations for the \(p\)-wave three-body state. The equations are analytically continued to the second energy sheet through the elastic scattering cut of the neutron on the bound neutron-core subsystem, and the virtual state energy is found as the pole of the resulting homogeneous equations. The only input scales are the neutron-core binding energy \(E_{nc}\) and the neutron-neutron virtual state energy \(E_{nn}=143\) keV, with the core mass number \(A_c\) varied to represent different halo nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{12}\mathrm{Be}\) (\(A_c=10\), \(E_{nc}=504\) keV), the \(p\)-wave virtual state energy is \(E_v = 813.65\) keV, giving \(B_v(A_c+2)/(E_{nc}A_c)=0.7373\).
* For \({}^{18}\mathrm{C}\) (\(A_c=16\), \(E_{nc}=729\) keV), the \(p\)-wave virtual state energy is \(E_v = 1227.54\) keV, giving \(B_v(A_c+2)/(E_{nc}A_c)=0.7694\).
* For \({}^{20}\mathrm{C}\) (\(A_c=18\), \(E_{nc}=162\) keV), the \(p\)-wave virtual state energy is \(E_v = 274.36\) keV, giving \(B_v(A_c+2)/(E_{nc}A_c)=0.7706\).
* For \({}^{20}\mathrm{C}\) using the alternative \(E_{nc}=530\) keV from Nakamura et al. (1999), the virtual state energy is \(E_v = 900.30\) keV, giving \(B_v(A_c+2)/(E_{nc}A_c)=0.7763\).
* The scaling function \(B_v(A_c+2)/(E_{nc}A_c)\) varies by only a factor of about three when \(A_c\) is varied between 0.001 and 1000, and is roughly independent of the ratio \(E_{nc}/E_{nn}\) over the range 0.01 to 1000.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal three-body phenomena in halo nuclei, following the suggestion by Fedorov and Jensen (1993) that \({}^{18}\mathrm{C}\) and \({}^{20}\mathrm{C}\) could host Efimov states, and the critical-condition analysis by Amorim, Tomio, and Frederico (1997) which concluded that \({}^{20}\mathrm{C}\) might have one Efimov state. The present paper shows that these non-Borromean systems instead exhibit a \(p\)-wave virtual state (a "pygmy dipole resonance") whose energy is determined solely by two-body scales, in contrast to the \(s\)-wave Efimov states which require a three-body input. The results enable experimental searches for these threshold-dominated excited states in light halo nuclei and provide a universal scaling prediction that can be tested against future measurements of neutron-core binding energies.
