# Process-independent effective coupling and the pion structure function

> We sketch the calculation of the pion structure functions within the DSE framework, following two alternative albeit consistent approaches, and discuss then their QCD evolution, the running driven by an effective charge, from a hadronic scale up to any larger one accessible to experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a process-independent effective strong coupling, derived from QCD's gauge sector, can be used to drive the DGLAP evolution of the pion's parton distribution function from a nonperturbative hadronic scale up to experimental scales, yielding a parameter-free prediction consistent with reanalysed E615 data, but only if one accounts for symmetry-preserving corrections to the handbag diagram and uses an appropriate effective charge that freezes in the infrared.

## Method

The authors compute the pion's valence-quark parton distribution function at a hadronic scale using two consistent Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approaches: one based on Mellin moments of the parton distribution obtained from the dressed quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, reconstructed via the Schlessinger point method; the other using an overlap representation of the light-front wave function derived from the Bethe-Salpeter wave function. They then evolve this distribution to the experimental scale ζ₅ = 5.2 GeV using leading-order DGLAP evolution equations, but with the strong running coupling replaced by a process-independent effective charge that matches the DSE interaction kernel in the infrared and connects smoothly to perturbative QCD in the ultraviolet.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale is set to ζ_H = m_α = 300 MeV, where m_α is the nonperturbative scale screening soft gluon modes, with Λ_QCD = 234 MeV.
- The E615 experiment scale is ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV, at which the reanalysed data (Wijesooriya 2005, Aicher 2010) are compared to the evolved prediction.
- For the m=1 Mellin moment (momentum fraction) with N_f=4, the singlet quark momentum fraction evolves as ⟨x_q⟩^S_ζ = 3/7 + (4/7) exp[-(56/36π) ∫ dt α(t)], and the gluon momentum fraction as ⟨x_G⟩^S_ζ = 4/7 [1 - exp(-(56/36π) ∫ dt α(t))], with asymptotic limits 3/7 and 4/7 respectively.
- The valence-quark distribution at the hadronic scale satisfies ∫₀¹ dx q^π(x;ζ_H) = 1 and exhibits the symmetry q^π(x;ζ_H) = q^π(1-x;ζ_H).

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based calculation of pion structure functions by Chang (2014) and Ding (2019), which identified symmetry-preserving corrections beyond the impulse approximation and provided realistic numerical solutions for the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. It resolves a long controversy: leading-order perturbative QCD analysis of the E615 Drell-Yan data (Conway 1989) suggested q^π(x) ∼ (1-x) near x→1, contradicting the parton model prediction of (1-x)²; subsequent reanalyses including soft-gluon resummation (Wijesooriya 2005, Aicher 2010) and continuum calculations (Hecht 2000, Chang 2014, Ding 2019) support the (1-x)²+γ behaviour. The process-independent effective charge, introduced by Binosi (2016) and improved by Rodriguez-Quintero (2018), enables unambiguous evolution from the nonperturbative scale and can be extended to spin-dependent structure functions and generalised parton distributions beyond the forward limit.
