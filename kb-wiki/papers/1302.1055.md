# On the measurement of $χ_2(^3P_2)$ quarkonium state in the processes $e^++e^-\to \bar p+p$ and $\bar p+p\to e^++e^-$

> The intermediate quarkonium state $χ_2(^3P_2)$ in electron-positron annihilation to proton and antiproton as well as in antiproton-proton annihilation to electron and positron can produce backward-forward asymmetry, when populated through two photon exchange. We use the dispersion relation method, which permits to express the asymmetry in terms of partial widths of quarkonium decay. The asymmetry dependence on the center of mass energy in the range near the resonance is presented. The comparison with a similar effect in these reactions with the neutral $Z$-boson in the intermediate state is given. We show that these effects are $\le 10^{-3}$. The main source of asymmetry is of pure QED origin ($\sim 10^{-2}$) which arises from the interference between initial and final state real photon emission.

## Key claim

The backward-forward asymmetry arising from the intermediate $\chi_2(^3P_2)$ quarkonium state in $e^+e^-\to \bar p p$ and $\bar p p\to e^+e^-$ is at most $\sim 10^{-4}$, contrary to a previous claim by Zhou (2011) that it could reach 40%; the dominant asymmetry source is pure QED at the $10^{-2}$ level.

## Method

The authors used a dispersion relation method to calculate the imaginary part of the $\chi_2$ conversion amplitude into electron-positron and proton-antiproton pairs via two-photon and two-gluon intermediate states, then restored the real part through dispersion integrals. The asymmetry was evaluated in the partial width approximation for the $\chi_2$ resonance, with the $\chi_2\to p\bar p$ vertex modeled by two-gluon exchange and the $\chi_2\to e^+e^-$ vertex by two-photon exchange, using experimentally known partial widths from Beringer (2012).

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry from $\chi_2$ resonance at $\sqrt{s}=M_{\chi}$ is $\A_\chi \approx 5\cdot 10^{-4}$ for $\beta=0.85$ and point-like proton approximation.
- The normalization constant for $\chi_2$ asymmetry is $\A_0 = 1.86 \cdot 10^{-5}$.
- The Z-boson exchange asymmetry at $\sqrt{s}=M_\chi$ is $\A_Z \approx 2.1\cdot 10^{-3}$.
- The pure QED asymmetry is $\sim 10^{-2}$, dominating over all other contributions.
- The $\chi_2$ hadronic width is taken as $\Gamma_{\chi_2\to gg}=1.6$ MeV and the two-photon width as $\Gamma_{\chi_2\to \gamma\gamma}=0.4$ MeV.
- The coupling constants are $g_\gamma = 3.3857 \cdot 10^{-4}$ GeV$^{-1}$ and $g_g = 0.13$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The phase from the two-photon conversion to leptons is $\tan\phi_e = \pi / \ln(M_{\chi_2}^2/m_e^2) = 0.17$.

## Context

This work contradicts the earlier claim by Zhou (2011) that two-photon exchange through the $\chi_2$ resonance could produce a 40% asymmetry in these reactions. The authors attribute the discrepancy to their use of a two-gluon intermediate state model for the $\chi_2\to p\bar p$ vertex, which introduces a strong coupling constant $\alpha_s$ and yields small coupling constants due to the narrow resonance width. The calculation extends the QED asymmetry treatment of Kuraev and Meledin and Ahmadov (2010) to the resonance region, and compares with the Z-boson exchange mechanism. The results are relevant for experiments at PANDA (Darmstadt), BES (Beijing), VEPP (Novosibirsk), and DAFNE (Frascati).
