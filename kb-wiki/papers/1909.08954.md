# Inclusive and diffractive dijet photoproduction in UPCs at the LHC in NLO QCD

> We present a next-to-leading order QCD calculation of inclusive dijet photoproduction in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC and show that the results agree very well with various kinematic distributions measured by the ATLAS collaboration. The effect of including these data in nCTEQ or EPPS16 nuclear parton density functions (nPDFs) is then studied using the Bayesian reweighting technique. For an assumed total error of 5\% on the final data, its inclusion would lead to a significant reduction of the nPDF uncertainties of up to a factor of two at small values of the parton momentum fraction. As an outlook, we discuss future analyes of diffractive nPDFs, which are so far completely unknown.

# Inclusive and diffractive dijet photoproduction in UPCs at the LHC in NLO QCD

## Key claim
Next-to-leading order QCD calculations of inclusive dijet photoproduction in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions agree very well with preliminary ATLAS kinematic distributions, but the data have not yet been unfolded for detector response. Inclusion of final data with an assumed 5% total uncertainty would reduce nuclear parton density function uncertainties by up to a factor of two at small parton momentum fractions, contingent on the Bayesian reweighting technique and the assumed precision.

## Method
The authors computed inclusive dijet photoproduction cross sections in NLO QCD for ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, using the photon flux from the relativistic pointlike charge formula with a minimum impact parameter of 14.2 fm, GRV NLO photon PDFs, and nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs. They compared their calculations to ATLAS data for distributions in total transverse energy and parton momentum fraction in the lead ion, then performed Bayesian reweighting with 10,000 replicas of nCTEQ15 and EPPS16 nuclear PDFs to assess the impact of hypothetical final data at various assumed precision levels. For diffractive dijet photoproduction, they applied the leading-twist model of nuclear shadowing based on Gribov-Glauber theory and HERA diffractive data.

## Key numbers
- Minimum impact parameter for Pb-Pb UPCs: b_min = 2.1 R_Pb = 14.2 fm
- Jet transverse energy cuts: E_T,1 > 20 GeV, E_T,2 > 15 GeV, H_T > 35 GeV
- Jet rapidity acceptance: |eta_1,2| < 4.4
- Combined jet mass cut: m_jets > 35 GeV
- For assumed data precision epsilon = 0.05, effective number of contributing replicas: N_eff = 4407 (nCTEQ15), 3982 (nCTEQ15np), 5982 (EPPS16)
- For assumed data precision epsilon = 0.2, effective number of contributing replicas: N_eff = 9464 (nCTEQ15), 9607 (nCTEQ15np), 9818 (EPPS16)
- Diffractive nuclear PDF shadowing suppression factor: approximately 0.15 relative to impulse approximation
- Center-of-mass energy for diffractive predictions: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV

## Context
This work extends previous NLO QCD calculations of inclusive jet and dijet photoproduction in ep collisions at HERA (Klasen 1994, 1995, 1997) to ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. It directly addresses the need for improved nuclear PDF constraints identified in the nCTEQ15 analysis (Kovarik 2015) and EPPS16 analysis (Eskola 2016), particularly for gluons at small x. The diffractive predictions build on the leading-twist model of nuclear shadowing (Frankfurt 2011) and HERA studies of diffractive dijet photoproduction (Klasen 2004, Guzey 2016), and enable future extraction of completely unknown diffractive nuclear PDFs as well as studies of QCD factorization breaking in nuclear collisions.
