# Effects of Pauli blocking and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections on the stopping power at low-intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> Three typical algorithms of Pauli blocking in the quantum molecular dynamics type models are investigated in the nuclear matter, the nucleus and the heavy ion collisions. The calculations in nuclear matter show that the blocking ratios obtained with the three algorithms are underestimated 13-25\% compared to the analytical values of blocking ratios. For the finite nucleus, the spurious collisions occur around the surface of the nucleus owing to the defects of Pauli blocking algorithms. In the simulations of heavy ion collisions, the uncertainty of stopping power from different Pauli blocking algorithms is less than 5\%. Furthermore, the in-medium effects of nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) cross sections on the nuclear stopping power are discussed. Our results show that the transport models calculations with free $NN$ cross sections result in the stopping power decreasing with the beam energy at the beam energy less than 300 MeV/u. To increase or decrease the values of stopping power, an enhanced or suppressed model dependent in-medium $NN$ cross section is required.

## Key claim

The three typical Pauli blocking algorithms used in quantum molecular dynamics models underestimate the blocking ratio by 13-25% compared to analytical values in nuclear matter, but the resulting uncertainty in the stopping power observable for heavy ion collisions is less than 5%.

## Method

The authors implemented three Pauli blocking algorithms (PB-Wigner, PB-Husimi, and PB-HSP) within the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model and compared their performance in nuclear matter box calculations, finite nucleus simulations of 124Sn, and heavy ion collisions. The nuclear matter calculations used periodic boundary conditions with density 0.16 fm-3, temperature range 2-10 MeV, and a constant nucleon-nucleon cross section of 40 mb to isolate the Pauli blocking effects from other model ingredients.

## Key numbers

- Blocking ratios from the three algorithms are underestimated by 13-25% compared to analytical values from the Fermi-Dirac distribution (PB-FD) in nuclear matter calculations
- At T=10 MeV, the ratio of effective in-medium cross section to true cross section is approximately 37-50% for the three algorithms
- The uncertainty in stopping power from different Pauli blocking algorithms is less than 5% in heavy ion collision simulations
- The successful collision rate in 124Sn at 1 fm/c is about 0.9-1.6 c/fm, with most spurious collisions occurring near the nuclear surface at r=5.2 fm
- Free nucleon-nucleon cross sections used in the model are set to 60 mb for nn/pp and 180 mb for np at lab momenta below 0.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of transport codes initiated by Zhang (2018) and Xu (2016) by specifically quantifying how different Pauli blocking algorithms affect the stopping power observable used to extract in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The results show that the extracted in-medium cross sections from QMD models are systematically smaller than true values due to underestimated Pauli blocking, and that improved algorithms are needed before comparing model calculations to experimental data for extracting real in-medium cross sections.
