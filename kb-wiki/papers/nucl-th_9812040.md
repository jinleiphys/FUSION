# Time-scale of breakup of the halo nucleus 11Be

> We investigate the post-acceleration of the $^{10}$Be fragment in Coulomb breakup reaction of the halo nucleus $^{11}$Be, assuming that the excitation of the projectile is to states in the low energy continuum. The method used retains all finite-range effects associated with the interactions between the breakup fragments and can use realistic wave functions for the halo nuclei. We apply the method to compute the average momenta of the $^{10}$Be fragment at four different scattering angles following breakup of $^{11}$Be on a Au target at 42 MeV/nucleon beam energy. Our results, which are in qualitative agreement with recent precision experimental data, are consistent with the picture of no post-acceleration effect. The implication is that the breakup takes place on a time-scale long compared to the collision time.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the average momentum of the \(^{10}\)Be fragment from Coulomb breakup of \(^{11}\)Be on a Au target at 42 MeV/nucleon shows no post-acceleration effect, implying the breakup occurs on a time-scale long compared to the collision time. This conclusion is qualified by the use of an approximate quantum mechanical model that assumes only the charged core interacts with the target via a point Coulomb interaction and that the important projectile excitations are to the low-energy continuum, treated adiabatically.

## Method

The author uses a fully quantum mechanical, non-perturbative adiabatic model of elastic Coulomb breakup that retains all finite-range effects associated with interactions between the breakup fragments. The transition amplitude separates into a vertex function containing the projectile structure information and a bremsstrahlung integral describing the reaction dynamics. The method is applied to compute the three-dimensional momentum distribution \(d^2\sigma / dp_c d\Omega_c\) of the \(^{10}\)Be core at four laboratory scattering angles (0.64°, 1.50°, 2.88°, and 4.39°) for breakup of \(^{11}\)Be on a Au target at 42 MeV/nucleon beam energy, assuming a 1\(s_{1/2}\) neutron configuration with separation energy 0.504 MeV and a Woods-Saxon binding potential.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 42 MeV/nucleon (experimental value 41.71 MeV/nucleon), corresponding to a beam velocity momentum of approximately 2806 MeV/c.
- Neutron separation energy in \(^{11}\)Be: 0.504 MeV.
- Four scattering angles measured: 0.64°, 1.50°, 2.88°, and 4.39°.
- The calculated average momenta of \(^{10}\)Be come around the beam velocity momentum value (approximately 2806 MeV/c), showing no post-acceleration.
- The experimentally deduced mean momenta are somewhat larger than 2797 MeV/c, which is the beam velocity momentum corresponding to 41.71 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical debate on post-acceleration in halo nucleus breakup, which has been controversial with conflicting results from different experiments and calculations. The author's results agree with the experimental data from Bush et al. (1998) at MSU and with earlier theoretical work by Banerjee and Shyam (1993) using post-form DWBA Coulomb breakup, as well as with the adiabatic model calculations of Banerjee, Thompson, and Tostevin (1998). The results contradict the classical calculations of Baur, Bertulani, and Kalassa (1992) which predict post-acceleration, and disagree with the time-dependent Schrödinger calculation of Kido, Yabana, and Suzuki (1994). The findings support the argument of Anne et al. (1994) that post-acceleration effects should be small because the collision time is much less than the characteristic disintegration time of the halo.
