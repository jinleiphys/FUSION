# Light-Front Dynamics

> The wave function in relativity is defined, in four-dimensional space, on a space-like three-dimensional plane. The plane, most close to the time-like region, is the light-front plane $ct+z=0$. Corresponding dynamical approach - the light-front dynamics - has considerable advantages. We describe, in a field-theoretical framework, the construction of light-front dynamics and illustrate it by some examples.

# Light-Front Dynamics

## Key claim

The light-front dynamics (LFD) approach provides a relativistic framework in which the vacuum state is "nothing but emptiness", the bare vacuum is also an eigenstate of the full Hamiltonian, but this property relies on defining the wave function on a light-front plane (e.g., $ct+z=0$) rather than on an equal-time plane, and the formalism requires careful handling of singularities on the light cone to maintain equivalence with standard field theory.

## Method

The paper constructs LFD within a field-theoretical framework by defining the state vector on a space-like plane $\omega \cdot x = \sigma$ where $\omega^2=0$, generalizing the standard light-front plane $t+z=0$. The S-matrix is derived from the time-dependent Schrödinger equation in the interaction representation, with time-ordering replaced by ordering in the light-front time $\sigma = \omega \cdot x$, and the perturbation theory is developed through a graph technique that introduces a fictitious "spurion" particle (with momentum $\omega\tau$) to account for off-energy-shell effects. The rules for constructing amplitudes involve labeling vertices, orienting particle lines, and integrating over spurion momenta, with vacuum vertices excluded because they cannot satisfy four-momentum conservation on the light front.

## Key numbers

- The light-front plane is defined by the equation $ct+z=0$ (or equivalently $\omega \cdot x = 0$ with $\omega^2=0$).
- In the front form, only three generators ($P_-$, $J_{1-}$, $J_{2-}$) contain the interaction; the other seven generators ($P_1$, $P_2$, $P_+$, $J_{12}$, $J_{-+}$, $J_{1+}$, $J_{2+}$) are free.
- For a free particle, the light-front energy-momentum relation is $p_- = (\vec{p}_\perp^2 + m^2)/p_+$, which contains no square root.
- The vacuum vertex conservation law $(k_1 + k_2 + k_3)^2 \geq 8m^2$ cannot be satisfied when the right-hand side is zero (since $\omega^2=0$), causing vacuum fluctuations to disappear.

## Context

This work extends Dirac's (1949) classification of three forms of relativistic dynamics (instant, point, and front forms) by developing the field-theoretical formulation of the front form. It builds on the old-fashioned perturbation theory formalism of Kadyshevsky (1964, 1968) and the explicitly covariant LFD version proposed by Karmanov (1976). The approach enables the study of relativistic composite systems (hadrons in quark models, nuclei at relativistic momenta) without the complication of vacuum fluctuations that plague instant-form calculations, and it provides a foundation for light-front QCD applications as pursued by Wilson and others.
