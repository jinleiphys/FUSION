# Parity-Projected Shell Model Monte Carlo Level Densities for fp-shell Nuclei

> We calculate parity-dependent level densities for the even-even isotopes 58,62,66 Fe and 58 Ni and the odd-A nuclei 59 Ni and 65 Fe using the Shell Model Monte Carlo method. We perform these calculations in the complete fp-gds shell-model space using a pairing+quadrupole residual interaction. We find that, due to pairing of identical nucleons, the low-energy spectrum is dominated by positive parity states. Although these pairs break at around the same excitation energy in all nuclei, the energy dependence of the ratio of negative-to-positive parity level densities depends strongly on the particular nucleus of interest. We find equilibration of both parities at noticeably lower excitation energies for the odd-A nuclei 59 Ni and 65 Fe than for the neighboring even-even nuclei 58 Ni and 66 Fe.

## Key claim

The authors find that parity equilibration in nuclear level densities depends strongly on the specific nucleus and its single-particle structure, not simply on pair-breaking energy; for even-even nuclei, positive parity dominates at low energies due to pairing, but the excitation energy at which negative and positive parity densities become equal varies from about 6 MeV in 62,66Fe to 11-12 MeV in 58Fe and up to 16 MeV in 58Ni.

## Method

The authors performed Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in the complete fp-gds model space (50 valence orbitals for both protons and neutrons) using a pairing+quadrupole residual interaction. They computed parity-projected level densities by evaluating parity-projected partition functions via the saddle-point approximation, extracting the parity-projected energy excitation functions from the thermal averages of the Hamiltonian with parity projection operators.

## Key numbers

- Parity equilibration in 58Fe occurs at excitation energy E ≈ 11-12 MeV
- Parity equilibration in 62,66Fe occurs around E ≈ 6 MeV
- Parity equilibration in 58Ni occurs at about Ex = 16 MeV
- Parity equilibration in 59Ni occurs at excitation energies around 13 MeV
- In 65Fe, equal level densities for both parities are found down to Ex ≈ 1.5 MeV
- For 58Fe, average g9/2 neutron occupation increases from 0.2 in the ground state to 0.3 at T=1 MeV (E ∼ 6 MeV) to 1.0 at T=2 MeV (E ∼ 23 MeV)
- For 62Fe, g9/2 occupation is about 1.0 in the ground state, growing to 2.1 at T=2 MeV
- For 66Fe, g9/2 occupation is about 3.0 in the ground state, reaching about 3.6 at T=2 MeV
- Pairing strengths in iron isotopes are reduced to about half of ground-state values around T=0.8 MeV (E ≈ 4 MeV)
- The difference in total level densities between 58Fe and 58Ni at modest excitation energies can be approximately accounted for by a constant energy shift of about 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous SMMC studies of level densities by Ormand, Nakada and Alhassid (1997), and Langanke (1998) to include parity projection for both even-even and odd-A nuclei in the fp-shell region. The results contradict the common assumption in astrophysical applications (using the backshifted Fermi gas model of Gilbert and Cameron) that parity equilibration occurs at the energies of interest for r-process nucleosynthesis, showing instead that parity dependence persists to significantly higher excitation energies in many nuclei. The calculations enable improved nuclear reaction rate estimates for astrophysical nucleosynthesis processes and provide a microscopic benchmark for simpler models such as the independent particle model with BCS pairing treatment described by Bertsch.
