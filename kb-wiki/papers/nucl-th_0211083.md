# Important experimental observables at RHIC

> In this talk I discuss the significance of the first RHIC measurements for establishing the properties of hot and dense QCD matter and for understanding the dynamics of the theory at the high parton density, strong color field frontier. Hopes and expectations for the future are discussed as well.

# Important experimental observables at RHIC

## Key claim
The first RHIC measurements provide experimental proof of a high degree of coherence in multi-particle production in nuclear collisions, challenging the assumption that these collisions are an incoherent superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon interactions; however, the author cautions that a definitive separation of initial-state versus final-state effects (such as jet energy loss versus parton saturation) requires forthcoming measurements in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions.

## Method
The paper reviews global observables (charged particle multiplicity, centrality dependence, rapidity distributions, azimuthal distributions) and hard processes (high transverse momentum particle suppression, azimuthal correlations, baryon-to-pion ratio, charm production) from the first RHIC Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies of 20, 130, and 200 GeV. The analysis compares experimental data to predictions from the Glauber model, the AGK cutting rules, parton saturation models, hydrodynamical calculations, and perturbative QCD approaches.

## Key numbers
- Charged particle multiplicity per participant pair in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV exceeds the nucleon-nucleon multiplicity by only about 50%, whereas independent collision scaling would predict a factor of 3 (using approximately 1050 collisions and 340 participants for a 0-6% centrality cut).
- The antiproton-to-proton ratio at RHIC energies is about 0.65, indicating baryon number diffusion to Bjorken x ~ 10^{-2}.
- The elliptic flow coefficient v2 reaches about 35% for peripheral collisions (small n_ch/n_max) at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV.
- The initial energy density inferred from particle multiplicities and transverse momentum spectra is about 20 GeV/fm^3.
- Charm production cross section scales with the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions within error bars, in contrast to light hadron production which shows strong nuclear effects.

## Context
This invited talk at Quark Matter 2002 reviews the first results from RHIC, extending earlier measurements at the CERN SPS (Pb-Pb collisions) and CERN ISR (alpha-alpha collisions) to much higher energies. The paper contrasts the data with predictions from the AGK cutting rules (Abramovsky, Gribov, Kancheli) and the Glauber model, and discusses the parton saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin; Mueller; Qiu; McLerran, Venugopalan). The results set the stage for future proton(deuteron)-nucleus runs needed to disentangle initial-state coherence from final-state thermalization effects, and for improved measurements of charmonium production and heavy flavor energy loss.
