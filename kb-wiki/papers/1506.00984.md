# Hadron-Quark Crossover and Hot Neutron Stars at Birth

> We construct a new isentropic equation of state (EOS) at finite temperature "CRover" on the basis of the hadron-quark crossover at high density. By using the new EOS, we study the structure of hot neutron stars at birth with the typical lepton fraction ($Y_l=0.3-0.4$) and the typical entropy per baryon ($S=1-2$). Due to the gradual appearance of quark degrees of freedom at high density, the temperature T and the baryon density at the center of the hot neutron stars with the hadron-quark crossover are found to be smaller than those without the crossover by a factor of 2 or more. Typical energy release due to the contraction of a hot neutron star to a cold neutron star with 1.4 solarmass is shown to be about 0.04 solarmass with the spin-up rate about 14%.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark crossover EOS "CRover" predicts that hot neutron stars at birth have central temperatures and densities smaller by a factor of 2 or more compared to models without crossover, for typical lepton fractions (Y_l=0.3-0.4) and entropies per baryon (S=1-2). This suppression arises from the gradual appearance of quark degrees of freedom under isentropic conditions.

## Method

The authors construct a finite-temperature equation of state by interpolating the Helmholtz free energy per baryon between a hadron+lepton EOS (from finite-temperature Hartree-Fock calculations with the TNI2 interaction) and a quark+lepton EOS (from the (2+1)-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) using a hyperbolic tangent weight function centered at 3 times nuclear saturation density. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for spherically symmetric stars under isentropic conditions with fixed lepton fraction and entropy per baryon, converting from isothermal to isentropic matter via the T-rho relationship constrained by constant entropy.

## Key numbers

- Crossover density: 3 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.17/fm^3 (normal nuclear matter density)
- Crossover width: rho_0
- Maximum mass of cold neutron star with hadron-quark crossover: 2.6 M_sun (using g_V/G_S = 0.5)
- Maximum mass of cold NS with TNI2-EOS alone (no crossover): 1.63 M_sun
- Energy release for contraction from hot to cold 1.4 M_sun neutron star: about 0.04 M_sun
- Spin-up rate for contraction from hot to cold 1.4 M_sun neutron star: about 14%
- Nuclear incompressibility used: kappa = 250 MeV
- UV cutoff in NJL model: 631.4 MeV
- Current quark masses in NJL model: m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cold neutron star studies (Masuda 2012) by generalizing the hadron-quark crossover to finite temperature, enabling study of proto-neutron stars at birth. It contrasts with earlier hadron-quark mixed phase approaches (Prakash 1995, Nakazato 2008, Pagliara, Chen 2013) which produce soft EOSs unable to sustain 2 M_sun neutron stars, whereas the crossover approach yields stiff EOSs consistent with observed massive neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The new EOS "CRover" enables future studies of neutron star cooling, contraction, and spin evolution during the first tens of seconds after birth, and the authors plan to post numerical tables in the EOS database (Ishizuka 2014).
