# Effective Chiral Theory for Radiative Decays of Mesons

> An extended $U(3)_L\bigotimes U(3)_R$ chiral theory which includes pseudoscalar and vector meson nonets as dynamic degrees of freedom is presented. We combine a hidden symmetry approach with a general procedure of including the $η'$ meson into chiral theory. The $U(3)_L\bigotimes U(3)_R$ and the SU(3) symmetries are broken by the mechanism based on quark mass matrix. Meson radiative decay widths are parameterized in terms of a single and real $η-η'$ mixing angle $θ_P$, a U(3)_V symmetry breaking scale parameter c_W, and the radiative decay constants $F_π, F_K, F_η, F_{η'}$, for the $π, η$, K, $η'$ mesons, respectively. Taking $F_π= 93 MeV$, a global fit to decay width data yields F_K/F_π= 1.16 \pm 0.11, F_η/F_π= 1.14\pm 0.04, F_{η'}/F_π= 1.09 \pm 0.04, c_W = -0.19 \pm0.03, θ_P =-(15.4 \pm 1.8)^o.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extended $U(3)_L\bigotimes U(3)_R$ chiral effective theory that includes both pseudoscalar and vector meson nonets as dynamical degrees of freedom, parameterizing radiative decay widths in terms of a single real $\eta-\eta'$ mixing angle $\theta_P$, a $U(3)_V$ symmetry breaking scale parameter $c_W$, and the radiative decay constants $F_\pi, F_K, F_\eta, F_{\eta'}$; the abstract glosses over the condition that the best fit requires both direct ($c_W \neq 0$) and indirect ($F_K \neq F_\pi$) symmetry breaking to be included simultaneously.

## Method

The authors combined the hidden symmetry approach of Bando et al. with a general procedure for including the $\eta'$ meson into chiral perturbation theory, constructing a most general lowest-order effective Lagrangian with $U(3)_L\bigotimes U(3)_R$ local symmetry. Symmetry breaking was introduced via a universal matrix $B$ proportional to the pseudoscalar meson mass matrix, and the Lagrangian was applied to calculate decay widths for anomalous processes $V^0 \to P^0 \gamma$, $P^0 \to V^0 \gamma$, and $P^0 \to \gamma\gamma$ with $P^0 = \pi, \eta, \eta'$ and $V^0 = \rho, \omega, \phi$. The five free parameters ($c_W$, $F_K/F_\pi$, $F_\eta/F_\pi$, $F_{\eta'}/F_\pi$, $\theta_P$) were fixed by fitting to experimental decay rates, using the $\omega \to \pi\gamma$ decay to determine $g_V$ and the $\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma$ decay to determine $g_P$.

## Key numbers

- $F_K/F_\pi = 1.16 \pm 0.11$ from a global fit to decay width data, with $F_\pi = 93$ MeV fixed
- $F_\eta/F_\pi = 1.14 \pm 0.04$ from the global fit
- $F_{\eta'}/F_\pi = 1.09 \pm 0.04$ from the global fit
- $c_W = -0.19 \pm 0.03$ from the global fit (solution II)
- $\theta_P = -(15.4 \pm 1.8)^\circ$ from the global fit (solution II)
- $G = (1.44 \pm 0.04) \times 10^{-5}$ and $g_V = 0.22 \pm 0.006$ from the $\omega \to \pi\gamma$ decay width of $(716 \pm 43)$ keV
- $\bar{G} = (4.9 \pm 0.07) \times 10^{-8}$ and $g_P = 0.073 \pm 0.001$ from the $\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma$ decay width of $(7.8 \pm 0.55)$ eV
- $c_W = -0.19 \pm 0.04$ from the ratio $\Gamma(K^{*0}K^0\gamma)/\Gamma(K^{*+}K^+\gamma) = 2.34 \pm 0.43$, using experimental widths $(117 \pm 10)$ keV and $(50 \pm 5)$ keV

## Context

This work extends the hidden symmetry approach of Bando et al. and the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler by incorporating the $\eta'$ meson as a dynamical variable and including both direct and indirect $SU(3)$ symmetry breaking. The extracted mixing angle $\theta_P = -(15.4 \pm 1.8)^\circ$ agrees with the more recent analysis of Bramon, Escribano and Scadron (1999) who obtained $\theta_P = -(15.5 \pm 1.3)^\circ$, but differs significantly from earlier values of $-19.5^\circ$ (Bramon et al., 1995) and $-(22 \pm 3.3)^\circ$ (Venugopal et al., 1998). The results enable precision tests of $U(3)$ symmetry breaking at facilities like DA$\Phi$NE through measurements of ratios such as $\Gamma(\eta'\gamma\gamma)/\Gamma(\eta\gamma\gamma)$ and $\Gamma(\phi\eta'\gamma)/\Gamma(\phi\eta\gamma)$.
