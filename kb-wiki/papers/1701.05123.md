# Effect of pre-equilibrium phase on $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ of heavy quarks in heavy ion collisions

> Heavy quark $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ have been calculated at RHIC energy considering initial conditions with and without pre-equilibrium phase to highlight the effect of the latter on heavy quark observables. The momentum evolution of the heavy quark has been studied by means of the Boltzmann transport equation. To model the pre-equilibrium phase we have used the KLN initial condition. We have found that the pre-equilibrium phase impacts the $R_{AA}$ of about 20-25 $\%$ whereas the impact on $v_2$ is very negligible. We have also calculated heavy quark $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ in the case of initializations with early thermalized quark-gluon plasma. We have checked that the particular form of the initial spectrum is not very important for $R_{AA}$, the larger energy density being more important. In fact, comparing the results obtained within the two initializations we have found that one can mimic the impact of the pre-equilibrium phase using a early thermalized QGP medium.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium phase impacts the heavy quark nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ by about 20-25% at RHIC energy, but the effect on the elliptic flow $v_2$ is negligible; the specific form of the initial spectrum is not important for $R_{AA}$, as the larger energy density in the early stage is the dominant factor, and the impact of the pre-equilibrium phase can be mimicked by an early thermalized QGP medium.

## Method

The momentum evolution of heavy quarks was studied using the Boltzmann transport equation with a pQCD-based elastic interaction (Combridge matrix with Debye screening). The pre-equilibrium phase was modeled using the factorized KLN (fKLN) initial condition with an initial time of 0.2 fm/c, while the standard case used a Glauber model with a thermalized spectrum starting at 0.6 fm/c and an initial central temperature of 340 MeV. Heavy quarks were initialized in momentum space according to the FONLL calculation and in coordinate space according to the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions; hadronization to D-mesons was performed via the Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- The pre-equilibrium phase suppresses $R_{AA}$ by approximately 20-25% compared to the calculation without it at RHIC energy.
- The standard Glauber initial condition used an initial time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c with a maximum initial temperature $T_0 = 340$ MeV at the center of the fireball.
- The KLN initialization used an initial time of 0.2 fm/c.
- The early thermalized QGP case used $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/c with $T_0 = 490$ MeV, keeping the multiplicity the same.
- The $p_T$ integrated $v_2$ values were 0.03320 for the KLN case, 0.03324 for the early thermalized Glauber case, and 0.0384 for the standard Glauber case.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark transport (Das 2015, Rapp 2009, Gossiaux 2009, Cao 2016) by explicitly including the pre-equilibrium phase, which had previously been approximated by free streaming. It builds on the finding by Das (2015) that heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients in the pre-equilibrium phase are comparable to those in a thermalized gluonic system, and on the observation by Ruggieri (2013) that the out-of-equilibrium KLN momentum distribution affects bulk elliptic flow. The results enable future studies at lower collision energies (low-energy RHIC run and FAIR) where the pre-equilibrium lifetime is longer and its effects may be more distinguishable from an early thermalized scenario.
