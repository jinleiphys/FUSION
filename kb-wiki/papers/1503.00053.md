# Neutral current quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering beyond the Fermi gas model at MiniBooNE and BNL kinematics

> Neutral current quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering cross sections on a $^{12}$C target are analyzed using a realistic spectral function $S(p,E)$ that gives a scaling function in accordance with the ($e,e'$) scattering data. The spectral function accounts for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations by using natural orbitals (NOs) from the Jastrow correlation method and has a realistic energy dependence. The standard value of the axial mass $M_A= 1.032$ GeV is used in all calculations. The role of the final-state interaction (FSI) on the spectral and scaling functions, as well as on the cross sections is accounted for. A comparison of the calculations with the empirical data of the MiniBooNE and BNL experiments is performed. Our results are analyzed in comparison with those when NN correlations are not included, and also with results from other theoretical approaches, such as the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), the relativistic mean field (RMF), the relativistic Green's function (RGF), as well as with the SuperScaling Approach (SuSA) based on the analysis of quasielastic electron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a realistic spectral function incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations via natural orbitals from the Jastrow method, combined with final-state interactions, can describe neutral current quasielastic (anti)neutrino scattering on carbon-12, but the agreement with MiniBooNE data is model-dependent and generally requires the standard axial mass value of 1.032 GeV rather than the enhanced values needed in simpler Fermi gas treatments.

## Method

The authors constructed a realistic spectral function for carbon-12 using natural orbital momentum distributions from the Jastrow correlation method with a Lorentzian energy dependence, then computed neutral current quasielastic cross sections within the plane-wave impulse approximation. Final-state interactions were included by replacing the energy-conserving delta function with a Lorentzian whose width and shift were determined from a relativistic optical potential. The resulting scaling functions were used to calculate flux-averaged differential cross sections for both neutrino and antineutrino scattering, which were compared with MiniBooNE and BNL E734 data alongside results from the relativistic Fermi gas, relativistic mean field, relativistic Green's function, and SuperScaling Approach models.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass fixed to the world-average value: M_A = 1.032 GeV in all calculations
- Lorentzian widths for the spectral function: Gamma_1p = 6 MeV, Gamma_1s = 20 MeV for the 1p and 1s states in carbon-12
- Weak mixing angle: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.23122(15)
- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.2695
- Strange magnetic moment: mu_s = -0.020; strange electric radius parameter: rho_s = 0.59; strange axial coupling: g_A^(s) = 0
- MiniBooNE data normalization uncertainty: 18.1% for neutrino case, 19.5% for antineutrino case
- BNL E734 data normalization uncertainty: 11.2% for neutrino case, 10.4% for antineutrino case

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous charged-current quasielastic study (Ivanov et al., 2014) to neutral current scattering, and compares with multiple theoretical approaches including the relativistic Fermi gas model, the relativistic mean field model, the relativistic Green's function model with democratic optical potential, and the SuperScaling Approach (SuSA) of Donnelly et al. The results show that the relativistic Green's function model gives the best agreement with MiniBooNE data over the full Q^2 range, while other models including the spectral function approach underestimate the data at low Q^2 for neutrinos and at high Q^2 for antineutrinos. This enables future work to disentangle nuclear structure effects from possible contributions of multinucleon processes and strangeness content in neutral current neutrino scattering.
