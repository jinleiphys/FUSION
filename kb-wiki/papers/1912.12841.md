# The multilayer structure of protons

> It is argued that the dynamics of the elastic scattering of high-energy protons at intermediate transferred momenta changes with the energy increase. It evolves from the multiple scattering at the external layer for energies about 10 GeV to the double scattering at the two subsequent layers within the colliding protons for energies about 10 TeV. The problem of the unitarity is considered in this context.

# The multilayer structure of protons

## Key claim

The paper argues that the elastic scattering dynamics of high-energy protons at intermediate transferred momenta changes with energy, evolving from multiple scattering at the external layer at energies around 10 GeV to double scattering at two subsequent layers within the colliding protons at energies around 10 TeV; this claim is conditional on interpreting the measured exponential slopes in terms of spatial sizes of distinct internal layers.

## Method

The author analyzes the differential cross section of elastic proton-proton scattering at 13 TeV measured by the TOTEM collaboration, comparing the exponential slope parameters at small and intermediate transferred momenta. The spatial interpretation of these slopes is used to infer the existence of distinct layers within the proton, and the unitarity condition is examined to understand how the observed t-exponential behavior at TeV energies replaces the sqrt(|t|)-exponential Orear regime seen at GeV energies.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section at small transferred momenta (0.04 < |t| < 0.2 GeV^2) at 13 TeV follows dσ/dt ∝ exp[-B|t|] with B ≈ 20.4 GeV^{-2}
- The differential cross section at intermediate transferred momenta (0.7 < |t| < 3.83 GeV^2) at 13 TeV follows dσ/dt ∝ exp[-C|t|] with C ≈ 4.3 GeV^{-2}
- From the relation C = Bβ/(B+β) using B = 20.4 GeV^{-2} and C = 4.3 GeV^{-2}, the inferred internal layer exponent is β ≈ 5.4 GeV^{-2}
- The internal layer is concentrated at a radius near 0.45 fm, twice smaller than the external size
- The dip in the differential cross section occurs at |t| = 0.47 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proposal (Dremin 2018) that the exponential slope at intermediate momenta reveals a deeply positioned layer inside protons, analogous to Rutherford's discovery of nuclei inside atoms. It contrasts with the three-layered proton model of Islam and collaborators, which used 17 adjustable parameters but achieved poor fits to data. The paper also addresses the "proton radius puzzle" (0.84 fm to 0.88 fm) and recent findings of a developing hollow at small impact parameters at 13 TeV (Dremin 2017, Csorgo and collaborators). The analysis enables further theoretical work to verify the validity of assumptions about the overlap function and real parts of the elastic amplitude in the unitarity condition.
