# Energy dependence of $J/ψ$ production in pp collisions with the PACIAE model

> In this work we investigate the $J/ψ$ production in proton-proton collisions at the center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s}$) equal to 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8 and 13 TeV with a parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.2a. It is based on PYTHIA but extended considering the partonic and hadronic rescatterings before and after hadronization, respectively. In the PYTHIA sector the $J/ψ$ production quantum chromodynamics processes are selected specially and a bias factor is proposed correspondingly. The calculated total cross sections, the differential cross sections as a function of the transverse momentum and the rapidity of $J/ψ$ in the forward rapidity region reproduce the corresponding experimental measurements reasonably well. In the mid-rapidity region, the double differential cross sections at $\sqrt{s}=$ 5.02, 7 and 13 TeV are also in a good agreement with the experimental data. Moreover, we interpolate the double differential cross section as well as the total cross section of $J/ψ$ in the mid-rapidity region at $\sqrt{s}=$ 8 TeV, which could be validated if the experimental data is available.

## Key claim

The PACIAE 2.2a model, which extends PYTHIA by adding partonic and hadronic rescattering stages, can reproduce the energy dependence of inclusive J/ψ production in pp collisions across five center-of-mass energies, but only after introducing a bias factor to correct for the special selection of color-singlet QCD subprocesses and only in the low-to-intermediate transverse momentum region below 7 GeV/c where color-octet contributions are neglected.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE 2.2a parton and hadron cascade model, which is based on PYTHIA 6.4 but includes partonic rescattering before hadronization and hadronic rescattering after hadronization. They selected only color-singlet J/ψ production subprocesses (gg → J/ψ g, gg → χc g, gg → χc, gg → J/ψ γ) by setting MSEL=0 and MSUB(i)=1 for i=86,87,88,89,104,105,106 in PYTHIA, then introduced a bias factor B defined as the ratio of the first moment of the experimental pT spectrum to that of the simulated spectrum to rescale the simulated cross sections. The K factor for LO-pQCD parton-parton cross sections was determined separately at each energy by minimizing χ² between simulated and experimental J/ψ pT differential cross sections in the forward rapidity region (2.5 < y < 4), and the same K factor was assumed for the mid-rapidity region (-0.9 < y < 0.9).

## Key numbers

- Total J/ψ cross section in one event (σJ/ψ) from PACIAE: 28.631 μb at 2.76 TeV, 57.349 μb at 5.02 TeV, 60.822 μb at 7 TeV, 79.524 μb at 8 TeV, and 132.111 μb at 13 TeV
- Best-fit K factors (minimum χ²/ndf): K=1.6 at 2.76 TeV (χ²/ndf=3.46/6), K=1.7 at 5.02 TeV (10.92/6), K=1.3 at 7 TeV (16.62/6), K=1.5 at 8 TeV (13.40/6), K=1.7 at 13 TeV (54.86/7)
- Interpolated bias factor at mid-rapidity for √s=8 TeV: B = 0.404 ± 0.032, from the parameterization B = (0.764 ± 0.159) + (-0.173 ± 0.068) ln(√s/TeV) fitted to data at 5.02, 7, and 13 TeV
- The total cross section at forward rapidity is approximately a factor of 1.5 larger than the rapidity differential cross section at a given energy

## Context

This work extends previous Monte Carlo studies of J/ψ production in pp collisions using PYTHIA 6.4 and 8.2 (Refs. mc_1, mc_2), EPOS3 (Ref. mc_2), and a modified UrQMD model (Ref. Urqmd_1), but with the addition of partonic and hadronic rescattering stages not present in standard PYTHIA. The approach differs from the color singlet model, non-relativistic QCD model, and color evaporation model by using a full transport simulation with a bias factor correction. The interpolated results at √s=8 TeV provide predictions that could be validated against future ALICE measurements, and the authors note that the model's ability to simultaneously describe J/ψ polarization and pT spectra remains to be tested.
