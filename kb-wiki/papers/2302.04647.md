> In this work, the recoil corrections to the correlation coefficients in the neutron beta decay with polarized neutron and electron are evaluated to order O(m_n^-2) in the Born approximation. # Recoil contributions in neutron beta decay and their corrections to the correlation coefficients

## Key claim

Recoil corrections to the angular correlation coefficients in neutron beta decay with polarized neutron and electron are evaluated to order O(m_n^{-2}) in the Born approximation, and the results show that a careful treatment of the expansion on m_n^{-1} and the inclusion of general coupling forms (including f_6, f_7, f_8) are necessary when aiming to reach the precision 10^{-5}, contrary to the approach in prior literature where the phase space factor and amplitude were expanded independently.

## Method

The authors directly expand the full differential scattering cross section d^5 sigma_n on m_n^{-1} before integrating over the solid angle of the neutrino, rather than expanding the phase space factor and the amplitude independently as done in prior work. They keep the most general coupling forms for the Wpn vertex (including vector, weak magnetism, scalar, axial vector, weak electricity, induced pseudoscalar, and two slope parameters f_7 and f_8) and compute analytic expressions for the correlation coefficients zeta, A, G, N, Q. Numerical comparisons are made between results using three different treatments of the phase space factor (the exact beta, beta_RM1, and beta_RM2) and between different choices of coupling parameters.

## Key numbers

- Neutron mass m_n = 939.56542 MeV, proton mass m_p = 938.27209 MeV, electron mass m_e = 0.51100 MeV (from PDG 2020)
- For the parameter set A (f_1=1, f_2=3.7058, f_4=-1.2767, f_3=f_5=f_6=f_7=f_8=0, g_{eV}=g_{eA}=1), the difference delta_1 between the authors' results with beta_RM2 and the reference results from Ivanov (2017) is at order 10^{-5} for zeta, A, G; at order 10^{-4} for N; and reaches order 10^{-3} for Q at large E_e
- For the parameter set B, f_6=228, f_7=2.5 f_1, f_8=1.92 f_4 (from form factor reference); the contributions from these additional form factors (delta_3) are at order 10^{-5} for zeta, A, G, N and reach 10^{-4} for Q at large E_e
- The difference delta_2 between using exact beta versus beta_RM1 as phase factor is at order 10^{-5} for zeta, A, G, Q and at order 10^{-6} for N

## Context

This work extends the calculations of recoil corrections to neutron beta decay correlation coefficients by Ivanov (2017, 2018, 2021) which used independent expansions of the amplitude and phase space factor on m_N^{-1} and kept only the leading coupling constants f_1, f_2, f_4. The authors show that the independent expansion method leads to differences at the 10^{-5} to 10^{-3} level compared to their direct expansion of the full cross section, and that additional form factors (induced pseudoscalar f_6 and slope parameters f_7, f_8) contribute at the 10^{-5} level. This work enables more precise theoretical predictions for extracting V_{ud} and the axial-vector coupling constant g_A from upcoming high-precision neutron beta decay experiments, where the target precision is 10^{-5}.
