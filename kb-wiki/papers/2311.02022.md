# Production of the $ΞN$ dibaryon as a weakly bound system in $pp$ collisions

> The $ΞN$ interaction plays an important role in our understanding on the long-anticipated $H$-dibaryon. Recent lattice QCD calculations verified the attractive nature of the $ΞN$ interaction. On the other hand, whether it is strong enough to generate a bound state remains inconclusive.In this work, assuming that it can generate a weakly bound state, we study the yields of the $ΞN$ dibaryon for different binding energies in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV using the coalescence model and the transport model PACIAE. The yields are estimated first numerically and then analytically adopting a Yukawa-type wave function. In particular, we find that in the weak binding limit, there exists a universal relation between the yield and the binding energy, valid for $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

Assuming the XiN interaction can generate a weakly bound state (which remains inconclusive from lattice QCD), the yield of the XiN dibaryon in pp collisions at 7 TeV follows a universal relation with binding energy in the weak binding limit, scaling as the square root of the binding energy.

## Method

The authors used the transport model PACIAE to simulate the phase-space distributions of primary hadrons in pp collisions at 7 TeV, tuning parameters to reproduce experimental yields of Xi, Omega, and nucleons. They then applied the coalescence model with a Wigner function approach, using a Yukawa-type wave function expanded in Gaussian bases, to compute the yields of the XiN dibaryon for different binding energies ranging from 0.1 MeV to 12.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For binding energy EB = 1.66 MeV (predicted by the ESC08c potential), the total yield of XiN dibaryon per pp event is 0.74 x 10^-4
- For binding energy EB = 0.10 MeV, the total yield is 0.24 x 10^-4; for EB = 12.5 MeV, the total yield is 1.17 x 10^-4
- The simulated yields of Xi- and Xi+ per pp event are 7.93 x 10^-3 and 8.06 x 10^-3 respectively, compared to experimental data of 8.0 x 10^-3 and 7.8 x 10^-3
- The simulated yields of Omega- and Omega+ per pp event are 0.684 x 10^-3 and 0.728 x 10^-3, compared to experimental data of 0.67 x 10^-3 and 0.68 x 10^-3
- The fitted parameter C2 in the universal relation is 2.3308 fm for the total yield

## Context

This work addresses the unsettled question of whether the XiN interaction is strong enough to form a bound state, where lattice QCD calculations by HAL QCD (2019) found attraction but Hiyama et al. (2019) concluded the system cannot bind, while the ESC08c potential predicts a shallow bound state of a few MeV (Garcilazo, 2016; Nagels, 2015). The study extends the coalescence model approach previously applied to Omega-N hypernuclei by Zhang et al. (2020, 2021) and to other exotic hadrons. The results enable future experimental searches for the XiN dibaryon at the LHC, with yields of order 10^-4 per event comparable to Omega production, and provide a method to extract the binding energy from measured production yields.
