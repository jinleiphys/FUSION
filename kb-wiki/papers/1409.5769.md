# Volume Effects on the Method of Extracting Form Factors at Zero Momentum

> The Rome method allows one to extract form factors using lattice computations performed strictly at zero momentum. We investigate the size of finite volume effects resulting from this method. As a test case, we focus on the pion charge radius and show how to ascertain the finite volume effect with the aid of chiral perturbation theory. The framework developed can easily be generalized to account for modified infrared physics of other low-energy matrix elements extracted at zero momentum.

# Volume Effects on the Method of Extracting Form Factors at Zero Momentum

## Key claim

The Rome method for extracting form factors at zero momentum can be extended to moments of form factors such as the pion charge radius, but momentum derivatives of rest-frame current matrix elements introduce problematic power-law Euclidean time dependence and divergent contact terms; these drawbacks are avoided by instead differentiating the current matrix element in an arbitrary frame, where the resulting Taylor-coefficient correlation functions cleanly isolate the charge radius.

## Method

The author extends the Rome method to compute the pion charge radius by taking second derivatives of the temporal component of the current matrix element with respect to initial and final spatial momenta in an arbitrary frame, rather than in the rest frame. To assess finite volume effects on this method, partially twisted boundary conditions are introduced on active quarks to enable a Taylor series expansion in momentum on a fixed-size lattice, and the finite volume corrections are computed using partially quenched chiral perturbation theory with an enlarged SU(5|3) flavor group.

## Key numbers

- For periodic boundary conditions, the minimum nonzero momentum transfer available to lattice QCD is approximately 300–500 MeV (from the relation 2π/L).
- The finite volume correction to the charge radius from the zero-momentum method is expected to be less than a few percent on current-size lattices.
- Higher moments of the charge distribution scale in finite volume as Δr^(2n) ~ L^(2(n-1)) √(m_π L) e^(-m_π L), with exponential overall scaling.

## Context

This work extends the Rome method proposed by de Divitiis et al. (2012) for computing form factors directly at zero momentum, applying it to the case of charge radii using the pion as a test case. The finite volume analysis builds on prior partially twisted chiral perturbation theory calculations by Jiang et al. (2006) in the rest frame and Jiang et al. (2008) in the Breit frame, but requires computation in an arbitrary frame where the extra form factor ΔG (originally found by Jiang et al. 2006 and linked to the Ward-Takahashi identity by Bijnens et al. 2014) drops out of all Taylor series coefficients. The framework enables future lattice studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (connected part only) and can be generalized to other low-energy matrix elements extracted at zero momentum, though disconnected current insertions remain inaccessible to the method.
