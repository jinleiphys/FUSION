# Momentum distribution of N$^*$ in nuclei

> Due to its dominance in the low energy eta-nucleon interaction, the S11 N$^*$(1535) resonance enters as an important ingredient in the analyses of experiments aimed at finding evidence for the existence of eta-mesic nuclei. The static properties of the resonance get modified inside the nucleus and its momentum distribution is used in deciding these properties as well as the kinematics in the analyses. Here we show that given the possibility for the existence of an N$^*$-$^3$He quasibound state, the relative momentum distribution of an N$^*$ and $^3$He inside such a $^4$He is narrower than that of neutron-$^3$He in $^4$He. Results for the N$^*$-$^{24}$Mg system are also presented. The present exploratory work could be useful in motivating searches of exotic N$^*$-nucleus quasibound states as well as in performing analyses of eta meson production data.

# Momentum distribution of N$^*$ in nuclei

## Key claim

Given the possibility for the existence of an N$^*$-$^3$He quasibound state, the relative momentum distribution of an N$^*$ and $^3$He inside such a $^4$He nucleus is narrower than that of a neutron-$^3$He system in $^4$He, because the N$^*$ is more loosely bound than a nucleon. This finding is exploratory and depends on the specific choice of $\pi$NN$^*$ and $\eta$NN$^*$ coupling constants, which remain uncertain.

## Method

The author constructs an N$^*$-nucleus potential by folding an elementary one-pion and one-eta exchange N N$^*$ $\to$ N N$^*$ interaction with known nuclear densities for $^3$He and $^{24}$Mg. The resulting potentials are fitted to Woods-Saxon forms, and the Schrödinger equation is solved analytically using hypergeometric functions to obtain bound-state wave functions, which are then Fourier transformed to yield the relative momentum distribution $T(p)$.

## Key numbers

- The N$^*$-$^3$He binding energy ranges from -0.04 MeV to -4.78 MeV depending on the coupling constant set used; the highest binding (-4.78 MeV) comes from the chiral constituent quark model with $g_{\pi N N^*}$ = 1.09 and $g_{\eta N N^*}$ = 2.07.
- The neutron-$^3$He binding energy used for comparison is -20.6 MeV, obtained with Woods-Saxon parameters $V_0$ = 66 MeV, $R$ = 1.97 fm, and $a$ = 0.65 fm.
- For the N$^*$-$^{24}$Mg system, three bound states are found at -50.3, -22.5, and -3.25 MeV using the coupling constants from the chiral constituent quark model.
- Varying the N$^*$ mass between 1400 and 1550 MeV changes the binding energy from 4.34 to 4.84 MeV for the highest-binding parameter set, producing only a very small change in the momentum distribution.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier prediction of possible N$^*$-nucleus quasibound states (Kelkar, 2015) by calculating the momentum distributions needed for experimental analyses of eta-mesic nuclei. The momentum distribution of the N$^*$ is narrower than that of a nucleon, contradicting the common approximation used in analyses by the WASA group and others, which substitute the nucleon momentum distribution for the N$^*$. The results are compared with few-body calculations by Nogga (using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with AV18+TM and CD-Bonn+TM potentials) for validation. This work enables more accurate detector acceptance calculations for reactions such as $d d \to (^3\text{He-N}^*) \to ^3\text{He} N \pi$ and motivates future few-body calculations for the N$^*$-p-n system.
