# Scaling of flow in heavy ion collisions and the low-energy frontier

> The common interpretation of elliptic flow $v_2$ in heavy ion collisions is that it is produced by hydrodynamic flow at low transverse momentum and by parton energy loss at high transverse momentum. Here, we discuss this interpretation in view of the dependence of $v_2$ with energy, rapidity and system size, and show that it is far from clear how the relevant properties necessary for this interpretation, low viscosity and high opacity, turn on. A low energy collider such as NICA is essential for this interpretation to be verified, understood and related to the fundamental properties of hadronic matter

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed scaling of elliptic flow \(v_2\) across energies, system sizes, and rapidities is surprisingly simple and challenges the standard interpretation that low-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) arises from hydrodynamic flow and high-\(p_T\) \(v_2\) from parton energy loss; the author claims it is "far from clear how the relevant properties necessary for this interpretation, low viscosity and high opacity, turn on," and that a low-energy collider such as NICA is essential to verify and understand this interpretation.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data on elliptic flow \(v_2\) scaling across different collision energies, system sizes, centralities, and rapidities, and compares these observations to theoretical expectations from hydrodynamic models and jet energy loss (tomographic) models. The analysis uses a parametrization of \(v_n(p_T) = \epsilon_n(b,A) F(p_T)\) and examines how scaling violations would arise from Knudsen number effects in hydrodynamics and from size-dependent opacity in the tomographic regime, including a numerical investigation with a longitudinally expanding ellipsoid background fitted to global multiplicity and system size.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum of the system is \(\langle p_T \rangle \sim \mathcal{O}(0.5-1)\) GeV, with the tomographic regime appearing at \(\mathcal{O}(3-4) \times \langle p_T \rangle\).
- The parametrization \(\langle v_2 \rangle / \epsilon = 4(10^{-3}) (1/S_T)(dN/dy)\) is used for the momentum-integrated elliptic flow.
- Scaling holds across different centralities up to \(p_T \simeq 20\) GeV at the LHC, with possible breakdown only at \(p_T \simeq 40\) GeV.
- The critical \(p_T\) for the tomographic regime scales as \(p_{T1}^{tomo} / p_{T2}^{tomo} \sim (S_2/S_1 \cdot dN_1/dy / dN_2/dy)^{\omega}\), where \(\omega = 1/2\) for collisional-dominated equilibration and could increase to \(3/2\) if radiative processes become important.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of elliptic flow scaling observed by CMS (2013), PHENIX (2014), and STAR (2014) across RHIC and LHC energies, and by the PHOBOS collaboration on limiting fragmentation. The paper highlights tension between the observed scaling and predictions from hydrodynamic models (Heinz, 2009; Ollitrault, 2008) and jet energy loss models (Baier, 1996; Chesler, 2014), and argues that a low-energy collider such as NICA is necessary to detect non-monotonic behavior in viscosity and opacity as functions of initial temperature, which would help connect these observables to the onset of deconfinement.
