# Shear viscosity of pionic and nucleonic components from their different possible mesonic and baryonic thermal fluctuations

> Owing to the Kubo relation, the shear viscosities of pionic and nucleonic components have been evaluated from their corresponding retarded correlators of viscous stress tensor in the static limit, which become non-divergent only for the non-zero thermal widths of the constituent particles. In the real-time thermal field theory, the pion and nucleon thermal widths have respectively been obtained from the pion self-energy for different meson, baryon loops and the nucleon self-energy for different pion-baryon loops. We have found a non-monotonic momentum distributions of pion and nucleon thermal widths, which have been integrated out by their respective Bose-enhanced and Pauli-blocked phase space factors during evaluation of their shear viscosities. The viscosity to entropy density ratio for this mixed gas of pion-nucleon system decreases and approaches toward its lower bound as the temperature and baryon chemical potential increase within the relevant domain of hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for a mixed pion-nucleon gas decreases and approaches the KSS lower bound as temperature and baryon chemical potential increase, but only when baryonic loop contributions to the pion thermal width are included alongside previously considered mesonic loops and nucleon self-energy loops.

## Method

The shear viscosities of pionic and nucleonic components were evaluated separately using the Kubo relation, which requires the retarded correlator of the viscous stress tensor in the static limit; this correlator becomes non-divergent only when finite thermal widths of the constituent particles are included. The pion thermal width was obtained from the imaginary part of the pion self-energy for both mesonic loops (πσ and πρ) and baryonic loops (involving eleven 4-star baryon resonances such as Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), etc.), while the nucleon thermal width was obtained from the nucleon self-energy for πB loops with the same baryon resonances, all calculated within the real-time thermal field theory formalism.

## Key numbers

- Pion thermal width from baryonic loops: the NΔ loop is the leading contributor among all baryonic loops, with its Landau cut region extending from M=0 to (m_Δ - m_N) = 0.292 GeV
- Coupling constants f/m_π for πNB interactions fixed from experimental decay widths: 15.7 for Δ(1232), 2.5 for N*(1440), 11.6 for N*(1520), 1.14 for N*(1535), 3.4 for Δ*(1600), 1.22 for Δ*(1620), 1.14 for N*(1650), 9.5 for Δ*(1700), 2.8 for N*(1700), 0.35 for N*(1710), and 1.18 for N*(1720)
- Physical masses used: m_π = 0.140 GeV, m_σ = 0.390 GeV, m_ρ = 0.770 GeV; coupling constants g_ρ = 6 and g_σ = 5.82 fixed from experimental decay widths
- The total shear viscosity η_T at μ_N = 0.5 GeV shows a rapid increment after T = 0.06 GeV, driven by the strongly increasing η_N(μ_N) component

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of shear viscosity in a pionic medium (Ghosh, 2013) and a pion-nucleon system (Ghosh, 2014) by now including baryonic loop contributions to the pion thermal width, which were absent in those earlier calculations. The approach follows the Kubo formalism used by Nicola (2012) and Weise (2012) for obtaining non-divergent shear viscosities via finite thermal widths, and the results enable more complete hydrodynamic modeling of hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions, where the temperature-dependent η/s ratio has been shown by Niemi (2011) to be highly sensitive to the extracted elliptic flow parameter.
