# Remarks on the Heavy-Quark Flavour Symmetry for doubly heavy hadronic molecules

> The possibility for a common effective field theory for hadronic molecules with different heavy-quark flavours is examined critically. It is argued that such a theory does not allow one to draw definite conclusions for doubly heavy molecules. In particular, it does not allow one to relate binding energies for the molecules in the c-quark and b-quark sectors with controlled uncertainties. Therefore, while this kind of reasoning does not preclude from employing heavy-quark spin symmetry for charmonium- and bottomonium-like states separately within a well established effective field theory framework, relations between different heavy-quark sectors can only be obtained using phenomenological approaches with uncontrolled uncertainties.

## Key claim

A common effective field theory (EFT) that relates hadronic molecules in the charm-quark and bottom-quark sectors with controlled uncertainties does not exist; while heavy-quark flavour symmetry (HQFS) reasoning does not preclude using heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) within each sector separately, relations between different heavy-quark sectors can only be obtained via phenomenological approaches with uncontrolled uncertainties.

## Method

The authors examine whether a single EFT can be constructed for doubly heavy hadronic molecules (systems of two heavy mesons) that respects heavy-quark flavour symmetry. They analyze the scaling of the action with heavy meson mass \(M\), the mass-dependence of contact interactions in a pionless theory, and the renormalisability condition for both pionless and pionful EFTs. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for S-wave scattering with contact plus one-pion-exchange potentials and study the regulator dependence of the binding momentum.

## Key numbers

- For a hypothetical \(X_b\) state near \(B\bar{B}^*\) threshold, using the \(X(3872)\) as input and assuming \(\tilde{\Lambda}_c \simeq \tilde{\Lambda}_b = \tilde{\Lambda} \simeq 500\) MeV, the binding energy takes values of order 10 MeV.
- The relation between binding momenta in the \(b\)- and \(c\)-sectors under the assumption of mass-independent contact interaction gives \(\gamma_b \simeq \frac{4}{3\pi}\Lambda\) when using \(M_b \approx 3M_c\) and \(\gamma_c \ll \Lambda\).
- In the pionless theory, renormalisability requires the bare contact interaction \(C_0\) to scale at least as \(1/M\) in the limit \(M \to \infty\), specifically \(C_0 \propto 1/M\) for \(-\infty < n \leq 1\) or \(C_0 \propto 1/M^n\) for \(n > 1\).

## Context

This work extends critical examinations of heavy-quark symmetry in heavy-heavy systems, following warnings in Jenkins (1992) and Flynn (2007) about difficulties in defining the large-\(M\) limit for such systems. It directly contradicts the approach of Nieves (2011) and Guo (2013), who used HQFS to predict a \(1^{++}\) bound state \(X_b\) near \(B\bar{B}^*\) threshold from the \(X(3872)\) as input. The paper enables a clearer separation between EFT-based calculations (which are possible within a single heavy-quark sector using HQSS, as in AlFiky (2005)) and phenomenological estimates that lack controlled uncertainty quantification.
