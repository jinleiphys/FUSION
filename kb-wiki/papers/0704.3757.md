# Consistency between renormalization group running of chiral operator and counting rule -- Case of chiral pion production operator --

> In nuclear chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), an operator is defined in a space with a cutoff which may be varied within a certain range. The operator runs as a result of the variation of the cutoff [renormalization group (RG) running]. In order for ChPT to be useful, the operator should run in a way consistent with the counting rule; that is, the running of chiral counter terms have to be of natural size. We vary the cutoff using the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation, and examine this consistency. As an example, we study the s-wave pion production operator for NN\to d pi, derived in ChPT. We demonstrate that the WRG running does not generate any chiral-symmetry-violating (CSV) interaction, provided that we start with an operator which does not contain a CSV term. We analytically show how the counter terms are generated in the WRG running in case of the infinitesimal cutoff reduction. Based on the analytic result, we argue a range of the cutoff variation for which the running of the counter terms is of natural size. Then, we numerically confirm this.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) running of the chiral s-wave pion production operator for \(NN\to d\pi\) is consistent with the chiral counting rule, provided the cutoff is varied within a physically reasonable range (500–800 MeV); the running of the counter terms remains of natural size [\(\mathcal{O}(1)\)] under this condition, but could become divergent if the cutoff variation exceeds \(\sim 4\pi f_\pi\).

## Method

The authors apply the Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation to reduce the sharp cutoff of the chiral next-to-leading order (NLO) s-wave pion production operator for \(NN\to d\pi\), using the CD-Bonn phenomenological nuclear force (with cutoff 800 MeV) as the \(NN\) interaction. They analytically derive the infinitesimal cutoff reduction of the leading chiral counter terms (couplings \(\hat{D}_{1a}\)–\(\hat{D}_{1i}\)) using the chiral LO nuclear force, and then numerically evolve the operator from \(\Lambda = 800\) MeV down to 700, 600, and 500 MeV, fitting the resulting low-momentum operator to the chiral NLO operator plus counter terms to extract the running of the low-energy constants (LECs).

## Key numbers

* The running of the leading counter term \(\hat{D}_{1a}\) from a one-loop diagram (WT' operator plus one-pion-exchange potential) for cutoff reduction from \(\bar{\Lambda}=800\) MeV to \(\Lambda=500\) MeV is estimated as \(\hat{D}^{\rm (1-loop)}_{1a} \sim 0.9\); including multi-loop ladder diagrams gives \(\hat{D}^{\rm (multi-loops)}_{1a} \sim 1.2\), both of natural size.
* The condition for the WRG running to remain of natural size is \(\bar{\Lambda}-\Lambda \ll 4\pi f_\pi\), where \(4\pi f_\pi \approx 4\pi \times 92.4\) MeV \(\approx 1160\) MeV; the physically reasonable range used (800–500 MeV, difference 300 MeV) satisfies this.
* The cutoff range studied numerically is 500 MeV \(\le \Lambda \le\) 800 MeV; the starting cutoff is \(\bar{\Lambda}=800\) MeV and the final cutoffs are 700, 600, and 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of renormalization group running in chiral nuclear operators, contrasting with prior studies by Birse (1998) and Harada and Kubo (2006) that focused on scaling near fixed points, and by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and Epelbaum and Meissner (2006) that examined renormalizability over very wide cutoff ranges. The authors use the WRG equation to directly compute the running of the chiral pion production operator derived by Lensky et al. (2005) with a modified power counting, and show that the hybrid approach (phenomenological nuclear force with chiral operators) passes a consistency test: no chiral-symmetry-violating terms are generated. This enables future work to apply the same WRG methodology to other chiral operators (e.g., electroweak currents) and to study the convergence of the chiral expansion under cutoff variation.
