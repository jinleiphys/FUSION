# Radial profile of bottom quarks in jets in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Angular correlations between heavy quark (HQ) and its tagged jet are potentially new tools to gain insight into the in-medium partonic interactions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we present the first theoretical study on the radial profiles of B mesons in jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The initial production of bottom quark tagged jet in p+p is computed by SHERPA which matches the next-to-leading order matrix elements with contributions of parton shower, whereas the massive quark traversing the QGP described by a Monte Carlo model SHELL which can simultaneously simulate light and heavy flavor in-medium energy loss within the framework of Langevin evolution. In p+p collisions, we find that at lower $p_T^Q$ the radial profiles of heavy flavors in jets are sensitive to the heavy quark mass. In $0-10\%$ Pb+Pb collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, we observe an inverse modification pattern of the B mesons radial profiles in jets at $\rm 4<p_T^Q<20$ GeV compared to that of D mesons: the jet quenching effects narrow the jet radial profile of B mesons in jets while broaden that of D mesons in jets. We find that in A+A collisions, the contribution dissipated from the higher $\rm p_T^Q> 20$ GeV region naturally has a narrower initial distribution and consequently leads to a narrower modification pattern of radial profile; however the diffusion nature of the heavy flavor in-medium interactions will give rise to a broader modification pattern of radial profile. These two effects consequently compete and offset with each other, and the b quarks in jets benefit more from the former and suffers less diffusion effect compared to that of c quarks in jets. These findings can be tested in the future experimental measurements at the LHC to gain better understanding of the mass effect of jet quenching.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical study of the radial profiles of B mesons in jets in Pb+Pb collisions, predicting an inverse modification pattern compared to D mesons: jet quenching narrows the radial profile of B mesons while broadening that of D mesons, a result that depends on the competition between energy dissipation from higher transverse momentum regions and diffusion effects, with the balance favoring narrowing for bottom quarks due to their larger mass.

## Method

The authors used the SHERPA event generator at NLO+PS (next-to-leading order QCD matched with parton shower) to compute the initial production of bottom-quark-tagged jets in p+p collisions. The in-medium evolution of heavy quarks traversing the quark-gluon plasma was simulated using the SHELL Monte Carlo model, which implements modified Langevin equations incorporating both collisional energy loss and medium-induced gluon radiation via higher-twist calculations, with a spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s = 4/(2\pi T)\) from lattice QCD and a jet transport parameter \(\hat{q} = q_0 (T/T_0)^3\) with \(q_0 = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with radius \(R = 0.3\) within \(|\eta^{\rm jet}| < 1.6\), requiring \(p_T^{\rm jet} > 60\) GeV, and heavy quark fragmentation used Peterson form fragmentation functions with \(\epsilon_c = 0.01\) and \(\epsilon_b = 0.001\).

## Key numbers

- In p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, for \(4 < p_T^Q < 20\) GeV, B mesons in jets peak at \(r \approx 0.20\) while D mesons peak at \(r \approx 0.07\), showing mass sensitivity at lower \(p_T^Q\).
- In \(0-10\%\) Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, the radial profile of B mesons in jets with \(4 < p_T^Q < 20\) GeV shifts toward smaller radii (narrowing), opposite to the broadening observed for D mesons.
- The angular deviation \(\Delta A\) of bottom quarks from their initial direction during in-medium propagation is smaller than that of charm quarks by nearly \(20-30\%\) across the transverse momentum range studied.
- The jet transport parameter used is \(q_0 = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, determined from global extraction of single hadron production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient is set to \(D_s = 4/(2\pi T)\) from lattice QCD, and the critical temperature for stopping in-medium propagation is \(T_c = 165\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of D meson radial profiles in jets (Wang 2019) and the CMS measurements of charm meson radial distributions in jets (Sirunyan 2019), providing the first theoretical prediction for bottom quarks. It contrasts with earlier studies of flavor-dependent jet quenching that focused on the magnitude of energy loss differences due to the dead-cone effect (Cao 2013, He 2014, Djordjevic 2015), by demonstrating that the initial radial distributions of heavy quarks in jets at different \(p_T^Q\) kinematic cuts can ultimately determine the modification pattern. The predictions can be tested against future LHC experimental measurements to improve understanding of the mass effect in jet quenching.
