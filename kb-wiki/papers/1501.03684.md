# Accuracy of the new pairing theory and its improvement

> Recently I proposed a new method for solving the pairing Hamiltonian with the pair-condensate wavefunction ansatz based on the Heisenberg equations of motion for the density matrix operators. In this work an improved version is given by deriving the relevant equations more carefully. I evaluate both versions in a large ensemble with random interactions, and the accuracy of the methods is given statistically in terms of root-mean-square derivations from the exact results. The widely used variational calculation is also done and the results and computing-time costs are compared.

# Accuracy of the new pairing theory and its improvement

## Key claim

The improved version (GDM2) of the generalized density matrix (GDM) pairing theory, which allows neighboring even-even nuclei to have different Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and occupation numbers, yields slightly better pair-transfer amplitudes than the original GDM1 version but worse occupation numbers; both GDM methods are close in accuracy to the variational calculation while requiring significantly less computing time.

## Method

The author derives an improved version of the GDM pairing theory by taking matrix elements of the Heisenberg equations of motion for density matrix operators between pair-condensate ground states of neighboring even-even nuclei, without assuming identical Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and occupation numbers for the two nuclei. Both the original (GDM1) and improved (GDM2) versions are applied to a large ensemble of 1000 random examples with random single-particle levels, energies, and pairing interactions, and their results for occupation numbers, pair-transfer amplitudes, and ground-state energies are compared statistically against exact diagonalization and variational calculations.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation for occupation numbers: sigma_n(GDM1) = 0.0122, sigma_n(GDM2) = 0.0188, sigma_n(VAR) = 0.0045
- Root-mean-square deviation for pair-transfer amplitudes: sigma_s(GDM1) = 0.0207, sigma_s(GDM2) = 0.0191, sigma_s(VAR1) = 0.0373, sigma_s(VAR2) = 0.0091
- Average ground-state energy errors (in MeV): E_bar_var = 0.045, E_bar_GDM1 = 0.061, E_bar_GDM2 = 0.070, compared to average pairing correlation energy E_bar_pair = 13.64 MeV
- The random ensemble consists of 1000 examples with single-particle levels selected from angular momenta 2j = 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13; single-particle energies uniformly distributed from -10 MeV to 0; pairing strength G_max uniformly distributed from 0 to 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previously proposed GDM pairing method (Jia, 2014) by deriving a more careful version that does not assume identical Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and occupation numbers for neighboring even-even nuclei. The method is compared against the standard variation-after-projection BCS approach (Dietrich, Robledo, Flocard, Braun, Dukelsky, Delft) and exact diagonalization with fixed seniority (Sen1, Sen2). The improved GDM2 theory includes the particle-particle random phase approximation (Ring_book) as its special case for N=1. The reduction in computing time by a factor of the number of non-degenerate single-particle levels makes the GDM approach potentially useful for ab-initio mean-field calculations of medium and heavy nuclei and for fitting interaction parameters.
