# Initial state fluctuations and higher harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> A framework combining Yang-Mills dynamics of the pre-equilibrium glasma with relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases is presented. Event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions and color charges are taken into account, leading to negative binomial fluctuations of gluon multiplicities. Experimental anisotropic flow coefficients v2-v5 of charged hadron distributions in heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider are well described. Furthermore, event-by-event distributions of v2, v3 and v4 measured by the ATLAS collaboration are reproduced.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma+music framework, which combines Yang-Mills dynamics of the pre-equilibrium glasma with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, reproduces experimental anisotropic flow coefficients v2-v5 and event-by-event distributions of v2, v3, and v4 from the ATLAS collaboration, but only when the initial shear stress tensor Πμν is set to zero under the assumption of efficient instability-driven isotropization rather than extracted from the non-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method

The IP-Glasma model combines the IP-Sat model of nucleon and nuclear wavefunctions with classical Yang-Mills dynamics to compute event-by-event gluon field configurations, including fluctuations of nucleon positions and color charges. The energy-momentum tensor from the Yang-Mills simulation is used to initialize 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic evolution with music, assuming zero initial shear stress and using an equation of state, followed by Cooper-Frye freeze-out and resonance decays to obtain final hadron distributions.

## Key numbers

- Anisotropic flow coefficients v2-v5 of charged hadron distributions at LHC energies are well described by the framework
- Event-by-event distributions of v2, v3, and v4 for 0-5% and 20-25% centrality classes are compared to ATLAS data using 750 events (0-5%) and 1300 events (20-25%)
- Integrated root-mean-square v2, v3, and v4 agree with ALICE vn{2} results from two-particle correlations
- The scale of transverse gluon field lumpiness is given by the inverse nuclear saturation scale Qs, corresponding to distance scales smaller than the nucleon size

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012) by coupling it to the music hydrodynamic code (Schenke 2010, 2011) to provide a complete description of heavy-ion collisions from initial glasma through hadron gas. It reproduces experimental flow data from the ATLAS collaboration (ATLAS 2012) and ALICE collaboration (ALICE 2011), demonstrating that initial-state fluctuations from the IP-Glasma model accurately describe the event-by-event distributions of anisotropic flow. The framework enables precise determination of transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases, and future work could incorporate the full 3+1 dimensional Yang-Mills dynamics including quantum fluctuations to provide the non-equilibrium shear stress evolution.
