# Heavy Baryon-Antibaryon Molecules in Effective Field Theory

> We discuss the effective field theory description of bound states composed of a heavy baryon and antibaryon. This framework is a variation of the ones already developed for heavy meson-antimeson states to describe the $X(3872)$ or the $Z_c$ and $Z_b$ resonances. We consider the case of heavy baryons for which the light quark pair is in S-wave and we explore how heavy quark spin symmetry constrains the heavy baryon-antibaryon potential. The one pion exchange potential mediates the low energy dynamics of this system. We determine the relative importance of pion exchanges, in particular the tensor force. We find that in general pion exchanges are probably non-perturbative for the $Σ_Q \barΣ_Q$, $Σ_Q^* \barΣ_Q$ and $Σ_Q^* \barΣ_Q^*$ systems, while for the $Ξ_Q' \barΞ_Q'$, $Ξ_Q^* \barΞ_Q'$ and $Ξ_Q^* \barΞ_Q^*$ cases they are perturbative If we assume that the contact-range couplings of the effective field theory are saturated by the exchange of vector mesons, we can estimate for which quantum numbers it is more probable to find a heavy baryonium state. The most probable candidates to form bound states are the isoscalar $Λ_Q \barΛ_Q$, $Σ_Q \barΣ_Q$, $Σ_Q^* \barΣ_Q$ and $Σ_Q^* \barΣ_Q^*$ and the isovector $Λ_Q \barΣ_Q$ and $Λ_Q \barΣ_Q^*$ systems, both in the hidden-charm and hidden-bottom sectors. Their doubly-charmed and -bottom counterparts ($Λ_Q Λ_Q$, $Λ_Q Σ_Q^{(*)}$, $Σ_Q^{(*)} Σ_Q^{(*)}$) are also good candidates for binding.

## Key claim

The authors extend the effective field theory (EFT) framework previously developed for heavy meson-antimeson molecules (such as the X(3872) and Z_c/Z_b resonances) to heavy baryon-antibaryon systems, finding that pion exchanges are probably non-perturbative for Σ_Q systems but perturbative for Ξ'_Q systems, and that the most probable candidates for bound states are isoscalar Λ_QΛ_Q, Σ_QΣ_Q, Σ_Q*Σ_Q, and Σ_Q*Σ_Q* systems as well as isovector Λ_QΣ_Q and Λ_QΣ_Q* systems, provided the contact-range couplings are saturated by vector meson exchange.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order EFT potential for heavy baryon-antibaryon molecules consisting of contact-range four-baryon vertices and one-pion exchange (OPE), constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and SU(3)-flavour symmetry. They determine whether pion exchanges are perturbative or non-perturbative by analyzing the tensor force strength, and estimate the likelihood of bound state formation by assuming that the contact-range couplings are saturated by the exchange of σ, ρ, ω, and φ mesons. The analysis considers heavy baryons where the light quark pair is in S-wave, organized into antitriplet (B_3) and sextet (B_6, B_6*) superfields.

## Key numbers

- The coupled channel scale Λ_CC for Σ_cΣ_c transitions is 400/564 MeV depending on the transition; for Σ_bΣ_b it is 350/495 MeV.
- The energy gap for the Ξ_c'Σ_c → Ω_cΞ_c' transition mediated by kaon exchange is Δ_CC = 240 MeV, with a coupled channel scale Λ_CC = 790 MeV.
- The mass thresholds for heavy baryon-baryon systems are approximately 4.5 GeV (Λ_cΛ_c), 4.7 GeV (Λ_cΣ_c), and 4.9 GeV (Σ_cΣ_c).
- The hard scale for chiral symmetry in the two-baryon sector is estimated as M ~ 0.5-1.0 GeV, while the soft scale Q includes m_π ~ 100-200 MeV and baryon momenta.
- The heavy quark masses are m_c ~ 1.5 GeV and m_b ~ 4.5 GeV, with Λ_QCD ~ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the EFT formalism developed for heavy meson-antimeson molecules (Valderrama 2012; Nieves 2012; Hidalgo Duque 2012; Guo 2013) to the heavy baryon-antibaryon sector, which has not yet been experimentally observed but is expected to be discovered in the near future. The analysis builds on the observation that the X(3872), Z_c(3900), Z_c(4020), Z_b(10610), and Z_b(10650) states are well-described as molecular states, and that the P_c(4380)+ and P_c(4450)+ pentaquarks may contain heavy baryon components. The framework enables future predictions of heavy baryonium states and their quantum numbers, with the Λ_c(2590)Σ_c system noted as an illustrative example (Geng 2017).
