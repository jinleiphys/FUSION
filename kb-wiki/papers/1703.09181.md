# Charmed mesons with a symmetry-preserving contact interaction

> A symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction is used to study charmed heavy-light mesons. The contact interaction is a representation of nonperturbative kernels used in Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations of QCD. The Dyson-Schwinger equation is solved for the $u,\,d,\,s$ and $c$ quark propagators and the bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes respecting spacetime-translation invariance and the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities associated with global symmetries of QCD are obtained to calculate masses and electroweak decay constants of the pseudoscalar $π,\,K$, $D$ and $D_s$ and vector $ρ$, $K^*$, $D^*$, and $D^*_s$ mesons. The predictions of the model are in good agreement with available experimental and lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

A symmetry-preserving contact interaction (CI) scheme, when combined with a subtraction method that isolates symmetry-violating contributions in Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for arbitrary momentum routing, can describe charmed heavy-light meson masses and decay constants in reasonable agreement with experimental and lattice QCD data, provided the interaction strength is taken as flavor-dependent.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for u, d, s, and c quark propagators using a vector-vector contact interaction as a simplified ansatz for the gluon propagator, with a flavor-dependent coupling strength. They then solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector meson bound states, employing a subtraction scheme that systematically removes symmetry-violating terms proportional to the integrals Aμν, Bμν, and Cμνρσ that arise from arbitrary momentum routing in divergent loop integrals.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar meson masses (in MeV): mπ = 138, mK = 495, mD = 1867, mDs = 1968
- Vector meson masses (in MeV): mρ = 775, mK* = 895, mD* = 2009, mDs* = 2112
- Pseudoscalar decay constants (in MeV): fπ = 92, fK = 110, fD = 207, fDs = 246
- Vector meson decay constants (in MeV): fρ = 153, fK* = 170, fD* = 340, fDs* = 377
- The model uses a flavor-dependent coupling: mG^l = 0.132 GeV for light quarks and mG^c = 0.178 GeV for charm quarks
- The infrared cutoff used to avoid unphysical quark-antiquark thresholds is ΛIR = 0.240 GeV

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-preserving contact interaction scheme of Gutierrez-Guerrero, Bashir, Cloet, and Roberts (2010) from light mesons and heavy charmonia to heavy-light charmed mesons. It addresses the failure of previous rainbow-ladder truncation calculations by Nguyen, Souchlas, and Tandy (2010) and Rojas, El-Bennich, and collaborators (2014) to reproduce the correct ordering of heavy-light decay constants and mass differences. The subtraction scheme, previously applied in NJL model calculations by Battistel and collaborators (2008, 2013) and by Farias and collaborators (2005-2016), allows systematic identification and removal of symmetry-violating terms that plague contact interaction models when the quark masses are very different. The results enable predictions for D and Ds meson properties that can be used in transport and hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
