# Study of $Λp$ and $\barΛ p$ scatterings via quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation

> Motivated by recent BESIII measurements of the $Λp \to Λp$ and $\barΛ p \to \barΛ p$ scattering processes, we investigate these reactions within the framework of the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation using an effective Lagrangian approach. The interaction potentials are constructed via a one-boson-exchange model incorporating pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector meson exchanges, along with coupled-channel effects from the $ΣN$ and $\barΣ N$ channels. For the $Λp \to Λp$ reaction, the total cross sections from threshold up to $\sqrt{s} = 2.5~\text{GeV}$ are well reproduced. A mild enhancement near the $ΣN$ threshold is attributed to coupled-channel dynamics. Using parameters constrained by the total cross section data, our model also predicts differential cross sections at $\sqrt{s} = 2.24~\text{GeV}$, which exhibit weak angular dependence, consistent with experimental observations. Partial-wave analysis indicates that the $1^+$ partial wave dominates over the entire energy range, while the $0^+$ wave plays a significant role near threshold. For the $\barΛ p \to \barΛ p$ reaction, our predicted total cross sections show good agreement with the BESIII data. The $1^-$ partial wave is found to dominate in most of the energy region. Notably, the calculated differential cross sections exhibit a strong forward peaking behavior, consistent with experimental findings and understood as resulting from constructive interference among various partial waves. This forward-peaked angular distribution persists across a range of energies, highlighting the distinct dynamics of the $\barΛ p$ interaction.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach, combined with an effective Lagrangian and one-boson-exchange model, can reproduce the BESIII total and differential cross section data for both $\Lambda p$ and $\bar{\Lambda} p$ scattering, but the agreement for $\Lambda p$ differential cross sections is qualified as "compatible within the sizable experimental uncertainties" rather than precise, and the model requires separate cutoff parameters for the two reactions (0.56 GeV for $\Lambda p$, 0.69 GeV for $\bar{\Lambda} p$).

## Method
The authors solve the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation in the spectator approximation, using interaction potentials derived from a one-boson-exchange model with pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $\eta$, $K$), scalar ($\sigma$), and vector ($\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$) meson exchanges. Coupled-channel effects from the $\Sigma N$ and $\bar{\Sigma} N$ channels are included, with partial wave decomposition performed up to total angular momentum $J \leq 4$ to obtain scattering amplitudes for individual $J^P$ states. The $\bar{\Lambda} p$ interaction is obtained from the $\Lambda p$ interaction via the G-parity rule, and a phenomenological repulsive potential is introduced to prevent unphysical short-range attraction.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 0.56$ GeV for $\Lambda p \to \Lambda p$ scattering, with $g_{\text{rep}} = 11$ for the short-range repulsive interaction
- Coupling constant $g_{\Lambda\Lambda\sigma} = 5$ for the $\Lambda\Lambda\sigma$ vertex
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 0.69$ GeV for $\bar{\Lambda} p \to \bar{\Lambda} p$ scattering
- Total cross section for $\bar{\Lambda} p \to \bar{\Lambda} p$ at $\sqrt{s} = 2.24$ GeV: $\sigma = 24.7$ mb, consistent with BESIII data
- Energy range studied: from threshold up to $\sqrt{s} = 2.5$ GeV for both reactions
- Differential cross sections predicted at $\sqrt{s} = 2.15$, 2.25, 2.35, and 2.45 GeV for both reactions
- $\sigma$ meson mass fixed at $550$ MeV
- Coupling constants from SU(3) flavor symmetry: $g_{NN\pi} = 0.989$, $g_{NN\omega} = 9.75$, $g_{NN\rho} = 3.25$, $\kappa_{NN\rho} = 19.82$, $g_{\Lambda\Lambda\omega} = 6.5$, $\kappa_{\Lambda\Lambda\omega} = -9.91$, $g_{N\Lambda K} = -1.03$, $g_{N\Lambda K^*} = -5.63$, $\kappa_{N\Lambda K^*} = -17.20$

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of hyperon-nucleon scattering beyond the tree-level analysis of Wang et al. (2024) by incorporating rescattering effects via the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation. It builds on meson-exchange models developed by the Julich group (Haidenbauer 2005) and Nijmegen group (Rijken 1998), as well as chiral effective field theory approaches at NLO (Haidenbauer 2019). The study is directly motivated by recent BESIII measurements (BESIII 2024) of both $\Lambda p$ and $\bar{\Lambda} p$ differential cross sections, and provides the first unified qBSE analysis of both scattering processes, enabling future comparisons with upcoming experimental data and improved constraints on hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron star physics.
