# Possible $S$-wave $ND^{(*)}$ and $N\bar B^{(*)}$ bound states in a chiral quark model

> $S$-wave bound-states composed of a nucleon($N$) and a heavy meson ($D$, $D^*$, $\bar{B}$ or $\bar{B}^*$) are investigated in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model by solving the resonating group method equation. The results reveal that the $ND$ and $ND^*$ interactions in the corresponding relative $S$-wave states are attractive, arising mainly from one boson exchange processes between light quarks. It is shown that these attractions are strong enough to form six $ND$ and $ND^*$ $S$-wave bound states in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with the binding energies in the range of $3-45$ MeV, and three $S$-wave bound states within the chiral SU(3) quark model with binding energies of $2-8$ MeV. In particular, the experimentally observed $Σ_c(2800)$ is interpreted to be most likely an $S-$wave $ND$ state with the total isopsin $I=0$ and spin-parity $J^P=1/2^-$, while $Λ_c(2940)^+$ as an $S-$wave $ND^*$ state with $I=0$ and $J^P=3/2^-$. Further information on the $ND$ and $ND^*$ interactions in the (unbound) scattering kinematics are obtained from the corresponding $S$-wave phase shifts. The $N\bar{B}$ and $N\bar{B}^*$ systems are also investigated within the present two models and some $S-$wave bound states with binding energies in the range of $1-60$ MeV are predicted in these systems: six (in total) within the extended chiral SU(3) quark model and, four, within the chiral SU(3) quark model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the experimentally observed $\Sigma_c(2800)$ and $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ are most likely $S$-wave $ND$ and $ND^*$ molecular states, respectively, but only within the extended chiral SU(3) quark model; the simpler chiral SU(3) quark model produces fewer bound states and cannot accommodate these assignments.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the five-quark system composed of a nucleon and a heavy meson ($D$, $D^*$, $\bar B$, or $\bar B^*$), using both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model. The interaction between the two color-singlet clusters arises only from one-meson-exchange processes between light quarks, since one-gluon-exchange and confinement potentials between clusters vanish identically. The models differ in that the extended version includes explicit vector-meson exchange contributions, while the chiral SU(3) quark model does not.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of six $ND$ and $ND^*$ $S$-wave bound states in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model: 3 to 45 MeV
- Binding energies of three $ND$ and $ND^*$ $S$-wave bound states in the chiral SU(3) quark model: 2 to 8 MeV
- Binding energies of $N\bar B$ and $N\bar B^*$ $S$-wave bound states: 1 to 60 MeV, with six total bound states in the extended model and four in the chiral SU(3) quark model
- The $\Sigma_c(2800)$ is interpreted as an $S$-wave $ND$ state with total isospin $I=0$ and spin-parity $J^P=1/2^-$
- The $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ is interpreted as an $S$-wave $ND^*$ state with $I=0$ and $J^P=3/2^-$

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model and extended chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for light-flavor systems such as $NN$, $NY$, $NK$, and pentaquark states, to heavy-flavor sectors involving $ND^{(*)}$ and $N\bar B^{(*)}$ systems. It builds on earlier studies that suggested $\Sigma_c(2800)$ and $\Lambda_c(2940)^+$ as molecular states using QCD sum rules (Zhang 2014), Bethe-Salpeter equation approaches (Wang 2018), and a chiral constituent quark model (Ortega 2013, 2016), but differs from those works by including vector-meson exchange contributions explicitly. The results enable future experimental searches for predicted $N\bar B$ and $N\bar B^*$ bound states and provide a framework for interpreting additional heavy-flavor molecular candidates.
