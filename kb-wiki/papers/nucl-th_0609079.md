# Few Body Reserch - Summary

> 21 pages, 2 tables, Summary talk at 18th International IUPAP Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics August 21-26. 2006 Santos SP Brazil; to be published in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim

Few-body research has achieved remarkable progress over half a century, but numerous discrepancies between rigorous three-body calculations and experimental data remain unresolved, indicating that current three-nucleon forces are inadequate and that the field still faces significant open challenges rather than approaching a complete "standard model."

## Method

The paper is a summary talk reviewing the history, achievements, current developments, and open problems in few-body physics, covering nucleon-nucleon potentials (high-precision phenomenological and chiral perturbation theory), rigorous three-body calculations using Faddeev and AGS equations, experimental techniques including exclusive measurements and detector systems, and comparisons between theory and data across multiple observables and energy ranges.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length $a_{nn}$ from $^2$H(n,p)nn: -21 ± 1 fm; from $^3$H(n,d)nn: -18 ± 3 fm (no theoretical uncertainties included)
- CD-Bonn potential reproduces pp data below 350 MeV with $\chi^2$/datum = 1.01
- Three-nucleon force contributes 0.5-1.0 MeV out of 8.5 MeV binding energy of $^3$H, and 6 out of 32 MeV for $^6$Li
- AV18+UIX average deviation from 17 states in A≤8: 2.02 MeV; AV18+IL3: 0.04 MeV
- $a_{pp} - a_{nn} = 1.6 \pm 0.5$ fm (experimental); chiral quark model predicts 1.37-1.67 fm
- $^3$H-$^3$He binding energy difference: 764 keV, with 65±10 keV attributed to charge symmetry breaking
- Neutron-neutron scattering length from nd breakup at 13 MeV: -18.72 ± 0.13stat ± 0.65sys fm (absolute cross section), -18.84 ± 0.47 fm (shape)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length from nd breakup at 17.4 MeV: -16.5 ± 0.69st ± 0.52sy fm
- Neutron-neutron scattering length from nd breakup at 16.6 MeV: -16.2 ± 0.3 fm; at 25.3 MeV: -16.3 ± 0.4 fm
- Upper limit on $^{76}$Ge neutrinoless double beta decay lifetime: 1.9×10$^{25}$ y (90% confidence)
- Limit on electron electric dipole moment: 1.6 × 10$^{-27}$ ecm; neutron: 6.3 × 10$^{-26}$ ecm
- Supersymmetric particle mass limits (95% CL): neutralino 46 GeV, chargino 94 GeV, selectron 73 GeV, squark 259 GeV, gluino 195 GeV

## Context

This summary extends the tradition of few-body conference reviews dating back to the first conference in London (1959) and builds on foundational work including Bethe's (1953) assessment of the nuclear force problem, Faddeev's (1960) exact solution of the quantum three-body problem, and the AGS equation developed by Sandhas et al. (1967). The paper identifies persistent puzzles such as the Ay puzzle (first noted by Tornow and Gruebler in the early 1980s), the space star anomaly, and discrepancies in $a_{nn}$ extraction that suggest inadequacies in current three-nucleon forces. It enables future work on improved three-nucleon force models, chiral effective field theory at higher orders, and experimental programs at facilities like JLab, KVI, and RIB facilities to resolve these open questions.
