# Probing the existence of η^3He mesic nucleus with a few-body approach

> Motivated by the two recent observations in the WASA-at-COSY detector, we investigate the $η^3$He nucleus with the $ηNNN$ few body method. We construct the effective $s$-wave energy dependent $ηN$ potential which reproduce the $ηN$ subthreshold scattering amplitude in the 2005 Green-Wycech model. It gives the $η$ separation energy and decay width of 0.19 MeV and 1.71 MeV, respectively. We also construct various sets of effective $s$-wave energy independent $ηN$ potentials where the corresponding complex scattering lengths (a) are within the range given in most theoretical models. We obtain the bound $η^3$He nucleus with decay width of about 5 MeV when a is (1.0 fm, 0.3 fm), and of about 10 MeV when a is (1.0 fm, 0.5 fm).

## Key claim
Using a few-body Gaussian expansion method with an energy-dependent ηN potential that reproduces the Green-Wycech (2005) subthreshold scattering amplitude, the authors find a weakly bound η³He state with η separation energy 0.19 MeV and decay width 1.71 MeV; however, with energy-independent potentials that allow larger imaginary scattering lengths, the decay width can reach about 5–10 MeV or even exceed 20 MeV, potentially matching the WASA-at-COSY observations which had large systematic uncertainties.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body ηNNN Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with AV8' NN interactions. They constructed an effective s-wave energy-dependent ηN potential that reproduces the subthreshold scattering amplitude from the Green-Wycech (2005) model, and also built multiple sets of energy-independent s-wave ηN Gaussian potentials with complex scattering lengths (a_R, a_I) spanning the range a_R = 0.2–1.0 fm and a_I = 0.2–0.5 fm. For the energy-dependent case, a self-consistent procedure determined the ηN center-of-mass energy in the η³He environment; for the energy-independent case, the complex Hamiltonian was fully diagonalized without perturbative treatment of the imaginary part.

## Key numbers
- η separation energy B_η = 0.19 MeV and decay width Γ = 1.71 MeV for the energy-dependent potential (μ = 4.0 fm⁻²) reproducing the Green-Wycech (2005) model.
- For energy-independent potential with μ = 4.0 fm⁻² and scattering length (1.0 fm, 0.3 fm): B_η = 1.65 MeV, Γ = 5.24 MeV.
- For energy-independent potential with μ = 4.0 fm⁻² and scattering length (1.0 fm, 0.5 fm): B_η = 1.46 MeV, Γ = 9.72 MeV.
- For energy-independent potential with μ = 4.0 fm⁻² and scattering length (1.0 fm, 1.0 fm): B_η = 2.71 MeV, Γ = 20.51 MeV.
- Minimum real scattering length to form a bound η³He (with only real potential): 0.7 fm for μ = 4.0 fm⁻², 0.83 fm for μ = 1.0 fm⁻².

## Context
This work extends few-body calculations of η-mesic nuclei by Barnea et al. (2015, 2017) and Fix (2017), who used stochastic variational methods or Alt–Grassberger–Sandhas equations with energy-dependent ηN potentials from coupled-channel models of the N*(1535) resonance. The present results with the Green-Wycech (2005) model are consistent with those earlier calculations, giving a weakly bound η³He. The new energy-independent potential calculations produce decay widths (5–20 MeV) that can approach the range reported by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Adlarson 2019), where observed decay widths were >20 MeV or 5–50 MeV with large error bars. This enables future filtering of ηN potentials once experimental binding energies and widths are more precisely determined.
