# Generalized parton distributions of the pion in a Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We calculate generalized parton distribution functions in a field theoretic formalism using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach for the determination of the bound-state wave function. We describe the procedure in an exact calculation in scalar Electrodynamics proving that the relevant corrections outside our scheme vanish. We extend the formalism to the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, a realistic theory of the pion. We go in both cases beyond all previous calculations and discover that all important features required by general physical considerations, like symmetry properties, sum rules and the polynomiality condition, are explicitly verified. We perform a numerical study of their behavior in the weak and strong coupling limits.

## Key claim

The authors calculate generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the pion using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach, demonstrating that all required symmetry properties, sum rules, and the polynomiality condition are explicitly verified, but only within a scheme where corrections beyond the handbag diagram vanish exactly in scalar electrodynamics and where the NJL model is regularized with Pauli-Villars subtraction.

## Method

The authors first develop the formalism in scalar electrodynamics with a zero-range interaction, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation to obtain an analytic bound-state amplitude and then computing the GPD as an integral over that amplitude. They then extend the calculation to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with spinor quarks, using the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the pion and evaluating the GPD via residue calculus in the regions \( \zeta < x < 1 \) (valence) and \( 0 < x < \zeta \) (non-valence), with Pauli-Villars regularization to handle divergences. The non-valence region additionally requires contributions from sigma-meson exchange diagrams to satisfy chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- In scalar electrodynamics with parameters \( m = 241\,\mathrm{MeV} \) and \( M = 139\,\mathrm{MeV} \), the root-mean-square radius of the scalar "pion" is \( \langle r_\pi \rangle^2 = (0.47\,\mathrm{fm})^2 \), compared to the experimental value of \( (0.66\,\mathrm{fm})^2 \).
- In the massless bound-state limit \( M = 0 \) of scalar electrodynamics, the analytic result for the root-mean-square radius is \( \langle r_\pi \rangle^2 = 3/(10\,m^2) = (0.45\,\mathrm{fm})^2 \).
- In the deep binding limit of scalar electrodynamics with \( M = m_\pi \approx 140 \) MeV, the GPD at zero momentum transfer takes the analytic form \( \mathcal{H}(x,\zeta,0) = 6\,\frac{2x-\zeta}{2}\left\{ \frac{x}{\zeta}\theta(\zeta-x)\theta(x) + \frac{1-x}{1-\zeta}\theta(x-\zeta)\theta(1-x) \right\} \).
- In the weak binding limit \( M^2 \rightarrow 4m^2 \) of scalar electrodynamics, the GPD reduces to a sum of two delta functions: \( \mathcal{H}(x,\zeta,t) = \frac{1}{2}\left[ \delta(x-1/2) + \delta(x-(\zeta+1)/2) \right] \frac{2-\zeta}{2}F(t) \).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of GPDs in models such as those by Polyakov (1999) and Tiburzi (2002) by using a fully covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach that preserves Lorentz and gauge invariance, sum rules, and the polynomiality condition. The NJL model calculation provides a realistic field-theoretic description of the pion as a Goldstone boson, enabling future comparisons with experimental measurements of deeply virtual Compton scattering on pions and guiding the development of more sophisticated QCD-based approaches.
