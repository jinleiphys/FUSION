# Dynamics of Resonances in Strongly Interacting Systems

> The effects of the propagation of particles which have a finite life-time and an according broad distribution in their mass spectrum are discussed in the context of a transport descriptions. In the first part some example cases of mesonic modes in nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures are presented. These equilibrium calculations illustrate the dynamical range of spectral distributions to be adequately covered by non-equilibrium description of the dynamics of two nuclei colliding at high energies. The second part addresses the problem of transport descriptions which properly account for the damping width of the particles. A systematic and general gradient approximation is presented in the form of diagrammatic rules which permit to derive a self-consistent transport scheme from the Kadanoff--Baym equation. The scheme is conserving and thermodynamically consistent provided the self-energies are obtained within the Phi-derivable two-particle irreducible (2PI) method of Baym. The merits, the limitations and partial cures of the limitations of this transport scheme are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent transport description of particles with broad mass-width (such as resonances in dense nuclear matter) can be obtained from the Kadanoff-Baym equations using a first gradient approximation together with the Phi-derivable two-particle irreducible (2PI) method of Baym, but only if the self-energies are obtained from that functional; the scheme is conserving and thermodynamically consistent, yet has limitations that are discussed along with partial cures.

## Method

The authors first perform self-consistent equilibrium calculations of the pion spectral function in nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures, using a model with nucleons, Delta(1232) resonances, and pions coupled via p-wave interactions plus a repulsive Migdal-type zero-range interaction. They then extend this to include rho and omega vector mesons coupled to the in-medium pion cloud, computing the di-lepton production rates from vector-meson decay. Finally, they present a systematic gradient approximation in diagrammatic form to derive a self-consistent transport scheme from the Kadanoff-Baym equation, based on the Phi-derivable 2PI method.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 nucleon per cubic femtometer (fm^3), corresponding to rho >= 10^15 g/cm^3 in nuclear collisions
* Temperatures in nuclear collisions exceed 100 MeV, corresponding to T >= 10^12 K
* Delta isobar and rho-meson resonances have vacuum lifetimes of less than 2 fm/c, with damping widths exceeding 100 MeV
* Typical mean temperatures in transport codes range between 50 to 150 MeV depending on beam energy
* At T=120 MeV and rho=rho_0, the omega-meson di-lepton rate shows comparable contributions from the fusion process (rho pi -> omega) and the scattering process (rho N -> omega N) at the nominal resonance position

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Kadanoff and Baym (1962) and the Phi-derivable method of Baym (1962) to systems where particles have broad damping widths that cannot be treated perturbatively, as occurs in high-energy nuclear collisions. It addresses deficiencies in earlier ad hoc recipes for including broad resonances in transport simulations, which violated detailed balance as discussed by Danielewicz and Bertsch (1991). The results enable improved transport descriptions of nuclear collisions at GSI, CERN, and RHIC, particularly for penetrating probes like di-leptons from vector-meson decay, and build on prior self-consistent calculations by Korpa and Malfliet, Lutz (2003), and others.
