# Novel picture of the soft modes at the QCD critical point based on the FRG method

> We investigate the soft mode at the QCD critical point (CP) on the basis of the functional renormalization group. We calculate the spectral functions in the meson channels in the two-flavor quark--meson model. Our result shows that the energy of the peak position of the particle--hole mode in the sigma channel becomes vanishingly small as the system approaches the QCD CP, which is a manifestation of the softening of the phonon mode. We also extract the dispersion curves of the mesonic and the phonon mode, a hydrodynamic mode which leads to a finding that the dispersion curve of the sigma-mesonic mode crosses the light-cone into the space-like momentum region, and then eventually merges into the phonon mode as the system approaches further close to the CP. This may suggest that the sigma-mesonic mode forms the soft mode together with the hydrodynamic mode at the CP.

## Key claim

The sigma-mesonic mode, not just the particle-hole (phonon) mode, can become soft at the QCD critical point, with the sigma-mesonic dispersion curve crossing the light-cone into the space-like momentum region and merging into the particle-hole mode as the system approaches the critical point.

## Method

The authors employed the functional renormalization group (FRG) method within the two-flavor quark-meson model, using a truncated effective average action with the local potential approximation for the meson part. They solved the flow equations for the two-point functions and performed analytic continuation to obtain the retarded spectral functions in the sigma and pion channels at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature \(T_c = 5.1 \pm 0.1\) MeV and critical chemical potential \(\mu_c = 286.6 \pm 0.2\) MeV, determined from the chiral condensate and sigma screening mass
- At \(T = T_c\) and \(\mu = 286.3\) MeV, the sigma-mesonic mode peak and particle-hole bump are visible in the spectral function
- At \(\mu = 286.575\) MeV, the sigma-mesonic dispersion relation touches the light cone
- At \(\mu = 286.59\) MeV, the sigma-mesonic mode with momentum \(p = 50\) MeV penetrates into the space-like momentum region and merges with the particle-hole bump
- The \(2\sigma\) decay threshold shifts downward as the system approaches the critical point, while the \(2\pi\) and \(\bar{\psi}\psi\) thresholds hardly change

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the O(4) model by Kamikado (2013) and the quark-meson model by Tripolt (2013, 2014) to analyze spectral functions near the QCD critical point. It confirms the softening of the particle-hole mode predicted by Fujii (2004) and Son (2004), but reveals a novel picture where the sigma-mesonic mode also becomes soft and merges with the hydrodynamic mode, contradicting earlier expectations that only the particle-hole mode serves as the soft mode. This enables future studies of mode coupling and level repulsion effects at the QCD critical point.
