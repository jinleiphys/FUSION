# Relativistic Ring-Diagram Nuclear Matter Calculations

> A relativistic extension of the particle-particle hole-hole ring-diagram many-body formalism is developed by using the Dirac equation for single-particle motion in the medium. Applying this new formalism, calculations are performed for nuclear matter. The results show that the saturation density is improved and the equation of state becomes softer as compared to corresponding Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. Using the Bonn A potential, nuclear matter is predicted to saturate at an energy per nucleon of --15.30 MeV and a density equivalent to a Fermi momentum of 1.38 fm$^{-1}$, in excellent agreement with empirical information. The compression modulus is 152 MeV at the saturation point.

# Relativistic Ring-Diagram Nuclear Matter Calculations

## Key claim

The relativistic ring-diagram approach improves nuclear matter saturation density and softens the equation of state compared to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations, but the ring effect is dramatically reduced in the relativistic framework relative to non-relativistic ring calculations, suggesting that DBHF may be a sufficient approximation for most purposes.

## Method

The authors extended the non-relativistic particle-particle hole-hole (pphh) ring-diagram formalism to a relativistic framework by using Dirac equation solutions for single-particle motion in the medium, with medium-dependent Dirac-spinor wavefunctions and relativistic self-energies (scalar and vector fields). They solved self-consistently for the scalar and vector fields using a relativistic model-space reaction matrix derived from the Thompson equation, then summed the infinite pphh ring-diagram series via a relativistic RPA-type secular equation. Calculations were performed for nuclear matter using the Bonn A potential with both model-space and continuous single-particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturates at an energy per nucleon of -15.30 MeV and a Fermi momentum of 1.38 fm$^{-1}$ using the Bonn A potential in the relativistic ring approach.
- The compression modulus at saturation is 152 MeV.
- The model-space momentum cutoff $k_M$ was set to 3.2 fm$^{-1}$.
- In non-relativistic ring calculations, saturation Fermi momentum is typically $\gtrsim$ 1.42 fm$^{-1}$; in DBHF calculations, $\gtrsim$ 1.40 fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic pphh ring-diagram formalism developed by the Stony Brook group (Y.H.K. et al., 1980s; J.M.K. et al., 1990) to include relativistic effects, building on the Dirac-Brueckner approach pioneered by the Brooklyn group (Anastasio et al., 1983) and developed by Horowitz and Serot (1984), Brockmann and Machleidt (1984, 1990), and ter Haar and Malfliet (1987). The results show that relativistic ring corrections to DBHF are relatively small, supporting the adequacy of DBHF as an approximation, while the softer equation of state and improved saturation density motivate further studies of higher-order diagrams in relativistic many-body theory.
