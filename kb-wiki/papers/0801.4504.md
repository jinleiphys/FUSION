# Critical review of [K- ppn] bound states

> We make a thorough study of the process of three body kaon absorption in nuclei, in connection with a recent FINUDA experiment which claims the existence of a deeply bound kaonic state from the observation of a peak in the Lambda d invariant mass distribution following K- absorption on Li6. We show that the peak is naturally explained in terms of K- absorption from three nucleons leaving the rest as spectators. We can also reproduce all the other observables measured in the same experiment and used to support the hypothesis of the deeply bound kaon state. Our study also reveals interesting aspects of kaon absorption in nuclei, a process that must be understood in order to make progress in the search for K- deeply bound states in nuclei.

# Critical review of [K⁻ppn] bound states

## Key claim

The peak in the Λd invariant mass distribution observed by FINUDA following K⁻ absorption on ⁶Li, which was interpreted as evidence for a deeply bound kaonic tribaryon state, is naturally explained by K⁻ absorption from three nucleons with the remaining three nucleons acting as spectators, without requiring any exotic bound state.

## Method

The authors model the ⁶Li nucleus as a local Fermi sea with a Thomas-Fermi potential and an energy gap Δ = 7.8 MeV adjusted to respect the reaction threshold for tritium emission. They perform Monte Carlo integration over the nuclear volume and Fermi momenta of three nucleons, weighted by the K⁻ atomic wavefunction for the 2p orbit, to generate distributions of the Λd invariant mass, angular correlations, missing mass, and momentum spectra for the reaction K⁻ + ³N → Λd, with the other three nucleons as spectators.

## Key numbers

- The calculated Λd invariant mass distribution peaks at M_Λd = 3252 MeV, matching the experimental peak position.
- The calculated width of the Λd invariant mass distribution is approximately 36 MeV, as reported in the experiment.
- The calculated total Λd momentum distribution peaks around 190 MeV/c, consistent with the experimental value.
- The Λ momentum distribution peaks around 635 MeV/c, with most events between 450 and 700 MeV/c.
- The energy gap parameter is Δ = 7.8 MeV, determined from the threshold condition for tritium emission.
- The experimental yield of the peak is Y_Λd = (4.4 ± 1.4) × 10⁻³ per stopped K⁻.
- From analysis of earlier ⁴He data, the three-body absorption rate is estimated at 0.65% with approximately 40% uncertainty from poor statistics.

## Context

This work directly challenges the interpretation of the FINUDA experiment (Agnello et al., 2007) which claimed a bound K⁻ tribaryon state with 58 MeV binding and 37 MeV width. The authors extend their earlier analysis of two-nucleon K⁻ absorption (Magas et al., 2006) to three-nucleon absorption, following the suggestion from a KEK experiment (Suzuki et al., 2007) that the peak could be a signature of three-body absorption rather than a bound state. The study enables a more complete understanding of kaon absorption mechanisms in nuclei, which is necessary for the continued search for possible deeply bound kaon states.
