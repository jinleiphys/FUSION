# The quantization of exotic states in SU(3) soliton models: A solvable quantum mechanical analog

> The distinction between the rigid rotor and Callan-Klebanov approaches to the quantization of SU(3) solitons is considered in the context of exotic baryons. A numerically tractable quantum mechanical analog system is introduced to test the reliability of the two quantization schemes. We find that in the equivalent of the large N_c limit of QCD, the Callan-Klebanov approach agrees with a numerical solution of the quantum mechanical analog. Rigid rotor quantization generally does not. The implications for exotic baryons are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

In a solvable quantum mechanical analog of SU(3) soliton quantization, the Callan-Klebanov approach reproduces the exact numerical spectrum up to expected 1/N_c corrections, while rigid-rotor quantization generally fails; this conclusion holds in the analog of the large N_c limit of QCD, where both the characteristic frequency of internal vibrations (ω_v) and the cyclotron frequency (ω_r) scale as N_c^0, so there is no parametric suppression of their mixing.

## Method

The authors constructed a quantum mechanical system of two point-like particles (one charged, one neutral) of equal mass M bound by a nearly harmonic potential with spring constant k, moving on a sphere of radius R with a magnetic monopole of strength g at its center. They solved the full quantum Hamiltonian numerically in a basis of Wigner D-functions for the charged particle and spherical harmonics for the neutral particle, truncating at J_max = L_max = 48 with qg = 40, and compared the lowest eigenvalues to semiclassical predictions from both rigid-rotor quantization (treating the composite as a single rigid charged particle) and Callan-Klebanov quantization (quantizing the three non-zero normal modes of the linearized classical equations of motion).

## Key numbers

* The rigid-rotor prediction for the lowest collective excitation energy is ΔE_R = qg/(2 M R^2) = ω_r, independent of the spring constant.
* The characteristic frequency of internal vibrations is ω_v = √(2k/M), and the ratio ω_v/ω_r = R^2 √(8Mk)/(qg) is of order N_c^0.
* Numerical calculations were performed with qg = 40 and basis cutoffs J_max = L_max = 48, yielding matrices of dimension approximately 21,000.
* In the limit ω_v/ω_r → ∞, the 1/N_c correction to the Callan-Klebanov prediction for the n00 state is (n² + 1)/(2 qg), giving corrections of 0.025, 0.0625, and 0.125 for the 100, 200, and 300 states respectively.
* At the largest computed ratio ω_v/ω_r = 3.84, the actual underprediction of the Callan-Klebanov formula was 0.0250, 0.0739, and 0.147 for the 100, 200, and 300 states.

## Context

This work extends the critique of rigid-rotor quantization for SU(3) solitons previously argued by Cohen (2003, 2004), Klebanov et al. (IKOR), and Pobylitsa (Pob), by providing explicit numerical verification that the Callan-Klebanov approach (Callan and Klebanov, 1990; IKOR) yields the correct spectrum in a tractable analog system. The model was introduced in earlier work by Cohen (2004) and Pobylitsa (Pob), but those studies did not demonstrate which quantization scheme actually produces the correct spectrum. The results contradict the rebuttal by Diakonov and Petrov (DP) and strongly suggest that the many calculations of exotic baryons using rigid-rotor quantization of SU(3) solitons are unjustified at large N_c.
