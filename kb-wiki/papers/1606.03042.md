# Parametrizations of the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors and Siegert's theorem

> The large $N_c$ limit provides relations that can be used to calculate the $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1232)$ quadrupole form factors at low and intermediate $Q^2$ under the assumption of the pion cloud dominance. There are two limitations in those parametrizations. First, the parametrization of the Coulomb quadrupole form factor underestimate the low $Q^2$ data. Second, when extrapolated for the timelike region, the form factors violate Siegert's theorem by terms of the order $1/N_c^2$. We propose here corrections to the parametrization of the electric quadrupole form factor, which violate Siegert's theorem only by terms of the order $1/N_c^4$. Combining the improved large $N_c$ pion cloud parametrizations with the valence quark contributions based on a covariant quark model for the quadrupole transition form factors, we obtain an extrapolation to the timelike region consistent with Siegert's theorem, and accomplish also a very good description of the data.

## Key claim

The large \(N_c\) pion cloud parametrizations of the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)\) quadrupole form factors violate Siegert's theorem by terms of order \(1/N_c^2\); the paper proposes a correction to the electric quadrupole form factor that reduces the violation to order \(1/N_c^4\), and when combined with valence quark contributions from a covariant spectator quark model, yields a description consistent with Siegert's theorem and the data.

## Method

The author modifies the large \(N_c\) relation for the electric quadrupole form factor \(G_E\) by introducing a factor \(1/(1 + Q^2/(M_\Delta^2 - M^2))\), which preserves the \(Q^2=0\) large \(N_c\) result while reducing the violation of Siegert's theorem at the pseudo-threshold to order \(1/N_c^4\). This improved pion cloud parametrization is then combined with valence quark contributions for \(G_E\) and \(G_C\) calculated from a covariant spectator quark model with \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave quark-diquark states, calibrated to lattice QCD data and extrapolated to the physical point.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-threshold is at \(Q_{pt}^2 = -(M_\Delta - M)^2\), where \(M\) and \(M_\Delta\) are the nucleon and \(\Delta(1232)\) masses.
* The original large \(N_c\) relations give a violation of Siegert's theorem \({\cal R}_{pt} = {\cal O}(1/N_c^2)\); the corrected parametrization gives \({\cal R}_{pt} = {\cal O}(1/N_c^4)\).
* The admixture of \(D\) states in the covariant spectator quark model is about 0.72% for both the \(D3\) and \(D1\) states.
* The pion cloud contribution for \(G_E\) falls as \(1/Q^8\) and for \(G_C\) as \(1/Q^6\); the valence quark contribution for \(G_E\) falls as \(1/Q^4\) and for \(G_C\) as \(1/Q^6\).
* The Galster parametrization uses parameters \(a=0.9\) and \(d=2.8\) for the neutron electric form factor.

## Context

This work extends large \(N_c\) pion cloud parametrizations of the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta(1232)\) quadrupole form factors (Buchmann 2009, Pascalutsa 2007) by enforcing consistency with Siegert's theorem, which previous parametrizations violated at the pseudo-threshold. The combined model improves upon earlier valence quark estimates (Capstick 1990, Stave 2008) that underestimated the data by about an order of magnitude, and provides a framework compatible with dynamical reaction models such as the Sato-Lee model (Juliá-Díaz 2007) and the DMT model (Kamalov 1999). Future lattice QCD simulations near the physical point could test the valence quark contributions at low \(Q^2\) and clarify the source of the remaining discrepancy in \(G_C\) below 0.2 GeV\(^2\).
