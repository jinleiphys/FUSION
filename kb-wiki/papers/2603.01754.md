# Spatially inhomogeneous confinement-deconfinement phase transition in accelerated gluodynamics

> This study explores confinement-deconfinement transition properties of SU($3$) Yang--Mills theory under weak accelerations at finite temperatures, using first-principles lattice simulations. The system is formulated in the Rindler spacetime, and the properties are studied from the perspective of a co-accelerating observer situated at the center of the lattice. We found that spatially separated confinement and deconfinement phases can coexist in the Rindler spacetime within certain intervals of temperature and acceleration. The position of the boundary between the phases is calculated as a function of temperature for several accelerations, and it is in accordance with the TE prediction, although a small deviation is observed. Moreover, in the weak acceleration regime, the critical temperature of the system is found to coincide with that of non-accelerated gluodynamics.

## Key claim

Spatially separated confinement and deconfinement phases can coexist in the Rindler spacetime within certain intervals of temperature and acceleration, with the boundary position following the Tolman-Ehrenfest (TE) prediction to within approximately 10% in the leading-order coefficient, although a small deviation persists in the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors formulated SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in Rindler spacetime and performed first-principles lattice simulations using the tree-level improved Symanzik action, with the Rindler metric incorporated by multiplying chromoelectric and chromomagnetic plaquettes by spatially dependent coefficients. They computed the renormalized local Polyakov loop and its susceptibility as functions of the spatial coordinate z for accelerations of 3, 6, 9, and 18 MeV, fitting the data to extract the critical distance z_c where the confinement-deconfinement transition occurs. Continuum limit extrapolations were performed using lattices with temporal extents N_t = 4, 5, 6, 8 while keeping fixed aspect ratios.

## Key numbers

- Accelerations studied: α = 3, 6, 9, 18 MeV, all satisfying α ≪ Λ ≈ 200 MeV (the characteristic energy scale of gluodynamics)
- Base lattice size: N_t × N_s² × N_z = 5 × 40² × 121; additional temporal extents N_t = 4, 6, 8 with fixed aspect ratios N_s/N_t = 8 and (N_z-1)/N_t = 24
- Critical temperature of accelerated gluodynamics T_c differs from non-accelerated critical temperature T_c0 by less than 0.3%
- Leading-order coefficient k_0 in the fit z_c = (k_0/α)(T/T_c - 1) + (k_1/α)(T/T_c - 1)² deviates from the TE prediction k_0 = 1 by more than 10% in the continuum limit
- Finite volume effects change k_0 by approximately 1% and T_c by less than 0.3% when extrapolating N_z → ∞ and N_s → ∞

## Context

This work extends the first non-perturbative study of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory under weak acceleration by Chernodub (2024), which assumed the validity of the TE law and incorporated acceleration via a temperature gradient, whereas the present study formulates the system directly in Rindler spacetime using lattice simulations. The results are compared with recent lattice studies of rotating gluodynamics by Braguta (2021, 2023, 2024), where a spatial transition separates central deconfined from outer confined regions but contradicts TE expectations due to asymmetry between chromomagnetic and chromoelectric fields; for the Rindler metric this asymmetry takes a simpler form. The findings enable future investigations of accelerated QCD at larger accelerations where Unruh temperature effects may become measurable.
