# Configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected triaxial relativistic mean-field wave functions. II. Microscopic analysis of low-lying states in magnesium isotopes

> The recently developed structure model that uses the generator coordinate method to perform configuration mixing of angular-momentum projected wave functions, generated by constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations for triaxial shapes (3DAMP+GCM), is applied in a systematic study of ground states and low-energy collective states in the even-even magnesium isotopes $^{20-40}$Mg. Results obtained using a relativistic point-coupling nucleon-nucleon effective interaction in the particle-hole channel, and a density-independent $δ$-interaction in the pairing channel, are compared to data and with previous axial 1DAMP+GCM calculations, both with a relativistic density functional and the non-relativistic Gogny force. The effects of the inclusion of triaxial degrees of freedom on the low-energy spectra and E2 transitions of magnesium isotopes are examined.

## Key claim

The inclusion of triaxial degrees of freedom (the gamma degree of freedom) via the 3DAMP+GCM framework generally lowers binding energies and increases B(E2) values in deformed magnesium isotopes, but the relativistic functional PC-F1 still predicts a spherical ground state for 32Mg at N=20, preserving the spherical shell closure, unless pairing strength parameters are specifically adjusted to empirical pairing gaps in that region.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF+BCS) calculations for triaxial shapes across the even-even magnesium isotopes 20-40Mg, using the relativistic point-coupling interaction PC-F1 in the particle-hole channel and a density-independent delta-interaction in the pairing channel. They then applied three-dimensional angular-momentum projection (3DAMP) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) for configuration mixing of the projected wave functions, solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin integral equation to obtain excitation spectra, B(E2) transition rates, and spectroscopic quadrupole moments. Results were compared to previous axial 1DAMP+GCM calculations with the same functional and with non-relativistic Gogny-force calculations by Guzman (2002).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state dynamical correlation energy reaches a maximum of approximately 4 MeV at N=14 and is reduced to about 1 MeV for the neutron magic numbers N=8 and N=20.
- For 32Mg with the original pairing strengths (Vn = -308 MeV fm3, Vp = -321 MeV fm3), the 3DAMP+GCM calculation yields a B(E2; 0+1 to 2+1) value about a factor three smaller than the empirical value; adjusting pairing strengths to Vn = -465 MeV fm3 and Vp = -350 MeV fm3 increases the calculated transition rate to 330.1 e2fm4.
- In 26Mg, the 3DAMP+GCM calculation yields an enhancement of approximately 25% for the B(E2; 0+1 to 2+1) compared to the axial 1DAMP+GCM result.
- For 20Mg, the 1DAMP+GCM calculation with PC-F1 gives B(E2; 0+1 to 2+1) = 332 e2fm4; for 36Mg, the same calculation gives 460 e2fm4.
- The liquid-drop model formula, using the recently measured E(2+1) of 1598(10) keV for 20Mg, predicts B(E2; 0+1 to 2+1) = 368.9(23) e2fm4; for 36Mg with E(2+1) = 660(6) keV, the prediction is 595.4(54) e2fm4.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic 3DAMP+GCM model previously tested on 24Mg (Yao 2010) to a systematic study of the magnesium isotopic chain, building on earlier axial 1DAMP+GCM studies using both relativistic functionals (Niksic 2006) and the non-relativistic Gogny force (Guzman 2002). It also relates to non-relativistic 3DAMP+GCM models based on Skyrme forces (Bender 2008) and the Gogny force (Rodriguez 2010). The results enable future applications of the relativistic 3DAMP+GCM framework to shape transitions and shape coexistence in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, and provide a benchmark for comparison with the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian approach (Niksic 2009).
