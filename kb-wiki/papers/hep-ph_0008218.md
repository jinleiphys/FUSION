# Van Hove Singularities in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> General arguments as well as different approximations for the in-medium quark propagator in a quark-gluon plasma lead to quark dispersion relations that exhibit a minimum in one branch (plasmino). This minimum causes Van Hove singularities in the dilepton production rate and mesonic correlators, which might have observable consequences.

# Van Hove Singularities in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The minimum in the plasmino branch of the quark dispersion relation, which appears in both the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation and a gluon condensate approach, causes Van Hove singularities in the dilepton production rate and mesonic spectral functions; however, whether these singularities can be observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions depends on how much they are covered and smeared out by higher order effects such as bremsstrahlung, damping, finite momenta, and the space-time evolution of the fireball.

## Method
The author computes the quark self energy and propagator using two independent approximations: the HTL resummed propagator (with effective thermal quark mass m_q^2 = g^2 T^2 / 6) and a non-perturbative gluon condensate propagator that incorporates chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates from lattice QCD at T = 2 T_c. The pole-pole contributions to the spectral function of the pseudo-scalar temporal correlator and the dilepton production rate are evaluated, with the Van Hove singularities arising from the vanishing group velocity at the minimum of the plasmino dispersion relation.

## Key numbers
- The plasmino branch minimum in the HTL approximation occurs at k = 0.408 m_q.
- The first Van Hove singularity in the pseudo-scalar spectral function appears at ω = 0.47 m_q, from the transition between the upper quark branch and the plasmino branch.
- The second Van Hove singularity occurs at the threshold ω = 1.86 m_q, from plasmino-antiplasmino annihilation.
- The effective thermal quark mass is m_q^2 = g^2 T^2 / 6, and the calculation uses m_q / T = 1, corresponding to g = √6.
- For the gluon condensate propagator, the dispersion relations are shown at T = 2 T_c.
- Effective quark masses of order 500 MeV are expected from quasiparticle models, placing structures below about 1 GeV invariant mass.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of Van Hove singularities, originally introduced in solid state physics by Van Hove (1953) and applied to two-dimensional electron gases in high-T_c superconductors by Markiewicz (1997), to the quark-gluon plasma. The HTL resummation technique was developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to cure gauge dependence and infrared divergences in finite-temperature gauge theories. The gluon condensate approach follows the work of Schaefer (1999) and uses lattice data from Boyd (1996). The temporal correlators are compared to lattice calculations by Boyd (1994) and Florkowski (1994). The results enable future lattice QCD studies of quark dispersion relations and spectral functions, and suggest that low-mass dilepton measurements at RHIC and LHC could reveal structures unique to the deconfined phase, in contrast to the hadronic phase where structures such as the rho peak are washed out by in-medium collisions (Rapp, 1999).
