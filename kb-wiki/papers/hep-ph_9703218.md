# The reaction $πN \to ππN$ above threshold in chiral perturbation theory

> Single pion production off nucleons is studied in the framework of relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory at tree level with the inclusion of the terms from the dimension two effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian. The five appearing low-energy constants are fixed from pion-nucleon scattering data. Despite the simplicity of the approach, most of the existing data for total and differential cross sections as well as for the angular correlation functions for incoming pion kinetic energies up to 400 MeV can be satisfactorily described.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a tree-level calculation in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory, including terms from the dimension-two pion-nucleon Lagrangian, can satisfactorily describe most existing data for total and differential cross sections and angular correlation functions for the reaction πN → ππN at incoming pion kinetic energies up to 400 MeV, despite the simplicity of the approach and the absence of loop contributions or explicit resonance degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic tree-level calculation of the reaction πN → ππN in chiral perturbation theory, including both the leading-order Born terms from the dimension-one Lagrangian and the next-order contributions from the dimension-two pion-nucleon Lagrangian characterized by five low-energy constants c₁, c₂′, c₂″, c₃, and c₄. These five constants were fixed from a tree-level fit to pion-nucleon scattering subthreshold parameters, with numerical values given in GeV⁻¹. The invariant amplitudes were computed from tree diagrams with at most one insertion from the dimension-two Lagrangian, treating nucleons as fully relativistic Dirac fields, and the resulting cross sections and angular correlation functions were compared to experimental data for five reaction channels.

## Key numbers

- Threshold amplitude D₁: Born plus cᵢ contribution gives (2.57 ± 0.10) fm³, compared to empirical value D₁ᵉˣᵖ = (2.26 ± 0.10) fm³
- Threshold amplitude D₂: Born plus cᵢ contribution gives (−9.46 ± 0.06) fm³, compared to empirical value D₂ᵉˣᵖ = (−9.05 ± 0.36) fm³
- Low-energy constants (in GeV⁻¹): c₁ = −0.64 ± 0.14, c₂′ = −5.63 ± 0.10, c₂″ = 7.41 ± 0.10, c₃ = −3.90 ± 0.09, c₄ = 2.25 ± 0.09
- Isospin-symmetric threshold kinetic energy: Tπᵗʰʳⁱˢᵒ = 170.71 MeV
- Pion mass Mπ = 139.57 MeV, nucleon mass m = 938.27 MeV, pion decay constant Fπ = 92.4 MeV, axial coupling gA = 1.32
- The calculation covers incoming pion kinetic energies up to Tπ = 400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous threshold calculation of the amplitudes D₁ and D₂ (Bernard, Kaiser, Meißner, 1995) to energies above threshold, motivated by the finding that the dominant corrections at threshold come from tree graphs with dimension-two insertions rather than loop graphs. The calculation is closest in spirit to the work of Jemi and Oset (1994), who supplemented Beringer's Born terms with explicit Δ and Roper contributions, but the present approach instead encodes resonance effects through the low-energy constants cᵢ without introducing explicit resonance degrees of freedom. The results enable future comparisons with full one-loop calculations above threshold and help identify kinematics where explicit resonance effects become important.
