# Nuclear chiral doublet bands data tables

> Since the prediction of nuclear chirality in 1997, tremendous progresses both theoretically and experimentally have been achieved. Experimentally, 59 chiral doublet bands in 47 chiral nuclei (including 8 nuclei with multiple chiral doublets) have been reported in $A\sim80,~100,~130$,~and~190 mass regions. The spins, parities, energies, ratios of the magnetic dipole transition strengths to the electric quadrupole transition strengths, and related references for these nuclei are compiled and listed in Table 1. For these nuclei with the magnetic dipole transition strengths and the electric quadrupole transition strengths measured, the corresponding results are given in Table 2. A brief discussion is provided after the presentation of energy $E$, energy difference $ΔE$, energy staggering parameter $S(I)$, rotational frequency $ω$, kinematic moment of inertia $\mathcal{J}^{(1)}$, dynamic moment of inertia $\mathcal{J}^{(2)}$, and ratio of the magnetic dipole transition strength to the electric quadrupole transition strength $B(M1)/B(E2)$ versus spin $I$ in each mass region.

## Key claim

This paper compiles all experimentally reported chiral doublet bands (59 bands in 47 nuclei) as of 2018, but notes that lifetime measurements essential for extracting absolute electromagnetic transition probabilities remain rare for most chiral nuclei candidates, meaning the identification of many candidates relies on indirect fingerprints rather than definitive transition data.

## Method

The authors collected published experimental data on spins, parities, energies, and B(M1)/B(E2) ratios for all reported chiral doublet bands across the A~80, 100, 130, and 190 mass regions. They organized these data into two tables: Table 1 compiles the basic spectroscopic information and references for all 59 bands, while Table 2 gives measured B(M1) and B(E2) values where available. They then generated systematic plots of energy E, energy difference ΔE, energy staggering parameter S(I), rotational frequency ω, kinematic moment of inertia J(1), dynamic moment of inertia J(2), and B(M1)/B(E2) versus spin I for each mass region.

## Key numbers

- 59 chiral doublet bands in 47 chiral nuclei (including 8 nuclei with multiple chiral doublets) have been reported as of 2018
- The lowest spin at which nuclear chirality occurs is 4ħ (in 106Mo and 110Ru), and the highest is 29ħ (in 136Nd)
- Energy differences ΔE between partner bands are below 600 keV for all chiral doublet bands except for 100Tc, 102Rh, 104Ag, 106Ag, 107Ag, and 126I
- The change in energy staggering parameter S(I) values is around 20 keV/ħ in the A~80 mass region, decreasing to 10 keV/ħ for most nuclei in A~100, 130, and 190 mass regions, except for 140Eu, 194Tl, and 198Tl where the change is around 20 keV/ħ
- For MχD in 103Rh, there are three pairs of chiral doublet bands; for 136Nd, there are five pairs

## Context

This work extends the data compilation efforts following the original prediction of nuclear chirality by Frauendorf and Meng (1997) and subsequent experimental discoveries across four mass regions. The tables and systematic plots enable researchers to test theoretical models such as the particle rotor model (PRM), tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT), and projected shell model (PSM) against the full body of experimental data. The compilation also highlights where lifetime measurements are still needed to confirm chiral assignments, particularly for nuclei where only B(M1)/B(E2) ratios extracted from gamma-ray intensities (rather than absolute transition probabilities) are available.
