# Closest approach of a quantum projectile

> We consider the simplest case of Rutherford scattering, i.e. the head-on collision, where the projectile is treated quantum mechanically. The convexity of repulsive Coulomb force invokes a disagreement between the Ehrenfest's and Hamilton's dynamics. We show that the quantum projectile cannot approach as close as the corresponding classical one, and that the average distance of closest approach depends on the position spread of the wave function describing the projectile.

# Closest approach of a quantum projectile

## Key claim

The paper shows that for head-on Rutherford scattering, a quantum projectile described by a wave packet cannot approach as close to the target nucleus as a classical projectile with the same initial position and momentum, and that the average distance of closest approach depends on the initial position spread of the wave function. This disagreement arises specifically because the convexity of the repulsive Coulomb force causes the Ehrenfest average force to differ from the classical force evaluated at the average position.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a Gaussian wave packet (representing an alpha particle) moving in the Coulomb potential of a fixed gold nucleus, using the Cayley form (Crank-Nicolson method) to maintain unitarity. They compare the quantum expectation values of position and force to the classical trajectories obtained by integrating Hamilton's equations for the same initial position and momentum. The initial wave packet is centered at x0 = -500 fm with average momentum p0 = 386.13 MeV/c, and the position spread sigma is varied between 20 fm and 50 fm.

## Key numbers

- Initial position of the projectile: x0 = -500 fm
- Initial momentum: p0 = 386.13 MeV/c, corresponding to a kinetic energy of 20 MeV
- Coulomb potential parameters: Z_He = 2, Z_Au = 79, hbar c = 197.3269631 MeV fm, fine-structure constant alpha = 1/137.035999679
- Position spreads studied: sigma = 20 fm and sigma = 50 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vyas (2020) on the disagreement between Ehrenfest and Hamilton dynamics in Coulomb scattering, by explicitly computing the time evolution of quantum wave packets. The results contradict the classical expectation that the projectile's trajectory is independent of its quantum state, and show that the convexity of the 1/x potential (unlike quadratic potentials) produces measurable deviations. The authors suggest that extending this study to two dimensions could help understand nuclear reactions where charged projectiles must overcome the Coulomb barrier of target nuclei.
