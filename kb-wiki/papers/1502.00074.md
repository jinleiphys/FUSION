# Tunneling of atoms, nuclei and molecules

> This is a brief review of few relevant topics on tunneling of composite particles and how the coupling to intrinsic and external degrees of freedom affects tunneling probabilities. I discuss the phenomena of resonant tunneling, different barriers seen by subsystems, damping of resonant tunneling by level bunching and continuum effects due to particle dissociation.

## Key claim

This review argues that tunneling probabilities for composite particles are significantly altered by coupling to intrinsic and external degrees of freedom, producing effects such as resonant tunneling, damping, and enhancement or suppression that are not captured by single-particle tunneling models.

## Method

The author reviews computational methods for time-dependent tunneling, including unitary time-evolution approximations applied to a space lattice, and presents results from coupled-channel calculations for two-body systems tunneling through rectangular barriers. Specific applications are drawn from the author's own work on deuteron fusion, proton-hydrogen and proton-helium charge exchange, and molecular diffusion in optical lattices.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(E_B = -2.224\) MeV, used as an example of a loosely-bound nuclear system
- For p + He charge exchange, the damping parameter \(\Gamma = 5\) eV reproduces numerical calculations of the exchange probability
- The exchange probability for p + He drops sharply to zero when the proton energy is smaller than 18.7 eV, the threshold for the \(1s^2(^1S_0) \rightarrow 1s2s(^3S)\) transition in He
- For molecular diffusion in an optical lattice, the spreading width follows \(\sigma(t) = C \left( \hbar^2 t / (m b \sigma_0^2) \right)^{1/2}\) after a transient time, with constants \(C\) and \(b\) depending on lattice parameters

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work on tunneling of composite particles (Bertulani 2011) and draws on specific calculations for two-body systems (Saito and Kayanuma 1994), molecular transmission (Garg and Singh 2005), and deuteron fusion (Bertulani, Flambaum, and Zelevinsky 2007). It also discusses the quantum Munchhausen effect (Flambaum and Zelevinsky 1999) and charge exchange in p + H and p + He collisions (Bertulani and Ponomarev 2000). The review enables further theoretical work on tunneling times, radiation emission during tunneling, and diffusion of molecules in optical lattices, while noting that the time dependence of dissociated atom spreading in lattices is not yet understood.
