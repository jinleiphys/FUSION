# Cancellation of the sigma mode in the thermal pion gas by quark Pauli blocking

> We calculate the pressure of the interacting pion gas using the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to the relativistic virial expansion with Breit-Wigner phase shifts for the $σ$- and $\varrho$- meson resonances. The repulsive phase shift $δ^2_0$ is taken from quark interchange model of Barnes and Swanson [Phys. Rev. D 46 (1992) 131] in very good agreement with experimental data. In this work we show that the cancellation of the attractive (I = 0) and repulsive (I = 2) isospin channel contributions to the scalar $π-π$ interaction in the low-energy region that is known for the vacuum phase shifts, takes place also at finite temperature. This happens despite the strong medium dependence of these phase shifts that enters our model by the temperature dependence of the $σ$- meson and constituent quark masses because for these masses the relation $m_σ(T) \approx 2 m_q (T)$ holds and the scattering length approximation is valid as long as the strong decay channel $σ\to ππ$ is open. Exploiting the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model for describing the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry in the vacuum and its restoration at finite temperature, we justify with our approach that the $σ$-meson should be absent from the hadron resonance gas description at low temperatures because the above cancellation holds. However, since this cancellation breaks down in the vicinity of the hadronization transition, where due to chiral symmetry restoration the decay channel $σ\to ππ$ closes and the $σ$- meson becomes a good resonance, the latter should be included into the statistical model description of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the cancellation of the attractive isospin-0 sigma meson channel against the repulsive isospin-2 channel in pion-pion scattering, known for vacuum phase shifts, persists at finite temperature as long as the relation M_sigma(T) approximately equals 2 m_q(T) holds and the strong decay channel sigma to pi pi remains open; this cancellation breaks down near the chiral restoration transition temperature, where the sigma meson becomes a good resonance and should be included in hadron resonance gas models.

## Method

The authors calculate the pressure of the interacting pion gas using the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach to the relativistic virial expansion, employing Breit-Wigner phase shifts for the sigma and rho meson resonances and a phase shift from the quark interchange model of Barnes and Swanson (1992) for the repulsive isospin-2 channel. They introduce temperature dependence into the phase shifts by using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the temperature-dependent sigma meson mass, constituent quark mass, and decay widths, then evaluate the partial pressure contributions from each channel via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula.

## Key numbers

* The sigma meson scattering length a^0_0 = 0.148 at T=0, increasing to 0.237 at T=0.2 GeV, while the isospin-2 scattering length a^0_2 = -0.036 at T=0, changing to -0.043 at T=0.2 GeV (NJL model results).
* The ratio a^0_0/(5 a^0_2) = -0.826 at T=0, evolving to -0.996 at T=0.19 GeV and -1.11 at T=0.2 GeV, showing near-cancellation below T approximately 0.75 T_c.
* The pseudocritical temperature of the chiral crossover is T_c = 0.225 GeV; the pion Mott dissociation temperature is T^pi_Mott = 0.244 GeV; the sigma meson decay channel closes at T^sigma_diss = 0.228 GeV.
* The vacuum constituent quark mass is m = 400 MeV, with NJL model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 0.5879 GeV, current quark mass m_0 = 5.582 MeV, scalar coupling G_s Lambda^2 = 2.442, vector coupling G_v Lambda^2 = 2.4.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of sigma meson cancellation in the pion gas by Venugopalan and Prakash (1992) and the discussion by Broniowski and collaborators (2015) on whether the sigma should be included in hadron resonance gas models. It uses the NJL model framework previously applied by Quack and collaborators (1994) for finite-temperature scattering lengths, and the quark interchange model of Barnes and Swanson (1992) for the repulsive phase shift. The results reconcile the apparent contradiction between the cancellation at low temperatures and the need to include the sigma meson in statistical model descriptions of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions near the hadronization transition, as argued by Andronic and collaborators (2008).
