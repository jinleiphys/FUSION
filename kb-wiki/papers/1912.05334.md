# Calculated solar-neutrino capture rate for a radiochemical 205 Tl-based solar-neutrino detector

> Radiochemical experiments for low-energy solar-neutrino detection have been making headlines by exploiting the isotopes \iso{Cl}{37} and \iso{Ga}{71}. Such a very low-threshold measurement of this type can also be performed using \iso{Tl}{205}, which has been considered for decades for this purpose. A unique feature of this detector nucleus is the integration is the solar-neutrino flux over millions of years owing to its long-living daughter \iso{Pb}{205}. In this study we have calculated for the first time the cross section for the charged-current solar-neutrino scattering off \iso{Tl}{205}. Taking into account the solar-model-predicted neutrino fluxes and the electron-neutrino survival probabilities, a solar-neutrino capture rate of 62.2 $\pm 8.6$ SNU is determined, a value significantly smaller than in previous estimates.

## Key claim

The calculated solar-neutrino capture rate for a radiochemical 205Tl-based detector is 62.2 ± 8.6 SNU, significantly smaller than previous estimates, but this result depends on adopting an effective axial-vector coupling in the range g_A = 0.75–1.00 and adjusting low-lying state energies to experimental values.

## Method

The neutrino-nucleus cross section was calculated using the Donnelly-Walecka method for semi-leptonic processes, with nuclear-structure calculations performed in the shell-model framework using the code NuShellX@MSU and the khhe Hamiltonian in the complete valence space for protons and neutrons. The energies of the dominating low-lying states in 205Pb were adjusted to their experimental values, and the effective axial-vector coupling was varied between g_A = 0.75 and 1.00 to estimate nuclear-structure uncertainties. The capture rate was then obtained by folding the cross section with solar-model-predicted neutrino fluxes from the BS05(OP) model and electron-neutrino survival probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for the 205Tl → 205Pb transition: 50.6 ± 0.5 keV, the lowest threshold among radiochemical approaches for solar-neutrino detection.
- Half-life of 205Pb: 1.73(7) × 10^7 years, enabling integration of solar-neutrino flux over 4.31 million years.
- Solar-neutrino capture rate for 205Tl: 62.2 ± 8.6 SNU (1 SNU = 10^-36 captures per target atom per second).
- Capture rate without electron-neutrino survival probability: 100.2 SNU with g_A = 0.75 and 132.4 SNU with g_A = 1.00.
- Electron-neutrino survival probabilities used: 0.54 for pp, 7Be, and 13N neutrinos; 0.50 for pep, 15O, and 17F neutrinos; 0.36 for 8B neutrinos.

## Context

This work provides the first calculated cross section for charged-current solar-neutrino scattering off 205Tl, updating earlier estimates from the LOREX experiment (LORandite EXperiment) studies by Freedman (1976, 1988), Kienle (1988), and Henning (1988), and later work by Pavicevic (1988, 2012). The significantly lower capture rate than previously anticipated resolves a major uncertainty for the proposed 205Tl radiochemical detector, which could monitor the average solar-neutrino flux over millions of years due to the long half-life of 205Pb, enabling studies of long-term solar stability.
