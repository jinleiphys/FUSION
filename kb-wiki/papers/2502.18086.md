# Probing $Λ$ potential at high and low densities via \(^3_Λ\)H production in C+C reactions

> The production of Lambda hyperons and light hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions provides critical insights into the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and hyperon interactions in dense matter, addressing the longstanding ``hyperon puzzle'' in neutron star physics. Using the INCL+ABLA and AMPT-HC models, we systematically investigate C+C collisions at beam energies of 1.1 and 1.9 GeV/nucleon to explore the sensitivity of hyperon and hypernuclei yields to the EoS and hyperon potential. Our results reveal that Lambda hyperon production is predominantly influenced by the nuclear EoS, while light hypernuclei formation (e.g., \(^3_Λ\)H) exhibits stronger sensitivity to the hyperon potential at high/low densities with the incident beam energies of 1.1/1.9 GeV. The study provides a framework for future experiments at facilities like HIAF and FAIR/GSI to resolve the hyperon puzzle, thus advancing our understanding of quantum chromodynamics in high-density regimes.

# Probing Λ potential at high and low densities via \(^3_Λ\)H production in C+C reactions

## Key claim

Light hypernuclei formation (e.g., \(^3_Λ\)H) exhibits stronger sensitivity to the hyperon potential than to the nuclear equation of state, but whether the high-density or low-density hyperon potential dominates depends on the beam energy, high-density potential dominates at 1.1 GeV/nucleon (sub-threshold) while low-density potential dominates at 1.9 GeV/nucleon (above-threshold). This is a model-dependent finding that requires experimental validation.

## Method

The authors used two complementary transport models, INCL+ABLA and AMPT-HC, to simulate \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C collisions at beam energies of 1.1 and 1.9 GeV/nucleon. The INCL+ABLA model provided double differential cross-sections and rapidity distributions of Λ hyperons and light hypernuclei, while the AMPT-HC model with a phase-space coalescence afterburner was used to systematically vary the nuclear equation of state (soft with incompressibility K=200 MeV vs. stiff with K=400 MeV) and the Λ hyperon potential (with/without potential, and with high-density-only potential) to isolate their respective effects on yields.

## Key numbers

- Λ hyperon to proton yield ratio is more sensitive to the nuclear EoS than to the hyperon potential at both beam energies; the effect is stronger at the sub-threshold energy of 1.1 GeV/nucleon.
- For \(^3_Λ\)H production at 1.1 GeV/nucleon: soft EoS with full hyperon potential yields 0.34×10\(^{-5}\) events; switching to stiff EoS reduces yield by 50%; removing the hyperon potential reduces yield by 53%; applying hyperon potential only at high densities reduces yield by 35%.
- For \(^3_Λ\)H production at 1.9 GeV/nucleon: soft EoS with full hyperon potential yields 0.17×10\(^{-4}\) events; switching to stiff EoS reduces yield by 6%; removing the hyperon potential reduces yield by 48%; applying hyperon potential only at high densities reduces yield by 6%.
- For Λ production at 1.1 GeV/nucleon: soft EoS with full hyperon potential yields 0.215×10\(^{-3}\) events; stiff EoS reduces yield by 23%; removing hyperon potential reduces yield by 10%; high-density-only hyperon potential reduces yield by 3%.
- For Λ production at 1.9 GeV/nucleon: soft EoS with full hyperon potential yields 0.533×10\(^{-2}\) events; stiff EoS reduces yield by 11%; removing hyperon potential reduces yield by 8%; high-density-only hyperon potential reduces yield by 1%.
- The laboratory beam energy threshold for Λ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is approximately 1.58 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Yong et al. (2021) which found that hyperon production is predominantly sensitive to the nuclear EoS rather than the hyperon potential. The novel contribution is the finding that light hypernuclei (e.g., \(^3_Λ\)H) can probe high-density hyperon potentials at sub-threshold beam energies and low-density potentials at above-threshold energies, which has not been previously investigated in low-energy, light reaction systems. The study provides a framework for future experiments at HIAF (China) and FAIR/GSI (Germany) to constrain the hyperon potential and address the neutron star hyperon puzzle. The authors note that these conclusions may be model-dependent and call for more comprehensive studies using different equations of state, hyperon potential forms, and reaction systems.
