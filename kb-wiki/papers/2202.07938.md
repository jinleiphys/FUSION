# Magnetic field induced hair structure in charmonium gluon-dissociation

> We study electromagnetic field effect on charmonium gluon-dissociation in quark-gluon plasma. With the effective Hamiltonian derived from QCD multipole expansion under an external electromagnetic field, we first solve the two-body Schrödinger equation for a pair of charm quarks with mean field potentials for color and electromagnetic interactions and obtain the charmonium binding energies and wave functions, and then calculate the gluon-dissociation cross-section and decay width by taking the color electric and magnetic dipole interactions as perturbations above the mean field and employing Fermi's Golden Rule. Considering the charmonium deformation in magnetic field, the discrete Landau energy levels make the dissociation cross-section grow hair, and the electric dipole channel is significantly changed, especially for the $P-$wave states $χ_{c0}$ and $χ_{c\pm}$. From our numerical calculation, the magnetic field strength $eB=5~m_π^2$ already changes the gluon dissociation strongly, which may indicate measurable effects in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an external magnetic field induces a "hair structure" (discrete peaks) in the gluon-dissociation cross-section of charmonia due to Landau energy levels in the final octet state, with the condition that the effect becomes significant at magnetic field strengths as low as \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\), which may be measurable in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors derived an effective Hamiltonian from QCD multipole expansion including an external electromagnetic field, then solved the two-body Schrödinger equation for a charm quark pair using mean-field potentials for both color (Cornell potential at zero temperature, screened potential at finite temperature) and electromagnetic interactions. They obtained charmonium binding energies and wave functions by expanding in spherical harmonics with a cutoff at \(l_{max}=7\) and solving via the inverse power method. The gluon-dissociation cross-section and decay width were then calculated by treating color electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) interactions as perturbations above the mean field and applying Fermi's Golden Rule, with the final octet state described by Landau levels (two-dimensional harmonic oscillator in the transverse plane and plane wave longitudinally).

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\) already changes the gluon dissociation strongly, indicating measurable effects in high-energy nuclear collisions.
- Charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.29\) GeV; Cornell potential parameters fitted at zero field: \(\alpha = 0.4105\) and \(\sigma = 0.2~\text{GeV}^2\).
- Critical temperature for deconfinement phase transition: \(T_c = 172\) MeV.
- Saturated binding energy at melting temperature: \(\epsilon(T_m) = (1+|m|) qB / m_c\).
- Saturated transverse mean square radius at melting temperature: \(\langle \rho^2 \rangle(T_m) = (1+|m|) 2 / (qB)\).
- In Au-Au collisions at RHIC, peak magnetic field \(eB \sim 5 m_\pi^2\); in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, \(eB \sim 70 m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium static properties in magnetic fields (Alford 2013, Marasinghe 2011, Zhao 2020) and gluon-dissociation calculations in hot medium (Liu 2013, Chen 2017, Chen 2018) by incorporating the external electromagnetic field into the dissociation dynamics. It also builds on the QCD multipole expansion framework developed by Peskin (1979), Bhanot (1979), Yan (1980), and Kuang (1981). The finding that magnetic fields create discrete Landau level structure in the dissociation cross-section enables future experimental searches for magnetic field effects on charmonium suppression patterns in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
