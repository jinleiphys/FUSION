# Finite volume corrections and low momentum cuts in the thermodynamics of quantum gases

> The conjecture, that the finite volume corrections to the thermodynamic functions can be correctly reproduced by using the thermodynamic limit with low particle momenta cutoff is examined in a very transparent example of an ideal boson gas in one dimension. We show that this conjecture is always true in principle, and derive convenient relations for the momentum cutoff dependence on thermal parameters in the asymptotic limits of large and small volume.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite volume corrections to thermodynamic functions of an ideal boson gas can be exactly reproduced in the thermodynamic limit by introducing a low-momentum cutoff, but the cutoff parameter is only simply related to the system size (k_c = pi/2L) for volumes larger than a few fermis; for smaller volumes the cutoff becomes a complicated function of the system parameters.

## Method

The authors analytically and numerically compare the exact thermodynamic potential of a one-dimensional ideal boson gas in a finite box (with Dirichlet boundary conditions) to its thermodynamic-limit integral approximation with a low-momentum cutoff. They derive the cutoff parameter n_c from the condition that the integral from n_c to infinity equals the exact sum, and examine its behavior in the large-volume and small-volume asymptotic limits using Euler-Maclaurin and exponential approximations, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The thermodynamic potential integral approximation is "good" (within 1% of exact) only for L > 166 fm at T = 120 MeV, m = 140 MeV, mu = 0
- The corrected approximation Omega_cor = Omega_int - (1/2)Omega_0 is good for L > 3.7 fm, with Omega_0 = -44.8 MeV at T = 120 MeV
- The cutoff approximation Omega_cut with n_c = 1/2 (k_c = pi/2L) is good for L > 5.6 fm at T = 120 MeV
- The correction term Omega_0 is well-approximated only for L > 8.4 fm
- The integral approximation for pressure is good for L > 4.4 fm
- In the small-volume limit, the condition L < 0.076 n_c fm must hold for the exponential approximation to be valid

## Context

This work extends the conjecture from numerical studies by BRS, KMR, and EKS (all circa 2015-2016) that finite volume effects in heavy-ion collision thermodynamics can be approximated by low-momentum cutoffs in the thermodynamic limit. The paper provides the first analytic proof of this conjecture using a one-dimensional ideal boson gas, and derives explicit cutoff relations that can be applied to statistical model analyses of particle production in small systems such as those created in elementary or peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results complement earlier finite-volume studies by Anton (2005-2015) and collaborators on exact conservation laws in small systems.
