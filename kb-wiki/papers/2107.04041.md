# A Large-$N$ Expansion for Minimum Bias

> Despite being the overwhelming majority of events produced in hadron or heavy ion collisions, minimum bias events do not enjoy a robust first-principles theoretical description as their dynamics are dominated by low-energy quantum chromodynamics. In this paper, we present a novel expansion scheme of the cross section for minimum bias events that exploits an ergodic hypothesis for particles in the events and events in an ensemble of data. We identify power counting rules and symmetries of minimum bias from which the form of the squared matrix element can be expanded in symmetric polynomials of the phase space coordinates. This expansion is entirely defined in terms of observable quantities, in contrast to models of heavy ion collisions that rely on unmeasurable quantities like the number of nucleons participating in the collision, or tunes of parton shower parameters to describe the underlying event in proton collisions. The expansion parameter that we identify from our power counting is the number of detected particles $N$ and as $N\to\infty$ the variance of the squared matrix element about its mean, constant value on phase space vanishes. With this expansion, we show that the transverse momentum distribution of particles takes a universal form that only depends on a single parameter, has a fractional dispersion relation, and agrees with data in its realm of validity. We show that the constraint of positivity of the squared matrix element requires that all azimuthal correlations vanish in the $N\to\infty$ limit at fixed center-of-mass energy, as observed in data. The approach we follow allows for a unified treatment of small and large system collective behavior, being equally applicable to describe, e.g., elliptic flow in PbPb collisions and the "ridge" in pp collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel expansion scheme for minimum bias events in hadron and heavy ion collisions, where the expansion parameter is the number of detected particles \(N\), and in the limit \(N \to \infty\) the squared matrix element becomes constant on phase space; the approach is explicitly conditional on binning events by multiplicity and does not provide a first-principles description of fluctuations in multiplicity itself.

## Method

The authors establish power counting rules for minimum bias events assuming a fixed center-of-mass energy \(Q\), a large detector pseudorapidity acceptance \(\eta_{\max} \gg 1\), and a large number of detected particles \(N \gg 1\) with transverse momenta \(p_\perp \gg m_\pi\). They integrate out unobservable beam-remnant particles, leaving a cross section expressed solely in terms of measured particle momenta, and expand the squared matrix element in symmetric polynomials of the phase space coordinates ordered in powers of \(1/Q\) and \(1/N\), using momentum conservation constraints to identify independent terms.

## Key numbers

* The volume of the smeared \(N\)-body phase space (with \(f\) and matrix element set to unity) is \((2\pi)^{4-3N} Q^{2N-2} \frac{2\pi^{N-1}}{(N-1)^2 (N-1)! (N-2)!}\).
* The leading term in the squared matrix element expansion is \(1 + \frac{c_1^{(2)}}{Q^2} \sum_{i=1}^N p_{\perp i}^2 + \mathcal{O}(Q^{-4})\), where the sum scales as \(1/N\) under the power counting \(p_\perp \sim Q/N\).
* The standard deviation of the net transverse momentum of detected particles scales like \(1/\sqrt{N}\) at fixed collision energy \(Q\).
* The transverse momentum distribution in the large-\(N\) limit implies a fractional dispersion relation \(\omega \propto k_\perp^{2/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the bootstrap approach to QCD (Paulos 2016, Cordova 2018, Karateev 2019) to high-multiplicity minimum bias events, contrasting with model-dependent descriptions such as Pythia (Sjostrand 2006) or Herwig (Bahr 2008) for proton collisions and hydrodynamics (Kolb 2003, Romatschke 2009) for heavy ion collisions that rely on unmeasurable quantities like impact parameter or temperature. The framework enables a unified treatment of collective behavior across system sizes, from elliptic flow in PbPb collisions to the ridge in pp collisions, and can be matched to perturbative QCD or hydrodynamics in appropriate limits.
