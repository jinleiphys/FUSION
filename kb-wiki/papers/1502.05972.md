# Linear Boltzmann-like equation, describing non-classical particle transport, and related asymptotic solutions for small mean free paths

> In classical kinetic or kinetic-like models a particle free path distribution is exponensial, but this is more likely to be an exception than a rule. In this paper we derive a linear Boltzmann-like equation for a general free path distribution in the framework of Alt's model J. Math. Biol. 9:147 (1980). In the special case that the free path distribution has at least first and second finite moments we construct an asymptotic solution of the equation for small mean free paths. The asymptotic solution becomes a diffusion approximation to the one-speed Boltzmann-like equation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a linear Boltzmann-like equation for particle transport with an arbitrary free path distribution (not necessarily exponential) and constructs an asymptotic diffusion approximation valid when the free path distribution has at least first and second finite moments and the mean free path is small, but only under the condition that scattering dominates over absorption (absorption is of order epsilon squared relative to scattering).

## Method

The author starts from Alt's model (1980) for non-classical particle transport, where the extinction and scattering coefficients depend on the free path traveled. By integrating the phase space density over the free path variable and using Laplace transforms, a linear Boltzmann-like equation with memory kernels is derived. An asymptotic expansion for small mean free paths is then constructed by scaling the extinction coefficient as 1/epsilon, representing the memory kernel as a distributional expansion in derivatives of delta functions, and separating the solution into outer and inner expansions to handle the initial layer.

## Key numbers

- The memory kernel expansion coefficients are given by phi_0 = 1 / <overline{l}> and phi_1 = <overline{l}^2> / (2 <overline{l}>^2) - 1, where <overline{l}> and <overline{l}^2> are the first and second moments of the free path distribution.
- The scattering albedo is taken as omega = 1 - epsilon^2 alpha(r), meaning absorption is of order epsilon^2 relative to scattering.
- The source term is scaled as F = epsilon * bar{F}, making it of order epsilon.
- The diffusion coefficient for the one-speed case is D^{outer}(r) = (v * <overline{l}> / 3) * [ <overline{l}^2> / (2 <overline{l}>^2) + K_1(r) / (1 - K_1(r)) ], where K_1 is the first Legendre moment of the scattering kernel.
- The absorption coefficient in the diffusion equation is kappa^{outer} = alpha * v / <overline{l}>.

## Context

This work extends Alt's model (1980) for non-classical particle transport, which was originally embedded in a biological context, to include absorption and sources explicitly. The asymptotic solution generalizes the classical diffusion approximation method of Larsen and Keller (1974) for the ordinary linear Boltzmann equation to the case of arbitrary free path distributions. In the steady-state one-speed case, the resulting diffusion approximation coincides with that obtained by Frank and Goudon (2010) and differs from the diffusion approximation in Larsen and Vasques (2011) by small second-order terms.
