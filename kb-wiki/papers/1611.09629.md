# Investigating convergence of the reaction $γp\toπ^\pmΔ$ and tensor meson $a_2$ exchange at high energy

> A Regge approach to the reaction processes $γp\toπ^-Δ^{++}$ and $γp\toπ^+Δ^0$ is presented for the description of existing data up to $E_γ= 16$ GeV. The model consists of the $t$-channel $π(139)+ρ(775)+a_2(1320)$ exchanges which are reggeized from the relevant Born amplitude. Discussion is given on the minimal gauge prescription for the $π$ exchange to render convergent the divergence of the $u$-channel $Δ$-pole in the former process. A new Lagrangian is constructed for the $a_2NΔ$ coupling in this work and applied to the process for the first time with the coupling constant deduced from the duality plus vector dominance. It is shown that, while the $π$ exchange dominates over the process, the role of the $a_2$ exchange is crucial rather than the $ρ$ in reproducing the cross sections for total, differential, and photon polarization asymmetry to agree with data at high energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Regge model incorporating \(t\)-channel \(\pi(139)+\rho(775)+a_2(1320)\) exchanges, with a minimal gauge prescription to control the divergence of the \(u\)-channel \(\Delta\)-pole, can describe existing data for \(\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}\) and \(\gamma p\to\pi^+\Delta^0\) up to \(E_\gamma=16\) GeV. The key hedge is that the minimal gauge prescription is necessary for convergence, and the role of the tensor meson \(a_2\) exchange is crucial rather than the \(\rho\) in reproducing the cross sections and photon polarization asymmetry.

## Method

The authors constructed a Regge model for the reactions \(\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}\) and \(\gamma p\to\pi^+\Delta^0\) using \(t\)-channel exchanges of \(\pi(139)\), \(\rho(775)\), and \(a_2(1320)\) mesons, reggeized from the relevant Born amplitudes. A minimal gauge prescription was applied to the \(\pi\) exchange amplitude to remove transverse components in the \(s\)-channel proton-pole and \(u\)-channel \(\Delta\)-pole terms, ensuring convergence at high energy. A new effective Lagrangian for the \(a_2N\Delta\) coupling was constructed, with the coupling constant \(f_{a_2N\Delta}/m_{a_2} = -3 f_{\rho N\Delta}/m_{\rho}\) deduced from duality and vector meson dominance, leaving no free parameters in the calculation.

## Key numbers

*   Total cross section for \(\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}\) shows a sharp peak of \(\sigma_{max}\approx 70\ \mu\)b around \(E_\gamma\approx 1.6\) GeV at the ELSA.
*   The model describes data up to photon energy \(E_\gamma = 16\) GeV.
*   The \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant is taken in the range \(1.7 \leq f_{\pi N\Delta} \leq 2.16\), with the value \(f_{\pi N\Delta}=2.0\) used in the calculation.
*   The \(\rho N\Delta\) coupling constant is \(f_{\rho N\Delta} = 8.57\), derived from \(f_{\rho N\Delta} = (6\sqrt{2}/5) f_{\rho NN}\).
*   The radiative decay constant for \(\rho\to\gamma\pi\) is \(g_{\gamma\pi\rho} = \pm 0.224\), from the decay width \(\Gamma_{\rho\to\gamma\pi} = 67.1\) keV.
*   The radiative decay constant for \(a_2\to\pi\gamma\) is \(g_{\gamma\pi a_2} = \pm 0.276\), from the decay width \(\Gamma_{a_2^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma} = (311\pm 25)\) keV.
*   The Regge trajectories used are: \(\alpha_\pi(t) = 0.7 (t - m_\pi^2)\), \(\alpha_\rho(t) = 0.8 t + 0.55\), and \(\alpha_{a_2}(t) = 0.85 (t - m_{a_2}^2) + 2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier Regge analyses of \(\gamma N\to\pi^\pm\Delta\) by Stichel (1968) and Clark and Donnachie (1975), who applied the minimal gauge prescription, and by Goldstein and Owens (1975), who used \(\pi+b_1+\rho+a_2\) Regge-pole exchanges. It also builds on the authors' previous studies of tensor meson \(a_2\) exchange in \(\gamma N\to\pi^\pm N\) (Yu, 2015) and \(K_2^*\) exchange in \(\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda(\Sigma^0)\) (Yu, 2015). The new Lagrangian for the \(a_2N\Delta\) coupling and the demonstration that \(a_2\) exchange is crucial rather than \(\rho\) exchange enable future studies of tensor meson contributions in other \(\pi\Delta\) photoproduction channels and at higher energies.
