# A Chiral Perturbation Theory Primer

> These lecture notes include the following topics. Chapter 1 deals with QCD and its global symmetries in the chiral limit, explicit symmetry breaking in terms of the quark masses, and the concept of Green functions and Ward identities reflecting the underlying chiral symmetry. In Chapter 2 the idea of a spontaneous breakdown of a global symmetry is discussed and its consequences in terms of the Goldstone theorem are demonstrated. Chapter 3 deals with mesonic chiral perturbation theory and the principles entering the construction of the chiral Lagrangian are outlined. In Chapter 4 the methods are extended to include the interaction between Goldstone bosons and baryons in the single-baryon sector.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide a pedagogical introduction to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) in both the mesonic and baryonic sectors, aiming to enable readers to perform simple calculations and read the current literature; the text explicitly notes that sections marked with an asterisk may be omitted in a first reading.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians based on the global flavor symmetries of QCD, treating the pseudoscalar octet (pi, K, eta) as Goldstone bosons from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. For the single-baryon sector, they extend the method to include baryon-to-baryon transition amplitudes in the presence of external fields, applying the Gell-Mann and Low formula of perturbation theory with an appropriate power-counting scheme.

## Key numbers

- The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy is given as Delta_{pi N} = 2.6%, using the empirical values m_N = 938.3 MeV, g_A = 1.267, F_pi = 92.4 MeV, and g_{pi N} = 13.21 (Schroder et al., 2001).
- The lowest-order prediction for the pion-nucleon form factor is G_{pi N}(t) = (m_N/F) g_A, where the axial-vector coupling constant G_A(0) equals g_A at this order.
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor at lowest order is G_P(t) = -4 m_N^2 g_A / (t - M_pi^2).

## Context

This work extends the mesonic ChPT framework (developed by Weinberg, Gasser, and Leutwyler) to the baryon sector, following the approach of Nambu (1960) and Goldberger and Treiman (1958) for the Goldberger-Treiman relation. It enables systematic calculations of low-energy hadronic processes such as pion-nucleon scattering, Compton scattering, and pion photoproduction, and provides the foundation for higher-order calculations including one-loop corrections.
