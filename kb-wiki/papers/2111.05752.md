# Modeling general-relativistic plasmas with collisionless moments and dissipative two-fluid magnetohydrodynamics

> Relativistic plasmas are central to the study of black hole accretion, jet physics, neutron star mergers, and compact object magnetospheres. Despite the need to accurately capture the dynamics of these plasmas and the implications for relativistic transients, their fluid modeling is typically done using a number of (overly) simplifying assumptions, which do not hold in general. This is especially true when the mean free path in the plasma is large compared to the system size, and kinetic effects start to become important. Going beyond common approaches used in the literature, we describe a fully relativistic covariant 14-moment based two-fluid system appropriate for the study of electron-ion or electron-positron plasmas. This generalized Israel-Stewart-like system of equations of motion is obtained directly from the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation. Crucially, this new formulation can account for non-ideal effects, such as anisotropic pressures and heat fluxes. We show that a relativistic two-fluid plasma can be recast as a single fluid coupled to electromagnetic fields with (potentially large) out-of-equilibrium corrections. In particular, we keep all electron degrees of freedom, which provide self-consistent evolution equations for electron temperature and momentum. The equations outlined in this paper are able to capture the full two-fluid character of collisionless plasmas found in black hole accretion and flaring processes around compact objects, as well Braginskii-like two-fluid magnetohydrodynamics applicable to weakly collisional plasmas inside accretion disks. This new formulation will be instrumental in the construction of a large class of next-generation simulations of relativistic transient phenomena produced around black holes and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully relativistic covariant 14-moment two-fluid system from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation that can account for non-ideal effects such as anisotropic pressures and heat fluxes, but the formulation is presented as a framework that will be instrumental for future simulations rather than a completed numerical implementation, and the authors note that the validity of the approach has been extensively investigated in the context of collisionless Newtonian plasmas and relativistic pair plasmas through comparison with fully kinetic simulations by others.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for a single particle species and perform a 14-moment reduction following the approach of Denicol (2012), systematically expanding the distribution function in an irreducible basis of particle momenta relative to the fluid four-velocity. They then generalize this to a two-fluid system appropriate for electron-ion or electron-positron plasmas, showing how the two-fluid description can be recast as a single fluid coupled to electromagnetic fields with out-of-equilibrium corrections, where the effective Ohm's law becomes dynamical and includes second-order terms such as advection of the electric current.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic reconnection rate for collisionless plasmas governed by the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation is given by v_inflow/v_A approximately 0.1, where v_A is the Alfven speed.
* Most simulations using general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics reproduce a reconnection rate that is too low by up to a factor of 10 compared to the collisionless rate.
* The paper adopts geometric units with G = c = k_B = 1 throughout.

## Context

This work extends the 14-moment approach of Denicol (2012) and Denicol (2019) from single-fluid descriptions to two-fluid plasmas, going beyond earlier relativistic two-fluid models such as those by Koide (2009) which treated each plasma component as a perfect fluid without heat fluxes or anisotropic pressure. The formulation is motivated by the need to model collisionless reconnection in black hole accretion and flaring processes, where scalar resistivity models (Palenzuela 2008, Ripperda 2019) fail to capture the correct reconnection rate, and builds on Newtonian ten-moment closures used in space-physics (2015 Physics of Plasmas) that have been successful in describing dissipation in planetary magnetospheres. The framework enables next-generation simulations of relativistic transient phenomena around black holes and neutron stars, including reconnection-powered flares, fast radio bursts, and electromagnetic precursors to neutron star mergers.
