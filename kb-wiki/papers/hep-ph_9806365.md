# Entanglement of Fock-space expansion and covariance in light-front Hamiltonian dynamics

> We investigate in a model with scalar ``nucleons'' and mesons the contributions of higher Fock states that are neglected in the ladder approximation of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. This leads to a breaking of covariance, both in light-front and in instant-form Hamiltonian dynamics. The lowest Fock sector neglected has two mesons in the intermediate state and corresponds to the stretched box. First we show in a simplified example that the contributions of higher Fock states are much smaller on the light-front than in instant-form dynamics. Then we show for a scattering amplitude above threshold that the stretched boxes are small, however, necessary to retain covariance. For an off energy-shell amplitude covariance is not necessarily maintained and this is confirmed by our calculations. Again, the stretched boxes are found to be small.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in light-front Hamiltonian dynamics, the contributions of higher Fock states (specifically the two-nucleon two-meson intermediate state, or "stretched box") are significantly smaller than in instant-form dynamics, but these contributions are nevertheless necessary to restore covariance in the scattering amplitude above threshold; for off-energy-shell amplitudes, covariance is not necessarily maintained even with these contributions included.

## Method

The authors study a scalar Yukawa model with "nucleons" (mass m) and "mesons" (mass μ) using light-front time-ordered perturbation theory. They compute the covariant box diagram and its decomposition into six light-front time-ordered diagrams, identifying the "stretched box" diagrams (containing four-particle intermediate states) that are neglected in the ladder approximation of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The ratio R_{4+} of the stretched box contribution to the full covariant amplitude is calculated numerically for on-shell scattering above threshold, with singularities handled by analytic subtraction and numerical integration using Mathematica and Fortran.

## Key numbers

- For the simplified case avoiding on-shell singularities (with slightly off-mass-shell external particles), the ratio R^{LF}_{4+} goes to zero as incoming momentum v → 0, while R^{IF}_{4+} has a finite contribution of 4.5% in the same limit.
- The deviation of the external mass m' from the physical mass m is less than 2.3% for v < 200 (MeV/c) and less than 9% for v < 400 (MeV/c).
- For physical nucleon mass m = 940 MeV/c² and meson mass μ = 140 MeV/c², the stretched box ratio R_4 to the absolute value of the amplitude is shown as a function of incoming momentum v up to approximately 400 MeV/c, with the real part of the amplitude having a zero near v = 280 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Brodsky et al. (1985) who noted that higher Fock components contribute to differences between the Bethe-Salpeter equation and light-front evolution, but gave no numerical estimates. It provides numerical confirmation of the smallness of higher Fock state contributions found by Mangin-Brinet and Carbonell (1997) and by Frederico et al. (1998) for binding energies, now applied to scattering amplitudes. The results enable quantitative assessment of covariance breaking in truncated Fock-space calculations and support the use of light-front dynamics for nuclear physics applications where rapid convergence of the Fock expansion is desired.
