# Two-particle multichannel systems in a finite volume with arbitrary spin

> The quantization condition for two-particle systems with arbitrary number of two-body open coupled channels, spin, momentum, and masses in a finite volume with either periodic or twisted boundary conditions is presented. Although emphasis is placed in cubic volumes, the result holds for asymmetric volumes. The result is relativistic, holds for all momenta below the three- and four-particle thresholds, and is exact up to exponential volume corrections that are governed by L/r, where L is the spatial extent of the volume and r is the range of the interactions between the particles. For hadronic systems the range of the interaction is set by the inverse of the pion mass, m_pi, and as a result the formalism presented is suitable for m_pi L >> 1. The condition presented is in agreement with all previous studies of two-body systems in a finite volume. Implications of the formalism for the studies of multichannel baryon-baryon systems are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general, model-independent relativistic quantization condition for two-particle systems in a finite volume that works for arbitrary spin, any number of open coupled channels, any masses, and any momenta below three- and four-particle thresholds, but the formalism is exact only up to exponential volume corrections governed by \(L/r\) and is suitable for \(m_\pi L \gg 1\).

## Method

The author derives the finite-volume quantization condition by writing the finite-volume two-particle propagator as a matrix in the \(|lS,Jm_J\rangle\) basis, then computing the difference \(\delta\mathcal{G}^V\) between the finite-volume and infinite-volume s-channel loops. The kernel (sum of two-particle irreducible s-channel diagrams) is taken to be diagonal in total angular momentum \(J\), and the poles of the finite-volume scattering amplitude \(\mathcal{M}^V\) yield the quantization condition \(\det[\mathcal{M}^{-1} + \delta\mathcal{G}^V] = 0\), where the determinant runs over open channels and the \(|lS,Jm_J\rangle\) basis. The result is expressed in terms of the generalized zeta functions \(\mathcal{Z}^{\mathbf{d}}_{lm}\) that encode the boundary conditions and boost.

## Key numbers

* The quantization condition is exact up to exponential volume corrections governed by \(L/r\), where \(L\) is the spatial extent and \(r\) is the range of interactions; for hadronic systems \(r \sim 1/m_\pi\), so the formalism is suitable for \(m_\pi L \gg 1\).
* For sufficiently large volumes satisfying \(m_\pi L \gsim 4\), finite volume effects have minimal impact on the low-lying single-particle spectrum of QCD.
* The c.m. relative momentum for the \(j\)th channel is given by \(k^{*2}_j = \left(E^{*2}/4 - (m_{j,1}^2+m_{j,2}^2)/2 + (m_{j,1}^2-m_{j,2}^2)^2/(4E^{*2})\right)\).
* The function \(c^{\mathbf{d}}_{lm}(k^{*2}_j; L) = \frac{\sqrt{4\pi}}{\gamma L^3}\left(\frac{2\pi}{L}\right)^{l-2} \mathcal{Z}^{\mathbf{d}}_{lm}[1;(k^*_j L/2\pi)^2]\).
* The symmetry factor \(n_j\) equals \(1/2\) if the two particles are identical and 1 otherwise.

## Context

This work generalizes the Luscher method (Luscher 1986, 1990) and its extensions by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995), Beane et al. (2003), and others, removing all previous restrictions on spin, number of channels, and masses. It unifies prior results for spinless systems (Hansen and Sharpe 2012, Briceno and Davoudi 2012), spin-1/2 systems (Bernard et al. 2008, Gockeler et al. 2012), and spin-1 systems (Ishizuka 2009, Briceno et al. 2013) into a single covariant framework. The formalism enables lattice QCD calculations of multichannel baryon-baryon systems such as hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon scattering, which are expected to have a larger immediate impact than nucleon-nucleon calculations due to the poor signal-to-noise problem in the latter.
