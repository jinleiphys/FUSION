# Hard breakup of the deuteron into two Delta-isobars

> We study high energy photodisintegration of the deuteron into two $Δ$-isobars at large center of mass angles within the QCD hard rescattering model (HRM). According to the HRM, the process develops in three main steps: the photon knocks the quark from one of the nucleons in the deuteron; the struck quark rescatters off a quark from the other nucleon sharing the high energy of the photon; then the energetic quarks recombine into two outgoing baryons which have large transverse momenta. Within the HRM, the cross section is expressed through the amplitude of $pn\rightarrow ΔΔ$ scattering which we evaluated based on the quark-interchange model of hard hadronic scattering. Calculations show that the angular distribution and the strength of the photodisintegration is mainly determined by the properties of the $pn\rightarrow ΔΔ$ scattering. We predict that the cross section of the deuteron breakup to $ Δ^{++}Δ^{-}$ is 4-5 times larger than that of the breakup to the $ Δ^{+}Δ^{0}$ channel. Also, the angular distributions for these two channels are markedly different. These can be compared with the predictions based on the assumption that two hard $Δ$-isobars are the result of the disintegration of the preexisting $ΔΔ$ components of the deuteron wave function. In this case, one expects the angular distributions and cross sections of the breakup in both $ Δ^{++}Δ^{-}$ and $ Δ^{+}Δ^{0}$ channels to be similar.

## Key claim

The cross section of deuteron photodisintegration into two Delta-isobars at large center-of-mass angles is predicted to be 4-5 times larger for the Delta++ Delta- channel than for the Delta+ Delta0 channel, with markedly different angular distributions, but this result depends on the hard rescattering model (HRM) and quark-interchange framework; if instead the Deltas arise from preexisting Delta-Delta components of the deuteron wave function, the angular distributions and cross sections for both channels would be similar.

## Method

The authors extended the QCD hard rescattering model (HRM) to compute the cross section of gamma + d -> Delta + Delta at large center-of-mass angles. The HRM factorizes the reaction into three steps: photon knockout of a quark from one nucleon, hard rescattering of that quark off a quark from the other nucleon, and recombination into two outgoing baryons. The cross section was expressed through the amplitude of pn -> Delta Delta scattering, which was evaluated using the quark-interchange model with SU(6) and diquark symmetry assumptions for the valence quark wave functions of baryons.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the gamma d -> Delta++ Delta- differential cross section to the gamma d -> pn cross section at theta_cm = 90 degrees is 2.01 (SU(6) model) or 0.47 (diquark model).
- The ratio of the gamma d -> Delta+ Delta0 differential cross section to the gamma d -> pn cross section at theta_cm = 90 degrees is 0.47 (SU(6) model) or 0.11 (diquark model).
- The cross section of deuteron breakup to Delta++ Delta- is predicted to be 4-5 times larger than that of breakup to Delta+ Delta0.
- The effective center-of-mass angle for the pn -> Delta Delta scattering at theta_cm = 90 degrees photodisintegration is approximately given by cos(theta^N_cm) approx (1 + cos(theta_cm))/2 in the high-energy limit s >> 4 m_Delta^2.

## Context

This work extends the hard rescattering model previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration into pn pairs (Frankfurt 1999) and to He-3 breakup (Sargsian 2008) to the production of two Delta-isobars. It provides a testable alternative to models that assume preexisting Delta-Delta components in the deuteron wave function, such as those studied by Harvey (1980) and Brodsky (1983, 1985) in the context of hidden color components. The predictions can be compared with ongoing experimental efforts at Jefferson Lab (Gao, DM) to probe non-nucleonic components of the deuteron.
