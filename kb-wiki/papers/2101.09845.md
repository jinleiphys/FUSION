# Electromagnetic fields from the extended Kharzeev-McLerran-Warringa model in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the Kharzeev-McLerran-Warringa (KMW) model that estimates strong electromagnetic (EM) fields generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we generalize the formulas of EM fields in the vacuum by incorporating the longitudinal position dependence, the generalized charge distributions and retardation correction. We further generalize the formulas of EM fields in the pure quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium by incorporating a constant Ohm electric conductivity and also during the realistic early-time stages QGP evolution by using a time-dependent electric conductivity. Using the extended KMW model, we observe a slower time evolution and a more reasonable impact parameter $b$ dependence of the magnetic field strength than those from the original KMW model in the vacuum. The inclusion of medium effects by using the lattice data helps to further prolong the time evolution of magnetic field, such that the magnetic field strength during the realistic QGP evolution at thermal freeze-out time can meet the $1σ$ bound constrained from experimentally measured difference in global polarizations of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy. These generalized formulations in the extended KMW model will be potentially useful for many EM fields relevant studies in heavy-ion collisions, especially at lower colliding energies and for various species of colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The extended Kharzeev-McLerran-Warringa (KMW) model, which incorporates longitudinal position dependence, generalized charge distributions, retardation correction, and medium effects with a time-dependent electric conductivity, yields a slower time evolution and more reasonable impact parameter dependence of the magnetic field than the original vacuum KMW model; the inclusion of medium effects using lattice data allows the magnetic field strength at thermal freeze-out to meet the 1σ bound from the measured difference in global polarizations of Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors generalized the original KMW model by replacing the "pancake-shaped disk" approximation with a Lorentz-contracted three-parameter Fermi (3pF) charge distribution for both spherically symmetric and axially deformed nuclei, and by incorporating the longitudinal position dependence of source points and retardation correction via Liénard-Wiechert potentials. They further extended the formulas to include medium feedback effects by embedding a constant Ohm electric conductivity for a pure quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium and a time-dependent electric conductivity for realistic early-time QGP evolution, using lattice QCD data for the conductivity. The magnetic field strength was then numerically evaluated for time evolution and impact parameter dependence, and compared to the original KMW model and experimental constraints.

## Key numbers

- At top RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV), the Lorentz contraction factor \(\gamma \simeq 106.5\) for gold nuclei, corresponding to beam rapidity \(Y_0 \simeq 5.36\).
- The magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be of order \(eB \sim m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{18}\) G at top RHIC energy and \(eB \sim 10\,m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{19}\) G at LHC energies.
- The parameter \(\alpha_y\) for the baryon-junction stopping rapidity distribution is taken as \(\alpha_y \simeq 0.5\), based on ALICE data; varying \(\alpha_y\) between 0.48 and 0.50 does not largely affect the time evolution of the magnetic field strength.
- The magnetic field strength during realistic QGP evolution at thermal freeze-out time can meet the 1σ bound from the measured difference in global polarizations of Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons in Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy.

## Context

This work extends the original KMW model (Kharzeev, McLerran, Warringa, 2008), which approximated colliding nuclei as "pancake-shaped disks" with uniform two-dimensional surface charge density and no medium effects. The extended model incorporates more realistic three-dimensional charge distributions (e.g., 3pF model from De Vries et al., 1987) and medium feedback via Ohm conductivity, building on prior studies of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Skokov et al., 2009; Tuchin, 2010; Gursoy et al., 2014; Deng and Huang, 2017). The results enable more accurate modeling of the chiral magnetic effect and other electromagnetic-field-driven phenomena, particularly for lower collision energies (e.g., RHIC Beam Energy Scan, FAIR, NICA, J-PARC) and for various colliding nuclei species, including deformed nuclei like \(^{96}\)Zr and \(^{96}\)Ru used in isobar runs.
