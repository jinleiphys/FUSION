# Finite Element Method for Solving the Collective Nuclear Model with Tetrahedral Symmetry

> We apply a new calculation scheme of a finite element method (FEM) for solving an elliptic boundary-value problem describing a quadrupole vibration collective nuclear model with tetrahedral symmetry. We use of shape functions constructed with interpolation Lagrange polynomials on a triangle finite element grid and compare the FEM results with obtained early by a finite difference method.

# Finite Element Method for Solving the Collective Nuclear Model with Tetrahedral Symmetry

## Key claim

The finite element method (FEM) with triangular Lagrange elements and Gaussian quadratures yields eigenvalues for the quadrupole vibration collective nuclear model of $^{156}$Dy that converge toward the finite difference method (FDM) results as the polynomial order increases, with discrepancies decreasing to about 1% for the 30 lowest states. However, the FEM approach is presented as the more promising path because the earlier FDM approach could not be generalized to the full six-dimensional collective space where the potential and metric tensor are given by $2\times 10^{6}$ table values.

## Method

The authors solve a self-adjoint elliptic boundary-value problem in a 2D domain describing quadrupole-octahedral collective vibrations of $^{156}$Dy with tetrahedral symmetry, using the finite element method with triangular Lagrange elements of orders p=1,2,3,4. The metric tensor components $g_{ij}(x)$ and weight function $g_0(x)$ are constructed from the collective inertia tensor $B_{ij}$ via transformation formulas, and the potential energy $V(x_1,x_2)$ is taken from earlier macroscopic-microscopic calculations. The FEM results are compared against eigenvalues previously obtained by a second-order finite difference method (FDM) by Mazurek et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- The minimum of the potential energy for $^{156}$Dy is $V_{\text{min}}(\alpha_{20},\alpha_{32}) = 0.685$ MeV.
- The ground state eigenvalue from FDM is $E_1^{\text{FDM}} = 0.85988$ MeV; the FEM values for orders p=1 through 4 are 0.96000, 0.91329, 0.90234, and 0.89065 MeV respectively.
- The first excited state (v=2) eigenvalue from FDM is $E_2^{\text{FDM}} = 0.97588$ MeV; FEM values are 1.11144, 1.04808, 1.03297, and 1.02068 MeV for p=1 through 4.
- The discrepancy between FDM and FEM eigenvalues decreases to about 1% as the FEM approximation order increases for the 30 lowest states.
- The metric tensor and potential are originally tabulated on a grid of $2\times 10^{6}$ points in the full six-dimensional collective space.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finite difference method solution by Mazurek et al. (2011) for the quadrupole vibration collective model of $^{156}$Dy in a 2D subspace, which was part of a larger boundary-value problem in six-dimensional collective space. The FDM approach could not be generalized to the full multidimensional domain where the potential and metric tensor are given by $2\times 10^{6}$ table values. The FEM scheme presented here is designed to be directly generalizable to the six-dimensional problem, enabling future work on the full quadrupole-octupole collective vibration model with tetrahedral symmetry, following the consistent approach to quadrupole-octupole collective vibrations developed by Dobrowolski et al. (2016, 2018).
