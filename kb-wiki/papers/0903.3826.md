# IBM-1 calculations towards the neutron-rich nucleus $^{106}$Zr

> The neutron-rich N=66 isotonic and A=106 isobaric chains, covering regions with varying types of collectivity, are interpreted in the framework of the interacting boson model. Level energies and electric quadrupole transition probabilities are compared with available experimental information. The calculations for the known nuclei in the two chains are extrapolated towards the neutron-rich nucleus $^{106}$Zr.

## Key claim

The paper predicts spectroscopic properties of the neutron-rich nucleus $^{106}$Zr using IBM-1 calculations, but explicitly states that these predictions depend on the assumption that $N=82$ remains a magic number in heavy zirconium isotopes; future experiments will test whether this hypothesis is correct.

## Method

The authors performed IBM-1 calculations for the $N=66$ isotonic chain ($^{112}$Pd, $^{110}$Ru, $^{108}$Mo) and the $A=106$ isobaric chain ($^{106}$Pd, $^{106}$Ru, $^{106}$Mo), fitting Hamiltonian parameters $\epsilon_d$, $\kappa$, $c_3$, and $c_4$ to experimental level energies via least-squares minimization. The quadrupole operator parameter $\chi$ was fixed from E2 transition rates in $^{106}$Pd using the consistent-Q formalism, and the boson effective charge $e_{\rm b}$ was fitted to measured $B$(E2) values in $^{106}$Pd. The two independent parameter sets were then extrapolated to predict the properties of $^{106}$Zr, which lies at the intersection of both chains.

## Key numbers

- For $^{106}$Zr, the predicted $2^+_1$ level energy is approximately 140 keV (from the $N=66$ chain) and 134 keV (from the $A=106$ chain)
- For $^{106}$Zr, the predicted $2^+_2$ ($\gamma$-band head) energy is 618 keV (from the $N=66$ chain) and 630 keV (from the $A=106$ chain)
- The predicted $B$(E2; $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1$) value for $^{106}$Zr is approximately 0.50–0.55 e$^2$b$^2$, consistent between both chains
- The predicted $\gamma$-band staggering amplitude $S(J)$ for $^{106}$Zr is approximately 10 keV
- The rms deviation between experimental and calculated level energies is 58 keV for the $N=66$ chain and 143 keV for the $A=106$ chain
- The boson effective charge used for all nuclei is $e_{\rm b} = 0.11$ eb, fitted to $^{106}$Pd

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of collectivity in neutron-rich $40 \leq Z \leq 50$ nuclei previously investigated by Urban (2004) and Lalkovski (2005), who documented the vibrational-to-rotational evolution in molybdenum and zirconium isotopes. The paper addresses the open question of whether $N=82$ shell quenching occurs in zirconium isotopes, as suggested by HFB calculations with the SkP interaction (Dobaczewski, 1994; Sharma, 2002), by providing IBM-1 predictions under the assumption that $N=82$ remains magic. The results enable future experimental studies of $^{106}$Zr to determine whether the mid-shell collectivity enhancement follows standard expectations or is modified by shell-structure changes.
