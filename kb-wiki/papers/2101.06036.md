# Statistical and dynamical bimodality in multifragmentation reactions

> The bimodal behavior of the order parameter is studied in the framework of Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model. In order to do that, simplified yet accurate method of BUU model is used which allow calculation of fluctuations in systems much larger than what was considered feasible in a well-known and already existing model. It is observed that depending on the projectile energy and centrality of the reaction, both entrance channel and exit channel effects can be at the origin of the experimentally observed bimodal behavior. Both dynamical and statistical bimodality mechanisms are associated in the theoretical model to different time scales of the reaction, and to different energy regimes.

# Statistical and dynamical bimodality in multifragmentation reactions

## Key claim
Depending on the incident energy and impact parameter of the reaction, both dynamical bimodality (from entrance channel fluctuations in collision rate) and statistical bimodality (from thermal phase transition in the exit channel) can be at the origin of the experimentally observed bimodal behavior of the largest cluster distribution, meaning both scenarios proposed in the literature are potentially observable in heavy ion data.

## Method
The authors used a modified Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that reduces computational cost by a factor of 1/N_test^2 compared to the existing Bauer method, allowing simulations of larger nuclear systems. The BUU dynamics were run until freeze-out (t=175 fm/c for 100 MeV/nucleon, t=400 fm/c for 40 MeV/nucleon), then coupled to the Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM) for secondary decay treatment of the primary fragments.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out time for 100 MeV/nucleon reactions: approximately t=175 fm/c for all impact parameters (ranging from t_f=150 fm/c for most peripheral to t_f=200 fm/c for central collisions)
- Freeze-out time for 40 MeV/nucleon reactions: approximately t=400 fm/c
- Mass cut of A_cut=37 used to distinguish stopped events (A_max >= 37) from crossed events (A_max < 37) at b=0 fm
- N_test=100 test particles used per nucleon in the transport calculation
- Cluster identification distance threshold: 2 fm between test particles
- Symmetric system studied: ^40Ca + ^40Ca at beam energies 25, 40, 50, and 100 MeV/nucleon

## Context
This work extends previous dynamical approaches by Lefevre, Zbiri, and Mallik (2016) that studied bimodality using semiclassical one-body transport equations but lacked correlations for proper fragment formation in the exit channel. It also connects to statistical model studies by Gulminelli, Chomaz, and Das Gupta that predicted thermal bimodality as a signature of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. The modified BUU method enables treatment of larger systems than was feasible with the existing model by Bauer, and the results suggest that both dynamical bifurcation mechanisms and thermal phase transition signals can be observed in intermediate-energy heavy-ion reactions, depending on the specific entrance channel conditions.
