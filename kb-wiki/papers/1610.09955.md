# Multiparton interactions: From pp to pA

> In the process of understanding nuclear collisions, reliable extrapolations from pp collisions, based on Glauber models, are highly desirable, though seldomly accurate. We review the inclusion of diffractive excitations and argue that they provide an important contribution to centrality observables in pA collisions. We present a method for distinguishing between diffractively and non-diffractively wounded nucleons, and a proof-of-principle for an extrapolation of multiparton interaction models built on this.

## Key claim
Reliable extrapolations from pp to pA collisions based on standard Glauber models are seldom accurate; the paper argues that including diffractive excitations provides an important contribution to centrality observables in pA collisions, and presents a proof-of-principle extrapolation method that distinguishes diffractively from non-diffractively wounded nucleons.

## Method
The authors use the DIPSY dipole-evolution model to calculate fluctuations in the proton transverse structure, comparing them to the Glauber-Gribov (GG) colour fluctuation parametrization. They then construct a simplified four-parameter model (two radii, a fluctuation probability, and an opacity) that allows separate treatment of absorptively and diffractively wounded nucleons, and couple this to a particle production model (FritiofP8) that treats additional absorptive sub-collisions as if they were diffractive excitations.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber-Gribov colour fluctuation approach of Alvioli (2013) by incorporating separate projectile and target fluctuations, enabling distinction between diffractive and absorptive wounded nucleons. It builds on the DIPSY dipole model of Flensburg (2011) and the wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976). The method enables more realistic extrapolations of multiparton interaction models from pp to pA collisions, with the FritiofP8 model showing better agreement with ATLAS data on forward sum E_perp and eta multiplicity distributions than a purely absorptive treatment.
