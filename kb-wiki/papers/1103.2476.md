# Microscopic Origin of the Shear Relaxation Time in Causal Dissipative Fluid Dynamics

> In this paper we show how to compute the shear relaxation time from an underlying microscopic theory. We prove that the shear relaxation time in Israel-Stewart-type theories is given by the inverse of the pole of the corresponding retarded Green's function, which is nearest to the origin in the complex energy plane. Consequently, the relaxation time in such theories is a microscopic, and not a macroscopic, i.e., fluid-dynamical time scale.

## Key claim

The shear relaxation time in Israel-Stewart-type causal dissipative fluid dynamics is determined by the inverse of the pole of the retarded Green's function that is nearest to the origin in the complex energy plane, making it a microscopic time scale (set by inter-particle scattering rates) rather than a macroscopic fluid-dynamical time scale. This result holds under the condition that a clear separation of scales exists (Knudsen number much less than 1) and that all other poles can be pushed to infinity.

## Method

The authors start from the linear relation between a dissipative current and its thermodynamic force in Fourier space, expressed via the retarded Green's function. They analyze the general analytical structure of this Green's function, assuming it has N poles in the complex frequency plane, and derive a differential equation of motion for the dissipative current. By applying a limiting procedure that pushes all poles except the one nearest to the origin to infinity, they obtain a relaxation-type equation and identify the relaxation time as the inverse of that first pole. They then apply this formalism to the linearized Boltzmann equation for a weakly coupled classical gas of hard spheres, computing the shear viscosity and relaxation time coefficients by expanding the solution in energy moments and truncating to a single term (corresponding to the Israel-Stewart 14-moment approximation).

## Key numbers

- For a weakly coupled classical gas of hard spheres in the 14-moment approximation, the shear viscosity is given by \(\eta = \beta_0 \frac{I_{42} I_{32}}{\mathcal{A}^{00}}\) and the shear relaxation time by \(\tau_\pi = \frac{I_{42}}{\mathcal{A}^{00}}\), where \(I_{nq}\) are thermodynamic integrals and \(\mathcal{A}^{00}\) is a matrix element of the collision operator.
- In the massless limit for hard spheres, \(\mathcal{A}^{00} = (3/5) I_{42} n_0 \sigma\), where \(\sigma\) is the total cross-section and \(n_0\) is the equilibrium number density.
- The ratio \(\eta / \tau_\pi = \beta_0 I_{32}\) is derived from the formalism.

## Context

This work extends the derivation of relativistic fluid dynamics from microscopic theory by providing a general method to compute the shear relaxation time from the analytical structure of retarded Green's functions, going beyond the gradient expansion approach of Chapman (1916) and Burnett (1935). The results confirm and generalize the earlier kinetic-theory calculations of Denicol, Koide, and Rischke (2010) for the relaxation time in Israel-Stewart theories (Israel and Stewart, 1979). The method enables the computation of transient dynamics in causal dissipative fluid dynamics without relying on the unstable gradient expansion, and can be applied to any microscopic theory where the retarded Green's function is known.
