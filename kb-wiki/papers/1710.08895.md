# Correlation of strange particles production with multiplicity in a multi-pomeron exchange model

> We propose a further development of a multi-pomeron exchange model of pp and p$\bar p$ collisions at high energy. The model describes consistently multiplicity, transverse momentum of charged particles and their correlation in wide energy range (from ISR to LHC) by introducing string collectivity effects in pp collisions. The particles species differentiation is implemented according to Schwinger mechanism. Due to the influence of higher resonances on the yields of observed particles, we implement the production of a large set of hadron resonances with cascade decays. We present the results on pion, kaon and proton yields as a function of the collision energy and the correlation of strange and multi-strange hadrons with multiplicity and compare the results with experimental data.

## Key claim

The multi-pomeron exchange model, extended to include a wide range of hadron resonances (mass range 135 MeV to 2.5 GeV) with cascade decays, qualitatively describes the strong growth of strange and multi-strange baryon yields with charged multiplicity in pp collisions at 7 TeV, but the agreement for mesons is poorer and requires further study; the results were obtained without any additional adjustment of the model parameters beyond those fixed for charged-particle correlations.

## Method

The authors extended a multi-pomeron exchange model for pp and p-pbar collisions by implementing particle species differentiation via the Schwinger mechanism and incorporating 380 hadron types (including resonances up to 2.5 GeV) with a spin-weight factor g_nu = 2S_nu + 1. The final hadron spectrum was corrected for cascade decays using an effective branching matrix M_mu_nu calculated with the THERMINATOR 2 Monte Carlo generator, and yields were normalized to conserve total charged multiplicity N_ch. The string collectivity parameter beta was fixed by fitting the <p_t>-N_ch correlation data from ISR to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Regge parameters used: Delta = 0.139, alpha' = 0.21 GeV^-2, gamma = 1.77 GeV^-2, R_0^2 = 3.18 GeV^-2, C = 1.5
- Hadron mass range considered: from 135 MeV to 2.5 GeV
- Number of hadron types in the model: 380, with nu=1 for light hadrons and nu=380 for pi^0 mesons
- The model was applied to pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV for strange particle multiplicity dependence

## Context

This work extends the multi-pomeron exchange model of Feofilov, Kovalenko, and Puchkov (earlier works [1-3]) by adding a full resonance decay treatment, improving agreement for pion, kaon, and proton yields compared to the simplified model [6] especially at LHC energies. The results address the ALICE (2017) observation of strangeness enhancement with multiplicity in pp collisions, which was poorly described by existing models but qualitatively captured by the string fusion model of Armesto et al. (1996) and the PYTHIA tune of Sjostrand and Skands (2004). The model enables further study of string collectivity effects and strangeness production without requiring additional parameter tuning.
