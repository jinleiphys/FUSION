# Dark decay of the neutron

> New decay channels for the neutron into dark matter plus other particles have been suggested for explaining a long-standing discrepancy between the neutron lifetime measured from trapped neutrons versus those decaying in flight. Many such scenarios are already ruled out by their effects on neutron stars, and the decay into dark matter plus photon has been experimentally excluded. Here we explore the decay into a dark Dirac fermion $χ$ and a dark photon $A'$, which can be consistent with all constraints if $χ$ is a subdominant component of the dark matter. Neutron star constraints are evaded if the dark photon mass to coupling ratio is $m_{A'}/g' \lesssim (45-60)\,$MeV, depending upon the nuclear equation of state. $g'$ and the kinetic mixing between U(1)$'$ and electromagnetism are tightly constrained by direct and indirect dark matter detection, supernova constraints, and cosmological limits.

## Key claim

The neutron lifetime discrepancy (a ~8 s, 4σ tension between bottle and in-beam measurements) could be explained by a new decay channel n → χ A', where χ is a dark Dirac fermion and A' is a dark photon, provided that χ constitutes only a subdominant component of the total dark matter and that the dark photon mass-to-coupling ratio satisfies m_A'/g' ≲ (45-60) MeV to evade neutron star constraints.

## Method

The authors constructed a low-energy effective Lagrangian with n-χ mixing and a dark photon interaction, then diagonalized the mass matrix to obtain the decay rate for n → χ A'. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure, treating the dark matter as a degenerate Fermi gas with repulsive self-interactions from dark photon exchange, and added dark matter pressure and energy density to nuclear equations of state from quantum Monte Carlo calculations. A renormalizable UV model with colored scalar triplets and a dark Higgs was built to generate the effective interactions, and cosmological constraints from relic density, Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, cosmic microwave background distortions, direct detection (CRESST-III), and indirect detection (Fermi-LAT dwarf spheroidal galaxies) were computed including Sommerfeld enhancement.

## Key numbers

- Neutron lifetime discrepancy: ~8 s difference with 4σ tension between bottle and in-beam measurements
- Reference dark matter mass: m_χ = 937.9 MeV, giving m_n - m_χ ≅ 1.67 MeV
- Required partial width to resolve discrepancy: 7 × 10^(-30) GeV
- Neutron star bound: m_A'/g' ≲ (45-60) MeV, depending on nuclear equation of state
- Mixing angle limits: θ < 1.3 × 10^(-11) (scenario A, m_A' = 1.35 MeV) and θ < 6.3 × 10^(-12) (scenario B, m_A' = 0.5 MeV)
- Coupling limits from neutron star bound combined with decay rate: g' ≳ 27 × 10^(-3) (scenario A) and g' ≳ 4.9 × 10^(-3) (scenario B)
- Direct detection limit (CRESST-III): g' ε < 9.85 × 10^(-11) (m_A'/MeV)^2 (Ω_DM/Ω_χ)^(1/2)
- CMB constraint: (Ω_χ/Ω_DM)^2 ⟨σ_ann v_rel⟩ ≲ 1.5 × 10^(-27) cm^3/s (scenario A) and ≲ 1.9 × 10^(-27) cm^3/s (scenario B)
- Fermi-LAT dwarf limit (scenario B): (Ω_χ/Ω_DM)^2 ⟨σ_ann v_rel⟩ < 2.8 × 10^(-28) cm^3/s at 95% CL
- Dark photon lifetime for A' → e+e-: τ ≅ 24 (10^(-10)/ε)^2 s
- Dark photon lifetime for A' → 3γ: τ ≅ 2 × 10^12 s (10^(-10)/ε)^2 (1 MeV/m_A')^9

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Fornal and Grinstein (2018) that neutron decay into dark particles could explain the lifetime discrepancy, following the experimental exclusion of n → χγ by Tang et al. (2018). It addresses the neutron star constraints identified by McKeen et al. (2018), Baym et al. (2018), and Motta et al. (2018), which showed that non-interacting dark matter from neutron decay would prevent neutron stars from reaching 2 solar masses. The paper quantifies the required dark photon mass-to-coupling ratio to evade these constraints and maps the full parameter space against direct detection (CRESST-III), indirect detection (Fermi-LAT), supernova 1987A neutrino observations, beam dump experiments (E137), BaBar searches, and cosmological limits from BBN and CMB. This enables future experimental searches to target specific regions of the m_A'/g' and ε parameter space.
