# Octet baryon masses and sigma terms in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We report an analysis of the octet baryon masses using the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with and without the virtual decuplet contributions. Particular attention is paid to the finite-volume corrections and the finite lattice spacing effects on the baryon masses. A reasonable description of all the publicly available $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD data is achieved.Utilyzing the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, we determine the nucleon sigma terms as $σ_{πN}=55(1)(4)$ MeV and $σ_{sN}=27(27)(4)$ MeV.

## Key claim

The covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) can describe all publicly available nf=2+1 lattice QCD data for octet baryon masses, but only when the data are limited to those satisfying Mπ² < 0.25 GeV² and MφL > 4 to ensure the theory stays in its applicability range.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit of the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) BChPT up to N3LO to lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, LHPC, QCDSF-UKQCD, HSC, and NPLQCD collaborations, including finite-volume corrections and finite lattice spacing effects up to O(a²). They determined 19 unknown low-energy constants (LECs) for the octet-only BChPT at O(p⁴), plus 4 additional combinations of LECs for the lattice spacing effects. The nucleon sigma terms were then extracted using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon pion-nucleon sigma term: σπN = 55(1)(4) MeV
- Nucleon strangeness sigma term: σsN = 27(27)(4) MeV
- Finite lattice spacing effects are at the order of 1-2% for lattice spacings up to 0.15 fm and pion mass up to 500 MeV
- The fit quality up to N3LO yields χ²/d.o.f. ≈ 1.0
- Fitted LQCD data were limited to those with Mπ² < 0.25 GeV² and MφL > 4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of octet baryon masses in EOMS BChPT (Ren 2012, Ren 2013) by including finite lattice spacing effects up to O(a²) and performing a simultaneous fit to data from five lattice collaborations. The results are consistent with recent lattice QCD and BChPT studies of sigma terms, though the authors note that further lattice simulations are needed to reduce the uncertainty of the nucleon strangeness-sigma term. The finding that virtual decuplet contributions do not significantly change the description of lattice data implies that octet baryon mass data alone cannot disentangle virtual decuplet contributions from octet and tree-level contributions.
