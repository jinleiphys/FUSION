# Strong Couplings of Charmed Mesons and Quarkonia

> We extract the strong coupling constants of three mesons, each of which is composed of either two charm quarks or one charm quark and one light (i.e., $u$, $d$, or $s$) quark, from the matrix elements for the transitions of two of these mesons induced by appropriate quark currents within the framework of a relativistic dispersion approach to the constituent-quark picture of mesons. Among others, we also analyse the impact of the violation of the SU(3) flavour symmetry by the quark masses. In the case of mesons containing one light quark, we observe, in two respects, discrepancies between our findings and the predictions of QCD sum rules: our strong couplings exceed considerably the ones emerging from QCD sum rules, and, in our approach, the replacement of a light quark by a strange quark entails, in contrast to QCD sum rules, a reduction of the magnitudes of the strong couplings.

## Key claim

The strong coupling constants of charmed mesons and charmonia extracted from a relativistic constituent-quark dispersion approach are considerably larger than those predicted by QCD sum rules, and, contrary to QCD sum-rule expectations, replacing a light quark by a strange quark reduces the magnitudes of these couplings.

## Method

The authors compute the transition form factors \(F_+^{P\to P'}(q^2)\), \(V^{P\to V}(q^2)\), and \(A_0^{P\to V}(q^2)\) within a relativistic dispersion approach based on a constituent-quark picture of mesons. These form factors are expressed as double dispersion integrals over spectral densities derived from one-loop Feynman diagrams, using Gaussian wave functions for the mesons. The strong couplings are then extracted from the residues of the form-factor poles at the relevant vector or pseudoscalar meson masses, combined with the known decay constants of those mesons.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{\eta_c\eta_c\psi} = 25.8 \pm 1.7\) (dimensionless), for the coupling of one \(J/\psi\) to two \(\eta_c\) mesons.
*   \(g_{\eta_c\psi\psi} = (10.6 \pm 1.5)\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\), for the coupling of one \(\eta_c\) to two \(J/\psi\) mesons.
*   For non-strange charmed mesons: \(g_{DD\psi} = 26.04 \pm 1.43\) (dimensionless); \(g_{DD^*\psi} = (10.7 \pm 0.4)\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\); \(g_{DD^*\eta_c} = 15.51 \pm 0.45\) (dimensionless); \(g_{D^*D^*\eta_c} = (9.76 \pm 0.32)\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
*   For strange charmed mesons: \(g_{D_sD_s\psi} = 23.83 \pm 0.78\) (dimensionless); \(g_{D_sD_s^*\psi} = (9.6 \pm 0.8)\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\); \(g_{D_sD_s^*\eta_c} = 14.15 \pm 0.52\) (dimensionless); \(g_{D_s^*D_s^*\eta_c} = (8.27 \pm 0.37)\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
*   Constituent-quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0.23\;\text{GeV}\), \(m_s = 0.35\;\text{GeV}\), \(m_c = 1.45\;\text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic dispersion approach to constituent-quark models (CQM) to compute strong couplings involving charmonia and charmed mesons, following earlier developments by the same authors (Lucha, Melikhov, Sazdjian, Simula). The results contradict predictions from QCD sum rules (Mat05, Bra14, Bra15), where the couplings are found to be more than a factor of two smaller and where SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking by the strange quark mass increases the couplings rather than decreasing them. The computed couplings enable improved modelling of hadronic transitions and decays involving charmed mesons and quarkonia, and provide a benchmark for future lattice QCD calculations.
