# Eccentricity fluctuation effects on elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study effects of eccentricity fluctuations on the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 at mid-rapidity in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt{s_NN}=200 GeV by using a hybrid model that combines ideal hydrodynamics for space-time evolution of the quark gluon plasma phase and a hadronic transport model for the hadronic matter. For initial conditions in hydrodynamic simulations, both the Glauber model and the color glass condensate model are employed to demonstrate the effect of initial eccentricity fluctuations originating from the nucleon position inside a colliding nucleus. The effect of eccentricity fluctuations is modest in semicentral Au+Au collisions, but significantly enhances v_2 in Cu+Cu collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that eccentricity fluctuations from event-by-event variations in nucleon positions significantly enhance the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) in Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, while the effect is only modest in semicentral Au+Au collisions at the same energy.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma phase with the JAM hadronic cascade model for the hadronic phase, solving the hydrodynamic equations in three spatial dimensions and time. Initial entropy density profiles were generated using Monte Carlo versions of both the Glauber model and the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) model, with event-by-event fluctuations included by averaging many shifted and rotated profiles to produce smooth initial conditions that preserve eccentricity fluctuation effects.

## Key numbers

- In semicentral Au+Au collisions (10-50% centrality), eccentricity fluctuations enhance the eccentricity parameter by 8-11% in the MC-Glauber model and 5-8% in the MC-KLN model.
- In the most central Au+Au collisions (0-5% centrality), the enhancement factor \(\varepsilon_{\mathrm{part}}/\varepsilon_{\mathrm{RP}}\) is 1.83 in the MC-Glauber model and 1.53 in the MC-KLN model.
- In Cu+Cu collisions at 10-50% centrality, the enhancement factor is 1.51-1.74 in the MC-Glauber model and 1.36-1.50 in the MC-KLN model.
- In the most central Cu+Cu collisions (0-5% centrality), the enhancement factor reaches 4.20 in the MC-Glauber model and 3.36 in the MC-KLN model.
- Eccentricity in the MC-KLN model is reduced by approximately 15% compared to the naive KLN model.
- Finite nucleon size in Monte Carlo approaches reduces initial eccentricity by about 10% with default Woods-Saxon parameters, requiring reparametrization: for gold, radius \(r_0 = 6.42\) fm and diffuseness \(d = 0.44\) fm; for copper, \(r_0 = 4.28\) fm and \(d = 0.50\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hirano, Heinz, Kharzeev, Lacey, and Nara (2006) that used ideal hydrodynamics with Glauber and CGC initial conditions but without fluctuation effects, and addresses the finding by Drescher and Nara (2006) that large initial eccentricity in the CGC picture was partly due to improper treatment of nuclear edge regions. The results enable future simultaneous analysis of \(v_2\) data in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions to constrain transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, and suggest that viscous effects in the QGP phase could be tested against Cu+Cu data where fluctuation effects are largest.
