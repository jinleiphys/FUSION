# Small Bits of Cold Dense Matter

> The behavior of QCD at high baryon density and low temperature is crucial to understanding the properties of neutron stars and gravitational waves emitted during their mergers. In this paper we study small systems of baryons in periodic boundary conditions to probe the properties of QCD at high baryon density. By comparing calculations based on nucleon degrees of freedom to simple quark models we show that specific features of the nuclear spectrum, including shell structure and nucleon pairing, emerge if nucleons are the primary degrees of freedom. Very small systems should also be amenable to studies in lattice QCD, unlike larger systems where the fermion sign problem is much more severe. Through comparisons of lattice QCD and nuclear calculations it should be possible to gain, at at least a semi-quantitative level, more understanding of the cold dense equation of state as probed in neutron stars.

## Key claim

By comparing nucleonic and quark-model calculations for small numbers of baryons in periodic boundary conditions, the authors show that shell structure and nucleon pairing emerge only if nucleons are the primary degrees of freedom, and that these signatures could be used to identify the transition from hadronic to quark degrees of freedom in future lattice QCD simulations, though the study is limited to small volumes that may suffer from finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors performed quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Green's function Monte Carlo and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo) for systems of 3 to 14 neutrons in cubic periodic volumes, using the Argonne AV18, AV8', and local N2LO chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions. They compared these results to simple quark models (free and interacting quarks with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-like pairing interaction) in the same periodic boundary conditions, adjusting the quark confinement energy to match the nuclear result for 14 neutrons at twice saturation density.

## Key numbers

- For four neutrons at twice saturation density (0.32 fm^-3), the s-wave state is roughly 100 MeV higher than the d-wave state in nuclear models
- The energy gap between N_B=4 and N_B=6 for paired quarks at twice saturation density (0.32 fm^-3) is approximately 120 MeV per baryon, compared to approximately 4 MeV per baryon for the interacting nuclear case
- At three times saturation density (0.48 fm^-3), the gap between N_B=4 and N_B=6 for paired quarks is approximately 310 MeV per baryon, compared to approximately 15 MeV per baryon for the interacting nuclear case
- The smallest box considered (N=4 at density 0.48 fm^-3) has a box size of L approximately 2.03 fm
- For free noninteracting quarks at density 0.48 fm^-3, the gap between N_B=6 and N_B=4 is approximately 170 MeV; at density 0.32 fm^-3 it is approximately 120 MeV
- The energy difference between s-wave and d-wave states in the quark model with gluon-exchange spin-interaction favors s-wave by approximately 27 MeV per baryon at twice saturation density and 45 MeV per baryon at three times saturation density

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron matter in periodic boundary conditions, building on prior calculations of two neutrons in finite volume using lattice QCD (Beane 2006, Ishii 2007, Berkowitz 2015) and quantum Monte Carlo methods (Klos 2016), as well as studies of three and four neutrons in external wells at low density (Gandolfi 2017). The paper enables future direct comparisons between nuclear model calculations and lattice QCD simulations for small baryon numbers, where the fermion sign problem is less severe than for larger systems, potentially providing semi-quantitative understanding of the cold dense equation of state relevant to neutron star properties and gravitational wave emission from mergers.
