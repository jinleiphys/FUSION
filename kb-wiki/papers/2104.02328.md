# Jet Transport Coefficient at the Large Hadron Collider Energies in a Color String Percolation Approach

> Within the color string percolation model (CSPM), jet transport coefficient, $\hat{q}$, is calculated for various multiplicity classes in proton-proton and centrality classes in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider energies for a better understanding of the matter formed in ultra-relativistic collisions. $\hat{q}$ is studied as a function of final state charged particle multiplicity (pseudorapiditydensity at midrapidity), initial state percolation temperature and energy density. The CSPM results are then compared with different theoretical calculations from the JET Collaboration those incorporate particle energy loss in the medium.

# Jet Transport Coefficient at the Large Hadron Collider Energies in a Color String Percolation Approach

## Key claim

Within the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM), the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ increases linearly with initial percolation temperature regardless of collision system or energy, but the authors note that their $\hat{q}$ values are significantly smaller than those published by the JET Collaboration because they use charged particle spectra and cannot reach the same initial temperatures.

## Method

The authors extract the color suppression factor $F(\xi)$ by fitting ALICE charged particle transverse momentum spectra (in the range 0.15-1.0 GeV/c) using a power-law parameterization modified by string interactions, for multiplicity-dependent pp collisions at 5.02 and 13 TeV, centrality-dependent Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, and Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV. The percolation density parameter $\xi$ is obtained from $F(\xi)$ via the relation $F(\xi) = \sqrt{(1-e^{-\xi})/\xi}$, and the initial percolation temperature is calculated using the Schwinger mechanism. The jet transport coefficient is then derived from the CSPM expression for shear viscosity-to-entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ using the relation $\hat{q} \approx (3/2) T^3 / (\eta/s)$.

## Key numbers

- Single string average transverse momentum: $\sqrt{\langle p_T^2 \rangle_1} = 207.2 \pm 3.3$ MeV, obtained at critical percolation density $\xi_c = 1.2$ with universal hadronization temperature $T_h = 167.7 \pm 2.6$ MeV
- Initial percolation temperature for most central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV: approximately 223 MeV
- Jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ ranges from approximately 0.02 GeV$^3$ at low temperatures to approximately 0.08 GeV$^3$ at high temperatures in the CSPM approach
- JET Collaboration values for a 10 GeV quark jet: $\hat{q} = 0.23 \pm 0.05$ GeV$^3$ at T = 346-373 MeV (RHIC Au-Au) and $\hat{q} = 0.37 \pm 0.13$ GeV$^3$ at T = 447-486 MeV (LHC Pb-Pb) at initial time $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c
- JET Collaboration scaled values: $\hat{q}/T^3 \approx 4.6 \pm 1.2$ at RHIC and $3.7 \pm 1.4$ at LHC
- Error in $F(\xi)$ extraction from varying fitting ranges: approximately 3%

## Context

This work extends the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) framework, previously used to describe thermodynamic and transport properties of QCD matter (Sahoo 2018, Sahu 2020, Mishra 2020), to the calculation of the jet transport coefficient. The results are compared with five theoretical models from the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013): GLV-CUJET, HT-BW (Berkeley-Wuhan), HT-M (Majumder), MARTINI, and MCGILL-AMY, which incorporate parton energy loss in the medium. The CSPM approach provides a non-perturbative alternative to the Color Glass Condensate and Glasma approaches, and the study enables experimental exploration of possible QGP-droplet formation in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies.
