# Analysis of uncertainties in alpha-particle optical-potential assessment below the Coulomb barrier

> Background: Recent high-precision measurements of alpha-induced reaction data below the Coulomb barrier have pointed out questions of the alpha-particle optical-model potential (OMP) which are yet open within various mass ranges. Purpose: The applicability of a previous optical potential and eventual uncertainties and/or systematic errors of the OMP assessment at low energies can be further considered on this basis. Method: Nuclear model parameters based on the analysis of recent independent data, particularly gamma-ray strength functions, have been involved within statistical model calculation of the (alpha,x) reaction cross sections. Results: The above-mentioned potential provides a consistent description of the recent alpha-induced reaction data with no empirical rescaling factors of the and/or nucleon widths. Conclusions: A suitable assessment of alpha-particle optical potential below the Coulomb barrier should involve the statistical-model parameters beyond this potential on the basis of a former analysis of independent data.

## Key claim

The previously established alpha-particle optical-model potential (OMP) for nuclei in the mass range 45 ≤ A ≤ 209 provides a consistent description of recent high-precision alpha-induced reaction data below the Coulomb barrier without requiring empirical rescaling factors of gamma and/or nucleon widths, provided that the statistical-model parameters (nuclear level densities, gamma-ray strength functions, and nucleon OMPs) are themselves based on independent measured data rather than fitted to the alpha-reaction data.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using the STAPRE-H95 code (and TALYS 1.8 for one case) with a consistent input parameter set: the alpha-particle OMP of Avrigeanu (2014), nucleon OMPs from Koning and Delaroche (2003) with local adjustments for low-energy neutrons on Ni and low-energy protons on Cu, back-shifted Fermi gas level densities fitted to low-lying levels and s-wave resonance spacings, and enhanced generalized Lorentzian (EGLO) gamma-ray strength functions validated against measured radiative strength function data and average radiation widths. The calculated cross sections were compared with recent high-precision measurements for Ni isotopes, 107Ag, 106Cd, and 112Sn, as well as with the TENDL-2015 evaluated library.

## Key numbers

* For Ni isotopes, the McFadden and Satchler OMP gives (50-100)% higher cross sections at the lowest incident energies but (10-20)% lower cross sections above 9 MeV compared to the Avrigeanu (2014) potential.
* For 64Ni(alpha,gamma)68Zn, replacing the adopted nucleon OMPs with the global Koning and Delaroche (2003) potentials changes the calculated cross section by less than 15%, with a maximum decrease of 23% near the minimum just above the (alpha,n) reaction threshold.
* For 106Cd, replacing the EGLO gamma-ray strength model with the GLO or SLO models decreases the minor (alpha,p) reaction cross sections below the particle-emission threshold by factors of 2-3 and 3-5, respectively.
* For 60Ni, the direct-interaction inelastic scattering cross section grows from about 1% to less than 6% of the alpha-particle total reaction cross section for alpha energies from 5 to 7.6 MeV.
* The global neutron OMP of Koning and Delaroche (2003) overestimates neutron total cross sections for Zn isotopes by about 40% for neutron energies below 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous alpha-particle OMP analysis (Avrigeanu 2014) and addresses questions raised by recent high-precision measurements on Ni (Sauerwein 2015), 107Ag (Cristallo 2015), 106Cd (Al-Abdullah 2015), and 112Sn (Lederer 2015). The paper contradicts the conclusion of Sauerwein (2015) that different best combinations of TALYS input parameters are needed for each Ni isotope, showing instead that a consistent parameter set works for all. It also contradicts the claim of Al-Abdullah (2015) that alpha-induced reaction data cannot constrain the alpha OMP for 106Cd, demonstrating that the earlier underestimation of (alpha,p) cross sections was due to deficient gamma-ray strength functions rather than the alpha OMP. The work enables reliable statistical model predictions for alpha-induced reactions below the Coulomb barrier without empirical rescaling, which is relevant for nuclear astrophysics and fusion technology applications.
