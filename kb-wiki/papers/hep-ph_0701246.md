# Associated Charmonium Production in p-pbar Annihilation

> In this paper we summarize our recent results for low energy associated charmonium production cross sections, using 1) crossing symmetry, and 2) an explicit hadronic model. These predictions are of relevance to the planned charmonium and charmonium hybrid production experiment PANDA at GSI.

## Key claim
The paper presents two theoretical estimates for low-energy associated charmonium production cross sections in proton-antiproton annihilation, finding that the methods agree within about a factor of two and that the explicit hadronic model shows a similar level of agreement with the few existing data points for the single reaction \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) from the E760 and E835 experiments at Fermilab.

## Method
The author uses two approaches: (1) a crossing-symmetry method (Lundborg et al., 2005) that relates the decay rate of \(J/\psi \to \pi^0 p\bar p\) to the production cross section \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) via a constant-amplitude approximation; (2) an explicit hadronic model (Barnes, 2006) with Feynman diagrams where a pion is emitted from an incoming proton or antiproton line, using measured \(p\bar p\) annihilation widths to fix the \(\Psi\)-\(p\bar p\) coupling. Cross sections are calculated for \(\Psi = \eta_c, J/\psi, \chi_0, \chi_1, \psi'\).

## Key numbers
- The measured cross section for \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) is roughly 100 pb near 3.5 GeV (from E760 and E835 experiments).
- The crossing-symmetry constant-amplitude prediction for \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) agrees with the data to within about a factor of two.
- The hadronic model predicts the cross section for \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 \eta_c\) is about a factor of 30 larger than that for \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 J/\psi\) in the center-of-mass energy range relevant to PANDA.
- The hadronic model predicts a characteristic node in the angular distribution for \(p\bar p \to \pi^0 \eta_c\) at \(\theta = \pi/2\) for center-of-mass energies from 3.2 to 5.0 GeV in steps of 0.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the crossing-symmetry approach of Lundborg et al. (2005) and the earlier hadronic model of Gaillard, Maiani, and Petronzio (1982) to a wider range of charmonium states. The predictions are directly relevant to the planned PANDA experiment at GSI, which will search for charmonium and charmonium hybrid states in \(p\bar p\) annihilation. The results enable experimental planning by providing cross-section estimates for multiple final states (\(\eta_c, J/\psi, \chi_0, \chi_1, \psi'\)) and suggest that some hybrid states may be produced with enhanced cross sections due to their coupling to two-gluon final states.
