# Restoring Broken Symmetries for Nuclei and Reaction Fragments

> In typical microscopic approaches, particularly when pairing correlations are present, nuclei and nuclear fragments do not have well defined quantum numbers and symmetries should be restored. I present here a formalism for the simultaneous projection of total particle numbers of a nucleus, particle number of reaction fragments, and of the reaction fragment intrinsic spins and of their correlation, and also for their symmetry restored densities and total energies. These new formulas for the symmetry restored quantities, are free of any singularities, unlike those in the previously introduced prescriptions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for simultaneously projecting total particle number, fragment particle number, and fragment intrinsic spins (and their correlations) that is free of singularities, unlike previously introduced prescriptions which suffer from singularities when occupation numbers equal 1/2 and the projection angle equals ±π/2.

## Method

The author derives formulas for symmetry-restored densities and total energies using functional derivatives of the overlap of generalized Slater determinants under gauge transformations, rather than applying the generalized Wick theorem to evaluate expectation values. The approach uses a double projection technique: first projecting the total particle number, then projecting the fragment particle number, with the overlap evaluated using the Onishi-Yoshida formula modified by a redefinition of the quasiparticle operators to avoid spurious frequency components. The formalism is developed for systems with even particle parity within a density functional theory framework.

## Key numbers

- The overlap for double projection is given by ⟨Ψ|Ψ(η₀, ηF)⟩ = √(det[δkl + (e^(2iη₀)-1)Okl + e^(2iη₀)(e^(iηF)-1)OklF]), where Okl = ⟨vk|vl⟩ and OklF = ⟨vk|ΘL,H|vl⟩
- The particle probability distribution in a fragment is P(N,NF) = ∫ dη₀/π ∫ dηF/(2π) Re[⟨Ψ|Ψ(η₀, ηF)⟩ e^(-iη₀N-iηFNF)] with η₀ ∈ [-π/2, π/2] and ηF ∈ [-π, π]
- The projected number density in canonical basis is n(ξ,ξ'|η₀) = ⟨Φ|Φ(η₀)⟩ Σk [ṽk*(ξ)ṽk(ξ') e^(2iη₀)] / [1+(e^(2iη₀)-1)nk]
- The projected anomalous density in canonical basis is κ(ξ,ξ'|η₀) = ⟨Φ|Φ(η₀)⟩ Σk [ṽk*(ξ)ũk(ξ') e^(2iη₀)] / [1+(e^(2iη₀)-1)nk]
- The overlap ⟨Φ|Φ(η₀)⟩ = Πk √[1+(e^(2iη₀)-1)nk] where canonical occupation numbers nk are double degenerate

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formalism in Bulgac (2019a) for symmetry restoration in density functional theory, correcting inaccuracies in that earlier paper. It addresses difficulties in applying Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov projection techniques to DFT studies discussed by Anguiano (2001), Robledo (2007), Dobaczewski (2007), Lacroix (2009), Bender (2009), Duguet (2009), and Bally (2021). The formulas were developed for fission applications but can be adapted for heavy-ion reactions, and have been used in recent works by Bulgac (2021) and Bulgac (2021b).
