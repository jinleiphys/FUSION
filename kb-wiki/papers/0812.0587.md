# Electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering from the quasielastic to the resonance region

> We present a model for electron- and neutrino-scattering off nucleons and nuclei focussing on the quasielastic and resonance region. The lepton-nucleon reaction is described within a relativistic formalism that includes, besides quasielastic scattering, the excitation of 13 N* and Delta resonances and a non-resonant single-pion background. Recent electron-scattering data is used for the state-of-the-art parametrizations of the vector form factors; the axial couplings are determined via PCAC and, in the case of the Delta resonance, the axial form factor is refitted using neutrino-scattering data. Scattering off nuclei is treated within the GiBUU framework that takes into account various nuclear effects: the local density approximation for the nuclear ground state, mean-field potentials and in-medium spectral functions. Results for inclusive scattering off Oxygen are presented and, in the case of electron-induced reactions, compared to experimental data and other models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified model for electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering that treats quasielastic scattering, 13 baryon resonances, and non-resonant single-pion background within the same relativistic formalism, but the accuracy of neutrino predictions depends on the axial form factors being refitted from neutrino data and on nuclear effects treated via the GiBUU framework.

## Method

The lepton-nucleon interaction is described with a relativistic formalism including quasielastic scattering, excitation of 13 N* and Delta resonances with masses below 2 GeV, and a non-resonant single-pion background. Vector form factors are taken from the MAID2005 analysis of electron scattering data, while axial couplings are determined via PCAC and, for the Delta resonance, refitted using neutrino-scattering data. Nuclear effects are incorporated through the GiBUU framework using a local density approximation for the nuclear ground state, mean-field potentials, and in-medium spectral functions.

## Context

This work extends previous GiBUU calculations by Buss et al. (2007) that included only the Delta resonance, now incorporating 12 additional resonances and a non-resonant background for neutrino scattering. The model builds on the MAID analysis of electron scattering (Tiator et al., 2006; Drechsel et al., 2007) for vector form factors and uses PCAC relations similar to those applied by Fogli et al. (1979) and Lalakulich et al. (2006) for axial couplings. The unified treatment of electron and neutrino scattering enables benchmarking nuclear corrections against precise electron data before applying them to neutrino reactions, which is essential for reducing systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as K2K, MiniBooNE, SciBooNE, T2K, MINOS, and MINERvA.
