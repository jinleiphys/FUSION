# Heavy-quark energy loss in pQCD and SYM plasmas

> We consider heavy-quark energy loss and pT-broadening in a strongly-coupled N=4 Super Yang Mills (SYM) plasma, and the problem of finite-extend matter is addressed. When expressed in terms of the appropriate saturation momentum, one finds identical parametric forms for the energy loss in pQCD and SYM theory, while pT-broadening is radiation dominated in SYM theory and multiple scattering dominated in pQCD.

## Key claim

When heavy-quark energy loss is expressed in terms of the appropriate saturation momentum, the parametric forms are identical in perturbative QCD (pQCD) and strongly-coupled N=4 Super Yang Mills (SYM) theory, but the underlying mechanisms for pT-broadening differ fundamentally: radiation-dominated in SYM versus multiple-scattering-dominated in pQCD. The claim is conditional on the existence of a saturation scale that can be identified in both regimes, and the results for finite-extend matter are inferred rather than derived from exact AdS/CFT calculations.

## Method

The author compares heavy-quark energy loss in weakly-coupled QCD plasma (using the standard pQCD multiple-scattering formalism with the dead-cone effect) with that in strongly-coupled N=4 SYM plasma (using the AdS/CFT correspondence and the trailing-string picture). For the SYM case, the classical string dynamics is solved via the Nambu-Goto action in an AdS black-hole background, identifying the saturation scale Q_s with the special point u = sqrt(gamma) u_h on the string. The finite-extend matter case is addressed by analogy with the pQCD treatment, using the coherence time t_c to distinguish infinite from finite matter regimes.

## Key numbers

- In pQCD, the transport coefficient is given by hat{q} ~ alpha_s T^3, and at RHIC temperature the value is hat{q} ~ 1 GeV^2/fm (though 5-10 GeV^2/fm seems favored by RHIC data).
- In the strongly-coupled SYM plasma, the energy loss rate for infinite matter is -dE/dt = (sqrt(lambda)/(2 pi)) u_h^2 gamma v^2, where u_h = pi T.
- For finite-extend matter in SYM, the saturation scale is Q_s = L T^2, giving energy loss -dE/dt proportional to sqrt(lambda) Q_s^2.
- For infinite matter in SYM, the saturation scale is Q_s = sqrt(gamma) u_h = sqrt(gamma) pi T.
- The pT-broadening in SYM for infinite matter gives dp_perp^2/dt proportional to sqrt(lambda gamma) T^3, and for finite matter gives dp_perp^2/dt proportional to sqrt(lambda) T^4 L.
- The L-dependence at strong coupling is Delta E ~ L^3 and Delta p_perp^2 ~ L^2 in SYM, compared to Delta E ~ L^2 and Delta p_perp^2 ~ L in pQCD.

## Context

This work extends the trailing-string calculations of heavy-quark energy loss in infinite SYM plasmas by Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006) to the finite-extend matter case, and provides a unified saturation-scale interpretation that bridges the pQCD formalism of Baier (1996) with the AdS/CFT approach. The identification of Q_s with the screening length found by Liu (2006) for quarkonium dissociation connects heavy-quark energy loss to other strongly-coupled phenomena. The results enable phenomenological estimates of heavy-quark suppression in heavy-ion collisions that can distinguish between weak and strong coupling scenarios, and the finite-length dependence provides a testable prediction for how energy loss scales with system size.
