# Cooperative internal conversion process

> A new phenomenon, called cooperative internal conversion process, in which the coupling of bound-free electron and neutron transitions due to the dipole term of their Coulomb interaction permits cooperation of two nuclei leading to neutron exchange if it is allowed by energy conservation, is discussed theoretically. General expression of the cross section of the process is reported in one particle nuclear and spherical shell models as well in the case of free atoms (e.g. noble gases). A half-life characteristic of the process is also determined. The case of $Ne$ is investigated numerically. The process may have significance in fields of nuclear waste disposal and nuclear energy production.

## Key claim

The paper theoretically proposes a new "cooperative internal conversion process" (CICP) in which two nuclei exchange a neutron via the coupling of bound-free electron and neutron transitions, mediated by the dipole term of their Coulomb interaction, but only if the sum of the rest energies of the initial nuclei exceeds that of the final nuclei (Δ > 0). The authors acknowledge that the calculated half-life for neon is extremely long (≈ 1.2 × 10⁹ years) and does not alter natural abundance observably, but suggest the process could be measurable and might have practical significance for nuclear waste disposal under favorable conditions.

## Method

The authors use standard time-independent second-order perturbation theory with two perturbations: the electric dipole term of the Coulomb interaction between a bound electron and a neutron (with effective neutron charge qₙ = -Z₁e/A₁) in the first nucleus, and a rectangular nuclear strong interaction potential for neutron capture by the second nucleus. The cross section is derived in a one-particle nuclear model and spherical shell model, using hydrogen-like 1s initial electron states and 0d shell model states for the neon isotopes, with plane waves for free particles and the long-wavelength approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction e + ²¹Ne + ²¹Ne → e′ + ²⁰Ne + ²²Ne + Δ: Δ = 3.603 MeV, Δ₋ = 1.310 MeV, E_Bi (K-shell binding energy) = 870.1 eV
- vσ_bf,sh(K) = 2.48 × 10⁻³⁴ cm³ s⁻¹ for bound-free CICP from the K shell of neon
- λ₁ > 1.8 × 10⁻¹⁷ s⁻¹ and τ₁/₂,₁ < 3.8 × 10¹⁶ s (1.2 × 10⁹ y) for neon gas at normal state (n = 2.652 × 10¹⁹ cm⁻³)
- r_tot > 1.25 cm⁻³ s⁻¹ for the total rate per unit volume of the neon sample
- K_bf/(2E₂₀) = 4.77 × 10⁻²⁵ cm³ s⁻¹ MeV⁻¹ / v, with 2E₂₀ = 3.26 MeV
- The Weisskopf-estimation indicates the cross section may increase very much with increasing atomic number; if neutron capture is not restricted to direct reaction, σ_bf,sh and λ₁ increase by a factor of about 240

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior theoretical study of the "solid state internal conversion process" for the p+d → ³He reaction (Kálmán and Keszthelyi, 2015) and is motivated by the anomalous screening effect observed in low-energy dd reactions (Raiola et al.; Huke et al.). The paper identifies specific long-lived fission products (e.g., ¹¹³ᵐCd, ¹²¹ᵐSn, ¹⁵¹Sm, ⁷⁹Se, ⁹³Zr, ¹⁰⁷Pd) that could participate in CICP, and also lists a family of e+d+_Z^A X → e′+p+_Z^{A+1}X+Δ reactions that may open new perspectives in nuclear energy production. The main open question is whether the process can be made practically relevant despite the very long half-lives calculated for neon, requiring high concentration and density of suitable isotopes in atomic state.
