# Scalar Mesons in $ϕ$ Radiative Decay: their implications for spectroscopy and for studies of CP-violation at $ϕ$ factories

> Existing predictions for the branching ratio for $ϕ\rightarrow K\bar{K}γ$ {\it via} $ϕ\rightarrow Sγ$ (where $S$ denotes one of the scalar mesons $f_0$(975) and $a_0$(980)) vary by several orders of magnitude. Given the importance of these processes for both hadron spectroscopy and CP-violation studies at $ϕ$ factories (where $ϕ\rightarrow K^0 \bar K^0 γ$ poses a possible background problem), this state of affairs is very undesirable. We show that the variety of predictions is due in part to errors and in part to differences in modelling. The latter variation leads us to argue that the radiative decays of these scalar states are interesting in their own right and may offer unique insights into the nature of the scalar mesons. As a byproduct we find that the branching ratio for $ϕ\rightarrow K^0\bar{K}^0γ$ is $\lapproxeq 0(10^{-7})$ and will pose no significant background to proposed studies of CP-violation.

## Key claim

The branching ratio for $\phi \rightarrow K^0\bar{K}^0\gamma$ is $\lesssim \mathcal{O}(10^{-7})$, which will pose no significant background to proposed studies of CP-violation at $\phi$ factories, but this conclusion depends on the dynamical nature of the scalar mesons $f_0(975)$ and $a_0(980)$; the authors show that the wide variation in prior predictions arises partly from errors and partly from genuine model dependence, and argue that measuring these radiative decays can discriminate among quarkonium, glueball, and $K\bar{K}$ molecule interpretations.

## Method

The authors compute the resonant contribution to $\phi \rightarrow K\bar{K}\gamma$ via the chain $\phi \rightarrow S\gamma$ followed by $S \rightarrow K\bar{K}$, where $S$ is either $f_0(975)$ or $a_0(980)$. They evaluate the charged kaon loop amplitude $\phi \rightarrow K^+K^- \rightarrow S\gamma$ using both a point-like field theory and a nonrelativistic treatment with a momentum-dependent form factor $\phi(k)$ for an extended $K\bar{K}$ bound state, solving the Schrödinger equation with a Gaussian potential to obtain the $K\bar{K}$ molecule wavefunction. They also compare predictions for different internal structures of the scalar mesons (quarkonium, glueball, $qq\bar{q}\bar{q}$ in various cluster configurations, and $K\bar{K}$ molecule) by computing the electric dipole transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for $\phi \rightarrow K^0\bar{K}^0\gamma$ is $\lesssim \mathcal{O}(10^{-7})$, posing no significant background to CP-violation studies.
- For the point-like field theory calculation, $\Gamma(\phi \rightarrow f_0\gamma) = 6 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV (using $m(f_0) = 975$ MeV and $g^2/4\pi = 0.6$ GeV$^2$).
- For the $K\bar{K}$ molecule interpretation with $R_{K\bar{K}} \simeq 1.2$ fm, the predicted branching ratio for $\phi \rightarrow S\gamma$ is $4 \times 10^{-5}$ (width $\simeq 10^{-4}$ MeV), about one-fifth of the point-like field theory result.
- The ratio $\Gamma(\phi \rightarrow a_0\gamma)/\Gamma(\phi \rightarrow f_0\gamma)$ can be approximately 0 (if $f_0$ is $s\bar{s}$ quarkonium and $a_0$ is OZI-decoupled), approximately 1 (if both are $K\bar{K}$ molecules or $(q\bar{s})(\bar{q}s)$ clusters), or approximately 9 (if both are diquark-antidiquark $(us)(\bar{u}\bar{s})$ type states).
- The glueball interpretation gives a branching ratio for $\phi \rightarrow f_0\gamma$ more than an order of magnitude smaller than the quarkonium prediction, estimated from $\Gamma(f_0\rightarrow\pi\pi)/\Gamma(^3P_0\rightarrow\pi\pi) \leq 1/20$.

## Context

This work addresses the confusion in prior predictions for $\phi \rightarrow K\bar{K}\gamma$ via scalar mesons, which varied by several orders of magnitude across Refs. [1-5]. The authors correct a numerical error in Ref. [1] and reconcile the differing results of Refs. [1-4] and Ref. [5], showing that apparent structure-dependence in Ref. [5] actually arises from using different $SK\bar{K}$ couplings rather than genuine dynamical effects. The paper extends the $K\bar{K}$ molecule interpretation of Weinstein and Isgur (1990) and the bag-model $qq\bar{q}\bar{q}$ picture of Jaffe (1977), and provides a test to distinguish these from the $^3P_0$ quarkonium interpretation of Barnes, Close, and others. The results enable future experiments at DA$\Phi$NE to use $\phi \rightarrow S\gamma$ decays as a probe of scalar meson structure, while reassuring that the $\phi \rightarrow K^0\bar{K}^0\gamma$ background to CP-violation measurements (proposed by Bigi and Sanda) is negligible.
