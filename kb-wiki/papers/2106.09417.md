# Inclusive Production of Heavy Quarkonia in pNRQCD

> We develop a formalism for computing inclusive production cross sections of heavy quarkonia based on the nonrelativistic QCD and the potential nonrelativistic QCD effective field theories. Our formalism applies to strongly coupled quarkonia, which include excited charmonium and bottomonium states. Analogously to heavy quarkonium decay processes, we express nonrelativistic QCD long-distance matrix elements in terms of quarkonium wavefunctions at the origin and universal gluonic correlators. Our expressions for the long-distance matrix elements are valid up to corrections of order $1/N_c^2$. These expressions enhance the predictive power of the nonrelativistic effective field theory approach to inclusive production processes by reducing the number of nonperturbative unknowns, and make possible first-principle determinations of long-distance matrix elements once the gluonic correlators are known. Based on this formalism, we compute the production cross sections of $P$-wave charmonia and bottomonia at the LHC, and find good agreement with measurements.

## Key claim

The authors develop a formalism for computing inclusive production cross sections of heavy quarkonia using potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), expressing NRQCD long-distance matrix elements in terms of quarkonium wavefunctions at the origin and universal gluonic correlators, valid up to corrections of order \(1/N_c^2\) and \(v^2\), with the condition that the formalism applies only to strongly coupled quarkonia where \(mv^2 \ll \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).

## Method

The authors match NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) for inclusive production onto pNRQCD by expanding the NRQCD Hamiltonian in inverse powers of the heavy quark mass and using quantum-mechanical perturbation theory to compute eigenstates order by order in \(1/m\). They express the projection operator \({\cal P}_{{\cal Q}(\bm{P})}\) in terms of quarkonium wavefunctions that are eigenfunctions of the pNRQCD Hamiltonian at leading order in \(v\), and compute the contact terms for color-singlet and color-octet operators using techniques previously developed for decay matrix elements. The resulting LDMEs depend on quarkonium wavefunctions at the origin (or their derivatives) and on vacuum expectation values of gluon fields.

## Key numbers

- Reference values for \(v^2\) are 0.3 in the charmonium case and 0.1 in the bottomonium case.
- The expressions for the long-distance matrix elements are valid up to corrections of order \(1/N_c^2\) and \(v^2\).
- The formalism applies in the strong coupling regime where \(mv^2 \ll \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), which is satisfied by non-Coulombic, strongly coupled quarkonia.

## Context

This work extends the application of strongly coupled pNRQCD, previously successfully applied to heavy quarkonium decay and exclusive electromagnetic production processes (Brambilla et al., 2001-2020), to inclusive production processes of heavy quarkonia. The formalism addresses the long-standing problem that NRQCD color-octet production LDMEs could not be computed from first principles and had to be obtained from fits to data, which have led to inconsistent determinations (Chung, 2018; Lansberg, 2019). The approach enables first-principle determinations of LDMEs once the universal gluonic correlators are known, and the authors compute production cross sections of \(P\)-wave charmonia and bottomonia at the LHC, finding good agreement with measurements.
