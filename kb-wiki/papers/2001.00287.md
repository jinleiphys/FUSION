# Thermomagnetic properties and Debye Screening for magnetized quark-gluon Plasma using the extended self-consistent quasiparticle model

> The thermomagnetic behavior of quark-gluon plasma has recently received a lot of attention. In this work, we make use of the extended self-consistent quasiparticle model to study the thermodynamic properties of magnetized (2+1) flavor quark-gluon plasma. The system is considered as a non-interacting system of quasiparticles with masses depending on both temperature and magnetic field. This allows obtaining the equation of state of the system and the other thermodynamic properties such as the speed of sound. We use the extended self-consistent model to obtain the magnetization and show that QGP has a paramagnetic nature. In addition, we study the pressure anisotropy and calculate the transverse pressure. The obtained anisotropic pressure may be used in hydrodynamic studies of magnetized QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions. Finally, we examine the screening properties of magnetized QGP in the longitudinal direction by calculating the Debye screening mass.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the extended self-consistent quasiparticle model, which treats QGP as non-interacting quasiparticles with thermomagnetic masses, can qualitatively reproduce the paramagnetic nature, pressure anisotropy, and enhanced Debye screening of magnetized (2+1) flavor QGP, but the authors explicitly note that their results are bound to be qualitative because the one-loop thermomagnetic coupling used is appropriate only at very high temperatures and in the strong magnetic field limit.

## Method

The authors model (2+1) flavor QGP as a non-interacting gas of quasiparticles whose masses depend on both temperature and magnetic field, obtained by solving self-consistent equations for the plasma frequencies modified by Landau level quantization. They compute the thermodynamic pressure, speed of sound, magnetization, transverse pressure, and longitudinal Debye screening mass from the grand canonical potential, using a one-loop running coupling that evolves with both momentum transfer and magnetic field in the Lowest Landau Level approximation. The magnetization is obtained by differentiating the grand potential with respect to the magnetic field, and the transverse pressure is derived from the longitudinal pressure minus the product of magnetic field and magnetization.

## Key numbers

- Thermodynamic pressure increases with magnetic field at fixed temperature, consistent with lattice QCD results from Bali (2014) and perturbative QCD results from Karmakar (2019).
- The speed of sound squared reaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of 1/3 asymptotically at high temperature.
- Magnetization is positive for all temperatures above the transition temperature, indicating paramagnetic behavior, with a small deviation observed for eB = 0.2 GeV² at higher temperatures due to higher Landau level contributions.
- Transverse pressure can become negative at high magnetic fields, suggesting the system may shrink in the transverse direction, consistent with lattice QCD results from Bali (2014) and Bali (2014a).
- Debye screening mass increases with both temperature and magnetic field, in agreement with lattice QCD simulations from Bonati (2017) and perturbative calculations from Bandyopadhyay (2016).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous extended self-consistent quasiparticle model from 2-flavor QGP (Koothottil, 2019) to (2+1) flavor QGP, and applies it to study magnetization, pressure anisotropy, and Debye screening. The results are qualitatively consistent with lattice QCD calculations (Bali, 2014; Bali, 2014a; Bonati, 2017), Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (Karmakar, 2019), and other phenomenological models (Kurian, 2017; Ghosh, 2018). The equation of state and anisotropic pressure obtained can serve as input for magnetohydrodynamic calculations of QGP in heavy-ion collisions, and the model could be extended to study transport coefficients and finite-density QGP relevant for strongly magnetized neutron stars.
