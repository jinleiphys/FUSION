# Evidence for $\barν_μ\to\barν_e$ Oscillations from the LSND Experiment at LAMPF

> A search for $\barν_μ\to \barν_{e}$ oscillations has been conducted at the Los Alamos Meson Physics Facility by using $\barν_μ$ from $μ^+$ decay at rest. The $\barν_e$ are detected via the reaction $\barν_e\,p \rightarrow e^{+}\,n$, correlated with a $γ$ from $np\rightarrow dγ$ ($2.2\,{\rm MeV}$). The use of tight cuts to identify $e^+$ events with correlated $γ$ rays yields 22 events with $e^+$ energy between 36 and $60\,{\rm MeV}$ and only $4.6 \pm 0.6$ background events. A fit to the $e^+$ events between 20 and $60\,{\rm MeV}$ yields a total excess of $51.8^{+18.7}_{-16.9} \pm 8.0$ events. If attributed to $\bar ν_μ\rightarrow \bar ν_e$ oscillations, this corresponds to an oscillation probability of ($0.31^{+0.11}_{-0.10} \pm 0.05$)\%.

## Key claim

The LSND collaboration reports an excess of events consistent with \(\bar\nu_\mu \to \bar\nu_e\) oscillations, but the result is presented as an oscillation probability of \((0.31^{+0.11}_{-0.10} \pm 0.05)\%\) derived from a fit to the full energy range \(20 < E_e < 60\) MeV, with the caveat that the favored regions in \(\Delta m^2\) versus \(\sin^2 2\theta\) are likelihood regions (not formal confidence regions) and have been enlarged to account for systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The experiment used \(\bar\nu_\mu\) from \(\mu^+\) decay at rest at the LAMPF beam stop, detected via the reaction \(\bar\nu_e p \to e^+ n\) followed by neutron capture \(n p \to d\gamma\) (2.2 MeV) in a 167-ton liquid scintillator detector located about 30 m from the source. Positron candidates were selected using particle identification based on Cherenkov and scintillation light, and correlated 2.2 MeV gamma rays were identified using a likelihood ratio \(R\) to separate signal from accidental coincidences. The analysis used two sets of selection criteria (A and B) applied to data collected in 1993, 1994, and 1995, with beam-off data providing cosmic-ray background subtraction and Monte Carlo simulations estimating beam-related backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- 22 events observed with \(e^+\) energy between 36 and 60 MeV and \(R>30\) (correlated gamma requirement), compared to a total estimated background of \(4.6 \pm 0.6\) events
- Probability that the 22 events are due to a statistical fluctuation of the background: \(4.1 \times 10^{-8}\)
- Fit to events in the range \(20 < E_e < 60\) MeV yields a net excess of \(51.8^{+18.7}_{-16.9} \pm 8.0\) events
- Oscillation probability from the fit: \((0.31^{+0.11}_{-0.10} \pm 0.05)\%\)
- Total charge delivered to the beam stop: 1787 C (1993), 5904 C (1994), 7081 C (1995)
- \(\bar\nu_\mu\) flux averaged over detector volume: \(7.6 \times 10^{-10} \bar\nu_\mu/\text{cm}^2\) per proton
- Flux ratio of \(\bar\nu_e\) to \(\bar\nu_\mu\) from beam backgrounds: \(7.8 \times 10^{-4}\) (Monte Carlo estimate)

## Context

This result extends the LSND collaboration's earlier report (Athanassopoulos 1995) on 1993-1994 data by including 1995 data and using improved selection criteria that increased signal efficiency by about 40%. The observed excess is in tension with limits from the KARMEN experiment at ISIS (Bodmann 1991, 1992), the E776 experiment at BNL (Borodovsky 1992), and the Bugey reactor experiment (Achkar 1995), which are shown as 90% C.L. limits in the \(\Delta m^2\) versus \(\sin^2 2\theta\) parameter space. The result motivated further searches for neutrino oscillations at higher precision and contributed to the eventual discovery of non-zero \(\theta_{13}\) and the understanding of neutrino mass ordering.
