# Nonperturbative Effects in Gluon Radiation and Photoproduction of Quark Pairs

> We introduce a nonperturbative interaction for light-cone fluctuations containing quarks and gluons. The $\bar qq$ interaction squeezes the transverse size of these fluctuations in the photon and one does not need to simulate this effect via effective quark masses. The strength of this interaction is fixed by data. Data on diffractive dissociation of hadrons and photons show that the nonperturbative interaction of gluons is much stronger. We fix the parameters for the nonperturbative quark-gluon interaction by data for diffractive dissociation to large masses (triple-Pomeron regime). This allows us to predict nuclear shadowing for gluons which turns out to be not as strong as perturbative QCD predicts. We expect a delayed onset of gluon shadowing at $x \leq 10^{-2}$ shadowing of quarks. Gluon shadowing turns out to be nearly scale invariant up to virtualities $Q^2\sim 4 GeV^2$ due to presence of a semihard scale characterizing the strong nonperturbative interaction of gluons. We use the same concept to improve our description of gluon bremsstrahlung which is related to the distribution function for a quark-gluon fluctuation and the interaction cross section of a $\bar qqG$ fluctuation with a nucleon. We expect the nonperturbative interaction to suppress dramatically the gluon radiation at small transverse momenta compared to perturbative calculations.

# Nonperturbative Effects in Gluon Radiation and Photoproduction of Quark Pairs

## Key claim

The authors introduce a nonperturbative interaction for light-cone fluctuations containing quarks and gluons, and claim that this interaction suppresses gluon radiation at small transverse momenta compared to perturbative QCD predictions, with the strength of the interaction fixed by data on diffractive dissociation to large masses (triple-Pomeron regime). The hedge is that gluon shadowing turns out to be not as strong as perturbative QCD predicts, with a delayed onset at x ≤ 10^{-2} relative to shadowing of quarks, and that gluon shadowing is nearly scale invariant only up to virtualities Q^2 ∼ 4 GeV^2 due to a semihard scale.

## Method

The authors model the nonperturbative interaction between quarks and gluons via a real potential in a Schrodinger-type evolution equation for the light-cone Green function of a qqbar or qG pair, with the potential parameterized by a Gaussian form in transverse separation. The parameters a_0 and a_1 for the qqbar potential are fixed by fitting the total photoabsorption cross section on protons (160 μb at sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and checked against forward diffraction dissociation and nuclear photoabsorption. The quark-gluon potential parameters b_0 and b_1 are fixed by data on diffractive dissociation of nucleons into large masses (triple-Pomeron regime). The dipole cross section sigma_qqbar(rho,s) is parameterized with a saturation form fitted to reproduce the pion-proton cross section and proton structure function data up to Q^2 ∼ 20 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Total photoabsorption cross section sigma^{gamma p}_{tot} = 160 μb at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, used to fix the qqbar potential parameters.
- The qqbar potential parameters are given by a_0^2 = v^{1.15} (112 MeV)^2 and a_1^2 = (1-v)^{1.15} (165 MeV)^2, where v varies between 0 and 1, producing constant total photoabsorption within 1%.
- The effective interaction cross section of a qqbar fluctuation with a nucleon is sigma^{qqbar N}_{eff} = 30 mb at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, corresponding to the pion-nucleon cross section sigma^{pi p}_{tot} = 31.7 mb at the same energy.
- The dipole cross section parameterization uses rho_0(s) = 0.88 fm (s_0/s)^{0.14} with s_0 = 1000 GeV^2, and sigma_0(s) = sigma^{pi p}_{tot}(s) (1 + 3 rho_0^2(s) / (8 <r^2_ch>_pi)), where <r^2_ch>_pi = 0.44 ± 0.01 fm^2.
- The Pomeron part of the pion-proton cross section is sigma^{pi p}_{tot}(s) = 23.6 (s/s_0)^{0.08} mb.
- Gluon shadowing is predicted to start at x < 0.01, with a steep x-dependence at Q^2 ≥ 4 GeV^2, and is nearly scale independent up to Q^2 ∼ 4 GeV^2.
- Nonperturbative suppression of gluon radiation at k_T = 0 is by almost two orders of magnitude compared to perturbative QCD predictions, with the difference remaining large up to a few GeV of momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone dipole approach of Kopeliovich and Schafer (1995) and the Green function method of Kopeliovich, Tarasov, and Zakharov (1994) by incorporating explicit nonperturbative interactions between quarks and gluons, rather than relying on effective quark masses. It contradicts the perturbative QCD predictions for gluon shadowing from Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1983) and from Frankfurt and Strikman (1998), finding weaker shadowing with delayed onset. The results enable improved predictions for nuclear shadowing of gluon distributions at small x, needed for calculations of hard reactions on nuclei such as deep-inelastic scattering, high-p_T jet production, and heavy flavor production, and also provide a framework for computing the transverse momentum distribution of gluon bremsstrahlung in nuclear matter.
