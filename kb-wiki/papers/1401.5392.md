# Shear viscosity of pion gas due to $ρππ$ and $σππ$ interactions

> We have evaluated the shear viscosity of pion gas taking into account its scattering with the low mass resonances, $σ$ and $ρ$ during propagation in the medium. The thermal width (or collisional rate) of the pions is calculated from $πσ$ and $πρ$ loop diagrams using effective interactions in the real time formulation of finite temperature field theory. A very small value of shear viscosity by entropy density ratio ($η/s$), close to the KSS bound, is obtained which approximately matches the range of values of $η/s$ used by Niemi et al. \cite{Niemi} in order to fit the RHIC data of elliptic flow.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) of a pion gas, calculated using effective ρππ and σππ interactions, is very small and approaches the KSS bound (1/4π) near the critical temperature, but this result depends on including both the ρ and σ resonances to obtain a smooth, non-divergent temperature dependence of η.

## Method
The thermal width (collisional rate) of pions was calculated from one-loop πσ and πρ self-energy diagrams using real-time finite-temperature field theory with effective Lagrangians for ρππ and σππ interactions. The narrow-width self-energies were then folded with the spectral functions of the σ and ρ resonances to account for their finite widths. The shear viscosity was obtained from the standard Kubo-relation expression involving the thermal width and the Bose-Einstein distribution.

## Key numbers
- η/s approaches the KSS bound of 1/4π near the critical temperature Tc ≈ 0.175 GeV.
- For the πρ loop, the Landau cut ends at m = mρ − mπ = 0.630 GeV; for the πσ loop, it ends at m = mσ − mπ = 0.250 GeV (using mπ = 0.140 GeV, mρ = 0.770 GeV, and σ parameters from Set 1: mσ = 0.390 GeV).
- The mean free path λπ reaches an average value of approximately 25 fm for momenta |k| ≥ 0.400 GeV at T = 0.150 GeV.
- Folding with the σ spectral function decreases the πσ loop contribution to Γπ by 50% compared to the narrow-width approximation at the on-shell point m = mπ.
- The effective pion mass m*π varies by at most 15% for the highest values of momentum and temperature considered.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of shear viscosity in pion gas that used either lowest-order chiral perturbation theory (Lang et al., 2008) or unitarized χPT (Fernandez-Fraile et al., 2009), which gave conflicting temperature dependences. By using an effective Lagrangian approach with explicit σ and ρ resonances, the authors obtain an η that increases with temperature for T > 0.100 GeV, supporting the trend found in unitarized χPT and in kinetic theory approaches (Mitra et al., 2012). The resulting η/s values match the range used by Niemi et al. (2012) to fit RHIC elliptic flow data, providing experimental motivation for including these resonances in dissipation calculations.
