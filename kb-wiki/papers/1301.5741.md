# Lambda(1405) poles obtained from pi0-Sigma0 photoproduction data

> We present a strategy to extract the position of the two $Λ(1405)$ poles from experimental photoproduction data measured recently at different energies in the $γp \to K^+ π^0 Σ^0 $ reaction at Jefferson Lab. By means of a chiral dynamics motivated potential but with free parameters, we solve the Bethe Salpeter equation in the coupled channels $\bar K N$ and $πΣ$ in isospin I=0 and parameterize the amplitude for the photonuclear reaction in terms of a linear combination of the $πΣ\to πΣ$ and $\bar K N \to πΣ$ scattering amplitudes in I=0, with a different linear combination for each energy. Good fits to the data are obtained with some sets of parameters, by means of which one can also predict the cross section for the $K^- p \to π^0 Σ^0 $ reaction. These later results help us decide among the possible solutions. The result is that the different solutions lead to two poles similar to those found in the chiral unitary approach. With the best result we find the two $Λ(1405)$ poles at $1385-68i\mev$and $1419-22i\mev$.

# Lambda(1405) poles obtained from pi0-Sigma0 photoproduction data

## Key claim

The two poles of the Lambda(1405) resonance can be extracted from photoproduction data by fitting a chiral-dynamics-inspired potential with free parameters to the gamma p -> K+ pi0 Sigma0 reaction, without needing a detailed model for the photoproduction mechanism itself. The best solution yields poles at 1385-68i MeV and 1419-22i MeV, but the authors note that multiple local minima exist and that the choice between solutions is guided by consistency with K- p -> pi0 Sigma0 cross section data.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels (Kbar N and pi Sigma in isospin I=0) using a 2x2 potential matrix inspired by lowest-order chiral Lagrangians but with five free parameters (three scaling the coupling coefficients and two scaling the subtraction constants in the loop regularization). The photoproduction amplitude for each of nine photon energies is parameterized as a linear combination of the T(pi Sigma -> pi Sigma) and T(Kbar N -> pi Sigma) scattering amplitudes, with complex coefficients that are free and energy-dependent. The fit is performed iteratively: first fixing the chiral parameters to unity and fitting the photoproduction coefficients, then fixing those coefficients and fitting the chiral parameters, repeating until chi^2/dof ~ 1 is achieved.

## Key numbers

* Best-fit poles (solution 1): 1385-68i MeV and 1419-22i MeV
* Alternative solution 2 poles: 1347-28i MeV and 1409-33i MeV
* chi^2/dof for solution 1: approximately 1.0
* chi^2/dof for solution 2: 0.60 (not statistically more significant than solution 1)
* chi^2/dof for the fixed chiral-unitary model (alpha_i=1): 1.76
* chi^2/dof for the best fit in the experimental paper (Moriya 2013): 2.15
* Fitted parameters for solution 1: alpha_1=1.15, alpha_2=1.17, alpha_3=1.15, alpha_4=1.03, alpha_5=0.88
* Fitted parameters for solution 2: alpha_1=1.88, alpha_2=1.89, alpha_3=1.57, alpha_4=0.93, alpha_5=0.87
* The fit range in M(pi Sigma) is 1350 to 1475 MeV
* The experimental W bins are [W-0.05, W+0.05] GeV for each of the nine energies

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oset (1998) and the two-pole picture of the Lambda(1405) established by Kaiser (1995), Oller (2000), and the "cola" collaboration, where one pole around 1390 MeV (wide, coupling mostly to pi Sigma) and another around 1420 MeV (narrow, coupling mostly to Kbar N) were predicted. The authors use the CLAS photoproduction data from Moriya (2012, 2013) and show that their fit quality (chi^2/dof ~ 1) is substantially better than the Breit-Wigner-based fit in the original experimental paper (chi^2/dof = 2.15). The approach is orthogonal to traditional fits using K- p scattering data; it enables future combined fits to both scattering and production data to determine whether an I=1 state around 1430 MeV exists, as hinted by Oller (2000) and the "cola" work.
