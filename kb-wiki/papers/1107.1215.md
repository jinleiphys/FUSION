# Hidden asymmetry and long range rapidity correlations

> Interpretation of long-range rapidity correlations in terms of the fluctuating rapidity density distribution of the system created in high-energy collisions is proposed. When applied to recent data of the STAR coll., it shows a substantial asymmetric component in the shape of this system in central Au-Au collisions, implying that boost invariance is violated on the event-by-event basis even at central rapidity. This effect may seriously influence the hydrodynamic expansion of the system.

## Key claim

Long-range rapidity correlations in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC reveal a substantial asymmetric component in the event-by-event shape of the created fireball, implying that boost invariance is violated even at central rapidity. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the assumption that statistical fluctuations around the average bin densities are approximately Poissonian, and that the observed asymmetry may also be influenced by hadronization effects, though they estimate these corrections to be small.

## Method

The authors analyze STAR collaboration data on multiplicity fluctuations in two rapidity bins symmetric about midrapidity (0.8 ≤ |y| ≤ 1.0) for Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, selected by centrality based on particle counts in a central bin. They extract the dynamical fluctuations of the fireball density by computing factorial moments of the observed multiplicity distribution, which under the assumption of Poissonian statistical noise directly give the moments of the underlying fluctuating density distribution. The symmetric and asymmetric components of the fireball shape are then quantified via the variances D₊² and D₋², constructed from the factorial moments F₂₀ and F₁₁.

## Key numbers

- For central Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV: D₋ = 5.1 ± 1.2 and D₊ = 15.1 ± 0.4, giving a ratio D₋/D₊ ≈ 1/3, indicating a substantial asymmetric component.
- For the same Au-Au data: D²_ff = 350 ± 17, D²_fb = 202 ± 17, and ⟨n_f⟩ = 96 ± 5.
- For pp collisions at the same energy: D²_ff = 0.572 ± 0.030, D²_fb = 0.027 ± 0.003, and ⟨n_f⟩ ≈ 0.46 ± 0.03 (from PHOBOS data), yielding D₋ = 0.21 ± 0.05 and D₊ = 0.26 ± 0.04.
- The correction to D₋ from resonance decay (ρ, ω, or heavy clusters) does not exceed 8-10% for Au-Au and up to 20% for pp, within quoted errors.

## Context

This work extends the method of factorial moments in rapidity bins, previously developed by Bialas and colleagues (2010) and applied to forward-backward correlations, to uncover the longitudinal shape of the fireball. It contradicts the common assumption of boost invariance in central rapidity for symmetric collisions, and suggests that remnants of the forward and backward moving projectiles, as discussed by Bialas and Bzdak (2007) and Bialas and Czyz (2005), may persist even at RHIC energies. The results enable future studies of higher-order moments to distinguish between two-component and three-component models of particle production, and motivate similar measurements at LHC energies where projectile remnants are expected to be smaller.
