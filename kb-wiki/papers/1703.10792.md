# Collective flow in 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> In this paper, we study and predict flow observables in 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV Pb +Pb collisions, using the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with TRENto and AMPT initial conditions and with different forms of the QGP transport coefficients. With properly chosen and tuned parameter sets, our model calculations can nicely describe various flow observables in 2.76 A TeV Pb +Pb collisions, as well as the measured flow harmonics of all charged hadrons in 5.02 A TeV Pb +Pb collisions. We also predict other flow observables, including $v_n(p_T)$ of identified particles, event-by-event $v_n$ distributions, event-plane correlations, (Normalized) Symmetric Cumulants, non-linear response coefficients and $p_T$-dependent factorization ratios, in 5.02 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. We find many of these observables remain approximately the same values as the ones in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Our theoretical studies and predictions could shed light to the experimental investigations in the near future.

## Key claim

The iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, with properly tuned parameters for TRENTo and AMPT initial conditions and temperature-dependent QGP transport coefficients, can simultaneously describe measured flow harmonics in both 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions, and predicts that many other flow observables at 5.02 A TeV remain approximately the same values as those at 2.76 A TeV.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which couples (2+1)-d viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with the UrQMD hadron cascade, to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV. Two initial condition models were employed: TRENTo (with a reduced thickness function and temperature-dependent specific shear and bulk viscosities from Bayesian calibration) and AMPT (with constant specific shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and zero bulk viscosity). The normalization factor s0 (TRENTo) or K (AMPT) was retuned to fit final charged-hadron multiplicities at 5.02 A TeV, while all other parameters were kept at values previously calibrated for 2.76 A TeV.

## Key numbers

- Final multiplicities increase by approximately 30% when collision energy is raised from 2.76 A TeV to 5.02 A TeV, corresponding to about 10% increase in initial temperature.
- For TRENTo initial conditions, the hydrodynamic starting time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c and switching temperature T_switch = 148 MeV were used, following Bernhard (2016).
- For AMPT initial conditions, the constant specific shear viscosity was η/s = 0.08, with Gaussian smearing factor σ = 0.6 and switching temperature T_switch = 148 MeV.
- The specific shear viscosity η/s(T) from TRENTo calibration has a minimum value close to the KSS bound near T_c, as reported by Bernhard (2016).

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of flow observables at 2.76 A TeV (e.g., by the Duke and OSU group using TRENTo initial conditions, and by Xu (2016) using AMPT initial conditions) to 5.02 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. It provides the first predictions for Symmetric Cumulants and non-linear response coefficients at 5.02 A TeV, which complement the IP-Glasma-based predictions by McDonald (2016) and earlier calculations by Niemi (2015) and Noronha-Hostler (2015). The results enable experimental tests of whether the QGP transport coefficients extracted at 2.76 A TeV remain valid at the higher collision energy.
