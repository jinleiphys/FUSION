# $ϕϕ$ and $J/ψϕ$ mass spectra in decay $B^0_s\to J/ψϕϕ$

> The mass spectra of the $ϕϕ$ and $J/ψϕ$ states in the decay $B^0_s\to J/ψϕϕ$ recently observed by LHCb are calculated in the model which takes into account the $J^P=0^+,0^-,2^+$ intermediate resonances $R_1$, $R_2$ in the $ϕϕ$ channel and the $J^P=1^+$ ones, $X_1$, $X_2$, in the $J/ψϕ$ channel. When obtaining the expressions for the effective amplitudes and mass spectra, the approximate threshold kinematics of the decay is used essentially. The $R_1-R_2$ and $X_1-X_2$ mixings arising due to the common decay modes $ϕϕ$ and $J/ψϕ$, respectively, are also taken into account. The obtained expressions for the mass spectra are applied for extracting the information about masses and coupling constants of the resonances in the $ϕϕ$ and $J/ψϕ$ final states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for the mass spectra of the $\phi\phi$ and $J/\psi\phi$ states in the decay $B^0_s\to J/\psi\phi\phi$ that accounts for intermediate resonances and their mixing, but the model relies on an approximate threshold kinematics that neglects higher partial waves, and the results are extracted from fits to unnormalized LHCb data, yielding only ratios of coupling constants rather than absolute values.

## Method

The author constructs effective decay amplitudes for $B^0_s\to J/\psi\phi\phi$ using the lowest nonvanishing powers of momenta, justified by the near-threshold kinematics where final particle momenta are small relative to their masses. The model includes two resonances $R_1$, $R_2$ in the $\phi\phi$ channel with quantum numbers $J^P=0^+,0^-,2^+$ and two resonances $X_1$, $X_2$ in the $J/\psi\phi$ channel with $J^P=1^+$, and incorporates $R_1-R_2$ and $X_1-X_2$ mixing via nondiagonal polarization operators. The expressions for the $\phi\phi$ and $J/\psi\phi$ mass spectra are then fitted to the LHCb data from 2016 to extract masses and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

* $m_{R_1}=2.089\pm0.004$ GeV for the $0^+$ model, $2.088\pm0.003$ GeV for $0^-$, and $2.081\pm0.001$ GeV for $2^+$ (masses of the first $\phi\phi$ resonance)
* $m_{R_2}=2.191\pm0.006$ GeV for $0^+$, $2.209\pm0.003$ GeV for $0^-$, and $2.211\pm0.001$ GeV for $2^+$ (masses of the second $\phi\phi$ resonance)
* $m_{X_1}=4.146\pm0.004$ GeV for $0^+$, $4.151\pm0.002$ GeV for $0^-$, and $4.151\pm0.001$ GeV for $2^+$ (masses of the first $J/\psi\phi$ resonance)
* $m_{X_2}=4.247\pm0.002$ GeV for $0^+$, $4.248\pm0.001$ GeV for $0^-$, and $4.247\pm0.001$ GeV for $2^+$ (masses of the second $J/\psi\phi$ resonance)
* $\chi^2/n_{\rm dof}=19/21$ for the $0^+$ model, $16/21$ for $0^-$, and $14/21$ for $2^+$ (goodness of fit to LHCb data)
* The invariant mass range for $\phi\phi$ is $2.04\leq m_{\phi\phi}\leq 2.27$ GeV, and for $J/\psi\phi$ is $4.12\leq m_{J/\psi\phi}\leq 4.35$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the decay $B^0_s\to J/\psi\phi\phi$ reported by LHCb (2016), which showed resonance structures in both the $\phi\phi$ and $J/\psi\phi$ mass spectra that could not be described by phase space alone. The model builds on prior studies of $\phi\phi$ resonances from the Particle Data Group and BESIII collaboration, and on the $X(4140)$ and $X(4274)$ resonances observed by LHCb in $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ decays (LHCb, 2016-2017). The approach enables extraction of coupling constants rather than just partial widths, and the formalism for resonance mixing follows earlier work by Achasov and collaborators (1997, 2000).
