# Measuring Nuclear Matter Parameters with NICER and LIGO/Virgo

> The NICER Collaboration recently reported the measurement of the mass and radius of a pulsar PSR J0030+0451. We here use this new measurement to constrain one of the higher-order nuclear matter parameters $K_{\mathrm{sym,0}}$. We further combine the tidal measurement of the binary neutron star merger GW170817 by LIGO/Virgo to derive a joint 1-$σ$ constraint as $K_{\mathrm{sym,0}} = -102^{+71}_{-72}$ MeV. We believe this is the most reliable bound on the parameter to date under the assumption that there is no new physics above the saturation density which impacts neutron star observations.

## Key claim

The authors combine NICER's mass-radius measurement of PSR J0030+0451 with LIGO/Virgo's tidal measurement from GW170817 to derive a joint 1-sigma constraint on the nuclear symmetry energy curvature parameter K_sym,0 = -102^{+71}_{-72} MeV, which they claim is the most reliable bound on this parameter under the assumption that no new physics above saturation density impacts neutron star observations.

## Method

The authors construct two-dimensional and three-dimensional Gaussian correlations between the nuclear parameter K_sym, the neutron star radius at a given mass, and the mass-averaged tidal deformability, using a population of 14 Skyrme-type, 5 relativistic mean field, and 38 phenomenological equations of state. They then marginalize the NICER mass-radius posterior (from MCMC samples of PSR J0030+0451) over these correlations, and combine with the LIGO/Virgo posterior on the tidal deformability from GW170817, to obtain probability distributions for K_sym.

## Key numbers

- Joint NICER (3-spot model) + LIGO/Virgo constraint: K_sym = -102^{+71}_{-72} MeV at 1-sigma confidence
- NICER-only constraint (3-spot model): K_sym = -58^{+79}_{-78} MeV at 1-sigma confidence
- NICER-only constraint (2-spot model): K_sym = -47^{+81}_{-80} MeV at 1-sigma confidence
- LIGO/Virgo-only constraint from prior work: K_sym = -114^{+87}_{-88} MeV at 1-sigma confidence
- The correlation coefficient between K_sym and neutron star radius is approximately 0.5 across various masses, weaker than the correlation found in Alam et al. (2016) using only Skyrme and RMF models

## Context

This work extends prior correlations between nuclear parameters and neutron star observables established by Alam et al. (2016) and Malik et al. (2018), and the authors' own previous work constraining K_sym from GW170817 alone. By including phenomenological equations of state alongside Skyrme and RMF models, the authors obtain more conservative correlations than Alam et al. (2016). The joint NICER+LIGO/Virgo constraint improves upon earlier bounds from Dong et al. (2012) and Xie et al. (2019), which the authors argue relied on assumptions or radius constraints with larger systematic uncertainties. Future work can incorporate radius measurements from quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries as studied by Steiner et al. (2017).
