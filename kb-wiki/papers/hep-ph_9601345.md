# On the Skyrme model prediction for the N-N spin-orbit force

> In the framework of the product ansatz as an approximation for the two-baryon system we review in details the derivation of the isoscalar nucleon-nucleon spin orbit potential coming from the sixth order term of the extended Skyrme model. We show that the sixth order term contributes with a positive sign, as is the case for the Skyrme term, contrary to the claims of Riska and Schwesinger. Those authors considered only one part of the force due to the sixth order term and omitted the second part which turns out to be the dominant one. Our result is independent of the parameters of the model.

## Key claim

The sixth-order term of the extended Skyrme model, corresponding to omega-meson exchange, gives a repulsive (positive) isoscalar nucleon-nucleon spin-orbit force, contrary to the claims of Riska and Schwesinger (1995) who reported an attractive sign; the present result is independent of the model parameters because the positive contribution from the exchange current, omitted in the earlier work, dominates over the negative part.

## Method

The author derived the isoscalar spin-orbit potential from the sixth-order Lagrangian term using the product ansatz for the two-baryon system, with hedgehog solitons and collective coordinates for spin and isospin. The baryon current was decomposed into single-baryon and exchange-current parts, and the kinetic energy was expanded to extract terms proportional to L·S. The exchange-current contribution, which Riska and Schwesinger (1995) had omitted, was computed in full, and the total spin-orbit function Σ₆ₜₒₜ was evaluated numerically for several parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Σ₆(r) (the part from single-baryon currents) is negative for all r, as found by Riska and Schwesinger (1995).
- Σ₆ₑₓ(r) (the exchange-current contribution) is positive and larger in magnitude than Σ₆(r), so the total Σ₆ₜₒₜ(r) is always positive.
- The result was checked numerically for "several sets of parameters" from the literature, both realistic and unrealistic, confirming the sign dominance.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme model analysis of the N-N spin-orbit force, where the Skyrme term alone gives the wrong (repulsive) sign compared to the phenomenological Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1987) and Paris potential (Lacombe et al., 1980). It corrects the earlier calculation by Riska and Schwesinger (1995), who omitted the exchange-current part of the sixth-order term, and also contradicts the result of Kälbermann and Eisenberg (1995), who included a dilaton field but similarly neglected the exchange current. The paper concludes that neither the Skyrme term nor the sixth-order term reproduces the attractive isoscalar spin-orbit force, and suggests that future work should investigate relativistic effects (e.g., Thomas precession) or the two-pion exchange potential within the Skyrme model, possibly including a scalar (sigma) meson or dilaton field.
