# Energy loss of a nonaccelerating quark moving through a strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills vacuum or plasma in strong magnetic field

> Using AdS/CFT correspondence, we find that a massless quark moving at the speed of light $v=1$, in arbitrary direction, through a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills (SYM) vacuum at $T=0$, in the presence of strong magnetic field $\mathcal{B}$, loses its energy at a rate linearly dependent on $\mathcal{B}$, i.e., $\frac{dE}{dt}=-\frac{\sqrtλ}{6π}\mathcal{B}$. We also show that a heavy quark of mass $M\neq 0$ moving at near the speed of light $v^2=v_{*}^2=1-\frac{4π^2 T^2}{\mathcal{B}}\simeq1$, in arbitrary direction, through a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma at finite temperature $T\neq 0$, in the presence of strong magnetic field $\mathcal{B}\gg T^2$, loses its energy at a rate linearly dependent on $\mathcal{B}$, i.e., $\frac{dE}{dt}=-\frac{\sqrtλ}{6π}\mathcal{B}v_{*}^2\simeq-\frac{\sqrtλ}{6π}\mathcal{B}$. Moreover, we argue that, in the strong magnetic field $\mathcal{B}\gg T^2$ (IR) regime, $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM and adjoint QCD theories (when the adjoint QCD theory has four flavors of Weyl fermions and is at its conformal IR fixed point $λ=λ^*$) have the same microscopic degrees of freedom (i.e., gluons and lowest Landau levels of Weyl fermions) even though they have quite different microscopic degrees of freedom in the UV when we consider higher Landau levels. Therefore, in the strong magnetic field $\mathcal{B}\gg T^2$ (IR) regime, the thermodynamic and hydrodynamic properties of $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM and adjoint QCD plasmas, as well as the rates of energy loss of a quark moving through the plasmas, should be the same.

## Key claim

Using AdS/CFT correspondence, a massless quark moving at the speed of light through a strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills vacuum at zero temperature in a strong magnetic field loses energy at a rate linearly dependent on the magnetic field; a heavy quark moving at near the speed of light through the finite-temperature plasma in a strong magnetic field also loses energy at a rate linearly dependent on the magnetic field, but only in the regime where the magnetic field is much larger than the temperature squared.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a heavy quark as a string propagating in a 5-dimensional gravity dual background. The background metric is a BTZ black hole in AdS3 (valid near the horizon in the strong magnetic field regime B >> T^2), and the string dynamics are governed by the Nambu-Goto action. The rates of energy and momentum loss are extracted from the spacetime momentum flow along the string, requiring the string endpoint to be nonaccelerating and imposing a worldsheet horizon condition to determine the critical radial location.

## Key numbers

- For a massless quark moving at v=1 through the N=4 SYM vacuum at T=0 in a strong magnetic field B: dE/dt = -(sqrt(lambda)/6pi) B
- For a heavy quark moving at near the speed of light v^2 = v_*^2 = 1 - (4pi^2 T^2)/B (approximately 1) through N=4 SYM plasma at finite T with B >> T^2: dE/dt = -(sqrt(lambda)/6pi) B v_*^2, which is approximately -(sqrt(lambda)/6pi) B
- The critical velocity for the heavy quark in the plasma is v_*^2 = 1 - (4pi^2 T^2)/B
- The bound on the heavy quark mass is sqrt(lambda) sqrt(B) >> M >> sqrt(lambda) T, corresponding to the geometrical bound u_0 << u << u_h on the gravity side

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark energy loss in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma without magnetic fields by Gubser (2006) and Herzog (2006), and studies of accelerating quarks in vacuum by Mikhailov (2003). It also connects to the analysis of drag force in magnetized plasmas by Chernicoff (2012) and to the specific cases of motion perpendicular and parallel to the magnetic field studied by Li and Yee (2016). The paper argues that in the strong magnetic field regime B >> T^2, N=4 SYM and adjoint QCD with four flavors at its conformal IR fixed point have the same low-energy degrees of freedom (gluons and lowest Landau levels of Weyl fermions), so the energy loss results should apply to both theories. This enables future lattice tests of AdS/CFT in a setup where supersymmetry is completely broken.
