# Neutron star mass formula with nuclear saturation parameters

> We derive the empirical formulas for the neutron star mass and gravitational redshift as a function of the central density and specific combination of the nuclear saturation parameters, which are applicable to the stellar models constructed with the central density up to threefold nuclear saturation density. Combining the both empirical formulas, one also estimates the neutron star radius. In practice, we find that the neutron star mass (radius) can be estimated within $\sim 10\%$ (a few percent) accuracy by comparing the mass and radius evaluated with our empirical formulas to those determined with the specific equation of state. Since our empirical formulas directly connect the neutron star mass and radius to the nuclear saturation parameters, one can discuss the neutron star properties with the specific values of nuclear saturation parameters constrained via nuclear experiments.

## Key claim

The authors derive empirical formulas that connect neutron star mass and gravitational redshift directly to specific combinations of nuclear saturation parameters (η and ξ), applicable for central densities up to three times nuclear saturation density, with the caveat that the mass can be estimated within approximately 10% accuracy and the radius within a few percent accuracy when compared to results from specific equations of state.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using a set of unified equations of state (EOS), including the phenomenological Oyamatsu-Iida (OI-EOS) models, Skyrme-type EOSs (KDE0v, KDE0v1, SLy2, SLy4, SLy9, SKa, SkI3, SkMp), the Shen EOS based on relativistic mean field theory, and the Togashi EOS from variational many-body calculations. They then performed systematic fitting of the neutron star mass and gravitational redshift as functions of the normalized central baryon number density (n_c/n_0) and specific combinations of nuclear saturation parameters (η and ξ), with the coefficients of the fitting formulas themselves expressed as polynomial functions of the normalized central density.

## Key numbers

- The neutron star mass can be estimated within approximately 10% accuracy using the empirical formulas M_ηξ(R_c, η, ξ) and z_ηξ(R_c, η, ξ)
- The neutron star radius for the canonical neutron star can be estimated within approximately 3% accuracy using the same empirical formulas
- The empirical formulas are applicable for central densities up to three times nuclear saturation density (n_c/n_0 = 3)
- The mass of neutron stars with n_c = 3n_0 constructed with the considered EOSs ranges from 0.68 M_⊙ to 1.90 M_⊙, with an average value of 1.34 M_⊙
- The fiducial values for nuclear saturation parameters are given as K_0 = 230 ± 40 MeV and L = 58.9 ± 16 MeV
- The constraint on L from SπRIT is 42 ≤ L ≤ 117 MeV, and from PREX-II is L = 106 ± 37 MeV
- The correlation between K_0 + K_sym and L is given by K_0 + K_sym = -75.86 + 371.8 × (L/100 MeV), yielding -186 ≤ K_sym ≤ 13 MeV when using fiducial K_0 and L values

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Sotani et al. 2014) which found that low-mass neutron star models (central density less than twice saturation density) could be expressed using the combination η = (K_0 L^2)^(1/3). The new formulas incorporate an additional parameter ξ involving higher-order saturation parameters (Q, K_sym, Q_sym) to extend applicability to central densities up to three times saturation density. This enables direct discussion of neutron star properties using nuclear saturation parameters constrained by terrestrial experiments such as SπRIT at RIKEN and PREX-II at Jefferson Lab, and complements astronomical constraints from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as gravitational wave constraints from GW170817.
