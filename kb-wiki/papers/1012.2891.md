# Chiral quark model approach for the study of baryon resonances

> We report the chiral quark model approach for the study of baryon resonances in meson photoproduction and meson-nucleon scattering processes. Focussing on the $蟺N$ and $K N$ scatterings, we show that the cross sections near threshold can be well accounted for by the chiral quark model with only few parameters. The $S$-wave dominance in both $蟺^- p\to 畏n$ and $K^- p\to 危^0蟺^0$ can be well understood in the quark model framework. In particular, the quark model provides a constraint on the relative signs for resonance coupling form factors, which seems to be consistent with results from isobaric models.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model, with only a few parameters, can account for meson-nucleon scattering cross sections near threshold, but this agreement relies on a specific cancellation mechanism between pairs of S-wave resonances (e.g., \(S_{11}(1535)\) and \(S_{11}(1650)\) in \(\pi^- p\to \eta n\)) that is a natural consequence of the non-relativistic constituent quark model symmetry.

## Method

The author uses an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling within a constituent quark model, computing tree-level s-channel and u-channel transition amplitudes for \(\pi^- p\to \eta n\) and \(K^- p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0\). The quark model wavefunctions provide form factors for the interaction vertices, and the resonance masses and widths are taken from the Particle Data Group. The model includes t-channel scalar meson exchange (e.g., \(a_0\) exchange for \(\pi^- p\to \eta n\) and \(\kappa\) exchange for \(K^- p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0\)) with additional parameters constrained by independent processes.

## Key numbers

* Quark model parameters: constituent quark mass \(m_q = 330\) MeV and harmonic oscillator strength \(\alpha = 400\) MeV.
* In \(\pi^- p\to \eta n\), the exclusive cross section from \(S_{11}(1535)\) alone is larger than the total data, but destructive interference with \(S_{11}(1650)\) produces a "second peak" in the total cross section around \(W\sim 1.7\) GeV.
* For \(K^- p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0\), the exclusive cross section from \(\Lambda(1405)S_{01}\) overshoots the data near threshold (center-of-mass energy \(W=1457\sim 1532\) MeV, or beam momentum \(P_K=200\sim 425\) MeV/c), requiring cancellation with \(\Lambda(1670)S_{01}\).
* Differential cross sections for \(K^- p\to \Sigma^0\pi^0\) are shown at beam momenta from \(P_K=475\) to \(775\) MeV/c (corresponding to \(W=1536\) to \(1687\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to photoproduction (Li 1995, Li 1997, Zhao 2002) to meson-nucleon scattering channels, specifically \(\pi N\) and \(K N\). It provides a microscopic framework that constrains the relative signs of resonance coupling form factors, which can be compared with results from isobaric models such as EBAC, SAID, MAID, Bonn-Gatchina, and Juelich (all cited in the NSTAR2009 proceedings). The observed cancellation between S-wave resonances offers a test of the constituent quark model's effective degrees of freedom and can guide future coupled-channel calculations that restore unitarity.
