# Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and a critical mass

> On a bounded, measurable domain of non-negative current-quark mass, realistic models of QCD's gap equation can simultaneously admit two inequivalent dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) solutions and a solution that is unambiguously connected with the realisation of chiral symmetry in the Wigner mode. The Wigner solution and one of the DCSB solutions are destabilised by a current-quark mass and both disappear when that mass exceeds a critical value. This critical value also bounds the domain on which the surviving DCSB solution possesses a chiral expansion. This value can therefore be viewed as an upper bound on the domain within which a perturbative expansion in the current-quark mass around the chiral limit is uniformly valid for physical quantities. For a pseudoscalar meson constituted of equal mass current-quarks, it corresponds to a mass m_{0^-}~0.45GeV. In our discussion we employ properties of the two DCSB solutions of the gap equation that enable a valid definition of <bar-q q> in the presence of a nonzero current-mass. The behaviour of this condensate indicates that the essentially dynamical component of chiral symmetry breaking decreases with increasing current-quark mass.

## Key claim
Realistic models of QCD's gap equation can simultaneously admit two inequivalent dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) solutions and a Wigner-mode solution only on a bounded domain of non-negative current-quark mass; the Wigner solution and one DCSB solution disappear when the current-quark mass exceeds a critical value, which also bounds the domain where the surviving DCSB solution possesses a convergent chiral expansion.

## Method
The authors solved QCD's gap equation (the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed-quark propagator) in two models: a simple four-fermion (NJL-type) model with constant mass functions, and a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow truncation with a momentum-dependent interaction kernel that preserves one-loop perturbative QCD ultraviolet behaviour. They tracked the evolution of the three distinct solutions (two DCSB solutions M_+ and M_-, and the Wigner solution M_W) as a function of current-quark mass, determined the critical mass where M_W and M_- coalesce, and computed the radius of convergence of the chiral expansion for M_+.

## Key numbers
- In the NJL-type model, the critical bare current-quark mass is m^{bm}_{cr} = 0.033 (in units of the cutoff Λ̃).
- In the NJL model, the radius of convergence of the chiral expansion for M_+ is m_rc = 0.034 ± 0.001 (in units of Λ̃), equal to m^{bm}_{cr} within numerical error.
- In the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow model, the critical renormalisation-group-invariant mass depends on the interaction width ω: for ω = 0.3 GeV, m̂_cr = 71 MeV; for ω = 0.4 GeV, m̂_cr = 63 MeV; for ω = 0.5 GeV, m̂_cr = 31 MeV.
- For a pseudoscalar meson composed of equal-mass current-quarks, the critical mass corresponds to a meson mass m_{0^-} ~ 0.45 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of the interplay between explicit and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, building on Dyson-Schwinger equation studies by Roberts and collaborators (1994, 1997) and lattice-QCD simulations by Bowman et al. (2002, 2005). The critical mass result coincides with the earlier estimate by Hatsuda (1990). The findings enable a well-defined, finite quark condensate for nonzero current-quark mass via Eq. (barsigma), and establish an upper bound on the domain where chiral perturbation theory is uniformly valid for physical quantities.
