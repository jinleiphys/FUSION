# Bayesian inference on the isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass from giant resonances in $^{208}$Pb

> From a Bayesian analysis of the electric dipole polarizability, the constrained energy of isovector giant dipole resonance, the peak energy of isocalar giant quadrupole resonance and the constrained energy of isocalar giant monopole resonance in $^{208}$Pb, we extract the isoscalar and isovector effective masses in nuclear matter at saturation density $ρ_0$ as $m_{s,0}^{\ast}/m=0.87_{-0.04}^{+0.04}$ and $m_{v,0}^{\ast}/m = 0.78_{-0.05}^{+0.06}$ at $90\%$ confidence level. The obtained constraints on $m_{s,0}^{\ast}$ and $m_{v,0}^{\ast}$ lead to a positive isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass in asymmetric nuclear matter of isospin asymmetry $δ$ at $ρ_0$ as $m_{n-p}^* / m=(0.20^{+0.15}_{-0.14})δ$. In addition, the symmetry energy at the subsaturation density $ρ^{\ast}=0.05~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ is determined to be $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(ρ^{\ast})=16.7\pm1.3$ MeV at $90\%$ confidence level.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the isoscalar and isovector effective masses in nuclear matter at saturation density from a Bayesian analysis of giant resonance data in 208Pb, finding a positive isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass in asymmetric nuclear matter, with the caveat that the results depend on the Skyrme energy density functional framework and the specific choice of prior ranges for model parameters.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock, constrained Hartree-Fock, and random phase approximation calculations for 208Pb using the Skyrme energy density functional with 10 model parameters expressed in terms of macroscopic quantities. They generated 2476 training parameter sets via maximin Latin cube sampling, trained Gaussian process emulators on the model predictions, and then performed Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling with 10 million steps using the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to obtain posterior distributions constrained by 7 experimental observables.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar effective mass at saturation density: \(m_{s,0}^{\ast}/m = 0.87_{-0.04}^{+0.04}\) at 90% confidence level
- Isovector effective mass at saturation density: \(m_{v,0}^{\ast}/m = 0.78_{-0.05}^{+0.06}\) at 90% confidence level
- Isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass: \(m_{n-p}^* / m = (0.20^{+0.15}_{-0.14})\delta\) at 90% confidence level, where \(\delta\) is the isospin asymmetry
- Symmetry energy at subsaturation density \(\rho^{\ast}=0.05~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\): \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho^{\ast}) = 16.7 \pm 1.3\) MeV at 90% confidence level
- Linear isospin splitting coefficient at saturation density: \(\Delta m_1^{\ast} = 0.20_{-0.14}^{+0.15}\) at 90% confidence level
- Higher-order isospin splitting coefficient \(\Delta m_3^{\ast}\) is less than 0.01 at 90% confidence level

## Context

This work extends the conventional analysis of Zhang (2016) that extracted isospin splitting from giant resonances using only 50 representative Skyrme interactions, by employing Bayesian inference to better quantify uncertainties and evaluate correlations. The results are consistent with constraints from global optical model analyses of nucleon-nucleus scattering data by Xu (2010) and Li (2015), and with the Bayesian analysis of giant dipole resonance in 208Pb by Xu (2020). The extracted symmetry energy at subsaturation density agrees with the result of Zhang (2015) that used electric dipole polarizability data without considering the quasideuteron effect. The work enables more reliable predictions for neutron star cooling and isospin dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors note limitations from the simple quadratic momentum dependence of the Skyrme interaction and the need to simultaneously describe giant resonances in light and heavy nuclei.
