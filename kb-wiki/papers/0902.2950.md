# The sound produced by a fast parton in the quark-gluon plasma is a "crescendo"

> We calculate the total energy deposited into the medium per unit length by fast partons traversing a quark-gluon plasma. The medium excitation due to collisions is taken to be given by the well known expression for the collisional drag force. The radiative energy loss of the parton contributes to the energy deposition because each radiated gluon acts as an additional source of collisional energy loss in the medium. We derive a differential equation which governs how the spectrum of radiated gluons is modified when this energy loss is taken into account. This modified spectrum is then used to calculate the additional energy deposition due to the interactions of radiated gluons with the medium. Numerical results are presented for the medium response for the case of two energetic back-to-back partons created in a hard interaction.

## Key claim
The total energy deposited into the quark-gluon plasma per unit length by a fast parton includes both collisional and radiative contributions, and the radiative contribution grows approximately linearly with path length rather than saturating, meaning the medium excitation becomes a "crescendo" that is dominated by the later stages of parton propagation.

## Method
The authors used the known expression for collisional drag force (Thoma 1991) to compute direct collisional energy deposition. They derived a partial differential equation governing how the spectrum of radiated gluons is modified as those gluons themselves lose energy through collisions in the medium, then solved this equation numerically for a primary quark with parameters α_s = 1/π, T = 350 MeV, and E_p = 50 GeV. The resulting modified gluon spectrum was used to calculate the additional energy deposition from radiated gluons, and the total energy deposition was treated as a local hydrodynamic source term in linearized hydrodynamics to compute the medium response.

## Key numbers
- Collisional energy loss formula: (dE/dξ)_C = (α_s C_2 m_D^2 / 2) ln(2 √(E_p T) / m_D), where C_2 = 4/3 for a quark and 3 for a gluon
- Radiative energy deposition fit: (dE/dt)_R ≈ 0.474 GeV/fm + 0.51 t GeV/fm²
- Parameters used: α_s = 1/π, T = 350 MeV, E_p = 50 GeV, η/s = 0.2, c_s/c = 0.57
- Medium response calculated at t = 8 fm for back-to-back quarks propagating until t = 6 fm
- Low-energy gluon cutoff: ω̅ ≡ 2 T, below which gluons are considered immediately part of the medium
- Maximum gluon energy: ω_max = E_p/2

## Context
This work extends prior studies of jet quenching and Mach cone formation in the quark-gluon plasma (Machcone, Renk 2005, Betz 2008, Neufeld 2008) by including the contribution of radiated gluons to medium excitation. The result is reminiscent of, but less dramatic than, the energy deposition increase found in strongly coupled supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Chesler 2008). The formalism enables calculation of the full hydrodynamic response to fast partons, and the authors note that the growth in energy deposition with path length may explain why measured peak emission angles of secondary hadrons correspond to a small sound velocity c_s/c ≈ 0.3 consistent with lattice QCD near T_c.
