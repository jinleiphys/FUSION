# Equation of state for cold and dense heavy QCD

> A previously derived three-dimensional effective lattice theory describing the thermodynamics of QCD with heavy quarks in the cold and dense region is extended through order $\sim u^5κ^8$ in the combined character and hopping expansion of the original four-dimensional Wilson action. The systematics of the effective theory is investigated to determine its range of validity in parameter space. We demonstrate the severe cut-off effects due to lattice saturation, which afflict any lattice results at finite baryon density independent of the sign problem or the quality of effective theories, and which have to be removed by continuum extrapolation. We then show how the effective theory can be solved analytically by means of a linked cluster expansion, which is completely unaffected by the sign problem, in quantitative agreement with numerical simulations. As an application, we compute the cold nuclear equation of state of heavy QCD. Our continuum extrapolated result is consistent with a polytropic equation of state for non-relativistic fermions.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously derived three-dimensional effective lattice theory for heavy QCD in the cold and dense regime through order ~u⁵κ⁸, and demonstrate that the effective theory can be solved analytically via a linked cluster expansion, yielding a continuum-extrapolated equation of state consistent with a polytropic form for non-relativistic fermions, but only within a limited parameter range where the hopping expansion converges and after removing severe lattice saturation effects.

## Method

The authors start from four-dimensional lattice QCD with Wilson fermions and derive a three-dimensional effective action by integrating over spatial link variables using a combined character expansion in the fundamental gauge coupling u(β) and a hopping expansion in κ, retaining terms through order u⁵κ⁸ in the low-temperature (large Nτ) limit. They then solve this effective theory both by numerical Monte Carlo simulations (using complex Langevin and Metropolis with reweighting) and by an analytic linked cluster expansion, which expands the free energy in powers of the effective two-point coupling v ~ κ². The equation of state is computed for Nf=2 degenerate heavy quarks, and continuum extrapolations are performed using the pure-gauge beta function for the lattice spacing.

## Key numbers

- The effective action is derived through order ~u⁵κ⁸, with effective couplings h₁ = exp[Nτ(aμ + log(2κ))] × exp[6Nτκ²u((1−u^{Nτ−1})/(1−u) + 4u⁴ − 12κ² + 9κ²u + 4κ²u² − 4κ⁴)] and h₂ = (κ²Nτ/Nc)[1 + 2(u−u^{Nτ})/(1−u) + 8u⁵ + 16κ²u⁴].
- The pure gauge couplings λᵢ are suppressed as λᵢ ~ u^{nNτ} with u(β)<1; for β ≤ 6.2 and Nτ ≥ 116, λ₁ ≲ 10⁻¹⁸.
- The baryon saturation density on the lattice is n_B^{sat} = 2N_f baryons per site (i.e., 4 for Nf=2).
- Continuum extrapolation is attempted for lattice spacings a ~ 0.1 fm, corresponding to am_π ~ 10.
- The linked cluster expansion parameter v ~ κ², with the expansion in the effective two-point coupling.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory approach of the same group (densek4, 2014) by adding higher orders in the hopping and character expansions, and introduces the linked cluster expansion as an analytic solution method free of the sign problem. The results are consistent with the phase diagram picture from earlier studies of heavy QCD (kappa, silver, densek4) and from continuum Polyakov loop models (Sasaki, 2015) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Fischer, 2014). The paper demonstrates that lattice saturation effects, which afflict any finite-density lattice calculation regardless of the sign problem, must be removed by continuum extrapolation. The approach enables fully analytic computation of thermodynamic functions for heavy nuclear matter, but the authors note that extending to physical quark masses via the hopping expansion appears impractical due to the slow gain in mass range per additional order.
