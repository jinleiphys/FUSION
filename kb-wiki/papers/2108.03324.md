# Beta equilibrium under neutron star merger conditions

> We calculate the nonzero-temperature correction to the beta equilibrium condition in nuclear matter under neutron star merger conditions, in the temperature range $1\,$MeV$ < T \lesssim 5\,$MeV. We improve on previous work by using a consistent description of nuclear matter based on the IUF and SFHo relativistic mean field models. This includes using relativistic dispersion relations for the nucleons, which we show is essential in these models. We find that the nonzero-temperature correction can be of order $10$ to $20\,$MeV, and plays an important role in the correct calculation of Urca rates, which can be wrong by factors of $10$ or more if it is neglected.

## Key claim

The nonzero-temperature correction to the beta equilibrium condition in neutrino-transparent nuclear matter under neutron star merger conditions can reach 10 to 20 MeV in the temperature range 1 MeV < T ≲ 5 MeV, and neglecting this correction can cause Urca rate calculations to be wrong by factors of 10 or more.

## Method

The authors calculated the direct Urca neutron decay and electron capture rates by performing the full five-dimensional phase space integral using relativistic dispersion relations for nucleons from the IUF and SFHo relativistic mean field models, rather than relying on the Fermi surface approximation. For modified Urca processes, they used the Fermi surface approximation with relativistic dispersion relations but non-relativistic matrix elements based on one-pion exchange. The nonzero-temperature correction Δμ was determined by adjusting the chemical potential difference until the neutron decay and electron capture rates balanced.

## Key numbers

- The nonzero-temperature correction Δμ reaches values of 10 to 20 MeV before neutrino trapping sets in at temperatures around 5 to 10 MeV.
- The temperature range studied is 1 MeV < T ≲ 5 MeV, corresponding to neutrino-transparent conditions in neutron star mergers.
- IUF predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 1.95 M⊙; SFHo predicts 2.06 M⊙.
- For a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, IUF predicts R = 12.7 km and SFHo predicts R = 11.9 km.
- The IUF equation of state has a direct Urca threshold slightly above 4 times nuclear saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³); SFHo has no direct Urca threshold.
- Neutrons at the Fermi surface become relativistic at densities above 2 to 3 n0; protons become relativistic starting at densities between 3 and 6 n0.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Alford (2018) that used a crude non-relativistic model of nucleons with vacuum masses, by employing consistent relativistic mean field models (IUF and SFHo) with fully relativistic dispersion relations. The results enable more accurate calculations of Urca rates and bulk viscosity in neutron star merger simulations, where temperatures of 1-5 MeV are reached and neutrino transparency applies. The paper contrasts with calculations by Fischer (2018), Roberts (2016), and Fu (2008) that focused on higher temperatures (T ≳ 5 MeV) where neutrinos are trapped, and with works by Leinson (2001) and Ding (2016) that used the Fermi surface approximation with a sharp direct Urca threshold.
