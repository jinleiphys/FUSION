# Evidence for higher order QED in e+ e- pair production at RHIC

> A new lowest order QED calculation for RHIC e+ e- pair production has been carried out with a phenomenological treatment of the Coulomb dissociation of the heavy ion nuclei observed in the STAR ZDC triggers. The lowest order QED result for the experimental acceptance is nearly two standard deviations larger than the STAR data. A corresponding higher order QED calculation is consistent with the data.

## Key claim
The STAR data on e+e- pair production accompanied by nuclear dissociation at RHIC are consistent with a higher-order QED calculation (1.67 mb) but are nearly two standard deviations below a new, more accurate lowest-order QED calculation (2.34 mb), providing the first evidence of higher-order QED effects in relativistic heavy-ion reactions.

## Method
The author calculated impact-parameter-dependent probabilities for e+e- pair production by solving the time-dependent Dirac equation in closed form, using a gauge that represents the electromagnetic fields of colliding ions as delta functions in the ultrarelativistic limit. The pair production probability was multiplied by a phenomenological mutual Coulomb dissociation probability (based on photodissociation data and Weizsacker-Williams calculations) and a Glauber-model factor to exclude hadronic interactions, then integrated over impact parameter with the STAR experimental acceptance cuts applied. The lowest-order perturbative result was obtained by taking the perturbative limit of the same amplitude, while the higher-order result retained the full solution.

## Key numbers
- Lowest-order QED cross section for the STAR acceptance: 2.34 mb
- STAR experimental result: 1.65 +/- 0.23 (stat) +/- 0.30 (syst) mb
- Higher-order QED calculation result: 1.67 mb
- Eikonal higher-order result: 1.80 mb
- STAR acceptance cuts: transverse momentum pT > 65 MeV/c, pseudorapidity |eta| < 1.15, at least one track with momentum p < 130 MeV/c, pair mass 140 MeV < Mee < 265 MeV, pair transverse momentum PT < 100 MeV/c, pair rapidity |Y| < 1.15
- Untagged cross section in approximate STAR acceptance from Hencken, Baur, and Trautmann (2006): 0.322 b, reproduced to 1% accuracy

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculations of impact-parameter-dependent e+e- pair production (Baltz 2006) and applies them to the STAR data (STAR Collaboration 2004), which had previously been compared only to lowest-order QED and equivalent-photon calculations. The higher-order QED result is consistent with the Coulomb correction calculations of Ivanov, Schiller, and Serbo (1999) and of Lee and Milstein (2000), and it goes beyond the simpler eikonal formulation of Baltz and McLerran (1998) and Segev and Wells (1998) that did not include the proper regularization. The results suggest that RHIC can indeed be used to test QED, contrary to a recent conclusion by Baltz, Hencken, and Trautmann (2007).
