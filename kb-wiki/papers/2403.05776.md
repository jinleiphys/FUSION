# Muon Capture on $^{6}$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O from $Ab~Initio$ Nuclear Theory

> Muon capture on nuclei is one of the most promising probes of the nuclear electroweak current driving the yet-hypothetical neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay. Both processes involve vector and axial-vector currents at finite momentum transfer, $q\sim 100$ MeV, as well as the induced pseudoscalar and weak-magnetism currents. Comparing measured muon-capture rates with reliable $ab~initio$ nuclear-theory predictions could help us validate these currents. To this end, we compute partial muon-capture rates for $^{6}$Li, $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, feeding the ground and excited states in $^{6}$He, $^{12}$B and $^{16}$N, using $ab~initio$ no-core shell model with two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions. We remove the spurious center-of-mass motion by introducing translationally invariant operators and approximate the effect of hadronic two-body currents by Fermi-gas model. We solve the bound-muon wave function from the Dirac wave equations in the Coulomb field created by a finite nucleus. We find that the computed rates to the low-lying states in the final nuclei are in good agreement with the measured counterparts. We highlight sensitivity of some of the transitions to the sub-leading three-nucleon interaction terms. We also compare summed rates to several tens of final states with the measured total capture rates and note that we slightly underestimate the total rate with this simple approach due to limited range of excitation energies.

## Key claim

The authors compute partial muon-capture rates for ⁶Li, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O using *ab initio* no-core shell model (NCSM) with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, finding good agreement with measured rates for low-lying final states but slightly underestimating total capture rates due to the limited range of excitation energies accessible in their model.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations using three different chiral effective field theory interactions (NN-N³LO+3Nₗₙₗ, NN-N⁴LO+3Nₗₙₗ, and NN-N⁴LO+3N*ₗₙₗ) with SRG-evolved Hamiltonians (λSRG = 1.8–2.0 fm⁻¹). They solved the bound-muon wave function from Dirac equations in the Coulomb field of a finite nucleus, removed spurious center-of-mass motion via translationally invariant operators, and approximated hadronic two-body currents by a Fermi-gas model adjusted to reproduce the effect of exact two-body currents in β decays of ⁶He and ¹⁴O.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ⁶Li: −31.7(1) MeV with NN-N⁴LO+3N*ₗₙₗ (experiment: −31.99 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of ¹²C: −92.5(10) MeV with NN-N⁴LO+3N*ₗₙₗ (experiment: −92.16 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of ¹⁶O: −127(2) MeV with NN-N⁴LO+3N*ₗₙₗ (experiment: −127.62 MeV)
- Spurious center-of-mass motion effect on ground-state-to-ground-state capture rates: about 4% for A=6 system, about 2% for heavier systems
- Two-body current correction δa(0) at q=0: −0.010 for A=6 with NN-N⁴LO+3Nₗₙₗ, −0.043 for A=12–16 with NN-N⁴LO+3Nₗₙₗ

## Context

This work extends previous *ab initio* muon-capture calculations using Green's Function Monte Carlo (Lovato 2019, King 2022) and valence-space IMSRG (Jokiniemi 2023) to additional nuclei and interactions, and builds on the NCSM framework developed by Navratil (2000) and Barrett (2013). The results enable validation of nuclear electroweak currents at momentum transfers q ∼ 100 MeV relevant for neutrinoless double-beta decay, and the authors note that future work should include exact two-body currents at finite momentum transfer rather than the Fermi-gas approximation used here.
