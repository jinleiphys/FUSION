# Occupation number-based energy functional for nuclear masses

> We develop an energy functional with shell-model occupations as the relevant degrees of freedom and compute nuclear masses across the nuclear chart. The functional is based on Hohenberg-Kohn theory with phenomenologically motivated terms. A global fit of the 17-parameter functional to nuclear masses yields a root-mean-square deviation of χ= 1.31 MeV. Nuclear radii are computed within a model that employs the resulting occupation numbers.

## Key claim

The paper develops an occupation number-based energy functional for nuclear masses that achieves a root-mean-square deviation of 1.31 MeV with 17 parameters, but this performance requires coupling the lower-level minimization of occupation numbers with the upper-level fit of coefficients; treating the functional as a mass formula with fixed occupations yields a much larger error of 3.38 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct an energy functional of shell-model occupation numbers for 15 major harmonic oscillator shells, guided by Hohenberg-Kohn theory, scaling arguments for saturation, and analytical results from solvable models. The functional includes volume, kinetic, two-body, deformation, pairing, isospin, and surface terms, plus Coulomb and pairing terms. Ground-state energies are obtained by minimizing the functional with respect to occupation numbers under particle-number constraints using sequential quadratic programming, while the 17 fit parameters are determined by minimizing chi-squared across the nuclear chart using the derivative-free POUNDerS algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation of the 17-parameter functional fit to nuclear masses: chi = 1.31 MeV
- With fixed naive level-filling occupations (mass formula approach), the error is chi = 3.38 MeV, one order of magnitude larger than the Duflo-Zuker mass formula
- The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass functionals by Goriely et al. achieve chi = 0.58 MeV with 14 parameters fit to nuclei with N,Z >= 8
- The UNEDF0 functional has chi = 1.46 MeV when fit to 72 even-even nuclei and chi = 1.61 MeV when applied to 520 even-even nuclei
- The Duflo-Zuker mass formula achieves chi = 0.35 MeV with 28 fit parameters
- The model space consists of 15 major shells, where shell p has dimension d_p = (p+1)(p+2)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Duflo-Zuker mass formulae (Duflo and Zuker, 1995) by making occupation numbers variables that minimize the functional rather than fixed input parameters, and builds on Hohenberg-Kohn density functional theory as applied in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov models such as those by Goriely et al. (2009) and the UNEDF functionals (Kortelainen et al., 2010). The method enables global mass-table computations with modest computational resources and provides a framework for computing nuclear radii from the resulting occupation numbers, opening the possibility for systematic improvement through the correlation-based term selection procedure described in the paper.
