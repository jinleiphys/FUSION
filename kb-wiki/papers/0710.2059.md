# Covariance, Dynamics and Symmetries, and Hadron Form Factors

> We summarise applications of Dyson-Schwinger equations to the theory and phenomenology of hadrons. Some exact results for pseudoscalar mesons are highlighted with details relating to the U_A(1) problem. We describe inferences from the gap equation relating to the radius of convergence for expansions of observables in the current-quark mass. We recapitulate upon studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, providing a comparison of the ln-weighted ratios of Pauli and Dirac form factors for the neutron and proton.

# Covariance, Dynamics and Symmetries, and Hadron Form Factors

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations provide a nonperturbative, symmetry-preserving framework for studying hadron properties, but the approach remains at a "model building and phenomenology" stage for baryons, roughly comparable to where meson studies were more than ten years ago. The chiral expansion of observables in current-quark mass has a finite radius of convergence, meaning chiral perturbation theory is only valid for pseudoscalar meson masses squared below approximately 0.2 GeV².

## Method
The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator (gap equation) and Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson bound states, using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation that preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. For baryons, a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation is truncated by treating two of the three dressed quarks as colour-anti-triplet diquark correlations (scalar 0⁺ and axial-vector 1⁺), with binding achieved through iterated exchange between the bystander quark and diquark-participant quarks. The nucleon electromagnetic current is constructed to preserve the Ward-Takahashi identity, incorporating the electromagnetic properties of the axial-vector diquark.

## Key numbers
- The radius of convergence for chiral expansions corresponds to a pseudoscalar meson mass of m_{ff}^{0-} = 0.45 GeV, meaning chiral perturbation theory is meaningful only for (m_{ff}^{0-})² ≲ 0.2 GeV²
- Scalar diquark mass: m_{[ud]_{0+}} = 0.74 - 0.82 GeV
- Axial-vector diquark masses: m_{(uu)_{1+}} = m_{(ud)_{1+}} = m_{(dd)_{1+}} = 0.95 - 1.02 GeV
- Electromagnetic size of scalar diquark: r_{[ud]_{0+}} ≈ 0.7 fm
- The calculated ratio μ_p G_E^p(Q²)/G_M^p(Q²) passes through zero at Q² ≈ 6.5 GeV²
- The neutron ratio μ_n G_E^n(Q²)/G_M^n(Q²) increases steadily until Q² ≈ 8 GeV²
- Neutron and proton weighted Pauli/Dirac form factor ratios cross at Q² ≈ 8.5 GeV²
- The model predicts η–η′ mixing angles of approximately −15° and π⁰–η angles of approximately 1°
- Strong neutron-proton mass difference: 0.75 (m̂_d − m̂_u)
- Preliminary quark-core contribution to nucleon magnetic moment variation: for proton, −(δμ_p/μ_p)/(δm/m) = 0.016; for neutron, 0.0042

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD developed over the preceding decade, building on the symmetry-preserving truncation scheme of Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996), and the proof that the pion is simultaneously a Goldstone mode and a bound state of massive constituents by Maris (1997). The baryon sector treatment follows the diquark correlation framework initiated by Cahill (1988) and applied to nucleon form factors by Alkofer (2004) and Holl (2005). The analysis of the U_A(1) problem and η′ mass via the axial anomaly term in the Ward-Takahashi identity is detailed in Bhagwat (2007). The results enable direct comparison with lattice QCD simulations (Bowman 2005) and provide constraints for chiral perturbation theory extrapolations, while the nucleon form factor predictions can be tested at high-luminosity facilities with momentum transfers Q² > M_N².
