# Quasi-Local Density Functional Theory and its Application within Extended Thomas-Fermi Approximation

> A generalization of the Density Functional Theory is proposed. The theory developed leads to single-particle equations of motion with a quasi-local mean-field operator, which contains a quasi-particle position-dependent effective mass and a spin-orbit potential. The energy density functional is constructed using the Extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. Within the framework of this approach the ground-state properties of the doubly magic nuclei are considered. The calculations have been performed using the finite-range Gogny D1S force. The results are compared with the exact Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of Density Functional Theory (DFT) that yields single-particle equations with a quasi-local mean-field operator containing a position-dependent effective mass and a spin-orbit potential, while still reproducing the exact ground-state energy of the interacting system. The key hedge is that this quasi-local reduction works because the energy functional depends on the *uncorrelated* kinetic-energy and spin densities from a Slater-determinant density matrix, not the exact correlated densities, which avoids the unproven mapping required in standard DFT for these quantities.

## Method

The authors define an energy functional based on a Slater-determinant density matrix built from an effective Hamiltonian with a finite-range Gogny D1S force, then apply a quasi-local reduction using the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation up to \(\hbar^2\) order to express the exchange energy in terms of local particle, kinetic-energy, and spin densities. The residual correlation energy is added as a phenomenological density-dependent term, and the resulting local Schrodinger equations (with position-dependent effective mass and spin-orbit potential) are solved self-consistently for doubly magic nuclei. The results are compared to exact Hartree-Fock calculations using the same Gogny D1S force.

## Context

This work extends the non-local DFT formalism of Gilbert (1975) by introducing a quasi-local reduction that avoids the difficulties of non-local single-particle potentials, while incorporating the position-dependent effective mass and spin-orbit potential essential for nuclear physics. It builds on the ETF expansion of the density matrix by Soubbotin and Tselyaev (2002) and the Negele-Vautherin (1972) and Campi-Bouyssy (1979) expansions, applying it specifically with the finite-range Gogny D1S force from Berger, Girod, and Gogny (1991). The approach enables practical DFT calculations with finite-range forces that previously required computationally expensive Hartree-Fock solutions in a harmonic oscillator basis (Gogny, 1975), and it provides a framework for future applications to deformed or exotic nuclei.
