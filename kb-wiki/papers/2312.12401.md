# Introducing the concept of the Widom line in the QCD phase diagram

> Critical phenomena emerging from the critical end point of a first-order transition are ubiquitous in nature. Here we bring the concept of a supercritical crossover, the Widom line, initially developed in the context of fluids, into the interacting matter described by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We show that the existence of the putative critical end point between hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma in the temperature versus chemical potential of the QCD phase diagram implies the existence of a Widom line emerging from it in the supercritical region. We survey the thermodynamic anomalies already identified in simplified theoretical models of QCD exhibiting a critical end point, to show that they can be interpreted in terms of a Widom line. Then we suggest possible directions where the Widom line concept could provide new light on the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The existence of a putative critical end point between hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma in the QCD phase diagram implies the existence of a Widom line emerging from it in the supercritical region, and thermodynamic anomalies already identified in simplified theoretical models of QCD can be interpreted in terms of this Widom line.

## Method

The authors survey two simplified theoretical models of QCD that exhibit a critical end point at finite temperature and finite baryon chemical potential: an Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton holographic model and a mapping of QCD onto the three-dimensional (3D) Ising model using the parametric model of Guida and Zinn-Justin (1997) and the linear parametric model of Schofield et al. (1969). They examine the baryon density, quark susceptibility, and entropy density from these models to identify loci of extrema in thermodynamic response functions, which serve as proxies for the Widom line.

## Key numbers

- In the Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton holographic model of DeWolfe et al. (2011), the QCD critical end point was estimated to occur at a critical chemical potential μ_c = 783 MeV and a critical temperature T_c = 143 MeV.
- In the mapping of QCD onto the 3D Ising model by Parotto et al. (2020), the QCD critical end point was estimated to occur at μ_c = 350 MeV and T_c ≈ 143.2 MeV.
- For the extended parametric model of the 3D Ising equation of state, the best fit parameters are a = -0.76201 and b = 0.00804, while epsilon expansion to order ε^3 gives a = -0.72 and b = 0.013 (Guida and Zinn-Justin, 1997).
- The crossover at μ = 0 from hadron gas to quark-gluon plasma found in lattice QCD calculations occurs at T ≈ 156.5 MeV and remains narrow up to μ ≈ 150 MeV (Bazavov et al., 2019; Borsanyi et al., 2020).

## Context

This work extends the concept of the Widom line, originally developed by Stanley and coworkers (2005) for supercooled water and later applied to electronic fluids in doped cuprates by Sordi et al. (2012), into the domain of QCD matter. The paper surveys prior theoretical models of QCD that contain a critical end point, including the holographic model of DeWolfe et al. (2011) and the 3D Ising mapping used by Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000), Nonaka and Asakawa (2005), and Parotto et al. (2020), and reinterprets their thermodynamic anomalies within the Widom line framework. The concept enables new approaches to locate the QCD critical end point by studying the convergence of response function extrema, and links the slope of the first-order transition to the speed of convergence of these extrema through linear scaling theory (Luo et al., 2014).
