# Chiral, parity-doublet, effective-Lagrangian mean-field theories for nuclear and astrophysical phenomenology

> Chiral-parity (parity-doublet) effective Lagrangian models provide a compact and symmetry-consistent framework for describing baryons and their negative-parity partners in terms of linearly-realized chiral symmetry. Unlike the conventional, linear, sigma model; the parity-doublet approach accommodates a chirally-invariant mass term, $m_0$, allowing finite baryon-masses even when the chiral condensate melts. This feature enables a unified treatment of hadronic matter across vacuum, nuclear and dense astrophysical regimes. This compact review summarizes the key structures of parity-doublet Lagrangians; outlines the mean-field formulation for nuclear and stellar matter; and highlights recent phenomenological and lattice constraints on the chirally-invariant mass. Emphasis is placed on mirror versus naïve chiral assignments; the role of vector interactions in achieving nuclear saturation; and the implications of parity doubling for the equation-of-state of dense matter and neutron-star cooling. The review concludes with current theoretical challenges and perspectives for extending these models beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Key claim

Parity-doublet effective Lagrangian models, unlike the conventional linear sigma model, allow baryons to retain finite masses even when the chiral condensate vanishes through a chirally invariant mass term \(m_0\), enabling a unified treatment of hadronic matter across vacuum, nuclear, and dense astrophysical regimes; however, the precise value of \(m_0\) remains model-dependent and typically lies in the range 500–900 MeV, with the mirror chiral assignment being the standard version in mean-field studies.

## Method

The parity-doublet framework combines the nucleon and its negative-parity partner into a single multiplet of the chiral group \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) using two independent Dirac fields that transform under the mirror assignment, where left- and right-handed components are interchanged between the two fields. A chirally invariant mass term \(m_0\) is constructed that survives even when the chiral condensate vanishes, and Yukawa couplings to scalar (\(\sigma\)) and pseudoscalar (\(\vec{\pi}\)) fields are included alongside vector mesons (\(\omega, \rho\)) in analogy with relativistic mean-field models. At the mean-field level, mesonic fields are replaced by their expectation values, and the grand-canonical potential density is minimized through gap equations solved self-consistently at given baryon density to obtain pressure, energy density, and nuclear saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation occurs at \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(B/A \simeq -16~\mathrm{MeV}\) and incompressibility \(K \sim 200\text{--}300~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- The chirally invariant mass \(m_0\) typically lies in the range \(500\text{--}900~\mathrm{MeV}\), with \(m_0 \sim 700~\mathrm{MeV}\) yielding optimal compressibility \(K \simeq 250~\mathrm{MeV}\) and effective mass ratio \(M_N^*/M_N \approx 0.7\) at saturation
- Parity-doublet equations of state support neutron-star masses of \(2.0\!-\!2.2\,M_\odot\), consistent with recent pulsar observations
- Symmetry energy slope parameters \(L \sim 50\!-\!70~\mathrm{MeV}\) are reproduced, consistent with constraints from isobaric analogue states and heavy-ion collisions
- Lattice-QCD results provide numerical evidence for a substantial chirally invariant mass component \(m_0 \sim 700~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Astrophysical constraints from massive pulsars and the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 favour \(m_0\) values in the range \(600\text{--}800~\mathrm{MeV}\)

## Context

This review synthesizes the parity-doublet framework originally proposed by DeTar and Kunihiro (1989), which extends conventional relativistic mean-field models and chiral effective field theory approaches that typically attribute the entirety of the nucleon mass to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The work connects to lattice-QCD simulations by Aarts et al. (2015) and Yamazaki et al. (2019) that observe parity doubling near the chiral crossover, and to systematic parameter fits by Motohiro, Kim and Harada (2015) that established the viable \(m_0\) range. The framework enables unified modelling of hadronic and quark degrees of freedom within a single effective framework, with applications to neutron-star structure, cooling phenomenology, and finite-temperature QCD phenomenology, while open theoretical challenges include beyond-mean-field treatments and improved lattice constraints on \(m_0\).
