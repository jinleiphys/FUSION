# Symmetries of the nucleon-nucleon $S$-matrix and effective field theory expansions

> The s-wave nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering matrix ($S$-matrix) exhibits UV/IR symmetries which are hidden in the effective field theory (EFT) action and scattering amplitudes, and which explain some generic features of the phase shifts. These symmetries offer clarifying interpretations of existing pionless EFT expansions, and suggest starting points for novel expansions. The leading-order (LO) $S$-matrix obtained in the pionless EFT with scattering lengths treated exactly is shown to have a UV/IR symmetry which leaves the sum of s-wave phase shifts invariant. A new scheme, which treats effective range corrections exactly, and which possesses a distinct UV/IR symmetry at LO, is developed up to NLO (next-to-LO) and compared with data.

## Key claim
The s-wave nucleon-nucleon S-matrix exhibits exact UV/IR symmetries that are hidden in the effective field theory action and scattering amplitudes, and these symmetries offer clarifying interpretations of existing pionless EFT expansions while suggesting starting points for novel expansions. The authors develop a new scheme that treats effective range corrections exactly and possesses a distinct UV/IR symmetry at leading order, but this scheme requires a specific correlation between scattering lengths and effective ranges to maintain the symmetry.

## Method
The authors analyze the s-wave nucleon-nucleon S-matrix using the effective range expansion and identify momentum-inversion transformations that leave the S-matrix invariant under specific conditions. They construct a separable, non-local leading-order potential that generates the effective range expansion with all shape parameters vanishing, using the UV/IR symmetry as a constraint to determine the potential's form. The scheme is developed up to next-to-leading order by treating the residual potential in perturbation theory and comparing with experimental phase shift data.

## Key numbers
- Singlet scattering length: a0 = -23.714(13) fm
- Triplet scattering length: a1 = 5.425(1) fm
- Singlet effective range: r0 = 2.73(3) fm
- Triplet effective range: r1 = 1.749(8) fm
- The momentum-inversion fixed point in the scattering length approximation is k^circ = |a|^{-1}, where a is the scattering length
- The momentum-inversion fixed point with effective range corrections is k^circ = sqrt(2)|a r|^{-1/2}

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of UV/IR symmetries in nucleon-nucleon scattering (Beane 2020, Beane 2021) and provides a new perspective on the pionless EFT program developed by van Kolck (1998), Kaplan (1998), and Birse (1998). The UV/IR symmetry framework offers a resolution to issues raised by the Wigner bound (Wigner 1955) regarding causality constraints on scattering parameters, and enables a new EFT expansion where range corrections are treated exactly rather than perturbatively. This approach contrasts with the standard pionless EFT expansion where momentum-dependent operators are treated in perturbation theory, and may provide more accurate leading-order predictions for nucleon-nucleon scattering.
