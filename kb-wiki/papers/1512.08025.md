# Hadron multiplicities and chemical freeze-out conditions in proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> New results of the NA61/SHINE Collaboration at the CERN SPS on mean hadron multiplicities in proton-proton (p+p) interactions are analyzed within the transport models and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) statistical model. The chemical freeze-out parameters in p+p interactions and central Pb+Pb (or Au+Au) collisions are found and compared with each other in the range of the center of mass energy of the nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.2-17.3$ GeV. The canonical ensemble formulation of the HRG model is used to describe mean hadron multiplicities in p+p interactions and the grand canonical ensemble in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions. The chemical freeze-out temperatures in p+p interactions are found to be larger than the corresponding temperatures in central nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperatures in proton-proton (p+p) interactions are found to be larger than the corresponding temperatures in central nucleus-nucleus (Pb+Pb or Au+Au) collisions across the center-of-mass energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 3.2-17.3 GeV, but this conclusion depends on the use of the canonical ensemble for p+p and the grand canonical ensemble for A+A, and the p+p temperature at the lowest SPS energy (6.3 GeV) shows a sudden drop correlated with large uncertainties due to missing antiproton data.

## Method

The authors analyzed mean hadron multiplicities from the NA61/SHINE Collaboration for p+p interactions and from the NA49 Collaboration for central Pb+Pb collisions, along with HADES data for p+p and Au+Au at lower energies, using the hadron resonance gas (HRG) statistical model. The canonical ensemble (CE) formulation was applied to p+p interactions (with fixed baryon number B=2, electric charge Q=2, and net strangeness S=0), while the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) was used for central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions, with strangeness-canonical ensemble (SCE) for the lowest A+A energies at SIS. The fits minimized chi-squared to extract temperature T, baryonic chemical potential mu_B, strangeness saturation factor gamma_S, and system radius R, and the results were compared with predictions from the UrQMD and HSD transport models.

## Key numbers

- Temperature in p+p at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV: T = 170.8 (+8.0/-7.0) MeV
- Temperature in p+p at sqrt(s_NN)=3.2 GeV: T = 141.0 (+15.9/-19.3) MeV
- Temperature in p+p at sqrt(s_NN)=6.3 GeV: T = 102.0 (+51.6/-2.8) MeV (with large error bars due to missing antiproton data)
- System radius in p+p at SPS energies: approximately constant at R ~ 1.5 fm (e.g., R = 1.44 (+0.19/-0.21) fm at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV)
- Strangeness saturation factor gamma_S in p+p at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV: 0.497 (+0.053/-0.045)
- Strangeness saturation factor gamma_S in p+p at sqrt(s_NN)=3.2 GeV: 0.242 (+0.086/-0.071)
- Chemical freeze-out line parametrization for A+A: T(mu_B) = a - b*mu_B^2 - c*mu_B^4 with a = 0.157 GeV, b = 0.087 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.092 GeV^{-3}
- mu_B parametrization for A+A: mu_B = d / (1 + e*sqrt(s_NN)) with d = 1.477 GeV, e = 0.343 GeV^{-1}
- Inclusion of heavy resonances (varying maximal resonance mass cut between 1.7 and 2.4 GeV) can decrease temperature by up to 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous systematic comparisons of hadron production in A+A and p+p collisions (e.g., Becattini (1997) for p+p at sqrt(s_NN) >= 19.4 GeV) down to sqrt(s_NN) = 3.2 GeV, using the latest NA61/SHINE and HADES data. The results provide a baseline for interpreting the energy and system-size scan by NA61/SHINE at SPS and for future experiments at FAIR and NICA. The finding that gamma_S decreases with collision energy in p+p at SPS energies contrasts with earlier studies (Castorina (2007)) that suggested a universal increase of gamma_S with energy, though the error bars remain large.
