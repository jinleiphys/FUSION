# Analytic structure of rho meson propagator at finite temperature

> We analyse the structure of one-loop self-energy graphs for the rho meson in real time formulation of finite temperature field theory. We find the discontinuities of these graphs across the unitary and the Landau cuts. These contributions are identified with different sources of medium modification discussed in the literature. We also calculate numerically the imaginary and the real parts of the self-energies and construct the spectral function of the rho meson, which are compared with an earlier determination. A significant contribution arises from the unitary cut of the pi-omega loop, that was ignored so far in the literature.

# Analytic structure of rho meson propagator at finite temperature

## Key claim

The unitary cut of the pi-omega loop, which was ignored in prior literature, contributes significantly to the rho meson self-energy at finite temperature, alongside the previously considered unitary cut of the pi-pi loop and Landau cuts of various pi-h loops. The two sources of medium modification (pion cloud changes and in-medium scattering) find a unified description in terms of branch cuts of the self-energy loop.

## Method

The authors evaluate one-loop self-energy graphs for the rho meson in the real time formulation of finite temperature field theory, using loops formed by a pion and another hadron (pi, omega, h1, or a1). The vertices are obtained from chiral perturbation theory, with coupling constants determined from observed decay rates. The self-energy tensor is decomposed into transverse and longitudinal components, and the discontinuities across unitary and Landau cuts are derived analytically and evaluated numerically to construct the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Rho meson vacuum mass: 775 MeV; pole position shifts to 763 MeV at T = 150 MeV
- Pi-pi loop vacuum contribution gives Gamma_rho = 153 MeV at M = m_rho
- Coupling constants from decay rates: F_rho = 154 MeV, G_rho = 69 MeV, g1 = 0.87, g2 = 1.0, g3 = 1.1
- Decay rates used: Gamma(rho^0 to e+e-) = 6.9 keV, Gamma(rho to 2pi) = 153 MeV, Gamma(omega to 3pi) = 7.6 MeV, Gamma(h1 to rho pi) approximately 360 MeV, Gamma(a1 to rho pi) approximately 400 MeV
- Pion decay constant F = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of in-medium rho meson propagation previously studied by Rapp and Gale (2000) and others, who constructed the self-energy from the unitary cut of the pi-pi loop and Landau cuts of pi-h loops but omitted the unitary cut of the pi-omega loop. The authors note that their results agree with Rapp and Gale to within about 20 percent in a wide region around the rho meson mass, but show a substantial increase at higher M due to the pi-omega unitary cut contribution. The calculation enables improved predictions for dilepton spectra emitted in heavy ion collisions, which depend on the complete two-point function.
