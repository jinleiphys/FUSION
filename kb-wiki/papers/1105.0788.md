# Spin-dependent pbar-d cross sections at low and intermediate energies

> Antiproton-deuteron (${\bar p}d$) scattering is calculated at beam energies below 300 MeV within the Glauber approach, utilizing the amplitudes of the Jülich $\bar N N$ models. A good agreement is obtained with available experimental data on upolarized differential and integrated $\bar p d$ cross sections. Predictions for polarized total ${\bar p}d$ cross sections are presented, obtained within the single scattering approximation including Coulomb-nuclear interference effects. It is found that the total longitudinal and transversal ${\bar p}d$ cross sections are comparable in absolute value to those for ${\bar p}p$ scattering. The kinetics of polarization buildup is considered.

# Spin-dependent pbar-d cross sections at low and intermediate energies

## Key claim
The spin-dependent total cross sections for antiproton-deuteron scattering are comparable in magnitude to those for antiproton-proton scattering, suggesting that elastic scattering off polarized deuterons could be used for antiproton polarization buildup at beam energies of 100-300 MeV with similar or possibly higher efficiency than scattering off polarized protons. However, the predictions exhibit a fairly strong model dependence due to uncertainties in the spin dependence of the elementary antiproton-nucleon interactions, and only concrete experimental data can confirm the feasibility.

## Method
The authors calculate antiproton-deuteron scattering within the Glauber-Sitenko multiple scattering theory using the Jülich model A and D amplitudes for the elementary antiproton-nucleon interactions. For the unpolarized total and differential cross sections, both single- and double-scattering terms are included, while the spin-dependent total cross sections are evaluated in the single-scattering approximation with Coulomb-nuclear interference effects added. The kinetics of polarization buildup is then analyzed using the spin-filtering formalism, relating the achievable beam polarization to the ratio of polarized to unpolarized total cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Total unpolarized antiproton-deuteron cross section: the single-scattering approximation overestimates the cross section by roughly 15% compared to the full calculation including double scattering; at beam energies as low as 10-25 MeV, double-scattering corrections are not larger than 20-25%.
- Slope parameter of the antiproton-nucleon amplitude: at beam energies of 10-25 MeV, β² is 40-50 (GeV/c)⁻², indicating a strongly forward-peaked amplitude.
- Longitudinal polarization degree P_|| for antiproton-deuteron scattering: maximal values of about 10-15% are predicted above 150 MeV beam energy for target polarization P_T=1.
- Transversal polarization degree P_⊥ for antiproton-deuteron scattering: maximum of around 5% at 150-250 MeV beam energy for target polarization P_T=1.
- For antiproton-deuteron scattering, the largest values of the polarized cross sections σ₁ and σ₂ are expected at beam energies below 10 MeV, where they are dominated by the Coulomb-nuclear interference term.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of antiproton-nucleon interactions (Uzikov and Haidenbauer, 2009) and the spin-filtering mechanism proposed by the PAX collaboration for producing polarized antiprotons at FAIR. The calculations use the Jülich antiproton-nucleon potential models (Hippchen et al., 1991; Mull et al., 1992) and follow the theoretical framework for polarization buildup developed by Milstein et al. (2004) and Nikolaev et al. (2007). The results are compared with earlier predictions based on the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis (Timmermans et al., 1995) and the Paris antiproton-nucleon model (Dmitriev et al., 2005). The predictions are intended to guide upcoming experiments at CERN (AD and AD-1 collaborations) that will measure spin-dependent antiproton-proton and antiproton-deuteron cross sections to discriminate between different antiproton-nucleon interaction models.
