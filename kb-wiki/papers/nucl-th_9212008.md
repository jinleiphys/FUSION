# Kaon-Nucleon Scattering Amplitudes and Z$^*$-Enhancements from Quark Born Diagrams

> We derive closed form kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the ``quark Born diagram" formalism, which describes the scattering as a single interaction (here the OGE spin-spin term) followed by quark line rearrangement. The low energy I=0 and I=1 S-wave KN phase shifts are in reasonably good agreement with experiment given conventional quark model parameters. For $k_{lab}> 0.7$ Gev however the I=1 elastic phase shift is larger than predicted by Gaussian wavefunctions, and we suggest possible reasons for this discrepancy. Equivalent low energy KN potentials for S-wave scattering are also derived. Finally we consider OGE forces in the related channels K$Δ$, K$^*$N and K$^*Δ$, and determine which have attractive interactions and might therefore exhibit strong threshold enhancements or ``Z$^*$-molecule" meson-baryon bound states. We find that the minimum-spin, minimum-isospin channels and two additional K$^*Δ$ channels are most conducive to the formation of bound states. Related interesting topics for future experimental and theoretical studies of KN interactions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) spin-spin interaction alone, treated in a perturbative quark Born diagram formalism with conventional quark model parameters, can reproduce the low-energy I=0 and I=1 S-wave kaon-nucleon phase shifts in reasonably good agreement with experiment, but for kaon laboratory momenta above 0.7 GeV the I=1 elastic phase shift is larger than predicted by Gaussian wavefunctions, suggesting additional mechanisms may be needed.

## Method

The authors calculate kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes using the quark Born diagram formalism, where the scattering is mediated by a single OGE spin-spin interaction between constituents in different incident hadrons, followed by quark line rearrangement to form color-singlet final states. They evaluate the Hamiltonian matrix element between nonrelativistic quark model Gaussian wavefunctions for the kaon and nucleon, sum coherently over all allowed quark line diagrams, and combine the resulting matrix element with relativistic phase space and kinematics to obtain partial wave amplitudes and phase shifts. Equivalent low-energy KN potentials for S-wave scattering are also derived, and the same OGE interaction is applied to the related channels KΔ, K*N, and K*Δ to identify channels with attractive interactions that might support bound states.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian wavefunction parameters α (nucleon) and β (kaon) are typically found to be approximately 0.3–0.4 GeV in hadron phenomenology.
- The color factors for the four independent diagrams are: I_color(D1) = +4/9, I_color(D2) = −2/9, I_color(D3) = −4/9, I_color(D4) = +2/9.
- The discrepancy in the I=1 elastic phase shift appears for klab > 0.7 GeV, where the predicted phase shift from Gaussian wavefunctions is smaller than experiment.
- The diagram weights for KN elastic scattering are: h_fi^KN(I=0) = [0, 1/6, 0, 1/6] and h_fi^KN(I=1) = [1/3, 1/18, 1/3, 1/18].

## Context

This work extends the quark Born diagram formalism previously applied by the same authors to I=2 ππ, I=3/2 Kπ, and I=1 KK scattering (Barnes and Swanson, 1992; Barnes, Swanson, and Weinstein, 1992) to the kaon-nucleon system, which is annihilation-free and lacks one-pion exchange. It contrasts with meson exchange models (which the authors argue are difficult to justify fundamentally due to range issues) and with earlier quark model studies by Bender, Dosch, Pirner, and Kruse (using the generator coordinate method) and Campbell and Robson (using the resonating group method), where the authors disagree about the size of the OGE contribution. The results enable predictions of which channels (minimum-spin, minimum-isospin channels and two additional K*Δ channels) might exhibit Z*-molecule meson-baryon bound states, and provide low-energy KN potentials for use in nuclear structure applications with kaon beams.
