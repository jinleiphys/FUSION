# Non-Efimovian two-neutron halos with an $s$-wave core-neutron resonance

> We consider two-neutron halo nuclei in which the neutron core subsystem displays a resonance close to threshold. Such resonances can be generated in an effective field theory in which the scattering length and effective range are summed to all orders. We show that no three-body parameter is required to make predictions in this case and map out the universal features of such systems. We furthermore study the dependence of these universal features on the core mass. We apply our framework to the two-neutron halo nucleus ${}^{22}$C.

## Key claim

The paper shows that two-neutron halo nuclei with a core-neutron $s$-wave resonance can be described in an effective field theory using only two-body input (scattering length and effective range), with no three-body parameter required for renormalization, provided the core-neutron effective range is large and negative. This universality class is distinct from the Efimov scenario and applies when the core-neutron subsystem has a resonance rather than a large scattering length alone.

## Method

The authors construct a Halo EFT Lagrangian with dimer fields for the neutron-neutron ($nn$) and core-neutron ($cn$) subsystems, summing the scattering length and effective range to all orders in the $cn$ channel. They derive coupled integral equations for the $cnn$ three-body system, solve them numerically by discretizing momentum, and determine the binding energy as a function of cutoff to verify renormalization. The threshold condition for a three-body bound state is mapped in the ($a_{cn}$, $r_{cn}$) plane for both unitary and physical $nn$ scattering lengths, and the dependence on the core mass number $A$ is studied.

## Key numbers

- For $a_{nn} = \infty$ and $A=20$, the three-body state at threshold occurs at $r_{cn}/a_{cn} = 1.929$, implying a resonance pole with $\Gamma/E = 3.64$.
- For $A=20$ near threshold, a fit to the binding energy yields $x_0 = 1.929$, $B_0 = 0.4439 / (m r_{cn}^2)$, and $C = 0.2093 / (m r_{cn}^2)$ in the relation $|B_{cnn}| \ln(B_0/|B_{cnn}|) = C(x_0 - r_{cn}/a_{cn})$.
- For the physical $nn$ scattering length, the threshold condition uses $1/a_{nn} = -9.87$ MeV/($\hbar c$).
- The slope $\alpha$ relating $r_{cn} = \alpha a_{cn}$ at threshold for $a_{nn}=\infty$ is always larger than $1/2$ and depends on $A$.

## Context

This work extends the non-Efimovian three-body universality class previously studied for identical bosons by Petrov (2004) and in EFT by Nishida (2012) and Griesshammer (2023) to the asymmetric core-neutron-neutron system relevant for halo nuclei. It contrasts with standard Halo EFT (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck, 1998; Hammer, 2017) which requires a three-body parameter for systems with large scattering lengths, and with the model of Hongo and Son (2022) for halos without $s$-wave $cn$ resonances. The results enable predictions for $^{22}$C and similar Borromean nuclei where $cn$ resonance parameters are measured, and provide a framework to distinguish this scenario from standard Efimov physics through precise $cn$ data or multiple $cnn$ observables.
