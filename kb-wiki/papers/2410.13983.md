# Theoretical description of proton-deuteron interactions using exact two-body dynamic of femtoscopic correlation method

> Modeling proton-deuteron interactions is particularly challenging. Due the deuteron's large size, the interaction can extend over several femtometers. The degree to which it can be modeled as a two-body problem might also be questioned. One way to study these interactions is through femtoscopic correlation measurements of particle pairs, extracting information using available theoretical models. In this work, we examine two approaches for describing proton-deuteron correlations: the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formalism and full numerical solutions of the Schrodinger equation. Our results show that the differences between these methods are significant. Furthermore, we demonstrate that incorporating higher-order partial waves-particularly p-wave -is the essential for accurately capturing the dynamics of proton-deuteron interactions and the full potential of the strong force.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the commonly used Lednický-Lyuboshits (L-L) asymptotic approximation for describing proton-deuteron femtoscopic correlations produces significant discrepancies compared to full numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation, particularly for small source sizes (R ≈ 2 fm), and that these discrepancies arise from the breakdown of the asymptotic form at short relative distances where the deuteron's large size (≈2.1 fm radius) makes the interaction range extend beyond the region where the approximation is valid.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical approaches for computing proton-deuteron (p-d) femtoscopic correlation functions: (1) the Lednický-Lyuboshits formalism, which uses an asymptotic parameterization of the scattering wave function with input scattering lengths from Black (1999) and zero effective-range approximation, and (2) full numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation implemented in the CorAL package, using square-well potentials fitted to experimental phase shifts from Black (1999) and improved phase shift measurements. Both methods incorporate Coulomb and strong interactions, and the CorAL calculations include contributions from s-wave, p-wave, and d-wave partial waves, with spin-orbit splittings averaged over total angular momentum J weighted by 2J+1.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron radius: approximately 2.1 fm
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2 MeV
- Phase shift measurements constrain potentials up to k* < 92 MeV/c
- The L-L formalism produces a characteristic peak structure at k* ≈ 20 MeV/c for small femtoscopic sources (R = 2 fm), which is absent in the Schrödinger-based calculations
- Source sizes considered: R = 2 fm, and larger sizes up to 3-7 fm (typical for heavy-ion collisions)
- The p-wave contribution significantly reduces the repulsive strength of the s-wave interaction, attributed to possible formation of the ³He bound state in the S=1/2 spin state

## Context

This work extends the comparison of two-body versus three-body approaches for p-d femtoscopy, following studies by Viviani (2023) and the ALICE collaboration (2023) which found that only three-body approaches successfully reproduced experimental correlation functions in pp collisions with small source sizes (≈1.5 fm). The authors show that even within the two-body framework, the L-L asymptotic approximation fails for small sources, and that the discrepancies previously attributed solely to three-body effects may also stem from the asymptotic approximation itself. The results enable more accurate modeling of p-d correlations in heavy-ion collisions (source sizes 3-7 fm) and provide guidance for experimental analyses from STAR and HADES collaborations.
