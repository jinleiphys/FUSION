# Study of fusion dynamics using Skyrme energy density formalism with different surface corrections

> Within the framework of Skyrme energy density formalism, we investigate the role of surface corrections on the fusion of colliding nuclei. For this, the coefficient of surface correction was varied between 1/36 and 4/36, and its impact was studied on about 180 reactions. Our detailed investigations indicate a linear relationship between the fusion barrier heights and strength of the surface corrections. Our analysis of the fusion barriers advocate the strength of surface correction of 1/36.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a surface correction coefficient of λ = 1/36 is the preferred choice for the kinetic energy density gradient term in the Skyrme energy density formalism, based on analysis of fusion barrier heights across 180 reactions; however, the authors acknowledge that no single value of λ explains fusion cross sections consistently, with different reactions favoring different λ values.

## Method

The authors employed the Skyrme energy density formalism (SEDF) with the SIII Skyrme force to calculate ion-ion interaction potentials for 180 reactions involving even-even nuclei with masses between 24 and 246. The kinetic energy density was separated into a Thomas-Fermi volume term and a surface correction term proportional to λ(∇ρ)²/ρ, where λ was varied between 1/36 and 4/36. Fusion barrier heights and positions were extracted from the total interaction potentials and compared to experimental data, then parameterized using linear fits in terms of the masses and charges of the colliding nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The surface correction coefficient λ was varied between 1/36 and 4/36 across 180 reactions
- Different values of λ can explain barrier heights within ±10% of experimental values
- Barrier positions showed deviations up to ±30% due to experimental uncertainties
- The analytical parametrization reproduces actual barrier heights and positions within ±2.5% for all values of λ
- For the reactions studied (²⁴Mg+²⁴Mg, ²⁸Si+²⁸Si, ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁴Ni+⁶⁴Ni, ⁴⁰Ca+⁵⁰Ti, and ⁴⁸Ti+⁵⁸Ni), barrier heights reduce linearly with increasing λ from 0.0 to 4/36

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the Skyrme energy density formalism for fusion dynamics (Wang 2006, Shen 2009) and addresses the long-standing controversy over the correct strength of the gradient term in the kinetic energy density, which was first proposed by von Weizsäcker (1935) and later discussed by Gräf (1980) with values ranging from 1/36 to 9/36. The finding that λ = 1/36 is preferred for barrier heights agrees with structural studies of heavy-ion reactions (Wang 2006, Gupta 1985, Breck 1985), but the inability of any single λ value to explain fusion cross sections across all reactions suggests that additional higher-order correction terms may be needed, opening the question of whether the surface correction parameter can be uniquely determined from fusion data alone.
