# Structure functions in the polarized Drell-Yan processes with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons: I. general formalism

> We discuss general formalism for the structure functions which can be investigated in the polarized Drell-Yan processes with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons. To be specific, the formalism can be applied to the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes. Because of the spin-1 nature, there are new structure functions which cannot be studied in the proton-proton reactions. Imposing Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance, we find that 108 structure functions exist in the Drell-Yan processes. However, the number reduces to 22 after integrating the cross section over the virtual-photon transverse momentum Q_T or after taking the limit Q_T->0. There are 11 new structure functions in addition to the 11 ones in the Drell-Yan processes of spin-1/2 hadrons. The additional structure functions are associated with the tensor structure of the spin-1 hadron, and they could be measured by quadrupole spin asymmetries. For example, the structure functions exist for "intermediate" polarization although their contributions vanish in the longitudinal and transverse polarization reactions. We show a number of spin asymmetries for extracting the polarized structure functions. The proton-deuteron reaction may be realized in the RHIC-Spin project and other future ones, and it could be a new direction of next generation high-energy spin physics.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that in polarized Drell-Yan processes with a spin-1/2 and a spin-1 hadron (such as proton-deuteron), there exist 108 independent structure functions under Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance; however, after integrating over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(Q_T\) or taking the limit \(Q_T \to 0\), the number reduces to 22, of which 11 are new and specific to the spin-1 hadron's tensor structure.

## Method

The authors develop a general formalism for the Drell-Yan reaction \(A\,(\text{spin }1/2) + B\,(\text{spin }1) \to \ell^+ \ell^- + X\) using both a Donohue-Gottlieb type helicity formalism and a Ralston-Soper type Lorentz-index expansion of the hadron tensor. They impose Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance to count independent structure functions, then integrate over the azimuthal angle \(\Phi\) of the virtual-photon transverse momentum to obtain the reduced set of 22 physically accessible structure functions. The cross section is expressed in terms of spin density matrices and \(D\)-matrices, with explicit polarization-angle dependence for the new spin-1 tensor contributions.

## Key numbers

* 108 independent structure functions exist in the full Drell-Yan process with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons.
* After integrating over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(Q_T\) or taking the limit \(Q_T \to 0\), the number reduces to 22 structure functions.
* 11 of these 22 structure functions are new and do not exist in Drell-Yan processes of spin-1/2 hadrons; the other 11 are the same as in spin-1/2 hadron reactions.
* In the spin-1/2 hadron case, there are 48 structure functions, so 60 additional ones appear when one hadron has spin-1.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering formalism of Ralston and Soper (1979) and Donoghue and Gottlieb (1979) for polarized Drell-Yan processes with spin-1/2 hadrons to the case where one hadron has spin-1, specifically for application to proton-deuteron reactions. It builds on earlier studies of spin-1 structure functions in lepton-deuteron scattering, where tensor structure functions \(b_1, b_2, b_3, b_4\) were identified by the MIT group and by Frankfurt and Strikman, and where a sum rule for \(b_1\) was proposed by Close and Kumano. The formalism enables future experimental investigations at the RHIC-Spin project or other facilities that may accelerate polarized deuterons, opening a new direction for studying tensor spin structure of the deuteron and other spin-1 hadrons.
