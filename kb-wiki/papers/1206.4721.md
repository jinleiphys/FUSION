# Pion and kaon elastic form factors in a refined light-front model

> Within the framework of light-front field theory, we reassess the electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon. Comparison with experiment is made for the full range of momentum transfer, q^2<0, including recent data. The light-front model's single regulator mass, m_R, of the \bar qq bound-state vertex function is initially adjusted to reproduce the weak decay constants, f_π and f_K, and both meson's charge radii, r_π and r_K. We study the behavior of these observables under variation of the quark masses and find an optimized parameter set, m_u=m_d, m_s and m_R, for which they are in sensibly better agreement with experiment than in a previous analysis; a feature also observed for the elastic form factors, in particular at small q^2. This model refinement is important in view of an extension to vector and heavy-light mesons.

# Pion and kaon elastic form factors in a refined light-front model

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a single regulator mass, m_R = 1.0 GeV, in a nonsymmetric light-front vertex model can simultaneously reproduce the pion's and kaon's weak decay constants, charge radii, and elastic form factors, provided the constituent quark masses are optimized to m_u = m_d = 220 MeV and m_s = 510 MeV. The abstract's claim of "sensibly better agreement" is qualified by the fact that the kaon charge radius (0.636 fm) and decay constant (126.9 MeV) still deviate significantly from experimental values (0.560 fm and 110.4 MeV, respectively), and the model's validity is restricted to the range 0.8 GeV ≲ m_R ≲ 1.3 GeV.

## Method
The authors employ light-front field theory with a nonsymmetric bound-state vertex function for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of pseudoscalar mesons, using the plus component J^+ of the electromagnetic current in the Drell-Yan frame. The elastic form factor is computed from a triangle diagram in the impulse approximation, where the valence contribution alone survives due to the nonsymmetric vertex choice. The three model parameters (m_u = m_d, m_s, and the regulator mass m_R) are varied simultaneously to fit the weak decay constants f_π and f_K, the charge radii ⟨r_π⟩ and ⟨r_K⟩, and the elastic form factors F_π(q^2) and F_K(q^2) for momentum transfers up to 10 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- f_π = 93.1 MeV (experimental: 92.42 MeV) for m_u = m_d = 220 MeV, m_s = 510 MeV, m_R = 1.0 GeV
- f_K = 126.9 MeV (experimental: 110.4 MeV) for the same parameter set
- ⟨r_π⟩ = 0.679 fm (experimental: 0.672 fm)
- ⟨r_K⟩ = 0.636 fm (experimental: 0.560 fm)
- The regulator mass range yielding acceptable form factors is 0.8 GeV ≲ m_R ≲ 1.3 GeV, with the optimal value m_R = 1.0 GeV
- Previous parameter set (m_u = m_d = 220 MeV, m_s = 419 MeV, m_R = 0.946 GeV) gave ⟨r_π⟩ = 0.672 fm, f_π = 101 MeV, ⟨r_K⟩ = 0.71 fm, f_K = 129 MeV

## Context
This work extends the light-front model of Frederico and collaborators (1994) and the nonsymmetric vertex approach of Pacheco and collaborators (1999, 2002) by optimizing the parameter set to simultaneously describe both pion and kaon observables. The previous analysis (2009) described pion data well but gave poorer agreement for the kaon charge radius (0.71 fm versus 0.560 fm experimental) and decay constant (129 MeV versus 110.4 MeV). The refined parameters enable future extensions to vector mesons, heavy-light mesons, and heavy-to-light transition form factors, as noted by the authors' references to ongoing work (2011, 2012).
