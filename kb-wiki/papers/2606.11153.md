# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of Zemach moments in $A\leq 9$ nuclei

> Modern atomic spectroscopy has reached a level of precision at which nuclear-structure effects can no longer be neglected and must be quantified reliably. In particular, hyperfine splittings depend on the Zemach radius, which encodes the convolution of the nuclear charge and magnetization distributions. The third electric Zemach moment provides a related finite-size measure and enters the elastic two-photon-exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic atoms. Here, we compute Zemach radii and other electromagnetic moments for light nuclei using quantum Monte Carlo techniques within modern \textit{ab initio} nuclear theory. Using Norfolk two- and three-body interactions derived within chiral effective field theory, we assess the model dependence and study the role of two-body currents. For $^6$Li, we obtain a Zemach radius larger than that extracted from atomic measurements, consistent with recent calculations, confirming that the discrepancy is not an artifact of the nuclear model. For $^9$Be, our results agree with experiment; the discrepancy of previous phenomenological evaluations is traced to a model-dependent input for the magnetic radius.

# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of Zemach moments in \(A\leq 9\) nuclei

## Key claim

Using *ab initio* quantum Monte Carlo methods with chiral effective field theory interactions, the authors compute Zemach radii and third electric Zemach moments for light nuclei up to \(^9\)Be, finding that the discrepancy between nuclear theory and atomic spectroscopy for \(^6\)Li persists and is not an artifact of the nuclear model, while for \(^9\)Be the previous phenomenological discrepancy is traced to a model-dependent input for the magnetic radius.

## Method

The authors solve the nuclear many-body problem using variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods, starting from Norfolk two- and three-body interactions derived at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) in chiral effective field theory. They compute charge and magnetization distributions using charge and current operators consistent with the Norfolk interactions, including two-body currents up to N3LO, and evaluate Zemach radii and third electric Zemach moments from the corresponding form factors. Model uncertainties are estimated from the spread of VMC calculations across five different Norfolk interaction classes (Ia, Ia\(^*\), Ib\(^*\), IIa\(^*\), IIb\(^*\)).

## Key numbers

- For \(^6\)Li, GFMC yields \(\langle R_Z \rangle = 3.75(20)\) fm, significantly larger than the experimental extraction of \(2.44(2)\) fm from Sun (2023), confirming a discrepancy of approximately 1.3 fm.
- For \(^9\)Be, GFMC yields \(\langle R_Z \rangle = 3.98(43)\) fm, agreeing within uncertainty with the experimental value \(4.07(5)(2)\) fm from Puchalski (2014) and \(4.048(3)\) fm from Dickopf (2024).
- For \(^3\)He, GFMC yields \(\langle R_Z \rangle = 2.50(3)\) fm, agreeing with the electron scattering analysis of Sick (2014) which gives \(2.528(16)\) fm.
- The magnetic radius \(R_M\) for \(^9\)Be from GFMC is \(3.73(22)\) fm, approximately 40% larger than the phenomenological value \(2.67(6)\) fm from DeJager (1974).
- Two-body currents contribute a small enhancement of approximately 2% to \(\langle R_Z \rangle\) for \(^6\)Li, \(^7\)Li, and \(^9\)Be, and approximately 3% and 5% to the third electric Zemach moment \(\langle R^3_E \rangle_{(2)}\) for \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He, respectively.

## Context

This work extends *ab initio* calculations of Zemach radii beyond the phenomenological Gaussian model of Yerokhin (2008), which had shown large discrepancies with atomic spectroscopy extractions for \(^6\)Li (approximately 50% difference) and \(^9\)Be (approximately 17% difference). The results are consistent with the independent neural-network VMC calculations of Yang (2025), which suggested that inelastic two-photon exchange (polarizability) effects may resolve the \(^6\)Li discrepancy. The calculations provide nuclear-structure input relevant for the QUARTET collaboration's program of high-precision X-ray spectroscopy of light muonic atoms, and enable more reliable extraction of charge radii from Lamb shift measurements by quantifying the elastic two-photon-exchange contribution through the third electric Zemach moment.
