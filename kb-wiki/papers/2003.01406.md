# Does the spin tensor play any role in non-gravitational physics?

> In view of the recent polarization measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, we discuss the possibility of a physical meaning of the spin angular momentum in quantum field theory and relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The spin tensor may have a physical meaning in non-gravitational physics if quantum states (specifically, the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator) are not invariant under pseudo-gauge transformations, meaning that different choices of stress-energy and spin tensor pairs could lead to different physical predictions for measurable quantities such as particle polarization.

## Method

The paper examines pseudo-gauge transformations in relativistic quantum field theory, which allow different pairs of stress-energy and spin tensors while preserving total energy, momentum, and angular momentum. It constructs the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator using both the Belinfante stress-energy tensor (where spin tensor vanishes) and the canonical tensors, showing that these operators are equivalent only under global equilibrium conditions. The analysis then compares the predictions for particle polarization from these two different density operators.

## Key numbers

- No specific numerical results are presented; the paper is a theoretical discussion of formalism and conceptual foundations.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of spin tensor physical significance from earlier theoretical work (Hehl, 1976) and recent debates (Becattini, 2012; Florkowski, 2018) triggered by polarization measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions (STAR Collaboration, 2017). The paper enables future work on whether experimental polarization data can be explained by the standard Belinfante local equilibrium description or whether an extended hydrodynamics including a spin potential is required, as currently being investigated (Florkowski, 2019).
