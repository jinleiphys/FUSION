# Meson2006 Summary: Theory

> This is a summary of theoretical plenary contributions to the biennial hadron physics conference Meson2006, which was the ninth in this series. The topics covered in the meeting include low energy pion-pion and pion-nucleon interactions, photoproduction and hadronic production of light mesons and baryons,in-medium effects, recent developments in charmed mesons, charmonia and B mesons, the status of exotica, and some related topics such as final state interactions. In this contribution we review and summarize the plenary talks presented by theorists at the meeting, and emphasize some of the main points of their presentations. Where appropriate we will add brief comments on some aspects of QCD spectroscopy. Finally, following tradition, we conclude with a new Feynman story.

# Meson2006 Summary: Theory

## Key claim

This is a conference summary of theoretical plenary talks at Meson2006, not a presentation of new original research; the author emphasizes that the connection between in-medium hadron mass shifts and the quark condensate is "very indirect" and that many such shifts can be explained by simple final-state interaction effects rather than fundamental QCD changes.

## Method

The author reviews and summarizes theoretical plenary contributions across several subfields of hadron physics: low-energy pion-pion and pion-nucleon interactions, baryon resonance photoproduction, in-medium effects on hadron masses and widths, heavy-quark spectroscopy via Dyson-Schwinger approaches, and the status of multiquark states including pentaquarks. Brief comments on QCD spectroscopy are added where appropriate, and the author includes a concluding Feynman story following conference tradition.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson pole position: 441(4) - 272(6)i MeV, determined from zeros on the first sheet of the pi-pi scattering amplitude (Leutwyler)
- K-minus-deuteron scattering length: approximately 1-2 fm (complex), with a theoretical accuracy goal of about 10% and an experimental goal of about 5% at DEAR/SIDDHARTA (Gal)
- D15(1675) to eta N branching fraction: varies from 0.7% to 17% depending on the assumed t-dependence of vector exchange (Tiator)
- NN-eta coupling squared over 4 pi: less than an order of magnitude smaller than expected from SU(3) symmetry (Tiator)
- Eta-prime-c mass from Dyson-Schwinger approach: 3.45 GeV (current result, work in progress) (Krassnigg)
- Suggested photon energy for gamma N to phi eta N study at JLAB: approximately 4 GeV (Soyeur)

## Context

This summary extends the Meson conference series tradition (since 1991) of reviewing hadron spectroscopy, covering topics from light mesons below 1 GeV to recent charm spectroscopy discoveries. The author references the HADRON05 Theory Summary (Barnes, 2005) regarding pentaquark issues, and cites Jaffe (1976) and Isgur (1985) on the theoretical problem of multiquark "fall-apart" modes. The work enables future experimental studies of baryon resonance photoproduction at JLAB and improved K-d scattering measurements at DAΦNE, while also highlighting open questions about the nature of the sigma meson and the reliability of in-medium mass shift interpretations.
