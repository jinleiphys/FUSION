# Two Meson Systems with Ginsparg-Wilson Valence Quarks

> Unphysical effects associated with finite lattice spacing and partial quenching may lead to the presence of unphysical terms in chiral extrapolation formulae. These unphysical terms must then be removed during data analysis before physical predictions can be made. In this work, we show that through next-to-leading order, there are no unphysical counterterms in the extrapolation formulae, expressed in lattice-physical parameters, for meson scattering lengths in theories with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks. Our work applies to most sea quark discretization, provided that chiral perturbation theory is a valid approximation. We demonstrate our results with explicit computations and show that, in favorable circumstances, the extrapolation formulae do not depend on the unknown constant C_Mix appearing at lowest order in the mixed action chiral Lagrangian. We show that the I=1 KK scattering length does not depend on C_Mix in contrast to the I=3/2 K-pi scattering length. In addition, we show that these observables combined with f_K / f_pi and the I=2 pi-pi scattering length share only two linearly independent sets of counterterms, providing a means to test the mixed action theory at one lattice spacing. We therefore make a prediction for the I=1 KK scattering length.

## Key claim

The authors show that through next-to-leading order in mixed action chiral perturbation theory, meson scattering lengths expressed in lattice-physical parameters (the pion mass and decay constant measured on the lattice) contain no unphysical counterterms from finite lattice spacing or partial quenching, despite the presence of such terms in the bare Lagrangian. This result holds for any sea quark discretization that recovers QCD in the continuum limit, provided chiral perturbation theory is valid.

## Method

The authors construct the mixed action chiral Lagrangian for Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks with arbitrary sea quark discretizations, working to next-to-leading order in a dual expansion in quark mass and lattice spacing. They compute the I=2 ππ, I=1 KK, and I=3/2 Kπ scattering lengths in SU(6|3) partially quenched chiral perturbation theory, then re-express these observables in terms of lattice-physical meson masses and decay constants. By projecting the NLO Lagrangian onto the valence sector and analyzing the structure of double-trace operators, they demonstrate that all sea quark mass dependence and lattice spacing dependence reduces to renormalizations of the leading-order constants f and B0, which are eliminated when using measured lattice quantities.

## Key numbers

- The I=2 ππ scattering length expressed in lattice-physical parameters is: m_π a_{ππ}^{I=2} = -(m_π^2)/(8π f_π^2) {1 + (m_π^2)/((4π f_π)^2)[3 ln(m_π^2/μ^2) - 1 - l_{ππ}^{I=2}(μ) - (Δ̃_{ju}^4)/(6 m_π^4)]}, where Δ̃_{ju}^2 = m̃_{jj}^2 - m_{uu}^2 is the partial quenching parameter involving sea-sea meson mass shifts.
- The mixed meson mass shift is given by Δ_Mix = 16 C_Mix / f^2, where C_Mix is the unknown constant appearing at lowest order in the mixed action chiral Lagrangian.
- The I=1 KK scattering length does not depend on C_Mix, while the I=3/2 Kπ scattering length does depend on C_Mix.
- The four observables (I=2 ππ, I=1 KK, I=3/2 Kπ scattering lengths and f_K/f_π) share only two linearly independent sets of counterterms at NLO, which are the corresponding physical counterterms of continuum chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the mixed action chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Bar et al. (2002, 2003) for Ginsparg-Wilson valence fermions on Wilson sea fermions, and by Bar et al. (2005) for staggered sea fermions. It builds upon the observation by Chen et al. (2005) that the I=2 ππ scattering length does not depend on C_Mix, and generalizes this result to show that all mesonic scattering lengths expressed in lattice-physical parameters are free of unphysical counterterms at NLO. The results enable lattice practitioners to perform chiral extrapolations of meson scattering lengths using only physical low-energy constants as fit parameters, and provide a means to test the mixed action formalism at a single lattice spacing by comparing the four observables that share only two independent counterterms.
