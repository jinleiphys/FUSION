# Asymptotic Behaviour of Pion--Pion Total Cross-Sections

> We derive a sum rule which shows that the Froissart-Martin bound for the asymptotic behaviour of the $ππ$ total cross sections at high energies, if modulated by the Lukaszuk-Martin coefficient of the leading $\log^2 s$ behaviour, cannot be an optimal bound in QCD. We next compute the total cross sections for $π^+ π^-$, $π^{\pm} π^0$ and $π^0 π^0$ scattering within the framework of the constituent chiral quark model (C$χ$QM) in the limit of a large number of colours $\Nc$ and discuss their asymptotic behaviours. The same $ππ$ cross sections are also discussed within the general framework of Large-\Nc~QCD and we show that it is possible to make an Ansatz for the isospin $I=1$ and $I=0$ spectrum which satisfy the Froissart-Martin bound with coefficients which, contrary to the Lukaszuk-Martin coefficient, are not singular in the chiral limit and have the correct Large-\Nc~counting. We finally propose a simple phenomenological model which matches the low energy behaviours of the $σ_{π^{\pm}π^0}^{\rm total}(s)$ cross section predicted by the C$χ$QM with the high energy behaviour predicted by the Large-$\Nc$ Ansatz. The magnitude of these cross sections at very high energies is of the order of those observed for the $pp$ and $p{\bar p}$ scattering total cross sections.

## Key claim

The Froissart-Martin bound for pion-pion total cross sections, when modulated by the Lukaszuk-Martin coefficient proportional to \(1/m_\pi^2\), cannot be an optimal bound in QCD because a sum rule shows the left-hand side diverges as \(\log m_\pi^2\) in the chiral limit while the right-hand side would diverge as \(1/m_\pi^2\), making the bound impossible to saturate.

## Method

The authors derive a sum rule from fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations (Roy equations) at \(t=0\) in the chiral limit, combining the amplitudes \(F_2\) and \(F_3\) which diagonalize the crossing matrix. They then compute the total cross sections for \(\pi^+\pi^-\), \(\pi^\pm\pi^0\), and \(\pi^0\pi^0\) scattering in the constituent chiral quark model (C\(\chi\)QM) in the large-\(N_c\) limit, and also construct an Ansatz for the isospin \(I=1\) and \(I=0\) spectrum within large-\(N_c\) QCD that satisfies the Froissart-Martin bound with coefficients that are not singular in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

* The Lukaszuk-Martin bound for pion-pion scattering gives \(\sigma^{\text{tot}}_{\pi\pi}(s) \leq (\pi/m_\pi^2) \log^2(s/s_0)\), with the coefficient \(C = \pi/m_\pi^2\).
* In the C\(\chi\)QM with \(g_A = 1\), the asymptotic \(\pi^+\pi^-\) total cross section goes to a constant: \(\sigma^{\text{tot}}_{\pi^+ \pi^-}(s) \sim (N_c n_f / 32\pi) (4 M_Q^2 / f_\pi^4)\), with \(M_Q = 240 \pm 10\) MeV.
* The asymptotic \(\pi^\pm\pi^0\) total cross section falls as \(\sigma^{\text{tot}}_{\pi^\pm \pi^0}(s) \sim (N_c n_f / 16\pi) (4 M_Q^2 / f_\pi^4) (M_Q^2 / s) \log(s / M_Q^2)\).
* The asymptotic \(\pi^0\pi^0\) total cross section goes to a constant: \(\sigma^{\text{tot}}_{\pi^0 \pi^0}(s) \sim (N_c n_f / 32\pi) (4 M_Q^2 / f_\pi^4) [1 - 2 (M_Q^2 / s) \log(s / M_Q^2)]\).
* The sum rule in the C\(\chi\)QM reproduces the chiral low-energy constants: \(2l_1 + 3l_2 = -2l_1 + l_2 = (2/3)(N_c / 16\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the critique of the Froissart-Martin-Lukaszuk bound for pion-pion scattering raised by Greynat and de Rafael (2013), showing that the bound's coefficient is incompatible with QCD's spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The constituent chiral quark model calculations follow the Manohar-Georgi (1984) approach, and the large-\(N_c\) analysis uses the Veneziano (1968) model for Regge trajectories with \(\alpha_1 \simeq 1/(2 M_\rho^2)\) and \(\alpha_0 \simeq 1/2\). The results enable future matching of low-energy chiral perturbation theory predictions with high-energy large-\(N_c\) QCD behavior, and suggest that gluonic rescattering may be needed to produce the \(\log^2 s\) enhancement seen in \(pp\) and \(p\bar{p}\) scattering.
