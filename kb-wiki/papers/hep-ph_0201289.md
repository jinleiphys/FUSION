# The eta-prime -> eta pi pi decay in U(3) chiral perturbation theory

> The dominant decay mode of the eta-prime, eta-prime -> eta pi pi, is investigated within the framework of infrared regularized U(3) chiral perturbation theory up to fourth chiral order and including one-loop corrections. We find reasonable agreement with experimental data and observe convergence of the chiral series.

## Key claim

The dominant decay mode of the eta-prime into eta pi pi is investigated using infrared regularized U(3) chiral perturbation theory up to fourth chiral order including one-loop corrections, and the authors find reasonable agreement with experimental data while observing convergence of the chiral series, though the amplitude is dominated by a momentum-dependent term whose coefficient must be fitted to the decay width due to large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors construct the effective Lagrangian for the pseudoscalar meson nonet up to fourth chiral order in the derivative and quark mass expansion, including the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom. They evaluate tree-level diagrams from both the second-order and fourth-order Lagrangians, then calculate one-loop corrections using infrared regularization to maintain a consistent chiral counting scheme despite the large eta-prime mass. The low-energy constants from the fourth-order Lagrangian are estimated using values from conventional SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, with 1/N_c suppressed OZI-violating corrections neglected, and the combination beta_{0,3,13} is fitted to reproduce the experimental decay width.

## Key numbers

* The measured decay widths are Gamma(eta' -> eta pi+ pi-) = (90 +/- 8) keV and Gamma(eta' -> eta pi0 pi0) = (42 +/- 4) keV, with a ratio r = 2.1 +/- 0.4 consistent with the isospin limit value of 2.
* The tree-level contribution from the second-order Lagrangian alone gives a decay width of 1.9 keV, about 20 times smaller than the experimental value.
* The fitted value of the low-energy constant combination beta_{0,3,13} = (1.1 +/- 0.4) x 10^{-3}, with the central point amplitude A_LO(s_0,t_0,u_0) = -6.4 at the phase-space center s_0 = 0.12 GeV^2, t_0 = u_0 = 0.567 GeV^2.
* The beta_{0,3,13} term dominates the amplitude with a contribution of -7.8 at the central point, compared to 1.3 from the cvtwid{2}{1} term and -1.7 from the beta_{5,18} term.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of eta-prime decays using chiral effective Lagrangians, particularly the tree-level large N_c analysis of Herrera-Siklody (1999) which did not include one-loop corrections, and the infrared regularized U(3) calculations of Borasoy (2001) and Beisert (2001) which only included tadpole loops. The inclusion of unitarity corrections for the first time provides a crucial test of convergence for infrared regularized U(3) chiral perturbation theory, and the results suggest that momentum-dependent terms from the fourth-order Lagrangian dominate the amplitude due to the requirement of Adler zeros, with the series expected to converge albeit slowly.
