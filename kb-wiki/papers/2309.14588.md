# Structure of $^{13}$C in the Cluster Shell Model

> We study the structure of $^{13}$C in the framework of the Cluster Shell Model. A comparison of the available experimental data with our model is made. Some predictions for level ordering and form factors are presented.

# Structure of $^{13}$C in the Cluster Shell Model

## Key claim
The Cluster Shell Model (CSM), treating $^{13}$C as a $^{12}$C core of three alpha particles in a triangular configuration plus an extra neutron, reproduces longitudinal form factors accurately but fails to reproduce transverse form factors, likely because the model assumes only the extra neutron contributes to transverse currents while neglecting the cluster core contribution.

## Method
The authors solve a single-particle Hamiltonian with central, spin-orbit, and Coulomb potentials generated by convoluting a Gaussian density distribution of three alpha particles in an equilateral triangular arrangement with a nucleon-alpha interaction. The single-particle states are classified by the double point group ${\cal D}'_{3h}$ symmetry, and the complete wave function combines intrinsic harmonic oscillator states with rotational wave functions. Longitudinal and transverse form factors are computed from Fourier transforms of the charge density and magnetization current operators, using the CSM wave functions for $^{13}$C.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameter $\beta = 1.71$ fm, obtained from elastic form factors, which determines the ground state rotational band as $\Omega = E_{5/2}$ with ground state $I^P = \frac{1}{2}^-$
- Central potential depth $V_0 = 32$ MeV, Gaussian width parameter $\alpha = 0.0511$ fm$^{-2}$, and spin-orbit strength $V_{0,so} = 17$ MeV fm$^{-2}$ used in solving the single-particle Hamiltonian
- Free neutron magnetic moment $\mu_n = -1.91$ nuclear magnetons used in transverse form factor calculations
- Effective charge of the extra neutron in $^{13}$C taken as zero for longitudinal form factors

## Context
This work extends the Cluster Shell Model, introduced by the same group (CSM1, CSM2, PPNP), from $^{12}$C to the neighboring nucleus $^{13}$C, addressing whether cluster structure persists when adding an extra nucleon. The approach is similar in spirit to the Nilsson model (Nilsson) but with the odd nucleon moving in the deformed field of a cluster core rather than a mean field. The results show that longitudinal form factors of $^{13}$C closely follow those of $^{12}$C, supporting the persistence of cluster structure, while the failure for transverse form factors indicates the need to include core contributions, possibly following the approach of Delorme and Figurau.
