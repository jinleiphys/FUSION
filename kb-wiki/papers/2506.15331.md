# Naive parton picture for kaon color transparency in $A(e,e'K^+)$

> Nuclear transparency in the electronuclear reaction $A(e,e'K^+)$ is investigated in parallel with our previous study of pion transparency in Phys.\ Rev.\ C {\bf 111}, 064608 (2025). Based on an extended Glauber framework that incorporates shadowing from the initial-state two-step process, kaon color transparency (CT) is analyzed to show that the steeper $Q^2$ dependence observed for kaon CT, compared with the pion case, is more naturally described by the naive parton model (NPM) than by the quantum diffusion model (QDM). The inclusion of initial-state shadowing further reduces the transparency and improves the agreement with the experimental data. The $Q^2$ and $A$ dependences of the kaon transparency are presented up to $Q^2=10$~GeV$^2/c^2$, together with the corresponding $Î±(Q^2)$ and the supplementary ratio $T_A/T_C$, for comparison with the Jefferson Lab (JLab) data obtained with the 6-GeV electron beam on $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au nuclei.

## Key claim

The steeper $Q^2$ dependence observed for kaon color transparency, compared with the pion case, is more naturally described by the naive parton model (NPM) than by the quantum diffusion model (QDM), provided that initial-state shadowing from vector-meson fluctuations is also included; the QDM only becomes comparable to the NPM if its mass parameter is reduced to an unphysically low value of $\Delta M^2=0.3$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The authors extended the standard Glauber framework for the reaction $A(e,e'K^+)$ by including two additional mechanisms: (1) initial-state shadowing from the two-step process $\gamma^*\to\phi(1020)\to K^+K^-$, parameterized by a coherence length $l_c$ and a $\phi N$ cross section of 18 mb, and (2) final-state color transparency implemented through a distance-dependent effective kaon-nucleon cross section $\sigma_{KN}^{\rm eff}(z;Q^2,p_K)$. The effective cross section was formulated in a unified expression that reduces to either the NPM (quadratic growth with propagation distance $z$) or the QDM (linear growth with $z$), using the kaon charge radius $R_K=0.58$ fm for the NPM formation length and $\Delta M^2=0.3$ or $0.7$ GeV$^2$ for the QDM formation length. Nuclear transparencies $T_A$ were calculated for $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au over $0.55\le Q^2\le 10$ GeV$^2/c^2$ and compared with Jefferson Lab data from the 6-GeV electron beam.

## Key numbers

- The NPM with $R_K=0.58$ fm (the measured kaon charge radius $\langle R_K^2\rangle = 0.34\pm 0.05$ fm$^2$) and shadowing with $\sigma_{\phi N}=18$ mb reproduces the $Q^2$ dependence of $T_A$ for $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au up to $Q^2=10$ GeV$^2/c^2$.
- The QDM with $\Delta M^2=0.7$ GeV$^2$ (commonly used in pion analyses) fails to reproduce the observed rise of kaon transparency; the QDM only becomes comparable to the NPM when $\Delta M^2$ is reduced to $0.3$ GeV$^2$.
- The parameter $\alpha(Q^2)$, defined by $T_A=A^{\alpha(Q^2)-1}$, rises from about 0.85 to 0.95 over the measured $Q^2$ range, indicating a substantial reduction of attenuation compared with the ordinary absorptive regime ($\alpha\simeq 0.78$ from $K^+$-nucleus scattering).
- The coherence length $l_c$ ranges from 0.65 fm at $Q^2=0.55$ GeV$^2/c^2$ to 0.36 fm at $Q^2=9.5$ GeV$^2/c^2$; the NPM formation length $l_f^{\rm NPM}$ ranges from 2.7 fm to 10.46 fm over the same $Q^2$ range.
- The $K^+N$ total cross section $\sigma_{KN}$ is taken as piecewise-constant values of 17-18 mb from the PDG.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pion transparency in Choi (2025) to the kaon channel, using the same extended Glauber framework with initial-state shadowing. It directly addresses the experimental data of Nuruzzaman (2011) for $A(e,e'K^+)$ on $^{12}$C, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{197}$Au at $Q^2=1.1$, 2.2, and 3.0 GeV$^2$, and the theoretical analysis of Das (2019) who found that a QDM-based treatment with reduced $\Delta M^2$ improved the description. The results enable a comparison of the onset of color transparency in the strangeness sector versus the pion sector, and motivate future dynamical transport calculations (e.g., GiBUU-type) to test whether the qualitative NPM preference remains robust under more microscopic treatment of Fermi motion, multistep collisions, and channel coupling.
