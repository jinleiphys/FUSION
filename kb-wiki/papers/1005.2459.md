# A Bayesian approach to QCD sum rules

> QCD sum rules are analyzed with the help of the Maximum Entropy Method. We develop a new technique based on the Bayesion inference theory, which allows us to directly obtain the spectral function of a given correlator from the results of the operator product expansion given in the deep euclidean 4-momentum region. The most important advantage of this approach is that one does not have to make any a priori assumptions about the functional form of the spectral function, such as the "pole + continuum" ansatz that has been widely used in QCD sum rule studies, but only needs to specify the asymptotic values of the spectral function at high and low energies as an input. As a first test of the applicability of this method, we have analyzed the sum rules of the rho-meson, a case where the sum rules are known to work well. Our results show a clear peak structure in the region of the experimental mass of the rho-meson. We thus demonstrate that the Maximum Entropy Method is successfully applied and that it is an efficient tool in the analysis of QCD sum rules.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) can be successfully applied to QCD sum rules to extract the spectral function directly from operator product expansion (OPE) results, without requiring the traditional "pole + continuum" ansatz, but only as a first test in the rho-meson channel where the sum rules are already known to work well.

## Method

The authors combine the Borel-transformed QCD sum rule dispersion relation with Bayesian inference, using the Shannon-Jaynes entropy as the prior probability and a Gaussian likelihood function for the OPE data. They discretize the spectral function on a grid of 600 points in energy (0 to 6.0 GeV) and use 100 data points for the Borel-transformed correlator in the range M_min = 0.77 GeV to M_max = 1.5 GeV, employing the Bryan algorithm with a modified convergence criterion to find the most probable spectral function. The default model m(omega) is chosen as a smooth function that is zero at low energy and approaches the perturbative value at high energy, with parameters omega_0 = 2.0 GeV and delta = 0.1 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The lower boundary of the Borel mass analysis region is set to M_min = 0.77 GeV, determined by the criterion that the highest-dimensional OPE operators contribute less than 10% of the sum of all OPE terms.
* The upper boundary of the Borel mass analysis region is set to M_max = 1.5 GeV, based on the dominance of the resonance pole over continuum contributions.
* The rho-meson peak position from the MEM analysis varies only within +/- 40 MeV depending on the choice of default model parameters omega_0 and delta.
* The input rho-meson mass used in mock data generation is m_rho = 0.77 GeV, with width parameters Gamma_rho and coupling g_rho_pi_pi = 5.45.
* The default model parameters adopted for the final analysis are omega_0 = 2.0 GeV and delta = 0.1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Maximum Entropy Method, previously applied to Monte-Carlo studies in condensed matter physics (Jarrel) and lattice QCD (Nakahara, Asakawa), to the analysis of QCD sum rules. The approach directly addresses the long-standing criticism of the traditional "pole + continuum" ansatz (Shifman, Reinders) by removing the need for a priori assumptions about the spectral function's functional form, and it enables future studies of channels where the standard ansatz is not appropriate, such as the sigma-meson channel investigated by Kojo.
