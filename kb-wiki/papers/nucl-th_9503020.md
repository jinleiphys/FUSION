# The eta-N S-wave scattering length in one resonance model

> We show that the S-wave $ηN$ scattering length can be extracted in a model independent way within the scope of the multichannel model, but with the restricting assumption that only one resonance is included per partial wave. One has only to use the information on $πN$ elastic S-wave T-matrix at the $η$ production threshold, and near threshold $π^{-} p \rightarrow ηn$ total cross section. The results are independent of the particular parametrization of the elastic T-matrix and of the number of channels. For the described model the unitarity requires that the number of channels must be at least three. These assumptions are more general then the assumptions of the existing single resonance models up to now used for extracting the $ηN$ S-wave scattering length. The agreement of our approach with former estimates is anticipated and confirmed for all cases for which the input data agree with the commonly accepted values.

## Key claim

The S-wave ηN scattering length can be extracted in a model-independent way within any single-resonance-per-partial-wave multichannel model, using only the πN elastic S-wave T-matrix at the η production threshold and the near-threshold π⁻p → ηn total cross section; the result is independent of the particular parametrization of the elastic T-matrix and of the number of channels, provided unitarity requires at least three channels.

## Method

The authors derive a formula (Eq. aetan) that expresses the ηN S-wave scattering length directly in terms of the πN elastic S-wave T-matrix at the ηN threshold (W=1487 MeV, pπ=432 MeV/c) and the near-threshold π⁻p → ηn total cross section divided by the η center-of-mass momentum. They apply this formula using πN elastic T-matrices from four different phase-shift analyses (Cutkosky 1979, Höhler 1983, Karlsruhe 1984, SAID) and the experimental η production cross section from Binnie (1973). They also define a ratio R = Im(r)/Re(r) to test whether a given model is truly a single-resonance model.

## Key numbers

- The near-threshold η production total cross section used as input: σtot(π⁻p → ηn)/pη = (21.2 ± 1.8) μb/MeV, taken from Binnie (1973).
- The lower bound on the imaginary part of the ηN scattering length from the optical theorem alone: Im aηN,0 ≥ (0.24 ± 0.02) fm.
- The ηN threshold energy used: W = 1487 MeV, with πN center-of-mass momentum pπ = 432 MeV/c.
- The resulting uncertainty in the ηN scattering length from input uncertainties is of order 0.03 fm for both real and imaginary parts, with the η production cross section uncertainty dominating and the T-matrix uncertainty contributing approximately 5%.

## Context

This work extends the single-resonance coupled-channel approach of Bhalerao and Liu (1985), who extracted aηN = (0.27 + i 0.22) fm, and provides a unified framework to understand the spread among prior single-resonance results: Wilkin (1993) obtained (0.55 ± 0.20 + i 0.30) fm, Abaev and Nefkens (1994) obtained (0.62 + i 0.30) fm, and Arima et al. (1992) obtained (0.98 + i 0.37) fm using a non-single-resonance quark model. The authors show that all single-resonance results agree with their model-independent formula when consistent input data are used, and they note that their own full multiresonance model (Batinić 1994, 1995) yields a significantly more attractive scattering length, indicating that single-resonance models are insufficient for a realistic description.
