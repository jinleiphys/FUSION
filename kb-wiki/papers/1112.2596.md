# Spontaneous generation of spin-orbit coupling in magnetic dipolar Fermi gases

> The stability of an unpolarized two-component dipolar Fermi gas is studied within mean-field theory. Besides the known instability towards spontaneous magnetization with Fermi sphere deformation, another instability towards spontaneous formation of a spin-orbit coupled phase with a Rashba-like spin texture is found. A phase diagram is presented and consequences are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Beyond the known instability toward spontaneous magnetization, an unpolarized two-component dipolar Fermi gas can become unstable toward spontaneous formation of a spin-orbit coupled phase with a Rashba-like spin texture, but this result is obtained within mean-field and random-phase approximation (RPA) and may be modified by short-range correlations beyond mean-field.

## Method

The authors apply mean-field and RPA theory to a uniform gas of fermionic atoms with magnetic dipole moment, using Landau Fermi liquid theory to expand the angular dependence of the response function in spherical harmonics. They compute the spectrum of collective zero-sound modes in the limit \(q \ll k_F\) and identify instabilities by vanishing or imaginary excitation frequencies, analyzing the stability matrix \(\mathcal{M}_{\Lambda\Lambda'}(0) = \delta_{\Lambda\Lambda'} + F_{\Lambda\Lambda'}\) for the special case of spin-1/2 atoms.

## Key numbers

- The stability condition for the \(\Lambda = 111\Jz\) channel (Rashba-like spin-orbit coupling instability) is \(mk_F c_{dd} < 4\pi\), where \(c_{dd} = d_0^2\) is the dipole-dipole coupling constant.
- The stability condition for the compressibility (collapse) channel is \(mk_F g > -2\pi^2\), independent of the dipole-dipole interaction.
- The exchange term of the dipole-dipole interaction for the \(\Lambda = 1100\) mode gives \(F^{dd(\mathit{Ex})}_{1100,1100} = mk_F c_{dd}/(2\pi)\), which is always positive (stable).
- For the coupled \(\Lambda = 011\Jz\) and \(211\Jz\) channels (spin susceptibility), the exchange contributions are \(F^{\rm dd(\mathit{Ex})}_{011\Jz,211\Jz}/\sqrt{2} = F^{dd(\mathit{Ex})}_{211\Jz,211\Jz} = mk_F c_{dd}/(12\pi)\).
- Numerical convergence of the response function is reached at \(J_{\mathit{max}} \sim 10\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Fermi surface deformation in dipolar Fermi gases (MSP 2008, FF 2009, FF 2010) and of the tensor force in nuclear matter (Jerzak, Pethick) by identifying a new instability toward spontaneous spin-orbit coupling. The predicted Rashba-like spin texture is similar to artificial spin-orbit coupling realized in cold atoms (Lin 2011) and discussed in two-dimensional systems (Sau 2011, Yu 2011, Jiang 2011). The results enable future studies of whether the spin-orbit coupled phase survives beyond mean-field, its behavior in trapped or quasi-two-dimensional systems, and its competition with magnetization and collapse phases.
