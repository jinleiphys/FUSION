# The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method: its application to finite nuclei and neutron-star crusts

> After a brief review of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with Skyrme effective interactions, we show how it can be applied to the description of various nuclear systems, from finite nuclei to neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method, as implemented in the Brussels-Montreal mass models, can be applied consistently to both finite nuclei and neutron-star crusts, but the reliability of extrapolations beyond the neutron drip line depends on additional constraints from neutron-matter equation of state and pairing gaps, not just on the fit to measured masses.

## Method

The authors review the HFB method using a conventional Skyrme effective interaction with a separate density-dependent pairing force, solving the HFB equations for finite nuclei and, for neutron-star crusts, applying the Extended Thomas-Fermi method with Strutinsky integral (ETFSI) corrections. For the inner crust, they solve the HFB equations perturbatively using Bloch boundary conditions and a Fourier expansion of periodic fields to compute the neutron pairing gap in the nearly-free particle approximation. The model parameters are fitted to 2149 measured atomic masses and constrained to reproduce the equation of state of neutron matter from Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) and the $^1S_0$ pairing gaps from Cao et al. (2006).

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of HFB-17 mass model from 2149 measured masses (nuclei with $N$ and $Z \ge 8$ from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation) is 0.581 MeV; mean deviation is -0.019 MeV.
- The last equilibrium nuclide in the outer crust just below the neutron drip threshold ($\rho_{\rm ND} \simeq 4\times 10^{11}$ g.cm$^{-3}$) is $^{120}$Sr.
- In the densest layers of the inner crust (average nucleon density $\rho = 0.070$ fm$^{-3}$), the neutron pairing gap $\widetilde{\Delta}_n$ is 1.48 MeV, compared to 1.79 MeV for uniform neutron matter at the same free-neutron density.
- At $\rho = 0.050$ fm$^{-3}$, $\widetilde{\Delta}_n = 2.45$ MeV versus $\Delta_n = 2.59$ MeV for uniform matter.
- Pairing contribution to energy per particle in uniform neutron matter (using HFB-16) is at most $\simeq 0.5\%$ (without rest mass energy).

## Context

This work extends the Brussels-Montreal series of nuclear mass models (HFB-16, HFB-17) by demonstrating their applicability to neutron-star crusts, building on the classical work of Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (1971) for outer-crust composition. The inner-crust treatment improves upon earlier Wigner-Seitz calculations (Negele and Vautherin, 1973; Baldo et al., 2007) by avoiding spurious neutron shell effects, and the perturbative HFB approach enables study of neutron superfluidity in the crust. The results enable more reliable predictions for highly neutron-rich nuclei and astrophysical environments such as supernova cores and neutron stars.
