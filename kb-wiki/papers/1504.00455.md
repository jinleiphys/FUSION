# Mass dependence of symmetry energy coefficients in Skyrme force

> Based on the semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi approach, we study the mass dependence of the symmetry energy coefficients of finite nuclei for 36 different Skyrme forces. The reference densities of both light and heavy nuclei are obtained. Eight models based on nuclear liquid drop concept and the Skyrme force SkM* suggest the symmetry energy coefficient $a_{\rm sym}=22.90 \pm 0.15 $ MeV at $A=260$, and the corresponding reference density is $ρ_A\simeq 0.1$ fm$^{-3}$ at this mass region. The standard Skyrme energy density functionals give negative values for the coefficient of the $I^4$ term in the binding energy formula, whereas the latest Weizsäcker-Skyrme formula and the experimental data suggest positive values for the coefficient.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei converges to \(a_{\rm sym}=22.90 \pm 0.15\) MeV at mass number \(A=260\) across eight different liquid-drop-type models, but this agreement holds only for heavy nuclei; for light nuclei and for the coefficient of the \(I^4\) term the results are model-dependent and often contradictory.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF2) approximation with 36 different Skyrme energy density functionals to compute the "macroscopic" part of the nuclear binding energy for spherical nuclei, removing Coulomb, Wigner, shell, and pairing effects. They fitted the calculated binding energies per particle as a function of isospin asymmetry \(I=(N-Z)/A\) to extract the mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients \(a_{\rm sym}(A)\) and \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}(A)\) for each force. The mass dependence was then extrapolated to infinite mass using a polynomial fit in powers of \(A^{-1/3}\) to obtain the nuclear-matter limits \(J\), \(a_{\rm ss}\), \(a_{\rm cs}\), and \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}(\infty)\).

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy coefficient for heavy nuclei: \(a_{\rm sym}=22.90 \pm 0.15\) MeV at \(A=260\) from eight liquid-drop-type models.
- ETF2 result with SkM*: \(a_{\rm sym}(260)=22.95\) MeV.
- Reference density at \(A=260\): \(\rho_A \simeq 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Asymptotic symmetry energy \(J\) from SkM*: 30.00 MeV; from SLy4: 31.99 MeV.
- Asymptotic binding energy per particle \(e_0(\infty)\): \(-15.78\) MeV for SkM*; \(-15.99\) MeV for SLy4.
- Coefficient of the \(I^4\) term for SkM* at \(A=160\): \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)} = -2.84\) MeV.
- Coefficient of the \(I^4\) term for SkM* at \(A=208\): \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)} = -2.9\) MeV; at \(A=40\): \(-2.2\) MeV.
- Average deviation between \(J\) from finite nuclei and \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0)\) from nuclear matter: 0.04 MeV.
- Average deviation between \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}(\infty)\) from this work and \(E_{\rm sat,4}\) from Chen (2009): 0.23 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical ETF2 approach previously applied to individual Skyrme forces (e.g., Bencheikh, Brack, Liu) to a systematic survey of 36 forces, establishing a clean connection between the symmetry energy of finite nuclei and that of nuclear matter. The results contradict the positive \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\) coefficient extracted from experimental masses by Jiang (2014) using the double-difference method, and highlight that the standard Skyrme functionals systematically yield negative \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\) values, whereas the WS4 model (Wang 2014) obtains a positive value via surface diffuseness corrections. This discrepancy explains the large (24.4 MeV) difference in predicted mass of \(^{168}\)Sn between the WS4 and HFB17 models (Goriely 2009), and suggests that future Skyrme functionals may need new terms to simultaneously reproduce masses and the \(I^4\) behavior.
