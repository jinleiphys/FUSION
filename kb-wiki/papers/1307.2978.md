# Nonequilibrium time evolution of higher order cumulants of conserved charges and event-by-event analysis

> We investigate the time evolution of higher order cumulants of conserved charges in a volume with the diffusion master equation. Applying the result to the diffusion of non-Gaussian fluctuations in the hadronic stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, we show that the fourth-order cumulant of net-electric charge at LHC energy is suppressed compared with the recently observed second-order cumulant at ALICE, if the higher order cumulants at hadronization are suppressed compared with their values in the hadron phase in equilibrium. The significance of the experimental information on the rapidity window dependence of various cumulants in investigating the history of the dynamical evolution of the hot medium created in relativistic heavy ion collisions is emphasized.

## Key claim

The authors show that the fourth-order cumulant of net-electric charge at LHC energy will be suppressed compared with the already-measured second-order cumulant at ALICE, *if* the higher order cumulants at hadronization are suppressed relative to their equilibrium values in the hadron phase. This prediction is conditional on the initial fluctuations being small and on the parameter \(c\) (related to the hadronization mechanism) not exceeding approximately 1.5.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of conserved charge fluctuations using the diffusion master equation, which describes Brownian particles hopping between discrete cells in rapidity space. They solve this equation analytically for factorial cumulants up to fourth order, then take the continuum limit to obtain cumulants of the net charge within a finite rapidity window \(\Delta\eta\). The results are applied to net-electric charge and net-baryon number diffusion in the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions, assuming a boost-invariant system with local initial fluctuations at hadronization time \(\tau_0\) that evolve until kinetic freezeout \(\tau_{\rm fo}\).

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless parameter controlling the evolution is \(X = \sqrt{D\tau}/\Delta\eta\), where \(D\) is the diffusion constant, \(\tau\) is the proper time, and \(\Delta\eta\) is the rapidity window size.
* The fourth-order cumulant \(\langle Q_{\rm(net)}^4 \rangle_{\rm c}\) is suppressed compared with the second-order cumulant \(\langle Q_{\rm(net)}^2 \rangle_{\rm c}\) for the parameter range \(c \lesssim 1.5\), where \(c = [M_{\rm(tot)}^2]_{\rm c} / [M_{\rm(tot)}]_{\rm c}\).
* In the \(\tau \to \infty\) limit, all cumulants approach the Skellam distribution values: \(\lim_{\tau\to\infty}\langle Q_{\rm(net)}^{2n+1} \rangle_{\rm c} = \Delta\eta [M_{\rm(net)}]_{\rm c}\) and \(\lim_{\tau\to\infty}\langle Q_{\rm(net)}^{2n} \rangle_{\rm c} = \Delta\eta [M_{\rm(tot)}]_{\rm c}\).
* The second-order cumulant \(\langle (\delta N_{\rm Q}^{\rm(net)})^2 \rangle\) measured by ALICE is suppressed compared with the equilibrated hadronic medium value calculated by lattice QCD and the hadron resonance gas model.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic diffusion equation approach of Shuryak (2000) for Gaussian fluctuations to higher order cumulants, using the diffusion master equation framework from Gardiner. It provides the first systematic study of the dynamical evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, motivated by the ALICE measurement of suppressed net-electric charge fluctuations at LHC energy (ALICE Collaboration, 2013) compared with lattice QCD results and the hadron resonance gas model of Karsch (2010). The results enable experimental extraction of the parameter \(c\), which is sensitive to the hadronization mechanism, and predict that the \(\Delta\eta\) dependence of higher order cumulants can reveal the history of dynamical evolution of the hot medium.
