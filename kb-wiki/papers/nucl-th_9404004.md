# A Revisit of SO(6) Dynamical Symmetry in Nuclear Structure

> According to the analysis based on the fermion dynamical symmetry model, nuclei previously regarded as SO(6)-like (e.g. $^{128}$Xe and $^{196}$Pt) are shown to be more akin to the transitional nuclei between SO(7) and SO(6) symmetries.

## Key claim

Nuclei previously regarded as SO(6)-like (e.g. ¹²⁸Xe and ¹⁹⁶Pt) are shown to be more akin to transitional nuclei between SO(7) and SO(6) symmetries, based on analysis using the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM) rather than the interacting boson model (IBM-1) alone.

## Method

The authors analyzed the spectroscopy of ¹²⁸Xe and ¹⁹⁶Pt within the fermion dynamical symmetry model (FDSM), adding a pairing interaction to the pure SO(6) Hamiltonian. They compared the predicted E2 branching ratios and absolute B(E2) values for transitions between excited 0⁺ and 2⁺ states against experimental data, using the ratio R = B(E2; 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) / B(E2; 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₂) as a diagnostic to distinguish SO(6) from SO(7) character.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²⁸Xe, the experimental branching ratio R = 14% for the 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₁ / 0⁺₂ → 2⁺₂ transitions, which is an order of magnitude larger than the 1–3% expected for pure SO(6).
- For ¹⁹⁸Pt, the experimental branching ratio R = 23% for the same transitions, again an order of magnitude larger than the pure SO(6) prediction.
- For a model calculation with Ω=20 and N=6, the ratio R reaches 38% at χ=0.175 in the E2 operator, showing that moderate values (10–30%) can signal SO(7) symmetry or a mixture of SO(6) and SO(7).
- The upper limit of the absolute B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) in ¹⁹⁶Pt shows an order of magnitude hindrance from the allowed SO(6) transitions.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Casten and Cizewski (1990), who argued that ¹⁹⁶Pt should still be considered SO(6)-like based on IBM-1 analysis of branching ratios for highly excited 2⁺ states. It also addresses concerns raised by Leviatan et al. (1988) that SO(5) selection rules, common to both SO(6) and U(5), cannot distinguish between parent symmetries, and by Fewell (1990) who argued for a U(5) interpretation. The paper enables future experimental measurements of absolute B(E2) values for the 0⁺₃ → 2⁺₂ transition in ¹⁹⁶Pt to confirm whether SO(7) or SO(6) character dominates.
