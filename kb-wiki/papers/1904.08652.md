# Medium Modification of Charm Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions due to Pre-equilibrium Dynamics of Partons at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm {NN}}}$= 0.2--5.02 TeV

> We study the production dynamics of charm quarks in the parton cascade model for relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The model is eminently suited for a study of the pre-equilibrium dynamics of charm quarks at modest transverse momenta. The treatment is motivated by QCD parton picture and describes the dynamics of an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision in terms of cascading partons which undergo scattering and multiplication while propagating. We find considerable suppression of charm quarks production in $AA$ collisions compared to those for $pp$ collisions at the same $\sqrt{s_{\text {NN}}}$ scaled by number of collisions. This may be important for an accurate determination of energy loss suffered by charm quarks while traversing the thermalized quark gluon plasma.

# Medium Modification of Charm Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions due to Pre-equilibrium Dynamics of Partons at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2–5.02 TeV

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium dynamics of parton scatterings and fragmentations, including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, produces a substantial suppression of charm quark production in nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions relative to scaled proton-proton (pp) collisions, before the system reaches a thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma phase. This suppression must be accounted for to avoid overestimating the energy loss (dE/dx) attributed to charm quarks traversing the thermalized QGP.

## Method

The authors used the parton cascade model VNI/BMS, a Monte Carlo implementation of the Boltzmann equation, to simulate the time evolution of parton densities in Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 ATeV and 5.02 ATeV for zero impact parameter. The model includes 2→2 scatterings among massless partons using leading-order pQCD matrix elements with a transverse momentum cut-off of 2 GeV, radiation processes (g→gg and q→qg) regularized by a virtuality cut-off mu_0 = 1 GeV, and heavy quark production via gg→Q anti-Q and q anti-q→Q anti-Q processes. The LPM effect was implemented by assigning a formation time tau = omega/k_T^2 to each radiated particle, during which the radiated particle does not interact and is removed if the emitter scatters.

## Key numbers

- Charm production in AA collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV shows suppression of about 10% at low p_T relative to scaled pp results, with the suppression approaching unity at intermediate p_T.
- At LHC energies (2.76 ATeV and 5.02 ATeV), the suppression of total charm production rises to about 40%.
- The transverse momentum cut-off for 2→2 scatterings was fixed at p_T^cut-off = 2 GeV.
- The virtuality cut-off mu_0 was taken as 1 GeV.
- The minimum Q^2 for heavy quark production processes is 4M_Q^2, which for charm quarks is more than 7 GeV^2.
- The formation time for radiated particles is given by tau = omega/k_T^2, where omega is the energy and k_T is the transverse momentum relative to the emitter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charm production in pp collisions using the parton cascade model with LPM effect (Srivastava 2018), which showed that pp collisions at LHC energies may lead to formation of a dense medium. It contrasts with earlier calculations that neglected the LPM effect (Srivastava 2017), where R_AA >= 1 was seen for low p_T, indicating that parton multiplications would otherwise lead to charm production beyond scaled pp results. The study provides the pre-equilibrium phase-space distribution of charm quarks as input for future hydrodynamic calculations including collisional and radiative energy loss, enabling more precise determination of charm quark energy loss in the QGP phase. The authors note that future work will incorporate modern structure functions (currently using GRV HO) and nuclear shadowing corrections.
