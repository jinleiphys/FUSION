# Holographic Pomeron and the Schwinger Mechanism

> We revisit the problem of dipole-dipole scattering via exchanges of soft Pomerons in the context of holographic QCD. We show that a single closed string exchange contribution to the eikonalized dipole-dipole scattering amplitude yields a Regge behavior of the elastic amplitude; the corresponding slope and intercept are different from previous results obtained by a variational analysis of semi-classical surfaces. We provide a physical interpretation of the semi-classical worldsheets driving the Regge behavior for (-t)>0 in terms of worldsheet instantons. The latter describe the Schwinger mechanism for string pair creation by an electric field, where the longitudinal electric field E_L=σ_T tanh(χ/2) at the origin of this non-perturbative mechanism is induced by the relative rapidity χ of the scattering dipoles. Our analysis naturally explains the diffusion in the impact parameter space encoded in the Pomeron exchange; in our picture, it is due to the Unruh temperature of accelerated strings under the electric field. We also argue for the existence of a "micro-fireball" in the middle of the transverse space due to the soft Pomeron exchange, which may be at the origin of the thermal character of multiparticle production in ep/pp collisions. After summing over uncorrelated multi-Pomeron exchanges, we find that the total dipole-dipole cross section obeys the Froissart unitarity bound.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a single closed string exchange between two dipole Wilson loops yields a Regge behavior for the elastic dipole-dipole scattering amplitude, but the slope and intercept differ from previous results obtained by variational analysis of semi-classical surfaces; the authors argue that only the minimal branch cut from the helicoidal surface is physical, while higher winding contributions from the variational method are artifacts.

## Method

The authors compute the connected expectation value of two Wilson loops in holographic QCD by evaluating the string partition function for a closed string exchange on a cylinder topology, using a flat-space approximation valid for the soft Pomeron regime where the string worldsheet stays near the infrared end point of the confining geometry. They implement twisted boundary conditions on the longitudinal coordinates of the string worldsheet to account for the relative rapidity of the two dipoles, perform a mode decomposition, and evaluate the Gaussian integrals using zeta function regularization. The resulting amplitude is interpreted via worldsheet T-duality as describing the Schwinger mechanism for string pair creation by an electric field induced by the relative rapidity.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal electric field induced by the relative rapidity chi is given by E_L = sigma_T tanh(chi/2), where sigma_T is the effective string tension at the infrared end point.
* For Witten's confining geometry, the effective string tension is sigma_T = (2/27 pi) M_KK^2 lambda, where M_KK is the confinement mass scale and lambda is the 't Hooft coupling.
* The string coupling at the infrared end point in Witten's geometry is g_s = lambda^(3/2) / (pi 3^(3/2) N_c).
* The universal Pomeron kernel from closed string exchange in flat space is s^(2 + (alpha'/2) t), where the intercept 2 should be replaced by D_perp/12 for a purely bosonic string, with D_perp the number of massless bosonic worldsheet fluctuations minus two from ghosts.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to dipole-dipole scattering initiated by Rho, Janik, and collaborators (2000-2001) using variational analysis of semi-classical surfaces, and clarifies the relation to the more thorough study of the Pomeron in holographic QCD by Tan (2004). The authors provide a physical interpretation of the semi-classical worldsheets in terms of the Schwinger mechanism, and argue that the Unruh temperature of accelerated strings under the electric field explains the diffusion in impact parameter space encoded in Pomeron exchange. The results enable a connection to the phenomenological dipole parametrization of deep-inelastic scattering cross sections by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998).
