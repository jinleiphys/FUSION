# Kaon induced Lambda(1405) production on a deuteron target at DAFNE

> The K^- induced production of Lambda(1405) in the K^- d to pi Sigma n reaction is investigated having in mind the conditions of the DAFNE facility at Frascati where kaons are obtained from the decay of slow moving phi mesons. We find that the K^- d to Lambda(1405) n process favors the production of Lambda(1405) initiated by the K^- p channel, which gives largest weight to the higher mass Lambda(1405) appearing at 1420 MeV in chiral theories. We find that the fastest kaons from the decay of the phi are well suited to see this resonance, particularly if one selects forward going neutrons in the center of mass, which reduce the contribution of single scattering and make the double scattering dominate where the signal of the resonance appears clearer.

## Key claim

The $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ reaction at DAFNE, using the most energetic kaons from moving $\phi$ decay (around 130 MeV/c), can produce a clean signal of the higher-mass $\Lambda(1405)$ state peaking near 1420 MeV, but only if one selects forward-going neutrons in the center-of-mass frame (angles below about 32 degrees) to suppress the distorting single-scattering contribution and let the double-scattering mechanism dominate.

## Method

The authors calculated the $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ cross section using a chiral unitary model for the $\bar K N \to \bar K N$ and $\bar K N \to \pi \Sigma$ scattering amplitudes, with the $\Lambda(1405)$ described by two poles. They evaluated three diagrams: a single-scattering (impulse approximation) term and two double-scattering terms with kaon exchange, using the deuteron wave function in momentum space. They then imposed angular cuts on the outgoing neutron in the center-of-mass frame to isolate the double-scattering contribution.

## Key numbers

- The higher-mass $\Lambda(1405)$ state appears at 1420 MeV in chiral theories, with the lower-mass state around 1390 MeV and a width of 130 MeV or larger.
- For 130 MeV/c incident kaon momentum, the double scattering becomes dominant for neutron CM angles below 32 degrees, producing a clean peak around 1420 MeV.
- The cross section for forward neutron angles at 130 MeV/c is about 0.1 mb/MeV, stated to be large enough for the DAFNE setup.
- At 100 MeV/c kaon momentum, the single scattering still dominates even at forward angles, making the resonance extraction unreliable; at 80 MeV/c the situation is worse.
- The kaon momentum range from DAFNE's moving $\phi$ decay is 80 MeV/c to 130 MeV/c.
- The estimated beam time needed is about eight months, based on present fluxes and calculated cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of Jido (2009) on the $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ reaction, which showed that double scattering via the $K^{-} p$ channel excites the higher-mass $\Lambda(1405)$ state. It contradicts the claim of Esmaili (2009) that the impulse approximation alone can provide the resonance shape, arguing that the required high-momentum components of the deuteron wave function (around 300 MeV/c) introduce uncertainties of orders of magnitude. The results support the two-pole structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$ predicted by chiral dynamics (Jido 2003) and seen in experiments such as $K^{-} p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ (Prakhov 2004) and $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ with kaons in flight (Braun 1977). The work enables a dedicated experiment at DAFNE using the new capability to produce $\phi$ mesons with small momentum (12 MeV/c), yielding kaons with higher momenta than previously available.
