# Gluonium nature of the sigma/f_0(600) from its coupling to KK

> We extract the K+K- couplings of the isoscalar scalar mesons sigma/f_0(600) and f_0(980) from pi pi to pi pi / K K scatterings and found: |g_{sigma K+K-}|/ |g_{sigma pi+pi-}|= 0.8 and |g_{f_0 K+K-}|/ |g_{f_0 pi+pi-}|= 1.7. These results, together with the tiny "direct" gamma gamma width of the sigma and its large hadronic width, are a strong indication for the gluonium/glueball nature of the sigma-meson, as predicted by QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) + some low-energy theorems (LET), while some other assignements (pi pi molecule, tetraquark state and ordinary qq meson) do not satisfy simultaneously these requirements from the data. These properties suggest that the sigma can be a scalar meson associated to the U(1)V conformal anomaly like is the eta'-meson for the U(1)A anomaly.

## Key claim
The sigma/f0(600) meson is likely a gluonium/glueball state, as indicated by its coupling ratio to KK and pipi being approximately 0.8, combined with its tiny direct gamma-gamma width and large hadronic width, which together rule out alternative interpretations as a pipi molecule, tetraquark, or ordinary qq meson.

## Method
The authors extracted the K+K- couplings of the sigma/f0(600) and f0(980) from pipi to pipi/KK scattering data using two complementary approaches: an analytic K-matrix model that resums unitarized Born amplitudes with resonance poles, and a coupled-channel S-matrix model constrained by forward dispersion relations, Roy equations, and GKPY equations. The fits used experimental phase shifts and inelasticities from Cohen (1980), Etkin (1982), and Hyams (1973), with theoretical constraints from dispersion relations applied to improve the determination of the S0 partial wave amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Ratio |g_sigma K+K-| / |g_sigma pi+pi-| = 0.8 (average from multiple analyses)
- Ratio |g_f0 K+K-| / |g_f0 pi+pi-| = 1.7 (average from multiple analyses)
- Direct gamma-gamma width of sigma: Gamma_sigma->gamma gamma^dir = (0.13 +/- 0.05) keV (model-dependent)
- Total gamma-gamma width of sigma: Gamma_sigma->gamma gamma^tot = (3.9 +/- 0.6) keV (model-independent)
- On-shell mass of sigma: M_sigma^os = 0.92 GeV
- On-shell width of sigma to pipi: Gamma_sigma->pi+pi-^os = 1.02 GeV
- On-shell direct gamma-gamma width: Gamma_sigma->gamma gamma^os,dir = (1.0 +/- 0.4) keV
- QSSR prediction for bare gluonium mass: M_sigma_B = (0.95 to 1.10) GeV
- QSSR prediction for bare gluonium width to pipi: Gamma_sigma_B->pi+pi- = 0.5 GeV
- QSSR prediction for bare gluonium gamma-gamma width: Gamma_sigma_B->gamma gamma = (0.2 to 0.6) keV
- QSSR prediction for a qq state (S2) width to pipi: Gamma_S2->pi+pi- = 120 MeV
- QSSR prediction for a four-quark state gamma-gamma width: 0.4 x 10^{-3} keV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Mennessier, Narison, and Ochs (2008) on gamma-gamma to pipi data, which first suggested the sigma could be a gluonic resonance, by adding the KK coupling channel as a further discriminant. The results contradict interpretations of the sigma as a pipi molecule, tetraquark state, or ordinary qq meson, which would predict either zero or very different KK couplings. The findings support earlier QCD spectral sum rule predictions by Narison (1984, 1998, 2006) and Veneziano (1989) for a light scalar glueball, and suggest the sigma may be associated with the U(1)_V conformal anomaly, analogous to the eta-prime meson for the U(1)_A anomaly. This enables future lattice calculations with dynamical fermions to test the gluonium interpretation and clarifies the role of the sigma in understanding non-perturbative QCD effects below 1 GeV.
