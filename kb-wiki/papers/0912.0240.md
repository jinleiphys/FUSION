# Momentum-space calculation of proton-deuteron scattering including Coulomb and irreducible three-nucleon forces

> Three-nucleon scattering equations with irreducible three-nucleon force are solved in momentum-space. The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included using the method of screening and renormalization. The need for the renormalization of the scattering amplitudes is demonstrated numerically. The Coulomb and Urbana IX three-nucleon force effects on the observables of elastic proton-deuteron scattering and breakup are studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the standard screening and renormalization method for including the Coulomb interaction in proton-deuteron scattering remains valid when an irreducible three-nucleon force is added, contrary to an alternative renormalization prescription proposed by Witala (2009) that claimed no renormalization was needed for elastic scattering amplitudes.

## Method

The Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for three-nucleon scattering were extended to include an irreducible three-nucleon force and solved in momentum-space partial-wave representation. The Coulomb interaction between the two protons was incorporated using the screening and renormalization method, where the screened Coulomb potential was added to the hadronic proton-proton potential and the resulting amplitudes were renormalized in the infinite-screening-radius limit. The AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential together with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force served as the hadronic interaction model.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius of R = 10 fm was sufficient for converged proton-deuteron elastic scattering results, while R = 30 fm was needed for breakup calculations
- Hadronic interaction was included in two-nucleon partial waves with total pair angular momentum I ≤ 5; screened Coulomb interaction in two-proton partial waves with pair orbital angular momentum L ≤ 14
- Total three-nucleon angular momentum up to J ≤ 59/2 was considered, but the three-nucleon force was only included in partial waves with J ≤ 19/2
- Calculations were performed at proton lab energies of 9 MeV and 135 MeV for elastic scattering, and at 13 MeV and 130 MeV deuteron lab energy for breakup
- The relative proton-proton energy in the (13°,13°,20°) breakup configuration at 130 MeV deuteron lab energy was below 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space treatment of proton-deuteron scattering by Deltuva (2005) to include irreducible three-nucleon forces, which previously had only been included in configuration-space calculations by Kievsky (2001) using the Kohn variational principle at energies below 50 MeV. The results agree with those configuration-space calculations at low energies, and provide first predictions for the AV18 + Urbana IX force model with Coulomb at higher energies and for breakup reactions. The method enables systematic studies of the interplay between Coulomb and three-nucleon force effects across a wide energy range up to pion-production threshold.
