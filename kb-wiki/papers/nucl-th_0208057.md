# Quasiparticle Properties in Effective Field Theory

> The quasiparticle concept is an important tool for the description of many-body systems. We study the quasiparticle properties for dilute Fermi systems with short-ranged, repulsive interactions using effective field theory. We calculate the proper self-energy contributions at order (K_f/Lambda)^3, where Lambda is the short-distance scale that sets the size of the effective range parameters and K_f the Fermi momentum. The quasiparticle energy, width, and effective mass to order O(K_f/Lambda)^3 are derived from the calculated self-energy.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first complete calculation of quasiparticle properties in a dilute Fermi gas with short-ranged repulsive interactions to order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\) in the low-density expansion, where \(\Lambda\) is the short-distance scale set by the effective range parameters; at this order both the S-wave effective range \(r_s\) and the P-wave scattering length \(a_p\) contribute, and the calculation is fully perturbative within an effective field theory framework.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) for nonrelativistic fermions with contact interactions, renormalized via dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction, and match the low-energy constants to the effective range parameters \(a_s\), \(r_s\), and \(a_p\). They calculate the proper self-energy \(\Sigma^*(k_0,k)\) at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\) by evaluating nine Hugenholtz diagrams, using analytical methods for diagrams (8) and (9) and numerical Monte Carlo integration (Vegas) for diagrams (6) and (7), then extract the quasiparticle energy \(\epsilon_k\), width \(\gamma_k\), and effective mass \(M^*\) by solving the Dyson equation perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle effective mass at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\):  
  \(\frac{M^{*}}{M} = \biggl(1+(g-1)\frac{8}{15\pi^2}(k_F a_s)^2(1-7\log 2) + \frac{(g+1)}{3\pi}(k_F a_p)^3 + \frac{(k_F a_s)^2 k_F r_s}{6\pi}(g-1) + 0.11(g-1)(g-3)(k_F a_s)^3 - 0.15(g-1)(k_F a_s)^3\biggr)^{-1}\) for spin degeneracy \(g\).

- Chemical potential at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\):  
  \(\mu_{(3)} = \frac{k_F^2}{2M}\biggl[(g-1)\frac{4}{15\pi}(k_F a_s)^2 k_F r_s + (g+1)\frac{8}{15\pi}(k_F a_p)^3 + (g-1)\{0.20+(g-3)0.15\}(k_F a_s)^3\biggr]\).

- Energy per particle at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\):  
  \(\left(\frac{E}{N}\right)_{(3)} = \frac{k_F^2}{2M}\biggl[(g-1)\frac{1}{10\pi}(k_F a_s)^2 k_F r_s + (g+1)\frac{1}{5\pi}(k_F a_p)^3 + (g-1)\{0.076+(g-3)0.057\}(k_F a_s)^3\biggr]\).

- Wave function renormalization at the Fermi surface:  
  \(Z(k_F)^{-1} = 1 - \frac{4(g-1)}{\pi^2} \log 2\, (k_F a_s)^2 + \frac{8(g-1)}{\pi^3} \{0.184-(g-3)\,0.224\}(k_F a_s)^3\).

- The quasiparticle width \(\gamma_k\) at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\) satisfies Luttinger's theorem, with the first nonvanishing contribution at \({\cal O}((1-v)^2)\) where \(v=k/k_F\).

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle calculations of Galitskii (1958) at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^2\) and the incomplete numerical study of Bund and Wajntal (1995) at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^3\), providing the first complete calculation including both particle-hole and effective range contributions. The results enable systematic improvement of the EFT description of dilute Fermi gases to higher orders, with the next step being the inclusion of three-body interactions at order \((k_F/\Lambda)^4\) as noted by Hammer and Furnstahl (2000); the approach also provides a controlled framework for future applications to cold fermionic atoms and, with modifications, to nuclear matter.
