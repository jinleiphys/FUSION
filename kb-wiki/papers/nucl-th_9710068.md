# Can the magnetic moment contribution explain the A_y puzzle?

> We evaluate the full one-photon-exchange Born amplitude for $Nd$ scattering. We include the contributions due to the magnetic moment of the proton or neutron, and the magnetic moment and quadrupole moment of the deuteron. It is found that the inclusion of the magnetic-moment interaction in the theoretical description of the $Nd$ scattering observables cannot resolve the long-standing $A_y$ puzzle.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the full one-photon-exchange electromagnetic amplitude (including magnetic moment and quadrupole moment contributions) in the theoretical description of nucleon-deuteron scattering produces effects that are far too small to resolve the long-standing \(A_y\) puzzle, even though the electromagnetic interaction mainly affects the analyzing powers.

## Method

The author evaluated the full one-photon-exchange Born amplitude for nucleon-deuteron scattering, treating the deuteron as a single spin-1 particle with charge, magnetic moment, and quadrupole moment. The electromagnetic amplitude was added to the nuclear \(N\!d\) amplitude calculated using phase shifts from the Pisa group at laboratory energies of 1, 2, and 3 MeV. For \(pd\) scattering, the plane-wave Born amplitude was modified with the Breit factor to approximate Coulomb distorted-wave effects.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_p = 3\) MeV (\(E_d = 6\) MeV), the inclusion of the full electromagnetic amplitude produced only a small enhancement in the forward direction for the proton analyzing power, far too small to explain the discrepancy with experimental data from Shimizu et al. (1995).
- At \(E_n = 3\) MeV (\(E_d = 6\) MeV), the effect of the electromagnetic amplitude on the neutron and deuteron vector analyzing powers only appeared at extreme forward angles, with no visible effect on deuteron tensor analyzing powers.
- The theoretical predictions using realistic \(N\!N\) potentials are systematically lower by about 30% than experimental measurements for low-energy \(pd\) and \(nd\) analyzing powers up to nucleon laboratory energies of about 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Stoks and de Swart (1990), who found that including the magnetic-moment interaction resolved a similar problem in \(N\!N\) scattering by modifying the spin-orbit combination of the \(^3P\) waves. The paper contradicts the possibility that electromagnetic contributions could explain the \(A_y\) puzzle, which had resisted resolution through modifications of the \(^3P_J\) partial waves (Witała and Glöckle, 1991) and inclusion of three-nucleon forces (Witała et al., 1994). The result leaves the \(A_y\) puzzle as an open problem requiring further investigation, potentially through alternative modifications to the short-range nuclear interaction.
