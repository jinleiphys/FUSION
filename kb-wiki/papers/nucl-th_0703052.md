# Multiplicity Fluctuations in Proton-Proton and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We study the scaled variances of multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies within the HSD transport model. The HSD results are compared with proton-proton data and with predictions of the hadron-resonance gas statistical model. We find that the HSD scaled variances $ω_i$ for negatively, positively, and all charged hadrons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions remain close to the $ω_i$ values in proton-proton collisions and increase with collision energy as the corresponding multiplicities per participating nucleon. The statistical model predicts very different behavior of $ω_i$. However, a comparison with preliminary NA49 data for the most central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies does not permit to distinguish the HSD and statistical model results. New measurements of the multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions in a wide energy region with large acceptance are needed to allow for a proper determination of the underlying dynamics.

# Multiplicity Fluctuations in Proton-Proton and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
The HSD transport model predicts that scaled variances of multiplicity fluctuations in central nucleus-nucleus collisions remain close to those in proton-proton collisions and increase with collision energy as the multiplicity per participating nucleon, but a comparison with preliminary NA49 data for the most central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies does not permit to distinguish the HSD and statistical model results due to limited acceptance and small differences at those energies.

## Method
The authors used the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (10-158 AGeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=62, 130, 200 GeV) at zero impact parameter. They calculated scaled variances omega_i for negatively, positively, and all charged hadrons, and compared these with results from proton-proton collisions, nucleon-nucleon collisions constructed from p+p, p+n, and n+n HSD simulations, and with predictions from the micro-canonical ensemble hadron-resonance gas (MCE HG) statistical model along the chemical freeze-out line.

## Key numbers
- In Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV with b=0, the mean number of participants is <N_P> = 392, and the scaled variance of participant number fluctuations is omega_P = 0.055.
- The NA49 acceptance probabilities for charged hadrons are q=0.038, 0.063, 0.085, 0.131, 0.163 at SPS energies of 20, 30, 40, 80, 158 AGeV, respectively.
- At sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the HSD values of omega_i are about 10 times larger than the corresponding MCE HG values.
- The MCE HG model predicts asymptotic values at RHIC energies: omega_plus/minus(MCE) = 0.3 and omega_ch(MCE) = 0.6.
- The ratio of HSD to MCE values of omega_plus/minus reaches a factor of 2 at the highest SPS energy 158 AGeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions using transport models, building on the HSD model's established success in describing inclusive charged hadron spectra from SIS to RHIC energies (Weber, 2003). The results contradict the predictions of the statistical hadron-resonance gas model (res_MCE, 2006) which gives approximately constant scaled variances at high energy, and instead support the wounded nucleon model picture where fluctuations in A+A collisions resemble those in N+N collisions (voka1, 2006). The study shows that new measurements with large acceptance at both SPS and RHIC energies are needed to distinguish between transport and statistical descriptions of the underlying dynamics.
