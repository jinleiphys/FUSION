# Explicit Representations for the T-Matrix on Unphysical Energy Sheets and Resonances in Two- and Three-Body Systems

> We discuss the structure of the two- and three-body T-matrices, scattering matrices, and resolvents continued to the unphysical energy sheets. Our conclusions arise due to the representations that have been found for analytically continued momentum-space kernels of the T-operators. These representations are explicitly written only in terms of the physical-sheet kernels of the T-matrix itself. One of advantages of the representations in the three-body case is that they show which portions of the physical-sheet three-body scattering matrix are ``responsible'' for the resonances associated with a particular unphysical sheet. A resonance appears to be the energy where the correspondingly truncated scattering matrix (taken on the physical sheet) has eigenvalue zero. We also mention applications of this approach to some specific three-body systems, based on the Faddeev differential equations.

## Key claim

Resonances on a given unphysical energy sheet correspond to zeros of a specifically truncated scattering matrix evaluated on the physical sheet, rather than to poles of the analytically continued scattering matrix itself.

## Method

The author derives explicit algebraic representations for the two-body and three-body T-matrices, resolvents, and scattering matrices on unphysical energy sheets, expressed solely in terms of the same quantities evaluated on the physical sheet. For the two-body case, the continuation is obtained by applying Lemma 1 (based on the residue theorem) to the Cauchy-type integrals appearing in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, yielding a closed-form expression involving the physical-sheet T-matrix and the inverse of the physical-sheet scattering matrix. For the three-body case, the Faddeev equations are analytically continued and explicitly solved in terms of the physical-sheet Faddeev components and a truncated scattering matrix \(S_{\ell}(z)\) that depends on the multi-index \(\ell\) labeling the unphysical sheet.

## Key numbers

* Two-body unphysical sheet \(\Pi_1\): the continued T-matrix is \(t(z)|_{\Pi_1} = t(z) - \ra_0(z) \, t(z) \rjd(z) [s(z)]^{-1} \rj(z) t(z)\), where \(\ra_0(z) = -\pi i \sqrt{z}\) and \(s(z)\) is the physical-sheet scattering matrix.
* Two-body continued scattering matrix: \(s(z)|_{\Pi_1} = \mathcal{E} [s(z)]^{-1} \mathcal{E}\), where \(\mathcal{E}\) is the inversion operator \((\mathcal{E}f)(\widehat{\mathbf{k}}) = f(-\widehat{\mathbf{k}})\).
* Three-body unphysical sheet \(\Pi_{\ell}\) with multi-index \(\ell = (\ell_0, \ell_1, \ell_2, \ell_3)\): the continued Faddeev components are \(M|_{\Pi_{\ell}} = M + Q_M \, L \, S_{\ell}^{-1} \, \widetilde{L} \, \widetilde{Q}_M\), where \(L = \mathrm{diag}(\ell_0, \ell_1, \ell_2, \ell_3)\) and \(\widetilde{L} = \mathrm{diag}(|\ell_0|, \ell_1, \ell_2, \ell_3)\).
* Two-body thresholds \(\varepsilon_\alpha\) (with \(\alpha=1,2,3\)) are square-root branching points; the three-body threshold at 0 is a logarithmic branching point.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier results (Motovilov and coauthors, 1993, 1997) on explicit analytic continuation of scattering quantities to unphysical sheets. It provides a practical alternative to the complex scaling method (Balslev and Combes, 1971; Hagedorn, 1979) and the Lax-Phillips approach (1967) for identifying resonances, by reducing the problem to finding zeros of a truncated scattering matrix on the physical sheet. The approach has been applied to three-body systems including the \(^4\)He trimer (Motovilov and coauthors, 2000, 1999) to study the mechanism of Efimov state emergence, and can be implemented using Faddeev differential equations.
