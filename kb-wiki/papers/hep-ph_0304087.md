# Chiral and U(1)_A restorations high in the hadron spectrum and the semiclassical approximation

> In quantum systems with large $n$ (radial quantum number) or large angular momentum the semiclassical (WKB) approximation is valid. A physical content of the semiclassical approximation is that the quantum fluctuations effects are suppressed and vanish asymptotically. The chiral as well as $U(1)_A$ breakings in QCD is a result of quantum fluctuations. Hence these breakings must be absent (suppressed) high in the spectrum and the spectrum of high-lying hadrons must exhibit symmetries of the classical QCD Lagrangian.

# Chiral and U(1)_A restorations high in the hadron spectrum and the semiclassical approximation

## Key claim

Chiral and U(1)_A symmetries of the classical QCD Lagrangian must be restored in highly excited isolated hadrons (those with large radial quantum number n or large angular momentum L) because the semiclassical (WKB) approximation becomes valid in such systems, suppressing the quantum fluctuations that are responsible for both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the U(1)_A anomaly. The claim is conditional: the restoration applies asymptotically as n or L become large, and the argument does not provide a detailed microscopic mechanism for how this occurs.

## Method

The paper argues by connecting the semiclassical approximation to the suppression of quantum fluctuations in quantum systems with large n or large L, using the path integral formulation where the action S(q) must satisfy S(q) >> hbar for the classical path to dominate. It then notes that both spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (via the quark condensate and dynamical mass generation in the Schwinger-Dyson equation) and the U(1)_A anomaly are pure quantum fluctuation effects, so they must vanish when quantum fluctuations are suppressed. The argument is supported by phenomenological evidence from the nucleon, delta, and meson spectra showing parity doubling at high excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate value: ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ -(240 ± 10 MeV)^3, cited as the order parameter for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking
- Excited nucleons around 1.7 GeV show three approximate parity doublets with spins 1/2, 3/2, and 5/2
- The lowest excitations with J=9/2 in the nucleon spectrum are well-established states showing parity doubling
- A well-established state with J=11/2 in the nucleon spectrum has no parity partner observed yet

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Cohen and Glozman (2002) that parity doubling in high-lying hadrons reflects effective chiral symmetry restoration, and by Glozman (2000) that such restoration occurs. It provides a general theoretical justification based on the semiclassical approximation, going beyond the operator product expansion argument of Shifman, Vainstein, and Zakharov (1979) which only applied to asymptotic spectral densities at large space-like momenta. The paper enables experimental searches for complete U(2)_L × U(2)_R multiplets in high-lying hadron spectra at facilities such as JLAB, BNL, SPRING 8, ELSA, and the proposed Darmstadt antiproton ring, and supports a string picture of highly excited hadrons with massless quarks of definite chirality at the string endpoints.
