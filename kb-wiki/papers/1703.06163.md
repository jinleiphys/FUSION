# Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distributions at Small-x

> We study the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distributions at small-x in a consistent framework that takes into account the TMD evolution and small-x evolution simultaneously. The small-x evolution effects are included by computing the TMDs at appropriate scales in terms of the dipole scattering amplitudes, which obey the relevant Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. Meanwhile, the TMD evolution is obtained by resumming the Collins-Soper type large logarithms emerged from the calculations in small-x formalism into Sudakov factors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distributions at small-x can be consistently described by simultaneously resumming two types of large logarithms: Collins-Soper type logarithms (Sudakov factors) and small-x evolution logarithms (Balitsky-Kovchegov equation), provided the two contributions are well separated in phase space.

## Method

The authors computed the TMD quark and gluon distributions using the color glass condensate (CGC) framework at small-x, expressing them in terms of dipole scattering amplitudes (Wilson line correlators) that obey the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation. They then applied the Collins 2011 scheme with soft factor subtraction to regulate light-cone singularities, and resummed the Collins-Soper type logarithms into Sudakov form factors. The final expressions for the Weizsacker-Williams (WW) gluon TMD and dipole gluon TMD were written as products of a Sudakov factor, a perturbative hard coefficient, and the appropriate Wilson line correlator evolved in rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The Sudakov factor exponent is defined as \( \mathcal{S}_{sud} = \int_{c_0^2/r_\perp^2}^{Q^2} \frac{d\mu^2}{\mu^2} \left[ A \ln\frac{Q^2}{\mu^2} + B \right] \), where \( c_0 = 2e^{-\gamma_E} \) with \( \gamma_E \) the Euler constant.
- For the quark TMD Sudakov factor, the one-loop coefficients are \( A_q^{(1)} = \frac{1}{2}C_F \) and \( B_q^{(1)} = -\frac{3}{4}C_F \).
- For the gluon TMD Sudakov factor, the one-loop coefficients are \( A_g^{(1)} = \frac{1}{2}C_A \) and \( B_g^{(1)} = -\beta_0 C_A \), where \( \beta_0 = (11 - 2N_f/3)/12 \).
- In the small \( k_\perp \) limit, the quark distribution saturates: \( xq(x,k_\perp)|_{k_\perp\to 0} \propto N_c/(4\pi^4) \).
- In the large \( k_\perp \) limit, the quark distribution has power-law behavior: \( xq(x,k_\perp)|_{k_\perp \gg Q_s} \propto Q_s^2/k_\perp^2 \), where \( Q_s^2 \) is the saturation momentum.

## Context

This work extends the unified treatment of Sudakov and small-x resummations previously applied at the cross section level by Mueller (2012, 2013) to the operator level for TMD parton distributions. It builds on the identification of WW and dipole gluon distributions with Wilson line correlators established by Dominguez (2010, 2011), and provides a consistent framework that combines the Collins-Soper evolution (Collins 1981, 2011) with the Balitsky-Kovchegov small-x evolution (Balitsky 1995, Kovchegov 1999). This enables future phenomenological applications at the Electron-Ion Collider and other facilities where both TMD and small-x effects are important.
