# Nuclear matter in the chiral limit and the in-medium chiral condensate

> We investigate nuclear matter, i.e. the nuclear equation-of-state (EOS) as well as the relativistic mean fields in the chiral limit. The investigations are based on a chiral nucleon-nucleon EFT interaction where the explicit and implicit pion mass dependence is known up to next-to-leading order. The nuclear bulk properties are found to remain fairly stable in the chiral limit. Based on the same interaction the in-medium scalar condensate is derived, both in Hartree-Fock approximation as well as from the Brueckner G-matrix, making thereby use of the Hellman-Feynman theorem. Short distance physics which determines the reduction of the in-medium nucleon mass is found to play only a minor role for the reduction of the chiral condensate.

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state and relativistic mean fields remain fairly stable when the pion mass is taken to zero (the chiral limit), based on a chiral effective field theory interaction where the quark mass dependence is known up to next-to-leading order. The short-distance physics that determines the in-medium nucleon mass reduction plays only a minor role in the reduction of the chiral condensate.

## Method

The authors used a chiral nucleon-nucleon effective field theory interaction at next-to-leading order (NLO), for which the explicit and implicit pion mass dependence is known analytically. They computed the nuclear self-energy components (scalar and vector) in Hartree-Fock approximation at tree level using projection techniques to map the two-body amplitudes onto a covariant operator basis. The in-medium scalar quark condensate was derived via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem both in Hartree-Fock approximation and from the Brueckner G-matrix to include short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- At a Fermi momentum of k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1} (nuclear density ρ_B = 0.166 fm^{-3}), the scalar and vector self-energy components from one-pion exchange approach constant values of about 30 MeV in the chiral limit.
- The two-pion exchange contributions to the scalar and vector self-energy components are reduced by approximately 2-5 MeV when going from the physical pion mass (m_π = 138 MeV) to the chiral limit.
- The physical nucleon mass M is expressed as M = M_0 + σ_N, where M_0 is the nucleon mass in the chiral limit and σ_N is the pion-nucleon sigma term.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ≃ 4π f_π ≃ 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic mean fields in nuclear matter from previous studies by Plohl, Fuchs, and van Dalen (2006) using chiral effective field theory interactions, and connects to the QCD sum rule approach of Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1991). The results enable a systematic determination of the in-medium chiral condensate from a chiral interaction with controlled quark mass dependence, addressing the long-standing question of how the nucleon mass shift relates to the reduction of the scalar quark condensate, previously discussed by Birse (1996).
