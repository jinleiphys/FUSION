# Single- and double-scattering production of four muons in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We discuss production of two $μ^+μ^-$ pairs in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at the LHC. We take into account electromagnetic (two-photon) double-scattering production and for a first time direct $γγ$ production of four muons in one scattering. We study the unexplored process $γγ\to μ^+μ^-μ^+μ^-$. We present predictions for total and differential cross sections. Measurable nuclear cross sections are obtained and corresponding differential distributions and counting rates are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of four-muon production in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions including both the double-scattering (two sequential γγ → μ⁺μ⁻ interactions) and the previously unexplored single-scattering (γγ → μ⁺μ⁻μ⁺μ⁻) mechanism, finding that the double-scattering cross section is considerably larger than the single-scattering one, making double-scattering the dominant production channel.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear cross sections for PbPb → PbPb μ⁺μ⁻μ⁺μ⁻ at √sNN = 5.02 TeV using the equivalent photon approximation with realistic nuclear charge form factors. For the double-scattering mechanism, they convolute two independent γγ → μ⁺μ⁻ subprocesses with photon fluxes and an absorption factor S²_abs(b). For the single-scattering mechanism, they compute the elementary γγ → μ⁺μ⁻μ⁺μ⁻ cross section using the KaTie event generator with recursive amplitude methods, then fold it with nuclear photon fluxes.

## Key numbers

- For double-scattering with cuts -2.5 < y_i < 2.5 and p_t > 0.5 GeV: cross section = 815 nb, corresponding to 815 counts for L_int = 1 nb⁻¹
- For double-scattering with cuts -2.5 < y_i < 2.5 and p_t > 1.0 GeV: cross section = 53 nb, corresponding to 53 counts for L_int = 1 nb⁻¹
- For double-scattering with cuts -0.9 < y_i < 0.9 and p_t > 0.5 GeV: cross section = 31 nb, corresponding to 31 counts for L_int = 1 nb⁻¹
- For double-scattering with cuts -0.9 < y_i < 0.9 and p_t > 1.0 GeV: cross section = 2 nb, corresponding to 2 counts for L_int = 1 nb⁻¹
- For double-scattering with cuts -2.4 < y_i < 2.4 and p_t > 4.0 GeV: cross section = 1.9 × 10⁻³ nb, corresponding to ≪1 count for L_int = 1 nb⁻¹
- The elementary γγ → μ⁺μ⁻ cross section is at least one order of magnitude larger than γγ → μ⁺μ⁻μ⁺μ⁻ at W = 20 GeV, and about four orders of magnitude larger for smaller W values
- The double-scattering four-muon cross section is more than three orders of magnitude smaller than the single μ⁺μ⁻ pair production cross section

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for four-electron production in UPC (Klusek-Gawenda 2016) by considering muon final states and adding the single-scattering mechanism for the first time. The double-scattering mechanism was previously studied for π⁺π⁻π⁺π⁻ production by the STAR collaboration (Abelev 2009) and theoretically by Klusek-Gawenda (2013). The results enable experimental searches by CMS and ALICE collaborations, who could observe hundreds of events with appropriate p_t cuts, while the ATLAS collaboration with p_t > 4 GeV cuts would find observation very difficult.
