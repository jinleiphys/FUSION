# An exactly solvable model of a superconducting to rotational phase transition

> We consider a many-fermion model which exhibits a transition from a superconducting to a rotational phase with variation of a parameter in its Hamiltonian. The model has analytical solutions in its two limits due to the presence of dynamical symmetries. However, the symmetries are basically incompatible with one another; no simple solution exists in intermediate situations. Exact (numerical) solutions are possible and enable one to study the behavior of competing but incompatible symmetries and the phase transitions that result in a semirealistic situation. The results are remarkably simple and shed light on the nature of phase transitions.

# An exactly solvable model of a superconducting to rotational phase transition

## Key claim

The paper presents a many-fermion model that exhibits a sharp phase transition from a superconducting to a rotational phase as a parameter in the Hamiltonian is varied, but only when the number of nucleons N equals 2κ (where κ is the number of fermion types); for N > 2κ, pair correlations cause mixing of SU(3) irreps in a highly coherent way such that rotational character is preserved, and those results are reported in a subsequent analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian H = αH₁ + (1-α)H₂, where H₁ = -χ Q·Q is an Elliott SU(3) quadrupole Hamiltonian for rotational motion and H₂ = -G S₊S₋ is a Kerman SU(2) quasispin Hamiltonian for superconductivity. They diagonalize this Hamiltonian exactly within a single Sp(3) irreducible representation built on the fermion vacuum, using the subalgebra chain Sp(3) ⊃ U(3) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3) ⊃ SO(2), with κ artificially assigned values in the 20-40 range to generate SU(3) irreps of sufficient deformation.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of lowest J = 0, 2, 4, 6, and 8 states are computed for N = 2κ, with κ = 5, 10, and 20
- Coupling constants used: G = 0.05 and χ = 0.005 (dimensionless units)
- The system is essentially in a superconducting phase for α < 0.5 and a rotational phase for α > 0.6, across all κ values shown
- For large κ and N = 2κ, there is essentially no region of α for which the model is both rotational and superconducting

## Context

This work extends the study of competing dynamical symmetries in nuclear structure, following earlier exactly solvable models by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965) and Chen, Browstein, and Rowe (1990). The model builds on Elliott's SU(3) model (1958) for rotations and Kerman's SU(2) quasispin model (1961) for superconductivity, which are incompatible symmetries. The results enable testing of mean-field approximations such as Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov and the random phase approximation (RPA), and the sharp phase transition observed is reminiscent of the Mottelson-Valatin effect (1960) where Coriolis forces suppress pairing in rotating nuclei. The model contracts to the Interacting Boson Model as κ → ∞.
