# Observing Chiral Nonanalytic Behavior with FLIC Fermions

> First results from lattice QCD revealing the chiral nonanalytic behavior of nucleon and Delta baryon magnetic moments are presented. Numerical simulations in the light quark mass regime employing the nonperturbatively O(a)-improved conserved vector current are enabled via FLIC fermions. Quenched chiral perturbation theory for the nucleon and Delta magnetic moments is derived to next to next to leading nonanalytic order. Numerical simulation results for the proton and Delta baryon magnetic moments in quenched QCD reveal dramatic signatures of the quenched meson cloud, which are in accord with the predictions of quenched chiral perturbation theory.

# Observing Chiral Nonanalytic Behavior with FLIC Fermions

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD results showing chiral nonanalytic behavior in nucleon and Delta baryon magnetic moments, but only in the quenched approximation. The observed dramatic signatures of the quenched meson cloud are in accord with quenched chiral perturbation theory, not full QCD.

## Method
The authors use Fat Link Irrelevant Clover (FLIC) fermions on 255 mean-field improved Luscher-Weisz gauge configurations with a lattice spacing of 0.128 fm. They compute electromagnetic form factors using the nonperturbatively O(a)-improved conserved vector current, and fit the results to finite-range regulator (FRR) quenched chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading nonanalytic order.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: 0.128 fm, determined by the Sommer scale r0 = 0.50 fm
- Gauge field configurations: 255 configurations of size 20^3 × 40
- Nucleon mass chiral expansion coefficient for m_pi^2 term: 2.84 ± 0.04 GeV^-1
- Nucleon mass chiral expansion coefficient for m_pi^4 term: 22.0 ± 1.6 GeV^-3
- Nucleon mass chiral expansion constant term: 0.897 ± 0.001 GeV
- Coefficient of the m_pi^6 term from N → N pi self energy: -75 ± 35 GeV^-5

## Context
This work extends the program of observing chiral nonanalytic behavior in lattice QCD, previously identified by Leinweber (2001, 2002) for baryon magnetic moments. The use of FLIC fermions enables access to the light quark mass regime where quenched chiral perturbation theory predictions can be tested. The finite-range regulator approach developed by Young (2002) for the nucleon mass is applied here to magnetic moments, providing estimates for higher-order terms while preserving model-independent features. The quenched artifacts in the Delta baryon, where the Delta → N pi decay channel contributes with opposite sign compared to full QCD, provide an unmistakable signal for the onset of quenched chiral nonanalytic behavior.
