# Matter-Neutrino Resonance Above Merging Compact Objects

> Accretion disks arising from neutron star- neutron star mergers or black hole- neutron star mergers produce large numbers of neutrinos and antineutrinos. In contrast to other astrophysical scenarios, like supernovae, in mergers the antineutrinos outnumber the neutrinos. This antineutrino dominance gives neutrinos from merger disks the opportunity to exhibit new oscillation physics, specifically a matter-neutrino resonance. We explore this resonance, finding that consequences can be a large transition of $ν_e$ to other flavors, while the $\barν_e$s return to their initial state. We present numerical calculations of neutrinos from merger disks and compare with a single energy model. We explain both the basic features and the conditions for a transition.

## Key claim

In accretion disks from neutron star-neutron star or black hole-neutron star mergers, where antineutrinos outnumber neutrinos, a matter-neutrino resonance (MNR) can occur when the neutrino self-interaction potential cancels the matter potential, causing a large flavor transition of electron neutrinos while antineutrinos return to their initial state; this transition requires the neutrino self-interaction potential to initially dominate over the matter potential and depends on a sufficiently large vacuum mixing angle.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Neutrino Flavor Isospin (NFIS) formalism with single-energy neutrinos and antineutrinos to derive analytic survival probability formulas for the MNR transition. They then perform numerical calculations for a multi-energy, three-flavor model of a merger disk with specified temperatures and radii, using the single-angle approximation and vacuum parameters consistent with measured values, to compute energy-integrated survival probabilities along an outflow trajectory.

## Key numbers

- Disk radius: \(R_0 = 4.5 \times 10^6\) cm; temperatures: \(T_{\nu_e} = 6.4\) MeV, \(T_{\bar{\nu}_e} = 7.1\) MeV, \(T_{\nu_{\mu,\tau}} = T_{\bar{\nu}_{\mu,\tau}} = 7.4\) MeV
- Vacuum parameters used: \(\delta m^2_{12} = 7.6 \times 10^{-5}\) eV\(^2\), \(\delta m^2_{32} = -2.4 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\), \(\theta_{12} = 0.60\), \(\theta_{13} = 0.16\), \(\theta_{32} = 0.76\)
- For the single-energy model with \(\alpha = 4/3\) and \(\theta_V = 0.15\), the transition occurs when the self-interaction potential initially dominates over the matter potential (e.g., \(V_e(l=0) = 1000\Delta\), \(\mu_\nu(l)(\alpha-1) = 10000\Delta e^{(-\Delta/10)l}\))
- In the merger disk calculation with mu/tau neutrino fluxes at 35% of blackbody, the asymmetry is \(\alpha = 1.37\) and the potential ratio scale height is \(\tau_{V_e/\mu} = 5.8 \times 10^6\) cm; the MNR transition occurs for \(\theta \gtrsim 2.3 \times 10^{-2}\)
- The transition is suppressed when muon neutrino flux \(\beta\) (ratio to electron neutrino flux) reaches 1.3, while it occurs at \(\beta = 0.7\)

## Context

This work extends the observation of a resonance in disks from stellar collapse (Malkus 2012) to the novel environment of compact object mergers, where antineutrino dominance enables behavior distinct from supernova collective oscillations (Duan 2006, Hannestad 2006) and the standard MSW effect (Lunardini 2003, Dighe 1999). The MNR transition occurs close to the disk surface and may influence wind-type nucleosynthesis including r-process (Surman 2008) and nickel production (Surman 2013), with potential observable consequences in the galactic inventory of elements or kilonova electromagnetic signals (Metzger 2011). Future work requires improved modeling of neutrino fluxes from merger environments and inclusion of multi-angle effects (Duan 2006, Mirizzi 2013) and halo effects (Cherry 2013).
