# Revisiting the axial anomaly for light mesons and baryons

> The axial anomaly is responsible for the masses and mixing of the mesons $η$ and $η'$. An open question is if (and to what extent) it affects also other hadrons. We show that anomalous terms can be important to understand the spectroscopy of the pseudotensor mesons $η_2 (1645)$ and $η_2 (1870)$. In fact, pseudotensor mesons belong to a so-called heterochiral multiplet, for which a quadratic mixing term between nonstrange and strange isoscalar members arises. On the contrary, for so-called homochiral multiplets, such as the ground-state (axial-)vector and tensor mesons, this mixing is not possible, hence one can easily understand why the isoscalar members of these multiplets are almost purely nonstrange and strange, respectively. Moreover, the axial anomaly can be also coupled to baryons (within the mirror assignment), and thus it helps to explain the large decay width $ N^{\ast }(1535)\rightarrow Nη$ and to clarify which baryons are chiral partners.

## Key claim

The axial anomaly, already known to explain the masses and mixing of the η and η' mesons, can also generate large strange-nonstrange mixing in heterochiral multiplets such as pseudotensor mesons (η₂(1645) and η₂(1870)), while homochiral multiplets such as ground-state vector and tensor mesons cannot receive such mixing, explaining why their isoscalar members are almost purely nonstrange or strange.

## Method

The author classifies light meson nonets into heterochiral and homochiral multiplets based on their transformation properties under SU(3)R × SU(3)L × U(1)A, then constructs anomalous Lagrangian terms proportional to (det Φ − det Φ†)² for each heterochiral multiplet. For baryons, the mirror assignment is used to couple the axial anomaly to baryonic chiral partners, generating enhanced decay couplings to η and η' mesons.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar mixing angle θP varies between −40° and −45° for realistic values of the anomalous parameter αA
- Pseudotensor mixing angle θPT ≃ −42° for the η₂(1645) and η₂(1870) states, extracted from phenomenological study of Ref. Adrian
- The anomalous term for pseudotensor mesons reduces to −γA(√2 η₂,N^μν + η₂,S^μν)² with γA ≃ cA^(3) φN²
- The pseudoscalar mixing angle formula: θP = −½ arctan[4√2 αA / (2(mK² − mπ² − αA))]

## Context

This work extends the known role of the axial anomaly from the pseudoscalar sector (η and η' masses, Feldmann) to pseudotensor mesons (η₂(1645) and η₂(1870)) and baryons. It builds on the heterochiral/homochiral classification of Giacosa (2017) and the mirror assignment for baryons of DeTar and Kunihiro, and enables future experimental verification at Jefferson Lab (GlueX) of the predicted large mixing in pseudotensor and pseudovector sectors.
