# Asymptotic-freedom and massive glueballs in a qubit-regularized SU(2) gauge theory

> We argue that a simple qubit-regularized $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ lattice gauge theory (LGT) on a plaquette chain serves as a pseudo-one-dimensional toy model for Yang-Mills (YM) theory in three spatial dimensions. We map the chain Hamiltonian to the Transverse Field Ising Model (TFIM) in a uniform magnetic field and demonstrate that it can be tuned to a continuum limit in which the short-distance physics is governed by the asymptotically free Ising conformal field theory (CFT) describing free Majorana fermions, while the long-distance regime contains massive excitations of the $E_8$ quantum field theory (QFT) that can be interpreted as one-dimensional analogues of glueballs. Furthermore, we find $\sqrtσ/m_1 = 0.249(1)$ where $σ$ is the string tension between two static quarks and $m_1$ is the mass of the lightest glueball.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a qubit-regularized SU(2) lattice gauge theory on a plaquette chain can be tuned to a continuum limit where short-distance physics is governed by the asymptotically free Ising conformal field theory, while long-distance physics contains massive excitations of the E8 quantum field theory interpretable as one-dimensional glueball analogues; this claim is conditional on restricting the theory to the even Hilbert space sector and setting specific Hamiltonian parameters (κr = 2, δ = 1, κc → 0).

## Method

The authors construct a qubit-regularized SU(2) pure gauge theory on a plaquette chain (a pseudo-one-dimensional ladder geometry) using the monomer-dimer tensor-network basis with link representations restricted to singlet and doublet irreps. They map the gauge theory Hamiltonian restricted to the even Hilbert space sector to the Transverse Field Ising Model (TFIM) in a uniform magnetic field, then use Density Matrix Renormalization Group (DMRG) calculations to extract the low-lying energy spectrum, glueball masses, and static quark potential for various system sizes and coupling values.

## Key numbers

- √σ/m₁ = 0.249(1), where σ is the string tension between two static quarks and m₁ is the mass of the lightest glueball, extracted from DMRG calculations across multiple system sizes and coupling values
- m₂/m₁ = 1.614(2) at κc = 0.01, L = 96, consistent with the analytic prediction 2cos(π/5) ≈ 1.61803 for the E8 quantum field theory
- ζ = 1.995(7), the parameter relating energy and length scales extracted from the dispersion relation fit at L = 96, κc = 0.01
- String tension values: σ = 0.00671(1) at κc = 0.01, L = 96; σ = 0.0779(1) at κc = 0.1, L = 28-48; σ = 0.1628(1) at κc = 0.2, L = 24; σ = 0.2507(3) at κc = 0.3, L = 16

## Context

This work extends the qubit regularization program for quantum field theories introduced by Chandrasekharan (2025) and builds on prior studies of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theories on plaquette chains by Nyhegn (2020), Pradhan (2022), Yao (2023), and others. The mapping to the TFIM and the identification of E8 quantum field theory behavior follows the analytic predictions of Zamolodchikov (1989) for the scaling Ising model. This construction provides an explicit example of traditional renormalization group flow in a qubit-regularized gauge theory, enabling future quantum computation of non-Abelian gauge theory observables and demonstrating that asymptotic freedom can emerge from simple qubit models.
