# Pairing renormalization and regularization within the local density approximation

> We discuss methods used in mean-field theories to treat pairing correlations within the local density approximation. Pairing renormalization and regularization procedures are compared in spherical and deformed nuclei. Both prescriptions give fairly similar results, although the theoretical motivation, simplicity, and stability of the regularization procedure makes it a method of choice for future applications.

## Key claim

Pairing renormalization and regularization procedures give fairly similar results for spherical and deformed nuclei, although the regularization method is preferred due to its theoretical motivation, simplicity, and stability with respect to the cutoff energy.

## Method

The authors compared two approaches for treating pairing correlations within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework using Skyrme energy functionals: (1) the pairing renormalization procedure, which adjusts the pairing strength for each cutoff energy to reproduce a fixed average neutron pairing gap in 120Sn; and (2) the pairing regularization procedure, which removes the divergent part of the abnormal density using the single-particle Green's function in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Calculations were performed for spherical and deformed nuclei using the HFBRAD code (coordinate-space basis) and the HFBTHO code (transformed harmonic oscillator basis) with SLy4 and SkP Skyrme parameterizations and contact density-dependent pairing forces.

## Key numbers

- The total energy in 120Sn varies by a few hundred keV with cutoff energy when pairing renormalization is applied, compared to several MeV without renormalization
- For the regularization procedure in coordinate-space calculations, variations in total energy do not exceed 40 keV; in the harmonic oscillator expansion, variations are about 150 keV
- For deformed 110Zr, fluctuations in total energy with regularization are down to about 40 keV
- Maximum difference between renormalization and regularization for two-neutron separation energies in Sn isotopes is about 100 keV (volume pairing) and 300 keV (mixed pairing)
- Maximum difference in neutron pairing gaps between the two procedures is about 50 keV (volume pairing) and 100 keV (mixed pairing)
- For deformed Dy isotopes with mixed pairing, maximum difference in total energy is about 360 keV, in two-neutron separation energy 160 keV, and in pairing gaps 110 keV
- Differences in nuclear radii between the two procedures are negligible (about 0.01 fm for Sn, less than 0.005 fm for Dy)

## Context

This work extends the pairing regularization scheme developed by Bulgac and collaborators (2001-2004) and compares it with the phenomenological renormalization procedure established by Dobaczewski et al. (1984, 1996). The study demonstrates that both methods yield similar results for a wide range of spherical and deformed nuclei, validating previous physical conclusions obtained with the renormalization scheme. The regularization method enables cutoff-independent HFB calculations without readjusting the pairing strength, making it suitable for future applications in nuclear density functional theory, particularly for nuclei far from stability where pairing effects are crucial.
