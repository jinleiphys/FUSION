# Resolving the SELEX--LHCb Double-Charm Baryon Conflict: The Impact of Intrinsic Heavy-Quark Hadroproduction and Supersymmetric Light-Front Holographic QCD

> In this paper we show that the intrinsic heavy-quark QCD mechanism for the hadroproduction of heavy hadrons at large $x_F$ can resolve the apparent conflict between measurements of double-charm baryons by the SELEX fixed-target experiment and the LHCb experiment at the LHC collider. We show that both experiments are compatible, and that both results can be correct. The observed spectroscopy of double-charm hadrons is in agreement with the predictions of supersymmetric light front holographic QCD.

# Resolving the SELEX–LHCb Double-Charm Baryon Conflict: The Impact of Intrinsic Heavy-Quark Hadroproduction and Supersymmetric Light-Front Holographic QCD

## Key claim

The apparent conflict between SELEX and LHCb measurements of double-charm baryons can be resolved because the two experiments observed different states: SELEX observed the spin-1/2 |[dc]c⟩ state at high Feynman x_F via the intrinsic heavy-quark QCD mechanism, while LHCb observed the heavier |u(cc)⟩ state produced by gluon–gluon fusion at x_F near zero. Both results can be correct provided one accounts for the distinct production mechanisms and the mass difference arising from the different diquark spin configurations.

## Method

The authors compare the production rates and kinematic distributions of double-charm baryons from the SELEX fixed-target experiment (600 GeV/c beams on copper/diamond targets, x_F > 0.1) and the LHCb collider experiment (8 TeV and 13 TeV pp collisions, x_F < 0.15). They relate the SELEX production ratio to the double J/ψ production measured by the NA3 experiment, both attributed to the materialization of double-intrinsic charm Fock states |uudc‾cc‾c⟩ in the projectile hadron. The mass difference between the SELEX and LHCb states is interpreted using supersymmetric light-front holographic QCD (SUSY LFHQCD), which predicts that the mass of the spin-1/2 double-charm baryon should equal that of the h_c(1P) meson, while the |u(cc)⟩ state mass corresponds to the χ_c2(1P) meson mass, with additional hyperfine splitting from the light quark.

## Key numbers

- SELEX observed the Ξ_cc^+ (|dcc⟩) at mass 3520.2 ± 0.7 MeV/c², with a signal of 15.9 events over 6.1 ± 0.1 background in the Λ_c⁺K⁻π⁺ channel and 5.62 signal events over 1.38 ± 0.13 background in the pD⁺K⁻ channel.
- LHCb observed the Ξ_cc^{++} (|ucc⟩) at mass 3621.40 ± 0.72 (stat) ± 0.27 (sys) ± 0.14 (Λ_c) MeV/c², with 313 ± 33 events at 13 TeV (1.7 fb⁻¹) and 113 ± 21 events at 8 TeV (2 fb⁻¹).
- The mass difference between the SELEX and LHCb states is 103 MeV/c².
- The SELEX production ratio R_Λ_c^SELEX = σ(Ξ_cc⁺)·Br(Ξ_cc⁺→Λ_c⁺K⁻π⁺)/σ(Λ_c⁺) is approximately 0.012–0.014, or as low as (0.5–0.6)×10⁻³ when accounting for intrinsic charm reconstruction efficiency.
- The NA3 double J/ψ production ratio σ(ψψ)/σ(ψ) = (3 ± 1)×10⁻⁴, with total x_ψψ > 0.6 at 150 GeV/c and > 0.4 at 280 GeV/c, and total p_T,ψψ = 0.9 ± 0.1 GeV/c.
- The intrinsic charm mechanism predicts ⟨x_F(Ξ_cc)⟩ = 0.33, in agreement with the SELEX measured value ⟨x_F(Ξ_cc⁺)⟩ ∼ 0.33.
- The SELEX state has lifetime τ(Ξ_cc⁺) < 33 fs at 90% confidence level; the LHCb analysis was optimized for a lifetime of 333 fs.
- The radiative transition rate for |q(cc)⟩_{3/2⁺} → |[qc]c⟩_{1/2⁺} + γ is estimated at ∼0.06 eV (lifetime ∼10 fs) for the J^P=3/2⁺ case, and ∼0.0003 eV (lifetime ∼12000 fs) for the J^P=1/2⁺ case.

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic heavy-quark QCD mechanism developed by Brodsky and collaborators (1980s–2010s) to resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the SELEX fixed-target experiment (Mattson 2002, Ocherashvili 2004) and the LHCb collider experiment (Aaij 2017) regarding double-charm baryon production. The authors connect the SELEX results to the NA3 double J/ψ production data (Badier 1982, 1985), showing consistency between the two experiments via the double-intrinsic charm Fock state mechanism. The spectroscopy is interpreted using supersymmetric light-front holographic QCD (Dosch 2015), which predicts baryon–meson mass relations. This resolution enables future searches for exotic heavy-quark states at high x_F, particularly in fixed-target LHC experiments such as SMOG@LHCb and AFTER@LHC, and suggests that LHCb should search for radiative transitions between double-charm baryon states.
