# Angular momentum content of the $ρ(1450)$ from chiral lattice fermions

> We identify the chiral and angular momentum content of the leading quark-antiquark Fock component for the $ρ(770)$ and $ρ(1450)$ mesons using a two-flavor lattice simulation with dynamical Overlap Dirac fermions. We extract this information from the overlap factors of two interpolating fields with different chiral structure and from the unitary transformation between chiral and angular momentum basis. For the chiral content of the mesons we find that the $ρ(770)$ slightly favors the $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ chiral representation and the $ρ(1450)$ slightly favors the $(1/2,1/2)_b$ chiral representation. In the angular momentum basis the $ρ(770)$ is then a $^3S_1$ state, in accordance with the quark model. The $ρ(1450)$ is a $^3D_1$ state, showing that the quark model wrongly assumes the $ρ(1450)$ to be a radial excitation of the $ρ(770)$.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD with dynamical Overlap fermions, the authors find that the $\rho(1450)$ is predominantly a $^3D_1$ state, contradicting the quark model assumption that it is the radial excitation ($2\;^3S_1$) of the $\rho(770)$. This conclusion depends on the sign of the ratio of overlap factors for vector and pseudotensor interpolators, which was incorrectly reported in prior studies.

## Method
The authors performed a two-flavor lattice simulation with dynamical Overlap Dirac fermions on a $16^3 \times 32$ lattice with spacing $a \sim 0.12$ fm and pion mass $m_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV. They constructed an $8 \times 8$ correlation matrix using vector and pseudotensor interpolators with four different Gaussian smearing widths, solved the generalized eigenvalue problem to extract masses and overlap factors, and used the ratio $a_V/a_T$ of these overlap factors together with a unitary transformation between the chiral and angular momentum bases to determine the $^3S_1$ and $^3D_1$ content of each state.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV
- Lattice spacing: $a \sim 0.12$ fm
- At resolution scale $R = 0.9$ fm, the ratio $a_V/a_T = 1.26 \pm 0.05$ for the $\rho(770)$ and $a_V/a_T = -(0.88 \pm 0.18)$ for the $\rho(1450)$
- The $\rho(770)$ wavefunction: $+ (0.998 \pm 0.002) \ket{^3S_1} - (0.05 \pm 0.025) \ket{^3D_1}$
- The $\rho(1450)$ wavefunction: $- (0.106 \pm 0.09) \ket{^3S_1} - (0.994 \pm 0.005) \ket{^3D_1}$
- Four smearing widths used: Super-Narrow ($R = 0.245 \pm 0.003$ fm), Narrow ($R = 0.455 \pm 0.005$ fm), Wide ($R = 0.530 \pm 0.017$ fm), Ultra-Wide ($R = 0.890 \pm 0.050$ fm)

## Context
This work extends the lattice methodology of Glozman (2009) for extracting chiral and angular momentum content from overlap factors, but corrects an error in the sign of the ratio for the excited state that led to different conclusions in Glozman (2011). The result contradicts the potential constituent quark model assignment of the $\rho(1450)$ as a radial excitation of the $\rho(770)$ (Agashe 2014). The study also demonstrates that removing low-lying Dirac eigenmodes drives the $\rho(770)$ toward a pure $(1,0)\oplus(0,1)$ chiral representation and the $\rho(1450)$ toward a pure $(1/2,1/2)_b$ representation, with both states becoming degenerate under an emergent $SU(4)$ symmetry as previously reported by Glozman (2014, 2015).
