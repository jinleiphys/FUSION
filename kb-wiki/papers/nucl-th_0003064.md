# A two-pion exchange three-nucleon force based on a realistic pi-N interaction

> The contribution of a $ππ$-exchange three-body force to the three-nucleon binding energy is calculated in terms of a $πN$ amplitude. The latter is based on a meson-theoretical model of $πN$ interaction developed by the Jülich group. Similar to a previous study based on simple phenomenological $πN$ potentials a very small effect of the resulting three-body force is found. Possible origins of the two-orders-of-magnitude descrepancy between the present result and the values obtained for the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force are investigated. Evidence is provided that this discrepancy is most likely due to strikingly different off-shell properties of the $πN$ amplitudes underlying the two approaches.

## Key claim

The contribution of a $\pi\pi$-exchange three-nucleon force to the triton binding energy, calculated using a meson-theoretical $\pi N$ interaction from the Jülich group, is very small (a few keV), similar to previous results from phenomenological separable $\pi N$ potentials, despite the Jülich model having a much harder $\pi NN$ form factor (cutoff mass ~0.7 GeV) that was previously suspected to be the cause of the two-orders-of-magnitude discrepancy with the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force. The authors provide evidence that the discrepancy is instead due to strikingly different off-shell properties of the $\pi N$ amplitudes underlying the two approaches.

## Method

The authors used the Jülich meson-theoretical $\pi N$ model, which includes s-channel and u-channel nucleon and delta-isobar pole diagrams together with correlated $\pi\pi$ exchange in the $\sigma$ and $\rho$ channels, and unitarized via a relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They represented the $\pi N$ amplitude in separable form using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) method to match the structure of their three-nucleon code, and calculated the contribution of the $\pi\pi$-exchange three-body force to the triton binding energy in first-order perturbation theory using a triton wave function from the PEST potential (a separable representation of the Paris $NN$ potential). The off-shell properties of the $\pi N$ amplitudes from the Jülich model were compared to those underlying the Tucson-Melbourne force.

## Key numbers

- The total contribution of the $\pi\pi$-exchange three-body force to the triton binding energy for the Jülich $\pi N$ models 1, 2, and 2' lies within a range of 15 keV.
- For model 2', the $\pi NN$ form factor decreases by slightly less than 4% from the pion pole to $p^2=0$, corresponding to a monopole cutoff mass of about 708 MeV.
- For the separable model $PJ$ used by Saito and Afnan, the decrease is almost 20%, corresponding to a monopole cutoff mass of 317 MeV.
- The $\pi N$ $\Sigma$ term for the Jülich models is 66.4 MeV (model 1) and 65.6 MeV (model 2), compared to the empirical value of 60 MeV.
- The expansion energy for the separable representation was chosen at 930 MeV, corresponding to a center-of-mass momentum of about 138 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Saito and Afnan (1994, 1995), who used phenomenological separable $\pi N$ potentials and found very small three-body force contributions, by employing the more theoretically founded Jülich meson-exchange $\pi N$ model. It directly addresses the criticism by Murphy and Coon (1996) that the soft $\pi NN$ form factor from the separable potentials was responsible for the two-orders-of-magnitude discrepancy with the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force (Coon et al., 1979; Coon and Glöckle, 1981). The authors show that even with a hard form factor comparable to the Tucson-Melbourne force, the binding energy contribution remains small, and they identify the off-shell behavior of the $\pi N$ amplitudes, rather than the form factor softness, as the likely origin of the discrepancy. This work enables future studies to focus on the off-shell extrapolation of $\pi N$ amplitudes in constructing three-nucleon forces.
