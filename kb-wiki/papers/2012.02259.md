# Description of the multidimensional potential energy surface in fission of $^{252}$Cf and $^{258}$No

> The microscopic studies on nuclear fission require the evaluation of the potential energy surface as a function of the collective coordinates. A reasonable choice of constraints on multipole moments should be made to describe the topography of the surface completely within a reasonable amount of computing time. We present a detailed analysis of fission barriers in the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach with the D1S parametrization of the Gogny nucleon-nucleon interaction. Two heavy isotopes representing different spontaneous fission modes - $^{252}$Cf (asymmetric) and $^{258}$No (bimodal) - have been chosen for the analysis. We have shown the existence of complicated structures on the energy surface that can not be fully described in two-dimensional calculations. We analyze apparent problems that can be encountered in this type of calculations: multiple solutions for given constraints and transitions between various potential energy surfaces. We present possible solutions on how to deal with these issues.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-dimensional potential energy surfaces (PES) in the quadrupole-octupole plane are insufficient to fully describe fission in heavy nuclei, as multiple coexisting local minima with different nuclear shapes can appear at the same values of the quadrupole and octupole constraints, leading to ambiguous or erroneous interpretations of fission paths.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1S parametrization of the Gogny nucleon-nucleon interaction, with constraints on quadrupole (Q20), octupole (Q30), hexadecapole (Q40), and triaxial quadrupole (Q22) moments. They computed the potential energy surfaces for 252Cf and 258No in multiple deformation planes, including Q20-Q30, Q20-Q22, and Q30-Q40 sections at fixed Q20 values, and used the density distance technique to detect discontinuities between different solutions.

## Key numbers

- Triaxial deformation reduces the first fission barrier height by 4.7 MeV in 252Cf and 3.7 MeV in 258No
- The first fission barrier including triaxiality is 7.0 MeV for 252Cf and 7.3 MeV for 258No (experimental value for 252Cf is 5.3 MeV)
- The second minimum lies at 1.3 MeV above ground state at Q20 = 50 b in 252Cf and 0.2 MeV below ground state at Q20 = 55 b in 258No
- Theoretical spontaneous fission half-lives are log10(t1/2/s) = 8.74 for 252Cf and log10(t1/2/s) = -1.94 for 258No (calculated in axial regime)
- The second barrier in 252Cf is 4.5 MeV high at (Q20, Q30) = (100 b, 0 b3/2); the second barrier in 258No at Q20 = 65 b is only 0.7 MeV above the second minimum
- The asymmetric valley in 252Cf has a second saddle at 3.1 MeV above ground state (1.8 MeV above second minimum)
- Triaxial deformation along the fission path is relatively small, with gamma less than or equal to 12 degrees

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent fission studies using the Gogny D1S interaction (Berger 1990, Delaroche 2006, Baran 2015) and addresses the problem of multiple solutions on the PES previously noted by Dubray (2012). The analysis of 252Cf (asymmetric fission) and 258No (bimodal fission) reveals that the compact and symmetric elongated fission modes identified by Staszczak (2009, 2013) using Skyrme functionals also appear in Gogny-based calculations. The results enable more careful interpretation of multidimensional PES calculations and highlight the need for including hexadecapole and triaxial degrees of freedom to avoid fake saddle points and artificial transitions between fission valleys.
