# Neutron occupancies and single-particle energies across the stable tin isotopes

> The occupancies and vacancies of the valence neutron orbitals across the stable tin isotopic chain from $112\leq A\leq 124$ have been determined. These were inferred from the cross sections of neutron-adding and -removing reactions. In each case, the reactions were chosen to have good angular-momentum matching for transfer to the low- and high-$\ell$ orbitals present in this valence space. These new data are compared to older systematic studies. The effective single-neutron energies are determined by combining information from energy centroids determined from the adding and removing reactions. Two of the five orbitals are nearly degenerate, below $N=64$, and approximately two MeV more bound than the other three, which are also degenerate.

## Key claim

The neutron occupancies and effective single-particle energies for the five valence orbitals ($0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $0h_{11/2}$) across the stable tin isotopes from $^{112}$Sn to $^{124}$Sn have been determined with uncertainties of a few tenths of a nucleon, resolving discrepancies of 20-40% in prior systematic studies. Two of the five orbitals are nearly degenerate below $N=64$ and approximately two MeV more bound than the other three, which are also degenerate, though this conclusion depends on the normalization procedure and the assumption that 70% of the missing $0g_{7/2}$ strength for $A>116$ lies in the removal reaction.

## Method

Neutron-adding reactions [($d$,$p$) and ($\alpha$,$^3$He)] and neutron-removing reactions [($p$,$d$) and ($^3$He,$\alpha$)] were measured on seven even-mass tin targets at the Maier-Leibnitz Laboratorium (MLL) and the Tandem-Alto facility at IJClab, using beam energies chosen for good angular-momentum matching to both low-$\ell$ (15 MeV deuterons, 21 MeV protons) and high-$\ell$ (41 MeV $\alpha$, 36 MeV $^3$He) orbitals. Cross sections were analyzed with the exact finite-range DWBA code Ptolemy, and spectroscopic factors were normalized using the Macfarlane-French sum rules to obtain occupancies and effective single-particle energies from energy centroids.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factor for low-$\ell$ ($\ell=0,2$) transfer: $\overline{N_{02}} = 1.07(9)$, with an rms spread of about 10% across all seven targets
- Normalization factor for high-$\ell$ ($\ell=4,5$) transfer: $\overline{N_{45}} = 0.71(4)$; using only $0h_{11/2}$ strength gives $\overline{N_{5}} = 0.69(3)$
- Fractional occupancy of $1d_{5/2}$ orbital remains approximately constant at 0.77-0.86 across all isotopes (e.g., $^{112}$Sn: 0.79(5); $^{124}$Sn: 0.78(5))
- Fractional occupancy of $0h_{11/2}$ orbital increases from 0.11(5) in $^{112}$Sn to 0.61(5) in $^{124}$Sn
- Fractional occupancy of $2s_{1/2}$ orbital increases from 0.11(5) in $^{112}$Sn to 0.64(5) in $^{124}$Sn
- Fractional occupancy of $1d_{3/2}$ orbital increases from 0.07(5) in $^{112}$Sn to 0.51(5) in $^{124}$Sn
- Effective single-particle energies decrease by approximately 50-100 keV per additional neutron for all five orbitals
- Resolution achieved: approximately 10 keV FWHM for ($p$,$d$) and ($d$,$p$) reactions; 70 keV for ($\alpha$,$^3$He); 90 keV for ($^3$He,$\alpha$)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Schiffer (2012) on stable nickel isotopes to the tin isotopic chain, using matched reaction pairs to overcome the 20-40% discrepancies found in earlier systematic studies such as Cohen and Price (1961), Schneid (1967), Cavanagh (1970), and Fleming (1982). The new data provide consistent occupancies across the longest chain of stable, closed-shell, even-mass isotopes, enabling comparison with shell-model calculations by Qi (2012) and motivating future measurements on unstable tin isotopes at radioactive ion beam facilities.
