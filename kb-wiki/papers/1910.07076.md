# Neutrino Oscillations and Energy-Momentum Conservation

> It is proposed that the energy-momentum expectation values of flavor states should be identified with the missing energy-momentum of neutrino processes so that the conservation of energy-momentum can be strictly imposed. It is also observed that there is a plausible condition to express neutrino mixing angles in terms of neutrino masses without invoking symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the energy-momentum expectation values of flavor neutrino states (rather than the eigenvalues of mass eigenstates) should be identified with the missing energy-momentum in neutrino processes, allowing strict energy-momentum conservation to be imposed. It also observes a plausible condition under which neutrino mixing angles can be expressed in terms of neutrino masses without invoking symmetries, though this is presented as a conjecture rather than a proven result.

## Method

The author computes energy-momentum expectation values for flavor states as weighted averages over mass eigenstates using the PMNS matrix elements squared, then inverts the relation to determine mass eigenstate energies and momenta from measured flavor-state values. For neutrino oscillations, the evolution of each mass eigenstate is treated independently with different arrival times, leading to an oscillation probability that is shown to be equivalent to the standard formula after rescaling energies and masses. The mixing angles are then derived by minimizing the effective masses of flavor states with respect to the mixing parameters.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to enforce energy-momentum conservation in neutrino oscillations, specifically addressing the approach of Cohen, Glashow, and Ligeti (2008) who constructed entangled Hamiltonian eigenstates for neutrino production processes. The paper also contrasts with the standard oscillation derivation that assumes a common energy or momentum for all mass eigenstates, as discussed by Akhmedov (2009), Giunti (2001), and Cohen (2008). The proposed method enables a framework where neutrino masses can be determined unambiguously from experimental data without the ambiguity in the standard oscillation formula, and suggests a possible mass-mixing relation that could be tested against future precision measurements.
