# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Pion Interaction in Nuclear Matter

> This paper is devoted to the interplay between p-wave, s-wave pion-nucleon/ nucleus interaction and in-medium pion-pion interaction with special emphasis on the role of the nuclear pionic scalar density driving a large amount of chiral symmetry restoration. In particular we show that the $πNN$ coupling constant and the Goldberger-Treiman relation are preserved in the nuclear medium within certain conditions. We also discuss the related problem of the in-medium pion-pion strength function.

# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and Pion Interaction in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The paper shows that the πNN coupling constant and the Goldberger-Treiman relation are preserved in nuclear matter to one-pion loop order, but this result is strictly valid only when short-range correlations affecting nucleon observables are neglected. The in-medium modification of the ππ potential cannot give genuine contributions to the two-pion strength function, contradicting the earlier conclusion of Oset et al. (1997).

## Method

The authors employ a chiral SU(2)×SU(2) non-linear Lagrangian to treat pion propagation, pion-pion interaction, and chiral symmetry restoration simultaneously in nuclear matter. They calculate the in-medium pion scalar density ⟨Φ²⟩ using the dressed pion propagator that includes p-wave pion-nucleon interaction effects, then derive the effective πNN Lagrangian, the in-medium pion-pion potential, and the pion self-energy to one-pion loop order. The analysis incorporates the p-wave pion polarizability from nucleon-hole and delta-hole excitations, with the standard Lindhard function and short-range correlation parameters g'.

## Key numbers

- The pion cloud contribution to the pion-nucleon sigma term Σ^(π)_N is evaluated at about 30 MeV, with approximately 10 MeV from the delta sector and 15 MeV from the scalar quark density inside the nucleon.
- The isospin-symmetric scattering length derived from the pion cloud contribution is a₀ ≈ -0.012 m_π⁻¹, close to the experimental value.
- At normal nuclear density, the quenching of g_A (axial coupling constant) due to short-range correlations is a 10% effect.
- The free-space ππ scattering amplitude at threshold (Weinberg result) is M₀(s=4m²_π, u=t=0) = -7 m²_π/f²_π.
- The soft-pion theorem gives M₀(s=u=m²_π, t=0) = m²_π/f²_π = Σ_{ππ}/f²_π.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective pionic modes (pisobars) proposed by Chanfray et al. (1994) and confirmed by Guichon et al. (1995) and Udagawa et al. (1995) in charge exchange reactions. It addresses the interplay between p-wave and s-wave pion-nucleon interactions in the context of chiral symmetry restoration, building on prior work by Oset et al. (1997) on vertex corrections to in-medium pion-pion interaction. The results are important for understanding the in-medium NN interaction from correlated two-pion exchange (discussed by Durso et al., 1996; Rapp et al., 1996) and for interpreting two-pion invariant mass spectra in A(π, 2π) reactions observed by the CHAOS collaboration. The paper enables future full calculations of the pion-nucleus sigma term using realistic longitudinal spin-isospin response functions including particle-hole, delta-hole, and 2p-2h sectors.
