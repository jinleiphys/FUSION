# Universal Relations for Elastic Hybrid Stars and Quark Stars

> Some compact stars may contain deconfined quark matter, forming hybrid stars or quark stars. If the quark matter forms an inhomogeneous condensate in the crystalline color superconducting phase, its rigidity may be high enough to noticeably alter the stellar properties. In this paper, we investigate whether these elastic stars follow the universal relations, i.e., relations insensitive to equations of state, that have been well established for fluid stars. We improve upon previous studies by allowing quark matter in the background, static, and spherically symmetric configuration to be sheared. Such background shear can be treated in the form of an effective pressure anisotropy. We then calculate the moment of inertia $I$, tidal deformability $λ_2$, and spin-induced quadrupole moment $Q$ of these models with pressure anisotropy. The $I$-$λ_2$-$Q$ universal relations for the elastic hybrid (quark) star models are valid up to a variation of $\approx2\,(3)\%$, larger than that for typical fluid star models, when the maximal magnitude of quark matter shear modulus is considered in the crystalline color superconducting phase from realistic calculations. The uncertainty in universal relations related to the stellar compactness for these elastic star models, on the other hand, remain comparable to those for typical fluid star models. Our results demonstrate the validity of universal relations for hybrid stars and quark stars with a realistic degree of pressure anisotropy due to the crystalline color superconducting quark matter.

# Universal Relations for Elastic Hybrid Stars and Quark Stars

## Key claim

The I-lambda_2-Q universal relations for elastic hybrid stars and quark stars with crystalline color superconducting quark matter are valid up to a variation of approximately 2% for hybrid stars and 3% for quark stars, which is larger than the typical variation for fluid star models, when the maximal magnitude of the quark matter shear modulus is considered. The uncertainty in universal relations involving stellar compactness for these elastic star models remains comparable to those for typical fluid star models.

## Method

The authors construct static, spherically symmetric background configurations of elastic hybrid stars and quark stars in general relativity using the constant speed of sound (CSS) parametrization for hybrid equations of state via Maxwell construction, with the APR or STOS equation of state for nuclear matter. Background shear from the crystalline color superconducting quark matter is treated as an effective pressure anisotropy, and the moment of inertia, tidal deformability, and spin-induced quadrupole moment are calculated by imposing perturbations to the metric up to second order in slow rotation or first order in small tidal deformation.

## Key numbers

- The shear modulus of crystalline color superconducting quark matter is modeled as mu = kappa * sqrt(rho), with a maximum kappa value of 7 x 10^26 cm^(1/2) g^(1/2) s^(-2), corresponding to a gap parameter Delta = 25 MeV
- The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff limit for elastic hybrid stars is enhanced by approximately 5% when the maximum shear modulus is considered compared to the fluid limit
- The I-lambda_2-Q universal relations for elastic hybrid star models show equation-of-state variation of approximately 2%, and for elastic quark star models approximately 3%
- The canonical hybrid star model uses transition pressure p_trans = 6 x 10^33 erg cm^(-3), energy gap Delta_rho = 2 x 10^13 g cm^(-3), and quark matter sound speed squared c_QM^2 = 0.33 c^2
- The transition density for the canonical hybrid star model on the nuclear matter side is 1.34 rho_0, where rho_0 = 2.6 x 10^14 g cm^(-3) is nuclear saturation density
- The canonical quark star model uses Delta_rho = 3.0 x 10^14 g cm^(-3) and c_QM^2 = 0.30 c^2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of universal relations for fluid neutron stars (Yagi 2013, Yagi 2016) and anisotropic neutron stars using phenomenological anisotropy models (Yagi 2015) by incorporating a physically motivated shear modulus from crystalline color superconducting quark matter (Mannarelli 2007). The results differ qualitatively from earlier work on elastic stars with unsheared background configurations (Dong 2024), where the tidal deformability was significantly reduced while moment of inertia and compactness remained unchanged, leading to deviations of tens of percent; here, all quantities shift systematically, partially canceling the deviation in compactness-related relations. The findings enable future development of full perturbation equations for polar deformations based on relativistic elastic theory.
