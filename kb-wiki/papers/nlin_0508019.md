# Nodal Domain Distribution for a Nonintegrable Two-Dimensional Anharmonic Oscillator

> We investigate the transition from integrable to chaotic dynamics in the quantum mechanical wave functions from the point of view of the nodal structure by employing a two dimensional quartic oscillator. We find that the number of nodal domains is drastically reduced as the dynamics of the system changes from integrable to nonintegrable, and then gradually increases as the system becomes chaotic. The number of nodal intersections with the classical boundary as a function of the level number shows a characteristic dependence on the dynamics of the system, too. We also calculate the area distribution of nodal domains and study the emergence of the power law behavior with the Fisher exponent in the chaotic limit.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the transition from integrable to chaotic dynamics by examining nodal domain statistics of wave functions in a two-dimensional quartic oscillator, finding that the number of nodal domains drops sharply when a small nonintegrable perturbation is introduced and then gradually increases toward the chaotic limit, but the authors note that the average number of inner nodal domains at the largest studied coupling (\(k=0.6\)) still fluctuates between two asymptotic values and does not appear to have converged.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the Hamiltonian \(H = \frac12(p_x^2 + \alpha p_y^2) + \frac12(x^4 + y^4) - k x^2 y^2\) (with \(\alpha = 1.01\)) in a large harmonic oscillator basis truncated at \(n_x + n_y \le 200\), obtaining wave functions for eigenstates symmetric under \(x\)- and \(y\)-reflection. They determine nodal domains by covering the classically allowed region with a square mesh, using the Hoshen-Kopelman algorithm to count connected regions of the same sign, and refine the mesh until convergence is reached. The parameter \(k\) controls the dynamics: \(k=0.0\) is integrable, and the system becomes increasingly chaotic as \(k\) increases to \(0.6\).

## Key numbers

*   At \(k=0.0\) (integrable), the average of \(N_i/i'\) (total nodal domains per corrected level number) is \(0.37\) with standard deviation \(0.17\) for levels \(1 \le i \le 1000\).
*   At \(k=0.1\) (weakly nonintegrable), the average of \(N_i/i'\) drops to \(0.049\) with standard deviation \(0.035\).
*   At \(k=0.6\) (almost chaotic), the average of \(N_i/i'\) rises to \(0.079\) with standard deviation \(0.029\).
*   The average of inner nodal domains per corrected level number (\(N^{\rm in}_i/i'\)) is \(0.29\) at \(k=0.0\), drops to \(0.014\) at \(k=0.1\), and rises to \(0.060\) at \(k=0.6\).
*   The estimated error from boundary effects on the number of nodal domains is \(3.1\%\) for \(k=0.1\) and \(2.2\%\) for \(k=0.6\).
*   The semiclassical model predicts an asymptotic average \(\langle N_i / i \rangle \approx 0.055\) for the quartic oscillator and \(\approx 0.062\) for a billiard model.

## Context

This work extends the study of nodal domain statistics, previously applied to the extremes of completely integrable (separable) billiards and chaotic billiards by Blum (2002) and Bogomolny (2002), to a nonintegrable system where a single parameter interpolates between these regimes. The authors develop a semiclassical model that generalizes the percolation-like approach of Bogomolny (2002) to systems with a soft potential, and they compare their numerical results for the quartic oscillator to the billiard predictions. The findings enable future studies of how the reduction factor and amplitude mixing width depend on the coupling parameter \(k\), and whether the average nodal domain number converges to the predicted quartic oscillator value at higher energies.
