# On the Nuclear Modification Factor at RHIC and LHC

> We show that pQCD factorization incorporated with pre-haronization energy-loss effect naturally leads to flatness of the nuclear modification factor R_{AA} for produced hadrons at high transverse momentum p_T. We consider two possible scenarios for the pre-hadronization: In scenario 1, the produced gluon propagates through dense QCD medium and loses energy. In scenario 2, all gluons first decay to quark-antiquark pairs and then each pair loses energy as propagating through the medium. We show that the estimates of the energy-loss in these two different models lead to very close values and is able to explain the suppression of high-p_T hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC. We show that the onset of the flatness of R_{AA} for the produced hadron in central collisions at midrapidity is about p_T\approx 15 and 25 GeV at RHIC and the LHC energies, respectively. We show that the smallness (R_{AA}<0.5) and the high-p_T flatness of R_{AA} obtained from the k_T factorization supplemented with the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation is rather generic and it does not strongly depend on the details of the BK solutions. We show that energy-loss effect reduces the nuclear modification factor obtained from the k_T factorization about 30÷50% at moderate p_T.

## Key claim

The paper shows that pQCD factorization incorporated with pre-hadronization energy-loss effects naturally leads to the flatness of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for produced hadrons at high transverse momentum \(p_T\), with the condition that the onset of flatness occurs at \(p_T \approx 15\) GeV at RHIC and \(p_T \approx 25\) GeV at LHC energies, and that the smallness (\(R_{AA} < 0.5\)) and flatness obtained from \(k_T\) factorization supplemented with the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation is rather generic and does not strongly depend on the details of the BK solutions.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for hadron production in nucleus-nucleus collisions using \(k_T\) factorization supplemented with the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation for the initial parton production stage. They then incorporate pre-hadronization energy-loss effects via two scenarios: in scenario 1, the produced gluon propagates through the dense QCD medium and loses energy; in scenario 2, all gluons first decay to quark-antiquark pairs, and each pair loses energy as it propagates through the medium. The average energy loss is computed by summing over inelastic interactions with nucleons in the nuclear medium, where each inelastic interaction reduces the energy of the propagating dipole by a factor \(z_h\), and the resulting \(R_{AA}\) is expressed in terms of the ratio of scaling functions evaluated at modified transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

* The onset of flatness of \(R_{AA}\) for produced hadrons in central collisions at midrapidity is about \(p_T \approx 15\) GeV at RHIC and \(p_T \approx 25\) GeV at the LHC.
* The energy-loss effect reduces the nuclear modification factor obtained from \(k_T\) factorization by about \(30 \div 50\%\) at moderate \(p_T\).
* The nuclear modification factor satisfies \(R_{AA} < 0.5\) at high \(p_T\).
* For the produced gluon, the perturbative regime is reached for \(p_T > 40\) GeV at RHIC and \(p_T > 60\) GeV at the LHC, which translates to hadron transverse momenta \(p_T \geq 40 z_A \approx 15\) GeV at RHIC and \(p_T \geq 60 z_A \approx 25\) GeV at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach and \(k_T\) factorization framework to explain the observed suppression and flatness of \(R_{AA}\) at high \(p_T\) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC, a feature that contradicts the naive pQCD factorization expectation that \(R_{AA} \to 1\). The authors build on prior work on the BK equation, the KLN saturation model (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi), and the LR approach (Levin, Rezaeian) for hadron production in \(pp\) collisions, and they show that the additional energy loss in nuclear collisions relative to proton-proton collisions is the main source of suppression. The results enable predictions for the LHC and suggest that the pre-hadronization energy-loss mechanism is a generic feature that does not depend strongly on the details of the BK solutions.
