# Electromagnetic corrections to the hadronic phase shifts in low energy pi+p elastic scattering

> We calculate the s and p-wave electromagnetic corrections which must be subtracted from the nuclear phase shifts obtained from the analysis of low energy pi+p elastic scattering data, in order to obtain hadronic phase shifts. We compare our results with earlier calculations and estimate the uncertainties in the corrections.

## Key claim

The authors calculate electromagnetic corrections for s- and p-wave pi+p elastic scattering using a potential model approach with relativised Schrodinger equations, finding that these corrections must be subtracted from nuclear phase shifts to obtain hadronic phase shifts. The paper explicitly acknowledges that the resulting "hadronic phase shifts" are not truly hadronic quantities because they are obtained using physical (not hadronic) masses of pi+ and p, and that any attempt to completely purge experimental data of all electromagnetic effects would be "very speculative."

## Method

The authors constructed energy-independent hadronic potentials for the s-wave (0+) and p-waves (1+, 1-) that reproduce hadronic phase shifts up to T_pi = 100 MeV, then added electromagnetic potentials (point Coulomb, extended charge, relativistic spin-orbit, and vacuum polarisation) in relativised Schrodinger equations. An iterative procedure was used: hadronic potentials were fitted to hadronic phase shifts, electromagnetic corrections were calculated from the full potentials, and a phase-shift analysis determined new hadronic phase shifts, repeated until stable values were achieved. The electromagnetic corrections were split into four components (point Coulomb, extended charge, relativistic, and vacuum polarisation) to assess their relative importance.

## Key numbers

- At T_pi = 100 MeV, the s-wave correction C_0+ = 0.123 +/- 0.018 degrees
- At T_pi = 100 MeV, the p_1/2-wave correction C_1- = 0.020 +/- 0.005 degrees
- At T_pi = 100 MeV, the p_3/2-wave correction C_1+ = -0.547 +/- 0.088 degrees
- At T_pi = 10 MeV, C_0+ = 0.081 +/- 0.003 degrees, C_1+ = -0.023 +/- 0.000 degrees, C_1- = 0.005 +/- 0.000 degrees
- The point Coulomb contribution dominates C_0+, ranging from 0.093 degrees at 10 MeV to 0.183 degrees at 100 MeV
- For C_1+, the point Coulomb contribution ranges from -0.023 degrees at 10 MeV to -0.464 degrees at 100 MeV
- The vacuum polarisation contribution is negligible (0.000 degrees) for C_0+ and C_1- at all energies shown
- The relativistic contribution to C_1+ reaches -0.119 degrees at 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the potential theory approach initiated by Oades and Rasche (1970s) and applied by Zimmermann (1980s), recalculating electromagnetic corrections for pi+p scattering to lower energies (down to 10 MeV) where new data from pion factories exist. The authors compare their approach to the dispersion theory method of Tromborg et al. (NORDITA group), noting that the dispersion method cannot give absolute values of electromagnetic corrections because it omits contributions from t- and u-channel exchange beyond u-channel nucleon exchange. The results enable future phase-shift analyses of low-energy pi+p data to extract hadronic phase shifts with controlled uncertainties, particularly important for the resonant p_3/2 wave where the correction (-0.547 degrees at 100 MeV) is large compared to the statistical error from phase-shift analysis (0.05 degrees).
