# Consistent Implementation of Finite-Range Terms into Hydrodynamics

> Non-zero-range interactions are often incorporated into mean field theories through gradient terms. Here, a formalism is developed to incorporate such terms into hydrodynamics. These terms alter expressions for the entropy, chemical potential, temperature and the stress-energy tensor. The formalism respects local conservation of energy, charge and entropy. The formalism leads to static solutions where the temperature, chemical potential and hydrodynamic acceleration all vanish, even when the density profile might be non-uniform. Profiles for a phase boundary and for correlation functions are calculated to illustrate the gradient modifications for various thermodynamic quantities. Also, for hydrodynamic calculations that add thermal noise to generate density-density correlations of the desired strength, an additional noise term is derived so that, at equilibrium, correlations are generated with both the correct size and length scale.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism to incorporate finite-range (gradient) interactions into hydrodynamics in a thermodynamically consistent way, such that local conservation of energy, charge, and entropy is respected and static solutions have vanishing temperature, chemical potential, and hydrodynamic acceleration gradients even with non-uniform density profiles.

## Method

The author starts from an ansatz for the entropy density that includes a gradient term proportional to \(\kappa \rho \nabla^2 \rho\), then derives the corresponding modifications to the temperature, chemical potential, stress-energy tensor, momentum density, and diffusion current by requiring local entropy conservation and consistency under rotations and boosts. The formalism is applied to two static examples: a liquid-gas phase boundary using a Van der Waals equation of state, and density-density correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- For the liquid-gas interface at \(T = 2T_c/3\) (with \(T_c = (8/27)a\rho_s\)), the density profile is calculated using \(\kappa = 1\), \(a = 1\), and \(\rho_s = 1\); the coordinate \(x\) is in units of \(\sqrt{\kappa/a}\).
- The critical temperature for the Van der Waals equation of state is given as \(T_c = (8/27)a\rho_s\).
- The density-density correlation function integrates to the susceptibility \(\chi = \frac{1}{V}\langle (Q - \langle Q\rangle)^2\rangle\).

## Context

This work extends mean-field Landau theory treatments of gradient terms, such as those applied to nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions by Ravenhall (1984) and to the chiral transition by Steinheimer (2012, 2013) and Randrup (2010), but where thermodynamic quantities were not consistently altered. The formalism enables consistent inclusion of gradient effects in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for modeling thermal fluctuations, phase boundaries, and correlations near critical points, and provides a noise term for stochastic hydrodynamics that generates correlations with correct size and length scale.
