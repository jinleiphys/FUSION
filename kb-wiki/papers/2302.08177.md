# $K^{*0}$ meson production using a transport and a statistical hadronization model at energies covered by the RHIC beam energy scan

> In this paper, we discuss the centrality and energy dependence of $K^{*0}$ resonance production using ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) and thermal models. The $K^{*0}/K$ ratios obtained from the UrQMD and thermal models are compared with measurements done by the STAR experiment in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV. The $K^{*0}/K$ ratio from the thermal model is consistent with data in most-peripheral collisions, however it overpredicts the ratio in central Au+Au collisions. This could be due to the fact that the thermal model does not have a hadronic rescattering phase, which is expected to be dominant in more central collisions. Furthermore, we have studied the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio from UrQMD by varying the hadron propagation time ($τ$) within the range 5 to 50 fm/c. It was found that the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio decreases with increasing $τ$. Comparison between data and UrQMD suggest, one needs to consider a $τ$ $\approx$ 10-50 fm/c to explain data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7-39 GeV in Au+Au collisions. We also predict the rapidity distribution of $K^{*0}$ from UrQMD which could be measured in the STAR beam energy scan phase II (BES-II) program.

## Key claim

The thermal model, which lacks a hadronic rescattering phase, overpredicts the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio in central Au+Au collisions while agreeing with data in peripheral collisions; the UrQMD transport model can explain the data across centralities only when the hadron propagation time ($\tau$) is set to approximately 10-50 fm/c, suggesting that hadronic rescattering of decay daughters is the dominant mechanism suppressing the $K^{*0}$ yield in central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 3.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, varying the hadron propagation time $\tau$ from 5 to 50 fm/c to control the duration of hadronic interactions. They reconstructed $K^{*0}$ mesons via the $K^{*0} \rightarrow K^{\pm}\pi^{\mp}$ decay channel using the invariant mass method with combinatorial background subtraction, identical to the experimental procedure used by STAR. Separately, they calculated $K^{*0}/K$ ratios from a statistical thermal model using the THERMUS package in the grand-canonical ensemble, with freeze-out parameters ($T$, $\mu_B$, $\mu_Q$, $\mu_S$) taken from fits to STAR data on stable hadron yields.

## Key numbers

- $K^{*0}$ lifetime is approximately 4.16 fm/c (from Particle Data Group).
- The $K^{*0}/K$ ratio from UrQMD decreases with increasing $\tau$; a $\tau$ of approximately 10-50 fm/c is needed to explain STAR data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7-39 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 GeV, UrQMD results with $\tau$ = 50 fm/c show better consistency with data than $\tau$ = 20 fm/c; at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 GeV, UrQMD calculations with $\tau$ = 20 fm/c explain the data.
- The $\phi$ meson shows only a 1-2% change in reconstructed mass and width compared to PDG values in UrQMD, consistent with its longer lifetime making it less affected by the hadronic medium.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from UrQMD particle yield ratios does not change appreciably with collision centrality.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements by the STAR Collaboration (STAR 2002, 2005, 2008, 2011) and other experiments (PHENIX 2014, NA49 2011, NA61 2020-2021, ALICE 2012-2022) that showed rescattering dominates the late-stage hadronic medium in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The study uses the recently published STAR BES data on $K^{*0}$ production (STAR 2020) to constrain the hadronic cascade lifetime in UrQMD, providing predictions for the STAR beam energy scan phase II (BES-II) program, which has collected high-statistics data with upgraded detectors allowing measurements at wider rapidity ($|y| < 1.5$). The results enable future comparisons that can clarify the rapidity dependence of hadronic rescattering effects.
