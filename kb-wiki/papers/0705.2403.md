# Chromomagnetic Instability and Induced Magnetic Field in Neutral Two-Flavor Color Superconductivity

> We find that the chromomagnetic instability existing in neutral two- flavor color superconductivity at moderate densities is removed by the formation of an inhomogeneous condensate of charged gluons and the corresponding induction of a magnetic field. It is shown that this inhomogeneous ground state is energetically favored over a homogeneous one. The spontaneous induction of a magnetic field in a color superconductor at moderate densities can be of interest for the astrophysics of compact stellar objects exhibiting strong magnetic fields as magnetars.

## Key claim
The chromomagnetic instability in neutral two-flavor color superconductivity at moderate densities is removed by the formation of an inhomogeneous condensate of charged gluons, which spontaneously induces a rotated magnetic field; this inhomogeneous ground state is energetically favored over a homogeneous one, but the analysis is performed near the critical point where the effective Meissner mass is very small.

## Method
The authors start from the mean-field effective action of the 2SC gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, expand the logarithm of the quark determinant to second order in gauge fields in the hard-dense-loop approximation, and restrict to the gauge sector depending on charged gluon fields and the rotated electromagnetic field. Near the critical point δμ = Δ/√2, where the Meissner mass squared becomes very small, they propose an ansatz for an inhomogeneous gluon condensate in the (x,y)-plane and solve the minimum equations using a linear approximation that yields a Bessel function solution for the condensate and an induced magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- The Meissner mass squared is given by m_M^2 = (2α_s μ̄^2)/(3π)[1 - 2δμ^2/Δ^2], where μ̄ = μ - μ_e/6 + μ_8/3 and α_s ≡ g^2/(4π).
- The instability region for the charged gluon doublet K_μ occurs when Δ > δμ > Δ/√2, i.e., the pairing mismatch δμ lies between Δ/√2 and Δ.
- The induced magnetic field magnitude is estimated to be in the range ∼10^16–10^17 G when δμ = Δ in the neutral 2SC model.
- The condensation free-energy density for the inhomogeneous gluon condensate is F̄_g ≈ -2(g^2 - q̃^2)|Ḡ|^4, which is negative because the strong coupling g dominates over the rotated electromagnetic coupling q̃.

## Context
This work extends the investigation of chromomagnetic instabilities in neutral two-flavor color superconductivity, first identified by Huang et al. (2002) and Igor et al. (2005), where gluon modes become tachyonic due to pairing mismatch from neutrality and β-equilibrium constraints. It contrasts with the homogeneous gluon condensate proposal of Miransky et al. (2005), which produces no magnetic field; the authors show their inhomogeneous condensate with induced magnetic field is energetically favored near the transition point. The spontaneous generation of a magnetic field in a color superconductor at moderate densities offers a new mechanism for producing strong magnetic fields in magnetars, potentially addressing criticisms of the standard magnetohydrodynamic dynamo explanation for these objects.
