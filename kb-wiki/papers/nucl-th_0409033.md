# Nuclear muon-positron conversion mediated by Majorana neutrinos

> We study lepton number violating (LNV) process of muon-positron conversion in nuclei mediated by the exchange of light and heavy Majorana neutrinos. Nuclear structure calculations have been carried out for the case of experimentally interesting nucleus 48Ti in the framework of renormalized proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation. We demonstrate that the imaginary part of the amplitude of light Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism gives an appreciable contribution to the muon-positron conversion rate. This specific feature is absent in the allied case of neutrinoless double beta decay. Using the present neutrino oscillations, tritium beta decay, accelerator and cosmological data we derived the limits on the effective masses of light <m>_{μe} and heavy <M_N^{-1}>_{μe} neutrinos. The expected rates of muon-positron nuclear conversion, corresponding to these limits, were found to be so small that even within a distant future the muon-positron conversion experiments will hardly be able to detect the neutrino signal. Therefore, searches for this LNV process can only rely on the presence of certain physics beyond the trivial extension of the Standard Model by inclusion of massive Majorana neutrinos.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the imaginary part of the amplitude for light Majorana neutrino exchange gives an appreciable contribution to the muon-positron conversion rate, a feature absent in neutrinoless double beta decay; however, the expected rates corresponding to current limits on neutrino masses are so small that even distant-future experiments will hardly detect the neutrino signal, so searches for this lepton number violating process can only rely on physics beyond the trivial extension of the Standard Model by inclusion of massive Majorana neutrinos.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear structure calculations for the muon-positron conversion process on the nucleus 48Ti using the renormalized proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA). They derived the amplitude and rate for both light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms, including the previously neglected imaginary part of the light neutrino exchange amplitude, and used closure approximation with an average intermediate nuclear excitation energy. The effective neutrino masses were constrained using neutrino oscillation data, tritium beta decay results, accelerator limits, and cosmological data.

## Key numbers

- Experimental upper limit on the muon-positron conversion branching ratio for 48Ti: R(mu e+)(Ti) < 4.3 x 10^{-12} (from PSI, Doh93)
- Muon atomic binding energy for 48Ti: epsilon_b = 1.45 MeV
- Neutrino mixing angles (best fit): sin^2 theta_{12} = 0.30, sin^2 theta_{13} = 0.006, sin^2 theta_{23} = 0.52
- Mass-squared differences: Delta m^2_{sol} = 6.9 x 10^{-5} eV^2, Delta m^2_{atm} = 2.6 x 10^{-3} eV^2
- Effective light neutrino mass for normal hierarchy: |<m_nu>_{mu e}| ~ (0.35 - 5.3) x 10^{-3} eV
- Effective light neutrino mass for inverted hierarchy: |<m_nu>_{mu e}| ~ (0.3 - 3.3) x 10^{-2} eV
- Quasi-degenerate hierarchy limit from cosmological data: |<m_nu>_{mu e}| less than or similar to 0.16 eV, m_0 < 0.23 eV (Spe03)
- Quasi-degenerate hierarchy limit from Troitsk tritium experiment: |<m_nu>_{mu e}| less than or similar to 1.46 eV, m_0 < 2.05 eV
- Heavy neutrino mass limit from LEP: M_N >= 39.5 GeV, giving |<M_N^{-1}>_{mu e}| <= (39.5 GeV)^{-1}
- For 0nu beta beta decay: |<m_nu>_{ee}| <= 0.55 eV, |<M_N^{-1}>_{ee}|^{-1} >= 9 x 10^7 GeV
- Dipole form factor parameters: Lambda_V = 0.71 GeV, Lambda_A = 1.09 GeV
- Weak axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.254

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of muon-positron conversion in nuclei, which is closely related to neutrinoless double beta decay (studied by Doi (1985), Faessler (1998), and Suhonen (1998)) but with important differences including the presence of a singular behavior in the light neutrino exchange amplitude giving a large imaginary part. The calculations for 48Ti are directly relevant to the SINDRUM II experiment at PSI (Doh93) and the planned PRIME experiment (Kuno), as well as the MECO experiment at Brookhaven (Mol02) using an aluminum target. The extremely low predicted rates imply that observation of this process would require new physics beyond the minimal extension of the Standard Model with massive Majorana neutrinos, such as R-parity violating supersymmetric models or leptoquark extensions.
