# Effects of nucleon resonances on $η$ photoproduction off the neutron reexamined

> We investigate $η$ photoproduction off the neutron target, i.e., $γn \to ηn$, employing an effective Lagrangian method combining with a Regge approach. As a background, we consider nucleon exchange in the $s$-channel diagram and $ρ$- and $ω$-meson Regge trajectories in the $t$ channel. The role of nucleon resonances given in the Review of Particle Data Group in the range of $W \approx 1500 - 2100$ MeV and the narrow nucleon resonance $N(1685,1/2^+)$ is extensively studied. The numerical results of the total and differential cross sections, double polarization observable $E$, and helicity-dependent cross sections $σ_{1/2}$, $σ_{3/2}$ are found to be in qualitative agreement with the recent A2 experimental data. The predictions of the beam asymmetry are also given.

## Key claim

The narrow bump-like structure observed in $\eta$ photoproduction off the neutron around $W \sim 1.68$ GeV can be described by explicitly including the narrow nucleon resonance $N(1685,1/2^+)$ within an effective Lagrangian approach combined with a Regge model, provided that the coupling constant $g_{\eta N N(1685,1/2^+)}$ is fitted to reproduce the A2 experimental data.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian method combined with a Regge approach for the $\gamma n \to \eta n$ reaction. Background contributions include nucleon exchange in the $s$-channel and $\rho$- and $\omega$-meson Regge trajectories in the $t$-channel, while fifteen nucleon resonances from the PDG in the range $W \approx 1500-2100$ MeV plus the narrow $N(1685,1/2^+)$ resonance are included in the $s$-channel. The magnetic transition moments are determined from PDG helicity amplitudes, and the strong coupling constants for $\eta N N^*$ vertices are extracted from partial decay widths and quark model predictions.

## Key numbers

- The narrow resonance $N(1685,1/2^+)$ is assigned a width of 30 MeV and a photo-coupling constant $h_1 = -0.315$ taken from Yang (2018)
- The fitted strong coupling constant $g_{\eta N N(1685,1/2^+)}$ is 1.4, corresponding to a branching ratio Br($N(1685,1/2^+) \to \eta N$) = 5.5%
- The product $\sqrt{\mathrm{Br}(N(1685) \to \eta N)} \, A_{1/2}^n$ is computed as $12.1 \times 10^{-3} \, \mathrm{GeV}^{-1/2}$, close to the A2 upper limit of $(12.3 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-3} \, \mathrm{GeV}^{-1/2}$
- The ratio Br($N(1685) \to K \Lambda$) / Br($N(1685) \to \eta N$) is found to be approximately 0.15, consistent with the Bn-Ga bound of less than 1/4
- The $\rho$-meson radiative decay width is $\Gamma_{\rho \to \eta \gamma} = 45.5$ keV and $\Gamma_{\omega \to \eta \gamma} = 3.82$ keV, giving coupling constants $g_{\rho\eta\gamma} = 0.91$ and $g_{\omega\eta\gamma} = 0.24$
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron is $\kappa_n = -1.91$

## Context

This work reexamines the "neutron anomaly", the narrow bump-like structure near $W \sim 1.68$ GeV seen only in $\eta$ photoproduction off the neutron, following earlier studies by Choi (2005, 2007) and Fix (2007) that interpreted it as the narrow $N(1685,1/2^+)$ resonance predicted by the chiral quark-soliton model. It contrasts with alternative interpretations from the Bonn-Gatchina partial-wave analysis group (Anisovich 2008, 2015, 2017) who attribute the structure to interference between $N(1535,1/2^-)$ and $N(1650,1/2^-)$, and from the EtaMAID model (Kashevarov 2018) which explains it without $N(1685,1/2^+)$ via interference of other resonances. The results enable further tests of the $N(1685,1/2^+)$ hypothesis through polarization observables and combined analyses with $K^0\Lambda$ photoproduction.
