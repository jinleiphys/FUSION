# Investigating Neutron Polarizabilities through Compton Scattering on $^3$He

> We examine manifestations of neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities in coherent Compton scattering from the Helium-3 nucleus. We calculate $γ^3$He elastic scattering observables using chiral perturbation theory to next-to-leading order (${\mathcal O}(e^2 Q)$). We find that the unpolarized differential cross section can be used to measure neutron electric and magnetic polarizabilities, while two double-polarization observables are sensitive to different linear combinations of the four neutron spin polarizabilities. [Note added in 2018] An erratum for this paper has been posted as arXiv:1804.01206. Overall conclusions are unchanged, but quantitative results are affected appreciably.

## Key claim

The authors argue that neutron electromagnetic polarizabilities (electric α, magnetic β, and spin polarizabilities γ₁–γ₄) can be extracted from Compton scattering on ³He, but the quantitative results were affected appreciably by a later erratum (2018), even though the overall conclusions remained unchanged.

## Method

The authors calculated γ³He elastic scattering observables using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to next-to-leading order (NLO, O(e²Q)). They employed a ³He wavefunction obtained from the Idaho-N³LO chiral potential together with the NNLO chiral three-nucleon force, and computed the scattering amplitude on a partial-wave Jacobi basis. The operator included both one-body γN amplitudes and two-body mechanisms where photons couple to a single pion exchange inside the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- χPT prediction at O(e²Q): α⁽ⁿ⁾ = 10 β⁽ⁿ⁾ = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- χPT prediction at O(e²Q): γ₁⁽ⁿ⁾ = 2 γ₂⁽ⁿ⁾ = 4 γ₃⁽ⁿ⁾ = -4 γ₄⁽ⁿ⁾ = 4.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Experimental value for neutron: α⁽ⁿ⁾ − β⁽ⁿ⁾ = (9.8 ± 3.6 (stat) ± 2.2 (mod.) +2.1/−1.1 (sys)) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from quasi-free Compton scattering on deuteron, Kossert et al.)
- Experimental values for proton: α⁽ᵖ⁾ = (12.1 ± 1.1 (stat) +0.5/−0.5 (th.)) × 10⁻⁴ fm³; β⁽ᵖ⁾ = (3.4 ± 1.1 (stat) +0.1/−0.1 (th.)) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Sensitivity of DCS to Δα⁽ⁿ⁾ was tested in range (−4…4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and to Δβ⁽ⁿ⁾ in range (−2…6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at 80 MeV
- Wavefunction variations produced changes of ≤ 15% in DCS at 120 MeV
- Wavefunction variations altered Δx and Δz predictions by ≤ 7.5%
- Estimated discrepancy from pion-production threshold placement: ~5% difference in DCS, ≤ 3% in Δz and Δx at 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of Compton scattering from the deuteron (Hildebrandt et al., 2004; Beane et al., 2005) to the ³He nucleus, providing the first calculations for this reaction. It enables experimental extraction of neutron polarizabilities from γ³He scattering at facilities such as HIγS at TUNL, complementing existing results from γd experiments at MAXLab (Fissum et al., 2004). The authors note that inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance is likely the most important future correction, as γd calculations including Δ effects showed sizeable impact on the DCS at backward angles near 100 MeV.
