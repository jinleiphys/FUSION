# K^* Mesons and Nucleon Strangeness

> We study contributions to the nucleon strange quark vector current form factors from intermediate states containing K^* mesons. We show how these contributions may be comparable in magnitude to those made by K mesons, using methods complementary to those employed in quark model studies. We also analyze the degree of theoretical uncertainty associated with K^* contributions.

## Key claim

The authors show that contributions from intermediate states containing K* mesons to the nucleon's strange quark vector current form factors may be comparable in magnitude to those from K mesons, but they emphasize that their one-loop truncation is model-dependent and they do not pretend to make reliable numerical predictions, using the calculation instead to illustrate theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute the K* meson loop contributions to the nucleon strangeness form factors using two complementary approaches: (a) an explicit one-loop calculation with monopole hadronic form factors at the meson-baryon vertices and point-like strange current insertions, and (b) a dispersion relation calculation where the strong N→YK*→N amplitudes are treated in Born approximation but the strangeness form factors of the intermediate states are given vector meson dominance structure. Both calculations include intermediate states with K*K, K*K*, and hyperon-antihyperon pairs, using effective Lagrangians with pseudoscalar and vector meson-baryon couplings taken from the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- The K* mass is taken as m_K* = 895 MeV, the kaon mass as m_K = 495 MeV, and the nucleon mass as m_N = 939 MeV.
- The pseudoscalar coupling constant is g_ps/√(4π) = -3.944, and the vector coupling constant is g_v/√(4π) = -1.588 with tensor-to-vector ratio κ = 3.26.
- The cutoff parameters for the monopole form factors are Λ_K* = 2.2 GeV (2.1 GeV in an alternate model) and Λ_K = 1.2 GeV (1.4 GeV in an alternate model).
- The K→K* transition form factor at the photon point is F_KK*(0) = 1.84 when using ω and φ dominance, but reduces to F_KK*^0 = 0.43 when using φ'(1680) dominance.
- The φ' mass is m_φ' = 1680 ± 20 MeV with width Γ_φ' = 150 ± 50 MeV, and its electronic width is Γ(φ'→e+e-) = 0.7 keV, giving f_φ' ≈ 23.
- The strong φ'→KK* coupling is |G_KK*φ'| ≈ 3.8 GeV^{-1}, obtained from the φ' decay with CM momentum k_F = 463 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the kaon cloud calculations of the nucleon's strange quark vector current form factors (Had, Jaf89) by including the next-heaviest OZI-allowed intermediate states containing K* mesons. It is similar in spirit to the study of Hammer, Hammer, and Ramsey-Musolf (1997) which compared 3π and KK contributions at one-loop order, and it amplifies on the themes of Geiger and Isgur (1997), Musolf and Ramsey-Musolf (1997), and others who demonstrated that truncations in hadronic excitation energy or coupling order are unreliable. The results enable future work to incorporate higher-lying intermediate states and to quantify theoretical uncertainties in strangeness form factor predictions needed for experiments at MIT-Bates, Mainz, and Jefferson Laboratory.
