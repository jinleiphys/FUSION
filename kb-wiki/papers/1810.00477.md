# Quarkonium: a theory overview

> Quarkonium has long been proposed as one of the golden probes to identify the phase transition from confined hadronic matter to the deconfined quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. Since then, we have achieved a better understanding, not only about the propagation of quarkonium in-medium, but also about its production. Recent theoretical developments in our comprehension of the quarkonium production mechanism in proton-proton collisions and on the propagation of quarkonia in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions are reviewed and discussed.

# Quarkonium: a theory overview

## Key claim
Quarkonium suppression remains a key signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, but a modern theoretical description must incorporate lattice QCD, effective field theory, and open quantum system approaches to capture both screening and dynamical dissociation; the extrapolation of cold nuclear matter effects from proton-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus collisions remains a matter of debate.

## Method
The paper reviews three main theoretical frameworks for quarkonium production in proton-proton collisions: the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM), the Colour-Octet Mechanism (COM) within Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD), and the Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM). It then surveys cold nuclear matter effects in proton-nucleus collisions, nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) saturation, coherent energy loss, and comover interactions, and finally discusses in-medium evolution in nucleus-nucleus collisions via open quantum systems, lattice QCD potentials, and transport models.

## Key numbers
- For charmonium, the typical heavy-quark velocity in the bound-state rest frame is v² = 0.3; for bottomonium, v² = 0.1.
- At LHC mid-rapidities, the quarkonium formation time in the target rest frame is boosted by more than a factor of 1000, making it larger than the Pb radius and rendering nuclear absorption negligible.
- The CDF analyses of direct J/ψ and ψ(2S) production at √s = 1.8 TeV showed measured rates more than an order of magnitude larger than leading-order CSM calculations.
- The CEM cross section formula integrates the Q\bar{Q} pair mass from the threshold 2m_Q to the open heavy-flavour threshold 2m_H, with one process-independent probability parameter F_Q per quarkonium state.

## Context
This review was presented at Quark Matter 2018 and extends the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that quarkonium suppression signals QGP formation. It discusses how Laine et al. (2007) demonstrated that the heavy-quark potential acquires an imaginary part at finite temperature, motivating open quantum system approaches by Brambilla et al. (2017) and Blaizot et al. (2017, 2018). The paper also addresses the "excited state puzzle" in proton-nucleus collisions, where comover interaction models by Ferreiro (2012, 2014, 2018) can explain the stronger suppression of ψ(2S) and Υ(2S,3S) relative to ground states. Future work should aim to disentangle shadowing from coherent energy loss using Drell-Yan processes, as proposed by Arleo (2015).
