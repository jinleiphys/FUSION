# A general-mass scheme for prompt charm production at hadron colliders

> In these proceedings, we apply the recently developed S-ACOT-MPS factorization scheme at the next-to-leading order to prompt charm production at hadron colliders. It provides a good agreement with experimental data on charm meson production measured by LHCb at 7 and 13 TeV. The low-$p_T$ data are on the margins of the theoretical error bands, emphasizing the importance of including contributions beyond the next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The S-ACOT-MPS factorization scheme at next-to-leading order provides good agreement with LHCb data on charm meson production at 7 and 13 TeV, but the low-transverse-momentum data lie at the margins of the theoretical error bands, indicating that contributions beyond next-to-leading order are needed.

## Method

The authors apply the simplified ACOT scheme with massive phase space (S-ACOT-MPS) to prompt charm production at hadron colliders. They separate open charm diagrams into flavor creation (FC) and flavor excitation (FE) terms, subtract overlapping contributions from collinear gluon splitting into heavy-quark pairs, and integrate all terms using massive phase space to enforce energy-momentum conservation near threshold. The charm quark is fragmented into D0 mesons using a fixed fragmentation fraction measured at e+e- colliders, and predictions are compared to LHCb data at 7 and 13 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Fragmentation fraction f(c → D0) = 0.565 ± 0.032, measured at e+e- colliders
- Default scale choice: μ_R = μ_F = μ_0 = √(p_T² + (2m_c)²), with independent variation by a factor of two
- Comparison with LHCb data at 7 TeV (Aaij 2013) and 13 TeV (Aaij 2015) in the rapidity region 2.0 < y < 4.5

## Context

This work extends the simplified-ACOT-χ scheme from deep-inelastic scattering (Tung 2001) to hadroproduction, following earlier general-mass variable flavor number schemes such as ACOT (Aivazis 1993), KKSS (Kniehl 2004), RT (Thorne 1997), and FONLL (Cacciari 1998). The S-ACOT-MPS scheme enables fully differential NLO calculations for heavy-flavor production at hadron colliders and provides publicly available subtracted and residual charm PDFs for use in other NLO calculations, including in the MCFM framework (Campbell 2019).
