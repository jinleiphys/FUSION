# Strangeness Balance in HADES Experiments and the Xi- Enhancement

> HADES data on a strangeness production in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76A GeV are analyzed within a minimal statistical model. The total negative strangeness content is fixed by the observed K^+ multiplicities on event-by-event basis. Particles with negative strangeness are assumed to remain in chemical equilibrium with themselves and in thermal equilibrium with the environment until a common freeze-out. Exact strangeness conservation in each collision event is explicitly preserved. This implies that Xi baryons can be released only in events where two or more kaons are produced. An increase of the fireball volume due to application of a centrality trigger in HADES experiments is taken into account. We find that experimental ratios of K-/K+, Lambda/K+ and Sigma/K+ can be satisfactorily described provided in-medium potentials are taken into account. However, the calculated Xi-/Lambda/K+ ratio proves to be significantly smaller compared to the measured value (8 times lower than the experimental median value and 3 times lower than the lower error bar). Various scenarios to explain observed Xi enhancement are discussed. Arguments are given in favor of the Xi production in direct reactions. The rates of the possible production processes are estimated and compared.

## Key claim

The minimal statistical model with explicit event-by-event strangeness conservation can satisfactorily describe the HADES ratios for \(K^-/K^+\), \(\Lambda/K^+\), and \(\Sigma/K^+\) when in-medium potentials are included, but the calculated \(\Xi^-/\Lambda/K^+\) ratio is 8 times lower than the experimental median value and 3 times lower than the lower error bar, indicating that the observed \(\Xi^-\) enhancement cannot be explained by the statistical model alone.

## Method

The authors analyzed HADES data on Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76\(A\) GeV using a minimal statistical model where the total negative strangeness content is fixed by the observed \(K^+\) multiplicities on an event-by-event basis. Particles with negative strangeness are assumed to remain in chemical equilibrium with themselves and in thermal equilibrium with the environment until a common freeze-out, with exact strangeness conservation preserved in each collision event. The fireball volume increase due to the HADES centrality trigger (LVL1, requiring more than 16 charged particles) was taken into account, and in-medium potentials acting on nucleons and strange hadrons were incorporated in the mean-field approximation.

## Key numbers

- \(K^+\) multiplicity: \(\mathcal{M}_{K^+} = (2.8 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-2}\) in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76\(A\) GeV
- \(K^-\) multiplicity: \(\mathcal{M}_{K^-} = (7.1 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-4}\) in the same system
- \(\Lambda+\Sigma^0\) multiplicity: \(\mathcal{M}_{\Lambda+\Sigma^0} = (4.09 \pm 0.59) \times 10^{-2}\)
- \(\Xi^-/\Lambda+\Sigma^0\) ratio: \(R_{\Xi/\Lambda} = (5.6 \pm 3) \times 10^{-3}\)
- Calculated \(\Xi^-/\Lambda/K^+\) double ratio is 8 times lower than the experimental median value and 3 times lower than the lower error bar
- \(\Sigma^++\Sigma^-\) multiplicity estimated via isospin asymmetry: \(\mathcal{M}_{\Sigma^+ + \Sigma^-}^{\rm (iso)} = (1.68 \pm 0.87) \times 10^{-2}\)
- \(\Sigma^++\Sigma^-\) multiplicity from HADES reconstruction: \(\mathcal{M}_{\Sigma^+ + \Sigma^-}^{\rm (Hades)} = (0.71 \pm 0.61) \times 10^{-2}\)
- \(K^-/K^+\) ratio: \(R_{K^-/K^+} = 2.54^{+1.21}_{-0.91} \times 10^{-2}\)
- \(\Lambda/K^+\) ratio: \(R_{\Lambda/K^+} = 1.46^{+0.49}_{-0.37}\)
- \(\Xi^-/\Lambda/K^+\) double ratio: \(R_{\Xi/\Lambda/K^+} = 0.20^{+0.16}_{-0.12}\)

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, and Stachel (2006) by incorporating explicit event-by-event strangeness conservation and centrality trigger effects, which are crucial at SIS energies where strangeness production is rare. The analysis addresses the puzzling \(\Xi^-\) enhancement observed by HADES that exceeds predictions from both the statistical model and the transport code of Chen (2004), and the authors argue in favor of \(\Xi\) production through direct reactions rather than thermal processes. The results enable future experimental searches for \(\Omega^-\) baryons at SIS energies, with estimated production rates provided for possible detection channels.
