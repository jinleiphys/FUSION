# Probing nuclear symmetry energy at high densities using pion, kaon, eta and photon productions in heavy-ion collisions

> The high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy is among the most uncertain properties of dense neutron-rich matter. Its accurate determination has significant ramifications in understanding not only the reaction dynamics of heavy-ion reactions especially those induced by radioactive beams but also many interesting phenomena in astrophysics, such as the explosion mechanism of supernova and the properties of neutron stars. The heavy-ion physics community has devoted much effort during the last few years to constrain the high-density symmetry using various probes. In particular, the pion-/pion+ ratio has been most extensively studied both theoretically and experimentally. All models have consistently predicted qualitatively that the pion-/pion+ ratio is a sensitive probe of the high-density symmetry energy especially with beam energies near the pion production threshold. However, the predicted values of the pion-/pion+ ratio are still quite model dependent mostly because of the complexity of modeling pion production and reabsorption dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, leading to currently still controversial conclusions regarding the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy from comparing various model calculations with available experimental data. As more pion-/pion+ data become available and a deeper understanding about the pion dynamics in heavy-ion reactions is obtained, more penetrating probes, such as the kaon+/kaon0 ratio, eta meson and high energy photons are also being investigated or planned at several facilities. Here, we review some of our recent contributions to the community effort of constraining the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions. In addition, the status of some worldwide experiments for studying the high-density symmetry energy, including the HIRFL-CSR external target experiment (CEE) are briefly introduced.

# Probing nuclear symmetry energy at high densities using pion, kaon, eta and photon productions in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The pion-/pion+ ratio is a sensitive probe of the high-density symmetry energy, but predicted values remain quite model dependent due to the complexity of modeling pion production and reabsorption dynamics, leading to currently still controversial conclusions about the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy when comparing various model calculations with available experimental data.

## Method

The authors review their recent contributions using the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model (IBUU04) and the relativistic transport model ART1.0 to simulate heavy-ion collisions. They compare transport model calculations with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI, Darmstadt, and examine the sensitivity of pion, kaon, eta meson, and high-energy photon production to different parametrizations of the density-dependent symmetry energy, using the variable x to mimic different forms predicted by many-body theories.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy has a value of $E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0)=30$ MeV at normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$ for both considered forms
- The isospin asymmetry of the high-density region around $\rho=2\rho_0$ with $E^b_{\rm sym}(\rho)$ is about twice that with $E^a_{\rm sym}(\rho)$ at 400 MeV/u beam energy
- The sensitivity of the pion-/pion+ ratio to the symmetry energy only shows up below 600 MeV/u beam energy, corresponding to nuclear matter density approximately lower than $2\rho_0$
- The high-density symmetry energy contributes about 80% of the total effect on the pion-/pion+ ratio
- The double ratio of K+/K0 in Ru+Zr and Zr+Ru systems differs by about 5% between the NL and NLρδ parametrizations at 1.528 GeV/u
- The eta meson multiplicity predictions with two symmetry energy parameters of x=0 and x=-2 differ by a factor of about 5 to 10 in the transverse momentum interval from about 200 to 300 MeV/c at 200 MeV/u beam energy
- The eta meson mass is 547.853 MeV/c^2

## Context

This review extends the community effort to constrain the high-density symmetry energy using various probes, following earlier work on the pion-/pion+ ratio by Li et al. (2005) and the kaon+/kaon0 ratio by Ferini et al. (2006). The authors address the controversial conclusions from different transport model analyses of the same FOPI pion data, where Xiao et al. (2009) found evidence for a super-soft symmetry energy using IBUU04 while Feng et al. (2010) reached an opposite conclusion using the IQMD model. The work enables future experimental studies at facilities including the HIRFL-CSR external target experiment (CEE) and motivates searches for more penetrating probes such as eta mesons and high-energy photons to overcome the model dependence of hadronic probes.
