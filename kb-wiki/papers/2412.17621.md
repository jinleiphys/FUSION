# Effects of a first-order QCD phase transition on light nucleus production

> Using an extended Polyakov-looped Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to describe the baryon density fluctuations of quark matter along the isentropic trajectories corresponding to different $s/ρ_B$ values extracted from Au+Au collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7-200$ GeV, we investigate the effects of the first-order phase transition on the light nucleus yield ratio $N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$. The results indicate that the second-order scaled density moment $y_2$, used to quantify density fluctuations, rapidly increases to form a peak when the isentropic trajectories pass through the phase coexistence region. We extract the yield ratios $N_t\times N_p/N_d^2$ at chemical freeze-out from the isentropic trajectories at different collision energies and found significant enhancements at 19.6 GeV and 27 GeV. This is similar to the trends observed by the STAR experiment, suggesting that the enhancements in the yield ratios $N_t\times N_p/N_d^2$ observed in the STAR experiment could be explained by the density fluctuations generated in the first-order phase transition region.

# Effects of a first-order QCD phase transition on light nucleus production

## Key claim
The enhancements in the light nucleus yield ratio $N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$ observed by the STAR experiment at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ and 27 GeV could be explained by density fluctuations generated in the first-order phase transition region, but the authors cannot rule out that the critical region (near a critical end point) could also produce similar enhancements in that energy range.

## Method
The authors used an extended Polyakov-looped Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with three eight-quark interactions to compute the QCD phase diagram and baryon density fluctuations. They calculated the second-order scaled density moment $y_2$ along isentropic trajectories (constant entropy per baryon $s/\rho_B$) corresponding to Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7-200$ GeV, then approximated the yield ratio $N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$ as $y_2/(2\sqrt{3})$ at chemical freeze-out temperatures extracted from experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The yield ratio $N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$ shows significant enhancements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV and 27 GeV, matching the energy range where the STAR experiment observed enhancements with a combined significance of 4.1$\sigma$.
- The initial entropy density $s_0$ ranges from 29.6 fm$^{-3}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV to 84.0 fm$^{-3}$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.
- The entropy per baryon $s/\rho_B$ values range from 17.5 at 7.7 GeV to 331.6 at 200 GeV; trajectories with $s/\rho_B < 56.8$ (collision energies below 39 GeV) pass through the phase coexistence region.
- The crossover temperature at vanishing baryon density is approximately 165 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD predictions.
- The critical end point temperature $T_c > 150$ MeV with the chosen scalar-vector coupling constant $G_{SV} = -3500\Lambda^{-8}$.
- Chemical freeze-out temperatures range from 143 MeV at 7.7 GeV to 162 MeV at 200 GeV; kinetic freeze-out temperatures range from 89 MeV at 200 GeV to 118 MeV at 11.5 GeV.

## Context
This work extends coalescence model studies by Sun (2018, 2021) that linked the yield ratio $N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$ to baryon density fluctuations and correlation lengths. It addresses the question raised by the STAR collaboration's observation of enhanced light nucleus yields at 19.6 and 27 GeV (Abdallah, 2023). The authors note that hydrodynamic studies by Steinheimer (2012, 2013) and transport models by Li (2017) and Sun (2022) have shown that spinodal instability during a first-order transition amplifies density fluctuations. The results suggest that first-order phase transition effects, rather than critical point proximity, may explain the STAR data, but the authors acknowledge that the critical region cannot be excluded without further transport modeling that includes correlation length effects.
