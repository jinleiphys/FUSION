# Combined Constraints on First Generation Leptoquarks

> In this article we perform a combined analysis of low energy precision constraints and LHC searches for leptoquarks which couple to first generation fermions. Considering all ten leptoquark representations, five scalar and five vector ones, we study at the precision frontier the constraints from $K\toπνν$, $K\toπe^+e^-$, $K^0-\bar K^0$ and $D^0-\bar D^0$ mixing, as well as from experiments searching for parity violation (APV and QWEAK). We include LHC searches for $s$-channel single resonant production, pair production and Drell-Yan-like signatures of leptoquarks. Interestingly, we find that the recent non-resonant di-lepton analysis of ATLAS provides stronger bounds than the resonant searches recasted so far to constrain $t$-channel production of leptoquarks. Taking into account all these bounds, we observe that none of the leptoquark representations can address the so-called "Cabibbo angle anomaly" via a direct contribution to super-allowed beta decays.

# Combined Constraints on First Generation Leptoquarks

## Key claim

None of the ten possible leptoquark representations (five scalar and five vector) can address the "Cabibbo angle anomaly" via a direct contribution to super-allowed beta decays, once all low-energy precision constraints and LHC searches are taken into account simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of low-energy precision constraints (K→πνν, K→πe⁺e⁻, K⁰–K̅⁰ and D⁰–D̅⁰ mixing, atomic parity violation from APV and QWEAK) together with LHC searches for s-channel single resonant production, pair production, and Drell-Yan-like signatures of leptoquarks. They matched all ten leptoquark representations onto dimension-six SMEFT operators at tree level and one-loop level, then derived constraints on the coupling-mass parameter space for each representation, considering both the down basis (β=0) and up basis (β=θ_c) for quark flavor rotations.

## Key numbers

- The Cabibbo angle anomaly prefers a charged-current coefficient C₁₁^{eν} ≈ −0.001 from beta decays, but no leptoquark representation can generate this value without conflict with other constraints.
- V_us^β = 0.2281(7) from super-allowed beta decays, compared to V_us^{Kμ3} = 0.22345(67) and V_us^{Kμ2} = 0.22534(42) from kaon decays.
- Limits on four-quark operators: |Re[C₁^D]| ≲ 3×10⁻⁷/TeV² for D⁰–D̅⁰ mixing and |Re[C₁^K]| ≲ 1.3×10⁻⁷/TeV² for K⁰–K̅⁰ mixing.
- The weak charge of the proton: Q_W(p) = 0.0719 ± 0.0045; the weak charge of cesium-133: Q_W(¹³³Cs) = −72.82 ± 0.42.
- Br[K⁺→π⁺νν̅] = (1.73⁺¹.¹⁵₋₁.₀₅)×10⁻¹⁰ from experiment.
- For vector leptoquarks, the on-shell production cross section is σ_{VLQ} = 2σ_{SLQ} + O(α_s) for equal couplings to fermions.
- The non-resonant di-lepton analysis from ATLAS provides stronger bounds than resonant searches for t-channel production of leptoquarks; for a leptoquark mass of 1 TeV the bound on the coupling is a factor ≈1.6 (constructive interference) to ≈2.1 (destructive interference) less stringent than the four-fermion approximation.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of leptoquark constraints by performing the first complete combined analysis of all ten leptoquark representations with couplings only to first-generation fermions. It builds on the classification of leptoquarks by Buchmuller, Ruckl, and Wyler (1986) and on earlier low-energy constraint calculations by Shanker (1981, 1982) and Leurer (1993, 1994). The analysis uses the recent ATLAS non-resonant di-lepton search (Aad, 2020) and the resonant search recasting by Buonocore, Greljo, and collaborators (2020). The paper finds that, contrary to earlier suggestions, leptoquarks cannot resolve the Cabibbo angle anomaly identified by Grossman (2019) and Seng (2020), because the required charged-current effect is excluded by kaon and D-meson mixing bounds as well as LHC di-lepton constraints. This enables future work to focus on other new physics explanations for the CKM unitarity deficit.
