# Transits of the QCD Critical Point

> We analyze the evolution of hydrodynamic fluctuations in a heavy ion collision as the system passes close to the QCD critical point. We introduce two small dimensionless parameters $λ$ and $Δ_s$ to characterize the evolution. $λ$ compares the microscopic relaxation time (away from the critical point) to the expansion rate $λ\equiv τ_0/τ_Q$, and $Δ_s$ compares the baryon to entropy ratio, $n/s$, to its critical value, $Δ_s\equiv (n/s - n_c/s_c)/(n_c/s_c)$. We determine how the evolution of critical hydrodynamic fluctuations depends parametrically on $λ$ and $Δ_s$. Finally, we use this parametric reasoning to estimate the critical fluctuations and correlation length for a heavy ion collision, and to give guidance to the experimental search for the QCD critical point.

# Transits of the QCD Critical Point

## Key claim

The growth of critical fluctuations in heavy ion collisions as the system passes near the QCD critical point will be regulated by non-equilibrium (Kibble-Zurek) dynamics before the detailed equilibrium scaling of the equation of state becomes relevant, for any trajectory with detuning parameter Δ_s smaller than a threshold Δ_KZ that is remarkably insensitive to the expansion rate.

## Method

The authors introduce two small dimensionless parameters: λ ≡ τ₀/τ_Q (comparing the microscopic relaxation time away from the critical point to the expansion rate) and Δ_s ≡ (n/s - n_c/s_c)/(n_c/s_c) (comparing the baryon-to-entropy ratio to its critical value). They analyze how critical hydrodynamic fluctuations depend parametrically on these two parameters during a transit of the critical point, using the (R,θ) parameterization of the Ising equation of state and fitting numerical data from Engels, Fromme and Seniuch (2002) for the correlation length scaling function. They then estimate the maximal fluctuations and correlation length for a heavy ion collision, determining when equilibrium scaling applies versus when Kibble-Zurek scaling dominates.

## Key numbers

- The threshold detuning parameter Δ_KZ = 0.86 × (λ/0.2)^0.096, where the exponent 0.096 makes this value remarkably insensitive to λ
- For λ = 0.2, the Kibble-Zurek time t_KZ ≈ 5.8 fm, compared to τ₀ ≈ 1.8 fm and τ_Q ≈ 8.9 fm
- The three length scales for λ = 0.2: ℓ₀ ≈ 1.2 fm, ℓ_KZ ≈ 1.6 fm, ℓ_max ≈ 2.7 fm
- The maximal fluctuation enhancement in the Kibble-Zurek regime: N^{n̂n̂}(t_KZ, k)/n ∼ 1.8 × (λ/0.2)^{-0.37}
- The Ising model parameters from fitting the correlation length scaling function: u₊ = 4.133, u₋ = 5.32, σ = 3, x₀ = 0.3431
- The universal constant relating the correlation length amplitude to the equation of state parameters: (c_M c_h) M₀ h₀ ξ₊^d = 0.1231
- The normalization constant g_ξ(0) = 0.4838 / (c_M c_h M₀ h₀)^{1/d}
- The constant c_M = 0.6145 and c_M c_h = 0.3486 from the normalization conventions
- The critical exponents used: β ≈ 0.32, γ ≈ 1.23, ν ≈ 0.63, α ≈ 0.11, z ≈ 4 (dynamical exponent)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of critical fluctuations in heavy ion collisions by Akamatsu et al. (2016) on non-equilibrium modes in expanding fluids, and builds on the Ising equation of state parameterization from Onuki (2002) and Nonaka and Asakawa (2004). The authors use numerical data from Engels, Fromme and Seniuch (2002) for the Ising model correlation length. The paper shows that for realistic heavy ion collisions, the detailed equilibrium scaling of the equation of state (such as that constructed by Parotto et al., 2018) has limited range of validity, and that non-equilibrium evolution along the lines of this work and Stephanov (2017) is essential for simulating trajectories with Δ_s < Δ_KZ. The results provide guidance for experimental searches for the QCD critical point, suggesting that the enhancement of fluctuations relative to Poissonian baseline is modest (approximately 80% for λ = 0.2) and that the correlation length at freezeout is at most twice the inter-particle spacing.
