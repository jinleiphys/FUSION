# Continuum and Symmetry-Conserving Effects in Drip-line Nuclei Using Finite-range Forces

> We report the first calculations of nuclear properties near the drip-lines using the spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field theory with a finite-range force supplemented by continuum and particle number projection effects. Calculations were carried out in a basis made of the eigenstates of a Woods-Saxon potential computed in a box, thereby garanteeing that continuum effects were properly taken into account. Projection of the self-consistent solutions on good particle number was carried out after variation, and an approximation of the variation after projection result was used. We give the position of the drip-lines and examine neutron densities in neutron-rich nuclei. We discuss the sensitivity of nuclear observables upon continuum and particle-number restoration effects.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for drip-line nuclei that simultaneously include continuum effects (via a Woods-Saxon basis) and particle-number projection using a finite-range Gogny force; the authors emphasize that the particle-number restoration must be performed *before* variation (RVAP) to stabilize nuclei near the drip-line, an effect that cannot appear in projection-after-variation (PAV) calculations.

## Method

The spherical HFB equations were solved by expanding quasiparticle wavefunctions on a basis of eigenstates of a Woods-Saxon potential computed in a box of radius 20 fm, with a mesh size of 0.1 fm and cutoffs of ℓ_max = 15 and n_max = 18 (or an energy cutoff of 400 MeV). The Gogny D1S finite-range effective interaction was used. Particle-number projection was applied after variation (PAV), and an approximate variation-after-projection (RVAP) method was implemented by scaling the pairing field by a factor x and minimizing the projected energy E^N(x) as a function of x.

## Key numbers

- Basis convergence: a mesh size of h ~ 0.1 fm guaranteed HFB convergence to within a few keV; cutoffs ℓ_max = 15 and n_max = 18 allowed calculations from drip-line to drip-line to within 10 keV; an energy cutoff of 400 MeV retained about 92% positive-energy continuum states.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, single-particle energies from the Woods-Saxon basis agreed with harmonic-oscillator basis results within numerical accuracy.
- In ⁴²Mg, the RVAP minimum occurred at x = 1.12, with a total energy gain of 460 keV relative to the standard HFB solution; the pairing energy changed from -9.22 MeV (x=1.0) to -13.83 MeV (x=1.12).
- The two-neutron separation energy S_2n for ⁴²Mg became negative at -252 keV in the RVAP calculation (compared to +40 keV in the unprojected case), indicating the nucleus became bound.
- Table 1 lists neutron and proton Fermi levels and two-nucleon separation energies for 46 neutron-rich and 46 proton-rich nuclei from ²⁰C to ²⁹⁶Cm; for example, ⁶⁴Ca has λ_n = -0.09 MeV and -S_2n = 0.06 MeV, while ²⁶⁴Pb has λ_n = -1.00 MeV and -S_2n = 1.46 MeV.
- Neutron densities for ¹³²,¹⁵⁰,¹⁷⁰Sn computed in the Woods-Saxon basis showed proper exponential tails, matching coordinate-space Skyrme HFB results from Dobaczewski et al. (1996) for ¹⁵⁰Sn.

## Context

This work extends finite-range mean-field calculations (Gogny D1S force) to weakly-bound nuclei, which previously had only been treated with zero-range Skyrme forces (Dobaczewski et al., 1996; Grasso et al., 2001) or relativistic mean-field models. The Woods-Saxon basis approach avoids the unphysical Gaussian asymptotic behavior of harmonic-oscillator bases and reproduces the long tails of neutron densities seen in coordinate-space calculations. The RVAP method demonstrates that particle-number restoration before variation can stabilize nuclei at the drip-line, shifting the drip-line position compared to standard HFB or PAV calculations; this effect is particularly relevant for nuclei near closed shells where pairing correlations would otherwise collapse.
