# Towards resolving the $_Λ^3$H lifetime puzzle

> Recent $_Λ^3$H lifetime measurements in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments have yielded values shorter by (30$\pm$8)% than the free $Λ$ lifetime $τ_Λ$, thereby questioning the naive expectation that $τ({_Λ^3}{\rm H})\approxτ_Λ$ for a weakly bound $Λ$ hyperon. Here we apply the closure approximation introduced by Dalitz and coworkers to evaluate the $_Λ^3$H lifetime, using $_Λ^3$H wavefunctions generated by solving three-body Faddeev equations. Our result, disregarding pion final-state interaction (FSI), is $τ({_Λ^3}{\rm H})$=(0.90$\pm$0.01)$τ_Λ$. In contrast to previous works, pion FSI is found attractive, reducing further $τ({_Λ^3}{\rm H})$ to $τ({_Λ^3}{\rm H})$=(0.81$\pm$0.02)$τ_Λ$. We also evaluate for the first time $τ({_Λ^3}{\rm n})$, finding it considerably longer than $τ_Λ$, contrary to the shorter lifetime values suggested by the GSI HypHI experiment for this controversial hypernucleus.

## Key claim

The authors find that the hypertriton lifetime is shorter than the free Λ lifetime by about 10% without pion final-state interaction, and by about 19% when attractive pion FSI is included, which partially resolves the discrepancy with recent heavy-ion measurements showing a (30±8)% shorter lifetime.

## Method

The authors applied the closure approximation of Dalitz and coworkers to evaluate the hypertriton mesonic decay rate, using three-body Faddeev equations with separable Yamaguchi-type s-wave interactions fitted to low-energy scattering parameters. They computed the exchange integral η(q) from the Faddeev wavefunctions and then introduced pion final-state interaction using distorted-wave approximation with pion-nuclear scattering amplitudes derived from SAID πN partial-wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton lifetime without pion FSI: τ(³ΛH) = (0.90±0.01)τΛ, where τΛ = 263±2 ps
- Hypertriton lifetime with pion FSI: τ(³ΛH) = (0.81±0.02)τΛ, corresponding to (213±5) ps
- Exchange integral η(q̄=96 MeV/c) = 0.146±0.021 from the Faddeev calculation
- Hypertriton Λ separation energy: BΛ(³ΛH) = 0.13±0.05 MeV
- Predicted ³Λn lifetime: τ(³Λn) ≈ 368 ps, with lower bound 350 ps
- Pion FSI enhancement factor for two-body decays: |FDW/FPW|² = 1.097
- Pion FSI enhancement factor for three-body decays: |FDW/FPW|² = 1.119
- Total pion FSI enhancement factor: (11±2)%

## Context

This work extends the closure-approximation calculation of Rayet and Dalitz (1966) and the Faddeev calculation of Kamada et al. (1998), both of which found hypertriton lifetimes only 4-6% shorter than τΛ. The authors correct the earlier Λd cluster model result of Congleton (1992) and demonstrate that pion FSI is attractive rather than repulsive as assumed by Rayet and Dalitz (1966). The calculation enables future four-body final-state models to fully resolve the discrepancy with recent STAR, ALICE, and HypHI measurements.
