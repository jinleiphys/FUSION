# Towards resolving the $_О›^3$H lifetime puzzle

> Recent $_О›^3$H lifetime measurements in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments have yielded values shorter by (30$\pm$8)% than the free $О›$ lifetime $П„_О›$, thereby questioning the naive expectation that $П„({_О›^3}{\rm H})\approxП„_О›$ for a weakly bound $О›$ hyperon. Here we apply the closure approximation introduced by Dalitz and coworkers to evaluate the $_О›^3$H lifetime, using $_О›^3$H wavefunctions generated by solving three-body Faddeev equations. ## Key claim

The authors find that the hypertriton lifetime is shorter than the free О› lifetime by about 10% without pion final-state interaction, and by about 19% when attractive pion FSI is included, which partially resolves the discrepancy with recent heavy-ion measurements showing a (30В±8)% shorter lifetime.

## Method

The authors applied the closure approximation of Dalitz and coworkers to evaluate the hypertriton mesonic decay rate, using three-body Faddeev equations with separable Yamaguchi-type s-wave interactions fitted to low-energy scattering parameters. They computed the exchange integral О·(q) from the Faddeev wavefunctions and then introduced pion final-state interaction using distorted-wave approximation with pion-nuclear scattering amplitudes derived from SAID ПЂN partial-wave analysis.

## Context

This work extends the closure-approximation calculation of Rayet and Dalitz (1966) and the Faddeev calculation of Kamada et al. (1998), both of which found hypertriton lifetimes only 4-6% shorter than П„О›. The authors correct the earlier О›d cluster model result of Congleton (1992) and demonstrate that pion FSI is attractive rather than repulsive as assumed by Rayet and Dalitz (1966). The calculation enables future four-body final-state models to fully resolve the discrepancy with recent STAR, ALICE, and HypHI measurements.
