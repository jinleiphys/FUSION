# Cross Sections for the Dissociation of J/psi and psi' by pi and rho at low energies

> Using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson, we evaluate the dissociation cross sections for $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ by $π$ and $ρ$ at low collision energies. In collisions with $π$ mesons near threshold the $π+ J/ψ$ dissociation cross section is predicted to be small, but the $π+ψ'$ cross section is found to be quite large, in qualitative agreement with experimental data on $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The $ρ+ J/ψ$ and $ρ+ ψ'$ dissociation cross sections are both predicted to be large numerically and are divergent near threshold. These $ρ+ J/ψ$ and $ρ+ ψ'$ cross sections show considerable sensitivity to the mass of the $ρ$ meson if it is off energy shell.

## Key claim
The quark-interchange model predicts that the low-energy pi + J/psi dissociation cross section is small (about 1 mb near threshold), while the pi + psi' cross section is large (6.2 mb at its first peak), in qualitative agreement with experimental data on charmonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions. The rho + J/psi and rho + psi' cross sections are both large and diverge near threshold due to exothermic reactions, with considerable sensitivity to the rho meson mass when it is off energy shell.

## Method
The authors used the nonrelativistic quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson, where the meson-meson scattering amplitude is given by the sum of four quark-line diagrams evaluated as overlap integrals of quark model wavefunctions. The quark-quark interaction was taken as a conventional Coulomb plus linear confining potential with a Gaussian-regularized spin-spin hyperfine term, with parameters fit to the meson spectrum and I=2 pi-pi phase shifts. Cross sections were computed for pi + J/psi, pi + psi', rho + J/psi, and rho + psi' dissociation into open-charm channels (D Dbar, D Dbar*, D* Dbar, D* Dbar*), averaging over "prior" and "post" forms of the interaction.

## Key numbers
- pi + J/psi dissociation cross section near threshold (E_KE = 0.65 GeV) is about 1 mb.
- pi + psi' dissociation cross section has a local maximum at E_KE ~ 0.1 GeV of 6.2(0.8) mb.
- pi + psi' dissociation cross section has a second local maximum at E_KE ~ 0.22 GeV of 4.6(1.8) mb.
- rho + J/psi total dissociation cross section at E_KE = 0.1 GeV is about 11(3) mb.
- rho + J/psi total dissociation cross section at E_KE = 0.2 GeV decreases to 6(2) mb.
- For off-shell rho mesons, the rho + J/psi cross section increases from about 1 mb to 3 mb as m_rho increases from 0.45 GeV to 0.55 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the quark-interchange model previously applied successfully to I=2 pi-pi scattering (Barnes and Swanson, 1992), I=3/2 K-pi scattering, and S-wave KN scattering. The results contradict the larger pi+J/psi cross section of 7 mb obtained by Martins et al. (1995) using the same formalism but different assumptions about the confining interaction. The calculations enable quantitative modeling of J/psi and psi' suppression in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the possible nonlinear absorption term from rho mesons that scales as L^2 with nuclear path length, which may be relevant for interpreting anomalous suppression in Pb+Pb collisions and distinguishing it from quark-gluon plasma formation.
