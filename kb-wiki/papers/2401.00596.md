# Bulk medium properties of heavy-ion collisions from the beam energy scan with a multistage hydrodynamic model

> We introduce a method to reconstruct full rapidity distributions of charged particle multiplicity and net proton yields, crucial for constraining the longitudinal dynamics of nuclear matter created in the beam energy scan program. Employing rapidity distributions within a multistage hydrodynamic model calibrated for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7-200\,$GeV, we estimate the total energy and baryon number deposited into the collision fireball, offering insights into initial dynamics and the identification of nuclear remnants. We explore the potential of rapidity-dependent measurements in probing equations of state at finite chemical potentials. Furthermore, we compare the freeze-out parameters derived from both hydrodynamics and thermal models, highlighting that the parameters extracted via thermal models represent averaged properties across rapidities.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to reconstruct full rapidity distributions of charged particle multiplicity and net proton yields for beam energies lacking direct measurements, but the author explicitly cautions that these reconstructed distributions are obtained without incorporating uncertainties and extrapolating beyond measurements would introduce non-trivial prior knowledge into inference processes.

## Method

The author utilizes a (3+1)-dimensional multistage hydrodynamic framework with parametric initial conditions to simulate Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV. The approach involves constructing initial entropy and baryon densities by extending the nucleus thickness function with parametrized longitudinal profiles, then simulating the evolution of the energy-momentum tensor and net baryon current using the MUSIC hydrodynamic code with dissipative effects from shear stress tensor and net baryon diffusion current. The author identifies universal scaling properties in existing rapidity-dependent experimental data, fits these data with universal curves, and uses these curves to reconstruct rapidity distributions for beam energies where measurements are unavailable.

- The total energy during hydrodynamic evolution and the incoming energy carried by participants agree within a discrepancy of less than 15% for 0-5% centrality at 19.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the (3+1)-dimensional multistage hydrodynamic modeling approach developed by Denicol (2018) and Du (2022) for heavy-ion collisions at beam energy scan energies, where boost-invariance breaks down and conserved charges become important. The reconstruction method builds on the limiting fragmentation phenomenon established by Benecke (1969) and universal scaling properties identified by the BRAHMS Collaboration (2009) and Braun-Munzinger (2020). The calibrated framework enables probing of initial baryon stopping mechanisms, identification of nuclear remnants in the fragmentation region, and investigation of how freeze-out parameters extracted from thermal models represent averaged properties across rapidities rather than local thermodynamic conditions.
