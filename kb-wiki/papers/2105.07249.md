# Measurements of $^{160}$Dy($p,γ$) at energies relevant for astrophysical $γ$ process

> Rare information on photodisintegration reactions of nuclei with mass numbers $A \approx 160$ at astrophysical conditions impedes our understanding of the origin of $p$-nuclei. Experimental determination of the key ($p,γ$) cross sections has been playing an important role to verify nuclear reaction models and to provide rates of relevant ($γ,p$) reactions in $γ$-process. In this paper we report the first cross section measurements of $^{160}$Dy($p,γ$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{161}$Dy($p,n$)$^{161}$Ho in the beam energy range of 3.4 - 7.0 MeV, partially covering the Gamow window. Such determinations are possible by using two targets with various isotopic fractions. The cross section data can put a strong constraint on the nuclear level densities and gamma strength functions for $A \approx$ 160 in the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model. Furthermore, we find the best parameters for TALYS that reproduce the A $\thicksim$ 160 data available, $^{160}$Dy($p,γ$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{162}$Er($p,γ$)$^{163}$Tm, and recommend the constrained $^{161}$Ho($γ,p$)$^{160}$Dy reaction rates over a wide temperature range for $γ$-process network calculations. Although the determined $^{161}$Ho($γ$, p) stellar reaction rates at the temperature of 1 to 2 GK can differ by up to one order of magnitude from the NON-SMOKER predictions, it has a minor effect on the yields of $^{160}$Dy and accordingly the $p$-nuclei, $^{156,158}$Dy. A sensitivity study confirms that the cross section of $^{160}$Dy($p$, $γ$)$^{161}$Ho is measured precisely enough to predict yields of $p$-nuclei in the $γ$-process.

## Key claim

The first cross section measurements of $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{161}$Dy($p,n$)$^{161}$Ho in the beam energy range of 3.4 - 7.0 MeV partially cover the Gamow window for the $\gamma$ process, but the determined $^{161}$Ho($\gamma$,p) stellar reaction rates at temperatures of 1 to 2 GK can differ by up to one order of magnitude from the NON-SMOKER predictions, yet this has only a minor effect on the yields of $^{160}$Dy and the $p$-nuclei $^{156,158}$Dy.

## Method

Cross sections were measured using the activation method with proton beams from tandem accelerators at the China Institute of Atomic Energy. Two targets with different isotopic compositions (natural Dy and $^{160}$Dy-enriched Dy$_2$O$_3$) were employed to decouple the contributions from $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho and $^{161}$Dy($p,n$)$^{161}$Ho reactions, which produce the same final nucleus $^{161}$Ho. The $\gamma$ activity from electron-capture decays of produced Ho isotopes was measured off-line using a CLOVER HPGe detector in a low-background shielding system, and cross sections were extracted from the 103-keV $\gamma$ transition of $^{161}$Ho.

## Key numbers

* $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho cross section at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 3.34(6)$ MeV: $3.25(58) \times 10^{-3}$ mb
* $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho cross section at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 6.93(3)$ MeV: $0.507(84)$ mb
* $^{161}$Dy($p,n$)$^{161}$Ho cross section at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 3.34(6)$ MeV: $3.72(70) \times 10^{-3}$ mb
* $^{161}$Dy($p,n$)$^{161}$Ho cross section at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 6.93(3)$ MeV: $19.4(36)$ mb
* Astrophysical $S$ factor for $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 3.34(6)$ MeV: $3.22 \times 10^{10}$ MeV b
* Astrophysical $S$ factor for $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$)$^{161}$Ho at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 6.93(3)$ MeV: $1.92 \times 10^{8}$ MeV b
* Half-life of $^{161}$Ho deduced from time evolution of the 103-keV $\gamma$ ray: 148.5(24) minutes, consistent with the literature value of 148.8(30) minutes

## Context

This work provides the first experimental cross sections for $^{160}$Dy($p,\gamma$) and $^{161}$Dy($p,n$) in the $A \sim 160$ mass region, where data were previously extremely rare due to low cross sections and target purification difficulties. The only prior data in this mass range was the $^{162}$Er($p,\gamma$) measurement by the activation method (2017). The new data constrain nuclear level densities and gamma strength functions for the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model, and the authors find best parameters for TALYS that reproduce the available $A \sim 160$ data including $^{162}$Er($p,\gamma$)$^{163}$Tm. The constrained $^{161}$Ho($\gamma$,p) reaction rates are recommended for $\gamma$-process network calculations over a wide temperature range, enabling more reliable modeling of $p$-nuclei production in the $A \sim 160$ region.
