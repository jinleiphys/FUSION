# Correlations in Hot Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The single-particle spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter are computed using the ladder approximation within the theory of finite temperature Green's functions. The internal energy and the momentum distributions of protons and neutrons are studied as a function of the density and the asymmetry of the system. The proton states are more strongly depleted when the asymmetry increases while the occupation of the neutron states is enhanced as compared to the symmetric case. The self-consistent Green's function approach leads to slightly smaller energies as compared to the Brueckner Hartree Fock approach. This effect increases with density and thereby modifies the saturation density and leads to smaller symmetry energies.

## Key claim

The self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach yields slightly smaller binding energies than the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach for asymmetric nuclear matter, with the difference increasing with density; this leads to a small reduction in the symmetry energy, but the reduction (about 1.4 MeV at saturation density) is weaker than the approximately 4 MeV decrease reported in earlier work using the Reid93 potential and a simplified three-delta representation of spectral functions.

## Method

The authors extended the self-consistent T-matrix (ladder) approximation of finite-temperature Green's function theory to asymmetric nuclear matter, solving Dyson's equation for the single-particle spectral functions of protons and neutrons. Calculations were performed using the charge-dependent Bonn (CDBONN) potential at a temperature of 5 MeV, for two densities (0.16 fm⁻³ and 0.32 fm⁻³) and various proton fractions, with partial waves up to J=9 included for the Hartree-Fock part and up to J=2 for the dispersive part. The self-consistent results were compared to continuous-choice BHF calculations under the same conditions.

## Key numbers

- At ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the repulsive effect of SCGF relative to BHF is 1.8 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter; at ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³, it increases to 3.2 MeV.
- The symmetry energy a_s at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is 30.0 MeV in BHF and 28.6 MeV in SCGF.
- The difference between BHF and SCGF chemical potentials is about 15 MeV across the full range of asymmetries at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Calculations were performed at a temperature of T = 5 MeV, chosen to be low enough for conclusions at T = 0 and high enough to avoid proton-neutron pairing instabilities.

## Context

This work extends the SCGF formalism, previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter (Frick 2003, Dickhoff 2003), to asymmetric nuclear matter where only BHF calculations were available (Bombaci 1991, Isayev 2003, Hassan 2003). The results show that the quadratic dependence of energy on asymmetry squared holds for both BHF and SCGF, but the SCGF approach predicts smaller symmetry energies and reduced differences between proton and neutron chemical potentials, which would imply smaller proton fractions in β-equilibrium than BHF predicts. The finding that proton momentum distributions are more depleted than neutron distributions in asymmetric matter contrasts with recent SCGF calculations by Bozek (2004) using the same CDBONN potential, and has implications for interpreting (e,e'p) experiments on heavy nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb.
