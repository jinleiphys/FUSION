# Small x physics and the initial conditions in heavy ion collisions

> At very high energies, the high parton densities (characterized by a semi-hard saturation scale Λ_s) ensure that parton distributions can be described by a classical effective field theory with remarkable properties analogous to those of spin glass systems. This color glass condensate (CGC) of gluons also provides the initial conditions for multi-particle production in high energy nuclear collisions. In this talk, we briefly summarize recent theoretical and phenomenological progress in the CGC approach to small x physics. In particular, we discuss recent numerical work on the real time gluodynamics of partons after a nuclear collision. The implications of this work for the theoretical study of thermalization in nuclear collisions and on the phenomenological interpretation of results of the recent RHIC experiments are also discussed.

# Small x physics and the initial conditions in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The color glass condensate (CGC) framework provides the initial conditions for multi-particle production in high energy nuclear collisions, and recent numerical work on real-time gluodynamics shows that the produced gluon momentum distributions closely resemble a two-dimensional Bose-Einstein form, though this is a purely classical result whose physical meaning is not yet clear.

## Method
The authors formulated the classical effective field theory (EFT) of the color glass condensate on a lattice by discretizing the transverse plane, assuming boost invariance in pseudorapidity to reduce the theory to 2+1 dimensions. They solved the classical Yang-Mills equations numerically for SU(2) gauge group with random color sources on the two light cones, then extrapolated results to SU(3). The gluon number was determined using two independent definitions: Coulomb gauge projection in the transverse plane and a gauge-invariant "cooling" technique based on free-field relaxation.

## Key numbers
- Saturation scale at RHIC: Λ_s = 2 GeV; at LHC: Λ_s = 4 GeV
- Initial transverse energy per unit rapidity for central Au-Au collisions: 2700 GeV at RHIC, 25000 GeV at LHC
- Formation time: τ_f = 0.3 fm at RHIC, τ_f = 0.13 fm at LHC
- Initial energy density: 66.5 GeV/fm³ at RHIC, 1.3 TeV/fm³ at LHC
- Gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity for central Au-Au: dN/dη ≈ 950 at RHIC, dN/dη ≈ 4300 at LHC
- Dimensionless function for energy: f_E = 0.21–0.26
- Dimensionless function for gluon number: f_N = 0.13 (RHIC), 0.15 (LHC)
- Product Λ_s L ≈ 120 for RHIC, ≈ 240 for LHC (with L = 11.6 fm for Au-Au)
- The momentum distribution saturates at k_⊥ ∼ Λ_s and the inverse two-dimensional "temperature" β is of order 1 in units of Λ_s, with an effective mass of order 0.1 Λ_s

## Context
This work extends the classical effective field theory approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the initial conditions for nuclear collisions derived by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995). The numerical results agree with the kinetic theory analysis of Baier et al. (2001) for proper times τ < 1/α_S^(3/2), where occupation numbers exceed unity, and the classical simulations automatically include Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference effects. The approach enables quantitative predictions for RHIC and LHC observables and provides initial conditions for studying thermalization via elastic scattering (Mueller, 2000) and inelastic 2→3 processes (Baier et al., 2001).
