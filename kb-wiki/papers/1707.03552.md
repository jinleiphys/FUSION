# Multiple multi-orbit pairing algebras in shell model and interacting boson models

> In nuclei with valence nucleons are say identical nucleons and say these nucleons occupy several-$j$ orbits, then it is possible to consider pair creation operator $S_+$ to be a sum of the single-$j$ shell pair creation operators $S_+(j)$ with arbitrary phases, $S_+=\sum_j α_j S_+(j); α_j=\pm 1$. In this situation, it is possible to define multi-orbit or generalized seniority that corresponds to the quasi-spin $SU(2)$ algebra generated by $S_+$, $S_-=(S_+)^\dagger$ and $S_0=(\hat{n} -Ω)/2$ operators; $\hat{n}$ is number operator and $Ω=[\sum_j (2j+1)]/2$. There are now multiple pairing quasi-spin $SU(2)$ algebras. Also, the $α_j$'s and the generators of the corresponding generalized seniority generating sympletic algebras $Sp(2Ω)$ in $U(2Ω) \supset Sp(2Ω)$ have one-to-one correspondence. Using these, derived are the special seniority selection rules for electromagnetic transitions. A particular choice for $α_j$'s as advocated by Arvieu and Moszkowski (AM) in the past gives pairing Hamiltonians having maximum correlation with well known effective interactions. The various results derived for identical fermion systems are shown to extend to identical boson systems with the bosons occupying several-$\ell$ orbits as for example in $sd$, $sp$, $sdg$ and $sdpf$ IBM's. The quasi-spin algebra here is $SU(1,1)$ and the generalized seniority quantum number is generated by $SO(2Ω)$ in $U(2Ω) \supset SO(2Ω)$. The different $SO(2Ω)$ algebras here will be important in the study of quantum phase transitions and order-chaos transitions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for identical nucleons occupying several-\(j\) orbits, there exist \(2^{r-1}\) distinct multi-orbit pairing quasi-spin \(SU(2)\) algebras (one for each choice of phases \(\alpha_j = \pm 1\)), and these algebras have a one-to-one correspondence with complementary symplectic \(Sp(2\Omega)\) algebras; the same structure extends to identical boson systems with \(SU(1,1)\) and \(SO(2\Omega)\) algebras, with the specific phase choice \(\alpha_j = (-1)^{\ell_j}\) (Arvieu-Moszkowski) giving maximum correlation with realistic effective interactions, though the correlation coefficient \(\zeta\) does not exceed 0.3.

## Method

The paper constructs generalized pair creation operators \(S_+ = \sum_j \alpha_j S_+(j)\) with \(\alpha_j = \pm 1\) and derives the quasi-spin \(SU(2)\) algebra for fermions and \(SU(1,1)\) for bosons, along with their complementary \(Sp(2\Omega)\) and \(SO(2\Omega)\) algebras. Using commutators of \(S_+\) with one-body operators, the tensorial nature (scalar \(T^0_0\) or vector \(T^1_0\)) of electromagnetic transition operators is determined with respect to each phase choice. Correlation coefficients \(\zeta(H, H_P)\) between realistic effective interactions and the pairing Hamiltonian are computed using French's spectral distribution method for three different model spaces.

## Key numbers

* For the \((^0f_{7/2}, ^0f_{5/2}, ^1p_{3/2}, ^1p_{1/2})\) space with GXPF1 interaction and \(m = 2-18\) particles, the phase choice \((+,+,+,+)\) gives \(\zeta = 0.36\) to \(0.33\).
* For the \((^0f_{5/2}, ^1p_{3/2}, ^1p_{1/2}, ^0g_{9/2})\) space with JUN45 interaction and \(m = 2-20\) particles, the phase choice \((+,+,+,-)\) gives \(\zeta = 0.42\) to \(0.21\).
* For the \((^0g_{7/2}, ^1d_{5/2}, ^1d_{3/2}, ^2s_{1/2}, ^0h_{11/2})\) space with jj55-SVD interaction and \(m = 2-30\) particles, the phase choice \((+,+,+,+,-)\) gives \(\zeta = 0.33\) to \(0.11\).
* The total number of single-particle states is \(N = 2\Omega\) where \(\Omega = [\sum_j (2j+1)]/2\).
* With \(r\) number of \(j\) shells, there are \(2^{r-1}\) quasi-spin \(SU(2)\) algebras for fermions and \(2^{r-1}\) quasi-spin \(SU(1,1)\) algebras for bosons.

## Context

This work extends the multi-orbit pairing formalism originally studied by Arvieu and Moszkowski (1966) in the context of surface delta interaction, and the seniority selection rules described by Talmi (1993). It provides a systematic algebraic framework for all possible phase choices \(\alpha_j = \pm 1\) in both shell model and interacting boson models (sd, sp, sdg, sdpf IBM's). The results enable the study of quantum phase transitions and order-chaos transitions in nuclei using the different \(SO(2\Omega)\) algebras in boson systems, and allow calculation of number-dependent matrix elements for electromagnetic transitions via Wigner-Eckart theorem applied to the quasi-spin algebras.
