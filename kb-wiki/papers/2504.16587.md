# Impact of spin polarization on the QCD equation of state

> Spin polarization provides a novel probe of the rotational properties of the quark-gluon plasma formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This work investigates the equation of state, particularly its transport and thermodynamic coefficients in noncentral O+O collisions, employing a parton distribution function that incorporates spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity. Within a kinetic theory framework, one finds that the magnitude of the squared speed of sound ($c_s^2$) is only weakly modified by spin polarization, whereas the specific shear viscosity ($η/s$), specific bulk viscosity ($ζ/s$), and mean free path ($λ$) show substantial changes. When spin polarization is included, both $c_s^2$ and $ζ/s$ develop a nonmonotonic dependence on the collision energy, with an inflection point near $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=27$ GeV, corresponding to an average parton chemical potential of $\langleμ_p\rangle=0.021$ GeV. These results suggest that spin polarization may serve as a useful probe for constraining the effective equation of state of QCD matter.

## Key claim

The squared speed of sound ($c_s^2$) is only weakly modified by spin polarization, whereas the specific shear viscosity ($\eta/s$), specific bulk viscosity ($\zeta/s$), and mean free path ($\lambda$) show substantial changes; both $c_s^2$ and $\zeta/s$ develop a nonmonotonic dependence on collision energy when spin polarization is included, with an inflection point near $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=27$ GeV.

## Method

The author employs the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate noncentral O+O collisions at fixed impact parameter $b=3$ fm, then extracts transport and thermodynamic coefficients within a kinetic theory framework using a spin-dependent parton distribution function that incorporates spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity. The energy-momentum tensor is computed with and without spin polarization, and the first-order coefficients (specific shear viscosity $\eta/s$, specific bulk viscosity $\zeta/s$, squared speed of sound $c_s^2$, and mean free path $\lambda$) are obtained from the standard kinetic theory definitions. Spin polarization is included only in the partonic stage and is assumed to arise solely from thermal vorticity, neglecting contributions from magnetic fields, thermal shear, and viscous effects.

## Key numbers

- The inflection point for the nonmonotonic behavior of $\tilde{c}_s^2/c_s^2$ and $(\tilde{\zeta}/\tilde{s})/(\zeta/s)$ occurs near $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=27$ GeV.
- The inflection point corresponds to an average parton chemical potential of $\langle\mu_p\rangle=0.021$ GeV.
- The nonmonotonic behavior constrains the inflection point to $\langle\mu_p\rangle \approx (0.017,0.021)$ GeV, corresponding to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx(39,27)$ GeV.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and proper time $\tau=0.2$ fm, the crossover where spin polarization changes from suppressing to enhancing $\zeta/s$ occurs near $R \approx 2$ fm.
- The critical temperature is fixed at $T_c=0.16$ GeV throughout the analysis.
- The impact parameter for all O+O collisions is fixed at $b=3$ fm.

## Context

This work extends studies of the QCD equation of state in small collision systems, specifically O+O collisions, which may represent a minimal system capable of generating small QGP droplets (Huss 2020, Eskola 2022, Nijs 2025). It builds on prior kinetic theory and hydrodynamic approaches to transport coefficients (Gale 2020, Shen 2020) and recent work exploring spin polarization as a probe of the QCD equation of state (Singh 2021, Palermo 2024). The results suggest that spin polarization may serve as a useful probe for constraining the effective equation of state of QCD matter, particularly at finite chemical potential, and could guide future investigations of QGP properties in rotating systems.
