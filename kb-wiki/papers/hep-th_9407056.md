# Hamiltonian Light-Front Field Theory and Quantum Chromodynamics

> Light-front coordinates offer a scenario in which a constituent picture of hadron structure can emerge from QCD, after several difficulties are addressed. Field theoretic difficulties force us to introduce cutoffs that violate Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance, and a new renormalization group formalism based on a similarity transformation is used with coupling coherence to fix cuonterterms that restore these symmetries. The counterterms contain functions of longitudinal momentum fractions that severely complicate renormalization, but they also offer possible resolutions of apparent contradictions between the constituent picture and QCD. The similarity transformation and coupling coherence are applied to QED; and it is shown that the resultant Hamiltonian leads to standard lowest order bound state results, with the Coulomb interaction emerging naturally. The same techniques are applied to QCD and with physically motivated assumptions it is shown that a simple confinement mechanism appears.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a constituent picture of hadrons can be derived from QCD using light-front coordinates, but only if one introduces cutoffs that violate Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance, and then restores these symmetries through a new renormalization group formalism based on a similarity transformation and coupling coherence.

## Method

The author develops a Hamiltonian light-front field theory approach in which cutoffs are introduced that break Lorentz covariance and gauge invariance, and a similarity transformation is used to construct a renormalization group that systematically removes high-energy degrees of freedom. Coupling coherence is then employed to fix counterterms perturbatively, restoring the broken symmetries. The method is first applied to QED to show that the Coulomb interaction and standard lowest-order bound state results emerge naturally, and then extended to QCD where a confinement mechanism is derived from infrared divergences in the spin-independent two-body interaction between partons.

## Key numbers

- The Bohr binding energy for positronium is derived from the QED Hamiltonian produced by the similarity transformation and coupling coherence (section 11).
- Bare masses for quarks and gluons diverge even when the cutoff is finite, but divergences in the spin-independent two-body interaction between all partons exactly cancel these mass divergences in color singlet states (abstract).
- The residual interactions after cancellation produce logarithmic potentials in both longitudinal and transverse directions (abstract).
- The coupling approaches one as the cutoff approaches Λ_QCD (section 2).
- The free energy for a particle of mass m is p^- = (p_perp^2 + m^2)/p^+ (section 3).

## Context

This work extends the light-front field theory program initiated by Dirac (1949), the infinite momentum frame approach of Weinberg (1966), and the DLCQ program of Brodsky and Pauli. It directly builds on the similarity transformation developed by Glazek and Wilson (1993, 1994) and the coupling coherence formalism (1993). The paper proposes a new confinement mechanism that emerges naturally from a perturbative renormalization group, in contrast to earlier approaches that assumed non-perturbative phenomena such as confinement and chiral symmetry breaking would arise naturally from the canonical QCD Hamiltonian. The approach enables future non-perturbative calculations using a finite-dimensional Hamiltonian that can be diagonalized numerically, with the goal of deriving constituent quark model parameters from QCD.
