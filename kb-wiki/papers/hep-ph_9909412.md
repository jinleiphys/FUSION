# Chiral Boson Theory on the Light-Front

> The {\it front form} framework for describing the quantized theory of chiral boson is discussed. It avoids the conflict with the requirement of the principle of microcausality as is found in the conventional equal- time treatment. The discussion of the Floreanini-Jackiw model and its modified version for describing the chiral boson becomes very transparent on the light-front.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Floreanini-Jackiw (FJ) model and its modified version for the chiral boson become transparent and avoid the conflict with microcausality when quantized on the light-front (LF), whereas the conventional equal-time (instant form) treatment produces a commutator that violates microcausality. The key hedge is that the LF commutator is nonlocal in the longitudinal coordinate \(x^{-}\) but does not conflict with microcausality because it is nonvanishing only on the light-cone, unlike the equal-time commutator which is nonvanishing for spacelike separations.

## Method

The paper studies the canonical quantization of a modified FJ Lagrangian, written in light-front coordinates as \(\mathcal{L} = (\partial_{+}\phi - \frac{1}{\alpha}\partial_{-}\phi)\partial_{-}\phi\), where \(\alpha\) is a fixed parameter. The Dirac procedure or Faddeev-Jackiw method is used to construct the Hamiltonian framework, leading to the LF Hamiltonian \(H^{lf} = \int dx^{-} \frac{1}{\alpha} (\partial_{-}\phi)^{2}\) and the equal-\(\tau\) commutator \([\phi(\tau,x^{-}),\phi(\tau,y^{-})] = -\frac{i}{4} \epsilon(x^{-}-y^{-})\). The field is then expanded in momentum space with creation/annihilation operators satisfying canonical commutation relations, and the dispersion relation \(k^{-} = \frac{1}{\alpha} k^{+}\) is derived from the Heisenberg equation of motion.

## Key numbers

* The equal-\(\tau\) commutator is \([\phi(\tau,x^{-}),\phi(\tau,y^{-})] = -\frac{i}{4} \epsilon(x^{-}-y^{-})\), which is nonlocal in \(x^{-}\) but does not violate microcausality.
* The dispersion relation from the modified model is \(k^{-} = \frac{1}{\alpha} k^{+}\), implying \(2k^{+}k^{-} = \frac{2}{\alpha}(k^{+})^{2}\).
* In the limit \(|\alpha| \to \infty\), the field \(\phi \to \phi_{R}(x^{-})\) describes a right-moving chiral boson, and the LF Hamiltonian \(H^{lf} \to 0\).
* The conserved translation generators satisfy \(P^{-} \equiv H^{lf} = \frac{1}{\alpha} P^{+}\), so \(2P^{+}P^{-} = \frac{2}{\alpha}(P^{+})^{2}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier Floreanini-Jackiw model (Floreanini and Jackiw, 1987) and its modified versions (Girotti, 1990) by reinterpreting them in Dirac's front form framework (Dirac, 1949). It contrasts with the conventional instant form treatment, where the equal-time commutator violates microcausality, and with Siegel's model (Siegel, 1984), which requires a Wess-Zumino term and couples chiral bosons to gravity. The LF approach enables a transparent quantization that respects microcausality and may be applied to other constrained systems, such as selfdual fields and two-dimensional gauge theories.
