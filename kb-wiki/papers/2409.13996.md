# Study of the EEC discrimination power on quark and gluon jet quenching effects in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV

> We present a systematic investigation of flavor-dependent jet quenching using energy-energy correlators (EEC) in $\sqrt{\rm s}=5.02$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Employing the improved SHELL model, which incorporates collisional and radiative energy loss, as well as medium response, we quantify distinct quenching signatures for quark and gluon jets. Key findings include: (1) Pure quark jets exhibit strong EEC enhancement at large angular scales, while gluon jets show a bimodal enhancement pattern at both small and large scales; (2) Dual-shift decomposition in the EEC ratio reveals shifts toward large primarily driven by energy loss, while small-$R_{L}$~shifts extend beyond selection bias and indicate intrinsic enhancement of the gluon-initiated jets; (3) Quark jets experience global suppression of averaged energy weight $\langle\mathrm{weight}\rangle(R_{L})$, whereas gluon jets exhibit concentration toward small $R_{L}$; (4) Mechanism decomposition identifies elastic energy loss concentrating $\langle\mathrm{weight}\rangle(R_{L})$ toward small $R_{L}$, radiative loss dominating quark jet modification, and medium response amplifying large $R_{L}$ ~enhancement via soft hadrons. The observed flavor dependence in EEC modifications is dominantly driven by intrinsic jet structure differences rather than medium-induced mechanisms. We propose photon-tagged jets as quark proxies and inclusive charged-hadron jets as gluon proxies, finding they reproduce the respective flavor-specific quenching patterns. Our work establishes the EEC as a precision probe of color-charge-dependent jet-medium interactions, providing new constraints for the detailed $\hat{q}$ extraction and QGP tomography, while highlighting the critical role of pre-quenching flavor asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy-energy correlator (EEC) can discriminate quark and gluon jet quenching patterns in Pb+Pb collisions, but the observed flavor dependence is dominantly driven by intrinsic jet structure differences in p+p rather than by differences in medium-induced energy loss between quarks and gluons.

## Method

The authors used the SHELL model, which incorporates collisional and radiative energy loss via the Higher-Twist (HT) formalism and Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) collisional energy loss, coupled with the CLVisc hydrodynamic model for the QGP fireball evolution. Pure quark and gluon jets were generated by constraining PYTHIA 8 to produce only quark or gluon hard processes, then reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.4 from charged particles with pT ≥ 1 GeV. The EEC was computed as the energy-weighted cross-section of particle pairs as a function of angular separation RL, and the A+A/p+p ratio was used to quantify medium modification.

## Key numbers

- Central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, jet pT interval 40-60 GeV
- EEC enhancement at RL > 0.2 and RL < 0.05, with suppression at RL around 0.05-0.2
- Pure quark jets show enhancement only at RL > 0.2 in A+A/p+p ratio
- Pure gluon jets show shifts toward both smaller and larger RL simultaneously in A+A/p+p ratio
- Photon-tagged jets require pTγ > 40 GeV with tagged charged jets in 40 < pT,jetch < 60 GeV

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of EEC in heavy-ion collisions by Yang (2023), Andres (2022, 2023), and Barata (2023) who studied gamma-jet EEC, and by Devereaux (2023) for EIC applications. It also builds on the analytical quark/gluon discrimination study of EEC in p+p by Larkoski (2013). The paper enables experimental tests of color-charge-dependent jet-medium interactions using double-ratio measurements of inclusive charged jets (gluon-dominated) and photon-tagged jets (quark proxies) at the LHC.
