# Particle-vibration coupling within covariant density functional theory

> Covariant density functional theory, which has so far been applied only within the framework of static and time dependent mean field theory is extended to include Particle-Vibration Coupling (PVC) in a consistent way. Starting from a conventional energy functional we calculate the low-lying collective vibrations in Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA) and construct an energy dependent self-energy for the Dyson equation. The resulting Bethe-Salpeter equation in the particle-hole ($ph$) channel is solved in the Time Blocking Approximation (TBA). No additional parameters are used and double counting is avoided by a proper subtraction method. The same energy functional, i.e. the same set of coupling constants, generates the Dirac-Hartree single-particle spectrum, the static part of the residual $ph$-interaction and the particle-phonon coupling vertices. Therefore a fully consistent description of nuclear excited states is developed. This method is applied for an investigation of damping phenomena in the spherical nuclei with closed shells $^{208}$Pb and $^{132}$Sn. Since the phonon coupling terms enrich the RRPA spectrum with a multitude of $ph\otimes$phonon components a noticeable fragmentation of the giant resonances is found, which is in full agreement with experimental data and with results of the semi-phenomenological non-relativistic approach.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory is extended beyond the static and time-dependent mean-field level to include particle-vibration coupling (PVC) in a fully consistent way, using the same energy functional (with no additional parameters) to generate the mean field, the residual particle-hole interaction, and the particle-phonon coupling vertices, with double counting avoided by a proper subtraction method.

## Method

Starting from the covariant density functional with the NL3 parameter set, the authors first solve the Dirac-Hartree equations for the single-particle spectrum and then compute low-lying collective vibrations within the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA). An energy-dependent self-energy for the Dyson equation is constructed from the particle-phonon coupling vertices, and the resulting Bethe-Salpeter equation in the particle-hole channel is solved in the Time Blocking Approximation (TBA), which restricts the propagation to configurations of at most 1p1h⊗phonon complexity. A subtraction technique is applied to avoid double counting between the correlations introduced by PVC and those already contained in the phenomenological density functional.

## Key numbers

* For ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹³²Sn, the inclusion of phonon coupling terms enriches the RRPA spectrum with a multitude of ph⊗phonon components, producing a noticeable fragmentation of the giant resonances that is in full agreement with experimental data.
* The method uses the NL3 parameter set (no numerical values for the parameters are given in the text) and the same set of coupling constants for the Dirac-Hartree spectrum, the static residual ph-interaction, and the particle-phonon coupling vertices.

## Context

This work extends covariant density functional theory, previously limited to static and time-dependent mean-field and RRPA (Rin.96, VALR.05), by incorporating particle-vibration coupling techniques from Landau-Migdal theory (Mig.67) and Nuclear Field Theory (BM.75, BBB.77). The approach is similar to earlier non-relativistic self-consistent theories of finite Fermi systems (KS.79, KS.80, FTT.00) and enables a fully consistent description of damping phenomena and spreading widths of giant resonances in spherical closed-shell nuclei, with the potential for application to exotic nuclei near the limits of stability.
