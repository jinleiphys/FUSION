# A systematic study on the binding energy of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> In this paper, we calculated the binding energy per baryon of the $Λ$ hypernuclei systemically, using the relativistic mean field theory (RMF) in a statistic frame. Some resemble properties are found among most of the hypernuclei found in experiments. The data show that a $Λ$ hypernucleus will be more stable, if it is composed of a $Λ$ hyperon adding to a stable normal nuclear core, or a $Λ$ hyperon replacing a neutron in a stable normal nuclear core. According to our calculations, existences of some new $Λ$ hypernuclei are predicted under the frame of RMF.

# A systematic study on the binding energy of Λ hypernuclei

## Key claim
Using relativistic mean field theory (RMF) with a static frame and the NL-SH parameter set, the authors find that Λ hypernuclei are more stable when formed by adding a Λ hyperon to a stable normal nuclear core, or by replacing a neutron in a stable normal nuclear core; however, the match radius was kept unchanged for all hypernuclei, and the authors note that for medium and heavy hypernuclei this introduces errors that increase with baryon number.

## Method
The authors applied relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL-SH parameter set to calculate the binding energy per baryon of ground-state Λ hypernuclei. They analyzed hypernuclei with baryon number A < 30 and A > 30 separately, comparing their RMF results to experimental data for 40 single-Λ hypernuclei. Based on observed patterns, particularly the stability of hypernuclei with baryon number 4n+1 and those built on magic-number cores, they predicted 71 new Λ hypernuclei and computed their binding energies.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per baryon of hypernuclei with baryon number larger than 120 is around 7.0 MeV and decreases slowly with increasing baryon number.
- For hypernuclei with baryon number between 30 and 65, binding energy per baryon is near 8.8 MeV.
- For hypernuclei with baryon number between 87 and 93, binding energy per baryon is around 7.5 MeV.
- Hypernuclei with nuclear core Kr have binding energy per baryon about 8.7 MeV; nearby hypernuclei with nuclear core Rb have binding energy per baryon near 7.6 MeV.
- For hypernuclei with baryon number larger than 111, binding energy per baryon is around 7.0 MeV.
- When baryon number is under 41, the error from keeping the match radius unchanged is 0; the error increases with baryon number for heavier systems.

## Context
This work extends systematic RMF studies of hypernuclei, following earlier calculations by Sandulescu et al. who used the RMF-BCS approach to treat resonant states. The authors use the NL-SH parameter set, previously shown to describe finite nuclei and hypernuclei well. The study predicts 71 new Λ hypernuclei (5 with baryon number under 30, 66 with baryon number above 30) and suggests that hypernuclei built on magic-number cores or α-cluster cores are particularly stable, enabling future experimental searches for these predicted species.
