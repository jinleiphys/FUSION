# Wakes in the quark-gluon plasma

> Using the high temperature approximation we study, within the linear response theory, the wake in the quark-gluon plasma by a fast parton owing to dynamical screening in the space like region. When the parton moves with a speed less than the average speed of the plasmon, we find that the wake structure corresponds to a screening charge cloud traveling with the parton with one sign flip in the induced charge density resulting in a Lennard-Jones type potential in the outward flow with a short range repulsive and a long range attractive part. On the other hand if the parton moves with a speed higher than that of plasmon, the wake structure in the induced charge density is found to have alternate sign flips and the wake potential in the outward flow oscillates analogous to Cerenkov like wave generation with a Mach cone structure trailing the moving parton. The potential normal to the motion of the parton indicates a transverse flow in the system. We also calculate the potential due to a color dipole and discuss consequences of possible new bound states and $J/ψ$ suppression in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Using linear response theory within the high-temperature (Hard Thermal Loop) approximation, the authors show that a fast parton moving through the quark-gluon plasma generates a wake structure that depends critically on whether the parton speed is below or above the average plasmon phase velocity (0.77c), with the latter case producing oscillatory Cerenkov-like wakes and Mach cone structures that were incompletely captured in prior work by Ruppert and Müller (2005).

## Method

The authors apply linear response theory to compute the induced color charge density and screening potential from a fast parton moving through a QGP described by the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) dielectric functions in the high-temperature limit. They evaluate the Fourier integrals for the induced charge density and wake potential in cylindrical coordinates, using the longitudinal dielectric function from HTL perturbation theory, and analyze the spatial structure for parton velocities below and above the average plasmon phase velocity. They also compute the color dipole potential from two moving charges and discuss implications for bound states and J/ψ suppression.

## Key numbers

- Average plasmon phase velocity: √(3/5)c = 0.77c, used as the threshold separating subsonic and supersonic parton motion regimes
- Debye screening mass: m_D² = g²T²(1 + N_f/6), where N_f is the number of light quark flavors
- Plasma frequency: ω_pl = √(m_D²/3)
- For v = 0.55c (below 0.77c), the induced charge density shows a screening cloud with one sign flip and a Lennard-Jones type potential with short-range repulsive and long-range attractive parts
- For v = 0.99c (above 0.77c), the induced charge density shows alternate sign flips and the wake potential oscillates with a Mach cone structure
- Mach opening angle estimate: φ_M ≈ arccos(c_s/v) with c_s = 0.48c within HTL, giving Δφ ≈ π ± 1.01
- For v = 0.99c, γv ≥ 4, corresponding to the ultra-relativistic limit where hard collisional and radiative energy loss dominate

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ruppert and Müller (2005) on wakes in the QGP by completing the HTL perturbation theory treatment that the earlier work had incompletely applied, and extends the authors' own prior work (Mustafa 2005) which was restricted to outward flow, small velocities (v ≤ 0.8c), and omitted magnetic interactions. The results enable quantitative predictions for Mach cone opening angles that can be tested against 3-particle correlation measurements at RHIC experiments (STAR and PHENIX), and provide a framework for studying possible colored bound states and J/ψ suppression mechanisms in the QGP.
