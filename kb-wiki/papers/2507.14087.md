# Global Bayesian Analysis of $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ Photoproduction on Proton and Lead Targets

> We perform a global Bayesian analysis of diffractive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production in $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions using a color glass condensate (CGC) based calculation framework. As past calculations have shown that CGC-based models typically overpredict the $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production in $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions at high center of mass energy, we address the question of whether it is possible to describe coherent and incoherent diffractive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ data from $γ+p$ collisions at HERA and the LHC, and from $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions at the LHC simultaneously. Our results indicate that a simultaneous description of $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ data is challenging, with results improving when an overall $K$-factor -- scaling $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ cross sections to absorb model uncertainties -- is introduced.

## Key claim

A simultaneous description of diffractive J/ψ photoproduction data from γ+p collisions at HERA and the LHC and from γ+Pb collisions at the LHC is challenging within the color glass condensate framework, and the tension cannot be easily removed by introducing an overall K-factor to absorb model uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed a global Bayesian analysis of coherent and incoherent diffractive J/ψ production in γ+p and γ+Pb collisions using a color glass condensate (CGC) based calculation framework. The dipole-target scattering amplitude was obtained by combining the McLerran-Venguopalan (MV) model at x_P = 0.01 with JIMWLK evolution to describe the energy dependence, and Gaussian process emulators were trained on approximately 1500 design points to enable efficient sampling of the 11-dimensional parameter space.

## Key numbers

- The infrared regulator m in the MV model was constrained with a MAP value of 0.31 GeV (K ≡ 1) and 0.51 GeV (variable K)
- The proton size parameter B_G had a MAP value of 2.99 GeV^{-2} (K ≡ 1) and 3.83 GeV^{-2} (variable K)
- The hot spot size B_q had a MAP value of 0.07 GeV^{-2} (K ≡ 1) and 0.30 GeV^{-2} (variable K)
- The magnitude of Q_s fluctuations σ had a MAP value of 0.99 (K ≡ 1) and 0.88 (variable K)
- The ratio Q_s/(g^2 μ) had a MAP value of 0.63 (K ≡ 1) and 0.37 (variable K)
- The JIMWLK infrared regulator m_JIMWLK had a MAP value of 0.12 GeV (K ≡ 1) and 0.16 GeV (variable K)
- The spatial Λ_QCD had a MAP value of 0.0097 GeV (K ≡ 1) and 0.0348 GeV (variable K)
- The K-factor MAP value was 0.308 when allowed to vary
- The χ^2/dof improved from 5.94 (K ≡ 1) to 1.82 (variable K)
- The closure test metric ⟨Δ⟩ = 0.040 ± 0.002, corresponding to normalized standard deviation σ ∼ 0.2
- The charm quark mass was fixed at m_c = 1.4 GeV
- The infrared regulator μ_0 in the running coupling was set to 0.28 GeV
- The number of flavors was set to n_f = 3

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based calculations from Mantysaari et al. (2022, 2023) that successfully described γ+p data but overpredicted γ+Pb cross sections at high center-of-mass energy W. The analysis systematically tests whether a more flexible initial state description, including modifications to the proton shape, hot spot structure, and ultraviolet damping, can resolve the tension between proton and lead target data. The results enable future work to explore next-to-leading order corrections, as suggested by Luszczak et al. (2024), and to incorporate preliminary ATLAS data once the discrepancy with ALICE data at midrapidity is resolved.
