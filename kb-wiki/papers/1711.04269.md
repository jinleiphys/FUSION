# Color superconductivity from the chiral quark-meson model

> We study the two-flavor color superconductivity of low-temperature quark matter in the vicinity of chiral phase transition in the quark-meson model where the interactions between quarks are generated by pion and sigma exchanges. Starting from the Nambu-Gor'kov propagator in real-time formulation we obtain finite temperature (real axis) Eliashberg-type equations for the quark self-energies (gap functions) in terms of the in-medium spectral function of mesons. Exact numerical solutions of the coupled nonlinear integral equations for the real and imaginary parts of the gap function are obtained in the zero temperature limit using a model input spectral function. We find that these components of the gap display a complicated structure with the real part being strongly suppressed above $2Δ_0$, where $Δ_0$ is its on-shell value. We find $Δ_0\simeq 40$ MeV close to the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) gap in the quark-meson model is a complex, frequency-dependent function with nontrivial structure, and that the on-shell gap value \(\Delta_0 \simeq 40\) MeV near the chiral phase transition is highly sensitive to the interaction strength, a 10% increase in coupling produces a four-fold increase in \(\Delta_0\).

## Method
The authors start from the Nambu-Gor'kov propagator in the real-time (Schwinger-Keldysh) formalism and derive finite-temperature Eliashberg-type equations for the quark self-energies (gap functions) in terms of the in-medium spectral functions of pions and sigma mesons. They then solve the coupled nonlinear integral equations for the real and imaginary parts of the gap function numerically in the zero-temperature limit, using a Gaussian approximation for the meson spectral functions with parameters matched to the quark-meson model.

## Key numbers
- On-shell gap value \(\Delta_0 \simeq 40\) MeV close to the chiral phase transition.
- Increasing the interaction strength by 10% (rescaling factor \(\eta = 1.1\)) produces a four-fold increase in \(\Delta_0\) relative to the \(\eta = 1.0\) case.
- For a larger rescaling \(\eta = 1.4\), the gap increases further to \(\Delta_0 \simeq 0.3\) GeV.
- The kernel function \(\lambda(\omega)\) is parameterized with \(\gamma = 0.0972\), \(g = 0.0077\), and \(\omega_0 = 0.1734\) (all in unspecified units consistent with the model).

## Context
This work extends previous perturbative QCD studies of 2SC pairing at asymptotically high densities (Schaefer 1999, Pisarski 2000, and others) to the regime near the chiral phase transition, where mesonic degrees of freedom are dominant. It also builds on recent functional renormalization group studies of the quark-meson model (Tripolt 2017) that found low-temperature entropy anomalies possibly indicating an instability toward color superconductivity. The results enable future calculations of frequency-dependent observables in color superconductors, such as the dynamical Meissner effect and transport coefficients like shear viscosity (Harutyunyan 2017).
