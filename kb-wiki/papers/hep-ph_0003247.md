# Multibaryons in the collective coordinate approach to the SU(3) Skyrme model

> We obtain the rotational spectrum of strange multibaryon states by performing the SU(3) collective coordinate quantization of the static multi-Skyrmions. These background configurations are given in terms of rational maps, which are very good approximations and share the same symmetries as the exact solutions. Thus, the allowed quantum numbers in the spectra and the structure of the collective Hamiltonians we obtain are also valid in the exact case. We find that the predicted spectra are in overall agreement with those corresponding to the alternative bound state soliton model.

# Multibaryons in the collective coordinate approach to the SU(3) Skyrme model

## Key claim

The rotational spectra of strange multibaryon states obtained via SU(3) collective coordinate quantization of static multi-Skyrmions (approximated by rational maps) are in overall agreement with those from the alternative bound state soliton model, but the predicted ordering of states differs for B=5, 8, and 9 at strangeness S=-B. The collective Hamiltonians and allowed quantum numbers are valid for the exact solutions because the rational map approximations share the same symmetries.

## Method

The authors embed SU(2) static multi-Skyrmion configurations (given by rational map Ansatze for baryon numbers B=3 through B=9) into the isospin subgroup of SU(3) flavor space. They perform rigid rotator quantization by introducing time-dependent collective coordinates for SU(3) flavor rotations and SO(3) spatial rotations, then diagonalize the resulting collective Hamiltonian (including flavor symmetry breaking terms from strange quark mass) using an expansion in SU(3) Wigner functions. The wavefunctions are projected onto one-dimensional irreducible representations of the soliton symmetry groups (platonic or dihedral) to determine allowed spin and isospin quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- For B=3, about 80% of the S=0 ground state wavefunction corresponds to the minimal SU(3) irrep; for B=9, the minimal irrep contributes less than 30%, with the rest distributed over nearly 10 irreps.
- The strange inertia parameter K^S decreases roughly as 1/B, while the flavor symmetry breaking parameter gamma increases as B^2.
- The classical soliton mass per baryon number M_sol/B lies above 1.5 GeV for all B up to 9, with values around 1.60 GeV.
- The energy cost for increasing strangeness by one unit is about 300 MeV, largely independent of baryon number for B >= 3.
- Three energy scales are identified: approximately 1 GeV (classical masses and S=0 rotational eigenvalues), about 300 MeV (one unit of strangeness excitation), and 10-100 MeV (spin-isospin excitations).
- For B=1, the Skyrmion mass is around 1.7 GeV using empirical f_pi = 93 MeV and e = 4.1, with f_K/f_pi = 1.29.

## Context

This work extends the rigid rotator approach (RRA) of Yabu and Ando (1988) to multi-Skyrmions with B >= 3, complementing prior bound state approach (BSA) studies of SU(3) multi-Skyrmions by Scoccola and Schat (1998) and Garcia-Recio, Scoccola, and Schat (2000). The rational map approximations follow Houghton, Manton, and Sutcliffe (1998), who showed these configurations share symmetries with exact numerical solutions found by Battye and Sutcliffe (1997). The results enable comparison of two quantization methods for strange multibaryons and suggest the approaches converge as baryon number increases, while also motivating future inclusion of Casimir effects (Manton and Klinkhamer, 1991; Walliser, 1998) to correct absolute mass predictions.
