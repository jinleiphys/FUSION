# Combination of complex momentum representation and Green's function methods in relativistic mean-field theory

> We have combined the complex momentum representation method with the Green's function method in the relativistic mean-field framework to establish the RMF-CMR-GF approach. This new approach is applied to study the halo structure of $^{74}$Ca. All the continuum level density of concerned resonant states are calculated accurately without introducing any unphysical parameters, and they are independent of the choice of integral contour. The important single-particle wave functions and densities for the halo phenomenon in $^{74}$Ca are discussed in detail.

# Combination of complex momentum representation and Green's function methods in relativistic mean-field theory

## Key claim
The RMF-CMR-GF approach yields continuum level densities for resonant states that are independent of the choice of integration contour in the complex momentum plane, provided the contour range is large enough to cover the resonances of interest.

## Method
The authors combined the complex momentum representation (CMR) method with the Green's function method within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework. The Dirac equation is solved in momentum space by discretizing the momentum integral over a finite set of points along a chosen complex contour, yielding a symmetric matrix eigenvalue problem. The continuum level density (CLD) is then obtained as the difference between the density of states from the full Hamiltonian and the density of continuum states from the asymptotic Hamiltonian, with the resonant states identified as peaks in the CLD.

## Key numbers
- The resonant state $1g_{7/2}$ in $^{74}$Ca was calculated using four different contours; contour1 used points $k_1 = 0$ fm$^{-1}$, $k_2 = 0.75 - i0.18$ fm$^{-1}$, $k_3 = 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$, and $k_{\text{max}} = 3.5$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The bound and resonant states in $^{74}$Ca were obtained with energies between $-10$ and $20$ MeV using the NL3 interaction.
- The Fermi surface in $^{74}$Ca is located at the last bound state $2d_{5/2}$.

## Context
This work extends the RMF-CMR approach developed by Li et al. (2016) for spherical nuclei and Fang et al. (2017) for deformed nuclei by incorporating the Green's function method to compute continuum level densities. It builds on prior CLD calculations in the relativistic framework by Shi et al. (2014, 2017) using complex scaling, which were not fully independent of the rotation angle. The method enables accurate determination of resonance parameters for both narrow and broad resonances in exotic nuclei, particularly for studying halo structures such as that in $^{74}$Ca, which was previously investigated using relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory by Meng et al. (2002) and relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory by Long et al. (2010).
