# On Neutrino Emission From Dense Matter Containing Meson Condensates

> We consider the rate at which energy is emitted by neutrinos from the dense interior of neutron stars containing a Bose condensate of pions or kaons. The rates obtained are larger, by a factor of 2, than those found earlier, and are consistent with those found for the direct Urca processes.

# On Neutrino Emission From Dense Matter Containing Meson Condensates

## Key claim

The neutrino energy emission rates from dense matter containing pion or kaon condensates, as originally calculated by Maxwell, Brown, Campbell, Dashen and Manassah (1977) and in nearly all subsequent studies, are too low by a factor of 2; the corrected rates are consistent with those found for the direct Urca process for nucleons. The error originates in the leptonic tensor, where the spin sum over final-state electrons was omitted, giving a factor of 2 too small in the matrix element.

## Method

The authors re-examined the leptonic contribution to the squared matrix element for neutrino-emitting reactions in matter with meson condensates, comparing the density-matrix formulation used by Maxwell, Brown, Campbell, Dashen and Manassah (1977) with the standard projection-operator approach. They identified that the leptonic tensor from Lifshitz and Pitaevskii (1973) as employed in the original work did not include a sum over final electron spins, leading to a missing factor of 2. The corrected emission rates are expressed in terms of the direct Urca rate from Lattimer, Pethick, Prakash and Haensel (1991), and the authors verified that the phase-space integrals in the original references were correctly performed.

## Key numbers

- The corrected neutrino energy emission rate for reactions involving a pion condensate is larger by a factor of 2 than the rate found by Maxwell, Brown, Campbell, Dashen and Manassah (1977).
- The direct Urca energy emission rate per unit volume is 2.21 x 10^26 (mu_e / 100 MeV) (1+3g_A^2) cos^2(theta_C) T_9^6 erg cm^{-3} sec^{-1}, where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K, mu_e is the electron chemical potential, g_A = 1.26 is the axial-vector coupling constant, and theta_C = 0.223 is the Cabibbo angle.
- For small condensate angle theta, the pion-condensate emission rate is (theta^2/4) g(p_e/k, p_n/k) [1 + (g_A k / p_e)^2] times the direct Urca rate, where the function g goes to unity in the limit k approaches 0.
- For kaon condensates, the emissivity for the reaction n(K) -> n(K) + e^- + anti-nu_e is (1/4) sin^2(theta) tan^2(theta_C) times the direct Urca rate; for p(K) -> p(K) + e^- + anti-nu_e it is sin^2(theta) tan^2(theta_C); and for n(K) <-> p(K) + e^- + anti-nu_e it is cos^2(theta/2).

## Context

This work corrects a long-standing error in the neutrino emission rates from meson-condensed matter originally computed by Maxwell, Brown, Campbell, Dashen and Manassah (1977) for pions and by Brown, Kubodera, Page and Pizzochero (1988) and Tatsumi (1988) for kaons. The corrected rates are now consistent with the direct Urca process rates calculated by Lattimer, Pethick, Prakash and Haensel (1991), resolving a discrepancy that had persisted in the literature. The correction does not significantly alter the relative importance of meson-induced cooling compared to other viable cooling processes for neutron stars, but it provides a consistent framework for comparing different neutrino emission mechanisms in dense matter.
