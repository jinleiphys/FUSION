# Bottomonium suppression at RHIC and LHC

> The strong suppression of heavy quarkonia is a good indicator that one has generated a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision. Recent advancements in first principles calculations of the heavy quark potential provide additional insight into this suppression and can be used to further pin down the in-medium properties of the QGP. Realistic 3+1d dissipative hydrodynamical models can be used to simulate the QGP and, with bottomonium as a probe, one can make inferences for the initial temperature of the QGP and other phenomenological parameters such as the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. However, progressing to LHC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions, one expects regeneration to have an increasingly important impact on the suppression observables. In this proceedings, we present a brief overview to set up the model and then provide model results for bottomonium suppression and regeneration, for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for bottomonium suppression and regeneration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions that uses two different heavy quark potentials (a pNRQCD-based Strickland-Bazow potential and a lattice-QCD-based Rothkopf potential) and finds that neither potential simultaneously describes both RHIC and LHC data perfectly: the Strickland-Bazow potential works well at LHC but underestimates suppression at RHIC, while the Rothkopf potential reproduces RHIC data but slightly overestimates suppression at LHC.

## Method

The authors used a 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) code to simulate the quark-gluon plasma, combined with two different heavy quark potentials to compute the binding energies of six bottomonium states as functions of energy density and momentum-space anisotropy. A first-order rate equation regeneration model was employed, using bottomonium state cross sections from experiment and a temperature-dependent breakup rate associated with the imaginary part of the binding energy, to compute local particle densities including bottom quark fugacity effects.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures for RHIC 200 GeV/nucleon Au-Au collisions: T0 = {0.442, 0.440, 0.439} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3}, respectively
- Initial temperatures for LHC 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions: T0 = {0.641, 0.632, 0.629} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3}, respectively
- Initial time τ0 = 0.25 fm/c for LHC √sNN = 5.02 TeV/nucleon Pb-Pb collisions
- Initial temperature range from comparisons between theory and experiment: T0 = 600-700 MeV at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c for LHC 5.02 TeV collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio range: 4π η/s ~ 1-3

## Context

This work extends previous bottomonium suppression studies by incorporating a more comprehensive model that includes both suppression and regeneration effects, using two different potentials: the pNRQCD-based Strickland-Bazow potential (Strickland 2011) and a new potential derived from first-principles lattice QCD studies (Rothkopf). The model enables future comparisons with continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD potential extractions and can be applied to other observables such as particle spectra, flow coefficients, and HBT radii as the aHydro framework advances (Almaalol 2018, Alqahtani 2017).
