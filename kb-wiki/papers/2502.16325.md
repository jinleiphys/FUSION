# Calorimetric analysis for long-baseline neutrino experiments

> In neutrino long-baseline experiments the energy of the incoming neutrino must be reconstructed from observations of the final state. We first discuss the problems that arise for energy conservation during the final-state interactions in a momentum-dependent potential. We then show, for the example of the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment, that the presence of such a potential is necessarily connected with a large uncertainty in the reconstructed energy. This same uncertainty also affects the determination of energy- and four-momentum-transfers in neutrino-nucleus reactions. We analyze the origins of these uncertainties using transport theory for the description of the evolution of the final state of the reaction. We show that the spectral functions of target nuclei play an essential role not only for the initial neutrino-nucleus interaction but also for the final state evolution.

## Key claim

The calorimetric method for reconstructing neutrino energy in long-baseline experiments like DUNE carries a significant intrinsic uncertainty even with a perfect detector, because the in-medium nuclear potential and binding energies of nucleons in the target cause energy to be redistributed among observed and unobserved final-state particles in ways that cannot be experimentally disentangled.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU event generator, which is based on Kadanoff-Baym non-equilibrium transport theory and includes a consistent position- and momentum-dependent nuclear potential for both the ground state and final-state propagation of hadrons. They generated neutrino-40Ar events using the DUNE flux, then applied the calorimetric method to reconstruct the missing energy (the difference between the energy transfer and the sum of observed final-state particle energies), comparing results with and without corrections for average binding energy. They also analyzed the full energy balance including nucleons that participate in final-state interactions but remain bound in the target remnant.

## Key numbers

- Up to about 20 nucleons are unbound in the final state for DUNE, with additional up to 6 nucleons excited but still bound in the potential
- Nucleons still bound in the target remnant carry an excitation energy of about 6 MeV each, typical of a compound nucleus
- The separation energy for nucleons at the Fermi surface is about 8 MeV, with a maximum binding of about 70 MeV
- Average binding energy corrections of 28 MeV and 38 MeV were tested in the missing energy analysis
- The number of nucleons involved in collisions but not making it out of the nucleus is about one half of the number of free, observed nucleons

## Context

This work extends the analysis of energy reconstruction uncertainties in long-baseline neutrino experiments, building on prior studies by Ankowski (2015) and Mosel (2016) on the kinematical method and by Ankowski (2015) on the calorimetric method. It directly responds to a recent study by Coyle (2025) showing that generators GENIE and NuWro give narrower missing energy distributions than GiBUU, which the authors attribute to those generators neglecting a final-state potential. The results imply that generators must contain a consistent in-medium potential and proper energy-momentum conservation in final-state collisions to reliably reconstruct neutrino energy, and that the same uncertainties affect reconstruction of energy transfer and four-momentum transfer Q².
