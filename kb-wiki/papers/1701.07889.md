# Effect of tetrahedral shapes in heavy and superheavy nuclei

> We search for effects of tetrahedral deformation $β_{32}$ over a range of $\sim 3000$ heavy and superheavy nuclei, $82\leq Z \leq 126$, using a microscopic-macroscopic model based on the deformed Woods-Saxon potential, well tested in the region. We look for the energy minima with a non-zero tetrahedral distortion, both absolute and conditional - with the quadrupole distortion constrained to zero. In order to assure reliability of our results we include 10 most important deformation parameters in the energy minimization. We could not find any cases of stable tetrahedral shapes. The only sizable - up to 0.7 MeV - lowering of the ground state occurs in superheavy nuclei $Z\geq 120$ for $N=173-188$, as a result of a {\it combined} action of two octupole deformations: $β_{32}$ and $β_{30}$, in the ratio $β_{32}/β_{30}\approx \sqrt{3/5}$. The resulting shapes are moderately oblate, with the superimposed distortion $β_{33}$ {\it with respect to the oblate axis}, which makes the equator of the oblate spheroid slightly triangular. Almost all found conditional minima are excited and not protected by any barrier, a handful of them are degenerate with the axial minima.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence for stable tetrahedral shapes in any of the approximately 3000 heavy and superheavy nuclei studied. The only sizable ground-state energy lowering (up to 0.7 MeV) occurs in superheavy nuclei with Z ≥ 120 and N = 173–188, but this results from a combined action of two octupole deformations (β₃₂ and β₃₀) in a specific ratio, producing an oblate shape with a triangular equator rather than a pure tetrahedral shape.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic-macroscopic model based on the deformed Woods-Saxon potential with the Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy, including 10 deformation parameters in the energy minimization. They performed three types of calculations: (1) conditional minima with quadrupole deformations fixed at zero, minimizing over seven other parameters; (2) ground-state minima over seven axial deformations; and (3) ground-state minima over ten deformations including β₂₂, β₃₂, and β₄₂. For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, blocking was used with minimization over configurations from 10 levels below to 10 levels above the Fermi level.

## Key numbers

- The search covered approximately 3000 nuclei with Z between 82 and 126.
- The largest β₃₂ values in conditional minima reach approximately 0.2.
- Fourteen conditional minima below 2 MeV excitation energy were found in neutron-rich Z=84–94 nuclei: nine in Po isotopes, four in Rn isotopes, and in ²²³Np.
- The barrier between the tetrahedral and axial prolate ground state in ²²²Rn is less than 300 keV.
- The maximal ground-state lowering by the combined oblate-β₃₃ deformation is 730 keV, occurring for the nucleus ²⁹⁶123.
- In ²⁹⁶123, the oblate-β₃₃ minimum lies 720 keV lower than the axially symmetric oblate minimum; in ³⁰⁵124 the difference is 530 keV.
- With a 10% increase in pairing strengths, the β₃₃ ground state in ²⁹⁶123 still lies 450 keV lower than the axially symmetric minimum.
- The effect occurs in approximately 40 superheavy nuclei with Z=119–126 and N=173–188.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei (e.g., the earlier work by Jachimowicz, Kowal, and Skalski from 2015) by expanding the deformation space to 10 parameters and including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei. The results contradict earlier predictions of stable tetrahedral minima in heavy nuclei and instead identify a new type of combined oblate-β₃₃ deformation in superheavy nuclei, previously reported for ³⁰⁸126 by Jachimowicz, Kowal, and Skalski (2010). The findings suggest that future experimental searches for exotic shapes in superheavy nuclei should focus on the predicted oblate-β₃₃ ground states rather than pure tetrahedral shapes.
