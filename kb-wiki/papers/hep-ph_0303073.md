# Impact of CFL Quark Matter on the Cooling of Compact Stars

> The cooling mechanism of compact stars with quark cores in the color-flavor locked phase is discussed. It is argued that the high thermal conductivity of the quark core plays a key role in the stellar cooling. It implies that the cooling time of compact stars with color-flavor locked quark cores is similar to that of ordinary neutron stars, unless the star is almost completely made of color-flavor locked quark matter.

# Impact of CFL Quark Matter on the Cooling of Compact Stars

## Key claim
The cooling time of compact stars with color-flavor locked (CFL) quark cores is similar to that of ordinary neutron stars, unless the star is almost completely made of CFL quark matter. This conclusion depends on the high thermal conductivity of the CFL core being sufficient to conduct heat to the outer nuclear layer, where neutrino and photon emission can occur efficiently.

## Method
The authors calculated the photon mean free path in CFL quark matter using the Thomson cross section and equilibrium electron/positron densities from finite-temperature plasma theory. They estimated the thermal conductivity from massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons (phi) and photons, using the core radius as the effective mean free path when interaction lengths exceed the core size. They then compared the thermal energy stored in the CFL core versus the nuclear matter layer and evaluated the implications for neutrino and photon cooling timescales.

## Key numbers
- Photon mean free path in CFL phase: less than or equal to 220 m for temperatures greater than or equal to 25 keV; exceeds 1 km for temperatures below 23.4 keV
- Thomson cross section used: 66.54 fm^2
- Thermal conductivity for T less than or equal to 25 keV: kappa_CFL = 1.2 x 10^32 T_MeV^3 R_0,km erg cm^-1 sec^-1 K^-1
- Thermal conductivity for T greater than or equal to 25 keV: kappa_CFL = 7.2 x 10^31 T_MeV^3 R_0,km erg cm^-1 sec^-1 K^-1
- Thermal energy of CFL core: E_CFL(T) = 2.1 x 10^42 R_0,km^3 T_MeV^4 erg
- Thermal energy of nuclear matter layer: E_NM(T) = 8.1 x 10^49 (M - M_0)/M_sun (rho/rho_0)^(-2/3) T_MeV^2 erg
- Mean free path for phi-phi scattering: approximately 8 x 10^5 mu_500^8 / T_MeV^9 km

## Context
This work extends the analysis of CFL quark matter cooling by Shovkovy and Ellis (2002) and addresses the role of thermal conductivity raised in earlier studies of compact star cooling. The authors build on the understanding of CFL phase properties from Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the low-energy effective theory of Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999). The paper contradicts suggestions by other authors (2001, 2002) that exotic quark components are required to explain observed neutron star cooling and radii. The results enable future observational tests: if a compact star is almost pure CFL quark matter, it would be very dim and difficult to detect except by gravitational means.
