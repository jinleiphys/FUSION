# A Reanalysis of the LSND Neutrino Oscillation Experiment

> We reanalyse the LSND neutrino oscillation results in the framework of the Projected Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (PQRPA), which is the only RPA model that treats the Pauli Principle correctly, and accounts satisfactorily for great majority of the weak decay observables around 12C. We have found that the employment of the PQRPA inclusive DIF 12C(nu_e,e-)12N cross-section, instead of the CRPA used by the LSND collaboration in the (nu_mu ->nu_e) oscillations study of the 1993-1995 data sample, leads to the following: 1) the oscillation probability is increased from (0.26 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.05) percents to (0.33 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.13) percents, and 2) the previously found consistence between the (sin^2 2theta, Delta m^2) confidence level regions for the (nu_mu -> nu_e) and the (bar{nu}_mu -> bar{nu}_e) oscillations is significantly diminished. These effects are not due to the difference in the uncertainty ranges for the neutrino-nucleus cross-section, but to the difference in the cross-sections themselves.

## Key claim

Using the Projected Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (PQRPA) instead of the Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) for the inclusive DIF 12C(nu_e,e-)12N cross-section increases the LSND oscillation probability from (0.26 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.05)% to (0.33 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.13)% and significantly diminishes the previously found consistency between the (sin^2 2theta, Delta m^2) confidence level regions for nu_mu -> nu_e and anti-nu_mu -> anti-nu_e oscillations, an effect driven by the difference in the cross-sections themselves rather than by uncertainty ranges.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the LSND 1993-1995 data sample for DIF nu_mu -> nu_e oscillations by replacing the CRPA cross-section used in the original LSND analysis with the PQRPA cross-section for the 12C(nu_e,e-)12N reaction. They computed the flux-averaged inclusive cross-sections and the oscillation probability using the same event selection efficiency, neutrino fluxes, and resolution function as the LSND collaboration, then performed a chi-squared minimization with a raster scan method to determine confidence regions in the (sin^2 2theta, Delta m^2) parameter space.

## Key numbers

- Oscillation probability with PQRPA: (3.3 +/- 1.0 +/- 1.3) x 10^{-3} (i.e., 0.33 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.13%)
- Oscillation probability with CRPA: (2.6 +/- 1.0 +/- 0.5) x 10^{-3} (i.e., 0.26 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.05%)
- PQRPA inclusive DIF cross-section for 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N: 13.0 x 10^{-40} cm^2
- CRPA inclusive DIF cross-section for 12C(nu_mu,mu-)12N: 21.1 to 22.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2
- PQRPA inclusive DAR cross-section for 12C(nu_e,e-)12N: 18.6 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- CRPA inclusive DAR cross-section for 12C(nu_e,e-)12N: 42.3 to 44.3 x 10^{-42} cm^2
- PQRPA systematic uncertainty on cross-section: 20%
- Total systematic error for PQRPA analysis: 28%
- Number of oscillation events with PQRPA: N^{osc}_nu = 21.5 +/- 6.6 +/- 8.5

## Context

This work extends the LSND oscillation analysis (Athanasopoulos 1998, Aguilar 2001) by applying the PQRPA nuclear model (Krmpotic 2002, 2005), which treats the Pauli principle correctly and accounts for most weak decay observables around 12C within 20%, unlike the CRPA (Kolbe 1994) used originally. The reanalysis shows that the choice of nuclear cross-section model significantly affects the inferred oscillation parameters, and the previously claimed consistency between the nu_mu -> nu_e and anti-nu_mu -> anti-nu_e LSND signals is weakened, which has implications for attempts to accommodate the LSND anomaly within four-neutrino or CPT-violating scenarios.
