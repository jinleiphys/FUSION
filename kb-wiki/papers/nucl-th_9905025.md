# Quark Dynamics on Phase Space

> We discuss the dynamics of quarks within a Vlasov approach. We use an interquark ($qq$) potential consistent with the indications of Lattice QCD calculations and containing a Coulomb term, a confining part and a spin dependent term. Hadrons masses are shown to arise from the interplay of these three terms plus the Fermi motion and the finite masses of the quarks. The approach gives a lower and an upper bound for hadrons. The theoretical predictions are shown to be in fairly good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The Vlasov equation with a Richardson-type interquark potential (Coulomb, confining, and spin-dependent terms) can reproduce hadron masses from heavy (c-cbar, b-bbar) to light (pion, nucleon, Delta) systems, but the radii of light hadrons are underestimated, suggesting the need for relativistic corrections to the potential term.

## Method

The author solves the Vlasov equation for the one-body distribution function of quarks in phase space, representing each quark by a large number of test particles (5000 to 50000) whose Hamilton equations of motion are integrated with a fourth-order Adams-Bashforth method. The interquark potential includes a Coulomb term, a confining term from the Richardson potential with scale parameter Lambda, and a chromomagnetic spin-dependent term approximated by a Gaussian of width sigma=0.5 fm. Hadron masses are found by minimizing the total energy (Fermi motion plus potential plus quark masses) as a function of the initial radius of the quark distribution.

## Key numbers

*   Pion (ud, S=0) mass: 0.140 GeV, with RMS radius 0.33 fm.
*   Nucleon (uud, S=1/2) mass: 0.85 GeV, with RMS radius 0.53 fm.
*   Delta (uud, S=3/2) mass: 1.3 GeV, with RMS radius 0.75 fm.
*   c-cbar system minimum mass: 2.9 GeV (compared to 3.1 GeV from Richardson (1979)).
*   b-bbar system minimum mass: 9.5 GeV (compared to 9.4 GeV from Richardson (1979)).
*   Fitted scale parameter Lambda: 0.250 GeV (for light mesons; 0.398 GeV for heavy systems).
*   Fitted strong coupling constant alpha_s_bar: 0.225.
*   Quark masses used: u=0.13 GeV, d=0.13 GeV, s=0.35 GeV, c=1.45 GeV, b=4.8 GeV, t=180 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Vlasov approach, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Bonasera et al., 1994), to the internal dynamics of quarks in hadrons. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic potential models of Povh et al. (1995) by including relativistic kinematics, which allows smaller (u,d) quark masses (0.13 GeV versus 300 MeV). The approach reproduces the quantum results of Richardson (1979) for heavy quarks and gives predictions for unobserved resonances. It enables future calculation of the equation of state of quark matter and the addition of a collision term for hadron-hadron dynamics.
