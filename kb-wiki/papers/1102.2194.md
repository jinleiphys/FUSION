# Medium Modifications and Production of Charmonia at LHC

> A previously constructed transport approach to calculate the evolution of quarkonium yields and spectra in heavy-ion collisions is applied to Pb-Pb($\sqrt{s}$=2.76\,ATeV) collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). In this approach spectral properties of charmonia are constrained by euclidean correlators from thermal lattice QCD and subsequently implemented into a Boltzmann equation accounting for both suppression and regeneration reactions. Based on a fair description of SPS and RHIC data, we provide predictions for the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ yields at LHC. The main uncertainty is associated with the input charm cross section, in particular its hitherto unknown reduction due to shadowing in nuclear collisions. Incomplete charm-quark thermalization and non-equilibrium in charmonium chemistry entail a marked reduction of the regeneration yield compared to the statistical equilibrium limit.

# Medium Modifications and Production of Charmonia at LHC

## Key claim

The authors predict that in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, the final inclusive J/psi yield will be dominated by regeneration from coalescence of charm and anti-charm quarks for centralities with more than 100-150 participants, but will remain well below the statistical hadronization model equilibrium limit by about a factor of three, implying a nuclear modification factor below one even without shadowing corrections.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann transport equation (reduced to a thermal rate equation) for charmonium abundances in a thermally expanding fireball background, using in-medium spectral properties (mass, width, binding energy) of J/psi, psi' and chi_c constrained by Euclidean correlators from thermal lattice QCD. Two limiting scenarios are investigated: a weak-binding scenario (WBS) with J/psi dissociation temperature of 1.2 T_c and a strong-binding scenario (SBS) with dissociation temperature of 2 T_c. The fireball evolution is constructed from measured charged-particle multiplicities, and the rate equation includes both suppression and regeneration reactions with a relaxation-time approximation for incomplete charm-quark thermalization.

## Key numbers

- Charged-hadron multiplicity for 0-5% central Pb-Pb: dN_ch/deta approximately 1600 (N_part approximately 380)
- Initial temperature in central Pb-Pb: T_0 = 610 MeV (compared to 330 MeV at RHIC)
- QGP lifetime at LHC: tau_QGP approximately 6 fm/c (compared to 3 fm/c at RHIC)
- Default midrapidity open-charm cross section in pp at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: dsigma_pp^{c cbar}/dy = 0.75 mb (estimated range 0.7-1 mb)
- Default J/psi cross section in pp at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: dsigma_pp^{J/psi}/dy = 4 micro-barn, implying a J/psi-to-c-cbar fraction of 0.53%
- Final inclusive J/psi nuclear modification factor R_AA in central Pb-Pb without shadowing: approximately 0.8 for both WBS and SBS
- R_AA in central Pb-Pb with 1/3 shadowing reduction of charm cross section: approximately 0.45
- Primordial J/psi suppression factor in central Pb-Pb: approximately a factor of 10
- Crossing between primordial and regenerated components in transverse momentum: p_t approximately 5-6 GeV for central collisions
- Time window for active regeneration: Delta tau approximately 2-3 fm/c in both scenarios

## Context

This work extends the transport approach of Zhao and Rapp (2010) from SPS (sqrt(s)=17.3 AGeV) and RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 AGeV) to LHC energies, where both weak-binding and strong-binding scenarios had been compatible with existing data despite a factor of approximately 10 difference in collision energy. The predictions contrast with the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. (2006, 2010), which assumes chemical equilibrium at hadrochemical freezeout; the authors find that inelastic charmonium reaction rates near T_c are too small to establish relative chemical equilibrium, leading to J/psi yields well below the statistical model limit. The results also provide a postdiction for the ATLAS collaboration's central-to-peripheral ratio measurement at high p_t, which is not sensitive to regeneration contributions.
