# Old and New Facets of y-Scaling: the Universal Features of Nuclear Structure Functions and Nucleon Momentum Distributions

> Some systematic general features of y-scaling structure functions, which are essentially independent of detailed dynamics, are pointed out. Their physical interpretation in terms of general characteristics, such as a mean field description and nucleon-nucleon correlations, is given and their relationship to the momentum distributions illustrated. A new scaling variable is proposed which allows a direct expression of the scaling function in term of an integral over the momemtum distributions thereby avoiding explicit consideration of binding corrections.

# Old and New Facets of y-Scaling: the Universal Features of Nuclear Structure Functions and Nucleon Momentum Distributions

## Key claim

The paper identifies universal, essentially dynamics-independent features of the y-scaling function f(y) for nuclei with A < 56, showing that f(y) can be decomposed into a mean-field contribution and a universal correlation contribution with an exponential tail whose slope parameter b is independent of nuclear mass number. The authors propose a new scaling variable y₂ that incorporates the excitation energy of the residual (A-1) system, allowing direct extraction of the longitudinal momentum distribution from experimental data without requiring theoretical binding corrections.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental inclusive quasi-elastic electron scattering data for nuclei with A < 56, extracting the scaling function F(y) and then the longitudinal momentum distribution f(y) by inverting the integral relation between f(y) and the nucleon momentum distribution n(k). They parametrized the observed systematic features of f(y) using a two-component analytic form: a mean-field term (A e^{-a²y²}/(α² + y²)) dominating at small y and a universal exponential term (B e^{-b|y|}) dominating at large y. A new scaling variable y₂ was derived from energy conservation by incorporating the excitation energy of the residual system, modeled using the physical picture that high-momentum nucleons are balanced by a correlated partner nucleon.

## Key numbers

- f(0) decreases monotonically with A from approximately 10 MeV⁻¹ for A=2 to approximately 3 MeV⁻¹ for heavy nuclei
- For y near zero, f(y) ~ (α² + y²)⁻¹ with α ranging from approximately 45 MeV for A=2 to approximately 140 MeV for A=56
- For 50 MeV ≤ |y| ≤ 200 MeV, F(y) ~ e^{-a²y²} with a ranging from approximately 50 MeV for A=2 to approximately 150 MeV for A=56
- For |y| ≥ 400 MeV, f(y) ~ B e^{-b|y|} with B ranging from 2.5 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹ for A=2 to 2 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹ for A=56, and b = 6 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ independent of A
- For ⁴He, the parametrization yields occupation probabilities S₀ = 0.76 (mean field) and S₁ = 0.24 (correlations), compared with theoretical values S₀ ~ 0.8 and S₁ ~ 0.2

## Context

This work extends the y-scaling formalism originally developed by West (1975) and the spectral function approach of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators, providing a systematic phenomenological analysis of scaling functions across a range of nuclei. The identification of a universal exponential tail in f(y) with a slope independent of A connects to the universal nature of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, and the proposed new scaling variable y₂ enables direct experimental access to momentum distributions without relying on theoretical binding corrections. The results enable comparison with many-body calculations of momentum distributions and occupation probabilities, such as those for ⁴He, and the authors note that improved inclusive data expected from CEBAF could further minimize experimental uncertainties.
