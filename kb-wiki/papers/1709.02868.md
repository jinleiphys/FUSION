# Time-Dependent Observables in Heavy Ion Collisions II: in Search of Pressure Isotropization in the $φ^4$ Theory

> To understand the dynamics of thermalization in heavy ion collisions in the perturbative framework it is essential to first find corrections to the free-streaming classical gluon fields of the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The corrections that lead to deviations from free streaming (and that dominate at late proper time) would provide evidence for the onset of isotropization (and, possibly, thermalization) of the produced medium. To find such corrections we calculate the late-time two-point Green function and the energy-momentum tensor due to a single $2 \to 2$ scattering process involving two classical fields. To make the calculation tractable we employ the scalar $φ^4$ theory instead of QCD. We compare our exact diagrammatic results for these quantities to those in kinetic theory and find disagreement between the two. The disagreement is in the dependence on the proper time $τ$ and, for the case of the two-point function, is also in the dependence on the space-time rapidity $η$: the exact diagrammatic calculation is, in fact, consistent with the free streaming scenario. Kinetic theory predicts a build-up of longitudinal pressure, which, however, is not observed in the exact calculation. We conclude that we find no evidence for the beginning of the transition from the free-streaming classical fields to the kinetic theory description of the produced matter after a single $2 \to 2$ rescattering.

## Key claim

The paper finds no evidence for the onset of isotropization after a single $2 \to 2$ rescattering of two classical fields in scalar $\varphi^4$ theory, contradicting the kinetic theory prediction that such a rescattering would generate longitudinal pressure and cause the energy density to decrease faster than $1/\tau$.

## Method

The authors calculate the late-time two-point Green function and energy-momentum tensor from a single $2 \to 2$ scattering process involving two classical fields in massless $\varphi^4$ theory, using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. They compute the relevant Feynman diagrams (labeled I, II, II', III, III') in both momentum space and coordinate space, then compare the exact diagrammatic results to the predictions from kinetic theory (Boltzmann equation) for the same process.

## Key numbers

- The classical (leading-order) correlation function scales as $G^{LO}_{22}(x_1, x_2) \propto 1/\tau$ at late proper times $\tau \gg 1/Q_s$, consistent with free streaming
- The classical energy-momentum tensor at late times gives $\epsilon = 2 P_T \sim 1/\tau$ and $P_L = 0$, characterizing a free-streaming anisotropic medium
- Kinetic theory predicts corrections at order $\lambda^2$ (where $\lambda$ is the $\varphi^4$ coupling) with $P_L = \lambda^2 B^{(1)}/\tau$ and $\epsilon$ decreasing faster than $1/\tau$ due to a $\ln(\tau/\tau_0)$ term
- The exact diagrammatic calculation yields results consistent with free streaming and inconsistent with the kinetic theory prediction

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis in Part I (Wu and Kovchegov, 2017) which showed that the ordering of time scales in the late-time limit determines whether Boltzmann equation predictions or free-streaming behavior emerges. The paper directly tests the "bottom-up thermalization" scenario proposed by Baier et al. (2000) and the applicability of Boltzmann equation to heavy ion collisions argued by Mueller (2002) and Arnold (2002). The results contradict kinetic theory predictions and support the earlier arguments by Kovchegov (2005) that perturbative corrections to classical gluon fields in heavy ion collisions may always lead to free streaming rather than isotropization.
