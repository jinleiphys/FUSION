# Harmonic Oscillator Trap and the Phase-Shift Approximation

> The energy-spectrum of two point-like particles interacting in a 3-D isotropic Harmonic Oscillator (H.O.) trap is related to the free scattering phase-shifts $δ$ of the particles by a formula first published by Busch et al. It is here used to find an expression for the \it shift \rm of the energy levels, caused by the interaction, rather than the perturbed spectrum itself. In the limit of high energy (large quantum number $n$ of the H.O.) this shift is shown to be given by $-2\fracδπ$, also valid in the limit of infinite as well as zero scattering length at all H.O. energies. Numerical investigation shows that the shifts differ from the exact result of Busch et al, by less than $<\frac{1}{2}\%$ except for $n=0$ when it can be as large as $\approx 2.5\%$. This approximation for the energy-shift is well known from another exactly solvable model, namely that of two particles interacting in a spherical infinite square-well trap (or box) of radius $R$ in the limit $R\rightarrow \infty$, and/or in the limit of large energy. It is in this context referred to as the \it phase-shift approximation \rm. It can be (and has been) used in (infinite) nuclear matter calculations to calculate the two-body effective interaction in situations where in-medium effects can be neglected. It has also been used in expressing the energy of free electrons in a metal.

# Harmonic Oscillator Trap and the Phase-Shift Approximation

## Key claim

The energy shift of two point-like particles interacting in a 3-D isotropic Harmonic Oscillator trap converges to the phase-shift approximation (shift = -2δ/π) in the limit of high quantum number n, and also exactly in the limits of infinite and zero scattering length at all H.O. energies; numerical comparison shows the approximation differs from the exact Busch formula by less than 0.5% except for n=0 where the discrepancy can reach approximately 2.5%.

## Method

The author recasts the Busch formula (Busch et al., 1998), which relates the two-particle energy spectrum in a 3-D H.O. trap to free scattering phase-shifts δ, into an expression for the energy shift Δ rather than the perturbed spectrum itself. Using the reflection formula for Gamma functions, the shift is expressed as Δ = -(2/π) arctan[tan(δ(k))/A(z)], where A(z) is a function that converges to 1 for increasing z. The phase-shift approximation Δ = -2δ/π is then compared numerically to the exact result for H.O. quantum numbers n = 0, 1, 2 as a function of scattering length a. A parallel analysis is performed for two particles in a spherical infinite square-well trap, where the energy shift is derived from boundary conditions on the wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The phase-shift approximation differs from the exact Busch result by less than 0.5% for all n except n=0, where the discrepancy can be as large as approximately 2.5%.
- For the H.O. trap, the largest discrepancy occurs for n=0 and negative scattering lengths; the correcting factor A(z) is approximately 0.9 for negative scattering lengths and approaches 1 for positive scattering lengths.
- For the spherical square-well trap, the deviation from the phase-shift approximation can be as large as 30%, compared to at most approximately 2.5% for the H.O. trap.
- In the square-well case, the phase-shift approximation becomes exact when a/R → 0 (scattering length small compared to box radius) and for large n.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Busch formula (Busch et al., 1998) for two particles in a 3-D H.O. trap, which has been used in interpreting atomic physics experiments (Sto et al., 2006; Osp et al., 2006) and in nuclear structure calculations (Rotureau, 2013; Yan et al., 2013). The phase-shift approximation was previously derived for two particles in a spherical infinite square-well trap in the large-box limit by Fuchs (1955), Gottfried (1966), Fukuda and Newton (1956), DeWitt (1956), and Riesenfeld and Watson (1956), and was used in early nuclear matter calculations (Brueckner, 1954; Brueckner and Gammel, 1960; Sood and Moszkowski, 1960). The present work shows that the phase-shift approximation is significantly more accurate for the H.O. trap than for the square-well trap, enabling its use as a simple alternative to the Busch formula for extracting phase-shifts from experimental data and for constructing two-body spectra in H.O. traps.
