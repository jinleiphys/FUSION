# Kaon-condensed hypernuclei as highly dense self-bound objects

> The structure of $K^-$-condensed hypernuclei, which may be produced in the laboratory in strangeness-conserving processes, is investigated using an effective chiral Lagrangian for the kaon-baryon interaction, combined with a nonrelativistic baryon-baryon interaction model. It is shown that a large number of negative strangeness is needed for the formation of highly dense and deeply bound state with kaon condensates and that part of the strangeness should be carried by hyperons mixed in the nucleus. The properties of kaon-condensed hypernuclei such as the ground state energy and particle composition are discussed. Such a self-bound object has a long lifetime and may decay only through weak interaction processes. Comparison with other possible nuclear states is also made, such as kaon-condensed nuclei without mixing of hyperons and noncondensed multistrange hypernuclei. Implications of kaon-condensed hypernuclei for experiments are mentioned.

## Key claim

The paper claims that kaon-condensed hypernuclei (KCYN) can exist as highly dense self-bound objects formed in strangeness-conserving processes, but only if a large number of negative strangeness is present and part of that strangeness is carried by hyperons mixed into the nucleus, not solely by the kaon condensate.

## Method

The author uses an effective chiral Lagrangian for s-wave kaon-baryon interactions combined with a nonrelativistic baryon-baryon interaction model to compute the volume energy of a spherical liquid-drop nucleus containing condensed K- mesons and mixed hyperons (Lambda, Xi-, Sigma-). The total energy includes surface energy (with a surface tension coefficient of 1 MeV/fm^2) and bulk Coulomb energy, and the ground state is found by solving the classical kaon field equation together with chemical equilibrium conditions for strong processes (K- p -> Lambda, K- n -> Sigma-, K- Lambda -> Xi-) and conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness.

## Key numbers

* The K- optical potential at nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is estimated to be -115 MeV for the standard parameter choice a_3 = -0.9.
* The kaon-nucleon sigma term Sigma_Kn is taken as 305 MeV for the standard parameter set.
* The Lambda potential depth in nuclear matter is set to -27 MeV, the Xi- potential depth to -16 MeV, and the Sigma- potential depth to +23.5 MeV (repulsive) with an isospin-dependent term b_SigmaN rho_0 = 40.2 MeV.
* Two baryon-baryon interaction parameter sets are used: H-EOS (A) gives nuclear incompressibility K = 306 MeV, and H-EOS (B) gives K = 236 MeV (within the empirical range 210 +/- 30 MeV).
* The surface tension coefficient is set to sigma = 1 MeV/fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of kaon condensation coexisting with hyperonic matter in neutron stars (Muto 2002, 2007) to a strangeness-conserving system that could be created in the laboratory. It contrasts with kaon-condensed nuclei without hyperon mixing and with noncondensed multistrange hypernuclei, and relates to the ongoing controversy between Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002, 2007) who propose a deep K- potential based on Lambda(1405) as a K- p bound state, versus Oset and Toki (2006) who argue for a shallow potential from chiral unitary models. The work also connects to studies of deeply bound kaonic nuclear states and their implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars.
