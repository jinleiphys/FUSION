# $^{22}$C: $S$-Wave Two-Neutron Halo

> A dripline nucleus $^{22}$C is studied in a Borromean three-body model of $^{20}$C+$n$+$n$. The valence neutrons, interacting via a realistic potential, are constrained to be orthogonal to the occupied orbits in $^{20}$C. We obtain ample results supporting that $^{22}$C is an ideal $S$-wave two-neutron halo nucleus: The ground state is bound by 390-570 keV, the root mean square neutron and proton radii are 4.0 and 2.4 fm, and the two neutrons are predominantly in $(s_{1/2})^2$ orbits. The binding mechanism of $^{22}$C is discussed. One- and two-body density distributions elucidate the halo character as well as the correlated motion of the neutrons. The reaction cross sections of $^{22}$C+$^{12}$C collisions are predicted.

# $^{22}$C: An S-Wave Two-Neutron Halo Nucleus

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^{22}$C is an almost pure S-wave two-neutron halo nucleus, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the $^{20}$C core has a closed $(0d_{5/2})^6$ configuration (the N=14 subshell closure) and that the valence neutrons are constrained to be orthogonal to the occupied orbits in $^{20}$C. The ground state binding energy is found to be 390-570 keV, with the actual value depending on the choice of the neutron-core potential.

## Method

The authors model $^{22}$C as a Borromean three-body system of $^{20}$C + n + n, using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis functions. The valence neutrons interact via the realistic G3RS nucleon-nucleon potential, and the neutron-core potential is constructed to satisfy two conditions: the $1s_{1/2}$ orbit is unbound (since $^{21}$C is unstable) and the $0d_{5/2}$ orbit is bound by at most 2.93 MeV. The Pauli principle is enforced by requiring the valence neutrons to be orthogonal to the occupied single-particle orbits ($0s_{1/2}$, $0p_{3/2}$, $0p_{1/2}$, and $0d_{5/2}$) of the $^{20}$C core.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: 390-570 keV (range across four potential sets A-D), consistent with the empirical value of -0.423 ± 1.140 MeV
- Root mean square neutron radius: 3.93-4.12 fm (across potential sets)
- Root mean square proton radius: 2.43-2.44 fm (across potential sets)
- Root mean square matter radius: 3.58-3.74 fm (across potential sets)
- Probability of $(s_{1/2})^2$ component: 0.915-0.968 (across potential sets)
- Probability of spin-singlet (S=0) component: 0.981-0.998 (across potential sets)
- For set B potential: $\langle v_{12}(\text{tensor+spin-orbit})\rangle$ = 7 keV, $\langle U_{1}(\text{spin-orbit})\rangle$ = 57 keV
- For set B potential: energy decomposition gives $2\times(7.185-6.436)-0.118-1.868 = -0.489$ MeV
- Root mean square matter radius of $^{20}$C core: 2.99 fm (neutron 3.23 fm, proton 2.37 fm)

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-rich carbon isotopes, where the N=14 subshell closure has been experimentally confirmed around $^{22}$O (Ozawa 2000, Thirolf, Cortina, Stanoiu, Becheva) and the competition between $0d_{5/2}$ and $1s_{1/2}$ neutron orbits determines the structure of A=15-20 carbon isotopes. The authors note that $^{22}$C is analogous to $^{11}$Li as a Borromean system, but differs because $^{11}$Li contains both $(0p_{1/2})^2$ and $(1s_{1/2})^2$ components (Thompson, Simon, Varga) while $^{22}$C is dominated by $(s_{1/2})^2$. The predicted reaction cross sections for $^{22}$C+$^{12}$C collisions are intended to guide future experimental measurements, which have not yet reached $^{22}$C (Ozawa).
