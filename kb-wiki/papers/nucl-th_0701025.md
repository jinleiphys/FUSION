# Calculation of beta-decay rates in a relativistic model with momentum-dependent self-energies

> The relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA) is applied in the calculation of beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei in the $Z\approx 28$ and $Z\approx 50$ regions. The study is based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation of nuclear ground-states, using effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, and also extended by the inclusion of couplings between the isoscalar meson fields and the derivatives of the nucleon fields. This leads to a linear momentum dependence of the scalar and vector nucleon self-energies. The residual QRPA interaction in the particle-hole channel includes the $π+ ρ$ exchange plus a Landau-Migdal term. The finite-range Gogny interaction is employed in the T=1 pairing channel, and the model also includes a proton-neutron particle-particle interaction. The results are compared with available data, and it is shown that an extension of the standard relativistic mean-field framework to include momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies naturally leads to an enhancement of the effective (Landau) nucleon mass, and thus to an improved PN-QRPA description of beta-decay rates.

## Key claim

Extending the standard relativistic mean-field framework to include momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies naturally enhances the effective (Landau) nucleon mass, leading to improved proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-QRPA) descriptions of beta-decay rates; however, even with the highest achievable effective mass of m* = 0.79 m, the results are not as good as those from advanced non-relativistic self-consistent density-functional plus continuum-QRPA calculations.

## Method

The authors performed fully consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) plus proton-neutron relativistic QRPA (PN-RQRPA) calculations of beta-decay half-lives for neutron-rich Fe, Ni, Zn, Cd, Sn, and Te isotopic chains. They used three effective interactions in the particle-hole channel: the standard density-dependent DD-ME1, the D³C model with momentum-dependent self-energies, and a newly adjusted D³C* interaction with an even higher effective nucleon mass (m* = 0.79 m). The residual QRPA interaction included π+ρ exchange plus a Landau-Migdal term, the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction was employed in the T=1 pairing channel, and a T=0 proton-neutron particle-particle interaction was also included.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon masses used: DD-ME1 (m*/m = 0.66), D³C (m*/m = 0.71), D³C* (m*/m = 0.79)
- Dirac masses: DD-ME1 (m_D/m = 0.58), D³C (m_D/m = 0.54), D³C* (m_D/m = 0.57)
- Nuclear matter compression moduli: DD-ME1 (K_∞ = 244.5 MeV), D³C (K_∞ = 232.5 MeV), D³C* (K_∞ = 224.9 MeV)
- Landau-Migdal strength parameters adjusted to reproduce Gamow-Teller resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb: g' = 0.55 (DD-ME1), 0.54 (D³C), 0.76 (D³C*)
- Transition energies for the strongest transition (ν 2p₁/₂ → π 2p₃/₂) in ⁷⁶Zn: 0.15 MeV (DD-ME1), -0.05 MeV (D³C), 1.74 MeV (D³C*)
- T=0 pairing strength V₀ = 125 MeV for Fe isotopes, V₀ = 300 MeV for Zn isotopes, V₀ = 235 MeV for Cd, Sn, and Te isotopes
- Effective weak axial nucleon coupling constant g_A = 1 used (quenched from g_A = 1.26)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RHB+RQRPA calculations (Marketin, Vretenar, Ring, 2005) which used an ad hoc interaction with tensor coupling to increase the effective mass. The momentum-dependent D³C model (Typel, 2003, 2005) provides a more natural solution by allowing higher effective nucleon mass while maintaining a small Dirac mass for correct spin-orbit splittings. The results are compared with non-relativistic self-consistent density-functional plus continuum-QRPA calculations by Borzov (2003, 2005, 2006) and the HFB+QRPA model with Skyrme interactions by Engel et al. (1999), which achieve better agreement with data. The authors note that further improvement would require going beyond mean-field to include particle-vibration coupling, and that first-forbidden transitions must be included for heavier neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis.
