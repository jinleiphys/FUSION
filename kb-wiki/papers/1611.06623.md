# Influence of Nambu-Goldstone mode on energy-weighted sum of excitation strengths in random-phase approximation

> Influence of the Nambu-Goldstone (NG) mode on the energy-weighted sum (EWS) of the excitation strengths is analyzed, within the random-phase approximation (RPA). When a certain symmetry is broken at the mean-field level, a NG mode emerges in the RPA, which can be represented by canonical variables forming a two-dimensional Jordan block. A general formula is rederived which separates out the NG-mode contribution to the EWS, via the projection on the subspace directed by the NG mode. As examples, the formula is applied to the $E1$ excitation and the rotational excitations in nuclei, further confirming theoretical consistency of the RPA.

## Key claim
The Nambu-Goldstone (NG) mode, which emerges from spontaneous symmetry breaking at the mean-field level, contributes a non-vanishing term to the energy-weighted sum (EWS) of excitation strengths in the random-phase approximation (RPA), even though the NG mode itself has zero excitation energy. This contribution is expressed in a general operator form that clarifies its role and is shown to be necessary for maintaining the theoretical consistency of the RPA sum rule.

## Method
The analysis uses the mathematical properties of RPA solutions, specifically the decomposition of the RPA space by a complete set of solutions and Jordan bases. A projection operator is applied to separate the subspace directed by the NG mode, which is represented by canonical variables forming a two-dimensional Jordan block. A general formula for the NG-mode contribution to the EWS is rederived in an operator form and then applied to the E1 excitation and rotational excitations in nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For the translational NG mode with the uncorrected E1 operator $\hat{T}^{(E1)\prime} = Z \mathbf{R}_p$, the NG-mode contribution to the EWS is $\Sigma_1^{(0)} = Z^2 / (2AM)$, where $A$ is the mass number and $M$ is the nucleon mass.
- After subtracting this NG-mode contribution, the EWS for the uncorrected operator recovers the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule value of $ZN / (2AM)$ for a single direction.
- For the corrected E1 operator $\hat{T}^{(E1)} = (ZN/A)(\mathbf{R}_p - \mathbf{R}_n)$, the NG-mode contribution $\Sigma_1^{(0)}$ vanishes because $[\mathbf{P}, \mathbf{R}_p - \mathbf{R}_n] = 0$.

## Context
This work extends the general analysis of RPA solutions by Nakada (2016) to explicitly examine the influence of Nambu-Goldstone modes on the energy-weighted sum rule. It provides a more convenient operator form of a formula previously given in an appendix by Sagawa and Jansen (2003). The results confirm the theoretical consistency of the RPA framework by showing how the NG-mode contribution correctly accounts for spurious center-of-mass motion in E1 excitations and is applicable to rotational excitations in deformed nuclei.
