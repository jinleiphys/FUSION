# Symmetry breaking of Gamow-Teller and magnetic-dipole transitions and its restoration in calcium isotopes

> Nuclear magnetic-dipole (M1) and Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions provide insight into the spin-isospin properties of atomic nuclei. By considering them as unified spin-isospin transitions, the M1/GT transition strengths and excitation energies are subject to isospin symmetry. The excitation properties associated to the M1/GT symmetry need to be clarified within consistent theoretical approach. In this work, the relationship between the M1 and GT transitions in Ca isotopes is investigated in a unified framework based on the relativistic energy-density functional (REDF) with point-coupling interactions, using the relativistic quasi-particle random-phase approximation (RQRPA). It is shown that the isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) residual interaction affects both transitions, and the symmetry of M1 and giant-GT transitions is disrupted by this interaction in closed-shell nuclei. In open-shell Ca isotopes, the proton-neutron pairing in the residual RQRPA interaction also plays a role in GT transitions. Due to the interplay between these interactions, the M1/GT symmetry can be restored especially in the $^{42}$Ca nucleus, i.e., the giant-GT strength can become comparable to that of the M1 mode in terms of the unified spin-isospin transitions by adjusting the PN-pairing strength to reproduce the experimental low-lying GT-excitation energies. The mirror symmetry of both M1 and GT transitions is also demonstrated for open-shell mirror partners, $^{42}$Ca and $^{42}$Ti. Further improvements are required to achieve simultaneous reproduction of M1 and GT-transition energies in the REDF framework.

## Key claim

The isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) residual interaction disrupts the symmetry between M1 and giant Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in closed-shell nuclei, but this symmetry can be restored in open-shell calcium isotopes through the interplay of IV-PV and proton-neutron pairing interactions, particularly in $^{42}$Ca where the giant-GT strength becomes comparable to the M1 mode by adjusting the pairing strength to reproduce experimental low-lying GT excitation energies.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic quasi-particle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) based on relativistic energy-density functionals (REDF) with point-coupling interactions (DD-PC1 and DD-PCX). For open-shell nuclei, isospin-triplet (T1) pairing was used for proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs, while isospin-singlet (T0) proton-neutron pairing was included for GT transitions with a strength parameter f=0.81 adjusted to reproduce the low-lying GT(-) excitation energy of $^{42}$Ca. The IV-PV residual interaction was included at the RQRPA level, and response functions were calculated for both M1 and GT transitions in calcium isotopes and $^{208}$Pb.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying GT(-) excitation energy of $^{42}$Ca used for adjusting the PN-pairing strength is 0.611 MeV (from Fujita 2020)
- For $^{48}$Ca with DD-PC1 and $\alpha_{\rm IV-PV}=1.70$ fm$^2$, the RRPA gives GT(-) peaks at 2.53 MeV and 13.43 MeV, and M1 excitation energy at 9.42 MeV (experimental M1: 10.23 MeV from Tompkins 2011; experimental low-lying GT: 2.517 MeV from Fujita 2020; experimental giant GT: approximately 10.8 MeV from Yako 2009)
- For $^{208}$Pb with $\alpha_{\rm IV-PV}=1.70$ fm$^2$ and DD-PC1, the M1 and GT(-) energies are overshot by 1-2 MeV relative to experiment (experimental M1 approximately 7.3 MeV from Laszewski 1988; experimental GT: 15.6 MeV from Akimune 1994 and Wakasa 2012)
- The non-perturbed M1 and GT responses for Ca isotopes show $B_X(^{48}{\rm Ca}) \cong 4B_X(^{42}{\rm Ca})$ for both M1 and GT, proportional to the number of valence neutrons around the $^{40}$Ca core
- The Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum rule for GT transitions is reproduced up to 90-95% with a small deficiency from omitting Dirac-sea states

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA studies of M1 transitions by Kruzic (2020) and Oishi (2020) and GT transitions by Paar (2004) and Niu (2012) to a unified framework treating both modes as spin-isospin transitions. The study demonstrates that the IV-PV residual interaction, which corresponds to the spin-isospin term in the Landau-Migdal interaction, disrupts the symmetry between M1 and giant GT transitions in closed-shell nuclei like $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, while proton-neutron pairing can restore this symmetry in open-shell nuclei like $^{42}$Ca. The results enable future systematic studies of spin-isospin properties across the nuclear chart but show that simultaneous reproduction of M1 and GT transition energies across different mass regions remains challenging within the current REDF framework, suggesting the need for system-dependent tuning of the IV-PV coupling constant or more complicated parameterization.
