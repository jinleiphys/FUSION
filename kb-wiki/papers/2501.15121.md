# Study on the Distribution Amplitude of the Scalar Meson $K_0^*(1430)$

> Based on sum rules, we explore the twist-2 distribution amplitude of the $K_0^*(1430)$ meson, treating it as the ground state of a quark-antiquark system. We posit that the spacetime distance $x$ should be infinitesimally close to the quark separation $ z$. By incorporating quark distance corrections, with $x^2 \approx z^2 \approx x z$, the calculated moments yield additional insights. Moreover, we employ light-cone sum rules to compute the form factors for the semi-leptonic decay process $B_s \rightarrow K$. The reliability of the computed distribution amplitude is confirmed through its comparison with the form factor.

# Study on the Distribution Amplitude of the Scalar Meson $K_0^*(1430)$

## Key claim

The authors claim that the twist-2 distribution amplitude of the $K_0^*(1430)$ meson can be computed more accurately by assuming the spacetime distance $x$ is infinitesimally close to the quark separation $z$, using the approximation $x^2 \approx z^2 \approx x z$, rather than the traditional assumption $z^2 = 0$. The reliability of the resulting distribution amplitude is confirmed through comparison with form factors for the $B_s \rightarrow K$ semileptonic decay, though the computed moments and Gegenbauer coefficients differ significantly from prior sum rules and light-front model results.

## Method

The authors construct a correlation function using interpolating currents within the framework of QCD sum rules, performing the operator product expansion (OPE) in the deep Euclidean region using Background Field Theory. They derive sum rules for the moments $\langle\xi^n\rangle$ of the twist-2 distribution amplitude of the $K_0^*(1430)$ meson, incorporating corrections from the quark separation distance approximation $x^2 \approx z^2 \approx x z$. The moments are then used to extract Gegenbauer coefficients via the Gegenbauer polynomial expansion of the distribution amplitude, and these coefficients are subsequently employed in light-cone sum rules to compute the form factors $f_+$, $f_-$, and $f_T$ for the $B_s \rightarrow K$ semileptonic decay process.

## Key numbers

- First four moments of the $K_0^*(1430)$ distribution amplitude: $\xi^1 = -0.337$, $\xi^2 = -0.116$, $\xi^3 = -0.224$, $\xi^4 = -0.105$
- First four Gegenbauer coefficients: $B_1 = 0.561$, $B_2 = 0.024$, $B_3 = 0.423$, $B_4 = 0.019$
- Form factors at $q^2 = 0$: $f_+ = +0.115$, $f_- = -0.115$, $f_T = +0.164$
- Input parameters: $\langle\bar{u} u\rangle = -2.417 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$, $\langle\bar{s} s\rangle = \kappa \langle\bar{u} u\rangle$ with $\kappa = 0.74$, $\langle g_s \bar{u} \sigma T G u\rangle = -1.934 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^5$, $\langle g_s \bar{u} u\rangle^2 = 2.082 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^6$
- Borel window for moments: $M^2 = 1.4 \sim 1.8$ GeV$^2$; threshold $s_0 = (1.944)^2$ GeV$^2$
- For form factors: $m_b = 4.8$ GeV, $m_B = 5.368$ GeV, $f_B = 0.23$ GeV, $s_0^B = 34$ GeV$^2$, $M^2 = 14$ GeV$^2$, scale $\mu = 2.4$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rules approach to meson distribution amplitudes previously applied by Cheng (2005) and Huang (2022), and contrasts with the light-front quark model results of Chen (2021). The authors treat the $K_0^*(1430)$ as a quark-antiquark ground state, in contrast to alternative interpretations as a four-quark state (Cheng, 2019), a molecular state (Han, 2013), or a glueball/hybrid state (Bharucha, 2015). The computed distribution amplitude exhibits a four-peak pattern unlike the bimodal pattern from the LCHO model (Huang, 2022) and light-front model (Chen, 2021), and the form factor results are compared with those from light-cone sum rules (Sun, 2010; Khosravi, 2022), sum rules (Yang, 2005), and perturbative QCD (Li, 2008). The method enables improved precision for distribution amplitude calculations that could be extended to other mesons.
