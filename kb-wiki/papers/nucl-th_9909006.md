# Quadrupole deformations of neutron-drip-line nuclei studied within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov approach

> We introduce a local-scaling point transformation to allow for modifying the asymptotic properties of the deformed three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator wave functions. The resulting single-particle bases are very well suited for solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for deformed drip-line nuclei. We then present results of self-consistent calculations performed for the Mg isotopes and for light nuclei located near the two-neutron drip line. The results suggest that for all even-even elements with $Z$=10--18 the most weakly-bound nucleus has an oblate ground-state shape.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis for solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in deformed drip-line nuclei, and the results suggest that for all even-even elements with Z=10-18 the most weakly-bound nucleus has an oblate ground-state shape, though the authors describe this as a preliminary study and note that extensive calculations throughout the mass table will be presented in a future publication.

## Method

The authors developed a local-scaling point transformation that modifies the asymptotic properties of deformed three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator wave functions, producing a THO basis with correct exponential decay at large distances while preserving the HO behavior near the origin. They then performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using this THO basis for Mg isotopes and light nuclei near the two-neutron drip line, restricting the analysis to axially symmetric shapes and using a spherical HO basis with a single common oscillator length.

## Key numbers

- The THO basis includes HO states up to 20 major shells
- The study covers even-even elements with Z=10-18 at the two-neutron drip line
- The asymptotic decay constant kappa is given by kappa = d_1/(2L_0), where d_1 is determined from the LST function parameters and L_0 is the common oscillator length

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the THO basis for spherical nuclei (Stoitsov 1998a, 1998b) to axially deformed weakly-bound nuclei, providing an alternative to coordinate-space solutions of the HFB equations that are difficult for deformed systems. The approach contrasts with methods using eigenstates of the particle-hole Hamiltonian truncated at several MeV into the continuum (Terasaki 1997, Tajima 1997), and with standard HO basis expansions that converge too slowly for drip-line nuclei. The results enable future systematic calculations throughout the mass table and more detailed analysis of pairing interactions.
