# $η'$ Production in Nucleus-Nucleus collisions as a probe of chiral dynamics

> We argue that, because of the peculiar properties of the $η'$ meson, it is a promising probe of "chiral" dynamics. In particular, we show that a rotating gluon-dominated plasma might lead to an enhanced production of $η'$ w.r.t. statistical model expectations. The presence of a strong topological susceptibility might give a similar effect. In both cases, unlike the statistical model,we expect a non-trivial dependence on event geometry, such as initial volume and impact parameter. Hence, an observation of $η'/π^0$ ratio depending strongly on impact parameter might be a good indication of chiral effects, either from vorticity or topological phases of QCD

# η′ Production in Nucleus-Nucleus collisions as a probe of chiral dynamics

## Key claim

The momentum-integrated η′/π⁰ ratio, measured as a function of impact parameter in nucleus-nucleus collisions, could signal chiral dynamics, either from vorticity in a rotating gluon-dominated plasma or from topological charge fluctuations, provided the observed centrality dependence deviates from the constant ratio expected from the statistical hadronization model. The author emphasizes that it is the *impact parameter dependence*, rather than the absolute value of the ratio, that constitutes the probe.

## Method

The author derives the η′ production rate from a rotating thermal quark-gluon plasma by treating the gluon-gluon-η′ vertex (which couples gluon momenta and polarizations via the Levi-Civita tensor) as a coalescence kernel at hadronization, assuming a Jüttner-type gluon distribution with a position-dependent temperature and flow velocity field. For topological charge fluctuations, the effective Hamiltonian acquires a θ-term, and the η′ abundance is estimated from the thermal occupation number of gluon modes in regions of correlated topological susceptibility, using a chiral chemical potential ansatz. Both mechanisms are compared to the statistical model baseline, where particle ratios depend only on freeze-out temperature and are volume-independent.

## Key numbers

- The η′ meson mass is considerably heavier than similar pseudoscalar states (π, K, η), attributed to U_A(1) breaking by instantons.
- The η′ → γγ branching ratio is 2.2%; the η′ → ρ⁰γ branching ratio is 30%.
- The vorticity-driven estimate uses Δ = 1 fm, T = 170 MeV, with angular momentum and volume taken from Fig. 3 of Becattini (2013) for top RHIC energies.
- The statistical model background for η′/π is estimated using SHARE and is of order exp[(-m_η′ + m_π)/T], independent of production volume.
- Lattice results (Fodor, 2016) show ⟨θ²⟩ ∼ χ/T⁴ remains approximately constant for T = 100–200 MeV and falls rapidly at higher temperatures.
- The topological correlation volume V_θ is estimated to be ∼1 fm in size.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Jeon and collaborators (2000) that η′ production via gluon-gluon fusion can probe polarized parton distributions, applying the same vertex to a thermal, rotating medium in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with prior proposals for measuring parity-odd bubbles via η′ mass shifts or η′–η mixing (Kharzeev, 1998; Pisarski, 1998), which the author argues are experimentally difficult because the η′ is too long-lived to show in-medium modifications. The paper suggests that a centrality-dependent η′/π ratio, unlike strangeness enhancement which scales with multiplicity (David, 2011), could distinguish vortical from topological chiral effects and motivates experimental searches with electromagnetic calorimetry at RHIC and LHC.
