# Structure of the nucleon and spin-polarizabilities

> Spin-polarizabilities are predicted by calculating the cross-section difference $σ_{3/2}-σ_{1/2}$ from available data for the resonance couplings $A_{3/2}$ and $A_{1/2}$ and CGLN amplitudes. The forward spin-polarizabilities are predicted to be $γ^{(p)}_0=-0.58\pm 0.20$ and $γ^{(n)}_0=+0.38\pm 0.22$ in units of $10^{-4}$fm$^4$ where the different signs are found to be due to the isospin dependencies of the $E_{0+}$ and the $(M,E)^{(1/2)}_{1+}$ amplitudes. The backward spin-polarizabilities are predicted to be $γ^{(p)}_π=-36.6$ and $γ^{(n)}_π=+58.3$, to be compared with the experimental values $γ^{(p)}=-36.4\pm 1.5$ and $γ^{(n)}_π=+58.6\pm 4.0$. Electric $γ_E$ and magnetic $γ_M$ spin-polarizabilities are introduced and discussed in terms of the $E1$ and $M1/E2$ components of the photo-absorption cross section of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper predicts forward spin-polarizabilities for the proton and neutron by calculating the cross-section difference σ₃/₂−σ₁/₂ from resonance couplings and CGLN amplitudes, with the hedge that these predictions are not directly measured but derived from world data on pion photoproduction and resonance parameters, and that the quoted errors of ±0.20 and ±0.22 reflect a 1σ uncertainty of 3–4% on the dominant contributions.

## Method

The authors calculate the spin-dependent cross-section difference σ₃/₂−σ₁/₂ by combining nonresonant contributions from CGLN multipole amplitudes (E₀₊, M₁₋, and (M,E)₁₊) with resonant contributions from nucleon resonances parameterized via the Walker formalism using resonance couplings A₁/₂ and A₃/₂ from the Particle Data Group. The forward spin-polarizability γ₀ is then obtained through the dispersion integral γ₀ = −(1/4π²) ∫ [σ₃/₂(ω)−σ₁/₂(ω)] dω/ω³, and the backward spin-polarizability γ_π is computed using a similar integral with additional kinematic factors and t-channel pole contributions from π⁰, η, and η' mesons. The method also introduces separate electric (γ_E) and magnetic (γ_M) spin-polarizabilities derived from the forward and backward combinations.

## Key numbers

- Forward spin-polarizability for the proton: γ₀^(p) = −0.58 ± 0.20 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Forward spin-polarizability for the neutron: γ₀^(n) = +0.38 ± 0.22 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Backward spin-polarizability for the proton: γ_π^(p) = −36.6 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, compared to experimental value −36.4 ± 1.5
- Backward spin-polarizability for the neutron: γ_π^(n) = +58.3 in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, compared to experimental value +58.6 ± 4.0
- Scaling factor for the P₃₃(1232) resonance: A(P₃₃(1232)) = 1.26, corresponding to Re δ = −4.4% for the E₂/M₁ ratio at its maximum
- The P₃₃(1232) resonance contributes −3.03 to γ₀^(p) and γ₀^(n), and +5.11 to γ_π^(p) and γ_π^(n)
- The E₀₊ nonresonant amplitude contributes +2.47 to γ₀^(p) and +3.18 to γ₀^(n)
- The π⁰ t-channel contributes −46.7 to γ_π^(p) and +46.7 to γ_π^(n)

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Schumacher (2005–2010) for describing nucleon Compton scattering in terms of partial contributions from one-photon and two-photon excitation mechanisms, and supplements the analysis of Pasquini (2010) which constructed forward amplitudes for polarized Compton scattering via dispersion integrals. The key advance is the use of an isospin decomposition of CGLN amplitudes from Drechsel (2007) combined with resonance couplings from the Particle Data Group, which avoids the two-pion channel problem present in CGLN-only analyses. The predicted forward spin-polarizabilities enable future experimental extraction from polarized Compton scattering experiments, and the introduction of electric and magnetic spin-polarizabilities provides a more transparent decomposition of the spin-dependent response of the nucleon.
