# Early quark deconfinement in compact star astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions

> Based on a recently developed relativistic density functional approach to color-superconducting quark matter and a novel quark-hadron transition construction which phenomenologically accounts for the effects of inhomogeneous pasta phases and quark-hadron continuity, we construct a class of hybrid equations of state applicable at the regimes typical for compact star astrophysics and heavy ion collisions. We outline that early quark deconfinement is a notable consequence of strong diquark pairing providing a good agreement with the observational data and driving the trajectories of the matter evolution during the supernovae explosions toward the regimes typical for the compact star mergers and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Early quark deconfinement in compact star matter is a notable consequence of strong diquark pairing, which drives the trajectories of matter evolution during supernova explosions toward regimes typical for compact star mergers and heavy-ion collisions, while providing good agreement with observational data.

## Method

The authors constructed a class of hybrid equations of state by combining a relativistic density functional approach to color-superconducting quark matter with a hadronic equation of state, using a two-zone interpolation scheme that phenomenologically accounts for inhomogeneous pasta phases and quark-hadron continuity. The quark matter model parameters were fixed to reproduce pion mass, decay constant, scalar meson mass, and vacuum chiral condensate. The hybrid equations of state were then applied to solve the relativistic hydrostatic equilibrium problem for cold and warm neutron stars.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic density functional approach of Kaltenborn (2017) and Ivanytskyi (2022) for quark matter, and the two-zone interpolation scheme of Ayriyan (2021) for mixed quark-hadron phases, to construct hybrid equations of state applicable to both compact star astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions. The approach enables simulations of supernova explosions, neutron star mergers, and heavy-ion collisions using a unified framework, and provides agreement with astrophysical constraints from Riley (2021), Miller (2021), LIGO Scientific (2018), Bauswein (2017), and Annala (2017).
