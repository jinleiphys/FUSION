# Pentaquark $őė^+$ production from the reaction $ő≥p\toŌÄ^+K^-őė^+$

> The cross section for $őė^+$ production from the reaction $ő≥p\toŌÄ^+K^-őė^+$, which was observed in the CLAS experiment at the Jefferson National Laboratory, is evaluated in a hadronic model that includes couplings of $őė^+$ to both $KN$ and $K^*N$. With their coupling constants determined from the empirical $ŌÄNN(1710)$ and $ŌĀNN(1710)$ coupling constants using the SU(3) symmetry, the cross section for this reaction has been evaluated by taking $őė^+$ to have spin 1/2 and isospin 0 but either positive or negative parity. We find that the cross section is 10-15 nb if $őė^+$ has positive parity as predicted by the chiral soliton model. The cross section is reduced by more than a factor of 10 if $őė^+$ has negative parity as given by lattice QCD studies. For both parities, the differential distribution peaks at small negative four momentum transfer as expected from the dominating $t$-channel kaon-exchange diagram that involves only the coupling of $őė^+$ to $KN$.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic model based on gauged SU(3) flavor symmetric Lagrangians, including couplings of the őė‚Āļ to both KN and K*N, with coupling constants g_KNőė and g_K*Nőė determined from the empirical ŌÄNN(1710) and ŌĀNN(1710) couplings via SU(3) relations assuming N(1710) is an ideal mixture of antidecuplet and octet pentaquark baryons. They evaluated the amplitudes for the t-channel kaon-exchange, s-channel, and u-channel diagrams for ő≥p ‚Üí KŐĄ*‚Āįőė‚Āļ, then multiplied by the 2/3 branching ratio for KŐĄ*‚Āį ‚Üí ŌÄ‚ĀļK‚ĀĽ to obtain the cross section for ő≥p ‚Üí ŌÄ‚ĀļK‚ĀĽőė‚Āļ, using covariant form factors with a cutoff parameter őõ = 0.8 GeV determined from charmed hadron production data.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Liu, Ko, and collaborators (2003) on őė‚Āļ production in reactions ő≥p ‚Üí KŐĄ‚Āįőė‚Āļ and ő≥n ‚Üí K‚ĀĽőė‚Āļ, where cross sections of a few hundred nb were predicted, to the reaction ő≥p ‚Üí ŌÄ‚ĀļK‚ĀĽőė‚Āļ observed by the CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab. The results show that measuring this cross section can help determine the parity of the őė‚Āļ pentaquark, which is debated between the positive parity predicted by the chiral soliton model (Diakonov, 1997) and the negative parity favored by lattice QCD studies (Sasaki, 2003; Ciskor, 2003). The calculation uses improved form factors following the method of Janssen (2001) for lambda photoproduction, and the results enable experimental comparison with CLAS data to distinguish between parity assignments.
