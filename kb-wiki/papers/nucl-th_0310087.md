# Pentaquark $Θ^+$ production from the reaction $γp\toπ^+K^-Θ^+$

> The cross section for $Θ^+$ production from the reaction $γp\toπ^+K^-Θ^+$, which was observed in the CLAS experiment at the Jefferson National Laboratory, is evaluated in a hadronic model that includes couplings of $Θ^+$ to both $KN$ and $K^*N$. With their coupling constants determined from the empirical $πNN(1710)$ and $ρNN(1710)$ coupling constants using the SU(3) symmetry, the cross section for this reaction has been evaluated by taking $Θ^+$ to have spin 1/2 and isospin 0 but either positive or negative parity. We find that the cross section is 10-15 nb if $Θ^+$ has positive parity as predicted by the chiral soliton model. The cross section is reduced by more than a factor of 10 if $Θ^+$ has negative parity as given by lattice QCD studies. For both parities, the differential distribution peaks at small negative four momentum transfer as expected from the dominating $t$-channel kaon-exchange diagram that involves only the coupling of $Θ^+$ to $KN$.

## Key claim

The cross section for the reaction γp → π⁺K⁻Θ⁺ is 10–15 nb if the Θ⁺ has positive parity (as predicted by the chiral soliton model), but is reduced by more than a factor of 10 if the Θ⁺ has negative parity (as given by lattice QCD studies), with the caveat that these values depend on the coupling constants determined via SU(3) relations from the N(1710) resonance and on the cutoff parameter in the hadronic form factors.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic model based on gauged SU(3) flavor symmetric Lagrangians, including couplings of the Θ⁺ to both KN and K*N, with coupling constants g_KNΘ and g_K*NΘ determined from the empirical πNN(1710) and ρNN(1710) couplings via SU(3) relations assuming N(1710) is an ideal mixture of antidecuplet and octet pentaquark baryons. They evaluated the amplitudes for the t-channel kaon-exchange, s-channel, and u-channel diagrams for γp → K̄*⁰Θ⁺, then multiplied by the 2/3 branching ratio for K̄*⁰ → π⁺K⁻ to obtain the cross section for γp → π⁺K⁻Θ⁺, using covariant form factors with a cutoff parameter Λ = 0.8 GeV determined from charmed hadron production data.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for positive parity Θ⁺: 10–15 nb at photon energy E_γ ∼ 3.5 GeV (with form factors and cutoff Λ = 0.8 GeV)
- Total cross section for negative parity Θ⁺: peak value about 1.5 nb (more than an order of magnitude smaller than for positive parity)
- Coupling constant g_KNΘ = 3.0 for positive parity Θ⁺ (from SU(3) relation giving Γ_Θ ∼ 9 MeV)
- Coupling constant g_KNΘ = 0.42 for negative parity Θ⁺ (reduced by factor ∼7 due to different width formula)
- Coupling constant g_K*NΘ = 1.8 for positive parity Θ⁺ (from ρNN(1710) decay width ∼15 MeV)
- Coupling constant g_K*NΘ = 0.25 for negative parity Θ⁺ (assuming same ratio g_K*NΘ/g_KNΘ as for positive parity)
- Cutoff parameter Λ = 0.8 GeV in covariant form factors (determined from charmed hadron production data at 6 GeV)
- Without form factors, cross sections are more than two orders of magnitude larger than with form factors

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Liu, Ko, and collaborators (2003) on Θ⁺ production in reactions γp → K̄⁰Θ⁺ and γn → K⁻Θ⁺, where cross sections of a few hundred nb were predicted, to the reaction γp → π⁺K⁻Θ⁺ observed by the CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab. The results show that measuring this cross section can help determine the parity of the Θ⁺ pentaquark, which is debated between the positive parity predicted by the chiral soliton model (Diakonov, 1997) and the negative parity favored by lattice QCD studies (Sasaki, 2003; Ciskor, 2003). The calculation uses improved form factors following the method of Janssen (2001) for lambda photoproduction, and the results enable experimental comparison with CLAS data to distinguish between parity assignments.
