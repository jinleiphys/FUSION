# Thermodynamics and fluctuations of conserved charges in Hadron Resonance Gas model in finite volume

> The thermodynamics of hot and dense matter created in heavy-ion collision experiments are usually studied as a system of infinite volume. Here we report on possible effects for considering a finite system size for such matter in the framework of the Hadron Resonance Gas model. The bulk thermodynamic variables as well as the fluctuations of conserved charges are considered. We find that the finite size effects are insignificant once the observables are scaled with the respective volumes. The only substantial effect is found in the fluctuations of electric charge which may therefore be used to extract information about the volume of fireball created in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

Finite volume effects on thermodynamic observables and conserved-charge fluctuations in the Hadron Resonance Gas model become insignificant once the observables are scaled by volume, except for electric charge fluctuations, which retain a substantial volume dependence and may therefore be used to extract the fireball volume in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors use the non-interacting Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, implementing a finite cubic volume by introducing a lower momentum cutoff \(p_{\text{min}} = \pi / R\) (where \(R\) is the system size). They compute the grand canonical partition function, thermodynamic variables (pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat), and second- and fourth-order susceptibilities for baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness at zero chemical potential. They also compute the ratios \(\sigma^2/M\), \(S\sigma\), and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for net proton, net electric charge, and net kaon distributions as functions of center-of-mass energy, using freeze-out parameters from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out volumes from HBT analysis range from 2000 fm³ to 3000 fm³ (Ref. Adamova); from UrQMD simulations of Pb-Pb collisions, freeze-out volumes range from 50 fm³ to 250 fm³ (Ref. Graef).
- Five representative system sizes are studied: \(R = 2\) fm, 4 fm, 6 fm, 8 fm, and 10 fm.
- At a temperature of 0.1 GeV, scaled thermodynamic quantities for \(R = 10\) fm are almost indistinguishable from infinite volume; by 0.2 GeV, systems down to \(R = 4\) fm reach the infinite-volume limit.
- For electric charge fluctuations, systems with volumes larger than 4 fm remain significantly different from infinite volume even after scaling.
- The lowest momentum cutoff for a finite system is \(\lambda = \pi / R\), and the pion mass (the lightest hadron in the electric charge sector) is comparable to this scale.

## Context

This work extends prior finite-volume studies in QCD-inspired models and lattice pure-gluon theory (e.g., Fischer, Braun, Kiriyama, and others) to the hadronic phase using the HRG model. It builds on earlier work by some of the same authors (Bhattacharyya et al., Sur) that found volume-dependent scaling violations near the phase boundary in two-flavor systems. The results suggest that net electric charge fluctuations, unlike baryon number or strangeness fluctuations, can serve as a volume probe in heavy-ion collision experiments, complementing HBT radius analyses (Adamova) and UrQMD-based volume estimates (Graef).
