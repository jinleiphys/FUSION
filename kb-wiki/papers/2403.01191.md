# Strangeness plus-one ($S=+1$) resonance-state $P^{+*}_0$ via $K^+n\to K^{*0}p$

> In our current study, we delve into the peak-like structure observed during the reaction process of $K^+n\to K^{0}p$ at approximately $\sqrt{s}\sim2.5$ GeV. Our focus centers on exploring the potential $S=+1$ resonance $P^{+*}_0\equiv P^*_0$ as an excited state within the extended vector-meson and baryon ($VB$) antidecuplet. To achieve this aim, we employ the effective Lagrangian method in conjunction with the $(u,t)$-channel Regge approach, operating within the tree-level Born approximation. We thoroughly examine various spin-parity quantum numbers for the resonance, resulting in a compelling description of the data, where $M_{P^*_0}\approx2.5$ GeV and $Γ_{P^*_0}\approx100$ MeV. Furthermore, we propose an experimental technique to amplify the signal-to-noise ratio ($S/N$) for accurately measuring the resonance. Notably, our findings reveal that background interference diminishes significantly within the $K^*$ forward-scattering region in the center-of-mass frame when the $K^*$ is perpendicularly polarized to the reaction plane. Additionally, we explore the recoil-proton spin asymmetry to definitively determine the spin and parity of the resonance. This study stands to serve as a valuable reference for designing experimental setups aimed at investigating and comprehending exotic phenomena in QCD. Specifically, our insights will inform future J-PARC experiments, particularly those employing higher kaon beam energies.

# Strangeness plus-one ($S=+1$) resonance-state $P^{+*}_0$ via $K^+n\to K^{*0}p$

## Key claim

The peak-like structure observed in the $K^+n\to K^{0}p$ reaction at approximately $\sqrt{s}\sim2.5$ GeV can be explained by a hypothetical $S=+1$ resonance $P^{+*}_0$ as an excited state within the extended vector-meson and baryon ($VB$) antidecuplet, but only if the background contributions from $u$-channel $\Lambda(1116)$ exchange and $t$-channel $\pi$ exchange are supplemented by a relativistic Breit-Wigner resonance with mass $M_{P^*_0}\approx2.5$ GeV and width $\Gamma_{P^*_0}\approx100$ MeV; the spin-parity quantum numbers cannot be uniquely determined from unpolarized cross-section data alone.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian method combined with $(u,t)$-channel Regge trajectories within the tree-level Born approximation to compute invariant amplitudes for the reaction $K^+n\to K^{*0}p$. They included $s$-channel $P^*_0$ resonance contributions with a relativistic Breit-Wigner form, $u$-channel hyperon exchanges ($\Lambda(1116)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1670)$, $\Sigma(1192)$, $\Sigma^*(1385)$) with Reggeized amplitudes, and $t$-channel Reggeized $\pi$ exchange, using phenomenological form factors with cutoff parameters fitted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass: $M_{P^*_0}\approx2.5$ GeV, with full decay width $\Gamma_{P^*_0}=100$ MeV (consistent across all spin-parity scenarios)
- Combined coupling constants for $P^*_0$ fitted to data: $g^{1/2^+}_{P^*_0}=0.65$, $g^{1/2^-}_{P^*_0}=0.15$, $g^{3/2^+}_{P^*_0}=0.70$, $g^{3/2^-}_{P^*_0}=0.23$
- Ground-state $P_0$ mass estimated at approximately $1.890$ GeV, based on $P_s(2071)$ mass from chiral-unitary approach and mass difference of $\sim180$ MeV from the non-topological chiral soliton model (Diakonov 1997)
- Cutoff parameters for form factors: $1.0$ GeV for $(s,t)$ channels and $0.45$ GeV for $u$ channel
- Regge trajectory parameters: $\alpha_{\Lambda}(u)=-0.65+0.94u$, $\alpha_{\Sigma}(u)=-0.79+0.87u$, $\alpha_{\Sigma^*}(u)=-0.27+0.9u$, $\alpha_{\pi}=0.53$
- Strong couplings for $\Lambda(1116)$: $g_{K^*N\Lambda}=-4.26$, $g_{KN\Lambda}=-13.4$, combined $57.08$
- Strong couplings for $\pi$: $f_{\pi NN}=0.989$, $g_{\pi K K^*}=3.76$, combined $3.72$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nam 2021) on the $S=0$ pentaquark-like bound state $P_s\sim uuds\bar{s}$ (interpreted as $K^*\Sigma$) to the $S=+1$ channel, where the hypothetical $P^*_0$ is assigned as an excited state of the $VB$ antidecuplet analogous to the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark in the $PB$ antidecuplet (Diakonov 1997). The study addresses the long-standing controversy surrounding the $S=+1$ $\Theta^+$ reported by LEPS (2008) but not confirmed by other experiments, and provides a theoretical framework for future J-PARC experiments using higher kaon beam energies (K1.8 beam-line, approximately $3.0$ GeV beam energy). The proposed experimental technique using $K^*$ forward-scattering with perpendicular polarization to enhance signal-to-noise ratio, along with recoil-proton spin asymmetry measurements, enables definitive spin-parity determination of the resonance.
