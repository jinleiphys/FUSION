# Interpreting the Neutron's Electric Form Factor: Rest Frame Charge Distribution or Foldy Term?

> The neutron's electric form factor contains vital information on nucleon structure, but its interpretation within many models has been obscured by relativistic effects. I demonstrate that, to leading order in the relativistic expansion of a constituent quark model, the Foldy term cancels exactly against a contribution to the Dirac form factor F_1 to leave intact the naive interpretation of G^n_E as arising from the neutron's rest frame charge distribution.

# Interpreting the Neutron's Electric Form Factor: Rest Frame Charge Distribution or Foldy Term?

## Key claim

Within the leading-order relativistic expansion of a constituent quark model, the Foldy term (which numerically nearly coincides with the measured neutron charge radius) cancels exactly against a contribution to the Dirac form factor \(F_1\), leaving the naive interpretation of \(G^n_E\) as arising entirely from the neutron's rest frame charge distribution intact. This conclusion holds to order \(1/m^2\) in the relativistic expansion, but the paper notes that nonvalence components such as \(p\pi^-\) configurations or \(q\bar q\) sea effects could still contribute.

## Method

The author performs a relativistic expansion to order \(1/m^2\) around the nonrelativistic limit of a constituent quark model, first illustrating the mechanism with a toy model of a scalar antiquark and a spin-1/2 Dirac particle bound in an S-wave. The calculation is then extended to the realistic case of three spin-1/2 quarks (\(ddu\)) bound by flavor-independent forces into nonrelativistic relative S-waves, where the charge-density matrix elements are expanded and Wigner-rotated components of the boosted wavefunctions are shown to be subleading.

## Key numbers

- The measured neutron charge radius from low-energy neutron-electron elastic scattering is \(r^2_{En} = -0.113 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^2\).
- The Foldy term for the neutron has the value \(-0.126\) fm\(^2\), nearly coinciding with the measured charge radius.
- The dipole form factor parameter is \(M_{dipole}^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\).
- The anomalous magnetic moments are \(F^p_2 = 1.79\) and \(F^n_2 = -1.91\).
- In the toy model with equal masses \(m_D = m_S \equiv m\), the zwitterbewegung charge radius equals the Foldy term: \(r^2_{E\tilde n_{\bar S D}} = 3e_D/4m^2 = r^2_{Foldy,\tilde n_{\bar S D}}\).
- In the valence quark model, the sum of the three quark charges is zero, so the zwitterbewegung terms (each proportional to \(-e_q Q^2/8m_q^2\)) produce no net \(Q^2/m^2\) term.

## Context

This paper addresses a long-standing confusion originating from Foldy (1951-1958), who showed that the neutron charge radius could be explained by a relativistic correction associated with the magnetic moment, seemingly unrelated to any intrinsic charge distribution. The work extends the constituent quark model calculations of Carlitz, Ellis, and Savit (1976) and Isgur, Karl, and Koniuk (1978-1981), which predicted a nonzero \(G^n_E\) from spin-spin forces producing charge segregation inside the neutron. The paper enables future interpretation of upcoming measurements of \(G^n_E(Q^2)\) in the GeV\(^2\) range (using the method of Arnold, Carlson, and Gross, 1981) as tests of the rest frame charge distribution predicted by quark models, while noting that the competing pion cloud explanation and the role of \(q\bar q\) sea components (studied by Geiger and Isgur, 1990-1997) remain open questions to be resolved by experiments such as HAPPEX.
