# Lattice Monte Carlo calculations for unitary fermions in a finite box

> We perform lattice Monte Carlo simulations for up to 66 unitary fermions in a finite box using a highly improved lattice action for nonrelativistic spin 1/2 fermions. We obtain a value of $0.366^{+0.016}_{-0.011}$ for the Bertsch parameter, defined as the energy of the unitary Fermi gas measured in units of the free gas energy in the thermodynamic limit. In addition, for up to four unitary fermions, we compute the spectrum of the lattice theory by exact diagonalization of the transfer matrix projected onto irreducible representations of the octahedral group for small to moderate size lattices, providing an independent check of our few-body simulation results. We compare our exact numerical and simulation results for the spectrum to benchmark studies of other research groups, as well as perform an extended analysis of our lattice action improvement scheme, including an analysis of the errors associated with higher partial waves and finite temporal discretization.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a value of $0.366^{+0.016}_{-0.011}$ for the Bertsch parameter of unitary fermions in the thermodynamic limit, but this result depends on a highly improved lattice action that systematically reduces discretization errors from higher partial waves and finite temporal spacing, and the quoted uncertainty includes both statistical and systematic components.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations for up to 66 nonrelativistic spin-1/2 fermions in a finite periodic box using a highly improved lattice action with open boundary conditions in time. They employed a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to induce two-body interactions via a Z2-valued auxiliary field, which eliminates the fermion sign problem and allows trivial importance sampling. The couplings were tuned by matching the lowest eigenvalues of the two-body transfer matrix to solutions of Luscher's formula at unitarity, and energies were extracted from the long-time exponential fall-off of multi-fermion correlation functions constructed from Slater determinants of single-particle propagators.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter (energy of unitary Fermi gas in units of free gas energy in thermodynamic limit): $0.366^{+0.016}_{-0.011}$
* Maximum number of unitary fermions simulated in a finite box: 66
* Largest deviation of two-body energy eigenvalues from noninteracting values on an $L=8$ lattice: approximately 2%
* Largest deviation of two-body energy eigenvalues from noninteracting values on an $L=16$ lattice: less than 0.6%
* Extrapolation of $N=2+1$ unitary fermion ground state energies using $N_\calO=1$ tuning yields better than 1% agreement with exact infinite-volume results from Pricoupenko and Castin (2007)

## Context

This work extends the lattice Monte Carlo approach for unitary fermions developed by Chen and Kaplan (2004) and Endres et al. (2011), using a highly improved action that reduces systematic errors from finite filling factor effects noted by Carlson et al. (2011), Drut et al. (2011), and Platter et al. (2012). The exact diagonalization results for up to four fermions provide independent checks against benchmark studies from other groups, and the analysis of higher partial wave errors and temporal discretization errors enables more reliable extrapolations to the thermodynamic limit for future many-body simulations of unitary fermions and related strongly coupled systems.
