# Anisotropic hydrodynamics with a scalar collisional kernel

> Prior studies of non-equilibrium dynamics using anisotropic hydrodynamics have used the relativistic Anderson-Witting scattering kernel or some variant thereof. In this paper, we make the first study of the impact of using a more realistic scattering kernel. For this purpose, we consider a conformal system undergoing transversally-homogenous and boost-invariant Bjorken expansion and take the collisional kernel to be given by the leading order 2 <-> 2 scattering kernel in scalar lambda phi^4. We consider both classical and quantum statistics in order to assess the impact of Bose enhancement on the dynamics. We also determine the anisotropic non-equilibrium attractor of a system subject to this collisional kernel. We find that, when the near-equilibrium relaxation-times in the Anderson-Witting and scalar collisional kernels are matched, the scalar kernel results in a higher degree of momentum-space anisotropy during the system's evolution, given the same initial conditions. Additionally, we find that taking into account Bose enhancement further increases the dynamically generated momentum-space anisotropy.

## Key claim

When the near-equilibrium relaxation times of the Anderson-Witting and scalar collisional kernels are matched, the scalar kernel (derived from leading-order 2-to-2 scattering in lambda phi^4 theory) results in a higher degree of momentum-space anisotropy during the system's evolution for the same initial conditions, and incorporating Bose enhancement further increases this dynamically generated anisotropy.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation for a conformal system undergoing transversally-homogeneous and boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, using the leading-order 2-to-2 scattering kernel in massless lambda phi^4 theory as the collisional kernel. They took moments of the Boltzmann equation to obtain 0+1d equations of motion for the anisotropy parameter xi and the scale parameter Lambda, evaluating the necessary eight-dimensional integrals of the collisional kernel using Monte-Carlo VEGAS integration. They matched the scalar kernel to the Anderson-Witting relaxation-time approximation (RTA) kernel by equating their near-equilibrium relaxation times, and considered both classical (a=0) and quantum Bose (a=1) statistics.

## Key numbers

* For classical statistics, the small-xi expansion coefficient for the zz moment of the scalar kernel is alpha_0 = 0.4394 +/- 0.0002; for quantum statistics, alpha_1 = 0.7773 +/- 0.0008.
* The matching relation between the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s and the coupling lambda is eta/s = (2 kappa_a)/(15 alpha_a lambda^2), where kappa_0 = 4/pi^2 for classical and kappa_1 = 4 zeta(5)/pi^2 for quantum statistics.
* For large xi, the function W(xi) scales as xi^(13/8), with w_0 = 1.1051 for classical and w_1 = 0.87962 for quantum statistics.
* At tau = 20 fm/c, all three results for the effective temperature T are within 1% of one another; maximal deviations in pressure anisotropy are on the order of 20% (quantum) and 11% (classical) between the scalar kernel and RTA at tau_0 ~ 0.7 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of non-equilibrium dynamics using anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro), which had exclusively used the Anderson-Witting scattering kernel (relaxation-time approximation) as in Strickland (2017) and earlier works by Florkowski (2010) and Martinez (2010). By introducing a more realistic leading-order scalar collisional kernel, the authors demonstrate that the choice of kernel affects the dynamics quantitatively but not qualitatively, and they provide the first determination of the anisotropic non-equilibrium attractor for a system subject to this scalar kernel. This enables future studies of more realistic scattering kernels in aHydro and tests of whether the qualitative features of the attractor persist with QCD-based collisional kernels.
