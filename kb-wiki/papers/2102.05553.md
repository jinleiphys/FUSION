# Transverse $Λ$ Polarization in $e^+ e^-$ collisions

> In this paper we study transverse polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in single-inclusive leptonic annihilation. We show that when the transverse momentum of the $Λ$ baryon is measured with respect to the thrust axis, a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism is required and the polarization is generated by the TMD polarizing fragmentation function (TMD PFF), $D_{1T}^\perp$. However, when the transverse momentum of the $Λ$ baryon is measured with respect to the momentum of the initial leptons, a collinear twist-3 formalism is required and the polarization is generated by the intrinsic collinear twist-3 fragmentation function $D_{T}$. Thus while these measurements differ from one another only by a change in the measurement axis, they probe different distribution functions. Recently, Belle measured a significant polarization in single-inclusive $Λ$ baryon production as a function of the transverse momentum with respect to the thrust axis. However, this data can in principle be re-analyzed to measure the polarization as a function of the transverse momentum of the $Λ$ baryon with respect to the lepton pair. This observable could be the first significant probe of the function, $D_{T}$. In this paper, we first develop a TMD formalism for $Λ$ polarization; we then present a recent twist-3 formalism that was established to describe $Λ$ polarization. Using the TMD formalism, we demonstrate that the $Λ$ polarization at OPAL and Belle can be described using the twist-2 TMD factorization formalism. Finally, we make a theoretical prediction for this polarization in the collinear twist-3 formalism at Belle.

## Key claim

The paper shows that transverse polarization of Λ hyperons in e⁺e⁻ collisions probes different fragmentation functions depending on the measurement axis: when transverse momentum is measured relative to the thrust axis, the polarization is generated by the TMD polarizing fragmentation function D₁T⊥, but when measured relative to the initial lepton momentum in the center-of-mass frame, it is generated by the collinear twist-3 fragmentation function D_T. The authors claim that existing Belle data can be re-analyzed to measure the latter observable, which would provide the first significant probe of D_T.

## Method

The authors develop a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism for Λ polarization in e⁺e⁻ → Λ(Thrust) X at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, including non-global logarithms and non-perturbative evolution. They also present a collinear twist-3 factorization formalism for e⁺e⁻ → Λ X in the center-of-mass frame, where the polarization is generated by the intrinsic twist-3 fragmentation function D_T. Using the TMD formalism, they describe existing OPAL and Belle data, and then make a theoretical prediction for the polarization in the collinear twist-3 formalism at Belle kinematics.

## Key numbers

- OPAL measured Λ polarization at the Z-pole, i.e., center-of-mass energy equal to the Z-boson mass (approximately 91.2 GeV); transverse polarization was found to be zero within error bars.
- Belle measured significant non-zero transverse polarization in e⁺e⁻ → Λ X as a function of transverse momentum j⊥ with respect to the thrust axis at a center-of-mass energy of approximately 10.58 GeV.
- The unpolarized cross section normalization factor σ₀ = 4N_c π α_em² / (3Q²), where Q is the center-of-mass energy.
- The perturbative Sudakov factor includes terms at O(α_s) and O(α_s²) with coefficients involving C_F, C_A, and T_R n_f.

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization formalism for unpolarized hadron production in e⁺e⁻ collisions (Kang 2020) to describe transversely polarized Λ production, and builds on the collinear twist-3 formalism for Λ polarization (Gamberg 2018). The paper distinguishes between two measurements that differ only by the choice of reference axis but probe different fragmentation functions: the thrust-axis measurement probes the TMD polarizing fragmentation function D₁T⊥, while the center-of-mass frame measurement probes the collinear twist-3 function D_T. This enables future global analyses of TMD polarizing fragmentation functions and provides a test of naive time-reversal invariance, which predicts a non-zero result for T-odd fragmentation in e⁺e⁻ annihilation but a zero result in inclusive DIS processes.
