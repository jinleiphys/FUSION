# Functional renormalization group study of the quark-meson model with omega and rho vector mesons

> We employ the functional renormalization group flow equations to investigate the phase structure of the two-flavor quark-meson model in the presence of a finite isospin chemical potential, incorporating interactions with omega and rho vector mesons. For comparison, we also compute the phase diagram in the chiral limit using the mean-field approximation. Our findings demonstrate that omega and rho mesons affect the phase structure in markedly distinct ways, and the introduction of an isospin chemical potential leads to significant modifications in the phase boundaries and critical region. Increasing the isospin chemical potential lowers the tricritical points temperature, and tends to suppress the unphysical ``back-bending" of the FRG phase boundary at low temperature.

## Key claim

The authors show that including omega and rho vector meson fluctuations via the functional renormalization group (FRG) modifies the chiral phase diagram of the two-flavor quark-meson model in qualitatively different ways for each meson, and that a finite isospin chemical potential lowers the tricritical point temperature and suppresses the unphysical "back-bending" of the FRG phase boundary at low temperature, though the vector couplings are treated phenomenologically rather than from first principles.

## Method

The authors solve coupled FRG flow equations for the scale-dependent effective potential of the two-flavor quark-meson model, treating quarks, sigma, and pions as dynamical fields while omega and rho vector mesons are treated as non-dynamical mean fields whose values are fixed at each RG scale by stationarity conditions. The flow equations are integrated from an ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV down to an infrared cutoff k_IR approximately 20 MeV using the Litim regulator, with the effective potential evolved on a grid in the chiral invariant phi^2 = sigma^2 + pi^2. For comparison, the phase diagram is also computed in the mean-field approximation in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV; infrared cutoff k_IR approximately 20 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV used to calibrate the vacuum
- Quark-meson coupling g_s = 3.2 for FRG calculations (g_s = 3.3 for mean-field calculations)
- Quartic coupling lambda = 8 for FRG calculations (lambda = 20 for mean-field calculations)
- Constituent quark mass M_vac = g_s f_pi approximately 307 MeV; sigma mass m_sigma = sqrt(2 lambda f_pi^2) approximately 588 MeV (mean-field values)
- Vector coupling ratio g_omega/m_omega = g_rho/m_rho = 0.006 MeV^{-1} used as a moderate representative value, with typical range 10^{-3} to 10^{-2} MeV^{-1}
- Omega and rho meson masses m_omega approximately m_rho approximately 1 GeV
- Baryon number density normalized by nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the quark-meson model that included scalar and pseudoscalar fluctuations (Schaefer 2004, Herbst 2013) and some studies with isovector vector and axial-vector mesons (Eser 2015, Jung 2016, Rennecke 2015), but where incorporation of omega and rho vector meson fluctuations within FRG frameworks had been limited primarily to Walecka-type nuclear models at low temperature and density (Drews 2014). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD at finite isospin chemical potential and provide guidance for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and future facilities such as NICA, FAIR, and J-PARC.
