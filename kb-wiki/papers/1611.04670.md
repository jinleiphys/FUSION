# Pseudoscalar condensation induced by chiral anomaly and vorticity for massive fermions

> We derive the pseudoscalar condensate induced by anomaly and vorticity from the Wigner function for massive fermions in homogeneous electromagnetic fields. It has an anomaly term and a force-vorticity coupling term. As a mass effect, the pseudoscalar condensate is linearly proportional to the fermion mass in small mass expansion. By a generalization to two-flavor and three-flavor cases, the neutral pion and eta meson condensates are calculated from the Wigner function and have anomaly parts as well as force-vorticity parts, in which the anomaly part of the neutral pion condensate is consistent to the previous result. We also discuss about possible observables of the condensates in heavy ion collisions such as collective flows of neutral pions and eta mesons which may be influenced by the electromagnetic field and vorticity profiles.

## Key claim

The paper derives a pseudoscalar condensate for massive fermions that is induced by both the chiral anomaly (via the **E·B** term) and a new force-vorticity coupling (via the **E·ω** term), where the condensate is linearly proportional to the fermion mass in the small-mass expansion and vanishes for massless fermions.

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner function formalism for massive fermions in homogeneous electromagnetic fields, using the equation that relates the pseudoscalar component to the divergence of the axial vector component. They compute the axial vector component at first order in field strength and vorticity, then integrate over four-momentum to obtain the chiral current and its divergence. By combining the anomalous nonconservation equation with the integrated Wigner-function relation, they extract the pseudoscalar condensate. The single-flavor result is generalized to two-flavor (u,d) and three-flavor (u,d,s) cases to obtain neutral pion and eta meson condensates.

## Key numbers

* The pseudoscalar condensate for a single fermion species is given by P = (1/(4π²)) ħ² Q² (E·B) (1/m) [C₁(βm,βμ)−1] + (1/(4π²)) ħ² Q (E·ω) β m C₂(βm,βμ), where C₁ and C₂ are dimensionless functions defined by integrals over Fermi-Dirac distributions.
* In vacuum at zero temperature and chemical potentials, the neutral pion condensate reduces to P_π^vac = −(ħ²/(8π² m_q)) Q_e² (E·B), consistent with previous NJL and chiral perturbation theory results.
* For two-flavor case with equal quark masses and chemical potentials, the pion condensate is P_π = (N_C/(24π²)) ħ² Q_e² (E·B) (1/m_q) [C₁(βm_q,βμ_q)−1] + (N_C/(8π²)) ħ² Q_e (E·ω) β m_q C₂(βm_q,βμ_q), with N_C = 3.
* The functions C₁ and C₂ satisfy C₁(βm,βμ)−1 ∼ O[(βm)²] and C₂(βm,βμ) ∼ O(1) for βm → 0, ensuring the condensate is linearly proportional to mass in the small-mass limit.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral anomaly effects in heavy-ion collisions, such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral vortical effect (CVE) studied by Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), and Son and Surowka (2009), to massive fermions, where the pseudoscalar condensate appears as a mass effect. The result reproduces the vacuum neutral pion condensate from chiral perturbation theory (Cao 2015) and provides a new force-vorticity term not previously derived. The formalism enables calculation of neutral pion and eta meson condensates in hot, dense quark matter, with potential observables in heavy-ion collisions such as modifications to collective flows of neutral pions and eta mesons, similar in spirit to disoriented chiral condensates studied by Blaizot (1992) and Rajagopal (1995).
