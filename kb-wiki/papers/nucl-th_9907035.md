# The QGP-Stall or Not ?

> In 1996, D.H. Rischke and M. Gyulassy proposed to use the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) as a signature for the possible formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In particular, they suggested to measure the ratio of the transversal interferometry radii, $R_{out}/R_{side}$, which can be obtained by fitting experimental BEC data with a parametrization introduced by G. Bertsch et al. in 1988. The transverse radius parameter $R_{out}$ has compared to the transverse radius parameter $R_{side}$ an additional temporal dependence which should be sensitive to a prolonged lifetime of a fireball, in case a QGP was formed in a relativistic heavy-ion collision. This latter phenomenon is known under the term ``QGP stall''. In the following, I shall explain why I believe that the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations is not a good signature for the possible formation of a QGP.

# The QGP-Stall or Not ?

## Key claim
The ratio of transverse interferometry radii \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) from Bose-Einstein correlations is not a reliable signature for quark-gluon plasma formation, because the ratio depends more on the initial conditions of the heavy-ion collision than on whether a phase transition to QGP occurs. When different equations of state are used with appropriately fitted initial conditions, the extracted \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) values become nearly identical regardless of the presence or absence of a QGP phase transition.

## Method
The author performed relativistic hydrodynamic simulations (HYLANDER-C code) using two different equations of state: EOS-I with a phase transition to QGP at \(T_C = 200\) MeV and EOS-II, a purely hadronic equation of state extracted from the RQMD transport code. For CERN/SPS beam energies, initial conditions were fitted simultaneously to hadronic single inclusive momentum spectra and Bose-Einstein correlation data from the NA44 and NA49 Collaborations. For BNL/RHIC beam energies, initial conditions for the hadronic EOS-II calculation were adjusted (IC-II) to match the single inclusive momentum spectra produced by the QGP-including EOS-I calculation with its original initial conditions (IC-I), and the resulting BEC radii ratios were compared.

## Key numbers
- For the NA44 experiment acceptance at CERN/SPS energies, \(R_{out}/R_{side} \approx 1.15\) was found using both EOS-I (with QGP phase transition) and EOS-II (purely hadronic), showing no significant difference.
- The QGP phase transition in EOS-I occurs at critical temperature \(T_C = 200\) MeV and critical energy density \(\epsilon_C = 1.35\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- For the PHENIX detector acceptance at BNL/RHIC beam energies, \(R_{out}/R_{side} \approx 1.65\) was found for both EOS-I with IC-I and EOS-II with IC-II, again showing no significant difference.
- The freeze-out temperature used in the calculations was \(T_f = 140\) MeV.

## Context
This work directly challenges the proposal by Rischke and Gyulassy (1996) that the time-delay signal in Bose-Einstein correlations, specifically the \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) ratio, could serve as a signature for QGP formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The author extends the earlier hydrodynamic analysis of Schlei (1997, 1999) by demonstrating that when initial conditions are properly adjusted to match measured spectra, the BEC ratio becomes insensitive to the equation of state. The results suggest that the larger \(R_{out}/R_{side}\) ratio expected at RHIC energies is a consequence of higher energy deposition rather than a QGP phase transition, and that more theoretical work on understanding the initial state of heavy-ion collisions is needed before BEC can be used as a reliable QGP signature.
