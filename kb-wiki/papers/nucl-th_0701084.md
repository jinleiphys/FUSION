# Deciphering the properties of hot and dense matter with hadron-hadron correlations

> Two classes of jet correlations in hot and dense matter are explored. Correlations between very high transverse momentum hadrons within a jet sample the gluon density of the medium, where, the minimal modification on the same side as the trigger is consistent with the picture and parameters of partonic energy loss. Lower momentum partons, sampled through softer correlations, due to their larger wavelengths are sensitive to the presence of composite structures in the medium. Scattering off such states may modify the dispersion relation of the radiated gluons resulting in conical patterns in the detected correlations.

## Key claim

Correlations between two very high transverse momentum hadrons within a single jet provide a consistency test of partonic energy loss parameters, while softer correlations between a high momentum trigger and lower momentum hadrons on the away side are sensitive to composite structures in the medium that may modify the gluon dispersion relation and produce conical (Cherenkov-like) patterns.

## Method

The author calculates the medium-modified dihadron fragmentation function for hard-hard correlations, using modified splitting functions that depend on the quark-gluon correlation function in the nucleus, with no additional parameters beyond those already used for single hadron fragmentation. For hard-soft correlations, the dispersion relation of a massless scalar field (representing the gluon) is computed in a thermal medium with two massive scalar fields (representing bound states) using a simple Phi^3 theory at finite temperature, and the resulting Cherenkov angles are extracted from the space-like dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The produced matter in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be 30 times as dense as normal nuclear matter, based on the suppression of the single inclusive high-pT hadron spectrum.
- In the hard-hard correlation analysis, the trigger hadron pT range is 8-15 GeV, and the associated hadron momentum range is 6 GeV < pT_assoc < pT_trig.
- The factor K = 2 accounts for higher order corrections in the hard parton cross section, identical to that used for single hadron cross sections.
- The associated hadron distribution in DIS off a nuclear target (N and Kr) shows a small suppression at large z2 compared to the suppression of single fragmentation functions.
- The theoretical predictions for associated yield in heavy-ion collisions bracket the experimental data, with the lower dashed line representing charged pions and the upper solid line representing p, pbar, K+, K- and pions inclusive of all decays.

## Context

This work extends the partonic energy loss framework previously applied to single inclusive hadron spectra (Guo and Wang, 2004; Wang, 2004) to dihadron correlations, testing the consistency of the energy loss parameters. The hard-soft correlation analysis addresses the observed double-humped structure on the away side in central Au+Au collisions (STAR Collaboration, 2005; PHENIX Collaboration, 2005), which has been alternatively proposed as a Mach cone effect (Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2006; Ruppert et al., 2005). The Cherenkov radiation mechanism explored here builds on earlier work by Koch et al. (2005) and Dremin (1981). The results enable future experimental and theoretical investigations into the degrees of freedom in the produced excited matter, particularly regarding possible bound states in the gluon sector of the quark-gluon plasma.
