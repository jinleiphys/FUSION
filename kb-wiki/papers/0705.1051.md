# Quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering cross sections on oxygen

> The charged-current quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrinos on oxygen target is computed for neutrino energies between 200 MeV and 2.5 GeV using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with relativistic optical potential, which was earlier successfully applied to describe electron-nucleus data. We study both neutrino and electron processes and show that the reduced exclusive cross sections for neutrino and electron scattering are similar. The comparison with the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM), which is widely used in data analyses of neutrino experiments, shows that the RFGM fails completely when applied to exclusive cross section data and leads to overestimated values of inclusive and total cross sections. We also found significant nuclear-model dependence of exclusive, inclusive and total cross sections for about 1 GeV energy.

## Key claim

The relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with a relativistic optical potential, previously successful for electron-nucleus data, is applied to charged-current quasi-elastic muon neutrino scattering on oxygen; the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) fails completely for exclusive cross sections and overestimates inclusive and total cross sections, with significant nuclear-model dependence appearing near 1 GeV neutrino energy.

## Method

The authors computed exclusive, inclusive, and total charged-current quasi-elastic cross sections for muon neutrinos on an oxygen-16 target using the RDWIA with the EDAD1 relativistic optical potential for final-state interactions, implemented via the LEA code. They compared these results with plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations and with the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM), and also evaluated the effect of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations by incorporating a high-momentum component (25% of the spectral function normalization) scaled by the FSI reduction factor Lambda.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range: 200 MeV to 2.5 GeV
- Axial mass M_A = 1.032 GeV used in the dipole parameterization of the axial form factor
- Normalization factors for oxygen orbitals: S(1p_{3/2}) = 0.66, S(1p_{1/2}) = 0.7, S(1s_{1/2}) = 1
- The high-momentum component of the spectral function incorporates 25% of the total normalization
- Fermi constant G = 1.16639 x 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}; cos(theta_C) = 0.9749
- Exclusive cross section data compared at Q^2 = 0.8 GeV^2 (JLab, beam energy 2.442 GeV, proton kinetic energy 427 MeV) and Q^2 = 0.3 GeV^2 (Saclay, beam energy 580 MeV, proton kinetic energy 160 MeV; and Saclay, beam energy 500 MeV, proton kinetic energy 100 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied to electron-nucleus scattering (tested against A(e,e'p) data at JLab and Saclay) to neutrino-nucleus charged-current quasi-elastic reactions. It contradicts the widely used relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) employed in neutrino experiment Monte Carlo codes, showing that RFGM overestimates cross sections and fails for exclusive channels. The results enable improved nuclear-physics input for neutrino oscillation experiments (Kamland, K2K, MINOS) by providing a more accurate treatment of nuclear effects, including final-state interactions and short-range correlations, building on earlier work by Butkevich and Kulagin and others (e.g., Refs. REV11-REV14, REV21, REV22).
