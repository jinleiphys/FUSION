# Relative phase between the three-gluon and one-photon amplitudes of the J/psi decays

> It is shown that the study of the ω-ρinterference pattern in the J/psi -> (rho+omega)eta -> pi+pi-eta decay provides evidence for the large (nearly 90 degrees) relative phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon decay amplitudes.

## Key claim

The study of the omega-rho interference pattern in the decay J/psi -> (rho+omega)eta -> pi+pi-eta provides evidence for a large (nearly 90 degrees) relative phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon decay amplitudes, though the two available experimental datasets (MARK III and DM2) give somewhat different central values of 60 degrees and 106 degrees respectively.

## Method

The author analyzed the omega-rho interference pattern in the decay J/psi -> (rho+omega)eta -> pi+pi-eta by fitting the pi+pi- invariant mass spectrum with a function containing Breit-Wigner terms for rho and omega production plus a polynomial background. The relative phase between the rho and omega production amplitudes was extracted from fits to data from the MARK III and DM2 Collaborations, then corrected for the known rho-omega mixing amplitude and the small isoscalar one-photon contribution to obtain the relative phase between the isovector one-photon and three-gluon decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- MARK III Collaboration: relative phase between rho and omega production amplitudes phi = (46 +/- 15) degrees, with N_omega/N_rho = 8.86 +/- 1.83
- DM2 Collaboration: relative phase between rho and omega production amplitudes phi = (-4.58 +/- 9.74) degrees, with N_omega/N_rho = 7.37 +/- 1.72
- Rho-omega mixing parameter at the omega mass: epsilon(m_omega) + g_omega-pi-pi/g_rho-pi-pi = (3.41 +/- 0.24) x 10^(-2) exp[i(102 +/- 1) degrees]
- Relative phase between isovector one-photon and three-gluon amplitudes from MARK III data: delta = (60 +/- 15) degrees (or (60 +/- 15) degrees minus 4 degrees depending on isoscalar phase assumption)
- Relative phase between isovector one-photon and three-gluon amplitudes from DM2 data: delta = (106 +/- 10) degrees (or (106 +/- 10) degrees minus 6 degrees depending on isoscalar phase assumption)
- Ratio of isoscalar photon to three-gluon amplitude for omega-eta production: approximately 1/9

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Castro (1995) and Suzuki (1998, 1999) who found large relative phases in J/psi decays to pseudoscalar meson pairs, but those analyses relied on SU_f(3) symmetry assumptions and did not correctly account for rho-omega mixing effects. The present analysis provides a more model-independent determination using the well-studied rho-omega mixing amplitude from Goldhaber (1969), Gourdin (1969), and Renard (1970). The results challenge descriptions based on perturbative QCD with factorization of short and long distances, and the author recommends further study at the KEDR and BES experiments, noting that a tau-charm factory could solve the problem exhaustively.
