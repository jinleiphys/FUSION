# Equation of state of dense matter in the multimessenger era

> While the equation of state (EOS) of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) at suprasaturation densities has been relatively well constrained from heavy-ion collisions, the EOS of high-density neutron-rich matter is still largely uncertain due to the poorly known high-density behavior of the symmetry energy. Using the constraints on the EOS of SNM at suprasaturation densities from heavy-ion collisions together with the data of finite nuclei and the existence of $2M_\odot$ neutron stars from electromagnetic (EM) observations, we show that the high-density symmetry energy cannot be too soft, which leads to lower bounds on dimensionless tidal deformability of $Λ_{1.4} \ge 193$ and radius of $R_{1.4} \ge 11.1$ km for $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star. Furthermore, we find that the recent constraint of $Λ_{1.4} \le 580$ from the gravitational wave signal GW170817 detected from the binary neutron star merger by the LIGO and Virgo Collaborations rules out too stiff high-density symmetry energy, leading to an upper limit of $R_{1.4} \le 13.3$ km. All these terrestrial nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations based on strong, EM and gravitational measurements together put stringent constraints on the high-density symmetry energy and the EOS of SNM, pure neutron matter and neutron star matter.

## Key claim

Using the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model to simultaneously analyze terrestrial nuclear data, the existence of a \(2M_\odot\) neutron star, and the gravitational-wave constraint \(\Lambda_{1.4} \le 580\) from GW170817, the authors show that the high-density symmetry energy cannot be too soft nor too stiff, but the quoted lower bounds \(\Lambda_{1.4} \ge 193\) and \(R_{1.4} \ge 11.1\) km are conditional on the specific choice of the slope parameter \(L(n_c) = 39.5\) MeV, while the central value \(L(n_c)=47.3\) MeV yields a stronger lower limit \(\Lambda_{1.4} \ge 261\).

## Method

The authors apply the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (eSHF) model, which includes additional momentum- and density-dependent two-body forces, to simultaneously describe nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron stars. They fix the higher-order parameters \(J_0\) (skewness) and \(K_{\rm sym}\) (symmetry-energy curvature) at various values while fitting the other 10 Skyrme parameters to binding energies, charge radii, giant monopole resonance energies, and spin-orbit splittings of spherical even-even nuclei. Neutron-star properties are then computed for the resulting parameter sets, and the allowed region in the \(J_0\)–\(K_{\rm sym}\) plane is determined by requiring consistency with heavy-ion flow data, the \(2M_\odot\) neutron-star mass, and the tidal deformability limit \(\Lambda_{1.4} \le 580\) from GW170817.

## Key numbers

* Lower bound on dimensionless tidal deformability for a \(1.4M_\odot\) neutron star: \(\Lambda_{1.4} \ge 193\) (with \(L(n_c)=39.5\) MeV); central value \(L(n_c)=47.3\) MeV gives \(\Lambda_{1.4} \ge 261\).
* Lower bound on radius for a \(1.4M_\odot\) neutron star: \(R_{1.4} \ge 11.1\) km (with \(L(n_c)=39.5\) MeV); central value gives \(R_{1.4} \ge 11.8\) km.
* Upper bound on radius from GW170817: \(R_{1.4} \le 13.3\) km; combined constraints yield \(R_{1.4} \in [11.8, 13.1]\) km for \(L(n_c)=47.3\) MeV.
* Allowed range for skewness coefficient: \(J_0 \in [-464^{+9}_{-11}, -342]\) MeV.
* Allowed range for symmetry-energy curvature: \(K_{\rm sym} \in [-175^{+37}_{-28}, -36\mp2]\) MeV.
* Symmetry energy at twice saturation density: \(E_{\rm sym}(2n_0) \in [39.4^{+7.5}_{-6.4}, 54.5^{+3.1}_{-3.2}]\) MeV.
* Maximum neutron-star mass: \(M_{\rm max} = 2.28 \mp 0.02\, M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collisions (Dan02), finite-nuclei data (Zha13, Zha14), and the existence of the \(2M_\odot\) neutron star PSR J0348+0432 (Ant13) by incorporating the gravitational-wave constraint \(\Lambda_{1.4} \le 580\) from GW170817 (Abb18NSMerger). It improves upon earlier studies that used separate models for nuclear and astrophysical systems by employing a single eSHF framework across all density regimes. The results provide tighter bounds on the high-density symmetry energy and neutron-star properties, and they enable future refinements from more precise measurements of the slope parameter \(L(n_c)\), heavier neutron stars, or tighter tidal deformability limits.
