# Mesonic Condensation in Isospin Matter under Rotation

> We investigate the mesonic condensation in isospin matter under rotation. Using the two-flavor NJL effective model under the presence of global rotation, we demonstrate two important effects of the rotation on its phase structure: a rotational suppression of the scalar-channel condensates, in particular the pion condensation region; and a rotational enhancement of the rho condensation region with vector-channel condensate. A new phase diagram for isospin matter under rotation is mapped out on the $ω-μ_I$ plane where three distinctive phases, corresponding to $σ$, $π$, $ρ$ dominated regions respectively, are separated by a second-order line at low isospin chemical potential and a first-order line at high rotation which are further connected at a tri-critical point.

## Key claim

Using a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model with global rotation, the authors demonstrate that rotation suppresses scalar-channel condensates (sigma and pion) while enhancing vector-channel rho condensation, leading to a new phase diagram on the rotation-isospin chemical potential plane. The abstract's claim of a "new phase diagram" is qualified by the mean-field approximation and the assumption that the system is homogeneous with no condensate vortices.

## Method

The authors adopt the two-flavor NJL model with four-fermion interactions in scalar, pseudo-scalar, and vector channels at finite isospin chemical potential. They introduce global rotation via a polarization term in the Lagrangian and compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation, assuming small rotation (omega r << 1). The coupled gap equations for sigma, pion, and rho condensates are solved numerically to determine the phase structure on the omega-mu_I plane.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass parameter m_0 = 5 MeV; scalar and vector coupling constants G_s = G_v = 5.03 GeV^{-2}; momentum cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV; vacuum chiral condensate sigma_0 = 2 x (250 MeV)^3.
- For the phase diagram at T = 10 MeV and quark chemical potential mu = 250 MeV, a tri-critical point (TCP) is located at (mu_I^c = 300 MeV, omega^c = 57 MeV).
- Pion condensation critical isospin chemical potential without rotation is about 140 MeV (second-order transition at T = 10 MeV).
- Calculations use radial coordinate r = 0.1 GeV^{-1} to ensure omega r << 1.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of rotational suppression of fermion pairing in zero angular momentum states, previously demonstrated for chiral condensates and color superconductivity by Jiang et al. (2016). It also builds on studies of pion condensation in isospin matter by He et al. (2005) and others, and on investigations of charged vector meson condensation induced by strong magnetic fields by Chernodub (2011, 2012). The results enable future studies of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in rotating systems such as peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, FAIR, and NICA, as well as rotating neutron star interiors.
