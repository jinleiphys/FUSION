# Medium induced gluon spectrum in dense inhomogeneous matter

> We calculate the spectrum of gluons sourced by the branching of an energetic quark in the presence of an inhomogeneous QCD medium, focusing on the soft radiation limit. We take into account multiple soft interactions between the partons and matter, treating the transverse variations of its parameters within a gradient expansion. Thus, we derive the general form of the medium induced spectrum up to the first order in gradients, and consider its simplifying limits. In particular, we show that to the leading order in matter gradients and using the harmonic approximation for the scattering potential, the full gluon spectrum can be written in a compact closed form suitable for numerical evaluation. The final gluon transverse momentum tends to align along the anisotropy direction, resulting in a non-trivial azimuthal pattern in the jet substructure.

## Key claim

The paper derives the medium-induced soft gluon spectrum from an energetic quark branching in an inhomogeneous QCD medium, showing that to first order in transverse gradients and using the harmonic approximation for the scattering potential, the spectrum acquires a term proportional to the gradient vector that causes the final gluon transverse momentum to align along the anisotropy direction, producing a non-trivial azimuthal pattern in jet substructure.

## Method

The authors calculate the resummed amplitude for gluon emission from a quark in the soft radiation limit (x = omega/E << 1), treating multiple soft interactions with the medium via a background stochastic color field. They perform a medium average assuming Gaussian statistics for color sources and expand the transverse variations of medium parameters (density rho and Debye mass mu) within a gradient expansion to first order. The final gluon distribution is expressed in terms of a broadening two-point function and an emission kernel, both evaluated up to first order in gradients using the Gyulassy-Wang model for the scattering potential.

## Key numbers

* The gluon spectrum is expanded as omega dI/(domega d^2 k) = omega dI_0/(domega d^2 k) + (hat g dot k) omega dI_1/(domega d^2 k) + O(hat g^2), where hat g is the gradient operator acting on medium parameters and dI_0 is the homogeneous spectrum.
* The broadening two-point function S_2 includes gradient corrections proportional to (L - bar z)^3/(3 omega), (L - bar z)^2/(2 omega), and (L - bar z)^2/(2 omega) times the gluon momentum k, where L is the medium extension.
* The emission kernel K includes gradient corrections through the factor (1 + (r(tau) + x_in)/2 dot hat g) multiplying the dipole potential V(r(tau) - x_in).

## Context

This work extends the gradient expansion approach previously developed for single parton evolution (jet broadening) in inhomogeneous matter by Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2022) to the case of in-medium branching. It generalizes the standard homogeneous-medium calculations of the medium-induced gluon spectrum (Zakharov 1996, Baier 1996, Wiedemann 2000) to include transverse variations of medium parameters, enabling jet tomography to resolve details of anisotropic matter expansion and initial out-of-equilibrium phases in heavy-ion collisions.
