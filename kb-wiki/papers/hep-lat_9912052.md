# A Lattice QCD Analysis of the Strangeness Magnetic Moment of the Nucleon

> The outcome of the SAMPLE Experiment suggests that the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, G_M^s(0), may be greater than zero. This result is very difficult to reconcile with expectations based on the successful baryon magnetic-moment phenomenology of the constituent quark model. We show that careful consideration of chiral symmetry reveals some rather unexpected properties of QCD. In particular, it is found that the valence u-quark contribution to the magnetic moment of the neutron can differ by more than 50% from its contribution to the Xi^0 magnetic moment. This hitherto unforeseen result leads to the value G_M^s(0) = -0.16 +/- 0.18 with a systematic error, arising from the relatively large strange quark mass used in existing lattice calculations, that would tend to shift G_M^s(0) towards small positive values.

## Key claim

The authors show that, contrary to the common constituent quark model assumption that quark magnetic moments are independent of their hadronic environment, the valence u-quark contribution to the neutron magnetic moment can differ by more than 50% from its contribution to the Xi^0 magnetic moment, leading to a value for the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment of G_M^s(0) = -0.16 +/- 0.18, with a systematic error that would tend to shift G_M^s(0) towards small positive values.

## Method

The authors derive exact QCD sum rules for octet-baryon magnetic moments under charge symmetry, expressing the strange-quark contribution G_M^s(0) in terms of experimentally measured baryon magnetic moments and two ratios of valence-quark contributions (u_p/u_Sigma+ and u_n/u_Xi0). They then incorporate chiral corrections by analyzing the leading non-analytic (LNA) behavior of valence and sea-quark contributions separately, using a Pade approximant form fitted to existing quenched lattice QCD data at pion masses above 600 MeV to extrapolate the valence-quark contributions to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- G_M^s(0) = -0.16 +/- 0.18 (the central value and statistical uncertainty from the chiral extrapolation)
- The SAMPLE collaboration measured G_M^s(0.1 GeV^2) = +0.61 +/- 0.17 +/- 0.21 mu_N (the experimental result that motivated the analysis)
- For G_M^s(0) to be positive with the sea-quark-loop ratio in the expected range (0,1), the valence-quark ratio u_n/u_Xi^0 must be greater than 1.725
- The LNA chiral coefficient chi for the u-valence sector of the neutron is +1.76, while for the Xi^0 it is -0.381 (both in units where the LNA contribution is chi * m_pi)
- The extrapolation of the neutron and proton magnetic moments using the Pade approximant method yielded mu_p = 2.85 +/- 0.22 mu_N and mu_n = -1.96 +/- 0.16 mu_N at the physical pion mass

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD analysis of the strangeness magnetic moment by Leinweber (1996) by incorporating recent progress in chiral extrapolation techniques developed by Leinweber (1998) and Thomas (1999). The analysis reconciles the positive G_M^s(0) suggested by the SAMPLE experiment with the successful baryon magnetic-moment phenomenology of the constituent quark model, by showing that chiral symmetry requires substantial environment dependence of valence-quark contributions. The results enable future full (unquenched) lattice QCD calculations at lower pion masses to refine the determination of G_M^s(0) and test the chiral extrapolation method.
