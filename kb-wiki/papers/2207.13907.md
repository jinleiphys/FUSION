# Resonance-to-bound transition of $^5$He in neutron matter and its analogy with heteronuclear Feshbach molecule

> We theoretically investigate the fate of a neutron-alpha $p$-wave resonance in dilute neutron matter, which may be encountered in neutron stars and supernova explosions. While $^5$He is known as a resonant state that decays to a neutron and an alpha particle in vacuum, this unstable state turns into a stable bound state in the neutron Fermi sea because the decay process is forbidden by the Pauli-blocking effect of neutrons. Such a resonance-to-bound transition assisted by the Pauli-blocking effect can be realized in cold atomic experiments for a quantum mixture near the heteronuclear Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The $^5$He $p$-wave resonance, which decays to a neutron and an alpha particle in vacuum, turns into a stable bound state in dilute neutron matter when the neutron Fermi energy exceeds the resonance energy, because the Pauli-blocking effect of neutrons forbids the decay process. This resonance-to-bound transition occurs around a neutron Fermi momentum of $k_{\rm F}=0.2$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to a density where the Fermi energy exceeds the vacuum resonance energy of approximately 0.94 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a two-channel model Hamiltonian, originally developed for cold atomic gases near narrow Feshbach resonances, to describe the $^5$He system as a closed-channel neutron-alpha state coupled to an open channel of a neutron and an alpha particle. They calculated the in-medium $^5$He spectral function at zero temperature by incorporating the medium effect through a self-energy that includes the Fermi distribution function of neutrons, with the chemical potential set equal to the neutron Fermi energy. The model parameters were determined by fitting the empirical $p$-wave scattering volume $a_{3/2}=-67.1$ fm$^3$ and effective range $r_{3/2}=-0.87$ fm$^{-1}$ for the $J^\pi=3/2^-$ resonance, using a momentum-dependent coupling with a cutoff scale $\Lambda_{3/2}=0.9$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum $^5$He $p$-wave resonance energy is approximately 0.94 MeV, as known from neutron-alpha scattering (10.1143/PTP.61.1327).
- The empirical $p$-wave scattering volume for the $J=3/2$ channel is $a_{3/2}=-67.1$ fm$^3$, and the effective range is $r_{3/2}=-0.87$ fm$^{-1}$ (PhysRevC.102.055802).
- The resonance-to-bound transition occurs around a neutron Fermi momentum $k_{\rm F}=0.2$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to a neutron density $\rho\simeq 2.7\times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The model parameters reproducing the empirical scattering data are $E_{\Phi,3/2}=449.607$ MeV and $M_{\rm r}g_{3/2}^2=113.388$ fm$^2$, yielding a calculated resonance energy $E_{\rm res.}\simeq 0.93$ MeV.
- The cutoff scale is set to $\Lambda_{3/2}=0.9$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on alpha clusters in neutron matter (PhysRevC.104.065801, PhysRevC.102.055802), which examined $s$-wave neutron-alpha interactions and the stabilization of $^8$Be and the Hoyle state, by now incorporating the $p$-wave neutron-alpha interaction associated with $^5$He. The resonance-to-bound transition is analogous to the polaron-molecule transition observed in cold atomic experiments near narrow $p$-wave Feshbach resonances (PhysRevLett.109.075302, PhysRevA.100.062712) and in Bose-Fermi mixtures where heteronuclear Feshbach molecules form (duda2021transition, duda2022long). The results enable future studies of finite-temperature effects, superfluid neutrons, and the role of $^5$He bound states in the formation of multi-alpha clusters such as $^9$Be in astrophysical environments.
