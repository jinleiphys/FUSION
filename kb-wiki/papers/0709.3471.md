# Possible solution of the J/psi production puzzle

> We argue that the s-channel cut contribution to J/psi hadroproduction can be significantly larger than the usual cut contribution of the color-singlet mechanism, which is known to underestimate the experimental measurements. A scenario accounting for intermediate $c\bar(c)$ interactions is proposed that reproduces the data at low- and mid-range transverse momenta P_T from the Fermilab Tevatron and BNL Relativistiv Heavy Ion Collider. The J/psi produced in this manner are polarized predominantly longitudinally.

## Key claim
The s-channel cut contribution to J/psi hadroproduction, when augmented by a phenomenological contact current that restores full gauge invariance for off-shell quarks, can be significantly larger than the usual color-singlet mechanism (CSM) cut and can reproduce experimental data at low- and mid-range transverse momenta, with the J/psi produced being predominantly longitudinally polarized. The authors acknowledge that the contact current is not uniquely constrained by gauge invariance and requires an interpolation between two limiting cases, parameterized by two adjustable values.

## Method
The authors compute the leading-order (LO) gluon-fusion process gg → J/psi g, including s-channel cut diagrams with a dressed c-cbar-J/psi vertex function (modeled as a Gaussian in relative momentum). To maintain gauge invariance for off-shell quarks, they introduce a phenomenological 4-point contact current (Gamma^(4)) constructed via an auxiliary function F that interpolates between the minimal-substitution prescription of Drell and Lee (1971) and the expected high-momentum fall-off, using two free parameters a and kappa. The polarized partonic cross sections are then convoluted with LO gluon distribution functions to obtain double-differential cross sections in transverse momentum P_T and rapidity.

## Key numbers
- At sqrt(s) = 1.8 TeV (Tevatron, pseudorapidity |eta| < 0.6), the model with parameters a=4 and kappa=4.5 GeV reproduces CDF direct J/psi cross-section data up to about P_T = 10 GeV.
- At sqrt(s) = 200 GeV (RHIC, rapidity |y| < 0.35), the same parameters (a=4, kappa=4.5 GeV) give a very good description of PHENIX data.
- The LO CSM from gg → J/psi g and from gg → J/psi c cbar are shown to underestimate the CDF data; the present s-channel cut contribution is significantly larger.
- The polarization parameter alpha is computed for prompt J/psi yield and compared to CDF data at sqrt(s) = 1.96 TeV, showing predominantly longitudinal polarization.

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of cut contributions by Lansberg (2005) that questioned the on-shell assumption of the CSM for heavy quarks forming quarkonia. It addresses the persistent disagreement between theoretical predictions (including NRQCD and its NLO corrections by Campbell (2007)) and experimental measurements of J/psi cross section and polarization at the Tevatron and RHIC. The approach provides a semi-phenomenological alternative to NRQCD color-octet matrix elements, and the authors expect similar enhancement from the s-channel cut in other production processes such as photon-photon collisions at LEP and photo-/lepto-production at HERA.
