# Pole Term and Gauge Invariance in Deep Inelastic Scattering

> In this paper we reconcile two contradictory statements about deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in manifestly covariant theories: (i) the scattering must be gauge invariant, even in the deep inelastic limit, and (ii) the pole term (which is not gauge invariant in a covariant theory) dominates the scattering amplitude in the deep inelastic limit. An ``intermediate'' answer is found to be true. We show that, at all energies, the gauge dependent part of the pole term cancels the gauge dependent part of the rescattering term, so that both the pole and rescattering terms can be separately redefined in a gauge invariant fashion. The resulting, redefined pole term is then shown to dominate the scattering in the deep inelastic limit. Details are worked out for a simple example in 1+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper reconciles the apparent contradiction that the pole (Born) term dominates deep inelastic scattering in the deep inelastic limit even though it is not gauge invariant in covariant theories, by showing that the gauge-dependent parts of the pole term and the rescattering term cancel exactly at all energies, allowing a gauge-invariant part of the pole term to be defined separately and shown to dominate at high momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors construct a separable bound-state model in 1+1 dimensions where a scalar or spin-1/2 "nucleon" is a bound state of a charged quark and an uncharged scalar diquark. They decompose the electromagnetic current into gauge-invariant and gauge-dependent parts using the Landau prescription, compute the pole term and rescattering (triangle) diagram explicitly, and compare their asymptotic behavior in the deep inelastic limit (Q^2 → ∞ with Bjorken x fixed).

## Key numbers

- The gauge-invariant part of the rescattering term falls as 1/Q^2 in the deep inelastic limit, specifically approaching (λ N x)/(π m_2^2 Q^2 (1-x)) times a kinematic factor, while the gauge-invariant part of the pole term does not fall with Q^2.
- The elastic form factor F(Q^2) falls as (N^2)/(4π m_2^2 Q^2) log(Q/m_2) at large Q^2 for the scalar model in 1+1 dimensions.
- The bubble integral b(s) at large s behaves as Im b(s) ∼ s^{-1} and Re b(s) ∼ s^{-1} log(s/m_2^2), so b(s) → 0 as s → ∞.
- The bound state condition for the scalar model requires λ = -2π √(-Δ(M^2, m_1^2, m_2^2)) [arctan(√(-Δ)/(M^2 - m_1^2 - m_2^2))]^{-1}, which is real and negative.

## Context

This work extends the covariant treatment of deep inelastic scattering to resolve a long-standing problem with gauge invariance for off-shell particles, building on the earlier "de Forest prescription" (de Forest, 1983) which replaced currents by their gauge-invariant parts without proof. The authors provide a theoretical justification for the Landau prescription (J^μ → J^μ + (J·q/Q^2) q^μ) and contrast it with the Weyl prescription and the de Forest prescription, which give different results for current components. The paper enables future covariant calculations of DIS structure functions using off-shell constituents without gauge invariance concerns, and the 1+1 dimensional toy model provides a framework for developing tools applicable to more realistic treatments.
