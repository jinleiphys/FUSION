# Viscosity of strongly interacting quantum fluids: spectral functions and sum rules

> The viscosity of strongly interacting systems is a topic of great interest in diverse fields. We focus here on the bulk and shear viscosities of \emph{non-relativistic} quantum fluids, with particular emphasis on strongly interacting ultracold Fermi gases. We use Kubo formulas for the bulk and shear viscosity spectral functions, $ζ(ω)$ and $η(ω)$ respectively, to derive exact, non-perturbative results. Our results include: a microscopic connection between the shear viscosity $η$ and the normal fluid density $ρ_n$; sum rules for $ζ(ω)$ and $η(ω)$ and their evolution through the BCS-BEC crossover; universal high-frequency tails for $η(ω)$ and the dynamic structure factor $S({\bf q}, ω)$. We use our sum rules to show that, at unitarity, $ζ(ω)$ is identically zero and thus relate $η(ω)$ to density-density correlations. We predict that frequency-dependent shear viscosity $η(ω)$ of the unitary Fermi gas can be experimentally measured using Bragg spectroscopy.

## Key claim

The authors derive exact, non-perturbative sum rules for the shear and bulk viscosity spectral functions of non-relativistic quantum fluids, valid for arbitrary temperature and interaction strength, without relying on weak-coupling or quasiparticle approximations. The key condition is that at unitarity (infinite scattering length), the bulk viscosity spectral function vanishes identically at all frequencies and temperatures, not just in the static limit.

## Method

The authors use Kubo formulas for the frequency-dependent shear viscosity spectral function η(ω) and bulk viscosity spectral function ζ(ω), expressed in terms of current-current and stress-stress correlation functions. They derive sum rules by relating the frequency integrals of these spectral functions to thermodynamic quantities, including the energy density ε and the Tan contact C, using the operator form of the Euler equation and spectral representations of response functions. For the dilute two-component Fermi gas, they specialize to s-wave interactions characterized by the scattering length a and the contact C, which describes the large-momentum tail of the momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity sum rule for dilute two-component Fermi gas: (1/π)∫₀^∞ dω [η(ω) − C/(10π√(mω))] = ε/3 − C/(10π m a), valid for arbitrary temperature and 1/(k_F a)
- Bulk viscosity sum rule: (1/π)∫₀^∞ dω ζ(ω) = (1/(72π m a²)) (∂C/∂a⁻¹)ₛ, where the derivative is at fixed entropy per particle s
- At unitarity (|a| = ∞), the bulk viscosity spectral function ζ(ω) = 0 at all frequencies and all temperatures
- The dynamic structure factor has a high-frequency tail S(q,ω) ∼ C q⁴/ω⁷/² for q → 0 and ω → ∞
- The shear viscosity spectral function has a high-frequency tail proportional to C/√ω

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT-based conjecture of a lower bound on η/s by Son, Starinets, and coworkers (2001-2005) to non-relativistic quantum fluids, specifically ultracold Fermi gases near a Feshbach resonance. The authors generalize the result of Son (2007) that the static bulk viscosity vanishes at unitarity, proving instead that the entire spectral function vanishes. The sum rules enable experimental measurement of the frequency-dependent shear viscosity of the unitary Fermi gas using Bragg spectroscopy, and connect to the Tan contact formalism (Tan, 2008) and quantum Monte Carlo data for the energy density at T=0 across the BCS-BEC crossover.
