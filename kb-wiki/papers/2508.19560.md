# $Λ$ and $Σ$ potentials in dense matter based on chiral EFT: Bridging heavy-ion collisions, hypernuclei, and neutron stars

> The $Λ$ and $Σ$ directed flows at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.5~\mathrm{GeV}$ are investigated to examine their sensitivity to the hyperon single-particle potentials. The single-particle potentials are obtained from $G$-matrix calculations with two- and three-body forces based on SU(3) chiral effective field theory. The $Λ+Σ^0$ directed flow shows sensitivity to the variation in the $Σ$ single-particle potential. Its effect is more pronounced for the $Σ^0$ directed flow.

## Key claim

The directed flow of \(\Lambda+\Sigma^0\) and \(\Sigma^0\) in heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.5~\mathrm{GeV}\) is sensitive to the \(\Sigma\) single-particle potential, but the effect on the \(\Lambda+\Sigma^0\) flow is comparable in magnitude to the uncertainty from the momentum dependence of the hyperon potentials.

## Method

The authors performed \(G\)-matrix calculations using two- and three-body forces based on SU(3) chiral effective field theory to obtain \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter. These potentials were implemented in a relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMDv) model within the Monte-Carlo event generator JAM2, and the directed flows were computed for mid-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.5~\mathrm{GeV}\). Two scenarios for the momentum dependence (Chi3momHard and Chi3momSoft) were used to simulate the uncertainty in chiral EFT.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Lambda\) single-particle potential at saturation density \(\rho_0\) and zero momentum is \(U_\Lambda(\rho_0,k=0) \approx -30~\mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with hypernuclear spectroscopy.
- The \(\Sigma\) single-particle potential at saturation density and zero momentum is \(U_\Sigma(\rho_0,k=0)=30\pm 20~\mathrm{MeV}\), inferred from \(\Sigma^-\) atoms and \((\pi^+,K^+)\) inclusive spectra.
- The \(\Lambda/\Sigma^0\) production ratio at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.5~\mathrm{GeV}\) is \(R = 3.05\) as simulated in JAM2; the HADES experiment at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.55~\mathrm{GeV}\) reports \(R=3.2\).
- The chiral 2BF used is NLO13 from the Julich-Bonn group with a cutoff of \(500~\mathrm{MeV}\).
- The momentum dependence scenarios are constructed to reproduce chiral EFT results up to \(k=2.5~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (Chi3momHard) and up to \(1.0~\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) (Chi3momSoft).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on \(\Lambda\) directed flow (Nara, 2022) by implementing a separate \(\Sigma\) single-particle potential from the same chiral EFT-based YN interaction, rather than assuming all hyperons feel the same potential as the \(\Lambda\). The results are compared with STAR data (STAR, 2020) and are consistent with quasi-empirical values from hypernuclear spectroscopy (Gal, 2016). The approach enables further constraints on the \(\Sigma\) single-particle potential, which has larger theoretical uncertainty than the \(\Lambda\) potential due to the lack of \(\Sigma\) hypernuclei except for \({}^{4}_{\Sigma}\mathrm{He}\).
