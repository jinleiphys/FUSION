# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter with chiral three-body forces

> Chiral effective field theory (EFT) enables a systematic description of low-energy hadronic interactions with controlled theoretical uncertainties. For strongly interacting systems, quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods provide some of the most accurate solutions, but they require as input local potentials. We have recently constructed local chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). Chiral EFT naturally predicts consistent many-body forces. In this paper, we consider the leading chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions in local form. These are included in auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations. We present results for the equation of state of neutron matter and for the energies and radii of neutron drops. In particular, we study the regulator dependence at the Hartree-Fock level and in AFDMC and find that present local regulators lead to less repulsion from 3N forces compared to the usual nonlocal regulators.

## Key claim

The leading chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces in local form, when included in auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations, produce less repulsion in neutron matter than the same forces with nonlocal regulators, with the difference already visible at the Hartree-Fock level and amounting to about half the contribution of nonlocal regulators at saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed local coordinate-space expressions for the leading chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) by Fourier transforming the momentum-space forms, then regularized them with smeared-out delta functions and long-range regulators consistent with previously developed local nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. They performed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) simulations of neutron matter and neutron drops in an external harmonic oscillator potential, studying the regulator dependence by varying the 3N cutoff between 1.0 and 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, the Hartree-Fock energy per particle for the local two-pion-exchange $V_C$ contribution is approximately 3 MeV, about half of the 5.5 MeV infinite-cutoff result and less than the nonlocal regulator result.
- For the softer NN potential ($R_0 = 1.2$ fm), the AFDMC energy per particle at saturation density is 12.3-12.5 MeV, with the NN-only energy at 11.4 MeV and the 3N $V_C$ contribution approximately 1 MeV.
- For the harder NN potential ($R_0 = 1.0$ fm), the AFDMC energy per particle at saturation density is 15.5-15.6 MeV, compared to 14.1 MeV for NN-only, with the 3N $V_C$ contribution approximately 1.5 MeV.
- The variation of the total energy with the 3N cutoff in the range $R_{3\text{N}} = 1.0-1.2$ fm is approximately 0.2 MeV, considerably smaller than the variation with the NN cutoff.
- For neutron drops with $N=8$ neutrons and cutoff $R_0 = 1.0$ fm, the N$^2$LO+$V_C$ energy is 135.59(5) MeV with rms radius 2.677(2) fm; for $N=20$ with the same cutoff, the energy is 440.67(10) MeV with rms radius 3.138(4) fm.
- For neutron drops with $N=70$ and cutoff $R_0 = 1.2$ fm, the N$^2$LO+$V_C$ energy is 2148.75(190) MeV with rms radius 3.711(4) fm and central density 0.31(2) fm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends the local chiral NN potentials developed by Gezerlis et al. (2013, 2014) to include consistent local three-nucleon forces at N$^2$LO, enabling quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter and neutron drops with chiral three-body interactions. The results show that local regulators produce less repulsion from 3N forces compared to nonlocal regulators used in prior work by Kruger et al. (2013) and Hebeler et al. (2010), leading to smaller neutron-matter energies than those from many-body perturbation theory (Tews et al., 2013), coupled-cluster theory (Hagen et al., 2014), and self-consistent Green's function methods (Carbone et al., 2014). The neutron drop results provide benchmarks for energy-density functionals and connect to the coupled-cluster calculations of Potter et al. (2014), with the next step being inclusion of the $V_D$ and $V_E$ terms (studied in Lynn et al., 2015) and extension to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO).
