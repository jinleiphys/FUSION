# Duality violations and spectral sum rules

> We study the issue of duality violations in the VV-AA vacuum polarization function in the chiral limit. This is done with the help of a model with an expansion in inverse powers of the number of colors, Nc, allowing us to consider resonances with a finite width. Due to these duality violations, the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) and the moments of the spectral function (e.g. the Weinberg sum rules) do not match at finite momentum, and we analyze this difference in detail. We also perform a comparative study of many of the different methods proposed in the literature for the extraction of the OPE parameters and find that, when applied to our model, they all fare quite similarly. In fact, the model strongly suggests that a significant improvement in precision can only be expected after duality violations are included. To this end, we propose a method to parameterize these duality violations. The method works quite well for the model, and we hope that it may also be useful in future determinations of OPE parameters in QCD.

## Key claim
Duality violations in the VV-AA vacuum polarization function cause the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) and spectral sum rules (such as the Weinberg sum rules) to disagree at finite momentum; the authors argue that significant improvement in precision of OPE parameter extraction can only be achieved by explicitly including these duality violations, rather than relying on any single existing method.

## Method
The authors construct a model of the VV-AA vacuum polarization in the chiral limit using two infinite towers of equally-spaced meson resonances (vector and axial) with constant decay widths, plus a separate rho meson and massless pion, expanding in inverse powers of the number of colors Nc. They analytically compute the full Green's function using Digamma functions, derive the OPE coefficients via Bernoulli polynomials, and compare the spectral moments to the OPE predictions. They then introduce finite resonance widths as a subleading 1/Nc effect and test several published methods for extracting OPE parameters against the model's exact results.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: F0 = 85.8 MeV
- Rho meson decay constant: Fρ = 133.884 MeV
- Rho meson mass: Mρ = 0.767 GeV
- Axial tower mass parameter: mA = 1.182 GeV
- Vector tower mass parameter: mV = 1.49371 GeV
- Tower spacing parameter: Λ = 1.2774 GeV
- Constant resonance decay width: F = 143.758 MeV
- The OPE coefficients C2 and C4 vanish identically due to imposed constraints (no dimension-two or dimension-four operators)
- Typical s0 values used in real QCD analyses: 1.5–3.0 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the study of duality violations first emphasized by Shifman (2000) and builds on the large-Nc resonance models of Golterman and Peris (2001, 2002). The model shares with QCD the property that the OPE is only an asymptotic expansion, not convergent on the positive real axis. The authors compare methods including the pinched-weight approach of Cirigliano (2003) and find that all existing methods fare similarly when applied to the model. The work enables future precision extractions of OPE parameters (such as αs from tau decays and quark masses) by providing a parameterization of duality violations that could be applied to real QCD data.
