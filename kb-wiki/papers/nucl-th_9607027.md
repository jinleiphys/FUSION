# Nonradiative proton--deuteron fusion in stellar plasma

> The nuclear reaction e+p+d -----> He3 + e is considered at thermonuclear energies. The motion of the electron is treated within the adiabatic approximation and the pd scattering state is constructed in the form of an antisymmetrized product of the bound state wave function of the deuteron and of the wave function of the pd relative motion. The latter is calculated using an effective pd potential constructed via the Marchenko inverse scattering method. The bound state wave function of He3 is obtained using Faddeev--type integrodifferential equations. The reaction rate thus obtained for the solar interior conditions is approximately 10^{-4} of the corresponding rate for the radiative capture pd ----> He3 + gamma .

## Key claim

The nonradiative proton–deuteron fusion reaction \(e + p + d \rightarrow {}^{3}\mathrm{He} + e\) proceeds at a rate approximately \(10^{-4}\) of the radiative capture \(p + d \rightarrow {}^{3}\mathrm{He} + \gamma\) under solar interior conditions, but the authors note that this ratio depends linearly on the electron density and could become significant at the higher electron densities present in the early universe.

## Method

The authors treat the electron motion within the adiabatic (fixed-scatterer) approximation and construct the \(pd\) scattering state as an antisymmetrized product of the deuteron bound-state wave function and the \(pd\) relative-motion wave function. The \(pd\) relative-motion wave function is obtained from an effective \(pd\) potential built via the Marchenko inverse scattering method, using a rational parametrization of the \(S\)-matrix that reproduces experimental \(nd\) phase shifts and Faddeev phase shifts at higher momenta. The bound-state wave function of \({}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) is computed using Faddeev-type integrodifferential equations (the IDEA approach) with the Malfliet–Tjon I-III nucleon–nucleon potential. The reaction rate is then obtained by averaging the fixed-scatterer \(T\)-matrix over Maxwell–Boltzmann distributions for the initial momenta of the proton, deuteron, and electron, and integrating over the final electron momentum.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of \({}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) obtained from the IDEA calculation with the Malfliet–Tjon I-III potential is 8.86 MeV; the root-mean-square radius is 1.685 fm.
- The experimental binding energies used for the energy release in the reaction are \(E_2 = 2.224574\) MeV (deuteron) and \(E_3 = 7.718109\) MeV (\({}^{3}\mathrm{He}\)).
- The Debye radius used for electron screening in the solar plasma is \(\rho_D = 21800\) fm.
- The reaction rate for the nonradiative process under solar interior conditions is approximately \(10^{-4}\) of the rate for the radiative capture \(p + d \rightarrow {}^{3}\mathrm{He} + \gamma\).
- The ratio of the two rates is given by \(\mathrm{ratio} = n_e \langle \Sigma \rangle / \langle \sigma v \rangle\), evaluated at an electron density \(n_e = 100 N_A \, \mathrm{cm}^{-3}\) corresponding to the solar interior.

## Context

This work extends the study of the \(pp\)-chain in stellar nucleosynthesis beyond the standard two-body radiative capture \(p + d \rightarrow {}^{3}\mathrm{He} + \gamma\) (Fowler 1975) by considering a three-body nonradiative mechanism involving an electron. The authors note that three-body processes have different selection rules and have not been properly studied despite their potential influence on light-element production. The results show that the nonradiative reaction is negligible in the present-day Sun but could have been significant at the early stages of the universe when electron densities were much higher, providing a constraint for Big Bang nucleosynthesis models (Turner 1988, Kolb and Dodelson 1990).
