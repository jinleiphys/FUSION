# A statistical model analysis of $K/π$ fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> We briefly describe two statistical hadronization models, based respectively on the presence and absence of light quark chemical equilibrium, used to analyze particle yields in heavy ion collisions. We then try to distinguish between these models using $K/π$ fluctuations data. We find that while the non-equilibrium model provides an acceptable description of fluctuations at top SPS and RHIC energies, both models considerably under-estimate fluctuations at low SPS energies.

## Key claim

The non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (with fitted light quark phase space occupancy γq) provides an acceptable description of K/π fluctuations at top SPS and RHIC energies, but both the equilibrium and non-equilibrium models considerably under-estimate fluctuations at low SPS energies, suggesting the system at these energies may not be completely thermalized.

## Method

The author fitted temperature T, baryochemical potential μB, strange quark phase space occupancy γs, and reaction volume to particle yields and ratios from data samples, using two statistical hadronization models: one assuming absolute chemical equilibrium (γq=1) and one allowing light quark chemical non-equilibrium (γq fitted). The dynamical fluctuations σdynK/π were then computed from these fits and compared to experimental data from SPS (NA49) and RHIC, considering two extreme cases of experimental acceptance (α=1 preserving correlations and α=0 destroying them).

## Key numbers

- At RHIC and highest SPS energies, the non-equilibrium model gives σdynK/π values compatible with experimental data
- Both models considerably under-estimate σdynK/π at low SPS energies
- The equilibrium model under-estimates σdynK/π at all energies under consideration
- For SPS results on σ(K++K-)/(π++π-), the resonance correlation term is non-negligible but not dominant (∼ K*/K ∼ 10%)
- For p/π fluctuations, resonance correlations (Δ, Λ, Σ, Σ* and Ξ decays into pπ) form ∼ 70% of the proton yields at all √s

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model comparison between equilibrium and non-equilibrium approaches previously studied by Jansbook, Becattini, and others, who found that yield fits alone could not distinguish the models. The paper builds on the fluctuation analysis framework of Fluctpaper (2006) and uses experimental data from SPS (Spsfluct) and RHIC (Supriya). The results suggest that K/π fluctuations may serve as a distinguishing observable between the two models, but the failure at low SPS energies raises questions about thermalization assumptions that could be tested by kinetic models incorporating initial condition fluctuations and detector acceptance effects.
