# Elastic proton scattering of medium mass nuclei from coupled-cluster theory

> Using coupled-cluster theory and interactions from chiral effective field theory, we compute overlap functions for transfer and scattering of low-energy protons on the target nucleus 40-Ca. Effects of three-nucleon forces are included phenomenologically as in-medium two-nucleon interactions. Using known asymptotic forms for one-nucleon overlap functions we derive a simple and intuitive way of computing scattering observables such as elastic scattering phase shifts and cross sections. As a first application and proof-of-principle, we compute phase shifts and differential interaction cross sections at energies 9.6 MeV and 12.44 MeV and compare with experimental data. Our computed diffraction minima are in good agreement with experiment, while we tend to overestimate the cross sections at large scattering angles.

# Elastic proton scattering of medium mass nuclei from coupled-cluster theory

## Key claim
Using coupled-cluster theory with chiral effective field theory interactions and a phenomenological treatment of three-nucleon forces, the authors compute elastic proton scattering observables for 40-Ca and find good agreement for diffraction minima but systematically overestimate cross sections at large scattering angles, likely due to missing absorption from excitations above the deuteron threshold.

## Method
The authors compute one-nucleon overlap functions between the ground state of 40-Ca and bound and scattering states of 41-Sc using coupled-cluster theory for the target and particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster theory for the residual nucleus. They match these overlap functions to known asymptotic forms (Whittaker functions for bound states, Coulomb wave functions for scattering states) at radii beyond 8 fm to extract elastic scattering phase shifts, from which differential cross sections are computed. The infinite-range Coulomb interaction is treated via an off-diagonal method in momentum space using a Fourier-Bessel basis with 50 mesh points, and the single-particle basis uses a Gamow-Hartree-Fock basis with N_max=17 major spherical oscillator shells at hbar-omega = 26 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Proton separation energy for 41-Sc: computed S_p^CC = 0.71 MeV, compared to experimental S_p^Exp = 1.09 MeV
- Differential cross sections computed at center-of-mass energies E_cm = 9.6 MeV and E_cm = 12.44 MeV
- Narrow resonance in p_3/2 partial wave at approximately 1.6 MeV (complex Gamow-Hartree-Fock gives E = 1.61 - 0.001i MeV)
- Broader resonance in p_1/2 partial wave at approximately 3.4 MeV (complex Gamow-Hartree-Fock gives E = 3.42 - 0.20i MeV)
- Fermi momentum parameter k_F = 0.95 fm^-1, low-energy constants c_D = -0.2 and c_E = 0.735 for the three-nucleon force
- Model space of N_max = 17 major spherical oscillator shells with oscillator frequency hbar-omega = 26 MeV

## Context
This work extends coupled-cluster theory, previously applied to nuclear structure calculations of closed-shell nuclei and their neighbors, into the domain of nuclear reactions for the first time. It builds on prior developments including the Resonating Group Method within the No Core Shell Model (RGM_NCSM) for light nuclei, Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations for elastic scattering on 4-He (GFMC_scat), and the Self Consistent Green's Function method applied to proton scattering on 16-O (SCGF_O16). The approach enables microscopic calculation of reaction observables without fitted optical potentials, and the authors identify that going beyond 2p-1h excitations in the A+1 wave functions is needed to improve agreement at large scattering angles.
