# Jet momentum broadening beyond the jet quenching parameter from QCD kinetic theory

> Using QCD kinetic theory, we study momentum broadening of jets using the full broadening probability (elastic collision kernel) $C(\mathbf{q_\perp})$ extracted during simulations of the nonequilibrium initial stages in heavy-ion collisions. We find that small momentum transfer is more likely than in thermal equilibrium, particularly along the beam axis. Our extraction represents a significant step towards a more realistic modeling of jet quenching during the initial stages.

## Key claim
Using QCD kinetic theory simulations of the nonequilibrium initial stages in heavy-ion collisions, the authors extract the full elastic collision kernel \(C(\mathbf{q_\perp})\) and find that small momentum transfer is more likely than in thermal equilibrium, particularly along the beam axis, going beyond the usual harmonic approximation that uses only the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\).

## Method
The authors numerically solve the Boltzmann equation for a pure-gluon system undergoing Bjorken expansion, initialized with overoccupied Kurkela-Zhu initial conditions inspired by the Glasma. From the evolving gluon distribution function \(f(\mathbf{p}, t)\), they compute both the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q^{ii}\) and the full collision kernel \(C(\mathbf{q_\perp})\) using hard-thermal-loop resummed propagators to screen soft-gluon exchanges. The collision kernel is extracted for different angles relative to the beam axis and compared to the Landau-matched thermal equilibrium kernel.

## Key numbers
- The jet quenching parameter \(\hat q(t)\) at initial time is comparable to Glasma results from Ipp (2020), with \(Q_s = 1.4\ \text{GeV}\).
- The anisotropy ratio in the jet quenching parameter reaches a maximum of about \(20\%\) for the chosen parameter values.
- The analytic thermal result for large \(q_\perp\) is \(C(q_\perp \gg T) = 2 C_A^2 g^4 n / q_\perp^4\), where \(C_A = N_C\) and \(n\) is the number density per degree of freedom.
- The initial condition uses \(\langle p_T\rangle = 1.8 Q_s\) and \(\xi \in \{4, 10\}\) with \(A(\xi=10) = 5.24171\).

## Context
This work extends the extraction of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) from QCD kinetic theory by Boguslavski (2023) to the full collision kernel \(C(\mathbf{q_\perp})\), which avoids the UV cutoff dependence inherent in \(\hat q\). It connects Glasma calculations by Ipp (2020) and Carrington (2022) with hydrodynamic regimes, and enables more realistic modeling of jet quenching during the initial stages using formalisms that go beyond the harmonic approximation, such as those by Caron-Huot (2010), Andres (2020), and Moore (2021).
