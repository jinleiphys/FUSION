# Hartree-Fock Gamow basis from realistic nuclear forces

> We present a simplified method to generate the Hartree-Fock Gamow basis from realistic nuclear forces. The Hartree-Fock iteration in the harmonic-oscillator basis is first performed, and then the obtained HF potential is analytically continued to the complex-k plane, finally by solving the Schrödinger equation in the complex-k plane the Gamow basis is obtained. As examples, the method is applied to 4He and 22O with the renormalized chiral N3LO potential. The basis obtained which includes bound, resonant and scattering states can be further used in many-body calculations to study weakly bound nuclei.

# Hartree-Fock Gamow basis from realistic nuclear forces

## Key claim
The authors present a simplified method to generate a Hartree-Fock Gamow (resonant) basis from realistic nuclear forces, where the HF potential is first obtained in a harmonic-oscillator basis and then analytically continued to the complex momentum plane via a direct basis transformation using complex Laguerre polynomials, avoiding the more involved double Fourier-Bessel transformation used in prior work (Ref. [ref23]). The method is demonstrated for closed-shell nuclei where single-particle states below the Fermi surface are bound, which the authors note is true in most cases.

## Method
The Hartree-Fock iteration is first performed in a harmonic-oscillator (HO) basis using the symmetry-preserved HF method with the intrinsic nuclear Hamiltonian, including the two-body nucleon-nucleon force and the kinetic energy correction from the center-of-mass motion. The converged HF potential in the HO basis is then analytically continued to the complex momentum plane by a direct basis transformation using the momentum-space radial wave functions of HO states, which involve generalized Laguerre polynomials whose analytic continuation is straightforward. The one-body Schrödinger equation in momentum space is solved on a contour in the complex k-plane using Gauss-Legendre quadrature, yielding a complex symmetric matrix eigenvalue problem whose diagonalization produces the Gamow HF basis including bound, resonant, and scattering states.

## Key numbers
- For $^4$He in the $p_{3/2}$ partial wave, a neutron resonant state is found at energy $E = 1.412 - 1.046i$ MeV.
- For $^{22}$O in the $d_{3/2}$ partial wave, a neutron resonant state is found at energy $E = 0.867 - 0.042i$ MeV.
- The contour used for $^4$He resonant calculations consists of three segments defined by points in fm$^{-1}$: (a) $0 \rightarrow 0.4-0.24i \rightarrow 0.8 \rightarrow 4$, (b) $0 \rightarrow 0.44-0.33i \rightarrow 0.8 \rightarrow 4$, (c) $0 \rightarrow 0.48-0.4i \rightarrow 0.8 \rightarrow 4$, with 20 Gauss-Legendre points per segment.
- The contour used for $^{22}$O resonant calculations: (a) $0 \rightarrow 0.2-0.01i \rightarrow 0.4 \rightarrow 4$, (b) $0 \rightarrow 0.2-0.02i \rightarrow 0.4 \rightarrow 4$, (c) $0 \rightarrow 0.2-0.03i \rightarrow 0.4 \rightarrow 4$ fm$^{-1}$, with 20 points per segment.
- Convergence test for the $^{22}$O $d_{3/2}$ resonant state shows that 20 points per segment yields precision better than 0.1 keV; the converged energy is $0.8666 - 0.0418i$ MeV.
- The HF calculations use the chiral N$^3$LO interaction renormalized by $V_{\text{low-}k}$ with cutoff $\Lambda = 2.1$ fm$^{-1}$, HO frequency $\hbar\omega = 22$ MeV, and basis truncation $N_{\text{max}} = 2n + l = 10$.
- Phase shift analysis from scattering states on the real axis yields a resonance at $0.87 - 0.04i$ MeV for the $^{22}$O $d_{3/2}$ channel, consistent with the complex-contour calculation.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to generate Berggren (Gamow) bases from realistic nuclear forces, specifically building on the momentum-space Hartree-Fock Gamow method of Ref. [ref23] but simplifying the analytic continuation step. The method provides a single-particle basis suitable for Gamow shell model calculations (Refs. [ref1,ref2,ref3,ref4,ref5,ref6,ref7,ref8,ref9,ref10]) and ab initio many-body calculations of weakly bound and unbound nuclei, where the Berggren completeness (Refs. [ref11,ref12,ref13,ref14,ref15]) treats bound, resonant, and scattering states on equal footing. The approach contrasts with earlier work using Woods-Saxon potentials fitted to experimental single-particle energies (Refs. [ref1,ref10]) and with coordinate-space methods requiring proper boundary conditions (Refs. [ref19,ref20,ref21,ref22]). The results enable fully microscopic many-body calculations for exotic nuclei near the drip lines.
