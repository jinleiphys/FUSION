# Electroweak Matrix Elements in the Two-Nucleon Sector from Lattice QCD

> We demonstrate how to make rigorous predictions for electroweak matrix elements in nuclear systems directly from QCD. More precisely, we show how to determine the short-distance contributions to low-momentum transfer electroweak matrix elements in the two-nucleon sector from lattice QCD. In potential model descriptions of multi-nucleon systems, this is equivalent to uniquely determining the meson-exchange currents, while in the context of nuclear effective field theory, this translates into determining the coefficients of local, gauge-invariant, multi-nucleon-electroweak current operators. The energies of the lowest-lying states of two nucleons on a finite volume lattice with periodic boundary conditions in the presence of a background magnetic field are sufficient to determine the local four-nucleon operators that contribute to the deuteron magnetic moment and to the threshold cross-section of n + p -> d + gamma. Similarly, the energy-levels of two nucleons immersed in a background isovector axial weak field can be used to determine the coefficient of the leading local four-nucleon operator contributing to the neutral- and charged-current break-up of the deuteron. This is required for the extraction of solar neutrino fluxes at SNO and future neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the short-distance contributions to low-momentum transfer electroweak matrix elements in the two-nucleon sector can be determined from lattice QCD by measuring energy-level shifts of two nucleons in a finite volume with background electroweak fields, rather than by computing prohibitively expensive five-point correlators. The authors note that this analysis will not apply for arbitrary values of the light-quark masses because the unnaturally large scattering lengths in the two-nucleon sector result from a fine-tuning that nature has presented.

## Method

The authors use the pionless effective field theory (EFT) to describe low-momentum nucleon interactions and introduce dynamical di-baryon fields to simplify computations. They show that the energies of the lowest-lying states of two nucleons on a finite volume lattice with periodic boundary conditions in the presence of a background magnetic field are sufficient to determine the local four-nucleon operators contributing to the deuteron magnetic moment and to the threshold cross-section of n + p → d + γ. Similarly, the energy-levels of two nucleons immersed in a background isovector axial weak field can determine the coefficient of the leading local four-nucleon operator contributing to neutral- and charged-current break-up of the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The scattering lengths in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels are a1 = -23.714 fm and a3 = 5.425 fm, respectively
- The deuteron magnetic moment from experiment is κd = 0.85741, while the single particle sum 2κ0 = 0.87976, giving an unnaturally small value of the two-nucleon parameter l̃2 = -0.0576 fm
- For a magnetic field of eB0 = 10^4 MeV^2, the effective scattering length A3 differs from the physical scattering length a3 by 10% for the physical value of l̃2
- The magnetic field must satisfy |eB| ≪ mπ^2 ∼ 2×10^4 MeV^2 to remain within the regime described by the pionless EFT
- The nucleon magnetic polarizabilities are βp = 1.2±0.7±0.3±0.4 × 10^(-4) fm^3 and βn = 6.5±2.4±3.0 × 10^(-4) fm^3

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume method of Lüscher (1986, 1990) for extracting scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD to the calculation of electroweak matrix elements in the two-nucleon sector. The approach builds on earlier lattice calculations of nucleon magnetic moments using background magnetic fields by Martinelli (1982) and Bernard (1982), and on the pionless EFT framework developed by Kaplan (1998), van Kolck (1998), and Chen (1999). The technique enables determination of meson-exchange currents (in potential model language) or local multi-nucleon-electroweak operator coefficients (in EFT language) that are required for extracting solar neutrino fluxes at SNO and future neutrino experiments, where the present uncertainty in the νd break-up matrix element will provide a significant uncertainty in neutrino mass differences and mixing parameters.
