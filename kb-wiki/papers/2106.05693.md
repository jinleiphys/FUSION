# Elastic scattering measurements for the $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb system at E$_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV

> Background: The influence of halo structure of $^6$He, $^8$B, $^{11}$Be and $^{11}$Li nuclei in several mechanisms such as direct reactions and fusion is already established, although not completely understood. The influence of the $^{10}$C Brunnian structure is less known. Purpose: To investigate the influence of the cluster configuration of $^{10}$C on the elastic scattering at an energy close to the Coulomb barrier. Methods: We present experimental data for the elastic scattering of the $^{10}$C+$^{208}$Pb system at $E_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV. The data are compared to the three- and the four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations assuming $^9$B+$p$, $^6$Be+$α$ and $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ configurations. Results: The experimental angular distribution of the cross sections shows the suppression of the Fresnel peak that is reasonably well reproduced by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations. However, the calculations underestimate the cross sections at backward angles. Couplings to continuum states represent a small effect. Conclusions: The cluster configurations of $^{10}$C assumed in the present work are able to describe some of the features of the data. In order to explain the data at backward angles, experimental data for the breakup and an extension of theoretical formalism towards a four-body cluster seem to be in need to reproduce the measured angular distribution.

## Key claim

The experimental angular distribution for the elastic scattering of the $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb system at E$_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV shows a suppression of the Fresnel peak that is reasonably well reproduced by continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations, but the calculations underestimate the cross sections at backward angles, indicating that the assumed cluster configurations of $^{10}$C are only partially able to describe the data.

## Method

The experiment used a radioactive $^{10}$C beam produced via the charge-exchange p($^{10}$B,$^{10}$C)n reaction at the Cyclotron Institute of Texas A&M University, with the beam directed onto two isotopically enriched $^{208}$Pb foils (2.37 mg/cm$^2$ thick each) on carbon backing. The detection system consisted of a double-sided silicon strip detector (DSSSD) $\Delta$E-E telescope for forward angles and a single DSSSD for backward angles, covering $\theta_{\rm lab} = 15\degree$ to $161\degree$. The elastic scattering angular distribution was compared to three-body and four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations assuming $^9$B+$p$, $^6$Be+$\alpha$, and $^8$Be+$p$+$p$ cluster configurations for $^{10}$C.

## Key numbers

* The $^{10}$C beam energy was E$_{\rm lab}$ = 66 MeV, corresponding to a Coulomb barrier of V$_{\rm B}$ = 61.3 MeV in the laboratory framework (58.8 MeV in the center-of-mass).
* The average production rate of the $^{10}$C beam was $5\times10^{3}$ particles per second for a 300 nA incident $^{10}$B$^{+5}$ beam, with purity better than 95%.
* The achieved energy resolution was about 2.9 MeV (Full Width at Half Maximum).
* The optical model fit yielded a reaction cross section of $\sigma_R = 753$ mb for the $^{10}$C + $^{208}$Pb system.
* For the $^{10}$C projectile, the adjusted Coulomb barrier parameters were R$_{\rm B}$ = 11.3 fm, V$_{\rm B}$ = 58.8 MeV, and $\hbar\omega$ = 5.2 MeV.
* The effective energy after correcting for target thickness was E$_{\rm eff}$ = 66.4 MeV.
* The calculated ground-state matter radii were $r_{\rm m} = 2.73$ fm for the $^9$B+$p$ cluster configuration and $r_{\rm m} = 2.61$ fm for the $^6$Be+$\alpha$ cluster configuration.

## Context

This work extends measurements of elastic scattering for the proton-rich nucleus $^{10}$C, which has a Brunnian (super-Borromean) structure, to a heavy $^{208}$Pb target at energies close to the Coulomb barrier, complementing previous studies on $^{10}$C+$^{58}$Ni at 35.3 MeV by Guimaraes et al. (2019) and $^{10}$C+$^{208}$Pb at 226 and 256 MeV by Yang et al. (2014). The results show a suppression of the Fresnel peak similar to that observed for other weakly-bound projectiles such as $^6$He, $^8$B, and $^{11}$Li, but the CDCC calculations underestimate backward-angle cross sections, suggesting that experimental data for breakup and an extension of theoretical formalism towards a full four-body cluster treatment are needed to fully reproduce the measured angular distribution.
