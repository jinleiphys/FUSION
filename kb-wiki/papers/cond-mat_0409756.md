# Fermion-mediated BCS-BEC Crossover in Ultracold Potassium-40 Gases

> Studies of Feshbach resonance phenomena in fermionic alkali gases have drawn heavily on the intuition afforded by a Fermi-Bose theory which presents the Feshbach molecule as a featureless Bose particle. While this model may provide a suitable platform to explore the lithium-6 system, we argue that its application to potassium-40, where the hyperfine structure is inverted, is inappropriate. Introducing a three-state Fermi model, where a spin state is shared by the open and closed channel states, we show that effects of ``Pauli blocking'' are recorded in the internal structure of the condensate wave function.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard Fermi-Bose theory, which treats the Feshbach molecule as a featureless boson, is inappropriate for ultracold potassium-40 gases because the inverted hyperfine structure forces a spin state to be shared between open and closed channels. The authors show that this three-state Fermi system exhibits "Pauli blocking" effects in the internal structure of the condensate wave function that are absent in the simpler Fermi-Bose model.

## Method

The authors introduce a three-state Fermi model for potassium-40 where the spin states are labeled as open channel states (1 and 3) and a closed channel state (2), with state 3 shared between both channels. They perform a numerical mean-field analysis using a generalized Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to minimize the free energy of the Hamiltonian, and complement this with analytical solutions of variational mean-field equations in the dilute (BEC) and dense (BCS) limits. The model uses a Gaussian pair interaction potential with a single bound state, and the system is characterized by three dimensionless parameters: the interaction strength u0, the exchange coupling gamma, and the detuning nu.

## Key numbers

- The phase diagram boundary is determined by the equation f(nu_c/2E0) = 1/[u0(gamma^2 u0 + 1)], where f(z) = 1 - sqrt(z) arctan(1/sqrt(z))
- The scattering length relation is approximated by (k_F a) E0/(nu_c - nu) ≈ 35
- The variational analysis uses u0 = 3.76 for the interaction strength parameter
- In the BEC regime, the Feshbach molecule size is k0 xi_23 = [E0/(nu/2 + |mu|)]^(1/2) while the open channel molecule size is k0 xi_13 = (E0/|mu|)^(1/2)
- In the BCS-like phase, the Feshbach molecule size becomes k0 xi_23 = [E0/(nu/2 - epsilon_F)]^(1/2) and the Cooper pair coherence length is xi_13 = v_F/|alpha Delta_13|
- The BCS gap parameter is Delta_13 = (8 epsilon_F/e^2) exp[-sqrt(E0/epsilon_F)(1/(alpha gamma u0) - 1)]
- The relative weight of state 1 increases from 30% at the crossover to almost 100% as (k_F a)^(-1) approaches negative infinity

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-Bose theory of Feshbach resonances developed by Holland (2001) and others, which treats the Feshbach molecule as a featureless boson and works well for lithium-6 systems. The paper specifically addresses the potassium-40 system where the hyperfine structure is inverted (negative hyperfine interaction) unlike lithium-6 (positive hyperfine interaction), building on the atomic structure calculations of Burke (1998). The results enable predictions for collective mode spectroscopy and condensate formation dynamics in potassium-40 experiments, with signatures expected in Raman spectroscopy and the dynamical response across the BCS-BEC crossover.
