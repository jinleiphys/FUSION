# Thermodynamic characterizations of exotic and missing states

> Thermal shifts and fluctuations at finite temperature below the deconfinement crossover of QCD from hadronic matter to the Quark-Gluon Plasma provide a viable way to look for exotic and missing states with given quantum numbers in the hadronic spectrum. We study a realization of the Hadron Resonance Gas model in the light quark ($uds$) flavor sector of QCD to study: i) the entropy shifts, and ii) the fluctuations of electric charge, baryon number and strangeness; and extract from them the possible existence of missing and exotic states from a comparison with lattice data. The analysis of the entropy shift based on the free energy of the Polyakov loop suggests the existence of exotic hybrids $g q \bar q$ and $gqqq$.

## Key claim

The analysis of entropy shifts derived from the Polyakov loop free energy suggests the existence of exotic hybrid states \(g q \bar q\) and \(gqqq\) beyond conventional missing hadrons, but only when the hadronic spectrum from the Relativistic Quark Model (RQM) is supplemented with MIT Bag model hybrid states to match lattice data.

## Method

The authors implement a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model for light quark (\(uds\)) flavors, computing the entropy shift from the Polyakov loop free energy and the fluctuations (susceptibilities) of electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness. They compare these thermal observables to lattice QCD data from Bazavov et al. (2016) and Bazavov et al. (2012), using three different hadron spectrum inputs: the PDG listing, the Relativistic Quark Model (RQM) of Godfrey (1985), and the RQM supplemented with MIT Bag model hybrid states.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Megias et al. (2013) and Megias et al. (2016) that relates the Polyakov loop to a hadronic representation of color singlet states, and builds on the HRG model of Hagedorn (1984). The results enable future studies of correlation properties of conserved charges and comparison with upcoming lattice QCD results, while also providing a thermodynamic method to identify missing conventional and exotic states in the QCD spectrum.
