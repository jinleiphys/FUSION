# Induced p-wave Superfluidity in Imbalanced Fermi Gases in a Synthetic Gauge Field

> We study pairing formation and the appearance of induced spin-triplet p-wave superfluidity in dilute three-dimensional imbalanced Fermi gases in the presence of a uniform non-Abelian gauge field. This gauge field generates a synthetic Rashba-type spin-orbit interaction which has remarkable consequences in the induced p-wave pairing gaps. Without the synthetic gauge field, the p-wave pairing occurs in one of the components due to the induced (second-order) interaction via an exchange of density fluctuations in the other component. We show that this p-wave superfluid gap induced by density fluctuations is greatly enhanced due to the Rashba-type spin-orbit coupling.

# Induced p-wave Superfluidity in Imbalanced Fermi Gases in a Synthetic Gauge Field

## Key claim

The p-wave superfluid gap induced by density fluctuations in an imbalanced three-dimensional Fermi gas is greatly enhanced by a synthetic Rashba-type spin-orbit coupling, even in the weak-coupling regime where the gap would otherwise be exponentially suppressed. The enhancement is shown to be up to a factor of about 100 compared to the case without the gauge field, but only for specific intermediate values of the spin-orbit coupling strength (maximum near α ≈ 0.14).

## Method

The authors model a dilute three-dimensional imbalanced Fermi gas with a short-range s-wave interaction between spin-up and spin-down species, plus a uniform non-Abelian gauge field that generates a Rashba-type spin-orbit coupling. They diagonalize the kinetic plus spin-orbit Hamiltonian into a helicity basis, then treat the interaction term using a BCS decoupling. The p-wave pairing gap induced directly by the Rashba spin-orbit coupling is obtained from the first-order gap equations. Separately, the p-wave pairing gap induced by density fluctuations is calculated via second-order perturbation theory (Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov approach), projecting the induced interaction onto the p-wave (l=1) Legendre polynomial. The total triplet p-wave gaps are written as sums of both contributions in the helicity basis.

## Key numbers

- The p-wave pairing gap induced by density fluctuations for the upper helicity branch, normalized to the Fermi energy, reaches a maximum at α ≈ 0.14 for all values of k_F a_s in the BCS range considered.
- For k_F a_s = -0.8 and α = 0, the induced p-wave gap ratio Δ^{↑↑}/E_F^{↑} is approximately 10^{-16}; with the synthetic gauge field at α ≈ 0.14, the corresponding ratio Δ̃_{++}/E_F^{+} is about 10^{2} times larger.
- The critical field for the topological quantum phase transition is given by h_c = √(μ² + Δ²), where the excitation energies vanish at k = 0.
- In the limit h ≫ h_c, the direct Rashba-induced p-wave gap scales as Δ_p ∼ C/h³, where C is a constant depending on λ.
- The Fermi momenta of the helicity states are given by k_{F,±} = √(2m(μ + mλ²) ± 2m√(h² + mλ²(mλ² + 2μ))).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of induced p-wave pairing in imbalanced Fermi gases without spin-orbit coupling, particularly the work of Aurel and collaborators, who found that p-wave gaps induced by density fluctuations are exponentially suppressed in weak coupling and at large imbalance. The authors also build on the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) formalism for induced interactions in the BCS limit, originally developed for balanced systems. The results enable future experimental detection of intra-species p-wave gaps in highly imbalanced ultracold Fermi gases by comparing images with and without the synthetic gauge field, and may stimulate investigations of topological superfluids supporting Majorana fermions in two-dimensional systems.
