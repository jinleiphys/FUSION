# Shape-phase transitions in odd-mass $γ$-soft nuclei with mass $A\approx 130$

> Quantum phase transitions between competing equilibrium shapes of nuclei with an odd number of nucleons are explored using a microscopic framework of nuclear energy density functionals and a particle-boson core coupling model. The boson Hamiltonian for the even-even core nucleus, as well as the spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of unpaired nucleons, are completely determined by a constrained self-consistent mean-field calculation for a specific choice of the energy density functional and pairing interaction. Only the strength parameters of the particle-core coupling have to be adjusted to reproduce a few empirical low-energy spectroscopic properties of the corresponding odd-mass system. The model is applied to the odd-A Ba, Xe, La and Cs isotopes with mass $A\approx 130$, for which the corresponding even-even Ba and Xe nuclei present a typical case of $γ$-soft nuclear potential. The theoretical results reproduce the experimental low-energy excitation spectra and electromagnetic properties, and confirm that a phase transition between nearly spherical and $γ$-soft nuclear shapes occurs also in the odd-A systems.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a shape-phase transition between nearly spherical and gamma-soft nuclear shapes, previously established for even-even Ba and Xe nuclei, also occurs in the corresponding odd-A Ba, Xe, La, and Cs isotopes, but the exact location of the phase transition depends on whether the unpaired nucleon is of the same type as the control parameter (neutron number) or of a different type.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic framework combining nuclear energy density functionals (relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with functional DD-PC1 and a separable pairing force) with the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM). The boson Hamiltonian parameters for the even-even core nuclei were determined by mapping self-consistent mean-field deformation energy surfaces onto the expectation value of the IBM Hamiltonian, while spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the odd nucleon were obtained from the same mean-field calculation at zero deformation. Only the strength parameters of the boson-fermion coupling (Gamma_0, Lambda_0, and A_0 for positive- and negative-parity states) were adjusted to reproduce experimental low-energy spectroscopic data for each odd-mass nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The boson Hamiltonian parameters for Ba isotopes include epsilon_d values ranging from 0.03 MeV (128Ba) to 1.15 MeV (136Ba), and kappa values from -0.102 MeV to -0.124 MeV
- The boson Hamiltonian parameters for Xe isotopes include epsilon_d values ranging from 0.06 MeV (128Xe) to 0.65 MeV (134Xe), and kappa values from -0.115 MeV to -0.144 MeV
- The fermion effective charges used were e_p = 0.250 e b for odd-proton nuclei and e_n = 0.125 e b for odd-neutron nuclei
- The ground-state spin of odd-A Ba isotopes changes from J^pi = 1/2+ to 3/2+ at neutron number N=79, and the lowest negative-parity state changes from 9/2- to 11/2- at N=77
- For odd-A Xe isotopes, the change in spin of the lowest positive and negative parity states occurs at N=77 and N=75, respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of shape-phase transitions in axially-deformed odd-mass Eu and Sm isotopes (Nomura 2016) to gamma-soft odd-A systems in the A approximately 130 mass region. The even-even Ba and Xe nuclei in this region had been identified by Casten (2000) as empirical realizations of the E(5) critical-point symmetry for second-order quantum phase transitions between spherical and gamma-soft shapes, originally described by Iachello (2000). The present study enables future investigations of how the odd particle affects the location and nature of phase transitions in odd-mass systems, and what observables can serve as quantum order parameters.
