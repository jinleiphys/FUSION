# Contraction Diagram Analysis in Pion-Kaon Scattering

> We study the contributions from the connected and disconnected contraction diagrams to the pion-kaon scattering amplitude within the framework of SU$(4|1)$ partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory. Combining this with a finite-volume analysis, we demonstrate that a lattice calculation of the easier computable connected correlation functions is able to provide valuable information of the noisier disconnected correlation functions, and may serve as a theory guidance for the future refinement of the corresponding lattice techniques.

# Contraction Diagram Analysis in Pion-Kaon Scattering

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that, under the condition that the unknown low-energy constant L0^r can be fixed from lattice data of the connected contraction diagrams in the I=3/2 channel, the exponential behavior of the noisier disconnected correlation function in the I=1/2 channel becomes fully predictable as a function of lattice parameters, providing a theory gauge for lattice techniques.

## Method
The authors employ SU(4|1) partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) to separate the connected and disconnected contraction diagrams in pion-kaon scattering, which are inseparable in ordinary three-flavor QCD. They calculate the two connected diagrams T_a and T_b up to one-loop accuracy (O(p^4)) by expressing them as physical scattering amplitudes in the extended quark sector with four fermionic quarks and one bosonic quark. A finite-volume analysis using single-channel Luscher's formula then relates the discrete energy levels from lattice correlation functions to the scattering lengths and the unknown LEC L0^r.

## Key numbers
- The SU(4|1) PQChPT generates 24 independent pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone particles (compared to 35 in SU(4|2) for pi-pi scattering).
- The leading-order squared masses are given by: M_pi^2 = 2B0*m_bar, M_K^2 = B0*(m_bar + m_s), M_eta^2 = (2/3)B0*(m_bar + 2m_s), satisfying the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula 3M_eta^2 = 4M_K^2 - M_pi^2.
- The four neutral particles {pi0, eta, sigma_a, sigma_b} have leading-order masses {M_pi, M_eta, M_pi, M_pi} respectively.
- The UV divergence coefficients Gamma_i for the O(p^4) Lagrangian are tabulated, with values ranging from 0 to 11/144.
- The LEC L0^r is estimated from a prior NNLO analysis as L0^r = 1.1(1.0) x 10^{-3}, with an order-of-magnitude improvement expected from the new formalism.
- The S-wave scattering lengths a0^alpha, a0^beta, and a0^gamma are expressed in terms of the threshold values (T_a)_thr, (T_b)_thr, and (T_c)_thr, with a0^beta being the only one that depends on the unphysical LEC L0^r.

## Context
This work generalizes the PQChPT contraction diagram analysis previously applied to pi-pi scattering by Guo (2013), Acharya (2017), and Acharya (2019), and to the parity-odd pi-N coupling by Guo (2018). The SU(4|1) graded algebra used here is simpler than the SU(4|2) needed for pi-pi scattering, requiring only one bosonic quark instead of two. The results enable future lattice studies to cross-check disconnected diagram calculations in the I=1/2 pi-K channel and can be generalized to pi-N scattering in a follow-up work. The analysis also provides a path to completely determine the SU(4|1) chiral Lagrangian to NLO once L0^r is fixed, allowing application to other processes such as pi-pi to K-Kbar scattering.
