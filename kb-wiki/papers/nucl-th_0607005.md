# Isospin effects on sub-threshold kaon production at intermediate energies

> We show that in collisions with neutron rich heavy ions at energies around the production threshold K^0 and K^+ yields might probe the isospin dependent part of the nuclearEquation of State (EoS) at high baryon densities. In particular we suggest the K^0/K^+ ratio as a promising observable. Results obtained in a fully covariant relativistic transport approach are presented for central Au+Au collisions in the beam energy range 0.8-1.8~AGeV. The focus is put on the EoS influence which goes beyond the "collision-cascade" picture. The isovector part of the in-medium interaction affects the kaon multiplicities via two mechanisms: i) a "symmetry potential" effect, i.e. a larger neutron repulsion in n-rich systems (isospin fractionation); ii) a "threshold" effect, due to the change in the self-energies of the particles involved in inelastic processes. Genuine relativistic contributions are revealed, that could allow to directly ``measure'' the Lorentz structure of the effective isovector interaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ratio of neutral to charged kaon yields (K^0/K^+) in collisions of neutron-rich heavy ions at beam energies around the production threshold (0.8-1.8 AGeV) can probe the Lorentz structure (scalar-vector decomposition) of the isovector part of the nuclear Equation of State at high baryon densities, not just the stiffness of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed fully covariant relativistic transport calculations (RBUU type) for central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.8 to 1.8 AGeV, using four different relativistic mean-field models for the isovector interaction: NL (no isovector fields), NLρ (isovector-vector ρ meson only), NLρδ (both ρ and isovector-scalar δ meson), and DDF (density-dependent couplings). They compared the time evolution and final yields of pions and kaons, focusing on the π⁻/π⁺ and K⁰/K⁺ ratios, and isolated the effects of the mean-field (isospin fractionation) and threshold (self-energy) mechanisms on particle production.

## Key numbers

*   The beam energy range studied is 0.8-1.8 AGeV for central Au+Au collisions.
*   The kaon production threshold in free space is 1.56 AGeV.
*   The variations in the K⁰/K⁺ ratio between the extreme DDF and NLρδ isovector interaction models are of the order of 14-16%, while for the π⁻/π⁺ ratio they reduce to about 8-10%.
*   At 1.0 AGeV, results for the π⁻/π⁺ and K⁰/K⁺ ratios from a model (NLDDρ) with the same stiff symmetry energy as NLρδ but without the δ field are closer to the NLρ results than to the NLρδ results.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on using particle production to constrain the high-density behavior of the nuclear Equation of State (EoS), particularly the work of Fuchs et al. (2001) and Hartnack et al. (1994) on kaon production and the soft EoS. It specifically addresses the isovector part of the EoS, building on earlier isospin transport studies by Baran et al. (2005) and flow analyses by Greco et al. (2003). The paper shows that the K⁰/K⁺ ratio is more sensitive to the Lorentz structure of the isovector interaction than the π⁻/π⁺ ratio, and it supports the study of particle production at new relativistic radioactive beam facilities.
