# Multi-alpha Boson Gas state in Fusion Evaporation Reaction and Three-body Force

> The experimental evidence for the $őĪ$ Boson gas state in the $^{11}$C+$^{12}$C$\rightarrow$$^{23}$Mg$^{\ast}$ fusion evaporation reaction is presented. By measuring the $őĪ$ emission spectrum with multiplicity 2 and 3, we provide insight into the existence of a three-body force among $őĪ$ particles. ## Context
This work extends studies of alpha clustering and the alpha Boson gas state (references [9,10] in the paper) to fusion evaporation reactions, providing experimental evidence for three-body forces among alpha particles that were previously studied theoretically (references [11-21]). The results enable further investigation of three-body forces in nuclear systems, particularly the momentum-dependent enhancement of three-body force effects near the Fermi momentum, and support the use of chiral effective field theory (ŌáEFT) and AV18-UX potentials for describing multi-alpha systems. The paper contrasts with models that include additional residual nucleus particles (2őĪ+residue and 3őĪ+residue models), which deviate from the experimental spectra, suggesting the dominance of the multi-alpha Boson gas state.
