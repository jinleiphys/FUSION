# Interpretation of SAMPLE and HAPPEX Experimental Results on Strange Nucleon Form Factors

> A behaviour of strange nucleon form factors is predicted by means of Jaffe's idea about the relations of $ω$ and $ϕ$ vector-meson coupling constant ratios. Its application to a specific eight-resonance unitary and analytic model of nucleon electromagnetic structure, describing also the time-like nucleon electromagnetic form factor data, explains the positive central values from recent SAMPLE and HAPPEX experiments.

## Key claim

The authors predict positive central values for strange nucleon form factors by applying Jaffe's idea about omega and phi vector-meson coupling constant ratios to an eight-resonance unitary and analytic model of nucleon electromagnetic structure, which simultaneously describes time-like nucleon electromagnetic form factor data; the prediction is conditional on the specific model assumptions and the omega-phi mixing angle deviation of 3.7 degrees.

## Method

The authors constructed unitary and analytic models of the isoscalar Dirac and Pauli form factors (F1 and F2) using four isoscalar vector mesons (omega, phi, omega', omega'') and similarly for the isovector parts using four isovector vector mesons (rho, rho', rho'', rho'''). They fitted the coupling constant ratios of these models to all available proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factor data in both space-like and time-like regions simultaneously. Using Jaffe's relations, which connect the isoscalar coupling constant ratios to the strange form factor coupling constant ratios via the omega-phi mixing angle, they then computed the strange Dirac and Pauli form factors and from them the strange electric and magnetic Sachs form factors.

## Key numbers

- The improved SAMPLE result for the strange magnetic form factor at t = -0.1 GeV^2: G^s_M(-0.1) = +0.61 ± 0.17 ± 0.21 ± 0.19 [mu_N]
- The HAPPEX result for the combination at t = -0.48 GeV^2: G^s_E(-0.48) + 0.39 G^s_M(-0.48) = +0.023 ± 0.034 ± 0.022 ± 0.026
- The predicted strange magnetic moment of the nucleon: mu_s = G^s_M(0) = +0.73 [mu_N]
- The predicted combination at t = -0.48 GeV^2: G^s_E(-0.48) + 0.39 G^s_M(-0.48) = +0.185
- The predicted combination at t = -0.23 GeV^2: G^s_E(-0.23) + 0.22 G^s_M(-0.23) = +0.135
- The omega-phi mixing angle deviation from ideal mixing: epsilon = 3.7 degrees

## Context

This work extends Jaffe's (1990s) idea about relating omega and phi coupling constants to strange form factors by applying it to a specific eight-resonance unitary and analytic model previously developed by Dubnicka and Dubnickova. The predictions explain the positive central values from the SAMPLE Collaboration (MIT/Bates) and HAPPEX Collaboration (TJNAF) experiments, which had large error bars but suggested positive strange form factors. The results enable further tests at the MAMI A4 running experiment, which is measuring the combination G^s_E(-0.23) + 0.22 G^s_M(-0.23), and motivate improved experimental precision to confirm the positive strange magnetic moment.
