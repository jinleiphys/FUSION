# Dark Matter Thermalization in Neutron Stars

> We study how many-body effects alter the dark matter (DM) thermalization time inside neutron stars. We find that Pauli blocking, kinematic constraints, and superfluidity and superconductivity in the neutron star significantly affect the DM thermalization time, in general lengthening it. This could change the final DM mass and DM-nucleon cross section constraints by considering black hole formation in neutron stars due to DM accretion. We consider the class of models in which DM is an asymmetric, complex scalar particle with a mass between 1 keV and 5 GeV which couples to regular matter via some heavy vector boson. Interestingly, we find that the discovery of asymmetric, bosonic DM could motivate the existence of exotic neutron star cores. We apply our results to the case of mixed sneutrino DM.

## Key claim

The paper argues that many-body effects, specifically Pauli blocking, kinematic constraints, and superfluidity/superconductivity, significantly lengthen the dark matter (DM) thermalization time inside neutron stars compared to previous estimates, which could alter the DM mass and DM-nucleon cross section constraints derived from black hole formation scenarios.

## Method

The authors calculate the DM thermalization time using a discretized sum of inverse scattering rates for successive collisions, where each collision reduces the DM energy until the average energy transfer falls below the neutron star temperature (T = 10^5 K ≈ 9 eV). They compute scattering rates using Fermi's Golden Rule with an effective Lagrangian for a complex scalar DM particle coupling to fermions via a heavy vector mediator, incorporating the fermion response function for a non-interacting Fermi gas of neutrons and electrons, and separately treating scattering with superfluid neutrons via a single-phonon response function.

## Key numbers

- DM mass range considered: 1 keV to 5 GeV
- Neutron star parameters: M = 1.4 M_sun = 2.8 × 10^30 kg, R = 10 km, core density ρ_c = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Incident DM velocity at neutron star surface: v ≈ 0.7 (semi-relativistic)
- Neutron star temperature for old, cold stars: T = 10^5 K ≈ 9 eV
- Thermalization radius: r_th ≈ 2.2 m × (T/10^5 K)^(1/2) × (1 GeV/m_χ)^(1/2)
- Analytic thermalization time for Fermi gas of neutrons: τ ≈ 3750 yrs × [γ/(1+γ)^2] × (2×10^(-45) cm^2 / σ) × (10^5 K / T)^2, where γ = m_χ/m_n
- Neutron Fermi velocity: v_F ≈ 0.35
- Neutron chemical potential at saturation density: μ_n ≈ 0.056 GeV
- Neutron superfluid energy gap Δ_3P2: 0.01–0.1 MeV (model-dependent)

## Context

This work extends the DM capture and black hole formation constraints from Kouvaris (2012) and Zurek (2013) by properly including Pauli blocking and kinematic effects that were previously treated only roughly. The authors show that these effects produce a suppression factor ξ_eff that differs significantly from the Pauli blocking factor used in Zurek (2013), and that in superfluid neutron phases, single-phonon emission cannot fully thermalize DM, requiring scattering with electrons instead. This enables more accurate constraints on asymmetric bosonic DM models and suggests that discovery of such DM could motivate exotic neutron star cores.
