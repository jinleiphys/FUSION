# Impact parameter dependencies in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb reactions -- hydrodynamical vs. cascade calculations

> We investigate the impact parameter dependence of the specific entropy S/A in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Especially the anti-Lambda/anti-proton ratio is found to be a useful tool to distinguish between chemical equilibrium assumptions assumed in hydrodynamics (here: the 3-fluid model) and the chemical non-equilibrium scenario like in microscopic models as the UrQMD model.

## Key claim
The anti-Lambda/anti-proton ratio can distinguish between chemical equilibrium (hydrodynamic 3-fluid model) and chemical non-equilibrium (UrQMD cascade model) in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV, but this distinction holds only when impact parameter dependence is considered: the ratio is constant with impact parameter in the 3-fluid model but drops rapidly from 1.3 to 0.5 in UrQMD.

## Method
The 3-fluid hydrodynamical model with an equation of state including a first-order phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma was used to calculate specific entropy S/A as a function of impact parameter, then hadron ratios were computed assuming chemical freeze-out at net baryon density ρ = ρ₀/2 or T = 160 MeV. The UrQMD microscopic model treated non-equilibrium dynamics via baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson collisions with resonance and color flux tube formation and decay, followed by rescattering.

## Key numbers
- The anti-Lambda/anti-proton ratio in the UrQMD model drops from 1.3 to 0.5 as impact parameter increases from central to peripheral collisions.
- In peripheral collisions, anti-Lambda production is suppressed relative to anti-proton production by a factor of 2 due to strange quark mass effects.
- The specific entropy S/A values from the 3-fluid model are parametrized as S/A = 3.945 + ln(d/p) + 4(π/n_p).
- Chemical freeze-out conditions used for the 3-fluid fireball calculation were net baryon density ρ = ρ₀/2 and temperature T = 160 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of entropy production in heavy-ion collisions (Brachmann et al., 1998) by examining impact parameter dependence, and contrasts the chemical equilibrium assumption of the 3-fluid model (used in prior hydrodynamic calculations) with the non-equilibrium dynamics of the UrQMD model. The results enable experimental discrimination between equilibrium and non-equilibrium scenarios in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, and suggest that chemical equilibrium may only be established in very central reactions where sufficient secondary collisions occur.
