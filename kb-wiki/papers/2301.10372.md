# Precision Control in Lattice Calculation of $x$-dependent Pion Distribution Amplitude

> We present a new Bjorken $x$-dependence analysis of a previous lattice quantum chromodynamics data for the pion distribution amplitude from MILC configurations with three lattice spacing $a=0.06,0.09, 0.12$~fm. A leading renormalon resummation in renormalization as well as the perturbative matching kernel in the framework of large momentum expansion generates the power accuracy of the matching to the light-cone amplitude. Meanwhile, a small momentum log resummation is implemented for both the quark momentum $xP_z$ and the antiquark momentum $(1-x)P_z$ inside a meson of boost momentum $P_z$ up to 1.72 GeV along the $z$ direction, allowing us to have more accurate determination of the $x$-dependence in the middle range. Finally, we use the complementarity between the short-distance factorization and the large momentum expansion to constrain the endpoint regions $x\sim 0, 1$, thus obtaining the full-range $x$-dependence of the amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a more accurate determination of the full-range Bjorken x-dependence of the pion distribution amplitude by combining three systematic improvements: leading renormalon resummation to eliminate linear power corrections, simultaneous resummation of large logarithms for both quark and antiquark momenta, and a complementarity method using short-distance factorization to constrain the endpoint regions where the large-momentum expansion breaks down.

## Method

The analysis uses previously published MILC lattice QCD configurations at three lattice spacings (a = 0.06, 0.09, 0.12 fm) with pion boost momenta Pz up to 1.72 GeV. The authors apply leading renormalon resummation (LRR) in both the renormalization of the linearly divergent Wilson-line self-energy and the perturbative matching kernel, using a principal-value prescription to define the scheme. They implement small-momentum log resummation (RGR) for the two physical scales xPz and (1-x)Pz simultaneously in the mid-x region, and use short-distance operator product expansion (OPE) to constrain the endpoint regions x near 0 and 1, combining LaMET results with OPE moments to obtain the full x-dependence.

## Key numbers

- Three lattice spacings used: a = 0.06, 0.09, and 0.12 fm
- Maximum pion boost momentum: Pz up to 1.72 GeV along the z direction
- The LRR correction to the matching kernel in the hybrid scheme involves numerical integration up to z_max = 10 fm
- The asymptotic-form LRR uses a regularization parameter epsilon_m in the range 20-100 MeV with a momentum-space step size Delta x = 0.01
- The leading renormalon strength for the asymptotic series is N_m(n_f=3) = 0.575 and N_m(n_f=4) = 0.552

## Context

This work extends the LaMET-based lattice calculations of the pion distribution amplitude by the LP3 Collaboration (Zhang et al., 2017) and the MSU group (Zhang et al., 2020), and the LPC Collaboration (Hua et al., 2020; LatticeParton, 2022). It addresses systematic limitations identified in previous fixed-order approaches, particularly the inaccuracy of the twist-three mass parameter m0 method examined in a recent LRR paper (2022). The new methodology enables model-independent determination of the mid-x region and model-dependent but constrained endpoint behavior, providing a path toward precision calculations of distribution amplitudes for exclusive processes such as B-meson decays.
