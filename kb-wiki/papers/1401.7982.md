# Complex saddle points in QCD at finite temperature and density

> The sign problem in QCD at finite temperature and density leads naturally to the consideration of complex saddle points of the action or effective action. The global symmetry $\mathcal{CK}$ of the finite-density action, where $\mathcal{C}$ is charge conjugation and $\mathcal{K}$ is complex conjugation, constrains the eigenvalues of the Polyakov loop operator $P$ at a saddle point in such a way that the action is real at a saddle point, and net color charge is zero. The values of $Tr_{F}P$ and $Tr_{F}P^{\dagger}$ at the saddle point, are real but not identical, indicating the different free energy cost associated with inserting a heavy quark versus an antiquark into the system. At such complex saddle points, the mass matrix associated with Polyakov loops may have complex eigenvalues, reflecting oscillatory behavior in color-charge densities. We illustrate these properties with a simple model which includes the one-loop contribution of gluons and massless quarks moving in a constant Polyakov loop background. Confinement-deconfinement effects are modeled phenomenologically via an added potential term depending on the Polyakov loop eigenvalues. For sufficiently large $T$ and $μ$, the results obtained reduce to those of perturbation theory at the complex saddle point. These results may be experimentally relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim

The sign problem in QCD at finite density can be resolved in mean-field-type calculations by analytically continuing Polyakov loop eigenvalues into the complex plane, provided the \(\mathcal{CK}\) symmetry (charge conjugation combined with complex conjugation) is respected; the resulting complex saddle points yield a real action and free energy, but the mass matrix for Polyakov loops may develop complex eigenvalues, indicating oscillatory color-charge densities.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological model for \(SU(3)\) gauge theory with two flavors of massless quarks at finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), combining the one-loop effective potential for gluons and quarks in a constant Polyakov loop background with an added confining potential \(V_d(P)\) (Model A of Meisinger (2001)). The Polyakov loop eigenvalues are parametrized as \(\{\theta - i\psi, -\theta - i\psi, 2i\psi\}\) to enforce \(\mathcal{CK}\) symmetry, and the free energy is obtained by extremizing the effective potential with respect to \(\theta\) and \(\psi\). The stability of saddle points is analyzed via the mass matrix \(M_{ab}\) for the Polyakov loop fields.

## Key numbers

- The confining potential parameter \(m\) is set to \(596\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to reproduce the pure gauge deconfinement temperature \(T_d \approx 270\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(T,\mu \gg m\), the saddle point values are approximately \(\theta \approx \frac{3m^2\pi}{8\pi^2 T^2 + 6\mu^2}\) and \(\psi \approx \frac{9m^4\pi^2 T\mu}{4(4\pi^2 T^2 + 3\mu^2)^3}\).
- The mass matrix eigenvalues to order \(m^2\) are \(g^2\left(\frac{4T^2}{3} + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}\right) + \frac{g^2 m^2 (9\mu^2 - 12\pi^2 T^2 \pm 2\pi T \sqrt{9\pi^2 T^2 - 12\mu^2})}{4\pi^2 (3\mu^2 + 4\pi^2 T^2)}\); the eigenvalues become a complex conjugate pair when \(T < 2\mu / \sqrt{3}\pi\).
- \(\mathrm{Tr}_F P\) and \(\mathrm{Tr}_F P^\dagger\) are shown as functions of \(T\) at constant \(\mu\) up to \(\mu = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and as functions of \(\mu\) at constant \(T\) up to \(T = 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the use of \(\mathcal{CK}\) symmetry and complex saddle points in finite-density QCD, complementing recent lattice simulations using Lefschetz thimbles (Cristoforetti 2012, 2013, 2014) and earlier mean-field studies of complex saddle points (Hands 2010, Hollowood 2011, 2012). The model generalizes the Polyakov-loop parametrization used in PNJL models (Fukushima 2003) and gauge theories with double-trace deformations (Myers 2007, Unsal 2007). The results suggest that oscillatory behavior in color-charge densities, signaled by complex mass matrix eigenvalues, might be observable in baryon number correlators at the CBM experiment at FAIR, building on suggestions by Patel (2011, 2012) for RHIC and LHC.
