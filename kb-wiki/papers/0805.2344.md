# Final-State Interactions in the Superscaling Analysis of Neutral-Current Quasielastic Neutrino Scattering

> Effects of strong final-state interactions in the superscaling properties of neutral-current quasielastic neutrino cross sections are investigated using the Relativistic Impulse Approximation as guidance. First- and second-kind scaling are analyzed for neutrino beam energies ranging from 1 to 2 GeV for the cases of 12C, 16O and 40Ca. Different detection angles of the outgoing nucleon are considered in order to sample various nucleon energy regimes. Scaling of the second kind is shown to be very robust. Validity of first-kind scaling is found to be linked to the kinematics of the process. Superscaling still prevails even in the presence of very strong final-state interactions, provided that some kinematical restrains are kept, and the conditions under which superscaling can be applied to predict neutral-current quasielastic neutrino scattering are determined.

## Key claim

Superscaling (both first- and second-kind) still prevails in neutral-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering even in the presence of very strong final-state interactions, provided that certain kinematical constraints are maintained; however, the validity of first-kind scaling is linked to the kinematics of the process, being well respected at larger nucleon detection angles (60 degrees and 80 degrees) but not at forward angles (20 degrees and 40 degrees).

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Impulse Approximation (RIA) with relativistic mean-field (RMF) potentials to describe both bound and ejected nucleon wave functions, incorporating strong final-state interactions. They computed inclusive neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 12C, 16O, and 40Ca by integrating semi-inclusive one-nucleon knockout cross sections over the unobserved scattered neutrino variables, then divided by the effective neutral-current single-nucleon cross section and plotted against the dimensionless scaling variable psi-u to analyze first- and second-kind scaling for neutrino beam energies from 1 to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino beam energies studied: 1, 1.5, and 2 GeV
- Nuclei analyzed: 12C, 16O, and 40Ca
- Characteristic momentum for 12C taken as k_A = 228 MeV/c, as suggested by superscaling analysis of electron scattering in Maieron (2001)
- Nucleon detection angles considered: 20, 40, 60, and 80 degrees
- For 12C(nu,p) at epsilon = 1 GeV, cross sections shown for outgoing nucleon angles of 40 degrees and 60 degrees

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis previously applied to electron scattering and charged-current neutrino scattering (Amaro 2004, Caballero 2005) to neutral-current neutrino processes, building on the relativistic Fermi gas study of neutral-current scaling by Amaro (2006). The RIA-RMF model was chosen because it reproduces the shape and magnitude of the experimental (e,e') scaling function, including its asymmetric tail at high energy transfers, which other theoretical models have failed to achieve. The results enable the potential application of the phenomenological SuperScaling Approach (SuSA) to predict neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections, provided the kinematical conditions for first-kind scaling are respected.
