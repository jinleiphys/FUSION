# Color Transparency Physics: A New Domain of Strong Interactions

> The concept of color transparency is introduced. This new feature of QCD is characteristic of a gauge theory. It enables strong interactions to be studied in a new domain: scattering amplitudes of transversally small color singlet objects. Experimental data are still few and somehow inconclusive. Future prospects are briefly discussed. This is a frontier problem of QCD which deserves much attention from both theorists and experimentalists in the coming years.

# Color Transparency Physics: A New Domain of Strong Interactions

## Key claim
Color transparency is a predicted feature of QCD where small color-singlet objects experience reduced strong interactions, but experimental data remain few and inconclusive, with the SLAC NE18 experiment showing no observable increase in the transparency ratio up to $Q^2=7 GeV^2$, casting doubt on the most optimistic onset of the phenomenon.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical basis for color transparency in exclusive hard reactions, where selecting the minimal Fock state at large momentum transfer $Q^2$ produces a transversally small color-singlet configuration (a "mini hadron") that cannot emit or absorb soft gluons. The author examines existing experimental results from Brookhaven pp elastic scattering at $90^\circ$ CM in nuclear media, the SLAC NE18 $(e,e'p)$ experiment, and diffractive electroproduction of vector mesons at Fermilab and CERN, then discusses future experimental prospects including the EVA detector at Brookhaven, the Hermes detector at HERA, and the proposed ELFE continuous electron beam.

## Key numbers
- SLAC NE18 experiment measured the color transparency ratio up to $Q^2 = 7 GeV^2$ without any observable increase
- Fermilab and CERN diffractive electroproduction data at $Q^2 \simeq 7 GeV^2$ show an interesting increase of the transparency ratio, with lepton energy around $E \simeq 200 GeV$
- The Hermes detector at HERA will operate at energies $10 \leq \nu \leq 22 GeV$
- The proposed ELFE beam would allow $(e,e'p)$ studies up to $Q^2 = 21 GeV^2$
- Frankfurt et al. estimate sizable effects at CEBAF energies for $Q^2$ values in the range $\sim 4 GeV^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10 (GeV/c)^2$
- For $\pi p \rightarrow \rho p$ at $s = 20.8 GeV^2$, $\theta_{CM} = 90^\circ$, the helicity-violating matrix element $\rho_{1-1} = 0.32 \pm 0.10$ in free space
- The $p-\Delta$ transition form factor was measured at $Q^2 = 3.2 GeV^2$

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework of color transparency introduced by earlier work (CT) and addresses the debate over the nature of the Pomeron in long-distance strong interactions. The paper discusses how the Brookhaven experiment (ASC88) and the SLAC NE18 experiment (SLAC) have produced conflicting indications, while the independent scattering mechanism (PR82) and the oscillating color transparency ratio (RP90) provide competing theoretical interpretations. The work enables future experimental tests of QCD in a new domain, particularly through $(e,e'p)$ reactions on nuclei, deuteron electrodisintegration studies (deut), and tests of hadronic helicity conservation (hel, gpr) in nuclear media.
