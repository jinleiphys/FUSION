# Glauber-based evaluations of the odd moments of the initial eccentricity relative to the even order participant planes

> Monte Carlo simulations are used to compute the centrality dependence of the odd moments of the initial eccentricity $ε_{n+1}$, relative to the even order (n) participant planes $Ψ^*_n$ in Au+Au collisions. The results obtained for two models of the eccentricity -- the Glauber and the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) models -- indicate magnitudes which are essentially zero. They suggest that a possible correlation between the orientations of the the odd and even participant planes ($Ψ^*_{n+1}$ and $Ψ^*_n$ respectively), do not have a significant influence on the calculated eccentricities. An experimental verification test for correlations between the orientations of the the odd and even participant planes is also proposed.

## Key claim
Monte Carlo simulations show that the odd moments of the initial eccentricity, computed relative to the even-order participant planes, are essentially zero for both the Glauber and factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) models, indicating that any correlation between the orientations of odd and even participant planes does not significantly influence the calculated eccentricities.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo calculations following the implementation schemes outlined in Lacey (2010) for both the MC-Glauber model (Alver 2006, Miller 2007) and the MC-KLN model (Lappi 2006, Drescher 2007). They computed the centrality dependence of the odd eccentricity moments ε₃, ε₅, etc., relative to the even-order participant planes Ψ₂, Ψ₄, etc., for Au+Au collisions. The odd moments were evaluated using the five-particle correlator ⟨cos(3φ₁ + 3φ₂ - 2φ₃ - 2φ₄ - 2φ₅)⟩/ε₂³ = ε²₃/Ψ₂.

## Key numbers
- ε₃ values obtained for both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN remain flat as a function of collision centrality and are essentially equal to zero.
- The magnitudes of ε₃ relative to Ψ₂ are minuscule when compared to the participant eccentricities ε₃ calculated with respect to Ψ₃ (Lacey 2010) and ε*₄ calculated with respect to Ψ₂ (Lacey 2010).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Alver (2010), Staig (2010), Qin (2010), and Lacey (2010) that claimed the odd and even participant planes can be considered uncorrelated for eccentricity evaluations. The results reaffirm these earlier conclusions and propose an experimental verification test using measurements of the Fourier coefficients vₙ₊₁ and vₙ with respect to the Ψₙ and Ψₙ₊₁ participant planes, respectively. The findings are relevant for ongoing efforts to extract the transport properties of the quark gluon plasma from anisotropic flow measurements.
