# Signal of the pion string at high-energy collisions

> We study the possible signals of a pion string associated with the QCD chiral phase transition in LHC Pb - Pb collision at energy $ \sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV. In terms of the Kibble-Zurek mechanism we discuss the production and evolution of the pion string. The pion string is not topologically stable, it decays into neutral pions and sigma mesons which in turn decay into pions. Our results show that all the neutral pions from the pion string are distributed at the low momentum and the ratio of neutral to charged pions from the pion string violates the isospin symmetry. For the momentum spectra of the total pions, the signal from the sigma particle decay which is from the pion string will be affected by the large decay width of the sigma significantly.

## Key claim

The pion string, a topological defect formed during the QCD chiral phase transition in LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV, could produce a detectable signal via a low-momentum enhancement of neutral pions and a violation of the neutral-to-charged pion ratio, although the signal from sigma meson decay is significantly smeared by the large decay width of the sigma.

## Method

The authors apply the Kibble-Zurek mechanism to estimate the formation density and evolution of pion strings in the expanding fireball of a Pb-Pb collision at LHC energies, using the linear sigma model as an effective theory of QCD. They calculate the Zurek freeze-out time and correlation length, then model the string network as Brownian with a scaling solution to obtain the number and length distribution of string loops at thermal freeze-out. The decay products (neutral pions directly from strings, and pions from sigma meson decay) are computed with momentum distributions, including the effect of the sigma decay width via a Breit-Wigner function.

## Key numbers

*   LHC Pb-Pb collision energy: sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV.
*   Initial fireball temperature: T_i=600 MeV at time t_i=0.2 fm.
*   Critical temperature for chiral phase transition: T_c=170 MeV at time t_c=8.793 fm.
*   Freeze-out temperature: T_f=120 MeV at time t_f=25 fm; freeze-out volume V(t_f)=2x10^4 fm^3.
*   Zurek freeze-out time: t_z=12.4 fm; Zurek correlation length: xi_z=1.33 fm.
*   Correlation length at freeze-out: xi_f=1.69 fm.
*   Total number of pion strings at freeze-out: between 4 (K=0.01) and 45 (K=0.1).
*   Total number of neutral pions from string decay: between 19 (K=0.01) and 332 (K=0.1).
*   Average momentum of neutral pions from string decay: <p>=21.1 MeV.
*   Average momentum of indirect pions from sigma decay: <p>=152.5 MeV (Gamma_sigma=300 MeV) and <p>=175.9 MeV (Gamma_sigma=600 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion strings in the linear sigma model by Zhang, Huang, and Brandenberger (1998) and of string defects in heavy-ion collisions by Balachandran and Digal (2002), providing the first quantitative estimates of the number and momentum distribution of decay pions from such strings. The results suggest that the low-momentum neutral pion enhancement and the isospin-violating ratio of neutral to charged pions could serve as a distinctive signal of the pion string, though the sigma decay signal is difficult to separate from thermal pions due to the large sigma width. The paper enables future experimental searches at LHC and motivates more detailed modeling of string decay and fireball dynamics.
