# High-momentum tails from low-momentum effective theories

> In a recent work \cite{Anderson:2010aq}, Anderson \emph{et al.} used the renormalization group (RG) evolution of the momentum distribution to show that, under appropriate conditions, operator expectation values exhibit factorization in the two-nucleon system. Factorization is useful because it provides a clean separation of long- and short-distance physics, and suggests a possible interpretation of the universal high-momentum dependence and scaling behavior found in nuclear momentum distributions. In the present work, we use simple decoupling and scale-separation arguments to extend the results of Ref. \cite{Anderson:2010aq} to arbitrary low-energy $A$-body states. Using methods that are reminiscent of the operator product expansion (OPE) in quantum field theory, we find that the high-momentum tails of momentum distributions and static structure factors factorize into the product of a universal function of momentum that is fixed by two-body physics, and a state-dependent matrix element that is the same for both and is sensitive only to low-momentum structure of the many-body state. As a check, we apply our factorization relations to two well-studied systems, the unitary Fermi gas and the electron gas, and reproduce known expressions for the high-momentum tails of each.

## Key claim

The authors extend the factorization of high-momentum operator expectation values from the two-nucleon system to arbitrary low-energy A-body states, showing that the high-momentum tails of momentum distributions and static structure factors factorize into a universal function of momentum fixed by two-body physics times a state-dependent matrix element sensitive only to low-momentum structure, but they caution that unlike in local quantum field theory the factorization formulas are not as controlled as those derived using the operator product expansion.

## Method

The authors use decoupling and scale-separation arguments based on renormalization group (RG) evolution, where a resolution scale Λ separates low- and high-momentum degrees of freedom. They recast the two-body factorization proof of Anderson et al. (2010) in second-quantized language, deriving the form of evolved creation/annihilation operators and their coupling functions. They then apply these results to general A-body states by evaluating the expectation value of the consistently evolved momentum distribution operator for q ≫ Λ, keeping only the leading term where δa†δa contributes and using the low-momentum nature of evolved states to simplify the sums.

## Key numbers

- The resolution scale Λ is the momentum scale at which decoupling occurs; low-energy states satisfy |E_n| ≲ Λ², and the high-momentum components of evolved wave functions are exponentially suppressed as exp(-q⁴/Λ⁴) for SRG transformations.
- For the two-body system, the high-momentum momentum distribution factorizes as ⟨ψ_α^∞|n_q|ψ_α^∞⟩ ≈ γ²(q;Λ) Z²_Λ |ψ_α^Λ(0)|², where γ(q;Λ) is a state-independent function and Z_Λ is a wave function renormalization factor.
- For A-body systems, the large-q tail of the momentum distribution factorizes as n_q ≈ Z²_Λ γ²(q;Λ) times a sum over low-momentum creation/annihilation operators in the evolved state.

## Context

This work extends the factorization results of Anderson et al. (2010), who demonstrated factorization for operator expectation values in the two-nucleon system using SRG evolution of the momentum distribution. The methods are reminiscent of the operator product expansion (OPE) in quantum field theory as developed by Wilson (1969, 1972), and the wave function factorization builds on Lepage's (1997) non-relativistic operator product expansion. The results provide an alternative to explanations based on short-range correlations for the universal scaling of nuclear momentum distributions, and the authors test their factorization relations by applying them to the unitary Fermi gas and the electron gas to reproduce known expressions for the high-momentum tails.
