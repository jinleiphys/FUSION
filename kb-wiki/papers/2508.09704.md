# Cooling of dark neutron stars

> We study the cooling of isolated dark-matter-admixed neutron stars, employing a realistic nuclear equation of state and realistic nuclear pairing gaps, together with fermionic dark matter of variable particle mass and dark-matter fraction. The related parameter space is scanned for the stellar structural and cooling properties. We find that a consistent description of all current cooling data requires fast direct Urca cooling and reasonable proton 1S0 gaps. Dark matter affects the cooling properties by a modification of the nuclear density profiles, but also changes stellar radius and maximum mass. Possible signals of a large dark matter content could be a very massive but slow-cooling star or a very light but fast-cooling star.

## Key claim

A consistent description of all current neutron star cooling data requires fast direct Urca cooling and reasonable proton 1S0 gaps, but dark matter affects cooling only indirectly through modification of nuclear density profiles and changes to stellar radius and maximum mass, with the caveat that very large dark matter fractions (tens of percent) are highly speculative and require exotic formation mechanisms beyond standard dark matter capture.

## Method

The authors solve two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for dark-matter-admixed neutron stars using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock nuclear equation of state based on the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential with compatible three-body forces, combined with a fermionic dark matter model with repulsive Yukawa self-interaction. They perform cooling simulations using the NSCool code, scanning over dark matter particle mass (0.5, 1, 2 GeV) and dark matter fraction (0 to 1), with scaled proton 1S0 pairing gaps and no neutron 3P2 pairing, comparing results against observational cooling data for about 60 objects.

## Key numbers

- Direct Urca threshold density: 0.371 fm^-3, corresponding to a neutron star mass of 1.03 solar masses
- Proton 1S0 gap vanishes at density 0.536 fm^-3, corresponding to a neutron star mass of 1.73 solar masses
- Maximum mass of pure neutron star: 2.34 solar masses
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass pure neutron star: 12.32 km
- Radius of 2.0 solar mass pure neutron star: 11.93 km
- Dark matter self-interaction cross section constraint: sigma/m_D = 1 cm^2/g = 4560/GeV^3

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Bhat (2020), Kumar (2022), Garcia (2022), Avila (2024), and Giangrandi (2024) that examined dark matter effects on neutron star cooling, but goes beyond those by scanning the full range of dark matter fraction from 0 to 1 and using realistic pairing gaps rather than a sharp direct Urca onset approximation. It builds on the authors' own prior work on global properties of dark neutron stars (Liu 2023, Liu 2024) and their cooling simulations for pure neutron stars (Taranto 2016, Fortin 2018, Wei 2019, Wei 2020, Das 2024). The results enable identification of possible observational signatures of large dark matter content, such as very massive but slow-cooling stars or very light but fast-cooling stars.
