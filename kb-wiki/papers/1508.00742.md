# General classical and quantum-mechanical description of magnetic resonance: An application to electric-dipole-moment experiments

> A general theoretical description of a magnetic resonance is presented. This description is necessary for a detailed analysis of spin dynamics in electric-dipole-moment experiments in storage rings. General formulas describing a behavior of all components of the polarization vector at the magnetic resonance are obtained for an arbitrary initial polarization. These formulas are exact on condition that the nonresonance rotating field is neglected. The spin dynamics is also calculated at frequencies far from resonance with allowance for both rotating fields. A general quantum-mechanical analysis of the spin evolution at the magnetic resonance is fulfilled and the full agreement between the classical and quantum-mechanical approaches is shown. Quasimagnetic resonances for particles and nuclei moving in noncontinuous perturbing fields of accelerators and storage rings are considered. Distinguishing features of quasimagnetic resonances in storage ring electric-dipole-moment experiments are investigated in detail. The exact formulas for the effect caused by the electric dipole moment are derived. The difference between the resonance effects conditioned by the rf electric-field flipper and the rf Wien filter is found and is calculated for the first time. The existence of this difference is crucial for the establishment of a consent between analytical derivations and computer simulations and for checking spin tracking programs. Main systematical errors are considered.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general classical and quantum-mechanical description of magnetic resonance that is exact on condition that the nonresonance rotating field is neglected, and demonstrates full agreement between the two approaches for spin dynamics linear in the spin, which is necessary for analyzing systematic errors in storage-ring electric-dipole-moment experiments.

## Method

The author derives general formulas for all components of the polarization vector at magnetic resonance for arbitrary initial polarization using the rotating-frame method in classical spin physics, then performs a quantum-mechanical analysis using the Foldy-Wouthuysen Hamiltonian and Pauli spin matrices, showing equivalence of the two approaches. The spin dynamics at frequencies far from resonance is calculated with allowance for both rotating fields, and the formalism is extended to moving particles and nuclei using the Thomas-Bargmann-Mishel-Telegdi equation and its extension for electric dipole moments in cylindrical and Frenet-Serret coordinate systems. Quasimagnetic resonances in noncontinuous perturbing fields of accelerators and storage rings are analyzed, with specific application to rf electric-field flippers and rf Wien filters.

## Key numbers

- The Rabi frequency (nutation frequency) at resonance is given by Ω = √[(ω₀ − ω)² + 𝔈²], where ω₀ is the spin precession frequency, ω is the driving frequency, and 𝔈 is the amplitude of the perturbing field in frequency units.
- For a purely magnetic storage ring, the perturbing field amplitude is 𝔈 = −(e/2m)(1/γ + G)𝓑, where G = (g−2)/2, γ is the Lorentz factor, and 𝓑 is the amplitude of the oscillatory magnetic field.
- The angular velocity of spin precession in the Frenet-Serret coordinate system for moving particles with EDM is given by Ω^(FS) = −(e/m)[G B − (Gγ/(γ+1))β(β·B) + (1/(γ²−1) − G)(β×E) + (η/2)(E − (γ/(γ+1))β(β·E) + β×B)], where η = 2mcd/(es) and d is the EDM.
- For weak perturbing fields (|𝔈| ≪ Ω), the minor spin components are of order |𝔈|/Ω, while changes in the major component are of order 𝔈²/Ω².

## Context

This work extends the standard theory of magnetic resonance presented in textbooks such as Slichter and Levitt to the specific requirements of storage-ring electric-dipole-moment experiments, building on the Thomas-Bargmann-Mishel-Telegdi equation (Thomas 1927, Bargmann-Mishel-Telegdi 1959) and its EDM extension (Nelson-Keefe-Farley-Semenov, Good-Bennett-Michel, Physica Scripta). The derived difference between resonance effects from rf electric-field flippers and rf Wien filters is calculated for the first time, which is crucial for reconciling analytical derivations with computer simulations and for checking spin tracking programs. This enables systematic error analysis for planned EDM experiments using deuterons (Lehrach 2012, Rathmann Wien Filter) and muons (muon g−2 experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory, Bennett 2006).
