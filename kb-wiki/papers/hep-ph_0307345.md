# Understanding Pentaquark States in QCD

> We estimate the mass of the pentaquark state with QCD sum rules and find that pentaquark states with isospin $I=0, 1, 2$ lie close to each other around $(1.55\pm 0.15)$ GeV. The experimentally observed baryon resonance $Θ^+ (1540)$ with $S=+1$ can be consistently identified as a pentaquark state if its $J^P={1\over 2}^-$. Such a state is expected in QCD. If its parity is positive, this pentaquark state is really exotic. Now the outstanding issue is to determine its quantum numbers experimentally.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculation estimates that pentaquark states with isospin I=0, 1, 2 all lie close to each other around (1.55 ± 0.15) GeV, and the experimentally observed Θ⁺(1540) can be consistently identified as a pentaquark state only if its spin-parity is Jᴾ = ½⁻; if its parity is instead positive, the state would be truly exotic.

## Method

The author constructs interpolating currents with definite isospin (I=0, 1, 2) for J=½ pentaquark states, ensuring they cannot couple to K⁺n molecular states. The two-point correlation functions are calculated using the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD, including perturbative terms and non-perturbative condensates up to dimension-6. Borel sum rules are then applied to extract the pentaquark masses from the spectral densities, with the continuum threshold s₀ and Borel parameter T varied to assess stability.

## Key numbers

- Pentaquark masses from QCD sum rules: M₂ = (1.53 ± 0.15) GeV (I=2), M₁ = (1.59 ± 0.15) GeV (I=1), M₀ = (1.56 ± 0.15) GeV (I=0), with central values at T = 2 GeV and s₀ = 4 GeV².
- The experimentally observed Θ⁺ resonance mass: 1.54 ± 0.01 GeV (LEPS Collaboration), 1539 ± 2 MeV (DIANA Collaboration), 1542 ± 5 MeV (CLAS Collaboration).
- Experimental widths: less than 25 MeV (LEPS), less than 9 MeV (DIANA), 21 MeV (CLAS, consistent with detector resolution).
- Statistical significances: 4.6σ (LEPS), 4.4σ (DIANA), 5.3σ (CLAS).
- Constituent quark model estimate for a positive-parity pentaquark: ~2050 MeV (4Mᵤ + Mₛ + ~400 MeV for orbital excitation, with Mᵤ ≈ 300 MeV, Mₛ ≈ 450 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method to pentaquark states, following the experimental discovery of the Θ⁺ resonance by LEPS (2003), DIANA (2003), and CLAS (2003). It contrasts with the chiral soliton model prediction of Diakonov et al. (1997), where only the I=0 member lies near 1540 MeV and other isospin states are several hundred MeV higher. The results also differ from the diquark-triquark constituent quark model estimate of Karliner and Lipkin (2003) (1592 MeV, I=0, Jᴾ = ½⁺) and the Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) diquark-diquark-antiquark model (Jᴾ = ½⁺). The paper enables future experimental determination of the Θ⁺ quantum numbers (parity, angular momentum, isospin) to distinguish between these theoretical scenarios, and suggests that the decay mode Θ′ → π⁰K⁺n could discriminate the isospin of the pentaquark.
