# Isospin mixing, Fermi transitions and signatures of nuclear deformation within a mean field approach

> Gamow-Teller and Fermi transitions are considered in a HF+BCS+pnQRPA theoretical framework. We show here how Gamow-Teller strength distributions can be used in a search for signatures of nuclear deformation in neutron deficient Pb, Hg and Po isotopes, as well as how Fermi transitions allow us to quantify isospin mixing in some even Kr isotopes around N=Z.

## Key claim

Gamow-Teller strength distributions in neutron-deficient Pb, Hg, and Po isotopes show a strong dependence on nuclear deformation that persists against changes in Skyrme forces and pairing treatment, potentially allowing shape identification from beta-decay patterns; Fermi transitions in even Kr isotopes around N=Z provide a quantitative measure of isospin mixing, but the magnitude of this mixing depends critically on whether BCS pairing correlations and QRPA correlations are included.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme interactions, followed by BCS pairing and proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) with separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions. For Gamow-Teller transitions, they computed strength distributions for each equilibrium deformation obtained from constrained HF+BCS calculations. For Fermi transitions, they quantified isospin mixing via the expectation value of T_perp^2 in the ground state, comparing results with and without Coulomb interaction and with weak versus realistic pairing gaps.

## Key numbers

- For 184Pb, the Gamow-Teller strength distribution shows a strong single peak at low energy in the prolate case, a single peak at higher energies in the spherical case, and a very fragmented distribution in the oblate case (qualitative, no numerical values given for peak positions or strengths)
- Isospin mixing in 72Kr with realistic pairing gaps (Delta ~ 1.5 MeV): <T_perp^2>_0 = 1.15 with Coulomb, 1.11 without Coulomb
- Isospin mixing in 72Kr with weak pairing (Delta = 0.1 MeV): <T_perp^2>_0 = 0.07 with Coulomb, 0.02 without Coulomb
- Isospin mixing in 70Kr with realistic pairing gaps: <T_perp^2>_0 = 0.43 with Coulomb, 0.41 without Coulomb
- Isospin mixing in 74Kr with realistic pairing gaps: <T_perp^2>_0 = 0.50 with Coulomb, 0.44 without Coulomb
- Isospin mixing in 76Kr with realistic pairing gaps: <T_perp^2>_0 = 0.30 with Coulomb, 0.23 without Coulomb
- Isospin mixing in 78Kr with realistic pairing gaps: <T_perp^2>_0 = 0.17 with Coulomb, 0.12 without Coulomb

## Context

This work extends the deformed pnQRPA formalism previously applied by Sarriguren et al. and by Moreno et al. to study beta-decay patterns in neutron-deficient nuclei. The authors show that Gamow-Teller strength distributions can serve as a signature of nuclear deformation, complementing traditional methods based on energy systematics. For isospin mixing, the results demonstrate that BCS pairing correlations induce large isospin-forbidden Fermi transitions, but QRPA correlations from isospin-conserving residual interactions partially restore isospin symmetry, building on earlier work by Moya de Guerra on angular momentum mixing in deformed nuclei. The planned measurements at ISOLDE/CERN in 2007 could test these predictions.
