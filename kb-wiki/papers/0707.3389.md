# In-medium chiral SU(3) dynamics and hypernuclear structure

> A previously introduced relativistic energy density functional, successfully applied to ordinary nuclei, is extended to hypernuclei. The density-dependent mean field and the spin-orbit potential are consistently calculated for a $Λ$ hyperon in the nucleus using the SU(3) extension of in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The leading long range $ΛN$ interaction arises from kaon-exchange and $2π$-exchange with $Σ$ hyperon in the intermediate state. Scalar and vector mean fields reflecting in-medium changes of the quark condensates are constrained by QCD sum rules. The model, applied to oxygen as a test case, describes spectroscopic data in good agreement with experiment. In particular, the smallness of the $Λ$ spin-orbit interaction finds a natural explanation in terms of an almost complete cancellation between scalar-vector background contributions and long-range terms generated by two-pion exchange.

## Key claim

The very small spin-orbit splitting observed in Λ hypernuclei is explained by an almost complete cancellation between the spin-orbit force generated by scalar-vector background mean fields (constrained by QCD sum rules) and a model-independent, "wrong-sign" spin-orbit term from in-medium two-pion exchange with an intermediate Σ hyperon, a mechanism that works only if the scalar-vector coupling ratio χ for the Λ is reduced below the naive quark-model value of 2/3 to values around 0.4–0.5 as suggested by QCD sum rules.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed relativistic energy density functional for ordinary nuclei to include a single Λ hyperon. The density-dependent Λ-nucleon scalar and vector couplings are decomposed into condensate background contributions (constrained by QCD sum rules) and contributions from in-medium kaon-exchange and two-pion-exchange with Σ intermediate states, calculated using chiral SU(3) perturbation theory. The Λ single-particle energies are obtained by solving coupled relativistic Kohn-Sham Dirac equations for the nucleon core and the Λ hyperon self-consistently in the no-sea approximation, with the spin-orbit correction from two-pion exchange evaluated in first-order perturbation theory using the nuclear density profile.

## Key numbers

- The Λ p-state energy in ¹⁶_ΛO is set to the empirical value ε_Λ^p = -1.86 ± 0.06 MeV (from Hashimoto review).
- The Λ s-state energy in ¹⁶_ΛO is ε_Λ^s = -12.42 ± 0.05 MeV (from Hashimoto review).
- The cutoff scale for short-distance dynamics is tuned to Λ̄ = 0.71 GeV, close to the value 0.7 GeV used in Kaiser (2004).
- The Λ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength from two-pion exchange is U_ls^Λ(k_f^(0)) ≃ -15 MeV fm² at k_f^(0) ≃ 1.36 fm⁻¹, opposite in sign to the nucleonic value of about 30 MeV fm².
- The ΣΛ mass splitting is M_Σ - M_Λ = 77.5 MeV.
- The spin-orbit splitting δ_Λ = ε_Λ(1p⁻¹_{1/2}) - ε_Λ(1p⁻¹_{3/2}) in ¹⁶_ΛO is reduced by about 1.3 MeV when the two-pion exchange spin-orbit term is added.
- The measured p_{3/2}-p_{1/2} spin-orbit splitting in ¹³_ΛC is 152 ± 90 keV, compared to 6 MeV in ordinary p-shell nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic energy density functional approach of Finelli (2003, 2005, 2006) from ordinary nuclei to hypernuclei, and implements the mechanism proposed by Kaiser (2004) for suppressing the Λ spin-orbit interaction at the nuclear matter level in finite systems. It contradicts earlier phenomenological relativistic mean-field models that required an unnaturally strong negative ωΛΛ tensor coupling (Pirner, Noble, and Jennings) to explain the small spin-orbit splitting, and goes beyond the Quark-Meson Coupling model of Tsushima and the density-dependent relativistic approach of Lenske (1999) which could not reproduce the anomalously small Λ-nucleus spin-orbit force. The work enables systematic calculations of Λ hypernuclear spectra across a wider range of nuclei using a microscopically grounded framework.
