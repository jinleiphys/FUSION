# Polarizability relations across real and virtual Compton scattering processes

> We derive two relations involving spin polarizabilities of a spin-1/2 particle and consider their empirical implications for the proton. Using the empirical values of the proton anomalous magnetic moment, electric and magnetic charge radii, moments of the spin structure functions $g_1$, $g_2$, and of two spin polarizabilities, the present relations constrain the low-momentum behavior of generalized polarizabilities appearing in virtual Compton scattering. In the case of the proton, the dispersive model evaluations of the spin and generalized polarizabilities appear to be consistent with these relations. The ongoing measurements of different electromagnetic observables at the MAMI, Jefferson Lab, and HI$γ$S facilities may be able to put these relations to a test, or use them to unravel the low-energy spin structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper derives two new sum rules that relate the slopes of generalized polarizabilities (GPs) measured in virtual Compton scattering (VCS) to spin polarizabilities from real Compton scattering (RCS) and moments of spin structure functions from inclusive electron scattering, but the relations are only valid for a spin-1/2 target and rely on a low-energy expansion around zero photon virtuality and zero energy.

## Method

The authors start from dispersion relations for the spin-dependent forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitudes S1 and S2, which relate their real parts to integrals over the spin structure functions g1 and g2. They perform a low-energy expansion (LEX) of these amplitudes around ν=0 and Q²=0, matching terms of the same order in ν² and Q² between the dispersion relation and the LEX expressed in terms of nucleon electromagnetic properties, spin polarizabilities, and generalized polarizabilities. The matching yields two sum rules: one from the Q²-derivative of the S1 dispersion relation at Q²=0, ν=0, and one from the ν²-term of the S2 dispersion relation at Q²=0.

## Key numbers

- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: κp ≈ 1.793
- Proton Pauli radius: ⟨r₂²⟩ = 0.58(4) fm² (from electric and magnetic radii of Bernauer (2013))
- Spin polarizability γE1M2 (non-pion-pole): DR with MAID2000 gives −0.03 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴; DR with MAID2007 gives −0.11 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴; experiment (Martel 2014) gives −0.7 ± 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Spin polarizability γE1E1 (non-pion-pole): DR with MAID2000 gives −4.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴; DR with MAID2007 gives −4.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴; experiment (Martel 2014) gives −3.5 ± 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- GP slope P′(M1,M1)1(0): DR with MAID2000 gives −5.8 GeV⁻⁵; DR with MAID2007 gives −4.7 GeV⁻⁵
- GP slope P′(L1,L1)1(0): DR with MAID2000 gives 3.4 GeV⁻⁵; DR with MAID2007 gives 4.3 GeV⁻⁵
- Sum rule prediction for I₁′(0): 6.8 GeV⁻² (MAID2000) and 4.0 GeV⁻² (MAID2007); experiment (Prok 2008) gives 7.6 ± 2.5 GeV⁻²
- Sum rule prediction for δLT: 1.45 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (MAID2000) and 1.51 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (MAID2007); MAID evaluation gives 1.34 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (Gerasimov 1965, Drell and Hearn 1966) and the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule to finite momentum transfer, connecting them to generalized polarizabilities measured in VCS that previously escaped such constraints. The new sum rules enable model-independent cross-checks between experiments at MAMI, Jefferson Lab, and HIγS that measure spin polarizabilities via RCS, GPs via VCS, and spin structure functions via inclusive electron scattering, and can test consistency of chiral perturbation theory calculations such as those by McGovern (2012) and Lensky (2014).
