# Nucleon generalized form factors with twisted mass fermions

> We present results on the nucleon form factors, momentum fraction and helicity moment for $N_f=2$ and $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions for a number of lattice volumes and lattice spacings. First results for a new $N_f=2$ ensemble at the physical pion mass are also included. The implications of these results on the spin content of the nucleon are discussed taking into account the disconnected contributions at one pion mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD results for nucleon form factors, momentum fraction, and helicity moment using twisted mass fermions, with the hedge that while results at the physical pion mass are consistent with experimental values, they still carry large statistical errors and require further high-statistics analysis to finalize.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using Nf=2 and Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass fermions at three lattice spacings smaller than 0.1 fm, including a new Nf=2 ensemble at the physical pion mass. They extracted nucleon matrix elements from three-point correlation functions using both the plateau method and the summation method to control excited state contamination, and computed disconnected contributions for the B55.32 ensemble at m_pi=373 MeV using approximately 150,000 statistics on 4700 configurations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a=0.0936(13)(25) fm for beta=1.90, a=0.0823(11)(35) fm for beta=1.95, and a=0.0646(7)(25) fm for beta=2.10 for the Nf=2+1+1 ensembles
- Lattice spacing a=0.0937(2)(2) fm for the Nf=2 physical ensemble
- The Sommer scale r0=0.495(6) fm determined from the nucleon mass using Nf=2+1+1 ensembles with pion mass less than 300 MeV
- The sigma-pi-N term sigma_piN=58(8)(7) MeV extracted from the chiral fit coefficient c1
- For the B55.32 ensemble at m_pi=373 MeV, fitting the scalar charge at t_s~1.0 fm underestimates the value by 25% compared to results at larger sink-source separations
- Disconnected contributions to g_A and g_s^{u+d} are about 10% of the connected part for the B55.32 ensemble
- For the isoscalar momentum fraction <x>_{u+d}, the disconnected contribution is consistent with zero

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure by Alexandrou et al. (2013) and Dinter et al. (2011) using twisted mass fermions, and provides first results at the physical pion mass for several observables. The findings enable more reliable predictions of nucleon sigma-terms, scalar and tensor charges that are crucial for constraining new physics at the TeV scale, as discussed by Bhattacharya et al. (2013), and will benefit from ongoing systematic studies by collaborations such as Green et al. (2012), Horsley et al. (2013), Capitani et al. (2012), Owen et al. (2012), and Bhattacharya et al. (2013) using simulations at near-physical pion masses.
