# Small Effects in Astrophysical Fusion Reactions

> We study the combined effects of vacuum polarization, relativity, Bremsstrahlung, and atomic polarization in nuclear reactions of astrophysical interest. It is shown that these effects do not solve the longstanding differences between the experimental data of astrophysical nuclear reactions at very low energies and the theoretical calculations which aim to include electron screening.

## Key claim

The combined effects of vacuum polarization, relativity, Bremsstrahlung, and atomic polarization are shown to be insufficient to resolve the longstanding discrepancy between experimental data for astrophysical fusion reactions at very low energies and theoretical calculations that include electron screening; these corrections never exceed a few percent and cannot explain the missing enhancement of fusion cross sections in atomic target experiments.

## Method

The authors calculated corrections to the fusion cross section using an s-wave WKB penetrability factor, computing the ratio of penetrabilities with and without each small effect for twelve astrophysically relevant reactions. For vacuum polarization they used the Uehling potential; for relativistic effects they employed a Darwin Hamiltonian to first order in 1/mc^2; for Bremsstrahlung they computed energy loss from the incoming branch of the trajectory using long-wavelength approximation; and for atomic polarization they evaluated second-order perturbation theory contributions from monopole and dipole virtual excitations of atomic electrons.

## Key numbers

- For the D(d,p)T reaction at E_min = 1.62 keV: vacuum polarization correction (1-R_VPol) = -0.95 x 10^-2, relativistic correction = 0.17 x 10^-3, Bremsstrahlung correction = 0.54 x 10^-3, atomic polarization dipole = 1.01 x 10^-5, atomic polarization monopole = 0.246 x 10^-1
- For the ^3He(^3He,2p)^4He reaction at E_min = 25 keV: vacuum polarization correction = -3.14 x 10^-2, relativistic correction = 1.75 x 10^-3, Bremsstrahlung correction = 0.58 x 10^-3, atomic polarization dipole = 0.35 x 10^-5, atomic polarization monopole = 0.321 x 10^-1
- For the ^10B(p,alpha)^7Be reaction at E_min = 18.70 keV: vacuum polarization correction = -2.38 x 10^-2, relativistic correction = 2.03 x 10^-3, Bremsstrahlung correction = 1.45 x 10^-3, atomic polarization dipole = 0.57 x 10^-5, atomic polarization monopole = 0.758 x 10^-1
- The vacuum polarization potential uses the fine structure constant alpha = 1/137 and the electron Compton wavelength lambda_e = 386 fm
- The Bremsstrahlung energy loss formula includes the Riemann zeta function value zeta(2,2) = 0.64493

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vacuum polarization in elastic scattering below the Coulomb barrier by Baur, Rosel, and Trautmann (1977) and in subbarrier fusion by Assenbaum, Langanke, and Soff (1988), as well as studies of other small effects in elastic scattering by Hussein et al. (1984, 1990) and Bertulani, Balantekin, and Ditzel (1994). The paper shows that none of these small corrections can explain the missing enhancement of fusion cross sections in atomic target experiments, supporting the suggestion by Bang, Ferreira, Maglione, and Hansteen (1996) that the discrepancy might instead be due to incorrect assumptions about the energy dependence of stopping power.
