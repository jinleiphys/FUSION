# Slow neutron production as a probe of hadron formation in high-energy $γ^*A$ reactions

> Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) experiments at the planned Electron-Ion Collider will be affected by details of the hadron formation inside the nuclear volume. Besides semi-inclusive particle production experiments decays of the target nucleus via emission of neutrons provide an additional opportunity to probe this domain. This paper reports on the hybrid dynamical+statistical calculations of low-energy neutron production in muon- and virtual photon-induced collisions with nuclei. We confirm the conclusion that the E665 data on neutron production in $μ^-$ + Pb DIS at 470 GeV indicate a strong suppression of the final state interaction for hadrons with momenta above $\sim 1$ GeV/c. Ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) can be used to test this suppression. The calculations of the neutron multiplicity distributions and $p_t$-spectra in photon - nucleus collisions at the energies accessible at the LHC and RHIC are presented for several models of hadron formation. We argue that studies of neutron production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions open a new window on the small-$x$ dynamics and hadron component of the photon wave function.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the E665 data on neutron production in muon-induced deep inelastic scattering on lead at 470 GeV indicate a strong suppression of final state interactions for hadrons with momenta above approximately 1 GeV/c, but this conclusion depends on neglecting nuclear shadowing effects that would strengthen the constraints on secondary interactions.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid dynamical+statistical calculations combining PYTHIA 6.4 for the initial hard gamma*-nucleon event, the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model for propagation of produced particles through the nucleus, and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for evaporation of low-energy neutrons from the excited nuclear residue. They tested three different prescriptions for hadron formation: the default GiBUU formation procedure, the quantum diffusion model (QDM), and a simple cutoff where only hadrons with momenta below a threshold value can interact.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum of 1 GeV/c for hadron interactions is required to reproduce the E665 data on neutron production in muon- + Pb DIS at 470 GeV
- The formation length is parameterized as l_form = a p_h with a = 0.56 fm c/GeV, based on analyses of pion electroproduction at JLab
- In the calculation with p_cut = 1 GeV/c, 57% of residual nuclei have A_res = 207 (one nucleon removed), while in the QDM calculation only 18% have one-hole events
- The average excitation energy of the residual nucleus follows approximately <E*_res> = 25 MeV (A - A_res)
- The average number of wounded nucleons per event is n_wound = 1/R_A ~ 1.25, accounting for nuclear shadowing effects

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical analysis by Strikman (1998) of the E665 data on slow neutron production in muon-induced DIS, which first demonstrated that only nucleons produced with momenta below 1 GeV/c could interact. The paper also builds on the GiBUU transport model studies by Gallmeister (2007) that described HERMES and EMC data on leading hadron attenuation. The authors propose that ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC can be used to test the suppression of final state interactions before the advent of the Electron-Ion Collider, opening a new window on small-x dynamics and the hadron component of the photon wave function.
