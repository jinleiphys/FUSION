# Thermalization at intermediate coupling

> We use the AdS/CFT conjecture to investigate the thermalization of large-N_c N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma in the limit of large but finite 't Hooft coupling. On the gravity side, we supplement the type IIB supergravity action by the full set of O(α'^3) operators, which enables us to derive O(λ^{-3/2}) corrections to the emission spectrum of prompt photons in one model of holographic thermalization. Decreasing the coupling strength from the λ=\infty limit, we observe a qualitative change in the way the photon spectral density approaches its thermal limit as a function of the photon energy. We interpret this behavior as a sign of the thermalization pattern of the plasma shifting from top/down towards bottom/up.

## Key claim

The paper uses the AdS/CFT conjecture to derive the leading O(λ^{-3/2}) corrections to the photon spectral density in a holographic model of thermalization, and interprets the qualitative change in the large-frequency behavior of the relative deviation from equilibrium as evidence that the thermalization pattern shifts from top/down toward bottom/up as the 't Hooft coupling is decreased from infinity.

## Method

The authors work within a model of holographic thermalization involving the gravitational collapse of a thin spherical shell in AdS₅ space, using a quasistatic approximation where the shell is treated as static when formulating boundary conditions for bulk fields. They supplement the type IIB supergravity action with the full set of O(α'³) operators, derive the γ-corrected AdS black hole metric and the corresponding Schrödinger equation for a U(1) vector field, then solve this equation both inside and outside the shell using junction conditions at the shell location. The photon spectral density is obtained from the retarded correlator of the R-symmetry current via the relation χ(ω) = (N_c² T²/2)(1 - 265γ/8) Im(Ψ'₊/Ψ₊)|_{u=0}, expanded to linear order in γ.

## Key numbers

- The γ parameter is defined as γ ≡ (1/8) ζ(3) λ^{-3/2}, where λ is the 't Hooft coupling.
- The γ-corrected relation between the horizon radius r_h and the field theory temperature is r_h = πT / (1 + 265γ/16).
- For r_s/r_h = 1.01, the minimum of the relative deviation R(ω) as a function of ω follows a power law ω_min(λ)/T ≈ α λ^β with α ≈ 1.25 and β ≈ 0.69.
- The photon production rate is given by dΓ_γ/dk₀ = (α k/π) n_B(k₀) χ(k₀)|_{k₀=k=2πTω}, where n_B(k₀) = (e^{k₀/T} - 1)^{-1}.

## Context

This work combines the approaches of Hassanain et al. (2011, 2012) on O(λ^{-3/2}) corrections to equilibrium photon production with the out-of-equilibrium collapsing shell model of Baier et al. (2012). It extends the λ = ∞ results of Baier et al. (2012) to finite coupling and complements the thermal finite-coupling calculations of Hassanain et al. (2011). The findings suggest that for λ values relevant to heavy-ion collisions (λ ∼ 20), the plasma may already be quite far from the strict top/down thermalization pattern seen at λ = ∞, motivating further study of energy-momentum tensor correlators and more realistic thermalization models.
