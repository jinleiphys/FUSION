# Decisive Role of Fragmentation Functions in Hard Hadron Production

> It is demonstrated that the fragmentation functions at large momentum fraction play a key role in hard hadron production from relativistic proton-proton collisions. We find that this region of the fragmentation functions is not strongly constrained by the electron-positron data. This freedom can be used (together with the transverse momentum distribution of partons) to reproduce hard pion-to-proton ratio data in relativistic proton-proton collisions.

# Decisive Role of Fragmentation Functions in Hard Hadron Production

## Key claim

The fragmentation functions at large momentum fraction (large z) dominate hard hadron production in proton-proton collisions, but this region is not strongly constrained by electron-positron data, leaving significant freedom to adjust the large-z behavior of fragmentation functions and the transverse momentum distribution of partons to reproduce observed proton-to-pion ratios.

## Method

The authors calculate hadron production cross sections in proton-proton collisions using perturbative QCD (pQCD) at leading order, convoluting parton distribution functions with partonic cross sections and fragmentation functions according to the factorization theorem. They define the ratio R_z(p_T) to quantify the contribution of different z-regions to the cross section, and they modify the parameter β in the standard fragmentation function parameterization D(z,μ₀²)=Nz^α(1-z)^β to explore the effect of changing the large-z behavior, using the KKP fragmentation functions as a baseline.

## Key numbers

- At √s=27.4 GeV and p_T=4 GeV, about 90% of the proton production cross section comes from z>0.6; at p_T=6 GeV, 90% comes from z>0.7
- At √s=130 GeV and p_T=4 GeV, approximately 60% of the cross section comes from z>0.5; at p_T=8 (16) GeV, 70% (95%) comes from z>0.5
- pQCD with original KKP proton fragmentation functions underestimates the p/π⁺ ratio at √s=27.4 GeV by up to a factor of 10
- Setting β=0.8β_KKP in the proton fragmentation function and using ⟨k_T²⟩=0.9 GeV² for both proton and pion brings the calculated p/π⁺ ratio close to data for p_T>6 GeV at √s=27.4 GeV
- Using ⟨k_T²⟩_p=2.6 GeV² for the proton and ⟨k_T²⟩_π=0.9 GeV² for the pion yields satisfactory agreement with data for p_T≳3 GeV at √s=27.4 GeV
- At √s=61 GeV, using β=0.8β_KKP and ⟨k_T²⟩_p=3 GeV² reproduces ISR data

## Context

This work extends the standard pQCD factorization approach to hadron production, where fragmentation functions are typically extracted from e⁺e⁻ data (Binnewies, Kniehl, and Kramer, 1995; Kniehl, Kramer, and Potter, 2001; Kretzer, Leader, and Christova, 2001; Bourhis et al., 2001). The authors show that the large-z region of fragmentation functions, which is poorly constrained by e⁺e⁻ data, is critical for hadron production at RHIC and LHC energies. This finding is relevant for understanding the anomalous proton-to-pion enhancement observed by PHENIX in Au+Au collisions (Adcox et al., 2002) and for jet tomography studies of the quark-gluon plasma (Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev, 2001; Wang and Wang, 2002). The work calls for a comprehensive study of fragmentation functions with particular attention to the large-z region, and suggests that available p/π ratios from pp collisions can be reproduced without invoking additional mechanisms beyond pQCD.
