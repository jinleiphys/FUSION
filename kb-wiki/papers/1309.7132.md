# Microscopic description of the odd-even effect in cold fission

> The time dependent equations of motion for the pair breaking effect were corroborated with a condition that fixes dynamically the number of particles on the two fission fragment. The single particle level scheme was calculated with the Woods-Saxon superasymmetric two center shell model. This model provides a continuous variation of the energies from one nucleus up to two separated fragments. The dissipated energy resorts from the time dependent pairing equations. A peculiar phenomenon was observed experimentally in cold fission: the odd partition yields are favored over the even ones. This odd-even effect for cold fission was explained microscopically.

## Key claim

The odd-even effect in cold fission, where odd-mass partitions are experimentally favored over even-mass ones, is explained microscopically by a dynamical mechanism in which the probability of realizing seniority-two (odd-odd) configurations at scission increases with internuclear velocity, while the probability of the seniority-zero (even-even) configuration decreases, and this velocity dependence is modulated by the specific inertia model used (cranking, non-adiabatic cranking, or Gaussian overlap approximation).

## Method

The time-dependent equations of motion for pair breaking were derived from a variational principle using a many-body Hamiltonian with pairing residual interactions, incorporating a Lagrange multiplier condition that dynamically fixes the number of particles in each fission fragment. The single-particle level scheme was calculated with the Woods-Saxon superasymmetric two-center shell model, providing continuous energy variation from the compound nucleus to separated fragments. The coupled channel equations were solved along a fission trajectory for the fragmentation ⁹⁰Kr+¹⁴⁴Ba (even-even) and ⁹⁰Rb+¹⁴⁴Cs (odd-odd) in ²³⁴U, using three different inertia approximations (cranking, non-adiabatic cranking, Gaussian overlap) and testing internuclear velocities from 5×10² to 3×10⁶ fm/fs.

## Key numbers

- Internuclear velocities tested: 5×10², 8×10², 10³, 3×10³, 10⁴, 3×10⁴, 10⁵, 3×10⁵, 10⁶, and 3×10⁶ fm/fs.
- Avoided level crossing regions identified at R ≈ 4.9, 15.3, 17.1, and 19.6 fm for neutrons, and at R ≈ 9.1, 14.7, 16, and 20 fm for protons.
- 32 seniority-two configurations for protons and 31 for neutrons were coupled to the seniority-zero configuration through avoided level crossings.
- The pairing active space was constructed with 58 single-particle levels around the Fermi energy.
- The fission barrier for the 144/90 partition shows a small bump at R ≈ 17 fm where the mass asymmetry parameter variation begins.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent pairing approach of Mirea (2009) and Mirea (2011) by incorporating a dynamical particle-number fixing condition and applying it to the specific cold fission odd-even effect observed experimentally by Schwab (1990) for ²³³U(n_th,f) and by Ham (1999, 2000) for Cf. The model provides a microscopic alternative to statistical explanations of the odd-even structure (Rejmund 2000, Avrigeanu 1999, Montoya 1984, Clerc 1986) and to arguments linking the effect to charge asymmetry evolution (Skh 2009, Skh 2010). The results enable future calculations of fission yield distributions as functions of excitation energy for different fragmentations, with the penetrability of the fission barrier determined by the classical action integral.
