# Scale Dependence of Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> The scale-dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which in recent years has been extensively analysed within the context of chiral effective field theory, is, in fact, inherent in any potential models constrained by a fit to scattering data. A comparison between a purely phenomenological potential and local interactions derived from chiral effective field theory suggests that--thanks to the ability to describe nucleon-nucleon scattering at higher energies, as well as the deuteron momentum distribution extracted from electro-disintegration data--phenomenological potentials are best suited for the description of nuclear dynamics at the scale relevant to neutron star matter.

## Key claim

Phenomenological potentials like Argonne \(v_{18}\) are better suited than local chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) potentials at next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) for describing nuclear dynamics at the high densities relevant to neutron star matter, because the \(\chi\)EFT potentials fail to accurately describe nucleon-nucleon interactions at center-of-mass energies exceeding \(\sim 100\) MeV.

## Method

The author compared the Argonne \(v_{18}\) (AV18) phenomenological potential to local \(\chi\)EFT potentials of Ref. [N2LO] by computing neutron-proton scattering phase shifts in the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel and the deuteron momentum distribution \(n(k)\). The phase shifts were obtained using the approach of Ref. [Calogero], and the momentum distribution was derived from the deuteron wave function and compared to data from exclusive \(\isotope[2][]{H}(e,e^\prime p)\) and inclusive \(\isotope[2][]{H}(e,e^\prime)\) experiments, including a \(y\)-scaling analysis.

## Key numbers

* The AV18 potential fits all phase shifts in the Nijmegen database up to laboratory energy \(E_{\rm lab} = 350\) MeV, while the \(\chi\)EFT potentials of Ref. [N2LO] are fitted only up to \(E_{\rm lab} = 150\) MeV (for regulator range \(R_0 = 1.0\) fm) and 100 MeV (for \(R_0 = 1.2\) fm).
* The AV18 potential accurately describes phase shift data up to \(E_{\rm lab} \approx 600\) MeV, corresponding to pure neutron matter densities as high as \(4n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 0.16\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\) is the equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter).
* The \(\chi\)EFT potentials of Ref. [N2LO] can only reliably describe pure neutron matter up to \(n \lesssim 2n_0\).
* The deuteron momentum distribution from AV18 agrees with data up to momenta \(k \sim 700\) MeV, while \(\chi\)EFT potentials provide an accurate description only at \(k \lesssim 300\) MeV; at \(k \sim 500\) MeV the uncertainty from the chiral expansion reaches \(\sim 50\%\).
* For the AV18 potential, the deuteron binding energy \(\langle E \rangle = -2.225\) MeV, kinetic energy \(\langle T \rangle = 19.791\) MeV, and interaction energy \(\langle V \rangle = -22.016\) MeV; the region \(k > 350\) MeV contributes about 16% of \(\langle T \rangle\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of scale dependence in nucleon-nucleon interactions, previously analyzed within \(\chi\)EFT by Weinberg (1990) and others, to show that it is inherent in any potential fitted to scattering data. The comparison with the phenomenological AV18 potential (Wiringa et al., 1995) and local \(\chi\)EFT potentials (Gezerlis et al., 2013, 2014) demonstrates that phenomenological models are better suited for neutron star matter at supra-nuclear densities, a conclusion consistent with the results of Tews et al. (2013) on the limited density range of \(\chi\)EFT. The analysis enables improved modeling of neutron star structure and gravitational-wave emission, and raises the question of when nucleonic degrees of freedom break down in dense matter.
