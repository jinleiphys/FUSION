# Exact renormalization group and many-fermion systems

> The exact renormalization group methods is applied to many fermion systems with short-range attractive force. The strength of the attractive fermion-fermion interaction is determined from the vacuum scattering length. A set of approximate flow equations is derived including fermionic and bosonic fluctuations. The numerical solutions show a phase transition to a gapped phase. The inclusion of bosonic fluctuations is found to be significant only in the small-gap regime.

## Key claim

The exact renormalization group (ERG) method, when applied to many-fermion systems with short-range attractive forces, predicts a phase transition to a gapped phase; however, the inclusion of bosonic fluctuations is significant only in the small-gap regime, and their effect on the gap itself is at most about 1% for \(1/|p_F a_0| < 1\).

## Method

The authors start from an effective field theory describing s-wave fermion-fermion interactions via a contact potential, with the coupling strength fixed by the vacuum scattering length \(a_0\). They introduce a boson field whose vacuum expectation value acts as the order parameter (the fermion energy gap \(\Delta\)) and write an Ansatz for the Legendre-transformed effective action \(\Gamma\). The ERG flow equation for \(\Gamma\) is then solved numerically, including both fermion and boson loop contributions, with regulators that cut off modes below a momentum scale \(k\). The flow starts at high \(k\) from a symmetric phase and evolves to lower \(k\), where a transition to a broken phase with nonzero \(\Delta\) occurs.

## Key numbers

- For neutron matter parameters (\(M = 4.76\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(p_F = 1.37\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)), the transition to the broken phase occurs at \(k \simeq 1.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The effect of boson loops on the gap is at most \(\sim 1\%\) for \(1/|p_F a_0| < 1\).
- For the case of neutron matter, the computed gaps are of the order of 30 MeV.
- The starting scale \(K\) must be \(K > 5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to avoid detectable dependence of results on the cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the ERG approach to many-fermion systems with pairing, building on earlier applications of the ERG in particle and condensed-matter physics (Wetterich, 1993; Berges, Tetradis, and Wetterich, 2002). The overall crossover from BCS pairing to BEC as a function of \(1/(p_F a_0)\) reproduces the picture found by Marani et al. (2004) using fermion loops only. The results enable future improvements such as including the effective range of the interaction and three-body forces, as well as explicit treatment of particle-hole channels (RPA phonons).
