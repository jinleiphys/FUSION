# An optimized basis for hadronic light-by-light scattering

> We present a new basis for the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) tensor that is optimized for the evaluation of narrow-resonance contributions to HLbL scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. As main advantage, kinematic singularities are manifestly absent for pseudoscalar, scalar, and axial-vector states, while the remaining singularities for tensor resonances are minimized, even avoided for special cases, and simple crossing relations among the scalar functions maintained. We scrutinize the properties of this new basis for the scalar-QED pion box, demonstrating that the partial-wave convergence even slightly improves compared to our previous work, and discuss the physical sum rules that ensure basis independence of the HLbL contribution. Finally, we provide explicit expressions for narrow (pseudo-)scalar, axial-vector, and tensor intermediate states in terms of their respective transition form factors.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new basis for the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) tensor that removes kinematic singularities for pseudoscalar, scalar, and axial-vector narrow-resonance contributions, and minimizes them for tensor resonances, but they find that complete removal of kinematic singularities for tensor states is not possible within the dispersive formalism of Colangelo et al. (2017).

## Method

The authors start from the Bardeen-Tung-Tarrach (BTT) decomposition of the HLbL tensor and the singly-on-shell basis of Colangelo et al. (2017), which contains 27 scalar functions. They construct a basis change by forming linear combinations of the 15 physical sum rules (nine independent) that leave scalar and axial-vector contributions singularity-free, using three free functions f, g, h to optimize the tensor contributions. The new basis is validated by computing the partial-wave convergence of the scalar-QED pion-box contribution and comparing it to the previous basis.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of the muon anomalous magnetic moment is a_mu^exp = 116,592,059(22) x 10^{-11}, with 0.19 ppm precision
- The HLbL contribution is a_mu^HLbL = 92(19) x 10^{-11}, needing improvement by at least a factor of 2
- The pion-box contribution benchmark is a_mu^{pi-box} = -16.42 x 10^{-11}
- In the new basis, the pion-box saturation reaches 95.0% at J=2 and 101.1% at J=4 (average of fixed-s,t,u), compared to 89.6% and 95.7% in the old basis
- At J=20, the new basis average saturation is 100.33% versus 99.71% in the old basis

## Context

This work extends the dispersive formalism for HLbL scattering developed by Colangelo et al. (2017) for the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. It addresses the problem that kinematic singularities in the singly-on-shell basis prevented straightforward evaluation of axial-vector and tensor resonance contributions. The new basis enables evaluation of axial-vector contributions without kinematic singularities and simplifies tensor contributions, though complete removal of tensor singularities requires the alternative dispersive approach in triangle kinematics recently worked out by Ludtke et al. (2023). The results provide explicit expressions for narrow pseudoscalar, scalar, axial-vector, and tensor intermediate states in terms of their transition form factors.
