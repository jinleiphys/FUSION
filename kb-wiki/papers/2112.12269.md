# Angular Momentum Eigenstates of the Isotropic 3-D Harmonic Oscillator: Phase-Space Distributions and Coalescence Probabilities

> The isotropic 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential can serve as an approximate description of many systems in atomic, solid state, nuclear, and particle physics. In particular, the question of 2 particles binding (or coalescing) into angular momentum eigenstates in such a potential has interesting applications. We compute the probabilities for coalescence of two distinguishable, non-relativistic particles into such a bound state, where the initial particles are represented by generic wave packets of given average positions and momenta. We use a phase-space formulation and hence need the Wigner distribution functions of angular momentum eigenstates in isotropic 3-dimensional harmonic oscillators. These distribution functions have been discussed in the literature before but we utilize an alternative approach to obtain these functions. Along the way, we derive a general formula that expands angular momentum eigenstates in terms of products of 1-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenstates.

## Key claim

The paper computes coalescence probabilities for two distinguishable, non-relativistic particles into bound states with well-defined orbital angular momentum in an isotropic 3-D harmonic oscillator potential, where the initial particles are represented by Gaussian wave packets with given average positions and momenta; the formalism assumes no spin-orbit coupling and averages over the magnetic quantum number, limiting direct applicability to cases where final-state polarization is not measured.

## Method

The authors first derive a general formula expanding 3-D angular momentum eigenstates (AME) of the isotropic harmonic oscillator in terms of products of 1-D harmonic oscillator eigenstates (FE), using generating functions for Hermite polynomials, associated Laguerre polynomials, and spherical harmonics. They then compute the Wigner distribution functions for these angular momentum eigenstates, averaged over the magnetic quantum number \(m\), by expressing the 3-D Wigner functions as sums of products of 1-D Wigner functions weighted by the expansion coefficients. Finally, they calculate coalescence probabilities by taking the overlap of the Wigner distributions of two initial Gaussian wave packets with the Wigner distributions of the bound states, integrating over the relative coordinates.

## Key numbers

* The ground state Wigner distribution is \(W_{00} = \frac{1}{\pi^3\hbar^3} e^{-\frac{q^2}{\hbar^2 \nu^2}-\nu^2 r^2}\).
* For the \(l=1\), \(k=0\) state, \(W_{01} = W_{00} \left( -1 +\frac{2}{3} \nu^2 r^2 +\frac{2}{3} \frac{q^2}{\hbar^2 \nu^2} \right)\).
* For the \(l=2\), \(k=0\) state, \(W_{02} = W_{00} \left( 1 + \frac{4}{15} \nu^4 r^4 -\frac{4}{3}\nu^2 r^2 + \frac{16}{15} \frac{r^2 q^2}{\hbar^2} - \frac{8}{15} \frac{(\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{q})^2}{\hbar^2} - \frac{4}{3} \frac{q^2}{\hbar^2 \nu^2} + \frac{4}{15} \frac{q^4}{\hbar^4 \nu^4} \right)\).
* For the \(l=0\), \(k=1\) state, \(W_{10} = W_{00} \left( 1 + \frac{2}{3} \nu^4 r^4 -\frac{4}{3}\nu^2 r^2 - \frac{4}{3} \frac{r^2 q^2}{\hbar^2} + \frac{8}{3} \frac{(\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{q})^2}{\hbar^2} - \frac{4}{3} \frac{q^2}{\hbar^2 \nu^2} + \frac{2}{3} \frac{q^4}{\hbar^4 \nu^4} \right)\).
* The energy quantum number \(N\) is defined as \(N = n_1+n_2+n_3 = 2k + l\), and the dimension of the degenerate subspace is \(d_N = \frac{1}{2} (N+1)(N+2)\).

## Context

This work extends the phase-space formulation of quark coalescence previously developed by some of the authors (Han, 2016) for energy eigenstates without well-defined orbital angular momentum to states with definite angular momentum quantum numbers. It provides an alternative derivation of Wigner distributions for angular momentum eigenstates compared to the Moshinsky-bracket approach of Shlomo and Prakash (1981), and generalizes results for low-\(l\) cases given by Baltz (1995) and others. The formalism enables calculation of coalescence probabilities for meson bound states (e.g., \(J/\psi\) and \(\chi_{c2}\)) in heavy-ion collisions and for light nuclei formation, where the initial particles are described by classical phase-space distributions from transport models.
