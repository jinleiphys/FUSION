# Construction of spectral functions for medium nuclei

> This article is aimed at improving the description of lepton-nucleus interactions in the sub-GeV energy range. Approximate spectral functions for oxygen, calcium, and argon are constructed and used to obtain the electron cross sections in a given scattering angle. Comparison with a sample of available experimental data shows satisfactory agreement. Discrepancy between the presented model and the systematic computations available for oxygen [O. Benhar et al., Phys. Rev. D 72, 053005 (2005)] is also found to be very small. Analysis of appropriate kinematical regions leads to the conclusion that the obtained argon spectral function should describe well neutrino scattering in the 800-MeV energy region. Several approximations used in the model are critically reviewed. All the details needed to implement the presented approach in Monte Carlo simulations are given.

## Key claim

The paper constructs approximate spectral functions for medium-mass nuclei (oxygen, calcium, argon) using a Gaussian parameterization of the mean-field part and a correlated part based on two-nucleon interactions, with the claim that the model should describe neutrino scattering at 800 MeV well, but only after validation against electron scattering data in specific kinematic regions where the impulse approximation is reliable (momentum transfer above approximately 350–400 MeV).

## Method

The authors decompose the spectral function into a mean-field part (80–90% of nucleons) described by Gaussian-broadened shell-model states with equal momentum distributions per level, and a correlated part (short-range two-nucleon correlations) following the Kulagin-Petti analytic form. They compute quasielastic electron-nucleus cross sections using the de Forest prescription for off-shell kinematics, restore electromagnetic current conservation via a correction term, and include final-state interactions through a time-independent optical potential with real and imaginary parts fitted to proton scattering data on calcium. The model is implemented for oxygen, calcium, and argon using momentum distributions from Bisconti, Arias, and Co (2005) separated into mean-field and correlated components.

## Key numbers

- For oxygen, the simplification of neglecting recoil energy in the correlated part yields a change in cross section of less than 0.2%.
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section used for final-state interactions is set to 17.4 mb, corresponding to 200-MeV protons at saturation density 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The real part of the optical potential is set to zero for nucleon momenta above 3.1 fm\(^{-1}\).
- For 0.8-GeV neutrino scattering, the relevant electron scattering angles are \(\theta_e \sim 23^\circ\) at \(E_e = 1.2\) GeV, \(\theta_e \sim 25^\circ\) at \(E_e = 1.08\) GeV, and \(\theta_e \sim 30^\circ\) at \(E_e = 0.88\) GeV.
- The impulse approximation is found to become unreliable when momentum transfer is smaller than approximately 350–400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ankowski and Sobczyk (2006) and Ankowski (2007) by improving the spectral function construction for medium-mass nuclei and including final-state interactions via optical potentials, following the treatment of Benhar et al. (2005) for oxygen and Butkevich and Mikheyev for calcium. The model enables implementation in Monte Carlo simulations for neutrino oscillation experiments using liquid argon targets, and its validation against electron scattering data for oxygen, calcium, and argon provides a basis for predicting neutrino cross sections at 800 MeV.
