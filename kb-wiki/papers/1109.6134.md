# Observable to explore high density behaviour of symmetry energy

> We aim to see the sensitivity of collective transverse in-plane flow to symmetry energy at low as well as high densities and also to see the effect of different density dependencies of symmetry energy on the same.

## Key claim

The paper claims that collective transverse in-plane flow is sensitive to the symmetry energy and its density dependence only at low incident energies (100 MeV/nucleon), not at higher energies (400 and 800 MeV/nucleon), because at high energies the brief duration of the high-density phase prevents the symmetry energy from affecting the flow.

## Method

The study used the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions. The transverse in-plane flow was calculated using both the slope of the average transverse momentum per nucleon as a function of rapidity and the directed transverse in-plane flow, for three different density dependencies of the symmetry energy: proportional to density (u), to u^0.4, and to u^2, where u is the ratio of density to saturation density.

## Key numbers

- At 100 MeV/nucleon incident energy, for the symmetry energy proportional to ρ^0.4, the directed transverse in-plane flow ⟨p_x^dir⟩ equals 0.
- Simulations were performed at incident energies of 100, 400, and 800 MeV/nucleon.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is known to be around 30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of the nuclear matter equation of state and symmetry energy, which are important for understanding radioactive nuclei and supernova explosions (Li, 2008). The paper uses the IQMD model (Hartnack, 1998) and the directed flow method (Gautam, 2010) to show that transverse flow is a useful observable only at low energies, contradicting the hope that it could probe high-density symmetry energy behavior. This result suggests that other observables must be found to explore the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities.
