# Nuclear Matter with Quark-Meson Coupling I: Comparison of Nontopological Soliton Models

> A system of nontopological solitons interacting through scalar and vector meson exchange is used to model nuclear matter. The models studied are of the Friedberg-Lee type, which exhibit dynamical bag formation due to the coupling of quarks to a scalar composite gluon field. It is shown that the chiral chromodielectric model gives the best fit to the empirical data. The presence of the scalar meson guarantees saturation and an increase of the proton charge radius with nuclear density consistent with the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The chiral chromodielectric (χCD) model provides the best fit to empirical nuclear matter data among the Friedberg-Lee type nontopological soliton models studied, but this conclusion depends on the leading power of the quark-σ coupling vertex and on the inclusion of explicit scalar and vector meson fields, which were absent in earlier Friedberg-Lee nuclear matter calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the Euler-Lagrange equations for quarks coupled to a scalar glueball field σ, plus scalar (φ) and vector (Vμ) meson fields, in the mean-field approximation with a Wigner-Seitz cell containing one nucleon. They compare three models: the original Friedberg-Lee (FL) model with linear quark-σ coupling, a quadratic-coupling variant (FL²), and the χCD model where the coupling depends on a chromodielectric function κ(σ). The scalar meson field φ₀ is determined by minimizing the total energy density, and the vector meson field V₀ is obtained from the average quark density in the cell.

## Key numbers

- The χCD model yields a nucleon mass of 1176 MeV and a proton rms radius of 0.876 fm after parameter fitting.
- The bag constant B is 46.6 MeV/fm³ and the glueball mass m_GB is 1.82 GeV for the χCD model parameters.
- The FL model breaks down at a Wigner-Seitz cell radius of approximately 0.8 fm, while the χCD model shows stable solutions down to at least 0.05 fm.
- The FL² model breaks down at a cell radius of approximately 0.25 fm, above the transition to the uniform plasma phase.
- The empirical nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ corresponds to a Wigner-Seitz cell radius R₀ = 1.12 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier Friedberg-Lee soliton calculations by Birse (1988) and Wilets (1989) that did not include background meson fields, and contrasts with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) which assumes non-overlapping MIT bags. The inclusion of scalar and vector mesons produces nuclear saturation and an increase of the proton charge radius with density consistent with the EMC effect, effects absent in prior Friedberg-Lee nuclear matter studies. The next paper in this series aims to improve the modeling of a liquid of solitons beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation.
