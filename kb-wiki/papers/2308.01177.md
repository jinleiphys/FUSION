# Thermalization of a jet wake in QCD kinetic theory

> We study the energy deposition of a high-momentum parton traveling through a Quark-Gluon Plasma using QCD kinetic theory. We show that the energy is first transported to the soft sector by collinear cascade and then isotropised by elastic scatterings. Remarkably, we find that the jet wake can be well described by a thermal distribution function with angle-dependent temperature. This could be used for effective phenomenological descriptions of jet thermalization in realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.

# Thermalization of a jet wake in QCD kinetic theory

## Key claim
The energy deposited by a high-momentum parton traversing a Quark-Gluon Plasma first thermalizes in each angular direction (via collinear splittings) before isotropizing (via elastic scatterings), such that the jet wake can be described by a thermal distribution with an angle-dependent temperature, but only in the linearized regime where the perturbation is small compared to the background.

## Method
The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation of QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) for a gluonic system, decomposing the full distribution into a background and a small perturbation representing a jet with initial energy E. They study two scenarios: a static thermal background (Bose-Einstein distribution) and an expanding non-equilibrium background following the initial condition of Kurkela (2015). The evolution of angular-dependent moments of the perturbation is used to extract effective temperatures and to compare the jet perturbation with an azimuthally symmetric perturbation obtained by scaling the background amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Jet initial energy set to E = 15 T, where T is the temperature of the static thermal background.
- The collinear splitting timescale t_{1↔2} is shorter than the elastic scattering timescale t_{2↔2} for reaching equilibrium values of particle number and transverse pressure.
- For the expanding background, the normalized pressure anisotropy A of the jet perturbation collapses onto that of the azimuthally symmetric perturbation around scaled time ω̃ ≈ 2, with A(ω̃≈2) ≠ 0.
- The jet initial momentum distribution is a Gaussian with width σ = 0.1 in momentum space.

## Context
This work extends the linearized EKT approach of Kurkela (2014) and Kurkela (2018) for jet perturbations, and the background hydrodynamization studies of Kurkela (2015) and Kurkela (2018). It demonstrates that jet thermalization proceeds in two stages (angular thermalization then isotropization), and that the jet hydrodynamizes later than background-scale perturbations due to its higher initial momentum scale. The results enable macroscopic linear-response descriptions of jet energy loss in expanding medium simulations, following the methodology of Kurkela (2018).
