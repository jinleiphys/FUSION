# BCS-BEC crossover in spatially modulated fermionic condensates

> Several novel multi-component fermionic condensates show universal behavior under imbalance in the number of fermionic species. Here I discuss their phase structure, thermodynamics, and the transition from the weak (BCS) to strong (BEC) coupling regime. The inhomogeneous superconducting phases are illustrated on the example of the Fulde-Ferrell phase which appears in the weak coupling regime, at low temperatures and large asymmetries. The inhomogeneous phases persist through the crossover up to (and possibly beyond) the transition to the strong coupling regime.

# BCS-BEC crossover in spatially modulated fermionic condensates

## Key claim
Inhomogeneous superconducting phases such as the Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase, which appear in the weak coupling regime at low temperatures and large asymmetries, persist through the BCS-BEC crossover up to (and possibly beyond) the transition to the strong coupling regime, though the exact evolution into the BEC regime is not fully understood.

## Method
The paper analyzes the phase structure and thermodynamics of imbalanced fermionic condensates by solving mean-field gap equations together with density equations for each fermionic component, using the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) framework extended to asymmetrical systems. The inhomogeneous Fulde-Ferrell phase is studied via an Ansatz where the gap takes a single plane-wave dependence on total momentum, and the condensate wave-function is examined across the weak-to-strong coupling transition.

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless coupling λ transitions from weak coupling (λ ≪ 1) to strong coupling (λ ≫ 1) across the BCS-BEC crossover.
- The oscillation period of the condensate wave-function in the weak coupling limit is l ≃ 2π k_F^{-1}, where k_F is the Fermi wave-number.
- The FF phase occupies the low-temperature and large asymmetry portion of the T-α phase diagram, with a tri-critical Lifshitz point where normal, BCS, and FF phases meet.

## Context
This work extends the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory of BCS-BEC crossover to imbalanced fermionic ensembles, building on prior studies of pairing in low-density nuclear matter by Lombardo et al. (2001) and experimental realizations in ultracold atomic vapors by Jin (2006). The paper generalizes earlier treatments of the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase to the strong coupling regime, and enables future investigation of whether the FF state has a strong-coupling counterpart where tightly bound states carry a super-current.
