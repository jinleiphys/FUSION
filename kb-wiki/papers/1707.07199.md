# Estimates and power counting in uniform matter with softened interactions

> Modern softened nucleon-nucleon interactions are well-suited for perturbative many-body calculations, but a many-body power counting scheme is lacking. Estimates of diagrammatic contributions at finite density are important ingredients in such a scheme. Here we show how to make quantitative estimates of the particle-particle and hole-hole channel in uniform nuclear matter for soft interactions. We also use estimates to assess the role of normal-ordered three-body forces for a pure contact interaction.

## Key claim

The authors show how to make quantitative estimates of particle-particle and hole-hole ladder contributions in uniform nuclear matter for softened nucleon-nucleon interactions, with the caveat that these estimates rely on approximations that factorize momentum integrations and are validated only for s-wave channels and for interactions softened via similarity renormalization group evolution.

## Method

The authors apply angle-averaging of Pauli blocking operators and replace total momentum and hole relative momentum by their root-mean-square values in the Fermi sea, which factorizes the momentum integrations in Goldstone diagram contributions to the energy per particle. They then construct a real symmetric kernel matrix from the potential matrix elements, particle phase space, and energy denominators, whose eigenvalues define an expansion parameter eta for the particle-particle ladder. For three-body forces, they estimate normal-ordered two-body contributions versus residual three-body terms using a pure contact interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

* For the 1S0 partial wave with AV18 at lambda = 4.0 fm^-1 near saturation density rho = 0.163 fm^-3, the absolute error between exact and estimated second-order energy per particle is 0.088 MeV, third-order error is 0.031 MeV, and fourth-order error is 6.8E-3 MeV.
* For the 1S0 partial wave with AV18 at lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 near saturation density rho = 0.163 fm^-3, the absolute error between exact and estimated second-order energy per particle is 0.11 MeV, third-order error is 0.010 MeV, and fourth-order error is 8.6E-4 MeV.
* For the 3S1-3D1 partial wave with AV18 at lambda = 4.0 fm^-1 near saturation density rho = 0.163 fm^-3, the absolute error between exact and estimated second-order energy per particle is 0.17 MeV, third-order error is 0.048 MeV, and fourth-order error is 0.27 MeV.
* For the 3S1-3D1 partial wave with AV18 at lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 near saturation density rho = 0.163 fm^-3, the absolute error between exact and estimated second-order energy per particle is 0.43 MeV, third-order error is 0.085 MeV, and fourth-order error is 0.028 MeV.
* The root-mean-square total momentum for two nucleons in the Fermi sea is Pav = sqrt(6/5) kF, and the root-mean-square hole relative momentum is kav = sqrt(3/10) kF.
* The expansion parameter eta for the pp ladder is defined as eta = (2m/pi hbar^2) times the absolute value of the largest kernel eigenvalue, and for lambda = 4.0 fm^-1 this parameter becomes less than 1 at saturation density for the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 partial waves.

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone hole-line expansion (Day 1967, Rajaraman 1967, Day 1978) which assumed potentials with strongly repulsive cores, to modern softened interactions from chiral effective field theory (Machleidt 2011, Epelbaum 2008) and similarity renormalization group evolution (Bogner 2009). The analysis is closely related to the Weinberg eigenvalue approach (Weinberg 1963) for assessing perturbativeness of the Born series, and the authors show their expansion parameter tracks the largest Weinberg eigenvalue from Ramanan (2007) in the zero-density limit. The results enable systematic power counting for many-body perturbation theory with soft interactions and provide a framework for credible error estimates in nuclear matter calculations.
