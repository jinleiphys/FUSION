# Near-threshold correlations of neutrons

> The appearance of charged-particle clustering in near-threshold configuration is a phenomenon that can be explained in the Open Quantum System description of the atomic nucleus. In this work we apply the realistic Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum to elucidate the emergence of neutron correlations in near-threshold many-body states coupled to l=1,2 neutron decay channels. Spectral consequences of such continuum coupling are briefly discussed together with the emergence of complex multi-neutron correlations.

## Key claim
The emergence of multi-neutron correlations in near-threshold states arises from the collective coupling of shell-model eigenstates via the continuum, analogous to charged-particle clustering, but the observability of such clustering depends critically on the neutron angular momentum and is expected only in a narrow energy window around the decay threshold.

## Method
The authors applied the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), a configuration-interaction approach that uses an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian to describe open quantum systems. They studied the continuum-coupling correlation energy and exceptional points (EPs) of the complex-extended SMEC Hamiltonian for J^π=0^+ states in ²⁰O and ¹⁶C coupled to ℓ=1 and ℓ=2 one-neutron decay channels, using the USDB and ZBM2C Hamiltonians respectively for the closed-system part.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰O coupled to the ℓ=2 neutron decay channel ¹⁹O(5/2⁺)+n(ℓ=2): the energy minimum of the continuum-coupling correlation energy for the ground state is shifted above the threshold by about 0.15 MeV, with a peak width (σ) of about 0.5 MeV.
- For ²⁰O with ℓ=2 coupling: the turning point (TP) energy E_TP ≃ 0.18 MeV lies above the neutron emission threshold, and significant configuration mixing is predicted up to E ≃ 1.2 MeV.
- For ¹⁶C coupled to the ℓ=1 neutron decay channel ¹⁵C(1/2⁻)+n(ℓ=1): the dramatic variation of the lowest eigenvalue around the threshold is restricted to a range of only few keV, and the width of the correlation energy peak is less than about 1 keV.
- The continuum coupling strength used in both calculations was V₀ = -1000 MeV·fm³.

## Context
This work extends the open quantum system framework previously applied to charged-particle clustering (Okołowicz et al., 2012) to the case of neutral-particle (neutron) correlations. It addresses the long-standing problem of multi-neutron clustering, revived by experimental reports of tetraneutron candidates in the disintegration of ¹⁴Be (Marqués et al., 2002), which spurred theoretical work ruling out bound tetraneutrons (Pieper et al., 2002). The results suggest that while multi-neutron correlations around ℓ=1 thresholds may appear across the nuclear landscape, their experimental detection is challenging due to the extremely narrow energy window; the closest approximation to genuine multi-neutron clusters are 2n-halos, though 2n-separation energies in known cases are too large for genuine dineutron configurations (Hagino and Sagawa, 2005).
