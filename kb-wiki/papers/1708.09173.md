# Interferometry correlations in central p+Pb collisions

> We present results on interferometry correlations for pions emitted in central p+Pb collisions at 5.02TeV in a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with initial conditions from the Glauber Monte Carlo model. The correlation function is calculated as a function of the pion pair rapidity. The extracted interferometry radii show a weak rapidity dependence, reflecting the lack of boost invariance of the pion distribution. A cross-term between the $out$ and $long$ directions is found to be nonzero. The results obtained in the hydrodynamic model are in fair agreement with recent data of the ATLAS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic model with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions reproduces the ATLAS Collaboration's measured HBT radii and the nonzero cross-term $R_{ol}$ in central p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the model's rapidity dependence of the HBT radii is slightly weaker than observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with initial entropy density from the Glauber Monte Carlo model, generating pions from the freeze-out hypersurface. They constructed three-dimensional correlation functions for same-charged pion pairs binned in relative momentum and average transverse momentum in the longitudinal comoving system, using a symmetrized plane wave two-pion wave function. The correlation functions were fitted with an exponential ansatz identical to that used by the ATLAS Collaboration to extract the HBT radii $R_{out}$, $R_{side}$, $R_{long}$, and the cross-term $R_{ol}$.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV for p+Pb collisions at the LHC
- The nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass frame is shifted by 0.465 rapidity units from the laboratory frame
- The fit range for relative momentum was $q_{low} < |\mathbf{q}| < 0.3$ GeV, with $q_{low} \in [0.02, 0.03]$ GeV
- The pair transverse momentum bin used for rapidity dependence studies was $0.2$ GeV $< k_T < 0.3$ GeV
- The rapidity bin used for transverse momentum dependence studies was $-1 < y_{\pi\pi} < 0$
- The model reproduces ATLAS data to 10% accuracy for the $k_T$ dependence of the HBT parameters
- The average number of participants is 1 for the proton and 19 for the Pb nucleus

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic calculations of HBT radii in p+Pb collisions by Bozek (2013) and others, and directly compares to recent ATLAS Collaboration data (Aaboud 2017). The study confirms that the hydrodynamic model can reproduce the space-time features of the emission source in high-energy p+Pb collisions, including the nonzero cross-term $R_{ol}$ predicted earlier by Bozek (2014) for asymmetric collisions. The results enable further refinement of initial condition models and support the interpretation that collective flow develops even in small collision systems.
