# A simple model for nuclear structure functions at small $x$ in the dipole picture

> A simple model for nuclear structure functions in the region of small x and small and moderate $Q^2$, is presented. It is a parameter-free extension, in the Glauber-Gribov approach to nuclear collisions, of a saturation model for the nucleon. A reasonable agreement with experimental data on ratios of nuclear structure functions is obtained. Nuclear effects in the longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratios are found to be small. Predictions of the model for values of $x$ smaller than those available to present experiments are given. The unintegrated gluon distribution and the behaviour of the saturation scale which result from this model are shown and discussed.

## Key claim

A parameter-free extension of the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff saturation model for the nucleon to nuclei, using the Glauber-Gribov approach, gives reasonable agreement with experimental nuclear structure function ratios for small x (≲ 0.01) and small-to-moderate Q² (≲ 20 GeV²), but the model is only a reasonable approximation for x ≳ 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ and does not include non-linear evolution effects or DGLAP evolution.

## Method

The nuclear structure function F₂A is computed in the dipole picture by integrating the dipole–nucleus cross section over the photon wave functions for transverse and longitudinal virtual photons. The dipole–nucleus cross section is obtained from the dipole–proton cross section of the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff model via the Glauber–Gribov formula, using a three-parameter Fermi nuclear density with no additional free parameters. The unintegrated gluon distribution is then derived from the dipole–nucleus cross section through a Bessel–Fourier transform.

## Key numbers

- For the 3-flavour version of the nucleon model: σ₀ = 23.03 mb, λ = 0.288, x₀ = 3.04·10⁻⁴ (with Q₀² = 1 GeV², m_u = m_d = m_s = 0.14 GeV).
- For the 4-flavour version: σ₀ = 29.12 mb, λ = 0.277, x₀ = 0.41·10⁻⁴ (with m_c = 1.5 GeV).
- Nuclear effects in the ratio σ_L/σ_T for nucleus over proton never exceed ±12% for C and Pb.
- The saturation scale for central Pb (b=0) is about 2.0 times that of the proton, not the naive factor of 5.9 expected from A¹/³.
- The model predicts Bjorken scaling is achieved at Q² ≈ 15 GeV² for x = 10⁻² and at Q² ≈ 300 GeV² for x = 10⁻⁶.
- The model is considered valid for 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶ < x < 10⁻² and Q² < 20 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff saturation model for the nucleon (2001) to nuclei using the Glauber–Gribov multiple-scattering formalism, providing a parameter-free prediction for nuclear structure functions at small x. It contrasts with DGLAP-based approaches (Eskola et al., 1999) that parametrize nuclear parton densities at a starting scale, and with high-density QCD approaches (Mueller, 2001; numerical solutions of non-linear evolution equations) that predict a steeper x-dependence of the saturation scale. The model enables predictions for unintegrated gluon distributions and saturation scales in nuclei, which can be used in k_T-factorization calculations for particle production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
