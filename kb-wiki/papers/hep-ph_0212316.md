# QCD Saturation and Deuteron-Nucleus Collisions

> We make quantitative predictions for the rapidity and centrality dependencies of hadron multiplicities in dA collisions at RHIC basing on the ideas of parton saturation in the Color Glass Condensate.

## Key claim

The authors predict that hadron multiplicities in deuteron-gold (d-Au) collisions at RHIC will exhibit a rapidity dependence that transitions from scaling with the number of participants from the deuteron at positive rapidities to scaling with the number of participants from the gold nucleus at negative rapidities, with the specific quantitative predictions depending on the saturation scales of both the proton and the nucleus; the abstract's claim of "quantitative predictions" is hedged by the authors' acknowledgment that the description of the proton structure "represents the main uncertainty in our calculations."

## Method

The authors use the KLN saturation model, based on the Color Glass Condensate framework, to compute hadron multiplicities in d-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV. They employ Glauber theory with a Woods-Saxon distribution for the gold nucleus and a Hulthen form for the deuteron wave function to determine the geometry, then apply a formula for inclusive gluon production that integrates over transverse momentum using unintegrated gluon distributions with two distinct saturation scales (one for the deuteron, one for the nucleus). The normalization constant is fixed by fitting to RHIC Au-Au data at 6% centrality, and the rapidity dependence of the saturation scales follows a power-law extracted from HERA data.

## Key numbers

- Total d-Au cross section: sigma_dAu = 2.26 +/- 0.1 bn at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, using NN inelastic cross section of 42 mb
- Saturation scale for gold nucleus: Q_s^2 = 1 to 2 GeV^2 depending on centrality in Au-Au collisions at RHIC
- Saturation scale for proton: Q_0^2(proton) ~ 0.3 GeV^2, derived from Q_s^2(Gold) scaled by participant ratio
- Power-law exponent for x-dependence of saturation scale: lambda = 0.288, from HERA data (Golec-Biernat and Wusthof, 1999)
- Normalization constant extracted from Au-Au data: Const(A-A) = 0.56, using dN_hadron/deta = 555 +/- 12(stat) +/- 35(syst) at eta=0 with N_part ~ 339 for 6% centrality
- Critical rapidity y_c ~ -3.5 to -4 where the two saturation scales become equal in d-Au collisions
- For minimum bias (0-100% centrality): average N_part^D = 1.37, average N_part^Au = 5.33, average N_coll = 7.09
- For most central (0-10% centrality): average N_part^D = 1.97, average N_part^Au = 11.24, average N_coll = 15.08

## Context

This work extends the KLN saturation model previously applied to Au-Au collisions (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001; Kharzeev and Levin, 2001) to deuteron-nucleus collisions, providing a crucial test of the Color Glass Condensate approach that is distinct from nucleus-nucleus data because d-A collisions involve two different saturation scales. The predictions are based on the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the Golec-Biernat and Wusthof (1999) parameterization of HERA data, and the authors note that the forthcoming d-Au run at RHIC will allow checking the "gluon liberation coefficient" calculated numerically on the lattice by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2002). The paper includes an erratum added in 2004 noting that while the predicted multiplicity distributions are consistent with subsequent experimental data within ~30%, discrepancies in the shape of rapidity distributions and centrality dependence arise primarily from differences between the optical Glauber model used in the paper and the Monte Carlo Glauber approach used by experimental collaborations.
