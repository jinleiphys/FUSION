# Event-by-Event Analysis of Proton-Induced Nuclear Multifragmentation: Determination of Phase Transition Universality-Class in System with Extreme Finite-Size Constraints

> A percolation model of nuclear fragmentation is used to interpret 10.2 GeV/c p+197Au multi-fragmentation data. Emphasis is put on finding signatures of a continuous nuclear matter phase transition in finite nuclear systems. Based on model calculations, corrections accounting for physical constraints of the fragment detection and sequential decay processes are derived. Strong circumstantial evidence for a continuous phase transition is found, and the values of two critical exponents, sigma = 0.5+-0.1 and tau = 2.35+-0.05, are extracted from the data. A critical temperature of T_c = 8.3+-0.2 MeV is found.

# Event-by-Event Analysis of Proton-Induced Nuclear Multifragmentation: Determination of Phase Transition Universality-Class in System with Extreme Finite-Size Constraints

## Key claim
Strong circumstantial evidence for a continuous (second-order) phase transition in nuclear matter is found from 10.2 GeV/c p+197Au multifragmentation data, but the claim is conditional on applying corrections for detector acceptance and sequential decay processes; without these corrections, the scaling collapse that supports the phase transition interpretation cannot be achieved.

## Method
A three-step percolation model was used: (1) energy deposition from the ISiS experimental data determined the bond-breaking probability via a relation between excitation energy and temperature (E* = a T^2 with a = A0/13); (2) the target nucleus was represented as a simple cubic lattice in an approximately spherical shape with nearest-neighbor bonds broken statistically independently; (3) sequential decay of excited pre-fragments was simulated using a code with eight decay modes (proton, neutron, deuteron, dineutron, diproton, triton, 3He, alpha) with Weisskopf decay weights. Detector acceptance effects were modeled with a filter software. Critical exponents sigma and tau and critical temperature Tc were extracted via a chi-squared optimization procedure that collapses scaled fragment yields onto a single straight line.

## Key numbers
- Critical exponent sigma = 0.5 +/- 0.1 extracted from the corrected experimental data
- Critical exponent tau = 2.35 +/- 0.05 extracted from the corrected experimental data
- Critical temperature Tc = 8.3 +/- 0.2 MeV extracted from the corrected experimental data
- For the unfiltered model calculation (without experimental corrections): sigma = 0.5 +/- 0.1, tau = 2.18 +/- 0.01, pc = 0.65
- Incident momentum of the proton beam: 10.2 GeV/c on a 197Au target

## Context
This work extends previous analyses by the ISiS collaboration (Beaulieu et al., 2001; Hsi et al., 1997) that had been interpreted using other phase transition models (SMM, EES, SIMON models) typically associated with a liquid-gas type phase transition. The percolation approach, originally introduced by Bauer et al. (1985), provides an alternative way to determine the order of the phase transition and the influence of finite size effects. Unlike prior analyses by Elliott et al. (2001) that neglected corrections for sequential decays and detector acceptance, this work applies both corrections and demonstrates that without them the scaling collapse fails. The extracted critical exponents are consistent with standard 3d percolation on infinite lattices (tau = 2.18, sigma = 0.45), though the authors note that pc is strongly affected by finite-size scaling corrections. This enables future event-by-event scaling analyses of nuclear multifragmentation data with proper accounting for experimental distortions.
