# The Isospin Dependence Of The Nuclear Equation Of State Near The Critical Point

> We discuss experimental evidence for a nuclear phase transition driven by the different concentration of neutrons to protons. Different ratios of the neutron to proton concentrations lead to different critical points for the phase transition. This is analogous to the phase transitions occurring in 4He-3He liquid mixtures. We present experimental results which reveal the N/A (or Z/A) dependence of the phase transition and discuss possible implications of these observations in terms of the Landau Free Energy description of critical phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper presents experimental evidence that the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is driven by the isospin concentration difference \(m = (N-Z)/A\) of fragments, with the data suggesting the existence of a line of first-order phase transitions terminating in a tricritical point, analogous to phase transitions in \(^{4}\)He-\(^{3}\)He liquid mixtures. The authors hedge that while the Landau free energy expansion to \(O(m^6)\) fits the data well, the precise determination of the nuclear equation of state near the critical point is not fully constrained by the present data.

## Method

Intermediate mass fragments (IMF) were detected using a Si detector telescope at 20 degrees from collisions of \(^{64,70}\)Zn and \(^{64}\)Ni beams at 40 A MeV on \(^{58,64}\)Ni, \(^{112,124}\)Sn, \(^{197}\)Au, and \(^{232}\)Th targets at the K-500 superconducting cyclotron at Texas A&M University. Yields of isotopes up to Z=18 were extracted using \(\Delta\)E-E techniques and moving source fits, then normalized to the \(^{12}\)C yield and analyzed using a modified Fisher model and a Landau free energy expansion in the order parameter \(m = (N-Z)/A\) to extract the free energy per particle and its dependence on the isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* The apparent temperature derived from fitting the normalized yield ratios to the ground state symmetry energy (25 MeV/A) is 6.0 MeV for the \(^{64}\)Ni + \(^{64}\)Ni system at 40 MeV/nucleon.
* For the \(^{64}\)Ni+\(^{124}\)Sn system at 40 MeV/nucleon, the mass distribution for I=0 fragments shows power law exponents of \(2.3 \pm 0.02\) for odd-odd nuclei and \(3.4 \pm 0.06\) for even-even nuclei.
* The linear fit of \(H/T\) versus \((I/A)_{cn}\) for all systems gives \(H/T = 0.47 + 1.6 (I/A)_{cn}\).
* The critical exponent \(\tau = 2.3\) is used in the modified Fisher model analysis.

## Context

This work extends the experimental evidence for a nuclear liquid-gas phase transition previously reported by the WCI collaboration and Bonasera (2000), and specifically addresses the role of isospin asymmetry as an order parameter, which had not been clearly delineated. The results are interpreted using the Landau free energy description of critical phenomena as applied by Huang (1987), and the analogy to superfluid \(^{4}\)He-\(^{3}\)He mixtures near the lambda point is drawn. The findings enable future studies to constrain the density and temperature dependence of the symmetry energy near the critical point and to explore the predicted line of first-order phase transitions and possible tricritical behavior in more neutron-rich or proton-rich systems.
