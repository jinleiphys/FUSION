# Electron scattering on ${\mathbf{A=3}}$ nuclei from quantum Monte Carlo based approaches

> We perform first-principle calculations of electron-nucleus scattering on $^3$He and $^3$H using the Green's function Monte Carlo method and two approaches based on the factorization of the final hadronic state: the spectral-function formalism and the short-time approximation. These three methods are benchmarked among each other and compared to the experimental data for the longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions of $^3$He, and the inclusive cross sections of both $^3$He and $^3$H. Since these three approaches are based on the same description of nuclear dynamics of the initial target state, comparing their results enables a precise quantification of the uncertainties inherent to factorization schemes. At sufficiently large values of the momentum transfer, we find an excellent agreement of the Green's function Monte Carlo calculation with experimental data and with both the spectral-function formalism and the short-time approximation. We also analyze the relevance of relativistic effects, whose inclusion becomes crucial to explain data at high momentum and energy transfer.

## Key claim

At sufficiently large momentum transfer, the Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculation agrees excellently with experimental data and with both the spectral-function (SF) formalism and the short-time approximation (STA), but the inclusion of relativistic effects becomes crucial to explain data at high momentum and energy transfer.

## Method

The authors performed first-principle calculations of electron-nucleus scattering on ^3He and ^3H using the Green's function Monte Carlo method, which solves the Schrodinger equation via imaginary-time propagation, and two factorization-based approaches: the spectral-function formalism, which approximates the final hadronic state as a plane wave times a spectator (A-1)-body system, and the short-time approximation, which evaluates real-time matrix elements for short times retaining two-nucleon final-state interactions. All three methods used the same Argonne v18 two-nucleon interaction and either Illinois-7 or Urbana IX three-nucleon forces for the initial target state, with one- and two-body electromagnetic currents including model-independent and model-dependent terms such as Delta-isobar contributions.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal and transverse electromagnetic response functions of ^3He were computed and compared to experimental data at momentum transfers up to approximately 1 GeV/c.
- The nonrelativistic scaling variable used a Fermi momentum of k_F = 180 MeV and binding parameter epsilon = 10 MeV for ^3H and 15 MeV for ^3He.
- The Argonne v18 potential has 40 parameters adjusted to fit the Nijmegen pn and pp scattering database of approximately 4300 data points in the range of 0-350 MeV.
- The STA does not reproduce correct threshold behavior at momentum transfer q less than or equal to 300 MeV/c.
- The GFMC method is currently limited to nuclei with A less than or equal to 12 nucleons.

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo calculations of inclusive electron scattering on ^4He and ^12C (Lovato 2016, Lovato 2017, Lovato 2020) to the A=3 nuclei ^3He and ^3H, benchmarking the factorization-based spectral-function formalism (Benhar 1994, Rocco 2015, Rocco 2018) and short-time approximation (Pastore 2019) against the virtually exact GFMC method. The comparison enables precise quantification of uncertainties inherent to factorization schemes and assesses the importance of relativistic effects, which is relevant for improving theoretical estimates of lepton-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino-oscillation experiments such as DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande.
