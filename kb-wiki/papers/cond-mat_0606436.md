# Entanglement Entropy beyond the Free Case

> We present a perturbative method to compute the ground state entanglement entropy for interacting systems. We apply it to a collective model of mutually interacting spins in a magnetic field. At the quantum critical point, the entanglement entropy scales logarithmically with the subsystem size, the system size, and the anisotropy parameter. We determine the corresponding scaling prefactors and evaluate the leading finite-size correction to the entropy. Our analytical predictions are in perfect agreement with numerical results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative method to compute ground-state entanglement entropy for interacting systems, applied to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model. At the quantum critical point, the entropy scales logarithmically with subsystem size, system size, and anisotropy parameter, with scaling prefactors that disagree with previous numerical studies (Latorre 2005) due to insufficient system sizes in those earlier works.

## Method

The authors map the LMG model of N mutually interacting spins onto two interacting bosonic modes using the Holstein-Primakoff representation. In the thermodynamic limit (N → ∞), the Hamiltonian becomes quadratic and exactly solvable, yielding the entanglement entropy E^(0). For finite-size corrections, they expand the Hamiltonian to order 1/N, obtaining a quartic term H^(1), then expand the reduced density matrix operator K = K^(0) + K^(1)/N and determine the coefficients by solving a system of six constraints derived from expectation values of bosonic operators, computed using a canonical transformation method.

## Key numbers

- At the quantum critical point h=1, the entanglement entropy scales as E ~ (1/6) ln N + (1/6) ln(1-γ) + (1/2) ln[τ(1-τ)], where τ = L/N is the subsystem fraction and γ is the anisotropy parameter
- Away from criticality, the leading finite-size correction to the entropy is E^(1) = -3(1-γ)^(1/2) / [8(h-1)^(3/2)] + O[(h-1)^(-1)]
- In the thermodynamic limit near criticality, E^(0) = -(1/4) ln|h-1| + (1/2) ln[τ(1-τ)] + (1/4) ln(1-γ) + 1 - x ln 2, where x=1 for h>1 and x=5/4 for h<1
- At h = √γ, the entropy E^(0) vanishes, indicating separable ground states

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entanglement in the LMG model (Vidal 2004, Dusuel 2004, Dusuel 2005) by providing the first analytical calculation of the entanglement entropy, which had only been studied numerically (Latorre 2005). The results contradict the numerical finding of a -1/6 ln|h-1| scaling, attributing the discrepancy to insufficient system sizes in the earlier work. The method enables analytical finite-size scaling analysis at the quantum critical point and can be extended to other collective models such as the Dicke model, where the predicted χ_N = 1/6 is consistent with numerical results (Lambert 2004).
