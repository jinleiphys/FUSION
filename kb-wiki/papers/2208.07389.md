# Bjorken sum rule with hyperasymptotic precision

> We obtain an improved determination of the normalization of the leading infrared renormalon of the Bjorken sum rule: $Z_B (n_f=3)= -0.407\pm 0.119 $. Estimates of higher order terms of the perturbative series are given. We compute the Bjorken sum rule with hyperasymptotic precision by including the leading terminant, associated with the first infrared renormalon. We fit the experimental data to the operator product expansion theoretical prediction with $\hat f_{3}^{\rm PV}$ as the free parameter. We obtain a good agreement with the experiment with $\hat f_{3}^{\rm PV}\times 10^3=32^{+187}_{-196}\;{\rm GeV}^{2}$ for $Q^2 \geq 1$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper achieves a hyperasymptotic determination of the Bjorken sum rule by including the leading terminant associated with the first infrared renormalon, but the extraction of the nonperturbative condensate \(\hat f_{3}^{\rm PV}\) from experimental data yields a result consistent with zero within large uncertainties, indicating that the data cannot yet resolve the leading power correction.

## Method

The authors compute the Bjorken sum rule with hyperasymptotic precision by splitting the Borel-summed perturbative series into a partial sum truncated at the minimal term plus the leading terminant, using the principal value (PV) prescription for the Borel integral. They determine the normalization of the leading infrared renormalon \(Z_B\) from the ratio of exact to asymptotic perturbative coefficients after conformal mapping, using NNNLO accuracy for the Bjorken and Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rules and NNLO for the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule. The theoretical prediction from the operator product expansion is then fitted to experimental data for the Bjorken sum rule with \(\hat f_{3}^{\rm PV}\) as the free parameter, including target-mass corrections via the Nachtmann variable.

## Key numbers

- Normalization of the leading infrared renormalon for the Bjorken sum rule with \(n_f=3\): \(Z_B = -0.407 \pm 0.119\)
- Fitted value of the nonperturbative condensate: \(\hat f_{3}^{\rm PV} \times 10^3 = 32^{+187}_{-196}\;{\rm GeV}^{2}\) for \(Q^2 \geq 1\) GeV\(^2\)
- Normalization of the leading infrared renormalon for the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule with \(n_f=3\): \(Z_{GLS} = -0.374 \pm 0.098\)
- Normalization of the leading infrared renormalon for the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule with \(n_f=3\): \(Z_{EJ} = -0.099 \pm 0.047\)
- Target-mass correction parameters used: \(d_2^{p-n} = 0.0080(36)\), \(a_2^{p-n} = 0.031(10)\)

## Context

This work extends the hyperasymptotic expansion method previously applied to other observables by Ayala and Pineda (2019, 2020) to the Bjorken sum rule, providing an improved determination of the leading infrared renormalon normalization compared to the earlier analysis by Campanario and Pineda (2005). The results enable a direct test of the operator product expansion in its nonperturbative form against experimental data without resorting to lattice simulations, though the current experimental precision at accessible \(Q^2\) values is insufficient to definitively isolate the leading power correction.
