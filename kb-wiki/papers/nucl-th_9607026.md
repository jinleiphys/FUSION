# Meson Exchange and Pion Rescattering Contributions to the Cross Section for $pp\to ppπ^0$

> Short range meson-exchange mechanisms such as $ρ\toωπ^0$ contribute significantly to the amplitude for $pp\rightarrow ppπ^0$ near threshold in addition to pion rescattering. The uncertainty of the latter contribution, which depends on poorly determined parameters, is estimated. Allowing for this uncertainty and taking the short range meson-exchange contributions into account makes it possible to reproduce the measured cross section.

## Key claim

Short-range meson-exchange mechanisms (particularly ρωπ⁰ exchange) contribute significantly to the pp→ppπ⁰ amplitude near threshold, and when combined with the uncertain pion-rescattering contribution (whose parameters are poorly determined), the measured cross section can be reproduced, but only within a large theoretical uncertainty that arises because the cross section is intrinsically very small.

## Method

The authors computed the pp→ppπ⁰ cross section near threshold by evaluating two-nucleon pion production amplitudes from several short-range meson-exchange diagrams: ρω exchange, ηa₀ exchange, σπ′ exchange, and intermediate N(1440) resonance excitation via σ and ω exchange. These were added to the standard impulse approximation, pion-rescattering (evaluated with two different sets of chiral perturbation theory parameters: O(q³) and O(q²)), and N̄N pair terms. The calculations used two different realistic NN potentials (Argonne V18 and Reid93) to estimate the uncertainty from nuclear short-range dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer in the reaction is ≈ 360 MeV/c.
- The ρω exchange coupling constant g_{πρω} ≈ -10.
- ρ-nucleon coupling: g_ρ²/4π = 1.2, κ_ρ = 6.1; ω-nucleon coupling: g_ω²/4π = 25, κ_ω = 0.
- The a₀(980) decay width Γ(a₀→πη) is taken as 175 MeV (mean of empirical range 50–300 MeV).
- For the ηa₀ exchange, assuming |r_{πηa₀}| ∼ 1 gives |r_{πηa₀} g_{πηa₀}| ∼ 0.21.
- For the σπ′ exchange, assuming |r_{πσπ′}| ∼ 1 gives |r_{πσπ′} g_{πσπ′}| ∼ 0.7.
- The π′NN coupling constant g_{π′NN}²/4π = 100; σNN coupling g_{σNN}²/4π = 8.38.
- The N(1440) scalar coupling g_σ* ≈ 1.1; ωNN* coupling g_ω* ≈ 1.7; πNN* pseudovector coupling f_π* = 0.62.
- The N(1440) enhances the scalar meson-exchange contribution by ∼ 25%.
- The O(q³) rescattering parameter C = −2.31; the O(q²) sub-threshold expansion gives C = −0.29.
- The cross section from the impulse plus O(q³) rescattering alone is too small by a factor 5–10.
- The integrand for pion rescattering peaks at about 1.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Cohen et al. (1996), which found that the impulse approximation and pion-rescattering nearly cancel, leaving a cross section too small by a factor 5–10. The authors add short-range meson-exchange mechanisms (ρω, ηa₀, σπ′) and N(1440) excitation, which were previously suggested by Lee and Riska (1993) and Horowitz et al. (1993) as relativistic two-nucleon operators. The results enable future chiral calculations to reduce uncertainties in πN scattering coefficients, NN wave functions, and mesonic coupling constants, and suggest that the ρω axial exchange current could help explain the nuclear enhancement of the axial charge observed in first-forbidden β-transitions (Warburton 1991, Warner 1992).
