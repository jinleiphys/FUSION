# Charged-current neutrino opacity within the relativistic Hartree-Fock framework for astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers

> Neutrinos and their weak interactions play a vital role in the physics of core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. Their description within astrophysical simulations, including the weak rates, is of pivotal importance not only for the prediction of accurate neutrino fluxes and spectra, including the associated conditions relevant to nucleosynthesis, neutrinos are also responsible for heating and cooling of the stellar plasma as well as the transport of lepton number and entropy. In the present article, we develop an essential improvement of the description of the underlying nuclear medium, necessary for the calculations of charged-current weak rates, with the inclusion of explicitly momentum-dependent nuclear interactions. To this end, we introduce the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach and the associated momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies. We discuss the resulting neutrino and antineutrino opacities and find large discrepancies comparing the weak rates at the RHF level with those of commonly used relativistic mean-field (RMF) models; in particular, we observe a substantial shift of previously reported large medium-dependent modifications associated with the RMF approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including explicitly momentum-dependent nuclear interactions via the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach leads to large discrepancies in charged-current neutrino and antineutrino opacities compared to the commonly used relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, with a substantial shift of previously reported large medium-dependent modifications associated with the RMF approach.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) framework for the nuclear medium, incorporating momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies decomposed into scalar, time-like, and vector components. They then computed charged-current neutrino absorption rates (opacities) by evaluating the contraction of lepton and hadron tensors within the RHF formalism, using effective (dressed) nucleon masses, momenta, and dispersion relations, and compared these results with those from the standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach.

## Key numbers

- The Cabibbo angle is taken as $\sin\theta_C=0.053$ for the charged-current processes considered.
- The axial-vector coupling constant is $g_A=1.27$, and the vector coupling constant is $g_V=1$.
- The pion mass is taken as $m_\pi=138$ MeV, consistent with the parametrization of the RHF nuclear model from Bouyssy (1987).
- The vector mass is $M_V=840$ MeV and the axial-vector mass is $M_A=1$ GeV.
- Neutron and proton magnetic moments are $\mathcal{Z}_p=2.793$ and $\mathcal{Z}_n=-1.913$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous developments by Reddy (1998) and Martinez-Pinedo (2012) and Fischer (2012), who treated charged-current weak rates at the mean-field level and applied them to self-consistent simulations of massive star explosions. The paper introduces the RHF approach to go beyond the mean-field treatment, enabling a more accurate description of the nuclear medium for neutrino opacities in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger simulations, and it enables future multi-dimensional simulations that require improved weak interaction rates.
