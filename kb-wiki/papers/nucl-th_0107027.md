# Regularized zero-range model and an application to the triton and the hypertriton

> We examine the regularized zero-range model in an application to three-fermion systems -- the triton and the hypertriton. We consider bound states and low-energy neutron-deuteron and lambda-deuteron scattering. The model is shown to provide an adequate quantitative description of these system on a par with finite-range potential models. The well known correlation between the doublet $nd$ scattering length and the triton binding energy (Phillips line) finds a natural explanation within the model.

## Key claim

The regularized zero-range model, using only two-body parameters (scattering length, effective range, and shape parameter) without an additional three-body force, can provide an adequate quantitative description of the triton and hypertriton bound states and low-energy neutron-deuteron and lambda-deuteron scattering, comparable to finite-range potential models. The Phillips correlation between the doublet nd scattering length and the triton binding energy arises naturally from the model because the adiabatic potentials form an effective one-parameter family.

## Method

The authors formulated the zero-range potential as a boundary condition on the wave-function at the origin, generalized to include effective range and shape parameter terms (up to k^4) to regularize the three-body problem and remove the Thomas collapse. They solved the three-body problem using the hyper-spheric adiabatic approximation, where the adiabatic eigenvalue λ(ρ) is obtained from the determinant of a 3×3 matrix derived from the boundary conditions, and the hyper-radial equation is solved numerically for bound states and scattering phase shifts. The model was applied to the nnp system (triton and nd scattering) and the Λnp system (hypertriton and Λd scattering), using three parameter sets for the nucleon-nucleon interactions and varied shape parameters for the Λ-nucleon interactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier coordinate-space regularization approach (Fedorov and Jensen, 2000) to three-fermion systems, providing a test against the triton and hypertriton. It contrasts with momentum-space regularization methods that require an additional three-body parameter (Adhikari and Amorim, 1997; Bedaque et al., 1999), as the present model uses only two-body parameters. The results are compared with finite-range potential models (Chen et al., 1989) and effective field theory (Bedaque and van Kolck, 1998), and the Phillips line is reproduced naturally. The work enables systematic study of three-body systems with contact interactions and establishes a Λd Phillips line for the hypertriton, which can guide future experimental and theoretical investigations of lambda-nucleon interactions.
