# ARIEL experiments and theory

> I present an overview of experiments at TRIUMF ARIEL and ISAC facilities covering both the current and the future envisioned programs. I also briefly review theory program at TRIUMF that relates to the ARIEL experimental program. I highlight several recent experimental results from the nuclear astrophysics, nuclear structure, fundamental symmetries, and the sterile neutrino search. Finally, I mention ongoing theoretical ab initio calculations of the proton capture on 7Li related to the X17 boson observation.

## Key claim

This overview paper describes the current and future experimental program at TRIUMF's ARIEL and ISAC facilities, along with the related theory program, but it is a conference summary rather than a presentation of a single new result; the abstract's mention of "highlights several recent experimental results" is qualified by the paper being an overview with no single unified claim.

## Method

The paper surveys the experimental stations at ISAC/ARIEL (DRAGON, TUDA, TIGRESS, GRIFFIN, IRIS, TITAN, TRINAT, Francium trapping) and summarizes recent results from each, including mass measurements of neutron-rich titanium and iron isotopes, elastic scattering of 10C on protons, Coulomb-excitation measurements of 23Mg and 23Na, a direct measurement of the 26mAl(p,γ) reaction, and the BeEST sterile neutrino search. It also describes the theory program at TRIUMF, focusing on ab initio No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) and Valence-Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (VS-IMSRG) methods, and presents preliminary NCSMC calculations of the 7Li(p,γ)8Be capture reaction and hypothetical X17 boson production.

## Key numbers

- TITAN mass measurements of 51-55Ti reduced uncertainties and established a weak shell effect at N=32; the range covered N=32 shell closure.
- High-precision mass measurements of neutron-rich Fe isotopes at TITAN used the newly commissioned MR-ToF-MS; these benchmarked a maximum of deformation in the N=40 island of inversion in Fe.
- The 10C(p,p)10Cgs differential cross section was measured at Ec.m.=4.15 MeV; the shape was reproduced only with the chiral NN+3N interaction that simultaneously reproduces the 11Be parity inversion.
- Coulomb-excitation measurements of 23Mg and 23Na improved the precision of the extracted E2 strength by a factor of six.
- The 26mAl(p,γ) reaction was directly measured using the DRAGON spectrometer; this is the first direct measurement of an astrophysical reaction using a radioactive beam of isomeric nuclei.
- The BeEST experiment improved limits on sterile neutrinos in the 100–850 keV mass range.
- The proposed TRISR storage ring would operate at E=0.1–2 AMeV with a 15m x 15m footprint; the favoured neutron generator has an unmoderated flux up to 5×10^13 n/s.
- Preliminary NCSMC calculations for 7Li(p,γ)8Be show the γ capture cross section compared to data from Zahnow (1995); the two peaks are dominated by M1 contributions from 1+ resonances in 8Be.
- The hypothetical X17 boson mass is ~17 MeV; cross sections for X17 emission are lower by several orders of magnitude than the γ capture cross section.

## Context

This paper extends the description of the TRIUMF experimental program from earlier facility overviews (ISAC_Dilling_2014, ARIEL_Dilling_2014) and summarizes recent results that test ab initio nuclear theory, including VS-IMSRG calculations for titanium and iron isotopes (PhysRevLett.120.062503, PhysRevC.105.L041301) and NCSMC calculations for 10C scattering (PhysRevLett.118.262502). The work enables future searches for the X17 boson via the DarkLight experiment at the ARIEL e-linac and the proposed TRISR storage ring for neutron capture measurements on radioactive nuclei, which would address open questions in r-process nucleosynthesis modelling.
