# An application of the 3-dimensional q-deformed harmonic oscillator to the nuclear shell model

> An analysis of the construction of a q-deformed version of the 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator, which is based on the application of q-deformed algebras, is presented. The results together with their applicability to the shell model are compared with the predictions of the modified harmonic oscillator.

# An application of the 3-dimensional q-deformed harmonic oscillator to the nuclear shell model

## Key claim
The q-deformed 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian, constructed from so_q(3) algebra, reproduces the energy spectrum of the modified harmonic oscillator (MHO) without spin-orbit term to leading order in the deformation parameter τ, but with the advantage that the q-deformed model uses the same parameters for all shells whereas the MHO requires shell-dependent parameters.

## Method
The authors constructed a q-deformed Hamiltonian for the 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator using q-deformed boson operators and the so_q(3) algebra, with the angular momentum operators expressed as irreducible tensor operators. They derived the eigenvalues of this Hamiltonian and expanded them in powers of the deformation parameter τ (where q = e^τ), then compared the resulting spectrum to that of the Nilsson modified harmonic oscillator. A q-deformed spin-orbit term was also introduced using a scalar product of q-deformed orbital and spin tensor operators.

## Key numbers
- For the q-deformed harmonic oscillator fit to MHO (with ℏω=1 for MHO and spin-orbit neglected): ℏω₀ = 0.94109 and τ = 0.021948
- The MHO parameter μ′ in the ²⁰⁸Pb region: μ′ = 0 for N=2, μ′ = 0.0263 for N=3, μ′ = 0.024 for N ≥ 4
- The MHO parameter k in the ²⁰⁸Pb region: k = 0.08 for N=2, k = 0.075 for N=3, k = 0.07 for N ≥ 4
- The q-deformed energy eigenvalue expansion to order τ²: ᵠE₃dim = ℏω₀ n − ℏω₀ τ (ℓ(ℓ+1) − n(n+1)) − ℏω₀ τ² (ℓ(ℓ+1) − (1/3)n(n+1)(2n+1)) + O(τ³)

## Context
This work extends the application of quantum algebras to nuclear structure, following earlier use of su_q(2) for rotational bands in deformed and superdeformed nuclei (Raychev et al., various years) and applications to molecular spectra. The paper directly compares with the Nilsson modified harmonic oscillator (Nilsson, 1955; Gustafson et al., 1967; Bengtsson and Ragnarsson, 1985; Nazarewicz and Riley, 1995) and shows that the q-deformed model naturally incorporates the shell-dependent effects that the MHO handles through variable parameters. This enables future application of the q-deformed oscillator to real nuclei, which the authors state will be addressed in a subsequent paper.
