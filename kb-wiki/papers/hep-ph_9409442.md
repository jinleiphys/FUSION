# What is a low-energy theorem ?

> We discuss the meaning of low-energy theorems (LETs) in the framework of the effective field theory of the standard model. Particular emphasis is put on the LET for neutral pion photoproduction off nucleons at threshold. The seemingly controversial situation surrounding this LET is clarified.

## Key claim

Low-energy theorems (LETs) are general predictions of chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) to a given order \( \mathcal{O}(p^n) \), meaning they are strict consequences of the standard model depending on low-energy constants like \( F_\pi, m, g_A, \kappa_p \) but without any model assumption for these parameters; the paper clarifies that the seemingly controversial LET for neutral pion photoproduction at threshold is actually a correct LET of \( \mathcal{O}(p^3) \), but earlier derivations omitted finite one-loop contributions that are required by the standard model.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), an effective field theory of the standard model, to systematically expand amplitudes in powers of small momenta and meson masses (the chiral expansion). They apply chiral power counting to determine the chiral dimension \( D \) of Feynman diagrams, then compute the electric dipole amplitude \( E_{0+} \) for \( \gamma N \to \pi^0 N \) at threshold order by order in \( M_\pi \), including tree diagrams and one-loop contributions. The derivation is contrasted with older current-algebra and PCAC methods that relied on off-shell extrapolations or unjustified analyticity assumptions.

## Key numbers

- The LET for the \( I=0 \) \( S \)-wave \( \pi\pi \) scattering length at leading order: \( a_0^0 = 7 M_\pi^2 / (32 \pi F_\pi^2) = 0.16 \), compared to the empirical value \( 0.26 \pm 0.05 \) (Petrov, year not given).
- The LET for \( a_0^0 \) to order \( p^4 \): \( a_0^0 = 0.20 \pm 0.01 \), including terms with the scalar radius of the pion \( \langle r^2 \rangle_S^\pi \simeq 0.6 \) fm\(^2\) and \( D \)-wave scattering lengths.
- The LET for neutral pion photoproduction on the proton at threshold to \( \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^2) \): \( E_{0+}(\pi^0 p) = -\frac{e g_A}{8\pi F_\pi} \left[ \frac{M_\pi}{m} - \frac{M_\pi^2}{2 m^2} (3+\kappa_p) - \frac{M_\pi^2}{16 F_\pi^2} + \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^3) \right] \), where \( \kappa_p = 1.793 \) is the proton anomalous magnetic moment.
- The LET for neutral pion photoproduction on the neutron at threshold to \( \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^2) \): \( E_{0+}(\pi^0 n) = -\frac{e g_A}{8\pi F_\pi} \left[ \frac{M_\pi^2}{2 m^2} \kappa_n - \frac{M_\pi^2}{16 F_\pi^2} + \mathcal{O}(M_\pi^3) \right] \), where \( \kappa_n \) is the neutron anomalous magnetic moment.
- The leading contribution to the sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon from Compton scattering at \( \mathcal{O}(p^3) \): \( f_1(\omega/M_\pi) = -11 g_A^2 \omega / (192 \pi F_\pi^2 M_\pi) + \mathcal{O}(\omega^2) \).

## Context

This work extends the modern effective field theory approach to low-energy hadronic processes, replacing older current-algebra and PCAC methods that were prone to errors from off-shell extrapolations and unjustified analyticity assumptions. The paper clarifies the status of the LET for neutral pion photoproduction at threshold, which had been debated following experimental findings (Saclay, 1991; Beck, 1991) that seemed to violate the original LET derived by Vainshtein and Zakharov (1970) and de Baenst (1970). The corrected LET, including finite one-loop contributions first computed by Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991), resolves the controversy and provides a systematic framework for computing higher-order corrections, though the authors note that the slow convergence of the \( M_\pi \) expansion and isospin-violating corrections make this process a poor test of the standard model.
