# Analytical Form of the Deuteron Wave Function Calculated within the Dispersion Approach

> We present a convenient analytical parametrization of the deuteron wave function calculated within dispersion approach as a discrete superposition of Yukawa-type functions, in both configuration and momentum spaces.

## Key claim

The paper provides a convenient analytical parametrization of the deuteron wave function calculated within the dispersion (potentialless inverse scattering) approach, but the parametrization is an approximation with quantified accuracy rather than an exact representation.

## Method

The authors express the deuteron S-wave and D-wave functions in configuration space as discrete superpositions of Yukawa-type exponential terms, with the D-wave terms including additional polynomial factors in 1/r. The coefficients are determined by fitting to the original MT (Muzafarov-Troitsky) wave functions, which were themselves calculated directly from experimental NN scattering phases and the mixing parameter in the 3S1-3D1 channel using dispersion relations, without any adjustable parameters. The corresponding momentum-space wave functions are obtained via Fourier transformation of the configuration-space parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic D/S state ratio from the fit is η = 0.02531511
- The parameter α = √(M ε_d) = 0.231625 fm⁻¹, where M is the nucleon mass and ε_d is the deuteron binding energy
- The accuracy of the S-wave parametrization: {∫₀^∞ dr [u(r) - u_a(r)]²}¹/² = 4.1 × 10⁻³
- The accuracy of the D-wave parametrization: {∫₀^∞ dr [w(r) - w_a(r)]²}¹/² = 2.2 × 10⁻³
- The fit uses n_u = 16 terms for the S-wave and n_w = 12 terms for the D-wave, with coefficients listed in Table 1 (e.g., C₁ = 0.87872995 fm⁻¹/², D₁ = 0.022245143 fm⁻¹/²)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-approach deuteron wave functions of Muzafarov and Troitsky (1981) by providing an algebraic parametrization suitable for practical calculations, following the same Yukata-superposition form previously used for the Paris potential by Lacombe et al. (1981) and for the CD-Bonn wave function by Machleidt (2001). The parametrization enables easier application of these "almost model independent" wave functions, which have been shown to give the best description of the deuteron tensor polarization T₂₀(Q²) compared to conventional nuclear model wave functions, and have been used in calculations of the neutron charge form factor.
