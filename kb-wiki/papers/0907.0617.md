# pi+Xi- Correlations and the $Ξ^{*}(1530)$ Puzzle

> The analysis of the preliminary RHIC data on $π^{+}-Ξ^{-}$ correlation function is carried out.The $Ξ^*(1530)$ resonance is successfully described. The values of the fireball radius and of the $π^{+}-Ξ^{-}$ scattering lengths are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Xi^*(1530)$ resonance can be reasonably described in $\pi^{+}-\Xi^{-}$ correlation functions using a simple Gaussian source model, but the model still somewhat overestimates the experimental peak in the resonance region, with the overestimate expected to decrease when a more realistic source function from Blast-wave simulations is used.

## Method

The authors model the $\pi^{+}-\Xi^{-}$ correlation function using a Gaussian source function in the pair rest frame and a multichannel wave function that includes Coulomb and strong final-state interactions, the $\Xi^*(1530)$ resonance in the $P$-wave with $J=3/2$, $I=1/2$, and $S$-wave phase shifts expressed through scattering lengths $a_{1/2}$ and $a_{3/2}$ for isospin $I=1/2$ and $I=3/2$. The calculation accounts for the coupled $\pi^{0}\Xi^{0}$ channel, spin structure including spin-flip, and the inner region $r<\epsilon \sim 1$ fm where the strong potential is assumed to vanish. The resulting correlation function is compared with preliminary STAR data from 10% most central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Fireball radius $R = 7.0$ fm used in the calculation that best describes the data (consistent with preliminary STAR value $R = (6.7 \pm 1.0)$ fm)
- Preliminary STAR shift parameter $\Delta_{out} = (-5.6 \pm 1.0)$ fm, indicating the $\Xi$ emission point is more to the outside of the fireball than the pion emission point
- Bohr radius of the $\pi^{+}\Xi^{-}$ system: $a_1 = -214$ fm (negative sign due to Coulomb repulsion)
- The $\Xi^*(1530)$ resonance decay widths: $\Gamma_1 \doteq 2\Gamma/3$ for the $\pi^{+}\Xi^{-}$ channel and $\Gamma_2 \doteq \Gamma/3$ for the $\pi^{0}\Xi^{0}$ channel

## Context

This work extends femtoscopy studies of non-identical hadron correlations, previously focused on identical pions and other systems at AGS, SPS, and RHIC accelerators (E895, NA49, CERES, STAR collaborations), to the exotic $\pi-\Xi$ system with one order of magnitude mass difference and $\Delta B=1/\Delta S=2$ quantum number gap. The analysis addresses the puzzle that the FSI model of Pratt and Petriconi over-predicted the $\Xi^*(1530)$ peak, and the present calculation brings the theoretical prediction closer to the experimental data. The results enable extraction of the space-time interval between different stages of fireball evolution, since multistrange baryons are expected to decouple earlier due to small hadronic cross-sections (Bass).
