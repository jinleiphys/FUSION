# Reliability of Taylor expansions in QCD

> We investigate the reliability of the Taylor expansion method in QCD with isospin chemical potentials using lattice simulations. By comparing the expansion of the number density to direct results, the range of validity of the leading- and next-to-leading order expansions is determined. We also elaborate on the convergence properties of the Taylor series by comparing the leading estimate for the radius of convergence to the position of the nearest singularity, i.e. the onset of pion condensation. Our results provide a handle for quantifying the uncertainties of Taylor expansions in baryon chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The Taylor expansion method for QCD at finite isospin chemical potential is reliable up to specific values of $\mu_I/m_\pi$ that depend on temperature and expansion order, but the naive expectation that contours of constant deviation follow lines of constant $\mu/T$ is not borne out by the data, especially for small deviations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of rooted staggered quarks at physical quark masses, using tree-level Symanzik improved gluon action and stout smearing, on ensembles with $N_t = 6, 8, 10, 12$ corresponding to lattice spacings $a = 0.20, 0.15, 0.12, 0.10$ fm. They computed the isospin density $n_I$ directly at finite $\mu_I$ and compared it to leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) Taylor expansions in $\mu_I/T$, using Taylor coefficients $c_2$ and $c_4$ from Borsanyi (2011). The deviation $\Delta = |n_I - n_I^{\rm LO/NLO}|$ was mapped across the $(T, \mu_I)$ plane and extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

* For the LO expansion, the contour $\Delta = 0.2\,m_\pi^3$ (corresponding to $3-6\%$ deviation in isospin density) gives reliability up to $\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.5$ to $0.6$.
* For the NLO expansion, the same $\Delta = 0.2\,m_\pi^3$ contour gives reliability up to $\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 1.0$ to $1.5$.
* The BEC phase boundary lies at $\mu_{I,c} \approx m_\pi/2$ for temperatures up to about $150$ MeV.
* The leading estimator $r_2(\chi_I)$ from the susceptibility lies surprisingly close to the BEC phase boundary at low temperatures, while $r_2(n_I)$ and $r_2(p)$ significantly overestimate the radius of convergence.
* The continuum extrapolation of the $\Delta^{\rm NLO}/m_\pi^3 = 0.61$ contour was performed for $T \geq 161$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the continuum phase diagram of QCD at finite isospin chemical potential from Brandt (2017) by testing the Taylor expansion method against direct lattice simulations. It provides a controlled testbed for the Taylor expansion approach widely used in baryon chemical potential studies (Gottlieb 1988, Gavai 2001, Allton 2002, Bazavov 2017, Borsanyi 2018), where the complex action problem prevents direct simulations. The results enable quantification of systematic uncertainties in Taylor expansions for baryon chemical potentials and guide interpretation of radius-of-convergence estimates used to search for a possible critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
