# Nuclear parton distributions and deviations from DGLAP at an Electron Ion Collider

> We explore the potential of an Electron Ion Collider to determine nuclear modifications of PDFs. We find that gluon shadowing can be accurately measured down to x=0.001, and discuss the possibility of detecting non--linear QCD effects with inclusive measurements.

## Key claim

The paper explores the potential of an Electron Ion Collider (EIC) to determine nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions (PDFs), finding that gluon shadowing can be accurately measured down to x=0.001, but this precision is conditional on the full energy stage II scenario; the possibility of detecting non-linear QCD effects with inclusive measurements is discussed but remains preliminary and requires further quantitative study.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo-data for inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) cross sections on Lead (208Pb) using the NNPDF2.0 proton PDF set, assuming an integrated luminosity of 4 fb-1 and a 2% systematic error, with nuclear effects modeled via K-factors that interpolate between the IP Non-sat dipole model at small x, a linear transition region, and the EPS09 nuclear structure function ratio at large x. They performed next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD fits within the NNPDF framework assuming collinear factorization, using kinematic cuts of Q2 >= 2 GeV2 and W2 >= 12.5 GeV2 to ensure DGLAP evolution validity, and compared results for a medium energy EIC (stage I, sqrt(s)=12-44 GeV) and a full energy EIC (stage II, sqrt(s)=63-124 GeV). To study deviations from DGLAP, they applied a kinematic cut Q2 < Qc2 x-1/3 with Qc2 scaled by APb1/3 ~ 6 relative to the proton case, following the strategy of Caola (2009, 2010).

## Key numbers

* Gluon shadowing in 208Pb can be determined down to x ~ 10-3 in stage II and down to x ~ 10-2 in stage I, at the initial evolution scale Q02 = 2 GeV2.
* The kinematic cuts applied for DGLAP validity were Q2 >= 2 GeV2 and W2 >= 12.5 GeV2.
* The saturation scale scaling argument uses APb1/3 ~ 6 to rescale the proton Qc2 range of 0.5-1.5 GeV2 to the nuclear case, giving Qc2 ~ 9 GeV2 for 208Pb.
* An integrated luminosity of 4 fb-1 was assumed for all energies in the pseudo-data generation.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of global PDF fits (NNPDF2.0 by Ball, 2010) and nuclear PDF parametrizations (EPS09 by Eskola, 2009) to assess the capabilities of a future EIC, specifically targeting the poorly known gluon nuclear modifications. It builds on the strategy of Caola (2009, 2010) for detecting deviations from DGLAP evolution, originally applied to proton HERA data, and adapts it to nuclear targets by scaling the saturation scale with atomic number A. The results enable future detailed studies of non-linear QCD effects such as gluon saturation and small-x resummation in heavy nuclei, and demonstrate that EIC data can determine nuclear gluon PDFs with precision comparable to global proton fits.
