# Finite-volume Hamiltonian method for $ππ$ scattering in lattice QCD

> Within a formulation of $ππ$ scattering, we investigate the use of the finite-volume Hamiltonian approach to resolving scattering observables from lattice QCD spectra. We consider spectra in the centre-of-mass and moving frames for both S- and P-wave cases. Furthermore, we investigate the multi-channel case. Here we study the use of the Hamiltonian framework as a parametrization that can be fit directly to lattice spectra. Through this method, the hadron properties, such as mass, width and coupling, can be directly extracted from the lattice spectra.

# Finite-volume Hamiltonian method for ππ scattering in lattice QCD

## Key claim
The finite-volume Hamiltonian formalism provides a parametrization that can be fit directly to lattice QCD spectra, enabling extraction of hadron properties such as mass, width, and coupling, while remaining equivalent to the established Lüscher method for single-channel cases and offering a more tractable approach for multi-channel systems.

## Method
The authors construct a Hamiltonian with non-interacting and interacting parts, where the interacting part includes vertex interactions (g) and two-body potentials (v). The Hamiltonian matrix is discretized in a finite volume by replacing continuum momenta with discrete momenta k_n = (2π/L) n for n in Z^3, and eigenvalues are solved from det[EI - H] = 0. The method is extended to moving frames (non-zero total momentum P) and P-wave scattering, where rotational symmetry of the moving box follows irreducible representations of the little group rather than SO(3).

## Key numbers
- For the ρ meson pole position extracted from recent lattice QCD data: m - iΓ/2 = 840.5 - i5.0 MeV
- Breit-Wigner parameters from the same fit: m = 854.1 MeV and Γ = 12.1 MeV
- The difference between pole position and Breit-Wigner parameters is 14 MeV in mass and 2 MeV in width
- The Hamiltonian spectra and Lüscher equation spectra are consistent for lattice sizes L > 3 fm

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume Hamiltonian formalism previously developed by Hall and Wu to ππ scattering, establishing equivalence with the Lüscher method (Lüscher 1986, 1991) for single-channel and coupled-channel systems. The method addresses limitations of the Lüscher approach in multi-channel cases, where three asymptotic scattering parameters (two phase shifts and one inelasticity) correspond to a single energy eigenstate at a given volume, requiring near-coincident eigenstates at three different volumes or momentum boosts (Guo). The formalism enables direct extraction of resonance properties from lattice QCD spectra, demonstrated here by analyzing JLab lattice data for the ρ meson, and provides a competitive alternative to the Lüscher formalism for future applications to various hadronic spectra.
