# Rapidity distribution as a probe for elliptical flow at intermediate energies

> Interplay between the spectator and participant matter in heavy-ion collisions is investigated within isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model in term of rapidity distribution of light charged particles. The effect of different types and size rapidity distributions is studied in elliptical flow. The elliptical flow patterns show important role of the nearby spectator matter on the participant zone. This role is further explained on the basis of passing time of the spectator and expansion time of the participant zone. The transition from the in-plane to out-of-plane is observed only when the mid-rapidity region is included in the rapidity bin, otherwise no transition occurs. The transition energy is found to be highly sensitive towards the size of the rapidity bin, while weakly on the type of the rapidity distribution. The theoretical results are also compared with the experimental findings and are found in good agreement.

## Key claim

The transition from in-plane to out-of-plane elliptical flow in heavy-ion collisions occurs only when the mid-rapidity region (|Y_red| less than 0.1) is included in the rapidity bin; if the mid-rapidity region is excluded, no transition is observed and the elliptical flow remains positive (in-plane) at all incident energies.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of semi-central Au+Au collisions at 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a hard equation of state and standard energy-dependent cross-sections. They analyzed the rapidity distribution of light charged particles (LCPs, with mass number A between 1 and 4) by dividing the reduced rapidity Y_red = Y_cm/Y_beam into five different zones, either including or excluding the mid-rapidity region (|Y_red| less than 0.1), and computed the elliptical flow parameter v_2 as a function of incident energy for each bin. The phase space was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method with a binding distance of 4 fm at a simulation time of 200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- The transition from in-plane to out-of-plane elliptical flow occurs around 100 MeV/nucleon, and maximum squeeze-out (most negative v_2) is observed around 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The passing time of the spectator decreases from 30 to 16 fm/c as the incident energy increases from 400 MeV/nucleon to 1.49 GeV/nucleon.
- No transition from in-plane to out-of-plane emission is observed for rapidity bins extending beyond |Y_red| less than or equal to 1.1.
- The rapidity distribution is studied over the range |Y_red| less than or equal to 1.75.
- The transition energy increases with the size of the rapidity bin for light charged particles.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of elliptical flow by the FOPI, INDRA, and PLASTIC BALL collaborations (Lukasik 2005, Andronic 2005, Lukasik 2004) and theoretical work by Danielewicz (2000) on the interplay between participant and spectator matter. The authors build on their own prior work (Sood 2009) using rapidity distributions to study participant-spectator contributions, and on studies of balance energy in directed flow (Sood 2006). The results enable a more precise determination of the transition energy in elliptical flow by showing its sensitivity to the choice of rapidity bin, and suggest that future comparisons with experimental data should use rapidity bins between |Y_red| less than or equal to 0.1 and |Y_red| less than or equal to 0.3 for best agreement.
