# Light Scalar Mesons in Photon-Photon Collisions

> The light scalar mesons, discovered over forty years ago, became a challenge for the naive quark-antiquark model from the outset. At present the nontrivial nature of these states is no longer denied practically anybody. Two-photon physics has made a substantial contribution to understanding the nature of the light scalar mesons. Recently, it entered a new stage of high statistics measurements. We review the results concerning two-photon production mechanisms of the light scalars, based on the analysis of current experimental data.

## Key claim

The two-photon decays of the light scalar mesons σ(600), f₀(980), and a₀(980) are four-quark transitions caused by rescattering mechanisms (σ → π⁺π⁻ → γγ, f₀(980) → (K⁺K⁻ + π⁺π⁻) → γγ, and a₀(980) → (K\bar{K} + π⁰η + π⁰η′) → γγ), in contrast to the direct two-quark transitions that dominate the two-photon decays of classic P-wave tensor q\bar{q} mesons; the direct coupling constants of these scalar resonances with the γγ system are small.

## Method

The authors review and analyze high-statistics Belle data on the reactions γγ → π⁺π⁻, γγ → π⁰π⁰, and γγ → π⁰η, using a theoretical framework based on four-quark (q²\bar{q}²) models and kaon-loop mechanisms for scalar resonance production. They compare these results with earlier experimental data from Crystal Ball, JADE, MARK II, and other collaborations, and contrast the predictions of the four-quark model with those of the naive quark-antiquark model and the K\bar{K} molecule model.

## Key numbers

- Average two-photon width of f₀(980) over resonance mass distributions: ⟨Γ_{f₀→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.19 keV
- Average two-photon width of a₀(980) over resonance mass distributions: ⟨Γ_{a₀→γγ}⟩_{πη} ≈ 0.4 keV
- Average two-photon width of σ(600) over resonance mass distributions: ⟨Γ_{σ→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.45 keV
- Belle result for Γ_{a₀→γγ}B(a₀ → π⁰η) = (0.128^{+0.003+0.502}_{-0.002-0.043}) keV
- PDG (2010) average for Γ_{a₀→γγ}B(a₀ → π⁰η) = (0.21^{+0.8}_{-0.4}) keV
- Crystal Ball (1986) result: Γ_{a₀→γγ}B(a₀ → π⁰η) = (0.19 ± 0.07^{+0.10}_{-0.07}) keV
- JADE (1990) result: Γ_{a₀→γγ}B(a₀ → π⁰η) = (0.28 ± 0.04 ± 0.10) keV
- Four-quark model estimate (1982): Γ_{a₀→γγ} ∼ Γ_{f₀→γγ} ∼ 0.27 keV
- K\bar{K} molecule model estimate: Γ_{a₀(K\bar{K})→γγ} = Γ_{f₀(K\bar{K})→γγ} ≈ 0.6 keV
- Two-photon widths of tensor mesons for comparison: Γ_{f₂→γγ} ≈ 2.6–3 keV, Γ_{a₂→γγ} ≈ 1 keV

## Context

This review extends the long-standing investigation of light scalar mesons that began with the discovery of a₀(980) and f₀(980) in the 1970s and the subsequent four-quark model proposal by Jaffe (1977). The work directly builds on the predictions of Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov (1982) that four-quark scalar mesons would have suppressed two-photon production rates, and on the kaon-loop model for φ radiative decays developed by Achasov and Ivanchenko (1989). The analysis of Belle data (Mori et al., 2007; Uehara et al., 2008, 2009) provides high-statistics confirmation of these predictions and enables future studies of scalar mesons in γγ* collisions and near K\bar{K} thresholds.
