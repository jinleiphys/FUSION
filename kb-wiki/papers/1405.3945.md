# Quartic oscillator potential in the γ-rigid regime of the collective geometrical model

> A prolate $γ$-rigid version of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a quartic anharmonic oscillator potential in $β$ collective shape variable is used to describe the spectra for a variety of vibrational-like nuclei. Speculating the exact separation between the two Euler angles and the $β$ variable, one arrives to a differential Schrödinger equation with a quartic anharmonic oscillator potential and a centrifugal-like barrier. The corresponding eigenvalue is approximated by an analytical formula depending only on a single parameter up to an overall scaling factor. The applicability of the model is discussed in connection to the existence interval of the free parameter which is limited by the accuracy of the approximation and by comparison to the predictions of the related $X(3)$ and $X(3)$-$β^{2}$ models. The model is applied to qualitatively describe the spectra for nine nuclei which exhibit near vibrational features.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical energy formula for a prolate γ-rigid Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with a quartic anharmonic oscillator potential in β, but the formula is based on a higher-order JWKB approximation and is reliable only for a limited interval of the free parameter λ, specifically from 0 to a cutoff value of 10.9802, where the ratio R₄/₂ ranges continuously from 2.286 down to 2.0.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian in the γ-rigid, axially symmetric prolate (γ=0) limit, which reduces the problem to three degrees of freedom: two Euler angles and the β shape variable. After separating angular variables, the radial Schrödinger equation in β contains a quartic anharmonic oscillator potential u(β) = (1/2)α₁β² + α₂β⁴ and a centrifugal term I(I+1)/(3β²). Using the scaling property of the quartic oscillator, the eigenvalue problem is reduced to a single parameter λ = α₁α₂⁻²/³, and the energies are approximated by an analytical formula derived from an eighth-order JWKB quantization condition.

## Key numbers

* The pure quartic oscillator case (λ=0) yields a maximum ground-band ratio R₄/₂ = 2.286.
* The upper limit of the free parameter is set at λ_cutoff = 10.9802, which corresponds to R₄/₂ = 2.0.
* A turning point in the ground-band energy evolution is identified at λ_c = 8.75, where R₄/₂ = 2.135.
* For the benchmark potential u(r) = (1/2)r² + 0.025 r⁴ (λ = 11.6961), the JWKB approximation overestimates the exact ground-state eigenvalue by 4.3%.
* The X(3)-β² model gives R₄/₂ = 2.13 and E(0⁺₂)/E(2⁺₁) = 2.
* For the nine nuclei studied, the extracted λ values range from 1.705 (¹³⁰Xe) to 10.949 (¹¹⁶Te), with deformation parameters β₂ from 0.012 (²²⁰Th) to 0.257 (¹¹⁶Te).

## Context

This work extends the γ-rigid collective model framework previously applied to the X(3) model (infinite square well potential) and the X(3)-β² model (harmonic oscillator) by introducing a quartic anharmonic oscillator potential. It provides an analytical bridge between the X(3)-β² and X(3)-β⁴ limiting cases, with continuous interpolation via the single parameter λ. The model is applied to nine vibrational-like nuclei and compared with the E(5)-β⁴ model predictions for ¹⁰⁰Pd, showing comparable agreement despite different underlying spaces of shape and angular degrees of freedom. Future work could extend the approach to γ-rigid nuclei with γ≠0 (as in the Z(4) model of Bonatsos) or to the γ-unstable case.
