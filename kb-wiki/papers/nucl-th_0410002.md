# Pionic atoms probing pi-NN resonances

> The pion optical potential generated by the hypothetical pi-NN-coupled NN-decoupled dibaryon resonance d'(2065) is calculated to the lowest order in nuclear matter density. The contribution to the pion optical potential is found to be within the empirical errors, so the d'(2065) existence currently does not contradict to the observed properties of the pi-nucleus bound states. Future progress in the pionic X-ray spectroscopy can reveal contributions of pi-NN resonances to energy levels and widths of the pionic atoms.

# Pionic atoms probing pi-NN resonances

## Key claim

The hypothetical dibaryon resonance d'(2065) contributes to the pion optical potential in nuclear matter at a level that falls within the current empirical errors of pionic atom data, meaning its existence is not ruled out by existing measurements. However, the calculation is performed only to lowest order in nuclear matter density, and the in-medium width of the d' is estimated to be 10 MeV (20 times the vacuum width), which formally goes beyond that lowest-order treatment.

## Method

The authors calculated the contribution of the hypothetical d'(2065) dibaryon to the pion optical potential in symmetric nuclear matter using a second-order diagram involving two proton holes and a pion coupled through the d' resonance. The calculation used the vacuum d' mass of 2065 MeV, width of 0.5 MeV, and pi-NN-d' coupling constant g = 9.2 GeV^{-1}, with a Jost function in the effective-range approximation to account for proton-proton final-state interactions. The resulting s-wave and p-wave contributions to the optical potential parameters B0 and C0 were evaluated at both saturation density rho0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} and the effective density rhoe ~ 0.6 rho0 where pion-nucleus binding is strongest.

## Key numbers

- d'(2065) mass: M = 2065 MeV, vacuum width Gamma = 0.5 MeV
- pi-NN-d' coupling constant: g = 9.2 GeV^{-1}
- In-medium d' width: Gamma* = 10 MeV (factor of 20 increase over vacuum value)
- Effective proton-proton scattering length (including Coulomb): a = 7.82 fm; effective range: r_e = 2.67 fm
- Enhancement factor eta_F ~ 3
- At effective density rhoe ~ 0.6 rho0: d' contribution to mu^4 ReB0 = 0.005, mu^4 ImB0 = 0.001, mu^6 ReC0 = 0.013, mu^6 ImC0 = 0.008
- Empirical values from standard model: mu^4 ReB0 = -0.15 +/- 0.04, mu^4 ImB0 = 0.054 +/- 0.002, mu^6 ReC0 = -0.28 +/- 0.01, mu^6 ImC0 = 0.062 +/- 0.003

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for dibaryon resonances, particularly the nonstrange pi-NN-coupled, NN-decoupled d' dibaryon with quantum numbers I=0, J^P=0^- first proposed by Mulders (1978) and Kon (1980) and interpreted from DCE cross sections by Martemyanov and Schepkin (1992). The paper uses the framework of pionic atom spectroscopy reviewed by Kienle and Yamazaki (2002) and compares its results to empirical optical potential parameters extracted by Friedman (2002) using both the standard model and the Weise-Friedman model. The results suggest that improved precision in pionic X-ray spectroscopy could either reveal or further constrain the existence of pi-NN resonances.
