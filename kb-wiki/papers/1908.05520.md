# Impact of the initial fluctuations on the dissipative dynamics of interacting Fermi systems: A model case study

> Standard methods used for computing the dynamics of a quantum many-body system are the mean-field (MF) approximations such as the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach. Even though MF approaches are quite successful, they suffer some well-known shortcomings, one of which is insufficient dissipation of collective motion. The stochastic mean-field approach (SMF), where a set of MF trajectories with random initial conditions are considered, is a good candidate to include dissipative effects beyond mean field. In this approach, the one-body density matrix elements are treated initially as a set of stochastic Gaussian c numbers that are adjusted to reproduce first and second moments of collective one-body observables. It is shown that the predictive power of the SMF approach can be further improved by relaxing the Gaussian assumption for the initial probabilities. More precisely, using Gaussian or uniform distributions for the matrix elements generally leads to overdamping for long times, whereas distributions with smaller kurtosis lead to much better reproduction of the long time evolution.

## Key claim

The predictive power of the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach for dissipative dynamics of interacting Fermi systems can be improved by replacing the standard Gaussian assumption for the initial fluctuations of one-body density matrix elements with distributions having smaller kurtosis, because the fourth quantum mechanical moment of one-body observables can be negative for fermionic systems and thus cannot be reproduced by any classical probability distribution.

## Method

The authors derived the third and fourth central moments of stochastic one-body density matrix elements required to match the quantum mechanical moments of an initial Slater determinant, showing that the fourth moment condition leads to a negative value that no probability distribution can satisfy. They then applied the SMF approach to a modified Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (mLMG) model with 6 particles in 12 single-particle states, comparing the dynamics obtained using Gaussian, uniform, and two-point (minimum kurtosis) distributions for the initial fluctuations of the density matrix elements against the exact quantum evolution computed via the iterated Crank-Nicolson method.

## Key numbers

- For the weak coupling case (v0 = 0.05 Δ), the mean-field evolution deviates from the exact solution at around t = 6 Δ⁻¹, while SMF evolutions start to deviate later at around t = 20 Δ⁻¹.
- The maximum one-body entropy for the half-filled 6-particle system is S = 2N ln 2 = 1.38 per particle.
- For the strong coupling case (v0 = 0.5 Δ), the mean-field solution deviates from the exact one at t = 0.7 Δ⁻¹ and SMF solutions start to deviate at around t = 2 Δ⁻¹.
- The SMF calculations used N = 10⁶ events for ensemble averaging.
- The standard deviation of the single-particle energies in each level was σε = 0.2 Δ.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach originally formulated by Ayik (2008) and Lacroix (2014), which assumed Gaussian initial fluctuations, and is closely related to the fermionic truncated Wigner approximation (f-TWA) introduced by Davidson (2017). The study demonstrates that the choice of initial probability distribution significantly affects the timescale over which SMF remains predictive, with the two-point distribution (minimum kurtosis) providing the best agreement with exact dynamics, particularly for weak coupling. This finding enables more accurate microscopic descriptions of dissipative processes in nuclear physics, such as fission and heavy-ion collisions, without requiring phenomenological assumptions about collective degrees of freedom or noise.
