# Some Comments on the Holographic Heavy Quark Potential in a Thermal Bath

> The heavy quark potential of a thermal Yang-Mills theory in strong coupling limit is explored in terms of the holographic principle. With a fairly general AdS/QCD metric the heavy quark potential displays a kink-like screening in the plasma phase. This behavior may conflict the causality of a field theory that is mathematically equivalent to the thermal Yang-Mills.

## Key claim

The heavy quark potential derived from a general class of AdS/QCD metrics at nonzero temperature always exhibits a kink-like screening (a discontinuous derivative in \(dV/dr\)) rather than a smooth exponential screening, and this kink-like behavior may conflict with the causality and unitarity requirements of the field theory that is mathematically equivalent to the thermal Yang-Mills theory.

## Method

The authors analyze the heavy quark potential by computing the Nambu-Goto action of a string world sheet in a general asymptotically AdS black hole metric of the form \(ds^2 = \frac{w(z)}{z^2}[f(z)d\tau^2 + \frac{1}{f(z)}dz^2 + d\vec{x}^2]\), with \(w(z)\) and \(f(z)\) satisfying standard UV and horizon conditions. They derive parametric expressions for the candidate potential \(\mathcal{A}(r)\) and the quark-antiquark separation \(r\) as functions of the world sheet turning point \(z_c\), then examine the asymptotic behavior as \(z_c\) approaches the horizon \(z_h\) or a point where \(F'(z_0)=0\). The heavy quark potential \(V(r)\) is taken as the minimum of \(\mathcal{A}(r)\) and zero, following the standard holographic prescription.

## Key numbers

* For the \(N=4\) super Yang-Mills plasma, the screening radius is \(r_s = 0.745/(\pi T)\), where the candidate potential \(\mathcal{A}\) becomes positive.
* The truncated Coulomb approximation to the super Yang-Mills potential has screening radius \(a = \frac{4\pi^2}{\Gamma^4(1/4)T} \simeq 0.736/(\pi T)\).
* The Coulomb-like attractive potential at short distances is \(\mathcal{A} \simeq -\frac{4\pi^2\sqrt{\lambda}}{\Gamma^4(1/4)r}\) for \(rT \ll 1\).
* For a general metric with \(F(z) \simeq K(z_h - z)^n\) near the horizon, only \(n=1\) avoids a curvature singularity at the horizon; this case yields the same qualitative kink-like screening as the super Yang-Mills case.

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of the heavy quark potential from the \(N=4\) super Yang-Mills case (Maldacena 1998, Brandhuber 1998, Rey 1998) to general AdS/QCD metrics, including those proposed by Karch (2006) and Andreev (2006) to generate the Cornell potential. The authors show that the kink-like screening found in the super Yang-Mills case persists under general conditions, contradicting proposals for smooth exponential screening. They specifically critique the complex-potential approach of Kovchegov (2007) and the tube-joining proposal of Yaffe (2007), and raise a fundamental concern: the Fourier transform of a kink-like potential grows exponentially with energy, preventing the definition of retarded and advanced Green's functions in the adjoint field theory, which may indicate a conflict with causality and unitarity.
