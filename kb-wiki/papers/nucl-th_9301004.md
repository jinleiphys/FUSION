# Pion interaction with the trinucleon up to the eta production threshold

> Pion elastic, charge exchange scattering and induced eta production on the trinucleon systems are investigated in a coupled-channels approach in momentum space with Fadeev wave functions. The channel $πN \rightarrow ηN$ is included using an isobar model with S-, P-, and D-wave resonances. While the coherent reactions like $^3$He($π,π)^3$He can be reasonably well reproduced up to $T_π$=500 MeV, large discrepancies appear for the incoherent processes, $^3$He($π^-,π^0)^3$H and $^3$He($π^-,η)^3$H at backward angles and energies above $Δ$-resonance. In the forward direction the $(π,η)$ calculations underestimate the experimental measurements very close to threshold but agreement with the data improves with increasing pion energy. Predictions are made for the asymmetries of the various reactions on polarized $^3$He.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a coupled-channels momentum-space approach using Fadeev wave functions can reasonably reproduce coherent pion reactions on trinucleon systems up to 500 MeV, but large discrepancies remain for incoherent processes (charge exchange and eta production) at backward angles and energies above the Delta resonance, indicating that isovector second-order effects beyond the first-order optical potential are needed.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation in momentum space with relativistic kinematics, using the KMT version of multiple scattering theory. The pion-nucleon input is taken from partial-wave analyses with separable off-shell form factors, and the nuclear structure is described by Fadeev wave functions from the Reid soft-core potential for the A=3 system. The coupled-channels formalism includes the πN → ηN channel via an isobar model with S-, P-, and D-wave resonances, and the calculations are compared with three nuclear models: a simple S-shell harmonic oscillator, a phenomenological model using electromagnetic form factors, and the full three-body Fadeev model.

## Key numbers

- Pion kinetic energies studied: Tπ = 100–600 MeV, with momentum transfer Q < 6 fm⁻¹
- At Tπ = 100 MeV, the simple S-shell model agrees well with the full three-body calculation and experimental data from Refs. [41,42]
- At Tπ = 295 MeV, full calculations fail to reproduce experimental data at large angles; measurements show an additional dip around Θ = 120° not present in the calculations
- At Tπ = 350–500 MeV, momentum transfer for backward angles reaches Q = 5 fm⁻¹, where differences between three-body and phenomenological models become visible
- For π⁺ scattering at Tπ = 100 MeV, the asymmetry Ay approaches a maximum value of almost +1 around Θ = 90°
- The off-shell πN form factor uses range parameter r₀ = 0.47 fm
- The harmonic oscillator radius parameter for the S-shell model is b = 1.65 fm for ³He

## Context

This work extends earlier momentum-space KMT calculations by Landau (1977) and Mach (1983) for pion scattering on trinucleon systems, which were limited to Tπ ≤ 200 MeV, into the higher-energy region up to the eta production threshold. The authors previously applied a similar approach to pion scattering on polarized ³He at low energies (Kamalov, Tiator, 1992). The study enables systematic tests of the reaction mechanism at momentum transfers where Fadeev wave functions still describe electromagnetic form factors well, and provides predictions for forthcoming experiments at LAMPF, PSI, and TRIUMF, including polarization observables and eta production channels.
