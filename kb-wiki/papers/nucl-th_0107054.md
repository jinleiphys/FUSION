# Magic numbers in exotic nuclei and spin-isospin properties of {\it NN} interaction

> The magic numbers in exotic nuclei are discussed, and their novel origin is shown to be the spin-isospin dependent part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclei. The importance and robustness of this mechanism is shown in terms of meson exchange, G-matrix and QCD theories. In neutron-rich exotic nuclei, magic numbers such as N = 8, 20, etc. can disappear, while N = 6, 16, etc. arise, affecting the structure of lightest exotic nuclei to nucleosynthesis of heavy elements.

# Magic numbers in exotic nuclei and spin-isospin properties of NN interaction

## Key claim

The spin-isospin dependent part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (Vτσ) is the primary mechanism driving the disappearance of traditional magic numbers (N=8, 20) and the emergence of new magic numbers (N=6, 16, 34) in neutron-rich exotic nuclei, with the effect depending specifically on the occupancy of spin-orbit partner orbits by protons.

## Method

The authors computed effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) from monopole Hamiltonians for neutron-rich nuclei using shell-model interactions (SDPF, USD, and Kuo G-matrix) in the sd and p-sd model spaces. They compared neutron ESPEs between stable and exotic nuclei at the same neutron number, and performed Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations with full configuration mixing to obtain occupation numbers. For p-shell nuclei, they modified the Cohen-Kurath Hamiltonian by shifting all T=0 matrix elements between 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 orbits by -2 MeV to strengthen the spin-isospin interaction.

## Key numbers

- The effective gap between 0d3/2 and 1s1/2 in 24O (N=16, Z=8) is approximately 6 MeV, creating a new magic number at N=16
- The effective gap between 0d3/2 and the pf shell in 30Si (stable, Z=14) is approximately 4 MeV, maintaining the N=20 magic structure
- The neutron effective 1s1/2-0p1/2 gap decreases from 8.6 MeV for 13C down to 0.8 MeV for 9He
- The V_{0d5/2 0d3/2}^{T=0} matrix element is the most attractive among all monopole matrix elements in the sd shell, exceeding the second most attractive one by approximately 1 MeV in the SDPF interaction
- The 0p3/2 and 0p1/2 single-particle energies in the PSDMK2 Hamiltonian are 1.38 MeV and 1.68 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the understanding of shell evolution beyond the stable nuclei studied by Mayer and Jensen (1949), building on the monopole Hamiltonian formalism of Poves and Zuker (1981) and the Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations of Utsuno et al. (1999) for N~20 exotic nuclei. The mechanism explains the N=16 magic number suggested by the binding energy systematics of Ozawa et al. (2000) and the parity inversion in 11Be and 9He first analyzed by Talmi and Unna (1960). The results enable predictions of new magic numbers (N=34, and modifications at N=82, 126) that may affect r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, and challenge mean-field approaches (Skyrme HF, Relativistic Mean Field) to properly include the spin-isospin interaction.
