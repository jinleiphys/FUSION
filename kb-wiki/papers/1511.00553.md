# Chiral phase transition of $N_f$=2+1 and 3 QCD at vanishing baryon chemical potential

> We present updated results on chiral phase structure in (2+1)-flavor ($N_f$=2+1) and 3-flavor ($N_f=3$) QCD based on the simulations using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks on lattices with temporal extent $N_τ$ =6 at vanishing baryon chemical potential. In $N_f$=2+1 QCD we have performed simulations with a strange quark fixed to its physical value and two degenerate light quarks whose values are adjusted to have 5 values of Goldstone pion masses in the region of 160 - 80 MeV in the continuum limit. The universal scaling behavior of chiral condensates as well as chiral susceptibilities is discussed and the tri-critical point is suggested to be located below the physical point, i.e. at smaller than physical strange quark mass. In $N_f$=3 QCD simulations with 6 different masses of 3 degenerate quarks corresponding to the Goldstone pion masses in the region of 230 - 80 MeV have also been performed. Our results suggest that the QCD transition with these values of quark masses is of crossover type and an upper bound of the critical pion mass where the first order phase transition starts is estimated to be about 50 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that for (2+1)-flavor QCD, the tri-critical point is located below the physical point (i.e., at a smaller strange quark mass than the physical value), and for 3-flavor QCD, the transition remains a crossover down to pion masses of about 80 MeV, with an upper bound on the critical pion mass for the onset of a first-order transition estimated at about 50 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=6 at zero baryon chemical potential. For Nf=2+1 QCD, the strange quark mass was fixed to its physical value and five light quark masses were varied corresponding to Goldstone pion masses from 160 to 80 MeV in the continuum limit. For Nf=3 QCD, six different masses of three degenerate quarks were simulated corresponding to pion masses from 230 to 80 MeV. The chiral condensates and chiral susceptibilities were analyzed using scaling fits to the magnetic equation of state for O(2) and Z(2) universality classes.

## Key numbers

- For Nf=2+1 QCD with O(2) scaling: Tc = 145.6(1) MeV, t0 = 3.34(4)×10⁻³, h0 = 4.56(4)×10⁻⁶
- For Nf=2+1 QCD with Z(2) scaling: mc/msphy = -0.0077(2), Tc = 141.9(2) MeV, t0 = 1.5(2)×10⁻⁴, h0 = 3.54(8)×10⁻⁶
- The ratio χsing/χM at T∼Tc increases from ∼72% at mπ=160 MeV to ∼88% at mπ=80 MeV
- For Nf=3 QCD: upper bound on critical quark mass amc ≤ 0.00039(1), corresponding to critical pion mass mπc ≲ 50 MeV
- Nf=3 QCD simulations used quark masses amq from 0.0009375 to 0.0075, corresponding to pion masses from 80 to 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase structure reported by Ding (2013) and addresses the Columbia plot framework originally described by Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) and Brown (1990). The results for the upper bound on the critical pion mass in Nf=3 QCD are consistent with calculations using stout fermions by Endrodi (2007) and Wilson fermions toward the continuum limit by Nakamura (2015). The findings enable more precise determination of the QCD phase diagram in the quark mass plane and constrain the location of the first-order phase transition region.
