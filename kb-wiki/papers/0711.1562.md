# Universal Fermi Gas with Two- and Three-Body Resonances

> We consider a Fermi gas with two components of different masses, with the s-wave two-body interaction tuned to unitarity. In the range of mass ratio 8.62<M/m<13.6, it is possible for a single contact interaction between heavy fermions to produce a resonance in a three-body channel. The resulting system is scale invariant and has universal properties, and is very strongly interacting. When M/m is slightly above the lower limit 8.62, the ground state energy of a 2:1 mixture of heavy and light fermions is less than 2% of the energy of a noninteracting gas with the same number densities. We derive exact relationships between the pressures of the unitary Fermi gases with and without three-body resonance when the mass ratio is close to the critical values of 8.62 and 13.6. Possible experimental realization with cold atoms in optical lattices is discussed.

# Universal Fermi Gas with Two- and Three-Body Resonances

## Key claim
For a two-component Fermi gas with s-wave two-body interaction tuned to unitarity and mass ratio between 8.62 and 13.6, a short-range contact interaction between heavy fermions can be fine-tuned to produce a resonance in the three-body channel while preserving scale invariance; the resulting system is universal and very strongly interacting, with ground state energy less than 2% of the noninteracting gas energy when the mass ratio is slightly above 8.62 and the mixture is 2:1 heavy to light.

## Method
The authors solve the three-body problem exactly in hyperspherical coordinates, determining the exponent gamma from a transcendental equation that yields the critical mass ratios for which a three-body resonance can be achieved without triggering the Efimov effect. They then analyze the many-body ground state using a variational approach based on treating trimers as pointlike fermions forming a dilute gas, and derive exact relationships between the pressures of the unitary Fermi gas with and without three-body resonance near the critical mass ratios using Bessel function asymptotics.

## Key numbers
- Lower critical mass ratio for three-body resonance: u_min = 8.62 (exact solution)
- Upper critical mass ratio before Efimov effect: u_max = 13.6 (exact solution)
- Born-Oppenheimer approximation gives u_min ≈ 7.77 and u_max ≈ 14.0
- For u slightly above 8.62 and x = 1/3 (2:1 heavy:light mixture): xi(u -> u_min, 1/3) < 1.93 × 10^(-2) (upper bound on ground state energy ratio)
- For comparison, symmetric unitary Fermi gas with equal masses has xi ≈ 0.4
- Exact relationship near u_min: lim_{u->u_min} [P(mu_l, mu_h) - P_0(mu_l, mu_h)] = [2(2M+m)]^(3/2) / (5 pi^2 hbar^3) * (2 mu_h + mu_l)^(5/2)
- For x = 1/3 and u -> u_min: xi^(-3/2) = xi_0^(-3/2) + 374
- For l=3 channel, critical mass ratio for fine-tuning is u = 70.1
- Lattice model parameters: U/(t_h + t_l) ≈ -3.957 for cb resonance; W/t_h ranges from -3.80 ± 0.03 at t_l/t_h = 8.62 to > -0.4 at t_l/t_h = 13.6

## Context
This work extends the study of universal Fermi gases at unitarity, previously explored for symmetric systems (Eagles, Leggett, Nozieres) and for unequal masses or densities (Ketterle-pol, Hulet-pol, SadeMelo-uneqm, Yip-uneqm), to a novel regime where both two- and three-body interactions are resonant. The critical mass ratio u_min ≈ 8.62 was previously identified in studies of three-body recombination by Petrov. The authors' epsilon-expansion approach near four spatial dimensions (Nishida 2006) and the Nussinov and Nussinov argument about ground state energy approaching zero near d=4 provide theoretical context. The results enable experimental realization with cold atoms in optical lattices and provide exact relationships that can be tested by quantum Monte Carlo simulations.
