# Rapidity and system size dependence of anisotropic flows in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We report results from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model on the rapidity and system size dependence of charged hadron anisotropic flows in nuclear collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model, the rapidity dependence of anisotropic flows suggests that a partonic matter is formed only around mid-rapidity during the early stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, while strings remain dominant at large rapidities; this interpretation is hedged by the fact that the default version (without string melting) describes the directed flow data at large pseudorapidity better than the string melting version.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, a hybrid model combining minijet partons from hard processes and strings from soft processes via HIJING initial conditions, with parton evolution described by the ZPC parton cascade model. Two versions were employed: the default version where minijet partons interact and then fragment into hadrons via the Lund string model, and a string melting version where hadrons from string fragmentation are converted to valence quarks/antiquarks that interact via ZPC before coalescing into hadrons. Final-state hadronic scatterings were modeled by the ART model, and the parton scattering cross section was treated as a parameter (3 mb or 10 mb) to study its effect on anisotropic flows.

## Key numbers

- In minimum bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, the string melting scenario with a parton scattering cross section of 10 mb describes PHOBOS data for elliptic flow (v2) around mid-pseudorapidity (|eta| <= 1.5), while a smaller cross section of 3 mb or the default version better describes both PHOBOS and STAR data at large pseudorapidity (|eta| >= 3).
- In minimum bias Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, the elliptic flow is about a factor of 3 smaller than in Au+Au collisions at the same energy, consistent with linear scaling of system size.
- For asymmetric Cu+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV, charged hadrons around mid-rapidity display a stronger directed flow (v1) compared to symmetric Au+Au collisions, and both v1 and v2 show asymmetry in forward and backward rapidities.

## Context

This work extends prior AMPT model studies by Zhang (1999) and Lin (2001) that established the sensitivity of elliptic flow to parton scattering cross sections, and by Chen (previous work) on high-order anisotropic flows. The results enable experimental tests of whether partonic matter formation is limited to mid-rapidity at RHIC, and provide predictions for asymmetric collision systems (Cu+Au) that can be verified by future data to test the AMPT model and understand partonic matter dynamics.
