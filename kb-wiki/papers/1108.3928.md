# The K d -> Lambda(1405) n reaction with the DAFNE set up and the $\bar{K}NN$ system revisited

> The $K^{-}$ induced production of $Λ(1405)$ in the $K^{-} d \to πΣn$ reaction is investigated having in mind the conditions of the DAFNE facility at Frascati. We find that the fastest kaons from the decay of the $ϕ$ at DAFNE are well suited to see this resonance if one selects forward going neutrons in the center of mass, which reduce the contribution of single scattering and stress the contribution of the double scattering where the signal of the resonance appears clearer. We take advantage to report briefly on a recent work in which in addition to the $\bar{K}NN$ system with total spin S=0, we find a less bound state (although with equally large width) with S=1, like in the $K^{-} d$ reported in the first part.

## Key claim

The $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ reaction can produce a clear signal of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance at the DAFNE facility, but only if forward-going neutrons in the center-of-mass frame are selected, which suppresses the single-scattering background and enhances the double-scattering contribution where the resonance appears.

## Method

The authors calculated the ${\cal T}$ matrix for the $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ reaction as the sum of three diagrams: single scattering, double scattering with a $K^{-}n \to K^{-}n$ intermediate step, and double scattering with a $K^{-}p \to \bar{K}^{0}n$ charge-exchange step. They used the deuteron wave function in momentum space and the scattering amplitudes for $\bar{K}N \to \bar{K}N$ and $\bar{K}N \to \pi \Sigma$ from chiral dynamics, then computed cross sections for different angular cuts on the emitted neutron in the center-of-mass frame. For the $\bar{K}NN$ system, they applied the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations to study both spin S=0 and S=1 states.

## Key numbers

- Incident $K^{-}$ momentum of 130 MeV/c was used for the $\pi^{0}\Sigma^{0}n$ invariant mass spectra calculations.
- For the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=0, the binding energy is around 40 MeV and the width (without $K^{-}$ absorption) is 50 MeV.
- For the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=1, the binding energy is about 27 MeV with a similar width to the S=0 state.

## Context

This work extends the study of the $K^{-} d \to \pi \Sigma n$ reaction previously reported by Sekihara et al. (2011) and measured in an earlier experiment, by adapting it to the low-energy kaon conditions of the DAFNE facility. The authors also revisit the $\bar{K}NN$ system, where prior theoretical work by Gal (2007), Sato (2007), and Hyodo (2008) found a bound state with S=0 using Faddeev equations and variational calculations; the present work finds an additional less-bound S=1 state. The results enable experimental searches for the $\Lambda(1405)$ at DAFNE and complicate the search for $\bar{K}NN$ bound states due to overlapping signals from the two spin states.
