# Contact formalism for coupled channels

> The contact formalism, a useful tool for analyzing short-range correlations, is generalized here for systems with coupled channels, such as in nuclear physics. The relevant asymptotic form is presented and contact matrices are defined. Generally, for the case of two coupled channels, two two-body functions are included in the asymptotic form, resulting a 2 x 2 contact matrix. Nevertheless, it is shown that if the coupling terms of the potential are very weak or very strong, only a single two-body function is needed, resulting a single contact. This universal result is directly relevant to nuclear systems, and provides a theoretical explanation for the fact that proton-neutron short-range correlations can be described using the single bound-state deuteron wave function. It is achieved by applying an appropriate boundary condition on the two-body functions. This boundary condition can be interpreted as a mean field potential imposed on the correlated pair due to the residual system.

# Contact formalism for coupled channels

## Key claim
The contact formalism is generalized to systems with coupled channels, showing that for two coupled channels a 2x2 contact matrix is generally required, but if the coupling terms of the potential are very weak or very strong, only a single two-body function and a single contact are needed. This provides a theoretical explanation for why proton-neutron short-range correlations in nuclei can be described using only the bound-state deuteron wave function, despite the deuteron channel having two coupled components (s-wave and d-wave).

## Method
The authors derive the asymptotic form of the many-body wave function for systems with coupled channels, defining contact matrices from the regular functions A^a that multiply the two independent zero-energy two-body solutions. They numerically solve the two-body Schrodinger equation with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential inside a spherical box of varying radius R, and also with a simpler Gaussian potential where the coupling strength S between channels is varied, to determine when the two independent solutions collapse into a single function.

## Key numbers
- For nuclear systems, a high-momentum tail from short-range correlations was identified for k > k_F ≈ 1.26 fm^{-1}
- Using the AV18 potential with a spherical box, the ratio η = c2/c1 becomes approximately constant for energies below about 30 MeV, independent of box radius R (tested for R = 10, 25, 50 fm)
- For the Gaussian potential with Λ^2 = 3 fm^{-2} and scattering length a = 10 fm, the ratio η converges to a single value for small energies when the coupling strength S = 2, but shows two distinct branches when S = 0.2 or S = 0.5
- The deuteron density ρ_pn(r) calculated from the lowest allowed energy in a box of radius R = 50 fm (E = 0.20 MeV) matches the exact bound-state deuteron density at small distances

## Context
This work extends the contact formalism originally developed by Tan (2008) for zero-range two-component fermions, and later generalized to nuclear systems by Weiss, Bazak, Barnea (2015, 2016) where contact matrices were defined assuming uncoupled channels. The paper provides a theoretical foundation for experimental observations by Fomin (2012), Arrington, Higinbotham, Rosner (2012), and others that electron-scattering cross sections sensitive to short-range correlations become approximately proportional to the deuteron cross section. It also explains numerical results by Wiringa, Schiavilla, Pieper (2014) showing that the pn momentum distribution of nuclei approximates the deuteron momentum distribution for k > 4 fm^{-1}. This work enables systematic treatment of coupled channels in short-range correlation studies and clarifies why the deuteron channel dominance alone does not explain the sufficiency of a single asymptotic function.
