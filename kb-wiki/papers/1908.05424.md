# Ab Initio Treatment of Collective Correlations and the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of $^{48}$Ca

> Working with Hamiltonians from chiral effective field theory, we develop a novel framework for describing arbitrary deformed medium-mass nuclei by combining the in-medium similarity renormalization group with the generator coordinate method. The approach leverages the ability of the first method to capture dynamic correlations and the second to include collective correlations without violating symmetries. We use our scheme to compute the matrix element that governs the neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti, and find it to have the value $0.61$, near or below the predictions of most phenomenological methods. The result opens the door to ab initio calculations of the matrix elements for the decay of heavier nuclei such as $^{76}$Ge, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe.

## Key claim
The authors compute the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element for $^{48}$Ca to $^{48}$Ti using a novel ab initio framework that combines the in-medium similarity renormalization group with the generator coordinate method, obtaining a value of 0.61. This result is qualified as being near or below most phenomenological predictions, with the caveat that the calculation neglects newly discovered chiral EFT corrections to the decay operator and many-body currents.

## Method
The authors start with chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (Entem-Machleidt N$^3$LO two-nucleon interaction with 500 MeV/c cutoff, SRG-evolved to resolution scales of 1.8 or 2.0 fm$^{-1}$, plus a three-nucleon interaction with 2.0 fm$^{-1}$ cutoff). They normal-order the three-body interaction to two-body (NO2B) level with respect to an A=48 reference ensemble, then evolve the Hamiltonian via the IMSRG flow equation using a Brillouin generator. The evolved Hamiltonian is used in particle-number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with variation after projection, followed by generator coordinate method superposition of states with different quadrupole deformations and isoscalar pairing amplitudes, projected onto good angular momentum and particle number.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matrix element $M^{0\nu} = 0.61$ for the $^{48}$Ca $\to$ $^{48}$Ti decay, with extrapolation uncertainty range of (+0.04, -0.05) using the EM1.8/2.0 interaction
- Ground-state energies of -418.26 MeV ($^{48}$Ca) and -422.27 MeV ($^{48}$Ti) with EM1.8/2.0 interaction
- $Q_{\beta\beta}$ value of 5.57 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.26 MeV
- $B(E2: 2_1^+ \to 0_1^+)$ in $^{48}$Ti changes from 101 $e^2$fm$^4$ to 96 $e^2$fm$^4$ when including isoscalar pairing configurations (EM1.8/2.0 interaction)
- NME values for EM1.8/2.0 at $\hbar\omega=16$ MeV range from 1.03 ($e_{\rm max}=6$) to 0.66 ($e_{\rm max}=10$)
- NME values for EM2.0/2.0 at $\hbar\omega=16$ MeV range from 1.02 ($e_{\rm max}=6$) to 0.75 ($e_{\rm max}=10$)
- With the unevolved $0\nu\beta\beta$ operator, the NME is 0.31 instead of 0.78 (EM1.8/2.0, $\hbar\omega=16$ MeV, $e_{\rm max}=8$)

## Context
This work extends the IMSRG method, previously applied only to spherical nuclei (Hergert 2016), to deformed nuclei by combining it with the generator coordinate method, which has been successful in nuclear density functional theory (Bender 2008, Yao 2010, Rodriguez 2010). The approach was previously tested in a small shell-model space with a phenomenological Hamiltonian (Yao 2018) and in light nuclei with A=6 (Basili 2019). The results open the door to ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements for heavier candidate nuclei such as $^{76}$Ge, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe, and improve upon previous ab initio calculations that severely underpredicted $B(E2)$ values (Parzuchowski 2017, Stroberg 2019).
