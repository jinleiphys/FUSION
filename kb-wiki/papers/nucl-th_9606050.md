# Density dependence of resonance broadening and shadowing effects in nuclear photoabsorption

> Medium effects as a function of the mass number $A$ are studied in the total photonuclear cross section from the $Δ$-resonance region up to the region where shadowing effects are known to exist. A consistent picture is obtained by simply assuming a density dependence of the different mechanisms of resonance broadening and shadowing. The $Δ$-mass shift is found to increase with $A$.

# Density dependence of resonance broadening and shadowing effects in nuclear photoabsorption

## Key claim
The total photonuclear cross section per nucleon can be described consistently from the Δ-resonance region up to the shadowing region by assuming a simple density dependence of resonance broadening and shadowing mechanisms, with the Δ-mass shift increasing with mass number A. The authors find that the Δ-mass shift is 12 MeV for ¹²C, 15 MeV for ⁶³Cu, and 18 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb, comparable to values extracted from experimental data by the Frascati group.

## Method
The nuclear photoabsorption cross section was calculated as the imaginary part of the forward Compton scattering amplitude, using a convolution over the nucleon momentum distribution with eight Breit-Wigner resonances fitted to the free nucleon cross section. Medium effects were incorporated through a density-dependent mass shift δM, collision broadening Γ*, Pauli blocking factor B_P, and Fermi suppression factor S_F, with parameters adjusted for ¹²C, ⁶³Cu, and ²⁰⁸Pb using nuclear density ρ, Fermi momentum p_F, and mass number A. For the shadowing region above 2 GeV, the correction was computed using an eikonal Green function approach for vector meson propagation (ρ, ω, φ) with two-body correlations parametrized by a Bessel function with q_c = 780 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Δ-mass shift δM = 12 MeV for ¹²C, 15 MeV for ⁶³Cu, and 18 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb
- Collision broadening Γ* for the Δ: 51 MeV (¹²C), 67 MeV (⁶³Cu), 74 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Collision broadening Γ* for other resonances (D₁₃(1520), F₁₅(1680), D₃₃(1700)): 218-235 MeV (¹²C), 280-305 MeV (⁶³Cu), 315-340 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Fermi momentum p_F = 0.221 GeV for ¹²C, 0.240 GeV for ⁶⁴Cu, 0.265 GeV for ²⁰⁸Pb
- Product σ*v = 40 mb for the NN* interaction cross section times velocity
- Correlation parameter q_c = 780 MeV
- Asymptotic shadowing constant: A_eff/A = A^{-0.09}
- Correlation contribution to A_eff/A: about 10% for copper and lead, about 5% for carbon

## Context
This work extends the generalized photonuclear sum-rule approach of Boffi, Golubeva, Kondratyuk, and Krivoruchenko (1995) by introducing explicit density dependence of resonance parameters, and builds on the photofission fit for ²³⁸U by Kondratyuk, Krivoruchenko, and collaborators. The results are compared with photoabsorption data from Frascati and Mainz, and with the shadowing formalism of Weise (1974) and Bauer, Spital, Yennie, and Pipkin (1978). The finding of antishadowing (A_eff/A > 1) below 2 GeV, also seen in ²³⁸U, suggests new measurements at CEBAF and ELSA energies are needed to clarify the onset of shadowing effects.
