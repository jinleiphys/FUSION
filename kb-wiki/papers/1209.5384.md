# Real-time calculations of many-body dynamics in quantum systems

> Real-time computation of time-dependent quantum mechanical problems are presented for nuclear many-body problems. Quantum tunneling in nuclear fusion at low energy is described using a time-dependent wave packet. A real-time method of calculating strength functions using the time-dependent Schroedinger equation is utilized to properly treat the continuum boundary condition. To go beyond the few-body models,we resort to the density-functional theory. The nuclear mean-field models are briefly reviewed to illustrate its foundation and necessity of state dependence in effective interactions. This state dependence is successfully taken into account by the density dependence, leading to the energy density functional. Photoabsorption cross sections in 238U are calculated with the real-time method for the time-dependent density-functional theory.

## Key claim

Real-time time-dependent methods can efficiently compute nuclear many-body dynamics (fusion, strength functions, and collective response) without explicitly constructing continuum eigenstates, but the method's accuracy depends on proper treatment of model-space truncation, as demonstrated by the finding that a weakly-bound halo neutron suppresses rather than enhances sub-barrier fusion cross sections, contradicting earlier predictions.

## Method

The paper applies time-dependent wave-packet propagation to solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for nuclear systems. For fusion, a Gaussian wave packet is evolved using a fourth-order Taylor expansion of the small-time evolution operator, and fusion probabilities are extracted from the energy distribution of initial and final wave packets. For strength functions, the initial state is constructed by applying the E1 operator to the ground state, then time-evolved; the Fourier transform of the time-dependent overlap yields the strength distribution. For many-body systems, the time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) is used, with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation solved self-consistently for superfluid nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: approximately 0.17 fm^-3, giving Fermi momentum k_F approximately 1.36 fm^-1 and Fermi energy T_F approximately 40 MeV
- Binding energy per particle: B/A approximately 16 MeV (extrapolated for infinite nuclear matter without surface and Coulomb energy)
- For the two-body fusion model (10Be on 208Pb), the time-dependent wave-packet calculation used K^2/2mu = 28 MeV and time evolution up to t_f = 10 MeV^-1 (approximately 2000 fm/c)
- For the three-body fusion model (11Be on 209Bi), the initial wave-packet centroid was at R_0 = 25 fm, and convergence required partial waves up to l_max approximately 70 when n-T potential was included
- For the 11Be E1 strength calculation, the 2s orbital energy was varied between -0.5 MeV and -2.0 MeV, showing a strong low-energy peak (threshold effect) that is reduced for deeper binding

## Context

This work extends the real-time computational approach to nuclear many-body problems, providing an alternative to time-independent methods that require explicit construction of continuum eigenstates. The three-body fusion calculations contradict earlier predictions by Tolsma-Suzuki (1991), Hussein (1992), Takigawa-Sato-Kobayashi (1993), Hagino (2000), and Diaz-Torres (2002) that weakly-bound neutrons enhance sub-barrier fusion; the discrepancy is attributed to insufficient partial-wave truncation in those earlier coupled-channel calculations. The density-functional approach builds on the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and effective interactions, resolving the inconsistency between independent-particle motion and nuclear saturation through density-dependent interactions and rearrangement potentials. The method enables calculation of photoabsorption cross sections in heavy deformed nuclei such as 238U and shape phase transitions in rare-earth isotopes like Nd.
