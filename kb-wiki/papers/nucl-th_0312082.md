# Collisional Effects on HFS Transitions of Antiprotonic Helium

> Collisions of metastable antiprotonic helium with medium atoms induce transitions between hyperfine structure sublevels as well as shift and broadening of the microwave M1 spectral lines. We consider these effects in the framework of a simple model with scalar and tensor interactions between $(\bar{p}\mathrm{He}^+)_{nL}$ and $\mathrm{He}$ atoms. $S$-matrix is obtained by solving coupled-channels equations involving 4 HFS sublevels $(F=L\pm 1/2, J=F\pm 1/2)$ of the $nL$ level and relative angular momenta up to $l=5$ at the kinetic energy $E\lesssim 10$ K. The calculated spin-flip cross sections are less than elastic ones by 4 orders of value. At the density $N=3\times 10^{20}$ cm$^{-3}$ and T=6 K we obtain the relaxation times $τ(FJ\to F'J')\gtrsim 160$ ns, the frequency shifts of M1 spectral lines $Δν\lesssim 66$ KHz for the favored transitions ($ΔF=\pm 1, ΔJ=\pm 1$) and frequency broadening of the M1 spectral lines $γ/2\lesssim 5.8$ MHz. The results are compatible with the recent experimental data obtained by a laser-microwave-laser resonance method.

# Collisional Effects on HFS Transitions of Antiprotonic Helium

## Key claim

The calculated collisional spin-flip cross sections, frequency shifts, and line broadenings for microwave M1 transitions in antiprotonic helium are compatible with the upper limits derived from recent laser-microwave-laser resonance experiments, despite the use of a simple model with scalar and tensor interactions between (p̄He⁺) and He atoms.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations involving the four hyperfine structure (HFS) sublevels (F = L ± 1/2, J = F ± 1/2) of a given nL level, including relative angular momenta up to l = 5 at kinetic energies E ≲ 10 K. The interaction potential between (p̄He⁺) and He was modeled with a scalar term V₀(R) (with a Van der Waals minimum and repulsive core) and a tensor term V₂(R), using two parameter sets: set A based on fitting E1 transition shifts, and set B estimated from ab initio potential surface calculations. Cross sections, collisional transition rates, and M1 line shifts and broadenings were computed from the S-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Spin-flip cross sections are smaller than elastic cross sections by 4 orders of magnitude.
- At density N = 3 × 10²⁰ cm⁻³ and temperature T = 6 K, relaxation times τ(FJ → F′J′) ≳ 160 ns.
- At the same conditions, frequency shifts of M1 spectral lines for favored transitions (ΔF = ±1, ΔJ = ±1) are Δν ≲ 66 kHz.
- At the same conditions, frequency broadening of M1 spectral lines is γ/2 ≲ 5.8 MHz.
- The calculated shift at T = 6 K and N = 3 × 10²⁰ cm⁻³ is Δν = 65.8 kHz.
- The calculated broadening at T = 6 K and N = 3 × 10²⁰ cm⁻³ is γ/2 = 5.8 MHz.
- The relaxation rate for F₋ → F₊ at these conditions is λ ≲ 6 × 10⁶ s⁻¹.
- The hyperfine splitting between F = L ± 1/2 sublevels is ν(F₋, F₊) ∼ 13 GHz.
- The splitting between J = F ± 1/2 subsublevels is ν(FJ₊, FJ₋) ∼ 150 MHz.
- Experimental central frequencies for the (n, L) = (37, 35) state: ν⁺HF = 12.89596(34) GHz and ν⁻HF = 12.92467(29) GHz, agreeing with isolated-system calculations to ≲ 30 ppm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of collisional effects from E1 transitions (previously modeled by Korenman and Yudin using a scalar potential, and by others with ab initio potential surfaces) to the hyperfine structure of antiprotonic helium, for which no published theoretical papers existed at the time. The results are compared with the first experimental data on HFS obtained by a laser-microwave-laser resonance method, which found that density shifts of M1 lines are very small (≲ 30 kHz) while line widths (γ ∼ 5.3 ± 0.7 MHz) leave room for collisional broadening. The calculations enable interpretation of these experimental limits and provide a framework for predicting collisional relaxation times and line shapes in future higher-precision measurements.
