# Bound states of anti-nucleons in finite nuclei

> We study the bound states of anti-nucleons emerging from the lower continuum in finite nuclei within the relativistic Hartree approach including the contributions of the Dirac sea to the source terms of the meson fields. The Dirac equation is reduced to two Schrödinger-equivalent equations for the nucleon and the anti-nucleon respectively. These two equations are solved simultaneously in an iteration procedure. Numerical results show that the bound levels of anti-nucleons vary drastically when the vacuum contributions are taken into account.

## Key claim

The bound-state energies of anti-nucleons in finite nuclei are drastically reduced (by about a factor of two) when the Dirac sea contributions to the meson-field source terms are included in the relativistic Hartree approach, compared to the relativistic mean-field treatment that only considers valence nucleons; this sensitivity makes anti-nucleon spectra a probe of the individual scalar and vector potentials, but the actual depth of the anti-nucleon potential remains experimentally unmeasured.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian with nucleons coupled to sigma, omega, rho mesons and the photon, including nonlinear scalar self-interactions. They reduce the Dirac equation to two Schrödinger-equivalent equations for nucleons and anti-nucleons, which are solved simultaneously in a self-consistent iteration. The Dirac-sea contributions to the scalar and baryon densities are evaluated via a derivative expansion up to leading order for the one-meson and one-nucleon loops, and the anti-nucleon wave functions are obtained from the converged mean fields with a truncated space of principal and angular quantum numbers (n=4, j=9 for 16O; n=5, j=11 for 40Ca; n=9, j=19 for 208Pb).

## Key numbers

* The single-particle energies of anti-protons calculated from the relativistic Hartree approach (RHA) are about half of those from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, for all nuclei studied.
* For 208Pb, the shell fluctuation delta-rho = rho_C(1.8 fm) - rho_C(0.0 fm) is -0.0023 fm^{-3} empirically; the RHA reproduces this value, while the RMF overestimates it by a factor of 3.
* The RHA yields a larger effective nucleon mass than the RMF; the best-fit RHA1 parameter set gives a larger chi-squared value than the NL1 RMF set because the larger effective mass (around 0.6) is preferred by the fit to ground-state properties of spherical nuclei.
* The spin-orbit splitting in the anti-nucleon sector is so small that the 1p-bar_{1/2} and 1p-bar_{3/2} states are nearly indistinguishable, while the spacing between the 1s-bar and 1p-bar states remains evident, especially for lighter nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approach (RHA) of Horowitz and Serot (1984) and Furnstahl (1989) by explicitly computing anti-nucleon bound states in finite nuclei, whereas previous RHA calculations focused only on nucleon ground states. The authors fit their RHA parameters to the same eight spherical nuclei used in the RMF fit of Reinhard (1986), showing that vacuum contributions improve the surface thickness and shell fluctuation compared to the RMF. The results enable future experimental searches for anti-nucleon bound states, which could test the physical necessity of strong scalar and vector potentials in the Dirac picture, and motivate studies of collective anti-nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as proposed by Greiner (1995).
