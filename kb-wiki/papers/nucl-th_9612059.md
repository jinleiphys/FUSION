# Electroproduction of phi meson from proton and the strangeness in the nucleon

> We analyze the process $e p \to e p ϕ$ near the threshold within $uud - s\bar s$ cluster model as a probe of the strangeness content of proton. Our consideration is based on the relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model which takes into account the Lorentz-contraction effect of the hadron wavefunctions. We find that the knockout mechanisms are comparable to the diffractive production of vector-meson-dominance model when only (3-5)% strange quark admixture is assumed, which should be compared with (10-20)% of the nonrelativistic quark model prediction. The cross sections of the $uud$- and $s\bar s$-knockout processes have qualitatively different dependence on the four-momentum transfer squared to the proton and may be distinguished experimentally. We also briefly discuss a way to determine the strangeness content of proton in 5-quark cluster model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the knockout mechanism for φ meson electroproduction becomes comparable to the diffractive vector-meson-dominance (VDM) background when the proton wavefunction contains only 3–5% strange quark admixture, which is significantly lower than the 10–20% range predicted by nonrelativistic quark models. This conclusion depends on using a relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model that accounts for Lorentz contraction of hadron wavefunctions.

## Method

The authors analyze the process e p → e p φ near threshold using a uud-s̄s cluster model of the proton, where the proton wavefunction is decomposed into a bare uud component and a uuds̄s component with relative orbital angular momentum ℓ=1. They calculate the knockout cross sections for both the s̄s-cluster and uud-cluster contributions using the relativistic harmonic oscillator model (RHOM), which incorporates Lorentz contraction effects, and compare these with the nonrelativistic quark model (NRQM) predictions and the VDM diffractive production cross section. The calculations are performed for incident electron energy 11.5 GeV and center-of-mass energy W=2.1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The s̄s admixture required for knockout mechanisms to be comparable to VDM is 3–5% in the RHOM, compared to 10–20% in the NRQM prediction
- The VDM photoproduction cross section is fixed at σφ(0,W) = 0.22 μb for W = 2.1 GeV
- The VDM slope parameter is bφ = 3.46 GeV⁻², with Rφ = ξ²Q²/Mφ² where ξ² = 0.33
- The proton rms radius is √⟨rp²⟩ = 0.83 fm and the φ meson rms radius is √⟨rφ²⟩ = 0.45 fm
- Quark masses used: Mq = Mu = Md = 330 MeV, Ms = 500 MeV
- The minimum value of bq² (three-momentum transfer squared) in the considered kinematics is about 3.6 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Henley et al. (1994) (HKW) by incorporating relativistic effects through the RHOM, which successfully describes proton form factors over a wide range of momentum transfers where the NRQM fails (above bq² ≥ 0.3 GeV²). The paper also includes calculations for the uud-knockout and its interference with s̄s-knockout, which were argued to be suppressed and omitted in the HKW analysis. The results enable experimental determination of the strangeness content of the proton by distinguishing the qualitatively different t-dependence of the uud- and s̄s-knockout cross sections.
