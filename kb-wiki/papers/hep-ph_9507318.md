# The effects of the kaonic cloud on the neutron electric form factor

> We investigate the effects of mesonic clouds on neutron electric and nucleon strange form factors in the framework of the chiral quark soliton model. We present a mechanism to identify the mesonic clouds and their Yukawa tail in the polarized Dirac sea. We find that, assuming hedgehog structures and semiclassical quantization, the neutron electric and the nucleon strange form factors are noticeably dependent on the value of the mass parameter in the Yukawa tails. A method is described to treat these Yukawa tails correctly. A hybrid way of calculating the neutron electric form factor is presented, which gives operationally both meson fields the proper Yukawa tails. This yields noticeably better agreement with experiment than previous calculations in the chiral quark soliton model and turns out to reproduce the data well.

## Key claim
The neutron electric form factor and nucleon strange form factors are noticeably dependent on the value of the mass parameter in the Yukawa tails of mesonic clouds, and a hybrid method that gives both pion and kaon fields their proper asymptotic Yukawa tails (physical pion mass of 139 MeV and physical kaon mass of 496 MeV) yields noticeably better agreement with experiment than previous calculations in the chiral quark soliton model.

## Method
The authors employ the chiral quark soliton (χQS) model in SU(3) flavor space, derived from the instanton picture of the QCD vacuum, where quarks interact via Goldstone bosons described by the chiral meson field U. They develop a meson expansion technique to identify the contributions of mesonic clouds (pion and kaon fields) from the polarized Dirac sea, and then use a saddle point approximation with hedgehog configurations to calculate the electric form factors. A hybrid method is introduced that operationally gives both meson fields their proper Yukawa tails by treating the asymptotic behavior of the pion field with the physical pion mass and the kaon field with the physical kaon mass.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: m_π = 139 MeV (used as the mass parameter for the pion Yukawa tail)
- Kaon mass: m_K = 496 MeV (used as the mass parameter for the kaon Yukawa tail)
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV (fixed at q² = 0)
- Current quark mass: m_0 = 8 MeV (for up and down quarks)
- Constituent quark mass (coupling mass): M = 420 MeV (canonical value, fixed in range 400-440 MeV by baryon mass-splittings)
- Kaon mass squared reproduced by: m_K² = (496 MeV)²

## Context
This work extends previous SU(2) chiral quark soliton model calculations by Wakamatsu and Górski et al., which found that the electric charge distribution of the neutron is strongly due to Dirac-sea quark polarization but overestimated the neutron electric form factor. The authors also build on SU(3) χQS model calculations by Kim et al., which showed that hidden strangeness plays a significant role but did not agree with experimental data. The paper provides a formalism that bridges the soliton picture (valence quarks plus polarized sea) with the baryon-meson picture (bare nucleon plus pion cloud plus kaon cloud), enabling proper treatment of both pion and kaon Yukawa tails for improved calculation of neutron electric and nucleon strange form factors.
