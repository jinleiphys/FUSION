# Toward a consistent evolution of the quark-gluon plasma and heavy quarks

> Heavy-quark observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, like the nuclear modification factor and the elliptic flow, give insight into the mechanisms of high-momentum suppression and low-momentum thermalization of heavy quarks. Here, we present a global study of these two observables within a coupled approach of the heavy-quark propagation in a realistic fluid dynamical medium, MC@sHQ+EPOS2, and compare to experimental data from RHIC and LHC experiments. The heavy quarks scatter elastically and inelastically with the quasiparticles of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), which are represented consistently with the underlying equation of state. We examine two scenarios: first, we interpret the lattice QCD equation of state as a sum of partonic and hadronic contributions, and second, as a gas of massive partonic quasiparticles. It is observed that independent of their momentum the energy loss of heavy quarks depends strongly on how the lattice QCD equation of state is translated into degrees of freedom of the QGP.

## Key claim

The energy loss of heavy quarks depends strongly on how the lattice QCD equation of state is translated into the degrees of freedom of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), independent of the heavy quark momentum; the paper examines two specific interpretations of the equation of state (a partonic-plus-hadronic parametrization versus a massive quasiparticle gas) and finds that both can reproduce the nuclear modification factor at high transverse momentum but yield different predictions for the elliptic flow at low momentum.

## Method

The authors coupled a Monte-Carlo solution of the Boltzmann equation for heavy quarks (MC@sHQ) to the 3+1 dimensional ideal fluid dynamical evolution of the QGP from the EPOS2 event generator. They computed elastic and inelastic (including gluon radiation with the Landau-Migdal-Pomeranchuk effect) scattering rates of charm quarks with light partons, using pQCD matrix elements with a running coupling and a reduced Debye screening mass. The scattering rates were then reduced by two different factors derived from the lattice QCD equation of state: a temperature-dependent fraction of partonic degrees of freedom from the EPOS parametrization, and a momentum- and temperature-dependent factor from treating the light partons as massive quasiparticles with masses fitted to the lattice entropy density.

## Key numbers

* The EPOS parametrization of the lattice equation of state uses a transition temperature \(T_f = 134.74\) MeV; the fraction of partonic degrees of freedom \(\lambda\) reaches only about 0.84 even at \(T \sim 1\) GeV.
* For the massive quasiparticle scenario, the gluon thermal mass is set to \(m_g = \sqrt{3} m_q\) relative to the quark thermal mass, using the perturbative HTL result.
* At LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV), the global rescaling factor for the cross sections was \(K_{\rm c}^{\rm LHC} = 1.5\) for purely collisional processes and \(K_{\rm c+r}^{\rm LHC} = 0.8\) for collisional+radiative (LPM) processes, chosen to match the \(R_{\rm AA}\) around \(p_T \sim 10\) GeV.
* For the standard massless-parton scenario with a decoupling temperature of \(T_c = 155\) MeV, the \(K\) factors were \(K_c = 1.22\) and \(K_{c+r} = 0.6\) to reproduce \(R_{\rm AA}(p_T \sim 10\,{\rm GeV}) \sim 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the coupled heavy-quark and fluid-dynamical approach of Nahrgang et al. (2013) by replacing the ad-hoc parametrization of hadronic bound states with two consistent interpretations of the lattice QCD equation of state (Borsanyi et al., 2013; Bazavov et al., 2014). It contrasts with the PHSD approach (Cassing, 2009; Song et al., 2015), which uses a microscopic transport treatment of light partons and a temperature-dependent coupling constant, whereas the present model uses an ideal fluid dynamical background and a momentum-transfer-dependent coupling. The results enable future discrimination between equation-of-state interpretations via combined measurements of \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_2\) at low transverse momentum.
