# DSE Perspective on QCD Modeling, Distribution Amplitudes, and Form Factors

> We describe results for the pion distribution amplitude (PDA) at the non-perturbative scale $μ=~$2GeV by projecting the Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave-function onto the light-front and use it to investigate the ultraviolet behavior of the electromagnetic form factor, $F_π(Q^2)$, on the entire domain of spacelike $Q^2$. The significant dilation of this PDA compared to the known asymptotic PDA is a signature of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) on the light front. We investigate the transition region of $Q^2$ where non-perturbative behavior of constituent-like quarks gives way to the partonic-like behavior of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The non-perturbative approach is based on the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework for continuum investigations in QCD. The leading-order, leading-twist perturbative QCD result for $Q^2 F_π(Q^2)$ underestimates the new DSE computation by just 15\% on $Q^2\gtrsim 8\,$GeV$^2$, in stark contrast with the result obtained using the asymptotic PDA.

# DSE Perspective on QCD Modeling, Distribution Amplitudes, and Form Factors

## Key claim
The leading-order, leading-twist perturbative QCD result for Q²Fπ(Q²) underestimates the new DSE computation by just 15% on Q² ≳ 8 GeV², but only when using the non-perturbative pion distribution amplitude (PDA) evaluated at a scale appropriate to the experiment rather than the asymptotic PDA; the asymptotic PDA result underestimates the DSE computation by a much larger factor.

## Method
The pion distribution amplitude at μ = 2 GeV was obtained by projecting the Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction onto the light front using generalized Nakanishi spectral representations for the dressed quark propagator and the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Fifty moments of the PDA were computed from the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework under two truncation schemes: rainbow-ladder (RL) and a DCSB-improved kernel (DB). The pion electromagnetic form factor Fπ(Q²) was then calculated on the entire spacelike domain using the same Nakanishi representations, reducing the computation to algebraic Feynman integrals.

## Key numbers
- The RL result for the PDA at 2 GeV is described by αRL = 0.29 with a₂RL = 0.0029; the DB result has αDB = 0.31 with a₂DB = -0.12.
- The PDA moment ⟨(2x-1)²⟩φπ = 0.27 ± 0.04 was obtained from lattice-QCD (Braun, 2006).
- ωφ²(4 GeV²) = 3.2 and ωφ²(100 GeV²) = 2.0; even at 1 TeV, ωφ² retains about 50% of its value at 2 GeV.
- At Q² = 4 GeV², the asymptotic expression yields Q²Fπ = 0.15, a factor of 2.7 smaller than the empirical value 0.41⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀·₀₃ at Q² = 2.45 GeV².
- The dynamical quark mass dressing yields a reduction of about 64% near the turnover region around Q² ∼ 8 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the DSE-based calculation of the pion form factor by Maris and Tandy (2000), which could not address Q² > 4 GeV², to arbitrarily large Q² using new methods (Chang, 2013). It contradicts the assumption that the asymptotic PDA becomes accurate at Q² ∼ 10 GeV², showing instead that DCSB-induced dilation of the PDA persists to remarkably high scales (μ ≳ 100 GeV). The results enable next-generation experiments (E12-06-101) to test the transition region where non-perturbative constituent-like quark behavior gives way to partonic QCD, and they unify the description of the pion's electromagnetic form factor and its valence-quark distribution amplitude within a single DSE interaction kernel.
