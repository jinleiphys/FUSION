# Dependence of spin induced structural transitions on level density and Neutron emission spectra

> The impact of spin induced deformation and shape phase transitions on nuclear level density and consequently on neutron emission spectra of the decay of compound nuclear systems 112^Ru to 123^Cs (N = 68 isotones) is investigated in a microscopic framework of Statistical theory of superfluid nuclei. Our calculations are in good accord with experimental data for evaporation residue of 119^Sb^* and 185^Re^* and show a strong correlation between spin induced structural transitions and NLD. We find that the inverse level density parameter K increases with increasing spin for all the systems, but it decreases with a deformation or a shape change that results in the enhancement of level density and emission probability. A sharp shape phase transition from oblate to uncommon prolate non-collective in well deformed nuclei leads to band crossing and enhancement of level density which fades away while approaching sphericity at or near shell closure manifesting shell effects.

# Dependence of spin induced structural transitions on level density and Neutron emission spectra

## Key claim

The inverse level density parameter K increases with increasing spin for all studied systems, but it decreases at specific spin values where a deformation or shape transition occurs, leading to enhanced level density and emission probability; this explains the decreasing K with spin reported in recent experimental works, which the abstract attributes to structural changes rather than a general trend.

## Method

The author computes deformation, shape, nuclear level density, and neutron emission probability for the decay of excited compound nuclear systems using a microscopic framework of the Statistical theory of superfluid nuclei, incorporating temperature, collective and non-collective rotation degrees of freedom. The equilibrium deformation and shape are determined by minimizing the free energy F = E - TS with respect to intrinsic shape parameters (beta, gamma) for N=68 isotones from 112Ru to 123Cs at excitation energies around 31 MeV. Neutron emission spectra are calculated using the standard statistical model expression relating the neutron yield to the level density of the residual nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For 112Ru, the inverse level density parameter K drops from 9.14 MeV at M = 14 hbar (oblate) to 7.67 MeV at M = 16 hbar (prolate non-collective) due to a sharp deformation shape phase transition.
- The study covers angular momentum M from 0 to 40 hbar for N=68 isotones from 112Ru to 123Cs at an excitation energy of approximately 31 MeV.
- For 119Sb*, the nuclear level density and neutron emission spectra are calculated at excitation energy E* = 31 MeV for angular momenta M = 12.5, 15, 15.5, and 19.5 hbar.
- The outgoing neutron energy En is varied from 0 to 8 MeV in the emission spectra calculations.
- The shape phase transition from oblate to prolate non-collective is observed at temperatures T > 1 MeV for neutron-rich stable nuclei 112Ru, 113Rh, 114Pd, 115Ag, and 116Cd.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous microscopic calculations (Aggarwal, 2015) that showed increasing K with angular momentum, and addresses conflicting experimental results: Kaushik (2015) reported decreasing K with spin for 119Sb*, Gohil (2015) found nearly constant K for 185Re*, and Balaram (2014) observed decreasing K for 97Tc. The study provides a unified explanation by showing that structural transitions at specific spin values cause local decreases in K, and it enables future experimental measurements with wider angular momentum ranges to validate the correlation between shape transitions and level density enhancement. The observation of the prolate non-collective shape phase in neutron-rich stable nuclei at T > 1 MeV is reported for the first time, having previously been seen only in proton-rich nuclei at lower temperatures (Aggarwal, 2013; Aggarwal, 2014).
