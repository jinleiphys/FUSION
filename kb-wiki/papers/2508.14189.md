# The role of the pion mass on the QCD phase diagram in the $T-eB$ plane

> We investigated the role of the pion mass on the QCD phase diagram in the $T-eB$ plane using an effective model treatment. Such treatments are able to capture the main features predicted by first-principles calculations. We also employed the model to estimate the pion mass beyond which the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect disappears. The value is found to be independent of the strength of the magnetic field.

## Key claim

Using a nonlocal Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a running coupling, the authors estimate a specific pion mass value beyond which the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect disappears in the QCD phase diagram; this threshold value is found to be independent of the magnetic field strength, within the model's uncertainty.

## Method

The authors employ a 2-flavor nonlocal NJL model with a form factor that introduces a running of the coupling constant with energy. They compute the chiral condensate as a function of temperature for several fixed pion masses (135, 220, 340, and 440 MeV) under external magnetic fields, and extract the chiral crossover temperature from the condensate behavior. By comparing the condensate at different magnetic field strengths, they identify the pion mass at which the ordering of condensate curves (the signature of IMC) reverses.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass values tested: 135, 220, 340, and 440 MeV, corresponding to the four panels of condensate plots.
* The IMC effect disappears at a pion mass value indicated by a green band in the right panel of Figure 2; the exact numerical value is not given in the provided text, but the band represents the model's uncertainty.
* The threshold pion mass is stated to be independent of the magnetic field strength.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD findings by D'Elia et al. (2018) and Endrodi (2019), who showed that the IMC effect depends on the pion mass and can disappear for sufficiently heavy pions. The authors previously demonstrated (Ali, 2024) that a nonlocal NJL model with a running coupling can qualitatively capture this behavior. The result enables future effective-model studies of QCD phase structure at unphysical quark masses and provides a benchmark for comparing model predictions with lattice QCD at heavier-than-physical pion masses.
