# Alpha scattering and capture reactions in the A = 7 system at low energies

> Differential cross sections for $^3$He-$α$ scattering were measured in the energy range up to 3 MeV. These data together with other available experimental results for $^3$He $+ α$ and $^3$H $+ α$ scattering were analyzed in the framework of the optical model using double-folded potentials. The optical potentials obtained were used to calculate the astrophysical S-factors of the capture reactions $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be and $^3$H$(α,γ)^7$Li, and the branching ratios for the transitions into the two final $^7$Be and $^7$Li bound states, respectively. For $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be excellent agreement between calculated and experimental data is obtained. For $^3$H$(α,γ)^7$Li a $S(0)$ value has been found which is a factor of about 1.5 larger than the adopted value. For both capture reactions a similar branching ratio of $R = σ(γ_1)/σ(γ_0) \approx 0.43$ has been obtained.

## Key claim

For the reaction $^3$H$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Li, the calculated astrophysical S-factor at zero energy is $S(0) = 0.100$ keV b, which is a factor of about 1.5 larger than the adopted value of 0.064 keV b from Fowler (1967), but the authors note that this value is in excellent agreement with several microscopic resonating group method calculations.

## Method

Differential cross sections for $^3$He-$^4$He elastic scattering were measured at 20 energies from 1 to 3.3 MeV using a windowless gas target system. These data, together with existing $^3$He-$^4$He and $^3$H-$^4$He scattering data, were analyzed in the optical model using double-folded potentials derived from nuclear charge distributions folded with a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction. The resulting parity-dependent optical potentials were then used as cluster-cluster potentials in a direct capture model to calculate the astrophysical S-factors for $^3$He$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Be and $^3$H$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Li without adjusting any parameters to the capture reaction data.

## Key numbers

- $^3$He$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Be: $S(0) = 0.516$ keV b, with $\dot{S}(0) = -3.67 \times 10^{-4}$ b (E in keV), from linear extrapolation to zero energy
- $^3$H$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Li: $S(0) = 0.100$ keV b, with $\dot{S}(0) = -1.02 \times 10^{-4}$ b (E in keV), from linear extrapolation to zero energy
- Branching ratio $R = \sigma(\gamma_1)/\sigma(\gamma_0) \approx 0.43$ for both capture reactions, nearly energy-independent in the range 0-1.4 MeV
- Volume integrals per nucleon pair for $^3$He-$^4$He: $J_R = 469.0$ MeV fm$^3$ (even partial waves) and $J_R = 595.6$ MeV fm$^3$ (odd partial waves)
- Volume integrals per nucleon pair for $^3$H-$^4$He: $J_R = 466.8$ MeV fm$^3$ (even partial waves) and $J_R = 578.5$ MeV fm$^3$ (odd partial waves)
- Calculated rms charge radius of $^7$Li: $< r^2 >^{1/2} \approx 2.40$ fm

## Context

This work extends the direct capture model with double-folded potentials to the A=7 system, building on earlier potential model calculations by Tombrello and Parker (1963), Kim et al. (1981), and Buck et al. (1985), as well as microscopic resonating group method calculations by Kajino and Arima (1984), Mertelmeier and Hofmann (1986), Langanke (1986), and Altmeyer et al. (1988). For $^3$He$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Be, the result confirms the adopted S-factor value used by Bahcall (1989) for solar neutrino flux calculations. For $^3$H$(\alpha,\gamma)^7$Li, the larger S(0) value challenges the standard adopted value from Fowler (1967) used in big bang nucleosynthesis models and supports the microscopic RGM calculations that predict $S(0) \approx 0.1$ keV b. The parity-dependent nature of the optical potential is shown to be essential for reproducing the experimental capture data.
