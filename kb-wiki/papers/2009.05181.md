# Strangeness-changing Rates and Hyperonic Bulk Viscosity in Neutron Star Mergers

> In this paper we present a computation of the rates of strangeness-changing processes and the resultant bulk viscosity in matter at the densities and temperatures typical of neutron star mergers. To deal with the high temperature in this environment we go beyond the Fermi surface approximation in our rate calculations and numerically evaluate the full phase space integral. We include processes where quarks move between baryons via meson exchange: these have generally been omitted in previous analyses but provide the dominant contribution to the rates of strangeness-changing processes and the bulk viscosity. The calculation of these rates is an essential step towards any calculation of dissipation mechanisms in hyperonic matter in mergers. As one application, we calculate the dissipation times for density oscillations at the frequencies seen in merger simulations. We find that hyperon bulk viscosity for temperatures in the MeV regime can probably be neglected in this context, but becomes highly relevant for keV-range temperatures.

## Key claim

The authors compute strangeness-changing rates and hyperonic bulk viscosity in neutron star merger conditions, going beyond the Fermi surface approximation and including one-meson exchange contributions that have generally been omitted in previous analyses. They find that hyperon bulk viscosity for temperatures in the MeV regime can probably be neglected in this context, but becomes highly relevant for keV-range temperatures.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the full twelve-dimensional phase space integral for four non-leptonic strangeness-changing weak processes (n+n ↔ p+Σ⁻, n+p ↔ p+Λ, n+n ↔ n+Λ, Λ+Λ ↔ Λ+n) using a relativistic mean field equation of state (PK1+H) extended to include Λ and Σ⁻ hyperons. They include both one-meson exchange contributions, where the weak W-boson exchange occurs inside one baryon and a strong interaction with the second baryon is modeled via meson exchange, and contact interaction contributions where a W boson is exchanged directly between baryons. The bulk viscosity is derived from the deviation from beta equilibrium during harmonic density oscillations at frequencies around 1 kHz.

## Key numbers

* The PK1+H equation of state allows neutron stars up to a maximum mass of 1.88 M⊙, putting it at the 3σ edge of compatibility with current constraints (Mmax ≥ 1.928 ± 0.017 M⊙ from Fonseca (2016) and Mmax ≥ 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ from Antoniadis (2013))
* Saturation density for PK1+H is n₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³
* The Fermi constant used is GF = 1.1663787 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
* Central densities in merging neutron stars are estimated to exceed two times saturation density (n₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³)
* Temperatures in merger simulations can reach tens of MeV, with some simulations predicting up to T ≈ 100 MeV
* Density oscillations have central frequencies of around 1 kHz
* The GM1'B hyperonic EOS has Mmax = 2.02 M⊙ and predicts a maximum bulk viscosity comparable to PK1+H in the relevant density and temperature range

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of hyperonic bulk viscosity that were performed exclusively at low (keV-range) temperatures, often in the context of r-mode instability studies by Jones (2001), Lindblom and Owen (2001), and van Dalen (2003). The authors go beyond the Fermi surface approximation used in those works and include one-meson exchange contributions, which van Dalen (2003) and Ofengeim (2019) showed dominate the rates even at low temperatures. The calculation enables future studies of dissipation mechanisms in hyperonic matter in neutron star mergers, including cooling, thermal transport, and phase conversion dissipation as studied by Alford (2014).
