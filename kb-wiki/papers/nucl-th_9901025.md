# Derivation of a Quark Confinement Potential from QCD

> A quark confinement potential is derived from QCD Lagrangial under the two assumptions in the energy region relevant to quark confinement. The possibility of perturbation in QCD is assumed, and the momentum of the gluon transfered by the quark is larger than the mass of the internal quark, so that the expansion of ${m^2 /{q^2}}$ is assumed to be reliable. This potential comes from the one loop diagrams in the expansion of $m^2 /{q^2}$, especially here, the vacuum polarization diagram is examined. This explains the quark confinement mechanism on the basis of QCD and the Regge trajectories which is governed by a linear potential.

## Key claim

A linear quark confinement potential can be derived from the QCD Lagrangian via one-loop vacuum polarization diagrams, but only under the assumptions that perturbation theory is applicable in the confinement energy region and that the gluon momentum transfer is larger than the internal quark mass, allowing an expansion in \(m^2/q^2\) rather than the usual \(q^2/m^2\) expansion used in QED.

## Method

The author calculates the one-loop vacuum polarization diagram in QCD, obtaining the finite part of the gluon self-energy \(\Pi_{\text{finite}}(q^2)\). This result is expanded in the small parameter \(-4m^2/q^2\) (the \(m^2/q^2\) expansion), and the modified gluon propagator is Fourier-transformed to configuration space, yielding an effective potential for a quark interacting with the remainder of the hadron. The resulting potential includes a Coulomb term, a Uehling-type logarithmic term, and a linear term proportional to \(r\).

## Key numbers

- The linear confinement potential coefficient from the derived potential is \(k = \frac{c_F \alpha_s^2}{2\pi} \sum_f m_f^2\), where the sum runs over quark flavors satisfying \(-4m_f^2/q^2 < 1\).
- For the Regge trajectory slope \(\alpha' = 0.93\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (from baryon resonances), the corresponding string tension is \(k = 0.85\ \text{GeV fm}^{-1}\).
- For a typical current quark mass \(m_q = 10\ \text{MeV}\), the estimated strong coupling constant is \(\alpha_s = 13.35\) and the gluon momentum is \(q = 66.74\ \text{MeV}\).
- The running coupling constants are estimated as \(\alpha_s(201.72\ \text{MeV}) = 0.988\) and \(\alpha_s(836.74\ \text{MeV}) = 0.451\).
- The expansion parameters \(m^2/q^2\) at the threshold energies of \(\pi_0\) and \(\rho_0\) are 0.0025 and 0.00014, respectively.
- The lightest quark mass is constrained to be less than 95.4 MeV from the condition \(v_q^{\text{rel}} < 1\) using the \(\pi_0\) mass.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between the Lamb shift in QED and quark confinement in QCD, arguing that the essential difference is the magnitude of the coupling constant rather than the Abelian versus non-Abelian nature of the theories. The derived linear potential matches the string model (Perkins) and explains Regge trajectories. The author contrasts this result with the nucleon-nucleon potential, which has a large coupling constant but no confinement due to the pion mass. The paper enables a perturbative QCD-based derivation of confinement, suggesting that quark masses should be treated as parameters obtained from hadronic spectra by solving the Dirac equation.
