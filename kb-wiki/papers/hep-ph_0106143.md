# Excitation of Nucleon Resonances

> I discuss developments in the area of nucleon resonance excitations that are necessary to bring our understanding of nucleon structure in the regime of strong QCD to a qualitatively new level. They involve the collection of high quality data in various channels, a more rigorous approach in the search for "missing" quark model states, an effort to compute some critical quantities in nucleon resonance excitations from first principles, i.e. QCD, and a proposal aimed at obtaining an understanding of a fundamental quantity in nucleon structure.

# Excitation of Nucleon Resonances

## Key claim

The paper argues that a comprehensive understanding of nucleon structure in the strong-QCD regime requires high-quality data across multiple channels, a rigorous search for "missing" quark-model states, first-principles QCD computations of resonance quantities, and a focused effort to describe the Q² evolution of the generalized Bjorken integral from small to large distances. The hedge is that this represents a *proposal* for what the community should achieve, not a claim of having done so; the author states that describing the spin integral from fundamental theory "would be a milestone" if successful, and that the Roper resonance's structure "is basically still unknown" more than 35 years after its discovery.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical progress in electromagnetic production of mesons for nucleon resonance excitation, focusing on the γNΔ(1232) transition, the second resonance region (1.4–1.6 GeV), and the search for missing baryon resonances predicted by the symmetric quark model. It discusses multipole analyses (R_EM = E₁₊/M₁₊ and R_SM = S₁₊/M₁₊), comparisons with lattice QCD, constituent quark models, and chiral perturbation theory, and proposes a five-year project to measure the Q² evolution of the first moment of the spin structure function g₁ for the proton-neutron difference.

## Key numbers

- R_EM = E₁₊/M₁₊ for the γNΔ(1232) transition: experimental value (-2.75 ± 0.50)% at the photon point; the only lattice QCD "prediction" gives (3 ± 8)%.
- The second resonance region spans the mass range 1.4 to 1.6 GeV, containing the N(1440)P₁₁ (Roper), N*(1535)S₁₁, and N*(1520)D₁₃ states.
- The Roper resonance mass predicted by the non-relativistic constituent quark model is above 1600 MeV, while its experimental mass is 1440 MeV.
- Gluonic hybrid states are predicted by bag models and QCD sum rules at masses around 1.5 GeV, and by the flux tube model at 1.8–1.9 GeV.
- The proposed Q² range for the spin integral measurement is 0.1–1.0 GeV² from Jefferson Lab experiments, with deep inelastic data available for Q² > 1.5 GeV².
- The Bjorken sum rule has been verified experimentally within 5–10%.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on nucleon resonance electroproduction and structure functions, building on prior experimental programs at GRAAL, Jefferson Lab (CLAS), ELSA (Crystal Barrel), Spring-8, and BEPC. It directly addresses the "missing" baryon resonance problem from the symmetric quark model of Isgur and Karl, and the search for gluonic excitations (hybrid baryons) predicted by bag models, flux tube models, and QCD sum rules. The proposed project to measure the Q² evolution of the generalized Bjorken integral would connect the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule at the photon point with perturbative QCD at high Q², using chiral perturbation theory (NLO HBChPT) and lattice QCD to bridge the intermediate region. This would enable the first description of a fundamental nucleon structure quantity from fundamental theory across all distance scales.
