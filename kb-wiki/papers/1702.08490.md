# Induced fission of 240Pu

> We study the fission dynamics of 240Pu within an implementation of the Density Functional Theory (DFT) extended to superfluid systems and real-time dynamics. We demonstrate the critical role played by the pairing correlations. The evolution is found to be much slower than previously expected in this fully non-adiabatic treatment of nuclear dynamics, where there are no symmetry restrictions and all collective degrees of freedom (CDOF) are allowed to participate in the dynamics.

# Induced fission of 240Pu

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that pairing correlations are critical for enabling fission dynamics in a fully non-adiabatic, symmetry-unrestricted time-dependent density functional theory treatment; without dynamically included pairing, the nucleus will not fission starting from the outer saddle, and with normal pairing strength the saddle-to-scission evolution is much slower (approximately 10,000 fm/c) than previously predicted.

## Method

The authors performed real-time simulations of induced fission of 240Pu using a time-dependent extension of density functional theory for superfluid systems, with the SLy4 parametrization for the normal energy density functional and a pairing functional from PRL (2003a). The nucleus was initialized near the outer fission barrier deformation, corresponding to an equivalent neutron energy of about 1.5 MeV in the 239Pu(n,f) reaction, and evolved on a 25^2 × 50 fm^3 spatial lattice with 256,000 coupled 3D time-dependent nonlinear complex partial differential equations for approximately 320,000 time steps. To demonstrate the role of pairing, the authors also performed a simulation with artificially enhanced pairing strength.

## Key numbers

- Saddle-to-scission time with normal pairing strength: approximately 14,000 fm/c (from figure caption).
- Saddle-to-scission time with enhanced pairing strength: approximately 1,400 fm/c, about ten times faster than with normal pairing.
- Simulation used 256,000 coupled 3D time-dependent complex partial differential equations on a 25^2 × 50 fm^3 spatial lattice.
- Simulation ran for about 320,000 time steps using 512 GPUs in about 47 hours, or 1602 GPUs in about 24 hours.
- Lattice constant corresponds to a cutoff momentum of approximately 500 MeV/c.
- The initial state corresponds to an equivalent neutron energy in the reaction 239Pu(n,f) of about 1.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent superfluid density functional theory approach previously developed by the same group (Bulgac, 2016) and demonstrates the first successful microscopic simulation of an actinide fissioning with a realistic energy density functional. The results contradict earlier predictions of much shorter saddle-to-scission times (PhysRevC, 1976; Bulgac, 2015) and show that previous microscopic approaches based on a limited number of collective degrees of freedom (such as those in PhysRevC, 2014) fail because they cannot capture the essential role of pairing correlations in redistributing single-particle occupation probabilities. The approach enables future microscopic studies of fission fragment properties including mass and charge distributions, total kinetic energy, and excitation energies without relying on phenomenological collective models.
