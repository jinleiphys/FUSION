# Regge-plus-resonance treatment of the p(gamma,K^+)Sigma^0 and p(gamma,K^0)Sigma^+ reactions at forward kaon angles

> An effective-Lagrangian framework for K Sigma photoproduction from the proton is presented. The proposed model is applicable at forward kaon angles and photon lab energies from threshold up to 16 GeV. The high-energy part of the p(gamma,K^+)Sigma^0 and p(gamma,K^0)Sigma^+ amplitudes is expressed in terms of Regge-trajectory exchange in the t channel. By supplementing this Regge background with a number of s-channel resonances, the model is extended towards the resonance region. The resulting ``Regge-plus-resonance'' (RPR) approach has the advantage that the background contributions involve only a few parameters, which can be largely constrained by the high-energy data. This work compares various implementations of the RPR model, and explores which resonance contributions are required to fit the data presently at hand. It is demonstrated that, through the inclusion of one K and two K* trajectories, the RPR framework provides an efficient and unified description of the K^+ Sigma^0 and K^0 Sigma^+ photoproduction channels over an extensive energy range.

## Key claim

The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework, which combines Regge-trajectory exchange in the t-channel with a limited set of s-channel nucleon and delta resonances, can provide a unified description of both the p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 and p(gamma,K0)Sigma+ photoproduction channels from threshold up to 16 GeV, but only if a second K* trajectory (with first materialization at 1414 MeV) is added to the K0Sigma+ amplitude to compensate for the absence of the K(494) exchange contribution in that channel.

## Method

The high-energy forward-angle amplitudes for both reactions were constructed using Regge propagators for the K(494) and K*(892) trajectories, with coupling constants constrained by high-energy data for the K+Sigma0 channel and by SU(3) flavor symmetry relations. For the K0Sigma+ channel, where the K-exchange diagram vanishes due to the neutral kaon charge, an additional K*(1410) trajectory was introduced based on a constituent-quark model calculation of the kaon spectrum. The model was extended to the resonance region by adding s-channel nucleon and delta resonances with Gaussian form factors that suppress their contributions at high energies.

## Key numbers

- Photon lab energy range: from threshold up to 16 GeV
- K(494) trajectory slope: 0.70 GeV^-2
- K*(892) trajectory slope: 0.85 GeV^-2
- K*(1410) trajectory slope: 0.83 GeV^-2 (from Bonn constituent-quark model variant A)
- K*(1410) first materialization mass: 1414 MeV (PDG value)
- SU(3) constraint on the strong coupling: 0.9 ≤ g_{K+Sigma0 p} / sqrt(4π) ≤ 1.3
- High-energy data for K+Sigma0: 48 differential cross section points at omega_lab = 5, 8, 11, and 16 GeV (Boyarski 1969), plus 8 photon beam asymmetry points at 16 GeV (Qui 1979)
- The four retained Regge model variants for K+Sigma0 all yield chi-squared values between 1.25 and 1.31 when fitted to the high-energy data
- The K0Sigma+ cross sections predicted by direct isospin conversion from the K+Sigma0 parameters overshoot the experimental data by approximately a factor of 10

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance approach previously applied to p(gamma,K+)Lambda photoproduction (Corthals 2005) to the KSigma channels, which are particularly interesting because delta resonances can participate unlike in Lambda production. The paper addresses a known difficulty in isobar models (Mart and Bennhold 1995, Stijn 2003) where reconciling the K+Sigma0 and K0Sigma+ channels required reducing the g_{KSigma p} coupling below SU(3) predictions or introducing destructive interferences; the RPR approach resolves this by adding a second K* trajectory based on constituent-quark model predictions from the Bonn group (2004). The results enable future analyses of new double-polarization and electroproduction data from CLAS, LEPS, and GRAAL collaborations.
