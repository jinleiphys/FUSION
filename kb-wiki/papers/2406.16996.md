# Study of Heavy Quarkonia in the presence of magnetic field by Nikiforov Uvarov method

> The N-dimensional radial Schrodinger equation has been solved using the Nikiforov Uvarov (NU) method, in which we used the medium modified form of Cornell potential and quasi-particle Debye mass with strong magnetic field background. The binding energies and the mass spectra of heavy quarkonium have been studied in the N-dimensional space for different values of magnetic field, the binding energy decreases with increasing magnetic field, which shows early dissociation of heavy quarkonium system. The influence of dimensionality number has also been discussed on binding energies of J/ψ and Υ for fixed value of magnetic field. It is found that with an increase in dimensionality, the binding energy starts decreasing from a higher initial value. The results obtained are quite consistent with recent studies.

## Key claim

The binding energy of heavy quarkonia decreases with increasing magnetic field, indicating earlier dissociation, but this result depends on the specific approximation scheme (binomial expansion around a characteristic radius) used to solve the N-dimensional Schrodinger equation with the medium-modified Cornell potential; the authors state their results are "quite consistent with recent studies" without claiming exact quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors solved the N-dimensional radial Schrodinger equation using the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method, applying a medium-modified Cornell potential that includes Coulombic, linear, and quadratic terms. The magnetic field effect was incorporated through a quasi-particle Debye mass that depends on both temperature and magnetic field strength. A binomial expansion approximation was applied around a characteristic radius parameter delta to linearize the equation, yielding an analytic expression for the binding energy and mass spectra of charmonium and bottomonium states.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quarkonia using the medium-modified Cornell potential, which had previously been solved with the supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) method by the same group (Sharma et al., 2023). It also builds on studies of magnetic field effects on quarkonia transport properties (Singh et al., 2023; Dey et al., 2023) and on the Debye mass formulation in strong magnetic fields (Dey et al., 2023). The results enable comparison with experimental mass spectra from the Particle Data Group and with other theoretical approaches such as the asymptotic iteration method and Laplace transformation method, providing a framework for studying quarkonium dissociation in the early universe or heavy-ion collision conditions where magnetic fields up to 0.3 GeV^2 are relevant.
