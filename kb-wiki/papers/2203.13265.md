# Hard probe path lengths and event-shape engineering of the quark-gluon plasma

> As particles traverse the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed during a heavy ion collision they undergo energy loss depending on the distance travelled. We study several temperature- and velocity-weighted path length distributions of non-interacting particles as they traverse the plasma using the Trajectum heavy ion code, including those of back-to-back path lengths. We use event-shape engineering (ESE) in combination with in-plane versus out-of-plane selection to accurately control these path lengths. Lastly, we show how soft observables depend on the different ESE classes.

## Key claim

The study shows that event-shape engineering (ESE) combined with in-plane versus out-of-plane selection can significantly control path lengths of hard probes traversing the quark-gluon plasma, but only when considering the direction of the probe relative to the event plane; ESE alone does not modify average path lengths when averaging over all emission angles.

## Method

The authors used the Trajectum heavy ion code (version 1.2) to generate 2.2 million hydrodynamic events at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, with boost-invariant initial conditions from a modified T RENTo model and second-order hydrodynamics. They computed path length distributions of non-interacting probes by generating lines from each nucleon-nucleon collision point outward at midrapidity until reaching the freeze-out surface at T = 150.1 MeV, and evaluated temperature-weighted and fluid-velocity-weighted integrals along these paths. Events were classified by centrality in 1% wide bins and by the reduced flow vector q_n for ESE selection.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature set to T = 150.1 MeV
- Hydrodynamic initialization time tau_hyd = 0.370 fm/c
- Path lengths show a sharp cut-off around 12 fm for 0-5% centrality
- The factor 1/gamma reduces path lengths by about an extra 10% beyond fluid-flow weighting
- Ratio of mean out-of-plane to in-plane path lengths reaches up to 2.5 for peripheral collisions with high q_2 selection and 11 degree Delta phi cuts
- For central collisions, the ratio is about 1.1 for the same selections
- Back-to-back shortest-to-longest path length ratio is between 0.4 and 0.5 for events up to about 50% centrality
- Average number of binary collisions varies by about 2-3% with ESE selection

## Context

This work extends studies of parton energy loss using in-plane versus out-of-plane selection at RHIC (STAR 2004, PHENIX 2006) and the LHC (2015-2021), and applies the ESE technique from Schukraft et al. (2013) to path length distributions. The results enable experimental measurements of nuclear modification factors for jets as a function of q_2 and emission angle classes, potentially clarifying the consistent underprediction of high-p_T v_2 found in many models. The authors note that fluctuations in energy loss are as important as path length variations for dijets, following findings from Milhano et al. (2016) and others.
