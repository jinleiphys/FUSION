# On Collective Properties of Dense QCD Matter

> A short review of the two recently analyzed collective effects in dense non-Abelian matter, the photon and dilepton production in nonequilibrium glasma and polarization properties of turbulent Abelian and non-Abelian plasmas, is given.

## Key claim

This review argues that collective effects in dense non-Abelian matter, specifically photon and dilepton production from nonequilibrium glasma and polarization properties of turbulent plasmas, can be described within a kinetic framework that accounts for over-occupied gluon states and stochastic turbulent fields, but the precise relation between glasma instabilities and low effective viscosity remains unclear.

## Method

The authors review two separate analyses: (1) a kinetic model of glasma evolution based on a transient gluon condensate with a parametrized gluon distribution function, from which photon and dilepton production rates are computed by integrating over time-dependent scales \(\Lambda_s\) and \(\Lambda\); (2) a Vlasov-based linear response calculation for turbulent QED and QCD plasmas, where stochastic turbulent fields are treated as Gaussian ensembles and the polarization tensor is expanded in gradients of the turbulent correlation length \(l\).

## Key numbers

- Photon production yield: \(dN_{\gamma}/dy d^2k_T = \alpha R_0^2 N_{part}^{2/3} (Q_{sat}/k_T)^\eta\) with \(\eta = (9-3\delta)/(1+2\delta)\), where \(\delta\) is the pressure anisotropy parameter between 0 and 1/3.
- Dilepton production from quark annihilation: \(dN_{DY}/dy dM^2 \sim \alpha^2 R_0^{\prime 2} N_{part}^{2/3} (Q_{sat}/M)^\eta\) with \(\eta = 4(3-\delta)/(1+2\delta)\).
- Dilepton production from three-gluon condensate decay: \(\eta^{\prime}_{perturbative} = 9(3-\delta)/(5+3\delta)\).
- Thermalization time: \(t_{therm} \sim t_0 (1/\alpha_s)^{7/(3-\delta)}\).
- Turbulent plasma frequency shifts: \((\omega^{\rm turb}_{\rm pl\;L})^{2} = \omega_{\rm pl}^{2} - (e^4 l^2 / 6\pi^2)[(16/3)\langle E^2\rangle + (8/3)\langle B^2\rangle]\); \((\omega^{\rm turb}_{\rm pl\;T})^{2} = \omega_{\rm pl}^{2} - (e^4 l^2 / 6\pi^2)[(128/15)\langle E^2\rangle + (8/3)\langle B^2\rangle]\).
- Critical value for transverse magnetic turbulent contribution: \(x^* \approx 0.43\) where the sign of \({\rm Im}[\chi_T^{(1)}(x)]\) changes, indicating instability for sufficiently strong turbulent fields.

## Context

This review extends the earlier analysis of Dremin and Leonidov (2010) on dense non-Abelian matter by incorporating recent results on glasma photon and dilepton production from Chiu (2012) and on turbulent polarization from Kirakosyan, Leonidov, and collaborators (2012a, 2012b). The glasma model builds on the kinetic framework of Blaizot (2012) describing a transient gluon condensate, while the turbulent plasma analysis generalizes earlier work on turbulent QED plasma by Tsytovich (1971) and Istomin (1991) to the non-Abelian case. The results enable quantitative comparison with RHIC data from PHENIX and motivate further study of the relation between glasma instabilities, turbulent cascades, and anomalous viscosity in heavy ion collisions.
