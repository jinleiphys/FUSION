# Physics of strong electromagnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> I discuss several roles of the strong electromagnetic fields created by relativistic heavy-ion collisions. These phenomena call for theoretical and experimental developments to understand dynamics of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as well as purely electromagnetic processes in the ultraperipheral collisions.

## Key claim

Strong electromagnetic fields created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions influence both hard probes (photons, dileptons, heavy quarks) and the hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, but the magnitude and sign of several predicted effects, such as the charge density induced by the interplay of magnetic field and vorticity, depend on correctly accounting for the orbital angular momentum of Landau levels, which was overlooked in earlier work (Hattori 2016).

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments in quantum field theory, kinetic theory, and relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) applied to heavy-ion collisions. It examines the vacuum polarization tensor in a constant external magnetic field to derive polarization-dependent refractive indices for photons, and analyzes the linear wave spectrum of relativistic MHD with anisotropic first-order transport coefficients. The magnetovortical effect is re-evaluated by computing the induced charge density from a thermodynamic partition function that includes both spin and orbital angular momentum contributions from Landau levels.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths in non-central collisions at RHIC and the LHC reach \(10^{18} - 10^{19}\) Gauss.
- The induced charge density from the magnetovortical effect is given by \(j^0 = - (C_A/2) \, \boldsymbol{\omega} \cdot \mathbf{B}\), where the anomaly coefficient for Dirac fermions is \(C_A = e^2/(2\pi^2)\) and \(\boldsymbol{\omega}\) is the vorticity; the sign is inverted compared to earlier predictions that omitted the orbital contribution.
- The refractive indices for photons in a magnetic field are \(n_\parallel^2 = (1+\chi_0+\chi_1)/(1+\chi_0+\chi_1\cos^2\theta)\) and \(n_\perp^2 = (1+\chi_0)/(1+\chi_0+\chi_2\sin^2\theta)\), where \(\theta\) is the angle between photon momentum and the magnetic field direction.

## Context

This work extends the study of strong-field effects in heavy-ion collisions beyond earlier treatments of vacuum birefringence and dichroism (Toll 1952, Adler 1971) and anomalous transport (Hattori 2016). It corrects the sign of the magnetovortical charge density predicted by Hattori (2016) by including the orbital angular momentum contribution identified by Fukushima (2024). The results enable more accurate predictions for charge-dependent flows observed at the LHC (ALICE 2019) and RHIC (STAR 2023), and connect heavy-ion physics to high-intensity laser physics (Fedotov 2022) and astrophysics (Enoto 2019).
