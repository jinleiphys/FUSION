# The Spectrum of Low-$p_T$ $J/ψ$ in Heavy-Ion Collisions in a Statistical Two-Body Fractal Model

> We establish a statistical two-body fractal (STF) model to study the spectrum of $J/ψ$. $J/ψ$ serves as a reliable probe in heavy-ion collisions. The distribution of $J/ψ$ in hadron gas is influenced by flow, quantum and strong interaction effects. Previous models have predominantly focused on one or two of these effects while neglecting the others, resulting in the inclusion of unconsidered effects in the fitted parameters. Here, we study the issue from a new point of view by analyzing the fact that all three effects induce a self-similarity structure, involving a $J/ψ$-$π$ two-meson state and a $J/ψ$, $π$ two-quark state, respectively. We introduce modification factor $q_{TBS}$ and $q_2$ into the probability and entropy of charmonium. $q_{TBS}$ denotes the modification of self-similarity on $J/ψ$, $q_2$ denotes that of self-similarity and strong interaction between \emph{c }and $\bar{c}$ on quarks. By solving the probability and entropy equations, we derive the values of $q_{TBS}$ and $q_2$ at various collision energies and centralities. Substituting the value of $q_{TBS}$ into distribution function, we successfully obtain the transverse momentum spectrum of low-$p_T$ $J/ψ$, which demonstrates good agreement with experimental data. The STF model can be employed to investigate other mesons and resonance states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum spectrum of low-\(p_T\) \(J/\psi\) in heavy-ion collisions can be described by a statistical two-body fractal (STF) model that unifies flow, quantum, and strong interaction effects through a self-similarity structure, rather than treating these effects separately as in previous models. The key qualifier is that the model is specifically applied to the low-\(p_T\) region and that the modification factors \(q_{TBS}\) and \(q_2\) are derived by solving probability and entropy equations, not from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a statistical two-body fractal model based on the observation that flow, quantum, and strong interaction effects all induce a self-similarity structure in the hadron gas, involving a \(J/\psi\)-\(\pi\) two-meson state and a \(J/\psi\), \(\pi\) two-quark state. They introduce two modification factors: \(q_{TBS}\), which accounts for the modification of self-similarity on the \(J/\psi\), and \(q_2\), which accounts for self-similarity and the strong interaction between the charm quark and anti-charm quark. By solving the probability and entropy equations of the system, the values of \(q_{TBS}\) and \(q_2\) are determined at various collision energies and centralities, and then \(q_{TBS}\) is substituted into the distribution function to obtain the transverse momentum spectrum.

## Context

This work extends previous approaches to \(J/\psi\) production in heavy-ion collisions that focused on only one or two of the effects (flow, quantum, strong interaction) while neglecting others, such as the models by various authors that the paper states "resulted in the inclusion of unconsidered effects in the fitted parameters."
