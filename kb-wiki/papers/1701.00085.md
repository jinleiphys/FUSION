# Anomalous transport and massive gravity theories

> We review the Kubo formulae relevant to study anomalous transport properties of relativistic fluids. We apply this formalism to perform a computation of the transport coefficients in a holographic massive gravity model including vorticity and external electromagnetic fields. We find an interesting phase in which the electric DC conductivity is negligible, while the anomalous conductivities turn out to be nonvanishing.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a holographic massive gravity model in 5 dimensions, there exists a regime where the electric DC conductivity vanishes (insulating behavior) while the anomalous chiral conductivities (chiral magnetic and chiral vortical) remain nonvanishing; importantly, the anomalous conductivities of charge currents are not affected by translational symmetry breaking effects, unlike the DC conductivity.

## Method

The author reviews Kubo formulae for anomalous transport coefficients in relativistic hydrodynamics, then applies them to a holographic massive gravity model in 5 dimensions. The model extends the Einstein-Maxwell action with Chern-Simons terms (to mimic chiral anomalies) and includes Stueckelberg scalar fields with a vacuum expectation value proportional to a parameter k that controls momentum relaxation and translational symmetry breaking. The DC electric conductivity is computed using the method of Donos and Gauntlett (2014), while the anomalous conductivities are extracted from the low-frequency, long-wavelength limits of retarded Green functions via the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key numbers

- DC electric conductivity formula: sigma_DC = r_h (1 - J k^2 / (2 r_h^2)) [1 + (1 - J k^2 / (2 r_h^2)) * (4 mu^2) / (k^2 M(r_h))], where M(r_h) = 1 + (2 J rho^2) / r_h^6, and J is the higher-derivative coupling parameter.
- Chiral magnetic conductivity: sigma^B = kappa * 4 mu, where kappa is the axial anomaly coefficient.
- Chiral vortical conductivity: sigma^V = kappa * 2 mu^2 + lambda * 16 pi^2 T^2, where lambda is the mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly coefficient.
- Chiral magnetic conductivity of energy current: sigma_epsilon^B = kappa * 2 mu^2 + lambda * 16 pi^2 T^2 + lambda * 2 k^2, showing dependence on the momentum relaxation parameter k.
- Chiral vortical conductivity of energy current: sigma_epsilon^V = kappa * (4/3) mu^3 + lambda * 32 pi^2 T^2 mu.
- At zero temperature, the DC conductivity vanishes at a particular value k_* = k(J), producing insulating behavior; this is shown for J values in the range approximately 0 to 0.5 with k/mu up to about 4.

## Context

This work extends the holographic massive gravity models of Baggioli (2016) and Gouteraux (2016) from 4 to 5 dimensions, and adds Chern-Simons terms to study anomalous transport. The results confirm that the chiral magnetic and chiral vortical conductivities of charge currents are universal and match weak-coupling results from Landsteiner (2011) and others, suggesting a non-renormalization theorem. The finding that anomalous conductivities survive when DC conductivity vanishes enables clean experimental study of chiral magnetic and vortical effects in systems with strong disorder, relevant for heavy ion physics and condensed matter systems.
