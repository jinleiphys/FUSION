# Interference Effects for $0νββ$ Decay in the Left-Right Symmetric Model

> Various mechanisms may contribute to neutrinoless double beta decay in the left-right symmetric model. The interference between these mechanisms also contribute to the overall decay rate. The analysis of the contributions of these interference terms is important for disentangling different mechanisms. In the present paper we study interference effects contributing to the decay rate for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay in the left-right symmetric model. The numerical values for maximum interference for several nuclides are calculated. It is observed that, for most of the interference terms, the contribution is smaller than 20$\%$ for all the nuclei considered in the study. However, the interference between the mass mechanisms (light and heavy) and $η$ mechanism is observed to be in the range 30$\%$-50$\%$. The variation of the interference effect with the $Q$ values is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that interference between different mechanisms contributing to neutrinoless double-beta decay in the left-right symmetric model is generally smaller than 20% for most mechanism pairs, but the interference between the mass mechanisms (light and heavy neutrino exchange) and the η mechanism reaches 30%-50% for all nuclei studied, depending on the Q-value of the decay.

## Method

The authors derived the inverse half-life formula for neutrinoless double-beta decay in the left-right symmetric model including four contributing mechanisms: the standard light-neutrino mass mechanism, heavy right-handed neutrino exchange, the λ mechanism, and the η mechanism. They computed the nuclear matrix elements and phase-space factors for six nuclei of experimental interest, then evaluated the six possible interference terms between pairs of mechanisms, studying how the interference varies with the Q-value of the decay.

## Key numbers

- For most interference terms, the contribution is smaller than 20% for all nuclei considered in the study.
- The interference between the mass mechanisms (light and heavy) and the η mechanism is in the range 30%-50%.
- The phase-space factor normalization constant is g^0ν = 2.8 × 10^(-22) yr^(-1).
- The axial-vector coupling constant is taken as g_A = 1.27.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in Ahmed and Horoi (2017), which studied interference between the standard mass mechanism and heavy right-handed neutrino exchange and found contributions no larger than 12% for all nuclei considered. The present paper extends the interference study to all relevant pairs of mechanisms in the left-right symmetric model for six nuclei of current experimental interest, enabling future experimental analyses to disentangle which mechanism or combination of mechanisms drives neutrinoless double-beta decay.
