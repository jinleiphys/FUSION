# Chiral symmetry breaking in a semi-localized magnetic field

> In this work, I'm going to explore the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking and restoration in a solvable magnetic distribution within Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The special semi-localized static magnetic field can roughly simulate the realistic situation in peripheral heavy ion collisions, thus the study is important for the dynamical evolution of quark matter. I find that the magnetic field dependent contribution from discrete spectra usually dominates over that from continuum ones and chiral symmetry breaking is locally catalyzed by both the magnitude and scale of the magnetic field.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry breaking is locally catalyzed by both the magnitude and the spatial scale of a semi-localized magnetic field, with the discrete-spectrum contribution dominating over the continuum contribution; the study uses a solvable magnetic field distribution \(B(x)=B\,{\rm sech}^2(x/\lambda)\) to approximate the inhomogeneous field in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, but the weak-field approximation and constant-mass-gap ansatz limit the direct applicability to realistic collision conditions.

## Method

The author formulates the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors in a static magnetic field \(B(x)=B\,{\rm sech}^2(x/\lambda)\) along the \(z\) direction, using the vector potential \(A_\mu=(0,0,-B\lambda\tanh(x/\lambda),0)\). The thermodynamic potential is computed by separating contributions from discrete bound states (exact eigenenergies of the Dirac equation in this magnetic field) and from continuum states; the discrete part is shown to dominate. For the spatially varying chiral condensate, a weak-field Taylor expansion to second order in \(m(x)\) is performed, reducing the integral gap equation to an algebraic equation for the local mass fluctuation \(m(x)\). Numerical results are obtained in both \(2+1\) and \(3+1\) dimensions, with parameters fixed by vacuum observables in the \(3+1\) case.

## Key numbers

* In \(3+1\) dimensions, the NJL model parameters are fixed to \(G=4.93~{\rm GeV}^{-2}\), \(\Lambda=0.653~{\rm GeV}\), and \(m_0=5~{\rm MeV}\) by fitting \(m_\pi=134~{\rm MeV}\), \(f_\pi=93~{\rm MeV}\), and \(\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle=-2\times (0.25~{\rm GeV})^3\).
* For the ratio \(\Delta\Omega_c^t/\Omega_b^t\) at \(\mu=0\), \(T=0.15~{\rm GeV}\), and \(m=0.3~{\rm GeV}\), the continuum contribution is shown to be very small compared to the discrete part (Fig. 3).
* In \(3+1\) dimensions, the mass \(m\) as a function of magnetic field scale \(\lambda\) for fixed magnitude \(B\) at zero temperature is shown in Fig. 5, with both \(B\) and \(\lambda\) catalyzing chiral symmetry breaking.
* The local mass fluctuation \(m(0)\) at \((|eB|^{1/2},\lambda)=(0.3~{\rm GeV},2~{\rm GeV}^{-1})\) is plotted as a function of temperature \(T\) and baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) in Fig. 8, showing insensitivity to \(T\) and \(\mu\) when the global mass is large.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral symmetry breaking in inhomogeneous magnetic fields, building on exact solutions for the fermion propagator in a \({\rm sech}^2(x/\lambda)\) field by Cangemi (1995) and Dunne (1997). It contrasts with the extensive literature on constant magnetic fields in the NJL model (e.g., Fukushima 2012, Cao 2015) and with lattice QCD results on inverse magnetic catalysis at finite temperature (Bali 2011, 2012). The results enable future studies of dynamical quark matter evolution in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, where the magnetic field is both strong and spatially varying, and suggest that fireball expansion (increasing \(\lambda\)) does not necessarily reduce magnetic catalysis as long as the field magnitude \(B\) persists.
