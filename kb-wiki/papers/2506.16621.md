# np spin correlations in the deuteron ground state

> The deuteron is the simplest atomic nucleus made of two particles - a proton and a neutron. In this work, we study how their spins are quantum entangled with each other. We study two cases: when the deuteron is in a fixed projection of total angular momentum, and when it exists in a superposition of all projections. Our findings show that the spins are most entangled when the total projection is zero, and that strong entanglement still exists even when all spin states are superposed.

## Key claim

The authors show that neutron-proton spin entanglement in the deuteron ground state is strongest when the total angular momentum projection is \(M=0\), and that significant entanglement persists even when the deuteron exists in a coherent superposition of all three \(M\) projections, though the exact amount depends on the relative phases between the \(M=\pm1\) components.

## Method

The authors constructed the reduced spin density matrix for the deuteron ground state by starting from the full wavefunction expressed in a decoupled basis using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, then tracing over the radial, orbital angular momentum, and isospin degrees of freedom. They computed bipartite mutual information and entanglement negativity for two cases: (i) a fixed projection \(M\) of the total angular momentum, representing a deuteron in a weak magnetic field; and (ii) an equal coherent superposition of all three \(M\) projections, representing the degenerate ground state in the absence of an external field. The calculations used experimentally known probabilities of the \(L=0\) and \(L=2\) orbital states, with the \(L=2\) probability taken from six different nuclear potential models.

## Key numbers

- For the NLO potential model (\(|\alpha_2|^2 = 3.62\%\)), mutual information \(I(A:B) = 1.2671\) and negativity \(\mathcal{N} = 0.4784\) for the \(M=0\) projection; for \(M = \pm 1\), \(I(A:B) = 0.0846\) and \(\mathcal{N} = 0.0\).
- For the Hamada-Johnston potential model (\(|\alpha_2|^2 = 7.02\%\)), mutual information \(I(A:B) = 1.1829\) and negativity \(\mathcal{N} = 0.4580\) for the \(M=0\) projection; for \(M = \pm 1\), \(I(A:B) = 0.1363\) and \(\mathcal{N} = 0.0\).
- For the superposition of all \(M\) projections with the Woods-Saxon potential (\(|\alpha_2|^2 = 6.66\%\)), when \(\zeta_{+1} \neq \zeta_{-1}\) the mutual information is \(I(A:B) = 1.1921\) and negativity \(\mathcal{N} = 0.4604\); when \(\zeta_{+1} = \zeta_{-1}\) the mutual information drops to \(I(A:B) = 0.2781\) and negativity to \(\mathcal{N} = 0.1468\).
- The experimentally known probability of the \(S\)-state (\(L=0\)) is approximately \(94\%\), corresponding to \(|\alpha_2|^2 \approx 6\%\).

## Context

This work extends the growing field of nuclear entanglement, which bridges quantum information science and nuclear physics, following recent studies of quantum correlations in the Lipkin model by the authors of nuclear_lipkin_PhysRevA.100.062104 and nuclear_lipkin_PhysRevA.104.032428, the seniority model by Kruppa and collaborators (2022), and two-nucleon systems by Kruppa (2021). The results enable experimental measurement of spin entanglement in the deuteron as proposed by Dong Bai (2023), and could provide a resource for quantum information processing using nucleons. The work also has practical implications for shell model calculations where entanglement entropy serves as a metric for truncating the computational model space, as discussed by Gorton and Johnson (2023).
