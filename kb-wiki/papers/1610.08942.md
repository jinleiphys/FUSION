# Lifetime and mass of rho meson in correlation with magnetic-dimensional reduction

> It is simply anticipated that in a strong magnetic configuration, the Landau quantization ceases the neutral rho meson to decay to the charged pion pair, so the neutral rho meson will be long-lived. To closely access this naive observation, we explicitly compute the charged pion-loop in the magnetic field at the one-loop level, to evaluate the magnetic dependence of the lifetime for the neutral rho meson as well as its mass.Due to the dimensional reduction induced by the magnetic field (violation of the Lorentz invariance), the polarization (spin $s_z=-1,0,+1$) modes of the rho meson, as well as the corresponding pole mass and width, are decomposed in a nontrivial manner compared to the vacuum case. To see the significance of the reduction effect, we simply take the lowest-Landau level approximation to analyze the spin-dependent rho masses and widths. We find that the "fate" of the rho meson may be more complicated because of the magnetic-dimensional reduction: as the magnetic field increases, the rho width for the spin $s_z=0$ starts to develop, reach a peak, to be vanishing at the critical magnetic field to which the folklore refers. On the other side, the decay rates of the other rhos for $s_z=-1,+1$ monotonically increase as the magnetic field develops. The correlation between the polarization dependence and the Landau-level truncation is also addressed.

## Key claim

The naive expectation that a strong magnetic field will make the neutral rho meson long-lived by closing its decay to charged pions is incomplete; the magnetic-dimensional reduction causes the rho meson's polarization (spin) modes to behave differently, with the spin s_z=0 width vanishing at the critical field while the spin s_z=±1 widths diverge there, at least within the lowest-Landau-level approximation.

## Method

The authors computed the charged pion-loop correction to the neutral rho meson propagator at one-loop level using a chiral effective model based on the coset space [SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R × U(1)_V]/[SU(2)_V × U(1)_V]. They employed the lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation for the charged pion propagator in a constant magnetic field oriented along the z-direction, then decomposed the resulting rho meson self-energy into polarization modes reflecting the broken Lorentz invariance. The pole masses and decay widths for the physical spin s_z=0 and s_z=±1 modes were extracted from the propagator.

## Key numbers

* The critical magnetic field for the naive kinematic closure of the ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻ channel is eB_c = (m_ρ² − 4m_π²)/4 ≈ 0.13 GeV².
* The vacuum rho meson mass used is m_ρ = 0.775 GeV, the charged pion mass is m_π± = 0.140 GeV, and the ρ-π-π coupling constant is g_{ρππ} = 5.98.
* The LLL approximation is constrained to the magnetic range 0.07 ≲ eB ≲ 0.13 GeV², where the lower bound comes from requiring that decay to next-to-LLL pions is not open.
* The one-loop computation is consistent only for eB ≪ 4π m_ρ² / g_{ρππ} ∼ 1 GeV².
* The ρ^(Q) (s_z=0) width scales as Γ_Q ∼ (eB) √[m_ρ² − 4(m_π² + eB)], reaching a peak near eB ≈ 0.09 GeV² and vanishing at eB_c ≈ 0.13 GeV².
* The ρ^(L/G) (s_z=±1) width scales as Γ_(L/G) ∼ (eB)² / √[m_ρ² − 4(m_π² + eB)], diverging at the critical point eB_c ≈ 0.13 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the naive kinematic argument for rho meson stabilization in magnetic fields by incorporating dynamical effects from dimensional reduction, which had been neglected in earlier folklore. The authors compare their results to the simple phase-space modification formula (Eq. 2 in the paper) and note that the spin-dependent behavior is more complicated. The study is relevant to hadron properties in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009) and neutron stars (Harding 2006, Lattimer 2006, Potekhin 2011, Lai 2014). The authors identify that the LLL approximation for the s_z=±1 mode may be unreliable due to Landau-level number violation, and suggest that full summation over Landau levels is needed for that mode, while the s_z=0 result is physically meaningful within the constrained magnetic range.
