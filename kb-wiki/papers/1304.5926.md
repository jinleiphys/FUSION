# Transverse charge densities in the nucleon in nuclear matter

> We investigated the transverse charge densities in the nucleon in nuclear matter within the framework of the in-medium modified Skyrme model. The medium modification of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are first discussed. The results show that the form factors in nuclear matter fall off faster than those in free space, as the momentum transfer increases. As a result, the charge radii of the nucleon become larger, as the nuclear matter density increases. The transverse charge densities in the nucleon indicate that the size of the nucleon tends to bulge out in nuclear matter. This salient feature of the swelling is more clearly observed in the neutron case. When the proton is transversely polarized, the transverse charge densities exhibit the distortion due to the effects of the magnetization.

## Key claim

The nucleon electromagnetic form factors in nuclear matter fall off faster with momentum transfer than in free space, leading to larger charge radii and a "swelling" of the nucleon; this swelling is more clearly observed in the neutron case, and the effects are quantitatively stronger than those predicted by the quark meson coupling (QMC) model.

## Method

The authors used an in-medium modified Skyrme model, starting from an effective chiral Lagrangian with medium functionals \(\alpha_s\), \(\alpha_p\), and \(\gamma\) that encode pion-nucleus scattering parameters and the saturation of symmetric nuclear matter. They computed the in-medium Sachs electromagnetic form factors \(G_E^*(Q^2)\) and \(G_M^*(Q^2)\) for the proton and neutron in the Breit frame, then performed Fourier transforms to obtain transverse charge densities for both unpolarized and transversely polarized nucleons at various nuclear matter densities.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\), the proton charge radius \(\sqrt{\langle r_p^{2}\rangle^*}\) is enhanced by about \(10\%\) compared to the free-space value of \(0.882\,\mathrm{fm}\).
- The magnitude of the neutron charge radius \(\langle r_n^{2}\rangle^*\) is reduced by about \(4\%\) at \(\rho_0\) (from \(-0.316\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) in free space to \(-0.304\,\mathrm{fm}^2\)).
- The proton magnetic moment increases by about \(18\%\) at \(\rho_0\) (from \(1.965\) to \(2.315\) nuclear magnetons), while the neutron magnetic moment increases by about \(30\%\) (from \(-1.238\) to \(-1.634\) nuclear magnetons).
- At \(Q^2=0.3\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\rho_0\), the proton electric form factor \(G_E^{p*}\) is reduced by about \(30\%\) relative to free space, whereas the QMC model gives only about \(8\%\) reduction.
- At \(Q^2=1\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\rho_0\), the neutron electric form factor \(G_E^{n*}\) is decreased by approximately \(30\%\).
- At \(Q^2=0.3\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\rho_0\), the proton and neutron magnetic form factors are reduced by about \(12\%\) and \(8\%\), respectively, compared to free space.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium Skyrme model approach previously used to study energy-momentum tensor form factors (Kim 2012) and pion properties in nuclear matter (Yakhshiev 2010). The results are compared with the quark meson coupling (QMC) model (Lu 1997), which shows weaker medium effects, and with a soliton model with vector mesons (Meissner 1988). The study enables future experimental measurements of medium-modified nucleon structure, such as those from deeply virtual Compton scattering on nuclear targets (Airapetian 2009).
