# Coherent pion production in neutrino nucleus collision in the 1 GeV region

> We calculate cross sections for coherent pion production in nuclei induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos of the electron and muon type. The analogies and differences between this process and the related ones of coherent pion production induced by photons, or the (p,n) and $(^3 He, t)$ reactions are discussed. The process is one of the several ones occurring for intermediate energy neutrinos, to be considered when detecting atmospheric neutrinos. For this purpose the results shown here can be easily extrapolated to other energies and other nuclei.

# Coherent pion production in neutrino nucleus collision in the 1 GeV region

## Key claim
Coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions provides a complementary probe of Delta and pion renormalization in the nuclear medium, with cross sections that are experimentally accessible at present facilities, but the calculation shows a reduction of about a factor three relative to the impulse approximation when medium effects (Delta selfenergy and pion distortion) are included.

## Method
The authors construct the amplitude for the reaction ν_l + A → l⁻ + A + π⁺ using a nonrelativistic reduction of the weak interaction Lagrangian, substituting the N→Δ transition via spin-isospin operators with coupling ratio f*/f = 2.12. The cross section is evaluated for a spin-saturated nucleus, keeping only the longitudinal part of the pion momentum, and includes the Δ selfenergy (with Pauli blocking, quasielastic scattering, and 2N/3N absorption terms) and a distorted pion wavefunction from the Klein-Gordon equation with a pion-nucleus optical potential.

## Key numbers
- For ν_e + ¹⁶O → e⁻ + ¹⁶O + π⁺ at neutrino energy 800 MeV and electron energy 545 MeV, the cross section dσ/dΩ_e dE_e dΩ_π is forward peaked and of order 10⁻¹⁵ fm²/MeV sr².
- The full calculation (with Δ selfenergy and pion distortion) reduces the cross section by about a factor three relative to the impulse approximation.
- The Δ peak in the excitation function appears around q⁰ = 255 MeV for ¹⁶O, compared to 338 MeV for the free Δ position and 275 MeV found in coherent π⁺ production with the (p,n) reaction.
- For ν_μ reactions, cross sections are decreased by about 20% relative to ν_e for all nuclei studied.
- Cross sections grow with nuclear mass number A for ¹⁶O, ³⁷Cl, and ⁷¹Ga at 1 GeV neutrino energy.

## Context
This work extends the formalism developed for coherent pion production in (p,p') and (³He,t) reactions (Fernandez de Cordoba, 1994; Pfeil, 1994) to neutrino-induced reactions, and contrasts with coherent π⁰ photoproduction (RCA; Lakh) where a sin²θ factor suppresses forward angles. The results are relevant for interpreting atmospheric neutrino detector data from IMB (Becker-Szendy, 1992) and Kamiokande (Hirata, 1992), where the observed ratio of muon to electron neutrinos is about one rather than the expected two. The calculations can be extrapolated to other nuclei and energies for use in neutrino oscillation studies.
