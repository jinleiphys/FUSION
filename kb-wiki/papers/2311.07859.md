# Relaxation time for the alignment between quark spin and angular velocity in a rotating QCD medium

> We compute the relaxation times for massive quarks and anti-quarks to align their spins with the angular velocity in a rigidly rotating medium at finite temperature and baryon density. The rotation effects are implemented using a fermion propagator immersed in a cylindrical rotating environment. The relaxation time is computed as the inverse of the interaction rate to produce an asymmetry between the quark (anti-quark) spin components along and opposite to the angular velocity. For conditions resembling heavy-ion collisions, the relaxation times for quarks are smaller than for anti-quarks. For semi-central collisions the relaxation time is within the possible life-time of the QGP for all collision energies. However, for anti-quarks this happens only for collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\gtrsim 50$ GeV. The results are quantified in terms of the intrinsic quark and anti-quark polarizations, namely, the probability to build the spin asymmetry as a function of time. Our results show that these intrinsic polarizations tend to 1 with time at different rates given by the relaxation times with quarks reaching a sizable asymmetry at a faster pace. These are key results to further elucidate the mechanisms of hyperon polarization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The relaxation time for quarks to align their spin with the angular velocity in a rotating QCD medium is short enough to occur within the QGP lifetime for all collision energies in semicentral heavy-ion collisions, but for antiquarks this is only true for collision energies above approximately 50 GeV, contingent on the specific values of temperature and baryochemical potential near the chiral symmetry restoration/deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop quark self-energy using a fermion propagator derived for a rigidly rotating cylindrical environment, with rotation effects implemented via the Dirac equation in curved spacetime. The interaction rate for spin alignment was obtained from the imaginary part of the self-energy using hard thermal loop (HTL) gluon propagators at finite temperature and baryochemical potential, with the relaxation time defined as the inverse of the phase-space-integrated difference between rates for spin projections along and opposite to the angular velocity.

## Key numbers

- For quarks at collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (angular velocity Omega = 0.052 fm^-1) and chemical potential mu = 0 or 100 MeV, the relaxation time tau is less than 5 fm for temperatures near 150 MeV.
- For quarks at collision energy sqrt(s_NN) = 10 GeV (angular velocity Omega = 0.071 fm^-1) and chemical potential mu = 0 or 100 MeV, the relaxation time tau is less than 5 fm.
- The angular velocities considered for the early stages of collision are in the range 10 MeV less than or similar to Omega less than or similar to 14 MeV.
- The strange quark mass used in the calculation is m = 100 MeV.
- The strong coupling is taken as alpha_s = 0.3.
- The temperature used for the interaction rate figures is T = 150 MeV with chemical potential mu = 100 MeV and impact parameter b = 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ayala 2020, Ayala 2019) that computed relaxation times using an effective spin-vorticity coupling, now improving the description by using a fermion propagator that explicitly incorporates rotation effects (Ayala 2021). The results are relevant for understanding the global polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons measured by STAR (2017, 2021, 2023) and HADES (2022), and can be used in core-corona models (Ayala 2023, Ayala 2022, Ayala 2021, Ayala 2020) to compute hyperon polarization from the transferring of rotational motion to spin degrees of freedom.
