# Long Range Energy-energy Correlator at the LHC

> We study the forward-backward azimuthal angular correlations of hadrons in association with multi-particle production in the central rapidity region in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. We apply the nucleon energy-energy correlator framework, where the spinning gluon distribution introduces a nontrivial $\cos(2ϕ)$ asymmetries. We will demonstrate that the fundamental helicity structure of QCD amplitudes predicts a unique power counting rule: $\cos(2ϕ)$ asymmetry starts at ${O}(α_s^2)$ order for dijet, ${O}(α_s)$ for three jet and ${O}(1)$ for four (and more) jet productions. Our results will help us to understand the long standing puzzle of nearside ridge behavior observed in high multiplicity events of $pp$ collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the long-range $\cos(2\phi)$ azimuthal asymmetry in forward-backward energy-energy correlators at the LHC follows a strict power-counting hierarchy depending on the number of jets produced in the central rapidity region: the asymmetry starts at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$ for dijet, ${\cal O}(\alpha_s)$ for three-jet, and ${\cal O}(1)$ for four-jet (and more) production. This hierarchy arises from the helicity structure of QCD amplitudes and the spinning gluon distribution in the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) framework, and the authors claim it can help explain the nearside ridge puzzle in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions.

## Method

The authors apply the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) framework to proton-proton collisions at the LHC, measuring energy-energy correlators along the beam directions with polar angles $\theta_{a,b}$ and azimuthal angles $\phi_{a,b}$. They compute the $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry for multi-jet production by factorizing the cross section into gluon NEEC distributions (including the off-diagonal spinning gluon distribution $d_{g,{\rm EEC}}$) and partonic hard-scattering cross sections. For dijet production, they perform a detailed next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculation in QCD perturbation theory, including virtual corrections, real soft gluon radiation, and jet functions, demonstrating cancellation of infrared divergences and deriving the finite $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* Dijet $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry starts at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$ order, as shown by explicit NNLO calculation.
* Three-jet $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry starts at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s)$ order.
* Four-jet (and more) $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry starts at ${\cal O}(1)$ order (leading order).
* For dijet production at the LHC with jet transverse momentum $P_\perp = 100$ GeV, the $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetry is shown as a function of jet rapidity $y$ for three values of the soft gluon upper bound $q_0$ (which must satisfy $q_0 \ll P_\perp$).
* The $\cos(2\phi_a+2\phi_b-4\phi_c)$ asymmetry for dijet production is about $10\%$ for typical LHC kinematics.
* The NLO contribution $\hat\sigma_2^{(1)}$ for dijet is proportional to $\epsilon$ (in dimensional regularization with $D=4-2\epsilon$), i.e., $\hat{\sigma}_2^{(1)}=\epsilon \sigma_2^{(1)\epsilon}$.
* The soft anomalous dimension matrix $\boldsymbol{\Gamma}^{(1)}$ and the gluon cusp anomalous dimension $\gamma_K^{[g](1)}=2C_A$ are used in the infrared divergence cancellation.

## Context

This work extends the NEEC framework, previously applied to deep inelastic scattering at the planned Electron-Ion Collider (Liu 2022, Cao 2023, Li 2023), to proton-proton collisions at the LHC. It builds on the authors' earlier study (Guo 2024) of Higgs boson and top quark pair production, which found sizable $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetries with opposite signs. The paper directly addresses the long-standing puzzle of the nearside ridge observed in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions at the LHC (CMS 2010), where event generators like PYTHIA show very small asymmetry. The results enable experimental tests of the NEEC framework at the LHC and suggest that higher-multiplicity events, which favor more jets, should exhibit larger $\cos(2\phi)$ asymmetries, qualitatively consistent with the ridge observations.
