# Level excitation and transition probabilities of some nuclei in the lower fp-shell

> Unrestricted shell model calculations in the lower $fp$-shell region for the nuclei $^{46}$Ti, $^{46}$Cr and $^{46}$V have been performed for the isovector T=1 positive parity states using the shell model code OXBASH for Windows by employing the effective interactions GXPF1, FPD6 and KB3G. The level schemes and transition strengths $B$($E$2;$\downarrow$) are compared with the recently available experimental data. A very good agreement were obtained for all nuclei.

## Key claim
Unrestricted shell model calculations using the FPD6, GXPF1, and KB3G effective interactions in the lower fp-shell can reproduce experimental level schemes and transition strengths for the nuclei 46Ti, 46Cr, and 46V, but no single interaction is optimal for both observables: FPD6 gives the best level spectra while GXPF1 gives the best transition probabilities.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed using the code OXBASH for Windows in the FP model space, which comprises the 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0f7/2, and 0f5/2 valence orbits outside the 40Ca core. Three effective interactions (FPD6, GXPF1, and KB3G) were employed to calculate level excitation energies and B(E2; down) transition strengths for the isovector T=1 positive parity states of 46Ti, 46Cr, and 46V. Transition strengths were computed using a harmonic oscillator potential with effective charges e_pi = 0.7e and e_nu = 0.5e for 46Ti, e_pi = 0.5e and e_nu = 0.4e for 46V, and unspecified effective charges for 46Cr.

## Key numbers
- For 46Ti, the calculated B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) and B(E2; 4+1 -> 2+1) values using the present work's effective charges (e_pi=0.7e, e_nu=0.5e) show better agreement with experiment than previous theoretical work (Th.1 through Th.6), though the exact numerical values are not given in the text.
- For 46V, the theoretical B(E2; 2+1 -> 0+1) and B(E2; 4+1 -> 2+1) values using GXPF1 are in excellent agreement with experimental values from FB01 and PB01, and are in better agreement than previous theoretical work Th.2 (FB01) and Th.3 (SM99), though the specific numbers are not provided in the text.
- The effective charges used for 46Ti were e_pi = 0.7e and e_nu = 0.5e; for 46V they were e_pi = 0.5e and e_nu = 0.4e.

## Context
This work extends shell model calculations in the lower fp-shell region, building on the effective interactions FPD6 (Warr 1991), GXPF1 (GX02), and KB3G (AP01), and compares with experimental data from PE01, FB01, and others. The results show that FPD6 best reproduces level spectra while GXPF1 best reproduces transition strengths, indicating that no single standard interaction is optimal for both observables in this mass region. This enables future work to refine effective interactions for the lower fp-shell, which is important for applications such as electron capture in supernova explosions.
