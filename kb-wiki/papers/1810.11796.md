# Direct determination of the neutron skin thicknesses in $^{40,48}$Ca from proton elastic scattering at $E_p = 295$ MeV

> The neutron density distributions and neutron skin thicknesses in $^{40,48}$Ca are determined from the angular distributions of the cross sections and analyzing powers of polarized proton elastic scattering at $E_p = 295$ MeV. Based on the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation with the density-dependent effective $NN$ interaction, the experimental data is successfully analyzed, providing precise information of neutron and proton density profiles of $^{40,48}$Ca with small uncertainties. The extracted neutron and proton density distributions give neutron skin thicknesses in $^{40,48}$Ca for $-0.010^{+0.022}_{-0.024}$ fm and $0.168^{+0.025}_{-0.028}$ fm, respectively. The results of the density profiles and the neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca are directly compared with the {\it ab initio} coupled-cluster calculations with interactions derived from chiral effective field theory, as well as relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functional theories.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca is directly determined to be $0.168^{+0.025}_{-0.028}$ fm from proton elastic scattering, but this result depends on the relativistic impulse approximation framework with a density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction calibrated on $^{58}$Ni data.

## Method

Polarized protons at 295 MeV were scattered from natural Ca and enriched $^{48}$Ca foils, and the angular distributions of differential cross sections and analyzing powers were measured over a momentum transfer range of 0.5–3.5 fm$^{-1}$ using the Grand Raiden magnetic spectrometer. The neutron density distributions were extracted by $\chi^2$ fitting within the relativistic impulse approximation using a medium-modified relativistic Love-Franey interaction, with the point-proton density derived from known charge distributions via electron scattering data and including the spin-orbit contribution for $^{48}$Ca.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness in $^{40}$Ca: $-0.010^{+0.022}_{-0.024}$ fm (experimental error only)
- Neutron skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca: $0.168^{+0.025}_{-0.028}$ fm (experimental error only)
- Neutron rms radius in $^{40}$Ca: $3.375^{+0.022}_{-0.023}$ fm (experimental error only)
- Neutron rms radius in $^{48}$Ca: $3.555^{+0.025}_{-0.028}$ fm (experimental error only)
- Proton rms radius in $^{40}$Ca: 3.385 fm
- Proton rms radius in $^{48}$Ca: 3.387 fm
- Charge radius in $^{40}$Ca: 3.480 fm
- Charge radius in $^{48}$Ca: 3.460 fm
- Reduced $\chi^2$ minima: 4.6 for $^{40}$Ca and 4.0 for $^{48}$Ca
- Beam energy: 295 MeV
- Angular range: $7^{\circ}$–$48^{\circ}$
- Momentum transfer range: 0.5–3.5 fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the proton elastic scattering method previously applied to tin and lead isotopes (Zenihiro 2010, Sakaguchi 2017) to the calcium region, enabling direct comparison with ab initio coupled-cluster calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions (Hagen 2015, Ekstrom 2018) that cannot be applied to $^{208}$Pb. The result for $^{48}$Ca is consistent with the dipole polarizability analysis (Birkhan 2017) but disagrees with the dispersive optical model analysis (Mahzoon 2017), and the extracted density distributions allow assessment of theoretical predictions beyond just the skin thickness value.
