# Hadron loops effect on mass shifts of the charmed and charmed-strange spectra

> The hadron loop effect is conjectured to be important in understanding discrepancies between the observed states in experiments and the theoretical expectations of non-relativistic potential model. We present that, in an easily operable procedure, the hadron loop effect could shift the poles downwards to reduce the differences and provide better descriptions of both the masses and the total widths, at least, of the radial quantum number $n=1$ charmed and charmed-strange states. The $1^1P_1-1^3P_1$ mixing phenomena could be naturally explained due to their couplings with common channels. The newly observed $D$ states are also addressed, but there are still some problems remaining unclear.

## Key claim

The hadron loop effect, implemented via a once-subtracted dispersion relation using imaginary parts from the quark pair creation (QPC) model, can shift the pole masses of charmed and charmed-strange mesons downward from non-relativistic potential model predictions, providing better agreement with experimental masses and total widths for the n=1 states, though the procedure has significant uncertainties for radially excited states and the newly observed D states remain problematic.

## Method

The authors compute the imaginary part of the meson self-energy from the QPC (³P₀) model for all open and nearby closed two-body hadronic channels, then use a once-subtracted dispersion relation (subtracted at the D⁰ mass squared) to obtain the real part of the self-energy analytically. The physical mass and width are extracted from the pole position of the inverse propagator on the unphysical Riemann sheet, solving det(ℙ⁻¹(s))=0 for the complex pole s_pole = (M_p − iΓ_p/2)². For states with identical spin-parity (e.g., 1¹P₁ and 1³P₁), mixing is treated via off-diagonal self-energy terms arising from coupling to common decay channels.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach previously applied to charmonium by Heikkila et al. (1983) and Pennington et al. (2007) to the full charmed and charmed-strange meson spectra, using the QPC model to provide analytic vertex functions with natural cutoffs. It addresses the longstanding mass discrepancies between the non-relativistic potential model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and experimental observations, particularly the large shifts needed for the 1³P₀ states D₀*(2400) and D_sJ*(2317). The scheme provides a natural explanation for 1¹P₁−1³P₁ mixing via common decay channels rather than a phenomenological mixing angle, and it enables systematic study of the newly observed BABAR states D(2550), D(2610), and D(2760), though the results for these radially excited states remain inconclusive due to uncertainties in the simple harmonic oscillator wave functions and the need for further experimental confirmation.
