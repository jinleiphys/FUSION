# Extracting the Asymptotic Behavior of S-matrix Elements from their Phases

> The asymptotic kinematic limits of S-matrices are dominated by large logarithms which, roughly speaking, fall into two categories: those which are controlled by a renormalization group (RG) scale, which we may think of as logs involving ratios of invariant mass scales, and those which are functions of ratios of rapidities, so called "rapidity logs". It has been pointed out by Caron-Huot and Wilhlem [1] that RG anomalous dimension can be extracted from the phase of the S-matrix, which can greatly simplify calculations via unitarity methods. In this paper we generalize the results of [1] to show that the phase can be used to reconstruct rapidity anomalous dimensions, by performing a complex boost. The methodology introduced here also allows one to calculate without the need for a rapidity regulator. We demonstrate the use of this method to derive the rapidity anomalous dimensions in the Sudakov form factor, the two parton soft function and the Regge trajectory in QCD.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the method of Caron-Huot and Wilhelm (2016) to show that rapidity anomalous dimensions (RAD) can be extracted from the phase of the S-matrix by performing a complex boost, rather than the dilatation used for RG anomalous dimensions, provided the amplitude is first factorized into soft and collinear sectors using SCET.

## Method

The authors derive a master formula relating the rapidity anomalous dimension to the S-matrix phase by acting with a modified boost generator \(\bar K_z\) that boosts each collinear sector separately, then using unitarity to relate the conjugated form factor to the S-matrix. They demonstrate the method by calculating the rapidity anomalous dimensions for the Sudakov form factor and the two-parton transverse momentum soft function at two-loop order in QCD, working within the effective theory where only soft graphs need to be evaluated and Glauber modes generate the phase.

## Key numbers

- The rapidity anomalous dimension for the Sudakov form factor at two loops is extracted using the master formula expanded to one-loop order: \(\gamma_\nu^{s(1)} {\cal F}^{\star(0)} = \frac{1}{\pi}\left((\mathcal{M} {\cal F}^\star)^{(1)} - 2 \text{Im}[Z_F^{-1}]^{(1)}{\cal F}^{\star(0)}\right)\)
- The soft function zero-bin subtraction for the one-loop soft correction to a current is given by \(I_S^{ZB}\equiv \int \frac{d^dk}{(-k_\perp^2+i \epsilon)} \frac{1}{(n \cdot k +i \epsilon)}\frac{1}{(\bar n \cdot k +i \epsilon)}\)
- The rapidity regulator factor for collinear eikonal Wilson line vertices is \((\frac{w^2 \mid n/\bar n \cdot {\cal P}\mid^{-\eta}}{\nu^{-\eta}})\) and for soft eikonal vertices \((\frac{w \mid 2{\cal P}_z\mid^{-\eta/2}}{\nu^{-\eta/2}})\)

## Context

This work extends the method of Caron-Huot and Wilhelm (2016), who showed that RG anomalous dimensions can be extracted from the S-matrix phase using dilatations, to the case of rapidity logs that are not summable by traditional RG methods. The rapidity renormalization group (RRG) formalism was developed by Chiu et al. (2011, 2012) to resum these logs, and the present paper provides an alternative calculational approach that avoids introducing a rapidity regulator. The method enables simpler calculations of rapidity anomalous dimensions at higher loops, with the authors noting that the Regge trajectory case will be treated in a forthcoming publication.
