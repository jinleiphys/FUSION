# Discovery potential of multi-ton xenon detectors in neutrino electromagnetic properties

> Next-generation xenon detectors with multi-ton-year exposure are powerful direct probes of dark matter candidates, in particular the favorite weakly-interacting massive particles. Coupled with the features of low thresholds and backgrounds, they are also excellent telescopes of solar neutrinos. In this paper, we study the discovery potential of ton-scale xenon detectors in electromagnetic moments of solar neutrinos. Relevant neutrino-atom scattering processes are calculated by applying a state-of-the-arts atomic many-body method--relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). Limits on these moments are derived from existing data and estimated with future experiment specifications. With one ton-year exposure, XENON-1T can improve the effective milli-charge constraint by a factor two. With LZ and DARWIN, the projected improvement on the solar neutrino effective milli-charge(magnetic moment) is around 7(2) times smaller than the current bound. If LZ can keep the same background level and push the electron recoil threshold to 0.5 keV, the projected improvement on milli-charge(magnetic moment) is about 10(3) times smaller than the current bound.

# Discovery potential of multi-ton xenon detectors in neutrino electromagnetic properties

## Key claim

Multi-ton xenon detectors can improve constraints on solar neutrino effective milli-charge by up to a factor of 7 and effective magnetic moment by up to a factor of 2-3 compared to current bounds, but only if the electron recoil threshold can be lowered to 0.5 keV while maintaining current background levels; the improvement on the effective charge radius squared is not competitive with existing reactor neutrino experiments.

## Method

The authors calculate neutrino-xenon atomic ionization cross sections for electromagnetic interactions (milli-charge, magnetic moment, charge radius) using the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA), an ab initio atomic many-body method benchmarked against photoabsorption data. These cross sections are convolved with solar neutrino fluxes from pp fusion and 7Be electron capture to predict event rates, and limits on effective electromagnetic moments are derived from existing XENON-10, XENON-100, PandaX-II, and XENON-1T data, with projections for LZ and DARWIN based on their expected exposures, thresholds, and background levels.

## Key numbers

- Current best limit on solar neutrino effective magnetic moment: μ_ν_S^eff < 2.8 × 10^{-11} μ_B (Borexino experiment)
- Current best limit on reactor anti-electron neutrino effective milli-charge: δ_ν̅_e^eff < 1.5 × 10^{-12} e_0 (GEMMA experiment)
- Current best limit on reactor anti-electron neutrino effective charge radius squared: ⟨r_ν̅_e^2⟩^eff < 3.3 × 10^{-32} cm^2 (TEXONO experiment)
- With 1 ton-year exposure, XENON-1T can improve the effective milli-charge constraint by a factor of 2
- With LZ (15.34 ton-year exposure, 1.5 keV threshold) and DARWIN (14.0 ton-year exposure, 2.0 keV threshold), projected improvement on effective milli-charge is about 7 times smaller than current bound, and on effective magnetic moment is about 2 times smaller
- If LZ can push the electron recoil threshold to 0.5 keV with the same background level (4.27 × 10^{-5} kg^{-1} keV^{-1} day^{-1}), projected improvement on milli-charge is about 10 times smaller and on magnetic moment is about 3 times smaller than current bound
- Solar neutrino fluxes used: φ_pp = 5.98 × 10^{10} cm^{-2} s^{-1}, φ_7Be = 5.00 × 10^{9} cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- XENON-1T derived bound: μ_ν_S^eff < 4.51 × 10^{-11} μ_B, δ_Q_S^eff < 0.64 × 10^{-12} e_0, ⟨r_ν_S^2⟩^eff < 2.31 × 10^{-30} cm^2 at 90% CL

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RRPA calculations for neutrino-germanium scattering (Chen et al., prior works) to xenon targets, and builds on the neutrino background calculations for xenon detectors (nubg_ER-RRPA, nubg_ER-FEA). It directly competes with the best existing limits from the GEMMA experiment (magnetic moment and milli-charge of reactor neutrinos), Borexino (solar neutrino magnetic moment), and TEXONO (milli-charge and charge radius). The results enable experimental collaborations (LZ, DARWIN, XENONnT) to quantify the physics reach of their detectors for beyond-Standard-Model neutrino electromagnetic properties, and motivate pushing electron recoil thresholds below 1 keV in future liquid xenon detectors.
