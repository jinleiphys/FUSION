# Geometrical scaling of prompt photons in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss geometrical scaling (GS) for the prompt (direct) photons produced in heavy ion collisions. To this end we first introduce the concept of GS and illustrate its emergence on the example of charged particles. Next, we analyse direct photon data from RHIC and from the LHC. We show that the data support the hypothesis of GS in terms of participant number related to different centrality classes. We also study GS at different energies, however a more detailed study will be possible only when data for at least three different energies from one experiment will be available and also when we use the data obtained from the collisions of large systems (Cu+Cu, Au+Au, Pb+Pb) and small systems (p+p, d+Au, p+Au).

## Key claim

The paper shows that prompt photon spectra from heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC exhibit geometrical scaling (GS) in terms of participant number for different centrality classes, but the quality of energy scaling between different experiments is poor and requires data from at least three energies from a single experiment to be properly tested.

## Method

The author applies the "method of ratios" to test geometrical scaling of direct photon yields from PHENIX Au+Au at 200 GeV and ALICE Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, using the scaling variable τ = p_T^2 / Q_sat^2 with saturation scale Q_sat^2 ∝ N_part^(δ/2) (p_T/W)^λ. For centrality scaling, the exponent δ is varied (1/3, 2/3, 1) and the ratios of scaled spectra at different centralities are examined for flatness near unity. For energy scaling, spectra from both experiments are plotted in terms of τ for λ = 0.2 and λ = 0.3.

## Key numbers

- For charged particles, GS holds up to √τ ≈ 1.8 for ALICE PbPb at 2.76 TeV, STAR AuAu at 200 and 130 GeV, and PHENIX AuAu at 200 and 130 GeV, using λ = 0.3 and δ = 2/3.
- For direct photons, centrality scaling with δ = 2/3 gives ratios R ≈ 1 for ALICE at 2.76 TeV (centralities 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-80% with N_part = 308, 157, 45.7 respectively) and for PHENIX at 200 GeV (centralities 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, 60-92% with N_part = 277.5, 135.6, 56.0, 12.5 respectively).
- Energy scaling between PHENIX at 200 GeV and ALICE at 2.76 TeV is tested with λ = 0.2 and λ = 0.3, but no clear preference for either value can be determined from the data.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical scaling framework previously applied to charged particles in pp collisions by McLerran and Praszalowicz (2010) and in heavy ion collisions by Praszalowicz (2015) to the case of prompt photons. It builds on the initial photon GS study by Klein-Bosing (2014) that assumed a p_T^(-n) functional form, replacing it with a model-independent ratio method. The results support the Color Glass Condensate picture where quarks produced in glasma inherit GS from the saturated gluon distribution, and enable future tests using recent PHENIX data (2018) and comparisons across different collision systems (Cu+Cu, Au+Au, Pb+Pb, p+p, d+Au, p+Au).
