# Nuclear systems under extreme conditions: isospin asymmetry and strong B-fields

> This thesis explores nuclear systems under extreme conditions of isospin asymmetry and strong magnetic fields. Its first chapter is devoted to the phase diagram of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter in the density-temperature plane. Four competing phases of matter are included into the consideration: the unpaired phase, the ordinary Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) phase, a phase where normal fluid and superfluid separate into distinct domains and the superfluid phase with a non-zero total momentum of Cooper pairs. The evolution of these phases from the weakly BCS limit to the strongly coupled Bose-Einstein-Condensate limit is studied by varying the density from high to low values at various temperatures and asymmetries. The emergence of tri-critical points (including a Lifshitz point) and various intrinsic properties of the condensate (pair wave-function, occupation numbers, quasiparticle spectra) are discussed. The second chapter studies the nuclei $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{20}$Ne in a magnetic field $B\approx 10^{17}\,$G by utilizing the SKY3D code, suitably extended to include the magnetic field. Our solutions of time-independent Hartree-Fock equations on a Cartesian three-dimensional grid with a Skyrme interaction demonstrate a number of interesting phenomena, such as level splitting in the magnetic field, spontaneous rearrangement of energy levels, changes in the nuclear shape, etc. The third chapter studies the pairing in low-density neutron matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field. In particular, we find the limiting field for the destruction of the superfluidity of neutrons in the crust and outer core of magnetars. This field is density-dependent but is generally of the order of $B\geq 10^{17}\,$G. The phase diagram of spin-polarized neutron matter is constructed in the case where only the BCS phase is allowed along with the (spin-polarized) normal phase.

## Key claim

This thesis systematically maps the phase diagram of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter across the BCS-BEC crossover, including exotic LOFF and phase-separated states, and finds that the limiting magnetic field for destroying neutron superfluidity in magnetar crusts is density-dependent but generally of order \(B \geq 10^{17}\) G.

## Method

The first chapter solves the gap equation for neutron-proton pairing in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel across a range of densities, temperatures, and isospin asymmetries, identifying four competing phases: unpaired, ordinary BCS, phase-separated normal-superfluid domains, and the LOFF phase with nonzero Cooper-pair momentum. The second chapter performs static Hartree-Fock calculations on a Cartesian three-dimensional grid using the SKY3D code extended to include a magnetic field, studying \(^{12}\)C, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{20}\)Ne at \(B \approx 10^{17}\) G. The third chapter computes the phase diagram of spin-polarized neutron matter in the \(^1S_0\) channel, allowing only the BCS phase and the spin-polarized normal phase, and determines the magnetic field required for a given magnetization.

## Key numbers

- The limiting magnetic field for destruction of neutron superfluidity in magnetar crusts and outer cores is density-dependent but generally of order \(B \geq 10^{17}\) G.
- For the static Hartree-Fock calculations on \(^{12}\)C, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{20}\)Ne, the magnetic field strength used is \(B \approx 10^{17}\) G.
- The critical temperature for pairing in the \(^1S_0\) channel of polarized neutron matter is lower than that in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel of isospin-symmetric matter, because the coupling strength in the \(^1S_0\) channel is weaker.

## Context

This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover theory of Nozières and Schmitt-Rink (1985) to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, incorporating the LOFF phase first proposed by Larkin, Ovchinnikov, Fulde, and Ferrell, and a phase-separation scenario. The study of nuclei in strong magnetic fields builds on earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by extending the SKY3D code to include magnetic field effects, relevant for white dwarfs and accreting neutron stars. The polarized neutron matter results are directly applicable to the inner crust of neutron stars, where phenomenological indications of neutron superfluidity exist, and provide a benchmark for future studies of magnetar interiors.
