# The neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca from a nonlocal-dispersive-optical-model analysis

> A nonlocal dispersive-optical-model analysis has been carried out for neutrons and protons in $^{48}$Ca. Elastic-scattering angular distributions, total and reaction cross sections, single-particle energies, the neutron and proton numbers, and the charge distribution have been fitted to extract the neutron and proton self-energies both above and below the Fermi energy. From the single-particle propagator resulting from these self-energies we have determined the charge and neutron matter distributions in $^{48}$Ca. A neutron skin of 0.249$\pm$0.023~fm is deduced. The energy dependence of the total neutron cross sections is shown to have strong sensitivity to the skin thickness.

## Key claim

A nonlocal dispersive-optical-model analysis of neutrons and protons in $^{48}$Ca yields a best-fit neutron skin thickness of 0.249$\pm$0.023 fm, but values up to 0.33 fm are still consistent with the data; this result is large compared to recent _ab initio_ coupled-cluster calculations predicting 0.12-0.15 fm.

## Method

The authors performed a nonlocal dispersive-optical-model (DOM) analysis fitting elastic-scattering angular distributions, total and reaction cross sections, single-particle energies, neutron and proton numbers, and the charge distribution to extract neutron and proton self-energies both above and below the Fermi energy. From the single-particle propagator determined by these self-energies via the Dyson equation, they calculated the charge and neutron matter distributions. A bootstrap method with 40 modified data sets, randomly renormalizing each angular distribution or excitation function within $\pm$10%, was used to estimate experimental uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.249 \pm 0.023$ fm from the best fit
- Values of $\Delta r_{np}$ up to 0.33 fm are still consistent with the total neutron cross section data
- For the symmetric $^{40}$Ca system, the extracted skin thickness is $\Delta r_{np} = -0.06$ fm (with bootstrap uncertainty $-0.065 \pm 0.008$ fm)
- The best-fit neutron rms radius $r_n = 3.67$ fm corresponds to the skin thickness of 0.249 fm
- The _ab initio_ result corresponds to $r_n \sim 3.56$ fm

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal DOM approach previously applied to the symmetric $^{40}$Ca system (Mahzoon 2014) to the neutron-rich $^{48}$Ca, allowing the asymmetry dependence of spectral strengths to be determined. The extracted skin thickness overlaps with the range of 0.12-0.26 fm predicted by 48 nuclear energy-density functionals (Piekarewicz 2012) but is large compared to the 0.12-0.15 fm range from _ab initio_ coupled-cluster calculations (Hagen 2016). The results strengthen the argument for a parity-violating electron scattering measurement on $^{48}$Ca and demonstrate that total neutron cross sections over a large energy range (6 to 200 MeV) exhibit strong sensitivity to the neutron skin, enabling future mapping of this quantity for many stable isotopes.
