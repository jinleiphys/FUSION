# A short review of the theory of hard exclusive processes

> We first present an introduction to the theory of hard exclusive processes. We then illustrate this theory by a few selected examples. The last part is devoted to the most recent developments in the asymptotical energy limit.

## Key claim

This review argues that hard exclusive processes in QCD can be systematically described through factorization theorems that separate perturbative hard partonic scattering from non-perturbative hadronic structure functions, but the validity of each factorization scheme depends on specific kinematic conditions (such as large virtuality Q² or asymptotically high energy) and the particular process under study.

## Method

The paper reviews collinear factorization for medium-energy exclusive processes (applicable at JLab, HERMES, COMPASS, BaBar, Belle, PANDA, Super-B) and kT-factorization for asymptotically high-energy collider experiments (HERA, Tevatron, LHC, ILC). It presents the theoretical framework for generalized parton distributions (GPDs), distribution amplitudes (DAs), generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), and transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), showing how each emerges from factorizing specific exclusive scattering amplitudes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), meson electroproduction, and γ*γ annihilation.

## Key numbers

- The QCD coupling satisfies α_s(Q) ≪ 1 for Q ≫ Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV, defining the perturbative regime
- The skewness ξ in DVCS relates to the Bjorken variable x_Bj as ξ = x_Bj/(2 − x_Bj), showing skewness effects are small at small x_Bj (typical at HERA collider)
- The DGLAP evolution sums terms of type Σ_n (α_s ln Q²)ⁿ (leading-log in Q) plus α_s Σ_n (α_s ln Q²)ⁿ (next-to-leading-log in Q)
- The BFKL evolution sums terms of type Σ_n (α_s ln 1/x_Bj)ⁿ (leading-log in x) plus α_s Σ_n (α_s ln 1/x_Bj)ⁿ (next-to-leading-log in x)
- Factorization for DVCS requires Q² ≫ Λ_QCD² and s ≫ −t; factorization for γ*γ → hadron pair requires Q² ≫ Λ_QCD² and s ≪ −t

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed from inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) to exclusive processes, building on the DGLAP evolution equations (Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, Parisi, 1972-1977) and the BFKL resummation (Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov, Balitsky, 1975-1978). It connects the formalism of GPDs (introduced in the late 1990s by Mueller, Radyushkin, Ji, and Collins) to experimental programs at HERA, JLab, COMPASS, and future facilities like the EIC and LHC, enabling access to three-dimensional hadron structure through exclusive channels such as DVCS and meson electroproduction.
