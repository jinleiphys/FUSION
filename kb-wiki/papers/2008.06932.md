# A bag model of matter condensed by the strong interaction

> Inspired by various astrophysical phenomenons, it is suggested that pulsar-like compact stars are comprised entirely of strangeons (quark-clusters with three-light-flavor symmetry) and a small amount of electrons. In order to better constrain the properties of strangeon stars, we propose a linked bag model to describe the condensed matter by the strong interaction (i.e., strong condensed matter) in both 2-flavoured (nucleons) and 3-flavoured (hyperons, strangeons, etc.) scenarios. The model parameters are calibrated to reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter, which are later applied to hyperon matter and strangeon matter. Compared with baryon matter, the derived energy per baryon of strangeon matter is reduced if the strangeon carries a large number of valence quarks, which stiffens the equation of state and consequently increases the maximum mass of strangeon stars. In a large parameter space, the maximum mass and tidal deformability of strangeon stars predicted by the linked bag model are consistent with the current astrophysical constraints. It is found that the maximum mass of strangeon stars can be as large as $\sim 2.5M_\odot$, while the tidal deformability of a $1.4M_\odot$ strangeon star lies in the range of $180\lesssim Λ_{1.4} \lesssim 340$.

## Key claim

The linked bag model can simultaneously describe 2-flavored (nucleon) and 3-flavored (hyperon, strangeon) strong condensed matter, and predicts that strangeon matter with a large number of valence quarks (Nq=9) yields stiffer equations of state and higher maximum stellar masses than conventional baryon matter, but only if the model parameters are calibrated to reproduce nuclear saturation properties and the damping factor f is chosen to keep bags connected.

## Method

The authors adopt a linked bag model based on the MIT bag model, where quark bags arranged in a simple cubic lattice overlap through windows on their surfaces as the lattice constant decreases. They use a Fermi-gas approximation with multiple reflection expansion for finite-size effects, include first-order QCD corrections, and introduce a density-dependent bag constant B with a third-order expansion in the chemical potential. The model parameters (C1, B2, B3, z0) are calibrated to reproduce the saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy E0 = 922 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, and symmetry energy Esym = 31.7 MeV of nuclear matter, then applied to hyperon matter and strangeon matter with Nq = 9.

## Context

This work extends the MIT bag model approach (Chodos 1974; DeGrand 1975) and previous studies of strangeon stars by Xu (2003) and Lai (2013) by providing a unified linked bag model that treats both 2-flavored and 3-flavored strong matter. It addresses the Hyperon Puzzle discussed by Vidana (2015) and others, where additional repulsion is needed to avoid hyperon softening, by showing that strangeon matter with Nq=9 naturally stiffens the equation of state. The results enable future comparisons with astrophysical observations from LIGO/Virgo (GW170817), NICER (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620), and upcoming facilities like FAST, SKA, and eXTP, while the authors note that spin-dependent interactions (color-magnetic part of one-gluon-exchange) are not included and should be considered in future work.
