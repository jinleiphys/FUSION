# Can the Cabibbo mixing originate from noncommutative extra dimensions?

> Treating hadronic flavor symmetries with quantum algebras U_q(su_n) leads to interesting consequences such as: new mass sum rules for hadrons 1^-, (1/2)^+, (3/2)^+ of improved accuracy; possibility to label different flavors topologically - by torus winding number; properly fixed deformation parameter q in case of baryons is linked in a simplest way to the Cabibbo angle θ_C, that suggests for θ_C the exact value π/{14}. In this connection, we discuss the possibility that this angle and the Cabibbo mixing as a whole take its origin in noncommutativity of some additional, with regard to 3+1, space-time dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Cabibbo angle \(\theta_{\rm\scriptscriptstyle{C}}\) may have a theoretically motivated exact value of \(\pi/14\), derived from the deformation parameter \(q\) of quantum algebras \(U_q(su_n)\) used to describe hadronic flavor symmetries. This value is suggested as a possible consequence of noncommutativity in additional, extra space-time dimensions beyond the usual 3+1.

## Method

The author applies the representation theory of quantum algebras \(U_q(su_n)\) to construct mass operators for vector mesons and baryons (octet and decuplet), replacing the conventional \(SU(n)\) flavor symmetry. By fixing the deformation parameter \(q\) as a root of specific polynomials (identified with Alexander polynomials of torus knots), the \(q\)-deformed mass sum rules reduce to realistic forms without singlet mixing. The resulting values of \(q\) for baryons are then linked to the Cabibbo angle through relations derived from the \(q\)-deformed mass formulas and from comparisons with the Weinberg angle in a \(q\)-deformed electroweak model.

## Key numbers

* The exact value suggested for the Cabibbo angle is \(\theta_{\rm\scriptscriptstyle{C}} = \pi/14\).
* For the decuplet baryon mass relation, the deformation angle \(\theta_{\bf 10} \simeq \pi/14\) provides excellent agreement with data, and the paper asserts \(\theta_{\bf 10} = \theta_{\rm\scriptscriptstyle{C}}\).
* For the octet baryon mass sum rule, the best agreement with data (0.07% accuracy) is achieved with \(q = e^{i\pi/7}\), corresponding to \(\theta_{\bf 8} = \pi/7\), and the paper asserts \(\theta_{\bf 8} = 2\theta_{\rm\scriptscriptstyle{C}}\).
* The vector meson mass relation for the \(\phi\) meson (identified with \(\omega_8\)) is satisfied without mixing when \(q = e^{i\pi/5}\), which coincides with the known \(\omega\)-\(\phi\) mixing angle of \(36^\circ\).
* The paper lists the accuracy of several octet baryon mass sum rules: the standard GMO rule has 0.58% deviation; the rule with \(q=e^{i\pi/7}\) has 0.07% deviation; the rule with \(q=e^{i\pi/6}\) has 0.22% deviation.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior program (Gavrilik, various years) of applying quantum algebras \(U_q(su_n)\) to hadron mass sum rules, showing that the deformation parameter can be fixed by topological invariants (Alexander polynomials of torus knots) rather than by phenomenological fitting. The paper connects this approach to the Cabibbo mixing angle, which is central to the CKM matrix parametrization (Wolfenstein, 1983) and weak decays. It also relates the deformation parameter to the Weinberg angle via a \(q\)-deformed electroweak model (Gavrilik, 1999), suggesting a unified noncommutative-geometric origin for both bosonic and fermionic mixings. This enables a speculative link between hadron mass relations, flavor mixing, and extra-dimensional noncommutative geometry.
