# Spin-1 diquark contributing to the formation of tetraquarks in light mesons

> We apply a mixing framework to the light meson systems and examine tetraquark possibility in the scalar channel. In the diquark-antidiquark model, a scalar diquark is a compact object when its color and flavor structures are in ($\bar{\bm{3}}_c$, $\bar{\bm{3}}_f$). Assuming that all the quarks are in an $S$-wave, the spin-0 tetraquark formed out of this scalar diquark has only one spin configuration, $|J,J_{12},J_{34}\rangle=|000\rangle$, where $J$ is the spin of the tetraquark, $J_{12}$ the diquark spin, $J_{34}$ the antidiquark spin. In this construction of the scalar tetraquark, we notice that another compact diquark with spin-1 in ($\bm{6}_c$, $\bar{\bm{3}}_f$) can be used although it is less compact than the scalar diquark. The spin-0 tetraquark constructed from this vector diquark leads to the spin configuration $|J,J_{12},J_{34}\rangle=|011\rangle$. The two configurations, $|000\rangle$ and $|011\rangle$, are found to mix strongly through the color-spin interaction. The physical states can be identified with certain mixtures of the two configurations which diagonalize the hyperfine masses of the color-spin interaction. Matching these states to two scalar resonances $a_0(980), a_0(1450)$ or to $K^*_0(800), K^*_0(1430)$ depending on the isospin channel, we find that their mass splittings are qualitatively consistent with the hyperfine mass splittings which can support their tetraquark structure. To test our mixing scheme further, we also construct the tetraquarks for $J=1,J=2$ with the spin configurations, $|111\rangle$ and $|211\rangle$, and discuss possible candidates in the physical spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that scalar tetraquarks in light mesons are not formed solely from the most compact spin-0 diquark, but involve strong mixing between two distinct four-quark configurations: one built from a scalar diquark and one built from a vector (spin-1) diquark. The authors claim that this mixing, driven by the color-spin interaction, can explain the large mass splittings between observed scalar meson pairs such as a0(980) and a0(1450), provided the physical states are identified with the mixed eigenstates.

## Method

The authors construct tetraquark wave functions in the diquark-antidiquark picture assuming all quarks are in an S-wave, using two different diquark configurations: a scalar diquark in (color antitriplet, flavor antitriplet) and a vector diquark in (color sextet, flavor antitriplet). They compute the hyperfine masses from the color-spin interaction and the color-electric masses for the spin configurations |000⟩, |011⟩, |111⟩, and |211⟩, including the off-diagonal mixing term between |000⟩ and |011⟩ in the scalar channel. The 2x2 hyperfine mass matrix in the scalar channel is diagonalized to obtain the physical eigenstates, and the resulting mass splittings are compared to experimental resonances in the isovector (I=1) and isodoublet (I=1/2) channels.

## Key numbers

* For the isovector channel (a0 resonances), the hyperfine mass matrix diagonalization yields a mass splitting of 471.7 MeV between the two physical eigenstates (Theory I), compared to the experimental mass difference of 494 MeV between a0(1450) at 1474 MeV and a0(980) at 980 MeV.
* With a different parameter choice (Theory II, v0 = (-199.6)^3 MeV^3), the calculated mass splitting is 522.8 MeV, about 29 MeV above the experimental value.
* The mixing term between the two scalar configurations in the isovector channel is ⟨000|V_CS|011⟩ = -222.3 MeV, indicating strong mixing.
* The hyperfine mass for the |011⟩ configuration in the isovector channel is -331.5 MeV, more negative than the |000⟩ value of -173.9 MeV, contrary to the expectation that the scalar diquark configuration is more bound.
* Constituent quark masses used are m_u = m_d = 330 MeV and m_s = 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-antidiquark tetraquark model originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) for light scalar mesons, which used only the scalar diquark in (color antitriplet, flavor antitriplet). The authors note that a similar mixing scenario was discussed by Jaffe (2004) and Black et al. (1998), but those works focused on explaining the low masses of the lightest scalar states without investigating the higher-mass partners. Black et al. (1999) proposed a different mixing between a P-wave qqbar and a tetraquark, whereas the present work introduces mixing between two four-quark states with different color and spin configurations. The approach enables a unified description of both the low-lying scalar mesons (a0(980), K0*(800)) and their higher-mass counterparts (a0(1450), K0*(1430)) as tetraquark states, and further predicts candidates for J=1 and J=2 tetraquarks from the same framework.
