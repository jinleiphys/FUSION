# Dynamics of Phase Transition in Quark-Gluon Plasma Droplet Formation under Magnetic Field

> Pre-existing density of states for a Quark-Gluon Phase, based on Thomas-Fermi and Bethe mode, is expanded by incorporation of new variables. Results from recent study indicate that perturbations in the form of a finite non-zero chemical potential T, B, dynamic thermal masses M and of course Temperature T are indeed vital to fully comprehend the formation and dynamics of QGP. Simulations depict an overall increase in the stability of QGP in the paradigm of the statistical model. On the top of Free Energy, Entropy and heat capacity are calculated for the phase transition. The overall qualitative behavior, of entropy or Heat Capacity determines the order of phase transition of the QGP. Investigation of order of phase transition is carried out in this study through Monte-Carlo based differential element, which ensures the inclusion of the randomness of the collisions at the particle colliders.

## Key claim

The authors extend a pre-existing statistical model of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by incorporating a finite non-zero chemical potential, an external magnetic field, and dynamic thermal masses, and they claim that these perturbations are vital to fully understand QGP formation and dynamics. The study concludes that the phase transition remains weakly first-order, with a discontinuity in entropy but a continuous heat capacity, and that the speed of sound respects the QCD bound of 1/3 within a 16% margin of error at the effective temperature.

## Method

The authors expand the density of states for quarks and gluons, originally based on the Thomas-Fermi and Bethe models, by adding a magnetic field term and dynamic temperature-dependent masses to the free energy integrals. Free energy is computed separately for quarks (three flavors), gluons, and the pion-ionic medium, using Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein statistics, and a surface energy term is constructed with magnetic field coupling. The total free energy is minimized to study droplet stability, and its temperature derivatives yield entropy, heat capacity, and speed of sound; the order of the phase transition is investigated via Monte-Carlo based differential elements to include collision randomness.

## Key numbers

- The transition temperature is taken as Tc = 175 MeV, with a chemical potential of μ = 400 MeV and a magnetic field strength of eB = 15 m_π^2 for key simulations.
- The pion mass is fixed at M_π ~ 138 MeV.
- The magnetic field range is set by LHC experiments, with a minimum of 0.98 and a maximum of 5.00 in units of (m_π^2 * 15 / 0.303).
- At T = Tc, averaging over the entire magnetic field range for a droplet radius of 4 fm shows a 16 times increase in the free energy peak compared to the basic statistical model.
- The speed of sound squared (C_s^2) at the effective temperature of 222 MeV is within a 16% margin of error of the QCD bound of 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model of Ramanathan et al. (2004) and Ramanathan et al. (2011), which previously incorporated only temperature and chemical potential and predicted a first-order phase transition. By adding an external magnetic field and dynamic thermal masses from Peshier (1994), the authors aim to better describe QGP formation in heavy-ion collisions at BNL and CERN, where non-central Au-Au/Pb-Pb collisions produce strong magnetic fields. The results enable future studies of reduced average droplet radius and reduced dependence on flow parameters, and they provide a testable prediction for the speed of sound near the transition temperature.
