# Experimental investigation of a linear-chain structure in the nucleus 14C

> It is a well-known fact that a cluster of nucleons can be formed in the interior of an atomic nucleus, and such clusters may occupy molecular-like orbitals, showing characteristics similar to normal molecules consisting of atoms. Chemical molecules having a linear alignment are commonly seen in nature, such as carbon dioxide. A similar linear alignment of the nuclear clusters, referred to as linear-chain cluster state (LCCS), has been studied since the 1950s, however, up to now there is no clear experimental evidence demonstrating the existence of such a state. Recently, it was proposed that an excess of neutrons may offer just such a stabilizing mechanism, revitalizing interest in the nuclear LCCS, specifically with predictions for their emergence in neutron-rich carbon isotopes. Here we present the experimental observation of α-cluster states in the radioactive 14C nucleus. Using the 10Be+α resonant scattering method with a radioactive beam, we observed a series of levels which completely agree with theoretically predicted levels having an explicit linear-chain cluster configuration. We regard this as the first strong indication of the linear-chain clustered nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors report the first strong indication of a linear-chain cluster state (LCCS) in a nucleus, specifically in 14C, by observing a series of α-cluster resonances that agree with theoretical predictions for an LCCS band, though they hedge that this constitutes an "indication" rather than definitive proof because the interpretation relies on agreement with a specific theoretical model and the experimental data show some discrepancies in the reduced widths.

## Method

The experiment used the 10Be+α resonant scattering method in inverse kinematics with a radioactive 10Be beam at 24.9±0.3 MeV impinging on a helium gas target at 700 Torr. Recoiling α particles were detected at forward laboratory angles (0–8°) by two silicon ΔE-E detector telescopes, and the excitation function for elastic scattering was reconstructed event-by-event for center-of-mass energies from 13.8 to 19.1 MeV. An R-matrix analysis using SAMMY8 was performed to extract resonance parameters including spin-parity assignments and α-particle decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The 10Be beam had a typical intensity of 2 × 10^4 particles per second with purity better than 95%
- The measured 10Be beam energy at the entrance of the helium gas target was 24.9±0.3 MeV
- The overall uncertainty in center-of-mass energy was 80–110 keV, depending on the energy
- Three resonances were identified at excitation energies of 15.1, 16.2, and 18.7 MeV with spin-parity assignments of 0+, 2+, and 4+ respectively
- The rotational parameter ħ²/2ℑ was 0.19 MeV, implying strong deformation consistent with a linear-chain configuration
- The channel radius for R-matrix calculations was taken as 5.0 fm, with a systematic error evaluated for ±0.5 fm variation

## Context

This work extends the long search for linear-chain cluster states that began with Morinaga (1956) and follows theoretical predictions by Suhara and En'yo (2010) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations, which predicted a prolate LCCS band in 14C stabilized by orthogonality to lower-lying triaxial states. The results contradict earlier LCCS candidates in 16O proposed by Chevallier (1967) that were later questioned, and provide the first experimental support for the neutron-excess stabilization mechanism proposed theoretically. The measurement enables future studies of more exotic nuclear topologies and confirms the utility of the 10Be+α resonant scattering method for selectively observing natural-parity α-cluster states.
