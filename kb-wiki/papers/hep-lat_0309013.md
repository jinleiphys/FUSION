# Baryon Decuplet to Octet Electromagnetic Transitions in Quenched and Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We calculate baryon decuplet to octet electromagnetic transition form factors in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory. We work in the isospin limit of SU(3) flavor, up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, and to leading order in the heavy baryon expansion. Our results are necessary for proper extrapolation of lattice calculations of these transitions. We also derive expressions for the case of SU(2) flavor away from the isospin limit.

## Key claim

The paper calculates baryon decuplet to octet electromagnetic transition form factors in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and leading order in the heavy baryon expansion, working in the isospin limit of SU(3) flavor. The results are necessary for proper extrapolation of lattice calculations of these transitions, but the authors note that in the quenched approximation the low-energy constants are unrelated to those in QCD, whereas in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory the constants are the same as in QCD, enabling physical predictions.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for both quenched and partially quenched QCD, constructing the effective Lagrangian for baryon octet and decuplet fields coupled to pseudo-Goldstone mesons and the electromagnetic field. They computed the three independent Lorentz-invariant form factors G1, G2, and G3 at zero momentum transfer (q^2=0) from tree-level dimension-5 and dimension-6 operators and from one-loop diagrams, keeping only contributions non-analytic in the meson mass. For the quenched case, additional hairpin contributions from the light eta-prime singlet were included, with coefficients listed in tables for each transition channel.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f is taken as 132 MeV.
- Tree-level coefficient alpha for Delta -> N gamma is 1/sqrt(3).
- Tree-level coefficient alpha for Sigma*0 -> Lambda gamma is -1/2.
- Tree-level coefficients alpha for Sigma*,- -> Sigma- gamma and Xi*,- -> Xi- gamma are both 0.
- The SU(3) coefficients beta_X^T and beta_X^B for loop diagrams are tabulated for seven transition channels, with values given for pi, K, eta_s, ju, ru, js, and rs mesons in partially quenched theory.
- In quenched theory, the hairpin contributions involve coefficients A_{XX'}, B_{XX'}, C_{XX'}, and D_{XX'} tabulated for eta_u and eta_s meson pairs.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of decuplet-to-octet electromagnetic transitions by Butler (1993), Napsuciale (1997), and Gellas (1998) to the quenched and partially quenched frameworks needed for lattice QCD extrapolation. It builds on the partially quenched chiral perturbation theory formalism developed by Sharpe (1997, 2000, 2001) and Golterman (1998), and on recent lattice calculations of the Delta to N gamma transition by Alexandrou (2002, 2003) that used the quenched approximation. The results enable future partially quenched lattice simulations of these form factors to be extrapolated to physical quark masses, providing model-independent predictions for QCD.
