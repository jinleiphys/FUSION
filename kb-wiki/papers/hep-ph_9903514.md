# Gluon Condensate and Quark Propagation in the QGP

> A calculation of the thermal quark propagator is presented taking the gluon condensate above the critical temperature into account. The quark dispersion relation following from this propagator is derived.

# Gluon Condensate and Quark Propagation in the QGP

## Key claim
The thermal quark propagator and resulting dispersion relation in the quark-gluon plasma can be computed by incorporating the gluon condensate as a non-perturbative effect, yielding an effective quark mass of approximately 1.15 T over the temperature range 1.1 T_c to 4 T_c, in contrast to the perturbative hard-thermal-loop (HTL) result of gT/√6.

## Method
The authors extend the zero-temperature quark propagator containing the gluon condensate to finite temperature using the imaginary time formalism. They expand the quark self-energy diagram for small loop momenta (k ≪ p, k₀ = 0) and relate the moments of the longitudinal and transverse gluon propagators to the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates. These condensates are extracted from lattice results for the space- and timelike plaquette expectation values Δ_σ and Δ_τ, and the quark dispersion relation is obtained numerically from the poles of the propagator decomposed into helicity eigenstates.

## Key numbers
- Zero-temperature gluon condensate: ⟨G²⟩₀ = (2.5 ± 1.0) T_c⁴
- Effective quark mass at p=0: m_eff = [(2π α_s/3) (⟨E²⟩_T + ⟨B²⟩_T)]^{1/4}
- Approximate effective mass over 1.1 T_c < T < 4 T_c: m_eff = 1.15 T
- Dispersion relations computed numerically at T = 1.1 T_c, T = 2 T_c, and T = 4 T_c

## Context
This work extends the zero-temperature gluon condensate approach of the authors' prior work to finite temperature, offering an alternative to lattice QCD and perturbative QCD for including non-perturbative effects in Green functions. The resulting quark dispersion relation qualitatively resembles the perturbative HTL result from the literature, but differs in the effective mass value. The authors suggest this effective quark propagator can be used to compute photon and dilepton production rates from the QGP.
