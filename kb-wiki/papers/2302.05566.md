# Updated analyses of gluon distribution functions for the pion and kaon from the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model

> In this work, we investigate the gluon distribution functions for the pion and kaon, in addition to the improved result of the valence-quark ones, in the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral-quark model (NL$χ$QM), in which the momentum dependence of the quark interactions is properly taken into account. We then analyze the gluon distribution functions, generated dynamically through the splitting functions in the DGLAP QCD evolution. By comparing with the recent lattice QCD results and JAM global analyses, it is found that the present numerical results for the gluon parton distribution functions for the pion exhibit a remarkable agreement, followed by the valence up-quark distribution results for the pion by reproducing the reanalyzed experimental data. Our prediction on the gluon distribution functions for the kaon is also consistent with the recent lattice data for the kaon within the errors.

## Key claim

The gluon distribution functions for the pion and kaon, dynamically generated via NLO DGLAP evolution from the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model, show remarkable agreement with recent lattice QCD results and JAM global analyses, but only for momentum fractions \(x \gtrsim 0.2\); for \(x \lesssim 0.2\) the authors note that more experimental statistics are needed to determine the correct behavior.

## Method

The authors computed valence-quark distribution functions for the pion and kaon using the gauge-invariant nonlocal chiral quark model (NLχQM), which incorporates momentum-dependent quark interactions. They then dynamically generated the gluon distribution functions by applying next-to-leading order (NLO) Dokshitzer–Gribov–Lipatov–Altarelli–Parisi (DGLAP) QCD evolution, using the singlet quark distribution functions and splitting functions. The model parameters were fixed by the normalization condition for the quark distribution functions at a model scale of \(\mu = 1\) GeV, with constituent quark mass \(M_0 = 300\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at zero momentum transfer: \(M_0 = 300\) MeV, determined to satisfy the QDF normalization condition at model scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV.
- Pion weak-decay constant: \(F_\pi = 93.2\) MeV; kaon weak-decay constant: \(F_K = 113.4\) MeV.
- Current quark masses: \(m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV, \(m_s = 100\) MeV.
- For \(xu_\pi(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 27\) GeV\(^2\), the power-law behavior near \(x=1\) is \((1-x)^{1.85}\).
- For \(xu_K(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 27\) GeV\(^2\), the power-law behavior near \(x=1\) is \((1-x)^{2.30}\).
- First moment of the pion gluon distribution at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\): \(\langle x \rangle_{g_\pi} = 0.605\).
- First moment of the kaon gluon distribution at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\): \(\langle x \rangle_{g_K} = 0.577\).
- Power-law behavior of \(xg_\pi(x)\) at large \(x\) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\): \((1-x)^{3.06}\).
- Power-law behavior of \(xg_K(x)\) at large \(x\) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\): \((1-x)^{3.17}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson parton distribution functions using the NLχQM (Nam 2012) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (BSE-NJL) model (Hutauruk 2021). The results are compared with recent lattice QCD calculations (Fan 2021; Salas-Chavira 2021) and the JAM global analysis (Barry 2021). The authors find that their momentum-dependent quark interactions reproduce the reanalyzed experimental Drell-Yan data (Aicher 2010) for the pion, whereas the BSE-NJL model without such momentum dependence fails to do so. The results provide predictions for gluon distributions in the kaon that can be tested by future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), the Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC), and the COMPASS++/AMBER experiment at CERN.
