# Topics in Nuclear Structure

## Key claim
The paper reviews single-j-shell studies over the last decade, focusing on four topics. The authors show that for an E(J_max) interaction acting only in the J_max=2j channel, the lowest eigenstates for I=0+ and I=1+ have simple analytic forms in terms of unitary 9-j symbols, while for I≥2 the eigenfunctions are numerically very close, but not exactly equal, to those same 9-j coefficients.

## Method
The authors perform shell-model calculations in a single-j configuration space, using an interaction E(J_max) where all two-body matrix elements vanish except for J=J_max=2j. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian for systems of two protons and two neutrons (or holes) in the g9/2 shell, specifically for 96Cd, and compare the resulting wave functions with analytic unitary 9-j symbols. They also examine degeneracies, the emergence of unique seniority-4 states under different interactions (pairing, Q·Q, CCGI), and the systematics of odd-odd nuclear spectra using a (2j-1) rule.

## Key numbers
- For the g9/2 shell, the normalization factor for the |[pp(9)nn(9)]^{I=2}⟩ state is N(9)^{-2} = 0.499993950935, giving N ≈ 1.414222 (very close to √2).
- For the |[pp(9)nn(7)]^{I=2}⟩ state, N(7)^{-2} = 0.250376267385, giving N ≈ 1.998497 (very close to 2).
- The overlap coupling term in Eq. (i2) for g9/2 is 0.00009113; with exact normalization factors it becomes 0.00025756.
- The coupling U9-j values decrease exponentially with shell: for p3/2 it is -0.1800, for d5/2 -0.021328, for f7/2 -0.002074, for g9/2 -0.0001822, for h11/2 -0.00001502, and for i13/2 -0.000001185.
- For the I=8+ state in 96Cd, the overlap between the single U9-j wave function and the full diagonalization result is 0.9998.
- In 48Cr, there are three J=1+, T=0 states in the (f7/2)^8 configuration; two have s=-1 at 7.775 and 9.258 MeV, one has s=+1 at 9.037 MeV.
- For the (2j-1) rule: in 44Sc, the I=6 state lies at 0.271 MeV (exp.) vs 0.381 MeV (theory), lower than I=5 (1.513/1.276) and I=7 (0.968/1.272); in 96Ag, I=8 is the ground state (0.000), with I=7 at 0.861 MeV (theory) and I=9 at 0.470/0.492 MeV.
- For 96Cd, the Pandya transformation method gives J=16+ at 3.898 MeV vs 5.245 MeV from the CCGI interaction.

## Context
This work extends earlier single-j-shell studies by Raduta, Moya de Guerra, and Zamick (2003) on the E(J_max) interaction, and by Escuderos and Zamick (2006) on unique seniority-4 states in the g9/2 shell. The authors address degeneracy problems that obscure the emergence of unique states, as previously noted with pairing interactions by Zamick (2007) and others. The paper also connects to counting of states by Talmi (2005) and Zhao and Arima (2005), and to M1 excitation reviews by Heyde, Von Neumann-Cosel, and Richter (2010). The results enable further study of isospin structure in single-j configurations and provide analytic benchmarks for more realistic interactions like CCGI.
