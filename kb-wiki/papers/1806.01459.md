# The Effect of Multiplicity Cut on Particle Ratios at 7 TeV Proton-Proton Collisions

> The final state hadronic composition of proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV as a function of charged particle multiplicity density is studied. The thermal model is adjusted to match the experimental conditions in order to understand the effect of the experimental cuts on rapidity and multiplicity. This study indicates that the measured hadronic composition under a simultaneous cut on rapidity and multiplicity does not coincide with the fireball hadronic composition. Excluding proton to pion ratio, the adjusted model can explain the measured particle ratios. Therefore, the observed strangeness enhancement as a function of charged particle multiplicity density could be an effect of the experimental cuts.

## Key claim

The observed strangeness enhancement as a function of charged particle multiplicity density in 7 TeV proton-proton collisions could be a pure effect of the experimental cuts on rapidity and multiplicity, rather than reflecting the true fireball hadronic composition, provided the proton-to-pion ratio is excluded as an anomaly also seen in Pb-Pb collisions.

## Method

The author adjusted the statistical hadronization model to match the experimental conditions by interpreting the multiplicity cut as a cut on fireball rapidities. Two fireballs (right-moving and left-moving) were assumed to be distributed uniformly along the rapidity axis, with their rapidity distributions fitted to the measured charged particle pseudorapidity distribution. Particle yields under simultaneous cuts on pseudorapidity (|eta| <= 0.5) and charged particle multiplicity density were calculated by summing over all fireball rapidity pairs that produce a given multiplicity, weighted by their probabilities. A simplified version using only the most probable fireball configuration (where one fireball dominates the midrapidity contribution) was then used to extract fireball rapidities by fitting experimental particle ratios.

## Key numbers

* The fireball hadronic composition probabilities (fraction of charged particles) used were: pions 0.84301, kaons 0.10739, protons 0.046562, Xi 0.002928, Omega 0.0002534, based on experimental yields at 7 TeV.
* The temperature was taken as 0.163 GeV, from ALICE (2017).
* The maximum fireball rapidity was calculated as Y_FB^inel = cosh^{-1}(0.5*sqrt(s_NN)/(m_p + <m_T^pi>)), where sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV.
* The fit of the pseudorapidity distribution yielded N_ch^0 = 43.4605 and n = -0.011393 with chi^2/Ndf = 0.5346.
* The Gaussian parameters converting fireball rapidity to charged particle multiplicity density at |eta| <= 0.5 from one fireball were C0 = 21.986, C1 = -0.0606016, C2 = 0.779198.
* The fireball ratios (without multiplicity cut) used were: (K_S^0)/(pi^+ + pi^-) = 0.1274, (p+bar{p})/(pi^+ + pi^-) = 0.055011, (Xi+bar{Xi})/(pi^+ + pi^-) = 0.003519, (Omega+bar{Omega})/(pi^+ + pi^-) = 0.000301, and (Lambda+bar{Lambda})/(pi^+ + pi^-) = 0.0334 (from SHM at zero-net baryon density and 0.1585 GeV temperature).
* The extracted fireball rapidity Y_FB1 (from fitting without proton-to-pion ratio) ranged from 0.8127 at multiplicity density 2.26 down to 0.4973 at multiplicity density 21.29, with chi^2/Ndf values from 0.50/2 to 3.52/2.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model (SHM) studies of Becattini (1996) and Das (2016) for pp collisions, and directly addresses the ALICE (2017) observation of strangeness enhancement with multiplicity at 7 TeV. Prior attempts by Vislavicius (2016) and Sharma (2018) to describe this data with canonical or grand canonical ensembles showed poor agreement (chi^2/Ndf from 3 to 10). The author argues that the rapidity cut alone has no effect at LHC energies (contra Becattini, 2003), but the combination with a multiplicity cut produces the observed enhancement. The work suggests that multiplicity scaling across different collision systems may be valid only if the multiplicity cut is the sole applied cut, and calls for further investigation at higher energies and in p-Pb collisions.
