# Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory for Fermionic Superfluids: from Cold Atomic Gases, to Nuclei and Neutron Stars Crust

> In cold atoms and in the crust of neutron stars the pairing gap can reach values comparable with the Fermi energy. While in nuclei the neutron gap is smaller, it is still of the order of a few percent of the Fermi energy. The pairing mechanism in these systems is due to short range attractive interactions between fermions and the size of the Cooper pair is either comparable to the inter-particle separation or it can be as big as a nucleus, which is still relatively small in size. Such a strong pairing gap is the result of the superposition of a very large number of particle-particle configurations, which contribute to the formation of the Copper pairs. These systems have been shown to be the host of a large number of remarkable phenomena, in which the large magnitude of the pairing gap plays an essential role: quantum shock waves, quantum turbulence, Anderson-Higgs mode, vortex rings, domain walls, soliton vortices, vortex pinning in neutron star crust, unexpected dynamics of fragmented condensates and role of pairing correlations in collisions on heavy-ions, Larkin-Ovchinnikov phase as an example of a Fermi supersolid, role pairing correlations control the dynamics of fissioning nuclei, self-bound superfluid fermion droplets of extremely low densities.

# Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory for Fermionic Superfluids: from Cold Atomic Gases, to Nuclei and Neutron Stars Crust

## Key claim

The paper argues that a time-dependent extension of Kohn-Sham density functional theory to superfluid fermionic systems (TDSLDA) can describe strongly paired superfluids across vastly different scales, from cold atomic gases to nuclei and neutron star crust, provided the energy density functional includes not only number and kinetic densities but also anomalous density and current densities. The key hedge is that while the formalism is exact in principle, the energy density functional must be constructed phenomenologically or from quantum Monte Carlo data, and for nuclear systems the accuracy of existing phenomenological functionals remains insufficient for many applications despite sub-percent binding energy predictions.

## Method

The author extends the Kohn-Sham local density approximation (LDA) of DFT to superfluid systems by introducing the anomalous density (pairing field) as an additional order parameter, and for time-dependent problems also the current density. The resulting TDSLDA equations take the form of time-dependent Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for quasiparticle amplitudes, which are discretized on a 3D spatial lattice and propagated in time using a 6th order predictor-corrector-modifier scheme. For the unitary Fermi gas, the energy density functional is constrained by dimensional analysis, symmetries, and Galilean covariance, with three dimensionless parameters fixed by quantum Monte Carlo results for the Bertsch parameter, pairing gap, and quasiparticle dispersion.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter for unitary Fermi gas: quantum Monte Carlo value 0.372(5) and experimental value 0.376(5), in good agreement
- Pairing gap in unitary Fermi gas can reach values comparable to the Fermi energy; in nuclei the neutron gap is of order a few percent of the Fermi energy
- Ground state energy of uniform unitary Fermi gas scales as E_gs = xi N (3/5) epsilon_F, where xi is the Bertsch parameter
- Comparison of SLDA predictions with quantum Monte Carlo for trapped unitary fermions shows agreement within 1-3% for most systems; largest disagreement is 15% for the two-fermion system (N_up=1, N_down=1)
- For the (N_up, N_down) = (15,14) system the discrepancy is 9.5%, attributed to inaccuracies in quantum Monte Carlo results
- Ground state energies of harmonically trapped unpolarized unitary fermions scale as sqrt(xi) E_NI proportional to hbar omega N^(4/3)
- Number of coupled complex 3D time-dependent nonlinear partial differential equations evolved can reach O(10^6)
- Vortex core density in unitary Fermi gas is approximately half the asymptotic density value

## Context

This work extends the Kohn-Sham DFT formulation (Kohn and Sham, 1965) and its earlier extension to superconductivity by Oliveira, Gross, and Kohn (1988) to time-dependent superfluid systems with strong pairing correlations. It directly addresses limitations of the Landau two-fluid hydrodynamics (Landau, 1941; Khalatnikov, 2000), which cannot describe quantized vortex formation, vortex crossing and recombination, or the conversion between superfluid and normal components. The Gross-Pitaevskii equation (Gross, 1961; Pitaevskii, 1961) for weakly interacting bosons is also inadequate for strongly interacting fermionic superfluids. The TDSLDA approach enables first-principles simulations of non-equilibrium phenomena such as quantum turbulence, vortex lattice formation, the Anderson-Bogoliubov-Higgs mode, and the Larkin-Ovchinnikov phase, and can be applied to cold atomic gases, nuclei, and neutron star crust. The paper notes that the dilute neutron matter in neutron star crust is close to a unitary Fermi gas, as first pointed out by Bertsch (1999).
