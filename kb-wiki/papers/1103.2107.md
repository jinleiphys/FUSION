# Shallow Efimov tetramer as inelastic virtual state and resonant enhancement of the atom-trimer relaxation

> We use exact four-boson scattering equations in the momentum-space framework to study the universal properties of shallow Efimov tetramers and their dependence on the two-boson scattering length. We demonstrate that, in contrast to previous predictions, the shallow tetramer in a particular experimentally unexplored regime is not an unstable bound state but an inelastic virtual state. This leads to a resonant behaviour of the atom-trimer scattering length and thereby to a resonant enhancement of the trimer relaxation in ultracold atom-trimer mixtures.

# Shallow Efimov tetramer as inelastic virtual state and resonant enhancement of the atom-trimer relaxation

## Key claim

Contrary to previous predictions that the shallow Efimov tetramer lies entirely below the atom-trimer threshold (ATT) as an unstable bound state, the tetramer in a particular experimentally unexplored interval of positive two-boson scattering length crosses the ATT twice and becomes an inelastic virtual state (IVS) rather than an unstable bound state. This crossing produces a resonant behavior of the atom-trimer scattering length and a resonant enhancement of trimer relaxation in ultracold atom-trimer mixtures.

## Method

The author solved exact Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) four-particle scattering equations in momentum-space partial-wave framework, using an S-wave separable two-boson potential with two different momentum-space form factors to demonstrate universality. The complex energy of the shallow tetramer was extracted from atom-trimer scattering amplitudes via pole expansions and effective range expansions, allowing determination of both the tetramer position and width as functions of the two-boson scattering length.

## Key numbers

- At the unitary limit (a to infinity), the universal shallow tetramer position relative to the associated trimer binding energy is (B_{n,2}/b_n - 1) x 10^3 = 2.28 for n >= 3, with width (Gamma_{n,2}/2b_n) x 10^4 = 2.38
- The critical scattering length ratios where the tetramer transitions between UBS and IVS are a_n^{dd}/a_n^{v,1} approximately 0.0729 and a_n^{dd}/a_n^{v,2} approximately 0.99836
- For n=0 (lowest tetramer), (B_{0,2}/b_0 - 1) x 10^3 = 41.9 with no width reported (true bound state)
- For n=1 with c2=0 form factor, (B_{1,2}/b_1 - 1) x 10^3 = 1.06 and (Gamma_{1,2}/2b_1) x 10^4 = 3.82
- The deeper tetramer (k=1) has B_{n,1}/b_n = 4.611(1) from prior work, compared to 4.58 from Stecher (2009) with about 1% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body physics of Efimov states first predicted by Efimov (1970) and later observed in ultracold atom experiments by Kraemer (2006), Knoop (2009), and others. It corrects the predictions of Stecher (2009) and Hammer (2007), who using standard bound-state techniques could not calculate the finite widths of unstable tetramers and predicted the shallow tetramer never intersects the atom-trimer threshold. The results enable experimental observation of the UBS-IVS conversion by measuring trimer relaxation rates in ultracold mixtures of atoms and excited Efimov trimers as a function of the two-boson scattering length, which can be controlled via external magnetic fields near Feshbach resonances.
