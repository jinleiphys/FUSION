# Reply to Baur's and Bertulani's Comment

> Shortcomings of a momentum-space treatment of strong absorption, as discussed in the previous Comment [nucl-th/9611025], are only of concern at low projectile energies, $γ$< 1.5. At intermediate and high energies, for which the quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectrum is intended, the quantum-mechanical cross sections are reduced relative to the semi-classical results whether one treats strong effects via a momentum-space or an impact-parameter (spatial) cut-off. At these energies the origin of the discrepancy between the predictions of full quantuym-mechanical and the semi-classical calulations cannot be traced to differences in the treatment of strong-interaction effects. Rather, they arise from quantum effects neglected in the semi-classical calculations.

# Reply to Baur's and Bertulani's Comment

## Key claim

The authors argue that the criticism raised in the preceding Comment, that discrepancies between quantum-mechanical and semi-classical Coulomb excitation calculations arise from an incomplete momentum-space treatment of strong absorption, is valid only at low projectile energies (γ < 1.5). At intermediate and high energies (γ > 1.5), the quantum-mechanical cross sections are reduced relative to semi-classical results regardless of whether strong absorption is treated via momentum-space or impact-parameter cut-off, and the discrepancy instead originates from quantum and kinematic effects neglected in semi-classical calculations.

## Method

The authors compare their own quantum-mechanical equivalent-photon spectrum calculations (using a momentum-space cut-off for strong absorption) with the earlier quantum eikonal calculation of Jäckle and Pilkuhn (which treats strong absorption via an impact-parameter cut-off, as advocated in the Comment) and with standard semi-classical results. They examine the equivalent photon number per charge for a 20 MeV dipole transition assuming a pointlike projectile, across a range of projectile energies, to isolate the effects of strong-absorption treatment from genuine quantum-mechanical versus semi-classical differences.

## Key numbers

- The criticism regarding momentum-space treatment of strong absorption is only justified at low projectile energies where γ < 1.5.
- The intermediate energy range of interest for the experimental data is 1.5 < γ < 3.0.
- The equivalent photon number comparison is shown for a 20 MeV dipole transition.
- Jäckle and Pilkuhn's quantum calculations find cross sections that are smaller at all energies than the usual semi-classical results, with comparable reductions to those found by Benesh et al. for all but the lowest projectile energies (γ < 1.5).
- The kinematic restriction on three-momentum transfer in the projectile's rest frame is given by √(-q²) ≥ ω_T / (γβ).

## Context

This reply addresses the Comment by Baur and Bertulani (nucl-th/9611025) on the authors' earlier work (Benesh et al.; Benesh and Friar) concerning Coulomb excitation in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. The authors compare their results with the earlier quantum eikonal calculation of Jäckle and Pilkuhn, which treats strong absorption via impact-parameter cut-off, and with the semi-classical approach of Baur and Bertulani. The work clarifies that at intermediate energies, quantum effects, not strong-absorption treatment, cause the reduction in cross sections, and the authors point to experimental evidence in single-neutron-removal cross sections (Tables III and IV of their earlier paper) supporting this conclusion.
