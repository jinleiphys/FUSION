# Pseudogap of Color Superconductivity in Heated Quark Matter

> We show that the pseudogap of the quark density of states is formed in hot quark matter as a precursory phenomenon of the color superconductivity on the basis of a low-energy effective theory. We clarify that the decaying process of quarks near Fermi surface to a hole and the diquark soft mode (qq)_{soft} is responsible for the formation of the pseudogap. Our result suggests that the pseudogap is a universal phenomenon in strong coupling superconductors.

## Key claim

The pseudogap in the quark density of states forms in hot quark matter as a precursory phenomenon of color superconductivity, and this effect arises specifically from the decay process of quarks near the Fermi surface into a hole and a diquark soft mode (qq)\(_{\rm soft}\), suggesting that the pseudogap is a universal phenomenon in strong coupling superconductors even in three-dimensional systems.

## Method

The authors employed a low-energy effective theory using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with scalar-diquark interaction in the chiral limit, with a three-dimensional momentum cutoff \(\Lambda=650\) MeV. They calculated the quark self-energy incorporating pre-forming pair-field fluctuations of color superconductivity in the T-matrix approximation (non-self-consistent), where amplitude fluctuations of the pair field were assumed dominant over phase fluctuations. The density of states was obtained from the spectral function of a single quark via the retarded Green function, using a compact analytic expression for the imaginary part of the particle-particle correlation function to improve numerical efficiency.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature \(T_c = 40.04\) MeV at chemical potential \(\mu = 400\) MeV.
- The ratio of density of states to free quark density of states at the Fermi energy: \(N(\omega)/N_0(\omega)|_{\omega=0} \simeq 0.64\) at reduced temperature \(\varepsilon \equiv (T-T_c)/T_c = 0.01\) and \(\mu = 400\) MeV.
- The pseudogap structure survives up to \(\varepsilon \approx 0.05\) (i.e., \(T \approx 1.05 T_c\)) at \(\mu = 400\) MeV.
- The \(\varepsilon\) dependence of the pseudogap width hardly changes for chemical potentials in the range \(320\) MeV \(< \mu < 500\) MeV.
- The three-dimensional momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 650\) MeV was used to reproduce the pion decay constant \(f_{\pi} = 93\) MeV and the chiral condensate \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle = (-250\ {\rm MeV})^3\).
- The diquark coupling constant was chosen as \(G_C = 3.11\ {\rm GeV}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of precursory fluctuation effects in color superconductivity, building on the earlier demonstration by Kitazawa, Koide, Kunihiro, and Nemoto (2003) that a soft mode of the diquark pair field develops above \(T_c\) according to the Thouless criterion. The pseudogap region found here is more than one order of magnitude wider in reduced temperature \(\varepsilon\) than in nuclear matter as studied by Schnell (1999), where the clear pseudogap was seen only up to \(\varepsilon \approx 0.0025\). This result enables future investigation of pseudogap observability in heavy-ion collisions and proto-neutron stars, and suggests that pseudogap phenomena may be universal in any strong coupling superconductivity without requiring low dimensionality as in high-\(T_c\) cuprates.
