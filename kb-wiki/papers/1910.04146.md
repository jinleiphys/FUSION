# Short-range correlations for neutrinoless double-beta decay and low-momentum NN potentials

> We approach the calculation of the nuclear matrix element of the neutrinoless double-beta decay process, considering the light-neutrino-exchange channel, by way of the realistic shell-model. In particular the focus of our work is spotted on the role of the short-range correlations, which should be taken into account because of the short-range repulsion of the realistic potentials. Our shell-model wave functions are calculated using an effective Hamiltonian derived from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, the latter renormalized by way of the so-called V-low-k approach. The renormalization procedure decouples the repulsive high-momentum component of the potential from the low-momentum ones by the introduction of a cutoff Lambda, and is employed to renormalize consistently the two-body neutrino potentials to calculate the nuclear matrix elements of candidates to this decay process in mass interval ranging from A=76 up to A=136. We study the dependence of the decay operator on the choice of the cutoff, and compare our results with other approaches that can be found in present literature.

## Key claim

The V-low-k renormalization of the neutrinoless double-beta decay operator, applied consistently with the renormalization of the nucleon-nucleon potential, produces only a tiny (about 2%) softening of the nuclear matrix element, in contrast to the 20-25% reduction from standard Jastrow-type short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors start from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and renormalize it via the V-low-k approach, which introduces a cutoff Lambda to decouple high-momentum components. They then derive shell-model effective Hamiltonians using many-body perturbation theory and diagonalize them to obtain wave functions for parent and grand-daughter nuclei of four double-beta decay candidates (A=76 to A=136). The two-body neutrino potentials for the light-neutrino-exchange channel are consistently renormalized using the same unitary transformation Omega_low-k, and the nuclear matrix elements are computed with both bare and renormalized operators for two different cutoff values.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge decay: bare operator NME = 3.35; with Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 NME = 3.29 (1.8% reduction); with Lambda=2.1 fm^-1 NME = 3.27 (2.4% reduction)
- For 82Se decay: bare operator NME = 3.30; with Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 NME = 3.25 (1.5% reduction); with Lambda=2.1 fm^-1 NME = 3.23 (2.1% reduction)
- For 130Te decay: bare operator NME = 3.27; with Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 NME = 3.22 (1.6% reduction); with Lambda=2.1 fm^-1 NME = 3.20 (2.1% reduction)
- For 136Xe decay: bare operator NME = 2.47; with Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 NME = 2.43 (1.6% reduction); with Lambda=2.1 fm^-1 NME = 2.41 (2.4% reduction)
- The tensor component contribution is 2-3 orders of magnitude smaller than the Fermi and Gamow-Teller components and is neglected

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations of Coraggio (2017) and Coraggio (2019) for two-neutrino double-beta decay to the neutrinoless case, using the same wave functions. The V-low-k renormalization approach produces a much smaller effect (about 2%) than the 20-25% reduction from Jastrow-type correlations used in earlier work, and is comparable to the 5-6% reduction from the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) reported by Menendez (2009). The authors plan to next build shell-model effective operators for the two-body decay operator using many-body perturbation theory, following the approach of Wu (1985) and Holt (2013), to achieve fully consistent calculations without parameters fitted to experiment.
