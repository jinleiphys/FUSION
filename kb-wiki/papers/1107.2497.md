# Hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars. For the hadronic sector, we use a microscopic equation of state involving nucleons and hyperons derived within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory with realistic two-body and three-body forces. For the description of quark matter, we employ the Dyson-Schwinger approach and compare with the MIT bag model. We calculate the structure of neutron star interiors comprising both phases and find that with the Dyson-Schwinger model, the hadron-quark phase transition takes place only when hyperons are excluded, and that a two-solar-mass hybrid star is possible only if the nucleonic equation of state is stiff enough.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars modeled with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model occurs only when hyperons are excluded from the hadronic equation of state, and a two-solar-mass hybrid star is possible only if the nucleonic equation of state is stiff enough.

## Method

The authors combined a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state for hadronic matter (including nucleons and hyperons, with two-body and three-body forces) with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model for quark matter, using a Gaussian-type effective interaction with a density-dependent parameter alpha. They applied both Maxwell and Gibbs constructions for the hadron-quark phase transition and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for purely nucleonic stars: 1.84 solar masses (UIX model) and 2.50 solar masses (BOB model)
- Maximum masses for stars including hyperons: 1.30 solar masses (UIX model) and 1.37 solar masses (BOB model)
- Maximum mass of MIT bag model hybrid star: 1.5 solar masses
- Effective bag constant for light quarks in vacuum: 90 MeV fm^-3
- Pressure difference between Nambu and Wigner phases for massless quarks: 45 MeV fm^-3
- Dyson-Schwinger model parameters: omega = 0.5 GeV, D = 1 GeV^2, strange quark mass = 115 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous investigations using different quark models (MIT bag model, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, color dielectric model) for hybrid star calculations. The authors find that the Dyson-Schwinger model produces a monotonically increasing effective bag constant with density, similar to results from the color dielectric model and the NJL model (Buballa 2005), but in contrast to the empirical density-dependent bag model used in earlier work (nsquark 2004). The results show that with the Dyson-Schwinger model, no phase transition occurs when hyperons are present, and hybrid stars reaching two solar masses require a stiff nucleonic equation of state such as the Bonn B potential.
