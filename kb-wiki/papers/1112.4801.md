# Single j-shell studies of cross-conjugate nuclei and isomerism

> It is noted that in a calculation with 4 nucleons with isospin 1 in a single j shell (f_{7/2}, g_{9/2}, h_{11/2}) the state with median angular momentum J = (J_{max}+1)/2 lies very low in energy becoming either an isomeric state or a ground state. Also states with J = J_{max} are isomeric for the orbits g_{9/2} and h_{11/2}. Comparisons with experiment are made. A hybrid mixture of shell model and rotational model arguments are used to explain the (J_{max} +1)/2 rule.

## Key claim

In single-j-shell calculations for four nucleons with isospin T=1 in the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells, the state with median angular momentum J = (Jmax+1)/2 lies very low in energy, becoming either an isomeric state or a ground state depending on whether the nucleus is the lighter or heavier member of a cross-conjugate pair; this pattern holds only when using the two-hole spectrum as input for the heavier member rather than the two-particle spectrum.

## Method

The authors performed single-j-shell calculations for systems of three protons and one neutron (or three neutron holes and one proton hole) in the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells, using two-body matrix elements derived from experimental spectra of two-particle systems (INTa for lighter members) and two-hole systems (INTb for heavier members). For the g9/2 shell, two interactions were used: INTc (with T=0 matrix elements from a delta interaction) and INTd (with the Jmax=9 two-body matrix element artificially lowered to simulate the two-hole behavior). For the h11/2 shell, a Q·Q interaction was employed. The calculated yrast spectra were compared with experimental data for cross-conjugate pairs such as 44Ti/52Fe, 44Sc/52Mn, and 96Ag/96Cd.

## Key numbers

- For f7/2 shell: Jmax = 11, so (Jmax+1)/2 = 6; the J=6+ state in 44Sc has a half-life of 58.6 hours (isomeric), while in the cross-conjugate 52Mn the J=6+ state is the ground state with a half-life of 5.591 days
- For g9/2 shell: Jmax = 15, so (Jmax+1)/2 = 8; in 96Ag, nearly degenerate J=2+ and J=8+ isomers have respective half-lives of 6.9(6) s and 4.40(6) s
- For h11/2 shell: Jmax = 19, so (Jmax+1)/2 = 10; the J=10 state is calculated at 0.21 MeV with only J=2 (0.11 MeV) and J=1 (0.00 MeV) below it
- In 44Ti, the 12+ state is calculated at 7.702 MeV (INTa) versus experimental 8.040 MeV; in 52Fe, the 12+ state is calculated at 6.514 MeV (INTb) versus experimental 6.958 MeV
- In 96Cd, the J=16+ state is calculated at 4.937 MeV (INTd), lower than both J=15+ (5.564 MeV) and J=14+ (5.030 MeV), guaranteeing isomerism

## Context

This work extends the single-j-shell studies of Zamick, Escuderos, and Bayman (2005) and earlier work by Zamick (2002) on cross-conjugate nuclei, applying the (Jmax+1)/2 rule to isomerism in four-nucleon T=1 systems. The results are compared with large-space shell-model calculations by Ur et al. (1998) using the KB3 interaction and by Puddu (2009) using GXPF1A for 52Fe, as well as experimental findings by Nara Singh et al. (2011) on the 16+ isomer in 96Cd and by Grawe et al. (2006) on spin-gap states in the 100Sn region. The work enables systematic predictions of isomeric states in cross-conjugate pairs across the f7/2, g9/2, and h11/2 shells, and connects the single-j-shell results to the Gallagher-Moszkowski (1958) scheme for ground-state spins of odd-odd nuclei.
