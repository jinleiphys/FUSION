# Approximations of the spectral function

> The ICARUS and future liquid argon neutrino experiments generate demand for evaluating the spectral function of argon. In this paper we use oxygen nucleus as a testing ground for our phenomenological approach to the spectral function and probe the influence of momentum distribution and treatment of the mean field spectral function on the differential cross sections. The obtained model reproduces very well results of the exact spectral function of oxygen and can be applied to heavier nuclei, such as calcium or argon.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a phenomenological approximation of the spectral function, combining a Gaussian-broadened mean-field term with the correct momentum distribution, reproduces the exact spectral function of oxygen to within the accuracy needed for neutrino cross-section calculations. The key condition is that the approximation works only when both the momentum distribution and the mean-field treatment are simultaneously improved, not when either is changed alone.

## Method
The author constructed a spectral function for oxygen-16 by replacing the delta-function energy dependence of the mean-field term with Gaussian distributions whose widths were fitted to the exact Benhar spectral function. The momentum distribution was taken directly from the Benhar spectral function rather than from the earlier Ciofi degli Atti and Simula parameterization. The resulting spectral function was used to compute quasielastic muon-neutrino differential cross sections dσ/dEμ, both with and without Pauli blocking, and compared to the exact Benhar spectral function results.

## Key numbers
- The threshold energy for the correlation part of the spectral function in oxygen is E^(2) = 26.33 MeV.
- The mean value of momentum for oxygen is |p| = 180 MeV/c, giving a recoil energy of approximately 1 MeV.
- The energy distribution S_MF(E) from the Benhar spectral function vanishes for E ≳ 87 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier simplest spectral function approximation of Ankowski and Sobczyk (2005) for argon, which showed discrepancies from the exact Benhar spectral function for oxygen. The paper uses oxygen as a test case because exact spectral functions exist only for a few double-closed-shell nuclei (Benhar, Fabrocini, Fantoni, and Sick; Benhar and Farina; Benhar and Pandharipande). The improved approximation is simple enough to apply to heavier nuclei such as calcium-40 and argon-40, where exact computations cannot be performed, enabling more accurate predictions for the ICARUS and future liquid-argon neutrino experiments.
