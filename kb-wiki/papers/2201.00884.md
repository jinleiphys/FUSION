# Emergence of pion parton distributions

> Supposing only that there is an effective charge which defines an evolution scheme for parton distribution functions (DFs) that is all-orders exact, strict lower and upper bounds on all Mellin moments of the valence-quark DFs of pion-like systems are derived. Exploiting contemporary results from numerical simulations of lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics (QCD) that are consistent with these bounds, parameter-free predictions for pion valence, glue, and sea DFs are obtained. The form of the valence-quark DF at large values of the light-front momentum fraction is consistent with predictions derived using the QCD-prescribed behaviour of the pion wave function.

## Key claim

The authors derive strict lower and upper bounds on all Mellin moments of valence-quark distribution functions (DFs) of pion-like systems, assuming only that an effective charge exists which defines an all-orders exact evolution scheme; using lattice QCD moments consistent with these bounds, they obtain parameter-free predictions for all pion DFs, with the valence-quark DF at large momentum fraction matching QCD-prescribed behavior.

## Method

The authors exploit the hypothesis (P1) that an effective charge exists such that one-loop DGLAP evolution becomes all-orders exact, which yields a recursion relation linking odd Mellin moments to even moments. They combine contemporary lattice-QCD results for low-order Mellin moments of the pion valence-quark DF from three independent groups (Joo 2019, Sufian 2019, Alexandrou 2021) at scales of 2 GeV and 5.2 GeV, then perform a chi-squared minimization to fit a symmetric hadron-scale distribution, generating an ensemble of DFs via a Monte Carlo acceptance procedure. Using the first moment at 5.2 GeV from an uncertainty-weighted average of lattice and continuum Schwinger method results, they evolve the ensemble to produce valence, glue, and sea DFs at that scale.

## Key numbers

- First Mellin moment of the pion valence-quark DF at ζ=2 GeV: ⟨x⟩ = 0.254(03) from Joo (2019); 0.18(3) from Sufian (2019); 0.23(3)(7) from Alexandrou (2021)
- Second moment at ζ=2 GeV: ⟨x²⟩ = 0.094(12) from Joo (2019); 0.064(10) from Sufian (2019); 0.087(05)(08) from Alexandrou (2021)
- Third moment at ζ=2 GeV: ⟨x³⟩ = 0.057(04) from Joo (2019); 0.030(05) from Sufian (2019); 0.041(05)(09) from Alexandrou (2021)
- Uncertainty-weighted average valence momentum fraction at ζ₅=5.2 GeV: ⟨2x⟩ = 0.435(12)
- Large-x exponent from lattice-based ensemble at ζ₅: β = 2.45(38), consistent with QCD prediction (1-x)^(2+γ)
- Glue momentum fraction at ζ₅: ⟨x⟩_g = 0.435(06); sea momentum fraction: ⟨x⟩_S = 0.125(05)
- Best-fit parameters for valence DF at ζ₅: α = -0.168(79), β = 2.49(40), γ = 1.51(74)

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger method (CSM) predictions for pion structure from Cui (2020) and Chang (2021) by incorporating lattice-QCD results from Joo (2019), Sufian (2019), and Alexandrou (2021) to produce parameter-free predictions. It contradicts analyses of E615 data (Conway 1989) that yield a large-x exponent β≈1.3, which violates QCD expectations, and instead supports the soft-gluon resummation analysis of Aicher (2010) giving β=2.57(6). The results enable benchmarking of existing data fitting methods and can be validated by forthcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab and other facilities.
