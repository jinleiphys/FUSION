# Properties of Nucleon Resonances by means of a Genetic Algorithm

> We present an optimization scheme that employs a Genetic Algorithm (GA) to determine the properties of low-lying nucleon excitations within a realistic photo-pion production model based upon an effective Lagrangian. We show that with this modern optimization technique it is possible to reliably assess the parameters of the resonances and the associated error bars as well as to identify weaknesses in the models. To illustrate the problems the optimization process may encounter, we provide results obtained for the nucleon resonances $Δ$(1230) and $Δ$(1700). The former can be easily isolated and thus has been studied in depth, while the latter is not as well known experimentally.

# Properties of Nucleon Resonances by means of a Genetic Algorithm

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a hybrid optimization scheme combining a Genetic Algorithm with a gradient-based routine can reliably extract resonance parameters and associated error bars from photo-pion production data, but the reliability depends on how well isolated the resonance is: the Delta(1232) is easily characterized while the Delta(1700) remains poorly constrained due to the complex multi-minimum structure of the chi-squared surface.

## Method
The authors fit a phenomenological effective-Lagrangian model for single-pion photoproduction (gamma p -> pi0 p) to 3,760 data points consisting of the real and imaginary parts of six electromagnetic multipoles (E0+, M1-, E1+, M1+, E2-, M2-) from the SAID energy-independent extraction up to 1.2 GeV. The optimization uses a Genetic Algorithm with 400 individuals run for 400 generations to survey parameter space, followed by the NAG library routine E04FCF (a quasi-Newton method) for fine-tuning; the procedure is repeated twenty times with different random seeds to map the minima landscape and estimate parameter uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- 3,760 data points were used in the fits (1,880 real parts plus 1,880 imaginary parts of electromagnetic multipoles).
- The cut-off Lambda in the best fit was 0.943 GeV, restricted to the range [0.1, 2.0] GeV.
- The Delta(1232) helicity amplitudes obtained were A^Delta_{1/2} = -0.120 GeV^{-1/2} and A^Delta_{3/2} = -0.229 GeV^{-1/2}.
- The Delta(1700) helicity amplitudes obtained were A^Delta_{1/2} = 0.139 GeV^{-1/2} and A^Delta_{3/2} = -0.127 GeV^{-1/2}.
- The NAG routine improved the minima found by the GA alone by approximately 2 percent.
- The computation required the equivalent of five CPU-days on a 2 GHz Opteron processor.

## Context
This work extends the hybrid GA-plus-gradient approach previously applied by Ireland et al. to establish the existence of resonances, here repurposed to determine parameters of well-established resonances and their error bars. The reaction model builds on the phenomenological Lagrangian framework developed by Fernandez-Ramirez et al. (2006) and includes all four-star PDG resonances up to 1.8 GeV. The method enables unattended, reproducible extraction of resonance parameters from multipole data, and the comparison of the well-constrained Delta(1232) with the poorly-constrained Delta(1700) illustrates how the optimization itself can diagnose model weaknesses and identify resonances requiring additional experimental input.
