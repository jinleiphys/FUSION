# Coulomb corrections to density and temperature in heavy ion collisions

> A recently proposed method, based on quadrupole and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, is modified in order to take into account distortions due to the Coulomb field. The classical and quantum limits for fermions are discussed. In the classical case we find that the temperature determined from ${}^3He$ and ${}^3H$, after the Coulomb correction, are very similar to those obtained from neutrons within the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) approach. In the quantum case, the proton temperature becomes very similar to neutron's, while densities are not sensitive to the Coulomb corrections.

## Key claim
The paper modifies a previously proposed method for extracting density and temperature from quadrupole and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions to account for Coulomb distortions in the exit channel. The authors show that after Coulomb correction, temperatures from charged particles become very similar to those from neutrons, but only under the assumption that mirror nuclei (e.g., ³He and ³H) share the same temperature and volume.

## Method
The authors used Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) simulations of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at b=1 fm and beam energies from 4 MeV/A to 100 MeV/A, generating about 250,000 events per energy. They derived analytic expressions for quadrupole fluctuations (QF) and multiplicity fluctuations (MF) including a Coulomb correction term proportional to Z_p Z_s / (p² V T), assuming a form factor of 1 and negligible initial momentum. For the classical case, they solved for temperature T and volume V using mirror nuclei pairs (³H, ³He) and (p, n). For the quantum (Fermi-Dirac) case, they solved coupled integral equations for T and density ρ from QF and MF for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies ranged from 4 MeV/A to 100 MeV/A in the laboratory system for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at impact parameter b=1 fm.
- About 250,000 events were generated for each beam energy.
- In the classical case, Coulomb-corrected temperatures from ³He and ³H became very close to neutron temperatures; the fragmentation region terminated around T=10 MeV for protons and neutrons, close to the critical temperature.
- In the quantum case, proton temperatures after Coulomb correction became much closer to neutron temperatures, while densities showed very small modification from Coulomb effects.

## Context
This work extends the fluctuation-based method for determining temperature and density from heavy ion collisions (Zheng et al., 2012; 2013) by adding Coulomb corrections that were previously neglected. It addresses discrepancies observed in earlier work (Sara et al., 2010) where classical temperatures from quadrupole fluctuations gave different values for different isotopes. The results support the "near ergodicity" of the system in the transverse direction and enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear equation of state in the liquid-gas phase transition region, particularly for asymmetric matter. The authors note that experimental investigations at well-determined sources and excitation energies (Alan et al., 2012; Justin et al., 2012; Qin et al., 2012; Borderie et al., 2012) would be important to further constrain the equation of state.
