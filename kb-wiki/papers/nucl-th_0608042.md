# Dispersive and absorptive corrections to the pion-deuteron scattering length

> We present a parameter--free calculation of the dispersive and absorptive contributions to the pion--deuteron scattering length based on chiral perturbation theory. We show that once all diagrams contributing to leading order to this process are included, their net effect provides a small correction to the real part of the pion--deuteron scattering length. At the same time the sizable imaginary part of the pion--deuteron scattering length is reproduced accurately.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free calculation of dispersive and absorptive corrections to the pion-deuteron scattering length using chiral perturbation theory, showing that once all leading-order diagrams are included, their net effect on the real part is small (about 6.5% of the experimental real part) due to efficient cancellations among individually sizable terms, while the sizable imaginary part is reproduced accurately.

## Method

The authors computed the hadronic dispersive corrections from the reaction chains πd → NN → πd and πd → γNN → πd using chiral perturbation theory with a power counting that accounts for the large momentum scale p_thr = sqrt(m_π M_N) characteristic of near-threshold NN → NNπ production. They evaluated direct and crossed diagrams for the hadronic part, including NN interactions in the intermediate state via the CD-Bonn potential, and calculated the photonic part using the full πN → γN transition amplitude at leading order in Coulomb gauge.

## Key numbers

- Experimental πd scattering length: a_πd^exp = (-26.1 ± 0.5 + i (6.3 ± 0.7)) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), where m_π is the charged pion mass
- Hadronic contribution to the imaginary part from the calculation: Im(a_πd)_hadronic = (4.25 ± 1.2) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), compared to the experimental hadronic part (3/4)Im(a_πd^exp) = (4.7 ± 0.5) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Electromagnetic contribution to the imaginary part: (1.4 ± 0.4) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), compared to the experimental photonic part (1/4)Im(a_πd^exp) = (1.6 ± 0.2) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Total dispersive (real) correction: a_πd^disp = -1.7 × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1), giving a_πd^disp/Re(a_πd^exp) ≈ 6.5%
- Ratio of πd → NN to πd → γNN cross sections at threshold: R = 2.83 ± 0.04 (from Highland et al.)
- Individual hadronic direct diagram contributions (in units of 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)): d1 = 0.49 + i 6.68, d2 = -0.8 + i 0.1, d3 = -6.02 - i 1.65; sum of all direct terms = -4.37 + i 4.25
- Sum of all crossed hadronic terms (real part only): 2.74 × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)
- Photonic contribution: (-0.1 + i 1.4) × 10^(-3) m_π^(-1)

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion-deuteron scattering (Beane et al., 2002; Baru et al., 2003) by consistently including dispersive corrections from the πd → NN → πd and πd → γNN → πd reaction chains, which were previously treated only in Faddeev calculations (Afnan and Thomas, 1974) or phenomenological approaches (Doring et al., 2004; Boudard and Koltun, 1982). The calculation enables a more precise extraction of the isoscalar πN scattering length a_+ from future PSI measurements with 0.5% precision on the real part and 4% on the imaginary part, and calls for a complete calculation of isospin-breaking corrections at next-to-next-to-leading order in the effective field theory with virtual photons.
