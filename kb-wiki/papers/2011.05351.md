# W-boson production in TMD factorization

> At hadron colliders, the differential cross section for $W$ production can be factorized and it is sensitive transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMD) for low boson transverse momentum. While, often, the corresponding non-perturbative QCD contributions are extrapolated from $Z$ boson production, here we use an existing extraction (based on the code Artemide) of TMD which includes data coming from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, to provide checks and predictions for the $W$ case. Including fiducial cuts with different configurations and kinematical power corrections, we consider transverse momentum dependent cross sections within several intervals of the vector boson transverse mass. We perform the same study for the $p_T^{W^-}/p_T^{W^+}$ and $p_T^Z/p_T^W$ distributions. We compare our predictions with recent extractions of these quantities at ATLAS and CMS and results from TeVatron. The results encourage a broader experimental and phenomenological work, and a deeper study of TMD for the $W$ case.

## Key claim

The authors use an existing extraction of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions, which includes data from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, to provide predictions for W-boson production at hadron colliders, rather than extrapolating non-perturbative QCD contributions from Z-boson production as is commonly done.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for W-boson production within TMD factorization, using the Artemide code and the zeta-prescription for TMD evolution. They implement fiducial cuts typical of LHC experiments, include kinematical power corrections, and integrate over the W-boson invariant mass Q^2 to express results in terms of the transverse mass m_T. The non-perturbative TMD parameters are taken from a previous combined fit to Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering data (Scimemi 2019), and the cross section is evaluated for several intervals of m_T and transverse momentum q_T.

## Key numbers

*   The W-boson mass used is M_W = 80.379 GeV, and the W-boson width is Gamma_W = 2.085 GeV.
*   The Z-boson mass used is M_Z = 91.1876 GeV, and the Z-boson width is Gamma_Z = 2.4952 GeV.
*   The Fermi constant used is G_F = 1.1663787 x 10^{-5} GeV.
*   The analysis considers three m_T intervals: [50,66] GeV, [66,99] GeV, and [99,120] GeV, corresponding to values below, around, and above the W-boson mass.
*   The TMD factorization is applied for q_T/m_T less than or approximately equal to 0.2, consistent with the range of validity found in prior work (Scimemi 2017, Bacchetta 2019, Scimemi 2019).
*   The cross section is computed for LHC center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 13 TeV with a q_T binning of 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends TMD studies from neutral vector boson production (Z boson) to charged W-boson production, which is sensitive to the flavor dependence of TMD distributions. Prior extractions of TMD non-perturbative parameters (Scimemi 2019) used only neutral boson processes and SIDIS data, while earlier work (Signori 2013, Bacchetta 2018) suggested that W-boson data at the LHC should be sensitive to non-trivial flavor structure. The results aim to enable a deeper study of TMD flavor dependence, which is relevant for future measurements at the Electron Ion Collider.
