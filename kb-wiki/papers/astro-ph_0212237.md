# Energy release due to antineutrino untrapping and diquark condensation in hot quark star evolution

> We study the consequences of antineutrino trapping in hot quark matter for quark star configurations with possible diquark condensation. Due to the conditions of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium the flavor asymmetry increases with the number density of trapped antineutrinos such that above a critical value of the antineutrino chemical potential of 30 MeV diquark condensation is inhibited. When the quark star cools a two-phase structure occurs: a superconducting quark matter core surrounded by a shell of normal quark matter. Below the critical temperature of about 1 MeV, the antineutrino mean free path becomes larger than the thickness of the normal quark matter shell so that they get untrapped within a sudden process. By comparing the masses of configurations with the same baryon number we estimate that the release of energy due to the antineutrino untrapping transition can be in the range of 10^{51} to 10^{52} erg.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a first-order phase transition triggered by the sudden untrapping of antineutrinos from a normal quark matter shell, which had been inhibiting diquark condensation, can release an energy of \(10^{51}\) to \(10^{52}\) erg, potentially powering supernova explosions and gamma-ray bursts. This mechanism depends on the antineutrino chemical potential exceeding a critical value of 30 MeV to initially prevent diquark condensation, and on the star cooling below about 1 MeV to allow the antineutrino mean free path to exceed the shell thickness.

## Method

The authors model hot quark matter in beta-equilibrium with trapped antineutrinos using a nonlocal chiral quark model in the mean-field approximation, solving gap equations for the mass gap and diquark gap order parameters under conditions of charge and color neutrality. They compute equations of state for different antineutrino chemical potentials and then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain static, spherically symmetric quark star configurations. The energy release from antineutrino untrapping is estimated by comparing the gravitational masses of configurations with the same total baryon number, one with trapped antineutrinos (initial state) and one without (final state).

## Key numbers

- Critical antineutrino chemical potential for inhibiting diquark condensation: \(\mu_{\bar \nu_e}^{min} = 30\) MeV at a baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B = 1\) GeV.
- Critical temperature below which antineutrino untrapping occurs: \(T \sim 1\) MeV.
- For a reference configuration without antineutrinos and mass \(M_f = 1.4 M_\odot\), the radius is \(R_f = 10.47\) km and central density \(n_q = 9.69 n_0\) (with nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- For an initial state with \(\mu_{\bar \nu_e} = 100\) MeV and the same baryon number (\(N_B = 1.51 N_\odot\)), the mass is \(M_A = 1.4025 M_\odot\); for \(\mu_{\bar \nu_e} = 150\) MeV, the mass is \(M_B = 1.41 M_\odot\).
- Mass defect for the transition: \(\Delta M_{Af} = 0.25\% M_\odot\) (for \(\mu_{\bar \nu_e}=100\) MeV) to \(\Delta M_{Bf} = 1\% M_\odot\) (for \(\mu_{\bar \nu_e}=150\) MeV), corresponding to an energy release of \(10^{51}\) to \(10^{52}\) erg.

## Context

This work extends studies of diquark condensation in compact stars (Blaschke 2003) by including the effects of trapped antineutrinos, which were previously neglected. It contrasts with earlier estimates by Hong (2001) that suggested a large energy release from Cooper pairing alone, showing that general relativistic effects can counterbalance such naive estimates. The proposed antineutrino untrapping mechanism provides a first-order phase transition scenario that could explain the engine of supernova explosions and gamma-ray bursts, and predicts a second pulse of antineutrinos as an observable signature.
