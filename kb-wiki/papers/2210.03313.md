# Slope parameters determined from CREX and PREX2 (published in Results in Physics)

> [Background] Very lately, the CREX group presents a skin value $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{48}({\rm CREX}) =0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)}=0.071\sim 0.171$~fm. Meanwhile, the PREX group reported a skin value $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX2}) = 0.283\pm 0.071=0.212 \sim 0.354$~fm. In our previous paper, we determined both the $L$--$ΔR_{\rm skin}^{48}$ relation and the $L$--$ΔR_{\rm skin}^{208}$ one, using 206 EoSs, where $L$ is a slope parameter. [Purpose] We determine $L$ from $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{48}({\rm CREX})$ and $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX2}) $, using 207 EoSs. [Results] The $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{48}({\rm CREX})$ yields $L({\rm CREX})=0 \sim 51$~MeV and the $ΔR_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX2}) $ does $L({\rm PREX2})=76 \sim 165$~MeV. [Conclusion] There is no overlap between $L({\rm CREX})$ and $L({\rm PREX2})$. This is a big problem to be solved.

## Key claim

Using 207 equations of state (EoSs), the authors find that the slope parameter \(L\) extracted from the CREX neutron-skin measurement on \(^{48}\)Ca (\(L({\rm CREX}) = 0 \sim 51\) MeV) and from the PREX2 measurement on \(^{208}\)Pb (\(L({\rm PREX2}) = 76 \sim 165\) MeV) have no overlap at the one-sigma level, posing a significant tension that the abstract presents as a "big problem to be solved."

## Method

The authors compiled 207 EoSs from the literature, including Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean-field models, and established linear correlations between the neutron-skin thickness \(\Delta R_{\rm skin}\) and the slope parameter \(L\) for both \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{208}\)Pb. Using the experimental skin values from CREX and PREX2, they then inverted these correlations to extract the corresponding ranges for \(L\).

## Key numbers

- \(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{48}({\rm CREX}) = 0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)} = 0.071 \sim 0.171\) fm.
- \(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX2}) = 0.283 \pm 0.071 = 0.212 \sim 0.354\) fm.
- \(L({\rm CREX}) = 0 \sim 51\) MeV, derived from the \(L\)-\(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{48}\) relation with correlation coefficient \(R=0.98\).
- \(L({\rm PREX2}) = 76 \sim 165\) MeV, derived from the \(L\)-\(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{208}\) relation with correlation coefficient \(R=0.99\).
- The \(L\)-\(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{48}\) relation is \(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{48} = 0.0009 L + 0.125\) fm, implying a lower limit of 0.125 fm for \(^{48}\)Ca.
- The \(L\)-\(\Delta R_{\rm skin}^{208}\) relation is \(L = 620.39\ \Delta R_{\rm skin}^{208} - 57.963\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Tagami, Wakasa, Yahiro, 2022) that used 206 EoSs to determine the \(L\)-\(\Delta R_{\rm skin}\) relations, now incorporating 207 EoSs and the latest CREX and PREX2 results. The extracted \(L({\rm PREX2})\) range is consistent with the earlier result of RocaMaza (2011) (\(L = 76 \sim 172\) MeV) and with Reed (2021) (\(L = 106 \pm 37\) MeV). The tension between the CREX and PREX2 constraints rules out the APR EoS (Akmal, 1998), considered the most reliable among the set, and calls for new experimental or theoretical work to resolve the discrepancy.
