# Novel NN interaction and the spectroscopy of light nuclei

> Nucleon-nucleon (NN) phase shifts and the spectroscopy of $A \le 6$ nuclei are successfully described by an inverse scattering potential that is separable with oscillator form factors.

## Key claim

A nucleon-nucleon (NN) inverse scattering potential with oscillator form factors, called JISP6, can describe both NN phase shifts and the spectroscopy of nuclei up to A=6 without requiring explicit three-nucleon (NNN) forces, because off-shell modifications of the two-body potential effectively mimic the effects of many-body interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed J-matrix inverse scattering potentials (JISP) by fitting to Nijmegen np phase shifts in an oscillator basis with hbar-omega=40 MeV, using a 9-hbar-omega inverse scattering tridiagonal potential (ISTP) in odd partial waves and an 8-hbar-omega ISTP in even partial waves. They then applied phase equivalent transformations (PETs) with specific rotation angles in the coupled sd waves (11.3 degrees) and in the 3p2, 3p1, 3d2, and 1p1 waves (with angles +8, -6, +25, and -16 degrees respectively) to improve the description of the deuteron quadrupole moment and the 6Li spectrum. The resulting JISP6 interaction was used in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations with a Lee-Suzuki transformation to obtain a two-body effective interaction in a new basis (hbar-omega=15 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.224575 MeV (JISP6), matching the experimental value
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.2915 fm^2 (JISP6), compared to the experimental value 0.2859(3) fm^2
- Deuteron D-state probability: 4.1360% (JISP6), compared to 5.635% (Nijmegen-II), 5.76% (AV18), and 4.85% (CD-Bonn)
- 3H binding energy: 8.461(5) MeV (JISP6, NCSM in 14-hbar-omega model space), compared to the experimental value 8.48 MeV
- 4He binding energy: 28.611(41) MeV (JISP6, NCSM in 12-hbar-omega model space), compared to the experimental value 28.30 MeV
- 6Li ground state energy: 31.48(27) MeV (JISP6, NCSM in 10-hbar-omega model space), compared to the experimental value 31.995 MeV
- 6Li excitation energy of the 3+ state: 2.102(4) MeV (JISP6), compared to the experimental value 2.186 MeV
- 6Li excitation energy of the 0+ state: 3.348(24) MeV (JISP6), compared to the experimental value 3.563 MeV
- 6He 2+ excitation energy: 2.59(13) MeV (JISP6, NCSM in 8-hbar-omega model space), compared to the experimental value 1.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix inverse scattering approach previously developed by the same group (Shirokov et al., 2003) to construct NN potentials without meson-exchange or QCD constraints, in contrast to conventional potentials like Argonne AV18, CD-Bonn, and Nijmegen. The authors demonstrate that their JISP6 potential, with only NN interactions, achieves results competitive with calculations using both NN and NNN potentials (such as AV18+Urbana IX and AV8'+Tucson-Melbourne') for light nuclei up to A=6, supporting the Polyzou-Glockle theorem that off-shell changes in two-body potentials can substitute for many-body forces. This work enables future applications to heavier nuclei (A >= 7) and scattering problems, though the authors note that NNN potentials may eventually be needed when the approach breaks down.
