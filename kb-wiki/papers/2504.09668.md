# Deciphering the viscous properties and the Bjorken expansion of the QGP medium at finite angular velocity

> We have studied the viscous properties as well as the Bjorken expansion of a rotating QGP medium. In the noncentral events of heavy-ion collisions, the produced medium can carry a finite angular momentum with a finite range of angular velocity. This rotation can significantly affect various properties, including viscous properties and the expansion of the QGP medium. Using a novel relaxation time approximation for the collision integral in the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation at finite angular velocity, we have calculated the shear and bulk viscosities and compared them with their counterparts in the standard relaxation time approximation within the kinetic theory approach. Our results show that the angular velocity increases both shear and bulk viscosities, suggesting an enhanced momentum transfer within the medium and greater fluctuations in local pressure. This rotational effect on viscosities is more evident at lower temperatures than at higher temperatures. Our analysis also shows that, compared to the standard relaxation time approximation, the shear viscosity is lower while the bulk viscosity is higher in the novel relaxation time approximation for all temperatures. Additionally, some observables related to the flow characteristic, fluid behavior and conformal symmetry of the medium are markedly impacted due to rotation. We have also studied the hydrodynamic evolution of matter within the Bjorken boost-invariant scenario and have found that the energy density evolves faster in the presence of finite rotation than in the nonrotating case. Consequently, rapid rotation accelerates the cooling process of the QGP medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that finite angular velocity increases both shear and bulk viscosities of the QGP medium, with the effect more evident at lower temperatures, and that rapid rotation accelerates the cooling process during Bjorken expansion; however, these results depend on the specific choice of the novel relaxation time approximation and the quasiparticle model with thermally massive partons.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation using both the standard relaxation time approximation (RTA) and a novel RTA that preserves microscopic conservation laws, incorporating angular velocity-dependent parton distribution functions derived from the partition function for a rotating system. Shear and bulk viscosities were computed for a QGP medium composed of thermally massive quarks, antiquarks, and gluons with flavor degrees of freedom included, using momentum-independent relaxation times in standard RTA and momentum-dependent relaxation times in the novel RTA. The Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion was then studied by incorporating the angular velocity-dependent equation of motion.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity range considered: 0.01 GeV to 0.1 GeV (from noncentral heavy-ion collisions generating angular momentum between 10^3 hbar and 10^5 hbar)
- Shear viscosity increases with angular velocity compared to the nonrotating case, with the effect stronger at lower temperatures
- Bulk viscosity also increases with angular velocity compared to the nonrotating case, with the effect stronger at lower temperatures
- In the novel RTA, shear viscosity is lower while bulk viscosity is higher compared to standard RTA for all temperatures
- Energy density evolves faster in the presence of finite rotation than in the nonrotating case, leading to accelerated cooling

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in rotating QGP media, following the recent calculation of charge and heat transport coefficients using the novel relaxation time approximation by Rath (2025). It contrasts with studies of magnetized QGP expansion by Umut (2014), Roy (2015), and Rath (2019), and with viscous hydrodynamic expansion studies by Kajantie (1984), Baym (1984), Hosoya (1985), and Muronga (2002). The authors state that no prior studies on the effect of rotation on QGP expansion properties were available in the literature, so this work provides the first systematic exploration of rotational effects on viscous properties and Bjorken expansion dynamics.
