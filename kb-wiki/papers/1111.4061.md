# Strong CP violation and chiral symmetry breaking in hot and dense quark matter

> We investigate chiral symmetry breaking and strong CP violation effects in the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter. We demonstrate the effect of strong CP violating terms on the phase structure at finite temperature and densities in a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) determinant term. This is investigated using an explicit structure for the ground state in terms of quark-antiquark condensates for both in the scalar and the pseudoscalar channels. CP restoring transition with temperature at zero baryon density is found to be a second order transition at $θ= π$ while the same at finite chemical potential and small temperature turns out to be a first order transition. Within the model, the tri-critical point turns out to be $(T_c,μ_c)\simeq(273,94)$ MeV at $θ= π$ for such a transition.

## Key claim
Within a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) determinant term, the CP restoring transition at zero baryon density is second order at θ=π, while at finite chemical potential and small temperature it becomes first order; the abstract does not specify that this behavior is model-dependent and contrasts with linear sigma model results where the transition at θ=π is first order.

## Method
The authors use a 3-flavor NJL model with a KMT determinant term that incorporates the CP-violating θ parameter. They construct a variational ground state ansatz with quark-antiquark condensates in both scalar and pseudoscalar channels, then apply thermofield dynamics to include finite temperature and chemical potential. The thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain coupled gap equations for the scalar and pseudoscalar constituent quark masses, which are solved self-consistently for different values of θ, temperature, and chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- At zero temperature and zero density, the constituent masses for u-d quarks are 368 MeV and for strange quarks 549 MeV (using cutoff Λ=0.6023 GeV, G_sΛ²=1.835, KΛ⁵=12.36, m_q=5.5 MeV, m_s=0.1407 GeV).
- For θ=0 at zero temperature, the first order chiral transition occurs at μ≈361 MeV for u and d quarks, with masses dropping discontinuously to about 52 MeV from 368 MeV.
- For θ=π/2 at zero temperature, the critical chemical potential for chiral transition is μ_c≈375 MeV, where scalar and pseudoscalar contributions to u-quark mass become 24 MeV and 12 MeV respectively from vacuum values of 266 MeV and 248 MeV.
- For θ=π at zero temperature, the first order transition occurs at μ_c≈368 MeV.
- The CP restoring transition temperature at zero baryon density for θ=π is 192 MeV.
- The tricritical point for the CP transition at θ=π is (T_c, μ_c)≈(273, 94) MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier two-flavor NJL studies of spontaneous CP violation at θ=π by Boer and Boomsma (2008, 2009) to three flavors and to finite chemical potential, and contrasts with linear sigma model calculations by Mizher and Fraga (2009) which found a first order transition at θ=π rather than second order. The results enable mapping the CP-violating phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane and identifying a tricritical point, which could inform searches for local CP violation in heavy ion collisions via the chiral magnetic effect.
