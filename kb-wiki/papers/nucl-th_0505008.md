# Relativistic models for quasi-elastic neutrino scattering

> We present quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the energy range from 150 MeV up to 5 GeV for the target nuclei 12C and 56Fe. A relativistic description of the nuclear dynamics and the neutrino-nucleus coupling is adopted. For the treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) we rely on two frameworks succesfully applied to exclusive electron-nucleus scattering: a relativistic optical potential and a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation. At lower energies, the optical-potential approach is considered to be the optimum choice, whereas at high energies a Glauber approach is more natural. Comparing the results of both calculations, it is found that the Glauber approach yields valid results down to the remarkably small nucleon kinetic energies of 200 MeV. We argue that the nuclear transparencies extracted from A(e,e'p) measurements can be used to obtain realistic estimates of the effect of FSI mechanisms on quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections. We present two independent relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) calculations of quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections. They agree at the percent level, showing the reliability of the numerical techniques adopted and providing benchmark RPWIA results.

## Key claim

The paper provides quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 12C and 56Fe across an energy range from 150 MeV to 5 GeV, showing that the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation yields valid results down to nucleon kinetic energies as low as 200 MeV, where the optical-potential approach is normally considered the optimum choice.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections within the relativistic impulse approximation using two independent frameworks for final-state interactions: a relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with complex optical potentials fitted to elastic proton-nucleus scattering data, and a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) that computes final-state interactions directly from elementary nucleon-nucleon scattering data. They also present two independent relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) calculations that serve as benchmark results. The nuclear current is described using a relativistic one-body vertex function with weak vector form factors related to electromagnetic ones via the conserved vector current hypothesis, and axial form factors with a dipole parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Energy range of the calculations: 150 MeV up to 5 GeV for incident neutrino energies
- Target nuclei studied: 12C and 56Fe
- The Glauber approach yields valid results down to nucleon kinetic energies of 200 MeV
- Two independent RPWIA calculations agree at the percent level, providing benchmark RPWIA results
- The Weinberg angle used: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.2224
- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.262
- Axial mass parameter: M = 1.032 GeV
- Reduction of quasi-elastic cross section from final-state interactions: at least 14% at incoming neutrino energies of 1 GeV (referencing prior work Mai et al.)
- Reduction of quasi-elastic cross section from nucleon re-scattering: as large as 15% at incoming neutrino energies of about 1 GeV (referencing prior work Bleve et al.)

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering by Martinez et al. (2004) and Meucci et al. (2004) that used relativistic shell models and optical potentials, and is the first application of a relativistic Glauber-inspired approach to neutrino-nucleus reactions. The paper provides benchmark RPWIA results that resolve discrepancies between different plane-wave limit calculations, and proposes that nuclear transparencies extracted from A(e,e'p) measurements can be used to estimate final-state interaction effects on quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections, which is relevant for experiments such as Minerva, Miniboone, and Finesse.
