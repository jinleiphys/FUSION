# Bayesian optimization and nonlocal effects method for $α$ decay of superheavy nuclei based on CPPM

> We combine nonlocal effects with Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) methods to enhance the prediction accuracy of $α$ decay half-lives. The results indicate that accounting for nonlocal effects significantly impacts the half-life calculations, while the BNN method markedly improves prediction accuracy and demonstrates strong extrapolation capabilities. Furthermore, we discuss the impact of nuclear deformation (the quadrupole deformation factor $β_2$) on machine learning predictions. Through Shapley Additive Explanations (SHAP), we conducted a quantitative comparison of six input features within the BNN, revealing that the $α$ decay energy $Q_α$ is the primary driving factor affecting the half-life $T_{1/2}$. Leveraging the remarkable extrapolation ability of the BNN, we successfully predicted the $α$ decay half-lives of the isotope chain ($Z=118, 120$), uncovering a significant shell effect at neutron number $N=184$. For the isotopic chains ($Z=118, 120$), the predicted $α$ decay half-lives and $Q_α$ values satisfy the Geiger-Nuttall (G-N) linear relationship. This result further confirms the predictive reliability of the proposed model. Keywords: $α$ decay, half-lives, nonlocal effects, Bayesian Neural Network, Coulomb and proximity potential model

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining nonlocal effects (coordinate-dependent effective mass) with a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) correction significantly improves the prediction accuracy of alpha decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei, but this improvement is contingent on the BNN's ability to extrapolate reliably and is validated only for the specific dataset of 401 nuclides and the isotope chains Z=118 and Z=120.

## Method

The authors calculated alpha decay half-lives for 401 superheavy nuclides using the Coulomb and Proximity Potential Model (CPPM) with a cluster formation model (CFM) for the preformation factor, then introduced a coordinate-dependent effective mass parameter (rho_s = -0.615) to account for nonlocal dynamical effects. They constructed a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) using mass number A, proton number Z, decay energy Q_alpha, orbital angular momentum l, preformation factor P_alpha, and quadrupole deformation beta_2 as input features to train on residuals between experimental and theoretical half-lives, optimizing the CPPM, nonlocally modified CPPM, and three parameterizations of the Universal Decay Law (UDL: Formula-A, Formula-B, Formula-C). The BNN was trained on 300 of the 401 datasets (80%) and validated on the remaining 101 (20%), with feature importance analyzed via Shapley Additive Explanations (SHAP).

## Key numbers

- The CPPM without nonlocal effects (rho_s=0) had a root-mean-square error (RMSE) of 0.7268; with nonlocal effects (rho_s=-0.615) the RMSE reduced to 0.4946, a decrease of 31.95%.
- After BNN correction, the CPPM RMSE decreased from 0.7268 to 0.4631 (36.28% improvement); the nonlocally modified CPPM RMSE decreased from 0.4946 to 0.3796 (23.25% improvement).
- For the validation set (101 cases), the CPPM with BNN correction achieved RMSE of 0.5509 (27.32% improvement from pre-correction RMSE of 0.7580); the nonlocally modified CPPM with BNN achieved RMSE of 0.4398 (12.60% improvement from pre-correction RMSE of 0.5032).
- The combined nonlocality plus BNN method on the full dataset gave a final RMSE of 0.3796, representing a 47.77% improvement over the original CPPM.
- For isotope Z=118, predicted log10 half-lives ranged from -6.793 (for 304118 using FRDM mass table with Q_alpha=13.38 MeV) to 0.091 (for 300118 using RCHB mass table with Q_alpha=10.47 MeV).
- For isotope Z=120, predicted log10 half-lives ranged from -7.977 (for 306120 using FRDM mass table with Q_alpha=14.26 MeV) to -1.775 (for 302120 using RCHB mass table with Q_alpha=11.51 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of nonlocal effects in alpha decay by Medeiros (2022) and Hu (2025), who demonstrated that incorporating coordinate-dependent effective mass improves half-life predictions for even-even nuclei, by applying the method within the CPPM framework and combining it with machine learning. The BNN approach builds on prior applications of Bayesian Neural Networks in nuclear physics by Niu (2019) and Neufcourt (2018, 2019) for mass and half-life predictions, and the use of SHAP for feature importance follows interpretability methods from machine learning. The predictions for Z=118 and Z=120 isotope chains, revealing a shell effect at neutron number N=184 and satisfying the Geiger-Nuttall linear relationship, enable future experimental searches for these superheavy elements and provide benchmarks for theoretical models of nuclear structure near the predicted island of stability.
