# The Long Slow Death of the HBT Puzzle

> Femtoscopic measurements at RHIC have been hailed as a source of insight into the bulk properties of QCD matter. However, hydrodynamic models, which have been successful in reproducing other observables have failed to satisfactorily explain femtoscopic two-particle correlation measurements. This failure has been labeled the "HBT puzzle". In this talk, I present correlations using a hybrid model composed of a viscous hydrodynamic module and a hadronic cascade. After incorporating early acceleration, viscosity and a stiffer equation of state, the extracted source sizes come much closer to experiment.

# The Long Slow Death of the HBT Puzzle

## Key claim

The HBT puzzle at RHIC can be resolved by a hybrid viscous hydrodynamic plus hadronic cascade model that incorporates pre-equilibrium flow, a stiffer equation of state, and shear viscosity, but no single modification accounts for more than half of the original discrepancy; instead, several changes conspire to push source sizes in the same direction by making the reaction more explosive.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid model coupling a viscous hydrodynamic module to a hadronic cascade, applied to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They sequentially introduced four modifications to a benchmark calculation (which had no pre-equilibrium flow, no viscosity, and a soft first-order equation of state): (1) moving the hydrodynamic start time from 1.0 fm/c to 0.1 fm/c to incorporate pre-equilibrium acceleration; (2) replacing the soft first-order equation of state (latent heat L=1.6 GeV/fm^3) with a stiffer one having speed of sound c_s^2 = 0.1c and no first-order transition; (3) adding shear viscosity at twice the KSS limit and a bulk viscosity peaking in the mixed phase; and (4) using a more realistic treatment of the pi-pi relative wave function in the Koonin formula for correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The benchmark calculation (no pre-equilibrium flow, no viscosity, soft equation of state) over-predicted the Rout/Rside ratio by approximately 40%.
- The latent heat of the first-order equation of state used in the benchmark was L = 1.6 GeV/fm^3.
- The stiffer equation of state set the speed of sound to c_s^2 = 0.1c.
- The shear viscosity in the partonic phase was set to twice the KSS limit.
- After all four improvements, the model reproduced experimental source sizes to better than 10% for all radii (Rout, Rside, Rlong).
- The model gave mean p_t values (in MeV/c) for central collisions: pions 433, kaons 714, protons 1027, which lie between the STAR values (pions 422±22, kaons 719±74, protons 1100±110) and PHENIX values (pions 453±33, kaons 674±78, protons 954±85).

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic models by Soff (2000), Teaney (2001), and Hirano (2002) that failed to match femtoscopic data, and contrasts with cascade models by Petersen (2008), Li (2008), Humanic (2006), and Lin (2002) that performed better despite ignoring the partonic phase. The results also affirm findings by Broniowski (2008) that the shape of the initial profile significantly affects source sizes, and are consistent with viscous hydrodynamic effects seen by Romatschke (2007). The resolution of the HBT puzzle opens the possibility of reproducing the entirety of soft hadronic observables (spectra, femtoscopic correlations, and collective flow) with a single model, though the authors note that the hydrodynamic code used here assumed radial symmetry and could not analyze elliptic flow v2.
