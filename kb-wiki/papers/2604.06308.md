# Anisotropic hybrid stars: Interplay of superconductivity and magnetic field leading to gravitational waves

> Neutron stars, at their cores, are highly dense and, thus, are expected to have a number of exotic processes. This includes a possible phase transition to deconfined quark matter at the core, leading to a hybrid star. The quark matter is expected to additionally be color superconducting. The physics of superconductivity plays an important role in understanding the high density matter in the interiors of neutron/hybrid stars. At their high densities, additionally, both proton superconductivity and neutron superfluidity are expected. We study the effect of superconducting (quark/proton) matter, along with the internal magnetic field, leading to pressure anisotropy within hybrid stars. We aim to probe the effect of superconductivity, especially from color superconducting quarks, in hybrid star structure. We propose new phenomenological model anisotropy profiles within a one-dimensional framework. We model quark matter using the vector interaction enhanced Bag model, and hadron matter with the DD2 equation of state. A Maxwell construction joins both phases. We further investigate the possible observational signatures of these hybrid stars. These include mass enhancement and continuous gravitational waves, possibly arising from the anisotropy induced deformation, helping us further constrain our model and its physical parameters.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that color superconducting quark cores in hybrid stars can produce pressure anisotropy, leading to enhanced maximum masses and continuous gravitational wave emission, but this outcome depends on the specific anisotropy profile (whether superconductivity supplements magnetic stresses or independently generates anisotropy) and on the competition between the superconducting gap, effective bag constant, and vector interaction strength.

## Method

The authors model hybrid stars by joining the DD2 hadronic equation of state with the vector-interaction enhanced Bag (vBag) model for quark matter via a Maxwell construction, incorporating color superconductivity through an additional pairing term proportional to Δ²μ² in the thermodynamic potential. They solve modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with pressure anisotropy parameterized by two new phenomenological profiles tied to the magnetic field, superconducting gap, and chemical potential, using a pressure-dependent magnetic field profile with transversely oriented fields.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses range from 2.095 M⊙ to 2.562 M⊙ across different combinations of ΔCSC (0–80 MeV), Beff (80–120 MeV/fm³), and B0 (10¹⁵–10¹⁸ G)
- For ΔCSC = 50 MeV, Beff = 100 MeV/fm³, K_v = 15 GeV⁻², a 2.1 M⊙ star is obtained
- The CFL superconducting gap is taken in the range ΔCFL ≈ 10–100 MeV
- Proton superconducting gaps are ΔSC ≲ 1 MeV
- The magnetic field profile uses P0 = 2.5 MeV/fm³, η = 0.01, γ = 1
- Nuclear saturation density is ρ0 = 0.153 fm⁻³ for the DD2 EoS

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zuraiq, 2025) on massive magnetized neutron stars as lower mass gap candidates, now incorporating deconfined quark matter and color superconductivity. It contrasts with the Bowers-Liang model (Bowers and Liang, 1974) by tying anisotropy to physical quantities (magnetic field and superconducting gap) rather than an arbitrary parameter, and with earlier work (Das and Sinha; Sinha and Mukhopadhyay) showing that magnetar cores may lack proton superconductivity. The results enable future searches for continuous gravitational waves from hybrid star deformations and help constrain the unknown microphysics of color superconductivity.
