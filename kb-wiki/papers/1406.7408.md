# Inverse magnetic catalysis in the linear sigma model with quarks

> We compute the critical temperature for the chiral transition in the background of a magnetic field in the linear sigma model, including the quark contribution and the thermo-magnetic effects induced on the coupling constants at one loop level. We show that the critical temperature decreases as a function of the field strength. The effect of fermions on the critical temperature is small and the main effect on this observable comes from the charged pions. The findings support the idea that the anticatalysis phenomenon receives a contribution due only to quiral symmetry effects independent of the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for the chiral transition in the linear sigma model with quarks decreases as a function of magnetic field strength when one-loop thermo-magnetic corrections to the couplings are included, with the main effect coming from charged pions rather than fermions; this inverse magnetic catalysis arises purely from chiral symmetry effects independent of the deconfinement transition, contrary to earlier model calculations that found an increasing critical temperature.

## Method

The authors computed the finite-temperature effective potential in the linear sigma model with quarks in a constant external magnetic field, using Schwinger's proper-time method and a high-temperature expansion in the small-to-intermediate field regime (qB/T² < 1). They included one-loop thermo-magnetic corrections to the boson self-coupling λ and the fermion-boson coupling g, evaluated at v=0 in the infrared limit, and incorporated plasma screening effects via resummation of ring diagrams. The critical temperature was determined by setting the second derivative of the effective potential to zero at vanishing order parameter.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature normalized to its zero-field value, T_c/T_c(B=0), is a decreasing function of b = qB/μ² for all parameter choices shown (Figs. 7 and 8); for example, with tree-level λ = 0.225 and g = 0.3, T_c/T_c(0) drops from approximately 1.0 at b=0 to about 0.92 at b=6 (Fig. 6, dashed curve).
- The effective boson self-coupling λ_eff(v=0) decreases with b = qB/μ²; for t = T/μ = 0.5, λ_eff drops from about 0.9 at b=0 to about 0.6 at b=6 (Fig. 3).
- The effective fermion-boson coupling g_eff(v=0) increases mildly with b; for t = T/μ = 0.5, g_eff rises from about 0.3 at b=0 to about 0.305 at b=6 (Fig. 5, inset).
- The condition for the high-temperature expansion requires qB/T² < 1.
- The total boson self-energy at high temperature is Π = λ T²/2 + N_f g² T²/6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of inverse magnetic catalysis in the Abelian Higgs model (Ayala, Loewe, Zamora, 2014) by including quark degrees of freedom in the linear sigma model. It contradicts earlier model calculations (Fraga, 2008; Mizher, 2010; Andersen, 2011) that found an increasing critical temperature with magnetic field, and instead supports lattice QCD results (Fodor; Bali, 2012) showing a decreasing transition temperature. The finding that inverse magnetic catalysis arises from chiral symmetry effects alone, without invoking the Polyakov loop or deconfinement, enables future work to disentangle the roles of chiral and deconfinement transitions in the presence of magnetic fields.
