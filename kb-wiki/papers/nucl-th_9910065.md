# Inclusive electron scattering from nuclei: y-scaling and final state interaction

> The recent TJLAB experimental data on inclusive electron scattering at high momentum transfer from complex nuclei are analyzed in terms of y-scaling, taking into account the final state interaction (FSI) of the struck, off-shell nucleon. It is shown that at large negative values of y (x>1), the Q^2 dependence of the FSI is mostly driven by the elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section, and that, as a result, the scaling function decreases with Q^2, in agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at large negative y (x>1), the Q² dependence of final state interactions in inclusive electron scattering is driven primarily by the elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section, which decreases with energy, rather than the constant total NN cross section; this causes the scaling function to decrease with Q², in agreement with TJNAF experimental data.

## Method

The authors analyzed TJNAF E89-008 inclusive electron scattering data on complex nuclei (²H, ⁴He, ⁵⁶Fe) at high momentum transfer and x>1 using y-scaling formalism. They introduced a new scaling variable y_CW that incorporates excitation energy of the (A-1) system due to nucleon-nucleon correlations, allowing direct access to longitudinal momentum distributions. Final state interactions were calculated using an optical potential for the shell-model spectator system and two-nucleon rescattering for correlated pairs, with careful treatment of off-shell kinematics for the struck nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The parameters b_A and c_A in the average excitation energy expression range from 17 MeV to 43 MeV and 6.00×10⁻² to 8.00×10⁻², for ³He and nuclear matter respectively
- For the deuteron, the binding energy E_th = |ε_D| = 2.225 MeV
- At high momenta k ≥ 1.5-2 fm⁻¹, nuclear momentum distributions are rescaled versions of the deuteron: n_A(k) ≅ C_A n_D(k)
- The average shell-model removal energy ⟨E_gr⟩ ranges from 5.5 MeV (³He) to 40.8 MeV (nuclear matter)

## Context

This work extends the y-scaling approach of West (1975) and Ciofi degli Atti (1990s) by introducing a new scaling variable that accounts for binding corrections from nucleon-nucleon correlations, following the method of Ciofi degli Atti and West (1999). The analysis enables direct extraction of nucleon momentum distributions from experimental data without the uncertainties of binding correction subtraction, and clarifies that FSI effects at high Q² are governed by the elastic NN cross section rather than the constant total cross section, resolving a long-standing ambiguity in interpreting scaling violations.
