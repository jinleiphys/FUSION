# Nuclear three-body problem in the complex energy plane: Complex-Scaling-Slater method

> The physics of open quantum systems is an interdisciplinary area of research. The nuclear "openness" manifests itself through the presence of the many-body continuum representing various decay, scattering, and reaction channels. As the radioactive nuclear beam experimentation extends the known nuclear landscape towards the particle drip lines, the coupling to the continuum space becomes exceedingly more important. Of particular interest are weakly bound and unbound nuclear states appearing around particle thresholds. Theories of such nuclei must take into account their open quantum nature. To describe open quantum systems, we introduce a Complex Scaling (CS) approach in the Slater basis. We benchmark it with the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model (GSM) by studying energies and wave functions of the bound and unbound states of the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He viewed as an $α$+ n + n cluster system. In the CS approach, we use the Slater basis, which exhibits the correct asymptotic behavior at large distances. To extract particle densities from the back-rotated CS solutions, we apply the Tikhonov regularization procedure, which minimizes the ultraviolet numerical noise. While standard applications of the inverse complex transformation to the complex-rotated solution provide unstable results, the stabilization method fully reproduces the GSM benchmark. We also propose a method to determine the smoothing parameter of the Tikhonov regularization. The combined suite of CS-Slater and GSM techniques has many attractive features when applied to nuclear problems involving weakly-bound and unbound states. While both methods can describe energies, total widths, and wave functions of nuclear states, the CS-Slater method, if it can be applied, can provide an additional information about partial energy widths associated with individual thresholds.

## Key claim

The Complex-Scaling-Slater (CS-Slater) method, when combined with Tikhonov regularization for back-rotation, can accurately reproduce the energies and wave functions of bound and unbound nuclear states obtained from the Gamow Shell Model (GSM), provided the regularization parameter is properly chosen to suppress ultraviolet noise.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body cluster Hamiltonian for ⁶He (α + n + n) using both the Complex Scaling method with a Slater-type basis and the Gamow Shell Model with a Berggren ensemble. In the CS approach, the Schrödinger equation was solved after complex rotation of coordinates, and the resulting square-integrable wave functions were back-rotated using Tikhonov regularization to recover the original Gamow states. The two-body interaction between valence neutrons was approximated by the finite-range central Minnesota force, and the neutron-core interaction used the KKNN potential.

## Key numbers

- The 0p₃/₂ resonant single-particle state in the Berggren basis was found at an energy of 0.737 − i0.292 MeV.
- The 2⁺ state width increased from approximately 250 keV in the full (p₃/₂, p₁/₂, s₁/₂, d₅/₂) model space to approximately 580 keV in the truncated p₃/₂-only space.
- The Berggren basis used a momentum cut-off of k_max = 3.5 fm⁻¹ and each continuum contour was discretized with sixty points, giving 241 neutron shells total.
- The harmonic oscillator basis expansion for the Minnesota interaction used up to n_max = 18 radial nodes with oscillator length b = 2 fm, and the Pauli-forbidden 0s₁/₂ state was projected out using b = 1.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the Complex Scaling method, previously applied in quantum chemistry and nuclear cluster calculations (e.g., Ikeda review, Aoyama 1995, Myo 2001), by introducing a Slater basis that exhibits correct asymptotic behavior for weakly-bound systems. The CS-Slater results are benchmarked against the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) of Michel et al. (review 2009), demonstrating equivalence for both energies and wave functions of ⁶He. The Tikhonov regularization procedure for back-rotation improves upon earlier unstable direct back-rotation attempts (Atkpal, backroterror) and Padé approximation methods, enabling future extraction of partial energy widths associated with individual thresholds.
