# Antikaons in nuclei and dense nuclear matter

> We present recent progress on the properties of antikaons in nuclei and dense nuclear matter as obtained from two {\bar K}N interaction models: one based on the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral lagrangian and the other derived from a meson-exchange picture.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two different theoretical models for the antikaon-nucleon interaction (a chiral Lagrangian model and a meson-exchange model) both predict an attractive antikaon potential in nuclear matter, but the precise depth of this potential at nuclear saturation density remains unconstrained by existing data, with values ranging from about -45 MeV to -200 MeV depending on the model and fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors compare two microscopic models for the antikaon-nucleon interaction: a chiral Lagrangian model (lowest-order meson-baryon Lagrangian with a cutoff of 630 MeV) and the Julich meson-exchange model. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels to obtain scattering amplitudes, then compute in-medium properties by incorporating Pauli blocking, self-consistent dressing of antikaon propagators, and (in the chiral model) dressing of pion propagators. The resulting antikaon self-energy is used to calculate spectral functions, optical potentials, and kaonic atom observables within a local density approximation.

## Key numbers

* The chiral model cutoff used to regularize the loop integral is q_max = 630 MeV.
* The chiral model gives a K^- deuteron scattering length A_Kd = -1.62 + i 1.91 fm (physical basis, full Fadeev series in fixed center approximation).
* The chiral model predicts a K^- optical potential depth of about -45 MeV at nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 for zero-momentum antikaons when pions are also dressed.
* The K^- p scattering branching ratio Gamma(K^- p -> pi+ Sigma-)/Gamma(K^- p -> pi- Sigma+) is 2.32 for the chiral model, compared to the experimental value 2.36 +/- 0.04.
* The I=0 scattering length from the chiral model is a^I=0 = -1.93 + i 1.68 fm, compared to the experimental value -1.70 + i 0.68 fm.
* At an antikaon momentum of about 500 MeV/c, including higher partial waves in the Julich model approximately doubles the size of the antikaon potential compared to the S-wave value.

## Context

This work extends microscopic coupled-channels calculations of antikaon properties in nuclear matter, building on earlier studies by Koch (1994), Waas, Weise, and Kaiser (1996), and Lutz (1998). The results are compared with kaonic atom data compiled by Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997) and with phenomenological fits by Friedman, Gal, and Batty (1994) that favored a deeper potential of around -200 MeV. The paper shows that the chiral model's moderately attractive potential (-45 MeV) can reproduce kaonic atom data, contradicting earlier claims that deeper potentials were required. The work enables future experimental tests through the DEAR experiment at Frascati and through proposed measurements of deeply bound kaonic states in (K-, gamma) or (K-, p) reactions.
