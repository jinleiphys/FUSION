# Analysis of a quenched lattice-QCD dressed-quark propagator

> Quenched lattice-QCD data on the dressed-quark Schwinger function can be correlated with dressed-gluon data via a rainbow gap equation so long as that equation's kernel possesses enhancement at infrared momenta above that exhibited by the gluon alone. The required enhancement can be ascribed to a dressing of the quark-gluon vertex. The solutions of the rainbow gap equation exhibit dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and are consistent with confinement. The gap equation and related, symmetry-preserving ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation yield estimates for chiral and physical pion observables that suggest these quantities are materially underestimated in the quenched theory: |<bar-q q>| by a factor of two and f_pi by 30%.

# Analysis of a quenched lattice-QCD dressed-quark propagator

## Key claim
Quenched lattice-QCD data on the dressed-quark propagator can be correlated with dressed-gluon data via a rainbow gap equation, but only if the equation's kernel possesses infrared enhancement above that exhibited by the gluon alone, which can be ascribed to dressing of the quark-gluon vertex. The resulting solutions yield estimates for chiral and physical pion observables that suggest these quantities are materially underestimated in the quenched theory: the chiral condensate by a factor of two and the pion decay constant by 30%.

## Method
The authors constructed a model for the gap equation's kernel by factorizing the effective interaction into a contribution from the lattice dressed-gluon propagator (parametrized from quenched lattice-QCD data of Leinweber (1998)) multiplied by a phenomenological vertex dressing function v(Q^2). The parameters of v(Q^2) were determined by fitting the rainbow gap equation solutions for the dressed-quark mass function M(p^2) to quenched lattice-QCD data from Bowman (2002) at three current-quark masses (am = 0.018, 0.036, 0.072), using a renormalization point of 19 GeV. The resulting kernel was then used to solve the symmetry-preserving ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for pion observables.

## Key numbers
- Chiral condensate at 1 GeV: -⟨q̄q⟩⁰_{1 GeV} = (0.19 GeV)³, compared to a best-fit phenomenological value of (0.24 ± 0.01 GeV)³, indicating the quenched condensate is smaller by approximately a factor of two.
- Pion decay constant f_π is underestimated by 30% in the quenched theory (exact numerical value from the calculation not provided in the text).
- Lattice gluon propagator parameters: A = 9.8^{+0.1}_{-0.9}, Λ_g = 1.020 ± 0.1 ± 0.025 GeV, α = 2.2^{+0.1+0.2}_{-0.2-0.3}, Z_g = 2.01^{+0.04}_{-0.05}, with lattice spacing a = 1/(1.885 GeV).
- Current-quark masses from fit: m(ζ=19 GeV) = 0.030, 0.055, 0.110 GeV corresponding to lattice am values 0.018, 0.036, 0.072 respectively.
- Vertex dressing parameters: a₁ = 1.5, a₂ = 7.35, a₃ = 63.0, b = 0.005.
- Λ_qu-QCD = 0.234 GeV for the quenched theory.
- Linear interpolation of M(p²_IR=0.38 GeV²) on accessible lattice masses: M = 0.18 + 1.42 m(ζ) GeV, but extrapolation to m=0 overestimates by 40%.
- Linear interpolation of M(p²=0): M = 0.37 + 0.68 m(ζ) GeV, but extrapolation to m=0 overestimates by 14%.

## Context
This work extends the program of correlating Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) calculations with lattice-QCD results, following earlier DSE studies by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) that modeled the effective interaction phenomenologically. The paper directly addresses the inconsistency noted by Frank, Roberts, and Tandy (1995) and Hawes, Roberts, and Williams (1997) between lattice gluon and quark propagators when used in the gap equation without vertex enhancement. The results enable quantitative estimates of quenching effects on hadron observables and provide a lattice-constrained kernel for future DSE studies of meson properties in quenched QCD. The analysis also demonstrates that light quarks in quenched QCD violate reflection positivity, consistent with confinement, as previously discussed by Roberts and collaborators.
