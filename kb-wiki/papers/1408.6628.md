# Dispersive estimate of the electromagnetic charge symmetry violation in the octet baryon masses

> We explore the electromagnetic contribution to the charge symmetry breaking in the octet baryon masses using a subtracted dispersion relation based on the Cottingham formula. For the proton-neutron mass splitting we report a minor revision of the recent analysis of Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller. For the electromagnetic structure of the hyperons we constrain our analysis, where possible, by a combination of lattice QCD and SU(3) symmetry breaking estimates. The results for the baryon mass splittings are found to be compatible with recent lattice QCD+QED determinations. The uncertainties in the dispersive analysis are dominated by the lack of knowledge of the hyperon inelastic structure.

# Dispersive estimate of the electromagnetic charge symmetry violation in the octet baryon masses

## Key claim
The electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass splitting is 1.04 ± 0.35 MeV, a minor revision of the Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller (2012) analysis, but the uncertainties in the dispersive analysis are dominated by the lack of knowledge of the hyperon inelastic structure, particularly the isovector magnetic polarisabilities and the mass scale characterizing the subtraction term.

## Method
The authors use a subtracted dispersion relation based on the Cottingham formula to compute the electromagnetic self-energy of octet baryons, separating the contribution into elastic, inelastic, subtraction, and counterterm components. For nucleons, elastic form factors are taken from the Kelly parameterisation of experimental data and inelastic structure functions from the Christy and Bosted parameterisations, with the Delta resonance region forced to be charge symmetric. For hyperons, elastic form factors are taken from lattice QCD results of the CSSM/QCDSF/UKQCD Collaborations, and inelastic contributions are estimated by scaling nucleon structure functions using lattice QCD estimates of quark momentum fraction ratios, with SU(3) breaking effects included.

## Key numbers
- Proton-neutron electromagnetic mass splitting: δMᵧ(p-n) = 1.04 ± 0.35 MeV
- Sigma charge splitting: δMᵧ(Σ⁺-Σ⁻) = 0.0 ± 1.1 MeV
- Xi charge splitting: δMᵧ(Ξ⁰-Ξ⁻) = -0.4 ± 1.1 MeV
- Elastic contribution to p-n splitting: δMᵉˡ(p-n) = 1.401(7) MeV
- Inelastic contribution to p-n splitting: δMⁱⁿᵉˡ(p-n) = 0.089 ± 0.038 ± 0.019 MeV (first error from structure functions, second from Λ₀² range)
- Subtraction term inelastic contribution for p-n: δMˢᵘᵇⁱⁿᵉˡ(p-n) = 0.18 ± 0.35 MeV
- Proton magnetic polarisability: βᴾ_M = (3.1 ± 0.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron magnetic polarisability: βᴺ_M = (4.1 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Isovector nucleon magnetic polarisability: β^(p-n)_M = (-1.0 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Mass scale for nucleon subtraction form factor: M_β = 460 ± 100 ± 40 MeV
- Lattice QCD+QED comparison from BMW Collaboration (Borsanyi 2013): δMᵧ(p-n) = 1.59 ± 0.46 MeV, δMᵧ(Σ⁺-Σ⁻) = 0.08 ± 0.36 MeV, δMᵧ(Ξ⁰-Ξ⁻) = -1.29 ± 0.17 MeV

## Context
This work extends the subtracted dispersion relation approach of Walker-Loud, Carlson and Miller (2012) from the nucleon sector to the full octet of baryons, including the Σ and Ξ hyperons. The analysis updates the earlier Cottingham formula result of Gasser (1974, 1982) which gave δMᵧ(p-n) = 0.76 ± 0.30 MeV. The results are compatible with recent lattice QCD+QED simulations from the BMW Collaboration (Borsanyi 2013) and with the lattice-constrained dispersive estimate of Thomas (2014). The work enables improved constraints on hyperon isovector magnetic polarisabilities through comparison with lattice calculations, and identifies the inelastic subtraction term as the dominant uncertainty requiring further theoretical or experimental study.
