# Introduction: GDH and related topics

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule, while derived from very general principles (causality, unitarity, Lorentz and gauge invariance), faces a saturation problem: its verification requires complete knowledge of the total photoabsorption cross section over all photon energies, and existing phenomenological analyses show systematic discrepancies, with most overestimating the proton integral and underestimating the neutron integral relative to the sum rule prediction.

## Method

The paper revisits the derivation of the GDH sum rule from forward Compton scattering amplitudes, using the Low Energy Theorem (LET), causality via dispersion relations, and the Optical theorem to relate the spin-flip amplitude to the helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections. It then generalizes the sum rule to virtual photon absorption (electron scattering) by introducing a \(Q^2\) dependence, connecting the GDH integral to the spin structure function \(g_1(x,Q^2)\) and to related sum rules such as the Ellis-Jaffe and Bjorken sum rules. Finally, it reviews phenomenological evaluations of the GDH integral for the nucleon and discusses extensions to nuclear targets (deuterium and \(^3\)He).

## Key numbers

- GDH sum rule prediction for the proton: \(I_p^{GDH} = 204\ \mu b\); for the neutron: \(I_n^{GDH} = 232\ \mu b\); difference: \(I_{p-n}^{GDH} = -28\ \mu b\) (from the anomalous magnetic moment values).
- Direct experimental evaluation from MAMI data (proton, 200–800 MeV range): \(216 \pm 6 \pm 13\ \mu b\).
- Ellis-Jaffe sum rule prediction for the proton: \(\Gamma_1^p(Q^2) = 0.185\) at the quoted scale; experimental value at \(Q^2 = 5\ (GeV/c)^2\): \(\Gamma^p_{1\ exp} = 0.141 \pm 0.011\).
- Bjorken sum rule prediction: \(\Gamma_1^p - \Gamma_1^n = 0.191 \pm 0.002\), in good agreement with the experimental value of about 0.20.
- For the deuteron target, the GDH sum rule phenomenological value is \(0.65\ \mu b\); theoretical contributions up to 550 MeV from the three channels sum to \(I_{GDH}(550) = -183\ \mu b\) (using theoretical values) or \(-19\ \mu b\) (using experimental multipole analysis for the \(\gamma d \rightarrow NN\pi\) channel).

## Context

This paper reviews the GDH sum rule and its connections to other sum rules in electron scattering, extending prior work by Baldin (1960), Drell-Hearn (1966), and Gerasimov (1965). It discusses the saturation problem that has motivated experimental programs at MAMI and ELSA, and relates the GDH integral to the spin structure function formalism used in deep inelastic scattering, including the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (1974) and the Bjorken sum rule (1966). The generalization to nuclear targets, particularly deuterium and \(^3\)He, aims to extract neutron information, building on the work of Hart (1999) and others. The paper enables future experimental verification of the sum rule and improved understanding of baryon resonance excitation and nucleon spin structure.
