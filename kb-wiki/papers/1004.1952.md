# Diffractive vector meson production at large-$t$ in coherent hadronic interactions at CERN LHC

> The diffractive vector meson photoproduction with hadron dissociation in coherent hadron-hadron interactions at LHC energies is studied assuming that the color singlet $t$-channel exchange carries large momentum transfer. We estimate the rapidity distribution and total cross section for the processes $pp \rightarrow p V X$ and $PbPb \rightarrow Pb V X$ ($V = ρ$ or $Υ$) considering the non-forward solution of the BFKL equation at high energy and large momentum transfer. Our results indicate that the experimental identification of these processes can be feasible at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the experimental identification of diffractive vector meson photoproduction with hadron dissociation at large momentum transfer can be feasible at the LHC, but only under the condition that the free parameters in the BFKL formalism (the strong coupling constant and the scale parameter Lambda) are fixed by fitting to existing HERA data for rho photoproduction.

## Method

The cross section for the process hh -> h V X (where h = p or Pb, V = rho or Upsilon) is calculated using the equivalent photon approximation, factorizing the hadronic cross section into a photon flux and the gamma h -> V X differential cross section. The latter is computed using the non-forward solution of the BFKL equation in leading logarithmic approximation, expressed as the product of parton-level cross sections and parton distribution functions (CTEQ6L for protons, EKS for nuclei). The free parameters alpha_s = 0.21 and Lambda^2 = beta M_V^2 + gamma |t| are fixed by fitting to HERA data for rho photoproduction, yielding beta = 1.0 and gamma = 0 for rho, while for Upsilon the authors assume beta = 1.0 and gamma = 0.25.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, the integrated cross section for diffractive rho photoproduction in the range 2.0 < |t| < 5.0 GeV^2 is 7510 nb, corresponding to 7510 events per second at design luminosity (10^34 cm^-2 s^-1).
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, the integrated cross section for rho production in the same t-range is 20 mb, corresponding to 8.4 events per second at design luminosity (4.2 x 10^26 cm^-2 s^-1).
- For Upsilon production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, the integrated cross section in the range 2.0 < |t| < 5.0 GeV^2 is 0.8 nb (8.0 events/second).
- For Upsilon production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, the integrated cross section in the same t-range is 0.26 mb (0.1 events/second).
- The nuclear effects (antishadowing from EKS parametrization) enhance the rho cross section by about 10% compared to the no-shadowing prediction in the 2.0 < |t| < 5.0 GeV^2 range.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of diffractive J/Psi photoproduction with hadron dissociation at large momentum transfer (Goncalves and Sauter, 2009) to the cases of rho and Upsilon mesons. It complements the coherent (target intact) vector meson production studies at t=0 by Goncalves and collaborators (2009) for rho and Upsilon, finding that the large-t rho cross section is a factor of 100 or more smaller than the coherent t=0 case, while for Upsilon the large-t cross section is similar to the coherent case. The results enable experimental searches at LHC detectors (CMS, ATLAS, ALICE) for these processes, which could provide constraints on BFKL dynamics at high energy and large momentum transfer.
