# Single-spin asymmetries of $d(γ,π)NN$ in the first resonance region

> Incoherent photoproduction of pions on the deuteron in the first resonance region is investigated with special emphasis on single-spin asymmetries. For the elementary pion production operator an effective Lagrangian model which includes the standard pseudovector Born terms and a resonance contribution from the $Δ$(1232)-excitation is used. Single-spin asymmetries, both for charged and neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron, are analyzed and calculated for the first time in the first resonance region. The linear photon asymmetry $Σ$, vector target asymmetry $T_{11}$ and tensor target asymmetries $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$, and $T_{22}$ for the reaction $d(γ,π)NN$ are predicted for forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculations of single-spin asymmetries (linear photon asymmetry Σ, vector target asymmetry T₁₁, and tensor target asymmetries T₂₀, T₂₁, T₂₂) for incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the Δ(1232)-resonance region, but these are predictions for forthcoming experiments with no experimental data yet available for comparison.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian model for the elementary γN → πN operator, containing standard pseudovector Born terms and a Δ(1232)-resonance contribution, within a time-ordered perturbation theory framework. The transition matrix elements for the γd → πNN reaction were evaluated in the laboratory frame using the impulse approximation, with the deuteron wave function taken from the Paris potential. Single-spin asymmetries were computed by integrating over the pion momentum and the relative momentum of the two outgoing nucleons, with numerical integration accuracy better than 1%.

## Key numbers

- Photon asymmetry Σ is always negative at forward and backward pion angles for charged and neutral pion channels, with only a very small positive value found for π⁺ production at 450 MeV.
- Vector target asymmetry T₁₁ is always negative for charged pion channels and always positive for the neutral π⁰ channel, vanishing at θπ = 0 and θπ = π.
- Tensor target asymmetry T₂₀ shows relatively large positive values at forward pion angles for charged channels, with negative values at forward angles for the neutral π⁰ channel.
- Tensor target asymmetry T₂₁ vanishes at θπ = 0 and θπ = π, similar to the vector target asymmetry.
- Tensor target asymmetry T₂₂ also vanishes at θπ = 0 and θπ = π, and shows sensitivity to the choice of deuteron wave function (Paris vs. Bonn potential) for the neutral pion channel.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the γd → πNN reaction by Darwish (2003) that focused on differential and total cross sections and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, as well as earlier work by Blaazer (1994) and Wilhelm and Arenhövel (1995) on coherent pion photoproduction. The calculations are motivated by experimental capabilities at facilities such as ELSA in Bonn, LEGS in Brookhaven, CEBAF in Newport News, and MAMI in Mainz, and the predictions are intended to guide planning of new polarization experiments. The paper notes that preliminary data for the photon asymmetry Σ in γd → π⁰np are being measured at LEGS Brookhaven National Laboratory.
