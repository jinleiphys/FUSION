# Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels calculations with core excitation

> The effect of core excitation in the elastic scattering and breakup of a two-body halo nucleus on a stable target nucleus is studied. The structure of the weakly-bound projectile is described in the weak-coupling limit, assuming a particle-rotor model. The eigenfunctions and the associated eigenvalues are obtained by diagonalizing this Hamiltonian in a square-integrable basis (pseudo-states). For the radial coordinate between the particle and the core, a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis is used. For the reaction dynamics, an extension of the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method, which takes into account dynamic core excitation and deexcitation due to the presence of non-central parts in the core-target interaction, is adapted to be used along with a pseudo-states (PS) basis.

## Key claim
Core excitation effects in the elastic scattering and breakup of halo nuclei are shown to be significant when treated with a full coupled-channels formalism (XCDCC) using a pseudo-state basis, contrary to earlier XCDCC calculations by Summers et al. (2006) that suggested very small effects, though those earlier calculations contained errors that have since been corrected by their authors.

## Method
The projectile (a two-body halo nucleus composed of a deformed core and valence particle) is described using a particle-rotor model in the weak-coupling limit, with eigenstates obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) pseudo-state basis. The reaction dynamics are treated with an extended Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (XCDCC) method that includes dynamic core excitation and deexcitation through non-central parts of the core-target interaction, with coupling potentials evaluated using the pseudo-state wave functions.

## Key numbers
- For the $^{11}$Be structure, the model uses a Woods-Saxon central potential with radius $R=2.483$ fm, diffuseness $a=0.65$ fm, parity-dependent strength ($V_c=-54.24$ MeV for positive parity, $V_c=-49.67$ MeV for negative parity), and spin-orbit strength $V_{so}=8.5$ MeV
- The $^{10}$Be core is assumed to have a permanent quadrupole deformation $\beta_2=0.67$, corresponding to deformation length $\delta_2=1.664$ fm
- The first excited state of the $^{10}$Be core is at $E_x=3.368$ MeV ($2^+$ state)
- The THO basis uses LST parameters $m=4$ and $b=1.6$ fm
- The $^{11}$Be breakup on a proton target is studied at 63.7 MeV/nucleon, with data covering two relative energy intervals: $E_{\rm rel}=0$–2.5 MeV and $E_{\rm rel}=2.5$–5.0 MeV
- A narrow $5/2^+$ resonance in $^{11}$Be appears at $E_{\rm rel}=1.28$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the XCDCC formalism of Summers et al. (2006) by using a pseudo-state (THO) basis instead of continuum bins obtained from direct integration of the multi-channel Schrodinger equation, and provides an independent assessment of core excitation effects in halo nucleus scattering. It also builds on no-recoil XDWBA calculations by Crespo et al. (2011) and Moro et al. (2012) that showed sizable core excitation effects in breakup, but which ignored higher-order effects like continuum-continuum couplings. The method is applied to $^{11}$Be reactions on light ($^1$H), medium ($^{64}$Zn), and heavy ($^{208}$Pb) targets, enabling systematic study of core excitation across different target masses.
