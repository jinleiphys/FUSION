# Weak Response of Nuclear Matter at low Momentum transfer

> A quantitative understanding of the weak nuclear response is a prerequisite for the computer simulations of astrophysical phenomena like supernov$æ$ explosions and neutron star cooling. In order to reduce the systematic uncertainties associated with the simulations, a consistent framework, able to take into account dynamical correlation effects, is needed to compute neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus reaction rates. In this paper we describe the many-body theory of the weak nuclear response at low energy regime. We show how to include both short and long correlations effects in a consistent fashion.

## Key claim

The paper describes a many-body theory framework for computing the weak nuclear response at low momentum transfer that consistently includes both short-range and long-range dynamical correlation effects, but the calculations are limited to the dominant one particle-one hole transition and the two-body cluster level of approximation.

## Method

The authors used correlated basis function (CBF) theory, starting from a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon interactions, to construct correlated states from Fermi gas states via a correlation operator. They derived an effective operator for weak transitions and an effective interaction using cluster expansion at the two-body level, then computed the nuclear response for Fermi transitions in symmetric nuclear matter using both the correlated Hartree-Fock approximation and the Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) to include long-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- Correlation effects in the transition matrix elements produce a quenching of approximately 15% in the nuclear response for Fermi transitions
- The collective excitation (plasmon-like mode) lies approximately 4 MeV above the upper limit of the particle-hole continuum at momentum transfer |q| = 0.3 fm^-1
- The diagonalization of the Hamiltonian was performed over a basis of approximately 3000 one particle-one hole states for each spin-isospin channel
- Calculations were performed at momentum transfers of |q| = 0.3 fm^-1, 1.8 fm^-1, and 3.0 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function formalism previously applied to nuclear matter binding energies and neutrino mean free path calculations (Shannon and collaborators, 2000s) to the weak response at low momentum transfer relevant for supernova simulations and neutron star cooling. The approach improves upon existing effective interaction models by including many-nucleon forces that become sizable at high density, and enables more consistent treatment of neutrino-nucleus reaction rates for astrophysical simulations. The TDA results show a collective excitation reminiscent of the plasmon mode in electron gas, which had not been previously demonstrated for the weak nuclear response in this framework.
