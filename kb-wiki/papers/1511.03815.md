# A simplified BBGKY hierarchy for correlated fermionic systems from a Stochastic Mean-Field approach

> The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach allows to treat correlations beyond mean-field using a set of independent mean-field trajectories with appropriate choice of fluctuating initial conditions. We show here, that this approach is equivalent to a simplified version of the Bogolyubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy between one-, two-, ..., N-body degrees of freedom. In this simplified version, one-body degrees of freedom are coupled to fluctuations to all orders while retaining only specific terms of the general BBGKY hierarchy. The use of the simplified BBGKY is illustrated with the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model. We show that a truncated version of this hierarchy can be useful, as an alternative to the SMF, especially in the weak coupling regime to get physical insight in the effect beyond mean-field. In particular, it leads to approximate analytical expressions for the quantum fluctuations both in the weak and strong coupling regime. In the strong coupling regime, it can only be used for short time evolution. In that case, it gives information on the evolution time-scale close to a saddle point associated to a quantum phase-transition. For long time evolution and strong coupling, we observed that the simplified BBGKY hierarchy cannot be truncated and only the full SMF with initial sampling leads to reasonable results.

# A simplified BBGKY hierarchy for correlated fermionic systems from a Stochastic Mean-Field approach

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach is equivalent to a simplified version of the BBGKY hierarchy where one-body degrees of freedom are coupled to fluctuations to all orders, but only specific terms of the general BBGKY hierarchy are retained; this simplified hierarchy can be truncated and used as an alternative to SMF in the weak coupling regime, but in the strong coupling regime it can only be used for short time evolution and the full SMF with initial sampling is required for reasonable results.

## Method

The authors derive coupled equations of motion for the moments of the one-body density from the SMF approach, showing that these equations form a simplified BBGKY hierarchy. They then apply this hierarchy to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model, truncating at second-order (QC-TDDM2), third-order (QC-TDDM3), and fourth-order (QC-TDDM4) to compute the time evolution of quasi-spin fluctuations, and compare these truncated results to exact solutions and full SMF calculations with initial sampling.

## Key numbers

- System of N=40 particles in the LMG model, initially in the Slater determinant state |j, -j⟩, corresponding to all particles occupying the lowest level with single-particle spins all -1/2.
- For weak coupling (χ=0.5), QC-TDDM2 provides correct evolution of Σ²_xx and Σ²_yy, with analytical expressions giving frequency ω₀ = 2√(Ω₊Ω₋) = 2ω₀ that drives the two-body evolution.
- Initial non-zero moments for the N=40 system include: Σ²_xx = Σ²_yy = 1/(4N), Σ³_yyz = Σ³_xxz = 1/(12N²), Σ⁴_xxxx = Σ⁴_yyyy = (3N-2)/(16N³), Σ⁴_xxyy = (3N-2)/(48N³), Σ⁴_xxzz = Σ⁴_yyzz = 1/(24N³).
- For strong coupling (χ=1.8), QC-TDDM2 gives a divergent behavior described by sinh²(ω₀t) reflecting the saddle-point instability, while QC-TDDM4 follows the exact result for longer time than QC-TDDM2 but still diverges after short time.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach previously applied to the LMG model (Lacroix 2012) and Hubbard model (Lacroix 2014b), where SMF showed better accuracy than Green function techniques including explicit two-body effects (Hertwig 2014). The simplified BBGKY hierarchy provides an alternative to the standard Time-Dependent Density Matrix (TDDM) approach (Cassing 1990, Gong 1990, Schmitt 1990) and clarifies why SMF can outperform truncation-based methods: it propagates correlations to all orders while retaining only specific terms. The work enables analytical expressions for quantum fluctuations in the weak coupling regime and identifies that in the strong coupling regime near a quantum phase-transition saddle point, no truncation scheme is adequate and full SMF sampling is required.
