# $v_4$, $v_5$, $v_6$, $v_7$: nonlinear hydrodynamic response versus LHC data

> Higher harmonics of anisotropic flow ($v_n$ with $n\ge 4$) in heavy-ion collisions can be measured either with respect to their own plane, or with respect to a plane constructed using lower-order harmonics. We explain how such measurements are related to event-plane correlations. We show that CMS data on $v_4$ and $v_6$ are compatible with ATLAS data on event-plane correlations. If one assumes that higher harmonics are the superposition of non-linear and linear responses, then the linear and non-linear parts can be isolated under fairly general assumptions. By combining analyses of higher harmonics with analyses of $v_2$ and $v_3$, one can eliminate the uncertainty from initial conditions and define quantities that only involve nonlinear hydrodynamic response coefficients. Experimental data on $v_4$, $v_5$ and $v_6$ are in good agreement with hydrodynamic calculations. We argue that $v_7$ can be measured with respect to elliptic and triangular flow. We present predictions for $v_7$ versus centrality in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

# $v_4$, $v_5$, $v_6$, $v_7$: nonlinear hydrodynamic response versus LHC data

## Key claim

Higher harmonics of anisotropic flow ($v_n$ with $n\ge 4$) can be decomposed into linear and nonlinear hydrodynamic response components, and by combining measurements of higher harmonics with those of $v_2$ and $v_3$, one can eliminate the uncertainty from initial conditions and define quantities that only involve nonlinear hydrodynamic response coefficients. The authors show that CMS data on $v_4$ and $v_6$ are compatible with ATLAS data on event-plane correlations, and that experimental data on $v_4$, $v_5$ and $v_6$ are in good agreement with hydrodynamic calculations, provided one assumes the linear and nonlinear parts are uncorrelated.

## Method

The authors derive relations between higher harmonic flow coefficients measured with respect to their own event plane and those measured with respect to lower harmonic planes, showing these are equivalent to Pearson correlation coefficients (event-plane correlations). They decompose $V_4$, $V_5$, $V_6$, and $V_7$ into linear response parts ($V_{nL}$) and nonlinear response terms involving powers of $V_2$ and $V_3$ with coefficients $\chi_n$, then isolate the nonlinear response coefficients using projected harmonics and moments of the $V_2$ and $V_3$ distributions. The nonlinear coefficients are calculated using ideal and viscous (constant $\eta/s=0.08$) hydrodynamics with a symmetric Gaussian initial profile, Lattice QCD equation of state, and freeze-out temperature $T_{fo}=150$ MeV, then compared to experimental data from CMS and ATLAS.

## Key numbers

- $\chi_4 = \frac{1}{2}$, $\chi_5 = 1$, $\chi_{62} = \frac{1}{6}$, $\chi_{63} = \frac{1}{2}$, $\chi_7 = \frac{1}{2}$ are the ideal hydrodynamic predictions at fixed, large $p_T$
- Viscous hydrodynamic calculation uses $\eta/s = 0.08$ (constant shear viscosity over entropy ratio)
- Initial time of hydrodynamic calculation: $\tau_0 = 1$ fm/$c$
- Freeze-out temperature: $T_{fo} = 150$ MeV
- Anisotropic flow averaged over particles with $p_T > 0.3$ GeV/$c$ (CMS acceptance)
- Predicted $v_7\{\Psi_{23}\}$ value is $0.05\%$ in the 25-30% centrality range in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV
- Anticipated experimental error on $v_7\{\Psi_{23}\}$ is of order $0.01\%$
- CMS data on $v_4$ and $v_6$ are compatible with ATLAS data on event-plane correlations (shown in Fig. 1)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of nonlinear hydrodynamic response developed by Gardim (2011), Teaney (2012), and Teaney (2013), and clarifies the relation between higher harmonic flow measurements and event-plane correlations measured by ATLAS (Aad, 2014). The authors show that CMS data (Chatrchyan, 2013) on $v_4$ and $v_6$ are compatible with ATLAS event-plane correlations, and that nonlinear response coefficients can be extracted without event-shape engineering (unlike the method of Jia, 2014). The results enable direct comparison between hydrodynamics and data with all dependence on initial state eliminated, and provide predictions for $v_7$ that could be tested by LHC experiments.
