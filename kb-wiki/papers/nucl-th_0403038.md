# Production of resonances in a thermal model: invariant-mass spectra and balance functions

> We present a calculation of the pi+ pi- invariant-mass correlations and the pion balance functions in the single-freeze-out model. A satisfactory agreement with the data for Au+Au collisions is found.

# Production of resonances in a thermal model: invariant-mass spectra and balance functions

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out thermal model, with all parameters fixed from pion, kaon, and proton spectra, reproduces the gross features of the STAR $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant-mass spectra and the shapes of the pion balance functions, but the calculated width of the balance function for the most central collisions ($\langle \delta \rangle = 0.66$) is somewhat larger than the experimental value ($0.594 \pm 0.019$), and the centrality dependence of the width cannot be reproduced by varying the transverse flow within limits consistent with single-particle spectra.

## Method

The authors calculated $\pi^+ \pi^-$ invariant-mass correlations and pion balance functions in the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski and Florkowski (2001), including flow, experimental kinematic cuts, detector efficiency corrections, and decays of higher resonances. For the invariant-mass spectra, correlated pion pairs were assumed to come from decays of neutral resonances ($\rho$, $K_S^0$, $\omega$, $\eta$, $\eta'$, $f_0/\sigma$, $f_2$) using a phase-shift formula, with the $\rho$ meson mass scaled down by 10%. For the balance functions, two contributions were summed: a resonance contribution from neutral resonances decaying to $\pi^+ \pi^-$ pairs, and a non-resonance contribution where opposite-charge pions are produced at the same space-time point with thermal velocities.

## Key numbers

- Temperature used in the calculation: $T = 165$ MeV
- Average transverse flow: $0.5c$
- Calculated total width of the balance function: $\langle \delta \rangle = 0.66$
- STAR experimental balance function widths: most central (0-10% centrality) $\langle \delta \rangle = 0.594 \pm 0.019$; mid-central (10-40%) $0.622 \pm 0.020$; mid-peripheric (40-70%) $0.633 \pm 0.024$; peripheric (70-96%) $0.664 \pm 0.029$
- $\rho$ meson mass scaled down by 10% in the invariant-mass calculation

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out thermal model, previously successful for hadron yields, spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii (Broniowski and Florkowski, 2001; Florkowski and Broniowski, 2002), to correlation observables measured by the STAR Collaboration. The invariant-mass spectra analysis follows the phase-shift approach of Pratt and Bauer (2003) and earlier resonance production work. The balance function results address the finding by Bass (2000) and Jeon and Koch (2000) that measured widths were smaller than model expectations, and complement the blast-wave model calculations of Pratt et al. (2003). The approach enables further tests of thermal freeze-out dynamics against correlation data, though the centrality dependence of balance function widths remains an open question.
