# Multiplicity, average transverse momentum and azimuthal anisotropy in U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV using AMPT model

> Using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model that includes the implementation of deformed Uranium nuclei, we have studied the centrality dependence of the charged particle multiplicity, <pT>, eccentricity (e2), triangularity (e3), their fluctuations, elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) for different configurations of U+U collisions at midrapidity for \sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The results are compared to the corresponding observations from Au+Au collisions. We find that for the U+U collisions the dNch/dηat midrapidity is enhanced by about 15-40% depending on the collision and model configuration chosen, compared to Au+Au collisions. The tip-to-tip collisions leads to the largest values of Nch,transverse energy (ET) and <pT>. The <e2> and its fluctuation shows a rich centrality dependence, whereas not much variations are observed for <e3> and its fluctuations. The U+U side-on-side collision configuration provides maximum values of <e2> and minimum values of eccentricity fluctuations, whereas for peripheral collisions and mid-central collisions minimum values of <e2> and maximum value of eccentricity fluctuations are observed for body-to-body configuration and the tip-to-tip configuration has minimum value of <e2> and maximum value of eccentricity fluctuations for central collisions. The calculated v2 closely correlates with the eccentricity in the model. It is smallest for the body-to-body configuration in peripheral and mid-central collisions while it is minimum for tip-to-tip configuration in central collisions. For peripheral collisions the v2 in U+U can be about 40% larger than in Au+Au whereas for central collisions it can be a factor 2 higher depending on the collision configuration. It is also observed that the v3(pT) is higher for tip-to-tip and body-to-body configurations compared to other systems for the collision centrality studied.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with deformed Uranium nuclei, the study finds that the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity in U+U collisions is enhanced by about 15-40% compared to Au+Au collisions at the same energy, but this enhancement depends on both the collision configuration (tip-to-tip, body-to-body, side-on-side) and the model version (default or string melting) chosen.

## Method

The authors implemented deformed Uranium nuclei in the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model using a Woods-Saxon density profile with deformation parameters beta2 = 0.28 and beta4 = 0.093 for Uranium-238. They simulated U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for three specific configurations (tip-to-tip, body-to-body, side-on-side) plus general (randomly oriented) collisions, using both the default and string melting versions of AMPT with a parton-parton interaction cross section of 10 mb. Results were compared to Au+Au collisions at the same energy.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity (dNch/deta) at midrapidity in U+U collisions is enhanced by 15-40% relative to Au+Au collisions, depending on collision and model configuration
- For central collisions (impact parameter less than 3.6 fm), the average transverse momentum for tip-to-tip configuration is about 30 MeV higher than body-to-body configuration
- The increase in average transverse momentum for U+U tip-to-tip relative to Au+Au collisions is about 10 MeV
- For peripheral collisions, v2 in U+U can be about 40% larger than in Au+Au; for central collisions it can be a factor of 2 higher depending on collision configuration
- In the string melting version, charged particle multiplicity is about 8% higher than default, while transverse energy is about 10% lower
- The maximum values of Nch and transverse energy in U+U collisions are about 15-35% higher than corresponding values from Au+Au collisions
- For the string melting version, v2 and v3 magnitudes are typically 40% and 80% higher respectively compared to the default version

## Context

This work extends previous Monte Carlo Glauber model studies of U+U collisions (Hiroshi, 2009; Mohanty, 2011) and hydrodynamic calculations (Heinz, 2006; Hirano, 2010) by implementing deformed Uranium nuclei in the AMPT transport model. The study enables experimental selection of specific U+U collision configurations at RHIC (planned for 2012) to constrain initial condition models through measurements of v2, v3, and their fluctuations, and to test path-length dependence of partonic energy loss and local parity violation signals.
