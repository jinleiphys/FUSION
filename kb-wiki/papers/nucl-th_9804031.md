# Solutions of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for the four nucleons scattering states

> The Faddeev-Yakubowsky equations in configuration space have been solved for the four nucleon system. The results with an S-wave interaction model in the isospin approximation are presented. They concern the bound and scattering states below the first three-body threshold. The elastic phase-shifts for the N+NNN reaction in different ($S,T$) channels are given and the corresponding low energy expansions are discussed. Particular attention is payed to the n+t elastic cross section. Its resonant structure is well described in terms of a simple NN interaction. First results concerning the S-matrix for the coupled N+NNN-NN+NN channels and the strong deuteron-deuteron scattering length are obtained.

## Key claim

The authors present the first solution of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for the four-nucleon scattering problem, but only within an S-wave interaction model and the isospin approximation, meaning Coulomb and mass difference effects are not included.

## Method

The Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations for four identical nucleons were reduced to two coupled equations for K-type and H-type amplitudes, expanded in angular momentum variables with all orbital angular momenta set to zero (S-wave approximation). The radial components were expanded using cubic Hermite splines on three-dimensional grids, and the resulting linear system was solved iteratively using the GMRES algorithm with preconditioning. Scattering observables were extracted from the asymptotic behavior of the FY amplitudes by matching to the known three-body Faddeev amplitudes at large intercluster distances.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 4He: 30.30 MeV (estimated accuracy 0.01 MeV), in good agreement with best existing calculations
- First excited 0+ state binding energy relative to N+NNN threshold: 0.257 MeV (with N+NNN threshold at E = -8.535 MeV)
- Triton binding energy used as asymptotic state: 8.535 MeV
- Number of FY components in S-wave approximation: N_c = 4 (N_K = N_H = 2)
- Grid parameters for ground state: N_g = 12; for excited state: N_g = 6
- GMRES convergence achieved after approximately 30 matrix applications, independent of matrix dimension

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach from bound state calculations (previously performed by SSK (1992), GK (1993), and others) to the four-nucleon scattering continuum, addressing a manifest lack of convergence among different groups and methods even with simple interactions. The results enable future inclusion of realistic interactions and higher partial waves, with some first results already reported in CCG (1997). The method provides a bridge between the relative simplicity of A=2,3 problems and the complexity of many-body systems, particularly for understanding the A=4 chart in terms of fundamental NN interactions.
