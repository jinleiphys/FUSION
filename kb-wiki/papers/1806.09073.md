# The $S_{E1}$ factor of radiative $α$ capture on $^{12}$C in cluster effective field theory

> The $S_{E1}$-factor of radiative $α$-capture on $^{12}$C is studied in effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. A modification of the counting rules for the radiative capture amplitudes is discussed. We find that only two unfixed parameters remain in the amplitudes, and those two parameters are fitted to the experimental $S_{E1}$ data. An $S_{E1}$ factor is calculated at the Gamow-peak energy as $S_{E1}=59\pm 3$ keV$\cdot$b, and the result is found to be about 30% smaller than the estimates reported recently. An uncertainty of the estimate in the present work is also discussed.

# The $S_{E1}$ factor of radiative $\alpha$ capture on $^{12}$C in cluster effective field theory

## Key claim
The $S_{E1}$ factor at the Gamow-peak energy is calculated as $59 \pm 3$ keV$\cdot$b, which is about 30% smaller than recent estimates from other theoretical approaches. The result carries an additional theoretical uncertainty from higher-order corrections that has not been fully quantified, as a complete set of next-to-next-to-leading order terms was not included.

## Method
The radiative $\alpha$ capture on $^{12}$C is studied using cluster effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-leading order (NLO), treating $\alpha$ and $^{12}$C as point-like particles and the $^{16}$O ground state as a source field. The $p$-wave dressed $^{16}$O propagator is enhanced due to large cancellations between effective range terms and Coulomb self-energy terms, modifying the standard counting rules and leaving two independent structures in the amplitudes. The two unknown parameters are fitted to experimental $S_{E1}$ data at $E = 0.9-3.0$ MeV using a Markov chain Monte Carlo method, with the effective range parameters for $l=1$ fixed from elastic scattering phase shift data.

## Key numbers
- $S_{E1} = 59 \pm 3$ keV$\cdot$b at the Gamow-peak energy $E_G = 0.3$ MeV, with about 5% uncertainty from fitted parameters and cutoff dependence
- Effective range parameters for $l=1$: $r_1 = 0.415272(9)$ fm$^{-1}$, $P_1 = -0.57473(9)$ fm, $Q_1 = 0.02018(3)$ fm$^3$, fitted to $N=273$ phase shift data points with $\chi^2/N = 0.74$
- Scattering volume $a_1 = -1.658 \times 10^5$ fm$^3$, fixed by the binding energy of the $1_1^-$ state at $E = -0.045$ MeV
- Expansion parameter $Q/\Lambda_H \sim 1/3$, with Gamow peak momentum $k_G \simeq 41$ MeV and large momentum scale $\Lambda_H \sim 150$ MeV
- Ratio of amplitudes $|(X^{(l=1)}_{(c)}+X^{(l=1)}_{(d+e)}+ X^{(l=1)}_{(f)})/X^{(l=1)}_{(a+b)}| \sim 380-30$ at $E=0.1-3$ MeV, showing strong suppression of the non-pole amplitude

## Context
This work applies cluster EFT to the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction for the first time, extending the author's previous EFT studies of the elastic scattering and bound states (Ando 2016, 2018). The result $S_{E1}=59\pm 3$ keV$\cdot$b is about 30% smaller than recent estimates from phenomenological $R$-matrix analyses by deBoer et al. (2017) and An et al. (2015), as well as cluster model calculations by Schurmann et al. (2012). The approach enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections and uncertainty quantification, though the present work does not yet examine convergence of the perturbative expansion. Future work could extend the method to study the $\beta$-delayed $\alpha$ emission spectrum of $^{16}$N or the $\gamma$ angular distribution of the capture process.
