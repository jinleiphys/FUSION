# Range Corrections to Doublet S-Wave Neutron-Deuteron Scattering

> We calculate the range corrections to S-wave neutron-deuteron scattering in the doublet channel (S=1/2) to first order in r/a where a is the scattering length and r the effective range. Ultraviolet divergences appearing at this order can be absorbed into a redefinition of the leading order three-body force. The corrections to the elastic scattering amplitude below the deuteron breakup threshold are computed. Inclusion of the range corrections gives good agreement with measured scattering data and potential model calculations.

# Range Corrections to Doublet S-Wave Neutron-Deuteron Scattering

## Key claim

The authors calculate the linear corrections in r/a (where a is the scattering length and r the effective range) to S-wave neutron-deuteron scattering in the doublet channel, finding that including these range corrections gives good agreement with measured scattering data and potential model calculations, but only when treated perturbatively rather than nonperturbatively.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) with a dibaryon formalism, where auxiliary fields with baryon number two represent two-nucleon states in the 3S1 and 1S0 channels. They compute the elastic scattering amplitude below the deuteron breakup threshold by solving the leading order integral equation and then adding the range corrections perturbatively to first order in r/a, absorbing ultraviolet divergences into a redefinition of the leading order three-body force.

## Key numbers

- The triplet effective range parameters: gamma_t = 45.68 MeV, r_t = 1.76 fm
- The singlet effective range parameters: gamma_s = -7.88 MeV, r_s = 2.75 fm
- The dimensionless parameter s0 ≈ 1.0064, determined by the asymptotic behavior of the integral equation
- The product gamma_t * r_t ≈ 0.4, which sets the size of the expansion parameter

## Context

This work extends the leading order calculation of doublet channel neutron-deuteron scattering phase shifts by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (2000), which worked at order (r/a)^0. It contrasts with the nonperturbative treatment of range corrections by Gabbiani (2001) and others, which found that treating range nonperturbatively eliminated the Phillips line and gave results in strong disagreement with experiment. The perturbative approach preserves the three-body force parameter and the Phillips line phenomenology, enabling systematic improvement of three-body EFT calculations.
