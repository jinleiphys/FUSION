# Dispersive determination of resonances from $ππ$ scattering data

> We provide a precise, model- and parametrization-independent dispersive determination of the $f_0(500)$, $ρ(770)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_2(1270)$, $f_0(1370)$, $ρ(1450)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $ρ_3(1690)$ resonance pole parameters. They are obtained from the analytic continuation, by means of continued fractions, of forward dispersion relations, whose input is a recent global dispersive analysis of $ππ$ scattering data. From this dispersive study, we find no indications of other resonant poles below 1.7 GeV. Beyond this energy, we also provide resonance pole parameters from the direct analytic continuation of Global Fits to the three existing incompatible datasets. Depending on the dataset we find poles for the $ρ(1700)$, $f_0(1710)$, $ρ(1900)$, $f_2(1950)$, and $f_0(2020)$ resonances. We also present the Argand diagrams of these Global Fits and illustrate that each resonance does not necessarily have to trace a full circle in the diagram.

## Key claim

The paper provides a precise, model- and parametrization-independent dispersive determination of resonance pole parameters for eight resonances below 1.7 GeV from ππ scattering data, using analytic continuation via continued fractions of forward dispersion relations; the authors find no indications of other resonant poles below 1.7 GeV, but above this energy the results depend on which of three incompatible experimental datasets is used.

## Method

The authors use forward dispersion relations (FDRs) for the π⁰π⁰, π⁰π⁺, and I_t=1 amplitudes, whose input is a recent global dispersive analysis of ππ scattering data (Global Fits I, II, III) that satisfies FDRs up to 1.4–1.6 GeV. They analytically continue the FDR output into the complex plane using continued fractions (the FDR_C_N method), sampling N from 11 to 51 equally spaced points in real energy segments, and collect tens of thousands of samples to identify stable poles corresponding to resonances. The spin of each resonance is identified from the partial-wave parametrizations used as input.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive resonance determination method of Peláez, Rabán, and Ruiz de Elvira (2022), which previously identified the f₀(1370), f₂(1270), and f₀(1500) poles from the F⁰⁰ amplitude, to now include isovector resonances via the F⁰⁺ amplitude and to cover all resonances coupling strongly to ππ below 1.7 GeV. The results are consistent with earlier Roy-equation and GKPY determinations for elastic-region resonances (Caprini 2005, Moussallam 2011, Garcia-Martin 2011) and provide the first model-independent dispersive determination of the ρ(1450) and ρ₃(1690) poles from ππ scattering data. Above 1.7 GeV, the method yields dataset-dependent poles for ρ(1700), f₀(1710), ρ(1900), f₂(1950), and f₀(2020) from direct analytic continuation of the Global Fits, but these are not dispersively constrained.
