# Heavy-quark spin polarization induced by the Kondo effect in a magnetic field

> We propose a new mechanism of the heavy-quark spin polarization (HQSP) in quark matter induced by the Kondo effect under external magnetic field. The Kondo effect is caused by a condensate between a heavy and a light quark called the Kondo condensate leading to a mixing of the heavy and light quark spins. Thus the HQSP is driven through the Kondo effect from light quarks coupling with the magnetic field in quark matter. For demonstration, we employ the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio type model under a magnetic field, and investigate the HQSP within the linear response theory with vertex corrections required by the $U(1)_{\rm EM}$ electromagnetic gauge invariance. As a result, we find that the HQSP arises significantly with the appearance of the Kondo effect. Our findings are testable in future sign-problem-free lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that heavy-quark spin polarization (HQSP) can arise significantly in quark matter through the Kondo effect under an external magnetic field, even in the heavy-quark mass limit \(m_Q \to \infty\) where the ordinary Zeeman interaction vanishes; the effect requires the presence of a Kondo condensate that mixes heavy- and light-quark spins, converting the magnetic response of light quarks into heavy-quark spin polarization.

## Method

The authors employ a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with a four-point interaction between light quarks (described by Dirac theory) and heavy quarks (described by heavy-quark effective theory, HQET), introducing a Kondo condensate via the hedgehog ansatz for the mean fields. They compute the HQSP response function within linear response theory, including vertex corrections required by \(U(1)_{\rm EM}\) gauge invariance, and evaluate the response in both the dynamical and static momentum limits.

## Key numbers

* The Kondo condensate \(\Delta\) scales as \(\Delta \sim \Lambda \exp\left(-\frac{1}{N_c g \rho(\mu)}\right)\) at zero temperature, with density of states \(\rho(\mu) = \mu^2/(2\pi^2)\) and \(N_c = 3\).
* The energy gap between the Kondo quasiparticle and quasihole at the Fermi surface is \(\delta E_{\rm min.} = \sqrt{8|\Delta|^2}\).
* The heavy-quark spin polarization vanishes in the absence of the Kondo condensate (\(\Delta = 0\)) due to the lack of direct magnetic coupling to the heavy quark in the Lagrangian.

## Context

This work extends the study of the QCD Kondo effect in dense quark matter, previously investigated by Yasui (2013) and Hattori (2015), and builds on the authors' own prior work on the chiral separation effect enhanced by the Kondo effect (Suenaga, 2020) and magnetic responses in hybridized systems (Araki, 2020). The proposed mechanism enables future tests in sign-problem-free lattice simulations and provides a new pathway for heavy-quark spin polarization without \(1/m_Q\) suppression.
