# Unbalanced low-density neutron matter

> We consider polarized neutron matter at low densities. We have performed Diffusion Monte Carlo simulations for normal neutron matter with different population numbers for each species. We analyze the competition between different phases in the grand canonical ensemble and mention aspects of neutron-star phenomenology that are impacted by the effects described.

# Unbalanced low-density neutron matter

## Key claim
Diffusion Monte Carlo simulations of polarized neutron matter at low densities show that the energetically favored state is phase separation into an unpolarized superfluid and a polarized normal neutron gas, rather than a homogeneous polarized superfluid; this conclusion is drawn from fixed-node QMC calculations that provide upper bounds to the true ground-state energies.

## Method
The authors performed Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) simulations for normal neutron matter with unequal spin populations, using a trial wave function consisting of a Jastrow factor multiplied by a product of two Slater determinants (one for each spin species) with plane-wave orbitals. They simulated five different polarization fractions (x = 0, 0.333, 0.579, 0.818, 1) at three total number densities, using the s-wave channel of the Argonne v18 potential with perturbative p-wave corrections, and extracted the energy as a function of density and polarization to construct free-energy functions and compute pressures in the grand canonical ensemble.

## Key numbers
- Three total number densities studied: n₁ = 6.65×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, n₂ = 2.16×10⁻³ fm⁻³, n₃ = 5.32×10⁻³ fm⁻³ (corresponding to 0.0042n_sat, 0.0135n_sat, and 0.033n_sat, where n_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³).
- Neutron-neutron scattering length a ≈ −18.6 fm; effective range r_e ≈ 2.7 fm.
- Dimensionless coupling strengths: k_F a = 5.02, 7.44, and 10.05 for the three densities; k_F r_e = 0.59, 0.88, and 1.19.
- For the unpolarized superfluid fit: g_{0,0} = 0.942, g_{0,1} r_e = 0.003 MeV⁻¹, g_{1,0}/a = 2.301 MeV.
- For a fixed average chemical potential μ = 1.94 MeV, the critical chemical potential splitting is δμ_c = 0.53 MeV.
- Superfluid gap Δ/E_F increases from 0.4 at n₁ to 0.44 at n₃.

## Context
This work extends Quantum Monte Carlo studies of neutron matter by Gezerlis and collaborators (2008, 2010, 2011) from the unpolarized superfluid case to polarized normal matter, and parallels studies of ultracold fermionic atoms by Pilati (2008) where phase separation between superfluid and normal phases is observed. The results provide microscopic input for neutron-star crust phenomenology, where magnetic fields can polarize neutrons and quench pairing, affecting heat transport and specific heat; they also offer a benchmark for Skyrme energy-density functionals, following the approach of Forbes (2013) which compared neutron polaron binding energies to Skyrme predictions.
