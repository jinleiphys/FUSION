# On the possibility of a discontinuous quark-mass dependence of baryon octet and decuplet masses

> We compute the quark-mass dependence of the baryon octet and decuplet masses within the $χ$-MS scheme at the one-loop level. The results are confronted with recent lattice QCD simulations of the MILC collaboration. A fair reproduction of the quark-mass dependence as suggested by the lattice simulations is obtained up to pion masses of about 700 MeV. Based on the chiral one-loop results we predict that the dependence of the baryon octet and decuplet masses on the quark-masses is discontinuous. Typically the pion-mass dependence is smooth up to about 300 MeV only. This is a consequence of self consistency imposed on the partial summation, i.e. the masses used in the loop functions are identical to those obtained from the baryon self energies. The 'mysterious' quark-mass dependence of the Xi mass predicted by the MILC collaboration is recovered in terms of a discontinuous chiral extrapolation.

# On the possibility of a discontinuous quark-mass dependence of baryon octet and decuplet masses

## Key claim

The authors predict that the dependence of baryon octet and decuplet masses on the quark masses is discontinuous, with the pion-mass dependence typically smooth only up to about 300 MeV, as a consequence of self consistency imposed on the partial summation where the masses used in the loop functions are identical to those obtained from the baryon self energies. This prediction is hedged by the caveat that further chiral correction terms may connect the various branches in a smooth manner, and that the results may merely indicate the chiral extrapolation stops making sense at quite small quark masses.

## Method

The authors compute baryon octet and decuplet self energies at the one-loop level within the relativistic chiral Lagrangian using the χ-MS scheme, applying a chiral loop expansion rather than a strict chiral expansion. The Q^2 counter terms (b_0, b_D, b_F, d_0, d_D) are fitted to reproduce the physical baryon masses and the SU(3) limit values M_[8] ≈ 1575 MeV and M_[10] ≈ 1710 MeV at m_π ≈ 690 MeV from MILC lattice simulations, with the infrared renormalization scale μ_IR varied between 350 and 550 MeV and μ_UV fixed at 800 MeV. The baryon masses are then solved from a set of coupled, non-linear algebraic equations where the masses in the loop functions must match the total masses from the self energies.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma term is predicted as σ_πN = 54 ± 2 MeV, based on the SU(3) symmetric point at m_π ≈ 690 MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma terms are σ_{K^- p} ≈ 380 MeV and σ_{K^- n} ≈ 355 MeV.
- The SU(3) limit values used from MILC simulations are M_[8] ≈ 1575 MeV and M_[10] ≈ 1710 MeV at m_π ≈ 690 MeV.
- The chiral loop expansion reproduces lattice data up to pion masses of about 700 MeV.
- The baryon masses are predicted to jump at pion masses as low as about 300 MeV, with the Ξ mass showing a critical pion mass of about 300-400 MeV.
- The physical baryon masses are reproduced with decompositions such as M_N = 939 MeV (e.g., 813 + 232 - 105 for μ_IR = 450 MeV) and M_Δ = 1232 MeV (e.g., 1108 + 164 - 39 for μ_IR = 450 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the partial summation scheme developed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002) and Semke and Lutz (2005), which was guided by manifest covariance, analyticity, and causality, and confronts it with lattice QCD simulations from the MILC collaboration (2001, 2004) using dynamical u-, d-, and s-quarks in the staggered approximation. The results are similar in spirit to an analysis by Frink and Meissner (2005) based on the cutoff scheme of Donoghue and Holstein (1998), and the predicted pion-nucleon sigma term of 54 MeV is compatible with Frink and Meissner (2005) who suggest σ_πN ≈ 52 MeV, and with Pascalutsa and Vanderhagen (2005) who arrive at σ_πN ≈ 57 MeV from a chiral SU(2) analysis. The work enables future lattice QCD simulations to test the predicted discontinuous quark-mass dependence and may signal a new type of phase transition or instability in QCD for certain parameter choices.
