# Collisional energy loss above the critical temperature in QCD

> We compute the collisional energy loss for a heavy quark above the critical temperature in Quantum ChromoDynamics (QCD). We work in the semi Quark-Gluon Plasma, which assumes that this region is dominated by the non-trivial holonomy of the thermal Wilson line. Relative to the result to leading order in perturbation theory, at a fixed value of the coupling constant we generically we find that collisional energy loss is suppressed by powers of the Polyakov loop, l < 1. For small values of the loop, this suppression is linear for the scattering off of light quarks, and quadratic for the scattering off of gluons, or for Compton scattering.

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss for a heavy quark in the semi-Quark-Gluon Plasma (sQGP) is suppressed relative to leading-order perturbative QCD by powers of the Polyakov loop \(l<1\), with the suppression being linear in \(l\) for scattering off light quarks and quadratic in \(l\) for scattering off gluons or Compton scattering, under the assumption that the region just above the critical temperature is dominated by non-trivial holonomy of the thermal Wilson line.

## Method

The authors compute the collisional energy loss for a heavy quark to leading logarithmic order in perturbation theory, but expand about a fixed background gluon field \(A_0^{\rm cl}\) that generates a non-trivial holonomy for the thermal Wilson line. The eigenvalues of the Wilson line are determined from lattice QCD results for the Polyakov loop, after subtracting perturbative corrections. The energy loss is then evaluated for Coulomb scattering off light quarks and for Compton scattering off gluons in the \(t\)-channel and \(u\)-channel, yielding suppression factors \(S^{\rm qk}(Q)\) and \(S^{\rm gl}(Q)\) that multiply the standard perturbative results.

## Key numbers

- For three colors, the suppression factor for scattering off light quarks in the nearly confining background field is \(S^{\rm qk}_{N_c=3}(Q \to Q_{\rm conf}) = \frac{1}{9} + \frac{8}{\pi \sqrt{3}} \ell + \mathcal{O}(\ell^2)\).
- For three colors, the suppression factor for scattering off gluons in the nearly confining background field is \(S^{\rm gl}_{N_c=3}(Q \to Q_{\rm conf}) = \frac{27}{4} \ell^2(1-\ell) + \mathcal{O}(\ell^3)\).
- In the perturbative regime (\(Q=0\)), both suppression factors equal unity: \(S^{\rm qk}(0)=1\) and \(S^{\rm gl}(0)=1\).
- In the confined phase of the pure glue theory, the quark suppression factor is \(S^{\rm qk}(Q_{\rm conf}) = 1/N_c^2 = 1/9\) for \(N_c=3\), and the gluon suppression factor vanishes: \(S^{\rm gl}(Q_{\rm conf}) = 0\).
- The perturbative result for Coulomb scattering off light quarks is \(\left. dE/dx \right|_{Q=0}^{\rm qk} = \alpha_s^2 T^2 N_f \frac{N_c^2-1}{12 N_c} \pi \ln(ET/m_D^2)\).
- The perturbative result for Compton scattering in the \(t\)-channel is \(\left. dE/dx \right|_{Q=0}^{\rm gl, t-ch} = \alpha_s^2 T^2 (N_c^2-1) \frac{\pi}{6} \ln(ET/m_D^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of heavy quark collisional energy loss by Braaten and Thoma (1991) to the semi-Quark-Gluon Plasma, a region where the Polyakov loop takes intermediate values between the confined and perturbative phases. The approach builds on the matrix model framework developed by Pisarski (2000), Dumitru (2003), and others, and uses lattice QCD results for the Polyakov loop from DeTar (2009) and Petreczky (2012) to determine the background field. The results enable future phenomenological studies of heavy quark energy loss at RHIC and LHC temperatures, and the authors note that similar suppression factors should apply to other electromagnetic probes such as dilepton and real photon production.
