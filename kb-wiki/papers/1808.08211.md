# Exploring Bayesian parameter estimation for chiral effective field theory using nucleon-nucleon phase shifts

> We recently developed a Bayesian framework for parameter estimation in general effective field theories. Here we present selected results from using that framework to estimate parameters with a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential derived using chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT): the semi-local NN potential of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (EKM). There are many NN scattering data, up to high energies, and with rather small errors, so imposing a penalty for unnatural low-energy constants (LECs) usually has a small effect on the fits. In contrast, we have found that including an estimate of higher orders in $χ$EFT plays an important role in robust parameter estimation.We present two case studies where our Bayesian machinery illuminates physics issues. The first involves the EKM potential at fourth order in the $χ$EFT expansion: the two-dimensional posterior probability density function (pdf) for the fourth-order $s$-wave LECs obtained from the Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts indicates these parameters in the NN potential are degenerate. We trace this feature of the pdf to the presence of an operator in the fourth-order NN potential that vanishes on-shell. The second case study examines the stability of LEC extractions as more data at higher energies are included in the fit. We show that as long as $χ$EFT truncation errors are properly accounted for in the parameter estimation, the LEC values extracted using our Bayesian approach are not sensitive to the maximum energy chosen for the fit.Uncorrelated and fully correlated models for the truncation errors are compared, pointing the way to the use of Gaussian processes to more generally model the correlation structure.

## Key claim

Including an estimate of higher-order chiral EFT truncation errors in the Bayesian parameter estimation framework is essential for robust extraction of low-energy constants (LECs), while imposing a naturalness penalty alone has little effect when fitting to the abundant, high-precision nucleon-nucleon scattering data.

## Method

The authors applied a Bayesian framework for parameter estimation to the semi-local chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon potential of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (EKM), fitting LECs to Nijmegen PWA93 phase shifts using Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling. They computed posterior probability density functions for LECs in various partial waves, comparing uncorrelated and fully correlated models for the truncation error covariance matrix, and examined the stability of LEC extractions as the maximum fit energy was varied.

## Key numbers

- The EKM potential uses a regulator parameter R = 0.9 fm and a breakdown scale Λ_b = 600 MeV.
- For fits to phase shifts, the energy mesh used was E_lab = 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, 200 MeV; nominal E_max values were 25 MeV (LO), 100 MeV (NLO and N2LO), and 200 MeV (N3LO and N4LO).
- For cross-section fits incorporating truncation errors, a finer mesh of 15 energies from 1 to 300 MeV was used.
- The naturalness prior for LECs used a fixed value of ā = 5 for the numerical results.
- The expansion parameter Q is defined as Q = μ/Λ_b, where μ is a low-energy scale and Λ_b = 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian framework for EFT parameter estimation developed by the same authors (Wesolowski et al., 2015; Furnstahl et al., 2015) and applies it specifically to chiral EFT nucleon-nucleon potentials. It contrasts with traditional least-squares fitting procedures used by Epelbaum et al. (2014) and Carlsson et al. (2015), which either neglect truncation errors or incorporate them via ad hoc penalty terms. The results demonstrate that proper Bayesian treatment of truncation errors yields LEC values stable with respect to the maximum fit energy, and the comparison of uncorrelated versus fully correlated error models motivates future use of Gaussian processes for more realistic correlation structures.
