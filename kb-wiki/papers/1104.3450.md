# A Gauge-Invariant Reorganization of Thermal Gauge Theory

> This dissertation is devoted to the study of thermodynamics for quantum gauge theories. The poor convergence of quantum field theory at finite temperature has been the main obstacle in the practical applications of thermal QCD for decades. In this dissertation I apply hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory, which is a gauge-invariant reorganization of the conventional perturbative expansion for quantum gauge theories to the thermodynamics of QED and Yang-Mills theory to three-loop order. For the Abelian case, I present a calculation of the free energy of a hot gas of electrons and photons by expanding in a power series in $m_D/T$, $m_f/T$ and $e^2$, where $m_D$ and $m_f$ are the photon and electron thermal masses, respectively, and $e$ is the coupling constant. I demonstrate that the hard-thermal-loop perturbation reorganization improves the convergence of the successive approximations to the QED free energy at large coupling, $e \sim 2$. For the non-Abelian case, I present a calculation of the free energy of a hot gas of gluons by expanding in a power series in $m_D/T$ and $g^2$, where $m_D$ is the gluon thermal mass and $g$ is the coupling constant. I show that at three-loop order hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory is compatible with lattice results for the pressure, energy density, and entropy down to temperatures $T \sim 2-3\;T_c$. The results suggest that HTLpt provides a systematic framework that can be used to calculate static and dynamic quantities for temperatures relevant at LHC.

## Key claim

Hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), a gauge-invariant reorganization of the conventional perturbative expansion for quantum gauge theories, improves the convergence of successive approximations to the QED free energy at large coupling (e ~ 2) and, at three-loop order, yields results for the Yang-Mills pressure, energy density, and entropy that are compatible with lattice data down to temperatures T ~ 2-3 T_c, suggesting the framework is systematic for LHC-relevant temperatures.

## Method

The author applied hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory to compute the free energy of a hot gas of electrons and photons (QED) and of a hot gas of gluons (Yang-Mills theory) to three-loop order. For QED, the free energy was expanded in a power series in m_D/T, m_f/T, and e^2, where m_D and m_f are the photon and electron thermal masses and e is the coupling constant; for Yang-Mills, the expansion was in m_D/T and g^2, where m_D is the gluon thermal mass and g is the coupling constant. The calculations used the imaginary-time formalism with dimensional regularization, evaluating sum-integrals over Euclidean momenta and employing HTL Feynman rules for propagators, vertices, and counterterms.

## Context

This work extends the application of hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory, previously developed for gauge theories, to three-loop order in both QED and Yang-Mills thermodynamics. It addresses the long-standing problem of poor convergence in finite-temperature quantum field theory, which has been the main obstacle in practical thermal QCD applications for decades. The results enable systematic calculations of static and dynamic quantities at temperatures relevant for the LHC, building on earlier HTLpt calculations by Andersen, Strickland, and collaborators, and providing a framework that can be compared with lattice QCD results from groups such as those of Bazavov et al. and Borsanyi et al.
