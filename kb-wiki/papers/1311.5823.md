# Probabilistic picture for medium-induced jet evolution

> A high energy jet that propagates in a dense medium generates a cascade of partons that can be described as a classical branching process. A simple generating functional for the probabilities to observe a given number of gluons at a given time is derived. This is used to obtain an evolution equation for the inclusive one-gluon distribution, that takes into account the dependence upon the energy and the transverse momentum of the observed gluon. A study of the explicit transverse momentum dependence of the splitting kernel leads us to identify large corrections to the jet quenching parameter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that medium-induced jet evolution can be described as a classical branching process where successive gluon splittings are independent, but the authors qualify that this picture holds only when the branching time is much shorter than the medium size and that the transverse momentum dependence of the splitting kernel reveals large radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) that are formally infrared divergent.

## Method

The authors construct a generating functional for the probabilities to observe a given number of gluons at a given time, based on iterating the elementary \(1 \to 2\) splitting process within the BDMPSZ framework. They derive an evolution equation for the inclusive one-gluon distribution that includes both transverse momentum broadening via medium rescattering and collinear gluon splittings, using the harmonic approximation for the dipole cross section and treating the medium as a random color field with a specific correlator.

## Key numbers

- The branching time is given by \(\tau_{\rm br} \simeq \sqrt{\omega_0 / \hat q_0}\), where \(\omega_0 \equiv z(1-z)p_0^+\) and \(\hat q_0 \equiv \hat q f(z)\) with \(f(z) = 1 - z(1-z)\).
- The maximum energy that can be taken away by an offspring gluon is \(\omega_c = \hat q_0 t_L^2\), where \(t_L\) is the medium size in light-cone time.
- The typical time between successive branchings is \(\Delta t_{\rm rad}(\epsilon p^+) \simeq (1/2\bar\alpha) \sqrt{\epsilon p^+ / \hat q}\), where \(\bar\alpha \equiv \alpha_s N_c / \pi\).
- The jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) is given by \(\hat q(t,\k^2) = g^2 N_c n(t) \int_\l \l^2 \gamma(\l) \approx 4\pi \alpha_s^2 N_c n(t) \ln(\k^2 / m_D^2)\), with the Debye mass \(m_D\) as the infrared cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPSZ framework for jet quenching (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) by providing a probabilistic description of the full in-medium gluon cascade, going beyond energy loss from the leading particle. It builds on earlier studies by the same authors (Blaizot 2012) that showed loss of color coherence occurs on time scales comparable to the branching process, and it generalizes the energy-only evolution equation studied in Blaizot (2013) to include transverse momentum dependence. The identification of radiative corrections to \(\hat q\) connects to recent work on transverse momentum broadening (Liou 2013).
