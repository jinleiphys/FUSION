# Simultaneously Constraining the Neutron Star Equation of State and Mass Distribution through Multimessenger Observations and Nuclear Benchmarks

> With ongoing advancements in nuclear theory and experimentation, together with a growing body of neutron star (NS) observations, a wealth of information on the equation of state (EOS) for matter at extreme densities has become accessible. Here, we utilize a hybrid EOS formulation that combines an empirical parameterization centered around the nuclear saturation density with a generic three-segment piecewise polytrope model at higher densities. We incorporate data derived from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD), and from experiments such as PREX-II and CREX. Furthermore, we examine the influence of a total of 129 NS mass measurements up to April 2023, as well as simultaneous mass and radius measurements derived from the X-ray emission from surface hot spots on NSs. Additionally, we consider constraints on tidal properties inferred from the gravitational waves emitted by coalescing NS binaries. To integrate this extensive and varied array of constraints, we utilize a hierarchical Bayesian statistical framework to simultaneously deduce the EOS and the distribution of NS masses. We find that incorporating data from $χ$EFT significantly tightens the constraints on the EOS of NSs near or below the nuclear saturation density. However, constraints derived from pQCD computations and nuclear experiments such as PREX-II and CREX have minimal impact.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating chiral effective field theory (χEFT) constraints significantly tightens the neutron star equation of state near or below nuclear saturation density, while constraints from perturbative QCD and nuclear experiments such as PREX-II and CREX have minimal impact on the inferred EOS.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid EOS parameterization combining an empirical nuclear model around saturation density with a three-segment piecewise polytrope at higher densities, introducing eight free parameters. They perform hierarchical Bayesian inference using nested sampling to simultaneously constrain the EOS and the neutron star mass distribution, incorporating data from χEFT calculations, perturbative QCD, PREX-II and CREX experiments, 70 highly reliable neutron star mass measurements, NICER X-ray observations of pulsars, and gravitational wave data from GW170817 and GW190425.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L = 54^{+10}_{-10} MeV (90% credibility)
- Curvature parameter of symmetry energy: K_sym = -158^{+73}_{-63} MeV (90% credibility)
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.34^{+0.43}_{-0.53} km (90% credibility)
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 436^{+109}_{-117} (90% credibility)
- Maximum mass of a non-rotating neutron star: 2.22^{+0.21}_{-0.19} solar masses (90% credibility)

## Context

This work extends the hybrid EOS parameterization approach developed by Biswas (2020) and applied in multi-messenger analyses, incorporating the most recent neutron star mass catalog from Fan (2023) and χEFT uncertainty quantification from the BUQEYE collaboration. The simultaneous inference of EOS and mass distribution within a hierarchical Bayesian framework enables unbiased parameter estimation by accounting for degeneracies between mass and EOS, and the results provide improved constraints for future multimessenger observations of binary neutron star mergers and NICER observations.
