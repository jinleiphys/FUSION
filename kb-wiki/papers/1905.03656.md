# Constraint on the maximum mass of neutron stars using GW170817 event

> We revisit the constraint on the maximum mass of cold spherical neutron stars coming from the observational results of GW170817. We develop a new framework for the analysis by employing both energy and angular momentum conservation laws as well as solid results of latest numerical-relativity simulations and of neutron stars in equilibrium. The new analysis shows that the maximum mass of cold spherical neutron stars can be only weakly constrained as $M_{\rm max} \alt 2.3M_\odot$. Our present result illustrates that the merger remnant neutron star at the onset of collapse to a black hole is not necessarily rapidly rotating and shows that we have to take into account the angular momentum conservation law to impose the constraint on the maximum mass of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of cold spherical neutron stars can be only weakly constrained to \(M_{\rm max} \lesssim 2.3M_\odot\) from GW170817, which is about \(0.1M_\odot\) larger than previous estimates, because the merger remnant neutron star at the onset of collapse to a black hole is not necessarily rapidly rotating and angular momentum conservation must be taken into account.

## Method

The authors developed a framework combining energy and angular momentum conservation laws with results from numerical-relativity simulations and equilibrium models of rotating neutron stars. They analyzed rigidly rotating neutron stars at marginally stable states (turning points) along equilibrium sequences for twelve piecewise polytropic equations of state, using the observed total binary mass \(M = 2.74^{+0.04}_{-0.01}M_\odot\) from GW170817 and estimates of ejecta mass, torus mass, gravitational-wave energy, and neutrino energy to derive the gravitational mass at collapse \(M_f\) and the ratio \(f_r = M_f / M_{\rm max}\).

## Key numbers

* The maximum mass of cold spherical neutron stars is constrained as \(M_{\rm max} \lesssim 2.3M_\odot\).
* The gravitational mass at the onset of collapse is \(M_f = 2.44^{+0.15}_{-0.12}M_\odot\) for \(M_{\rm out}=0.06 \pm 0.04M_\odot\).
* The total dissipated energy (gravitational waves plus neutrinos) is \((E_{\rm GW}+E_\nu)c^{-2} = (0.20 \pm 0.08)M_\odot\).
* The inspiral gravitational-wave energy is \(E_{\rm GW,i} = 0.035\)–\(0.045M_\odot c^2\) for \(\Lambda_{1.35} \leq 1000\).
* The initial angular momentum of the binary at merger is \(J_0 \approx 5.8 \times 10^{49}\)–\(6.3 \times 10^{49}\,{\rm erg\,s}\) for neutron stars with \(R_{1.35}=10.5\)–\(14\,{\rm km}\).
* The maximum angular momentum of rigidly rotating neutron stars along the turning point sequence ranges from \(J_{\rm MS,R} \approx 3.567 \times 10^{49}\,{\rm erg\,s}\) (EOS-2) to \(5.548 \times 10^{49}\,{\rm erg\,s}\) (EOS-11).
* The ratio \(f_r = M_f / M_{\rm max}\) at the mass-shedding limit ranges from 1.175 (EOS-2) to 1.234 (EOS-11) across the equations of state studied.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Shibata (2017) and Margalit and Metzger (2017), which suggested a maximum mass \(\lesssim 2.2M_\odot\) under the assumption that the merger remnant was rapidly rotating at collapse. It also contrasts with Rezzolla (2018) and Ruiz (2018), who derived tighter constraints of approximately \(2.17M_\odot\) and \(2.16\)–\(2.28M_\odot\) respectively. The present result shows that the upper bound is weaker by about \(0.1M_\odot\) when angular momentum conservation and the uncertain post-merger evolution are properly included, and it enables future work to refine the constraint by better determining the post-merger gravitational-wave and neutrino emission.
