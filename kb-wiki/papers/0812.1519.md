# Coherent and incoherent diffractive hadron production in pA collisions and gluon saturation

> We study coherent and incoherent diffractive hadron production in high energy quarkonium--heavy nucleus collisions as a probe of the gluon saturation regime of QCD. Taking this process as a model for pA collisions, we argue that the coherent diffractive gluon production, in which the target nucleus states intact, exhibits a remarkable sensitivity to the energy, rapidity and atomic number dependence. The incoherent diffractive gluon production is less sensitive to the details of the low-x dynamics but can serve as a probe of fluctuations in the color glass condensate. As a quantitative measure of the nuclear effects on diffractive hadron production we introduce a new observable -- the diffractive nuclear modification factor. We discuss possible signatures of gluon saturation in diffractive gluon production at RHIC, LHC and EIC.

## Key claim

Coherent diffractive gluon production in quarkonium–heavy nucleus collisions is a sensitive probe of gluon saturation, while incoherent diffractive gluon production is less sensitive to low-x dynamics but can serve as a probe of fluctuations in the color glass condensate; however, the kinematic regions of RHIC and LHC are not asymptotic, and the ratio of coherent to incoherent diffractive hadron production at midrapidity increases from RHIC to LHC only if gluon saturation effects in the proton are assumed to be small.

## Method

The author derives cross sections for coherent and incoherent diffractive gluon production in quarkonium–nucleus collisions using the color glass condensate framework and the dipole model of Mueller, starting from the Glauber–Mueller formula for the forward elastic scattering amplitude. The quasi-classical approximation (neglecting low-x evolution) is used first, then low-x evolution effects are included by replacing the quasi-classical amplitudes with solutions of the Balitsky–Kovchegov equation for the nucleus and the BFKL equation for the proton, and by convoluting with the dipole density from BFKL evolution in the incident quarkonium. The cross sections are expressed in terms of integrals over impact parameter, dipole sizes, and gluon transverse coordinates, with the incoherent case involving the dispersion of the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale in the quasi-classical approximation is defined as \(Q_{s0}^2 = \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s N_c}{N_c^2-1} \rho T_A(\mathbf{b}) xG(x,1/\mathbf{r}^2)\), with the gluon distribution \(xG(x,1/\mathbf{r}^2) = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi} \ln \frac{1}{\mathbf{r}^2 \mu^2}\).
* The proton profile function is \(S_p(\mathbf{b}) = \frac{1}{\pi R_p^2} e^{-b^2/R_p^2}\) with \(R_p \approx 1\) fm (from \(B = 12.6\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
* The condition for the expansion used in the incoherent cross section to hold is \(\frac{1}{4} Q_s^2 r^2 \ll 2 A^{1/6}\); for gold (\(A=197\)) and \(\lambda = 0.25\), there are about 6 units of rapidity between the onset of saturation and the breakdown of this expansion.
* The elastic \(pN\) cross section is \(\sigma^{pN}_{\text{el}} = \frac{(\sigma^{pN}_{\text{tot}})^2}{8\pi R_p^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the author’s earlier studies of coherent diffractive hadron production (Li and Tuchin, 2008) by calculating the incoherent diffractive gluon production cross section, which is proportional to the dispersion of the quasi-classical scattering amplitude in impact parameter space, as expected from Glauber theory (Glauber, 1955). The results generalize previous work on diffractive production at large and small invariant masses (Wusthoff, 1997; Bartels, 1999; Kopeliovich, 1999; Kovchegov, 2001; Golec-Biernat, 2005; Marquet, 2004, 2007; Munier, 2003) to all invariant masses and include gluon evolution at all rapidity intervals, explicitly incorporating gluon saturation effects in the nucleus unlike models based on leading twist nuclear shadowing (Frankfurt, 2003; Guzey, 2005). The formalism enables phenomenological studies at RHIC, LHC, and the EIC, and the absence of the predicted increase in the coherent-to-incoherent ratio could serve as evidence for gluon saturation in the proton.
