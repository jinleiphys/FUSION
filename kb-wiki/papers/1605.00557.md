# Strangeness in nuclear physics

> Extensions of nuclear physics to the strange sector are reviewed, covering data and models of Lambda and other hypernuclei, multi-strange matter, and anti-kaon bound states and condensation. Past achievements are highlighted, present unresolved problems discussed, and future directions outlined.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the entire field of strangeness in nuclear physics, covering data and models of Lambda and other hypernuclei, multi-strange matter, and antikaon bound states and condensation, while highlighting past achievements, unresolved problems, and future directions without presenting a single new experimental result or theoretical calculation.

## Method

The authors compiled and synthesized experimental data from emulsion studies, counter experiments using (K⁻,π⁻), (π⁺,K⁺), (e,e'K⁺), and stopped-kaon reactions, along with theoretical frameworks including the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA), shell-model calculations, and mean-field models. They organized the material by hypernuclear species, production reactions, decay mechanisms, and theoretical approaches, drawing on conference proceedings, special volumes, and review articles spanning several decades.

## Key numbers

- Lambda separation energy for ⁵_ΛHe: 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV (from 1784 emulsion events)
- Lambda separation energy for ¹²_ΛC: 10.76 ± 0.19 MeV (from 6 emulsion events; a reanalysis gives 10.80 ± 0.18 MeV)
- Lambda-nucleus well depth: approximately 30 MeV (extracted from continuum data of several medium-mass hypernuclei)
- Formation rate for s-shell states in ¹²_ΛC from stopped K⁻ on ¹²C: (0.98 ± 0.12) × 10⁻³ per stopped kaon
- Formation rate for p-shell states in ¹²_ΛC from stopped K⁻ on ¹²C: (2.3 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³ per stopped kaon
- Ratio of Sigma to Lambda production in K⁻ capture at rest on carbon: approximately 5 times as many Sigma's as Lambda's
- Branching ratio for capture on multinucleon clusters with no emitted pions (R_m) in carbon: 0.19 (19%)
- Ground-state formation probability for ¹²_ΛB from (K⁻_stop,π⁰) reaction: (0.28 ± 0.08) × 10⁻³ per stopped kaon

## Context

This review extends and consolidates the work presented in earlier reviews by Hashimoto and Tamura (2006), Botta, Bressani, and Garbarino (2012), and Feliciello and Nagae (2015), as well as the triennial conference series on Hypernuclear and Strange Particle Physics (Gal and Hungerford, 2005; Pochodzalla and Walcher, 2007; Gibson et al., 2010; Julia-Diaz et al., 2013). The paper enables future work by identifying unresolved problems such as the precise spin-dependence of the Lambda-nucleon interaction, the nature of charge asymmetry in mirror hypernuclei, and the in-medium modification of the antikaon-nucleon interaction, while providing a comprehensive reference for experimental and theoretical practitioners entering the field.
