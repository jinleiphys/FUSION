# Geometrical scaling for energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider to those at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Based on the recent RHIC and LHC experimental results, the $\langle p_T\rangle$ dependence of identified light flavour charged hadrons on $\sqrt{(\frac{dN}{dy})/S_{\perp}}$, relevant scale in gluon saturation picture, is studied from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 GeV up to 5.02 TeV. This study is extended to the slopes of the $\langle p_T\rangle$ dependence on the particle mass and the $\langleβ_T\rangle$ parameter from Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) fits of the $p_T$ spectra. A systematic decrease of the slope of the $\langle p_T\rangle$ dependence on $\sqrt{(\frac{dN}{dy})/S_{\perp}}$ from BES to the LHC energies is evidenced. While for the RHIC energies, within the experimental errors, the $\langle p_T\rangle$/$\sqrt{(\frac{dN}{dy})/S_{\perp}}$ does not depend on centrality, at the LHC energies a deviation from a linear behaviour is observed towards the most central collisions. The influence of the corona contribution to the observed trends is discussed. The slopes of the $\langle p_T\rangle$ particle mass dependence and the $\langleβ_T\rangle$ parameter from BGBW fits scale well with $\sqrt{(\frac{dN}{dy})/S_{\perp}}$. Similar systematic trends for pp at $\sqrt{s}$=7 TeV are in a good agreement with the ones corresponding to Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV pointing to a system size independent behaviour.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the average transverse momentum dependence on the geometrical scaling variable sqrt((dN/dy)/S_perp) shows a systematic decrease of its slope from RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies to LHC energies, but this trend is qualified by the observation that at LHC energies the most central collisions deviate from a linear behavior, showing a saturation trend.

## Method

The authors used Glauber Monte Carlo simulations to estimate the transverse overlapping area S_perp of colliding nuclei for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV from RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 and 5.02 TeV from LHC. They combined these geometrical estimates with published identified charged hadron densities to construct the scaling variable sqrt((dN/dy)/S_perp), then performed linear fits of the average transverse momentum dependence on this variable for pions, kaons, and protons across all energies and centralities. The analysis was extended to the slopes of the average transverse momentum versus particle mass and to the Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave fit parameter beta_T.

## Key numbers

- The slope of the average transverse momentum dependence on sqrt((dN/dy)/S_perp_geom) for pions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 0.08 +/- 0.02, decreasing to 0.03 +/- 0.01 at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 0.05 +/- 0.01 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The slope for protons at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 0.35 +/- 0.07, decreasing to 0.18 +/- 0.02 at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 0.19 +/- 0.02 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The offset for pions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 0.25 +/- 0.03, increasing to 0.39 +/- 0.03 at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 0.39 +/- 0.04 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The offset for protons at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 0.18 +/- 0.10, increasing to 0.63 +/- 0.05 at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 0.63 +/- 0.06 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The hadron density dN/dy for the most central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 476.7 +/- 22.5, increasing to 1335.8 +/- 57.1 at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The hadron density dN/dy for the most central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV is 2837.0 +/- 144.0, increasing to 3320.6 +/- 131.4 at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- The geometrical overlapping area S_perp_geom for the most central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV is 146.1 +/- 0.7 fm^2, and for Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV is 170.2 +/- 0.7 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the study of geometrical scaling in heavy ion collisions from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (7.7 to 39 GeV) up to the highest LHC energies (5.02 TeV), building on the local parton-hadron duality picture of Levin and Lapidus (various years) and the Color Glass Condensate framework of McLerran, Iancu, and others (various years). The results enable systematic comparison of the scaling variable sqrt((dN/dy)/S_perp) across a factor of approximately 650 in collision energy, and the observed similarities between pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 and 5.02 TeV point to a system size independent behavior that can inform future hydrodynamic and saturation model calculations.
