# Influence of effective interactions and nuclear densities on the dynamics of heavy-ion fusion

> The study aims to explore the mechanism of heavy-ion fusion using various effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and nuclear density distributions. The nuclear potentials are obtained by folding the relativistic effective NN interaction (R3Y) with densities derived from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach. The RMF formalism with the NL3$^*$ parameter set and the relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with the DDME2 parameter set are used to obtain the medium-independent R3Y and density-dependent R3Y (DDR3Y) NN potentials. The results of these relativistic interactions are compared with the Reid and Paris M3Y NN interactions and their density-dependent versions. Nuclear potentials are used to calculate the fusion barrier characteristics and cross-sections within the $\ell$-summed Wong model for $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm, $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm, and $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U reactions. The relativistic R3Y and DDR3Y NN potentials provide higher cross-sections than both the Reid and Paris versions. The inclusion of in-medium effects in all interactions results in more repulsive potentials, leading to higher fusion barriers and reduced cross-sections. The impact of nuclear shape degrees of freedom is included for $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm and $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U reactions involving deformed targets. Results using RMF-NL3$^*$ densities are also compared with those from the two-parameter Fermi (2pF) formula for $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm, showing higher cross-sections with RMF densities. A comparison of calculated cross-sections with experimental data shows that the R3Y NN potential with RMF-NL3$^*$ densities provides good agreement for reactions involving both spherical and deformed nuclei.

# Influence of effective interactions and nuclear densities on the dynamics of heavy-ion fusion

## Key claim

The relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential combined with nuclear densities from the relativistic mean-field formalism using the NL3* parameter set provides the best agreement with experimental fusion cross-section data for reactions involving both spherical and deformed target nuclei, but only when the in-medium effects are excluded from the relativistic interaction; the inclusion of density dependence in any interaction (relativistic or non-relativistic) produces more repulsive potentials that hinder fusion.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear interaction potentials using the double-folding model, folding six different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions (relativistic R3Y and DDR3Y; non-relativistic Reid and Paris M3Y; and their density-dependent versions RDDM3Y and PDDM3Y) with nuclear densities from the RMF-NL3* and RHB-DDME2 approaches. Fusion barrier characteristics and cross-sections were computed within the ℓ-summed Wong model using the Hill-Wheeler parabolic barrier approximation for four reactions: ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm, ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb, ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm, and ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U, with target deformation included for the two reactions involving deformed targets.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm reaction, the R3Y-NL3* nuclear potential provided the most attractive potential and lowest fusion barrier among all six NN interactions tested.
- The Paris M3Y NN potential uses a one-pion exchange potential value of J₀₀ = -592 MeV fm³, while the Reid M3Y uses J₀₀ = -276 MeV fm³.
- The BDM3Y1 version of the Reid density-dependent M3Y interaction has nuclear matter incompressibility K = 232 MeV; the CDM3Y5 version of the Paris density-dependent M3Y interaction has K = 241 MeV.
- The highest fusion barriers were found for PDDM3Y-NL3* in the ¹⁶O+¹⁴⁴Sm and ¹⁶O+¹⁵⁴Sm reactions, and for RDDM3Y-NL3* in the ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb and ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U reactions.
- Cross-sections calculated with different nuclear potentials overlapped at above-barrier energies due to suppression of nuclear structure effects and dominance of angular momentum effects.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach to heavy-ion fusion previously developed by Bhuyan et al. (2018, 2020) for Ni-based and superheavy systems, which had used only spherically symmetric densities. It addresses concerns raised by Chushnyakova et al. (2022) about the use of spherical densities for deformed targets in those earlier studies. The present paper systematically compares relativistic R3Y and DDR3Y interactions against the well-established Reid and Paris M3Y interactions from Bertsch et al. (1977) and their density-dependent versions from Khoa et al. (1995, 1997), enabling future work to select the optimal combination of effective interaction and density model for predicting fusion cross-sections in superheavy element synthesis.
