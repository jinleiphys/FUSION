# Non-Hermitian $p$-wave superfluid and effects of the inelastic three-body loss in a one-dimensional spin-polarized Fermi gas

> We theoretically investigate non-Hermitian $p$-wave Fermi superfluidity in one-dimensional spin-polarized Fermi gases which is relevant to recent ultracold atomic experiments. Considering an imaginary atom-dimer coupling responsible for the three-body recombination process in the Lindblad formalism, we discuss the stability of the superfluid state against the atomic loss effect. Within the two-channel non-Hermitian BCS-Leggett theory, the atomic loss is characterized by the product of the imaginary atom-dimer coupling and the $p$-wave effective range. Our results indicate that for a given imaginary atom-dimer coupling, a smaller magnitude of the effective ranges of $p$-wave interaction is crucial for reaching the non-Hermitian $p$-wave Fermi superfluid state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a non-Hermitian $p$-wave Fermi superfluid state can survive under three-body atomic loss in one-dimensional spin-polarized Fermi gases, but only when the magnitude of the $p$-wave effective range is sufficiently small; the abstract's claim that "a smaller magnitude of the effective ranges is crucial" hedges that the superfluid solution disappears entirely at larger effective ranges for the experimentally relevant loss rate.

## Method

The authors develop a non-Hermitian BCS-Leggett mean-field theory starting from a two-channel Hamiltonian for a one-dimensional spin-polarized Fermi gas near a $p$-wave Feshbach resonance, incorporating an imaginary atom-dimer coupling $g_3$ to represent inelastic three-body recombination via the Lindblad formalism. They solve the complex gap equation and number equation self-consistently to obtain the ground-state energy and free energy, then map the phase diagram by locating where the superfluid solution vanishes at the exceptional point (for positive real chemical potential) or at the diffusive gapless mode (for negative real chemical potential). The imaginary coupling is related to the experimentally measured atom-dimer collision rate $K_{\rm ad}$.

## Key numbers

- The normalized imaginary three-body coupling estimated from the experiment of PhysRevLett.125.263402 is $\gamma_3 = 1.5 \times 10^{2}$, where $\gamma_3 = -\mathrm{Im}[g_3] n_{\rm a}/E_{\rm F}$.
- The experimental atom-dimer collision rate is $K_{\rm ad} \simeq 0.67\ \mathrm{cm/s}$, with system length $\ell \simeq 2 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{cm}$, total atom number $N_{\rm a} \simeq 9 \times 10^{4}$, and Fermi energy $E_{\rm F} \simeq 4.8\ \mu\mathrm{K}$.
- The quasi-one-dimensional effective range estimated from the same experiment is $r \simeq -4.3\ \mu\mathrm{m}$, giving $k_{\rm F}r \simeq -47$, which lies far outside the superfluid region in the phase diagram.
- The topological phase transition from BCS to BEC in the zero-range limit occurs at $(k_{\rm F}a)^{-1} = 4/\pi \simeq 1.27$.
- For $(k_{\rm F}a)^{-1} = -1$, the inverse imaginary effective scattering length is approximately $(k_{\rm F}a_{\rm eff,I})^{-1} \simeq 0.9$.

## Context

This work extends the non-Hermitian BCS-Leggett theory previously applied to one-dimensional two-component $p$-wave Fermi superfluids with two-body loss (PhysRevA.107.033331) to the case of three-body loss in spin-polarized Fermi gases, which has not been treated before. The formalism connects microscopically to the two-step cascade model used to analyze $p$-wave loss experiments (PhysRevLett.120.193402, PhysRevLett.125.263402). The results suggest that reducing the $p$-wave effective range, for example via optical control methods (Haibin 2012, Arunkmar 2019) or strong transverse confinement (PhysRevLett.100.170404, PhysRevLett.112.250401), could enable realization of the $p$-wave superfluid state under postselected quantum trajectories.
