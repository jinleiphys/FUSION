# Probing CP Violation with the Deuteron Electric Dipole Moment

> We present an analysis of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the deuteron as induced by CP-violating operators of dimension 4, 5 and 6 including theta QCD, the EDMs and color EDMs of quarks, four-quark interactions and the Weinberg operator. We demonstrate that the precision goal of the EDM Collaboration's proposal to search for the deuteron EDM, (1-3)\times 10^{-27} e cm, will provide an improvement in sensitivity to these sources of one-two orders of magnitude relative to the existing bounds. We consider in detail the level to which CP-odd phases can be probed within the MSSM.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a proposed deuteron EDM measurement with sensitivity of (1-3)×10⁻²⁷ e cm would improve sensitivity to CP-violating sources (θ̅ QCD, quark EDMs and color EDMs, four-quark operators, and the Weinberg operator) by one to two orders of magnitude relative to existing bounds, though the theoretical uncertainties in the QCD sum-rule calculations are substantial (30-40% for the θ̅ contribution and with non-Gaussian errors for the CEDM contribution).

## Method

The authors calculate the deuteron EDM as the sum of the neutron and proton EDMs plus a meson-exchange contribution dominated by the isospin-triplet CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling g̅⁽¹⁾_πNN. They use QCD sum-rule techniques to relate these hadronic quantities to underlying CP-violating operators at 1 GeV, including the θ-term, quark EDMs and color EDMs, and the Weinberg operator. The analysis then maps these operators onto the MSSM with universal boundary conditions, computing the deuteron EDM as a function of SUSY parameters and comparing its sensitivity to that of neutron, mercury, and thallium EDMs.

## Key numbers

- Projected experimental sensitivity: |d_D| < (1-3)×10⁻²⁷ e cm
- Deuteron EDM from θ̅: d_D(θ̅) ≈ -e [(3.5 ± 1.4) + (1.4 ± 0.4)]×10⁻³ θ̅ GeV⁻¹, implying a null result would give |θ̅| < 3×10⁻¹¹
- Deuteron EDM from CEDMs: d_D(d_q, d̃_q) ≈ -e (d̃_u-d̃_d)[5_{-3}^{+11} + (0.6 ± 0.3)] - (0.2 ± 0.1)e(d̃_u+d̃_d) + (0.5 ± 0.3)(d_u+d_d)
- The meson-exchange contribution: d_D^πNN ≈ -(1.3 ± 0.3) e g̅⁽¹⁾_πNN GeV⁻¹
- The deuteron binding energy used: ε = 2.23 MeV
- For the MSSM with θ_A=0, θ_μ=π/10, the projected deuteron EDM sensitivity extends to squark masses beyond 10 TeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadronic EDMs from Crewther et al. (1979) and the QCD sum-rule approach of Pospelov and Ritz (2001) to the deuteron, complementing existing constraints from neutron EDM experiments and mercury EDM measurements (Khriplovich and Korkin, 2000). The deuteron offers a significant advantage over mercury due to more transparent nuclear physics and smaller theoretical uncertainties. The projected sensitivity would probe the "decoupling" solution to the SUSY CP problem (Nath, 1991) and test CP-odd phases in the MSSM at the level of 10⁻³ or below, enabling discrimination between models with and without Peccei-Quinn symmetry.
