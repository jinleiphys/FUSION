# K^- p scattering length from scattering experiments

> The strong K^- p scattering length is extracted within chiral SU(3) unitary approaches from a very large variety of fits to low-energy K^- p scattering data. Very good overall agreement with available scattering data is obtained and the resulting scattering length is compared with the new accurate kaonic hydrogen data from DEAR. The pole structures of the obtained fits to experiment are critically examined.

## Key claim

The strong K^- p scattering length extracted from scattering data alone, using chiral SU(3) unitary approaches, is inconsistent with the DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurement at the 1-sigma confidence level, regardless of which variant of the chiral unitary approach is used; fits compatible with DEAR require a chi-squared per degree of freedom of at least 3.3 in the most sophisticated approach.

## Method

The authors performed a large number of least-squares fits to low-energy K^- p scattering data, threshold branching ratios, and the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum, using three variants of chiral SU(3) unitary coupled-channel approaches: one with only the Weinberg-Tomozawa contact term (WT), one adding Born diagrams (WTB), and one including also next-to-leading order contact interactions (full). The fits employed a Monte Carlo routine combined with a conjugate gradient method, varying up to 14 parameters (subtraction constants and low-energy constants) within generous ranges, and the resulting T-matrix poles in the Lambda(1405) region were critically examined to reject unphysical solutions.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit K^- p scattering lengths: WT approach a = (-0.73 + i 0.91) fm; WTB approach a = (-1.09 + i 0.84) fm; full approach a = (-1.05 + i 0.75) fm
- Best-fit isospin scattering lengths in the full approach: a_0 = (-1.64 + i 0.75) fm, a_1 = (-0.06 + i 0.57) fm
- Minimum chi-squared per degree of freedom: 1.28 (WT), 0.88 (WTB), 0.71 (full)
- Fits compatible with DEAR error ranges have chi-squared/d.o.f. of at least 6.1 (WT), 5.5 (WTB), and 3.3 (full)
- The 1-sigma confidence region is defined by adding Delta-chi-squared/d.o.f. = 1.05 to the minimum value, based on 171 data points

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Borasoy, Meissner, and Nissler (2005) by performing a much larger statistical exploration of parameter space to provide a realistic estimate of theoretical uncertainties. The results contradict claims by Oller and collaborators (2005, 2006) that both scattering data and DEAR data can be accommodated within a chiral unitary approach, and disagree with the isospin scattering lengths from Oset and Ramos (1998) and Oset, Magas, and Ramos (2002). The extracted isospin scattering lengths provide essential input for the upcoming spectroscopy study of kaonic deuterium at DAΦNE and for anticipated experiments with more complex light kaonic nuclei.
