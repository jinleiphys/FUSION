# Two-pion exchange and strong form-factors in covariant field theories

> In this work improvements to the application of the Gross equation to nuclear systems are tested. In particular we evaluate the two pion exchange diagrams, including the crossed-box diagram, using models developed within the spectator-on-mass-shell covariant formalism. We found that the form factors used in these models induce spurious contributions that violate the unitary cut requirement. We tested then some alternative form-factors in order to preserve the unitarity condition. With this new choice, the difference between the exact and the spectator-on-mass-shell amplitudes is of the order of the one boson scalar exchange, supporting the idea that this difference may be parameterized by this type of terms.

## Key claim

The authors find that the strong form-factors used in existing spectator-on-mass-shell (Gross equation) models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction introduce spurious complex singularities that violate the unitary cut requirement when the full Bethe-Salpeter box and crossed-box diagrams are evaluated, but that alternative form-factors depending only on the three-momentum (via a Lorentz invariant) can restore unitarity while keeping the difference between the exact and spectator amplitudes of the order of a one-boson scalar exchange term.

## Method

The authors analytically and numerically evaluated the fourth-order box and crossed-box two-pion exchange amplitudes for two spin-1/2 Dirac particles of equal mass, using the full Bethe-Salpeter equation with scalar and pseudoscalar/pseudovector couplings. They decomposed the amplitudes into contributions from nucleon poles (positive and negative energy) and meson poles, and tested the effect of different strong form-factor parameterizations, specifically the conventional form-factors from Gross and Riska (1987) and Stadler (1996) versus new form-factors that depend only on the Lorentz-invariant three-momentum squared, on the unitarity of the resulting amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For the scalar test case (scalar particles, no form-factors), parameters used were \(g^2/(4\pi M^2)=0.5\) and \(\mu=M/7\).
- For the scalar meson exchange between nucleons, the sigma meson parameters were: \(g_\sigma^2/(4\pi)=4.82\), \(\mu=m_\sigma=498\) MeV, \(\Lambda_\sigma=\Lambda_m=1141\) MeV, and \(\Lambda_N=1862\) MeV.
- Results were computed for laboratory kinetic energies \(T_{lab}=100\), 200, and 300 MeV.
- The new form-factor of Eq. (fPI3) yields results differing by at most 10% from the version without the retardation-like term, except where the amplitude vanishes in the \(I=1\) channel.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Gross (spectator) equation beyond the static limit and scalar-particle case studied by Gross (1969) and Maung and Gross (1990), and beyond the single-energy evaluation by Tjon (1982). It directly tests the validity of the spectator approximation for Dirac particles exchanging pions with pseudovector coupling, as used in recent three-nucleon calculations by Stadler (1996) and Gross (1992). The results enable systematic improvement of the Gross equation kernel by parameterizing the difference between exact and spectator amplitudes as an effective one-boson scalar exchange term, and provide a prescription for form-factors that satisfy unitarity in the full Bethe-Salpeter framework.
