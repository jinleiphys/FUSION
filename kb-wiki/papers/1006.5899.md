# Squeezed K^+ K^- correlations in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions may modify some hadronic properties. In particular, if hadron masses are shifted in-medium, it was demonstrated that this could lead to back-to-back squeezed correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs. Although well-established theoretically, the squeezed correlations have not yet been discovered experimentally. A method has been suggested for the empirical search of this effect, which was previously illustrated for phi-phi pairs. We apply here the formalism and the suggested method to the case of K^+ K^- pairs, since they may be easier to identify experimentally. The time distribution of the emission process plays a crucial role in the survival of the BBC's. We analyze the cases where the emission is supposed to occur suddenly or via a Lorentzian distribution, and compare with the case of a Levy distribution in time. Effects of squeezing on the correlation function of identical particles are also analyzed.

# Squeezed K⁺K⁻ Correlations in High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Back-to-back squeezed correlations (BBC) of K⁺K⁻ pairs, arising from in-medium mass shifts, may be experimentally observable if the particle emission process is sufficiently short (Δt ≤ 1 fm/c) and if the time distribution is not too broad; however, the signal strength depends critically on the functional form of the emission time distribution, with Lévy distributions causing more dramatic suppression than Lorentzian distributions.

## Method

The authors apply a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation to relate asymptotic creation/annihilation operators to in-medium quasi-particle operators, deriving analytical expressions for the squeezed correlation function C_s(k₁,k₂) for K⁺K⁻ pairs in a non-relativistic framework with a finite-size, radially expanding system. They compute the correlation for exactly back-to-back pairs as a function of shifted mass m* and momentum |k|, then extend the analysis to the (K₁₂, q₁₂)-plane using average and relative momentum variables, comparing instantaneous emission with Lorentzian and symmetric α-stable Lévy time distributions.

## Key numbers

- Kaon asymptotic mass: m_K± ≈ 494 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T = 177 MeV (from PHENIX experimental fits)
- Radial flow parameter values considered: ⟨u⟩ = 0 and ⟨u⟩ = 0.5
- Squeezing region radius: R = 7 fm
- For Lorentzian distribution with Δt = 2 fm/c, the BBC strength decreases by approximately three orders of magnitude compared to instantaneous emission
- For Lévy distribution with α = 1 and Δt = 1 fm/c, the signal remains potentially measurable; for α = 1.35 with Δt = 2 fm/c, the signal reduces essentially to unity (C(k,-k) - 1 becomes too small for axis scale precision)
- Lévy distribution parameters from PHENIX: α ≈ 1 for 0.2 < m_T < 0.3 GeV, α = 1.35 for 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c
- The squeezed correlation shows maximum intensity for shifted masses m* = 350 MeV and m* = 650 MeV (symmetrically below and above the asymptotic mass)
- Signal expected in momentum region: 0 ≤ 2|K| ≤ 60-150 MeV/c and 500 ≤ |q| ≤ 2000 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for squeezed back-to-back correlations originally proposed by Asakawa et al. (1999) for boson-antiboson pairs, and previously applied to φφ pairs by the same authors. It builds on earlier studies of static infinite media (Asakawa et al., 1999; Panda et al., 2001) and finite-size expanding systems (Padula et al., 2005). The paper specifically addresses K⁺K⁻ pairs because they may be easier to identify experimentally than φφ pairs, and introduces Lévy time distributions motivated by PHENIX Collaboration analyses of Bose-Einstein correlations. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC and LHC, providing specific predictions for the correlation function's behavior in the (K₁₂, q₁₂)-plane and identifying conditions under which the signal might survive finite emission durations.
