# Global analysis of parity-violating asymmetry data for elastic electron scattering

> We perform a statistical analysis of the full set of parity-violating asymmetry data for elastic electron scattering including the most recent high precision measurement from $Q$-weak. Given the basis of the present analysis, our estimates appear to favor non-zero vector strangeness, specifically, positive (negative) values for the electric (magnetic) strange form factors. We also provide an accurate estimate of the axial-vector nucleon form factor at zero momentum transfer, $G_A^{ep}(0)$. Our study shows $G_A^{ep}(0)$ to be importantly reduced with respect to the currently accepted value. We also find our analysis of data to be compatible with the Standard Model values for the weak charges of the proton and neutron.

## Key claim

The global fit of all elastic parity-violating asymmetry data, including the Q-weak measurement, favors non-zero vector strangeness (positive electric and negative magnetic strange form factors) and yields a value for the axial-vector form factor at zero momentum transfer that is significantly reduced relative to the currently accepted value, though both results carry large uncertainties that overlap with zero or the accepted value.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared minimization using all 31 elastic parity-violating asymmetry data points from the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, PVA4, G0, and Q-weak experiments on hydrogen and helium-4 targets, plus an additional constraint on the axial-vector form factor at Q^2=0. They fitted five free parameters simultaneously: the weak neutral current vector couplings for the proton (xi_V^p) and neutron (xi_V^n), the electric and magnetic strange form factor parameters (rho_s and mu_s), and the axial-vector form factor at zero momentum transfer (G_A^ep(0)). The electromagnetic form factors were taken from the GKex prescription, and dipole Q^2-dependences were assumed for the strange and axial form factors.

## Key numbers

- Proton weak charge: xi_V^p = 0.070 +/- 0.013 (from the global fit)
- Neutron weak charge: xi_V^n = -0.946 +/- 0.044 (from the global fit)
- Axial-vector form factor at Q^2=0: G_A^ep(0) = -0.62 +/- 0.41, compared to the previously accepted value of -1.04 +/- 0.44 from Liu et al. (2007)
- Electric strange form factor parameter: rho_s = 0.92 +/- 0.58
- Magnetic strange form factor parameter: mu_s = -0.26 +/- 0.26
- Reduced chi-squared: chi^2_MIN/27 = 1.30
- Combined analysis with atomic parity violation in cesium yields: xi_V^p = 0.070 +/- 0.013 and xi_V^n = -0.978 +/- 0.012

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of Young et al. (2006, 2007) and Liu et al. (2007) by including all available elastic data without restriction on Q^2 range, and by fitting five parameters simultaneously rather than a reduced set. The authors note that previous analyses using data only up to |Q^2|=0.6 (GeV/c)^2 (Young et al. 2006) or 0.3 (GeV/c)^2, with particular assumptions for form factor Q^2-expansion, motivated this more complete treatment. The results are compatible with the Standard Model predictions for the weak charges of the proton and neutron from the Particle Data Group (2012), and the combined analysis with atomic parity violation in cesium (Wood et al. 1997) provides tighter constraints on the weak neutral current quark couplings. The reduced value of G_A^ep(0) may indicate important radiative correction effects or alternative Q^2-dependences for the axial form factor, and the authors caution that the Q-weak analysis used only 4% of the full dataset.
