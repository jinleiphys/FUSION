# Improved Coupled Channels and R-Matrix Models: Predictions to 1 Gev

> The $NN$ scattering near inelastic threshold is sensitive to the long-range diagonal interaction in the produced isobar channel. Earlier models included meson exchange potentials in the $NN$ sector and connecting that sector to isobar channels, as well as an $R$-matrix description of short-range quark effects. Including the diagonal pion exchange contributions in the $NΔ$ channels coupled to the very inelastic ${}^1D_2$ and ${}^3F_3$ $NN$ channels substantially improves those phase parameters and the observables $Δσ_{L}$ and $Δσ_{T}$. The same improvement is made to the other $I=1$ $NN$ channels. In the ${}^1S_0$ $NN$ channel coupling to the $ΔΔ$ channel is unusually important due to the $L=2$ angular momentum barrier in the $NΔ$ channel. The pion exchange transition potentials between isobar channels are included in this partial wave to obtain the correct equilibrium between one- and two-pion decay channels. Other improvements to earlier models have been made, in the specification of isobar channels and the inclusion of decay width effects in more channels. A comparison is made with all the $pp$ data for $T_L \le 800$~MeV, producing a very good fit over the whole energy range.

## Key claim

Including the diagonal one-pion exchange potential in the \(N\Delta\) channels, which was previously omitted and represented only by the short-range boundary condition, substantially improves the phase parameters and observables \(\Delta\sigma_L\) and \(\Delta\sigma_T\) for the very inelastic \(^1D_2\) and \(^3F_3\) \(NN\) channels, though the \(\Delta\sigma_T\) peak position remains somewhat shifted due to a slightly low minimum value of \(\eta(^1D_2)\).

## Method

The authors extend a coupled-channel \(R\)-matrix model that uses meson exchange potentials (including \(\pi,\eta,\rho,\omega\) and two-pion exchange) for the long-range interaction outside a boundary radius \(r_0 = 1.05\) fm, with a meromorphic boundary condition at \(r_0\) representing short-range quark effects from the Cloudy Bag Model. They add diagonal one-pion exchange potentials in the \(N\Delta\) channels and one-pion exchange transition potentials between isobar channels (e.g., \(N\Delta\) to \(\Delta\Delta\)), discretize the Breit-Wigner mass distribution of isobars to include decay width effects, and treat previously combined isobar channels separately. The model is fitted to all \(pp\) data for laboratory kinetic energy \(T_L \le 800\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- \(g^2_{NN\pi}/4\pi = 14.40\) (replacing the earlier value 14.94 from Ref. [5]), used uniformly across all partial waves.
- \(r_0 = 1.05\) fm for all partial waves, determined from the Cloudy Bag Model and consistent with the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) states.
- \(f^2_{N\Delta\pi}/4\pi = 0.35\) (from the \(\Delta\) decay width), \(f^2_{NN^*\pi}/4\pi = 0.015\) (from the \(N^*(1440)\) decay), and \(f^2_{\Delta\Delta\pi}/4\pi = 0.003\) (from the quark model).
- For the \(^1S_0\) system, the pole of the \(0^+\) six-quark configuration is at \(W_p = 2.71\) GeV/\(c^2\) (corresponding to \(T_L = 2.04\) GeV), with residues \(\rho_{NN,NN} = 0.149\) GeV, \(\rho_{NN,\Delta\Delta0} = 0.133\) GeV, and \(\rho_{\Delta\Delta0,\Delta\Delta0} = 0.118718\) GeV.
- The singlet scattering length is \(a^{pp}_s = -7.82\) fm.
- For the \(^1S_0\) channel at \(T_L = 800\) MeV, the contribution to \(\sigma(pp \to pp\pi^+\pi^-)\) is about one-third of the experimental total value of 3 \(\mu\)b.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel \(R\)-matrix models of Lomon (Refs. [1-3,8]) by adding diagonal pion exchange in \(N\Delta\) channels and transition potentials between isobar channels, which were previously omitted or approximated by the boundary condition. It corrects earlier difficulties in the \(^1S_0\) channel where excessive inelasticity near 800 MeV (predicting \(\eta = 0.84\) versus the phase shift analysis value of 0.98) could not be resolved without producing too much two-pion production. The improved model enables better predictions for \(pp\) spin observables and total cross sections up to 1 GeV, and provides a framework for future calculations of three-body final state distributions.
