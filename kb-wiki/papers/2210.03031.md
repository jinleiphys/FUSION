# Microscopic nucleus-nucleus optical potentials from nuclear matter with uncertainty analysis from chiral forces

> Nucleus-nucleus optical potentials are constructed from an energy density functional approach first outlined by Brueckner et al. The interaction term of the energy density functional comes from the complex nucleon self-energy computed in nuclear matter with two- and three-body chiral nuclear forces. Nuclear density distributions are calculated from Skyrme functionals constrained to the equations of state calculated from the same chiral forces used for the self-energy. Predictions for elastic scattering cross sections and fusion cross sections are compared to experimental data. Very good agreement is found with experiment for elastic scattering of heavier nucleus-nucleus systems at energies in the range of $20 < E < 90$ MeV/N, while accurate descriptions of lighter and lower-energy systems may require the inclusion of collective excitations.

## Key claim

Nucleus-nucleus optical potentials constructed from an energy density functional using chiral nuclear forces achieve very good agreement with experimental elastic scattering data for heavier systems at energies between 20 and 90 MeV/N, but accurate descriptions of lighter and lower-energy systems may require the inclusion of collective excitations.

## Method

The nucleus-nucleus optical potential is constructed by integrating the energy density of the combined system minus the energy densities of each isolated nucleus over space for a given separation distance, using the frozen density approximation where the two nuclear densities simply add together. The interaction term of the energy density functional comes from the complex nucleon self-energy computed in nuclear matter to second order in many-body perturbation theory using two- and three-body chiral nuclear forces at N³LO and N²LO respectively. Nuclear density distributions are calculated from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock functionals constrained to equations of state from the same chiral forces, and three chiral interactions with momentum space cutoffs of Λ = 414, 450, and 500 MeV are employed to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering cross sections are compared for systems ranging from ¹⁶O+²⁸Si to ⁸⁶Kr+²⁰⁸Pb at projectile energies from E_Lab = 6 MeV/N to E_Lab = 94 MeV/N
- Very good agreement with experiment is found for energies in the range 20 < E < 90 MeV/N
- Fusion cross sections are calculated for energies of approximately 2-3 MeV/N
- Three chiral interactions with momentum space cutoffs of Λ = 414, 450, and 500 MeV are used for uncertainty estimation

## Context

This work extends the energy density functional approach first outlined by Brueckner et al. (1968) and later generalized to include imaginary terms by Gupta (1978) and others, using modern chiral effective field theory forces with quantified uncertainties. It contrasts with double-folding approaches such as those by Satchler (1979), Durant et al. (2018, 2020, 2022), and Minomo et al. (2014, 2016), where the latter showed the importance of including collective excitations in coupled-channels calculations. The results may be combined with the WLH global nucleon-nucleus optical potential (Whitehead, 2021) for consistent microscopic treatment of effective interactions in few-body reaction models, and future work should address Pauli blocking between the two nuclei and inclusion of surface excitations.
