# Pion TMDs in light-front constituent approach, and Boer-Mulders effect in the pion-induced Drell-Yan process

> At leading twist the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions of the pion consist of two functions, the unpolarized $f_{1,π}(x,\boldsymbol k^{\, 2}_\perp)$ and the Boer-Mulders function $h_{1,π}^{\perp}(x,\boldsymbol k^{\, 2}_\perp)$. We study both functions within a light-front constituent model of the pion, comparing the results with different pion models and the corresponding nucleon distributions from a light-front constituent model. After evolution from the model scale to the relevant experimental scales, the results for the collinear pion valence parton distribution function $f_{1,π}(x)$ are in very good agreement with available parameterizations. Using the light-front constituent model results for the Boer-Mulders functions of the pion and nucleon, we calculate the coefficient $ν$ in the angular distribution of Drell-Yan dileptons produced in pion-nucleus scattering, which is responsible for the violation of the Lam-Tung relation. We find a good agreement with data, and carefully discuss the range of applicability of our approach.

## Key claim

The light-front constituent model (LFCM) for the pion, when combined with the nucleon Boer-Mulders function from the same LFCM approach, reproduces the Drell-Yan data on the coefficient ν that quantifies violation of the Lam-Tung relation, but only within a limited range of applicability that the authors carefully discuss.

## Method

The authors used a light-front constituent model with a minimal qq̅ Fock-space configuration for the pion, employing an exponential momentum-space wave function with parameters fitted to the pion charge radius and decay constant. They computed the unpolarized TMD f1,π and the Boer-Mulders function h1,π⊥ via overlap of light-front amplitudes, including the one-gluon exchange mechanism for the T-odd function, then evolved the distributions from the model scale to experimental scales using LO and NLO QCD evolution.

## Key numbers

- The initial scale for the pion LFCM was determined as μ0,LO = 460 MeV (αLO(μ0²)/4π = 0.225) and μ0,NLO = 555 MeV (αNLO(μ0²)/4π = 0.0938)
- The valence quark momentum fraction at Q² = 4 GeV² is ⟨x⟩v = 0.47 ± 0.02, used to fix the initial scale
- The quark mass parameter is m = 0.250 GeV and the wave function parameter β = 0.3194 GeV, fitted to the pion charge radius and decay constant
- For the nucleon case, the initial scales were μ0,LO = 420 MeV and μ0,NLO = 508 MeV, numerically close to the pion values

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent model approach, previously applied to nucleon TMDs by Pasquini and collaborators (2008-2011), to the pion for the first time. The results for the pion Boer-Mulders function are compared with previous spectator and bag model calculations by Lu and collaborators (2004-2012) and Burkardt (2007), as well as lattice QCD results by Engelhardt (2013). The calculation of the ν coefficient in pion-nucleus Drell-Yan provides a nonperturbative mechanism within TMD factorization for the violation of the Lam-Tung relation observed by Falciano and Guanziroli (1986-1987) and Conway (1989), enabling further tests of TMD evolution and factorization in meson-induced processes.
