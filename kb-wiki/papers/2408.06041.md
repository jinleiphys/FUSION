# Logic and numbers related to solar neutrinos

> In this work, first of all, we analyze a number of hidden aspects of the concept of particle oscillations. The key element of this concept, which do not comply with the principle of least action, is the notion of a mixture of particles, introduced by Gell-Mann and Pais for neutral $K$-mesons. It has been proven that the law of conservation of energy-momentum in the processes of electron neutrino production does not allow solving the problem of solar neutrinos based on the assumption of Gribov and Pontecorvo about their oscillations. It has been established that the consequences of Wolfenstein's equation contradict the results of the SNO and Super-Kamiokande collaborations and that the assertion by Mikheev and Smirnov on the conversion of solar neutrinos is erroneous. Another part of the work is devoted to a logically clear solution to this problem based on the hypothesis of the existence of a new interaction, the carrier of which is a massless pseudoscalar boson, which has a Yukawa coupling with electron neutrinos and nucleons. At each act of interaction of an electron neutrino with the nucleons of the Sun, caused by such interaction, the handedness of the neutrino changes from left to right and vice versa, and also the neutrino energy decreases. The hypothesis provides good agreement between the theoretical and experimental values of the rates of all five observed processes with solar neutrinos. This serves as a significant criterion for both the confidence of such a solution to the problem of solar neutrinos and the confidence of the existence of a new interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the solar neutrino problem cannot be solved by neutrino oscillations because the law of conservation of energy-momentum in electron neutrino production processes forbids the required mass-squared difference, and that Wolfenstein's equation contradicts experimental results from SNO and Super-Kamiokande; instead, the author proposes a new interaction mediated by a massless pseudoscalar boson with Yukawa coupling to electron neutrinos and nucleons, which changes neutrino handedness and reduces energy at each scattering.

## Method

The author analyzes the Gell-Mann-Pais concept of particle mixtures and its application to neutrino oscillations by Gribov and Pontecorvo, deriving an inequality from energy-momentum conservation in neutron decay that limits the mass-squared difference of neutrino components. The consequences of Wolfenstein's equation for solar neutrinos are numerically evaluated using the adiabatic approximation with oscillation parameters from Super-Kamiokande, and the resulting survival probability is compared to experimental rates for five solar neutrino processes. The author then constructs a model based on a new interaction Lagrangian with a massless pseudoscalar boson coupled to electron neutrinos and nucleons, calculating the product of Yukawa coupling constants and describing the kinematics of neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The upper limit on the mass-squared difference from energy-momentum conservation in neutron decay is \(|m_{1}^{2}-m_{2}^{2}| < 5.9 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{eV}^{2}\), derived from the neutron width \(\Gamma = 7.5 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{eV}\) and maximum neutrino momentum \((p_{\nu})_{\text{max}} = 0.782 \, \text{MeV}\).
- The SNO and Super-Kamiokande collaborations claim \(|m_{1}^{2}-m_{2}^{2}|\) of order \(10^{-5} \, \text{eV}^{2}\), which the author argues violates the energy-momentum conservation bound.
- For the \(^{37}\text{Cl} \rightarrow ^{37}\text{Ar}\) transition, the ratio of experimental to theoretical rate is \((2.56 \pm 0.24)/(7.9 \pm 2.6) = 0.32 \pm 0.14\).
- For elastic scattering \(\nu_{e} + e^{-} \rightarrow \nu_{e} + e^{-}\), the ratio is \((2.32 \pm 0.07)/(5.79 \times (1 \pm 0.23)) = 0.40 \pm 0.10\).
- For deuteron disintegration by charged currents \(\nu_{e} + D \rightarrow e^{-} + p + p\), the ratio is \((1.76 \pm 0.11)/(5.79 \times (1 \pm 0.23)) = 0.30 \pm 0.09\).
- For the \(^{71}\text{Ga} \rightarrow ^{71}\text{Ge}\) transition, the ratio is \((65.4 \pm 2.9)/(131 \pm 10) = 0.50 \pm 0.06\).
- The product of Yukawa coupling constants for the new interaction is \(g_{\nu_{e}ps} g_{Nps} / 4\pi = (3.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-5}\).
- The relative change in matter density over one oscillation length does not exceed \(1.7 \times 10^{-3}\) for the oscillation parameters \(\Delta m^{2} = 4.8 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{eV}^{2}\) and \(\sin^{2} \theta = 0.334\).
- The ratio of left-handed to right-handed electron neutrino fluxes near Earth is estimated as 0.516:0.484.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior publication (Slad, 2023) proposing a new interaction to solve the solar neutrino problem, and directly contradicts the standard oscillation framework established by Gribov and Pontecorvo (1969), Wolfenstein (1978), and Mikheev and Smirnov (1985). The paper argues that the SNO and Super-Kamiokande collaborations (2001-2008) have not provided analytical formulas connecting their event rates to oscillation parameters, and that the Mikheev-Smirnov conversion scenario is based on an arbitrary choice of mixing angle evolution. The proposed massless pseudoscalar boson interaction is distinguished from the Peccei-Quinn axion (1977) and is not coupled to electrons at tree level, enabling future experimental searches for the boson via its conversion to gamma quanta in matter.
