# Nuclear Isospin Diffusivity

> The isospin diffusion and other irreversible phenomena are discussed for a two-component nuclear Fermi system. The set of Boltzmann transport equations, such as employed for reactions, are linearized, for weak deviations of a system from uniformity, in order to arrive at nonreversible fluxes linear in the nonuniformities. Besides the diffusion driven by a concentration gradient, also the diffusion driven by temperature and pressure gradients is considered. Diffusivity, conductivity, heat conduction and shear viscosity coefficients are formally expressed in terms of the responses of distribution functions to the nonuniformities. The linearized Boltzmann-equation set is solved, under the approximation of constant form-factors in the distribution-function responses, to find concrete expressions for the transport coefficients in terms of weighted collision integrals. The coefficients are calculated numerically for nuclear matter, using experimental nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The isospin diffusivity is inversely proportional to the neutron-proton cross section and is also sensitive to the symmetry energy. At low temperatures in symmetric matter, the diffusivity is directly proportional to the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper derives the isospin diffusivity for nuclear matter from a linearized Boltzmann transport equation, finding that the diffusivity is inversely proportional to the neutron-proton cross section and directly proportional to the symmetry energy at low temperatures in symmetric matter, with the qualification that these results hold for small deviations from equilibrium under the approximation of constant form-factors in the distribution-function responses.

## Method

The authors linearize the coupled Boltzmann transport equations for a two-component Fermi system (neutrons and protons) under weak deviations from uniformity, following the Chapman-Enskog strategy adapted for fermions with mean-field effects. They solve the linearized equations under the approximation of constant form-factors in the distribution-function responses to obtain concrete expressions for transport coefficients in terms of weighted collision integrals. The coefficients are then calculated numerically for nuclear matter using experimental nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At temperature T ~ 60 MeV and normal density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, with σ_np ~ 40 mb, the isospin diffusivity D_I ~ 0.2 fm c (mean-free-path estimate)
- At normal density, the interaction contribution to the asymmetry coefficient a_I^v ≈ 14 MeV
- Shear viscosity estimate at T ~ 60 MeV and σ ~ 40 mb: η ~ 30 MeV/(fm^2 c)
- Heat conduction estimate at T ~ 60 MeV and σ ~ 40 mb: κ ~ 0.06 c/fm^2

## Context

This work extends prior studies of irreversible linear transport in nuclear matter that were primarily directed at momentum and energy, such as the shear viscosity and heat conductivity calculations by Tomonaga (1938), Galitskii et al. (1979), Danielewicz (1984), and Hakim and Mornas (1993). The derivation follows the general strategy of Chapman and Enskog for a Fermi system with mean-field effects, and the fermion Boltzmann equation extension first discussed by Uhlenbeck and Uehling (1933, 1934). The results enable the use of isospin diffusivity as a universal characterization for nuclear reactions with varying isospin content, analogous to how nuclear viscosity has been used to formulate conclusions from comparisons of reaction simulations to data on stopping.
