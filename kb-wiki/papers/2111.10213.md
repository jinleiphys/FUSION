# First measurement of the $^{10}{\rm B}(α,n)^{13}{\rm N}$ reaction in an inertial confinement fusion implosion at the National Ignition Facility: Initial steps toward the development of a radiochemistry mix diagnostic

> We report the first measurement of the $^{10}{\rm B}(α,n)^{13}{\rm N}$ reaction in a polar-direct-drive exploding pusher (PDXP) at the National Ignition Facility (NIF). This work is motivated by the need to develop alternative mix diagnostics, radiochemistry being the focus here. The target is composed of a $65/35\,\rm at.\,\%$ deuterium-tritium (DT) fill surrounded by a roughly $30\,μ\rm m$ thick beryllium ablator. The inner portion of the beryllium ablator is doped with $10\,\rm at.\,\%$ of $^{10}{\rm B}$. Radiation-hydrodynamics calculations were performed in 1D to optimize both the remaining boron rho-R and the DT neutron yield. A charged-particle transport post-processor has been developed to study $α$-induced reactions on the ablator material. Results indicate a large $^{13}{\rm N}$ production from $α$-induced reactions on $^{10}{\rm B}$, measurable by the radiochemical analysis of gaseous samples system at the NIF. The PDXP target N201115-001 was successfully fielded on the NIF, and nitrogen from the $^{10}{\rm B}(α,n)^{13}{\rm N}$ reaction was measured. The $^{13}{\rm N}$ production yield, as well as the DT neutron yield, was, however, lower than expected. Some of the reduced yields can be explained by the oblate shape, but the ratios of the various radiochemical signals are not commensurate with expectations based on a simple reduction of the 1D results. Preliminary 2D radiation-hydrodynamics computations are consistent with the experimental measurements, and work is ongoing to extend the radiochemistry analysis into higher dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of the \(^{10}{\rm B}(\alpha,n)^{13}{\rm N}\) reaction in an inertial confinement fusion implosion at the National Ignition Facility, but the measured \(^{13}{\rm N}\) production yield and DT neutron yield were both lower than expected from 1D simulations, and the ratios of radiochemical signals did not match simple reductions of the 1D results, though preliminary 2D simulations were consistent with the experimental data.

## Method

A polar-direct-drive exploding pusher target with a 65/35 at.% deuterium-tritium fill and a beryllium ablator doped with 10 at.% \(^{10}{\rm B}\) was fielded on NIF shot N201115-001. 1D radiation-hydrodynamics simulations using the xRAGE code were performed for design optimization and post-shot analysis, and a charged-particle transport post-processor was developed to calculate \(\alpha\)-induced reaction yields by tracking alpha energy loss through the plasma using Zimmerman stopping power. The radiochemical analysis of gaseous samples system measured the \(^{13}{\rm N}\) and \(^{41}{\rm Ar}\) yields, while the gamma reaction history system attempted to measure 4.4 MeV gamma rays from the \(^{9}{\rm Be}(\alpha,n)^{12}{\rm C}^{*}\) reaction.

## Key numbers

* DT neutron yield: \(4.040 \pm 0.126 \times 10^{14}\) from the N201115-001 PDXP shot, measured by the Well-NAD detector.
* \(^{13}{\rm N}\) yield: \(7.0 \pm 1.4 \times 10^{6}\) from the RAGS system, with a detection threshold of \(10^{5}\).
* \(^{41}{\rm Ar}\) yield: \(\sim 1.0 \times 10^{5}\) from the RAGS system, at the detection threshold of \(10^{5}\).
* 4.4 MeV gamma ray yield: \(< 2 \times 10^{12}\) from the GRH detector, with a detection threshold of \(4 \times 10^{10}\).
* Bang time: \(4.5 \pm 0.5\) ns from GRH and \(4.83 \pm 0.13\) ns from SPIDER.
* Burn width: \(600 \pm 75\) ps from GRH and \(618.50 \pm 270.59\) ps from SPIDER.
* 1D xRAGE simulation predicted DT neutron yield: \(3.31 \times 10^{15}\), \(^{13}{\rm N}\) yield: \(4.65 \times 10^{8}\), \(^{41}{\rm Ar}\) yield: \(1.94 \times 10^{7}\), and 4.4 MeV gamma yield: \(7.30 \times 10^{10}\).
* The \(^{10}{\rm B}\) dopant level in the fabricated target was 10 at.%, and the \(^{40}{\rm Ar}\) concentration was 0.3 at.%.

## Context

This work extends the development of radiochemistry mix diagnostics for inertial confinement fusion, motivated by the need for alternative methods when x-ray techniques are ineffective, such as in double-shell and pushered single-shell designs (Montgomery 2018, Dewald 2019). The measurement of the \(^{10}{\rm B}(\alpha,n)^{13}{\rm N}\) reaction builds on the concept of using alpha-particle interactions with ablator material as a mix diagnostic (Colvin 2008) and on the use of the RAGS facility for quantitative measurements of gaseous reaction products (Shaughnessy 2012). The results enable future work to refine the platform for mix sensitivity, including extending the radiochemistry analysis into higher dimensions and modifying the target design to be more sensitive to mixing.
