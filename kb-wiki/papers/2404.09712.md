# 1/2$^-$ $α$ cluster resonances of $^{13}$C studied by the analytic continuation in the coupling constant

> The 1/2$^-$ resonant states in $^{13}{\rm C}$ are investigated to search for the Hoyle-analog state. In order to treat the resonance states located around the 3$α+n$ threshold, the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) has been combined with the real-time evolution method (REM). The properties of the 1/2$^-$ resonance states such as the radii and monopole transition probabilities are calculated. We show the 1/2$^-_3$ and 1/2$^-_4$ states are well-developed $α$ cluster states, and the 1/2$^-_4$ state is a candidate of the Hoyle-analog state.

## Key claim

The 1/2⁻₄ state of ¹³C is a candidate for the Hoyle-analog state (a 3α+n structure analogous to the Hoyle state in ¹²C), but the authors hedge that this assignment depends on the choice of spin-orbit interaction strength (V_ls = 800 or 2000 MeV), and that the 1/2⁻₃ state also shows well-developed α cluster structure.

## Method

The authors combined the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) with the real-time evolution method (REM) to treat resonance states in the 3α+n continuum. They used the Brink-Bloch wave function for the 3α+n system, generated basis states via the equation of motion from the time-dependent variational principle, and performed angular momentum and parity projection followed by the generator coordinate method. The ACCC was applied using the spin-orbit interaction strength as the coupling constant to analytically continue bound-state trajectories to the physical resonance point.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies by Chiba (2020) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics and by Funaki (2015) using the orthogonality condition model, which disagreed on whether a 1/2⁻ Hoyle-analog state exists in ¹³C. The authors' previous REM study (Shin, 2021) could not identify resonances at high excitation energies due to continuum contamination. The combination of ACCC with REM enables identification of resonance properties (energies, widths, radii, monopole transitions) for states near the 3α+n threshold, and the results suggest the 1/2⁻₄ state as a candidate for experimental investigation as the Hoyle analog.
