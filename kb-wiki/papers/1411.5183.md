# Derivative corrections to the symmetry energy and the isovector dipole-resonance structure in nuclei

> The effective surface approximation is extended accounting for derivatives of the symmetry energy density per particle. Using the analytical isovector surface energy constants within the Fermi-liquid droplet model, one obtains energies and sum rules of the isovector dipole resonance structure in a reasonable agreement with the experimental data and other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The effective surface approximation is extended to include derivative corrections of the symmetry energy density per particle (the slope parameter L and curvature parameter K_-), and these corrections produce a reasonable but not perfect agreement with experimental data for isovector dipole resonance energies and sum rules, with the agreement depending significantly on the specific Skyrme force parameters and the value of L.

## Method

The authors extend the effective surface (ES) approximation within the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approach by including terms with the slope parameter L and curvature parameter K_- in the expansion of the symmetry energy density per particle. They solve the variational Lagrange equations for isoscalar and isovector particle densities analytically up to second order in the small parameter gamma (related to the diffuseness), then compute surface symmetry-energy constants, neutron skin thickness, and isovector stiffness. These quantities are used as input to the Fermi-liquid droplet (FLD) model based on the Landau-Vlasov equation to calculate the energies and energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR) of the isovector dipole resonance (IVDR) structure, including the splitting into main and satellite (pygmy) modes.

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry energy constant J ≈ 30 MeV
- Isoscalar incompressibility modulus K_+ ≈ 220-245 MeV
- Slope parameter L ranges from approximately 20 to 120 MeV across different Skyrme forces
- Curvature parameter K_- ranges from approximately -470 to 140 MeV
- Diffuseness parameter a ≈ 0.5-0.6 fm
- For SLy5* force with L=50 MeV in 132Sn: main mode energy E1 = 17.18 MeV, satellite mode energy E2 = 19.86 MeV, with EWSR fractions S1 = 88.8% and S2 = 11.2%
- For SVsym32 force with L=60 MeV in 132Sn: main mode energy E1 = 14.69 MeV, satellite mode energy E2 = 17.98 MeV, with EWSR fractions S1 = 75.5% and S2 = 24.5%
- Neutron skin in 208Pb: R_n - R_p ≈ 0.10-0.13 fm, compared to experimental value 0.12-0.14 fm
- For SLy5* in 132Sn with L=50 MeV: surface symmetry energy k_S = -14.2 MeV, isovector stiffness Q = 48 MeV, neutron skin tau/I = 0.54
- For SVsym32 in 132Sn with L=60 MeV: surface symmetry energy k_S = 17.6 MeV, isovector stiffness Q = -37 MeV, neutron skin tau/I = 0.69

## Context

This work extends the effective surface approximation previously developed by the authors (BMRV, BMRPhysScr2014) by including derivative corrections to the symmetry energy (L and K_- parameters) that were neglected in earlier treatments. It builds on the liquid droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki (Myers and Swiatecki, various years) and the Fermi-liquid droplet model of Kolomietz and collaborators (Kolomietz and Magner, Kolomietz et al.). The results are compared with self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme forces by various groups (Chabanat et al., Reinhard et al., Pastore et al.) and with experimental data on neutron skin thickness from Vinas et al. and the Osaka group. The work enables improved determination of the poorly-constrained surface symmetry energy and isovector stiffness constants through comparison with IVDR splitting data, particularly for exotic nuclei far from stability.
