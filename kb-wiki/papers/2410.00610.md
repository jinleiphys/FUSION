# Electric dipole polarizability of $^{58}$Ni

> The electric dipole strength distribution in $^{58}$Ni between 6 and 20 MeV has been determined from proton inelastic scattering experiments at very forward angles at RCNP, Osaka. The experimental data are rather well reproduced by quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations including vibration coupling, despite a mild dependence on the adopted Skyrme interaction. They allow an estimate of the experimentally inaccessible high-energy contribution above 20 MeV, leading to an electric dipole polarizability $α_\mathrm{D}(^{58}{\rm Ni}) = 3.48(31)$ fm$^3$. This serves as a test case for recent extensions of coupled-cluster calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions to nuclei with two nucleons on top of a closed-shell system.

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability of $^{58}$Ni is determined to be 3.48(31) fm$^3$, but this value relies on a theory-aided estimate of the experimentally inaccessible high-energy contribution above 20 MeV, where the decomposition of the proton scattering spectra into E1 strength and background becomes unreliable.

## Method

The $^{58}$Ni(p,p') reaction was measured at RCNP, Osaka, with 295 MeV protons at laboratory scattering angles from 0.4° to 5.15° and excitation energies from 5 to 25 MeV. A multipole decomposition analysis separated E1, M1, and higher-multipole contributions using theoretical angular distributions from distorted wave Born approximation calculations. The E1 cross sections were converted to photoabsorption cross sections via the virtual photon method, and the polarizability was computed by integrating the photoabsorption cross section divided by excitation energy squared from 6 to 20 MeV. The contribution above 20 MeV was estimated from quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations including quasiparticle vibration coupling, normalized to the experimental data using the KDE033 Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers

- Electric dipole polarizability of $^{58}$Ni: $\alpha_\mathrm{D} = 3.48(31)$ fm$^3$ (total, including high-energy estimate)
- Experimental contribution from 6 to 20 MeV: $\alpha_\mathrm{D} = 2.57(28)$ fm$^3$
- Theoretical estimate for excitation energies above 20 MeV: $\alpha_\mathrm{D}(E_x > 20\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 0.91$ fm$^3$ with an uncertainty of 0.04 fm$^3$ from normalization and 0.13 fm$^3$ from model dependence
- Incident proton energy: 295 MeV
- Energy resolution: 22 keV (full width at half maximum)
- The IVGDR centroid is at about 18 MeV

## Context

This work extends measurements of electric dipole polarizability via proton inelastic scattering at forward angles, previously applied to nuclei such as $^{40,48}$Ca (Bir17, Fea23) and $^{68}$Ni (Ros13), to the open-shell nucleus $^{58}$Ni. The result serves as a test case for newly developed two-particle-attached coupled-cluster calculations (Bonaiti 2024) that extend ab initio methods beyond closed-shell systems. The comparison reveals that current coupled-cluster calculations with chiral interactions (1.8/2.0 (EM), $\Delta$NLO$_{\rm GO}(450)$, $\Delta$NNLO$_{\rm GO}(450)$) reproduce the low-energy E1 strength well but place the IVGDR about 20 MeV too high, indicating the need for higher-order many-body correlations in the two-particle-attached expansion.
