# Vector Mesons and Baryon Resonances in Nuclear Matter

> We calculate the effect of many-body interactions in nuclear matter on the spectral function of $ρ$ and $ω$ meson. In particular, we focus on the role played by baryon resonances in this context.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the in-medium spectral functions of ρ and ω mesons in nuclear matter at zero temperature, focusing on the role of baryon resonances, but the authors note that their self-consistent treatment of resonance feedback only includes collisional broadening and not the corresponding mass shift, which remains to be calculated.

## Method

The authors model vector meson-nucleon scattering using a resonance model, where the in-medium selfenergy is derived from the excitation of baryon resonances that couple to the ρ and ω channels, with coupling constants taken from the Manley (1992) analysis of πN scattering. For the ω meson, where experimental Nω couplings are not established, the authors perform a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) analysis of electromagnetic decay vertices using helicity amplitudes from Arndt (1996) to estimate the coupling constants f_{RNω}. The feedback effect of the medium-modified ρ on the baryon resonances is calculated self-consistently by computing the Nρ decay width of the resonances using the in-medium ρ spectral function, including Pauli blocking for all decay channels.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1520) resonance has a vacuum Nρ decay width of about 25 MeV, leading to a large coupling constant f_{RNρ}
- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀ and zero momentum, the in-medium Nρ decay width of the N*(1520) at its pole mass is approximately 100 MeV
- The in-medium width of the ω meson at ρ₀ is about 40 MeV
- The ω meson mass shifts slightly upward by about 20 MeV at nuclear saturation density
- Subthreshold resonances considered have masses m_R < m_N + m_ρ ≈ 1.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the many-body treatment of vector mesons in nuclear matter beyond the low-density approximation, building on prior calculations by Klingl (1997), Rapp and Wambach (1999), and Peters et al. (1998). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton spectra from the CERES collaboration (1995), which indicate mass shifts of ρ and ω mesons that have been interpreted as signatures of chiral symmetry restoration; the paper shows that conventional many-body effects from baryon resonance excitation can produce significant medium modifications without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation. The self-consistent treatment of resonance feedback opens the door to more complete calculations that would also include the mass shift of the resonances, which the authors note is currently in progress.
