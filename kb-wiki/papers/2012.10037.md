# Enhanced production of strange baryons in high energy nuclear collisions from a multiphase transport model

> We introduce additional coalescence factors for the production of strange baryons in a multiphase transport (AMPT) model in order to describe the enhanced production of multistrange hadrons observed in Pb-Pb collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV at the Large hadron Collider (LHC) and Au+Au collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC).This extended AMPT model is found to also give a reasonable description of the multiplicity dependence of the strangeness enhancement observed in high multiplicity events in $pp$ collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV and $p$-Pb collisions at $\rm \sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. We find that the coalescence factors depend on the system size but not much on whether the system is produced from A+A or p+A collisions. The extended AMPT model thus provides a convenient way to model the mechanism underlying the observed strangeness enhancement in collisions of both small and large systems at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors introduce additional coalescence factors for strange baryons in the AMPT model to describe the observed enhancement of multistrange hadrons, finding that these factors depend on the system size but not much on whether the system is produced from A+A or p+A collisions.

## Method

The authors extend the string melting version of the AMPT model by adding hyperon enhancement factors (r_Y) to the baryon coalescence criterion, which controls whether a quark forms a baryon or meson based on relative distances. They also randomize the initial strange and antistrange quark distributions from melted strings to correct an asymmetry at midrapidity. The model is then tuned to reproduce the total yields of Lambda, Xi, and Omega hyperons in central heavy-ion collisions, and applied to p-Pb and pp collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- In central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: r_Lambda = 1.1, r_Xi = 1.2, r_Omega = 1.5, with Lund parameters a = 0.55, b = 0.15 GeV^-2, and r_BM = 0.61.
- In central (0-5%) Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: r_Lambda = 1.1, r_Xi = 1.2, r_Omega = 1.5, with Lund parameters a = 0.30, b = 0.15 GeV^-2, and r_BM = 0.61.
- In p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: r_Lambda = 1.2, r_Xi = 1.2, r_Omega = 1.3, with Lund parameters a = 0.30, b = 0.15 GeV^-2, and r_BM = 0.54.
- The hadronic cascade reduces Xi and Omega yields by up to 20% and proton yield by 10%, while increasing Lambda yield by 10% in heavy-ion collisions.
- For Lambda in p-Pb collisions, the enhancement due to r_Y factors is up to 50% in central and mid-central collisions and 30% at 80-100% centrality.
- For Xi in p-Pb collisions, the enhancement is 40% for central and mid-central collisions, 30% at 60-80% centrality, and 20% at 80-100% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) which previously failed to reproduce multistrange baryon yields, as noted in earlier studies (Pal et al., 2001; He et al., 2017; Jin et al., 2018). The approach is motivated by ALICE observations of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity pp and p-Pb collisions (ALICE Collaboration, 2017), which standard Monte Carlo models like PYTHIA (Sjostrand et al., 2007) could not describe. The extended model enables systematic studies of strangeness production across system sizes from pp to Pb-Pb collisions, and could be applied to the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC for studying the QCD phase diagram.
