# A method to measure the antikaon-nucleon scattering length in lattice QCD

> We propose a method to determine the isoscalar \bar K N scattering length on the lattice. Our method represents the generalization of Lüscher's approach in the presence of inelastic channels (complex scattering length). In addition, the proposed approach allows one to find the position of the S-matrix pole corresponding the the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of Lüscher's finite-volume method to extract the complex-valued isoscalar antikaon-nucleon scattering length from lattice QCD, specifically for the case where inelastic channels (the \(\Sigma\pi\) channel, with threshold about 100 MeV below \(\bar K N\)) are present and the scattering length is therefore complex. The method also allows determination of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance pole position, but the extraction relies on measuring the volume dependence of the first excited energy level around the \(\bar K N\) threshold, not the ground state.

## Method

The authors formulate a two-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation in non-relativistic effective field theory for the \(\bar K N\) (channel 1) and \(\Sigma\pi\) (channel 2) systems with total isospin \(I=0\). They derive a finite-volume secular equation that yields a "pseudophase" \(\delta(s,L)\) which depends on the box size \(L\) and the energy \(\sqrt{s}\), and show that measuring this pseudophase at three specific energies (at threshold, where \(\tan\delta\) diverges, and where \(\tan\delta\) vanishes) allows extraction of the three real parameters \(H_{11}, H_{12}, H_{22}\) at threshold. These parameters then determine the complex scattering length via a closed-form expression. The method is demonstrated on a coupled-channel model with an explicit \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Sigma\pi\) threshold lies about 100 MeV below the \(\bar K N\) threshold.
* In the demonstration model, the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance is placed at \(\text{Re}\,\sqrt{s_R} = 1406\ \text{MeV}\) with width \(-2\,\text{Im}\,\sqrt{s_R} = 50\ \text{MeV}\).
* The model parameters used are \(H_{11} = -1.47573\ \text{fm}\), \(H_{12} = 0.91581\ \text{fm}\), \(H_{22} = -0.34159\ \text{fm}\).
* These parameters yield a scattering length \(a_{11} = a_0(K^- N) = (-1.26 + i\,0.70)\ \text{fm}\).
* The constants appearing in the \(1/L\) expansion are \(c_1 = -2.837297\ldots\) and \(c_2 = 6.375183\ldots\).
* The ground state level flattens around \(E = 1406\ \text{MeV}\) for \(M_\pi L \simeq 2\ldots 3\).
* The avoided level crossing in the excited levels occurs between 1430 MeV and 1440 MeV, which is not related to the physical resonance but to the \(\bar K N\) threshold.

## Context

This work extends Lüscher's original finite-volume method (Lüscher, 1986, 1990) to the case of coupled channels with a complex scattering length, which is necessary for the \(\bar K N\) system due to the open \(\Sigma\pi\) channel and the nearby \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance. The approach is similar to a treatment within potential scattering theory by He (no year given), but uses non-relativistic effective field theory to systematically include particle creation/annihilation and relativistic corrections. The method is intended to provide a lattice QCD determination of the \(\bar K N\) scattering length that is independent of the model-dependent unitarized chiral perturbation theory approaches used by Borasoy (2004, 2006) and Oller (2006), and complementary to the upcoming SIDDHARTA experiment at DA\(\Phi\)NE which aims to measure kaonic hydrogen and deuterium with high precision.
