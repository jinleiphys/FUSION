# Charmonium Excitation functions in $\bar p$A Collisions

> We study the excitation function of the low-lying charmonium state: $Ψ$(3686) in $\bar p$ Au collisions taking into account their in-medium propagation. The time evolution of the spectral functions of the charmonium state is studied with a BUU type transport model. We calculated the excitation function of $Ψ$(3686) production and show that it is strongly effected by the medium. The energy regime will be available for the PANDA experiment.

# Charmonium Excitation functions in $\bar p$A Collisions

## Key claim
The in-medium mass shift of the $\Psi$(3686) charmonium state can be observed by comparing its excitation function in $\bar p$Au collisions (where medium effects are present) versus $\bar p$C collisions (which serve as a vacuum reference), particularly near threshold where the charmonium is slower and spends more time inside the nucleus.

## Method
The authors use a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) type transport model with off-shell propagation to simulate the time evolution of the $\Psi$(3686) spectral function in $\bar p$Au collisions. The in-medium self-energy includes a real part from the second-order Stark effect and D-meson loops, and an imaginary part from vacuum width plus collisional broadening. Unknown cross sections are calculated using a statistical model, and charmonium absorption cross sections of 7.6 mb for $\Psi$(3686) are applied.

## Key numbers
- The mass shift parameter $\Delta m_V$ for $\Psi$(3686) is $-100$ MeV from the second-order Stark effect and $-30$ MeV from D-meson loops, giving a total of $-130$ MeV at normal nuclear density $n_0$.
- The charmonium absorption cross section used for $\Psi$(3686) is 7.6 mb, taken from Linnyk (2006).
- The D-meson loop contribution to the mass shift is only 25–30 MeV, with the remainder attributed to the second-order Stark effect.
- The simulation is performed for $\bar p$Au collisions at 4 GeV beam kinetic energy.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Wolf et al., Physics Letters) on charmonium in-medium modification well above threshold, where a two-peak structure in the dilepton spectrum was found from decays inside and outside the nucleus. The present paper focuses on the near-threshold regime, where the medium contribution to the dilepton yield is much larger relative to the vacuum contribution. The approach builds on off-shell transport methods previously applied to $\omega$ and $\rho$ mesons at HADES energies (Kämpfer and Wolf, 2010; Almasi). The results are relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, which will provide the necessary energy regime. The authors propose comparing $\bar p$Au with $\bar p$C collisions to isolate the in-medium effect.
