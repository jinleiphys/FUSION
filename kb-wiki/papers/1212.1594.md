# Modification of hadronic spectral functions under extreme conditions: An approach based on QCD sum rules and the maximum entropy method

> Studies of quarkonium spectral functions at finite temperature, based on an approach combining QCD sum rules and the maximum entropy method are briefly reviewed. QCD sum rules for heavy quarkonia incorporate finite temperature effects in form of changing values of gluonic condensates that appear in the operator product expansion. These changes depend on the energy density and pressure at finite temperature, which we extract from quenched lattice QCD calculations. The maximum entropy method then allows us to obtain the most probable spectral function from the sum rules, without having to introduce any specific assumption about its functional form. Our findings suggest that the charmonium ground states of both S-wave and P-wave channels dissolve into the continuum already at temperatures around or slightly above the critical temperature T_c, while the bottomonium states are less influenced by temperature effects, surviving up to about 2.5 T_c or higher for S-wave and up to about 2.0 T_c for P-wave states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charmonium ground states in both S-wave and P-wave channels dissolve into the continuum at temperatures around or slightly above the critical temperature T_c, while bottomonium states survive up to about 2.5 T_c or higher for S-wave and up to about 2.0 T_c for P-wave states, based on an approach that combines QCD sum rules with the maximum entropy method and uses gluonic condensate values extracted from quenched lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors combine QCD sum rules with the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract quarkonium spectral functions at finite temperature without assuming a specific functional form. Finite temperature effects enter through temperature-dependent gluonic condensates in the operator product expansion (OPE), whose values are obtained from the energy density and pressure extracted from quenched lattice QCD calculations. The MEM is then applied to invert the Borel-transformed dispersion relation and obtain the most probable spectral function for each channel.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium ground state peaks in all channels (vector, pseudoscalar, scalar, axial-vector) vanish slightly above T_c, with zero-temperature peak positions reproducing experimental values with a precision of about 50 MeV
- Bottomonium S-wave spectral functions still exhibit a clear peak at T = 2.0 T_c and start to dissolve at about 2.5 T_c
- Bottomonium P-wave states disappear at temperatures around 2.0 T_c
- Bottomonium zero-temperature peaks are found at 150-500 MeV above the experimental values of the respective ground states, attributed to unresolved excited states

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of quarkonia at finite temperature by Morita (2011) and applies the MEM technique developed by Gubler (2011) to both charmonium and bottomonium channels. The results are consistent with lattice QCD studies using MEM by Asakawa (2001) and Ding (2012), and provide a complementary approach to extracting spectral functions without relying on Euclidean time correlators. The approach enables systematic study of how different quarkonium states melt in the quark-gluon plasma, with the next steps being inclusion of higher-order perturbative corrections and higher-dimensional gluonic condensates from lattice QCD.
