# $η'$ photoproduction on the nucleons in the quark model

> A chiral quark-model approach is adopted to study the $γp\rightarrow η'p$ and $γn\rightarrow η' n$. Good descriptions of the recent observations from CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS are obtained. Both of the processes are governed by $S_{11}(1535)$ and $u$ channel background. Strong evidence of an $n=3$ shell resonance $D_{15}(2080)$ is found in the reactions, which accounts for the bump-like structure around $W=2.1$ GeV observed in the total cross section and excitation functions at very forward angles. The $S_{11}(1920)$ seems to be needed in the reactions, with which the total cross section near threshold for the $γp\rightarrow η' p$ is improved slightly. The polarized beam asymmetries show some sensitivities to $D_{13}(1520)$, although its effects on the differential cross sections and total cross sections are negligible. There is no obvious evidence of the $P$-, $D_{13}$-, $F$- and $G$-wave resonances with a mass around 2.0 GeV in the reactions.

## Key claim

The chiral quark-model approach provides good descriptions of recent CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS data for both $\gamma p\rightarrow \eta'p$ and $\gamma n\rightarrow \eta' n$, with the processes governed by $S_{11}(1535)$ and $u$ channel background; however, the claim of strong evidence for the $n=3$ shell resonance $D_{15}(2080)$ is conditional on its role in producing the bump-like structure around $W=2.1$ GeV observed in total cross sections and forward-angle excitation functions, while the need for $S_{11}(1920)$ is qualified as only slightly improving the near-threshold total cross section for $\gamma p\rightarrow \eta' p$.

## Method

A chiral quark-model approach in the SU(6)$\otimes$O(3) harmonic oscillator basis was used to compute $s$- and $u$-channel transition amplitudes for $\eta'$ photoproduction on nucleons, with $t$-channel contributions omitted to avoid double counting. The transition amplitudes for $n=3$ shell resonances ($S_{11}$, $D_{13}$, $D_{15}$, $G_{17}$, $G_{19}$) were derived in the symmetry limit, and resonance parameters (masses, widths, coupling strengths) for $n\leq 2$ shell resonances were taken from a prior study of $\eta$ photoproduction (Zhong 2011). The $\eta' NN$ coupling constant was treated as a free parameter fitted to the experimental data, and the Lorentz boost factor was applied to account for relativistic effects.

## Key numbers

* $D_{15}(2080)$ mass determined as $M\simeq 2080$ MeV with width $\Gamma\simeq 80$ MeV from fits to $\gamma p\rightarrow \eta' p$ data.
* $S_{11}(1920)$ mass determined as $M\simeq 1920$ MeV with width $\Gamma\simeq 90$ MeV from fits to $\gamma p\rightarrow \eta' p$ data.
* Fitted $\eta' NN$ coupling constant $g_{\eta' NN}\simeq 1.86$ (i.e. $\alpha_{\eta'}\equiv g^2_{\eta' NN}/4\pi=0.275$), consistent with the upper limit $g_{\eta' NN}\lesssim 2$ from Nakayama (2005).
* The $\eta-\eta'$ mixing angle derived from the coupling ratio gives $\phi_P\simeq 41.2^\circ$, within the range $\phi_P\simeq 34^\circ\sim 44^\circ$ for flavor mixing angle $\theta_P\simeq -20^\circ\sim -10^\circ$.
* Constituent quark mass $m_q=330$ MeV and harmonic oscillator strength $\alpha^2=0.16$ GeV$^2$ adopted as standard values.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-model approach previously applied to $\eta$ photoproduction on free and quasifree nucleons (Zhong 2011) to the higher-threshold $\eta'$ photoproduction channels, using the same parameter set for $n\leq 2$ shell resonances. It contradicts earlier effective Lagrangian analyses (Zhang 1995) that excluded low-lying resonances and instead attributed the main contribution to $D_{13}(2080)$, and also differs from the Reggeized model (Chiang 2002) and relativistic meson exchange model (Nakayama 2004) which emphasized $t$-channel exchanges and different resonance assignments. The findings enable future experimental measurements to clarify the role of $D_{15}(2080)$ and $S_{11}(1920)$ in $\eta'$ photoproduction, and the flavor symmetry relation between $\eta$ and $\eta'$ couplings provides constraints for quark-model descriptions of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction.
