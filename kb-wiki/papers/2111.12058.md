# Insignificance of the anomalous magnetic moment of the quarks in presence of chiral imbalance

> We incorporate the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the framework of PNJL model to study hot and dense magnetised matter with chiral imbalance. For this purpose, the eigen energy solution of the Dirac equation is obtained in presence of constant background magnetic field and chiral chemical potential (CCP) along with the minimal anomalous magnetic moment interaction of the fermion. Although there is a marginal enhancement in the IMC behaviour of the quark condensate due to the combined effects of AMM and CCP, we find that the overall behaviour of the Polyakov loop and the chiral charge density is dominated by the chiral chemical potential. It is further shown that the AMM effects in presence of CCP remains insignificant even after consideration of thermo-magnetically modified moments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that, within the PNJL model with constant coupling, the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks has an insignificant effect on the Polyakov loop and chiral charge density when a chiral chemical potential (CCP) is present, even though the AMM produces a marginal enhancement of inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) in the quark condensate.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for quarks with non-zero AMM in a constant background magnetic field and chiral chemical potential to obtain the energy eigenvalues. They then incorporated these eigenvalues into the two-flavor PNJL model at zero quark chemical potential, using a smooth three-momentum cutoff regularization scheme to avoid artifacts, and solved the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass, Polyakov loop, and chiral charge density self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: cutoff Lambda = 568.7 MeV, coupling G*Lambda^2 = 1.857, bare quark mass m0 = 5.6 MeV, chosen to reproduce vacuum chiral condensate (-243.5 MeV)^3, pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, and pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV.
- Vacuum AMM values: kappa_u = 0.02399 GeV^{-1}, kappa_d = 0.09595 GeV^{-1}, chosen to reproduce nucleon magnetic moments (proton 2.7928 mu_N, neutron -1.9130 mu_N).
- Background magnetic fields studied: eB = 0.0, 0.05, 0.10, and 0.15 GeV^2.
- Polyakov potential parameters: a0 = 3.51, a1 = -2.47, a2 = 15.2, b3 = -1.75, T0 = 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chiral imbalance effects on the QCD phase structure using NJL and PNJL models (Fukushima 2010, Yu 2014, Yu 2015, Chao 2013) and separate studies of AMM effects in magnetized matter (Fayazbakhsh 2014, Chaudhuri 2019, Chaudhuri 2020, Ghosh 2021) by combining both AMM and chiral chemical potential for the first time. The results enable future investigations of whether more sophisticated treatments of the AMM (e.g., thermo-magnetically modified moments) or different regularization schemes could alter the conclusion that AMM effects remain subdominant to CCP effects.
