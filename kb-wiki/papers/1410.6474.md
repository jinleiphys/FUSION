# Massive fermions without fermion bilinear condensates

> We study a lattice field theory model containing two flavors of massless staggered fermions with an onsite four-fermion interaction. The model contains a $SU(4)$ symmetry which forbids non-zero fermion bilinear mass terms, due to which there is a massless fermion phase at weak couplings. However, even at strong couplings fermion bilinear condensates do not appear in our model, although fermions do become massive. While the existence of this exotic strongly coupled massive fermion phase was established long ago, the nature of the transition between the massless and the massive phase has remained unclear. Using Monte Carlo calculations in three space-time dimensions, we find evidence for a direct second order transition between the two phases suggesting that the exotic lattice phase may have a continuum limit at least in three dimensions. A similar exotic second order critical point was found recently in a bilayer system on a honeycomb lattice.

## Key claim

The authors provide Monte Carlo evidence that a direct second-order phase transition exists between the massless fermion phase (PMW) and the exotic massive fermion phase without fermion bilinear condensates (PMS) in a three-dimensional lattice four-fermion model, contradicting earlier mean-field predictions of either an intervening conventional massive phase or a first-order transition.

## Method

The authors study a lattice field theory with two flavors of massless staggered fermions and an onsite four-fermion interaction in three space-time dimensions, using the fermion bag approach to perform Monte Carlo simulations. They compute the fermion bilinear susceptibilities χ₁ and χ₂ (defined from correlation functions of the six onsite fermion bilinears) and the local four-point condensate ρₘ, using three complementary update algorithms: a block algorithm, a worm algorithm, and a heat-bath sweep algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The model is studied on cubic lattices of equal size L in each direction with anti-periodic boundary conditions; the action contains an onsite four-fermion coupling U.
- At strong coupling, the fermionic correlation function decays exponentially as exp(-(4ℓ+4) ln Y), where Y² = U and ℓ is determined by the shortest path length between sites, proving fermions become massive.
- The mean-field critical coupling for N_f=2 flavors is Y_c^w = Y_c^s = d/2, where d is the spatial dimension, suggesting the FM phase may be absent.
- The worm algorithm uses an enhancement factor e = 10 in the worm creation step to improve acceptance rates.

## Context

This work revisits a lattice four-fermion model that is a limiting case of a lattice Yukawa model studied by Lee et al. (1989), where earlier four-dimensional studies found a PMS phase at strong couplings. The results contradict mean-field predictions by Stephanov (1990) and Ebihara (1991) that predicted a first-order transition, and instead support a second-order transition similar to one recently found by Slagle (2014) in a bilayer-honeycomb lattice system. The findings suggest that exotic mass generation mechanisms without fermion bilinear condensates may have continuum limits in three dimensions.
