# Complex phase structure of the meson-baryon $T$-matrix

> The full complex phase structure of the meson-baryon reaction amplitude in coupled channels approach is investigated, including also the photon-baryon channel. The result may be viewed as a generalization of the well-known Watson's theorem. Furthermore, the complex phase structure is exhibited for the pole and nonpole parts of the reaction amplitude in such a way that it will serve as a convenient common starting point for constructing models with different levels of approximation, in particular, for building isobar models where the basic properties of the $S$-matrix can be maintained. Such models should be useful, especially, in coupled multichannel calculations, where a large amount of experimental data are considered in resonance analyses, a situation encountered in modern baryon spectroscopy. In particular, it is shown that the unitarity of the pole part of the $T$-matrix arises automatically from the dressing mechanism inherent in the basic scattering equation. This implies that no separate conditions are required for making this part of the resonance amplitude unitary as it has been done in some of the existing isobar models.

## Key claim

The paper derives the full complex phase structure of the meson-baryon reaction amplitude in a coupled-channels approach, showing that the unitarity of the pole part of the T-matrix arises automatically from the dressing mechanism inherent in the basic scattering equation, meaning no separate unitarization conditions are required for the resonance amplitude as has been done in some existing isobar models.

## Method

The authors express the T-matrix in terms of the K-matrix and a generalized Watson's factor, then decompose both the T-matrix and K-matrix into pole (resonance) and nonpole (background) parts. They derive the phase structure of each part by solving the scattering equations in coupled-channel form, using the decomposition of the two-body propagator into real and imaginary parts, and applying the one-photon approximation for photoproduction channels.

## Key numbers

- The generalized Watson's factor for the final state is given as \(N_{\alpha'} = 1/(1 + i\hat{K}_{\alpha'\alpha'} G^I_{\alpha'})\), where \(G^I\) is the imaginary part of the propagator and \(\hat{K}\) is the complex K-matrix.
- For the two-channel case with stable particles, the transition amplitude takes the form \(T_{\alpha'\alpha} = \left\{\frac12 (\eta_{\alpha'} e^{i2\delta_{\alpha'}} + 1)\right\} K_{\alpha'\alpha} \left(e^{i\delta_\alpha}\cos\delta_\alpha\right)\), where \(\eta\) is the inelasticity and \(\delta\) is the phase shift.
- In the one-photon approximation for photoproduction, Watson's theorem yields \(T_{\alpha'\gamma} = \left(e^{i\delta_{\alpha'}} \cos\delta_{\alpha'}\right) K_{\alpha'\gamma}\), with the initial-state Watson factor approaching unity.

## Context

This work generalizes Watson's theorem (Watson, 1954) for photoproduction to the full complex phase structure of meson-baryon reactions in coupled channels, including the photon-baryon channel. The result provides a common starting point for constructing isobar models with varying levels of approximation, such as those used by the Mainz group (DHKT99, DKT07, TDKV11) and the etaMAID model, where unitarization of the resonance amplitude previously required separate conditions. The formalism enables consistent coupled multichannel calculations for baryon spectroscopy analyses that incorporate large amounts of experimental data from facilities worldwide.
