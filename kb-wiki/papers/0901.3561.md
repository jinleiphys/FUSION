# Pauli blocking and final-state interaction in electron-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> The nucleon final-state interaction in inclusive electron-nucleus quasielastic scattering is studied. Based on the unitarity equation satisfied by the scattering-wave operators, a doorway model is developed to take into account the final-state interaction including the Pauli blocking of nucleon knockout. The model uses only experimental form factors as the input and can be readily applied to light- and medium-mass nuclei. Pauli blocking effects in these latter nuclei are illustrated with the case of the Coulomb interaction. Significant effects are noted for beam energies below ~ 350 MeV and for low momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper develops a doorway model for the nucleon final-state interaction (FSI) in inclusive electron-nucleus quasielastic scattering that uses only experimental form factors as input, avoiding the need for explicit optical potentials. The model implements orthogonality between bound and scattering states at all FSI energies on the same footing, with the hedge that Pauli blocking effects are significant only for beam energies below approximately 350 MeV and for low momentum transfers.

## Method

The author starts from the unitarity equation satisfied by scattering-wave operators and derives a doorway model where the FSI is represented by subtracting bound-state projectors from the unit operator. The nuclear response functions are expressed in terms of experimental ground-state and transition form factors, with the Pauli blocking correction (PBC) given by the probability that the struck nucleon remains bound. For nuclei with mass number A ≤ 5 the calculation is exact using only the ground-state form factor, while for 1p-shell nuclei like ¹²C the approximation using only the ground-state form factor is shown to be accurate to within 2%.

## Context

This work extends prior approaches to FSI in inclusive quasielastic scattering that used phenomenological energy-dependent potentials (Horikawa 1980, Kawazoe 1985) and addresses the orthogonality problem identified in earlier studies (Shakin 1970, Capuzzi 1991). The doorway model provides a computationally simple alternative to optical-potential methods used in exclusive experiments (Izumoto 1981, Chinn 1995) and can be readily applied to light and medium-mass nuclei. The approach enables systematic inclusion of Pauli blocking effects using experimentally measured form factors, with the main open question being the application to heavy-mass nuclei where systematic knowledge of NES transition form factors is lacking.
