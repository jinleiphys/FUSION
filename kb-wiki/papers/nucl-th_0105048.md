# The S Wave Pion Deuteron Scattering Length in Effective Field Theory

> The S wave pion deuteron scattering length is presented at NLO in the Effective Field Theory formulation of the two nucleon system with perturbative pions. At this order, this observable is determined by the iso-scalar S wave pion nucleon scattering length a^+, by pion re-scattering contributions inside the deuteron and by two nucleon two pion contact interactions that involve two unknown parameters which renormalise the appearing divergences. By choosing their values accordingly and in agreement with their natural sizes predicted by the power counting, we are able to accommodate the available experimental data. However, a^+ cannot be determined from pion deuteron scattering directly. An ongoing experiment at PSI aiming to reduce the error bars by an order of magnitude will make it possible to pin down the size of these counter terms more accurately. We compare our findings to an approach in which pions are treated non-perturbatively.

## Key claim
The S-wave pion-deuteron scattering length at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the KSW effective field theory scheme is not fully determined by chiral symmetry alone; it requires two unknown parameters from two-nucleon two-pion contact interactions to renormalize divergences, and the iso-scalar pion-nucleon scattering length a^+ cannot be extracted from pion-deuteron scattering directly.

## Method
The authors computed the S-wave pion-deuteron scattering amplitude at zero momentum using the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory, treating pions perturbatively. The calculation included one-body contributions from the iso-scalar pion-nucleon amplitude embedded in the deuteron, and two-body contributions from pion re-scattering diagrams and two-nucleon two-pion contact interactions. Divergences were renormalized using the PDS scheme, and the unknown contact parameters were fixed by comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The experimental pion-deuteron scattering length is a_pi d = [(-0.0261 ± 0.0005) + i(0.0063 ± 0.0007)] m_pi^{-1} (Hauser 1998).
- The HBChPT prediction for the iso-scalar pion-nucleon scattering length is a^+_HBChPT = [-0.01 ... +0.006] m_pi^{-1} = (-0.002 ± 0.008) m_pi^{-1} (Fettes 1998, Fettes 2000).
- The experimental extraction of a^+ from pionic hydrogen gives a^+_exp,piN = (-0.0022 ± 0.0043) m_pi^{-1} (Schroder 1999).
- The phenomenological extraction by Ericson, Loiseau and Thomas (2000) gives a^+_phen,ELT = (-0.0017 ± 0.0002(stat) ± 0.0008(sys)) m_pi^{-1}.
- The pion mass used is m_pi = 138.039 MeV; the pion decay constant is f_pi = 130 MeV; the axial coupling is g_A = 1.27; the nucleon mass is M = 938.918 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory treatment of pion-deuteron scattering beyond the Weinberg counting approach of Beane (1998), which used phenomenological deuteron wavefunctions and found no undetermined parameters. By using the perturbative KSW scheme, the authors show that two unknown contact parameters (D_2, E_2, F_2) are required at NLO, introducing model dependence. The ongoing PSI experiment R-98.01 aims to reduce error bars on a^+ by an order of magnitude, which will help constrain these counterterms. The result contrasts with the Weinberg approach where chiral symmetry alone was thought to determine a_pi d.
