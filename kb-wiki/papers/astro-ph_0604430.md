# Neutrino Astronomy and Cosmic Rays Spectroscopy at Horizons

> A new air-showering physics may rise in next years at horizon, offering at different angles and altitudes a fine tuned filtered Cosmic Rays astrophysics and an upward Neutrino induced air-showering astronomy. Most of this opportunity arises because of neutrino masses, their mixing and the consequent replenishment of rarest tau flavor during its flight in Space. Horizontal air atmosphere act as a filter for High energy Cosmic Rays (CR) and as a beam dump for Ultra High Energy (UHE) neutrinos and a powerfull amplifier for its tau decay in air by its wide showering areas. Earth sharp shadows plays the role of a huge detector volume for UHE neutrino and a noise-free screen for upcoming EeVs tau air-showers (as well PeVs anti-neutrino electron air interactions). Projects for Tau Airshowers are growing at the top of a mountains or at the edge of a cliff. ASHRA in Hawaii and CRNTN in Utah are tracking fluorescence lights, while other novel projects on Crown array detectors on mountains, on balloons and satellites are elaborated for Cherenkov lights. AUGER, facing the Ande edges, ARGO located within a deep valley are testing inclined showers; MILAGRO (and MILAGRITO) may be triggered by horizontal up-going muon bundles from the Earth edges; HIRES and AUGER UHECR detectors, linking twin array telescopes along their axis may test horizontal Cerenkov blazing photons. MAGIC (Hess, Veritas) and Shalon Telescopes may act already like a detector for few PeVs and Glashow resonance neutrino events; MAGIC pointing downward to terrestrial ground acts as a massive tens of km^3 detector, making it the most sensitive dedicated neutrino telescope. MAGIC facing the sea edges must reveal mirrored downward UHECR Air-showers Cherenkov flashes. Magic-crown systems may lead to tens km^3, neutrino detectors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that horizontal and up-going air-showers at the Earth's horizon, particularly those induced by tau neutrinos interacting in the Earth's crust and decaying in the atmosphere, will open a new window for ultra-high energy (UHE) neutrino astronomy and cosmic ray spectroscopy, contingent on neutrino masses and flavor mixing replenishing the tau flavor during cosmic flight.

## Method

The author reviews the physics of horizontal air-showers, contrasting them with vertical ones, and describes how the Earth's edge acts as a beam dump for UHE neutrinos and a noise-free screen for tau air-showers. The paper surveys existing and proposed experiments (ASHRA, CRNTN, AUGER, ARGO, MILAGRO, MAGIC, HIRES, EUSO, and others) and discusses how their configurations, such as mountains, valleys, or sea edges, can be adapted to detect tau air-showers from Earth-skimming neutrinos, including the Glashow resonance for anti-neutrino-electron interactions.

## Key numbers

- The Glashow resonance for anti-neutrino-electron interactions occurs at an energy of about 6.3 PeV.
- The GZK cut-off for ultra-high energy cosmic rays is at an energy of about 4 times 10 to the 19 eV.
- The oscillation length for muon neutrino to tau neutrino conversion is given as 8.3 parsecs times (E_nu / 10 to the 19 eV) times (Delta m squared / 10 to the minus 2 eV squared) to the minus 1.
- For the Z-burst model, the incoming UHE neutrino energy required for resonance is about 10 to the 22 eV times (m_nu / 0.4 eV) to the minus 1, leading to a nucleon energy of about 2 times 10 to the 20 eV times (m_nu / 0.4 eV) to the minus 1.
- For a lightest neutrino mass of 0.05 eV, the Z-burst resonance energy is about 8 times 10 to the 22 eV, with a resulting nucleon energy bump at about 1.6 ZeV.
- The paper estimates that AUGER might observe about two tau air-shower events per year from GZK neutrino flux within a solid angle of about 6 times 10 to the minus 2 steradians.
- EUSO is estimated to have a mass equivalent of nearly 100 sr km cubed water equivalent for Earth-skimming neutrinos, with a predicted 4 to 6 events per year.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposals (Fargion 1999, Fargion 2002a) for tau air-shower detection from Earth-skimming neutrinos, building on the discovery of atmospheric neutrino anomalies by Super-Kamiokande (1997) and the consequent expectation of tau neutrino appearance. It contrasts with conventional muon-based neutrino telescopes (such as AMANDA) and argues that tau neutrinos become more penetrating than muons at EeV energies, enabling a new astronomy. The paper enables future searches by suggesting that existing gamma-ray and cosmic-ray observatories (MAGIC, AUGER, ARGO, MILAGRO) can be repurposed or optimized to detect tau air-showers, potentially opening the PeV-EeV neutrino window.
