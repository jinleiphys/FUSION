# Progress Towards Determining the Density Dependence of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy Using Heavy-Ion Reactions

> The latest development in determining the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions is reviewed. Within the IBUU04 version of an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model using a modified Gogny effective interaction, recent experimental data from NSCL/MSU on isospin diffusion are found to be consistent with a nuclear symmetry energy of $E_{sym}(ρ)\approx 31.6(ρ/ρ_{0})^{1.05}$ at subnormal densities. Predictions on several observables sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities accessible at GSI and the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) are also made.

## Key claim
The paper reports that recent experimental data on isospin diffusion from NSCL/MSU are consistent with a nuclear symmetry energy of \(E_{sym}(\rho)\approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_{0})^{1.05}\) at subnormal densities, but this result is conditional on using the IBUU04 transport model with a modified Gogny effective interaction and the specific parameter \(x=-1\).

## Method
The authors used the IBUU04 version of an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model based on a modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI) to simulate heavy-ion reactions. They compared the calculated degree of isospin diffusion in the reaction \(^{124}\)Sn + \(^{112}\)Sn with experimental data from NSCL/MSU, varying the parameter \(x\) in the interaction to mimic different density dependences of the symmetry energy. The symmetry energy was extracted by finding the \(x\) value that best reproduced the measured isospin diffusion observable \(R_i\).

## Key numbers
- The extracted symmetry energy at subnormal densities is parameterized as \(E_{sym}(\rho) \approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_{0})^{1.05}\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density.
- The incompressibility \(K_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\) is set to 211 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at normal density is fixed to 30 MeV.
- The isospin diffusion process occurs mainly from about 30 fm/c to 80 fm/c, corresponding to an average central density from about \(1.2\rho_0\) to \(0.3\rho_0\).
- For the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio at supranormal densities, the \(\pi^{-}\) multiplicity increases by about 20% when the \(x\) parameter is changed from -2 to 1, while the \(\pi^{+}\) multiplicity remains about the same; the multiplicity of \(\pi^{-}\) is about 2 to 3 times that of \(\pi^{+}\).
- For the isospin asymmetry of free nucleons at midrapidity, as the parameter \(x\) increases from -2 to 1, \(\delta_{free}\) decreases by about a factor of 2.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to determine the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy using heavy-ion reactions, building on the transport model framework of Li et al. (2003) and the experimental isospin diffusion data from Tsang et al. (2004). The extracted symmetry energy at subnormal densities provides a benchmark for comparison with results from other observables and with predictions from many-body theories, which diverge widely at both low and high densities. The predictions for observables at supranormal densities (pion ratios, isospin fractionation, and neutron-proton differential flow) are intended to guide future experiments at GSI and the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA).
