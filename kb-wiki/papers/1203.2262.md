# Empirical Fit to electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim
The paper presents an empirical fit to inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data for nuclei with atomic number A > 2, valid over the kinematic range 0 < W < 3.2 GeV and 0.2 < Q² < 5 GeV², with the caveat that it can be used with caution at lower Q². The fit builds on previous empirical fits to electron-proton and electron-neutron scattering by adding Fermi motion effects and a substantial extra "MEC" contribution that fills the dip between the quasi-elastic peak and the Δ(1232) resonance.

## Method
The fit combines a Fermi-smeared sum of free proton and neutron structure functions with an additional "MEC" (meson-exchange current) term that fills the dip between the quasi-elastic and Δ(1232) peaks, multiplied by an EMC-effect parametrization from S. Rock (1994). The quasi-elastic contribution uses form factors from Sick with Fermi momentum and binding energy parameters tabulated for different nuclear mass ranges, plus a Pauli suppression factor and a scaling function F(ψ′). Coulomb corrections are applied using a simple energy gain/loss method with a potential V = 0.775 × (3/2) × α_F (Z-1)/R_0 where R_0 = 1.1 A^(1/3) + 0.86 A^(-1/3).

## Key numbers
- Fit valid for 0 < W < 3.2 GeV and 0.2 < Q² < 5 GeV²
- Fermi momentum k_F ranges from 0.115 GeV (A=3) to 0.245 GeV (A>60)
- Binding energy E_s ranges from 0.001 GeV (A=3) to 0.028 GeV (38<A<56)
- MEC term parameter P_18 varies from 70 (A=3) to 235 (20<A<51) and 230 (A>50)
- For W > 1.2 GeV, agreement with world data is generally within 5%, and better than 3% on average
- In the quasi-elastic peak region, agreement is within 5% on average
- The MEC peak is centered near W = 1.05 GeV
- The fine structure constant α_F = 1/137

## Context
This work extends the earlier empirical fit to inclusive electron-deuteron scattering by Bosted and provides a parametrization for heavier nuclei needed for applications such as radiative corrections to Jefferson Lab data from Mamyan and background evaluation in polarized ammonia scattering. The fit uses the free proton structure functions from Christy and neutron structure functions from Bosted, the quasi-elastic formalism from Sick, and an unpublished EMC-effect parametrization by S. Rock (1994). The results enable reliable evaluation of radiative corrections and background subtraction for inclusive electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab and elsewhere.
