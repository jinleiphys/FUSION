# Antikaon angular distributions in the reaction $γd \to K^-Θ^+p \to K^-K^+np$ near the threshold and the parity of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark

> Within spectator model we study the reaction $γd \to K^-Θ^+p \to K^-K^+np$ in the threshold energy region. We present the predictions for the exclusive and inclusive antikaon angular distributions in the laboratory system for this reaction calculated for two possible parity states of the $Θ^+$ resonance at 1.5 and 1.75 GeV beam energies with and without imposing the relevant kinematical cuts on those parts of the sampled phase space where contribution from the main background sources associated with the $ϕ(1020)$, $Λ(1520)$ production as well as with the $K^-p$--rescattering in the final state, is expected to be dominant. We show that under chosen kinematics these distributions are sensitive to the $Θ^+$ parity and, therefore, can be used as a filter for the determination of its parity.

## Key claim

The angular distributions of antikaons produced in the reaction γd → K⁻Θ⁺p → K⁻K⁺np near threshold are sensitive to the parity of the Θ⁺ pentaquark even when the reaction occurs on a bound neutron in the deuteron, provided appropriate kinematic cuts are applied to suppress background contributions from φ(1020) and Λ(1520) production and K⁻p rescattering.

## Method

The authors employed a spectator model within the Impulse Approximation, where the reaction γd → K⁻Θ⁺p → K⁻K⁺np is treated as Θ⁺ photoproduction off a neutron in the deuteron followed by Θ⁺ decay into K⁺n, with the proton acting as a spectator. The off-shell differential cross section for the elementary γn → K⁻Θ⁺ process was parametrized using results from previous theoretical calculations (Nam et al., 2005; Kim et al., 2005), with isotropic angular distribution for positive Θ⁺ parity and sin²θ′ behavior for negative Θ⁺ parity. The calculations incorporated kinematic cuts to eliminate phase space regions where φ(1020) production (K⁺K⁻ invariant mass 1.00–1.04 GeV), Λ(1520) production (K⁻p invariant mass 1.485–1.551 GeV), and K⁻p rescattering (relative momentum below 100 MeV/c) dominate.

## Key numbers

- The Θ⁺ mass is taken as m_Θ⁺ = 1.54 GeV
- Calculations were performed at photon beam energies of 1.5 and 1.75 GeV
- The K⁺ momentum in the Θ⁺ rest frame from its decay into K⁺n is 269.7 MeV/c
- At E_γ = 1.8 GeV, the positive and negative parity cases give total cross sections of 100 nb and 2 nb, respectively; at E_γ = 2.5 GeV, these are 230 nb and 28 nb
- The nucleon momentum distribution in the deuteron peaks near 45 MeV/c with a tail above 150 MeV/c
- The K⁻p invariant mass cut to suppress rescattering is M_cut = 1.447 GeV, corresponding to relative momentum q_K⁻p ≥ 100 MeV/c
- The spectator proton momentum cut is p_cut = 280 MeV/c
- The branching ratio BR(Θ⁺ → K⁺n) = 1/2 for both parities

## Context

This work extends the theoretical studies of Nam et al. (2005) and Kim et al. (2005), who demonstrated that the unpolarized differential cross section for γn → K⁻Θ⁺ near threshold shows distinct angular patterns depending on Θ⁺ parity (isotropic for positive, sin²θ′ for negative). The present paper addresses the practical complication that the neutron target is bound in the deuteron with Fermi motion, which could smear the predicted angular distributions. The results enable experimental determination of Θ⁺ parity using unpolarized observables at facilities such as JLab, where the CLAS Collaboration (2004) observed the Θ⁺ in the same reaction channel. The approach avoids the need for polarized beams or targets, which would require more complex experimental setups.
