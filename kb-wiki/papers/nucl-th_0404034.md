# Color screening in a constituent quark model of hadronic matter

> The effect of color screening on the formation of a heavy quark-antiquark ($Q\bar{Q}$) bound state--such as the $J/ψ$ meson--is studied using a constituent-quark model. The response of the nuclear medium to the addition of two color charges is simulated directly in terms of its quark constituents via a string-flip potential that allows for quark confinement within hadrons yet enables the hadrons to separate without generating unphysical long-range forces. Medium modifications to the properties of the heavy meson, such as its energy and its mean-square radius, are extracted by solving Schrödinger's equation for the $Q\bar{Q}$ pair in the presence of a (screened) density-dependent potential. The density dependence of the heavy-quark potential is in qualitative agreement with earlier studies of its temperature dependence extracted from lattice calculations at finite temperature. In the present model it is confirmed that abrupt changes in the properties of the $J/ψ$-meson in the hadronic medium ({\it plasma}), correlate strongly with the deconfining phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that abrupt changes in the energy and mean-square radius of the $J/\psi$ meson in a hadronic medium correlate strongly with the deconfining phase transition, but only within a constituent quark model that uses a string-flip potential and a one-parameter variational wave function to describe the nuclear-to-quark-matter transition dynamically.

## Method

The authors used a string-flip model with a many-body potential that confines quarks into color-singlet clusters while allowing cluster separation without long-range van der Waals forces. They performed variational Monte Carlo simulations to compute the optimal variational parameter $\lambda$ for a system of light quarks at various densities, then introduced a static heavy quark-antiquark pair to extract a density-dependent heavy-quark potential $V_{Q\bar{Q}}(r;\rho)$. Finally, they solved the Schrödinger equation for the $c\bar{c}$ pair in this screened potential to obtain the in-medium energy and mean-square radius of the $J/\psi$ meson.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for the deconfining phase transition is $\rho_{\rm c}\simeq 0.09$ (in dimensionless units).
- The free-space $J/\psi$ ground-state energy is $E_{J/\psi}=11.342$ (in units where $k=m=1$).
- The free-space $J/\psi$ mean-square radius is $r_{J/\psi}^{2}=0.168$ (in the same units).
- The charm quark mass is fixed at $m_{c}/m=5$ relative to the light constituent quark mass.
- The change in the mean-square radius of the $J/\psi$ between $\rho=0$ and $\rho=0.08$ is less than 6\%.
- At a density just above the transition ($\rho=0.10$), the energy decreases by almost 50\% and the mean-square radius increases by close to 25\%, relative to free-space values.
- The variational parameter for an isolated nucleon is $\lambda_{0}=1/\sqrt{3}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color screening in a constituent quark model by Toledo Sanchez and Piekarewicz (2002) and complements finite-temperature lattice QCD calculations by Karsch et al. (2001) and others, which are unable to simulate hadronic systems at finite baryon density. The results support the long-standing tenet of Matsui and Satz (1986) that medium modifications to the $J/\psi$ meson serve as a robust signature for the deconfining phase transition, while also addressing recent questions about $J/\psi$ suppression raised by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) and others regarding statistical recombination effects.
