# Bulk viscosity from Urca processes: $npeμ$ matter in the neutrino-transparent regime

> We study the bulk viscosity of moderately hot and dense, neutrino-transparent relativistic $npeμ$ matter arising from weak-interaction direct Urca processes. This work parallels our recent study of the bulk viscosity of $npeμ$ matter with a trapped neutrino component. The nuclear matter is modeled in a relativistic density functional approach with two different parametrizations -- DDME2 (which does not allow for the low-temperature direct-Urca process at any density) and NL3 (which allows for low-temperature direct-Urca process above a low-density threshold). We compute the equilibration rates of Urca processes of neutron decay and lepton capture, as well as the rate of the muon decay, and find that the muon decay process is subdominant to the Urca processes at temperatures $T\geq 3$MeV in the case of DDME2 model and $T\geq 1$MeV in the case of NL3 model. Thus, the Urca-process-driven bulk viscosity is computed with the assumption that pure leptonic reactions are frozen. As a result the electronic and muonic Urca channels contribute to the bulk viscosity independently and at certain densities the bulk viscosity of $npeμ$ matter shows instead of the standard one-peak (resonant) form a "flattened" shape. In the final step, we estimate the damping timescales of density oscillations by the bulk viscosity. We find that, e.g., at a typical oscillation frequency $f=1$kHz, the damping of oscillations is most efficient at temperatures $3\leq T\leq 5$MeV and densities $n_B\leq 2n_0$ where they can affect the evolution of the post-merger object.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of neutrino-transparent $npe\mu$ matter from direct Urca processes can exhibit a "flattened" two-peak shape instead of the standard single resonant peak, because the muon decay process is subdominant to the Urca processes at temperatures $T\geq 3$ MeV (DDME2 model) or $T\geq 1$ MeV (NL3 model), allowing electronic and muonic Urca channels to contribute independently.

## Method

The authors computed the equilibration rates of direct Urca processes (neutron decay and lepton capture) and muon decay in relativistic $npe\mu$ matter using a covariant density functional approach with two parametrizations (DDME2 and NL3). They evaluated the bulk viscosity coefficient from the linearized balance equations for particle densities under small-amplitude density oscillations, assuming pure leptonic reactions are frozen because muon decay is much slower than Urca processes. The damping timescales of density oscillations were then estimated from the bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Muon decay is subdominant to Urca processes at temperatures $T\geq 3$ MeV for the DDME2 model and $T\geq 1$ MeV for the NL3 model.
- At a typical oscillation frequency $f=1$ kHz, damping of oscillations is most efficient at temperatures $3\leq T\leq 5$ MeV and densities $n_B\leq 2n_0$.
- The low-temperature limit of the Urca process rate is $\Gamma = \frac{\alpha}{2} G^2 T^5 \mu_n^* (p_{Fp}^2 + p_{Fl}^2 + 2\mu_l \mu_p^* - p_{Fn}^2) \theta(p_{Fl} + p_{Fp} - p_{Fn})$ with $\alpha \simeq 0.0168$.
- The low-temperature limit of the muon decay rate is $\Gamma_{\mu\to e\bar\nu\nu} = \frac{\alpha}{2} G^2 T^5 \mu_\mu (p_{Fe}^2 - p_{F\mu}^2) \theta(p_{Fe} - p_{F\mu})$.
- The bulk viscous damping timescale is in the range $1$-$10$ ms for the neutrino-transparent regime.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of bulk viscosity in $npe\mu$ matter with trapped neutrinos (Alford 2021c) to the neutrino-transparent regime, and parallels earlier work on muonless nuclear matter (Alford 2019a, Alford 2020) which found bulk viscous damping is most efficient in this temperature range. The results are relevant for numerical simulations of binary neutron star mergers (Perego 2019, Hanauske 2019, Bernuzzi 2015) where bulk viscous dissipation by weak interactions is expected to be the most efficient damping mechanism for density oscillations in post-merger matter (Alford 2018a, Celora 2022, Most 2022). The inclusion of the NL3 parametrization allows assessment of uncertainties associated with the choice of nuclear density functional.
