# Mean field at finite temperature and symmetry breaking

> For an infinite system of nucleons interacting through a central spin-isospin schematic force we discuss how the Hartree-Fock theory at finite temperature T yields back, in the T=0 limit, the standard zero-temperature Feynman theory when there is no symmetry breaking. The attention is focused on the mechanism of cancellation of the higher order Hartree-Fock diagrams and on the dependence of this cancellation upon the range of the interaction. When a symmetry breaking takes place it turns out that more iterations are required to reach the self-consistent Hartree-Fock solution, because the cancellation of the Hartree-Fock diagrams of order higher than one no longer occurs. We explore in particular the case of an explicit symmetry breaking induced by a constant, uniform magnetic field B acting on a system of neutrons. Here we compare calculations performed using either the single-particle Matsubara propagator or the zero-temperature polarization propagator, discussing under which perturbative scheme they lead to identical results (if B is not too large). We finally address the issue of the spontaneous symmetry breaking for a system of neutrons using the technique of the anomalous propagator: in this framework we recover the Stoner equation and the critical values of the interaction corresponding to a transition to a ferromagnetic phase.

# Mean field at finite temperature and symmetry breaking

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock (HF) theory at finite temperature reproduces the zero-temperature Feynman theory in the T→0 limit only when no symmetry breaking occurs; when symmetry is broken (either explicitly by an external magnetic field or spontaneously), the cancellation of higher-order HF diagrams no longer holds, requiring more iterations to reach self-consistency and making the Matsubara and Feynman frameworks yield different results unless the external field is not too large.

## Method

The authors study an infinite homogeneous system of nucleons interacting through a central spin-isospin schematic exchange force, solving the HF equations numerically through iterative self-consistency at finite temperature using Matsubara Green's functions. They compare results for zero-range and finite-range Yukawa-type interactions, and examine both explicit symmetry breaking (via a constant uniform magnetic field B acting on neutrons) and spontaneous symmetry breaking (using the anomalous propagator technique to recover the Stoner equation for ferromagnetic phase transitions).

## Key numbers

- Fermi temperature for the finite-range interaction with λ=140 MeV and V₁=50 MeV·fm³: k_B T_F = 34.48 MeV (β_F = 0.029 MeV⁻¹)
- Fermi temperature for the same interaction with V₁=150 MeV·fm³: k_B T_F = 26.7 MeV (β_F = 0.037 MeV⁻¹)
- Density used in numerical calculations: ρ = 0.17 nucleons/fm³
- Fermi momentum: k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹
- The first-order self-energy at T=0 at the Fermi surface yields a constant shift of 3.89 MeV for the case with λ=140 MeV and V₁=50 MeV·fm³
- For a contact interaction, the critical interaction strength V_crit from ring approximation matches the generalized Hartree result, while RPA yields a value lower by a factor of 2/3

## Context

This work extends the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward (KLW) theorem (Koh60, Lut60) from the ground-state energy to the single-particle propagator, showing that the cancellation of higher-order HF diagrams holds strictly only at T=0 for finite-range forces but remains operative over a wide temperature range up to near T_F. The study of spontaneous symmetry breaking recovers the Stoner equation and critical interaction values previously found in Huang (1998) and uses the anomalous propagator technique from Matuszewski (1968). The results enable quantitative assessment of when finite-temperature HF calculations can be reliably approximated by first-order perturbation theory and establish the range of magnetic field strengths for which linear response theory can recover the T=0 Feynman results despite symmetry breaking.
