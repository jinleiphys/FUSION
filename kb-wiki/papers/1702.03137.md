# Methods for analysis of two-particle rapidity correlation function in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Two-particle rapidity (or pseudorapidity) correlation function $C(y_1, y_2)$ was used in analysing fluctuation of particle density distribution in rapidity in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. In our research, we argue that for a centrality window, some additional correlation may be caused by a centrality span, when the mean two- and single-particle densities over a centrality window are used directly in the calculation , just like $\left<N(y_1, y_2) \right> / \left[\left<N(y_1)\right>\left<N(y_2)\right>\right]$. We concentrate on removing the influence of collision-centrality span on correlation function, and two calculation methods are raised. In one method, correlation coefficients are considered to be the ratios of probabilities (not the particle density). In the other method, a relative multiplicity is introduced to unity the events of different centralities. For testing the methods, {\sc ampt} model is used and a toy granular model is built to simulate the fluctuation of particle density in rapidity.

## Key claim

The authors argue that when a centrality window is not narrow enough, using the mean two- and single-particle densities directly in the standard ratio formula for the two-particle rapidity correlation function introduces an additional positive correlation due to the mixing of events of different centralities, and they propose two alternative calculation methods to remove or reduce this centrality-span effect.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate the additional positive correlation using the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, comparing the correlation function for centrality windows 5%-15%, 5%-10%, and 10%-15% calculated with the standard density-ratio formula. They then develop two methods: the C_definition method, which computes the correlation function from the ratio of same-event pair probability density to mixed-event pair probability density; and the C_relative method, which introduces a relative multiplicity (particle count in a bin divided by a reference multiplicity) to unify events of different centralities, with a Gaussian correction for reference multiplicity fluctuations. Both methods are tested against the AMPT model and a toy granular model that simulates event-by-event rapidity density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- AMPT model of Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with partonic scattering cross section of 3 mb is used for testing.
- For the granular model, parameters are set to μg = 10 (expectation of number of particles per granule) and σy = 1.0 (half-width of granule rapidity distribution).
- More than 2 × 10^5 random events are generated with the granular model.
- In the C_relative method, the correction factor (1 + 1/⟨Nref⟩) appears in the formula, where Nref is the reference multiplicity.
- For the AMPT model, the correlation function of the 5%-15% centrality window lies between those of the 5%-10% and 10%-15% sub-windows when using the C_average and C_relative methods, as expected.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-particle rapidity correlation functions used in previous studies of event-by-event rapidity density fluctuations (He et al., 2013; Qian et al., 2016), where Legendre polynomial decomposition of the correlation function yields the variance parameters ⟨a_n²⟩. The authors identify a systematic bias in the standard density-ratio formula when applied to finite centrality windows, which was previously addressed by dividing events into narrow centrality intervals (Qian et al., 2016). The proposed C_definition and C_relative methods enable more accurate extraction of the correlation function and the derived ⟨a_n²⟩ moments from data with broader centrality bins, and the granular model provides a controlled testbed for validating these analysis techniques.
