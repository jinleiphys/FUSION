# Refinement of the $n-α$ and $p-α$ fish-bone potential

> The fishbone potential of composite particles simulates the Pauli effect by nonlocal terms. We determine the $n-α$ and $p-α$ fish-bone potential by simultaneously fitting to the experimental phase shifts. We found that with a double Gaussian parametrization of the local potential can describe the $n-α$ and $p-α$ phase shifts for all partial waves.

# Refinement of the $n-\alpha$ and $p-\alpha$ fish-bone potential

## Key claim
The authors claim that a double Gaussian parametrization of the local potential, with only six parameters and no explicit angular momentum dependence, can simultaneously describe the $n-\alpha$ and $p-\alpha$ phase shifts for all partial waves, provided the composite structure and Pauli principle are properly built into the nonlocal fish-bone terms. The hedge is that this claim applies to low-energy scattering data, and the fit quality is described as "very good" rather than perfect.

## Method
The authors adopted the fish-bone model, which incorporates the Pauli principle through nonlocal terms that remove or suppress Pauli-forbidden states in the relative motion Hilbert space. They solved the resulting Schrödinger-like equation using a Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation expanded in Coulomb-Sturmian basis functions. The local part of the potential was parametrized as a double Gaussian plus a spin-orbit term, and the six parameters (harmonic oscillator width $a$, two Gaussian strengths $v_1$, $v_2$, two Gaussian ranges $\alpha_1$, $\alpha_2$, and spin-orbit strength $v_{so}$) were fitted simultaneously to experimental $n-\alpha$ and $p-\alpha$ phase shifts for the $\mbox{}^{2}S_{1/2}$, $\mbox{}^{2}P_{1/2}$, and $\mbox{}^{2}P_{3/2}$ partial waves.

## Key numbers
- Harmonic oscillator width parameter: $a = 0.214\:\mbox{fm}^{-2}$
- Double Gaussian parameters: $v_1 = -84.068\:\mbox{MeV}$, $\alpha_1 = 0.3199\:\mbox{fm}^{-2}$, $v_2 = 81.66\:\mbox{MeV}$, $\alpha_2 = 1.7719\:\mbox{fm}^{-2}$
- Spin-orbit coupling strength: $v_{so} = -0.28497$ (dimensionless)
- Fish-bone $\epsilon$ parameter set to $\epsilon = 60000\:\mbox{MeV}$, with dependence beyond the fifth significant digit
- Norm kernel eigenvalues: in $\mbox{}^{2}S_{1/2}$ channel: $1, 1/16, 1/16^2, \ldots$; in $\mbox{}^{2}P_{1/2}$ and $\mbox{}^{2}P_{3/2}$ channels: $-1/4, -1/(4\cdot16), -1/(4\cdot16)^2, \ldots$

## Context
This work extends the fish-bone model approach previously applied to the $\alpha-\alpha$ system by the same authors (Smith, Woodhouse, Papp, 2012), where a single Gaussian local term sufficed. It refines the earlier $n-\alpha$ and $p-\alpha$ fish-bone potential of Hahn (1990), which used a Wood-Saxon shape with spin-orbit term but required independent parameters for each partial wave and different spin-orbit strengths for $\mbox{}^{2}P_{1/2}$ and $\mbox{}^{2}P_{3/2}$. The present result enables future studies of three-body systems such as $\alpha-\alpha-n$ and $\alpha-\alpha-p$, as the authors state in a forthcoming work.
