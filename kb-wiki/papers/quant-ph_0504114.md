# On the Original Proof by Reductio ad Absurdum of the Hohenberg-Kohn Theorem for Many-Electron Coulomb Systems

> It is shown that, for isolated many-electron Coulomb systems with Coulombic external potentials, the usual reductio ad absurdum proof of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem is unsatisfactory since the to-be-refuted assumption made about the one-electron densities and the assumption about the external potentials are not compatible with the Kato cusp condition. The theorem is, however, provable by more sophisticated means and it is shown here that the Kato cusp condition actually leads to a satisfactory proof.

# On the Original Proof by Reductio ad Absurdum of the Hohenberg-Kohn Theorem for Many-Electron Coulomb Systems

## Key claim
The original reductio ad absurdum proof of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem is unsatisfactory for isolated many-electron Coulomb systems with Coulombic external potentials, because the assumption that two different external potentials can yield the same ground-state one-electron density is incompatible with the Kato cusp condition. However, the theorem itself remains correct and can be proved using the Kato theorem directly.

## Method
The author analyzes the logical structure of the original Hohenberg-Kohn proof, identifying four possible cases (I-IV) that follow from the contradiction in Eq. (8). For case IV, the author demonstrates that the to-be-refuted assumptions (i) and (ii) are incompatible with assumption (5) for Coulombic external potentials, specifically because the Kato cusp condition forces a one-to-one correspondence between the cusp positions in the ground-state density and the nuclear positions and charges. The author then shows that the Kato theorem itself provides a valid proof: the electron-nucleus cusp condition (Eq. 11) allows one to uniquely determine the Coulombic external potential from the ground-state one-electron density by locating the cusps and evaluating their radial logarithmic derivatives.

## Key numbers
- The Kato cusp condition for the one-electron density: d/dr ρ_Ψ^av(r) |_{r=R_α} = -2Z_α ρ_Ψ(R_α), where Z_α is the nuclear charge at position R_α (in atomic units).
- The external Coulomb potential is of the form v(r) = -Σ_{α=1}^M Z_α / |r - R_α|, with M nuclei each at position R_α with charge Z_α.
- The domain of external potentials is L^{3/2}(R^3) + L^∞(R^3), to which any Coulomb potential or finite linear combination of Coulomb potentials belongs.

## Context
This work extends the foundational analysis of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964) by identifying a previously overlooked incompatibility in its original proof when restricted to Coulombic external potentials. The author builds on the Kato theorem (Kato, 1957) regarding electron-nucleus cusp conditions, and references earlier proposals by Coleman (1960s), Bamzai and Deb (1980s), Smith (1980s), and E. Bright Wilson (quoted by Lowdin, 1980s) that the density cusps determine the external potential. The paper enables a rigorous foundation for density functional theory for Coulomb systems, and connects to the Levy-Lieb energy density functional (Levy, 1979; Lieb, 1983) and the local-scaling deformation approach (Kryachko and Ludena, 1990s).
