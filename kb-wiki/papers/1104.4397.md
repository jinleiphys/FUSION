# A new approach to investigate dineutron correlation and its application to $^{10}$Be

> We propose a new framework by means of the dineutron condensate (DC) wave function to describe the dineutron correlation, which is characterized by the spatially strong correlation of a spin-zero neutron-neutron pair, in neutron-rich nuclei with an active deformed core surrounded by valence neutrons. Using the DC wave function for a 2$α$+2n system, which corresponds to a toy model for the $^{10}$Be system, we investigate the neutron-neutron correlation around the $2α$ core and discuss the mechanism of the dineutron formation at the surface of finite nuclei. To investigate dineutron correlations in realistic nuclear systems, we superpose the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave functions and the DC wave functions. Applying the AMD+DC method to $^{10}$Be, we show effects of the DC wave functions in the ground and excited $0^+$ states of $^{10}$Be and discuss the dineutron correlation in them.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the dineutron correlation enhancement at the nuclear surface arises from a combination of Pauli repulsion from the core (which creates a kinetic energy barrier) and additional attraction from the core, rather than solely from the neutron-neutron interaction; this mechanism is demonstrated in a toy 2α+2n model for ¹⁰Be but is suggested to be general.

## Method

The authors construct a dineutron condensate (DC) wave function for a 2α+2n system, where two α particles form a deformed core and two valence neutrons are described as a spin-zero pair with a Gaussian relative wave function and a Gaussian center-of-mass distribution around the core. They then superpose these DC wave functions with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave functions for ¹⁰Be (the AMD+DC method), projecting to 0⁺ states and solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the ground and excited states. The energy of the dineutron is analyzed as a function of the dineutron size parameter \(b_n\) and the spread parameter \(\beta\) for fixed α-α distances \(d\).

## Key numbers

- The energy minimum for the 2α+2n system occurs at \((\beta, b_n) \sim (1.9, 1.5)\) fm for the fixed α-α distance \(d = 2\) fm, which gives the lowest total energy.
- The favored dineutron size is \(b_n \sim 1.5\) fm, with a rapid energy increase when \(b_n\) deviates from this value.
- The common width parameter for all single-particle wave functions in the AMD part is \(\nu = 0.235\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- The Volkov No.2 central force parameters are \(v_1 = -60.65\) MeV, \(v_2 = 61.64\) MeV, \(a_1 = 1.80\) fm, \(a_2 = 1.01\) fm; the G3RS spin-orbit parameters are \(a_1 = 0.447\) fm, \(a_2 = 0.6\) fm, with \(v_1 = -v_2 = 1600\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dineutron correlations in neutron-rich nuclei, such as the HFB calculations by Matsuo (2005) for medium-heavy nuclei and three-body model studies of halo nuclei like ⁶He and ¹¹Li by Hagino (2005) and others. The new DC wave function approach allows treatment of deformed cores (unlike spherical-core three-body models) and is applied to ¹⁰Be as a first step; it enables systematic investigation of dineutron correlations across a range of neutron-rich nuclei with deformed structures.
