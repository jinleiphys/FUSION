# Ab Initio Excited States from the In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group

> We present two new methods for performing ab initio calculations of excited states for closed-shell systems within the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) framework. Both are based on combining the IMSRG with simple many-body methods commonly used to target excited states, such as the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) and equations-of-motion (EOM) techniques. In the first approach, a two-step sequential IMSRG transformation is used to drive the Hamiltonian to a form where a simple TDA calculation (i.e., diagonalization in the space of $1$p$1$h excitations) becomes exact for a subset of eigenvalues. In the second approach, equations-of-motion (EOM) techniques are applied to the ground-state-decoupled IMSRG Hamiltonian to access excited states. We perform proof-of-principle calculations for parabolic quantum dots in two-dimensions and the closed shell nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{22}$O. We find that the TDA-IMSRG approach gives better accuracy than the EOM-IMSRG when calculations converge, but is otherwise lacking the versatility and numerical stability of the latter. Our calculated spectra are in reasonable agreement with analogous EOM-coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) calculations, which paves the way for more interesting applications of the EOM-IMSRG to calculations of consistently evolved observables such as electromagnetic strength functions and nuclear matrix elements, and extensions to nuclei within 1-2 nucleons of a closed shell by generalizing the EOM ladder operator to include particle-number nonconserving terms.

## Key claim

The paper presents two new methods for calculating excited states of closed-shell nuclei within the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) framework, but the TDA-IMSRG approach, while more accurate when it converges, suffers from numerical instability and loss of unitarity due to truncation errors, whereas the EOM-IMSRG is more versatile and stable, though less accurate.

## Method

The first method (TDA-IMSRG) applies a two-step sequential IMSRG transformation: first a ground-state decoupling, then a second transformation that decouples different particle-hole excitation ranks so that a Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) diagonalization in the 1p1h space becomes exact for a subset of eigenvalues. The second method (EOM-IMSRG) applies equations-of-motion techniques with single- and double-excitation operators directly to the ground-state-decoupled IMSRG Hamiltonian, avoiding a second transformation and including a perturbative correction for omitted triple-excitation terms.

## Key numbers

* For the closed-shell nuclei 16O and 22O, the calculated spectra from both methods show reasonable agreement with analogous EOM-coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) calculations.
* The IMSRG(2) ground-state evolution is considered converged when the magnitude of the second-order perturbation theory contribution to the ground-state energy is less than 10^-6 MeV.
* The TDA- and vTDA-IMSRG(2) evolution is considered converged when all excited states within the decoupled space satisfy |E_nu(n) - E_nu(n-1)| < 10^-6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG framework, previously used primarily for ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei (Hergert 2013, Hergert 2014, Hergert 2016), to directly target excited states, similar to how EOM techniques are used in coupled-cluster theory (Ekstrom 2014, Jansen 2012). The results pave the way for applications of the EOM-IMSRG to consistently evolved observables such as electromagnetic strength functions and nuclear matrix elements, and for extensions to nuclei within 1-2 nucleons of a closed shell by generalizing the EOM ladder operator to include particle-number nonconserving terms.
