# Oscillations above the barrier in the fusion of 28Si + 28Si

> Fusion cross sections of 28Si + 28Si have been measured in a range above the barrier with a very small energy step (DeltaElab = 0.5 MeV). Regular oscillations have been observed, best evidenced in the first derivative of the energy-weighted excitation function. For the first time, quite different behaviors (the appearance of oscillations and the trend of sub-barrier cross sections) have been reproduced within the same theoretical frame, i.e., the coupled-channel model using the shallow M3Y+repulsion potential. The calculations suggest that channel couplings play an important role in the appearance of the oscillations, and that the simple relation between a peak in the derivative of the energy-weighted cross section and the height of a centrifugal barrier is lost, and so is the interpretation of the second derivative of the excitation function as a barrier distribution for this system, at energies above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

Regular oscillations in the fusion excitation function of 28Si + 28Si above the Coulomb barrier have been observed for the first time with sufficient precision, and both these oscillations and the sub-barrier cross sections have been reproduced within the same coupled-channel model using the shallow M3Y+repulsion potential, but the simple relation between each oscillation peak and the height of a centrifugal barrier is lost due to strong coupling effects.

## Method

Fusion evaporation residues from 28Si + 28Si collisions were detected at θlab = 3° using an electrostatic beam separator at the XTU Tandem accelerator of LNL-INFN. Two separate runs measured the excitation function from approximately 62 to 78 MeV laboratory energy (above the barrier) with a very small energy step of ΔElab = 0.5 MeV, collecting at least 10,000 events per energy point to keep statistical errors at 1% or less. The absolute cross section scale was fixed by normalizing relative yields to previously published cross sections at corresponding energies.

## Key numbers

- Energy step: ΔElab = 0.5 MeV (corresponding to ΔEcm = 0.75 MeV for the derivative)
- Beam energy uncertainty: ±0.13% (≈90 keV at 70 MeV), with relative uncertainty a factor 3-4 lower when energy is monotonically decreased
- Two well-defined peaks in the derivative D(E) at Ec.m. ≈ 35 MeV and 36.5 MeV appear in both runs; a third peak at Ec.m. ≈ 33.5-34 MeV is clearly observed only in the second run
- The Ch10 calculation used a density radius R = 3.125 fm (modified from 3.135 fm) and imaginary potential diffuseness aw = 0.3 fm (modified from 0.2 fm), improving χ²/N from 1.7 to 1.2 for the sub-barrier data
- The experimental peaks near 33, 35, and 37 MeV correlate with angular momenta L = 16, 18, and 20 in the Ch10 calculation, but each peak receives contributions from several L-values

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of oscillatory structures in light heavy-ion fusion systems such as 12C + 12C, 12C + 16O, and 16O + 16O (Sperr, 1976; Kovar, 1979; Tserruya, 1978), and the theoretical analysis by Esbensen (2008) that suggested such oscillations could appear in 28Si + 28Si. Previous data on this system by Gary (1985) had energy steps and errors too large for clear conclusions, while Aguilera (2006) using the gamma-ray technique found no oscillations in the range Elab ≈ 58-100 MeV. The results enable testing the shallow M3Y+repulsion potential (Misicu, 2001) against the alternative hindrance model by Ichikawa (2012) that uses damping of collective excitations, and suggest a future experiment on the nearby system 30Si + 30Si to check the role of the oblate deformation of 28Si.
