# Covariant transport approach for strongly interacting partonic systems

> The dynamics of partons, hadrons and strings in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons (DQPM) matched to reproduce recent lattice-QCD results - including the partonic equation of state - in thermodynamic equilibrium. Scalar- and vector-interaction densities are extracted from the DQPM as well as effective scalar- and vector-mean fields for the partons. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks), respectively, obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality as well as energy-momentum conservation. Since the dynamical quarks and antiquarks become very massive close to the phase transition, the formed resonant 'pre-hadronic' color-dipole states ($q\bar{q}$ or $qqq$) are of high invariant mass, too, and sequentially decay to the groundstate meson and baryon octets increasing the total entropy. When applying the PHSD approach to Pb+Pb colllisions at 158 A$\cdot$GeV we find a significant effect of the partonic phase on the production of multi-strange antibaryons due to a slightly enhanced $s{\bar s}$ pair production from massive time-like gluon decay and a larger formation of antibaryons in the hadronization process.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, when applied to Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV, shows a significant effect of the partonic phase on the production of multi-strange antibaryons, but this effect arises not from a general enhancement of strangeness production but from a slightly enhanced \(s\bar{s}\) pair production from massive time-like gluon decay and a larger formation of antibaryons in the hadronization process.

## Method

The authors construct the PHSD approach by combining the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) for partons, which reproduces lattice QCD results for the equation of state, with off-shell transport equations and covariant transition rates for hadronization. The DQPM provides temperature-dependent effective masses and widths for gluons and quarks, as well as scalar and vector mean-field potentials extracted from the potential-energy density. The hadronization of partons into mesons and baryons is described by local covariant fusion rates that conserve four-momentum, flavor currents, and color neutrality, with the formed pre-hadronic color-dipole states subsequently decaying to ground-state hadrons.

## Key numbers

* The effective gluon mass \(M_g\) and width \(\gamma_g\), as well as quark mass \(M_q\) and width \(\gamma_q\), are plotted as functions of \(T/T_c\) (Fig. 1); these values are smaller than those in Andre (2005) due to updated lattice QCD calculations with smaller bare fermion masses.
* In central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV (impact parameter \(b=1\) fm), about 60% of the initial kinetic energy of nucleons is converted to partons and mesons within the first ~3 fm/c; the partonic phase ends at \(t > 9\) fm/c.
* Slightly more than 1500 partons are produced during the passage time of the nuclei in central Pb+Pb at 158 A·GeV (initial nucleon number is 416).
* The scalar parton density \(\rho_s\) at which partons hadronize dynamically is in the range \(\rho_s \approx 1\) to 2 fm\(^{-3}\).
* The width parameter \(b\) for the pre-hadron phase-space distribution is fixed by \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = b = 0.66\) fm (in the rest frame), corresponding to an average rms radius of mesons.

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework of Cassing (2006, 2007) and the earlier study of covariant transition rates in Cassing (2008) by updating the DQPM parameters to match the more precise lattice QCD calculations of Cheng (2008) for 2+1 flavors with almost physical quark masses. It also extends the earlier Hadron-Strings-Dynamics (HSD) approach by incorporating explicit partonic degrees of freedom and a dynamical hadronization process that conserves entropy, in contrast to simple coalescence models (e.g., Koal (2001), AMPT) which violate the second law of thermodynamics. The enhanced formation of strange antibaryons in central Pb+Pb collisions supports the statistical hadronization model suggestion of Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1999). The authors caution that the present PHSD realization describes a crossover transition, whereas a first-order phase transition and critical point are expected at lower SPS and FAIR energies, and that the critical temperature from Cheng (2008) may be revised by later lattice calculations from the Wuppertal group (2009).
