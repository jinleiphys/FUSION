# Nuclear Heavy Quark Photoproduction in a Saturation Model

> We calculate the nuclear inclusive and diffractive cross sections for heavy quark photoproduction within a phenomenological saturation approach. The nuclear cross section is obtained by the extension of the saturation model through Glauber-Gribov formalism. We predict large nuclear heavy quark cross section at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors predict large nuclear heavy quark cross sections at LHC energies, but they note that the nuclear heavy quark production is not strongly modified by high density effects because the process is dominated by small dipole configurations, meaning the nuclear cross sections can be reasonably approximated by A times the nucleon cross section (color transparency).

## Method

The authors extend the phenomenological saturation model for the nucleon (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) to nuclear targets using the Glauber-Gribov formalism, replacing the dipole-nucleon cross section with a nuclear dipole cross section that sums multiple elastic rescattering diagrams. They compute the inclusive and diffractive cross sections for charm and bottom photoproduction using the color dipole approach, where the photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair that then interacts with the target. The nuclear profile function is obtained from a 3-parameter Fermi distribution for the nuclear density, and the model contains no new parameters beyond those fitted to HERA data.

## Key numbers

- Charm photoproduction cross section on lead at W = 10^3 GeV: approximately 2 × 10^3 μb (2 mb)
- Bottom photoproduction cross section on lead at W = 10^3 GeV: approximately 40 μb (0.04 mb)
- Diffractive charm photoproduction cross section on lead at W = 10^3 GeV: approximately 250 μb
- Diffractive bottom photoproduction cross section on lead at W = 10^3 GeV: approximately 0.8 μb
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Bottom quark mass used: m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Saturation model parameters from 4-flavor fit: σ_0 = 29.12 mb, λ = 0.277, x_0 = 0.41 × 10^(-4)
- Diffractive cross section for open charm on proton: approximately 100 nb at W = 200 GeV
- Inclusive cross section for charm on proton at W ≈ 200 GeV: underestimates HERA data by a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) to nuclear targets following the Glauber-Gribov extension proposed by Armesto (2002), and addresses heavy quark photoproduction specifically, which was not covered in the earlier nuclear analysis by Bartels and Levin (2002). The results agree with predictions from an approach resumming fan diagrams of BFKL pomerons (Braun and Armesto, 2002). The work enables predictions for heavy quark production at RHIC and LHC energies and suggests that diffractive cross sections, with their A^(4/3) dependence, could be used to constrain nucleon-level saturation dynamics.
