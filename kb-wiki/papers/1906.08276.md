# Electromagnetic stress on nucleon structure

> External electromagnetic fields can provoke stress, and thus modifications of the internal structure of nucleons. Working with this hypothesis, one can derive a simple description of the charge dependence of the EMC effect. This first result is confirmed by two explicit models of the structure functions of deformed nucleons in the atomic nucleus. For large nuclei a continuous model is used. For small nuclei a discrete distribution of nuclear matter gives better results.

## Key claim

External electromagnetic fields can provoke stress and modifications of the internal structure of nucleons, and this mechanism provides a simple description of the charge dependence of the EMC effect. The author notes that this is a "working hypothesis" and that the first result is "confirmed by two explicit models," with the hedge that for large nuclei a continuous model is used while for small nuclei a discrete distribution gives better results.

## Method

The author derives a linear expansion of the structure function ratio in Bjorken x, where the slope depends on the average electric force within the nucleus, which scales as Z^(1/3) from Gauss's law assuming constant charge density. This prediction is tested against EMC ratio data from eight nuclei (He, Be, C, Al, Ca, Fe, Ag, Au) using data from Gomez (1993). The author then implements the electromagnetic stress idea in the Edin model (Edin 1998, Edin 1999) for the nucleon structure function, dividing nucleons in a large nucleus into three categories (stretched, undeformed, squeezed) with different longitudinal radii, and fits the iron EMC data with a deformation parameter delta and three probability parameters.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic dipole moment is given by p = 4 pi alpha_E E, with alpha_E approximately 10^(-3) fm^3 from measurements at very low Q^2 (Holstein 2013)
- The relative deformation between two neighboring protons is estimated as delta = Delta r / r ~ alpha_E / r_p^3 ~ 0.001
- For aluminum, the linear fit gives Delta^27,13_EMC(x) = (1.07 +/- 0.01) - (0.26 +/- 0.02) * x, with x_0 = 0.27 +/- 0.02
- The best fit for the charge dependence of the slope gives g' = 0.11 +/- 0.02 and d' = 0.072 +/- 0.007
- The Edin model parameter is sigma_tilde = 1/(r_p m_p) = 0.213
- The best fit for iron uses deformation delta = 0.17 with probabilities p_a = 0.50, p_b = 0.24, p_c = 0.26
- The deformation as a function of charge follows delta(Z) = 0.05 * Z^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the study of the EMC effect, first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (Aubert 1983), by proposing a new mechanism based on electromagnetic stress rather than the existing explanations such as nuclear binding (Akulinichev 1985), pion excess (Ericson 1983), multi-quark clustering (Jaffe 1982), dynamical rescaling (Nachtmann 1983, Close 1983), medium modification (Bentz 2001, Cloet 2006), or short-range correlations (Ciofi Degli Atti 1989, Weinstein 2010). The author notes that the charge dependence of the EMC effect was previously observed by Gomez (1993) but that the electromagnetic stress perspective has not been found in the literature. The work enables future tests of whether the deformation parameter delta follows the predicted Z^(1/3) scaling across more nuclei and at different Q^2 values.
