# Goldstone Theorem and Diquark Confinement Beyond Rainbow-Ladder Approximation

> The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equation are studied in a truncation scheme that extends the rainbow-ladder approximation such that, in the chiral limit, the isovector, pseudoscalar meson remains massless. Quark-quark (diquark) correlations, which are bound in rainbow-ladder approximation, are destabilised by repulsive contributions that only appear at higher order in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The net effect of higher order terms on the meson bound-state masses is small.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that extending the rainbow-ladder truncation of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equation to next order (O(G^2)) preserves the Goldstone theorem for the pion in the chiral limit, while the repulsive terms that appear at this order destabilize diquark bound-states that were present in the rainbow-ladder approximation. The net effect of these higher-order terms on meson masses is small, but the diquark channels become unbound.

## Method
The authors study the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) and the meson and diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) using a truncation scheme that extends beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation. They employ a model gluon propagator that is singular in the infrared with no Lehmann representation, and solve the quark DSE at O(G^2) including vertex corrections. The Bethe-Salpeter kernel is constructed at the same order by a systematic replacement procedure that ensures Goldstone's theorem is preserved, and the bound-state equations are solved for meson and diquark channels in the chiral limit and with a nonzero current-quark mass.

## Key numbers
- In the chiral limit (m_q=0), the pseudoscalar meson mass is 0 at O(G):O(G) and O(G^2):O(G^2), demonstrating Goldstone theorem preservation; at O(G):O(G^2) it becomes 0.30 GeV.
- The vector meson mass in the chiral limit is 0.750 GeV at O(G):O(G), 0.745 GeV at O(G):O(G^2), and 0.823 GeV at O(G^2):O(G^2).
- Diquark masses in the chiral limit are 1.19 GeV (0+) and 1.30 GeV (1+) at O(G):O(G), but become "Unbound" at both O(G):O(G^2) and O(G^2):O(G^2).
- With m_q=0.012 GeV, the pseudoscalar meson mass is 0.140 GeV at O(G):O(G), 0.328 GeV at O(G):O(G), and 0.136 GeV at O(G^2):O(G^2).
- The vector meson mass with m_q=0.012 GeV is 0.767 GeV at O(G):O(G), 0.760 GeV at O(G):O(G^2), and 0.770 GeV at O(G^2):O(G^2).
- The model gluon propagator parameter is set to eta=1.06 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the rainbow-ladder approximation studies of meson and diquark spectroscopy (summarized in the DSE review by Roberts and collaborators) by including the first correction beyond that truncation. The rainbow-ladder approach, which had been successful in describing meson spectra and decays (Jain and Munczek, 1993), had the defect of admitting (bar 3)_c diquark bound-states (Praschifka, Cahill, and Roberts, 1989) that are not observed. The present paper shows that the repulsive terms appearing at O(G^2) eliminate these diquark bound-states while preserving the Goldstone boson nature of the pion, and explains why the rainbow-ladder approximation worked well for mesons due to near-cancellation of attractive and repulsive terms. This enables future systematic studies of hadron spectroscopy with controlled truncations that respect chiral symmetry and eliminate spurious colored bound-states.
