# Two-Proton Radioactivity with 2p halo in light mass nuclei A$=$18$-$34

> Two-proton radioactivity with 2p halo is reported theoretically in light mass nuclei A $=$ 18-34. We predict $^{19}$Mg, $^{22}$Si, $^{26}$S, $^{30}$Ar and $^{34}$Ca as promising candidates of ground state 2p-radioactivity with S$_{2p}$ $<$ 0 and S$_{p}$ $>$ 0. Observation of extended tail of spatial charge density distribution, larger charge radius and study of proton single particle states, Fermi energy and the wave functions indicate 2p halo like structure which supports direct 2p emission. The Coulomb and centrifugal barriers in experimentally identified 2p unbound $^{22}$Si show a quasi-bound state that ensures enough life time for such experimental probes. Our predictions are in good accord with experimental and other theoretical data available so far.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that five light-mass nuclei, ¹⁹Mg, ²²Si, ²⁶S, ³⁰Ar, and ³⁴Ca, are promising candidates for ground-state two-proton radioactivity, but the claim is hedged by noting that for ³⁴Ca and ²⁶S the relativistic mean-field calculations show an anomaly (S₂ₚ > 0 for ³⁴Ca and for ²⁶S with one parameter set), whereas experimental data and other theories indicate S₂ₚ < 0, requiring further investigation.

## Method
The authors employed two theoretical formalisms: (i) the Relativistic Mean-Field plus state-dependent BCS (RMF+BCS) approach with TMA, NL3*, NLSH, and PK1 parameter sets, and (ii) the Macroscopic-Microscopic approach with the Nilsson-Strutinsky prescription. They calculated one- and two-proton separation energies for proton-rich Mg, Si, S, Ar, and Ca isotopes near the proton drip line, and analyzed charge density distributions, charge radii, proton single-particle states, Fermi energies, and wave functions to search for halo-like structures and quasi-bound states.

## Key numbers
- ¹⁹Mg, ²²Si, ²⁶S, ³⁰Ar, and ³⁴Ca are identified as 2p-unbound with S₂ₚ < 0 and Sₚ > 0 in the mass range A = 18–34.
- The gap between the 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ proton single-particle states in ²²Si is 1.6 MeV (using NL3*), compared to about 6 MeV in ³⁴Si; similar reductions are seen with TMA (1.9 MeV) and NLSH (2.5 MeV).
- The occupancy of the proton 2s₁/₂ state in ²²Si is 0.33, whereas all other ²⁴–⁴⁸Si isotopes have zero occupancy in that state.
- Deformation parameters β for ¹⁹–²¹Mg are (0.23, 0.19, 0.28), for ²⁶–²⁸S are (0.26, 0.26, 0.26), and for ³⁰–³²Ar are (0.22, 0.21, 0.20); ²²Si and ³⁴Ca are spherical (β = 0.0).
- The binding energy per nucleon of ²²Si is 6.058 MeV, while that of its daughter ²⁰Mg is 6.724 MeV.

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of two-proton radioactivity and halo structure in light nuclei, following experimental observations of 2p emission in ¹⁹Mg (Mukha et al.), ²²Si (Xu et al.), and ³⁰Ar (Mukha et al.), as well as earlier theoretical work by Aggarwal et al. and Saxena et al. The paper provides structural evidence linking 2p halo formation to direct 2p emission, and suggests that deformation does not play a major role in 2p correlations for these nuclei, contrary to some earlier speculations. The results enable future experimental searches for 2p radioactivity in ²⁶S, ³⁰Ar, and ³⁴Ca, and call for further theoretical investigation of the anomalies found for ³⁴Ca and ²⁶S.
