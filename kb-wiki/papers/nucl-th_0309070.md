# Limiting fragmentation and $ϕ$ meson production at RHIC

> The PHOBOS's limiting fragmentation etc. three empirical scaling rules for charged multiplicity in $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC are investigated by a hadron and string cascade model LUCIAE. Similar studies are performed for the $ϕ$ meson exploring its production mechanism via comparing with the charged multiplicity. The LUCIAE results for charged multiplicity are compatible with PHOBOS observations. However, for the $ϕ$ meson the three empirical scaling rules are either kept only or kept better in the LUCIAE calculations without reduction mechanism of the $s$ quark suppression extra introduced for the strangeness in LUCIAE model. These results seem indicating a universal production mechanism for charged particle and $ϕ$ meson in string fragmentation regime. It is discussed that the PHOBOS's empirical scaling rules are model dependent indeed.

# Limiting fragmentation and φ meson production at RHIC

## Key claim

The three empirical scaling rules for charged particle production observed by PHOBOS (limiting fragmentation, similarity to e+e- collisions, and participant pair scaling) are model-dependent because they rely on the definition and calculation of the number of participant nucleons, which PHOBOS extracted using the HIJING model; for the φ meson, these scaling rules are either kept only or kept better in LUCIAE calculations without the extra reduction mechanism of s quark suppression.

## Method

The authors used the hadron and string cascade model LUCIAE (based on FRITIOF) to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies of √s_nn = 19.6, 130, and 200 GeV. They first adjusted the a and b parameters of the LUND string fragmentation function to match experimental charged multiplicity data, then applied those fitted parameters to study the three empirical scaling rules for both charged particles and φ mesons, comparing calculations with and without a reduction mechanism for s quark suppression in string fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Charged multiplicity (|η| ≤ 4.7) in 6% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 19.6 GeV: PHOBOS 1670 ± 100, LUCIAE (modified parameters) 1572
- Charged multiplicity (|η| ≤ 4.7) in 6% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 130 GeV: PHOBOS 4020 ± 200, LUCIAE (modified parameters) 4191
- Charged multiplicity (|η| ≤ 4.7) in 6% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 200 GeV: PHOBOS 4810 ± 240, LUCIAE (modified parameters) 4964
- φ meson rapidity density (-0.5 < y < 0.5) in Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 130 GeV: STAR 5.73 ± 1.06, LUCIAE (modified parameters) 5.08
- φ meson rapidity density (-0.5 < y < 0.5) in Au+Au collisions at √s_nn = 200 GeV: STAR 7.20 ± 0.40, LUCIAE (modified parameters) 8.02
- Fitted a and b parameters for LUND string fragmentation: a = 0.5, b = 0.38 at √s_nn = 19.6 GeV; a = 0.05, b = 1.16 at √s_nn = 130 GeV; a = 0.02, b = 1.46 at √s_nn = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the PHOBOS collaboration's observations (2001-2002) of three empirical scaling rules for charged multiplicity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and tests whether these rules also apply to φ meson production. The LUCIAE model had previously reproduced charged multiplicity and enhanced strange baryon production at SPS energies (Sa et al., 2002). The results suggest a universal production mechanism for charged particles and φ mesons in the string fragmentation regime, and enable further investigation of whether the empirical scaling rules are truly universal or depend on the model used to extract participant number. The authors note that PHOBOS extracted <N_part> based on the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991), making the scaling rules model-dependent.
