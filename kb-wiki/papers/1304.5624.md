# Exotic hadron holography from anomalous dimensions

> The anomalous dimensions of hadronic interpolators contain dynamical information on the properties of the associated hadron states. We point out that they provide, in particular, a link by which gauge-invariant information on exotic contributions to hadronic wavefunctionals can be obtained from approximate gravity duals for QCD. This is demonstrated by the holographic description of a dominant tetraquark component in the lightest scalar mesons.

# Exotic hadron holography from anomalous dimensions

## Key claim

The anomalous dimensions of hadronic interpolators provide a gauge-invariant link by which information on exotic (multiquark) contributions to hadronic wavefunctionals can be obtained from approximate gravity duals for QCD, demonstrated specifically for a dominant tetraquark component in the lightest scalar mesons. The mechanism is conditional on the anomalous dimension satisfying a lower bound of γ ≥ −4 to prevent collapse of the dual modes into the AdS₅ boundary.

## Method

The author implements anomalous dimensions of hadronic interpolators as mass corrections in the five-dimensional bulk modes of an AdS/QCD dilaton soft-wall gravity dual. For tetraquarks, the anomalous dimension γ(z) of the local four-quark operator J_{q̄²q²} adds a universal contribution ΔV(z) = γ(z)[γ(z)+8]/z² to the Sturm-Liouville potential in the radial bulk equation, with the scaling dimension set to Δ_{q̄²q²} = 6+γ(z). A power ansatz γ(z) = −az^η + bz^κ is adopted to estimate quantitative impacts until direct QCD information on the renormalization group flow of γ becomes available.

## Key numbers

- The baryon excitation spectrum is given by M_{n,L}² = 4λ²(n+L+3/2) − 2(M_Δ² − M_N²)κ, where λ is the IR scale of the metric soft-wall gravity dual, n is the radial excitation level, L is the angular momentum, and κ is the "good-diquark fraction"
- The lower bound on the tetraquark mass is M_{q̄²q²,0} ≥ M_{Δ=2,0} = 2λ, saturated when γ ≡ −4
- The lightest tetraquark mass drops from approximately 40% above the q̄q ground-state mass (for γ ≡ 0) down to approximately 20% below M_{q̄q,0} = √6 λ when near-maximal binding is produced
- Tetraquark excitations M_{q̄²q²,n} are pushed beyond the corresponding M_{q̄q,n} from around n ≳ 2

## Context

This work extends the author's prior holographic description of diquark correlations in baryons (Forkel 2009, 2010) to the more challenging case of exotic hadrons, specifically the light scalar meson sector where a tetraquark component was expected (Jaffe 1977; Close 2002). The anomalous-dimension-induced binding mechanism is model-independent as it arises from the AdS/CFT dictionary's prescription for the mass term, and should therefore appear in other AdS/QCD duals as well. The approach enables future extension to other exotics including heavy tetraquarks, pentaquarks, and hybrids, and opens exploration of other aspects of hadronic structure encoded in anomalous-dimension corrections.
