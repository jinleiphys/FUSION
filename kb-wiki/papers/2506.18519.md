# Nuclear and neutron matter in the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory with next-to-leading order covariant chiral nuclear force

> The symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are investigated by the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with the covariant chiral nuclear forces up to the next-to-leading order~(NLO). A fitting scheme to ensure the naturalness of the low-energy constants is proposed, which plays a crucial role in the proper description of nuclear matter. With a momentum cutoff $Λ=590$ MeV, the empirical saturation energy and density, as well as the incompressibility coefficient at the saturation density are reproduced well. The EoSs show less dependence on the momentum cutoff and become softer at densities above saturation density, in comparison with the previous leading order results. Given the good description for the saturation properties of nuclear matter, the present work encourages future studies of the finite nuclei in the framework of the RBHF theory with the NLO covariant chiral nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with next-to-leading order (NLO) covariant chiral nuclear forces can reproduce the empirical saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, but only when the low-energy constants (LECs) are fitted with a naturalness constraint; without this constraint, the LECs become unnaturally large and the RBHF calculations for nuclear matter fail to converge.

## Method

The authors solved the Thompson equation for the in-medium G-matrix using covariant chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to NLO, regularized with a Gaussian-like cutoff function. The LECs were determined by fitting to PWA93 neutron-proton phase shifts for partial waves with J ≤ 2 at laboratory kinetic energies of 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV, using a three-step fitting procedure that included a constraint term to enforce naturalness of the LECs. The self-consistent RBHF equations were then solved for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at various densities.

## Key numbers

- For momentum cutoff Λ = 590 MeV, the energy per nucleon at saturation density is -16.05 MeV, in agreement with the empirical value of -16 ± 1 MeV (Bethe 1971)
- For Λ = 590 MeV, the saturation density ρ₀ = 0.167 fm⁻³, in agreement with the empirical value of 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³ (Bethe 1971)
- For Λ = 590 MeV, the incompressibility coefficient K∞ at saturation density is 270 MeV, compared to the empirical value of 240 ± 20 MeV (Garg 2018)
- The momentum cutoff uncertainty in the NLO calculation of the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter is reduced by half compared to the LO uncertainty, with a residual uncertainty of approximately 16 MeV at saturation density
- For pure neutron matter at ρ = 0.25 fm⁻³, the momentum cutoff uncertainty is about 10% at NLO, compared to about 30% at LO

## Context

This work extends the previous LO covariant chiral nuclear force calculations in RBHF theory (Zou 2023) to NLO, demonstrating systematic order-by-order convergence. Unlike non-relativistic chiral nuclear forces, where saturation properties require three-nucleon forces at N²LO (Sammarruca 2021), the relativistic approach with NLO covariant chiral forces can describe nuclear saturation reasonably well using only two-nucleon forces. The results encourage future studies of finite nuclei in the RBHF framework with NLO covariant chiral nuclear forces.
