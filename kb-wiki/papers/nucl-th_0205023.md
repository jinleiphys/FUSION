# Calculation of strongly-coupled rotational bands in terms of the tilted axis cranking model

> Recently observed strongly-coupled rotational bands associated with the $ν[505]{11/2}^-$ quasiparticle state are studied by means of a microscopic tilted axis cranking (TAC) model. The results of calculation for the routhians and the $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios are investigated in the light of other existing models, i.e. the strong-coupling model and the conventional cranking model. It is demonstrated that only the TAC model can successfully reproduce these two observables at the same time. The reason of the success is clarified by making connections between these models.

## Key claim

The Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) model can simultaneously reproduce both the routhians and the B(M1)/B(E2) ratios for strongly-coupled rotational bands associated with the ν[505]11/2⁻ quasiparticle state, while the strong-coupling model and the conventional Principal Axis Cranking (PAC) model each fail to reproduce one of these observables. The success depends on using constant pairing gaps (reduced by factors 0.7 and 0.9 from experimental even-odd mass differences for neutrons and protons respectively) rather than selfconsistent pairing calculations.

## Method

The authors applied the two-dimensional TAC model using a Nilsson single-particle potential with monopole pairing, keeping deformation parameters and pairing gaps fixed while determining the chemical potential by the number condition. They calculated routhians and B(M1)/B(E2) ratios for the ν[505]11/2⁻ band in ¹⁵³Sm, ¹⁵⁵Gd, ¹⁵⁷Gd, ¹⁵⁷Dy, and ¹⁵⁹Dy, comparing results with the strong-coupling model and the conventional PAC model. The selfconsistent tilting angle θ_J was determined at each rotational frequency by minimizing the routhian with respect to the tilting angle of the frequency vector.

## Key numbers

- The selfconsistent TAC solutions for the ν[505]11/2⁻ band in ¹⁵⁷Gd are energetically favoured compared to conventional PAC solutions (θ_ω = 90°) by about 300–400 keV
- The calculated moment of inertia is systematically smaller than experimental values by about 10% across all nuclei studied
- The ratio |⟨∑(ẑx̂)ₐ⟩|/|⟨Q̂₀⟩| is less than 5 × 10⁻³ at all rotational frequencies in the calculations
- The band head frequency ω_BH in the strong-coupling model is given by ω_BH = K/𝒥, where K = 11/2 for the ν[505]11/2⁻ band
- The rotational bands were measured up to high-spin states with I ≲ 22ℏ for the strongly-coupled band and I ≲ 25ℏ for the positive- and negative-parity yrast bands

## Context

This work extends the TAC model developed by Frauendorf (1993, 2000, 2001) to strongly-coupled high-K rotational bands, which were previously described by the Bohr-Mottelson strong-coupling model (1975) and the conventional cranking model (Cranked Shell Model, Bengtsson and Frauendorf 1979). The calculations are compared with recent experimental data from JAERI on ¹⁵³Sm (Hayakawa 2000), ¹⁵⁵Gd (Hayakawa 1999), ¹⁵⁷Gd (Hayakawa 2000), ¹⁵⁷Dy (Hayakawa 2000), and ¹⁵⁹Dy (Sugawara 2001). The results demonstrate that the TAC model can describe both the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition properties of these bands, enabling future studies of high-spin phenomena in well-deformed nuclei where the simple strong-coupling model fails due to changes in moment of inertia and alignment effects.
