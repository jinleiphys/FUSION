# Ground state properties of rare-earth nuclei in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

> The relativistic mean-field effective interaction with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings DD-ME1 is tested in the calculation of deformed nuclei. Ground-state properties of six isotopic chains ($60\le Z \le 70$) in the region of rare-earth nuclei are calculated by using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-ME1 mean-field interaction, and with the Gogny D1S force for the pairing interaction. Results of fully self-consistent RHB calculations for the total binding energies, charge isotope shifts and quadrupole deformation parameters are compared with the available empirical data.

# Ground state properties of rare-earth nuclei in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

## Key claim

The DD-ME1 density-dependent relativistic mean-field interaction, when used in the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with Gogny D1S pairing, reproduces experimental ground-state properties of rare-earth nuclei with high accuracy, but the maximum deviation from experimental binding energies reaches 0.2% for five specific nuclei near the N=82 shell closure, rather than the sub-0.1% level seen for most isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent RHB calculations for six even-Z isotopic chains (Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Yb; Z=60-70) using the DD-ME1 density-dependent meson-nucleon interaction in the particle-hole channel and the Gogny D1S force for pairing correlations. The Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov equations and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields were solved by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, using 12 oscillator shells for nucleon fields and 20 for meson fields.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energies: maximum deviation below 0.1% for all isotopes except 142Nd, 144Sm, 146Gd, 148Dy, and 150Er, where deviation is 0.2%
- Charge isotope shifts: for 164Dy, the deviation of the theoretical charge radius from the empirical value is only 0.3%
- Charge radius calculation uses a Gaussian proton-charge distribution with rms radius of 0.8 fm, and the relation r_c = sqrt(r_p^2 + 0.64) fm
- Reference nuclei for isotope shifts: N=82 isotope for Nd, Sm, Gd, Er chains; 156Dy for Dy chain; 168Yb for Yb chain

## Context

This work extends the DD-ME1 effective interaction, previously tested on spherical Sn and Pb isotopic chains by Niksic et al. (2002), to deformed rare-earth nuclei for the first time. It builds on the density-dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) model developed by Fuchs, Lenske, and Wolter (1995) and the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework of Gonzalez (1996). The results demonstrate that density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings can match the performance of standard nonlinear interactions like NL3, which was used by Lalazissis, Ring, and Vretenar (1999) in a comprehensive study of 1315 even-even nuclei. This enables future application of DD-ME1 to exotic deformed nuclei far from stability, where the density-dependent formulation may offer advantages over nonlinear models.
