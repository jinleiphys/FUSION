# Structure and Coulomb dissociation of 23O within the quark-meson coupling model

> We study the ground-state structure of nuclei in the vicinity of the one-neutron dripline within the latest version of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with a particular emphasis on 23O. For this nucleus the model predicts a [22O(0+) + n (2s_{1/2})] configuration for its ground state, with a one neutron separation energy in close agreement with the corresponding experimental value. The wave function describing the valence neutron-core relative motion was then used to calculate the Coulomb dissociation of 23O on a lead target at a beam energy of 422 MeV/nucleon. The experimental neutron-core relative energy spectrum and the total one-neutron removal cross sections are well described by the calculations. The widths of the longitudinal momentum distributions of the 22O fragment are found to be broad, which do not support the formation of a neutron halo in this nucleus.

## Key claim

The QMC-III model predicts a ground-state configuration of \(^{22}\text{O}(0^+)\otimes n(2s_{1/2})\) for \(^{23}\text{O}\) with a one-neutron separation energy of 2.86 MeV, which is close to the experimental value of 2.74 MeV; however, the calculated longitudinal momentum distributions of the \(^{22}\text{O}\) fragment are broad (FWHM about 85 MeV/c), which does not support the formation of a neutron halo in this nucleus despite the valence neutron occupying an s-orbit.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC-III) model, a relativistic mean-field approach where quarks in non-overlapping MIT bags interact self-consistently with \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, to calculate ground-state properties of neutron-rich nuclei near the one-neutron dripline. For \(^{23}\text{O}\), the valence neutron-core wave function from the QMC model was then used as input to a post-form finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory of Coulomb dissociation, which includes electromagnetic interactions to all orders and requires no knowledge of continuum states. The Coulomb dissociation of \(^{23}\text{O}\) on a lead target at 422 MeV/nucleon beam energy was calculated, and the results were compared to experimental data on the neutron-core relative energy spectrum and total one-neutron removal cross sections.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{23}\text{O}\) predicted by QMC-III: 2.86 MeV (experimental value: 2.74 ± 0.13 MeV).
* Neutron skin thickness in \(^{23}\text{O}\): 0.46 fm (difference between neutron and proton root-mean-square radii).
* Total electromagnetic one-neutron removal cross section for Coulomb dissociation of \(^{23}\text{O}\) on Pb at 422 MeV/nucleon: 79.26 mb (QMC-III wave function) and 77.15 mb (Woods-Saxon wave function); experimental value: 97 ± 10 mb.
* Full width at half maximum (FWHM) of the longitudinal momentum distribution of the \(^{22}\text{O}\) fragment: about 85 MeV/c (both QMC-III and Woods-Saxon wave functions).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{23}\text{N}\): -2.701 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -2.494 ± 0.360 MeV (experimental).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{31}\text{Ne}\): -0.004 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -0.290 ± 1.640 MeV (experimental).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{35}\text{Mg}\): -0.861 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -0.728 ± 0.463 MeV (experimental).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{37}\text{Na}\): -1.054 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -0.750 ± 0.180 MeV (experimental).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{45}\text{S}\): -1.592 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -2.208 ± 1.786 MeV (experimental).
* One-neutron separation energy for \(^{49}\text{Ar}\): -2.726 MeV (QMC-III) vs. -2.501 ± 0.590 MeV (experimental).

## Context

This work extends the application of the quark-meson coupling model (QMC-III), previously used for normal nuclei and hypernuclei (Gui et al., 2008), to light neutron-rich nuclei near the one-neutron dripline, a region not previously explored with this model. The results contradict the suggestion by Kanungo et al. (2001) that \(^{23}\text{O}\) is a neutron-halo candidate based on a 12% rise in interaction cross sections between \(^{22}\text{O}\) and \(^{23}\text{O}\), instead supporting the conclusion from later measurements (Gil et al., 2004; Nociforo et al., 2005) that the ground state has \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) and does not exhibit a halo. The study enables future microscopic investigations of breakup reactions for other dripline nuclei using the QMC model as a competitive alternative to non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock or relativistic mean-field approaches.
