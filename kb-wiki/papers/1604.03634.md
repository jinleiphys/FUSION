# Theoretical study of photoproduction of $η^{\prime} N$ bound state on deuteron target with forward proton emission

> Possibilities of observing a signal of an $η^{\prime} n$ bound state are investigated by considering photoproductions of the $η$ and $η^{\prime}$ mesons on a deuteron target with forward proton emission. For this purpose, we take the $η^{\prime} n$ interaction from the linear sigma model with a coupling to $ηn$, in which an $s$-wave $η^{\prime} n$ bound state can be dynamically generated, and we fix the $γp \to ηp$ and $η^{\prime} p$ scattering amplitudes so as to reproduce the experimental cross sections with forward proton emission. By using these $γp \to η^{( \prime )} p$ and $η^{( \prime )} n \to η^{( \prime )} n$ amplitudes, we calculate cross sections of the $γd \to ηn p$ and $η^{\prime} n p$ reactions with forward proton emission in single and $η^{(\prime )}$-exchange double scattering processes. As a result, we find that the signal of the $η^{\prime} n$ bound state can be seen below the $η^{\prime} n$ threshold in the $ηn$ invariant mass spectrum of the $γd \to ηn p$ reaction and is comparable with the contribution from the quasifree $η^{\prime}$ production above the $η^{\prime} n$ threshold. We also discuss the behavior of the signal of the $η^{\prime} n$ bound state in several experimental conditions and model parameters.

## Key claim

The signal of an $\eta^{\prime} n$ bound state can be observed below the $\eta^{\prime} n$ threshold in the $\eta n$ invariant mass spectrum of the $\gamma d \to \eta n p$ reaction, and this signal is comparable in magnitude to the quasifree $\eta^{\prime}$ production contribution above the $\eta^{\prime} n$ threshold, though the exact strength depends on the treatment of the exchanged meson energy (Watson vs. truncated Faddeev approach).

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for $\gamma d \to \eta n p$ and $\gamma d \to \eta^{\prime} n p$ reactions with forward proton emission, using a linear sigma model to generate the $\eta^{\prime} N$ interaction and its scattering amplitude in a coupled-channels $\eta^{\prime} N$-$\eta N$ framework. They fixed the $\gamma p \to \eta p$ and $\gamma p \to \eta^{\prime} p$ scattering amplitudes to reproduce experimental differential cross sections, then computed the deuteron reaction cross sections from single-scattering and double-scattering (with $\eta$ or $\eta^{\prime}$ exchange) diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta^{\prime} n$ bound state pole position is at $1889.5 - 6.3 i$ MeV.
- The $\eta^{\prime} N$ compositeness $X_{\eta^{\prime} N} = 1.01 + 0.00 i$, indicating the bound state is dominated by the $\eta^{\prime} N$ component.
- The integrated signal contribution in the $\eta n$ spectrum below the $\eta^{\prime} n$ threshold (range $[1.86\ \text{GeV}, M_{\eta^{\prime}} + M_n]$) is $0.011\ \mu\text{b}/\text{sr}$.
- The integrated quasifree and tail contributions above the $\eta^{\prime} n$ threshold (range $[M_{\eta^{\prime}} + M_n, 2.0\ \text{GeV}]$) is $0.055\ \mu\text{b}/\text{sr}$.
- The ratio of the bound state signal to other contributions is $0.20$.
- The $\gamma p \to \eta^{\prime} p$ and $\gamma p \to \eta p$ vertex parameters were fixed as $V_{\gamma 1} = 0.348\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$ and $V_{\gamma 2} = 0.354\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model predictions of an $\eta^{\prime} N$ bound state by Sakai (2013) to a concrete photoproduction reaction on a deuteron target, building on the formulation developed by Jido (2009, 2010, 2012) and Yamagata-Sekihara (2012). The results provide a theoretical basis for experimental searches for $\eta^{\prime}$ bound states in nuclei, which have been motivated by the expected reduction of the $\eta^{\prime}$ mass in nuclear matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry (Jido 2011). The study enables future experimental comparisons with data from facilities such as those measuring $\gamma p \to \eta^{\prime} p$ reactions (Williams 2009, Sumihama 2009) and the $\pi^{-} p \to \eta^{\prime} n$ reaction (Moyssides 1983).
