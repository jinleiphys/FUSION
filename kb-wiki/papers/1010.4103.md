# $ Žē(\to ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0) $ meson photoproduction on proton

> The cross section is estimated for the $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ invariant mass distribution in the $╬│p$ reaction in the GeV region. This reaction is assumed to proceed through the formation of the $Žē$ meson in the intermediate state, since the production cross section for this meson in the $╬│p$ reaction in GeV region is significant and it has large branching ratio (88.8%) in the $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ channel. The cross sections for this reaction have been calculated using the energy dependent reaction amplitude, i.e., $f_{╬│p \to Žēp}(0)$, extracted from the latest $Žē$ meson photoproduction data. We use established procedure to calculate other factors, like width and propagator of the $Žē$ meson, so that our calculation can provide reliable cross section. The calculated results reproduce the measured $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^0$ invariant mass distribution spectra in the $╬│p$ reaction.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the cross section for the $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ invariant mass distribution in the $\gamma p$ reaction assuming the process proceeds exclusively through the formation of an $\omega$ meson in the intermediate state, using the energy-dependent forward reaction amplitude $f_{\gamma p \to \omega p}(0)$ extracted from the latest $\omega$ meson photoproduction data. The calculated results reproduce the measured peak positions of the invariant mass spectra, but the widths show a mismatch attributed to detector resolution, and the calculated cross sections require normalization to match the absolute magnitudes of the measured spectra.

## Method

The differential cross section $d\sigma/dm$ for the $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ invariant mass distribution is calculated using the reaction $\gamma p \to \omega p$; $\omega \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$, with the $\omega$ meson propagator and decay widths evaluated using established formulas from Sakurai (1960) and the Particle Data Group. The forward amplitude $f_{\gamma p \to \omega p}(0)$ is extracted from the measured four-momentum transfer distribution $d\sigma/dq^2$ for $\gamma p \to \omega p$ using SAPHIR data for $E_\gamma \le 2.6$ GeV and data from Refs. [shmt, stk] for $E_\gamma \ge 2.6$ GeV, covering the beam energy range $E_\gamma = 1.1 - 9.3$ GeV. For tagged photon beams, the cross section is modulated with a bremsstrahlung beam profile function $W(E_\gamma) \propto 1/E_\gamma$.

## Key numbers

- $\omega$ meson mass $m_\omega \simeq 782$ MeV, with total free-space decay width $\Gamma_\omega(m_\omega) \approx 8.43$ MeV.
- Branching ratios for $\omega$ decay: $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ (88.8%), $\pi^0\gamma$ (8.5%), $\pi^+\pi^-$ (2.21%), $l^+l^-$ ($\sim 10^{-4}\%$).
- $\Gamma_{\omega \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0}(m_\omega \simeq 782\ \text{MeV}) \simeq 7.49$ MeV; $\Gamma_{\omega \to \pi^0\gamma}(m_\omega) \simeq 0.72$ MeV; $\Gamma_{\omega \to \pi^+\pi^-}(m_\omega) \simeq 0.19$ MeV.
- Calculated peak cross section for $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ invariant mass distribution at fixed beam energies: 7.25 mb/GeV at $E_\gamma = 2.8$ GeV, increasing to $\sim 10.51$ mb/GeV at $E_\gamma = 9.3$ GeV.
- For energy bins with beam profile: peak cross section $\sim 2.31$ mb/GeV at $E_\gamma = 1.1-1.5$ GeV, increasing to $\sim 7.73$ mb/GeV at $E_\gamma = 2.5-6.0$ GeV.
- For the narrow energy bin $1.2 < E_\gamma (\text{GeV}) < 1.25$ with $0.3 < q^2_{min}-q^2 (\text{GeV}^2) < 0.4$, the calculated peak cross section is about 1.7 mb/GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of $\omega$ meson photoproduction via the $\pi^0\gamma$ decay channel (Das, 2010) to the dominant $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ decay channel, which provides a better signal for $\omega$ meson production. The calculation uses the vector dominance model framework and compares with experimental data from Ballam et al. (1973), the BUBBLE chamber group (1973), and the SAPHIR collaboration at ELSA (Barber et al., 2003). The results enable future studies of nucleonic resonances through the $\omega p$ system, which is advantageous due to the narrow width of the $\omega$ meson, though restricted to isospin $I=1/2$ resonances.
