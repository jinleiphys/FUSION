# Chiral transition in a finite system and possible use of finite size scaling in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate finite-size effects on the chiral phase diagram of strong interactions within the linear sigma model coupled to quarks. We estimate the modification of the pseudocritical transition line and isentropic trajectories for sizes that are representative of the systems created in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments. The corrections are clearly non-negligible and might significantly affect signatures of the chiral critical endpoint based on estimates for an infinite system. We argue that a finite-size scaling analysis should be tested in the process of data analysis of the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and in future experiments at FAIR-GSI, through the use of full scaling plots and a chi-squared method as tools for searching the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects on the chiral phase diagram of strong interactions are non-negligible for system sizes representative of those created in relativistic heavy ion collisions (2–10 fm), shifting pseudocritical transition lines and the pseudocritical endpoint significantly relative to the thermodynamic limit; the authors propose that finite-size scaling analysis of experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future FAIR experiments could help locate the genuine QCD critical endpoint and determine its universality class.

## Method

The authors use the linear sigma model coupled to two-flavor quarks (N_f = 2) in the mean-field approximation, integrating over quark degrees of freedom to one loop. Finite-size effects are incorporated by replacing continuum momentum integrals with discrete sums over momentum modes, using either periodic boundary conditions (PBC) or antiperiodic boundary conditions (APC) for a cubic system of linear size L. They compute the pseudocritical transition line and isentropic trajectories for system sizes between 2 fm and 10 fm, comparing results to the infinite-volume limit.

## Key numbers

- For L = 2 fm with PBC at zero chemical potential, the crossover transition temperature increases by up to ~80% relative to the infinite-volume limit.
- The pseudocritical endpoint chemical potential (mu_CEP) is about 30% larger for PBC compared to APC at the same system size.
- The range of system sizes studied is between 2 fm and 10 fm, motivated by estimated plasma sizes formed in high-energy heavy ion collisions at RHIC.
- The linear sigma model parameters used: f_pi = 93 MeV, m_pi ≈ 138 MeV, lambda = 20, m_sigma = 600 MeV, g = 3.3, yielding constituent quark mass M_q = g f_pi in vacuum.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of finite-size effects in the quark-gluon plasma by Spieles (1997) and Fraga (2003), and complements lattice estimates by Gopie (1998) and studies within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model. The authors argue that finite-size scaling techniques, well-established in statistical mechanics (Cardy, 1996) and lattice simulations, should be applied to heavy ion collision data to extract information about the genuine QCD critical endpoint, building on proposals by Stephanov (1998, 2008) for using fluctuations of particle multiplicities as critical signals. The approach enables a systematic method to search for criticality across different collision centralities and beam energies, potentially determining the universality class of the chiral transition.
