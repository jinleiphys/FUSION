# Importance of resonance widths in low-energy scattering of weakly-bound light-mass nuclei

> What effect do particle-emitting resonances have on the scattering cross section? What physical considerations are necessary when modelling these resonances? These questions are important when theoretically describing scattering experiments with radioactive ion beams which investigate the frontiers of the table of nuclides, far from stability. Herein, a novel method is developed that describes resonant nuclear scattering from which centroids and widths in the compound nucleus are obtained when one of the interacting bodies has particle unstable resonances. The method gives cross sections without unphysical behavior that is found if simple Lorentzian forms are used to describe resonant target states. The resultant cross sections differ significantly from those obtained when the states in the coupled channel calculations are taken to have zero width, and compound-system resonances are better matched to observed values.

## Key claim
The paper develops a method to include particle-emission widths of target states in low-energy resonant scattering calculations, which avoids unphysical behavior at the scattering threshold and restores causality. The method requires an energy-dependent scaling of the target-state widths combined with a dispersion-relation correction to the target-state centroid energy.

## Method
The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space using a Hilbert-Schmidt expansion of the interaction potential into sturmian functions. Target-state particle-instability is incorporated by adding a complex component to the target-state energy, with the imaginary part representing the width. To eliminate unphysical asymptotic behavior at the scattering threshold, the width is multiplied by an energy-dependent scaling function U(E) that vanishes at E ≤ 0 and approaches zero at high energy, and a dispersion-relation correction Δε_c(E) is added to the real part of the target-state centroid to restore causality.

## Key numbers
- For the 1/2⁻ resonance in ⁹Be: calculation with no target-state widths gives a width 9% of the experimental value; calculation with width scaling and causality correction gives 72% of the known value; calculation with unscaled widths gives 112% of the known value.
- For the 5/2⁺ resonance in ⁹Be: calculation with no target-state widths gives a width 44% of the experimental value; calculation with width scaling and causality correction gives 144%; calculation with unscaled widths gives 260%.
- The first excited state of ⁸Be (2⁺₁) has a centroid at 4.81 MeV (lab frame) above which the reaction cross section becomes non-zero when no target-state widths are considered.
- The scaling function U(E) uses parameters q=1 and Z=2 in the Wigner-based form for the calculations shown.

## Context
This work extends the MCAS method of Amos (2003) to include particle-unstable target states, addressing unphysical behavior previously noted in a technical note by Canton (2011) when simple Lorentzian forms were used. The energy-dependent width scaling follows concepts going back to Wigner (1952) and Teichmann (1952), while the causality-restoring centroid correction applies dispersion relations used in phenomenological optical models by Mahaux (1986) and Capote (1991). The method enables more accurate theoretical descriptions of scattering experiments with radioactive ion beams probing nuclei far from stability, particularly for light-mass systems where compound-system resonances are important.
