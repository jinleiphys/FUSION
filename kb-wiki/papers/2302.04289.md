# Framework for phase transitions between the Maxwell and Gibbs constructions

> By taking the nucleon-to-quark phase transition within a neutron star as an example, we present a thermodynamically consistent method to calculate the equation of state of ambient matter so that transitions that are intermediate to those of the familiar Maxwell and Gibbs constructions can be described. This method does not address the poorly known surface tension between the two phases microscopically (as, for example, in the calculation of the core pasta phases via the Wigner-Seitz approximation) but instead combines the local and global charge neutrality conditions characteristic of the Maxwell and Gibbs constructions, respectively. Overall charge neutrality is achieved by dividing the leptons to those that obey local charge neutrality (Maxwell) and those that maintain global charge neutrality (Gibbs). The equation of state is obtained by using equilibrium constraints derived from minimizing the total energy density. The results of this minimization are then used to calculate neutron star mass-radius curves, tidal deformabilities, equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds, and nonradial $g$-mode oscillation frequencies for several intermediate constructions. Various quantities of interest transform smoothly from their Gibbs structures to those of Maxwell as the local-to-total electron ratio $η$, introduced to mimic the hadron-to-quark interface tension from $0$ (Gibbs) to $\infty$ (Maxwell), is raised from $0$ to $1$. A notable exception is the $g$-mode frequency for the specific case of $η=1$ for which a gap appears between the quark and hadronic branches.

## Key claim

The paper presents a thermodynamically consistent method to calculate equations of state for first-order phase transitions that are intermediate between the Maxwell and Gibbs constructions, using a parameter η (local-to-total electron ratio) to mimic the effect of hadron-to-quark interface tension from 0 (Gibbs) to infinity (Maxwell), without microscopically modeling the surface tension itself.

## Method

The authors introduce three distinct electron clouds (eN, eQ, eG) where eN and eQ electrons enforce local charge neutrality in hadronic and quark phases respectively, while eG electrons maintain global charge neutrality across both phases. The parameter η controls the fraction of electrons participating in local versus global charge neutrality, and the equations of state are obtained by minimizing the total energy density with respect to particle fractions and volume fractions, subject to baryon number conservation and charge neutrality constraints. The resulting equilibrium conditions are then used to calculate neutron star mass-radius curves, tidal deformabilities, sound speeds, and nonradial g-mode oscillation frequencies for several intermediate constructions between η=0 (Gibbs) and η=1 (Maxwell).

- The vMIT bag model for quarks uses: mu = 5.0 MeV, md = 7.0 MeV, ms = 150.0 MeV, a = 0.20 fm^2, B^(1/4) = 165.0 MeV
- Nuclear saturation density is nsat ≈ 0.16 fm^(-3)
- The outer crust equation of state from SLy4 is used for baryon densities below 0.05 fm^(-3)
- The squared equilibrium speed of sound csqeq = dP/dε becomes zero in the flat region of the Maxwell construction

## Context

This work extends the standard Maxwell and Gibbs constructions for first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, which were established by Glendenning (1992, 2001). The method provides a continuous spectrum between these two limiting cases without requiring the poorly known surface tension between hadronic and quark phases, which ranges from a few to hundreds of MeV/fm^2 according to studies by Alford (2001), Lugones (2013, 2018), and others. The framework enables calculation of neutron star properties including g-mode oscillations, which were previously problematic in quarkyonic matter models where the adiabatic speed of sound becomes infinite at the onset of quarks, as noted by Constantinou (2021).
