# Exploration of In-Medium Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions

> The study focuses on exploring the changes in the hyperon-baryon interaction at various nuclear densities. This approach starts by building a vacuum hyperon-nucleon interaction model based on Boson -Exchange maintaining SU(3) flavor symmetry. Bethe-Goldstone equation is then explored to investigate the medium properties over the bare interaction. A detailed investigation of the density dependence revealed clear changes in the low energy parameters with the variation of the medium density shown for different strangeness channels.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in-medium hyperon-nucleon interactions show clear density-dependent changes in low-energy parameters, but the authors emphasize that for the hyperon sector there are not yet precise quantitative results, so their findings serve as a qualitative theoretical prediction rather than a definitive measurement.

## Method

The authors constructed a vacuum hyperon-nucleon interaction model using a one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) with SU(3) flavor symmetry, fitting coupling constants to available $\Sigma^{+}p$ and $\Lambda p$ scattering cross section data by solving coupled three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equations. They then incorporated medium effects by applying the Bethe-Goldstone equation with a Pauli projector $Q_F$ to investigate how the interaction changes at various nuclear densities, studying scattering lengths and effective ranges for S=-1 and S=-2 strangeness channels.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum interaction fit achieved a $\chi^2$ value of 6.68 when fitted to available $\Sigma^{+}p$ and $\Lambda p$ cross section data using only the $^{1}S_{0}$ partial wave
- For the $\Lambda n \rightarrow \Lambda n$ channel: free-space scattering length $a_s^{free} = -1.50$ (units not specified in text) and effective range $r_e^{free} = 2.34$; at saturation density $a_s^{sat} = -0.76$ and $r_e^{sat} = 2.01$
- For the $\Sigma^{+} p$ channel: free-space scattering length $a_s^{free} = -1.44$ and effective range $r_e^{free} = 5.18$; at saturation density $a_s^{sat} = -0.86$ and $r_e^{sat} = 5.34$
- For the $\Sigma^{+} \Sigma^{+}$ channel: free-space scattering length $a_s^{free} = -5.75$ and effective range $r_e^{free} = 1.94$; at saturation density $a_s^{sat} = -1.23$ and $r_e^{sat} = 2.78$
- The mixing angles used were ideal mixing values: pseudoscalar $\theta = -23$ degrees, scalar $\theta = 37.05$ degrees, vector $\theta = 35.26$ degrees

## Context

This work extends earlier meson-exchange approaches to hyperon-nucleon interactions developed by the Juelich group (Jue89) and the Extended Soft Core (ESC) model of Yamamoto (2014), as well as chiral effective field theory ($\chi$EFT) models. The authors note that large uncertainties remain due to the scarcity of hyperon-nucleon scattering data, and they aim to use hypernuclear data as independent additional constraints on interactions among octet baryons. The approach enables future studies of hypernuclear structure properties through Hartree-Fock formalism and can be extended to higher strangeness channels and three-body forces.
