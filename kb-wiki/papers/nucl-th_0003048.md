# Reduction of superfluid gap by scattering

> The effect nucleon dressing by scattering on the pairing gap in nuclear matter is discussed. Numerical results from self-consistent T-matrix calculations are compared to quasi-particle approximations. The dominant effect of scattering can be accounted for by a renormalization of the quasi-particle strength. The exponential sensitivity of the pairing gap and the critical temperature on the pairing strength makes it strongly dependent on many-body effects.

## Key claim

The dominant effect of nucleon dressing by scattering on the pairing gap in nuclear matter can be accounted for by a renormalization of the quasi-particle strength (the Z-factor), rather than requiring a full off-shell treatment; however, the exponential sensitivity of the pairing gap to the pairing strength means that even this renormalization produces a large reduction compared to the mean-field BCS result.

## Method

Self-consistent T-matrix calculations were performed for nuclear matter using a simple S-wave Yamaguchi interaction, including full spectral functions for off-shell nucleon propagators in the ladder resummation for the self-energy and the gap equation. The results were compared to quasi-particle approximations where the spectral function is replaced by a delta-function peak at the quasi-particle pole, with and without renormalization by the Z-factor. The critical temperature was obtained from the Thouless criterion (singularity in the T-matrix at zero total energy and momentum), and the superfluid gap below T_c was solved from the gap equation with full spectral functions and with quasi-particle approximations.

## Key numbers

- At a density of 0.45 of normal nuclear density ρ₀, the critical temperature was reduced from T_c = 5 MeV in the mean-field gap equation with Hartree-Fock single-particle energies to T_c = 1.6 MeV in the T-matrix approximation with off-shell propagation.
- Using the renormalized quasi-particle approximation (with Z_p² factor), the critical temperature was T_c = 2.2 MeV, closer to the full off-shell result of 1.6 MeV.
- The Z-factor at the Fermi momentum was Z_{p_f} ≈ 0.7 in the model calculation.
- The superfluid gap at zero temperature in the quasi-particle approximation with renormalization was Δ(p_F) = 6 MeV, compared to Δ(p_F) = 4.3 MeV from the full off-shell gap equation and Δ(p_F) = 10.8 MeV from the mean-field BCS solution.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent nuclear matter calculations by Bozek (1999) that found a strong reduction of the critical temperature and superfluid gap when using full spectral functions, compared to the quasi-particle approximation. It identifies the Z-factor renormalization as the dominant mechanism, rather than the background part of the spectral function. The results contradict simpler mean-field treatments and suggest that realistic nuclear matter calculations (e.g., using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock single-particle energies) should include a Z-factor renormalization of the pairing interaction to account for nucleon dressing by scattering. This enables improved gap equation calculations that can use results from advanced G-matrix calculations as input.
