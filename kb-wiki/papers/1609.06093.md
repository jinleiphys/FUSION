# Heavy flavor $R_\text{AA}$ and $v_n$ in event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamics

> Recently it has been shown that a realistic description of the medium via event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics plays an important role in the long-standing $R_\text{AA}$ vs. $v_2$ puzzle at high $p_T$. In this proceedings we begin to extend this approach to the heavy flavor sector by investigating the effects of full event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds on the nuclear suppression factor and $v_2\{2\}$ of heavy flavor mesons and non-photonic electrons at intermediate to high $p_T$. We also show results for $v_3\{2\}$ of $B^0$ and D$^0$ for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds, previously important for the high-pT R_AA vs. v_2 puzzle in light hadrons, also significantly affect heavy flavor observables; the triangular flow v_3{2} for D^0 and B^0 mesons is more sensitive to the choice of energy loss model than the elliptic flow v_2{2}, potentially offering a better tool to study jet-medium interactions.

## Method

The authors developed a modular framework that couples heavy quark propagation (charm and bottom) to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds from the v-USPhydro code, using MCKLN initial conditions and a shear viscosity to entropy ratio of eta/s = 0.11 for PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV. Heavy quarks are sampled at the beginning of each hydrodynamic event (1000 events per centrality class) with initial momentum distributions from FONLL pQCD calculations, and they lose energy via a simple model dE/dx = alpha * Gamma_flow * f(T,E,x) where the coupling alpha is fixed by fitting D^0 meson R_AA data in central collisions and bottom quark alpha is fit using electron R_AA data. The heavy quarks propagate until the local temperature drops below T_d = 120 MeV, then fragment using Pythia8, with no coalescence effects included; the 2-particle cumulant flow coefficients v_n{2} are computed by correlating soft and heavy flow harmonics event-by-event.

## Key numbers

- D^0 meson R_AA and heavy flavor electron R_AA are computed for central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV and compared to ALICE and CMS data
- Two energy loss models are tested: dE/dx ~ T^2 and dE/dx = constant
- The freeze-out temperature is set to T_FO = 140 MeV
- The jet-medium decoupling temperature is T_d = 120 MeV
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio is eta/s = 0.11
- 1000 hydrodynamic events are used for each centrality class
- v_3{2} for D^0 mesons and heavy flavor electrons is presented for non-central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV for the first time

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic approach previously applied to the high-pT R_AA vs. v_2 puzzle in light hadrons (Noronha-Hostler 2016, Betz 2016) into the heavy flavor sector. The results are compared to prior calculations by Cao (2016) and Das (2015) that did not include coalescence effects, and the authors note that their v_2{2} results at intermediate pT < 10 GeV are comparable to those earlier works. The framework enables future calculations of multi-particle cumulants of harmonic flow and the inclusion of coalescence effects to describe the intermediate pT region.
