# QCD Analysis of $ΔS=0$ Hadronic Parity Violation

> We present a QCD analysis of the effective weak Hamiltonian at hadronic energy scales for strangeness-nonchanging ($ΔS=0$) hadronic processes. Performing a leading-order renormalization group analysis in QCD from the $W$ to the ${\cal O}(2\,\rm GeV)$ energy scale, we derive the pertinent effective Hamiltonian for hadronic parity violation, including the effects of both neutral and charged weak currents. We compute the complete renormalization group evolution of all isosectors and the evolution through heavy-flavor thresholds for the first time. We show that the additional four-quark operators that enter below the $W$ mass scale from QCD operator mixing effects form a closed set, and they result in a $12\times 12$ anomalous dimension matrix. Computing the resulting effective Hamiltonian and comparing to earlier results, we affirm the importance of operator mixing effects and find, as an example, that the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant, using the factorization Ansatz and an assessment of the pertinent quark charge of the nucleon in lattice QCD at the 2 GeV scale, is in better agreement with recent experiments.

## Key claim
The paper presents a complete leading-order QCD renormalization group analysis of the parity-violating effective weak Hamiltonian for strangeness-nonchanging processes, including operator mixing and heavy-flavor threshold effects for all isospin sectors for the first time. The authors find that the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant computed using their Hamiltonian and lattice QCD inputs is in better agreement with recent experiments than earlier estimates, though the result carries significant uncertainties from scale variation, higher-order corrections, and the factorization approximation.

## Method
The authors constructed the effective parity-violating Hamiltonian at the W mass scale from both neutral (Z⁰) and charged (W±) weak current contributions, then performed a leading-order renormalization group evolution in QCD down to the hadronic scale of 2 GeV, matching across heavy-flavor thresholds (bottom, charm). They computed the complete 12×12 anomalous dimension matrix for the four-quark operators that form a closed set under QCD mixing, separated the analysis into isovector (I=1) and isoscalar+isotensor (I=0⊕2) sectors, and verified that the same low-energy Hamiltonian is obtained regardless of whether isospin projection is performed before or after renormalization group evolution.

## Context
This work extends the foundational analysis of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1979), who estimated QCD evolution effects via a phenomenological enhancement factor K without including operator mixing. The authors show that the earlier results fall outside the range possible through scale variation and higher-order effects when complete operator mixing is included. The construction of the complete effective Hamiltonian at 2 GeV supports lattice QCD studies of two-nucleon matrix elements (Nicholson 2021), enabling future theoretical studies where the factorization approximation of hadronic matrix elements would no longer be necessary.
