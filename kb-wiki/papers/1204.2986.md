# Fluctuating initial condition and smoothening effect on elliptic and triangular flow

> In heavy ion collisions, event-by-event fluctuations in participating nucleon positions can lead to triangular flow. Generally, one uses Monte-Carlo Glauber model to obtain the participating nucleon positions. To use in a hydrodynamic model, the positions needs to be smoothened. We study the effect of smoothening of Glauber Monte-Carlo initial conditions on elliptic and triangular flow. It is shown that integrated as well as differential elliptic and triangular flow remain largely unaltered, irrespective of functional form of the smoothening function, or the smoothening parameter

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, both integrated and differential elliptic and triangular flow are largely insensitive to the functional form (Gaussian or Woods-Saxon) or the magnitude of the smoothening parameter used to convert discrete nucleon positions into a continuous energy density.

## Method

The authors generated event-by-event initial conditions for 30-35% centrality Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV using a Monte-Carlo Glauber model, then smoothed the discrete participant nucleon positions with either a Woods-Saxon function (varying diffuseness parameter a from 0.05 to 0.5 fm) or a Gaussian function (varying width σ from 0.1 to 0.5 fm). The smoothed energy densities were evolved with the ideal hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA assuming boost-invariance, using a lattice-based equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, with initial time τ_i = 0.6 fm, zero initial fluid velocity, and freeze-out temperature T_F = 130 MeV. Harmonic flow coefficients were computed from the freeze-out surface via the Cooper-Frye formalism for 100 events per parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity was constrained to dN/dy ≈ 210 for all smoothening functions and parameters
- For Gaussian smoothening, differential v_2 at p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV changed by approximately 3% when the width σ was increased from 0.1 to 0.5 fm (a factor of 5)
- For Gaussian smoothening, differential v_3 at p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV was reduced by approximately 10% for the same factor-of-5 increase in σ
- For Woods-Saxon smoothening, differential v_3 at p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV changed by approximately 7% for a factor-of-10 change in diffuseness parameter a (from 0.05 to 0.5 fm)
- The Woods-Saxon half-radius was fixed at C = 1 fm; diffuseness values tested were a = 0.05, 0.1, 0.25, and 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamic framework using Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, where prior studies by Alver and collaborators (2010) and Teaney (2010) established that fluctuating nucleon positions generate odd harmonic flow including triangular flow. The paper addresses a procedural gap in the existing literature, which generally does not elaborate on the smoothening procedure for converting discrete participant positions into continuous density distributions. The finding that flow coefficients are robust to the smoothening method validates the common practice of using such initial conditions for extracting transport properties like the viscosity-to-entropy ratio, and enables future studies to focus on other sources of uncertainty without concern for the specific smoothening prescription.
