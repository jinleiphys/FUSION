# Charmed mesons at finite temperature and chemical potential

> We compute the masses of the pseudoscalar mesons $π^+$ , $K^0$ and $D^+$ at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. The computations are based on a symmetry- preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector four quark contact interaction. The results found for the temperature dependence of the meson masses are in qualitative agreement with lattice QCD data and QCD sum rules calculations. The chemical potential dependence of the masses provide a novel prediction of the present computation.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the pseudoscalar meson masses (π⁺, K⁰, D⁺) computed using a symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction shows qualitative agreement with lattice QCD and QCD sum rules, but the chemical potential dependence of the masses is a novel prediction of this specific model. The authors note that for sufficiently low chemical potential, the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions in the Bethe-Salpeter equation play no significant role for temperatures below the pseudocritical temperature and can be neglected.

## Method

The authors used a confining, symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) treatment of a vector-vector four-quark contact interaction, extended to finite temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ via Matsubara frequency sums. They solved the gap equation for constituent quark masses and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for pseudoscalar meson masses, employing a subtraction scheme to remove symmetry-violating terms from divergent integrals. The model parameters (cutoff Λ = 0.653 GeV, coupling GΛ² = 19.26, and current quark masses) were fitted to vacuum meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum meson masses obtained: m_π = 0.139 GeV, m_{K⁰} = 0.493 GeV, m_{D⁺} = 1.864 GeV
- Vacuum constituent quark masses: M_l = 0.328 GeV, M_s = 0.599 GeV, M_c = 2.095 GeV
- Model parameters: Λ = 0.653 GeV, GΛ² = 19.26, m_l = 0.005 GeV, m_s = 0.161 GeV, m_c = 1.544 GeV
- As chemical potential μ increases, the pseudocritical temperature for chiral restoration decreases

## Context

This work extends a contact-interaction DSE/BSE model previously used in vacuum (Serna and Krein, 2016) to finite temperature and chemical potential, and compares results with QCD sum rules (Suzuki, 2015) and earlier NJL model calculations (Blaschke, 2011; Gottfried, 1992). The results are in qualitative agreement with lattice QCD data and enable future calculations of charmed-hadron production in heavy-ion collisions and transport properties of charmed hadrons, as well as comparisons with confining chiral models and chiral soliton models.
