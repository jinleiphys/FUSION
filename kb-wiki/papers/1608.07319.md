# A Euclidean bridge to the relativistic constituent quark model

> ${\bf Background}$ Knowledge of nucleon structure is today ever more of a precision science, with heightened theoretical and experimental activity expected in coming years. At the same time, a persistent gap lingers between theoretical approaches grounded in Euclidean methods (e.g., lattice QCD, Dyson-Schwinger Equations [DSEs]) as opposed to traditional Minkowski field theories (such as light-front constituent quark models). ${\bf Purpose}$ Seeking to bridge these complementary worldviews, we explore the potential of a Euclidean constituent quark model (ECQM). This formalism enables us to study the gluonic dressing of the quark-level axial-vector vertex, which we undertake as a test of the framework. ${\bf Method}$ To access its indispensable elements with a minimum of inessential detail, we develop our ECQM using the simplified quark $+$ scalar diquark picture of the nucleon. We construct a hyperspherical formalism involving polynomial expansions of diquark propagators to marry our ECQM with the results of Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE) analyses, and constrain model parameters by fitting electromagnetic form factor data. ${\bf Results}$ From this formalism, we define and compute a new quantity --- the Euclidean density function (EDF) --- an object that characterizes the nucleon's various charge distributions as functions of the quark's Euclidean momentum. Applying this technology and incorporating information from BSE analyses, we find the dressing effect on the proton's axial-singlet charge to be small in magnitude and consistent with zero. ${\bf Conclusions}$ The scalar quark $+$ diquark ECQM is a step toward a realistic quark model in Euclidean space, and urges additional refinements. The small size we obtain for the impact of the dressed vertex on the axial-singlet charge suggests that models without this effect are on firm ground to neglect it.

## Key claim

The authors formulate a Euclidean constituent quark model (ECQM) using a simplified quark + scalar diquark picture of the nucleon, finding that the gluonic dressing effect on the proton's axial-singlet charge is small in magnitude and consistent with zero, suggesting that models neglecting this effect are on firm ground.

## Method

The authors construct a hyperspherical formalism involving polynomial expansions of diquark propagators to marry their ECQM with results from Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE) analyses, constraining model parameters by fitting electromagnetic form factor data from the Kelly parametrization (2004) for momentum transfers up to 1 GeV. They then define and compute a new quantity called the Euclidean density function (EDF), which characterizes the nucleon's charge distributions as functions of the quark's Euclidean momentum, and use this to incorporate BSE information on the dressed axial-vector vertex.

## Key numbers

- The fitted model yields a constituent quark mass m = 0.637 GeV, diquark mass m_D = 0.947 GeV, cutoff parameter Lambda = 0.228 GeV, and coupling g = 79.104, with a chi-squared per datum of 0.00297 when fitting to the Kelly parametrization (2004) at five points in the domain 0 <= q <= 1 GeV.
- The bare (undressed) axial-singlet charge a_0 predicted by the fitted model is 0.784.
- The first moment of the electric charge EDF M^1_{f1} is 0.1985 GeV^2, and the first moment of the axial-singlet charge EDF M^1_{a0} is 0.08125 GeV^2.
- The proton magnetic moment predicted by the fitted model is 2.843 mu_N.

## Context

This work extends the long history of quark-diquark models used in deep inelastic scattering analyses (Meyer 1990, Mulders 1992, Jakob 1997, Gross 2012) and elastic scattering (Cloet 2012), but formulates such a model directly in Euclidean space for the first time. The formalism enables a direct interface between constituent quark models and Euclidean field-theoretic approaches such as lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter Equation analyses, allowing the incorporation of nonperturbative gluon dressing effects from BSE studies (Maris 1997, Bhagwat 2002, 2007, Chang 2012) into the calculation of nucleon spin structure.
