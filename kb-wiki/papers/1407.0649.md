# Using the Single Quark Transition Model to predict nucleon resonance amplitudes

> We present predictions for the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast$ helicity amplitudes, where $N^\ast$ is a member of the $[70,1^-]$ supermultiplet. We combine the results from the single quark transition model for the helicity amplitudes with the results of the covariant spectator quark model for the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1535)$ and $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1520)$ transitions. The theoretical estimations from the covariant spectator quark model are used to calculate three independent functions $A,B$, and $C$ of $Q^2$, where $Q^2=-q^2$ and $q$ is the momentum transfer. With the knowledge of the functions $A,B$, and $C$ we estimate the helicity amplitudes for the transitions $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1650)$, $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast(1700)$, $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1620)$, and $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1700)$. The analysis is restricted to reactions with proton targets. The predictions for the transition amplitudes are valid for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper uses the covariant spectator quark model's results for the well-known $N^\ast(1535)$ and $N^\ast(1520)$ transitions to determine three functions $A, B, C$ of $Q^2$, which then predict helicity amplitudes for four other resonances in the $[70,1^-]$ supermultiplet, but only for proton targets and only reliably for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$ where valence quark effects dominate.

## Method

The author combined the single quark transition model (SQTM) parametrization of the $\gamma^\ast N \to N^\ast$ helicity amplitudes with numerical results from the covariant spectator quark model for the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ transitions. Using the SQTM relations, the three independent functions $A, B, C$ of $Q^2$ were extracted from the known amplitudes, then applied to predict the $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$ amplitudes for the $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $S_{31}(1620)$, and $D_{33}(1700)$ states. Two models were considered: model 1 with only valence quark contributions (setting $A_{3/2}^{D13}=0$), and model 2 including a meson cloud parametrization for $A_{3/2}^{D13}$.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angles used are $\theta_S = 31^\circ$ for the $S_{11}$ states and $\theta_D = 6^\circ$ for the $D_{13}$ states.
- The covariant spectator quark model is considered reliable for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, with good description of $N^\ast(1535)$ data for $Q^2 > 2.5$ GeV$^2$ and $N^\ast(1520)$ data for $Q^2 > 1.5$ GeV$^2$.
- The meson cloud parametrization for $A_{3/2}^{D13}$ uses $\Lambda_4^2 = 20$ GeV$^2$ and $\Gamma_\rho^0 = 0.149$ GeV.
- High $Q^2$ parametrization parameters for model 2 at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$ include: $D = 68.90 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ with $\Lambda^2 = 3.35$ GeV$^2$ for $S_{11}(1650)$ $A_{1/2}$; $D = -8.51 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ with $\Lambda^2 = 2.82$ GeV$^2$ for $D_{13}(1700)$ $A_{1/2}$; $D = 4.36 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ with $\Lambda^2 = 3.61$ GeV$^2$ for $D_{13}(1700)$ $A_{3/2}$; $D = 39.22 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ with $\Lambda^2 = 2.69$ GeV$^2$ for $D_{33}(1700)$ $A_{1/2}$; $D = 42.15 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ with $\Lambda^2 = 8.42$ GeV$^2$ for $D_{33}(1700)$ $A_{3/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the SQTM formalism of Burkert (2003) and the covariant spectator quark model calculations for the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances (Ramalho, 2012-2013) to predict amplitudes for higher-mass resonances in the same $[70,1^-]$ supermultiplet. The predictions are intended for comparison with future CLAS data on double charged pion electroproduction at $Q^2 = 2$ to $5$ GeV$^2$, which will test the quark core contributions for resonances with masses above 1.6 GeV. The approach contrasts with other quark models (Capstick, 1995; Bijker, 1996; Santopinto, 1998) that predict small nonzero $A_{3/2}$ amplitudes, while this work attributes the dominant $A_{3/2}$ contribution to meson cloud effects.
