# Transverse momentum fluctuation in ultra-central Pb+Pb collision at the LHC

> The ATLAS collaboration has recently observed that the variance of the transverse momentum per particle ($[ p_t ]$), when measured as a function of the collision multiplicity ($N_{ch}$) in Pb+Pb collisions, decreases by a factor $2$ for the largest values of $N_{ch}$, corresponding to ultra-central collisions. We show that this phenomenon is naturally explained by invoking impact parameter ($b$) fluctuations, which contribute to the variance, and gradually disappear in ultra-central collisions. It implies that $N_{ch}$ and $[ p_t ]$ are strongly correlated at fixed $b$, which is explained by the local thermalization of the QGP medium.

## Key claim
The steep factor-of-2 decrease in the variance of transverse momentum per particle observed by ATLAS in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions is naturally explained by the gradual disappearance of impact-parameter fluctuations at the highest multiplicities, rather than by a change in intrinsic quantum fluctuations.

## Method
The authors model the joint probability distribution of transverse momentum per particle and charged-particle multiplicity at fixed impact parameter as a two-dimensional correlated Gaussian, characterized by means, variances, and a Pearson correlation coefficient. They fit this model to ATLAS data using three free parameters, separating the variance into a contribution from impact-parameter fluctuations and a contribution from intrinsic (quantum) fluctuations at fixed impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- The variance of transverse momentum per particle decreases by a factor of 2 for the largest values of $N_{ch}$ (ultra-central collisions), around $N_{ch} = 3700$.
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between $[p_t]$ and $N_{ch}$ at fixed impact parameter is $r_{N_{ch}} = 0.676$.
- The correlation between $N_{ch}$ and $[p_t]$ in HIJING is smaller by a factor of approximately 10 compared to hydrodynamic simulations at $b=0$.
- The average transverse momentum at $b=0$ is $\overline{p_t} = 1074$ MeV/c, with average multiplicity $\overline{N_{ch}} = 6692$.

## Context
This work explains the ATLAS (2022) observation of a steep variance drop in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, which could not be described by models such as HIJING (Gyulassy, 1994) that treat collisions as independent nucleon-nucleon scatterings. The analysis extends the centrality fluctuation framework of Das (2017) and provides the first evidence for local thermalization of the QGP that does not rely on anisotropic flow measurements. The methodology enables separation of geometric and quantum fluctuations, and the predicted increase of mean transverse momentum with multiplicity can be used to extract the speed of sound in the QGP, as recently measured by CMS (2023).
