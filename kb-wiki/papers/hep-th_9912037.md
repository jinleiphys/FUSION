# On The Varying Speed of Light in a Brane-Induced FRW Universe

> We investigate a string/M theoretic realization of the varying speed of light scenario. We consider a 3+1 dimensional probe-brane universe in the background of a black hole in the bulk formed by a stack of branes, in the spirit of Kiritsis (hep-th/9906206). We generalize the dynamics of the system at hand by including rotation and Hubble damping of the bulk space-time and show that this may lead to a mechanism to stabilize the brane-universe and hence fix the speed of light at late times.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a varying speed of light on a probe brane universe can be stabilized at late times by including rotation of the bulk black hole, Hubble damping from bulk expansion, and virialization of bulk density fluctuations, thereby fixing the speed of light to a constant small value rather than allowing it to vanish at the black hole horizon.

## Method

The author considers a 3+1 dimensional probe D3 brane universe moving in the background of a rotating black hole formed by a stack of N D3 branes in Type IIB string theory, using the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action to compute the induced metric on the probe brane. The effective speed of light on the probe brane is extracted from the Maxwell equations derived from the DBI action, and the geodesic equations of motion for the probe brane are derived and solved for circular orbits. The analysis incorporates rotation of the black hole background following Cai, and introduces bulk Hubble expansion and virialization as mechanisms to damp the probe brane's motion and stabilize its orbit.

## Key numbers

- The effective speed of light on the probe brane is given by \(c_{eff} = \sqrt{f(r)} = \sqrt{1 - (r_0 / r)^4}\), where \(r_0\) is the Schwarzschild horizon position and \(r\) is the radial distance of the probe brane from the black hole.
- The metric parameters include \(H_3(r) = 1 + L^4 / r^4\) and \(f(r) = 1 - r_0^4 / r^4\), where \(L\) determines the AdS throat size and \(r_0\) the horizon position.
- The rotating D3 brane metric includes an angular momentum parameter \(l\) and a charge parameter \(\sinh \alpha\) proportional to the total D3 brane charge \(N_{D3}\).

## Context

This work extends the varying speed of light scenario of Albrecht and Magueijo (1998) and the brane-based realization by Kiritsis (1999) in string/M theory, where a probe brane moving toward a black hole yields a time-varying speed of light. The paper addresses the problem that in Kiritsis's original setup the speed of light vanishes at the horizon, which is inconsistent with observations, by introducing rotation, bulk expansion, and virialization to stabilize the brane at a fixed radius. This enables a mechanism for the speed of light to become constant at late times, providing a potential string-theoretic solution to the horizon problem that avoids some fine-tuning issues of inflation.
