# Decomposition of flow and nonflow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a method to separate Δη-dependent and Δη-independent azimuthal correlations using two- and four-particle cumulants between pseudo-rapidity (η) bins in symmetric heavy-ion collisions. The Δη-independent correlation may be dominated by harmonic flows, a global correlation to the common collision geometry. The Δη-dependent correlation can be identified as nonflow, particle correlations unrelated to the common geometry. Our method exploits the ηsymmetry of the average harmonic flows and is "data-driven." We use the AMPT and HIJING event generators to illustrate our method. We discuss the decomposed Δη-independent and Δη-dependent correlations regarding flow and nonflow in the models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a data-driven method to separate azimuthal correlations into a Δη-independent component (dominated by harmonic flow) and a Δη-dependent component (identified as nonflow), but explicitly notes that the decomposition separates Δη-dependent and Δη-independent correlations rather than flow and nonflow per se, because flow fluctuations may also have a Δη-dependent part and away-side nonflow may be Δη-independent.

## Method

The method uses two- and four-particle cumulants between pairs of pseudorapidity (η) bins in symmetric heavy-ion collisions. By taking the difference of cumulants for two pairs of η bins (ηα, ηβ) and (ηα, -ηβ), the Δη-independent terms (average flow, flow fluctuations that factorize, and Δη-independent nonflow) cancel due to the η-symmetry of the collision system. The remaining differences isolate the Δη-dependent flow fluctuation (from four-particle cumulants) and the sum of Δη-dependent flow fluctuation and Δη-dependent nonflow (from two-particle cumulants), allowing extraction of the Δη-dependent nonflow correlation. The method is illustrated using the AMPT and HIJING event generators for 200 GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- AMPT events: 1.1×10^7 minimum-bias events generated; analysis focuses on impact parameter 5 < b < 11 fm (approximately 10-60% centrality)
- HIJING events: 4.7×10^7 minimum-bias events generated
- For second-harmonic flow fluctuation in AMPT (10-60%): fit parameter c = -0.07 ± 0.02 with χ²/ndf = 83/77, describing ΔV₂{4} = c(Δη₂ - Δη₁)
- Maximum η-gap used: Δη_max = 9.6
- Fit χ² values for Δη-dependent nonflow parameterization: 2144/288 (n=2, AMPT 10-60%), 898/288 (n=3, AMPT 10-60%), 306/288 (n=2, AMPT 0-10%), 303/288 (n=3, AMPT 0-10%), 417/288 (n=2, HIJING 10-60%), 142/66 (n=3, HIJING 10-60%)
- Particles used: all particles with pT < 2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to separate flow and nonflow in heavy-ion collisions, which have relied on model-dependent assumptions (e.g., Borghini et al. on nonflow suppression in multi-particle cumulants, and studies of flow fluctuations by Alver et al., Sorensen et al., and others). The method enables data-driven extraction of the Δη-dependent nonflow correlation without assuming the η-dependence of flow, applicable to symmetric collision systems. It can be used to quantify residual nonflow in two-particle cumulant measurements and to test model predictions of nonflow from jet correlations and resonance decays.
