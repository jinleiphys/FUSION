# Nuclear Suppression of Jets and R_AA at the LHC

> The nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadron production at the LHC is predicted from jet energy loss induced by gluon bremsstrahlung. The Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe formalism is used, together with an ideal hydrodynamical model.

## Key claim
The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for charged hadrons in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC is predicted using the AMY gluon bremsstrahlung formalism combined with an ideal hydrodynamic model, but the reliability of the prediction depends on the validity of hydrodynamics at LHC energies and on the assumed value of the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).

## Method
The authors calculated jet energy loss via gluon bremsstrahlung using the Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) formalism, with the medium evolution described by a boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic model initialized from perturbative QCD plus saturation. The initial jet momentum distribution was computed from pQCD including nuclear shadowing effects, and the evolution of the jet momentum distribution in the medium was obtained by solving coupled rate equations involving partonic transition rates that depend on the temperature and flow profiles. The final hadron spectrum was obtained by fragmenting the surviving jets, and \(R_{AA}\) was computed as the ratio of the hadron yield in Pb+Pb collisions to that in pp collisions, normalized by the number of binary collisions.

## Key numbers
- The calculation is for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) ATeV in central collisions at mid-rapidity at the LHC.
- A constant \(\alpha_s=0.33\) was found to describe experimental data in most central collisions at RHIC; predictions at the LHC are presented for \(\alpha_s=0.33\) and \(\alpha_s=0.25\).
- The initial temperature at the LHC is about twice larger than that at RHIC.

## Context
This work extends the AMY-based jet energy loss formalism, previously applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Qin 2007), to predict \(R_{AA}\) at LHC energies. The hydrodynamic evolution used initial conditions from Eskola (2005) and the results for \(\pi_0\) production at RHIC agreed with preliminary PHENIX data and with 3D hydrodynamics results (Qin 2007). The prediction provides a baseline for future LHC measurements of jet suppression and tests the applicability of ideal hydrodynamics and fixed \(\alpha_s\) at higher temperatures.
