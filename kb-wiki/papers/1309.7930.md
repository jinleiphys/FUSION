# Heavy Flavor Suppression: Boltzmann vs Langevin

> The propagation of heavy flavor through the quark gluon plasma has been treated commonly within the framework of Langevin dynamics, i.e. assuming the heavy flavor momentum transfer is much smaller than the light one. On the other hand a similar suppression factor $R_{AA}$ has been observed experimentally for light and heavy flavors. We present a thorough study of the approximations involved by Langevin equation by mean of a direct comparison with the full collisional integral within the framework of Boltzmann transport equation. We have compared the results obtained in both approaches which can differ substantially for charm quark leading to quite different values extracted for the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. In the case of bottom quark the approximation appears to be quite reasonable.

## Key claim

The Langevin equation, commonly used to model heavy flavor propagation in quark-gluon plasma, is a good approximation for bottom quarks but deviates substantially from the full Boltzmann transport equation for charm quarks, overestimating the interaction strength by about 30% at intermediate momentum over typical QGP evolution times of 5-6 fm/c.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Langevin equation and the full Boltzmann transport equation in a box containing only gluons at T = 0.4 GeV, using pQCD elastic cross sections enhanced by a factor of 2 to match a spatial diffusion coefficient D_x = 6/(2π T). They compared the resulting momentum spectra and nuclear suppression factor R_AA for charm and bottom quarks at several evolution times, and quantified the deviation of the Langevin approximation from the Boltzmann result.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks at t = 4 fm, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann spectrum ratio deviates from unity by about 25% at p = 7 GeV; at t = 6 fm the deviation grows to 35%-40% at p = 7 GeV.
- For bottom quarks, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann spectrum ratio remains approximately unity at all times considered (up to 6 fm).
- To obtain a similar nuclear suppression factor R_AA from both approaches for charm quarks, the diffusion coefficient in the Langevin equation must be reduced by about 30%.
- The box temperature was set to T = 0.4 GeV, and the strong coupling constant used was α_s = 0.35 with Debye screening mass m_D = gT.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark diffusion in QGP that relied on the Fokker-Planck/Langevin framework (Hees and Rapp, 2004; Moore and Teaney, 2005; Das et al., 2010) by directly testing the validity of the small-momentum-transfer approximation against the full Boltzmann collision integral. The results show that for charm quarks at LHC temperatures, the Langevin approach systematically overestimates energy loss, requiring a 30% reduction in the extracted diffusion coefficient to match the Boltzmann result. This has direct implications for extracting transport coefficients from experimental R_AA data at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that a realistic fireball evolution calculation is underway.
