# Neutron-gamma competition for $β$-delayed neutron emission

> We present a coupled Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation and Hauser-Feshbach (QRPA+HF) model for calculating delayed particle emission. This approach uses microscopic nuclear structure information which starts with Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the daughter nucleus, and then follows the statistical decay until the initial available excitation energy is exhausted. Explicitly included at each particle emission stage is $γ$-ray competition. We explore this model in the context of neutron emission of neutron-rich nuclei and find that neutron-gamma competition can lead to both increases and decreases in neutron emission probabilities, depending on the system considered. A second consequence of this formalism is a prediction of more neutrons on average being emitted after $β$-decay for nuclei near the neutron dripline compared to models that do not consider the statistical decay.

# Neutron-gamma competition for β-delayed neutron emission

## Key claim
The coupled QRPA+HF model predicts that neutron-gamma competition can lead to both increases and decreases in neutron emission probabilities depending on the specific nucleus, and that near the neutron dripline the model predicts more neutrons emitted on average per β-decay compared to models that do not include statistical decay. The authors caution that most measured systems do not exhibit large neutron-gamma competition, so near stability the results do not deviate substantially from previous models.

## Method
The authors combine Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations of Gamow-Teller β-decay strength distributions in the daughter nucleus with Hauser-Feshbach (HF) statistical decay theory. The β-decay intensities from QRPA populate excited states in the daughter nucleus, and the subsequent decay by neutron or γ-ray emission is followed recursively through multiple generations of compound nuclei until all available excitation energy is exhausted. Neutron transmission coefficients use the Koning-Delaroche global optical potential, γ-ray transmission coefficients use a generalized Lorentzian γ-strength function for E1 transitions, and level densities use the Gilbert-Cameron formula with shell corrections.

## Key numbers
- For ⁸⁶Ga decay: QRPA-1 model predicts P₁ₙ ∼ 61% and P₂ₙ ∼ 13%; QRPA+HF yields P₁ₙ ∼ 69% and P₂ₙ ∼ 6%; experimental value is P₂ₙ = 20(10)%
- For ⁹³As: QRPA-1 model gives P₁ₙ ∼ 84%, P₂ₙ ∼ 14%, P₃ₙ ∼ 2%; QRPA+HF gives P₁ₙ ∼ 94%, P₂ₙ ∼ 6%, P₃ₙ ∼ 0%
- For ¹⁴⁵Cs: QRPA-1 predicts P₀ₙ ∼ 98% and P₁ₙ ∼ 2%; QRPA+HF predicts P₀ₙ ∼ 78% and P₁ₙ ∼ 22%; measured value is P₁ₙ ∼ 15%
- Average deviation from 225 measured P₁ₙ values: QRPA+HF gives 15.3%; QRPA-1 gives 15.4%; QRPA-2 (including first-forbidden transitions) gives 12.2%
- Roughly 50% of P₁ₙ predictions are within ±5% of measurements for all models tested
- Neutron-gamma competition extends roughly 100 keV above the neutron separation energy in ⁸⁶Ge (Sₙ = 4.93 MeV)
- Increasing the γSF by one order of magnitude changes P₁ₙ by at most ΔP₁ₙ = 3%

## Context
This work extends the approach of Kawano (2008), which was limited to near-stable isotopes, to the entire chart of nuclides. It improves upon the "cutoff" method used in earlier QRPA models such as Möller (1997) (QRPA-1, Gamow-Teller only) and Möller (2003) (QRPA-2, including first-forbidden transitions), which used a schematic energy-window formula without statistical decay. The model also contrasts with systematic approaches like the Kratz-Herman formula and the approximation of Miernik (2014). The results enable future full reaction-network calculations to determine the impact of enhanced multi-neutron emission on r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly near the neutron dripline where the authors find average neutron emission exceeding ⟨n⟩ ∼ 5.
