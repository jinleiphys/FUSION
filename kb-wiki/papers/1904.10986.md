# Variational and Parquet-diagram theory for strongly correlated normal and superfluid systems

> We develop the variational and correlated basis functions/parquet-diagram theory of strongly interacting normal and superfluid systems. The first part of this contribution is devoted to highlight the connections between the Euler equations for the Jastrow-Feenberg wave function on the one hand side, and the ring, ladder, and self-energy diagrams of parquet-diagram theory on the other side. We will show that these subsets of Feynman diagrams are contained, in a local approximation, in the variational wave function. In the second part of this work, we derive the fully optimized Fermi-Hypernetted Chain (FHNC-EL) equations for a superfluid system. Close examination of the procedure reveals that the naïve application of these equations exhibits spurious unphysical properties for even an infinitesimal superfluid gap. We will conclude that it is essential to go {\em beyond\/} the usual Jastrow-Feenberg approximation and to include the exact particle-hole propagator to guarantee a physically meaningful theory and the correct stability range. We will then implement this method and apply it to neutron matter and low density Fermi liquids interacting via the Lennard-Jones model interaction and the Pöschl-Teller interaction. While the quantitative changes in the magnitude of the superfluid gap are relatively small, we see a significant difference between applications for neutron matter and the Lennard-Jones and Pöschl-Teller systems. Despite the fact that the gap in neutron matter can be as large as half the Fermi energy, the corrections to the gap are relatively small. In the Lennard-Jones and Pöschl-Teller models, the most visible consequence of the self-consistent calculation is the change in stability range of the system.

## Key claim

The paper develops a variational and parquet-diagram theory for strongly correlated superfluid systems, but demonstrates that the naive application of Fermi-Hypernetted Chain Euler-Lagrange (FHNC-EL) equations to superfluid states exhibits spurious unphysical properties even for an infinitesimal superfluid gap, requiring inclusion of the exact particle-hole propagator beyond the Jastrow-Feenberg approximation to guarantee a physically meaningful theory and correct stability range.

## Method

The authors first establish the connections between Euler equations for the Jastrow-Feenberg wave function and the ring, ladder, and self-energy diagrams of parquet-diagram theory, showing these diagram subsets are contained in a local approximation within the variational wave function. They then derive the fully optimized FHNC-EL equations for superfluid systems, identify the spurious instabilities arising from the collective or single-pole approximation for the particle-hole propagator, and implement a corrected method that includes the exact superfluid particle-hole propagator. The method is applied to neutron matter and low-density Fermi liquids interacting via the Lennard-Jones model interaction and the Poschl-Teller interaction.

## Key numbers

- The superfluid gap in neutron matter can be as large as half the Fermi energy, yet the corrections to the gap from the self-consistent calculation are relatively small.
- For the Lennard-Jones liquid, one spinodal point appears at negative pressure at about 60 percent of the equilibrium density, characterized by the Fermi liquid Landau parameter F0^s approaching -1.
- The accuracy of the FHNC//0 approximation for helium fluids is better than 90 percent overall, and below about 25 percent of the helium saturation density the accuracy is better than 1 percent.
- For Coulomb systems, both bosons and fermions, the FHNC//0 approximation reproduces results at the percent level or better.

## Context

This work extends the variational Jastrow-Feenberg and parquet-diagram methods, previously well-established for normal Fermi systems, to superfluid systems. It builds on prior work by Jackson et al. (parquet1) establishing parquet diagrams as a minimum requirement for microscopic many-body treatment, and by Feenberg (FeenbergBook) on variational methods. The paper also follows up on previous calculations by the authors (HNCBCS, CBFPairing) and others on BCS-BEC crossover in cold Fermi gases and superfluidity in neutron matter, as reviewed by duine (2004) and chen (2005). The development enables a diagrammatically complete theory for superfluid systems that avoids the spurious instabilities of the standard Jastrow-Feenberg approach, allowing reliable calculations of pairing phenomena in neutron matter and model Fermi systems.
