# RAINIER: A Simulation Tool for Distributions of Excited Nuclear States and Cascade Fluctuations

> A new code has been developed named RAINIER that simulates the $γ$-ray decay of discrete and quasi-continuum nuclear levels for a user-specified range of energy, angular momentum, and parity including a realistic treatment of level spacing and transition width fluctuations. A similar program, DICEBOX, uses the Monte Carlo method to simulate level and width fluctuations but is restricted to $γ$-ray decay from no more than two initial states such as de-excitation following thermal neutron capture. On the other hand, modern reaction codes such as TALYS and EMPIRE populate a wide range of states in the residual nucleus prior to $γ$-ray decay, but do not go beyond the use of deterministic functions and therefore neglect cascade fluctuations. This combination of capabilities allows RAINIER to be used to determine quasi-continuum properties through comparison with experimental data. Several examples are given that demonstrate how cascade fluctuations influence experimental high-resolution $γ$-ray spectra from reactions that populate a wide range of initial states.

## Key claim

RAINIER is a new Monte Carlo simulation tool that models γ-ray cascades from a user-specified range of initial excitation energies, angular momenta, and parities, incorporating realistic level spacing and transition width fluctuations; unlike DICEBOX, it is not restricted to decay from only one or two initial states, and unlike TALYS and EMPIRE, it goes beyond deterministic functions to include cascade fluctuations.

## Method

RAINIER constructs a nuclear level scheme by reading known discrete levels below a user-defined energy threshold from the RIPL-3 database and generating levels above that threshold using nuclear level density (NLD) models such as the Back Shifted Fermi Gas or constant temperature model, with spin distributions from a Fermi gas model and parity equipartition or an energy-dependent parity distribution. It then populates a user-specified distribution of initial states (single state, selection of states, spread of states, or full reaction from an EJΠ histogram) and depopulates them using gamma strength function (GSF) models for E1, M1, and E2 transitions, with transition widths sampled from a Porter-Thomas or user-defined χ² width fluctuation distribution.

## Context

RAINIER extends the Monte Carlo approach of DICEBOX (Becvar, 1998), which was restricted to γ-ray decay from no more than two initial states such as thermal neutron capture, to handle arbitrary initial state distributions from reactions like β-decay, transfer reactions, and compound reactions. It also complements deterministic reaction codes like TALYS and EMPIRE, which populate a wide range of states but neglect cascade fluctuations. RAINIER enables testing of experimental techniques such as the Oslo Method (Guttormsen, 1987), generating feeding time distributions for quasi-continuum lifetime studies, and using observed discrete state populations to determine a nucleus's underlying angular momentum distribution.
