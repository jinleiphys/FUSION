# Theoretical Response to the Discovery of Deeply Bound Pionic States in 208Pb(d,3He) reactions

> Recently, deeply bound pionic states were found experimentally in (d, $^3$He) reactions on $^{208}$Pb. They found an isolated peak structure in the bound region below the pion production threshold. We study theoretically these excitation functions in (d, $^3$He) reactions on $^{208}$Pb at T$_d$=600 MeV. We found very good agreement with the (d, $^3$He) excitation functions and could identify the underlying structures of the pionic states. We study the energy dependence of the (d, $^3$He) reactions and the change of the excitation functions with the incident energy.

## Key claim

The theoretical excitation functions for the (d, 3He) reaction on 208Pb at T_d = 600 MeV show "almost perfect" agreement with the experimental data, but the authors note that the peak seen experimentally actually consists of two unresolved states: [1 ⊗ p_{1/2}^{-1}] and [1 ⊗ p_{3/2}^{-1}], with the latter contributing twice the cross section of the former.

## Method

The authors used the effective number approach, where the (d, 3He) cross section is expressed as the product of the elementary d+n → 3He+π- differential cross section in the laboratory frame and an effective number N_eff that accounts for the initial neutron in a shell-model orbit, the final pion in a pionic atom orbit, and distortion effects estimated via the eikonal approximation. For bound pionic states, bound pion wave functions were used; for continuum states, scattering pion wave functions obtained from a distorted wave optical model replaced the bound wave functions. The calculations included neutron orbitals from two major shells below the Fermi surface (50 < N < 126) and used the Seki-Masutani optical potential for the pionic states.

## Key numbers

- Incident deuteron energy: T_d = 600 MeV, chosen because the elementary cross section peaks and momentum transfer is small at this energy.
- Neutron separation energies used: s_n = 7.367 MeV for p_{1/2}, s_n = 7.937 MeV for f_{5/2}, S_n = 8.264 MeV for p_{3/2}, and S_n = 9.000 MeV for i_{13/2}.
- The peak and shoulder structure appears at Q = -136 MeV (approximately 5 MeV binding energy).
- A constant background of 20 μb/sr/MeV was assumed in the theoretical spectra.
- Two spectral resolutions were used: 500 keV (to compare with experiment) and 200 keV (to reveal underlying structure).
- Calculations were also performed at T_d = 500, 800, and 1000 MeV with ΔE = 200 keV resolution.

## Context

This work provides the detailed theoretical response to the experimental discovery of deeply bound pionic states in 208Pb(d,3He) reactions by Yamazaki et al. (1996), which confirmed earlier predictions by Toki and Yamazaki of quasi-static deeply bound pionic atoms in heavy nuclei. The study extends prior theoretical work on (n,d) reactions and (p,pp) reactions on 208Pb targets, which had identified some strength below the pion production threshold but lacked sufficient resolution and statistical accuracy. The results enable future experiments with better resolution (200 keV) to resolve the individual pionic atom-neutron hole states, and the energy dependence shown at 500, 800, and 1000 MeV provides guidance for experimental design at different beam energies.
