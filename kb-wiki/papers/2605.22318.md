# Shielded inner-shell transitions in atomic samarium for tests of fundamental physics

> Forbidden atomic transitions provide some of the most stringent low-energy tests of physics beyond the Standard Model, with sensitivity set by the interplay between the sought-for signals and systematics suppressed by symmetry. Here we identify the previously unobserved $4f^{6}6s^{2}\,{}^{5}$D$_{0}$ level of neutral samarium at $14\,564.90(2)\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$, opening the ${}^{7}$F$_{0}\rightarrow{}^{5}$D$_{0}$ inner-shell transition for precision spectroscopy. Candidate lines extracted from dual-comb absorption spectra were assigned using double-resonance population-depletion and sequential-excitation measurements. The observed pressure broadening, $0.12(2)\,\mathrm{MHz/torr}$, and pressure shift, $0.145(4)\,\mathrm{MHz/torr}$, indicate an inner-shell $4f$-transition shielded from external perturbations. Many-body calculations predict a $\sim\!120\,\mathrm{ms}$ metastable lifetime (quality factor $\mathcal{Q}\sim 3\times 10^{14}$), large sensitivity coefficients for variation of the fine-structure constant, and a nuclear-spin-dependent parity-violation amplitude comparable to that of cesium. Crucially, the $J=0\rightarrow J=0$ selection rule suppresses by symmetry both the nuclear-spin-independent parity-violation channel and the M1 and E2 backgrounds that complicated previous heavy-atom experiments, yielding a uniquely clean window onto the nuclear anapole moment. The two stable spin-$7/2$ isotopes of samarium provide a remarkable opportunity to largely cancel atomic-structure uncertainties by measuring the ratio of parity-violation effects in the two isotopes. These results establish neutral samarium as a platform for inner-shell precision spectroscopy and tests of physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The previously unobserved $4f^{6}6s^{2}\,{}^{5}$D$_{0}$ level of neutral samarium has been identified at $14\,564.90(2)\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$, enabling a $J=0\rightarrow J=0$ inner-shell transition that is uniquely clean for measuring nuclear-spin-dependent parity violation because the selection rule suppresses by symmetry both the nuclear-spin-independent parity-violation channel and the M1 and E2 backgrounds that complicated earlier heavy-atom experiments.

## Method

Candidate spectral lines were extracted from dual-frequency-comb absorption spectra of an atomic samarium vapor and assigned using double-resonance population-depletion and sequential-excitation spectroscopy to filter transitions from the $^{7}$F$_{1}$ level to even-parity upper states. Pressure broadening and shift of the $^{7}$F $\rightarrow$ $^{5}$D transition were measured via Faraday rotation spectroscopy. Many-body calculations using configuration interaction and combined CI-perturbation theory methods were performed to predict the metastable lifetime, parity-violation amplitudes, and sensitivity coefficients for variation of the fine-structure constant.

## Key numbers

- Energy of the $4f^{6}6s^{2}\,{}^{5}$D$_{0}$ level: $14\,564.90(2)\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$
- Pressure broadening: $0.12(2)\,\mathrm{MHz/torr}$
- Pressure shift: $0.145(4)\,\mathrm{MHz/torr}$
- Predicted metastable lifetime: $\sim 120\,\mathrm{ms}$
- Quality factor: $\mathcal{Q} \sim 3 \times 10^{14}$
- M1 transition frequency: $14\,272.32\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$ ($\nu \approx 4.28\times10^{14}\,\mathrm{Hz}$)
- Reduced NSD-PV transition amplitude: $\langle ^5\mathrm{D}_0\,I || d_{\rm NSD} || ^7\mathrm{F}_0\, I \rangle \approx 3 \times 10^{-13}\, i \kappa \, |e| a_0$
- Sensitivity coefficient $q$ for $^{5}$D$_{0}$: $-3529\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$
- Sensitivity coefficient $q$ for $^{7}$P$_{2}$: $6091\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$
- Differential sensitivity $q(^7P_2)-q(^5D_0)$: $9620\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$
- Enhancement factor $\mathcal{K}$ for the $0.43$ THz transition between $^{5}$D$_{0}$ and $^{7}$P$_{2}$: $1336$
- Energy separation between $^{5}$D$_{0}$ and $^{7}$P$_{2}$ states: $14.4\,\mathrm{cm}^{-1}$
- Natural linewidth of the $^{5}$D$_{0}$ state: $1.3\,\mathrm{Hz}$

## Context

This work extends the search for the $4f^{6}6s^{2}\,{}^{5}$D$_{J}$ states in samarium that began with Dzuba (1986) and Barkov (1988), who identified the $^{5}$D$_{1}$, $^{5}$D$_{2}$, and $^{5}$D$_{3}$ levels but not the $^{5}$D$_{0}$ or $^{5}$D$_{4}$ states. Earlier attempts to measure parity violation in the identified M1 transitions by Davies (1989) and Wolfenden (1993) were unsuccessful, later explained by Rochester (1999) as resulting from erroneous term assignments. The newly identified $^{5}$D$_{0}$ level enables a $J=0\rightarrow J=0$ transition free from nuclear-spin-independent parity violation, offering a cleaner system than cesium (the only element where nuclear-spin-dependent parity violation has been observed, by Wood (1997)) and enabling ratio measurements between the two stable spin-$7/2$ isotopes $^{147}$Sm and $^{149}$Sm to cancel atomic-structure uncertainties.
