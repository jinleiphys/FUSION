# Bulk Viscosity of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Strong Magnetic Fields

> We investigate the viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma in strong magnetic fields within the leading-log and lowest Landau level (LLL) approximations. We first show that the bulk viscosity in the direction parallel to the magnetic field is the only component that has a contribution from the quarks occupying the LLL. We then compute the bulk viscosity from the Kubo formula and find an intriguing quark-mass dependence as a consequence of a competition between the suppression of the bulk viscosity by conformal symmetry and an enhancement of the mean-free path by chirality conservation, which governs the behavior in the massless limit. The quark contribution to the viscosity along the magnetic field becomes larger than the one in the absence of a magnetic field. We also briefly estimate the other transport coefficients by considering the contribution of gluons. We show that the shear viscosities are suppressed compared to their values in the absence of a magnetic field.

## Key claim

The longitudinal bulk viscosity of quark-gluon plasma in a strong magnetic field receives a contribution from quarks in the lowest Landau level (LLL) that is proportional to the square of the current quark mass, a result of competition between suppression by conformal symmetry and enhancement of the mean-free path by chirality conservation; this mass dependence is weaker than the quartic dependence at zero magnetic field, and the overall magnitude is enhanced when the magnetic field is strong.

## Method

The authors computed the longitudinal bulk viscosity using the Kubo formula within the leading-log and LLL approximations, assuming a strong magnetic field satisfying \(|B_f| \gg T^2 \gg m_f^2\). They evaluated the retarded Green's function for the longitudinal pressure via the real-time formalism, resumming the quark spectral function with a finite damping rate from 1-to-2 scattering processes, which dominate over 2-to-2 scatterings at weak coupling due to the dimensional mismatch between LLL quarks (1+1 dimensional) and gluons (3+1 dimensional).

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal bulk viscosity scales as \(\zeta_\parallel \sim eB T^2 (m_f^2/T^2)^2 T / [g^2 m_f^2 \ln(T/m_f)]\), with explicit numerical prefactor \(\mathcal{N}_c \sum_f |B_f|/(2\pi) \cdot m_f^2 / [g^2 C_f T \ln(T/m_f)] \cdot [4/\pi^2 - (56/3)\zeta'(-2)]\), where \(\zeta'(-2) \simeq -0.0304\).
* The quark damping rate for 1-to-2 scattering in the case \(M_g \ll m_f \ll T\) is \(\epsilon_k^L \xi_k \simeq (g^2 C_f m_f^2)/(4\pi) [1/2 + n_B(\epsilon_k^L)] \ln(T/m_f)\), with \(M_g^2 \equiv (1/2)(g^2/\pi) \sum_f |B_f|/(2\pi)\).
* The bulk viscosity at zero magnetic field scales as \(\zeta_{B=0} \sim T^4 (m_f^2/T^2)^2 / [g^4 T \ln(1/g)]\), showing that the strong-field result has a weaker \(m_f\) dependence (quadratic vs. quartic) and a stronger coupling dependence (\(g^{-2}\) vs. \(g^{-4}\)).

## Context

This work extends the LLL-based transport calculations of Hattori et al. (2016) for electrical conductivity to the bulk viscosity, and complements the magnetohydrodynamic transport coefficient framework of Huang et al. (2011). It contrasts with zero-field results from Arnold et al. (2006) and with weak-field shear viscosity calculations by Li et al. (2017) and Tuchin (2011), as well as holographic studies by Rebhan and Steineder (2011) and Giataganas and Soltanpanahi (2013). The results enable implementation of anisotropic transport coefficients in magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, where strong transient magnetic fields are generated.
