# Asymmetric Neutrino Production in Strongly Magnetized Proto-Neutron Stars

> We calculate the neutrino production cross-section through the direct URCA process in proto-neutron star matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field. We assume isoentropic conditions and introduce a new equation of state parameter-set in the relativistic mean-field approach that can reproduce neutron stars with $M > 1.96$ M$_\odot$ as required by observations. We find that the production process increases the flux of emitted neutrinos along the direction parallel to the magnetic field and decreases the flux in the opposite direction. This means that the neutrino flux asymmetry due to the neutrino absorption and scattering processes in a magnetic field becomes larger by the inclusion of the neutrino production process.

## Key claim

The neutrino production cross-section through the direct URCA process in strongly magnetized proto-neutron star matter is calculated to be asymmetric: the flux of emitted neutrinos increases along the direction parallel to the magnetic field and decreases in the opposite direction, and this asymmetry has the same sign and magnitude as the previously calculated asymmetry from neutrino absorption, so the total asymmetry from both processes should be about twice that from absorption alone.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with a new equation-of-state parameter-set that reproduces neutron stars with masses above 1.96 solar masses, using an isoentropic model (entropy per baryon S/A = 1 or 2) rather than an isothermal one. They calculated the neutrino absorption and production cross-sections through the direct URCA process perturbatively in the presence of a uniform dipole magnetic field of 10^17 G, treating the magnetic field as a perturbation on the spin interaction while ignoring the convection current contribution. The cross-sections were separated into a B-independent part and a part proportional to B, and the production cross-section was computed by integrating over the electron momentum distribution including its magnetic-field-dependent part.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density ρ_B = ρ_0 with entropy S/A = 1, the magnetic part of the absorption cross-section is suppressed by about 8.3% in the direction parallel to the magnetic field.
- At ρ_B = ρ_0 with entropy S/A = 2, the absorption cross-section suppression is about 4% in the parallel direction.
- At ρ_B = ρ_0 with entropy S/A = 1, the magnetic part of the production cross-section is enhanced by about 10% for θ_ν = 0° and suppressed by about 6% for θ_ν = 180°, giving an about 8% asymmetry.
- At ρ_B = ρ_0 with entropy S/A = 2, the production asymmetry is about 6% at θ_ν = 0° and 4% at θ_ν = 180°.
- The magnetic field strength used in the calculations is B = 10^17 G, corresponding to μ_N B = 0.32 MeV.
- The new equation of state reproduces neutron stars with M > 1.96 M_⊙ as required by observations.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same group (Maruyama et al., 2011, 2012) that computed neutrino scattering and absorption cross-sections in magnetized neutron-star matter including hyperons, but did not include the neutrino production process through the direct URCA process. The authors note that earlier work by Kisslinger and collaborators reported that Landau levels due to a magnetic field can cause asymmetry in neutrino emission leading to pulsar kick velocities, and that Voskresensky and Parenkov reported angular dependence of neutrino production in a pion condensation phase. The present results enable future calculations of pulsar kick velocities and spin-down that include all three magnetic effects (production, absorption, and scattering) simultaneously.
