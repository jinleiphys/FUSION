# GCM Analysis of the collective properties of lead isotopes with exact projection on particle numbers

> We present a microscopic analysis of the collective behaviour of the lead isotopes in the vicinity of Pb208. In this study, we rely on a coherent approach based on the Generator Coordinate Method including exact projection on N and Z numbers within a collective space generated by means of the constrained Hartree-Fock BCS method. With the same Hamiltonian used in HF+BCS calculations, we have performed a comprehensive study including monopole, quadrupole and octupole excitations as well as pairing vibrations. We find that, for the considered nuclei, the collective modes which modify the most the conclusions drawn from the mean-field theory are the octupole and pairing vibrations.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for lead isotopes near \(^{208}\)Pb, the collective modes that most significantly modify conclusions drawn from mean-field theory are octupole and pairing vibrations, with the GCM approach providing a systematic improvement over HF+BCS and Lipkin-Nogami methods, though the magnitude of correlation energies remains small (about 3 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock BCS calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent interaction for the pairing channel, generating collective spaces for monopole, quadrupole, and octupole shape vibrations as well as pairing vibrations. They then applied the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with exact projection on neutron and proton numbers to construct correlated ground states and collective excited states for five lead isotopes (\(^{204,206,208,210,212}\)Pb). The GCM solutions were obtained by solving the Hill-Wheeler equations, with approximate angular momentum projection used for quadrupole vibrations and parity projection for octupole vibrations.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy from octupole vibrations: approximately 1.4 MeV additional lowering relative to the Lipkin-Nogami reference, with an almost \(N\)-independent value across the five isotopes
- Correlation energy from monopole vibrations: approximately 0.2 MeV additional lowering relative to the Lipkin-Nogami reference
- Pairing vibration correlation energy: decreases by about 0.6 MeV over the five isotopes from \(^{204}\)Pb to \(^{212}\)Pb
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, the difference between projected Lipkin-Nogami energy and the variational minimum from pairing collective space: \(-0.21\) MeV
- For \(^{204}\)Pb, the difference between projected Lipkin-Nogami energy and the variational minimum from pairing collective space: \(-1.51\) MeV
- The BCS two-neutron separation energy drop between \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{210}\)Pb exceeds experimental data by approximately 1.8 MeV; GCM with pairing fluctuations improves this by about 0.6 MeV (33%)
- First excited monopole GCM state in \(^{208}\)Pb: 13.4 MeV, agreeing with monopole giant resonance data
- Nuclear matter incompressibility of the SLy4 force: 220 MeV
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, the Lipkin-Nogami projected state has neutron number fluctuation \(\sqrt{\langle\Delta\hat{N}^2\rangle} = 1.36\) and proton number fluctuation \(\sqrt{\langle\Delta\hat{Z}^2\rangle} = 1.17\); the variational minimum has \(\sqrt{\langle\Delta\hat{N}^2\rangle} = 1.92\) and \(\sqrt{\langle\Delta\hat{Z}^2\rangle} = 1.12\)

## Context

This work extends the GCM approach previously applied to quadrupole (KBF96) and octupole (SHB93) degrees of freedom, as well as coupled dipole-octupole modes in \(^{152}\)Sm and superdeformed \(^{190}\)Hg (HSk96), to a systematic study of five lead isotopes near the \(N=126\) magic number. The paper tests the validity of the Lipkin-Nogami prescription (Lip60, NZu64, PNL73) as an approximation to variation after projection, finding that its accuracy is nucleus-dependent and that it does not improve the mean-field two-neutron separation energies at the shell closure. The results enable future studies of how collective correlations affect ground state properties in magic and near-magic nuclei, and suggest that effective forces should be parametrized to slightly underbind to leave room for the small correlation corrections computed here.
