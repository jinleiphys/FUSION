# Fine structure of Vavilov-Cherenkov radiation near the Cherenkov threshold

> We analyze the Vavilov-Cherenkov radiation (VCR) in a dispersive nontransparent dielectric air-like medium both below and above the Cherenkov threshold, in the framework of classical electrodynamics. It is shown that the transition to the subthreshold energies leads to the destruction of electromagnetic shock waves and to the sharp reduction of the frequency domain where VCR is emitted. The fine wake-like structure of the Vavilov-Cherenkov radiation survives and manifests the existence of the subthreshold radiation in the domain of anomalous dispersion. These domains can approximately be defined by the two phenomenological parameters of the medium, namely, the effective frequency of oscillators and the damping describing an interaction with the other degrees of freedom.

## Key claim
The paper claims that Vavilov-Cherenkov radiation (VCR) persists below the conventional Cherenkov threshold velocity, but only in the domain of anomalous dispersion of the medium; this subthreshold radiation manifests as a fine wake-like structure rather than a sharp shock wave, and its existence depends on the specific phenomenological parameters of the medium (effective oscillator frequency and damping).

## Method
The authors solve Maxwell's equations in classical electrodynamics for a point charge moving uniformly in a dispersive dielectric medium, using a complex dielectric function with a single-resonance Lorentz oscillator model (plasma frequency, resonance frequency, and damping parameter). They numerically evaluate the electromagnetic field strengths, Poynting vector, and spectral energy flux radiated through a cylindrical surface coaxial with the charge trajectory, for velocities both above and below the Cherenkov threshold.

## Key numbers
- The particle velocity considered is \(\beta \sim 0.999\) (where \(\beta = v/c\)).
- The Cherenkov threshold velocity \(\beta_c\) is varied from \(0.99\) to \(0.9999\), corresponding to an approximately 100-fold decrease in gas pressure.
- The cylinder radius used for field evaluation is \(\rho = 10\,[c/\omega_0]\), where \(\omega_0\) is the resonance frequency of the medium.
- The period of rapid oscillations in the radiation intensity is estimated as \(T \sim 10^{-15}\) seconds for air-like media.
- For \(\beta > \beta_c\), all frequencies in the interval \(0 < \omega < \omega_0\) contribute to energy losses; for \(\beta < \beta_c\), this interval diminishes.

## Context
This work extends the classical Tamm-Frank theory of Cherenkov radiation (Tamm and Frank, 1937) and Fermi's treatment (Fermi, 1940) by including a realistic dispersive dielectric function with damping, motivated by recent experimental measurements of VCR induced by relativistic electrons in air (Ruzicka) and by lead ions in the NA49 experiment (CERN, JINR, Comenius University). The analysis enables quantitative predictions for subthreshold radiation in air-like media and identifies the experimental challenge of resolving the predicted rapid temporal oscillations (period \(\sim 10^{-15}\) s) with current detectors.
