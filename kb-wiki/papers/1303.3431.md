# Relativistic distribution function for particles with spin at local thermodynamical equilibrium

> We present an extension of relativistic single-particle distribution function for weakly interacting particles at local thermodynamical equilibrium including spin degrees of freedom, for massive spin 1/2 particles. We infer, on the basis of the global equilibrium case, that at local thermodynamical equilibrium particles acquire a net polarization proportional to the vorticity of the inverse temperature four-vector field. The obtained formula for polarization also implies that a steady gradient of temperature entails a polarization orthogonal to particle momentum. The single-particle distribution function in momentum space extends the so-called Cooper-Frye formula to particles with spin 1/2 and allows to predict their polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the freeze-out.

# Relativistic distribution function for particles with spin at local thermodynamical equilibrium

## Key claim

The authors extend the relativistic single-particle distribution function to include spin degrees of freedom for massive spin-1/2 particles at local thermodynamical equilibrium, arguing that a net polarization arises proportional to the vorticity of the inverse temperature four-vector field. The derivation relies on an ansatz that the global equilibrium form generalizes to local equilibrium with space-time dependent thermodynamic functions, and the result is valid only for weakly interacting particles and to lowest order in gradients of the beta field.

## Method

The authors start from the covariant Wigner function for the Dirac field and define spin-dependent distribution functions as 2x2 matrices in polarization space. They derive the form of these matrices at global thermodynamical equilibrium with rotation using the density operator with angular momentum, then conjecture an extension to Fermi-Dirac statistics. For local thermodynamical equilibrium, they promote the intensive thermodynamic parameters to space-time dependent fields, assuming the same formal expression holds. The tensor coupling to spin is identified with the antisymmetric part of the gradient of the inverse temperature four-vector, up to second-order gradient corrections.

## Key numbers

- The spin tensor expression (equation for S^{lambda,mu nu}) involves an integral over momentum space with the factor 1/(4m) times traces of gamma matrix products, where m is the particle mass.
- The expansion of the trace of X (the matrix inside the spinor sandwich) yields: tr X approx = 4 n_F + (1/2) n_F (1-n_F)(1-2n_F) varpi:varpi, where n_F = 1/(exp[beta(x)*p - xi(x)] + 1) and varpi is the dimensionless tensor coupling to spin.
- The charge density correction at lowest order is quadratic in varpi: j^0(x) = 2 integral d^3p (n_F - nbar_F) + (varpi:varpi)/4 integral d^3p [n_F(1-n_F)(1-2n_F) - nbar_F(1-nbar_F)(1-2nbar_F)].
- At rotational equilibrium, the condition for the expansion to be valid is hbar omega/(kT) << 1 (natural units restored).

## Context

This work extends the standard Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) for particle spectra at freeze-out in relativistic heavy ion collisions to include spin-1/2 degrees of freedom. It builds on prior results for global thermodynamical equilibrium with rotation by Becattini and collaborators (2012) and on the relativistic Kubo formalism by Hosoya and collaborators (1984). The formalism enables predictions of particle polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the hydrodynamical decoupling stage, provided local thermodynamical equilibrium applies to spin degrees of freedom as well.
