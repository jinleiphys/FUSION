# Radial flow in a multi phase transport model at FAIR energies

> Azimuthal distributions of radial velocities of charged hadrons produced in nucleus-nucleus $(AB)$ collisions are compared with the corresponding azimuthal distribution of charged hadron multiplicity in the framework of a multiphase transport (AMPT) model at two different collision energies. The mean radial velocity seems to be a good probe for studying radial expansion. While the anisotropic part of the distributions indicates a kind of collective nature of radial expansion, the isotropic part characterizes a thermal motion. The present investigation is carried out keeping the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment to be held at the Facility for Anti-proton Ion Research (FAIR) in mind. As far as high-energy heavy-ion interactions are concerned, CBM will supplement the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) experiments. In this context our simulation results at high baryochemical potential would be interesting, when scrutinized from the perspective of almost a baryon free environment achieved at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The azimuthal anisotropy of final-state charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies (E_lab = 10A and 40A GeV) is predominantly due to the asymmetry of particle multiplicity distribution, with only a small fraction of the asymmetry arising from kinematic (radial velocity) effects; the mean radial velocity, which removes multiplicity influences, shows significantly smaller elliptic anisotropy than the total radial velocity or multiplicity distributions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with a parton scattering cross section of sigma = 3 mb to simulate Au+Au minimum bias events at E_lab = 10A and 40A GeV. They constructed azimuthal distributions of total radial velocity, mean radial velocity, and charged hadron multiplicity, and performed Fourier decomposition retaining only the n=0 (isotropic) and n=2 (elliptic) terms. The centrality dependence was characterized using a Monte Carlo Glauber model to determine the number of participating nucleons (N_part).

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity distributions were generated from samples of one million Au+Au events at each energy (E_lab = 10A and 40A GeV)
- Average transverse momentum saturates at approximately 0.365 GeV/c for the most central collisions at both energies
- The parton scattering cross section was set to sigma = 3 mb, chosen to match previously studied collective behavior at FAIR energies
- The analysis of v_2 as a function of transverse momentum was restricted to p_T up to 2.0 GeV/c due to rare production of high-p_T hadrons at FAIR energies
- The v_0 values from mean radial velocity at E_lab = 10A GeV are consistently higher than those at 40A GeV, while v_0 from multiplicity shows the opposite energy dependence

## Context

This simulation study extends prior work on elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies (Adler 2001, Abelev 2007, Adamczyk 2013, Chatrchyan 2012, Aad 2012) to the high baryochemical potential regime relevant for the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR. The authors compare with previous AMPT model results at FAIR energies (Partha 2010, Sarkar 2017a, Sarkar 2017b) and with NA49 experimental results. The work enables future modeling of radial flow in terms of thermodynamic and hydrodynamic parameters of the intermediate fireball produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
