# Quantum Closures for Neutrino Moment Transport

> A computationally efficient method for calculating the transport of neutrino flavor in simulations is to use angular moments of the neutrino one-body reduced density matrix, i.e., `quantum moments'. As with any moment-based radiation transport method, a closure is needed if the infinite tower of moment evolution equations is truncated. We derive a general parameterization of a quantum closure and the limits the parameters must satisfy in order for the closure to be physical. We then derive from multi-angle calculations the evolution of the closure parameters in two test cases which we then progressively insert into a moment evolution code and show how the parameters affect the moment results until the full multi-angle results are reproduced. This parameterization paves the way to setting prescriptions for genuine quantum closures adapted to neutrino transport in a range of situations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general parameterization of a quantum closure for neutrino moment transport, showing that for two neutrino flavors the closure is described by four parameters that must satisfy specific physical constraints, and demonstrates that progressively inserting these parameters into a moment evolution code can reproduce full multi-angle results.

## Method

The authors start from a general ansatz relating the pressure tensor moment \(P_{rr}\) to the energy density moment \(E\) via \(P_{rr} = L E R\) and derive the constraints on the matrices \(L\) and \(R\) required for Hermiticity. They then express the closure in terms of the eigenvalues of the moments and the unitary matrices that diagonalize them, identifying four free parameters for two neutrino flavors. Using multi-angle calculations in two test cases, they extract the exact evolution of the closure parameters and progressively insert them into a moment evolution code to compare with the full multi-angle results.

## Key numbers

* The Frobenius norm of a \(2\times 2\) Hermitian matrix \(M\) is given by \(||M||_F^2 = (M^{\widetilde{t}})^2 + |\vec{M}|^2\), where the scalar components are defined with a factor of \(1/\sqrt{2}\) relative to the Pauli matrices.
* For a positive semi-definite \(2\times 2\) matrix \(M\), the speed \(v = |\vec{v}| = |\vec{M}|/M^{\widetilde{t}}\) satisfies \(0 \leq v \leq 1\).
* The unitary matrix \(\Upsilon\) that diagonalizes \(M\) has scalar components \(\Upsilon^{\widetilde{x}} = \sqrt{2}\sin(\theta/2)\cos\phi\), \(\Upsilon^{\widetilde{y}} = \sqrt{2}\sin(\theta/2)\sin\phi\), \(\Upsilon^{\widetilde{z}} = \sqrt{2}\cos(\theta/2)\), with \(|\vec{\Upsilon}|^2 = 2\) and \(\det(\Upsilon) = -1\).
* The angle \(\Xi\) between two matrices \(M\) and \(N\) is defined by \(\cos\Xi = \langle M,N\rangle_F / (||M||_F ||N||_F)\), which equals \(\cos\eta_M\cos\eta_N + \sin\eta_M\sin\eta_N[\cos\theta_M\cos\theta_N + \sin\theta_M\sin\theta_N\cos(\phi_M-\phi_N)]\).

## Context

This work extends the classical closure problem in neutrino transport, which has been studied with prescriptions such as Minerbo (1978), Levermore (1984), and Cernohorsky (1994), into the quantum domain where flavor coherence must be accounted for. It builds on prior quantum moment approaches that used ad hoc scalar closures (Myers 2021) or semi-classical extensions of the Minerbo closure limited to small flavor coherence (Froustey 2024). The parameterization enables future development of genuine quantum closure prescriptions adapted to neutrino transport in core-collapse supernovae and compact object mergers, where flavor evolution affects dynamics, nucleosynthesis, and the neutrino signal.
