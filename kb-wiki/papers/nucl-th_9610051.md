# Electromagnetic Corrections to Pi Pi Scattering: Some Lessons for the Implementation of Meson Exchange Models

> The leading non-Coulombic electromagnetic contributions to pi pi s-wave scattering lengths are computed in Chiral Perturbation Theory. It is shown that these corrections are zeroth order in the chiral expansion and associated with electromagnetic contact terms in the effective Lagrangian, i.e. that they do not involve explicit photon fields in the low-energy effective theory. It is pointed out that, if one followed the standard meson-exchange-model ansatz for removing electromagnetic effects, i.e. of subtracting contributions associated with explicit photon exchange and radiative corrections, as determined by the photon coupling vertices of the effective hadronic theory, one would completely miss these contributions and arrive at the erroneous conclusion that the strong interactions exhibited very large isospin breaking in pi pi scattering. Implications for electromagnetic "subtraction" procedures in other hadronic systems and the utility of the effective Lagrangian method for avoiding such errors are obvious.

# Electromagnetic Corrections to Pi Pi Scattering: Some Lessons for the Implementation of Meson Exchange Models

## Key claim

The leading non-Coulombic electromagnetic contributions to pi pi s-wave scattering lengths are zeroth order in the chiral expansion and arise from electromagnetic contact terms in the effective Lagrangian (involving no explicit photon fields), not from one-photon exchange or radiative corrections. Standard meson-exchange-model subtraction procedures that remove only explicit photon-exchange and radiative corrections would completely miss these contact contributions and erroneously imply very large strong isospin breaking in pi pi scattering.

## Method

The authors computed the leading (zeroth chiral order) electromagnetic contact contributions to s-wave pi pi scattering lengths using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with SU(2)xSU(2) symmetry, fixing the contact term coefficient c_pi from the known electromagnetic charged-neutral pion mass splitting. They then compared the resulting scattering length corrections to the previously computed radiative corrections of Roig and Swift (1976), and demonstrated the discrepancies that would arise if one attempted to extract strong isospin-breaking from "electromagnetically subtracted" experimental data using only one-photon-exchange removal.

## Key numbers

- The contact term coefficient is fixed as c_pi = (m^2_pi+ - m^2_pi0)/2, using the known purely electromagnetic charged-neutral pion mass splitting.
- At lowest chiral order, the strong s-wave scattering lengths are a0^0 = 7 m_pi^2 / (32 pi F^2) = 0.16 and a0^2 = -m_pi^2 / (16 pi F^2) = -0.045 (Weinberg values).
- The one-loop strong ChPT values are a0^0 = 0.20 +/- 0.01 and a0^2 = -0.042 +/- 0.008 (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984).
- The zeroth-order EM contact corrections to the five scattering lengths range from 0% (for pi0 pi0 -> pi0 pi0) to 10% (for pi+ pi+ -> pi+ pi+).
- The radiative corrections of Roig and Swift (1976) are much smaller: 0%, -0.2%, -0.6%, -1.3%, and +2.2% for the five channels respectively.
- If one incorrectly assumed all EM effects were removed by one-photon-exchange subtraction, extracting a0^2 from different combinations of amplitudes would yield -0.0507 and -0.0355, a 43% discrepancy.
- For the experimentally used channels (pi+ pi+ -> pi+ pi+ and pi+ pi- -> pi0 pi0), subtracting the contact EM contamination shifts a0^0 from 0.21 to 0.20 and a0^2 from -0.031 to -0.035 (4% and 14% corrections respectively), improving agreement with one-loop ChPT.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) for pi pi scattering by incorporating the leading electromagnetic contact contributions that had been omitted in previous treatments. It directly contradicts the standard meson-exchange-model approach to electromagnetic subtraction, exemplified by the radiative correction calculation of Roig and Swift (1976) for pi pi scattering and by analogous procedures used for NN scattering to extract strong charge-independence-breaking differences. The paper demonstrates that electromagnetic contact terms, necessarily present in any low-energy effective theory of QCD, produce numerically significant effects that cannot be reproduced by form-factor modifications to explicit photon-exchange graphs. This enables improved extraction of the strong pi pi scattering lengths from experimental data and warns that similar contact contributions must be accounted for in electromagnetic subtractions for other hadronic systems such as pi NN couplings and NN scattering lengths.
