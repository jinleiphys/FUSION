# Locating the Gribov horizon

> We explore whether a tree-level expression for the gluon two-point function, supposed to express effects of an horizon term introduced to eliminate the Gribov ambiguity, is consistent with the propagator obtained in simulations of lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In doing so, we insist that the gluon two-point function obey constraints that ensure a minimal level of consistency with parton-like behaviour at ultraviolet momenta. In consequence, we are led to a position which supports a conjecture that the gluon mass and horizon scale are equivalent emergent mass-scales, each with a value of roughly $0.5\,$GeV; and wherefrom it appears plausible that the dynamical generation of a running gluon mass may alone be sufficient to remove the Gribov ambiguity.

## Key claim
The paper supports the conjecture that the gluon mass and the Gribov horizon scale are equivalent emergent mass-scales, each with a value of roughly 0.5 GeV, and that the dynamical generation of a running gluon mass may alone be sufficient to remove the Gribov ambiguity. This conclusion is conditional on imposing partonic constraints on the gluon propagator that ensure consistency with ultraviolet behaviour.

## Method
The authors fit a modified tree-level gluon propagator (Eq. overlineD), which includes a horizon term and dimension-two condensates, to lattice QCD results for the gluon two-point function in Landau gauge. They impose either a sufficient or a necessary condition for parton-like behaviour at ultraviolet momenta, derived from convexity requirements on the configuration-space Schwinger function. The fits are performed on both quenched (N_f=0) and unquenched (N_f=4) lattice data, with parameters determined by minimising the rms relative-difference between the model and lattice results on an infrared momentum domain up to an optimised scale k_0.

## Key numbers
- The Gribov horizon scale m_gamma and gluon mass m_g are both approximately 0.5 GeV, with specific values: for quenched fits under the necessary condition, m_g = 0.53 GeV and m_gamma = 0.56 GeV; under the sufficient condition, m_g = 0.59 GeV and m_gamma = 0.39 GeV.
- The inflection point in the gluon propagator, indicating confinement, lies at k_ip^2 = (0.48 GeV)^2 for quenched fits under both sufficient and necessary conditions.
- The first zero of the configuration-space gluon Schwinger function, interpreted as a confinement scale, is at tau_z = 0.96-0.97 fm for quenched fits under both constraints.
- The hadronisation length (where the Schwinger function deviates from partonic behaviour by 50%) is tau_H = 0.67 fm (sufficient condition) or 0.81 fm (necessary condition) for quenched data.
- The dimension-two condensate m_{A^2} inferred from quenched fits is 0.86 GeV (sufficient condition) or 2.22 GeV (necessary condition).

## Context
This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger framework (Gribov 1977, Zwanziger 1989) by testing whether a modified gluon propagator with a horizon term can simultaneously satisfy partonic ultraviolet constraints and describe lattice QCD results. It builds on earlier studies of the gluon propagator's infrared behaviour (Aguilar 2006, Cucchieri 2007, Dudal 2008, Bogolubsky 2009) and on the operator product expansion analysis of dimension-two condensates (Dudal 2010, Boucaud 2011). The results enable a clearer interpretation of the Gribov horizon as an emergent mass scale equivalent to the dynamically generated gluon mass, and suggest that future unquenched lattice simulations with better infrared sensitivity could confirm the equivalence m_gamma = m_g.
