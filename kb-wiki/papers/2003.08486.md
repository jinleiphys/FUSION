# Complete flavor decomposition of the spin and momentum fraction of the proton using lattice QCD simulations at physical pion mass

> We evaluate the gluon and quark contributions to the spin of the proton using an ensemble of gauge configuration generated at physical pion mass. We compute all valence and sea quark contributions to high accuracy. We perform a non-perturbative renormalization for both quark and gluon matrix elements. We find that the contribution of the up, down, strange and charm quarks to the proton intrinsic spin is $\frac{1}{2}\sum_{q=u,d,s,c}ΔΣ^{q^+}=0.191(15)$ and to the total spin $\sum_{q=u,d,s,c}J^{q^+}=0.285(45)$. The gluon contribution to the spin is $J^g=0.187(46)$ yielding $J=J^q+J^g=0.473(71)$ confirming the spin sum. The momentum fraction carried by quarks in the proton is found to be $0.618(60)$ and by gluons $0.427(92)$, the sum of which gives $1.045(118)$ confirming the momentum sum rule. All scale and scheme dependent quantities are given in the $\mathrm{ \overline{MS}}$ scheme at 2 GeV.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD simulations at physical pion mass with dynamical up, down, strange, and charm quarks, the authors provide the first complete flavor decomposition of the proton spin and momentum fraction, including both valence and sea quark contributions as well as gluon contributions, with all quantities renormalized non-perturbatively in the MS scheme at 2 GeV. The abstract's claim of "confirming the spin sum" and "confirming the momentum sum rule" is qualified by the fact that the sums are consistent within statistical uncertainties, with the total spin J=0.473(71) and total momentum fraction 1.045(118), both compatible with the expected values of 0.5 and 1.0 respectively.

## Method
The authors computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions on an N_f=2+1+1 twisted mass fermion ensemble with lattice spacing a=0.0801(4) fm and spatial extent L=5.12 fm at physical pion mass m_pi=0.1393(7) GeV. Connected three-point functions were evaluated using sequential propagators through the sink for seven sink-source separations from 0.64 fm to 1.60 fm with increasing statistics up to 48,000 measurements per configuration, while disconnected quark loop contributions were computed using hierarchical probing combined with deflation of low modes for light quarks and stochastic techniques for strange and charm quarks. The gluon contribution to the total angular momentum J^g was obtained by computing the generalized form factor B_20(Q^2) at non-zero momentum transfer and extrapolating to Q^2=0, using the gluon field strength tensor constructed from stout-smeared gauge links.

## Key numbers
- Quark intrinsic spin contribution: (1/2) * sum_{q=u,d,s,c} Delta Sigma^{q+} = 0.191(15) in the MS scheme at 2 GeV
- Quark total spin contribution: sum_{q=u,d,s,c} J^{q+} = 0.285(45)
- Gluon total spin contribution: J^g = 0.187(46)
- Total proton spin: J = J^q + J^g = 0.473(71)
- Quark momentum fraction: 0.618(60)
- Gluon momentum fraction: 0.427(92)
- Total momentum fraction: 1.045(118)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0801(4) fm
- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.1393(7) GeV
- Nucleon mass: m_N = 0.3813(19) in lattice units (approximately 0.94 GeV)

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (Alexandrou 2017, 2019) by using an N_f=2+1+1 ensemble with physical pion mass instead of N_f=2, performing a more elaborated analysis of excited state contributions, using larger statistics, computing the gluon contribution to the proton spin including the generalized form factor B_20(0), and employing non-perturbative renormalization for both quark and gluon operators. It provides the first complete quark flavor decomposition of the proton spin from lattice QCD at the physical point, enabling direct comparison with experimental results from COMPASS, RHIC, and future Electron-Ion Collider measurements, and supersedes earlier quenched approximations by QCDSF (Gockeler 1996, Horsley 2012) and chiQCD (Deka 2013, Yang 2016, 2018).
