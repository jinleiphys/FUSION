# Initial-state geometry and multiplicity distributions in pp and pPb collisions

> This work investigates the possibility of accessing the initial geometric shape of the proton in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC. In particular, we look for manifestations of the configuration in which the proton is made of three quarks linked by a Y-shape gluon string, called baryon junction. This initial state spatial configuration has been used in the past to describe data on baryon rapidity distributions, diffractive $J/ψ$ production and multiplicity distributions in pp collisions. In spite of its success in explaining the data, the evidence of the baryon junction still needs confirmation. Further studies will be undertaken at the electron-ion collider. In this work we study multiplicity distributions measured in pp and pPb collisions. Different initial state geometries are used as input in a Monte Carlo event generator which implements the $k_T$-factorization formalism of the CGC with KLN unintegrated gluon distributions. The results show that the data on multiplicity distributions are good enough to discriminate between different initial state geometries. Moreover, they indicate that it is crucial to take into account the intrinsic fluctuations of the saturation scale.

# Initial-state geometry and multiplicity distributions in pp and pPb collisions

## Key claim
Multiplicity distributions measured in pp and pPb collisions at the LHC can discriminate between different initial-state geometries of the proton, but the evidence for the baryon junction (Y-shape gluon string) configuration still needs confirmation; the data favor the baryon junction models only in ultracentral pp collisions (z >= 4) and in pPb collisions, where BJ1 is clearly favored, provided that intrinsic fluctuations of the saturation scale are included.

## Method
The authors used the MC-KLN Monte Carlo event generator, which implements the kT-factorization formalism of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) with KLN unintegrated gluon distributions, to simulate gluon production in pp and pPb collisions. Four different initial-state proton geometries were tested: hard-sphere, Gaussian, an analytical baryon junction (BJ1), and a numerical baryon junction (BJ2). The lead nucleus was modeled using a Woods-Saxon distribution with 208 nucleons. Parton-hadron duality was assumed to convert produced gluons into charged hadrons, and intrinsic fluctuations of the saturation scale were included via a log-normal distribution with width sigma.

## Key numbers
- pp collisions were studied at sqrt(s) = 2.76, 5.02, 7, 8, and 13 TeV; pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 and 8.16 TeV, in the range 0.15 < pT < 10 GeV/c and -0.8 < eta < 0.8.
- The hard-sphere model uses sigma_0 = 42 mb for the transverse area of large-x partons.
- The Gaussian nucleon width B_p is approximately 4 GeV^-2, related to the proton root mean square radius as r_p = sqrt(2B_p).
- For BJ1, the hot-spot size B_h = 0.17 fm, the gluon fraction kappa = 0.5, and the quark separation distance d = 1.5 fm.
- For BJ2, the characteristic proton size B_r = 0.41^2 fm^2 and the gluon flux tube squared radius B_t = 0.15^2 fm^2.
- The saturation scale parameter bar-lambda = 0.23.
- The pion mass used in the y-to-eta Jacobian is m_pi approximately 140 MeV.
- The lead nucleus radius is 6.62 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Kharzeev (2000) on baryon junction mechanisms for baryon stopping, by Schenke et al. on diffractive J/psi production with stringy proton configurations, and by Lappi et al. (2016) on multiplicity distributions in pp collisions with saturation scale fluctuations. It also builds on the work of Deb et al. (2020) on BJ initial conditions in pp collisions, now extending to pPb collisions and comparing with recent ALICE data (2024). The results suggest that pPb collisions are better probes of proton geometry than pp collisions, and that further studies at the electron-ion collider will be needed to confirm the baryon junction.
