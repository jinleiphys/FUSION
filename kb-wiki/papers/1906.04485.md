# Bayesian Evaluation of Incomplete Fission Yields

> Fission product yields are key infrastructure data for nuclear applications in many aspects. It is a challenge both experimentally and theoretically to obtain accurate and complete energy-dependent fission yields. We apply the Bayesian neural network (BNN) approach to learn existed fission yields and predict unknowns with uncertainty quantification. We demonstrated that BNN is particularly useful for evaluations of fission yields when incomplete experimental data are available. The BNN results are quite satisfactory on distribution positions and energy dependencies of fission yields.

## Key claim

The Bayesian neural network (BNN) approach can evaluate incomplete fission product yields with uncertainty quantification, but its reliability is strongest for distribution positions and energy dependencies, while detailed peak structures are less accurate; the method is most successful when the learning set includes neighboring isotopes.

## Method

The authors applied a feed-forward Bayesian neural network with inputs of charge number, neutron number, mass number of the fission fragment, and excitation energy of the compound nucleus. They trained the network on evaluated independent fission yields from the JENDL library for 30 actinide nuclei, using Hybrid Markov Chain Monte Carlo integration for posterior inference. A reinforcement learning scheme with resampling of points showing large deviations was employed, and in some cases the BNN was combined with the Talys code (using the GEF model) to learn residuals.

## Key numbers

- For single fission of n+235U at 0.5 MeV with 6 neurons and 107 data points, the learning achieved a total χ²_N of 2.25×10⁻⁶ after 100,000 BNN samplings; resampling improved this to 2.05×10⁻⁶.
- For the full multi-nucleus learning set (5029 data points), the best pure BNN performance (BNN-32-resample) gave learning χ²_N of 1.314×10⁻⁵ and validation χ²_N of 1.179×10⁻⁵.
- The best BNN+Talys combination (BNN-40) achieved learning χ²_N of 1.139×10⁻⁵ and validation χ²_N of 1.134×10⁻⁵.
- Default Talys calculations (pre-neutron FPY) gave learning χ²_N of 17.56×10⁻⁵ and validation χ²_N of 8.964×10⁻⁵.

## Context

This work extends the application of Bayesian neural networks from nuclear mass predictions (Witek 2018, Liu, Jorge, Zhang) to fission product yield evaluation, addressing the limitation that major nuclear data libraries (ENDF, JENDL, JEFF, CENDL) only provide complete evaluations at thermal, 0.5 MeV, and 14 MeV incident energies. The BNN approach is contrasted with conventional phenomenological evaluations using the Brosa model (Brosa) and the Talys/GEF codes, which cannot be applied when few experimental data are available. The method enables evaluation of incomplete fission yields at intermediate energies (e.g., 1.37, 4.49, 8.9, 14.8 MeV) for fast reactor applications, and could be improved by incorporating microscopic fission models such as time-dependent generator-coordinate method calculations.
