# The microscopic structure of $πNN$, $πNΔ$ and $πΔΔ$ vertices in a hybrid constituent quark model

> We present a microscopic description of the strong $πNN$, $πNΔ$ and $πΔΔ$ vertices. Our starting point is a constituent-quark model supplemented by an additional $3qπ$ non-valence component. In the spirit of chiral constituent-quark models, quarks are allowed to emit and reabsorb a pion. This multichannel system is treated in a relativistically invariant way within the framework of point-form quantum mechanics. Starting with a common $SU(6)$ spin-flavor-symmetric wave function for $N$ and $Δ$, we calculate the strength of the $πNN$, $πNΔ$ and $πΔΔ$ couplings and the corresponding vertex form factors. Our results are in accordance with phenomenological fits of these quantities that have been obtained within purely hadronic multichannel models for baryon resonances.

### Key claim
The paper presents a microscopic calculation of the πNN, πNΔ, and πΔΔ vertices from a constituent-quark model with explicit pionic degrees of freedom, finding that the resulting coupling ratios and form factors agree with phenomenological fits from hadronic multichannel models, but only after the model parameters are tuned to reproduce the physical N and Δ masses via pion-loop renormalization.

### Method
The authors start from a constituent-quark model with a 3qπ non-valence component, where quarks emit and reabsorb pions via a pseudovector interaction. They treat the system relativistically using point-form quantum mechanics and the Bakamjian-Thomas construction, formulating the mass eigenvalue problem as a two-channel system (3q and 3qπ). After eliminating the 3qπ channel, the problem reduces to a hadronic one with bare baryons dressed by pion loops, where the quark substructure enters only through the vertex form factors. The bare N and Δ are assumed degenerate with a common SU(6) spin-flavor-symmetric wave function parameterized by a Gaussian, and the model parameters (M₀ = 1.552 GeV, α = 2.56 GeV⁻¹, f_πqq = 0.6953) are fixed by fitting the physical N and Δ masses.

### Key numbers
- The bare N and Δ mass is M₀ = 1.552 GeV (degenerate), with constituent-quark mass m_q = 263 MeV.
- The oscillator parameter for the Gaussian 3q wave function is α = 2.56 GeV⁻¹.
- The πqq coupling strength is f_πqq = 0.6953.
- The extracted coupling ratio is f_πN₀Δ₀ : f_πN₀N₀ : f_πΔ₀Δ₀ = 1.208 : 1 : 0.608.
- The normalization point for the πN₀Δ₀ vertex is Q₀² = -0.090 GeV², close to the pion pole.
- The πN₀N₀ form factor is compared up to Q² ≈ 1 GeV² with other models.

### Context
This work extends the chiral constituent-quark model approach of Glozman (1995) by providing explicit microscopic predictions for the πNN, πNΔ, and πΔΔ vertices, which are input to purely hadronic dynamical coupled-channel models such as those of Kamano (2013) and Polinder (2005). The results enable future calculations of electromagnetic form factors for the nucleon and Δ, as well as N→Δ transition form factors, where pionic effects are expected to be significant at low Q² (≲ 0.5 GeV²). The authors note that their coupling ratios differ from the non-relativistic SU(6) predictions of Brown (1975) due to relativistic effects, and that their form factor falls off slower at high Q² than those from Melde (2008) or Kamano (2013).
