# Spacetime structure of massive gravitino

> We present reasons as to why an ab initio analysis of the spacetime structure of massive gravitino is necessary. Afterwards, we construct the relevant representation space, and finally, give a new physical interpretation of massive gravitino.

## Key claim

The massive gravitino's representation space naturally contains three distinct sectors (tau = a, b, c) with different eigenvalues of the dragged second Casimir operator, and the standard Rarita-Schwinger framework's imposition of supplementary conditions to enforce a pure spin-3/2 interpretation is unjustified; the tau = b and c sectors should not be treated as redundant spin-1/2 components but as physically necessary for mathematical completeness.

## Method

The authors constructed the (1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2) spinor sector and the (1/2,1/2) vector sector ab initio using boost operators in momentum space, then formed the tensor product representation space for the spinor-vector psi^mu. They evaluated the action of the divergence operator p_mu, the gamma-trace operator gamma_mu, and the dragged Pauli-Lubanski pseudovector on all sixteen basis spinor-vectors, classifying them into three sectors by their eigenvalues of the dragged second Casimir operator.

## Key numbers

- The tau = a sector has dragged second Casimir eigenvalue lambda_c = 15/4, corresponding to the Rarita-Schwinger sector
- The tau = b and tau = c sectors both have lambda_c = 3/4, but differ in their behavior under p_mu and gamma_mu
- For an unpolarized ensemble of massive gravitinos at rest, a spin measurement yields spin 3/2 with probability one half, and spin 1/2 with probability one half (distributed uniformly as one quarter each over tau = b and tau = c)
- The tau = a sector contains 8 spinor-vectors (4 particle, 4 antiparticle), while tau = b and tau = c each contain 4 spinor-vectors

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ab initio construction of the (1/2,1/2) representation space (Ahluwalia and Kirchbach, 2001), which showed that the Proca description is incomplete and naturally contains the Stuckelberg contribution. It contradicts the standard Rarita-Schwinger framework (Rarita and Schwinger, 1941) by arguing that the supplementary conditions gamma_mu psi^mu = 0 and partial_mu psi^mu = 0 are not physically mandated, and that the kinematic acausality problems noted by Johnson and Sudarshan (1961) and Velo and Zwanziger (1969) arise from incorrectly imposing these conditions on the tau = b and c sectors. The analysis is motivated by empirical clustering of N and Delta resonances (Kirchbach, 2000) in representations without supplementary conditions, and enables a reinterpretation of massive gravitinos as objects characterized by dragged Casimir eigenvalues rather than definite spin.
