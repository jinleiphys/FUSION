# Momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors of doubly-closed shell nuclei in correlated basis function theory

> The momentum distributions, natural orbits, spectroscopic factors and quasi-hole wave functions of the C12, O16, Ca40, Ca48, and Pb208 doubly closed shell nuclei, have been calculated in the framework of the Correlated Basis Function theory, by using the Fermi hypernetted chain resummation techniques. The calculations have been done by using the realistic Argonne v8' nucleon-nucleon potential, together with the Urbana IX three-body interaction. Operator dependent correlations, which consider channels up to the tensor ones, have been used. We found noticeable effects produced by the correlations. For high momentum values, the momentum distributions show large enhancements with respect to the independent particle model results. Natural orbits occupation numbers are depleted by about the 10\% with respect to the independent particle model values. The effects of the correlations on the spectroscopic factors are larger on the more deeply bound states.

## Key claim

Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theory, using the Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) resummation technique with operator-dependent correlations including tensor channels, predicts that natural orbit occupation numbers are depleted by about 10% relative to the Independent Particle Model (IPM), with the effects of correlations on spectroscopic factors being larger for more deeply bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrodinger equation variationally using a Hamiltonian with the Argonne v'8 nucleon-nucleon potential and Urbana IX three-body force, expressing the A-body wave function as a correlated product of a Slater determinant and a many-body correlation operator containing channels up to tensor terms. They applied the Fermi Hypernetted Chain equations in the Single Operator Chain (SOC) approximation to compute the one-body density matrix, momentum distributions, natural orbits, occupation numbers, quasi-hole wave functions, and spectroscopic factors for the doubly closed shell nuclei C12, O16, Ca40, Ca48, and Pb208.

## Key numbers

- Natural orbit occupation numbers are depleted by about 10% relative to IPM values for all nuclei studied.
- The momentum distributions of correlated and IPM calculations start to deviate at about 1.9 fm^-1 for C12 and 1.5 fm^-1 for Pb208.
- The Fermi momentum of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is 1.36 fm^-1.
- Proton spectroscopic factors for the 1s1/2 state range from 0.91 (C12) to 0.77 (Pb208) with full operator-dependent correlations (f6), compared to 0.96 (C12) to 0.93 (Pb208) with scalar correlations only (f1).
- Proton spectroscopic factors for the 1p3/2 state range from 0.94 (C12) to 0.77 (Pb208) with full correlations (f6).
- For O16, the 1s1/2 proton occupation number is 0.873 with full correlations (f6), compared to 0.956 with scalar correlations only (f1).
- For O16, the 1p3/2 proton occupation number is 0.921 with full correlations (f6), compared to 0.973 with scalar correlations only (f1).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CBF/FHNC calculations (Bisconti et al. 2006) on binding energies and charge distributions to momentum-space observables and spectroscopic factors. The results contradict findings by Friar (2005) on asymmetric nuclear matter where correlation effects were stronger for protons than neutrons, while agreeing with Bozek (2004). The calculations improve upon first-order cluster expansion methods (Arias de Saavedra 1997) which underestimated high-momentum enhancements by orders of magnitude, and provide a benchmark for comparison with other ab initio approaches such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method applied to O16 (Dickhoff 2004) and the coupled cluster method for O16 (Heisenberg 1999, Mihaila 1999).
