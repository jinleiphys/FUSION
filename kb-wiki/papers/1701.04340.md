# Investigating the impact of the gluon saturation effects on the momentum transfer distributions for the exclusive vector meson photoproduction in hadronic collisions

> The exclusive vector meson production cross section is one of the most promising observables to probe the high energy regime of the QCD dynamics. In particular, the squared momentum transfer ($t$) distributions are an important source of information about the spatial distribution of the gluons in the hadron and about fluctuations of the color fields. In this paper we complement previous studies on exclusive vector meson photoproduction in hadronic collisions presenting a comprehensive analysis of the $t$ - spectrum measured in exclusive $ρ$, $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $pp$ and $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC. We compute the differential cross sections taking into account gluon saturation effects and compare the predictions with those obtained in the linear regime of the QCD dynamics. Our results show that gluon saturation suppresses the magnitude of the cross sections and shifts the position of the dips towards smaller values of $t$.

## Key claim

Gluon saturation effects suppress the magnitude of differential cross sections and shift the position of diffractive dips toward smaller values of squared momentum transfer \(t\) for exclusive \(\rho\), \(\phi\), and \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction in \(pp\) and \(PbPb\) collisions at LHC Run 2 energies, with the effect being larger for lighter vector mesons and for nuclear targets.

## Method

The authors compute differential cross sections \(d\sigma/dY\,dt\) for exclusive vector meson photoproduction using the color dipole formalism, with the dipole-proton scattering amplitude given by the impact parameter Color Glass Condensate (bCGC) model. For nuclear targets, the dipole-nucleus amplitude is constructed from the dipole-proton cross section via a Glauber-type expression, and both coherent and incoherent contributions are calculated separately. To isolate saturation effects, the predictions are compared with a linearized version of the bCGC model that omits the non-linear (saturating) part of the amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For exclusive \(\rho\) photoproduction in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV and \(Y=0\), the linear and non-linear predictions differ by a factor \(\approx 2\).
- For exclusive \(\rho\) photoproduction in \(PbPb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV and \(Y=0\), the linear and non-linear predictions differ by a factor of order 10.
- For exclusive \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction in \(PbPb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV and \(Y=0\), the linear and non-linear predictions differ by a factor \(\approx 2\).
- In \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV and \(Y=0\), the shift in dip position due to saturation is \(\Delta |t| \approx 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) for \(\rho\) and \(\Delta |t| \approx 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) for \(J/\Psi\).
- The bCGC model parameters used are: \(\gamma_s = 0.6599\), \(\kappa = 9.9\), \(B_{CGC} = 5.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\mathcal{N}_0 = 0.3358\), \(x_0 = 0.00105\), and \(\lambda = 0.2063\), fitted to HERA data.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Goncalves et al., 2015, 2016) that successfully described LHC Run 1 data on rapidity distributions and total cross sections for exclusive vector meson photoproduction using the color dipole formalism with saturation effects. The present paper provides the first systematic predictions for the \(t\)-spectra at LHC Run 2 energies, complementing alternative approaches such as those by Guzey et al. (2016). The results enable experimental tests of non-linear QCD dynamics through the measurement of dip positions in transverse momentum distributions, particularly for \(\phi\) meson production, which is identified as a sensitive probe of saturation.
