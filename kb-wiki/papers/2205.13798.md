# Two-dimensional extrapolation procedure for ab initio study of nuclear size parameters and the properties of halo nucleus 6He

> A new two-dimensional procedure for extrapolation of the values of matter, neutron, and proton radii obtained in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations to infinite size of its basis is proposed. A relationship between the radii is used as an additional test. Together with the JISP16 potential, which is frequently used in NCSM calculations of the radii, the Daejeon16 potential is applied for these purposes for the first time. Halo nucleus 6He is the object of studies. The small spread of radii values, successful testing using relationship between the values of these three radii, and reasonable agreement between the obtained results and experimental data as well as the results of other advanced ab initio calculations demonstrate the high efficiency of the developed approach and, therefore, good prospects for its application. The performed investigations and analysis of the results of other studies indicate that the halo of 6He has a large size 0.7 - 0.8 fm.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new two-dimensional extrapolation procedure (twisted tape extrapolation, TTE) for no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of nuclear radii that simultaneously accounts for both the basis cutoff parameter and the oscillator frequency, and claims that the halo of 6He has a large size of 0.7–0.8 fm, though this conclusion depends on which experimental data set one considers more reliable.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations using the Bigstick code with the JISP16 and Daejeon16 internucleon potentials, computing matter, neutron, and proton root-mean-square radii for 6He at various basis truncation parameters (N*_max up to 14) and oscillator frequencies (hbar-omega). They developed a two-dimensional extrapolation formula r^2(N*_max, hbar-omega) = r^2_infinity + P_k(hbar-omega) * exp(-alpha * sqrt(N*_max)), where P_k is a polynomial of degree k, and fitted the parameters using chi-square minimization with the TMinuit package. The extrapolation was performed over several ranges of hbar-omega values, and the consistency of the three radii was checked using the relation A*r_m^2 = Z*r_p^2 + N*r_n^2.

## Key numbers

* Matter radius for JISP16 potential: r_m = 2.342(7) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Neutron radius for JISP16 potential: r_n = 2.582(3) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Point-proton radius for JISP16 potential: r_p = 1.799(6) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Matter radius for Daejeon16 potential: r_m = 2.430(6) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Neutron radius for Daejeon16 potential: r_n = 2.663(3) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Point-proton radius for Daejeon16 potential: r_p = 1.871(16) fm (extrapolated, optimal result)
* Neutron halo size (r_n - r_p): 0.792 fm for JISP16 and 0.783 fm for Daejeon16 (extrapolated)
* Violation factor Delta for the radius relation: -0.36% for JISP16 and 0.09% for Daejeon16 (extrapolated)
* Experimental matter radii from literature: 2.33(4) fm (exp1), 2.30(7) fm (exp2), 2.44(7) fm (exp7), 2.29(6) fm (exp8)
* Experimental point-proton radii from literature: 1.938(23) fm (exp4, using R_p=0.877(7) fm) and 1.953(22) fm (exp4, using R_p=0.84184(67) fm)

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM studies of 6He radii that used the crossover prescription (e.g., Cockrell et al. (2012) and other works cited as ai6, ai8) by introducing a two-dimensional extrapolation procedure that simultaneously handles both N*_max and hbar-omega dependence. The Daejeon16 potential is applied to radius calculations for the first time, and the results are compared with experimental data from several groups (exp1, exp2, exp7, exp8) and with other ab initio calculations including NCSMC results from the work cited as ai11. The paper argues that the larger matter radius measurement (2.44(7) fm from exp7) is theoretically confirmed, and that the halo size of 6He is about 0.7–0.8 fm, which is approximately 1.5 times larger than the values from exp1, exp2, and exp8.
