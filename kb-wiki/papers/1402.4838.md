# Deformed Richardson-Gaudin model

> The Richardson-Gaudin model describes strong pairing correlations of fermions confined to a finite chain. The integrability of the Hamiltonian allows for its eigenstates to be constructed algebraically. In this work we show that quantum group theory provides a possibility to deform the Hamiltonian while preserving integrability. More precisely, we use the so-called Jordanian r-matrix to deform the Hamiltonian of the Richardson-Gaudin model. In order to preserve its integrability, we need to insert a special nilpotent term into the auxiliary L-operator which generates integrals of motion of the system. Moreover, the quantum inverse scattering method enables us to construct the exact eigenstates of the deformed Hamiltonian. These states have a highly complex entanglement structure which requires further investigation.

# Deformed Richardson-Gaudin model

## Key claim
The authors show that the Richardson-Gaudin model can be deformed using a Jordanian r-matrix while preserving integrability, but only by inserting a special nilpotent term into the auxiliary L-operator. The resulting eigenstates have a highly complex entanglement structure that requires further investigation, meaning the work is a proof-of-principle rather than a complete solution.

## Method
The authors start from the standard Richardson-Gaudin Hamiltonian for a spin-1/2 periodic chain and its mapping to a pairing model. They apply a Jordanian twist to the classical r-matrix, adding a term proportional to the deformation parameter ξ, and modify the L-operator accordingly. Using the quantum inverse scattering method, they construct the twisted transfer matrix and extract the deformed Hamiltonian as the operator coefficient in its expansion at λ → ∞. Bethe states for the deformed model are built using a product of operators B_M(μ₁,...,μ_M) = X⁻(μ₁)(X⁻(μ₂)+ξ)...(X⁻(μ_M)+ξ(M-1)) acting on the ferromagnetic state.

## Key numbers
- The undeformed coupling constant g is connected to the parameter c = 1/g.
- The deformation parameter is denoted ξ, appearing in the Jordanian r-matrix term ξ(h ⊗ X⁺ − X⁺ ⊗ h).
- The Bethe equations for the undeformed model are: 2c + Σ_k ℓ_k/(μ_i − ε_k) − Σ_{j≠i} 2/(μ_i − μ_j) = 0 for i = 1,…,M.
- The deformed Hamiltonian is given by J₂ = c Σ_j ε_j X_j⁺ + 2ξ{ (Σ_j ε_j h_j) X⁺_{gl} − h_{gl} Σ_j ε_j X_j⁺ } + (h_{gl}² + 2h_{gl} + 4X⁻_{gl}X⁺_{gl}).

## Context
This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin model, originally solved by Richardson (1963) and later connected to Gaudin models, by applying quantum group deformations previously studied for other integrable systems (e.g., by Cambiaggio et al.). The deformed model provides a new class of integrable pairing Hamiltonians with potential applications in mesoscopic and nuclear physics where small fermion numbers prohibit conventional BCS theory. The authors note that more complicated deformations can be obtained using r-matrices related to higher-rank Lie algebras, and the entanglement properties of the eigenstates remain under investigation.
