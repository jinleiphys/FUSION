# Nuclear chiral dynamics and thermodynamics

> This presentation reviews an approach to nuclear many-body systems based on the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry of low-energy QCD. In the low-energy limit, for energies and momenta small compared to a characteristic symmetry breaking scale of order 1 GeV, QCD is realized as an effective field theory of Goldstone bosons (pions) coupled to heavy fermionic sources (nucleons). Nuclear forces at long and intermediate distance scales result from a systematic hierarchy of one- and two-pion exchange processes in combination with Pauli blocking effects in the nuclear medium. Short distance dynamics, not resolved at the wavelengths corresponding to typical nuclear Fermi momenta, are introduced as contact interactions between nucleons. Apart from a set of low-energy constants associated with these contact terms, the parameters of this theory are entirely determined by pion properties and low-energy pion-nucleon scattering observables. This framework (in-medium chiral perturbation theory) can provide a realistic description of both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter. The importance of three-body forces is emphasized, and the role of explicit Delta(1232)-isobar degrees of freedom is investigated in detail. Nuclear chiral thermodynamics is developed and a calculation of the nuclear phase diagram is performed. This includes a successful description of the first-order phase transition from a nuclear Fermi liquid to an interacting Fermi gas and the coexistence of these phases below a critical temperature T_c. Density functional methods for finite nuclei based on this approach are also discussed. Effective interactions, their density dependence and connections to Landau Fermi liquid theory are outlined. Finally, the density and temperature dependence of the chiral (quark) condensate is investigated.

## Key claim

This review argues that in-medium chiral perturbation theory, based on the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry of low-energy QCD, can provide a realistic description of both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, including the nuclear phase diagram. The key hedge is that the framework is applicable only for energies and momenta small compared to the chiral symmetry breaking scale of order 1 GeV, and that short-distance dynamics must be introduced as contact interactions with parameters fitted to data.

## Method

The authors develop nuclear forces from a systematic hierarchy of one- and two-pion exchange processes combined with Pauli blocking effects in the nuclear medium, using chiral effective field theory with nucleons and pions as explicit degrees of freedom. Short-distance dynamics are introduced as contact interactions between nucleons, with low-energy constants determined from pion-nucleon scattering data and nucleon-nucleon phase shifts. The framework is extended to include explicit Delta(1232)-isobar degrees of freedom and three-body forces, and nuclear chiral thermodynamics is developed to calculate the nuclear phase diagram including the first-order phase transition from a nuclear Fermi liquid to an interacting Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \( f_\pi = 92.4 \) MeV, defined by the matrix element connecting the pion state with the QCD vacuum via the isovector axial vector current.
- Pion mass: \( m_\pi \simeq 138 \) MeV, reflecting explicit chiral symmetry breaking by small quark masses.
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \( \Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1 \) GeV.
- Chiral quark condensate at renormalization scale about 2 GeV: \( \langle 0 |\bar{q} q |0 \rangle \simeq -(0.36 \) GeV\()^3\), with average quark mass \( \frac{1}{2}(m_u + m_d) \simeq 3.5 \) MeV.
- Nucleon sigma term: \( \sigma_N \simeq (45 \pm 8) \) MeV, deduced from low-energy pion-nucleon data using dispersion relation techniques.
- Delta(1232) excitation energy: \( \Delta = M_\Delta - M_N \simeq 293 \) MeV.
- Axial polarizability from Delta(1232): \( \alpha_A^{(\Delta)} = g_A^2 / [f_\pi^2 (M_\Delta - M_N)] \simeq 5 \) fm\(^3\).
- Cutoff scale for chiral NN-potential regulator: \( \Lambda = 500 - 700 \) MeV.
- Natural scale for three-nucleon contact interaction: \( \Lambda = 700 \) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear many-body systems, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1966/67) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) in chiral perturbation theory, as well as the modern NN-potential developments reviewed by Epelbaum, Hammer, and Meissner, and Machleidt. The framework enables systematic calculations of nuclear matter properties, the nuclear phase diagram including the liquid-gas phase transition, and density functional methods for finite nuclei, while also connecting to Landau Fermi liquid theory and investigations of the chiral condensate at finite density and temperature.
