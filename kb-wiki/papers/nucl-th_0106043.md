# Parton Energy Loss with Detailed Balance

> Stimulated gluon emission and thermal absorption in addition to induced radiation are considered for an energetic parton propagating inside a quark- gluon plasma. In the presence of thermal gluons, stimulated emission reduces while absorption increases the parton's energy. The net effect is a reduction of the parton energy loss. Though decreasing asymptotically as $T/E$ with the parton energy, the relative reduction is found to be important for intermediate energies. The modified energy dependence of the energy loss will affect the shape of suppression of moderately high $p_T$ hadrons due to jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Parton Energy Loss with Detailed Balance

## Key claim

Stimulated gluon emission and thermal absorption partially cancel, producing a net reduction of the radiative energy loss of an energetic parton in a quark-gluon plasma. The relative reduction scales asymptotically as T/E and is therefore important only for intermediate parton energies, not for asymptotically high energies.

## Method

The authors extend the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) opacity expansion to first order in opacity by including Bose-Einstein enhancement factors for gluon emission and a thermal gluon distribution for absorption, alongside the standard zero-temperature induced radiation. They compute the net energy loss as the sum of final-state absorption without rescattering and rescattering-induced absorption, subtracting the vacuum and zero-temperature contributions to isolate the medium effect. The Debye screening mass is taken as μ² = 4πα_s T², and the gluon mean-free-path is evaluated in the Gyulassy-Wang (GW) model.

## Key numbers

- The relative reduction of effective parton energy loss due to thermal absorption is about 30–10% for intermediate parton energies (E/μ in the range where the ratio plotted in Fig. 1 drops from ~0.7 to ~0.9), for L/λ_g = 3, 5 and α_s = 0.3.
- The asymptotic form of the rescattering-induced absorption energy gain is ΔE_abs^(1)/E ≈ (π α_s C_F / 3) (L T² / λ_g E²) [ln(μ²L/T) – 1 + γ_E – 6ζ'(2)/π²], where γ_E ≈ 0.5772 and ζ'(2) ≈ –0.9376.
- The final-state absorption (no rescattering) gives ΔE_abs^(0)/E ≈ (π α_s C_F / 3) (T²/E²) [ln(4ET/μ²) + 2 – γ_E + 6ζ'(2)/π²].
- The zero-temperature induced radiation energy loss (GLV result) is ΔE_rad^(1)/E ≈ – (α_s C_F μ² L² / 4 λ_g E) [ln(2E/μ²L) – 0.048].

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of medium-induced radiative energy loss (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff 1997, Zakharov 1996, Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev 2000, Guo-Wang 2000, Wiedemann 2000) by incorporating detailed balance (stimulated emission and thermal absorption) for a parton in a hot QCD plasma. The authors show that the net effect reduces the energy loss at intermediate energies, which modifies the predicted suppression pattern for moderately high p_T hadrons (p_T < 10 GeV/c) in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The results suggest that resummation of higher-order absorption and emission processes, as previously studied in QED by Weldon (1983), may further reduce the effective energy loss.
