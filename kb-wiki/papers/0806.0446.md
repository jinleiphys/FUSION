# Quenching of light hadrons at RHIC in a collisional energy loss scenario

> We evaluate the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{AA}(p_T)$ for light hadrons by taking into account the collisional energy loss. We show that in the measured $p_T$ domain of RHIC the elastic process is the dominant mechanism for the partonic energy loss.

# Quenching of light hadrons at RHIC in a collisional energy loss scenario

## Key claim

The authors claim that collisional (elastic) energy loss, rather than radiative energy loss from induced bremsstrahlung, is the dominant mechanism for partonic energy loss in the measured transverse momentum range of light hadrons at RHIC, but only for parton energies up to a critical value of approximately 85 GeV for quarks and 60 GeV for gluons; above these energies radiative loss may become important.

## Method

The authors solve the Fokker-Planck equation for the phase-space distribution of partons, using transport coefficients (drag and diffusion) computed from hard thermal loop (HTL) corrected gluon propagators to handle infrared divergences. The space-time evolution of the medium is modeled with Bjorken hydrodynamics, and the nuclear suppression factor R_AA for neutral pions and eta mesons is obtained by convoluting the parton distribution with fragmentation functions, averaging over jet production geometry and path length in the plasma.

## Key numbers

- Collisional energy loss dominates over radiative loss for parton energies up to E_c ~ 85 GeV for quarks and E_c ~ 60 GeV for gluons (Fig. 1b).
- The nuclear suppression factor R_AA for neutral pions in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is reproduced reasonably well with initial temperature T_i = 450 MeV, sound velocity squared c_s^2 = 0.2, and thermalization time t_i = 0.15 fm/c.
- The data can be explained with T_i in the range 450-500 MeV depending on the equation of state (velocity of sound).
- The transition temperature T_c is taken as 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Roy, Alam, and Dutt-Mazumder (2006) and Dutt-Mazumder (2005) that revisited the importance of collisional energy loss in the context of RHIC. It contrasts with most previous calculations (reviewed by Gyulassy et al.) that considered only radiative energy loss from induced bremsstrahlung and reproduced the observed nuclear suppression of light hadrons. The authors note that the non-photonic single electron spectrum from heavy meson decays measured by the PHENIX Collaboration showed larger suppression than expected from radiative loss alone, motivating a renewed interest in collisional energy loss for both light and heavy quarks. The results enable future detailed calculations combining both collisional and radiative loss mechanisms to delineate the importance of each.
