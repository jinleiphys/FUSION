# Transport Theoretical Approach to the Nucleon Spectral Function in Nuclear Matter

> The nucleon spectral function in infinite nuclear matter is calculated in a quantum transport theoretical approach. Exploiting the known relation between collision rates and correlation functions the spectral function is derived self-consistently. By re-inserting the spectral functions into the collision integrals the description of hard processes from the high-momentum components of wave functions and interactions is improved iteratively until convergence is achieved. The momentum and energy distributions and the nuclear matter occupation probabilities are in very good agreement with the results obtained from many-body theory.

# Transport Theoretical Approach to the Nucleon Spectral Function in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The nucleon spectral function in infinite nuclear matter can be calculated self-consistently using quantum transport theory, and the resulting momentum and energy distributions agree very well with state-of-the-art many-body theory results even when a constant, momentum-independent nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude is used. The agreement suggests that the spectral function in the hole-sector is dominated by phase space effects rather than by the specific energy-momentum structure of the interaction, provided a fully self-consistent calculation is performed.

## Method
The authors solve the self-consistency problem linking collision rates and correlation functions in quantum transport theory at zero temperature. Starting from an initial guess for the spectral function (a constant width of 0.1 MeV), they iteratively compute the correlation self-energies Sigma^> and Sigma^< using a constant, momentum-independent nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude as the only interaction input, then extract the updated spectral function from the imaginary part of the self-energy. The six-dimensional integrals for the self-energies are evaluated on a grid with boundaries |omega| <= 0.5 GeV and p <= 1.25 GeV with a mesh size of 5 MeV in both directions, and the iteration is repeated until convergence is achieved.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum: p_F = 1.33 fm^{-1}
- Nuclear matter density: rho = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per particle (Fermi energy): omega_F = -16 MeV
- Adjusted average scattering amplitude: (overline{|M|^2})^{1/2} = 207 MeV fm^3
- This amplitude corresponds to a constant on-shell cross section of about 20 mb
- Grid boundaries: |omega| <= 0.5 GeV, p <= p_max = 1.25 GeV
- Grid mesh size: 5 MeV in both energy and momentum directions
- Initial spectral function width for iteration start: 0.1 MeV
- First iteration describes about 30% of global features (location and height of peak structures)

## Context
This work extends the transport theoretical approach proposed by Danielewicz and Bertsch (1991) and is motivated by the authors' own implementation of off-shell effects in transport calculations for heavy-ion collisions. The results are compared directly to the many-body calculations of Benhar et al. (1994) using correlation-basis theory and to the global parameterization of spectral functions by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1995). The approach goes beyond perturbative many-body methods by including higher-order polarization effects through self-consistency, and it provides a posteriori justification for including off-shell effects in nuclear transport calculations as developed in the authors' earlier work. The method enables a non-perturbative summation of a whole series of n-particle m-hole diagrams.
