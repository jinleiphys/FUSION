# Binary reaction decays from 24Mg+12C

> Charged particle and gamma decays in 24Mg* are investigated for excitation energies where quasimolecular resonances appear in 12C+12C collisions. Various theoretical predictions for the occurence of superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with resonance structures with low spin are discussed within the measured 24Mg* excitation energy region. The inverse kinematics reaction 24Mg+12C is studied at E_lab(24Mg) = 130 MeV, an energy which enables the population of 24Mg states decaying into 12C+12C resonant break-up states. Exclusive data were collected with the Binary Reaction Spectrometer in coincidence with EUROBALL IV installed at the VIVITRON Tandem facility at Strasbourg. Specific structures with large deformation were selectively populated in binary reactions and their associated gamma decays studied. Coincident events associated with inelastic and alpha-transfer channels have been selected by choosing the excitation energy or the entry point via the two-body Q-values. The analysis of the binary reaction channels is presented with a particular emphasis on 24Mg-gamma, 20Ne-gamma and 16O-gamma coincidences. New information (spin and branching ratios) is deduced on high-energy states in 24Mg and 16O, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the well-known 12C+12C quasimolecular resonances represent true cluster states in the 24Mg compound system or simply reflect scattering states in the ion-ion potential, a question that remains unresolved. The authors use binary reaction decays from 24Mg+12C at a single bombarding energy to selectively populate highly deformed states, but the search for direct gamma decay from 12C+12C resonant states in 24Mg remains inconclusive due to the complexity of the resonance structure and the small expected branching ratios.

## Method

The inverse kinematics reaction 24Mg+12C was studied at Elab(24Mg) = 130 MeV using the Binary Reaction Spectrometer (BRS) in coincidence with the EUROBALL IV gamma-ray spectrometer at the VIVITRON Tandem facility. The BRS consisted of two large-area gas-detector telescopes covering forward angles from 12.5 degrees to 45.5 degrees, providing charge identification (Z) and two-body Q-value reconstruction for binary exit channels. Coincident events from inelastic and alpha-transfer channels were selected by choosing excitation energy regions via two-body Q-values, with particular emphasis on 24Mg-gamma, 20Ne-gamma, and 16O-gamma coincidences.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: Elab(24Mg) = 130 MeV, with beam intensity kept constant at approximately 5 pnA
- Target thickness: 200 microgram/cm^2 foils of natural 12C
- Excitation energy range covered in 24Mg: up to E* = 30 MeV
- Each BRS telescope solid angle: 187 msr, covering Delta-theta = 12.5 degrees to 45.5 degrees (theta = 29 degrees +/- 16.5 degrees)
- Position resolution: approximately 0.5 mm; time resolution: approximately 200 ps
- Absolute total gamma-ray efficiency of EUROBALL IV: approximately 8.5 percent
- Doppler-shift corrected gamma-ray energy resolution: better than 40-59 keV FWHM for 3-4 MeV gamma rays (approximately 1.3 percent average)
- Most prominent 24Mg gamma-ray transitions: 2+1 to 0+1 at 1368.6 keV (100 percent relative intensity) and 4+1 to 2+1 at 2754.0 keV (39.3 percent relative intensity)
- Highest observed 24Mg state: 8+2 level at 13.21 MeV (3.17 percent relative intensity)
- Two previously unobserved transitions in 16O from the decay of the 3+ state at 11.08 MeV were identified for the first time

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of 12C+12C quasimolecular resonances (Erb 1985, Beck 1994) and resonant break-up states in 24Mg (Fulton 1986, Curtis 1995, Singer 2000) by using charged-particle-gamma-ray coincidence techniques. The study is motivated by theoretical predictions of superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands in light N=Z nuclei from the cranked alpha-cluster model (Marsh 1986), Nilsson-Strutinsky approach (Leander 1975, Aberg 1994), and Hartree-Fock calculations (Flocard 1984). The results enable future experimental investigations of low-spin (0 or 2 hbar) states in 24Mg and further studies of hyperdeformed shapes in 36Ar using ternary decay measurements, which require higher selectivity than achieved in this work.
