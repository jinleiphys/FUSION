# Test of phi renormalization in nuclei through phi photoproduction

> We propose an experimental procedure to find out the medium modifications of the $ϕ$ meson. The reaction is inclusive $ϕ$ photoproduction in nuclei, looking for $K^+ K^-$ pairs from the $ϕ$ decay with total momentum smaller than 100-150 $MeV/c$, which are made possible at energies of present laboratories from center of mass $ϕ$ backward production and the help of Fermi motion. We have conducted a many body calculation of the mass distribution of the $ϕ$ adapted to the experimental set up of a recent JLAB experiment where the backwards $ϕ$ photoproduction has been measured. Using recent results for the in medium properties of the $ϕ$, we find that the width of the invariant mass distribution for photoproduction on medium to heavy nuclei is larger than the free $ϕ$ width by a factor of two or more.

# Test of phi renormalization in nuclei through phi photoproduction

## Key claim

The paper proposes that inclusive phi photoproduction on nuclei, with a kinematic cut selecting phi mesons having total momentum below 100-150 MeV/c, can reveal medium modifications of the phi meson. The claim is conditional on the cut being essential: without the momentum cut, the observed invariant mass distribution remains close to the free width and is insensitive to medium corrections.

## Method

The authors perform a many-body calculation of the photon selfenergy in nuclear matter, accounting for phi-particle-hole intermediate states, and use the Lindhard function for particle-hole excitation. They evaluate the inclusive nuclear photoproduction cross section by integrating the imaginary part of the photon selfenergy over the nuclear volume, incorporating a density-dependent phi width from prior work (Oset and Ramos, 1998; Klingl et al., 1997) while assuming negligible mass shift. The calculation includes a momentum cut on the outgoing phi, an acceptance factor for K- absorption using the K- selfenergy from Schaffner-Bielich et al. (2000), and an average over the phi decay position within the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The phi momentum in the nucleon rest frame for backward CM production is 235 MeV/c at photon energy 3.6 GeV and 168 MeV/c at 6 GeV.
- The phi width at nuclear saturation density (rho_0) from Oset and Ramos (1998) is about 22 MeV; from Klingl et al. (1997) it is about 45 MeV.
- For Ca-40 with a 100 MeV/c momentum cut, the invariant mass distribution shows a phi width of about 10 MeV; with a 150 MeV/c cut, the width is about 8 MeV, nearly twice the free phi width.
- The nuclear cross section for Ca-40 with a 150 MeV/c cut yields about 85 percent of the elementary backward cross section; for O-16 it yields about 45 percent.
- The elementary backward cross section d sigma / d t = 1 nb/GeV^2 at 3.6 GeV from the CLAS experiment (Burkert, 1999) gives |C|^2 = 650 nb.
- The Coulomb correction to the invariant mass from K+ K- is less than 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of vector meson modification in nuclei, particularly for the rho meson (Chanfray et al., 1996; Klingl et al., 1997; Rapp and Wambach, 1999), and prior calculations of phi renormalization in matter (Kuwabara et al., 1995; Haglin, 1995; Smith, 1998; Klingl et al., 1997; Oset and Ramos, 1998). The proposed reaction provides a cleaner alternative to dilepton measurements in heavy ion collisions, which require disentangling background contributions. The results enable experimental tests of phi medium modifications at existing facilities such as Jefferson Lab and Spring8/Osaka, and could serve as a consistency check for theories of kaon property changes in nuclei, which remain debated (Gal, 2001; Schaffner-Bielich et al., 2000; Lutz, 1998; Koch, 1994).
