# Effective potential from zero-momentum potential

> We obtain the centre-of-mass frame effective potential from the zero-momentum potential in Ruijsenaars-Schneider type 1-dimensional relativistic mechanics using classical inverse scattering methods.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the centre-of-mass frame effective potential for a two-particle relativistic Ruijsenaars-Schneider system can be obtained from the zero-momentum potential using classical inverse scattering methods, but only for the specific case of 1-dimensional relativistic mechanics and under the condition that the zero-momentum potential is known from the non-relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors start from the Ruijsenaars-Schneider Ansatz for two particles in 1+1 dimensions, which defines the Hamiltonian, momentum, and Lorentz boost generators in terms of canonical phase space variables. They reduce the two-particle problem to an effective one-particle problem in the centre-of-mass frame, where the dynamics follows a conservation law that resembles a non-relativistic problem with a state-dependent rescaling factor. Using classical inverse scattering techniques, including the Landau-Lifshitz formula for bound states and time-delay analysis for scattering states, they reconstruct the effective potential from scattering data (time delay or displacement) and bound-state data (oscillation period).

## Key numbers

- For the MR-type potential \(U(x) = mg^2 / \sinh^2(x/\ell)\), the scattering displacement is \(X(v) = (\ell/2) \ln(1 + g^2/v^2)\).
- For the LA-type potential \(U(x) = -mg^2 / \cosh^2(x/\ell)\), the bound-state period is \(P(h) = \ell\pi / \sqrt{h}\) and the scattering displacement is \(X(v) = (\ell/2) \ln(1 + g^2/v^2)\).
- For the RC-type potential with parameters \(B\), \(\ell\), and \(\xi\), the bound-state period is \(P(h) = \ell\pi / \sqrt{h} - \ell\pi / \sqrt{h + B\xi}\).
- In the RC example, the non-perturbative constant in the low-energy expansion is \(p_o = -\ell\pi / \sqrt{B\xi}\).

## Context

This work extends the classical inverse scattering approach previously applied to non-relativistic one-dimensional potentials (Landau and Lifshitz, 1976) to the relativistic Ruijsenaars-Schneider model introduced by Ruijsenaars and Schneider (1986). The paper enables the reconstruction of relativistic effective potentials from scattering data, which can be applied to models such as the Sine-Gordon model (corresponding to the \(\gamma=1\) case of the LA potential). The results provide a bridge between relativistic integrable systems and classical inverse scattering methods.
