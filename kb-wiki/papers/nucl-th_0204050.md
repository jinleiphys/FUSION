# Pion-nucleon amplitude near threshold: the sigma-term and scattering lengths beyond few loops

> The pion-nucleon amplitude is considered in the vicinity of the elastic scattering threshold within a relativistic dynamical model dressing the pi N N and pi N Delta vertices self-consistently with an infinite number of meson loops. The dressing is formulated as solution of a system of coupled integral equations incorporating unitarity, crossing symmetry and analyticity constraints. The calculated scattering lengths and the sigma-term agree with recent data analyses. The dressing is important in this model both below and at threshold. The contribution of the Delta resonance is discussed, including effects of the consistent dressing of the pi N Delta vertex. A comparison with the approaches of chiral perturbation theory and the Bethe-Salpeter equation is outlined.

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon amplitude near threshold is calculated using a relativistic dynamical model that dresses the πNN and πNΔ vertices self-consistently with an infinite number of meson loops, and the calculated scattering lengths and sigma-term agree with recent data analyses without any free parameters in the near-threshold calculation, as all parameters were fixed at intermediate energies in prior work.

## Method

The model solves a system of 14 coupled nonlinear integral equations for the imaginary and real parts of the πNN form factors (G_PV, G_PS), nucleon self-energy functions (A, B), πNΔ form factor (G_Δ), and Δ self-energy functions (A_Δ, B_Δ), using Cutkosky rules to compute discontinuities and dispersion integrals to obtain principal-value parts. The dressed vertices and propagators are then used to construct the s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel contributions to the invariant amplitudes A± and B±, with the ρ and σ meson propagators calculated in a one ππ loop approximation. The iteration procedure converges after about 30 steps for the chosen parameters, with the bare πNN vertex taken as purely pseudovector and the bare form factors having an exponential form with half-width Λ_N².

## Context

This work extends the relativistic dynamical model developed in Kondratyuk (1999) and Kondratyuk (2000, 2001) by treating the Δ resonance consistently with the nucleon, dressing both the πNΔ vertex and Δ self-energy to infinite order, whereas earlier versions computed the Δ self-energy to only one πN loop and did not dress the πNΔ vertex. The approach contrasts with chiral perturbation theory (Gasser 1981, Becher 2001) which may be unreliable near threshold due to singularities, and with Bethe-Salpeter equation approaches (Lahde 1999) which typically do not preserve crossing symmetry. The model enables simultaneous description of both scattering lengths and subthreshold coefficients within a single dynamical framework incorporating unitarity, crossing symmetry, and analyticity constraints.
