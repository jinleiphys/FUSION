# Multi-nucleon correlations in Deep Inelastic Scattering at large Bjorken xB

> Using realistic nuclear many-body wave functions in the collinear factorization framework, we evaluate nuclear Deep Inelastic Scattering at large Bjorken xB, where correlations are needed to exceed the kinematic limits of a single free nucleon. We find that recent experimental data on cross-section ratios from the CLAS collaboration exhibit features that are not reproduced by modern 2- and 3-nucleon correlations in the nuclear wave function. New physical mechanisms are needed to explain data in the 2<xB<3 range.

## Key claim

Using realistic nuclear many-body wave functions with 2- and 3-nucleon correlations in a collinear factorization framework, the authors find that the second plateau in CLAS cross-section ratios at 2 < xB < 3 is not reproduced, indicating that single-nucleon degrees of freedom are inadequate and new physical mechanisms beyond realistic inter-nucleon potentials are needed.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear deep inelastic scattering structure functions by convolving free-nucleon parton distribution functions (CTEQ5L at leading order) with non-relativistic nucleon momentum distributions from Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations that include 2- and 3-nucleon correlations, and from the Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (CS) parametrization that includes only 2-nucleon correlations. They use the nucleon rest frame (N-frame) for collinear factorization to properly define bound-nucleon PDFs, and compute cross-section ratios A/3He for comparison with CLAS data at Q2 ~ 2 GeV2.

## Key numbers

* CLAS data cover the range 1.6 GeV2 < Q2 < 2.4 GeV2 at Elab = 4.5 GeV, with cross-section ratios showing plateaus at xB > 1.5 and xB > 2.25
* VMC nucleon momentum distributions show correlation effects for nucleon momenta l > 2 fm-1, with 3-nucleon correlations becoming dominant at l > 4 fm-1
* The computed ratios at Q2 = 2 GeV2 describe CLAS data for light nuclei up to xB ~ 1.5, but fail to reproduce the second plateau in the range 2 < xB < 3
* At Q2 = 5 GeV2 and Elab = 9 GeV (accessible at the 12 GeV JLAB upgrade), the onset of correlation effects moves to xB ~ 1 with increased plateau slope
* Sensitivity of A/B ratios to different PDF choices is at most 5%

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of CLAS data (Egiyan, 2005) and earlier SLAC data on A/2H ratios (Frankfurt, 1993) and A/4He ratios (Day, 1988), where plateaus were attributed to 2- and 3-nucleon short-range correlations. The authors show that realistic nuclear wave functions from Pieper (1992) and Ciofi degli Atti (1995) can explain the first plateau but not the second, pointing toward the need for mechanisms such as 6- and 9-quark clusters, multi-nucleon clusters (Frankfurt, 1981; Frankfurt, 1988), or dynamical short-range nucleon correlations (Piasetzky, 2006). The formalism is designed to be generalizable to include these additional physical mechanisms.
