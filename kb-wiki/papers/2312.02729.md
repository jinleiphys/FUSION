# High-$K$ isomers in a self-consistent mean-field approach with the Gogny force

> High-$K$ isomeric states in even-even and odd-mass nuclei are described within a mean-field framework with full blocking and using the finite range Gogny force. Theoretical calculations of low energy spectra of several nuclei across the nuclear chart are compared with equal filling approximation results and experimental data. Despite the global character of the employed interactions, a good agreement between the different many-body methods and experimental data is found.

## Key claim

High-$K$ isomeric states can be successfully described at the mean-field level using the blocking method with density-dependent finite range Gogny forces, with the mechanism responsible for reducing excitation energies of multiquasiparticle configurations identified as the quenching of pairing correlations; the paper claims good agreement with experimental data despite the global character of the interactions, but restricts its demonstration to even-even nuclei that are well characterized experimentally, deferring applications to neutron-rich astromers to future work.

## Method

The authors use the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation with full blocking to describe two- and four-quasiparticle excitations in even-even nuclei, employing the finite-range Gogny force in its D1S and D1M parametrizations. The self-consistent blocking procedure allows different deformation parameters and pairing properties for each multiquasiparticle excitation, and the results are compared with the equal filling approximation (EFA) that neglects time-odd fields. Calculations are performed for selected nuclei across the nuclear chart, with the Gogny force parameters fixed from global fits to nuclear matter and finite nuclei properties.

## Key numbers

- In $^{254}$No, the $K=16^{+}$ four-quasiparticle isomer has a half-life of 184 $\mu$s and is built from two-proton and two-neutron excitations in $\Omega=9/2$ and $\Omega=7/2$ orbitals.
- In $^{178}$Hf, the $K=16^{+}$ isomer has a lifetime of 31 years, the longest among known high-$K$ isomeric states in even-even nuclei.
- The $K=16^{+}$ four-quasiparticle state in $^{254}$No shows almost no static pairing correlations, indicating severe quenching of pairing in the isospin channel of the excitation.
- The difference between D1S and D1M parametrization results for excitation energies is small, giving the same level of confidence in studying high-$K$ isomeric states.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of two-quasiparticle excitations in actinide and super-heavy nuclei using Gogny D1S (Delaroche 2006), which preserved time-reversal symmetry in most applications and did not consider four-quasiparticle excitations. The approach contrasts with mic-mac models (Nazarewicz 1990, Jachimowicz 2015) and the projected shell model (Sun 2004), which use fixed deformation parameters and pairing gaps for all configurations, limiting their ability to capture self-consistent blocking effects. The universal character of the Gogny force enables consistent description of decay products and facilitates future calculations of electromagnetic decay using angular-momentum-projected wave functions.
