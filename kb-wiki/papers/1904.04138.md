# A Lattice Story of Proton Spin

> In this contribution, I summarized the recent Lattice QCD consensuses on the quark helicity, plus the investigations on the gluon helicity and orbital angular momenta. The preliminary non-perturbative normalized and renormalized Ji quark and gluon angular momentum results are also reported and compared with the previous 1-loop perturbative renormalized results.

# A Lattice Story of Proton Spin

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of the proton spin decomposition have reached consensus on quark helicity contributions (Δu, Δd, Δs) that agree with phenomenological determinations from deep inelastic scattering, but the gluon helicity and orbital angular momentum components remain preliminary and subject to uncontrolled systematic uncertainties from excited-state contamination, chiral extrapolation, and continuum extrapolation.

## Method

The χQCD collaboration and other lattice groups computed nucleon matrix elements of axial-vector currents for quark helicity, gauge-fixed gluon spin operators for gluon helicity, and energy-momentum tensor form factors T₁ and T₂ for Ji quark and gluon angular momentum. Calculations used overlap fermions on domain wall sea ensembles (RBC 24I at a=0.111 fm, 32ID at a=0.143 fm), clover fermions on HISQ sea, and twisted mass fermions, with non-perturbative renormalization including quark-gluon mixing for the energy-momentum tensor. Excited-state contamination was studied via two-state fits and axial-vector Ward identity ratios R₁ and R₂.

## Key numbers

- Δu ∈ [0.82, 0.85], Δd ∈ [-0.45, -0.42], Δs ∈ [-0.11, -0.08] at Q² = 3 GeV² from COMPASS global fit (Adolph 2015)
- g_A³ = 1.2723(23) from neutron weak decay experiment (Patrignani 2016)
- Preliminary non-perturbatively renormalized Ji angular momentum fractions (400 MeV pion mass, a=0.143 fm, uncontrolled systematics): 2J_u = 0.46(6), 2J_d = 0.00(3), 2J_s = 0.02(3), 2J_g = 0.52(10)
- Alternative Ji result using T₂(0) from 32ID ensemble to correct momentum fractions: 2J_u = 0.57(6), 2J_d = 0.00(5), 2J_s = 0.04(3), 2J_g = 0.39(10)
- Corresponding quark orbital angular momenta: 2L_u = -0.28(7), 2L_d = 0.41(6), 2L_s = 0.08(3)
- Gluon helicity from phenomenology: Δg ≈ 0.4 at MSbar 10 GeV² (de Florian 2014), dropping x < 0.05 contribution
- Lattice spacing for overlap fermion calculations: a = 0.111 fm (RBC 24I) and a = 0.143 fm (32ID)
- Pion mass range for gluon spin study: [140, 400] MeV
- Nucleon momentum for gluon spin: up to P_z = 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing lattice QCD effort to compute proton spin components from first principles, following the quenched clover calculation of Deka (2013) and the 2-flavor twisted mass calculation of Alexandrou (2017). The quark helicity results now agree across three different fermion actions (overlap, clover, twisted mass) and with phenomenological determinations from de Florian (2009), NNPDFpol1.1 (Nocera 2014), and COMPASS (Adolph 2015), with lattice QCD providing more precise predictions for Δs than phenomenology. The preliminary gluon angular momentum results show a larger gluon contribution than previous 1-loop perturbatively renormalized results, but require further study of excited-state contamination (particularly from πN states as suggested by chiral perturbation theory in Bar 2018) and continuum extrapolation. The work enables future precision determinations of the full proton spin decomposition including orbital angular momentum, with the LaMET framework providing a path to connect lattice-calculable gluon spin under Coulomb or temporal gauge to the experimentally relevant gluon helicity.
