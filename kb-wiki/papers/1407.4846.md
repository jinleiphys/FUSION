# Two-Particle Elastic Scattering in a Finite Volume Including QED

> The presence of long-range interactions violates a condition necessary to relate the energy of two particles in a finite volume to their S-matrix elements in the manner of Luscher. While in infinite volume, QED contributions to low-energy charged particle scattering must be resummed to all orders in perturbation theory (the Coulomb ladder diagrams), in a finite volume the momentum operator is gapped, allowing for a perturbative treatment. The leading QED corrections to the two-particle finite-volume energy quantization condition below the inelastic threshold, as well as approximate formulas for energy eigenvalues, are obtained. In particular, we focus on two spinless hadrons in the A1+ irreducible representation of the cubic group, and truncate the strong interactions to the s-wave. These results are necessary for the analysis of Lattice QCD+QED calculations of charged-hadron interactions, and can be straightforwardly generalized to other representations of the cubic group, to hadrons with spin, and to include higher partial waves.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading QED corrections to the two-particle finite-volume energy quantization condition for elastic scattering, showing that in a finite volume the momentum operator is gapped (scale set by 1/L), allowing QED to be treated perturbatively in α rather than requiring the all-orders resummation of Coulomb ladder diagrams needed in infinite volume. The results are specifically obtained for two spinless hadrons in the A1+ irreducible representation of the cubic group with strong interactions truncated to s-wave.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT) to separate QCD and QED interactions, then generalize the Lüscher quantization condition to include QED by computing the finite-volume two-point function with a single insertion of a Coulomb photon. They regulate divergent sums using dimensional regularization with a modified minimal subtraction scheme (MS̅_FV) and relate the finite-volume strong interaction coefficients to their infinite-volume counterparts through renormalization. The quantization condition is obtained by equating the inverse of the finite-volume strong interaction to the finite-volume two-point function, with the leading QED correction appearing as a double sum over integer triplets involving the Coulomb photon propagator.

## Key numbers

- The finite-volume Coulomb potential between charges is given by U(r,L) = (α/πL) Σ_{n≠0} (1/|n|²) exp(i2π n·r/L), where α = e²/4π and L is the spatial extent of the cubic volume
- The geometric constant R = -178.42(01), which appears in the regulated double sum S₂(x) for the QED correction
- The integer sum I ≈ -8.913632, which appears in the shifted ERE parameters a'_C and r'_0 due to single-particle finite-volume corrections
- The inelastic threshold for the two-hadron state is at √(2πM/L) + O(1/M), set by the lowest photon energy in the finite volume (E = 2π/L)
- The parameter η in finite volume scales as η ∼ αML, so QED is perturbative when ML ≪ 1/α

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher formalism (Lüscher 1986, 1990) for extracting two-body scattering amplitudes from finite-volume energies to include QED, which was previously excluded because long-range interactions violate the finite-range assumption required by the original formalism. The authors build on the Bethe (1949) analysis of Coulomb-modified effective range expansion as reformulated in EFT by Kong and Ravndal (1999), and on finite-volume QED treatments for hadron masses by Hayakawa (2008) and Davoudi (2014). These results enable lattice QCD+QED calculations of charged-hadron interactions (such as π⁺π⁺ scattering) where electromagnetic corrections are relevant, and can be generalized to other cubic group representations, hadrons with spin, and higher partial waves.
