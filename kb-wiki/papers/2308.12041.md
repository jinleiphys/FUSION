# Deciphering $\Xi^-$ capture events in light emulsion nuclei

## Key claim
The recently reported $^{14}$N capture events KINKA and IRRAWADDY are more likely $\Xi^0_{1p}$â€“$^{14}$C nuclear states than $\Xi^-_{1s}$â€“$^{14}$N states, because the $1s_{\Xi^-}$ assignment would imply considerably smaller values of the $\Xi$-nuclear potential depth $V_\Xi$ and would conflict with the expected capture rate hierarchy where $1s_{\Xi^-}$ capture rates are roughly 1% of $1p_{\Xi^-}$ capture rates.

## Method
The authors calculate $\Xi^-$ atomic and nuclear bound states in $N=Z$ nuclei using a finite-size Coulomb potential plus a $t\rho$ optical potential $V_{\rm opt}^\Xi$ with density-dependent $\Xi N$ isoscalar scattering amplitude $b_0^A(\rho)$, accounting for long-range Pauli correlations. For the $\Xi^0_{1p}$â€“$^{14}$C alternative, they add a strong-interaction Lane term for isospin $T\neq0$ nuclear cores, with sign and strength estimated from the $\Xi N$ $s$-wave HALQCD underlying interaction. They fit one or two interaction parameters ($b_0$ for $\Xi N$ induced potential and $B_0$ for $\Xi NN$ induced three-body potential) to reproduce observed binding energies from emulsion capture events.

## Key numbers
- $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}(^{12}{\rm C}) = 0.82 \pm 0.15$ MeV from two KEK-E176 events, fitted by ${\rm Re}\,b_0 = 0.495 \pm 0.030$ fm, leading to nuclear-matter depth $V_\Xi = 21.2 \pm 0.7$ MeV
- IRRAWADDY binding energy $B_{\Xi^-} = 6.27 \pm 0.27$ MeV; KINKA+IRRAWADDY weighted average $B_{\Xi^-} = 6.13 \pm 0.25$ MeV or $6.46 \pm 0.25$ MeV
- $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}(^{14}{\rm N}) = 1.27 \pm 0.21$ MeV from J-PARC E07 IBUKI event
- $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}(^{14}{\rm N}) = 1.03 \pm 0.18$ MeV from KEK E373 KISO event
- $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}(^{14}{\rm N}) = 1.18 \pm 0.22$ MeV from KEK E176 event 14-03-35
- $B_{\Xi^-}^{1p}(^{12}{\rm C}) = 0.82 \pm 0.17$ MeV and $0.82 \pm 0.14$ MeV from KEK E176 events 10-09-06 and 13-11-14
- $2P$ atomic binding energies: $B_{\Xi^-}^{2P} = 0.285$ MeV for $^{12}$C, $0.393$ MeV for $^{14}$N
- $3D$ atomic binding energies: 126 keV in C, 175 keV in N, 231 keV in O
- $\Xi^0$+$^{14}$C threshold at $-6.18$ MeV relative to $\Xi^-$+$^{14}$N
- When fitting both $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N $1p$ states plus IRRAWADDY $B_{\Xi^-}^{1s}=6.27$ MeV: $V_\Xi^{(2)} = 30.6 \pm 1.7$ MeV, $V_\Xi^{(3)} = -28.2 \pm 3.9$ MeV, total $V_\Xi = 2.4 \pm 2.2$ MeV
- When fitting both $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N $1p$ states plus KINKA higher-binding $B_{\Xi^-}^{1s}=8.00$ MeV: $V_\Xi^{(2)} = 26.4 \pm 2.6$ MeV, $V_\Xi^{(3)} = -15.4 \pm 5.7$ MeV, total $V_\Xi = 11.0 \pm 3.1$ MeV
- Predicted $B_{\Xi^-}^{1s}(^{11}{\rm B})$ ranges from 4.20 MeV to 9.00 MeV depending on input assumptions

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Friedman and Gal, 2021) showing that five KEK and J-PARC uniquely assigned two-body $\Xi^-$ capture events in CNO light emulsion nuclei are consistent with Coulomb-assisted $1p_{\Xi^-}$ nuclear states in a $\Xi$-nuclear potential of depth $V_\Xi \gtrsim 20$ MeV. The paper challenges the assignment of KINKA and IRRAWADDY as $\Xi^-_{1s}$â€“$^{14}$N states reported by the J-PARC E07 collaboration (Nakazawa et al.), proposing instead a $\Xi^0_{1p}$â€“$^{14}$C interpretation (Friedman and Gal, 2023). This resolves the capture rate hierarchy problem, since $1s_{\Xi^-}$ capture rates are estimated at roughly 1% of $1p_{\Xi^-}$ capture rates (Zhu et al., 1991; Koike, 2017). The analysis uses a Woods-Saxon potential depth $V_\Xi = 17 \pm 6$ MeV deduced from the $^9$Be($K^-,K^+$) quasi-free $\Xi^-$ spectrum (Harada and Hirabayashi, 2021) as a reference, contrasting with smaller values from HALQCD (2020), EFT@NLO (Haidenbauer and Meissner, 2019; Kolesnikov, 2019), and RMF (Gaitanos, 2021). The upcoming J-PARC E70 $^{12}$C$(K^-,K^+)^{12}_{\Xi}$Be experiment with 2 MeV FWHM simulated resolution aims to observe $\Xi^-_{1s}$â€“$^{11}$B signals, for which this work predicts a wide range of binding energies depending on which capture data are accepted.
