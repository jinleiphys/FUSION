# Self-Consistent Hartree-Fock Based Random Phase Approximation And The Spurious State Mixing

> We use a fully self-consistent Hartree$-$Fock (HF) based continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) to calculate strength functions $S(E)$ and transition densities $ρ_t(r)$ for isoscalar giant resonances with multipolarities $L = 0$, 1 and 2 in $^{80}Zr$ nucleus. In particular, we consider the effects of spurious state mixing (SSM) in the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) and extend the projection method to determine the mixing amplitude of spurious state so that properly normalized $S(E)$ and $ρ_t(r)$ having no contribution due to SSM can be obtained. For the calculation to be highly accurate we use a very fine radial mesh (0.04 fm) and zero smearing width in HF$-$CRPA calculations. We first use our most accurate results as a basis to establish the credibility of the projection method, employed to eliminate the SSM, and then to investigate the consequences of the common violation of self-consistency, in actual implementation of HF based CRPA and discretized RPA (DRPA), as often encountered in the published literature. The HF$-$DRPA calculations are carried out using a typical box size of 12 fm and a very large box of 72 fm, for different values of particle-hole energy cutoff ranging from 50 to 600 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock based continuum random phase approximation (HF-CRPA) calculations with very fine radial mesh (0.04 fm) and zero smearing width, spurious state mixing (SSM) in the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) is negligible; however, common violations of self-consistency in actual implementations, such as coarse mesh, finite smearing, and limited particle-hole energy cutoffs, introduce significant SSM that can be accurately removed using an extended projection method.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent HF-CRPA calculations for isoscalar giant resonances (L=0, 1, 2) in ⁸⁰Zr using a simplified Skyrme interaction (t₀ = -1800 MeV·fm³, t₃ = 12871 MeV·fm^(3(α+1)), α = 1/3) with spin-orbit and Coulomb interactions switched off. They extended the projection operator method to determine the mixing amplitude of the spurious state and properly normalize the strength function S(E) and transition density ρ_t(r) for the ISGDR, then compared these highly accurate results with calculations using coarser meshes, finite smearing widths, and discretized RPA (DRPA) with various box sizes (12 fm and 72 fm) and particle-hole energy cutoffs (50 to 600 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter properties from the Skyrme interaction: binding energy per nucleon E/A = -15.99 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.157 fm⁻³, incompressibility coefficient K_nm = 226 MeV
- Radial mesh sizes used: dr_HF = 0.04 fm and dr_RPA = 0.04 fm for the most accurate calculations, with N_HF = 900 and N_RPA = 300 mesh points
- For the fully self-consistent CRPA calculation, the spurious state appears at 0.079 MeV and exhausts 99.99% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for the operator f₁ = rY₁M
- The spurious state mixing amplitude b_n² is ~10⁻⁸ for the fully self-consistent CRPA calculation, indicating negligible SSM
- For the free response (uncorrelated), only 6.8% of the EWTS for operator f₃ contributes to intrinsic excitations for E < 20 MeV after projection
- Centroid energies for the most accurate calculation: ISGMR E₀ ≈ 21.5 MeV, ISGDR E₁ ≈ 35.5 MeV, ISGQR E₂ ≈ 16.5 MeV (peak energies are higher by about 0.5 MeV for ISGMR and 0.15 MeV for ISGDR)
- Using dr = (0.24, 0.24) fm, the spurious state energy shifts to 0.7 MeV; centroid energies change by only about 0.08 MeV for ISGDR
- For DRPA with box size 12 fm and E_ph^max = 50 MeV, the total FEWSR is 5-6% lower than with E_ph^max = 200 MeV for Γ = 0.5 MeV, and 9-10% lower for Γ = 2 MeV
- For DRPA with E_ph^max = 50 MeV, the ISGMR centroid energy is overestimated by 0.5 MeV compared to the CRPA result

## Context

This work extends the projection method for eliminating spurious state mixing in ISGDR calculations, previously described by Shlomo and Sanzhur (2000), by properly normalizing S(E) and ρ_t(r) and determining the mixing amplitude of the spurious state. The paper addresses the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental data for the ISGDR centroid energy, which had raised doubts about extracting the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient K_nm from compression mode energies (Dumitrescu, 1983; Blaizot, 1980). The results enable accurate determination of K_nm from comparison with high-precision experimental data obtained at Texas A&M University using the beam analysis system and MDM spectrometer (Clark, 2001), and provide benchmarks for assessing the reliability of common HF-RPA implementations in the literature.
