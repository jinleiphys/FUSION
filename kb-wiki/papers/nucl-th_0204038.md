# Structures and Transitions in Light Unstable Nuclei

> We study the structures of the unstable Be isotopes with the theoretical method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. It is found that various structures of the excited states appear in the low-energy region of neutron-rich Be nuclei. Focusing on the 2$α$ clustering, we analyze the intrinsic structures with the help of the experimental data of Gamow-Teller transitions.

## Key claim

The ground state of \(^{12}\)Be is not the ordinary neutron closed \(p\)-shell state but a 2-particle-2-hole state with developed clustering structure, as evidenced by the Gamow-Teller transition strength being about one third of the value expected for the closed \(p\)-shell configuration.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after projection (VAP) to calculate the excited states of \(^{12}\)Be and \(^{11}\)Be. They used the MV1 case3 central force with Majorana parameter \(m=0.65\) and the G3RS spin-orbit force, testing two strengths: \(u_{ls}=3700\) MeV and \(u_{ls}=2500\) MeV. After obtaining intrinsic states from VAP calculations for various \(J^\pi_n\) states, they improved the wave functions by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and norm matrices simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The experimental \(B(GT)\) from \(^{12}\)Be(\(0^+\)) to \(^{12}\)B(\(1^+\)) is about one third of the value expected for the closed \(p\)-shell \(^{12}\)Be(\(0^+\)).
- Predicted \(B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) in the ground band of \(^{12}\)Be is 14 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- Predicted \(B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_2)\) in the second \(K^\pi=0^+\) band of \(^{12}\)Be is 7 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- Binding energies of \(^{11}\)Be are 58 MeV in case (1) with \(u_{ls}=3700\) MeV and 54 MeV in case (2) with \(u_{ls}=2500\) MeV.
- The highest spin in the \(K^\pi=3/2^-\) band of \(^{11}\)Be is \(15/2^-\) in case (1) and \(13/2^-\) in case (2).
- The lowest \(3/2^-\) and \(5/2^-\) states in the \(K^\pi=1/2^-\) band of \(^{11}\)Be have more than 10% breaking of the \(\alpha\) clusters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD studies on \(^{10}\)Be (Kanada-En'yo 1998, Dote 1997) which found molecule-like structures coexisting with shell-model-like states in the low-energy region. The results are consistent with the shell model analysis by Suzuki (1999) regarding the disappearance of the neutron magic number 8 in neutron-rich Be isotopes. The calculations enable future studies of clustering and molecular structures in light unstable nuclei, and the comparison with Gamow-Teller transition data provides a method to probe \(\alpha\)-cluster dissociation in daughter nuclei.
