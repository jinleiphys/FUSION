# Quark Counting, Drell-Yan West, and the Pion Wave Function

> The relation between the pion's quark distribution function, $q(x)$, its light-front wave function, and the elastic charge form factor, $F(Δ^2)$ is explored. The square of the leading-twist pion wave function at a special probe scale, $ζ_H$, is determined using models and Poincare covariance from realistic results for $q(x)$. This wave function is then used to compute form factors with the result that the Drell-Yan-West and quark counting relationships are not satisfied. A new relationship between $q(x)$ and $F(Δ^2)$ is proposed.

## Key claim

The Drell-Yan-West and quark counting relationships between the pion's quark distribution function and its elastic charge form factor are not generally satisfied when using realistic non-perturbative wave functions; the authors propose a modified relation where the form factor falls as log(Δ²)/(Δ²)^((n_H+1)/2) rather than the simple power laws predicted by the original relations.

## Method

The authors use the relation between the pion's light-front wave function and its quark distribution function at a hadronic scale ζ_H where only dressed valence quarks carry all pion momentum. They employ two model wave functions: Model 1 derived from the parameter-free prediction of the pion valence-quark distribution from Cui et al. (2021), and Model 2 from Ding et al. (2019), both respecting Poincare covariance. The form factors are then computed from these wave functions using the standard light-front expression for the electromagnetic form factor, and the asymptotic behavior is extracted analytically for power-law wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For power-law wave functions with n=1 (q(x) ~ (1-x)^1), the asymptotic form factor behaves as 6(log²(Δ²)-4log(Δ²)+8)/(2Δ²) - 2(log(Δ²)+2)/Δ⁴
- For n=2 (q(x) ~ (1-x)^2), the asymptotic form factor behaves as 180√π((Δ²-6)log(√Δ²+√(Δ²/4+1))/(Δ²)^(5/2) + (16-5√(Δ²+4))/(2Δ⁴))
- For n=3 (q(x) ~ (1-x)^3), the asymptotic form factor behaves as 840(3(log²(Δ²)-3log(Δ²)+7)/Δ⁶ + (3log(Δ²)-14)/(6Δ⁴))
- Model 1 uses M=134 MeV to reproduce measured data; Model 2 uses Λ²=0.36 GeV² corresponding to M=140 MeV
- Model 2's quark distribution has endpoint behavior ~(1-x)^3.27, while Model 1 has ~(1-x)^2, yet their first 11 moments agree to better than 1%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Drell and Yan (1969) and West (1970) who proposed a relation between the high-x behavior of quark distributions and the asymptotic form factor, and the quark counting rules of Brodsky (1973, 1974), Farrar (1975, 1979), and Sivers (1982). The authors use the hadronic scale concept from Cui et al. (2021) and Ding et al. (2019) where dressed valence quarks carry all momentum. The results challenge the applicability of both Drell-Yan-West and quark counting relations for the pion, showing that non-perturbative effects produce logarithmic modifications to the asymptotic power laws. The work enables future experimental tests at JLab and the EIC, particularly in the unexplored high-Δ² region where the two models show significant differences.
