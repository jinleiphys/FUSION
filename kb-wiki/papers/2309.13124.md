# Investigating nonflow contribution subtraction in d-Au collisions with AMPT model

> This paper presents research that focuses on nonflow contribution subtraction in heavy-ion collisions, using a multiphase transport model (AMPT). Specifically, the study aims to investigate the behavior of charged particle elliptic flow ($v_{\rm 2}$) in d-Au collisions at a collision energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV and to determine the impact of nonflow sources, such as jet correlations and resonance decays, in small collision systems. To reduce nonflow effects, the per-trigger yield distribution in peripheral d-Au collisions or pp collisions with the same collision energy is subtracted. Our results show that the nonflow effects in central and mid-central collisions are not strongly dependent on subtracting the per-trigger yield distribution in peripheral d-Au collisions or pp collisions. Furthermore, the elliptic flow of charged particles, after removing nonflow effects through two subtracting methods from this work, exhibits consistency in various collision centrality classes. We also discuss comparisons with measurements from d-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV. Overall, this work provides valuable insights and serves as a reference for researchers studying nonflow contribution subtraction in experiments with small collision systems.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model for d-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, the nonflow effects (such as jet correlations and resonance decays) in central and mid-central collisions are not strongly dependent on whether the per-trigger yield distribution is subtracted using peripheral d-Au collisions or pp collisions, and the two subtraction methods yield consistent elliptic flow results.

## Method

The study used the string-melting version of the AMPT model to generate approximately 10 million events for d-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV and pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV. Two-particle azimuthal correlations of charged hadrons with \(0.5 < p_T < 3.5\) GeV/\(c\) were measured, requiring a pseudorapidity separation of \(0.48 < |\Delta\eta| < 0.7\) to suppress small-angle correlations. The per-trigger yield distributions from peripheral d-Au collisions (50–88% centrality) or from pp collisions were subtracted from the central and mid-central collision yields to remove nonflow contributions, and the resulting \(\Delta Y(\Delta\phi)\) distributions were fit with a Fourier series to extract the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\).

## Key numbers

- The maximum deviation between the \(v_2\) values obtained by subtracting peripheral d-Au collisions versus pp collisions for 0–5% most central d-Au collisions is less than 2%.
- The maximum deviation between the \(v_2\) values with nonflow subtraction (using peripheral d-Au) and without nonflow subtraction for 0–5% most central d-Au collisions is less than 6%.
- Charged hadrons with \(0.5 < p_T < 3.5\) GeV/\(c\) were used, with each pair including at least one particle at low \(p_T\) (\(0.5 < p_T < 0.75\) GeV/\(c\)).
- The analysis used collision centrality intervals of top 5%, 10–20%, 20–30%, 30–40%, 40–50%, and 50–88% of the total charged particle elliptic flow distributions.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonflow subtraction methods in small collision systems, which were previously applied in experimental analyses by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC (Adare 2013, Adare 2014, Adamczyk 2015) and by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at the LHC (CMS 2012, Abelev 2012, Aad 2012). The paper provides a model-based comparison of two subtraction techniques (using peripheral d-Au collisions versus pp collisions) for the same collision system, which had not been calculated by both methods in experimental analyses. This enables researchers to better interpret nonflow subtraction in small collision system experiments at RHIC and the LHC.
