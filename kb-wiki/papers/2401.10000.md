# The possible $K \bar{K}^*$ and $D \bar{D}^*$ bound and resonance states by solving Schrodinger equation

> The Schrodinger equation with a Yukawa type of potential is solved analytically. When different boundary conditions are taken into account, a series of solutions are indicated as Bessel function, the first kind of Hankel function and the second kind of Hankel function, respectively. Subsequently, the scattering processes of $K \bar{K}^*$ and $D \bar{ D}^*$ are investigated. In the $K \bar{K}^*$ sector, the $f_1(1285)$ particle is treated as a $K \bar{K}^*$ bound state, therefore, the coupling constant in the $K \bar{K}^*$ Yukawa potential can be fixed according to the binding energy of the $f_1(1285)$ particle. Consequently, a $K \bar{K}^*$ resonance state is generated by solving the Schrodinger equation with the outgoing wave condition, which lie at $1417-i18$MeV on the complex energy plane. It is reasonable to assume that the $K \bar{K}^*$ resonance state at $1417-i18$MeV might correspond to the $f_1(1420)$ particle in the review of Particle Data Group(PDG).In the $D \bar{D}^*$ sector, since the $X(3872)$ particle is almost located at the $D \bar{ D}^*$ threshold, the binding energy of it equals to zero approximately. Therefore, the coupling constant in the $D \bar{ D}^*$ Yukawa potential is determined, which is related to the first zero point of the zero order Bessel function. Similarly to the $K \bar{K}^*$ case, four resonance states are produced as solutions of the Schrodinger equation with the outgoing wave condition. It is assumed that the resonance states at $3885-i1$MeV, $4029-i108$ MeV, $4328-i191$MeV and $4772-i267$MeV might be associated with the $Zc(3900)$, the $X(3940)$, the $χ_{c1}(4274)$ and $χ_{c1}(4685)$ particles, respectively. It is noted that all solutions are isospin degenerate.

# The possible $K \bar{K}^*$ and $D \bar{D}^*$ bound and resonance states by solving Schrodinger equation

## Key claim
The authors claim that the $f_1(1285)$ and $X(3872)$ particles can be treated as $K \bar{K}^*$ and $D \bar{D}^*$ bound states respectively in a one-pion-exchange Yukawa potential, and that solving the Schrodinger equation with outgoing wave boundary conditions then generates resonance states which "might correspond to" known PDG particles -- but the identification is explicitly hedged as an assumption, not a proof, and all solutions are isospin degenerate, meaning the method cannot distinguish charged from neutral channels.

## Method
The radial Schrodinger equation with S-wave approximation and a Yukawa-type potential $V(r) = -g^2 e^{-mr}/d$ (where $d=1/m$ and $m$ is the pion mass) is solved analytically by transformation to a Bessel equation. The coupling constant $g$ is fixed by requiring the first zero of the Bessel function $J_\rho(\alpha)$ to match the binding energy of a known bound state ($f_1(1285)$ for $K \bar{K}^*$, $X(3872)$ for $D \bar{D}^*$). Resonance states are then obtained by imposing the outgoing wave condition $H_\rho^{(2)}(\alpha)=0$ (second kind Hankel function) with the same $\alpha$, yielding complex eigenenergies $E = M - i\Gamma/2$.

## Key numbers
- $K \bar{K}^*$ bound state $f_1(1285)$: binding energy = 105 MeV relative to $K \bar{K}^*$ threshold; Bessel order $\rho = 3.703$; first zero $\alpha = 7.1831$; coupling constant $g = 1.682$
- $K \bar{K}^*$ resonance state: $1417 - i18$ MeV (complex energy), proposed to correspond to $f_1(1420)$ (PDG mass $1426.3 \pm 0.9$ MeV, width $54.5 \pm 2.6$ MeV)
- $D \bar{D}^*$ bound state $X(3872)$: binding energy $\approx 0$ MeV (at $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold); Bessel order $\rho = 0$; first zero of $J_0(\alpha)$ = 2.405; coupling constant $g = 0.323$
- $D \bar{D}^*$ resonance states (from $D^0 \bar{D}^{*0}$ channel): $3885 - i1$ MeV, $4029 - i108$ MeV, $4328 - i191$ MeV, $4772 - i267$ MeV
- Alternative treatment of $X(3872)$ as $D^- D^{*+}$ bound state: binding energy = 8.11 MeV; Bessel order $\rho = 1.796$; first zero $\alpha = 4.8583$; coupling constant $g = 0.6520$; resonance states at $3880 - i24$ MeV and $3947 - i256$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the method previously applied to proton-neutron resonance states with one-pion-exchange potential (Sun, in preparation; Zhang, 2004) to the meson-meson sector. It contrasts with the unitary coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach of Roca (2005) and Oset (2002), where vector meson exchange (from SU(3) hidden gauge symmetry) dominates and only one pole is generated dynamically. The authors claim that by using a one-pion-exchange potential instead, they can relate the $f_1(1285)$ bound state to the $f_1(1420)$ resonance, and the $X(3872)$ bound state to multiple charmonium-like resonances including $Zc(3900)$, $X(3940)$, $\chi_{c1}(4274)$, and $\chi_{c1}(4685)$. This enables a unified description of bound and resonance states from the same potential with different boundary conditions, though the isospin degeneracy of the solutions limits comparison with experiment.
