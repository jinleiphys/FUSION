# Phenomenological description of the states $0^+$ and $2^+$ in some even-even nuclei

> A sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian is used to describe the states $0^+$ and $2^+$ identified in several nuclei by various types of experiments. Two alternative descriptions of energy levels are proposed. One corresponds to a semi-classical approach of the model Hamiltonian while the other one provides the exact eigenvalues. Both procedures yield close formulas for energies. The first procedure involves four parameters, while the second involves a compact formula with five parameters. In each case the parameters are fixed by a least-square fit procedure. Applications are performed for eight even-even nuclei. Both methods yield results which are in a surprisingly good agreement with the experimental data. We give also our predicted reduced transition probabilities within the two approaches, although the corresponding experimental data are not yet available.

## Key claim

A sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian, treated both semi-classically and exactly, can describe the complete sets of $0^+$ and $2^+$ states in eight even-even nuclei with surprisingly good agreement using only four or five parameters, although the predicted reduced transition probabilities cannot yet be compared to experiment as the corresponding data are not yet available.

## Method

The authors used a sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian and applied two alternative approaches: a semi-classical treatment via a Time Dependent Variational Principle with coherent states, yielding a four-parameter energy formula, and an exact diagonalization in the boson basis $|Nv\alpha JM\rangle$ yielding a five-parameter formula. Parameters were fixed by least-square fits to experimental excitation energies of $0^+$ and $2^+$ states for eight even-even nuclei: $^{152,154}$Gd, $^{162}$Dy, $^{168}$Er, $^{176}$Hf, $^{180,184}$W, $^{190}$Os.

## Key numbers

* For $^{152}$Gd, 11 states $0^+$ (energies below 3000 keV) and 34 states $2^+$ (below 3500 keV) were described; fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 548.789 keV, B = -21.281 keV, C = -141.8 keV, F = 1.135 keV.
* For $^{154}$Gd, 15 states $0^+$ and 60 states $2^+$ were described; fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 294.746 keV, B = -6.726 keV, C = -40.0 keV, F = 0.21 keV.
* For $^{168}$Er, 26 states $0^+$ and 79 states $2^+$ were described; fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 394.2 keV, B = -10.4 keV, C = -280 keV, F = 0.3865 keV.
* For $^{162}$Dy, 12 states $0^+$ and 11 states $2^+$ were described; fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 1063.4 keV, B = -93.0 keV, C = -470 keV, F = 11.0 keV.
* For $^{176}$Hf, fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 1140.6 keV, B = -111.787 keV, C = -700 keV, F = 15.145 keV.
* For $^{180}$W, fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 1030.37 keV, B = -92.32 keV, C = -656 keV, F = 11.45 keV.
* For $^{184}$W, fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 781.966 keV, B = -50.0314 keV, C = -523 keV, F = 4.3282 keV.
* For $^{190}$Os, fitted semi-classical parameters: A = 856.71 keV, B = -52.904 keV, C = -567.372 keV, F = 4.51 keV.
* For $^{168}$Er, 12 newly identified $2^+$ states were described using the same parameters from a prior fit, with energies such as 2580.4 keV (N=70, v=2) and 2683.2 keV (N=71, v=1).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological description of $0^+$ and $2^+$ states in even-even nuclei using a sixth-order quadrupole boson Hamiltonian previously developed by Raduta (2007). It directly addresses the complete data sets from $(p,t)$ experiments on $^{168}$Er by Bucurescu (2006, 2007), which were previously described only qualitatively by the projected shell model (PSM) and quasiparticle phonon model (QPM), both of which have inherent drawbacks such as restricted fermion spaces or Pauli principle violations. The compact analytical formulas enable prediction of additional states that may be confirmed by higher-resolution experiments, and the potential energy surfaces for Gd isotopes show signatures of the spherical-to-deformed shape transition, with $^{154}$Gd exhibiting features of the critical-point X(5) symmetry.
