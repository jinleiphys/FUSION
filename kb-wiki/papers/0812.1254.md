# Recent topics of mesic atoms and mesic nuclei -- $ϕ$ mesic nuclei exist ?--

> We study $ϕ$-meson production in nuclei to investigate the in-medium modification of the $ϕ$-meson spectral function at finite density. We consider (${\bar p},ϕ$), ($γ,p$) and ($π^-,n$) reactions to produce a $ϕ$-meson inside the nucleus and evaluate the effects of the medium modifications to reaction cross sections. The structures of the bound states, $ϕ$-mesic nuclei, are also studied. For strong absorptive interaction cases, we need to know the spectrum shape in a wide energy region to deduce the properties of $ϕ$.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether bound states of phi mesons in nuclei (phi-mesic nuclei) can be observed experimentally, concluding that due to the large decay width of the phi meson in the nuclear medium, clear peak structures in missing mass spectra will be difficult to observe even when bound states exist.

## Method

The authors calculated phi meson optical potentials from the phi meson selfenergy using the local density approximation, considering both a theoretical shallow potential (Re V(0) approximately -7.5 MeV) and a scaled deep potential (Re V(0) = -30 MeV) to simulate a 3% mass reduction. They solved the Klein-Gordon equation selfconsistently to obtain binding energies and widths of phi meson bound states in various nuclei, and used the Green's function method to calculate formation spectra for (p-bar,phi), (gamma,p), and (pi-,n) reactions on carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical optical potential depth: Re V(0) approximately -7.5 MeV at E = m_phi
- Scaled deep potential depth: Re V(0) = -30 MeV at E = m_phi
- For the deep potential on boron-11: 1s state binding energy 10.2 MeV with width 17.9 MeV
- For the deep potential on potassium-39: 1s state binding energy 28.5 MeV with width 20.0 MeV
- For the deep potential on indium-123: 1s state binding energy 34.5 MeV with width 18.7 MeV
- For the deep potential on thallium-207: 1s state binding energy 40.1 MeV with width 17.5 MeV
- For the shallow potential on indium-123: 1s state binding energy 2.34 MeV with width 21.6 MeV
- For the shallow potential on thallium-207: 1s state binding energy 3.73 MeV with width 22.5 MeV
- (p-bar,phi) reaction calculated at p_bar = 1.3 GeV/c
- (gamma,p) reaction calculated at p_gamma = 2.7 GeV/c
- (pi-,n) reaction calculated at p_pi- = 2.0 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of mesic atoms and mesic nuclei, which have been systematically studied for pionic and kaonic systems, to the case of phi mesic nuclei. The study is motivated by QCD sum rule calculations and KEK data suggesting a 3% mass reduction of the phi meson at normal nuclear density, while selfenergy calculations by other groups indicated a significantly smaller attractive potential. The results suggest that further theoretical investigations are needed to extract reliable information from experimental data, and that the conversion spectrum from the (p-bar,phi) reaction coincident with particle emissions from phi decay may provide an important observable.
