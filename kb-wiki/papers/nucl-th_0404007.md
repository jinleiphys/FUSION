# Lifetimes of the $α$ decay chains of superheavy element 115

> The lifetimes of the consecutive $α$ decay chains of recently synthesized new element 115, $^{287}115$ and $^{288}115$, have been calculated theoretically within the WKB approximation using microscopic $α$-nucleus interaction potentials obtained by folding in the densities of the $α$ and the daughter nuclei with a realistic effective interaction. M3Y effective interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange along with the density dependence, has been used for calculating the nuclear interaction potentials. Spherical charge distributions have been used for calculating the Coulomb interaction potentials. These calculations provide consistent estimates for the lifetimes of the consecutive $α$ decay chains of the superheavy element 115.

# Lifetimes of the α decay chains of superheavy element 115

## Key claim
The WKB-based calculations using microscopic double-folding potentials with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction provide consistent estimates for the α-decay half-lives of the chains originating from ²⁸⁷115 and ²⁸⁸115, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that two theoretical results are underestimated, possibly because the centrifugal barrier required for spin-parity conservation could not be included due to the unavailability of spin-parities for the decay-chain nuclei.

## Method
The nuclear interaction potentials were obtained by double-folding the density distributions of the α particle (Gaussian form) and the daughter nucleus (spherical Fermi form) with the M3Y effective interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for single-nucleon exchange and a density-dependent factor. The Coulomb interaction was calculated assuming spherical charge distributions, and the total interaction energy included the centrifugal barrier. Half-lives were computed using the WKB barrier penetration probability with the action integral evaluated between the two turning points, using experimentally measured Q-values and zero-point vibration energies proportional to Q.

## Context
This work extends the application of microscopic double-folding potentials with the DDM3Y interaction to the newly synthesized superheavy element 115, whose decay chains were reported by Oganessian et al. (2004) from the ²⁴³Am + ⁴⁸Ca reaction. The calculations improve significantly over the Viola-Seaborg formula with Sobiczewski constants (VSS), which overestimated half-lives for six of the nine nuclei, while the present DDM3Y results are inconsistent for only one case (²⁸⁴113, where the experimental Q-value uncertainty can account for the overestimation). The method enables reliable half-life predictions for superheavy α-decay chains and reiterates the success of this microscopic approach for the superheavy domain, with the caveat that spin-parity information is needed to include centrifugal barrier effects for hindered decays.
