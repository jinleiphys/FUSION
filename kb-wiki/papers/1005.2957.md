# Associated strangeness production in the pp to pK^+K^-p and pp to pK^+ pi^0 Sigma^0 reactions

> The total and differential cross sections for associated strangeness production in the $pp \to pK^+K^-p$ and $pp \to pK^+π^0Σ^0$ reactions have been studied in a unified approach using an effective Lagrangian model. It is assumed that both the $K^-p$ and $π^0Σ^0$ final states originate from the decay of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance which was formed in the production chain $pp\to p(N^*(1535)\to K^+Λ(1405))$. The available experimental data are well reproduced, especially the ratio of the two total cross sections, which is much less sensitive to the particular model of the entrance channel. The significant coupling of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance to $Λ(1405) K$ is further evidence for large $s \bar{s}$ components in the quark wave function of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the non-phi contributions to both the pp → pK⁺K⁻p and pp → pK⁺π⁰Σ⁰ reactions at a beam energy of 2.83 GeV can be described in a unified model where the K⁻p and π⁰Σ⁰ final states both originate from decay of the Λ(1405) resonance, which itself is produced via the N*(1535) isobar acting as a doorway state. The key hedge is that the model assumes a single Λ(1405) pole framework with parameters from the PDG, even though the authors acknowledge that a chiral unitary description requiring two Λ(1405) poles could produce similar results.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian model with t-channel pion exchange for the pp → pN*(1535) production step, followed by N*(1535) → K⁺Λ(1405) decay and subsequent Λ(1405) → K⁻p or Λ(1405) → π⁰Σ⁰ decay. The N*(1535)Λ(1405)K coupling constant was determined by fitting to π⁻p → Λ(1405)K⁰ total cross section data, while the Λ(1405) width was parametrized with energy-dependent partial widths for πΣ and K⁻N channels using a separable potential model. Final-state interactions between the two emerging protons in the ¹S₀ wave were included via a Jost function formalism.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1535)Λ(1405)K coupling constant was determined as g²_{N*Λ*K}/4π = 0.28, derived from fitting π⁻p → Λ(1405)K⁰ total cross section data.
- The Λ(1405)K⁻N coupling constant at the K⁻N threshold was g²_{Λ*K⁻N}/4π = 0.27, obtained from the energy-dependent width parametrization with a_{πΣ} = 0.22 GeV/c² and b_{K⁻N} = 0.49 GeV/c².
- The predicted non-phi pp → pK⁺K⁻p total cross section at excess energy ε = 108 MeV (T_p = 2.83 GeV) was 100 nb, compared to the experimental value of (98 ± 8 ± 15) nb.
- The predicted pp → pK⁺π⁰Σ⁰ total cross section at the same beam energy (ε = 212 MeV) was 4.0 μb, compared to the experimental value of (1.5 ± 0.3 ± 0.6) μb.
- The ratio R_{Kπ} = σ(pp → pK⁺K⁻p)/σ(pp → pK⁺π⁰Σ⁰) was predicted as approximately 25 × 10⁻³, compared to the experimental value of (65 ± 24) × 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Wilkin (2009) who suggested that the Λ(1405) could be important for non-phi kaon pair production, and builds upon analyses of BES data on J/ψ → p p̄ η and p̄ Λ K⁺ decays and COSY data on pp → pΛK⁺ near threshold that indicated large N*(1535)KΛ coupling. The model provides a unified description of two COSY-ANKE datasets from Zychor et al. (2008) and Maeda et al. (2008) at 2.83 GeV, and the authors note that further data from the HADES collaboration at 3.5 GeV could provide an independent check on the ratio R_{Kπ}.
