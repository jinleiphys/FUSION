# Thermal effects on tidal deformability of a coalescing binary neutron star system

> The study of neutron star mergers by the detection of the emitted gravitational waves is one of the most promised tools to study the properties of dense nuclear matter at high densities. It is worth claiming that, at the moment, strong evidence that the temperature of the stars is zero during the last orbits before coalescing does not exist. Contrariwise, there are some theoretical predictions suggesting that the star's temperature might even be a few MeV. According to the main theory, the tides transfer mechanical energy and angular momentum to the star at the expense of the orbit, where friction within the star converts the mechanical energy into heat. During the inspiral these effects are potentially detectable. Different treatments have been used to estimate the transfer of the mechanical energy and the size of the tidal friction, leading to different conclusions about the importance of pre-merger tidal effects. The present work is dedicated to the study of the effect of temperature on the tidal deformability of neutron stars during the inspiral of a neutron star system just before the merger. We applied a class of hot equations of state originated from various nuclear models and found that even for low values of temperature ($T<1$ MeV) the effects on the basic ingredients of tidal deformability are not negligible. However, according to the main finding, the effect of the temperature on the tidal deformability is indistinguishable. The consequences of this unexpected result are discussed and analyzed.

## Key claim

Although temperature affects the radius and the tidal Love number individually, the tidal deformability lambda (proportional to k2 times R^5) remains almost constant for temperatures up to 1 MeV, making thermal effects on the tidal deformability indistinguishable.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the differential equation for the tidal Love number, using a set of hot equations of state (Lattimer-Swesty, Shen, Steiner, Banik, and MDI-APR) for temperatures from 0.01 to 10 MeV. They computed the mass, radius, Love number k2, and tidal deformability for neutron stars of various masses, focusing on the 1.4 solar mass canonical star, and also calculated the effective tidal deformability for binary systems with mass ratios corresponding to the GW170817 and GW190425 events.

## Key numbers

* For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state, the tidal deformability lambda remains 2.73 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2 for temperatures from 0.01 MeV up to 1 MeV, despite the radius increasing from 12.21 km to 12.82 km and k2 decreasing from 0.1005 to 0.0788.
* At T = 5 MeV, lambda increases to 2.79 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2; at T = 10 MeV, lambda reaches 3.02 x 10^36 g cm^2 s^2.
* The temperature range studied for the main result is T = 0.01 to 1 MeV, motivated by theoretical predictions that neutron star interiors may reach a few MeV before merger.

## Context

This work extends studies of tidal heating during binary neutron star inspiral, where earlier work by Meszaros and Rees (1992), Bildsten (1992), and Kochanek (1992) predicted heating to temperatures comparable to supernova explosions, while Arras and Weinberg (2019) studied Urca reactions driven by tidal motion. The paper is the first systematic study of how temperature affects tidal deformability, finding that the insensitivity of lambda to temperature means gravitational wave observations cannot easily distinguish hot from cold neutron stars, but that accurate radius measurements from other methods could constrain the pre-merger temperature.
