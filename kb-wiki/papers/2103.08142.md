# Predictive power for superheavy nuclear mass and possible stability beyond the neutron drip line in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The predictive power of the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) for nuclear mass is examined in the superheavy region, $102 \le Z \le 120$. The accuracy of predicting the 10 (56) measured (measured and empirical) masses is $0.635$ ($0.642$) MeV, in comparison with $0.515$ ($1.360$) MeV by WS4 and $0.910$ ($2.831$) MeV by FRDM. Possible stability against multineutron emission beyond the two-neutron drip line is explored by the DRHBc theory, which takes into account simultaneously the deformation effects, the pairing correlations, and the continuum effects. Nuclei stable against two- and multineutron emissions beyond the two-neutron drip line are predicted in $_{106}$Sg, $_{108}$Hs, $_{110}$Ds, and $_{112}$Cn isotopic chains, forming a peninsula of stability adjacent to the nuclear mainland. This stability is mainly due to the deformation which significantly affects the shell structure around the Fermi surface. The pairing correlations and continuum influence the stability peninsula in a self-consistent way.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) predicts a "peninsula of stability" beyond the two-neutron drip line for superheavy nuclei with Z = 106 to 112, but this stability depends critically on deformation effects that alter the shell structure around the Fermi surface; the pairing correlations and continuum effects modify the extent of this peninsula in a self-consistent manner.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis using the density functional PC-PK1, with a pairing strength of -325.0 MeV fm^3 and a saturation density of 0.152 fm^-3. The calculations included deformation degrees of freedom via Legendre expansion up to λ_max = 10, with an energy cutoff of 300 MeV and angular momentum cutoff of J_max = 23/2 ħ for the basis. They computed two-neutron separation energies S_2n for even-even superheavy nuclei with 102 ≤ Z ≤ 120 and identified nuclei stable against two-neutron and multineutron emission by comparing binding energies relative to the drip-line nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation for 10 measured masses in the superheavy region (102 ≤ Z ≤ 120): 0.635 MeV for DRHBc, compared to 0.515 MeV for WS4 and 0.910 MeV for FRDM.
- Root-mean-square deviation for 56 measured and empirical masses: 0.642 MeV for DRHBc, compared to 1.360 MeV for WS4 and 2.831 MeV for FRDM.
- A shell closure at neutron number N = 258 is indicated by a gap of about 3 MeV between occupied and unoccupied single-neutron levels in ^366Hs.
- For ^384Hs and ^396Hs, fission barrier heights are approximately 2.0 MeV and 4.6 MeV, respectively.
- For ^384-396Hs, Q_β- values are around 12-15 MeV, corresponding to β- decay half-lives of order 10 ms.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron emission stability beyond the two-neutron drip line, which were performed using nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Stoitsov 2003, Erler 2012) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations (Afanasjev 2013), but those used harmonic oscillator bases unsuitable for weakly bound systems. The DRHBc approach improves upon the earlier relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory (Meng 1998) by including deformation degrees of freedom, and it enables future studies of stability against neutron emission in other mass regions as well as investigations of triaxial deformation and higher multipole effects.
