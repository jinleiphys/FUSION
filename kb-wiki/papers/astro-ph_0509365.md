# Nucleosynthesis in the Outflow from Gamma Ray Burst Accretion Disks

> We examine the nucleosynthesis products that are produced in the outflow from rapidly accreting disks. We find that the type of element synthesis varies dramatically with the degree of neutrino trapping in the disk and therefore the accretion rate of the disk. Disks with relatively high accretion rates such as 10 M_solar/s can produce very neutron rich nuclei that are found in the r process. Disks with more moderate accretion rates can produce copious amounts of Nickel as well as the light elements such as Lithium and Boron. Disks with lower accretion rates such as 0.1 M_solar/s produce large amounts of Nickel as well as some unusual nuclei such as Ti-49, Sc-45, Zn-64, and Mo-92. This wide array of potential nucleosynthesis products is due to the varying influence of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos emitted from the disk on the neutron-to-proton ratio in the outflow. We use a parameterization for the outflow and discuss our results in terms of entropy and outflow acceleration.

# Nucleosynthesis in the Outflow from Gamma Ray Burst Accretion Disks

## Key claim

The type of nucleosynthesis produced in outflows from gamma-ray burst accretion disks varies dramatically with the accretion rate and degree of neutrino trapping, but the results depend on a parameterized outflow model and a range of assumed entropies and acceleration parameters, rather than a self-consistent hydrodynamic calculation.

## Method

The authors used disk models from Popowski et al. (1999) for low accretion rates and from Di Matteo et al. (2002) for high accretion rates, parameterizing the outflow as a wind with a velocity profile controlled by an acceleration parameter β. They calculated the electron fraction evolution under neutrino interactions from the disk, then followed nuclear recombination using an intermediate nuclear network including strong, electromagnetic, and weak interactions, and finally a network handling neutron capture, photodisintegration, beta decay, charged-current neutrino interactions, and beta-delayed neutron emission.

## Context

This work extends studies of nucleosynthesis in gamma-ray burst disk winds by Pruet et al. (2003) and Fujimoto et al. (2003, 2004), and builds on the suggestion by McLaughlin and Surman (2005) that neutrino interactions can drive material neutron-rich in high accretion rate disks. The results show that low accretion rate disks typical of the collapsar model (MacFadyen and Woosley 1999) can produce nuclei such as ⁴⁵Sc, ⁴⁹Ti, ⁶⁴Zn, ⁹²Mo, and ⁹⁴Mo with overproduction factors of 100-1000, while high accretion rate disks can produce r-process nuclei including the A=195 peak. The work also connects to observations of metal-poor halo stars by Sneden et al. and meteoritic data by Qian and Wasserburg (1998) that suggest two distinct r-process sites. Future hydrodynamic studies of the outflow are needed to determine which nucleosynthesis patterns actually occur in nature.
