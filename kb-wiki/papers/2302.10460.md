# How to measure the reactor neutrino flux below the inverse beta decay threshold with CE$ν$NS

> Most antineutrinos produced in a nuclear reactor have energies below the inverse beta decay threshold, and have not yet been detected. We show that a coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiment with an ultra-low energy threshold like NUCLEUS can measure the flux of reactor neutrinos below 1.8 MeV. Using a regularized unfolding procedure, we find that a meaningful upper bound can be placed on the low energy flux, but the existence of the neutron capture component cannot be established.

## Key claim

The authors show that a coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) experiment with an ultra-low energy threshold like NUCLEUS can place a meaningful upper bound on the reactor neutrino flux below 1.8 MeV, but the existence of the neutron capture component of the flux cannot be established even with optimistic future upgrades.

## Method

The authors simulate a NUCLEUS-like germanium detector placed 72 m and 102 m from two 4.25 GW reactor cores at the CHOOZ nuclear power plant. They generate 3000 Monte Carlo datasets assuming Poisson statistics in each nuclear recoil energy bin, then apply a Tikhonov regularized unfolding procedure to extract the low-energy neutrino flux from the observed CEνNS spectrum. The regularization parameter β is chosen as the smallest value that yields a positive definite flux at all energies.

## Key numbers

- Roughly 70% of reactor antineutrinos have energies below the 1.8 MeV inverse beta decay threshold and have not been experimentally detected
- The NUCLEUS experiment has achieved a 20 eV threshold using a 0.5 g Al₂O₃ prototype; NUCLEUS-1kg is expected to reach a 5 eV threshold
- With a 5 eV threshold, only neutrinos above 0.41 MeV can be detected; with a 1 eV threshold, neutrinos above 0.18 MeV can be detected
- The neutron capture component produces neutrinos with energies below about 1.3 MeV
- Scenario 1 (1 kg·year, 100 counts/keV/kg/day background, 5 eV threshold) and Scenario 2 (3 kg·year, 1 count/keV/kg/day background, 5 eV threshold) cannot distinguish the neutron capture component
- Scenario 3 (300 kg·year, 1 count/keV/kg/day background, 1 eV threshold) is illustrative but may be unrealistic in the foreseeable future
- The regularization parameter β = 230 for scenario 1 and β = 100 for scenario 2 yields positive definite fluxes

## Context

This work extends the use of CEνNS beyond neutrino property studies (reviewed by Papoulias (2019)) to directly measure the reactor neutrino flux below the IBD threshold for the first time. The low-energy flux has only been estimated via the summation method, which suffers from the Pandemonium effect (Hardy 1977) leading to underestimated flux below 2 MeV (Algora 2020). A measurement would help refine summation method calculations and test the electron spectral shapes used in the conversion method (Mueller 2011, Huber 2011, Kopeikin 2021), potentially shedding light on the 5 MeV bump observed by Daya Bay (2015), RENO (2015), and Double Chooz (2014).
