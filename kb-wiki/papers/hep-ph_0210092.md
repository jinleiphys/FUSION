# Long distance regularization in chiral perturbation theory with decuplet

> We investigate the use of long distance regularization in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory with decuplet fields. The one-loop decuplet contributions to the octet baryon masses, axial couplings, S-wave nonleptonic hyperon decays and magnetic moments are evaluated in a chirally consistent fashion by employing a cutoff to implement long distance regularization. The convergence of the chiral expansions of these quantities is improved compared to the dimensionally regularized version which indicates that the propagation of Goldstone bosons over distances smaller than a typical hadronic size, which is beyond the regime of chiral perturbation theory but included by dimensional regularization, is removed by use of a cutoff.

## Key claim

Long distance regularization (LDR) using a dipole cutoff improves the convergence of the chiral expansion for octet baryon observables compared to dimensional regularization, because it removes the short-distance contributions from Goldstone boson propagation that lie outside the regime of chiral perturbation theory but are included by dimensional regularization.

## Method

The authors evaluate one-loop decuplet contributions to octet baryon masses, axial couplings, S-wave nonleptonic hyperon decays, and magnetic moments within SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. They replace dimensional regularization with a dipole cutoff function F(q^2) = (Lambda^2/(Lambda^2 - q^2))^2, with the cutoff parameter Lambda in the range 300-600 MeV, to isolate long-distance physics. The power-law divergences in Lambda are absorbed into phenomenologically-determined counterterms, while the remaining chiral corrections are shown to be small.

## Key numbers

- The average octet-decuplet mass splitting Delta = 231 MeV, which does not vanish in the chiral limit
- The dipole cutoff parameter is chosen in the range 300 MeV < Lambda < 600 MeV
- For baryon masses in dimensional regularization, the sum of octet and decuplet contributions gives: N = -0.46 GeV, Sigma = -1.48 GeV, Lambda = -1.02 GeV, Xi = -1.83 GeV
- For baryon masses with LDR at Lambda = 500 MeV, the sum gives: N = -0.00 GeV, Sigma = 0.07 GeV, Lambda = 0.04 GeV, Xi = 0.10 GeV
- The coupling constant |C| = 1.5 +/- 0.3 from Delta -> N pi decay
- The weak coupling h_c = (-0.10 +/- 0.19) x 10^{-7} GeV from nonleptonic hyperon decays
- Input meson masses: m_pi = 138 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, m_eta = 566 MeV (from Gell-Mann-Okubo relation)
- Pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Axial couplings in the SU(6) limit: D = 0.75, F = 0.5, giving g_A = 1.25

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LDR method (Borasoy, Holstein, Lewis 2001) from octet-only calculations to include decuplet intermediate states, which become degenerate with octet states in the 1/N_c limit. The approach matches onto chirally motivated model-dependent approaches such as the cloudy bag model and the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Krehl et al. The Adelaide group (Leinweber, Young, Wright) has advocated similar methods for extrapolating lattice QCD calculations from heavy quark masses down to physical values. The paper demonstrates that LDR preserves chiral invariance and provides an alternative regularization scheme for heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory that yields better convergence than dimensional regularization.
