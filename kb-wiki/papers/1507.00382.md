# A practical parametrization for line shapes of near-threshold states

> Numerous quarkonium(like) states lying near $S$-wave thresholds are observed experimentally. We propose a self-consistent approach to these near-threshold states compatible with unitarity and analyticity. The underlying coupled-channel system includes a bare pole and an arbitrary number of elastic and inelastic channels treated fully nonperturbatively. The resulting analytical parametrization is ideally suited for a combined analysis of the data available in various channels that is exemplified by an excellent overall description of the data for the charged $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a model-independent parametrization for the line shapes of near-threshold states that is consistent with unitarity and analyticity, but the practical applicability relies on neglecting direct interactions within inelastic channels and assuming separable forms for transition vertices, which are justified phenomenologically for the specific systems considered.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) framework that includes a bare pole, an arbitrary number of elastic open-flavor channels, and an arbitrary number of inelastic hidden-flavor channels. They make simplifying assumptions: direct interactions in inelastic channels are neglected, elastic transition vertices take a separable form, and the elastic potential is approximated by a constant matrix. These assumptions allow an analytic solution that reduces the problem to inverting matrices of size equal to the number of elastic channels (typically two). The resulting production amplitudes are then used to fit experimental data for the Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) states.

## Key numbers

- The fit to Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) data achieves a confidence level of 76%.
- The ratio of sources for the elastic channels, xi = g_{[pi Upsilon(5S)][B* Bbar*]} / g_{[pi Upsilon(5S)][B Bbar*]}, is found to be -0.84 +/- 0.05, deviating from the heavy-quark spin symmetry prediction of -1.
- The ratio g_{[pi h_b(1P)][B* Bbar*]} / g_{[pi h_b(1P)][B Bbar*]} is 2.4 +/- 0.6, deviating from the heavy-quark spin symmetry prediction of 1.
- The ratio g_{[pi h_b(2P)][B* Bbar*]} / g_{[pi h_b(2P)][B Bbar*]} is 2.4 +/- 0.7.
- The experimental resolution is modeled by convolution with a Gaussian of sigma = 6 MeV.
- The parameter kappa, related to the range of forces, is fixed to 1 GeV as it is unconstrained by the fit.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Hanhart (2011) and Baru (2010) for near-threshold states by providing a practical parametrization that can handle multiple elastic and inelastic channels simultaneously. It directly addresses the need for adequate theoretical tools for data analysis of states like X(3872), Z_b(10610), Z_b(10650), and Z_c(3900), which are observed near S-wave thresholds. The approach enables combined analysis of data across different decay channels, as demonstrated for the Z_b states using Belle data from Adachi (2012) and Belle (2011), and provides predictions for line shapes in pi Upsilon(nS) channels that can be tested in future multidimensional analyses.
