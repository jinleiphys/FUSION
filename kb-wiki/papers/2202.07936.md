# Static and Dynamic Bethe-Salpeter Equations in the $T$-Matrix Approximation

> While the well-established $GW$ approximation corresponds to a resummation of the direct ring diagrams and is particularly well suited for weakly-correlated systems, the $T$-matrix approximation does sum ladder diagrams up to infinity and is supposedly more appropriate in the presence of strong correlation. Here, we derive and implement, for the first time, the static and dynamic Bethe-Salpeter equations when one considers $T$-matrix quasiparticle energies as well as a $T$-matrix-based kernel. The performance of the static scheme and its perturbative dynamical correction are assessed by computing the neutral excited states of molecular systems. Comparison with more conventional schemes as well as other wave function methods are also reported. Our results suggest that the $T$-matrix-based formalism performs best in few-electron systems where the electron density remains low.

## Key claim

The paper derives and implements, for the first time, static and dynamic Bethe-Salpeter equations using T-matrix quasiparticle energies and a T-matrix-based kernel; the authors suggest that this T-matrix-based formalism performs best in few-electron systems where the electron density remains low, but note that the overall accuracy still needs to be assessed for larger systems where screening is more important.

## Method

The authors solve the static Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) using quasiparticle energies from the one-shot G0T0 scheme (a T-matrix approximation) and a static T-matrix kernel, then apply a perturbative dynamical correction within the dynamical Tamm-Dancoff approximation (dTDA) to obtain dynamically-corrected excitation energies. The T-matrix is constructed from the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the particle-particle random-phase approximation (pp-RPA) problem, and the formalism is implemented in the QuAcK electronic structure package using a Hartree-Fock starting point with Gaussian basis sets.

## Key numbers

- For the hydrogen molecule (H2) with the cc-pVTZ basis, BSE@G0T0 and BSE@G0W0 yield similar excitation energies; the dynamical correction for the lowest singlet state (B 1Σu+) improves results at small internuclear distances for both schemes, with improvement only visible at the T-matrix level for larger bond lengths.
- For beryllium hydride (BeH2) with the cc-pVDZ basis, dBSE@G0T0 is clearly better than dBSE@G0W0 for the four lowest singlet excited states, while the opposite trend is observed for the four lowest triplet states.
- For water (H2O) at equilibrium geometry with the aug-cc-pVDZ basis, BSE@G0W0 overestimates excitation energies by about 0.1 eV compared to FCI, while BSE@G0T0 systematically underestimates the excitation energies and outperforms CIS, CIS(D), and TDHF for singlet states only.

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix approximation, previously applied to charged excitations in nuclear physics (Bethe 1957), condensed matter (Liebsch 1981), and quantum chemistry (Zhang 2017, Li 2021b), to neutral excitations via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. It contrasts with the well-established GW approximation (Hedin 1965), which sums direct ring diagrams and is suited for weakly-correlated systems, while the T-matrix sums ladder diagrams for strongly-correlated regimes. The results enable future work on combining GT and GW via range separation of the Coulomb operator and on applying the formalism to ground-state correlation energies within the adiabatic connection fluctuation dissipation framework.
