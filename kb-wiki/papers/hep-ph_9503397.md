# On Signals of Quark-Gluon Plasma Freeze-Out

> The time-scales of rehadronization are considered for a baryon-free QGP at RHIC and LHC energies. The non-equilibrium nucleation scenario leads to mechanical instability of the supercooled QGP phase which then may be suddenly converted in a timelike deflagration from the supercooled state to a (super)heated hadronic matter. In a model simulation such a sudden process was indeed possible and satisfied energy and momentum conservation with non-decreasing entropy. It is possible to reach a hadronic state frozen out immediately after the timelike deflagration. If a TD leads to a simultaneous hadronization and freeze-out, the conditions of a pion-flash are satisfied. This rehadronization mechanism is signalled by a reduced difference between the sidewards and outward components of Bose-Einstein correlation functions, in the observation of the free masses of the resonances in the dilepton spectra and in a clean strangeness signal of the QGP.

# On Signals of Quark-Gluon Plasma Freeze-Out

## Key claim

The time-scales of freeze-out estimated from extrapolations of present HBT data at RHIC and LHC energies are short enough that the supercooled QGP may undergo a sudden timelike deflagration directly to a frozen-out hadronic state, bypassing the slow nucleation and reheating process; this mechanism is possible only if the timelike deflagration satisfies energy-momentum conservation with non-decreasing entropy, and it requires that the freeze-out time from HBT data coincides with the time when the system reaches the deepest supercooled state in nucleation calculations.

## Method

The authors compare the freeze-out proper-times estimated from an extrapolation of the scaling relation between HBT radius parameters and charged particle rapidity density at CERN SPS energies to the time-scales of homogeneous nucleation in a baryon-free QGP undergoing Bjorken expansion. They then apply the relativistic Taub adiabat, Rayleigh line, and Poisson adiabat for timelike deflagrations to determine whether a sudden transition from a supercooled QGP phase mixture to a hadronic gas state is thermodynamically allowed, using the bag model equation of state with critical temperature T_c = 169 MeV and varying the effective number of quark flavours and the dimensionality of the expansion.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is first reached at t_c ≈ 3 fm/c for a longitudinally expanding gas of gluons and massless quarks.
- Noticeable nucleation begins when the temperature falls to about 0.95 T_c, and the bottom temperature T_b ≈ 0.8 T_c is reached at t_b ≈ 7 fm/c.
- The completion time for the nucleation scenario is t_com = 50 fm/c, a delay of about 11 fm/c relative to the idealized Maxwell-Boltzmann construction.
- The pressure of the supercooled QGP vanishes at T = 0.98 T_c, and the system reaches T = 0.7–0.9 T_c (120–150 MeV) in the supercooled phase.
- The charged particle rapidity density at midrapidity for central 32S + 238U collisions at CERN SPS is about 133, corresponding to R_L = 4.5 ± 0.5 fm.
- The freeze-out proper-time estimates from HBT data extrapolation are: 6–10–12 fm/c at CERN SPS for Pb+Pb, 8–13–16 fm/c at RHIC for Au+Au, and 11–16–22 fm/c at LHC for Pb+Pb collisions.
- For a d=1 Bjorken expansion starting at t_I = 3.5 fm/c with T_I = 1.6 T_c, the critical temperature is reached at t_c(1) = 14.3 fm/c; for d=3 expansion, t_c(3) = 5.6 fm/c.
- In a model simulation with n_F = 0 and d=3 scaling Bjorken expansion, the new channel for timelike deflagration opens at t = 7.3 fm/c when the entropy ratio first becomes equal to 1.
- The characteristic transition times for quantum mechanical tunneling in the linear sigma model may be as short as 0.5 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the nucleation calculations of Csernai and Kapusta (1992) and the bubble kinetics studies of DeGrand and Kajantie (1984) by showing that the freeze-out times inferred from HBT data at SPS energies are comparable to the time-scales for reaching the deepest supercooled state in the nucleation scenario, making a sudden timelike deflagration possible. The proposed mechanism provides a dynamical basis for the ALCOR model of Biró, Lévai, and Zimányi (1994), which successfully describes strangeness production at CERN SPS energies assuming sudden hadronization, and it offers an explanation for the observed equality of side and out HBT radii at SPS (NA35 and NA44 collaborations), the unchanged resonance masses in dilepton spectra, and the enhanced strange antibaryon production reported by the WA85 collaboration.
