# Nuclear beta decays and CKM unitarity

> Nuclear beta decays between (J^pi,T) = (0^+,1) analog states yield the best value for the Vud element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. Current world data establish the corrected Ft values of 14 separate superallowed transitions to a precision of order 0.1% or better. The validity of the small theoretical correction terms is confirmed by excellent consistency among the 14 Ft values and by recent measurements that compare pairs of mirror superallowed transitions. With consistency established, the results now yield |Vud| = 0.97420(21). This value is consistent with the considerably less precise results obtained from beta decays of the neutron, the pion and T=1/2 mirror nuclei, which are hampered by experimental challenges.

## Key claim

The corrected Ft values from 14 superallowed nuclear beta decays are consistent with one another, confirming the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis and validating the small theoretical correction terms; this consistency justifies extracting |Vud| = 0.97420(21), which dominates the world average for this CKM matrix element.

## Method

The authors performed a critical survey of all half-life, decay-energy, and branching-ratio measurements for 20 superallowed 0+ → 0+ beta decays, incorporating 222 individual measurements from 177 published references, updated through 2017 with more than a dozen new measurements. They applied transition-dependent radiative corrections (δ_R′ and δ_NS) and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections (δ_C) derived from shell-model wave functions tuned to reproduce measured binding energies, charge radii, and isobaric multiplet mass equation coefficients. The corrected Ft values were then averaged to extract the vector coupling constant GV and, from it, |Vud| via the relation |Vud| = GV/GF.

## Key numbers

- |Vud| = 0.97420(21) from nuclear superallowed decays
- K = 8120.2776(9) × 10^(-10) GeV^(-4) s (constant in Ft equation)
- Neutron mean-life: 879.8(8) s from world average, with beam measurements giving 888.1(20) s and bottle measurements giving 879.4(7) s
- |Vud| from neutron decay: 0.9754(13) (average method) or range 0.9700 ≤ |Vud| ≤ 0.9760 (range method)
- |Vud| from T=1/2 mirror nuclei: 0.9727(14)
- |Vud| from pion beta decay: 0.9749(26)
- |Vus| = 0.2243(11) (weighted average of semileptonic and leptonic kaon decay results)
- |Vub| = 0.0039(4) (PDG value)
- CKM unitarity sum: |Vud|^2 + |Vus|^2 + |Vub|^2 = 0.99939(64)

## Context

This work updates the authors' 2015 critical survey (Hardy and Towner, 2015) of superallowed beta decays, incorporating new measurements for 10C, 14O, 38Ca, and 42Sc. The results confirm the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis and validate the nuclear-structure-dependent corrections, with additional confirmation from mirror-pair measurements of 38Ca/38mK (Park et al., 2014; Park et al., 2015) and preliminary results for A=26 and A=34 pairs. The recommended |Vud| value enables a CKM unitarity test at the 0.06% level, though further improvement is limited by the transition-independent radiative correction Δ_RV, which dominates the uncertainty budget and affects all measurement methods equally.
