# Neutron matter based on consistently evolved chiral three-nucleon interactions

> We present the first results for the neutron matter equation of state (EOS) using nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon chiral effective field theory interactions that are consistently evolved in the framework of the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG). The dependence of the EOS on the SRG resolution scale is greatly reduced when induced three-nucleon forces (3NF) are included and the residual variation, which in part is from missing induced four-body interactions, is comparable to estimated many-body perturbation theory truncation errors. The relative growth with decreasing resolution of the 3NF contributions to the energy per neutron is of natural size, but it accelerates at the lowest resolutions where strong renormalization of the long-range 3NF matrix elements is also observed.

## Key claim

The residual resolution-scale dependence of the neutron matter equation of state after including consistently evolved three-nucleon forces is comparable to estimated many-body perturbation theory truncation errors, but the authors cannot make a definitive statement about the size of induced four-body contributions, and the small increase in energy below λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ might be attributed to induced four-body forces.

## Method

The authors solved Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) flow equations simultaneously for nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) chiral effective field theory interactions in a continuous plane-wave momentum basis, using a generator η_s = [T_rel, H_s]. The neutron matter equation of state was then calculated using many-body perturbation theory, with NN contributions resummed in the ladder approximation and 3N contributions computed in Hartree-Fock approximation, for resolution scales λ ranging from infinity down to 1.5 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- The maximal energy variation at saturation density (n_s = 0.16 fm⁻³) for the 3N-induced calculations was about 390 keV (left panel) and 470 keV (right panel), and for the 3N-full calculations about 650 keV (left panel) and 450 keV (right panel), compared to a total variation of about 3.6 MeV (left panel) and 4.2 MeV (right panel) when 3NF were completely neglected.
- The 3N-full energy per neutron at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ at saturation density was about 0.5–1.5 MeV higher than found in calculations with the same initial NN interaction but with unevolved N²LO 3NF.
- The exact Hartree-Fock energy through total three-body angular momentum J = 9/2 was converged within 0.4 percent at nuclear saturation density.
- The initial 3NF used low-energy constants c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹ and c₃ = -3.2 GeV⁻¹ (for the N³LO 500 MeV potential) or c₃ = -3.4 GeV⁻¹ (for the N²LO 450/500 MeV potential).

## Context

This work extends prior neutron matter calculations based on chiral EFT interactions (Hebeler 2010) by performing the first consistent SRG evolution of both NN and 3N forces, whereas previous calculations had not evolved three-nucleon interactions consistently. The results show that RG evolution to lower resolution scales improves the convergence of many-body perturbation theory, enabling controlled theoretical error estimates for neutron matter. This enables future calculations of both neutron and nuclear matter with higher-order diagrams including 3NF, and will allow inclusion of 3NF at N³LO (Bernard 2008, 2011) once initial matrix elements in partial wave representation are available.
