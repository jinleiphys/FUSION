# Solvable models for the gamma deformation having X(5) as limiting symmetry. Removing some drawbacks of the existent descriptions

> Two solvable Hamiltonians for describing the dynamic gamma deformation, are proposed. The limiting case of each of them is the X(5) Hamiltonian. Analytical solutions for both energies and wave functions, which are periodic in $γ$, are presented in terms of spheroidal and Mathieu functions, respectively. Moreover, the gamma depending factors of the transition operator can be treated.

## Key claim

Two solvable Hamiltonians for the dynamic gamma deformation are proposed, each having the X(5) Hamiltonian as a limiting case, but unlike previous approximations they produce wave functions that are periodic in gamma and Hamiltonians that are Hermitian with respect to the correct liquid-drop measure.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian and construct two exactly solvable models for the gamma degree of freedom. The first model uses a potential of the form \(U = u_1 \cos 3\gamma + u_2 \cos^2 3\gamma\) plus a singular term \(9K^2/(4\sin^2 3\gamma)\); after a change of variable \(x = \cos 3\gamma\), the eigenvalue equation reduces to the spheroidal oblate differential equation. The second model uses a potential with an additional \(A/(4\sin^2\gamma)\) term and, for small gamma, approximates the equation to the Mathieu equation, whose periodic solutions are the elliptic cosine and sine functions.

## Key numbers

* For the spheroidal model with \(u_1 = 0\), the energy is related to the spheroidal eigenvalue by \(E = 9\lambda_{nm}\), with \(m = K/2\) and \(c^2 = u_2/9\).
* For small \(|c|\), the asymptotic expansion for the spheroidal energies is \(E_{nm} \approx 9n(n+1) - \frac{2[n(n+1)+m^2-1]}{(2n-1)(2n+3)}u_2 + \dots\).
* For the Mathieu model, the energy spectrum is given by \(E^\pm_n = c^\pm_n - \frac{10}{9}q\), where \(c^\pm_n\) are the characteristic values of the Mathieu equation and \(q = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{9}{8}u_1 + \frac{9}{4}u_2\).
* For small \(|q|\), the Mathieu energies are \(E^+_0 \approx u - \frac{10}{9}q - \frac{q^2}{2}\) and \(E^+_n \approx E^-_n \approx u + n^2 - \frac{10}{9}q + \frac{q^2}{2(n^2-1)}\) for \(n \ge 3\).
* For large \(|q|\), the Mathieu energies behave as \(E^+_n \approx E^-_{n+1} \approx -\frac{28}{9}q + 2(2n+1)\sqrt{q} - \frac{1}{4}(2n^2+2n+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the critical-point symmetry approach of Iachello (2001), who introduced the X(5) symmetry for first-order phase transitions between spherical and axially deformed nuclei. The authors explicitly address a drawback of the standard X(5) treatment: expanding the gamma potential around \(\gamma=0\) yields wave functions that are not periodic and a Hamiltonian that is not Hermitian with respect to the liquid-drop measure \(|\sin 3\gamma| d\gamma\). The proposed spheroidal and Mathieu solutions cure these problems while retaining X(5) as a limiting symmetry, enabling future calculations of gamma-dependent transition operators with proper periodic wave functions.
