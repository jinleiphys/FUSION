# Nonlinear Pulse Dynamics in a Magnetized and Nonextensive Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We investigate the propagation of nonlinear energy density waves in a nonextensive quark-gluon plasma under the influence of a magnetic field using the reductive perturbation technique. For a nonextensive MIT bag equation of state, we obtain the governing equation for the first order perturbation of the energy density. We observe that an increase in the strength of the magnetic field results in the localization of the waves.

## Key claim
The authors claim that increasing the strength of a uniform magnetic field localizes nonlinear energy-density waves in a hot quark-gluon plasma described by nonextensive (Tsallis) statistics, but this result is obtained under the lowest Landau level approximation and for a one-dimensional, ideal (non-dissipative) magnetohydrodynamic model.

## Method
The authors use the reductive perturbation technique on one-dimensional Euler's equation with a constant magnetic field, together with the entropy continuity equation, to derive a governing equation for the first-order perturbation of the energy density. They evaluate the required thermodynamic variables (energy density, pressure, speed of sound) from a nonextensive MIT bag equation of state, computing the fermionic contributions in the lowest Landau level approximation using Mellin-Barnes contour integration. The resulting nonlinear wave equation is solved numerically for an initial sech-squared perturbation profile.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strengths considered: \(eB = m_\pi^2\), \(eB = 4.4\,m_\pi^2\), \(eB = 8.3\,m_\pi^2\), and \(eB = 11.5\,m_\pi^2\).
- Background temperature used in most simulations: \(T = 200\) MeV.
- Nonextensivity parameter values used: \(q = 1.10\) and \(q = 1.20\).
- Initial perturbation widths: \(B = 0.5\) fm, \(B = 2\) fm, and \(B = 5\) fm.
- Initial perturbation amplitudes: \(A = 1\), \(A = 2\), and \(A = 5\).
- At \(T = 200\) MeV and \(q = 1.20\), waves regain stability at \(eB = 8.3\,m_\pi^2\); at the same temperature with \(q = 1.10\), stability is regained at \(eB = 11.5\,m_\pi^2\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of nonlinear perturbations in a nonextensive QGP (Bhattacharyya 2020, Sarwar 2021) by adding a uniform magnetic field, and generalizes earlier work on wave propagation in a magnetized cold QGP (Fogaca 2014) to a hot, nonextensive medium. The results suggest that a sufficiently strong magnetic field can stabilize energy-density perturbations within the QGP lifetime, which may be relevant for understanding jet-induced wave propagation in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC. The analytical expressions for thermodynamic variables of a nonextensive quantum gas in a magnetic field are presented as new results that could be used in other studies of magnetized nonextensive systems.
