# An Overview of the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> We report on the recent results of the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM). The model contains a spin independent three-quark interaction which is inspired by Lattice QCD calculations and reproduces the average energy values of the SU(6) multiplets. The splittings are obtained with a SU(6)-breaking interaction, which can include also an isospin dependent term. The model has been used for predictions concerning the electromagnetic transition form factors giving a good description of the medium $Q^2$-behaviour. In particular the calculated helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance agrees very well with the recent CLAS data. Furthermore, we have shown for the first time that the decreasing of the ratio of the elastic form factors of the proton is due to relativistic effects. Finally, the elastic nucleon form factors have been calculated using a relativistic version of the hCQM and a relativistic quark current.

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM), using a spin-independent three-quark interaction inspired by Lattice QCD and a perturbative SU(6)-breaking hyperfine interaction, reproduces the average energies of SU(6) multiplets and provides a good description of electromagnetic transition form factors at medium Q², with the calculated helicity amplitude A₁/₂ for the S₁₁(1535) resonance agreeing very well with recent CLAS data; however, discrepancies remain at low Q², which the authors attribute to the lack of explicit quark-antiquark configurations rather than to relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a hypercentral potential V(x) = -τ/x + αx, where x is the hyperradius of the three-quark system, and added a standard hyperfine interaction H_hyp to produce SU(6) multiplet splittings. They calculated electromagnetic transition amplitudes A₁/₂ and A₃/₂ using a transverse electromagnetic interaction operator with pointlike quarks, and computed elastic nucleon form factors using a relativistic version of the hCQM with a relativistic quark current expanded to lowest order in quark momenta, including boosts to the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The potential parameters fitted to the baryon spectrum are α = 1.16 fm⁻² and τ = 4.59 (dimensionless).
- The proton r.m.s. radius corresponding to these parameters is 0.48 fm.
- In the isospin-dependent extension, the hyperfine interaction contributes only about 35% of the N-Δ mass difference, while the spin-isospin term contributes 50% and the isospin term contributes 15%.
- The ratio between the electric and magnetic proton form factors deviates from unity by almost 50%, in qualitative agreement with TJNAF data.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) approach previously developed by the same group (Giannini et al., various years) and contrasts with the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur and Karl, 1978) and the algebraic model (Bijl et al., 1993). The model enables parameter-free predictions for electromagnetic transition amplitudes and elastic nucleon form factors, with the relativistic version showing for the first time that the decreasing ratio of proton elastic form factors is due to relativistic effects, in qualitative agreement with recent JLab data (Jones et al., 2000; Gayou et al., 2002).
