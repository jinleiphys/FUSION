# Thermal description of hadron production in e+e- collisions

> This thesis gives a comprehensive analysis of hadron production in $e^{+}e^{-}$ collisions at different center of mass energies in the framework of the Statistical Model of the hadron resonance gas. The model used for the analysis is formulated in the canonical ensemble with exact conservation of five quantum numbers. The corresponding canonical partition function in quantum statistics is derived and the canonical factor as the striking feature of the canonical framework will be investigated in detail. The parameters of the underlying model were determined using a fit to the average multiplicities of the latest measurements at $\sqrt{s}$=10, 29-35, 91 and 130-200 GeV. The measurements are compared to the pure thermal production like in heavy ion collisions as well as to the production in a second scenario accounting for hard collisions and the resulting enhancement of heavy quark. The results demonstrate that, within the accuracy of the experiments, none of the data sets is satisfactorily described with this approach, calling into question the notion that particle production in $e^{+}e^{-}$ collisions is thermal in origin. Nevertheless it is observed that the charm and bottom particle yields produced in hard collisions can be explained by large canonical enhancements of such particles in heavy jets, almost independent of the model parameters.

## Key claim

The thesis tests whether hadron production in e+e- collisions can be described by a thermal model, finding that within the high precision of the experiments (a few percent), none of the data sets at sqrt(s)=10, 29-35, 91, and 130-200 GeV is satisfactorily described by this approach, calling into question the thermal origin of particle production in these collisions. However, charm and bottom particle yields from hard collisions can be explained by large canonical enhancements in heavy jets, almost independent of model parameters.

## Method

The author formulated a Statistical Model of the hadron resonance gas in the canonical ensemble with exact conservation of five quantum numbers (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness, charmness, and bottomness), using quantum statistics rather than Boltzmann statistics. A C++ program was developed to simulate particle production, and model parameters were determined via chi-squared fits to average multiplicity measurements from the Particle Data Group at sqrt(s)=10, 29-35, 91, and 130-200 GeV. Two scenarios were compared: pure thermal production (as in heavy ion collisions) and a second scenario accounting for hard collisions and the resulting enhancement of heavy quark production.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures deduced from earlier thermal model fits to e+e- collisions were near T=160 MeV, with volume increasing with energy and strangeness under-saturated
- The strong coupling constant at sqrt(s)=91 GeV is 0.12 +/- 0.0031 (Bethke 2004)
- The string energy density kappa is approximately 1 GeV/fm
- The tunneling probability for quark-antiquark pair production in the string model scales as exp(-pi * m_perp^2 / kappa), giving quark flavor ratios u : d : s : c approximately 1 : 1 : 0.3 : 10^{-11}
- The hadronic width of the Z0 is 1.67 GeV, with relative branching ratios Gamma_uubar/Gamma_hadron approximately 17% and Gamma_ddbar/Gamma_hadron approximately 22%

## Context

This work extends earlier thermal model analyses of e+e- collisions by Becattini (1995, 1996, 1997) at 14-43 GeV and 91 GeV, which found good descriptions with local quantum number conservation, and contrasts with the successful thermal description of heavy ion collisions from AGS to RHIC energies (Andronic 2005). The results question whether the statistical behavior observed in heavy ion collisions is a unique feature of nucleus-nucleus collisions or also applicable to elementary collisions, and suggest that the thermal-like features may instead be a generic fingerprint of hadronization (Stock 1999, Heinz) or a feature of the excited QCD vacuum (Castorina).
