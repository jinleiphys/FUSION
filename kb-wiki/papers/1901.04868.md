# Quantum Monte Carlo Methods in Nuclear Physics: Recent Advances

> In recent years, the combination of precise quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods with realistic nuclear interactions and consistent electroweak currents, in particular those constructed within effective field theories (EFTs), has lead to new insights in light and medium-mass nuclei, neutron matter, and electroweak reactions. This compelling new body of work has been made possible both by advances in QMC methods for nuclear physics, which push the bounds of applicability to heavier nuclei and to asymmetric nuclear matter and by the development of local chiral EFT interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order and minimally nonlocal interactions including $Δ$ degrees of freedom. In this review, we discuss these recent developments and give an overview of the exciting results for nuclei, neutron matter and neutron stars, and electroweak reactions.

## Key claim

Recent advances in quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods, combined with realistic nuclear interactions from effective field theories (EFTs) and consistent electroweak currents, have enabled new insights into light and medium-mass nuclei, neutron matter, and electroweak reactions, but local chiral interactions suffer from regulator artifacts that must be carefully considered when interpreting results.

## Method

The authors review the combination of Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) methods with nuclear interactions, including phenomenological potentials (Argonne \(v_{18}\), Urbana IX, Illinois 7) and local chiral EFT interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO). They discuss how GFMC sums over all spin/isospin states with exponential scaling in nucleon number \(A\), limiting it to \(A \leq 12\), while AFDMC uses Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations for polynomial scaling, enabling calculations for larger systems such as \(^{16}\)O and neutron matter. The review also covers the construction of consistent electroweak currents within both the Standard Nuclear Physics Approach (SNPA) and chiral EFT.

## Key numbers

- GFMC has been used to successfully predict spectra and electroweak processes of nuclei with \(A \leq 12\) with percent-level accuracy.
- The Argonne \(v_{18}\) (AV18) potential is expressed in terms of 18 operators and was fit to the Nijmegen NN scattering database with \(\chi^2/{\rm datum}\simeq 1\).
- The Urbana IX (UIX) 3N interaction is fit to reproduce the ground-state energies of \(^{3}\)H and \(^{4}\)He and the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter.
- The Illinois 7 (IL7) interaction was fit to the low-lying spectra of nuclei in the mass range \(A = 3\)–10.
- The nucleon axial-vector coupling constant is taken to be \(g_A = 1.2723\) and the axial mass \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV.
- The Weinberg angle is \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.23122\).
- Leading-order results with pionless EFT indicate that for \(m_\pi = 805\) MeV and \(m_\pi = 510\) MeV, \(^{16}\)O is not stable against breakup into four \(^{4}\)He nuclei.
- Chiral interactions up to fifth order reproduce pp and np scattering data from the Granada-2013 database with \(\chi^2/{\rm datum}\sim 1\).

## Context

This review extends the body of work on QMC methods in nuclear physics previously summarized by Carlson (2014) and covers developments in local chiral EFT interactions by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) and Piarulli (2014, 2016). It enables future applications of QMC methods to heavier nuclei and neutron star matter using systematically improvable interactions with quantified theoretical uncertainties, while highlighting the need to address regulator artifacts in local chiral interactions, particularly in the three-nucleon sector as discussed by Tews (2015) and Dyhdalo (2016).
