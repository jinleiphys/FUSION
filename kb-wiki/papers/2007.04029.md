# Particle polarization, spin tensor and the Wigner distribution in relativistic systems

> Particle spin polarization is known to be linked both to rotation (angular momentum) and magnetization of a many particle system. However, in the most common formulation of relativistic kinetic theory, the spin degrees of freedom appear only as degeneracy factors multiplying phase-space distributions. Thus, it is important to develop theoretical tools that allow to make predictions regarding the spin polarization of particles, which can be directly confronted with experimental data. Herein, we discuss a link between the relativistic spin tensor and particle spin polarization, and elucidate the connections between the Wigner function and average polarization. Our results may be useful for theoretical interpretation of heavy-ion data on spin polarization of the produced hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a formal connection between the relativistic spin tensor (derived from Noether's theorem for Lorentz invariance) and particle spin polarization, showing that the volume integral of the canonical spin tensor is strongly related to the polarization state of fundamental field excitations, but this relationship is complicated by time-dependent mixed terms involving creation and destruction operators that have no clear particle-antiparticle interpretation.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical spin tensor for a free Dirac field, derived from Noether's theorem applied to Lorentz transformations, and compute its volume integral in terms of creation and destruction operator expectation values. They then evaluate the matrix elements of the spin operator using explicit forms of the massive Dirac eigenspinors in the Dirac representation, expressing the results in terms of two-component spinors and Pauli matrices. The analysis shows that the volume integral of the canonical spin tensor contains both diagonal terms (closely related to particle polarization) and off-diagonal mixed terms involving particle-antiparticle pairs that introduce explicit time dependence.

## Key numbers

- The canonical spin tensor for the Dirac field is given by ${\cal S}_c^{\lambda , \mu\nu}(x) = \frac{i}{8}\bar\Psi(x) \left\{ \gamma^\lambda,\left[\gamma^\mu,\gamma^\nu \right] \right\}\Psi(x)$, which is totally antisymmetric under exchange of any indices
- The matrix elements for the spin operator in the i'th direction are: $U^\dagger_r({\bf p}) \Sigma_i U_s({\bf p}) = 2m \;\phi_r\sigma_i\phi_s +\frac{2p_i}{E_{\bf p} +m} \phi_r({\bf p}\cdot{\boldsymbol \sigma})\phi_s$ for particles, and $U^\dagger_r({\bf p}) \Sigma_i V_s(-{\bf p}) = -2 i \sum_{j,k} \varepsilon_{ijk} \, p_j\, \phi_r \sigma_k \chi_s$ for the mixed particle-antiparticle terms
- The degeneracy factor in standard kinetic theory is $g_S=2S+1$, which for spin-1/2 particles equals 2

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic kinetic theory framework (which treats spin only as a degeneracy factor multiplying phase-space distributions) to include polarization degrees of freedom, addressing limitations noted in the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription (Cooper 1974). The paper builds on the pseudo-gauge transformation formalism of Hehl (1976) and the Belinfante symmetrization procedure, and is complementary to recent reviews by Wang (2017), Huang (2020), and Becattini (2020). The results provide theoretical tools needed to interpret heavy-ion collision data on hadron spin polarization, where the standard approach using Eqs. (1) for current and stress-energy tensor cannot distinguish between different spin states.
