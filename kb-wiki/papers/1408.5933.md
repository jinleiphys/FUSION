# A relativistic, model-independent, three-particle quantization condition

> We present a generalization of Luescher's relation between the finite-volume spectrum and scattering amplitudes to the case of three particles. We consider a relativistic scalar field theory in which the couplings are arbitrary aside from a Z2 symmetry that removes vertices with an odd number of particles. The theory is assumed to have two-particle phase shifts that are bounded by π/2 in the regime of elastic scattering. We determine the spectrum of the finite-volume theory from the poles in the odd-particle-number finite-volume correlator, which we analyze to all orders in perturbation theory. We show that it depends on the infinite-volume two-to-two K-matrix as well as a nonstandard infinite-volume three-to-three K-matrix. A key feature of our result is the need to subtract physical singularities in the three-to-three amplitude and thus deal with a divergence-free quantity. This allows our initial, formal result to be truncated to a finite dimensional determinant equation. At present, the relation of the three-to-three K-matrix to the corresponding scattering amplitude is not known, although previous results in the non-relativistic limit suggest that such a relation exists.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic, model-independent quantization condition relating the finite-volume energy spectrum of a scalar field theory to infinite-volume two-to-two and three-to-three K-matrices, under the assumption that two-particle phase shifts are bounded by π/2 in the elastic regime and that the theory has a Z₂ symmetry removing odd-particle vertices.

## Method

The authors analyze the finite-volume correlator for odd particle number to all orders in perturbation theory, using a sum-minus-integral identity to convert finite-volume loop sums into infinite-volume integrals plus a finite-volume function F₃. They subtract physical singularities from the three-to-three amplitude to obtain a divergence-free three-to-three K-matrix, then sum the resulting geometric series to express the finite-volume correlator in closed form, from which the quantization condition follows as a determinant equation.

## Key numbers

* The analysis applies for center-of-mass energies in the range m < E* < 5m, where m is the particle mass.
* Two-particle phase shifts are assumed bounded by π/2 in the elastic scattering regime.
* The finite-volume corrections are exponentially suppressed as exp(-mL) for single-particle states, with L the spatial extent of the finite volume.
* The quantization condition involves the function F₃, which contains a phase-space factor ρ that is diagonal in angular momentum and defined via the PV pole prescription.

## Context

This work generalizes Lüscher's relation (Lüscher, 1986) from two-particle to three-particle systems in relativistic quantum field theory, extending earlier non-relativistic three-particle quantization conditions (Beane, 2007; Tan, 2007). The result enables lattice QCD calculations of three-particle scattering amplitudes, though the relation between the three-to-three K-matrix and the physical scattering amplitude remains unknown, with previous non-relativistic results suggesting such a relation exists.
