# $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions and $p_T$ broadening

> We have analysed the NA50 data, on the centrality dependence of $p_T$ broadening of $J/ψ$'s, in Pb+Pb collisions, at the CERN-SPS. The data were analysed in a QCD based model, where $J/ψ$'s are suppressed in 'nuclear' medium. Without any free parameter, the model could explain the NA50 $p_T$ broadening data. The data were also analysed in a QGP based threshold model, where $J/ψ$ suppression is 100% above a critical density. The QGP based model could not explain the NA50 $p_T$ broadening data. We have also predicted the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression and $p_T$ broadening at RHIC energy. Both the models, the QGP based threshold model and the QCD based nuclear absorption model, predict $p_T$ broadening very close to each other.

## Key claim

The QCD-based "unconventional" nuclear absorption model, without any free parameters, can explain the NA50 data on the centrality dependence of \(p_T\) broadening of \(J/\psi\) mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN-SPS, while the QGP-based threshold model fails to fit the same data; however, both models predict very similar \(p_T\) broadening at RHIC energy, suggesting that \(p_T\) broadening may not be a good signal for deconfinement.

## Method

The authors analyzed NA50 data on the transverse energy (\(E_T\)) dependence of the mean squared transverse momentum \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle\) of \(J/\psi\) mesons in 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions. They used a QCD-based nuclear absorption model where \(J/\psi\) suppression arises from \(c\bar{c}\) pairs gaining relative momentum in the nuclear medium, and a QGP-based threshold model where 100% suppression occurs above a critical density \(n_c = 3.7\) fm\(^{-2}\). The average number of gluon-nucleon subcollisions \(N_{AB}(b)\) was computed in a Glauber model, and the effect of \(E_T\) fluctuations at fixed impact parameter was included via a Gaussian \(E_T\)-\(b\) correlation function.

## Key numbers

* \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle_{NN} = 1.15\) GeV\(^2\) for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS (158 A GeV), obtained from a logarithmic fit to existing data up to 60 GeV.
* \(\sigma_{gN}\delta_0 = 0.442 \pm 0.056\) (gluon-nucleon cross section times average broadening per collision), extracted from a fit to NA38 \(p_T\) broadening data in S+U collisions at 200 GeV/c.
* At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV), \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle_{NN} = 2.45\) GeV\(^2\) (extrapolated, with caution noted).
* The QGP-based threshold model predicts 4% less \(p_T\) broadening at large \(E_T\) compared to the nuclear absorption model, and fails to reproduce the increasing trend beyond \(E_T = 100\) GeV.
* The nuclear absorption model reproduces the NA50 data within 2%.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of \(J/\psi\) suppression in Pb+Pb collisions by the NA50 collaboration, which had previously been interpreted in terms of successive melting of charmonium states in QGP (Matsui and Satz, 1986) but also explained by models without QGP (Blaizot, Dinh, and Ollitrault, 2000; Chaudhuri, 2001; Capella, Ferreiro, and Kaidalov, 2000). The paper directly addresses the suggestion by Kharzeev, Nardi, and Satz (1997) that decreasing \(p_T\) broadening at large \(E_T\) could distinguish QGP from nuclear matter, showing that the "unconventional" nuclear absorption model also produces such a decrease. The analysis enables future experimental tests at RHIC, where both models predict nearly identical \(p_T\) broadening, indicating that this observable may not distinguish deconfinement.
