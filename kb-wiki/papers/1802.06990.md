# Multiplicity distribution and normalized moments in p$-$p collisions at LHC in forward rapidity using Weibull model

> The measured charged particle multiplicity distribution in the forward rapidity region of the p$-$p collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 0.9 TeV, 7 TeV and 8 TeV at the LHC have been described using the Weibull distribution function. The higher order (up to $5^{th}$ order ) normalized moments and the factorial moments are also calculated using the extracted parameters. The multiplicity distributions in forward region are observed to be well described by the Weibull regularity and the higher order moment calculations confirm the violation of KNO scaling that has been observed at mid-rapidity and lower energies. The Weibull parameters and moments for p$-$p collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV are also estimated for the forward region.

## Key claim

The Weibull distribution function describes the measured charged-particle multiplicity distributions in the forward rapidity region of p-p collisions at LHC energies (0.9, 7, and 8 TeV), and the extracted higher-order moments confirm the violation of KNO scaling that had previously been observed only at mid-rapidity and lower energies.

## Method

The authors fitted the ALICE experimental data for charged-particle multiplicity distributions in five pseudorapidity intervals (from |eta| < 2.0 to -3.4 < eta < 5.0) for two event classes (NSD and INEL>0) using a two-parameter Weibull distribution. They extracted the shape parameter k and scale parameter lambda from the fits, then used these parameters to calculate normalized raw moments C_n and normalized factorial moments F_n up to fifth order. The energy dependence of k and lambda was parameterized by a power law to extrapolate predictions for 13 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For NSD events at 0.9 TeV, |eta| < 2.0: k = 1.265 +/- 0.007, lambda = 17.54 +/- 0.091, chi^2/ndf = 1.56
- For NSD events at 7 TeV, |eta| < 2.0: k = 1.068 +/- 0.007, lambda = 25.72 +/- 0.224, chi^2/ndf = 1.37
- For NSD events at 8 TeV, |eta| < 2.0: k = 1.03 +/- 0.007, lambda = 27.93 +/- 0.23, chi^2/ndf = 1.03
- For NSD events at 8 TeV, -3.4 < eta < 5.0: k = 1.12 +/- 0.013, lambda = 52.93 +/- 0.68, chi^2/ndf = 0.46
- For INEL>0 events at 8 TeV, -3.4 < eta < 5.0: k = 1.15 +/- 0.02, lambda = 55.15 +/- 1.1, chi^2/ndf = 0.42
- Predicted for NSD at 13 TeV, |eta| < 2.0: k = 0.987, lambda = 30.447, C_2 = 2.026, C_3 = 6.201, C_4 = 25.411, C_5 = 130.597
- Predicted for NSD at 13 TeV, -3.4 < eta < 5.0: k = 1.08, lambda = 57.814, C_2 = 1.859, C_3 = 5.318, C_4 = 19.471, C_5 = 88.032

## Context

This work extends the Weibull model description of multiplicity distributions, previously applied to central rapidity data by the same authors (Nayak and Dash, earlier works), to the forward rapidity region measured by the ALICE experiment at the LHC. The results confirm the violation of KNO scaling in forward rapidity, consistent with earlier observations at mid-rapidity by CMS and ALICE, and provide predictions for 13 TeV p-p collisions that can be tested by future LHC measurements. The study enables further constraints on QCD-inspired Monte Carlo event generators such as PYTHIA and PHOJET, which have been found to underestimate the forward multiplicity data.
