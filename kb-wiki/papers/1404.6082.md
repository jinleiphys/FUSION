# Unraveling models of CP violation through electric dipole moments of light nuclei

> We show that the proposed measurements of the electric dipole moments of light nuclei in storage rings would put strong constraints on models of flavor-diagonal CP violation. Our analysis is exemplified by a comparison of the Standard Model including the QCD theta term, the minimal left-right symmetric model, a specific version of the so-called aligned two-Higgs doublet model, and briefly the minimal supersymmetric extension of the Standard Model. By using effective field theory techniques we demonstrate to what extend measurements of the electric dipole moments of the nucleons, the deuteron, and helion could discriminate between these scenarios. We discuss how measurements of electric dipole moments of other systems relate to the light-nuclear measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that measurements of electric dipole moments (EDMs) of light nuclei (deuteron, helion, triton) in storage rings can discriminate between different models of flavor-diagonal CP violation, but only if multiple nuclear EDM measurements are combined, nucleon EDMs alone are insufficient to disentangle the scenarios.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory (χPT) extended to include CP-violating operators up to dimension six, calculating the EDMs of nucleons, the deuteron, and helion from four distinct CP-violation scenarios: the QCD θ term, the minimal left-right symmetric model (mLRSM), the aligned two-Higgs-doublet model (a2HDM), and briefly the minimal supersymmetric extension of the Standard Model (MSSM). For each scenario, they derive the low-energy CP-odd Lagrangian at the quark-gluon level, evolve it to the chiral scale (~1 GeV), and then compute the resulting hadronic and nuclear EDMs using χPT with nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- The proposed storage-ring experiments aim to measure proton and deuteron EDMs with a precision of 10⁻²⁹ e·cm, three orders of magnitude better than the current neutron EDM limit.
- The neutron EDM limit from Baker (2006) constrains the QCD θ parameter to θ̅ ≲ 10⁻¹⁰.
- In the mLRSM, the bound from the neutron EDM gives v² Im(V_L^{ud*} Ξ₁(M_W)) ≤ 8 × 10⁻⁵.
- The right-handed W boson mass in the mLRSM is constrained to M_{W_R} ≥ 3.1 TeV from K- and B-meson mixing.
- The electroweak symmetry breaking scale is v ≃ 246 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies (Pospelov and de Vries, 2011; de Vries et al., 2011; Bsaisou et al., 2013) that analyzed individual CP-violating operators in isolation, by instead studying realistic scenarios where multiple operators contribute simultaneously. The paper shows that measurements of the deuteron and helion EDMs provide complementary information because the deuteron acts as an isospin filter favoring isospin-breaking interactions, while the helion allows both isospin-conserving and isospin-breaking contributions. This enables future storage-ring experiments to discriminate between the QCD θ term, left-right symmetric models, and two-Higgs-doublet models.
