# Overview of Dynamic Models for gamma N -> Delta

> We present an overview of dynamic and related models for pion electroproduction on the nucleon due to Delta(1232) excitation. The electromagnetic multipoles and their ratios obtained from these models and from effective field theories are compared, and the existing contradictory results for the pion cloud effects are discussed.

## Key claim

Dynamic models (DMs) and effective field theories (EFTs) for pion electroproduction on the nucleon via the Delta(1232) resonance produce complementary results, but the splitting between short-distance and long-distance (pion cloud) contributions remains non-unique because it depends on the renormalization scale and on model-dependent choices such as the cutoff for loop integrals and the treatment of off-shell effects.

## Method

The authors compare several dynamic models (Sato-Lee, Dubna-Mainz-Taipei), phenomenological models (MAID, SAID, Yerevan-JLab), and effective field theories (Pascalutsa-Vanderhaeghen, Gail-Hemmert) for the reaction gamma* N -> pi N. The dynamic models solve Bethe-Salpeter or Lippmann-Schwinger equations with a quasi-potential for non-resonant background and bare transition operators for the Delta resonance, yielding unitary T-matrices. The electromagnetic multipoles M_{1+}, E_{1+}, and S_{1+} are extracted at the K-matrix pole and compared across models, with particular attention to the ratios R_EM = E_{1+}/M_{1+} and R_SM = S_{1+}/M_{1+}.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 0, the imaginary part of M_{1+} at the K-matrix pole ranges from 36.0 (MAID) to 37.9 (Sato-Lee and dispersion relations) in units of 10^{-3}/m_{pi+}.
- At Q^2 = 0, R_EM = E_{1+}/M_{1+} at the K-matrix pole ranges from -1.3% (SAID, SP06 solution, p pi^0 kinematics) to -2.7% (Sato-Lee).
- At Q^2 = 0.2 GeV^2, R_EM ranges from -0.9% (SAID) to -3.2% (Sato-Lee).
- At Q^2 = 0, R_SM = S_{1+}/M_{1+} ranges from -2.3% (Sato-Lee) to -6.6% (MAID).
- The Delta(1232) pole position is given as W_R approximately (1210 - 50i) MeV; the Particle Data Group lists M_Delta = (1232 +/- 1) MeV.
- The experimental ratio R_EM is approximately -2.5% at the real photon point.

## Context

This overview extends the comparison of dynamic models for pion electroproduction initiated by Sato and Lee (1996, 2001) and the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei collaboration (Kamalov et al., 1999, 2001), and contrasts them with effective field theories by Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen (2005) and Gail and Hemmert (2005). The work enables systematic comparison of Delta(1232) transition form factors extracted from different theoretical frameworks, and highlights that the pion cloud contribution to the E2/M1 ratio remains controversial due to model-dependent separation of short- and long-distance physics. The authors note that future experiments on radiative pion photoproduction at MAMI aim to determine the magnetic dipole moment of the Delta(1232).
