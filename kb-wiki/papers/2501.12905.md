# The impact of hyperons on neutron star mergers: gravitational waves, mass ejection and black hole formation

> We study the influence of hyperons in binary neutron star (NS) mergers considering a total of 14 temperature dependent equations of state (EoSs) models which include hyperonic degrees of freedom and partly delta resonances. Thermally produced hyperons induce a higher heat capacity and a lower thermal index, i.e. a reduced thermal pressure for a given amount of thermal energy, compared to purely nucleonic models. We run a large set of relativistic hydrodynamics simulations of NS mergers to explore the impact on observables of these events. In symmetric binaries, we describe a characteristic increase of the dominant postmerger gravitational-wave (GW) frequency by a few per cent, which is specifically linked to the occurrence of hyperons and can thus be potentially used as a discriminator between purely nucleonic and hyperonic systems. We corroborate that this effect occurs similarly for asymmetric binaries and becomes more prominent with increasing total binary mass. Hyperonic models tend to stick out in relations between the dominant postmerger GW frequency and the tidal deformability of massive stars providing a signature to identify the presence of hyperons. Distinct secondary postmerger GW spectral features are differently affected by the presence of hyperons, in the sense that one feature exhibits a characteristic frequency shift due to the specific thermal properties of hyperonic EoSs while the other does not. The dynamical mass ejection of mergers is tentatively enhanced for hyperonic models in comparison to nucleonic EoSs which yield roughly the same stellar properties of cold NSs. This may serve useful to identify exotic degrees of freedom in these systems by kilonova observations. Also, for hyperonic EoSs the threshold mass for prompt black hole formation is reduced by about 0.05 $M_{\odot}$ in comparison to nucleonic systems with the same stellar parameters of cold NSs.

## Key claim

Thermally produced hyperons in neutron star merger remnants induce a characteristic increase of the dominant postmerger gravitational-wave frequency by a few percent, which can potentially discriminate hyperonic from purely nucleonic systems, but this signature relies on the cold part of the equation of state being independently known to provide a reference value.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic hydrodynamics simulations of binary neutron star mergers using 14 temperature-dependent equations of state that include hyperonic degrees of freedom and, in some models, delta resonances. They compared the merger outcomes to simulations using purely nucleonic equations of state, analyzing gravitational-wave spectra, dynamical mass ejection, and the threshold mass for prompt black hole formation across symmetric and asymmetric binaries with varying total masses.

## Key numbers

- Dominant postmerger gravitational-wave frequency shift of up to 150 Hz for hyperonic models compared to purely nucleonic models with the same cold equation of state
- Threshold mass for prompt black hole formation reduced by about 0.05 solar masses for hyperonic equations of state compared to nucleonic systems with the same cold stellar parameters
- For equal-mass binaries of total mass 2.8 solar masses, dynamical mass ejection values range from 3.0 to 33.5 x 10^{-3} solar masses across hyperonic models (e.g., SFHOY: 33.5 x 10^{-3} solar masses; DNS: 3.0 x 10^{-3} solar masses)
- For the same 2.8 solar mass equal-mass binaries, nucleonic models show dynamical mass ejection from 1.7 to 26.2 x 10^{-3} solar masses (e.g., TM1: 1.7 x 10^{-3} solar masses; SFHo: 20.5 x 10^{-3} solar masses)
- Mass-averaged thermal index in the remnant for hyperonic models ranges from 0.99 to 1.69 (e.g., DD2YDelta1.2-1.3: 0.99; DNS: 1.69), while nucleonic models range from 1.47 to 1.83
- Dominant postmerger frequency for 1.4-1.4 solar mass hyperonic binaries ranges from 2.51 kHz (DNS) to 3.60 kHz (SFHOY)
- Onset density of hyperons at low temperature is around 2 times nuclear saturation density for most models, with some models (SFHOY, QMC-A) predicting onset above 3 times saturation density

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of hyperonic effects in neutron star mergers initiated by Blacker (2023), which first identified the thermal signature of hyperons in postmerger gravitational-wave frequencies using a smaller set of equations of state. The study addresses the "hyperon puzzle" and the "masquerade problem" raised by Alford (2004) in the context of hadron-quark phase transitions, where different compositions can produce similar mass-radius relations for cold neutron stars. The results enable future gravitational-wave detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer to potentially identify exotic degrees of freedom in dense matter through combined gravitational-wave and kilonova observations.
