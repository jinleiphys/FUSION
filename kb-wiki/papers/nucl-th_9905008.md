# ``Day 1'' Physics at RHIC: Predictions from RQMD

> I discuss predictions based on the transport theoretical approach ``relativistic quantum molecular dynamics''. They can be tested rather soon by the upcoming experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider at BNL (RHIC). Here I focus on the question whether hadronic observables are sensitive to the Equation of State (EOS) in the ultradense matter. A new version of RQMD has been developed recently in which the EOS can be varied, for instance like the one observed in lattice gauge studies. It is found that -- somewhat paradoxically -- the partial transparency of the two nuclei provides new avenues to assess the influence of baryon number in hot matter. One of the signals which are most sensitive to the dynamics is the directed flow of nucleons. As a function of rapidity it changes its direction three times. Surprisingly, even one of the most simple observables -- the average transverse momenta or slopes -- displays some significant sensitivity to the EOS in the phase transition region between hadronic and quark matter.

## Key claim

Hadronic observables at RHIC, particularly the directed flow of nucleons and the average transverse momentum as a function of rapidity, show significant sensitivity to the Equation of State (EOS) in ultradense matter, including the phase transition region between hadronic and quark matter, but this sensitivity is conditional on the partial transparency of the two nuclei and the resulting baryon density gradients away from midrapidity.

## Method

The author used the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) transport model, in a new version where the EOS can be varied, to simulate Au(100AGeV) on Au(100AGeV) collisions at RHIC. Two EOS implementations were compared: a resonance matter EOS and an EOS with a first-order phase transition based on a bag-type model of quarks and gluons with temperature-dependent masses and bag constant, which agrees well with lattice data. The calculations were performed at impact parameter b=3 fm, and the resulting rapidity distributions, average transverse momenta, and directed flow of nucleons were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at RHIC reaches values above 1 GeV/fm^3, the region where the EOS is expected to be softest.
- The system at RHIC stays at temperatures above T_c ≈ 160 MeV in the center for 6 fm/c.
- Thermalization proceeds faster at RHIC (after 3 fm/c) compared to SPS energy (after 5 fm/c).
- The average rapidity loss for baryons in central heavy-ion collisions is Δy ∼ -2.
- The rapidity gap between projectile and target at RHIC is five units in rapidity.
- The resonance matter EOS has p/e ∼ 6.

## Context

This work extends transport model studies of heavy-ion collisions from SPS energies (Pb(158AGeV) on Pb) to RHIC energies, where the author finds that the partial transparency of nuclei at higher beam energies creates new opportunities to study the EOS via baryon number effects. The paper builds on prior studies of elliptical flow sensitivity to the EOS (Sorge 1999) and the "softest point" concept (Mishra, Mohanty, Satz 1995), and contrasts with SPS results where resonance gas dynamics showed good agreement with data (Sorge 1997). The findings enable future experimental tests at RHIC to distinguish between different EOS scenarios, including the possibility of a tricritical point in the T-μ_B plane, and suggest that directed flow may be a more sensitive observable than radial flow for probing the early evolution of the system.
