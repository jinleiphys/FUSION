# A Review on Partial-wave Dynamics with Chiral Effective Field Theory and Dispersion Relation

> The description of strong interaction physics of low-lying resonances is out of the valid range of perturbative QCD. Chiral effective field theories have been developed to tackle the issue. Partial wave dynamics is the systematic tool to decode the underlying physics and reveal the properties of those resonances. It is extremely powerful and helpful for our understanding of the non-perturbative regime, especially when dispersion techniques are utilized simultaneously. Recently, plenty of exotic/ordinary hadrons have been reported by experiment collaborations, e.g. LHCb, Belle, and BESIII, etc.. In this review, we summarize the recent progress on the applications of partial wave dynamics combined with chiral effective field theories and dispersion relations, on related topics, with emphasis on $ππ$, $πK$, $πN$ and $\bar{K}N$ scatterings.

## Key claim

This review argues that combining chiral effective field theory (χEFT) with dispersion relations and partial-wave dynamics provides a rigorous framework for describing low-energy hadron scattering and resonance properties, but notes that unitarization methods (such as K-matrix, Padé approximation, and Bethe-Salpeter equations) necessarily break crossing symmetry and may introduce spurious poles, making dispersive approaches like Roy-type equations and the production representation method more rigorous alternatives.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches that impose S-matrix principles (unitarity, analyticity, crossing symmetry) on χEFT amplitudes for two-body scatterings, focusing on ππ, πK, πN, and K̅N systems. They review unitarization methods (K-matrix, Padé approximation, inverse amplitude method, Lippmann-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, relativistic Friedrichs-Lee scheme) and dispersive methods (Roy-type equations, production representation), then discuss applications to light scalar mesons, baryon resonances, and exotic hadrons reported by LHCb, Belle, and BESIII.

## Context

This review extends the program of combining chiral perturbation theory (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1980s) with S-matrix principles, building on earlier work using Roy-type equations for ππ scattering (Ananthanarayan et al., 2000; Pelaez, 2015) and the production representation method (Zheng et al., 2003; Zhou et al., 2004). It enables systematic extraction of resonance poles (σ, κ, f₀(980), a₀(980), ρ, K*(890), Λ(1405), D*_{s0}(2317), D*_0(2400), P_c pentaquarks) from experimental data while maintaining analyticity constraints, and provides a framework for analyzing the growing number of exotic hadron candidates from modern experiments.
