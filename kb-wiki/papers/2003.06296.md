# Dispersion (asymptotic) theory of charged particle transfer reactions at low energies and nuclear astrophysics: I. the "non-dramatic" case

> A new dispersion (asymptotic) theory is proposed for the peripheral sub- and above-barrier charged particle transfer $A(x,y)B$ reaction in the three-body ($A$, $a$ and $y$) model where $ x= y +a$ and $B=A+a$, and $ a$ is a transferred particle. It is based on the combination of the dispersion theory and the conventional DWBA method. The explicit forms have been derived for the exact three-body pole amplitude and differential cross section in which the contribution of the three-body ($A$, $a$ and $y$) Coulomb dynamics of the transfer mechanism in the peripheral partial amplitudes, corresponding to partial waves with $l_i>>1$, is taken into account correctly. For the specific peripheral proton and triton transfer reactions, the comparative analysis of the peripheral partial amplitudes at $l_i>>1$ , which correspond to the one-step pole and exact three-body pole amplitudes as well as those of the "post"-approximation and the post form of the conventional DWBA, is performed with each other. It shows the absolute inapplicability of the "post"-approximation usually applied for getting an information about specific asymptotic normalization coefficients being astrophysical interest.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new dispersion (asymptotic) theory for peripheral charged particle transfer reactions that combines dispersion theory with conventional DWBA, but this theory is only applicable to the "non-dramatic" case where the Coulomb parameters η_x, η_B, or their sum are not near a natural number; for the "dramatic" case where these parameters are near a natural integer, the conventional DWBA and its "post"-approximation are absolutely inapplicable.

## Method

The authors derive explicit expressions for the exact three-body pole amplitude and differential cross section for peripheral sub- and above-barrier charged particle transfer reactions A(x,y)B within a three-body (A, a, and y) model, where x = y + a and B = A + a. They combine dispersion theory with the conventional DWBA method, introducing Coulomb renormalized factors (CRFs) that account for the three-body Coulomb dynamics of the transfer mechanism in peripheral partial amplitudes with l_i >> 1. The theory is tested by comparing the CRFs for the one-step pole amplitude, the "post"-approximation, the post form of conventional DWBA, and the exact three-body pole amplitude for specific proton and triton transfer reactions.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion peripheral model of DDM1973 and Av86 for charged particle transfer reactions, building on earlier work by KMYa1988 for peripheral neutron transfer reactions induced by heavy ions. The theory addresses limitations of the modified DWBA approach used by Ar96 and Mukh2, which assumed the first-order term over the optical Coulomb polarization potential was small. The paper shows that for the "dramatic" case, the ANC value for ⁷Be + p → ⁸B recommended in Azh1999-1 and Tab06 is underestimated compared to IgYa2008 and TYAS2016, leading to an underestimated astrophysical S factor for the direct radiative capture ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B reaction at solar energies. The proposed theory enables more accurate extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients for astrophysical applications, but only for reactions in the "non-dramatic" regime.
