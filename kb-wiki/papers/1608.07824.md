# The axial charges of the hidden-charm pentaquark states

> With the chiral quark model, we have calculated the axial charges of the pentaquark states with $(I,I_3)=(\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{2})$ and $J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{\pm},\frac{3}{2}^{\pm},\frac{5}{2}^{\pm}$. The $P_c$ states with the same $J^P$ quantum numbers but different color-spin-flavor configurations have very different axial charges, which encode important information on their underlying structures. For some of the $J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{\pm}$ or $\frac{5}{2}^{\pm}$ pentaquark states, their axial charges are much smaller than that of the proton.

## Key claim

The axial charges of hidden-charm pentaquark states with the same spin-parity quantum numbers but different color-spin-flavor configurations are very different, and for some \(J^{P}=3/2^{\pm}\) or \(5/2^{\pm}\) states the axial charges are much smaller than that of the proton, providing a new independent probe of the inner structures of the \(P_c\) states beyond mass and magnetic moment.

## Method

The authors constructed spin-flavor wave functions for pentaquark states with \((I,I_3)=(1/2,1/2)\) and \(J^{P}=1/2^{\pm},3/2^{\pm},5/2^{\pm}\) under SU(2) flavor symmetry, considering the molecular, diquark-triquark, and diquark-diquark-antiquark models. Using the chiral quark model Lagrangian, they computed the matrix elements of the \(\bar u \sigma_z u - \bar d \sigma_z d\) operator between these wave functions to extract the axial charges at the quark level, then matched to hadron-level \(P_c P_c \pi\) couplings to obtain numerical values for \(g_{P_1}\), \(g_{P_3}\), and \(g_{P_5}\) in units of the nucleon axial charge \(g_A\).

## Key numbers

- The axial charge of the \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) molecular state composed of \(\bar D \Sigma_c\) (\(^2S_{1/2}\)) is \(g_{P_1}=0.68\).
- The axial charge of the \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) molecular state composed of \(\bar D^{*} \Sigma_c\) (\(^2S_{1/2}\)) is \(g_{P_1}=-0.39\).
- The axial charge of the \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\) molecular state composed of \(\bar D \Sigma_c^{*}\) (\(^4S_{3/2}\)) is \(g_{P_3}=0.34\).
- The axial charge of the \(J^{P}=3/2^{-}\) molecular state composed of \(\bar D^{*} \Sigma_c\) (\(^4S_{3/2}\)) is \(g_{P_3}=0.14\).
- The axial charge of the \(J^{P}=5/2^{-}\) molecular state composed of \(\bar D^{*} \Sigma_c^{*}\) (\(^6S_{5/2}\)) is \(g_{P_5}=0.15\).
- For the \(2_{1f}\) representation, the \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) state with configuration \(0^{+}\otimes 1/2^{-}\otimes 0^{+}\) has axial charge \(g_{P_1}=0\).
- The transition coupling \(g_{22}\) between \(\bar D^{*} \Sigma_c\) \(^4P_{1/2}\) and \(\bar D^{*} \Sigma_c\) \(^2P_{1/2}\) is \(-0.50\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 92\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of hidden-charm pentaquark states following the LHCb discovery of \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) (Aaij 2015), where previous studies focused on mass spectra and decay widths (e.g., Karliner 2015, Chen 2015, Maiani 2015) and magnetic moments (Wang 2016). The axial charge is shown to be more sensitive than magnetic moments to the color, spin, and isospin configurations of the light quarks, enabling future discrimination between molecular, diquark-triquark, and diquark-diquark-antiquark models. The results can be tested through semileptonic decays of \(P_c\) states or through the process \(P_c \to P_c + \pi \to J/\psi + p + \pi\) once the \(P_c(4380)\) state is produced.
