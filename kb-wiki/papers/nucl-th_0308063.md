# NN($^1S_0$) pairs in $^3$He and in p$^3$He backward elastic scattering

> It is shown that the shoulder observed in the cross section of $p^3He$ backward elastic scattering at proton beam energies of 0.4 -- 0.7 GeV can be explained by the mechanism of virtual $π$-meson production with a dominant contribution from the subprocesses $pd^*\to ^3$He$ π^0$ and $p(pp)\to^3$He$ π^+$, where $d^*$ denotes the singlet deuteron and the $pp$ pair is in the $^1S_0$ state. At high energies, 1-3 GeV, the mechanism of $np$-pair transfer dominates and probes high-momentum $NN(^1S_0$) correlations in $^3$He.

## Key claim

The shoulder observed in the cross section of p³He backward elastic scattering at proton beam energies of 0.4–0.7 GeV can be explained by the mechanism of virtual π-meson production with dominant contributions from subprocesses involving singlet NN(¹S₀) pairs (the singlet deuteron d* and the diproton pp pair), rather than by the sequential transfer mechanism that dominates at other energies.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section of p³He backward elastic scattering using a one-pion exchange (OPE) model with triangle diagrams, incorporating subprocesses pd* → ³He π⁰ and p(pp) → ³He π⁺. They expressed the combined contribution of singlet NN pairs via the experimental cross section of pd → ³He π⁰ using isospin relations, and included distortions in eikonal approximation. The sequential transfer mechanism was calculated separately using a 3N wave function from the CD Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- The OPE model with deuteron exchange alone yields a cross section one order of magnitude smaller than the data at Tp = 0.4–1.5 GeV
- The combined contribution of d* and pp pairs (with Tpp = 2Td* from isospin invariance) brings the calculation into qualitative agreement with data
- The sequential transfer mechanism dominates at Tp < 0.3 GeV and Tp > 1.5 GeV
- The OPE model describes the energy dependence for Tp = 0.4–1.5 GeV
- The shoulder region of interest is at 0.4–0.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous DWBA studies by Blu (1996) and Uzikov (1998) that used Faddeev equations with the Reid RSC NN potential, which demonstrated dominance of the sequential transfer mechanism over Tp = 0.1–2 GeV except near 0.3 GeV. The present paper shows that the dip region (0.4–0.7 GeV) is instead dominated by OPE mechanisms involving singlet NN(¹S₀) pairs. The results enable future measurements of spin observables at 0.2–0.4 GeV, as planned by Hatan (2003), to provide additional information on these correlations.
