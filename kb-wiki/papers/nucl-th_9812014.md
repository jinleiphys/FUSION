# An Effective Model for Hot Gluodynamics

> We consider an effective Lagrangian containing contributions from glueball and gluon degrees of freedom with a scale-invariant coupling between the two. The thermodynamic potential is calculated taking into account thermal fluctuations of both fields. The glueball mean field dominates at low temperature, while the high temperature phase is governed by low-mass gluon-like excitations. The model shows some similarities to the lattice results in the pure glue sector of QCD. In particular, it exhibits a strong first order phase transition at a critical temperature of approximately 265 MeV when reasonable parameters are taken.

## Key claim

The model exhibits a strong first-order phase transition at a critical temperature of approximately 265 MeV when reasonable parameters are taken, but the energy density just above the transition disagrees with lattice data because the effective gluon mass becomes proportional to temperature too quickly, suggesting the model is incomplete in the region \(T_c < T < 2T_c\).

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian with a scalar glueball field and an Abelian vector field representing constituent gluons, coupled via a scale-invariant masslike term. The glueball potential is chosen to reproduce the QCD trace anomaly. The thermodynamic potential is computed by decomposing the fields into mean-field and fluctuating parts, treating thermal fluctuations of both fields with a Gaussian weighting method that resums the Taylor expansion exactly. The mean-field equation and effective mass equations are solved self-consistently for temperatures from zero to well above the critical point.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for the \(\nu=14\) (effective \(SU(3)\)) case: \(T_c = 265\) MeV.
* For \(\nu=6\) (roughly \(SU(2)\)) the critical temperature rises to \(T_c = 340\) MeV; reducing the gluon condensate by a factor of \(2/3\) gives \(T_c = 300\) MeV.
* The vacuum glueball mass is taken as \(m_\chi = 1.7\) GeV, following Sexton et al.
* The vacuum gluon condensate parameter is \(B_0 = (391\ \mathrm{MeV})^4\), consistent with the updated average \((399\pm13\ \mathrm{MeV})^4\) given by Narison (1997).
* The effective number of gluon degrees of freedom is \(\nu \approx 14\) to reproduce the lattice asymptotic value \(\mathcal{E}/T^4 \approx 4.7\) at high temperature (Boyd et al., 1996).
* At high temperature for \(\nu=14\), the ratio \(m_A^*/T = 0.18\) and \(m_\chi^*/T = 4.5\).
* The pure glueball case (\(\nu=0\)) gives \(T_c = 490\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pure glueball model of Agasyan (1993) by including gluon degrees of freedom and a more exact treatment of thermal fluctuations. It is structurally similar to the dual Ginzburg-Landau model (Monden, 1991) but uses a logarithmic glueball potential instead of a quartic one. The model reproduces the deconfinement temperature found in lattice calculations (Boyd et al., 1996; Laermann, 1996) but fails to match the lattice energy density just above \(T_c\), where the latent heat is too large and \(\mathcal{E}/T^4\) overshoots its asymptotic value from above rather than approaching from below. The authors suggest that modifications inspired by the color dielectric model or the inclusion of quark degrees of freedom could improve the description.
