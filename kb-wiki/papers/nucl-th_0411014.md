# Towards a Connection Between Nuclear Structure and QCD

> As we search for an ever deeper understanding of the structure of hadronic matter one of the most fundamental questions is whether or not one can make a connection to the underlying theory of the strong interaction, QCD. We build on recent advances in the chiral extrapolation problem linking lattice QCD at relatively large ``light quark'' masses to the physical world to estimate the scalar polarizability of the nucleon. The latter plays a key role in modern relativistic mean-field descriptions of nuclei and nuclear matter (such as QMC) and, in particular, leads to a very natural saturation mechanism. We demonstrate that the value of the scalar polarizability extracted from the lattice data is consistent with that needed for a successful description of nuclei within the framework of QMC. In a very real sense this is the first hint of a direct connection between QCD and the properties of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The scalar polarizability of the nucleon, extracted from lattice QCD data via finite-range regularization (FRR) chiral extrapolation, is consistent with the value needed for nuclear saturation in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, providing a first hint of a direct connection between QCD and finite-nuclei properties. The authors stress that, unlike the determination of low-energy coefficients which is model-independent, the scalar polarizability extraction requires a specific choice of regulator (a dipole of mass 0.8 GeV) and thus carries some model dependence.

## Method

The authors fit the nucleon mass as a function of pion mass using a chiral expansion that includes nucleon-pion and delta-pion loop self-energies, plus a tadpole term, evaluated with finite-range regularization (FRR) using four different regulator forms (sharp cutoff, monopole, dipole, Gaussian). The analytic coefficients up to order \(m_\pi^6\) are fitted to two-flavor lattice QCD data from the CP-PACS and JLQCD collaborations, together with the physical nucleon mass, and the resulting expansion is used to infer how the nucleon mass changes when a chiral-invariant scalar field (equivalent to a shift in quark mass at fixed pion mass) is applied.

## Key numbers

* The scalar polarizability extracted from the FRR fit including the tadpole term gives a coefficient of \(-0.6\) for the \(g_\sigma^2\) term in the expression for \(g_{\sigma\sigma}\), in agreement with the bag-model value \(g_{\sigma\sigma} = -0.11 R g_\sigma^2\) for \(R = 1\ \mathrm{fm} = 5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
* The reduction of the effective \(\sigma\)-nucleon coupling at nuclear matter density, where \(g_\sigma \sigma \in (150,200)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), is around 15–20%.
* The extrapolated nucleon mass at the physical pion mass using FRR (NLNA, including tadpole) is \(0.959 \pm 0.116\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (dipole regulator), with all FRR results lying within a range of 1%.
* The sigma commutator \(\sigma_N\) extrapolated to the physical pion mass is \(33.3 \pm 13.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (dipole regulator).

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1987) and Saito (1994), which describes nuclear saturation via a density-dependent \(\sigma\)-nucleon coupling arising from the internal quark structure of nucleons, by connecting its scalar polarizability to lattice QCD data. It builds on the finite-range regularization (FRR) chiral extrapolation method developed by Young, Leinweber, and Thomas (2002) and Leinweber (2003), which resolves the poor convergence of dimensional regularization for baryon masses. The result enables a direct link between QCD calculations of hadron properties and relativistic mean-field descriptions of nuclear matter, and it motivates further study of in-medium nucleon structure changes, as suggested by recent experimental evidence from Strauch (2002) and Dieterich (2000).
