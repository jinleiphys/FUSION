# Examination of directed flow as a signature of the softest point of the equation of state in QCD matter

> We analyze the directed flow of protons and pions in high-energy heavy-ion collisions in the incident energy range from $\sqrt{s_{\scriptscriptstyle NN}}=7.7$ to 27 GeV within a microscopic transport model. Standard hadronic transport approaches do not describe the collapse of directed flow below $\sqrt{s_{\scriptscriptstyle NN}}\simeq 20$ GeV. By contrast, a model which simulates effects of a softening of the equation of state, well describes the behavior of directed flow data recently obtained by the STAR Collaboration~\cite{STARv1}. We give a detailed analysis of how directed flow is generated. Particularly, we found that softening of effective equation of state at the overlapping region of two nuclei, i.e. the reaction stages where the system reaches high baryon density state, is needed to explain the observed collapse of proton directed flow within a hadronic transport approach.

## Key claim
The observed collapse of proton directed flow at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 11.5 and 19.6 GeV can be explained by a softening of the effective equation of state in the high-baryon-density overlap region of the two nuclei, but only if that softening occurs during the specific time interval when the baryon density is highest (approximately 1 to 2 fm/c), not at earlier or later stages.

## Method
The authors used the hadronic transport model JAM, imposing attractive orbits for all two-body hadron-hadron scatterings to simulate the effects of a softened equation of state, rather than selecting scattering angles randomly as in a standard cascade. They extracted an effective equation of state from the simulations by computing local pressure and energy density at each collision point in the central reaction zone. The directed flow of protons and pions was calculated for Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair from 7.7 to 27 GeV and compared with STAR Collaboration data.

## Key numbers
- Proton directed flow slope from standard JAM cascade at 11.5 and 19.6 GeV is much larger than STAR data; JAM with attractive orbits yields almost zero slope at 11.5 GeV and negative slope at 19.6 GeV, matching the data.
- At 7.7 GeV, JAM with attractive orbits produces results far from the data, indicating no large EoS softening at this energy.
- The crossing time of two gold nuclei in the center-of-mass frame ranges from 3.27 fm/c at 7.7 GeV to 0.906 fm/c at 27 GeV.
- Average number of baryon-baryon collisions in semicentral Au+Au collisions is 490, 400, 340, and 310 for 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, and 27 GeV, respectively.
- The effective equation of state from standard JAM gives pressure approximately 0.12 to 0.15 times the energy density, similar to previous extractions from AGS to SPS energies.

## Context
This work addresses the discrepancy between standard hadronic transport models (such as UrQMD, RQMD, and PHSD/HSD) which do not produce negative proton directed flow slopes below 20 GeV, and the STAR Collaboration data showing such negative slopes at 11.5 and 19.6 GeV. The authors extend earlier studies by Sorge (1998) and Kahana (1994) on using scattering orbits to modify the equation of state, and contrast their findings with hybrid transport+hydrodynamics approaches by Steinheimer (2014) which found no sensitivity of directed flow to the equation of state, and with three-fluid model calculations by Ivanov (2014) which indicated crossover deconfinement is consistent with data up to 11.5 GeV. The results suggest that the softening of the equation of state at high baryon density is needed to explain the observed flow collapse, and that detailed experimental studies around beam energies below 10 GeV are needed to determine the onset energy of this softening.
