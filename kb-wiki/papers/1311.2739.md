# Effects of the symmetry energy on the kaon condensates in the QMC Model

> In this work we investigate protoneutron star properties within a modified version of the quark coupling model (QMC) that incorporates a omega-rho interaction plus kaon condensed matter at finite temperature. Fixed entropy and trapped neutrinos are taken into account. Our results are compared with the ones obtained with the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model for similar values of the symmetry energy slope. Contrary to GM1, within the QMC the formation of low mass black-holes during cooling are not probable. It is shown that the evolution of the protoneutron star may include the melting of the kaon condensate driven by the neutrino diffusion, followed by the formation of a second condensate after cooling. The signature of this complex proccess could be a neutrino signal followed by a gamma ray burst. We have seen that both models can, in general, describe very massive stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, the formation of low-mass black holes during protoneutron star cooling is not probable, contrary to predictions from the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific values of the symmetry energy slope and the inclusion of the omega-rho interaction.

## Method

The authors used a modified version of the quark meson coupling (QMC) model that incorporates an omega-rho interaction and kaon condensed matter at finite temperature, treating nucleons and kaons as MIT bags with quark degrees of freedom. They solved the equations of motion for the meson fields in the mean-field approximation under beta-equilibrium conditions, considering three different snapshots of protoneutron star evolution: warm matter with trapped neutrinos (entropy per baryon S=1, lepton fraction Y_l=0.4), neutrino-free matter with S=2, and cold deleptonized matter with S=0. The results were compared with the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model for three similar values of the symmetry energy slope L (approximately 59.5, 70.5, and 93.5 MeV).

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy slope values used: L = 93.5, 70.5, and 59.3 MeV for QMC; L = 93.8, 70.8, and 59.6 MeV for GM1 (saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 for QMC, 0.153 fm^-3 for GM1)
* Kaon optical potential at saturation: V_K = -123 MeV for QMC, V_K = -125 MeV for GM1
* Maximum gravitational masses for QMC cold stars (S=0): 1.98 M_sun (L=93.5 MeV), 1.94 M_sun (L=70.5 MeV), 1.95 M_sun (L=59.03 MeV)
* Maximum gravitational masses for GM1 cold stars (S=0): 2.14 M_sun (L=93.8 MeV), 2.06 M_sun (L=70.8 MeV), 2.06 M_sun (L=59.6 MeV)
* Radii of 1.4 M_sun stars for QMC cold matter: 13.58 km (L=93.5 MeV), 13.19 km (L=70.5 MeV), 13.06 km (L=59.03 MeV)
* Radii of 1.4 M_sun stars for GM1 cold matter: 13.82 km (L=93.8 MeV), 13.23 km (L=70.8 MeV), 13.06 km (L=59.6 MeV)
* Kaon onset energy densities for QMC cold matter: 2.93 fm^4 (L=93.5 MeV), 2.96 fm^4 (L=70.5 MeV), 3.07 fm^4 (L=59.03 MeV)
* Maximum mass difference within QMC for different L values is not larger than approximately 0.04 M_sun, except for the S=2, L=93.5 MeV case

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars by Gupta and collaborators (2013) and Pons and collaborators (2001) by incorporating finite temperature effects and the omega-rho interaction into the QMC model, allowing tuning of the symmetry energy slope. The results are compared with the GM1 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model from Glendenning (2000). The paper enables predictions for protoneutron star evolution scenarios where kaon condensates may melt during neutrino diffusion and reform after cooling, potentially producing observable neutrino signals followed by gamma ray bursts. The findings also contribute to the ongoing debate about neutron star radii constraints from analyses of quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries by Guillot and collaborators and by Lattimer and collaborators (2013).
