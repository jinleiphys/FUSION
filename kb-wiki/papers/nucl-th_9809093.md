# NN Interactions in QCD: Old and New Techniques

> Since QCD is believed to be the underlying theory of the strong interaction, it is appropriate to study techniques that take into account more features of its rich and complex structure. We begin by discussing aspects of physics that are ill-reproduced by the usual one- or two-meson exchange approaches and identify the source of the deficiencies of these models. We then reveal promising methods for curing some of these ills, such as new quark potential models, baryon chiral perturbation theory, soluble strongly-interacting field theories, and large N_c QCD.

## Key claim

While meson exchange models achieve excellent fits to NN scattering data, they fail at short distances (below about 2 fm) where nucleon substructure becomes important, and QCD-based techniques such as quark models, chiral perturbation theory, and large \(N_c\) expansions are needed to understand the origin of features like the repulsive core.

## Method

The paper reviews deficiencies of one- and two-meson exchange models for the NN interaction, using a geometric "parable" about hadron sizes to illustrate why meson exchange breaks down at short range. It then surveys several QCD-based approaches: inverse scattering methods that reconstruct local potentials from phase shifts; nonlocal potentials arising from quark substructure; quark models with one-gluon exchange and confinement; effective field theories including baryon chiral perturbation theory; and large \(N_c\) QCD as an organizing principle for nuclear interactions.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} \approx 0.6\) fm; typical light meson size set by QCD scale: \(r \approx 1/\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 1/(300\ {\rm MeV}) = O(0.5\ {\rm fm})\); meson cannot mediate NN interaction below approximately 2.2 fm based on hard-sphere picture.
- Experimental relative mass splitting between nucleon and \(\Delta\): \((m_\Delta - m_N) / \frac{1}{2}(m_\Delta + m_N) = 0.27\); large \(N_c\) prediction gives \(3/N_c^2 = 0.33\) when \(N_c=3\).
- Expansion parameter for large \(N_c\) corrections: \(1/N_c = 1/3\) in our universe; some quantities receive first corrections at order \(1/N_c^2 = 1/9\).

## Context

This invited talk (Lebed, 1998) reviews the status of QCD-based approaches to the NN interaction, contrasting with the successful phenomenological fits of the Paris, Bonn, Nijmegen, and Argonne potential models. It extends earlier work on the 't Hooft model (QCD in 1+1 dimensions with large \(N_c\)) to illustrate the failure of single-meson dominance for heavy quarks, and discusses the first large \(N_c\) analysis of the NN interaction by Kaplan and Manohar. The paper enables future work connecting quark substructure to nuclear forces, particularly through effective field theories and large \(N_c\) expansions.
