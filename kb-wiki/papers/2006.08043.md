# Neutrino mixing in nuclear rapid neutron-capture processes

> A possible mechanism for the formation of heavy-mass elements in supernovae is the rapid neutron-capture-mechanism (r-process). It depends upon the electron-fraction $Y_e$, a quantity which is determined by beta-decay-rates. In this paper, we focus on the calculation of electroweak decay-rates in presence of massive neutrinos. The resulting expressions are then used to calculate nuclear reactions entering the rapid-neutron capture. We fix the astrophysical parameters to the case of a core-collapse supernova. The neutrino sector includes a mass scheme and mixing angles for active neutrinos, and also by including the mixing between active and sterile neutrinos. The results of the calculations show that the predicted abundances of heavy-mass nuclei are indeed affected by the neutrino mixing.

## Key claim
The predicted abundances of heavy-mass nuclei produced by the r-process in core-collapse supernovae are affected by neutrino mixing, but only when mixing between active and sterile neutrinos is included; mixing among active neutrino flavors alone does not change the decay rates or abundances relative to the standard case without oscillations.

## Method
The authors derived an expression for the beta-decay rate in the presence of neutrino oscillations, including both active-active and active-sterile mixing, by writing the electron-neutrino as a superposition of mass eigenstates and using a Gaussian wave-packet formalism. They incorporated this modified decay rate into the r-java 2.0 open-use code, which solves the reaction network for rapid neutron capture in a core-collapse supernova wind scenario, including beta decay, neutron capture, alpha processes, photo-dissociation, and fission. The calculations were performed for a high-entropy wind with initial temperature \(T_0 = 3 \times 10^9\) K, initial density \(\rho_0 = 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\), expansion speed \(V_{\rm exp} = 7500\) km/s, and initial electron fraction \(Y_e = 0.3\).

## Key numbers
- The mixing angle for active-sterile oscillations \(\theta_{14}\) was varied; for \(\Theta_{14} = \pi/20\) the ratio \(f = \Gamma_{\rm osc}/\Gamma_{\rm no-osc} = 0.97553\), while for \(\Theta_{14} = 0\) the ratio is 1.00000.
- The mass-square difference for active-sterile mixing was fixed at \(\Delta m_{14}^2 = 1\) eV\(^2\); for active-active mixing \(\Delta m_{13}^2 = 2 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\) and \(\sin^2(2\theta_{13}) = 0.09\).
- The stability time \(t_{\rm sta}\) was taken as either \(0\) s or \(4.354 \times 10^{17}\) s.
- The initial wind radius was \(R_0 = 390\) km and the expansion timescale \(\tau = 0.1\) s.
- The abundances most sensitive to active-sterile mixing include \(^{80}{\rm Se}\), \(^{114}{\rm Cd}\), \(^{115}{\rm In}\), \(^{121}{\rm Sb}\), \(^{123}{\rm Sb}\), and \(^{126}{\rm Te}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutrino oscillations in supernova environments, such as those by Wu (2015), Woosley (1994), and Tamborra (2012), by explicitly calculating the impact of both active-active and active-sterile mixing on beta-decay rates and r-process abundances. The results contradict the common assumption that neutrino mixing has negligible effect on r-process nucleosynthesis, showing that active-sterile mixing with \(\theta_{14}\) as small as \(\pi/20\) can alter abundances, particularly in the mass regions A = 70–125 and above A ≈ 180. The findings enable future work to constrain sterile neutrino parameters using observed elemental abundances from r-process sites.
