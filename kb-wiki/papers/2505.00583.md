# Gluon Parts of Gravitational Form Factors and Mass Distribution

> The parton structure of the nucleon and pion is investigated in an exploratory model that allows one to assess whether the dressing of quarks can, by itself, produce realistic gluon contributions to light-cone momentum fractions, gravitational form factors, mass/energy distributions and their radii. The model is the Dyson-Schwinger Equations in Rainbow-Ladder truncation. For the parton mass/energy distributions as a function of momentum transfer, we directly calculate matrix elements of the Energy-Momentum Tensor by utilizing its similarity to the momentum fraction moment of GPDs associated with deep inelastic scattering. A variety of gravitational form factors are obtained including the D-term.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether the quark dressing mechanism alone, as implemented in a Dyson-Schwinger Equations (DSE) Rainbow-Ladder truncation model, can generate realistic gluon contributions to gravitational form factors and mass/energy distributions for the nucleon and pion, without explicit gluon degrees of freedom beyond those induced by quark self-interactions.

## Method

The authors directly calculate matrix elements of the Energy-Momentum Tensor (EMT) by exploiting its mathematical similarity to the generalized momentum fraction moment (second Mellin moment) of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) associated with deep inelastic scattering. They employ the DSE Rainbow-Ladder truncation to compute dressed quark propagators and vertices, using the pion to fix the gluon-in-quark kernel parameters (model scales mu0_pi = 0.9 GeV, mu0_N = 0.64 GeV) and then applying that kernel unchanged to the nucleon. The nucleon matrix element uses a model with diquark correlations and SU(6) spin-flavor structure, previously fit to Faddeev calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pion model scale: mu0_pi = 0.9 GeV; nucleon model scale: mu0_N = 0.64 GeV
- At Q^2 = 0 and scale mu = 2 GeV, the non-conservation form factors are: Cbar^N_q(0) = -0.08, Cbar^N_g(0) = 0.08; Cbar^pi_q(0) = -0.02, Cbar^pi_g(0) = 0.02
- Total D-term at mu = 2 GeV: D_pi(0) = -0.89, D_N(0) = -1.73
- Radii at mu = 2 GeV for pion: R_q(A) = 0.397 fm, R_g(A) = 0.485 fm, R_g/R_q = 1.22; R_q(E) = 1.178 fm, R_g(E) = 1.197 fm, R_g/R_q = 1.017
- Radii at mu = 2 GeV for nucleon: R_q(A) = 0.627 fm, R_g(A) = 0.655 fm, R_g/R_q = 1.044; R_q(E) = 0.766 fm, R_g(E) = 0.765 fm, R_g/R_q = 0.998
- Ratio of quark radii to empirical charge radii (R_ch_pi = 0.659 fm, R_ch_N = 0.843 fm): for pion R_q(A)/R_ch = 0.60, R_q(E)/R_ch = 1.79; for nucleon R_q(A)/R_ch = 0.75, R_q(E)/R_ch = 0.912
- Nucleon gluon mass/energy distribution radius R_E_g = 0.765 fm, compared to LQCD result 0.81 fm (Hackett 2023) and data analysis 0.778 fm (Meziani 2024)

## Context

This work extends the DSE-RL approach previously applied to standard parton momentum fractions (Tandy 2023) to gravitational form factors and mass/energy distributions, complementing recent LQCD calculations of nucleon and pion gravitational form factors (Hackett 2023). The results are compared with global data analysis (Hou 2019), LQCD angular momentum results (Alexandrou 2020), and other theoretical approaches including the Instanton Liquid Model (Liu 2024), light-front Hamiltonian method (Xu 2022), contact quark-quark interaction model (Sultan 2024), and algebraic GPD model (Raya 2021). The approach enables future extension to calculate the Cbar_p(Q^2) form factor and incorporation of more realistic nucleon states.
