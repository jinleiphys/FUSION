# Imprints of dynamic fluidization on dilepton production

> We present a newly developed hybrid hadronic transport + hydrodynamics framework geared towards heavy ion collisions at low to intermediate beam energies, and report on the resulting excitation function of dileptons. In this range of energies, it is unclear how to properly initialize the hydrodynamic evolution. Due to the cumulative electromagnetic radiation throughout the collision, dilepton observables are sensitive to the initial condition. In this work, we study how the dilepton ``thermometer'' is affected by employing dynamical initial conditions, in contrast to the traditional fixed-time approach.

## Key claim

The choice of dynamical versus fixed-time initialization of hydrodynamics in hybrid models significantly affects the extracted dilepton effective temperature, with differences ranging from 8 MeV at low beam energies to 30 MeV at higher energies, making the "dilepton thermometer" less reliable than previously assumed.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid model combining hadronic transport with viscous hydrodynamics based on Israel-Stewart equations, implementing a "dynamic fluidization" (DynFlu) approach where hadrons are converted to fluid when local energy density exceeds a threshold, rather than the traditional fixed-time (iso-τ) initialization. Dilepton emission was calculated via the shining method for off-equilibrium contributions in SMASH and thermal radiation using Rapp-Wambach-Hees model rates from vHLLE, for Au+Au collisions across beam energies from 3.5 to 19.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature difference between DynFlu and iso-τ: ΔT_eff ≈ 8 MeV at √s_NN = 3.5 GeV, increasing to ΔT_eff ≈ 30 MeV at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV
- For pT-integrated spectra, correlation fit slope κ = 1.88 (DynFlu) versus κ = 1.52 (iso-τ), with intercept c = -0.13 GeV (DynFlu) versus c = -0.09 GeV (iso-τ)
- For high pT bin (2.1 ≤ pT/GeV ≤ 2.6), correlation slope κ = 1.19 (DynFlu) versus κ = 0.95 (iso-τ), with intercept c = -0.05 GeV (DynFlu) versus c = -0.02 GeV (iso-τ)
- Reference fit from Churchill (2023) found κ = 1.55(2) and c = -0.093(3) GeV for iso-τ initialization

## Context

This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Churchill (2023), who found a strong correlation between dilepton slope and initial temperature using fixed-time initialization, and the dynamic fluidization method introduced by Góes-Hirayama (2025). The results show that the "dilepton thermometer" concept is sensitive to initialization assumptions, but that applying a pT cut improves the correlation between effective temperature and maximum medium temperature, suggesting a path toward more reliable temperature extraction from dilepton spectra.
