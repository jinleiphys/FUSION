# Quantification of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV

> The Multi-Phase Transport model, AMPT, and the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics model, AVFD, are used to assess a possible chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation ($ΔS$) recently measured with the ${R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)}$ correlator in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV. The Comparison of the experimental and simulated ${R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)}$ distributions indicates that background-driven charge separation is insufficient to account for the measurements. The AVFD model calculations, which explicitly account for CME-driven anomalous transport in the presence of background, indicate a CME signal quantified by the $P$-odd Fourier dipole coefficient ${a_1'}\approx 0.5\%$ in mid-central collisions. A similar evaluation for the $Δγ$ correlator suggests that only a small fraction of this signal ($f_{\rm CME}=Δγ_{\rm CME}/Δγ\approx 25\%$) is measurable with this correlator in the same collisions. The related prediction for signal detection in isobaric collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr are also presented.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is quantified as a modest P-odd Fourier dipole coefficient a1' ≈ 0.5% in mid-central collisions, but only about 25% of this signal is measurable with the standard Δγ correlator, while the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator captures approximately 76% of it.

## Method

The authors used the Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) and the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics model (AVFD) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with varying strengths of CME-driven charge separation, controlled by the dipole coefficient a1'. They compared the simulated RΨ2(ΔS) distributions and extracted inverse variances σ⁻²(RΨ2) against experimental data from the STAR collaboration. Calibration curves of σ⁻²(RΨ2) versus (a1')² and Δγ versus (a1')² were constructed for both models, and the same procedure was applied to isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions.

## Key numbers

- The extracted P-odd Fourier dipole coefficient for mid-central (30-50%) Au+Au collisions is a1' = 0.50 ± 0.03%, corresponding to an initial axial charge density n5/s = 0.1.
- The fraction of the RΨ2(ΔS) correlator signal attributable to the CME is fCME(RΨ2) ≈ 76% for Au+Au collisions.
- The fraction of the Δγ correlator signal attributable to the CME is fCME(Δγ) ≈ 25% for the same Au+Au collisions.
- For isobaric collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at 30-40% centrality with n5/s = 0.1, the CME fractions are fCME(RΨ2) ≈ 25% and fCME(Δγ) ≈ 13%.
- The signal loss from the primordial dipole coefficient a1' to the final hadron observable ã1 is about 30% for Au+Au collisions in AVFD.
- The AMPT model requires a1'(AMPT) ≈ 6 × a1'(AVFD) to produce the same RΨ2(ΔS) correlator response.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions, following the STAR collaboration's recent measurements (2020) that suggested possible CME-driven charge separation. The authors use the AVFD model (Shi 2017, Jiang 2016) which explicitly implements CME-driven anomalous transport, and the AMPT model (Lin 2004) which mimics CME effects via parton-level charge swapping (Ma 2011). The calibration establishes that background-driven charge separation alone cannot explain the data, and provides quantitative predictions for the upcoming isobaric collision program at RHIC, where Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions are expected to have different magnetic field strengths but similar backgrounds.
