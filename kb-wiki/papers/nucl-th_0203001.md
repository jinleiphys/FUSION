# Chiral corrections to the isovector double scattering term for the pion-deuteron scattering length

> The empirical value of the real part of the pion-deuteron scattering length can be well understood in terms of the dominant isovector $πN$-double scattering contribution. We calculate in chiral perturbation theory all one-pion loop corrections to this double scattering term which in the case of $πN$-scattering close the gap between the current-algebra prediction and the empirical value of the isovector threshold T-matrix $T_{πN}^-$. In addition to closing this gap there is in the $πd$-system a loop-induced off-shell correction for the exchanged virtual pion. Its coordinate space representation reveals that it is equivalent to $2π$-exchange in the deuteron. We evaluate the chirally corrected double scattering term and the off-shell contribution with various realistic deuteron wave functions. We find that the off-shell correction contributes at most -8% and that the isovector double scattering term explains at least 90% of the empirical value of the real part of the $πd$-scattering length.

## Key claim

The isovector double scattering term explains at least 90% of the empirical value of the real part of the pion-deuteron scattering length, with the off-shell correction contributing at most -8%, but this conclusion depends on including chiral one-pion loop corrections that close the gap between the current-algebra prediction and the empirical isovector pion-nucleon threshold T-matrix.

## Method

The author calculates in chiral perturbation theory all one-pion loop corrections to the isovector double scattering term for pion-deuteron scattering at threshold. The chirally corrected double scattering term and the loop-induced off-shell correction for the exchanged virtual pion are evaluated by Fourier-transforming into coordinate space and integrating with various realistic deuteron wavefunctions (Paris, Bonn-CD, Idaho, and several Nijmegen potentials). The short-distance singularity in the off-shell correction is regularized by starting the radial integration at a nonzero minimum radius corresponding to the equivalent momentum cut-off.

## Key numbers

- The empirical isovector pion-nucleon threshold T-matrix is \(T^-_{\pi N} = (1.847 \mp 0.086)\,{\rm fm}\) from pionic hydrogen experiments.
- The empirical real part of the pion-deuteron threshold T-matrix is \({\rm Re}\, T_{\pi d} = (-0.496 \pm 0.016)\,{\rm fm}\) from pionic deuterium experiments.
- The leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg prediction is \(T^-_{\pi N}({\rm tree}) = m_\pi/2 f_\pi^2 = 1.61\,{\rm fm}\), about 13% smaller than the empirical central value.
- The chirally corrected double scattering contribution \(T^{(d)}_{\pi d}\) ranges from \(-0.459\) fm (NL5 potential) to \(-0.500\) fm (NNL5 potential).
- The off-shell correction \(\delta T^{(o)}_{\pi d}\) ranges from 0.008 fm (NL5 potential) to 0.036 fm (NNL5 and NNL6 potentials), contributing at most -8%.
- The equivalent momentum cut-off is \(\Lambda = 857\,{\rm MeV}\), corresponding to a minimum radial integration start of \(r_{min} = 1/\Lambda = 0.23\,{\rm fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Weinberg (1990s) for relating pion-nucleon scattering to nuclear scattering processes, and addresses the shortcoming noted in Beane et al. that the leading-order isovector double scattering contribution comes out about 25% too small. The calculation uses the one-pion loop corrections from PinLoop (2000) that close the gap between the current-algebra prediction and the empirical isovector pion-nucleon threshold T-matrix. The results agree with the findings of Tony (2001) who investigated a host of other small corrections to the real part of the pion-deuteron threshold T-matrix, and the off-shell correction found here has a well-defined conceptual origin as the unique byproduct of the pion-loop graphs.
