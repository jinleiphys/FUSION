# Three-Nucleon Forces Revisited - Some Historical Thoughts

> Historic steps in the emergence, the derivation and the use of three-nucleon forces, genuine and effective, for calculations of few-nucleon systems and of the structure of heavier nuclei are recalled. The research focus is on few-nucleon systems. The need of three-nucleon forces for a successful description of some data and the remaining puzzles of other data, not explainable despite the inclusion of three-nucleon forces, are discussed.

# Three-Nucleon Forces Revisited - Some Historical Thoughts

## Key claim

Genuine three-nucleon forces are not uniquely determined by nature but are theoretical constructs that arise when non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (such as the Delta isobar) are frozen out of the Hilbert space; even with the inclusion of three-nucleon forces, some few-nucleon data remain unexplained, indicating that the current dynamical description is still incomplete.

## Method

The author reviews the historical development of three-nucleon forces, distinguishing between "genuine" forces (derived from meson theory or chiral effective field theory by freezing non-nucleonic degrees of freedom) and "effective" forces (which arise from truncating the Hilbert space or from smoothing transformations of the Hamiltonian). For few-nucleon calculations, the coupled-channel potential CD Bonn + Delta is used, which keeps the Delta isobar as an explicit degree of freedom in an expanded Hilbert space, thereby generating consistent two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon forces without additional tuning. Momentum-space integral equations with Coulomb inclusion are solved for three-nucleon and four-nucleon bound and scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The Fujita-Miyazawa part of the three-nucleon force contributes roughly twice as much as all other three-nucleon-force contributions combined to the binding of few-nucleon systems.
- The effect of the four-nucleon force on binding is an order of magnitude smaller than the three-nucleon-force effect.
- The coupled-channel calculations cover two-nucleon reactions (NN → NN, NN → dπ, NN → NNπ, dπ → dπ, dπ → NNπ, dπ → NN) up to 0.5 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- The space-star kinematics puzzle at 13 MeV nucleon lab energy shows a sizable difference between pd and nd breakup data that theory cannot account for, with Coulomb effects being minor.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on microscopic nuclear structure that began with Brueckner theory (Brueckner, 1955) and the shell-model calculations of Kuo and Brown (1966). The author's early collaboration with Vary (Vary, 1973) and with Pradhan (Pradhan, 1972) on phase-equivalent off-shell variations is recalled as a precursor to modern approaches such as the "ab exitu" method (Shirokov, 2007) and the similarity renormalization group (Bogner, 2003). The work enables a unified framework for testing nuclear forces across few-nucleon bound states and reactions, while identifying persistent puzzles (such as the Ay spin observable and the space-star breakup discrepancy) that motivate further development of three-nucleon and many-nucleon force models.
