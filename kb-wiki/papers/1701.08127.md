# Irreducible 3-body forces contributions to the self-energy

> The inclusion of the three-nucleon forces (3NFs) in \textit{ab initio} many-body approaches is a formidable task, due to the computational load implied by the treatment of their matrix elements. For this reason, practical applications have mostly been limited to contributions where 3NFs enter as effective two-nucleon interactions. In this contribution, we derive the algebraic diagrammatic construction (ADC) working equations for a specific Feynman diagram of the self-energy that contains a fully irreducible three-nucleon force. This diagram is expected to be the most important among those previously neglected, because it connects dominant excited intermediate state configurations.

## Key claim

The paper derives the algebraic diagrammatic construction (ADC) working equations for a specific Feynman diagram of the self-energy that contains a fully irreducible three-nucleon force (3NF), which is expected to be the most important among previously neglected diagrams because it connects dominant excited intermediate state configurations, though the authors note that diagrams with 3p2h and 3h2p intermediate state configurations are energetically less favourable and expected to play a minor role at the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors apply the algebraic diagrammatic construction (ADC) formalism to the perturbative expansion of the irreducible self-energy in the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach, extending it to include interaction-irreducible three-nucleon forces. They derive explicit expressions for the coupling matrices M and N and interaction matrices C and D at second order (ADC(2)) for both effective two-nucleon forces and irreducible 3NFs, and outline the procedure for third-order (ADC(3)) contributions involving 2p1h and 2h1p intermediate state configurations. The Dyson equation is reformulated as an energy-independent Hermitian eigenvalue problem that can be solved using large-scale diagonalisation algorithms such as Lanczos or Arnoldi.

## Key numbers

* The coupling matrix for 2p1h intermediate states via effective 2NF is given by M^(I-2N)_{(n1 n2 k3) alpha} = (1/sqrt(2)) * X_mu^{n1} X_nu^{n2} Y_lambda^{k3} * tilde{V}_{munu,alpha lambda} (Eq. 13).
* The coupling matrix for 3p2h intermediate states via irreducible 3NF is given by M^(I-3N)_{(n1 n2 n3 k4 k5) alpha} = (1/sqrt(12)) * X_mu^{n1} X_nu^{n2} X_lambda^{n3} Y_rho^{k4} Y_eta^{k5} * W_{munulambda,alpha eta rho} (Eq. 15).
* The coupling matrix for 2h1p intermediate states via effective 2NF is N^(I-2N)_{alpha (k1 k2 n3)} = (1/sqrt(2)) * tilde{V}_{alpha lambda,munu} * Y_mu^{k1} Y_nu^{k2} X_lambda^{n3} (Eq. 14).
* The coupling matrix for 3h2p intermediate states via irreducible 3NF is N^(I-3N)_{alpha (k1 k2 k3 n4 n5)} = (1/sqrt(12)) * W_{alpha eta rho,munulambda} * Y_mu^{k1} Y_nu^{k2} Y_lambda^{k3} X_rho^{n4} X_eta^{n5} (Eq. 16).

## Context

This work extends the SCGF formalism for including 3NFs laid down in CarAr13, where treatment of 3NFs in terms of effective one- and two-nucleon forces was described along with Feynman rules, but the working equations for interaction-irreducible 3NFs had not been investigated previously. The ADC method was originally developed in quantum chemistry by Schirmer (1982, 1983) for the two-particle polarization propagator and one-body propagator of finite Fermi systems. This derivation enables future numerical implementations that include irreducible 3NF contributions in ab initio nuclear structure calculations, particularly for the dominant diagram among those previously neglected.
