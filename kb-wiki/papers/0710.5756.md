# Signals of the QGP phase transition - a view from microscopic transport models

> In this contribution the results from various transport models on different observables - considered as possible signals of the phase transition from hadronic matter to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) - are briefly reviewed.

## Key claim

The HSD and UrQMD transport models, which include only hadronic and string degrees of freedom, systematically underestimate the experimental maximum ("horn") of the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio at 20–30 A·GeV and the inverse slope parameters of kaons in central Au+Au collisions above ~5 A·GeV, suggesting that additional pressure from a pre-hadronic/partonic phase (the QGP) is required to explain the data.

## Method

The author reviews results from the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) and UrQMD transport approaches, which employ hadronic and string degrees of freedom and are matched to reproduce nucleon-nucleon, meson-nucleon, and meson-meson cross section data. For charmonium, the HSD approach implements both a "threshold melting" scenario (where charmonia dissociate when local energy density exceeds a threshold) and a "comover absorption" scenario (where charmonia are suppressed by inelastic scattering with hadronic correlators), with the cross sections for charmonium recreation via $D + \bar{D} \to J/\Psi + meson$ fixed by detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy density for QGP formation is estimated at ~0.7–1 GeV/fm³ (Karsch).
- The HSD transport model misses the maximum in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio at 20–30 A·GeV by ~40%.
- The energy density in a central Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV reaches up to 30 GeV/fm³.
- Default threshold energy densities for charmonium dissociation in the "threshold melting" scenario are: $\varepsilon_1 = 16$ GeV/fm³ for $J/\Psi$, $\varepsilon_2 = 2$ GeV/fm³ for $\chi_c$, and $\varepsilon_3 = 2$ GeV/fm³ for $\Psi^\prime$.
- The energy density cut used in some comover scenarios is $\epsilon_{cut} = 1$ GeV/fm³.
- At RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV), at least 20% of $J/\Psi$ survive according to PHENIX data.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strangeness and charm observables as signals of the QGP phase transition, following predictions by Rafelski and Muller (1982) and Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999) for "kink," "horn," and "step" signatures. The HSD and UrQMD results are compared to NA49 experimental data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC, showing that purely hadronic transport models fail to reproduce the observed strangeness enhancement and charmonium suppression patterns. The findings suggest that pre-hadronic/partonic interactions in a strong QGP (sQGP) dominate the dynamics at RHIC energies, and that neither the "comover absorption" nor the "threshold melting" scenario alone can describe the centrality and rapidity dependence of $J/\Psi$ suppression, motivating further study of sQGP properties.
