# Effects of shape coexistence and configuration mixing on low-lying states in tellurium isotopes

> Low-energy quadrupole collective states in even-even tellurium (Te) isotopes are studied using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing. The corresponding Hamiltonian is determined by means of the microscopic nuclear structure calculations within the self-consistent mean-field method employing a given energy density functional and pairing interaction. Calculated low-energy levels for nonyrast states show a parabolic behavior characteristic of the shape-coexisting structure. The intruder prolate-shape configuration is shown to mix strongly with the normal oblate-shape configuration, and play an important role in determining the low-lying structure in the Te isotopes near the middle of the neutron major shell closures.

## Key claim

The intruder prolate-shape configuration mixes strongly with the normal oblate-shape configuration in tellurium isotopes near the middle of the neutron major shell closures, and this mixing plays an important role in determining the low-lying structure, with the calculated low-energy levels for nonyrast states showing a parabolic behavior characteristic of shape-coexisting structure.

## Method

The author performed constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) self-consistent mean-field calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to obtain potential energy surfaces for even-even \(^{108-126}\)Te isotopes. These microscopic inputs were then used to determine the parameters of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM2-CM) Hamiltonian, which includes normal (0p-0h) and intruder (2p-2h) boson configurations. The IBM2-CM Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the boson \(m\)-scheme basis to compute low-energy spectra and electromagnetic transition properties for \(^{114-122}\)Te, while standard IBM-2 calculations without configuration mixing were performed for the other isotopes.

## Key numbers

* The RHB-SCMF potential energy surfaces show an oblate global minimum at \(\beta\approx 0.15-0.2\) and a prolate or triaxial local minimum at \(\beta\approx 0.3\) in transitional nuclei \(^{114-120}\)Te.
* The calculated \(R_{4/2}=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) ratios take almost constant values \(R_{4/2}\approx2.3-2.4\) for \(^{114-126}\)Te, compared to experimental values \(R_{4/2}\approx2.0\).
* For \(^{108,110}\)Te, the IBM-2 calculation yields \(R_{4/2}\) ratios of 3.13 and 2.78 respectively, higher than experimental values of 2.06 and 2.13.
* The intruder configuration contributions to the \(0^+_2\) state wave function are 63% for \(^{114}\)Te, 81% for \(^{116}\)Te, 91% for \(^{118}\)Te, 86% for \(^{120}\)Te, and 96% for \(^{122}\)Te.
* The effective charges used in the IBM2-CM are \(e_{\nu,1}=e_{\pi,1}=0.085\) \(e\)b for the normal configuration and \(e_{\nu,3}=e_{\pi,3}=0.110\) \(e\)b for the intruder configuration.
* The effective \(g\) factors adopted are \(g_{\nu,1}=g_{\nu,3}=0.24\) \(\mu_N\) and \(g_{\pi,1}=g_{\pi,3}=0.5\) \(\mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends previous IBM2-CM studies on shape coexistence in Cd isotopes (Nomura 2018) and Sn isotopes (Spieker 2018, Petrache 2019, Ortner 2020) to the tellurium isotopic chain near the proton \(Z=50\) major shell closure. The results are compared with recent experimental data for \(^{116}\)Te (von Spee 2024) and \(^{118}\)Te (Mihai 2011), as well as with RHB+QCH calculations (Suzuki 2024) that used the same microscopic inputs. The study enables further theoretical investigations into regions of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient \(Z>50\) nuclei and addresses the open question of developing a consistent way to derive the energy offset parameter \(\Delta\) that simultaneously reproduces both potential energy surfaces and spectroscopic properties in the IBM2-CM mapping.
