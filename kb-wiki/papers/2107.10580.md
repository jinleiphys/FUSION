# Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock Theory in Infinite Nuclear Matter

> On the way of a microscopic derivation of covariant density functionals, the first complete solution of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations is presented for symmetric nuclear matter. In most of the earlier investigations, the $G$-matrix is calculated only in the space of positive energy solutions. On the other side, for the solution of the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) equations, also the elements of this matrix connecting positive and negative energy solutions are required. So far, in the literature, these matrix elements are derived in various approximations. We discuss solutions of the Thompson equation for the full Dirac space and compare the resulting equation of state with those of earlier attempts in this direction.

## Key claim

The first complete solution of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) equations in full Dirac space for symmetric nuclear matter is presented, including matrix elements connecting positive and negative energy states that were previously approximated. The resulting equation of state agrees with earlier approximate treatments below saturation density but deviates above it, with the discrepancy attributed to different schemes for the starting energy in the Bethe-Goldstone equation.

## Method

The authors solved the Thompson equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, for the G-matrix in full Dirac space using the relativistic bare nucleon-nucleon potential Bonn A. The self-consistent RBHF problem was solved iteratively: for each density characterized by the Fermi momentum k_F, the G-matrix was determined from the Bethe-Goldstone equation, then used to construct the relativistic Hartree-Fock single-particle potential, which in turn enters the Thompson equation. The Pauli operator allowed only scattering into unoccupied states above the Fermi momentum, and the starting energy W was treated with negative energy states considered as occupied, following the procedure of Shen (2018).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for RBHF Bonn A: 0.188 fm^{-3}, with binding energy per nucleon -15.40 MeV, compression modulus 258 MeV, and Dirac mass M_D*/M = 0.55
- For RBHF Bonn B: saturation density 0.164 fm^{-3}, binding energy -13.36 MeV, compression modulus 206 MeV, Dirac mass 0.61
- For RBHF Bonn C: saturation density 0.144 fm^{-3}, binding energy -12.09 MeV, compression modulus 150 MeV, Dirac mass 0.65
- Non-relativistic BHF with Bonn A gives saturation density 0.428 fm^{-3} and binding energy -23.55 MeV, compared to empirical values of 0.16 ± 0.01 fm^{-3} and -16 ± 1 MeV
- Single-particle potentials U_S, U_0, and U_V were computed at saturation density k_F = 1.34 fm^{-1} as a function of momentum; the momentum-independence approximation shows uncertainties exceeding 100 MeV for the single-particle potentials

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach by providing the first full solution including negative energy states, which were previously treated in various approximations such as the Green's function method of Huber (1995), the momentum-independence approximation of Brockmann (1990), and the projection methods of Horowitz (1987) and Gross-Boelting (1999). The results enable future investigations of asymmetric nuclear matter in full Dirac space to clarify the isospin dependence of single-particle potentials, and the authors note that going beyond the Brueckner approximation (including more than two hole lines) may be necessary to resolve ambiguities in the starting energy prescription.
