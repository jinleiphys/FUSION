# Three-nucleon lepton-number-violating potentials in chiral EFT and their matrix elements in light nuclei

> We derive the three-nucleon neutrinoless double beta decay potential in $Δ$-full chiral effective field theory through next-to-next-to-next-to leading order in Weinberg's power counting. The matrix elements of the resulting operators are computed in light nuclei using Variational Monte Carlo with wave functions constructed from the Norfolk family of nuclear interactions. We find that three-nucleon corrections induce a modest quenching of the total nuclear matrix elements. We discuss model dependencies and the potential impact of these corrections on the sensitivity of experimental programs to probe lepton number violating parameters. These results provide a benchmark of many-body methods capable of reaching heavier nuclei of experimental interest.

## Key claim
Three-nucleon lepton-number-violating potentials in neutrinoless double beta decay, derived through next-to-next-to-next-to leading order in chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta resonances, induce a modest quenching of total nuclear matrix elements in light nuclei, but the size of this quenching depends on the power counting scheme used (Weinberg versus Friar) and on model dependencies in the short-range couplings.

## Method
The authors derived the three-nucleon neutrino potential for neutrinoless double beta decay using chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta isobars, working through next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N3LO) in Weinberg's power counting. They computed the matrix elements of these operators in light nuclei (A=6, 8, 12) using Variational Monte Carlo methods with wave functions from the Norfolk family of nuclear interactions, and compared results with and without the three-nucleon corrections.

## Key numbers
- The three-nucleon corrections induce a "modest quenching" of the total nuclear matrix elements relative to two-nucleon contributions (specific quenching percentages not provided in the truncated text)
- The nucleon axial coupling is g_A = 1.27, while the Delta-nucleon axial coupling is h_A = 1.40 ± 0.05 and the Delta axial coupling is g_1 = 2.32 ± 0.26, based on large N_c relations
- The pion decay constant is F_pi = 92.3 MeV, and the chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi ~ 4 pi F_pi ~ 1 GeV
- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is Delta, and the LECs c_3 and c_4 are related to Delta contributions as c_3^Delta = -2c_4^Delta = -4h^2_A/(9 Delta)

## Context
This work extends previous chiral EFT derivations of the two-nucleon neutrino potential (Cirigliano et al., 2017-2022) to include three-nucleon contributions that first appear at N2LO in Weinberg's power counting. It addresses the long-standing problem of "quenching" the axial coupling g_A in beta decay calculations, which has been studied by Chou (1993), Wildenthal (1983), and Martinez-Pinedo (1996), and more recently in the context of neutrinoless double beta decay by Jokiniemi (2022) and Coello Perez (2018). The results provide benchmarks for many-body methods that can reach the heavier nuclei of experimental interest, such as 76Ge, 136Xe, and 130Te, where current experiments (GERDA, LEGEND, KamLAND-Zen, CUORE) have set half-life limits at the level of 10^24-10^26 years.
