# The role of quark mass in cold and dense perturbative QCD

> We consider the equation of state of QCD at high density and zero temperature in perturbation theory to first order in the coupling constant $α_s$. We compute the thermodynamic potential including the effect of a non-vanishing mass for the strange quark and show that corrections are sizable. Renormalization group running of the coupling and the strange quark mass plays a crucial role. The structure of quark stars is dramatically modified.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including a non-zero strange quark mass with renormalization-group running produces sizable corrections to the equation of state of cold dense QCD, even at first order in α_s, and that these corrections dramatically modify the mass-radius relation for quark stars, though the authors caution that their numerical results are only suggestive and will be modified by higher-order corrections and a more realistic hadronic matching.

## Method
The authors compute the thermodynamic potential for cold quark matter at zero temperature to first order in α_s in the MS-bar scheme, treating up and down quarks as massless and the strange quark as massive. They include renormalization-group running of both α_s and the strange quark mass using known 4-loop coefficients, impose beta equilibrium and electric charge neutrality with electrons, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain the mass-radius relation for quark stars.

## Key numbers
- At μ = 600 MeV (where α_s ~ 0.43), including a fixed strange quark mass decreases the thermodynamic potential by ~15%; the running of the mass brings an additional ~8% decrease.
- For massless strange quarks, the effective bag constant is B_eff^(1/4) ≃ 117 MeV with a ≃ 2.81; including the running strange quark mass raises these to B_eff^(1/4) ≃ 137 MeV and a ≃ 3.17.
- The most massive star for the m_s = 0 equation of state has M/M_⊙ ≃ 3.2 and radius ~17 km with central baryon density n_B ≃ 0.5 fm^(-3); for the massive equation of state, the heaviest star has M/M_⊙ ≃ 2.16 at ~12 km with n_B ≃ 0.83 fm^(-3) (at μ = 470 MeV).
- The strange quark mass reaches m_s ~ 137 MeV at μ = 500 MeV.
- The running coupling and mass are fixed by requiring α_s ≃ 0.3 and m_s ≃ 100 MeV at Λ-bar = 2 GeV, yielding Λ_MS-bar ≃ 380 MeV and invariant mass m̂_s ≃ 262 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state for cold dense matter, which had mostly neglected quark masses (Fraga 2001, Fraga 2001) or used fixed masses in a different renormalization scheme (strangematter). It also contrasts with bag-model approaches (Witten 1984, bag) that estimated finite mass effects to be less than 5%. The results enable more realistic modeling of quark star structure and are consistent with gravitational redshift constraints from EXO 0748-676 (Cottam 2002). The authors note that full α_s^2 corrections and matching to a hadronic equation of state will be needed for quantitative predictions.
