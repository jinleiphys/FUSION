# Correlations among neutron-proton and neutron-deuteron elastic scattering observables

> We employ two models of the nucleon-nucleon force: the OPE-Gaussian as well as the chiral N4LO and N4LO+ interactions with semilocal regularization in momentum space to study correlations among two-nucleon and three-nucleon elastic scattering observables. These models contain a number of free parameters whose values and covariance matrices are evaluated from a fit to the two-nucleon data. Such detailed knowledge of parameters allows us to create, using various sets of statistically generated parameters, numerous versions of these potentials and next apply them to two- and three-nucleon scattering to make predictions of various observables at the reaction energies up to 200 MeV. This permits a systematic analysis of correlations among two-nucleon and three-nucleon observables, basing on a relatively big sample of predictions. We found that most observables in neutron-proton and neutron-deuteron systems are uncorrelated, but there are exceptions revealing strong correlations, which depend on the reaction energy and scattering angle. This information may be useful for precise fixing free parameters of two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces and for understanding dependencies and correlations between potential parameters and observables.

## Key claim

The paper finds that most neutron-proton and neutron-deuteron elastic scattering observables are uncorrelated, but there are exceptions showing strong correlations that depend on the reaction energy and scattering angle; this information may be useful for precisely fixing free parameters of two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, though the authors caution that uncertainties in correlation coefficients are relatively high, especially for small values.

## Method

The authors used two models of the nucleon-nucleon force (the OPE-Gaussian and the chiral N4LO and N4LO+ interactions with semilocal regularization in momentum space) whose free parameters and covariance matrices were evaluated from fits to two-nucleon data. They generated 50 sets of statistically sampled parameters from the multivariate normal distribution for each potential, then solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-nucleon scattering and the Faddeev equation for three-nucleon scattering to compute observables at energies up to 200 MeV. Correlation coefficients between pairs of observables were calculated using the standard sample estimator, with uncertainties estimated via Fisher transformation and bootstrap resampling.

## Key numbers

* Correlation coefficient between differential cross section and asymmetry A' in np scattering at Elab = 10 MeV: r = 0.99 at theta_cm = 90 degrees for the chiral N4LO SMS force (strong positive correlation)
* Correlation coefficient between differential cross section and asymmetry A' in np scattering at Elab = 135 MeV: r values around 0.24-0.35 at various angles (weak correlation)
* Correlation coefficient between neutron analyzing power Ay(n) and deuteron vector analyzing power iT11 in nd scattering at Elab = 13 MeV: r = 0.95 at theta_cm = 30 degrees for chiral N4LO SMS force (strong correlation)
* Correlation coefficient between neutron analyzing power Ay(n) and deuteron vector analyzing power iT11 in nd scattering at Elab = 135 MeV: r = 0.99 at theta_cm = 30 degrees for chiral N4LO SMS force (strong correlation)
* Half confidence interval at gamma = 0.9 for correlation coefficients: approximately 0.05 for r approximately 0.9, and approximately 0.25 for r approximately 0.25

## Context

This work extends the approach of Skibinski (2018) and Volkotrub (2020), who first determined theoretical uncertainties for elastic nucleon-deuteron observables using covariance matrices of potential parameters. It goes beyond earlier correlation studies such as Perez (2016), which focused on bound-state binding energies (the Tjon line), and Kirscher (2010), which studied correlations in pionless Effective Field Theory at very low energies. The results enable more informed selection of observables for fixing the thirteen new free parameters expected for three-nucleon forces at N4LO, as discussed by the Pisa group, and could help avoid biases from using strongly correlated observables in parameter determination.
