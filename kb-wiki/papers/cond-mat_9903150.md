# An SU(4) Approach of High-Temperature Superconductivity and Antiferromagnetism

> We present an SU(4) model of high-$T_c$ superconductivity. One dynamical symmetry of this model corresponds to the previously proposed SO(5) model for unification of superconductivity and antiferromagnetism, but there are two additional dynamical symmetries: SO(4), associated with antiferromagnetic order and SU(2), associated with a D-wave pairing condensate. These provide a 3-phase microscopic model of high-$T_c$ superconductivity and permit a clear understanding of the role played by the SO(5) symmetry.

# An SU(4) Approach to High-Temperature Superconductivity and Antiferromagnetism

## Key claim

The paper proposes that high-temperature superconductivity in the cuprates results from a U(4) symmetry realized dynamically, with the previously proposed SO(5) model appearing as a subgroup of this larger algebra; however, the SU(4) model contains two additional dynamical symmetries (SO(4) and SU(2)) that correspond to distinct phases, and the SO(5) symmetry is interpreted not as a direct unification of superconductivity and antiferromagnetism but as a "spin-glass" phase that interpolates between them over a range of doping.

## Method

The authors construct a U(4) Lie algebra from bilinear fermion operators that include singlet D-wave pairs, triplet pi-pairs, spin operators, and staggered magnetization operators, then identify three dynamical symmetry chains: SU(2) associated with superconductivity, SO(4) associated with antiferromagnetic order, and SO(5) associated with a spin-glass phase. They build a model Hamiltonian from linear combinations of Casimir operators for all subgroups and analyze the ground states in each symmetry limit using the collective subspace of SU(4) irreps labeled by (Omega/2, 0, 0), which reduces the full 2^(2Omega)-dimensional space to a subspace scaling as Omega^4.

## Key numbers

- The collective subspace dimension for irreps (Omega/2, 0, 0) is (1/12)(Omega/2 + 1)(Omega/2 + 2)^2(Omega/2 + 3), corresponding to a truncation factor of 10^54 for Omega ~ 100.
- In the SU(2) (superconducting) symmetry limit, the pairing gap Delta = (1/2) Omega (1 - x^2)^(1/2), where x = 1 - n/Omega is the doping parameter.
- In the SO(4) (antiferromagnetic) symmetry limit, the staggered magnetization Q = (1/2) Omega (1 - x) = n/2 at half filling, and the pairing gap Delta = (1/2) Omega (x(1 - x))^(1/2), which is zero at half filling.
- In the SO(4) limit, the energy minimum lies at beta = [(1 - x)/4]^(1/2), where beta is related to the antiferromagnetic order parameter.
- The SU(4) Casimir operator expectation value for the collective subspace is constant: <C_su(4)> = (Omega/2)(Omega/2 + 4).

## Context

This work extends the SO(5) model of Zhang (1997) for unifying superconductivity and antiferromagnetism by embedding it in a larger U(4) fermion algebra, following the dynamical symmetry approach developed in nuclear structure physics by Wu (1994) and the interacting boson model. The paper provides a microscopic foundation for the SO(5) symmetry and shows that the SO(5) phase acts as a doorway between the SU(2) superconducting phase and the SO(4) antiferromagnetic phase, with zero-temperature phase transitions controlled by doping. The model enables a natural explanation of the cuprate phase diagram and predicts the appearance of pseudogaps in the underdoped regime, to be discussed in a separate paper by Sun (1999).
