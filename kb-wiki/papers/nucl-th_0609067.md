# Modern compact star observations and the quark matter equation of state

> We present a hybrid equation of state (EoS) for dense matter that satisfies phenomenological constraints from modern compact star (CS) observations which indicate high maximum masses (M = 2 M_sun) and large radii (R> 12 km). The corresponding isospin symmetric EoS is consistent with flow data analyses of heavy-ion collisions and a deconfinement transition at approx. 0.55 fm^{-3}. The quark matter phase is described by a 3-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model that accounts for scalar diquark condensation and vector meson interactions while the nuclear matter phase is obtained within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach using the Bonn-A potential. We demonstrate that both pure neutron stars and neutron stars with quark matter cores (QCSs) are consistent with modern CS observations. Hybrid star configurations with a CFL quark core are unstable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hybrid stars (neutron stars with a quark matter core) can satisfy all modern compact star observational constraints, including high masses (around 2 solar masses) and large radii (above 12 km), provided the quark matter equation of state is stiffened by a vector meson interaction channel. The authors qualify that this conclusion depends on tuning the diquark and vector coupling parameters within ranges that also satisfy heavy-ion flow constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state using a two-phase description with a Maxwell construction for the phase transition. The nuclear matter phase is described by the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with the Bonn-A potential, while the quark matter phase is modeled by a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that includes scalar diquark condensation, vector meson interactions, and isospin-symmetric constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data. The free parameters (vector coupling eta_V and diquark coupling eta_D) are tuned to satisfy both the flow constraint for symmetric matter and the mass-radius constraints from compact star observations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of hybrid star configurations reaches 2.1 solar masses for vector coupling eta_V = 0.50
- Deconfinement phase transition occurs at approximately 0.55 fm^{-3}
- Critical star masses for onset of quark matter core range from 1.35 solar masses (eta_D = 1.02) to 1.0 solar masses (eta_D = 1.03)
- Direct Urca threshold in the DBHF hadronic EoS occurs at n_DU = 0.375 fm^{-3}, corresponding to a compact star mass of M_DU = 1.26 solar masses
- Nuclear matter compressibility K = 230 MeV for the DBHF EoS
- Pulsar J0751+1807 initially reported at M = 2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses (later corrected to 1.26 solar masses in a note added in proof)
- RX J1856 radius constraint: R > 12 km, with radius exceeding 13.5 km for masses below 1.4 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the hybrid star masquerade concept of Alford (2004) by replacing the APR hadronic EoS with the ab initio DBHF approach, which yields larger radii and masses. The paper contradicts earlier claims by Ozel (2006) and Trumper (2003) that deconfined quark matter cannot exist in compact stars with observed high masses and radii. The results enable future studies using more realistic QCD-based approaches such as Dyson-Schwinger equations, and suggest that moment of inertia measurements for massive objects (around 2.0 solar masses) with 10% precision could distinguish pure neutron stars from hybrid stars.
