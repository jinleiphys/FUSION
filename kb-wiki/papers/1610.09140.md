# Benchmark calculation of p-3H and n-3He scattering

> p-3H and n-3He scattering in the energy range above the n-3He but below the d-d thresholds is studied by solving the 4-nucleon problem with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. Three different methods -- Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas, Hyperspherical Harmonics, and Faddeev-Yakubovsky -- have been employed and their results for both elastic and charge-exchange processes are compared. We observe a good agreement between the three different methods, thus the obtained results may serve as a benchmark. A comparison with the available experimental data is also reported and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three different theoretical methods (AGS, HH, and FY) produce results in good agreement for p-³H and n-³He scattering in the energy range above the n-³He threshold but below the d-d threshold, establishing a benchmark for four-nucleon scattering calculations. However, the abstract's claim of "good agreement" is qualified by the observation that larger differences (up to 10%) are found for inelasticity parameters, particularly due to slow convergence within the HH method.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using three distinct techniques: the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space with screening and renormalization for Coulomb interactions, the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) expansion method with the Kohn variational principle, and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations in configuration space with Lagrange-mesh basis functions. All calculations employed the N3LO500 chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon potential, and results were compared for elastic and charge-exchange processes at neutron laboratory energies of 1, 2, and 3.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- n-³He inelasticity parameter η^(0-) = 0.553 and phase-shift δ^(0-) = -10.5 degrees at E_n = 1.0 MeV, obtained with the AGS method using the N3LO500 potential
- n-³He inelasticity parameter η^(0-) = 0.505 and phase-shift δ^(0-) = -10.5 degrees at E_n = 1.0 MeV, obtained with the HH method using ℓ_max = 5
- n-³He inelasticity parameter η^(0+) = 0.603 and phase-shift δ^(0+) = +21.9 degrees at E_n = 1.0 MeV, obtained with the HH method using ℓ_max = 1
- The numerical uncertainty of HH-calculated phase-shifts from numerical integration is around 0.1%, and the uncertainty from basis truncation is estimated to be at most 0.5%
- The partial wave expansion for FY equations was truncated by imposing max(l_x, l_y, l_z) ≤ 4
- The HH basis was truncated to include states with ℓ_max around 5 or 6 and K ≤ K_max

## Context

This work extends previous benchmark calculations for n-³H and p-³He elastic scattering (Deltuva et al., 2011) to the more complex case of p-³H and n-³He scattering where both channels are coupled and involve total isospin T=0 and T=1 states. The results provide a set of converged reference values that can serve as benchmarks for future four-nucleon scattering calculations, including tests of new chiral effective field theory potentials and comparisons with experimental data accumulated over the last 50 years.
