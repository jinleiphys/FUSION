# Search for Production of Strangelets in Quark Matter using Particle Correlations

> We present a new technique for observing the strangelet production in quark matter based on unlike particle correlations. A simulation is presented with a two-phase thermodynamical model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that unlike-particle correlations (specifically \(K^+K^-\) pairs) can serve as a novel observable for the transient existence of strange quark matter droplets (strangelets) in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, even if those droplets decay on strong interaction time scales. The claim is conditional on the bag model constant \(B^{1/4}\) being sufficiently small (below about 180 MeV) to allow strangeness distillation and the formation of metastable or stable strangelets.

## Method

The authors simulate the dynamical evolution of a mixed quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron gas phase using a two-phase thermodynamical model that enforces Gibbs equilibrium conditions at the phase boundary and incorporates non-equilibrium hadron evaporation from the fireball surface. They then feed the predicted time-dependent particle yields (especially \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) emission times and source charges) into a quantum two-particle final-state interaction code, including three-body Coulomb effects, to compute the ratio of correlation functions \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) for \(K^+K^-\) pairs selected by the sign of \(\vec{k}\cdot\vec{v}\).

## Key numbers

- For set 1 (bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 160\) MeV, representing Au+Au at AGS energies), the ratio \((1+R^+)/(1+R^-)\) shows a \(30-35\%\) dip relative to equal mean time production, extending up to \(q \approx 30\) MeV/c.
- For set 2 (bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 235\) MeV, same initial conditions), a weaker effect of \(10\%\) is found, extending up to \(q \approx 40\) MeV/c.
- For set 3 (bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 235\) MeV, representing S+Au at SPS energies with initial mass \(A=100\) and entropy per baryon \(S/A=45\)), the effect is nearly gone.
- The quark chemical potential \(\mu_q\) decreases from about 115 MeV to about 15 MeV during hadronization; the strange quark chemical potential \(\mu_s\) increases from 0 MeV to several tens of MeV.
- For low bag constants, the strangelet emerges with a radius of about 2.5 fm and a negative charge-to-mass ratio (\(Z/A = -0.1\) for stable, \(Z/A = -0.45\) for metastable droplets).
- The strangeness fraction \(f_s\) attained is about 1.5–2.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions of strangelet stability by Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) and the MIT bag model calculations of Chin and Kerman (1979), as well as the strangeness distillation concept of Greiner et al. (1987). It provides a new experimental observable (unlike-particle correlations) that complements direct strangelet searches at the AGS (E864, E878) and CERN SPS (NA52). The technique builds on prior experimental demonstrations of time-ordering in (p,d) correlations at GANIL by Ghi et al. (1993), Era et al. (1996), Gourio et al. (1996), and Nouais et al. (1997), and on the quantum correlation formalism of Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1982, 1996). The method enables future combined studies with identical-particle interferometry (HBT) and hydrodynamical or microscopic (UrQMD) simulations to probe the space-time expansion of the QGP phase.
