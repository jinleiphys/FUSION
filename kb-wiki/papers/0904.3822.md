# Quantum phase transitional patterns in the SD-pair shell model

> Patterns of shape-phase transition in the proton-neutron coupled systems are studied within the $SD$-pair shell model. The results show that some transitional patterns in the $SD$-pair shell model are similar to the $U(5)-SU(3)$, $U(5)-SO(6)$ transitions with signatures of the critical point symmetry of the interacting boson model.

## Key claim

Patterns of vibration-rotation and vibration-γ-soft shape phase transitions in the proton-neutron coupled SD-pair shell model (SDPSM) are similar to the corresponding U(5)-SU(3) and U(5)-SO(6) transitions in the interacting boson model (IBM), with signatures of the X(5)-like and E(5)-like critical point symmetries also appearing in the fermionic model.

## Method

The authors used the SD-pair shell model with a schematic Hamiltonian combining monopole pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions for both identical nucleons and between protons and neutrons. They studied a system with Nπ = Nν = 3 in the gds shell, varying a control parameter α from 0 (vibrational U(5) limit) to 1 (either SU(3) rotational or SO(6) γ-soft limit), and computed energy ratios, D-pair number operator expectation values, and B(E2) ratios as effective order parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the vibration-rotation transition: energy ratio R42 = E4₁⁺/E2₁⁺ = 2 at α=0 (vibrational) and 3.33 at α=1 (rotational), with rapid change in the region 0.3 ≤ α ≤ 0.6 indicating a first-order phase transition.
- At the X(5)-like critical point (α=0.54): R42 = 2.91, R60 = E6₁⁺/E0₂⁺ = 1.05, and E0₂⁺/E2₁⁺ = 5.32, close to the IBM results of 2.91, 1.0, and 5.67 respectively.
- For the vibration-γ-soft transition: R42 = 2 at α=0 and 2.47 at α=1, with smooth variation across the transition.
- At the E(5)-like critical point (α=0.54): E4₁⁺/E2₁⁺ = 2.19, matching the IBM value of 2.19 for N=5; E0₂⁺/E2₁⁺ = 2.59 (IBM: 3.68).
- Rotational limit energy ratios: E4₁⁺/E2₁⁺ = 3.33 and E6₁⁺/E2₁⁺ = 6.96, close to the IBM results of 3.33 and 7.

## Context

This work extends previous SDPSM studies that reproduced vibrational, rotational, and γ-soft limiting spectra (Luo et al., 2005) and vibration-rotation phase transitions for identical nucleon systems (Luo et al., 2006) to the more realistic proton-neutron coupled case. The results parallel those from the IBM (Iachello, 1987) and its proton-neutron extension (IBM-II), and show that the fermionic SDPSM can reproduce the X(5) critical point symmetry (Iachello, 2001) and E(5) symmetry (Iachello, 2000) previously identified in bosonic models. This enables future studies of quantum phase transitions in other fermion systems using the SD-pair truncation.
