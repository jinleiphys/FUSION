# The Inverse Amplitude Method and Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory applied to pion-nucleon scattering

> We report on our present work, where by means of the Inverse Amplitude Method we unitarize the elastic pion nucleon scattering amplitudes of Heavy Barion Chiral Perturbation Theory at O(q^3). We reproduce the scattering up to the inelastic thresholds including the Delta(1232) resonance. The fitted chiral constants are rather different from those obtained by fitting the extrapolated threshold parameters for the non-unitarized theory.

# The Inverse Amplitude Method and Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory applied to pion-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

The Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) applied to Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) at O(q^3) can reproduce pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts up to the first inelastic threshold, including the Delta(1232) resonance as a dynamically generated pole, but the fitted chiral constants differ substantially from those obtained by fitting extrapolated threshold parameters in the non-unitarized theory.

## Method

The authors unitarize the elastic pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes from HBChPT at O(q^3) using the Inverse Amplitude Method, which enforces exact elastic unitarity by constructing a Pade approximant of the perturbative series. They fit the nine relevant chiral parameters (four at O(q^2) and five at O(q^3)) to low-energy phase shift data, rather than to extrapolated threshold parameters as in previous HBChPT fits. The resulting amplitude is then used to search for the Delta(1232) pole on the second Riemann sheet.

## Key numbers

- The Delta(1232) resonance pole is found on the second Riemann sheet at sqrt(s) = 1209 - i 46 MeV.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi = 4 pi f_pi approximately 1.2 GeV.
- The IAM fit reproduces phase shifts up to at least the first inelastic threshold, with some channels described even up to 320 MeV of center-of-mass energy.
- The fitted chiral constants from the IAM (e.g., a_1 = 9.1, a_2 = -8.7, a_3 = 0.75, a_5 = 27.76) differ markedly from the HBChPT threshold fit values (e.g., a_1 = -2.6, a_2 = 1.4, a_3 = -1, a_5 = 3.3).

## Context

This work extends the successful application of the Inverse Amplitude Method from meson-meson scattering (IAM, using O(p^4) ChPT, which reproduced the sigma, f_0, a_0, rho, kappa, K*, and octet phi resonances up to about 1.2 GeV) to the baryon sector. It contrasts with previous O(q^3) HBChPT calculations by Mojzis (1998) and Meissner (1998), which fit threshold parameters and showed slow convergence with contributions from different orders frequently comparable. The approach also differs from other O(q^3) calculations that include an explicit Delta(1232) field and reproduce phase shifts up to E_cm approximately 100 MeV; here the Delta is generated dynamically without explicit resonance fields. The results enable further study of whether the Delta should be included as an explicit degree of freedom in the Lagrangian and suggest that the strong dependence of chiral parameters on the fitted observables and energy range, noted by Fettes and Meissner (1998), persists in the unitarized approach.
