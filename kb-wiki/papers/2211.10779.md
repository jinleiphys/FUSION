# Seebeck and Nernst coefficients of a magnetized hot QCD medium with number conserving kernel

> We have studied the thermoelectric response of a hot and magnetized QCD medium created in the noncentral events at heavy-ion collider experiments. The collisional aspects of the medium have been embedded in the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation (RBTE) using Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision integral, which insures the particle number conservation, unlike the commonly used relaxation time approximation (RTA). We have incorporated the thermal medium effects in the guise of a quasiparticle model, where the interaction among the partons is embodied in the medium dependent mass of the quark, which has been evaluated using the framework of the perturbative thermal QCD with a magnetic field in the background. In the absence of $B$, the Seebeck coefficient for individual quark flavors gets slightly reduced in the BGK term in comparison to naive RTA, while it gets enhanced for the composite partonic medium. In the presence of strong magnetic field ($B$), the BGK term enhances the Seebeck coefficient for the individual flavors as well as that for medium. The medium Seebeck coefficient rises with the strength of quark chemical potential ($μ$) in the absence as well as that in the strong $B$. We observe chirality dependence in the Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in the weak magnetic field as the degeneracy in the masses of left- and right-handed chiral modes gets lifted in the weak $B$. Seebeck coefficient is larger for left-handed modes contrary to the Nernst coefficient which is higher for the right-handed modes. Seebeck coefficient is almost similar in the weak field for both the collision term, while Nernst coefficient gets reduced (enhanced) for $L$ ($R$) modes considerably in the BGK collision term.

## Key claim

The BGK collision integral, which conserves particle number unlike the commonly used relaxation time approximation (RTA), modifies the Seebeck and Nernst coefficients of a hot QCD medium in a manner that depends on magnetic field strength and quark chirality, with the direction of modification (enhancement or reduction) varying between individual quark flavors and the composite medium.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation with a BGK collision integral for a quasiparticle model of hot QCD matter, where medium effects enter through temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent quark masses computed from the Dyson-Schwinger equation in perturbative thermal QCD. They calculated the Seebeck coefficient in the absence of magnetic field and in the strong field regime (lowest Landau level approximation), and both Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in the weak field regime where chirality dependence arises from nondegenerate left- and right-handed mode masses. Results were compared with those obtained using the standard relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field at RHIC is around \(m_\pi^2\) and at LHC around \(15m_\pi^2\), as cited from prior work.
- The running coupling \(\alpha_s(T)\) is given by \(\alpha_s(T) = 6\pi / [(33-2N_f) \ln(Q/\Lambda_{QCD})]\) with \(Q = 2\pi\sqrt{T^2 + \mu^2/\pi^2}\).
- The relaxation time is \(\tau_i(T) = 1 / [5.1 T \alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s) (1+0.12(2N_f+1))]\).
- The thermal mass in the absence of magnetic field is \(m_{iT}^2 = g'^2 T^2 (1 + \mu^2/(\pi^2 T^2))/6\).
- The quark mass in strong magnetic field is \(m_{i,B}^2 = (g^2 |q_i B|/(3\pi^2)) [\pi T/(2m_{i,0}) - \ln(2) + 7\mu^2 \zeta(3)/(8\pi^2 T^2) - 31\mu^4 \zeta(5)/(32\pi^4 T^4)]\).
- In the weak field, left- and right-handed mode masses are \(m_L^2 = m_{th}^2 + 4g^2 C_F M^2\) and \(m_R^2 = m_{th}^2 - 4g^2 C_F M^2\), where \(M^2(T,\mu,B) = (|q_i B|/(16\pi^2))(\pi T/(2m_{i0}) - \ln 2 + 7\mu^2\zeta(3)/(8\pi^2 T^2))\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermoelectric transport in hot QCD matter, which had used the relaxation time approximation (RTA) that violates particle number conservation, such as those by Das (2020), Kurian (2021), Zhang (2021), and Dey (2020, 2021). The BGK collision integral was previously applied to study dielectric functions and dispersion relations in electromagnetic plasmas by Carrington (2004), collective modes in anisotropic QCD by Schenke (2006), and electrical conductivity in magnetized QCD by Khan (2021). The present results enable more realistic modeling of charge and heat transport in the quark-gluon plasma produced in noncentral heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic fields and thermal gradients coexist.
