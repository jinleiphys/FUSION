# Pion transverse momentum spectrum, elliptic flow and interferometry in the granular source model for RHIC and LHC heavy ion collisions

> We systematically investigate the pion transverse momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry in the granular source model for the heavy ion collisions of Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2.76 TeV with different centralities. The granular source model can well reproduce the experimental results of the heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We examine the parameters involved in the granular source model. The experimental data of the momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and HBT radii for the two collision energies and different centralities impose very strict constraints on the model parameters. They exhibit certain regularities for collision centrality and energy. The space-time structure and expansion velocities of the granular sources for the heavy ion collisions at the RHIC and LHC energies with different centralities are investigated.

## Key claim

The granular source model can simultaneously reproduce experimental data for pion transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii for Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC across different centralities, but only when the model parameters are tightly constrained by combining all three observables; the model shows limitations at high transverse momentum (above 2.5 GeV/c) where elliptic flow results exceed experimental data.

## Method

The authors modeled heavy ion collisions as a superposition of independently evolving QGP droplets initially distributed within a cylinder, with position-dependent initial velocities parameterized by magnitude and exponent parameters in transverse and longitudinal directions. They performed hydrodynamic evolution of each droplet using the S95p-PCE165-v0 equation of state, with pions emitted from droplet surfaces at freeze-out temperatures distributed between 80 MeV and the chemical freeze-out temperature of 165 MeV. The eight free model parameters (three source geometry parameters and five droplet velocity parameters) were determined by simultaneously fitting experimental data for pion transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV with 0-5%, 10-20%, and 30-50% centralities, and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with 10-20% and 40-50% centralities.

## Key numbers

- Source geometry parameters for RHIC 0-5% centrality: R_T = 5.8 fm, Delta R_T = 0.7 fm, R_z = 3.9 fm
- Source geometry parameters for LHC 10-20% centrality: R_T = 6.0 fm, Delta R_T = 0.9 fm, R_z = 5.5 fm
- Velocity parameter a_z is approximately 0.593 for RHIC and 0.590-0.592 for LHC, nearly independent of centrality and energy
- Transverse velocity parameter b_T ranges from 0.76 (RHIC 0-5%) to 0.23 (LHC 40-50%), decreasing with centrality
- Average emission time for RHIC central collision: 8.39 fm/c; for RHIC peripheral collision: 9.11 fm/c
- Average r_out values: 2.52 fm (RHIC central), 2.78 fm (RHIC peripheral), 2.64 fm (RHIC 10-20%), 3.59 fm (LHC 10-20%)
- Initial droplet energy density set to 2.2 GeV/fm^3 for all collisions; initial droplet radius standard deviation sigma_d = 2.5 fm
- Freeze-out parameters: f_dir = 0.75, Delta T_dir = 10 MeV, Delta T_dec = 90 MeV, T_chem = 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends the granular source model previously developed by Zhang et al. (2004, 2006, 2009) that explained HBT data for RHIC experiments, and earlier work by the same group (2011) that analyzed HBT for most central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. The paper systematically applies the model to multiple centrality bins at both RHIC and LHC energies, demonstrating that combined analysis of transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii imposes strict constraints on model parameters. The results enable future investigations of source space-time structure and expansion velocities as functions of collision centrality and energy, and reveal regularities in how model parameters scale with these collision conditions.
