# Effects of consistent and inconsistent isobar coupling in the nuclear medium

> We investigate effects of consistent (conserving the number of degrees of freedom) and inconsistent pion-nucleon-isobar couplings on the isobar propagator in vacuum and in nuclear medium. Using the consistent coupling in conjunction with a convenient basis leads to significant simplification of the isobar vacuum and in-medium self energy and dressed propagator compared to the case of inconsistent interaction. The higher-derivative nature of the consistent interaction requires a suitable compensation by an additional form-factor term or adjustment in the used cut-off values. This modification is straightforward to perform and assures that the physical observables connected to the spin-3/2 sector in vacuum and nuclear medium acquire values indistinguishable from the ones obtained by using an inconsistent coupling.

## Key claim

Using the consistent (gauge-invariant) pion-nucleon-isobar coupling in a convenient orthogonal basis leads to significant simplification of the isobar self-energy and dressed propagator compared to the inconsistent coupling, but the higher-derivative nature of the consistent interaction requires compensation via an additional form-factor term or adjustment of cut-off values to make physical observables indistinguishable from those obtained with the inconsistent coupling.

## Method

The author computed the complete relativistic isobar propagator in vacuum and in nuclear medium using both the inconsistent coupling (with off-shell parameter a) and the consistent coupling, based on the pion-nucleon loop. The propagator and self-energy were expanded in a 40-term orthogonal basis of projectors depending on the isobar four-momentum and the four-velocity of the nuclear medium. The vacuum and in-medium results were compared by examining the coefficients of the dominant helicity-3/2 and helicity-1/2 projectors, and the nuclear photoabsorption cross section was calculated using the magnetic dipole coupling for the gamma-N-Delta interaction.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant for consistent interaction: g^(con)_(pi N Delta)/M_Delta = 19 GeV^(-1) with cut-off Lambda = 0.97 GeV in the vacuum form-factor
- Additional form-factor cut-off: Lambda_c = 1.2 GeV used to compensate the higher-derivative nature of the consistent coupling
- In-medium calculations performed at 0.8 times saturation density with Fermi momentum k_F = 250 MeV
- Nucleon mean-field shifts: scalar Sigma_N^s = -0.28 GeV, vector Sigma_N^v = 0.232 GeV
- In-medium pion propagator form-factor cut-off: Lambda_pi = 1.2 GeV (dipole form)
- For consistent coupling with adjusted parameters: vacuum cut-off Lambda = 0.93 GeV, off-shell cut-off Lambda_pi = 1.45 GeV, isobar in-medium mass increased by 10 MeV
- Inconsistent coupling induces spin-1/2 terms in the vacuum propagator that are about two orders of magnitude smaller than the spin-3/2 term when the off-shell parameter a is of order unity

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Pascalutsa (1998) on gauge-invariant couplings for spin-3/2 fields and the basis introduced by Leupold and Korpa (2002) and used by Korpa and Doring (2004) for in-medium isobar calculations. The paper addresses the concern raised by Vrancx (2011) that the higher-derivative nature of consistent couplings introduces additional energy powers that could shift the mass peak of high-spin particles. The results enable straightforward correction of the consistent coupling to match observables from the widely used inconsistent coupling of Nath, Etemadi, and Kimel (1971), while maintaining the advantage of eliminating spurious spin-1/2 contributions in vacuum.
