# Numerical Implementation of Three-Body Forces in Bound State Faddeev Calculations in Three Dimensions

## Key claim
The Faddeev equations for the three-body bound state can be solved directly as three-dimensional integral equations using vector Jacobi momenta without partial wave decomposition, and this approach makes the incorporation of genuine three-body forces less cumbersome than in traditional partial-wave-based methods, especially on parallel architectures. The authors demonstrate numerical feasibility for a model with scalar forces, but the results are limited to spin-independent three-body forces of a simple Yukawa-exchange type.

## Method
The authors solve the Faddeev equation for three identical particles interacting via two-body forces of the Malfliet-Tjon type and a scalar-meson-exchange three-body force, working directly with the magnitudes of the Jacobi momenta p and q and the angle between them. The three-body force is decomposed into three symmetric parts W^(i), and its matrix elements are evaluated by interpreting it as two sequential interactions in different two-body subsystems, requiring three-dimensional interpolations using cubic Hermite splines. The bound state eigenvalue equation is solved iteratively using Lanczos-type techniques (method of iterated orthogonal eigenvectors) on a discretized grid of typical size 65×65×42.

## Key numbers
- Three-body binding energy with two-body forces only: E_t = 7.699 MeV
- Three-body binding energy with two-body forces plus three-body force: E_t = 8.590 MeV (close to the measured value)
- Three-body force parameters: g²_s/4π = 5.0, m_s = 305.8593 MeV, Λ_s = 1000.0 MeV, a_s = -1.73
- Two-body force parameters: attractive piece g²_a/4π = -3.5775, m_a = 330.2104 MeV, Λ_a = 1500 MeV; repulsive piece g²_r/4π = 9.4086, m_r = 612.4801 MeV, Λ_r = 1500 MeV
- Typical grid size: 65 (p) × 65 (q) × 42 (angle)
- Two-body t-matrix solved as a 2500×2500 linear system for about 60 different vectors b
- 5 to 7 energy iterations needed to find the ground state energy

## Context
This work extends the three-dimensional approach to Faddeev calculations previously developed for two-body forces only (Ref. trit3d) by incorporating genuine three-body forces. It contrasts with traditional partial-wave-based three-nucleon bound state calculations (Picklesimer 1995, Statler 1994, NBench collaboration) which require 34 or more isospin, spin, and orbital angular momentum combinations and large storage for coupling coefficients (Hueber 1997). The method enables efficient parallel computation on MPP systems with almost perfect load balance, and the authors note that the three-dimensional approach is less cumbersome for incorporating three-nucleon forces than partial-wave methods.
