# The leading nuclear-structure electrostatic correction in arbitrary $β$ decays

> We develop a systematic theoretical framework to improve theoretical predictions for nuclear $β$ decays of arbitrary angular momentum $J$, leading to a model-independent nuclear-structure electrostatic correction to the Coulomb interaction between the emitted lepton and the nuclear charge distribution, useful for ongoing and future precision searches for physics beyond the Standard Model. The formalism is based on nuclear matrix elements expanded in multipole operators, as commonly used in \emph{ab initio} calculations. First-order Coulomb corrections are derived from one-photon exchange preserving the full multipole and angular structure of the decay rate, and are subsequently expanded in the relevant small parameters of the nuclear problem, suppressing the leading nuclear structure correction to a few per-mills for medium mass nuclei with natural beta decay properties. We show that within this formalism, the leading Coulomb correction originates from three modifications of the original weak-only interaction: a modification of the nuclear charge form factor, which yields a correction similar to the known Fermi function, a shift of the momentum transfer within the lepton traces, and the same shift but inside the nuclear multipole operators. We additionally provide explicit results for allowed Gamow--Teller and unique first-forbidden transitions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic, model-independent theoretical framework for the leading nuclear-structure electrostatic correction to nuclear beta decays of arbitrary angular momentum, showing that the correction is suppressed to a few per-mill for medium mass nuclei with natural beta decay properties, and that the dependence on nuclear structure enters only through the nuclear charge radius.

## Method

The formalism expands nuclear matrix elements in multipole operators as used in ab initio calculations, deriving first-order Coulomb corrections from one-photon exchange between the emitted lepton and the nuclear charge distribution while preserving the full multipole and angular structure of the decay rate. The Coulomb-distorted lepton wave functions are obtained from the Dirac equation in the nuclear electrostatic potential, and the correction is expanded in the small parameters of the nuclear problem, separating the point-nucleus contribution (recovering the Fermi function) from the finite-size nuclear correction. Explicit results are provided for allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden transitions.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter governing the correction is ε_EC = αZ E_e R ≈ 4 × 10^(-5) (E_e / 1 MeV) Z A^(1/3), using a typical beta-decay lepton energy E_e ~ 1 MeV and the phenomenological relation R ≈ 1.2 A^(1/3) fm
- The leading nuclear structure correction is suppressed to a few per-mill for medium mass nuclei with natural beta decay properties
- The Fermi coupling constant is G_F = 1.1663787 × 10^(-5) GeV^(-2), and the CKM matrix element V_ud = 0.97370
- The hierarchy MR ≈ (m_N r_0) A^(4/3) ≈ 5.7 A^(4/3) >> 1 ensures k_γ << k for all nuclei
- Higher-order contributions are expected at orders (αZ) · ε_EC and (E_e R) · ε_EC

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of Coulomb effects in beta decay, which incorporates the Fermi function from solving the Dirac equation for a point-like charge, and the finite-size extensions by Behrens and Buhring (1982) that depend on model choices for the nuclear charge density. The formalism complements previous studies by Glick-Magid (2022, 2023) where electrostatic corrections were not included, and is readily compatible with modern ab initio calculations, enabling controlled estimates of theoretical uncertainties for ongoing and future precision searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
