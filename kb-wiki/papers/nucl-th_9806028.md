# Are Pions Perturbative in Effective Field Theory?

> It is shown that pions can be included perturbatively into effective field theory only for the external momenta, well below the pion mass. But for such low energies it is not necessary to include pions explicitly.

## Key claim

Pions can be included perturbatively in effective field theory only for external momenta well below the pion mass; for such low energies, explicit inclusion of pions is not necessary. The paper argues that the Power Divergent Subtraction (PDS) scheme also does not allow perturbative inclusion of pions for momenta up to the pion mass, contrary to claims by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996-1998).

## Method

The author performs analytic calculations for \(^1S_0\) wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, including contact interaction terms up to sub-leading order and the one-pion exchange potential perturbatively. Using subtractions at \(p^2 = -\mu^2\) and also the PDS scheme, the amplitude is derived and phase shifts are computed by matching to the effective range expansion. The author compares results from perturbative versus non-perturbative inclusion of the second-order contact term \(C_2(p^2 + p'^2)\) to test the validity of the perturbative expansion.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants for \(\mu = 40\) MeV: \(C_0 \sim (57\ \text{MeV})^{-2}\), \(C_2 \sim (80\ \text{MeV})^{-4}\).
- For \(\mu = 40\) MeV and floating normalization \(\mu = p\), agreement with effective range expansion is satisfactory for momenta up to 60 MeV, with significant deviation at higher momenta.
- In the PDS scheme with normalization point equal to the pion mass, phase shifts deviate from effective range expansion already at \(\sim 30\) MeV.
- The difference between perturbative and non-perturbative inclusion of the second-order contact term becomes significant for momenta above \(\sim 50\) MeV (subtraction scheme) and \(\sim 100\) MeV (PDS scheme).
- Best-fit coupling constants from Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998): \(C_0(m_\pi) = -3.34\ \text{fm}^2\), \(D_2(m_\pi) = -0.42\ \text{fm}^4\), \(C_2(m_\pi) = 3.24\ \text{fm}^4\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on Weinberg's power counting for nucleon-nucleon interactions in chiral effective field theory, directly challenging the claims of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996-1998) that pions can be included perturbatively up to momenta of 300 MeV using the PDS scheme. The paper supports the cut-off approach of Ordonez (1994), Park (1998), and Lepage (1997) as the only systematic way to incorporate Weinberg's ideas for not very low energies, and notes related problems addressed by Steele and Furnstahl (1998) and Cohen and Hansen (1998).
