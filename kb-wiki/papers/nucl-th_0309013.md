# EFT for electroweak processes of light nuclei

> Recently we succeeded to make a reliable EFT prediction in a totally parameter-free manner for the $S$ factors for the solar $pp$ and $hep$ processes, $p+p\to d + e^+ +ν_e$ and $\He3+p \to \He4 + e^+ + ν_e$. The strategy used in there is to embed a highly sophisticated standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) exploiting realistic potentials into an EFT framework, that we refer to as EFT*. Up to next-next-to-leading order in chiral expansion, it turnes out that there is effectively only one counter-term relevant to this process, the coefficient of which -- ($\hat d^R$) -- has been renormalized to reproduce the experimental value of the tritium-beta decay. Our study has also led to {\em very} accurate EFT calculations on two-body weak processes that also receive contributions from the $\hat d^R$ term, $μ-d$ capture rate, and $ν-d$ scattering cross section.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the EFT* approach, which embeds standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) wave functions into an effective field theory framework, yields parameter-free predictions for the astrophysical S-factors of the solar pp and hep processes, with the single counter-term $\hat d^R$ fixed by tritium beta-decay data. The hedge is that the hep result shows a $\sim$10% variation in the two-body amplitude across cutoff values $\Lambda = 500$–$800$ MeV, and the final S-factor error spans this range.

## Method
The authors use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$\chi$PT) up to next-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) to derive the Gamow-Teller (GT) two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC), consisting of one-pion-exchange and contact (zero-range) terms. They evaluate the matrix elements of these currents between accurate SNPA wave functions obtained with the Argonne $v_{18}$ (AV18) two-nucleon potential plus Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential, using the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) method for $A=3$ and $A=4$ systems. The unknown contact-term strength $\hat d^R$ is fixed by reproducing the experimental tritium beta-decay rate, and the same regularization scheme is applied consistently across all systems.

## Key numbers
- $S_{pp}(0) = 3.94 \times (1 \pm 0.0015 \pm 0.0010) \times 10^{-25}$ MeV-b, where the first error is from one-body input uncertainties and the second from two-body uncertainties.
- $S_{hep}(0) = (8.6 \pm 1.3) \times 10^{-20}$ keV-b, where the error spans the $\Lambda$-dependence for $\Lambda = 500$–$800$ MeV.
- For the $pp$ process, the two-body GT matrix element $\mathcal{M}_{\rm 2B}$ is $0.041 \pm 0.002$ fm, stable across $\Lambda = 500$, $600$, and $800$ MeV.
- The contact-term strength $\hat d^R$ varies with cutoff: $1.00 \pm 0.07$ at $\Lambda = 500$ MeV, $1.78 \pm 0.08$ at $\Lambda = 600$ MeV, and $3.90 \pm 0.10$ at $\Lambda = 800$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the EFT* formalism previously developed by the author and collaborators (Park et al., 2003; Kubodera, 2003) to the solar pp and hep processes, which are critical for solar neutrino physics. The hep reaction has been a long-standing challenge because theoretical estimates varied by orders of magnitude due to strong suppression of the one-body contribution and large cancellations with two-body terms. The results enable more reliable predictions for other weak processes involving the same counter-term $\hat d^R$, such as muon capture on deuteron and neutrino-deuteron scattering, and the method is being applied to the hen process ($^3$He + n $\rightarrow$ $^4$He + $\gamma$) as a further test of EFT* as a bona-fide EFT of QCD.
