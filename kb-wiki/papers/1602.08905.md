# Multi-Strangeness Production in Hadron Induced Reactions

> We discuss in detail the formation and propagation of multi-strangeness particles in reactions induced by hadron beams relevant for the forthcoming experiments at FAIR. We focus the discussion on the production of the decuplet-particle $Ω$ and study for the first time the production and propagation mechanism of this heavy hyperon inside hadronic environments. The transport calculations show the possibility of $Ω$-production in the forthcoming \panda-experiment, which can be achieved with measurable probabilities using high-energy secondary $Ξ$-beams. We predict cross sections for $Ω$-production. The theoretical results are important in understanding the hyperon-nucleon and, in particular, the hyperon-hyperon interactions also in the high-strangeness sector. We emphasize the importance of our studies for the research plans at FAIR.

## Key claim
The paper claims that $\Omega$-baryon production is possible in the forthcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR, but only with measurable probabilities when using high-energy secondary $\Xi$-beams rather than primary antiproton beams alone; the abstract glosses over the fact that direct antiproton-proton annihilation yields extremely low cross sections (several nb only) and that secondary scattering processes are the dominant mechanism.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport calculations using the GiBUU model, implementing new parametrizations for $N\bar{N}\to \Omega\bar{\Omega}$ scattering and other elementary channels. They simulated antiproton-induced reactions on a ${}^{93}$Nb target and $\Xi^{-}$-induced reactions on a ${}^{64}$Cu target at various beam energies, using Monte Carlo methods to model the collision integral. The mean-field was treated in the relativistic mean-field approximation with NL3 parametrization, and meson-hyperon couplings were obtained from quark-counting arguments.

## Key numbers
- $\Omega$-production cross section from antiproton-proton annihilation reaches a maximum of several nb above the threshold of $\sqrt{s}\geq 3.344$ GeV (corresponding to $E_{lab}=4$ GeV or $p_{lab}=4.9$ GeV/c in the laboratory frame)
- For the $\Xi N \to \Omega K N$ channel, the threshold is $\sqrt{s}\geq 3.107$ GeV (corresponding to $E_{lab}\geq 3.75$ GeV kinetic energy or $p_{lab}\geq 3.5$ GeV/c beam momentum)
- For $\bar{p}+{}^{93}$Nb at 4 GeV incident energy, secondary scattering processes contribute 1.148 nb to the total $\Omega$-production yield, with total cross section $\sigma_{\Omega}=1.15$ nb
- The $\Omega$-baryon mass is 1.672 GeV
- In-medium potentials at saturation density and zero kinetic energy: $U_N=-46$ MeV, $U_\Lambda=-38$ MeV, $U_\Sigma=-39$ MeV, $U_\Xi=-22$ MeV
- Densities achieved in these reactions range from $0.5\rho_{sat} \leq \rho \leq 1.5\rho_{sat}$

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Gaitanos et al. (2014) on multi-strangeness hyperon-nucleon interactions in the $S=-2$ sector, now considering $\Omega$-production in the $S=-3$ sector. It addresses the hyperon puzzle in neutron star physics (Demorest 2010; Antoniadis 2013) where hyperon formation at high densities conflicts with measured neutron star masses. The results are relevant for the PANDA experiment at FAIR (panda1, panda2) and enable future experimental searches for $\Omega$-baryons using secondary $\Xi$-beams, which could constrain hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions in the high-strangeness sector.
