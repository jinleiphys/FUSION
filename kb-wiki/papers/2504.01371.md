# A simple schematic model for a cross section deficit in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion reactions

> A cross section deficit phenomenon has been observed in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion reactions at astrophysical energies, at which fusion cross sections are suppressed in the off-resonance regions as compared to fusion cross sections for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system. I here construct a simple schematic model which simulates this phenomenon. The model consists of a random matrix Hamiltonian based on the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), which is coupled to an entrance channel Hamiltonian in the discrete basis representation. I show that the transmission coefficients are almost unity when both the level density and the decay widths of the GOE configurations are large, realizing the strong absorption regime. On the other hand, when these parameters are small, the transmission coefficients are significantly structured as a function of energy. In that situation, the transmission coefficients at resonance energies reach unity in this model, that is consistent with the experimental finding of the cross section deficit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple schematic model coupling a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) random matrix to an entrance channel can qualitatively reproduce the cross section deficit observed in \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C fusion, where transmission coefficients at resonance energies reach unity while off-resonance values are suppressed, but only when the level density and decay widths of the GOE configurations are small enough to produce isolated resonances.

## Method

The model couples a GOE random matrix Hamiltonian (dimension \(N_{\rm GOE}\)) to free scattering states in a discrete basis representation, with each GOE configuration assigned a decay width \(\gamma\). The transmission coefficients (absorption probabilities) are computed from the \(S\)-matrix derived by solving the coupled equations for the scattering wave functions and GOE amplitudes. Two parameter regimes are compared: one with large \(N_{\rm GOE}\) and large \(\gamma\) (overlapping resonances) and one with smaller \(N_{\rm GOE}\) and smaller \(\gamma\) (isolated resonances).

## Key numbers

- For \((N_{\rm GOE},\gamma)=(100,0.6)\): transmission coefficients are close to unity, consistent with the strong absorption limit.
- For \((N_{\rm GOE},\gamma)=(40,0.05)\): transmission coefficients show structured energy dependence with prominent resonance peaks reaching unity at resonance energies.
- The average level spacing is \(1/\rho_{\rm GOE}=0.03\) for \(N_{\rm GOE}=100\) and \(0.05\) for \(N_{\rm GOE}=40\) (with \(v_g=0.1\)).
- The Moldauer factor for \(N_{\rm GOE}=40\) with \(\Gamma=0.05\) and \(\rho_{\rm GOE}=1/0.05\) is 0.998.
- Ensemble average of transmission coefficients uses 20 ensembles.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the cross section deficit phenomenon first identified by Jiang (2013) in \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C fusion, where off-resonance cross sections are suppressed compared to the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{13}\)C system. The model is taken from Appendix B of Bertsch (2023) and provides a qualitative explanation for why resonance-energy cross sections in \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C coincide with those of \(^{12}\)C+\(^{13}\)C, a question not addressed by the Moldauer factor alone. The approach enables future extensions using more realistic many-body Hamiltonians (e.g., shell model Hamiltonians) for quantitative studies of resonance reactions in nuclear astrophysics.
