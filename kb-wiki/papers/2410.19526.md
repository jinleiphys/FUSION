# Deciphering the mechanism of $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering

> The low-energy $J/ψN$ scattering is important for various reasons: it is related to the hidden-charm $P_c$ pentaquark states, provides insights into the role of gluons in nucleon structures, and is relevant to the $J/ψ$ properties in nuclear medium. The scattering can happen through two distinct mechanisms: the coupled-channel mechanism via open-charm meson-baryon intermediate states, and the soft-gluon exchange mechanism. We investigate the $J/ψN$ $S$-wave scattering length through both mechanisms, and find that the soft-gluon exchange mechanism leads to a scattering length at least one order of magnitude larger than that from the coupled-channel mechanism and thus is the predominant one. The findings can be verified by lattice calculations and will enhance our understanding of the scattering processes breaking the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka rule.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the soft-gluon exchange mechanism is the dominant contribution to low-energy $J/\psi N$ scattering, with the $S$-wave scattering length from this mechanism being at least one order of magnitude larger than that from the coupled-channel mechanism via open-charm meson-baryon intermediate states. The authors qualify that the soft-gluon exchange result is an upper bound ($a_{J/\psi N} \lesssim -0.16$ fm) rather than a precise value, and that the coupled-channel result carries a large uncertainty from fitting to LHCb data.

## Method

The authors calculated the $S$-wave $J/\psi N$ scattering length from two distinct mechanisms separately. For the coupled-channel mechanism, they used the chiral effective field theory framework from Du (2021) that solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for a system including $J/\psi N$, $\eta_c N$, $\Lambda_c\bar D^{(*)}$ and $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar D^{(*)}$, with parameters fixed by fitting to LHCb data on $\Lambda_b^0 \rightarrow J/\psi p K^{-}$ decay. For the soft-gluon exchange mechanism, they used dispersion relations to evaluate the exchange of correlated $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar K$ pairs (which carry vacuum quantum numbers), employing the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation with Omnès matrix elements from Ropertz (2018) and chiral Lagrangians up to next-to-leading order, with the chromopolarizability $\alpha^{(21)}$ fixed by fitting to BESII and ATLAS data on $\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ decay.

## Key numbers

- Coupled-channel scattering length: $a_{0,J/\psi N}^\text{CC} \in [-10, -0.1]\times 10^{-3}$ fm
- Soft-gluon exchange scattering length (assuming $c^{(11)}_{1,2,m}=c^{(21)}_{1,2,m}$): $\tilde a_{J/\psi N}$ in the range $-0.16$ to $-0.19$ fm as cutoff $\Lambda$ varies from 1.0 GeV to 1.5 GeV
- Soft-gluon exchange scattering length (with Schwartz inequality): $a_{J/\psi N} \lesssim -0.16$ fm
- Off-diagonal chromopolarizability: $|\alpha^{(21)}| = (1.18 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.05)$ GeV$^{-3}$
- Fitted parameter $\kappa = 0.26 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.01$
- $\tilde a_{\psi(2S) N}$ in the range $-0.14$ to $-0.17$ fm (using $\psi(2S)$ mass in the same formalism)

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing debate about the dominant mechanism for low-energy $J/\psi N$ scattering, which has implications for understanding the trace anomaly contribution to the nucleon mass and the nature of hidden-charm $P_c$ pentaquark states discovered by LHCb (2015, 2019). Previous lattice QCD studies by Yokokawa (2006), Beane (2014), and Skerbis (2018) gave inconsistent results ranging from strong attraction to very weak interaction. The coupled-channel mechanism alone, as studied by Du (2020) in the context of $J/\psi$ photoproduction, yields a very weak scattering length. The present work shows that the soft-gluon exchange mechanism dominates, enabling future lattice QCD calculations to verify the result and laying the foundation for using $J/\psi N$ scattering to probe the gluonic structure of the nucleon. The authors note that a lattice QCD calculation by Lyu (2024) appearing after this work gives $a_{J/\psi N}$ in the range $-0.42$ to $-0.28$ fm, consistent with their result.
