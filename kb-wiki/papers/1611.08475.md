# Leading order relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Motivated by the successes of relativistic theories in studies of atomic/molecular and nuclear systems and the need for a relativistic chiral force in relativistic nuclear structure studies, we explore a new relativistic scheme to construct the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the framework of covariant chiral effective field theory. The chiral interaction is formulated up to leading order with covariant power counting and a Lorentz invariant chiral Lagrangian. We find that the relativistic scheme induces all six spin operators needed to describe the nuclear force. A detailed investigation of the partial wave potentials shows a better description of the $^1S_0$ and $^3P_0$ phase shifts than the leading order Weinberg approach, and similar to that of the next-to-leading order Weinberg approach. For the other partial waves with angular momenta $J\geq 1$, the relativistic results are almost the same as their leading order non-relativistic counterparts.

## Key claim

The authors construct a leading-order relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction using covariant power counting and a Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian, finding that the relativistic scheme naturally generates all six spin operators needed to describe the nuclear force and yields a better description of the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_0\) phase shifts than the leading-order Weinberg approach, comparable to next-to-leading order Weinberg results, while for partial waves with \(J \geq 1\) the relativistic results are almost identical to their leading-order non-relativistic counterparts.

## Method

The authors start from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangian and construct the nucleon-nucleon potential up to leading order (LO) in covariant chiral effective field theory, using a covariant power counting scheme based on naive dimensional analysis. They employ the Kadyshevsky three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain scattering amplitudes, and regularize the potential with a separable cutoff function \(\exp\left(\frac{-\bm{p}^{2n}-\bm{p}'^{2n}}{\Lambda^{2n}}\right)\) with \(n=2\). The low-energy constants (LECs) are determined by fitting to the Nijmegen partial wave phase shifts of the \(np\) channel.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and the axial vector coupling as \(g_A = 1.267\).
- The contact potential contributes to all partial waves with \(J=0,1\) through seven independent combinations of the five LECs \(C_S, C_A, C_V, C_{AV}, C_T\).
- The static limit of the relativistic LO potential reduces to the heavy baryon (HB) scheme form: \(V^\mathrm{HB} = (C_S+C_V) - (C_{AV}-2C_T)\bm{\sigma}_1\cdot\bm{\sigma}_2 - \frac{g_A^2}{4f_\pi^2}\bm{\tau}_1\cdot\bm{\tau}_2 \frac{\bm{\sigma}_1\cdot\bm{q}\bm{\sigma}_2\cdot\bm{q}}{\bm{q}^2 + m_\pi^2}\).
- The relativistic corrections to the \(^1S_0\) partial wave potential are suppressed by \(1/M_N^{2n}\) (\(n=1,2,\cdots\)) for the OPEP, but the contact interaction has an independent LEC \((C_{1S0}+\hat{C}_{1S0})\) determining its relativistic corrections.

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral effective field theory approach, which has shown faster convergence than non-relativistic heavy baryon schemes in the one-baryon sector (Geng 2008, Ren 2012, Ren 2014), to the two-nucleon sector. It differs from the approach of Epelbaum (2012), where relativistic effects were included perturbatively with focus on renormalization group invariance, by constructing a fully relativistic potential suitable for use in relativistic many-body calculations such as Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Brockmann 1990, Shen 2016, Shen 2017). The work enables future inclusion of higher orders to provide a high-precision relativistic chiral nuclear force for relativistic nuclear structure studies.
