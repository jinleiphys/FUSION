# Holographic charm and bottom pentaquarks II: Open and hidden decay widths

> We analyze the decay modes of the three $[\frac 12\frac 12^-]_{S=0,1}$ and $[\frac 12\frac 32^-]_{S=1}$ non-strange pentaquarks with hidden charm and bottom, predicted by holographic QCD in the heavy quark limit. In leading order, the pentaquarks %are composed of heavy-light mesons in bulk bound to an instanton core. They are degenerate and stable by heavy quark symmetry. At next to leading order, the spin interactions lift the degeneracy and cause the pentaquarks to decay. We show that the open charm (bottom) decay modes dwarf the hidden charm (bottom) ones, with total widths that are consistent with those recently reported by LHCb for charm pentaquarks. Predictions for bottom pentaquarks are given.

## Key claim

The three non-strange pentaquark states with hidden charm and bottom, which are degenerate and stable in the heavy quark limit, acquire finite decay widths at next-to-leading order from spin interactions; the open charm (bottom) decay modes dominate over the hidden charm (bottom) ones, and the total widths for charm pentaquarks are consistent with the LHCb measurements, but the analysis is carried out using the decay kinematics of the $P_c(4440)$ state and the brane tension $\tilde\kappa$ is fixed by one measured width.

## Method

The decay vertices are derived by expanding the holographic effective action (DBI, Chern-Simons, and mass terms) about the instanton core in bulk, coupling the classical heavy-light field $\Phi_M$ to fluctuating heavy-light meson fields $\delta\Phi_M$. The transition amplitudes for open charm decays ($P_c \to \Lambda_c + \bar D$, $P_c \to \Lambda_c + \bar D^*$, $P_c \to \Sigma_c + \bar D$, $P_c \to \Sigma_c^* + \bar D$) are computed from the linearized Hamiltonian, including a gauge transformation that allows angular momentum transitions. The hidden charm decay ($P_c \to J/\Psi + p$) is extracted from a Witten diagram using the bulk-to-boundary U(1) vector propagator and the vector meson eigenmodes of the Sakai-Sugimoto model.

## Key numbers

- For $P_c(4440)$ decaying to $\Lambda_c + \bar D$, the final meson decay momentum is $|\vec p| \approx 778$ MeV.
- For $P_c(4440)$ decaying to $\Sigma_c + \bar D$, the final momentum is $|\vec p| \approx 502$ MeV.
- The model-independent ratio of decay widths for $P_c \to \Lambda_c + \bar D^*$ versus $P_c \to \Lambda_c + \bar D$ is $3:1$.
- The model-independent ratio of decay widths for the three pentaquark states to $\Lambda_c + \bar D$ is $\Gamma(S=0, J=1/2) : \Gamma(S=1, J=1/2) : \Gamma(S=1, J=3/2) = 1/2 : 5/6 : 1/3$.
- The ratio $\Gamma_{P_c(J,S)\to \Sigma_c + D} / \Gamma_{P_c(J,S)\to \Lambda_c + D} = 0.574$, using the $P_c(4440)$ kinematics.
- The decay $P_c \to \Sigma_c^* + \bar D$ has zero width because the average of the moduli coefficient $\alpha$ vanishes for the $l=1$ transition.
- The LHCb measured masses and widths for charm pentaquarks are: $m_{P_c}=4311.9\pm 0.7$ MeV, $\Gamma=9.8\pm 2.7$ MeV; $m_{P_c}=4440.3\pm 1.3$ MeV, $\Gamma=20.6\pm 4.9$ MeV; $m_{P_c}=4457.3\pm 0.6$ MeV, $\Gamma=6.4\pm 2.0$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD analysis of pentaquarks by Liu, Nowak, and Zahed (2021) that included spin effects, following the prediction of three degenerate stable pentaquark states in the heavy quark limit by Liu (2017) and Liu (2017). The results are consistent with the molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquark states reported by Aaij (2019) and Aaij (2015), and the holographic approach avoids the ambiguities of form factors and meson-exchange models used by Burns (2015), Richard (2016), Lebed (2016), Esposito (2016), Olsen (2017), Guo (2017), Karliner (2017), Eides (2017), Lin (2019), and Du (2021). The analysis enables predictions for the yet-unobserved bottom pentaquark decay widths and provides model-independent ratios that can be tested by future experiments.
