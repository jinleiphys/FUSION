# Discussions on the Line-shape of $X(4660)$ Resonance

> A careful reanalysis is made on $e^+e^-\to X(4660)\to (Λ_c\barΛ_c)/(ψ'ππ)$ processes, aiming at resolving the apparent conflicts between Belle and BESIII data above $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ threshold. We use a model containing a Breit-Wigner resonance and $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ four-point contact interactions, with which the enhancement right above the $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ threshold is well explained by a virtual pole generated by $Λ_c\barΛ_c$ attractive final state interaction, located at $M_V=4.566\pm0.007$ GeV. Meanwhile, $X(4660)$ remains to be a typical Breit-Wigner resonance, and is hence of confinement nature. Our analysis strongly suggests the existence of the virtual pole with statistical significance of $4.2$ standard deviation ($σ$). Nevertheless, the conclusion crucially depends on the line-shape of cross sections which are of limited statistics, hence we urge new experimental analyses from Belle II, BESIII, and LHCb to settle the issue.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the threshold enhancement in $e^+e^-\to \Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ data, which appears incompatible between Belle and BESIII measurements, can be explained by a virtual pole from $\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ attractive final state interactions rather than by the $X(4660)$ resonance itself; however, the conclusion depends on the limited-statistics line-shape of cross sections and requires confirmation from future experiments.

## Method

The authors construct a $K$-matrix model combining a Breit-Wigner resonance for $X(4660)$ with $\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ four-point contact interactions (vector and axial-vector terms) to describe final state interactions. They perform a combined fit to Belle and BESIII data for $e^+e^-\to X(4660)\to \Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ and $e^+e^-\to X(4660)\to \psi'\pi\pi$ processes, including a constant coherent background, and compare the fit quality to a model without the contact interaction.

## Key numbers

* Virtual pole mass: $M_V = 4.566 \pm 0.007$ GeV, located below the $\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ threshold.
* Statistical significance of the virtual pole: $4.2$ standard deviations ($\sigma$), obtained from the $\chi^2$ difference between the full model ($\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.}=26.6/33$) and a model with only Breit-Wigner resonance ($\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.}=44.4/34$).
* $X(4660)$ pole mass: $M_{pole} = 4.645 \pm 0.028$ GeV; pole width: $\Gamma_{pole} = 0.078 \pm 0.029$ GeV.
* Ratio of decay widths: $\Gamma(X\to \Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c) / \Gamma(X\to \psi'\pi\pi) \simeq 23$.

## Context

This work addresses the apparent conflict between Belle data (Pakhlova 2008, Wang 2014) and BESIII data (Ablikim 2017) for the $e^+e^-\to \Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ cross section near threshold, which earlier models (e.g., Guo 2010) could not simultaneously describe. The analysis extends prior studies of $X(4660)$ and $X(4630)$ as possibly the same state (Dai 2017, Tanabashi 2018) by introducing a virtual pole mechanism, and applies the pole counting rule (Morgan 1992) to argue that $X(4660)$ is of confinement nature rather than a molecular state. The results motivate new experimental measurements from Belle II, BESIII, and LHCb to confirm the virtual pole's existence.
