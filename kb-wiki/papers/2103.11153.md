# Study of resonances in exotic drip-line nuclei by the use of super-symmetric quantum mechanics

> In this paper, a nice theoretical scheme is presented to investigate resonant and bound states in weakly bound nuclear systems by the use of isospectral potentials together with hyperspherical harmonics expansion. In this scheme, a new potential is constructed which is strictly isospectral with the original shallow potential and has properties that are desirable to calculate resonances more accurately and elegantly. The effectiveness of the method has been checked in terms of its application to the $0^+$ resonances in the neutron-rich isotopes $^{18,20}$C in the two-neutron plus core three-body cluster model. The results are in excellent agreement with the available data.

# Study of resonances in exotic drip-line nuclei by the use of super-symmetric quantum mechanics

## Key claim

The authors claim that constructing a one-parameter family of strictly isospectral potentials via supersymmetric quantum mechanics, starting from the shallow effective potential of a weakly bound three-body system, enables more accurate computation of resonance energies and widths than direct calculation on the original potential. The method is validated for the 0+ resonances in 18C and 20C, but the accuracy gain depends on a judicious choice of the parameter λ to avoid both numerical errors from an overly narrow well and the original problem of a wide, low barrier.

## Method

The authors use a three-body cluster model (2n + core) with hyperspherical harmonics expansion and the hyperspherical adiabatic approximation to obtain the ground state energy and wavefunction for 18C and 20C, employing the GPT nucleon-nucleon potential and the SBB core-neutron potential. From the ground state wavefunction, they construct a one-parameter family of strictly isospectral potentials using the algebra of supersymmetric quantum mechanics, where the parameter λ controls the depth and width of the resulting well-barrier combination. They then solve the hyperradial Schrödinger equation for positive energies on the isospectral potential, compute the trapping probability within the enhanced well-barrier region as a function of energy, and identify the resonance energy from the peak of this probability; the resonance width is obtained by back-transforming the wavefunction to the original potential.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated two-neutron separation energy for 18C: 4.91 MeV (converged value from Kmax extrapolation).
- Extrapolated two-neutron separation energy for 20C: 3.51 MeV (converged value from Kmax extrapolation).
- For 18C, when λ decreases from 0.1 to 0.0001, the depth of the isospectral potential well increases from -24.2 MeV at 2.7 fm to -247.6 MeV at 1.3 fm, and the barrier height increases from 5.4 MeV at 5.1 fm to 121.5 MeV at 1.9 fm.
- Resonance energy for the 0+ state in 18C: approximately 1.98 MeV.
- Resonance energy for the 0+ state in 20C: approximately 3.75 MeV.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energies agree with observed values of 4.910 ± 0.030 MeV for 18C and 3.510 ± 0.240 MeV for 20C (Audi et al 2003).
- The lx=0 partial wave contributes approximately 74% of the two-neutron separation energy in 18C and approximately 96% in 20C.

## Context

This work extends the supersymmetric quantum mechanics approach previously applied to two-body systems (e.g., 15Be by Dutta et al 2018, 11Be by Dutta et al 2014, 15C by Mahapatra et al 2011) and to the three-body system 22C by Hasan et al 2019, now applying it to the neutron-rich carbon isotopes 18C and 20C. The method enables the study of broad resonances in weakly bound halo nuclei where the shallow original potential makes direct resonance calculations numerically difficult; it reproduces ground state properties in agreement with Audi et al 2003 and Yamaguchi et al 2011, and provides a pathway to compute resonance parameters for other drip-line nuclei using the three-body cluster model.
