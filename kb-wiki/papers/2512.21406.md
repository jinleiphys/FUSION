# On the Cooling of Compact Stars in Light of the HESS J1731-347 Remnant

> Recent analyses on the central compact object in the HESS J1731-347 supernova remnant reported not only surprising structural properties (mass $M$ and radius $R$), but also an interesting thermal evolution. More precisely, it has been estimated that $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}M_\odot$ and $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km (at the $1σ$ level), while a redshited surface temperature of $153^{+4}_ {-2}$ keV at an age of 2-6 kyrs has been reported. In the present work, we conduct an in-depth investigation on the possible nature (hadronic, hybrid, quark) of this compact object by attempting to not only explain its mass and radius but also the corresponding estimations for its temperature and age. In the case of hybrid stars we also examine possible effects of the symmetry energy on the activation of different neutrino emitting process, and hence on the resulting cooling curves. We found that the reported temperature and age may be compatible to hadronic stellar configurations regardless of whether pairing effects are included. In the scenario of hybrid stars, we found that the strange quark matter core has to be in a superconducting state in order to reach an agreement with the observational constraints. In addition, the hadronic phase must be soft enough so that the direct Urca process is not activated. Furthermore, we have shown that the considered cooling constraints can be reconciled within the framework of strange stars. However, quark matter has to be in a superconducting state and the quark direct Urca process needs to be blocked.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed temperature and age of the HESS J1731-347 central compact object can be compatible with hadronic stellar configurations regardless of whether pairing effects are included, but for hybrid stars the strange quark matter core must be in a superconducting state and the hadronic phase must be soft enough to block the direct Urca process; for strange stars, quark matter must be superconducting and the quark direct Urca process must be blocked.

## Method

The authors constructed hadronic equations of state using an expansion around nuclear saturation density parametrized by the incompressibility K0 and symmetry energy slope L, and quark matter equations of state using the vector MIT bag model with both constant and density-dependent bag parameters. They computed cooling curves by solving the thermal evolution equations including neutrino emission processes (direct Urca, modified Urca, and bremsstrahlung) in both hadronic and quark matter, incorporating pairing effects via suppression factors for superfluidity and superconductivity. Hybrid star configurations were constructed using the Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition, and the resulting cooling curves were compared to the observational constraints on mass, radius, temperature, and age of the HESS J1731-347 object.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the HESS J1731-347 central compact object: M = 0.77+0.20-0.17 solar masses (at 1 sigma level)
- Radius: R = 10.4+0.86-0.78 km (at 1 sigma level)
- Redshifted surface temperature: Ts_infinity = 153+4-2 keV
- Age of the object: 2-6 kyr
- Quark masses used: mu = 2.3 MeV, md = 4.8 MeV, ms = 95 MeV
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.155 x 10^39 cm^-3
- Critical proton fraction for nucleon direct Urca activation: xc = 1/9
- Critical proton fraction for proton branch of modified Urca: xcp = 1/65
- Minimum superconducting gap for CFL state to be energetically favored: Delta_0q > approximately 15 MeV (derived from condition Delta_0q > 3ms^2/(2 mu_B) with mu_B at least 930 MeV)
- Critical temperature for CFL phase: T_cq = 0.4 Delta_0q/kB
- BCS critical temperature relation for low gaps: T_cq approximately 0.57 Delta_0q/kB
- Critical temperature for 1S0 proton pairing: kB T_cj approximately 0.5669 Delta_0jj
- Critical temperature for 3P2 neutron pairing: kB T_cj approximately 0.8416 Delta_0jj

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the HESS J1731-347 central compact object beyond structural properties (mass and radius) to include thermal evolution, following the initial mass-radius measurement by Doroshenko (2022) and subsequent studies by Di Clemente (2023), Horvath (2023), and Sagun (2023). It builds on the cooling formalism of Yakovlev (2001) and Page (2006) for hadronic matter and Iwamoto (1982) for quark matter, and incorporates pairing effects following Blaschke (2000, 2001) and Negreiros (2012). The study enables future work to discriminate between hadronic, hybrid, and strange star scenarios for low-mass compact objects by combining structural and thermal observational constraints.
