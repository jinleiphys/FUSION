# Nuclear pygmy modes and the dynamics of the nuclear skin

> The information on pygmy resonances reveals new aspects on the isospin dynamics of the nucleus with important astrophysical consequences. In this connection, the precise knowledge of nuclear response functions plays a key role in the determination of photonuclear reactions cross sections which are of importance for the synthesis of heavy neutron-rich elements. For that purpose, a theoretical method based on density functional theory and multi-phonon approach is applied for investigations of nuclear excitations with different multipolarities and energies in stable and exotic nuclei. The possible relation of low-energy modes to the properties of neutron or proton skins is systematically investigated for isotonic and isotopic chains. Our studies of dipole and quadrupole response functions and the corresponding transition densities indicate new pygmy dipole and pygmy quadrupole resonances, describing oscillations of the nuclear skin. Also, the presence of skins is found to affect the magnetic response of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pygmy dipole and pygmy quadrupole resonances represent distinct nuclear excitation modes describing oscillations of the neutron or proton skin, with the caveat that this interpretation relies on theoretical calculations using density functional theory and a multi-phonon approach, and that the identification of these modes depends on analysis of transition densities and state vector structure rather than direct experimental observation.

## Method

The authors apply a theoretical framework combining density functional theory (DFT) for ground state properties with quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) calculations for excited states. Ground state densities are obtained by adjusting Wood-Saxon potential parameters to reproduce binding energies, charge radii, and proton-neutron RMS radius differences from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. Excited states are described using QRPA phonons and multi-phonon configurations up to three-phonon components, with transition densities analyzed to distinguish pygmy modes from giant resonances.

## Key numbers

- In Sn isotopes with A≥106, neutron distributions begin to extend beyond the proton density, forming a neutron skin that increases with neutron excess up to 132Sn
- In 100-102Sn, a tiny proton skin appears
- Low-lying one-phonon 1- states in 110-132Sn appear at excitation energies E* = 6-7.5 MeV with neutron contributions and less than 1% proton contribution
- In 100-104Sn, lowest dipole excitations appear at E* = 8.1-8.3 MeV dominated by proton excitations
- In 112,122,132Sn, up to E* = 8 MeV protons and neutrons oscillate in phase in the nuclear interior while only neutron transitions contribute at the surface
- In N=50 isotones, QRPA 1- states below E* < 9 MeV are related to PDR
- QPM calculations in the energy interval 0<E<8 MeV give about twice as much total E1-strength as QRPA calculations
- In 104-134Sn, low-energy 2+ states at E* ≈ 2-4 MeV show a sizable increase of B(E2) strength for heaviest tin isotopes (130Sn and 134Sn)
- The most important proton contribution in all Sn isotopes for 2+ excitations is the [1g9/2 2d5/2]π 2QP component, which never exceeds 5%
- In 124Sn, QRPA calculations show strong neutron oscillations at the nuclear surface in the PQR energy range

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei by Tsoneva, Lenske, and collaborators (Tso04, Tso08) and Volpe et al. (Vol06) and Schiller et al. (Sch08), and builds on earlier QPM calculations by Soloviev (Sol76). The systematic investigation of both dipole and quadrupole pygmy modes across isotopic chains (Z=12,13,50) and isotonic chains (N=50,82) enables a unified picture of skin oscillations, with the identification of a pygmy quadrupole resonance (PQR) as a new mode. The results enable future experimental searches for these modes in exotic nuclei and provide input for photonuclear reaction cross sections relevant to nucleosynthesis of heavy neutron-rich elements.
