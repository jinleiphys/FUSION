# Theoretical Description of the pd -> pd eta Reaction Near Threshold

> The contributions of three different types of driving terms are included in the estimation of the pd -> pd eta reaction at low energies. Near threshold, it is predicted that a two-step model involving an intermediate pion should be the most important but, as the energy approaches the threshold for eta production in the free nucleon--nucleon reaction, a pick-up mechanism with a spectator proton should become dominant. The total cross sections are underestimated by about a factor of two compared to experimental data but the discrepancies in the angular distributions are more serious, with no sign in the data for the peaks corresponding to the pick--up diagram.

## Key claim

The total cross section for the pd → pdη reaction is underestimated by about a factor of two compared to experimental data, and the angular distributions show more serious discrepancies with no sign in the data for the peaks corresponding to the pick-up diagram.

## Method

The authors evaluated three types of driving terms for the pd → pdη reaction: a pick-up term (quasi-free pn → dη on a neutron in the target), an impulse approximation (triangle) diagram with quasi-free pN → pNη, and a two-step model with an intermediate pion (pp → dπ⁺ followed by π⁺n → ηp). The spin-averaged matrix elements were computed using threshold amplitudes for the sub-reactions, deuteron wave functions, and form factors, with interferences neglected due to phase uncertainties. Total cross sections and differential distributions (invariant masses and angular distributions) were compared to experimental data from Saclay and CELSIUS.

## Key numbers

- The experimental data for the total cross section lie about a factor of two above the theoretical curve at high energies and a little bit more near threshold.
- The impulse approximation (triangle diagram) lies about two orders of magnitude below the experimental data.
- The two-step contribution dominates the pick-up by an order of magnitude in the near-threshold region of low Q.
- The two contributions cross at Q ≈ 95 MeV, where the two-step term levels off at Q ≈ 80 MeV and eventually decreases at higher energies.
- For the pick-up term, |G|² = (0.046 ± 0.011) fm⁴ from pn → dη data.
- For the triangle diagram, |W₁,s|² ≈ 1.9 × 10⁸ μb and |W₀,t|² ≈ 23 × 10⁸ μb from pp and pn data, with |3W₁,s + W₀,t|² = 80 × 10⁸ μb and |3W₁,s − W₀,t|² = 0.4 × 10⁸ μb assuming in-phase amplitudes.
- At the threshold for η production, the mean energy defect ΔE₀ ≈ −15 MeV for the two-step model.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to the pd → ³Heη reaction (Faldt and Wilkin, 1997) to the pd → pdη final state, where the final ³He is not formed. The authors compare their calculations to experimental data from Saclay (Hibou, 1999) and CELSIUS (Złomańczuk, 2005). The results enable further study of the pd → ppnη four-body final state and analogous sub-threshold reactions such as pd → K⁺Λd measured at COSY (Valdau, 2005).
