# Pairing Matrix Elements and Pairing Gaps with Bare, Effective and Induced Interactions

> The dependence on the single-particle states of the pairing matrix elements of the Gogny force and of the bare low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential $v_{low-k}$ is studied in the semiclassical approximation for the case of a typical finite, superfluid nucleus ($^{120}$Sn). It is found that the matrix elements of $v_{low-k}$ follow closely those of $v_{Gogny}$ on a wide range of energy values around the Fermi energy $e_F$, those associated with $v_{low-k}$ being less attractive. This result explains the fact that around $e_F$ the pairing gap $Δ_{Gogny}$ associated with the Gogny interaction (and with a density of single-particle levels corresponding to an effective $k$-mass $m_k\approx 0.7 m$) is a factor of about 2 larger than $Δ_{low-k}$,being in agreement with $Δ_{exp}$= 1.4 MeV. The exchange of low-lying collective surface vibrations among pairs of nucleons moving in time-reversal states gives rise to an induced pairing interaction $v_{ind}$ peaked at $e_F$. The interaction $(v_{low-k}+ v_{ind})Z_ω$ arising from the renormalization of the bare nucleon-nucleon potential and of the single-particle motion ($ω-$mass and quasiparticle strength $Z_ω$) due to the particle-vibration coupling leads to a value of the pairing gap at the Fermi energy $Δ_{ren}$ which accounts for the experimental value.

# Pairing Matrix Elements and Pairing Gaps with Bare, Effective and Induced Interactions

## Key claim

The pairing matrix elements of the bare low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential $v_{low-k}$ follow those of the Gogny force closely around the Fermi energy but are less attractive, explaining why the Gogny pairing gap is about a factor of 2 larger than the $v_{low-k}$ gap; however, when the induced interaction from surface vibrations and the quasiparticle strength renormalization are added, the resulting renormalized gap accounts for the experimental value of 1.4 MeV in $^{120}$Sn.

## Method

The authors computed diagonal and off-diagonal pairing matrix elements ($J^\pi=0^+$) for $^{120}$Sn using the Gogny D1 force, the $v_{low-k}$ potential (derived from the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential with a momentum cutoff of 2.1 fm$^{-1}$), and the induced interaction from exchange of low-lying collective surface vibrations (multipolarities L=2, 3, 4). They employed both full quantum mechanical calculations with Woods-Saxon single-particle states and semiclassical Thomas-Fermi approximations to average over shell effects, then solved generalized Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations to obtain state-dependent pairing gaps.

## Key numbers

- The experimental pairing gap for $^{120}$Sn is $\Delta_{exp} = 1.4$ MeV.
- The Gogny D1 interaction yields a pairing gap $\Delta_{Gogny} \approx 1.4$ MeV at the Fermi energy (with effective $k$-mass $m_k \approx 0.7 m$), which is about a factor of 2 larger than $\Delta_{low-k}$ obtained with $v_{low-k}$.
- The induced interaction matrix elements have an average value of -0.15 MeV and are peaked within about 5 MeV of the Fermi energy; outside this range they fall below 50 keV in absolute value.
- The Gogny diagonal matrix element at the Fermi energy is about -0.3 MeV.
- The low-lying vibrational modes used have energies $\hbar\omega_2 = 1.17$ MeV, $\hbar\omega_3 = 2.42$ MeV, $\hbar\omega_4 = 2.47$ MeV and deformation parameters $\beta_2 = 0.12$, $\beta_3 = 0.15$, $\beta_4 = 0.07$.
- The total pairing gap from $v_{low-k} + v_{ind}$ is about 2 MeV at the Fermi energy, approximately 25% higher than the result from full Dyson-Gor'kov calculations including all polarization effects.
- The renormalized gap $\Delta_{ren} \approx 1.35 \pm 0.15$ MeV, accounting for the quasiparticle strength $Z_\omega$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing in $^{120}$Sn using the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential with polarization effects (Barranco et al., 2004) by systematically comparing matrix elements of the Gogny force, $v_{low-k}$, and the induced interaction in a semiclassical framework. It builds on the Nuclear Field Theory approach (Broglia et al.) and the concept of $k$-mass and $\omega$-mass from Mahaux (1985). The results enable future quantitative studies of the isotopic dependence of pairing gaps and the different behavior of $v_{Gogny}$ versus $v_{low-k}+v_{ind}$ under varying temperature and rotational frequency.
