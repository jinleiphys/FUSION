# Analysis of the $b_1$ meson decay in local tensor bilinear representation

> We explore the validity of vector meson dominance in the radiative decay of the $b_1(1235)$ meson. In order to explain the violation of the vector meson dominance hypothesis in this decay process, we investigate a model where the $b_1$ meson strongly couples with the local current in tensor bilinear representation. The tensor representation is investigated in the framework of the operator product expansion and we found a low energy decay process that does not follow the usual vector meson dominance hypothesis. The $ω$-like intermediate meson state of quantum numbers $I^G(J^{PC}) = 0^{-}(1^{--})$ is found to have a nontrivial role in the decay process of the $b_1$ meson. The spectral structure of the $ω$-like state is found to be close to a $π$-$ρ$ hybrid state, which provides a mechanism that evades the usual vector meson dominance hypothesis. Precise measurements of various decay channels of the $b_1$ meson are, therefore, required to unravel the internal structure of axial vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the radiative decay \(b_1(1235) \to \pi\gamma\) cannot be fully explained by the standard vector meson dominance (VMD) hypothesis, which underestimates the decay width; instead, an additional mechanism involving a tensor bilinear representation of the spin-1 mesonic current and an \(\omega\)-like hybrid state (\(\bar{\omega}\)) provides a dominant contribution that evades the usual VMD picture.

## Method

The authors compute the correlation function for the tensor current \(\bar{q} T^A \sigma_{\mu\nu} q\) in the framework of operator product expansion (OPE) and apply QCD sum rules with Borel weighting to extract spectral properties. They analyze the \(b_1 \to \pi \omega\) decay amplitude in the soft-pion limit using chiral rotation, relating the tensor-current states (\(\bar{b}_1, \bar{\omega}\)) to physical mesons, and evaluate the mixed correlator \(\langle \mathcal{T}[J^0_\alpha(x) J^0_{\mu\bar{\mu}}(0)]\rangle\) to estimate the non-VMD contribution to \(b_1 \to \pi\gamma\).

## Key numbers

- Mass of the \(b_1(1235)\) meson: \(1229.5 \pm 3.2~\text{MeV}\) (from Particle Data Group).
- Total decay width of \(b_1\): \(\Gamma(b_1) = 142 \pm 9~\text{MeV}\), dominated by \(b_1 \to \pi\omega\).
- \(D\)-wave to \(S\)-wave amplitude ratio in \(b_1 \to \pi\omega\): \(D/S = 0.277 \pm 0.027\).
- Experimental radiative decay width: \(\Gamma(b_1 \to \pi^{\pm} \gamma) = 230 \pm 60~\text{keV}\).
- VMD-based prediction using the two-term interaction form (with \(f = 3.70\), \(h = -11.04\)): \(\Gamma(b_1 \to \pi\gamma) \approx 160~\text{keV}\), about 2/3 of the measured value.
- Momentum magnitudes: \(|\bm{p}_\gamma| = 607.0~\text{MeV}\) for radiative decay, \(|\bm{k}| = 346.5~\text{MeV}\) for strong decay (with \(M_{b_1}=1.230~\text{GeV}\), \(M_\omega=0.783~\text{GeV}\), \(M_\pi=0.140~\text{GeV}\)).
- Gluon condensate value used: \(\langle (\alpha_s/\pi) G^2 \rangle = (0.33~\text{GeV})^4\).

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of \(b_1\) meson decays, particularly addressing the failure of the VMD hypothesis noted by Rosner (1981) and by Ishida, Oda, and Yamada (1989), who predicted a much smaller width (\(\approx 69~\text{keV}\)) using a covariant oscillator quark model. The paper introduces a tensor-current representation for spin-1 mesons, building on the chiral symmetry framework of Cohen and Ji (1996), and uses QCD sum rules following Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) to analyze the spectral structure. The results suggest that the \(\bar{\omega}\) state is a \(\pi\)-\(\rho\) hybrid, not a simple one-particle state, and that precise measurements of \(b_1\) decay channels are needed to clarify the internal structure of axial-vector mesons.
