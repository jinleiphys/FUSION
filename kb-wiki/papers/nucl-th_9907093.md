# High p_T hadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the hadron spectra in high-energy pp, pA and AA collisions within a pQCD parton model. Analyses of experimental data show that the modification of the spectra in pA and AA as compared to that in $pp$ collisions is consistent with multiple parton scattering scenario and is dictated by the transition from absorptive soft interaction at low p_T to incoherent hard parton scattering at high p_T. This analysis not only sheds new light on the limits to the physics analysis based on thermal fire-ball models but also provides a more quantitative baseline results on which one can study the effects of parton energy loss in the hadron spectra at high p_T.

# High p_T hadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The modification of hadron spectra in pA and AA collisions relative to pp collisions is consistent with multiple parton scattering and a transition from absorptive soft interactions at low p_T to incoherent hard parton scattering at high p_T, but this analysis shows no evidence of parton energy loss at SPS energies, implying the lifetime of dense partonic matter may be shorter than the mean free path of a propagating parton.

## Method

The author calculates single-inclusive hadron spectra within a pQCD parton model using a factor K ≈ 2 to account for higher-order QCD corrections, with fragmentation functions parameterized from e+e- data. For pA and AA collisions, the initial transverse momentum k_T of beam partons is broadened by an amount proportional to the number of scatterings the projectile suffers inside the nucleus, using a p_T-dependent broadening per nucleon-nucleon collision. The ratio R_AB(p_T) between spectra in AB and NN collisions normalized by the average number of binary collisions is defined to study the transition from soft to hard processes.

## Key numbers

- The initial k_T variance is parameterized as ⟨k²_T⟩_N(Q) = 1 (GeV²) + 0.2 α_s(Q²) Q², with parameters chosen to reproduce experimental data at SPS energies.
- The k_T broadening per nucleon-nucleon collision is δ²(Q²) = 0.225 ln²(Q/GeV) / (1 + ln(Q/GeV)) GeV²/c².
- The transition between soft coherent interaction and hard parton scattering occurs around p_T = 1.5 GeV.
- Calculated charged pion spectra in p+p collisions at E_lab = 200 GeV are compared to experimental data from Cronin et al.
- Calculated π⁰ spectra in A+B collisions at CERN SPS energies agree with WA80 and WA98 data for p_T above 2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD parton model approach of Wang and collaborators to systematically describe hadron spectra across pp, pA, and AA collisions, providing a baseline for studying parton energy loss. The analysis contradicts thermal fire-ball models (e.g., those used to extract freeze-out temperature and collective flow velocity) by showing that the nuclear modification of spectra in the p_T region around 1 GeV can be explained by multiple parton scattering rather than hydrodynamic flow. The results enable quantitative studies of parton energy loss at higher energies such as RHIC, where dramatic suppression of high p_T hadron spectra is predicted by Wang, Gyulassy (1992) and others.
