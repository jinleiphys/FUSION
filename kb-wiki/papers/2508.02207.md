# Gravitational form factors of the nucleon from the chiral effective model

> We investigate the confining pressure and associated global property, i.e, D-term, of the nucleon using the skyrmion approach formulated within scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory. In this framework, the nucleon is modeled as a skyrmion, and a scalar meson is introduced to incorporate the effects of the scale anomaly via low-energy theorems. The contributions from the current quark mass and gluonic dynamics to the scale anomaly are encoded through the pion and scalar meson masses, respectively. By decomposing the nucleon's energy-momentum tensor, we isolate the anomalous components and analyze their role in generating pressure. We find that the gluonic contribution to the scale anomaly plays a dominant role in producing confining pressure. In comparison with results from conventional chiral perturbation theory in the chiral limit, the total pressure derived from sChPT provides improved qualitative agreement with lattice QCD results. We also evaluate the D-term and compare it with recent lattice and model-independent determinations.

# Gravitational form factors of the nucleon from the chiral effective model

## Key claim
The gluonic contribution to the scale anomaly plays a dominant role in producing confining pressure inside the nucleon, with the total pressure from scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory (sChPT) providing improved qualitative agreement with lattice QCD results compared to conventional chiral perturbation theory in the chiral limit.

## Method
The authors model the nucleon as a skyrmion within scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory (sChPT), which incorporates a scalar meson to encode scale anomaly effects via low-energy theorems. The energy-momentum tensor is decomposed into traceless (dynamical) and trace (anomalous) parts, with the anomalous part further separated into quark and gluon contributions. The coupled differential equations for the chiral field profile function F(r) and conformal compensator profile C(r) are solved numerically under hedgehog ansatz boundary conditions to obtain the baryon number one skyrmion solution.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: fπ = 68 MeV, Skyrme parameter: e = 5.45, chosen to reproduce nucleon and delta masses
- Pion mass: mπ = 140 MeV (experimental value)
- Scalar meson decay constant: fφ = 240 MeV, scalar meson mass: mφ0 = 720 MeV
- D-term value: D = -4.12
- Anomalous dimension of quark mass set to γm = 0 (nonzero values do not affect main conclusions)

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model approach to nucleon structure by incorporating scale anomaly effects through sChPT, building on the framework of Lee (2003). The results are compared with lattice QCD data from Shanahan (2018) and conventional ChPT results from Cebulla (2007). The D-term calculation is compared with recent lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) and model-independent analyses from Cao (2024). The decomposition of the energy-momentum tensor into anomalous and dynamical components, following the approach of GarciaMartin-Caro (2023), enables isolation of the scale anomaly's role in generating confining pressure, which had not been previously analyzed in this manner.
