# Transfer/Breakup Channel Couplings in Sub-barrier Fusion Reactions

> With the recent availability of state-of-the-art radioactive ion beams, there has been a renew interest in the investigation of nuclear reactions with heavy ions near the Coulomb barrier. The role of inelastic and transfer channel couplings in fusion reactions induced by stable heavy ions can be revisited. Detailed Analysis of recent experimental fusion cross sections by using standard coupled-channel calculations is first discussed. Multi-neutron transfer effects are introduced in the fusion process below the Coulomb barrier by analyzing 32S+90,96Zr as benchmark reactions. The enhancement of fusion cross sections for 32S+96Zr is well reproduced at sub-barrier energies by NTFus code calculations including the coupling of the neutron-transfer channels following the Zagrebaev semi-classical model. Similar effects for 40Ca+90Zr and 40Ca+96Zr fusion excitation functions are found. The breakup coupling in both the elastic scattering and in the fusion process induced by weakly bound stable projectiles is also shown to be crucial. In the second part of this work, full coupled-channel calculations of the fusion excitation functions are performed by using the breakup coupling for the more neutron-rich reaction and for the more weakly bound projectiles. we clearly demonstrate that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel calculations are capable to reproduce the fusion enhancement from the breakup coupling in 6Li+59Co.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multi-neutron transfer channels with positive Q-values produce a significant enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the neutron-rich system $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr, but this enhancement is absent for $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr where neutron transfer Q-values are negative. For weakly bound projectiles, Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) calculations can reproduce the fusion enhancement from breakup coupling, though the effect on total fusion is small below the barrier.

## Method

Standard coupled-channel (CC) calculations were performed for $^{32}$S+$^{90,96}$Zr fusion excitation functions using the NTFus code, which implements the Zagrebaev semi-classical model to include coupling of up to four sequential neutron-transfer channels. For $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, CDCC calculations were performed with and without $^{6}$Li $\rightarrow$ $\alpha$ + $d$ breakup couplings to the continuum, using a binning scheme matched to measured continuum excitation energy ranges. The experimental fusion cross sections were measured via evaporation residue detection at the HI-13 tandem accelerator (CIAE, Beijing) for the S+Zr systems and via $\gamma$-ray techniques at VIVITRON (Strasbourg) and Pelletron (São Paulo) for the Li+Co systems.

## Key numbers

- For $^{32}$S+$^{96}$Zr, the NTFus code including neutron-transfer coupling (up to +4n channels) reproduces the sub-barrier fusion enhancement, whereas standard CC calculations without neutron-transfer coupling do not fit the data at sub-barrier energies.
- The $^{96}$Zr nucleus has a 2$^+$ state at 1.748 MeV and a 3$^-$ state at 1.897 MeV with B(E3;3$^-$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^+$) = 51 W.u.
- For $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, CDCC calculations with breakup couplings reproduce elastic scattering angular distributions at incident energies of 30 MeV, 26 MeV, 18 MeV, and 12 MeV (shown in Fig. 4).
- The $^{6}$Li first resonant state contributing to sequential breakup is the 3$^+$ state at 2.186 MeV; no significant contribution from the 4.31 MeV 2$^+$ or 5.65 MeV 1$^+$ states was observed for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co.
- For $^{32}$S+$^{90}$Zr, a small over-estimation of fusion cross sections occurs at energies between about 82 MeV and 90 MeV above the barrier.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of multi-neutron transfer effects in sub-barrier fusion previously studied for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr by Timmers (1998) and for $^{36}$S+$^{96}$Zr by Stefanini (2000). The NTFus code calculations confirm earlier CC calculations by Zagrebaev (2003) and recent fragment-$\gamma$ coincidence measurements for $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr by Corradi (2011). The CDCC analysis of $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co builds on earlier work by Beck (2003, 2007) and provides a benchmark for understanding breakup coupling effects that will be essential for interpreting future experiments with radioactive ion beams at SPIRAL2 and with active-target time-projection chambers at MSU/NSCL.
