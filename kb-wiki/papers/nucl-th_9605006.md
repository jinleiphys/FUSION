# The Effect of Nuclear Shape

> The effect of projectile shape on cross sections and momentum distributions of fragments from heavy ion reactions is studied. We propose a new approach that implements the underlying symmetries of each isotope with a few parameters directly in the density. Various densities and their nuclear structure are then analyzed in the reactions of $^{12}$C and $^{11}$Li, $^{11}$Be, and $^{11}$C on a carbon target.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new approach that implements the underlying symmetries of each isotope with a few parameters directly in the density, rather than parameterizing the effective interaction, and claims this method can reproduce experimental reaction cross sections and fragment momentum distributions for neutron-rich nuclei, though the authors acknowledge that the hyperspherical functions method in the K_min approximation extends too far for protons and not far enough for neutrons, requiring direct modification of the density to improve agreement.

## Method

The authors use the hyperspherical functions (HSF) method to construct translationally invariant wave functions for light nuclei, expanding the radial functions in a basis of (3A-3)-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions. They then implement nuclear structure directly in the densities by using separate oscillator parameters for the s-shell, p-shell, and halo neutrons, with the scale set by the binding energy and fitted to reproduce experimental root-mean-square radii. These densities are used as input for Monte Carlo simulations of nucleon-nucleon collisions to calculate total reaction cross sections, inclusive fragment cross sections, and transverse momentum distributions for reactions of 12C, 11Li, 11Be, and 11C on a carbon target.

## Key numbers

* For 12C ground state, the overlap integral of the lowest hyperspherical radial function with the lowest oscillator function is 98%, with expansion coefficients C0 = 0.984, C1 = -0.061, C2 = 0.158, C3 = 0.055.
* For 12C + 12C at energies above 200 MeV/nucleon, the transverse momentum distribution of 11C fragments from the n(chi) density gives a single Gaussian width sigma_B = 103 MeV/c, compared to the experimental sigma_B = 105 MeV/c.
* For 11Li + 12C at 790 MeV/c, the momentum distribution of 9Li fragments has experimental broad and narrow Gaussian components with widths of 80 and 21 MeV/c and a ratio of areas B/N of 1.5.
* The outer pair of halo neutrons in 11Li has an rms radius of about 5.23 fm for the n(11Li_h) density, consistent with experimental values of 4.8 +/- 0.8 fm and 5.1(+0.6/-0.9) fm.
* Total reaction cross sections for the three A=11 isotopes on carbon are: sigma_11C = 880 mb, sigma_11Be = 946 mb, sigma_11Li = 994 mb.
* For 6Li, the calculated rms radius is 2.41 fm (experimental 2.56 fm); for 12C, the calculated rms radius is 2.48 fm (experimental 2.48 fm after adjustment).

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical functions method previously applied to lithium isotopes by Burov et al. (1994) and builds on the Monte Carlo decay method developed by Harvey (1985, 1994) for studying projectile shape effects in heavy ion reactions. The approach enables systematic investigation of nuclear shape and halo structure in neutron-rich isotopes near the drip line, and the authors note that their results for 11Li are consistent with experimental data from Tanihata et al. (1985, 1988, 1992) and Kobayashi et al. (1988).
