# The leading asymptotic terms of the three-body Coulomb scattering wave function

> The asymptotic wave function derived by Alt and Mukhamedzhanov [Phys. Rev. A 47, 2004 (1993)] and Mukhamedzhanov and Lieber [Phys. Rev. A 54, 3078 (1996)] has been refined in the region where the pair $ (β,γ)$ remains close to each other while the third particle $α$ is far away from them $(ρ_α \to \infty$, $r_α/ρ_α\to 0)$. The improved wave function satisfies the Schrödinger equation up to the terms of order $O(1/{ρ_α}^{3})$, provides the leading asymptotic terms of the three-body scattering wave function with Coulomb interactions and gives further insight into the continuum behavior of the three-charged-particle wave function and helps to obtain $3\to 3$ scattered wave. This opens up further ways of solving and analysing the three-body Schrödinger equation by numerical means.

## Key claim

The paper refines the asymptotic three-body incident wave function in the region where a pair of particles \((\beta,\gamma)\) remains close together while the third particle \(\alpha\) is far away, improving the satisfaction of the Schrödinger equation up to terms of order \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{3})\) rather than the previously achieved \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{2})\).

## Method

The authors start from the exact three-body Schrödinger equation in configuration space and use the 3C (three-Coulomb) wave function as an initial ansatz, which satisfies the equation up to \(O(1/r_{\nu}^{2})\) in the \(\Omega_{0}\) region. They then derive the asymptotic expansion of the incident wave in the \(\Omega_{\alpha}\) region by expanding the Coulomb potentials and solving for terms of order \(O(1)\), \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha})\), and \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{2})\) analytically, ensuring matching with the Redmond asymptotic term in \(\Omega_{0}\). The terms of order \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{2})\) satisfy first-order differential equations.

## Key numbers

* The improved asymptotic wave function satisfies the Schrödinger equation up to terms of order \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{3})\) in the asymptotic region \(\Omega_{\alpha}\).
* The leading asymptotic terms derived include orders \(O(1)\), \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha})\), and \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{2})\); the previously known terms from Alt and Mukhamedzhanov (1993) and Mukhamedzhanov and Lieber (1996) covered only \(O(1)\) and \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha})\).
* The three-body scattered wave is of order \(O(1/R^{5/2})\) where \(R\) is the hyperradius, making the next-order incident term \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{3})\) inferior to the scattered wave.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic analysis of Alt and Mukhamedzhanov (1993) and Mukhamedzhanov and Lieber (1996), which derived the leading asymptotic terms up to \(O(1/\rho_{\alpha})\) for three charged particles. It also builds on the Redmond asymptotic term (Redmond, 1972) and the 3C wave function used by Merkuriev and Faddeev (1993) and Garibotti and Miraglia (1980). The refined wave function enables more accurate numerical solutions of the three-body Schrödinger equation and provides the leading asymptotic terms needed to extract \(3\to 3\) scattering amplitudes, complementing the work on three-body scattered waves by Kadyrov et al. (2003).
