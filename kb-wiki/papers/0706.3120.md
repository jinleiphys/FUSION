# Some Thermodynamic Aspects of Pure Glue, Fuzzy Bags and Gauge/String Duality

> The thermodynamic properties of a SU(3) gauge theory without quarks are calculated using a string formulation for 1.2T_c < T < 3T_c. The results are in good agreement with the lattice data. We also comment on SU(N) gauge theories.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a string dual model with a deformed AdS-black-hole metric reproduces lattice thermodynamics of pure SU(3) gauge theory for 1.2 T_c < T < 3 T_c, and that the leading two terms in a 1/T^2 expansion (the "truncated model") agree with Pisarski's fuzzy bag model to better than 10% accuracy.

## Method

The author constructs a 10-dimensional string background by deforming the Euclidean AdS_5 black hole metric with a z-dependent exponential factor H = exp(½ c z^2) that breaks conformal invariance and introduces Λ_QCD. The entropy density is computed from the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy of the horizon at z = z_T = 1/(π T), yielding a series in τ = T_c^2/T^2. The pressure is obtained by integrating s = dp/dT, and the speed of sound and gluon condensate are derived from standard thermodynamic relations. The parameter c ≈ 0.9 GeV^2 is fixed from the zero-temperature ρ meson trajectory, and the overall normalization s_0 is fixed either by fitting the lattice interaction measure or by matching the bag model coefficient.

## Key numbers

- c ≈ 0.9 GeV^2, fixed from the ρ meson trajectory at zero temperature.
- T_c ≈ 300 MeV, estimated from T_c = (1/π)√c; the lattice value for SU(3) pure gauge theory is about 270 MeV.
- s_0 = 6.8 ± 0.3, from fitting the interaction measure to lattice data; the bag model gives s_0 = (32/45)π^2 ≈ 7.0.
- b ≈ 0.039, the integration constant in the pressure series determined from p(T_c) = 0.
- The truncated model (p_1 = 1 − τ) gives pressure with error less than 10% for T > 1.2 T_c.
- The speed of sound from the full model agrees with lattice to within 15% at T = 1.2 T_c and very well for T ≳ 1.7 T_c; the truncated model agrees to within 6% at T = 1.2 T_c.
- The gluon condensate at finite temperature is approximated by G_2(T) ≈ −(s_0/2) T_c^2 T^2.
- For SU(N), the ratio p/p_0 is a function of T/T_c only, independent of N, and supported by lattice data for N = 4 and N = 8 (Teper).
- The parameter 𝔤 = R^2/α' varies slowly with N: 0.93 at N = 2, 1.12 at N = 3, 1.32 at N = ∞.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/QCD approach of Andreev and collaborators (2006) that successfully described zero-temperature spectra, heavy quark potentials, and the spatial string tension of pure gauge theory. It directly tests the fuzzy bag model proposed by Pisarski (2006) for the pressure of pure glue, finding that the leading two terms in 1/T^2 provide a reliable approximation. The model enables a string dual description of SU(N) gauge thermodynamics without quarks, with predictions that can be compared to lattice data from Karsch (2002) and Teper (2005) for N = 3, 4, and 8.
