# Wigner Phase-Space Densities of Nuclear Clusters and Hypernuclei

> We solve the Schrödinger equation for few-body systems to obtain the wave function for light nuclear clusters and hypernuclei from d to $\rm ^5_{ΛΛ}He$ employing realistic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-$Λ$ potentials. We project the solution to the hyperspherical harmonic basis states to obtain the corresponding density matrices and the Wigner densities. The experimental root mean square (rms) radii and binding energies of the different clusters are well reproduced. The Wigner densities obtained will allow to improve the present coalescence approaches to identify clusters, created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for few-body nuclear clusters and hypernuclei using realistic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-Λ potentials, but they neglect spin-related potentials and genuine three-body forces, taking only spin-averaged two-body interactions. The resulting Wigner densities are intended to improve coalescence models for cluster production in heavy-ion collisions, though the approach assumes that the wavefunction can be well approximated by the dominant ground-state hyperspherical harmonic component.

## Method

The authors solve the N-body Schrödinger equation using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method, employing the Argonne v18 potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions and the Usmani potential for nucleon-Λ interactions. They project the wavefunctions onto hyperspherical harmonic basis states with total angular momentum L=0 and grand angular quantum numbers K ≤ 4 (for 2-4 body systems) or K ≤ 2 (for 5-body systems), then perform a Wigner transformation to obtain the phase-space densities. The radial probability distributions and Wigner densities are computed numerically for clusters from deuteron (d) to double-Λ hyperhelium (⁵_ΛΛHe).

## Context

This work extends prior ab initio calculations of nuclear clusters and hypernuclei (such as those using Faddeev equations by Gloeckle and collaborators, or variational methods by Kamimura) by providing not just binding energies and radii but also the full Wigner phase-space densities needed for coalescence models. Previous coalescence approaches (e.g., Scheibl and Heinz, 1998; Zhu and collaborators, 2015) used Gaussian approximations for the Wigner density, while this work provides realistic Wigner functions derived from the actual wavefunctions. The results enable improved coalescence calculations for cluster and hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as RHIC, LHC, and FAIR.
