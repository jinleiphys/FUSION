# Negative-parity baryon spectra in quenched anisotropic lattice QCD

> We investigate the negative-parity baryon spectra in quenched lattice QCD. We employ the anisotropic lattice with standard Wilson gauge and O(a) improved Wilson quark actions at three values of lattice spacings with renormalized anisotropy xi=a_sigma/a_tau=4, where a_sigma and a_tau are spatial and temporal lattice spacings, respectively. The negative-parity baryons are measured with the parity projection. In particular, we pay much attention to the lowest SU(3) flavor-singlet negative-parity baryon, which is assigned as the Lambda(1405) in the quark model. For the flavor octet and decuplet negative-parity baryons, the calculated masses are close to the experimental values of corresponding lowest-lying negative-parity baryons. In contrast, the flavor-singlet baryon is found to be about 1.7 GeV, which is much heavier than the Lambda(1405). Therefore, it is difficult to identify the Lambda(1405) to be the flavor-singlet three-quark state, which seems to support an interesting picture of the penta-quark (uds qbar q) state or the N-Kbar molecule for the Lambda(1405).

## Key claim

The authors find that the flavor-singlet negative-parity baryon mass computed in quenched lattice QCD is about 1.7 GeV, which is much heavier than the experimentally observed Λ(1405), making it difficult to identify the Λ(1405) as a flavor-singlet three-quark state and instead supporting interpretations as a penta-quark (uds q̄q) state or an N K̄ molecule.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using anisotropic lattices with standard Wilson plaquette gauge action and O(a) improved Wilson quark actions at three lattice spacings with renormalized anisotropy ξ = aσ/aτ = 4. They measured negative-parity baryon correlators with parity projection using standard interpolating operators for octet, decuplet, and singlet SU(3) flavor representations, with Gaussian smearing of quark fields. Baryon masses were extracted from single exponential fits to the correlators and extrapolated to the chiral limit using the pseudoscalar meson mass squared.

## Key numbers

- Flavor-singlet negative-parity baryon mass found to be about 1.7 GeV, much heavier than the Λ(1405) at 1405 MeV
- Three lattice spacings used with spatial lattice scales aσ⁻¹ ≈ 1.034(6) GeV (β=5.75), 1.499(9) GeV (β=5.95), and 1.871(14) GeV (β=6.10)
- 400 gauge configurations used at each β value
- Hopping parameters covered quark masses roughly in the range mq ≈ ms to 2ms
- The Λ(1405) lies about 30 MeV below the N K̄ threshold

## Context

This work extends quenched lattice QCD studies of excited-state hadrons that have recently begun (Nemoto et al. 2001, Lee 2002, UKQCD 2002) and directly tests the quark model assignment of the Λ(1405) as a flavor-singlet three-quark state. The results contradict the simple quark model predictions (Isgur and Karl 1979, Capstick and Isgur 1986) that describe octet and decuplet negative-parity baryons well, and instead support the alternative interpretation of the Λ(1405) as a penta-quark system or N K̄ hadronic molecule. The study enables future investigations of the mixing between three-quark and five-quark components in the physical Λ(1405) state, particularly through comparisons with dynamical (unquenched) lattice simulations.
