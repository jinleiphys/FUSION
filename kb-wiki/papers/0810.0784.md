# Density matrix renormalization group approach to two-fluid open many-fermion systems

> We have extended the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approach to two-fluid open many-fermion systems governed by complex-symmetric Hamiltonians. The applications are carried out for three- and four-nucleon (proton-neutron) systems within the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) in the complex-energy plane. We study necessary and sufficient conditions for the GSM+DMRG method to yield the correct ground state eigenvalue and discuss different truncation schemes within DMRG. The proposed approach will enable configuration interaction studies of weakly-bound and unbound strongly interacting complex systems which, because of a prohibitively large size of Fock space, cannot be treated by means of the direct diagonalization.

## Key claim

The paper extends the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approach to two-fluid (proton-neutron) open many-fermion systems governed by complex-symmetric Hamiltonians, but the method can fail if the reference subspace is chosen too narrowly: for the three-nucleon case, the DMRG procedure became trapped in a local minimum unless additional positive-parity scattering shells were included in the reference subspace during the warm-up phase.

## Method

The authors applied the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) with a Berggren ensemble of single-particle states, using a Woods-Saxon potential plus Coulomb for protons and a Surface Gaussian Interaction for the two-body residual interaction. The DMRG procedure was implemented in the J-scheme with a warm-up phase that gradually adds non-resonant continuum shells, followed by sweeping phases (sweep-down and sweep-up) where the density matrix is diagonalized and truncated either by keeping a fixed number of eigenvectors (N_opt) or by controlling the truncation error epsilon on the partial trace of the reduced density matrix. Calculations were performed for three-nucleon (⁷Li) and four-nucleon (⁸Li) systems, with the reference subspace initially consisting of the 0p₃/₂ Hartree-Fock poles and later extended to include selected d₅/₂ scattering shells.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷Li with 50 shells total (6 non-resonant continuum shells each for p₃/₂, p₁/₂, s₁/₂, d₅/₂ for both protons and neutrons), the exact GSM ground state energy from direct Lanczos diagonalization is E_ex = (-26.6620, 0.2486) MeV (dimension D = 7,796).
- With N_opt = 80 and the extended reference subspace including πd₅/₂ and νd₅/₂ shells, the DMRG energy deviates from the exact result by less than 1 keV for the real part and less than 0.1 keV for the imaginary part.
- The largest matrix diagonalized for N_opt = 80 has rank d_H^max = 1,469 (about 19% of the full GSM matrix dimension D = 7,796).
- For N_opt = 40, the largest matrix rank is d_H^max = 716 (about 9.1% of D).
- The asymptotic values extracted from exponential convergence analysis for ⁷Li are Re(E^(∞)_DMRG) = -26.6622 ± 0.0002 MeV and Im(E_DMRG^(∞)) = 0.248580 ± 0.000004 MeV.
- The bound single-particle p₃/₂ states in the ⁷Li basis are at energies -5.605 MeV (neutrons) and -7.098 MeV (protons).
- The SGI interaction parameters are α₁₀ = -600 MeV·fm³, β₁₀ = -50 MeV·fm³, α₃₀ = -625 MeV·fm³, β₃₀ = -100 MeV·fm³.

## Context

This work extends the previous GSM+DMRG study by Rotureau et al. (2008) from neutron-only systems to proton-neutron (two-fluid) systems, addressing the failure mode where the DMRG converges to a wrong solution when the reference subspace contains only negative-parity shells. The method enables configuration interaction studies of weakly-bound and unbound nuclei that cannot be treated by direct diagonalization due to prohibitively large Fock spaces, building on the Gamow Shell Model framework developed by Michel et al. (2002, 2004, 2006) and Berggren (1968). The approach opens the door to calculations of heavier open-shell nuclei beyond the current limits of direct diagonalization, though the choice of reference subspace must be carefully tailored to include all relevant partial waves.
