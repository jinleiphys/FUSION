# Anatomy of relativistic pion loop corrections to the electromagnetic nucleon coupling

> We present a relativistic formulation of pion loop corrections to the coupling of photons with nucleons on the light-front. Vertex and wave function renormalization constants are computed to lowest order in the pion field, including their nonanalytic behavior in the chiral limit, and studied numerically as a function of the ultraviolet cutoff. Particular care is taken to explicitly verify gauge invariance and Ward-Takahashi identity constraints to all orders in the m_pi expansion. The results are used to compute the chiral corrections to matrix elements of local operators, related to moments of deep-inelastic structure functions. Finally, comparisons of results for pseudovector and pseudoscalar coupling allows the resolution of a long-standing puzzle in the computation of pion cloud corrections to structure function moments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully relativistic light-front formulation of pion loop corrections to the electromagnetic nucleon coupling, using pseudovector (PV) coupling, satisfies gauge invariance and Ward-Takahashi identities to all orders in the pion mass expansion, and that the pseudovector and pseudoscalar (PS) theories yield different results for the vertex renormalization factors, resolving a long-standing puzzle in the computation of pion cloud corrections to structure function moments.

## Method

The authors computed vertex and wave function renormalization constants to lowest order in the pion field using light-front perturbation theory, evaluating all one-loop diagrams including rainbow, tadpole, Kroll-Ruderman contact terms, and bubble diagrams for both pseudovector and pseudoscalar pion-nucleon couplings. They performed the loop integrations using a cutoff regularization scheme, with the $k^-$ integration cutoff $\mu = 1$ GeV and a variable $k_\perp$ cutoff $\Lambda$, and verified gauge invariance through the relation $(1-Z_1^N) = (1-Z_1^\pi) + (1-Z_1^{\rm KR})$ for the PV case. The results were then applied to compute chiral corrections to matrix elements of twist-2 operators related to moments of deep-inelastic structure functions.

## Key numbers

- $f_\pi = 93$ MeV (pion decay constant)
- $g_A = 1.267$ (nucleon axial vector charge)
- $g_{\pi NN} \approx 13.4$ (pion-nucleon coupling constant)
- The $k^-$ integration cutoff was set to $\mu = 1$ GeV for numerical illustrations
- The leading nonanalytic behavior of the nucleon self-energy is $m_\pi^3$ for PV coupling, in contrast to $m_\pi^2 \log m_\pi^2$ for PS coupling
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation $g_A/f_\pi = g_{\pi NN}/M$ connects the coupling constants

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of pion loop effects on the nucleon self-energy (Ji, Melnitchouk, Thomas 2009) to the more physically relevant case of the electromagnetic vertex, and builds on earlier studies of twist-2 matrix elements by Chen and Ji (2001) and Arndt and Savage (2002) within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The results enable the computation of chiral corrections to quark distribution functions of the nucleon in a fully relativistic light-front framework, and resolve discrepancies between heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations and parton-level Sullivan process analyses regarding the nonanalytic behavior of twist-2 moments.
