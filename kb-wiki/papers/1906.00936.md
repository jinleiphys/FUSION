# Mapping the Phases of Quantum Chromodynamics with Beam Energy Scan

> We review the present status of the search for a phase transition and critical point as well as anomalous transport phenomena in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), with an emphasis on the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider at Brookhaven National Laboratory. We present the conceptual framework and discuss the observables deemed most sensitive to a phase transition, QCD critical point, and anomalous transport, focusing on fluctuation and correlation measurements. Selected experimental results for these observables together with those characterizing the global properties of the systems created in heavy ion collisions are presented. We then discuss what can be already learned from the currently available data about the QCD critical point and anomalous transport as well as what additional measurements and theoretical developments are needed in order to discover these phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current status of the search for a QCD phase transition and critical point as well as anomalous transport phenomena, with the hedge that while some experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan program are already available, definitive discovery of these phenomena requires additional measurements and theoretical developments.

## Method

The authors present a conceptual framework for mapping the QCD phase diagram through heavy ion collisions at varying beam energies, focusing on fluctuation and correlation measurements as the observables most sensitive to a phase transition, critical point, and anomalous transport. They review selected experimental results from the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, particularly cumulant measurements of net-proton, net-charge, and net-kaon distributions in Au+Au collisions, and discuss what can be learned from currently available data.

## Key numbers

- STAR Collaboration measured cumulants of net-proton distributions in Au+Au collisions (Adamczyk 2013)
- STAR Collaboration measured cumulants of net-charge distributions in Au+Au collisions (Adamczyk 2014)
- STAR Collaboration measured cumulants of net-kaon distributions in Au+Au collisions (Adamczyk 2017)
- For a bi-modal distribution fitting the measured STAR cumulants, the difference between the two component means is approximately 15 particles (ave(N_a) - ave(N_b) approximately 15)
- For the negative binomial distribution, the factorial cumulant is given by C_hat_i = (i-1)! * (average N)^i / k^(i-1), where k measures deviation from Poisson distribution

## Context

This review extends the theoretical and experimental framework established by the Beam Energy Scan Theory (BEST) Topical Collaboration and builds upon earlier work on QCD phase structure and critical phenomena. The paper synthesizes results from multiple experimental measurements at RHIC and identifies what additional measurements and theoretical developments are needed to discover the QCD critical point and anomalous transport, enabling future higher-statistics runs and improved theoretical calculations.
