# Coherent Pair State of Pion in Constituent Quark Model

> A coherent state of pions is introduced to the nonrelativistic quark model. The coherent pair approximation is employed for the pion field in order to maintain the spin-isospin symmetry. In this approximation the pion is localized in the momentum space, and the vertex form factor in the pion-quark interaction is derived from this localization. The nucleon masses and wave functions are calculated using this model, and our results are compared to those of the quark model with the one pion exchange potential. Similar result is obtained for the mass spectrum, but there exists a clear difference in the internal structure of nucleon resonances.

# Coherent Pair State of Pion in Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The coherent pair approximation (CPA) for the pion field, when introduced into the nonrelativistic constituent quark model, yields a nucleon mass spectrum qualitatively similar to that of the one-pion exchange potential (OPEP) model, but produces a clear difference in the internal structure of nucleon resonances, specifically in the mixing coefficients of negative parity states and the composition of the first positive parity excitation.

## Method

The authors construct a model Hamiltonian consisting of three constituent quarks in a harmonic oscillator confinement potential, a free pion field, and a pseudoscalar pion-quark interaction. The pion field is treated nonperturbatively using the coherent pair approximation, where the vacuum state contains scalar-isoscalar pion pairs and the 1-unpaired pion state carries orbital angular momentum and isospin. The momentum distribution function of the pion field is determined variationally by minimizing the ground state nucleon energy, and the nucleon states are expanded in a basis of three-quark states (up to 2ħω excitation) combined with 0- and 1-unpaired coherent pair states. Results are compared to those from the standard OPEP model with a Gaussian regularization function.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV (experimental value used)
- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 300 MeV
- Confinement potential strength in CPA model: ω = 380 MeV
- Pion-quark coupling constant in CPA model: g = 5.10
- Confinement potential strength in OPEP model: ω = 550 MeV
- Pion-quark coupling constant in OPEP model: g = 1.75
- OPEP regularization parameter: κ = 5.0 fm⁻²
- N-Δ mass difference: about 120 MeV in CPA model, about 80 MeV in OPEP model (observed value ~290 MeV)
- Negative parity state masses in CPA model: 1537 MeV and 1641 MeV
- Negative parity state masses in OPEP model: 1529 MeV and 1646 MeV
- Ground state nucleon N(940) in CPA model: 86.0% three-quark state with 0-unpaired pion, with 29.3% and 33.4% admixtures from 1-unpaired pion components
- Ground state nucleon in OPEP model: 99.8% pure three-quark state
- Δ(1232) in CPA model: 89.8% three-quark state with 0-unpaired pion, 38.9% from 1-unpaired pion component
- Δ(1232) in OPEP model: 99.98% pure three-quark state

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) of Isgur and collaborators (1978, 1979) by introducing a nonperturbative treatment of the pion field via the coherent pair approximation, previously applied to the ground state nucleon by Goeke et al. (1988) and by Alkofer et al. (1999). The authors compare their results to the OPEP model of Glozman et al. (1996, 1998) and Kowalska et al. (1998), which treats pions perturbatively. The CPA model avoids the spin-isospin symmetry breaking of the hedgehog ansatz used in other nonperturbative approaches, and does not require the projection methods needed for that ansatz. The work enables future studies of baryon resonances where the pion field fluctuations are comparable to quark excitation energies, and suggests that additional degrees of freedom beyond pions are needed to explain the Roper resonance N(1440) and πN/ηN reaction data.
