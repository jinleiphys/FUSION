# Neutrino signals in electron-capture storage-ring experiments

> Neutrino signals in electron-capture decays of hydrogen-like parent ions P in storage-ring experiments at GSI are reconsidered, with special emphasis placed on the storage-ring quasi-circular motion of the daughter ions D in two-body decays P --> D + e-neutrino. It is argued that to the extent that daughter ions are detected, these detection rates might exhibit modulations with periods of order seconds, similar to those reported in the GSI storage-ring experiments for two-body decay rates [1,2]. New dedicated experiments in storage rings, or using traps, could explore these modulations.

# Neutrino signals in electron-capture storage-ring experiments

## Key claim
The paper argues that modulations in detection rates of daughter ions from two-body electron-capture decays in storage rings, similar to those reported at GSI with periods of order seconds, can arise from the quasi-circular storage-ring motion of the daughter ions themselves, provided some coherence is sustained after electron cooling disentangles the daughter ion from the emitted neutrino.

## Method
The author derives the time evolution of daughter ions in storage-ring confined quasi-circular motion, starting from the entangled parent-ion decay state expanded in mass-eigenstate neutrino basis. The external magnetic field of the storage ring is argued to disentangle the daughter ion from the neutrino momentarily upon decay, allowing the daughter-ion amplitude to be expressed as a sum over mass-eigenstate components weighted by probabilities |U_ej|^2 rather than amplitudes. The interference terms between different mass-eigenstate components of the daughter ion are then evaluated, with spatial phase differences suppressed due to the confined motion, leaving only temporal phase differences that produce oscillations.

## Key numbers
- The GSI modulation angular frequency ω_EC corresponds to an energy splitting ħω_EC ≈ 0.83 × 10^{-15} eV in the parent-ion rest frame.
- The modulation amplitude A = 0.107 ± 0.024 for the GSI experiment on 142Pm.
- The squared-mass difference Δm_ν^2 = (7.6 ± 0.2) × 10^{-5} eV^2 for the two dominant mass-eigenstate neutrinos.
- The rest mass of 142Pm is M_P ≈ 132 GeV, giving |E_ν2 - E_ν1| = Δm_ν^2 / (2M_P) = 0.29 × 10^{-15} eV, approximately one-third of ħω_EC.
- The predicted daughter-ion oscillation period in the lab frame is T_osc^(L) = 10.17 ± 0.26 s, compared to the reported GSI value of 7.12 ± 0.11 s, with a ratio of 1.43 ± 0.06 consistent with γ = 1.42.
- The predicted modulation amplitude from the model is A ≈ 0.733 ± 0.021, using sin^2(2θ) = 0.846 ± 0.021.
- The Lorentz factor for the storage ring is β = 0.71 and γ = 1.42.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of time modulations in two-body electron-capture decays first reported by GSI (2008, 2013) for hydrogen-like ions such as 142Pm in the ESR storage ring. It addresses the critique by Giunti (2008), Kienert (2008), and others that amplitude interference cannot occur in standard rectilinear decays because mass-eigenstate neutrinos must be summed incoherently. The author argues that the storage-ring magnetic field disentangles the daughter ion from the neutrino, making the daughter-ion motion distinct from rectilinear cases studied by Dolgov (1997) and Kayser and collaborators (2012). The work enables future dedicated storage-ring or trap experiments to test whether daughter-ion oscillations produce the observed modulation, and calls for further study of electron cooling effects on coherence.
