# Concomitant Modulated Superfluidity In Polarized Fermionic Gases

> Recent groundbreaking experiments studying the effects of spin polarization on pairing in unitary Fermi gases encountered mutual qualitative and quantitative discrepancies which seem to be a function of the confining geometry. Using novel numerical algorithms we study the solution space for a 3-dimensional fully self-consistent formulation of realistic systems with up to $10^{5}$ atoms. A study of the three types of solutions obtained demonstrates a tendency towards metastability as the confining geometry is elongated. One of these solutions, which is consistent with Rice experiments at high trap aspect ratio, supports a state strikingly similar to the long sought Fulde-Ferrel-Larkin-Ovchinnikov state. Our study helps to resolve the long-standing controversy concerning the discrepancies between the findings from two different experimental groups and highlights the versatility of actual-size numerical calculations for investigating inhomogeneous fermionic superfluids.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the apparent discrepancies between Rice and MIT experiments on spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases can be resolved by recognizing that the Rice experiment likely observed a metastable, LDA-violating state rather than the global free-energy minimum. The authors claim that one of these metastable solutions, the P-SF state, contains a phase "strikingly similar" to the long-sought Fulde-Ferrel-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) state, but they hedge that this identification is based on similarity rather than definitive proof.

## Method
The authors solved the 3D Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently for a cigar-shaped harmonic trap at unitarity, using a piece-wise linear finite-element basis and a modified Broyden's method for iteration. They fixed total atom number N and polarization P, and explored the solution space by starting from different initial ansätze for the superfluid gap, including an LDA-like ansatz and axially shortened ansätze that mimic the condensate nucleation process described by Parish (2010). Calculations were performed for systems with up to 5×10^4 atoms at a small temperature T = 0.02 E_F/k_B.

## Key numbers
- Total particle numbers up to N = 5×10^4 were studied, with trap aspect ratio α = 50 and polarization P = 0.3.
- Free energies per particle for the different states at N=50000, α=50, P=0.3: P-N state 0.670 E_F, P-SF state 0.658 E_F, SF state 0.655 E_F, and LDA state 0.644 E_F.
- A signal-to-noise ratio of approximately 6.5 is required to observe at least half of the FFLO phase in the doubly integrated axial spin density.
- The Rice experimental parameters used for comparison were N ≈ 260000, P ≈ 0.35, α = 45.23, and T < 0.05 E_F/k_B.
- For large particle numbers (N ≳ 10^4), the LDA-like solution has the lowest free energy but is not accessed when starting from a shortened initial ansatz.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical efforts using the local density approximation (LDA) by Mueller (2005) and others, which could not capture states like the FFLO phase. It directly addresses the controversy between the Rice experiments (2006-2007) which observed deformed superfluid cores in cigar-shaped traps, and the MIT experiments (2006) which found no such deformations in more spherical traps. The calculations build on variational studies by Yoshida and Yip (2007) and Bulgac and Forbes (2008) that suggested larger FFLO stability regions, and on the transport model of Parish (2010) for condensate nucleation. The work enables future experimental searches for the FFLO state in elongated traps and demonstrates that fully self-consistent 3D calculations with realistic particle numbers are now feasible for finite fermionic systems.
