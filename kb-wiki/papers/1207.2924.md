# Endpoint of $rp$ process using relativistic mean field approach and a new mass formula

> Densities from relativistic mean field calculations are applied to construct the optical potential and, hence calculate the endpoint of the rapid proton capture ($rp$) process. Mass values are taken from a new phenomenological mass formula. Endpoints are calculated for different temperature- density profiles of various X-ray bursters. We find that the $rp$ process can produce significant quantities of nuclei upto around mass 95. Our results differ from existing works to some extent.

## Key claim

Using relativistic mean field (RMF) densities with the FSU Gold Lagrangian and a new phenomenological mass formula (rms error 0.376 MeV), the authors find that the rapid proton capture (rp) process endpoint lies around mass 95 for X-ray burst conditions, but they note that their results "differ from existing works to some extent," implying the endpoint is not a fixed value and depends on the specific temperature-density profile of the burster.

## Method

The authors constructed microscopic optical potentials by folding density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interactions with nuclear density profiles from spherical RMF calculations using the FSU Gold Lagrangian. They obtained nuclear masses from a new phenomenological mass formula fitted to 2140 known nuclei with an rms error of 0.376 MeV, and calculated proton capture rates using the Hauser-Feshbach code TALYS. A reaction network extending to A=110 was built, incorporating (p,gamma) and (gamma,p) reactions via the reciprocity theorem, together with beta-decay half-lives from Audi et al. and Möller et al., and alpha-decay where relevant, to determine the nucleosynthesis path and relative abundances for different X-ray burster temperature-density profiles.

## Key numbers

- The new mass formula predicts known masses of 2140 nuclei with an rms error of 0.376 MeV.
- The rp process can produce significant quantities of nuclei up to around mass 95.
- The zero-range pseudo-potential in the DDM3Y interaction is J00(E) = -276 (1 - 0.005 E/A) MeV fm^3.
- The density-dependent factor uses constants C = 2.07 and beta = 1.624 fm^2.
- X-ray bursts have a typical timescale of 100 seconds and a peak proton flux density of order 10^6 gram/cm^3, with peak temperatures of 1-2 GK.
- The photodisintegration rate formula includes the factor exp(-11.605 Q / T) where Q is in MeV and T in GK.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Schatz et al., who used masses from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and rates from the Hauser-Feshbach code NON-SMOKER, finding a different endpoint. The authors' microscopic approach using RMF densities and a new mass formula aims to reduce uncertainties in reaction rates for nuclei near the proton drip line, where experimental data are unavailable. The same mass formula had previously been successfully applied to predict the proton drip line and rp process up to mass 80, and to predict peaks in the r process, so this work extends that analysis to higher masses and the endpoint region.
