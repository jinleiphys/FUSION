# Effective tensor forces and neutron rich nuclei

> We study the effects of the tensor term of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction on nuclear excited states. Our investigation has been conducted by using a self-consistent Random Phase Approximation approach. We investigate various nuclei in different regions of the isotopes chart. Results for a set of calcium isotopes are shown.

# Effective tensor forces and neutron rich nuclei

## Key claim

The tensor term of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, usually neglected in Hartree-Fock (HF) and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations, produces negligible effects on bulk nuclear properties such as binding energies and density distributions, but significantly affects single-particle energies and specific excited states, particularly in nuclei where neutron spin-orbit partner levels are not fully occupied.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent HF and RPA calculations for 16 doubly magic nuclei across the oxygen, calcium, zirconium, tin, and lead regions, adding a tensor-isospin term based on the Argonne V8' microscopic potential (multiplied by a short-range correlation function with a single free parameter b) to the Gogny D1S and D1M interactions. They iteratively adjusted the parameter b to reproduce the experimental energy of the first 0⁻ excited state in ¹⁶O and modified the spin-orbit term to match the neutron 1p₃/₂–1p₁/₂ splitting, producing two new parametrizations called D1SV8 and D1MV8.

## Key numbers

- Experimental energy of the first 0⁻ excited state in ¹⁶O: 10.96 MeV (Led 1978)
- 4⁻ excited state energy in ⁴⁰Ca: experimental 7.66 MeV; D1S calculation 7.50 MeV; D1SV8 calculation 7.41 MeV; D1M calculation 7.04 MeV; D1MV8 calculation 6.70 MeV
- 4⁻ excited state energy in ⁴⁸Ca: experimental 6.11 MeV; D1S calculation 7.91 MeV; D1SV8 calculation 8.61 MeV; D1M calculation 6.81 MeV; D1MV8 calculation 7.30 MeV
- 4⁻ excited state energy in ⁵²Ca: no experimental value; D1S calculation 7.04 MeV; D1SV8 calculation 9.03 MeV; D1M calculation 6.77 MeV; D1MV8 calculation 7.84 MeV
- Binding energy variations from tensor force inclusion: less than 1% of values obtained without tensor terms
- Density distribution differences between calculations with and without tensor force: few parts per thousand

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor force effects on single-particle energies by Otsuka (2005) to self-consistent RPA calculations of excited states, using the Gogny D1S (Berger 1991) and D1M (Goriely 2009) interactions. The results contradict the common practice of absorbing tensor effects into other force parameters, showing that explicit tensor terms are necessary for accurate description of neutron-rich nuclei. The findings enable improved effective interactions for nuclear structure calculations in exotic neutron-rich systems, particularly for excitations dominated by specific particle-hole pairs such as the 4⁻ states in calcium isotopes.
