## Key claim
The paper claims that the $N^{\star}$ resonance (a quasi-bound state of the $K\bar{K}N$ system with $I=1/2$, $J^\pi=1/2^+$) can be observed as a bump in the $\pi\Sigma K$ and $\pi\eta N$ mass spectra from $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K$ reactions, but the shape and visibility of the signal depend on the specific $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ potential model used and are affected by branch points from two-body resonances like $\Lambda(1405)$ and $a_0(980)$.

## Method
The author solved the homogeneous and inhomogeneous Faddeev AGS equations for the $K\bar{K}N$ three-body system using separable potentials for the two-body interactions ($\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$, $K\bar{K}-\pi\pi-\pi\eta$, and $KN$). Transition probabilities for the $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K \rightarrow \pi\Sigma K$ and $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K \rightarrow \pi\eta N$ reactions were calculated for initial spectator momenta $\bar{P}_K = 100$ to $400$ MeV/c, using three different $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ potentials: two phenomenological SIDD potentials (one-pole and two-pole versions of $\Lambda(1405)$) and one energy-dependent chiral SU(3) potential.

## Key numbers
- The $K\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound state binding energies relative to the $K\bar{K}N$ threshold are $-24.5$ MeV (chiral potential), $-38.3$ MeV (SIDD$^1$), and $-43.6$ MeV (SIDD$^2$).
- The width of the three-hadron decay is estimated to be $45 \sim 110$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance has a binding energy of $27$ MeV relative to the $\bar{K}N$ threshold.
- The $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances are taken with mass $980$ MeV and width $60$ MeV.
- The $KN$ scattering length for $I=1$ is $a^{I=1}_{KN} = -0.310 \pm 0.003$ fm, reproduced with strength parameter $\lambda^{I=1}_{KN} = 2.794$ fm$^{-2}$.
- The range parameter for the $KN$ potential is $\Lambda_{KN} = 3.9$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The $K\bar{K}$ potential range parameters are $\Lambda_{K\bar{K}}^0 = 3.570$ fm$^{-1}$ and $\Lambda_{K\bar{K}}^1 = 3.396$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The transition probabilities for $\pi\eta N$ from $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K$ are $2$ to $4$ times larger than those for $\pi\Sigma K$.

## Context
This work extends prior variational calculations by Jido et al. (2010) who found a $K\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound state with binding energy $19 \sim 41$ MeV and width $90 \sim 100$ MeV, and coupled-channel relativistic Faddeev calculations by other groups. The present results confirm that the $K\bar{K}N$ pole position is in the same range as those earlier studies. The paper enables experimental searches for this $N^{\star}$ resonance in $\gamma p$ and $pp$ reactions, specifically through the $\pi\Sigma K$ and $\pi\eta N$ mass spectra, and suggests that the CLAS experiment at JLab and the HADES experiment at GSI could observe the signal.
