# Implications of the GALLEX Source Experiment for the Solar Neutrino Problem

> We argue that, prior to the recent GALLEX $^{51}$Cr source experiment, the excited state contributions to the $^{71}$Ga capture cross section for $^{51}$Cr and $^7$Be neutrinos were poorly constrained, despite forward-angle (p,n) measurements. We describe the origin of the uncertainties and estimate their extent. We explore the implications of the source experiment for solar neutrino capture in light of these uncertainties. A reanalysis of the $^7$Be and $^8$B flux constraints and MSW solutions of the solar neutrino puzzle is presented.

## Key claim

Prior to the GALLEX 51Cr source experiment, the excited state contributions to the 71Ga capture cross section for 51Cr and 7Be neutrinos were poorly constrained despite forward-angle (p,n) measurements, and the source experiment removes most of this nuclear structure uncertainty, allowing the 7Be cross section to be determined to within about 3% and revealing that the solar neutrino data are in serious conflict with astrophysical explanations.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the (p,n) measurements of Krofcheck et al. (1985) for the 5/2- (175 keV) and 3/2- (500 keV) excited states in 71Ge, demonstrating that an L=2 tensor operator contribution to the (p,n) reaction can mimic Gamow-Teller strength for weak transitions. They used shell model calculations with Cohen-Kurath and Brown-Wildenthal wave functions to quantify this effect, then combined the GALLEX source experiment result with the reanalyzed (p,n) constraints to derive new bounds on the unknown BGT values and re-evaluate the 7Be and 8B flux constraints and MSW solutions.

## Key numbers

- BGT(gs) for 71Ga to 71Ge ground state transition: 0.087 ± 0.001, from the known 11.43 day lifetime of 71Ge
- GALLEX source experiment ratio R = 1.04 ± 0.12 (1 sigma), the ratio of measured 71Ge atoms to expected
- Nuclear-structure-free source experiment result R0 = 1.09 ± 0.13 (1 sigma), where R0 = E[1 + 0.667 BGT(5/2-)/BGT(gs) + 0.218 BGT(3/2-)/BGT(gs)]
- (p,n) BGT values from Krofcheck et al.: BGT(p,n)(5/2-) < 0.005 and BGT(p,n)(3/2-) = 0.011 ± 0.002
- Reanalysis yields allowed ranges: 0 ≤ BGT(5/2-) ≤ 0.087 and 0 ≤ BGT(3/2-) ≤ 0.057
- Source experiment constraint: α = 0.667 BGT(5/2-)/BGT(gs) + 0.218 BGT(3/2-)/BGT(gs) = 0.09 ± 0.13 (1 sigma)
- Residual nuclear structure uncertainty in 7Be cross section after source experiment: at most 3% contribution
- GALLEX total capture rate: 79 ± 10 ± 6 SNU (1 sigma)
- With maximal BGT values, efficiency E could be as low as 0.54, giving effective pp and 8B rates of 38 and 9 SNU in the Bahcall-Pinsonneault standard solar model

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the GALLEX collaboration's 51Cr source experiment (Anselmann et al., 1995) by challenging the accepted 5% excited state contribution estimate from Bahcall and Ulrich (1988). The authors show that previous systematics arguments from Bahcall (1978) and (p,n) measurements from Krofcheck et al. (1985) were unreliable for weak transitions, using comparisons with known beta decay transitions in the 1p and 2s1d shells to demonstrate that (p,n) BGT values can exceed beta decay values by factors of 7 to 100 for very weak transitions. The reanalysis enables precise determination of the 7Be neutrino capture cross section and allows the authors to show that nonstandard solar models, which generally reduce 8B flux more than 7Be flux, are in contradiction with the combined data from SAGE, GALLEX, Kamiokande, and Homestake experiments.
