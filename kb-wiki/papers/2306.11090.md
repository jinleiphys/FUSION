# Distinctive nuclear signatures of low-energy atmospheric neutrinos

> New probes of neutrino mixing are needed to advance precision studies. One promising direction is via the detection of low-energy atmospheric neutrinos (below a few hundred MeV), to which a variety of near-term experiments will have much-improved sensitivity. Here we focus on probing these neutrinos through distinctive nuclear signatures of exclusive neutrino-carbon interactions -- those that lead to detectable nuclear-decay signals with low backgrounds -- in both neutral-current and charged-current channels. The neutral-current signature is a line at 15.11 MeV and the charged-current signatures are two- or three-fold coincidences with delayed decays. We calculate the prospects for identifying such events in the Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO), a large-scale liquid-scintillator detector. A five-year exposure would yield about 16 neutral-current events (all flavors) and about 16 charged-current events (mostly from $ν_e + \barν_e$, with some from $ν_μ+ \barν_μ$), and thus roughly 25\% uncertainties on each of their rates. Our results show the potential of JUNO to make the first identified measurement of sub-100 MeV atmospheric neutrinos. They also are a step towards multi-detector studies of low-energy atmospheric neutrinos, with the goal of identifying additional distinctive nuclear signatures for carbon and other targets.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy atmospheric neutrinos (below a few hundred MeV) can be probed through exclusive neutrino-carbon interactions with distinctive nuclear-decay signals, and that a five-year exposure in JUNO would yield about 16 neutral-current and 16 charged-current events, leading to roughly 25% uncertainties on each rate, but this is a first-step estimate with cross-section uncertainties at the few tens of percent level, and the true detection efficiency will be somewhat less than unity.

## Method

The authors calculated exclusive neutrino-carbon cross sections using the elementary particle treatment (EPT) method for superallowed transitions within the A=12 triad (carbon, nitrogen, boron), focusing on neutral-current excitation of the 15.11-MeV state in 12C and charged-current transitions to ground states of 12N and 12B. They combined these cross sections with atmospheric-neutrino fluxes from Zhuang (2021) calculated for the JUNO site, including neutrino mixing effects via the nuCRAFT code, and estimated event rates for a 17-kton fiducial volume over five years in JUNO.

## Key numbers

- Neutral-current signal: about 16 events from all neutrino flavors in a five-year exposure (85 kton-yr), with a gamma-ray line at 15.11 MeV
- Charged-current signal: about 16 events (mostly from νe + ν̄e, with some from νμ + ν̄μ) in the same exposure
- Threshold energies: NC excitation at 15.11 MeV; CC νe threshold 17.34 MeV; CC ν̄e threshold 14.39 MeV; CC νμ threshold 122.49 MeV; CC ν̄μ threshold 119.54 MeV
- 12N ground-state beta-decay halflife: 11 ms with Q-value 17.34 MeV
- 12B ground-state beta-decay halflife: 20 ms with Q-value 13.37 MeV
- Muon decay halflife in carbon-based scintillator: 1.4 μs, with about 8% of μ− undergoing nuclear capture
- JUNO energy resolution: ~3%/√(E/MeV); position resolution: ≤10 cm
- Fiducial mass: 17 kton; number of carbon targets: ~7.5 × 10^32

## Context

This work extends earlier inclusive atmospheric-neutrino studies in JUNO by Cheng (2020) and the JUNO collaboration (2021), which did not call attention to these exclusive channels. It builds on prior theoretical work on superallowed transitions in the A=12 triad by Fukugita (1988), Engel (1996), Kubodera (1993), and Kolbe (1996), and on experimental cross-section measurements from KARMEN and LSND. The results enable a path toward the first identified measurement of sub-100 MeV atmospheric neutrinos and motivate multi-detector studies of additional distinctive nuclear signatures for carbon and other targets.
