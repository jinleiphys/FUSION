# On Isgur's "Critique of a Pion Exchange Model for Interquark Forces"

> The conceptual issues of low-energy baryon physics are discussed. In particular, a comparison between the naive one gluon exchange model for the interaction between constituent quarks in hadrons and the Goldstone boson exchange picture is made. The "defects" of the Goldstone boson exchange model for baryons, indicated by Isgur are examined in detail. {\it All} of the purported ``defects'' are shown to lack a valid basis.

# On Isgur's "Critique of a Pion Exchange Model for Interquark Forces"

## Key claim

All of Isgur's purported "defects" of the Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) model for baryons are shown to lack a valid basis; the paper argues that the GBE model, not the one gluon exchange (OGE) model, correctly describes low-energy baryon physics because chiral symmetry breaking and Goldstone boson exchange are inseparable consequences of nonperturbative QCD dynamics in the light quark sector.

## Method

The paper systematically rebuts each of Isgur's criticisms by comparing the conceptual foundations of the OGE and GBE models for baryon spectroscopy. It examines the spectral ordering of positive and negative parity states in nucleon, Delta, and Lambda spectra, the spin-orbit problem, baryon internal wave functions and mixing angles, meson hyperfine splittings, and the connection to heavy quark baryons, using exact three-body calculations, lattice QCD results, and phenomenological analyses from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The positive parity state N(1440) lies approximately 100 MeV below the negative parity multiplet N(1535)-N(1520) in the nucleon spectrum
- The lowest positive parity state in the Lambda spectrum lies 100-200 MeV above the lowest negative parity doublet
- A model with no confining interaction relying exclusively on the Coulomb part of OGE cannot provide the required 500 MeV gap between ground state baryons and the first negative parity excitation band
- The hyperfine D-D* splitting is 141.4 MeV and D_S-D_S* splitting is 143.8 MeV, showing absence of 1/m_q behavior when light quark mass changes by 30-50%
- The hyperfine B-B* splitting is 45.7 MeV and B_S-B_S* splitting is 47.0 MeV
- The spin-spin force in light baryons provides splitting at the level of 30% of hadron mass (N-Delta splitting), compared to approximately 3% in charmonium
- The net spin-orbit splittings observed in L=1 light and strange baryons are small, 10-50 MeV

## Context

This paper directly responds to Isgur's critique of the GBE model, which was developed by Glozman, Riska, and collaborators (1990s) as an alternative to the Isgur-Karl OGE model (1970s-1980s). The work extends the debate over whether baryon spectroscopy is governed by perturbative gluon exchange or by chiral symmetry breaking dynamics, citing lattice calculations by Liu et al. and the RIKEN-BNL-Columbia-KEK collaboration that support the GBE picture. The paper enables further development of chiral constituent quark models and their application to baryon form factors, as demonstrated by subsequent relativistic calculations by Wagenbrunn and collaborators.
