# Trace anomaly as signature of conformality in neutron stars

> We discuss an interpretation that a peak in the sound velocity in neutron star matter, as suggested by the observational data, signifies strongly-coupled conformal matter. The normalized trace anomaly is a dimensionless measure of conformality leading to the derivative and the non-derivative contributions to the sound velocity. We find that the peak in the sound velocity is attributed to the derivative contribution from the trace anomaly that steeply approaches the conformal limit. Smooth continuity to the behavior of high-density QCD implies that the matter part of the trace anomaly may be positive definite. We discuss a possible implication of the positivity condition of the trace anomaly on the $M$-$R$ relation of the neutron stars.

## Key claim

The peak in the sound velocity observed in neutron star matter is not a violation of the conformal bound but rather a signature of strongly-coupled conformal matter, arising from the derivative contribution of the normalized trace anomaly as it steeply approaches zero.

## Method

The authors decompose the sound velocity \(v_s^2 = dP/d\varepsilon\) into derivative and non-derivative contributions expressed in terms of the normalized trace anomaly \(\Delta \equiv (\varepsilon-3P)/(3\varepsilon)\). They extract \(\Delta\) from four independent equations of state (EoSs) inferred from neutron star observations, including Gaussian process interpolations between chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT) at low density and perturbative QCD (pQCD) at high density. A minimal parametrization of \(\Delta\) as a monotonic function of logarithmic energy density \(\eta = \ln(\varepsilon/\varepsilon_0)\) is used to demonstrate the decomposition of the sound velocity.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) and the corresponding energy density is \(\varepsilon_0 = 150\,\mathrm{MeV}/\mathrm{fm}^3\).
- The fit parameters for the monotonic parametrization of \(\Delta\) are \(\kappa = 3.45\), \(\eta_c = 1.2\), \(A = 2\), and \(B = 20\).
- In a simple vector-interaction model, the density for \(v_s^2 \to 1/3\) is reached at \(\nB \sim N_c \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^3 / (4C)\), which is lower than the density for \(\Delta \to 0\) at \(\nB \sim N_c \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^3/(2C)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of the trace anomaly from finite-temperature QCD, where lattice simulations (Boyd 1995, 1996) show a peak in the normalized trace anomaly at the deconfinement temperature, to finite baryon density in neutron stars. It contradicts the earlier conjecture (Cherman 2009) that \(1/3 - v_s^2 \ge 0\) at all densities, which is in tension with two-solar-mass pulsar observations (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The analysis enables future tests of the proposed positivity condition \(\Delta \ge 0\) using neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformability measurements from multimessenger observations.
