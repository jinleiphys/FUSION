# Signatures of chirality in the core-particle-hole systems

> An odd-odd nucleus is treated as the core-particle-hole system. The core is described by the Bohr Hamiltonian. Different collective potentials of the core are investigated. The odd particle and hole are assumed to be in the symmetric [proton h11/2]x[neutron hole h11/2] configuration. Signatures of chirality in the odd-odd nucleus spectra are observed. The sufficient condition for the appearance of signatures of chirality in the core-particle-hole system is the alpha-symmetry of the core provided the particle-hole configuration of the odd valence particles is symmetric.

# Signatures of chirality in the core-particle-hole systems

## Key claim

The sufficient condition for the appearance of all signatures of chirality in the core-particle-hole system is the alpha-symmetry of the core, provided the particle-hole configuration of the odd valence particles is symmetric (proton-neutron symmetric). This condition holds regardless of the core's rigidity or softness, but even a small deviation from alpha-symmetry causes the signatures to vanish immediately.

## Method

The odd-odd nucleus is treated as a three-body system consisting of an even-even core described by the Bohr Hamiltonian, a proton, and a neutron-hole coupled through quadrupole-quadrupole forces with a coupling constant of 40 MeV/b^2. The particle and hole are restricted to the symmetric πh_{11/2} ⊗ νh_{11/2}^{-1} configuration. The authors vary the collective potential of the core (well in γ, γ-independent, barrier in γ, and α-asymmetric potentials) and compute energy levels, electromagnetic moments, and transition probabilities from the laboratory frame without assuming chiral geometry.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant χ₂ = 40 MeV/b² used in the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.
- Proton number Z = 57 and neutron number N = 71, corresponding to a fictitious nucleus based on ¹²⁸La.
- Core equilibrium deformation β = 0.25, with E(2⁺₁) = 354 keV and B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 0.282 e²b², matched to experimental values for ¹²⁸Ba.
- Vibrational inertial parameter B_{ββ} = 250/MeV.
- For α-asymmetric cores, ground state mean γ values are 20.7° (h₁ = 2 MeV) and 15.0° (h₁ = 8 MeV).
- Electric quadrupole moments in all α-symmetric core cases are close to zero, smaller than the corresponding single-particle estimation.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Droste et al. (2009) which considered only the Davydov-Filippov rigid rotor with γ = 30° and the Wilets-Jean γ-soft core. The present paper systematically investigates cores of intermediate rigidity and α-asymmetric cores, showing that α-symmetry is the key condition for chirality signatures. The results enable future work on asymmetric particle-hole configurations, which the authors state will be published in a separate paper by Droste et al. (2010). The model is a special version of the Core-Quasiparticle-Pair Coupling model by Klein et al. (2004) and Rohozinski et al. (2005), with pairing neglected.
