# Bottom-up thermalization in heavy ion collisions

> We describe how thermalization occurs in heavy ion collisions in the framework of perturbative QCD. When the saturation scale $Q_s$ is large compared to $Λ_{QCD}$, thermalization takes place during a time of order $α^{-13/5}Q_s^{-1}$ and the maximal temperature achieved is $α^{2/5}Q_s$.

## Key claim

Thermalization in high-energy heavy ion collisions proceeds via a "bottom-up" scenario where soft gluons, initially produced by hard gluons, first form a thermal bath that then draws energy from the hard gluons; full thermalization occurs at a time of order α^{-13/5}Q_s^{-1} and a maximal temperature of α^{2/5}Q_s, but these are parametric estimates valid only in the limit α ≪ 1 and with logarithmic accuracy.

## Method

The authors analyze the time evolution of the gluon distribution in the central rapidity region of a heavy ion collision assuming boost invariance and one-dimensional expansion, using perturbative QCD with a saturation-scale initial condition. They divide the evolution into regimes separated by proper times Q_sτ ∼ α^{-3/2}, α^{-5/2}, and α^{-13/5}, and describe each regime using kinetic equations that include elastic scattering, inelastic gluon production via the Bethe-Heitler and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) mechanisms, and a Boltzmann equation for hard branching (2→3 processes). The soft sector is treated as a thermal bath characterized by a temperature T, and the energy flow from hard to soft gluons is computed analytically in the regime α^{-5/2} ≪ Q_sτ ≪ α^{-13/5}.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time: τ ∼ α^{-13/5} Q_s^{-1}
- Maximal temperature achieved: T_max ∼ α^{2/5} Q_s
- Saturation scale estimates: Q_s ≈ 1 GeV at RHIC, 2–3 GeV at LHC
- Hard gluon density: N_h ∼ Q_s^3 / [α (Q_s τ)]
- Soft gluon number at Q_sτ ∼ α^{-5/2}: N_s becomes comparable to N_h
- Temperature of soft bath when it forms (Q_sτ ∼ α^{-5/2}): T ∼ α^{1/2} Q_s
- Temperature growth in the regime α^{-5/2} ≪ Q_sτ ≪ α^{-13/5}: T = c_T α^3 Q_s^2 τ, with c_T = (3b/8π^5) N_c^3 c ln(⟨k_t²⟩/m_D²), where b ≈ 4.96
- Debye mass at early times: m_D² ∼ Q_s²/(Q_s τ)
- Hard gluon longitudinal momentum at early times: p_z ∼ Q_s/(Q_s τ)^{1/3}
- Soft gluon typical momentum in the intermediate regime: k_s ∼ α^{1/2} Q_s
- LPM scale: k_LPM ∼ α^{1/2} Q_s

## Context

This paper extends earlier work on thermalization in heavy ion collisions that considered only elastic scattering (Mueller, 1999; also Mueller, 2000) and did not include particle production, and it contrasts with approaches that used relaxation-time approximations (Wong, 1996) or different initial conditions (Biro, 1993; Shuryak, 1992; Geiger and Muller, 1992). The "bottom-up" scenario provides a parametric framework for understanding how thermalization can occur within the perturbative QCD regime at sufficiently high energies (LHC or even RHIC), and it enables future work to incorporate more realistic initial conditions and to treat the high-momentum tails of the distributions that require longer thermalization times.
