# Charge Exchange Processes between Excited Helium and Fully Stripped Ions

> We made a classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) calculation of state selective cross sections for processes between some light ions and excited helium. The results, useful for analysis of spectroscopic data of fusion devices, are in good agreement with theoretical predictions of scaling laws.

## Key claim

The paper presents classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) calculations of state-selective cross sections for charge exchange between fully stripped ions and excited helium, finding that the results are in good agreement with theoretical predictions of scaling laws, but only for the specific case of single-electron capture (double electron processes are excluded due to much smaller cross sections) and for collision energies below 110 KeV/amu with ionic charges from 3 to 8.

## Method

The authors performed CTMC calculations for the four-body system of helium plus a fully stripped ion, replacing the helium nucleus and one electron with a point-like particle interacting through a central model potential. Initialization followed the Reinhold and Falcon procedure, with initial internuclear distances set to 10 Z (checked up to 20 Z). For each energy, 4,000 to 27,000 runs were made to keep statistical error below 10 percent for the most frequent processes, considering only distribution on principal quantum number n due to complete l-mixing before spontaneous decay.

## Key numbers

- The model potential's first eigenvalues are -0.904 a.u. and -0.156 a.u. (the latter being the ionization potential of excited helium used in scaling relations)
- Collision energy range: below 110 KeV/amu
- Ionic charge range: from 3 to 8
- The maximum in the n-distribution of cross section follows n_max = Z^(3/4) / sqrt(-2I), where I = -0.156 a.u.
- For verification, calculations were repeated for Beryllium at 10 KeV/amu using a different model potential from references [9,10], showing good agreement

## Context

This work extends CTMC calculations, previously applied to hydrogen and ground-state helium, to charge exchange processes involving excited helium, which are important for interpreting spectroscopic data from fusion devices. The results confirm scaling laws suggested by prior work (e.g., the scaling in equation (4) resembles those from similar calculations with H or He in the ground state, and the ionization scaling is similar to results from close-coupling calculations on lithium by the authors of reference [8]). The findings enable more accurate analysis of visible-region emission from fusion plasmas, where excited neutrals may dominate charge exchange signals even though they constitute a small fraction of the beam.
