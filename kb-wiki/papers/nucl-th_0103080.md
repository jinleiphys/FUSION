# Hadronic degrees of freedom

> I report on the research activities performed under the (italian) MURST-PRIN project "Fisica Teorica del Nucleo e dei sistemi a piú corpi" covering part of the topics on hadronic degrees of freedom. The most recent achievements in the field are summarized focusing on the specific role of the nuclear physics community.

# Hadronic degrees of freedom

## Key claim

The general parametrization method shows that the non-relativistic quark model reproduces hadron properties at a quantitative level not because it is fundamentally correct, but because QCD dynamics produces a hierarchy of parameters in the exact expansion that mimics the simpler model; the Coleman-Glashow mass formula works to remarkable precision because three-quark-index terms are suppressed by approximately (1/3)^3 relative to leading terms.

## Method

The author reviews research activities under the Italian MURST-PRIN project, summarizing work on the general parametrization method of Morpurgo and Dillon, which expresses exact QCD baryon masses as expectation values of a parametrized mass operator acting on simple three-quark auxiliary states. The method is applied to baryon masses, electromagnetic transition form factors calculated within non-relativistic and light-front constituent quark models, electroweak structure of the nucleon including strange form factors from neutrino scattering and parity-violating electron scattering, and deep inelastic scattering structure functions from relativistic quark models.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass parametrization coefficients (in MeV): M0 = 1076, B = 192, C = 45.6, D = -13.8 ± 0.3, (a+b) = -16.0 ± 1.4, E = 5.1 ± 0.3, c = 1.1 ± 0.7, d = 4 ± 3
- Each additional P^S factor in the parametrization implies a reduction of approximately 0.3 (e.g., ratio D/C)
- Each additional index at equal number of P^S factors gives a reduction of approximately 0.37 (e.g., ratio E/D)
- Coleman-Glashow mass formula: left-hand side = -1.29 MeV, right-hand side = -1.58 ± 0.25 MeV, with three-quark-term contribution estimated at (4 ± 3)%
- BNL-734 experiment ratios: R_nu = 0.153 ± 0.007 ± 0.017, R_antinu = 0.218 ± 0.012 ± 0.023, R = 0.302 ± 0.019 ± 0.037
- Folded integral asymmetry from BNL-734: ⟨A_p⟩ = 0.136 ± 0.008 (stat) ± 0.019 (syst)
- Strange axial constant from deep inelastic data: g_A^s = -0.10 ± 0.03 (later analysis gives -0.13 ± 0.03)
- Strange magnetic form factor from SAMPLE at Bates: G_M^s(0.1 GeV^2) = 0.23 ± 0.37 ± 0.15 ± 0.19
- Magnetic moment of Delta++: μ_Δ++ = (5.6 ± 1.9) μ_N
- Predicted integrated cross section for inelastic photon scattering through Delta resonance: approximately 5 nb
- Harmonic oscillator parameter α = 0.229 GeV reproduces proton charge radius; α = 0.410 GeV corresponds to confinement radius of approximately 0.5 fm

## Context

This paper reviews the Italian nuclear physics community's contributions to hadronic physics, extending the general parametrization approach of Morpurgo (1998) and Dillon and Morpurgo to explain the success of non-relativistic quark models. The work on electromagnetic transition form factors by Aiello et al. (1998) is compared across five different potential models, showing that reproducing elastic form factors does not guarantee correct transition form factors. The analysis of strange form factors from BNL-734 neutrino data by Alberico et al. extends earlier deep inelastic scattering results on the proton spin crisis, while the parity-violating electron scattering studies by Moscani et al. propose target asymmetry measurements as complementary to the SAMPLE beam asymmetry approach for determining strange quark contributions to the nucleon.
