# Retarded Correlators of Charge Transport in a Magnetic Field

> We study charge transport in a magnetized relativistic plasma using kinetic theory within the relaxation-time approximation. By exactly solving the linearized Boltzmann equation in a uniform magnetic field, we obtain an analytic solution for the distribution function in terms of Bessel functions. Using this solution, we compute the full set of retarded current-current correlators and verify the Ward identities. In the hydrodynamic limit, we extract the charge diffusion modes, demonstrating that the transverse diffusion coefficient is strongly suppressed by the magnetic field, scaling as $1/B_0^2$ in the strong-field regime, while the longitudinal diffusion remains unaffected. Furthermore, we analyze the non-hydrodynamic branch cuts in the complex frequency plane, determining their kinematic thresholds and identifying the underlying wave-particle interactions as longitudinal Landau damping and transverse cyclotron damping.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a magnetized relativistic plasma, the transverse charge diffusion coefficient is strongly suppressed by the magnetic field, scaling as \(1/B_0^2\) in the strong-field regime, while the longitudinal diffusion coefficient remains unaffected. This result is derived within kinetic theory using the relaxation-time approximation, and the authors note that the non-hydrodynamic branch cuts arise from transverse cyclotron damping and longitudinal Landau damping.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized relativistic Boltzmann equation in a uniform background magnetic field using the relaxation-time approximation. By transforming the transverse momentum components into polar coordinates and employing the Jacobi-Anger expansion in terms of Bessel functions, they obtain an exact analytic series solution for the perturbed distribution function. Using this solution, they compute the full set of retarded current-current correlators and verify the Ward identities, then extract the hydrodynamic diffusion modes and analyze the non-hydrodynamic branch cuts in the complex frequency plane.

## Key numbers

* The transverse diffusion coefficient scales as \(1/B_0^2\) in the strong-field regime (no explicit numerical value for the coefficient is given in the text).
* The static charge susceptibility is given by \(\chi = \frac{e^{\mu_0/T_0}}{2\pi^2} m^2 K_2(m/T_0)\), where \(K_2\) is the modified Bessel function of the second kind.
* The relativistic cyclotron frequency is defined as \(\omega_c = B_0/p^0\), and the dimensionless parameter \(\xi = k_\perp r_L\) where \(r_L = p_\perp/B_0\) is the relativistic Larmor radius.
* The background magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions can be on the order of \(eB_0 \sim m_\pi^2\) or larger (cited from prior work Skokov (2009) and Deng (2012)).

## Context

This work extends kinetic-theory calculations of retarded correlators in the relaxation-time approximation, previously studied by Romatschke (2015) and Bajec (2024) for systems without a background field, to the case of a uniform magnetic field. It also builds on studies of magnetized transport in quark-gluon plasma by Hattori (2017) and others. The results enable systematic analysis of charge diffusion and non-hydrodynamic modes in magnetized relativistic plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, and the early universe, and provide a framework for testing the separation between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic sectors in the presence of a magnetic field.
