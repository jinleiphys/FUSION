# Electromagnetic Interactions in a Chiral Effective Lagrangian for Nuclei

> Electromagnetic (EM) interactions are incorporated in a recently proposed effective field theory of the nuclear many-body problem. Earlier work with this effective theory exhibited EM couplings that are correct only to lowest order in both the pion fields and the electric charge. The Lorentz-invariant effective field theory contains nucleons, pions, isoscalar scalar (σ) and vector (ω) fields, and isovector vector (ρ) fields. The theory exhibits a nonlinear realization of SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R chiral symmetry and has three desirable features: it uses the same degrees of freedom to describe the currents and the strong-interaction dynamics, it satisfies the symmetries of the underlying QCD, and its parameters can be calibrated using strong-interaction phenomena, like hadron scattering or the empirical properties of finite nuclei. It has been verified that for normal nuclear systems, the effective lagrangian can be expanded systematically in powers of the meson fields (and their derivatives) and can be truncated reliably after the first few orders. The complete EM lagrangian arising from minimal substitution is derived and shown to possess the residual chiral symmetry of massless, two-flavor QCD with EM interactions. The uniqueness of the minimal EM current is proved, and the properties of the isovector vector and axial-vector currents are discussed, generalizing earlier work. The residual chiral symmetry is maintained in additional (non-minimal) EM couplings expressed as a derivative expansion and in implementing vector meson dominance. The role of chiral anomalies in the EM lagrangian is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic interactions are incorporated into a chiral effective field theory for nuclei by deriving the complete minimal-substitution EM Lagrangian, which possesses the residual chiral symmetry of massless two-flavor QCD with EM interactions; this corrects earlier work where EM couplings were accurate only to lowest order in both the pion fields and the electric charge.

## Method

The author starts from a Lorentz-invariant QHD effective field theory containing nucleons, pions, and isoscalar scalar (σ), isoscalar vector (ω), and isovector vector (ρ) fields, with a nonlinear realization of SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R chiral symmetry. Ordinary derivatives in the EFT Lagrangian are replaced with EM gauge-covariant derivatives to produce the minimal EM couplings, and the resulting minimal EM current is derived and shown to be the conserved source term in Maxwell's equations. Non-minimal EM couplings are introduced via a derivative expansion, and vector meson dominance contributions are implemented while maintaining the residual chiral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The EFT Lagrangian is truncated at terms with index ν ≤ 4, where ν ≡ d + n/2 + b, with d the number of derivatives, n the number of nucleon fields, and b the number of non-Goldstone boson fields.
- The pion decay constant f_π appears in the definition of the pion field combinations U ≡ exp(i τ·π / f_π) and ξ ≡ exp(i τ·π / 2 f_π).
- The rho meson enters with a free parameter ḡ_ρ in the covariant field tensor ρ_μν = D_μ ρ_ν − D_ν ρ_μ + i ḡ_ρ [ρ_μ, ρ_ν].
- The EM charge operator is Q = ∫ d³x [½ N† (1 + τ₃) N + (π × P_π)₃ + (ρ_ν × P_ρ^ν)₃].

## Context

This work extends the QHD effective field theory proposed by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997) by providing the full structure of the EM Lagrangian, correcting and generalizing the earlier brief discussion that included only lowest-order EM couplings. The derived Lagrangian serves as the basis for forthcoming calculations of Lorentz-covariant one- and two-nucleon amplitudes for electron scattering and pion photoproduction in nuclei, and it enables extension of the theory to include the Δ resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in EM interactions.
