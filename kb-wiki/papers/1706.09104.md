# Theoretical study on the hyperfine interaction constants and the isotope shift factors for the $3s^2~^1 S_{ 0} ~-~ 3s3p~^{3,1}P^o_1$ transitions in Al$^+$

> We calculated the magnetic dipole and the electric quadrupole hyperfine interaction constants of 3s3p $^{3,1}P^o_1$ states and the isotope shift, including mass and field shift, factors for transitions from these two states to the ground state 3s$^2~^1S_0$ in Al$^+$ ions using the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock method. The effects of the electron correlations and the Breit interaction on these physical quantities were investigated in detail based on the active space approach. It is found that the CC and the higher-order correlations are considerable for evaluating the uncertainties of the atomic parameters concerned. The uncertainties of the hyperfine interaction constants in this work are less than 1.5\%. Although the isotope shift factors are highly sensitive to the electron correlations, reasonable uncertainties were obtained by exploring the effects of the electron correlations. Moreover, we found that the relativistic nuclear recoil corrections to the mass shift factors are very small and insensitive to the electron correlations for Al$^{+}$. These atomic parameters present in this work are valuable for extracting the nuclear electric quadrupole moments and the mean-square charge radii of Al isotopes.

## Key claim
The authors calculated hyperfine interaction constants and isotope shift factors for Al⁺ transitions using the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock method, claiming uncertainties below 1.5% for hyperfine constants, but the isotope shift factors are highly sensitive to electron correlations and carry larger uncertainties (up to 47% for specific mass shift in one transition).

## Method
The multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method with an active space approach was used to systematically include valence-valence (VV), core-valence (CV), core-core (CC), and higher-order electron correlations. The Breit interaction was included in relativistic configuration interaction (RCI) calculations, and the relativistic nuclear recoil corrections to mass shift factors were evaluated. The computational model was built by expanding active orbitals layer by layer and using multireference (MR) configuration sets to capture higher-order correlations.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic dipole hyperfine constant A for 3s3p ³P°₁: 1327.3(10.2) MHz
- Electric quadrupole hyperfine constant B for 3s3p ³P°₁: −15.1(0.2) MHz
- Magnetic dipole hyperfine constant A for 3s3p ¹P°₁: 197.1(2.8) MHz
- Electric quadrupole hyperfine constant B for 3s3p ¹P°₁: 22.1(0.3) MHz
- Non-relativistic normal mass shift factor ΔK_NMS = −615.67 GHz u for the 3s² ¹S₀ − 3s3p ³P°₁ transition
- Non-relativistic normal mass shift factor ΔK_NMS = −983.86 GHz u for the 3s² ¹S₀ − 3s3p ¹P°₁ transition
- Relativistic nuclear recoil corrections to NMS and SMS factors are 24 GHz u and 13 GHz u respectively for the 3s² ¹S₀ − 3s3p ³P°₁ transition
- Total uncertainties for the 3s² ¹S₀ − 3s3p ¹P°₁ transition: about 15% for NMS factors, 17% for SMS factors, and 3% for FS factor
- Total uncertainties for the 3s² ¹S₀ − 3s3p ³P°₁ transition: about 4% for NMS factors, 47% for SMS factors, and 2% for FS factor

## Context
This work extends prior MCDHF calculations on Al⁺ by Kang et al. (2009) and Andersson et al. (2010), who calculated off-diagonal hyperfine interaction constants for lifetime studies, and provides the first reported isotope shift factors for these transitions. The calculated atomic parameters enable extraction of nuclear electric quadrupole moments and mean-square charge radii of Al isotopes from experimental hyperfine structures and isotope shifts measured by collinear laser spectroscopy or Al⁺ ion optical clocks, particularly for exotic isotopes like ²³Al, ²⁶Al, and ³¹⁻³³Al where existing nuclear data have large uncertainties.
