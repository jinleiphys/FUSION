# Preparation for Quantum Simulation of the 1+1D O(3) Non-linear σ-Model using Cold Atoms

> The 1+1D O(3) non-linear σ-model is a model system for future quantum lattice simulations of other asymptotically-free theories, such as non-Abelian gauge theories. We find that utilizing dimensional reduction can make efficient use of two-dimensional layouts presently available on cold atom quantum simulators. A new definition of the renormalized coupling is introduced, which is applicable to systems with open boundary conditions and can be measured using analog quantum simulators. Monte Carlo and tensor network calculations are performed to determine the quantum resources required to reproduce perturbative short-distance observables. In particular, we show that a rectangular array of 48 Rydberg atoms with existing quantum hardware capabilities should be able to adiabatically prepare low-energy states of the perturbatively-matched theory. These states can then be used to simulate non-perturbative observables in the continuum limit that lie beyond the reach of classical computers.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a rectangular array of 48 Rydberg atoms with existing quantum hardware capabilities can adiabatically prepare low-energy states of the 1+1D O(3) non-linear sigma model that are perturbatively matched to the continuum, but the authors hedge that reproducing the full step-scaling function requires specific lattice sizes and that a true quantum advantage would require simulations on lattices of size 18x6 with parameters outside the reach of perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors used the D-theory mapping, placing spin-1/2 degrees of freedom on a 2D rectangular lattice with antiferromagnetic Heisenberg interactions, where dimensional reduction in the limit L_x >> L_y >> 1 produces the 1+1D O(3) non-linear sigma model. They performed density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) calculations using the C++ ITensor library to obtain vacuum states for lattices of size 6xL_y and 8xL_y with open boundary conditions, and computed the step-scaling function F_s(z) for s=4/3 by varying the coupling anisotropy J_x/J_y. They also performed time-dependent variational principle (TDVP) simulations of an adiabatic spiral protocol for a rectangular array of rubidium-87 atoms to model realistic quantum hardware implementation.

## Key numbers

- A rectangular array of 48 Rydberg atoms (6x8 or 8x6 lattice) is sufficient to reproduce the ultraviolet physics of the O(3) non-linear sigma model
- The step-scaling function was computed for anisotropy ratios in the range 0.1 ≤ J_x/J_y ≤ 1.3 for nearest-neighbor couplings, and 0.1 ≤ (a_y/a_x)^6 ≤ 1.3 for the 1/r^6 coupling
- For the 1/r^6 D-theory Hamiltonian with L_y=6, the step-scaling function was reproduced over anisotropy range 0.45 ≤ (a_y/a_x)^6 ≤ 0.95
- The DMRG calculations for the 18x6 lattice required a bond dimension of 2000 to converge
- The Rydberg atom simulations assumed C_6 = 5.42 × 10^6 MHz μm^6, vertical lattice spacing of 11 μm, maximum Rabi frequency of 25 MHz, and maximum coherence time of 4 μs
- Horizontal lattice spacings used ranged from 11.1 μm to 12.5 μm, with energy penalties between 0.3 and 0.56 MHz

## Context

This work extends the D-theory approach to the O(3) non-linear sigma model previously studied by Chandrasekharan (2002), Brower (2003), and Caspar (2022), but adapts it for open boundary conditions required by current analog quantum simulators. The authors introduce a new definition of the renormalized coupling suitable for open boundary conditions, building on prior work by Bhattacharya (2020) and others. This enables perturbative matching between lattice and continuum theories on quantum hardware, and the authors suggest that their methods could be extended to simulate the O(3) model in 2+1 dimensions using 3D cold-atom arrays demonstrated by Barredo (2020).
