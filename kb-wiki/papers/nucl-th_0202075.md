# Negative specific heat in out-of-equilibrium nonextensive systems

> We discuss the occurrence of negative specific heat in a nonextensive system which has an equilibrium second-order phase transition.The specific heat is negative only in a transient regime before equilibration, in correspondence to long-lasting metastable states. For these states standard equilibrium Bolzmann-Gibbs thermodynamics does not apply and the system shows non-Gaussian velocity distributions, anomalous diffusion and correlation in phase space. Similar results have recently been found also in several other nonextensive systems, supporting the general validity of this scenario. These models seem also to support the conjecture that a nonexstensive statistical formalism, like the one proposed by Tsallis, should be applied in such cases. The theoretical scenario is not completely clear yet, but there are already many strong theoretical indications suggesting that, it can be wrong to consider the observation of an experimental negative specific heat as an unique and unambiguous signature of a standard equilibrium first-order phase transition.

# Negative specific heat in out-of-equilibrium nonextensive systems

## Key claim

Negative specific heat occurs in a nonextensive system with an equilibrium second-order phase transition, but only in a transient out-of-equilibrium regime before equilibration, corresponding to long-lasting metastable quasi-stationary states (QSS). The observation of negative specific heat in experiments should not be taken as an unambiguous signature of a standard equilibrium first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed numerical microcanonical simulations of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, a system of N fully-coupled classical rotators with long-range interactions. They initialized the system using water-bag initial conditions (all angles at zero, total energy distributed uniformly over momenta) and tracked the time evolution of temperature (via average kinetic energy per particle) and specific heat using the Lebowitz-Percus-Verlet (LPV) formula and the Pearson-Haliciouglu-Tiller (PHT) formula. The simulations were run for system sizes N=500, N=10000, and N=100000 with a time step of 0.2.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy density of the HMF model is U_c = E_c/N = 0.75, corresponding to critical temperature T_c = 0.5
- For energy density U=0.69 (below the critical point), the equilibrium temperature is T_eq = 0.476, while the quasi-stationary state temperature converges to T_infinity = 0.38 for infinite system size
- Complete equilibration below the critical point requires 10^6 to 10^7 time steps, depending on system size
- The relative numerical error in the simulations was in the range 10^-7 < Delta E/E < 10^-3
- The temperature deviation from equilibrium in the QSS regime is approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends numerical investigations of negative specific heat in nonextensive systems, previously studied in self-gravitating systems and modified Lennard-Jones potentials, to the HMF model. The results contradict the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs thermodynamic expectation that specific heat cannot be negative, and support the conjecture that Tsallis' nonextensive statistical formalism should be applied to such out-of-equilibrium metastable states. The findings serve as a warning against interpreting experimental negative specific heat measurements in nuclear multifragmentation (as reported in experiments on nuclei and atomic clusters) as unambiguous evidence for a first-order phase transition, and call for more detailed theoretical investigations and more precise experimental data.
