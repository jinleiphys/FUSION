# Constraints on Nuclear Saturation Properties from Terrestrial Experiments and Astrophysical Observations of Neutron Stars

> Taking into account the terrestrial experiments and the recent astrophysical observations of neutron stars and gravitational-wave signals, we impose restrictions on the equation of state (EoS) for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. Using the relativistic mean-field model with SU(3) flavor symmetry, we investigate the impacts of effective nucleon mass, nuclear incompressibility, and slope parameter of nuclear symmetry energy on the nuclear and neutron-star properties. It is found that the astrophysical information of massive neutron stars and tidal deformabilities as well as the nuclear experimental data plays an important role to restrict the EoS for neutron stars. Especially, the softness of the nuclear EoS due to the existence of hyperons in the core gives stringent constraints on those physical quantities. Furthermore, it is possible to put limits on the curvature parameter of nuclear symmetry energy by means of the nuclear and astrophysical calculations.

## Key claim

By combining terrestrial nuclear experiments with astrophysical observations of neutron stars (including the GW170817 tidal deformability) and explicitly including hyperons in the core, the authors find that the effective nucleon mass at saturation density must be restricted to \(0.65 \le M_N^*/M_N \le 0.70\), a tighter range than the \(0.65\)–\(0.75\) allowed by nuclear scattering data alone, and that the curvature parameter of the symmetry energy can be constrained to \(-128 \le K_{\rm sym} \le -33\) MeV for the best-fit case.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with nonlinear scalar and vector self-interactions and SU(3) flavor symmetry to describe baryonic matter including the full octet of baryons (nucleons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\)). They fix the nucleon-meson couplings by reproducing saturation properties (binding energy \(-16.0\) MeV, symmetry energy \(32.0\) MeV at \(n_0=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)) while varying the effective nucleon mass, incompressibility \(K_0\), and symmetry-energy slope \(L\) over wide ranges. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron-star structure and compute the tidal deformability, comparing against the observed masses of PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01\pm0.04\,M_\odot\)) and MSP J0740+6620 (\(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09}\,M_\odot\)) and the GW170817 constraint \(70 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 580\).

## Key numbers

* Effective nucleon mass at saturation: the optimum value for reproducing the Schrödinger-equivalent potential is \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.69\); the range allowed by combining scattering data with the \(2\,M_\odot\) hyperonic-star constraint is \(0.65 \le M_N^*/M_N \le 0.70\).
* Incompressibility \(K_0\): for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.69\) and the \(2.01\,M_\odot\) mass constraint, the allowed range is \(215 \le K_0 \le 260\) MeV; for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.65\) and the \(2.14\,M_\odot\) constraint, it narrows to \(215 \le K_0 \le 235\) MeV.
* Slope parameter \(L\): for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.69\) and the \(2.01\,M_\odot\) constraint, \(40 \le L \le 85\) MeV; for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.65\) and the \(2.14\,M_\odot\) constraint, \(40 \le L \le 65\) MeV.
* Curvature parameter \(K_{\rm sym}\): for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.69\) and \(M_{\rm max}/M_\odot = 2.01\), \(-128 \le K_{\rm sym} \le -33\) MeV; for \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.65\) and \(M_{\rm max}/M_\odot = 2.14\), \(-84 \le K_{\rm sym} \le -10\) MeV.
* Tidal deformability constraint used: \(70 < \Lambda_{1.4} < 580\) from GW170817 (Abbott 2018).

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the nuclear equation of state by combining terrestrial data (heavy-ion collision analyses by Tsang (2012) and Russotto (2016), Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Katayama (2013), and chiral effective field theory by Sammarruca (2014)) with neutron-star observations. It specifically incorporates hyperonic degrees of freedom, which soften the EoS and make the \(2\,M_\odot\) mass constraint more restrictive than in purely nucleonic models. The resulting bounds on \(K_{\rm sym}\) are tighter than the extensive survey of over 520 models by Dutra (2012, 2014) and are comparable to those of Malik (2018). The work enables future studies that include additional exotic degrees of freedom (e.g., kaon condensates or quark matter) to further refine the saturation properties.
