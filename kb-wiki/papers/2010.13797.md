# Comprehensive analysis of beta decays within and beyond the Standard Model

> Precision measurements in allowed nuclear beta decays and neutron decay are reviewed and analyzed both within the Standard Model and looking for new physics. The analysis incorporates the most recent experimental and theoretical developments. The results are interpreted in terms of Wilson coefficients describing the effective interactions between leptons and nucleons (or quarks) that are responsible for beta decay. New global fits are performed incorporating a comprehensive list of precision measurements in neutron decay, superallowed $0^+\rightarrow 0^+$ transitions, and other nuclear decays that include, for the first time, data from mirror beta transitions. The results confirm the $V$-$A$ character of the interaction and translate into updated values for $V_{ud}$ and $g_A$ at the $10^{-4}$ level. We also place new stringent limits on exotic couplings involving left-handed and right-handed neutrinos, which benefit significantly from the inclusion of mirror decays in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the V-A character of the weak interaction and provides updated values for V_ud and g_A at the 10^-4 level, while also placing new stringent limits on exotic couplings involving left-handed and right-handed neutrinos; however, the global fit shows a 3.2 sigma preference for non-standard tensor current interactions involving the right-handed neutrino, driven by a single recent measurement (a_n by the aSPECT collaboration) and thus should be taken with caution.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of precision measurements in allowed nuclear beta decays and neutron decay, incorporating for the first time data from mirror beta transitions in a systematic and consistent fashion. The analysis used a complete and model-independent effective field theory approach, constructing a likelihood function for all Wilson coefficients C_X^+ and C_X^- simultaneously, including all correlations, and interpreting the results in terms of effective interactions between leptons and nucleons (or quarks) responsible for beta decay.

## Key numbers

- V_ud extracted at the 10^-4 level from global fits including superallowed 0+ to 0+ transitions, neutron decay, and mirror beta decays
- g_A = 1.251(33) from FLAG'19 averages, used as input for the axial charge of the nucleon
- g_S = 1.022(100) and g_T = 0.989(33) from FLAG'19 averages, used for scalar and tensor charges
- Delta_R^V = 0.02467(22) from Seng et al. evaluation, used for short-distance radiative corrections
- Neutron lifetime phase space factor f_n = 1.6887(1) and outer radiative correction delta_n = 0.014902(2)
- F t values for mirror transitions: 2292.4(2.7) s for 17F, 1721.44(92) s for 19Ne, 4071(4) s for 21Na, 4764.6(7.9) s for 29P, 5688.6(7.2) s for 35Ar, 4605.4(8.2) s for 37K
- Correlation coefficients for mirror transitions: tilde A = 0.960(82) for 17F, tilde A_0 = -0.03871(91) for 19Ne (Combs 2020), tilde a = 0.5502(60) for 21Na, tilde A = 0.681(86) for 29P, tilde A = 0.430(22) for 35Ar, tilde A = -0.5707(19) and tilde B = -0.755(24) for 37K
- Including mirror decays in the global fit improves model-independent constraints on C_X^+ and C_X^- by approximately a factor of two

## Context

This work extends previous global fits of beta decay data by Paul (1970), Boothroyd (1984), Severijns (2006), and Gonzalez-Alonso (2018), but for the first time constructs the complete likelihood function for all Wilson coefficients simultaneously, including all correlations, and incorporates mirror beta transitions systematically. The analysis enables more stringent model-independent constraints on non-standard currents involving left-handed and right-handed neutrinos, and provides a framework for interpreting future precision measurements in mirror beta transitions as a cross-check on V_ud determinations from superallowed and neutron decays, which are affected by completely different systematic uncertainties.
