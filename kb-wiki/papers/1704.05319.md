# Thermal effects on $ρ$ meson properties in an external magnetic field

> A detailed study of the analytic structure of 1-loop self energy graphs for neutral and charged $ρ$ mesons is presented at finite temperature and arbitrary magnetic field using the real time formalism of thermal field theory. The imaginary part of the self energy is obtained from the discontinuities of these graphs across the Unitary and Landau cuts, which is seen to be different for $ρ^0$ and $ρ^\pm$. The magnetic field dependent vacuum contribution to the real part of the self energy, which is usually ignored, is found to be appreciable. A significant effect of temperature and magnetic field is seen in the self energy, spectral function, effective mass and dispersion relation of $ρ^0$ as well as of $ρ^\pm$ relative to its trivial Landau shift. However, for charged $ρ$ mesons, on account of the dominance of the Landau term, the effective mass appears to be independent of temperature. The trivial coupling of magnetic moment of $ρ^\pm$ with external magnetic field, when incorporated in the calculation, makes the $ρ^\pm$ to condense at high magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper presents a detailed calculation of the 1-loop self energy for neutral and charged rho mesons at finite temperature and arbitrary magnetic field strength, finding that the magnetic field dependent vacuum contribution to the real part of the self energy, which is usually ignored, is appreciable; however, for charged rho mesons the effective mass appears independent of temperature due to the dominance of the Landau term.

## Method

The authors use the real time formalism of thermal field theory with the full Schwinger proper time propagator for charged pions in the loop, without approximating the magnetic field as strong or weak. They compute the 11-component of the self energy matrix for both neutral and charged rho mesons, extract the analytic functions via the bar-notation, and evaluate the imaginary parts from discontinuities across Unitary and Landau cuts. The real part includes both the magnetic field dependent vacuum contribution and the thermal-magnetic contribution, with the vacuum part obtained using Feynman parametrization and dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

* Effective coupling constant: g_{rho pi pi} = 20.72 GeV^{-2}, fixed from the vacuum rho -> pi pi decay width Gamma = 150 MeV
* Temperature range studied: 100 MeV <= T <= 160 MeV
* Magnetic field values used in numerical results: eB = 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, and 0.20 GeV^2
* For eB >= 0.05 GeV^2, results are well convergent with 200 Landau levels; for the eB -> 0 limit, up to 300 Landau levels were used
* Pion masses taken equal: m_0 = m_+/- = m_pi

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of rho meson properties in a magnetic field by including the full Schwinger propagator without weak or strong field approximations, and by incorporating the magnetic field dependent vacuum contribution to the real part of the self energy that earlier studies neglected (e.g., DOlivo (2002), Hattori (2012)). The analytic structure of the self energy at finite temperature and non-zero magnetic field, including the distinct Unitary and Landau cut contributions for neutral versus charged rho mesons, is discussed for the first time. The results are relevant for understanding dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where magnetic fields of eB ~ 15 m_pi^2 can be generated (Skokov (2009)), and for studies of magnetic field induced vacuum superconductivity (Chernodub (2012)).
