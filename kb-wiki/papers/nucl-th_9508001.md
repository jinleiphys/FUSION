# Thermal fission rate around super-normal phase transition

> Using Langer's $Im F$ method, we discuss the temperature dependence of nuclear fission width in the presence of dissipative environments. We introduce a low cut-off frequency to the spectral density of the environmental oscillators in order to mimic the pairing gap. It is shown that the decay width rapidly decreases at the critical temperature, where the phase transition from super to normal fluids takes place. Relation to the recently observed threshold for the dissipative fission is discussed.

# Thermal fission rate around super-normal phase transition

## Key claim

The nuclear fission width rapidly decreases at the critical temperature where the pairing gap disappears and the nucleus undergoes a phase transition from superfluid to normal fluid; this sudden decrease may indicate a second critical temperature distinct from the threshold for dissipative fission discussed by Thoennessen and Bertsch (1993), though the effect could be smeared out in actual nuclei by the gradual disappearance of the pairing gap with temperature.

## Method

The authors applied Langer's Im F method to calculate the thermal fission width of a metastable nuclear system coupled to a dissipative heat bath, using a Caldeira-Leggett type model with a low cutoff frequency introduced into the spectral density of environmental oscillators to mimic the pairing gap in even-even nuclei. The temperature dependence of the pairing gap was described by a simplified BCS-type expression near the critical temperature, and the decay width was evaluated for the fission of 248Cf using a reduced mass for symmetric fission and a potential from the literature, with a bilinear coupling form factor and a reduced dissipation coefficient of 20×10^21/sec.

## Key numbers

- For 248Cf: ℏω₀ = 1.18 MeV (curvature at local minimum), ℏω_b = 1.06 MeV (curvature at barrier), q_b = 3.4 fm (barrier position), V_b = 3.67 MeV (barrier height)
- Reduced dissipation coefficient β ≡ η/M = 20×10^21/sec
- Critical temperature for super-normal phase transition T_c^pair = 0.432 MeV for 248Cf
- Crossover temperature from quantum to thermal decay in absence of environment: T_c = 0.169 MeV
- Zero-temperature pairing gap estimated as 12/√A, with critical temperature related by T_c^pair ∼ 0.567 Δ₀
- Effective pairing gaps from time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for 236U are 2.5 to 7.5 times larger than standard values

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear dissipation in fission by incorporating the temperature dependence of the pairing gap and the superfluid-to-normal phase transition, building on the Caldeira-Leggett model for dissipative quantum systems. The authors relate their findings to the systematics of dissipative fission thresholds established by Thoennessen and Bertsch (1993) and to earlier work by Dossing and Widom (1987) on the role of pairing in spontaneous fission. The results suggest that the sudden decrease in fission rate at the critical temperature could be connected to the sudden decay of superdeformed bands at critical angular momentum, and the work calls for more detailed studies of the coupling form factor and the temperature and coordinate dependence of the friction constant.
