# Correlation between mean transverse momentum and multiplicity of charged particles in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions: from ISR to LHC

> We present our analysis of the available experimental data on correlation between mean transverse momentum and charged particles multiplicity ($\langle p_T \rangle$-$N_{ch}$) at central rapidity in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ from 17 GeV to 7 TeV. A multi-pomeron exchange model based on Regge-Gribov approach provides quantitative description of $\langle p_T \rangle$-$N_{ch}$ correlation data and their energy dependence. Results are found to be in agreement with string fusion model hypothesis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified multi-pomeron exchange model with string collectivity effects can quantitatively describe the correlation between mean transverse momentum and charged particle multiplicity in pp and p-pbar collisions from 17 GeV to 7 TeV, but the model's success depends on introducing an energy-dependent parameter k (mean multiplicity per string) that grows logarithmically with energy, reducing the number of free parameters from three to two.

## Method

The authors fit a modified multi-pomeron exchange model based on the Regge-Gribov approach to available experimental data on the correlation between mean transverse momentum and charged particle multiplicity at central rapidity in pp and p-pbar collisions. The model incorporates string fusion effects through a parameter beta and string tension t, with the mean number of charged particles per string per rapidity unit (k) fixed by fitting charged particle multiplicity density data and assumed to follow a smooth logarithmic growth with energy. The two remaining free parameters beta and t are then extracted by fitting the experimental correlation data at each collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The parameter k follows the logarithmic growth: k = 0.255 + 0.0653 log(sqrt(s)), where sqrt(s) is in GeV
- Two subsets of string tension t are obtained: t = 0.566 GeV^2 and t = 0.428 GeV^2, with the upper value used for further analysis
- The parameter beta is approximated by beta = beta_0 * ((1 - log(sqrt(s)) - beta_2)^beta_1) with beta_0 = 1.16 +/- 0.39, beta_1 = 0.19 +/- 0.08, beta_2 = 2.52 +/- 0.03
- The ratio k/(<n>^beta * t) = 0.87 +/- 0.08, which is found to be approximately energy independent
- The mean number of pomerons reaches about 3 at LHC energies (7 TeV)
- Single pomeron exchange contributes mean pT values from about 0.32 GeV/c to 0.4 GeV/c for collision energies from 17 GeV to 7000 GeV

## Context

This work extends the multi-pomeron exchange model of ADF (2013) by introducing an energy-dependent parameter k, whereas previous works assumed it to be constant. The analysis covers data from ISR energies (17 GeV) through Tevatron to LHC (7 TeV), and the results are found to be in agreement with the string fusion model hypothesis of BP (1990). The model enables extrapolation to higher collision energies and could be extended to study long-range correlations between mean transverse momentum and multiplicity in separated pseudorapidity intervals.
