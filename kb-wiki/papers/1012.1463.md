# Exclusive Measurement of the $pp \to nnπ^+π^+$ Reaction at 1.1 GeV

> First exclusive data for the $pp \to nnπ^+π^+$ reaction have been obtained at CELSIUS with the WASA detector setup at a beam energy of $T_p$ = 1.1 GeV. Total and differential cross sections disagree with theoretical calculations, which predict the $ΔΔ$ excitation to be the dominant process at this beam energy. Instead the data require the excitation of a higher-lying $Δ$ state, most likely the $Δ(1600)$, to be the leading process.

## Key claim

The first exclusive data for the $pp \to nn\pi^+\pi^+$ reaction at 1.1 GeV show that the dominant process is not the $\Delta\Delta$ excitation as predicted by theory, but rather the excitation of a higher-lying $\Delta$ state, most likely the $\Delta(1600)$, though the data statistics are limited and the conclusion depends on the shape of differential distributions.

## Method

The reaction was measured at the CELSIUS storage ring using the WASA detector with a hydrogen pellet target. Positively charged pions were detected and identified in the central detector (minidrift chamber, plastic scintillator barrel, and CsI calorimeter), while neutrons were identified in the forward detector by requiring no signal in the thin-walled window hodoscope, straw tracker, and quirl but a signal from recoil protons in the range hodoscope. The trigger required two charged particles in the central detector and two neutron candidates in the forward detector; events were subjected to a kinematic fit with two overconstraints, and absolute normalization was achieved via simultaneously measured elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $pp \to nn\pi^+\pi^+$ at $T_p = 1.1$ GeV: a factor of five larger than predicted by the Valencia calculations (Alvarez-Ruso et al.).
- The measured total cross section is in good agreement with previous bubble-chamber data from KEK (Shimizu et al.).
- Neutron detection efficiency was determined using the $pp \to pn\pi^+$ reaction, requiring an energy deposit greater than 40 MeV in a segment of the forward range hodoscope.
- The $\Delta(1600) \to \Delta\pi$ process, when added coherently and destructively with the $\Delta\Delta$ process (as required by isospin decomposition), provides good agreement with the differential distributions, particularly the $\pi^+\pi^+$ invariant mass and opening angle spectra.

## Context

This work extends earlier total cross section measurements from bubble-chamber experiments (Shimizu 1982, Eisner 1965) and a COSY-TOF upper limit (Evdokimov 2008), and directly tests the Valencia group calculations (Alvarez-Ruso 1998) that predicted $\Delta\Delta$ dominance above 1 GeV. The data contradict those calculations and instead support the proposal from an isospin decomposition study (Skorodko 2009) that a higher-lying $\Delta$ state, likely the $\Delta(1600)$, is the leading process. The results enable refined theoretical models of two-pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, particularly for channels that selectively probe $I=3/2$ resonance excitations.
