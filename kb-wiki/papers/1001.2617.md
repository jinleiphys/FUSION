# Neutrino emission from spin waves in neutron spin-triplet superfluid

> The linear response of a neutron spin-triplet superfluid onto external weak axial-vector field is studied for the case of $^{3}P_{2}$ pairing with a projection of the total angular momentum $m_{j}=0$. The problem is considered in the BCS approximation discarding Fermi-liquid effects. The anomalous axial-vector vertices of neutron quasiparticles possess singularities at some frequencies which specify existence of undamped spin-density waves in the Cooper condensate. The spin waves are of a low excitation energy and are kinematically able to decay into neutrino pairs through neutral weak currents. We evaluate the neutrino emissivity from the spin wave decays in the bulk neutron superfluid in old neutron stars. This calculation predicts significant energy losses from within a neutron star at lowest temperatures when all other mechanisms of neutrino emission are killed by the neutron and proton superfluidity.

# Neutrino emission from spin waves in neutron spin-triplet superfluid

## Key claim

Spin-density waves with excitation energy smaller than the superfluid energy gap exist in the neutron spin-triplet ($^{3}P_{2}$, $m_{j}=0$) condensate and can decay into neutrino pairs via neutral weak currents, producing significant neutrino energy losses at the lowest temperatures ($T \ll T_{c}$) when all other neutrino emission mechanisms are suppressed by superfluidity, but this result is obtained in the BCS approximation discarding Fermi-liquid effects, and the Landau parameters for particle-hole interactions in asymmetric nuclear matter are unknown.

## Method

The linear response of a neutron spin-triplet superfluid to an external weak axial-vector field was studied using the Matsubara calculation technique in the BCS approximation, discarding Fermi-liquid effects. The anomalous axial-vector vertices were obtained from Dyson's equations in the ladder approximation, and the dispersion equation for collective spin oscillations was solved to find the eigenfrequencies $\omega_s$ as a function of the temperature parameter $y = \Delta(T)/T$. The neutrino emissivity from spin wave decays was then evaluated by computing the imaginary part of the weak polarization tensor at the pole corresponding to the spin wave frequency, using the Standard Model of weak interactions with axial-vector coupling constant $C_A \simeq 1.26$.

## Key numbers

- Spin wave frequency just below critical temperature: $\omega_s(T \rightarrow T_c) \simeq 0.42\Delta$, corresponding to dimensionless frequency $\Omega_s \simeq 0.21$
- Spin wave frequency at zero temperature: $\omega_s(T \rightarrow 0) \simeq 0.30\Delta$, corresponding to $\Omega_s \simeq 0.15$
- Dimensionless function $\Lambda(y)$ tends to a plateau at $\Lambda = 1.35$ at low temperatures
- Neutrino emissivity formula: $\epsilon_{SWD} \simeq 6.3 \times 10^{20} \frac{erg}{cm^3 s} \left( \frac{M^*}{M} \right) \left( \frac{p_F}{Mc} \right) T_9^7 C_A^2 F(\Delta(T)/T)$, where $T_9 = T/(10^9 K)$
- Numerical evaluation performed at density $\rho = 2 \times 10^{14}~g~cm^{-3}$ with effective nucleon mass $M^* = 0.7M$ and critical temperature $T_c = 10^9 K$
- Coefficient $\varkappa = 5.32 \times 10^{-2}$ appearing in the low-temperature expansion of the function $A$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous prediction of spin waves in the neutron spin-triplet condensate (Leinson 2009) by calculating the neutrino emissivity from their decay. It contrasts with prior studies of spin modes in p-wave superfluid liquid $^3He$ (Maki; Wolfle), which cannot be directly applied to neutron matter because the pairing interaction in neutron matter arises from spin-orbit interactions lacking separate rotational invariance of spin and orbital coordinates. The predicted spin wave decay mechanism could affect the minimal cooling paradigm (Page 2004, Page 2009) by providing significant neutrino emission at the final stage of neutrino cooling when pair-recombination processes (Yakovlev, Kaminker, Levenfish) are suppressed, and may also be noticeable in enhanced cooling scenarios if proton spin-singlet superfluidity suppresses the direct Urca process.
