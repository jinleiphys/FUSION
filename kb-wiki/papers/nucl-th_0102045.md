# Collective response of nuclei: Comparison between experiments and extended mean-field calculations

> The giant monopole, dipole and quadrupole responses in $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb are investigated using linear response treatment based on a stochastic one-body transport theory. Effects of the coupling to low-lying surface modes (coherent mechanism) and the incoherent mechanism due to nucleon-nucleon collisions are included beyond the usual mean-field description. We emphasize the importance of both mechanism in the fragmentation and damping of giant resonance. Calculated spectra are compared with experiment in terms of percentage of Energy-Weighted Sum-Rules in various energy regions. We obtained reasonable agreement in all cases. A special attention as been given to the fragmentation of the Giant Quadrupole Resonance in calcium and lead. In particular, the equal splitting of the $2^{+}$ in $^{40}$Ca is correctly reproduced. In addition, the appearance of fine structure in the response $^{208}$Pb is partly described by the calculations in which the coherent mechanism play an important role.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the coherent mechanism (coupling to low-lying surface modes) and the incoherent mechanism (nucleon-nucleon collisions) are important for describing the fragmentation and damping of giant resonances in cold nuclei, contrary to the usual assumption that the incoherent mechanism is negligible at low temperature. The authors find that the two mechanisms produce approximately additive effects on the mean energy shift and width increase.

## Method

The authors performed linear response calculations based on a stochastic one-body transport theory, solving the extended mean-field equations in the small-amplitude limit. They computed the strength functions for giant monopole, dipole, and quadrupole resonances in 40Ca, 90Zr, 120Sn, and 208Pb using the Skyrme force SLy4, including separately the coherent self-energy (coupling to RPA phonons up to L=5) and the incoherent self-energy (with a modified Skyrme interaction using a Gaussian cutoff factor with beta=1.4 fm). The calculations were performed at zero temperature using a discrete basis in a large harmonic oscillator representation (12 major shells for 40Ca, 15 for other nuclei) with a fixed imaginary part eta=0.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the GQR in 40Ca, the coherent mechanism induces a splitting of the main RPA peak into two peaks, correctly reproducing the equal splitting of the 2+ state
- The incoherent mechanism reduces the mean-energy of low-lying 2+ states by -1.3 MeV for 90Zr and 120Sn, and by -0.7 MeV for 208Pb
- The RPA calculations used a harmonic oscillator basis with 12 major shells for 40Ca and 15 major shells for 90Zr, 120Sn, and 208Pb
- The Gaussian cutoff parameter for the modified Skyrme interaction was beta=1.4 fm
- The imaginary part of the forcing frequency was set to eta=0.5 MeV
- For the GQR in 40Ca, the coherent self-energy imaginary part is larger than the incoherent self-energy imaginary part, giving a larger damping width from the coherent mechanism
- The coherent mechanism induces a shift of strength toward both lower and higher energies, while the incoherent mechanism induces only a reduction of mean energy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear collective response that treated either the coherent damping mechanism (e.g., Bertsch (1983), Bortignon (1981)) or the collisional damping mechanism separately. The authors had previously investigated the incoherent damping mechanism alone (Lacroix (1998), Ayik (2000)) and found it sizeable at low temperature for light and medium nuclei. This paper combines both mechanisms in a consistent framework based on stochastic transport theory, enabling a more complete description of giant resonance fragmentation and damping. The results enable future studies of fine structure in nuclear response, particularly for the GQR in 40Ca and 208Pb where high-resolution experiments have been performed.
