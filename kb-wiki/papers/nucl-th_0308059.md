# The properties of the three-nucleon system with the dressed-bag model for nn interaction. I: New scalar three-body force

> A multi-component formalism is developed to describe three-body systems with nonstatic pairwise interactions and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. The dressed-bag model for $NN$ interaction based on the formation of an intermediate six-quark bag dressed by a $σ$-field is applied to the $3N$ system, where it results in a new three-body force between the six-quark bag and a third nucleon. Concise variational calculations of $3N$ bound states are carried out in the dressed-bag model including the new three-body force. It is shown that this three-body force gives at least half the $3N$ total binding energy, while the weight of non-nucleonic components in the $^3$H and $^3$He wavefunctions can exceed 10%. The new force model provides a very good description of $3N$ bound states with a reasonable magnitude of the $σNN$ coupling constant. The model can serve as a natural bridge between dynamical description of few-nucleon systems and the very successful Walecka approach to heavy nuclei and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The new scalar three-body force arising from the dressed-bag model gives at least half the total binding energy of the three-nucleon system, while the weight of non-nucleonic components in the \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He wavefunctions can exceed 10%. This contrasts with conventional models where three-body forces contribute only about 15% of the total binding energy.

## Method

The authors developed a multi-component formalism for the three-nucleon system where the total wavefunction includes a pure three-nucleon component and three dressed six-quark bag plus nucleon components. They applied the dressed-bag model for NN interaction, which describes the formation of an intermediate six-quark bag dressed by a \(\sigma\)-field, and extended it to the 3N system by including a new scalar three-body force arising from meson exchange (\(\pi\) and \(\sigma\)) between the dressed bag and the third nucleon. Concise variational calculations of 3N bound states were carried out using a single-pole approximation for the dressed-bag resolvent, with the effective interaction reducing to a sum of effective pairwise interactions that depend on the total three-body energy and the momentum of the spectator nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The new three-body force gives at least half the total 3N binding energy (no explicit binding energy value given for 3N in the truncated text).
- The weight of non-nucleonic components in the \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He wavefunctions can exceed 10%.
- In the deuteron, the weight of the dressed bag component varies from 2.5% to 3.6% in different versions of the model.
- The deuteron binding energy in DBM is 2.22454 MeV (variant a) and 2.22459 MeV (variant b), compared to the experimental value of 2.224575 MeV.
- The deuteron asymptotic mixing parameter \(\eta\) (D/S) is 0.0259 (variant a) and 0.0263 (variant b), compared to the experimental average of 0.0263.
- The deuteron quadrupole moment in DBM is 0.2754 fm\(^2\) (variant a) and 0.2768 fm\(^2\) (variant b), compared to the experimental value of 0.2859 fm\(^2\).
- The deuteron magnetic moment in DBM is 0.8548 \(\mu_N\) (variant a) and 0.8538 \(\mu_N\) (variant b), compared to the experimental value of 0.8574 \(\mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends the dressed-bag model (DBM) previously developed by Kukulin et al. for NN interactions to the three-nucleon system. It contrasts with conventional three-body force models such as Urbana-Illinois or Tucson-Melbourne, which are based on two-pion exchange with intermediate \(\Delta\)-isobar production and give only about 15% contribution to the 3N binding energy, whereas the DBM approach gives at least half the binding energy from the new scalar three-body force. The model can serve as a natural bridge between dynamical descriptions of few-nucleon systems and the Walecka approach to heavy nuclei and nuclear matter.
