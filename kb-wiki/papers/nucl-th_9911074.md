# Quarks and Neutron Halo Nuclei, Nuclear Clusters and Nuclear Molecules

> It is shown that the hole in the centre of $ ^{3}H $, $ ^{3}He $ and $ ^{4}He $, the neutron halos in nuclei, the $ α- $ and other clustering effects in nuclei and the nuclear molecules all basically arise due to the same underlying effect. We shall show that all these ground state properties of nuclei are manifestations of quark effects. The role of triton clustering in very neutron rich nuclei is emphasized. All these require the concept of hidden colour states which arise from confinement ideas of QCD for the multi-quark systems. This provides comprehensive understanding of diverse nuclear effects and makes unique predictions.

# Quarks and Neutron Halo Nuclei, Nuclear Clusters and Nuclear Molecules

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hole in the central density of 3H, 3He, and 4He, neutron halos in neutron-rich nuclei, alpha clustering, and nuclear molecules all arise from the same underlying quark effect: hidden colour states in multi-quark systems produce short-range repulsion that prevents nucleons from overlapping strongly, and the SU(12) group structure limits the number of quarks that can occupy the lowest s-state to 12, forcing extra neutrons to remain outside the core. The claim is conditional on the assumption that constituent quarks in the s-state describe nucleon structure and that hidden colour components are uniquely defined when clusters overlap strongly at distances of 1 fm or less.

## Method

The author applies group-theoretical calculations of hidden colour components for 6-quark, 9-quark, and 12-quark systems using the group chain SU(12) ⊃ SU(4)_SF ⊗ SU(3)_C, where SU(3)_C is the QCD colour group and SU(4)_SF contains spin and isospin. The hidden colour fraction is computed from coefficients of fractional parentage for these multi-quark systems. The resulting repulsive effect is then used to explain the central density depression in light nuclei, the formation of neutron halos as neutrons ricochet off compact "tennis-ball" cores, and the stability of alpha clusters and nuclear molecules.

## Key numbers

- The hidden colour component of the 6-quark system in deuteron is 80% (the |CC> part of the wavefunction)
- The hidden colour component of the 9-quark system is 97.6%
- The hidden colour component of the 12-quark system is 99.8%
- The rms radius of 11Li is 3.2 fm, while that of 9Li is 2.3 fm
- The size of 4He is 1.674 fm; the size of 3H is 1.7 fm; the size of 3He is 1.88 fm
- The binding energy of 4He is 28.29 MeV; the binding energy of 3H is 8.48 MeV; the binding energy of 3He is 7.7 MeV
- The inter-triton cluster bond energy extracted from binding energies of neutron-rich nuclei is approximately 5.4 MeV, with values ranging from 5.09 MeV (15B) to 6.63 MeV (9Li)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier predictions (Abbas, 1986; Abbas, 1988) that the central density depression in 3H, 3He, and 4He is a quark signature, and applies the same hidden colour mechanism to neutron halo nuclei discovered by Tanihata (1996) and Hansen (1993), to alpha clustering phenomena reviewed by Hodgson (1994) and Merchant and Rae (1994), and to nuclear molecules studied by Greiner, Park and Scheid (1995). The paper contradicts claims by Gonzalez and Vento (1992) that hidden colour is not a useful concept, arguing that hidden colour becomes uniquely defined when clusters overlap strongly. The work enables a unified description of diverse nuclear structure phenomena and makes specific predictions about which neutron-rich nuclei should exhibit neutron halos based on triton clustering.
