# On chiral extrapolations of charmed meson masses and coupled-channel reaction dynamics

> We perform an analysis of QCD lattice data on charmed meson masses. The quark-mass dependence of the data set is used to gain information on the size of counter terms of the chiral Lagrangian formulated with open-charm states with J^P= 0^- and J^P =1^- quantum numbers. Of particular interest are those counter terms that are active in the exotic flavour sextet channel. A chiral expansion scheme where physical masses enter the extrapolation formulae is developed and applied to the lattice data set. Good convergence properties are demonstrated and an accurate reproduction of the lattice data based on ensembles of PACS-CS, MILC, ETMC and HSC with pion and kaon masses smaller than 600 MeV is achieved. It is argued that a unique set of low-energy parameters is obtainable only if additional information from HSC on some scattering observables is included in our global fits. The elastic and inelastic s-wave pi D and eta D scattering as considered by HSC is reproduced faithfully. Based on such low-energy parameters we predict 15 phase shifts and in-elasticities at physical quark masses but also for an additional HSC ensemble at smaller pion mass. In addition we find a clear signal for a member of the exotic flavour sextet states in the eta D channel, below the bar K D_s threshold. For the isospin violating strong decay width of the D_{s0}(2317) we obtain the range (104-116) keV.

## Key claim

The quark-mass dependence of lattice QCD data on charmed meson masses can be used to constrain the counter terms of the chiral Lagrangian that are active in the exotic flavour sextet channel, but a unique set of low-energy parameters is obtainable only if additional information from HSC on scattering observables is included in the global fits.

## Method

The authors developed a chiral expansion scheme where physical masses enter the extrapolation formulae, adapting the chi-MS approach previously applied to baryon masses to the open-charm sector with J^P = 0^- and J^P = 1^- mesons. One-loop mass corrections were computed in a finite box using a relativistic chiral Lagrangian, with all finite box effects determined by the volume dependence of universal scalar loop functions. The D meson masses were determined numerically as solutions of a non-linear system for each set of low-energy constants, and the parameter set was fitted to lattice data from PACS-CS, MILC, ETMC and HSC ensembles with pion and kaon masses smaller than 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The isospin violating strong decay width of the D*_{s0}(2317) is obtained in the range (104-116) keV.
- The parameter g_P, from decay of charged D*-mesons, has magnitude |g_P| = 0.57 +/- 0.07.
- The parameter c_1 is estimated as c_1 ~ 0.44 - 0.47 from previous work.
- The combination c_3 + c_5 is estimated as c_3 + c_5 ~ 1.0 - 1.4 from previous work, with a complementary estimate from lattice QCD giving c_3 + c_5 ~ 1.
- Lattice data from ensembles with pion and kaon masses smaller than 600 MeV were used in the analysis.

## Context

This work extends the chi-MS chiral extrapolation scheme previously developed for baryon octet and decuplet states (Semke 2005, Semke 2011, Lutz 2014, Lutz 2018) to the open-charm meson sector. It addresses the open question of whether the chiral force in the flavour sextet sector leads to the formation of exotic open-charm meson states, which depends on the precise form of chiral correction terms (Hofmann 2003, Lutz 2007). Earlier studies (Liu 2012, Altenbuchinger 2013, Cleven 2014, Du 2016) using lattice QCD scattering lengths showed unnaturally large counter terms or residual dependence on the coupled-channel computation setup; this work uses the quark-mass dependence of D meson ground-state masses instead, aiming for less model-dependent results. The findings enable predictions of 15 phase shifts and in-elasticities at physical quark masses and for an additional HSC ensemble at smaller pion mass, and provide a clear signal for a member of the exotic flavour sextet states in the eta D channel below the bar K D_s threshold.
