# Covariant QCD Modeling of Light Meson Physics

> We summarize recent progress in soft QCD modeling based on the set of Dyson--Schwinger equations truncated to ladder-rainbow level. This covariant approach to hadron physics accommodates quark confinement and implements the QCD one-loop renormalization group behavior. We compare the dressed quark propagator, pseudoscalar and vector meson masses as a function of quark mass, and the rho -> pi pi coupling to recent lattice-QCD data. The error in the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation with increasing quark mass is quantified by comparison to the exact pseudoscalar mass relation as evaluated within the ladder-rainbow Dyson-Schwinger model.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equation model reproduces the one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD and implements quark confinement, but the error in the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GMOR) relation grows with quark mass: for the kaon the GMOR error is 4%, at m_Q = 0.4 GeV the error is 14%, and at the D meson the error is 30%.

## Method

The authors employ a Poincare covariant model defined by the rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator and the ladder truncation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states. The effective quark-antiquark interaction has an infrared Gaussian form with parameters fitted to the chiral condensate, pion mass, kaon mass, and pion decay constant, plus a perturbative QCD tail that preserves the one-loop renormalization group behavior. The dressed quark propagator amplitudes, pseudoscalar and vector meson masses as functions of quark mass, and the rho to pi pi coupling are computed and compared to lattice-QCD data and experiment.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate: calculated value (0.241 GeV)^3, fitted to experimental estimate (0.236 GeV)^3
- Pion mass: calculated 0.138 GeV, fitted to experimental 0.1385 GeV
- Pion decay constant: calculated 0.131 GeV, fitted to experimental 0.131 GeV
- Kaon mass: calculated 0.497 GeV, fitted to experimental 0.496 GeV
- Kaon decay constant: calculated 0.155 GeV, compared to experimental 0.160 GeV
- GMOR error for kaon: 4%; at m_Q = 0.4 GeV: 14%; at D meson: 30%
- g_rho to pi pi: calculated 5.14, experimental 6.02, lattice-QCD 6.08 (+2.04/-1.00)
- g_phi to K K: calculated 4.25, experimental 4.64
- g_K*+ to K0 pi+: calculated 4.81, experimental 4.60
- Vector meson masses: M_V is 5% too low for rho and 5% too high for phi
- Predicted eta_b mass if fit continued to Upsilon: 10.0 GeV
- Onset of pQCD behavior for form factors: 15-20 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation modeling of Maris and Roberts (1997, 1999) by comparing the dressed quark propagator to lattice-QCD results from Bowman (2002) using staggered fermions, and comparing vector meson mass variation with quark mass to unquenched lattice data from CP-PACS (Aoki, 1999) and UK-QCD (Allton, 1998). The exact pseudoscalar mass relation derived from the axial vector Ward-Takahashi identity quantifies the breakdown of the GMOR relation at higher quark masses, and the vector meson strong decay couplings are compared to lattice-QCD results from McNeile (2002). The work enables future interplay between lattice simulations and continuum modeling to improve understanding of nonperturbative QCD for hadron physics.
