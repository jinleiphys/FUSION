# Hyperons in neutron stars within Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld theory of gravity

> We investigate the mass-radius relation of neutron star (NS) with hyperons inside its core by using the Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld (EiBI) theory of gravity. The equation of state of the star is calculated by using the relativistic mean field model under which the standard SU(6) prescription and hyperons potential depths are used to determine the hyperon coupling constants. We found that, for $4\times 10^{6}~\rm{m^2}~\lesssim~κ\lesssim~6\times 10^{6}~\rm{m^2}$, the corresponding NS mass and radius predicted by the EiBI theory of gravity is compatible with observational constraints of maximum NS mass and radius. The corresponding $κ$ value is also compatible with the $κ$ range predicted by the astrophysical-cosmological constraints. We also found that the parameter $κ$ could control the size and the compactness of a neutron star.

# Hyperons in neutron stars within Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld theory of gravity

## Key claim

The hyperon puzzle (the difficulty of reaching 2 solar mass neutron stars when hyperons soften the equation of state) can be resolved within Eddington-inspired Born-Infeld (EiBI) gravity for a specific range of the EiBI parameter κ, namely 4×10⁶ m² ≲ κ ≲ 6×10⁶ m², without requiring exotic physics beyond modified gravity.

## Method

The authors solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations derived from EiBI gravity using the Palatini formalism, with the neutron star core equation of state computed from an extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model. Hyperon coupling constants are determined using the standard SU(6) prescription combined with empirical hyperon potential depths in symmetric nuclear matter (U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV). The crust equation of state is taken from Miyatsu et al. (2013), and the full system is integrated numerically using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The EiBI parameter range compatible with both the 2 M⊙ pulsar mass constraint and astrophysical-cosmological bounds is 4×10⁶ m² ≲ κ ≲ 6×10⁶ m².
- The maximum neutron star mass reaches ≥ 2.0 M⊙ for κ ≳ 4×10⁶ m², consistent with the pulsar J0348+0432 mass of 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙ from Antoniadis et al. (2013).
- The canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star radius falls within the range R₁.₄ = 10.7–13.1 km from Steiner et al. (2010).
- The cosmological-astrophysical upper bound from Avelino (2012) gives κ ≲ 6×10⁶ m² for a neutron star with radius R ∼ 12 km and core density above ρ ∼ 56.17 MeV fm⁻³.
- The redshift z for masses M ≳ 1.5 M⊙ reaches z = 0.35, consistent with the EXO 0748-676 constraint of z ≥ 0.33 from Cottam et al. (2002).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sotani (2014), who showed that EiBI gravity can adjust the maximum neutron star mass by varying κ, but used a different equation of state without hyperons. The authors demonstrate that the hyperon puzzle, which arises in general relativity when hyperons soften the equation of state and prevent reaching 2 M⊙ (see Lonardoni et al., Yamamoto et al., and Artyom et al.), is naturally resolved in EiBI gravity for the allowed κ range. The study also connects to work by Avelino (2012) on astrophysical-cosmological constraints on κ, and to studies of neutron stars in f(R) gravity by Artyom et al. (2014) and with anisotropic pressure by Sulaksono and Agustin (2014). The results suggest that neutron stars can serve as astrophysical probes to test deviations from general relativity.
