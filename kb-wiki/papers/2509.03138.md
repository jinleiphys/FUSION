# Suppression of dynamical momentum-space shell by chiral symmetry

> We investigate the appearance of quark degrees of freedom in dense isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. We employ the parity doublet model to incorporate chiral dynamics. Specifically, we contrast quarkyonic matter, in which quarks occupy states above the nucleon Fermi surface, with baryquark matter, in which quarks populate states inside the nucleonic Fermi sea. We find that while baryquark matter is generally energetically favored over quarkyonic matter, the self-consistent treatment of the momentum-space shell reveals that purely hadronic matter provides the lowest free energy up to densities well beyond nuclear saturation. Consequently, the contribution of quarks is not relevant within the model's domain of applicability, even though chiral symmetry becomes restored. This demonstrates that the onset of quark degrees of freedom and the restoration of chiral symmetry need not coincide.

# Suppression of dynamical momentum-space shell by chiral symmetry

## Key claim
The self-consistent treatment of the momentum-space shell reveals that purely hadronic matter provides the lowest free energy up to densities well beyond nuclear saturation, even though chiral symmetry becomes restored; the onset of quark degrees of freedom and the restoration of chiral symmetry need not coincide.

## Method
The authors employ the SU(2) parity doublet model (PDM) in a relativistic mean-field approximation at zero temperature to incorporate chiral dynamics into dense isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. They contrast two configurations, quarkyonic matter, where quarks occupy states above the nucleon Fermi surface, and baryquark matter, where quarks populate states inside the nucleonic Fermi sea, and treat the momentum-space shell width either via a power-law parametrization or as a free parameter determined by energy minimization at fixed baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Chiral invariant mass fixed to \(m_0 = 800\ \text{MeV}\)
- QCD scale parameter \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 300\ \text{MeV}\) used in the power-law shell parametrization \(\Delta = \Lambda_{\text{QCD}} (\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/k_F)^\alpha\)
- In the baryquark model with physical quark mass \(m_q = m_N/3\), the onset of quark degrees of freedom occurs around \(n_B \simeq 8n_0\), corresponding to \(\mu_B \simeq 1.5\ \text{GeV}\)
- For nearly massless quarks (\(m_q = m_N/50\)) in the baryquark model, a minimum in energy density appears at \(Y_q \simeq 0.08\) at both \(n_B = 4n_0\) and \(n_B = 8n_0\)

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet model studies of dense matter (Motohiro, Minamikawa 2020, Gao 2024) by embedding both quarkyonic matter (McLerran 2007) and baryquark matter (Koch 2022) into a chiral-symmetric framework. It contradicts the expectation from models with constituent quarks and hard-core repulsion (Koch 2022) that baryquark matter is energetically favored over purely hadronic matter at moderate densities; here, the self-consistent mean-field treatment with chiral dynamics delays the onset of quarks to densities at the border of the model's applicability. The results enable future work incorporating diquark pairing, color superconductivity, finite isospin asymmetry, and neutron-star phenomenology.
