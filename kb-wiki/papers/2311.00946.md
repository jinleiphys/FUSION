# Study of bulk properties of the system formed in U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ =~2.12~GeV using JAM model

> The Lanzhou Cooling-Storage-Ring facility is set to conduct experiments involving Uranium-Uranium collisions at the center of mass energies ranging from 2.12 to 2.4 GeV. Our investigation is focused on various bulk observables, which include charged particle multiplicity ($N_{\text{ch}}$), average transverse momentum ($\langle p_{\text{T}}\rangle$), initial eccentricity ($ε_{n}$), and flow harmonics ($v_{n}$), for different orientations of U+U collisions within the range of $0^{\circ} < θ< 120 ^{\circ}$ at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 2.12$ GeV ($p_{\mathrm lab}$ = 500 MeV). Among the various collision configurations at this energy, the tip-tip scenario emerged with the highest average charged particle multiplicity, denoted as $\langle N_{\text{ch}} \rangle$. Notably, both the second and third-order eccentricities, $ε_{2,3}$, revealed intricate patterns as they varied with impact parameter across distinct configurations. The tip-tip configuration displayed the most pronounced magnitude of rapidity-odd directed flow ($v_{1}$), whereas the body-body configuration exhibited the least pronounced magnitude. Concerning elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) near mid-rapidity ($η< 1.0$), a negative sign is observed for all configurations except for the side-side exhibited a distinctly positive sign. Within the spectrum of configurations, the body-body scenario displayed the highest magnitude of $v_{2}$. For reaction plane correlated triangular flow ($v_{3}$), the body-body configuration emerged with the largest magnitude while the side-side exhibited the smallest magnitude. Our study seeks to establish a fundamental understanding of various U+U collision configurations in preparation for the forthcoming CEE experiment.

## Key claim

The JAM transport model predicts that for U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 2.12\) GeV, the sign and magnitude of elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) near mid-rapidity depend strongly on the nuclear orientation: all configurations except side-side show a negative \(v_2\), while the side-side configuration uniquely displays a positive sign. This result is conditional on using the JAM model in mean-field mode with the MD2 equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM) in its mean-field mode (RQMD.RMF with MD2 equation of state, nuclear incompressibility \(K = 380\) MeV) to simulate U+U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 2.12\) GeV. They selected four specific nuclear orientations (tip-tip, body-body, side-side, and general) by fixing the polar and azimuthal angles of the projectile and target uranium nuclei relative to their symmetry axes, and generated approximately 1 million events per configuration over an impact parameter range of 0.0 to 15.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- The tip-tip configuration produced the highest average charged particle multiplicity (\(\langle N_{\text{ch}} \rangle\)) among all orientations.
- For the most central collisions (impact parameter \(b = 2.52\) fm), the average number of participating nucleons \(\langle N_{\text{part}} \rangle\) was 389.70 (tip-tip), 397.57 (body-body), 378.37 (side-side), and 389.07 (general).
- For peripheral collisions at \(b = 13.8\) fm, \(\langle N_{\text{part}} \rangle\) was 24.35 (tip-tip), 64.86 (body-body), 17.16 (side-side), and 32.85 (general).
- The body-body configuration exhibited the highest magnitude of elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) near mid-rapidity (\(\eta < 1.0\)), while the side-side configuration showed a distinctly positive sign for \(v_2\).
- The body-body configuration displayed the largest magnitude of reaction-plane-correlated triangular flow (\(v_3\)), and the side-side configuration exhibited the smallest magnitude.
- The tip-tip configuration displayed the most pronounced magnitude of rapidity-odd directed flow (\(v_1\)), while the body-body configuration showed the least pronounced magnitude.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of deformed uranium-uranium collisions at RHIC energies (STAR Collaboration, 2015; Haque et al., 2011) down to the much lower energy of \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 2.12\) GeV, relevant for the upcoming Cooling-Storage-Ring External Target Experiment (CEE) at Lanzhou. The results provide a baseline for distinguishing nuclear orientation effects in bulk observables at intermediate energies, and the authors plan to systematically vary the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) in future JAM simulations, following the linear correlation between \(\langle v_2^2 \rangle\) and \(\beta_2^2\) established by Giacalone et al. (2021) for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
