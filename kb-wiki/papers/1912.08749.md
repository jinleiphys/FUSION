# Covariant multipole expansion of local currents for massive states of any spin

> We study the structure of scalar, vector, and tensor currents for on-shell massive particles of any spin. When considering higher values for the spin of the particle, the number of form factors (FFs) involved in the decomposition of the matrix elements associated with these local currents increases. We identify all the fundamental structures that give rise to the independent FFs, systematically for any spin value. These structures can be conveniently organised using an expansion in covariant multipoles, built solely from the Lorentz generators. This approach allows one to uniquely identify the terms which are universal and those that arise because of spin. We derive counting rules which relate the number of FFs to the total spin $j$ of the state, showing explicitly that these rules match all the well-known cases up to spin 2.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic, spin-independent parametrization of scalar, vector, and rank-2 tensor current matrix elements for massive on-shell particles of any spin, using both a tensor-product approach and a covariant multipole expansion built from Lorentz generators; the counting rules for form factors are derived generally, but the explicit verification is shown only up to spin 2, and the method relies on the assumption that the generalized polarization tensors satisfy specific subsidiary conditions to eliminate unphysical spin states.

## Method

The authors first derive parametrizations using a tensor-product approach, where "seed" Lorentz structures are identified for each current type and a "tower" of elements is generated by contracting with metric tensors and momentum-transfer vectors, with the number of tower elements depending on the spin floor \( \lfloor j \rfloor \). They then develop an alternative covariant multipole expansion based on symmetric and traceless products of the Lorentz generators \( S^{\mu\nu} \), which truncates at order \( 2j+1 \) and guarantees linear independence of the basis structures. Both methods are applied to integer-spin states in the \( (n/2, n/2) \) representation and half-integer-spin states in the \( ((n+1)/2, n/2) \oplus (n/2, (n+1)/2) \) representation, using on-shell identities and discrete symmetry constraints (P, T, Hermiticity) to eliminate redundant structures.

## Key numbers

* Scalar operator: number of form factors = \( \lfloor j \rfloor + 1 \), where \( \lfloor j \rfloor \) is the floor of the spin (largest integer ≤ \( j \)); for spin-0: 1 FF; spin-1/2: 1 FF; spin-1: 2 FFs; spin-3/2: 2 FFs; spin-2: 3 FFs.
* Vector operator: number of form factors = \( 2j + 1 \); for spin-0: 1 FF; spin-1/2: 2 FFs; spin-1: 3 FFs; spin-3/2: 4 FFs; spin-2: 5 FFs.
* Tensor operator: number of form factors = \( 4j + 5\lfloor j \rfloor + 3 - \theta(j \geq 1) \), where \( \theta(j \geq 1) = 1 \) for \( j \geq 1 \) and 0 otherwise; for spin-0: 3 FFs; spin-1/2: 5 FFs; spin-1: 9 FFs; spin-3/2: 14 FFs; spin-2: 20 FFs.
* The symmetric conserved part of the tensor operator is parametrized by \( 2(j+1) + 3\lfloor j \rfloor - \theta(j \geq 1) \) form factors.

## Context

This work extends the well-established counting of electromagnetic form factors for arbitrary spin (Cheshkov 1963, Scadron 1968, Williams 1970, Lorce 2009) to scalar and rank-2 tensor currents, resolving past ambiguities where different parametrizations for the energy-momentum tensor gave conflicting form-factor counts (Cheshkov 1963, Scadron 1968, Williams 1970). The covariant multipole expansion technique, building on earlier suggestions for spin-1 hadrons (Cosyn 2019) and recent work on Lorentz-group multipoles (Bautista 2019), provides a systematic framework that can be extended to higher-rank operators and non-local currents such as those in parton distribution functions, generalized parton distributions, and transverse-momentum-dependent distributions.
