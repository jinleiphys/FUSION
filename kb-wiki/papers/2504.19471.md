# Properties of $X(3872)$ from hadronic potentials coupled to quarks

> The concept of compositeness is used to quantitatively discuss the hadronic molecular nature in exotic hadrons. In this work, we develop a formulation to explicitly introduce the compact quark state contribution in the hadronic potentials together with the direct four-point interaction. We derive analytic expressions of the compositeness from the effective potential between hadrons when the system has a bound state. Applying this formulation to the $X(3872)$, we examine the variation of the compositeness with respect to the binding energy, energy of $χ_{c1}(2P)$, cutoff momentum, and strength of the direct $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theoretical framework that explicitly introduces a compact quark (c̄c) state contribution alongside a direct four-point hadron interaction to describe the X(3872), and finds that the compositeness (molecular probability) of the X(3872) is 0.99 under representative parameters, but this value varies significantly with the binding energy, bare χc1(2P) energy, cutoff momentum, and direct interaction strength, with the near-unity compositeness being a consequence of the extremely small binding energy rather than a robust prediction.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel Hamiltonian for the c̄c and D⁰D̄*⁰ systems, applying the Feshbach method to derive an effective energy-dependent non-local D⁰D̄*⁰ potential that includes both a direct hadron interaction term and a term from coupling to the χc1(2P) compact quark state. Using a Yukawa-type transition form factor with coupling constant g₀ and cutoff μ, they solve the Schrödinger equation analytically for the bound state wave function and derive analytic expressions for the compositeness X from both the wave function normalization and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They then vary four parameters (binding energy B, bare energy E₀, cutoff μ, and direct interaction strength ωʰ) one at a time while adjusting g₀ to maintain the fixed binding energy, and compute the resulting compositeness.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of X(3872): B = 40 keV (measured from D⁰D̄*⁰ threshold)
- Bare energy of χc1(2P) state: E₀ = 78 MeV (from constituent quark model, Godfrey 1985)
- Cutoff parameter: μ = 140 MeV (pion mass)
- Compositeness with representative parameters (ωʰ = 0): X = 0.99
- Lower bound on direct interaction strength: ωʰ_b = -1/I(-B) (negative, indicating attraction)
- Range of binding energy studied: 40 keV ≤ B ≤ 2.5 MeV (upper bound from singularity κ = μ/2)
- Range of bare energy studied: -B ≤ E₀ ≤ 78 MeV
- Range of cutoff studied: 140 MeV ≤ μ ≤ 1 GeV
- Range of direct interaction studied: ωʰ_b ≤ ωʰ ≤ -ωʰ_b

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Feshbach-based framework (Terashima 2023) by explicitly introducing a direct D⁰D̄*⁰ four-point interaction term, enabling a more complete description of the X(3872) as a superposition of D⁰D̄*⁰ molecular and compact c̄c components. The compositeness analysis follows the Weinberg (1962, 1965) formalism for s-wave shallow bound states, and the parameter dependence study parallels discussions in Kinugawa (2023) and Song (2023). The results enable future calculations of observable quantities such as phase shifts, scattering length, and effective range in relation to the compositeness variation.
