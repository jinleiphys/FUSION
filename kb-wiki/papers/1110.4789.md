# Determination of 141Pr(alpha,n)144Pm cross sections at energies of relevance for the astrophysical p-process using the gamma-gamma coincidence method

> The reaction 141Pr(alpha,n)144Pm was investigated between E_alpha=11 MeV and 15 MeV with the activation method using the gamma-gamma coincidence method with a segmented clover-type high-purity Germanium (HPGe) detector. Measurements with four other HPGe detectors were additionally made. The comparison proves that the gamma-gamma coincidence method is an excellent tool to investigate cross sections down to the microbarn range. The (alpha,n) reaction at low energy is especially suited to test alpha+nucleus optical-model potentials for application in the astrophysical p-process. The experimentally determined cross sections were compared to Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using different optical potentials and generally an unsatisfactory reproduction of the data was found. A local potential was constructed to improve the description of the data. The consequences of applying the same potential to calculate astrophysical (gamma,alpha) rates for 145Pm and 148Gd were explored. In summary, the data and further results underline the problems in global predictions of alpha+nucleus optical potentials at astrophysically relevant energies.

## Key claim

The measured 141Pr(alpha,n)144Pm cross sections are generally not well reproduced by Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using existing global alpha+nucleus optical potentials, and a local potential was constructed to improve the description of the data; however, the results underline the persistent problems in global predictions of alpha+nucleus optical potentials at astrophysically relevant energies, even when using the gamma-gamma coincidence method to reach cross sections down to the microbarn range.

## Method

The 141Pr(alpha,n)144Pm reaction was studied using the activation method at alpha beam energies between 11.0 MeV and 15.0 MeV at the cyclotron of the Physikalisch-Technische Bundesanstalt (PTB) in Braunschweig. Thin metallic praseodymium targets (areal densities 1.36-1.88 x 10^18 cm^-2) were irradiated for 1-17 hours, and the gamma-ray activity from the decay of 144Pm (half-life 363 days) was measured off-beam using a segmented clover-type HPGe detector in gamma-gamma coincidence mode, with additional measurements using four other HPGe detectors (including one at the UDO underground laboratory) for cross-check. The experimentally determined cross sections were compared to Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using different optical potentials, and a local potential was constructed to improve the fit.

## Key numbers

* Cross section at E_alpha = 11047 +/- 28 keV: 0.008 +/- 0.001 mb (measured with Cologne clover detector)
* Cross section at E_alpha = 11305 +/- 28 keV: 0.015 +/- 0.001 mb (Cologne clover), 0.015 +/- 0.001 mb (ULB detector), 0.014 +/- 0.001 mb (PTB 50% detector)
* Q value of 141Pr(alpha,n)144Pm: -10246.19 +/- 2.70 keV; reaction threshold: 10537.23 +/- 2.78 keV
* Half-life of 144Pm: 363 +/- 14 days
* Areal particle densities of targets: between 1.36 and 1.88 x 10^18 cm^-2 (corresponding to 318-440 microgram/cm^2)
* Beam energy uncertainty: +/- 25 keV

## Context

This work extends experimental data on alpha-induced reactions relevant for the astrophysical gamma process, where previous measurements on 144Sm(alpha,gamma) by Somorjai et al. (1998) found cross sections more than an order of magnitude below standard predictions at astrophysical energies. The study enables improved testing of alpha+nucleus optical-model potentials at lower energies than previously available for this target, complementing elastic scattering experiments by Kiss et al. (2011) which sometimes suffer from ambiguous solutions, and provides data that can help construct better global optical potentials for predicting (gamma,alpha) rates on nuclei such as 145Pm and 148Gd in the problematic mass region A=150-165.
