# Entanglement Rearrangement in Self-Consistent Nuclear Structure Calculations

> Entanglement properties of $^4$He and $^6$He are investigated using nuclear many-body calculations, specifically the single-nucleon entanglement entropy, and the two-nucleon mutual information and negativity. Nuclear wavefunctions are obtained by performing active-space no-core configuration-interaction calculations using a two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from chiral effective field theory. Entanglement measures within single-particle bases, the harmonic oscillator (HO), Hartree-Fock (HF), natural (NAT) and variational natural (VNAT) bases, are found to exhibit different degrees of complexity. Entanglement in both nuclei is found to be more localized within NAT and VNAT bases than within a HO basis for the optimal HO parameters, and, as anticipated, a core-valence (tensor product) structure emerges from the full six-body calculation of $^{6}$He. The two-nucleon mutual information shows that the VNAT basis, which typically exhibits good convergence properties, effectively decouples the active and inactive spaces. We conclude that measures of one- and two-nucleon entanglement are useful in analyzing the structure of nuclear wave functions, in particular the efficacy of basis states, and may provide useful metrics toward developing more efficient schemes for ab initio computations of the structure and reactions of nuclei, and quantum many-body systems more generally.

## Key claim

The entanglement structure of light helium nuclei is more localized in natural (NAT) and variational natural (VNAT) single-particle bases than in a harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, and a core-valence tensor-product structure emerges from the full six-body calculation of $^{6}$He, but the authors note that the NAT and VNAT bases are difficult to distinguish using single-orbital entanglement measures alone, requiring two-orbital mutual information to reveal differences.

## Method

Active-space no-core configuration-interaction calculations were performed for $^{4}$He and $^{6}$He using the bare NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from chiral effective field theory, with all single-particle bases expanded on a set of 7 HO shells with frequency $\hbar\Omega = 30$ MeV. Entanglement measures (single-orbital von Neumann entropy, two-orbital mutual information, and negativity) were computed within four different single-particle bases: harmonic oscillator (HO), Hartree-Fock (HF), natural (NAT, diagonalizing the one-body density matrix), and variational natural (VNAT, obtained by solving a multi-configurational self-consistent field equation). The model space size was varied from 2 to 7 major shells to study convergence.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of $^{4}$He in the VNAT basis: -21.40 MeV (2 shells), -24.89 MeV (3 shells), -26.61 MeV (4 shells), -27.27 MeV (5 shells), -27.47 MeV (6 shells), -27.50 MeV (7 shells, full configuration space).
* Ground-state energy of $^{4}$He in the HO basis: -19.15 MeV (2 shells), -23.29 MeV (3 shells), -25.72 MeV (4 shells), -26.88 MeV (5 shells), -27.44 MeV (6 shells), -27.50 MeV (7 shells).
* Sum of single-orbital entanglement entropies $S^{(1)}_{tot}$ for $^{4}$He in the VNAT basis: 0.441 (2 shells), 0.746 (3 shells), 1.063 (4 shells), 1.042 (5 shells), 0.963 (6 shells), 1.006 (7 shells).
* Sum of single-orbital entanglement entropies $S^{(1)}_{tot}$ for $^{4}$He in the HO basis: 0.596 (2 shells), 1.143 (3 shells), 1.065 (4 shells), 1.348 (5 shells), 1.264 (6 shells), 1.217 (7 shells).
* Maximum possible single-orbital entanglement entropy: $\ln(2)$, achieved when the occupation number is exactly 1/2.
* The 0p-0h component of the $^{4}$He wavefunction in the HF basis decreases from approximately 98-94% for small model spaces to approximately 70% when the 5th shell is included, and to approximately 53% when including the 6th shell.

## Context

This work extends the study of orbital entanglement in nuclei, previously explored in $^{64}$Ge using DMRG with a phenomenological shell model interaction (PhysRevC.92.051303), and complements the work of Gorton and Johnson (2019) on proton-neutron entanglement entropy in phenomenological shell-model calculations. The results suggest that entanglement measures could provide metrics for developing more efficient ab initio computation schemes, potentially enabling hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for nuclear structure and reactions, and may help explain the success of phenomenological models such as the nuclear shell model.
