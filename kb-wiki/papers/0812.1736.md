# Classical QGP : III. The free energy

> We explore further the classical QGP using methods from classical liquids. The partition function of an ensemble of SU(N_c) colored charge spheres is constructed. We analyze it using a cumulant expansion (low density) and a loop expansion (high temperature) after resumming the Debye screening effects. The pertinent free energies are derived in both limits and compared to recent molecular dynamics results.

## Key claim

The free energy of a classical quark-gluon plasma (cQGP) can be derived analytically using methods from classical liquid theory, specifically a cumulant expansion at low density and a loop expansion at high temperature, but only after resumming Debye screening effects and introducing a hard-sphere repulsive core of diameter sigma to prevent classical collapse.

## Method

The authors constructed the grand canonical partition function for an ensemble of SU(N_c) colored charge spheres with a hard-sphere repulsive core of diameter sigma, treating the color charges as smeared uniformly on the sphere surfaces. They analyzed the partition function using a cumulant (virial) expansion valid at low density and a loop expansion valid at high temperature, both after resumming Debye screening effects. The free energies from both expansions were derived to several orders and compared to molecular dynamics simulation results from Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed (2006).

## Context

This work extends the classical QGP model developed by Gelman, Shuryak and Zahed (2006) by applying analytical methods from classical liquid theory, specifically the primitive model (PM) and restricted primitive model (RPM) used for ionic liquids by Hansen and McDonald (1976) and Caillol and Raimbault (2000). The results enable systematic comparison with molecular dynamics simulations of the cQGP and provide a framework for calculating thermodynamic properties beyond the Debye-Huckel approximation, including the effects of a finite hard-core size and non-Abelian color structure.
