# QCD at nonzero chemical potential: recent progress on the lattice

> We summarise recent progress in simulating QCD at nonzero baryon density using complex Langevin dynamics. After a brief outline of the main idea, we discuss gauge cooling as a means to control the evolution. Subsequently we present a status report for heavy dense QCD and its phase structure, full QCD with staggered quarks, and full QCD with Wilson quarks, both directly and using the hopping parameter expansion to all orders.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics, combined with gauge cooling to control the evolution in the complexified configuration space, can simulate QCD at nonzero baryon chemical potential without a sign problem, but the method's reliability depends on the equilibrium distribution being well localised in the imaginary direction and on a posteriori verification of correctness criteria.

## Method

The authors apply complex Langevin dynamics to lattice QCD, where the gauge links evolve in SL(N,C) rather than SU(N) due to the complex action. They use gauge cooling, which applies complex gauge transformations to reduce unitarity norms (measures of distance from SU(N)) without affecting observables, and an adaptive Langevin stepsize to keep the process controlled. Simulations are performed for heavy dense QCD (HDQCD), full QCD with staggered quarks, and full QCD with Wilson quarks, including a hopping parameter expansion to all orders.

## Key numbers

* For heavy dense QCD on a 4^4 lattice at beta=5.6, kappa=0.12 and N_f=2, the unitarity norm Tr(U_4 U_4^dagger)/3 is shown evolving over Langevin time for two values of the chemical potential mu, without gauge cooling (Aarts 2008).
* Gauge cooling reduces the unitarity norm and brings a configuration in SL(N,C) as close as possible to SU(N), with the reduction quantified by the change in norm d_1' - d_1 = -(alpha/N) f_{ax}^2 < 0 after a gauge transformation at site x.

## Context

This work extends earlier complex Langevin simulations of heavy dense QCD (Aarts 2008) and addresses the sign problem that prevents standard lattice QCD at nonzero chemical potential. It provides a status report on applying the method to full QCD with both staggered and Wilson fermions, building on recent first applications (Sexty 2013, Aarts 2014). The results enable future first-principles determinations of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density, which is relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments and neutron star physics.
