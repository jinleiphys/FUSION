# Low-Dimensional Fluctuations and Pseudogap in Gaudin-Yang Fermi Gases

> Pseudogap is a ubiquitous phenomenon in strongly correlated systems such as high-$T_{\rm c}$ superconductors, ultracold atoms and nuclear physics. While pairing fluctuations inducing the pseudogap are known to be enhanced in low-dimensional systems, such effects have not been explored well in one of the most fundamental 1D models, that is, Gaudin-Yang model. In this work, we show that the pseudogap effect can be visible in the single-particle excitation in this system using a diagrammatic approach. Fermionic single-particle spectra exhibit a unique crossover from the double-particle dispersion to pseudogap state with increasing the attractive interaction and the number density at finite temperature. Surprisingly, our results of thermodynamic quantities in unpolarized and polarized gases show an excellent agreement with the recent quantum Monte Carlo and complex Langevin results, even in the region where the pseudogap appears.

## Key claim

The pseudogap effect can be visible in the single-particle excitation spectra of the one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang Fermi gas at finite temperature, but only in the parameter region where the diagrammatic approach is validated by agreement with quantum Monte Carlo results for thermodynamic quantities; the approach has an artificial limitation in the strong-coupling regime where the four-point vertex denominator crosses zero around \(\lambda \gtrsim 3.1\).

## Method

The authors applied a diagrammatic \(T\)-matrix approach to the attractive Gaudin-Yang model in one dimension, incorporating the Hartree self-energy as a building block and then including pairing fluctuation effects through the four-point vertex \(\Gamma(q,i\nu_\ell)\). The self-energy for fluctuation corrections was computed from \(\Gamma\) and the Hartree Green's function, and the dressed Green's function was used to obtain the number density, single-particle spectral function, and density of states. The approach was benchmarked against quantum Monte Carlo results for unpolarized gases and complex Langevin results for polarized gases.

## Key numbers

- The interaction parameter \(\lambda^2 = m g^2 / T\) characterizes the ratio between the two-body binding energy \(E_{\rm b} = 1/(m a^2)\) and temperature \(T\).
- The calculated number density \(n/n_0\) as a function of \(\mu/T\) shows excellent agreement with 1D QMC results for \(\lambda = 1, 2, 2.5, 3\).
- The Hartree-Fock result deviates from QMC results in the strong-coupling regime (\(\lambda \geq 2\)) where \(E_{\rm b} = \lambda^2 T/4 \geq T\).
- The denominator of \(\Gamma(q,0)\) crosses zero around \(\lambda \gtrsim 3.1\), indicating the artificial limitation of the approach.
- The polarization \(P = (n_\uparrow - n_\downarrow)/(n_\uparrow + n_\downarrow)\) at \(\lambda = 1\) and \(\lambda = 2\) for \(h/T = 0.5, 1, 2\) agrees with complex Langevin results.

## Context

This work extends the study of pseudogap phenomena, previously explored in two-dimensional and three-dimensional ultracold atomic systems (Chen, Strinati, Ohashi, Zwerger, Mueller), to the one-dimensional Gaudin-Yang model, where pairing fluctuations are expected to be enhanced. The results agree with recent quantum Monte Carlo simulations by Hoffman et al. (DrutQMC) and complex Langevin simulations by Drut et al. (DrutCL), and the approach enables future investigation of fluctuation effects on Fulde-Ferrel-Larkin-Ovchinikov (FFLO)-like pairing states, transport in quantum wires, and multi-polaronic excitations.
