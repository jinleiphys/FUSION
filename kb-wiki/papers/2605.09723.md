# Cooling of Isolated Neutron Stars with Hyperon-mixed Kaon-Condensation Matter

> We investigate the thermal evolution of isolated neutron stars containing hyperon--mixed kaon--condensed matter, focusing on the role of proton superconductivity. The equation of state utilized for cooling calculation is based upon the minimal relativistic mean--field framework supplemented by chiral SU(3) dynamics for kaon condensation with an additional component on the three-baryon force, which ensures stiffness at high densities enough to meet astrophysical constraints on neutron-star masses and radii. We show that the nucleonic direct Urca processes operate at relatively low stellar masses ($M \gtrsim 1.3\,M_\odot$), erasing any observable signature of strangeness in the absence of superfluidity. However, if the proton $^1{\rm S}_0$ superconductivity works, because of suppression of fast neutrino cooling processes, the cooling scenario could become relevant with the strangeness, depending on the density regions of the pairing gap. In particular, if the proton superconductivity is so strong in high-density regions ($T_{c,p}\sim10^{10}~{\rm K}$), the nucleon and hyperon direct Urca processes shut down, which makes the kaon-induced Urca processes dominant in massive neutron stars. This scenario is in good agreement with several cold isolated neutron stars identified recently. Hence, we suggest that strong proton superconductivity can render kaon condensation observationally visible through cold neutron-star observations, providing a potential signature of strangeness in dense matter.

## Key claim

The authors show that strong proton superconductivity (with critical temperature \(T_{c,p}\sim10^{10}~{\rm K}\)) in high-density regions can shut down both nucleon and hyperon direct Urca processes, making kaon-induced Urca processes dominant in massive neutron stars; this scenario can make kaon condensation observationally visible through cold neutron-star observations, but only if the proton pairing gap extends deep enough into the inner core.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for hyperon-mixed kaon-condensed matter using a minimal relativistic mean-field framework supplemented by chiral SU(3) dynamics for kaon condensation and a phenomenological three-baryon force (the string-junction model SJM2) to maintain stiffness at high densities. They computed cooling curves for isolated neutron stars using an extended version of the NSCool code that includes hyperonic and kaon-induced neutrino emission processes together with baryon superfluidity and proton superconductivity, varying the kaon-neutron sigma term \(\Sigma_{Kn}\) between 300 and 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Nucleonic direct Urca processes operate at stellar masses \(M \gtrsim 1.3\,M_\odot\), erasing observable strangeness signatures in the absence of superfluidity.
* Strong proton superconductivity with critical temperature \(T_{c,p}\sim10^{10}~{\rm K}\) shuts down nucleon and hyperon direct Urca processes in high-density regions.
* The kaon-neutron sigma term \(\Sigma_{Kn}\) is taken as 300 and 400 MeV, corresponding to kaon optical potentials at nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16~{\rm fm}^{-3}\) of \(U_K = -111~{\rm MeV}\) and \(-131~{\rm MeV}\), respectively.
* The nuclear saturation density is set to \(n_0 = 0.16~{\rm fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(B_0 = -16.3~{\rm MeV}\), incompressibility \(K_0 = 240~{\rm MeV}\), symmetry energy \(S_0 = 31.5~{\rm MeV}\), and slope parameter \(L = 65~{\rm MeV}\).
* The kaon-induced Urca processes become dominant in massive neutron stars when strong proton superconductivity is present, yielding surface temperatures consistent with several cold isolated neutron stars identified recently.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron-star cooling in hyperonic matter (Negreiros 2018, 2018MNRAS.475.4347R, 2019MNRAS.487.2639R) and kaon-condensed matter (1988PhRvD..37.2042B, 2001ApJ...553..382P, 2019JKPS...74..547L) by computing cooling curves with both hyperons and kaon condensation simultaneously for the first time (see also the very recent work 2026arXiv260322164W). The results suggest that strong proton superconductivity can render kaon condensation observable through thermal signatures in cold neutron stars, providing a potential direct probe of strangeness in dense matter that complements mass-radius constraints from NICER.
