# Early quark production and approach to chemical equilibrium

> We perform real-time lattice simulations of out-of-equilibrium quark production in non-Abelian gauge theory in 3+1-dimensions. Our simulations include the backreaction of quarks onto the dynamical gluon sector, which is particularly relevant for strongly correlated quarks. We observe fast isotropization and universal behavior of quarks and gluons at weak coupling and establish a quantitative connection to previous pure glue results. In order to understand the strongly correlated regime, we perform simulations for a large number of flavors and compare them to those obtained with two light quark flavors. By doing this we are able to provide estimates of the chemical equilibration time.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first fully 3+1-dimensional real-time lattice simulations of out-of-equilibrium quark production in non-Abelian gauge theory that include the backreaction of quarks onto the dynamical gluon sector, but they restrict themselves to SU(2) gauge group with non-expanding systems and weak gauge coupling, such that the classical-statistical approximation is valid only for sufficiently small values of the gauge coupling \(\alpha_s \equiv g^2/(4\pi)\).

## Method

The authors employ classical-statistical lattice gauge theory with SU(2) color and include dynamical fermions via the male/female low-cost fermion method, which replaces mode function expansions with stochastic ensembles of spinor fields to reduce computational cost from \(N^{2d}\) to \(N_{\text{sto}} N^d\) in \(d\) dimensions. They solve the coupled equations of motion for the gauge links, chromoelectric fields, and fermion fields on a \(64^3\) spatial lattice, using temporal-axial gauge and enforcing the Gauss constraint. Three types of initial conditions are studied: overpopulated gluons (isotropic occupancy \(\sim 1/g^2\) up to scale \(Q_s\)), Nielsen-Olesen-type magnetic condensate (chromomagnetic field along the 3-direction), and flux tube (both chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields aligned longitudinally).

## Key numbers

- Gauge coupling set to \(g^2 = 10^{-2}\) for weak-coupling simulations with \(N_f = 2\) light quark flavors.
- Chromoelectric field decays after about \(t \simeq 30/\sqrt{gB}\) and chromomagnetic field decays after about \(t \simeq 50/\sqrt{gB}\) for anisotropic initial conditions.
- Simulations performed on a \(64^3\) spatial lattice.
- Initial gluon occupancy for overpopulation scenario is \(f_g(|\mathbf{p}|,t_0) = (1/g^2) \Theta(|\mathbf{p}| - Q_s)\), giving total quasi-particle energy \(\varepsilon \sim Q_s^4/g^2\) and total quasi-particle number \(n_g^{(0)} \sim Q_s^3/g^2\).
- Final thermal temperature estimated as \(T \sim Q_s/\sqrt{g}\), with thermal gluon number \(n_g^{\text{th}} \sim Q_s^3/g^{3/2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous pure-glue real-time lattice simulations by Berges (2014) that identified the thermalization scenario at weak coupling, and earlier 2+1-dimensional boost-invariant estimates by Gelis (2005) or simulations neglecting backreaction by Tanji (2015). By including dynamical quarks with backreaction in 3+1 dimensions for the first time, the results enable quantitative connection to kinetic descriptions such as those by Kurkela (2015) and open the possibility to simulate strong-interaction aspects by taking \(g^2 N_f\) of order one, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
