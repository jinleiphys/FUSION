# Superfluid Heat Conduction and the Cooling of Magnetized Neutron Stars

> We report on a new mechanism for heat conduction in the neutron star crust. We find that collective modes of superfluid neutron matter, called superfluid phonons (sPhs), can influence heat conduction in magnetized neutron stars. They can dominate the heat conduction transverse to magnetic field when the magnetic field $B \gsim 10^{13}$ G. At density $ρ\simeq 10^{12}-10^{14} $ g/cm$^3$ the conductivity due to sPhs is significantly larger than that due to lattice phonons and is comparable to electron conductivity when temperature $\simeq 10^8$ K. This new mode of heat conduction can limit the surface anisotropy in highly magnetized neutron stars. Cooling curves of magnetized neutron stars with and without superfluid heat conduction could show observationally discernible differences.

## Key claim

Superfluid phonons (sPhs), collective modes of superfluid neutron matter in the inner crust, can dominate heat conduction transverse to the magnetic field when the field is greater than about 10^13 G, and can be comparable to electron conductivity at temperatures around 10^8 K, thereby limiting the surface temperature anisotropy in highly magnetized neutron stars.

## Method

The authors computed the thermal conductivity of superfluid phonons using kinetic theory, where the conductivity depends on the specific heat of the phonon gas, the phonon velocity, and the phonon mean free path. They estimated the mean free path by considering three scattering processes: Rayleigh scattering off impurities, absorption due to mixing with longitudinal lattice phonons (which are strongly damped by electrons), and decay into two lattice phonons. They found that absorption via mixing with lattice phonons is the dominant damping mechanism and used this to calculate the sPh conductivity across the inner crust density range (10^12 to 10^14 g/cm^3). They then incorporated this new conductivity into magnetar cooling simulations to assess its effect on thermal evolution.

## Key numbers

- sPh thermal conductivity at fiducial values: κ_sPh = 1.5 × 10^22 erg cm^-1 s^-1 K^-1 × (T/10^8 K)^3 × (0.1/v_s)^2 × (λ_sPh/cm), where v_s is the sPh velocity and λ_sPh is the mean free path.
- Electron thermal conductivity in the inner crust at T = 10^8 K is approximately 10^18 erg cm^-1 s^-1 K^-1.
- Rayleigh scattering mean free path for sPhs: λ_Ray = 450 cm × (v_s/0.1)^4 × (x/10)^3 × (10 fm/r_0)^3 × T_7^(-4), where x = d/r_0 is the diluteness parameter and T_7 is temperature in 10^7 K.
- sPh decay mean free path: λ_decay ≈ (863/f(α)) × (10^(-6)/g_mix^2) × (c_s/0.01)^7 × (v_s/0.1) × (Λ_0)^4 × T_7^(-5) cm, where Λ_0 = Λ/50 MeV and f(α) is a function of the velocity ratio α = c_s/v_s.
- Mixing parameter g_mix ≈ 10^(-3) and Λ ≈ 50 MeV for typical crust conditions.
- Cooling simulations used a poloidal crustal magnetic field of strength B = 5 × 10^13 G at the magnetic pole.
- sPhs are relevant when T ≈ 10^8 K and B ≥ 10^14 G in the direction transverse to the magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heat conduction in neutron star crusts, which typically considered only relativistic electrons and lattice phonons (lPhs). Previous work by Pons (2005) and Chugunov (2007) showed that lattice phonon conduction is unimportant at temperatures above 10^6 K and in low magnetic fields. The authors also build on calculations of electron conductivity in strong magnetic fields by Potekhin (1999) and on earlier magnetar cooling simulations by Aguilera (2007). This new superfluid phonon mechanism can reduce the temperature anisotropy in the inner crust of magnetized neutron stars, which is relevant for interpreting multiwavelength observations of isolated neutron stars and accreting systems. Future work could investigate anisotropy in phonon conductivity due to Landau quantization of electrons and scattering by superfluid vortices.
