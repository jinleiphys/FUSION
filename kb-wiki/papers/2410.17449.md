# Production of double strange hypernuclei and exotic nuclei in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=3 GeV

> We extend the theoretical approach which includes the dynamical and statistical stages for the description of the nucleosynthesis in central collisions of relativistic ions. Previously, this approach was successfully applied to describe experimental data on both normal nuclei and single strange hypernuclei production in the GSI and RHIC-BES energy range. We predict the multiplicities of double strange hypernuclei up to $^4_{ΛΛ}$H and further intermediate mass nuclei up to $^8$Be for Au+Au central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=3 GeV, recently explored by the STAR experiments. These new nuclei can be identified by the measurement of the correlated particles coming after their decay. Such observations are a crucial test for the nucleosynthesis mechanism.

## Key claim

The authors predict that double strange hypernuclei (up to \(^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)H) and intermediate-mass exotic nuclei (up to \(^8\)Be) can be produced in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)=3 GeV, but the yields depend strongly on the initial baryon phase-space distributions, with the UrQMD-based approach giving much lower yields for the heaviest species than the phase-space generation (PSG) method.

## Method

The authors combine the UrQMD transport model (for the dynamical stage) or a phase-space generation (PSG) method (for a simplified isotropic baryon distribution) with a clusterization of baryons (CB) procedure that groups baryons by coordinate and velocity proximity at a freeze-out time of 20–40 fm/c. The resulting excited baryonic clusters, at subnuclear density around \(\rho_c \approx \frac{1}{6}\rho_0\), are then decayed statistically using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) extended to hypernuclei, producing final cold nuclei and hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Average of 3.2 produced \(\Lambda\) hyperons per event in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)=3 GeV (from UrQMD).
- Average center-of-mass kinetic energy per baryon is 366 MeV, leading to \(E_0 = 366 A_0\) MeV for PSG calculations with \(A_0 = 394\) (including 3 \(\Lambda\)s).
- The velocity parameter \(v_c\) used for clusterization is 0.22\(c\), consistent with the range 0.1\(c\)–0.25\(c\) established previously.
- Excited clusters have baryon density \(\rho_c \approx \frac{1}{6}\rho_0\) (with \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)) and excitation energies around 10 MeV per nucleon.
- Predicted yield of \(^8\)Be is around \(10^{-4}\)–\(10^{-5}\) per central event using PSG nucleon distributions; with UrQMD the yield is \(\lesssim 10^{-6}\).
- Predicted yield of \(\Lambda\Lambda\) (H-dibaryon) is around \(10^{-2}\) per event assuming zero binding.
- Predicted yield of \(^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)H is at the level of \(10^{-5}\)–\(10^{-4}\) per event for all calculation scenarios.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid approach (Buyukcizmeci et al., 2023; Botvina et al., 2021, 2022), which successfully described normal nuclei and single strange hypernuclei production in GSI and RHIC-BES energy range, to predict double strange hypernuclei and exotic nuclei for the STAR experiment at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\)=3 GeV. The approach contrasts with simpler coalescence models or global equilibrium hypotheses, and the predicted particle correlations from decays (e.g., \(^8\)Be \(\rightarrow\) 2 \(^4\)He, \(^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)H \(\rightarrow \pi^- + ^4_{\Lambda}\)He) provide a test for the local chemical equilibrium nucleation mechanism, with implications for hypermatter properties relevant to astrophysics (supernovae, neutron star mergers).
