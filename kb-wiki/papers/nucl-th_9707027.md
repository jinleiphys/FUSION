# Total and Parity-Projected Level Densities of Iron-Region Nuclei in the Auxiliary Fields Monte Carlo Shell Model

> We use the auxiliary-fields Monte Carlo method for the shell model in the complete $(pf+0g_{9/2})$-shell to calculate level densities. We introduce parity projection techniques which enable us to calculate the parity dependence of the level density. Results are presented for $^{56}$Fe, where the calculated total level density is found to be in remarkable agreement with the experimental level density. The parity-projected densities are well described by a backshifted Bethe formula, but with significant dependence of the single-particle level-density and backshift parameters on parity. We compare our exact results with those of the thermal Hartree-Fock approximation.

## Key claim

The auxiliary-fields Monte Carlo shell model (SMMC) calculation of the total level density for $^{56}$Fe agrees remarkably well with the experimental level density, but this agreement depends on using a sign-problem-free effective interaction that reproduces collective features rather than a fully realistic Hamiltonian. The parity-projected densities are well described by a backshifted Bethe formula, but with significant dependence of the single-particle level-density and backshift parameters on parity.

## Method

The authors used the auxiliary-fields Monte Carlo method for the shell model in the complete $(pf+0g_{9/2})$-shell to calculate thermal averages and level densities for $^{56}$Fe. They introduced a parity-projection technique using the Hubbard-Stratonovich representation and projection operators $P_\pm = (1 \pm P)/2$ to calculate parity-projected energies $E_\pm(\beta)$ and densities $\rho_\pm(E)$. The level density was obtained from the partition function via the saddle-point approximation, using the relation $\rho(E) = (2\pi \beta^{-2} C)^{-1/2} e^{S}$ where $S = \beta E + \ln Z(\beta)$.

## Key numbers

- Total level density fit parameters for $^{56}$Fe (energy range $4.5~{\rm MeV}<E_x<20~{\rm MeV}$): $a = 5.780\pm 0.055~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$ and $\Delta = 1.560\pm 0.161~{\rm MeV}$, compared to experimental BBF parameters $a=5.80~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$ and $\Delta=1.38~{\rm MeV}$.
- Positive-parity level density fit parameters: $a_+ = 5.611\pm 0.073 ~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$, $\Delta_+ = 0.550\pm 0.196 ~{\rm MeV}$.
- Negative-parity level density fit parameters: $a_- = 6.209\pm 0.625 ~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$, $\Delta_- = 3.172\pm 1.637 ~{\rm MeV}$.
- The average energy of the quadrupole excitation $\overline E(Q^{(2,0)})$ was calculated as $2.12\pm 0.11$~MeV, in agreement with the experimental value of approximately $2.16$~MeV.
- The pairing strength $g_0=0.212$~MeV was adopted as a constant mean value for nuclei in the mass region $A=40$--$80$.
- The interaction strength $\chi=0.026$~MeV$^{-1}$fm$^2$ was used for $^{56}$Fe.
- The calculation is meaningful up to $E_x\sim 20$~MeV, and positive- and negative-parity densities become approximately equal above $E_x\simeq 17$~MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method to include parity projection, building on earlier SMMC calculations of thermal properties for $^{54}$Fe in a full $pf$-shell by Dean et al. (1995), which had statistical errors too large for accurate level densities. The results are compared with the thermal Hartree-Fock approximation, which shows a shape phase transition around $\beta \sim 1.3$ MeV$^{-1}$ that is washed out in the SMMC due to two-body correlations. The parity-dependent level density parameters obtained here enable improved estimates of neutron-capture reaction rates for nucleosynthesis, particularly for $s$-process nuclei where the abundance is inversely proportional to the neutron-capture cross-section.
