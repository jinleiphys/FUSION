# Rotating Neutron Stars in a Chiral SU(3) Model

> We study the properties of rotating neutron stars within a generalized chiral SU(3)-flavor model. The influence of the rotation on the inner structure and the hyperon matter content of the star is discussed. We calculate the Kepler frequency and moments of inertia of the neutron star sequences. An estimate for the braking index of the associated pulsars is given.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) model, which successfully describes non-rotating neutron stars, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei with the same parameter set, predicts that rotation increases the maximum gravitational mass of a neutron star from 1.64 solar masses (static) to 1.94 solar masses, but the model does not produce a back-bending of the moment of inertia because it lacks a phase transition at high densities and low temperatures.

## Method

The authors extended their static chiral SU(3)-flavor model, which includes the full baryon octet (p, n, Σ−/0/+, Ξ−/0) and scalar (σ, ζ, χ) and vector (ω, ρ, φ) mesons, to rotating stars using the Hartle-Thorne formalism. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for the static case and then integrated the differential equations for the metric perturbations up to quadrupole order to obtain the rotating star's mass, radius, moment of inertia, and Kepler frequency. The equation of state was computed at zero temperature under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality, with a standard crust parametrization for the low-density tail.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of the rotating neutron star: 1.94 solar masses, increased from 1.64 solar masses for the static solution.
- Onset of hyperon population in neutral hadronic matter occurs at a density of approximately 2 times nuclear matter saturation density (2ρ₀); for the neutron star (including leptons), Λ and Σ states are populated at densities higher than 2.6 ρ₀.
- Minimum rotational period (Kepler period) is increased by about 25% compared to the Newtonian estimate due to general relativity corrections.
- Mass increase of up to 30% at the mass-shedding frequency (Kepler frequency) for the chiral model.
- Radius increase of up to 10% close to the Kepler frequency.
- At maximum rotation, the star starts with a central density of 2.3 times nuclear matter density, which is below the threshold for hyperon occurrence; hypermatter becomes energetically favorable below a frequency of approximately 5600/s (period of about 1.1 ms).
- Moment of inertia calculated for a fixed baryonic number of 1.22 × 10⁵⁷ (approximately one solar baryonic mass) as a function of rotational frequency.
- Braking index is reduced by a factor of up to 1/3 for fast rotating stars in the case of magnetic dipole radiation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous static neutron star calculations (Hanauske et al., 2002) using the same chiral SU(3) model that had been applied to nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei (Zschiesche et al., 2002; Beckmann et al., 2002). The results are compared with the TM1 relativistic mean-field model (Sugahara and Toki, 1994), also extended to include hyperons, showing that the Kepler frequency and general star properties are relatively insensitive to the different models despite their distinct hyperon content. The study enables more accurate analyses of pulsar spin-down and age estimates by quantifying the frequency-dependent deviation of the braking index from a simple power law, and it sets the stage for future work on the effects of higher baryonic resonances on rotating neutron star structure.
