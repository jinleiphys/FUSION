# Recent progress on few-body hypernuclei

> Few-body $Λ$ hypernuclei provide valuable information towards understanding strange matter. Recent experimental progress by the STAR Collaboration at the RHIC facility and by the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC has been matched by theoretical progress reviewed here: (i) lifetimes of the hypertriton $_Λ^3$H, $_Λ^3$n if particle-stable, $_Λ^4$H and $_Λ^4$He and their charge symmetry breaking, and (ii) the onset of $ΛΛ$ hypernuclear binding.

# Recent progress on few-body hypernuclei

## Key claim
The hypertriton lifetime puzzle between STAR and ALICE appears resolved by new STAR measurements, but the theoretical understanding remains conditional on the precise value of the hypertriton binding energy, with a strong correlation between binding energy and lifetime found in pion-EFT calculations that favors the ALICE preliminary binding energy over the STAR published value.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical calculations of weak decay rates for few-body Λ hypernuclei (A=3,4) using closure approximation, Faddeev methods, and pion-EFT (πEFT) and pionless-EFT (π̸EFT) approaches at leading order, with wavefunctions constrained by emulsion binding energies. For ΛΛ hypernuclei, the onset of binding is studied using LO π̸EFT calculations with ΛΛ scattering lengths varied over a broad range and ΛNN low-energy constants fitted to single-Λ hypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- Hypertriton lifetime τ(³_ΛH) = 221±15±19 ps from new STAR measurements in Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 3 and 7.2 GeV (STAR 2021)
- Hypertriton lifetime τ(³_ΛH) = 242⁺³⁴₋₃₈±17 ps from ALICE in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (ALICE 2019)
- Hypertriton binding energy B_Λ(³_ΛH) = 0.13±0.05 MeV from emulsion studies
- ALICE preliminary binding energy B_Λ(³_ΛH) = 0.05±0.06±0.10 MeV; STAR published value 0.41±0.12±0.11 MeV
- Theoretical estimate for ⁴_ΛH lifetime: τ_th(⁴_ΛH) = 195±10 ps; for ⁴_ΛHe: τ_th(⁴_ΛHe) = 263±13 ps
- New STAR lifetime for ⁴_ΛH: τ(⁴_ΛH) = 218±6±13 ps
- OPE charge symmetry breaking contributions: ΔB_Λ^{J=0} ≈ 175±40 keV, ΔB_Λ^{J=1} ≈ -50±10 keV for the ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe mirror pair
- ΔB_ΛΛ(⁶_ΛΛHe) = 0.67±0.17 MeV from the Nagara emulsion event
- Calculated B_Λ(⁵_ΛΛH) ∼ 1 MeV, robustly indicating particle stability

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework of Gal, Hungerford, and Millener (2016) on hypernuclear physics and addresses the long-standing hypertriton lifetime puzzle that began with conflicting emulsion and bubble-chamber measurements in the 1960s (Block 1962, Keyes 1973). The work enables future searches for the lightest double-Λ hypernucleus ⁵_ΛΛH at J-PARC Experiment E75 (Fujioka 2021), and provides theoretical benchmarks for interpreting charge symmetry breaking in the A=4 mirror hypernuclei, building on the Dalitz and von Hippel (1964) one-pion exchange mechanism.
