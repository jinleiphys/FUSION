# Mean field approaches for $Ξ^-$ hypernuclei and current experimental data

> Motivated by the recently observed hypernucleus (Kiso event) $^{15}_Ξ$C ($^{14}$N$+Ξ^-$), we identify the state of this system theoretically within the framework of the relativistic-mean-field and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models. The $ΞN$ interactions are constructed to reproduce the two possibly observed $Ξ^-$ removal energies, $4.38\pm 0.25$ MeV or $1.11\pm 0.25$ MeV. The present result is preferable to be $^{14}{\rm N}({\rm g.s.})+Ξ^-(1p)$, corresponding to the latter value.

## Key claim

The authors find that the Kiso event observation of $^{15}_{\Xi}$C is theoretically preferable as an excited state $^{14}$N(g.s.)$+\Xi^-(1p)$ with a $\Xi^-$ removal energy of $1.11\pm0.25$ MeV, rather than the ground state $^{14}$N(g.s.)$+\Xi^-(1s)$ with $4.38\pm0.25$ MeV, because only the excited-state interpretation allows simultaneous reproduction of the binding energies for both $^{15}_{\Xi}$C and $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic mean field (RMF) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models to calculate $\Xi^-$ removal energies for $^{15}_{\Xi}$C and $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be. For RMF, the scalar coupling constant $g_{\sigma\Xi}$ was adjusted to reproduce either the $s$-state or $p$-state binding energy of $^{15}_{\Xi}$C, while vector coupling $g_{\omega\Xi}=g_\omega/3$ was fixed from the naive quark model. For SHF, three parameter sets (SL0, SL2, SL3) with different combinations of volume, surface, and density-dependent terms were constructed, guided by Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of the $\Xi^-$ single-particle potential in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The two possible $\Xi^-$ removal energies for $^{15}_{\Xi}$C from the Kiso event are $4.38\pm0.25$ MeV (ground state interpretation) and $1.11\pm0.25$ MeV (excited state interpretation)
- For the $p$-state interpretation (preferred), RMF calculations give $\Xi^-$ removal energies for $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be of 6.1-6.2 MeV, consistent with the empirical value of about 5 MeV
- For the $s$-state interpretation, RMF calculations give $\Xi^-$ removal energies for $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be of only 1.7-2.7 MeV, which are less bound than the empirical value
- The spin-orbit splitting of the $1p$ state in $^{15}_{\Xi}$C is very small at 0.06 MeV, with the $1p_{3/2}$ orbit being lower
- The Woods-Saxon potential depth used in prior analysis of $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be was about 14 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of strangeness $S=-2$ hypernuclei following the first clear observation of a bound $\Xi^-$ hypernucleus (the Kiso event) from the KEK-E373 experiment reported by Nakazawa (2015). Prior cluster model calculations by Hiyama (2009) predicted $\Xi^-$ binding energies for $^{12}_{\Xi}$Be of about 5 MeV with Coulomb interaction, while AMD approaches gave 3-5.5 MeV. The present mean-field calculations support the excited-state interpretation of the Kiso event and enable future predictions for the ground state binding energy of $^{15}_{\Xi}$C (predicted to be 7.2-9.4 MeV depending on the model) and for heavier $\Xi^-$ hypernuclei.
