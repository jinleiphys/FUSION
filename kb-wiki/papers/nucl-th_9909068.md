# Pion Electroproduction on the Nucleon and the Generalized GDH Sum Rule

> We present predictions for the spin structure functions of the proton and the neutron in the framework of a unitary isobar model for one-pion photo- and electroproduction. Our results are compared with recent experimental data from SLAC. The first moments of the calculated structure functions fulfill the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rules within an error of typically 5-10% for the proton. For the neutron target we find much bigger deviations, in particular the sum rule for I_1(0)+I_2(0) is heavily violated.

## Key claim

The unitary isobar model (UIM) for one-pion photo- and electroproduction predicts that the first moments of the spin structure functions fulfill the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) and Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rules within 5-10% for the proton, but for the neutron target the sum rule for I_1(0)+I_2(0) is heavily violated, suggesting either slow convergence of the unweighted integral or violation of the BC sum rule by higher twist terms at low Q^2.

## Method

The authors used a Unitary Isobar Model (UIM) based on effective Lagrangians for Born terms (background) and resonance contributions, with multipoles constructed in a gauge-invariant and unitary way for each partial wave, up to a total center-of-mass energy W_max = 1.7 GeV and Q^2 ≤ 2 (GeV/c)^2. They included single-pion, eta, and multi-pion channels, with the eta production modeled similarly and multi-pion contributions extracted from total cross section data. The model incorporated a mixing of pseudoscalar and pseudovector πNN coupling with a mixing parameter Λ_m = 450 MeV, and K-matrix unitarization to account for pion rescattering effects.

## Key numbers

- The GDH and BC sum rules are fulfilled within an error of typically 5-10% for the proton target.
- For the integral I_1(Q^2), the zero-crossing occurs at Q^2 = 0.75 (GeV/c)^2 with only one-pion contribution, lowered to 0.52 (GeV/c)^2 with eta inclusion, and to 0.45 (GeV/c)^2 with multi-pion contributions.
- At Q^2 = 0.5 (GeV/c)^2, the SLAC analysis yields I_1 = 0.1 ± 0.06, while the model result is slightly positive.
- For the proton at the photon point (Q^2 = 0), the total I_1 = -0.746 (sum rule: -0.804), I_2 = 1.338 (sum rule: 1.252), and I_3 = 0.592 (sum rule: 0.448), integrated up to W_max = 2 GeV.
- For the neutron at the photon point, the total I_1 = -0.581 (sum rule: -0.912), I_2 = 1.364 (sum rule: 0.912), and I_3 = 0.783 (sum rule: 0), integrated up to W_max = 2 GeV.
- The mixing parameter for PS-PV πNN coupling was found to be Λ_m = 450 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Isobar Model (UIM) previously developed by Drechsel et al. (1998) to calculate generalized GDH integrals and the BC sum rule for both proton and neutron targets, comparing with experimental data from SLAC (Abe et al., 1998). The results indicate that while the model agrees well with sum rules for the proton, the neutron case shows substantial deviations that may require improved treatment of multi-pion channels or may reflect fundamental issues with the BC sum rule at low Q^2 as argued by Ioffe et al. (1984). The upcoming measurements with real photons (Ahrens et al., 1992) and virtual photons (Burkert et al., 1991) are expected to provide new precision data in the resonance region.
