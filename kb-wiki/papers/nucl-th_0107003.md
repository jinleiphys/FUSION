# Renormalization of equations governing nucleon dynamics and nonlocality of the NN interaction

> We discuss the problem of renormalization of dynamical equations which arises in an effective field theory description of nuclear forces. By using a toy model of the separable NN potential leading to logarithmic singularities in the Born series, we show that renormalization gives rise to nucleon dynamics which is governed by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator.

## Key claim

Using a toy model of a separable nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential whose form factor has a specific ultraviolet behavior (alpha = 1/2) leading to logarithmic singularities in the Born series, the authors show that renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation yields a T matrix that does not satisfy the Lippmann-Schwinger equation but instead satisfies a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator.

## Method

The authors construct a separable NN potential model with a form factor psi(p) ~ |p|^{-1/2} at large momenta, which produces logarithmic divergences in the Born series. They solve the generalized dynamical equation (a differential equation for the T matrix derived from a nonlocal-in-time quantum dynamics formalism) and compare the resulting T matrix to that obtained by dimensionally regularizing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the same separable potential, with the coupling strength adjusted to reproduce the deuteron bound-state energy.

## Key numbers

- The form factor UV behavior is psi(p) ~ |p|^{-alpha} with alpha = 1/2, which gives logarithmic singularities in the Born series.
- The leading asymptotic term of the T matrix for large |z| is b_1 ln^{-1}(-z) with b_1 = -(4 pi mu)^{-1}, where mu is the reduced nucleon mass.
- The deuteron bound-state energy used to fix the free parameter is E_B = -2.2246 MeV.
- The model is extended to the case -1/2 < alpha <= 1/2, with ordinary quantum mechanics only permitting alpha > 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics (GQD) formalism developed by Gainutdinov (1999) to the renormalization of effective field theories (EFTs) for nuclear forces, which were previously treated with regulated Schrodinger potentials by Mehen and Stewart (1998) and van Kolck (1994). The paper shows that renormalization of a singular separable potential, previously studied by Phillips, Afnan, and Henry-Edwards (2000) using dimensional regularization, leads to dynamics governed by a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator rather than the Schrodinger equation. This enables a new approach where the generalized interaction operator can be constructed from an EFT consistent with QCD symmetries, after which no further UV divergences appear.
