# Structure effect on one neutron removal reaction using relativistic mean field densities in Glauber model

> We calculate the one neutron removal reaction cross-section $(σ_{-1n})$ for few stable and neutron-rich halo nuclei with $^{12}$C as target, using relativistic mean field (RMF) densities, in the frame work of Glauber model.The results are compared with the experimental data. Study of the stable nuclei with the deformed densities have shown a good agreement with the data,however, it differs significantly for the halo nuclei. We observe that while estimating the $σ_{-1n}$value from the difference of reaction cross-section of two neighboring nuclei with mass number A and that of A-1 in an isotopic chain, we get good agreement with the known experimental data for the halo cases.

## Key claim

The standard Glauber-model calculation of one-neutron removal cross-section (σ₋₁ₙ) using spherical or deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) densities fails for halo nuclei like ¹⁹C, but the alternative method of taking the difference of total reaction cross-sections between neighboring isotopes (σ₋₁ₙ(II) = σᵣ(A) − σᵣ(A−1)) yields good agreement with experimental data specifically for halo cases, while the direct calculation (σ₋₁ₙ(I)) works better for stable and deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors used spherical densities from the RMF (NL3 parameter set) and E-RMF (G2 parameter set) formalisms, and axially deformed densities from the RMF (NL-SH parameter set), as input to the Glauber model to calculate total reaction cross-sections σᵣ and one-neutron removal cross-sections σ₋₁ₙ for ¹²C-target systems. Two methods for σ₋₁ₙ were compared: a direct calculation (σ₋₁ₙ(I)) using the formalism of Abu-Ibrahim et al. (2003), and a difference method (σ₋₁ₙ(II)) obtained by subtracting the total reaction cross-section of the A−1 isotope from that of the A isotope in the same isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁹C+¹²C at 910 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 231 ± 51 mb; spherical RMF (NL3) gives 134 mb; spherical E-RMF (G2) gives 128 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) gives 152 mb; the difference method σ₋₁ₙ(II) with deformed densities gives 263 mb, closest to experiment.
- For ¹⁵C+¹²C at 54 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 137 ± 16 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) σ₋₁ₙ(I) = 130 mb; spherical RMF (NL3) gives 95 mb.
- For ⁹C+¹²C at 285 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 48 ± 8 mb; spherical RMF (NL3) gives 81 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) σ₋₁ₙ(I) = 66 mb.
- For ¹²C+¹²C at 1050 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 44.7 ± 0.3 mb; spherical RMF (NL3) gives 39 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) σ₋₁ₙ(I) = 49 mb.
- For ¹⁴B+¹²C at 50 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 153 ± 15 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) σ₋₁ₙ(I) = 97 mb.
- For ¹⁵B+¹²C at 43 MeV: experimental σ₋₁ₙ = 108 ± 13 mb; deformed RMF (NL-SH) σ₋₁ₙ(I) = 104 mb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Panda and Patra, 2009) on total reaction cross-sections and elastic scattering using RMF densities in the Glauber model, now focusing on one-neutron removal. It tests the method of Abu-Ibrahim et al. (2003) for calculating σ₋₁ₙ from the difference of total reaction cross-sections, which was proposed for halo projectiles. The results show that deformation effects are crucial for σ₋₁ₙ calculations (unlike for σᵣ), and that the direct Glauber calculation fails for halo nuclei like ¹⁹C, while the difference method succeeds specifically for such cases, enabling a practical way to extract halo structure information from reaction cross-section measurements.
