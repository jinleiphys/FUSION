# Do Proton-Proton collisions at the LHC energies produce Droplets of Quark-Gluon Plasma?

> The proton-proton ($pp$) collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), CERN, Switzerland has brought up new challenges and opportunities in understanding the experimental findings in contrast to the conventional lower energy $pp$ collisions. Usually $pp$ collisions are used as the baseline measurement at the GeV and TeV energies in order to understand a possible high density QCD medium formation in heavy-ion collisions. However, the TeV $pp$ collisions have created a new domain of research, where scientists have started observing heavy-ion-like features (signatures) in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions. This warrants a relook into TeV $pp$ collisions, if at all QGP-droplets are produced in such collisions. In this presentation, I discuss some of the new findings and concepts emerging out in $pp$ collisions at the LHC energies along with some of the new emergent phenomena in particle production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies may produce droplets of quark-gluon plasma (QGP), but this remains an open question requiring further theoretical and experimental exploration. The hedge is that while heavy-ion-like signatures (strangeness enhancement, long-range correlations, collectivity) are observed, definitive conclusions about QGP-droplet formation in small systems are not yet possible.

## Method

The author reviews experimental observations from the ALICE and CMS collaborations at the LHC, focusing on event topology studies using transverse spherocity and charged-particle multiplicity as discriminators. The analysis compares high-multiplicity pp collisions with p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions, and discusses theoretical models including DIPSY (rope hadronization), PYTHIA8 (color reconnection), and multipartonic interactions (MPI) to interpret the observed heavy-ion-like features.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: T_kin = 163 ± 10 MeV, comparable to the critical temperature T_c for deconfinement (T_c ~ 150-170 MeV)
- Collective radial flow velocity from Blast-wave model fitting: <beta> = 0.49 ± 0.02 in high-multiplicity pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
- Critical energy density for deconfinement from lattice QCD: epsilon_c ~ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Threshold for MPI dominance: N_ch ~ 20 charged particles
- Transverse momentum threshold indicating a new domain of particle production: p_T ~ 8 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the discussion of QGP signatures from heavy-ion collisions (first observed at RHIC, as noted by STAR (2005)) to the small-system regime of LHC pp collisions. It builds on observations by ALICE (2017) of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity pp collisions and by CMS (2010) of near-side long-range ridge-like correlations. The paper enables future differential studies using event-shape observables like transverse spherocity to disentangle soft and hard QCD processes, and calls for connecting particle production from hadronic to nuclear collisions via transport models.
