# Gauge invariance in the presence of a cutoff

> We use the method of gauging equations to construct the electromagnetic current operator for the two-nucleon system in a theory with a finite cutoff. The employed formulation ensures that the two-nucleon T-matrix and corresponding five-point function, in the cutoff theory, are identical to the ones formally defined by a reference theory without a cutoff. A feature of our approach is that it effectively introduces a cutoff into the reference theory in a way that maintains the long-range part of the exchange current operator; for applications to effective field theory (EFT), this property is usually sufficient to guarantee the predictive power of the resulting cutoff theory. In addition, our approach leads to Ward-Takahashi (WT) identities that are linear in the interactions. From the point of view of EFT's where such a WT identity is satisfied in the reference theory, this ensures that gauge invariance in the cutoff theory is maintained order by order in the expansion.

## Key claim

The authors present a method for constructing the electromagnetic current operator for the two-nucleon system in a theory with a finite cutoff that ensures the two-nucleon T-matrix and five-point function in the cutoff theory are identical to those formally defined by a reference theory without a cutoff, provided the completeness relation \((G_0\Theta)^\mu + (G_0\bar \Theta)^\mu = G_0^\mu\) is satisfied and the free two-body current \((G_0\Theta)^\mu\) obeys the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Method

The authors use the method of gauging equations to construct the electromagnetic current operator, defining the T-matrix and potential in the cutoff theory via equations that incorporate projector operators \(\Theta\) and \(\bar\Theta\) onto low and high relative momentum spaces, respectively, with the cutoff treated as part of the two-nucleon propagator. The free two-body current in the cutoff theory \((G_0\Theta)^\mu\) is constructed explicitly in momentum space using the single-nucleon electromagnetic vertex \(\Gamma_i^\mu\) and step functions \(\theta(\Lambda-p)\), with a regularizing term proportional to \((\theta(\Lambda-p')-\theta(\Lambda-p))/(p'^2-p^2)\) to maintain regularity and the linear form of the Ward-Takahashi identity. The interaction current operator \(V^\mu\) in the cutoff theory is then defined through an equation involving the reference interaction current \(V_{\rm ref}^\mu\) and the gauged high-momentum propagator \((G_0\bar\Theta)^\mu\), ensuring that \(T^\mu\) equals \(T_{\rm ref}^\mu\) exactly and that the long-range parts of \(V^\mu\) and \(V_{\rm ref}^\mu\) are identical.

## Key numbers

* Cutoff momentum \(\Lambda\) is introduced via the projector \(\Theta\) with matrix element \(\langle \p'|\Theta|\p\rangle = \delta(\p'-\p)\theta(\Lambda-|{\bf p}|)\).
* The free two-nucleon propagator in the center-of-mass frame is \(\langle \p'|G_0(E)|\p\rangle = \delta(\p'-\p) \, M/(ME-\p^2+i\eta)\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass.
* The single-nucleon current vertex is \(\Gamma^\mu_i(\p',\p)=ie_i\left(1,\, (\p'+\p)/(2M)\right)\).

## Context

This work extends previous approaches to maintaining gauge invariance in cutoff-regularized nuclear interactions, particularly building on the method of gauging equations from Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (earlier work) and the three-dimensional gauge-invariant reduction formalisms of light-front dynamics and spectator reduction. The formalism enables the consistent calculation of electromagnetic reactions in chiral effective field theory with a finite cutoff, including bremsstrahlung, breakup reactions, and bound-state form factors, while preserving exact gauge invariance order by order in the EFT expansion. The authors note that the approach is not restricted to EFT applications and can be applied to other cutoff-regularized theories such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.
