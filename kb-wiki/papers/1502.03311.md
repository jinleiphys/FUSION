# Determination of the structure of the $X(3872)$ in $\bar p A$ collisions

> Currently, the structure of the $X(3872)$ meson is unknown. Different competing models of the $c\bar c$ exotic state $X(3872)$ exist, including the possibilities that this state is either a mesonic molecule with dominating $D^0 \bar D^{*0} +c.c.$ composition, a $c \bar c q \bar q$ tetraquark, or a $c \bar c$-gluon hybrid state. It is expected that the $X(3872)$ state is rather strongly coupled to the $\bar p p$ channel and, therefore, can be produced in $\bar p p$ and $\bar pA$ collisions at PANDA. We propose to test the hypothetical molecular structure of $X(3872)$ by studying the $D$ or $\bar D^{*}$ stripping reactions on a nuclear residue.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the molecular structure of the X(3872) can be tested by measuring the light cone momentum fraction distribution of D or D* mesons produced in stripping reactions on nuclear targets in antiproton-nucleus collisions, where a sharp peak at alpha approximately 1 at small transverse momenta would unambiguously identify the weakly coupled D D* + c.c. molecular composition.

## Method

The authors apply Glauber theory and the generalized eikonal approximation to calculate the total proton-X(3872) cross section and the momentum differential cross section for D* (or D) production in X(3872)-proton collisions, assuming the X(3872) is a D D* molecule. They then extend this to antiproton-nucleus collisions using a multiple scattering formalism that includes nuclear absorption via survival probabilities, with the X(3872) formed on a proton via the antiproton-proton channel and subsequently producing a D* or D meson through interaction with a second nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The total pX cross section is 26 mb for the D0 D*0 component and 23 mb for the D+ D*- component at a molecule momentum of 7 GeV/c in the proton rest frame.
- The binding energy of D0 D*0 is set to 0.5 MeV, corresponding to a range parameter kappa = 0.16 fm^-1; the binding energy of D+ D*- is set to 8 MeV, corresponding to kappa = 0.64 fm^-1.
- The assumed total Dp cross section is sigma_pD_tot = 14 mb, with slope parameter B_pD = 4 GeV^-2.
- The partial decay width Gamma_X(3872) to p pbar is taken as approximately 30 eV.
- The D*0 and D0 cross sections peak at alpha approximately 1.04 and 0.96 respectively, with widths much smaller and peak values much larger than for D*± and D± production.

## Context

This work extends the concept of spectator kinematics stripping reactions, previously applied to study the deuteron structure at short distances in hadron-, lepton-, and nucleus-deuteron interactions (Frankfurt 1976, Frankfurt 1979), to the exotic charmonium state X(3872). The proposed method enables a test of the molecular hypothesis for X(3872) at the PANDA facility, complementing existing studies of radiative and isospin-violating decays that have yielded conflicting conclusions about the molecular versus charmonium composition (Aceti 2012, Swanson 2004).
