# Four-nucleon force in chiral effective field theory

> We derive the leading contribution to the four-nucleon force within the framework of chiral effective field theory. It is governed by the exchange of pions and the lowest-order nucleon-nucleon contact interaction and includes effects due to the nonlinear pion-nucleon couplings and the pion self interactions constrained by the chiral symmetry of QCD. The resulting four-nucleon force does not contain any unknown parameters and can be tested in few- and many-nucleon studies.

## Key claim

The leading four-nucleon force (4NF) in chiral effective field theory appears at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) and is parameter-free, but its actual numerical contribution to few-nucleon binding energies is estimated to be small (on the order of 80 keV for the alpha particle), leaving room for verification via explicit calculations.

## Method

The author derived the leading 4NF using the method of unitary transformation within chiral effective field theory, starting from the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons at lowest order (including the pion decay constant, axial-vector coupling, and leading NN contact interactions). Connected tree diagrams at order ν=4 were evaluated, while disconnected tree diagrams, disconnected loop graphs, and relativistic 1/m-corrections were shown to yield vanishing contributions. The resulting 4NF is local and depends only on the low-energy constant C_T from the NN contact interaction, with no unknown parameters.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: F_π = 92.4 MeV
- Nucleon axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.267
- Estimated strength of the 3π-exchange 4NF contribution to the α-particle binding energy: ~80 keV, derived from the r-space potential scaling (1/(4π)³) × (2 g_A⁴/(2 F_π)⁶) × M_π⁷
- The leading 4NF appears at order ν=4 in the chiral expansion (N³LO), while the leading 2NF starts at ν=0 and the first non-vanishing 3NF appears at ν=3
- Binding energies of light nuclei can be obtained with numerical accuracy reaching 0.2% for the α-particle and 1-2% for heavier nuclei

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT framework for nuclear forces, where two-nucleon forces (2NFs) have been worked out up to N³LO (Entem 2003, Epelbaum 2004) and three-nucleon forces (3NFs) up to N²LO (Epelbaum 2002). The derivation enables future explicit calculations of 4NF contributions in few- and many-nucleon systems, providing a test of the understanding of nuclear forces beyond the 2NF and 3NF levels. The paper notes that existing studies (Nogga 2001) leave little room for 4NF contributions, but this needs verification via explicit calculations.
