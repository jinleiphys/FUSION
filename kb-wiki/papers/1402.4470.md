# Solutions of the Dirac equation with the shifted Deng-Fan potential including Yukawa-like tensor interaction

> By using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, we give the approximate analytical solutions of the Dirac equation with the shifted Deng-Fan potential including the Yukawa-like tensor interaction under the spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions. After using an improved approximation scheme, we solved the resulting schrödinger-like equation analytically. Numerical results of the energy eigenvalues are also obtained, as expected, the tensor interaction removes degeneracies between spin and pseudospin doublets.

# Solutions of the Dirac equation with the shifted Deng-Fan potential including Yukawa-like tensor interaction

## Key claim
The paper claims that approximate analytical solutions of the Dirac equation with the shifted Deng-Fan potential and a Yukawa-like tensor interaction can be obtained under spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, but only after applying an improved approximation scheme (Pekeris-type) to handle the centrifugal term, and the results are valid primarily for short-range potentials where the parameter *a* is small.

## Method
The authors used the Nikiforov-Uvarov (NU) method to solve the Dirac equation for a spin-half particle with scalar and vector potentials given by the shifted Deng-Fan potential, plus a Yukawa-like tensor interaction of the form *U(r) = -(A/r)e^{-ar}*. A Pekeris-type approximation scheme was applied to the centrifugal (and pseudo-centrifugal) term, replacing *1/r²* with *a²/(1 - e^{-ar})²*, which is valid for short-range potentials. The resulting Schrödinger-like equations under both spin symmetry (Δ(r) = constant) and pseudospin symmetry (Σ(r) = constant) were transformed using the variable *z = e^{-ar}* and solved analytically, yielding energy eigenvalue equations and wave functions expressed in terms of Jacobi polynomials.

## Key numbers
- The Yukawa tensor interaction parameter *A* is defined as *A = γZ*, where *γ = (137.037)⁻¹* is the fine-structure constant and *Z* is the atomic number.
- For the spin symmetry case with *r_e = 0.4* and *A = 0* (no tensor interaction), degeneracy is observed between spin doublet states such as *p₃/₂* and *p₁/₂*, *d₅/₂* and *d₃/₂*, *f₇/₂* and *f₅/₂*, *g₉/₂* and *g₇/₂*.
- For the spin symmetry case with *A = 0.5*, the tensor interaction removes the degeneracy between these states.
- For the pseudospin symmetry case with *C_ps = 0*, *r_e = 0.8 fm⁻¹*, and *A = 0*, degeneracy is observed between states such as *s₁/₂* and *d₃/₂*, *p₃/₂* and *f₅/₂*, *d₅/₂* and *g₇/₂*, *f₇/₂* and *h₉/₂*.
- Decreasing the equilibrium inter-nuclear distance *r_e* by half produces a very small increment in the energy eigenvalues.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the Dirac equation with the Deng-Fan potential by Maghsoodi et al. (2012), who used supersymmetric quantum mechanics with a Coulomb-like tensor interaction, and by Hamzavi et al. (2013), who introduced the shifted Deng-Fan potential for the Schrödinger equation. The novel contribution is replacing the usual Coulomb-like tensor with a Yukawa-like tensor interaction, following recent proposals by Hassanabadi et al. (2012) and Aydogdu et al. (2013). The results enable calculation of bound-state energies and wave functions for nuclear systems where the shifted Deng-Fan potential models the mean field and the Yukawa tensor accounts for short-range tensor coupling, with the degeneracy removal between spin and pseudospin doublets providing a tool for studying symmetry breaking in nuclear spectroscopy.
