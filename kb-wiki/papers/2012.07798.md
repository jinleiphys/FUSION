# Chemical potential on the lattice: Universal or Unique?

> Lattice techniques are the most reliable ones to investigate non-perturbative aspects of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) such as its phase diagram in the temperature-baryon density plane. They are, however, well-known to be beset with a variety of problems as one increases the density. We address here the old question of placing the baryonic (quark) chemical potential on the lattice. We point out that it may have important consequences for the current and future experimental searches of QCD critical point.

# Chemical potential on the lattice: Universal or Unique?

## Key claim
The way baryonic (quark) chemical potential is introduced in lattice QCD actions leads to different numerical results for higher-order fluctuations (skewness, kurtosis, and beyond) that persist in the continuum limit, violating universality. Only the linear prescription for the chemical potential faithfully reproduces the continuum behavior, despite having mu-dependent divergences that other prescriptions were designed to eliminate.

## Method
The author compares three prescriptions for introducing chemical potential on the lattice, linear (L), exponential (E), and square-root (S) forms, by analyzing their fermion matrix derivatives and the resulting expressions for quark number susceptibilities. The analysis shows that while the first derivative of the chemical potential functions is identical for all prescriptions, all higher derivatives differ, and these differences propagate into the higher-order susceptibilities (chi^4 and above) that serve as Taylor expansion coefficients for the pressure. The author also examines the current conservation equation on the lattice and finds that only the linear prescription yields a conserved charge that remains independent of the chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- For the exponential prescription: f_E'''(0) = 1, f_E''''(0) = 1
- For the square-root prescription: f_S'''(0) = 3, f_S''''(0) = 9
- The critical end-point from Taylor series estimates: T_E/T_c = 0.94(1), mu_B/T_E = 1.68(5) (from DGG study on finer lattices)
- An earlier estimate gave T_E/T_c = 0.94 and mu_B/T_E = 1.8(1) (from GG2 study)
- The STAR experiment at sqrt(s) = 19.6 GeV shows maximal deviation from models without a critical end-point

## Context
This work extends the long-standing discussion of the sign problem in lattice QCD at finite baryon density, which has been addressed through multiple approaches including Taylor expansion (Gavai and Gupta, 2003, 2005), imaginary chemical potential, and improved actions. The author shows that the popular exponential prescription (Hasenfratz and Karsch, 1983; Kogut et al., 1983) and the square-root prescription, which were introduced to eliminate mu-dependent quadratic divergences present in the linear prescription (Bilic and Gavai, 1987), actually produce non-universal results for higher-order fluctuations. This has direct consequences for interpreting experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC, which uses net proton fluctuations as a proxy for baryon number fluctuations to search for the QCD critical point. The work also connects to proposals that sixth-order fluctuations (chi^6_B) could reveal whether the zero-density crossover is a shadow of O(4) criticality in the chiral limit (Karsch and Redlich, 2011).
