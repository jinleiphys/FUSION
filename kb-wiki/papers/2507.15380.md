# Radiative corrections to the parity-violating spin asymmetry

> The parity-violating spin asymmetry Apv for elastic electron scattering from spin-zero nuclei, together with its QED corrections, is evaluated non-perturbatively within the phase-shift analysis. Dispersion corrections, taking into account low-lying transient nuclear excitations, are estimated with the help of the lowest-order $γZ$ box diagrams. Collision energies between 5 $-$ 500 MeV are considered, and results are provided for the $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei. In addition, we have evaluated Apv at GeV energies and small scattering angles -- relevant for the Pb Radius Experiment (PREx) -- revealing that the low-lying nuclear excited states give no measurable dispersive contribution. However, they are important at lower energies and backward angles.

## Key claim
The parity-violating spin asymmetry Apv for elastic electron scattering from spin-zero nuclei is evaluated non-perturbatively within phase-shift analysis, and the authors find that low-lying nuclear excited states give no measurable dispersive contribution at GeV energies and small scattering angles relevant for the Pb Radius Experiment (PREx), but these states are important at lower energies and backward angles.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation using the Fortran code RADIAL to obtain electron scattering wavefunctions, incorporating QED corrections (vacuum polarization via the Uehling potential, and vertex plus self-energy corrections) as additional potentials. Dispersion corrections from low-lying transient nuclear excitations were estimated using lowest-order gamma-Z box diagrams, with transition densities obtained from nuclear Energy Density Functionals (SkM* for carbon, SkP for lead) solved via Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation. The parity-violating spin asymmetry was calculated from the relative cross section difference between positive and negative helicity states.

## Key numbers
- For $^{12}$C at 953 MeV and scattering angle 4.7 degrees: Apv = 5.033 x 10^{-7}, with relative QED correction dApv^{QED} approx -0.51% and dispersive correction dApv^{box} = -0.19%
- Weak charge of $^{12}$C: Qw = -5.55 at tree level, and -5.51 including radiative corrections to Qp^w = 0.0719 +/- 0.0045 and Qn^w = -0.9890 +/- 0.0007
- Weak charge of $^{208}$Pb: Qw = -118.76 (including universal radiative corrections)
- Collision energy range considered: 5 to 500 MeV for main results, plus GeV energies for PREx geometry
- Excited states considered for $^{12}$C: six states with L <= 3 and excitation energies below 30 MeV (including 2+ state at 4.439 MeV, 3- state at 9.64 MeV, 1- states at 19.6 and 27.8 MeV)
- Excited states considered for $^{208}$Pb: ten states with L <= 3 and excitation energies below 30 MeV (including 3- state at 2.615 MeV, 2+ states at 4.085 and 10.9 MeV, 1- states at 5.512, 12.6, 14.3, 14.6, and 15.3 MeV)

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of parity-violating spin asymmetry by treating QED effects non-perturbatively within phase-shift analysis, rather than in Born approximation as done in earlier universal radiative correction estimates by Gorchtein and Horowitz (2009) and Navratil (2024). The dispersion treatment using explicit low-lying nuclear excitations from Energy Density Functionals builds on the approach of Jakubassa-Amundsen (2022) for the beam-normal spin asymmetry, and differs from the closure approximation of Friar and Rosen (1974) or the photoproduction cross-section method of Aleksejevs and collaborators (2004). The results enable interpretation of future precision experiments at the MESA facility on parity-violating asymmetry for low-energy electron scattering as proposed by Aulenbacher and collaborators (2011).
