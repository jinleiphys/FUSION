# Heavy-Flavor Fragmentation from HF-NRevo: Status, Prospects, and Intrinsic Charm

> We report on recent developments of the Heavy-Flavor Non-Relativistic evolution (HF-NRevo) scheme, a framework designed to describe heavy-hadron formation through leading-power fragmentation at moderate and large transverse momentum. The approach combines short-distance inputs obtained from next-to-leading-order NRQCD calculations with collinear scale evolution in a variable-flavor-number scheme, ensuring a consistent treatment of heavy-flavor thresholds and partonic hierarchies. Within this setup we have constructed the NRFF1.0 family of fragmentation functions for $S$-wave heavy quarkonia in their leading NRQCD Fock states. We discuss prospective applications of the HF-NRevo framework in the heavy-ion environment, where it can provide a perturbative baseline for investigating medium-induced modifications of heavy-flavor fragmentation. Its explicit treatment of partonic channels and heavy-flavor thresholds makes it particularly suitable for exploring jet-quenching sensitivity, energy-loss mechanisms, and the emergence of medium-modified fragmentation patterns in the quark-gluon plasma. The HF-NRevo scheme has also been extended to the exotic sector through the TQ4Q1.x and newly released TQ4Q2.0 fragmentation sets, which describe the formation of fully heavy tetraquarks in multiple quantum configurations. These developments open a novel pathway to study quarkoniumlike states and to probe the intrinsic charm content of the proton in forward hadron-collision environments. Altogether, this program broadens the phenomenological reach of heavy-flavor fragmentation studies at the HL-LHC and future collider facilities, opening access to previously unexplored aspects of QCD and potential portals to New Physics.

## Key claim

The Heavy-Flavor Non-Relativistic evolution (HF-NRevo) scheme provides a systematically improvable framework for constructing heavy-hadron fragmentation functions, but its current release (NRFF1.0) is limited to color-singlet S-wave quarkonia in their leading NRQCD Fock states, with color-octet contributions and general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme implementations left as future developments.

## Method

The HF-NRevo scheme combines next-to-leading-order (NLO) NRQCD short-distance coefficients as initial-scale inputs for fragmentation functions, then evolves them through DGLAP equations using a two-stage procedure: first, an expanded and decoupled symbolic evolution (EDevo) to handle heavy-flavor thresholds, followed by full numerical DGLAP evolution to all orders (AOevo). Missing higher-order uncertainties are quantified through correlated variations of renormalization and factorization scales by a factor of two around their central values, following a Monte Carlo replica-like procedure. The framework has been extended to fully heavy tetraquarks through the TQ4Q1.x and TQ4Q2.0 fragmentation sets, using NRQCD-based inputs for gluon and heavy-quark initiated channels.

## Key numbers

- Gluon-to-J/psi and gluon-to-Upsilon fragmentation functions evolved with mu_F spanning 30 to 120 GeV (Figures 1 and 2)
- Charm-to-J/psi and bottom-to-eta_b fragmentation functions evolved with mu_F varied between 30 and 120 GeV (Figure 2)
- B_c production suppression relative to B mesons confirmed at the level of approximately 0.1% (from prior work cited as Celiberto (2024))
- Forward-rapidity tetraquark phenomenology studied at 13 TeV LHC and prospective 100 TeV FCC energies

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior development of the NRFF1.0 fragmentation functions (Celiberto 2025) and the broader HF-NRevo program (Celiberto 2024, 2025), which itself builds on earlier NLO NRQCD calculations for vector quarkonia (Braaten 1993, Zheng 2019, 2021) and previous VFNS-evolved FF sets such as ZCW19+ and ZCFW22. The framework enables precision studies at HL-LHC, EIC, and future colliders, and provides a perturbative baseline for investigating medium-induced modifications of heavy-flavor fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, including jet quenching and energy-loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma. The extension to fully heavy tetraquarks opens a new pathway to probe intrinsic charm content of the proton in forward kinematics, with axial-vector tetraquark states identified as particularly clean probes.
