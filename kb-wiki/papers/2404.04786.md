# Reconciling the HESS J1731-347 constraints with Parity doublet model

> The recent discovery of a central compact object (CCO) within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, characterized by a mass of approximately $0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_{\odot}$ and a radius of about $10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km, has opened up a new window for the study of compact objects. This CCO is particularly intriguing because it is the lightest and smallest compact object ever observed, raising questions and challenging the existing theories. To account for this light compact star, a mean-field model within the framework of parity doublet structure is applied to describe the hadron matter. Inside the model, part of the nucleon mass is associated with the chiral symmetry breaking while the other part is from the chiral invariant mass $m_0$ which is insensitive to the temperature/density. The value of $m_0$ affects the nuclear equation of state for uniform nuclear matter at low density and exhibits strong correlations with the radii of neutron stars. We point out that HESS J1731-347 can be explained as the lightest neutron star for $m_0 \simeq 850$\,MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the central compact object HESS J1731-347, with mass approximately 0.77 solar masses and radius about 10.4 km, can be explained as the lightest neutron star within a parity doublet model, but only for a specific chiral invariant mass value of m0 approximately 850 MeV and a small slope parameter L of 40 MeV; the claim is conditional on the slope parameter being small, as larger values would rule out this interpretation.

## Method

The authors construct a unified equation of state by combining a hadronic parity doublet model (PDM) for densities up to 2n0 (n0 being nuclear saturation density) with an NJL-type quark model for densities above 5n0, using a fifth-order polynomial interpolation in baryon chemical potential between these regimes. The PDM includes a vector meson mixing term (omega-squared rho-squared) with coupling lambda_omega_rho, and the model parameters are fitted to reproduce vacuum hadron masses and nuclear saturation properties (n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy 16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV). The slope parameter L is used as an input to determine the strength of the omega-rho mixing, and the authors vary both the chiral invariant mass m0 and L to find parameter combinations that satisfy observational constraints from NICER, gravitational wave detections, and the HESS J1731-347 measurement.

## Key numbers

- HESS J1731-347 mass: 0.77 (+0.20, -0.17) solar masses; radius: 10.4 (+0.86, -0.78) km
- Chiral invariant mass m0 approximately 850 MeV is required to explain HESS J1731-347 as a neutron star
- For m0 = 800 MeV and L = 40 MeV, the radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is about 11.5 km; for L = 80 MeV it is about 12.6 km
- For m0 = 900 MeV with L = 40 MeV, the maximum neutron star mass falls below 2 solar masses, excluding this parameter combination
- The critical density for baryon overlap is estimated as n_c^per = 0.57 (+0.12, -0.09) fm^-3, corresponding to 3 to 4.3 times n0
- The sound velocity peak density constraint yields an allowed chiral invariant mass range of 600 to 800 MeV for L = 40 MeV
- The ambiguity from changing the interpolation range (1.5n0 to 5n0 versus 2.5n0 to 5n0) shifts radii by only about 0.1 km at 1 solar mass

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model approach previously developed by Gao (2022) and Minamikawa (2020, 2021) for constructing neutron star equations of state, and applies it to the recently discovered HESS J1731-347 central compact object reported in 2022. The study contrasts with alternative explanations that consider HESS J1731-347 as a quark star, as explored by Chu (2023), Oikonomou (2023), and others. The paper enables future constraints on the chiral invariant mass m0 and slope parameter L through more precise measurements of neutron star radii and the slope parameter, noting that if future experiments show a large slope parameter (as suggested by the PREX-2 experiment with L = 109 plus or minus 36.41 MeV), then HESS J1731-347 cannot be explained as a neutron star within this model.
