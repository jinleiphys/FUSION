# Three-Dimensional Approach Applied to Quasi-stationary States of Deformed $α$-Emitters

> We apply a three-dimensional (3D) approach to investigate the quasi-stationary states of well-deformed $α$-emitters. With a splitting of the anisotropic 3D potential into internal and external parts at a separation surface, the 3D $α$-cluster decay width is determined by the initial wave function of a true bound state of an anisotropic harmonic oscillator potential and a non-resonance scattering wave function of Coulomb potential. Substantial difference between the 1D and 3D decay width is found for typical $α$-emitters with large quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations.

## Key claim

The authors apply a three-dimensional (3D) two-potential approach (3D-TPA) to compute α-decay widths of well-deformed emitters, finding that the 3D decay width differs substantially from the 1D result for nuclei with large quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations. The key hedge is that the inner 3D bound-state wave function is approximated by the exact solution of an anisotropic harmonic oscillator potential, and the scattering wave function is treated as isotropic on the separation surface, with the authors noting that the numerical solution of the true inner wave function at large distances remains an open challenge.

## Method

The 3D α-core potential is split into an inner bound-state part and an outer scattering part at a separation surface inside the Coulomb barrier. The decay width is computed from the overlap of the inner bound-state wave function (taken as the analytical solution of an anisotropic harmonic oscillator potential of Nilsson form, including a L² correction term) and the outer non-resonance scattering wave function (solved from the Coulomb potential with deformed multipole terms). The calculation is performed for ground-state-to-ground-state α transitions of five well-deformed even-even nuclei, using a single set of parameters (a_r = 0.7883, κ = 0.0054, G = 22, n_z = 10, m = 0) for all emitters.

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach (TPA) from 1D spherical treatments (Buck, 1992; Xu, 2006; Mohr, 2006) to a full 3D treatment for deformed α-emitters, addressing a limitation noted in earlier empirical angle-averaging methods. The 3D-TPA reproduces the experimental systematics of α-decay half-lives for well-deformed nuclei far from shell closures (Z = 82, N = 126) with a single parameter set, whereas 1D-TPA requires parameter adjustment per nucleus. The approach enables future combination with microscopic clustering models such as the quartetting wave function approach (QWFA) of Xu (2016, 2017) and the THSR framework to predict α-decay half-lives for unknown nuclei far from stability.
