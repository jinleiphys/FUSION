# Effect of pairing on one- and two-nucleon transfer below the Coulomb barrier: a time-dependent microscopic description

> The effect of pairing correlation on transfer reaction below the Coulomb barrier is investigated qualitatively and quantitatively using a simplified version of the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock + BCS approach. The effect of particle number symmetry breaking on the description of reaction and dedicated methods to extract one and two-nucleon transfer probabilities (P_{1n} and P_{2n}) in a particle number symmetry breaking approach are discussed. Influence of pairing is systematically investigated in the ^{40}Ca+ ^{40,42,44,46,48,50}Ca reactions. A strong enhancement of the two-particle transfer probabilities due to initial pairing correlations is observed. This enhancement induces an increase of the ratio of probabilities P_{2n} / (P_{1n})^2 compared to the case with no pairing. It is shown that this ratio increases strongly as the center of mass energy decreases with a value that could be larger than ten in the deep sub-barrier regime. An analysis of the pair transfer sensitivity to the type of pairing interaction, namely surface, mixed or volume, used in the theory is made. It is found that the pair transfer is globally insensitive to the type of force and mainly depends on the pairing interaction strength.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that initial pairing correlations strongly enhance two-nucleon transfer probabilities below the Coulomb barrier, with the ratio \(P_{2n} / (P_{1n})^2\) increasing as center-of-mass energy decreases and potentially exceeding ten in the deep sub-barrier regime; however, this enhancement is globally insensitive to the type of pairing interaction (volume, mixed, or surface) and depends primarily on the pairing interaction strength.

## Method

The authors used a simplified Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock + BCS (TDHF+BCS) approach with the Frozen Occupation Approximation (FOA) to simulate central collisions between \(^{40}\)Ca and various calcium isotopes (\(^{40,42,44,46,48,50}\)Ca) below the fusion barrier. Transfer probabilities \(P_{1n}\) and \(P_{2n}\) were extracted using projection onto good particle number in the full space and in the subspace of one nucleus, with a Pfaffian technique employed for evaluation. The no-pairing reference was obtained using a filling approximation for the last occupied shell to avoid deformation effects, and three types of pairing interactions (volume, mixed, surface) were compared after adjusting their strengths to reproduce experimental neutron pairing gaps along the calcium isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- Fusion barrier \(B_0\) for \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca: 53.090 MeV (both filling and BCS), compared to experimental 53.6 MeV.
- Fusion barrier \(B_0\) for \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{46}\)Ca: 52.069 MeV (filling) and 52.049 MeV (BCS).
- Fusion barrier \(B_0\) for \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{48}\)Ca: 51.935 MeV (both filling and BCS), compared to experimental 51.8 MeV.
- The ratio \(P_{2n} / (P_{1n})^2\) could be larger than ten in the deep sub-barrier regime.
- For the reaction \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{46}\)Ca at \(E_{\text{c.m.}} = 43.7\) MeV, the ratio \(P_{2n}(\text{BCS})/P_{2n}(\text{MF})\) increased by approximately an order of magnitude at the physical pairing strength.
- Pairing interaction strengths used: volume (\(\eta=0\), \(V_0^{nn}=585\) MeV·fm\(^3\)), mixed (\(\eta=0.5\), \(V_0^{nn}=798\) MeV·fm\(^3\)), surface (\(\eta=1\), \(V_0^{nn}=1256\) MeV·fm\(^3\)).

## Context

This work extends the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock approach to include pairing correlations for transfer reactions, building on earlier studies of pair transfer in superfluid systems using Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov and Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation methods (e.g., Bes and Broglia in the 1960s–1970s, and more recent work by Potel, Graziani, and others). The TDHF+BCS method with frozen occupations provides a unified microscopic framework that treats nuclear structure and reaction dynamics simultaneously, unlike approaches that treat these aspects separately. The findings enable future studies of pair transfer in deeper sub-barrier regimes and suggest that experimental ratios \(P_{2n} / (P_{1n})^2\) could serve as a sensitive probe of pairing correlations, independent of the detailed form of the pairing interaction.
