# Some Aspects of Three-Quark Potentials

> We analytically evaluate the expectation value of a baryonic Wilson loop in a holographic model of an SU(3) pure gauge theory. We then discuss three aspects of a static three-quark potential: an aspect of universality which concerns properties independent of a geometric configuration of quarks; a heavy diquark structure; and a relation between the three and two-quark potentials.

## Key claim
The holographic model of an SU(3) pure gauge theory predicts that the static three-quark potential is geometry-dependent at both short and long distances, contradicting the universal behavior assumed by the phenomenological Delta-law and Y-law ansätze.

## Method
The author analytically evaluates the expectation value of a baryonic Wilson loop using a five-dimensional effective string theory with a deformed AdS metric (soft wall model). The calculation involves Nambu-Goto strings joining at a baryon vertex in the interior, with the vertex action parameterized by a single fitted parameter κ. The three-quark potential is derived for two geometries: an equilateral triangle and a symmetric collinear configuration, and the results are compared to lattice data and phenomenological models.

## Key numbers
- The model uses parameters γ = 0.176, σ = 0.44 GeV², κ = -0.083, and C = 1.87 GeV, fitted to lattice data from the equilateral triangle geometry.
- For the equilateral triangle at short distances: α₃Q/α_QQbar ≈ 0.495 and σ₀/σ ≈ 1.007, where α_QQbar is the Coulomb coefficient of the quark-antiquark potential.
- For the symmetric collinear geometry at short distances: α₃Q/α_QQbar ≈ 0.475 and σ₀/σ ≈ 0.926.
- The ratio of short-distance coefficients between geometries: α₃Q^c/α₃Q^t ≈ 0.960 and σ₀^c/σ₀^t ≈ 0.920.
- The difference in the constant term at long distances between geometries: c^c - c^t ≈ 0.06 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous holographic studies of three-quark potentials (Andreev 2013, 2014) and the quark-antiquark potential (Andreev and Zakharov 2006). The model reproduces lattice data from multiple groups (Petersson et al. 2002, Suganuma et al. 2006, 2013) for the equilateral triangle geometry with a single free parameter. The results challenge the Delta-law (sum of two-quark potentials) and Y-law (minimal string network) phenomenological models, showing that the three-quark potential is geometry-dependent at both short and long distances, unlike the universal behavior these models assume. This enables predictions for hybrid three-quark potentials and provides a framework for testing against future lattice simulations.
