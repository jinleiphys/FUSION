# Characterization and analysis of azimuthally sensitive correlations

> A unified framework for describing the azimuthal dependence of two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions is introduced, together with the methods for measuring the corresponding observables. The generalization to azimuthal correlations between more than two particles is presented.

# Characterization and analysis of azimuthally sensitive correlations

## Key claim

The paper introduces a unified, model-independent framework for characterizing azimuthally sensitive two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions using Fourier coefficients, but the final step of relating these observables to specific physical mechanisms is necessarily model-dependent.

## Method

The author defines pair-flow Fourier coefficients $v_{c,n}^{\rm pair}$ and $v_{s,n}^{\rm pair}$ by parameterizing the distribution of particle-pair azimuthal angles with respect to the reaction plane as a Fourier series, after isolating the pair orientation into a single angle $\phi_{\rm pair} = x\phi_1 + (1-x)\phi_2$. Existing methods for measuring single-particle anisotropic flow (event-plane methods, two-particle correlators, and multiparticle cumulants or Lee-Yang zeroes) are then adapted with minor modifications to measure these pair-flow coefficients, replacing the single-particle azimuth by the pair angle and, for sine coefficients, replacing cosine terms with sine terms.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle probability distribution is written as $p(\phi-\Phi_R) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} v_n e^{in(\phi-\Phi_R)}$, with $v_0=1$ and $v_{-n}=(v_n)^*$.
- The pair-flow distribution is $p(\phi_{\rm pair}-\Phi_R) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sum_{n=-\infty}^{+\infty} v_n^{\rm pair} e^{in(\phi_{\rm pair}-\Phi_R)}$, with $v_0^{\rm pair}=1$ and $v_{-n}^{\rm pair}=(v_n^{\rm pair})^*$.
- In the real form, $v_{c,n}^{\rm pair} = \langle \cos n(\phi_{\rm pair}-\Phi_R) \rangle$ and $v_{s,n}^{\rm pair} = \langle \sin n(\phi_{\rm pair}-\Phi_R) \rangle$, related by $v_n^{\rm pair} = v_{c,n}^{\rm pair} - i v_{s,n}^{\rm pair}$.
- For event-plane methods, $v_{c,n}^{\rm pair} = \langle \cos n(\phi_{\rm pair}-\Psi_R) \rangle / \langle \cos n(\Psi_R-\Phi_R) \rangle$, where $\Psi_R$ is the event-plane azimuth.
- For two-particle correlator methods, $\langle \cos n(\phi-\phi_{\rm pair}) \rangle = v_n v_{c,n}^{\rm pair}$ and $\langle \sin n(\phi-\phi_{\rm pair}) \rangle = v_n v_{s,n}^{\rm pair}$, where $v_n$ is the integrated single-particle flow.

## Context

This work extends the Fourier decomposition approach to anisotropic flow, originally developed for single-particle distributions by Voloshin (1994) and Ollitrault (1997), to two-particle correlations in a model-independent way. It directly addresses the problem that two-particle methods for measuring flow (such as those used by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations, e.g., Adler 2002, Adams 2004) are contaminated by nonflow correlations from resonance decays, jets, and quantum interference, which Borghini (2000) showed are of the same order as flow correlations at ultrarelativistic energies. The framework enables systematic measurement of azimuthally sensitive two-particle correlations using multiparticle methods (cumulants from Borghini 2001 and Lee-Yang zeroes from Bhalerao 2003) that suppress nonflow effects, and can be generalized to correlations between three or more particles for studying the flow of particles like $\rho$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$, and $\omega$ that decay into multiple daughters.
