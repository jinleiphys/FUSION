# The newly observed $Λ_b(6072)^0$ structure and its $ρ-$mode nonstrange partners

> Inspired by the newly observed $Λ_b(6072)$ structure, we investigate its strong decay behaviors under various assignments within the $^3P_0$ model. Compared with the mass and total decay width, our results suggest that the $Λ_b(6072)$ can be regarded as the lowest $ρ-$mode excitation in $Λ_b$ family. Then, the strong decays of $ρ-$mode nonstrange partners for the $Λ_b(6072)$ are calculated. It is found that the $J^P=5/2^-$ $Λ_b$ and $Λ_c$ states are relatively narrow, and mainly decay into the $Σ_b^{(*)} π$ and $Σ_c^{(*)} π$ final states, respectively. These two states have good potentials to be observed in future experiments, which may help us to distinguish the three-quark model and diquark model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the newly observed $\Lambda_b(6072)^0$ resonance is best interpreted as the lowest $\rho$-mode excitation in the $\Lambda_b$ family, rather than as a $\lambda$-mode $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state or a $\Sigma_b(1P)$ state, based on a comparison of calculated strong decay widths with the experimental total width of $72 \pm 11 \pm 2$ MeV.

## Method

The authors used the $^3P_0$ model to calculate the strong decay widths of the $\Lambda_b(6072)^0$ under three different assignments: a $\lambda$-mode $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state, a $\lambda$-mode $\Sigma_b(1P)$ state, and a $\rho$-mode $\tilde{\Lambda}_b(1P)$ state. They then compared the predicted total widths and branching ratios with the experimental data. After favoring the $\rho$-mode interpretation, they extended the calculation to predict the strong decays of the $\rho$-mode nonstrange partner states $\tilde{\Lambda}_c(1P)$, $\tilde{\Sigma}_b(1P)$, and $\tilde{\Sigma}_c(1P)$ using the same model and theoretical masses from the relativized quark model of Capstick (1986).

## Key numbers

- The experimental mass of $\Lambda_b(6072)^0$ is $6072.3 \pm 2.9 \pm 0.6 \pm 0.2$ MeV and its total decay width is $72 \pm 11 \pm 2$ MeV, as reported by the LHCb Collaboration.
- The predicted total width for the $\Lambda_b(6072)$ as a pure $\lambda$-mode $\Lambda_b(2S)$ state is 9.27 MeV, which is significantly smaller than the experimental value.
- The predicted total width for the $\Lambda_b(6072)$ as a pure $\rho$-mode $\tilde{\Lambda}_{b0}(1/2^-)$ state is effectively zero (forbidden), while the $\tilde{\Lambda}_{b1}(1/2^-)$ and $\tilde{\Lambda}_{b1}(3/2^-)$ states have widths of 523.28 MeV and 459.71 MeV, respectively, which are too large.
- With a mixing angle of $22^\circ$, the $|1P~1/2^- \rangle_1$ $\tilde{\Lambda}_b(1P)$ state yields a total width of about 73 MeV, consistent with the experimental data, and decays predominantly to $\Sigma_b \pi$ (99.7% branching ratio).
- The predicted total widths for the pure $j=2$ $\tilde{\Lambda}_{b2}(3/2^-)$ and $\tilde{\Lambda}_{b2}(5/2^-)$ states are 3.77 MeV and 3.13 MeV, respectively, at their predicted masses.
- The predicted total widths for the $\tilde{\Lambda}_{c2}(3/2^-)$ and $\tilde{\Lambda}_{c2}(5/2^-)$ states are 70.39 MeV and 67.66 MeV, respectively, at their predicted masses.

## Context

This work extends the study of excited singly heavy baryons, specifically addressing the newly observed $\Lambda_b(6072)^0$ by the LHCb Collaboration (2020). It contradicts the initial suggestion by the LHCb Collaboration that the state could be a $\lambda$-mode $\Lambda_b(2S)$ radial excitation, as well as alternative interpretations as a $\Sigma_b(1P)$ state or a Roper-like resonance proposed by Arifi (2020). The paper builds on previous calculations of $\lambda$-mode states, such as the assignment of $\Lambda_b(6146)$ and $\Lambda_b(6152)$ as $\Lambda_b(1D)$ states by Liang (2019) and Wang (2019), and uses the $^3P_0$ model framework established by Micu (1968). The key prediction is that the $J^P=5/2^-$ $\Lambda_b$ and $\Lambda_c$ states, which are $\rho$-mode excitations, are relatively narrow and could be observed in $\Sigma_b^{(*)} \pi$ and $\Sigma_c^{(*)} \pi$ final states, providing a test to distinguish between the three-quark model and the diquark model, which freezes $\rho$-mode excitations.
