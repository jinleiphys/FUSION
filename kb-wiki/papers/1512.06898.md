# Precise measurement of alpha_K and alpha_T for the 150.8-keV E3 transition in 111Cd: Test of internal-conversion theory

> We have measured the K-shell and total internal conversion coefficients, alpha_K and alpha_T, for the 150.8-keV E3 transition in 111Cd to be 1.449(18) and 2.217(26) respectively. The alpha_K result agrees well with Dirac-Fock calculations in which the effect of the K-shell atomic vacancy is accounted for; it extends our precision tests of alpha_K calculations to Z = 48, the lowest Z yet measured. However, the result for alpha_T disagrees by about two standard deviations from the calculated alpha_T value, whether or not the atomic vacancy is included.

## Key claim

The measured K-shell internal conversion coefficient (α_K = 1.449(18)) agrees well with Dirac-Fock calculations that include the atomic vacancy, extending precision tests to Z = 48; however, the total internal conversion coefficient (α_T = 2.217(26)) disagrees by about two standard deviations from the calculated value regardless of whether the atomic vacancy is included.

## Method

The authors produced 111Cd via neutron activation of enriched 110CdO electroplated onto aluminum foils, then recorded gamma-ray and K x-ray spectra with a precisely calibrated HPGe detector whose relative efficiency is known to ±0.15% over a wide energy range. For the 150.8-keV E3 transition in 111Cd, they extracted α_K from the ratio of K x-ray to gamma-ray counts using Eq. (2), which accounts for the cascade of two transitions (150.8 keV and 245.4 keV), and extracted α_T from the intensity balance between the two gamma rays using Eq. (3). Corrections were applied for impurities, Lorentzian peak shape, true-coincidence summing, and differential attenuation.

## Key numbers

- α_K for the 150.8-keV E3 transition in 111Cd: 1.449(18), measured to ±1.7% precision
- α_T for the same transition: 2.217(26), measured to ±0.8% precision
- The α_K result agrees with Dirac-Fock calculations including the atomic vacancy (1.451(1)) to within −0.1(12)%, and disagrees with the no-vacancy calculation (1.425(1)) by +1.7(12)%
- The α_T result is lower than the vacancy-included calculation (2.284(1)) by −2.9(12)% and lower than the no-vacancy calculation (2.257(1)) by −1.8(12)%
- The 245.4-keV E2 transition's α_K was taken as 0.05326(17) and its α_T as 0.06351(17), encompassing both vacancy and no-vacancy calculations
- The fluorescence yield ω_K for cadmium (Z=48) was taken as 0.842(4) from the recommended evaluation by Schoetzig (1996)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous precision tests of internal conversion theory (Nica 2004, 2005, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2014; Hardy 2014) from the range 50 < Z < 78 down to Z = 48, the lowest Z yet measured. The α_K result supports the decision by Kibédi et al. (2008) to adopt the "frozen orbital" vacancy treatment in the BrIcc database used by the National Nuclear Data Center. The α_T discrepancy may indicate penetration effects in this strongly hindered transition (hindered by ~10^4 relative to the Weisskopf limit), similar to anomalies previously suggested for other hindered transitions but not observed in the similarly hindered 127.5-keV E3 transition in 134Cs (Nica 2007).
