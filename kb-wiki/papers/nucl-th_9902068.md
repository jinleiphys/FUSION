# BB Intermeson Potentials in the Quark Model

> In this paper we derive quark model results for scattering amplitudes and equivalent low energy potentials for heavy meson pairs, in which each meson contains a heavy quark. This "BB" system is an attractive theoretical laboratory for the study of the nuclear force between color singlets; the hadronic system is relatively simple, and there are lattice gauge theory (LGT) results for V_BB(r) which may be compared to phenomenological models. We find that the quark model potential (after lattice smearing) has qualitative similarities to the LGT potential in the two B*B* channels in which direct comparison is possible, although there is evidence of a difference in length scales. The quark model prediction of equal magnitude but opposite sign for I=0 and I=1 potentials also appears similar to LGT results at intermediate r. There may however be a discrepancy between the LGT and quark model I=1 BB potentials. A numerical study of the two-meson Schrodinger equations in the (bqbar)(bqbar) and (cqbar)(cqbar) sectors with the quark model potentials finds a single "molecule", in the I=0 BB* sector. Binding in other channels might occur if the quark model forces are augmented by pion exchange.

## Key claim

The quark model, using a Coulomb-plus-linear confining interaction with one-gluon-exchange spin-spin terms, produces intermeson potentials for heavy BB systems that show qualitative agreement with lattice gauge theory (LGT) results in the two B*B* channels where direct comparison is possible, although there is evidence of a difference in length scales; the quark model prediction of equal magnitude but opposite sign for I=0 and I=1 potentials also appears similar to LGT results at intermediate r, but there may be a discrepancy between the LGT and quark model I=1 BB potentials.

## Method

The authors evaluated the lowest (Born) order T-matrix element of an interquark Hamiltonian (color Coulomb, spin-spin hyperfine, and linear scalar confinement terms) between two-meson scattering states using simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wavefunctions for the mesons, then Fourier transformed the T-matrix to obtain equivalent local low-energy potentials V_BB(r). The calculation was carried out for BB, BB*, and B*B* systems in both I=0 and I=1 isospin channels, with the heavy quark mass taken to infinity (lambda=1) for the final numerical results. The resulting potentials were then used in a two-meson Schrodinger equation to search for bound molecular states in the (bqbar)(bqbar) and (cqbar)(cqbar) sectors.

## Key numbers

- The color Coulomb contribution to the I=1 BB potential at small r approaches the bare result -2*alpha_s/(9r), with alpha_s the strong coupling constant.
- The spin-spin hyperfine contribution to the I=1 BB potential is a Gaussian: + (2^(1/2)/(9*pi^(1/2))) * (alpha_s * beta^3 / mbar^2) * exp(-beta^2 * r^2 / 2), where beta is the SHO wavefunction parameter and mbar is the light antiquark mass.
- The linear confinement contribution to the I=1 BB potential at r=0 gives a contact value of (b/(6*beta)) * (2^(3/2)/pi^(1/2) - 4/pi^(1/2)), where b is the string tension.
- A numerical study of the two-meson Schrodinger equations found a single "molecule" bound state in the I=0 BB* sector; no binding was found in other channels with the quark model potentials alone.

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach previously applied successfully to I=2 pipi scattering (Barnes and Swanson, 1992) and I=3/2 Kpi scattering (Barnes, Swanson, and Weinstein, 1992) to the heavy BB system, allowing direct comparison with lattice gauge theory results for V_BB(r) from the UKQCD collaboration (1997) and other LGT groups. The paper enables future comparisons between quark model predictions and more precise LGT results as they become available, particularly for separated spin and isospin channels, and suggests that pion exchange may need to be added to the quark model forces to produce binding in channels beyond the I=0 BB* sector.
