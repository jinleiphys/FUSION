# Charged-current inclusive neutrino cross sections in the SuperScaling model including quasielastic, pion production and meson-exchange contributions

> Charged current inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections are evaluated using the superscaling model for quasielastic scattering and its extension to the pion production region. The contribution of two-particle-two-hole vector meson-exchange current excitations is also considered within a fully relativistic model tested against electron scattering data. The results are compared with the inclusive neutrino-nucleus data from the T2K and SciBooNE experiments. For experiments where $\langle E_ν\rangle \sim 0.8$ GeV, the three mechanisms considered in this work provide good agreement with the data. However, when the neutrino energy is larger, effects from beyond the $Δ$ also appear to be playing a role. The results show that processes induced by two-body currents play a minor role at the kinematics considered.

# Charged-current inclusive neutrino cross sections in the SuperScaling model including quasielastic, pion production and meson-exchange contributions

## Key claim

The SuSAv2 model, including quasielastic scattering, Delta-resonance pion production, and vector meson-exchange current contributions, provides good agreement with T2K and SciBooNE inclusive neutrino-nucleus data only when the average neutrino energy is around 0.8 GeV; at higher neutrino energies, effects from processes beyond the Delta resonance (such as higher resonances, multi-pion production, and deep inelastic scattering) become significant and the model underpredicts the data.

## Method

The authors used the superscaling (SuSAv2) model for quasielastic scattering, extended it to the Delta-resonance region via a phenomenological non-QE scaling function extracted from electron-carbon scattering data, and added vector 2p2h meson-exchange current contributions from a fully relativistic calculation parameterized by Megias et al. (2014). They compared the resulting flux-folded double-differential and total cross sections with inclusive charged-current neutrino-carbon data from the T2K and SciBooNE experiments, using the axial N-to-Delta form factors from Alvarez-Ruso et al. (1998).

## Key numbers

- Average neutrino energy for T2K muon-neutrino data: ⟨E_νμ⟩ ∼ 0.85 GeV; for T2K electron-neutrino data: ⟨E_νe⟩ ∼ 1.3 GeV; for SciBooNE data: ⟨E_ν⟩ ∼ 0.76 GeV (unfolded up to 5 GeV).
- The 1π (Delta-resonance) contribution to the T2K muon-neutrino cross section has a high-energy tail (ψ_Δ > 0.5) that contributes less than 10-12% of the 1π cross section at T2K kinematics.
- For T2K electron-neutrino data, contributions from processes above the Delta may provide 20-30% more strength in some kinematic regions; at high Q²_QE the shortfall is about 30%.
- For SciBooNE neutrinos, the QE contribution saturates near E_ν ∼ 1 GeV at ∼ 5 × 10⁻³⁹ cm²; the 1π contribution saturates near E_ν ∼ 1.5 GeV at ∼ 5 × 10⁻³⁹ cm².
- At SciBooNE neutrino energies above 1.5 GeV, the model underpredicts the data by approximately a factor of two.
- The high-inelasticity tail (ψ_Δ ≥ 0.5) contributes about 30% of the total integrated cross section at high energies for SciBooNE.
- Vector 2p2h MEC contributions are minor: at most ∼7% of the total cross section at the highest SciBooNE energies for neutrinos, and about 9% for antineutrinos.
- Uncertainty from different axial N-to-Delta form factor parametrizations is less than 5.5% of the total cross section at the highest energies.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA model of Amaro et al. (2004) by incorporating the improved SuSAv2 version with separate longitudinal and transverse scaling functions from Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2014), and adds vector 2p2h MEC contributions from De Pace et al. (2003) parameterized by Megias et al. (2014). The results contrast with those of Martini et al. (2014), who found larger MEC contributions, and with RPA-based calculations by Nieves et al. (2011) and Martini et al. (2014) that were restricted to neutrino energies below about 1.2 GeV. The model enables future inclusion of axial MEC contributions (work in progress) and extension to higher-energy processes such as higher resonances, multi-pion production, and deep inelastic scattering.
