# Chiral Disorder and Diffusion of Light Quarks in the QCD Vacuum

> We give a pedagogical introduction to the concept that light quarks diffuse in the QCD vacuum following the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. By analogy with disordered electrons in metals, we show that the diffusion constant for light quarks in QCD is $D=2F_π^2/|\la\bar{q}q\to|$ which is about 0.22 fm. We comment on the correspondence between the diffusive phase and the chiral phase as described by chiral perturbation theory, as well as the cross-over to the ergodic phase as described by random matrix theory. The cross-over is identified with the Thouless energy $E_c=D/\sqrt{V_4}$ which is the inverse diffusion time in an Euclidean four-volume $V_4$.

## Key claim

Light quarks in the QCD vacuum undergo diffusive motion in four-dimensional Euclidean space, with a diffusion constant given by $D=2F_\pi^2/|\langle\bar{q}q\rangle| \approx 0.22$ fm, but this description is restricted to the diffusive regime where eigenvalues of the Dirac operator lie between the Thouless energy and twice the constituent quark mass.

## Method

The authors draw an analogy between disordered electronic systems in condensed matter and the QCD vacuum, treating the gluonic background as a disordered medium. They analytically continue the pion correlation function to complex mass $z=m-i\lambda/2$ and identify the resulting diffusion pole structure, showing that the pion propagator plays the role of the "diffuson" after integrating over fast gluonic variables. The return probability for a light quark is then expressed in terms of the analytically continued pion correlation function, yielding a diffusion constant $D=2F_\pi^2/\Sigma$ where $\Sigma=|\langle\bar{q}q\rangle|$.

## Key numbers

- Diffusion constant for light quarks in QCD: $D = 2F_\pi^2/|\langle\bar{q}q\rangle| \approx 0.22$ fm
- Thouless energy: $E_c = D/\sqrt{V_4}$, where $V_4$ is the Euclidean four-volume
- Average level spacing of the Dirac operator: $\Delta = \pi/\Sigma V_4$
- Dimensionless ratio for lattice studies: $E_c/\Delta = (2/\pi)F_\pi^2 L^2$
- Coherence length for nonzero quark mass: $L_{coh} = 1/m_\pi$, consistent with the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation
- In the instanton model, the diluteness ratio $\bar{r}/R = 0.2-0.3$ explains the small diffusion constant

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher (1980) suggestion that light quarks undergo random walks in the QCD vacuum, and builds on earlier instanton liquid model studies by Diakonov and Petrov (1984) and Shuryak (1982). The authors establish a quantitative correspondence between the diffusive regime and chiral perturbation theory as formulated by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), and show that the ergodic regime below the Thouless energy reproduces the universal predictions of chiral random matrix theory developed by Verbaarschot (1994) and others. The results enable direct lattice tests of the Thouless energy scaling, as subsequently performed by Berbenni-Bitsch et al. (1998), and open the way to studying finite temperature effects, chemical potential dependence, and external field modifications of the QCD vacuum.
