# Charge Exchange Spin-Dipole Excitations of 90Zr and 208Pb and Neutron Matter Equation of State

> Charge exchange spin-dipole (SD) excitations of $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb are studied by using a Skyrme Hartree-Fock(HF) + Random Phase approximation (RPA). The calculated spin-dipole strength distributions are compared with experimental data obtained by $^{90}$Zr (p,n) $^{90}$Nb and $^{90}$Zr (n,p) $^{90}$ Nb reactions. The model-independent SD sum rule values of various Skyrme interactions are studied in comparison with the experimental values in order to determine the neutron skin thickness of $^{90}$Zr. The pressure of the neutron matter equation of state (EOS) and the nuclear matter symmetry energy are discussed in terms of the neutron skin thickness and peak energies of SD strength distributions.

## Key claim
The model-independent spin-dipole sum rule provides a direct determination of the neutron skin thickness of 90Zr with 1% accuracy, comparable to that expected from parity violation electron scattering, but the extracted value depends on the specific Skyrme interaction used and requires a quenching factor of 0.68 to match experimental strength distributions.

## Method
The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for charge exchange spin-dipole excitations in 90Zr and 208Pb using four Skyrme interactions (SIII, SGII, SkI3, SLy4). The calculated strength distributions were compared with experimental data from 90Zr(p,n)90Nb and 90Zr(n,p)90Y reactions, and the model-independent sum rule values were extracted to determine neutron skin thickness and correlations with the neutron matter equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Neutron skin thickness of 90Zr: r_n - r_p = 0.07 ± 0.04 fm, extracted from the SD sum rule analysis
- Neutron radius of 90Zr: sqrt(<r^2>_n) = 4.26 ± 0.04 fm, from the model-independent SD sum rule
- Experimental SD sum rule values for 90Zr: S_- = 271 ± 14 fm^2, S_+ = 124 ± 11 fm^2, ΔS = 147 ± 13 fm^2
- Quenching factor of 0.68 applied to both t_- and t_+ channels to match experimental data
- Peak energies for t_- channel in 90Zr range from 26.1 MeV (SLy4) to 29.3 MeV (SkI3)
- Peak energies for t_+ channel in 90Zr range from 11.4 MeV (SLy4) to 13.5 MeV (SIII)
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.125 fm (SIII), 0.135 fm (SGII), 0.228 fm (SkI3), 0.160 fm (SLy4)
- SD sum rule ΔS for 208Pb: 1086 fm^2 (SIII), 1072 fm^2 (SGII), 1154 fm^2 (SkI3), 1098 fm^2 (SLy4)

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Brown, Furnstahl, and collaborators (early 2000s) on the relationship between neutron matter equation of state and neutron skin thickness using Skyrme HF and relativistic mean field models. It also builds on experimental SD excitation studies by Wakasa (2000) and Yako (2006) on 90Zr, and by the SD-Pb collaboration on 208Pb. The results enable more precise determination of the neutron matter EOS and symmetry energy through the strong linear correlation with neutron skin thickness, and provide crucial input for evaluating neutrino reaction cross-sections on lead targets for supernova neutrino detection.
