# $D^*$ $ΞN$ bound state in strange three-body systems

> The recent update of the strangeness $-2$ ESC08c Nijmegen potential incorporating the NAGARA and KISO events predicts a $ΞN$ bound state, $D^*$, in the $^3S_1 (I=1)$ channel. We study if the existence of this two-body bound state could give rise to stable three-body systems. For this purpose we solve the bound state problem of three-body systems where the $ΞN$ state is merged with $N$'s, $Λ$'s, $Σ's$ or $Ξ$'s, making use of the most recent updates of the two-body ESC08c Nijmegen potentials. We found that there appear stable states in the $ΞNN$ and $ΞΞN$ systems, the $ΞΛN$ and $ΞΣN$ systems being unbound.

# The $D^*$ $\Xi N$ bound state in strange three-body systems

## Key claim
The existence of a $\Xi N$ bound state (called $D^*$) in the $^3S_1 (I=1)$ channel, predicted by the updated ESC08c Nijmegen potential, can give rise to stable three-body systems only in the $\Xi NN$ and $\Xi\Xi N$ configurations, while the $\Xi\Lambda N$ and $\Xi\Sigma N$ systems remain unbound.

## Method
The authors solved the bound-state Faddeev equations for three-baryon systems where all particles are in S-wave states, using two-body amplitudes constructed from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with local Yukawa potentials fitted to the most recent updates of the ESC08c Nijmegen potentials in the strangeness $-1$, $-2$, $-3$, and $-4$ sectors. The two-body potentials for $\Lambda N$, $\Sigma N$, $\Xi N$, $\Lambda\Xi$, $\Sigma\Xi$, and $\Xi\Xi$ channels were parameterized as an attractive plus a repulsive Yukawa term, with parameters obtained by fitting the low-energy data of each channel.

## Key numbers
- The $D^*$ $\Xi N$ bound state in the $^3S_1 (I=1)$ channel has a binding energy of 1.67 MeV (from the fitted potential; the abstract mentions 1.56 MeV from the original update).
- The $\Xi NN$ system has a bound state with $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ lying 13.54 MeV below the $\Xi d$ threshold (separation energy).
- The $\Xi NN$ system has a bound state with $(I)J^P=(\frac{3}{2})\frac{1}{2}^+$ lying 1.33 MeV below the corresponding threshold $2m_N+m_\Xi-B_2$.
- The $\Xi\Xi N$ system has a bound state with $(I)J^P=(\frac{3}{2})\frac{1}{2}^+$ lying 2.85 MeV below the lowest threshold $2m_\Xi+m_N-B_2$.
- The $\Xi N$ $(0,1)$ channel has scattering length $a = -5.357$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 1.434$ fm.
- The $\Xi N$ $(1,1)$ channel has scattering length $a = 4.911$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 0.527$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of $\Xi NN$ bound states (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2015 and 2016) to include $\Xi\Lambda N$, $\Xi\Sigma N$, and $\Xi\Xi N$ systems. It is motivated by the KISO event (Nakazawa, 2015) and the NAGARA event (Takahashi, 2001), which provided experimental evidence for strangeness $-2$ bound states and led to the update of the ESC08c Nijmegen potential (Nagels, 2015; Rijken, 2016). The results complement lattice QCD calculations suggesting attractive $\Xi N$ interaction (Sasaki, 2015) and earlier $\Xi$ hypernuclei studies using Nijmegen models (Yamamoto, 2010; Hiyama, 2008). The predicted stable three-body states could be tested by the J-PARC E07 experiment.
