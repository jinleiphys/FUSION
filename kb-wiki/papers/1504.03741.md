# Jet propagation within a Linearized Boltzmann Transport Model

> A Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model has been developed for the study of jet propagation inside a quark-gluon plasma. Both leading and thermal recoiled partons are transported according to the Boltzmann equations to account for jet-induced medium excitations. In this talk, we present our study within the LBT model in which we implement the complete set of elastic parton scattering processes. We investigate elastic parton energy loss and their energy and length dependence. We further investigate elastic energy loss and transverse shape of reconstructed jets. Contributions from the recoiled thermal partons are found to have significant influences on the jet energy loss and transverse profile.

## Key claim

The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model with the complete set of elastic 2-to-2 QCD processes shows that analytic small-angle approximations overestimate elastic energy loss by almost a factor of 2 for partons with initial energy below 10 GeV, with agreement improving at higher energies; the inclusion of recoiled thermal partons and "negative" partons (representing the wake depletion) significantly influences both the jet energy loss and transverse profile.

## Method

The authors implemented the complete set of elastic 2-to-2 parton scattering processes in QCD within a Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) Monte Carlo model, using fixed strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3 and a Lorentz-invariant regularization condition with Debye screening mass. Both leading jet shower partons and thermal recoiled partons were transported according to the Boltzmann equation, with "negative" partons tracked to account for the back-reaction and depletion of the thermal medium. A modified anti-k_t algorithm in FASTJET was used to reconstruct the leading jet in a static and homogeneous quark-gluon plasma, considering only elastic processes.

## Key numbers

- Fractional elastic energy loss per unit length for a gluon and a quark was studied at medium temperatures T = 200 MeV and T = 300 MeV, with propagation length L = 8 fm.
- The analytic small-angle approximation overestimates elastic energy loss by almost a factor of 2 for partons with initial energy below 10 GeV.
- For jet studies, a gluon with initial energy E = 100 GeV was propagated in a static medium at T = 400 MeV, using a jet-cone size R = 0.3.
- The fixed strong coupling constant was set at alpha_s = 0.3 throughout the study.
- The Debye screening mass squared is given by mu_D^2 = (N_c + N_f/2) g^2 T^2 / 3.

## Context

This work extends the Linearized Boltzmann Transport model of Wang (2013) and Li (2010) by implementing the complete set of elastic 2-to-2 QCD processes beyond the small-angle approximation, and by including flavor-changing annihilation and creation processes. The study builds on earlier estimates of elastic energy loss by Bjorken (1982) and detailed finite-temperature QCD calculations by Thoma (1991). The results enable more accurate modeling of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for understanding the role of recoiled thermal partons and jet-induced wake effects on reconstructed jet energy and transverse profiles.
