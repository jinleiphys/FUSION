# Gross-Witten-Wadia transition in a matrix model of deconfinement

> We study the deconfining phase transition at nonzero temperature in a SU(N) gauge theory, using a matrix model which was analyzed previously at small N. We show that the model is soluble at infinite N, and exhibits a Gross-Witten-Wadia transition. In some ways, the deconfining phase transition is of first order: at a temperature $T_d$, the Polyakov loop jumps discontinuously from 0 to1/2, and there is a nonzero latent heat $\sim N^2$. In other ways, the transition is of second order: e.g., the specific heat diverges as $C \sim 1/(T-T_d)^{3/5}$ when $T \rightarrow T_d^+$. Other critical exponents satisfy the usual scaling relations of a second order phase transition. In the presence of a nonzero background field $h$ for the Polyakov loop, there is a phase transition at the temperature $T_h$ where the value of the loop =1/2, with $T_h < T_d$. Since $\partial C/\partial T \sim 1/(T-T_h)^{1/2}$ as $T \rightarrow T_h^+$, this transition is of third order.

## Key claim

The deconfining phase transition in an SU(N) gauge theory, modeled by a matrix model soluble at infinite N, exhibits a Gross-Witten-Wadia transition that combines first-order features (a discontinuous jump of the Polyakov loop from 0 to 1/2 at T_d with nonzero latent heat ~ N^2) with second-order features (a diverging specific heat C ~ 1/(T-T_d)^(3/5) as T → T_d^+), but only at infinite N; at finite N the transition is first order for N ≥ 3.

## Method

The authors study a matrix model for the deconfining phase transition in SU(N) gauge theory at nonzero temperature, using a potential composed of two terms: a one-loop perturbative term ~ V_2(q) and a term ~ V_1(q) that drives confinement. They solve the model analytically at infinite N by introducing an eigenvalue density ρ(q) and reducing the integral equation of motion to a differential equation, yielding a solution ρ(q) = 1 + b cos(d q) for q in [-q_0, q_0]. The transition occurs when d(T_d) = 2π, at which point the eigenvalue density coincides identically with that of the Gross-Witten model. They also analyze the model with a nonzero background field h for the Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

- At the deconfining temperature T_d, the Polyakov loop jumps discontinuously from 0 to 1/2.
- The specific heat diverges as C ~ 1/(T-T_d)^(3/5) when T → T_d^+.
- The critical exponent β for the Polyakov loop near T_d^+ is β = 2/5, with ℓ_1(T) - 1/2 ~ (T_d - T)^(2/5).
- The latent heat is e(T_d^+)/(N^2 T_d^4) = 1/π^2 ≈ 0.10, about four times smaller than lattice results of ~0.39 from Datta (2010).
- At the transition point d(T_d) = 2π, the eigenvalue density is ρ(q) = 1 + cos(2π q).
- For nonzero background field h at T = T_d, ℓ_1 - 1/2 ~ h^(2/5), giving δ = 5/2.
- The effective potential at T_d^+ expands as Γ(ℓ_1) ~ (ℓ_1 - 1/2)^(7/2).
- At T_h < T_d (where ℓ_1 = 1/2), the third derivative of the pressure diverges as ∂^3 p/∂T^3 ~ 1/(T-T_h)^(1/2) as T → T_h^+, indicating a third-order transition.
- For finite N, the specific heat peak sharpens significantly only for N ≥ 40, within ~0.2% of T_d.

## Context

This work extends matrix models previously analyzed at small N by Dumitru (2010, 2012) and Kashiwa (2012) to infinite N, showing they become soluble and exhibit a Gross-Witten-Wadia transition similar to that found on a femtosphere by Sundborg (1999) and Aharony (2003). The results suggest that the Gross-Witten-Wadia transition may not be an artifact of finite volume but could occur for SU(∞) gauge theories in infinite volume. The model enables future study of whether additional couplings (as in Dumitru 2004) wash out the transition, and motivates lattice simulations at very large N (N ≥ 40) to search for this critical behavior.
