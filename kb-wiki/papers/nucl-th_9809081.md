# Supernova electron capture rates for 55Co and 56Ni

> We have calculated the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the ground states and first excited states in 55Co and 56Ni. These calculations have been performed by shell model diagonalization in the pf shell using the KB3 interaction. The Gamow-Teller distributions are used to calculate the electron capture rates for typical presupernova conditions. Our 55Co rate is noticeably smaller than the presently adopted rate as it is dominated by weak low-lying transitions rather than the strong Gamow-Teller (GT) resonance which is located at a higher excitation energy in the daughter than usually parametrized. Although our 56Ni rate agrees with the presently adopted rate, we do not confirm the conventional parametrization of the GT centroid. Our results support general trends suggested on the basis of shell model Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim

The electron capture rate on 55Co is more than an order of magnitude smaller than the presently adopted rate because the Gamow-Teller resonance is located at higher excitation energy in the daughter nucleus than previously parametrized, making the rate dominated by weak low-lying transitions rather than the strong GT resonance. For 56Ni, the total rate agrees with the recommended value, but the conventional parametrization of the GT centroid is incorrect: the bulk of the GT strength lies about 1 MeV lower in excitation energy than assumed.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalizations in the complete pf shell using the KB3 residual interaction and the ANTOINE code. For 56Ni, the GT strength distribution was calculated in a model space allowing up to 6 particles to be excited from the f7/2 shell to the rest of the pf shell in the daughter nucleus 56Co; for 55Co, a truncated calculation fulfilling the Ikeda sum rule allowed up to 5 particles to be excited out of the f7/2 orbital. The axial-vector coupling constant was renormalized to gA = 1 to account for the overestimate of GT strength in 0-hbar-omega shell model calculations. Electron capture rates were then computed for typical presupernova conditions using the calculated GT strength distributions and the standard rate formula including nuclear partition functions, Q-values, and electron chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

* Total GT strength for 56Ni ground state: B(GT) = 10.1 gA^2 (full pf shell), compared to the independent particle model value of 13.7 gA^2 and the SMMC value of (9.8 +/- 0.4) gA^2.
* Total GT strength for 55Co ground state: B(GT) = 8.7 gA^2; for each of the two excited J=3/2 states: 8.9 gA^2, compared to the independent particle model value of 12 gA^2.
* Calculated electron capture rate for 55Co at T9 = 3.26 and rho7 = 4.32: 1.6 x 10^-3 s^-1, compared to the recommended rate of 5.1 x 10^-2 s^-1 and the FFN rate of 8.4 x 10^-2 s^-1.
* Calculated electron capture rate for 56Ni at T9 = 3.26 and rho7 = 4.32: 12.6 x 10^-3 s^-1, compared to the recommended rate of 8.6 x 10^-3 s^-1 and the FFN rate of 7.5 x 10^-3 s^-1.
* Calculated terrestrial lifetime of 55Co: 16.7 hours, compared to the experimental value of 17.53 hours.
* Calculated terrestrial lifetime of 56Ni: 6.7 days, compared to the experimental value of 6.08 days.
* GT centroid for 55Co ground state: about 6 MeV excitation energy in 55Fe; for the excited states: about 9-10 MeV.
* GT centroid for 56Ni ground state: about 2.5-3 MeV excitation energy in 56Co, compared to the FFN placement at 3.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the shell model Monte Carlo calculations of Dean et al. (1997) by providing the detailed spectroscopy needed for odd-A nuclei, where the SMMC method cannot resolve the weak low-lying transitions that dominate the capture rate. It contradicts the parametrization of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN, 1980-1985) and the updated rates of Aufderheide et al. (1994), which placed the GT resonance at incorrect excitation energies for both nuclei. The results confirm the general trend suggested by Dean et al. (1997) that for even-even parent nuclei the GT strength lies at lower excitation energies than assumed by FFN, while for odd-A nuclei the centroid is too high to affect the capture rate significantly. The revised rates imply that 56Ni becomes the dominant electron capture nucleus at the onset of collapse and that the rate of change of the electron fraction dYe/dt is reduced by nearly a factor of 2, motivating a full update of electron capture rates on all important nuclei for presupernova collapse simulations.
