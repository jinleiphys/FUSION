# On the role of induced electric field in the time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm effect

> Whether the time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm (AB) effect even exists or not has been the subject of long-standing debate. There are two factors complicating the problem. First, in the closed spacetime line integral of the vector potential that is thought to give the AB-phase shift, how to treat the time-varying vector potential is highly nontrivial. Second, the time-varying magnetic flux generates induced electric field even outside the solenoid. In the present paper, motivated by a recent work by Gao, we re-investigate the role of the induced electric field with the utmost care. This analysis reveals a highly nontrivial effect of the induced electric field, which turns out to be useful for verifying the very existence of the time-dependent AB-effect.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm effect likely exists, and the induced electric field from the time-varying magnetic flux does not cancel the time-dependent AB-phase shift; rather, the same phase shift expression obtained by neglecting the induced electric field is recovered even when its effect is included, but only under specific assumptions about the electron paths and re-encounter conditions that the paper clarifies.

## Method

The author reanalyzes the time-dependent AB effect using Gao's elementary formulation, solving the equation of motion for the orbital angular momentum of electrons traveling on semicircular paths around an infinitely long solenoid with time-dependent magnetic flux. The time-dependent AB-phase shift is computed from the closed spacetime line integral of the vector potential, incorporating the Lorentz force from the induced electric field, and the result is compared with the earlier derivation that neglected this force. The analysis also determines the azimuthal angle at which the two electron beams re-encounter, showing it depends on the electron mass, charge, and path radius.

## Key numbers

- The time-dependent AB-phase shift is given by \(\phi_{AB} = e \frac{1}{T} \int_0^T \Phi(t) dt\), where \(T\) is the arrival time and \(\Phi(t)\) is the time-dependent magnetic flux.
- For a sinusoidally oscillating flux \(\Phi(t) = \Phi_0 + \Phi_1 \sin \Omega t\), the AB-phase shift becomes \(\phi_{AB} = e \Phi_0 f(\Omega T)\) with \(f(\Omega T) = 1 + (\Phi_1/\Phi_0)(\cos \Omega T / \Omega T - 1/\Omega T)\).
- The re-encounter azimuthal angle is \(\phi_f = \pi - (e \Phi_1 / 2\pi m \rho) T (\cos \Omega T / \Omega T - 1/\Omega T)\), which depends on the electron mass \(m\), charge \(e\), and path radius \(\rho\).
- In the limit \(\Omega \rightarrow 0\) (static flux), \(\phi_{AB} \rightarrow e \Phi_0\) and \(\phi_f \rightarrow \pi\).
- In the limit \(\Omega \rightarrow \infty\) (rapid oscillation), \(\phi_{AB} \rightarrow e \Phi_0\) and \(\phi_f \rightarrow \pi\), both approaching their limits while oscillating as a function of \(T\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Wakamatsu 2025) that resolved a long-standing debate over the cancellation argument by Singleton and collaborators (Singleton and Vaman 2013, Murray and Singleton 2014, Akhmeteli and Singleton 2016), who claimed the time-dependent AB-phase shift is cancelled by the induced electric field. The paper is motivated by a recent work by Gao (2025) that obtained the same AB-phase shift expression even including the induced electric field, but the present analysis reveals that Gao's treatment left ambiguous the re-encounter point of the electron beams. The results enable experimental verification of the time-dependent AB effect by measuring the oscillation of interference peak position as a function of electron velocity, contrasting with the dispersionless nature of the static AB effect noted by Zeilinger (1986) and Peshkin (1999).
