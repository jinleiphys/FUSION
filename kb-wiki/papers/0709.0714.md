# The permutation group S_N and large Nc excited baryons

> We study the excited baryon states for an arbitrary number of colors Nc from the perspective of the permutation group S_N of N objects. Classifying the transformation properties of states and quark-quark interaction operators under S_N allows a general analysis of the spin-flavor structure of the mass operator of these states, in terms of a few unknown constants parameterizing the unknown spatial structure. We explain how to perform the matching calculation of a general two-body quark-quark interaction onto the operators of the 1/Nc expansion. The inclusion of core and excited quark operators is shown to be necessary. Considering the case of the negative parity L=1 states transforming in the MS of S_N, we discuss the matching of the one-gluon and the Goldstone-boson exchange interactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the permutation group \(S_N\) provides a formal symmetry justification for the "core + excited quark" decomposition used in the \(1/N_c\) expansion for excited baryons, and that the criticism by Matagne and Stancu (2006, 2007) of the standard \(1/N_c\) expansion approach is unfounded because it ignored operators transforming as non-singlets under \(S_N^{sf}\).

## Method

The authors classify spin-flavor states of \(N_c\) quarks into irreducible representations of \(SU(4) \times S_N^{sf}\) and decompose the most general two-body quark-quark interaction Hamiltonian into operators transforming as specific irreps of \(S_N^{orb} \times S_N^{sf}\) (symmetric \(S\), mixed-symmetric \(MS\), and other irreps \(E\) and \(A'\)). They then perform explicit matching calculations for the one-gluon exchange and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions onto the operators of the \(1/N_c\) expansion, computing reduced matrix elements for the \(L=1\) negative parity baryons transforming in the \(MS\) irrep of \(S_N^{sf}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(N_c=3\), the \(L=1\) negative parity baryons transform in the \(70^-\) of SU(6) (or the \(20^-\) of SU(4)), containing the spin-isospin multiplets \(^48 \oplus ^28 \oplus ^210 \oplus ^21\).
* The matrix of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients \(M_{ij}\) for the \(MS \times MS \to S\) inner product is given explicitly for arbitrary \(N\) in Eq. (Mmatrix), with diagonal elements \(1\) and off-diagonal elements \(-1/(N-1)\).
* The matrix element of a symmetric two-body operator on the \(MS\) states is \(\langle V^{symm} \rangle = \frac{2}{N(N-1)} \langle {\cal R}^S \rangle \langle {\cal O}^S \rangle + \frac{1}{N} \langle {\cal R}^{MS} \rangle \langle {\cal O}^{MS} \rangle + \frac{N(N-3)}{4(N-1)} \langle {\cal R}^{E} \rangle \langle {\cal O}^{E} \rangle\).
* The reduced matrix element for the spin-spin interaction's \(MS\) orbital operator is \(\langle {\cal R}^{MS} \rangle = (N-2)({\cal I}_{\rm dir} - {\cal I}_{s}) - 2 {\cal I}_{\rm exc}\), where the overlap integrals \({\cal I}_s, {\cal I}_{\rm dir}, {\cal I}_{\rm exc}\) are defined in Eqs. (overlap).

## Context

This work extends the \(S_N\) analysis of Collins and Georgi (1998) from \(N_c=3\) to arbitrary \(N_c\), and provides a formal symmetry justification for the operator construction of Carlson et al. (1998) used in the \(1/N_c\) expansion for excited baryons. The results enable systematic treatment of higher excitations with more complicated spin-flavor structure and settle objections raised by Matagne and Stancu (2006, 2007) regarding the validity of the core+excited decomposition approach.
