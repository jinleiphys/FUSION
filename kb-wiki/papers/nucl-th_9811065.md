# Hadrons in the Nuclear Medium -- Introduction and Overwiew

> In this talk I first discuss predictions based on QCD sum rules and on hadronic models for the properties of vector mesons in the nuclear medium. I then describe possible experimental signatures and show detailed predictions for dilepton invariant mass spectra with a special emphasis on nuclear reactions involving elementary incoming beams and nuclear targets. I will in particular illustrate that the sensitivity of pion and photon induced reactions to in-medium effects is comparable to that of heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

The original QCD sum-rule prediction of a dramatic drop in vector meson masses in nuclear matter (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992) is too naive because it assumed a sharp resonance state; more recent analyses show that QCD sum rules are compatible with a wide range of masses and widths, and that a significant broadening of the meson at nearly constant pole position is also compatible with the data.

## Method

The paper reviews two complementary approaches to calculating in-medium properties of vector mesons: QCD sum rules, which relate the current-current correlator evaluated via the Operator Product Expansion to a hadronic spectral function, and hadronic models that calculate the vector meson selfenergy by dressing propagators with hadronic loops involving nucleon resonances. For experimental observables, the author uses semiclassical transport theory (the Coupled Channel BUU method) to simulate dilepton production in pion-induced, photon-induced, and heavy-ion reactions, propagating nucleons, resonances up to 2 GeV mass, and mesons from initial contact through final stage.

## Key numbers

- The N(1520) D13 resonance has a significant ρ decay branch of about 20%.
- In π⁻ + Pb reactions at 1.3 GeV, the dilepton yield in the range of about 600–700 MeV is increased by in-medium effects by up to a factor of 2.5.
- In γ + ²⁰⁸Pb reactions at 2.2 GeV, the sensitivity in the region between about 300 and 700 MeV amounts to a factor of about 3.
- At momenta of about 100 MeV/c, the ρ meson mass shift turns from negative to positive, with a width staying remarkably constant at about 250 MeV, corresponding to a collisional broadening width δΓ ≈ 100 MeV.
- The calculated δΓρ of about 100 MeV translates into a mean free path λρ of about 2 fm at high momenta.
- For an energy transfer of 10 GeV (Hermes regime), the condition for the shadowing regime is Q² ≤ 1.5 GeV².

## Context

This invited talk reviews and extends work on in-medium vector meson properties, triggered by the QCD sum-rule prediction of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and the scaling arguments of Brown and Rho. The paper contrasts these early predictions with hadronic models such as those of Friman and Pirner, and Peters et al., which yield broad spectral functions rather than simple mass shifts. The results enable experimental searches using elementary beams (pions at GSI, photons at CEBAF) that can test polarization-dependent effects and avoid the averaging inherent in heavy-ion collisions, and also provide a quantitative explanation for the disappearance of the second resonance region in nuclear photoabsorption observed by Bianchi et al.
