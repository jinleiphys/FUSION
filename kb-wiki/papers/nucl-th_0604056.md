# Multinucleon Mechanisms in Inclusive Subthreshold K+ Production in p+C12 Collisions at 1 GeV

> We analyze recent inclusive data on K+ production in proton induced Lambda-multi nucleon knock out on Carbon 12. We describe collective effects among the incoming p and bound nucleons in a pi, rho rescattering model via the excitation of various baryon resonances. We find the dominance of Lambda N and Lambda NN final states for K+ momenta below 200 MeV/c, while for momenta above 400 MeV/c the data reflect a continuum with an unbound Lambda together with the bound residual nucleus C 12.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed inclusive K+ production in p+C12 collisions at 1 GeV/c, which had been interpreted as requiring collectivity among 6-7 bound nucleons, can instead be explained by a microscopic rescattering model involving only 1-2 nucleons in the continuum at low K+ momenta and a single unbound Lambda at high momenta, with the structure arising from an interplay of phase space, rescattering strength, and momentum sharing constraints.

## Method

The authors model the reaction using a pi and rho meson exchange rescattering framework, where the K+Lambda system is produced directly from the incoming proton via excitation of baryon resonances N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720), while rescattering on bound nucleons in C12 is dominated by Delta(1232) isobar excitation. They represent C12 as s and p shell nucleons in a harmonic oscillator potential with a size parameter from electron scattering, and evaluate the transition amplitude including pi and rho rescattering up to 6th order, allowing knockout of up to six nucleons into the continuum.

## Key numbers

- Proton momentum in the laboratory system: 1 GeV/c
- K+ momentum range analyzed: from 150 MeV/c to above 500 MeV/c
- Dominance of Lambda N and Lambda NN final states for K+ momenta below 200 MeV/c
- For K+ momenta above 400 MeV/c, the data reflect a continuum with an unbound Lambda together with the bound residual nucleus C12
- Harmonic oscillator size parameter for C12 taken from electron scattering: a = 1.67 fm
- Characteristic momentum transfer onto the target estimated as q ~ sqrt(3) * M ~ 1500 MeV/c (where M is the nucleon mass)
- p-shell momentum density peak around 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the analysis of inclusive K+ production data from Koptev (year not given in text) on the reaction p(1 GeV/c) C12 -> K+ X, which had been interpreted as requiring high collectivity among 6-7 bound nucleons. The authors instead apply a microscopic rescattering model similar to approaches used in other near-exclusive high momentum transfer processes, finding that the data can be explained with fewer nucleons. The results are preliminary and the authors call for further data, particularly on the transition to nearly exclusive K+ fusion as studied by Fetisov (year not given), and for covariant model calculations including full retardation.
