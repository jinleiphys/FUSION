# Skyrme-based extrapolation for the static response of neutron matter

> The study of inhomogeneous neutron matter can provide insights into the structure of neutron stars as well as their dynamics in neutron-star mergers. In this work we tackle pure neutron matter in the presence of a periodic external field by considering a finite (but potentially large) number of particles placed in periodic boundary conditions. We start with the simpler setting of a noninteracting gas and then switch to a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach, showing static-response results for five distinct Skyrme parametrizations. We explain both the technical details of our computational approach, as well as the significance of these results as a general finite-size extrapolation scheme that may be used by ab initio practitioners to approach the static-response problem of neutron matter in the thermodynamic limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents a finite-size extrapolation scheme for the static response of neutron matter that uses Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (rather than a noninteracting Fermi gas) to correct for finite-size effects in ab initio many-body methods, with the caveat that the prescription works best when the Skyrme parametrization captures the relevant interaction physics of the system being extrapolated.

## Method

The authors compute the static response function χ(q) of pure neutron matter by placing N particles in a periodic box with a monochromatic external potential v(r) = 2v_q cos(qz), then extracting χ(q) from a quartic polynomial fit to the energy per particle as a function of v_q. They perform these calculations for both a noninteracting Fermi gas and five Skyrme-Hartree-Fock parametrizations (SLy4, SkM, KDE0v1, NRAPR, SKRA) at small particle number (N=66) and large particle number (N=8250), then apply the prescription ΔĒ(TL) = ΔĒ_ab initio(N_small) - ΔĒ_NI(N_small) + ΔĒ_NI(N_big) to extrapolate to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- The noninteracting Fermi gas at density 0.10 fm⁻³ shows a maximum relative error of 29.3% in the linear response function at q/q_F ≈ 1.005 for N=66 particles, compared to 1.03% for N=8250 particles at the same q value
- At q/q_F = 0.503, the relative error is 3.26% for N=66 and 1.67% for N=8250
- At q/q_F = 3.015, the relative error drops to 0.575% for N=66 and 0.0261% for N=8250
- The external potential strengths used are 2v_q/E_F = 0.25, 0.3, 0.35, and 0.5
- The particle numbers used for the extrapolation scheme are N_small = 66 and N_big = 8250

## Context

This work extends the finite-size extrapolation approach previously applied to quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations of neutron matter by Buraczynski et al. (2016, 2017), where a noninteracting Fermi gas prescription produced large discrepancies at low q compared to the compressibility sum rule. The improved Skyrme-based scheme reported in Buraczynski et al. (2021) is here explained in full technical detail, enabling ab initio practitioners (particularly those using QMC or other first-principles methods) to extrapolate static-response results to the thermodynamic limit while accounting for interaction-driven finite-size effects that the free-gas prescription misses.
