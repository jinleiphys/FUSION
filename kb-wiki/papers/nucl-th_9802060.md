# Inclusive Muon Capture in Light Nuclei

> We study total muon capture rates in light nuclei, taking into account renormalizations of the nuclear vector and axial vector strengths. We estimate the influence in the results of uncertainties of the spin-isospin interaction parameter and nuclear densities. A few of these reactions are theoretical benchmarks for physics involving searches for neutrino oscillations. New experiments in muon capture in several targets are suggested, in the light of some discrepancies with theory, crudeness of some experimental results and relevance to neutrino physics.

## Key claim

The authors calculate total muon capture rates in light nuclei (A ~ 6-18) using a local density approximation that includes particle-hole and Delta-hole correlations, finding that the Landau-Migdal parameter g' and nuclear density uncertainties produce theoretical errors of about 6% for 12C, and that the method significantly improves agreement with experiment compared to their earlier work (Mukhopadhyay et al. 1994), though disagreements remain for 7Li and 14N.

## Method

The authors model inclusive muon capture as a reaction between a muon in the 1S atomic state and a proton from a local Fermi sea, producing a neutrino and a neutron in another local Fermi sea, using the local density approximation with nuclear densities from electron scattering. They compute the capture rate by integrating the differential rate over neutrino energy, using a Lindhard function for particle-hole excitations modified by experimental Q-values, and include RPA correlations via a Landau-Migdal interaction that allows Delta-hole excitations on top of particle-hole excitations. The Landau-Migdal parameter g' is varied in the range 0.7 ± 0.1, and nuclear density parameters are varied within experimental errors to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* 12C total muon capture rate: (3.60 ± 0.22) × 10^4 s^-1 (theoretical), compared to experimental world average (3.80 ± 0.10) × 10^4 s^-1
* Varying g' from 0.6 to 0.8 for 12C changes the rate from 3.78 × 10^4 s^-1 to 3.43 × 10^4 s^-1, a ±5% effect around the central value 3.60 × 10^4 s^-1
* Nuclear density uncertainties for 12C produce a ±2% variation, from 3.45 × 10^4 s^-1 to 3.60 × 10^4 s^-1
* 16O total muon capture rate: 1.16 × 10^5 s^-1 (theoretical), compared to experimental (1.026 ± 0.006) × 10^5 s^-1
* 14N total muon capture rate: 8.67 × 10^4 s^-1 (theoretical), compared to experimental (6.93 ± 0.008) × 10^4 s^-1
* 7Li total muon capture rate: 3.41 × 10^3 s^-1 (theoretical), compared to experimental (2.26 ± 0.12) × 10^3 s^-1
* 13C/12C isotope ratio: 0.82 (theoretical), compared to experimental 0.90 ± 0.01 and Primakoff formula prediction 0.71
* 18O/16O isotope ratio: 0.78 (theoretical), compared to experimental 0.86 ± 0.15 and Primakoff formula prediction 0.59
* Q-value range for light nuclei: from 0.67 MeV (14N to 14C) to 21.15 MeV (14C to 14B)

## Context

This work extends the local density approximation method previously applied to inclusive photonuclear reactions (Carrasco et al. 1992), electron scattering response functions (Gil et al. 1997), and deep inelastic scattering (Marco et al. 1996), now applied to muon capture in light nuclei. The authors compare their results with continuum RPA calculations by Kolbe et al. (1994) and HF-RPA calculations by Auerbach et al. (1994), noting that while Kolbe et al. obtain NMC rates within 5% of experiment for 12C, they overestimate the inverse (nu_mu, mu-) cross section by 45-50%; the present method overestimates that cross section by only 15-20%. The work enables improved benchmarks for neutrino oscillation searches at KARMEN and LSND, and suggests new experiments in 6Li, 7Li, 14C, and 14N targets to resolve discrepancies with theory.
