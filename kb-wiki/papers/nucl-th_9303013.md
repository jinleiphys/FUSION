# Angular Momentum Analysis of Spin 1/2 x 1/2 Scattering including Singlet-Triplet Mixing

> The angular momentum (partial wave) reduction of the Lippmann--Schwinger equation describing the interaction of two spin 1/2 particles is extended to the case in which the spin singlet and triplet states are coupled. A straight forward method for obtaining the angular momentum decomposition of the general potential is indicated. The derived formalism is needed to describe the interaction between two nonidentical spin--1/2 particles or between two nucleons when isospin symmetry is violated. The resulting modification of the Stapp phase--shift analysis is given.

## Key claim

The paper extends the partial-wave (angular momentum) reduction of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two spin-1/2 particles to cases where the spin singlet and triplet states are coupled, which is necessary when the two fermions are not identical or when isospin symmetry is violated. The authors note that while prior formalisms (Goldberger and Watson, Stapp, Goddard) assumed the vanishing of the "f" amplitude due to isospin symmetry, their work explicitly includes this term.

## Method

The authors start from the most general form of the scattering T-matrix and potential for two spin-1/2 particles, which includes six complex amplitudes (a through f). They expand both V and T in partial waves using spin-angle functions, then substitute these expansions into the momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Using orthogonality of the spin-angle functions, they derive coupled one-dimensional integral equations: equations (mix1)-(mix2) for singlet-triplet coupling arising from the V_f term, and equations (triplet1)-(triplet2) for coupling within the triplet state from tensor forces. They then evaluate the potential matrix elements in the spin basis using the Madison Convention coordinate system, and invert the angular momentum decomposition via projection onto associated Legendre polynomials to obtain the partial-wave potential matrix elements V^{j(s's)}_{l'l}(k',k).

## Key numbers

- The new term V_f has nonvanishing matrix elements only between singlet and triplet states: ⟨0,0|(σ^p_n - σ^t_n)|1,1⟩ = ⟨0,0|(σ^p_n - σ^t_n)|1,-1⟩ = -i√2
- The relation between on-energy-shell T-matrix elements and phase shifts includes a mixing angle γ̄_l between singlet and triplet states: -2iρ T^{j(ts)}_{j j}(k₀,k₀) = -i sin 2γ̄_l exp[i(δ̄_j + δ̄_jj)]
- The density of states factor ρ = 2k₀ [E_p(k₀) E_t(k₀)] / [E_p(k₀) + E_t(k₀)]

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave analysis of Goldberger and Watson (1964), Stapp, Ypsilantis, and Metropolis (1957), and Goddard (1977), which treated the nucleon-nucleon case where the f amplitude vanishes due to isospin symmetry. The formalism is needed for describing polarized proton scattering from polarized ³He and ¹³C nuclei (Mefford and Landau, in preparation), and for interactions between nonidentical spin-1/2 particles or identical hadrons when isospin symmetry is violated. The authors note that Gersten (1978, 1981) provided a partial-wave analysis but in the helicity representation rather than the angular momentum representation. The work enables calculation of all 36 possible experimental spin observables from the a-f amplitudes using relations from La France and Winternitz (1980).
