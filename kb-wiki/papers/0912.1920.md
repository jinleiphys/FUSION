# A Model Analysis of Triaxial Deformation Dynamics in Oblate-Prolate Shape Coexistence Phenomena

> From a viewpoint of oblate-prolate symmetry and its breaking, we adopt the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian to study dynamics of triaxial deformation in shape coexistence phenomena. It accommodates the axially symmetric rotor model, the $γ$-unstable model, the rigid triaxial rotor model and an ideal situation for the oblate-prolate shape coexistence as particular cases. Numerical solutions of this model yield a number of interesting suggestions. (1) The relative energy of the excited 0+ state can be a signature of the potential shape along the $γ$ direction. (2) Specific E2 transition probabilities are sensitive to the breaking of the oblate-prolate symmetry. (3) Nuclear rotation may induce the localization of collective wave functions in the ($β, γ$) deformation space.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the relative energy of the excited 0+ state and specific E2 transition probabilities can serve as signatures of the potential shape along the γ direction and of oblate-prolate symmetry breaking, but these conclusions are drawn from a simplified (1+3)D model that freezes the β degree of freedom and uses parametrized forms for the collective potential and mass functions.

## Method

The authors reduce the five-dimensional (5D) quadrupole collective Hamiltonian of Bohr-Mottelson type to a (1+3)-dimensional model by fixing the axial deformation parameter β at a constant value β₀² = 0.1, retaining only the γ vibrational degree of freedom and the three rotational Euler angles. They parametrize the collective potential as V(γ) = V₀ sin²3γ + V₁ cos3γ and the collective mass functions as D_γγ(γ) = Dβ₀²(1 + ε cos3γ) and D_k(γ) = D(1 + ε cosγ_k), with D = 50 MeV⁻¹, and solve the resulting collective Schrödinger equation numerically using a discretization method similar to that of Kumar and Baranger (1967).

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the first excited 0+ state at V₀ = 0, denoted E(0₂⁺), is 1.8 MeV for the adopted parameters β₀² = 0.1 and D = 50 MeV⁻¹.
- A level crossing of the 0₂⁺ and 2₂⁺ states occurs at V₀/E(0₂⁺) ≈ 3 when the barrier-height parameter V₀ is varied.
- In the γ-unstable limit (V₀ = 0, V₁ = 0), the ratio E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) equals 2.5; for sufficiently large |V₁| this ratio increases to 3.3.
- The collective potential parameters used for three typical situations are (V₀, V₁) = (-2.0, 1.0) MeV, (1.0, 0.5) MeV, and (10.0, 1.0) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological treatment of shape coexistence by introducing a simple model that interpolates between the axially symmetric rotor model, the γ-unstable model of Wilets and Jean (1956), the rigid triaxial rotor model of Davydov and Filippov (1958), and an ideal oblate-prolate shape coexistence limit. It is intended to be complementary to the microscopic adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method calculations of Hinohara et al. (2008, 2009) for ⁶⁸Se, and enables systematic study of how barrier height, potential asymmetry, and mass asymmetry affect excitation spectra and transition probabilities in triaxial deformation dynamics.
