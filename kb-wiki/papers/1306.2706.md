# Rapid convergence of the Weinberg expansion of the deuteron stripping amplitude

> Theories of $(d,p)$ reactions frequently use a formalism based on a transition amplitude that is dominated by the components of the total three-body scattering wave function where the spatial separation between the incoming neutron and proton is confined by the range of the $n$-$p$ interaction, $V_{np}$. By comparison with calculations based on the CDCC method we show that the $(d,p)$ transition amplitude is dominated by the first term of the expansion of the three-body wave function in a complete set of Weinberg states. We use the \nuc{132}{Sn}(d,p)\nuc{133} {Sn} reaction at 30 and 100 MeV as examples of contemporary interest. The generality of this observed dominance and its implications for future theoretical developments are discussed.

# Rapid convergence of the Weinberg expansion of the deuteron stripping amplitude

## Key claim
The $(d,p)$ transition amplitude is dominated by the first term of the expansion of the three-body scattering wave function in a complete set of Weinberg states, but this dominance is demonstrated only for the specific case of the $^{132}$Sn$(d,p)^{133}$Sn reaction at 30 and 100 MeV, and the authors note that the convergence of higher Weinberg components ($i>1$) was not achieved.

## Method
The authors constructed Weinberg distorted waves $\chi_i^W$ from full continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) wave functions using transformation coefficients $C_{ij}$ that connect the CDCC bin states to the Weinberg states. They then performed one-channel transfer reaction calculations using only the first Weinberg component (called DW$\chi_1$A) and compared these to calculations including higher Weinberg components and to full CDCC results for the $^{132}$Sn$(d,p)^{133}$Sn reaction at 30 and 100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The $^{132}$Sn$(d,p)^{133}$Sn reaction was studied at deuteron incident energies of 30 and 100 MeV (corresponding to 15 and 50 MeV/nucleon).
- The CDCC continuum bins were calculated up to $n$-$p$ relative momenta $k_{max}=1.4$ fm$^{-1}$ for 100 MeV and $k_{max}=0.75$ fm$^{-1}$ for 30 MeV, corresponding to maximum continuum energies of 81.9 and 23.5 MeV, respectively.
- Bins up to a maximum continuum energy of 25 MeV were sufficient for convergence of $\chi_1^W$ for both deuteron incident energies.
- The $i>1$ Weinberg components were about two orders of magnitude smaller than $\chi_1^W$ in the most important radial region for the transfer amplitude, approximately $R \approx 7$ fm.
- The neutron separation energy used was 2.47 MeV, with a Woods-Saxon potential using $r_0=1.25$ fm and $a_0=0.65$ fm.
- The Hulthén potential for $V_{np}$ used parameters $V_0=-84.86$ MeV and $\beta=1.22$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context
This work extends the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) approach of Johnson and Tandy (1974) and the Weinberg states expansion method of Laid et al. (1993) by demonstrating that the first Weinberg component dominates the $(d,p)$ transition amplitude when constructed from full CDCC wave functions. The results suggest that corrections to the adiabatic approximation can be developed by focusing on improving the calculation of only the first Weinberg component, which would be especially important for incident energies of 3-10 MeV per nucleon typical of facilities like TRIUMF, HRIBF at ORNL (where the $^{132}$Sn$(d,p)^{133}$Sn reaction has been measured), and ISOLDE, where closed channels prevent reliable generation of this component using the described scheme.
