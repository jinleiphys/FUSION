# LOCV calculation of the equation of state and properties of rapidly rotating neutron stars

> In this paper, we have investigated the structural properties of rotating neutron stars using the numerical RNS code and the equation of states which have been calculated within the lowest order constrained variational approach. In order to calculate the equation of state of nuclear matter, we have used UV$_{14}$ $+$TNI and AV$_{18}$ potentials. Here, we have computed the maximum mass of the neutron star and the corresponding equatorial radius at different angular velocity. We have also computed the structural properties of Keplerian rotating neutron star for maximum mass configuration, $M_{K}$, $R_{K}$, $f_{K}$ and $j_{max}$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the outer crust structure of a neutron star has a negligible effect on the maximum mass and corresponding radius but a significant effect on the maximum dimensionless spin parameter \(j_{max}\), which lies in the narrow range \(\sim 0.64\)–\(0.7\) for stars with an outer crust, whereas without the outer crust \(j_{max}\) is larger than \(0.7\).

## Method

The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with UV\(_{14}\) + TNI and AV\(_{18}\) two-body potentials to compute the equation of state (EOS) of nuclear matter. They combined these core EOS with the Douchin–Haensel inner crust EOS and the Baym–Pethick–Sutherland outer crust EOS, then solved the Einstein field equations for rapidly rotating neutron stars using the numerical RNS code.

## Key numbers

- For the UV\(_{14}\) + TNI potential at \(f = 716\) Hz, the maximum mass is \(M_{max}/M_{\odot} \simeq 2.0278\) (with outer crust) and \(\simeq 2.0275\) (without outer crust); the corresponding equatorial radius is \(9.8\) km (with crust) and \(9.7\) km (without crust).
- For the AV\(_{18}\) potential at \(f = 716\) Hz, the maximum mass is \(M_{max}/M_{\odot} \simeq 1.653\) (with outer crust) and \(\simeq 1.649\) (without outer crust); the corresponding equatorial radius is \(8.22\) km (with crust) and \(8.11\) km (without crust).
- For the UV\(_{14}\) + TNI potential, the Keplerian frequency is \(f_K \simeq 1.93\) kHz (with crust) and \(\simeq 1.96\) kHz (without crust), with corresponding Keplerian masses \(M_K \simeq 2.36 M_{\odot}\) and \(\simeq 2.40 M_{\odot}\).
- For the AV\(_{18}\) potential, the Keplerian frequency is \(f_K \simeq 2.23\) kHz (with crust) and \(\simeq 2.24\) kHz (without crust), with corresponding Keplerian masses \(M_K \simeq 1.95 M_{\odot}\) and \(\simeq 1.98 M_{\odot}\).
- The maximum spin parameter \(j_{max}\) for neutron stars with outer crust lies in the range \(\sim 0.64\)–\(0.7\) for \(M \geq 0.5 M_{\odot}\); for the UV\(_{14}\) + TNI potential \(j_{max} = 0.682\) (with crust) and \(0.707\) (without crust); for the AV\(_{18}\) potential \(j_{max} = 0.683\) (with crust) and \(0.71\) (without crust).
- The saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_s = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) (UV\(_{14}\) + TNI) and \(0.31\) fm\(^{-3}\) (AV\(_{18}\)); the corresponding incompressibilities are \(K_0 = 261\) MeV and \(301\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier LOCV-based calculations by the same group (Bigdeli et al., previous work) to the case of rapidly rotating neutron stars, and it directly addresses the debate between Lo and Lin (2011) and Qi et al. (2015) about the role of the crust in setting the maximum spin parameter. The results confirm the universal relation \(M_k \simeq (1.203 \pm 0.022) M^{stat}_{max}\) proposed by Breu and Rezzolla (2016) and provide a new fitted formula for the spin parameter as a function of normalized frequency, enabling more precise comparisons with observed quasi-periodic oscillations in systems like Circinus X-1.
