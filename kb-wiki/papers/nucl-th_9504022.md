# Multiple Electromagnetic Excitation in Fast Peripheral Heavy Ions Collisions

> We study the corrections of first order electromagnetic excitation due to higher order electromagnetic interactions. An effective operator is introduced which takes these effects into account in the sudden approximation. Evaluating the matrix-elements of this operator between the relevant states corrections to the first order result are obtained in a simple way. As an example we discuss the excitation of the first excited state in 11Be. It tends to improve the agreement between experiment and theory.

## Key claim

Higher-order electromagnetic interactions can reduce the first-order excitation probability for loosely bound nuclei like 11Be, but this reduction only partly explains the observed discrepancy between measured cross sections and first-order predictions; the authors estimate a reduction of 5.5% to 10.1% for the GANIL experiment, whereas the measured cross section is about 60% lower than the first-order expectation.

## Method

The authors introduce an effective operator in the sudden approximation (valid when the adiabaticity parameter ξ is small) to account for multiple electromagnetic excitations to all orders. They evaluate the reduction factor r as the ratio of the squared sudden-approximation amplitude to the squared first-order amplitude at ξ=0, using simple model wave functions with correct asymptotic behavior for the initial (1/2+) and final (1/2-) states of 11Be. The total cross section is then computed by integrating the product of the reduction factor r(b) and the first-order excitation probability over impact parameters, with a minimum impact parameter b_min=10 fm corresponding to grazing collisions.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be at GANIL (45·A MeV, v/c≈0.32, b=15 fm): z≈0.38, with reduction of cross section by 5.5% (model I) or 10.1% (model II)
- For 11Be at RIKEN (65·A MeV, v/c≈0.38, b=15 fm): z≈0.32, with reduction by 3.7% (model I) or 6.9% (model II)
- The product χ(E1)fi·ξ takes values 0.00375 and 0.00399 (for the two models) under GANIL conditions
- At b=10 fm and GANIL energy, first-order excitation probability is 3.4% and 3.9% for the two models, decreasing with b−2
- The adiabatic cutoff impact parameter is γv/ω≈200 fm
- For 32Mg at 49.2·A MeV with b=15 fm: strength parameter C≈0.37, for which higher-order effects are negligible
- The ratio of E1-E2 amplitude to E1-E1-E1 amplitude is approximately 0.13 for Z=82 at GANIL energy

## Context

This work extends the sudden approximation framework for Coulomb excitation (Ald75) and the low-ξ approximation (Typ94a, Typ94b) to treat multiple electromagnetic excitations in fast peripheral heavy-ion collisions. It addresses a discrepancy observed by Ann94 in 11Be Coulomb excitation at GANIL, where the measured cross section of (191±26) mb was significantly lower than the first-order prediction of (490±50) mb. The approach provides a simpler alternative to coupled-channels calculations and enables estimates of higher-order effects for loosely bound nuclei; the authors note that Bertulani, Canto, and Hussein obtained similar conclusions in a coupled-channel study.
