# Strong Coupling Lattice QCD in the Continuous Time Limit

> We present results for lattice QCD with staggered fermions in the limit of infinite gauge coupling, obtained from a worm-type Monte Carlo algorithm on a discrete spatial lattice but with continuous Euclidean time. This is obtained by sending both the anisotropy parameter $ξ=a_σ/a_τ$ and the number of time-slices $N_τ$ to infinity, keeping the ratio $aT=ξ/Nτ$ fixed. The obvious gain is that no continuum extrapolation $N_τ\rightarrow \infty$ has to be carried out. Moreover, the algorithm is faster and the sign problem disappears. We derive the continuous time partition function and the corresponding Hamiltonian formulation. We compare our computations with those on discrete lattices and study both zero and finite temperature properties of lattice QCD in this regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the continuous time limit of strong coupling lattice QCD, obtained by sending both the anisotropy parameter ξ = aσ/aτ and the number of time-slices Nτ to infinity while keeping the ratio aT = ξ/Nτ fixed, eliminates the need for a continuum extrapolation in the temporal direction, removes the sign problem, and yields a faster algorithm, but the precise value of the renormalization constant κ relating the bare temperature to the physical temperature depends on the choice of extrapolation ansatz and carries a systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors start from the strong coupling limit (β = 0) of lattice QCD with staggered fermions, where the gauge links are integrated out analytically to obtain a dual representation in terms of mesonic dimers, monomers, and oriented baryon loops. They then take the continuous time limit by sending Nτ → ∞ and γ → ∞ (where γ is the bare anisotropy) while keeping the ratio γ²/Nτ fixed, which removes all discretization errors from finite temporal lattice spacing. A worm-type Monte Carlo algorithm is developed to sample the resulting continuous time partition function, which is expressed in terms of spatial dimers and static baryons, with the temperature dependence entering through a high-temperature expansion in the number of spatial dimers.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy correction factor κ = lim_{ξ→∞} ξ/γ² is extrapolated to κ = 0.7824(1) using a Taylor expansion in 1/ξ² (fit range ξ ≥ 2), and to κ = 0.8017(2) using a three-parameter fit with the smallest reduced chi-squared; the systematic error due to the choice of fit ansatz is not quantified.
- For Nc = 3, the three types of vertex weights are v(0|2) = v(2|0) = 1 and v(1|1) = 2/√3.
- The mean-field critical temperature in the 1/d expansion is given by γ_c² = Nτ d (Nc+1)(Nc+2) / [6(Nc+3)], suggesting aT_c ∝ γ_c²/Nτ.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strong coupling lattice QCD that were based on mean-field theory in the 1/d expansion (Kawamoto 1981, Bilic 1991a) or on Monte Carlo simulations with discrete temporal lattices (Rossi 1984, Karsch 1989, de Forcrand 2010). The continuous time formulation improves upon earlier work by one of the authors (Unger 2012) and enables the study of zero and finite temperature properties, including the chiral phase transition, the nuclear liquid-gas transition, and temporal correlation functions, without the ambiguities arising from the anisotropy parameter γ and without the need for a continuum extrapolation in the temporal direction.
