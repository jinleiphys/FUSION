# Behavior of Charmonium Systems after Deconfinement

> We present a study of charmonia in hot gluonic plasma, for temperatures upto three times the deconfinement transition temperature Tc. q \bar{q} systems with quark masses close to the charm mass and different spin-parity quantum numbers were studied on very fine isotropic lattices. The analysis of temporal correlators, and spectral functions constructed from them, shows that the J/psi and eta_c survive up to quite high temperatures, with little observable change up to 1.5 Tc, and then gradually weakening and disappearing by 3 Tc. For the scalar and axial vector channels, serious modifications are induced by the hot medium already close to Tc, possibly dissociating the mesons by 1.1 Tc.

## Key claim

The paper claims that J/ψ and η_c survive as bound states in hot gluonic plasma up to temperatures of about 3 T_c, with little observable change up to 1.5 T_c, while scalar and axial vector charmonia are seriously modified already close to T_c and possibly dissociate by 1.1 T_c; however, these conclusions depend on the Maximum Entropy Method reconstruction of spectral functions from lattice correlators, which introduces systematic uncertainties from the choice of default model and limited temporal data points.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the isotropic Wilson action for gluons and clover-improved Wilson fermions for valence quarks with masses close to the charm quark mass, on very fine isotropic lattices with lattice spacings in the range 0.02–0.05 fm and temperatures up to 3 T_c. They calculated Euclidean temporal correlators for four charmonium channels (pseudoscalar η_c, vector J/ψ, scalar χ_c0, and axial vector χ_c1) and extracted spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) with Bryan's algorithm, employing default models that incorporate the high-energy part of the spectral function from lower-temperature calculations.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ and η_c show little observable change up to 1.5 T_c, then gradually weaken and disappear by 3 T_c.
- Scalar and axial vector channels show serious modifications already close to T_c, possibly dissociating by 1.1 T_c.
- Lattice spacings used: approximately 0.02–0.05 fm (corresponding to inverse lattice spacings of 4.04, 4.86, and 9.72 GeV for β = 6.499, 6.64, and 7.192 respectively).
- Zero-temperature masses from spatial correlators: for set II (close to physical charm), η_c mass = 3.071(6) GeV, J/ψ mass = 3.141(12) GeV, χ_c0 mass = 3.526(53) GeV, χ_c1 mass = 3.622(117) GeV.
- Hyperfine splitting for set II: 70 ± 12 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of charmonia in hot gluonic plasma (Datta et al. 2002, Umeda et al. 2002) that first found J/ψ and η_c surviving well above T_c, in sharp contrast to potential model predictions by Matsui and Satz (1986) and later potential model calculations (Karsch et al., Digal et al.) which predicted J/ψ dissolution around 1.1 T_c. The results enable more reliable predictions for J/ψ suppression as a signal of deconfinement in heavy ion collisions, and provide input for dilepton rate calculations via the relation between the vector spectral function and the differential thermal cross section for dilepton production.
