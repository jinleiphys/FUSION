# Damping Rate of Quasiparticles in Degenerate Ultrarelativistic Plasmas

> We compute the damping rate of a fermion in a dense relativistic plasma at zero temperature. Just above the Fermi sea, the damping rate is dominated by the exchange of soft magnetic photons (or gluons in QCD) and is proportional to $(E-μ)$, where E is the fermion energy and $μ$ the chemical potential. We also compute the contribution of soft electric photons and of hard photons. As in the nonrelativistic case, the contribution of longitudinal photons is proportional to $(E-μ)^2$, and is thus non leading in the relativistic case.

# Damping Rate of Quasiparticles in Degenerate Ultrarelativistic Plasmas

## Key claim

The damping rate of a fermion in a degenerate ultrarelativistic plasma at zero temperature is dominated by the exchange of soft magnetic photons (or gluons in QCD) and is proportional to (E-μ), where E is the fermion energy and μ the chemical potential, but this result holds only when (E-μ) is small compared to the Debye mass M, and the calculation is restricted to the zero-temperature, zero-electron-mass case.

## Method

The authors computed the fermion damping rate from the imaginary part of the self-energy using the imaginary time formalism at finite temperature and chemical potential, then taking the zero-temperature limit. They employed the Braaten-Pisarski resummation method, dressing only the photon propagator with hard dense loops while keeping bare electron propagators and vertices, and separated the calculation into soft (q < q*) and hard (q > q*) momentum transfer regions using an intermediate cutoff q*.

## Key numbers

- The leading contribution to the damping rate from soft transverse photon exchange is γ_soft^T(E) ≈ (e^2 / 24π) (E-μ), where e is the QED coupling and (E-μ) is the fermion energy above the Fermi surface.
- The soft longitudinal photon contribution is γ_soft^L(E) ≈ (e^2 M^2 / 16π) (E-μ)^2 (π/(4M^3) - 1/(3q*^3)), which is subleading compared to the transverse contribution for small (E-μ).
- The Debye mass M is given by M^2 = (e^2/π^2)(μ^2 + π^2T^2/3), which at T=0 reduces to M^2 = (e^2 μ^2)/π^2.
- The total damping rate after cancellation of the cutoff dependence is γ(E) ≈ (e^2/24π)(E-μ) + (e^2 M^2/32π)(E-μ)^2 (π/(2M^3) - 1/q_max^3), where q_max ≈ μ is the maximum momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic calculation of the damping rate by Quinn and Ferrell (1958) to the relativistic case, where magnetic photon exchange becomes important unlike in the nonrelativistic case where it is suppressed by powers of (v/c)^2. The authors used the resummation method of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) based on hard dense loops, and their result contrasts with the nonrelativistic case where the damping rate is proportional to (E-μ)^2 due to Debye screening of the Coulomb interaction. The calculation resolves the infrared divergences from magnetic photon exchange using dynamical screening, which had been problematic in earlier work by Pisarski (1993) for the nonzero temperature case, and the results were independently obtained by Ollitrault and Vanderheyden.
