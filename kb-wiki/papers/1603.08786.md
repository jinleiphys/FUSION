# Coherency in Neutrino-Nucleus Elastic Scattering

> Neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering provides a unique laboratory to study the quantum mechanical coherency effects in electroweak interactions, towards which several experimental programs are being actively pursued. We report results of our quantitative studies on the transitions towards decoherency. A parameter ($α$) is identified to describe the degree of coherency, and its variations with incoming neutrino energy, detector threshold and target nucleus are studied. The ranges of $α$ which can be probed with realistic neutrino experiments are derived, indicating complementarity between projects with different sources and targets. Uncertainties in nuclear physics and in $α$ would constrain sensitivities in probing physics beyond the standard model. The maximum neutrino energies corresponding to $α$>0.95 are derived.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the transition from coherent to decoherent neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering using a parameter α (the average cosine of the phase misalignment between nucleon scattering amplitudes), finding that the commonly assumed threshold of Enu < 50 MeV for full coherency is too optimistic, significant decoherency already occurs at Enu = 50 MeV for heavy nuclei, with α values as low as 0.57 for xenon.

## Method

The authors define a coherency parameter α ∈ [0,1] derived from the average phase misalignment angle between nucleon scattering amplitudes in the neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering cross-section. They compute α and an alternative parameter ξ (the ratio of the actual cross-section to the fully coherent cross-section) as functions of neutrino energy Enu, nuclear recoil detection threshold T0, and target nucleus (neutron, argon, germanium, xenon), using the Helm form factor with standard nuclear parameters (s = 0.5 fm, R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm). They then calculate the experimentally accessible averaged values ⟨α⟩ and ⟨ξ⟩ weighted by the product of neutrino flux and cross-section for realistic sources (reactor antineutrinos, solar 8B neutrinos, and decay-at-rest pion neutrinos).

- For DAR-π antimuon neutrinos (half-maxima 37.5–52.6 MeV), ⟨α⟩ = 0.85 for argon, 0.79 for germanium, and 0.71 for xenon at T0 = 0 (Table II).

## Context

This work provides the first quantitative study of the transition from coherency to decoherency in neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering, extending the qualitative understanding that coherency requires Enu < 50 MeV. The results show complementarity between different experimental programs: reactor and solar 8B neutrino experiments probe the full coherency region (α > 0.9), while DAR-π sources allow measurements in the transition region (0.9 > α > 0.1). The findings are relevant for the TEXONO collaboration's reactor neutrino program (TEXONO Collaboration, 2015), the COHERENT experiment at the Spallation Neutron Source (Scholberg, 2015), and dark matter experiments that must account for neutrino-nucleus scattering as an irreducible background. The paper enables future studies of beyond-Standard-Model physics by identifying the kinematic regimes where nuclear form factor uncertainties and decoherency effects minimally constrain new physics sensitivities.
