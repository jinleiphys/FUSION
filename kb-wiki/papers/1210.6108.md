# Production of hyperon resonances induced by kaon on a deuteron target

> The $K^- d \to πYN$reaction is theoretically studied so as to investigate the $K^{-}$-induced production of the hyperon resonances $Σ(1385)$ and $Λ(1405)$. For this purpose we take into account the p-wave amplitudes for the meson-baryon two-body scatterings as well as the s-wave amplitudes. Due to the fact that the hyperon resonances are selectively produced from the $\bar{K} N$ channel in this reaction, the $Λ(1405)$ peak appears at 1420 MeV, which implies that $Λ(1405)$ and $Σ(1385)$ could be separately seen in the missing mass spectrum of the emitted nucleon in the $K^{-} d \to n X$ reaction. The $πY$ invariant mass spectrum in this study is consistent with experimental data both for $Σ(1385)$ and $Λ(1405)$. The pion exchange contributions are also included and are found to give smooth background without distorting the peak structure of the hyperon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the $K^{-}d \to \pi Y N$ reaction, the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance peak appears at 1420 MeV rather than its nominal 1405 MeV when selectively produced from the $\bar{K} N$ channel, and that this peak can be cleanly separated from the $\Sigma(1385)$ resonance in the missing mass spectrum of the emitted nucleon, provided both $s$- and $p$-wave amplitudes are included in the calculation.

## Method

The authors calculated the $K^{-}d \to \pi Y N$ reaction using a chiral unitary approach for the meson-baryon scattering amplitudes, including both $s$-wave and $p$-wave contributions. They evaluated impulse and double-scattering diagrams for the production of $\Sigma(1385)$ and $\Lambda(1405)$ from the $\bar{K} N$ channel, using a deuteron wave function parameterization and including pion exchange contributions as background. The amplitudes were computed in the laboratory frame with the deuteron at rest, and the $\pi Y$ invariant mass spectra were obtained by integrating over the final-state phase space.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1405)$ peak appears at 1420 MeV in the $\pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}$ invariant mass spectrum, rather than at the nominal 1405 MeV.
- The $\Sigma(1385)$ production cross section in the $K^{-}d \to \pi^{-}\Lambda p$ reaction is calculated as 179 $\mu$b at 800 MeV/$c$ incident $K^{-}$ momentum, compared to the experimental value of $252 \pm 30$ $\mu$b at 778 MeV/$c$.
- The $\Sigma(1385)$ branching ratio to $\pi\Lambda$ is 88%, and the $\Lambda(1405)$ branching ratio to $\pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}$ is 33%.
- The $\Sigma(1385)$ contribution appears as a shoulder around 1390 MeV in the $\pi^{+}\Sigma^{-}$ invariant mass spectrum.
- The pion exchange background was evaluated using a form factor cutoff $\Lambda = 630$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Jido et al. (2009) for $\Lambda(1405)$ production in $K^{-}d$ reactions by including $p$-wave amplitudes to also describe $\Sigma(1385)$ production, and by incorporating pion exchange background contributions. The results are compared with experimental data from Braun (1977) from bubble-chamber experiments at $K^{-}$ momenta between 686 and 844 MeV/$c$. The study enables future experiments at J-PARC to separately identify $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ resonances in the missing mass spectrum, and provides input for understanding strange few-body bound systems such as $\bar{K}NN$ and $\pi\Lambda N$.
