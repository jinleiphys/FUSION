# Pion masses in 2-flavor QCD with $η$ condensation

> We investigate some aspects of 2-flavor QCD with $m_u\not= m_d$ at low-energy, using the leading order chiral perturbation theory including anomaly effects. While nothing special happens at $m_u=0$ for the fixed $m_d\not=0$, the neutral pion mass becomes zero at two critical values of $m_u$, between which the neutral pion field condenses, leading to a spontaneously CP broken phase, the so-called Dashen phase. We also show that the "topological susceptibility" in the ChPT diverges at these two critical points. We briefly discuss a possibility that $m_u=0$ can be defined by the vanishing the "topological susceptibility. We finally analyze the case of $m_u=m_d=m$ with $θ=π$, which is equivalent to $m_u= - m_d=-m$ with $θ= 0$ by the chiral rotation. In this case, the $η$ condensation occurs at small $m$, violating the CP symmetry spontaneously. Deep in the $η$ condensation phase, three pions become Nambu-Goldstone bosons, but they show unorthodox behavior at small $m$ that $m_π^2 = O(m^2)$, which, however, is shown to be consistent with the chiral Ward-Takahashi identities.

# Pion masses in 2-flavor QCD with η condensation

## Key claim

Using leading-order chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) including a determinant term for the anomaly, the authors show that in 2-flavor QCD with non-degenerate quark masses, the neutral pion mass becomes zero at two critical values of m_u (for fixed m_d ≠ 0), between which the neutral pion field condenses into a spontaneously CP-broken Dashen phase; however, nothing special happens at m_u = 0 itself, contrary to the "massless up quark" solution to the strong CP problem.

## Method

The authors analyze the vacuum structure of 2-flavor QCD at low energy using leading-order ChPT with a U(N_f) field and a det U term (rather than the standard (log det U)² term) to incorporate the U(1) axial anomaly. They minimize the effective potential to determine the phase diagram in the m_u–m_d plane, compute pseudo-scalar meson masses by expanding around the vacuum, and derive the topological susceptibility from the U(1) axial Ward-Takahashi identity. They also analyze the degenerate case m_u = m_d = m with θ = π, which is equivalent by chiral rotation to m_u = –m_d = –m with θ = 0.

## Key numbers

- The neutral pion mass squared becomes zero at two critical lines: (m_d + m_u)Δ + m_d m_u = 0 and (m_d + m_u)Δ – m_d m_u = 0 (boundaries between phase A and phase B), and at (m_d – m_u)Δ + m_d m_u = 0 and (m_d – m_u)Δ – m_d m_u = 0 (boundaries between phase B and phase C).
- At m_u = 0 with fixed m_d ≠ 0, the neutral pion mass squared is m_π̃₀² = (|m_d| + 2Δ – √(m_d² + 4Δ²))/(2f²), showing no singularity.
- The topological susceptibility χ in ChPT vanishes at m_u = 0: 2N_f χ = 0.
- The topological susceptibility diverges negatively on all phase boundaries where the neutral pion becomes massless.
- For degenerate 2-flavor QCD at θ = π, the η condensation occurs for m² < 4Δ², and three pions become massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons at m = 0 with m_π² = m²/(2f²Δ) near m = 0, in contrast to the orthodox PCAC relation m_π² = |m|/(2f²).

## Context

This work extends and formalizes earlier arguments by Creutz (1995, 2003, 2005, 2013) that the "massless up quark" solution to the strong CP problem is ill-defined when other quarks are massive, and that a Dashen phase with spontaneous CP breaking exists in non-degenerate QCD. The authors use ChPT to confirm five statements from those works and to derive the phase diagram analytically. The results enable a new definition of m_u = 0 via the condition χ = 0, and predict unorthodox pion mass behavior in degenerate QCD at θ = π that could be tested by lattice QCD simulations, despite sign problems. The phase C (U₀ = ±τ³) is a new finding, though the authors note it may be an artifact of the det U term and may disappear if the (log det U)² term is used instead.
