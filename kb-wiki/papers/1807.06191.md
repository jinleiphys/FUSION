# Collective effects in nuclear collisions: theory overview

> In these proceedings I review recent developments concerning the hydrodynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). I report on the progress towards more realistic simulations and discuss new features about the QGP transport coefficients of the hot (and baryon dense) medium formed in heavy ion collisions. A brief discussion is included on the applicability of hydrodynamics and its extension to the far-from-equilibrium regime.

## Key claim

This review argues that relativistic hydrodynamics remains the successful framework for describing quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy ion collisions, but its regime of applicability is being re-evaluated because the gradient expansion that defines it is now known to diverge, meaning the theory cannot be systematically improved to arbitrary order by adding more gradient terms.

## Method

The paper reviews recent developments in the hydrodynamic description of the QGP presented at Quark Matter 2018, covering three main areas: initial state modeling (including IP-Glasma and EKRT approaches), viscous hydrodynamic evolution based on Israel-Stewart theory with relaxation equations for shear stress, bulk stress, and baryon diffusion, and final state hadronic interactions. The review also discusses calculations of transport coefficients from weak-coupling QCD, holographic models, and hadronic kinetic theory (SMASH), as well as the extension of hydrodynamics to far-from-equilibrium regimes via hydrodynamic attractors and anisotropic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The Knudsen number estimate for heavy ion collisions uses microscopic scale ℓ ∼ 1/T, showing K_N should not be small, especially in small systems (Niemi 2014, Noronha-Hostler 2015)
- Elliptic flow v2{2} in Xe+Xe collisions at √s = 5.44 TeV is larger by 25% than in Pb+Pb at √s = 5.02 TeV in the 0-5% centrality window (Giacalone 2017)
- A new bound from effective kinetic theory gives τπ T / (η/s) ≥ 5 for ultrarelativistic particles (Ghiglieri 2018)
- The holographic KSS result gives η/s = 1/(4π) (Kovtun 2004)
- Bulk viscosity ζ/s with a peak value of ∼ 0.3 was needed when using IP-Glasma initial conditions (Ryu 2015)
- The BEST collaboration EOS covers the region 30 MeV ≤ T ≤ 800 MeV and 0 ≤ μB ≤ 450 MeV (Parotto 2018)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for heavy ion collisions summarized by Heinz (2013) and addresses new connections between nuclear structure and flow measurements, following the prediction by Giacalone (2017) that nuclear deformation affects elliptic flow. The paper discusses how Bayesian analyses (Bernhard 2016) favor saturation-based initial state models, and how the divergence of the gradient series (Denicol 2016, Heller 2016) challenges the traditional definition of hydrodynamics as a systematic expansion in Knudsen number. The review enables future work on extracting QGP transport coefficients from data, modeling low-energy collisions at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan, and developing far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic theories via attractor behavior (Heller 2015, Strickland 2017) and anisotropic hydrodynamics (Alqahtani 2017).
