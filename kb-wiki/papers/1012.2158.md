# Gluon Condensate in Pion Superfluid beyond Mean Field Approximation

> We study gluon condensate in a pion superfluid, through calculating the equation of state of the system in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. While in mean field approximation the growing pion condensate leads to an increasing gluon condensate, meson fluctuations reduce the gluon condensate and the broken scalar symmetry can be smoothly restored at finite isospin density.

## Key claim

While mean field approximation predicts that the gluon condensate increases with growing pion condensate in a pion superfluid, including meson fluctuations beyond mean field reverses this behavior, causing the gluon condensate to decrease and the broken scale symmetry to be smoothly restored at finite isospin density.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the thermodynamic potential, separating it into a mean field part and a fluctuation part from meson modes. The gluon condensate is then extracted via the trace anomaly relation \(\epsilon-3p = -\frac{9}{8}\langle\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}G^2\rangle_{T,\mu}\), using the pressure and energy density obtained from the full thermodynamic potential including quantum fluctuations in the pole approximation.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum gluon condensate taken as \(\langle\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}G^2\rangle_0 = (360\ \text{MeV})^4\) (empirical value from Reinders (1985)).
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5\ \text{MeV}\), coupling constant \(G = 4.93\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), cutoff \(\Lambda = 653\ \text{MeV}\), yielding vacuum pion mass \(m_\pi = 134\ \text{MeV}\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\), and effective quark mass \(M_q = 310\ \text{MeV}\).
- At \(T = \mu_B = 0\), the gluon condensate ratio \(R_g^{MF+FL}\) is constant in the normal phase (\(\mu_I < m_\pi\)) and drops monotonically in the pion superfluid phase (\(\mu_I > m_\pi\)).
- For \(\mu_I = 200\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\mu_B = 600\ \text{MeV}\), the critical temperature \(T_c\) is about \(110\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the mean field study of gluon condensate in pion superfluid by He (2009) and contrasts with earlier mean field results from the Lee-Huang-Yang model and NJL model that showed an increasing gluon condensate at high isospin density. The inclusion of meson fluctuations aligns the behavior of gluon condensate at finite isospin density with its known temperature dependence from lattice QCD and effective models (e.g., Cheng (2008)), showing a gradual decrease and restoration of scale symmetry. This enables more realistic modeling of QCD thermodynamics in isospin-asymmetric matter relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors.
