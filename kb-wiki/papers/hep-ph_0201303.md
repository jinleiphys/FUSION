# Limits on Cosmological Variation of Strong Interaction and Quark Masses from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, Cosmic, Laboratory and Oklo Data

> Recent data on cosmological variation of the electromagnetic fine structure constant from distant quasar (QSO) absorption spectra have inspired a more general discussion of possible variation of other constants. We discuss variation of strong scale and quark masses. We derive the limits on their relative change from (i) primordial Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN); (ii) Oklo natural nuclear reactor, (iii) quasar absorption spectra, and (iv) laboratory measurements of hyperfine intervals.

## Key claim

The paper derives limits on cosmological variation of the strong interaction scale and quark masses from multiple independent datasets, but emphasizes that the most stringent constraints come from the deuteron binding energy's sensitivity to pion mass changes during Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, with the caveat that the sign and magnitude of the derivative relating deuteron binding to pion mass remain theoretically uncertain.

## Method

The authors analyze four independent datasets: (i) primordial Big Bang Nucleosynthesis yields of deuterium and lithium-7, (ii) neutron capture cross sections from the Oklo natural nuclear reactor (active 1.8 billion years ago), (iii) quasar absorption spectra, and (iv) laboratory measurements of hyperfine intervals. They relate changes in hadronic observables to variations of the pion mass parameter δπ (defined as half the relative change of light quark mass to QCD scale) using model potentials for the deuteron and helium-5, and estimate limits by comparing calculated yield variations to observational uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Limit on relative variation of strong scale relative to gravity from BBN: δ(Λ_QCD/M_P)/(Λ_QCD/M_P) < 0.1
- Limit on relative variation of deuteron binding energy from deuterium yield: (δ|E_d|/|E_d|)_d < 0.075
- Limit on relative variation of deuteron binding energy from lithium-7 yield: (δ|E_d|/|E_d|)_Li7 < 0.1
- For the Hamada-Johnston potential model: δ|E_d|/|E_d| ≈ -18 δπ
- For the one-pion-exchange perturbation estimate: δ|E_d|/|E_d| ≈ 3 δπ
- Limit on pion mass modification from helium-5 binding: δπ^(He5) > -0.052
- The lowest neutron resonance in Sm-149 at Oklo: E_0 = 0.0973 ± 0.0002 eV

## Context

This work extends the discussion of cosmological constant variation from electromagnetic fine structure constant measurements (Webb et al. 2001) to strong interaction parameters, motivated by Grand Unification models of Calmet and Fritzsch (2001) and Langacker, Segre and Strassler (2001) which predict correlated variations. The paper provides the first combined analysis of BBN, Oklo, quasar, and laboratory constraints on quark mass and QCD scale variation, and identifies deuteron binding as the most sensitive BBN observable, while noting that definitive calculations require future lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory studies.
