# Why $Ξ(1690)$ and $Ξ(2120)$ are so narrow?

> The $Ξ$ baryons are expected to be naturally narrower as compared to their nonstrange and strange counterparts since they have only one light quark and, thus, their decay involves producing either a light meson and doubly strange baryon or both meson and baryon with strangeness which involves, relatively, more energy. In fact, some $Ξ$'s have full widths of the order of even 10-20 MeV when, in principle, they have a large phase space to decay to some open channels. Such is the case of $Ξ(1690)$, for which the width has been found to be of the order of 10 MeV in the latest {\it BABAR} and BELLE data. In this manuscript we study why some $Ξ$'s are so narrow. Based on a coupled channel calculation of the pseudoscalar meson-baryon and vector meson-baryon systems with chiral and hidden local symmetry Lagrangians, we find that the answer lies in the intricate hadron dynamics. We find that the known mass, width, spin-parity and branching ratios of $Ξ(1690)$ can be naturally explained in terms of coupled channel meson-baryon dynamics. We find another narrow resonance which can be related to $Ξ(2120)$. We also look for exotic states $Ξ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{--}$ but find none. In addition we provide the cross sections for $ \bar K Λ, \bar K Σ\rightarrow πΞ$ which can be useful for understanding the enhanced yield of $Ξ$ reported in recent studies of heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The narrow widths of Ξ(1690) and Ξ(2120) arise not from simple phase-space suppression but from intricate coupled-channel meson-baryon dynamics, specifically the interference and cancellation effects in the pseudoscalar-meson–baryon and vector-meson–baryon systems, which the authors claim can reproduce the experimental masses, widths, spin-parities, and branching ratios without invoking exotic quark-model mechanisms.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel approach for strangeness -2 systems, including pseudoscalar meson–baryon channels (πΞ, ηΞ, K̄Σ, K̄Λ) and vector meson–baryon channels (ρΞ, ωΞ, φΞ, K̄*Σ, K̄*Λ). The vector-meson–baryon interaction was constructed from s-, t-, and u-channel diagrams plus a contact term using hidden-local-symmetry Lagrangians, while the pseudoscalar–baryon amplitudes came from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian. Transition amplitudes between pseudoscalar and vector channels were included via a Kroll-Ruderman term modified for vector-meson dominance.

## Key numbers

- Ξ(1690) mass: BABAR collaboration found M = (1684.7 ± 1.3 +2.2/-1.6) MeV; BELLE collaboration found M = (1688 ± 2) MeV.
- Ξ(1690) width: BABAR found Γ = (8.1 +3.9+1.0/-3.5-0.9) MeV; BELLE found Γ = (11 ± 4) MeV.
- Ξ(1690) spin-parity: both BABAR and BELLE determined J^π = 1/2^-.
- Branching ratio for Ξ^0(1690): B(Ξ^0(1690) → K^- Σ^+) / B(Ξ^0(1690) → K̄^0 Λ) = 0.50 ± 0.26 (BELLE).
- Ξ(2120) mass from earlier experiment: M = 2123 ± 7 MeV with Γ = 25 ± 12 MeV (Gay 1976); another measurement gave M = 2137 ± 4 MeV with Γ ∼ 20 MeV (Vorobev 1979).
- Pseudoscalar decay constants used: f_π = 93 MeV, f_η = 120.9 MeV, f_K = 113.46 MeV, f_ρ = f_ω = 153.45 MeV, f_φ = 168.33 MeV, f_K* = 159.96 MeV.
- Axial coupling constants: F' = 0.46, D' = 0.8, giving g_A = 1.26.

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic-dynamical studies of Ξ baryons by Ramos (1999), Kolomeitsev (2003), Garcia-Recio (2003), and others, who predicted Ξ(1690) as a dynamically generated resonance from meson-baryon interactions. It improves upon the chiral quark model calculation by Xiao (2013), which gave a total width of 25 MeV for Ξ(1690), and the hadronic calculation by Sekihara (2015), which reproduced the mass but found a width of only about 1 MeV. The present results enable predictions for cross sections of K̄Λ → πΞ and K̄Σ → πΞ, which are relevant for understanding enhanced Ξ production observed in heavy-ion collisions by the HADES collaboration, and provide guidance for future experiments at J-PARC and PANDA.
