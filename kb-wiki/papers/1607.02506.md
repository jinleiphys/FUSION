# Production of Energetic Light Fragments in CEM, LAQGSM, and MCNP6

> We extend the cascade-exciton model (CEM), and the Los Alamos version of the quark-gluon string model (LAQGSM), event generators of the Monte-Carlo N-particle transport code version 6 (MCNP6), to describe production of energetic light fragments (LF) heavier than 4He from various nuclear reactions induced by particles and nuclei at energies up to about 1 TeV/nucleon. In these models, energetic LF can be produced via Fermi break-up, preequilibrium emission, and coalescence of cascade particles. Initially, we study several variations of the Fermi break-up model and choose the best option for these models. Then, we extend the modified exciton model (MEM) used by these codes to account for a possibility of multiple emission of up to 66 types of particles and LF (up to 28Mg) at the preequilibrium stage of reactions. Then, we expand the coalescence model to allow coalescence of LF from nucleons emitted at the intranuclear cascade stage of reactions and from lighter clusters, up to fragments with mass numbers A < 8, in the case of CEM, and A < 13, in the case of LAQGSM. Next, we modify MCNP6 to allow calculating and outputting spectra of LF and heavier products with arbitrary mass and charge numbers. The improved version of CEM is implemented into MCNP6. Finally, we test the improved versions of CEM, LAQGSM, and MCNP6 on a variety of measured nuclear reactions. The modified codes give an improved description of energetic LF from particle- and nucleus-induced reactions; showing a good agreement with a variety of available experimental data. They have an improved predictive power compared to the previous versions and can be used as reliable tools in simulating applications involving such types of reactions.

## Key claim

The authors extend the cascade-exciton model (CEM) and the Los Alamos version of the quark-gluon string model (LAQGSM) to describe production of energetic light fragments heavier than ⁴He, but the improvement is conditional: the modified codes show "good agreement with a variety of available experimental data" and "improved predictive power compared to the previous versions," yet the authors acknowledge that none of the currently available simulation tools are able to accurately predict emission of light fragments from arbitrary reactions.

## Method

The authors modified three reaction mechanisms within CEM and LAQGSM: (1) they tested several variations of the Fermi break-up model with different cut-off values (A_Fermi = 12, 14, and 16) and chose the best option; (2) they extended the modified exciton model (MEM) to allow multiple emission of up to 66 types of particles and light fragments (up to ²⁸Mg) at the preequilibrium stage; (3) they expanded the coalescence model to allow coalescence of light fragments from nucleons emitted during the intranuclear cascade, up to fragments with mass numbers A ≤ 7 for CEM and A ≤ 12 for LAQGSM. The improved version of CEM was implemented into MCNP6, and the codes were tested against measured nuclear reactions.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi break-up cut-off value A_Fermi was tested at values of 12, 14, and 16; the authors kept the previous value of 12 after analysis.
- The extended MEM accounts for possible emission of up to 66 types of particles and light fragments, up to ²⁸Mg.
- The coalescence model in CEM allows coalescence up to fragments with mass numbers A ≤ 7; in LAQGSM, up to A ≤ 12.
- CEM03.03 is used as default for reactions induced by nucleons, pions, and photons at energies up to 4.5 GeV; LAQGSM03.03 is used for higher energies up to about 1 TeV/nucleon.
- For the reaction p + ⁹Be at 190 MeV, CEM results showed "reasonably good agreement" with measured ⁶Li and ⁷Be spectra (Fig. 3).
- For p + ¹²C at 1.2/1.9/2.5 GeV, ⁴He spectra at 35 degrees supported the limiting fragmentation hypothesis (Fig. 4).
- In an independent test by Konobeyev and Fischer (2014), CEM03.03 agreed better with experimental data than Bertini, ISABEL, INCL4.2+ABLA, and TALYS for 190 MeV and 300 MeV p + ⁹Be reactions, using nine different deviation factors.

## Context

This work extends the CEM and LAQGSM event generators within MCNP6, which previously described spectra of particles and fragments up to ⁴He well but failed to predict high-energy tails of light fragments heavier than ⁴He. It builds on prior work by Cugnon and colleagues on the Liège intranuclear cascade (INCL) code for emission of light fragments heavier than ⁴He, and on studies by Konobeyev and Korovin (two decades prior) on ⁷Be emission at the preequilibrium stage. The improved codes enable more reliable simulations for applications including electronics performance in space, human radiation dosages, proton and heavy-ion therapy, and accelerator shielding.
