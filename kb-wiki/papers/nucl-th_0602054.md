# Omega attenuation in nuclei

> We study inclusive omega photoproduction in nuclei and propose a measurement of the nuclear transparency ratio as a means to learn about the in-medium properties of the omega meson. To this end we are using the semi-classical coupled-channel BUU transport approach that allows for a consistent treatment of all nuclear effects. The conditions of our calculations are chosen such as to match the setup of existing experimental facilities at ELSA or MAMI C. We show that the observables (total production cross section and transparency) indeed are sensitive to the omega N interaction in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency ratio for inclusive ω photoproduction is sensitive to the ωN interaction in the nuclear medium, but extracting the real part of the ω in-medium self energy requires a complete understanding of the elementary production process, which is not yet available.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical coupled-channel Giessen BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport approach to simulate inclusive ω photoproduction on nuclear targets (12C, 40Ca, 93Nb, 120Sn, 208Pb). They modeled the ωN interaction using parametrized elastic and inelastic cross sections from Lykasov (1998), included collisional broadening via the low density theorem, and considered modifications to the ω decay width through the Gell-Mann-Sharp-Wagner process with an in-medium ρ spectral function. Calculations were performed at a fixed photon beam energy of 1.5 GeV and as a function of energy in the threshold region.

## Key numbers

- Free ω pole mass: m₀ = 782 MeV; vacuum width: Γ₀ = 8.4 MeV
- Collisional width of ω at rest at normal nuclear matter density: 37 MeV
- ω→ρπ decay width in vacuum: 7.5 MeV; at normal nuclear matter density: 31.8 MeV
- Photon beam energy for fixed-energy calculations: Eγ = 1.5 GeV
- Free ω photoproduction threshold: approximately 1.1 GeV
- Elastic ωN cross section parametrization: σ_el = [5.4 + 10 exp(-0.6|q|)] mb, where |q| is ω laboratory momentum in GeV
- Inelastic ωN cross section parametrization: σ_in = [20 + 4/|q|] mb
- Nuclear radius parameter: r₀ = 1.143 fm; normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Attractive mass shift parameter: α = 0.16
- Invariant mass cut applied: 0.75 GeV ≤ M ≤ 0.81 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BUU studies of ρ, ω, and φ meson medium modifications via e⁺e⁻, π⁰γ, and K⁺K⁻ photoproduction (Effenberger 1999, Muhlich 2003, Muehlich 2005). It proposes an attenuation measurement as an alternative to the invariant mass reconstruction approach used by Trnka (2005) at ELSA, following the methodology of early ρ meson attenuation experiments by Alvensleben (1970) and more recent φ photoproduction work by Cabrera (2003) and Muehlich (2005). The results enable experimental extraction of the ωN absorption cross section from nuclear transparency measurements at facilities like ELSA or MAMI C, though a unique mapping to particular medium modifications remains difficult without a complete description of the elementary ω photoproduction process.
