# Chiral symmetry and the properties of hadrons in the Generalised Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> Various aspects of the Generalised Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for QCD in four dimensions are reviewed. The properties of mesonic excitations are discussed in detail, with special attention paid to the chiral pion. The spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the vacuum and the effective chiral symmetry restoration in the spectrum of highly excited mesons and baryons are described microscopically.

## Key claim

The Generalised Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model provides a microscopic description of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the vacuum and effective chiral symmetry restoration in the spectrum of highly excited mesons and baryons, but only within the large-\(N_C\) (Bogoliubov) approximation and for a confining potential with exponent \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 2\) that ensures finite integrals.

## Method

The model is defined by a Hamiltonian with a current-current interaction mediated by an instantaneous confining potential \(V_0(|\mathbf{x}|) = K_0^{\alpha+1} |\mathbf{x}|^\alpha\). A Bogoliubov transformation is performed to dress the quarks, introducing a chiral angle \(\varphi_p\) that parametrises the vacuum. The gap equation for \(\varphi_p\) is derived from minimising the vacuum energy or from the Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator, and mesonic bound states are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rest frame.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate is given by \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle = -\frac{N_C}{\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dp\; p^2 \sin\varphi_p\) (Eq. (chircond)), with the asymptotic behaviour \(\varphi_p \mathop{\approx}\limits_{p\to\infty} -\frac{\pi}{N_C} \Gamma(\alpha+2) K_0^{\alpha+1} \sin\left(\frac{\pi\alpha}{2}\right) \frac{\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle}{p^{\alpha+4}}\) (Eq. (asym)).
* The confining potential is chosen as \(V_0(|\mathbf{x}|) = K_0^{\alpha+1} |\mathbf{x}|^\alpha\) with \(0 \leq \alpha \leq 2\) (Eq. (potential)); the limiting case \(\alpha \to 0\) gives a logarithmic potential \(\tilde{V}_0 = K_0 \ln(K_0 |\mathbf{x}|)\) (Eq. (ln)).
* The vacuum energy functional for \(m=0\) scales as \(E_{\text{vac}}(\xi) = C_1 \xi^{d+1} + C_2 K_0^{\alpha+1} \xi^{d-\alpha}\) (Eq. (Evac11)), where \(d=3\) and \(\xi\) is a scaling parameter; for \(\alpha = d\) a logarithmic term appears (Eq. (Evac12)).

## Context

This review extends the two-dimensional 't Hooft model (Bars and Green, 1977) to four dimensions by generalising the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) to include a non-local, confining interaction. It builds on earlier work by Amer et al. (1983), Le Yaouanc et al. (1983, 1984), and Bicudo et al. (1989–2002) that developed the Hamiltonian formalism and the Bethe-Salpeter approach for mesons. The model enables microscopic study of chiral symmetry breaking and its effective restoration in highly excited hadrons, complementing lattice results by Denissenya et al. (2014) and Glozman et al. (2014).
