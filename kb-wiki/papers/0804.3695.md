# Interpretation of K+K0bar pair production in pp collisions

> A combined analysis of the published data on the pp -> dK+K0bar reaction at excess energies of 47.4 MeV and 104.7 MeV is presented. Evidence is found for both the production of the a0+(980)scalar resonance and for a strong K0bar:d final state interaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that both the production of the a0+(980) scalar resonance and a strong K0bar:d final state interaction contribute significantly to the pp -> dK+K0bar reaction, but their effects tend largely to mask each other in the K+K0bar mass spectrum. The ratio of the K0bar:d to K+d mass distributions depends primarily on the K0bar:d final state interaction and only weakly on the parameters of the a0+(980) resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of published data on the pp -> dK+K0bar reaction at excess energies of 47.4 MeV and 104.7 MeV, using a partial-wave decomposition that respects angular momentum and parity conservation. They fitted the measured differential distributions using a constant-amplitude ansatz with positivity constraints, then introduced multiplicative final-state interaction factors: a Flatte parameterisation for the a0+(980) resonance in the K+K0bar s-wave and a scattering-length approximation for the K0bar:d s-wave interaction.

## Key numbers

- Excess energies of the two data sets: 47.4 MeV and 104.7 MeV, corresponding to proton beam energies of 2.65 GeV and 2.83 GeV respectively.
- In the combined fit with constant coefficients, the K+K0bar subsystem is in an s-wave with probability 84%, the K0bar:d system in an s-wave with probability 65%, and the K+d system in an s-wave with probability 21%.
- The K0bar:d scattering length used is approximately a_Kbar:d = (-1.0 + i1.2) fm.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the combined constant-coefficient fit is 251/100.
- The Flatte parameters used for comparison with the Achasov parameterisation are: g_pi_eta = 0.614, R = 1.650, M_a0 = 1.020 GeV/c^2; for the Oller parameterisation: g_pi_eta = 0.698, R = 1.463, M_a0 = 0.959 GeV/c^2.

## Context

This work extends the separate analyses of Kleber (2004) and Dzyuba (2006) on the pp -> dK+K0bar reaction by performing a combined fit that includes positivity constraints and final-state interaction effects. The strong variation in the K0bar:d to K+d ratio is similar to that found in the K-pp/K+pp ratio measured in the pp -> ppK+K- reaction by Yoshi and collaborators. The results enable future studies of the interplay between the a0(980) resonance and antikaon-nucleon interactions, and suggest that total cross section data closer to threshold would provide greater sensitivity to these effects.
