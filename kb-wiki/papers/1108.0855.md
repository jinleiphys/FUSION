# Correlations of Heavy Quarks Produced at Large Hadron Collider

> We study the correlations of heavy quarks produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions and find them to be quite sensitive to the effects of the medium and the production mechanisms. In order to put this on a quantitative footing, as a first step, we analyze the azimuthal, transverse momentum, and rapidity correlations of heavy quark-anti quark ($Q\overline{Q}$) pairs in $pp$ collisions at $\cal{O}$($α_{s}^{3}$). This sets the stage for the identification and study of medium modification of similar correlations in relativistic collision of heavy nuclei at the Large Hadron Collider. Next we study the additional production of charm quarks in heavy ion collisions due to multiple scatterings, {\it viz.}, jet-jet collisions, jet-thermal collisions, and thermal interactions. We find that these give rise to azimuthal correlations which are quite different from those arising from prompt initial production at leading order and at next to leading order.

## Key claim

The authors find that azimuthal correlations of heavy quark-antiquark pairs are sensitive to medium effects and production mechanisms, but the abstract's claim that these correlations are "quite sensitive" is hedged by the condition that this sensitivity is established only after first quantitatively analyzing pp collisions at NLO pQCD as a baseline, and that the additional production channels in heavy ion collisions (jet-jet, jet-thermal, thermal) produce azimuthal correlations "quite different" from prompt initial production at LO and NLO.

## Method

The authors performed NLO pQCD calculations (using the Mangano-Nason-Ridolfi code) for charm and bottom quark production in pp collisions at LHC energies, analyzing azimuthal, transverse momentum, and rapidity correlations of Q-Qbar pairs. For Pb+Pb collisions, they extended the study to include additional heavy quark production from jet-jet collisions, jet-thermal interactions, and thermal parton scattering, using a Bjorken space-time correlation model with parametrized jet distributions from LO pQCD and CTEQ5M structure functions, with initial temperatures estimated from ALICE multiplicity data.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass fixed at m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass at m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Factorization/renormalization scale factor C = 2 for charm quarks, C = 1 for bottom quarks
- Nuclear thickness function for central Pb+Pb collisions: T_AA = 292 fm^-2
- Initial QGP temperature estimated as T_0 = 653 MeV at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV/nucleon and T_0 = 664 MeV at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV/nucleon
- Particle multiplicity density dN/dy = 2850 at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV/nucleon, extrapolated to 3000 at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV/nucleon
- Initial formation time for QGP: tau_i = 0.1 fm/c
- Jet formation time: tau_i = 0.1 fm/c for partons with p_T > 2 GeV
- K-factor of 2.5 used for jet distributions to account for higher order effects
- Flow velocity |v_f| approximately 0.6, leading to modification of charm quark azimuthal correlation for p_T <= 1.2 GeV and bottom quarks for p_T <= 3.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark production in heavy ion collisions by Levai et al. and by the authors themselves, moving beyond the nuclear modification factor R_AA (which Renk and others have shown cannot seriously discriminate between energy loss mechanisms) to use correlations as more discerning probes. The authors set the stage for identifying medium modifications of heavy quark correlations at LHC energies by first establishing the pp baseline at NLO, and they note that additional production mechanisms (jet-jet, jet-thermal, thermal) could necessitate revision of energy loss estimates obtained from R_AA analyses. The work enables future studies using azimuthal, momentum, and rapidity correlations to disentangle LO and NLO processes and to probe flavor dependence of energy loss.
