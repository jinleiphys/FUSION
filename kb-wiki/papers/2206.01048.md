# Universal van der Waals Force Between Heavy Polarons in Superfluids

> We investigate the long-range behavior of the induced Casimir interaction between two spinless heavy impurities, or polarons, in superfluid cold atomic gases. With the help of effective field theory (EFT) of a Galilean invariant superfluid, we show that the induced impurity-impurity potential at long distance universally shows a relativistic van der Waals-like attraction ($\sim 1/r^7$) resulting from the exchange of two superfluid phonons. We also clarify finite temperature effects from the same two-phonon exchange process. The temperature $T$ introduces the additional length scale $c_s/T$ with the speed of sound $c_s$. Leading corrections at finite temperature scale as $T^6/r$ for distances $r \ll c_s/T$ smaller than the thermal length. For larger distances the potential shows a nonrelativistic van der Waals behavior ($\sim T/r^6$) instead of the relativistic one. Our EFT formulation applies not only to weakly coupled Bose or Fermi superfluids but also to that composed of strongly-coupled unitary fermions with a weakly coupled impurity. The sound velocity controls the magnitude of the van der Waals potential, which we evaluate for the fermionic superfluid in the BCS-BEC crossover.

## Key claim

The induced Casimir interaction between two heavy impurities (polarons) in a superfluid universally exhibits a relativistic van der Waals-like attraction scaling as \(1/r^7\) at long distances, arising from two-phonon exchange; this power-law behavior dominates over the previously known Yukawa potential at distances larger than the healing length \(\xi\), and the result holds under the assumptions that the medium is a Galilean invariant superfluid and the impurity is weakly coupled via \(s\)-wave contact interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) of a Galilean invariant superfluid coupled to heavy impurities via contact density-density interactions, expanding the superfluid Lagrangian in gradients of the phonon field. They computed the induced impurity-impurity potential from the exchange of two superfluid phonons at leading order in the impurity-medium coupling \(g\), using Feynman diagrams and dimensional regularization, and extended the calculation to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature induced potential scales as \(V(r) = -\frac{43 g^2}{128 \pi^3 m^2 c_s^3} \frac{1}{r^7}\), where \(m\) is the medium particle mass and \(c_s\) is the speed of sound.
* At intermediate distances \(\xi \ll r \ll c_s/T\), the finite-temperature correction scales as \(\Delta V_T(r) \simeq -\frac{g^2}{128 \pi^3 m^2 c_s^3} \frac{128 \pi^6}{135 c_s^6} \frac{T^6}{r}\).
* At longer distances \(r \gg c_s/T\), the potential becomes \(V_T(r) = -\frac{3 g^2}{16 \pi^2 m^2 c_s^4} \frac{T}{r^6}\).
* The ratio of the van der Waals potential to the Yukawa potential is evaluated as a function of the interaction parameter \(-(k_F a)^{-1}\) at fixed \(r = 8\xi\), using experimental data for \(c_s/v_F\) from Hoinka (2017).

## Context

This work extends previous results for the Casimir force in one-dimensional systems (Schecter 2014, Reichert 2019a, Reichert 2019b, Will 2021) to three dimensions, and provides explicit analytic expressions for the induced potential. The universal power-law behavior dominates over the single-Bogoliubov-mode Yukawa potential (Pethick 2008, Nakano 2016, Pascal 2018, Camacho 2018) at distances \(r \gtrsim \xi\), and the EFT formulation applies across the entire BCS-BEC crossover including strongly correlated unitary Fermi gases, enabling experimental detection via Ramsey interferometry or microtrap oscillation frequency shifts.
