# Possibly to Test the Mechanism of Elastic Backward Proton-Deuteron Scattering ?

> The elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the invariant expansion of the reaction amplitude. The relativistic invariant equations for all the polarization observables are presented. Within the impulse approximation the relation of the tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ and the polarization transfer $κ_0$ to $P$-wave components of the deuteron wave function is found. The comparison of the theoretical calculations with experimental data is presented. An experimental verification of the reaction mechanism is suggested by constructing some combinations of different observables.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the inclusion of a small \(P\)-wave component in the relativistic deuteron wave function (DWF) can improve the description of experimental data for the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) and the polarization transfer \(\kappa_0\) in elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering, but the description remains unsatisfactory even with this addition. The authors propose that measuring specific combinations of polarization observables can experimentally verify whether the one-nucleon exchange (ONE) mechanism is the dominant reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors analyze elastic backward \(pD \to Dp\) scattering within a covariant formalism using an invariant expansion of the reaction amplitude into four helicity amplitudes. They calculate all polarization observables within the impulse approximation (ONE model), expressing the helicity amplitudes in terms of both positive-energy (\(S\)- and \(D\)-wave) and negative-energy (\(P\)-wave) components of the relativistic deuteron wave function. The theoretical results for \(T_{20}\) and \(\kappa_0\) are compared with existing experimental data, and a "Magic Circle" relation between these two observables is derived to isolate the \(P\)-wave contribution.

## Key numbers

* The \(P\)-wave probability in the DWF that brings the polarization transfer \(\kappa_0\) closest to experimental data is \(P_V = 0.4\% - 0.5\%\).
* The shape of \(T_{20}\) changes at proton laboratory momenta \(P_{\text{lab}} > 0.2\) GeV/c when \(P\)-wave components are included.
* The "Magic Circle" relation in the \(\kappa_0\)–\(T_{20}\) plane is given by \((\kappa_0^N)^2 / (9/8) + (T_{20}^N + 1/(2\sqrt{2}))^2 / (9/8) = 1 - (4 \Delta^N / \Sigma^N)^2\), where \(\Delta^N\) depends purely on the \(P\)-wave components.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of backward \(pD\) scattering that could not describe data using only the conventional \(S\)- and \(D\)-wave deuteron wave functions, as shown by Ker69. The authors build on the relativistic formalism of Gross (1979, 1982, 1992) and Keister and Tjon (1981, 1982) to include negative-energy \(P\)-wave components. The paper suggests that the effects of \(P\)-waves are analogous to secondary interaction corrections (e.g., triangle graphs with a pion in the intermediate state) studied by Kon77, Kon81, and Lyk. The proposed experimental tests could enable future measurements at facilities like COSY to distinguish between the ONE mechanism and more complex reaction dynamics.
