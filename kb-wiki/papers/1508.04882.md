# Weak decay of Lambda_c^+ for the study of Lambda(1405) and Lambda(1670)

> We study the Lambda_c decay process to pi^+ and the meson-baryon final state for the analysis of Lambda resonances. Considering the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix, color suppression, diquark correlation and the kinematical condition, we show that the final meson-baryon state should be in a pure I=0 combination, when the meson-baryon invariant mass is small. Because the I=1 contamination usually makes it difficult to analyze Lambda resonances directly from experiments, the Lambda_c decay is an ideal process to study Lambda resonances. Calculating the final state interaction by chiral unitary approaches, we find that the piSigma invariant mass distributions have the same peak structure in the all charge combination of the piSigma states related to the higher pole of the two poles of the Lambda(1405). Furthermore, we obtain a clear the Lambda(1670) peak structure in the KbarN and etaLambda spectra.

## Key claim

The weak decay \(\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ MB\) produces the final meson-baryon pair in a pure \(I=0\) combination when the meson-baryon invariant mass is small, because the dominant diagram involves a spectator \(ud\) diquark and the \(sud\) cluster is in a pure \(I=0\) state; the authors argue that this makes the process ideal for studying \(\Lambda\) resonances by suppressing the \(I=1\) contamination that complicates other experiments.

## Method

The authors analyze the \(\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ MB\) decay using a three-step framework: first, the charm quark decays weakly via \(c \to s + u + \bar{d}\) to produce a \(\pi^+\) and a \(sud\) cluster; second, a \(\bar{q}q\) pair is created to hadronize the cluster into a meson-baryon pair; third, the final-state interaction of the \(MB\) pair is calculated using chiral unitary approaches (coupled-channel unitarity with chiral perturbation theory). The invariant mass distributions are computed from the decay amplitude, which includes tree-level production and rescattering via the meson-baryon scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The two poles of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) are obtained at \(1424-26i\) MeV and \(1381-81i\) MeV in the model of Ikeda et al. (2012) that includes next-to-leading-order chiral terms and SIDDHARTA constraints.
* The \(\Lambda(1670)\) pole is obtained at \(1678-20i\) MeV in the model of Oset et al. (2001) using the isospin basis.
* The \(\Lambda(1405)\) peak structure appears around 1420 MeV in the \(\pi\Sigma\) invariant mass distributions.
* The \(\Lambda(1670)\) peak structure appears around 1700 MeV in the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\eta\Lambda\) channels.

## Context

This work extends the approach used for \(\Lambda_b \to J/\psi \Lambda(1405)\) decay by Roca et al. (2015) and Feijoo et al. (2015), where the \(I=0\) dominance was also found, to the \(\Lambda_c\) decay, which has larger production rates and better-known branching fractions. The \(\Lambda_c\) decay is proposed as an alternative to photoproduction experiments (LEPS, CLAS) and proton-induced reactions (HADES) where \(I=1\) contamination and \(\Sigma(1385)\) overlap complicate the analysis. The results enable future experimental studies of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) two-pole structure and the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance using the \(\Lambda_c^+ \to \pi^+ MB\) process, particularly through the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(\eta\Lambda\) channels.
