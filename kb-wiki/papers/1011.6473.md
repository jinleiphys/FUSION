# ADHYDRO --- hydrodynamics-like model for highly anisotropic systems

> A new framework (ADHYDRO) is presented that has been designed for description of highly-anisotropic systems produced at the very early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The structure of ADHYDRO is very much similar to perfect-fluid hydrodynamics, however, ADHYDRO includes dissipation effects. Dissipation is defined by the form of the entropy source that depends on the pressure anisotropy and vanishes for the isotropic systems. With a simple ansatz for the entropy source, that obeys general physical requirements, we obtain the system of equations describing isotropization of the anisotropic systems.

## Key claim
ADHYDRO provides a hydrodynamics-like framework for modeling highly anisotropic systems at the very early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where standard viscous hydrodynamics may be inadequate. The model includes dissipation through an entropy source that depends on pressure anisotropy and vanishes for isotropic systems, but the specific form of the entropy source is an ansatz that obeys general physical requirements rather than being derived from first principles.

## Method
The authors define an energy-momentum tensor with separate transverse and longitudinal pressures, plus an entropy flux proportional to the fluid four-velocity. They impose energy-momentum conservation and entropy production, with the entropy source given by Σ = (1-√x)²/√x · σ/τ_eq, where x is the momentum-space anisotropy parameter (x ≈ (P_⟂/P_∥)^(4/3) for massless partons) and τ_eq is a relaxation time. For boost-invariant one-dimensional systems, they solve the resulting coupled ordinary differential equations numerically for the energy density and entropy density as functions of proper time τ.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.2 fm and initial asymmetry x₀ = 100 used for numerical evolution
- Three relaxation times tested: τ_eq = 0.25 fm, 0.5 fm, and 1.0 fm
- For τ_eq = 0.25 fm, entropy increases by about 70% during the anisotropic stage
- For τ_eq = 0.5 fm, entropy increases by about 85%
- For τ_eq = 1.0 fm, entropy increases by about 105%
- System approaches local equilibrium (x ≈ 1) for τ ≥ 2 τ_eq
- Evolution studied over time interval 0.2 fm ≤ τ ≤ 10 fm

## Context
This work extends earlier formulations of anisotropic (magneto)hydrodynamics by Florkowski (2008, 2009) by relaxing the assumption of isentropic flow. It contrasts with standard Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics (reviewed by Heinz, 2009), which is formally applicable only for small anisotropies, while ADHYDRO is designed for the highly anisotropic early stages where longitudinal pressure is much smaller than transverse pressure. The approach is similar to work by Martinez and Strickland (2010) who derived analogous equations from the Boltzmann equation with relaxation-time approximation, and it enables modeling of the transition from the initial anisotropic state to the perfect-fluid stage in heavy-ion collisions.
