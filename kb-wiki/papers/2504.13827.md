# Extracting the chiral anomaly from $e^+e^-\to 3π$

> The strength of the interaction of three pions and a photon, $F_{3π}$ is predicted by the axial anomaly in terms of the pion decay constant, a relation that is frequently used to constrain low-energy radiative processes involving pions, but only tested experimentally at the $10\%$ level. Here, we present a new avenue to test this prediction, via a fit of a dispersive description of the $γ^*\to3π$ amplitude to data for $e^+e^-\to 3π$. From the global fit to SND, CMD-2, and BaBar data we obtain $F_{3π}=33.1(1.7)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}$, in agreement with the chiral prediction at the level of $5\%$. We also consider dispersive fits to the recent data by Belle II, in which case we observe tensions with the dispersive constraints, the width parameters of $ω$ and $ϕ$, and the chiral anomaly.

## Key claim
The authors extract the chiral anomaly parameter \(F_{3\pi}\) from a dispersive fit to \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) data, obtaining \(F_{3\pi}=33.1(1.7)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\) from a global fit to SND, CMD-2, and BaBar data, which agrees with the chiral prediction at the 5% level. However, fits to the recent Belle II data show tensions with dispersive constraints, the width parameters of \(\omega\) and \(\phi\), and the chiral anomaly, indicating that the Belle II data set may have unresolved systematic issues.

## Method
The authors use a dispersive Khuri-Treiman formalism for the \(\gamma^*\to 3\pi\) amplitude, incorporating \(\pi\pi\) rescattering corrections and \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing effects. The chiral anomaly enters via the subtraction constant \(\alpha_A = C_A/3\) in the global normalization function \(a(q^2)\), which is left as a free fit parameter. The cross section for \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) is computed and fitted to experimental data from SND, CMD-2, BaBar, and Belle II, with systematic uncertainties assessed by varying the order of a conformal polynomial and the phase of \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing.

## Key numbers
- \(F_{3\pi}^\text{global fit} = 33.1(1.7)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\) from the global fit to SND, CMD-2, and BaBar data, corresponding to \(C_A/C_A^\text{WZW} = 1.028(53)\).
- The chiral prediction is \(F_{3\pi}^\text{WZW} = 32.19(10)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\) from Eq. (2), based on the pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92.32(10)\,\text{MeV}\).
- The global fit yields \(a_\mu^{3\pi}|_{\leq 1.8\,\text{GeV}} = 45.91(53)\times 10^{-10}\) for the contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
- For Belle II data, the extracted \(\omega\) width is \(\Gamma_\omega = 8.43(8)\,\text{MeV}\) (with reweighting), compared to the global fit value \(8.711(26)\,\text{MeV}\), a \(3.2\sigma\) deficit.
- For Belle II data, the extracted \(\phi\) width is \(\Gamma_\phi = 3.98(11)\,\text{MeV}\) (with reweighting), compared to the global fit value \(4.270(13)\,\text{MeV}\), a \(2.6\sigma\) deficit.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive formalism developed by Hoferichter et al. (2019, 2023) for \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) to extract the chiral anomaly, rather than using it as a constraint. Previous experimental determinations from Primakoff reactions (Antipov, 1986) and \(\pi^-e^-\to \pi^-e^-\pi^0\) (Giller, 2005) tested the anomaly at the 10% level, while preliminary COMPASS results (Ecker, 2023; Maltsev, 2025) suggest 6% precision. The results enable a consistency check on the chiral constraints used in hadronic light-by-light scattering calculations for the muon \(g-2\), and the tensions with Belle II data motivate further investigation of that data set's systematic uncertainties.
