# Out-of-Equilibrium Photon Production in the Late Stages of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In this work, we assess the importance of non-equilibrium dynamics in the production of photons from the late stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The p$_\mathrm{T}$-differential spectra and $v_2$ of photons from the late hadronic stage are computed within a non-equilibrium hadron transport approach, and compared to the results of a local equilibrium evolution using ideal relativistic hydrodynamics. It is found that non-equilibrium dynamics enhance the late-stage photon production at low p$_\mathrm{T}$ and decreases it at higher p$_\mathrm{T}$ compared to the estimate from hydrodynamics. This same comparison points to a significant increase in the momentum anisotropies of these photons due to non-equilibrium dynamics. Once combined with photons produced above the particlization temperature in the hydrodynamics evolution, the differences between the two approaches appear modest in what concerns the p$_\mathrm{T}$ differential spectra, but are clearly noticeable at low p$_\mathrm{T}$ for the elliptic flow: non-equilibrium dynamics enhance the photon $v_2$ below p$_\mathrm{T}$ $\approx 1.4$ GeV.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium dynamics in the hadronic afterburner enhance the elliptic flow (v2) of late-stage photons at low transverse momentum (pT below approximately 1.4 GeV) compared to estimates from ideal hydrodynamics with thermal rates, even though the effect on the pT-differential spectra is modest once combined with photons from earlier stages.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model coupling the relativistic ideal hydrodynamics code MUSIC (with Trento initial conditions) to the hadronic transport code SMASH. Photon production in the late hadronic stage was computed microscopically in SMASH via 2-to-2 scatterings (πρ → πγ) and bremsstrahlung (ππ → ππγ), and compared to results from extending ideal hydrodynamics with thermal photon emission rates down to lower temperatures (120-140 MeV). The study was performed for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV at impact parameter b = 5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Photon v2 from the SMASH hadronic transport is enhanced by a factor of 1-2 relative to hydrodynamics at both RHIC and LHC in the presented pT range.
- The combined v2 (including photons from T > 150 MeV) from the non-equilibrium setup is enhanced by up to 30% at RHIC and up to 20% at LHC compared to the local-equilibrium setup when photons are emitted down to T = 120 MeV.
- Non-equilibrium dynamics enhance the photon v2 below pT ≈ 1.4 GeV.
- The particlization temperature used for switching from hydrodynamics to transport was T = 150 MeV.
- The initial time for hydrodynamics was τ = 0.4 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to describe photon production in heavy-ion collisions, which have faced the "direct photon puzzle" where measured photon v2 is nearly as large as that of pions (see e.g. PHENIX and ALICE measurements). Prior approaches typically used thermal photon emission rates with hydrodynamics (Paquet 2015, vanHees 2011, Shen 2013, Chatterjee 2011) or fully microscopic calculations (Linnyk 2015). This study provides a consistent comparison between equilibrium and non-equilibrium treatments using the same hadronic model for both, enabling quantification of non-equilibrium effects on late-stage photon observables. The results indicate that proper non-equilibrium dynamics are essential for photon v2 at low pT, while equilibrium treatments may be sufficient for pT spectra.
