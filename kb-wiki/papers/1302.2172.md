# Strength of the Vortex-Pinning Interaction from Real-Time Dynamics

> We present an efficient and general method to compute vortex-pinning interactions - which arise in neutron stars, superconductors, and trapped cold atoms - at arbitrary separations using real-time dynamics. This method overcomes uncertainties associated with matter redistribution by the vortex position and the related choice of ensemble that plague the typical approach of comparing energy differences between stationary pinned and unpinned configurations: uncertainties that prevent agreement in the literature on the sign and magnitude of the vortex-nucleus interaction in the crust of neutron stars. We demonstrate and validate the method with Gross-Pitaevskii-like equations for the unitary Fermi gas, and demonstrate how the technique of adiabatic state preparation with time-dependent simulation can be used to calculate vortex-pinning interactions in fermionic systems such as the vortex-nucleus interaction in the crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper presents a real-time dynamical method to compute vortex-pinning interactions that overcomes the uncertainties in sign and magnitude that plague traditional stationary-energy-difference approaches, particularly for the vortex-nucleus interaction in neutron star crusts where the choice of ensemble (fixed particle number vs. fixed chemical potential) confounds results.

## Method

The authors use adiabatic state preparation with time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii-like equations for the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) to prepare initial states with a vortex at controlled distances from a pinning site. They then observe the dynamical deflection of the vortex as the pinning potential moves at constant subsonic velocity, extracting the force directly from Eq. ## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to calculate pinning forces in neutron star crusts using condensation energy estimates (Anderson 1975, Alpar 1977, 1984), Ginzburg-Landau frameworks (Epstein 1988, Link 1991), and local density approximations with Gogny and Argonne interactions (Pizzochero 1997) that gave results smaller by almost an order of magnitude. It directly addresses the controversy between self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations in cylindrical geometry (Donati 2004, Avogadro 2007, 2008) that disagree on whether pinning is repulsive or attractive, and the local-density-approximation calculations (Donati 2003, 2006) suggesting attractive pinning over substantial regions. The method enables fully 3D unconstrained simulations using time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) that can resolve the vortex-nucleus interaction without the symmetry constraints and ensemble ambiguities of stationary approaches.
