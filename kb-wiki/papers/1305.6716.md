# Neutron-rich hypernuclei: Lambda-6H and beyond

> Recent experimental evidence presented by the FINUDA Collaboration for a particle-stable Lambda-6H has stirred renewed interest in charting domains of particle-stable neutron-rich Lambda hypernuclei, particularly for unbound nuclear cores. We have studied within a shell-model approach several neutron-rich Lambda hypernuclei in the nuclear p shell that could be formed in (pi-,K+) or in (K-,pi+) reactions on stable nuclear targets. Hypernuclear shell-model input is taken from a theoretically inspired successful fit of gamma-ray transitions in p-shell Lambda hypernuclei which includes also Lambda-N <-> Sigma-N coupling (Lambda<->Sigma coupling). The particle stability of Lambda-6H is discussed and predictions are made for binding energies of Lambda-9He, Lambda-10Li, Lambda-12Be, Lambda-14B. None of the large effects conjectured by some authors to arise from Lambda<->Sigma coupling is borne out, neither by these realistic p-shell calculations, nor by quantitative estimates outlined for heavier hypernuclei with substantial neutron excess.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large effects of ΛΣ coupling on binding energies of neutron-rich hypernuclei, previously conjectured by Akaishi and Yamazaki (1999), are not borne out by realistic shell-model calculations; the ΛΣ coupling contributions are modest and do not dramatically enhance binding even with substantial neutron excess.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations within a 0ħω model space for neutron-rich p-shell hypernuclei, using 0pN0sΛ effective interactions constrained by fits to γ-ray transition data from p-shell Λ hypernuclei. They included explicit ΛN ↔ ΣN coupling via 0pN0sΛ ↔ 0pN0sΣ effective interactions derived from the same NSC97 YN interaction models used in s-shell hypernuclear calculations, with the ΣN model-space limited to 0pN0sΣ to maintain the coherent coupling approximation.

## Key numbers

- For Λ⁶H(0⁺_g.s.), the ΛΣ coupling contribution to binding is 821 keV (total) compared to 574 keV in Λ⁴H, a gain of only 247 keV
- The predicted B₂n(Λ⁶H) = 0.1 ± 0.4 MeV, compared to FINUDA's experimental value of 0.8 ± 1.2 MeV
- The predicted BΛ(Λ⁶H) = 3.8 ± 0.2 MeV, compared to FINUDA's 4.5 ± 1.2 MeV
- For Λ⁹He(½⁺), total beyond-mean-field contribution ΔB_g.s._Λ = 0.879 MeV, with ΛΣ total contribution of 0.253 MeV
- For Λ¹⁰Li(1⁻), total ΔB_g.s._Λ = 1.022 MeV, with ΛΣ total of 0.275 MeV
- For Λ¹²Be(0⁻), total ΔB_g.s._Λ = 0.748 MeV, with ΛΣ total of 0.158 MeV
- For Λ¹⁴B(1⁻), total ΔB_g.s._Λ = 0.785 MeV, with ΛΣ total of 0.255 MeV
- Predicted BΛ(Λ⁹He) = 8.37 ± 0.10 MeV; BΛ(Λ¹⁰Li) = 9.44 ± 0.11 MeV; BΛ(Λ¹²Be) = 11.25 ± 0.06 MeV; BΛ(Λ¹⁴B) = 12.05 ± 0.33 MeV
- For Λ⁴⁹Ca, total ΛΣ contribution to binding is only 24 keV
- For Λ²⁰⁹Pb, total ΛΣ contribution to binding is only 53 keV

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of neutron-rich hypernuclei following the FINUDA Collaboration's evidence for particle-stable Λ⁶H, and directly contradicts the earlier predictions of Akaishi and Yamazaki (1999) and Myint et al. (2002) who claimed large ΛΣ coupling enhancements in Λ⁶H. The calculations use the same shell-model methodology as Millener (2012) and are constrained by the γ-ray data compilation of Tamura (2010). The results enable experimental searches at J-PARC using (π⁻,K⁺) and (K⁻,π⁺) reactions on stable targets (⁶Li, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B, ¹²C, ¹⁴N) to test these predictions, and suggest that ΛΣ coupling effects in heavier neutron-rich systems like Λ⁴⁹Ca and Λ²⁰⁹Pb are negligible.
