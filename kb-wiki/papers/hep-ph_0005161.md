# Effective Lagrangian induced by the anomalous Wess-Zumino action and the exotic resonance state with $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ in the $ρπ$, $ηπ$, $η'π$, and $K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K$ channels

> A simple model for the exotic waves with $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ in the reactions $VP\to VP$, $VP\to PP$, and $PP\to PP$ is constructed beyond the scope of the quark-gluon approach. The model satisfies unitarity and analyticity and uses as a "priming" the "anomalous" nondiagonal $VPPP$ interaction which couples together the four channels $ρπ$, $ηπ$, $η'π$, and $K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K$. The possibility of the resonancelike behavior of the $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ amplitudes belonging to the $\{10\}- \{\bar{10}\}$ and $\{8\}$ representations of SU(3) as well as their mixing is demonstrated explicitly in the $1.3-1.6$ GeV mass range which, according to the current experimental evidence, is really rich in exotics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unitarized model based on the anomalous Wess-Zumino effective Lagrangian, using only the nondiagonal VPPP interaction as a "priming" mechanism, can generate resonancelike behavior in exotic \(I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})\) amplitudes in the 1.3-1.6 GeV mass range, but the model cannot simultaneously generate both the 1.4 GeV and 1.6 GeV exotic states that are discussed in the literature.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel model for exotic \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\) waves by summing all s-channel loop diagrams generated by the anomalous Wess-Zumino VPPP interaction, which couples the \(\rho\pi\), \(\eta\pi\), \(\eta'\pi\), and \(K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K\) channels. The unitarized amplitudes are obtained by solving a matrix equation for the auxiliary spinless amplitudes, then projecting onto physical p-wave amplitudes with proper Lorentz structures and angular dependences. Three limiting cases are considered: (i) amplitudes belonging to the \(\{10\}-\{\bar{10}\}\) representation of SU(3) (\(h'=0\)), (ii) amplitudes belonging to the octet representation (\(h=0\)), and (iii) nonet symmetry for pseudoscalars (\(h'=h\)).

## Key numbers

- The effective coupling constant \(\tilde h \equiv F_\pi^3 h\) (with \(F_\pi \approx 130\) MeV) is taken near 0.1 for illustrative calculations, with acceptable range \(|\tilde h| \leq 0.4\)
- The resonancelike enhancements are demonstrated in the mass ranges \(\sqrt{s} \approx 1.3-1.4\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} \approx 1.5-1.6\) GeV
- For comparison, the \(a_2(1320)\) resonance production cross sections are \(\sigma(\rho^0\pi^- \to a_2 \to \rho^0\pi^-) \approx 5.7\) mb and \(\sigma(\rho^0\pi^- \to a_2 \to \eta\pi^-) \approx 2.36\) mb at \(\sqrt{s}=m_{a_2}=1.32\) GeV
- The \(\pi_1\) signal in the charge exchange reaction \(\pi^-p \to \eta\pi^0 n\) at 32, 38, and 100 GeV/c is only 3.5 times smaller than the \(a_2(1320)\) signal intensity
- In \(\pi^-p \to \eta\pi^- p\) at 37 GeV/c and 18 GeV/c, the \(\pi_1\) production intensity is about 15 and 30 times smaller than \(a_2(1320)\) production, respectively

## Context

This work extends the approach of using effective chiral Lagrangians with unitarization (previously applied to \(\pi\pi\) scattering with \(\sigma\) and \(\rho\) resonances by various authors) to exotic \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\) channels. It contrasts with quark-gluon based approaches such as the MIT-bag model, constituent gluon model, flux-tube model, and QCD sum rules. The model provides a mechanism for generating exotic resonances without invoking explicit quark-gluon degrees of freedom, using only the anomalous Wess-Zumino interaction. The results support the plausibility of exotic states in the 1.3-1.6 GeV mass range where experiments have observed signals, and suggest that photoproduction experiments at Jefferson Laboratory and hadroproduction studies with the CERN COMPASS spectrometer could further test these predictions.
