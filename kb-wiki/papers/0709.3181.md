# A gauge invariant chiral unitary framework for kaon photo- and electroproduction on the proton

> We present a gauge invariant approach to photoproduction of mesons on nucleons within a chiral unitary framework. The interaction kernel for meson-baryon scattering is derived from the chiral effective Lagrangian and iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation. Within the leading order approximation to the interaction kernel, data on kaon photoproduction from SAPHIR, CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS are analyzed in the threshold region. The importance of gauge invariance and the precision of various approximations in the interaction kernel utilized in earlier works are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors construct a minimal chiral unitary approach to kaon photoproduction that is exactly gauge invariant and unitary, but they restrict the interaction kernel to leading order (LO) from the chiral effective Lagrangian, noting that higher-order terms are necessary for better agreement with experiment, particularly at energies away from threshold.

## Method

The meson-baryon scattering amplitude is derived from the leading-order chiral effective Lagrangian and iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) to infinite order, generating resonances dynamically. The photon is then coupled to all external and internal lines of the hadronic skeleton (including internal meson and baryon lines in the bubble chain) to ensure gauge invariance, unlike previous works that omitted such contributions. The authors retain the full off-shell dependence of the interaction kernel rather than using the on-shell approximation common in earlier chiral unitary studies.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constants used are D = 0.8 and F = 0.46 for the baryon axial-vector couplings.
* The pseudoscalar decay constant in the chiral limit is denoted f0 (no numerical value given in the text).
* Six meson-baryon channels are included: pПЂ0, nПЂ+, pО·, О›K+, ОЈ0K+, ОЈ+K0.
* The analysis covers data from SAPHIR, CLAS, and CBELSA/TAPS in the threshold region for kaon photoproduction.

## Context

This work extends chiral unitary approaches to photoproduction (e.g., Kaiser (1996), Nacher (1998), Caro-Ramon (1999), Borasoy (2002)) by enforcing exact gauge invariance, which was violated in earlier treatments where the photon was coupled only to the initial state and final-state interactions were added separately. The method enables systematic inclusion of higher-order chiral terms in future work and provides a framework to study the importance of gauge invariance and the on-shell approximation in interpreting kaon photoproduction data from SAPHIR, CLAS, and CBELSA/TAPS.
