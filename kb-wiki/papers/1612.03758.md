# Lambda-nuclear interactions and hyperon puzzle in neutron stars

> Brueckner theory is used to investigate the in-medium properties of a $Λ$-hyperon in nuclear and neutron matter, based on hyperon-nucleon interactions derived within SU(3) chiral effective field theory (EFT). It is shown that the resulting $Λ$ single-particle potential $U_Λ(p_Λ=0,ρ)$ becomes strongly repulsive for densities $ρ$ of two-to-three times that of normal nuclear matter. Adding a density-dependent effective $ΛN$-interaction constructed from chiral $ΛNN$ three-body forces increases the repulsion further. Consequences of these findings for neutron stars are discussed. It is argued that for hyperon-nuclear interactions with properties such as those deduced from the SU(3) EFT potentials, the onset for hyperon formation in the core of neutron stars is expected to be shifted to extremely high baryon density, thus potentially resolving the so-called hyperon puzzle.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars could be resolved if the actual hyperon-nucleon interaction resembles the one predicted by SU(3) chiral effective field theory, where the diagonal ΛN interaction is weak and the ΛN–ΣN channel coupling is strong, rather than the conventional one-boson-exchange models. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the specific nature of the interaction being realized in nature, and the authors state that the onset for hyperon formation "could be shifted to much higher density" rather than definitively resolved.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner G-matrix calculations for a Λ hyperon in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, using hyperon-nucleon (YN) two-body potentials derived from SU(3) chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order (NLO) with cutoffs of 450 MeV and 500 MeV. They solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently for the Λ single-particle potential U_Λ(p_Λ=0,ρ) as a function of density, and then added a density-dependent effective ΛN interaction derived from leading chiral ΛNN three-body forces, using decuplet saturation and a negative value for the contact-term parameter H' = -1/f_π².

## Key numbers

- The Λ single-particle potential at saturation density is U_Λ(0,ρ₀) ≈ -30 MeV for both cutoff choices, consistent with empirical hypernuclear data.
- At densities ρ ≈ 2ρ₀, the Λ single-particle potential U_Λ(0,ρ) turns over to net repulsion when using only the chiral EFT two-body interactions.
- Including the density-dependent ΛN interaction from three-body forces increases the repulsion further, with effects becoming noticeable already at ρ = ρ₀ and significant at higher densities.
- The nucleon single-particle potential U_N(p_N,ρ) was taken from calculations using the Argonne v₁₈ potential, with results available up to high densities; the difference in U_Λ using different U_N parameterizations is less than 1 MeV at ρ₀.
- The Fermi momentum at saturation density is k_F ≈ 1.34 fm⁻¹ in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier G-matrix studies by Haidenbauer (2014) and Petschauer (2016) that focused on densities around normal nuclear matter density, now reaching densities of (2-3)ρ₀ relevant for neutron star cores. It contrasts with conventional one-boson-exchange YN models such as the Jülich '04 potential (Haidenbauer 2005) and the Nijmegen NSC97f potential (Rijken 1999), which produce persistently attractive Λ potentials at high densities. The results enable a possible resolution of the hyperon puzzle without invoking ad-hoc mechanisms like multi-Pomeron exchange (Yamamoto 2014) or adjusted repulsive ΛNN three-body forces (Lonardoni 2015, Vidana 2010), provided the chiral EFT picture of the YN interaction is correct.
