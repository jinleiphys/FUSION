# Axial-vector nucleon-to-delta transition form factors using the complex-mass renormalization scheme

> We investigate the axial-vector nucleon-to-delta transition form factors in the framework of relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory at the one-loop order using the complex-mass renormalization scheme. We determine the available six free parameters by fitting to an empirical parametrization of the form factors obtained from the BNL neutrino bubble chamber experiments. A unique feature of our calculation is the prediction of a non-vanishing form factor $C_3^A(Q^2)$. Moreover, our results show a surprising sensitivity to the coupling constant $\texttt{g}_1$ of the leading-order Lagrangian ${\cal L}^{(1)}_{πΔ}$.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the axial-vector nucleon-to-delta transition form factors at one-loop order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using the complex-mass renormalization scheme, finding that the form factor \(C_3^A(Q^2)\) is predicted to be non-vanishing and that the results show a surprising sensitivity to the coupling constant \(\texttt{g}_1\) of the leading-order \(\pi\Delta\) Lagrangian, though the total results for \(C_5^A(Q^2)\) differ by less than 1% between the two \(\texttt{g}_1\) values considered.

## Method

The authors compute the axial-vector nucleon-to-delta transition form factors \(C_3^A\), \(C_4^A\), \(C_5^A\), and \(C_6^A\) in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order (up to and including \(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\)) using the complex-mass renormalization scheme, where the delta resonance is treated as an unstable particle with complex pole mass \(z_\Delta = m_\Delta - i\Gamma_\Delta/2\). They evaluate loop diagrams in dimensional regularization at renormalization scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV using the modified minimal subtraction scheme of ChPT, and determine the six free parameters by fitting to an empirical parametrization of the form factors obtained from BNL neutrino bubble chamber experiments, using two different values for the coupling constant \(\texttt{g}_1\) (the quark-model estimate 2.30 and the value \(-1.21\) from a fit to \(\pi N\) phase shifts).

## Key numbers

* The complex pole position of the \(\Delta(1232)\) is \(z_\Delta \approx (1210 - i\,50)\) MeV.
* For \(\texttt{g}_1 = 2.30\), the fitted low-energy constants are \(\beta = 0.335\), \(\gamma = -84.7\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\delta = -3.20\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\epsilon = 59.3\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and \(\zeta = -11.5\).
* For \(\texttt{g}_1 = -1.21\), the fitted low-energy constants are \(\beta = -0.0691\), \(\gamma = -19.8\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\delta = -1.22\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\epsilon = 13.4\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and \(\zeta = -3.40\).
* The mean-square axial transition radius is \(\langle r^2_{AN\Delta}\rangle = 0.345\) fm\(^2\), compared to 0.427 fm\(^2\) from the empirical parametrization.
* The fit to \(C_5^A(Q^2)\) differs by less than 3% from the empirical parametrization in the \(Q^2\) range considered.
* For \(C_6^A(Q^2)\) with \(\texttt{g}_1 = 2.30\), the fit deviates by less than 2.6% from the empirical result for \(Q^2 \lessapprox 0.16\) GeV\(^2\), reaching \(-23\%\) at \(Q^2 = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\); for \(\texttt{g}_1 = -1.21\), the maximal deviation is \(-3.2\%\) at \(Q^2 = 0.3\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the complex-mass scheme approach previously applied to the electromagnetic nucleon-to-delta transition by Hilt et al. (2017) to the axial-vector case at one-loop order, building on earlier chiral effective field theory calculations by Zhu et al. (2002), Geng et al. (2008), Procura et al. (2008), and Unal et al. (2018). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Alexandrou et al. (2006, 2009, 2010) and provide a framework for extracting the \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant \(g_{\pi N\Delta}\) and the generalized Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy, though the authors note that very little empirical information exists on these form factors beyond the BNL neutrino bubble chamber data.
