# A Bayesian approach to chiral extrapolations

> The determination of low-energy constants from data is an important component of most effective field theory programs, including that of chiral perturbation theory. We propose a novel method based on Bayesian probability theory which allows us to address several shortcomings of the standard approach to parameter extraction. Using a toy-model we argue that the Bayesian approach is ideally suited for the application in effective field theories. We also discuss the application to lattice QCD data.

# A Bayesian approach to chiral extrapolations

## Key claim
The authors propose that Bayesian probability theory, combined with marginalization over the order of the effective field theory expansion and the naturalness parameter, provides a more systematic extraction of low-energy constants than standard least-squares fitting, particularly when data are limited and imprecise. The method avoids the need to choose a specific order for the fit and incorporates uncertainties from truncation and naturalness assumptions into the final parameter uncertainties.

## Method
The authors apply Bayes' theorem to express the posterior probability density for the low-energy constants of interest, using a maximum-entropy-motivated Gaussian prior that encodes naturalness with a scale parameter R. They then marginalize over higher-order low-energy constants, the polynomial order P (from 2 to 8), and the naturalness parameter R (from 0.1 to 10), using a constant prior for P and a scale-invariant prior pr(R)=1/R for R. The final parameter estimates and uncertainties are obtained from the first and second moments of the marginalized posterior distribution.

## Key numbers
- For the toy model with data in the range 0 < x ≤ 1/π, the Bayesian method yields a0 = 0.246 ± 0.021 and a1 = 1.63 ± 0.37, compared to the underlying values of approximately 0.25 and 1.57.
- For the extended data range 0 < x ≤ 2/π, the Bayesian method yields a0 = 0.241 ± 0.048 and a1 = 2.23 ± 0.74.
- In the standard chi-squared approach for the first data set, the quadratic fit (P=2) gives a0 = 0.260 ± 0.022 and a1 = 1.31 ± 0.39 with chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.06, while the seventh-order fit (P=7) gives a0 = 1.05 ± 0.792 and a1 = -56.3 ± 56.5 with chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.47.
- For the extended data set, the standard chi-squared approach at P=2 gives a0 = 0.392 ± 0.033 and a1 = -0.387 ± 0.351 with chi-squared per degree of freedom = 5.35.

## Context
This work extends the standard approach to extracting low-energy constants in effective field theories, which typically uses least-squares or maximum-likelihood fitting and suffers from sensitivity to the chosen order of the expansion and the energy range of data. The authors build on their earlier development of Bayesian methods for low-energy constant extraction (Schindler 2008) and apply the technique to a toy model that mimics features common in chiral perturbation theory. The method enables more reliable extraction of low-energy constants from lattice QCD data, particularly for quantities such as the chiral limit value of the nucleon mass and the nucleon sigma term, where the authors note ongoing work with more sophisticated priors incorporating information on the pion decay constant and axial coupling.
