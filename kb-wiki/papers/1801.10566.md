# Schwinger mechanism in the SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with an electric field

> In this work we study the electrized quark matter under finite temperature and density conditions in the context of the SU(2) and SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio models. To this end, we evaluate the effective quark masses and the Schwinger quark-antiquark pair production rate. For the SU(3) NJL model we incorporate in the Lagrangian the 't Hooft determinant and we present a set of analytical expressions more convenient for numerical evaluations. We predict a decrease of the pseudocritical electric field with the increase of the temperature for both models and a more prominent production rate for the SU(3) model when compared to the SU(2).

## Key claim
The authors predict that the pseudocritical electric field for chiral symmetry restoration decreases with increasing temperature in both the SU(2) and SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models, and that the Schwinger quark-antiquark pair production rate is more prominent in the SU(3) model than in the SU(2) model, but this difference is most noticeable at lower temperatures and electric fields near the transition region.

## Method
The authors used the SU(2) and SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models in the mean-field approximation, incorporating the 't Hooft determinant for the SU(3) case, to study quark matter under a constant electric field at finite temperature and chemical potential. They employed an analytical continuation technique from the corresponding magnetized expressions to obtain regularized analytical formulas for the real parts of the effective potential and gap equation, avoiding numerically difficult principal-value integrals. The Schwinger pair-production rate was then computed from the imaginary part of the effective potential, with the effective quark masses determined self-consistently from the gap equations.

## Key numbers
- For the SU(3) NJL model, the parameters used were: cutoff \(\Lambda = 631.4\) MeV, current quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 5.5\) MeV and \(m_s = 135\) MeV, coupling \(G\Lambda^2 = 1.835\), and 't Hooft coupling \(K\Lambda^5 = 9.29\), fitted to reproduce \(f_\pi = 93.0\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, and condensates \(\langle \bar{u}u \rangle^{1/3} = \langle \bar{d}d \rangle^{1/3} = -246.9\) MeV, \(\langle \bar{s}s \rangle^{1/3} = -267.0\) MeV.
- For the SU(2) NJL model, the fitted parameters were: \(\Lambda = 632.66\) MeV, \(G\Lambda^2 = 2.17\), and \(m = 5.38\) MeV.
- At \(T = 0\), the effective masses of the u and d quarks in the SU(3) model show a shift starting at \(eE \sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) due to the difference in their electric charges.
- At \(T = 200\) MeV, the pseudocritical electric field decreases to approximately \(eE_c \sim 0.15\) GeV\(^2\).
- At a fixed electric field \(eE = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\), the Schwinger production rate at \(T \sim 100\) MeV is more than four times greater than the rate at \(eE = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of electrized quark matter in the NJL model by including the 't Hooft determinant for the SU(3) flavor sector and using non-zero current quark masses, in contrast to earlier work by Tatsumi and by Cao. The authors also provide a more systematic regularization scheme using analytical continuation from magnetic-field results, which had been overlooked in some prior studies. The results enable more realistic predictions for Schwinger pair production in heavy-ion collisions, where strong electric fields of order \(eE \sim 0.4\) GeV\(^2\) are predicted by simulations from Deng and from Skokov, and they highlight the importance of the strange quark sector for the production rate.
