# Beyond the Adiabatic Approximation: the impact of thresholds on the hadronic spectrum

> In the adiabatic approximation, most of the effects of quark-antiquark loops on spectroscopy can be absorbed into a static interquark potential. I develop a formalism which can be used to treat the residual nonadiabatic effects associated with the presence of nearby hadronic thresholds for heavy quarks. I then define a potential which includes additional high energy corrections to the adiabatic limit which would be present for finite quark masses. This "improved" potential allows a systematic low energy expansion of the impact of thresholds on hadronic spectra.

# Beyond the Adiabatic Approximation: the impact of thresholds on the hadronic spectrum

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism to treat residual nonadiabatic effects of quark-antiquark loops on heavy quarkonium spectroscopy that cannot be absorbed into a renormalized static interquark potential, and defines an "improved" potential that incorporates high-energy corrections to the adiabatic limit for finite quark masses, enabling a systematic low-energy expansion of threshold effects on hadronic spectra.

## Method

The author constructs a two-channel Hamiltonian that isolates a single hadronic threshold channel (Q\bar q)_α (q\bar Q)_β from the adiabatic potential, then computes the energy shift ΔE_i^{(αβ)} of a Q\bar Q state i via second-order perturbation theory as the difference between the full dynamical treatment and the adiabatic approximation. For finite quark masses, a "local approximation" is introduced for the pair-creation matrix element, and an improved effective potential V^{improved}_{n_f} is defined by replacing the adiabatic denominator br-(ε_α+ε_β) with a form using finite-mass threshold energies ε^{(m_Q)}_α+ε^{(m_Q)}_β and the finite-mass coupling function c^{(m_Q)}_{αβ}(ηr).

## Key numbers

- The string tension renormalization: b_{n_f} = b, the physical string tension, with the net effect of quark loops being to renormalize the string tension rather than change its linear form at large r.
- The running coupling renormalization: α_s^{(0)} → α_s^{(n_f)} = 12π / [(33-2n_f) ln(Q^2/Λ²_QCD)].
- The adiabatic shift ΔV^{adiabatic}_{αβ}(r) has strength of order Λ_QCD and range of order Λ_QCD^{-1} for low-lying thresholds.
- In the m_Q → ∞ limit, the kinetic energy terms ⟨p²⟩_i/(2μ_{Q\bar Q}), b⟨r⟩_i, and q²/(2μ_{αβ}) each vanish like (Λ_QCD/m_Q)^{1/3} Λ_QCD, while ε_α+ε_β is of order Λ_QCD.
- The deviation of ⟨|c_{αβ}(r)|²/|c_{αβ}(0)|²⟩_i - 1 scales as (Λ_QCD/m_Q)^{2/3}, negligible compared to ⟨br/(ε_α+ε_β)⟩_i ∼ (Λ_QCD/m_Q)^{1/3} as m_Q → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the "unquenching the quark model" program of Geiger and Isgur (1990-1997), which showed that most effects of q\bar q loops can be absorbed into a renormalized static potential via the adiabatic approximation. The paper addresses the residual nonadiabatic effects near hadronic thresholds that the earlier adiabatic treatment could not capture, particularly for finite quark masses where the heavy-quark limit breaks down. It enables systematic calculations of threshold-induced mass shifts in charmonium and bottomonium systems, and provides a framework for studying heavy-quark symmetry violations in heavy-light systems.
