# Sterile Neutrinos in a 6x6 Matrix Approach

> Quark-lepton symmetry invites consideration of the existence of sterile neutrinos. Long ago, we showed that this approach predicts large neutrino mixing amplitudes. Using a Weyl spinor approach, we show, in an analytic example, how this, and pseudo-Dirac pairing, can develop within a reduced rank version of the conventional see-saw mechanism, from small intrinsic mixing strengths. We show by numerical examples that mixing of active and sterile neutrinos can affect the structure of oscillations relevant to extraction of neutrino mixing parameters from neutrino oscillation data.

# Sterile Neutrinos in a 6x6 Matrix Approach

## Key claim
Using a reduced-rank version of the conventional see-saw mechanism with a 6x6 mass matrix, mixing of active and sterile neutrinos can produce large neutrino mixing amplitudes and pseudo-Dirac pairing from small intrinsic mixing strengths, which may affect the extraction of neutrino mixing parameters from oscillation data if analyses assume only 2x2 mixing.

## Method
The author constructs a 6x6 mass matrix in the Weyl spinor basis, with active and sterile neutrino fields, where the sterile Majorana mass matrix is taken to have rank less than three (specifically rank one in the analytic example). Rotations in sterile flavor space and CKM-like mixing in the Dirac mass sector are applied, and the eigenvalue problem is solved analytically for the two-flavor case and numerically for the three-flavor case to examine mass eigenstates and mixing patterns.

## Key numbers
- The see-saw scale for the sterile Majorana mass is taken as M ~ 10^16 GeV (GUT scale), with Dirac masses m ~ 10^±3 GeV.
- In the two-flavor analytic solution, the four eigenvalues are given as: μ₁ = +m₀ - a²/M - (a²/m₀M²)(m₀² - a²/2 - b²), μ₂ = -m₀ - a²/M + (a²/m₀M²)(m₀² - a²/2 - b²), μ₃ = -b²/M + O(M⁻³), μ₄ = M + (2a²+b²)/M + O(M⁻³), where m₀² = m₁²cos²θ + m₃²sin²θ.
- Large flavor mixing occurs when m₃/m₁ ~ cotθ, making the ratio α/β from Eq. (14) of order one.
- The model produces two pseudo-Dirac pairs and one very light mostly active neutrino plus one very heavy mostly sterile neutrino in the three-flavor case.

## Context
This work extends the earlier Monte Carlo study by Goldman and Stephenson (GS) that predicted large neutrino mixing amplitudes from random mass matrix entries, and the see-saw mechanism developed by Gell-Mann, Ramond, Slansky, and Stephenson (GMRS). It builds on the concept of pseudo-Dirac neutrinos introduced by Wolfenstein and the reduced-rank sterile mass matrix approach studied by Goldman, Stephenson, and McKellar (SMG) and others such as Mohapatra. The results enable a framework for global multichannel analysis of neutrino oscillation data, suggesting that analyses using only 2x2 mixing may extract invalid parameter values, and that with eV-scale masses, sterile neutrinos could contribute significantly to dark matter.
