# Nucleon Strange Magnetic Moment and Relativistic Covariance

> We calculate the corrections to the strange matrix elements of the nucleon resulting from the breaking of rotational invariance on the light-cone. In the meson cloud model, the strange magnetic moment mu_S is seen to change sign once the spurious form factors arising from this violation are subtracted. The resulting mu_S is small and slightly positive, in agreement with the trend of the recent data from the SAMPLE experiment. The value of the strange magnetic form factor is predicted to be largely Q^2 independent over the range accessible in upcoming experiments.

## Key claim

After correcting for the violation of rotational invariance on the light-cone by subtracting spurious form factors, the strange magnetic moment of the nucleon changes sign from negative to small and positive, becoming largely independent of the cut-off mass parameter in the kaon cloud model.

## Method

The authors used the covariant light-front formulation of Karmanov and Mathiot (1996) to decompose the strange current operator into five form factors, including three unphysical ones arising from Lorentz covariance breaking. They then calculated the contributions to the physical magnetic form factor \(G_M^S\) and the spurious form factor \(B_1^S\) from both the \(\Lambda\) hyperon and kaon loop diagrams in the meson cloud model, using a pseudoscalar \(i\gamma_5\) \(KN\Lambda\) interaction with a monopole vertex function.

## Key numbers

*   The SAMPLE experiment measured \(G_M^S(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2) = +0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19\) nucleon magnetons (n.m.) for the proton at an average \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   For a cut-off mass \(\Lambda_{K\Lambda} = 1\ \text{GeV}\), the physical strange magnetic moment \(\mu_S = G_M^S(0) = +0.010\) n.m., compared to the uncorrected value \(\widetilde{\mu}_S = -0.074\) n.m.
*   At \(\Lambda_{K\Lambda} = 1\ \text{GeV}\), the \(\Lambda\)-interaction contributes \(G_M^{(\Lambda)}(0) = 0.044\) n.m. and the kaon-interaction contributes \(G_M^{(K)}(0) = -0.034\) n.m. to the total \(\mu_S\).

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud model calculations of Melnitchouk and Malheiro (1997) by correcting for the violation of Lorentz covariance inherent in the impulse approximation, a problem previously studied for the neutron's electromagnetic form factors by Karmanov and Mathiot (1996). The resulting small positive \(\mu_S\) contradicts earlier negative estimates from instant-form calculations by Holstein (1990), Koepf, Henley, and Pollock (1992), and others, and agrees with the trend of the SAMPLE data. The prediction of a largely \(Q^2\)-independent strange magnetic form factor can be tested by upcoming parity-violating experiments at Jefferson Lab.
