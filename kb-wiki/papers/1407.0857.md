# Simple regularization scheme for multi-reference density functional theories

> Background: Extensions of single-reference (SR) energy-density-functionals (EDFs) to multi-reference (MR) applications involve using the generalized Wick theorem (GWT), which leads to singular energy kernels that cannot be properly integrated to restore symmetries, unless the EDFs are generated by true interactions. Purpose: We propose a new method to regularize the MR EDFs, which is based on using auxiliary quantities obtained by multiplying the kernels with appropriate powers of overlaps. Methods: Regularized matrix elements of two-body interactions are obtained by integrating the auxiliary quantities and then solving simple linear equations. Results: We implement the new regularization method within the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach and we perform a proof-of-principle angular-momentum projection (AMP) of states in odd-odd nucleus 26Al. We show that for EDFs generated by true interactions, our regularization method gives results identical to those obtained within the standard AMP procedure. We also show that for EDFs that do not correspond to true interactions, it gives stable and converging results that are different than unstable and non-converging standard AMP values. Conclusions: The new regularization method proposed in this work may provide us with a relatively inexpensive and efficient tool to generalize SR EDFs to MR applications, thus allowing for symmetry restoration and configuration mixing performed for typical nuclear EDFs, which most often do not correspond to true interactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a regularization method for multi-reference energy-density-functionals (MR EDFs) that replaces singular projected matrix elements with regularized ones by multiplying the integrand by appropriate powers of the overlap, but the method assumes the MR EDF is regularizable and works only when the regularization power is sufficient to cancel all poles; for functionals with fractional-power density dependence (η=1/6), even the quadratic regularization scheme cannot fully stabilize the solution.

## Method

The authors implement the regularization within the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach using the HFODD solver, applying angular-momentum projection (AMP) to states in the odd-odd nucleus 26Al. They define auxiliary quantities V^(2B,n)_(IMK) by multiplying the two-body energy kernel by the n-th power of the overlap ⟨Ψ|Ψ̃⟩^n before integration, then expand the regularized kernel and the overlap in Wigner D-functions and solve linear equations (for n=1, linear regularization LR) or quadratic equations (for n=2, quadratic regularization QR) to obtain the regularized matrix elements. The method is tested by examining convergence of the lowest I=0 energy and sum-rule residuals as functions of the maximum angular momentum I_max included in the calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the true interaction SV_T (with tensor EDF terms), standard AMP and LR give identical E_(I=0) values stable to a fraction of eV for I_max ≥ 10, with sum rules converging above I_max = 20.
- For the original SV functional (without tensor terms), the LR E_(I=0) converges only at I_max = 20 and differs from the standard AMP value by 2 keV.
- For the density-dependent SIII functional with Slater approximation of Coulomb exchange, standard AMP sum-rule residuals stagger around zero at 50 keV (Skyrme) and 50 eV (Coulomb), while LR stabilizes them; the LR E_(I=0) is shifted down by almost 50 keV compared to standard AMP.
- For the SLy4 functional (fractional-power density dependence with η=1/6), QR lowers the I=0 energy compared to LR but cannot fully stabilize the solution.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to regularize MR EDFs that focused on removing self-pairing effects (Lac09, Ben09), and addresses the singularities arising from the generalized Wick theorem when EDFs are not generated by true interactions. The method enables symmetry restoration and configuration mixing for typical nuclear EDFs that do not correspond to true interactions, and is currently being applied to particle-number restoration within the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian (Raf14). The approach contrasts with the development of density-independent effective interactions (Was12, Sad13, Ben13a) which avoid the singularity problem but have unsatisfactory spectroscopic quality.
