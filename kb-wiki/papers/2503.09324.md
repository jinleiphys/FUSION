# Exploratory study on the masses of odd-$Z$ nuclei and $r$-process simulation based on the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> Nuclear masses of exotic nuclei are important for both nuclear physics and astrophysics. The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) is capable of providing proper descriptions for exotic nuclei by simultaneously including deformation, pairing correlation and continuum effects, and a mass table of even-$Z$ nuclei with $8 \leqslant Z \leqslant 120$ has been developed based on the DRHBc theory. This work employs a methodology to estimate the masses of odd nuclei using neighboring even nuclei's masses and microscopic pairing gaps, and the performance of microscopic pairing gaps are validated by comparing with empirical ones. Combining the DRHBc masses of even-$Z$ nuclei and the estimated masses of odd-$Z$ nuclei, a pseudo DRHBc mass table is developed, with the root-mean-square (rms) deviation from available mass data $σ=1.47$ MeV. Then this mass table is employed in the $r$-process simulation; results show that the differences in the details of pairing gaps do not yield qualitative discrepancy in $r$-process abundances, while the deformation effects can influence the $r$-process path and thus affect the $r$-process abundance. In particular, the nuclear shape transitions can even lead to the discontinuity of the $r$-process path, suggesting that incorporating triaxiality or beyond-mean-field effects would be valuable for further improvement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a pseudo mass table for odd-\(Z\) nuclei, constructed by interpolating from even-\(Z\) DRHBc masses using microscopic pairing gaps, achieves a root-mean-square deviation of 1.47 MeV from experimental data, and that while the details of pairing gaps do not qualitatively alter \(r\)-process abundance patterns, deformation effects, including shape transitions, can significantly influence the \(r\)-process path and even cause discontinuities.

## Method

The authors estimate masses of odd-\(Z\) nuclei by interpolating the binding energies of neighboring even-\(Z\) nuclei from the existing DRHBc mass table, using microscopic average pairing gaps (Eq. 8) as approximations for the three-point odd-even mass differences. They validate this interpolation by comparing pseudo binding energies for even-odd nuclei against self-consistent DRHBc calculations, then construct a full pseudo mass table for \(8 \leq Z \leq 120\) and use it in a classical \(r\)-process model with 16 neutron flows to simulate abundance patterns.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo DRHBc mass table yields a root-mean-square (rms) deviation from available mass data of \(\sigma = 1.47\) MeV when rotational corrections are included.
* For even-odd nuclei, the rms difference between pseudo and real DRHBc binding energies is 0.24 MeV without rotational correction and 0.41 MeV with rotational correction.
* The pseudo mass table contains 9480 bound nuclei in total, of which 2413 have measured masses.
* The rms deviation for one-neutron separation energies \(S_n\) is 0.67 MeV without rotational correction and 0.74 MeV with rotational correction.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table project (Zhang 2022, Guo 2024), which previously covered only even-\(Z\) nuclei, by providing a systematic estimation for odd-\(Z\) nuclei. The pseudo mass table enables \(r\)-process simulations that reveal the importance of deformation effects over pairing-gap details, and the authors suggest that incorporating triaxiality or beyond-mean-field effects (e.g., two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian methods) would be valuable for further improvement.
