# Neutrinoless Quadruple Beta Decay

> We point out that lepton number violation is possible even if neutrinos are Dirac particles. We illustrate this by constructing a simple model that allows for lepton number violation by four units only. As a consequence, neutrinoless double beta decay is forbidden, but neutrinoless quadruple beta decay is possible: $(A,Z) \to (A,Z+4) + 4 e^-$. We identify three candidate isotopes for this decay, the most promising one being Nd-150 due to its high $Q_{0ν4β}$-value of 2 MeV. Analogous processes, such as neutrinoless quadruple electron capture, are also possible. The expected lifetimes are extremely long, and experimental searches are challenging.

# Neutrinoless Quadruple Beta Decay

## Key claim

Lepton number violation by four units is possible even if neutrinos are Dirac particles, which would forbid the more commonly searched-for neutrinoless double beta decay. The authors construct a simple model in which a gauged U(1)_{B-L} symmetry is broken by a scalar with charge 4, leaving a discrete Z_4^L symmetry that protects the Dirac nature of neutrinos while still allowing effective Delta L = 4 operators.

## Method

The authors introduce three right-handed neutrinos to the Standard Model and gauge U(1)_{B-L}, then break this symmetry with a scalar field phi carrying B-L charge 4 and a second scalar chi carrying charge -2. The scalar potential is arranged so that chi does not acquire a vacuum expectation value, preventing Majorana mass terms for neutrinos. Integrating out chi generates an effective dimension-six operator (anti-nu_R nu_R^c)^2 that mediates Delta L = 4 processes. The authors then identify candidate isotopes for neutrinoless quadruple beta decay and related processes using nuclear data charts, and estimate the expected half-lives using dimensional analysis and comparison to known two-neutrino double beta decay rates.

## Key numbers

- Q-value for Nd-150 neutrinoless quadruple beta decay: 2.079 MeV
- Q-value for Zr-96 neutrinoless quadruple beta decay: 0.629 MeV
- Q-value for Xe-136 neutrinoless quadruple beta decay: 0.044 MeV
- Observed two-neutrino double beta decay half-life of Nd-150: 7 x 10^18 years
- Observed two-neutrino double beta decay half-life of Zr-96: 2 x 10^19 years
- Observed two-neutrino double beta decay half-life of Xe-136: 2 x 10^21 years
- Estimated ratio of half-lives for Nd-150: tau_{1/2}^{0nu4beta} / tau_{1/2}^{2nu2beta} approximately 10^46 times (Lambda / 1 TeV)^4, where Lambda is the scale of the effective Delta L = 4 operator
- Natural abundance of Nd-150: 5.6 percent
- Natural abundance of Zr-96: 2.8 percent
- Natural abundance of Xe-136: 8.9 percent
- Q-values for neutrinoless quadruple electron capture candidates: Xe-124 (0.577 MeV), Ba-130 (0.090 MeV), Gd-148 (1.138 MeV), Dy-154 (2.063 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the conceptual framework of lepton number violation by showing that Majorana neutrinos are not required for such violation, contrary to the standard connection established by Schechter and Valle (1982). The authors construct a specific model based on gauged B-L symmetry, building on earlier work by Chen, Mahanthappa, and Ratz (2012) on anomaly-free discrete R-symmetries. The paper identifies three candidate isotopes for neutrinoless quadruple beta decay (Zr-96, Xe-136, Nd-150) that are already familiar from neutrinoless double beta decay searches, and suggests that existing experiments such as NEMO and EXO could potentially constrain this new decay mode. The work opens the possibility of experimental searches for Delta L = 4 processes, which would provide complementary information about the nature of lepton number violation.
