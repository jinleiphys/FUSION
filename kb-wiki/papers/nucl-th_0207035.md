# A computer program for nuclear scattering at intermediate and high energies

> A computer program is presented which calculates the elastic and inelastic scattering in intermediate and high energy nuclear collisions. A coupled-channels method is used for Coulomb and nuclear excitations of E1, E2, E3, M1, and M2 multipolarities, respectively. The program applies to an arbitrary nucleus, specified by the spins and energies of the levels and by reduced matrix elements. For given bombarding conditions, the angular distribution of elastic and inelastic scattered particles and angular distributions of gamma-rays from the excited nucleus are computed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Fortran-77 computer program (DWEIKO) that calculates elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections for nuclear collisions at intermediate and high energies (E_lab ≥ 50 MeV/nucleon) using the eikonal approximation, but the method is explicitly limited to forward scattering angles (θ ≪ 1 radian) and small energy transfers, and the inelastic couplings have no effect on the obtained elastic scattering cross sections because they are calculated separately.

## Method

The program uses eikonal wavefunctions to describe the scattering, solving coupled-channels equations for the time-dependent Coulomb plus nuclear field expanded into multipoles (E1, E2, E3, M1, M2) using a four-point Runge-Kutta procedure. Elastic scattering is calculated purely with the eikonal approximation, while inelastic amplitudes are obtained from a separate semiclassical coupled-channels calculation assuming straight-line trajectories with a Coulomb deflection correction. The optical potential can be specified either as Woods-Saxon functions, as a double-folding M3Y interaction, or via the "t-ρρ" approximation using nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The program is applicable for bombarding energies E_lab ≥ 50 MeV/nucleon
- Typical running time is about 2 minutes on a 1 GHz Intel P4 processor for inclusion of 5 nuclear states
- Memory required to execute with typical data: 8 Mbytes of RAM and 1 MB of hard disk space
- The M3Y interaction parameters are: A = 7999 MeV, B = -2134 MeV, C = -276 MeV·fm³, β₁ = 4 fm⁻¹, β₂ = 2.5 fm⁻¹
- The imaginary part of the optical potential is parameterized as Im U_opt = λ U_M3Y with λ = 0.6–0.8
- Table I provides nucleon-nucleon scattering parameters for energies from 100 to 2200 MeV/nucleon, e.g., σ_pp = 33.2 mb at 100 MeV/nucleon and σ_pp = 47.2 mb at 1000 MeV/nucleon
- Table II provides isospin-averaged nucleon-nucleon parameters for lower energies, e.g., ⟨σ_NN⟩ = 19.6 fm² at 30 MeV/nucleon and ⟨σ_NN⟩ = 6.1 fm² at 85 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approximation methods of Glauber (1959) to a practical computer code for analyzing experiments with stable and unstable nuclear beams at intermediate-energy heavy ion accelerators. The program complements existing low-energy DWBA codes such as FRESCO and DWUCK4, and provides a tool for calculating Coulomb excitation probabilities that builds on the semiclassical formalism of Alder and Winther (1965) and the relativistic Coulomb excitation theory of Winther and Alder (1979). The code enables analysis of elastic and inelastic scattering data for exotic nuclei produced at radioactive beam facilities, where the eikonal approximation is particularly well-suited due to the forward-focused scattering at these energies.
