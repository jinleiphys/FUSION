# The nuclear force problem: Are we seeing the end of the tunnel?

> Embedded in the historical context, we review recent progress in the development of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials based upon chiral effective field theory. A major breakthrough is the construction of the first NN potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (fourth order) of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). The accuracy of this potential concerning the reproduction of the NN data below 290 MeV lab. energy is comparable to the one of phenomenological high-precision potentials. Since NN potentials of order three or less of ChPT are known to be deficient in quantitative terms, the recent advances show that the fourth order of ChPT is necessary and sufficient for a reliable NN potential derived from chiral effective Lagrangians. This recent substantial progress raises hopes that we might be getting closer to a solution of the nuclear force problem at low energies that has plagued the community for more than half a century.

# The nuclear force problem: Are we seeing the end of the tunnel?

## Key claim

The first nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential constructed at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO, fourth order) of chiral perturbation theory (χPT) achieves an accuracy comparable to phenomenological high-precision potentials, showing that fourth order is necessary and sufficient for a reliable NN potential derived from chiral effective Lagrangians. However, the authors caution that based on historical experience, one may be seeing the end of one tunnel but not necessarily the last one.

## Method

The authors constructed an NN potential in momentum space based on chiral effective field theory at N³LO, including 24 contact terms (contributing to partial waves with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 2) and all two-pion exchange contributions up to N³LO, with three-pion exchange shown to be negligible. The potential was multiplied by a regulator function with cutoff Λ = 0.5 GeV, applied in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain the T-matrix, and fitted to reproduce np and pp scattering data below 290 MeV laboratory energy using 29 parameters (26 contact parameters plus three low-energy constants treated as semi-free).

## Key numbers

- The N³LO potential reproduces np data below 290 MeV with χ²/datum = 1.10 (2402 data points), compared to 10.1 at NNLO and 36.2 at NLO
- The N³LO potential reproduces pp data below 290 MeV with χ²/datum = 1.50 (2057 data points), compared to 35.4 at NNLO and 80.1 at NLO
- For np data in the 0-100 MeV bin, N³LO achieves χ²/datum = 1.06 (1058 data points); for 100-190 MeV, 1.08 (501 data); for 190-290 MeV, 1.15 (843 data)
- For pp data in the 0-100 MeV bin, N³LO achieves χ²/datum = 1.05 (795 data points); for 100-190 MeV, 1.50 (411 data); for 190-290 MeV, 1.93 (851 data)
- The regulator cutoff is Λ = 0.5 GeV; the chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λχ ≈ 1 GeV
- The axial-vector coupling constant used is gA = 1.29; the pion decay constant is fπ = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to NN interactions pioneered by Weinberg and developed by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (mid-1990s) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and by Epelbaum et al. who constructed the first momentum-space NN potential at NNLO. The N³LO potential achieves the precision required for conclusive few-body calculations and microscopic nuclear structure predictions, matching the accuracy of phenomenological high-precision potentials like the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla, 1995), while providing well-defined many-body forces consistent with the two-body force under chiral symmetry.
