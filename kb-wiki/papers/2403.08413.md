# Electron scattering on $^4$He from coupled-cluster theory

> We present a coupled-cluster calculation for the electron-$^4$He scattering in the region of the quasi-elastic peak. We show the longitudinal and transverse responses separately, and discuss results within two distinct theoretical methods: the Lorentz integral transform and spectral functions. The comparison between them allows to investigate the role of final state interactions, two-body currents and relativistic effects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that coupled-cluster calculations using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT-CC) and spectral function (SF) approaches can be compared on equal footing for electron-4He scattering, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the response channel: the longitudinal response is well described by LIT-CC with one-body currents alone, while the transverse response requires two-body currents that are not yet included.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations within the singles and doubles approximation (CCSD) using the chiral NNLO_sat Hamiltonian with two- and three-body forces. They computed the longitudinal and transverse nuclear response functions for electron-4He scattering at momentum transfers of 300 and 400 MeV/c using two distinct methods: the Lorentz integral transform (LIT-CC), which treats final state interactions consistently within a nonrelativistic framework, and the spectral function (SF) approach, which factorizes the response into a ground-state spectral function and a free-nucleon interaction vertex, allowing for relativistic extensions.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal response at q = 300 MeV/c: LIT-CC with one-body currents shows very good agreement with experimental data; the SF approach overestimates the quasi-elastic peak by approximately 30% and shifts it toward higher energy transfers by around 15 MeV.
- Transverse response at q = 300 and 400 MeV/c: LIT-CC underestimates experimental data by about 20%, attributed to missing two-body currents.
- At q = 400 MeV/c, relativistic effects (comparing nonrelativistic to semi-relativistic kinematics) become visible, affecting the high-energy tail above the quasi-elastic peak; the difference between fully relativistic and semi-relativistic calculations is of the order of a few percent in the response strength.

## Context
This work extends the coupled-cluster program for neutrino-nucleus cross sections (motivated by DUNE and Hyper-K experiments) by benchmarking against electron scattering data on 4He, a closed-shell nucleus. The comparison between LIT-CC and SF approaches, both using the same chiral Hamiltonian, allows the authors to isolate the roles of final state interactions, two-body currents, and relativistic effects. The analysis builds on prior LIT-CC calculations for dipole responses (Bacca 2013, 2014) and spectral function calculations for 4He (Sobczyk 2022), and the results enable improved theoretical models for neutrino oscillation experiments by clarifying which approximations are valid in different kinematic regimes.
