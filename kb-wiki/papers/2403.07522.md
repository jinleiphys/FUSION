# Holographic spin alignment for vector mesons

> We develop a general framework for studying the spin alignment $ρ_{00}$ for flavorless vector mesons by using the gauge/gravity duality. Focusing on the dilepton production through vector meson decay, we derive the relation between production rates at each spin channel and meson's spectral function, which can be evaluated by holographic models for a strongly coupled system. As examples, we study $ρ_{00}$ for $J/ψ$ and $ϕ$ mesons, induced by the relative motion to a thermal background, within the soft-wall model. We show that $ρ_{00}$ in the helicity frame for $J/ψ$ and $ϕ$ mesons have positive and negative deviations from 1/3 at $T=150$ MeV, respectively, which consequently leads to different properties for their global spin alignments.

## Key claim

The paper develops a holographic framework for computing the spin alignment $\rho_{00}$ of flavorless vector mesons, and shows that at $T=150$ MeV the $J/\psi$ and $\phi$ mesons exhibit positive and negative deviations from 1/3 respectively in the helicity frame, but these results depend on the specific choice of quantization axis and the meson's momentum relative to the thermal background.

## Method

The authors derive a relation between the dilepton production rate for each spin channel and the meson's spectral function, which is obtained from the imaginary part of the retarded current-current correlation function. They compute this correlation function using the gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT) with a generic Maxwell-type bulk action for the vector meson, then apply the QCD soft-wall model with a dilaton field $\Phi(\zeta)=c\zeta^2$ to evaluate the spectral functions for $J/\psi$ and $\phi$ mesons at finite temperature. The spin alignment $\rho_{00}$ is obtained by integrating the spin-dependent dilepton production rates over the meson's invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- For $J/\psi$ meson at $T=150$ MeV, the spin alignment in the helicity frame shows a positive deviation from 1/3, with the deviation more significant at lower temperatures (results shown for $T=0.15$ GeV and $T=0.2$ GeV as functions of momentum $|{\bf p}|$).
- For $J/\psi$ at $T=150$ MeV with $p_T=2$ GeV and rapidity $Y=0$, the azimuthal angle dependence of $\rho_{00}^y$ is fitted by $\rho_{00}^y-1/3=c_1+c_2 \cos(2\varphi)$ with $c_1=0.006125$ and $c_2=-0.01838$.
- For $J/\psi$ at $T=150$ MeV with $p_T=2$ GeV and rapidity $Y=1$, the fit parameters are $c_1=-0.02205$ and $c_2=-0.07892$.
- The vacuum mass of $J/\psi$ is taken as $m_{J/\psi}=3.096$ GeV, with width $\Gamma\approx100$ MeV, and the dilaton parameter $c_{J/\psi}=m_{J/\psi}^2/4\approx2.40$ GeV$^2$.
- The invariant mass integration range for $J/\psi$ is restricted to $2.1$ GeV $\leq M \leq 4.9$ GeV.
- The Hawking temperature is given by $T=1/(\pi\zeta_h)$.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic-statistical model of Becattini (2007) and the quark recombination model of Liang (2004) and Yang (2017), which predicted spin alignments lower than experimental measurements by STAR (2022) and ALICE (2022). The holographic approach provides a non-perturbative framework to systematically study the difference between $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ spin alignments, which had no consistent theoretical explanation. The framework enables future calculations of spin alignment for other vector mesons and can incorporate more realistic holographic QCD models beyond the soft-wall model, such as those by Braga (2016) or Mamani (2022).
