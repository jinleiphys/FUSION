# Reconstruction of baryon number distributions

> The maximum entropy method (MEM) and the Gaussian process (GP) regression, which are both well-suited for the treatment of inverse problems, are used to reconstruct net-baryon number distributions based on a finite number of cumulants of the distribution. Baryon number distributions across the chiral phase transition are reconstructed. It is found that with the increase of the order of cumulants, distribution in the long tails, i.e., far away from the central number, would become more and more important. We also reconstruct the distribution function based on the experimentally measured cumulants at the collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV. Given the sizable error of the fourth-order cumulant measured in experiments, the calculation of MEM shows that with the increasing fourth-order cumulant, there is another peak in the distribution function developed in the region of large baryon number. This unnaturalness observed in the reconstructed distribution function might in turn be used to constrain the cumulants measured in experiments.

## Key claim

The maximum entropy method (MEM) and Gaussian process regression (GP) can reconstruct net-baryon number distributions from a finite number of cumulants, but the reconstruction quality depends on the order of cumulants included and the method used; the MEM produces an artifact with a spuriously large fourth-order cumulant when only odd-order cumulants are used as input, and the GP requires additional boundary conditions to enforce positivity of the distribution.

## Method

The authors used the maximum entropy method (MEM) with Shannon entropy and Lagrange multipliers to reconstruct discrete baryon number distributions from the first two, three, or four cumulants, and separately used Gaussian process regression with a radial basis function kernel, where hyperparameters were determined by minimizing the negative logarithm likelihood. Both methods were applied to cumulants obtained from functional renormalization group (fRG) calculations at T=130 MeV and mu_B=406 MeV with volume V=542 fm^3, and to experimental cumulants from STAR at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

- First four cumulants from fRG at T=130 MeV, mu_B=406 MeV, V=542 fm^3: kappa_1=28.74, kappa_2=31.46, kappa_3=37.54, kappa_4=54.60 (Table I)
- MEM reconstruction from kappa_1 and kappa_2 only yields kappa_3 approximately 0 and kappa_4 approximately 0 (Table I)
- MEM reconstruction from kappa_1, kappa_2, kappa_3 yields an artifact with kappa_4=1.099x10^4 (Table I)
- GP optimized hyperparameters: sigma_c=0.025, l=8.0 (Figure 3)
- GP with sigma_c=0.25 and l=2.0 shows significantly increased variance compared to optimized case (Figure 4)
- Pseudo-critical temperature of chiral crossover at mu_B=406 MeV: T_pc approximately 137 MeV (Section V.A)

## Context

This work extends the use of inverse problem methods (MEM and GP) from their prior applications in spectral function reconstruction (Jarrell 1996, Asakawa 2000, Burnier 2013) and parton distribution functions from lattice QCD (Alexandrou 2020) to the reconstruction of baryon number distributions in QCD phase transition studies. The reconstructed distributions can be used in transport models of heavy-ion collisions (Chen 2022) and may help constrain experimentally measured cumulants from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC, particularly the fourth-order cumulant at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV where sizable errors exist.
