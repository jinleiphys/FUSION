# Gravitational form factors of glueballs in Yang-Mills theory

> This work presents preliminary results of the first determination of the energy-momentum tensor form factors of the scalar glueball, referred to as gravitational form factors (GFFs). The calculation has been carried out in lattice Yang-Mills theory at a single lattice spacing. Using variationally optimized operators, the matrix elements are extracted from ratios of three-point functions to two-point functions. The glueball GFFs and their kinematic dependence are compared to those of other hadrons from previous calculations.

## Key claim

This work presents the first determination of the energy-momentum tensor form factors (gravitational form factors) of the scalar glueball in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, but the results are preliminary and obtained at a single lattice spacing with a limited variational basis.

## Method

The calculation uses lattice Yang-Mills theory with the SU(3) Wilson gauge action on a 24^3 × 48 volume at β=5.95 (a=0.098 fm). Two interpolating operators constructed from Wilson loops are used in a generalized eigenvalue problem to extract the ground-state scalar glueball and construct optimized interpolators. Gravitational form factors A(t) and D(t) are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point functions of the energy-momentum tensor, with renormalization imposed by the sum rule A(0)=1.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume: 24^3 × 48, lattice spacing a = 0.098 fm (set via Sommer parameter)
- Approximately 10^7 gauge configurations generated using 10^5 independent streams
- Two-point functions computed for all |p|^2 ≤ 6 (2π/L)^2, yielding 7 distinct momentum magnitudes
- Three-point functions computed for all |p'|^2 ≤ 6 (2π/L)^2 and |Δ|^2 ≤ 10 (2π/L)^2
- Data for each irrep grouped into 12 bins using k-means clustering on momentum transfer squared t = Δ^2
- Fits to ratios constrained to ts_min ≥ 5, ts_max ≤ 15, τ ≥ 2, τ ≤ ts - 2 with at least 8 data points

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of glueball spectroscopy (Athenodorou 2020, Vadacchino 2023 review) to the internal structure of glueballs via gravitational form factors. The only prior attempt at glueball form factors used SU(2) gauge theory with a plaquette probe (Tickle 1989). The results are compared to gluon gravitational form factors of the pion, rho meson, nucleon, and Delta baryon from a single lattice QCD ensemble with a ≈ 0.12 fm and mπ ≈ 450 MeV (Pefkou 2021). This calculation enables future studies of glueball internal structure in full QCD and investigation of heavier glueball states.
