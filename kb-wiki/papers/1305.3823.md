# Azimuthal correlations of heavy quarks in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC ($\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV)

> In this paper we study the azimuthal correlations of heavy quarks in Pb+Pb collisions with $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV at LHC. Due to the interaction with the medium heavy quarks and antiquarks are deflected from their original direction and the initial correlation of the pair is broadened. We investigate this effect for different transverse momentum classes. Low-momentum heavy-quark pairs lose their leading order back-to-back initial correlation, while a significant residual correlation survives at large momenta. Due to the larger acquired average deflection from their original directions the azimuthal correlations of heavy-quark pairs are broadened more efficiently in a purely collisional energy loss mechanism compared to including radiative corrections. This discriminatory feature survives when next-to-leading order production processes are included.

## Key claim

The azimuthal correlations of heavy-quark pairs are broadened more efficiently by purely collisional energy loss than by a mechanism that includes radiative corrections, and this difference persists even when next-to-leading-order production processes are included. The authors qualify that this discriminatory feature is observed for the specific model parameters (K=1.5 for collisional, K=0.8 for collisional plus radiative) and for the particular Monte Carlo implementation of the energy-loss mechanisms.

## Method

The authors used the MC@sHQ Monte Carlo approach coupled to a 3+1D ideal fluid dynamical medium evolution from the EPOS model, with a lattice-QCD-based equation of state. Heavy quarks (charm with mass 1.5 GeV, bottom with mass 5.1 GeV) were propagated through the medium via a Boltzmann equation, using either purely collisional 2-to-2 processes (with K=1.5 rescaling) or a combination of collisional and radiative 2-to-3 processes including LPM suppression (with K=0.8 rescaling). Initial heavy-quark pairs were generated either from leading-order processes (delta peak at Delta phi = pi) or from a next-to-leading-order Monte Carlo implementation (MC@NLO) with parton shower evolution.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass: 5.1 GeV
- Nucleon-nucleon cross sections at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: dsigma_c/dy at y=0 = 769 microbarns, dsigma_b/dy at y=0 = 25 microbarns
- Global rescaling factor K=1.5 for purely collisional energy loss; K=0.8 for collisional plus radiative energy loss
- Transition temperature for taking heavy quarks locally: T_c = 155 MeV
- Fluid dynamical evolution start time: tau_0 = 0.35 fm
- Minimum fraction of longitudinal momentum for emitted gluons: x = 0.05
- Flux tube radius used to mimic viscous effects: 1 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of azimuthal correlations of heavy-quark pairs (Zhu 2006, Gossiaux 2006, Zhu 2007, Akamatsu 2009, Younus 2013) by systematically comparing collisional and radiative energy-loss mechanisms, and by including both LO and NLO initializations. The authors use an improved version of their MC@sHQ approach with a 3+1D fluid dynamical medium from EPOS (Werner 2010, Werner 2012) and a lattice-QCD equation of state (Borsanyi 2010), replacing an earlier 2+1D fluid description with a first-order phase transition equation of state. The work enables future experimental discrimination between energy-loss mechanisms using heavy-flavor correlation measurements at LHC.
