# Tensor Structure Function b$_1$(x) For Spin-One Hadrons

> High-energy spin physics became a popular topic recently after the EMC finding for the proton's spin content. There exist unmeasured spin-dependent structure functions ($b_1$, $b_2$, $b_3$, and $b_4$) for spin-one hadrons such as the deuteron. The tensor structure function $b_1(x)$ could be measured by the proposed 15 GeV European Electron Facility. The measurement provides important clues to physics of non-nucleonic components in spin-one nuclei and to tensor structures on the quark-parton level.

# Tensor Structure Function b₁(x) For Spin-One Hadrons

## Key claim

The tensor structure function b₁(x) for spin-one hadrons such as the deuteron could be measured by the proposed 15 GeV European Electron Facility, and its measurement would provide clues to non-nucleonic components in spin-one nuclei and to tensor structures at the quark-parton level; however, b₁ is expected to be very small compared with the unpolarized structure function F₁, requiring an intense electron accelerator to detect it.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for spin-one hadron structure functions using the operator product expansion and a quark-parton model, following the formalism of Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989). It derives b₁(x) in terms of quark tensor polarizations δqᵢ(x), which measure the difference between quark distributions in a hadron with spin projection zero versus the average of spin projections ±1. A phenomenological sum rule for the integral of b₁(x) is developed by relating it to the deuteron's electric quadrupole form factor F_Q(t) in the limit t→0, analogous to the Gottfried sum rule.

## Key numbers

- The ratio b₁(x)/F₁(x) from a pion-exchange calculation is approximately 0.02(x−0.3) as a rough parametrization.
- The integral sum rule ∫ dx b₁(x) = 0 is predicted in a naive parton model where sea quarks have no tensor polarization.
- If sea quarks are tensor polarized, the integral becomes ∫ dx b₁(x) = (1/9) δQ_sea, where δQ_sea is the integrated tensor polarization of sea quarks.
- The D-state admixture in the deuteron, denoted sin²α, enters the convolution formula for b₁(x) and is small, making b₁ much smaller than F₁.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of spin-one structure functions by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989) and builds on earlier investigations by Pais (1967) and Frankfurt and Strikman (1983). The paper proposes a phenomenological sum rule analogous to the Gottfried sum rule, which became a topic of interest following results from the New Muon Collaboration (1991). The measurement of b₁(x) would enable studies of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei such as pions and rho mesons, and could test whether sea quarks carry tensor polarization, breaking the naive sum rule.
