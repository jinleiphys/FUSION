# Constructing Effective Pair Wave Function from Relativistic Mean Field Theory with a Cutoff

> We propose a simple method to reproduce the ^1S_0 pairing properties of nuclear matter, which are obtained using a sophisticated model, by introducing a density-independent cutoff into the relativistic mean field model. This can be applied successfully to the physically relevant density range.

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a density-independent momentum cutoff into the relativistic mean field (RMF) model's gap equation can reproduce the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap and coherence length obtained with the sophisticated Bonn-B potential, but only for Fermi momenta above 0.2 fm\(^{-1}\) and with deviations near the critical density where the superfluid phase disappears.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled gap equation and effective mass equation for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) RMF model with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm c}\). They performed a \(\chi^2\) minimization over 11 Fermi momenta from 0.2 to 1.2 fm\(^{-1}\) to find the cutoff that best reproduces both the pairing gap \(\Delta(k_{\rm F})\) and the coherence length \(\xi\) obtained from the Bonn-B potential, using the same single-particle states from the RMF model in both cases.

## Key numbers

- Optimal cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm c} = 3.60\) fm\(^{-1}\) for symmetric nuclear matter (\(\gamma = 4\))
- Fit performed over \(k_{\rm F} = 0.2, 0.3, \ldots, 1.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) (11 points)
- RMF parameters: \(M = 939\) MeV, \(m_\sigma = 550\) MeV, \(m_\omega = 783\) MeV, \(g_\sigma^2 = 91.64\), \(g_\omega^2 = 136.2\)
- The method covers neutron matter densities corresponding to \(0.2 \lesssim k_{\rm F} \lesssim 1.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) in the inner crust of neutron stars

## Context

This work extends the first relativistic study of pairing in nuclear matter by Krewald (1991), which gave gaps three times larger than non-relativistic standards, and subsequent attempts by various groups classified into three types. The authors aim to construct a relativistic effective force usable in the particle-particle channel, analogous to the Gogny force in non-relativistic calculations (Gogny, 1975), enabling consistent relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov studies of neutron stars and finite nuclei where the same interaction is used in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels.
