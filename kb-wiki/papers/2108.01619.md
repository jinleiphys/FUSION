# Pion photoproduction in chiral perturbation theory with explicit treatment of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance

> We study the reaction of pion photoproduction on the nucleon in the framework of chiral perturbation theory with explicit $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom. In the covariant approach, we give results up to order $ε^3$ in the small scale expansion scheme. Furthermore, we provide $Δ$-less and $Δ$-full results obtained in the heavy-baryon scheme to analyze the differences to the covariant approach. Low energy constants are fitted to multipole amplitudes using theoretical truncation errors estimated by a Bayesian approach. We also compare our findings to data of neutral pion production cross sections and polarization asymmetries. The description of the reaction is clearly improved by the explicit treatment of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance as an active degree of freedom in chiral perturbation theory improves the description of pion photoproduction data, particularly for neutral pion channels, but the improvement is scheme-dependent and the low-energy constants cannot be directly compared between the covariant and heavy-baryon formalisms.

## Method

The authors computed pion photoproduction on the nucleon using chiral perturbation theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom, performing calculations up to order ε³ in the small scale expansion (SSE) scheme within a covariant extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) framework. They also provided Δ-less and Δ-full results in the heavy-baryon scheme for comparison, and fitted low-energy constants to multipole amplitudes using a Bayesian approach to estimate theoretical truncation errors.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232)-nucleon mass split Δ ≡ m_Δ − m_N is treated as being of the same order as the pion mass, i.e., Δ ∼ m_π ∼ ε, in the SSE scheme.
- The pion production threshold lies at a center-of-mass energy of √s = m_N + m_π.
- The electric charge is e ≈ 0.303.
- The leading-order γNΔ coupling constant is denoted h_A, with subleading coupling h_1 estimated in the analysis.

## Context

This work extends prior covariant calculations of pion photoproduction by Bernard et al. (2005) and Hilt et al. (2013) by adding explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom, and contrasts with the heavy-baryon studies of Hemmert et al. (1996) and Cawthorne et al. (2015). It enables future applications to nuclear electroweak currents and more complex reactions such as radiative pion photoproduction, and provides a comparison to the δ-scheme calculations of Blin et al. (2016) and Navarro et al. (2020).
