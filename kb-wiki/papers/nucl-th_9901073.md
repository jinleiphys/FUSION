# Computer Study of Isotope Production in High Power Accelerators

> Methods for radionuclide production calculation in a high power proton accelerator have been developed and applied to study production of 22 isotopes by high-energy protons and neutrons. These methods are readily applicable to accelerator, and reactor, environments other than the particular model we considered and to the production of other radioactive and stable isotopes. We have also developed methods for evaluating cross sections from a wide variety of sources into a single cross section set and have produced an evaluated library covering about a third of all natural elements. These methods also are applicable to an expanded set of reactions. A 684 page detailed report on this study, with 37 tables and 264 color figures is available on the Web at http://t2.lanl.gov/publications/publications.html, or, if not accessible, in hard copy from the authors.

## Key claim

The authors developed and applied computational methods for calculating radionuclide production in a high-power proton accelerator, using the Accelerator Production of Tritium (APT) target/blanket assembly as a test case, but they explicitly note that their results assume any irradiation fixtures would have no effect on the fluxes and that commenting on the economic viability of producing any particular isotope is beyond the scope of the work.

## Method

The authors used the MCNPX version 2.1.1 Monte Carlo code to calculate neutron and proton fluxes throughout a computer model of the APT target/blanket assembly, running simulations for beam energies of 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, 1.6, 1.7, and 1.8 GeV at 100 milliamps, each following 120,000 incident protons. They constructed evaluated excitation functions for reactions on 70 target nuclides of 25 elements by combining experimental data with calculations from the CEM95 and LAHET codes above 100 MeV, the HMS-ALICE code for protons below 100 MeV and neutrons between 20 and 100 MeV, and the European Activation File EAF-97 for neutrons below 20 MeV. Production rates for 22 end-product nuclides were then computed as flux-weighted cross sections integrated over the tallied fluxes at nearly 500 locations throughout the target and blanket.

## Key numbers

*   The APT target/blanket assembly model used a 16 cm x 160 cm beam area.
*   Beam energies studied were 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, 1.6, 1.7, and 1.8 GeV, all at 100 milliamps.
*   The Monte Carlo runs followed 120,000 incident protons for each beam energy.
*   Flux tallies were taken in approximately 183 cells, which after accounting for reflective symmetry yielded 102 tallies.
*   The production rates for natural fluorine target to F-18 in the forward blanket at 1.7 GeV were 7.96E-03 Ci/g-hr from protons and 7.69E-02 Ci/g-hr from neutrons, for a total of 8.48E-02 Ci/g-hr.
*   The production rates for natural fluorine target to F-18 in the downstream blanket at 1.7 GeV were 7.73E-02 Ci/g-hr from protons and 2.23E-01 Ci/g-hr from neutrons, for a total of 3.00E-01 Ci/g-hr.
*   Production rates increased with beam energy by factors between 1.5 and 5 from 1.0 to 1.8 GeV.
*   Within the beam cavity, production rates peaked at a distance of 140 cm.

## Context

This work extends a previous study by the same authors (Van Riper et al., 1998) that considered production of only F-18 and I-131 at two locations, now covering 22 isotopes at nearly 500 locations and multiple beam energies. The study was motivated by a report from the Medical University of South Carolina (1998) discussing medical radioisotope production at the proposed APT facility, and it addresses the US domestic production shortfall for radionuclides. The cross section evaluation methods build on the International Code Comparisons for Intermediate Energy Nuclear Data organized by NEA/OECD (1994-1997) and benchmarks by Titarenko et al. (1998), and the results are intended to be applicable to other high-power accelerators such as those for accelerator transmutation of nuclear wastes (ATW) and new spallation sources in the USA, Europe, and Japan.
