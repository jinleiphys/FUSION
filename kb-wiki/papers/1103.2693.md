# Extraction of nucleon resonances from global analysis of meson production reactions at EBAC

> We report the current status of exploring the dynamical aspect of the excited nucleon states through the comprehensive coupled-channels analysis of meson production reactions at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center of Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) analysis demonstrates that the correspondence between bare states from static hadron structure calculations and physical resonance states is complicated due to reaction dynamics, with the Roper resonance exhibiting a two-pole structure from the πΔ unitarity cut and a one-to-many mapping between bare and physical states.

## Method

The analysis uses a dynamical coupled-channels model (EBAC-DCC) that fully incorporates multi-channel unitarity including the three-body ππN channel, fitting hadronic and electromagnetic parameters to data from πN → πN, πN → ηN, γN → πN, and N(e,e'π)N reactions. The model was extended to include KΛ and KΣ channels for a combined analysis of πN, γN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ reactions up to W=2.1 GeV. N* properties are extracted from pole positions and residues of scattering amplitudes in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- πN → πN analysis covers W < 2.0 GeV (2006-2009) and extended to W < 2.1 GeV (2010-)
- γN → πN analysis covers W < 1.6 GeV (2006-2009) and extended to W < 2.0 GeV (2010-)
- N(e,e'π)N analysis covers W < 1.6 GeV and Q² < 1.5 GeV² (2006-2009), extended to Q² < 6 GeV² (2010-)
- πN → ηN analysis covers W < 2.0 GeV in both periods
- γp → K⁺Λ preliminary results shown at Eγ = 1421 MeV (W = 1883 MeV)
- The model includes 5 coupled channels (πN, ηN, πΔ, σN, ρN) in 2006-2009 and 7 channels (adding KΛ, KΣ) from 2010

## Context

This work extends the EBAC-DCC analysis program at Jefferson Lab, which previously established models for hadronic reactions (Julin-Diaz et al. 2007, Durand et al. 2008) and electromagnetic reactions (Julin-Diaz et al. 2008, Joo et al. 2009). The extraction of N-N* electromagnetic transition form factors beyond Δ(1232) within a fully dynamical coupled-channels framework is reported as a first. The analysis enables future reinvestigation of the N* spectrum with inclusion of ηN and KY reaction data, extension of form factors to Q² up to 6 GeV², and application of the coupled-channels approach to meson physics for hybrid meson searches at GlueX and CLAS12.
