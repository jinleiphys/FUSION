# An extended Skyrme momentum dependent potential in asymmetric nuclear matter and transport models

> Based on an extended Skyrme momentum-dependent interaction (MDI), we derive an isospin asymmetric equation of state, isospin-dependent single-particle potential and the Hamiltonian which can be used in the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model and the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model at the beam energy less than 1 GeV/u. As an example of the applications of extended Skyrme MDI, we also present the results obtained with the extended Skyrme momentum-dependent interaction in the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD), and the influence of the effective mass splitting on the isospin sensitive observables, i.e., the single and double neutron-to-proton ratios, is discussed again.

## Key claim

The paper derives an extended Skyrme momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) that can be used in transport models (BUU and QMD) for beam energies below 1 GeV/u, but the standard Skyrme MDI is only valid below approximately 300 MeV/u because it violates optical potential data at higher momenta; the extended version overcomes this limitation by including relative momentum terms up to sixth order.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard Skyrme interaction by adding momentum-dependent terms up to order \(k^6\) (Eq. 4), then derive the corresponding single-particle potential, equation of state, and Hamiltonian for use in the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model. They fit the extended MDI parameters to reproduce the Hama optical potential data and nuclear matter properties (saturation density, incompressibility, effective masses, symmetry energy). As an application, they implement the extended MDI in ImQMD and re-examine the effect of effective mass splitting on neutron-to-proton yield ratios.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy limit: less than 1 GeV/u for the extended Skyrme MDI (abstract); standard Skyrme MDI is limited to below 300 MeV/u (from Fig. 1 caption, kinetic energy less than 300 MeV).
- 123 Skyrme parameter sets were selected with constraints: \(K_0 = [200, 280]\) MeV, \(S_0 = [25, 35]\) MeV, \(L = [30, 120]\) MeV, \(m^*_s/m = [0.6, 1.0]\), \(f_I = m/m_s^* - m/m_v^* = [-0.5, 0.4]\).
- 25 Skyrme parameter sets (blue lines in Fig. 1) describe the Hama data with reduced \(\chi^2 < 0.7\) at momentum less than 0.8 GeV/c.
- Difference between single-particle potential at T=0 and T=20 MeV is less than 9% at momentum less than 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme interaction approach (e.g., as used in ImQMD by Zhang et al. (2014)) which fails at beam energies above ~300 MeV/u due to unphysical divergence of the single-particle potential. It builds on the extended Skyrme pseudopotential formalism of Davesne (2015) and the work of R. Wang (2018) who first applied it to nuclear matter. The new framework enables consistent transport model simulations across a wider energy range and allows systematic variation of nuclear matter parameters (e.g., effective mass splitting) to study isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions.
