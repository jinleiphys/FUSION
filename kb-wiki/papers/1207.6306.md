# Clustering of energy levels

> It is noted that in single j-shell calculations certain odd-spin states in even--even nuclei lie in a narrow energy band; likewise certain states in odd--odd nuclei.

## Key claim

In single j-shell calculations for certain even-even and odd-odd nuclei, states of odd spin (even-even) or several low-lying states (odd-odd) cluster into a narrow energy band, but the authors offer no fundamental explanation for this behavior and note that experimental agreement is far from perfect.

## Method

The authors performed single j-shell calculations using the \(f_{7/2}\) and \(g_{9/2}\) shells for even-even nuclei \(^{44}\)Ti, \(^{52}\)Fe, and \(^{96}\)Cd, and for odd-odd nuclei \(^{46}\)Sc and \(^{94}\)Rh. Input two-body matrix elements for \(^{44}\)Ti and \(^{52}\)Fe were taken from the spectra of \(^{42}\)Sc and \(^{54}\)Co respectively, while those for \(^{96}\)Cd came from Corragio et al. (2012). Energies of selected states were tabulated to highlight the clustering.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{44}\)Ti (even-even), odd-spin states cluster between 5.669 MeV (J=1) and 6.043 MeV (J=7), a spread of 0.374 MeV.
- For \(^{52}\)Fe (even-even), odd-spin states lie between 5.518 MeV (J=1) and 6.271 MeV (J=5), with J=7 at 5.972 MeV.
- For \(^{96}\)Cd (even-even), odd-spin states cluster from 4.269 MeV (J=1) to 4.635 MeV (J=7), with J=9 at 4.565 MeV.
- For \(^{46}\)Sc (odd-odd), four states (J=2,3,4,6) are calculated below 0.100 MeV, with J=5 at 0.2805 MeV.
- For \(^{94}\)Rh (odd-odd), four states (J=2,3,5,7) lie near 0.52 MeV, with J=4 at 0.3282 MeV, J=6 at 0.2033 MeV, J=8 at 0.0000 MeV, and J=9 at 0.7183 MeV.
- Experimental energies for \(^{46}\)Sc (J=2+ to 6+) are 0.4441, 0.2278, 0.0000, 0.2807, and 0.0520 MeV, showing clustering despite imperfect agreement.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own prior single j-shell calculations (Zamick and Escuderos, 2012) by highlighting the clustering phenomenon without offering a theoretical explanation. The results are not compared to experiment in detail because many of the odd-spin states in even-even nuclei have not been observed. The paper aims to stimulate further research into odd-J even-parity states in even-even nuclei and clustering in odd-odd nuclei, which have received little focus.
