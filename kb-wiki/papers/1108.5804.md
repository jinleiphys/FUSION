# Meson-baryon interactions and baryon resonances

> Meson-baryon interactions are the fundamental building blocks to study the structures of baryon resonances and meson properties in few-body nuclear systems. We review the recent progress in the investigation of the meson-baryon interaction in the strangeness S=-1 sector and the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance. In particular, we present an attempt to construct a realistic Kbar N-pi Sigma interaction in chiral SU(3) dynamics in response to the precise measurement of the kaonic hydrogen, and discuss the subthreshold extrapolation of the Kbar N interaction with the information of the pi Sigma channel.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single state but a superposition of two independent poles in the complex energy plane, with the higher-energy pole originating from a Kbar N bound state and the lower-energy pole from a pi Sigma resonance, though this conclusion depends on using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the dominant term and may be modified by next-to-leading-order contributions.

## Method

The author applies chiral SU(3) dynamics, combining the chiral low-energy meson-baryon interaction (Weinberg-Tomozawa term, Born terms, and next-to-leading-order contact terms) with unitarization via a dispersion relation that yields a scattering equation T = V + VGT. The model is fitted to experimental data including K- p total cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the new SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen, with pole positions extracted from the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen 1s level energy shift: Delta E = 283 +/- 36(stat) +/- 6(syst) eV (SIDDHARTA collaboration, 2011)
- Kaonic hydrogen 1s level width: Gamma = 541 +/- 89(stat) +/- 22(syst) eV (SIDDHARTA collaboration, 2011)
- NLO model pole positions: 1424 - 26i MeV (higher pole) and 1381 - 81i MeV (lower pole) for the isospin I=0 amplitude
- NLO model chi-squared per degree of freedom: 0.96 for the fit to SIDDHARTA data, threshold branching ratios, and K- p total cross sections
- WT model pole positions: 1422 - 16i MeV and 1384 - 90i MeV
- WTB model pole positions: 1421 - 17i MeV and 1385 - 105i MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oller (2000), and Jido (2003) for meson-baryon scattering, and directly responds to the precise kaonic hydrogen measurement by the SIDDHARTA collaboration (Bazzi, 2011). The double-pole structure of Lambda(1405) was first identified by Jido (2003), and the present analysis confirms it with improved constraints. The results enable more reliable predictions for Kbar N bound states in nuclei (kaonic nuclei), which have been studied by Akaishi (2002) and Shevchenko (2006), and the proposed method for extracting pi Sigma scattering lengths from Lambda_c decays (Hyodo, 2011) offers a new experimental handle to further constrain the subthreshold Kbar N interaction.
