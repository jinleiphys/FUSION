# Impact of finite density on spectroscopic parameters of decuplet baryons

> The decuplet baryons, $Δ$, $Σ^{*}$, $Ξ^{*}$ and $Ω^{-}$, are studied in nuclear matter by using the in-medium QCD sum rules. By fixing the three momentum of the particles under consideration at the rest frame of the medium, the negative energy contributions are removed. It is obtained that the parameters of the $Δ$ baryon are more affected by the medium against the $Ω^{-}$ state, containing three strange quarks, whose mass and residue do not affected by the medium, considerably. We also find the vector and scalar self energies of these baryons in nuclear matter. By the recent progresses at $\bar{P}$ANDA experiment at FAIR and NICA facility it may be possible to study the in-medium properties of such states even the multi-strange $Ξ^{*}$ and $Ω^{-}$ systems in near future.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the spectroscopic parameters (mass, residue, and self-energies) of decuplet baryons in nuclear matter are affected differently depending on their strangeness content, with the Delta baryon being most affected and the Omega-minus (containing three strange quarks) being least affected; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the three-momentum fixed at 270 MeV in the rest frame of the medium and on the removal of negative-energy contributions.

## Method

The authors employed in-medium QCD sum rules, constructing a correlation function for each decuplet baryon (Delta, Sigma*, Xi*, Omega-) using interpolating currents with appropriate quark content and normalization constants. They calculated the hadronic side by inserting a complete set of baryonic states and removing spin-1/2 contaminations, then matched it to the operator product expansion (OPE) side computed in terms of in-medium quark and gluon condensates up to dimension-six operators. The negative-energy pole contributions were eliminated by working at fixed three-momentum in the nuclear matter rest frame and using a specific weight function in the Borel transform.

## Key numbers

- Three-momentum fixed at |p| = 270 MeV for all calculations
- Nuclear matter density rho_N = (0.11)^3 GeV^3
- Light quark masses: m_u = 2.2 MeV, m_d = 4.7 MeV, m_s = 96 MeV
- Vacuum quark condensate: <q_bar q>_0 = (-0.241)^3 GeV^3
- Strange quark condensate in vacuum: <s_bar s>_0 = 0.8 * <q_bar q>_0
- Sigma term sigma_N = 0.059 GeV
- Parameter y (strangeness content of nucleon) = 0.04 +/- 0.02
- m_0^2 = 0.8 GeV^2
- Vacuum gluon condensate: <(alpha_s/pi) G^2>_0 = (0.33 +/- 0.04)^4 GeV^4

## Context

This work extends previous in-medium QCD sum rule studies of nucleons (Cohen 1994, Drukarev 1988, Hatsuda 1991, Adami 1991, Azizi 2014) and the few existing studies of hyperons and decuplet baryons (Azizi 2015, Jin 1994, Jin 1995, Savage 1996, Miyatsu 2009, Beane 2012, Ouellette 1997) by systematically calculating the mass shifts, residue shifts, and vector/scalar self-energies for all four decuplet baryons simultaneously. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the PANDA experiment at FAIR and the NICA facility, particularly for the multi-strange Xi* and Omega- systems, and may help address the "hyperon puzzle" and "Delta puzzle" in neutron star physics.
