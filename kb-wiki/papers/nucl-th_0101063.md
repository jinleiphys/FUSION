# Collectivity of the low-lying dipole strength in relativistic random phase approximation

> The relativistic random phase approximation is applied in the analysis of the evolution of the isovector dipole response in nuclei with a large neutron excess. The self-consistent framework of relativistic mean-field theory, which has been very successfully applied in the description of ground-state properties of nuclei far from the valley of $β$-stability, is extended to study the possible onset of low-energy collective isovector dipole modes in nuclei with extreme isospin values.

## Key claim

The relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) predicts that low-energy isovector dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei evolves from purely single-particle excitations in light isotopes (O, Ca) toward collective pygmy dipole resonances in medium-heavy nuclei (Ni, Sn, Zr), but only in the heavier systems does a coherent superposition of multiple neutron particle-hole configurations emerge.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) calculations based on the NL3 effective interaction, using a configuration space that includes both positive-energy particle-hole pairs and pairs formed from occupied states and negative-energy Dirac sea states. The isovector dipole response was computed for chains of O, Ca, Ni, Sn, and Zr isotopes using the isovector dipole operator, and the discrete spectra were averaged with a Lorentzian distribution of width 0.5 MeV. Transition densities and velocity fields were analyzed to distinguish single-particle from collective excitations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{28}$O, the most collective RRPA peak at 15.2 MeV exhausts 24% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR)
- For $^{22}$O, $^{24}$O, and $^{28}$O, the RRPA finds 2.5%, 7.0%, and 8.6% of the EWSR below 10 MeV, respectively
- For $^{60}$Ca, low-energy states below 10 MeV together exhaust 10% of the EWSR, compared to 40% exhausted by the main isovector giant dipole resonance (GDR) peak at 15.2 MeV
- For $^{78}$Ni, the ratio of energy-weighted moments $m_{1,low}/m_{1,high}$ (E ≤ 10 MeV vs. E > 10 MeV) is 0.06
- For $^{132}$Sn, a state at 8.6 MeV exhausts 1.4% of the EWSR, with proton particle-hole excitations contributing only 10.4% of the total RRPA intensity
- For $^{122}$Zr, the pygmy dipole state at 7.7 MeV exhausts 3.3% of the EWSR, with proton particle-hole excitations contributing 10.5%
- Without inclusion of negative-energy Dirac sea states in $^{208}$Pb, only 72.8% of the energy-weighted sum rule is exhausted, compared to the full RRPA calculation

## Context

This work extends the RRPA analysis of pygmy dipole resonances previously applied to $^{208}$Pb (Vretenar et al., 2000) to a systematic study of neutron-rich isotopes across multiple mass regions. The results contradict the predictions of the two-fluid hydrodynamical model of Suzuki, Ikeda, and Sato (1990), which predicted pygmy resonance energies increasing with neutron excess, whereas the RRPA centroid energies decrease with neutron number. The calculations also disagree with density functional theory results for Ca isotopes (Chabanat et al., 1994), which found collective low-energy dipole strength in $^{48}$Ca where the RRPA finds none. The work enables future experimental searches for pygmy resonances in medium-heavy neutron-rich nuclei and provides a benchmark for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic approaches to the isovector response.
