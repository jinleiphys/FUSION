# Further explorations of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas. III: Role of particle-number projection

> Starting from HFB-6, we have constructed a new mass table, referred to as HFB-8, including all the 9200 nuclei lying between the two drip lines over the range of Z and N > 6 and Z < 122. It differs from HFB-6 in that the wave function is projected on the exact particle number. Like HFB-6, the isoscalar effective mass is constrained to the value 0.80 M and the pairing is density independent. The rms errors of the mass-data fit is 0.635 MeV, i.e. better than almost all our previous HFB mass formulas. The extrapolations of this new mass formula out to the drip lines do not differ significantly from the previous HFB-6 mass formula.

## Key claim

The new HFB-8 mass table, which incorporates particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami prescription followed by exact projection after variation, achieves a better fit to experimental masses than almost all previous HFB mass formulas, but its extrapolations to the neutron drip line do not differ significantly from the earlier HFB-6 table that lacked this projection.

## Method

Starting from the HFB-6 model and its BSk6 Skyrme force, the authors performed a new fit of force parameters (yielding BSk8) within a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework that includes approximate Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection before variation and exact particle-number projection after variation. The wave function is projected on exact particle number using a discretized Fomenko prescription with 11 gauge angles for both protons and neutrons, and the rotational correction is treated with a modified cranking formula using parameters b=0.65 and c=4.5. The model retains a density-independent delta-function pairing force, an isoscalar effective mass constrained to 0.80 M, and a phenomenological Wigner term.

## Key numbers

- RMS error of mass fit: 0.635 MeV for 2149 nuclei in the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation (AME), compared to 0.666 MeV for HFB-6
- RMS error of mass fit: 0.659 MeV for 2135 nuclei in the 2001 AME, compared to 0.684 MeV for HFB-6
- RMS deviation of charge radii: 0.0250 fm for 523 nuclei, compared to 0.0262 fm for HFB-6
- Isoscalar effective mass M*_s/M constrained to 0.80
- Symmetry energy J constrained to 28.0 MeV
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.1589 fm^-3 for BSk8 (0.1575 fm^-3 for BSk6)
- Energy per nucleon at equilibrium a_v = -15.824 MeV for BSk8 (-15.749 MeV for BSk6)
- Incompressibility K_v = 230 MeV for BSk8 (229 MeV for BSk6)
- Pairing strength for neutrons V+_n = -314.0 MeV fm^3 (decreased by about 2% from BSk6)
- Pairing strength for protons V+_p = -293.0 MeV fm^3 (decreased by about 11% from BSk6)
- Rotational correction parameters: b = 0.65, c = 4.5
- RMS deviation of shape isomer energies: 0.64 MeV with modified cranking prescription, compared to 1.62 MeV with mixed prescription and 0.95 MeV with rigid prescription

## Context

This work extends the series of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas by the same authors, following HFB-1 through HFB-7 (Samyn et al. 2002, Goriely et al. 2002, Samyn et al. 2003, Goriely et al. 2003). Paper I (Samyn et al. 2003) examined density-dependent pairing, and Paper II (Goriely et al. 2003) studied the effective nucleon mass. The present paper introduces particle-number projection, which had not been attempted in any previous global HFB mass fit by this group. The improved description of shape isomer energies using the modified cranking formula addresses a known deficiency of the earlier mixed-prescription approach used in HFB-1 through HFB-7. The mass table enables more reliable extrapolations for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations and neutron star matter studies, though the authors note that vibrational zero-point corrections remain unaddressed.
