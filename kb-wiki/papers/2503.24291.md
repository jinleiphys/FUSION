# Quark production in the bottom-up thermalization

> We investigate the impact of quark production on bottom-up thermalization in heavy-ion collisions. First, we extend the parametric estimates of bottom-up thermalization in pure gluon systems by incorporating quark production in the weak-coupling (high-energy) limit. Our analysis reveals that quark production does not alter the qualitative features of the three-stage thermalization process in this limit. Furthermore, we obtain the scaling behavior of the quark number density over time at each stage. Then, by solving the Boltzmann equation in diffusion approximation (BEDA) for longitudinally boost-invariant systems, we demonstrate how our detailed numerical simulations approach the predicted three-stage thermalization picture as the strong coupling $α_s$ decreases. Finally, we carry out a detailed comparison of our BEDA results with those obtained by solving the QCD effective kinetic theory for intermediate values of $α_s$, observing remarkably good quantitative agreement between the two approaches.

# Quark production in the bottom-up thermalization

## Key claim
Quark production does not alter the qualitative three-stage thermalization process observed in pure gluon systems in the weak-coupling limit, and quarks become relevant only in the final stage of thermalization where they form a QGP thermal bath. This conclusion holds under the condition that the system starts with no initial quarks or antiquarks and that the coupling is sufficiently small.

## Method
The authors extended parametric estimates of bottom-up thermalization from pure gluon systems (Baier 2000) by incorporating quark production through both 2-to-2 conversion and 1-to-2 splitting processes. They then solved the Boltzmann equation in diffusion approximation (BEDA) for longitudinally boost-invariant systems using a GPU-based algorithm with logarithmic momentum grids and power-law velocity grids, and compared results with QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) solutions at intermediate coupling.

## Key numbers
- Saturation momentum at LHC energies: \(Q_s \approx 1\text{--}2\) GeV
- For weak coupling simulations: \(\alpha_s = 0.002\), with initial anisotropy parameters \(\xi = 1.0\) and \(\xi = 5.0\)
- For intermediate coupling comparison: \(\alpha_s = 0.265\) (\(\lambda = 10\)), with \(N_f = 3\) active quark flavors
- Thermalization completion time: \(Q_s \tau \sim \alpha_s^{-13/5}\)
- Post-thermalization Bjorken scaling: \(T_* \sim \alpha_s^{-7/15} Q_s (Q_s \tau)^{-1/3}\), \(n_q \sim n_g \sim \alpha_s^{-7/5} Q_s^2 \tau^{-1}\)
- Hydrodynamization occurs at \(Q_s \tau \gtrsim 50\) for the intermediate coupling case

## Context
This work extends the parametric estimates of bottom-up thermalization originally developed for pure gluon systems by Baier (2000) and subsequent quantitative studies using EKT by Kurkela (2015, 2018) and Du (2020). It builds on recent BEDA developments for spatially homogeneous systems by Barrera Cabodevila (2022) and Cabodevila (2023). The results enable future phenomenological studies of pre-equilibrium photon and di-lepton production, as well as heavy quark thermalization at early stages of heavy-ion collisions. The comparison between BEDA and EKT shows BEDA as a computationally efficient alternative for studying nonequilibrium QCD dynamics, with future work planned for higher-dimensional simulations of small collision systems.
