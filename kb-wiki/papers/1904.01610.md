# Nuclear correlations and modifications of the nucleon-nucleon potential due to the QCD critical mode

> The scalar-isoscalar mode of QCD becomes lighter/nearly massless close to the chiral transition/second-order critical point. From nuclear physics we know that this mode is the main responsible for the attractive part of the nucleon-nucleon potential at inter-particle distances of 1-2 fm. Therefore one expects that close to the critical point there is a long-range strong attraction among nucleons. Using a Walecka-Serot model for the NN potential we study the effects of the critical point in a finite system of nucleons and mesons by solving classical Molecular Dynamics+Langevin equations for the freeze-out conditions of heavy-ion collisions. Going beyond the mean-field approximation allows us to account for strong nucleon correlations in the time evolution, leading to baryon clustering. We observe that light cluster formation, together with an enhancement of higher-order cumulants of the proton distribution can signal the presence of the critical point.

# Nuclear correlations and modifications of the nucleon-nucleon potential due to the QCD critical mode

## Key claim
The scalar-isoscalar (sigma) mode of QCD, which becomes lighter near the chiral transition or critical point, enhances the attractive part of the nucleon-nucleon potential and produces long-range attraction among nucleons; this effect can generate baryon clustering and increase higher-order cumulants of the proton distribution, but the magnitude depends on how much the sigma mass is reduced (the authors consider at most a factor of sqrt(6) reduction from its vacuum value) and on the finite time the fireball spends near the critical region.

## Method
The authors use a Walecka-Serot model for the nucleon-nucleon potential, parameterized by sigma and omega meson exchanges, and modify the sigma mass and coupling to simulate critical behavior. They solve classical nonrelativistic Molecular Dynamics equations with Langevin dynamics (including stochastic and drag forces) for finite systems of nucleons (N=32 to N=128) at temperatures near freeze-out (T=120-150 MeV), simulating heavy-ion collision conditions at Beam Energy Scan energies. They compute proton cumulants and cluster formation for different potentials (V_A', V_B1, V_B2, V_C) with increasing criticality, and compare to STAR experimental data at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV as a non-critical baseline.

## Key numbers
- The vacuum sigma mass is m_sigma=500 MeV, omega mass m_omega=782 MeV, with couplings g_sigma^2=267.1 m_sigma^2/m_N^2 and g_omega^2=195.9 m_omega^2/m_N^2 (where m_N=938 MeV) in the original Serot-Walecka model.
- The modified potential V_A' increases the omega coupling by 40% relative to the mean-field parameters.
- The most critical potential V_C reduces m_sigma^2 by a factor of 6 from its vacuum value.
- Simulations use N=32 nucleons at T=150 MeV with baryonic density n=0.3 fm^{-3}, duration Delta t=5 fm, and 10^5 events for statistics.
- For cold configurations (T=10^{-3} MeV), N=4 nucleons form a tetrahedron with inter-particle distances at the potential minimum; N=13 forms an icosahedron with distance ratios 1:sqrt(2-2/sqrt(5)):sqrt(2+2/sqrt(5)):2.
- The Langevin drag coefficient is lambda=0.256 fm^{-1}.
- For cluster identification, 4-nucleon clusters require inter-particle distance Delta r < 2 fm and momentum difference Delta p < 0.22 GeV per component.

## Context
This work extends the proposal by Stephanov (1998, 1999, 2004, 2008, 2011) that the QCD critical point produces large fluctuations of the sigma field and enhances higher-order cumulants of net-proton distributions, as studied experimentally by the STAR collaboration (Luo 2015, Adamczyk 2017). The authors go beyond mean-field approaches by using Molecular Dynamics with Langevin dynamics (following Gelman 2006) to account for nucleon correlations that mean-field treatments miss. The work connects to the Bonn potential (Machleidt 2000) and the Serot-Walecka model (Serot 1984) for nuclear interactions. It enables predictions for light-nuclei yields (triton, deuteron, helium-3, helium-4) as additional signatures of the critical point, building on statistical thermal model results (Andronic 2017) and preliminary NA49 data (Anticic 2016) and STAR data (Liu 2019) on the ratio N_t N_p / N_d^2.
