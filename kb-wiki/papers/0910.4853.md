# Nuclear suppression at low energy heavy ion collisions

> The effects of non-zero baryonic chemical potential on the drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks propagating through a baryon rich quark gluon plasma have been studied. The nuclear suppression factor, $R_{\mathrm AA}$ for non-photonic single electron spectra resulting from the semileptonic decays of hadrons containing heavy flavours have been evaluated for low energy collisions. The role of non-zero baryonic chemical potential on $R_{\mathrm AA}$ has been highlighted.

# Nuclear suppression at low energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The nuclear suppression factor $R_{\mathrm AA}$ for non-photonic single electrons from heavy-flavor hadron decays is evaluated for low-energy heavy-ion collisions ( $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ from 7.7 to 39 GeV) using a Fokker-Planck approach; the authors find that at large $p_T$ the suppression is similar across all energies studied because high-temperature/low-baryon-density collisions are compensated by low-temperature/high-baryon-density collisions, but the detectability of the non-zero baryonic chemical potential effects depends on overall experimental performance.

## Method

The authors solve the Fokker-Planck equation for charm and bottom quarks propagating through a thermalized quark-gluon plasma with non-zero baryonic chemical potential, using elastic scattering processes ($Qq \rightarrow Qq$, $Q\bar{q} \rightarrow Q\bar{q}$, $Qg \rightarrow Qg$) with pQCD cross sections. The drag and diffusion coefficients are computed as functions of temperature and quark chemical potential, and the heavy quark distributions are evolved in a (1+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic background with radial profile parametrization. The final $p_T$ distributions of non-photonic electrons are obtained by convolving the evolved heavy quark distributions with Peterson fragmentation functions and semileptonic decay kinematics for $D$ and $B$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient for charm quarks from gluon scattering: $8.42 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-1}$ at $T=140$ MeV and $1.86 \times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-1}$ at $T=190$ MeV.
- Diffusion coefficient for charm quarks from gluon scattering: $1.42 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=140$ MeV and $4.31 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=190$ MeV.
- Drag coefficient for bottom quarks from gluon scattering: $3.15 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-1}$ at $T=140$ MeV and $6.93 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-1}$ at $T=190$ MeV.
- Diffusion coefficient for bottom quarks from gluon scattering: $1.79 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=140$ MeV and $5.38 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=190$ MeV.
- Initial temperatures and quark chemical potentials used: $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=39$ GeV ($T_i=240$ MeV, $\mu_q=62$ MeV); $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=27$ GeV ($T_i=199$ MeV, $\mu_q=70$ MeV); $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=17.3$ GeV ($T_i=198$ MeV, $\mu_q=100$ MeV); $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=7.7$ GeV ($T_i=197$ MeV, $\mu_q=165$ MeV).
- With pQCD cross sections enhanced by a factor of 2, the resulting suppressions in $R_{\mathrm AA}$ are between 20% and 30% for $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=39$ to 7.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Das et al., 2009) of $R_{\mathrm AA}$ at RHIC $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}=200$ GeV to lower collision energies where baryon chemical potential is significant, using the parametrization of $\mu_B$ from hadronic ratios by Ristea (2009) and Andronic (2006). The approach follows the Fokker-Planck treatment of heavy quarks developed by Svetitsky (1988) and Moore and Teaney (2005), and the results are compared with the leading-order pQCD diffusion coefficient computed by Caron-Huot (2008). The paper enables predictions for nuclear suppression at GSI-FAIR and low-energy RHIC runs, though the authors note that the low production rates of charm and bottom at these energies make experimental detection challenging.
