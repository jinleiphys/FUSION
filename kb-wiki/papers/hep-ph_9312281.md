# QCD Evolution Equations for High Energy Partons in Nuclear Matter

> We derive a generalized form of Altarelli-Parisi equations to decribe the time evolution of parton distributions in a nuclear medium. In the framework of the leading logarithmic approximation, we obtain a set of coupled integro- differential equations for the parton distribution functions and equations for the virtuality (``age'') distribution of partons. In addition to parton branching processes, we take into account fusion and scattering processes that are specific to QCD in medium. Detailed balance between gain and loss terms in the resulting evolution equations correctly accounts for both real and virtual contributions which yields a natural cancellation of infrared divergences.

## Key claim

The authors derive a generalized form of Altarelli-Parisi equations that describes the time evolution of parton distributions in a nuclear medium, extending the standard vacuum QCD evolution by including fusion and scattering processes specific to dense matter. The key hedge is that the derivation applies only under conditions where medium-induced effects (color screening, rescattering) provide dynamical cut-offs on a scale short compared to the confinement scale \(\Lambda^{-1}\), and the authors explicitly neglect color screening, long-range color correlations, and parton shadowing in the present work.

## Method

The authors relate the \(Q^2\)-evolution of parton number densities to their time evolution by using time-dependent perturbation theory in the interaction picture, connecting the lifetime of virtual parton states to their virtuality. They then formulate a set of coupled integro-differential equations for the phase-space distributions of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons that include \(1 \rightarrow 2\) branching processes, \(2 \rightarrow 1\) fusion processes, and \(2 \rightarrow 2\) scattering processes, with detailed balance between gain and loss terms ensuring cancellation of infrared divergences. The approach is applied to the idealized problem of a single high-energy parton injected into infinitely extended nuclear matter, where shower partons are distinguished from nuclear partons and only interactions between the shower and the medium are considered.

## Key numbers

* The ordering condition for time-like virtualities in vacuum is \(Q_0^2 \gg Q_1^2 \gg \ldots \gg Q_j^2 \gg Q_{j+1}^2 \gg \ldots \gg \mu_0^2\), where \(\mu_0\) is the minimum virtuality scale where perturbative description fails.
* For a proton-nucleus collision in the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass frame, the longitudinal momenta are \(P_z^{(p)} = +P\) and \(P_z^{(A)} = -A P\), with \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2P\).
* At RHIC, the maximum momentum \(P\) is 250 GeV for \(p+p\), 125 GeV for \(p+{}^{16}O\), and 100 GeV for \(p+{}^{197}Au\); at LHC the available energy is a factor of 30 larger.
* The partonic center-of-mass energy squared for a scattering between two partons moving in opposite directions is \(\hat s \approx 4|x_1 x_2| P^2\).
* The invariant mass squared for fusion of two partons moving in the same direction is \(M^2 = Q_1^2 (1 + x_2/x_1) + Q_2^2 (1 + x_1/x_2)\).

## Context

This work extends the probabilistic rederivation of Altarelli-Parisi-Lipatov equations by Durand and Putikka (1990) and Collins and Qiu (1990) to include space-time development and medium-specific processes, building on the parton cascade model (PCM) of Geiger and Müller (1992). The approach is motivated by the McLerran-Venugopalan (1993) insight that perturbative QCD becomes applicable for small-\(x\) partons in a thick nuclear target due to color screening. The derived equations can be applied to jet quenching in a QCD plasma (Gyulassy and Wang, 1991) and to fragmentation cascades after deep-inelastic scattering in a large nucleus (Frankfurt and Strikman, 1990), and are intended as a supplementary diagnostic tool for analyzing the early stage of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
