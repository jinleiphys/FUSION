# Effective potential for Polyakov loops from a center symmetric effective theory in three dimensions

> We present lattice simulations of a center symmetric dimensionally reduced effective field theory for SU(2) Yang Mills which employ thermal Wilson lines and three-dimensional magnetic fields as fundamental degrees of freedom. The action is composed of a gauge invariant kinetic term, spatial gauge fields and a potential for the Wilson line which includes a "fuzzy" bag term to generate non-perturbative fluctuations. The effective potential for the Polyakov loop is extracted from the simulations including all modes of the loop as well as for cooled configuration where the hard modes have been averaged out. The former is found to exhibit a non-analytic contribution while the latter can be described by a mean-field like ansatz with quadratic and quartic terms, plus a Vandermonde potential which depends upon the location within the phase diagram.

## Key claim
The effective potential for the Polyakov loop extracted from lattice simulations of a center-symmetric effective theory in three dimensions exhibits a non-analytic contribution proportional to \(\sqrt{\ell^2}\) that arises dynamically, not from the bare action; this contrasts with the potential for long-wavelength (block-spin) modes, which can be described by a mean-field-like ansatz with quadratic and quartic terms plus a Vandermonde term whose coefficient depends on the couplings \(\beta\) and \(m^2\).

## Method
Lattice simulations of a three-dimensional effective field theory for SU(2) Yang-Mills were performed, using thermal Wilson lines and spatial gauge fields as degrees of freedom, with an action including a gauge-invariant kinetic term, a Wilson plaquette term for magnetic fields, and a mass term for the Polyakov loop trace. The effective potential for the absolute value of the Polyakov loop \(\rho = \sqrt{\ell^2}\) was extracted from histograms of the eigenvalue distribution over all lattice sites, and separately from block-spin averaged configurations (side length \(k=3\)) to isolate long-wavelength modes. The Metropolis algorithm with over-relaxation was used on \(N_s=24\) cubic lattices, with 5000 independent configurations per parameter set.

## Key numbers
- The phase transition for \(m^2=0\) occurs at \(\beta_C \approx 0.9\).
- For \(m^2=0\) and \(\beta > \beta_C\), the non-analytic coefficient \(b\) in the potential \(V_{\rm fit}(\rho) = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1-\rho^2) + a - b\rho + c\rho^2\) follows \(b(\beta) = b_0 (\beta-\beta_C)^r\) with \(b_0 = 7.1(1)\) and \(r = 0.82(4)\).
- For block-spin averages with \(k=3\), the Vandermonde coefficient \(d_0\) saturates at about \(d_0 \approx 80\) for large \(\beta\) or \(m^2\).
- At \(k=3\), the minimum of the fitted effective potential differs from the Polyakov loop expectation value by at most \(\simeq 0.03\) at the phase transition, and less than \(\simeq 0.01\) away from it.

## Context
This work extends the dimensionally reduced effective theory approach of Pisarski (2006) and Meisinger (2002), which adds a "fuzzy bag" term to the one-loop GPY potential to induce a deconfining phase transition. The simulations provide a lattice test of such center-symmetric effective theories, showing that the effective potential for the Polyakov loop differs from mean-field Landau-Ginzburg forms due to a dynamically generated non-analytic term. The results inform the structure of Polyakov-loop potentials used in models like the Polyakov-NJL model (Fukushima, 2003), suggesting that a temperature-dependent Vandermonde contribution should be allowed, as already considered in some recent works (Hell, 2009).
