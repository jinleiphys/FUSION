# Excess of soft dielectrons and photons

> Spectra of unbound electron-positron pairs (dielectrons, in brief) and photons from decays of parapositronia produced in ultraperipheral collisions of electrically charged objects are calculated. Their shapes at energies of the NICA collider are demonstrated. Soft dielectrons and photons are abundantly produced. The relevance of these processes to the astrophysical problem of cooling electron-positron pairs and the intense emission of 511 keV photons from the Galactic center is discussed.

# Excess of soft dielectrons and photons

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral collisions of heavy ions produce an abundant excess of very soft dielectrons (unbound electron-positron pairs) and photons from parapositronium decays, with masses concentrated near a few electron masses; however, the equivalent photon approximation used for the calculations is strictly valid only at very high energies, and preasymptotic corrections may become important at NICA energies, making the quantitative predictions less certain than the qualitative trend.

## Method

The author calculates dielectron and photon spectra using the equivalent photon (Weizsäcker-Williams) approximation, where the electromagnetic fields of colliding Au\(^{79+}\) ions are treated as fluxes of virtual photons. The total cross section for ultraperipheral production is obtained by folding the photon flux densities with the two-photon cross section for \(e^+e^-\) pair creation or parapositronium resonance production, then integrating over the photon energy fractions.

## Key numbers

- Dielectron mass distribution peaks at masses of order several electron masses \(m = 0.511\) MeV, effectively below 50 MeV, for NICA energies \(\sqrt{s_{nn}} = 11\) GeV, 8 GeV, and 6.45 GeV per nucleon.
- Total cross section for ultraperipheral dielectron production: \(\sigma(ZZ(\gamma\gamma)\rightarrow ZZe^+e^-) = \frac{28}{27} \frac{Z^4\alpha^4}{\pi m^2} \ln^3\frac{u^2 s_{nn}}{4m^2}\), with \(u = 0.02\).
- Total cross section for ultraperipheral parapositronium production: \(\sigma_{Ps} = \frac{16Z^4\alpha^2\Gamma}{3m^3} \ln^3\frac{u\sqrt{s_{nn}}}{m}\), where \(\Gamma \approx 5.2 \times 10^{-15}\) GeV is the parapositronium decay width.
- Direct ultraperipheral parapositronium production is about a million times less effective than dielectron creation.
- The cross section for further cooling via \(e^{\pm}e^{\pm}\rightarrow e^{\pm}e^{\pm}e^+e^-\) at small dielectron masses is \(\sigma \propto \pi r_0^2 \approx 250\) mb, with classical electron radius \(r_0 \approx 2.8 \times 10^{-15}\) m.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{nn}} = 6.45\) GeV, the total cross section for ultraperipheral dielectron production is about 5 kb.

## Context

This work extends the classic treatment of two-photon collisions in electromagnetic fields, originally formulated by Fermi (1924), Weizsäcker and Williams (1934), and Landau and Lifshitz (1934), to the specific energy range of the NICA collider. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of soft dilepton excesses observed in accelerator experiments, most recently by the ALICE Collaboration at LHC in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV, where dielectrons below 150 MeV remained inaccessible. The results also connect to the astrophysical problem of the intense 511 keV photon line from the Galactic center, suggesting that ultraperipheral production of soft dielectrons could seed the formation of slow-moving parapositronia responsible for this emission, a hypothesis that requires further theoretical development.
