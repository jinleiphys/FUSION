# Deformed nuclear halos

> Deformation properties of weakly bound nuclei are discussed in the deformed single-particle model. It is demonstrated that in the limit of a very small binding energy the valence particles in specific orbitals, characterized by a very small projection of single-particle angular momentum onto the symmetry axis of a nucleus, can give rise to the halo structure which is completely decoupled from the rest of the system. The quadrupole deformation of the resulting halo is completely determined by the intrinsic structure of a weakly bound orbital, irrespective of the shape of the core.

## Key claim

In the limit of very small binding energy, valence particles in specific deformed single-particle orbitals characterized by a very small projection of angular momentum onto the symmetry axis can produce a halo structure completely decoupled from the core; the quadrupole deformation of the resulting halo is determined entirely by the intrinsic structure of the weakly bound orbital, irrespective of the shape of the core.

## Method

The authors solved the single-particle Schrodinger equation for a prolate spheroidal finite square well potential, separating the equation into three ordinary differential equations in spheroidal coordinates. They performed multipole decomposition of the deformed wave functions into spherical partial waves with well-defined orbital angular momentum, and analyzed the asymptotic behavior of radial moments, normalization integrals, rms radii, and quadrupole moments in the limit of vanishing binding energy. The calculations were carried out for various deformations and binding energies down to 5 keV.

## Key numbers

- For negative-parity halos (pi=-, Lambda=0 or 1), the quadrupole deformation beta_2 approaches the asymptotic limit of +0.63 for Lambda=0 and -0.31 for Lambda=1 as binding energy goes to zero, independent of core deformation.
- For positive-parity halos (pi=+, Lambda=0), beta_2 vanishes linearly with binding energy in the zero-binding limit.
- The rms radius diverges as (-epsilon_nu)^(-1) for positive-parity, Lambda=0 halos, and as (-epsilon_nu)^(-1/2) for negative-parity halos with Lambda=0 or 1.
- The normalization integral for positive-parity, Lambda=0 states diverges as (-epsilon_nu)^(-1/4), causing the probability to find the neutron in the outer region to approach 1 for zero binding.
- The spheroidal square well parameters used were U_0 = -80 MeV and R_0 = 4 fm, with superdeformed shape corresponding to delta=0.6.
- The quadrupole moment of the 1/2+ ground state of 11Be is associated with a neutron separation energy S_n = 504 keV; the core 10Be has |Q| = 229 mb.
- The quadrupole moment of the 2+ ground state of 8B is |Q| = 68 mb.

## Context

This work extends the spherical halo analysis of Riisager (1992) to deformed systems, and generalizes the asymptotic treatment of weakly bound states in deformed potentials. The results contradict the implicit assumption in earlier deformed calculations by Bouten et al. (1978), Ragnarsson et al. (1981), and Muta and Otsuka (1995) that the core deformation determines the halo shape; instead, the halo deformation decouples completely at very low binding. The findings enable future self-consistent mean-field calculations to account for the extreme shape softness of halo systems, where the quadrupole moment becomes extremely sensitive to small changes in single-particle energy, and provide a framework for interpreting experimental data on 11Be and 8B.
