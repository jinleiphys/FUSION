# Phase Diagrams of Relativistic Selfinteracting Boson System

> Within the Canonical Ensemble, we investigate a system of interacting relativistic bosons at finite temperatures and finite isospin densities in a mean-field approach. The mean field contains both attractive and repulsive terms. Temperature and isospin-density dependencies of thermodynamic quantities were obtained. It is shown that in the case of attraction between particles in a bosonic system, a liquid-gas phase transition develops against the background of the Bose-Einstein condensate. The corresponding phase diagrams are given. We explain the reasons why the presence of a Bose condensate significantly increases the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition compared to that obtained for the same system within the framework of the Boltzmann statistics. Our results may have implications for the interpretation of experimental data, in particular, how sensitive the critical point of the mixed phase is to the presence of the Bose-Einstein condensate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a relativistic boson system with both attractive and repulsive mean-field interactions, a liquid-gas phase transition develops against the background of Bose-Einstein condensation, and that the presence of the Bose condensate significantly increases the critical temperature of this liquid-gas transition compared to what would be obtained using Boltzmann statistics for the same system. This result is conditional on the attraction parameter being in the "weak" regime (kappa <= 1), where only the dominant component (pi- mesons) can condense.

## Method

The authors model a relativistic boson particle-antiparticle system (conventionally called "pions") at finite temperature and fixed isospin density within the Canonical Ensemble, using a thermodynamic mean-field approach. The mean field is parametrized with a Skyrme-like form U(n) = -A n + B n^2, containing both attractive (A) and repulsive (B) terms, where the attraction coefficient A is varied via a dimensionless parameter kappa = A/(2 sqrt(m B)). The system of self-consistent equations for total particle density and isospin density is solved numerically for temperatures above and below the Bose-Einstein condensation critical temperature, with the condensate phase treated by including a condensate component for pi- mesons when the condition U(n) - mu_I = -m is satisfied.

## Key numbers

* The repulsive coefficient is fixed at B/m = 2.025 fm^6, corresponding to a particle radius r_0 ≈ 0.3 fm and v_0 = 0.45 fm^3, with boson mass m = 139 MeV.
* For kappa = 0.1 and isospin densities n_I = 0.041, 0.065, and 0.1 fm^{-3}, the Bose-Einstein condensation transition temperatures are T_cd = 80, 102, and 127 MeV, respectively.
* At kappa = 0.1, the mean field changes sign at isospin density n_I0 ≈ 0.14 fm^{-3}, where U(n_I0) = 0.
* The critical value of the attraction coefficient is A_c = 2 sqrt(m B), which defines the boundary between weak (kappa <= 1) and strong (kappa > 1) attraction regimes.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of interacting boson systems at zero isospin density (Anchishkin and Mishustin, 2019; Mishustin and Anchishkin, 2019) and at finite isospin density within the Canonical Ensemble (Anchishkin, 2022, Physical Review C). The paper focuses on isospin-density dependencies at fixed temperature, complementing earlier temperature-dependent calculations. The results may inform interpretation of experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC regarding the sensitivity of the critical point of the mixed phase to the presence of Bose-Einstein condensate, and connect to lattice QCD studies of dense pion systems (Brandt, 2016; Brandt, 2017; Brandt, 2018).
