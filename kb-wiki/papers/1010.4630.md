# Gluon saturation and inclusive production at low transverse momenta

> In this letter we suggest the generalization of $k_T$-factorization formula for inclusive gluon production for the dense-dense parton system scattering. It turnes out that the soft gluon production with transverse momentum $p_T$ is suppressed by additional Sudakov-like factor that depends on $p^2_T/Q^2_s$ ratio in a good agreement with the first numerical calculation in Colour Glass Condensate approach by J.P. Blaizot, T. Lappi and Y. Mehtar-Tan.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the $k_T$-factorization formula for inclusive gluon production to the case of dense-dense parton system scattering, finding that soft gluon production with transverse momentum $p_T$ is suppressed by an additional Sudakov-like factor when $p_T$ is smaller than both saturation momenta. The author claims this suppression resolves the unphysical divergence of the $k_T$-factorization cross section as $p_T \to 0$ and agrees with numerical calculations in the Colour Glass Condensate approach.

## Method

The author derives a modified inclusive gluon production cross section by analyzing the double-logarithmic contribution from the first diagram in a series of gluon emissions, using the Low-Gribov theorem generalized to QCD. The calculation integrates over the kinematic region where the transverse momenta of intermediate gluons lie between $p_T$ and $Q_s$, yielding a Sudakov-like suppression factor $T(p_T^2/Q_s^2) = \exp\{-(\bar{\alpha}_s/2) \ln^2(Q_s^2/p_T^2)\}$. The result is compared to the numerical solution of Blaizot, Lappi and Mehtar-Tani (2010) for inclusive gluon production in the Colour Glass Condensate framework.

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor is $T(p_T^2/Q_s^2) = \exp\{-(\bar{\alpha}_s/2) \ln^2(Q_s^2/p_T^2)\}$, where $\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi$.
- The double-logarithmic contribution from the first diagram yields a factor $\ln^2(Q_s^2/p_T^2)$ in the amplitude.
- The average number of emitted soft gluons is $\langle n \rangle = (\bar{\alpha}_s/2) \ln^2(Q_s^2/p_T^2)$.
- For the comparison with numerical results, the author uses $\bar{\alpha}_s = 0.1$ and $Q_s = 1.5 g^2\mu$.

## Context

This work extends the $k_T$-factorization formalism of Catani, Ciafaloni and Hautmann (1991) and Collins and Ellis (1991), which was proven for dilute-dense scattering by Kovchegov and Tuchin (2002), to the dense-dense scattering regime where two saturation scales are present. The result contradicts the naive expectation that $k_T$-factorization holds for all $p_T$ and instead shows suppression for $p_T < Q_s$, in agreement with the numerical calculation of Blaizot, Lappi and Mehtar-Tani (2010). The suppression mechanism is analogous to the Sudakov form factor in Drell-Yan processes studied by Dokshitzer, Diakonov and Troian (1978) and Parisi and Petronzio (1979).
