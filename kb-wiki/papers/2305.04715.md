# Dense Baryonic Matter Predicted in "Pseudo-Conformal Model"

> The World-Class University/Hanyang Project launched in Korea in 2007 led to what's now called ``pseudo-conformal model" that addresses dense compact-star matter and is confronted in this short note with the presently available astrophysical observables, with focus on those from gravity waves. The predictions made nearly free of parameters by the model involving ``topology change" remain more or less intact ``un-torpedoed" by the data.

## Key claim

The "pseudo-conformal model" (PCM), built on a topology change from baryons to half-skyrmions at a density around 2-4 times nuclear saturation density, predicts that its parameter-free results for compact-star observables remain "un-torpedoed" by current gravitational-wave and NICER/XMM-Newton data, though the author acknowledges that the tidal deformability near the crossover density is in an "uncharted wilderness" for theory and that ruling out an equation of state based on the precise value of Lambda_1.4 would be premature.

## Method

The authors construct a "G_n_EFT" based on hidden local symmetry (HLS) and hidden scale symmetry (HSS), incorporating vector mesons (rho, omega) and a genuine dilaton scalar as relevant degrees of freedom beyond the pions and nucleons of standard chiral EFT. A "double-decimation" strategy applies a Wilsonian renormalization group to strongly-correlated fermions on the Fermi sphere, first to a Landau-Fermi liquid fixed point and then beyond it using V_lowK RG. The PCM replaces the V_lowK RG for densities above the topology-change density n_1/2 with a two-parameter analytic form matched at n_1/2, which reproduces the full V_lowK RG results for the entire density range.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E/A = -16.7 MeV, compression modulus K_0 = 250 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 30.2 MeV; symmetry energy slope: L = 67.8 MeV
- Topology-change (hadron-quark continuity) density range: n_HQC ~ n_1/2 ~ (2-4) n_0
- Maximum neutron star mass prediction: M_max ~ 2.05 M_sun, with radius R_2.0 ~ 12.8 km and central density ~ 5.1 n_0; compared to PSR J0740+6620: M_max = 2.08 +/- 0.07 M_sun, R_2.0 = 12.35 +/- 0.75 km
- 1.44 M_sun star radius prediction: R_1.44 ~ 12.8 km; compared to PSR J0030+0451: R_1.44 = 12.45 +/- 0.65 km
- Tidal deformability prediction: Lambda_1.4 ~ 550; compared to GW170817: Lambda_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120}
- Pseudo-conformal sound speed prediction: (v_s^{pcs}/c)^2 ~ 1/3 for n >= n_1/2
- Vector manifestation fixed point density: n_VM >= 25 n_0

## Context

This work extends the "pseudo-conformal model" developed from the World-Class University/Hanyang Project (2008-2013) and earlier work at the Korea Institute of Advanced Studies, building on the skyrmion-half-skyrmion topology change idea from Park and Vento (early 2000s) and the hidden local symmetry framework of Harada and Yamawaki. The model is contrasted with standard chiral effective field theory (SchiEFT), which works well up to n_0 but breaks down at higher densities, and with covariant density functional approaches that require arbitrary higher-dimension operators. The predictions enable direct confrontation with gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and NICER/XMM-Newton observations of neutron star radii and masses, though the author notes that the sound velocity bump near the crossover density remains a subject of intense debate, with some analyses (such as the "H-bump" from NICER/XMM-Newton data) potentially supporting the PCM's pseudo-conformal behavior.
