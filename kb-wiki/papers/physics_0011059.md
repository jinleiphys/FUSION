# On a correspondence between classical and quantum particle systems

> An exact correspondence is established between a $N$-body classical interacting system and a $N-1$-body quantum system with respect to the partition function. The resulting quantum-potential is a $N-1$-body one. Inversely the Kelbg potential is reproduced which describes quantum systems at a quasi-classical level. The found correspondence between classical and quantum systems allows also to approximate dense classical many body systems by lower order quantum perturbation theory replacing Planck's constant properly by temperature and density dependent expressions. As an example the dynamical behaviour of an one - component plasma is well reproduced concerning the formation of correlation energy after a disturbance utilising solely the analytical quantum - Born result for dense degenerated Fermi systems. As a practical guide the quantum - Bruckner parameter $r_s$ has been replaced by the classical plasma parameter $Γ$ as $r_s\approx0.3 Γ^{3/2}$

## Key claim

An exact correspondence is established between a classical N-body interacting system and a quantum N-1-body system with respect to the partition function, though the resulting quantum potential is a highly complex N-1-body nonlocal potential; the practical application is that dense classical systems can be approximated by lower-order quantum perturbation theory when Planck's constant is replaced by temperature- and density-dependent expressions.

## Method

The author rewrites the classical N-particle configuration integral using Meyer's cluster expansion and a complete set of N-1-particle wave functions, then defines an eigenvalue problem that maps the classical partition function onto a quantum N-1-particle Schrödinger equation with a nonlocal potential. The inverse mapping recovers the Kelbg potential for quantum systems at a quasi-classical level. As a phenomenological application, the author fits the quantum Born result for degenerate Fermi systems to classical one-component plasma simulations by replacing the Bruckner parameter r_s with the classical plasma parameter Γ.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit relation between the quantum Bruckner parameter r_s and the classical plasma parameter Γ is given as r_s ≈ 0.3 Γ^{3/2} (abstract) and equivalently r_s^fit = c √(3/8) Γ^{3/2} with c ≈ 0.5 (equation fit).
- The fit works throughout the range 1 ≤ Γ ≤ 10 for the classical one-component plasma.
- The thermal wave length λ is found to be nearly equal to the interparticle distance d in the range Γ = 1...10, where (3Γ)^{1/4} = 1...2.
- The third-order effective potential is given analytically as V^eff_3 ∼ (1/x)[erf²(x/√2) + (2^{3/2}x/√π) ∫_x^∞ (dz/z) e^{-z²/2} erf(z/√2)] with x = r/l.

## Context

This work extends conjectures from prior literature that a correspondence exists between quantum systems and higher-dimensional classical systems, specifically the mapping of a two-dimensional classical spin system onto a one-dimensional quantum system by Chung (1999) and the chaotic quantisation approach of BMM (2000). The paper also recovers the Kelbg potential for Coulomb systems, originally developed by Kelbg (1964) and others, as a special case of the correspondence. The practical result enables the use of quantum Born calculations to approximate the dynamics of strongly correlated classical plasmas, as demonstrated by comparison with the numerical simulations of GZ (1999) for the one-component plasma.
