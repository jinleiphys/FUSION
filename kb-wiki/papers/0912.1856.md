# Cold Quark Matter

> We perform an O(alpha_s^2) perturbative calculation of the equation of state of cold but dense QCD matter with two massless and one massive quark flavor, finding that perturbation theory converges reasonably well for quark chemical potentials above 1 GeV. Using a running coupling constant and strange quark mass, and allowing for further non-perturbative effects, our results point to a narrow range where absolutely stable strange quark matter may exist. Absent stable strange quark matter, our findings suggest that quark matter in compact star cores becomes confined to hadrons only slightly above the density of atomic nuclei. Finally, we show that equations of state including quark matter lead to hybrid star masses up to M~2M_solar, in agreement with current observations. For strange stars, we find maximal masses of M~2.75M_solar and conclude that confirmed observations of compact stars with M>2M_solar would strongly favor the existence of stable strange quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper performs an O(α_s²) perturbative calculation of the equation of state for cold dense QCD matter with two massless and one massive (strange) quark flavor, finding that perturbation theory converges reasonably well for quark chemical potentials above 1 GeV, but the existence of absolutely stable strange quark matter is only possible within a narrow range of parameters when allowing for non-perturbative effects and using a running coupling constant and strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors computed the grand potential of QCD at zero temperature to order g⁴ = (4πα_s)² using dimensional regularization in the MSbar scheme, separating contributions into massless quark loops, massive quark loops, two- and three-loop two-gluon-irreducible (2GI) vacuum diagrams, and the plasmon ring sum. The calculation included complete strange quark mass effects at order α_s², which had previously been dropped at that order, and used the running coupling constant and strange quark mass with renormalization group equations to evaluate the equation of state across different chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Perturbation theory converges reasonably well for quark chemical potentials above 1 GeV
- Strange quark mass at 2 GeV: m(2 GeV) ≃ 100 ± 30 MeV
- Atomic nuclei correspond to a quark chemical potential of roughly 300 MeV
- Λ_MSbar = 0.378 +0.034 -0.032 GeV (from matching α_s(2 GeV) = 0.2994 +0.0152 -0.0141)
- Hybrid star masses up to M ∼ 2 M_sun, in agreement with current observations
- Strange star maximal masses of M ∼ 2.75 M_sun
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n_sat ∼ 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the pioneering order α_s² calculations of Freedman and McLerran (1976) and Baluni (1977), who included strange quark mass effects only up to order α_s and dropped the mass entirely at order α_s². The authors argue that their perturbative equation of state with running α_s and strange quark mass should replace the MIT bag model (Chodos 1974) for semi-quantitative results, and their findings enable more reliable predictions for hybrid star and strange star masses that can be compared with astrophysical observations of compact stars.
