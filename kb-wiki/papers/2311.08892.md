# Parameters of nucleon densities and the Coulomb barrier in heavy-ion collisions

> When modeling nuclear processes which occur in heavy-ion reactions, it is necessary to calculate the potential energy of interaction between two nuclei. One of the main features determining the dynamics of the nucleus-nucleus collision is the Coulomb barrier, the knowledge of which is especially important when low- and intermediate-energy reactions are being studied. Our goal is to establish a parameterization of nucleon density distributions for calculation of the nucleus-nucleus double-folding potential in nuclear reactions. Special attention is paid to the description of the Coulomb barrier. The study analyzes experimental data on charge radii, diffuseness, and neutron skin thickness of atomic nuclei. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated in the framework of the double-folding method with the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction taken in the form of the zero-range Migdal potential. Based on this analysis and comparison with the Bass potential, parameters of nucleon density distributions are fitted to reproduce the Coulomb barrier. A method for correcting the parameters of nucleon densities to reproduce the Coulomb barrier with the double-folding potential is proposed. The presented way to correct nucleon densities allows obtaining a satisfactory description of the Coulomb barrier that is important for modeling near-barrier collisions of heavy ions.

# Parameters of nucleon densities and the Coulomb barrier in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose a method for correcting nucleon density parameters (specifically diffuseness) so that the double-folding potential with the zero-range Migdal nucleon-nucleon interaction reproduces the semi-empirical Bass potential barriers, but only for near-barrier collisions where the diabatic approximation with "frozen" densities is approximately valid and the correction is needed because such collisions are not strictly diabatic.

## Method

The study analyzes experimental data on charge radii, charge diffuseness, and neutron skin thickness from electron scattering, muonic atom experiments, and antiprotonic atom X-ray data. The nucleus-nucleus potential is calculated using the double-folding method with the zero-range Migdal effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the resulting Coulomb barriers are compared with the semi-empirical Bass potential. Based on this comparison, the diffuseness parameters of nucleon density distributions are fitted to reproduce the Bass barriers for specific colliding systems.

## Key numbers

- The charge rms radius approximation $R_{ch}=r_0 (1-bI+c/A) A^{1/3}$ yields coefficients $r_0=0.9560(14)$ fm, $b=0.1527(67)$, $c=2.326(63)$ with an rms deviation of 0.41 fm from experimental data for 813 nuclei with $Z\geq8$, $A\geq16$
- The average experimental charge diffuseness value is 0.556(4) fm based on data from DeVries (1987)
- The neutron skin thickness linear fit $\Delta r_{np}=aI+b$ from antiprotonic atom data gives $a=0.90(15)$ fm, $b=-0.03(2)$ fm
- The proton rms charge radius used is 0.841 fm (CODATA2018 value)
- The Migdal potential reference constant $C^*=300$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ corresponds to $\rho^*_{00}=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$; the normalization constant $C$ varies from 281 MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ (for $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O) to 312 MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ (for $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb)
- For $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, the difference $C-C^*=4.6$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ changes the potential minimum by 0.5 MeV against a Coulomb barrier of 177 MeV

## Context

This work extends the parameterization of nucleon density distributions for double-folding potential calculations, building on the experimental charge density compilations of DeVries (1987) and Angeli (2013), and the neutron skin thickness measurements from antiprotonic atoms by Trzcinska (2001) and Jastrzebski (2004). The authors compare their results with the semi-empirical Bass potential (Bass, 1977) and with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using SLy4 forces. The proposed correction method enables improved modeling of near-barrier heavy-ion collisions, which is relevant for fusion reactions used in superheavy element synthesis and for multinucleon transfer reactions.
