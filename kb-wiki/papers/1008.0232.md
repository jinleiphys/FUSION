# Measurement of the in-medium phi-meson width in proton-nucleus collisions

> The production of phi mesons in the collisions of 2.83 GeV protons with C, Cu, Ag, and Au at forward angles has been measured via the phi -> K+K- decay using the COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer. The phi meson production cross section follows a target mass dependence of A^0.56+/-0.02 in the momentum region of 0.6-1.6 GeV/c. The comparison of the data with model calculations suggests that the in-medium phi width is about an order of magnitude larger than its free value.

## Key claim

The in-medium width of the phi meson in cold nuclear matter is substantially larger than its free vacuum value of 4.3 MeV/c^2, but the exact extracted width depends on the theoretical model used to interpret the transparency ratio data, yielding values between about 45 and 73 MeV/c^2.

## Method

The experiment used a 2.83 GeV proton beam from COSY incident on thin C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets, detecting phi mesons via their K+K- decay channel using the ANKE magnetic spectrometer at forward angles (0-8 degrees) over a phi momentum range of 0.6-1.6 GeV/c. The transparency ratio R = (12 * sigma_pA) / (A * sigma_pC) was measured for each target relative to carbon, and the resulting A-dependence of the production cross section was compared to two different theoretical models (the Valencia local Fermi sea model and the Paryev nuclear spectral function model) to extract the in-medium phi width.

## Key numbers

- Free phi meson vacuum width: 4.3 MeV/c^2
- Target mass dependence exponent for phi production: alpha_phi = 0.56 +/- 0.02 over the momentum range 0.6-1.6 GeV/c
- Transparency ratios (relative to carbon): Cu/C = 0.479 +/- 0.011 (stat) +/- 0.035 (syst); Ag/C = 0.387 +/- 0.009 (stat) +/- 0.033 (syst); Au/C = 0.292 +/- 0.007 (stat) +/- 0.021 (syst)
- Fitted in-medium phi width from Valencia model (single-step, N=Z): Gamma = 27 (+5, -3) MeV/c^2; with isospin corrections and two-step processes: Gamma = 45 (+17, -9) MeV/c^2
- Fitted in-medium phi width from Paryev model (full calculation): Gamma = 73 (+14, -10) MeV/c^2 in the phi rest frame at saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, corresponding to about 50 MeV/c^2 in the nuclear rest frame at typical phi momentum of 1.1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of in-medium phi meson properties from the KEK-PS-E325 experiment (Muto, 2005), which found a width increase by a factor of 3.6 at normal nuclear density, and from photoproduction experiments at SPring8 (Ishikawa, 2004) and JLab (Wood, 2010), which indicated even larger broadening. The results are compared to theoretical predictions from the Valencia group (Cabrera, 2003; Magas, 2004) and from Paryev (2008), as well as ongoing BUU transport calculations (Schade, 2010). The measured width values clearly exceed the naive expectation of about 22 MeV/c^2 from a free phi-N cross section of 10 mb, indicating non-trivial medium effects, though the transparency ratio method cannot disentangle the various mechanisms for medium modification such as phi/omega mixing.
