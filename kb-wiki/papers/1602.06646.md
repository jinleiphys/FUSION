# Constraints on $s-\bar s$ asymmetry of the proton in chiral effective theory

> We compute the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry in the proton in chiral effective theory, using phenomenological constraints based upon existing data. Unlike previous meson cloud model calculations, which accounted for kaon loop contributions with on-shell intermediate states alone, this work includes off-shell terms and contact interactions, which impact the shape of the $s-\bar s$ difference. We identify a valence-like component of $s(x)$ which is balanced by a $δ$-function contribution to $\bar s(x)$ at $x=0$, so that the integrals of $s$ and $\bar s$ over the experimentally accessible region $x > 0$ are not equal. Using a regularization procedure that preserves chiral symmetry and Lorentz invariance, we find that existing data limit the integrated value of the second moment of the asymmetry to the range $-0.07 \times 10^{-3} \leq \langle x(s-\bar s) \rangle \leq 1.12 \times 10^{-3}$ at a scale of $Q^2=1 $GeV$^2$. This is too small to account for the NuTeV anomaly and of the wrong sign to enhance it.

## Key claim

The paper computes the $s-\bar s$ asymmetry in the proton using chiral effective theory with a regularization procedure that preserves chiral symmetry and Lorentz invariance, finding that existing data limit the integrated second moment of the asymmetry to a range that is too small to account for the NuTeV anomaly and of the wrong sign to enhance it. The key hedge is that the result depends on phenomenological constraints from $\Lambda$ hadroproduction data and on the choice of cutoff parameters $\mu_1$ and $\mu_2$ within the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme.

## Method

The authors computed the full set of lowest-order diagrams in chiral SU(3) effective theory, including kaon rainbow, kaon bubble, hyperon rainbow, tadpole, and Kroll-Ruderman diagrams, which go beyond previous meson cloud model calculations that only included on-shell intermediate states. They used Pauli-Villars regularization with two cutoff parameters ($\mu_1$ and $\mu_2$) to handle ultraviolet divergences while preserving chiral symmetry and Lorentz invariance, and constrained $\mu_1$ by fitting to $pp \to \Lambda X$ data from the CERN proton synchrotron. The strange quark PDFs in hyperons were related to proton PDFs using SU(3) symmetry, and the kaon PDFs were taken from a fit by Aicher et al. (2010).

## Key numbers

- The best fit value for the cutoff $\mu_1$ from $pp \to \Lambda X$ data is 545 MeV, with a lower limit of 526 MeV from a fit two standard deviations below the central values.
- The momentum fractions carried by $\bar s$ quarks in the nucleon range from $3.4 \times 10^{-3}$ to $5.7 \times 10^{-3}$.
- The integrated second moment of the strange asymmetry at $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ lies in the range $-0.07 \times 10^{-3} \leq \langle x(s-\bar s) \rangle \leq 1.12 \times 10^{-3}$.
- For $\mu_1 = 545$ MeV, the upper limit on $\mu_2$ is 600 MeV, giving $S^-$ in the range $-0.07 \times 10^{-3} \leq S^- \leq 0.42 \times 10^{-3}$.
- For $\mu_1 = 526$ MeV, the upper limit on $\mu_2$ is 894 MeV, giving $S^-$ in the range $-0.01 \times 10^{-3} \leq S^- \leq 1.12 \times 10^{-3}$.
- The correction to $\sin^2\theta_W$ from the strange asymmetry at $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ is $-7.7 \times 10^{-4} \leq \Delta(\sin^2\theta_W) \leq -6.7 \times 10^{-7}$, which reduces the NuTeV anomaly by less than $0.5\sigma$.

## Context

This work extends earlier meson cloud model calculations by Signal and Thomas (1987) and others (Malheiro et al., 1997; Cao et al., 2003; Hobbs et al., 2015) that only included on-shell intermediate states, by incorporating off-shell terms and contact interactions required by chiral symmetry. The results contradict the possibility that a large $s-\bar s$ asymmetry could explain the NuTeV anomaly, as the computed asymmetry is too small and of the wrong sign to enhance it. The analysis enables future empirical determination of $s-\bar s$ through associated production of charm with weak bosons at the LHC, as suggested by Alekhin et al. (2015), and calls for generalized PDF parametrizations that can accommodate the newly identified $\delta$-function contribution to $\bar s$ at $x=0$ and the valence-like component of $s(x)$.
