# Deconfinement Phase Transition in the $SU(3)$ Instanton-dyon Ensemble

> Confinement remains one the most interesting and challenging nonperturbative phenomenon in non-Abelian gauge theories. Recent semiclassical (for SU(2)) and lattice (for QCD) studies have suggested that confinement arises from interactions of statistical ensembles of instanton-dyons with the Polyakov loop. In this work, we extend studies of semiclassical ensemble of dyons to the $SU(3)$ Yang-Mills theory. We find that such interactions do generate the expected first-order deconfinement phase transition. The properties of the ensemble, including correlations and topological susceptibility, are studied over a range of temperatures above and below $T_c$. Additionally, the dyon ensemble is studied in the Yang-Mills theory containing an extra trace-deformation term. It is shown that such a term can cause the theory to remain confined and even retain the same topological observables at high temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper extends semiclassical instanton-dyon ensemble simulations from SU(2) to SU(3) Yang-Mills theory and finds that dyon interactions generate the expected first-order deconfinement phase transition, but only under the condition that the average Polyakov loop is restricted to be real, which reduces the holonomy to a single parameter with ν in [0, 1/2] and enforces symmetry between M1- and M2-type dyons.

## Method

The authors construct a partition function for an ensemble of six dyon species (three dyon types and their antidyons) in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, including the Gross-Pisarski-Yaffe perturbative potential, classical Coulomb-like interactions with a modified short-range parametrization, repulsive cores, and the one-loop Diakonov determinant from the metric of collective variables. They perform Monte-Carlo simulations with 120-122 dyons in a periodic box with 26 image boxes, compute the free energy density via integration over a coupling parameter λ in 10 steps with 2000 configurations each, and locate global minima in the three-dimensional parameter space (ν, n_M, n_L) for each temperature by quadratic fitting.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition occurs at instanton action S_0 = 13.18, corresponding to a critical temperature T_c.
- The temperature range studied is 0.62 T_c < T < 2.04 T_c, corresponding to approximately 160 MeV < T < 530 MeV in physical units.
- At T → T_c⁻ (confined phase): ν = 1/3, ⟨P⟩ = 0, n_M = 0.550, n_L = 0.550.
- At T → T_c⁺ (deconfined phase): ν = 0.236, ⟨P⟩ = 0.392, n_M = 0.529, n_L = 0.068.
- The Polyakov loop jumps from 0 to approximately 0.4 at the phase transition.
- The dyon action in the confined phase is S_0/3, with a lower bound of S_i ≈ 2.67 at S_0 = 8.
- In the deconfined phase, the M-dyon action remains nearly constant at S_M ≈ 3 up to 2 T_c.

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical studies of instanton-dyon ensembles in SU(2) gauge theory by Larsen and Shuryak (2015) and Lopez-Ruiz and Shuryak (2016), which found a second-order deconfinement transition, to the SU(3) case where lattice studies by Kaczmarek (2002) show a first-order transition. The results confirm that the dyon ensemble reproduces the first-order nature of the SU(3) deconfinement transition and enable future comparisons with lattice data on Polyakov loop temperature dependence and dyon spatial correlations, as well as studies of the trace-deformed Yang-Mills theory where the ensemble remains confined at high temperatures.
