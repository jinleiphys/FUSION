# Do we know how to count powers in pionless and pionful effective field theory?

> In this article I summarize recent progress in the effective field theory approach to low energy nuclear systems, with a focus on the power counting issue. In the pionless sector, where the power counting is quite well understood at the nucleon-nucleon (NN) level, I discuss some recent developments toward few- and many-body calculations. In the pionful sector, I focus on the actively debated issue of power counting in the NN sector and some recent developments toward a model-independent NN interaction. Finally, the scenario that the power counting might depend on the number of particles is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while power counting in pionless effective field theory (EFT) is well understood at the nucleon-nucleon (NN) level and reasonably understood at the few-body level, the power counting in pionful EFT remains actively debated and unresolved, with the possibility that power counting may depend on the number of particles in the system.

## Method

The author reviews the current status of power counting in both pionless and pionful EFT by examining renormalization group (RG) requirements, the need for higher-body forces, and the failure of Weinberg's prescription (WPC) to satisfy RG invariance. For pionful EFT, the paper compares three alternative power counting schemes (Birse, Valderrama, and BY) that treat subleading corrections perturbatively and discusses the "peratization" argument against using cutoffs above the breakdown scale.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale for pionless EFT is M_hi ~ 140 MeV, since effects of pion-exchange are not included
- The estimated breakdown scale for pionful EFT ranges from M_hi = 600-1000 MeV, depending on whether the sigma or f0(600) is counted or the value 4*pi*f_pi ~ 1000 MeV from chiral perturbation theory is adopted
- The neutron-proton scattering length a_np = -23.7 fm in the 1S0 channel and 5.4 fm in the 3S1-3D1 channel, much larger than the range ~ 1/m_pi = 1-2 fm
- The pion decay constant f_pi ~ 93 MeV and the axial coupling constant g_A ~ 1.27
- The mass difference between Delta(1232) excitation and nucleon is smaller than 300 MeV
- For singular and attractive P-waves in the BY counting, amplitudes scale as (p_cm,p_typ)^3/M_lo^3 at LO, (p_cm,p_typ)^5/(M_lo^3 M_hi^2) at NNLO, and (p_cm,p_typ)^6/(M_lo^3 M_hi^3) at N3LO

## Context

This review extends the discussion of power counting from the well-established pionless EFT framework (where analytical solutions exist for NN systems and three-body forces are required at LO) to the more controversial pionful sector. The paper contrasts Weinberg's prescription (Weinberg, 1990, 1991), which iterates the chiral potential non-perturbatively but fails RG requirements as shown by Nogga, Yang, and others, with alternative schemes by Birse, Valderrama, and BY that treat subleading corrections perturbatively. The work enables future numerical studies to determine whether the conjecture that A-body forces enter at N^{A-3}LO for bosonic systems holds, and to establish the applicability limits of pionless EFT for heavier nuclei.
