# Chiral Particle Decay of Heavy-Light Mesons in a Relativistic Potential Model

> Partial decay widths of the heavy-light mesons, $D, D_s, B,$ and $B_s$, emitting one chiral particle ($π$ or $K$) are evaluated in the framework of a relativistic potential model. Decay amplitudes are calculated by keeping the Lorentz invariance as far as possible and use has been made of the Lorentz-boosted relativistic wave functions of the heavy-light mesons. One of predictions of our calculation is very narrow widths of a few keV for yet undsicovered $B_s(0^+, 1^+)$ mesons corresponding to ${^{2S+1}L_J}={^3P_0}$ and $"{^3P_1}"$ assuming their masses to be 5617 and 5682 MeV, respectively, as calculated in our former paper. In the course of our calculation, new sum rules are discovered on the decay widths in the limit of $m_Q\to \infty$. Among these rules, $ Γ(D_{s0}^*(2317)\to D_s+π)=Γ(D_{s1}(2460)\to D_s^*+π) $ and $ Γ(B_{s0}^*(5615)\to B_s+π)=Γ(B_{s1}(5679)\to B_s^*+π) $ are predicted to hold with a good accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the yet-undiscovered $B_s(0^+, 1^+)$ mesons (corresponding to ${^3P_0}$ and ${^3P_1}$ states) have very narrow decay widths of a few keV, assuming their masses are 5617 and 5682 MeV respectively as calculated in the authors' prior work; this prediction depends on these states being below the threshold for kaonic decay, forcing them to decay only through $\pi^0$-$\eta$ mixing.

## Method

The authors evaluate partial decay widths for heavy-light mesons ($D$, $D_s$, $B$, $B_s$) emitting one chiral particle ($\pi$ or $K$) using a relativistic potential model with linear and Coulombic potentials. They compute Lorentz-invariant transition amplitudes by boosting the heavy-light meson wave functions from the rest frame to the Breit frame, replacing the plane wave of the emitted particle with a phase factor $e^{-2im_QVz}$ that accounts for recoil effects. The chiral interaction is taken from the Georgi-Manohar model, and the axial-vector coupling constant $g$ is fixed by fitting the calculated decay width of $D^{*+}(2010)$ to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- $g^2 = 0.608$ (axial-vector coupling constant squared), determined by fitting $D^{*+}(2010)$ decay widths
- $\epsilon = 1.00 \times 10^{-2}$ (mixing parameter for $\pi^0$-$\eta$ mixing), leading to a factor of $\epsilon^2 = 1.00 \times 10^{-4}$ in decay widths for kinematically forbidden strange decays
- $D_{s0}^*(2317) \to D_s + \pi$: $\Gamma_{th} = 3.8 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (with $k_R = 297.7$ MeV/$c$)
- $D_{s1}(2460) \to D_s^* + \pi$: $\Gamma_{th} = 3.9 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (with $k_R = 297.3$ MeV/$c$)
- $B_{s0}^*(5615) \to B_s + \pi^0$: $\Gamma_{th} = 1.6 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (with $k_R = 204.2$ MeV/$c$)
- $B_{s1}(5679) \to B_s^* + \pi^0$: $\Gamma_{th} = 1.9 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (with $k_R = 224.0$ MeV/$c$)
- $D_1(2420)$ ("$^1P_1$") total width: $\Gamma_{th} = 6.9$ MeV (sum over $D^*\pi$ channels) vs. $\Gamma_{exp} = 20.4 \pm 1.7$ MeV
- $D_1(2430)$ ("$^3P_1$") total width: $\Gamma_{th} = 1.9 \times 10^2$ MeV (sum over $D^*\pi$ channels) vs. $\Gamma_{exp} = 384^{+107}_{-75} \pm 74$ MeV
- $B_2^*(5747)^0$ total width: $\Gamma_{th} = 30$ MeV (sum over $B\pi + B^*\pi$) vs. $\Gamma_{exp} = 22.7^{+3.8+3.2}_{-3.2-10.2}$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic potential model calculations of heavy-light meson mass spectra (Matsuki and Seo, prior paper) and semileptonic weak form factors (Matsuki and Seo, 2003) to chiral particle decays. It improves upon earlier calculations by Goity and Roberts (1999) and Di Pierro and Eichten (2001), who used wave functions in the rest frame and neglected both relativistic wave function effects and recoil effects of the heavy-light mesons. The paper discovers new sum rules in the $m_Q \to \infty$ limit, including $\Gamma(D_{s0}^*(2317) \to D_s + \pi) = \Gamma(D_{s1}(2460) \to D_s^* + \pi)$ and the analogous relation for $B_s$ mesons, which hold to within 10% or better. These results enable predictions for yet-unobserved $B_s$ states and provide a framework for testing the relativistic potential model against future experimental data.
