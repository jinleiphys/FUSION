# Wobbling motion in triaxial nuclei

> The experimental evidence for the collective wobbling motion of triaxial nuclei is reviewed. The classification into transverse and longitudinal in the presence of quasiparticle excitations is discussed. The description by means of the quasiparticle+triaxial rotor model is discussed in detail. The structure of the states is analyzed using the spin-coherent-state and spin-squeezed-state representations of the reduced density matrices of the total and particle angular momenta, which distill the corresponding classical precessional motions. Various approximate solutions of the quasiparticle+triaxial rotor model are evaluated. The microscopic studies of wobbling in the small-amplitude random phase approximation are discussed. Selected studies of wobbling by means of the triaxial projected shell model are presented, which focus on how this microscopic approach removes certain deficiencies of the semi-microscopic quasiparticle+triaxial rotor model.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the experimental evidence for collective wobbling motion in triaxial nuclei, arguing that the classification of wobbling states should be based on the topology of classical angular momentum orbits relative to a separatrix, rather than restricting the term "wobbling" to harmonic precession as some authors have proposed.

## Method

The author analyzes the triaxial rotor (TR) model and the quasiparticle+triaxial rotor (QTR) model, using spin-coherent-state (SCS) representations of reduced density matrices to visualize the classical precessional motion of the total and particle angular momenta. The eigenstates are classified by their correspondence with classical orbits of the angular momentum vector, distinguishing between wobbling states (orbits inside the separatrix revolving around the medium axis) and flip states (orbits near the separatrix), with transverse and longitudinal wobbling classifications based on the precession cone topology relative to the principal axes.

## Key numbers

- The triaxial rotor asymmetry parameter κ = (2A₁ − A₂ − A₃)/(A₂ − A₃) ranges from −1 (prolate spheroid) to 1 (oblate spheroid), with maximal triaxiality at κ = 0
- For the TR calculation shown, moments of inertia are Jₘ = 30 ℏ²/MeV, Jₛ = 10 ℏ²/MeV, Jₗ = 5 ℏ²/MeV, corresponding to κ = 0.24
- The harmonic wobbling frequency is ℏω_w = 2I[(A₁ − A₂)(A₃ − A₂)]^{1/2} for the lowest states far below the separatrix
- For γ = 30° with irrotational-flow MoI's, the ratios are Jₘ:Jₛ:Jₗ = 4:1:1 (symmetric top limit)
- For γ = 10° with irrotational-flow MoI's, the ratios are Jₘ:Jₛ:Jₗ = 29:19:1 (κ = 0.96)
- The TR calculation for ¹¹²Ru uses γ = 19° and J₀ = 30(1 + 0.2I) ℏ²/MeV
- For ¹⁶³Lu TSD bands, the QTR calculation uses parameters ε = 0.4, γ = 20°, and MoI's Jₘ,ₛ,ₗ = (64, 56, 13) ℏ²/MeV

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Bohr and Mottelson (Nuclear Structure II) who introduced the concept of wobbling as precessional motion of the nuclear axes, and addresses the controversy with Lawrie et al. (2020) who proposed restricting "wobbling" to harmonic oscillations and calling other orbits "Tilted Precessions" (TiP). The paper discusses the discovery of wobbling in triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) nuclei by the Copenhagen group in ¹⁶¹⁻¹⁶⁷Lu and ¹⁶⁷Ta, the inconsistencies in early PTR interpretations by Hamamoto and Hagemann (1986) who exchanged the moments of inertia, and the subsequent resolution through the transverse/longitudinal classification. The review enables future microscopic studies using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) to address deficiencies of the semi-microscopic QTR model, particularly for γ-soft nuclei.
