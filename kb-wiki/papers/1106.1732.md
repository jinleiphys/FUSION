# A Fast Numerical Scheme for Causal Relativistic Hydrodynamics with Dissipation

> In this paper, we develop a stable and fast numerical scheme for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics based on Israel-Stewart theory. Israel-Stewart theory is a stable and causal description of dissipation in relativistic hydrodynamics although it includes relaxation process with the timescale for collision of constituent particles, which introduces stiff equations and makes practical numerical calculation difficult. In our new scheme, we use Strang's splitting method, and use the piecewise exact solutions for solving the extremely short timescale problem. In addition, since we split the calculations into inviscid step and dissipative step, Riemann solver can be used for obtaining numerical flux for the inviscid step. The use of Riemann solver enables us to capture shocks very accurately. Simple numerical examples are shown. The present scheme can be applied to various high energy phenomena of astrophysics and nuclear physics.

## Key claim
The authors develop a stable and efficient numerical scheme for causal relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics based on Israel-Stewart theory, using Strang splitting and piecewise exact solutions to overcome the stiffness introduced by relaxation timescales that are much shorter than the hydrodynamic timescale.

## Method
The relativistic dissipative fluid equations are split into an inviscid (ideal fluid) part and a dissipative part using Strang's splitting method. The inviscid part is solved with a relativistic Riemann solver to accurately capture shocks, while the stiff evolution equations for dissipation variables (bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, heat flux) are integrated using the Piecewise Exact Solution (PES) method, which employs formal solutions to eliminate the Courant condition for the relaxation timescale. The scheme is applied to one-dimensional and multi-dimensional test problems in flat Cartesian coordinates using the Eckart decomposition for the four-velocity.

## Key numbers
- Relaxation times in the non-relativistic limit (beta → infinity, Gamma = 5/3): tau_Pi = (2/5)(zeta beta^2 / p), tau_q = (2 kappa T beta)/(5 p), tau_pi = eta/p
- Relaxation times in the ultra-relativistic limit (beta → 0, Gamma = 4/3): tau_Pi = (72 zeta)/(beta^4 p), tau_q = (5 kappa T)/(4 p), tau_pi = (3 eta)/(2 p)
- The orthogonality conditions reduce the physical degrees of freedom of the heat flux vector from 4 to 3 components and the viscosity tensor from 10 to 6 components

## Context
This work extends the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979), which provides a causal and stable description of relativistic dissipation, by addressing the numerical difficulty of integrating stiff relaxation equations. The first numerical scheme for Israel-Stewart theory was proposed by Molnar et al. (2010), who used SHASTA for shock capturing and explicit differencing for dissipation variables, requiring extremely high resolution to resolve both macroscopic and relaxation timescales. The present scheme enables efficient simulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics for applications in high-energy astrophysics (ultra-relativistic jets, GRBs, neutron star mergers) and nuclear physics (quark-gluon plasma at RHIC).
