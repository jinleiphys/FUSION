# Equivalence of pion loops in equal-time and light-front dynamics

> We demonstrate the equivalence of the light-front and equal-time formulations of pionic corrections to nucleon properties. As a specific example, we consider the self-energy of a nucleon dressed by pion loops, for both pseudovector and pseudoscalar pion-nucleon couplings. We derive the leading and next-to-leading nonanalytic behavior of the self-energy on the light-front, and show explicitly their equivalence in the rest frame and infinite momentum frame in equal-time quantization, as well as in a manifestly covariant formulation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the light-front and equal-time formulations of pionic corrections to nucleon properties are equivalent, provided one carefully handles loop integrations and compares the same theories (pseudovector or pseudoscalar pion-nucleon interactions). The apparent conflict between light-front meson cloud model results and covariant chiral perturbation theory is resolved when arc contributions at infinity and end-point contributions at momentum fractions 0 and 1 are properly included.

## Method

The authors computed the self-energy of a nucleon dressed by a pion loop using three formalisms: manifestly covariant dimensional regularization, equal-time time-ordered perturbation theory (in both the nucleon rest frame and the infinite momentum frame), and light-front dynamics. They derived the leading and next-to-leading nonanalytic behavior of the self-energy for both pseudovector and pseudoscalar pion-nucleon couplings, and compared the results across all frameworks.

## Key numbers

- Pseudovector coupling constant: f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi ≈ 0.08
- Pseudoscalar coupling constant: g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi ≈ 14.3
- Axial vector charge: g_A = 1.267
- Pion decay constant: f_\pi ≈ 93 MeV
- Leading nonanalytic term in the self-energy: - (3 g_A^2 / (32 π f_\pi^2)) m_\pi^3
- Next-to-leading nonanalytic term: - (3 g_A^2 / (32 π f_\pi^2)) × (1/(2π)) (m_\pi^4 / M) log m_\pi^2
- The positive energy diagram contributes m_\pi^3 + (3/(4π)) (m_\pi^4 / M) log m_\pi^2 to the LNA behavior
- The Z-graph contributes - (1/(4π)) (m_\pi^4 / M) log m_\pi^2 to the LNA behavior

## Context

This work extends the demonstration of equivalence between light-front and covariant formalisms previously shown by Bakker et al. for the vector two-point function and pseudoscalar charge form factor in 1+1 dimensions, and by Sawicki for scalar φ^3 theory. The paper resolves a conflict between the leading nonanalytic behavior of twist-two matrix elements computed in the meson cloud model on the light-front by Thomas, Melnitchouk, and Schreiber (which used pseudoscalar coupling) and results from covariant chiral perturbation theory by Detmold, Xu, Dong, Ji, and Savage (which used pseudovector coupling). The techniques developed here enable consistent calculation of chiral corrections to generalized parton distributions on the light-front, which will be the subject of a subsequent work.
