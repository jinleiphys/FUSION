# The Deuteron as a Canonically Quantized Biskyrmion

> The ground state configurations of the solution to Skyrme's topological soliton model for systems with baryon number larger than 1 are well approximated with rational map ansätze, without individual baryon coordinates. Here canonical quantization of the baryon number 2 system, which represents the deuteron, is carried out in the rational map approximation. The solution, which is described by the 6 parameters of the chiral group SU(2)$\times$SU(2), is stabilized by the quantum corrections. The matter density of the variational quantized solution has the required exponential large distance falloff and the quantum numbers of the deuteron. Similarly to the axially symmetric semiclassical solution, the radius and the quadrupole moment are, however, only about half as large as the corresponding empirical values. The quantized deuteron solution is constructed for representations of arbitrary dimension of the chiral group.

# The Deuteron as a Canonically Quantized Biskyrmion

## Key claim

The deuteron can be described as a canonically quantized baryon number 2 skyrmion (biskyrmion) using six collective coordinates from the chiral SU(2)×SU(2) group, with quantum corrections stabilizing the solution; however, the predicted radius and quadrupole moment are only about half as large as the empirical values, similar to the semiclassical axially symmetric solution.

## Method

The authors employ the rational map ansatz for the B=2 skyrmion, which approximates the classical ground state configuration with better than 3% accuracy, and treat the six parameters of the chiral SU(2)×SU(2) group as collective dynamical coordinates for canonical quantization. The canonical quantization procedure, developed previously by Fujii et al. (1987) and Acus et al. (1998), treats the field variables as quantum-mechanical ab initio, generating negative quantum corrections that stabilize the soliton. The Hamiltonian is expressed in terms of two independent angular momentum operators, and the deuteron state is constructed as an eigenstate with spin S=1 and isospin T=0 from the eigenstates of two subsystems representing the neutron and proton.

## Key numbers

- The classical rational map ansatz approximates the ground state energy of the B=2 skyrmion with an accuracy of better than 3%.
- For the fundamental representation (j=1/2), the predicted deuteron mass is 1868 MeV, charge radius 1.10 fm, matter radius 1.22 fm, and quadrupole moment 0.140 fm², compared to empirical values of 1876 MeV, 2.13 fm, 1.97 fm, and 0.286 fm² respectively.
- For the j=1 representation, the predicted deuteron mass is 1926 MeV, charge radius 1.17 fm, matter radius 1.29 fm, and quadrupole moment 0.158 fm².
- For the j=3/2 representation, the predicted deuteron mass is 1998 MeV, charge radius 1.24 fm, matter radius 1.35 fm, and quadrupole moment 0.177 fm².
- For the reducible representation 1⊕1/2⊕1/2, the predicted deuteron mass is 1907 MeV, charge radius 1.15 fm, matter radius 1.27 fm, and quadrupole moment 0.152 fm².
- The falloff mass m, describing the exponential decay of the matter density at large distances, is 54.6 MeV for j=1/2, 90.5 MeV for j=1, 110.0 MeV for j=3/2, and 82.3 MeV for the reducible representation, compared to the empirical value of 91.4 MeV (2√(B M_n)).
- The maximum mass density of the deuteron solution is 1150 MeV·fm⁻³, with density maxima forming a ring of diameter 1.424 fm.
- The pion decay constant f_π is fitted to values between 55.71 and 57.68 MeV across representations, compared to the empirical 93 MeV.
- The Skyrme parameter e is fitted to values between 3.79 and 4.325 across representations.

## Context

This work extends the canonical quantization approach of Fujii et al. (1987) and Acus et al. (1998) from the B=1 nucleon to the B=2 deuteron system, using the rational map ansatz of Houghton et al. (1998) which approximates the classical ground state solutions of Battye et al. (1997) and Manton et al. (1997). The results are similar to the semiclassical axially symmetric description of Braaten et al. (1988), which also yields radii and quadrupole moments about half the empirical values. The approach enables construction of dibaryon states from nucleons and Δ resonances, and the toroidal density structure found here has been shown to arise in quantum mechanical treatments of the two-nucleon system with realistic interactions (Forest et al., 1996). More realistic static parameters for the deuteron have been obtained using Skyrme's product ansatz for the two-nucleon system (Nyman et al., 1987).
