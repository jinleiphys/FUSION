# Photon and dilepton production from a non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate leading-order medium photon and dilepton yields from a quark-gluon plasma using (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. In the case of dileptons, at leading-order it is sufficient to take into account non-equilibrium corrections to the rate through the use of anisotropic distribution functions. In the case of photons, non-equilibrium corrections to the rate are taken into account using a self-consistent modification of the distribution functions and the corresponding anisotropic hard-loop quark self-energies. We present predictions for the high-energy photon and dilepton spectrum and the photon elliptic flow as a function of invariant mass, transverse momentum, shear viscosity, and initial momentum-space anisotropy. Our findings indicate that both high-energy photon and dilepton production are sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma. In addition, we find that the photon elliptic flow depends on the initial momentum-space anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both high-energy photon and dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma are sensitive to the assumed level of initial momentum-space anisotropy, and that photon elliptic flow depends on this anisotropy; however, these conclusions are based on leading-order calculations using a spheroidal approximation for the distribution functions and a (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics model that holds final particle multiplicity fixed as shear viscosity is varied.

## Method

The authors computed leading-order medium photon and dilepton yields from a quark-gluon plasma using (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. For dileptons, non-equilibrium corrections were included through anisotropic distribution functions of spheroidal form for quarks and anti-quarks. For photons, the total rate was computed as a sum of hard contributions (from annihilation and Compton processes) and soft contributions (using the Keldysh formalism with hard-loop resummed fermion propagators), with an infrared cutoff regulating the ultraviolet divergence in the soft contribution.

## Key numbers

- For dilepton pairs at midrapidity, the invariant mass and transverse momentum spectra were computed for shear viscosity values $4\pi \eta/s \in \{0, 0.1, 1, 2, 3, 6, 10\}$, showing no significant dependence on $\eta/s$.
- For dilepton transverse momentum spectra, initial anisotropy parameters $\xi_0 = -0.9, 0, 10,$ and $100$ were tested with $4\pi \eta/s = 3$, showing sensitivity to initial anisotropy particularly at large $p_\perp$.
- For photons with $4\pi\eta/s = 3$, at $q_\perp = 6$ GeV, the QGP photon spectrum varied by approximately an order of magnitude when varying the initial anisotropy in the range shown.
- For photon elliptic flow $v_2$, increasing shear viscosity resulted in at most a 300% increase in photon $v_2$ for the values of $\eta/s$ shown (with $4\pi\eta/s = 1, 2, 3$).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production by Mauricio (2007), Martinez (2008), and photon production by Schenke (2006, 2008) by incorporating a realistic (3+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics model of the background evolution. The results indicate that electromagnetic observables can serve as probes of early-time momentum-space anisotropies in the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that future work should include non-spheroidal corrections to assess their impact on photon elliptic flow and spectra.
