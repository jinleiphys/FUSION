# Transition sum rules in the shell model

> An important characterization of electromagnetic and weak transitions in atomic nuclei are sum rules. We focus on the non-energy-weighted sum rule (NEWSR), or total strength, and the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR); the ratio of the EWSR to the NEWSR is the centroid or average energy of transition strengths from an nuclear initial state to all allowed final states. These sum rules can be expressed as expectation values of operators, in the case of the EWSR a double commutator. While most prior applications of the double-commutator have been to special cases, we derive general formulas for matrix elements of both operators in a shell model framework (occupation space), given the input matrix elements for the nuclear Hamiltonian and for the transition operator. With these new formulas, we easily evaluate centroids of transition strength functions, with no need to calculate daughter states. We apply this simple tool to a number of nuclides, and demonstrate the sum rules follow smooth secular behavior as a function of initial energy, as well as compare the electric dipole (E1) sum rule against the famous Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn version. We also find surprising systematic behaviors for ground state electric quadrupole (E2) centroids in the $sd$-shell.

## Key claim

The authors derive general formulas for the non-energy-weighted sum rule (NEWSR) and energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) operators in a shell model occupation-space framework, enabling direct computation of transition strength centroids without calculating any final states; however, the EWSR double-commutator form requires the transition operator to satisfy the spherical tensor adjoint condition, which means for charge-changing transitions like Gamow-Teller one must sum both beta-minus and beta-plus transitions rather than treating them separately.

## Method

The authors express the NEWSR and EWSR as expectation values of one-body plus two-body operators in the configuration-interaction shell model, using fermion creation and annihilation operators with good angular momentum. They derive explicit formulas for the one-body and two-body matrix elements of both sum rule operators in terms of the Hamiltonian matrix elements and the reduced one-body matrix elements of the transition operator. They implement these formulas in the BIGSTICK shell model code and test them by fully diagonalizing modest model spaces (M-scheme dimensions of a few thousand) to compute transition strengths between all states for verification.

## Key numbers

- For 27Ne in the sd shell, the total beta-minus strength is 21.239 g_A^2 and the total beta-plus strength is 0.239 g_A^2, satisfying the Ikeda sum rule
- The ground state E1 energy-weighted sum rule for Z=N nuclides in the p-sd5/2 space shows enhancement over the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn prediction between 40% and 125%
- The first 3- state in 16O is placed at approximately 6.1 MeV above the ground state in the cross-shell interaction
- The sd shell calculations use harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions with hbar Omega = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV
- Effective charges of 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons are used for E2 transitions in the sd shell

## Context

This work extends prior applications of double-commutator sum rules, which had been limited to special cases, by providing general formulas applicable to any one-body transition operator in a shell model framework. The authors build on the spectral distribution theory approach of Johnson (2015) for the NEWSR secular behavior, and compare their E1 results to the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule as formulated by Towner (1977). The methodology enables systematic studies of transition strength centroids across many nuclides, with potential applications to no-core shell model calculations, investigations of g_A quenching, and tests of the Brink-Axel hypothesis.
