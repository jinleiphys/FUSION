# Velocity of sound in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We have studied the rapidity distribution of secondary hadrons produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra-relativistic energies within the ambit of the Landau's hydrodynamical model. A reasonable description of the data can also be obtained by using the Bjorken's hydrodynamical model if the boost invariance is restricted to a finite rapidity range. The sensitivity of the hadronic spectra on the equation of state vis- a -vis the velocity of sound has been discussed. The correlation between the velocity of sound and the freeze-out temperature has been indicated. The effects of the non-zero widths of various mesonic and baryonic degrees of freedom up to the mass value ~ 2.5 GeV is seen to be small.

## Key claim

The velocity of sound squared extracted from rapidity distributions of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions at AGS and SPS energies is approximately \(c_s^2 \sim 1/5\), a value significantly lower than the ideal gas limit of 1/3, and this value appears to be universal across different hadron species and collision energies near the freeze-out stage.

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state for a hadron gas including all hadrons up to the strange sector with vacuum spectral functions, then derived the velocity of sound from the temperature dependence of the effective statistical degeneracy. They fitted the rapidity distributions of protons (AGS), pions, and kaons (SPS) using Landau's hydrodynamical model with a fixed freeze-out temperature of 120 MeV (132 MeV for K\(^-\)), treating the velocity of sound as a free parameter and evaluating the fit quality via \(\chi^2\). They also applied Bjorken's hydrodynamical model with a finite rapidity range as a free parameter to the same data.

## Key numbers

- For protons at AGS (4, 6, 8, 11.6 AGeV Au+Au), Landau model with \(c_s^2 = 1/5\) yields \(\chi^2\) values of 0.65, 2.15, 7.1, and 1.49 respectively, while \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) gives \(\chi^2\) values of 34.5, 40.2, 74.8, and 31.1.
- For \(\pi^-\) at SPS (40, 80, 158 AGeV Pb+Pb), Landau model with \(c_s^2 = 1/5\) and \(T_f = 120\) MeV yields \(\chi^2\) values of 1.03, 2.6, and 19.0 respectively; with \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) the \(\chi^2\) values are 51.7, 66.3, and 146.0.
- For K\(^+\) at SPS (40, 80, 158 AGeV), Landau model with \(c_s^2 = 1/5\) and \(T_f = 120\) MeV gives \(\chi^2\) values of 0.463, 0.4, and 0.39 respectively; for K\(^-\) at the same energies, \(c_s^2 = 1/5\) with \(T_f = 132\) MeV gives \(\chi^2\) values of 0.56, 3.0, and 3.8.
- The Gaussian width \(\sigma\) of the fluid rapidity distribution (from Eq. 6prime) for pions is 0.9, 1.1, and 1.25 at beam energies 40, 80, and 158 AGeV respectively; for kaons the corresponding \(\sigma\) values are 1.25, 1.45, and 1.95.
- The velocity of sound squared from the hadron gas model at zero baryonic chemical potential is compared with lattice QCD results; the effect of finite baryonic chemical potential (200 MeV) leads to slightly lower \(c_s^2\) at freeze-out temperature of 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of Landau's hydrodynamical model (Landau and collaborators, 1950s) and Bjorken's hydrodynamical model (Bjorken, 1983) to extract the velocity of sound from experimental rapidity distributions at AGS and SPS energies. The extracted value \(c_s^2 \sim 1/5\) is compared with lattice QCD calculations (Karsch, 2002) and a confinement model (Rischke et al., 1995), showing agreement near the critical temperature. The results enable future studies of the equation of state of hadronic matter and the space-time evolution of the system formed in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the maintenance of thermal equilibrium during expansion.
