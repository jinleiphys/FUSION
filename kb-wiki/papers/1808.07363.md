# A multiquark approach to excited hadrons and Regge trajectories

> We propose a novel approach to construction of hadron spectroscopy. The case of light nonstrange mesons is considered. By assumption, all such mesons above 1 GeV appear due to creation of constituent quark-antiquark pairs inside the pi or rho (omega) mesons. These spin-singlet or triplet pairs dictate the quantum numbers of formed resonance. The resulting classification of light mesons turns out to be in a better agreement with the experimental observations than the standard quark model classification. It is argued that the total energy of quark components should be proportional to the hadron mass squared rather than the linear mass. As a byproduct a certain relation expressing the constituent quark mass via the gluon and quark condensate is put forward. We show that our approach leads to an effective mass counting scheme for meson spectrum and results in the linear Regge and radial Regge trajectories by construction. An experimental observation of these trajectories might thus serve as an evidence not for string but for multiquark structure of highly excited hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that all light nonstrange mesons above 1 GeV arise from the creation of constituent quark-antiquark pairs inside the pion or rho (omega) mesons, rather than from orbital angular momentum excitations of a single quark-antiquark pair as in the standard quark model. The author argues that the total energy of quark components should be proportional to the hadron mass squared rather than linear mass, and that this approach produces linear Regge trajectories by construction, potentially serving as evidence for multiquark structure rather than string structure of highly excited hadrons.

## Method

The author postulates three basic excitations inside the pion: a "spin flip" converting the spin-singlet quark-antiquark pair (pion) to a spin-triplet pair (rho meson) with effective energy E_rho; formation of a spin-singlet constituent quark-antiquark pair Q_0 with effective mass E_0; and formation of a spin-triplet constituent quark-antiquark pair Q_1 with effective mass E_1. The mass squared of any excited meson is given by m_h^2 = Lambda * (sum of appropriate E_rho, E_0, E_1 contributions) + m_pi^2, where Lambda = 1830 MeV is derived from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. The quantum numbers of each resonance are dictated by the spin-singlet or triplet nature of the constituent pairs, and the scheme produces linear Regge and radial Regge trajectories by construction.

## Key numbers

- Lambda = 1830 MeV, derived from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation using standard values: quark condensate = -(250 MeV)^3, m_u + m_d = 11 MeV, f_pi = 92.4 MeV, yielding m_pi = 140 MeV
- E_rho approximately 310 MeV, fixed from the averaged mass of charged rho meson, m_rho = 766.5 +/- 1.1 MeV
- E_0 approximately 430-510 MeV, estimated from fitting to masses of h_1(1170) and b_1(1230) states
- E_1 approximately 570-580 MeV, estimated from fitting to masses of f_1(1285) and f_2(1270) states
- Constituent quark mass M_c approximately 310 MeV, estimated from the gluon condensate value (alpha_s/pi) * <G^2> = 0.012(3) GeV^4 and quark condensate <q-bar q> = -(0.25 GeV)^3 with n_f = 2
- The relation m_Delta^2 = m_N^2 + Lambda * M_c yields m_Delta approximately 1.2 GeV, in agreement with Delta(1232)
- The averaged slopes of spin and radial trajectories in light nonstrange mesons are reported as a approximately a' approximately 1.14 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the long-standing quark model concept by proposing an alternative to the string picture for explaining linear Regge trajectories, which has been pursued by many authors (e.g., in Refs. [string, string2, string3, string4, string5, string6, string7, string8]) but has not produced a satisfactory quantized hadron string. The approach addresses phenomenological flaws of the standard quark model classification noted in the Particle Data Group reviews, such as the unexplained scalar mesons below 1 GeV (for which a tetraquark structure is often assumed by Pelaez) and the experimental absence of predicted states like J^PC = 2^{--}. The scheme reproduces known relations in certain limits, including the Weinberg relation m_{a1}^2 = 2 m_rho^2 and relations first appearing in variants of the Veneziano amplitude incorporating the Adler self-consistency condition.
