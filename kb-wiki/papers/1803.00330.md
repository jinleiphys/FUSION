# Faddeev approach to the reaction $K^- d \to πΣn$ at $p_{K} = 1$ GeV/c

> The reaction $K^-d \to πΣn$ is studied within a Faddeev-type approach, with emphasis on the specific kinematics of the E31 experiment at J-PARC, i.e. $K^-$ beam momentum of $p_K = 1$ GeV/c and neutron angle of $θ_n=0^\circ$. The employed Faddeev approach requires as main input amplitudes for the two-body subsystems $\bar KN \to \bar KN$ and $\bar KN \to πΣ$. For the latter results from recently published chiral unitary models of the $\bar KN$ interaction are utilized. The $\bar KN \to \bar KN$ amplitude itself, however, is taken from a recent partial-wave analysis. Due to the large incoming momentum of the $K^-$, the $\bar KN$ interaction is probed in a kinematical regime where those chiral potentials are no longer applicable. A comparison of the predicted spectrum for various $πΣ$ charge channels with preliminary data is made and reveals a remarkable agreement as far as the magnitude and the line shape in general is concerned. Noticeable differences observed in the $πΣ$ spectrum around the $\bar KN$ threshold, i.e. in the region of the $Λ$(1405) resonance, indicate a sensitivity to the details of the employed $\bar KN \to πΣ$ amplitudes and suggest that pertinent high-precision data could indeed provide substantial constraints on the structure of the $Λ$(1405).

## Key claim

The Faddeev-type calculation of $K^-d \to \pi\Sigma n$ at $p_K = 1$ GeV/c and $\theta_n=0^\circ$ achieves remarkable agreement with preliminary E31 data in magnitude and line shape, but only when the full partial-wave $\bar KN \to \bar KN$ amplitude from a partial-wave analysis is used for the initial scattering step, rather than the $s$-wave-only chiral potentials that had been used in prior work.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for the coupled $\bar KNN$-$\pi\Sigma N$ system, using the KSU partial-wave analysis for the $\bar KN \to \bar KN$ amplitude (including partial waves up to $j=7/2$) and $s$-wave $\bar KN \to \pi\Sigma$ amplitudes from three chiral unitary models: TW1 by Cieplý and Smejkal (2012), the energy-dependent model of Ohnishi et al. (2016), and the Oset-Ramos-Bennhold (ORB) potential (2002). The boosted two-body $t$-matrices were embedded in a Poincaré-invariant few-body framework, and the deuteron wave function from the Nijmegen Nijm93 potential was used.

## Key numbers

* Beam momentum: $p_K = 1$ GeV/c; neutron angle: $\theta_n = 0^\circ$ (kinematics of J-PARC E31 experiment)
* $\bar KN$ threshold (isospin-averaged masses): 1434.6 MeV
* $\bar KN$ c.m. energy at $p_{K^-}=1$ GeV/c: 1795 MeV
* TW1 scattering lengths: $a_{\bar KN}(I=0) = -1.61 + i\,1.02$ fm, $a_{\bar KN}(I=1) = 0.60 + i\,0.50$ fm; poles at 1433$-$i25 MeV and 1371$-$i54 MeV
* $V^{E-dep.}$ scattering lengths: $a_{\bar KN}(I=0) = -1.89 + i\,1.11$ fm, $a_{\bar KN}(I=1) = 0.45 + i\,0.53$ fm; poles at 1429$-$i15 MeV and 1344$-$i49 MeV
* ORB scattering lengths: $a_{\bar KN}(I=0) = -1.72 + i\,0.89$ fm, $a_{\bar KN}(I=1) = 0.52 + i\,0.64$ fm; poles at 1426$-$i16 MeV and 1390$-$i66 MeV
* Cutoff mass for off-shell form factor: $\Lambda = 800$ MeV (varied by 10% with negligible effect)

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-step calculations of Kamano and Lee (2014) and the authors' own initial Faddeev study (Miyagawa et al., 2015) by treating the three-body dynamics rigorously rather than perturbatively, and by incorporating higher partial waves in the $\bar KN \to \bar KN$ amplitude from the KSU partial-wave analysis. The prior chiral-potential-based calculations (Ohnishi et al., 2016; Miyagawa et al., 2012) had underestimated the cross section by a factor of five because they used only $s$-wave $\bar KN$ amplitudes. The results show that the $\pi\Sigma$ spectrum around the $\bar KN$ threshold is sensitive to the details of the $\bar KN \to \pi\Sigma$ amplitudes from different chiral models, suggesting that high-precision data from E31 can constrain the pole structure of the $\Lambda$(1405).
