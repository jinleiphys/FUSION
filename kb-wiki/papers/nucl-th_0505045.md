# HBT search for new states of matter in A+A collisions

> A method allowing studies of the hadronic matter at the early evolution stage in A+A collisions is developed. It is based on an interferometry analysis of approximately conserved values such as the averaged phase-space density (APSD) and the specific entropy of thermal pions. The plateau found in the APSD behavior vs collision energy at SPS is associated, apparently, with the deconfinement phase transition at low SPS energies; a saturation of this quantity at the RHIC energies indicates the limiting Hagedorn temperature for hadronic matter. It is shown that if the cubic power of effective temperature of pion transverse spectra grows with energy similarly to the rapidity density (that is roughly consistent with experimental data), then the interferometry volume is inverse proportional to the pion APSD that is about a constant because of limiting Hagedorn temperature. This sheds light on the HBT puzzle.

## Key claim
The paper develops a method to infer properties of the early, hot stage of heavy-ion collisions from final-state observables, using approximately conserved quantities (averaged phase-space density and specific entropy of thermal pions). The plateau in the averaged phase-space density versus collision energy at SPS is associated with the deconfinement phase transition, while saturation at RHIC energies indicates the limiting Hagedorn temperature for hadronic matter.

## Method
The authors express the averaged phase-space density (APSD) and specific entropy of thermal pions in terms of measured single-particle spectra and HBT interferometry radii, using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization. They extract the pion chemical potential at thermal freeze-out from the APSD, assuming a uniform freeze-out temperature of 120 MeV, and then compute the specific entropy. The analysis uses experimental data from E895, E802 (AGS), NA49 (SPS), and STAR/PHENIX (RHIC) collaborations for central heavy-ion collisions, with corrections for resonance decay contributions via a factor κ (0.65 for SPS, 0.7 for RHIC).

## Key numbers
- At √s_NN = 12.3 GeV (SPS), the extracted APSD is (2π)³⟨f⟩ = 0.203 ± 0.06, compared to a linear extrapolation from AGS giving 0.477.
- The specific entropy of thermal negative pions is approximately 4 at SPS energies, dropping to 3.79 at the top SPS energy, and 3.82 at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC).
- The ratio of the cube of effective temperatures of negative pions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC) to that at 40 AGeV (SPS) is approximately 2, while the ratio of mid-rapidity densities is approximately 3.
- The interferometry volume at RHIC for 0–5% centrality is obtained by scaling the PHENIX 0–30% centrality data by a factor of 1.215.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Akkelin and Sinyukov (2004) for using averaged phase-space density as a conserved observable in heavy-ion collisions. It addresses the HBT puzzle by showing that the interferometry volume is inversely proportional to the pion APSD times the cube of effective temperature, explaining why the volume grows slowly with energy despite increasing multiplicity. The results suggest that the "extra pion" production at SPS energies may be related to the QCD critical end point, as discussed by Stephanov (2004) and others, and could also be connected to the "horn" puzzle in kaon-to-pion ratios observed by NA49.
