# Diphoton Generalized Distribution Amplitudes

> We calculate the leading order diphoton generalized distribution amplitude by calculating the amplitude of the process gamma* gamma -> gamma gamma in the low energy and high photon virtuality region at the Born order and in the leading logarithmic approximation. As in the case of the anomalous photon structure functions, the gamma gamma generalized distribution amplitude exhibits a characteristic \ln Q^2 behaviour and obeys inhomogeneous evolution equations.

# Diphoton Generalized Distribution Amplitudes

## Key claim

The diphoton generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) can be defined and calculated perturbatively at Born order in the threshold kinematics where the diphoton invariant mass squared W² = 0, and they exhibit a characteristic logarithmic dependence on the photon virtuality Q², obeying inhomogeneous QCD evolution equations analogous to those of anomalous photon structure functions, but only in the leading logarithmic approximation and at zeroth order in the strong coupling constant α_S.

## Method

The authors compute the six Feynman diagrams (with quarks in the loop) for the process γ* γ → γ γ at leading order in the electromagnetic coupling and zeroth order in α_S, restricting to the threshold kinematics W² = 0. They perform the loop integration using Sudakov variables, regulate ultraviolet divergences via dimensional regularization, and separate the amplitude into quark and photon operator contributions through renormalization group mixing. The resulting Born-order GDAs are then evolved using the ERBL evolution equation modified by an inhomogeneous term, diagonalized in the basis of Gegenbauer polynomials C_p^(3/2)(2z-1).

## Key numbers

- The Born-order unpolarized diphoton GDA Φ₁^q(z,ζ,0) is proportional to (N_C e_q²)/(2π²) log(Q²/m²), with explicit z- and ζ-dependent piecewise functions given in Eqs. (H1) and (H3).
- The polarized GDA Φ₃^q(z,ζ,0) has the same prefactor (N_C e_q²)/(2π²) log(Q²/m²) with a different piecewise function.
- The QCD evolution kernel V_NS at leading order is (α_s(Q²)/(2π)) C_F times the standard ERBL kernel, with C_F = (N_C²-1)/(2N_C).
- The anomalous dimensions γ_qq(p) = C_F(1/2 - 1/[(p+1)(p+2)] + 2 Σ_{k=2}^{p+1} 1/k).
- The evolved non-singlet GDA at large Q² is proportional to (e_q² - e_q'²) log(Q²/m²) times a sum over odd p of f'_p(ζ) / (1 + 6 γ_qq(p)/(33-2N_f)) C_p^(3/2)(2z-1).
- The numerical Gegenbauer expansion used 41 terms for regions 0<z<ζ and 1-ζ<z<1, and 201 terms for ζ<z<1-ζ.

## Context

This work extends the concept of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), previously developed for meson pairs by Diehl et al. (1998) and for ρ mesons by Polyakov (1999), to the case of two photons in the final state. It is related by crossing to the photon generalized parton distributions studied by El Beiyad et al. (2008) for deeply virtual Compton scattering on a photon target. The calculation is reminiscent of the perturbative treatment of two-ρ GDAs in terms of single ρ distribution amplitudes. The results enable future studies of exclusive two-photon production at high virtuality, though the authors note that hadronic backgrounds from pseudoscalar meson decays will dominate any phenomenological study.
