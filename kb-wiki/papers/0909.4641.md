# More effective theory of nuclear forces

> I outline why the renormalisation group is needed to analyse the scale dependence and hence determine the power counting for effective theories of strongly interacting systems. I summarise the results of several such analyses for two- and three-body forces in nuclear physics. These show that a number of terms should be significantly promoted relative to naive dimensional analysis.

# More effective theory of nuclear forces

## Key claim
The renormalisation group (RG) must be used to determine the correct power counting for effective field theories of nuclear forces, rather than relying on naive dimensional analysis (Weinberg power counting). Several terms in two- and three-body nuclear potentials are significantly promoted relative to naive expectations, but the exact counting for the three-body contact interaction in the presence of tensor one-pion exchange remains unknown.

## Method
The author applies RG analysis to two-body short-range forces, identifying fixed points of the RG flow and expanding around the nontrivial (unitary) fixed point to derive power counting. For long-range forces, the short-distance behaviour of wave functions is analysed: the central part of one-pion exchange (OPE) gives a 1/r singularity that does not alter power-law wave functions, while the tensor part of OPE gives a 1/r^3 singularity that strongly promotes short-range interactions in spin-triplet waves with L ≤ 2. The analysis is extended to three-body forces by examining wave function behaviour in the hyperradius.

## Key numbers
- The "unnatural" strength of OPE is set by λ_NN = 16π F_π^2 / (g_A^2 M_N) ≃ 290 MeV, which is only ~2m_π
- For S-wave scattering lengths, 1/a ≲ 40 MeV
- For three bosons near the unitary limit, the three-body wave function behaves as ψ(R) ∝ R^{-2 ± i s_0} with s_0 ≃ 1.006, requiring promotion of the leading three-body force by four orders to order Q^{-1}
- For waves with L ≥ 3, the critical momentum to resolve the tensor OPE singularity is p_c ≳ 2 GeV; for waves with L ≤ 2, p_c ≲ 3m_π
- The leading contact interaction in spin-triplet waves with L=1,2 is promoted to order Q^{-1/2}
- The three-body OPE operator coupling ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 NN channels is promoted to order Q^{5/4}

## Context
This work extends the RG-based approach to power counting developed by Weinberg (1979, 1990, 2009) and the KSW counting for systems with large scattering lengths (van Kolck 1998, Kaplan et al. 1998). It builds on analyses of short-range forces by Birse et al. (1998) and the Efimov effect (Efimov 1971). The RG analysis of three-body forces with limit cycles follows Bedaque et al. (1998) and Bedaque & van Kolck (2004). The promotion of contact interactions in triplet waves due to tensor OPE was observed by Nogga et al. (2005) and analysed by Birse (2005, 2007). The results enable a new power counting for nuclear EFTs up to N^2LO, with implications for three-body forces in P-wave channels that may resolve discrepancies in current descriptions (e.g., Machleidt 2009).
