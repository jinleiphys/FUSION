# Emergence of a secondary rainbow and the dynamical polarization potential for 16O on 12C at 330 MeV

> Background: An anomaly in the elastic scattering of O-16 on C-12 around 300 MeV was resolved by including collective excitations of both nuclei, leading to a secondary rainbow. There is little systematic knowledge of the contribution of collective excitations to the interaction between nuclei, particularly in the overlap region of heavy nuclei. Purpose: To study the dynamic polarization potential (DPP) generated by channel coupling that had been validated for a case where scattering is sensitive to the nuclear potential over a wide radial range; to exhibit evidence of the non-locality due to collective coupling; to validate, or otherwise, the representation of DPPs by uniform renormalization of potentials. Methods: S-matrix to potential inversion yields local potentials reproducing the elastic channel S-matrix of coupled channel calculations. Subtracting the elastic channel uncoupled potential yields a local L-independent representation of the DPP. The dependence of the DPP on the nature of the coupled states and other parameters can be studied. Results: Local DPPs were found due to the excitation of C-12 and the combined excitation of O-16 and C-12. The radial forms found were very different from uniform renormalization of the potential. Full coupling led to a 10 percent increase in the volume integral of the real potential. Evidence for the non-locality of the underlying formal DPP and the effect of direct coupling between the collective states is found. Conclusions: The local DPP generating the secondary rainbow is found. DPPs have forms depending on the specific excitations and cannot be represented by a uniform renormalization of the potential. The method is useful for study of the contribution of collective excitations to internuclear potentials, concerning which remarkably little is known in general.

# Emergence of a secondary rainbow and the dynamical polarization potential for 16O on 12C at 330 MeV

## Key claim

The dynamic polarization potential (DPP) generated by collective excitations of both 16O and 12C resolves an anomaly in elastic scattering around 300 MeV and produces a secondary rainbow, but the DPP cannot be represented by a uniform renormalization of the folding model potential; its radial form depends specifically on which nuclear states are excited and on whether direct coupling between those states is included.

## Method

S-matrix to potential inversion (iterative-perturbative IP inversion) was applied to the elastic channel S-matrix from coupled channel calculations that included the 2+ and 3- states of 12C and the 3- and 2+ states of 16O. Subtracting the uncoupled (bare) double folding potential from the inverted local potential yielded a local L-independent representation of the DPP for each set of coupled channels. The DPPs were compared for cases with and without direct coupling between excited states and for excitation of 12C states alone versus full coupling including 16O states.

## Key numbers

- Full coupling led to a 10 percent increase in the volume integral of the real potential (ΔJR = 28.74 MeV fm3 for full coupling, compared to a bare potential volume integral of 278.48 MeV fm3)
- For coupling to the 2+ state of 12C alone: ΔJR = 7.67 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 20.68 MeV fm3, Δ reaction cross section = 11.8 mb, inelastic cross section = 11.4 mb
- For coupling to the 3- state of 12C alone: ΔJR = -0.34 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 2.37 MeV fm3, Δ reaction cross section = 4.3 mb, inelastic cross section = 3.9 mb
- For coupling to both 2+ and 3- states of 12C with direct coupling: ΔJR = 5.85 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 28.56 MeV fm3, Δ reaction cross section = 15.6 mb, inelastic cross section = 14.8 mb
- For full coupling including 16O states: ΔJR = 28.74 MeV fm3, ΔJI = 35.96 MeV fm3, Δ reaction cross section = 70.4 mb, inelastic cross section = 57.4 mb
- Bare reaction cross section without coupling: 1633.6 mb for the potential used with only 12C states, 1411.7 mb for the potential used with full coupling

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Ohkubo, Hirabayashi, and collaborators (2014) that including collective excitations of both 12C and 16O resolves an anomaly in 16O+12C elastic scattering around 300 MeV, where a global model that worked from 62 to 1503 MeV failed at 281 MeV. The method provides a systematic tool to study DPPs for strongly overlapping composite nuclei, where prior work was limited to light projectiles (A ≤ 6) or used the less accurate trivially equivalent local potential (TELP) approach. The results enable future studies of how specific nuclear collectivities modify internuclear interactions beyond what global models or uniform renormalization of folding potentials can capture.
