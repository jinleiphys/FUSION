# Improved description of threshold pion electroproduction in chiral perturbation theory

> We investigate neutral pion electroproduction off protons in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The chiral expansion of the S--wave multipoles $E_{0+}$ and $L_{0+}$ is carried out to three orders. There appear several undetermined low--energy constants. Three are taken from a recent study of the new TAPS threshold $π^0$ photoproduction data, one is fixed from the proton Dirac form factor and the novel S--wave constants appearing at orders $q^4$ and $q^5$ are determined from a best fit (constrained by resonance exchange) to the recent NIKHEF and MAMI data at photon momentum transfer squared $k^2 = -0.1 \, \, {\rm GeV}^2$. The inclusion of a particular set of dimension five operators is forced upon the fit by a soft--pion theorem which severely constrains the momentum dependence of the longitudinal S--wave multipole $L_{0+}$ at order $q^4$. We give predictions for lower photon virtualities for the various multipoles and differential cross sections. Further improvements are briefly touched upon.

## Key claim

The chiral expansion of the S-wave multipoles for neutral pion electroproduction off protons requires the inclusion of a particular dimension-five operator from the order \(q^5\) Lagrangian to satisfy a soft-pion theorem that constrains the momentum dependence of the longitudinal S-wave multipole \(L_{0+}\); without this higher-order term, the fit to existing NIKHEF and MAMI data at \(k^2 = -0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\) fails.

## Method

The authors performed a heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) to three orders (up to \(q^5\)) and the P-wave multipoles to two orders (\(q^3\)), using tree graphs, one-loop diagrams, and counter terms from the effective Lagrangian. Three low-energy constants were taken from a fit to TAPS photoproduction data, one was fixed from the proton Dirac form factor radius, and the novel S-wave constants at orders \(q^4\) and \(q^5\) were determined by a best fit (constrained by resonance exchange) to differential cross section data at \(k^2 = -0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\) from NIKHEF and MAMI.

## Key numbers

- The photon momentum transfer squared for the primary fit is \(k^2 = -0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The neutral pion mass is \(M_\pi = 134.97\ \text{MeV}\) and the proton mass is \(m = 938.27\ \text{MeV}\).
- The charged pion mass used in loops is \(\omega_c = 140.11\ \text{MeV}\).
- The experimental proton Dirac charge radius is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_1^{p,\text{exp}} = 0.774 \pm 0.008\ \text{fm}^2\).
- The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\) and the axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.328\) (from the Goldberger-Treiman relation).
- The strong pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(g_{\pi N} = 13.4\).
- The P-wave low-energy constant \(b_P\) is taken as either \(13.0\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) (from TAPS data) or \(15.8\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\) (from SAL data).
- The sum of S-wave counter terms \(a_1 + a_2\) is \(7.85\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\) (with the approximation used) or \(6.60\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\) (from the full \(\omega\)-dependence).
- The vector meson contribution to \(a_1^V + a_2^V\) is \(2.67\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\) and to \(b_P^V\) is \(3.13\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory study of neutral pion photoproduction (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner, 1995) to the electroproduction case with virtual photons, building on earlier relativistic calculations (Bernard et al., 1992). It enables predictions for lower photon virtualities (\(|k^2|\) down to \(0.04\ \text{GeV}^2\)) and provides a framework for analyzing upcoming data from MAMI at \(k^2 = -0.06\ \text{GeV}^2\), while also highlighting the need for further improvements such as a full two-loop calculation.
