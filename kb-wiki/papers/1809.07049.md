# Dilepton Radiation in Heavy-Ion Collisions at Small Transverse Momentum

> We study the invariant-mass distributions of dileptons produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at very low pair transverse momenta, $P_T\leq 0.15$ GeV. Specifically, we investigate the interplay of thermal radiation with initial photon annihilation processes, $γγ\to l^+ l^-$, triggered by the coherent electromagnetic fields of the incoming nuclei. For the thermal radiation, we employ the emission from the QGP and hadronic phases with in-medium vector spectral functions which describes the inclusive excess radiation observed over a wide range of collision energies. For the coherent photon fusion processes, whose spectrum is much softer than for thermal radiation, we employ initial fluxes from the Fourier transform of charge distributions of the colliding nuclei in the equivalent-photon approximation. We first verify that the combination of photon fusion, thermal radiation and final-state hadron decays gives a fair description of the low-$P_T$ dilepton mass spectra as recently measured by the STAR collaboration in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions for different centrality classes, including experimental acceptance cuts. The coherent contribution dominates in peripheral collisions, while thermal radiation shows a markedly stronger increase with centrality. We perform similar calculations at lower collision energies ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=17.3 GeV) and compare to the acceptance-corrected dimuon excess spectra measured by the NA60 experiment at the CERN SPS; here, the contribution from photoproduction is subleading. We also provide predictions for the ALICE experiment at the LHC; the pertinent excitation function from SPS to LHC energies reveals a nontrivial interplay of photoproduction and thermal radiation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the low-transverse-momentum (P_T ≤ 0.15 GeV) dilepton excess in heavy-ion collisions arises from an interplay between thermal radiation from the hot QCD medium and coherent photon-photon fusion from the electromagnetic fields of the colliding nuclei, with the relative dominance depending strongly on collision centrality and energy; the coherent contribution dominates in peripheral collisions at RHIC energies but is subleading at SPS energies, while thermal radiation increases more rapidly with centrality.

## Method

The authors combine two dilepton production mechanisms: (1) coherent γγ → l⁺l⁻ fusion calculated using the equivalent-photon approximation with realistic nuclear charge form factors from Fourier transforms of charge densities, integrated over impact parameter with the optical Glauber model for centrality selection; and (2) thermal radiation from quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phases using in-medium vector spectral functions with a fireball expansion parameterized by hydrodynamics-guided volume evolution. They compare the sum of these sources, plus a hadronic decay cocktail, to STAR Au+Au data at √s_NN = 200 GeV and NA60 In+In data at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV, then extrapolate to LHC Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Pair transverse momentum cut: P_T ≤ 0.15 GeV for RHIC and LHC comparisons; P_T < 0.2 GeV for SPS comparisons
- At RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au): the γγ contribution dominates in peripheral (60-80%) collisions; in semi-central (10-40%) collisions all three contributions (γγ, thermal, cocktail) are of similar magnitude
- At SPS (√s_NN = 17.3 GeV In+In): the γγ contribution reaches up to ten percent of thermal radiation near the dimuon threshold, then rapidly falls off with increasing mass
- The excitation function from √s_NN ≈ 20 GeV to 5 TeV shows γγ fusion saturates above √s_NN ≈ 100 GeV, while thermal radiation increases more gradually with s_NN

## Context

This work extends the analysis of low-P_T dilepton production beyond ultraperipheral collisions (where γγ fusion dominates, as studied by PHENIX, ALICE, and ATLAS) into peripheral and semi-central collisions where thermal radiation from the fireball also contributes. It follows up on the STAR collaboration's observation (Adam 2018) of a low-P_T excess in peripheral Au+Au collisions that could not be explained by thermal radiation plus hadronic cocktail alone, and on earlier calculations of J/ψ photoproduction in semi-central collisions by Klusek-Gawenda (2015). The results enable systematic centrality and energy-dependent tests of both thermal emission models and coherent photon fusion calculations, particularly relevant for ALICE plans to lower single-electron p_T cuts and measure very-low-mass spectra.
