# Lattice QCD at nonzero temperature and density

> In this talk I review the current status of lattice QCD calculations at nonzero temperature and density. I focus on the QCD phase structure and bulk QCD thermodynamics at zero and nonzero chemical potentials.

## Key claim

This review of lattice QCD at nonzero temperature and density reports that the continuum-extrapolated equation of state for $N_f=2+1$ QCD from two independent collaborations (using HISQ and stout fermions) now agree, but the paper's abstract omits that the chiral first-order phase transition region in 3-flavor QCD may be very small (critical pion mass $m_\pi^c \lesssim 50$ MeV from HISQ or around 100 MeV in the continuum limit from Wilson fermions), meaning this region may have only mild influence on real-world thermodynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations using multiple fermion discretizations: Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action, stout-smeared staggered fermions, clover-improved Wilson fermions, and Domain Wall fermions, on lattices with temporal extents $N_\tau$ ranging from 4 to 12. Calculations employ the Taylor expansion method and imaginary chemical potential approach to circumvent the sign problem at nonzero baryon density, and use the T-integration method, gradient flow, and fixed-topology-sector techniques for equation of state and topological susceptibility computations.

## Key numbers

- Critical pion mass upper bound for the first-order chiral phase transition in 3-flavor QCD: $m_\pi^c \lesssim 50$ MeV from HISQ fermions on $N_\tau=6$ lattices (Bazavov et al., 2017)
- Critical pion mass in the continuum limit from clover-improved Wilson fermions: approximately 100 MeV (Takeda et al., 2016)
- Critical energy density for the chiral crossover in $N_f=2+1$ QCD: $0.18 < \epsilon_c < 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Charm quark contribution to trace anomaly becomes non-negligible starting at $T \approx 300$ MeV and reaches maximum at $T \approx 500$ MeV
- $U(1)_A$ symmetry remains broken at physical pion masses and only starts to become restored at $T \gtrsim 200$ MeV
- Freeze-out baryon chemical potential range at RHIC beam energy scan: $\mu_B = 20$ to 400 MeV with freeze-out temperature $140 \lesssim T \lesssim 170$ MeV

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD program summarized in earlier reviews by Ding (2015), Meyer (2011), and Schmidt (2017), and updates plenary talks by Sexty (2014), Meyer (2015), Borsanyi (2015), Bazavov (2015), D'Elia (2015), and Scorzato (2015). The continuum-extrapolated equation of state results from the HISQ collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2014) and the stout fermion collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2013) now agree, enabling reliable input for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions. The topological susceptibility results from multiple groups (Borsanyi et al., 2015; Bonati et al., 2015; Petreczky et al., 2016) show discrepancies that require further study, and the equation of state including charm quarks (Borsanyi et al., 2016) can now be extended to include bottom quarks for cosmology applications.
