# Shedding light on the pion production in heavy-ion collisions and application into the neutron star matter properties

> Within the framework of the quantum molecular dynamics transport model, the pion production and constraint of the high-density symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energy have been thoroughly investigated. The energy conservation in the decay of resonances and reabsorption of pions as well as in the inelastic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-resonance collisions are taken into account. The isospin diffusion in the low-density region (0.2$ρ_{0}$ - 0.8$ρ_{0}$) and high-density region (1.2$ρ_{0}$ - 1.8$ρ_{0}$) is investigated by analyzing the spectra of neutron/proton and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios in the isotopic reactions of $^{132}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn and $^{108}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn at the incident energy of 270 MeV/nucleon, in which the symmetry energy manifests the opposite effect in the different density domain. The controversial conclusion of the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio for constraining the high-density symmetry energy by different transport models with the FOPI data has been clarified. A soft symmetry energy with the slope parameter of $L(ρ_{0}) = 42\pm 25$ MeV by using the standard error analysis within the range of $1σ$ is obtained by analyzing the experimental data from the S$π$RIT collaboration. The neutron stars with the maximal mass of 2 $M_{\odot}$ and radius of 11-13 km are obtained with the constrained symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the controversial "pion puzzle" regarding the use of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio to constrain the high-density symmetry energy can be resolved by properly accounting for energy conservation in resonance decays and reabsorption, and by recognizing that the symmetry energy has opposite effects on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in low-density (0.2ρ₀–0.8ρ₀) versus high-density (1.2ρ₀–1.8ρ₀) regions.

## Method

The authors used the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model to simulate the isotopic reactions ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn and ¹⁰⁸Sn + ¹¹²Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon. They implemented energy conservation in resonance decay (Δ ↔ Nπ) and reabsorption (πN ↔ Δ), as well as in inelastic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-resonance collisions, and included a momentum-dependent pion-nucleon potential. The neutron/proton and π⁻/π⁺ ratios were analyzed as functions of kinetic energy and transverse momentum, and double ratios between the two isotopic systems were used to reduce Coulomb and other uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to L(ρ₀) = 42 ± 25 MeV from a standard error analysis within 1σ using SπRIT collaboration data.
- The soft, linear, and hard symmetry energy parameterizations correspond to slope parameters L(ρ₀) of 42, 82, and 139 MeV, respectively.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) has a value of 31.5 MeV.
- Neutron stars with the constrained symmetry energy have a maximal mass of 2 M⊙ and radius of 11–13 km.
- At high transverse momentum (pT ≥ 150 MeV/c), the double ratios of π⁻/π⁺ exhibit about 20% sensitivity to the high-density symmetry energy.
- The incompressibility modulus for isospin symmetric nuclear matter is K = 230 MeV at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of the "pion puzzle" by Feng et al. (2010) and Xiao et al. (2009), which reached opposite conclusions about the high-density symmetry energy from FOPI data. The authors clarify that the discrepancy arises from different treatments of energy conservation and pion potentials. The results are consistent with the SπRIT collaboration measurements (Estee et al., 2021) and with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and J0740+6620. The constrained symmetry energy enables predictions for neutron star mass-radius relations and can be further tested by future experiments on collective flows and differential flows of pions.
