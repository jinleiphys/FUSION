# Light Front Field Theory Calculation of Deuteron Properties

> Light front dynamics is a promising approach for calculating the deuteron wave function and form factors at high momentum transfers. However, in light-front dynamics rotational invariance is not manifest, which results in a splitting in the binding energies of states with different magnetic quantum numbers and a breaking of the angular condition for the matrix elements of the deuteron current operator. The objective of this work is to investigate the symptoms of broken rotational invariance. We first consider the Wick-Cutkosky model. The binding energies of states with different m values are split when calculated with the OME potential, but this splitting is reduced for all states when the TME potential is included. Next, we derive light-front nucleon-nucleon OME and TME potentials from an effective nuclear Lagrangian. We consider first the potentials generated by the exchange of pions only, then the potentials generated by all the mesons. For both models, there is a splitting in the binding energies between the m=0 and m=1 deuteron states when the OME potential is used, which is reduced when we include the TME potential. The deuteron wave functions are used to calculate the electromagnetic and axial current matrix elements and form factors. The matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator have better transformation properties under rotations when we use the OME potential instead of the OME+TME potential to calculate the wave functions. At momentum transfers greater than about 2 GeV^2, the breaking of rotational invariance causes less uncertainty in the A and B form factors than do the uncertainties in the nucleon form factors.

## Key claim

Light front dynamics offers a promising approach for calculating deuteron properties at high momentum transfers, but rotational invariance is not manifest in this framework, leading to a splitting in binding energies of states with different magnetic quantum numbers and a breaking of the angular condition for current matrix elements; the work investigates these symptoms of broken rotational invariance and finds that including two-meson-exchange (TME) potentials reduces the splitting but that using only one-meson-exchange (OME) potentials gives better rotational transformation properties for the electromagnetic current matrix elements.

## Method

The author first studied the Wick-Cutkosky model, calculating binding energies for states with different magnetic quantum numbers using OME and TME potentials. Light-front nucleon-nucleon OME and TME potentials were then derived from an effective nuclear Lagrangian, first with only pion exchange and then with all mesons. The deuteron wave functions obtained from these potentials were used to calculate electromagnetic and axial current matrix elements and form factors.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy splitting between m=0 and m=1 deuteron states is present when using the OME potential and is reduced when the TME potential is included, for both the pion-only and full-meson models.
- At momentum transfers greater than about 2 GeV^2, the breaking of rotational invariance causes less uncertainty in the A and B form factors than do the uncertainties in the nucleon form factors.
- For the Wick-Cutkosky model, the spectra for the TBE:UX, TBE:X, and Bethe-Salpeter equation (ladder plus crossed) all lie close to each other, while the modified-Green's-function (MGF) spectrum deviates.

## Context

This work extends the light front dynamics approach to nuclear physics, specifically addressing the rotational invariance problem that has limited its application to deuteron structure calculations. The investigation of OME and TME potentials builds on earlier work by Cooke (2000) on light-front techniques and by Machleidt (1987, 1989) on nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results enable future calculations of deuteron form factors at high momentum transfers where the rotational invariance breaking is less significant than uncertainties in nucleon form factors.
