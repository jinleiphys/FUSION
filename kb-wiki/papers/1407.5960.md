# Dispersive evaluation of the D-term form factor in deeply virtual Compton scattering

> We present a dispersive representation of the D-term form factor for hard exclusive reactions, using unsubtracted $t$-channel dispersion relations. The $t$-channel unitarity relation is saturated with the contribution of two-pion intermediate states, using the two-pion distributions amplitude for the $γ^*γ\rightarrow ππ$ subprocess and reconstructing the $ππ\rightarrow N\bar N$ subprocess from available information on pion-nucleon partial-wave helicity amplitudes. Results for the D-term form factor as function of $t$ as well as at $t=0$ are discussed in comparison with available model predictions and phenomenological parametrizations.

# Dispersive evaluation of the D-term form factor in deeply virtual Compton scattering

## Key claim
The D-term form factor for deeply virtual Compton scattering can be evaluated using unsubtracted t-channel dispersion relations saturated by two-pion intermediate states, but the unsubtracted dispersion integral converges slowly; a subtracted dispersion relation improves convergence at the cost of introducing a subtraction constant at t=0 that must be fitted to experimental data.

## Method
The authors set up unsubtracted t-channel dispersion relations for the D-term form factor, saturating the t-channel unitarity relation with two-pion intermediate states. The gamma*gamma -> pi pi subprocess is described using two-pion generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) at leading twist, while the pi pi -> N anti-N subprocess is reconstructed from pion-nucleon partial-wave helicity amplitudes from Hohler et al. (1983). The numerical evaluation includes S-wave and D-wave contributions, restricting to the n=1 term in the Gegenbauer expansion of the D-term, and uses Omnes representation for the two-pion GDAs with pi pi phase shifts up to the inelastic threshold at t approximately 0.78 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- At t=0, the quark D-term form factor d1^Q(0) = -1.59 using pion PDFs from Owens (1984) and d1^Q(0) = -1.92 using pion PDFs from Gluck et al. (1991), both at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2.
- The total scale-independent D-term at t=0 is d(0) = -3.32 in the asymptotic limit.
- The subtracted dispersion integral converges by t approximately 0.6 GeV^2, while the unsubtracted integral shows only slow convergence up to the integration limit of 0.78 GeV^2.
- Extending the integration to 1.78 GeV^2 changes the unsubtracted result by approximately 10% and the subtracted result by only a few percent.
- The t-dependence is parametrized by a function F_D = d1^Q(0)/[1 - t/(alpha M_D^2)]^alpha with M_D = 0.487 GeV and alpha = 0.841.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach to DVCS amplitudes (Anikin et al., 2007; Diehl et al., 2007) by providing a microscopic interpretation of the D-term form factor in terms of t-channel exchanges. The results are compared with the chiral quark soliton model (Goeke et al., 2007) giving d1^Q(0) = -2.35, the Skyrme model (Cebulla et al., 2007) giving d1^Q(0) = -4.48, and a Regge-improved diquark model (Mueller et al., 2011) giving d1^q(0) = -2.01. The work enables improved phenomenological parametrizations of DVCS observables, as the dispersive results favor a non-dipole t-dependence contrary to the dipole form commonly assumed in phenomenological studies (Guidal et al., 2013).
