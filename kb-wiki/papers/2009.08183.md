# Two-photon exchange effects in $e^+ e^-\rightarrowŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ at small $ \sqrt{s}$

> In this work, the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effects in $e^+e^- \rightarrow ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$ at small $\sqrt{s}$ are discussed within a hadronic model. In the limit $m_e\rightarrow 0$, the TPE contribution to the amplitude can be described by one scalar function $\overline{c}_{1}^{(2╬│)}$. The ratio between this function and the corresponding contribution in one-photon exchange $c_{1}^{(1╬│)}$ reflects all the information of the TPE corrections. The numerical results on this ratio are presented and an artificial function is used to fit the numerical results. The latter can be used conveniently in the further experimental data analysis. The numerical results show the asymmetry of the differential cross sections in $e^+e^- \rightarrow ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$ is about $-4\%$ at $\sqrt{s}\sim 0.7$ GeV.

## Key claim

The two-photon-exchange (TPE) correction to the unpolarized differential cross section in $e^+e^- \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ is about $-4\%$ at $\sqrt{s}\sim 0.7$ GeV, but this result is obtained within a hadronic model that neglects the phase of the pion electromagnetic form factor and is limited to $\sqrt{s} < 0.7$ GeV where resonance and re-scattering effects are expected to be small.

## Method

The authors use a hadronic model with a Lagrangian that introduces a form factor $f(q^2)$ related to the pion electromagnetic form factor $F_\pi(q^2)=1+q^2 f(q^2)$. They compute the one-photon-exchange (OPE) and TPE amplitudes for $e^+e^- \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ in the limit $m_e \rightarrow 0$, where the TPE contribution reduces to a single scalar function $\overline{c}_{1}^{(2\gamma)}$. The infrared-divergent part is subtracted using the Tsai-Mao soft-photon approximation, and the remaining finite TPE correction is evaluated numerically using FeynCalc and LoopTools, with the pion form factor approximated by a simple monopole form $F_\pi(q^2)=-\Lambda^2/(q^2-\Lambda^2+i\epsilon)$ with $\Lambda=m_\rho\approx 0.77$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The TPE correction to the unpolarized differential cross section is about $-4\%$ at $\sqrt{s}\sim 0.7$ GeV at $\theta=0$.
- The real part of the TPE correction to the amplitude ratio $\textrm{Re}[\delta_{c_1}^{\pi}] \equiv \textrm{Re}[\overline{c}_{1,\pi}^{(2\gamma)}/c_{1,\pi}^{(1\gamma)}]$ reaches about $2\%$ when $\sqrt{s}=0.7$ GeV at $\theta=0$.
- The TPE corrections are studied in the energy range $\sqrt{s}\in[0.3,0.7]$ GeV.
- The fitted parameters for $\textrm{Re}[\delta_{c_1}^{\pi}] = (c_{11}^{\pi}+c_{12}^{\pi}s^2) \cos\theta + (c_{21}^{\pi}+c_{22}^{\pi}s^2)s\cos^3\theta$ are: $c_{11}^{\pi}=0.00064324$, $c_{12}^{\pi}=0.0106567$, $c_{21}^{\pi}=-0.0556441$, $c_{22}^{\pi}=-0.122082$.
- The imaginary part $\textrm{Im}[\delta_{c_1}^{\pi}]$ is much smaller than the real part.
- The coefficient $\delta_{c_2}^{\pi}$ (proportional to $m_e$) is very small when using the physical electron mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of TPE effects in $e^+e^- \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-$ from the high-energy region (previously treated with pQCD factorization by the same group in 2018) down to small $\sqrt{s}$ using a hadronic model. The approach uses a similar interaction Lagrangian as Zhou (2011) for pion-photon interactions and a monopole form factor similar to that used by Blunden (2010) for the pion. The results provide a fitted formula for the TPE correction that can be used in future experimental data analysis of pion electromagnetic form factor measurements in the timelike region, where TPE effects are directly measurable unlike in the spacelike region.
