# Regarding confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QED3

> We establish that QED3 can possess a critical number of flavours, N_f^c, associated with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking if, and only if, the fermion wave function renormalisation and photon vacuum polarisation are homogeneous functions at infrared momenta when the fermion mass function vanishes. The Ward identity entails that the fermion-photon vertex possesses the same property and ensures a simple relationship between the homogeneity degrees of each of these functions. Simple models for the photon vacuum polarisation and fermion-photon vertex are used to illustrate these observations. The existence and value of N_f^c are contingent upon the precise form of the vertex but any discussion of gauge dependence is moot. We introduce an order parameter for confinement. Chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement are coincident owing to an abrupt change in the analytic properties of the fermion propagator when a nonzero scalar self-energy becomes insupportable.

# Regarding confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QED3

## Key claim
QED3 can possess a critical number of flavours, N_f^c, above which dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is impossible if, and only if, the fermion wave function renormalisation and photon vacuum polarisation are homogeneous functions at infrared momenta when the fermion mass function vanishes. The existence and value of N_f^c are contingent upon the precise form of the fermion-photon vertex, but any discussion of gauge dependence is moot because Landau gauge results define the model entirely.

## Method
The authors analyse the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the fermion and photon propagators in QED3, studying the conditions under which a nonzero scalar self-energy solution can exist in the chiral limit. They derive necessary and sufficient conditions for a critical number of flavours by examining the infrared scaling behaviour of the fermion wave function renormalisation A(p), the photon vacuum polarisation Pi(k), and the fermion-photon vertex Gamma_mu, using the Ward-Takahashi identity to relate their homogeneity degrees. Simple model Ansatze for the vacuum polarisation and vertex are employed to illustrate the theoretical results numerically, and a confinement order parameter based on the analytic structure of the fermion propagator is introduced.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order 1/N_f expansion yields N_f^c = 32/pi^2 = 3.24, with infrared anomalous dimension delta(N_f^c) = 1/12 = 0.083.
- The illustrative model with internally consistent feedback (ell_P=1, ell_V=1) exhibits a critical number of flavours at N_f^c = 3.24.
- The model parameters for the vacuum polarisation Ansatz are a = 0.20, b = 0.088, and c = 7.8, calculated for N_f = 1.
- The confinement order parameter kappa(N_f) = x_c(N_f)/x_c(N_f=1) shows simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement at N_f^c.
- Extant studies using reasonable vertex Ansatze yield N_f^c approximately 3.5.
- Lattice-regularised QED3 can currently state only that N_f^c > 1, if it exists at all.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing debate on whether QED3 possesses a critical number of flavours for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, initiated by Appelquist (1988) who found N_f^c = 3.24 using a truncated DSE system, versus the alternative proposal by Pennington (1990) that the mass function is exponentially suppressed rather than vanishing. The analysis crystallises the conditions for a critical number of flavours, introducing the concept of infrared collusion between Schwinger functions, and demonstrates that all studies reporting exponential suppression have suppressed this mechanism. The results enable future work on the phase diagram of QED3 at nonzero temperature and chemical potential, extending the study by He (2007), and are relevant to QCD phase diagram studies such as those by Gies (2005) and Kurachi (2006). The confinement order parameter based on the fermion propagator's analytic structure provides a model-independent tool applicable to other gauge theories.
