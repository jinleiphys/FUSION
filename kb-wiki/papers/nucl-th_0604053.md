# Universality in a 2-component Fermi System at Finite Temperature

> Thermodynamic properties of a fermionic system close to the unitarity limit, where the 2-body scattering length |a| approaches infinity is studied at high temperature Boltzmann regime. For dilute systems the virial expansion coefficients in the Boltzmann regime are expected, from general arguments, to be universal. A model independent finite temperature T calculation of the third virial coefficient b3(T) is presented. At the unitarity limit, b3(T) = -0.48 is an universal number. The energy density up to the third virial expansion is derived. These results are relevant for dilute neutron matter and could be tested in current atomic experiments on dilute fermionic gases near the Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The third virial coefficient \(b_3(T)\) for a two-component Fermi system at the unitarity limit (where the two-body scattering length \(|a|\) approaches infinity) is shown to be a universal number, approximately \(-0.48\), in the high-temperature Boltzmann regime where the virial expansion is valid for \(n\lambda^3 \ll 1\).

## Method

The author uses a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) with a four-fermion interaction tuned to reproduce the large scattering length, and organizes the thermodynamic pressure as a virial expansion in the fugacity \(z = e^{\mu/T}\). The pressure and number density are computed diagrammatically up to third order in \(z\), summing infinite series of Feynman diagrams for the dimer and trimer propagators to handle the large scattering length. The third virial coefficient is extracted from the three-body contribution to the number density via an integral equation for the trimer propagator projected onto the \(S\)-wave.

## Key numbers

* The third virial coefficient at the unitarity limit is \(b_3^\infty \approx -0.48\) (the text also gives \(\bar b_3^\infty \approx -0.54\) for the interacting part \(\bar b_3 = b_3 - b_3^{(1)}\)).
* The second virial coefficient at unitarity is \(b_2^{(2)} = 1/\sqrt{2}\).
* The virial expansion converges for \(T_F/T \lesssim 0.49\), corresponding to temperatures \(T \gtrsim 2 T_F\).
* The energy density up to third order is given by \(\epsilon^\infty = \frac{3}{2}T n \left[1 + \frac{1}{2^{5/2}}\frac{n\lambda^3}{2} + \frac{n^2\lambda^6}{4}\left(\frac{1}{8} - \frac{2}{9\sqrt{3}}\right)\right] + \epsilon^\infty_\mathrm{int}\), with the interaction part \(\epsilon^\infty_\mathrm{int}\) expressed in terms of the virial coefficients.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to virial coefficients previously developed by Bedaque and Rupak (2005) for bosons, where the third coefficient is not universal due to three-body bound states. For fermions, the absence of a leading-order three-body operator makes the third virial coefficient universal at unitarity. The results are relevant for dilute neutron matter at temperatures around \(T \sim 4\) MeV and densities \(n \sim 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) as encountered in supernova neutrinospheres, and can be tested in atomic experiments on \(^6\)Li and \(^{40}\)K gases near Feshbach resonances. The calculation enables model-independent equation-of-state predictions for systems where microscopic physics is poorly known, and can serve as a check for lattice calculations at intermediate temperatures.
