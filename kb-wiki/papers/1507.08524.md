# Dynamics of quasifission

> Quasifission is the primary reaction mechanism that limits the formation of superheavy nuclei and consequently an important ingredient for choosing the best target-projectile combinations for the heavy element searches. Quasifission is characterized by nuclear contact-times that are much longer than those found in deep-inelastic reactions, resulting in a substantial mass and charge transfer. In this manuscript we employ the fully microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory to study quasifission. New results are presented for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system.

## Key claim

Quasifission in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system is limited to a small range of center-of-mass energies (209-211 MeV) for central collisions, with fusion occurring above 211 MeV and quasielastic collisions below 204 MeV; the authors define fusion arbitrarily as reactions with contact times exceeding 35 zeptoseconds.

## Method

The authors performed fully microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with no symmetry restrictions, using the Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional including all relevant time-odd terms. Static Hartree-Fock wave functions for the two nuclei were generated on the grid with an initial separation of 30 fm, then boosted and time-propagated using a Taylor series expansion (orders 10-12) with a time step of 0.4 fm/c. Density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) was used to calculate nucleus-nucleus potentials and fragment excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions of $^{48}$Ca with the side of $^{249}$Bk: quasielastic collisions occur below $E_{c.m.}=204$ MeV; quasifission is limited to $E_{c.m.}=209-211$ MeV; fusion occurs above 211 MeV
- Contact times for quasifission are 8-30 zeptoseconds (zs) at $E_{c.m.}=218$ MeV, falling sharply for deep-inelastic collisions at larger impact parameters
- At $E_{c.m.}=218$ MeV, the heavy fragment contains approximately 60 MeV of excitation energy and the light fragment approximately 40 MeV for impact parameters corresponding to quasifission
- Quasifission at $E_{c.m.}=218$ MeV produces light fragments with charge doubling from 20 to 40 and mass from 48 to approximately 100
- At impact parameter $b=2$ fm, the light fragment is a neutron-rich Zr isotope with $A\approx 102-106$

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of quasifission in heavy systems, particularly the $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{238}$U system studied by Oberacker (2014), where the formation of strongly bound deformed Zr isotopes was also observed. The results demonstrate that TDHF can provide microscopic insight into quasifission dynamics relevant for superheavy element searches, enabling future calculations of mass-angle distributions and fragment total kinetic energies (TKEs) for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system.
