# Non-extensive statistics effects in quark-gluon plasma and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The presence of memory effects and color long-range forces among the many-parton system in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions can affect the particle statistical behavior at the freeze-out temperature. In this context, we calculate, in the framework of the equilibrium generalized non-extensive thermostatistics, the shape of pion transverse mass spectrum and the value of the transverse momentum correlation function of the pions emitted during the central Pb+Pb collisions and we show that the experimental results is well reproduced assuming very small deviations from the standard statistics.

# Non-extensive statistics effects in quark-gluon plasma and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The experimental transverse momentum spectrum and transverse momentum correlation function of pions emitted in central Pb+Pb collisions can be reproduced by assuming very small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, specifically with a Tsallis parameter q = 1.038, provided that the quark-gluon plasma around the critical temperature is not a weakly interacting plasma but exhibits memory effects and long-range color forces.

## Method

The authors apply the equilibrium generalized non-extensive thermostatistics (Tsallis statistics) to calculate the pion transverse mass spectrum and the transverse momentum fluctuation measure Φ_{p⊥} for pions produced in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. They derive analytic expressions for the transverse mass spectrum to first order in (q-1) and for the mean occupation number and particle fluctuations in the non-extensive framework, then compare these calculations to experimental data from the NA35 and NA49 Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The plasma parameter Γ ≈ 2/3 at T = T_c ≈ 200 MeV, with α_s ≈ 0.5 and <r> ≈ 1 fm, placing the system in an intermediate region between ideal and strongly coupled plasma.
- The experimental transverse momentum distribution from NA35 (S+S collisions) is fitted with q = 1.038 in the Tsallis framework.
- Standard statistical calculations (q=1) give Φ^{st}_{p⊥} = 24.7 MeV at T = 170 MeV, μ = 60 MeV.
- Non-extensive statistics with q = 1.038 yields Φ^{st}_{p⊥} = 5 MeV at T = 170 MeV, μ = 60 MeV, matching the NA49 experimental statistical contribution of Φ^{st}_{p⊥} = (5 ± 1.5) MeV.
- The total experimental correlation measure from NA49 is Φ^{exp}_{p⊥} = (0.6 ± 1) MeV, composed of Φ^{st}_{p⊥} = (5 ± 1.5) MeV and an anti-correlation from two-track resolution of Φ^{tt}_{p⊥} = (-4 ± 0.5) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis non-extensive statistics, previously proposed by Tsallis (1988) and applied to heavy-ion collisions by Wilk and collaborators, to the specific observables of transverse momentum spectra and fluctuation correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies. The authors argue that around the critical temperature, where lattice QCD shows strong non-perturbative effects (as discussed by Schafer and Bannur), the plasma parameter lies in an intermediate regime where standard perturbative QCD and Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics are inadequate. The results enable using the sign and magnitude of Φ_{p⊥} as a diagnostic tool for detecting non-extensive statistical behavior in future heavy-ion experiments at higher energies.
