# CURRENTS AND THEIR COUPLINGS IN THE WEAK SECTOR OF THE STANDARD MODEL

> Beta-decay and muon-capture experiments in nuclei are reviewed. The conserved vector current hypothesis is confirmed through the observed constancy of the vector coupling constant determined from the superallowed Fermi transitions and from the measurement of the weak-magnetism term in mirror Gamow-Teller transitions. The axial-vector and pseudoscalar coupling constants in the nucleon are determined from neutron decay and muon capture on the proton respectively. In finite nuclei, evidence for these coupling constants being reduced relative to their free-nucleon values is discussed. Meson-exchange currents are shown to be an important correction to the time-like part of the axial current as evident in first-forbidden beta decays. Tests of the Standard Model are discussed, as well as extensions beyond it involving right-hand currents and scalar interactions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental evidence confirming the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis through the observed constancy of the vector coupling constant extracted from superallowed Fermi transitions, but notes that the extracted coupling constant depends on theoretical corrections (radiative and charge-dependent) that introduce nuclear-structure-dependent uncertainties at the level of about 0.01.

## Method

The authors survey world data on nine superallowed \(0^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) beta transitions, compiling measured decay energies (\(Q_{EC}\)), half-lives, and branching ratios to compute experimental \(ft\)-values. They then apply calculated radiative corrections (\(\delta_R\), \(\Delta_R^V\)) and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections (\(\delta_C\)) to extract corrected \(\mathcal{F}t\)-values, from which the vector coupling constant \(G_V = G_F V_{ud}\) is determined. The weak-magnetism form factor is tested by comparing beta-decay measurements of the lepton energy spectrum or angular correlation with the analogous M1 gamma-transition rate.

## Key numbers

- Corrected \(\mathcal{F}t\)-value averaged over nine superallowed emitters: \(3073.1 \pm 1.1\) s (with \(\chi^2/(N-1) = 1.34\))
- Individual \(\mathcal{F}t\)-values range from \(3069.2 \pm 2.8\) s (\({}^{14}\)O) to \(3078.7 \pm 2.4\) s (\({}^{42}\)Sc)
- CKM matrix element \(|V_{ud}| = 0.9741 \pm 0.0017\) from nuclear beta decay relative to muon decay
- Weak-magnetism form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(g_M(0) = F_2^V(0) = 3.70\) (from nucleon anomalous magnetic moments: \(\mu_p - 1 = 1.79\), \(\mu_n = -1.91\))
- Axial-vector coupling constant from neutron decay: \(g_A(0) \approx -1.26\) (experimental value, compared to naive quark model prediction of \(-5/3\))

## Context

This review extends the program of testing CVC initiated by Feynman and Gell-Mann (1958) and updates the survey of superallowed transitions by Hardy and Towner (1990). The results enable a precision test of CKM matrix unitarity, which probes extensions to the Standard Model such as right-handed currents or extra \(U(1)\) gauge groups. The discussion of meson-exchange current corrections to the axial current in first-forbidden beta decays connects to ongoing work on nuclear-structure-dependent renormalization of coupling constants in finite nuclei.
