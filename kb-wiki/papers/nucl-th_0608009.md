# Relativistic Hartree-Fock theory. Part I: density-dependent effective Lagrangians

> Effective Lagrangians suitable for a relativistic Hartree-Fock description of nuclear systems are presented. They include the 4 effective mesons $σ, ω, ρ$ and $π$ with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The criteria for determining the model parameters are the reproduction of the binding energies in a number of selected nuclei, and the bulk properties of nuclear matter (saturation point, compression modulus, symmetry energy). An excellent description of nuclear binding energies and radii is achieved for a range of nuclei encompassing light and heavy systems. The predictions of the present approach compare favorably with those of existing relativistic mean field models, with the advantage of incorporating the effects of pion-nucleon coupling.

## Key claim

The paper presents a density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory that, for the first time, systematically applies the RHF approach to effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, achieving a good quantitative description of nuclear systems without dropping the Fock terms. The authors hedge that while the approach compares favorably with existing relativistic mean field models, the pion-nucleon coupling can now be incorporated naturally, which was previously impossible in the RMF framework due to parity conservation.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians including four mesons (sigma, omega, rho, and pion) with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, then derive the Hamiltonian in nucleon space using the no-sea approximation and neglecting retardation effects. The Hartree-Fock equations are solved self-consistently for spherical nuclei by transforming the integro-differential system into an equivalent system of coupled differential equations, with pairing correlations treated in the BCS approximation using a zero-range density-dependent interaction. The model parameters are determined by fitting binding energies of 12 selected nuclei and bulk properties of symmetric nuclear matter using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, yielding three parameter sets (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) with 8 free parameters each.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.1520 fm^{-3} (PKO1), 0.1510 fm^{-3} (PKO2), 0.1530 fm^{-3} (PKO3)
- Compression modulus K = 250.24 MeV (PKO1), 249.60 MeV (PKO2), 262.47 MeV (PKO3)
- Symmetry energy J = 34.371 MeV (PKO1), 32.492 MeV (PKO2), 32.987 MeV (PKO3)
- Binding energy per particle E/A = -15.996 MeV (PKO1), -16.027 MeV (PKO2), -16.041 MeV (PKO3)
- Dirac effective mass M_S^*(p_f)/M = 0.5900 (PKO1), 0.6025 (PKO2), 0.5862 (PKO3)
- Non-relativistic effective mass M_nr^*(p_f)/M = 0.7459 (PKO1), 0.7636 (PKO2), 0.7416 (PKO3)
- Sigma meson mass m_sigma = 525.769 MeV (PKO1), 534.462 MeV (PKO2), 525.668 MeV (PKO3)
- Coupling constant g_rho(0) = 2.629 (PKO1, fixed to experimental value), 4.068 (PKO2), 3.832 (PKO3)
- Pion coupling f_pi(0) = 1.000 (PKO1 and PKO3, fixed to experimental value)

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic Hartree-Fock approaches by Bouyssy (1987) and Bernardos (1993), which lacked density dependence or gave unsatisfactory descriptions of nuclear incompressibility and binding energies, particularly for light nuclei. The DDRHF approach improves upon the relativistic mean field (RMF) models such as PK1, NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), and DD-ME1 (Niksic 2002) by incorporating Fock exchange terms and pion-nucleon coupling, which are absent in RMF due to parity conservation. The new parameter sets enable systematic applications to nuclear matter and finite nuclei across the periodic table, with a forthcoming paper (Long 2006b) planned for further results.
