# Nuclear effects in Neutrino Nuclear Cross-sections

> Nuclear effects in the quasielastic and inelastic scattering of neutrinos(antineutrinos) from nuclear targets have been studied. The calculations are done in the local density approximation which take into account the effect of nucleon motion as well as renormalisation of weak transition strengths in the nuclear medium. The inelastic reaction leading to production of pions is calculated in a $Δ$ dominance model taking into account the renormalization of $Δ$ properties in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim
Nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, RPA correlations, and \(\Delta\) self-energy modifications) reduce charged-current quasielastic cross sections on \(^{12}\text{C}\) by 20% at 1.0 GeV and by 18% at 2 GeV relative to the free-nucleon case, with larger reductions at lower energies; the abstract does not specify that these reductions are relative to the free case and that RPA effects are the dominant source of suppression.

## Method
The authors compute neutrino-nucleus cross sections using a local density approximation (LDA) with a local Fermi gas model. For quasielastic scattering, the hadronic tensor is renormalized via random phase approximation (RPA) using a nucleon-nucleon potential from pion and rho exchanges, and Coulomb distortion of the outgoing muon is included via a modified effective momentum approximation. For inelastic pion production, a \(\Delta\)-dominance model is employed, with the \(\Delta\) mass and width modified by a nuclear self-energy \(\Sigma_\Delta\) (including Pauli blocking, two-body absorption \(\Delta N \rightarrow NN\), and three-body absorption \(\Delta N N \rightarrow NNN\)), and final-state interactions of pions are treated with a Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers
- Reduction in charged-current quasielastic cross section on \(^{12}\text{C}\) (with RPA effects) relative to free case: 70% at \(E_\nu = 0.2\) GeV, 40% at 0.4 GeV, 20% at 1.0 GeV, 18% at 2.0 GeV.
- Reduction without RPA effects (only Fermi motion and Pauli blocking): 45% at 0.2 GeV, 16% at 0.4 GeV, 9% at 1.0 GeV, 7% at 2.0 GeV.
- Contribution of quasielastic-like inelastic processes (from \(\Delta\) absorption) to the \(Q^2\) distribution averaged over the MiniBooNE flux: 12–15% in the peak \(Q^2\) region.
- Reduction in CC1\(\pi^+\) production cross section due to nuclear medium effects alone: 12–15% for neutrino energies 0.6–3 GeV; with pion absorption included, total reduction is 30–40%.
- \(\Delta\) mass shift in medium: \(M_\Delta \rightarrow M_\Delta + 40 (\rho/\rho_0)\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections using the local Fermi gas model (Singh, prior work) and the NUANCE Monte Carlo generator used by the MiniBooNE collaboration (MiniBooNE, 2007). It incorporates RPA correlations and \(\Delta\) self-energy modifications from Oset et al. (Oset, prior work) to improve agreement with MiniBooNE’s measured CC1\(\pi^+\) cross sections and \(Q^2\) distributions. The results enable more accurate modeling of nuclear effects for neutrino oscillation experiments such as K2K and MiniBooNE, particularly in the energy region around 0.7–1.3 GeV.
