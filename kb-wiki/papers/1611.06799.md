# Phase transition in the SRG flow of nuclear interactions

> We use a chiral interaction at N3LO in the 1S0 channel of the nucleon- nucleon interaction in order to investigate the on-shell transition along the similarity renormalization group flow towards the infrared limit. We find a crossover at a scale that depends on the number of grid points used to discretise the momentum space.

## Key claim

The paper reports a crossover (not a sharp phase transition) in the on-shell transition of the similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow for a chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction, where the critical scale depends on the number of grid points used to discretize momentum space.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the Wegner SRG flow equation for the N3LO chiral potential in the 1S0 channel, evolving the interaction from high to low resolution scales (lambda from 2 to 0 fm^-1). They defined the Frobenius norm of the potential as an order parameter and its derivative (the "similarity susceptibility") to locate the crossover scale, using N=10, 20, and 30 grid points for momentum discretization up to 4 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- The critical similarity cutoff for N=30 grid points is lambda_c ~ 0.9 fm^-1.
- The SRG evolution was performed in the range 0 < lambda < 2 fm^-1.
- The maximum momentum used for the N3LO potential was 4 fm^-1.
- The number of grid points tested were N=10, 20, and 30.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Timoteo, Arriola, Szpigel, 2015-2016) that used toy models for the nuclear force, now applying the analysis to a realistic chiral N3LO potential from Entem and Machleidt. The observed crossover behavior is analogous to the chiral crossover transition in QCD studied via thermal susceptibility. The finding that the on-shell limit is reached quickly for finite systems with discretized momentum enables practical applications of SRG-evolved interactions in nuclear structure calculations.
