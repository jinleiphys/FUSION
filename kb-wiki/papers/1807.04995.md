# Longitudinal fluctuations and decorrelations of anisotropic flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the longitudinal decorrelations of elliptic, triangular and quadrangular flows in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC energies. The event-by-event CLVisc (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics model, combined with the fully fluctuating AMPT initial conditions, is utilized to simulate the space-time evolution of the strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma. Detailed analysis is performed for the longitudinal decorrelations of flow vectors, flow magnitudes and flow orientations. We find strong correlations between final-state longitudinal decorrelations of anisotropic flows and initial-state longitudinal structures and collision geometry: while the decorrelation of elliptic flow shows a non-monotonic centrality dependence due to initial elliptic geometry, typically the longitudinal flow decorrelations are larger in lower energy and less central collisions where the mean lengths of the string structure are shorter in the initial states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that longitudinal decorrelations of anisotropic flows in heavy-ion collisions are strongly correlated with the initial-state longitudinal structure and collision geometry, with the decorrelation of elliptic flow showing a non-monotonic centrality dependence due to initial elliptic geometry, while longitudinal flow decorrelations are typically larger in lower energy and less central collisions where the mean lengths of the initial string structures are shorter.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event CLVisc (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics model combined with fully fluctuating AMPT initial conditions to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. They computed longitudinal decorrelation functions for flow vectors, flow magnitudes, and flow orientations using the Qn-vector method, and quantified the decorrelation effects using slope parameters f[n,k], fM[n,k], and fΦ[n,k] as defined by the ATLAS Collaboration. The k-means algorithm was applied to extract the lengths of initial string structures from the AMPT model to correlate with final-state decorrelations.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature used in the Cooper-Frye formula: Tfz = 137 MeV
- Slope parameters f[n,k], fΦ[n,k], and fM[n,k] (with k=1) were computed for elliptic (n=2), triangular (n=3), and quadrangular (n=4) flows for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV and 2.76A TeV, and for Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV
- Five centrality bins were analyzed: 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50%
- Three collision energies were compared: 200A GeV (RHIC), 2.76A TeV (LHC), and 5.02A TeV (LHC)

## Context

This work extends previous studies on anisotropic flow fluctuations and correlations in the transverse direction to the longitudinal (pseudorapidity) direction, following the approach of the ATLAS Collaboration (Aaboud 2017) for defining slope parameters. The study builds on earlier work by Pang (2014, 2015) on longitudinal decorrelations and uses the CLVisc hydrodynamics model (Pang 2018) combined with AMPT initial conditions (Lin 2004). The results enable a deeper understanding of how initial-state longitudinal structures, particularly the mean lengths of string structures, influence final-state flow decorrelations across different collision energies and centralities at RHIC and the LHC.
