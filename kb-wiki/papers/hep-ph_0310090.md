# Large mass Q-Qbar production from the Color Glass Condensate

> We compute quark-antiquark pair production in the context of the Color Glass Condensate model for central heavy-ion collisions. The calculation is performed analytically to leading order in the density of hard sources present in the projectiles, and is applicable to quarks with a mass large compared to the saturation momentum. The formulas derived in this paper are compared to expressions derived in the framework of collinearly factorized perturbative QCD and in kt factorization models. We comment on the breaking of kt factorization which occurs beyond leading order in our approach.

## Key claim

The authors compute quark-antiquark pair production in the Color Glass Condensate model analytically to leading order in the density of hard sources, but the result is explicitly applicable only to quarks with a mass large compared to the saturation momentum, and the leading-order approximation is justified only when the quark mass or transverse momentum provides a scale large compared to the saturation scale.

## Method

The authors compute the pair production amplitude from the classical color fields generated by the two nuclear projectiles, expanding the fields to order O(ρ₁ρ₂) in the hard color source densities. They then square the amplitude and average over the source distributions using the McLerran-Venugopalan Gaussian model, obtaining an expression for the pair production probability and cross-section. The resulting formula is compared to the standard k⊥-factorization and collinear factorization results, and the authors identify the effective Lipatov vertex in their amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The typical transverse momentum spread from nuclear size is Δk⊥ ∼ 1/R ∼ 30 MeV for a nuclear radius R = 6 fm.
- The relation between the saturation scale and the color charge density squared per unit area is Q_s²(x, x⊥) = α_s N_c μ²(x, x⊥) ln(g²μ²/Λ_QCD²).
- The leading-order contribution to pair production is of order O(ρ₁²ρ₂²) in the hard color source densities.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach to heavy quark hadroproduction, following earlier CGC studies of inclusive gluon production by Kovchegov and Rischke (2001) and by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2001-2003). The authors compare their results to the k⊥-factorization formalism developed by Collins, Ellis, and others (1991) and to the collinear factorization approach of Nason, Dawson, and Ellis (1988-1989). The paper enables future studies of k⊥-factorization breaking at transverse momenta below the saturation scale, and provides a framework for numerical solutions that include all orders in the source densities.
