# Pair condensation and bound states in fermionic systems

> We study the finite temperature-density phase diagram of an attractive fermionic system that supports two-body (dimer) and three-body (trimer) bound states in free space. Using interactions characteristic for nuclear systems, we obtain the critical temperature T_c2 for the superfluid phase transition and the limiting temperature T_c3 for the extinction of trimers. The phase diagram features a Cooper-pair condensate in the high-density, low-temperature domain which, with decreasing density, crosses over to a Bose condensate of strongly bound dimers. The high-temperature, low-density domain is populated by trimers whose binding energy decreases toward the density-temperature domain occupied by the superfluid and vanishes at a critical temperature T_c3 > T_c2.

## Key claim

The paper maps the finite temperature-density phase diagram of dilute isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, showing that the ratio of three-body to two-body binding energies remains constant (η = 3.38) independent of temperature, even as both binding energies vanish at a critical temperature T_c3 that lies above the superfluid critical temperature T_c2.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature BCS gap equation for neutron-proton pairing in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel using a Malfliet-Tjon (MF-III) potential, obtaining the superfluid critical temperature T_c2 and the chemical potential self-consistently. They then solved the homogeneous three-body Faddeev-type integral equations in the medium, incorporating two-body and three-body phase-space occupation factors Q_2 and Q_3, to determine the in-medium trimer binding energy as a function of density and temperature. The two-body T-matrix was computed from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the same potential, and the three-body bound-state wave functions were obtained by iteration in a partial-wave basis.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy in free space: E_d = -2.23 MeV (from MF-III potential)
- Triton binding energy in free space: E_t = -7.53 MeV (from MF-III potential)
- Ratio of three-body to two-body binding energy: η = E_t(β)/E_d(β) = 3.38, constant independent of temperature
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Triplet neutron-proton scattering length: a = 5.4 fm
- Chemical potential at zero density and zero temperature: μ(∞) = -1.1 MeV (half the deuteron binding energy)
- Strong-coupling regime realized for f = n_0/n ≥ 40, corresponding to diluteness parameter na^3 = 0.63 < 1
- Crossover from weak to strong coupling (chemical potential changes sign) occurs at f ≈ 80
- BCS weak-coupling parameters modified: α ~ 0.2 and β ~ 0.9 (instead of α = 1 = β)
- Critical temperature T_c3 > T_c2; trimers vanish at T_c3 where E_t = 0

## Context

This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover theory for neutron-proton pairing in symmetric nuclear matter (previously studied by the authors and others such as BCS_BEC1, BCS_BEC2, SD_SYMMETRIC) to include three-body bound states. It builds on the in-medium three-body equations formulated by Sedrakian and Roepke (2000) and the Faddeev approach to nuclear matter pioneered by Bethe (1965). The results enable future studies of the full phase diagram of dilute nuclear matter including alpha-particle clusters (A=4) and higher-mass clusters, which are relevant for supernova and neutron-star physics.
