# Heavy flavor correlations and Quarkonia production in high energy pp collisions in the EPOS4 framework

> In QCD, the production of heavy quark-antiquark pairs can proceed through different mechanisms, each imprinting characteristic correlations between the heavy quarks. In this work, we use the EPOS4 event generator to study how these correlations affect quarkonium production in pp collisions. Our results demonstrate that the observed correlations between heavy mesons directly reflect the underlying production mechanisms and simultaneously shape the transverse-momentum distributions of quarkonia.

## Key claim

The observed correlations between heavy mesons directly reflect the underlying production mechanisms and simultaneously shape the transverse-momentum distributions of quarkonia, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the quantal N-body Wigner density can be replaced by the average of classical phase space distributions from EPOS4.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS4 event generator (version EPOS4.0.1.s9) to simulate heavy quark-antiquark pair production in pp collisions through three processes: flavor creation, flavor excitation, and gluon splitting. They applied a quantum density matrix formalism where the probability of forming a specific quarkonium state is given by the trace of the meson density matrix with the N-body heavy quark density matrix, with the Wigner density of quarkonia approximated using a 3-D isotropic harmonic oscillator potential. The classical momentum space distributions of heavy quarks were provided by EPOS4, while the relative distance between quark and antiquark in their center-of-mass frame was assumed to follow a Gaussian distribution with effective width sigma_{c\bar c} = 0.35 fm for all charmonium states.

## Key numbers

- The effective width for the relative distance between charm and anticharm quarks was set to sigma_{c\bar c} = 0.35 fm for all charmonium states
- The Wigner density widths were 0.348 fm for J/psi, 0.426 fm for chi_c, and 0.452 fm for psi(2S)
- J/psi mesons with transverse momentum p_T greater than approximately 4 GeV are produced almost exclusively from charm quarks originating in gluon-splitting processes
- The pp collisions were studied at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the quantum density matrix approach for quarkonium production previously developed by Song (2017) and Zhao (2023) for pp collisions, and by Villar (2022) and Song (2023) for heavy-ion collisions. The study uses the EPOS4 event generator framework from Werner (2023) and demonstrates that ignoring the correlations between charm and anticharm quarks leads to underestimation of high-p_T charmonium production relative to experimental data. The angle-dependent Wigner density for psi(2S) may become important for excited-state production in anisotropic systems, though it can be neglected in pp collisions.
