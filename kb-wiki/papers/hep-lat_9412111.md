# Monte Carlo and Renormalization Group Effective Potentials in Scalar Field Theories

> We study constraint effective potentials for various strongly interacting $ϕ^4$ theories. Renormalization group (RG) equations for these quantities are discussed and a heuristic development of a commonly used RG approximation is presented which stresses the relationships among the loop expansion, the Schwinger-Dyson method and the renormalization group approach. We extend the standard RG treatment to account explicitly for finite lattice effects. Constraint effective potentials are then evaluated using Monte Carlo (MC) techniques and careful comparisons are made with RG calculations. Explicit treatment of finite lattice effects is found to be essential in achieving quantitative agreement with the MC effective potentials. Excellent agreement is demonstrated for $d=3$ and $d=4$, O(1) and O(2) cases in both symmetric and broken phases.

## Key claim

The renormalization group effective potential, when explicitly modified to account for finite lattice artifacts (the "L-RG" method), provides a spectacularly accurate description of lattice Monte Carlo constraint effective potentials for strongly interacting φ⁴ theories, whereas the continuum RG (C-RG) and truncated Schwinger-Dyson methods yield only qualitative agreement at best.

## Method

The authors derived a heuristic RG equation for the effective potential by starting from the one-loop perturbative correction and imposing self-consistency at each momentum scale, yielding a differential equation in the cutoff Λ. They solved this equation in two ways: a "continuum RG" (C-RG) treating Λ as a continuous variable with a polynomial expansion in φ, and a "lattice RG" (L-RG) that explicitly sums over the discrete momentum modes of a finite cubic lattice. Monte Carlo constraint effective potentials were extracted from lattice simulations using the Metropolis algorithm with a windowing technique to sample a wide range of average field values.

## Key numbers

- For d=3, O(1) symmetric phase on a 16³ lattice with λ₀=10: MC bare mass squared m₀² = -2.16² yields renormalized mass m_fit = 0.285 and λ_fit = 1.49; L-RG requires m₀² = -2.14² (differing by less than 1%) and gives λ = 1.43
- For d=3, O(1) symmetric phase: C-RG requires m₀² = -1.91² (differing by about 12% from MC) and gives λ = 1.89
- For d=3, O(1) symmetric phase: latticized Schwinger-Dyson requires m₀² = -2.61² to give m_SD = 0.285 and yields λ_SD = 0.65 versus MC λ_fit = 1.49
- For d=4, O(1) symmetric phase: MC m_fit = 0.276 using m₀² = -1.88²; latticized SD requires m₀² = -2.13² and gives λ_SD = 1.28 versus MC λ_MC = 3.42
- For d=3, O(1) broken phase: L-RG bare mass squared m₀² = -2.23² differs from MC value -2.24² by less than 1%
- For d=4, O(2) case on an 8⁴ lattice: MC mass m = 0.06 (correlation length ~15)
- L-RG bare masses never differ by more than 1.5% from MC values across all cases studied

## Context

This work extends the RG effective potential approach of Hasenfratz and Hasenfratz (1984) by explicitly incorporating finite lattice effects, enabling direct comparison with lattice Monte Carlo without extrapolation to infinite volume. The authors contrast their L-RG method with truncated Schwinger-Dyson equations (Rivers, 1987) and the continuum RG approach, finding that only the L-RG achieves quantitative agreement. This enables accurate semianalytic calculation of effective potentials at a tiny fraction of the computational cost of Monte Carlo, and the momentum expansion framework allows systematic improvements through next-to-leading order corrections.
