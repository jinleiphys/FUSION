# From QCD to Physical Resonances

> In this talk, we present the first chiral extrapolation of a resonant scattering amplitude obtained from lattice QCD. Finite-volume spectra, determined by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration at $m_π= 236$ MeV, for the isotriplet $ππ$ channel are analyzed using the Lüscher method to determine the infinite-volume scattering amplitude. Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory is then used to extrapolate the scattering amplitude to the physical light quark masses. The viability of this procedure is demonstrated by its agreement with the experimentally determined scattering phase shift up to center-of-mass energies of 1.2 GeV. Finally, we analytically continue the amplitude to the complex plane to obtain the $ρ$-pole at $\left[755(2)(1)\left({}^{20}_{02}\right) - \frac{i}{2} 129(3)(1)\left({}^7_1\right)\right]$ MeV.

# From QCD to Physical Resonances

## Key claim

The paper presents the first chiral extrapolation of a resonant scattering amplitude obtained from lattice QCD, demonstrating that Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory (UχPT) can be used to extrapolate the isotriplet ππ scattering amplitude from unphysically heavy quark masses (mπ = 236 MeV) to the physical point, with the caveat that the extrapolation relies on neglecting few-body thresholds such as 4π, 6π, and KK̅ which lie well above the ρ resonance at the simulated quark masses.

## Method

Finite-volume energy levels for the (I,J)=(1,1) ππ channel, previously computed by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration at mπ = 236 MeV, were analyzed using the Lüscher method to extract the infinite-volume scattering amplitude. The amplitude was parametrized using SU(2) Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory via the Inverse Amplitude Method, with two linear combinations of next-to-leading order low-energy coefficients (α1 and α2) fitted to 22 lattice energy levels. The resulting amplitude was then extrapolated to the physical pion mass and analytically continued to the complex plane to extract the ρ-meson pole.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson pole at the physical point was found at [755(2)(1)(+20/-02) - i/2 × 129(3)(1)(+7/-1)] MeV, where the first uncertainty is statistical, the second is systematic from mπ and anisotropy determination, and the third (with asymmetric superscript/subscript) is systematic from lattice spacing determination.
- At the simulated pion mass mπ = 236 MeV, the ρ pole was found at Eρ = 782(2) - i/2 × 85(2) MeV.
- The fitted low-energy coefficients at renormalization scale μ = 770 MeV were α1 = 14.7(4)(2)(1) × 10⁻³ and α2 = -28(6)(3)(+01/-11) × 10⁻³, with a statistical correlation of -0.98 and χ² per degree of freedom of 1.26.
- The extrapolated scattering amplitude agrees with experimental phase shift data up to center-of-mass energies of 1.2 GeV.
- The lattice calculation used mπL ≈ 4.4, ensuring exponentially suppressed finite-volume corrections.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of the ρ resonance by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Wilson 2015) at mπ = 236 MeV by providing a chiral extrapolation to the physical point using UχPT, which had been previously advocated as a tool for extrapolating lattice results (Chen 2012, Doring 2012, Bernard 2010). The method enables comparison with experimental ππ scattering data (Protopopescu 1973, Estabrooks 1974) and provides a framework for future studies of more phenomenologically interesting isoscalar channels and electromagnetic processes involving resonances, where lattice QCD calculations are still in early stages (Dudek 2013, Briceno 2015).
