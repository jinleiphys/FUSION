# Effect of magnetic screening mass on the diffusion of heavy quarks

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma (QGP) have been estimated by shielding both the electric and magnetic type infra-red divergences. The electric type screening in perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) has been widely studied and used in evaluating the diffusion coefficient of heavy quarks (HQs). To our knowledge the impact of magnetic screening in diffusion coefficients of HQs is not studied before. It is found that the effect of magnetic screening mass on the drag and diffusion coefficients of HQs is quite significant and its contribution should not be ignored for explaining the experimental data of heavy quark observables.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including a non-zero magnetic screening mass for gluons, in addition to the standard electric (Debye) screening mass, significantly reduces the drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks in quark-gluon plasma, and that this contribution should not be ignored when comparing theoretical calculations to experimental data. The hedge is that the magnetic mass value is not uniquely determined, and the authors adopt a range of \(0.4 < m_{mag}/m_D < 0.6\) based on lattice QCD results, using the lower bound \(x=0.4\) for their numerical results.

## Method

The authors compute the drag and diffusion coefficients of charm quarks in a thermal quark-gluon plasma using the Fokker-Planck equation, with scattering matrix elements evaluated at tree level in perturbative QCD. The infrared divergences in the \(t\)-channel gluon exchange diagrams are regulated by introducing a combined screening mass \(m' = \sqrt{m_D^2 + m_{mag}^2}\), where the Debye mass \(m_D\) is taken to next-to-leading order and the magnetic mass \(m_{mag}\) is taken from lattice QCD estimates. The thermal averages over scattering processes (gluon + charm, light quark + charm) are evaluated numerically using the matrix elements modified by the magnetic mass.

## Key numbers

* Drag coefficient reduction: approximately 38% at \(T = 250\) MeV and 36% at \(T = 400\) MeV for charm quarks at zero momentum, due to magnetic screening (with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\)).
* Change in drag at \(T = 250\) MeV and \(p \sim 0\) is about 23% for \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).
* Estimated enhancement of the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\): approximately 14.4% due to magnetic screening, based on a back-of-the-envelope calculation using average drag values of \(\gamma_{WO} = 0.06\) fm\(^{-1}\) (without magnetic mass) and \(\gamma_{W} = 0.038\) fm\(^{-1}\) (with magnetic mass) over the temperature range \(T = 0.250 - 0.400\) GeV and a QGP lifetime \(\Delta\tau = 6\) fm.
* Magnetic mass range used: \(m_{mag}/m_D \equiv x\) with \(0.4 < x < 0.6\), taken from lattice QCD calculations by Maezawa, Hart, Nakamura, and Bak.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark diffusion, which only included electric (Debye) screening to regulate infrared divergences (Svetitsky, 1988; Le Bellac). It builds on earlier studies of magnetic mass effects on jet energy loss by Djordjevic et al., who showed that magnetic screening enhances energy loss. The present paper is the first to study the impact of magnetic screening on the drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks. The results enable more complete theoretical calculations of heavy quark observables (nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\)) in heavy-ion collisions, and suggest that future phenomenological studies should include magnetic screening alongside other effects.
