# Fission barriers and probabilities of spontaneous fission for elements with Z$\geq$100

> This is a short review of methods and results of calculations of fission barriers and fission half-lives of even-even superheavy nuclei. An approvable agreement of the following approaches is shown and discussed: The macroscopic-microscopic approach based on the stratagem of the shell correction to the liquid drop model and a vantage point of microscopic energy density functionals of Skyrme and Gogny type selfconsistently calculated within Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. Mass parameters are calculated in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov cranking approximation. A short part of the paper is devoted to the nuclear fission dynamics. We also discuss the predictive power of Skyrme functionals applied to key properties of the fission path of $^{266}$Hs. It applies the standard techniques of error estimates in the framework of a $χ^2$ analysis.

## Key claim
This review paper demonstrates that while macroscopic-microscopic (MM) and self-consistent (Skyrme and Gogny) approaches show "approvable agreement" on topological properties of fission barriers for superheavy nuclei with Z≥100, they differ quantitatively in barrier heights by up to 2-3 MeV, with self-consistent models generally predicting higher barriers.

## Method
The authors compare three theoretical approaches: the macroscopic-microscopic method using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with Strutinsky shell correction and Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy; the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the SkM* functional; and the Gogny-HFB method with the D1S interaction. Mass parameters are calculated in the HFB cranking approximation, with a scaling factor of 1.3 applied to the Inglis cranking mass to simulate the adiabatic time-dependent HFB (ATDHFB) mass. Fission half-lives are computed using the WKB approximation with Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization to determine the collective ground state energy.

## Key numbers
- Fission barriers from the MM model are smaller than 7 MeV for all considered superheavy nuclei, with the highest values for ²⁷⁰108₁₆₂, ²⁹²114₁₇₈, and nuclei around Z≈100, N≈150.
- The MM barrier for ³¹²126₁₈₆ is 1.43 MeV, while self-consistent models predict about 12 MeV for a neighboring nucleus.
- In the Woods-Saxon MM model, the barrier for Z=114, N=178 reaches 6.34 MeV.
- For 18 first and 22 second barriers in even-even actinides, the MM model gives rms deviations of 0.5 and 0.7 MeV, respectively, compared to experimental data.
- In the Skyrme SkM* model, barriers in the experimentally known region are of order 6-7 MeV, while for the heaviest nuclei they reach 8-10 MeV.
- With the Gogny D1S force, the longest fission half-life is predicted for ²⁷⁰Hs with log(t_SF/s) = 12.83.
- Minimal fission half-lives in the Gogny model, not longer than t_SF = 10³ s, occur around neutron number N=170 in each superheavy element.
- In the Gogny model, half-lives for isotopes with N=182 extend far above t_SF = 10²⁰ s.
- Triaxial deformation can decrease the saddle point energy by as much as 3 MeV in some isotopes with N<174 in relativistic mean-field calculations.

## Context
This review extends the work of Sobiczewski and Pomorski (2007), the last comprehensive review of theoretical properties of superheavy elements, and incorporates recent developments by the Lublin-Madrid collaboration, the Lublin-Oak Ridge-Warsaw group, and the Erlangen-Darmstadt team. The paper compares MM results from Kowal (2010) and Möller (2015) with self-consistent calculations using Skyrme and Gogny functionals, and discusses discrepancies between approaches, particularly the higher barriers predicted by self-consistent models. The work enables future improvements by identifying the need for unified treatment of collective inertia using the same ATDHFB cranking model across all approaches, and by highlighting the importance of including multiple collective coordinates and improved energy density functionals.
