# Are we close to the QGP? - Hadrochemical vs. microscopic analysis of particle production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> Ratios of hadronic abundances are analyzed for pp and nucleus-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s)=20 GeV using the microscopic transport model UrQMD. Secondary interactions significantly change the primordial hadronic cocktail of the system. A comparison to data shows a strong dependence on rapidity. Without assuming thermal and chemical equilibrium, predicted hadron yields and ratios agree with many of the data, the few observed discrepancies are discussed.

## Key claim

Without assuming thermal and chemical equilibrium, the microscopic transport model UrQMD can reproduce many measured hadron abundance ratios in pp and nucleus-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s) ≈ 20 GeV, but large discrepancies (> 50%) remain for the K⁰_S/Λ and Ω/Ξ ratios, and the strong rapidity dependence of the ratios casts doubt on the assumption of global thermal and chemical equilibrium that underlies previous fireball and equilibrium-model analyses.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model version 1.0, which treats 55 isospin-degenerate baryon species and 32 isospin-degenerate meson species, with string excitation for masses above 2 GeV (baryons) and 1.5 GeV (mesons). They calculated hadron yields and ratios for pp collisions, isospin-weighted nucleon-nucleon cocktails (pp, pn, nn), and full S+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon, applying experimental acceptance cuts in rapidity and transverse momentum where specified, and compared the results to experimental data compilations from the AGS and SPS.

## Key numbers

- For S+Au at 200 GeV/nucleon around midrapidity (y_lab = 3 ± 0.5), a thermal model fit to the UrQMD calculation yields T = 145 MeV and baryo-chemical potential μ_B = 165 MeV.
- Isospin weighting enhances the primordial p̄/p ratio by ~30% and the Λ/(p − p̄) ratio by ~40% relative to pp alone.
- Rescattering changes hadron ratios by 20%–50%; ratios involving antibaryons change by factors of 3–5 (e.g., Ξ̄/Ξ suppressed by factor 5, p̄/p by factor 3, Λ̄/Λ by factor 2, Ξ⁻/Λ enhanced by factor 2, K⁰_S/Λ̄ enhanced by factor 3).
- For Pb+Pb, rescattering effects are larger: Ξ̄/Ξ suppressed by factor 20, p̄/p by factor 8, K⁰_S/Λ̄ enhanced by factor 3.
- The φ/(ρ+ω), K⁰_S/Λ, and Ω/Ξ ratios show large discrepancies (> 50%) between UrQMD and experimental data.
- In elementary pp collisions, φ-production is underestimated by a factor of 2.
- If the proton-antiproton annihilation cross section as a function of √s is used for all baryon-antibaryon annihilations instead of rescaling to equivalent relative momenta, the Ξ̄ yield in central Pb+Pb at 200 GeV/nucleon would be enhanced by a factor of 3, p̄ yields by 50%, and anti-hyperon yields by 25%.

## Context

This work extends the non-equilibrium transport approach of earlier Quantum Molecular Dynamics models (Peilert 1988, Hartnack 1989, Aichelin 1991, Sorge 1989, Lehmann 1995) and directly confronts the conclusions of thermal and chemical equilibrium fits to AGS and SPS data by Braun-Munzinger (1997) and by hadronizing QGP droplet models (Spieles 1997, Barz 1988). The strong rapidity dependence and rescattering effects shown here challenge the assumption that the hadronic final state reflects a system near or above the critical temperature for QGP formation, and the authors predict that Pb+Pb hadron ratios will deviate far from elementary nucleon-nucleon values, enabling future tests of antibaryon annihilation cross sections and the role of secondary interactions.
