# Statistical Behavior of Finite-Size Partially Equilibrated Systems

> We examine deviations from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics for partially equilibrated systems of finite size. We find that such systems are characterized by the Levy distribution whose non-extensivity parameter is related to the number of internally equilibrated subsystems and to correlations among them. This concept is applied to Quark-Gluon Plasma formation.

## Key claim

Partially equilibrated systems of finite size are characterized by the Lévy distribution, with the non-extensivity parameter \(q\) related to the number of internally equilibrated subsystems and to correlations among them; the derivation relies on the strong assumption that the total energy equals the ensemble mean energy of all subsystems at temperature \(T\).

## Method

The author considers a Gibbs ensemble of finite-size system replicas, each consisting of a finite number of internally equilibrated subsystems at various temperatures. A Poissonian distribution for the number of subsystems at a given temperature is assumed, leading to a Gamma distribution for the temperature parameter; averaging the Boltzmann-Gibbs factor over this Gamma distribution yields the Lévy distribution. The entropy non-extensivity is then derived by approximating the Tsallis entropy for subsystems with \(q_0\) close to unity and incorporating correlations via a correction term \(\kappa\).

## Key numbers

- The Lévy distribution parameter \(q\) satisfies \(1 \le q < 2\), with the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential recovered in the limit \(q \rightarrow 1\).
- The parameter \(\alpha\) is defined as \(\alpha = 1/(q-1)\); when \(q \rightarrow 2\), \(\alpha = 1\) (only one subsystem at temperature \(T_0\)), and when \(q \rightarrow 1\), \(\alpha \rightarrow \infty\) (infinite number of subsystems at \(T_0\)).
- For the first moment of the Lévy distribution to be finite, \(q \le 3/2\), so complete lack of statistical equilibration cannot be achieved.
- The ensemble mean number of subsystems at temperature \(T\) is \(\langle k \rangle = T_0 / T = \lambda_0 \beta\), where \(\lambda_0 = T_0\) and \(\beta = 1/T\).

## Context

This work extends the observation of Wilk and Wlodarczyk (1999) that the Lévy distribution arises as the mean of the Boltzmann-Gibbs factor over a Gamma distribution of the temperature parameter, by providing an alternative physical origin based on finite-size partially equilibrated systems. It also builds on the work of Gross and Votyakov (1999) on small systems and Prosper (1993) on temperature fluctuations in finite heat baths. The results enable a statistical description of Quark-Gluon Plasma formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where partial equilibration and finite-size effects are expected, and connect to the non-extensive Tsallis statistics used by Walton and Rafelski (1999) for charmed quark diffusion.
