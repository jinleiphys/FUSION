# Double neutral pion photoproduction at threshold

> We consider the chiral expansion of the threshold amplitude for the reaction $γp \to π^0 π^0 p$ to order ${\cal O}(M_π^2)$. We substantiate a claim that this photoproduction channel is significantly enhanced close to threshold due to pion loops. A precise measurement of the corresponding cross sections is called for which allows to test chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The chiral expansion of the threshold amplitude for γp → π⁰π⁰p is well-behaved, with the leading-order (q³) pion-loop contribution being at least 1.5 times larger than the next-to-leading-order (q⁴) correction, but the total cross-section prediction carries a significant uncertainty from the Roper resonance contribution, which the abstract does not mention.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute all terms up to and including O(M_π²) in the threshold amplitude for γp → π⁰π⁰p. They evaluated tree graphs from the dimension-one through dimension-four effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian, pion loop graphs with at most one dimension-two insertion, and estimated higher-dimension contact terms via resonance saturation, including contributions from the Roper resonance N*(1440), double Δ excitation, and kaon-loop diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Threshold photon energy in the lab frame: E_γ^thr = 308.8 MeV
- Born contribution to threshold amplitude: M_00^Born = 3.7 GeV⁻³
- Leading (q³) one-pion loop contribution: M_00^q³-loop = (26.9 + 16.2 i) GeV⁻³
- Next-to-leading (q⁴) one-loop contribution: M_00^q⁴-loop = (−10.3 − 17.6 i) GeV⁻³
- Tree-level contribution from low-energy constants c_i: M_00^c_i = 18.4 GeV⁻³
- Upper limit for Roper resonance contribution: M_00^N* < 11.7 GeV⁻³
- Total threshold amplitude (maximal Roper): M_00 ≃ (53 + 7 i) GeV⁻³
- Predicted total cross section (upper limit): σ_tot(E_γ) ≤ 0.91 nb × [(E_γ − E_γ^thr) / 10 MeV]²
- Predicted total cross section (central Roper couplings): 0.63 nb × [(E_γ − E_γ^thr) / 10 MeV]²

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (1995) which first identified the large chiral loop enhancement in double neutral pion photoproduction at threshold. The calculation incorporates higher-order terms and resonance contributions, including the Roper resonance which plays a major role in models by Oset and collaborators that describe the energy dependence over a larger range. The prediction enables a direct test of chiral perturbation theory using new precision data from the TAPS collaboration at MAMI, which were being analyzed at the time of publication.
