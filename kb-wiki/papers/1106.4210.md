# Extraction of shear viscosity in stationary states of relativistic particle systems

> Starting from a classical picture of shear viscosity we construct a stationary velocity gradient in a microscopic parton cascade. Employing the Navier-Stokes ansatz we extract the shear viscosity coefficient $η$. For elastic isotropic scatterings we find an excellent agreement with the analytic values. This confirms the applicability of this method. Furthermore for both elastic and inelastic scatterings with pQCD based cross sections we extract the shear viscosity coefficient $η$ for a pure gluonic system and find a good agreement with already published calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a stationary velocity gradient can be constructed in a microscopic parton cascade to extract shear viscosity via the Navier-Stokes ansatz, but only if finite-size effects are accounted for by replacing the mean free path with an effective scale in the analytical rapidity profile.

## Method

The authors used the BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of Multi Parton Scatterings) transport model to simulate a gluon gas between two moving thermal walls, establishing a stationary velocity gradient. They derived an analytical expression for the rapidity profile that includes finite-size effects via the mean free path, then extracted the shear viscosity coefficient from the Navier-Stokes relation using the numerically obtained shear tensor and the fitted rapidity gradient. For pQCD interactions, elastic \(gg \to gg\) and inelastic \(gg \leftrightarrow ggg\) processes were included with fixed coupling constant \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

- For elastic isotropic constant cross sections with \(\lambda_{\rm mfp} = 0.02\) fm, the numerically extracted shear viscosity agreed with the analytical value \(\eta^{NS} \approx 1.2654 \, T/\sigma = 1.2654 \, n T \lambda_{\rm mfp}\) within standard deviation.
- For pQCD interactions with only \(2\to 2\) processes at \(\alpha_s = 0.01\): \(\eta/s = 192.5 \pm 23\); at \(\alpha_s = 0.6\): \(\eta/s = 0.55 \pm 0.06\).
- For pQCD interactions including \(2\to 2\) and \(2\leftrightarrow 3\) processes at \(\alpha_s = 0.01\): \(\eta/s = 43.6 \pm 5.2\); at \(\alpha_s = 0.6\): \(\eta/s = 0.08 \pm 0.01\).
- The relaxation time for the setup with \(L=2\) fm and \(\lambda_{\rm mfp}=0.2\) fm was estimated as \(t \approx 24\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends prior shear viscosity extractions from BAMPS using gradient expansion methods (Xu et al., 2007) and the entropy principle underlying Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics (El et al., 2008), as well as Green-Kubo relation calculations (Wesp et al., 2008). The new stationary-gradient method provides a direct numerical verification of the Navier-Stokes ansatz in relativistic transport and enables extraction of \(\eta/s\) for nearly perfect fluids where the mean free path is small relative to system size. The results for pQCD interactions show the expected \(1/(\alpha_s^2 \log(1/\alpha_s))\) scaling behavior from leading-order pQCD calculations (Baym et al., 1990; Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2003).
