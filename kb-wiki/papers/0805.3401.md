# Direct photons from relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We recall the seminal developments in the study of radiation of direct photons from relativistic heavy ion collisions, which have helped to enhance the scope of single photons as a probe of the quark gluon plasma considerably. There is a mounting evidence that in addition to providing information about the initial temperature of the plasma as envisaged originally, these radiations measure the momentum anisotropy of the deconfined quarks and gluons, energy loss of the quarks, the initial spatial asymmetry of the plasma, and the history of evolution of the system. After a brief description of the theoretical developments and results for direct photons at SPS energies, we discuss the expectations and findings at RHIC.

## Key claim

Direct photons from relativistic heavy-ion collisions can probe not only the initial temperature of the quark-gluon plasma, but also the momentum anisotropy of deconfined quarks and gluons, energy loss of quarks, initial spatial asymmetry of the plasma, and the history of the system's evolution; however, the interpretation depends on correctly accounting for isospin effects, shadowing, and energy loss of final-state quarks, which earlier scaling approaches from pp collisions neglect.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations of direct photon production from various sources: prompt photons from Compton scattering (q g → q γ) and annihilation (q q̅ → g γ) using NLO pQCD; thermal photons from the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phase; jet-photon conversion and jet-induced bremsstrahlung from quarks traversing the plasma; and photons from hadronic reactions using effective Lagrangians with mesonic fields and baryons. These calculations are compared to experimental data from SPS (S+Au at 200A GeV, Pb+Pb at 158A GeV) and RHIC (Au+Au at √s = 200A GeV) collisions, with the initial temperature determined by relating entropy to measured particle rapidity density.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ₀ ≈ 0.2 fm/c used for QGP formation in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS and Au+Au at RHIC
- Phase transition temperature T_C ≈ 160 MeV assumed for QGP to hadronic matter conversion
- Freeze-out temperature of about 140 MeV for the hadronic gas
- Jet-photon conversion makes a large contribution at p_T ≈ 4–8 GeV at RHIC and LHC energies
- Initial temperature of about 300–500 MeV at τ₀ of about 0.6–0.2 fm/c for Au+Au at 200A GeV
- Single photons clearly identified for p_T > 4 GeV for more central Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Upper limits established for p_T down to about 2 GeV for Au+Au at RHIC
- Decay photons from π⁰ and η account for up to 90–98% of all detected photons

## Context

This review extends earlier work on direct photons as a probe of QGP, building on foundational studies by Feinberg (1976) and subsequent theoretical developments by several groups including the author's collaborators. The paper synthesizes results from SPS experiments (WA80, WA98) and RHIC experiments (PHENIX), showing that single photon measurements can now probe momentum anisotropy via elliptic flow (v₂), jet energy loss, and spatial asymmetry. The work enables future studies at LHC energies and provides a framework for comparing different models of jet quenching and plasma evolution, as well as for using photon-tagged jets to differentiate between quenching models.
