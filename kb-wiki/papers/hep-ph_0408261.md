# Extraction of two-photon contributions to the proton form factors

> Significant discrepancies have been observed between proton form factors as measured by Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer techniques. There are indications that this difference may be caused by corrections to the one photon exchange approximation that are not taken into account in standard radiative correction procedures. In this paper, we constrain the two-photon amplitudes by combining data from Rosenbluth, polarization transfer, and positron-proton scattering measurements. This allows a rough extraction of these two-photon effects in elastic electron-proton scattering, and provides an improved extraction of the proton electromagnetic form factors.

# Extraction of two-photon contributions to the proton form factors

## Key claim
By combining Rosenbluth separation, polarization transfer, and positron-proton scattering data under the assumption that two-photon exchange fully explains the discrepancy between these techniques, the two-photon amplitudes can be constrained and the true Born-level proton form factors extracted, albeit with additional uncertainties of 50–100% beyond experimental errors.

## Method
The author uses the general formalism for parameterizing contributions beyond the one-photon exchange approximation, which introduces three complex amplitudes: ΔG_E, ΔG_M, and Y_{2γ}. The Y_{2γ} amplitude is extracted from the difference between the Rosenbluth and polarization transfer form factor ratios at Q² values from 0.6 to 6.0 GeV². The ΔG_M amplitude is then determined by requiring that the two-photon contribution to the cross section at ε=1 be cancelled by ΔG_M, using the constraint from positron-proton scattering data at large ε. The ΔG_E amplitude is taken as zero with an uncertainty equal to the larger of the other two amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- The extracted Y_{2γ} amplitude is fit as Y_{2γ} = 0.035 · (1 − exp(−Q²/1.45)) with Q² in GeV².
- The extracted ΔG_M/G_M is fit as ΔG_M/G_M = (0.0124 Q² − 0.0445 √Q²) %.
- The correction to the polarization transfer ratio μ_p G_E/G_M reaches 35% at Q² = 5.6 GeV².
- The corrected form factor ratio yields μ_p G_E/G_M = 1 − 0.158 Q² from a linear fit.
- The uncertainty in the two-photon amplitudes is approximately 40–50% for Q² above 3–4 GeV² and reaches 100% for Q² near 1 GeV².
- The positron-to-electron cross section ratio data yield χ² = 18.2 for 28 data points when compared to the two-photon prediction, versus χ² = 23.9 assuming no two-photon effects.
- The best linearity limit at Q² = 2.5 GeV² from the SLAC NE11 experiment gives a quadratic coefficient P₂ = 0.0 ± 0.11.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between proton form factors extracted via Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer techniques, first highlighted by Jones (2000) and Gayou (2002). It extends the formalism of Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2003) by including constraints from positron-proton scattering data from Mar (1968) to determine ΔG_M in addition to Y_{2γ}. The extracted two-photon amplitudes enable corrected form factors that can be compared to lattice QCD calculations and nucleon structure models. Future measurements planned at Novosibirsk and Jefferson Lab could test the assumptions made here and reduce the uncertainties from two-photon corrections to levels comparable to experimental uncertainties.
