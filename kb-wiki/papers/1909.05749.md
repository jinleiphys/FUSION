# Three-body unitarity versus finite-volume $π^+π^+π^+$ spectrum from lattice QCD

> Strong three-body interactions above threshold govern the dynamics of many exotics and conventional excited mesons and baryons. Three-body finite-volume energies calculated from lattice QCD promise an ab-initio understanding of these systems. We calculate the three-$π^+$ spectrum unraveling the three-body dynamics that is tightly intertwined with the $S$-matrix principle of three-body unitarity and compare it with recent lattice QCD results. For this purpose, we develop a formalism for three-body systems in moving frames and apply it numerically.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-body unitarity, a fundamental S-matrix principle, is directly visible in lattice QCD data for the first time, by showing that the structure of the spectator-isobar exchange term (which arises from three-body unitarity) correctly predicts the relative and absolute sizes of excited-state energy shifts in the three-pion spectrum without fitting any three-body force.

## Method

The authors use the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to one loop to predict the I=2 pion-pion S-wave and D-wave amplitudes from chiral perturbation theory low-energy constants (LECs). They then input these two-body amplitudes into a relativistic three-body finite-volume formalism based on the isobar-spectator picture, which they extend to moving frames (boosted systems) to match the lattice QCD calculation of Horz and Hanlon (2019). The three-body spectrum is predicted with a vanishing three-body force, and the predictions are compared to lattice eigenvalues in multiple irreducible representations.

## Key numbers

- Correlated chi-squared per degree of freedom for the three-body sector: chi^2_(3)/n ≈ 9.5/11 (approximately 0.86) for LECs from Gasser and Leutwyler (1984)
- Correlated chi-squared per degree of freedom for the combined two- and three-body sectors: chi^2_(2&3)/n ≈ 39.4/22 (approximately 1.79) for the same LECs
- Correlated chi-squared per degree of freedom for the two-body sector alone: chi^2_(2)/n ≈ 21.2/11 (approximately 1.93)
- Removing one low-energy point (sigma^(1/2) ≈ 2.4 m_pi in the A_1^+(1) irrep) reduces the combined chi^2_(2&3)/n to approximately 28/21 ≈ 1.33
- The pion mass of the lattice calculation is m_pi ≈ 200 MeV
- The D-wave phase shift in the elastic region stays below one degree at this pion mass

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Mai and Doring (2017, 2018) from the three-body rest frame to moving frames, enabling comparison with the lattice QCD results of Horz and Hanlon (2019). It contrasts with earlier work by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2007, Detmold 2008) which had less precise data, and with the independent analysis of Blanton et al. (2019) that appeared simultaneously. The study enables future extraction of three-body forces from lattice data and provides a benchmark for understanding three-body dynamics in more complicated systems such as the Roper resonance and axial mesons.
