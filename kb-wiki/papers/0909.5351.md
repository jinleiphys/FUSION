# Shell-model study of the N=82 isotonic chain with a realistic effective hamiltonian

> We have performed shell-model calculations for the even- and odd-mass N=82 isotones, focusing attention on low-energy states. The single-particle energies and effective two-body interaction have been both determined within the framework of the time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory, starting from a low-momentum interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. In this way, no phenomenological input enters our effective Hamiltonian, whose reliability is evidenced by the good agreement between theory and experiment.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a fully realistic shell-model Hamiltonian for the N=82 isotones, with both single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via the V-low-k renormalization procedure and time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory, yields results in quite good agreement with experiment. However, the cutoff momentum Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 is treated as a parameter chosen to reproduce the experimental single-particle spectrum of 133Sb, and the absolute single-particle energies show poor convergence, meaning the reliability is limited to relative energies and spectroscopic properties rather than absolute ground-state energies.

## Method

The authors derived an effective shell-model Hamiltonian for the proton 2s1d0g0h model space using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion, including one- and two-body Goldstone diagrams through third order in the residual interaction. The starting point was a V-low-k interaction obtained from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential with a cutoff momentum Lambda=2.6 fm^-1, with the Coulomb force added explicitly for protons. The harmonic oscillator potential was used as the auxiliary one-body potential with hbar-omega=7.88 MeV, and the folded-diagram series was summed to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Shell-model calculations were performed for even-mass N=82 isotones up to 154Hf and odd-mass ones up to 149Ho.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Lambda=2.6 fm^-1 was used for the V-low-k interaction, corresponding to a laboratory energy of about 350 MeV at the inelastic threshold (the standard value is about 2.1 fm^-1).
- The harmonic oscillator parameter was hbar-omega=7.88 MeV for A=132.
- Calculated relative single-particle energies in 133Sb: 0g7/2 at 0.000 MeV, 1d5/2 at 1.045 MeV, 1d3/2 at 2.200 MeV, 2s1/2 at 2.006 MeV, 0h11/2 at 3.645 MeV; experimental values: 0g7/2 at 0.000 MeV, 1d5/2 at 0.962 MeV, 1d3/2 at 2.440 MeV, 0h11/2 at 2.793 MeV.
- The absolute single-particle energy of the 1d5/2 level relative to 132Sn was -5.954 MeV at Nmax=15, with no sign of convergence (values were -4.468 and -5.225 MeV at Nmax=11 and 13, respectively).
- Calculated B(E2;2+1 -> 0+1) values in Weisskopf units: 134Te 5.5 (expt. 6.3 +/- 2.0), 136Xe 9.06 (expt. 16.6 +/- 2.4), 138Ba 11.1 (expt. 10.8 +/- 0.5), 140Ce 14.5 (expt. 13.8 +/- 0.3), 142Nd 16.26 (expt. 12.03 +/- 0.22), 144Sm 16.6 (expt. 11.9 +/- 0.4).
- The discrepancy between theoretical and experimental ground-state energies per valence proton for even-mass isotones is about 2.4 MeV, essentially constant along the chain.
- Discrepancies for yrast 2+ and 4+ state excitation energies do not exceed 350 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fully realistic shell-model calculations for p-shell and sd-shell nuclei (Coraggio 2005, Coraggio 2007) to the medium-heavy N=82 isotonic chain, where earlier realistic calculations by Covello (1997), Holt (1997), and Suhonen (1998) used experimental single-particle energies and different renormalization procedures (Bonn A potential with Brueckner G-matrix). The present study is the first to derive both single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements from the same realistic potential for this mass region, though the cutoff momentum must be tuned to reproduce the experimental single-particle spectrum. The results enable future investigations into the role of three-body forces in improving the calculated single-particle energies, particularly for the 0h11/2 and 2s1/2 orbitals.
