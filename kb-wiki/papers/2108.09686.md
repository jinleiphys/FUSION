# Exploring the string shoving model in PYTHIA8 for collective behaviors in $pp$ collisions

> Two-particle correlations have been used to study property of a produced medium called Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and a long-range correlation among produced particles has been considered as a strong evidence of strongly interacting QGP. Strikingly, clear long-range correlations have been also observed in small collision systems like $pp$ and $p$+$A$ at a wide range of collision energies. A model introducing a repulsive force between flux tubes called string shoving can qualitatively describe the long-range near-side correlations in high multiplicity $pp$ collisions. This model implemented in the PYTHIA Monte Carlo event generator allows us to perform detailed study. In this work, a full data analysis to calculate associated yields and flow coefficients is performed with PYTHIA events, and the results are compared with experimental results. We find that the string shoving model overestimates long-range near-side correlations particularly in low multiplicity events. We also introduce a method to subtract such overestimated correlations in low multiplicity to have a more relevant comparison with the data.

## Key claim

The string shoving model implemented in PYTHIA8 qualitatively describes long-range near-side correlations in high-multiplicity pp collisions, but it overestimates these correlations in low-multiplicity events (Nch < 50) and underestimates the flow coefficient v2,2 compared to ATLAS data, primarily because the model introduces a long-range correlation from jets that is not observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors generated millions of non-diffractive PYTHIA8 pp events at √s = 13 TeV with both the default configuration and the string shoving model. They calculated two-particle correlation functions in Δη and Δφ for charged hadrons, applied the Zero-Yield-At-Minimum (ZYAM) procedure to isolate correlated yields, and extracted associated yields and flow coefficients v2,2 via the template fit method developed by ATLAS (Aad 2015). A subtraction procedure using low-multiplicity events (20 ≤ Nch < 30) was introduced to remove overestimated jet-like correlations.

## Key numbers

- Event multiplicity defined with charged hadrons in |η| < 2.4 and pT > 0.4 GeV/c; analysis performed at √s = 13 TeV.
- Long-range correlations defined in the range 2 < |Δη| < 4; one-dimensional Δφ correlations averaged over this range.
- Associated yields for 1 < pT < 2 GeV/c in high-multiplicity events (Nch > 120) from the string shoving model agree with CMS results (Khachatryan 2015), but overestimate yields at Nch < 50.
- v2,2 from the string shoving model is significantly lower than ATLAS results (Aad 2015) for charged hadrons in 0.5 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c; v2,2 from the default PYTHIA8 configuration is close to zero.
- Associated yields from events without multi-parton interactions are consistent with normal low-multiplicity events (Nch < 20), indicating jet-induced correlations.
- Subtraction procedure using associated yields in 20 ≤ Nch < 30 shows larger impact at lower multiplicity and yields similar multiplicity dependence to CMS results.

## Context

This work extends the string shoving model proposed by Bierlich (2016) for collective behavior in small collision systems, implementing it in PYTHIA8 for quantitative comparison with experimental data from CMS (Khachatryan 2015) and ATLAS (Aad 2015). The study contradicts the expectation that the model fully explains long-range correlations, as it overestimates low-multiplicity yields and underestimates v2,2 compared to data. The results enable future refinements of the string shoving mechanism, particularly the need to suppress jet-induced long-range correlations at low multiplicity, and provide a subtraction method for more relevant comparisons with experimental results.
