# Change in the Nuclear Moment of Inertia at the Number of Neutrons N = 98 and Isotopic Shift of Atomic Levels

> From analysis of experimental data on nuclear rotation, it is found that the so-called rigid-body value of the nuclear moment of inertia and the nuclear-radius values associated with the moment of inertia as functions of the number of nucleons change their behavior at the number of neutrons $N = 98$. This phenomenon is confirmed by experimental data on the isotopic shifts of atomic levels.

# Change in the Nuclear Moment of Inertia at the Number of Neutrons N = 98 and Isotopic Shift of Atomic Levels

## Key claim
The rigid-body value of the nuclear moment of inertia and the associated nuclear radius parameter \(r_0\) change their behavior at neutron number \(N = 98\), with \(r_0\) decreasing from \(1.1 \times 10^{-13}\) cm to \(1.0 \times 10^{-13}\) cm for nuclei with \(N \geq 98\) up to the magic number \(N = 126\). This claim is conditional on the interpretation of backbending data in rotational bands, where the rigid-body line must be recalculated with the smaller radius to maintain consistency with the observed angular velocity behavior.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental yrast-line data for tungsten isotopes (\({}^{166}\)W through \({}^{179}\)W) and rotational bands in hafnium and osmium isotopes, extracting the rigid-body moment of inertia from the intersection of the angular velocity curve with the rigid-body line at the backbending point. They compared these dynamical determinations of nuclear radius with independent data on isotopic shifts of atomic levels in ytterbium isotopes, specifically the mean-square nuclear radius as a function of neutron number.

## Key numbers
- The standard nuclear radius parameter is \(r_0 = 1.1 \times 10^{-13}\) cm for most nuclei, obtained from electron scattering and isotopic shift data.
- For nuclei with neutron number \(N \geq 98\) up to \(N = 126\), the radius parameter decreases to \(r_0 = 1.0 \times 10^{-13}\) cm.
- The rigid-body moment of inertia is given by \(\mathcal{J}_0 = \frac{2}{5} M R^2\), where \(M\) is nuclear mass and \(R\) is the nuclear radius.
- At the backbending point \(I_c\), the angular velocity takes the rigid-body value \(\hbar\Omega_{nc} = \frac{\hbar^2}{\mathcal{J}_0} I_c\).
- The effective moment of inertia near the backbending point behaves as \(\mathcal{J} \simeq \frac{j}{I - I_c}\) for \(I \to I_c + 0\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier semi-empirical relations (Kamchatnov and Nosov, 1997-1998) connecting nuclear rotation to rigid-body moments of inertia, and confirms a sharp decrease in the rigid-body moment of inertia at \(N = 98\) first reported in their prior study (Kamchatnov and Nosov, 1998). The finding is independently supported by isotopic shift data in ytterbium isotopes (referenced as [16] in the text), which show a break in the mean-square nuclear radius at the same neutron number. This enables a dynamical method for determining nuclear radii from rotational band data and suggests a universal structural change at \(N = 98\) that warrants further experimental investigation in tungsten, hafnium, and osmium isotopes where isotopic shift measurements are currently insufficient.
