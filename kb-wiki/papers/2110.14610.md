# Heavy quarks in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC: Brownian motion versus diffusion in the evolving Glasma

> We study the transverse momentum, $p_T$, broadening of charm and beauty quarks in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions. We aim to compare the diffusion in the evolving Glasma fields with that of a standard, Markovian-Brownian motion in a thermalized medium with the same energy density of the Glasma fields. The Brownian motion is studied via Langevin equations with diffusion coefficients computed within perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. We find that for small values of the saturation scale, $Q_s$, the average $p_T$ broadening in the two motions is comparable, that suggests that the diffusion coefficient in the evolving Glasma fields is in agreement with the perturbative calculations. On the other hand, for large $Q_s$ the $p_T$ broadening in the Langevin motion is smaller than that in the Glasma fields. This difference can be related to the fact that heavy quarks in the latter system experience diffusion in strong, coherent gluon fields that leads to a faster momentum broadening due to memory, or equivalently to a strong correlation in the transverse plane.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for small values of the saturation scale Q_s, the average transverse momentum broadening of heavy quarks in the evolving Glasma fields is comparable to that in a standard Markovian-Brownian motion described by Langevin equations with perturbative QCD diffusion coefficients, but for large Q_s the Langevin motion underestimates the broadening because heavy quarks in the Glasma experience diffusion in strong, coherent gluon fields with memory effects.

## Method

The authors simulated the diffusion of charm and beauty quarks in evolving Glasma fields by solving classical Yang-Mills equations for the gluon fields with Glasma initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, coupled to Wong equations for the heavy quark motion. They compared this to Langevin dynamics simulations in a thermalized gluon medium with the same energy density as the Glasma fields, using perturbative QCD diffusion coefficients computed from heavy quark-gluon scattering amplitudes with Debye screening. The comparison was performed in both static box geometry and with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks with initial p_T = 0.5 GeV and Q_s = 3 GeV, the transverse momentum broadening sigma_p in the evolving Glasma shows a t^2 growth in the very early stage (below approximately 0.1 fm/c) before transitioning to linear growth
- The memory time in the evolving Glasma fields is tau_mem approximately 0.1 fm/c, estimated from the decay of the electric field correlator as tau_mem approximately 1/Q_s
- The duration of the pre-hydrodynamic stage is approximately 1 fm/c for AA collisions at RHIC energy and approximately 0.3 fm/c for AA collisions at LHC
- For Q_s = 2 GeV in the longitudinally expanding system, the energy density decays as tau^{-1} after a transient around Q_s t approximately 1 (t approximately 0.1 fm/c)
- The time-averaged sigma_p in the expanding box is lower by a factor of approximately 2 to 5 compared to the static box case

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ruggieri (2018), Sun (2019), Liu (2019), and Boguslavski (2020) that established memory effects make heavy quark diffusion in the early stage qualitatively different from Brownian motion. The paper provides a systematic comparison between evolving Glasma diffusion and Langevin dynamics with pQCD coefficients, showing that for small Q_s the diffusion coefficients agree but for large Q_s the pQCD Langevin approach underestimates momentum broadening. This enables future work on how the early-stage diffusion affects observables such as the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow of heavy-flavor hadrons.
