# Constraining Gluon Shadowing Using Photoproduction in Ultraperipheral pA and AA Collisions

> Photoproduction of heavy quarks and exclusive production of vector mesons in ultraperipheral proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions depend significantly on nuclear gluon distributions. In the present study we investigate quantitatively the extent of the applicability of these processes at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) in constraining the shadowing component of nuclear gluon modifications.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively investigates the extent to which photoproduction of heavy quarks and exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at the LHC can constrain the shadowing component of nuclear gluon modifications, finding that the sensitivity is generally small for heavy quark production but significantly larger for exclusive vector meson production due to the quadratic dependence on the gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors calculate cross sections and rapidity distributions for inclusive photoproduction of charm and bottom quark pairs (including both direct photon-gluon fusion and resolved contributions) and for exclusive J/Psi and Upsilon(1s) vector meson production in ultraperipheral pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=8.8 TeV and PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV. They convolute equivalent photon fluxes from the nuclei and protons with photoproduction cross sections computed using leading-order perturbative QCD, employing three nuclear gluon modification sets (EPS08, EPS09, HKN07) and three photon parton distribution sets (GRV, SaS1d, CJK2) to assess the sensitivity to different shadowing strengths.

## Key numbers

- For c-cbar photoproduction in pPb collisions, the direct gamma-p cross section with MSTW08 is 5570 microbarns; the direct gamma-Pb cross section is 607 microbarns with MSTW08, 471 microbarns with EPS09, and 376 microbarns with EPS08
- The resolved contribution to c-cbar production in pPb is approximately 12% of the total for SaS1d photon PDFs, 18% for GRV, and 21% for CJK2
- The gamma-Pb contribution to total c-cbar cross section in pPb is approximately 10.6% for MSTW08, 8.8% for EPS09, and 7.4% for EPS08
- The no-modification (MSTW08) total cross section for c-cbar in pPb is reduced by approximately 4% by EPS08 modifications and 2% by EPS09 modifications
- For the rapidity asymmetry ratio in pPb at y=-3,3, the MSTW08 ratio is reduced by about 9% by EPS08 and 5% by EPS09; at y=-4,4 the reductions are 15% and 10%; at y=-5,5 the reductions are 23% and 14%
- The Lorentz factors at the LHC are gamma_L = 7455 for pp, 4690 for pPb, and 2930 for PbPb collisions
- The slope parameter for the proton form factor in vector meson production is b = 4.5 GeV^{-2}
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb-208 are: central density rho_0 = 0.16/fm^3, radius R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm, skin depth d = 0.549 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of lead-lead peripheral collisions (Adeluyi and Bertulani, 2011) by including both direct and resolved photoproduction processes and considering proton-lead collisions in addition to lead-lead collisions, as well as exclusive vector meson production in pPb. The approach builds on the original proposal by Goncalves and collaborators (2001) to use ultraperipheral collisions to probe nuclear gluon distributions, and the results demonstrate that while heavy quark photoproduction shows limited sensitivity to gluon shadowing, exclusive vector meson production provides a much more sensitive probe due to the quadratic dependence of the cross section on the gluon distribution, enabling future constraints on nuclear gluon modifications from LHC data.
