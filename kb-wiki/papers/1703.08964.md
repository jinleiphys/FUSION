# Variational path-integral approach to back-reactions of composite mesons in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> For the investigation of back-reactions of composite mesons in the NJL model, a variational path-integral treatment is formulated which yields an effective action $\mathscr{A}_{\rm eff}[D_σ,D_π; S]$, depending on the propagators $D_σ$, $D_π$ of $σ-$ and $π-$mesons and on the full quark propagator $S$. The stationarity conditions $δ\mathscr{A}_{\rm eff}/ δS = 0$, $δ\mathscr{A}_{\rm eff}/ δD_σ= 0$, $δ\mathscr{A}_{\rm eff}/ δD_π= 0$, then lead to coupled Schwinger-Dyson (SD) equations for the quark self-energy and the meson polarization functions. These results reproduce and extend results of the so-called "$Φ-$derivable" approach and provide a functional formulation for diagrammatic resummations of $1/N_c-$corrections in the NJL model. Finally, we perform a low-momentum estimate of the quark and meson loop contributions to the polarization function of the pion and on this basis discuss the Goldstone theorem.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a variational path-integral treatment of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that yields an effective action depending on quark and meson propagators, whose stationarity conditions produce coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for the quark self-energy and meson polarization functions. The approach reproduces and extends the so-called "Phi-derivable" approach, but the actual derivation requires a truncation prescription that discards all 2P-reducible ring diagrams with higher powers of the meson-exchange self-energy to avoid identical cancellations, limiting the explicit results to subleading order in 1/N_c.

## Method

The authors start from the NJL Lagrangian with a local four-fermion interaction and introduce composite meson fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They then add and subtract a meson-exchange quark self-energy term, perform the path integral over quark fields using a truncated vertex expansion that keeps only the linear term in the meson-exchange self-energy, and finally integrate over meson fields using a cumulant expansion. The stationarity conditions of the resulting effective action with respect to variations of the quark propagator and meson propagators yield coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for the quark self-energy (including Hartree and meson-exchange contributions) and for the meson polarization functions, which are then evaluated in a low-momentum (pole) approximation.

## Key numbers

* The Hartree mass (tadpole) contribution to the quark self-energy is of order O(1) in the 1/N_c expansion, while the meson-exchange contribution Sigma_M is of order O(1/N_c).
* The meson propagator D_M carries a factor 1/N_c, D_M = O(1/N_c), and a closed quark loop contributes a color factor N_c.
* The leading quark-loop diagram for the effective action is of order O(N_c), the next diagram (quark loop with one meson propagator) is of order O(1), and diagrams with two crossed meson propagators or two quark loops connected by three meson propagators are of subleading order O(1/N_c).
* The pion correction to the quark self-energy carries an isospin multiplicity factor of 3, dominating over the sigma correction.
* The meson-quark coupling constant g^2_{Mqq} is of order O(1/N_c), as shown explicitly by the expression g^2_{Mqq} = [-2i N_f N_c I_2(m_M^2; m_tilde, m_tilde)]^{-1}.
* The pion mass is given by m^2_pi = (N_c/(2G_r)) g^2_{pi qq} (m_{0r}/m_tilde) + delta m^2_pi, and the sigma mass by m^2_sigma = 4 m_tilde^2 + (N_c/(2G_r)) g^2_{sigma qq} (m_{0r}/m_tilde) + delta m^2_sigma.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic treatment of 1/N_c corrections to the NJL model previously considered by Refs. [5,6,10] (Klevansky and Lemmer, 1989; Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994; and others), providing a functional foundation for those approaches. The variational path-integral method reproduces and partly enlarges the results of the "Phi-derivable" approximation developed by Luttinger and Ward (1960), Baym (1962), and others. The paper enables a self-consistent treatment of back-reactions from composite mesons on quark propagation, including momentum-dependent contributions to the quark self-energy, and provides a framework for discussing the Goldstone theorem and chiral symmetry relations in the presence of 1/N_c corrections.
