# Neutron removal cross section as a measure of neutron skin

> We study the relation between neutron removal cross section ($σ_{-N}$) and neutron skin thickness for finite neutron rich nuclei using the statistical abrasion ablation (SAA) model. Different sizes of neutron skin are obtained by adjusting the diffuseness parameter of neutrons in the Fermi distribution. It is demonstrated that there is a good linear correlation between $σ_{-N}$ and the neutron skin thickness for neutron rich nuclei. Further analysis suggests that the relative increase of neutron removal cross section could be used as a quantitative measure for the neutron skin thickness in neutron rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a strong linear correlation between neutron removal cross section ($\sigma_{-N}$) and neutron skin thickness ($S_n$) for neutron-rich nuclei using the statistical abrasion ablation (SAA) model, but the authors note that the Fermi distribution used may not describe nuclear densities in the presence of Pygmy Dipole Resonance, and further investigation on the effect of density shape is needed.

## Method

The authors used the statistical abrasion ablation (SAA) model, which describes heavy-ion collisions as a two-stage process of abrasion (nucleon-nucleon collisions in the overlap zone) followed by evaporation (deexcitation via statistical emission of light particles). They varied the neutron skin thickness from 0.0 fm to 0.5 fm in steps of 0.1 fm by adjusting the neutron diffuseness parameter in the Fermi distribution for the projectile nuclei. Calculations were performed for isotopes of Na, P, Ca, and Ni on a $^{12}$C target at 1$A$ GeV, and the correlation between $\sigma_{-N}$ and $S_n$ was analyzed.

## Key numbers

- Six values of neutron skin thickness $S_n$ were chosen: 0.0 fm to 0.5 fm with step of 0.1 fm.
- For one isotope with the same neutron skin thickness below 0.4 fm, the mean uncertainty of the difference factor $d$ is less than $\pm1.2\%$.
- The fitted function for the slope dependence on charge number $Z$ is $f(Z) = 0.5848 + 0.04554Z - 5.356\times10^{-4}Z^2$, with reduced $\chi^2 = 7.6\times10^{-6}$.
- A 5% error in $\sigma_{-N}$ corresponds to around 0.05 fm error in $S_n$ for Na isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mach (2008) who observed a strong linear correlation between neutron removal cross section and neutron skin thickness, by using the SAA model to propose a quantitative observable ($\bar{d}_{eff}$) for extracting $S_n$ experimentally. The method offers an alternative to parity-violating electron scattering proposed at JLab (Horowitz, 2001) and to proton-nucleus elastic scattering and antiprotonic atom data methods (Karataglidis, 2002; Clark, 2003; Brown, 2007; Trzcinska, 2001). The authors suggest that measuring $\sigma_{-N}$ could provide smaller uncertainties than the interaction cross section method of Suzuki (1995), which gave $S_n$ errors of $\pm0.06$ to $\pm0.3$ fm for Na isotopes.
