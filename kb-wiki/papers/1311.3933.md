# Two-Baryon Correlation Functions in 2-flavour QCD

> We present first results for two-baryon correlation functions, computed using $N_f=2$ flavours of O($a$) improved Wilson quarks, with the aim of explaining potential dibaryon bound states, specifically the H-dibaryon. In particular, we use a GEVP to isolate the groundstate using two-baryon (hyperon-hyperon) correlation functions $\big(\langle C_{XY}(t)C_{XY}(0) \rangle$, where $XY=ΛΛ, ΣΣ, NΞ, \cdots\big)$, each of which has an overlap with the H-dibaryon. We employ a `blocking' algorithm to handle the large number of contractions, which may easily be extended to N-baryon correlation functions. We also comment on its application to the analysis of single baryon masses ($n$, $Λ$, $Ξ$, $\cdots$). This study is performed on an isotropic lattice with $m_π= 460$ MeV, $m_πL = 4.7$ and $a = 0.063$ fm.

# Two-Baryon Correlation Functions in 2-flavour QCD

## Key claim
The authors present first results for two-baryon correlation functions computed using two flavours of O(a) improved Wilson quarks, but at the current level of statistics their analysis does not clearly indicate whether the dibaryon channels studied are bound or unbound; a definitive conclusion on the H-dibaryon ground state requires further statistical improvement.

## Method
The authors compute six hyperon-hyperon correlation functions (ΛΛ-ΛΛ, ΣΣ-ΣΣ, NΞ-NΞ, ΛΛ-ΣΣ, ΛΛ-NΞ, ΣΣ-NΞ) on an isotropic lattice using a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) to isolate the ground state. A blocking algorithm pre-contracts baryons on the source side to handle the factorial growth of contractions, and Gaussian smearing with APE smeared links is applied at both source and sink to improve operator overlap.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.063 fm
- Pion mass: mπ = 451 MeV
- Spatial extent: mπL = 4.7
- Lattice volume: 64 × 32³
- Number of independent gauge configurations: 900
- Number of source positions per configuration: 16
- Total measurements: 14400
- Statistical error on single baryon fitted masses: ~0.5%
- Effective mass error in ΛΛ-ΛΛ channel decreases from σ₂ ≈ 0.060 to σ₁₆ ≈ 0.022 as sources increase from 2 to 16

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of the H-dibaryon, a flavour singlet dibaryon state predicted by Jaffe, moving away from the SU(3) flavour limit where previous dynamical calculations by NPLQCD, HAL QCD Collaboration, and others reported hints of such states. The blocking algorithm generalizes approaches by Endres and Det, and can be extended to multi-baryon systems with mass number A > 2, enabling future computation of light nuclei. The authors plan to extend the GEVP analysis by adding smeared-local and local-local operators to expand the correlation matrix from 3×3 to 6×6 entries.
