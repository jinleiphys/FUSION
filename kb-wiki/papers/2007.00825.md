# Energy scan/dependence of kinetic freeze-out scenarios of multi-strange and other identified particles in central nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The transverse momentum (mass) spectra of the multi-strange and non-multi-strange (i.e. other identified) particles in central gold-gold (Au-Au), lead-lead (Pb-Pb), argon-muriate (Ar-KCl) and nickel-nickel (Ni-Ni) collisions over a wide energy range have been studied in this work. The experimental data measured by various collaborations have been analyzed. The blast-wave fit with Tsallis statistics is used to extract the kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity from the experimental data of transverse momentum (mass) spectra. The extracted parameters increase with the increase of collision energy and appear with the trend of saturation at the Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). This saturation implies that the onset energy of phase transition of partial deconfinement is 7.7 GeV and that of whole deconfinement is 39 GeV. Furthermore, the energy scan/dependence of kinetic freeze-out scenarios are observed for the multi-strange and other identified particles, though the multiple freeze-out scenarios are also observed for various particles.

## Key claim

The saturation of kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity at Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies implies that the onset energy for partial deconfinement is 7.7 GeV and for whole deconfinement is 39 GeV, though these values are inferred from the trend of extracted parameters rather than directly measured.

## Method

The authors performed blast-wave fits with Tsallis statistics to transverse momentum (or transverse mass) spectra of pions, kaons, protons, kaon-short, lambda, phi, cascade, and omega particles produced at mid-rapidity in central Au-Au, Pb-Pb, Ar-KCl, and Ni-Ni collisions. The kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity were extracted simultaneously from the fits across a collision energy range from 2.25 GeV to 2.76 TeV, using experimental data from the E866, E895, E802, STAR, PHENIX, HADES, CMS, and ALICE collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature for pions in central Au-Au collisions increases from 0.024 +/- 0.005 GeV at 2.7 GeV to 0.089 +/- 0.005 GeV at 200 GeV, and reaches 0.094 +/- 0.006 GeV in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV
- Transverse flow velocity for pions in central Au-Au collisions increases from 0.339 +/- 0.009 c at 2.7 GeV to 0.489 +/- 0.009 c at 200 GeV, and reaches 0.500 +/- 0.011 c in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV
- For omega particles in central Au-Au collisions, T0 is 0.125 +/- 0.005 GeV at 7.7 GeV and 0.149 +/- 0.007 GeV at 200 GeV, with beta_T of 0.243 +/- 0.012 c at 7.7 GeV and 0.271 +/- 0.014 c at 200 GeV
- For phi mesons in central Au-Au collisions, T0 saturates around 0.108-0.109 GeV from 7.7 to 39 GeV, then increases to 0.133 +/- 0.006 GeV at 200 GeV
- For protons in central Au-Au collisions, T0 saturates at 0.075-0.076 GeV from 7.7 to 39 GeV, then increases to 0.091 +/- 0.004 GeV at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kinetic freeze-out parameters by Waqas and Liu (previous works) using the blast-wave model with Tsallis statistics across a wider energy range and particle species. The results support the existence of multiple freeze-out scenarios, where different particle species (multi-strange versus non-multi-strange) require different freeze-out parameters, contradicting the single freeze-out scenario proposed in earlier literature. The extracted onset energies for deconfinement (7.7 GeV for partial, 39 GeV for whole) provide specific targets for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program to investigate the QCD phase transition region.
