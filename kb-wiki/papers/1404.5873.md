# Pion structure in the nuclear medium

> Using the light-front pion wave function based on a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model, we study the properties of the pion in symmetric nuclear matter. The pion model we adopt is well constrained by previous studies to explain the pion properties in vacuum. In order to consistently incorporate the constituent up and down quarks of the pion immersed in symmetric nuclear matter, we use the quark-meson coupling model, which has been widely applied to various hadronic and nuclear phenomena in a nuclear medium with success. We predict the in-medium modifications of the pion lectromagnetic form factor, charge radius and weak decay constant in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Using a light-front pion wave function derived from a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model combined with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for symmetric nuclear matter, the authors predict in-medium modifications of the pion electromagnetic form factor, charge radius, and weak decay constant, under the approximation that the pion mass remains unchanged up to normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors model the pion in vacuum using a symmetric light-front vertex function and constituent quark masses (220 MeV), then embed the pion in symmetric nuclear matter described by the QMC model, which self-consistently couples scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mean fields to the confined valence quarks of the nucleons. The in-medium quark propagator and pion vertex are modified by the scalar potential (effective quark mass shift) and vector potential (momentum shift), though the vector potential cancels in the loop integral after a variable transformation. The pion electromagnetic form factor is computed from the plus component of the current in the Breit frame with the Drell-Yan condition (q⁺=0), using the symmetric vertex function and the in-medium constituent quark masses obtained from the QMC model.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ (k_F⁰ = 1.305 fm⁻¹), with binding energy fitted to -15.7 MeV per nucleon.
* Nuclear incompressibility K = 320.9 MeV for the model with vacuum quark mass m_q = 220 MeV (compared to K = 279.3 MeV for m_q = 5 MeV).
* Effective nucleon mass at saturation density: m_N* = 698.6 MeV for m_q = 220 MeV (compared to 754.6 MeV for m_q = 5 MeV).
* The pion mass modification at ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is estimated from prior work as δm_π ≈ +3 MeV, justifying the approximation m_π* ≈ m_π up to ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends the light-front pion model of de Melo et al. (Pacheco1, 2004) from vacuum to symmetric nuclear matter, using the QMC model originally introduced by Guichon (1988) and Frederico et al. (1989) to treat nuclear matter at the quark level. It contrasts with prior hadronic-level studies such as Roy (2005), which examined in-medium pion form factors via ρ-ω mixing, and with NJL model studies by Hatsuda and Kunihiro, Vogl, and Caldas that addressed pion mass and decay constant in medium. The results enable future comparisons with experimental searches for partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter and provide a quark-level baseline for in-medium pion structure that can be extended to other mesons or finite nuclei.
