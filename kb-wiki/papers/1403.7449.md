# A rational approach to $Î·$ and $Î·^\prime$ transition form factors

> The $Î·$ and $Î·^\prime$ transition form factors in the space-like region are analyzed at low and intermediate energies in a model-independent way through the use of rational approximants. The slope and curvature parameters of the form factors as well as their values at infinity are extracted from experimental data. The impact of these results on the mixing parameters of the $Î·$-$Î·^\prime$ system are also discussed.

# A rational approach to $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ transition form factors

## Key claim
The slope and curvature parameters of the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ transition form factors can be extracted from experimental data in a model-independent way using sequences of rational approximants (Pade Approximants), provided that systematic errors are conservatively estimated by comparing different approximant sequences against known model predictions.

## Method
The author fits experimental data from CELLO, CLEO, BABAR, and L3 collaborations for the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ transition form factors in the space-like region using two types of Pade Approximant sequences: single-pole approximants $P^N_1(Q^2)$ and diagonal approximants $P^N_N(Q^2)$. The fits impose the condition that the form factor is non-singular at $Q^2=0$ and fix the normalizations to the experimental two-photon partial decay widths. Systematic errors are estimated as the worst relative error between exact model predictions and the Pade Approximant predictions across several models and sequences.

## Key numbers
- $\eta$ slope parameter: $b_\eta = 0.60(6)_{\text{stat}}(3)_{\text{sys}}$
- $\eta$ curvature parameter: $c_\eta = 0.37(10)_{\text{stat}}(7)_{\text{sys}}$
- $\eta^\prime$ slope parameter: $b_{\eta^\prime} = 1.30(15)_{\text{stat}}(7)_{\text{sys}}$
- $\eta^\prime$ curvature parameter: $c_{\eta^\prime} = 1.72(47)_{\text{stat}}(34)_{\text{sys}}$
- $\eta$ two-photon partial width: $\Gamma_{\eta\to\gamma\gamma} = 0.516(18)$ keV (combining PDG average and KLOE-2 result)
- $\eta^\prime$ two-photon partial width: $\Gamma_{\eta^\prime\to\gamma\gamma} = 4.35(14)$ keV (from PDG fit)
- Normalization values: $|F_{\eta\gamma\gamma}(0)| = 0.274(5)$ GeV$^{-1}$, $|F_{\eta^\prime\gamma\gamma}(0)| = 0.344(6)$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Asymptotic value for $\eta$: $\lim_{Q^2\to\infty} Q^2 F_{\eta\gamma^\ast\gamma}(Q^2) = 0.160(24)$ GeV
- Mixing parameters: $F_q/F_\pi = 1.06(1)$, $F_s/F_\pi = 1.56(24)$, $\phi = 40.3(1.8)^\circ$, with $F_\pi = 92.21(14)$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the model-independent Pade Approximant approach previously applied to the $\pi^0$ transition form factor by Masjuan (2012) to the $\eta$ and $\eta^\prime$ cases. It provides a systematic method that bridges the low-energy region described by Chiral Perturbation Theory (Ametller, 1991) and the high-energy region described by perturbative QCD (Lepage and Brodsky, 1980), while also enabling reliable extraction of the $\eta$-$\eta^\prime$ mixing parameters in the quark-flavour basis. The mixing parameters obtained agree impressively with those from a separate analysis of radiative decays by Escribano (2005), despite using only transition form factor information.
