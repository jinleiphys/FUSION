# Study of $β^+$/EC-decay properties of $sd$ shell nuclei using nuclear shell model

> Our study employs the nuclear shell model to systematically compute the half-lives of $β$ -decay for nuclei in the mass range of $A = 18-39$, encompassing the majority of $sd$ shell nuclei. This analysis utilizes the USDB and SDNN Hamiltonians. The theoretical outcomes contain calculations of various parameters such as $Q$ -values, half-lives, excitation energy, log$ft$ values, and branching ratios. We explore these results with axial-vector coupling constant for weak interactions, denoted as $g_A$$(= 1.27)$, and $κ$ value $(= 6289)$. We perform calculations of Gamow Teller matrix elements for 116 decay processes to calculate the quenching factor; we found a quenching factor of $q = 0.794\pm0.05 $ for the USDB interaction and $q = 0.815\pm0.04 $ for the SDNN interaction. We have also calculated superallowed transitions $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ for seven nuclei. Further, we have also included the electron capture phase space factor for the required nuclei to calculate the half-lives. This inclusion leads to small contribution in results, particularly for nuclei where electron capture (EC) plays a significant role. The overall results are in agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The nuclear shell model, using the USDB and SDNN Hamiltonians, can reproduce experimental beta-decay half-lives for sd-shell nuclei (A=18-39) when a quenching factor is applied to the Gamow-Teller operator, with the overall results showing agreement with experimental data, though the quenching factor differs between the two interactions (q = 0.794±0.05 for USDB and q = 0.815±0.04 for SDNN).

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations using the NuShellX@MSU code within the sd model space, employing the USDB and SDNN effective interactions. They computed Gamow-Teller matrix elements for 116 decay processes to determine the quenching factor by averaging the ratios between experimental and theoretical R(GT) values, then calculated half-lives, Q-values, excitation energies, logft values, and branching ratios for 37 proton-rich and N=Z nuclei, including electron capture phase space factors where relevant.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor q = 0.794 ± 0.05 for the USDB interaction, determined from 116 decay processes
- Quenching factor q = 0.815 ± 0.04 for the SDNN interaction, determined from 116 decay processes
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.27 and κ = 6289 s used in the calculations
- Superallowed 0+ → 0+ transitions calculated for seven nuclei
- Mass range A = 18-39 covered, encompassing the majority of sd shell nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of beta-decay by Kumar, Srivastava, and collaborators (2016, 2022, 2023) for fp and fpg shell nuclei to the sd shell region, using the USDB Hamiltonian from Brown and Richter (2016) and the SDNN Hamiltonian from Akkoyun (2021). The results enable improved predictions for beta-decay properties of proton-rich sd-shell nuclei relevant to the rp-process and nuclear astrophysics, and provide systematic quenching factors for future shell model calculations in this mass region.
