# Neutron Lifetime Discrepancy as a Sign of a Dark Sector?

> We summarize our recent proposal of explaining the discrepancy between the bottle and beam measurements of the neutron lifetime through the existence of a dark sector, which the neutron can decay to with a branching fraction 1%. We show that viable particle physics models for such neutron dark decays can be constructed and we briefly comment on recent developments in this area.

## Key claim

The 4.0 sigma discrepancy between bottle-measured (879.6 s) and beam-measured (888.0 s) neutron lifetimes could be explained if the neutron decays to a dark sector with a branching fraction of approximately 1%, provided the dark decay products have a total mass between 937.900 MeV and 939.565 MeV to satisfy nuclear stability constraints.

## Method

The authors construct effective field theories and two explicit renormalizable particle physics models for neutron dark decays. Model 1 introduces a Dirac fermion chi and a colored scalar Phi to mediate n -> chi + gamma, while Model 2 adds an intermediate Dirac fermion and a dark scalar phi to mediate n -> chi + phi. The models are matched to effective Lagrangians to compute dark decay rates, and parameter values are chosen to yield the required 1% branching fraction while avoiding collider and nuclear stability constraints.

## Key numbers

- Bottle experiment average neutron lifetime: 879.6 +/- 0.6 s (PDG average of five experiments)
- Beam experiment average neutron lifetime: 888.0 +/- 2.0 s (PDG average of two experiments)
- Tension between bottle and beam results: 4.0 sigma
- Required branching fraction for neutron dark decays: approximately 0.99 for Standard Model decays, 1% for dark decays
- Allowed dark particle mass window from Be-9 stability: 937.900 MeV < M_f < 939.565 MeV
- For n -> chi + gamma with chi as dark matter, allowed photon energy range: 0.782 MeV < E_gamma < 1.664 MeV
- For Model 1 with m_chi = 937.9 MeV, required parameter relation: M_Phi / sqrt(|lambda_q lambda_chi|) approximately 400 TeV
- For Model 2 with m_chi = 937.9 MeV, m_phi approximately 0, m_tildechi = 2 m_n, required parameter relation: M_Phi / sqrt(|lambda_q lambda_tildechi lambda_phi|) approximately 300 TeV
- Bound on neutron invisible decays from SNO and KamLAND: tau_n > 5.8 x 10^29 years

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Fornal and Grinstein (2018) to explain the neutron lifetime discrepancy first noted between bottle experiments (Mampe, Serebrov 2004, Pichlmaier 2010, Steyerl 2012, Arzumanov 2015) and beam experiments (Byrne 1996, Yue 2013). The paper enables experimental searches for monochromatic photons from n -> chi gamma (performed by Tang 2018), searches for n -> chi e+e- (performed by Sun 2018), and nuclear dark decay searches in Be-11 at CERN-ISOLDE. It also inspired theoretical work on neutron star stability (McKeen 2018, Baym 2018, Motta 2018), self-interacting dark matter models (Cline 2018, Karananas 2018), and low-scale baryogenesis (Bringmann 2018, Elor 2018). Alternative explanations for the discrepancy include neutron-mirror neutron oscillations (Berezhiani 2018) and a large Fierz interference term (Ivanov 2018).
