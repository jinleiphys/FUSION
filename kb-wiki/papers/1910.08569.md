# Renormalization and Matching for the Collins-Soper Kernel from Lattice QCD

> The Collins-Soper kernel, which governs the energy evolution of transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs), is required to accurately predict Drell-Yan like processes at small transverse momentum, and is a key ingredient for extracting TMDPDFs from experiment. Earlier we proposed a method to calculate this kernel from ratios of the so-called quasi-TMDPDFs determined with lattice QCD, which are defined as hadronic matrix elements of staple-shaped Euclidean Wilson line operators. Here we provide the one-loop renormalization of these operators in a regularization-independent momentum subtraction (RI$^\prime$/MOM) scheme, as well as the conversion factor from the RI$^\prime$/MOM-renormalized quasi-TMDPDF to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme. We also propose a procedure for calculating the Collins-Soper kernel directly from position space correlators, which simplifies the lattice determination.

## Key claim

The Collins-Soper kernel can be extracted from lattice QCD via ratios of quasi-TMDPDFs at two different proton momenta, provided the quasi-TMDPDFs are renormalized in a regularization-independent momentum subtraction (RI'/MOM) scheme and then converted to the MS scheme; the method works even though a fully perturbative matching between quasi-TMDPDFs and TMDPDFs has not been proven to all orders, because the nonperturbative soft factor cancels in the ratio.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop renormalization of staple-shaped Euclidean Wilson line operators (quasi-TMDPDFs) in the RI'/MOM scheme, and derive the conversion factor from RI'/MOM to the MS scheme using continuum perturbation theory in d dimensions. They also propose a new procedure to determine the Collins-Soper kernel directly from position-space correlators, avoiding the Fourier transform that would require a finite number of lattice b^z values, by working with the position-space quasi-TMDPDF \(\tilde f_\ns(b^z, \bt, \mu, P^z)\) and its ratio at two different proton momenta.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop matching coefficient \(C_\ns(\mu, x P^z)\) for the nonsinglet channel is given by \(C_\ns(\mu, x P^z) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi} \left[ -\ln^2\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} + 2 \ln\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} -4 + \frac{\pi^2}{6} \right] + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\).
- The Collins-Soper kernel extraction formula uses \(\gamma^q_\zeta(\mu, b_T) = \frac{1}{\ln(P^z_1/P^z_2)} \ln \frac{C_\ns(\mu,x P_2^z)\, \tilde f_\ns(x, \bt, \mu, P^z_1)}{C_\ns(\mu,x P_1^z)\, \tilde f_\ns(x, \bt, \mu, P^z_2)}\), where \(P^z_1 \ne P^z_2\) are two different proton momenta.
- The Wilson line self-energies yield an overall power-law divergence \(e^{\delta m (L_{\rm tot}^{\rm B} - \frac12 L_{\rm tot}^{\rm S})} = e^{-\delta m b^z}\) for \(b^z < L\), which must be absorbed by the UV renormalization factor.

## Context

This work extends the LaMET-based method for computing the Collins-Soper kernel from lattice QCD proposed by Ebert, Stewart, and Zhao (2018) and Ebert, Stewart, and Zhao (2019) by providing the necessary one-loop renormalization and scheme conversion factors. It enables a first-principles lattice determination of the nonperturbative part of the Collins-Soper kernel, which is currently extracted from global fits with significant discrepancies between groups such as Scimemi and Vladimirov (2017) and Bacchetta et al. (2017). The position-space method proposed here simplifies the lattice analysis by avoiding the inverse problem of Fourier transforming a finite set of b^z values.
