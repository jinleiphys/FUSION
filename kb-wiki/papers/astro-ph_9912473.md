# Constraining Antimatter Domains in the Early Universe with Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We consider the effect of a small-scale matter-antimatter domain structure on big bang nucleosynthesis and place upper limits on the amount of antimatter in the early universe. For small domains, which annihilate before nucleosynthesis, this limit comes from underproduction of He-4. For larger domains, the limit comes from He-3 overproduction. Most of the He-3 from antiproton-helium annihilation is annihilated also. The main source of He-3 is photodisintegration of He-4 by the electromagnetic cascades initiated by the annihilation.

## Key claim
The paper places upper limits on the amount of antimatter in the early universe from big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN), but the limits depend critically on the size of the antimatter domains: for small domains (annihilating before nucleosynthesis) the constraint comes from underproduction of He-4, while for larger domains (annihilating during or after nucleosynthesis) the constraint comes from overproduction of He-3 via photodisintegration of He-4 by electromagnetic cascades, not from direct antiproton-helium annihilation products.

## Method
The authors performed numerical computations of inhomogeneous BBN including nuclear reactions, diffusion, hydrodynamic expansion, annihilation, spreading of annihilation products, photodisintegration of He-4, and disintegration by fast neutrons. They modeled antimatter domains as spherical regions of diameter 2r separated by average distance 2L, with equal homogeneous initial densities for matter and antimatter regions, using a baryon-to-photon ratio eta = 6x10^-10 near the upper end of the standard BBN range. The code did not incorporate antinucleosynthesis due to lack of data on annihilation yields between nuclei and antinuclei, which could affect results at the 10% level.

## Key numbers
- For domain radii r between 10^5 m and 10^7 m (comoving at T = 1 keV), the amount of antimatter can be at most a few per cent to avoid He-4 underproduction (Y_p must not be lower than 0.22).
- For larger domains, the limit reaches R = 2x10^-4 (antimatter/matter ratio) at r ~ 10^9 m, from He-3 overproduction (He-3/H must not exceed 10^-4.5).
- Standard BBN yields for eta = 6x10^-10 are Y_p = 0.248 and He-3/H = 1.1x10^-5.
- Domains smaller than 100 m at 1 MeV (2x10^-5 pc today) would annihilate well before nucleosynthesis and leave no observable remnant.
- For domains smaller than r = 10^5 m, there is no BBN constraint on antimatter.
- The CMB spectral distortion constraint limits energy release after T = 1 keV to below 6x10^-5 of the CMB energy.
- In antiproton-helium annihilation, a He-3 nucleus is produced in 21.0 +/- 0.9% of cases, and a tritium nucleus in 43.7 +/- 3.2% of cases.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of antimatter and BBN, particularly the work of Rehm and Jedamzik (1998) who considered annihilation immediately before nucleosynthesis at temperatures 80 keV to 1 MeV and found limits of R < few per cent for domain sizes r_A ~ 1 cm at T = 100 GeV. The authors extend these results to larger domain sizes where annihilation occurs during or after nucleosynthesis, and find that photodisintegration of He-4 by cascade photons is the dominant He-3 production mechanism, not direct antiproton-helium annihilation as considered in earlier work by other authors. The results provide BBN-based upper limits on antimatter that are stronger than CMB spectral distortion limits for domain sizes up to r ~ 10^11 m (10 parsecs today), and enable future work on the cascade spectrum at temperatures below 100 eV to refine constraints at the largest scales.
