# Microscopic Description of Nuclear Fission: Fission Barrier Heights of Even-Even Actinides

> We evaluate the performance of modern nuclear energy density functionals for predicting inner and outer fission barrier heights and energies of fission isomers of even-even actinides. For isomer energies and outer barrier heights, we find that the self-consistent theory at the HFB level is capable of providing quantitative agreement with empirical data.

## Key claim

The UNEDF1 energy density functional predicts fission barrier heights and isomer energies for even-even actinides that agree well with empirical data, but the inner barrier heights are systematically overestimated by about 0.5–1.0 MeV for uranium and plutonium isotopes, and the empirical uncertainty on barrier heights is at least 0.3 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the UNEDF1 Skyrme functional and the SkM* functional as a benchmark, with a density-dependent mixed-pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel. They used the symmetry-unrestricted nuclear DFT solver HFODD with a harmonic oscillator basis of 1140 states from 31 deformed shells, and explored configurations breaking axial symmetry (for inner barriers) and reflection symmetry (for outer barriers). Results were compared to experimental data and to predictions from the Gogny D1S functional and the macroscopic-microscopic FRLDM model.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation for inner barrier height \(E_A\): UNEDF1 = 1.03 MeV, D1S = 0.709 MeV, SkM* = 1.61 MeV, FRLDM = 1.52 MeV
- RMS deviation for fission isomer energy \(E_{II}\): UNEDF1 = 0.357 MeV, D1S = 0.339 MeV, SkM* = 0.351 MeV, FRLDM = 0.675 MeV
- RMS deviation for outer barrier height \(E_B\): UNEDF1 = 0.690 MeV, D1S = 1.14 MeV, SkM* = 1.39 MeV, FRLDM = 1.13 MeV
- Empirical uncertainty on barrier heights is at least 0.3 MeV
- Quasi-particle cutoff energy used in calculations: \(E_{\rm cut} = 60\) MeV
- Inner barrier heights calculated with all microscopic models are consistently 0.5–1.0 MeV too high for U and Pu isotopes
- Zero-point energy corrections can lower inner fission barrier heights by approximately 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends systematic DFT studies of nuclear fission using the Skyrme functional (Erler et al., 2012; Staszczak et al., 2013), the Gogny force (PhysRevC.66.014310, Warda et al., 2012), and relativistic mean-field approaches (Abusara et al., 2010; Lu et al., 2012), and benchmarks the UNEDF1 functional (Kortelainen et al., 2012) against the SkM* functional (Bartel et al., 1982), the Gogny D1S interaction (Delaroche et al., 2006), and the macroscopic-microscopic FRLDM (Moller et al., 2009). The results enable future optimization of energy density functionals by considering additional fit observables such as one-neutron separation energy to better constrain fission barrier heights, and highlight the need to account for beyond-mean-field corrections and excitation energy effects when using ground-state barriers in reaction models.
