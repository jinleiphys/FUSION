# Extrapolation method in shell model calculations with deformed basis

> An extrapolation method in shell model calculations with deformed basis is presented, which uses a scaling property of energy and energy variance for a series of systematically approximated wave functions to the true one. Such approximated wave functions are given by variation-after-projection method concerning the full angular momentum projection. This extrapolation needs energy variance, which amounts to the calculation of expectation value of square of Hamiltonian $\hat{H}^2$. We present the method to evaluate this matrix element and show that large reduction of its numerical computation can be done by taking an advantage of time-reversal symmetry. The numerical tests are presented for $fp$ shell calculations with a realistic residual interaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extrapolation method for shell model calculations that uses deformed basis states (single Slater determinants with angular momentum projection) rather than spherical basis states, claiming that the exact energy can be estimated by exploiting a scaling property between energy and energy variance for a series of systematically approximated wave functions, with the hedge that the method works well for low-spin yrast states but deteriorates for higher spins.

## Method

The method constructs a series of systematically approximated wave functions by taking a variation-after-projection (VAP) wave function for the J=0 ground state and then scaling the coefficients of single-particle orbits grouped into upper and lower sets (e.g., grouping {f7/2} and {p3/2, f5/2, p1/2}) using a parameter x, where x=1 corresponds to the VAP minimum. For each value of x, the angular-momentum-projected energy and energy variance (expectation value of H^2) are computed, and the exact energy is estimated by extrapolating the energy-variance relation to zero variance. The computation of H^2 matrix elements is simplified using Wick's theorem, normal-ordered four-body operators, and time-reversal symmetry to reduce the angular momentum projection integral domain.

## Key numbers

- For 60Zn in the fp shell with FPD6 interaction: the Hartree-Fock energy is -243.03 MeV; the present extrapolation method agrees with the previous spherical-basis extrapolation method within 0.1 MeV.
- For 60Zn: the previous extrapolation method with spherical basis (Mizusaki and Imada, 2002, 2003) required four days of computation, while the new deformed-basis approach requires less than half a day.
- For 48Cr with KB3 interaction: extrapolated energies for yrast states (0+, 2+, 4+, 6+, 8+) agree with exact Lanczos diagonalization results within 0.1 MeV.
- For 60Zn: the truncated spherical shell model spaces used for comparison had dimensions of 4,794,006 (t=3), 31,077,402 (t=4), and 133,533,398 (t=5).

## Context

This work extends the scaling-property extrapolation method previously introduced by Mizusaki and Imada (2002, 2003) for spherical-basis shell model calculations, and is related to the exponential convergence method (ECM) of Horoi, Volya, and Zelevinsky (1999) and the no-core shell model extrapolation of Zhan, Nogga, Barrett, Vary, and Navratil (2004). The key advance is replacing spherical basis states with deformed basis states (single Slater determinants with angular momentum projection), which is expected to be more efficient for deformed nuclei where spherical-basis truncation converges slowly. The method enables shell model calculations for nuclei near the middle of shells (such as A~80 nuclei in the fpg shell) where the dimension of relevant spherical-basis truncation spaces becomes prohibitively large, and provides a natural connection to mean-field interpretations.
