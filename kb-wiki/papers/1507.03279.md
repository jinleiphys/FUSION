# Symmetry potential of $Δ(1232)$ resonance and its effects on the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions near the pion production threshold

> Effects of the completely unknown symmetry (isovector) potential of the \D on the total and differential \rpi in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies from 100 to 1000 MeV/A are explored within an isospin-dependent transport model IBUU. The effects are found to be negligible at beam energies above the pion production threshold due to the very short lifetimes of less than 2 fm/c for $Δ$ resonances with masses around $m_Δ=1232$ MeV, leaving the $π^-/π^+$ ratios of especially the energetic pions still a reliable probe of the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy $E_{sym}(ρ)$. However, as the beam energy becomes deeply sub-threshold for pion production, effects of the $Δ$ symmetry potential becomes appreciable especially on the \rpi of low-energy pions from the decays of low-mass $Δ$ resonances which have lived long enough to be affected by their mean-field potentials, providing a useful tool to study the symmetry potential and spectroscopy of $Δ$ resonances in neutron-rich nuclear matter. Interestingly though, even at the deeply sub-threshold beam energies, the differential \rpi of energetic pions remains sensitive to the \esym at supra saturation densities with little influence from the uncertain symmetry potential of the $Δ$ resonance.

## Key claim

The effects of the unknown symmetry (isovector) potential of the Δ(1232) resonance on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio are negligible at beam energies above the pion production threshold, leaving the ratio of energetic pions a reliable probe of the high-density symmetry energy; however, at deeply sub-threshold beam energies the Δ symmetry potential appreciably affects the ratio of low-energy pions, providing a tool to study the Δ resonance itself.

## Method

The study used the isospin-dependent transport model IBUU to simulate heavy-ion collisions at beam energies from 100 to 1000 MeV/A. The Δ symmetry potential was parameterized by multiplying a baseline prescription (derived from Clebsch-Gordon coefficients in Δ ↔ πN processes) by a variable factor fΔ, with values of 1, 3, and 6 explored. The mass-dependent lifetime of the Δ resonance was modeled via a modified Breit-Wigner distribution, and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio was tracked as a function of time and pion kinetic energy for head-on Au+Au reactions.

## Key numbers

- The Δ resonance at its centroid mass of 1232 MeV has a mean lifetime of about 1.7 fm/c.
- At a beam energy of 200 MeV/A, increasing fΔ from 1 to 6 increases the total π⁻/π⁺ ratio by about 25%.
- The effect of the Δ symmetry potential on the differential π⁻/π⁺ ratio is mainly on pions with kinetic energy below about 70 MeV.
- The maximum total multiplicity of Δ resonances in all charge states for ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn at 400 MeV/A and impact parameter 1 fm is about 4, which is about 3.2% of the total number of nucleons involved.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, first proposed by Li (2002), and addresses the previously neglected role of the Δ isovector potential. It builds on prior work by Cozma (2014), Drago (2014), and Cai (2014) who noted that essentially nothing is known about the Δ isovector potential. The results enable future experiments at RIKEN and NIRS to use the differential π⁻/π⁺ ratio of energetic pions to constrain the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, while also suggesting that low-energy pions from sub-threshold collisions could be used to study Δ spectroscopy in neutron-rich matter.
