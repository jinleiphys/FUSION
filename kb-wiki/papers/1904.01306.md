# Equation-of-state Constraints and the QCD Phase Transition in the Era of Gravitational-Wave Astronomy

> We describe a multi-messenger interpretation of GW170817, which yields a robust lower limit on NS radii. This excludes NSs with radii smaller than about 10.7 km and thus rules out very soft nuclear matter. We stress the potential of this type of constraints when future detections become available. A very similar argumentation may yield an upper bound on the maximum mass of nonrotating NSs. We also discuss simulations of NS mergers, which undergo a first-order phase transition to quark matter. We point out a different dynamical behavior. Considering the gravitational-wave signal, we identify an unambiguous signature of the QCD phase transition in NS mergers. The occurrence of quark matter through a strong first-order phase transition during merging leads to a characteristic shift of the dominant postmerger frequency. The frequency shift is indicative for a phase transition if it is compared to the postmerger frequency which is expected for purely hadronic EoS models. A very strong deviation of several 100 Hz is observed for hybrid EoSs in an otherwise tight relation between the tidal deformability and the postmerger frequency. We address the potential impact of a first-order phase transition on the electromagnetic counterpart of NS mergers. Our simulations suggest that there would be no significant qualitative differences between a system undergoing a phase transition to quark matter and purely hadronic mergers. The quantitative differences are within the spread which is found between different hadronic EoS models. This implies on the one hand that GW170817 is compatible with a possible transition to quark matter. On the other hand these considerations show that it may not be easy to identify quantitative differences between purely hadronic mergers and events in which quark matter occurs considering solely their electromagnetic counterpart or their nucleosynthesis products. (abridged)

## Key claim

The paper argues that a multi-messenger interpretation of GW170817 yields a robust lower limit on neutron star radii of about 10.7 km, ruling out very soft nuclear matter, but this constraint is not very restrictive as only the softest equations of state are excluded; future events with higher total binary masses will lead to stronger lower limits.

## Method

The authors derived a lower bound on neutron star radii by combining the measured total binary mass of GW170817 (2.74 solar masses) with the condition that the merger did not promptly form a black hole, as inferred from the observed electromagnetic counterpart. They used an empirical relation between the threshold mass for prompt black-hole formation and stellar properties (maximum mass and radius at 1.6 solar masses), combined with a causality limit on the maximum mass for a given radius. For the phase transition study, they performed numerical simulations of binary neutron star mergers with 1.35-1.35 solar mass binaries, comparing a purely hadronic relativistic mean-field model (DD2F) with seven hybrid models featuring a first-order phase transition to quark matter described by a two-flavor string-flip model with Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

* Lower limit on neutron star radius: R_1.6 > 10.7 km for a remnant lifetime of at least 10 ms, derived from GW170817 with total binary mass M_tot = 2.74 solar masses
* Lower limit on the radius of the maximum-mass configuration: R_max > 9.6 km
* Lower limit on tidal deformability: Lambda_1.4 > 200, converted from the radius constraint using a tight empirical relation between R_1.6 and Lambda_1.4
* Onset density of the phase transition in hybrid models: between 0.466 1/fm^3 and 0.580 1/fm^3 across the seven DD2F-SF-n models
* Maximum deviation from the tight Lambda-f_peak relation for purely hadronic models: about 100 Hz; for hybrid models with phase transition: up to about 0.5 kHz
* Maximum rest-mass density in the early postmerger evolution for the purely hadronic DD2F model: about 9.5 x 10^14 g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends the multi-messenger interpretation of GW170817 first presented in Bauswein (2017), providing a lower radius limit complementary to the upper limit from tidal deformability measurements by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (Abbott 2017, 2019). The phase transition signature builds on merger simulations by Bauswein (2012a, 2013a) and Hotokezaka (2013), and the identified frequency shift in the Lambda-f_peak relation provides an unambiguous observational signature of a first-order QCD phase transition that cannot be mimicked by purely hadronic models including hyperonic matter (Banik 2014, Fortin 2018) or continuous transitions (Alford 2005). Future detections with improved detector sensitivity in the kHz range will enable both stronger radius constraints from multi-messenger arguments and direct identification of phase transitions in neutron star mergers.
