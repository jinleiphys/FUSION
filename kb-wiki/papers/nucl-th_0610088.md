# Time evolution of Mach-like structure in a partonic transport model

> The time evolution of Mach-like structure (the splitting of the away side peak in di-hadron $Δϕ$ correlation) is presented in the framework of a dynamical partonic transport model. With the increasing of the lifetime of partonic matter, Mach-like structure can be produced and developed by strong parton cascade process. Not only the splitting parameter but also the number of associated hadrons ($N_{h}^{assoc}$) increases with the lifetime of partonic matter and partonic interaction cross section. Both the explosion of $N_{h}^{assoc}$ following the formation of Mach-like structure and the corresponding results of three-particle correlation support that a partonic Mach-like shock wave can be formed by strong parton cascade mechanism. Therefore, the studies about Mach-like structure may give us some critical information, such as the lifetime of partonic matter and hadronization time.

## Key claim

Mach-like structure (splitting of the away-side peak in di-hadron azimuthal correlations) can be produced and developed by a strong parton cascade process in a partonic transport model, but only when the lifetime of the partonic matter exceeds approximately 1.5 fm/c; the structure is not observed for shorter partonic lifetimes.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which includes initial conditions from HIJING, parton cascade via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) model with two-body pQCD scattering, and hadronization via quark coalescence. They artificially varied the lifetime of the partonic matter (the time after which partonic interactions cease and all remaining partons suddenly coalesce into hadrons) and studied di-hadron azimuthal correlations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, using both two-particle and three-particle correlation methods with mixing-event background subtraction.

## Key numbers

- Mach-like structure appears only for partonic lifetimes greater than approximately 1.5 fm/c; no structure is observed at 0.5 or 1.0 fm/c.
- The splitting parameter D (half-distance between the two away-side Gaussian peaks) saturates around 5 fm/c, consistent with the saturation time of parton elliptic flow and the time when energy density and d-quark temperature reach critical values.
- For 10 mb partonic cross section, the production rate of associated hadrons on the away side is 0.12 +/- 0.01 per fm/c for lifetimes up to 2 fm/c, and increases to 0.69 +/- 0.01 per fm/c for lifetimes between 2 and 6 fm/c.
- For 3 mb partonic cross section, the corresponding away-side production rates are 0.10 +/- 0.01 per fm/c (up to 2 fm/c) and 0.26 +/- 0.01 per fm/c (2 to 6 fm/c).
- The near-side production rate of associated hadrons is constant at 0.35 +/- 0.01 per fm/c (10 mb) and 0.18 +/- 0.01 per fm/c (3 mb) for all lifetimes up to 6 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies (Ma et al., 2006) that showed the splitting parameter D increases with number of participants, by explicitly demonstrating the time evolution of the Mach-like structure. It supports the interpretation that the structure arises from a partonic Mach-cone shock wave, consistent with hydrodynamical models (Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2005; Thorsten et al., 2005) but in contrast to a linearized hydrodynamical model (Chaudhuri, 2005) that found no Mach-like cone. The results suggest that studies of Mach-like structure can provide information about the lifetime of partonic matter and hadronization time in heavy-ion collisions.
