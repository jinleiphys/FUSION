# Higher order cumulants of net baryon-number distributions at non-zero $渭_B$

> Using recent results on higher order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations from lattice QCD, we construct mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis, hyper-skewness and hyper-kurtosis of net-baryon number distributions for small baryon chemical potentials $渭_B$. These cumulant ratios are of direct relevance for comparisons with corresponding ratios measured by STAR in the BES-I and II runs at beam energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\ge 20$ GeV. ## Key claim

Using lattice QCD results for higher-order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations, the authors construct net baryon-number cumulant ratios up to sixth order for small baryon chemical potentials, finding that STAR measurements of skewness and kurtosis at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 54.4\) GeV are consistent with thermal QCD predictions, but the sign change observed in experimental hyper-kurtosis data cannot be reproduced by equilibrium lattice QCD at next-to-leading order in \(\mu_B\).

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) simulations with quark masses tuned to the physical point, using rational hybrid Monte Carlo on lattices with temporal extent \(N_{\tau}=8\) and \(N_{\tau}=12\) and aspect ratio \(N_{\sigma}/N_{\tau}=4\). Generalized susceptibilities \(\chi^{BQS}_{ijk}\) up to order \(i+j+k=8\) were computed via Taylor expansion around \(\mu_B=0\), imposing strangeness neutrality (\(n_S=0\)) and fixed electric charge to baryon number density ratio (\(n_Q/n_B=0.4\)) order by order. Continuum extrapolations were obtained by jointly fitting results from different lattice spacings using a rational polynomial ansatz that includes \(1/N_{\tau}^2\) corrections.

## Key numbers

* First and second order cumulants (mean and variance) were computed to NNNLO, third and fourth order cumulants to NNLO, and fifth and sixth order cumulants to NLO in \(\mu_B/T\).
* Continuum extrapolations for the pseudo-critical transition line \(T_{pc}(\mu_B)\) were used up to baryon chemical potentials \(\mu_B\simeq 300\) MeV, as reported by HotQCD (Bazavov 2019).
* The curvature of the kurtosis ratio \(R^{B}_{42}\) was found to be about a factor three larger than that of the skewness ratio \(R^{B}_{31}\), consistent with earlier findings by HotQCD (Bazavov 2017) and remaining true after inclusion of NNLO contributions.
* The ratio of mean to variance \(R^{B}_{12}\) shows deviations from leading order linear behavior that are weaker than predicted by Hadron-Resonance-Gas (HRG) models; HRG models agree with lattice calculations up to about 125 MeV but underestimate them at larger \(\mu_B\).
* Leading order contributions to hyper-skewness \(R^{B}_{51}\) and hyper-kurtosis \(R^{B}_{62}\) are nearly identical and negative throughout the probed \(\mu_B/T\) region.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies by HotQCD (Bazavov 2017) with significantly increased statistics (factor of three to five for \(N_{\tau}=8\) lattices and factor of six to eight for \(N_{\tau}=12\) lattices near \(T_{pc}\)), enabling computation of cumulant ratios up to sixth order. The results provide first-principle thermal QCD baselines for comparison with net proton-number cumulant measurements from STAR's Beam Energy Scan (BES-I and II) at beam energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\ge 20\) GeV, and enable constraints on freeze-out parameters. The finding that the sign change in experimental hyper-kurtosis data cannot be reproduced by equilibrium QCD at NLO opens the question of whether large NNLO contributions or non-equilibrium effects are responsible.
