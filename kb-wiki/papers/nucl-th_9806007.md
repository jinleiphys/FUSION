# Liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> Within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) a quantal nuclear system with only 16 nucleons shows a clearly visible liquid-gas phase transition. The FMD model is an approximation to the many-body problem which describes the system by antisymmetrized many-body states in which each nucleon is occupying a Gaussian shaped time-dependent wave-packet. The statistical ensemble is obtained by time averaging.

## Key claim

Within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), a quantal nuclear system with only 16 nucleons shows a clearly visible liquid-gas phase transition, but the result is obtained under the condition that the system is placed in an external container potential that prevents evaporated nucleons from escaping, allowing equilibration between liquid and vapor phases.

## Method

The authors used Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) to evolve a time-dependent antisymmetrized many-body state composed of Gaussian wave packets for each nucleon, with an effective two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction adjusted to reproduce binding energies for nuclei with mass numbers 4 ≤ A ≤ 40. The nuclear system was placed in an external harmonic oscillator container potential with frequency ħω to control the accessible volume and vapor pressure. The system was excited by randomly displacing wave packets from their ground-state positions, then evolved for about 10000 fm/c for equilibration, followed by time-averaging over another 10000 fm/c interval. Temperature was determined by coupling a separate thermometer system of distinguishable particles in a harmonic oscillator potential to the nuclear system.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O at excitation energy 3.5 MeV per particle, the temperature after equilibration is about 4 MeV
- The container potential at the surface of the 20 fm cubes is only 1.2 MeV higher than in the center
- Vapor pressure estimates yield 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻² MeV/fm³, compared to a critical pressure of about 0.5 MeV/fm³
- For ħω = 1 MeV container, the coexistence plateau appears at T ≈ 5 to 6 MeV, extending from E*/A ≈ 3 MeV to about E*/A ≈ 11 MeV
- The latent heat at pressure close to zero is about 8 MeV
- For ħω = 6 MeV container, the plateau shifts to T ≈ 7 MeV and the latent heat decreases
- For ħω = 18 MeV container, the coexistence region almost vanishes and the critical temperature is reached
- The critical temperature Tc is estimated at about 10 MeV
- For ¹⁶O, the ground state energy with ħω = 6 MeV container goes up to E/A ≈ -5 MeV

## Context

This work extends mean-field calculations of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, such as those by Jaqaman et al. (1984) using Skyrme ZR3 interaction, Bonche et al. (1984) using SKM and SIII interactions, and Cao and Yang (1996) using Gogny's D1 interaction, by treating the coexistence region dynamically without Maxwell construction. The FMD results show striking similarity with the caloric curve measured by the ALADIN group (Pochodzalla et al., 1995) from spectator decay in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that excited spectator matter may equilibrate faster into the coexistence region than it expands and cools. The approach enables direct comparison between equilibrium calculations and experimental multifragmentation data, and can be extended to treat nucleus-nucleus collisions as well as equilibrium situations.
