# B(E2) strength in $^{36,38}$Ca and in mirror nuclei 36S, 38Ar

> Recently, $B(E2, 0^+_1 \to 2^+_1)$ transition strength have been measured in $^{36}$Ca and $^{38}$Ca. Surprisingly, the measured value in $^{36}$Ca: $B(E2\ \uparrow) = 131(20)$ e$^2$fm$^4$, is significantly larger than in $^{38}$Ca, where $B(E2\ \uparrow) = 101(11)$ e$^2$fm$^4$, whereas an opposite tendency of $B(E2)$ values is seen in the mirror nuclei $^{36}$S and $^{38}$Ar. The resonance $2^+_1$ in $^{36}$Ca lies 465 keV above the proton emission threshold and its description requires inclusion of the coupling to the continuum. In this work, we are analyzing $B(E2\ \uparrow)$ values in $^{36}$Ca, $^{36}$S, $^{38}$Ca, and $^{38}$Ar using the real-energy continuum shell model, the so-called shell model embedded in the continuum, in the $(1s_{1/2}\, 0d_{3/2}\, 0f_{7/2}\, 1p_{3/2})$ model space with the monopole adjusted effective interaction ZBM-IO.

## Key claim

The measured B(E2) strength in 36Ca (131 e2fm4) is larger than in 38Ca (101 e2fm4), opposite to the trend in their mirror nuclei, and the authors show using the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) that this anomaly arises from strong mirror symmetry breaking in the 36Ca/36S pair, driven by the proximity of the 2+1 state in 36Ca to the proton emission threshold and its coupling to the continuum.

## Method

The authors applied the real-energy continuum shell model, called the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC), using the ZBM-IO effective interaction in the (1s1/2 0d3/2 0f7/2 1p3/2) model space with nucleon excitations restricted to 2 particles. They adjusted the T=1 cross-shell monopole terms and the continuum coupling strength V0 to reproduce the proton (or neutron) separation energies and 2+1 excitation energies in 36Ca, 38Ca, 36S, and 38Ar, then computed the B(E2) transition probabilities and analyzed the wave function fractions and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Experimental B(E2) in 36Ca: 131 ± 20 e2fm4 (from PhysRevC.107.034306, PhysRevC.108.L061301)
- Experimental B(E2) in 38Ca: 101 ± 11 e2fm4 (from PhysRevC.107.034306, PhysRevC.108.L061301)
- Experimental B(E2) in 36S: 89 ± 9 e2fm4 (from 399d5bb09b5d11dcbee902004c4f4f50)
- Experimental B(E2) in 38Ar: 125 ± 4 e2fm4 (from PRITYCHENKO201773)
- The 2+1 resonance in 36Ca lies 465 keV above the proton emission threshold
- Optimal SMEC B(E2) values at the chi2 minimum: 120.9 e2fm4 for 36Ca, 93.375 e2fm4 for 38Ca, 75.826 e2fm4 for 36S, 126.4190 e2fm4 for 38Ar
- Optimal continuum coupling strength V0 ≈ -85 MeV fm3 (chi2/4 = 0.82)
- Effective charges used: ep = 1.236, en = 0.409

## Context

This work extends the analysis of B(E2) measurements in 36,38Ca reported by PhysRevC.107.034306 and PhysRevC.108.L061301, which showed surprising results compared to the mirror nuclei 36S and 38Ar. The authors use SMEC, previously applied to 36Ca in APP2008, to demonstrate that continuum coupling explains the anomalous B(E2) values and reveals strong mirror symmetry breaking in the 36Ca/36S pair, while mirror symmetry is well preserved in 38Ca/38Ar. The results enable further study of how proximity to particle emission thresholds affects nuclear structure and mirror symmetry in exotic nuclei.
