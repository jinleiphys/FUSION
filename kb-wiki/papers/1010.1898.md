# Dirac Quantization and Fractional Magnetoelectric Effect on Interacting Topological Insulators

> We use Dirac quantization of flux to study fractional charges and axion angles θin interacting topological insulators with gapless surface modes protected by time-reversal symmetry. In interacting topological insulators, there are two types of fractional axion angle due to conventional odd and nontrivial even flux quantization at the boundary. On even flux quantization in a gapped time reversal invariant system, we show that there is a halved quarter fractional quantum Hall effect on the surface with Hall conductance of p/4q e2/2h with p and q odd integers. The gapless surface modes can be characterized by a nontrivial Z2 anomaly emerged from the even flux quantization. It is suggested that the electron can be regarded as a bound state of fractionally charged quarks confined by a nonabelian color gauge field on the Dirac quantization of complex spinor fields.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in interacting topological insulators with gapless surface modes protected by time-reversal symmetry, a nontrivial even flux quantization at the boundary yields a halved quarter fractional quantum Hall effect on the surface, with the caveat that this result depends on the system having a gapped time-reversal invariant state and spin structures on the 4D manifold.

## Method

The authors apply Dirac quantization of flux to complex spinor fields representing antisymmetric partons (fractionally charged fermionic constituents of electrons) on a (3+1)D spin manifold. They construct an effective field theory with a U(1) x SU(N) gauge group, derive boundary Dirac quantization conditions for both conventional odd and nontrivial even flux quantization, and compute the resulting effective axion angle and surface Hall conductance from the topological term in the Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Surface Hall conductance: sigma_H^s = (p/4q) * (e^2/2h) with p and q odd integers, arising from even flux quantization.
- Effective axion angle from odd flux quantization: theta_eff = theta_1 / N_1^c = 0, +/- pi/N_1^c, +/- 3pi/N_1^c, +/- 5pi/N_1^c, ... for odd N_1^c.
- Effective axion angle from even flux quantization: theta_eff = theta_1 / (2N_1^c) = 0, +/- pi/(2N_1^c), +/- 3pi/(2N_1^c), +/- 5pi/(2N_1^c), ... for odd N_1^c.
- Fractionalized charge of topological objects: 1/2, emerged from even flux quantization.
- Parton charge from odd flux quantization: q_1 = e/N_1^c.
- Parton charge from even flux quantization: q_1 = e/(2N_1^c).

## Context

This work extends the theory of topological insulators from noninteracting systems to strongly interacting systems, building on the projective construction of fractional quantum Hall states by Jain (1989) and Wen (1991) and the SU(N) gauge theory approach to fractional topological insulators by Maciejko et al. (2010). The paper addresses the problem of time-reversal symmetry breaking in the large-N limit of SU(N) gauge theory noted by Witten (1998), and proposes that even flux quantization can preserve time-reversal invariance. The results enable the theoretical study of fractional topological insulators with deconfined fractional excitations and degenerate ground states on topologically nontrivial spaces.
