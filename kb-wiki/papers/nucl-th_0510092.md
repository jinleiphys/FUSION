# Hubble-like flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the dynamical appearance of scaling solutions in relativistic hydrodynamics. The phase transition effects are included through the temperature dependent sound velocity. If a pre-equilibrium transverse flow is included in the initial conditions, then it may reach the form of the asymptotic Hubble flow, r/t, in short evolution times, 7-15 fm. The numerical solutions are found to support to the freeze-out models (Blast-Wave, Buda-Lund, Cracow).

## Key claim
If a pre-equilibrium transverse flow is included in the initial conditions, the velocity profiles in relativistic hydrodynamics may approach the asymptotic Hubble-like form \(r/t\) within short evolution times of 7–15 fm, supporting the parameterizations used in freeze-out models.

## Method
The author solved relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric ideal fluid with zero net baryon density, using a temperature-dependent sound velocity derived from QCD lattice calculations. Initial conditions included a pre-equilibrium transverse flow profile parameterized by a Hubble constant \(H_0\), with the initial temperature profile proportional to the cube root of the nuclear thickness function. Numerical solutions for the transverse velocity and temperature were obtained as functions of time and radial distance.

## Key numbers
- Evolution time for the velocity profile to approach the asymptotic Hubble-like form: 7–15 fm (when pre-equilibrium flow with \(H_0 = 0.25\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) is included).
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: \(t_0 = 1\ \text{fm}\).
- Initial central temperature: \(T_0 = 2\,T_c\), where \(T_c = 170\ \text{MeV}\).
- Pre-equilibrium flow Hubble constant used in the initial condition: \(H_0 = 0.25\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Without pre-equilibrium flow (\(H_0 = 0\)), scaling solutions do not form for evolution times \(t < 10\ \text{fm}\).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Baym (1983) by incorporating a temperature-dependent sound velocity and pre-equilibrium transverse flow into relativistic hydrodynamics. It supports the freeze-out parameterizations of the Blast-Wave model (Schnedermann, 1993), the Buda-Lund model (Csörgő, 1999), and the Cracow model (Broniowski, 2001). The results enable further study of how early-time collective dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC can lead to Hubble-like expansion, linking hydrodynamic evolution to phenomenological freeze-out models.
