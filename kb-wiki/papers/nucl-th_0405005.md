# Splashing and evaporation of nucleons from excited nuclei

> The energy spectrum and the emission rate of particles emitted from excited nucleus due to both the evaporation and the splashing (emission from a cold vibrating nucleus) are calculated. We show that the collective motion of the nuclear Fermi liquid is accompanied by direct non-statistical emission of nucleons via the dynamical distortion of the Fermi surface.

# Splashing and evaporation of nucleons from excited nuclei

## Key claim
The paper shows that collective nuclear motion (specifically the giant monopole resonance) is accompanied by direct non-statistical emission of nucleons via dynamical distortion of the Fermi surface, and that this "splashing" mechanism can dominate over thermal evaporation in cold vibrating nuclei, but only under the condition that the amplitude of oscillation is small enough that the increase in Fermi-sphere radius alone is insufficient to cause particle emission, making the emission arise purely from the dynamic Fermi-surface shift.

## Method
The author uses a semiclassical phase-space approach with a local-equilibrium distribution function that includes a velocity field from collective excitation, calculating the flux density of emitted neutrons from the nuclear surface. The particle emission rate is derived for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) within the scaling model, where the displacement field is taken as proportional to the gradient of r², and the time-averaged spectral density of emission rate is compared between the cold splashing mechanism and thermal evaporation. The formalism is applied to a nucleus with mass number A=90 using standard nuclear parameters.

## Key numbers
- For A=90, the lifetime with respect to neutron splashing from the ISGMR is τ_splash = 6.0·10⁻²⁰ s.
- For the same nucleus, the lifetime for particle evaporation at equivalent excitation energy is τ_evap = 5.3·10⁻¹⁹ s (about 9 times longer).
- The ISGMR energy is taken as E_GMR = 82·A⁻¹/³ MeV (approximately 18.2 MeV for A=90).
- The neutron separation energy is λ = 7 MeV, the Fermi energy is ε_F = 37 MeV, and the nuclear radius parameter is R₀ = 1.12·A¹/³ fm.
- The Fermi momentum is k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical treatment of particle emission from collective nuclear states, building on the phase-space approach of Friedman and Lynch (1983) and the nuclear fluid dynamics of Kolomietz and Shlomo (2004). It contrasts with pure quantum mechanical studies of direct particle emission from giant resonances by Stringari and Vautherin (1979), van der Woude (1987, 1990), Borghols et al. (1989, 1990), and Muraviev and Urin (1994). The approach enables future application to large-amplitude collective motion such as the descent from the fission barrier to scission point and the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, where both statistical and direct emission mechanisms compete.
