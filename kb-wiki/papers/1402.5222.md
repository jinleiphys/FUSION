# Heavy quark symmetry in multi-hadron systems

> We discuss the properties of hadronic systems containing one heavy quark in the heavy quark limit. The heavy quark symmetry guarantees the mass degeneracy of the states with total spin and parity $(j-1/2)^{P}$ and $(j+1/2)^{P}$ with $j \geq 1/2$, because the heavy-quark spin is decoupled from the total spin $j$ of the light components called brown muck. We apply this idea to heavy multi-hadron systems, and formulate the general framework to analyze their properties. We demonstrate explicitly the spin degeneracy and the decomposition of the wave functions in exotic heavy hadron systems generated by the one boson exchange potential. The masses of the brown muck can be extracted from theoretical and experimental hadron spectra, leading to the color non-singlet spectroscopy.

## Key claim

The heavy quark symmetry (HQS) guarantees that, in the heavy quark limit, hadronic systems containing one heavy quark exhibit mass degeneracy between states with total spin and parity \((j-1/2)^P\) and \((j+1/2)^P\) for \(j \geq 1/2\), because the heavy-quark spin decouples from the total spin \(j\) of the light components (the "brown muck"); this degeneracy holds irrespective of whether the brown muck is a compact quark-gluon object or an extended multi-hadron composite (the "spin-complex").

## Method

The authors formulate a general framework for heavy multi-hadron systems by decomposing the total angular momentum \(J\) into the heavy-quark spin \(S\) and the conserved total spin \(j\) of the brown muck, which they term the "spin-complex" when it includes hadronic degrees of freedom. They demonstrate the spin degeneracy and wave-function decomposition explicitly for exotic heavy hadron systems generated by a one-boson-exchange potential, using a brown-muck component (BMC) basis that can be transformed unitarily to a physical particle basis. The masses of the brown muck are extracted from theoretical and experimental hadron spectra via the heavy-quark effective theory (HQET) mass formula, enabling color non-singlet spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

* Mass splitting between \(\bar{D}\) (\(J=0\)) and \(\bar{D}^*\) (\(J=1\)) mesons is 140 MeV, and between \(B\) and \(B^*\) mesons is 45 MeV, compared to \(\sim 600\) MeV between \(\pi\) and \(\rho\) and \(\sim 400\) MeV between \(K\) and \(K^*\).
* Mass splitting between \(\Sigma_c\) (\(J=1/2\)) and \(\Sigma_c^*\) (\(J=3/2\)) baryons is 65 MeV, and between \(\Sigma_b\) and \(\Sigma_b^*\) is 20 MeV, compared to 192 MeV between \(\Sigma\) and \(\Sigma^*\).
* Mass splitting between \(\Lambda_c^*(1/2^-)\) and \(\Lambda_c^*(3/2^-)\) is 33 MeV, and between \(\Lambda_b^*(1/2^-)\) and \(\Lambda_b^*(3/2^-)\) is 8 MeV, compared to 115 MeV between \(\Lambda^*(1/2^-)\) and \(\Lambda^*(3/2^-)\).

## Context

This work extends the heavy quark symmetry framework, previously applied to single hadrons (Isgur and Wise, 1989; Neubert, 1993; Manohar, 2000) and to exotic hadrons with hidden charm or bottom (Bondar, 2011; Voloshin, 2011; Ohkoda, 2011, 2012), to multi-hadron systems such as \(\bar{P}^{(*)}N\) bound states and \(\bar{P}^{(*)}NN\) dibaryons, as initiated in Yasui (2013) and Yamaguchi (2013). It enables the systematic classification of hadronic molecules and the extraction of brown-muck masses from experimental spectra, providing a tool for color non-singlet spectroscopy that can be applied to both confinement-phase and deconfinement-phase systems (Shuryak, 2003, 2004; Alford, 2007; Fukushima, 2010).
