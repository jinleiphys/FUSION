# Quarkonium Phenomenology from a Generalised Gauss Law

> We present an improved analytic parametrisation of the complex in-medium heavy quark potential derived rigorously from the generalised Gauss law. To this end we combine in a self-consistent manner a non-perturbative vacuum potential with a weak-coupling description of the QCD medium. The resulting Gauss-law parametrisation is able to reproduce full lattice QCD data by using only a single temperature dependent parameter, the Debye mass $m_D$. Using this parametrisation we model the in-medium potential at finite baryo-chemical potential, which allows us to estimate the $Ψ^\prime$/$J/Ψ$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at different beam energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved analytic parametrisation of the complex in-medium heavy quark potential derived from a generalised Gauss law, which reproduces full lattice QCD data using only a single temperature-dependent parameter (the Debye mass \(m_D\)), but the authors note that the imaginary part of the string contribution required regularisation due to an unphysical logarithmic divergence arising from the absence of explicit string breaking in the vacuum Cornell potential.

## Method

The authors combine a non-perturbative vacuum potential (the Cornell potential, consisting of a Coulombic term and a linear string term) with a weak-coupling description of the QCD medium via the hard thermal loop (HTL) permittivity in linear response theory. They apply a generalised Gauss law to derive separate expressions for the in-medium modification of the Coulombic and string parts, obtaining closed-form analytic expressions for both the real and imaginary parts of the potential. The string imaginary part required regularisation by introducing a scale \(\Delta\) to cure an infrared divergence, with the regularisation parameter fixed by requiring the asymptotic large-distance behaviour to match that of the Coulombic part.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum Cornell potential parameters from continuum extrapolation: \(\alpha = 0.513 \pm 0.0024\) GeV, \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.412 \pm 0.0041\) GeV, \(c = -0.161 \pm 0.0025\) GeV, with charm quark fit mass \(m_c^{\mathrm{fit}} = 1.4692\) GeV.
* Non-perturbative corrections to the Debye mass interpolation: \(\kappa_1 = 0.686 \pm 0.221\) and \(\kappa_2 = -0.317 \pm 0.052\).
* Regularisation parameter for the string imaginary part: \(\Delta_D = \Delta/m_D \simeq 3.0369\), giving \(\chi(\infty) \simeq 1\).
* Lattice QCD data used for vetting: \(48^3 \times 12\) lattices with \(N_f = 2+1\) flavours, pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 300\) MeV, transition temperature \(T_C \approx 175\) MeV (Burnier 2014, Bazavov 2011).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Burnier (2015) by deriving the in-medium potential from a generalised Gauss law rather than using an ad-hoc assumption about the functional form of the real-space in-medium permittivity. The parametrisation reproduces lattice QCD data (Burnier 2014) and enables phenomenological applications at finite baryo-chemical potential, a regime currently inaccessible to lattice QCD simulations. The authors compute the \(\Psi^\prime\)/\(J/\Psi\) production ratio at different beam energies using the statistical hadronisation model of Andronic (2017) for thermal parameters, finding good agreement with experimental data from NA50, ALICE, and CMS collaborations.
