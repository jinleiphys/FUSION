# Initial spin fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and where to find them

> Collective spin phenomena in the final states of heavy-ion collisions are typically understood to originate from vorticity and shear in the quark-gluon plasma. Here, we ask whether spin could already be present in the initial condition of the collisions. In particular, we argue that if a spin density exists at the beginning of the QGP expansion, it should experience event-by-event fluctuations due to the finite number of participant nucleons. In this contribution, we propose a simple model of fluctuating spin initial conditions for event-by-event spin hydrodynamics based on the Glauber Monte Carlo paradigm. We postulate that, if the net spin of the events is conserved from the initial to the final state, then initial state fluctuations of spin should manifest in specific spin correlations of $Λ$ hyperons. Within our picture, we predict that this signal is much larger in central O+O collisions than in central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if a spin density exists at the beginning of the quark-gluon plasma expansion, it should experience event-by-event fluctuations due to the finite number of participant nucleons, and that these fluctuations would manifest in specific spin correlations of Λ hyperons that are much larger in central O+O collisions than in central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Method

The authors construct a Glauber Monte Carlo model where each participant nucleon is assigned a random spin projection (up or down) with a common quantization axis, generating an event-by-event spin density at midrapidity. They simulate minimum-bias Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, and O+O collisions to compute the standard deviation of the polarization parameter per participant as a function of centrality. They then derive an observable spin correlation coefficient vΛ² from the angular distribution of proton pairs from Λ decays, which isolates the event-by-event fluctuation of the polarization magnitude.

## Key numbers

- The standard deviation of the polarization parameter, std(𝒫), shows a clear 1/√Npart scaling, increasing by about an order of magnitude from central to peripheral collisions
- The 1/√Npart scaling is nearly perfect when moving from Pb+Pb to Xe+Xe collisions, but is strongly broken by O+O collisions
- For S0 ≈ 0.1, the model would still generate percent-level signals in the spin correlation observable vΛ²
- The signal is predicted to be much larger in central O+O collisions than in central Pb+Pb collisions, and nearly identical in O+O data from both RHIC and LHC collision energies

## Context

This work extends the paradigm of initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (Alver 2010, Giacalone 2023) to the spin degree of freedom, which has not been explored in this context. Most theoretical descriptions assume polarization arises solely from thermal vorticity or shear at freeze-out (Becattini 2024), successfully predicting global polarization of Λ hyperons but facing limitations for local polarization. The proposed observable would probe the nuclear two-body density in spin space, and could enable tests of spin conservation and relaxation, as well as novel methods to probe the spin structure of nuclei at high energy using approaches such as Nuclear Lattice or Quantum Monte Carlo methods.
