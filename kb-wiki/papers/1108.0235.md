# Role of colliding geometry on the N/Z dependence of balance energy

> We study the role of colliding geometry on the N/Z dependence of balance energy using isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. Our study reveals that the N/Z dependence of balance energy becomes much steeper for peripheral collisions as compared to the central collisions. We also study the effect of system mass on the impact parameter dependence of N/Z dependence of balance energy. The study shows that lighter systems shows greater sensitivity to colliding geometry towards the N/Z dependence.

## Key claim

The N/Z dependence of balance energy (E_bal) becomes much steeper for peripheral collisions compared to central collisions, with lighter systems showing greater sensitivity to colliding geometry than heavier systems. This dependence is attributed primarily to the enhanced role of repulsive symmetry energy at higher N/Z ratios, with the effect being more pronounced at peripheral geometries.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate reactions of isotopic series of Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Xe with N/Z ratios varying from 1.0 to 2.0 in steps of 0.2. They employed a soft equation of state with standard isospin- and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections reduced by 20% (sigma = 0.8 sigma_nn_free). The balance energy was extracted from the directed transverse momentum for three impact parameter bins: b/b_max = 0.2-0.4, 0.4-0.6, and 0.6-0.8.

## Key numbers

- Slopes of N/Z dependence of E_bal for Ca series: 33 (b/b_max = 0.2-0.4), 58 (b/b_max = 0.4-0.6), and 187 (b/b_max = 0.6-0.8)
- Slopes of N/Z dependence of E_bal for Sn series: 15 (b/b_max = 0.2-0.4), 18 (b/b_max = 0.4-0.6), and 36 (b/b_max = 0.6-0.8)
- For Ca series, slope increases by approximately 400% when moving from central to peripheral collisions; for Xe series, the increase is approximately 150%
- Percentage change in slope (Delta m(%)) when symmetry energy is turned off: 67% for Ca and 93% for Xe at b/b_max = 0.2-0.4; 64% for Ca and 89% for Xe at b/b_max = 0.4-0.6
- Power law parameter tau for E_bal versus system mass at b/b_max = 0.4-0.6: -0.50 +/- 0.02 (N/Z=1.0), -0.46 +/- 0.02 (N/Z=1.2), -0.41 +/- 0.02 (N/Z=1.4), -0.34 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=1.6), -0.33 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=1.8), -0.22 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=2.0)
- Power law parameter tau at b/b_max = 0.6-0.8: -1.03 +/- 0.01 (N/Z=1.0), -0.87 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=1.2), -0.81 +/- 0.02 (N/Z=1.4), -0.72 +/- 0.02 (N/Z=1.6), -0.63 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=1.8), -0.59 +/- 0.03 (N/Z=2.0)
- Slopes of tau versus N/Z: 0.13 (b/b_max = 0.2-0.4), 0.27 (b/b_max = 0.4-0.6), and 0.43 (b/b_max = 0.6-0.8)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Gautam et al. (2010) on isospin effects in balance energy and by Sood (2010) on the N/Z dependence of E_bal for Ca isotopic series, which showed sensitivity to symmetry energy. The present study generalizes these findings across the full range of colliding geometries and system masses, demonstrating that the N/Z dependence of balance energy can serve as a probe of symmetry energy, with peripheral collisions and lighter systems providing the strongest sensitivity. This enables future experimental investigations using radioactive ion beams to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
