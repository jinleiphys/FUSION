# Structure and tidal deformability of a hybrid star within the framework of the field correlator method

> The structure of hybrid stars within the nonperturbative framework of the field correlator method, extended to zero-temperature limit as a quark model, has been studied. For the hadronic sector, we have used the lowest-order constraint variational method by employing AV18 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented by the phenomenological Urbana-type three-body force. For an adapted value of the gluon condensate, G2 = 0:006 GeV4, which gives the critical temperature of about Tc ? 170 MeV, stable hybrid stars with a maximum mass of 2:04M? are predicted. The stability of hybrid star has been investigated for a wide range of gluon condensate value, G2, and quark-antiquark potential, V1. A hybrid equation of state fulfills the constraints on tidal deformability and hence on the radii of the stars, extracted from the binary GW170817. Moreover, tidal deformability for different chirp masses and different binary mass ratios of hybrid stars have been studied. The mass-radius relation satisfies the new constraint obtained from the neutron star interior composition explorer (NICER). A comprehensive analysis on the structure of a hybrid star and also its compactness, tidal Love number, and tidal deformability has been conducted for several parameter sets of the quark equation of state. The influence of different crustal equations of state on the mentioned quantities has been studied. Our calculations suggest the value of quark-antiquark potential, V1, to be around 0.08 GeV. The results achieved in this study are in strong concurrence with the other calculations reported on this subject.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that stable hybrid stars with a pure quark core and a maximum mass of 2.04 solar masses are possible only for a specific combination of the field correlator method parameters: a gluon condensate value of G2 = 0.006 GeV^4 and a quark-antiquark potential V1 around 0.08 GeV, with stability lost for larger values of either parameter.

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using the lowest-order constraint variational method with the AV18 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented by the phenomenological Urbana-type three-body force. The quark phase was modeled using the nonperturbative field correlator method extended to zero temperature, with the hadron-quark phase transition treated via the Maxwell construction (sharp first-order transition). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then integrated to obtain the mass, radius, and tidal deformability of hybrid stars for various combinations of the gluon condensate G2 and the static quark-antiquark potential V1.

## Key numbers

* Maximum stable hybrid star mass of 2.04 solar masses predicted for G2 = 0.006 GeV^4 and V1 = 0.08 GeV
* For G2 = 0.004 GeV^4, stable hybrid stars predicted up to V1 = 0.09 GeV with maximum mass 1.92 solar masses
* For G2 = 0.005 GeV^4, stable hybrid stars predicted up to V1 = 0.09 GeV with maximum mass 2.03 solar masses
* For G2 = 0.006 GeV^4, stable hybrid stars predicted up to V1 = 0.08 GeV with maximum mass 2.04 solar masses
* The gluon condensate value G2 = 0.006 GeV^4 gives a critical temperature of about Tc = 170 MeV at zero chemical potential
* The gluon condensate has a nominal value of G2 = 0.012 +/- 0.006 GeV^4 from QCD sum rules
* The correlation length lambda = 0.34 fm (full QCD)
* The onset of phase transition occurs around 2 times nuclear saturation density (rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
* For V1 = 0 and G2 = 0.017 GeV^4, maximum mass of 2.13 solar masses predicted (unstable)
* For V1 = 0.01 GeV and G2 = 0.017 GeV^4, maximum mass of 2.16 solar masses predicted (unstable)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Khanmohamadi et al. (2019) that investigated hybrid stars within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio and MIT bag models, which predicted maximum masses below 2 solar masses for stable configurations. The field correlator method used here includes confinement from first principles, unlike the NJL model, and the results satisfy constraints from the binary neutron star merger GW170817 on tidal deformability and from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) on the mass-radius relation. The study enables future work to explore the full temperature-chemical potential phase diagram using the FCM framework, which is not possible with many other quark models.
