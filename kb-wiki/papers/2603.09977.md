# Does hot QCD have a conformal manifold in the chiral limit?

> Recent lattice evidence suggests the chiral phase transition in QCD is second-order for $N_f \ge 2$ massless flavors. We constrain CFT descriptions of a critical line in temperature $T$ and imaginary baryon chemical potential $θ_B = iμ_B/T$. An 't Hooft anomaly at general $θ_B$ constrains the transition even at $θ_B = 0$, leaving only three minimal scenarios. The best-motivated scenario for $N_f\ge3$, and perhaps also $N_f = 2$, is beyond Ginzburg-Landau, featuring a conformal manifold of $θ_B$-dependent universality classes with an exactly marginal operator related to baryon density.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in massless QCD for \(N_f \ge 2\) may be described by a conformal manifold of distinct 3D CFTs parameterized by \(\theta_B = i\mu_B/T\), rather than by a single Ginzburg-Landau universality class, provided the 't Hooft anomaly constraints are satisfied and the \((2+\epsilon)\)D expansion extrapolates reliably to three dimensions.

## Method

The authors constrain possible 3D conformal field theories (CFTs) describing the critical line of the chiral phase transition in massless QCD compactified on \(\mathbb{R}^3 \times S^1\) with temperature \(T\) and imaginary baryon chemical potential \(\theta_B = i\mu_B/T\). They impose consistency with the 't Hooft anomaly of the 4D theory reduced to 3D, with high-temperature perturbative results (Roberge-Weiss, 1986), and with low-temperature chiral symmetry breaking. They analyze three minimal scenarios: a Landau scenario, a Landau-DQCP scenario, and a conformal-manifold scenario, using \((2+\epsilon)\)D expansions of the \(SU(N_f)\) principal chiral model and existing conformal bootstrap bounds to assess viability for \(N_f = 2, 3, 4, 5\).

## Key numbers

* \(\Delta_E = 1.94 \pm 0.56\) for \(N_f = 2\), estimated from Padé-Borel resummation of the \((2+\epsilon)\)D expansion, for the relevant scalar operator \(\mathcal{O}_E\) in the 3D \(SU(N_f)_0\) CFT.
* \(\Delta_\phi = 0.54 \pm 0.02\) for \(N_f = 2\), estimated from Padé-Borel resummation, for the lowest-dimension primary scalar in the \(\boldsymbol{N_f}\otimes\overline{\boldsymbol{N_f}}\) representation.
* \(\Delta_E = 1.76 \pm 0.67\) for \(N_f = 3\), estimated from Padé-Borel resummation.
* \(\Delta_\phi = 0.64 \pm 0.02\) for \(N_f = 3\), estimated from Padé-Borel resummation.
* Conformal bootstrap for \(N_f = 2\) gives an island with \(1.64 \lesssim \Delta_E \lesssim 1.68\) and \(0.517 \lesssim \Delta_\phi \lesssim 0.521\) (Kos et al., 2015).
* Conformal bootstrap for \(N_f = 3\) gives a rough kink at \(\Delta_\phi = 0.62 \pm 0.01\) (Kousvos et al., 2022).

## Context

This work extends recent lattice evidence (Cuteri et al., 2021; Dini et al., 2021; Nakamura et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2022, 2024, 2025; Klinger et al., 2025, 2026; D'Ambrosio et al., 2025) suggesting the chiral transition is second-order for \(N_f \ge 2\), contradicting the earlier Ginzburg-Landau prediction of a first-order transition for \(N_f \ge 3\) (Pisarski and Wilczek, 1983). It constrains possible CFT descriptions using 't Hooft anomalies (Yonekura, 2019; Nishimura and Zhong, 2019; Kobayashi et al., 2023) and proposes a conformal-manifold scenario beyond the Landau paradigm, analogous to deconfined quantum critical points in condensed matter (Senthil et al., 2003, 2004). The work enables lattice tests distinguishing the three scenarios by measuring the infrared behavior of the baryon density operator \(\rho_B\) at \(\theta_B = 0\) and \(\theta_B = \pi\).
