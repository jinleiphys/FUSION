# Box diagram in the elastic electron-proton scattering

> We present an evaluation of box diagram for the elastic $ep$ scattering with proton in the intermediate state. Using analytic properties of the proton form factors we express the amplitude via twofold integral, which involves the form factors in the space-like region only. Therefore experimentally measured form factors can be used in the calculations directly. The numerical calculation is done with the form factors extracted by Rosenbluth, as well as by polarization transfer methods. The dependence of the results on the form factor choice is small for $Q^2 < 6 GeV^2$, but becomes sizable at higher $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (box diagram) amplitude for elastic electron-proton scattering can be expressed as a twofold integral involving proton form factors only in the space-like region (t < 0), so that experimentally measured form factors can be used directly without needing to know the poorly constrained time-like behavior; the dependence of the results on the choice of form factor parameterization is small for Q² < 6 GeV² but becomes sizable at higher Q².

## Method

The authors evaluated the box and crossed-box diagrams with a proton in the intermediate state by performing analytic integration over the loop momentum as far as possible, using Wick rotation and the analytic properties of the proton form factors. The resulting amplitude was reduced to a twofold integral over the squared momentum transfers t₁ and t₂, both restricted to the space-like region (t₁, t₂ ≤ 0), with explicitly known kernel functions K_ij(t₁,t₂). Numerical calculations were then performed using three different form factor parameterizations: a dipole fit, a fit from Bosted (assuming G_E/G_M = const), and a fit from JLabFit (based on polarization transfer data).

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange correction δG_M/G_M reaches up to ~3% at Q² = 10 GeV².
- For the generalized electric form factor at Q² = 10 GeV², the difference between form factor parameterizations reaches 50% for small ε (virtual photon polarization).
- The dependence on form factor choice is small for Q² < 6 GeV².
- Calculations were performed at Q² values of 1, 3, 6, and 10 GeV².

## Context

This work extends earlier approximate treatments of the two-photon exchange diagram, including the soft-photon approximation of Tsai (1961) and the hard-photon approximation of Kuraev (1985), as well as exact calculations using specific form factor parameterizations by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003, 2005). The key advance is that the method does not require form factors in the time-like region (t > 0), which are poorly known, unlike the earlier approach of Blunden et al. (2005) that used a sum-of-monopoles parameterization that did not reproduce the vector-meson pole structure. This enables direct use of experimentally measured space-like form factors and can be extended to contributions from the Δ(1232) resonance and other intermediate states, as well as to scattering off other spin-1/2 particles such as the neutron or ³He.
