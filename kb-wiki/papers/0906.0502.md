# Recent progress in lattice QCD at finite temperature

> I review recent progress in finite temperature lattice calculations, including the study of the nature of the deconfinement transition in QCD, equation of state, screening of static quarks and meson spectral functions.

# Recent progress in lattice QCD at finite temperature

## Key claim

The finite-temperature transition in QCD with physical quark masses is not a true phase transition but a rapid crossover, though the precise location of the crossover temperature remains controversial, with different lattice actions yielding values that differ by about 20 MeV.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations using improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, and stout) on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12. The transition temperature is extracted from peaks in fluctuations of the chiral condensate and Polyakov loop, while the equation of state is obtained by computing the interaction measure (ε − 3p) and integrating it to obtain pressure, energy density, and entropy density. The singlet free energy of static quark-antiquark pairs is calculated from correlation functions of temporal Wilson lines in Coulomb gauge, and meson spectral functions are reconstructed from Euclidean correlators using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM).

## Key numbers

- For 3-flavor QCD with improved staggered quarks on Nτ = 4 lattices, the first-order transition region exists only for pseudo-scalar meson masses of about 60 MeV.
- The transition temperature from p4 action calculations with combined continuum and chiral extrapolation gives Tc = 192(7)(4) MeV, using the Sommer parameter r0 = 0.469(7) fm to set the scale.
- The deconfinement temperature from stout action calculations on Nτ = 8, 10, and 12 lattices is 170(4)(3) MeV from the Polyakov loop and 169(3)(3) MeV from the strangeness susceptibility, using the kaon decay constant fK to set the scale.
- The chiral transition temperature from stout action calculations is 146–157 MeV, depending on the observable.
- At temperatures of about 4Tc in pure gauge theory, the deviation from the ideal gas value is only 15%.
- At high temperatures, the entropy density is 10% or less below the ideal gas limit.
- The singlet free energy shows exponential screening at distances rT > 0.8, with screening mass mD proportional to temperature.
- Quarkonium spectral function studies in quenched approximation show that 1S charmonia (ηc and J/ψ) appear to survive up to 1.6Tc, though this interpretation has been challenged by potential model calculations showing dissolution below 1.2Tc.

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD program summarized in earlier reviews (e.g., the 2008 review cited in the paper) and addresses ongoing controversies between different lattice actions. The p4 action results (from the MILC collaboration and others, e.g., 2006) give a higher transition temperature than the stout action results (Fodor et al., 2009), with the discrepancy attributed to coarse lattice spacings in the p4 calculations. The work enables improved understanding of quark-gluon plasma properties, particularly for heavy ion phenomenology, and highlights the need for continuum-extrapolated calculations of spectral functions to resolve the fate of quarkonium states in the deconfined phase.
