# Constraining The Universal Lepton Asymmetry

> The relic cosmic background neutrinos accompanying the cosmic microwave background (CMB) photons may hide a universal lepton asymmetry orders of magnitude larger than the universal baryon asymmetry. At present, the only direct way to probe such an asymmetry is through its effect on the abundances of the light elements produced during primordial nucleosynthesis. The relic light element abundances also depend on the baryon asymmetry, parameterized by the baryon density parameter (eta_B = n_B/n_gamma = 10^(-10)*eta_10), and on the early-universe expansion rate, parameterized by the expansion rate factor (S = H'/H) or, equivalently by the effective number of neutrinos (N_nu = 3 + 43(S^2 - 1)/7). We use data from the CMB (and Large Scale Structure: LSS) along with the observationally-inferred relic abundances of deuterium and helium-4 to provide new bounds on the universal lepton asymmetry, finding for eta_L, the analog of eta_B, 0.072 +/- 0.053 if it is assumed that N_nu = 3 and, 0.115 +/- 0.095 along with N_nu = 3.3^{+0.7}_{-0.6}, if N_nu is free to vary.

## Key claim

The universal lepton asymmetry η_L may be orders of magnitude larger than the baryon asymmetry, and the authors find η_L = 0.072 ± 0.053 if the effective number of neutrinos N_ν is fixed to 3, or η_L = 0.115 ± 0.095 if N_ν is allowed to vary, with the caveat that these bounds depend on the adopted primordial abundances of deuterium and helium-4.

## Method

The authors combine Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) predictions for deuterium and helium-4 with independent constraints on the baryon density parameter η_10 and the effective number of neutrinos N_ν from cosmic microwave background (CMB) and large scale structure (LSS) data. They use analytic fitting formulas from Kneller and Steigman (2004) that relate the observed light-element abundances to the baryon density, the expansion rate factor S (or N_ν), and the electron neutrino degeneracy parameter ξ_e, assuming neutrino flavor equilibration prior to BBN so that ξ_e = ξ_μ = ξ_τ. The analysis is performed first with N_ν fixed to 3 (standard expansion rate) and then with N_ν free, marginalizing over the baryon density.

## Key numbers

- For N_ν = 3: ξ_e = 0.035 ± 0.026 from BBN alone; after combining with CMB/LSS constraint on η_10, ξ_e = 0.037 ± 0.026, and η_L = 0.072 ± 0.053.
- For N_ν free: from BBN plus CMB/LSS constraints on η_10 and N_ν, ξ_e = 0.056 ± 0.046, N_ν = 3.3^{+0.7}_{-0.6}, and η_L = 0.115 ± 0.095.
- At 95% confidence, |ξ_e| ≤ 0.09 for N_ν = 3, giving Σ_α ΔN_ν(ξ_α) ≤ 0.01; for N_ν free, Σ_α ΔN_ν(ξ_α) ≤ 0.03 at 95% confidence.
- The baryon density from CMB/LSS alone is η_10 = 6.14^{+0.16}_{-0.11} (from Simha and Steigman 2008).
- The adopted primordial deuterium abundance is y_D = 2.70^{+0.22}_{-0.20} (10^5 D/H), and the adopted primordial helium-4 mass fraction is Y_P = 0.240 ± 0.006.
- For N_ν = 3, the predicted lithium abundance is [Li] = 2.63^{+0.04}_{-0.05}; for N_ν free, [Li] = 2.62^{+0.05}_{-0.06}, both higher than the observationally inferred value of 2.1 ± 0.1 from metal-poor halo stars.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Barger et al. (2006) and Serpico and Raffelt (2005) by using improved constraints on N_ν and η_10 from the WMAP 5-year and other CMB/LSS data, yielding considerably more restrictive bounds on the lepton asymmetry. The results are consistent with the earlier analysis by Simha and Steigman (2008) which assumed ξ_e = 0. The authors note that the lithium problem, a discrepancy between BBN-predicted and observed primordial lithium-7, persists even when a non-zero lepton asymmetry is allowed, and may require either improved stellar physics models (such as the gravitational settling corrections proposed by Korn et al. 2006) or new physics beyond the standard model. Future CMB experiments such as PLANCK (expected σ(N_ν) ~ 0.24) and CMBPOL (σ(N_ν) ~ 0.09) could further narrow the allowed ranges.
