# Neutrino propagation in the neutron star with uncertainties from nuclear, hadron, and particle physics

> In the present work, we investigate the neutral-current neutrino-nucleon scattering in the nuclear medium using various energy-density functional (EDF) models such as the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) and SLy4, together with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for the nucleon form factors at finite density. The differential cross section (DCS) and neutrino mean free path (NMFP) are computed numerically, considering the density-dependent nucleon form factors (DDFF) and neutrino structural properties such as the neutrino magnetic moment (NMM) and its electric charge radius (NCR). It turns out that the DDFF decreases the scattering cross-section, while the NCR increases it considerably. The effect of the NMM turns out to be almost negligible. We also observe that the value of the neutron effective mass is of importance in the neutron-star cooling process, indicating that for the neutron effective mass larger than the mass in free space, the neutrino can interact with matter at densities $ρ\gtrsim 1.5 ρ_0$ in the neutron star with radius 13 km.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a finite neutrino charge radius (NCR) can drastically increase the neutrino-nucleon scattering cross-section and decrease the neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in neutron star matter, but only if the NCR value is sufficiently large (e.g., \(3.5 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) from the LAMPF experiment); a smaller upper-limit NCR from COHERENT data (\(1.28 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-1}\)) produces negligible changes.

## Method

The authors computed the neutral-current neutrino-nucleon differential cross-section (DCS) and neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in homogeneous neutron star matter using four non-relativistic energy-density functional (EDF) models (KIDS0, KIDS-A, KIDS0-m*87, SLy4) combined with density-dependent nucleon form factors (DDFFs) from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. They also incorporated experimental constraints on the neutrino magnetic moment (NMM) and neutrino charge radius (NCR) into the electromagnetic vertex of the effective Lagrangian, then integrated the DCS over energy and momentum transfer to obtain the NMFP at zero temperature for neutrino energy \(E_\nu = 5\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) and binding energy per nucleon \(E_{\rm B} = 16\) MeV are identical for all four EDF models (KIDS0, KIDS-A, KIDS0-m*87, SLy4).
- The reduction rate of the DCS maximum when including DDFF versus vacuum form factor (VFF) is 0.83 at \(\rho = \rho_0\), 0.77 at \(2\rho_0\), and 0.71 at \(3\rho_0\), weakly dependent on the EDF model.
- The NMM value used is \(\mu_\nu = 2.9 \times 10^{-11} \mu_{\rm B}\) from the experiment Beda (2013), giving negligible contribution to the DCS.
- Three NCR values are used: NCR1 = \(3.5 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) from Allen (1992), NCR2 = \(1.28 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) from Cadeddu (2018), and NCR3 = \(2.48 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) as an intermediate value.
- Including NCR1 decreases the NMFP by 3–5 km on average across densities; for the KIDS0 and KIDS-A models (where neutron effective mass \(M^*_n \gtrsim M_n\)), the NMFP becomes shorter than the neutron star radius of 13 km for densities \(\rho \gtrsim 1.5 \rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Hutauruk 2022) that used only point-like nucleons with the KIDS density functional, by adding nucleon structure via DDFFs from the QMC model and neutrino properties (NMM and NCR) from experimental constraints. It shows that the neutron effective mass is the dominant nuclear physics uncertainty for neutrino propagation, while the symmetry energy stiffness (comparing KIDS0 and KIDS-A) has little effect up to \(3\rho_0\). The results enable future calculations of neutron star thermal evolution and can be combined with coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE\(\nu\)NS) data from COHERENT to constrain beyond-standard-model neutrino properties more tightly.
