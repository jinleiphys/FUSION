# QCD Resummation in Hard Diffractive Dijet Production at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Diffractive dijet production at the electron-ion collider (EIC) has been proposed to study the gluon Wigner distribution at small-$x$. We investigate the soft gluon radiation associated with the final state jets and an all order resummation formula is derived. We show that the soft gluon resummation plays an important role to describe E791 data on $π$-induced diffractive dijet production at Fermilab. Predictions for the EIC are presented, and we emphasize that the soft gluon resummation is an important aspect to explore the nucleon/nucleus tomography through these processes.

## Key claim

The authors derive an all-order resummation formula for soft gluon radiation in hard diffractive dijet production and show that this resummation is essential for describing the total transverse momentum distribution of the dijet system; however, the quantitative impact depends on the jet radius and coupling constant, and the azimuthal modulation \(v_2\) is strongly suppressed by soft radiation, making direct measurement of the recoil nucleon momentum necessary to extract gluon Wigner distributions.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order cross section for diffractive dijet production in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, including energy evolution via the JIMWLK equations. They then incorporate all-order soft gluon resummation by convolving the leading-order cross section with a soft factor \(S(\lambda_\perp)\) derived from the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye (BMS) evolution equation, which accounts for both Sudakov and non-global logarithms. The soft factor is obtained by numerically solving the BMS equation and fitting the resulting probability \(P(\tau)\) to an analytical form, then differentiating to obtain \(S(\lambda_\perp)\).

## Key numbers

- For the E791 data fit, the jet transverse momentum is about 2 GeV and the parameter \(\beta \approx 0.6\) is used, corresponding to fixed coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and jet radius \(R \approx 0.25\).
- For the EIC predictions, the kinematic setup uses \(W = 140\) GeV, \(Q^2 = 25\) GeV\(^2\), \(|P_\perp| = 3.5\) GeV, and \(z = \bar{z} = 0.5\).
- The soft factor reduces the cross section by roughly a factor of 5–10 in the back-to-back limit at small \(|q_\perp|\).
- The elliptic modulation \(v_2\) from the unconvoluted cross section reaches up to about 7%, but with soft gluon resummation at \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) the maximal \(v_2\) is less than about 0.5%.
- For the E791 comparison, the coherent diffraction dominates at very low \(q_\perp\), while incoherent diffraction takes over at moderate \(q_\perp\); the soft factor contribution is important across the whole kinematic region.

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order analyses of diffractive dijet production at the EIC (Hatta et al. 2016, Mantysaari et al. 2019) by including higher-order soft gluon radiation effects. It also applies the resummation formalism developed for non-global logarithms in jet processes (Dasgupta and Salam 2001, Dasgupta and Salam 2002, Banfi, Marchesini, and Smye 2002) to the diffractive case. The comparison with E791 data (Aitala et al. 2000) provides a benchmark test, and the results show that direct measurement of the recoil nucleon momentum is imperative for extracting gluon Wigner distributions at the EIC, rather than relying on the dijet total transverse momentum as a proxy.
