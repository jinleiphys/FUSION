# Attenuation of phi mesons in gamma A reactions

> We present a theoretical analysis of inclusive photoproduction of phi mesons in nuclei. In particular the dependence of the total phi meson yield on the target mass number is investigated. The calculations are done using the semi-classical BUU transport approach that combines the initial state interaction of the incoming photon with the coupled-channel dynamics of the final state particles. The conditions of the calculations are chosen such as to match the set up of a recent experiment performed at SPring8/Osaka. Whereas the observables prove to be rather sensitive to the phi self energy in the medium, the attribution of deviations from the standard scenario to a particular in-medium effect seems to be impossible.

## Key claim
The observed attenuation of phi mesons in photon-nucleus reactions cannot be fully explained by standard in-medium effects such as collisional broadening and kaon self-energy modifications; an additional absorption mechanism corresponding to a total phi-nucleon cross section of about 27 mb is required to match the experimental transparency data from SPring8/Osaka, but the attribution of this deviation to a specific in-medium effect remains ambiguous.

## Method
The authors used the semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport approach to simulate inclusive photoproduction of phi mesons on Li, C, Al, and Cu nuclei at photon energies of 1.5-2.4 GeV. The calculations included initial-state photon interactions, Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, nuclear shadowing, coupled-channel final-state interactions, and relativistic kaon/antikaon potentials derived from chiral mean-field theory. The total phi-nucleon cross section was parameterized using a Regge-based quark model and then scaled by a constant K-factor to fit the measured nuclear transparency ratios.

## Key numbers
- Total phi-nucleon cross section extracted from data: approximately 27 mb, obtained with a K-factor of 2.6 multiplying the vacuum Regge estimate.
- Collisional width of phi meson at rest at normal nuclear matter density: 18 MeV, consistent with calculations by Cabrera (2003).
- In-medium phi decay width to K+K- at normal nuclear matter density: up to 20 MeV (vacuum value: 2.18 MeV), due to kaon mass renormalization.
- Combined in-medium phi width at saturation density: about 40 MeV from collisional broadening plus kaon renormalization effects.
- A-dependence exponent alpha for total phi yield: 0.72 from BUU calculations (omitting Pb), in agreement with the experimental value of 0.72 ± 0.07 from Ishikawa (2005).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical analysis of phi meson in-medium properties beyond earlier studies by Oset (2000) and Cabrera (2003), who proposed attenuation measurements as a probe of the phi self energy. The authors had previously shown (Muehlich, 2002) that K+K- invariant mass measurements face severe distortions from kaon absorption and Coulomb effects. The present results confirm the conclusion of Ishikawa (2005) and Cabrera (2003) that standard vacuum cross sections underestimate the observed attenuation, and provide a quantitative extraction of the phi-nucleon cross section needed to reproduce the SPring8/Osaka data. The findings enable future experiments to disentangle whether the enhanced absorption arises from modified phi-nucleon interactions, altered kaon cross sections, or changes in the primary production mechanism.
