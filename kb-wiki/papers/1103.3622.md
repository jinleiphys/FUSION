# Chiral two-body currents in nuclei: Gamow-Teller transitions and neutrinoless double-beta decay

> We show that chiral effective field theory (EFT) two-body currents provide important contributions to the quenching of low-momentum-transfer Gamow-Teller transitions, and use chiral EFT to predict the momentum-transfer dependence that is probed in neutrinoless double-beta decay. We then calculate for the first time the neutrinoless double-beta decay operator based on chiral EFT currents and study the nuclear matrix elements at successive orders. The contributions from chiral two-body currents are significant and should be included in all calculations.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory two-body currents naturally produce the quenching of low-momentum-transfer Gamow-Teller transitions, and their momentum-transfer dependence predicts that the quenching is reduced at the ~100 MeV momentum transfers probed in neutrinoless double-beta decay, so the naive quenching factor from beta decay (q^2 ~ 0.55) overestimates the reduction of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements.

## Method

The authors normal-order the chiral two-body axial currents to an effective one-body level by summing the second nucleon over occupied states in a spin- and isospin-symmetric Fermi-gas reference state, obtaining a correction to the axial coupling that depends on density and momentum transfer. They then compute the neutrinoless double-beta decay operator using these currents at successive chiral orders (Q^0, Q^2, Q^3) and evaluate the nuclear matrix elements for six candidate nuclei (48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 124Sn, 130Te, 136Xe) using state-of-the-art shell-model calculations with the code NATHAN.

## Key numbers

- The long-range parts of chiral two-body currents (c_D=0) predict a quenching factor q = 0.85...0.66 for the p=0 Gamow-Teller operator, depending on the c_3, c_4 couplings and for densities rho = 0.10...0.12 fm^{-3}.
- For the empirical quenching q=0.74, the extracted short-range coupling c_D ranges from -0.70 to +1.11 across different NN potential sets and density ranges.
- For a smaller quenching q=0.96, the extracted c_D ranges from -2.51 to -1.06.
- The pseudoscalar term contributes p g_P(p^2) ~ 7.9 for p ~ 100 MeV in neutrinoless double-beta decay.
- The Q^2 form-factor and pseudoscalar contributions each reduce the nuclear matrix element by about 20% (M_ff/M ~ -0.20, M_gP/M ~ -0.20).
- At order Q^3, chiral two-body currents change the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements by -35% to +10% across the six nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends chiral effective field theory currents, previously applied only in the lightest nuclei (Park, Gazit, and others), to medium-mass nuclei for Gamow-Teller transitions and neutrinoless double-beta decay. It provides a microscopic explanation for the phenomenological quenching of g_A observed in shell-model calculations (Wildenthal, 1983) and other many-body approaches, and it enables first-principles predictions of the momentum-transfer dependence that is crucial for neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, which have been a major uncertainty in interpreting experimental searches.
