# The Solar pp and hep Processes in Effective Field Theory

> The strategy of modern effective field theory is exploited to pin down accurately the flux $S$ factors for the $pp$ and $hep$ processes in the Sun. The technique used is to combine the high accuracy established in few-nucleon systems of the "standard nuclear physics approach" (SNPA) and the systematic power counting of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) into a consistent effective field theory framework. Using highly accurate wave functions obtained in the SNPA and working to \nlo3 in the chiral counting for the current, we make totally parameter-free and error-controlled predictions for the $pp$ and $hep$ processes in the Sun.

# The Solar pp and hep Processes in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
The authors combine standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) wave functions with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) to make parameter-free predictions for the solar pp and hep S-factors, with the only free constant fixed by tritium beta decay data. The predictions are claimed to be error-controlled, but the hep result retains a residual cutoff dependence corresponding to approximately 15% uncertainty that the authors attribute to missing N4LO corrections.

## Method
The authors use highly accurate correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) wave functions from the Argonne v18 potential (plus Urbana-IX three-nucleon force for A>=3) as input from SNPA. They compute the Gamow-Teller transition operators up to N3LO in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one-body currents, one-pion-exchange two-body currents, and a contact-term two-body current whose strength is fixed by fitting the tritium beta decay rate. The operators are regularized with a Gaussian cutoff function, and calculations are performed for cutoff values Lambda = 500, 600, and 800 MeV to test cutoff independence.

## Key numbers
- The pp S-factor: S(pp) = 3.94 x 10^{-25} MeV-barn, with uncertainties of 0.15% from one-body and 0.1% from two-body contributions
- The two-body contribution to the pp process relative to the one-body contribution: delta_2B^{pp} = (0.86 +/- 0.05)%
- The hep S-factor: S(hep) = (8.6 +/- 1.3) x 10^{-20} keV-b, where the error spans the range for Lambda = 500-800 MeV
- The low-energy constant dR determined from tritium beta decay: dR = (1.00 +/- 0.07, 1.78 +/- 0.08, 3.90 +/- 0.10) for Lambda = (500, 600, 800) MeV respectively
- The one-body GT matrix element for hep is -0.81 (arbitrary units), while the two-body contribution ranges from 0.49 to 0.59 depending on cutoff
- The maximum neutrino energy for the hep process: E_nu^{max}(hep) = 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends the program of applying effective field theory to solar fusion processes, building on the strategy outlined by the same authors in prior work (Park et al., "strategy" reference). The results support the latest SNPA calculation by Marcucci et al. (MSVKRB), which gave S(hep) = 9.64 x 10^{-20} keV-b, while improving upon earlier estimates that ranged from Salpeter's original 630 x 10^{-20} keV-b to the "standard" value of approximately 2 x 10^{-20} keV-b from Carlson et al. (1991) and Schiavilla et al. (1992). The approach enables parameter-free predictions for both pp and hep processes within a single EFT framework, and the authors suggest that including N4LO corrections (which involve no additional unknown parameters) could further reduce the residual cutoff dependence in the hep result.
