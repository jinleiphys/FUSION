# Precise calculation of the two-step process for K- d -> pi Sigma N in the Lambda (1405) resonance region

> The reaction K- d -> pi Sigma N is investigated taking into account single scattering and the two-step process due to Kbar N -> pi Sigma rescattering. The influence of some common approximations are examined. It is found that the treatment of the kinematics in the Green's function that appears in the loop integral of the rescattering process has a rather strong impact on the resulting lineshape of the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum. Specifically, a calculation with correct kinematics where the three-body unitarity cut due to the nK- p threshold occurs at the physical value yields a pronounced peak in the invariant mass spectrum at this threshold and, at the same time, suppresses the signal in the region of the Lambda(1405) resonance. On the other hand, an approximation applied in past calculations shifts that threshold down and, consequently, leads to an accidental and therefore erroneous enhancement of the signal of the Lambda(1405) in the pi Sigma invariant mass spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the apparent peak of the Lambda(1405) resonance seen in previous calculations of the K- d -> pi Sigma N reaction is actually an artifact of an approximation that shifts the three-body unitarity cut (n K- p threshold) to an unphysical lower energy; when correct kinematics are used, the threshold peak suppresses the signal in the Lambda(1405) region.

## Method

The authors calculated the K- d -> pi Sigma N reaction including both single-scattering (impulse approximation) and the two-step rescattering process where the kaon first scatters off one nucleon and then undergoes Kbar N -> pi Sigma on the other nucleon. They solved the loop integral for the two-step process without the factorization approximation used in prior work, and treated the kinematics in the Green's function so that the three-body unitarity cut for the intermediate n K- p system occurs at the physical threshold. The calculation used both the Oset-Ramos chiral interaction and the Juelich meson-exchange model for the elementary Kbar N -> pi Sigma amplitudes, with the deuteron wave function taken from the Nijmegen soft-core potential Nijm93.

## Key numbers

* The K- beam momentum was fixed to 600 MeV/c and the neutron angle to theta_n = 0 degrees, matching the kinematics of the J-PARC experiment.
* The physical n K- p threshold occurs at M_piSigma approximately 1432 MeV.
* The approximation used in prior work (Jido et al., 2009) shifts the n K- p threshold downward by 14 MeV compared to its actual physical value.
* The single-scattering (process A) contribution is a factor of 10^2 to 10^3 smaller than the dominant two-step process (B2) in the considered region.
* The Oset-Ramos interaction produces poles at 1426 + i16 MeV and 1390 + i66 MeV in the I=0 channel.
* The Juelich model produces poles at 1436 + i26 MeV and 1334 + i62 MeV.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the prior calculation by Jido et al. (2009), which used the same Oset-Ramos interaction but applied a kinematic approximation to the Green's function that shifted the three-body threshold and produced artificial peaks attributed to the Lambda(1405) resonance. The authors also compare results from the Oset-Ramos chiral model with the Juelich meson-exchange model (Mueller-Groeling et al., 1990), which generates two poles at different positions. The study enables more reliable interpretation of the proposed J-PARC experiment for spectroscopic study of hyperon resonances below the Kbar N threshold via the (K-,n) reaction on a deuteron target.
