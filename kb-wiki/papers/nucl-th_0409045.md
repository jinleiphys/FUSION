# Hydrodynamic afterburner for the CGC at RHIC

> Firstly, we give a short review about the hydrodynamic model and its application to the elliptic flow phenomena in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Secondly, we show the first approach to construct a unified model for the description of the dynamics in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first approach to construct a unified model that combines a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial condition with a hydrodynamic afterburner for relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the authors explicitly assume that the system of gluons initially produced from the CGC reaches local thermal equilibrium at a short time scale, leaving the non-equilibrium description of the collision stage for future work.

## Method

The authors employ the kT-factorization formula for gluon production from CGC collisions following the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) approach, using a simplified unintegrated gluon distribution function with a saturation scale determined by solving an implicit equation. They match the number density of produced gluons to a thermalized massless free parton gas to obtain initial temperature fields, then evolve the system using (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with an equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition between QGP and hadron gas. The Cooper-Frye formula is used for freezeout to compute final particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time assumed: τ ~ 0.6 fm/c for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 and 200 GeV
- Average saturation momentum: ⟨Qs²(x=0.01)⟩ ~ 2.0 GeV²/c² in Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=0 at RHIC
- Cut-off momentum for low pT partons assumed to thermalize: pT,cut = 3 GeV/c
- Critical temperature used: Tc = 170 MeV
- Latent heat in the first-order phase transition EoS: ~1 GeV/fm³
- Hydrodynamic response v2/ε ~ 0.2-0.24 at SPS and RHIC energies
- Thermal freezeout temperature used for mean pT calculations: Tth = 100 MeV
- Impact parameters for centrality bins at √sNN = 200 GeV: 2.4, 4.5, 6.3, 7.9, and 9.1 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach that successfully described elliptic flow at RHIC (as reviewed from STAR data and calculations by Kolb, Heinz, Huovinen, and others) by replacing the conventional parametrized initial conditions with initial conditions derived from the CGC picture. The paper contrasts with purely hadronic transport models (e.g., Bass and Srivastava) that fail to reproduce the large elliptic flow at RHIC, and with classical Yang-Mills simulations based on CGC (Krasnitz, Nara, Venugopalan) that yield transverse energy per particle ~1.5 GeV versus the experimental ~0.6 GeV. The unified model enables future studies of both soft bulk matter and hard probes (jet quenching) within a single framework, and the authors identify the proper treatment of the boundary between QGP and hadron gas in hybrid models as an open problem.
