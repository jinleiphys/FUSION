# Microscopic study of strange hadron productions at the LHC with$\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> Ratio of the yield of strange hadrons to pions is considered as an important observable in studying the properties of the system produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Production of strange hadrons $K, \bar{K}, Λ, Σ, Ξ$ and $Ω$ have been evaluated microscopically using rate equations by considering their hadronic interaction cross sections in an expanding medium. The yields obtained from rate equations are normalized with thermal pions and compared with the measurements from Pb-Pb collisions at various multiplicities at LHC energy. The calculation has been done for various initial and freeze out conditions. At 2760 GeV, LHC energy, $Λ, Ξ, Ω$ freeze out close to $T_C$ and $K^0_s$ freezes out little later. But there is a subtle difference in freeze out temperatures of different species which may be distinguishable at lower colliding energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed yield ratios of strange hadrons to pions in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies can be reproduced by a microscopic rate-equation model with hadronic interactions and Bjorken expansion, but only when the freeze-out temperatures of different strange species are allowed to differ, with multi-strange baryons freezing out closer to the critical temperature \(T_C\) than kaons.

## Method

The authors solve a set of coupled rate equations for the number densities of \(K, \bar{K}, \Lambda, \Sigma, \Xi\), and \(\Omega\) in a hadron gas undergoing longitudinal (Bjorken) expansion. The production and absorption terms in the equations are computed from thermal averages of hadronic cross sections (\(\langle \sigma v \rangle\)) for meson-meson, meson-baryon, and baryon-baryon channels, including strangeness-exchange reactions for multi-strange baryons. The non-strange mesons and baryons are treated as a thermal background, and the system evolves from an initial temperature down to chemical freeze-out, with the temperature and baryon chemical potential following the Bjorken cooling law.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, comparing with ALICE data at various charged-particle multiplicities.
* The production rate for \(\Xi\) from strangeness-exchange channels (e.g., \(\Lambda\Lambda \rightarrow N\Xi\), \(\bar{K}\Lambda \rightarrow \pi\Xi\)) is found to be about \(10^6\) times larger than that from the \(p\bar{p} \rightarrow \Xi\bar{\Xi}\) channel.
* The \(\Xi/\pi\) and \(\Omega/\pi\) yield ratios in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are reported to be 1.6 and ~3.3 times larger, respectively, than in p-p collisions at 7 TeV (citing ALICE data).
* The freeze-out temperature of \(\Lambda, \Xi, \Omega\) is found to be close to the critical temperature \(T_C\), while \(K^0_s\) freezes out at a slightly lower temperature.

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic transport and rate-equation studies of strangeness production (e.g., Nayak et al. (2010), Bass et al. (1999)) that successfully explained kaon yields at SIS, AGS, and SPS energies but failed to reproduce multi-strange baryon yields. It also contrasts with the Statistical Hadronisation Model (Andronic et al. (2009)), which assumes a common chemical freeze-out temperature for all species and cannot simultaneously fit the \(p/\pi\) ratio and multi-strange hadron ratios at LHC. The results support a sequential freeze-out scenario, as argued by Bellewied (2018), and enable future work with full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics to refine the freeze-out parameters.
