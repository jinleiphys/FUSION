# Triton photodisintegration with realistic potentials

> The photodisintegration of $^{3}$H is treated by means of coupled integral equations using separable versions of the Paris and the Bonn potentials in their kernel. The differential cross section for the inverse reaction is obtained via detailed balance. For the latter process good agreement with the data is found when including final-state interaction, meson exchange currents, higher partial waves in the potential, and electric quadrupole contributions in the electromagnetic interaction.

# Triton photodisintegration with realistic potentials

## Key claim

The differential cross section for the radiative capture process p + d → γ + ³He shows good agreement with experimental data at 12.1 MeV and 15.3 MeV when final-state interaction, meson exchange currents, higher partial waves in the potential, and electric quadrupole contributions are included; however, the relevance of the better agreement of Bonn potentials with ³H photodisintegration data below 25 MeV is somewhat questionable given the experimental errors.

## Method

The authors solved coupled integral equations (Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations reduced to effective two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equations) using separable Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) expansions of the Paris, Bonn A, and Bonn B potentials. The triton wave function was projected onto 34 channels (j ≤ 4), capturing more than 99.6% of the wave function, and the photodisintegration amplitude was obtained by replacing the inhomogeneous term with an off-shell extension of the Born approximation. The differential cross section for the inverse radiative capture reaction was obtained via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energies: PEST potential gives -7.369 MeV, Paris gives -7.381 MeV; BAEST gives -8.285 MeV, Bonn A gives -8.294 MeV; BBEST gives -8.088 MeV, Bonn B gives -8.101 MeV
- The norm of the difference between EST wave functions and two-dimensional calculations for the original potential is always less than 10⁻³
- The wave function projection onto 34 channels (j ≤ 4) captures more than 99.6% of the whole wave function
- The energy range studied is from threshold up to 40 MeV for the γ + t → n + d reaction
- Radiative capture results are shown at 12.1 MeV and 15.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Schadow 1997) by replacing the W-matrix representation with the EST expansion of the Paris and Bonn potentials, using an improved parametrization by Haidenbauer. The study confirms the strong potential dependence in the peak region observed in that prior work and the importance of higher subsystem partial waves at energies above the peak region. The results lie between two competing experimental datasets (Kosiek 1966 and Skopik 1981), and the strong correlation between peak heights and triton binding energies is demonstrated across multiple potentials including the Malfliet-Tjon MT I+III potential (Schadow 1997). More accurate low-energy measurements are needed to clarify discrepancies between data and to evaluate the quality of the potential models.
