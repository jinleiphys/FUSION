# Review of Gamow-Teller and Fermi Transition Strength Functions

> We studied the temperature effect in isospin-singlet pairings in Gamow-Teller excitations. We use theories of a hole-particle in the mean field shell model studied decay transition using the one-particle-one-hole model for the $β$-decay of odd-even isotopes and the two-particle-hole models for the $β$-decay of even-even and/or odd-odd isotopes. Our reference isotopes for the one-particle-one-hole model are \ce{^{15}O}, \ce{^{15}N}, \ce{^{17}F}, and \ce{^{41}Sc}, whereas for the two-particle-hole model we use \ce{^{16}N} (for $β^-$-decay) and \ce{^{56}Ni} and \ce{^{40}Sc} (for $β^+$/EC). The calculations involve evaluating the matrix elements of Gamow -Teller and Fermi transitions, then calculate the reduced transition probabilities of Gamow-Teller and Fermi, from which we evaluate the half-lives and the strength function $ft$. The results are compared with the available experimental data. For one-particle-one-hole model we found there is a deviation from experimental values which indicates that the model is not valid for beta decay for the even-even nuclei in the ground state due to the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction. As for a two-particle-hole model, we calculated the transition amplitude, from which we calculated the strength of the transition $\log ft$ values. We found an excellent agreement between experimental and theoretical results. By drawing the relationship between temperature versus $\log ft$ values, we found the general trend is that the strength function values slowly decrease as temperatures increases. There are fluctuations $\log ft$ due to the strongly dependent of $\log ft$ on the shell configuration of the valence nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the one-particle-one-hole shell-model description is not valid for beta decay of even-even nuclei in the ground state due to residual nucleon-nucleon interactions, while the two-particle-hole model yields excellent agreement with experimental data. The authors also find that strength function values slowly decrease as temperature increases, though with fluctuations strongly dependent on the shell configuration of valence nucleons.

## Method

The authors applied one-particle-one-hole and two-particle-hole models within the mean-field shell-model framework to calculate Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition matrix elements for selected isotopes. They evaluated reduced transition probabilities and half-lives from these matrix elements, then computed the strength function values (log ft) and compared them with available experimental data. The temperature dependence of the strength functions was studied by plotting log ft values against temperature.

## Key numbers

- For the EC decay of 15O to 15N, the calculated f0t1/2 value is 4041.4 s, yielding log(ft) = 3.61, which matches the experimental superallowed value of 3.6
- The reduced Gamow-Teller transition probability BGT for the π0p1/2 → ν0p1/2 transition in 15O was calculated as 0.521 using gA = 1.25
- The constant κ used in half-life calculations is 6147 s
- The Fermi coupling constant GF is 1.16639(1) × 10−5 GeV−2
- The W-boson mass is 80.403 ± 0.029 GeV/c2

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of beta-decay strength functions as developed in Suhonen (2017) and applies it to specific test cases including 15O, 15N, 17F, 41Sc, 16N, 56Ni, and 40Sc. The work builds on the classification scheme of beta-decay transitions from Wu (1966) and the phase-space factor formalism from Gove (1971). The study enables future investigations of temperature effects on beta-decay rates relevant to astrophysical nucleosynthesis and tests of the Standard Model through precision beta-decay measurements.
