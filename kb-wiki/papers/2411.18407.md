# Collectivity in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion and e+A collisions

> We review recent theoretical progress in describing collective effects in photon+nucleus collisions. The approaches considered range from the color glass condensate where correlations are encoded in the initial state, to hydrodynamic frameworks, where a strong final state response to the initial geometry of the collision is the key ingredient to generate momentum-space correlations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical progress on collective effects in photon+nucleus collisions, arguing that while hydrodynamic models can describe the measured elliptic flow in ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, color glass condensate calculations also produce compatible results when experimental binning is accounted for, so no single explanation is fully ruled out.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical approaches for computing azimuthal anisotropies in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions (treated as photon+nucleus collisions): (1) a color glass condensate (CGC) framework using the dilute-dense picture with dipole operators and the factorized dipole approximation, and (2) a 3+1D hydrodynamic model where the photon projectile is modeled as a vector meson and parameters are fit to p+Pb collision data before predicting gamma*+Pb results. Both approaches compute the elliptic flow coefficient v2 from two-particle correlations and compare to ATLAS data.

## Key numbers

- Photon virtuality in ultra-peripheral collisions: Q ~ 30 MeV, much less than Lambda_QCD
- In the CGC calculation of Shi et al. (2020), the transverse spread parameter B_p = 25 GeV^{-2} and the saturation scale Q_s^2 = 5 GeV^2 were used, yielding good agreement for v2(p_T) in the region p_T < 2 GeV
- The hydrodynamic model reproduces the ATLAS elliptic flow measurement, which is smaller than that in p+Pb collisions; the next harmonic v3 is underestimated by the hydro model but experimental error bars are large

## Context

This work extends the study of collective effects from heavy-ion collisions (Gale, 2013) and small systems like p+p and p+Pb (CMS, 2010; CMS, 2012; ALICE, 2012; ATLAS, 2012) to the new regime of ultra-peripheral collisions, following the ATLAS observation (2021) of long-range azimuthal correlations in such events. The CGC approach builds on earlier calculations for p+A collisions (Mace, 2018) and the first UPC calculation (Shi, 2020), while the hydrodynamic approach (Zhao, 2022; Shen, 2022) extends frameworks successful in p+Pb collisions. The opposite dependence of v2 on projectile transverse size predicted by the two approaches could be tested at the future Electron Ion Collider.
