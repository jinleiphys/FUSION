# Pion reactions on two--nucleon systems

> We review recent progress in our understanding of elastic and inelastic pion reactions on two nucleon systems from the point of view of effective field theory. The discussion includes pion-deuteron scattering, gamma d to pi-nn, and NN to NNpi. At the end some remarks are made on strangeness production reactions.

# Pion reactions on two-nucleon systems

## Key claim

Effective field theory (chiral perturbation theory) provides a systematic and controlled framework for describing elastic and inelastic pion reactions on two-nucleon systems, but the standard Weinberg counting must be modified for reactions like $NN\to NN\pi$ where the initial momentum scale $\sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$ introduces a new expansion parameter. The paper argues that when this scale is properly accounted for, the apparent convergence problems in earlier EFT calculations are resolved and satisfying agreement with data is achieved.

## Method

The author reviews the application of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to three classes of reactions: $\pi d$ scattering, $\gamma d\to\pi^+ nn$, and $NN\to NN\pi$ (specifically $pp\to d\pi^+$). For each reaction, transition operators are constructed from the chiral Lagrangian at successive orders, then convoluted with proper nuclear wave functions following Weinberg's prescription. The analysis focuses on the role of $\pi NN$ cuts and nucleon recoil corrections, showing that taking the static nucleon limit ($M_N\to\infty$) prior to integration is not always allowed due to branch point singularities at pion production threshold. For $NN\to NN\pi$, a modified power counting is employed where the large initial momentum $p_{thr}=\sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$ and the pion mass are treated as independent expansion parameters.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $\pi d$ scattering length: $a_{\pi d}^{\text{exp}} = (-26.1 \pm 0.5 + i(6.3 \pm 0.7)) \times 10^{-3} \, m_\pi^{-1}$, where $m_\pi$ is the charged pion mass
- The theoretical limit for extracting the isoscalar $\pi N$ scattering length $a_+$ from $\pi d$ scattering: $\Delta a^{\text{theo}} \sim 5 \times 10^{-4} \, m_\pi^{-1}$
- The initial momentum threshold for $NN\to NN\pi$: $p_{thr} = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$
- The expansion parameter for strangeness production $NN\to N\Lambda K$ is 0.85 (initial momentum in units of nucleon mass); for $\pi d\to K\Lambda N$ it is 0.6
- The relative suppression of diagram (b) compared to diagram (a) in $NN\to NN\pi^+$ loops is $(m_\pi/M_N)^{3/2} \sim 1/20$; explicit calculation showed a factor of 50 suppression
- The projected uncertainty for extracting the $nn$ scattering length from $\gamma d\to\pi^+ nn$ is 0.1 fm

## Context

This review extends the program of applying chiral perturbation theory to few-nucleon systems, building on Weinberg's (1992) original suggestion to convolute perturbative transition operators with nuclear wave functions. It addresses convergence problems observed when the original Weinberg counting was applied to $NN\to NN\pi$ by Park et al. and others, where loop corrections at NNLO were found to be large. The proper treatment of the $\sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$ scale was presented by Hanhart et al. (2003). The work enables controlled extractions of fundamental quantities: the isoscalar $\pi N$ scattering length from $\pi d$ scattering (complementary to pionic hydrogen data) and the $nn$ scattering length from $\gamma d\to\pi^+ nn$ (with precision comparable to competing reactions $pd\to nnp$ and $\pi^- d\to \gamma nn$). For strangeness production reactions where chiral expansion breaks down due to large momentum transfer, dispersion theory is proposed as an alternative.
