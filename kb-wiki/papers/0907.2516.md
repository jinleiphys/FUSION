# Conical Correlations, Bragg Peaks, and Transverse Flow Deflections in Jet Tomography

> We use (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics to describe a variety of different jet energy loss scenarios for a jet propagating through an opaque medium. The conical correlations obtained for fully stopped jets, revealing a Bragg peak, are discussed as well as results from pQCD and AdS/CFT. Moreover, we investigate transverse flow deflection. It is demonstrated that a double-peaked away-side structure can be formed due to the different contributions of several possible jet trajectories through an expanding medium.

## Key claim
Using (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics, the authors show that a double-peaked away-side structure in azimuthal di-hadron correlations can be formed by averaging over several possible jet trajectories through an expanding medium, rather than by a "true" Mach cone; the emission angle of such a structure is therefore not directly connected to the equation of state.

## Method
The authors solve (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic equations with a source term for jet energy and momentum deposition, using a SHASTA algorithm. They model the medium with Glauber initial conditions for a gold nucleus (radius 6.4 fm, maximum temperature 200 MeV) as an ideal gas of massless SU(3) gluons. For the expanding medium, they consider a 5 GeV trigger parton (corresponding to trigger pT of 3.5 GeV) depositing energy and momentum scaled as [T(t,x)/Tmax]^3, with seven different jet starting angles (phi = 90, 120, 150, 180, 210, 240, 270 degrees) at radius r = 5 fm to model surface emission, and perform Cooper-Frye freeze-out to obtain azimuthal correlations.

## Key numbers
- Jet initial velocity: v_jet = 0.999, with energy loss rate a ≈ -1.3607 GeV/fm for a jet that stops after Δx = 4.5 fm (Bethe-Bloch formalism)
- Maximum temperature of the medium: T_max = 200 MeV
- Gold nucleus radius: r = 6.4 fm
- For the expanding medium: dE/dt_0 = 1 GeV/fm and dM/dt_0 = 1/v dE/dt_0
- Trigger parton energy: 5 GeV, corresponding to trigger pT = 3.5 GeV (assuming fragmenting jet creates particles with ~70% of its energy)
- Jet starting radius for surface emission: r = 5 fm
- Cooper-Frye freeze-out evaluated at pT = 5π T0 ~ 3.14 GeV and y = 0 for different jet velocities (v = 0.58, 0.75, 0.9)
- Associated particle pT values: pT_assoc = 1 GeV and pT_assoc = 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of jet energy loss mechanisms ranging from weak coupling (Neufeld) to strong coupling (AdS/CFT, Noronha) and builds on prior hydrodynamic treatments of jet-medium interactions (Betz 2008). It contradicts the interpretation that the double-peaked away-side structure observed in azimuthal di-hadron correlations (2pc) necessarily arises from Mach cones (MachConeIdea), instead showing that path averaging of distorted wakes can produce such a shape. The results enable further investigation of longitudinal expansion, finite impact parameter, and different freeze-out prescriptions (such as coalescence) to fully understand jet tomography in heavy-ion collisions.
