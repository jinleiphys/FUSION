# Jet Quenching of the Heavy Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma and the Nonadditive Statistics

> Using the Plastino-Plastino (PP) equation, we calculate transport coefficients of the heavy-quarks traversing inside the quark-gluon plasma, and generalize their relationship with differential energy loss. The PP equation indicates anomalous diffusion of the probe particles and yields a quasi-exponential stationary distribution obtained also from the nonadditive statistics proposed by C. Tsallis. We estimate energy loss in a nonadditive quark-gluon medium, and calculate the jet-quenching parameter ($\hat{q}$) for the PP dynamics. With the help of the estimate of $\hat{q}$, we calculate the nuclear suppression factor ($R_{\text{AA}}$) of the heavy-quarks passing through a nonadditive quark-gluon plasma using the model proposed by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev. In many a case, the parameters in the analysis are fixed from the experimental results to minimize arbitrariness. There is a good agreement between the theoretical calculation and experimental $R_{\text{AA}}$ data, indicating that fast heavy-quarks may be subjected to anomalous diffusion inside the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Plastino-Plastino (PP) equation, which describes anomalous diffusion of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), yields theoretical predictions for the nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) that agree well with experimental data, but this agreement depends on fixing parameters from experimental results and assumes the specific values q_h = q_b = 1.10, q = 1.002, T_h = 0.4 GeV, and T_b = 0.2 GeV.

## Method

The authors start from the generalized Boltzmann transport equation (gBTE) with a q-deformed molecular chaos hypothesis, apply the Landau kinetic approximation to derive the Plastino-Plastino (PP) equation, and compute the PP drag and diffusion coefficients using QCD matrix elements for heavy quark scattering with light quarks and gluons. They then generalize the Rafelski-Walton formula to relate differential energy loss to the PP transport coefficients, compute the jet-quenching parameter (q_hat) for both collisional and radiative processes, and finally calculate the nuclear suppression factor (R_AA) using the Dokshitzer-Kharzeev (DK) formula.

## Key numbers

- The entropic parameters used are q_h = q_b = 1.10 for the heavy quark and medium distributions, and q = 1.002 for the generalized molecular chaos, with temperatures T_h = 0.4 GeV and T_b = 0.2 GeV.
- The Plastino-Plastino energy loss for charm quarks (mass m = 1.5 GeV) starts surpassing the Fokker-Planck counterpart at around 2 GeV momentum of the incoming charm quark.
- The charm quark mass is taken as m = 1.5 GeV, and the longitudinal momentum is P_L = 10 GeV for the jet-quenching parameter calculations.
- The parameters for the vacuum D-meson cross-section are b = 1.7, n = 11, and the path length is ell = 3 fm for the R_AA calculation.

## Context

This work extends the Rafelski-Walton (1995) relationship connecting transport coefficients and energy loss to the nonadditive statistics framework, and generalizes previous estimates by Bhattacharyya et al. (2021) that used the relaxation time approximation. The authors compare their Plastino-Plastino results with the standard Fokker-Planck dynamics from Svetitsky (1988), and use the Dokshitzer-Kharzeev (1998) formula to connect the jet-quenching parameter to the nuclear suppression factor. This enables future studies of anomalous diffusion effects on heavy quark observables in heavy-ion collisions.
