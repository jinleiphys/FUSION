# Influence of vector interactions on the hadron-quark/gluon phase transition

> The hadron-quark/gluon phase transition is studied in the two-phase model. As a further study of our previous work, both the isoscalar and isovector vector interactions are included in the Polyakov loop modified Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (PNJL) for the quark phase. The relevance of the exchange (Fock) terms is stressed and suitably accounted for. The calculation shows that the isovector vector interaction delays the phase transition to higher densities and the range of the mixed phase correspondingly shrinks. Meanwhile the asymmetry parameter of quark matter in the mixed phase decreases with the strengthening of this interaction channel. This leads to some possible observation signals being weakened, although still present. We show that these can be rather general effects of a repulsion in the quark phase due to the symmetry energy. This is also confirmed by a simpler calculation with the MIT--Bag model. However, the asymmetry parameter of quark matter is slightly enhanced with the inclusion of the isoscalar vector interaction, but the phase transition will be moved to higher densities. The largest uncertainty on the phase transition lies in the undetermined coupling constants of the vector interactions. In this respect new data on the mixed phase obtained from Heavy Ion Collisions at Intermediate Energies appear very important.

## Key claim

The isovector vector interaction in the quark phase delays the hadron-quark phase transition to higher densities and shrinks the mixed phase range, while the isoscalar vector interaction also moves the transition to higher densities but slightly enhances the asymmetry parameter of quark matter; however, the largest uncertainty on the phase transition lies in the undetermined coupling constants of the vector interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a two-phase model coupling a nonlinear Walecka model (NLρδ) for hadronic matter with a Polyakov loop modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for quark matter, including both isoscalar and isovector vector interaction channels. The Hartree-Fock approximation was used to account for exchange (Fock) terms, which were shown to effectively renormalize the coupling constants, and the Gibbs conditions (thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium) were applied to construct the mixed phase under baryon number and isospin conservation. Additional calculations with an Iso-MIT-Bag model were performed to confirm the generality of the results.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry parameter α = 0.2 was chosen for asymmetric matter calculations, with the largest possible value in heavy-ion collisions being α = 0.227 for 238U+238U collisions.
- The PNJL model parameters used were Λ = 651 MeV, G = 5.04 GeV⁻², and m_{u,d} = 5.5 MeV, fitted to chiral condensate, fπ, and Mπ.
- The Polyakov potential parameter T₀ was set to 210 MeV, with the deconfinement transition temperature from lattice QCD given as T^c = 173 ± 8 MeV.
- For the MIT-Bag model, a Bag constant B = (160 MeV)⁴ was chosen, and the ρ-coupling was fixed by f_{ρ,N} = g_{ρ,N}²/m_ρ² = 3.15 fm² from the hadron sector.
- The isoscalar vector coupling ratio r_ω = 0.2 was used, close to the value arising from exchange terms of scalar channels.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Shao et al., 2011) by including both isoscalar and isovector vector interactions in the PNJL model for the quark phase, which had previously only considered scalar channels. The results are consistent with findings from lattice QCD (Karsch, 2001; Kaczmarek, 2005) and other effective model studies (Costa, 2010; Carignano, 2010), and the isospin distillation effect observed here could be probed at planned facilities such as FAIR at GSI-Darmstadt and NICA at JINR-Dubna. The work enables future experimental constraints on the vector coupling constants from heavy-ion collision data at intermediate energies.
