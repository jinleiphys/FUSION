# Poles from the conserved kinetic equation: The emerging gradient structure and causality riddle of relativistic hydrodynamics

> In this work, the poles and the resulting dispersion spectra from the relativistic kinetic equation have been analyzed with the help of a proposed collision kernel that conserves both the energy-momentum tensor and particle current by construction. The dispersion relations, which originally come out in the form of logarithmic divergences, in the long wavelength limit exhibit the systematic gradient structure of the relativistic hydrodynamics. The key result is that, in the derivative expansion series, the spatial gradients appear in perfect unison with the temporal gradients in the non-local relaxation operator like forms. It is then shown that this dispersion structure, including non-local temporal derivatives, is essential for the preservation of causality of the theory truncated at any desired order.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dispersion relations derived from a relativistic kinetic equation with a fully conserved collision kernel naturally produce a gradient expansion where spatial and temporal derivatives appear in a balanced, non-local operator form; this structure is essential for preserving causality when the theory is truncated at any finite order, contradicting the notion that causality issues arise from the microscopic theory itself rather than from truncation choices.

## Method

The author proposes a linearized collision kernel for the relativistic Boltzmann equation that conserves both the energy-momentum tensor and particle current by construction, expressed in terms of dissipative field corrections (Δn, Δε, ΔW^μ) without imposing any hydrodynamic frame choice. The kinetic equation is solved in Fourier space, and the resulting system of simultaneous equations for the field perturbations yields a determinant whose zeros give the pole structure. The poles are analyzed in the long-wavelength (hydrodynamic) limit by expanding the logarithmic angular integrals in powers of the wave number k, producing infinite series for the shear, diffusion, and sound dispersion relations.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of poles from relativistic kinetic theory (Romatschke 2015, Bajec 2025, Brants 2024) by introducing a collision kernel that conserves both particle current and energy-momentum without frame constraints, building on the conserved linearized kernel of Rocha (2021). The resulting gradient structure with non-local temporal operators (1+iωτ_R) supports the argument of Mitra (2024) that causality issues in hydrodynamic theories arise from truncation schemes rather than from the underlying microscopic theory. The analysis enables the construction of causal hydrodynamic theories by showing that the non-local relaxation operators must be treated as whole entities or recast into local form via additional non-fluid degrees of freedom, as discussed by Hoult (2023) and Bhattacharyya (2024, 2025).
