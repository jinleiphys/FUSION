# From Lead to Helium: Discovery Potential for Jet Quenching in the Smallest Collision Systems

> We present perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) predictions for the modification to the yield of high-momentum particles in very light ion collisions - ${}^{10}\mathrm{B} + {}^{10}\mathrm{B}$, ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li} + {}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$, ${}^{4}\mathrm{He} + {}^{4}\mathrm{He}$, and ${}^{3}\mathrm{He} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ - both with and without medium-induced energy loss. We show that there is non-trivial suppression expected from our partonic energy loss model in symmetric systems from ${}^{208}\mathrm{Pb} + {}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ to ${}^{3}\mathrm{He} + {}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and in asymmetric systems $A + B$, and that the energy loss scales approximately with $(\sqrt{A B})^{1 / 3}$. Further, we find that deep inelastic scattering measurements in ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$ tightly constrain the nPDF baseline, making these isotopes a particularly clean environment for observing final-state partonic energy loss induced by the formation of a quark-gluon plasma in these very small systems.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that jet quenching (final-state partonic energy loss) can be discovered in very light ion collisions as small as lithium-lithium and helium-3-helium-3, provided that the nuclear modification factor can be distinguished from initial-state nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) effects. The authors hedge that the discovery potential depends on overcoming experimental and theoretical difficulties in asymmetric systems, and that their predictions are conservative with respect to the magnitude of suppression.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations at next-to-leading order using the EPPS21 nPDF sets and the DSS fragmentation functions to compute the nPDF-only baseline nuclear modification factor. They then applied a pQCD-based partonic energy loss model that includes both collisional and radiative energy loss channels, with the effective strong coupling calibrated to large-system data from lead-lead collisions at the LHC. The model uses IP-Glasma initial conditions with event-by-event fluctuating geometries, including Woods-Saxon distributions for lithium and boron and variational Monte Carlo configurations for helium-3 and helium-4, and computes the nuclear modification factor for pion production in minimum-bias and 0-10% centrality collisions.

## Key numbers

- Expected significance for distinguishing energy loss from nPDF-only in minimum-bias collisions: 2.3 sigma for lithium-lithium, 1.7 sigma for helium-3-helium-3, 2.7 sigma for boron-10-boron-10, and 1.5 sigma for helium-4-helium-4, evaluated at 9.6 GeV to 12 GeV transverse momentum.
- Expected significance for 0-10% centrality collisions: 3.0 sigma for lithium-lithium, 2.6 sigma for helium-3-helium-3, 3.5 sigma for boron-10-boron-10, and 2.3 sigma for helium-4-helium-4, assuming 10% experimental uncertainty.
- The effective strong coupling extracted from fits to lead-lead data is alpha_s^eff = 0.37(11:8) at LHC energies.
- The jet transport coefficient normalized to temperature cubed is q-hat / T^3 = 11 +/- 3.
- Lithium-lithium collisions yield approximately 50 particles; helium-3-helium-3 collisions yield approximately 30 particles.
- The energy loss scales approximately with (sqrt(A B))^(1/3), where A and B are the mass numbers of the colliding nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the recent discovery of high-transverse-momentum hadron suppression in oxygen-oxygen collisions at the LHC (CMS 2025) to even smaller systems, motivated by the missing signature of jet quenching in asymmetric small systems such as proton-lead and deuteron-gold collisions (ALICE 2014, ATLAS 2022, CMS 2025). The authors use the EPPS21 nPDF set (Eskola 2021) which has larger uncertainties and more suppression than other sets like TUJU21 (Helenius 2021), nNNPDF3.0 (AbdulKhalek 2022), and nCTEQ15 (Kusina 2015), making their predictions conservative for discovery potential. The results suggest that lithium-lithium and helium-3-helium-3 collisions at the LHC could test the emergence of collective behavior and quark-gluon plasma formation in systems of O(10) particles, analogous to recent observations in cold fermionic atom systems (Brandstetter 2023).
