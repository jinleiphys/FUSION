# Removal of Spurious Admixture in a Self-consistent Theory of Adiabatic Large Amplitude Collective Motion

> In this article we analyse, for a simple model, the properties of a practical implementation of a fully self-consistent theory of adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion using the local harmonic approach. We show how we can deal with contaminations arising from spurious modes, caused by standard simplifying approximations. This is done both at zero and finite angular momentum. We analyse in detail the nature of the collective coordinate in regions where they cross spurious modes and mixing is largest.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spurious admixtures arising from Nambu-Goldstone modes in the Local Harmonic Approximation can be removed by constructing approximate conjugate momentum operators and applying a projection technique, but only when the covariant derivative terms in the Local Harmonic Equation are neglected as a simplifying approximation.

## Method

The authors implement the Local Harmonic Approximation for adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion using a single j-shell Pairing Plus Quadrupole model within the Hartree-Bogoliubov framework. They construct a projection operator by first approximating the conjugate momentum for each spurious coordinate (particle number, angular momentum components) via singular value decomposition of the potential matrix, then diagonalizing the overlap matrix to enforce canonical orthogonality between coordinates and momenta. The projection is applied to both the potential and mass matrices before solving the QRPA eigenvalue problem at each point along the collective path.

## Key numbers

- Model space: j=9/2 shell with N=4 fermions, interaction strengths kappa=1 and chi=0.333 for the axial symmetric case
- Pairing strength chi varied from 0.28 to 0.34 in the investigation of collective potential dependence
- For the rotating case, angular momentum range 0 <= J_x <= 6, with pairing field collapsing to zero at J_x=3
- Quadrupole strength kappa=10 for the finite rotational velocity calculations

## Context

This work extends the Local Harmonic Approximation method reviewed by Dang, Klein, and Walet (2000) from earlier Hartree-Fock applications to include pairing via the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field, building on prior studies of two-level crossing models by Nakatsukasa and Walet (1998) and O(4) models by Kobayasi, Nakatsukasa, Matsuo, and Matsuyanagi (2000). The single j-shell Pairing Plus Quadrupole model serves as a bridge between schematic models and the semi-realistic calculations using two major shells for neutrons and protons performed by Almehed and Walet (2003, 2004). The method enables future application to rotating nuclei at finite angular momentum without contamination from spurious modes.
