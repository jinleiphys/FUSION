# Comment on "K^- d -> pi Sigma n reactions and structure of the Lambda(1405)" by S. Ohnishi, Y. Ikeda, T. Hyodo, E. Hiyama, W. Weise

> We strongly doubt the claimed priority of this work as the first full three-body calculation of the considered reaction.

## Key claim

The authors strongly doubt the claimed priority of Ohnishi et al. (2014) as the first full three-body calculation of the \(K^- d \to \pi \Sigma n\) reaction, noting that a calculation of the same reaction within the same theoretical framework and with the same goal was published earlier in Révai (2014) without being cited.

## Method

The comment compares the theoretical frameworks and \(\bar{K}N\) potentials used in the two calculations, evaluating the quality of their reproduction of experimental data. It identifies that the original contributions of Ohnishi et al. (2014) relative to Révai (2014) are the inclusion of higher partial waves and an energy-dependent potential. The comment also questions the validity of using nonrelativistic AGS equations for an initial kaon momentum of 1 GeV/c, arguing that relativistic corrections should have been addressed before publication.

## Key numbers

- Initial kaon momentum of 1 GeV/c is used in the calculation by Ohnishi et al. (2014), which the comment argues makes the use of nonrelativistic AGS equations highly questionable.

## Context

This comment extends a priority dispute regarding the first full three-body calculation of the \(K^- d \to \pi \Sigma n\) reaction, directly contradicting the claim made by Ohnishi et al. (2014). It builds on prior work by Révai (2014), which reported a calculation of the same reaction within the same theoretical framework and with the same goal. The comment also references a discussion on approximations used in the potentials of Ohnishi et al. (2014) that can be found in Révai and Shevchenko (2014).
