# Chiral pions in a magnetic background

> We investigate the modification of the pion self-energy at finite temperature due to its interaction with a low-density, isospin-symmetric nuclear medium embedded in a constant magnetic background. To one loop, for fixed temperature and density, we find that the pion effective mass increases with the magnetic field. For the $π^{-}$, interestingly, this happens solely due to the trivial Landau quantization shift $\sim |eB|$, since the real part of the self-energy is negative in this case. In a scenario in which other charged particle species are present and undergo an analogous trivial shift, the relevant behavior of the effective mass might be determined essentially by the real part of the self-energy. In this case, we find that the pion mass decreases by $\sim 10%$ for a magnetic field $|eB|\sim m_π^2$, which favors pion condensation at high density and low temperatures.

## Key claim

The pion effective mass increases with magnetic field for fixed temperature and density, but this increase for the π⁻ is solely due to the trivial Landau quantization shift ∼ |eB|, since the real part of the self-energy is negative. If other charged particle species undergo an analogous trivial shift, the relevant behavior might be determined by the real part of the self-energy, in which case the pion mass decreases by ∼10% for |eB| ∼ m_π², favoring pion condensation at high density and low temperatures.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop pion self-energy in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature and low density, using relativistic chiral perturbation theory in a constant magnetic background. They evaluate the Weinberg-Tomozawa and one-pion exchange contributions separately for each pion charge eigenstate, employing the lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation for the proton propagator in the Landau gauge. The effective pion mass is obtained by solving the dispersion relation at vanishing three-momentum, including the Landau quantization shift.

## Key numbers

- For a magnetic field |eB| ∼ m_π², the pion mass decreases by ∼10% when the trivial Landau shift is subtracted.
- The effective mass is computed at saturation density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Magnetic field values considered range from eB/m_π² = 0.01 to 10.
- Calculations are performed at zero temperature and at T = 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends a previous treatment of the fermion self-energy in relativistic chiral perturbation theory (Colucci et al., 2013) to the pion self-energy in a magnetic background. It addresses the long-standing problem of pion condensation introduced by Migdal (1973), where at high density the electron chemical potential grows until it becomes favorable for a neutron to turn into a proton and a negatively charged pion. The results suggest that strong magnetic fields, such as those found in compact stars and supernovae, could lower the pion effective mass and thus favor pion condensation, motivating further investigation of neutron star matter and supernova phenomenology.
