# WKB to all orders and the accuracy of the semiclassical quantization

> We perform a systematic WKB expansion to all orders for a one-dimensional system with potential $V(x)=U_0/\cos^2{(αx)}$. We are able to sum the series to the exact energy spectrum. Then we show that at any finite order the error of the WKB approximation measured in the natural units of the mean energy level spacing does not go to zero when the quantum number goes to infinity. Therefore we make the general conclusion that the semiclassical approximations fail to predict the individual energy levels within a vanishing fraction of the mean energy level spacing.

# WKB to all orders and the accuracy of the semiclassical quantization

## Key claim

For the one-dimensional potential \(V(x)=U_0/\cos^2{(\alpha x)}\), the WKB series can be summed to all orders and converges to the exact energy spectrum; however, at any finite truncation order, the error in the predicted energy levels measured in units of the mean level spacing does not vanish as the quantum number goes to infinity, implying that semiclassical approximations cannot predict individual energy levels within a vanishing fraction of the mean spacing.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic WKB expansion to all orders for the Pöschl–Teller potential \(V(x)=U_0/\cos^2{(\alpha x)}\). Using the recursion relation of Bender, Olaussen and Wang (1977), they compute the even-order WKB integrals \(\oint d\sigma_{2k}\) explicitly by substituting \(z=\tan(\alpha x)\) and evaluating the resulting contour integrals. They sum the infinite series to recover the exact analytic energy spectrum known from the Schrödinger equation, and then evaluate the error of any finite-order WKB approximation relative to the exact mean level spacing.

## Key numbers

- The exact energy spectrum is \(E_{\nu}^{ex} = A [(\nu + 1/2) + (1/2)\sqrt{1+B^2}]^2\), where \(A = \alpha^2 \hbar^2/(2m)\) and \(B = \sqrt{8mU_0}/(\alpha \hbar)\), with \(\nu = 0,1,2,\dots\).
- The torus (Bohr–Sommerfeld–Maslov) quantization gives \(E_{\nu}^{tor} = A[(\nu + 1/2) + (1/2)B]^2\).
- The fourth-order WKB approximation is \(E_{\nu}^{(4)} = A[(\nu + 1/2) + (1/2)B + 1/(4B) - 1/(16B^3)]^2\).
- The relative error for an \(N\)th-order WKB approximation in the limit \(\nu \to \infty\) is \((E_{\nu}^{ex} - E_{\nu}^{(N)})/\Delta E_{\nu} \sim (1/2) \binom{1/2}{N+1} B^{-(2N+1)}\) as \(B \to \infty\) (i.e., \(\hbar \to 0\)).

## Context

This work extends the observation by Prosen and Robnik (1993) and Graffi, Manfredi and Salasnich (1994) that the leading-order semiclassical approximation fails to predict individual energy levels within a vanishing fraction of the mean spacing. By summing the WKB series to all orders for a solvable potential, the authors show that this failure persists at every finite order of the WKB expansion. The result implies that semiclassical methods cannot capture the fine structure of energy spectra (e.g., level repulsion), although they remain useful for long-range spectral statistics such as the \(\Delta(L)\) spectral rigidity of Berry (1985). The work also casts doubt on the rigorous validity of the Berry–Tabor (1977) prediction of Poissonian statistics for integrable systems, noting known counterexamples by Bleher et al. (1993).
