# A new effective interaction for the trapped Fermi gas

> We apply the configuration-interaction method to calculate the spectra of two-component Fermi systems in a harmonic trap, studying the convergence of the method at the unitary interaction limit. We find that for a fixed regularization of the two-body interaction the convergence is exponential or better in the truncation parameter of the many-body space. However, the conventional regularization is found to have poor convergence in the regularization parameter, with an error that scales as a low negative power of this parameter. We propose a new regularization of the two-body interaction that produces exponential convergence for systems of three and four particles. From the systematics, we estimate the ground-state energy of the four-particle system to be (5.05 +- 0.024)hbar omega.

## Key claim

The configuration-interaction (CI) method can achieve exponential convergence for trapped Fermi gases at unitarity, but only if the two-body interaction is regularized with a new separable effective interaction that exactly reproduces all low-energy s-wave eigenvalues of the two-particle system, rather than using the conventional renormalized contact interaction which converges only as a low negative power of the regularization parameter.

## Method

The authors applied the configuration-interaction (CI) method using a harmonic oscillator single-particle basis truncated to shells N=0 through N_max, with two-body matrix elements computed via Talmi-Moshinsky brackets. They constructed two different regularized interactions in a truncated q-space of relative-coordinate s-wave oscillator states: a conventional renormalized contact interaction whose strength is fixed by requiring the two-particle ground-state energy to match the exact unitary value, and a new separable effective interaction whose parameters are chosen to reproduce all q+1 lowest s-wave eigenvalues of the two-particle unitary spectrum exactly. The many-particle Hamiltonian was constructed and diagonalized using the nuclear shell model code oxbash for A=3 and A=4 systems.

## Key numbers

- For the A=3 ground state (exact energy 4.2727243... hbar omega), the new interaction yields an extrapolated energy of (4.274 ± 0.004) hbar omega, an accuracy of 0.1%
- For the A=3 first excited L=0 state (exact energy 4.6662... hbar omega), the new interaction yields an extrapolated energy of (4.646 ± 0.025) hbar omega, an accuracy better than 0.6%
- For the A=4 L=0 ground state, the new interaction gives an estimated energy of (5.051 ± 0.024) hbar omega, with an upper bound of E^(3)_7 = 5.075 hbar omega
- The conventional renormalized contact interaction converges in q as ~ q^{-alpha} with alpha in the range 0.5-1.5 for A=3; for the A=2 excited system the analytic exponent is 1/2
- The new interaction shows exponential convergence in q, with average decrease rates of 3.28 (A=3 ground state), 1.83 (A=3 excited state), and 2.14 (A=4 ground state)

## Context

This work extends the configuration-interaction method, widely used in atomic, molecular, and nuclear spectroscopy, to cold trapped Fermi gases at the unitary limit. The authors compare with fixed-node Monte Carlo estimates for the A=4 system by Chang (2007) giving (5.1 ± 0.1) hbar omega and by Stetcu (2007) giving (5.069 ± 0.009) hbar omega, finding agreement within uncertainties. The new effective interaction has connections to Suzuki's unitary transformation method (Suzuki 1982) used in nuclear physics, but is simpler due to its separable form. The results suggest that leading-order effective field theory treatments will require derivative terms to accurately model trapped fermion systems, and the method could be extended to interaction strengths away from unitarity and to uniform systems using plane-wave bases.
