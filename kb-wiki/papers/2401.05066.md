# Exploring the QCD phase diagram with three flavors of Möbius domain wall fermions

> We present an update on the study of the QCD phase transition with 3 flavors of Möbius domain wall fermions at zero baryon density. We performed simulations on lattices of size $36^3\times12\times16$ and $24^3\times12\times32$ with a variety of quark masses at a fixed lattice spacing $a=0.1361(20)$ fm, which correspond to a temperature 121(2) MeV. By analyzing the chiral condensate, chiral susceptibilitities and Binder cumulant on $36^3\times12\times16$ lattices together with the result obtained from our previous study on $24^3\times12\times16$ lattices, we identified a crossover occurring at quark mass around $m_q^{\mathrm{\overline {MS}}}(2\, \mathrm{GeV}) \sim 3-4$ MeV for this temperature. Besides, we show the effects of residual chiral symmetry breaking on chiral condensate and chiral susceptibilities between $L_s=16$ and 32.

## Key claim

The study identifies a crossover transition (not a first-order or second-order phase transition) for three-flavor QCD at a temperature of 121(2) MeV, with the transition occurring at a quark mass around 3-4 MeV in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV; the authors hedge that if a first-order region exists in the Columbia plot, the critical quark mass would be smaller than 4 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three degenerate flavors of Möbius domain wall fermions using the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action at fixed gauge coupling β=4.0, corresponding to lattice spacing a=0.1361(20) fm. They generated ensembles on 36³×12×16 lattices with 7 bare quark masses and 24³×12×32 lattices with 5 bare quark masses, each with approximately 20,000 trajectories, and analyzed the chiral condensate, disconnected and total chiral susceptibilities, and Binder cumulant to determine the nature and location of the transition.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1361(20) fm, corresponding to temperature T = 121(2) MeV
- Transition quark mass from disconnected chiral susceptibility: m_q^MSbar(2 GeV) ~ 4 MeV
- Transition quark mass from total chiral susceptibility: m_q^MSbar(2 GeV) ~ 3 MeV
- Residual mass for L_s=16: m_res = 0.00613(9) (mass-independent extrapolation)
- Residual mass for L_s=32: m_res = 0.00325(3) (mass-independent extrapolation)
- Lattice volumes: 36³×12×16 and 24³×12×32, with comparison to previous 24³×12×16 results

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the N_f=3 QCD phase transition using Möbius domain wall fermions (Zhang 2022) and addresses the long-standing question of the order of the chiral phase transition in the lower-left corner of the Columbia plot. The results are consistent with findings using Wilson and staggered fermions, which also suggest a crossover or very weak first-order transition, and with recent staggered studies (Cuteri 2021) that found no evidence of a phase transition in the pion mass range 80-140 MeV. The study enables future work on larger volumes (48³×12×16) and lower temperatures (N_t=14, corresponding to 104(2) MeV) to directly probe for a possible first-order region.
