# Microscopic description of fission in nobelium isotopes with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> Constrained mean-field calculations, based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional, have been carried out to describe fission in the isotopes $^{250-260}$No. The even-even isotopes have been considered within the standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliobov (HFB) framework while for the odd-mass ones the Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) has been employed. Ground state quantum numbers and deformations, pairing energies, one-neutron separation energies, inner and outer barrier heights as well as fission isomer excitation energies are given. Fission paths, collective masses and zero-point quantum vibrational and rotational corrections are used to compute the systematic of the spontaneous fission half-lives t$_\mathrm{SF}$ both for even-even and odd-mass nuclei. Though there exists a strong variance of the predicted fission rates with respect to the details involved in their computation, it is shown that both the specialization energy and the pairing quenching effects, taken into account within the self-consistent HFB-EFA blocking procedure, lead to larger t$_\mathrm{SF}$ values in odd-mass nuclei as compared with their even-even neighbors. Alpha decay lifetimes have also been computed using a parametrization of the Viola-Seaborg formula. The high quality of the Gogny-D1M functional regarding nuclear masses leads to a very good reproduction of $Q_α$ values and consequently of lifetimes.

## Key claim

The Gogny-D1M energy density functional, combined with the Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) for odd-mass nuclei, predicts systematically larger spontaneous fission half-lives for odd-mass nobelium isotopes compared to their even-even neighbors, due to both specialization energy and pairing quenching effects; however, the authors note a "strong variance" of the predicted fission rates with respect to the computational details (choice of collective mass prescription and zero-point energy parameter E0).

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M functional were performed for even-even 250-260No isotopes, while odd-mass isotopes were treated using the Equal Filling Approximation (HFB-EFA) which preserves time-reversal invariance by averaging over blocked quasiparticle states and their Kramers partners. Fission paths were obtained by constraining the axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole operators, and spontaneous fission half-lives were computed via the WKB formalism using both Adiabatic Time Dependent (ATD) and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) collective masses with zero-point rotational and vibrational corrections. Alpha decay half-lives were computed using the Viola-Seaborg formula with Qα values from the calculated binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Inner barrier height for 252No: 10.75 MeV (at Q20 = 30 b)
- Outer barrier height for 252No: 4.23 MeV (at Q20 = 70 b)
- Fission isomer excitation energy for 252No: 1.38 MeV (at Q20 = 52 b)
- Inner barrier height for 253No (K0 = 9/2 path): 11.67 MeV (at Q20 = 30 b)
- Outer barrier height for 253No (K0 = 9/2 path): 5.42 MeV (at Q20 = 70 b)
- Fission isomer excitation energy for 253No (K0 = 9/2 path): 2.22 MeV (at Q20 = 52 b)
- For 252No with E0 = 1.0 MeV: log10 tSF = 8.7919 s (ATD scheme) and log10 tSF = 6.9928 s (GCM scheme)
- For 252No with E0 = 1.5 MeV: log10 tSF = 5.7252 s (ATD scheme) and log10 tSF = 4.7378 s (GCM scheme)
- Ratio of ATD to GCM collective mass at Q20 = 20 b for 252No: B_ATD/B_GCM = 1.77
- Ground state quadrupole moment for 252No and 253No: Q20 = 16 b

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Gogny-D1M fission studies on Ra, U, and Pu isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2014-2016) to the nobelium region (Z=102), and represents the first EDF calculations of fission in odd-mass No isotopes. The results enable comparison with experimental data on No isotopes (such as the known 9/2- ground state of 253No) and provide a systematic theoretical baseline for spontaneous fission half-lives in the superheavy element region, though the strong dependence on the choice of collective mass (ATD vs GCM) and the E0 parameter highlights the need for improved theoretical treatment of collective inertias and zero-point energies.
