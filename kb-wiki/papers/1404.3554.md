# The relativistic Green's function model in charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering at MINER$ν$A kinematics

> The analysis of charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final-state interactions. We compare the results of the relativistic Green's function model with the data recently published by the MINER$ν$A Collaboration. The model is able to describe both MINER$ν$A and MiniBooNE data.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, using the standard nucleon axial mass value of 1.03 GeV/c² without any enhancement, can describe both MINERνA and MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino and antineutrino scattering data, though the agreement with MiniBooNE data is better than that of other impulse-approximation-based models.

## Method

The authors computed CCQE neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on a hydrocarbon (CH) target using the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which describes final-state interactions via a complex relativistic optical potential whose imaginary part redistributes flux among all open channels. Two parametrizations of the optical potential were employed: the Energy-Dependent and A-Independent (EDAI) potential and the more recent Democratic (DEM) global parametrization. The calculated cross sections were folded with the MINERνA neutrino and antineutrino fluxes and compared to the published MINERνA data, using the standard axial mass M_A = 1.03 GeV/c².

## Key numbers

- Neutrino CCQE flux-averaged total cross section per nucleon: RGF-EDAI = 0.97 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², RGF-DEM = 0.91 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², compared to experimental value 0.93 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.11 (syst) × 10⁻³⁸ cm²
- Antineutrino CCQE flux-averaged total cross section per nucleon: RGF-EDAI = 0.71 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², RGF-DEM = 0.68 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², compared to experimental value 0.604 ± 0.008 (stat) ± 0.075 (syst) × 10⁻³⁸ cm²
- For antineutrino scattering, differences between the two RGF optical potential parametrizations are always less than 10% across the MINERνA kinematic range
- The RGF-EDAI and RGF-DEM cross sections differ mainly at low four-momentum transfer squared, Q²_QE less than about 0.5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGF model studies of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering (Meucci et al., 2003-2014) and CCQE MiniBooNE neutrino scattering (Meucci et al., 2011-2013) to the higher-energy MINERνA kinematics (1.5 to 10 GeV). The results contrast with the relativistic mean field (RMF) and superscaling approximation (SuSA) models of Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2014), which also describe MINERνA data with M_A = 1.03 GeV/c² but underestimate MiniBooNE data; the RGF model gives larger cross sections at MiniBooNE kinematics due to inelastic channels recovered by the imaginary part of the optical potential. The work enables further investigation into the role of multinucleon processes and the need for improved optical potential parametrizations satisfying dispersion relations.
