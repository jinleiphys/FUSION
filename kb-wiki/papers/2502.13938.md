# Electromagnetic Radiation from High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
Electromagnetic radiation (real and virtual photons) emitted in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can serve as a pristine probe of the quark-gluon plasma because photons suffer little final state interactions, but the interpretation requires careful treatment of non-equilibrium effects across all collision stages. The paper argues that while thermal emission rates from equilibrated matter are well understood, the pre-equilibrium phase contributes significantly to the total photon yield (approximately 30% or more at intermediate transverse momentum) and must be included consistently to match experimental data.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical formalism for computing photon and dilepton emission rates from strongly interacting matter, starting from the finite-temperature retarded photon self-energy expressed as a current-current correlator. For equilibrium systems, rates are derived from the imaginary part of the photon self-energy using either field-theoretic methods (Eq. 1-2) or kinetic theory with matrix elements for partonic processes such as Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation. For non-equilibrium systems, the author describes a multistage approach combining IP-Glasma initial conditions, the KøMPøST kinetic theory framework for the pre-hydrodynamic phase, and viscous fluid dynamics, with photon rates corrected for shear and bulk viscosity effects using Chapman-Enskog or Grad methods.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of electromagnetic to strong coupling is α_EM/α_s << 1, justifying the use of photons as clean probes.
- Photon production rates at NLO in the strong coupling show an enhancement of approximately 20% over LO results for relevant values of α_s.
- At RHIC (Au+Au at 200 A GeV, 20-40% centrality), non-equilibrium photon spectra from different models differ by up to an order of magnitude at p_T ≈ 1 GeV and roughly a factor of two at p_T ≈ 3 GeV.
- At RHIC (0-20% centrality, 200 A GeV), pre-equilibrium, thermal, and pQCD photon contributions are comparable at p_T ≈ 3 GeV.
- At LHC (Pb+Pb at 2760 A GeV, 0-20% centrality), pre-equilibrium photons represent ≥30% of the net photon yield at p_T ≈ 3.5 GeV.
- The ALICE Collaboration released data on direct photons from Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, which agree with model predictions within statistical uncertainties.
- The direct photon elliptic flow v_2 measured by PHENIX at RHIC is underestimated by the multistage model, while at LHC the deviation has considerably less statistical significance.
- Early magnetic fields in non-central collisions reach B ≈ 10^18 G, the strongest known in Nature.

## Context
This review extends the long line of work on electromagnetic probes of the quark-gluon plasma dating back to Shuryak (1978), Kajantie and Miettinen (1981), and Domokos and Goldman (1980), who first proposed using lepton pair spectra to extract an effective temperature analogous to black body radiation. The paper builds on the development of Hard Thermal Loop resummation by Kapusta, Baier, and collaborators for curing collinear singularities in photon rates, and on the complete leading-order photon production rates from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) with NLO results from Ghiglieri, Moore, and Teaney (2013). The multistage modeling approach follows the work of Schenke, Tribedy, and Venugopalan (2012) for IP-Glasma initial conditions, Kurkela and collaborators (2018) for KøMPøST kinetic theory, and Gale, Jeon, and Schenke (2013) for viscous hydrodynamics. The "direct photon puzzle", the tension between measured photon spectra and elliptic flow simultaneously, remains an open problem that has driven re-examination of emission rates near the cross-over temperature and the development of relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions.
