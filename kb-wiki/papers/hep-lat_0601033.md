# Finite volume corrections to pi-pi scattering

> Lattice QCD studies of hadron-hadron interactions are performed by computing the energy levels of the system in a finite box. The shifts in energy levels proportional to inverse powers of the volume are related to scattering parameters in a model independent way. In addition, there are non-universal exponentially suppressed corrections that distort this relation. These terms are proportional to exp(-m_pi L) and become relevant as the chiral limit is approached. In this paper we report on a one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation of the leading exponential corrections in the case of I=2 pi-pi scattering near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper reports a one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation of the leading exponential corrections to the universal finite-volume relation for I=2 pi-pi scattering near threshold, showing that these corrections are proportional to exp(-m_pi L) and become relevant as the chiral limit is approached, but are relatively small (approximately 1% for typical current simulation parameters).

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop calculation in two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for the I=2 pi-pi scattering amplitude in a finite cubic box, separating the power-law volume dependence (which gives the Luscher relation) from the exponentially suppressed corrections. They computed the finite-volume loop integrals using Poisson resummation, evaluated the difference between finite-volume sums and infinite-volume integrals for all one-loop diagrams (s-channel, t-channel, u-channel, and wavefunction renormalization), and extracted the exponential correction term Delta(k cot delta) to the modified Luscher relation.

## Key numbers

- The exponential correction to k cot delta(s) near threshold is given by: Delta(k cot delta) = -(m_pi / sqrt(2 pi)) * sum_{n=|n| != 0} c(n) * exp(-n m_pi L) / sqrt(n m_pi L) * [1 - (227/24)/(n m_pi L) + ...], where c(n) are multiplicity factors (e.g., c(1)=6, c(sqrt(2))=12, c(sqrt(3))=8)
- For a pion mass m_pi ~ 290 MeV and box size L ~ 2.5 fm, the finite volume correction is approximately 1% of the one-loop value of k cot delta
- The asymptotic expansion of the exponential correction to the scalar loop integral i Delta J_exp(p_s) is: i Delta J_exp(p_s) ~ sqrt(2 pi)/(4 pi)^2 * 1/(m L)^(3/2) * sum c(n) * exp(-n m_pi L)/n^(3/2) * [1 - 5/8/(n m_pi L) + ...]
- The pion mass shift at finite volume is: Delta m_pi^2 / m_pi^2 = 1/(2^(5/2) pi^(3/2)) * (m_pi / L f_pi^2) * sum c(n) * exp(-n m_pi L)/n^(3/2) * 1/sqrt(m_pi L) * [1 + 3/8/(n m_pi L) + ...]

## Context

This work extends the Luscher finite-volume method (Luscher 1986, 1991) for extracting scattering parameters from lattice QCD energy levels by computing the non-universal exponential corrections that become important near the chiral limit. It builds on previous ChPT calculations of finite-volume effects for single-hadron observables (Gasser 1987, Colangelo 2003, Beane 2004) and provides the explicit correction term to be added to the Luscher relation for I=2 pi-pi scattering. The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to correct for these exponential volume effects in simulations approaching physical pion masses, particularly relevant for the work of Beane et al. (2005) who determined the I=2 pi-pi scattering length at m_pi ~ 290 MeV.
