# Dynamical simulation of DCC formation in Bjorken rods

> Using a semi-classical treatment of the linear sigma model, we simulate the dynamical evolution of an initially hot cylindrical rod endowed with a longitudinal Bjorken scaling expansion (a ``Bjorken rod''). The field equation is propagated until full decoupling has occurred and the asymptotic many-body state of free pions is then obtained by a suitable Fourier decomposition of the field and a subsequent stochastic determination of the number of quanta in each elementary mode. The resulting transverse pion spectrum exhibits visible enhancements below 200 MeV due to the parametric amplification caused by the oscillatory relaxation of the chiral order parameter. Ensembles of such final states are subjected to various event-by-event analyses. The factorial moments of the multiplicity distribution suggest that the soft pions are non-statistical. Furthermore, their emission patterns exhibit azimuthal correlations that have a bearing on the domain size in the source. Finally, the distribution of the neutral pion fraction shows a significant broadening for the soft pions which grows steadily as the number of azimuthal segments is increased. All of these features are indicative of disoriented chiral condensates and it may be interesting to apply similar analyses to actual data from high-energy nuclear collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) formation can occur in a more realistic scenario combining longitudinal Bjorken expansion with finite transverse geometry, but the authors caution that the idealized "Bjorken matter" results may overestimate the observable signals, and the finite rod geometry causes faster relaxation than the infinite matter case.

## Method

The authors used a semi-classical treatment of the SU(2) linear sigma model, solving the second-order nonlinear field equation of motion on a cartesian lattice with periodic boundary conditions. They prepared initial field configurations by sampling from thermal ensembles at specified temperatures, then imposed a longitudinal Bjorken scaling expansion by transforming to comoving variables (proper time tau and rapidity eta). For the Bjorken rod scenario, they additionally imposed a Saxon-Woods temperature profile in the transverse plane with surface width w = 0.8 fm and radii R0 of 6 or 10 fm. The asymptotic final state of free pions was obtained by Fourier decomposition of the classical field and stochastic determination of quantum occupancy numbers via coherent state mapping.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum value of the order parameter (pion decay constant) is f_pi = 92 MeV.
- The initial proper time for the expansion is tau_0 = 1 fm/c.
- The lattice spacing used is 0.2 fm in the transverse plane and Delta_eta = 0.2 longitudinally.
- The transverse pion spectrum exhibits visible enhancements below 200 MeV due to parametric amplification.
- For Bjorken matter at T0 = 240 MeV, the softest pions show enhancement up to a factor of two relative to a Bose-Einstein fit.
- The Bose-Einstein fit is performed over the energy interval 200 < E_k < 1000 MeV.
- The rod radii studied are R0 = 6 fm and R0 = 10 fm, with box sides L_x = L_y = 44 fm and L_eta = 8 rapidity units.
- The first complete oscillation of the order parameter in the rod interior takes about 4 fm/c.
- The effective masses in the hot interior are approximately 300 MeV, with free values of 138 MeV for pions and 600 MeV for sigma mesons.

## Context

This work extends earlier DCC studies using the linear sigma model, particularly the exploratory work of Asakawa et al. (1995) who employed a constant longitudinal field with a two-dimensional geometry. The present study provides a more refined initialization procedure with finite correlation length and smooth surface region, enabling quantitative analysis of experimental observables. The results enable comparison with data from high-energy nuclear collision experiments such as those at RHIC, and the analysis methods (factorial moments, azimuthal correlations, neutral pion fraction distributions) can be applied to actual experimental data from the MiniMax and WA98 collaborations.
