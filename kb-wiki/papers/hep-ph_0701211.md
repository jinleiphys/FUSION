# Gluonic phases, vector condensates, and exotic hadrons in dense QCD

> We study the dynamics in phases with vector condensates of gluons (gluonic phases) in dense two-flavor quark matter. These phases yield an example of dynamics in which the Higgs mechanism is provided by condensates of gauge (or gauge plus scalar) fields. Because vacuum expectation values of spatial components of vector fields break the rotational symmetry, it is naturally to have a spontaneous breakdown both of external and internal symmetries in this case. In particular, by using the Ginzburg-Landau approach, we establish the existence of a gluonic phase with both the rotational symmetry and the electromagnetic U(1) being spontaneously broken. In other words, this phase describes an anisotropic medium in which the color and electric superconductivities coexist. It is shown that this phase corresponds to a minimum of the Ginzburg-Landau potential and, unlike the two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase, it does not suffer from the chromomagnetic instability. The dual (confinement) description of its dynamics is developed and it is shown that there are light exotic vector hadrons in the spectrum, some of which condense. Because most of the initial symmetries in this system are spontaneously broken, its dynamics is very rich.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that a gluonic phase with vector condensates of gluons exists as a stable ground state in dense two-flavor quark matter, and that this phase avoids the chromomagnetic instability that plagues the conventional two-flavor superconducting (2SC) phase when the mismatch between Fermi momenta satisfies \(\bar{\Delta} < \sqrt{2}\delta\mu\).

## Method

Using a gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that retains gluonic degrees of freedom, the authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau analysis of the effective potential including both diquark and gluon fields. They decompose the fields with respect to the unbroken \(SU(2)_c\) subgroup and compute the one-loop quark contribution in the hard dense loop approximation, then minimize the potential to determine the ground state configuration of homogeneous vector gluon condensates.

## Key numbers

- The gluonic phase exists for \(\bar{\Delta} < \sqrt{2}\delta\mu\), where \(\bar{\Delta}\) is the diquark gap and \(\delta\mu\) is the mismatch between Fermi momenta of pairing quarks.
- The confinement scale in the 2SC phase is \(\Lambda'_{\rm QCD} \lesssim \mathcal{O}(\text{10 MeV})\) or much smaller, justifying the introduction of gluonic degrees of freedom at energy scales around and below \(\bar{\Delta} \sim \mathcal{O}(\text{50 MeV})\).
- In the toy renormalizable model (gauged linear \(SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y\) sigma-model), the vacuum expectation value of the \(W\) boson field is given by \(|\langle W^{(-)}_z \rangle|^2 = \frac{\mu_Y v_0}{\sqrt{2}g} - \frac{v_0^2}{4}\), with \(v_0\) defined in Eq. (v0) of the text.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chromomagnetic instabilities first identified in the 2SC and gapless 2SC (g2SC) phases by Huang et al. (2004) and later connected to tachyonic plasmon spectra by Gorbar et al. (2006). The gluonic phase resolves these instabilities through vector condensates of gluons, in contrast to the LOFF phase with inhomogeneous diquark condensates studied by Alford et al. (2000) and others. The results enable further investigation of exotic vector hadrons in the confinement picture via the complementarity principle, and suggest that similar vector condensates may exist in the three-flavor gapless color-flavor locked (gCFL) phase.
