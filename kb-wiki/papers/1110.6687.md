# Confrontation of QMD model with the experimental data for $^{40}Ar$ +$^{45}$Sc, $^{197}Au$ +$^{197}$Au and $^{129}Xe$ +$^{119}$Sn reactions

> In the present work, we make confrontation of our theoretical calculations using quantum molecular dynamics model with the experimental data for the reactions of $^{40}Ar$ +$^{45}$Sc, $^{197}Au$ +$^{197}$Au and $^{129}Xe$ +$^{119}$Sn at different incident energies. In these reactions, we display the charge distribution and energy dependence of fragments multiplicity. Our results indicate good agreement with the experimental data for all the reactions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model calculations show good agreement with experimental data for charge distributions and fragment multiplicities across three different reaction systems, but this agreement is demonstrated only for central collisions and for a limited set of incident energies (85 and 115 MeV/nucleon for Ar+Sc, 150 MeV/nucleon for Au+Au, and 25 and 50 MeV/nucleon for Xe+Sn).

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model, where each nucleon is represented by a coherent state wave function with time-dependent position and momentum parameters, and the total n-body wave function is a direct product of these states (antisymmetrization neglected but Pauli principle included). The interaction potential includes Skyrme (local two-body and three-body), Coulomb, and Yukawa terms, with a soft equation of state and standard energy-dependent Cugnon nucleon-nucleon cross-section. Fragment formation was determined using the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) clusterization algorithm with a distance cutoff of 2-4 fm, applied to the phase-space output after 200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}Ar$ + $^{45}Sc$ at 85 MeV/nucleon central collision: charge distribution (dn/dz) shows a linear decrease with charge, with a steeper slope at 115 MeV/nucleon indicating total disassembly.
- For $^{197}Au$ + $^{197}Au$ at 150 MeV/nucleon central collision: charge distribution (dn/dz) decreases with increasing charge, with rapidity cut applied to remove spectator particles.
- For $^{129}Xe$ + $^{119}Sn$ at 25 MeV/nucleon central collision: two separate zones appear, a negative slope for light fragments and a 'u' shape for heavy fragments, with nearly no intermediate mass fragments.
- For $^{129}Xe$ + $^{119}Sn$ at 50 MeV/nucleon central collision: steepening of charge distribution with charge is observed, with good agreement to experimental data from INDRA at GANIL and GSI.
- For $^{129}Xe$ + $^{119}Sn$ central collisions: the number of intermediate mass fragments (Z>3) increases with beam energy, with fewer IMFs at 25 MeV/nucleon and more at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the validation of the QMD model by comparing its predictions to experimental data from the FOPI array at Michigan State University (NSCL) for Ar+Sc and Au+Au reactions, and from the INDRA detector at GANIL and GSI for Xe+Sn reactions. The study builds on prior multifragmentation observations by Perfilov et al., Lozkin et al., Jakobsson et al. (1982), and Ogilvie et al. using the ALADIN spectrometer, and tests the QMD model's ability to reproduce charge distributions and the rise-and-fall of intermediate mass fragments across a range of incident energies. The results enable further confidence in using the QMD model with MST clusterization for predicting fragment production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for central collisions.
