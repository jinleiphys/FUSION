# Theory and phenomenology of helicity amplitudes for high energy exclusive leptoproduction of the rho-meson

> We review here two approaches to describe hard leptoproduction of transversally polarized rho-meson, based on recent calculation of the gamma*T -> rhoT impact factor up to twist 3 accuracy in the collinear factorization frame, including 2- and 3- particles Fock-states. The first approach uses a model of the unintegrated gluon density (the proton impact factor) which allows a comparison of our predictions with H1 and ZEUS data for the ratios of helicity amplitudes T(gamma*T -> rhoT)/T(gamma*L -> rhoL) and T(gamma*T -> rhoL)/T(gamma*L -> rhoL). In the second approach, we transform the gamma*T -> rhoT impact factor into the impact parameter space. We show that the transformed amplitude factorizes according to conventional dipole picture. We shortly discuss a way to implement the nucleon saturation effects in our approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the twist-3 impact factor for the transition of a transversely polarized virtual photon to a transversely polarized rho-meson is consistent with the conventional color dipole picture, but only after using the QCD equations of motion; the factorized form is not a straightforward consequence of the Fourier transform of the hard part.

## Method

The authors compute the gamma*_T to rho_T impact factor up to twist-3 accuracy in the collinear factorization framework, including both 2-parton and 3-parton Fock states. They use two approaches: first, a model of the unintegrated gluon density (proton impact factor) based on the Gunion-Soper parameterization with two parameters M and A; second, a transformation of the impact factor into impact parameter space to obtain a dipole representation. The soft parts are parameterized by five 2-parton distribution amplitudes (DAs) and two 3-parton DAs, with Wandzura-Wilczek contributions and genuine 3-parton contributions separated using QCD equations of motion.

## Key numbers

* The ratio T(gamma*_T to rho_T)/T(gamma*_L to rho_L) is compared to H1 data for Q^2 values; good agreement is found for the proton impact factor parameter M between 0.9 GeV and 5 GeV.
* The dependence on the infrared cutoff lambda shows that gluons with momenta below Lambda_QCD (approximately 0.2 GeV) give a small contribution, while gluons with momenta around 1 GeV cannot be neglected.
* The rho-meson mass m_rho and decay constant f_rho appear explicitly in the impact factor expressions, with the rho-meson mass m_rho = 0.775 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of the gamma*_T to rho_T impact factor (Anikin 2009, Anikin 2010) and their phenomenological estimates (Anikin 2011). It builds on the long-established twist-2 calculation of the gamma*_L to rho_L transition by Ginzburg, Panfil, and Serbo. The paper shows consistency with the color dipole picture introduced by Mueller and by Nikolaev and Zakharov, and proposes that the dipole representation can incorporate saturation effects using models such as that of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff. The approach enables future studies of saturation at fixed impact parameter for planned collider experiments such as EIC and LHeC.
