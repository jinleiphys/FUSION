# Determining the existence and nature of the quark-gluon plasma by Upsilon suppression at the LHC

> Minijet production in $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC is expected to produce a gluon-dominated plasma with large initial temperatures and energy densities. We discuss the implications of the high initial temperatures on the screening mass and on the relative suppression of members of the $Υ$ family. We demonstrate that $p_T$-dependence of the $Υ'/Υ$ ratio is only expected if a quark-gluon plasma with significant energy density is present, and that the precise dependence of this ratio will strongly constrain the QGP models. We also propose that the $p_T$ spectra of the $Υ$ resonances themselves will provide valuable information by comparing these spectra to that of the $Z$ boson, which should not be influenced by plasma production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the high initial temperatures (around 1 GeV) from minijet production in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC could allow suppression of even the tightly bound Upsilon resonance by a quark-gluon plasma, contrary to earlier assumptions that Upsilon suppression would be unlikely. The authors hedge that the actual suppression pattern depends strongly on the unknown functional form of the screening mass at high temperature, with two bracketing scenarios giving very different breakup temperatures.

## Method

The authors compute initial energy densities and temperatures from minijet production using perturbative QCD with various parton distribution functions, then apply a nonrelativistic potential model with color screening to determine breakup temperatures for each bottonium state. They use the color evaporation model at next-to-leading order to predict Upsilon production rates in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV, normalizing to fixed-target and collider data. Two screening mass scenarios are considered: one from SU(N) lattice simulations and one from perturbative QCD with three quark flavors.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: epsilon(t0) = 1170 GeV/fm^3 with MRS D-prime parton densities and p0 = 2 GeV
- Initial temperature: T0 = 1.14 GeV at LHC, compared to T0 = 0.46 GeV at RHIC
- Upsilon breakup temperature: 391 MeV for SU(N) scenario (Tc = 260 MeV) versus 1125 MeV for 3-flavor QGP scenario (Tc = 150 MeV)
- Upsilon-prime breakup temperature: 260 MeV for SU(N) scenario versus 447 MeV for 3-flavor scenario
- Direct Upsilon production cross section in pp at 5.5 TeV: f^d * sigma_tilde = 426 nb
- Expected number of directly produced Upsilon in Pb+Pb: 2.02 x 10^6, yielding 4.99 x 10^4 muon pairs
- Upsilon-prime/Upsilon ratio from fixed-target data: 0.53 +/- 0.13

## Context

This work extends earlier quarkonium suppression studies by Matsui and Satz (1986) and Karsch, Mehr, and Satz (1988) by incorporating the much higher initial temperatures from minijet production, which were not considered in earlier estimates such as Satz (1991). The paper proposes that Upsilon suppression at the LHC could provide a cleaner signature than J/psi suppression, which is complicated by nuclear absorption and comover interactions as discussed by Gavin and Vogt (1994) and others. The results enable experimental tests using the CMS detector's muon capabilities, with the Z boson proposed as a reference process unaffected by plasma production.
