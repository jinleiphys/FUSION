# Remembering Steven Weinberg

## Key claim

This is a personal remembrance and tribute to Steven Weinberg, not a research paper presenting new scientific results. The author reflects on Weinberg's influence as a physicist and mentor, emphasizing that Weinberg's impact came primarily through his writings and his singular approach to physics, including his own statement that he built only one model in his life (the electroweak unification model).

## Method

The author recounts his personal experiences as a postdoc in Weinberg's research group at the University of Texas at Austin from 1990 to 1992, describing the group's structure, weekly seminars, lunch rituals, and Weinberg's interactions with colleagues and postdocs. The narrative also covers Weinberg's advocacy for the Superconducting Super Collider (SSC) and the author's subsequent career at CERN, with reflections on Weinberg's published works and their lasting influence.

## Key numbers

- 46 papers by Weinberg listed in the Inspire database with more than 500 citations each
- 78% of Weinberg's highly-cited papers are single-authored
- The author spent two years (1990-1992) as a postdoc in Weinberg's group in Austin, Texas
- The SSC was cancelled approximately one year after the author left Austin in Fall 1992
- LEP2 started its gradual energy increase in 1996
- LHC construction was approved in December 1994

## Context

This contribution appears in the volume "In Memory of Steven Weinberg" for Nuclear Physics B. It is a personal memoir rather than a technical paper, providing historical context about Weinberg's working style and his role in shaping modern theoretical physics through his 1967 electroweak unification paper and his 1979 paper on Phenomenological Lagrangians, which established effective field theory as a standard tool. The piece enables future generations to understand Weinberg's intellectual legacy and his approach to physics research.
