# $J/ψ$ near $T_c$

> We calculate the mass shift and thermal decay width of the $J/ψ$ near the QCD transition temperature $T_c$ by imposing two independent constraints on these variables that can be obtained first by solving the Schrödinger equation and second from the QCD sum rule approach. While the real part of the potential is determined by comparing the QCD sum rule result for charmonium and the D meson to that from the potential model result, the imaginary potential is taken to be proportional to the perturbative form multiplied by a constant factor, which in turn can be determined by applying the two independent constraints. The result shows that the binding energy and the thermal width becomes similar in magnitude at around $T=1.09T_c$, above which the sum rule analysis also becomes unstable, strongly suggesting that the $J/ψ$ will melt slightly above $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the binding energy and thermal width of the J/ψ become similar in magnitude at around T = 1.09 Tc, above which the QCD sum rule analysis becomes unstable, strongly suggesting that the J/ψ will melt slightly above Tc. This conclusion depends on combining two independent constraints on the mass shift and thermal width, one from solving the Schrödinger equation and one from QCD sum rules.

## Method

The authors model the heavy quark potential as a combination of the free energy (80%) and internal energy (20%) from lattice QCD at long distances, with the short-distance behavior close to the free energy potential. They add an imaginary potential proportional to the perturbative hard-thermal-loop (HTL) resummed form multiplied by a free parameter K, then solve the Schrödinger equation for the charmonium system to obtain constraints between the mass shift δm and thermal width Γ. Separately, they use QCD sum rules with a Borel-transformed dispersion relation and a pole-plus-continuum spectral function to derive an independent constraint between δm and Γ. The intersection of these two constraints at each temperature determines the actual values of δm, Γ, and the imaginary potential strength K.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy and thermal width become similar in magnitude at T = 1.09 Tc
- The multiplicative constant K for the imaginary potential starts from 0 at T = 0.98 Tc and rises to about 4 at T = 1.09 Tc
- The real part of the potential uses a transition from short-distance potential VS(r,T)=F(r,T) to long-distance potential VL(r,T)=0.8 F(r,T)+0.2 U(r,T) at r0 = 1 fm with width δ = 0.25 fm
- The bare charm quark mass used is mc = 1.25 GeV
- For T between Tc and 1.09 Tc, the extracted transport coefficients yield -10.78 ≤ γ/T³ ≤ -5.63 and 0 ≤ κ/T³ ≤ 6.97, assuming a0 = 0.23 fm and Tc = 155 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of J/ψ mass shift near Tc by Morita (2007) and Gubler (2010, 2011), and earlier potential model work by Lee (2013) and Gubler (2020) that showed the short-distance charmonium potential should be dominated by the free energy. The results are consistent with lattice QCD extractions by Lafferty (2019) and with partonic HTL resummation calculations by Hong (2018). The findings provide reliable nonperturbative input for understanding charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions and for determining heavy quark transport coefficients near Tc.
