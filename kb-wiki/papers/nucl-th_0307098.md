# Evidence for nonhadronic degrees of freedom in the transverse mass spectra of kaons from relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions?

> We investigate transverse hadron spectra from relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions which reflect important aspects of the dynamics - such as the generation of pressure - in the hot and dense zone formed in the early phase of the reaction. Our analysis is performed within two independent transport approaches (HSD and UrQMD) that are based on quark, diquark, string and hadronic degrees of freedom. Both transport models show their reliability for elementary $pp$ as well as light-ion (C+C, Si+Si) reactions. However, for central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at bombarding energies above $\sim$ 5 A$\cdot$GeV the measured $K^{\pm}$ transverse mass spectra have a larger inverse slope parameter than expected from the calculation. Thus the pressure generated by hadronic interactions in the transport models above $\sim$ 5 A$\cdot$GeV is lower than observed in the experimental data. This finding shows that the additional pressure - as expected from lattice QCD calculations at finite quark chemical potential and temperature - is generated by strong partonic interactions in the early phase of central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions.

## Key claim

The measured inverse slope parameters of kaon transverse mass spectra in central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at bombarding energies above approximately 5 A·GeV are larger than can be reproduced by hadronic transport models (HSD and UrQMD), indicating that additional pressure from partonic interactions, rather than purely hadronic dynamics, is required to explain the data.

## Method

The authors compared experimental transverse mass spectra of pions and kaons from central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at energies from 2 A·GeV to 21.3 A·TeV with calculations from two independent transport models (HSD and UrQMD), which include quark, diquark, string, and hadronic degrees of freedom but no parton-parton scattering. They also tested the effects of kaon self-energies from hadronic potentials and string-string interactions (via increased string tension) to see if these could account for the discrepancy.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 A·GeV, the transverse pressure from UrQMD is about 0.07 GeV/fm³; at 40 A·GeV it is 0.1 GeV/fm³; at 160 A·GeV it is 0.15 GeV/fm³.
- Lattice QCD calculations at T = 1.1 Tc (~190 MeV) give pressures of 0.15 GeV/fm³ (μB = 0), 0.17 GeV/fm³ (μB = 0.21 GeV), 0.2 GeV/fm³ (μB = 0.33 GeV), 0.22 GeV/fm³ (μB = 0.42 GeV), and 0.26 GeV/fm³ (μB = 0.53 GeV).
- At Tc (~172 MeV), lattice QCD pressure drops to about 0.02-0.03 GeV/fm³, lower than the transport model results for energies ≥ 10.7 A·GeV.
- String-string interactions with a transverse string radius Rs ≤ 0.25 fm give only a 15% hardening of kaon spectra at 160 A·GeV, much less than observed in data.
- Kaon potentials in hot pion matter (T = 170 MeV) give mass shifts of about -52 MeV and vector potentials of about +49 MeV, but hardening of K± spectra is less than 10%.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of transport models (HSD and UrQMD) to heavy-ion data from the AGS and SPS, following earlier studies that showed reasonable agreement for light-ion collisions (C+C, Si+Si) at 160 A·GeV. The failure to reproduce kaon slopes in heavy systems points to the need for partonic degrees of freedom, consistent with lattice QCD predictions by Fodor (2002) for increased pressure above Tc. The finding complements other signals of a phase transition onset near 5 A·GeV, such as the K+/π+ ratio and elliptic flow v2 excitation function, and motivates further experimental study with weakly interacting probes like φ-mesons.
