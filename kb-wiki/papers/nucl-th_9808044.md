# Multiquark picture for Sigma(1620)

> In this work we report on a new QCD sum rule analysis to predict masses of the excited baryon states (e.g. Sigma(1620) and Lambda(1405)) by using multiquark interpolating fields ($(q\bar{q})(qqq)$). For the Sigma(1620) we consider the $\bar{K}N$, $πΣ$, and $πΛ$ (I=1) multiquark interpolating fields. The calculated mass from those multiquark states is about 1.592 GeV. For the Lambda(1405) we first show the result using the $π^+Σ^- + π^0Σ^0 + π^-Σ^+$ (I=0) multiquark interpolating field, and compare the calculated mass to that of our previous result using the $π^0Σ^0$ multiquark state. We then show that the mass 1.405 GeV is well reproduced when using the $\bar{K}N$ (I=0) multiquark state. The uncertainties in our sum rules are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the masses of excited baryons such as the Sigma(1620) and Lambda(1405) can be predicted using QCD sum rules with multiquark interpolating fields of the form (q qbar)(qqq), rather than the usual three-quark fields; the calculated mass for the Sigma(1620) is about 1.592 GeV, which is somewhat lower than the experimental value of 1.620 GeV, and the result depends on the choice of continuum threshold and on neglecting gluon condensate contributions.

## Method

The author constructs QCD sum rules for the chiral-odd (Pi_1) structure using multiquark interpolating fields corresponding to the Kbar N, pi Sigma, and pi Lambda channels with isospin I=1 for the Sigma(1620), and for the pi^+ Sigma^- + pi^0 Sigma^0 + pi^- Sigma^+ (I=0) and Kbar N (I=0) channels for the Lambda(1405). The operator product expansion (OPE) is computed including quark condensates up to dimension 7, neglecting gluon condensates, and the continuum threshold s_0 is determined by requiring that the Kbar N multiquark mass equals the sum of the kaon and nucleon masses (1.435 GeV) in the fiducial Borel interval; the physical baryon mass is then taken as the value where the coupling strength lambda^2 has its maximum.

## Key numbers

*   Sigma(1620) mass predicted from the average of three I=1 multiquark states (Kbar N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda) at s_0 = 3.852 GeV^2: 1.592 GeV (with individual values 1.589, 1.606, and 1.581 GeV respectively).
*   Lambda(1405) mass from the Kbar N (I=0) multiquark state at s_0 = 3.082 GeV^2: 1.405 GeV.
*   Lambda(1405) mass from the pi^+ Sigma^- + pi^0 Sigma^0 + pi^- Sigma^+ (I=0) multiquark state at s_0 = 3.082 GeV^2 with quark condensate = -(0.230 GeV)^3: 1.424 GeV.
*   Lambda(1600) mass from the average of Kbar N and pi Sigma (I=0) multiquark states at s_0 = 3.852 GeV^2: 1.601 GeV.
*   Input parameters: quark condensate = -(0.230 GeV)^3, strange quark condensate = 0.8 times the quark condensate, strange quark mass = 0.150 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCD sum rule analysis for the Lambda(1405) using a pi^0 Sigma^0 multiquark field (Choe, 1998) to the isospin I=1 sector for the Sigma(1620) and to the full I=0 pi Sigma basis. The approach contrasts with conventional three-quark QCD sum rules for excited baryons (Liu, 1984; Leinweber, 1990; Jido, Kodama and Oka, 1996; Lee and Kim, 1997; Kim and Lee, 1997) and aims to describe states that may have significant meson-baryon molecular components. The results enable future calculations that include isospin breaking effects, gluon condensates, and instanton contributions to refine the mass predictions and distinguish between I=0 and I=1 states.
