# Isospin relaxation time in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Using an isospin-dependent transport model, we have studied the isospin and momentum relaxation times in the heavy residues formed in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. It is found that only at incident energies below the Fermi energy, chemical or thermal equilibrium can be reached before dynamical instability is developed in the heavy residues. Also, the isospin relaxation time is shorter (longer) than that for momentum at beam energies lower (higher) than the Fermi energy.

# Isospin relaxation time in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

## Key claim

Using an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model, the authors find that chemical (isospin) and thermal (momentum) equilibrium can be completely established in heavy residues only at beam energies below the Fermi energy; at higher energies, dynamical instability (spinodal decomposition) sets in before equilibrium is reached. The isospin relaxation time is shorter than the momentum relaxation time at beam energies below the Fermi energy, but longer at beam energies above the Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors employed an isospin-dependent BUU transport model that incorporates different total and differential cross sections, Pauli blocking, and mean fields for protons and neutrons. Heavy residues were identified as collections of nucleons with densities above 1/10 of normal nuclear matter density. Chemical equilibrium was characterized by the quantity λ_I(t), defined as the ratio of neutron-to-proton ratios for positive and negative rapidity nucleons in the residue rest frame, with the isospin relaxation time τ_I defined as the time when (λ_I(t)-1)/(λ_I(0)-1) reaches 0.01. Thermal equilibrium was characterized using the quadrupole moment Q_zz(t) in the residue rest frame, with the momentum relaxation time τ_p defined similarly as the time when Q_zz(t)/Q_zz(0) reaches 1%. Dynamical instability was studied via the square of the adiabatic sound velocity v_s^2, with negative values indicating spinodal decomposition.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca+¹²⁴Sn collisions at impact parameter 1 fm and beam energy 25 MeV/nucleon, the residue remains dynamically stable up to 300 fm/c, allowing both thermal and chemical equilibrium to be fully established.
- At beam energies above 50 MeV/nucleon, significant compression followed by expansion leads the residue into the adiabatic spinodal region after about 50 fm/c, before thermal or chemical equilibrium is reached.
- At E/A=300 MeV, a 20 fm/c difference in relaxation time is observed between isospin and momentum relaxation, with momentum relaxation being faster.
- The system studied has an initial λ_I(0)=1.48 for ⁴⁰Ca+¹²⁴Sn collisions.
- The soft nuclear equation of state parameters used are a=-358.1 MeV, b=304.8 MeV, and σ=7/6.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of momentum relaxation in nuclear matter using the Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation (Bertsch 1978, Randrup 1979, Wong 1982) and BUU calculations for finite nuclei (Casini 1987, Bonasera 1997), while addressing the limited prior work on chemical equilibrium rates (Li 1995, Donangelo 1997). The finding that isospin relaxation is faster than momentum relaxation at low energies agrees with results from deep inelastic heavy-ion collisions (Gatty 1975, Udo 1984), while the reversal at higher energies provides new constraints for statistical models of multifragmentation. The results enable improved modeling of reactions with radioactive beams having large isospin asymmetry and inform whether statistical or dynamical descriptions are appropriate for multifragmentation at different beam energies.
