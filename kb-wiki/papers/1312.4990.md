# Progress in resolving charge symmetry violation in nucleon structure

> Recent work unambiguously resolves the level of charge symmetry violation in moments of parton distributions using 2+1-flavor lattice QCD. We introduce the methods used for that analysis by applying them to determine the strong contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference. We also summarize related work which reveals that the fraction of baryon spin which is carried by the quarks is in fact structure-dependent rather than universal across the baryon octet.

# Progress in resolving charge symmetry violation in nucleon structure

## Key claim
Charge symmetry violation (CSV) in parton distribution moments has been unambiguously resolved using 2+1-flavor lattice QCD, revealing CSV at the ~0.20 ± 0.06% level in quark momentum fractions, an order of magnitude improvement over previous phenomenological bounds, though the analysis relies on chiral extrapolation from the SU(3) symmetric point to physical quark masses.

## Method
The authors applied an SU(3) chiral expansion to lattice QCD simulation results from the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration, extrapolating from the SU(3) symmetric point to determine CSV contributions at physical quark masses. The same technique was first demonstrated on octet baryon mass splittings, where isospin-averaged lattice data constrained chiral perturbation theory expressions, requiring only the up-down quark mass ratio as additional input to extract the strong contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference.

## Key numbers
- CSV in quark momentum fractions: ~0.20 ± 0.06%, corresponding to a ~0.4–0.6% correction to the parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) asymmetry
- Charge symmetry-breaking derivative from PACS-CS lattice data: m_π² d/dω (M_n − M_p) = 20.3 ± 1.2 MeV
- Charge symmetry-breaking derivative from QCDSF lattice data: m_π² d/dω (M_n − M_p) = 16.6 ± 1.2 MeV
- Current phenomenological bounds on CSV in parton distributions: 5–10% (Martin 2003)
- Phenomenological estimates suggest CSV could cause ~1.5–2% variations in the PVDIS asymmetry (Martin 2003)

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of charge symmetry violation by the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration (Cloet 2012, Horsley 2010) and provides an order-of-magnitude improvement over phenomenological bounds reported by Martin (2003). The results are significant for the Jefferson Laboratory PVDIS program aiming for sub-1% precision (JLab), and reduce the NuTeV anomaly from 3σ to 2σ. The same chiral extrapolation techniques also reveal that quark spin fractions are structure-dependent across the baryon octet, contradicting assumptions of universality and supported by Cloudy Bag Model calculations (Myhrer 1988, 2007; Schreiber 1988).
