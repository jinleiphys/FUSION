# Imaging dynamical chiral symmetry breaking: pion wave function on the light front

> We project onto the light-front the pion's Poincare'-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave-function, obtained using two different approximations to the kernels of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. At an hadronic scale both computed results are concave and significantly broader than the asymptotic distribution amplitude, ϕ_π^{asy}(x)=6 x(1-x); e.g., the integral of ϕ_π(x)/ϕ_π^{asy}(x) is 1.8 using the simplest kernel and 1.5 with the more sophisticated kernel. Independent of the kernels, the emergent phenomenon of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking is responsible for hardening the amplitude.

## Key claim

The pion's light-front valence-quark distribution amplitude (PDA) at a hadronic scale is significantly broader than the asymptotic form \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm asy}(x)=6x(1-x)\), and this broadening is a direct expression of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB), independent of the specific truncation used for the Dyson-Schwinger equation kernels.

## Method

The authors solved QCD's gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations using two symmetry-preserving truncation schemes: rainbow-ladder (RL) and a DCSB-improved kernel (DB). They computed the moments of the pion's PDA via Eq. (2) from the resulting dressed-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, then reconstructed the full PDA using a Gegenbauer-\(\alpha\) polynomial expansion optimized for rapid convergence.

## Key numbers

- The integral \(\int_0^1 dx \, \varphi_\pi(x)/\varphi_\pi^{\rm asy}(x)\) equals 1.8 for the RL kernel and 1.5 for the DB kernel, at the hadronic scale.
- The RL result at \(\zeta=2\) GeV is \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm RL}(x) = 1.74 [x(1-x)]^{\alpha_-^{\rm RL}} [1 + a_2^{\rm RL} C_2^{\alpha_{\rm RL}}(2x-1)]\) with \(\alpha_{\rm RL}=0.79\), \(a_2^{\rm RL}=0.0029\).
- The DB result at \(\zeta=2\) GeV is \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm DB}(x) = 1.81 [x(1-x)]^{\alpha_-^{\rm DB}} [1 + a_2^{\rm DB} C_2^{\alpha_{\rm DB}}(2x-1)]\) with \(\alpha_{\rm DB}=0.81\), \(a_2^{\rm DB}=-0.12\).
- The second moment \(\langle (2x-1)^2 \rangle\) is 0.28 for RL and 0.25 for DB, compared to 0.2 for the asymptotic distribution and \(0.27\pm 0.04\) from lattice QCD (Braun 2006).
- The central values are \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm RL}(1/2)=1.16\) and \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm DB}(1/2)=1.29\), compared to \(\varphi_\pi^{\rm asy}(1/2)=1.5\) and a QCD sum rule result of \(1.2\pm 0.3\) (Braun 1988).

## Context

This work extends DSE-based calculations of the pion PDA beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation (Maris 2003) by employing DCSB-improved kernels (Chang 2011) that incorporate the dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment. The results unify predictions for pion elastic and transition form factors, pi-pi scattering, and nucleon properties (Eichmann 2011). The computed PDAs are not directly usable in leading-twist formulas for processes like \(\gamma^*\gamma\to\pi^0\) because subleading contributions remain important up to at least \(Q^2\lesssim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
