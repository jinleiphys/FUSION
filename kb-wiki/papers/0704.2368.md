# Lightest scalar in the SU_L(2)*SU_R(2) linear Žāmodel

> We consider the lightest scalar meson in the frame of the $SU_L(2)\times SU_R(2)$ linear $Žā$ model, keeping in mind that this model could be the low energy realization of the two-flavour QCD. We show that the $Žā$ field is described by its four-quark component at least in the $Žā$ resonance energy (virtuality) region and the $Žā\to╬│╬│$ decay is the four quark transition. We emphasize that residues of the $Žā$ pole in the $ŽĆŽĆ\toŽĆŽĆ$ and $╬│╬│\toŽĆŽĆ$ amplitudes do not give an idea about the $Žā$ meson nature, and the progress in studying the $Žā$ meson production mechanisms in different processes could essentially further us in understanding its nature.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the SU_L(2) x SU_R(2) linear sigma model, the sigma meson field is described by its four-quark component at least in the sigma resonance energy region, and that the sigma to gamma gamma decay is a four-quark transition; however, they emphasize that the residues of the sigma pole in the pi pi to pi pi and gamma gamma to pi pi amplitudes do not give an idea about the sigma meson nature.

## Method

The authors use the SU_L(2) x SU_R(2) linear sigma model with a Dyson equation for the isoscalar scalar pi pi scattering amplitude, including only real intermediate pi pi states in every rescattering act. They obtain a solution satisfying unitarity and Adler's self-consistency conditions, then compute the S-wave gamma gamma to pi pi amplitudes using a field theory approach with triangle diagrams for the rescattering loop gamma gamma to pi+ pi- to pi pi.

## Key numbers

* Bare sigma meson mass m_sigma = 0.93 GeV, obtained from fitting delta^0_0 in the region 2m_pi <= sqrt(s) <= 0.87 GeV.
* Breit-Wigner resonance mass M_res = 0.43 GeV, with width Gamma_res(M^2_res) = 0.67 GeV and renormalized width Gamma^{renorm}_res(M^2_res) = 0.53 GeV.
* Sigma pole position: sqrt(s_R) = (0.52 - i0.25) GeV, corresponding to s_R = (0.21 - i0.26) GeV^2.
* Scattering lengths: a^0_0 = 0.18 m_pi^{-1}, a^2_0 = -0.04 m_pi^{-1}; Adler zero positions: (s_A)^0_0 = 0.45 m^2_pi, (s_A)^2_0 = 2.02 m^2_pi.
* Sigma to gamma gamma decay width: Gamma(sigma -> gamma gamma) approx 2 keV.
* Direct coupling contribution to sigma -> gamma gamma: Gamma^{(0)}_{sigma gamma gamma} approx 0.0034 keV.
* Ratio g_gamma/g_pi = g_gamma^{res}/g_pi^{res} = (-1.61 + i1.21) x 10^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Achasov and Shestakov, 1994) showing that chiral dynamics naturally shields wide light scalar mesons, a concept that triggered new theoretical and experimental searches for the sigma and kappa mesons reviewed by the Particle Data Group (2006). The results agree qualitatively with the Roy equation analysis of Leutwyler (2001) but only semiquantitatively, as the authors' approximation does not include the K anti-K channel, the f_0(980) meson, or the SU_L(3) x SU_R(3) linear sigma model. The work enables future studies of sigma meson production mechanisms in different processes, which the authors argue is essential for understanding the light scalar meson nature rather than relying on pole residues.
