# Reply to "Comment on `Lattice determination of Sigma - Lambda mixing' "

> In this Reply, we respond to the above Comment. Our computation [Phys. Rev. D 91 (2015) 074512] only took into account pure QCD effects, arising from quark mass differences, so it is not surprising that there are discrepancies in isospin splittings and in the Sigma - Lambda mixing angle. We expect that these discrepancies will be smaller in a full calculation incorporating QED effects.

## Key claim

The authors' lattice computation of Sigma-Lambda mixing (Phys. Rev. D 91, 074512) included only pure QCD effects from quark mass differences, so discrepancies with experimental isospin splittings and the mixing angle are expected; the authors anticipate these discrepancies will be smaller in a full calculation that incorporates QED effects.

## Method

The authors performed a lattice QCD calculation of the baryon octet masses using a fit function with all SU(3) symmetry constraints built in, which automatically obeys the Coleman-Glashow and Dalitz-von-Hippel relations to the appropriate order. They then applied a flavor analysis to QED effects, identifying five independent electromagnetic coefficients that contribute to the baryon masses and the Sigma-Lambda mixing matrix element, and compared their pure-QCD results to both the leading-order relations and to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- The quark mass ratio (m_d - m_u)/(m_s - m_u) was found to be approximately 0.022 from the lattice simulations.
- Violations of the Coleman-Glashow relation are at O(delta m^3), while violations of the Dalitz-von-Hippel relation are at O(delta m^2).
- The leading-order pure QCD relations for isospin splittings (e.g., M_n^2 - M_p^2 approx (m_d - m_u)/(m_s - m_u) (M_Xi0^2 - M_Sigma+^2)) hold reasonably well with only minor higher-order corrections.

## Context

This reply responds to a Comment by Gal (2015) on the authors' earlier lattice determination of Sigma-Lambda mixing (Phys. Rev. D 91, 074512). The work extends the pure-QCD lattice approach by analyzing the expected QED contributions through a general flavor decomposition, and notes that simulations with QED included (Borsanyi et al.) show QED effects on isospin splittings are comparable to the effects of the (m_d - m_u) difference. The authors are currently working on combined QED+QCD simulations to resolve the discrepancies with experimental values.
