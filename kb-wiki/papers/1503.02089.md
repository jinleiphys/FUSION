# Nonmesonic Weak Decay Dynamics from proton spectra of $Λ$-Hypernuclei

> A novel comparison between the data and the theory is proposed for the nonmesonic (NM) weak decay of hypernuclei. Instead of confronting the primary decay rates, as is usually done, we focus attention on the effective decay rates that are straightforwardly related with the number of emitted particles. Proton kinetic energy spectra of $^5_Λ$He, $^7_Λ$Li, $^9_Λ$Be, $^{11}_Λ$B, $^{12}_Λ$C, $^{13}_Λ$C, $^{15}_Λ$N and $^{16}_Λ$O, measured by FINUDA, are evaluated theoretically. The Independent Particle Shell Model (IPSM) is used as the nuclear structure framework, while the dynamics is described by the One-Meson-Exchange (OME) potential. Only for the $^{5}_Λ$He, $^{7}_Λ$Li, and $^{12}_Λ$C hypernuclei is it possible to make a comparison with the data, since for the rest there is no published experimental information on number of produced hypernuclei. Considering solely the one-nucleon-induced ($1N$-NM) decay channel, the theory reproduces correctly the shapes of all three spectra at medium and high energies ($E_p \geq 40 $ MeV). Yet, it greatly overestimates their magnitudes, as well as the corresponding transition rates when the full OME ($π+K+ η+ρ+ω+K^*$) model is used. The agreement is much improved when only the $π+K$ mesons with soft dipole cutoff parameters participate in the decay process. We find that the IPSM is a fair first order approximation to disentangle the dynamics of the $1N$-NM decay, the knowledge of which is indispensable to inquire about the baryon-baryon strangeness-flipping interaction. It is shown that the IPSM provides very useful insights regarding the determination the $2N$-NM decay rate. In a new analysis of the FINUDA data, we derive two results for this quantity with one of them close to that obtained previously.

## Key claim
The authors propose that comparing experimental proton kinetic energy spectra to theoretical calculations using only the one-nucleon-induced nonmesonic decay channel, without including final state interactions or two-nucleon-induced decay, is sufficient to disentangle the baryon-baryon strangeness-flipping interaction, provided the comparison is restricted to medium and high proton energies (above 40 MeV).

## Method
The authors calculated proton kinetic energy spectra for eight hypernuclei (⁵_ΛHe, ⁷_ΛLi, ⁹_ΛBe, ¹¹_ΛB, ¹²_ΛC, ¹³_ΛC, ¹⁵_ΛN, ¹⁶_ΛO) using the Independent Particle Shell Model for nuclear structure and a One-Meson-Exchange potential for the weak decay dynamics. They compared three parametrizations of the OME potential: the full pseudoscalar and vector meson octet (P1), only π+K exchange with standard cutoffs (P2), and soft π+K exchange with reduced cutoff parameters (P3). Only three hypernuclei (⁵_ΛHe, ⁷_ΛLi, ¹²_ΛC) could be compared to FINUDA data because published experimental information on the number of produced hypernuclei was unavailable for the others.

## Key numbers
- The theory reproduces the shapes of all three spectra at medium and high energies (E_p ≥ 40 MeV) when considering only the one-nucleon-induced decay channel.
- The full OME model (π+K+η+ρ+ω+K*) greatly overestimates the magnitudes of the spectra and transition rates; agreement is much improved when only π+K mesons with soft dipole cutoff parameters (Λ_π = 0.7 GeV, Λ_K = 0.9 GeV) are used.
- For ⁵_ΛHe, the experimental proton decay rate Γ_p = 0.27 ± 0.11 (in units of Γ_Λ) for E_thres = 15 MeV; for ⁷_ΛLi, Γ_p = 0.41 ± 0.16; for ¹²_ΛC, Γ_p = 0.52 ± 0.18.
- The total weak decay width of hypernuclei follows Γ_W(A) = (0.990 ± 0.094) + (0.018 ± 0.010)A for A = 4-12.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of nonmesonic weak decay beyond the traditional comparison of primary decay rates, following the approach of Bauer, Garbarino, Parreño, and Ramos (2010-2011) who used the Fermi Gas Model for ¹²_ΛC. The authors argue that the Independent Particle Shell Model provides a fair first-order approximation for disentangling the one-nucleon-induced decay dynamics, which is necessary to study the baryon-baryon strangeness-flipping interaction. The analysis enables a new determination of the two-nucleon-induced nonmesonic decay rate from FINUDA data, with one result close to the previously obtained value from KEK and FINUDA (branching ratio Γ₂⁰/Γ_NM ∼ 25-30%).
