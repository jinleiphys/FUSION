# Proton dripline in a new formula for nuclear binding energy

> The location of the proton dripline in a new phenomenological mass formula is calculated. Predictions of different mass formulas for the dripline are compared. The implications of the new mass formula for rapid proton nucleosynthesis beyond $^{56}$Ni are discussed. It is seen that the new formula indicates that masses up to A=80 are easily synthesized in a typical X-ray burst.

## Key claim

The new phenomenological mass formula from Ref. I predicts the location of the proton dripline accurately in most cases, performing better than the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) but slightly worse than the Duflo-Zuker formula; however, the authors note that because the formula has an r.m.s. error of 376 keV, predictions for nuclei with very small or negative calculated proton separation energies (e.g., 0.047 MeV or -0.030 MeV) may not definitively locate the dripline.

## Method

The authors used the mass formula from Ref. I (a phenomenological binding energy formula with an r.m.s. error of 0.376 MeV for 2140 nuclei) to calculate proton separation energies and determine the proton dripline (the nucleus beyond which the proton separation energy becomes zero or negative) for neutron numbers from N=18 to N=123. They compared these predictions to experimental dripline locations derived from the Audi mass table and recent measurements, as well as to predictions from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and the Duflo-Zuker (D-Z) formula. For the astrophysical part, they constructed a small reaction network for the rp-process around the waiting point nucleus 64Ge, calculating effective half-lives and abundance evolution at temperatures of 1.2 GK and 1.5 GK using a microscopic optical potential from the DDM3Y potential folded with densities from the Relativistic Mean Field model (FSU Gold Lagrangian).

## Key numbers

- The r.m.s. error of the new mass formula is 0.376 MeV for 2140 nuclei.
- For N=93, the calculated binding energy of the last proton in 170Lu is 0.047 MeV; for N=94 in 171Lu it is 0.012 MeV; for N=102 in 183Tl it is -0.030 MeV.
- The measured Q-value of the 64Ge(p,gamma) reaction from Schury et al. is -0.255 +/- 0.104 MeV; a recent measurement gives -0.090 +/- 0.085 MeV; the new formula predicts -0.116 MeV.
- The Duflo-Zuker formula predicts a minimum effective half-life of 64Ge of nearly 30 seconds, while the present formula and the Audi-Wapstra table decrease this lifetime by more than one order of magnitude.
- At 1.2 GK, after 100 seconds, the population reaching A=80 or beyond is more than 1.5 times the corresponding quantity at 1.5 GK.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological mass formula introduced in Ref. I (Lahiri and Gangopadhyay, 2011) by testing its predictions for the proton dripline and for rp-process nucleosynthesis beyond 56Ni. It compares the new formula's dripline predictions to those from the Finite Range Droplet Model (Moller et al., 1995) and the Duflo-Zuker formula (Duflo and Zuker, 1995), finding the new formula performs better than FRDM but worse than D-Z. The study of the 64Ge waiting point builds on earlier work by the same authors (Lahiri and Gangopadhyay, 2010) and highlights how different mass predictions (from Audi et al., Schury et al., and others) lead to dramatically different conclusions about whether the rp-process can bridge waiting points and synthesize nuclei up to A=80 in typical X-ray bursts.
