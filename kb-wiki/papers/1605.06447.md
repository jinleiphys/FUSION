# Linearized Boltzmann transport model for jet propagation in the quark-gluon plasma: Heavy quark evolution

> A Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model coupled with hydrodynamical background is established to describe the evolution of jet shower partons and medium excitations in high energy heavy-ion collisions. We extend the LBT model to include both elastic and inelastic processes for light and heavy partons in the quark-gluon plasma. A hybrid model of fragmentation and coalescence is developed for the hadronization of heavy quarks. Within this framework, we investigate how heavy flavor observables depend on various ingredients, such as different energy loss and hadronization mechanisms, the momentum and temperature dependences of the transport coefficients, and the radial flow of the expanding fireball. Our model calculations show good descriptions of the $D$ meson suppression and elliptic flow observed at the LHC and RHIC. The prediction for the Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$=5.02~TeV is provided.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to include both elastic and inelastic processes for heavy quarks, and demonstrates that the model can describe D meson suppression and elliptic flow at both RHIC and LHC, but only after introducing non-perturbative Gaussian K-factors to modify the momentum and temperature dependence of the transport coefficient at low momentum and near the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors couple a linearized Boltzmann equation for heavy quark propagation to a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic background (VISHNew). Elastic scattering rates are computed from leading-order matrix elements for all 2→2 channels with Debye screening, while medium-induced gluon radiation is included using the higher-twist formalism with a Poisson distribution for multiple gluon emission. Heavy quarks are hadronized via a hybrid model combining coalescence (using Wigner functions with harmonic oscillator wavefunctions) and PYTHIA fragmentation, with the relative probability determined by the coalescence Wigner function.

## Key numbers

- Elastic energy loss rate for 30 GeV charm quarks in a 300 MeV static medium: \(\hat{e}=0.668\) GeV/fm
- Elastic energy loss rate for 30 GeV beauty quarks in a 300 MeV static medium: \(\hat{e}=0.382\) GeV/fm
- For 30 GeV heavy quarks at 300 MeV temperature with \(\alpha_s=0.3\): \(\hat{q}/T^3 = 6.25\) for charm quarks and 5.24 for beauty quarks
- Critical temperature for hadronization: \(T_c=165\) MeV
- Oscillator frequency \(\omega=0.33\) GeV for charm and beauty mesons, 0.43 GeV for charm baryons, and 0.41 GeV for beauty baryons
- Root mean squared charge radii used: 0.43 fm for \(D^+\), 0.62 fm for \(B^+\), 0.39 fm for both \(\Lambda_c\) and \(\Lambda_b\)
- Thermal masses used in coalescence: 300 MeV for u/d quarks, 475 MeV for s quarks, 600 MeV for gluons; bare masses 1.27 GeV for c quarks and 4.19 GeV for b quarks
- Hydrodynamic starting time: \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm; shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio \(\eta/s=0.08\)

## Context

This work extends the LBT model previously developed for light partons (Wang 2013, He 2015) to include heavy flavor evolution, and builds upon earlier Langevin transport approaches for heavy quarks (Cao 2013, Cao 2015). The hybrid coalescence-fragmentation hadronization model follows the approach of Oh (2009). The results enable systematic comparison with D meson data from RHIC and LHC, and provide predictions for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, while the authors note that a precise extraction of non-perturbative parameters via Bayesian methods is left for future work.
