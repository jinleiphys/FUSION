# $V_{lowk}$ Renormalization Group Flow, Vector Manifestation and Sound Velocity in Massive Compact Stars

> The $V_{lowk}$-renormalization group approach on the surface of Fermi liquid for nuclear matter to which Tom Kuo made a pioneering contribution at Stony Brook is found to inject the pivotal input in the formulation of the generalized nuclear effective field theory with acronym ``G$n$EFT" applicable to superdense compact-star physics. A topology change in terms of skyrmions and half-skyrmions is shown to play the role of the ``putative" hadron-quark continuity (HQC)" conjectured in QCD. Crucially involved are hidden local symmetry (``HLS") and hidden scale symmetry (``HSS") with the vacuum sliding with density in nuclear medium, with the nuclear tensor force emerging as a Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point quantity. A possibly novel paradigm, a ``Cheshire Catism," in nuclear correlations is suggested.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $V_{lowk}$-renormalization group approach, when combined with hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry in a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G$n$EFT), predicts a "pseudo-conformal" sound velocity $v_{pcs}^2/c^2 \approx 1/3$ in massive compact stars, but only if the vector manifestation fixed point is located at asymptotically high density ($n_{VM}=25n_0$) rather than at lower density ($n_{VM}=6n_0$). The authors hedge that both scenarios cannot yet be ruled out by current astrophysical observations.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-chiral Lagrangian $\cal{L}_{\psi {\cal V} \chi}$ incorporating hidden local symmetry (HLS) with vector fields $\rho_\mu, \omega_\mu$ and hidden scale symmetry (HSS) with a dilaton field $\sigma_d$, with parameters subject to Brown-Rho scaling that slides with density. They implement a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at density $n_{1/2} \sim (2-4)n_0$ to capture the putative hadron-quark continuity, and treat the system at the mean-field level equivalent to a Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation. The sound velocity is computed using the $V_{lowk}$-RG approach on the Fermi surface, with the nuclear tensor force emerging as a Fermi-liquid fixed-point quantity.

## Key numbers

* The normal nuclear matter density is $n_0 \sim 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, and the possible central density of massive stars is $n_{cs} \sim (4-7)n_0$.
* The topology change density $n_{1/2}$ is found in the range $n_{1/2} \approx n_{hqc} \sim (2-4)n_0$.
* For $n_{1/2} \geq 4.0 n_0$, the maximum star mass accommodated is $M_{max} \sim 2.4 M_\odot$, which is ruled out by causality.
* The half-skyrmion phase sets in when the lattice size $L_{1/2} \lesssim 2.9$ fm.
* For the pseudo-conformal parameterization at $n_{1/2}=2.0n_0$, the parameters are $B_{\alpha=(0,1)}= (570\text{ MeV},\, 686\text{ MeV})$ and $D_{\alpha=(0,1)}= (440\text{ MeV},\, 253\text{ MeV})$.
* The sound velocity converges to $v_{pcs}^2/c^2 \approx 1/3$ for $n_{VM}=25n_0$, while for $n_{VM}=6n_0$ the sound speed violates causality at high density.

## Context

This work extends the $V_{lowk}$-RG approach pioneered by Tom Kuo at Stony Brook, combined with the Brown-Rho scaling framework (Brown and Rho, 1991), to formulate a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G$n$EFT) for compact-star physics. It builds on prior skyrmion crystal simulations (Dong et al.; Park, Krivoruchenko, Lee; and others) that found a skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition, and on the hidden local symmetry formalism of Harada and Yamawaki. The paper enables future precision tests of whether the sound velocity in massive neutron stars exhibits pseudo-conformal behavior, which would distinguish between hadronic and quark-like descriptions of dense matter, and connects to the long-standing $g_A$ quenching problem in nuclear beta decay.
