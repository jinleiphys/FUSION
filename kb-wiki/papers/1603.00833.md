# Thermo-magnetic properties of the strong coupling in the local Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the thermo-magnetic properties of the strong coupling constant G and quark mass M entering the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. For this purpose, we compute the quark condensate and compare it to lattice QCD (LQCD) results to extract the behavior of G and M as functions of the magnetic field strength and temperature. We find that at zero temperature, where the LQCD condensate is found to monotonically increase with the field strength, M also increases whereas G remains approximately constant. However, for temperatures above the chiral/deconfinement phase transitions, where the LQCD condensate is found to monotonically decrease with increasing field, M and G also decrease monotonically. For finite temperatures, below the transition temperature, we find that both G and M initially grow and then decrease with increasing field strength. To study possible consequences of the extracted temperature and magnetic field dependence of G and M, we compute the pressure and compare to LQCD results, finding an excellent qualitative agreement. In particular, we show that the transverse pressure, as a function of the field strength, is always negative for temperatures below the transition temperature whereas it starts off being positive and then becomes negative for temperatures above the transition temperature, also in agreement with LQCD results. We also show that for the longitudinal pressure to agree with LQCD calculations, the system should be described as a diamagnet. We argue that the turnover of M and G as functions of temperature and field strength is a key element that drives the behavior of the quark condensate going across the transition temperature and provides clues for a better understanding of the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon.

## Key claim

The strong coupling constant \(G\) and dynamically generated quark mass \(M\) in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, extracted by matching the model's quark condensate to lattice QCD results, show a turnover behavior as functions of magnetic field strength for temperatures below the chiral transition: they initially increase and then decrease, while above the transition they decrease monotonically. This turnover is argued to be a key element driving the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon, but the extraction relies on averaging over light-quark flavors and scaling the model's critical temperature to match the lattice value.

## Method

The authors used the NJL model in the mean-field approximation to compute the quark condensate as a function of temperature \(T\) and magnetic field strength \(eB\), employing Schwinger's proper-time method for the quark propagator in a magnetic field and Matsubara formalism for finite temperature. They then solved the gap equation and condensate expression as a transcendental system, using lattice QCD data for the average light-quark condensate from Bali et al. (2012) as input to extract the thermo-magnetic behavior of the coupling \(G(B,T)\) and mass \(M(B,T)\). The vacuum contribution was separated and regularized with a proper-time cutoff, and the model's critical temperature was linearly scaled to match the lattice value of 158 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum condensate \(-\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle_0^{1/3} = 0.220\) GeV for the first parameter set (cutoff \(\tau_0 = 1.27\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), and \(0.260\) GeV for the second set (\(\tau_0 = 0.74\) GeV\(^{-2}\)).
* Vacuum dynamically generated mass \(M_0 = 0.224\) GeV (first set) and \(0.192\) GeV (second set).
* Vacuum coupling \(G_0 = 5.08\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (first set) and \(2.66\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (second set).
* Current quark mass \(m = 0.00758\) GeV (first set) and \(0.00465\) GeV (second set).
* Model critical temperature at \(eB=0\): \(T_c^{NJL} = 0.267\) GeV (first set) and \(0.228\) GeV (second set); scaled to the lattice value \(T_c = 0.158\) GeV.
* At \(T = 0\) MeV, the mass \(M\) increases monotonically with \(eB\) while \(G\) remains approximately constant.
* At \(T = 176\) MeV (above \(T_c\)), both \(M\) and \(G\) decrease monotonically with increasing \(eB\).
* At \(T = 113\) MeV and \(130\) MeV (below \(T_c\)), both \(M\) and \(G\) initially grow and then decrease with increasing field strength.
* Transverse pressure is always negative for \(T = 113\) MeV (below \(T_c\)), but starts positive and becomes negative for \(T = 176\) MeV (above \(T_c\)).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ayala et al. (2015) and others who proposed that magnetic screening/antiscreening of the QCD coupling explains inverse magnetic catalysis, by directly extracting the coupling from lattice QCD condensate data of Bali et al. (2012). It contrasts with earlier NJL model studies (e.g., Boomsma et al., Loewe et al., Agasian, Fraga, Andersen, Blaschke, Scoccola) that did not include such thermo-magnetic modifications and thus failed to describe the condensate behavior at high temperature. The extracted coupling behavior enables future parametrizations (as in Krein et al., 2016) and provides a microscopical link between the coupling's field dependence and the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon, with implications for heavy-ion collision and compact star physics.
