# Study of light kaonic nuclei with a Chiral SU(3) - based KbarN interaction

> We have investigated the prototype kaonic nucleus, $ppK^-$, using the method of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD). In the present study we use a realistic $NN$ potential with strongly repulsive core, and a Chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N$ interaction which is energy-dependent and includes both $s$- and p-wave interactions. We find that no self-consistent solutions exist when the range parameter of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction is less than 0.67 fm. Due to the strong repulsion of the $NN$ interaction at short distance, the two nucleons in $ppK^-$ keep a distance of about 1.2 fm and the total binding energy of $ppK^-$ does not exceed about 50 MeV in the scope of our current analysis.

## Key claim

The authors find that no self-consistent solutions for the $ppK^-$ system exist when the range parameter of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction is less than 0.67 fm, and the total binding energy of $ppK^-$ does not exceed about 50 MeV in the scope of their current analysis, which they state should still be considered preliminary.

## Method

The authors used the Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) variational method to study the $ppK^-$ system, employing a realistic Argonne v18-like $NN\) potential with a strongly repulsive core and a Chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N\) interaction that includes both energy-dependent $s$-wave and $p$-wave components. They performed a self-consistent calculation by iterating the assumed kaon binding energy until it matched the obtained kaon binding energy, treating the $p$-wave interaction perturbatively and omitting imaginary parts of the interactions.

## Key numbers

- No self-consistent solutions exist when the range parameter $a$ of the $\bar{K}N\) interaction is less than 0.67 fm.
- For $a = 0.67$ fm, the total binding energy of $ppK^-$ is 52.5 MeV, the kaon binding energy $B(K)$ is 195.4 MeV, and the mean distance between the two protons $R_{pp}$ is 1.04 fm.
- For $a = 0.90$ fm, the total binding energy is 18.0 MeV, $B(K)$ is 84.5 MeV, and $R_{pp}$ is 1.41 fm.
- The two nucleons in $ppK^-$ keep a distance of about 1.2 fm due to the strong repulsion of the $NN\) interaction at short distance.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and earlier AMD calculations that suggested deeply bound kaonic nuclei, but uses a more realistic $NN\) potential (Argonne v18-like) with a strongly repulsive core rather than the smoothed $G$-matrix potential used previously, and a Chiral SU(3)-based $\bar{K}N\) interaction instead of a phenomenological one. The results indicate that the strong repulsion of the $NN\) interaction prevents the system from becoming as compact as earlier studies suggested, and the authors note that the next step will be to introduce two-nucleon correlation functions in the AMD trial wave function and to calculate the decay width, which is crucial for assessing experimental observability.
