# Linear correlations between 4He trimer and tetramer energies calculated with various realistic 4He potentials

> In a previous work [Phys. Rev. A 85, 022502 (2012)] we calculated, with the use of our Gaussian expansion method for few-body systems, the energy levels and spatial structure of the 4He trimer and tetramer ground and excited states using the LM2M2 potential, which has a very strong short-range repulsion. In this work, we calculate the same quantities using the presently most accurate 4He-4He potential [M. Przybytek et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 104, 183003 (2010)] that includes the adiabatic, relativistic, QED and residual retardation corrections. Contributions of the corrections to the tetramer ground-(excited-)state energy, -573.90 (-132.70) mK, are found to be, respectively, -4.13 (-1.52) mK, +9.37 (+3.48) mK, -1.20 (-0.46) mK and +0.16 (+0.07) mK. Further including other realistic 4He potentials, we calculated the binding energies of the trimer and tetramer ground and excited states, B_3^(0), B_3^(1), B_4^(0) and B_4^(1), respectively. We found that the four kinds of the energies for the different potentials exhibit perfect linear correlations between any two of them over the range of binding energies relevant for 4He atoms (namely, six types of the generalized Tjon lines are given). The dimerlike-pair model for 4He clusters, proposed in the previous work, predicts a simple universal relation B_4^(1)/B_2 =B_3^(0)/B_2 + 2/3, which precisely explains the correlation between the tetramer excited-state energy and the trimer ground-state energy, with B_2 being the dimer binding energy.

## Key claim

The four binding energies of the 4He trimer and tetramer (ground and excited states) calculated with seven different realistic 4He potentials exhibit perfect linear correlations between any two of them over the range of binding energies relevant for 4He atoms, and the dimerlike-pair model predicts a simple universal relation B4^(1)/B2 = B3^(0)/B2 + 2/3 that precisely explains the correlation between the tetramer excited-state energy and the trimer ground-state energy.

## Method

The authors used the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for ab initio variational calculations of the 4He trimer and tetramer bound states, expanding the total wave function in terms of totally symmetrized few-body Gaussian basis functions with ranges in geometric sequences. They calculated binding energies using the PCKLJS potential (which includes adiabatic, relativistic, QED, and residual retardation corrections) and six other realistic 4He potentials: LM2M2, TTY, HFD-B, HFD-B3-FCI1, SAPT96, and CCSAPT07. The total basis sizes were 4400 for the trimer and 29056 for the tetramer.

## Key numbers

- For the PCKLJS potential: trimer ground-state binding energy B3^(0) = 131.84 mK, trimer excited-state binding energy B3^(1) = 2.6502 mK, tetramer ground-state binding energy B4^(0) = 573.90 mK, tetramer excited-state binding energy B4^(1) = 132.70 mK
- Contributions of individual corrections to the tetramer ground-state energy (-573.90 mK): adiabatic correction -4.13 mK, relativistic correction +9.37 mK, QED correction -1.20 mK, residual retardation correction +0.16 mK
- Contributions of individual corrections to the tetramer excited-state energy (-132.70 mK): adiabatic correction -1.52 mK, relativistic correction +3.48 mK, QED correction -0.46 mK, residual retardation correction +0.07 mK
- Dimer binding energy for PCKLJS: B2 = 1.6154 mK with average separation 47.09 Angstrom
- Dimer binding energies for other potentials range from 1.3094 mK (LM2M2) to 1.7443 mK (SAPT96)
- Trimer ground-state binding energies range from 126.45 mK (TTY) to 134.02 mK (SAPT96)
- Tetramer ground-state binding energies range from 558.70 mK (TTY) to 580.01 mK (SAPT96)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations using the LM2M2 potential (Hiyama and Kamimura, 2012) to the more accurate PCKLJS potential and six other realistic potentials, providing systematic results for six types of generalized Tjon lines. The linear correlations found between trimer and tetramer binding energies are compared with universal scaling functions from leading-order effective theory (Braaten and Platter, 2003; Platter, 2004), and the dimerlike-pair model prediction explains the B4^(1)-B3^(0) correlation. This enables precise predictions of tetramer binding energies from trimer binding energies and vice versa for 4He clusters.
