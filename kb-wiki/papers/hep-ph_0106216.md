# Parity Violating Photoproduction of $Ď€^\pm$ on the $Î”$ Resonance

> We analyze the real photon asymmetry $A^\pm_Îł$ for the parity violating (PV) $Ď€^\pm$ production on the $Î”$ resonance via the reactions ${\vec Îł} + p \to Î”^+ \to Ď€^+ + n$ and ${\vec Îł} + d \to Î”^0 + p \to Ď€^- + p + p$. This asymmetry is nonvanishing due to a PV $ÎłN Î”$ coupling constant, $d^\pm_Î”$. We argue that an experimental determination of this coupling would be of interest for hadron dynamics, possibly shedding light on the S-wave/P-wave puzzle in the hyperon nonleptonic decays and the violation of Hara's theorem in weak radiative hyperon decays.

## Key claim

The parity-violating (PV) real photon asymmetry \(A^\pm_\gamma\) for \(\pi^\pm\) photoproduction on the \(\Delta\) resonance could be as large as \(\sim 1.3 \times 10^{-6}\), significantly enhanced over the "natural" scale of \(\sim \text{few} \times 10^{-8}\) expected from naive dimensional analysis, if the same dynamics that produce anomalously large SU(3)-violation in \(\Delta S=1\) hyperon weak radiative decays also generate enhanced SU(4)-breaking in the \(\Delta S=0\) sector.

## Method

The authors compute the PV asymmetry \(A^\pm_\gamma\) for the resonant reactions \(\vec{\gamma} + p \to \Delta^+ \to \pi^+ + n\) and \(\vec{\gamma} + d \to \Delta^0 + p \to \pi^- + p + p\) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) at leading order (\(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\)). They estimate the unknown low-energy constant \(d^\pm_\Delta\) characterizing the PV \(\gamma N \Delta\) coupling by assuming resonance saturation from \(\frac{3}{2}^-\) intermediate states, scaling the \(\Delta S=0\) weak amplitudes from known \(\Delta S=1\) weak matrix elements extracted from fits to hyperon nonleptonic decays.

## Key numbers

* The PV asymmetry on the \(\Delta\) resonance is given by \(A^\pm_\gamma \approx -2 d^\pm_\Delta / C_3^V \cdot M_N / \Lambda_\chi\), where \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_\pi \sim 1\) GeV and \(C_3^V \sim 2\).
* The "best value" estimate for the low-energy constant is \(|d^\pm_\Delta(\text{best})| = 25 g_\pi\), where \(g_\pi \sim G_F F_\pi^2 / 2\sqrt{2} \sim 5 \times 10^{-8}\).
* This yields a "reasonable estimate" for the asymmetry of \(A^\pm_\gamma \sim 1.3 \times 10^{-6}\).
* The "reasonable range" for the low-energy constant is \(|d^\pm_\Delta| = (1-100) g_\pi\).
* The \(\Delta\) resonance enhancement over non-resonant background is \(\sigma^\Delta / \sigma^{NR} \sim (2 M_\Delta / \Gamma_\Delta)^4 \sim 2 \times 10^4\).
* With one month of running at Jefferson Lab (luminosity 0.25 MHz/nb, photon polarization \(P_\gamma \sim 0.5\)), a 15% (statistical) determination of \(A^\pm_\gamma\) could be achieved.

## Context

This work proposes a new probe of the strangeness-conserving hadronic weak interaction via PV \(\pi^\pm\) photoproduction on the \(\Delta\) resonance, extending the study of parity violation from nucleonic systems (as in prior PV experiments) to transitions between the nucleon and its lightest spin-\(\frac{3}{2}\) partner. The analysis connects the \(\Delta S=0\) PV \(\gamma N \Delta\) coupling to the \(\Delta S=1\) hyperon weak radiative decays where Hara's theorem is violated (with experimental asymmetries \(\alpha^{\Sigma^+ p} = -0.76 \pm 0.08\) and \(\alpha^{\Xi^0 \Sigma^0} = -0.63 \pm 0.09\), roughly four to five times the naive SU(3)-breaking expectation). A measurement of \(A^\pm_\gamma\) could determine whether anomalously large symmetry breaking is a general feature of low-energy hadronic weak interactions or a peculiarity of hyperon decays, and would also remove the dominant theoretical uncertainty from the planned PV \(\pi\) electroproduction asymmetry measurement at Jefferson Lab.
