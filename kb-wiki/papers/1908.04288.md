# Clustering of Four-Component Unitary Fermions

> Ab initio nuclear physics tackles the problem of strongly interacting four-component fermions. The same setting could foreseeably be probed experimentally in ultracold atomic systems, where two- and three-component experiments have led to major breakthroughs in recent years. Both due to the problem's inherent interest and as a pathway to nuclear physics, in this Letter we study four-component fermions at unitarity via the use of quantum Monte Carlo methods. We explore novel forms of the trial wave function and find one which leads to a ground state of the eight-particle system whose energy is almost equal to that of two four-particle systems. We investigate the clustering properties involved and also extrapolate to the zero-range limit. In addition to being experimentally testable, our results impact the prospects of developing nuclear physics as a perturbation around the unitary limit.

## Key claim
The eight-particle four-component unitary fermion system has a ground-state energy that is within one standard deviation of twice the four-particle energy, indicating clustering into two four-particle subsystems. This result depends on using a sufficiently sophisticated trial wave function that captures both clustering and intercluster correlations, as simpler wave functions (BCS-type or basic cluster forms) fail to reach this near-threshold energy.

## Method
The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations for eight four-component fermions at unitarity, using Gaussian two-body and three-body potentials tuned to produce two-body unitarity and a nonzero four-body energy. They tested three trial wave functions: a four-component BCS-type wave function, a simple cluster wave function inspired by 1950s nuclear cluster models, and a novel correlated cluster wave function (psi_T^C) that includes intracluster Jastrow correlations, intercluster pair correlations, and a center-of-mass correlation function. The fixed-node approximation was used to control the fermion-sign problem, and calculations were performed at several potential ranges to extrapolate to the zero-range limit.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of eight-particle to four-particle energy extrapolated to the zero-range limit is \(E_8/E_4 = 2.04 \pm 0.05\).
- For the simple cluster wave function (psi_T^B), the eight-particle energy was within 5% of \(2E_4\).
- The four-body ground-state energy is related to the three-body ground-state energy by \(E_4 = 4.611 E_3\), taken from Deltuva (2010).
- Calculations were performed at a dimensionless range parameter \(\bar\mu R_4(\bar\mu) = 13.64\) for the main comparison of trial wave functions.

## Context
This work extends quantum Monte Carlo studies from two-component unitary Fermi gases (Carlson 2003, Astrakharchik 2004) and three-component systems (Blume 2008, Dawkins 2017) to the four-component case, which is directly relevant to nuclear physics where protons and neutrons each have two spin states. The eight-particle system is the analog of the \(^8\)Be nucleus, which is observed as a narrow resonance near the two-\(^4\)He threshold (Benn 1967, Wustenbecker 1992). The results support the program of developing nuclear physics as a perturbation around the unitary limit (Konig 2017, van Kolck 2017) and suggest that clustering is a universal feature of weakly bound multicomponent fermion systems, potentially enabling calculations of heavier nuclear systems as small corrections to cold-atom results.
