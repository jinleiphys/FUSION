# Many-particle many-hole states near the magic number 20: deformed and superdeformed states

> Many-particle many-hole states near the magic number 20 have been investigated in the relativistic mean field formalism using the fixed configuration method. Neutron particle-hole states in neutron-rich nuclei with $N\sim 20$, {\em i.e.} $^{30,32,34}$Mg,and $^{28,30}$Ne are studied to find out the ground state configuration. The ground state of $^{32}$Ne,Mg as well as possibly $^{34}$Mg comes out actually as particle-hole states. Proton-neutron excitation across the shell gap at 20 in nuclei with $N=Z$, {\em i.e.} $^{32}$S, $^{36}$Ar, $^{40}$Ca and $^{44}$Ti is investigated as possible origins of superdeformed configuration. Observed superdeformed bands in $^{36}$Ar and $^{40}$Ca can be described as many-particle many-hole states.

## Key claim

The ground states of neutron-rich nuclei near N=20 (specifically ³²Mg, ³⁰Ne, and possibly ³⁴Mg) are actually particle-hole (2p-2h or 2ħω) configurations rather than normal 0ħω configurations, while for ³⁰Mg and ²⁸Ne the normal configuration remains the ground state; in N=Z nuclei, the superdeformed bands in ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁰Ca can be described as many-particle many-hole states (4p-8h and 8p-8h respectively), though the normal deformed band in ⁴⁰Ca comes too high in energy compared to experiment.

## Method

The authors applied the fixed configuration deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach using the NL3 force, assuming axial and reflection symmetry with a deformed harmonic oscillator basis and 12 fermion and boson shells. For each nucleus, specific particle-hole configurations were constructed by selectively emptying and filling Nilsson-model orbitals (e.g., 3/2⁺[202] and 1/2⁺[200] orbitals emptied, 1/2⁻[330] and 3/2⁻[321] orbitals filled) and solving the Hartree-BCS equations self-consistently. The energy of the 0⁺ band head was obtained from the intrinsic solution using the cranking model formula to subtract the rotational energy contribution, with pairing included via BCS using constant gaps of 1.0 MeV.

## Key numbers

- ³²Mg: the 2p-2h 0⁺ state has binding energy 252.50 MeV (after angular momentum correction), making it the ground state; the spherical 0ħω solution has binding energy 250.97 MeV; experimental binding energy is 249.69 MeV; charge deformation β_C = 0.457, mass deformation β_A = 0.492
- ³⁰Mg: the 0ħω ground state has binding energy 243.66 MeV; the 2p-4h 0⁺ state lies 1.5 MeV higher at 242.17 MeV; experimental binding energy is 241.63 MeV; β_C = 0.522, β_A = 0.601
- ³⁴Mg: the 0ħω state has binding energy 260.48 MeV (β_C = 0.345, β_A = 0.327); the 2ħω state has binding energy 260.42 MeV (β_C = 0.511, β_A = 0.562); experimental binding energy is 256.59 MeV
- ³⁰Ne: the 2ħω 0⁺ state has binding energy 216.62 MeV, making it the ground state; the 0ħω spherical solution has binding energy 215.93 MeV; experimental binding energy is 212.08 MeV
- ²⁸Ne: the 2ħω state lies 1.75 MeV above the 0ħω ground state (binding energy 210.57 MeV); experimental binding energy is 208.82 MeV
- ⁴⁰Ca: the 8p-8h SD band head excitation energy is 5.86 MeV; the SD band quadrupole moment is 1.89 eb (experimental Q₀ = 1.80⁺⁰·³⁹₋₀·₂₉ eb); the ND band quadrupole moment is 1.14 eb (experimental Q₀ = 0.74 ± 0.14 eb)
- ³⁶Ar: the 4p-8h band head excitation energy is 5.97 MeV (experimental band head at 4.33 MeV); calculated intrinsic quadrupole moment is 1.37 eb (experimental Q₀ = 1.18 ± 0.09 eb)
- ³²S: a 12p-4h solution has excitation energy 6.22 MeV and β ∼ 1.0
- ⁴⁴Ti: an 8p-4h solution has excitation energy 3.36 MeV and charge deformation 0.530

## Context

This work extends the fixed configuration deformed mean field approach previously applied by Zheng et al. (2000) using Skyrme Hartree-Fock to the Relativistic Mean Field formalism with the NL3 force, which naturally accommodates spin-orbit splitting. The results address the ongoing debate about the boundary of the "island of inversion" near N=20, where different calculations disagree: some HFB calculations (e.g., one by Lalazissis et al.) predict spherical ground states for ³²Mg while others (e.g., another HFB calculation) predict deformed intruder configurations. The study also provides RMF descriptions of superdeformed bands in ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁰Ca, complementing cranked Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations by Bender et al. (1999) and cranked RMF calculations without pairing. The findings enable future work incorporating configuration mixing between different np-nh states and more accurate treatment of pairing via the Bogoliubov approach with continuum coupling.
