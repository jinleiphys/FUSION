# Spin correlation in two-proton emission from $^6$Be

> This paper presents a theoretical evaluation of spin correlation in the two-proton ($2p$) radioactive emission. The three-body model of $^{6}$Be with the proton-proton interaction, which is adjusted to reproduce the experimental energy release, is utilized. Time-dependent calculation is performed to compute the coupled-spin state of the emitted two protons. The spin-correlation function $S$ as the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) indicator is evaluated as $|S| \cong 2.65$. Namely, the $2p$-spin correlation beyond the limit of local-hidden-variable (LHV) theory is suggested. This correlation is sensitive to the proton-proton interaction. The short-lived (broad-width) $2p$~state has the weaker spin correlation. In parallel, the core-proton interactions do not harm this correlation during the time-dependent decaying process. The CHSH measurement can be a novel probe into the effective nuclear interaction inside finite systems. Two-proton emitters can provide a testing field for identical-particle entanglement.

## Key claim

The two-proton spin-correlation function S, evaluated as the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) indicator, reaches |S| ≈ 2.65 for the time-dependent decaying state of ⁶Be, which exceeds the local-hidden-variable (LHV) theory limit of |S| ≤ 2; however, this correlation is sensitive to the proton-proton interaction strength, and a short-lived (broad-width) two-proton state has weaker spin correlation.

## Method

A three-body model of ⁶Be with a rigid ⁴He core and two valence protons was employed, using a time-dependent method to simulate the two-proton emission process. The Hamiltonian included a Woods-Saxon potential for core-proton interactions and a spin-dependent Gaussian potential for the proton-proton interaction, adjusted with an additional surface-dependent term to reproduce the experimental two-proton energy release of 1.372(5) MeV. The CHSH indicator S was computed from the coupled-spin state of the emitted two protons by integrating the coordinate-dependent wave function over the spatial degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The CHSH indicator for the default proton-proton interaction (f_pp = 1.0) at ct = 1600 fm is S = 2.65, exceeding the LHV-theory limit of |S| ≤ 2.
- With reduced proton-proton interaction strength f_pp = 0.83, the two-proton energy is E_2p = 2.056 MeV, the width is Γ_2p = 0.177 MeV (τ = 3.7 × 10⁻²¹ s), and S = 2.34.
- With f_pp = 0.66, the two-proton energy is E_2p = 2.616 MeV, the width is Γ_2p = 0.428 MeV (τ = 1.5 × 10⁻²¹ s), and S = 1.98, falling below the LHV-theory limit.
- The experimental two-proton energy for ⁶Be is E_2p = 1.372(5) MeV and width Γ_2p = 0.092(6) MeV (Ajzenberg-Selove 1988, 1991).

## Context

This work extends the experimental measurement of spin correlation in two-proton systems by Sakai (2003), who deduced S_expt = 2.83 ± 0.24_stat ± 0.07_sys from the reaction ²H + p → ²He + n, and the earlier Bell-inequality test in low-energy proton-proton scattering by Lamehi-Rachti and Mittig (1976). The results suggest that two-proton emitters like ⁶Be can serve as a testing field for identical-particle entanglement, and that CHSH measurement could become a novel probe into effective nuclear interactions inside finite systems; however, mass production of two-proton emitters for sufficient statistics and closing experimental loopholes remain open challenges.
