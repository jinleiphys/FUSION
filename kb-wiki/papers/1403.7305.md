# Broadening of $ρ(\to e^+e^-)$ meson produced coherently in the photonuclear reaction

> The $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution spectra are calculated to estimate the hadron parameters of the $ρ$ meson produced coherently in the photonuclear reaction. The elementary reaction occurring in the nucleus is considered to proceed as $ γN \to ρ^0 N ; ~ ρ^0 \to e^+e^- $. We describe the elementary $ρ$ meson photoproduction by the experimentally determined reaction amplitude $ f_{ γN \to ρ^0 N } $. The $ρ$ meson propagator is presented by the eikonal form, and the $ρ$ meson nucleus optical potential $V_{Oρ}$ appearing in it is estimated using the "$t\varrho$" approximation. The $ρ$ meson nucleon scattering amplitude $ f_{ ρN } $ extracted from the measurements is used to generate this potential. The calculated $e^+e^-$ invariant mass distribution spectra are compared with those measured at Jefferson Laboratory. The calculated results for the transparency ratio are also presented.

# Broadening of ρ(→ e⁺e⁻) meson produced coherently in the photonuclear reaction

## Key claim

The calculated e⁺e⁻ invariant mass distribution spectra for coherent ρ⁰ meson photoproduction on ¹²C, ⁴⁸Ti, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb reproduce the shape of the measured Jefferson Laboratory data reasonably well, but the calculated transparency ratio shows an anomalous result for the Pb nucleus that is inconsistent with the systematic trend seen for lighter nuclei.

## Method

The elementary reaction γN → ρ⁰N is described using the experimentally determined reaction amplitude f_{γN→ρN}, extracted from measured differential cross sections. The ρ meson propagator is treated in the eikonal form, and the ρ-nucleus optical potential V_{Oρ} is estimated via the "tϱ" approximation using the ρ-nucleon scattering amplitude f_{ρN} (extracted from photoproduction data). The differential cross section for the e⁺e⁻ invariant mass distribution is calculated for the coherent (γ, ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻) reaction, with the photon beam energy weighted in six bins (1.0–3.5 GeV) to simulate the CLAS beam profile, and the ρ meson momentum constrained to 0.8–3.0 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium ρ meson spectral function width is enhanced from 146 MeV (free space) to 546 MeV at nuclear saturation density ϱ_s = 0.17 fm⁻³, with the peak shifted from 760 MeV to 830 MeV.
- The peak cross section for ρ meson angular distribution on ¹²C is reduced by a factor of 2.55 (from ∼35.25 μb/GeV to 13.81 μb/GeV) due to the ρ-nucleus optical potential.
- The maximum cross section for the ρ meson mass distribution occurs at 2.5 GeV beam energy with a value of 7.18 nb/GeV.
- Peak cross sections are attenuated from 56.42 nb/GeV to 18.43 nb/GeV for ¹²C, from 196.88 nb/GeV to 43.78 nb/GeV for ⁵⁶Fe, and from 262.12 nb/GeV to 73.78 nb/GeV for ²⁰⁸Pb when the optical potential is included; all peaks appear near 740 MeV with a shift ≤10 MeV.
- The width enhancement due to the ρ-nucleus interaction is 6 MeV for ¹²C, 35 MeV for ⁵⁶Fe, and 92 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- For ρ mesons decaying inside the nucleus, the widths are 327.69 MeV (¹²C), 347.68 MeV (⁵⁶Fe), and 401.53 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb), with peaks at 710, 620, and 540 MeV respectively.
- For ρ mesons decaying outside the nucleus, the peak appears at 750 MeV with width ∼150 MeV for all nuclei.
- The transparency ratios are T_A = 0.34 for ¹²C, 0.26 for ⁵⁶Fe, and 0.32 for ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of ρ meson in-medium properties in photonuclear reactions by Effenberger et al. (semiclassical transport model with collision broadening and mass-shift from Hatsuda-Lee potential) and Riek et al. (hadronic many-body theory with meson/pomeron exchange at E_γ ≥ 2 GeV and s-wave resonances at lower energy). Unlike those approaches, the present calculation uses experimentally determined inputs (the ρN scattering amplitude extracted from γN → ρN data) rather than parameterized models. The calculated results are compared with CLAS Jefferson Laboratory data (Nasseripour et al., Wood et al.) and show reasonable agreement for the mass distribution shape, though the transparency ratio for Pb is anomalous compared to the consistent results reported by Riek et al. (2011). The work enables further investigation of the ρ meson decay inside versus outside the nucleus as a function of nuclear size.
