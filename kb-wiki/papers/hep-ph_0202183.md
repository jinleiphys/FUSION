# Extraction of the neutron charge form factor from the charge form factor of deuteron

> We extract the neutron charge form factor from the charge form factor of deuteron obtained from $T_{20}(Q^2)$ data at $0\le Q^2\le$ 1.717 (GeV$^2$). The extraction is based on the relativistic impulse approximation in the instant form of the relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics. Our results (12 new points) are compatible with existing values of the neutron charge form factor of other authors. We propose a fit for the whole set (35 points) taking into account the data for the slope of the form factor at $Q^2 = 0$.

## Key claim

The authors extract 12 new data points for the neutron charge form factor \(G^n_E(Q^2)\) from the deuteron charge form factor, using a relativistic impulse approximation in the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics; the extraction is conditional on the choice of deuteron wave functions, with the best description of the \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) data given by the wave functions of Murzin and Troitsky (1981).

## Method

The neutron charge form factor is obtained by inverting the relativistic impulse-approximation equation for the deuteron charge form factor \(G_C(Q^2)\), which couples the nucleon charge and magnetic form factors to the deuteron structure via the relativistic functions \(G_{CC}(Q^2)\) and \(G_{CM}(Q^2)\). The functions \(G_{CC}\) and \(G_{CM}\) are computed using deuteron wave functions from the dispersion variant of the inverse scattering method (Murzin and Troitsky 1981), which are chosen because they best reproduce the experimental \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) data. The nucleon form factors \(G^p_E\), \(G^p_M\), and \(G^n_M\) are taken from the DRN–GK(3) fit of Lomon (2001).

## Key numbers

* 12 new points for \(G^n_E(Q^2)\) are extracted from deuteron charge form factor data over the range \(0 \le Q^2 \le 1.717\) (GeV\(^2\)).
* The slope of \(G^n_E\) at \(Q^2 = 0\) is \(0.0199 \pm 0.0003\) fm\(^2\), taken from thermal neutron scattering (Kopeliovich 1995).
* A two-parameter fit to all 35 points (12 new plus 23 from prior work) gives \(a = 0.942\) (fixed from the slope, accuracy \(\approx 1.5\%\)) and \(b = 4.61\) with \(\chi^2 = 69.0\).
* The first extracted point at \(Q^2 \approx 0.16\) (GeV\(^2\)) is almost the same as in Jones et al. (1991), but with smaller errors.
* At \(Q^2 \ge 1\) (GeV\(^2\)), the extracted \(G^n_E\) values are strictly positive, in contrast to results from Luning et al. (1993) which are consistent with zero.

## Context

This work extends prior extractions of the neutron charge form factor from deuteron and \(^3\)He data (e.g., Hachmann et al. 1973, Jones et al. 1991, Luning et al. 1993, Eden et al. 1994, Meyerhoff et al. 1994, Passchier et al. 1999, Ostrick et al. 1999, Rohe et al. 1999, Herberg et al. 1999, Golak et al. 2001) by using a relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics approach that explicitly accounts for relativistic effects and the choice of deuteron wave function. The new points are consistent with existing data, and the combined fit provides a parametrization that respects the precisely measured slope at \(Q^2 = 0\). The results enable improved modeling of the neutron electromagnetic structure and can be used in future analyses of few-body systems, though additional experiments in the region of the first dip of the deuteron charge form factor are needed to resolve discrepancies between data sets (Theissen et al. 1991, Abbott et al. 2000).
