# Hyperon-rich Matter in Neutron Stars

> We study the equation of state of hyperon-rich matter for neutron stars using an extended relativistic mean-field model. We take special care of the recently proposed non-linear behaviour of the vector field which gives a much better description of Dirac-Brueckner calculations. The hyperon-hyperon interaction is also implemented by introducing additional meson exchanges. These new terms avoid the instability found at high densitites in previous works while keeping the excellent description for finite nuclear systems. We also demonstrate within the mean-field approach that the presence of hyperons inside neutron stars on one hand and the hyperon-hyperon interactions on the other hand make the onset of kaon condensation less favourable.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including hyperon-hyperon interactions via additional strange meson exchanges (the scalar $\sigma^*$ and vector $\phi$ fields) avoids the instability from negative effective masses found at high densities in previous relativistic mean-field models, and that these interactions, together with the presence of hyperons, make the onset of kaon condensation less favourable even at very high densities.

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for neutron star matter by including all baryons of the octet (p, n, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma^+$, $\Sigma^0$, $\Sigma^-$, $\Xi^0$, $\Xi^-$) and adding a quartic vector field self-interaction term. Hyperon-hyperon interactions were implemented through two additional meson fields: the scalar $f_0(975)$ (denoted $\sigma^*$) and the vector $\phi(1020)$, with coupling constants fixed by SU(6) symmetry and hypernuclear potential depths. The equation of state was solved under $\beta$-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions for several parameter sets fitted to finite nuclei, including sets with (TM1, TM2) and without (NL-Z, NL-SH, PL-Z, PL-40) vector self-interactions.

## Key numbers

- Hyperon potential depths used: $U_\Lambda^{(N)} = U_\Sigma^{(N)} = -30$ MeV, $U_\Xi^{(N)} = -28$ MeV (model 1); hyperon-hyperon interaction potentials: $U^{(\Xi)}_\Xi \approx U^{(\Xi)}_\Lambda \approx 2U^{(\Lambda)}_\Xi \approx 2U^{(\Lambda)}_\Lambda \approx -40$ MeV (model 2)
- For set NL-Z, instability (negative effective nucleon mass) appears in neutron star matter around $\rho \approx 0.5$ fm$^{-3}$; for set NL-SH at $\rho \approx 0.8$ fm$^{-3}$ (model 1) and $1.0$ fm$^{-3}$ (model 2)
- For set TM1 with vector self-interaction and hyperon-hyperon interactions (model 2), all effective masses remain positive up to $10\rho_0$; hyperons appear at $2\rho_0$ ($\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^-$) and $\Xi^-$ at $3\rho_0$
- The antikaon optical potential at normal nuclear density is taken as $U^{\bar K}_{\rm opt} \approx -200 \pm 20$ MeV for one family of solutions and $-120$ MeV for another
- The electrochemical potential reaches a maximum of about 200 MeV around $\rho \approx 2-3\rho_0$; with the $\delta$-meson included, this maximum is about 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach of Boguta (1977) and Reinhard (1988) by incorporating the vector self-interaction proposed by Bodmer (1991) and the hyperon-hyperon interaction via strange meson exchanges developed by Schaffner et al. (1993, 1994). It contradicts the predictions of kaon condensation at $3-4\rho_0$ from chiral perturbation theory (Brown 1992, 1994) and the work of Muto (1993), while supporting the conclusion of Ellis et al. (1995) that hyperons shift the kaon condensation threshold to higher densities. The results enable more realistic neutron star equation of state calculations that avoid the instabilities found in earlier RMF models and provide a framework for studying the interplay between hyperonic degrees of freedom and kaon condensation.
