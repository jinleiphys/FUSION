# Direct triple-$α$ process in non-adiabatic approach

> Triple-$α$ reaction rates have been determined well with the sequential process via the narrow resonances. However, the direct triple-$α$ process at off-resonant energies still remains in unsolved problems. In the present report, the direct triple-$α$ contribution is estimated with a non-adiabatic method, and it is found to be 10$^{-15}$--10$^{-3}$ pb order in photodisintegration cross sections of $^{12}$C(2$^+_1 \rightarrow$ 0$^+$) for $0.15 < E < 0.35$ MeV. This is far below the values predicted by the recent adiabatic models. In spite of the large difference, the derived rates are found to be concordant with NACRE at the helium burning temperatures.

# Direct triple-α process in non-adiabatic approach

## Key claim
The direct triple-α contribution to photodisintegration cross sections of ¹²C(2⁺₁ → 0⁺) is estimated to be 10⁻¹⁵–10⁻³ pb for 0.15 < E < 0.35 MeV using a non-adiabatic method, which is far below values predicted by recent adiabatic models; however, despite this large difference in cross sections, the derived reaction rates are concordant with NACRE at helium burning temperatures.

## Method
The author solves the three-body Schrödinger equation for the 3α system using the Faddeev hyper-spherical harmonics and R-matrix (HHR*) expansion method, translating Jacobi coordinates into hyper-spherical coordinates to obtain coupled-channel equations for hyper-radial waves. The coupled-channel equations are solved by matrix diagonalization with a matrix size of 8,800×8,800 for the 0⁺ state in ¹²C, and the R-matrix expansion method is used to expand continuum states with scattering boundary conditions, including long-range Coulomb couplings solved numerically via the R-matrix propagation technique. Photodisintegration cross sections of ¹²C(2⁺₁ → 0⁺) are calculated from the resulting wavefunctions, and energy-averaged reaction rates are derived from the cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Photodisintegration cross sections σ_g for ¹²C(2⁺₁ → 0⁺) are 10⁻¹⁵–10⁻³ pb for the energy range 0.15 < E < 0.35 MeV.
- The prominent narrow resonance of 0⁺₂ is found at E = 0.3795 MeV.
- Calculated α-width Γ_α = 5.1 eV and γ-width Γ_γ = 4.3 meV, compared to experimental data (NACRE) of Γ_α = 8.3 eV and Γ_γ = 3.7 meV.
- The derived reaction rates are consistent with NACRE for 0.08 < T₉ < 3, including helium burning temperatures.
- At T₉ = 0.05, the present result is reduced by 10⁻⁴ compared to CMF and ACF predictions.

## Context
This work extends the non-adiabatic Faddeev hyper-spherical harmonics approach of Nguyen et al. (2013) and contrasts with adiabatic approaches such as the Coulomb modified Faddeev (CMF) method of Ishikawa (2014) and the adiabatic channel function (ACF) expansion method of Suno (2016). The author finds that adiabatic models overestimate cross sections above 0.15 MeV due to an assumed long resonant tail of the 0⁺₂ state, which enhances the sequential decay process at off-resonant energies. The results enable a clearer separation of direct and sequential triple-α contributions and show that the direct process has negligible astrophysical impact at helium burning temperatures, though the non-adiabatic description remains important for understanding off-resonant cross sections.
