# The Few-Body Coulombian Problem

> Recent advances in the treatment of scattering of charged composite particles are reviewed. In a first part I report on developments of the theory. Specifically I describe the recent completion of the derivation of the co-ordinate space asymptotic behaviour of the wave function for three charged particles in the continuum. This knowledge is increasingly being made use of in attempts to `derive' three-Coulomb particle wave functions to be used in all of configuration space which are solutions of the Schroedinger equation, though not everywhere but at least in one or preferably all of the asymptotic regions. Their practical application in approximate calculations of ionisation and breakup processes is pointed out. The asymptotic three-charged particle wave functions find further use in investigations of asymptotic and analytic properties of matrix elements of the three-body Coulomb resolvent. An important example is the nonperturbative derivation, valid for all energies, of the large-distance behaviour of the optical potential. In the second part I describe renewed attempts to establish the few-body approach as a valuable tool for calculating (energetic) atomic collision processes. At the end I briefly touch upon the recent successful above-breakup-threshold calculation of proton-deuteron elastic scattering for a realistic potential.

# The Few-Body Coulombian Problem

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent theoretical advances in treating scattering of charged composite particles, particularly the completion of the derivation of the coordinate-space asymptotic behavior of the wave function for three charged particles in the continuum. The key hedge is that the asymptotic forms are known and increasingly used, but the wave functions that extrapolate them into all of configuration space are "highly non-unique and, thus, open to further optimisation", they are solutions of the Schrödinger equation "though not everywhere but at least in one or preferably all of the asymptotic regions."

## Method

The review covers two main lines of work. First, it describes the derivation of asymptotic wave functions for three charged particles in the continuum, distinguishing the "Redmond asymptotics" in region Omega_0 (all inter-particle distances grow uniformly) from the "AM asymptotics" in regions Omega_nu (one particle recedes faster than the other two), where a long-ranged, non-central, velocity-dependent three-body correlation appears. Second, it reports the nonperturbative derivation of the large-distance behavior of the optical potential for elastic scattering of a charged particle off a charged composite target, using the asymptotic three-body Coulomb wave functions to evaluate the leading singularity in the zero-momentum-transfer limit.

## Key numbers

- The local momentum in region Omega_alpha is given by k_alpha(rho_alpha) = k_alpha + a_alpha(hat_rho_alpha)/rho_alpha, where the vector a_alpha scales as |a_alpha| ~ (M_alpha/q_alpha) * |(e_beta/m_beta) - (e_gamma/m_gamma)| in the limit k_alpha -> 0, inversely proportional to the relative velocity of particle alpha and the pair center of mass.
- The nonstatic part of the optical potential behaves as -a/(2 rho_alpha^4) in coordinate space for large intercluster separation rho_alpha, with the same polarizability a as found in perturbative approaches.
- The static potential in momentum space behaves as 4 pi e_alpha e_beta_gamma / Delta_alpha^2 in the limit Delta_alpha -> 0, corresponding to e_alpha e_beta_gamma / rho_alpha in coordinate space.
- The off-shell nonstatic optical potential behaves as C * Delta_alpha + o(Delta_alpha) in the zero-momentum-transfer limit, with an energy- and momentum-dependent strength factor C = C(E, q_alpha).

## Context

This review extends the line of work on three-body Coulomb scattering that began with Redmond's asymptotic form (1972) and was advanced by Alt and Mukhamedzhanov (1992), who derived the correct asymptotic behavior in the Omega_nu regions including the new three-body correlation effect. The paper also reports the nonperturbative derivation of the optical potential's long-range behavior (Alt and Mukhamedzhanov, 1995), confirming that the polarizability a from second-order perturbation theory (Kleinman and Mullin, 1988) remains valid above the three-body threshold. The work enables more accurate approximate calculations of ionization and breakup processes, as well as the first reliable calculations of proton-deuteron scattering above breakup threshold for realistic potentials (Alt, Mukhamedzhanov, and Sattarov, 1998).
