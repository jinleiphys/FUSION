# Global polarization in heavy-ion collisions based on axial vortical effect

> Global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ is calculated based on the axial vortical effect (AVE). Simulations are performed within the model of the three-fluid dynamics. Equations of state with the deconfinement transition result in a good agreement with STAR data for both $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ polarization, in particular, with the $Λ$-$\barΛ$ splitting. Suppression of the gravitational-anomaly contribution required for the data reproduction is in agreement with predictions of the QCD lattice simulations. Predictions for the global polarization in forthcoming experiments at lower collision energies are made. These forthcoming data will provide a critical test for the AVE and thermodynamic mechanisms of the polarization.

## Key claim

The axial vortical effect (AVE) approach can reproduce STAR data on global polarization of both Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons, including the Λ-\(\bar{\Lambda}\) splitting, but only when equations of state with a deconfinement transition are used and the gravitational-anomaly contribution is suppressed by more than one order of magnitude; the purely hadronic equation of state fails to reproduce the data.

## Method

Simulations were performed within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the early nonequilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions using two counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids and a fireball fluid for newly produced particles. The global polarization was calculated from the axial current of strange quarks, using the AVE expression that includes a chemical potential term and a gravitational-anomaly term scaled by a parameter κ, with averaging over the central rapidity region \(|y| \lesssim 0.5\) at fixed impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Global polarization of Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons was calculated for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV up to 200 GeV, with fixed impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm corresponding to 20-50% centrality.
- At the lowest collision energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the data better agree with results without the gravitational term (\(\kappa = 0\)).
- At higher collision energies, the gravitational term must be suppressed by more than one order of magnitude (fitted \(\kappa\) values are displayed in figures, EoS-dependent but same for Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\)).
- The overall uncertainty of the polarization estimate generally does not exceed 20%.

## Context

This work extends the AVE approach previously applied within the Quark-Gluon-String Model (Baznat 2017) and a multiphase transport model (Sun 2017) to the three-fluid dynamics framework. It contrasts with the thermodynamic approach (Becattini 2013, Becattini 2016, Fang 2016) which describes global polarization well but fails for local longitudinal polarization and \(\phi\)/\(K^*\) meson alignment. The results enable critical tests of AVE versus thermodynamic mechanisms using forthcoming data from the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) and Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) at lower collision energies.
