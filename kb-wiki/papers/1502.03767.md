# Electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions: Messengers from the hot and dense phase

> Due to their penetrating nature, electromagnetic probes, i.e., lepton-antilepton pairs (dileptons) and photons are unique tools to gain insight into the nature of the hot and dense medium of strongly-interacting particles created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including hints to the nature of the restoration of chiral symmetry of QCD. Of particular interest are the spectral properties of the electromagnetic current-correlation function of these particles within the dense and/or hot medium. The related theoretical investigations of the in-medium properties of the involved particles in both the partonic and hadronic part of the QCD phase diagram underline the importance of a proper understanding of the properties of various hadron resonances in the medium.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (dileptons and photons) provide direct insight into the hot and dense medium created in heavy-ion collisions, but the paper emphasizes that a proper understanding of the in-medium properties of various hadron resonances is essential for interpreting these probes, and that the medium modifications of vector mesons are dominated by interactions with baryons (including antibaryons) even at high collision energies where the net-baryon number is low.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models for the electromagnetic current-correlation function in both the partonic (QGP) and hadronic phases, using effective hadronic models based on vector-meson dominance (VMD) and many-body quantum field theory. They compare these models to dilepton data from HADES (GSI SIS), NA60 (CERN SPS), and STAR (RHIC) by integrating thermal dilepton rates over fireball evolution models, including transport models (GiBUU, UrQMD), hydrodynamical models, and coarse-graining approaches that bridge transport simulations with equilibrium thermal rates.

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic coupling constant is α_em ≃ 1/137.
- The low-mass vector-meson region is M ≲ 1 GeV, showing ρ, ω, and φ resonances; the intermediate-mass range is 1 GeV ≲ M ≲ 3 GeV.
- The temperature extracted from the NA60 invariant-mass spectrum (after subtracting Drell-Yan and D-meson decay contributions) is T_(M-slope) ≃ 205-230 MeV, well above the pseudo-critical temperature T_c ≃ 160 MeV.
- The ρ-meson vacuum mass is m_ρ ≃ 770 MeV.
- Collision energies covered range from ∼1 A GeV (SIS at GSI) to 200 A GeV (top RHIC energy).
- Specific comparisons shown: 3.5 GeV pp collisions and 1.76 A GeV Ar+KCl collisions (HADES), and 158 A GeV In+In collisions (NA60).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Rapp and Wambach (1999) for in-medium vector-meson spectral functions and the hadronic many-body approach to dilepton production, building on earlier work by Shuryak (1978) that first identified electromagnetic probes as useful observables. The paper reviews comparisons with data from HADES, NA60, and STAR collaborations, and enables predictions for excitation functions (invariant-slope temperature, dilepton excess yield, fireball lifetime) as functions of beam energy, which will be tested by the beam-energy-scan program at RHIC and the future CBM experiment at FAIR. The results also connect to lattice-QCD calculations by Ding (2010) and Brandt (2012) for the QGP spectral function.
