# The Proton Electric Pygmy Dipole Resonance

> The evolution of the low-lying E1 strength in proton-rich nuclei is analyzed in the framework of the self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA). Model calculations are performed for a series of N=20 isotones and Z=18 isotopes. For nuclei close to the proton drip-line, the occurrence of pronounced dipole peaks is predicted in the low-energy region below 10 MeV excitation energy. From the analysis of the proton and neutron transition densities and the structure of the RQRPA amplitudes, it is shown that these states correspond to the proton pygmy dipole resonance.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that proton-rich nuclei near the proton drip-line can exhibit a proton pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in the low-energy region below 10 MeV, despite the general expectation that light nuclei lack collective low-lying dipole strength and that proton skins are suppressed by the Coulomb barrier.

## Method
The authors employed the self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) to calculate the electric dipole response of N=20 isotones and Z=18 isotopes. The density-dependent effective interaction DD-ME1 was used in the particle-hole channel and the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the pairing channel. The discrete RQRPA spectra were averaged with a Lorentzian distribution of width 1 MeV.

## Key numbers
- In N=20 isotones, a pronounced low-energy peak was found at 10.15 MeV in 44Cr and at 9.44 MeV in 46Fe.
- The energy-weighted sum of the strength below 11 MeV in 46Fe corresponds to 2.5% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule.
- In 32Ar, prominent proton PDR peaks were obtained at 8.30, 8.94, 9.36, and 9.60 MeV.
- The energy-weighted sum of the strength below 10 MeV in 32Ar takes 5.6% of the TRK sum rule.
- For the GDR state at 18.78 MeV in 46Fe, the ratio of proton to neutron contribution to the QRPA amplitude was approximately 2, whereas for the pygmy states proton 2qp configurations accounted for approximately 99% of the QRPA amplitude.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei (Vretenar 2001, Paar 2003, Paar 2005) to the proton-rich side of the nuclear chart. Prior theoretical investigations had focused on the neutron PDR in neutron-rich systems using various approaches including Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus quasiparticle RPA with phonon coupling (Colo 2001), the quasiparticle phonon model (Ryezayeva 2002, Enders 2003), and relativistic RPA (Vretenar 2001). The prediction of a proton PDR enables future experimental searches for this mode in proton-rich nuclei near the drip-line and has implications for understanding nuclear structure far from stability.
