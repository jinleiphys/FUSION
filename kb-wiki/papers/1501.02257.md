# Problems with meson spectroscopy involving perturbative loop corrections

> In this talk we review the limitations of including meson loops as perturbative corrections in a solvable quark model. We first discuss meson-meson scattering within a formalism which treats confined quark pairs and mesons on an equal footing. The interaction between the mesons proceeds through $s$-channel meson-exchange diagrams. Next, we develop a perturbative expansion of the model, and show that the resonance poles found in such a treatment, even by accounting for contributions up to fourth order, do not coincide with those obtained with the full model. We conclude that the resonance predictions based on perturbative approximations in quark models are not reliable, especially in those cases where the coupling to the scattering channels is large.

## Key claim

The paper argues that resonance predictions from quark models which include meson loops as perturbative corrections are unreliable, especially when the coupling to scattering channels is large; even including contributions up to fourth order in the perturbative expansion fails to reproduce the pole positions obtained from the exact nonperturbative solution.

## Method

The authors construct a solvable nonrelativistic quark model that treats confined quark-antiquark pairs and free meson-meson states as coupled systems via a delta-shell transition potential, with the interaction proceeding through s-channel exchange of confined quark pairs. They then develop a perturbative expansion of this model in powers of the coupling constant λ², and compare the resonance pole positions from the exact S-matrix with those from the perturbative approximation at leading (Born) order and up to fourth order (N⁴LO), using the K*(892) resonance in P-wave Kπ scattering as a test case.

## Key numbers

- The bare ground-state mass of the nonstrange-strange quark-antiquark system is 1.199 GeV (from Eq. (enl) with E₀₀).
- At the physical coupling λ=1, the exact pole position for the K*(892) resonance is (0.972 − i0.026) GeV.
- The Born (N⁰LO) perturbative approximation gives satisfactory pole positions only for couplings up to λ ≈ 0.3.
- The fourth-order (N⁴LO) perturbative approximation gives well-determined pole positions up to λ ≈ 0.75, but fails completely at λ=1.
- The transition potential parameter a is taken in the range 1.7–2.5 GeV⁻¹ (0.34–0.5 fm).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonperturbative coupled-channel formalism (Khemchandani et al., IJTPGTNO11p179; PRD80p094011) and directly contradicts the common practice in quark models of treating meson loops as perturbative corrections, as done in many spectroscopic studies of exotic hadrons such as the Zc/Zb states (reviewz; hosaka; marina). The results enable future work to avoid unreliable perturbative approximations in favor of exact coupled-channel treatments when extracting resonance parameters from experimental data.
