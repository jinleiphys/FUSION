# Cooper pairing and tripling in one-dimensional spinless fermions with attractive two- and three-body forces

> We theoretically investigate in-medium three-body correlations in one-dimensional spinless fermions with antisymmetrized two- and three-body attractive interactions. By investigating the variational problem of three-body states above the Fermi sea, we illuminate the fate of the in-medium three-body cluster states both in the special case with pure attractive three-body interaction as well as in the case with the coexistence of two- and three-body interactions. Our results testify that the fermion-dimer repulsion is canceled by including the three-body interactions, and stable three-body clusters can be formed. We further feature a phase diagram consisting of the $p$-wave Cooper pairing and Cooper tripling phases in a plane of $p$-wave two- and three-body coupling strengths.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in one-dimensional spinless fermions, stable in-medium three-body clusters (Cooper triples) can form when both attractive two- and three-body interactions are present, because the three-body force cancels the fermion-dimer repulsion that otherwise prevents clustering; however, with only a pure three-body attraction and no two-body force, the in-medium three-body bound state does not exist without an in-vacuum counterpart, unlike in three-dimensional three-component Fermi gases.

## Method

The authors solve a variational three-body problem for states above the Fermi sea using a trial wave function with zero center-of-mass momentum, derived from a Hamiltonian with antisymmetrized short-range p-wave two-body and three-body interactions. They obtain closed integral equations for the three-body energy \(E_3\) and the auxiliary function \(\mathcal{B}(p)\), which are solved numerically via iteration with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 10 k_{\rm F}\). The three-body coupling \(U_3\) is normalized by the in-vacuum critical coupling \(U_{\rm c}\) where a three-body bound state first appears in vacuum.

## Key numbers

* The in-medium three-body bound state with pure three-body interaction appears only when \(U_3/U_{\rm c} \gtrsim 1.41\), where the binding energy \(E_3 + 3E_{\rm F}\) becomes negative; a squeezed Cooper triple regime exists for \(1.38 < U_3/U_{\rm c} < 1.41\) where \(E_3 + 3E_{\rm F} > 0\).
* For the coexistence case at \(1/(k_{\rm F}a) = -1.0\), the three-body coupling is taken as the in-medium critical coupling \(U_{\rm c}^{\rm med} = 0.986\,U_{\rm c}\).
* The phase boundary between p-wave pairing and Cooper tripling is determined by \(E_3 = E_{2,p}\); the transition line shows a threshold around \(1/(k_{\rm F}a) \simeq 2\) below which the triple phase cannot exist even with strong two-body attraction.
* The momentum cutoff is set to \(\Lambda = 10 k_{\rm F}\) and discretization uses \(N = 1000\) points with convergence criterion \(10^{-8}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Guo 2022) which found that one-dimensional spinless fermions with only attractive p-wave two-body interactions are stable against three-body clustering due to fermion-dimer repulsion. It also builds on earlier work (Sekino 2021) that introduced a constant three-body coupling phenomenologically. The present paper uses a more realistic antisymmetrized three-body interaction with minimal momentum dependence, and provides a phase diagram showing the competition between p-wave Cooper pairing and Cooper tripling. The results enable future experimental tests via three-body loss measurements in cold atomic systems and may inform studies of three-body forces in nuclear physics.
