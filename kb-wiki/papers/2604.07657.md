# Gaussian pseudogauge invariant hydrodynamics with spin

> Extending the Gaussian covariant hydrodynamics approach [1] using torsion as an auxiliary field we formulate a fluctuating hydrodynamics with spin which is covariant with respect to pseudo-gauge transformations as well as generally covariant with respect to foliations. This is done via the second order gravitational Ward identities, derived here in the torsionful case. This ensures that, while angular momentum observables depend covariantly on the pseudo-gauge, the dynamics is pseudo-gauge independent, thus clarifying the role of the pseudo-gauge in hydrodynamics with spin

## Key claim

The authors extend the Gaussian covariant hydrodynamics approach to include spin by using torsion as an auxiliary field, formulating a fluctuating hydrodynamics that is covariant with respect to pseudo-gauge transformations and generally covariant with respect to foliations, but only under the condition that the partition function can be approximated by a Gaussian and that the dynamics is defined at the ensemble level in terms of correlators rather than deterministic evolution equations.

## Method

The authors derive second-order gravitational Ward identities in the torsionful case by differentiating the covariant conservation laws for energy-momentum and spin with respect to the Vierbein and spin connection, obtaining relations between two-point correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor and spin tensor. They then construct a Gaussian partition function ansatz where the probability of a given configuration of energy-momentum and spin tensors is determined by their averages and correlators, and show that the Ward identities together with linear response relations can evolve this ensemble in a pseudo-gauge independent manner.

## Key numbers

- The partition function expansion to leading order (Eq. lnzexpansion) contains second derivatives of ln Z with respect to beta_mu and Omega_{gamma rho}, evaluated at equilibrium, which define the Gaussian kernel coefficients C, Q, and W
- The Ward identities (Eqs. 2point1, 2point2, 2point3) contain delta-function contact terms proportional to 1/sqrt(-e) times combinations of average energy-momentum and spin tensors
- The linear response evolution equations (Eqs. Tuvcorr, Suvcorr) involve Fourier transforms in the space-like directions with an infinitesimal positive constant epsilon in the adiabatic switching-on procedure

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian covariant hydrodynamics approach of Torrieri (2021) to include spin degrees of freedom, building on prior spin hydrodynamics formulations by Becattini et al. (2015), Florkowski et al. (2018), and the torsion-based approaches of Gallegos and Gurses (2020). The paper addresses the conceptual problem of pseudo-gauge dependence in spin hydrodynamics, which has been debated by Speranza (2020), Jain (2022), and others, by showing that pseudo-gauge transformations can be treated as redundancies analogous to ghosts in quantum field theory when fluctuations are included non-perturbatively. This enables a causal, pseudo-gauge independent formulation of spin hydrodynamics that can be implemented numerically via a Crooks theorem based algorithm, and clarifies why spin is a slowly equilibrating variable despite not being associated with a conserved quantity.
