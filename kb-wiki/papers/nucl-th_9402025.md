# On the Momentum Dependence of the Nucleon - Nucleus Optical Potential

> The momentum dependence of the mean-field contribution to the real part of the optical model potential is investigated employing realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. Within a non-relativistic approach a momentum dependence originates from the non-locality of the Fock exchange term. Deducing the real part of the optical model from a relativistic Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock approximation for the self-energy of the nucleons yields an additional momentum dependence originating from the non-relativistic reduction of the self-energy. It is demonstrated that large Fock terms are required in the non-relativistic approach to simulate these relativistic features. A comparison is made between a local density approximation for the optical model and a direct evaluation in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the real part of the nucleon-nucleus optical potential can be described equally well within either a non-relativistic or a relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock framework, but the physical origin of that dependence is fundamentally different in the two approaches. The paper demonstrates that strong Fock exchange terms are required in the non-relativistic approach to simulate the relativistic features that arise from the non-relativistic reduction of the Dirac self-energy.

## Method

The authors compared non-relativistic and relativistic parameterizations of the G-matrix derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. For the non-relativistic approach they used the M3Y parameterization of the G-matrix, evaluating the local equivalent potential including the Fock exchange term via the Negele-Vautherin approximation with the Slater approximation for the mixed density. For the relativistic approach they employed a density-dependent Dirac-Hartree and Dirac-Hartree-Fock parameterization of the G-matrix from the Bonn A potential, calculating scattering phase shifts by expanding the Dirac spinors in a discrete basis of spherical Bessel functions and extracting phase shifts from the asymptotic form of the wavefunctions at a large radius.

## Key numbers

- The scalar isoscalar direct part of the M3Y interaction is given by: V_dir(r) = 7999.0 exp(-4r)/(4r) - 2134.25 exp(-2.5r)/(2.5r) MeV.
- The scalar isoscalar exchange part of the M3Y interaction is given by: V_exc(r) = 4631.375 exp(-4r)/(4r) - 1787.125 exp(-2.5r)/(2.5r) - 7.8474 exp(-0.7072r)/(0.7072r) MeV.
- The attraction of the Schroedinger equivalent potential (SEP) for nucleons with an energy of 100 MeV is reduced by more than a factor of 2 compared to the SEP for nucleons at an energy of 5 MeV, for both 16O and 40Ca.
- The Hartree term in the non-relativistic M3Y approach yields only about 25 percent of the total attraction in nuclear matter.
- The local density approximation (LDA) overestimates the calculated binding energy per nucleon by 3.2 MeV for 16O and by 2.3 MeV for 40Ca.
- The meson mass parameters used in the relativistic Dirac-Hartree calculation were m_sigma = 550 MeV and m_omega = 783 MeV.
- The pion coupling constant used in the M3Y parameterization is g_pi^2/(4pi) ≈ 13.9, with C_pi = 3.4877 MeV and mu_pi = 0.7072 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends earlier Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for finite nuclei by Fritz et al. (1993) and compares them with the non-relativistic M3Y parameterization of Bertsch et al. (1977). The study shows that a local density approximation (LDA) captures the main energy dependence of the optical potential but fails to reproduce precise radial shapes and binding energies. The paper concludes that empirical data on nucleon-nucleus scattering cannot distinguish between the relativistic and non-relativistic approaches, and suggests that heavy-ion scattering data might allow such a distinction.
