# Quark-diquark models and baryonic fluctuations in QCD

> We study the baryonic fluctuations of electric charge, baryon number and strangeness, by considering a realization of the Hadron Resonance Gas model in the light flavor sector of QCD. We elaborate on the idea that the susceptibilities can be saturated with excited baryonic states with a quark-diquark structure with a linearly confining interaction identical up to a constant to the quark-antiquark potential, $V_{qD}^\prime(r) = V_{q{\bar q}}^\prime(r)$. We obtain an overall good agreement with the spectrum obtained with other quark models and with lattice data for the fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the asymptotic three-body phase space for confined $qqq$ systems (scaling as $M^{12}$) is much larger than the behavior actually observed in the Relativized Quark Model (scaling as $M^{6}$ to $M^{8}$), and this mismatch suggests that excited baryons effectively behave as two-body quark-diquark systems, which can then saturate the baryonic susceptibilities measured on the lattice.

## Method

The authors compute baryonic susceptibilities (electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness fluctuations) within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, using a baryon spectrum generated from a relativistic quark-diquark Hamiltonian with a linearly confining potential $V_{qD}(r) = -\tau/r + \sigma r + \mu$, where the string tension $\sigma = (0.42\ \mathrm{GeV})^2$ and $\tau = \pi/12$. The spectrum is obtained variationally using a 3-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator basis, and the model parameters (constituent quark mass $m_{\mathrm{cons}}$, strange current quark mass $\hat{m}_s$, and additive constant $\mu$) are fitted to lattice data for the second-order baryonic susceptibilities at temperatures $T \le 165\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key numbers

* String tension: $\sigma = (0.42\ \mathrm{GeV})^2$, used in the quark-diquark confining potential.
* Mass difference between scalar and axial-vector diquarks: $\Delta m_D \simeq 0.21\ \mathrm{GeV}$, adopted from Jaffe (2004).
* Best-fit parameters: $m_{D,\mathrm{ns}} = 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $m_{u,d} = 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $\hat{m}_s = 0.10\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $\mu = -0.459\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
* The strange current quark mass from the fit is compatible with the PDG range: $80\ \mathrm{MeV} \lesssim \hat{m}_s \lesssim 120\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* The constituent quark mass from the fit lies in the range: $100\ \mathrm{MeV} \lesssim m_{\mathrm{cons}} \lesssim 400\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* The additive constant $\mu$ in the potential lies in the range: $-0.7\ \mathrm{GeV} \lesssim \mu \lesssim 0\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
* The quark-diquark spectrum agrees with the Relativized Quark Model spectrum for baryon masses below $M < 2400\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas approach to baryonic fluctuations by using a quark-diquark model for the baryon spectrum, motivated by the observation that the cumulative number of baryonic states in the Relativized Quark Model of Capstick (1986) scales as $M^{6}$-$M^{8}$ rather than the $M^{12}$ expected for three-body systems. The study builds on earlier lattice QCD evidence for diquark correlations in the nucleon from Alexandrou (2006) and DeGrand (2007), and on previous quark-diquark models by Santopinto (2014). The results enable a global thermodynamic test of the quark-diquark picture against lattice data for baryonic fluctuations, complementing traditional one-to-one mapping of resonance states.
