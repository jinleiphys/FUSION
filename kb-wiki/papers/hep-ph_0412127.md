# Triquark correlations and pentaquarks in a QCD sum rule approach

> The role of quark correlations in the description of hadron dynamics in many domains of physics, from low energy dynamics to very hot(dense) systems, is being appreciated. Strong correlations of two quarks (diquark) have been widely investigated in this respect. Recently, we have proposed a dynamical scheme to describe the $Θ^+$ pentaquark in which also three quark correlations (triquark) were instrumental in producing a low mass exotic state. We perform a study, within the QCD sum rule approach including OPE and direct instanton contributions, of triquark correlations and obtain two quasi-bound light $ud\bar{s}$ color quark clusters of 800 MeV and 930 MeV respectively.

# Triquark correlations and pentaquarks in a QCD sum rule approach

## Key claim

Within the QCD sum rule approach including both operator product expansion (OPE) and direct instanton contributions, the authors obtain two quasi-bound light $ud\bar{s}$ color quark clusters of 800 MeV and 930 MeV respectively, but only when instanton effects are included, without instantons, no stability plateau exists and the masses cannot be determined. The existence of these triquark states supports a triquark-diquark clusterization scheme for the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, but the authors note that the experimental status of the $\Theta^+$ is controversial and that the study of quark correlations is valuable regardless of the pentaquark's existence.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule analysis of $ud\bar{s}$ triquark color clusters with isospin $I=0$, spin $S=1/2$, and color $C=3_c$, considering two types of color structure for the $ud$ subsystem: state A with $C_{ud}=\bar{3}_c$ and state B with $C_{ud}=6_c$. They compute the correlator of interpolating currents using OPE up to dimension six, then incorporate direct instanton contributions via the zero-mode approximation for the quark propagator in an instanton background, using the Shuryak instanton model with a delta-function density $n(\rho)=n_{eff}\delta(\rho-\rho_c)$. Only chirality-odd sum rules are used because they show stability plateaus when instanton contributions are included, while chirality-even sum rules do not.

## Key numbers

- Triquark state A (color $C_{ud}=\bar{3}_c$) mass: $M_{tri}^A \approx 800$ MeV, obtained with a mixed A-type current where $f \approx -t$ and threshold $s_0 = 1.8$ GeV
- Triquark state B (color $C_{ud}=6_c$) mass: $M_{tri}^B \approx 930$ MeV, obtained with threshold $s_0 = 1.72$ GeV
- Nucleon mass reproduced as $M_N = 940$ MeV for average instanton size $\rho_c \approx 1.6$ GeV$^{-1}$ with threshold $s_0 = 1.75$ GeV
- Input parameters used: $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -(250\ \text{MeV})^3$, $b = 0.24\ \text{GeV}^4$, $m_0^2 = 1\ \text{GeV}^2$, $m_s = 150\ \text{MeV}$, $f_s = 0.8$
- For comparison, a $ud$-diquark color system was previously found to have mass $m_{ud} \approx 420 \sim 450$ MeV in instanton-inclusive sum rule calculations (Shuryak and Faccioli)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model study (Lee, Kochelev, Vento, 2004) of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark, where instanton-induced interactions produced a light $ud\bar{s}$ triquark cluster of approximately 750 MeV. It builds on prior QCD sum rule studies of diquark correlations including instanton effects (Shuryak; Faccioli) and on the triquark-diquark clustering scheme proposed by Karliner and Lipkin (2003) using generalized color magnetic interactions. The results suggest that all published QCD sum rule calculations of pentaquark masses (Ioffe; various SR pentaquark papers) should be reanalyzed including direct instanton contributions, and the work aims to inspire lattice studies of quark correlations and the role of instantons in exotic hadron dynamics.
