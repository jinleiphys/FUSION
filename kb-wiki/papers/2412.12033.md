# The causal structure of the quark propagator

> We study the causal structure of the quark propagator with the spectral DSE. The spectral gap equation is solved with the input of the spectral representation of the gluon and a causal STI-construction for the quark-gluon vertex. The latter includes a potential infrared enhancement of the vertex strength of the classical tensor structure that accommodates for the physical strength of chiral symmetry breaking. We find a critical vertex strength, below which the quark has a Källén-Lehmann representation. While the nature of the first singularity does not change above the critical strength, we find that the quark propagator features at least two additional pairs of complex conjugate poles that are located approximately at the sum of quark pole mass and peak position of the quark-gluon coupling. These additional poles lead to violations of causality, if they persist in $S$-matrix elements. While the vertex strength of the classical tensor structure in full QCD is below the critical one, that of commonly used vertex models, which rely solely on the classical vertex structure, is typically above it. Finally, we discuss how these additional poles could be avoided in full QCD, where part of chiral symmetry breaking is generated by the other tensor structures in the quark-gluon vertex.

## Key claim

The quark propagator's causal structure changes qualitatively above a critical vertex strength: while below this threshold the propagator admits a Källén-Lehmann representation, above it at least two additional pairs of complex conjugate poles emerge, leading to causality violations if they persist in S-matrix elements; however, the vertex strength of the classical tensor structure in full QCD is below this critical value.

## Method

The authors solve the spectral Dyson-Schwinger equation (spectral DSE) for the inverse quark propagator in the full complex frequency plane, using spectral representations for the gluon propagator and a Slavnov-Taylor identity (STI)-based construction for the quark-gluon vertex that retains only the classical tensor structure. The vertex dressing is proportional to the inverse ghost dressing function with a global strength factor η, and the spectral gap equation is solved iteratively by scanning η to track qualitative changes in the analytic structure of the quark propagator.

## Key numbers

- The peak position of the quark-gluon coupling is located at approximately p_glue^peak ≈ 0.6 GeV.
- The critical transition occurs for a relative coupling strength α_η/α_phys between 1.245 and 1.344, corresponding to η values of 0.829 and 0.872 respectively.
- For η = 0.829 (α_η/α_phys = 1.245), the quark propagator has a real pole at m_pole = 342 MeV; for η = 0.872 (α_η/α_phys = 1.344), the real pole is at m_pole = 214 MeV.
- The additional complex conjugate poles are located approximately at the sum of the quark pole mass and the peak position of the quark-gluon coupling (≈ 0.6 GeV).
- The physical coupling peak ratio from full QCD computations (Ihssen 2024) is α_phys; the vertex model requires α_η/α_phys ≈ 1.4 to reproduce the physical amount of chiral symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends the spectral DSE framework developed by Horak et al. (2020-2023) for Yang-Mills theories to the quark propagator, using gluon spectral reconstructions from Horak et al. (2021, 2023). It contradicts earlier results by Stainsby and Maris (1990s) and Fischer et al. (2008) that found complex first singularities, attributing this to effective interactions that diverge in the soft gluon limit. The findings suggest that full QCD computations with all twelve tensor structures of the quark-gluon vertex (as in Cyrol 2017, Gao 2021, Ihssen 2024) may avoid causality violations, while commonly used vertex models relying solely on the classical tensor structure are prone to generating unphysical complex conjugate poles.
