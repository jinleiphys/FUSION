# Cancellation of infrared divergences at finite temperature

> We consider a typical hard scattering process in a heat bath of photons and electrons at temperature, $T$, in finite temperature QED. We show that, when the hard scattering scale is much larger than the temperature, the infrared pieces of both the real and virtual parts of the cross section factorise; these can be exponentiated and cancel between each other to all orders in perturbation theory. We use the technique of Grammer and Yennie to give a prescription for the extraction of the infrared divergent parts, and for the form of the finite remainder. Symmetry arguments are used to show the finiteness of new terms arising in the $T \neq 0$ part of the computation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the exact (non-eikonal) hard scattering cross section in finite-temperature QED is infrared finite to all orders in perturbation theory, provided the hard scattering scale is much larger than the temperature, and that the eikonal approximation is therefore valid for such calculations.

## Method

The author uses the Grammer-Yennie technique to separate the photon propagator (for virtual photons) and the polarization sum (for real photons) into a K-type part containing infrared divergences and a G-type part that is infrared finite. The K-type contributions are shown to factorize and exponentiate, while the G-type contributions are proven finite using symmetry arguments under \(k \to -k\) that soften logarithmic subdivergences arising from the finite-temperature Bose distribution function. The analysis is performed for a hard scattering process \(\gamma^*(q) e(p) \rightarrow e(p')\) with \(-q^2 \gg T^2\) in a heat bath of photons and electrons at temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- The hard scattering scale condition: \(-q^2 \gg T^2\), where \(T\) is the temperature of the heat bath
- The Bose distribution function for thermal photons: \(N(|{\bf k}|) = (\exp(|{\bf k}|/T) - 1)^{-1}\), which behaves as \(\sim 1/k\) for small \(k\)
- The Fermi-Dirac distribution for thermal electrons: \(N_f(|{\bf k}|) = (\exp(|{\bf k}|/T) + 1)^{-1}\), which tends to \(1/2\) in the infrared limit \(k \to 0\)
- The virtual K-photon contribution factorizes as \(B = \frac{i e^2}{2(2\pi)^4} \int d^4 k J^2 \left[ \frac{1}{k^2 + i\epsilon} - 2\pi i \delta(k^2) N(|{\bf k}|) \right]\), where \(J_\mu(k) = \left( \frac{(2p' - k)_\mu}{k^2 - 2p' \cdot k} - \frac{(2p - k)_\mu}{k^2 - 2p \cdot k} \right)\)

## Context

This work extends the proof of infrared finiteness in thermal QED by Weldon (1990s), who demonstrated the result within the eikonal approximation, to the exact case without that approximation. It uses the Grammer-Yennie (1970s) technique originally developed for zero-temperature QED and adapts it to finite temperature, showing that the Bloch-Nordsieck theorem holds at finite temperature. The result enables reliable calculation of hard scattering processes in thermal environments such as the early universe or quark-gluon plasma, where the eikonal approximation can now be safely employed.
