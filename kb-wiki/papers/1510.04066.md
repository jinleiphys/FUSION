# New formulae for the $(-2)$ moment of the photo-absorption cross section, $σ_{_{-2}}$

> Two new formulae for the $(-2)$ moment of the photo-absorption cross section, $σ_{_{-2}}$, have been determined, respectively, from the 1988 compilation of Dietrich and Berman and a mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficient, $a_{sym}(A)$. The data for $A\gtrsim50$ follow, with a {\small $RMS$} deviation of 6\%, the power-law $σ_{_{-2}}=2.4 A^{5/3}$$μ$b/MeV, which is in agreement with Migdal's calculation of $σ_{_{-2}}=2.25A^{5/3}$$μ$b/MeV based on the hydrodynamic model and the $σ_{_{-2}}$ sum rule. The additional inclusion of $a_{sym}(A)$ provides a deeper insight to the nuclear polarization of $A\geqq10$ nuclei.

# New formulae for the $(-2)$ moment of the photo-absorption cross section, $\sigma_{_{-2}}$

## Key claim

The 1988 Dietrich and Berman photoneutron compilation yields a new empirical power-law $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 2.4 A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV for $A \gtrsim 50$ with 6% RMS deviation, which is lower than Levinger's 1957 formula of $3.5 A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV and closer to Migdal's hydrodynamic prediction of $2.25 A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV; however, this agreement holds only for nuclei where photoneutron channels dominate, and the missing photoproton contributions cause large deviations for $A \lesssim 50$ nuclei.

## Method

The $(-2)$ moment $\sigma_{_{-2}} = \int \sigma_{\text{total}}(E_\gamma) / E_\gamma^2 \, dE_\gamma$ was computed from the 1988 Dietrich and Berman compilation of photoneutron cross-section data (including $(\gamma,n)$, $(\gamma,pn)$, $(\gamma,2n)$, $(\gamma,3n)$, and $(\gamma,F)$ channels) using integration between the $(\gamma,n)$ threshold and an upper limit of approximately 20–50 MeV. A power-law fit to these data was performed, and a second formula was derived by substituting a mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficient $a_{\text{sym}}(A) = S_v (1 - S_s / (S_v A^{1/3}))$ from Tian et al. (2013) into Migdal's hydrodynamic expression for nuclear polarizability.

## Key numbers

- $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 2.4 A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV from the Dietrich and Berman compilation, with RMS deviation of 6% for $A \gtrsim 50$ (excluding $^{58}$Ni)
- Migdal's calculation: $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 2.25 A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV, based on $a_{\text{sym}} = 23$ MeV and $r_0 = 1.2$ fm
- Levinger's 1957 empirical formula: $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 3.5 \kappa A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV, with $\kappa = 1$ for $A \gtrsim 20$
- Mass-dependent formula: $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 1.8 A^2 / (A^{1/3} - 1.27)$ μb/MeV for $A \geq 10$, using $S_v \approx 28.32$ MeV and $S_s / S_v \approx 1.27$
- RMS deviation of 30% for the power-law fit over all $A$ with $\kappa = 1$

## Context

This work updates the empirical $\sigma_{_{-2}}$ formula from Levinger (1957), which was based on only eleven data points with approximate corrections, using the more comprehensive Dietrich and Berman (1988) compilation of photoneutron data from monoenergetic photon beams. The new formula agrees with the earlier review by Berman and Fultz (1975), who found $\sigma_{_{-2}} = 2.39(20) A^{5/3}$ μb/MeV for $A \gtrsim 60$. The mass-dependent symmetry energy approach from Tian et al. (2013) provides a parameter-free prediction that accounts for the larger polarization effects in light nuclei ($10 \leq A \lesssim 20$) and may estimate missing photoproton contributions. This work enables improved treatment of the polarization potential in Coulomb-excitation codes such as GOSIA, which currently use Levinger's formula, and provides a test for three-nucleon forces and neutron star models.
