# Nucleon axial charge and pion decay constant from two-flavor lattice QCD

> The axial charge of the nucleon $g_A$ and the pion decay constant $f_π$ are computed in two-flavor lattice QCD. The simulations are carried out on lattices of various volumes and lattice spacings. Results are reported for pion masses as low as $m_π=130\,\mbox{MeV}$. Both quantities, $g_A$ and $f_π$, suffer from large finite size effects, which to leading order ChEFT and ChPT turn out to be identical. By considering the naturally renormalized ratio $g_A/f_π$, we observe a universal behavior as a function of decreasing quark mass. From extrapolating the ratio to the physical point, we find $g_A^R=1.29(5)(3)$, using the physical value of $f_π$ as input and $r_0=0.50(1)$ to set the scale. In a subsequent calculation we attempt to extrapolate $g_A$ and $f_π$ separately to the infinite volume. Both volume and quark mass dependencies of $g_A$ and $f_π$ are found to be well decribed by ChEFT and ChPT. We find at the physical point $g_A^R=1.24(4)$ and $f_π^R=89.6(1.1)(1.8)\,\mbox{MeV}$. Both sets of results are in good agreement with experiment. As a by-product we obtain the low-energy constant $\bar{l}_4=4.2(1)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) can be accurately computed from two-flavor lattice QCD, but only after accounting for large finite-size effects that are identical at leading order in chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). The key hedge is that the ratio \(g_A/f_\pi\) is free of these finite-size corrections and renormalization uncertainties, providing a more robust determination of \(g_A\) than direct extrapolation.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of nonperturbatively \(O(a)\) improved Wilson fermions and the Wilson plaquette action, at three lattice spacings (\(a = 0.076\), \(0.071\), and \(0.060\) fm) and multiple volumes, covering pion masses down to \(m_\pi = 130\) MeV. They computed the bare axial charge \(g_A\) from ratios of two- and three-point nucleon correlation functions and the bare pion decay constant \(af_\pi\) from standard methods, then applied finite-size corrections from ChEFT/ChPT to extrapolate to infinite volume. The renormalized axial charge was obtained both from the ratio \(g_A/f_\pi\) (which cancels the renormalization constant \(Z_A\)) and from separate infinite-volume extrapolations of \(g_A\) and \(f_\pi\) using nonperturbatively computed \(Z_A\).

## Key numbers

*   From the ratio \(g_A/f_\pi\) extrapolated to the physical point: \(g_A^R = 1.29(5)(3)\), using the physical value \(f_\pi^R = 92.2\) MeV as input and \(r_0 = 0.50(1)\) fm to set the scale.
*   From separate infinite-volume extrapolations at the lightest pion mass \(m_\pi = 130(5)\) MeV: \(g_A^R = 1.24(4)\) and \(f_\pi^R = 89.7(1.5)(1.8)\) MeV.
*   From a chiral fit to \(f_\pi^R\) data including adjacent points: \(f_\pi^R = 89.6(1.1)(1.8)\) MeV at the physical point.
*   Low-energy constant from the \(f_\pi\) chiral fit: \(\bar{l}_4 = 4.2(1)\).
*   Chiral fit to \(g_A^R\) data gave \(g_A^0 = 1.26(7)\) and \(B_9^r(m_{\pi\,{\rm phys}}) = (-2.1 \pm 1.0)\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of \(g_A\) (e.g., Edwards (2005), Yamazaki (2008), Alexandrou (2010), Lin (2011)) that typically underestimated the experimental value, by including simulations at nearly physical pion mass and multiple volumes to control finite-size effects. The approach of using the ratio \(g_A/f_\pi\) to cancel finite-size corrections was motivated by earlier work by Beane (2004), Khan (2006), and Colangelo (2005). The results enable reliable first-principles determinations of nucleon axial structure and provide a benchmark for future three-flavor and dynamical quark simulations, while also demonstrating that ChEFT/ChPT describes the volume and quark-mass dependence of both \(g_A\) and \(f_\pi\) well.
