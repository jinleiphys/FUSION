# Measurement of direct neutron capture by neutron-rich sulfur isotopes

> Thermal neutron capture cross sections for $^{34}$S(n,$γ$)$^{35}$S and $^{36}$S(n,$γ$)$^{37}$S have been measured and spectroscopic factors of the final states have been extracted. The calculated direct-capture cross sections reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim
The direct-capture (DC) mechanism dominates thermal neutron capture for the neutron-rich sulfur isotopes $^{34}$S and $^{36}$S, and the measured thermal cross sections allow extraction of spectroscopic factors that are less model-dependent than those from transfer reactions, provided there are no compound-nucleus levels near threshold.

## Method
Thermal neutron capture cross sections for $^{34}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{35}$S and $^{36}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{37}$S were measured at the BR1 reactor in Mol, Belgium using a Ge detector on an enriched elemental sulfur sample (93.26% $^{34}$S, 5.933% $^{36}$S). Neutron flux and detector efficiency were calibrated using $^{35}$Cl, and data were corrected for multiple neutron scattering (5.6% for $^{34}$S, 7% for $^{36}$S). Spectroscopic factors were extracted from the experimental thermal cross sections using a direct-capture formalism with folding potentials based on experimental charge distributions and thermal elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers
- Total thermal capture cross section for $^{34}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{35}$S: 247 $\pm$ 9 mbarn (average from primary and secondary transitions)
- Total thermal capture cross section for $^{36}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{37}$S: 242 $\pm$ 12 mbarn (average from primary and secondary transitions)
- Systematic uncertainties: 3.6% for $^{34}$S, 4.8% for $^{36}$S
- Multiple neutron scattering corrections: 5.6% for $^{34}$S, 7% for $^{36}$S
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3/2$^-$ state at 2.348 MeV in $^{35}$S: 0.48 from (n,$\gamma$), compared to 0.32-0.55 from (d,p)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3/2$^-$ state at 0.647 MeV in $^{37}$S: 0.54 from (n,$\gamma$), compared to 0.44-0.70 from (d,p)
- Sum of calculated direct-capture cross sections for $^{34}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{35}$S: 190-295 mbarn, compared to experimental sum of 245 mbarn
- Sum of calculated direct-capture cross sections for $^{36}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{37}$S: 162-261 mbarn, compared to experimental sum of 239 mbarn

## Context
This work extends the direct-capture approach previously applied to $^{36}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{37}$S by Beer et al. (1995) and provides new measurements for $^{34}$S(n,$\gamma$)$^{35}$S. The results contradict statistical model (Hauser-Feshbach) calculations by Rauscher et al. (1996), which overestimate the cross sections by about an order of magnitude due to low level density in the compound nuclei. The extracted spectroscopic factors enable more reliable calculation of direct-capture contributions at thermonuclear energies relevant to s-process nucleosynthesis in the S-Cl-Ar-Ca region, inhomogeneous big-bang scenarios, and alpha-rich freeze-out in supernova type II neutrino bubbles.
