# Parity-Violating Electron-Deuteron Scattering

> The longitudinal asymmetry due to $Z^0$ exchange is calculated in quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering at momentum transfers $|Q^2| \simeq 0.1$ GeV$^2$ relevant for the SAMPLE experiment. The deuteron and $pn$ scattering-state wave functions are obtained from solutions of a Schrödinger equation with the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential. Electromagnetic and weak neutral one- and two-nucleon currents are included in the calculation. The two-nucleon currents of pion range are shown to be identical to those derived in Chiral Perturbation Theory. The results indicate that two-body contributions to the asymmetry are small ($\simeq$ 0.2%) around the quasi-elastic peak, but become relatively more significant ($\simeq$ 3%) in the high-energy wing of the quasi-elastic peak.

## Key claim

The longitudinal asymmetry in quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering at momentum transfers relevant for the SAMPLE experiment is calculated including two-nucleon currents, showing that two-body contributions are small (about 0.2%) near the quasi-elastic peak but become relatively more significant (about 3%) in the high-energy wing of the peak, though the abstract does not specify that these results depend on using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential and that axial two-body contributions are neglected.

## Method

The deuteron and \(pn\) scattering-state wave functions were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential. Electromagnetic and weak neutral one- and two-nucleon currents were included, with two-nucleon currents of pion range shown to be identical to those derived in Chiral Perturbation Theory; shorter-range currents were evaluated using the Riska prescription. The asymmetry was computed from nuclear response functions in the quasi-elastic regime at momentum transfers \(|Q^2| \simeq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), with final-state interactions included in partial waves up to \(J_{\rm max} > 7\).

## Key numbers

- Two-body contributions to the asymmetry are about 0.2% around the quasi-elastic peak at \(|Q^2| \simeq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\).
- Two-body contributions become relatively more significant, about 3%, in the high-energy wing of the quasi-elastic peak.
- The contribution to the asymmetry associated with the electromagnetic-axial current interference response function is about 20%.
- The nucleon axial coupling constant used is \(g_A = 1.2654\), with cutoff mass \(\Lambda_A = 1\) GeV/c\(^2\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the parity-violating asymmetry in electron-deuteron scattering, such as those by Hadjimichael et al. (1997) which included one-body currents and final-state interactions but neglected two-body currents, and by Hwang et al. which included pion-exchange currents only in the electromagnetic sector. The results are directly relevant to the SAMPLE experiment at Bates and enable more precise extraction of the nucleon's strange magnetic and axial form factors; the calculation can also be repeated for other momentum transfers relevant to the planned SAMPLE-Lite experiment and JLab's G0 experiment.
