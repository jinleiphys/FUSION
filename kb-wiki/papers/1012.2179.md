# Energy systematics of heavy nuclei -- mean field models in comparison

> We compare the systematics of binding energies computed within the standard and extended versions of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model and the Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) model. The general trends for the binding energies for super-heavy nuclei are significantly different for these models. The SHF models tend to underbind the superheavy nuclei, while, RMF models show just the opposite trend. The extended RMF model seems to provide remarkable improvements over the results obtained for the standard RMF model.

# Energy systematics of heavy nuclei -- mean field models in comparison

## Key claim

The systematic trends in binding energy errors for super-heavy nuclei (A > 220) are opposite between Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) models, which tend to underbind, and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, which tend to overbind; however, the extended RMF model BSR4, which includes all cross-interaction terms for sigma, omega and rho mesons up to quartic order, achieves errors comparable to the best SHF models, suggesting that the specific form of the non-linear meson couplings is the crucial ingredient for improving the trend.

## Method

Binding energies were computed for all experimentally known even-even nuclei using four parameter sets each for the RMF model (NL3, TM1, FSUGold, BSR4) and the SHF model (SkI3, SLy6, BSk4, SV-min). The RMF equations were solved by expanding Dirac spinors in axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis functions with major oscillator shells N_F = 18 and N_B = 20; pairing correlations were treated in the constant gap approximation with Delta = 11.2/sqrt(A) MeV, and quadrupole correlation energies were estimated using a simple formula E_rot = 2.2 sqrt(beta_2 - 0.05) m/m* MeV. The SHF models used microscopic treatments for center-of-mass correction, pairing with a density-dependent zero-range force, and rotational projection corrections.

## Key numbers

- RMS errors for binding energies over 513 even-even nuclei: BSR4 (2.6 MeV), FSUGold (6.5 MeV), NL3 (3.8 MeV), TM1 (5.9 MeV), SV-min (1.6 MeV), BSk4 (1.1 MeV), SkI3 (2.6 MeV), SLy6 (2.3 MeV)
- For nuclei with mass number A >= 200, the center-of-mass energy computed by the two different methods (microscopic vs. 31 A^{-1/3} MeV) differs by at most ~0.5 MeV
- Reliable binding energies for super-heavy nuclei required at least N_F = 18 oscillator shells; for Pb-208, N_F >= 12 was sufficient
- The pairing gap used in the constant gap approximation was Delta = 11.2/sqrt(A) MeV

## Context

This work extends the observation from Klu09a that SHF models systematically underbind super-heavy nuclei while RMF models overbind them, confirming this trend on a much larger data basis of 513 even-even nuclei. The study compares four RMF parameterizations with different non-linear meson coupling schemes (standard NL3, extended TM1, FSUGold, and BSR4) against four SHF parameterizations with different fitting biases (SkI3, SLy6, BSk4, SV-min). The results show that the BSR4 parameter set, which includes isoscalar and isovector cross-couplings with the sigma fields, achieves binding energy errors for super-heavy nuclei comparable to the best SHF models, whereas other RMF models (NL3, TM1, FSUGold) yield much larger absolute errors. The authors suggest the remaining differences between SHF and RMF trends may originate from different modeling of the surface energy, particularly the isovector surface (surface symmetry) energy, and from the different effective masses produced by the two model classes (RMF gives m*/m ~ 0.6 while SHF has more freedom).
