# Spin-Electromagnetic Hydrodynamics and Magnetization Induced by Spin-Magnetic Interaction

> The hydrodynamic model including the spin degree of freedom and the electromagnetic field was discussed. In this derivation, we applied electromagnetism for macroscopic medium proposed by Minkowski. For the equation of motion of spin, we assumed that the hydrodynamic representation of the Pauli equation is reproduced when the many-body effect is neglected. Then the spin-magnetic interaction in the Pauli equation was converted to a part of the magnetization. The fluid and spin stress tensors induced by the many-body effect were obtained by employing the algebraic positivity of the entropy production in the framework of the linear irreversible thermodynamics, including the mixing effect of the irreversible currents. We further constructed the constitutive equation of the polarization and the magnetization. Our polarization equation is more reasonable compared to another result obtained using electromagnetism for macroscopic medium proposed by de Groot-Mazur.

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-relativistic spin-electromagnetic hydrodynamic model (sEMHD) by combining the hydrodynamic representation of the Pauli equation with Minkowski's formulation of electromagnetism for macroscopic media, using linear irreversible thermodynamics to incorporate many-body effects. The key hedge is that the model is non-relativistic and the authors acknowledge that a consistent relativistic model should reproduce this result in the non-relativistic limit, while also noting that the definition of electromagnetic momentum density in macroscopic media remains unsettled.

## Method

The authors first re-express the Pauli equation in hydrodynamic form using the particle probability density, probability velocity, and spin vector, following the approach of Bohm et al. (1950s). They then extend this single-particle result to many-body systems by introducing fluid and spin stress tensors, where deviations from the Pauli equation due to many-body effects are determined by requiring algebraic positivity of entropy production within linear irreversible thermodynamics. The Minkowski definition of electromagnetic momentum density is adopted, and the spin-magnetic interaction from the Pauli equation is converted into a magnetization term. The constitutive equations for polarization and magnetization are constructed, and the irreversible currents (viscous tensors, heat current, spin diffusion) are obtained using the Curie principle and Onsager's reciprocal theorem.

## Context

This work extends the spin-magnetohydrodynamics model of Marklund and Brodin (2007) by incorporating energy conservation, viscous tensors, and heat currents through linear irreversible thermodynamics, which were absent in that earlier barotropic fluid treatment. It also addresses the unresolved debate among the Minkowski, Hertz-Abraham, and de Groot-Mazur definitions of electromagnetic momentum density in macroscopic media, following the approach of Eu and Oppenheim (1986) but explicitly ensuring positivity of entropy production, which Felderhof and Kroh (1979) had achieved only for the de Groot-Mazur definition. The model enables dynamical studies of spin effects in systems such as relativistic heavy-ion collisions where coherent magnetic fields may form, though the authors note that a relativistic generalization is needed for that application.
