# Higgs picture of the QCD-vacuum

> The functional integral for QCD is reformulated by introducing explicitly an integration over the fluctuations of composite quark-antiquark bound states. Chiral symmetry breaking by the color singlet scalar field induces masses for the fermions. Our formulation with scalar fluctuations may be useful for lattice gauge theories by modifying the spectrum of the Dirac operator in the vacuum and permitting a simple connection to chiral perturbation theory. We propose that a ``condensate'' of quark-antiquark bound states in the color octet channel generates masses of the gluons by the Higgs mechanism. A simple effective action for quarks, gluons and (composite) scalars yields a surprisingly good description of the charges, masses and interactions of all low mass physical excitations - baryons, pseudoscalars and vector mesons. Dressed quarks appear as baryons and dressed gluons as vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the QCD vacuum can be described by a "Higgs picture" where a condensate of quark-antiquark bound states in the color octet channel generates gluon masses via the Higgs mechanism, complementing the usual confinement picture rather than contradicting it. The author explicitly notes that local gauge symmetries cannot be spontaneously broken in a strict sense, so the Higgs and confinement descriptions are considered different facets of the same physical state.

## Method

The functional integral for QCD is reformulated using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce explicit integration over composite scalar fields representing quark-antiquark bound states. A simple effective action is constructed containing quarks, gluons, and composite scalars (color singlet and color octet) with only renormalizable interactions up to dimension four, including a scalar potential, Yukawa couplings, and covariant derivatives. The minimum of the effective potential is assumed to occur for non-zero values of both the color singlet field (responsible for chiral symmetry breaking) and the color octet field (responsible for the Higgs mechanism for gluons).

## Key numbers

- The effective action is described by seven real parameters: \(g, h, \tilde h, \sigma_0, \chi_0, \nu\) and \(\nu'\), with three parameters (\(g, \sigma_0, \chi_0\)) remaining for description of pseudoscalars, vector mesons, and their interactions after fixing others
- The pion decay constant is identified as \(f_\pi = 2\bar{Z}^{1/2}\bar{\sigma}\), where \(\bar{\sigma}\) is related to the quark-antiquark condensate by \(\bar{\sigma} = -\frac{1}{2}\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle\)
- The parameter \(B\) relating the condensate to the pion decay constant is given by \(B = \frac{2\bar{\sigma}}{f_\pi^2} = -\frac{\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle}{f_\pi^2}\)
- The matching to perturbative QCD is expected for momenta above approximately \((2\ \text{GeV})^2\)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal of "spontaneous breaking of color" (Wetterich, prior work) and draws on the complementarity between Higgs and confinement descriptions established for toy models with fundamental scalar fields (Banks, 1980s; earlier toy models). The approach contrasts with pure gluodynamics (QCD without quarks) where low excitations are glueballs, and offers a framework that may connect to lattice QCD simulations by modifying the Dirac operator spectrum. The paper also notes analogies to color-flavor locking at high baryon density (proposed for neutron star interiors) while emphasizing that different physical situations (vacuum vs. high density) and distinct symmetry breaking patterns are involved.
