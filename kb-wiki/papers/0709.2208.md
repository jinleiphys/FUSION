# Glueball Decay in Holographic QCD

> Using holographic QCD based on D4-branes and D8-anti-D8-branes, we have computed couplings of glueballs to light mesons. We describe glueball decay by explicitly calculating its decay widths and branching ratios. Interestingly, while glueballs remain less well understood both theoretically and experimentally, our results are found to be consistent with the experimental data for the scalar glueball candidate f_0(1500). More generally, holographic QCD predicts that decay of any glueball to 4 $π_0$ is surpressed, and that mixing of the lightest glueball with $q\bar{q}$ mesons is small.

## Key claim

Using holographic QCD based on D4-branes and D8-anti-D8-branes, the authors compute glueball decay widths and branching ratios and find consistency with experimental data for the scalar glueball candidate f0(1500), but only at leading order in the large 't Hooft coupling expansion and in the large Nc limit, where the holographic duality is valid.

## Method

The authors combine two sectors of holographic QCD: glueball states described by supergravity fluctuations in Witten's background (following Brower (2000)), and q-qbar mesons described by the Sakai-Sugimoto model using probe D8-branes. They substitute the fluctuation wave functions for the lightest scalar glueball (JPC=0++) and for pions and rho mesons into the D8-brane action, integrate over extra dimensions, and extract the interaction Lagrangian. Decay widths are then computed from these couplings.

## Key numbers

- Mass squared of the lightest scalar glueball: M^2 = 7.308 R^2/L^4, from solving the eigen-equation of the 7-dimensional AdS supergravity
- Normalization factor for the glueball fluctuation eigenfunction: (H(Z=0))^{-1} = 0.00978 g_YM N_c^{3/2} M_KK
- Coupling constant c1 = 44.3 / (g_YM N_c^{3/2} M_KK) for the G-pion-pion interaction
- Coupling constant c3 = 49.3 / (g_YM N_c^{3/2} M_KK) for the G-rho-rho interaction
- Coupling constant c5 = 1.43 x 10^3 / (g_YM^2 N_c^{5/2} M_KK) for the G-pion-pion-rho interaction
- Eigenvalue for the rho meson wavefunction: lambda_1 = 0.669, giving m_rho^2 = lambda_1 M_KK^2

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD program of Brower (2000) for glueball spectra and Sakai and Sugimoto (2004, 2005) for meson dynamics by computing the previously unknown couplings between these two sectors. It provides a first-principles calculation of glueball decay widths that lattice QCD (Morningstar (1999), Lee (1999)) cannot yet supply for dynamical quantities. The results enable experimental identification of glueball candidates such as f0(1500) at the LHC, and predict that glueball decay to 4 pi0 is suppressed and that mixing of the lightest glueball with q-qbar mesons is of order 1/sqrt(N_c) and thus small.
