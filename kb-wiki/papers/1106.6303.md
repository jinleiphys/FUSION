# Jet Quenching in Non-Conformal Holography

> We use our non-conformal holographic bottom-up model for QCD described in 1012.0116 to further study the effect of the QCD trace anomaly on the energy loss of both light and heavy quarks in a strongly coupled plasma. We compute the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for bottom and charm quarks in an expanding plasma with Glauber initial conditions. We find that the maximum stopping distance of light quarks in a non-conformal plasma scales with the energy with a temperature (and energy) dependent effective power.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a non-conformal holographic plasma, the maximum stopping distance of light quarks scales with energy as a temperature- and energy-dependent effective power, rather than the fixed $E^{1/3}$ scaling found in conformal plasmas; this effective power asymptotes to the conformal value of $1/3$ only at large energies or far from the critical temperature $T_c$.

## Method

The authors used a non-conformal holographic bottom-up model of QCD, based on a 5-dimensional gravity theory coupled to a scalar field, to compute heavy quark energy loss via the trailing string ansatz and light quark stopping distances via open strings falling toward the black brane horizon. For heavy quarks, they solved the differential equation $dp(t)/dt = [dE/dx](p(t), T(\vec{x}_\perp,\phi,t))$ with Glauber initial conditions and an expanding plasma temperature profile, then averaged over transverse production points and azimuthal directions to obtain the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$. For light quarks, they extracted the maximum stopping distance $\Delta x_{max}$ as a function of energy from the string dynamics and determined the effective stopping power $n_{eff}(T,E)$.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark $R_{AA}$ at RHIC asymptotes to a value of about 0.2 at $p_T \sim 10$ GeV.
- Charm quark $R_{AA}$ is predicted to be heavily suppressed, below 0.1.
- Initial temperatures at the center of the plasma were 265 MeV for RHIC and 357 MeV for LHC.
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-sections used were 4.2 fm$^2$ for RHIC and 6.3 fm$^2$ for LHC.
- The freeze-out temperature $T_{fo}$ was chosen as 150 MeV.
- The initial jet production time $t_i$ was chosen as 1 fm/c.
- For charm quarks, the trailing string ansatz becomes inapplicable when $T \sim 290$ MeV, where $r_H \approx r_m$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-conformal holographic model (Ficnar, Noronha, Gyulassy, 2010) by computing $R_{AA}$ for heavy quarks and analyzing light quark stopping distances. It contrasts with conformal holographic results (e.g., conformal $R_{AA}$ calculations and the $E^{1/3}$ scaling found by Gubser (2008) and Chesler (2009)) by showing that the QCD trace anomaly modifies the energy loss and stopping distance scaling near $T_c$. The results enable future calculations of instantaneous light quark energy loss and more realistic $R_{AA}$ predictions, though the authors note that a consistent treatment of light quark energy loss remains an open problem currently being explored.
