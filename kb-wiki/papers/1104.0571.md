# Exclusive production of large invariant mass pion pairs in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> The cross section for exclusive production of $π^+ π^-$ and $π^0 π^0$ meson pairs in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is calculated for LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 3.5 TeV taking into account photon-photon mechanism. We concentrate on the production of large two-pion invariant masses where the mechanism of the elementary $γγ\to ππ$ process is not fully understood. In order to include a size of nuclei we perform calculation in the impact-parameter equivalent photon approximation (EPA). Realistic charge densities are used to calculate charged form factor of $^{208}$Pb nucleus and to generate photon fluxes associated with ultrarelativistic heavy ions. Sizeable cross sections are obtained that can be measured at LHC. The cross section for elementary $γγ\to ππ$ is calculated in the framework of pQCD Brodsky-Lepage (BL) mechanism with the distribution amplitude used to descibe recent data of the BABAR collaboration on pion transition form factor, using hand-bag mechanism advocated to describe recent Belle data as well as $t$ and $u$-channel meson/reggeon exchanges. We present distributions in two-pion invariant mass as well as the pion pair rapidity for the nuclear process.

# Exclusive production of large invariant mass pion pairs in ultraperipheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The cross section for exclusive π⁺π⁻ and π⁰π⁰ pair production in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy √s_NN = 3.5 TeV is sizeable enough to be measured, but the mechanism of the elementary γγ → ππ process at large two-pion invariant masses (above about 3 GeV) remains ambiguous, with the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD approach, the hand-bag model, and meson exchanges each describing different aspects of existing data.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear cross sections using the impact-parameter equivalent photon approximation (b-space EPA) with realistic charge densities for ²⁰⁸Pb to generate photon fluxes, imposing an absorption cutoff at impact parameter b > R₁ + R₂ ≈ 14 fm. The elementary γγ → ππ cross section is computed via three mechanisms: (1) leading-order pQCD Brodsky-Lepage formalism with the Wu-Huang pion distribution amplitude (B = 0.6, m_q = 0.3 GeV) including ERBL evolution; (2) the hand-bag model of Diehl, Kroll, and Vogt with parameters a_u = 1.375 GeV², n_u = 0.4175, a_s = 0.5025 GeV², n_s = 1.195; and (3) reggeized ω-meson exchange in t and u channels with trajectory α(t) = 0.64 + 0.8 t and cutoff Λ_ω = 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear cross section enhancement over elementary γγ → ππ is of order 10⁴, which is less than the naive Z₁²Z₂² expectation for ²⁰⁸Pb (Z = 82).
- For the hand-bag model, the ratio of π⁰π⁰ to π⁺π⁻ cross section is exactly 1/2, independent of scattering angle.
- In the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD approach with |cos θ| ≤ 0.8, the π⁺π⁻ nuclear cross section is about one order of magnitude larger than the π⁰π⁰ cross section.
- The z-averaged ratio σ(π⁰π⁰)/σ(π⁺π⁻) for |cos θ| < 0.6 is about 0.2 in the pQCD calculation.
- The hand-bag approach describes Belle data for γγ → π⁰π⁰ at √s > 3 GeV, while ω-exchange contributes only at large |cos θ|.
- The pQCD cross section with the Wu-Huang distribution amplitude lies below experimental data for γγ → ππ at energies up to about 4 GeV.
- The cut-off parameter Λ_reg in the pQCD regularization form factor is expected to be of order 1 GeV.
- The ω trajectory is parametrized as α(t) = 0.64 + 0.8 t with s₀ = 1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of exclusive ρ⁰ρ⁰ pairs (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010), muonic pairs (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010), and heavy-quark pairs (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2010) in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions. The elementary γγ → ππ analysis builds on the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD formalism (Brodsky and Lepage, 1981), the hand-bag model of Diehl, Kroll, and Vogt (2000), and meson exchange treatments (Achasov and Shestakov, 1997; Minkowski, Nakhmanson, and Wess, 1976). The Wu-Huang distribution amplitude (Wu and Huang, 2010) is used to describe BABAR data on the pion transition form factor. The results enable future measurements at ALICE (LHC) and STAR (RHIC) to discriminate between pQCD and hand-bag mechanisms at dipion invariant masses above 6 GeV and transverse momenta p_t > 3 GeV, a region not accessible in e⁺e⁻ collisions.
