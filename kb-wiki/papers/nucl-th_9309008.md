# Pairproduction in a semiclassical transport theory

> In high-energy nucleon-nucleon-collisions large meson production rates are observed. As an alternative to meson exchange models here we propose a nonperturbative treatment of the quark-antiquark production from the mean field of colliding nucleon bags. Source terms for particles and antiparticles are developed in a semiclassical quark transport description and the time evolution of the quark density is studied for different mean fields numerically.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a nonperturbative treatment of quark-antiquark production from the mean field of colliding nucleon bags, as an alternative to meson exchange models, but the semiclassical transport description is only valid for slow potential changes and must be supplemented by explicit source terms to handle pair production.

## Method

The authors first analyze Klein's paradox for a potential step and for Woods-Saxon potentials to derive reflection and transmission coefficients for pair production. They then develop a semiclassical transport model by adding source terms to the relativistic Vlasov equation, using a weighted test particle method to simulate the time evolution of phase space densities for incoming, reflected, and transmitted particles.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass used in numerical studies: \( m = 0.01 \, \text{GeV} \)
- Potential height used for Woods-Saxon studies: \( V = 0.3 \, \text{GeV} \)
- Rising length of Woods-Saxon potentials varied between \( 0.025 \, \lambda_c \) and \( 0.25 \, \lambda_c \), where \( \lambda_c \) is the Compton wavelength of a quark
- Gaussian wave packet width used: \( \Delta = 1 \, \text{fm} \)
- Energies of Gaussian wave packets studied: \( E = 0.1 \, \text{GeV} \) and \( E = 0.26 \, \text{GeV} \)
- Woods-Saxon potential rising lengths for pair production rate study: \( \lambda = 0.5 \, \text{fm} \), \( 1 \, \text{fm} \), \( 2 \, \text{fm} \), and \( 5 \, \text{fm} \)

## Context

This work extends the BUU/VUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck or Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport approach, previously applied to heavy ion collisions, to the quark level for nucleon-nucleon collisions in the few GeV range. It builds on earlier quark transport theory work by Kalm (year not given) and provides a practical method to incorporate pair production into semiclassical simulations, enabling more realistic modeling of dynamical quark systems in nuclear collisions.
