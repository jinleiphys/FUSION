# Relativistic Heavy Ion Excitation of Giant Resonances

> Giant resonances and giant resonances built on other giant resonances in nuclei are observed with very large cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions. A theoretical effort is underway to understand the reaction mechanism which leads to this process, as well as a better understanding of the microscopic properties of multiphonon states, e.g., their strength, energy centroids, widths and anharmonicities.

# Relativistic Heavy Ion Excitation of Giant Resonances

## Key claim
Giant resonances and multiphonon giant resonances (e.g., the double giant dipole resonance, DGDR) are excited with very large cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the harmonic oscillator model systematically underpredicts the measured cross sections by up to 30%, and the ratio of experimental to theoretical cross sections depends strongly on the experimental probe used (pion versus Coulomb excitation).

## Method
The author reviews the semiclassical approach to relativistic Coulomb excitation, where the relative motion of the nuclei is treated classically while the internal nuclear degrees of freedom are treated quantum mechanically. The excitation probabilities for a given multipolarity are obtained from the equivalent photon method, relating them to photonuclear cross sections via equation (1): \(P_{\pi\lambda}(E,b) = (1/E) N_{\pi\lambda}(E,b) \sigma_{\gamma}^{\pi\lambda}(E)\). For multiphonon states, the harmonic oscillator model based on the Axel-Brink hypothesis is used, with the inclusion of unitarity corrections via an exponential factor (equation 2) and a convolution over intermediate energies (equation 3). Coupled-channels calculations are also discussed to account for the strong coupling between the ground state and the GDR.

## Key numbers
- The excitation cross section for the DGDR (GDR²) in relativistic heavy ion collisions is of the order of hundreds of millibarns (mb).
- For the reaction \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{208}\)Pb at 640 MeV/nucleon, the calculated cross section for the DGDR is \(\sigma_{DGDR} = 620\) mb for a centroid energy of \(E_{DGDR} = 20\) MeV, 299 mb for \(E_{DGDR} = 24\) MeV, and 199 mb for \(E_{DGDR} = 27\) MeV.
- The ratio of the DGDR width to the GDR width satisfies \(\sqrt{2} \leq \Gamma_{DGDR}/\Gamma_{GDR} \leq 2\).
- The contribution of two-phonon \(1^{-}\) states to the total cross section in the DGDR region for \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{208}\)Pb (640 MeV/nucleon) is of the order of 15%.
- The anharmonic shift of the DGDR energy centroid is estimated as \(\Delta^{(2)}E < E_{GDR}/(50A) \sim A^{-4/3}\) MeV.
- The asymptotic excitation probability of the GDR in a grazing collision of \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{208}\)Pb at 640 MeV/nucleon is approximately 40%.

## Context
This paper reviews the theoretical understanding of giant resonance excitation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, extending earlier work on the semiclassical Coulomb excitation formalism by Bertulani and collaborators (1990s) and the harmonic oscillator model for multiphonon states. It addresses discrepancies between theory and experiment first observed in pion scattering at the Los Alamos Pion Facility (1980s) and later in Coulomb excitation experiments at GSI/Darmstadt (1990s). The work enables future studies of exotic particle decay from multiphonon states and beam monitoring applications at facilities such as CERN and Brookhaven, while noting that open questions remain regarding the width, strength, and reaction mechanism of the DGDR.
