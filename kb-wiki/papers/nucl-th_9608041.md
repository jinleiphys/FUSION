# Competition Between T=0 and T=1 Pairing in Proton-Rich Nuclei

> A cranked mean-field model with two-body T=1 and T=0 pairing interactions is presented. Approximate projection onto good particle-number is enforced via an extended Lipkin-Nogami scheme. Our calculations suggest the simultaneous presence of both T=0 and T=1 pairing modes in N=Z nuclei. The transitions between different pairing phases are discussed as a function of neutron/proton excess, T$_z$, and rotational frequency, $\hbarω$. The additional binding energy due to the T=0 $np$-pairing correlations, is suggested as a possible microscopic explanation of the Wigner energy term in even-even nuclei.

# Competition Between T=0 and T=1 Pairing in Proton-Rich Nuclei

## Key claim

The previously suggested exclusiveness of T=0 and T=1 pairing phases is a generic feature of the BCS approximation for N=Z nuclei, but this phase transition becomes smeared out in number-projected Lipkin-Nogami calculations, where T=0 np-pairing correlations coexist with T=1 nn- and pp-pairing correlations over a broad range of interaction strength and rotational frequency. The additional binding energy from T=0 np-pairing correlations is suggested as a possible microscopic explanation of the Wigner energy term in even-even nuclei.

## Method

A cranked mean-field model was constructed using a deformed phenomenological single-particle potential with two-body T=1 and T=0 pairing interactions, employing a generalized Bogolyubov transformation with antilinear simplex symmetry. Approximate particle-number projection was enforced via an extended Lipkin-Nogami scheme, which modifies the HFB equations by renormalizing the single-particle field and pairing field with additional Lagrange-type parameters. The calculations were performed as a function of neutron/proton excess (T_z) and rotational frequency (ħω), with the T=0 pairing strength scaled relative to the average T=1 pairing strength via the parameter x^{T=0} = G_{np}^{T=0}/G_{np}^{T=1}.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of the strength necessary to activate T=0 np-pairing in number-projected calculations is x^{T=0}_{crit} ≈ 1.1 for N=Z nuclei.
- The extended Thomas-Fermi model shows a systematic binding energy offset of ~2 MeV at the N=Z line that may be associated with the lack of np-pairing correlations.
- Based on the ETFSI results, the estimated strength of the T=0 pairing force is G^{T=0} ≈ 1.2 G^{T=1}; the shell-model estimate of Rosenfeld (1948) yields G^{T=0} ≈ 1.3 G^{T=1}.
- Calculations for ⁴⁶Cr and ⁴⁸Cr show a sudden onset of T=0 pairing at a critical rotational frequency ħω_{crit}, with the dynamical moment of inertia in the T=0 phase exceeding the value obtained for a system without pairing.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field treatments of np-pairing by Goswami (1964, 1965), Goodman (1972), and the cranked formalism of Nicolini (1978) and Müllner (1982), which suggested exclusive T=0 and T=1 pairing phases. The authors show that approximate particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method removes this exclusivity for N=Z nuclei, enabling coexistence of both pairing modes. The results provide a microscopic foundation for the phenomenological Wigner energy term used in macroscopic-microscopic mass calculations by Myers (1966) and Möller (1995), and suggest that T=0 np-pairing can be generated at high rotational frequencies even when absent in the ground state, dramatically altering the mechanism of angular momentum building.
