# Revealing baryon number fluctuations from proton number fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Baryon number cumulants are invaluable tools to diagnose the primordial stage of heavy ion collisions if they can be measured. In experiments, however, proton number cumulants have been measured as substitutes. In fact, proton number fluctuations are further modified in the hadron phase and different from those of baryon number. We show that the isospin distribution of nucleons at kinetic freezeout is binomial and factorized. This leads to formulas that express the baryon number cumulants sorely in terms of proron number fluctuations, which are experimentally observable.

# Revealing baryon number fluctuations from proton number fluctuations in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors show that baryon number cumulants can be expressed solely in terms of experimentally observable proton number cumulants, provided that the isospin distribution of nucleons at kinetic freeze-out is binomial and factorized, a condition they argue holds for beam energies $\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 10$ GeV, but not at lower energies where pions are insufficient to drive charge-exchange reactions.

## Method

The authors derive analytic relations between cumulants of net and total proton numbers and cumulants of net baryon number, using the assumption that after multiple charge-exchange reactions via $\Delta$ resonances in the hadronic stage, the isospin of each nucleon becomes randomized and uncorrelated, leading to a binomial distribution of protons and neutrons. They evaluate the mean time for a proton to undergo charge exchange with thermal pions using a Breit-Wigner cross section for $\Delta$ production, finding $3-4$ fm at $T=150-170$ MeV, which is shorter than the typical hadronic interaction time of several tens of fm at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- Mean time for a proton to undergo charge exchange via $\Delta$ resonance: $3-4$ fm at $T=150-170$ MeV (using $m_N=940$ MeV, $m_\pi=140$ MeV, $E_\Delta=1232$ MeV, $\Gamma=110$ MeV, $\sigma_\Delta=20$ fm$^2$)
- $\Delta$ resonance lifetime: $1/\Gamma \simeq 1.8$ fm
- Ratio of probabilities for $\Delta^+$ and $\Delta^0$ production from a proton: $5:4$, determined by isospin SU(2) algebra
- Applicable beam energy range: $\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 10$ GeV, based on the condition $T_{\rm chem} > m_\pi$ and sufficient pion production
- Experimental deviation from HRG model predictions for skewness and kurtosis observed at $\sqrt{s} \lesssim 50$ GeV (STAR collaboration)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for using conserved charge fluctuations as probes of the QCD phase diagram, following earlier proposals that cumulants of conserved charges can signal the critical point (Stephanov 1998, Hatta 2003, Stephanov 2008) and that higher-order cumulants change signs around the QCD phase boundary (Asakawa 2009, Friman 2011, Stephanov 2011). The authors address the problem that while baryon number cumulants are the ideal diagnostic, experiments (STAR collaboration 2011) can only measure proton number fluctuations, which are modified by hadronic-stage charge-exchange reactions. The derived formulas enable direct extraction of baryon number cumulants from proton data without model assumptions, and the authors note that the binomial isospin randomization explains why STAR results appear close to hadron resonance gas model predictions (Karsch 2010), the hadronic-stage effects suppress the primordial baryon number fluctuation signal in the proton channel.
