# Low-energy Antiproton Interaction with Helium

> An ab initio potential for the interaction of the neutral helium atom with antiprotons and protons is calculated using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. Using this potential, the annihilation cross section for antiprotons in the energy range 0.01 microvolt to 1 eV is calculated.

# Low-energy Antiproton Interaction with Helium

## Key claim
The annihilation cross section for antiprotons on neutral helium atoms shows a transition in energy dependence around 10 μeV, varying as 1/v² above that energy and as 1/v below it; however, the calculation uses the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and does not consider corrections beyond it, which could influence the conclusion that no barrier or inflection in the potential exists.

## Method
An ab initio electrostatic potential for the antiproton-helium system was calculated using a variational wave function expanded in spherical harmonics and Laguerre polynomials about the helium nucleus, within the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The annihilation cross section was then computed by solving the Schrödinger equation with this electrostatic potential plus a nuclear optical-model potential (Woods-Saxon form with radius 2 fm and diffuseness 0.5 fm for 4 nucleons) over the full radial range from 0 to 80 Å. The same variational approach was applied to the proton-helium system for validation against prior calculations by Kolos and Peek.

## Key numbers
- The antiproton-helium electrostatic potential was fitted with a polarizability coefficient β = 1595 meV·Å⁴, compared to 1557 meV·Å⁴ from Davison and 1678 meV·Å⁴ from Singh.
- At R = 3.704 Å, the proton-helium potential from the (4,4) calculation gave −8.10 meV, compared to −8.32 meV from Kolos and Peek.
- The s-wave annihilation cross section above ~10⁻⁵ eV is given by σ_abs = 0.536π/k².
- The transition between 1/v² and 1/v behavior occurs around 10 μeV (approximately 20 μV for f=0.1).
- Using the calculated cross section at 4.2 K, the predicted annihilation rate is ~1.2×10⁻³ 1/sec, compared to an observed maximum rate of ~8×10⁻³ 1/sec in the trap experiment.

## Context
This work was motivated by the trapping experiment of Holzscheiter (1990s), which observed a sharp drop in antiproton annihilation rate on helium during cooling, suggesting a possible potential barrier of a few millivolts. The calculation extends prior variational work by Kolos and Peek on the proton-helium potential and provides the first ab initio antiproton-helium potential and annihilation cross section in the 0.01 μeV to 1 eV range. The results enable interpretation of antiproton trap measurements as a thermometer for low-energy antiprotons and provide input for models of cosmic antiproton interactions with interstellar helium.
