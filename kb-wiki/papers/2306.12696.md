# Efficient solver of relativistic hydrodynamics with implicit Runge-Kutta method

> We propose a new method to solve the relativistic hydrodynamic equations based on implicit Runge-Kutta methods with a locally optimized fixed-point iterative solver. For numerical demonstration, we implement our idea for ideal hydrodynamics using the one-stage Gauss-Legendre method as an implicit method. The accuracy and computational cost of our new method are compared with those of explicit ones for the (1+1)-dimensional Riemann problem, as well as the (2+1)-dimensional Gubser flow and event-by-event initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions generated by TrENTo. We demonstrate that the solver converges with only one iteration in most cases, and as a result, the implicit method requires a smaller computational cost than the explicit one at the same accuracy in these cases, while it may not converge with an unrealistically large $Δt$. By showing a relationship between the one-stage Gauss-Legendre method with the iterative solver and the two-step Adams-Bashforth method, we argue that our method benefits from both the stability of the former and the efficiency of the latter.

## Key claim

The authors propose that an implicit Runge-Kutta method (specifically the one-stage Gauss-Legendre method) combined with a locally optimized fixed-point iterative solver can be more computationally efficient than explicit methods for relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collisions, contrary to typical expectations, provided the time step is not unrealistically large.

## Method

The authors implement ideal relativistic hydrodynamics using the Kurganov-Tadmor scheme for spatial discretization and compare the one-stage Gauss-Legendre (GL1) implicit method with Heun's explicit method, both of second-order accuracy. For the implicit solver, they use a fixed-point iteration method with optimizations including using the previous time step's solution as the initial guess and checking convergence cell-by-cell to skip updating converged cells. They test their method on three benchmark problems: the (1+1)-dimensional Riemann problem, the (2+1)-dimensional Gubser flow, and event-by-event initial conditions from the TRENTo model for heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- For the Riemann problem with a conformal equation of state (P = epsilon/3), the analytical solution uses epsilon_max = 10 and epsilon_min = 1 (in arbitrary units), with the speed of sound c_s = sqrt(1/3).
- The vacuum floor value for energy density is set to epsilon_vac = 10^(-100) fm^(-4) to avoid undefined velocities.
- The small regulator s used to enforce the physical condition T^(tt) >= (1+s)K is chosen as 10^(-15).
- The flux limiter parameter theta in the MUSCL reconstruction is set to 1.1, following Schenke (2010).

## Context

This work extends the application of implicit time integration methods to relativistic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions, an area where existing codes (such as those using SHASTA, KT, or HLLE schemes) exclusively use explicit methods. The authors build on the Kurganov-Tadmor spatial discretization used by Schenke (2010) and Pang (2018), and relate their method to the two-step Adams-Bashforth method. This approach could enable more efficient simulations for complex systems such as fluctuating hydrodynamics, non-equilibrium chiral fluid dynamics, and magnetohydrodynamics, which are anticipated to be stiff systems requiring impractically small time steps with explicit methods.
