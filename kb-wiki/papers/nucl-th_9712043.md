# N-Nucleon Effective Generators of the Poincare Group Derived from a Field Theory

> It is shown that the ten generators of the Poincare group acting in the Fock space of nucleons and mesons and based on standard Lagrangians can be blockdiagonalized by one and the same unitary transformation such that the space of a fixed number of nucleons is separated from the rest of the Fock space. The existence proof is carried through in a formal power series expansion in the coupling constant to all orders. In this manner one arrives at effective generators of the Poincare group which act in the two subspaces separately.

# N-Nucleon Effective Generators of the Poincaré Group Derived from a Field Theory

## Key claim
The ten generators of the Poincaré group acting in the full Fock space of nucleons and mesons can be blockdiagonalized by a single unitary transformation, thereby separating the space of a fixed number of nucleons from the rest of the Fock space, but this existence proof is carried through only as a formal power series expansion in the coupling constant to all orders, not as a convergent or non-perturbative construction.

## Method
The authors start from a field theory Lagrangian with a free part and an interaction term linear in the coupling constant and linear in meson creation and absorption operators. They adopt the instant form of the Poincaré generators, where the Hamiltonian and boost operators carry interactions while momentum and angular momentum are free. Following the Okubo method, they construct a unitary operator parameterized by an operator A that connects the N-nucleon subspace (η) and its complement (Λ). By expanding A as a power series in the coupling constant and solving the recursive conditions for blockdiagonalization of all ten generators simultaneously, they prove that the same A satisfies the conditions for the Hamiltonian, boost, momentum, and angular momentum operators order by order in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- The interaction Hamiltonian H_I and boost operators K_I are proportional to the coupling constant g to first order (H_I ∼ g, K_I ∼ g).
- The operator A is expanded as A = Σ_{ν=1}^{∞} A_ν g^ν, with no zeroth-order term since the free theory is already blockdiagonal.
- The recursion relations for A_n are solved explicitly for n=1,2 and for general n≥2 using energy denominators of the form 1/(E_Λ − E_η).
- The proof that A_H solves the boost condition is carried through for n≥4 by induction, with separate verification required for n=3 and n=4.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gloeckle and Müller (1990s), who proposed the blockdiagonalization method and worked it out to lowest order in the coupling constant for a field theory of scalar nucleons interacting with a scalar meson field. The present paper generalizes that derivation to arbitrary order in the coupling constant, providing an existence proof for effective Poincaré generators in the N-nucleon subspace. This enables future studies of whether these effective generators automatically satisfy cluster separability, a known defect in the Bakamjian-Thomas scheme (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) that was later addressed by Sokolov (1970s) and by Coester and Polyzou (1980s). Numerical studies by Müller et al. (1990s) and by Gloeckle and collaborators have shown that the effective Hamiltonian obtained in lowest order reproduces the relativistic energy-momentum relation for two-body states and determines contributions that remain undetermined in the 1/c² expansion approach of Foldy and Krajcik (1970s).
