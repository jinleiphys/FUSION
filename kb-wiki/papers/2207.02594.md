# 3-dimensional flow analysis: A novel tool to study the collision geometry and the Equation-of-State

> We propose to extend the commonly known flow analysis in the transverse $p_x$-$p_y$ plane to novel flow coefficients based on the angular distribution in the $p_x$-$p_z$ and $p_y$-$p_z$ planes. The new flow coefficients, called $u_n$ and $w_n$ (in addition to $v_n$), turn out to be also highly sensitive to the nuclear Equation-of-State and can be used to explore the EoS in more detail than is possible using only $v_n$. As an example to quantify the effect of the EoS, the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model is used to investigate 20-30\% central Au+Au collisions at E$_\mathrm{lab}=1.23~A$GeV.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that extending flow analysis from the transverse plane to the \(p_x\)-\(p_z\) and \(p_y\)-\(p_z\) planes yields new Fourier coefficients \(u_n\) and \(w_n\) which are highly sensitive to the nuclear Equation-of-State, potentially allowing more precise constraints than using only the standard \(v_n\) coefficients. This claim is demonstrated for semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at a single beam energy using the UrQMD model, but the authors note that experimental extraction would require event-plane reconstruction analogous to the standard method.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD 3.5) model to simulate 20-30% central Au+Au collisions at a kinetic beam energy of 1.23 \(A\)GeV. They extracted the standard transverse flow coefficients \(v_n\) from the angular distribution in the \(p_x\)-\(p_y\) plane and introduced new coefficients \(u_n\) and \(w_n\) from the \(p_x\)-\(p_z\) and \(p_y\)-\(p_z\) planes, respectively, by Fourier decomposition of the angular distributions. The simulations were performed with a hard Skyrme-type EoS, a soft Skyrme-type EoS, and in cascade mode (no potential interactions), and the coefficients were studied as functions of the out-of-plane momentum.

## Key numbers
- The analysis is performed for 20-30% central Au+Au collisions at E\(_\mathrm{lab}=1.23~A\)GeV, corresponding to an impact parameter range of \(6.6\leq b\leq8.1\) fm.
- The new \(u_n\) and \(w_n\) coefficients show significant signals up to the 20th order, in contrast to standard \(v_n\) coefficients which are strongly damped for \(n>6\).
- For the hard EoS, the tilt angle of the peak in the \(p_x\)-\(p_z\) plane rotates continuously from its value at \(p_y=0.2\) GeV towards smaller angles for larger and smaller \(p_y\), with the hard EoS showing a stronger rotation than the soft EoS or cascade mode.
- The symmetric \(u_n\) coefficients from the hard EoS show an alternating sign pattern: coefficients of order \(4n+2\) have an overall negative sign, while those of order \(4n\) have an overall positive sign.

## Context
This work extends the standard Fourier decomposition of transverse flow (the \(v_n\) coefficients) used in heavy-ion collisions to extract the nuclear Equation-of-State, as previously applied by Danielewicz (2002), Hillmann (2018, 2019), and Mohs (2020). The new coefficients \(u_n\) and \(w_n\) are proposed as a tool to study the 3-dimensional expansion geometry, particularly at low beam energies where the system expands nearly isotropically. The authors suggest that these coefficients could also be used to study anti-flow, as discussed by Brachmann (1999), Bozek (2010), and Nara (2021), and note that the rotation of the emission source resembles the 'cat eye' structure predicted for HBT correlations by Graef (2013).
