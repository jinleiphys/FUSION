# Magnetic Catalysis: A Review

> We give an overview of the magnetic catalysis phenomenon. In the framework of quantum field theory, magnetic catalysis is broadly defined as an enhancement of dynamical symmetry breaking by an external magnetic field. We start from a brief discussion of spontaneous symmetry breaking and the role of a magnetic field in its a dynamics. This is followed by a detailed presentation of the essential features of the phenomenon. In particular, we emphasize that the dimensional reduction plays a profound role in the pairing dynamics in a magnetic field. Using the general nature of underlying physics and its robustness with respect to interaction types and model content, we argue that magnetic catalysis is a universal and model-independent phenomenon. In support of this claim, we show how magnetic catalysis is realized in various models with short-range and long-range interactions. We argue that the general nature of the phenomenon implies a wide range of potential applications: from certain types of solid state systems to models in cosmology, particle and nuclear physics. We finish the review with general remarks about magnetic catalysis and an outlook for future research.

## Key claim

Magnetic catalysis is a universal and model-independent phenomenon in quantum field theory where an external magnetic field enhances dynamical symmetry breaking, with the essential mechanism being dimensional reduction (D → D-2) of charged Dirac fermions in the low-energy sector, which leads to dynamical mass generation even at the weakest attractive interaction between fermions.

## Method

The review examines magnetic catalysis through analysis of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models in both (2+1) and (3+1) dimensions, using Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equations in the mean-field approximation and the large N approximation. The author also employs the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation approach to study bound states with quantum numbers of Nambu-Goldstone bosons, and uses proper-time regularization to compute effective potentials and condensates.

## Key numbers

- In (2+1)-dimensional NJL model at weak coupling, the dynamical mass is approximately |m| ≈ G|eB|/(2π)
- In (2+1)-dimensional NJL model with large N approximation at weak coupling, the dynamical mass is m ≈ GN|eB|/(2π)
- In (3+1)-dimensional NJL model, the nonperturbative dynamical mass is m ≈ √(|eB|/π) exp(Λ²/(2|eB|)) exp(-2π²/(G|eB|))
- The area density of degenerate states in the lowest Landau level is |eB|/(2π) in both (2+1) and (3+1) dimensions
- For higher Landau levels (n>0) in (3+1) dimensions, the area density is |eB|/π
- In (3+1)-dimensional NJL model at zero magnetic field, the critical coupling for symmetry breaking is g=1 (where g ≡ GΛ²/(2π)²)
- In (2+1)-dimensional NJL model at zero magnetic field, the critical coupling is g=√π (where g ≡ NΛG/π)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on magnetic catalysis by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1994), who first explained the essential role of dimensional reduction and established that magnetic fields lead to dynamical mass generation even at the weakest attractive interaction. The review covers subsequent tests across numerous models including four-fermion interactions, QED-like gauge theories, chiral perturbation theory, QCD, lattice calculations, and holographic dual models, as well as applications to solid state systems including graphene and high-temperature superconductivity. The work enables further investigation of magnetic catalysis in cosmology, heavy ion collisions, neutron stars, and condensed matter systems.
