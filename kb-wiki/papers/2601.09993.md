# Average pairing correlation properties and effective pairing residual interactions

> This paper describes a method to determine the intensities of effective pairing residual interactions, extending what has been done for the seniority force model [Phys. Rev. C 110, 024311 (2024)]. It has been tested in Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations using residual pairing zero-range interactions. The average pair condensation energy is the key quantity connecting the determination of constant pairing matrix elements to the estimation of delta interaction intensities. From individually fitted delta pairing strengths of $28$ well and rigidly deformed nuclei whose proton number $Z$ ranges from $50$ to $82$ evaluated at the ground-state, we have determined average interaction intensities. They reproduce equally well the data on MoI as what is obtained within the seniority force ansatz with a r.m.s. deviation of about $2 \: \hbar^2 \mbox{MeV}^{-1}$. This approach provides a non-ambiguous way to determine reasonably well the strengths of pairing interactions at the ground-state of well deformed nuclei. It allows to perform, with some reasonable level of confidence, calculations for other nuclei in the corresponding nuclear region as well as beyond their ground states in particular to assess deformation properties as, e.g., to evaluate fission barriers or spectral properties of quasi-particle states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to determine intensities of effective pairing residual interactions (specifically volume delta interactions) by transferring information from the seniority force model, using the average pair condensation energy as the key connecting quantity. The method is validated for ground states of well and rigidly deformed nuclei, with the caveat that averaged interaction intensities over a nuclear region actually reproduce moments of inertia better than individually fitted ones.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF-BCS) calculations using the Skyrme SIII parametrization for the particle-hole channel, with residual pairing zero-range (volume delta) interactions for neutrons and protons separately. They determined average pair condensation energies from the seniority force framework and adjusted the delta interaction strengths \(W_q\) to reproduce these energies, using an iterative procedure that converged rapidly. The method was tested on 28 well and rigidly deformed even-even nuclei with proton numbers \(Z\) from 50 to 82, selected by the criterion that the ratio of first \(4^+\) to \(2^+\) state energies \(R_{4/2} \ge 3.290\).

## Key numbers

- Sample of 28 well and rigidly deformed nuclei with proton number \(Z\) ranging from 50 to 82, selected by \(R_{4/2} \ge 3.290\)
- Average neutron delta strength: \(\tilde{W}_n = -355\) MeV with standard deviation \(\sigma^n_W = 4\) MeV
- Average proton delta strength: \(\tilde{W}_p = -396\) MeV with standard deviation \(\sigma^p_W = 7\) MeV
- Root-mean-square deviation of moments of inertia using averaged delta strengths: \(1.91 \: \hbar^2 \mbox{MeV}^{-1}\)
- Root-mean-square deviation using individually fitted delta strengths: \(2.53 \: \hbar^2 \mbox{MeV}^{-1}\)
- Root-mean-square deviation using averaged seniority force strengths: \(2.04 \: \hbar^2 \mbox{MeV}^{-1}\)
- Average seniority force parameters: \(\tilde{G}_n = 19.57\) MeV (\(\sigma^n_G = 0.13\) MeV), \(\tilde{G}_p = 18.22\) MeV (\(\sigma^p_G = 0.20\) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the seniority force approach of Koh, Quentin, and Bonneau (2024) and Hao et al. (2024), which determined average pairing matrix elements from Strutinsky-averaged single-particle level densities and Moller-Nix pairing gap formulae. The method enables calculations beyond ground states of well deformed nuclei, such as fission barriers and quasi-particle spectra, by providing a way to define residual interaction parameters from the region where pairing correlations can be safely related to data. The results are comparable in quality to direct regional fits of moments of inertia performed by Hafiza et al. (2019) using the same Skyrme SIII interaction.
