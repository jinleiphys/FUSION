# Threshold-Aligned Pygmy Dipole Strength in Astrophysical $(n,γ)$ and $(γ,n)$ Reactions

> Reaction-rate calculations relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis depend sensitively on the nuclear $γ$-strength function ($γ$SF). Here we investigate the impact of low-lying pygmy dipole strength (PDS) in $(n,γ)$ and $(γ,n)$ reactions using $γ$SF based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional theory and propagate these strengths into Hauser--Feshbach statistical model calculations of the reaction rates. We show that considerable reaction-rate enhancements at temperatures relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis are governed by the alignment of the pygmy dipole strength energy with the neutron separation threshold $S_n$ rather than by the total low-energy strength. Consequently, nuclei such as $^{68}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn, where the PDS energy-$S_n$ alignment occurs, exhibit the strongest effects on reaction-rate enhancements. These results demonstrate that modeling reliable reaction rates in r-process nucleosynthesis necessitates accurate microscopic descriptions of low-energy dipole strength, in close synergy with experimental investigations in the vicinity of neutron threshold.

## Key claim

The enhancement of (n,γ) and (γ,n) reaction rates relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis is governed primarily by the alignment of the pygmy dipole strength (PDS) peak energy with the neutron separation threshold S_n, rather than by the total low-energy dipole strength alone; nuclei such as ⁶⁸Ni and ¹³²Sn, where this alignment occurs, exhibit the strongest effects.

## Method

The authors employed the zero-temperature limit of the finite-temperature relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-RQRPA) based on a relativistic nuclear energy density functional with the DD-PCX point-coupling interaction to calculate electric dipole γ-ray strength functions (γSF) for even-even ⁵⁶⁻⁸²Ni and ¹⁰⁰⁻¹⁵⁰Sn isotopic chains. The discrete RQRPA dipole strengths were folded with a Lorentzian function using an energy-dependent width and shift to produce continuous γSFs, which were then used as input to the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model code TALYS-2.0 to compute (n,γ) and (γ,n) cross sections and Maxwellian-averaged reaction rates, comparing results obtained with γSFs including only the giant dipole resonance (GDR) versus those including both GDR and PDS.

## Key numbers

- For neutron-rich nuclei, the cross-section ratio σ(GDR+PDS)/σ(GDR) exceeds values of 50–70 for (n,γ) reactions and 200–400 for (γ,n) reactions in the vicinity of the PDS region.
- Thermal averaging reduces the net rate enhancement to factors of 1–20 across the temperature range up to T = 10 GK.
- At kT = 30 keV, the (n,γ) rate enhancement from PDS is about 1.5 for Ni isotopes and up to 4–5 for Sn isotopes.
- For (γ,n) reactions, rate ratios become significant at kT = 200 keV, with rapidly rising values especially in Sn isotopes as S_n decreases to 2–4 MeV.
- The local cross-section enhancement at the pygmy peak can reach factors of 20–30, but thermal averaging suppresses the rate modification to ∼1.5 for Ni and up to 4–5 for Sn.
- The interference coefficients used in the Lorentzian folding are C_G = −0.584 and C_E = −1.149.

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of dipole strength using relativistic energy density functional theory (Paar, 2007; PhysRevC.67.034312) and the recently developed FT-RQRPA framework (PhysRevC.109.014314) to systematically quantify the impact of PDS on astrophysical reaction rates. It contradicts the assumption that total low-energy dipole strength alone determines reaction-rate enhancements, instead identifying the alignment of PDS peak energy with S_n as the key factor. The results enable more reliable r-process nucleosynthesis modeling by demonstrating that accurate microscopic descriptions of low-energy dipole strength near the neutron threshold are essential, and they motivate further experimental investigations of dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei at radioactive-beam facilities.
