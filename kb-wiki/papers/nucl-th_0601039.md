# Formula for proton-nucleus reaction cross section at intermediate energies and its application

> We construct a formula for proton-nucleus total reaction cross section as a function of the mass and neutron excess of the target nucleus and the proton incident energy. We deduce the dependence of the cross section on the mass number and the proton incident energy from a simple argument involving the proton optical depth within the framework of a black sphere approximation of nuclei, while we describe the neutron excess dependence by introducing the density derivative of the symmetry energy, L, on the basis of a radius formula constructed from macroscopic nuclear models. We find that the cross section formula can reproduce the energy dependence of the cross section measured for stable nuclei without introducing any adjustable energy dependent parameter. We finally discuss whether or not the reaction cross section is affected by an extremely low density tail of the neutron distribution for halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors construct a formula for the proton-nucleus total reaction cross section that reproduces the energy dependence for stable nuclei at 100-1000 MeV without any adjustable energy-dependent parameter, but the formula is explicitly limited to energies above 100 MeV where Coulomb effects and resonance features are negligible, and it does not account for possible enhancement from extremely low-density neutron halos.

## Method

The authors combine a black sphere approximation of nuclei with an optical depth argument for incident protons, using a trapezoidal density distribution with fixed central density 0.16 fm^-3 and surface thickness 2.2 fm. They derive the energy dependence solely from the empirical nucleon-nucleon total cross sections, and introduce the neutron excess dependence through a scale factor based on a radius formula from macroscopic nuclear models that depends on the density derivative of the symmetry energy L.

## Key numbers

- The black sphere radius at 800 MeV scales as a_0 = 1.214 A^{1/3} fm for stable nuclei
- The critical optical depth tau is set to 0.9, consistent with values for C-12, Ni-58, Sn-124, and Pb-208
- The formula reproduces data for T_p = 100-1000 MeV; below 100 MeV the measured cross section shows resonance features and sharp decrease from Coulomb repulsion
- For the C-12-C-12 reaction, the formula works for energy per nucleon down to 200 MeV but overestimates at lower energies
- The scale factor f for neutron excess dependence uses R_p = 0.915 A^{1/3} - 0.102 + 0.389(delta - 0.880)^2 fm and R_n = 0.880 A^{1/3}(1 + 0.00635 L delta^2 - 0.000172 L^2 delta^4) + 0.302 + 0.193 delta fm
- For halo nuclei, the formula with L=100 MeV gives values about 10% smaller than data for Li-11 but consistent values for He-6 and He-8
- The deviation between empirical data and the formula with L=100 MeV is within +/-10% for the systems studied
- Nuclear deformation with quadrupolar factor |alpha_2| ~ 0.2 gives only about 2% enhancement of the cross section

## Context

This work extends the black sphere picture previously developed by the same authors (Kohama, Iida, Oyamatsu, 2004-2005) for proton elastic scattering and reaction cross sections at 800-1000 MeV, generalizing it to a wider energy range and to unstable nuclei. The formula differs from the semiempirical parametrization of Kox et al. (1987), which introduced an adjustable energy-dependent parameter for surface transparency, and from microscopic optical potential models (Ernst, 1979; Ray, 1979; Deb et al., 2005) that produce too strong an energy dependence through the phase shift function. The work enables systematic extraction of the density symmetry coefficient L from future reaction cross section measurements on unstable nuclei, and provides a baseline for identifying possible neutron halo effects when the formula underpredicts measured cross sections.
