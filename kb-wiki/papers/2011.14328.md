# Charged-particle multiplicity dependence of charm-baryon-to-meson ratio in high-energy proton-proton collisions

> We propose that the charged-particle multiplicity dependence of the charm-baryon-to-meson ratio observed in high-energy $pp$ collisions can be explained by canonical treatment of quantum charges in the statistical hadronization model (SHM). Taking the full particle listings of PDG complemented by additional charm-baryon states from relativistic quark model predictions, we evaluate the canonical partition function and the charm-hadron chemical factors that measure the canonical suppression arising from the requirement of strict conservation of quantum charges. We demonstrate that, while charm number conservation induces common suppression on the production of both charm-baryons and -mesons, baryon (strangeness) number conservation causes further suppression on charm-baryons (charm-strange mesons) relative to nonstrange charm-mesons, thereby resulting in a decreasing $Λ_c/D^0$ ($D_s/D^0$) ratio toward smaller multiplicity events. The charm-hadron thermal densities thus computed are then used as pertinent weights to perform charm-quark fragmentation simulations yielding $p_T$-dependent $Λ_c/D^0$ and $D_s/D^0$ ratios at varying multiplicities in fair agreement with ALICE measurements.

## Key claim

The charged-particle multiplicity dependence of the charm-baryon-to-meson ratio in high-energy proton-proton collisions can be explained by canonical treatment of quantum charges in the statistical hadronization model, where strict baryon number conservation causes additional suppression of charm-baryons relative to charm-mesons in smaller systems, while the abstract does not specify that this explanation depends on the correlation volume being set to twice the rapidity density to match the data.

## Method

The authors evaluated the canonical partition function using the full particle listings from the Particle Data Group complemented by additional charm-baryon states from relativistic quark model predictions. They computed chemical factors for each charm-hadron as a measure of canonical suppression relative to the grand-canonical limit, then used the resulting thermal densities as weights to perform charm-quark fragmentation simulations within the FONLL scheme, generating transverse-momentum-dependent ratios at varying charged-particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- At volume V=20 fm³, temperature T_H=170 MeV, and γ_s=γ_c=1, the chemical factor for D⁰ is 0.00520, for Λ_c⁺ is 0.00458, and for D_s⁺ is 0.00485
- The Λ_c⁺/D⁰ ratio of chemical factors decreases from 0.990860 at V=200 fm³ to 0.655254 at V=10 fm³ (with T_H=170 MeV, γ_s=0.6, γ_c=15)
- The D_s⁺/D⁰ ratio of chemical factors decreases from 0.986047 at V=200 fm³ to 0.610774 at V=10 fm³ (with T_H=170 MeV, γ_s=0.6, γ_c=15)
- The total thermal density ratio Λ_c⁺/D⁰ (with 50% branching ratio scenario) increases from 0.284956 at V=10 fm³ to 0.476244 at V=200 fm³, approaching the grand-canonical value of 0.483060
- The total thermal density ratio D_s⁺/D⁰ increases from 0.168420 at V=10 fm³ to 0.268829 at V=200 fm³, approaching the grand-canonical value of 0.272540
- The selective baryon-number canonical ensemble gives a suppression factor I₁(x_B)/I₀(x_B) ≃ 0.752 for baryons relative to mesons at V=20 fm³, T_H=170 MeV, γ_s=γ_c=1
- The selective charm canonical ensemble gives a suppression factor I₁(x_C)/I₀(x_C) ≃ 0.00627 for charm-hadrons at V=20 fm³, T_H=170 MeV, γ_s=γ_c=1

## Context

This work extends the grand-canonical statistical hadronization model investigation of charm-baryon production by He (2019) to the canonical ensemble, explaining the multiplicity dependence observed by ALICE in √s=13 TeV proton-proton collisions. The approach builds on canonical statistical hadronization treatments by Becattini (1995, 1997, 2009) and Vovchenko (2019), and uses the same charm-baryon spectrum from relativistic quark model predictions by Ebert (2011) that was previously shown to account for the large Λ_c/D⁰ ratio in minimum-bias collisions. The results enable quantitative predictions for how charm-hadron ratios vary with system size in small collision systems, and can be tested against future measurements of charm-baryon production at varying multiplicities.
