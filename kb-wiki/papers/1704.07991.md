# Isovector properties of the nuclear energy density functional from the quark-meson coupling model

> Background: The Skyrme energy density functional is widely used in mean-field calculations of nuclear structure and dynamics. However, its reliance on phenomenology may compromise its isovector properties and its performance for exotic nuclear systems. Purpose: This work investigates the possibility of removing some phenomenology from the density functional by drawing on the high-energy degrees-of-freedom of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The QMC model has microscopically derived isovector properties and far fewer adjustable parameters. Method: The parameters of the Skyrme interaction are fixed using the energy density functional of the QMC model, to give the Skyrme-QMC (SQMC) parameterisation. Results: Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations of the Sn, Pb and $N=126$ chains are reported, in which SQMC performs with an accuracy comparable to modern phenomenological functionals. Conclusions: The isovector terms of the energy density functional are significant for the binding energies of neutron-rich nuclei. The isovector dependence of the nuclear spin-orbit interaction must be taken into account for calculations of r-process nucleosynthesis abundances.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-QMC (SQMC) parameterisation, derived from the quark-meson coupling model without fitting to experimental nuclear masses, achieves accuracy comparable to the modern phenomenological UNEDF1 functional for binding energies of neutron-rich Sn, Pb, and N=126 chains, but only when the restricted density dependence (alpha=1/6) yielding low incompressibility (~220 MeV) is used; the high-incompressibility variant SQMC^{alpha1,alpha2} performs poorly.

## Method

The parameters of the Skyrme energy density functional were fixed by equating its terms to those of the microscopically derived QMC energy density functional, producing the SQMC parameterisation. Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the hfbtho code were then performed for the Sn (Z=50) and Pb (Z=82) isotopic chains and the N=126 isotonic chain, with mixed surface and volume pairing adjusted to give a neutron pairing gap of 1.245 MeV for 120Sn. The results were compared to experimental binding energies from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation and to the UNEDF1 phenomenological functional.

## Key numbers

- QMC model nuclear matter saturation properties: density rho0 = 0.1607 fm^-3, energy per nucleon e_infinity = -15.95 MeV, symmetry energy a_s = 29.93 MeV
- SQMC nuclear matter saturation properties: rho0 = 0.1588 fm^-3, e_infinity = -15.84 MeV, incompressibility K_infinity = 218.7 MeV, symmetry energy a_s = 29.87 MeV
- SQMC spin-orbit ratio W0'/W0 = 1.78, compared to UNEDF1 ratio of 1.86 and standard Skyrme ratio of 1
- SQMC^{alpha1,alpha2} yields binding energies approximately 60 MeV away from experiment for tin nuclei
- Two-neutron and two-proton separation energies for all three parameterisations generally lie within 2 MeV of experimental data

## Context

This work extends the approach of Guichon et al. (2006), who produced the QMC700 parameterisation with only 6 parameters, by adding the isovector parameters x1-3 and W0' to create SQMC. It contrasts with the UNEDF1 functional of Kortelainen et al. (2012), which was fitted to experimental masses including those of Sn and Pb isotopes. The microscopically derived isovector spin-orbit dependence (W0'/W0 = 1.78) closely matches the phenomenological UNEDF1 value (1.86), supporting the QMC model's predictions for exotic neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
