# Hadron tomography for pion and its gravitational form factors

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are three-dimensional structure functions of hadrons, and they can reveal the orbital-angular-momentum contributions to the nucleon spin. Therefore, GPDs are important for solving the proton spin puzzle. The generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) are the $s$-$t$ crossed quantities of the GPDs, and the GDAs can be investigated in two-photon process ($γ^* γ\to h\bar h$) which is accessible at KEKB. The pion GDAs were obtained by analyzing the Belle measurements for $π^0 π^0$ production in the $e^+ e^-$ collision. From the obtained GDAs, the form factors of energy-momentum tenor were calculated for pion in the timelike region. In order to study the gravitational radii for the pion, the form factors of energy-momentum tenor were obtained in the spacelike region by using the dispersion relation. Then, the mass radius was calculated as 0.32 $\sim$ 0.39 fm and the mechanical radius was also estimated for the pion as 0.82 $\sim$ 0.88 fm by using the spacelike form factors. This is the first finding on gravitational form factors and radii of hadrons from actual experimental measurements. In the near future we can expect more precise measurements of $γ^* γ\to h\bar h$ as the Belle II started data taking by the higher luminosity Super KEKB, so that the GDAs of other hadrons could be studied as well.

# Hadron tomography for pion and its gravitational form factors

## Key claim
The pion's gravitational form factors and radii can be extracted from experimental measurements of the two-photon process $\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ using generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), but the extracted values carry a range (mass radius 0.32–0.39 fm, mechanical radius 0.82–0.88 fm) because an additional phase shift above the $KK$ threshold can be assigned either to the S-wave or D-wave phase shift.

## Method
The Belle measurements of the differential cross section for $\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ in $e^+ e^-$ collisions at KEKB were analyzed using the asymptotic form of pion GDAs, with resonance effects from $f_0(500)$ and $f_2(1270)$ included. The GDAs were extracted by fitting parameters to the experimental cross section data. From the obtained GDAs, the timelike gravitational form factors $\Theta_1$ and $\Theta_2$ of the energy-momentum tensor were calculated, and the spacelike form factors were then obtained via dispersion relations.

## Key numbers
- Mass radius of the pion: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mass}}} = 0.32 \sim 0.39$ fm, extracted from the slope of the spacelike form factor $\Theta_2$ at $t=0$.
- Mechanical radius of the pion: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{mech}}} = 0.82 \sim 0.88$ fm, extracted from the spacelike form factor $\Theta_1$.
- Charge radius of the pion (for comparison): $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_{\text{charge}}} = 0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm from Patrignani (2016).
- The $f_2(1270)$ resonance peak is clearly seen around $W = 1.27$ GeV in the cross section.

## Context
This work extends the GDA analysis of Kumano (2017) by applying the formalism to the Belle measurements of $\pi^0 \pi^0$ production reported by Masuda (2015). It provides the first extraction of gravitational form factors and radii of hadrons from actual experimental data, complementing GPD studies that address the proton spin puzzle. The results enable future studies of other hadrons using the higher-luminosity Super KEKB and Belle II, which began data taking in 2018, where more precise measurements of $\gamma^* \gamma \to h\bar h$ are expected.
