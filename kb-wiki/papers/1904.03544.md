# Charge asymmetry dependence of the elliptic flow splitting in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The elliptic flow splitting $Δv_2$ between $\bar{u}$ and $u$ quarks as well as between $π^-$ and $π^+$ in midcentral Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV has been studied, based on the framework of an extended multiphase transport model with the partonic evolution described by the chiral kinetic equations of motion. Within the available statistics, the slope of $Δv_2$ between $\bar{u}$ and $u$ quarks with respect to the electric charge asymmetry $A_{ch}$ from the linear fit is found to be negative, due to the correlation between the velocity and the coordinate in the initial parton phase-space distribution. Simulations with the magnetic field in QGP overestimate the splitting of the spin polarization between $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ observed experimentally, with the latter more consistent with results under the magnetic field in vacuum. Considering the uncertainties from the magnetic field, the quark-antiquark vector interaction, and the hadronization, as well as the hadronic evolution, our study shows that the experimentally observed positive slope of $Δv_2$ with respect to $A_{ch}$ is not likely due to the chiral magnetic wave.

# Charge asymmetry dependence of the elliptic flow splitting in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The experimentally observed positive slope of the elliptic flow difference between π⁻ and π⁺ with respect to electric charge asymmetry is not likely due to the chiral magnetic wave, once realistic initial parton distributions, magnetic field evolution in QGP, hadronization, and hadronic evolution are included in transport simulations. The negative slope observed for quarks arises from a correlation between the z-component velocity and the x coordinate in the initial parton phase-space distribution from the AMPT/HIJING model.

## Method
The authors extended the multiphase transport (AMPT) model by incorporating chiral kinetic equations of motion for partonic evolution under a magnetic field generated by spectator protons, with the magnetic field calculated both in vacuum and in QGP using a time-dependent electrical conductivity from lattice QCD. They simulated midcentral Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, including quark-antiquark vector interactions via the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, spatial coalescence hadronization, and hadronic evolution with the ART model. The elliptic flow splitting between negatively and positively charged particles was analyzed as a function of electric charge asymmetry Ach at the partonic freeze-out, initial hadronic, and final hadronic stages.

## Key numbers
- Slope of v₂(ū) - v₂(u) versus Ach with magnetic field in QGP and GV = 0: r = -3.244 ± 2.139%
- Slope of v₂(ū) - v₂(u) versus Ach with uncorrelated initial parton distribution: r = 1.161 ± 2.073%
- Slope of v₂(π⁻) - v₂(π⁺) versus Ach at freeze-out hadronic stage with magnetic field in QGP and GV = 0: r = 0.301 ± 1.154%
- Slope of v₂(π⁻) - v₂(π⁺) versus Ach at freeze-out hadronic stage with magnetic field in vacuum: r = 0.861 ± 1.14%
- Experimentally observed slope of v₂(π⁻) - v₂(π⁺) versus Ach: about 3%
- Average impact parameter for midcentral (20-50%) collisions: 8.87 fm
- Critical temperature Tc ≈ 0.165 GeV used for electrical conductivity calculation
- Magnetic field drops by factor of 5-10 in first 0.15-0.20 fm/c
- Partonic scatterings end at approximately t ~ 3.7 fm/c
- Momentum cutoff in NJL model: 750 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Sun et al. (2016) that found a negative slope of elliptic flow splitting using chiral kinetic equations with a parameterized decaying magnetic field and thermalized initial conditions. It contradicts the expectation that the chiral magnetic wave, proposed by Burnier et al. (2011) as producing an electric quadrupole moment leading to charge-dependent elliptic flow, explains the positive slope observed by the STAR Collaboration (2015). The study also connects to measurements of Λ and Λ̄ spin polarization by STAR (2007, 2018), finding that the magnetic field in vacuum better reproduces the negligible splitting between Λ and Λ̄ spin polarizations, while the magnetic field in QGP overestimates this splitting. The results enable future investigations of mean-field potential effects as alternative explanations for the observed positive slope.
