# Quasifree photoabsorption on neutron-proton pairs in 3He

> Three-body photodisintegration of 3He is calculated in the photon energy range 200 - 400 MeV assuming quasifree absorption on np pairs both in initial quasideuteron and singlet configurations. The model includes the normal nucleonic current, explicit meson exchange currents and the Delta(1232)-isobar excitation. The total cross section is increased by a factor of about 1.5 compared with free deuteron photodisintegration. Well below and above the Delta region also some spin observables differ significantly from the ones of free deuteron disintegration due to the more compressed wave function of the correlated np pairs in 3He compared to the deuteron. The initial singlet state causes a significant change in the analyzing power Ay. These differences could presumably be seen at the conjugate angles where two-body effects are maximized and where photoreactions could complement similar pion absorption experiments. # Quasifree photoabsorption on neutron-proton pairs in 3He

## Key claim

The total cross section for three-body photodisintegration of 3He in the photon energy range 200–400 MeV is increased by a factor of about 1.5 compared with free deuteron photodisintegration, but this enhancement arises from the compressed wave function of correlated np pairs in 3He rather than from the number of quasideuteron pairs present. The differences in spin observables between the quasifree and free reactions are most significant well below and above the Delta resonance region, while near the resonance the changes from different mechanisms cancel.

## Method

The authors calculated three-body photodisintegration of 3He using a quasifree model where the photon is absorbed on a correlated np pair (either in a quasideuteron or singlet configuration) while the third nucleon acts as a spectator. The model includes the normal nucleonic current, explicit pi- and rho-meson exchange currents, and Delta(1232)-isobar excitation via a coupled NN-NDelta channel approach, using the Bonn OBEPR potential for the final-state interaction and several models for the initial pair wave functions including a correlation function from Faddeev calculations with the Reid soft core potential.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for 3He photodisintegration is increased by a factor of about 1.5 compared with free deuteron photodisintegration in the 200–400 MeV photon energy range
- The quasideuteron cross section is enhanced by nearly a factor of 3 relative to the free deuteron case due to the compressed wave function, then reduced by a statistical factor of 1/2 to give the overall factor of about 1.5
- The D-state probability of the quasideuteron wave function is 10.5%
- The spectator momentum distribution from TAGX collaboration peaks around 100 MeV/c, corresponding to about 5 MeV kinetic energy
- The Delta-resonance energy corresponds to approximately 320 MeV photon energy
- The bound pair is treated as being bound by 10 MeV more than the deuteron (5 MeV from actual binding energy difference and 5 MeV average kinetic energy for the spectator)

## Context

This work extends the model of Wilhelm, Leidemann, and Arenhoevel (1990s) for deuteron photodisintegration to the quasifree situation in 3He, complementing earlier theoretical work by Laget (1980s) on three-body photodisintegration and parallel studies of pion absorption on nucleon pairs in 3He by Niskanen and collaborators. The results are compared to experimental data from the TAGX collaboration and earlier measurements by D'Hose and colleagues. The paper enables experimental tests at facilities like LEGS at Brookhaven and suggests that photon energies below the Delta region are most promising for studying differences in initial pair wave functions, in contrast to the Delta region where observables are insensitive to wave function details.
