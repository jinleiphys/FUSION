# Impact of the chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations on transport coefficients of heavy quarks and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of quark-gluon plasma

> The chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) play an important role as these field fluctuations result energy gain of heavy quarks. We consider these fluctuations and evaluate the transport coefficients, e.g., drag and diffusion coefficients of charm quarks and shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$) of the QGP. We find a significant effect of such fluctuations on the transport coefficients. These fluctuations cause a reduction of the drag and diffusion coefficients. We also observe that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the QGP is closer to the value obtained in Lattice QCD (LQCD) and functional renormalization group calculations when the effects of such fluctuations are included.

## Key claim

The inclusion of chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) reduces the drag and diffusion coefficients of charm quarks and increases the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s), bringing it closer to values obtained from Lattice QCD (LQCD) and functional renormalization group calculations, but only when the fluctuations are treated via a semiclassical approximation equivalent to the Hard Thermal Loop approximation in the weak coupling limit.

## Method

The authors model heavy quark propagation through an isentropically expanding QGP medium with cylindrical geometry, using initial conditions for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (initial temperature T0 = 430 MeV, proper time τ0 = 0.3 fm, freeze-out time τf = 6 fm). They compute collisional energy loss via the Braaten-Thoma (BT) formalism, radiative energy loss via the first-order opacity expansion of the DGLV reaction operator formalism, and energy gain from chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations using a leading-log (LL) semiclassical approximation. The net energy loss (collisional + radiative minus gain) is used to derive drag and diffusion coefficients via the Fokker-Planck equation, and η/s is obtained from the relation η/s ≈ 1.25 T³/ˆq, where ˆq is related to the diffusion coefficient.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 = 430 MeV for minimum-bias Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Charm quark mass Mc = 1.25 GeV; strong coupling constant αs = 0.3; number of active quark flavors nf = 2
- Average path length ⟨L⟩ = 4.16 fm; impact parameter ⟨b⟩ = 9.68 fm; number of participants Npart = 113
- Critical temperature Tc ≈ 155 MeV (from LQCD, used as reference)
- The reduction of the drag coefficient due to fluctuations is significant for charm quark momentum p < 25 GeV; above 25 GeV the effect is negligibly small
- The obtained η/s values are close to LQCD calculations (Mayer 2007, Mayer 2009, Borsanyi 2014) and functional renormalization group results (Haas) within their uncertainties when fluctuations are included

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients and η/s (Das 2010, Mazumder 2011, Rapp 2009, Mustafa 2005) that considered only collisional and radiative energy loss, by adding the energy gain from chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations previously quantified by the same group (Sheikh 2018). The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy flavor suppression in heavy-ion collisions and bring η/s estimates closer to first-principles LQCD results (Borsanyi 2014, Mayer 2007, Mayer 2009) and functional renormalization group calculations (Haas 2014), though the values near Tc fall slightly below the AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/(4π).
