# Probing nuclear observables via primordial nucleosynthesis

> We study the dependence of primordial nuclear abundances on fundamental nuclear observables such as binding energies, scattering lengths, neutron lifetime, \textit{etc.} by varying these quantities. The numerical computations were performed with four publicly available codes, thus facilitating an investigation of the model-dependent (systematic) uncertainties on these dependences. Indeed deviations of the order of a few percent are found. Moreover, accounting for the temperature dependence of the sensitivity of the rates to some relevant parameters leads to a reduction of the sensitivity of the final primordial abundances, which in some cases is appreciable. These effects are considered to be relevant for studies of the dependence of the nuclear abundances on fundamental parameters such as quark masses or couplings underlying the nuclear parameters studied here.

## Key claim

The paper systematically studies how primordial nuclear abundances depend on fundamental nuclear observables (binding energies, scattering lengths, neutron lifetime) and finds that accounting for the temperature dependence of the sensitivity of reaction rates to these parameters leads to a reduction of the sensitivity of final abundances, which in some cases is appreciable, a nuance that previous studies (e.g., Dent 2007, Berengut 2009, Bedaque 2010, Berengut 2013) neglected by assuming temperature-independent Q-value changes.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Dent (2007), Berengut (2009), Bedaque (2010), and Berengut (2013) that derived bounds on fundamental parameters (quark masses, fine-structure constant) from BBN but used a temperature-independent approximation for Q-value changes in reaction rates. By incorporating the full temperature dependence of the sensitivity, the authors find a reduction in sensitivity that had been overlooked. The study also provides the first systematic comparison of four public BBN codes to quantify model-dependent systematic uncertainties, enabling future work to derive more reliable constraints on variations of quark masses and the QCD خ¸-parameter from primordial abundances.
