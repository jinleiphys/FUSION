# Effect of colour singletness of quark-gluon plasma in quark-hadron phase transition

> Consequences of the constraint of SU(3) colour singletness of quark-gluon plasma are studied. This restriction increases the free energy barrier for the formation of hadronic bubble in supercooled phase and influences significantly the dynamics of the initial stage of quark-hadron phase transition. It also introduces terms dependent on the volume occupied by the plasma in the energy density and the pressure. These modifactions vanish in the limit of an infinite volume. The last stage of the hadronization of the QGP likely to be formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions is necessarily characterized by a decreasing volume containing the quark matter, and thus these corrections become important. The nucleation of plasma droplets at AGS energies is also seen to be strongly affected by the requirement of colour singletness, and the choice of prefactor.

## Key claim
The requirement of SU(3) colour singletness for the quark-gluon plasma increases the free energy barrier for hadronic bubble nucleation and introduces volume-dependent corrections to the energy density and pressure, which vanish only in the infinite-volume limit; these corrections become significant during the late stages of hadronization in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the plasma volume is necessarily decreasing.

## Method
The authors incorporate a colour-singlet equation of state for a baryon-free quark-gluon plasma, derived from a projected grand canonical partition function, into homogeneous nucleation theory for the quark-hadron phase transition. They compute the free energy change for hadronic bubble formation, including the colour-singlet corrections, and solve for the critical bubble radius and nucleation rate using the dynamical prefactor of Csernai and Kapusta (1992). The time evolution of the phase transition is then modeled using both Bjorken longitudinal hydrodynamics and Cooper-Frye-Schönberg spherical hydrodynamics, coupling the temperature evolution to the fraction of space converted to hadronic matter.

## Key numbers
- The degree of supercooling for the colour singlet case is about 30%, which is 12.5% more than for the nonsinglet case.
- The colour singlet requirement results in 10% extra entropy generation compared to the colour nonsinglet case.
- At the end of hadronization, a remnant of quark matter of mass ~5-10 GeV occupying a volume ~10-15 fm³ remains unconverted per unit rapidity in the central region.
- For longitudinal expansion, the early-stage critical hadronic bubble radius is ~1 fm for the colour singlet case compared to ~0.5 fm for the nonsinglet case.
- For plasma droplet nucleation in superheated hadronic matter, the colour singlet restriction increases the critical radius by a factor of ~3-5 over the temperature range studied.
- The nucleation rate of plasma droplets at T ~ 170 MeV is suppressed by four orders of magnitude due to colour singletness, while at 250 MeV it is enhanced by one order of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical treatment of the quark-hadron phase transition developed by Csernai and Kapusta (1992) and Csernai et al. (1993) by incorporating the colour singlet constraint on the quark-gluon plasma equation of state, previously studied by Mustafa (1994) and others. The results show that colour singletness significantly slows hadronization and increases supercooling compared to the standard Stefan-Boltzmann treatment, and it predicts a possible remnant of unhadronized quark matter. The findings also contrast with the idealized Maxwell construction often used as a model of hadronization, and they suggest that the dynamics of nucleation at AGS energies are strongly affected by both the colour singlet requirement and the choice of prefactor, with implications for the seeding mechanism proposed by Kapusta and Vischer (1995).
