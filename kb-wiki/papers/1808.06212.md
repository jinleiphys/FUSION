# A resummed method of moments for the relativistic hydrodynamic expansion

> The relativistic method of moments is one of the most successful approaches to extract second order viscous hydrodynamics from a kinetic underlying background. The equations can be systematically improved to higher order, and they have already shown a fast convergence to the kinetic results. In order to generalize that method, we introduced long range effects in the form of effective (medium dependent) masses and gauge (coherent) fields. The most straightforward generalization of the hydrodynamic expansion is problematic, or simply ill-defined, at higher order. Instead of introducing an additional set of approximations, we propose to rewrite the series in terms of moments resumming the contributions of infinite non-hydrodynamics modes. The resulting equations are consistent with hydrodynamics and well defined at all order. We tested the new approximation against the exact solutions of the Boltzmann-Maxwell-Vlasov equations in $(0+1)$-dimensions, finding a fast and stable convergence to the exact results.

## Key claim

The standard method-of-moments expansion for relativistic hydrodynamics, when generalized to include long-range interactions via effective masses and gauge fields, becomes ill-defined at higher orders due to infrared divergences in moments with negative energy index; the authors propose a resummation of the moment series using a Gaussian weight that cures this problem while remaining consistent with second-order viscous hydrodynamics at leading order.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation with effective masses and electromagnetic fields, writing exact evolution equations for the standard moments of the distribution function. They identify that moments with energy index \(r+l<-2\) diverge in the ultra-relativistic limit \(m/T\to 0\), making the straightforward higher-order expansion problematic. Instead of truncating or approximating these divergent moments, they define a new set of "resummed moments" \(\Phi^{\mu_1\cdots\mu_l}(x,\xi^2)\) by inserting a Gaussian factor \(e^{-\xi^2(p\cdot u)^2}\) into the momentum integral, which re-sums contributions from infinitely many standard moments. They derive the exact evolution equation for these resummed moments and test the resulting hierarchy of approximations against exact solutions of the Boltzmann-Maxwell-Vlasov equations in \((0+1)\)-dimensions for a massless particle-antiparticle gas with a longitudinal electric field, using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the collision kernel.

## Key numbers

- The resummed moment expansion was tested against exact solutions in \((0+1)\)-dimensions for a massless particle-antiparticle gas with a longitudinal electric field evolving via Maxwell equations.
- The leading order (LO) approximation corresponds to dynamical tensors up to rank 2; successive orders include tensors up to rank 4 (NLO), rank 6 (NNLO), rank 8, and rank 12.
- The figures show relative precision of the approximation, with the rank-12 approximation (light blue, long dot-dashed line) nearly indistinguishable from the exact solution (black solid line) across the plotted range.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic method of moments for viscous hydrodynamics, previously developed for the Boltzmann equation by Denicol (2014) and shown to converge faster than gradient expansions by Denicol (2016), to include long-range interactions via effective masses and gauge fields. The resummation technique avoids the infrared divergences that plague the straightforward moment expansion when \(m/T\to 0\), which were noted as problematic by Denicol (2012). The approach enables systematic improvement of hydrodynamic descriptions for systems with electromagnetic fields, such as those encountered in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the authors suggest future extension to the full quantum case using Wigner functions.
