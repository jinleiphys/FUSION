# On the asymptotic methods for nuclear collective models

> Contractions of orthogonal groups to Euclidean groups are applied to analytic descriptions of nuclear quantum phase transitions. The semiclassical asymptotic of multipole collective Hamiltonians are also investigated.

# On the asymptotic methods for nuclear collective models

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that contractions of orthogonal groups SO(n+1) to Euclidean groups E(n) provide an analytic framework for describing nuclear quantum phase transitions, specifically for the E(5), E(2n+1), and X(2n+1) models, but the method relies on asymptotic approximations (e.g., Bessel function zeros approximated by Laguerre polynomial zeros) rather than exact solutions.

## Method
The authors apply Lie algebra and Lie group contraction techniques, mapping the orthogonal group SO(n+1) to the Euclidean group E(n) in the limit R → ∞ (where R is the sphere radius), using R⁻¹ as the contraction parameter. They derive asymptotic relations between basis functions, specifically expressing Bessel functions as limits of Laguerre polynomials and associated Legendre polynomials. For the semiclassical analysis, they apply the Birkhoff-Gustavson normal form method to the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian H_n with polynomial potential of degree n, and use logarithmic perturbation theory (hbar-expansion) for the radial Schrodinger equation of a spherical multipole Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- The contraction parameter is R⁻¹, where R is the radius of the sphere S_n, taken in the limit R → ∞
- For the Henon-Heiles-type Hamiltonian H_3, the potential V_n includes terms with exponents 2m+3m' ≤ n, where m,m' ≥ 0
- For n=4, the separatrix condition is given by λ = 9c₀₁²/(32c₁₀c₂₀) = 1, where c₁₀, c₂₀ > 0
- The semiclassical quantum spectrum for the integrable form is K(n₁+1/2, n₂+1/2), where n₁ and n₂ are non-negative integers
- The energy spectrum from logarithmic perturbation theory is obtained up to order hbar⁵, with explicit expressions for E₁, E₂, E₃, and E₄
- E₁ = 1/2 + m, where m = 2n + l + N (n = radial quantum number, l = angular quantum number, N = dimension parameter)
- The quantization condition gives ∮ f₁(β) dβ = 2πi(2n + l + N)

## Context
This work extends the contraction methods originally introduced by Segal (1951) and Inonu and Wigner (1953) for Lie groups, applying them to nuclear collective models. The authors build on the E(5) model of Iachello (2000) and their own prior work (Gheorghe and Raduta, cited as RGF) on approximating Bessel function zeros using Laguerre functions. The results enable analytic descriptions of transitional nuclei near critical points of quantum phase transitions, complementing the work of Cejnar and Iachello (2007) and Casten, Cejnar, and Iachello on phase transitions in nuclear structure. The semiclassical analysis of the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian extends the Henon-Heiles (1964) Hamiltonian studies using the Birkhoff-Gustavson normal form method.
