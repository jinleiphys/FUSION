# Invariant amplitudes, unpolarized cross sections, and polarization asymmetries in (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering

> At leading order in weak and electromagnetic couplings, cross sections for (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering are determined by four nucleon form factors that depend on the momentum transfer $Q^2$. Including radiative corrections in the Standard Model and potential new physics contributions beyond the Standard Model, eight invariant amplitudes are possible, depending on both $Q^2$ and the (anti)neutrino energy $E_ν$. We review the definition of these amplitudes and use them to compute both unpolarized and polarized observables including radiative corrections. We show that unpolarized accelerator neutrino cross-section measurements can probe new physics parameter space within the constraints inferred from precision beta decay measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that unpolarized accelerator neutrino cross-section measurements can probe new physics parameter space within the constraints inferred from precision beta decay measurements, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that beyond-Standard-Model contributions to the invariant amplitudes scale with lepton mass in the same way as Standard Model contributions, and that the new physics can be identified with modifications to tree-level electroweak form factors.

## Method

The authors decompose the matrix element for charged-current elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering into eight invariant amplitudes that depend on both the squared momentum transfer \(Q^2\) and the (anti)neutrino energy \(E_\nu\), including radiative corrections and potential new physics contributions. They compute unpolarized cross sections and all possible single-spin asymmetries (target, recoil, and lepton) in terms of these amplitudes, and compare the predicted observables to constraints from precision beta decay measurements on the scalar amplitude \(f_3\), tensor amplitude \(f_T\), and second-class axial amplitude \(f_{A3}\).

## Key numbers

- Beta decay constraint on the real part of the scalar amplitude: \(\mathfrak{Re}f_3(0)/f_1(0) = 0.0 \pm 1.8\) from Hardy (2020)
- Beta decay constraint on the imaginary part of the scalar amplitude: \(\mathfrak{Im}f_3(0) = 13 \pm 54\) from Gonzalez-Alonso (2018)
- Beta decay constraint on the real part of the tensor amplitude: \(\mathfrak{Re}f_{T}(0) = -9.3 \pm 10.3\) from Gonzalez-Alonso (2018)
- Beta decay constraint on the imaginary part of the tensor amplitude: \(\mathfrak{Im}f_{T}(0) = -1.9 \pm 15.4\) from Gonzalez-Alonso (2018)
- Beta decay constraint on the second-class axial amplitude: \(\mathfrak{Re}f_{A3}(0) = 0 \pm 0.075\) from Day (2012)
- The dipole form factor scale for new physics amplitudes is taken as \(\Lambda \approx 1~\mathrm{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Llewellyn-Smith (1971) for (anti)neutrino-nucleon elastic scattering by including all eight invariant amplitudes that arise from radiative corrections and beyond-Standard-Model physics, building on previous studies of single-spin asymmetries by Tomalak (2020) and the Snowmass 2021 white paper by Alvarez-Ruso (2022). The paper enables future experimental measurements at DUNE and Hyper-K to constrain new physics through both unpolarized cross sections and polarization asymmetries, complementing constraints from precision beta decay measurements by Gonzalez-Alonso (2018) and Hardy (2020).
