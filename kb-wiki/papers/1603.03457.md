# New class of hybrid EoS and Bayesian M-R data analysis

> We explore systematically a new class of two-phase equations of state (EoS) for hybrid stars that is characterized by three main features : (1) stiffening of the nuclear EoS at supersaturation densities due to quark exchange effects (Pauli blocking) between hadrons, modelled by an excluded volume correction, (2) stiffening of the quark matter EoS at high densities due to multiquark interactions and (3) possibility for a strong first order phase transition with an early onset and large density jump. The third feature results from a Maxwell construction for the possible transition from the nuclear to a quark matter phase and its properties depend on the two parameters used for (1) and (2), respectively. Varying these two parameters one obtains a class of hybrid EoS that yields solutions of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for sequences of hadronic and hybrid stars in the mass-radius diagram which cover the full range of patterns according to the Alford-Han-Prakash classification following which a hybrid star branch can be either absent, connected or disconnected with the hadronic one. The latter case often includes a tiny connected branch. The disconnected hybrid star branch, also called "third family", corresponds to high-mass twin stars characterized by the same gravitational mass but different radii. We perform a Bayesian analysis and demonstrate that the observation of such a pair of high-mass twin stars would have a sufficient discriminating power to favor hybrid EoS with a strong first order phase transition over alternative EoS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a new class of hybrid equations of state (EoS) with a strong first-order phase transition and an early onset can produce high-mass twin stars (a disconnected "third family" branch in the mass-radius diagram), and that a Bayesian analysis shows observing such a pair of twin stars would have sufficient discriminating power to favor these hybrid EoS over alternative EoS, provided the actual neutron star radii are large (around 15 km) as reported by Bogdanov (2012) and Hambaryan (2014).

## Method

The authors constructed a two-parameter family of hybrid EoS by combining a hadronic RMF EoS (DD2 or DD2F, with three symmetry energy variations each) with excluded volume corrections parameterized by p, and a quark matter EoS from an NJL model with multiquark interactions parameterized by eta_4. A Maxwell construction determined the first-order phase transition between phases, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to generate mass-radius sequences. A Bayesian analysis was performed using observational constraints: the maximum mass of PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses), a radius measurement of PSR J0437-4715 (15.5 km), and a gravitational mass versus baryonic mass constraint from PSR J0737-3039(B), along with fictitious radius measurements for two high-mass pulsars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass constraint: PSR J0348+0432 has mean mass 2.01 solar masses with variance 0.04 solar masses
- Radius constraint: PSR J0437-4715 has mean radius 15.5 km with variance 1.5 km
- Gravitational mass of PSR J0737-3039(B): 1.249 solar masses with error bar +/- 0.001 solar masses
- Baryon mass from evolution model for PSR J0737-3039(B): 1.366 solar masses with statistical uncertainty 0.003 solar masses
- Low-mass neutron star companion of PSR J1756-2251: gravitational mass 1.249 +/- 0.007 solar masses
- Saturation density: n_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Excluded volume parameter p ranges from 0 to 80 (dimensionless, where p = 10 x v[fm^3])
- Fictitious radius measurement masses: PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 solar masses and PSR J1614-2230 at 1.94 solar masses, both with error bars +/- 0.04 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the Alford-Han-Prakash classification (2013) of hybrid star sequences by systematically exploring a new class of two-phase EoS that includes stiffening of both the hadronic phase (via excluded volume corrections mimicking Pauli blocking) and the quark phase (via multiquark interactions). It builds on the high-mass twin star concept introduced by Benic (2014) and addresses the hyperon puzzle, the masquerade problem (Alford, 2004), and the reconfinement case (Lastowiecki, 2011; Zdunik, 2012). The Bayesian analysis methodology follows earlier work by Steiner (2010) and the authors' own previous Bayesian studies (Alvarez-Castillo, 2014; Blaschke, 2014; Ayriyan, 2015), but uses different observables including the gravitational mass versus baryonic mass relation from Podsiadlowski (2005). The results enable future discrimination between hybrid EoS with strong first-order phase transitions and alternative EoS through precise radius measurements of high-mass pulsars.
