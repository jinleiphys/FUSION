# Three-body resonant radiative capture reactions in astrophysics

> We develop the formalism based on the S-matrix for $3 \to 3$ scattering to derive the direct three-body resonant radiative capture reaction rate. Within this formalism the states, which decay only/predominantly directly into three-body continuum, should also be included in the capture rate calculations. %The results obtained indicate that some theoretical calculations are %incomplete. Basing on the derivation, as well as on the modern experimental data and theoretical calculations concerning $^{17}$Ne nucleus, we significantly update the reaction rate for $^{15}$O($2p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{17}$Ne process in explosive environment. We also discuss possible implementations for the $^{18}$Ne($2p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{20}$Mg, $^{38}$Ca($2p$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{40}$Ti, and $^4$He($nﾎｱ$,$ﾎｳ$)$^{9}$Be reactions.

## Key claim

The total three-body resonant radiative capture reaction rate is given by a single formula that correctly and completely includes both sequential capture (via intermediate two-body resonances) and direct capture (from the three-body continuum), contrary to some previous calculations that omitted the direct capture contribution and therefore underestimated the rate for certain astrophysically important reactions.

## Method

The authors derived the three-body radiative capture reaction rate using S-matrix formalism for 3-to-3 scattering, decomposing the three-body plane wave over hyperspherical harmonics and obtaining the scattering amplitude in the asymptotic region where the hyperradius goes to infinity. They then compared this direct capture formalism with the standard sequential capture formalism based on rate equations for intermediate resonant states, showing that the two approaches are complementary and combine into a single total rate expression depending on the sum of partial widths for decay into both binary and three-body channels.

## Key numbers

- For the 15O(2p,gamma)17Ne reaction at 0.3 GK, the rate from this work is 4.9 x 10^-19 cm^6/s, compared to 2.9 x 10^-23 cm^6/s from Gor95, an increase of up to 4 orders of magnitude; the upper limit from this work is 2.9 x 10^-14 cm^6/s
- At 0.5 GK, the rate from this work is 2.1 x 10^-15 cm^6/s versus 6.0 x 10^-18 cm^6/s from Gor95, with an upper limit of 1.3 x 10^-12 cm^6/s
- At 1.0 GK, the rate from this work is 3.5 x 10^-12 cm^6/s versus 5.0 x 10^-14 cm^6/s from Gor95, with an upper limit of 1.3 x 10^-11 cm^6/s
- The 3/2- state of 17Ne at 1288 keV has a 2p width of 4.1 x 10^-16 MeV (theoretical, from Gri03) and an experimental lower lifetime limit of tau > 26 ps corresponding to Gamma_2p < 2.5 x 10^-11 MeV (from Chr02)
- The 5/2- state of 17Ne at 1764 keV has a gamma width of 1.7 x 10^-3 eV calculated from B(E2)=124(18) e^2fm^4 (from Chr02), about 30 times larger than the mirror state in 17N
- For the 9Be 5/2- state at 2.429 MeV, the branching to the three-body channel is Br(3) ~ 0.93-0.95 and to the binary channel Br(2) ~ 0.07-0.05, with the 8Be ground state resonance width Gamma_x = 6.8 eV

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Fowler (1967) for three-body resonant capture rates, which was based on complete thermal equilibrium and detailed balance, by providing a derivation from S-matrix theory that clarifies the role of direct three-body capture. The authors show that previous calculations by Gor95 for the 15O(2p,gamma)17Ne reaction were incomplete because they omitted states that decay directly into the three-body continuum, such as the first 3/2- excited state of 17Ne. The updated rates are relevant for rp-process waiting points in explosive hydrogen burning, where reactions like 15O(2p,gamma)17Ne, 18Ne(2p,gamma)20Mg, 38Ca(2p,gamma)40Ti, and 4He(nalpha,gamma)9Be can bridge waiting points more efficiently than waiting for beta+ decay. The formalism enables future calculations for other three-body radiative capture reactions where direct decay channels may be significant.
