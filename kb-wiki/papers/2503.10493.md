# Explanation of the observed violation of isospin symmetry in relativistic nucleus-nucleus reactions

> The violation of isospin symmetry in nucleus-nucleus reactions, as shown in the ratio ${R_K=(K^++K^-)/(K^0+\bar{K}^0)}$ presented by NA61/SHINE, can be understood by introducing results from color-string fragmentation in $e^+e^-$ to nuclear reactions. This novel input allows for a consistent description of the $e^+e^-$ data, proton+proton data and finally nucleus-nucleus data at all investigated energies. We conclude that the observed isospin violation in nucleus-nucleus reactions is explained by asymmetric production of up- and down-quarks in the elementary color field fragmentation process.

## Key claim
The observed violation of isospin symmetry in nucleus-nucleus reactions, as quantified by the kaon ratio R_K > 1 reported by NA61/SHINE, is not a genuine nuclear effect but originates from an asymmetric production probability of up- versus down-quarks in the elementary color field fragmentation process, which is already present in e+e- annihilations.

## Method
The authors implemented an asymmetric up- to down-quark production probability ratio P(u):P(d) ≈ 3:1 (instead of the standard isospin-symmetric 1:1) into the UrQMD event generator's color field fragmentation parameters. They then compared the resulting kaon momentum and rapidity distributions to experimental data from BESIII for e+e- annihilations at √s = 3.050 GeV, from NA61/SHINE for proton+proton collisions at √s = 12.3 GeV, and for central Ar+Sc collisions at √s_NN = 11.9 GeV, as well as to the world data compilation on R_K across nucleus-nucleus systems.

## Key numbers
- R_K = 1.144 ± 0.026 (averaged over all available energies in nucleus-nucleus reactions), indicating more charged (anti-)kaons than neutral (anti-)kaons and violating isospin symmetry expectations.
- P(u):P(d) ≈ 3:1, the refitted ratio of up-quark to down-quark production probability in color field fragmentation, replacing the standard assumption of 1:1.
- Weak interactions contribute an uncertainty in R_K of approximately 0.13%, and symmetry breaking from u/d quark mass differences in the hadron resonance gas model increases R_K by at most ≈ 3%.
- The observed asymmetry in e+e- is consistent with the charm-sector ratio D0/D+ ≈ 2.2–2.7 from the Particle Data Group (2024).

## Context
This work resolves the puzzle raised by the NA61/SHINE collaboration's observation of isospin-symmetry violation in nucleus-nucleus collisions (NA61/SHINE, 2023). Prior theoretical explorations by Brylinski (2023) and Giacosa (2024) considered weak interactions, electromagnetic processes, and quark mass effects but found no explanation. The authors show that the same asymmetry appears in e+e- data from BESIII (2022, 2025) and in proton+proton data from NA61/SHINE (2017, 2024), and that NNLO fragmentation function approaches assuming isospin symmetry (Gao, 2024) cannot describe the low-momentum asymmetry. This enables a consistent description of kaon production across all collision systems without invoking new nuclear physics.
