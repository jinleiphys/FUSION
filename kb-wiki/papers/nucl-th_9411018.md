# Nuclear Breathing Mode in the Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The breathing-mode giant monopole resonance is studied within the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. Using a broad range of parameter sets, an analysis of constrained incompressibility and excitation energy of isoscalar monopole states in finite nuclei is performed. It is shown that the non-linear scalar self-interaction and the resulting surface properties influence the breathing-mode considerably. It is observed that dynamical surface properties respond differently in the RMF theory than in the Skyrme approach. A comparison is made with the incompressibility derived from the semi-infinite nuclear matter and with constrained nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculaions.

# Nuclear Breathing Mode in the Relativistic Mean Field Theory

## Key claim

The incompressibility of a finite nucleus in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory does not depend on the nuclear matter incompressibility in a simple monotonic way, and the surface incompressibility can have a magnitude different from the bulk incompressibility, in contrast to the Skyrme ansatz where the surface incompressibility scales approximately as the negative of the bulk value.

## Method

Constrained RMF calculations were performed by solving the Dirac equation with a Lagrange multiplier term proportional to r² to fix the nuclear rms radius at various values, yielding the constrained energy as a function of radius. The constrained incompressibility K_C(A) was obtained from the second derivative of the energy with respect to radius, and the inertial parameter B_rel(A) was derived by applying a local Lorentz boost to the constrained spinors and solving the continuity equation for the velocity field. The breathing-mode frequency was then computed as ω_C = sqrt(K_C / B_rel) for spherical nuclei using five parameter sets (NL1, NL-SH, NL2, HS, L1) with nuclear matter incompressibilities ranging from 211.7 to 626.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility K_NM values for parameter sets: NL1 = 211.7 MeV, NL-SH = 354.9 MeV, NL2 = 399.2 MeV, HS = 545 MeV, L1 = 626.3 MeV
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with NL1: constrained incompressibility K_C = 116.0 MeV, collective mass B_rel = 0.7825 MeV⁻¹, breathing-mode energy ω_C = 12.2 MeV
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with NL-SH: K_C = 197.7 MeV, B_rel = 0.8084 MeV⁻¹, ω_C = 15.6 MeV
- For ¹⁶O with NL1: K_C = 74.0 MeV, B_rel = 0.2282 MeV⁻¹, ω_C = 18.0 MeV
- For ¹⁶O with NL-SH: K_C = 105.6 MeV, B_rel = 0.2911 MeV⁻¹, ω_C = 19.0 MeV
- Empirical GMR energy for ²⁰⁸Pb: 13.9 ± 0.3 MeV; for ⁹⁰Zr: 16.4 ± 0.4 MeV
- Surface incompressibility K_S for NL1: -333.1 MeV; for NL-SH: -610.1 MeV
- Ratio of surface to bulk incompressibility: 1.58 for NL1, 1.72 for NL-SH
- Ratio B_sr/B_rel for ¹⁶O with NL1: 0.742; with NL-SH: 0.544
- Ratio B_sr/B_rel for ²⁰⁸Pb with NL1: 0.991; with NL-SH: 0.939

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of the breathing mode by Maruyama and Suzuki (1989) and Boersma et al. (1991) that used only the linear Walecka model, which cannot describe surface properties correctly. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach, where Treiner et al. (1981) showed that surface incompressibility scales approximately as -K_NM, leading to a simple monotonic relationship between finite-nucleus incompressibility and nuclear matter incompressibility. The results enable a more nuanced extraction of nuclear matter incompressibility from GMR data, showing that the empirical ²⁰⁸Pb GMR energy of 13.9 MeV is encompassed by constrained calculations with K_NM between 280 and 320 MeV, while the ⁹⁰Zr value is overestimated by about 1.5-2 MeV by the corresponding curve.
