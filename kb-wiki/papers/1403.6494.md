# Ensemble mean $\bf p_t$ vs charged-hadron multiplicities in high energy nuclear collisions

> Measurements of event-ensemble mean transverse momentum $\langle p_t \rangle$ vs charged-hadron multiplicity $n_{ch}$ for $p_t$ spectra from 5 TeV p-Pb and 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions and from p-p collisions for several energies have been reported recently. While in all cases $\langle p_t \rangle$ increases monotonically with $n_{ch}$ the rate of increase is very different from system to system. Comparisons with several theory Monte Carlos reveal substantial disagreements and lead to considerable uncertainty on how to interpret the $\langle p_t \rangle$ data. In the present study we develop a two-component (soft+hard) model (TCM) of $p_t$ production in high energy nuclear collisions and apply it to the $\langle p_t \rangle$ data. The soft component is assumed to be a universal feature of high energy collisions independent of A-B system or energy. The hard-component model is based on the observation that dijet production in p-p collisions does not satisfy the eikonal approximation but does so in A-A collisions. Hard-component properties are determined from previous measurements of hadron spectrum hard components, jet spectra and fragmentation functions. The TCM describes the p-p and Pb-Pb $\langle p_t \rangle$ data accurately, within data uncertainties, and the p-Pb data appear to transition smoothly from p-p to A-A $n_{ch}$ trends.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the increase of ensemble mean transverse momentum ⟨p_t⟩ with charged-hadron multiplicity n_ch in all collision systems (p-p, p-Pb, Pb-Pb) is entirely due to jet production, not radial flow, using a two-component (soft+hard) model that describes the data within uncertainties, though the p-Pb data appear to transition smoothly from p-p to A-A trends.

## Method

The author develops a two-component model (TCM) where hadron production near mid-rapidity consists of a universal soft component from nucleon dissociation and a hard component from dijet fragmentation, with the hard component scaling as n_h = α n_s² in p-p collisions (non-eikonal) but following Glauber model eikonal trends in A-A collisions. The TCM is applied to published ⟨p_t⟩ vs n_ch data from LHC for p-p at 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV, p-Pb at 5 TeV, and Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, using previously measured jet spectra, fragmentation functions, and soft-component model functions to isolate the hard-component contribution.

## Key numbers

- Soft component ⟨p_t⟩_s = 0.385 GeV/c is assumed universal for all systems and energies
- In p-p collisions, hard multiplicity scales as n_h = α n_s² with α ≈ 0.006 within Δη = 1 acceptance (from 200 GeV data)
- For 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb, the hard-component product x(ν)⟨p_t⟩_h(ν) is extracted; the NSD p-p value is x_pp⟨p_t⟩_h,pp ≈ 0.05 GeV/c
- The soft-component model uses Lév y distribution with T = 0.145 GeV and n = 12.8
- For 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb, the soft multiplicity scaling factor 1.85 ≈ ln(2760/10)/ln(200/10) predicts n_s,NSD ≈ n_pp → 4.6
- The sharp transition in jet structure for Pb-Pb occurs near ν ≈ 2 at 2.76 TeV (shifted from ν ≈ 3 at 200 GeV)
- The hard-component mean ⟨p_t⟩_h(ν) for Pb-Pb is modeled as 1.00 + 1.70{1 + tanh[(ν - ν₀)/0.5]}/2

## Context

This work extends the two-component model previously applied to 200 GeV p-p and Au-Au collisions at RHIC (Trainor, 2008; Trainor, 2010) to LHC energies, contradicting the conventional radial-flow interpretation of ⟨p_t⟩ increases in A-A collisions (blast-wave model, Schnedermann et al., 1993). The TCM successfully describes p-p and Pb-Pb data and shows that p-Pb data transition smoothly from p-p to A-A behavior, enabling quantitative separation of soft and hard contributions to hadron production across collision systems without invoking collective flow mechanisms.
