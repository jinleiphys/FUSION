# Has a thick neutron skin in ${}^{208}$Pb been ruled out?

> The Lead Radius Experiment (PREX) has provided the first model-independent evidence in favor of a neutron-rich skin in ${}^{208}$Pb. Although the error bars are large, the reported large central value of 0.33\,fm is particularly intriguing. To test whether such a thick neutron-skin in ${}^{208}$Pb is already incompatible with laboratory experiments or astrophysical observations, we employ relativistic models with neutron-skin thickness in ${}^{208}$Pb ranging from 0.16 to 0.33 fm to compute ground state properties of finite nuclei, their collective monopole and dipole response, and mass-{\sl vs}-radius relations for neutron stars. No compelling reason was found to rule out models with large neutron skins in ${}^{208}$Pb from the set of observables considered in this work.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the large central value of the neutron-skin thickness in ${}^{208}$Pb reported by PREX (0.33 fm) is already ruled out by existing laboratory experiments or astrophysical observations, and finds no compelling reason to exclude models with such thick neutron skins from the set of observables considered, though the electric dipole polarizability measurement can only be satisfied at the 2$\sigma$ level.

## Method

The authors constructed three new relativistic nuclear energy density functionals (labeled TFa, TFb, TFc) with neutron-skin thicknesses in ${}^{208}$Pb ranging from 0.25 to 0.33 fm, alongside existing models NL3, FSUGold, and IU-FSU. They computed ground-state properties (binding energies and charge radii) of ten magic and semi-magic nuclei, centroid energies of the giant monopole resonance in four nuclei, the electric dipole polarizability of ${}^{208}$Pb, and mass-radius relations for neutron stars, then compared these predictions against experimental and observational data.

## Key numbers

- PREX reported neutron-skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb: $r_{\rm skin}^{208} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}$ fm (central value with 1$\sigma$ error)
- Neutron-skin predictions for the six models: NL3 = 0.28 fm, FSU = 0.21 fm, IU-FSU = 0.16 fm, TFa = 0.25 fm, TFb = 0.30 fm, TFc = 0.33 fm
- Symmetry energy slope $L$ values: NL3 = 118.2 MeV, FSU = 60.5 MeV, IU-FSU = 47.2 MeV, TFa = 82.5 MeV, TFb = 122.5 MeV, TFc = 135.2 MeV
- The RCNP measurement of electric dipole polarizability in ${}^{208}$Pb rules out models with $r_{\rm skin}^{208} \lesssim 0.12$ fm or $r_{\rm skin}^{208} \gtrsim 0.24$ fm at the 1$\sigma$ level
- Two massive neutron stars of about 2 solar masses were reported by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the PREX result (Abrahamyan et al., 2012) by testing whether its large central value is compatible with existing data, contradicting several prior analyses that found much smaller neutron-skin values: Carbone et al. (2010) reported $r_{\rm skin}^{208} = (0.196 \pm 0.023)$ fm, Steiner and Gandolfi (2011) found $(0.171 \pm 0.007)$ fm, and Lattimer (2012) obtained $(0.175 \pm 0.014)$ fm. The paper enables future work by showing that improved measurements of neutron skins, dipole polarizabilities, and stellar radii are needed to definitively constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
