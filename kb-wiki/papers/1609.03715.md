# Hidden Local Symmetry and Beyond

> Gerry Brown was a godfather of our hidden local symmetry (HLS) for the vector meson from the birth of the theory throughout his life. The HLS is originated from very nature of the nonlinear realization of the symmetry G based on the manifold G/H, and thus is universal to any physics based on the nonlinear realization. Here I focus on the Higgs Lagrangian of the Standard Model (SM), which is shown to be equivalent to the nonlinear sigma model based on G/H= SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R/SU(2)_V with additional symmetry, the nonlinearly realized scale symmetry. Then the SM does have a dynamical gauge boson of the SU(2)_V HLS, "SM rho meson", in addition to the Higgs as a pseudo dilaton as well as the NG bosons to be absorbed into the W and Z. Based on the recent work done with S. Matsuzaki and H. Ohki, I discuss a novel possibility that the SM rho meson acquires kinetic term by the SM dynamics itself, which then stabilizes the skyrmion dormant in the SM as a viable candidate for the dark matter, what we call "Dark SM skyrmion (DSMS)".

## Key claim

The Standard Model Higgs Lagrangian, even with a 125 GeV Higgs, can be rewritten as a scale-invariant nonlinear sigma model that is gauge equivalent to a Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model containing a dynamical "SM rho meson" gauge boson; this SM rho meson can acquire a kinetic term through the SM's own quantum dynamics, stabilizing a skyrmion within the SM itself as a viable dark matter candidate, without requiring physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors rewrite the Standard Model Higgs Lagrangian in the form of a scale-invariant nonlinear sigma model based on G/H = SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R / SU(2)_V, with the Higgs boson identified as a pseudo-dilaton (pseudo Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry). They then show this Lagrangian is gauge equivalent to a scale-invariant Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian containing an auxiliary SU(2)_V gauge boson ("SM rho meson"). Using one-loop calculations from the general nonlinear sigma model, they demonstrate that this auxiliary gauge boson dynamically acquires a kinetic term through the SM dynamics itself, which then stabilizes a skyrmion configuration (the "Dark SM skyrmion" or DSMS) as a dark matter candidate.

- Higgs mass: M_φ = 125 GeV, with quartic coupling λ = (1/2)(M_φ/v)^2 ≈ 1/8, where v = 246 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Hidden Local Symmetry framework developed by Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1984, 1987) and the Vector Manifestation of chiral symmetry by Harada and Yamawaki (2000, 2003), applying it to the Standard Model Higgs sector. It builds on the observation by Fukano, Matsuzaki, and Yamawaki (2015) that the SM Higgs Lagrangian can be rewritten as a scale-invariant nonlinear sigma model, similar to the effective theory of walking technicolor by Matsuzaki and Yamawaki (2015). The dynamical generation of the HLS gauge boson kinetic term follows the one-loop calculation of Harada and Yamawaki (2003). The skyrmion stabilization mechanism is analogous to that in QCD where the HLS rho meson stabilizes the skyrmion (Igarashi, 1985; Park, 2003; Ma, 2013), but is novel in that the dark matter candidate exists within the SM itself rather than requiring new physics beyond the SM. The work enables a new dark matter phenomenology where the DSMS coupling to the Higgs is determined by the low energy theorem of scale symmetry (Carruthers, 1971), yielding very light dark matter masses (≲ 18 GeV) in contrast to typical WIMP candidates of order 100 GeV.
