# Search for the QCD Critical Point by Transverse Velocity Dependence of Anti-deuteron to Deuteron Ratio

> We propose the transverse velocity ($β_T$) dependence of the anti-deuteron to deuteron ratio as a new observable to search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions. The QCD critical point can attract the system evolution trajectory in the QCD phase diagram, which is known as focusing effect. To quantify this effect, we employ thermal model and hadronic transport model to simulate the dynamical particle emission along a hypothetical focusing trajectory near critical point. We found the focusing effect can lead to anomalous $β_T$ dependence of $\bar{p}/p$, $\bar{d}/d$ and $^3\overline{\text{He}}/^3\text{He}$ ratios. We examined the $β_T$ dependence of $\bar{p}/p$ and $\bar{d}/d$ ratios of central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC. Surprisingly, we only observe a negative slope in $β_T$ dependence of $\bar{d}/d$ ratio at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV, which indicates the trajectory evolution has passed through the critical region. In the future, we could constrain the location of the critical point and/or width of the critical region by making precise measurements on the $β_T$ dependence of $\bar{d}/d$ ratio at different energies and rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the transverse velocity (β_T) dependence of the anti-deuteron to deuteron ratio can serve as a more robust observable than the previously studied antiproton-to-proton ratio for detecting the QCD critical point focusing effect, because light nuclei production is exponentially more sensitive to the baryon chemical potential (μ_B) and is not contaminated by decay contributions.

## Method

The authors model the QCD critical point focusing effect by assuming a hypothetical system evolution trajectory in the T-μ_B plane that starts at a conjectured critical point (T=162 MeV, μ_B=360 MeV) and ends at the chemical freeze-out point for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV (T_ch=152 MeV, μ_B=188 MeV). They use the THERMUS thermal model to compute particle yields along this trajectory and the UrQMD hadronic transport model to obtain the relation between emission time and transverse velocity for protons and antiprotons. The β_T dependence of the anti-particle to particle ratios with focusing effect is obtained by convolving the time evolution of these ratios from the thermal model with the β_T-t distributions from UrQMD.

## Key numbers

* The conjectured QCD critical point is placed at (T, μ_B) = (162, 360) MeV.
* The chemical freeze-out point for central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV is (T_ch, μ_B) = (152, 188) MeV.
* The β_T dependence of the anti-deuteron to deuteron ratio shows a negative slope only at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV in central Au+Au collisions (0-10% centrality) measured by STAR, while all other energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV show positive slopes.
* The analysis covers the p_T/A range from 0.5 to 2 GeV/c, where A is the mass number of the light nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the QCD critical point focusing effect, previously applied to the antiproton-to-proton ratio by Hatta and Ikeda (2008) and Luo (2009), to anti-deuteron and heavier anti-nuclei. The authors note that the STAR experiment did not observe the predicted anomalous β_T dependence in the antiproton-to-proton ratio (Adamczyk et al., 2017), motivating the search for a more sensitive observable. The observed negative slope in the anti-deuteron to deuteron ratio at 19.6 GeV is qualitatively consistent with the non-monotonic energy dependence of net-proton cumulant ratios and neutron density fluctuations measured by STAR at the same energy, and could be further constrained by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase II (BES-II, 2019-2021).
