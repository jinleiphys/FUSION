# $Δ$-admixed neutron stars: spinodal instabilities and dUrca processes

> Within the covariant density functional theory of nuclear matter we build equations of state of $Δ$-admixed compact stars. Uncertainties in the interaction of $Δ(1232)$ resonance states with nuclear matter, due to lack of experimental data, are accounted for by varying the coupling constants to scalar and vector mesonic fields. We find that, over a wide range of the parameter space allowed by nuclear physics experiments and astrophysical observations, cold catalyzed star matter exhibits a first order phase transition which persists also at finite temperature and out of $β$-equilibrium in the neutrino-transparent matter. Compact stars featuring such a phase transition in the outer core have small radii and, implicitly, tidal deformabilities. The parameter space is identified where simultaneously $Δ$-admixed compact stars obey the astrophysical constraint on maximum mass and allow for dUrca processes, which is otherwise forbidden.

## Key claim

Cold catalyzed neutron star matter with Δ(1232) resonances exhibits a first-order phase transition over a wide range of the Δ–nucleon coupling parameter space allowed by experiments and observations, but only the low-density instability (driven by Δ⁻ onset) is compatible with the 2 M⊙ maximum mass constraint; the higher-density instabilities from other Δ isobars are ruled out by this astrophysical limit.

## Method

The author builds equations of state for Δ-admixed neutron star matter using covariant density functional theory with the DDME2 nucleonic parameterization, extending it to include the four Δ-isobar states (Δ⁻, Δ⁰, Δ⁺, Δ⁺⁺). The coupling constants of Δs to scalar (σ), vector (ω), and isovector (ρ) mesonic fields are varied over ranges constrained by experimental data (x_{σΔ} = 0.9–1.5, x_{ωΔ} = x_{σΔ} ± 0.2, x_{ρΔ} = 0.7–1.3) to account for uncertainties. Thermodynamic instabilities are identified via negative eigenvalues of the curvature matrix of the energy density with respect to baryon and lepton densities, and phase coexistence is determined by Maxwell construction in the hybrid ensemble at zero lepton chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The purely nucleonic DDME2 equation of state gives a maximum neutron star mass of M^{(N)}_{max} = 2.48 M⊙ and a radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star of R_{1.4} = 13.3 km.
- The onset density of Δ⁻ is significantly lower than those of Δ⁰, Δ⁺, and Δ⁺⁺; for the considered parameter sets, Δ⁻ onset occurs at densities n_B ≳ n_s ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Δ-driven instabilities occur over a narrow range of coupling constant differences: 0.15 ≤ x_{σΔ} − x_{ωΔ} ≤ 0.2.
- The low-density instability (driven by Δ⁻) occurs over n_s ≲ n_B ≲ 2 n_s (outer core); the high-density instability (driven by Δ⁰, Δ⁺, Δ⁺⁺) occurs over 2.5 n_s ≲ n_B ≲ 4 n_s.
- For the parameter set (x_{σΔ}=1.05, x_{ωΔ}=0.85, x_{ρΔ}=1.00), two instability domains are present; for (x_{σΔ}=1.20, x_{ωΔ}=1.00, x_{ρΔ}=1.00), only the low-density instability remains.
- The Maxwell construction causes an extra reduction of neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities by several percent compared to the case without phase transition.
- For x_{σΔ}=1.1 and x_{ωΔ} ≥ 1.1, nucleonic dUrca remains forbidden; for most other parameter sets, nucleation of Δs opens nucleonic dUrca and Δ⁻ → n + e⁻ + ν̃_e processes.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of Δ-driven spinodal instabilities by Lavagno and Pigato (2019), who found such instabilities in hypernuclear stars but suggested they are ruled out by the 2 M⊙ constraint. The present study systematically scans the Δ–nucleon coupling parameter space using the DDME2 nucleonic equation of state (Lalazissis 2005) and shows that a low-density phase transition (n_s ≲ n_B ≲ 2 n_s) is compatible with the 2 M⊙ limit, while higher-density instabilities are not. The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling, as the opening of dUrca processes by Δ⁻ nucleation may help explain thermal data from faint X-ray transients like SAX J1808.4-3658 and 1H 1905+000, which current covariant density functional models with fitted couplings have not simultaneously described (Fortin, cooling review).
