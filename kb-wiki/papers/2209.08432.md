# Weinberg's Compositeness

> Nearly 60 years ago Weinberg suggested a criterion for particle "compositeness", which has acquired new life with the discovery of new, exotic hadrons. His idea resonates with model-based intuition. I discuss the role it plays in the context of another of Weinberg's creations, the model-independent framework of effective field theories.

## Key claim

Weinberg's compositeness criterion, expressed through the parameter $Z$, is reinterpreted within effective field theory (EFT) as a measure of the relative splitting between two shallow $S$-wave poles, rather than a fundamental distinction between "elementary" and "composite" particles; the choice of degrees of freedom in an EFT is to some extent arbitrary as far as observables go, and the naive interpretation that an "elementary" particle is one associated with an explicit field is muddled by the fact one can always introduce an auxiliary field.

## Method

The paper constructs a short-range EFT for nonrelativistic two-body scattering at momenta much smaller than the particle mass and the inverse force range, using contact interactions with increasing numbers of derivatives. The author considers three cases: natural $S$-waves with no shallow poles, one fine-tuning producing a single shallow pole (as in the two-nucleon system), and two fine-tunings producing two shallow $S$-wave poles. The two-pole case is solved at leading order by resumming the $C_0$ and $C_2$ contact interactions, and is also reformulated using an auxiliary "diparticle" field to show how Weinberg's $Z$ parameter tracks the relative importance of the diparticle kinetic term.

## Key numbers

- The two-pole case requires $r_0 < 0$ for real $C_{0,2}^{(0)}$, consistent with the Wigner bound $r_0 \leq 2R(1 - R/a_0 + R^2/3a_0^2)$.
- The pole positions at LO are $\kappa_\pm = (1/|r_0|)(\sqrt{1+2|r_0|/a_0} \pm 1)$, with $\kappa_+ > \kappa_- > 0$ for $a_0 > 0$.
- Weinberg's $Z$ parameter is given by $Z = 1 - (1+2|r_0|/a_0)^{-1/2}$, with $1 > Z > 0$.
- The Weinberg relations at LO are $a_0 = [(1-Z)/(1-Z/2)] \kappa_-^{-1}$ and $r_0 = -[Z/(1-Z)] \kappa_-^{-1}$, valid up to corrections of ${\cal O}(\aleph R)$.
- The LO diparticle coupling is $g_0^{(0)} = \sqrt{(1-Z)/Z \cdot 2\kappa_-/m}$, and the LO residual mass is $\Delta^{(0)}(\Lambda) = (2\kappa_-/m)(1/Z)[(1-Z)\theta_1\Lambda - (1-Z/2)\kappa_-]$.

## Context

This paper extends Weinberg's original compositeness criterion (Weinberg, 1965) by embedding it within the modern EFT framework that Weinberg himself pioneered (Weinberg, 1978). It contrasts the two-pole case with the standard one-pole EFT for the two-nucleon system (van Kolck, 1998; Kaplan, 1998) and with the natural case where all ERE parameters are of order $R$. The work builds on a recent formulation of EFT for short-range forces with two fine-tunings (Habashi, 2020) that was focused on resonances, and extends those results to bound/virtual state pairs. The interpretation enables future work on generalizing Weinberg's criterion to resonances and clarifies the relationship between field choices and power counting in EFT.
