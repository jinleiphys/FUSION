# Fine structure of giant resonances: What can be learned

> Fine structure of giant resonances (GR) has been established in recent years as a global phenomenon across the nuclear chart and for different types of resonances. A quantitative description of the fine structure in terms of characteristic scales derived by wavelet techniques is discussed. By comparison with microscpic calculations of GR strength distributions one can extract information on the role of different decay mechanisms contributing to the width of GRs. The observed cross-section fluctuations contain information on the level density (LD) of states with a given spin and parity defined by the multipolarity of the GR.

# Fine structure of giant resonances: What can be learned

## Key claim

Fine structure of giant resonances is a global phenomenon across the nuclear chart, and wavelet analysis can extract characteristic scales that reveal the role of different decay mechanisms contributing to the giant resonance width, but the interpretation depends on comparison with microscopic calculations and the method works best when a single excitation mode dominates the cross section.

## Method

High-resolution inelastic scattering and charge-exchange reactions (primarily (p,p') and (e,e') experiments at iThemba LABS, RCNP, and the S-DALINAC) were used to measure giant resonance spectra with sufficient resolution to observe fine structure. Continuous and discrete wavelet transforms were applied to the experimental spectra to extract characteristic energy scales from the power spectrum, and these scales were compared with random-phase approximation (RPA) and second random-phase approximation (SRPA) calculations to identify the damping mechanisms. For level density extraction, an autocorrelation analysis of the background-subtracted, stationary spectrum was performed, with the autocorrelation function at zero shift related to the mean level spacing via the formalism of Jonson (1976).

## Key numbers

- The ISGQR in 208Pb studied with (e,e') reaction (Kuhn 1981) shows characteristic scales in the wavelet power spectrum that arise from coupling to low-lying vibrations, with the SRPA calculation reproducing fine structure while RPA alone shows no scales (the maximum at small scale values results from folding with experimental resolution).
- For the ISGQR in 150Nd, characteristic scales around 1 MeV are observed in both experimental and theoretical wavelet power spectra, caused by splitting between K = 0, 1, and 2 components of the resonance.
- Level density of 1+ states in 90Nb extracted from the 90Zr(3He,t) reaction data (Kalinov 2006) for excitation energies of about 5 to 9 MeV shows good agreement with backshifted Fermi gas model parameterizations (Rauscher 1997, Egidy 2005), while HF-BCS (Demetriou 2001), HFB (Goriely 2008), and two-component Fermi gas (Leblond 2006) calculations underpredict the absolute density.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of giant resonance fine structure that began with high-resolution (p,p') experiments at iThemba LABS and RCNP (Shevchenko 2004, 2009; Usman 2011, 2017) and (e,e') experiments (Kuhn 1981; von Neumann-Cosel 1999). The wavelet analysis method was established as a tool for nuclear spectra by Shevchenko (2008), and the level density extraction technique builds on the autocorrelation formalism of Jonson (1976). The results enable extraction of level densities for specific spin and parity states above particle thresholds, addressing open questions about parity dependence in shell regions (Kalinov 2007), collective enhancement factors in deformed nuclei (Ozen 2013), and spin distributions of total level densities, which cannot be obtained from neutron resonance spacings, the Oslo method (Schiller 2000), or particle emission spectra (Voinov 2014).
