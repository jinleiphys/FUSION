# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry for heavy deformed nuclei: nuclear spectra

> The systematics of experimental energy differences between the levels of the ground state band and the gamma-1 band in even-even nuclei are studied as a function of the angular momentum L, demonstrating a decrease of the energy differences with increasing L, in contrast to what is seen in vibrational, gamma-unstable, and triaxial nuclei. After a short review of the relevant predictions of several simple collective models, it is shown that this decrease is caused in the framework of the proxy-SU(3) scheme by the same three-body and/or four body operators which break the degeneracy between the ground state band and the gamma-1 band, predicting in parallel the correct form of odd-even staggering within the gamma-1 bands.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the experimental energy difference between the ground state band and the gamma-1 band decreases with increasing angular momentum in most deformed even-even nuclei, and that this decrease can be explained within the proxy-SU(3) scheme using three-body and/or four-body operators, but only in the framework of the exactly separable Davidson (ESD) model among the simple collective models considered.

## Method

The authors first review analytical predictions for the energy difference between the ground state band and the gamma-1 band in several collective models: the U(5) vibrational case, the O(6) gamma-unstable case, the triaxial rotor model (TRM), the exactly separable Davidson (ESD) model, and the Z(5)-D model. They then compile experimental energy differences from even-even nuclei across several isotopic chains, normalizing to the first member of each band. Finally, they perform preliminary fits using a Hamiltonian with a K-band splitting operator and the four-body operator Lambda within the proxy-SU(3) scheme for four deformed nuclei (162Er, 160Dy, 166Yb, 178Hf), using parameters listed in Table 1.

## Key numbers

- For the U(5) vibrational case: E(L_gamma)-E(L_g) = 2A, a constant independent of L.
- For the O(6) gamma-unstable case: E(L_gamma)-E(L_g) = A(L+4), growing with L.
- For the triaxial rotor model (TRM) with even L: E(L_gamma)-E(L_g) = 12A(L-1), growing with L.
- For the Z(5)-D model: the derivative of the energy difference with respect to L is always positive, so the difference increases with L.
- For the ESD model: the derivative of the energy difference with respect to L is always negative, so the difference decreases with L.
- Fitted parameters for 162Er: a = 1443 (x10^-2 keV), b = 408 keV, c = 440 (x10^-7 keV), d = 1258 (x10^-5 keV); rms deviation 34 keV.
- Fitted parameters for 178Hf: a = 1225 (x10^-2 keV), b = 588 keV, c = 748 (x10^-7 keV), d = 890 (x10^-5 keV); rms deviation 58 keV.
- For 162Er, the experimental ground state band energies range from 102 keV (L=2) to 4463 keV (L=20); theoretical values range from 93 keV to 4388 keV.
- For 178Hf, the experimental ground state band energies range from 93 keV (L=2) to 4837 keV (L=20); theoretical values range from 85 keV to 4795 keV.

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry framework, previously applied to deformation parameters and B(E2) ratios (Bonatsos 2017, Martinou 2017), into the domain of nuclear spectra. It contrasts with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) where the ground state band and gamma-1 band belong to different irreducible representations and require higher-order terms only for triaxial shapes (Chen, Moreau). The paper shows that the decreasing energy difference between the gamma-1 band and ground state band is a signature of prolate deformed nuclei, in contrast to the increasing difference seen in vibrational, gamma-unstable (O(6) symmetry), and triaxial nuclei. The results enable future comparisons with the Vector Boson Model (Raychev, Minkov) and with the work of Jolos and von Brentano on different mass coefficients for the ground state and gamma-1 bands, as well as extension to B(E2) transition rates.
