# Neural network reconstruction of the dense matter equation of state from neutron star observables

> The Equation of State (EoS) of strongly interacting cold and hot ultra-dense QCD matter remains a major challenge in the field of nuclear astrophysics. With the advancements in measurements of neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities, from electromagnetic and gravitational wave observations, neutron stars play an important role in constraining the ultra-dense QCD matter EoS. In this work, we present a novel method that exploits deep learning techniques to reconstruct the neutron star EoS from mass-radius (M-R) observations. We employ neural networks (NNs) to represent the EoS in a model-independent way, within the range of $\sim$1-7 times the nuclear saturation density. The unsupervised Automatic Differentiation (AD) framework is implemented to optimize the EoS, so as to yield through TOV equations, an M-R curve that best fits the observations. We demonstrate that this method works by rebuilding the EoS on mock data, i.e., mass-radius pairs derived from a randomly generated polytropic EoS. The reconstructed EoS fits the mock data with reasonable accuracy, using just 11 mock M-R pairs observations, close to the current number of actual observations.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that an unsupervised automatic differentiation framework using two coupled neural networks can reconstruct the dense matter equation of state from neutron star mass-radius observations in a model-independent way, but only within the density range of approximately 1-7 times nuclear saturation density and with the caveat that the method was tested on mock data rather than actual observations.

## Method
The authors trained a WaveNet neural network (the "TOV-Solver Network") on 228,569 piecewise polytropic equations of state to emulate the numerical solution of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, mapping an input EoS to its corresponding mass-radius curve. They then coupled this frozen network to a second neural network (the "EoS Network") that represents the EoS as a function of density, and optimized the EoS Network parameters via automatic differentiation to minimize the chi-squared difference between the predicted mass-radius curve and mock observational data. The EoS Network used three 1D convolutional layers with non-negative weights to enforce monotonicity, a necessary condition for any physical EoS.

## Key numbers
- The EoS was reconstructed within the density range of approximately 1-7 times nuclear saturation density (rho_sat ~ 2x10^14 g cm^-3)
- The TOV-Solver Network achieved a coefficient of determination R^2 ~ 99.9% in reproducing mass-radius curves from unseen test EoSs
- Only 11 mock mass-radius pairs were used for reconstruction, close to the current number of actual observations
- The EoS Network used a total of 4353 parameters for defining P_theta(rho)
- 500 mock data samples were drawn from normal distributions with 10% relative standard deviation (sigma_M = 0.1M, sigma_R = 0.1R) for uncertainty quantification
- The training dataset consisted of 94,462 EoSs for PS, 61,273 for SLy, and 72,834 for DD2 low-density regimes, after excluding those failing to support a 1.9 solar mass neutron star

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian inference approaches for EoS reconstruction (Steiner 2010, Raithel 2017, Traversi 2020) and supervised machine learning methods (Fujimoto 2018, Morawski 2020, Ferreira and Providencia 2021, Krastev 2021) by introducing the first unsupervised learning algorithm for this inverse problem. The method enables model-independent EoS reconstruction from limited neutron star observations without requiring parametric priors, and the differentiable TOV-Solver Network is approximately 10^6 times faster than conventional numerical integration methods, which could facilitate rapid analysis of the increasing observational data expected from NICER and LIGO-Virgo.
