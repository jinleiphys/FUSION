# Shape Coexistence in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach

> The phenomenon of shape coexistence is studied in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework. Standard relativistic mean-field effective interactions do not reproduce the ground state properties of neutron-deficient Pt-Hg-Pb isotopes. It is shown that, in order to consistently describe binding energies, radii and ground state deformations of these nuclei, effective interactions have to be constructed which take into account the sizes of spherical shell gaps.

## Key claim

Standard relativistic mean-field effective interactions (such as NL3) fail to reproduce the ground state shapes of neutron-deficient Hg and Pb isotopes, but it is possible to construct an effective interaction (NL-SC) that consistently describes binding energies, charge radii, and ground state deformations in this mass region if the sizes of spherical shell gaps in the single-nucleon spectra are taken into account during parameter adjustment.

## Method

The authors employed the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, which provides a unified description of mean-field and pairing correlations, solving the Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov equations and Klein-Gordon equations for meson fields by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential. In the particle-hole channel they used the standard NL3 interaction and a newly constructed NL-SC interaction, while pairing correlations were described by the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction. Constrained calculations of binding energy curves as functions of quadrupole deformation were performed for even-A Hg and Pb isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The NL1 effective interaction overbinds neutron-deficient Hg and Pb nuclei by 10-12 MeV compared to empirical binding energies.
- For Hg isotopes, the oblate minimum is located at beta_2 approximately -0.15 and the prolate minimum at beta_2 approximately 0.3, in agreement with values from Nazarewicz (1993) using the Nilsson-Strutinsky approach.
- The NL3 interaction predicts prolate ground states for Hg isotopes with A = 180, 182, 184, 186, whereas experimentally all Hg isotopes with A greater than or equal to 178 have oblate ground states with the prolate minimum estimated at 300-800 keV above the ground state.
- The NL-SC interaction reproduces the relative positions of prolate and oblate minima in Hg isotopes, with the difference between binding energies of prolate and oblate states agreeing with empirical data from Carpenter (1997).
- For Pb isotopes, NL3 predicts oblate ground states for A = 188-194, contradicting experimental data; only from A = 196 do calculated Pb ground states become spherical.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pt-Hg-Pb isotopes by Yoshida (1994, 1997), Patra (1994), and Takigawa (1996), which used the NL1 and NL-SH interactions and often produced results at variance with experimental data as pointed out by Heyde (1996). The analysis also responds to the study by Bender (2001) which found that NL3 predicted deformed ground states for several Pb isotopes. The paper demonstrates that effective interactions can be tailored for specific mass regions by incorporating spherical shell gap sizes, enabling more reliable extrapolation of mean-field models to exotic nuclei far from stability, though the authors caution that NL-SC is not a general-purpose interaction and does not perform as well as NL3 for light nuclei.
