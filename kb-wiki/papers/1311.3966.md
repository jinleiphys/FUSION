# AdS/CFT and applications

> In this talk I review some selected applications of the AdS/CFT correspondence to the study of nonequillibrium dynamics of strongly coupled plasma. I briefly describe the AdS/CFT methods and five recent studies dealing with various features of plasma evolution, in particular the transition to hydrodynamics, thermalization, very high order hydrodynamics, the dynamics of shock wave collisions and preequilibrium radial flow.

## Key claim

The AdS/CFT correspondence provides a framework for studying nonequilibrium dynamics of strongly coupled plasma that does not presuppose hydrodynamics and is applicable even in very out-of-equilibrium contexts, but the results are obtained for the supersymmetric N=4 gauge theory rather than QCD, so they serve as qualitative insight and a point of reference rather than direct predictions for real-world quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author reviews five selected recent studies that use numerical relativity methods to solve 5D Einstein's equations with negative cosmological constant, where the evolving plasma system is described through an effective 5D geometry. Physical observables such as the energy-momentum tensor are extracted from the asymptotic behaviour of the geometry near the boundary at z=0, and local entropy density is obtained from the area element of an apparent horizon transported to the boundary through a null geodesic.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density for strongly coupled gauge theories is universal and equal to 1/4π
- For boost-invariant plasma from various initial conditions, viscous hydrodynamics works to very high accuracy as soon as w > 0.7, where w = T_eff * τ
- At the transition to hydrodynamics, the plasma has a sizable pressure anisotropy Δp_L ≡ 1 - p_L/(ε/3) ∼ 0.7
- In the study of boost-invariant plasma with radial flow, good agreement with hydrodynamics was established from τ = 0.35 fm, with initial conditions set at τ_ini = 0.12 fm and initial effective temperature adapted to a realistic value at τ = 0.6 fm
- In the hybrid treatment of central nuclear collisions, the AdS/CFT input included significant nontrivial radial flow already at the beginning of the applicability of hydrodynamics

## Context

This talk reviews work that extends the initial calculation of shear viscosity for strongly coupled N=4 plasma by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) and the realization of the universal viscosity-to-entropy ratio by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005). The studies described build on the first numerical work on applying numerical methods in this context by Chesler and Yaffe (2009) dealing with isotropization of spatially uniform plasma. The findings enable further investigation of preequilibrium radial flow and its experimental consequences, as demonstrated in the hybrid AdS/CFT plus hydro plus cascade model by van der Schee, Romatschke, and Pratt (2013).
