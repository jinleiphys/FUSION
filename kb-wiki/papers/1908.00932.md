# Nuclear Theory and Event Generators for Charge-Changing Neutrino Reactions

> Semi-inclusive $CCν$ cross sections based on factorized cross sections are studied for a selection of spectral function models with the objective of facilitating the choice of models for use as input into event generators. The basic formalism for such cross sections is presented along with an introduction to constructing spectral functions for simple models based on the independent particle shell model (IPSM), the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFG) and a local density approximation (LDA) based on the RFG. Spectral functions for these models are shown for $^{16}$O along with a more sophisticated model which includes nucleon-nucleon interactions \cite{ALVAREZRUSO20181}. Inclusive and semi-inclusive cross sections are calculated for these models. Although the inclusive cross sections are all of similar size and shape, the semi-inclusive cross sections are subtantially different depending upon whether the spectral functions contain some features associated with the nuclear shell model or are based on the RFG and LDA models. Calculations of average values and standard deviations of the initial neutrino energy using the semi-inclusive cross section for the various models are presented and indicate that there may be simple kinematical descriptions of the average neutrino energy which is common to all of these models.

## Key claim

While inclusive cross sections for charge-changing neutrino reactions on oxygen are similar across all tested nuclear models (agreeing to about 10-20%), the semi-inclusive cross sections differ substantially depending on whether the spectral functions contain shell-model features or are based on the relativistic Fermi gas and local density approximation models, indicating that event generators must use more sophisticated models for semi-inclusive reactions than for inclusive ones.

## Method

The authors computed semi-inclusive charged-current neutrino cross sections using a factorized plane-wave impulse approximation formalism, where the cross section separates into a single-nucleon weak interaction part and a nuclear spectral function. They constructed spectral functions for oxygen-16 using four models: an independent particle shell model with relativistic mean-field wave functions (IPSM-RMF), a relativistic Fermi gas model (RFG) with Fermi momentum 230 MeV/c, a local density approximation (LDA) based on the RFG, and a state-of-the-art spectral function from the Rome group that includes nucleon-nucleon correlations. Inclusive and semi-inclusive cross sections were calculated for each model, and average values and standard deviations of the reconstructed initial neutrino energy were extracted from the semi-inclusive results.

## Key numbers

- The RFG spectral function uses a Fermi momentum of \(k_F = 230\) MeV/c for oxygen-16, a reasonable choice according to Megias (2017).
- Inclusive cross section predictions from dramatically different models agree to about 10-20%, as noted in the introduction referencing Alvarez-Ruso (2018).
- The semi-inclusive cross section depends on five independent kinematic variables: the magnitudes of the muon momentum \(k'\) and nucleon momentum \(p_N\), the muon angle relative to the beam, the nucleon polar angle \(\theta_N^L\), and the nucleon azimuthal angle \(\phi_N^L\).
- The missing momentum \(p_m\) is computed from the kinematic variables via an expression involving \(k_0\), \(k'\), \(p_N\), \(\theta_l\), \(\theta_N^L\), and \(\phi_N^L\).

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino-nucleus reactions from inclusive cross sections, which have been the focus of most theoretical activity, to the semi-inclusive reactions required by modern event generators. It directly compares the IPSM-RMF model using wave functions from Horowitz and Serot (1981), the RFG model, the LDA extension, and the Rome spectral function from Benhar et al. (1994, 2005) which incorporates data from \((e,e'p)\) measurements and nuclear matter calculations. The results enable event generator developers to select appropriate nuclear models for semi-inclusive event classes, such as those involving muon detection together with proton detection in liquid argon TPCs, and show that simple models adequate for inclusive cross sections are insufficient for semi-inclusive predictions.
