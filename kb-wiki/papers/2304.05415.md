# $ν$DoBe -- A Python Tool for Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We present $ν$DoBe, a Python tool for the computation of neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) rates in terms of lepton-number-violating operators in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT). The tool can be used for automated calculations of $0νββ$ rates, electron spectra and angular correlations for all isotopes of experimental interest, for lepton-number-violating operators up to and including dimension 9. The tool takes care of renormalization-group running to lower energies and provides the matching to the low-energy effective field theory and, at lower scales, to a chiral effective field theory description of $0νββ$ rates. The user can specify different sets of nuclear matrix elements from various many-body methods and hadronic low-energy constants. The tool can be used to quickly generate analytical and numerical expressions for $0νββ$ rates and to generate a large variety of plots. In this work, we provide examples of possible use along with a detailed code documentation. The code can be accessed through: GitHub: https://github.com/OScholer/nudobe Online User-Interface: https://nudobe.streamlit.app

## Key claim

νDoBe automates the computation of neutrinoless double beta decay rates from lepton-number-violating operators in SMEFT up to dimension 9, but it cannot yet handle scenarios involving light sterile neutrinos, which require a modified framework.

## Method

The tool implements an end-to-end effective field theory chain: SMEFT operators at a high scale Λ are evolved via renormalization-group running to the electroweak scale, matched to the low-energy EFT (LEFT), then further evolved to the GeV scale and matched to chiral EFT for hadronic and nuclear physics. The user specifies Wilson coefficients at either the SMEFT or LEFT level, chooses nuclear matrix elements from various many-body methods, and the tool computes half-lives, electron spectra, and angular correlations for all isotopes of experimental interest.

## Key numbers

- The KamLAND-Zen lower limit on the half-life in ¹³⁶Xe is 2.3 × 10²⁶ yr.
- Limits on the effective Majorana mass m_ββ from the KamLAND-Zen limit, without short-range contributions, range from 36 meV to 146 meV across different nuclear matrix element sets.
- Including short-range contributions, the limits on m_ββ tighten to a range of 25 meV to 68 meV.
- Limits on higher-dimensional LEFT operators at the matching scale m_W range from approximately 10⁻¹⁰ (for C_S^(6)) to 10⁻⁵ (for C_V^(7), C_S1^(9), C_V^(9), and C_Ṽ^(9)).
- These operator limits translate to new physics scales from approximately 2 TeV to 20 PeV for LEFT operators, and approximately 1 TeV to 400 TeV for SMEFT operators.

## Context

This work extends the EFT framework for 0νββ developed by Cirigliano et al. (2017, 2018) by providing an automated computational tool. It enables rapid analysis of any high-scale lepton-number-violating model, including the minimal left-right symmetric model (mLRSM) studied by Cirigliano et al. (2018) and Graf et al. (2022), and allows systematic comparison of different nuclear matrix element calculations (QRPA, EDF, IBM2, shell model) and assessment of short-range contributions identified by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019). The tool facilitates interpretation of experimental limits from KamLAND-Zen (2022) and other experiments, and enables generation of limits on effective Majorana masses and higher-dimensional operators.
