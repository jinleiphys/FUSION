# delta-pairing forces and collective pairing vibrations

> The collective pairing hamiltonian is obtained in the framework of the generator coordinate method in the gaussian overlap approximation with a slightly modified BCS function used as a generator function. The collective variable alpha, measuring the monopole moment of the pairing field, and the gauge transformation angle phi are chosen as generator coordinates. The vibrational ground states are calculated by diagonalisation of the collective pairing hamiltonian in the harmonic oscillator basis.

# δ-pairing forces and collective pairing vibrations

## Key claim

The paper derives a collective pairing Hamiltonian using the Generator Coordinate Method with Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GCM+GOA) for a δ-pairing interaction, rather than the monopole pairing interaction used in prior work. The collective coordinate α = Σ u_k v_k is shown to be a natural choice for this interaction, and the resulting ground state vibrational energies are approximately 2.5 MeV for protons and 4.5 MeV for neutrons in rare earth nuclei, though these states are typically higher than two-quasiparticle excitations and therefore only ground state correlations are relevant.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian consisting of a single-particle Nilsson potential plus a δ-pairing interaction, with separate strengths for protons (240 MeV fm³) and neutrons (230 MeV fm³). They construct a collective pairing Hamiltonian using the GCM+GOA framework with the BCS-like state |αφ⟩ as the generator function, where α = Σ u_k v_k is the monopole moment of the pairing field and φ is the gauge angle. The collective Schrödinger equation is solved by diagonalization in a harmonic oscillator basis of up to 19 shells, after transforming to a coordinate x in which the mass parameter is approximately constant.

## Key numbers

- Proton pairing strength V₀ = 240 MeV fm³; neutron pairing strength V₀ = 230 MeV fm³
- First excited collective pairing vibrational states appear at energies close to 2.5 MeV for protons and 4.5 MeV for neutrons in even-even rare earth nuclei
- The ratio of the cranking mass to the GCM mass for the α component is close to 2/3 near the minimum of the GCM potential
- For ¹⁴⁸Ce protons, the equilibrium α value is 9.3 and the most probable α value is 7.6, with the shift implying an increase of M_{αα} by a factor of 1.5 on average
- The particle number projected energy is about 2 MeV deeper than the BCS energy minimum

## Context

This work extends the GCM+GOA treatment of collective pairing vibrations from the monopole pairing case studied by Pomorski (2001) and collaborators to the δ-pairing interaction used by Quentin (1990), Baran (1995), and Meng (1998). The collective coordinate α follows the choice of Bes (1965) and Meyer (1992) as a natural variable for this interaction. The results enable coupling of pairing vibrations with shape degrees of freedom in collective Bohr model calculations, as done by Zajac (2003), which improves agreement with experimental data for quadrupole vibrations.
