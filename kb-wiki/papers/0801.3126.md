# Two Photon Exchange Contributions to Elastic e + p --> e + p Process in a Nonlocal Field Formalism

> We compute the two photon exchange contributions to elastic scattering of polarized electrons from target protons. We use a nonlocal field theory formalism for this calculation. The formalism maintains gauge invariance and provides a systematic procedure for making this calculation. The results depend on one unknown parameter \bar b. We compute the two photon exchange correction to the ratio of electric to magnetic form factors extracted using the polarization transfer experiments. The correction is found to be small if \bar b ~ 1. However for larger values of \bar b>3, the correction can be quite significant. The correction to the polarization transfer results goes in the right direction to explain their difference with the ratio measured by Rosenbluth separation method. We find that the difference between the two experimental results can be explained for a wide range of values of the parameter \bar b. We also find that the corrections due to two photon exchange depend on the photon longitudinal polarization epsilon. Hence we predict an epsilon dependence of the form factor ratio extracted using the polarization transfer technique. Finally we obtain a limit on \bar b by requiring that the non-linearity in epsilon dependence of the unpolarized reduced cross section is within experimental errors.

# Two Photon Exchange Contributions to Elastic e + p → e + p Process in a Nonlocal Field Formalism

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange correction to the ratio of electric to magnetic form factors extracted from polarization transfer experiments is small when the unknown parameter $\bar b \sim 1$, but becomes quite significant for $\bar b > 3$, and the difference between polarization transfer and Rosenbluth separation results can be explained for a wide range of $\bar b$ values.

## Method

The authors compute the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons from protons using a nonlocal field theory Lagrangian truncated to dimension-five operators, which maintains gauge invariance and depends on one free parameter $\bar b$. The calculation includes box and cross-box diagrams plus an additional diagram proportional to $\bar b^2$, with infrared divergences removed by fitting the $\mu^2$ dependence of the amplitudes and extrapolating to $\mu^2 = 0$. The form factors $G_E$ and $G_M$ are modeled using a multi-pole fit with complex parameters (Model I from Jain (2007)).

## Key numbers

- The two-sigma limit on the parameter $\bar b$ is $|\bar b| < 6.91$, obtained from the nonlinearity in $\varepsilon$ dependence of the unpolarized reduced cross section at $Q^2 = 2.64$ GeV$^2$.
- The best-fit value of the nonlinearity parameter $\mathcal{P}_2$ (scaled by $10^4$) at $Q^2 = 2.64$ GeV$^2$ is $2.02$, with a two-sigma limit of $-9.64$.
- The fitting parameter $\mathcal{R}^{\bar b}_2$ at $Q^2 = 2.64$ GeV$^2$ is $-11.73 \times 10^{-6}$, and $\mathcal{R}^{\rm BCB}_2$ is $-4.04 \times 10^{-4}$.
- The two-photon exchange corrections are computed for $q^2$ values of $-2.47$, $-2.97$, $-3.50$, $-3.97$, $-4.75$, and $-5.54$ GeV$^2$.
- The polarization transfer data used for comparison are in the kinematic regime with $\varepsilon$ between approximately 0.7 and 0.8 for $3.5 \ge Q^2 \ge 2.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal field formalism developed by Jain (2007) to compute two-photon exchange corrections to polarization transfer experiments, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between JLAB polarization transfer measurements (Jones, Gayou, Punjabi) and SLAC Rosenbluth separation experiments (Walker, Andivahis). The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted $\varepsilon$ dependence of the form factor ratio extracted from polarization transfer, which could determine the parameter $\bar b$. The nonlinearity of the reduced cross section has also been computed by Carlson using the GPD formalism.
