# Correlations in neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We present a detailed study of charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and of the influence of correlations on one- and two-nucleon knockout processes. The quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections, including the influence of long-range correlations, are evaluated within a continuum random phase approximation approach. The short-range correlation formalism is implemented in the impulse approximation by shifting the complexity induced by the correlations from the wave functions to the operators. The model is validated by confronting $(e,e^\prime)$ cross-section predictions with electron scattering data in the kinematic region where the quasielastic channel is expected to dominate. Further, the $^{12}$C$(ν,μ^-)$ experiments are studied. Double differential cross sections relevant for neutrino-oscillation $^{12}$C$(ν,μ^-)$ cross sections, accounting for long- and short-range correlations in the one-particle emission channel and short-range correlations in the two-particle two-hole channel, are presented for kinematics relevant for recent neutrino-nucleus scattering measurements.

## Key claim

Long-range correlations (via continuum random phase approximation) and short-range correlations (via a correlation operator method) both reduce the quasielastic peak in charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering on carbon-12, but the paper's validation relies on comparing electron-scattering predictions with data in the kinematic region where the quasielastic channel is expected to dominate, acknowledging that measurements include contributions from Delta excitations and two-particle knockout not yet fully captured.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections using a non-relativistic Hartree-Fock mean-field model with a Skyrme SkE2 interaction. Long-range correlations are incorporated through a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) Green's function formalism, while short-range correlations are treated by applying a many-body correlation operator (central and tensor parts) to uncorrelated wave functions, shifting the complexity into effective two-body currents. The model is validated against double-differential electron-scattering data for carbon-12, oxygen-16, and calcium-40, then applied to carbon-12 neutrino-muon scattering at kinematics relevant for oscillation experiments.

## Key numbers

- Double differential electron-scattering cross sections on carbon-12, oxygen-16, and calcium-40 are compared with data from Barreau (1983), O'Connell (1984), and others; the CRPA and HF results are comparable for Q^2 values above 0.25 (GeV/c)^2, while differences are sizable for Q^2 <= 0.25 (GeV/c)^2.
- Exclusive carbon-12(nu_mu,mu- N_a N_b) cross sections are shown at E_nu_mu = 750 MeV, E_mu = 550 MeV, theta_mu = 15 degrees, and T_p = 50 MeV, with outgoing nucleons in the lepton scattering plane.
- Double differential carbon-12(nu_mu,mu-) cross sections are presented for three kinematics relevant for accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments, showing that both long- and short-range correlations decrease the cross section at the quasielastic peak.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior CRPA and short-range correlation models, previously applied to exclusive electron scattering (Ryckebusch 1988, 1989), low-energy and supernova neutrino processes (Jachowicz 1998, 2002, 2004), and inclusive electroweak scattering at intermediate energies (Pandey 2013, 2014). The short-range correlation formalism builds on a model developed for exclusive and semi-exclusive electron-nucleus scattering (VanderSluys 1995, Ryckebusch 1997, Janssen 1999, Vanhalst 2014). The results enable improved predictions for neutrino-oscillation experiments by providing a framework that includes both one- and two-nucleon knockout channels, and the exclusive cross section predictions may help interpret events like the "hammer events" seen by the ArgoNeuT collaboration (Acciarri 2014).
