# Rapidity distribution of protons from the potential version of UrQMD model and the traditional coalescence afterburner

> Rapidity distributions of both E895 proton data at AGS energies and NA49 net proton data at SPS energies can be described reasonably well with a potential version of the UrQMD in which mean-field potentials for both pre-formed hadrons and confined baryons are considered, with the help of a traditional coalescence afterburner in which one parameter set for both relative distance $R_0$ and relative momentum $P_0$, (3.8 fm, 0.3 GeV$/$c), is used. Because of the large cancellation between the expansion in $R_0$ and the shrinkage in $P_0$ through the Lorentz transformation, the relativistic effect in clusters has little effect on the rapidity distribution of free (net) protons. Using a Woods-Saxon-like function instead of a pure logarithmic function as seen by FOPI collaboration at SIS energies, one can fit well both the data at SIS energies and the UrQMD calculation results at AGS and SPS energies. Further, it is found that for central Au+Au or Pb+Pb collisions at top SIS, SPS and RHIC energies, the proton fractions in clusters are about 33$\%$, 10$\%$, and 0.7$\%$, respectively.

## Key claim

The potential version of UrQMD (UrQMD/M) combined with a traditional coalescence afterburner using a single parameter set (R0=3.8 fm, P0=0.3 GeV/c) can describe rapidity distributions of E895 proton data at AGS energies and NA49 net proton data at SPS energies reasonably well, but discrepancies remain at mid-rapidities at SPS energies, leaving room for more systematic description of the early-stage dynamical evolution.

## Method

The authors used a mean-field potential version of the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD/M) model that includes potentials for both pre-formed hadrons and confined baryons, coupled with a traditional phase-space coalescence afterburner. The coalescence model groups baryons into clusters when their relative distance is less than R0=3.8 fm and relative momentum is less than P0=0.3 GeV/c, with the transport calculation stopped at t_cut=50 fm/c and over 10,000 events per reaction. The relativistic effect of Lorentz transformation on both relative distance and relative momentum in the coalescence afterburner was examined and found to largely cancel.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence parameter set: R0 = 3.8 fm, P0 = 0.3 GeV/c, used for all energies
- For central Au+Au or Pb+Pb collisions at top SIS energies: proton fraction in clusters is about 33%
- For central Au+Au or Pb+Pb collisions at top SPS energies: proton fraction in clusters is about 10%
- For central Au+Au or Pb+Pb collisions at top RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV): proton fraction in clusters is about 0.7%
- The Woods-Saxon-like fit to the clustered proton fraction excitation function achieved an adjusted R-squared of 0.99
- At SIS energies, the FOPI data showed a linear dependence of clustered proton fraction on logarithmic beam energy from 0.2 to 1.5 GeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD calculations (Yuan 2010) that failed to describe free proton yields at AGS and SPS energies using the cascade mode (UrQMD/C). It builds on the FOPI collaboration finding (Reisdorf 2010) that at SIS energies the clustered proton fraction follows a logarithmic function of beam energy, showing that this extrapolation fails at higher energies and proposing a Woods-Saxon-like function instead. The results enable improved modeling of cluster formation across a wide energy range from SIS to RHIC, with the remaining discrepancies at SPS mid-rapidity pointing to needed refinements in the equation of state stiffness (Li 2007, Li 2008, Xu 2013) and cross-section modifications (AbdelWaged 2004, Steinheimer 2015).
