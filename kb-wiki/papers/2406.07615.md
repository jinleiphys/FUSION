# Jet quenching in the glasma phase: medium-induced radiation

> Inspired by the recent considerations of parton momentum broadening in the glasma phase, we study the medium-induced soft gluon radiation of jet partons at early times in heavy-ion collisions. The glasma state is assumed to be comprised of independent color domains with homogenous longitudinal fields that vary event by event, and we further complete this model with an event-averaging procedure accounting for the finite correlation length. Using this description, we evaluate the rate of medium-induced radiation from an energetic parton at midrapidity in the glasma phase. We mainly focus on SU(2) color fields for simplicity, also referring to the U(1) case and comparing with the BDMPS-Z rate to gain further insight. Our results show that there is an intricate interplay of the synchrotron-like radiation in a single color domain with the destructive interference between different color domains, after the medium averaging is performed. Thus, we find that the emission rate is sensitive to the matter structure, decreasing for a glasma state populated by smaller color domains, i.e. for a glasma with a larger characteristic saturation scale. Our approach can be applied to more realistic backgrounds, and sets the stage for the modelling of jet evolution in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that medium-induced gluon radiation in the glasma phase is sensitive to the spatial structure of the color fields, with the emission rate decreasing for smaller color domains (i.e., larger saturation scale \(Q_s\)), but this conclusion depends on the specific model of SU(2) color fields and the event-averaging procedure used to account for finite correlation length.

## Method

The authors model the glasma as a collection of independent color domains of fixed size \(\ell \sim 1/Q_s\), each containing a constant chromoelectric field aligned with the beam axis, with fields varying event-by-event according to a Gaussian distribution. They compute the rate of soft medium-induced gluon radiation from an energetic parton at midrapidity by evaluating the in-medium emission kernel in the soft-gluon limit, focusing primarily on SU(2) color fields for simplicity while also comparing with the U(1) case and the BDMPS-Z rate. The calculation involves path integrals for the emitted gluon propagator and Wilson lines accounting for color precession, followed by event averaging over the Gaussian-distributed fields.

## Key numbers

* The jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) in the SU(2) model, after averaging over initial position within a flux tube, is \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle_{\text{SU(2)},\,z_{in}} = \frac{3E_0^2}{16\pi^2}\ell\), where \(E_0\) is the width of the Gaussian field distribution and \(\ell\) is the domain size.
* The jet quenching parameter in the SU(3) model, after averaging over initial position, is \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle_{\text{SU(3)},\,z_{in}} = \frac{E_0^2}{2\pi^2}\ell\).
* For comparison, the jet quenching parameter in the QGP phase at \(T = 200\,\mathrm{MeV}\) is \(\hat{q} \simeq 0.12\, \mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\), while glasma simulations suggest \(\hat{q} \geq 5 \mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\) during the first \(0.3\, \mathrm{fm}/c\) of evolution (citing Avramescu (2023)).

## Context

This work extends recent analytical studies of parton momentum broadening in the glasma phase (Carrington (2020, 2021, 2022)) and numerical simulations of \(\hat{q}\) in the glasma (Ipp (2020), Avramescu (2023)) by computing the medium-induced radiation rate, which had only been addressed in a few earlier works assuming constant classical fields (Shuryak (2002), Zakharov (2008)) or specific time-varying fields without event averaging (Aurenche (2012)). The results enable future modeling of jet evolution that includes the early glasma stage of heavy-ion collisions, complementing the well-studied BDMPS-Z framework for the quark-gluon plasma phase.
