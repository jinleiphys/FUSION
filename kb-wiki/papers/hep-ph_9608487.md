# Symmetrization Selection Rules

> We introduce strong and electromagnetic interaction selection rules for the two-body decay and production of exotic J^{PC} = 0^{+-}, 1^{-+}, 2^{+-}, 3^{-+} hybrid mesons, four-quark states and glueballs. The rules arise from symmetrization in states. Examples include various decays to eta' eta, eta pi, eta' pi and pi^{\pm} pi^0. The symmetrization rules can discriminate between hybrid and four-quark interpretations of a 1^{-+} signal.

## Key claim

The paper derives selection rules for the strong and electromagnetic two-body decay and production of exotic mesons (hybrids, four-quark states, and glueballs) that arise purely from symmetrization of identical final-state particles, without assuming SU(3) flavor symmetry and in some cases without assuming isospin symmetry. The rules can discriminate between hybrid and four-quark interpretations of a 1⁻⁺ signal, but the selection rules are broken if the internal structure of the two final-state mesons differs.

## Method

The author considers fully relativistic two-body decay and production processes A → BC in the rest frame of A, where B and C are J=0 mesons (hybrid or conventional) identical in all respects except possibly flavor and momentum. The amplitude is written as a sum of two diagrams corresponding to the two ways a quark from A can end up in B or C; under exchange of the labels B ↔ C (equivalent to momentum reversal), the amplitude must transform with a factor f = ±1. When f = (-1)^(L+1) where L is the partial wave between B and C, the amplitude vanishes because analyticity requires it to transform as (-1)^L under momentum reversal. The conditions for f = (-1)^(L+1) are analyzed for different decay topologies (connected hybrid, connected four-quark, and non-connected) and for cases with indistinguishable flavors or with isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The selection rules apply to exotic J^PC = 0⁺⁻, 1⁻⁺, 2⁺⁻, 3⁻⁺, ... states, which are not found in the quark model.
- QCD sum rule calculations cited predict a tiny ηπ mode for hybrid 1⁻⁺ of ∼ 0.3 MeV (versus 600 MeV for ρπ and 300 MeV for K*K).
- QCD sum rule calculations cited predict η'π of 4 MeV or 3 MeV (versus ρπ of 270 MeV and K*K of 8 MeV).
- The candidate state ρ̂(1405) has width 180 ± 20 MeV, possibly decaying to ηπ but absent in ρπ.

## Context

This work extends selection rules previously noted using the Wigner-Eckart theorem for SU(3) flavor symmetry (Meshkov, 1960s) and rules recognized for hybrid 1⁻⁺ → ηπ, η'π within isospin symmetry (Pene, 1990s; Lipkin, 1990s). The paper provides a classification of all possible rules of this kind, going beyond SU(3) reduction to isospin. The rules enable discrimination between hybrid and four-quark interpretations of exotic signals: a wide 1⁻⁺ state could be interpreted as a four-quark state, while measurement of ηπ, η'π decay samples the strength of the s\bar{s} component in a u,d four-quark state. The paper notes that a subset of the rules has been shown to arise in quenched Euclidean QCD (Pene, 1990s) and suggests lattice gauge theory and Dyson-Schwinger techniques as future tests.
