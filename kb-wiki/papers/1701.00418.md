# Identifying the QCD Phase Transitions via the Gravitational Wave Frequency

> We investigate the nonradial oscillations of newly born neutron stars (NSs) and strange quark stars (SQSs). This is done with the relativistic nuclear field theory with hyperon degrees of freedom employed to describe the equation of state for the stellar matter in NSs, and with both the MIT bag model and the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model adopted to construct the configurations of the SQSs. We find that the gravitational-mode ($g$-mode) eigenfrequencies of newly born SQSs are about one order of magnitude lower than those of NSs, which is independent of models implemented to describe the equation of state for the strange quark matter. Meanwhile the eigenfrequencies of the other modes of nonradial oscillations, e.g., fundamental ($f$)- and pressure ($p$)-modes, are much larger than those of the $g$-mode. In the light of the first direct observation of gravitational waves, it is promising to employ the gravitational waves to identify the QCD phase transition in high density strong interaction matter.

## Key claim

The gravitational-mode (g-mode) eigenfrequencies of newly born strange quark stars (SQSs) are about one order of magnitude lower than those of neutron stars (NSs), a result that is independent of which model (MIT bag model or Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) is used to describe the strange quark matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors solved the Newtonian equations for nonradial stellar oscillations using the Cowling approximation (neglecting perturbations of the gravitational potential) for newly born neutron stars and strange quark stars. For neutron stars, the equation of state was constructed using relativistic mean field theory with baryon octet degrees of freedom (including hyperons) at finite temperature, assuming beta equilibrium and fixed entropy per baryon and lepton fraction. For strange quark stars, the MIT bag model (with bag constant B^{1/4}=154.5 MeV, m_u=m_d=0, m_s=150 MeV) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model were used separately to describe the strange quark matter equation of state.

## Key numbers

- g-mode eigenfrequencies of newly born SQSs are about one order of magnitude lower than those of NSs (the paper does not provide exact numerical values for the frequencies in the truncated text)
- MIT bag model parameters: m_u = m_d = 0, m_s = 150 MeV, bag constant B^{1/4} = 154.5 MeV
- The MIT bag model produces a mass per baryon of 928 MeV for SQS matter, slightly smaller than 931 MeV in the stablest nucleus 56Fe
- RMF parameters for NS matter: saturation baryon density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, effective nucleon mass m_N* = 0.75 m_N at saturation, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy E_s = 30.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study Fu (2008) by including hyperon degrees of freedom in the neutron star equation of state and by examining additional nonradial oscillation modes (f-mode and p-mode) for comparison. The results suggest that gravitational wave observations from core-collapse supernovae or newly born compact stars could potentially distinguish between neutron stars and strange quark stars, thereby identifying the QCD phase transition to deconfined quark matter at high density. Prior work by Burrows (2005, 2006) and Ott (2006) had indicated that g-mode oscillations of supernova cores may be excited and serve as efficient gravitational wave sources.
