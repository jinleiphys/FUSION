# Emission times and opacities from interferometry in non-central Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The nuclear overlap zone in non-central relativistic heavy ion collisions is azimuthally very asymmetric. By varying the angle between the axes of deformation and the transverse direction of the pair momenta, the transverse HBT radii oscillate in a characteristic way. It is shown that these oscillations allow determination of source sizes, deformations as well as the opacity and duration of emission of the source created in any non-central high energy nuclear collisions. The behavior of the physical quantities with centrality of the collisions is discussed --- in particular changes caused by a possible phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma.

# Emission times and opacities from interferometry in non-central Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

By measuring the azimuthal modulation of transverse HBT radii relative to the reaction plane in non-central collisions, one can separately determine the source size, deformation, opacity, and duration of emission, quantities that remain degenerate when the reaction plane is not determined. The method works for any non-central high-energy nuclear collision, and tracking these quantities with centrality may reveal a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma if sudden changes are observed.

## Method

The paper extends the standard Hanbury-Brown–Twiss (HBT) interferometry method to azimuthally asymmetric sources created in non-central collisions. The source is parametrized as a Gaussian in the transverse plane with different radii \(R_x\) and \(R_y\) along and perpendicular to the reaction plane, and a Gaussian in freeze-out time with width \(\delta\tau\). For opaque sources, Glauber absorption is introduced via an absorption factor \(\exp(-\int_x \sigma\rho(x')dx')\), with mean free path \(\lambda_{\rm mfp}=(\sigma\rho(0))^{-1}\). The Bose-Einstein correlation function is computed in the Gaussian approximation, yielding analytic expressions for the HBT radii \(R_s\), \(R_o\), and \(R_{os}\) as functions of the azimuthal angle \(\phi\) between the transverse pair momentum and the reaction plane, with opacity-dependent factors \(g_s\), \(g_o\), and \(g_{os}\).

## Key numbers

* Source size estimate from initial nuclear overlap: \(R_x = R_A - b/2\) and \(R_y = \sqrt{R_A^2 - b^2/4}\) for nuclear radius \(R_A\) and impact parameter \(b\); deformation \(\delta = b/(2R_A)\).
* Typical heavy ion source size \(R \sim 5\) fm, so interference occurs when relative momentum \(q \lesssim \hbar/R \sim 40\) MeV/c.
* Typical particle momenta \(p_i \gtrsim P \sim 300\) MeV/c.
* For a Gaussian source that is moderately opaque (\(\lambda_{\rm mfp}/R = 1\)), numerical calculation gives \(g_s \simeq 1.4\) and \(g_o \simeq 0.9\).
* For a black-body disk source (\(\lambda_{\rm mfp} \ll R\)), \(g_s = 4/3\) and \(g_o = 4(2/3 - (\pi/4)^2) \simeq 0.2\).
* Reduction factor \(\lambda \sim 0.5\) for pions and \(\lambda \sim 0.9\) for kaons.
* For semicentral Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, \(\langle u_x^2\rangle - \langle u_y^2\rangle \sim v_2\) is only a few percent, much less than \(\delta \simeq 0.5\) for semi-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the HBT formalism of Wiedemann (1998) for transparent deformed sources to include opacity effects, building on the opaque-source calculations of Heiselberg and Vischer (1998). It also connects to the experimental determination of the reaction plane developed at the Bevalac, AGS, and NA49 collaborations, and to the elliptic flow measurements of Ollitrault (1992) and Voloshin (1997). The paper enables experimental separation of source opacity from emission duration, a degeneracy that has prevented unambiguous interpretation of existing NA49 and NA44 data, where the sign of \(\langle R_o^2 - R_s^2\rangle\) differs between the two experiments. If a phase transition occurs, hydrodynamic calculations by Bernard, Rischke, Maruhn, and Greiner (1997) predict drastic increases in emission time and duration, which this method could detect.
