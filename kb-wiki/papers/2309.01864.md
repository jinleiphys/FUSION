# Far-from-equilibrium bulk-viscous transport coefficients in neutron star mergers

> We investigate the weak-interaction-driven bulk-viscous transport properties of $npe$ matter in the neutrino transparent regime. Previous works assumed that the induced bulk viscosity correction to pressure, near beta equilibrium, is linear in deviations from the equilibrium charge fraction. We show that this is not always true for (some) realistic equations of state at densities between one and three times saturation density. This nonlinear nature of the perturbation around equilibrium motivates a far-from-beta-equilibrium description of bulk-viscous transport in neutron star mergers, which can be precisely achieved using a new Israel-Stewart formulation with resummed bulk and relaxation time transport coefficients. The computation of these transport coefficients depends on out-of-beta-equilibrium pressure corrections, which can be computed for a given equation of state. We calculate these coefficients for equations of state that satisfy the latest constraints from multi-messenger observations from LIGO/VIRGO and NICER. We show that varying the nuclear symmetry energy $J$ and its slope $L$ can significantly affect the transport coefficients and the nonlinear behavior of the out-of-equilibrium pressure corrections. Therefore, having better constraints on $J$ and $L$ will directly impact our understanding of bulk-viscous processes in neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the weak-interaction-driven bulk-viscous correction to pressure in neutron star merger matter is not always linear in deviations from beta equilibrium, contrary to previous assumptions. This nonlinearity, found at densities between one and three times saturation density for realistic equations of state, motivates a far-from-equilibrium description using a resummed Israel-Stewart formulation where the transport coefficients themselves depend on the out-of-equilibrium pressure correction.

## Method
The authors compute the resummed bulk viscosity coefficient and relaxation time for npe matter in the neutrino-transparent regime using the exact Israel-Stewart formulation derived by Gavassino (2023). They employ relativistic mean-field equations of state with varying nuclear symmetry energy J (29 to 34 MeV) and its slope L (35 to 75 MeV), satisfying constraints from LIGO/VIRGO and NICER observations, and evaluate the transport coefficients under a zero-temperature approximation for the equation of state while including temperature dependence through the weak interaction rates.

## Key numbers
- Densities considered: between one and three times nuclear saturation density (n_sat = 0.148 fm^-3)
- Symmetry energy J varied from 29 MeV to 34 MeV across six equations of state (J1-J6)
- Symmetry energy slope L varied from 35 MeV to 75 MeV across five equations of state (L1-L5)
- Nuclear compressibility K held fixed at 275 MeV for all equations of state
- Binding energy E_b held fixed at -15.677 MeV for all equations of state

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Gavassino (2023), who demonstrated that reactive fluid mixtures can be exactly mapped to a resummed Israel-Stewart bulk-viscous theory even far from equilibrium. It builds on prior calculations of bulk viscosity from linear response theory by Alford (2018, 2021, 2023) and the Israel-Stewart formulation near equilibrium by Gavassino (2020). The results enable numerical simulations of neutron star mergers, such as those by Most (2022), to be interpreted as inherently bulk-viscous systems, and show that better constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy J and its slope L will directly improve understanding of dissipative processes in mergers.
