# Calculations of three-body observables in ^8B breakup

> We discuss calculations of three-body observables for the breakup of ^8B on a ^{58}Ni target at low energy using the coupled discretised continuum channels approach. Calculations of both the angular distribution of the ^7Be fragments and their energy distributions are compared with those measured at several laboratory angles. In these observables there is interference between the breakup amplitudes from different spin-parity excitations of the projectile. The resulting angle and the energy distributions reveal the importance of the higher-order continuum state couplings for an understanding of the measurements.

# Calculations of three-body observables in ^8B breakup

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that full three-body coupled discretised continuum channels (CDCC) calculations, which include higher-order continuum-state couplings, are essential for reproducing measured ^7Be fragment angular and energy distributions from ^8B breakup on ^58Ni at 25.8 MeV; the abstract's claim of "importance of higher-order continuum state couplings" is qualified by the finding that convergence of three-body observables requires a significantly more extended space of excitation energies (up to at least 8-10 MeV) than was needed for simpler center-of-mass cross section calculations.

## Method

The authors apply the CDCC method to model the breakup of ^8B (treated as a ^7Be core plus proton) on a ^58Ni target at Elab = 25.8 MeV, constructing continuum bin states up to a maximum relative energy of 10 MeV for each spin-parity channel with orbital angular momenta up to ℓ=3. The coupled channels equations are solved using the code FRESCO, and the resulting two-body inelastic amplitudes are interpolated and combined with bin-state overlap integrals to construct full three-body breakup T-matrices, which are then integrated over unobserved proton angles to compute laboratory-frame ^7Be fragment angular and energy distributions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: Elab = 25.8 MeV for ^8B on ^58Ni target
- Continuum bins extended up to maximum relative energy Emax = 10 MeV for each Jp' state
- 32 bins used for the s1/2 continuum; 16 bins for each of the other Jp' states
- Partial waves up to Lmax = 1000 and coupling radii up to Rcoup = 500 fm used for projectile-target relative motion
- Bin state radii r ≤ rbin = 60 fm
- Potential multipoles up to q ≤ 3 required for converged calculations (q ≤ 4 and ℓ=4 contributions shown to be small)
- ^7Be detectors subtended solid angle ΔΩc of a circle of radius 6° about nominal laboratory angle, with Gaussian efficiency profile of full width half maximum 10.9°
- Proton separation energy from ^59Cu(g.s.) is Sp = 3.42 MeV
- Calculated proton energy distributions peak at Ep ≈ 3.8 MeV with width Γ ≈ 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC calculations by Nunes et al. (1996) and Esbensen and Bertsch (1996) that showed the importance of higher-order breakup couplings for the ^8B* center-of-mass cross section angular distribution, but which used only bins up to 3 MeV relative energy and multipoles q ≤ 2. The present calculations demonstrate that three-body observables require a more extended model space and reveal that continuum-continuum couplings suppress the E1/E2 interference asymmetry seen in distorted waves Born approximation (DWBA) and truncated coupled channels calculations, an effect also noted by Esbensen (1998) at higher energy. The results enable detailed comparison with new experimental data from the University of Notre Dame (1999-2000) and provide a framework for constraining ^8B structure and proton-target optical potentials through exclusive breakup measurements.
