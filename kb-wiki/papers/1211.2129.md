# Particle-number conserving analysis for the 2-quasiparticle and high-$K$ multi-quasiparticle states in doubly-odd ${}^{174, 176}$Lu

> Two-quasiparticle bands and low-lying excited high-$K$ four-, six-, and eight-quasiparticle bands in the doubly-odd ${}^{174, 176}$Lu are analyzed by using the cranked shell model (CSM) with the pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The proton and neutron Nilsson level schemes for ${}^{174, 176}$Lu are taken from the adjacent odd-$A$ Lu and Hf isotopes, which are adopted to reproduce the experimental bandhead energies of the one-quasiproton and one-quasineutron bands of these odd-$A$ Lu and Hf nuclei, respectively. Once the quasiparticle configurations are determined, the experimental bandhead energies and the moments of inertia of these two- and multi-quasiparticle bands are well reproduced by PNC-CSM calculations. The Coriolis mixing of the low-$K$ ($K=|Ω_1-Ω_2|$) two-quasiparticle band of the Gallagher-Moszkowski doublet with one nucleon in the $Ω= 1/2$ orbital is analyzed.

# Particle-number conserving analysis for the 2-quasiparticle and high-$K$ multi-quasiparticle states in doubly-odd ${}^{174, 176}$Lu

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with particle-number conserving (PNC) treatment of pairing correlations, using Nilsson level schemes taken from adjacent odd-$A$ Lu and Hf isotopes, can reproduce experimental bandhead energies and moments of inertia for two- and multi-quasiparticle bands in ${}^{174,176}$Lu without including the neutron-proton residual interaction, except for cases where the $\pi 1/2^-[541]$ orbital energy is poorly reproduced and where level sequence inversions near the neutron Fermi surface remain unresolved.

## Method

The cranked shell model (CSM) Hamiltonian with monopole pairing was diagonalized in a truncated cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space of about 700 proton and 800 neutron configurations, using deformation parameters from Lund systematics ($\varepsilon_2 = 0.262, \varepsilon_4 = 0.0402$ for ${}^{174}$Lu; $\varepsilon_2 = 0.260, \varepsilon_4 = 0.0512$ for ${}^{176}$Lu). Proton and neutron Nilsson level schemes were taken from adjacent odd-$A$ ${}^{173}$Lu and ${}^{175}$Hf (for ${}^{174}$Lu) and from ${}^{177}$Lu and ${}^{177}$Hf (for ${}^{176}$Lu), adjusted to reproduce experimental one-quasiproton and one-quasineutron bandhead energies. The Coriolis mixing of low-$K$ Gallagher-Moszkowski doublet bands involving $\Omega = 1/2$ orbitals was analyzed by admixing high-$K$ and low-$K$ states.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths for ${}^{174}$Lu: $G_p = 0.33$ MeV (protons), $G_n = 0.30$ MeV (neutrons); for ${}^{176}$Lu: $G_p = 0.30$ MeV, $G_n = 0.28$ MeV
- Deformation parameters: ${}^{174}$Lu $\varepsilon_2 = 0.262$, $\varepsilon_4 = 0.0402$; ${}^{176}$Lu $\varepsilon_2 = 0.260$, $\varepsilon_4 = 0.0512$
- CMPC space dimensions: approximately 700 for protons, 800 for neutrons; truncated CMPC energies about $0.8\hbar\omega_0$ for protons and $0.7\hbar\omega_0$ for neutrons, where $\hbar\omega_0 = 41A^{-1/3}$ MeV
- For ${}^{174}$Lu: experimental bandhead of $K^\pi = 1^-$ (ground state) at 0 keV, $K^\pi = 8^-$ at 772 keV (calculated 657 keV); $K^\pi = 13^+$ at 1856 keV (calculated 1638 keV); $K^\pi = 26^-$ at 5850 keV (calculated 6119 keV)
- For ${}^{176}$Lu: experimental bandhead of $K^\pi = 7^-$ (ground state) at 0 keV, $K^\pi = 0^-$ at 123 keV; $K^\pi = 0^+, 7^+$ ($\pi 7/2^-[523] \otimes \nu 7/2^-[514]$) at 1057, 1274 keV (calculated 1047 keV)
- Coriolis mixing of 50% between high-$K$ and low-$K$ states reproduced data for $K^\pi = 2^+$ ($\pi 1/2^-[541] \otimes \nu 5/2^-[512]$) band in ${}^{174}$Lu at higher $\hbar\omega$; at very low $\hbar\omega < 0.05$ MeV, mixing was about 30%
- For ${}^{176}$Lu, the $K^\pi = 3^-$ ($\pi 7/2^+[404] \otimes \nu 1/2^-[521]$) GM doublet energy splitting was 50 keV, smaller than the typical ~100 keV

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach previously applied to even-even and odd-$A$ Hf and Lu isotopes in the $170 \leq A \leq 178$ region by Zhang (2009), now treating doubly-odd ${}^{174,176}$Lu. The method contrasts with conventional BCS or Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov approaches by conserving particle number exactly and accounting for Pauli blocking effects. The results enable configuration assignments for multi-quasiparticle isomers, including the $K^\pi = 22^+$ and $22^-$ states at 4710 keV in ${}^{174}$Lu, and confirm assignments from Kondev (2009) while identifying cases where multiple configurations remain possible. The analysis of Coriolis mixing in Gallagher-Moszkowski doublets with $\Omega = 1/2$ orbitals builds on the work of O'Neil (1972) and Jain (1998).
