# Alpha-Cluster Model, Charge Symmetry of Nuclear Force and Single Particle Bound State Potential in Symmetrical Nuclei

> A phenomenological alpha-cluster model based on np-pair interactions and the charge symmetry of nuclear force allows one to estimate the Coulomb energy, the Coulomb radius R_C, the Coulomb energy of the last proton interaction with the residual nucleus and the radius of its position R_p in a symmetrical nucleus. The values R_C and R_p obtained for the symmetrical nuclei with 5<= Z<=45 are used in a long standing task of determination of the parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential of neuton/proton bound state used in DWBA analysis of direct one nucleon transfer reactions. According to the charge symmetry of nuclear force a requirement of equality of the nuclear potentials of the last neutron and the last proton in a symmetrical nucleus is added to the standard well-depth procedure in solving the Shrödinger equation, which makes R_C the crucial parameter to determine the parameters of the nuclear potential and the value of the last proton rms radius <r_p^2>^{1/2} in the wave representation. Comparative analysis of the radii <r_p^2>^{1/2} and R_p, R_C, the experimental radii and the nuclear potential radii obtained at the calculations shows that for the nuclei with Z>16 it is inappropriate to represent a single particle bound state by the Woods-Saxon potential. Using asymptotic coefficient of wave function allows one to estimate error in obtaining spectroscopic factor caused by using the standard parameters in DWBA analysis of pure peripheral reactions. It is shown that using these may bring an error up to 48%.

## Key claim

A phenomenological alpha-cluster model based on np-pair interactions and charge symmetry of nuclear force allows estimation of Coulomb radius and last proton position radius for symmetrical nuclei with 5 ≤ Z ≤ 45; these radii are then used to determine Woods-Saxon potential parameters under the condition that neutron and proton nuclear potentials are equal in symmetrical nuclei, but for nuclei with Z > 16 it is inappropriate to represent a single particle bound state by the Woods-Saxon potential.

## Method

The Coulomb radius R_C and last proton position radius R_p were estimated from nuclear binding energies using an alpha-cluster model based on np-pair interactions, with the Coulomb energy calculated from the sum of alpha-cluster and cluster-cluster Coulomb energies. These radii were then used as input to a modified DWUCK program implementing the equality of neutron and proton nuclear potentials (EPN condition) together with the standard well-depth procedure to solve the Schrödinger equation for the Woods-Saxon potential parameters r_0, a_0, and V_0 at fixed diffuseness a_0 = 0.65 fm. The asymptotic coefficients b_n and b_p of the bound state wave functions were calculated from the resulting potentials and compared with those obtained using standard parameters (r_0 = 1.25 fm, a_0 = 0.65 fm).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of the EPN condition for symmetrical and mirror nuclei (Nie, 2009-2011) and applies it to the long-standing problem of determining Woods-Saxon potential parameters for single particle bound states, which has been addressed by many authors using standard parameters around r_0 = 1.25 fm and a_0 = 0.65 fm (various authors, 1960s-2000s). The approach enables estimation of errors in spectroscopic factors obtained from DWBA analysis of direct one-nucleon transfer reactions, particularly for peripheral reactions where the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method has been developed by other authors (Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk, 1990s-2000s). The finding that Woods-Saxon potential is inappropriate for nuclei with Z > 16 challenges the standard practice in DWBA analysis and suggests the need for alternative representations of single particle bound states for heavier symmetrical nuclei.
