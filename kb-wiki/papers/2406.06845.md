# Time-dependent Relativistic Hartree-Fock model with spherical symmetry

> This work establishes the time-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (TD-RHF) model with spherical symmetry for the first time. The time-dependent integro-differential Dirac equations are solved by expanding Dirac spinors on the spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis. The numerical verification demonstrates the high conservation qualities for both the total binding energy and the particle number, as well as the time-reversal invariance of the system, which ensures the precision and reliability of the newly developed TD-RHF model. Subsequently, the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) mode of $^{208}$Pb is investigated using the RHF Lagrangian PKO1. The constrained energy of the ISGMR calculated by PKO1 is found to be in close agreement with the experimental data, and the strength function is similar to the results given by the relativistic Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation. Based on the advantage of the TD-RHF model in avoiding complicated calculations of the residual interactions, the ISGMR mode of $^{208}$Pb is calculated by twelve relativistic effective Lagrangians. The results indicate that the value of the incompressibility of nuclear matter $K_\infty$ constrained by relativistic effective Lagrangians is in the range of $237\sim246$ MeV, which is lower than the previous investigations based on the relativistic models.

## Key claim

The newly developed time-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (TD-RHF) model with spherical symmetry is established for the first time, and when applied to the ISGMR of $^{208}$Pb using twelve relativistic effective Lagrangians, it constrains the nuclear matter incompressibility $K_\infty$ to a range of 237–246 MeV, which is lower than previous relativistic-model investigations.

## Method

The time-dependent integro-differential Dirac equations are solved by expanding Dirac spinors on a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon (DWS) basis. The time evolution of the expansion coefficients is computed using a predictor-corrector strategy with a Taylor expansion of the time-evolution operator, and the ISGMR is excited by applying an instantaneous boost with the operator $\hat F_{\text{IS}} = r^2$. The strength function is obtained from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent $F$-signal deviation.

## Key numbers

- The constrained energy of the ISGMR in $^{208}$Pb calculated with the RHF Lagrangian PKO1 agrees with the experimental constrained energy of $13.5\pm0.1$ MeV (Patel 2013).
- The nuclear matter incompressibility $K_\infty$ constrained by twelve relativistic effective Lagrangians falls in the range $237\sim246$ MeV.
- With a time step $\Delta t = 0.1$ fm/c and Taylor expansion order $m=4$, the relative total energy deviation is approximately $10^{-6}$ and the relative particle number deviation is approximately $10^{-8}$ for $^{208}$Pb.
- For $m=8$, the relative particle number deviation reaches approximately $10^{-11}$.

## Context

This work extends the static relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory to its time-dependent (TD-RHF) form, complementing earlier time-dependent relativistic mean field (TD-RMF) models (Vretenar 1997, 1999) and non-relativistic time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) approaches. By avoiding the complicated calculation of residual interactions required in RPA-based frameworks, the TD-RHF model enables systematic testing of multiple relativistic effective Lagrangians (including PKO1, DD-ME2, DD-PC1, and others) for the ISGMR, and provides a lower constraint on $K_\infty$ than earlier relativistic RPA studies (Ma 2001, Vretenar 2003). This enables future investigations of the role of Fock terms in both small-amplitude collective oscillations and large-amplitude nuclear dynamics.
