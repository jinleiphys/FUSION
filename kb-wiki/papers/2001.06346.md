# QCD parameters and $f_{B_c}$ from heavy quark sum rules

> We report results of our recent works [1,2] where we where the correlations between the c,b-quark running masses{m}_{c,b}, the gluon condensate<α_s G^2> and the QCD coupling α_s in the MS-scheme from an analysis of the charmonium and bottomium spectra and the B_c-meson mass. We use optimized ratios of relativistic Laplace sum rules (LSR) evaluated at the μ-subtraction stability point where higher orders PT and D< 6-8-dimensions non-perturbative condensates corrections are included. We obtain [1] α_s(2.85)=0.262(9) and α_s(9.50)=0.180(8) from the (pseudo)scalar M_{χ_{0c(0b)}}-M_{η_{c(b)}} mass-splittings at μ=2.85(9.50) GeV. The most precise result from the charm channel leads to α_s(M_τ)=0.318(15) and α_s(M_Z)=0.1183(19)(3) in excellent agreement with the world average: α_s(M_Z)=0.1181(11)[3,4]. Updated results from a global fit of the (axial-)vector and (pseudo)scalar channels using Laplace and Moments sum rules @ N2LO [1] combined with the one from M_{B_c} [2] lead to the new tentative QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) average : m_c(m_c)|_average= 1266(6) MeV and m_b(m_b)|_average=4196(8) MeV. The values of the gluon condensate <α_s G^2> from the (axial)-vector charmonium channels combined with previous determinations in Table 1, leads to the new QSSR average [1]: <α_s G^2>_average=(6.35\pm 0.35)x 10^{-2} GeV^4. Our results clarify the (apparent) discrepancies between different estimates of <α_s G^2> from J/ψsum rule but also shows the sensitivity of the sum rules on the choice of the μ-subtraction scale. As a biproduct, we deduce the B_c-decay constants f_{B_c}=371(17) MeV and f_{B_c}(2S)< 139(6) MeV.

## Key claim
The paper presents a correlated extraction of the QCD coupling $\alpha_s$, the running charm and bottom quark masses $\overline{m}_{c,b}$, and the gluon condensate $\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle$ from heavy quark sum rules, claiming that the results clarify apparent discrepancies in previous estimates of the gluon condensate, but only under the condition that the analysis is performed at the $\mu$-subtraction stability point where higher-order perturbative and non-perturbative corrections are included.

## Method
The analysis uses optimized ratios of relativistic Laplace sum rules (LSR) evaluated at the $\mu$-subtraction stability point, including higher-order perturbative corrections at N2LO and non-perturbative condensate contributions up to dimension 6-8. The spectral functions for the (axial-)vector and (pseudo)scalar charmonium and bottomium channels are parametrized using a narrow width approximation for the known resonances and a QCD continuum above a threshold $t_c$. The QCD parameters are extracted from a global fit of these channels, combined with the $B_c$-meson mass, using the ratio of moments ${\cal R}^c_0$ to determine hadron masses.

## Key numbers
- $\alpha_s(2.85) = 0.262(9)$ and $\alpha_s(9.50) = 0.180(8)$, extracted from the (pseudo)scalar $M_{\chi_{0c(0b)}} - M_{\eta_{c(b)}}$ mass-splittings at $\mu = 2.85(9.50)$ GeV.
- $\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.1183(19)(3)$, derived from the charm channel, in agreement with the world average of $0.1181(11)$.
- $\overline{m}_c(\overline{m}_c)|_{\text{average}} = 1266(6)$ MeV and $\overline{m}_b(\overline{m}_b)|_{\text{average}} = 4196(8)$ MeV, from a new tentative QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) average.
- $\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle|_{\text{average}} = (6.35 \pm 0.35) \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^4$, from a new QSSR average combining (axial)-vector charmonium channels with previous determinations.
- $f_{B_c} = 371(17)$ MeV and $f_{B_c}(2S) \leq 139(6)$ MeV, deduced as a byproduct.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analyses (Narison 2012a, 2012b, 2018) and the original SVZ sum rule framework (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov 1979) to provide a correlated determination of fundamental QCD parameters. It addresses the long-standing discrepancies in the gluon condensate value, which have varied widely in the literature from analyses of charmonium sum rules (e.g., Bell-Bertlmann 1982, Ioffe-Zyablyuk 2007) and tau-decay moments (e.g., ALEPH, OPAL collaborations). The results enable more precise predictions for heavy quarkonium properties and the $B_c$ decay constant, and they motivate further lattice calculations of the gluon condensate, which currently yield values 2-7 times higher than the phenomenological estimates.
