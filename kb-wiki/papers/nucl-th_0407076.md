# Energy Systematics of Low-lying Collective States within the Framework of the Interacting Vector Boson Model

> In a new application of the algebraic Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), we exploit the reduction of its Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry group to Sp(4,R) x SO(3), which defines basis states with fixed values of the angular momentum L. The relationship of the latter to $U(6) \subset U(3)x U(2), which is the rotational limit of the model, means the energy distribution of collective states with fixed angular momentum can be studied. Results for low-lying spectra of rare-earth nuclei show that the energies of collective positive parity states with L=0,2,4,6... lie on second order curves with respect to the number of collective phonons n or vector bosons N=4n out of which the states are built. The analysis of this behavior leads to insight regarding the common nature of collective states, tracking vibrational as well as rotational features.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energies of low-lying collective positive parity states with fixed angular momentum L in even-even rare-earth nuclei follow a parabolic (second-order) dependence on the number of collective phonons n or vector bosons N=4n, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of the Sp(4,R) reduction chain of the Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry group and the use of a Hamiltonian expressed in terms of Casimir operators of the subgroups in that chain.

## Method

The authors exploit the reduction of the Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry group of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) to Sp(4,R) x SO(3), which defines basis states with fixed angular momentum L. They use the correspondence between this symplectic reduction chain and the unitary U(6) chain to employ the same Hamiltonian, expressed as a linear combination of Casimir invariants, which is diagonal in the basis. The eigenvalues for states of given L yield a parabolic energy dependence on the boson number N, and the authors perform chi-squared fitting procedures to determine the Hamiltonian parameters by matching experimental energies of 0+, 2+, and 4+ states in five rare-earth nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For 148Sm: a = 0.03096, b = -0.00010 (parameters for 0+ states); beta_3 = 0.03929 (inertia parameter); chi-squared values range from 0.0002 to 0.0023 across L=0,2,4,6 states.
* For 144Nd: a = 0.02389, b = -0.00003; beta_3 = 0.04074; chi-squared values of 0.0001 (L=0), 0.0008 (L=2), and 0.0004 (L=4).
* For 168Er: a = 0.04270, b = -0.00017; beta_3 = 0.01295; chi-squared values from 0.0006 (L=0) to 0.0022 (L=2).
* For 178Hf: a = 0.02666, b = -0.00007; beta_3 = 0.01482; chi-squared values from 0.0008 (L=2) to 0.0016 (L=4).
* For 154Gd: a = 0.05218, b = -0.00019; beta_3 = 0.01634; chi-squared values from 0.00008 (L=5) to 0.0482 (L=2).
* The first excited 2+ state lies at about 1 MeV for vibrational nuclei and about 0.07 MeV for well-deformed rotational nuclei.

## Context

This work extends an earlier empirical analysis by Garistov (2003) that classified experimental energies of 0+ excited states in well-deformed nuclei using a simple Hamiltonian for parabolic energy spectra, and it generalizes that approach to include states with L=2,4,6 and to cover both deformed and nearly spherical nuclei. The IVBM approach builds on the symplectic shell model framework developed by Rowe and Draayer, and the results confirm that the energy systematics can be understood through the Sp(4,R) reduction of the Sp(12,R) dynamical symmetry group, enabling future studies of the common nature of vibrational and rotational collective motion within a single algebraic framework.
