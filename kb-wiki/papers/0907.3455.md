# On the physical Relevance of the Study of gamma* gamma -> pi0 pi0 at small t and large Q2

> We discuss the relevance of a dedicated measurement of exclusive production of a pair of neutral pions in a hard gamma* gamma scattering at small momentum transfer. In this case, the virtuality of one photon provides us with a hard scale in the process, enabling us to perform a QCD calculation of this reaction rate using the concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDA). Those are related by sum rules to the pion axial form factor F^pi_A and, as a direct consequence, a cross-section measurement of this process at intense beam electron-positron colliders such as CLEO, KEK-B and PEP-II, or Super-B would provide us with a unique measurement of the neutral pion axial form factor F^pi0_A at small scale.

## Key claim

The paper argues that measuring the exclusive process $\gamma^\star \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ at small momentum transfer $t$ and large $Q^2$ can provide a unique experimental determination of the neutral pion axial form factor $F^{\pi^0}_A$ at small scale, which has never been measured. This claim is conditional on the validity of the factorisation framework using Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) and on the availability of high-luminosity electron-positron colliders.

## Method

The authors factorise the amplitude for $\gamma^\star \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ into a hard scattering part calculable in perturbative QCD and non-perturbative objects: the pion distribution amplitude (DA) for the final-state pion and the axial $\gamma \to \pi^0$ Transition Distribution Amplitude (TDA). The TDA is defined as the Fourier transform of a matrix element $\langle \pi^0 | \bar{\psi} \gamma^\mu \gamma^5 \psi | \gamma \rangle$ and is related by a sum rule to the quark contribution to the pion axial form factor. The authors evaluate the differential cross section using a toy model for the TDA based on double distributions, assuming asymptotic forms for the pion DA and specific isospin relations between quark flavours.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section $\frac{d\sigma_{e\gamma \to e \pi^0 \pi^0}^{TDA}}{dQ^2 dt d\xi}$ is evaluated for $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, $t = -0.5$ GeV$^2$, and three values of $s_{e\gamma}$: 20, 30, and 40 GeV$^2$.
- The scaling law for the amplitude is ${\cal M}^{TDA}_{\gamma^\star \gamma}(Q^2, \xi,t) \sim \frac{\alpha_s\sqrt{-t}}{Q}$, up to logarithmic corrections.
- The cross section for the neutral pion case is roughly one sixth of the charged pion case obtained in Lansberg (2006).
- The vector form factor for charged pions is given as $F_V^{\pi^{\pm}} = 0.017 \pm 0.008$ from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends the factorisation theorems for exclusive processes, previously applied to reactions such as $B_1 \overline{B}_2 \to \gamma^\star \gamma$ and $\gamma_T^\star B_1 \to B_2 \gamma$, to the case of $\gamma_L^\star \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0$ in the small-$t$, large-$W_{\pi\pi}^2$ regime. It contrasts with the low-$W_{\pi\pi}^2$ region studied by BaBar (2009) in the context of the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances, which is analysed using generalised two-meson distribution amplitudes. The proposed measurement would provide a new experimental constraint on the neutral pion axial form factor, complementing a possible determination from DVCS on a virtual neutral pion as discussed by Amrath (2008).
