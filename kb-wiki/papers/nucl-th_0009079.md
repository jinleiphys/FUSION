# Asymmetry Effects on Nuclear Fragmentation

> We show the possibility of extracting important information on the symmetry term of the Equation of State ($EOS$) directly from multifragmentation reactions using stable isotopes with different charge asymmetries. We study n-rich and n-poor $Sn + Sn$ collisions at $50AMeV$ using a new stochastic transport approach with all isospin effects suitably accounted for. For central collisions a chemical component in the spinodal instabilities is clearly seen. This effect is reduced in the neck fragmentation observed for semiperipheral collisions, pointing to a different nature of the instability. In spite of the low asymmetry tested with stable isotopes the results are showing an interesting and promising dependence on the stiffness of the symmetry term, with an indication towards an increase of the repulsion above normal density.

# Asymmetry Effects on Nuclear Fragmentation

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that multifragmentation reactions using stable isotopes with different charge asymmetries can extract information on the symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State, with an indication toward a stiff symmetry term (increased repulsion above normal density), though the effects are not large due to the low asymmetry accessible with stable beams.

## Method
The authors performed stochastic transport simulations (Stochastic Iso-BNV code) of n-rich (124Sn+124Sn) and n-poor (112Sn+112Sn) collisions at 50 AMeV, using a Skyrme force with a soft symmetric matter EOS (K=201 MeV) and two different density-dependent symmetry terms (asy-stiff and asy-soft). They analyzed 100 events per system at impact parameters b=2 fm (semi-central) and b=6 fm (semi-peripheral), tracking time evolution of mass, asymmetry, fragment multiplicity, and freeze-out properties.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 50 AMeV for both 124Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn collisions
- Symmetric matter EOS compressibility: K=201 MeV (soft EOS)
- Impact parameters studied: b=2 fm (semi-central) and b=6 fm (semi-peripheral)
- Intermediate mass fragment (IMF) definition: 3 ≤ Z ≤ 12
- Freeze-out times decrease with increasing impact parameter (qualitative, no explicit numerical values given)
- Mean IMF multiplicity vs. average charge of heaviest fragment shows systematic differences between asy-stiff and asy-soft choices, with effects more evident for asy-stiff in central collisions (low <Z>_heavy)

## Context
This work extends earlier linear response and nuclear matter box calculations of chemical effects on spinodal instabilities (Mue95, Bao197, Colonna et al. 1998) to full "ab initio" reaction simulations. It contrasts with statistical multifragmentation calculations (Dyst99) where the chemical effect is much reduced. The results are consistent with transverse flow calculations in the same energy range (Scd99) and enable future studies with radioactive beams to probe the symmetry term of astrophysical interest for supernovae dynamics and neutron star cooling.
