# Heavy quarkonium correlators at finite temperature: QCD sum rule approach

> We investigate the properties of heavy quarkonia at finite temperature in detail using QCD sum rules. Extending previous analyses, we take into account a temperature dependent effective continuum threshold and derive constraints on the mass, the width, and the varying effective continuum threshold. We find that at least one of these quantities of a charmonium changes abruptly in the vicinity of the phase transition. We also calculate the ratio of the imaginary time correlator to its reconstructed one, $G/G_{\text{rec}}$, by constructing a model spectral function and compare it to the corresponding lattice QCD results. We demonstrate that the almost constant unity of $G/G_{\text{rec}}$ can be obtained from the destructive interplay of the changes in each part of the spectral modification which are extracted from QCD sum rules.

# Heavy quarkonium correlators at finite temperature: QCD sum rule approach

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the near-constant unity of the ratio of the imaginary time correlator to its reconstructed one, $G/G_{\text{rec}}$, observed in lattice QCD can be reproduced from the destructive interplay of changes in each part of the spectral modification extracted from QCD sum rules, but only if at least one property of charmonium (mass, width, or effective continuum threshold) changes abruptly near the phase transition temperature.

## Method

The authors extend previous QCD sum rule analyses of heavy quarkonia at finite temperature by incorporating a temperature-dependent effective continuum threshold and applying a Borel transformation approach. They solve the dispersion relation for the Borel-transformed correlation function using a model spectral function consisting of a relativistic Breit-Wigner pole plus a perturbative continuum, then determine the optimal set of spectral parameters (mass, width, and continuum threshold) by minimizing the $\chi^2$ deviation of the Borel curve from flatness within a Borel window defined by OPE convergence and pole dominance criteria. Using the extracted spectral parameters, they construct model spectral functions and compute the imaginary time correlators for comparison with lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: $m_c(p^2=-m_c^2)=1.262$ GeV; bottom quark mass: $m_b(p^2=-m_b^2)=4.12$ GeV
- Strong coupling: $\alpha_s(8m_c^2)=0.21$ for charmonia; $\alpha_s(8m_b^2)=0.158$ for bottomonia
- For $J/\psi$ at $T=1.04T_c$ with $\sqrt{s_0}=3.28$ GeV and $\Gamma=0$: $\chi^2=1.89\times10^{-4}$ GeV$^2$; with $\Gamma=100$ MeV: $\chi^2=8.55\times10^{-4}$ GeV$^2$
- For $J/\psi$ at $T=1.12T_c$: stability requires finite width of $\Gamma=274$ MeV with $\sqrt{s_0}=3.08$ GeV ($\chi^2=1.69\times10^{-5}$ GeV$^2$) or $\Gamma=336$ MeV with $\sqrt{s_0}=3.5$ GeV ($\chi^2=1.9\times10^{-5}$ GeV$^2$)
- Borel window criteria: dimension-four correction terms less than 30% of total; continuum contribution less than 30% of total perturbative term; perturbative radiative correction term $\alpha_s(M^2)a^J(\nu)$ less than 0.3 for bottomonia

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses by Morita, Lee, and collaborators (2008-2009) that studied heavy quarkonia at finite temperature using moment sum rules, and by Song (2009) and Lee, Morita, and Song (in preparation). The approach contrasts with potential model calculations (Mocsy, 2007-2008) that suggest $J/\psi$ melts at $T=1.2T_c$, and with lattice QCD studies using the maximum entropy method (Asakawa, 2004; Datta, 2004; Umeda, 2005) that indicate $J/\psi$ survives up to $T\sim 1.6T_c$. The results enable direct comparison between QCD sum rule predictions and lattice QCD imaginary time correlator data, providing a framework to disentangle the competing effects of mass shift, broadening, and continuum threshold changes on the observed spectral modifications.
