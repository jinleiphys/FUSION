# Pions in the nuclear medium and Drell-Yan scattering

> We investigate the modification of the pion-cloud in the nuclear medium and its effect on the nuclear Drell-Yan process. The pion's in-medium self-energy is calculated in a self-consistent delta-hole model, with particle-hole contribution also included. Both the imaginary and real part of the pion's and delta's self-energy are taken into account and related through a dispersion relation assuring causality. The resulting in-medium pion light-cone momentum distribution shows only a slight enhancement compared to the one of the free nucleon. As a consequence the ratio of the cross-section for Drell-Yan scattering on nuclear matter and nucleonic target is close to unity in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The in-medium pion light-cone momentum distribution shows only a slight enhancement compared to the free-nucleon case, and consequently the ratio of Drell-Yan cross-sections on nuclear matter versus a nucleonic target is close to unity, consistent with experiment, but this result depends on using a self-consistent delta-hole model that includes both real and imaginary parts of the pion's and delta's self-energy related through a dispersion relation to assure causality.

## Method

The authors calculated the pion's in-medium self-energy using a self-consistent delta-hole model that includes particle-hole contributions, solving Schwinger-Dyson equations (without vertex corrections) for the delta and pion self-consistently while treating the nucleon in a mean-field approximation. The pion light-cone momentum distribution in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature was computed via two approaches: explicit summation of diagrams involving the dressed pion propagator with integration over initial and final nucleon momenta, and an alternative method based on the nuclear-matter response function. The resulting in-medium pion distribution was then used to compute the ratio of Drell-Yan cross-sections for proton-nucleus versus proton-deuteron scattering.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used: \(p_F = 0.256\) GeV (slightly below saturation density)
- Nucleon effective mass: \(M_* = 0.85\) GeV; energy shift: \(c_0 = 0.04\) GeV, giving Fermi energy \(E_F - M = -11\) MeV
- \(\pi NN\) coupling: \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.5\); monopole form-factor cut-off: \(\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 0.8\) GeV
- \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling: \(g_{\pi N\Delta} = 20\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (on-shell value 14 GeV\(^{-1}\)); exponential form-factor cut-off: \(\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta} = 0.38\) GeV (dipole form-factor cut-off: 0.51 GeV)
- Bare delta mass: 1.27 GeV
- For \(g'_{NN}\) values of 0, 0.4, and 0.6 (with \(g'_{N\Delta} = g'_{\Delta\Delta} = 0.3\)), increasing \(g'\) parameters reduces the enhancement of the pion distribution
- The momentum-conservation sum-rule is satisfied with accuracy better than 1% for \(g'\) parameters in the region from 0 to 0.4

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Ericson and Thomas (1980s) on medium modifications of the pion propagator in connection with the EMC effect, and by Bickerstaff et al. on pion-cloud enhancement. It addresses the apparent absence of pion-cloud enhancement in Drell-Yan scattering previously explained by Brown et al. in terms of partial restoration of chiral symmetry, and by Szczurek et al. who noted that correct normalization of the physical nucleon state reduces the enhancement. The self-consistent treatment of the delta-hole contribution including both real and imaginary parts (ensuring causality) differs from earlier crude approximations that included only the real part, and the results enable more reliable calculations of nuclear Drell-Yan ratios and sea-quark distributions in nuclei.
