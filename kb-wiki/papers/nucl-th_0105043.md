# Isospin Breaking in Neutron $β$-decay and SU(3) Violation in Semi-leptonic Hyperon Decays

> Present precision measurements of the neutron life time lead to a CKM matrix element $|V_{ud}|$ which is three standard deviations off the value inferred from heavy quark decays etc. We investigate the possibility whether isospin-breaking effects in the neutron-to-proton vector current transition matrix element $< p|V^+_0|n >=1+δg_V$ could eventually close this gap. For that we calculate in chiral perturbation theory the effect of pion and kaon loops on the matrix element $< p|V^+_0|n >$ taking into account the mass differences of the charged and neutral mesons. We find a negligibly small isospin-breaking effect of $δg_V \simeq -4 \cdot 10^{-5}$. The crucial quantity in the analysis of neutron beta-decay precision measurements is thus the radiative correction term $Δ_R$. Furthermore, we calculate in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory the SU(3) breaking effects on the vector transition charges of weak semi-leptonic hyperon decays. We find for these quantities channel-dependent relative deviations from the SU(3) limit which range from -10% to $+1%$.

## Key claim

Isospin-breaking effects in the neutron-to-proton vector current transition matrix element are too small (δg_V ≈ -4·10⁻⁵) to resolve the three-standard-deviation discrepancy between |V_ud| extracted from neutron beta-decay and the value inferred from CKM unitarity; the radiative correction term Δ_R is therefore the crucial quantity for precision neutron beta-decay analysis.

## Method

The isospin-breaking correction δg_V to the vector coupling constant g_V in neutron beta-decay is calculated using chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order, evaluating pion, kaon, and pion-Δ(1232) loop diagrams with isospin-violating meson propagators (using the charged-neutral mass splittings of pions and kaons) but isospin-symmetric interaction vertices. For semi-leptonic hyperon decays, the SU(3)-breaking corrections δ_V(B→B') to the vector transition charges at zero momentum transfer are computed in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory from (π,K,η)-loop diagrams, with SU(3) violation entering solely through the different masses of the pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons.

## Key numbers

- δg_V ≈ -4·10⁻⁵ (total isospin-breaking effect in neutron beta-decay vector current)
- δg_V^(π-loop) ≈ -3.2·10⁻⁵ (pion loop contribution)
- δg_V^(K-loop) ≈ -0.7·10⁻⁵ (kaon loop contribution)
- δg_V^(πΔ-loop) ≈ +0.4·10⁻⁵ (pion-Δ(1232) loop contribution)
- Δ_R = (2.46 ± 0.09)·10⁻² (radiative correction term for neutron beta-decay)
- δ_V(Λ→p) ≈ -10% (SU(3) breaking in Λ→p vector transition)
- δ_V(Σ⁰→p) = δ_V(Σ⁻→n) ≈ +1% (SU(3) breaking in Σ⁰→p and Σ⁻→n transitions)
- δ_V(Ξ⁻→Λ) ≈ -6% (SU(3) breaking in Ξ⁻→Λ transition)
- δ_V(Ξ⁰→Σ⁺) = δ_V(Ξ⁻→Σ⁰) ≈ -10% (SU(3) breaking in Ξ⁰→Σ⁺ and Ξ⁻→Σ⁰ transitions)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isospin violation in neutron beta-decay beyond the estimate of Behrends and Sirlin (1960), finding δg_V an order of magnitude larger than their ~10⁻⁶ estimate but still negligible for current precision. The result confirms that the CVC hypothesis g_V=1 holds to 10⁻⁴ accuracy, redirecting attention to the radiative correction Δ_R calculated by Sirlin (1978) as the key uncertainty. For hyperon decays, the SU(3)-breaking corrections (ranging from -10% to +1%) are sizeable and channel-dependent, improving upon the Ademollo-Gatto theorem (1964) by providing explicit numerical values; these should be incorporated in reanalyses of semi-leptonic hyperon decay data such as those by Bourquin et al. (1983).
