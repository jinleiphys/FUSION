# Photon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The production of single photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS, BNL RHIC and CERN LHC energies is re-examined in view of the recent studies of Aurenche et al which show that the rate of photon production from quark gluon plasma, evaluated at the order of two loops far exceeds the rates evaluated at one-loop level which have formed the basis of all the estimates of photons so far. We find that the production of photons from quark matter could easily out-shine those from the hadronic matter in certain ideal conditions.

# Photon Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
If a chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma is formed, the newly evaluated two-loop photon production rates (from bremsstrahlung and annihilation with scattering) allow quark matter to outshine hadronic matter over a wide transverse momentum window at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, but this conclusion depends on the assumption of chemical equilibration at the initial time.

## Method
The author integrates the two-loop photon emission rates from Aurenche et al. (bremsstrahlung and quark-antiquark annihilation with scattering) over the space-time history of a hydrodynamically expanding fireball, assuming a chemically and thermally equilibrated QGP formed at proper times of 1 fm/c (SPS) and 0.5 fm/c (RHIC, LHC). The evolution includes a mixed phase and hadronic phase with a hadronic equation of state containing all hadrons and resonances up to 2.5 GeV, and photon rates from hadronic matter are taken from a two-loop approximation of the photon self-energy including pi-rho interactions and the A1 resonance.

## Key numbers
- At SPS energies (central Pb+Pb, dN/dy = 825), quark matter outshines hadronic matter up to pT = 2 GeV when using the new two-loop rates.
- At RHIC energies (dN/dy = 1734), the window over which quark matter outshines hadronic matter extends to almost 3 GeV.
- At LHC energies (dN/dy = 5625), the window extends beyond 4 GeV, with quark matter photons potentially an order of magnitude larger than hadronic matter photons.
- The one-loop rate from Kapusta et al. is given by E dN/d^4x d^3k = (1/2pi^2) alpha alpha_s (sum e_f^2) T^2 e^{-E/T} ln(cE/alpha_s T) with c ≈ 0.23.
- For the two-loop bremsstrahlung rate, J_T ≈ 4.45 and J_L ≈ -4.26 for 2 flavours and 3 colours; for 3 flavours, J_T ≈ 4.80 and J_L ≈ -4.52.
- Phase transition temperature is taken as 160 MeV; freeze-out temperature is 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the one-loop photon rate calculations of Kapusta et al. (1991) and the soft-photon bremsstrahlung estimates of Pal et al. by incorporating the two-loop results of Aurenche et al., which show that bremsstrahlung and especially annihilation-with-scattering processes dominate hard photon emission. The results enable experimental searches at SPS (WA98), RHIC, and LHC to target specific pT windows where quark matter photons may dominate, and suggest that pre-equilibrium contributions from parton cascade models (as in the work of the PCM group) could further widen these windows. The author notes that chemical non-equilibration would reduce the rates, but higher temperatures from parton cascade models and pre-equilibrium contributions may offset this loss.
