# Nuclear condensation and the equation of state of nuclear matter

> The isothermal compression of a dilute nucleonic gas invoking cluster degrees of freedom is studied in an equilibrium statistical model; this clusterized system is found to be more stable than the pure nucleonic system. The equation of state (EoS) of this matter shows features qualitatively very similar to the one obtained from pure nucleonic gas. In the isothermal compression process, there is a sudden enhancement of clusterization at a transition density rendering features analogous to the gas-liquid phase transition in normal dilute nucleonic matter. Different observables like the caloric curves, heat capacity, isospin distillation, etc. are studied in both the models. Possible changes in the observables due to recently indicated medium modifications in the symmetry energy are also investigated.

# Nuclear condensation and the equation of state of nuclear matter

## Key claim

The isothermal compression of dilute nucleonic gas in a nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) model, which includes cluster degrees of freedom, produces an equation of state qualitatively similar to that from a pure nucleonic (mean-field) model, but the clusterized system is thermodynamically more stable; the transition to bulk clusterization in the NSE model for asymmetric matter occurs over a temperature interval implying a continuous transition, whereas for symmetric matter the transition is first order at constant temperature.

## Method

The authors employ a grand-canonical nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) model treating the interacting dilute nucleon gas as a noninteracting mixture of nucleons and nucleonic clusters in thermal and chemical equilibrium, with fragment number densities computed from Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distributions. Binding energies are taken from the Danielewicz mass formula (with the Myers-Swiatecki formula used for comparison), and the internal free energy of excited fragments is calculated in the Fermi gas approximation with a level density parameter of A/16. The pressure is obtained from the total free energy as the sum of partial pressures of all species, and the system is studied under isothermal compression and isochoric cooling for both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, with maximum cluster size limited to A=5000.

## Key numbers

- Volume energy coefficient: a_v = 15.6163 MeV
- Volume symmetry energy coefficient: α = 32.6655 MeV
- Surface energy coefficient for symmetric matter at T=0: σ(0,0) = 17.9878 MeV
- Critical temperature of symmetric nuclear matter: T_c = 14.61 MeV (from SkM* effective interaction)
- Pairing coefficient: a_p = 10.8714 MeV (set to zero for NSE calculations as pairing vanishes above T ~ 1 MeV)
- Maximum cluster size used in calculations: A_f^{max} = 5000 (convergence achieved at ~5000)
- Densities explored: up to ρ ~ ρ_0/8 (where ρ_0 is normal nuclear matter density)
- Symmetry energy reduction tested: 40% reduction at T = 5 MeV
- Boiling temperature found to be rather insensitive to neutron-proton asymmetry, with nominal decrease with X_t

## Context

This work extends the mean-field (NM) model studies of gas-liquid phase transitions in nuclear matter by Müller and Serot (1995) and Sil et al. (2001) by incorporating cluster degrees of freedom via the NSE approach, which has been used in understanding nuclear multifragmentation data and astrophysical nucleosynthesis by Meyer (1993), Ishizuka et al. (2003), and Botvina et al. (2002). The paper enables comparison of thermodynamic observables (caloric curves, heat capacities, isospin distillation) between the clusterized and pure nucleonic descriptions, and tests sensitivity to medium modifications of the symmetry energy as suggested by experimental analyses from Lefevre et al. (2005) and Iglio et al. (2006).
