# Comment on "Nucleon form factors and a nonpointlike diquark"

> Authors of Phys. Rev. C 60, 062201 (1999) presented a calculation of the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon using a diquark ansatz in the relativistic three-quark Faddeev equations. In this Comment it is pointed out that the calculations of these form factors stem from a three-quark bound state current that contains overcounted contributions. The corrected expression for the three-quark bound state current is derived.

## Key claim

The calculation of nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Bloch et al. (1999) using a diquark ansatz in relativistic three-quark Faddeev equations is based on a three-quark bound state current that contains overcounted contributions; the correct impulse approximation consists of only three of the five terms they computed, not all five.

## Method

The authors apply the gauging of equations method to the three-quark Faddeev integral equations, deriving the correct expression for the bound state electromagnetic current. They decompose the overcounted one-body current into five terms using the Faddeev components of the wave function and the separable (diquark) approximation for the two-body t-matrix, then compare these terms with the subtraction term that removes overcounting. The correct impulse approximation is obtained by retaining only the three terms that survive after cancellation.

## Key numbers

* The correct impulse approximation for the nucleon current in the diquark model consists of the sum of three diagrams (labeled $\Lambda^2_\mu$, $\Lambda^3_\mu$, and $\Lambda^5_\mu$ in Bloch et al.), not the sum of all five $\Lambda^i_\mu$ terms.
* The equality $\Lambda^1_\mu = \Lambda^5_\mu$ is derived from the Faddeev equations and symmetry properties, which the authors note is not reflected in the numerical results of Table II of Bloch et al. (1999).

## Context

This Comment corrects the work of Bloch et al. (1999) on nucleon form factors calculated using a diquark ansatz in four-dimensional Faddeev equations. It extends the general formalism for avoiding overcounting in electromagnetic currents of few-body systems developed by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1999) in two companion papers (Phys. Rev. C 60, 044003 and 044004). The corrected expression enables future calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors within the diquark model to use only the three valid impulse-approximation diagrams rather than the five originally employed.
