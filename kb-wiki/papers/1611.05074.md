# Dynamics of semi-superfluid fluxtubes in color-flavor locked quark matter

> At very high densities, as for example in the core of a neutron star, matter may appear in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, which is a superfluid. This phase features topologically stable vortex solutions, which arise in a spinning superfluid as localized configurations carrying quanta of angular momentum. Despite the topological stability of these vortices they are not the lowest energy state of the system at neutron star densities and decay into triplets of semi-superfluid fluxtubes. In these proceedings we report on the progress of our numerical study in the Ginzburg-Landau approach, where we investigate lattices of semi-superfluid fluxtubes. The fluxtubes are obtained through controlled decay of global vortex configurations in the presence of a gauge field. Understanding the dynamics of semi-superfluid string configurations is important in the context of angular momentum transfer from a quark matter core of a neutron star beyond the core boundary, since not vortex-, but fluxtube pinning seems to be the relevant mechanism in this scenario.

## Key claim

Global superfluid vortices in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of quark matter, despite being topologically stable, are energetically unstable at neutron star densities and decay into triplets of semi-superfluid fluxtubes; the authors present numerical simulations of this decay process in the Ginzburg-Landau approach, but note that the results are preliminary and the simulations have not yet reached fully equilibrated configurations.

## Method

The authors use a Ginzburg-Landau effective theory with a complex scalar field Phi (the CFL condensate) coupled to a gauge field, with condensate self-couplings lambda1 and lambda2 and gauge coupling g. They initialize the system with multiple global vortices (up to 19) on a 350x350 lattice, set the gauge field to zero initially, add small random perturbations to trigger the instability, and evolve the system in time using both open and fixed boundary conditions. The decay of each global vortex into three semi-superfluid fluxtubes is observed through the time evolution of the energy density.

## Key numbers

- The energy density away from the core of a global vortex is epsilon_sf = 3 * bar_phi^2 / r^2, while for a semi-superfluid fluxtube it is epsilon_ssft = (1/3) * bar_phi^2 / r^2, making the triplet of fluxtubes lower in energy by a factor of three.
- The simulation of 19 global vortices (decaying into 57 semi-superfluid fluxtubes) was performed on a 350x350 lattice.
- Snapshots of the time evolution are shown at t=0, t=240, t=480, and t=720 (in simulation time units).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of global vortex dynamics without gauge fields (Alford 2016) by including full gauge dynamics, allowing simulation of semi-superfluid fluxtubes. It builds on theoretical work showing that global vortices in CFL matter are unstable (Nakano 2007, Eto 2013, Alford 2016) and on weak-coupling extrapolations of coupling parameters relevant for neutron star cores (Iida 2000, Giannakis 2001). The ultimate goal is to understand pinning phenomena in superfluid quark matter cores of neutron stars, which could provide explanations for neutron star glitches. The authors plan to extend this work to asymmetric quark masses (Eto 2009) and to implement GPU-based computations for larger lattices.
