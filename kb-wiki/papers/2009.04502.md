# $J/ψ$-$p$ Scattering Length from the Total and Differential Photoproduction Cross Sections

> The $J/ψ$-$p$ scattering length, $α_{J/ψp}$, can be extracted from the $J/ψ$ photoproduction cross section near threshold using the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model to relate the reaction $γp \to J/ψp$ to $J/ψp \to J/ψp$. Such estimates based on experimental data result in values for $|α_{J/ψp}|$, which are much lower than most of the theoretical predictions. In this work, we study the relations between the different results, depending on the use of the total or the differential cross sections, and the method of extrapolating the data to threshold in the case of a low-statistics data sample, such as the near threshold $J/ψ$ photoproduction dataset. We estimate a range for $|α_{J/ψp}|$ of $0.003$ to $0.025$~fm as extracted from experimental data within the VMD model and discuss possible reasons for such lower values compared to the theoretical results.

## Key claim

The paper estimates a range for the absolute value of the $J/\psi$-$p$ scattering length, $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}|$, of 0.003 to 0.025 fm as extracted from experimental data within the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model, noting that these values are much lower than most theoretical predictions and that the applicability of the VMD model near threshold is questionable because the $J/\psi$ is not fully formed.

## Method

The authors extract the $J/\psi$-$p$ scattering length from $J/\psi$ photoproduction cross sections near threshold using the VMD model to relate $\gamma p \to J/\psi p$ to $J/\psi p \to J/\psi p$. They compare results from total cross section data (GlueX Collaboration) fitted with an odd-power polynomial or a two- and three-gluon exchange model, and from differential cross section data (GlueX and SLAC) extrapolated to threshold either at $t = t_{thr}$ or at the non-physical point $t = 0$, assuming energy independence of the differential cross section near threshold.

## Key numbers

*   $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (3.08 \pm 0.55) \times 10^{-3}$ fm from GlueX total cross section data using an odd-power polynomial fit (Strakovsky et al.).
*   $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (3.64 \pm 0.26) \times 10^{-3}$ fm from GlueX total cross section data using a three-gluon exchange model (this work).
*   $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (3.83 \pm 0.98) \times 10^{-3}$ fm from GlueX differential cross section data at 10.7 GeV extrapolated to $t = t_{thr}$ assuming energy independence (this work).
*   $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (46 \pm 5) \times 10^{-3}$ fm from SLAC differential cross section data above 13 GeV extrapolated to $t = 0$ via a global fit (Vanderhaeghen et al.).
*   $|\alpha_{J/\psi p}| = (24.5 \pm 3.9) \times 10^{-3}$ fm from GlueX differential cross section data at 10.7 GeV extrapolated to $t = 0$ assuming energy independence (this work).
*   The slope of the differential cross section fit to GlueX data is $b = (1.67 \pm 0.35)$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Strakovsky et al. (2020) which extracted the scattering length from GlueX total cross section data, and contrasts it with the method of Vanderhaeghen et al. which used SLAC differential cross sections extrapolated to $t = 0$. The paper reconciles the order-of-magnitude difference between these results by showing they correspond to different kinematic points ($t = t_{thr}$ vs. $t = 0$) and explains the low experimental values relative to most theoretical predictions (e.g., lattice QCD results from Sugiura (2019) giving 200-700 $\times 10^{-3}$ fm) by invoking the "young" $J/\psi$ effect where the charmonium has not fully formed near threshold. The results enable future experimental studies of the energy dependence of the differential cross section near threshold to test the assumption of energy independence used in the extrapolation.
