# Quantum versus classical statistical dynamics of an ultracold Bose gas

> We investigate the conditions under which quantum fluctuations are relevant for the quantitative interpretation of experiments with ultracold Bose gases. This requires to go beyond the description in terms of the Gross-Pitaevskii and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field theories, which can be obtained as classical (statistical) field-theory approximations of the quantum many-body problem. We employ functional-integral techniques based on the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action. The role of quantum fluctuations is studied within the nonperturbative 2PI 1/N expansion to next-to-leading order. At this accuracy level memory-integrals enter the dynamic equations, which differ for quantum and classical statistical descriptions. This can be used to obtain a 'classicality' condition for the many-body dynamics. We exemplify this condition by studying the nonequilibrium evolution of a 1D Bose gas of sodium atoms, and discuss some distinctive properties of quantum versus classical statistical dynamics.

## Key claim

Quantum fluctuations become relevant for ultracold Bose gas dynamics when the scattering length is large or the density is low, violating a reparametrization invariance that holds for classical statistical descriptions; the authors derive a "classicality" condition that is not based on thermal equilibrium assumptions and applies to far-from-equilibrium dynamics.

## Method

The authors employ functional-integral techniques based on the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action, using a nonperturbative 1/N expansion to next-to-leading order for a non-relativistic complex scalar field theory. They compare the quantum and classical statistical dynamic equations, which differ only by specific vertex terms, and use this difference to derive a classicality condition. The condition is exemplified by numerically studying the nonequilibrium evolution of a homogeneous one-dimensional Bose gas of sodium atoms starting from a far-from-equilibrium initial state.

## Key numbers

- The diluteness parameter \(a^3 N/V\) can become larger than one near Feshbach resonances, where the s-wave scattering length \(a\) exceeds the mean interatomic distance \((N/V)^{-1/3}\).
- For a one-dimensional trap, the dimensionless interaction parameter \(\gamma = g_\mathrm{1D} m V/(\hbar^2 N)\) defines the Tonks-Girardeau regime when \(\gamma \gg 1\), with \(g_\mathrm{1D}=2\hbar^2 a/(m l_\perp^2)\) for a cylindrical trap with transverse harmonic oscillator length \(l_\perp\).
- The decay of correlations with the initial state happens faster in the classical statistical theory for the one-dimensional Bose gas; including quantum corrections the system remembers longer the details about the initial conditions.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of quantum and classical statistical nonequilibrium dynamics from relativistic scalar field theory (Aarts 2002, Berges 2002) to non-relativistic Bose gases with a nonzero macroscopic field. It goes beyond the Gross-Pitaevskii equation and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field theories, which are classical statistical approximations. The derived classicality condition enables experimenters to identify signatures of quantum fluctuations when preparing and probing ultracold gas dynamics, and the approach can be applied to study thermalization and far-from-equilibrium dynamics in strongly interacting systems.
