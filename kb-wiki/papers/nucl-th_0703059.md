# Symplectic Symmetry and the Ab Initio No-Core Shell Model

> The symplectic symmetry of eigenstates for the $0^+_{gs}$ in $^{16}$O and the $0^+_{gs}$ and lowest $2^+$ and $4^+$ configurations of $^{12}$C that are well-converged within the framework of the no-core shell model with the JISP16 realistic interaction is examined. These states are found to project at the 85-90% level onto very few symplectic representations including the most deformed configuration, which confirms the importance of a symplectic no-core shell model and reaffirms the relevance of the Elliott $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ model upon which the symplectic scheme is built.

# Symplectic Symmetry and the Ab Initio No-Core Shell Model

## Key claim
The ground state of 16O and the lowest 0+, 2+, and 4+ states in 12C, calculated within the no-core shell model using the JISP16 realistic interaction, project at the 85-90% level onto very few symplectic Sp(3,R) irreducible representations, with the dominant representation carrying close to 70% of the wavefunction for 12C and 75% for 16O at 15 MeV oscillator strength.

## Method
The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 12C and 16O using the JISP16 realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, with model spaces up to 6 hbar-Omega (Nmax=6). They constructed all 0p-0h and 2p-2h symplectic Sp(3,R) bandheads and generated their irreducible representations up to Nmax=6, then computed overlaps between these symplectic basis states and the converged NCSM eigenstates to determine the probability distribution across symplectic irreps.

## Key numbers
- 12C 0+_gs: 85.78% of the NCSM wavefunction projects onto the dominant 0p-0h and 2p-2h Sp(3,R) irreps at hbar-Omega=15 MeV, with the leading (0 4) S=0 irrep carrying 64.84%
- 12C 2+_1: 84.81% projects onto the same symplectic irreps, with the (0 4) S=0 irrep carrying 64.95%
- 12C 4+_1: 82.59% projects onto the same symplectic irreps, with the (0 4) S=0 irrep carrying 68.78%
- 16O 0+_gs: 84.85% projects onto the dominant symplectic irreps, with the (0 0) S=0 irrep carrying 75.02% at hbar-Omega=15 MeV
- For 12C 0+_gs at hbar-Omega=11 MeV and Nmax=6, the total overlap reaches 90%
- The symplectic basis dimension for 12C at Nmax=6 is 8.5 x 10^-3% of the full NCSM space (approximately 10^2 versus 10^7 basis states)
- B(E2; 2+_1 -> 0+_gs) values from the symplectic reconstruction range from 101% to 107% of the NCSM results

## Context
This work extends the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) and the symplectic shell model of Rosensteel and Rowe (1980) by demonstrating that ab initio NCSM calculations with realistic interactions exhibit the same Sp(3,R) symmetry previously only studied with phenomenological interactions. The results confirm the validity of the symplectic approach for realistic nuclear forces and suggest that a symplectic extension of the NCSM (Sp-NCSM) could enable calculations in much larger model spaces, potentially reaching convergence for B(E2) values without effective charges and enabling studies of heavier nuclei and cluster phenomena.
