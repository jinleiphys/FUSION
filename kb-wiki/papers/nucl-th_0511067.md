# Relativistic effects in neutrino-Fermi gas interactions

> We study neutrino interactions in a hadron gas within a relativistic framework. The hadron matter is described by a non-interacting Fermi gas in beta equilibrium. We show that the introduction of relativistic effects causes a sizable enhancement of the neutrino-scattering cross sections.

## Key claim

The introduction of relativistic effects in the description of a non-interacting Fermi gas in beta equilibrium causes a sizable enhancement of neutrino-scattering cross sections, with the larger differences arising from the relativistic description of the Fermi distribution rather than from relativistic dynamics in the neutrino-nucleon interaction vertex.

## Method

The authors computed neutrino interaction cross sections in a hadron gas described by a non-interacting Fermi gas in beta equilibrium, using first-order perturbation theory with the full hadron vertex function from Llewellyn-Smith. The differential cross section was calculated by integrating over Fermi distributions for initial-state nucleons and including Pauli blocking for final-state particles, with the normalization factor determined to give the scattering cross section per nucleon. Relativistic and non-relativistic Fermi distributions were compared by using either \(E(p)=\sqrt{p^2+m^2}\) or \(E(p)=p^2/2m\) in the distribution function.

## Key numbers

- At \(kT=10\) MeV and density \(n=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the relativistic Fermi distribution gives a slightly larger weight at higher momenta compared to the non-relativistic one, especially affecting the tail of the distribution.
- The incident neutrino energy was taken to be three times the temperature of the nucleon matter for the mean free path calculations shown in Fig. 1.
- Relativistic opacities (inverse mean free path) are considerably smaller than the non-relativistic results of Reddy (Ref. [reddy]), meaning relativistic cross sections are generally larger.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino scattering in nucleon matter at supra-nuclear densities by Reddy, Burrows, and Horowitz, which investigated nuclear dynamics and weak nucleon current effects. The paper shows that relativistic corrections to the Fermi gas description, previously neglected in non-relativistic treatments, significantly enhance neutrino opacities in core-collapse supernova and neutron star cooling contexts. The authors note that further work on relativistic effects and implementation of correlations in the cross section calculation is in progress (Vantournhout).
