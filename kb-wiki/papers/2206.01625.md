# Correcting the $B_A$ coalescence factor at GSI-HADES and RHIC-BES energies

> We investigate the coalescence factors $B_2$ and $B_3$ at low collision energies ($\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}<6$ GeV) with special focus on the HADES and RHIC-BES experiments. It is shown that, in order to properly interpret the coalescence factors $B_A$, two important corrections are necessary: I) $B_2$ has to be calculated using the proton $\times$ neutron yields in the denominator, instead of the square of the proton yield, and II) the primordial proton (neutron) densities have to be used for the normalization and not the final state (free) protons (neutrons). Both effects lead to a drastic reduction of $B_2$ and $B_3$ at low energies. This reduction decreases the discrepancy between the volumes extracted from HBT measurements and the volumes extracted from the coalescence factor ($V\propto 1/B_2$). While at HADES and low RHIC-BES energies these corrections are substantial, they become irrelevant above $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}>6$ GeV. The proposed correction method is model independent and is only based on the measurement of protons, clusters and charged pions.

## Key claim

The apparent increase of the coalescence factors \(B_2\) and \(B_3\) at low collision energies (\(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}<6\) GeV) is an artifact of using final-state proton distributions and using the proton yield as a proxy for the neutron yield; when corrected to primordial (pre-coalescence) proton and neutron densities, the \(B_A\) values decrease with decreasing energy and become consistent with HBT volume measurements.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (v3.5, cascade mode) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 1.23\(A\) GeV upward. They reconstructed the primordial proton rapidity and transverse-momentum distributions by adding back the protons bound in measured clusters (deuterons, tritons, \(^3\)He) to the final-state free proton distributions. The primordial neutron distributions were estimated from the reconstructed primordial proton distributions using an isospin correction factor \(\delta_{\mathrm{iso}}^{\mathrm{prim}}\) derived from the initial gold nuclei asymmetry (\(N_{\mathrm{Au}}/Z_{\mathrm{Au}}=1.49\)) and the measured charged-pion difference \(\Delta\pi = N_{\pi^-} - N_{\pi^+}\). The corrected \(B_2\) and \(B_3\) were then computed using these primordial nucleon distributions instead of the final-state ones.

## Key numbers

* At \(E_\mathrm{beam}=1.23A\) GeV, only 60% of protons are observed as free protons in the final state; the rest are bound in clusters.
* The isospin correction factor at 1.23\(A\) GeV evaluates to \(\delta_{\mathrm{iso}}^{\mathrm{prim}}=1.32\), between the initial gold asymmetry of 1.49 and complete isospin equilibration (1.0).
* At \(p_\mathrm{T}/A=0\) GeV for central Au+Au at 1.23\(A\) GeV, the fully corrected \(B_2\) (using primordial protons and neutrons) is reduced by a factor of approximately 4 compared to the uncorrected value using final-state protons squared.
* The corrections are substantial for \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}<6\) GeV and become irrelevant above \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}>6\) GeV.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between coalescence factors \(B_A\) measured at low energies (FOPI, HADES, low RHIC-BES) and the source volumes extracted from HBT measurements by STAR (2014). Prior analyses (e.g., Braun-Munzinger 2018) had shown that \(B_2\) increased strongly at low energies while HBT volumes suggested a decrease. The correction method is model-independent and enables direct comparison of coalescence data across all collision energies, using only measured protons, clusters, and charged pions.
