# Domain walls in neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids in neutron stars

> We work out domain walls in neutron $^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids realized in the core of neutron stars. Adopting the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory as a bosonic low-energy effective theory, we consider configurations of domain walls interpolating ground states, i.e., the uniaxial nematic (UN), D$_{2}$-biaxial nematic (D$_{2}$-BN), and D$_{4}$-biaxial nematic (D$_{4}$-BN) phases in the presence of zero, small and large magnetic fields, respectively. We solve the Euler-Lagrange equation from the GL free energy density, and calculate surface energy densities of the domain walls. We find that one extra Nambu-Goldstone mode is localized in the vicinity of a domain wall in the UN phase while a U(1) symmetry restores in the vicinity of one type of domain wall in the D$_{2}$-BN phase and all domain walls in the D$_{4}$-BN phase. Considering a pile of domain walls in the neutron stars, we find that the most stable configurations are domain walls perpendicular to the magnetic fields piled up in the direction along the magnetic fields in the D$_{2}$-BN and D$_{4}$-BN phases. We estimate the energy released from the deconstruction of the domain walls in the edge of a neutron star, and show that it can reach an astrophysical scale such as glitches in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Domain walls in neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids can release energy on an astrophysical scale (such as glitches in neutron stars) when they are deconstructed at the edge of a neutron star, but this estimate depends on the assumption that the domain walls are piled up in the direction along the magnetic field, which is the most stable configuration in the D\(_{2}\)-BN and D\(_{4}\)-BN phases.

## Method

The authors adopt the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory as a bosonic low-energy effective theory for neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids, expanding the free energy up to eighth order in the order parameter and including magnetic field terms up to fourth order. They solve the Euler-Lagrange equation derived from the GL free energy density for one-dimensional domain wall configurations (kinks) interpolating between degenerate ground states (UN, D\(_{2}\)-BN, D\(_{4}\)-BN phases), assuming the order parameter is diagonal and the domain wall surface is flat. The surface energy densities are calculated for different domain wall orientations relative to the applied magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The surface energy density of domain walls is calculated for the UN, D\(_{2}\)-BN, and D\(_{4}\)-BN phases; the most stable configuration is domain walls perpendicular to the magnetic field piled up along the field direction in the D\(_{2}\)-BN and D\(_{4}\)-BN phases.
- The energy released from deconstruction of domain walls at the edge of a neutron star can reach an astrophysical scale comparable to glitches in neutron stars (no explicit numerical value for the energy is given in the provided text).
- The GL coefficients include the zeta functions \(\zeta(3)\), \(\zeta(5)\), and \(\zeta(7)\); for example, \(\beta^{(0)} = \frac{7\,\zeta(3)N(0)p_{F}^{4}}{60\,(\pi T_{c0})^{2}}\) and \(\delta^{(0)} = \frac{127\,\zeta(7)N(0)p_{F}^{8}}{387072\,(\pi T_{c0})^{6}}\).
- The magnetic moment of a neutron is \(\gamma_{n}=1.2 \times 10^{-13}\) MeV/T (in natural units).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of topological defects in neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids, such as spontaneously magnetized vortices (Muzikar 1980, Sauls 1982), half-quantized non-Abelian vortices (Masuda 2016), and topological defects on boundaries (Yasui 2019). It also draws analogy to domain walls (textures) in \(^{3}\)He superfluidity (PhysRevB.14.118, PhysRevB.16.182, PhysRevB.17.1088). The results enable future modeling of how domain wall piles could produce observable astrophysical signals like pulsar glitches, and they provide a framework for studying bosonic collective modes and phase transitions in neutron star cores.
