# Cherenkov Glue in Opaque Nuclear Medium

> The spectrum of Cherenkov gluons is calculated within the framework of in-medium QCD. It is compared with experimental data on the double-humped structure around the away-side jet obtained at RHIC. The values of the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear permittivity are obtained from these fits. It is shown that accounting for an additional smearing due to resonance-like production of final hadrons allows to achieve an agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The double-humped structure observed in away-side azimuthal correlations at RHIC can be reproduced by a model of Cherenkov gluon radiation in an opaque nuclear medium, but only when an additional ad-hoc transverse smearing of the Cherenkov gluons during hadronization is included to fill the "dead zones" that would otherwise appear at angles larger than π/2 relative to the away-side jet.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte-Carlo simulation using PYTHIA to generate initial hard parton-parton scattering events at RHIC energies, selecting events where the trigger jet produced a pion within specific pseudorapidity and transverse momentum intervals for STAR and PHENIX. They then generated Cherenkov gluon radiation from the away-side gluon jet using a Breit-Wigner-like angular spectrum derived from in-medium QCD with a complex chromodielectric permittivity, and converted the gluons into hadrons using a fragmentation function that included a Gaussian transverse smearing parameter Δ⊥. The three free parameters, the real part ε₁ and imaginary part ε₂ of the permittivity, and the transverse smearing scale Δ⊥, were fitted to reproduce the experimental angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- For STAR data (trigger p⊥ 3–4 GeV, associated p⊥ 1–2 GeV): ε₁ = 5.4, ε₂ = 0.7, Δ⊥ = 0.7 GeV, θ_max = 1.04 rad.
- For PHENIX data (trigger p⊥ 2–3 GeV, associated p⊥ 2–3 GeV): ε₁ = 9.0, ε₂ = 2.0, Δ⊥ = 1.1 GeV, θ_max = 1.27 rad.
- The ratio (ε₂/ε₁)² is less than or approximately 0.05 for both fits.
- The maximum gluon energy for Cherenkov emission was set to ω_max = 3.5 GeV.
- The transverse smearing rate per fermi is approximately Δ⊥²/fm ∼ 0.2 GeV²/fm, assuming a typical path length L ≃ 5 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of Cherenkov gluon radiation in a transparent medium (Dremin 1979, 1981; Tamm and Frank 1937) by incorporating the opacity of the nuclear medium through a complex permittivity, following the in-medium QCD framework of MQCD. It provides an alternative to the Mach-cone interpretation of the double-humped structure (Mishra and Ranjan 2005; Neufeld et al. 2008), which has been challenged by evidence for a strong diffusion wake (Betty et al. 2008). The fitted permittivity values, with ε₁ significantly larger than 1, suggest the medium behaves more like a liquid than a gas, and the model enables future quantitative studies of the dependence on transverse momentum and the central domain of the angular distribution.
