# Excited-State-Specific Kohn-Sham Formalism for the Asymmetric Hubbard Dimer

> Building on our recent study [https://doi.org/10.1021/acs.jpclett.3c02052, J. Phys. Chem. Lett. 14, 8780 (2023)], we explore the generalization of the ground-state Kohn-Sham (KS) formalism of density-functional theory (DFT) to the (singlet) excited states of the asymmetric Hubbard dimer at half-filling. While we found that the KS-DFT framework can be straightforwardly generalized to the highest-lying doubly-excited state, the treatment of the first excited state presents significant challenges. Specifically, using a density-fixed adiabatic connection, we show that the density of the first excited state lacks non-interacting $v$-representability. However, by employing an analytic continuation of the adiabatic path, we demonstrate that the density of the first excited state can be generated by a complex-valued external potential in the non-interacting case. More practically, by performing state-specific KS calculations with exact and approximate correlation functionals -- each state possessing a distinct correlation functional -- we observe that spurious stationary solutions of the KS equations may arise due to the approximate nature of the functional.

## Key claim

While the Kohn-Sham (KS) framework of density-functional theory can be straightforwardly generalized to the highest-lying doubly-excited state of the asymmetric Hubbard dimer, the first excited state lacks non-interacting v-representability under a density-fixed adiabatic connection, though its density can be generated by a complex-valued external potential in the non-interacting case via analytic continuation.

## Method

The authors study the asymmetric Hubbard dimer at half-filling with two electrons, using Gorling's stationary principle applied to Lieb's formulation to construct state-specific universal functionals F^(m)(ρ) for each singlet state. They perform a density-fixed adiabatic connection by linearly scaling the electron-electron interaction with coupling constant λ, and investigate whether each interacting state can be connected to a non-interacting KS reference state at λ=0. For the first excited state, they extend the adiabatic path into the complex plane by allowing complex-valued external potentials and using the c-product inner product for complex symmetric operators.

## Key numbers

- The Hubbard dimer Hamiltonian has three singlet eigenvalues and eigenvectors, labeled E^(m) and Ψ^(m), with site-occupation difference Δn ranging from -2 to 2 (reduced quantity ρ = Δn/2 ranges from -1 to 1)
- For U=1 and ρ=1/4, the ground state (m=0) and doubly-excited state (m=2) are smoothly connected to their KS states for all λ, while the two branches of the first excited state (1∪ and 1∩) merge at a critical value λ = λ_c and disappear beyond this point
- The hopping parameter t is set to t=1/2 throughout the paper

## Context

This work builds on the authors' previous study (GiaLoo, J. Phys. Chem. Lett. 2023) which found that distinct universal functionals exist for each singlet state of the asymmetric Hubbard dimer. It extends the ground-state KS-DFT formalism to excited states following the stationarity principle developed by Gorling (1996, 1999) and addresses the v-representability problem for excited states, complementing recent efforts by Gould, Pittalis and collaborators (GX24 functional, 2024) and Ayers and Yang (2024) on state-specific functionals. The findings highlight the need for developing dedicated state-specific density-functional approximations for excited-state calculations.
