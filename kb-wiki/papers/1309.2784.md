# New approach to determine proton-nucleus interactions from experimental bremsstrahlung data

> A new approach is presented to determine the proton-nucleus interactions from the analysis of the accompanying photon bremsstrahlung. We study the scattering of $p + ^{208}{\rm Pb}$ at the proton incident energies of 140 and 145~MeV, and the scattering of $p + ^{12}{\rm C}$, $p + ^{58}{\rm Ni}$, $p + ^{107}{\rm Ag}$ and $p + ^{197}{\rm Au}$ at the proton incident energy of 190~MeV. The model determines contributions of the coherent emission (formed by an interaction between the scattering proton and nucleus as a whole without the internal many-nucleon structure), incoherent emission (formed by interactions between the scattering proton and nucleus with the internal many-nucleon structure), and transition between them in dependence on the photon energy. The radius-parameter of the proton-nucleus potential for these reactions is extracted from the experimental bremsstrahlung data analysis. We explain the hump-shaped plateau in the intermediate- and high-energy regions of the spectra by the essential presence of the incoherent emission, while at low energies the coherent emission predominates which produces the logarithmic shape spectrum. We provide our predictions (in absolute scale) for the angular distribution of the bremsstrahlung photons in order to test our model, results and analysis in further experiments.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new approach to extract the radius-parameter of the proton-nucleus potential from experimental bremsstrahlung data, but the extraction relies on the assumption that the most intensive photon emission is caused by the relative motion of the proton with respect to the nucleus, while contributions from internal nucleon motions inside the nucleus are treated as corrections.

## Method
The authors develop a generalized Pauli equation for a system of A+1 nucleons describing proton-nucleus scattering, then separate the Hamiltonian into coherent emission (proton interacting with the nucleus as a whole) and incoherent emission (proton interacting with individual nucleons inside the nucleus). They calculate the matrix element for photon emission using a wave function that factorizes into center-of-mass motion, relative proton-nucleus motion, and a many-nucleon Slater determinant for the internal nuclear structure. The radius-parameter of the proton-nucleus potential is then extracted by fitting the calculated bremsstrahlung spectra to experimental data for p + 208Pb at 140 and 145 MeV, and for p + 12C, p + 58Ni, p + 107Ag, and p + 197Au at 190 MeV.

## Key numbers

## Context
This work extends the bremsstrahlung studies of Edington (1966) and Clayton (1992) who observed a hump-shaped plateau in p + 208Pb spectra, and the TAPS collaboration measurements by Goethem (2002) that confirmed this plateau. The approach builds on the theoretical framework of Nakayama (1986, 1989) who emphasized the role of individual nucleon-nucleon interactions in proton-nucleus bremsstrahlung, and on the authors' previous quantum developments in Maydanyuk (2012). The method enables extraction of proton-nucleus potential parameters from bremsstrahlung data, which could provide an alternative to traditional optical model analyses that suffer from parameter ambiguities.
