# Constraining phases of quark matter with studies of r-mode damping in neutron stars

> The r-mode instability in rotating compact stars is used to constrain the phase of matter at high density. The color-flavor-locked phase with kaon condensation (CFL-K0) and without (CFL) is considered in the temperature range 10^8K < T <10^{11} K. While the bulk viscosity in either phase is only effective at damping the r-mode at temperatures T > 10^{11} K, the shear viscosity in the CFL-K0 phase is the only effective damping agent all the way down to temperatures T > 10^8 K characteristic of cooling neutron stars. However, it cannot keep the star from becoming unstable to gravitational wave emission for rotation frequencies f ~ 56-11 Hz at T ~ 10^8-10^9 K. Stars composed almost entirely of CFL or CFL-K0 matter are ruled out by observation of rapidly rotating neutron stars, indicating that dissipation at the quark-hadron interface or nuclear crust interface must play a key role in damping the instability.

# Constraining phases of quark matter with studies of r-mode damping in neutron stars

## Key claim

Pure quark stars composed entirely of the color-flavor-locked phase with kaon condensation (CFL-K0) or without (CFL) are ruled out by observations of rapidly rotating neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs), even though the shear viscosity from kaons in the CFL-K0 phase provides effective damping down to temperatures as low as 10^8 K. Dissipation at the quark-hadron interface or nuclear crust interface must play a key role in damping the instability, as the internal viscous mechanisms alone cannot keep the star stable at observed rotation frequencies.

## Method

The authors computed the r-mode instability critical frequency curve for neutron stars composed of CFL-K0 quark matter by evaluating the gravitational radiation growth timescale and the damping timescales from bulk viscosity, shear viscosity (from both phonons and kaons), and mutual friction. They used the equation of state for CFL-K0 matter including terms up to O(m_s^4), with a superconducting gap Delta = 100 MeV and MIT Bag constant B = 80 MeV/fm^3, and solved the criterion 1/tau_total = 0 at the critical rotation frequency Omega_c for temperatures in the range 10^8 K to 10^11 K.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity damping timescale for the CFL-K0 phase at T = 10^9 K is tau_eta = 4.9 x 10^13 s, compared to the gravitational wave growth timescale tau_GW = -7.6 x 10^2 s (for a 1.4 solar mass star with central density 2.526 times nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 2.54 x 10^14 g/cm^3).
- The bulk viscosity damping timescale for CFL-K0 at T = 10^9 K is tau_zeta = 9.6 x 10^22 s, far longer than the gravitational wave growth timescale.
- The critical rotation frequency for CFL-K0 stars falls in the range nu approximately 56-11 Hz at temperatures T approximately 10^8-10^9 K, well below the observed LMXB spin rates of 300-700 Hz.
- The Kepler frequency for the CFL-K0 star model is Omega_K = 6696 rad/s (approximately 1066 Hz).
- The phonon shear viscosity is eta_Phonon = 3.745 x 10^6 (mu_q/MeV)^8 T_9^{-5} g/(cm s), where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K.
- The kaon shear viscosity shows a T^{-5} dependence, with numerical values calculated for coupling parameter C = 1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-mode damping in normal (ungapped) quark matter by Madsen (1999) and in superconducting phases such as CFL by Jaikumar (2008) and Mannarelli (2008), who found that mutual friction in the CFL phase is too weak to damp r-modes at T less than 10^8 K. The authors specifically address the open question of r-mode damping in the temperature regime 10^8 K to 10^10 K for color-superconducting phases, focusing on the CFL-K0 phase proposed by Bedaque (2001) as energetically favorable when the strange quark mass cost exceeds the kaon mass. The results enable future work on hybrid stars with a CFL-K0 core, where the crust-core interface and quark-hadronic matter layer must be key ingredients for obtaining constraints on gravitational wave emission from stars containing superfluid quark matter.
