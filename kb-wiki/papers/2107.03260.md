# Tracing the dynamical interplay of low-energy reaction processes of exotic nuclei using a two-center molecular continuum

> The competition among reaction processes of a weakly-bound projectile at intermediate times of a slow collision has been unraveled. This has been done using a two-center molecular continuum within a semiclassical, time-dependent coupled-channel reaction model. Dynamical probabilities of elastic scattering, transfer and breakup agree with those derived from the direct integration of the time-dependent Schrödinger equation, demonstrating the usefulness of a two-center molecular continuum for gaining insights into the reaction dynamics of exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-center molecular continuum, implemented within a semiclassical time-dependent coupled-channel model, can trace the dynamical interplay of elastic scattering, transfer, and breakup channels at intermediate times of a slow collision, solving a non-unitarity issue that prevented previous one-center continuum models from following the reaction during its evolution.

## Method

The authors model a valence neutron initially bound to a projectile nucleus colliding with a target nucleus, treating the projectile-target motion classically and the neutron quantum mechanically on a one-dimensional grid. They expand the time-dependent neutron wavefunction in a truncated basis of two-center molecular pseudostates obtained by diagonalizing the system Hamiltonian at each internuclear distance, then solve the time-dependent coupled-channel equations using a Chebyshev propagator. The results are validated against direct numerical integration of the time-dependent Schrodinger equation using a Runge-Kutta fourth-order method.

## Key numbers

- Projectile potential well has one weakly-bound state of energy -0.54 MeV; target potential well has one unoccupied bound state of energy -0.96 MeV.
- The model uses M=500 box states on a grid of L=±350 fm with dx=0.3 fm, including the first N=50 molecular states.
- Initial internuclear distance is 80 fm; distance of minimal approach is R_min=8 fm.
- For the E=0.05 MeV collision, time step dt=0.5×10^{-22} s is used; for E=0.5 MeV, dt=0.16×10^{-22} s, both giving |2dR|=0.13 fm.
- Norm of the wavefunction and sum of squared amplitudes are preserved with accuracy of ~10^{-5} during the entire collision.

## Context

This work extends the one-center continuum approach of Moschini and Diaz-Torres (2021) (Ref. [MMVPRC103]), which could only calculate asymptotic results due to non-unitarity of the time-evolution operator and could not follow the reaction during its evolution. The two-center molecular continuum, a technique established in atomic physics by Delos (1981) and Fritsch and Lin (1991), is here applied for the first time to nuclear reaction theory of weakly-bound nuclei. The results enable future development of a realistic three-dimensional quantitative reaction model for exotic nuclei.
