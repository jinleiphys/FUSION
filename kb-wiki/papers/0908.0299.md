# The fate of the Mach cone in covariant transport theory

> An intriguing potential signature of hydrodynamic behavior in relativistic A+A reactions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies is conical flow induced by fast supersonic particles traversing the hot and dense medium. Here I present first results on the evolution of Mach shocks in 2->2 covariant transport theory, in a static uniform medium.

## Key claim

Mach cone-like flow patterns can emerge in covariant transport theory with elastic 2→2 interactions, but only when the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is very low (η/s ≈ 0.075); at four times larger viscosity the disturbances are largely washed out.

## Method

The author solved Lorentz-covariant on-shell Boltzmann transport theory with elastic 2→2 rates, coupling the system to an external current representing a fast jet moving through a static, uniform thermal bath of massless quarks and gluons (3 flavors, 3 colors). Two scenarios were studied: a "perturbative" scenario where the jet deposits both energy and momentum via 2→2 interactions, and a "pure energy" scenario where only thermalized energy is deposited with no momentum transfer. Jet recoil was explicitly turned off, similar to linear response studies.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the thermal bath: T₀ = 0.385 GeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η ≈ 0.075 s (low-viscosity case)
- Energy density of the medium: e₀ ≈ 44.7 GeV/fm³
- Mean free path: λ = 0.125 fm
- Jet energy in perturbative scenario: E = 8.25 GeV
- Energy loss rate in perturbative scenario: dE/dL ≈ dp_z/dL ≈ 20 GeV/fm³
- Energy loss rate in pure energy scenario: dE/dL ≈ 75 GeV/fm³
- Source velocity in pure energy scenario: v = 0.9c
- Jet radius in perturbative scenario: R = 0.2 fm
- Gaussian width in pure energy scenario: σ = 0.3 fm
- Time of observation: t = 2.5 fm
- Higher viscosity case: η ≈ 0.3 s (four times larger mean free path)

## Context

This work extends ideal hydrodynamic calculations of Mach shocks in heavy-ion collisions, specifically those by Betz et al. (2009), by demonstrating that covariant transport theory can reproduce similar conical flow patterns while incorporating finite viscosity and self-consistent jet-medium coupling. The results show that covariant transport provides a feasible alternative to 3+1D causal dissipative hydrodynamics for studying how collision dynamics, phase transitions, and dissipation affect conical flow signatures at RHIC energies. The study opens the door to future 3+1D investigations of conical flow with viscosity and self-consistent coupling, though the author notes that no clear signals were found in angular distributions dN/dy dφ or dN/d cosθ at the modest statistics used.
