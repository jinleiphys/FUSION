# Relaxation and Kinetics in Scalar Field Theories

> A new approach to the dynamics of relaxation and kinetics of thermalization in a scalar field theory is presented that incorporates the relevant time scales through the resummation of hard thermal loops. An alternative derivation of the kinetic equations for the ``quasiparticle'' distribution functions is obtained that allows a clear understanding of the different ``coarse graining'' approximations usually involved in a kinetic description. This method leads to a systematic perturbative expansion to obtain the kinetic equations including hard-thermal loop resummation and to an improvement including renormalization, off-shell effects and contributions that change chemical equilibrium on short time scales. As a byproduct of these methods we establish the relation between the relaxation time scale in the linearized equation of motion of the quasiparticles and the thermalization time scale of the quasiparticle distribution function in the ``relaxation time approximation''. Hard thermal loop resummation dramatically modifies the scattering rate for long wavelength modes as compared to the usual (semi) classical estimate. Relaxation and kinetics are studied both in the unbroken and broken symmetry phases of the theory. The broken symmetry phase also provides the setting to obtain the contribution to the kinetic equations from processes that involve decay of a heavy scalar into light scalar particles in the medium.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new approach to deriving kinetic equations for scalar field theories that incorporates hard thermal loop resummation, establishing that the relaxation time scale in the linearized equation of motion for quasiparticles is directly related to the thermalization time scale of the quasiparticle distribution function in the relaxation time approximation, but only under the condition that the Breit-Wigner approximation is valid for times shorter than approximately \(1/\Gamma \ln(\omega_p/\Gamma)\).

## Method

The authors use non-equilibrium real-time field theory methods with a contour in the complex time plane, implementing hard thermal loop resummation by writing the bare mass as a temperature-dependent renormalized quasiparticle mass plus a counterterm. They derive the linearized equation of motion for the scalar field expectation value using the tadpole method, obtaining the retarded self-energy to two-loop order in the unbroken symmetry phase and to one-loop order in the broken symmetry phase. The kinetic equations for quasiparticle distribution functions are obtained through a systematic perturbative expansion that incorporates the resummed propagators and allows identification of the relevant coarse-graining scales.

## Key numbers

* For the unbroken symmetry phase at high temperature (\(T \gg \sqrt{\lambda}T \gg |m_R|\)), the zero-momentum collisional relaxation rate is \(\Gamma(\vec p=0; T) = \lambda^{3/2}T / (64\sqrt{24\pi})\).
* The leading term of the thermal mass in the high-temperature limit is \(m^2_R(T) = \lambda T^2 / 24 + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^{3/2} T^2)\) for \(T > \sqrt{\lambda} T > |m_R|\).
* The energy density deposited in heavy ion collisions is approximately \(1\ \text{GeV}/\text{fm}^3\), corresponding to temperatures \(T \approx 200\ \text{MeV}\) with relaxation time scales of order \(t \approx 1\ \text{fm}/c\).
* For the quark-gluon plasma at \(T \approx 200\ \text{MeV}\), thermal masses of light quarks are of order \(M_q(T) \approx gT > m_q \approx 5\text{--}10\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop resummation program of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to the kinetic description of scalar field theories, and builds upon the self-consistent treatment proposed by Lawrie (1992) for incorporating microscopic time scales. The method provides a systematic framework for studying thermalization and relaxation in contexts such as the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, and the reheating stage in cosmological inflationary scenarios as studied by Kofman, Brandenberger, and others (1994). The approach enables a direct proof of the relation between quasiparticle damping rates and thermalization rates that was proposed by Weldon (1983) but not previously proven within the Boltzmann equation derived from microscopic theory.
