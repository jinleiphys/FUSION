# Parametric Instability of Classical Yang-Mills Fields in an Expanding Geometry

> We investigate the instability of classical Yang-Mills field in an expanding geometry under a color magnetic background field within the linear regime. We consider homogeneous, boost-invariant and time-dependent color magnetic fields simulating the glasma configuration. We introduce the conformal coordinates which enable us to map an expanding problem approximately into a nonexpanding problem. We find that the fluctuations with finite longitudinal momenta can grow exponentially due to parametric instability. Fluctuations with finite transverse momenta can also show parametric instability, but their momenta are restricted to be small. The most unstable modes start to grow exponentially in the early stage of the dynamics and they may affect the thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that classical Yang-Mills fields in an expanding geometry with a time-dependent color magnetic background can exhibit parametric instability, but only under the condition that the effective momenta of fluctuations are time-dependent and the instability is restricted to modes with finite longitudinal momenta or small transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors consider a homogeneous, boost-invariant, time-dependent color magnetic background field in an SU(2) gauge theory, simulating the glasma configuration in heavy-ion collisions. They introduce conformal coordinates to map the expanding problem approximately into a nonexpanding problem, then perform a linear stability analysis of fluctuations around the background field by solving the linearized equations of motion in Fourier space.

## Key numbers

- The background field at later times is described by the Jacobi elliptic function cn(θ; k) with modulus k = 1/√2 and period T ≈ 7.42.
- For the initial condition θ₀ = 0.01, Ã(θ₀) = 1, and dÃ/dθ(θ₀) = 0, the final amplitude is B_eff^(1/2) ≈ 4.68 and the time shift is Δ ≈ 1.69.
- The growth rate for Lamé's equation at λ = -1 has a maximum value of 0.66 at p² = 0, and for λ = 2 the maximum is 0.23 at p² = 0.
- The proper time is evaluated as Q_s τ = 0.67, 3.46, and 21.1 for dimensionless conformal times θ B_eff^(1/2) = 1, 3, and 10, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parametric instability in classical Yang-Mills fields in a nonexpanding geometry (Berges 2012a; Tsutsui 2014) to the more realistic expanding geometry relevant for heavy-ion collisions. It also builds on studies of parametric instability in scalar field theory in expanding geometries (Berges 2012b; Dusling 2012). The results suggest that parametric instability may play an essential role in the thermalization process in heavy-ion collisions, potentially helping to resolve the puzzle of early thermalization required by hydrodynamical analyses.
