# Electric dipole moment of ^{129}Xe atom

> The parity (P) and time-reversal (T) odd coupling constant associated with the tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) electron-nucleus interaction and the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) have been determined by combining the result of the measurement of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of ^{129}Xe atom and calculations based on the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. Calculations using various relativistic many-body methods have been performed at different levels of approximation and their accuracies are estimated by comparing the results of the calculated dipole polarizability of the ground state of the above atom with its most precise available experimental data. The non-linear terms that arise in the RCC theory at the singles and doubles approximation were found to be crucial for achieving high accuracy in the calculations. Our results for the ^{129}Xe EDM due to the odd T-PT interaction and the NSM are, respectively, d_A=0.501 x 10^{-20} C_T <σ_N > |e|cm and d_A=0.336 x 10^{-17} S/(|e| fm^3) |e|cm. These results in combination with the future EDM measurements in atomic Xe could provide the most accurate limits for the T-PT coupling constant and NSM.

# Electric dipole moment of ^{129}Xe atom

## Key claim

The authors determine the parity- and time-reversal-odd coupling constant for the tensor-pseudotensor electron-nucleus interaction and the nuclear Schiff moment in ^{129}Xe by combining relativistic coupled-cluster calculations with the existing experimental EDM limit. The extracted limits for C_T and S are not superior to those from ^{199}Hg (about three orders of magnitude weaker), but the authors argue that combining their calculated values with upcoming Xe EDM experiments could yield the most accurate limits.

## Method

The authors perform relativistic many-body calculations using the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian at several levels of approximation: Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DF), third-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT(3)), coupled-perturbed Hartree-Fock (CPHF), linearized coupled-cluster singles and doubles (LCCSD), and full relativistic coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD). They compute the dipole polarizability of the ground state of Xe to benchmark the accuracy of each method against the experimental value, then apply the same methods to compute the EDM enhancement factors eta (for T-PT interaction) and zeta (for NSM). The final recommended values come from the CCSD method, which includes non-linear terms absent in LCCSD and CPHF.

## Key numbers

- Dipole polarizability of Xe ground state: CCSD result = 27.744 e a_0^3; experimental value = 27.815(27) e a_0^3
- EDM enhancement factor for T-PT interaction: eta = 0.501 x 10^{-20} (dimensionless factor such that d_A = eta x C_T x <sigma_N> x |e| cm)
- EDM enhancement factor for NSM: zeta = 0.336 x 10^{-17} (dimensionless factor such that d_A = zeta x S/(|e| fm^3) x |e| cm)
- Limit from combining CCSD results with experimental d_A < 4.1 x 10^{-27} |e| cm: C_T < 1.6 x 10^{-6} and S < 1.2 x 10^{-9} |e| fm^3
- Best experimental limit for diamagnetic atomic EDM: d_A(^{199}Hg) < 3.1 x 10^{-29} |e| cm (Griffith et al.)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of Xe EDM enhancement factors by Dzuba et al. and Martensson-Pendrill, reproducing their results at the DF and CPHF levels and going beyond them with CCSD. The authors show that non-linear terms in the CCSD method are crucial for accuracy, as LCCSD and CPHF overestimate the enhancement factors. The results enable future Xe EDM experiments (Inoue et al., Fierlinger et al., Schmidt et al.) to extract improved limits on the T-PT coupling constant and nuclear Schiff moment, which in combination with nuclear structure calculations (Yoshinaga et al.) could constrain the QCD theta term and quark chromo-EDMs.
