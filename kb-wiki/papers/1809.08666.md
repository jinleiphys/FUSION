# Forward-backward multiplicity fluctuations in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions with wounded quarks and fluctuating strings

> We analyze a generic model where wounded quarks are amended with strings in which both end-point positions fluctuate in spatial rapidity. With the assumption that the strings emit particles independently of one another and with a uniform distribution in rapidity, we are able to analyze the model semi-analytically, which allows for its detailed understanding. Using as a constraint the one-body string emission functions obtained from the experimental data for collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, we explore the two-body correlations for various scenarios of string fluctuations. We find that the popular measures used to quantify the longitudinal fluctuations are limited with upper and lower bounds and assume close values for the most likely models of the end-point distributions, which may explain why various approaches yield here very similar results.

## Key claim

The popular Legendre $a_{nm}$ coefficients used to quantify longitudinal multiplicity fluctuations are bounded between upper and lower limits, and take close values for the most likely models of string end-point distributions, which explains why various approaches yield very similar results despite different underlying assumptions.

## Method

The authors constructed a generic model where wounded quarks are associated with strings whose both end-point positions fluctuate independently in spatial rapidity, with uniform particle emission between the end-points. They extracted the one-body string emission function from experimental PHOBOS data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV for d-Au and Au-Au collisions, then used this constraint to explore three scenarios for the end-point distributions: (i) both end-points identically distributed, (ii) non-overlapping supports for the two end-points, and (iii) one end-point following valence quark parton distribution functions with the other adjusted to match the data. The model was analyzed semi-analytically to compute two-body correlations and Legendre $a_{nm}$ coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The proportionality constant $k$ in the wounded quark scaling $N_{\rm ch} = k (\langle N_A\rangle + \langle N_B\rangle)$ is independent of centrality or mass numbers at a given collision energy, increasing with collision energy
- The maximum of the one-body emission profile $f(\eta)$ occurs at $\eta_{\rm max} \simeq 2.5$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- The $a_{nm}$ coefficients can be larger by a factor of $\sim 3$ when both end-points fluctuate (case i) compared to single end-point fluctuations (case ii)
- The covariance on the diagonal follows ${\rm cov}(\eta,\eta) = f(\eta)[\omega - f(\eta)]$, where $\omega = 2f(\eta_{\rm max})$ for case i) and $\omega = f(\eta_{\rm max})$ for case ii)
- The fall-off from the diagonal in the anti-diagonal direction is proportional to $4 f(\eta_{1,2}^{(0)})[g_1(\eta) + g_2(\eta)]$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Broniowski (2015) where only single end-point fluctuations were considered, by allowing both string end-points to fluctuate in spatial rapidity. The wounded quark model builds on the Glauber model approach of Bialas (1976) and the wounded quark scaling of Bialas (1977), which has been phenomenologically successful at RHIC and LHC energies. The results explain why different string model implementations (such as Lund string models or the dual parton model) yield similar longitudinal correlation measures, and provide a framework for interpreting experimental forward-backward fluctuation data in terms of the underlying string end-point dynamics.
