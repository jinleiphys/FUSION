# Jet-fluid interaction in the EPOS3-Jet framework

> EPOS3-Jet is an integrated framework for jet modelling in heavy ion collisions, where the initial hard (jet) partons are produced along with soft (medium) partons in the initial state EPOS approach. The jet partons then propagate in the hydrodynamically expanding medium. The energy and momentum lost by the jet partons is added to the hydrodynamic medium via the source terms. The full evolution proceeds in a concurrent mode, without separating hydrodynamic and jet parts. In this report, we examine the medium recoil effects in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV LHC energy in the EPOS3-Jet framework.

## Key claim
The EPOS3-Jet framework implements a concurrent, two-way coupling between jet partons and a hydrodynamically expanding medium, but the authors conclude that the assumption of instant thermalization of the energy-momentum lost by jet partons makes it difficult to observe medium recoil effects on jet observables. The study is explicitly qualitative and does not aim to fit experimental data.

## Method
Initial hard partons are sampled from EPOS3 initial state calculations and evolved via a Monte Carlo DGLAP parton cascade with leading-order splitting functions, from an initial virtuality scale equal to the parton's transverse momentum down to a minimal virtuality of 0.6 GeV. The parton shower uses vacuum splitting functions with two medium modifications: an effective increase of virtuality via a temperature-dependent transport coefficient \(\hat{q}_R(T)\), and collisional energy loss modeled via Langevin-type longitudinal drag and random transverse kicks with temperature- and momentum-dependent coefficients from Gossiaux (2009). The energy and momentum lost by each jet parton is added to the corresponding fluid cells via a Gaussian-smeared source term in the hydrodynamic equations, with the full evolution proceeding concurrently in the same time steps.

## Key numbers
- Simulations performed for most central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- Minimal virtuality scale for DGLAP evolution: \(Q_\downarrow = 0.6\) GeV.
- Transport coefficients multiplied by a correction factor \(K = 0.166\) to approach the temperature-dependent jet transport coefficient from the MARTINI model.
- Resulting parton-level \(R_{\rm AA} \approx 0.6\) at \(p_\perp^{\rm parton} = 10\)–30 GeV.
- Gaussian smearing kernel width for source terms: \(R_g = 0.4\) fm/c in the transverse direction.
- Trigger requirement: at least one hard parton with \(p_\perp > 50\) GeV per event.

## Context
This work extends the EPOS3-Jet framework by including the back reaction of the hydrodynamic medium on jet evolution, in contrast to most existing models that implement only one-way jet-hydro coupling. The authors note that recent studies such as Park (2018) conclude that medium recoil must be included to reproduce modifications of radial momentum distributions within jets in AA collisions. The present qualitative study sets the stage for future work with higher grid resolution and more realistic thermalization assumptions to observe medium recoil effects on jet observables.
