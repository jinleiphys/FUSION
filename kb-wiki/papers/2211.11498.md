# Kaon-meson condensation and $Δ$ resonance in hyperonic stellar matter within a relativistic mean-field model

> We study the equation of state of dense baryon matter within the relativistic mean-field model, and we include $Δ$(1232) isobars into IUFSU model with hyperons and consider the possibility of kaon meson condensation. We find that it is necessary to consider the $Δ$ resonance state inside the massive neutron star. The critical density of Kaon mesons and hyperons is shifted to a higher density region, in this respect an early appearance of $Δ$ resonances is crucial to guarantee the stability of the branch of hyperonized star with the difference of the coupling parameter $x_{σΔ}$ constrained based on the QCD rules in nuclear matter. The $Δ$ resonance produces a softer equation of state in the low density region, which makes the tidal deformability and radius consistent with the observation of GW170817. As the addition of new degrees of freedom will lead to a softening of the equation of state, the $σ$-cut scheme, which states the decrease of neutron star mass can be lowered if one assumes a limited decrease of the $σ$-meson strength at ${ρ_B}$($ρ_B > ρ_0$), finally we get a maximum mass neutron star with $Δ$ resonance heavier than 2$M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Δ(1232) resonances in hyperonic neutron star matter, combined with a σ-cut scheme that limits the decrease of the σ-meson strength at high baryon density, can simultaneously satisfy the observational constraints from GW170817 (tidal deformability and radius) and produce a maximum neutron star mass heavier than 2 solar masses, but only for specific choices of the Δ-σ coupling parameter x_{σΔ} and the kaon optical potential U_K.

## Method

The authors extended the IUFSU relativistic mean-field model to include the full baryon octet (Λ, Σ, Ξ), the four charge states of the Δ(1232) resonance (Δ^{++}, Δ^{+}, Δ^{0}, Δ^{-}), and K^- meson condensation, with meson fields σ, ω, ρ, and φ. They solved the equations of motion under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, applied a σ-cut scheme to stiffen the equation of state at high density, and computed neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons, Δ resonance, and K^- (U_K = -160 MeV) without σ-cut: 1.58 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.05, 1.54 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.1, 1.51 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.15
- Maximum neutron star mass with σ-cut (c_σ=0.15) and same composition: 2.12 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.05, 2.09 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.1, 2.05 M_⊙ for x_{σΔ}=1.15
- Threshold density for K^- condensation without σ-cut at U_K = -160 MeV: 5.24 ρ_0 for x_{σΔ}=1.05, 6.79 ρ_0 for x_{σΔ}=1.1, 6.73 ρ_0 for x_{σΔ}=1.15
- Radius of 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star without σ-cut: 10.37 km (x_{σΔ}=1.05), 10.27 km (x_{σΔ}=1.1), 9.9 km (x_{σΔ}=1.15)
- Tidal deformability Λ_{1.4} from GW170817 constraint: 190^{+390}_{-120} at 90% confidence level

## Context

This work extends the IUFSU model of Fattoyev (2010) and Grill (2014) to address the "hyperon puzzle" (Burgio 2021, Logoteta 2021) and the "Δ puzzle" (Drago 2014) by simultaneously including hyperons, Δ resonances, and kaon condensation. The σ-cut scheme was previously applied by Maslov (2015) to stiffen the equation of state. The results show that Δ resonances soften the equation of state at low density (consistent with GW170817 constraints from LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2018) while the σ-cut scheme enables massive neutron stars above 2 M_⊙, matching observations of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010, Fonseca 2016), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013), and MSP J0740+6620 (Fonseca 2021). The work enables future studies of neutron star composition with combined constraints from gravitational wave events and NICER radius measurements.
