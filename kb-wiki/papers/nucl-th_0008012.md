# Distorted wave impulse approximation analysis for spin observables in nucleon quasi-elastic scattering and enhancement of the spin-longitudinal response

> We present a formalism of distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) for analyzing spin observables in nucleon inelastic and charge exchange reactions leading to the continuum. It utilizes response functions calculated by the continuum random phase approximation (RPA), which include the effective mass, the spreading widths and the Δdegrees of freedom. The Fermi motion is treated by the optimal factorization, and the non-locality of the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix by an averaged reaction plane approximation. By using the formalism we calculated the spin-longitudinal and the spin-transverse cross sections, ID_q and ID_p, of 12C, 40Ca (\vec{p},\vec{n}) at 494 and 346 MeV. The calculation reasonably reproduced the observed ID_q, which is consistent with the predicted enhancement of the spin-longitudinal response function R_L. However, the observed ID_p is much larger than the calculated one, which was consistent with neither the predicted quenching nor the spin-transverse response function R_T obtained by the (e,e') scattering. The Landau-Migdal parameter g'_NΔfor the NΔtransition interaction and the effective mass at the nuclear center m^*(r=0) are treated as adjustable parameters. The present analysis indicates that the smaller g'_{NΔ}(\approx 0.3) and m^*(0) \approx 0.7 m are preferable. We also investigate the validity of the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with the effective nucleon number approximation for the absorption, by means of which R_L and R_T have conventionally been extracted.

## Key claim

The paper presents a DWIA formalism that reasonably reproduces the observed spin-longitudinal cross section \(ID_q\) for \((\vec{p},\vec{n})\) reactions on \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{40}\)Ca at 494 and 346 MeV, consistent with the predicted enhancement of the spin-longitudinal response function \(R_L\); however, the calculated spin-transverse cross section \(ID_p\) is much smaller than the observed one, contradicting both the predicted quenching of \(R_T\) and the \(R_T\) extracted from \((e,e')\) scattering.

## Method

The authors developed a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) formalism for nucleon inelastic and charge-exchange reactions to the continuum, using response functions calculated by the continuum random phase approximation (RPA) that include the effective mass, spreading widths, and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom. Fermi motion was treated by the optimal factorization approximation, and the non-locality of the nucleon-nucleon \(t\)-matrix was handled by an averaged reaction plane approximation. The spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse cross sections \(ID_q\) and \(ID_p\) were calculated for \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{40}\)Ca \((\vec{p},\vec{n})\) at 494 and 346 MeV, with the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'_{N\Delta}\) and the effective mass at the nuclear center \(m^*(r=0)\) treated as adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{40}\)Ca \((\vec{p},\vec{n})\) at 494 and 346 MeV, the calculation reasonably reproduced the observed spin-longitudinal cross section \(ID_q\), consistent with enhancement of \(R_L\).
- The observed spin-transverse cross section \(ID_p\) was much larger than the calculated one, inconsistent with both the predicted quenching of \(R_T\) and \(R_T\) from \((e,e')\) scattering.
- The analysis indicates a preferred value of the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'_{N\Delta} \approx 0.3\) and effective mass at the nuclear center \(m^*(0) \approx 0.7 m\) (where \(m\) is the free nucleon mass).

## Context

This work extends earlier DWIA calculations by Ichimura et al. (1989) for \((\vec{p},\vec{p}')\) and by De Pace (1995) using the Glauber approximation, and addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the predicted enhancement of the spin-longitudinal response \(R_L\) and the ratio \(R_L/R_T < 1\) extracted from experiments at LAMPF and RCNP using plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with the effective nucleon number approximation. The present DWIA analysis shows that the contradiction regarding the ratio stems from the large underestimation of \(ID_p\) by theory, and enables more reliable extraction of nuclear response functions by testing the validity of the conventional PWIA extraction method.
