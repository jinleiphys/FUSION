# Interplay between shape-phase transitions and shape coexistence in the Zr isotopes

> We investigate the evolution of structure in the zirconium isotopes where one of the most complex situations encountered in nuclear physics occurs. We demonstrate the role of two concurrent types of quantum phase transitions, sharing a common critical point. The first type, involves an abrupt crossing of coexisting normal and intruder configurations. The second type, involves a gradual shape-phase transition within the intruder configuration, changing from weakly-deformed to prolate-deformed and finally to gamma-unstable. Evidence for this scenario is provided by a detailed comparison with experimental data, using a definite algebraic framework.

## Key claim

The Zr isotopes exhibit two concurrent types of quantum phase transitions sharing a common critical point: an abrupt crossing of coexisting normal and intruder configurations, and a gradual shape-phase transition within the intruder configuration itself. The authors claim that the Zr chain is exceptional in that the crossing is abrupt, the separate configurations retain their purity before and after the crossing, and the shape evolution of the intruder configuration can be cast in terms of its own phase transition.

## Method

The authors employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using a [N] ⊕ [N+2] boson space with total boson number N = 1,2,...,8 for 92-106Zr and N̄ = 7,6 for 108,110Zr. They performed a numerical diagonalization of the IBM-CM Hamiltonian and evaluated matrix elements of the E2 operator between its eigenstates, with parameters determined from a combined fit to experimental spectra and E2 transition data across the entire 92-110Zr chain. A classical analysis was also performed by diagonalizing the two-by-two matrix of energy surfaces to obtain eigen-potentials in the (β,γ) plane.

## Key numbers

- In 98Zr, the ground state 0+1 has a^2 = 98.2% in the normal A-configuration; in 100Zr, the ground state 0+1 has b^2 = 87.2% in the intruder B-configuration, showing an abrupt crossing between neutron numbers 58 and 60.
- The 0+2 state in 98Zr has b^2 = 98.2% in the B-configuration; in 100Zr it has a^2 = 80.2% in the A-configuration, confirming the role exchange.
- In 100Zr, the first excited 0+ state of the B-configuration (0+3) has b^2 = 92.9%, with a low excitation energy characteristic of the X(5) critical-point symmetry.
- In 102Zr, the 2+1 excitation energy is 151.78 keV with R4/2 = 3.15; in 104Zr, E(2+1) = 139.3 keV with R4/2 = 3.24, indicating strongly deformed prolate shapes.
- In 106Zr, E(2+2) = 607.0 keV and E(4+1) = 476.5 keV; in 110Zr, E(4+1) = 565 keV and E(2+2) = 485 keV, signatures of γ-unstable SO(6) symmetry.
- The boson effective charges in the E2 operator are e(A) = 0.9 (W.u.)1/2 and e(B) = 2.24 (W.u.)1/2.
- The isotope shift parameters are α = 0.235 fm2 and η = 0.264 fm2.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in nuclei, previously applied to shape changes within a single configuration in the neutron number 90 region for Nd-Sm-Gd isotopes (Cejnar, Jolie, and Casten, 2010) and to shape coexistence in light Pb-Hg isotopes (Heyde, 2011). The results resemble those from Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations (Togashi, 2016) but differ from mean-field based calculations (Delaroche, 2010; Nomura, 2016; Mei, 2012) which smooth out the phase transitional behavior. The framework enables future studies of intertwined quantum phase transitions in other mass regions where configuration mixing and shape evolution occur simultaneously.
