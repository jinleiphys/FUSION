# Structure functions of the nucleon and nuclei in neutrino reactions

> Determination of parton distribution functions in nuclei is important for calculating nuclear corrections in oscillation experiments, from which detailed information should be extracted on neutrino properties. First, nuclear parton distributions are discussed for explaining high-energy nuclear reaction data. Possible nuclear modification is explained for valence-quark and antiquark distributions. It is rather difficult to determine gluon distributions in nuclei. Next, reversing the topic, we discuss structure functions which could be investigated by neutrino reactions. Determination of polarized parton distributions in the nucleon is discussed in polarized neutrino reactions. In addition, neutrino reactions should be important for finding nuclear modification of valence-quark distributions at small x if structure function ratios F_3^A/F_3^D are measured for various nuclei.

## Key claim
Neutrino reactions could determine nuclear valence-quark distributions at small x if structure function ratios F_3^A/F_3^D are measured for various nuclei, but such measurements are not possible at this stage due to lack of accurate deuteron data.

## Method
The author performed a leading-order chi-squared analysis of deep-inelastic-scattering F_2^A/F_2^D data (309 total data points) to extract nuclear parton distribution functions, using weight functions w_i(x,A,Z) that multiply the MRST nucleon parton distributions at Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2. The weight functions were parametrized with a cubic polynomial form including a (1 - 1/A^{1/3}) factor, and the distributions were evolved to experimental Q^2 points using DGLAP evolution equations. Three parameters were fixed by charge, baryon-number, and momentum conservation sum rules.

## Key numbers
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the cubic analysis was chi^2_min/d.o.f. = 1.82 for 309 total data points.
- The ratio F_2^A/F_2^D is about 15% smaller than unity for a medium size nucleus at medium x.
- Nuclear modification of parton distribution functions is typically less than 20% for medium size nuclei.
- The initial Q^2 for the nuclear parton distributions was chosen as Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The weight functions were shown at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 for calcium, and the fit curves were compared with data at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier analysis (Saga01) of nuclear parton distributions using only F_2^A/F_2^D data, with Drell-Yan data analysis in progress (Prog). It contrasts with the approach of Eskola, Honkanen, Kolhinen, Ruuskanen, and Salgado (EKR), who divided the x region into three parts with different functional forms and included Drell-Yan data. The paper enables future neutrino factory measurements to determine valence-quark shadowing at small x and polarized parton distributions, which could resolve the controversy over the quark spin content Delta Sigma (currently ranging from 0.05 to 0.3 from existing data).
