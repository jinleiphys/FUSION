# Higher Order Collins Modulations in Transversely Polarized Quark Fragmentation

> The Collins effect describes the modulation of the hadron production by a transversely polarized quark with the sine of the polar angle, $φ$, between the produced hadron's transverse momentum and the quark spin. We employ a quark-jet model to describe multiple hadron emissions by such a quark, taking the Collins effect into account. The resulting hadron distributions exhibit modulation up to fourth order in $\sin(φ)$ when only two hadron emissions are allowed, rising with any further increase in the number of emitted hadrons. These new effects are a direct consequence of the quark-jet mechanism for quark hadronization, which do not depend on the details of the model used for elementary hadron emission. The size and the sign of the higher order terms are directly connected with the probabilities of quark spin flip in the elementary emission process, with opposing sign favored and unfavored Collins functions only being generated if quark spin flip is preferential. Experimental studies of these effects should therefore provide a critical test of the quark hadronization mechanism, which in turn will lead to a deeper understanding of the transverse spin structure of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within a quark-jet model of hadronization, the fragmentation function of a transversely polarized quark necessarily acquires higher-order Collins modulations (up to fourth order in sin(φ) for two hadron emissions, and rising further with more emissions) as a direct consequence of the multiple hadron emission mechanism, independent of the specific model used for the elementary emission vertex.

## Method

The authors employed the NJL-jet model, using tree-level diagrams for unpolarized fragmentation functions and a spectator model for the Collins function, with light-cone spinors to evaluate quark spin-flip probabilities in each hadron emission step. They performed Monte Carlo simulations of the quark hadronization process, using the elementary fragmentation function for a transversely polarized quark calculated within the NJL model as input, and extracted the z, P⊥², and φ dependence of the resulting hadron number densities for pions and kaons produced by light and strange quarks.

## Key numbers

- The quark spin non-flip and spin flip probabilities are proportional to |a₁|² ∼ l_x² and |a₂|² ∼ l_y² + (M₂ - (1-z)M₁)², respectively, where M₁ and M₂ are constituent masses of fragmenting and remnant quarks
- The analysis covered 7×100×100 = 7·10⁴ data points for all fragmentation functions of a u quark (to seven pions and kaons) across discrete values of z and P⊥²
- For simulations with only two hadron emissions, a fourth order polynomial in sin(φ) yields χ²_dof in the vicinity of 1 for all fits
- The general form for the polarized fragmentation function for R hadron emissions is a polynomial in sin(φ) of order 1+3×(R-1)

## Context

This work extends the NJL-jet model calculations of Matevosyan et al. (2012) for Collins fragmentation functions, which previously showed that unfavored Collins functions have opposite sign and comparable magnitude to favored ones for pions. The paper contradicts earlier spectator model calculations (Amrath 2005, Bacchetta 2007, Gamberg 2003) that were limited to single hadron emission approximations and consequently yielded zero unfavored fragmentation functions. The results enable experimental tests of the quark hadronization mechanism and could improve extraction of unpolarized and Collins fragmentation functions from high-precision data from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab.
