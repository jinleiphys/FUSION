# Hadron Physics and Lattice QCD

> A review of recent lattice QCD hadron structure calculations is presented. Important hadronic properties such as the axial charge and spin content of the nucleon, as well as, the mass and axial charge of hyperons and charmed baryons are discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that lattice QCD simulations with near-physical parameters now successfully reproduce the low-lying baryon spectrum across multiple discretization schemes, but that a persistent discrepancy in the nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) at near-physical pion masses remains unresolved and requires further investigation of excited-state contamination and lattice systematics.

## Method

The paper surveys lattice QCD calculations of hadron masses, form factors, and spin structure using multiple fermion discretizations (twisted mass, Clover, domain wall, staggered, and hybrid actions). It compares results from two-point and three-point correlation functions for baryon masses, axial charges, electromagnetic form factors, and generalized parton distribution moments, with particular attention to chiral extrapolation, volume, and cutoff effects.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge \(g_A\): LQCD results from \(N_f=2\) TMF, \(N_f=2+1+1\) TMF, \(N_f=2+1\) DWF, and hybrid actions agree for \(m_\pi \gtrsim 250\) MeV, but Clover results at \(m_\pi \sim 150\) MeV and \(m_\pi \sim 180\) MeV underestimate the experimental value.
- SU(3) symmetry breaking parameter \(\delta_{\rm SU(3)} = g_A^N - g_A^\Sigma + g_A^\Xi\): at the physical point, \(\delta_{\rm SU(3)} \sim 15\%\) from TMF and hybrid results.
- Quark spin contribution to nucleon spin: \(\Delta u + \Delta d + \Delta s = 0.45(4)(9)\) with \(\Delta s = -0.020(10)(4)\) at \(\mu = \sqrt{7.4}\) GeV from Clover fermions including disconnected diagrams.
- Total spin of u- and d-quarks at physical pion mass: \(J^u \sim 1/4\) and \(J^d \sim 0\) from TMF results.

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD hadron structure program by Alexandrou and the ETM Collaboration (2010–2012) and compares with results from QCDSF-UKQCD (Bietenholz 2011), PACS-CS (Aoki 2009), the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards 2011), and LHPC (Bratt 2010). It enables systematic comparison of lattice predictions for hyperon and charmed baryon axial couplings and nucleon spin decomposition, and identifies the need for simulations at physical pion mass with controlled systematics to resolve the \(g_A\) discrepancy.
