# Electromagnetic Probes of the Quantum Chromodynamical Plasma

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, electromagnetic (EM) radiation has been used as a sensitive probe of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) properties, owing to the smaller EM coupling relative to QCD coupling. To better understand the constraining power of EM emissions on transport properties of the QGP, a deeper understanding of both the theory and phenomenology of EM signals is required. A selection of recent developments in those two areas of QGP EM probes is discussed, with an outlook on how Bayesian model-to-data comparisons can help further quantify our understanding of QGP transport coefficients.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (real photons and dileptons) can constrain QGP transport properties such as shear and bulk viscosity, the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\), and the nuclear equation of state, but a full quantitative extraction requires Bayesian model-to-data comparisons that include theoretical systematic uncertainties from off-equilibrium corrections and multi-messenger combinations.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical and phenomenological status of electromagnetic (EM) radiation from the QGP, covering both thermal-equilibrium and off-equilibrium production rates. It discusses perturbative QCD calculations of the EM spectral function at next-to-leading order, non-perturbative lattice QCD results, chiral effective Lagrangian and functional renormalization group approaches for hadronic degrees of freedom, and the inclusion of shear and bulk viscous corrections via Grad 14-moment and Chapman-Enskog expansions. It then surveys applications to heavy-ion collisions, including sensitivity to the QCD equation of state, the dynamical production of quarks from an initially gluonic medium, and jet-medium interactions.

## Key numbers

* The EM fine-structure constant \(\alpha_{EM} \approx 1/137\) sets the small coupling that allows photons and dileptons to leave the QGP with negligible interaction.
* At high temperatures \(T \gtrsim 200\) MeV, photons from parton interactions are the main source; at \(T \lesssim 200\) MeV, hadronic decays of \(\rho, \omega, \phi\) mesons dominate the low invariant mass dilepton spectrum (\(M \lesssim 1\) GeV).
* The intermediate invariant mass range \(1 \lesssim M \lesssim 2.5\) GeV shows a featureless dilepton spectrum; high invariant masses \(M \gtrsim 2.5\) GeV contain heavy quarkonia decays.
* Photon radiation from the partonic sector dominates at intermediate transverse momenta \(1 \lesssim p^\gamma_T \lesssim 4\) GeV, while hadronic processes dominate for \(p^\gamma_T \lesssim 1\) GeV.
* Chiral symmetry restoration is suggested by overlapping \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) spectral functions at \(T = 170\) MeV in chiral effective models, and at \(T = 300\) MeV in functional renormalization group calculations; the pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD is \(T_{pc} = 156 \pm 1.5\) MeV.
* About 30% of photons in the range \(5 \lesssim p_T \lesssim 8\) GeV originate from jet-medium interactions.
* A first-order phase transition in the QCD equation of state can increase the dilepton yield by about a factor of two compared to a system without a phase transition.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on EM probes of the QGP summarized in earlier reviews by Kapusta (2023) and Rapp (2009, 2014), and builds on perturbative QCD calculations by Ghiglieri (2013, 2014), Laine (2013), and Ghisoiu (2014), as well as lattice QCD results from the HotQCD collaboration (2018). It connects to Bayesian analyses of QGP transport coefficients performed by the JETSCAPE collaboration (2020, 2024) and to hydrodynamical studies of dilepton and photon production by Vujanovic (2013, 2016, 2017, 2019), Paquet (2015), and Churchill (2023). The work enables future Bayesian multi-messenger analyses that combine EM probes with soft hadronic and jet observables to quantitatively constrain QGP properties, and it identifies the need for improved measurements of dilepton anisotropic flow \(v_2\) at the LHC by the ALICE collaboration.
