# Exceptional points in the scattering continuum

> The manifestation of exceptional points in the scattering continuum of atomic nucleus is studied using the real-energy continuum shell model. It is shown that low-energy exceptional points appear for realistic values of coupling to the continuum and, hence, could be accessible experimentally. Experimental signatures are proposed which include the jump by $2π$ of the elastic scattering phase shift and a salient energy dependence of cross-sections in the vicinity of the exceptional point.

# Exceptional points in the scattering continuum

## Key claim
Low-energy exceptional points (double-poles of the S-matrix) can appear in the scattering continuum of atomic nuclei for realistic values of the continuum coupling strength, making them potentially accessible experimentally. The authors show that the characteristic jump by 2π of the elastic scattering phase shift and a salient energy dependence of cross-sections persist not only at the exact exceptional point but also in its neighborhood (subcritical and overcritical regimes of coupling).

## Method
The authors used the real-energy continuum shell model, specifically the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), to study exceptional points in the one-nucleon continuum of 16Ne. They constructed an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian H_QQ(E) that includes coupling between discrete shell-model states and the continuum of scattering states, with the residual coupling generated by a contact force V0 δ(r1-r2). By scanning the energy dependence of eigenvalues over a range of the continuum coupling strength V0, they identified real-energy crossings and width crossings that evolve into exceptional points at specific combinations of V0 and scattering energy E.

## Key numbers
- The lowest exceptional point for Jπ=1- states in 16Ne occurs at critical coupling V0(cr) = -1617.4 MeV·fm3 and energy E = 2.33 MeV.
- Experimental decay widths of the ground state 1/2_1+ and first excited state 5/2_1+ in 15F are reproduced using V0 = -3500 ± 450 MeV·fm3 and V0 = -1100 ± 50 MeV·fm3, respectively.
- All six possible 1- exceptional points (maximal number for 3 SM states) exist at E < 20 MeV in the physical range of V0 values (1100 MeV·fm3 < |V0| < 3500 MeV·fm3).
- The 2+ doublet in 8Be has resonance energies and widths of 16623 ± 3 keV, 107 ± 0.5 keV and 16925 ± 3 keV, 74.4 ± 0.4 keV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the study of exceptional points from simple models and microwave cavity experiments (Philipp et al., 2000) to realistic nuclear many-body systems using the SMEC framework. It builds on earlier analyses of the 2+ doublet in 8Be by von Brentano (1990) and Hernandez and Mondragon (1994), who showed that true resonance crossing can be obtained by varying two parameters. The finding that exceptional points exist for physical coupling strengths in 16Ne enables experimental searches for these degeneracies in nuclear scattering, providing a stringent test of effective nucleon-nucleon interactions and configuration mixing in open quantum systems near the drip lines.
