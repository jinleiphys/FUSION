# Gluon confinement and the two IR solutions

> We examine the two solutions (massive and scaling) for the covariant Yang-Mills Dyson-Schwinger equations within stochastic quantization, and find that the scaling solution does not survive outside Landau gauge. We also see that the (rainbow) massive solution has less Faddeev-Popov effective action. Finally, we argue that gluon confinement has only been marginally established in experiment and suggest further empirical work.

## Key claim

The scaling solution for Yang-Mills Dyson-Schwinger equations exists only in Landau gauge (the limit a→0 of stochastic quantization) and does not survive for finite gauge-fixing parameter a, while the massive (decoupling) solution exists for both Landau gauge and finite a; the massive solution also has lower Faddeev-Popov effective action than the scaling solution.

## Method

The authors solved the rainbow Dyson-Schwinger equations for the transverse and longitudinal gluon propagator dressing functions Z_T and Z_L within stochastic quantization, using Zwanziger's gauge-fixing procedure with a parameter a that controls the gauge-restoring force. They evaluated the Faddeev-Popov effective action for bare, massive, and scaling propagator solutions. They also examined experimental constraints on gluon confinement from meson decays and proposed collider-based bounds.

## Key numbers

- The Euclidean gluon propagator for the massive solution behaves as Z(k^2)/k^2 ∝ 1/(k^2 + m^2), with a dynamically generated gluon mass scale.
- The scaling solution has power-law behaviors Z(k^2)/k^2 ∝ (k^2)^(2κ-1) and G(k^2)/k^2 ∝ (k^2)^(-κ-1) with κ > 0.5.
- A liberated gluon would have a mean free path of about 0.6 cm in detector material, based on the estimated gluon-proton cross section.
- The bound on Υ → ggg is σ_(b\bar{b})→ggg < 0.1 μbarn, with about 40 keV of the Υ width currently unaccounted for.
- Gluon liberation bounds at the μbarn to nbarn level should be expected in pp collisions at the LHC.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate between the massive (decoupling) solution found in lattice simulations by Cucchieri (2009) and the scaling solution studied by Alkofer (2003), using stochastic quantization as developed by Parisi (1980) and Zwanziger (1988). The finding that the scaling solution only exists in Landau gauge suggests it may be a feature of Gribov horizon formation. The paper also builds on earlier empirical work by Perl (2009) on quark confinement and by Bergsma (1984) on searches for free quarks, and proposes new experimental searches for free gluons at hadron colliders to address what the authors argue is a lack of solid empirical evidence for gluon confinement.
