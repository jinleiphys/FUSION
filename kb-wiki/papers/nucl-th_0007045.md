# On the origin of order from random two-body interactions

> We investigate the origin of order in the low-lying spectra of many-body systems with random two-body interactions. Contrary to the common belief our study based both on analytical as well as on numerical arguments shows that these are the higher $J$-sectors whose ground states are more orderly than the ones in the J=0-sector. A predominance of J=0 ground states turns out to be the result of putting on together states with different characteristic energy scales from different $J$-sectors.

# On the origin of order from random two-body interactions

## Key claim
Contrary to the prevailing interpretation that J=0 ground states are most ordered in many-body systems with random two-body interactions, the higher J-sectors (particularly J=2 and J=4) actually exhibit more orderly ground states than the J=0 sector. The apparent predominance of J=0 ground states is an artifact of mixing states with different characteristic energy scales from different J-sectors.

## Method
The authors analyze a shell-model system of 6 identical particles in the sd shell (identified as 22O) using the Random Quasiparticle Ensemble (RQE) and Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE). They derive analytical expressions for the probability distributions of off-diagonal matrix elements in different J-sectors, showing that the geometrical factors for J=0 produce Gaussian-like tails while J>0 sectors exhibit slower exponential fall-off. They quantify the degree of order using information entropy normalized to the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) limit and compare ground state energy gap distributions using two different definitions of the average level spacing.

## Key numbers
- The model consists of 6 identical particles in the sd shell, with all single-particle energies set to zero
- Number of states per J-sector: M0=14, M1=19, M2=33, M3=29, M4=26
- The J=0 distribution of off-diagonal matrix elements decays as exp(-|v|^2) for large v, while J>0 sectors decay as exp(-|v|) |v|^(N/2-1)

## Context
This work extends the finding by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) that J=0 ground states predominate in random two-body interactions, which was previously unexplained. The authors contradict the interpretation of that result, showing that the effect is an artifact of mixing J-sectors with different energy scales. The paper also builds on the Two-Body Random Ensemble formalism of French and Wong (1970) and the Random Quasiparticle Ensemble of Johnson (1998), while providing a more adequate approach than the multipole expansion method of Zelevinsky, Brown, Frazier and Horoi (1996) for understanding matrix element distributions in realistic shell-model calculations. This work enables a corrected understanding of how order emerges from randomness in nuclear many-body systems and identifies J=2 and J=4 as the sectors where ordering is strongest.
