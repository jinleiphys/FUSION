# Connecting the nuclear EoS to the interplay between fusion and quasifission processes in low-energy nuclear reactions

> Within the Time Dependent Hartree Fock (TDHF) approach, we investigate the impact of several ingredients of the nuclear effective interaction, such as incompressibility, symmetry energy, effective mass, derivative of the Lane potential and surface terms on the exit channel (fusion vs quasifission) observed in the reaction $^{238}$U+$^{40}$Ca, close to the Coulomb barrier. Our results show that all the ingredients listed above contribute to the competition between fusion and quasifission processes, however the leading role in determining the outcome of the reaction is played by incompressibility, symmetry energy and the isoscalar coefficient of the surface term. This study unravels the complexity of the fusion and quasifission reaction dynamics and helps to understand the microscopic processes responsible for the final outcome of low energy heavy ion collisions in terms of relevant features of the nuclear effective interaction and associated equation of state (EoS).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the competition between fusion and quasifission in the reaction $^{238}$U+$^{40}$Ca near the Coulomb barrier is sensitive to multiple ingredients of the nuclear effective interaction, but the leading roles are played by incompressibility, symmetry energy, and the isoscalar coefficient of the surface term; the abstract's claim that "all ingredients contribute" is qualified by the finding that the outcome (fusion vs. quasifission) is primarily determined by these three specific properties.

## Method

The authors performed Time Dependent Hartree Fock (TDHF) calculations for central collisions of $^{238}$U+$^{40}$Ca at a center-of-mass energy of 203 MeV and zero impact parameter, using a 3D lattice mesh of 96×40×20 points with a mesh step of 0.8 fm and a time step of 0.36 fm/c. They employed a set of Skyrme effective interactions (based on the SAMi-J family) that systematically vary one nuclear matter property at a time, incompressibility, symmetry energy, effective mass, derivative of the Lane potential, and isoscalar/isovector surface terms, while keeping other properties fixed relative to a reference interaction (SAMi-J31). The reaction outcome was diagnosed by monitoring the quadrupole moment time evolution and density contour plots.

## Key numbers

- The reaction $^{238}$U+$^{40}$Ca was studied at $E_{cm}=203$ MeV and impact parameter b=0 fm.
- The 3D lattice mesh was $96\times 40\times 20$ with a mesh step of 0.8 fm and a time step $\Delta t = 0.36$ fm/c; the initial distance between nuclei was 26.4 fm.
- For the SAMi-J interactions, the isoscalar effective mass is $m^*_s = 0.675~m$ and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting is $m^*_n - m^*_p = 0.023~mI$ at saturation density, with the parameter $f_I = -0.0251$.
- The three SAMi-J interactions have symmetry energy values J = 27, 31, and 35 MeV at saturation density; for $^{238}$U, the neutron skin thickness varies from 0.125 fm (SAMi-J27) to 0.285 fm (SAMi-J35).
- For side collisions, SAMi-J35 (J=35 MeV) leads to quasifission, while SAMi-J27 and SAMi-J31 lead to fusion.

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of the $^{238}$U+$^{40}$Ca reaction by Oberacker (2014) and Umar (2015), which investigated the reaction for a specified EoS, by systematically varying individual nuclear matter properties to decouple their effects. The study connects the reaction dynamics to the nuclear Equation of State (EoS) and enables future comparisons with experimental data to constrain poorly known aspects of the nuclear interaction, such as incompressibility and symmetry energy, which are also relevant to nuclear structure and astrophysics.
