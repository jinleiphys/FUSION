# Two-pion production processes, chiral symmetry and NN interaction in the medium

> We study the two-pion propagator in the nuclear medium. This quantity appears in the $π-π$ T-matrix and we show that it also enters the QCD scalar susceptibility. The medium effects on this propagator are due to the influence of the individual nucleon response to a scalar field through their pion clouds. This response is appreciably increased by the nuclear environment. It produces an important convergence effect between the scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities, reflecting the reshaping of the scalar strengh observed in $2π$ production experiments. While a large modification of the $σ$ propagator follows, due to its coupling to two pion states, we show that the NN potential remains instead unaffected.

# Two-pion production processes, chiral symmetry and NN interaction in the medium

## Key claim

The medium-induced modification of the two-pion propagator produces a significant convergence between the scalar and pseudoscalar QCD susceptibilities at normal nuclear matter density, reflecting partial chiral symmetry restoration; however, despite large medium effects on the sigma propagator, the NN potential remains unaffected because the scalar meson responsible for nuclear binding is the chiral-invariant s field rather than the sigma meson.

## Method

The authors studied the two-pion propagator in nuclear matter by dressing pion lines with particle-hole and delta-hole excitations in the RPA ring approximation, using the linear sigma model with a symmetry-conserving 1/N expansion. They computed the QCD scalar susceptibility from the derivative of the quark condensate with respect to the pion mass squared, relating it to the zero-energy two-pion propagator. The effective nucleonic scalar susceptibility was evaluated by comparing the free nucleon result (obtained from the longitudinal response function in the dilute limit) with the in-medium result at normal density, exploring dependence on the form factor cutoff Lambda and Landau-Migdal parameters.

## Key numbers

- Free nucleon scalar susceptibility at zero density ranges from -5.5 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} (Lambda = 5 m_pi) to -6.7 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} (Lambda = 1000 MeV), depending on the form factor cutoff.
- At normal nuclear matter density rho_0, the effective nucleonic scalar susceptibility increases in magnitude to between -7.3 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} and -11.3 x 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} for (g_{pi N Delta}/g_{pi NN})^2 = 3.8, depending on Lambda and the Landau-Migdal parameter g'_Delta.
- The ratio of the scalar susceptibility to the vacuum pseudoscalar susceptibility at rho_0 is approximately 0.45, while the pseudoscalar susceptibility in the same units is approximately 0.65.
- The medium correction to the sigma propagator at zero energy is approximately 4 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2} at rho_0, compared to the bare vacuum value of approximately 2 x 10^{-6} MeV^{-2} for a sigma mass of 700 MeV.
- At 1.6 rho_0, the overall scalar susceptibility ratio becomes approximately 0.47, close to its value at rho_0.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of two-pion production experiments on nuclei (Schuck et al. 1988, Chanfray et al. 1991) by establishing a link between the observed softening of scalar strength near the 2pi threshold and chiral symmetry restoration through the QCD scalar susceptibility. The authors build on their previous distinction (Chanfray et al. 2002) between the sigma meson as chiral partner of the pion and the scalar meson exchange of nuclear physics, showing that the chiral-invariant s field (introduced via polar coordinates) is the relevant mediator for nuclear binding and remains unaffected by medium modifications. The results enable a unified understanding of how the same two-pion propagator governs both the 2pi production data and the convergence of scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities, while resolving the apparent paradox that large sigma propagator modifications do not generate strong many-body forces in the NN potential.
