# Calculation of the ${}^6$Li ground state within the hyperspherical harmonic basis

> We have studied the solution of the six-nucleon bound state problem using the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) approach. For this study we have considered only two-body nuclear forces. In particular we have used a chiral nuclear potential evolved with the similarity renormalization group unitary transformation. A restricted basis has been selected by performing a careful analysis of the convergence of different HH classes. Finally, the binding energy and other properties of ${}^6$Li ground state are calculated and compared with the results obtained by other techniques. Then, we present a calculation of matrix elements relevant for direct dark matter search involving ${}^6$Li. The results obtained demonstrate the feasibility of using the HH method to perform calculation beyond $A = 4$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method can be successfully applied to the six-nucleon bound state problem for ${}^6$Li, but only when using a carefully restricted basis selected by convergence analysis of different HH classes and employing a softened chiral potential evolved via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation; the method is not yet demonstrated for "bare" chiral interactions without SRG evolution.

## Method

The six-nucleon Schrödinger equation was solved using a variational expansion of the wave function in hyperspherical harmonic functions, with the basis restricted by selecting states based on the sum of particle angular momenta ($\ell_{\text{sum}}$) and by classifying HH states into six classes (C1 through C6) according to their orbital angular momentum components and correlation structure. Only two-body nuclear forces were considered, specifically a chiral nuclear potential evolved with the SRG unitary transformation, and the Hamiltonian matrix elements were computed using transformation coefficients to handle antisymmetrization and eliminate linearly dependent states.

## Key numbers

- The number of independent antisymmetric HH states for $K=10$, $L=0$, $S=1$, $J=1$, $T=0$, $\pi=+$ is 509, compared to 187,842 total antisymmetric states for that channel.
- The hyperradial variational parameter $\gamma$ was optimized in the range 3.5–5.5 fm$^{-1}$, with a chosen value of $\gamma = 4$ fm$^{-1}$.
- Convergence to the third decimal digit in binding energy required a maximum number of Laguerre polynomials $l_{max}=16$.
- The complete calculation included about 7000 HH states.
- The value $\hbar^2/m = 41.47$ MeV fm$^2$ was used for all potentials.

## Context

This work extends the HH approach previously applied to $A=4$ systems by Viviani (2005) to the $A=6$ sector, addressing the challenge of describing the clustered $\alpha$-deuteron structure of ${}^6$Li. The results enable future inclusion of three-body forces and extension to nuclei up to $A=8$, and provide an independent benchmark for comparison with No-Core-Shell-Model (NCSM) calculations using the same SRG-evolved chiral potentials.
