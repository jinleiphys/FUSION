# Two-loop Jet-Function and Jet-Mass for Top Quarks

> We compute the two-loop heavy quark jet-function in the heavy quark limit. This is one of the key ingredients in next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and next-to-next-to-leading-log order (NNLL) computations of the invariant mass distribution of top-jets at a future e+e- collider. The shape of the top invariant mass distribution is affected by large logs which we compute at NNLL order. Exploiting the non-abelian exponentiation theorem, a definition of the top jet-mass is given which is transitive and whose renormalization group evolution is determined by the cusp-anomalous dimension to all orders in perturbation theory. Relations of the jet-mass to the pole, MSbar, and 1S masses are presented at two-loop order.

## Key claim

The paper computes the two-loop heavy quark jet-function in the heavy quark limit, which is a necessary ingredient for NNLO and NNLL predictions of the top invariant mass distribution at a future e+e- collider; the authors also introduce a definition of the top jet-mass that is transitive and whose renormalization group evolution is determined by the cusp-anomalous dimension to all orders, but this definition relies on the non-abelian exponentiation theorem and is presented as a scheme that avoids the infrared sensitivity of the pole mass.

## Method

The authors compute the two-loop heavy quark jet-function using HQET, reducing the computation to vacuum matrix elements of Wilson lines. They use dimensional regularization with d=4-2epsilon and the MSbar scheme for renormalization, and exploit the non-abelian exponentiation theorem to show that the position-space jet function exponentiates. The two-loop result is obtained by combining the known two-loop heavy quark propagator from Broadhurst and Grozin with direct computation of the remaining Feynman diagrams in Feynman gauge, treating all quarks other than top as massless with n_f flavors.

## Key numbers

* The top quark width is Gamma_t = 1.43 GeV, which acts as an infrared cutoff for the top quark since tops decay before they hadronize.
* The latest Tevatron analyses give m_t = 170.9 +/- 1.8 GeV, a measurement at the 1% level.
* A 2 GeV upward shift in m_t causes the indirect determination of the Higgs mass central value to shift upward by 15%, and the 95% CL bound m_H < 182 GeV shifts upward by the same amount.
* The two-loop coefficients of the cusp anomalous dimension and the non-cusp anomalous dimension are given in terms of the color factors C_A, C_F, and n_f, with the beta-function coefficients beta_0 = (11 C_A/3) - (2 n_f/3), beta_1 = (34 C_A^2/3) - (10 C_A n_f/3) - 2 C_F n_f, and beta_2 = (2857 C_A^3/54) + (C_F^2 - 205 C_F C_A/18 - 1415 C_A^2/54) n_f + (11 C_F/9 + 79 C_A/54) n_f^2.

## Context

This work extends the factorization theorem for e+e- -> t tbar far from threshold derived by Fleming et al. (2007), which separates the cross-section into hard functions, heavy-quark jet functions, and a soft function. The two-loop jet-function computation is the first step toward NNLO and NNLL predictions for the invariant mass spectrum d^2 sigma / dM_t^2 dM_tbar^2, and the jet-mass scheme introduced here provides a well-defined mass anomalous dimension at any order, unlike definitions based on cutoff moments or peak locations. The results enable resummation of large logs in the cross-section that modify the invariant mass distribution, and the two-loop relations of the jet-mass to the pole, MSbar, and 1S masses are presented.
