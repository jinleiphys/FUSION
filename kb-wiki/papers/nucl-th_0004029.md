# In-Plane Elliptic Flow of Resonance Particles in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We analyze the second Fourier coefficient $v_2$ of the pion azimuthal distribution in non-central heavy-ion collisions in a relativistic hydrodynamic model. The exact treatment of the decay kinematics of resonances leads to almost vanishing azimuthal anisotropy of pions near the midrapidity, while the matter elliptic flow is in-plane at freeze-out. In addition, we reproduce the rapidity dependence of $v_2$ for pions measured in non-central Pb + Pb collisions at 158$A$ GeV. This suggests that resonance particles as well as stable particles constitute the in-plane flow and are important ingredients for the understanding of the observed pion flow.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of pion elliptic flow near midrapidity in non-central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV does not necessarily indicate partial thermalization; instead, it can be explained by the decay kinematics of resonance particles within a fully thermalized hydrodynamic model, where the Jacobian singularity in the Lorentz transformation from resonance rest frames to the laboratory frame causes pions from resonance decays to have nearly vanishing azimuthal anisotropy even though the underlying matter flow is strongly in-plane.

## Method

The author performed fully three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV, using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition between a quark-gluon plasma phase (free gas of quarks and gluons) and a hadron resonance gas phase (all baryons and mesons up to 2 GeV mass with excluded volume correction). The momentum distributions of hadrons were obtained via the Cooper-Frye formula at a freeze-out energy density of 60 MeV/fm^3, and negative pions from decays of rho, omega, K*, and Delta resonances were included by integrating over the resonance momentum distribution with the appropriate Jacobian for the Lorentz transformation to the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- The observed v2 for charged pions in non-central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV is of the order of four percent around midrapidity (Y=2.92) for low transverse momentum (50 < pT < 350 MeV/c) at impact parameters 6.5 < b < 8.0 fm.
- The critical velocity for the Jacobian singularity in rho -> pi pi decays is V* ~ 0.93c.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is t0 = 1.44 fm/c after the two nuclei touched.
- The central energy density E0 = 3.9 GeV/fm^3 and baryon number density nB0 = 0.46 fm^{-3} were used.
- The freeze-out temperature used in the simple model calculation was Tf = 120 MeV.
- The critical temperature at zero baryon density is Tc(nB=0) = 160 MeV.
- Pions from resonance decays reduce v2 for total pions by about 26% at midrapidity.
- When integrating over full pT range, the reduction factor is 11%.

## Context

This work extends the two-dimensional hydrodynamic model of Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (1999), which assumed Bjorken scaling and could not obtain the rapidity dependence of elliptic flow, and which found only a 10-15% reduction from resonance decays. The paper contradicts the interpretation of Heiselberg and Levy (1999) and Voloshin and Poskanzer (2000) that the suppression of elliptic flow is due to partial thermalization, instead arguing that full thermalization including resonances explains the data. The results enable future comparisons with cascade calculations such as RQMD (Liu et al., 1999) and UrQMD (Soff et al., 1999) to see if the Jacobian singularity mechanism is also present in those approaches.
