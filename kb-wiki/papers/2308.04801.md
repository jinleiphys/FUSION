# STAR Experimental Highlights at Hard Probes 2023

> We highlight the STAR experiment's hard probes results, including jets and heavy flavor production in heavy-ion collisions to study the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. Various jet-substructure observables in proton-proton collisions are presented to explore both perturbative and non-perturbative regimes of quantum chromodynamics. Finally, we discuss the STAR experiment's hard probes physics program for ongoing data taking.

## Key claim

The paper presents STAR experiment highlights from the Hard Probes 2023 conference, claiming that jet-substructure observables in proton-proton collisions at RHIC reveal a universal momentum scale for the confinement transition, and that various jet-medium interaction signatures in heavy-ion collisions provide evidence for quark-gluon plasma properties; however, the results are presented as preliminary or conference-level findings with ongoing analyses, and the claims about universal scales and QGP properties are qualified by the need for further theoretical comparisons and data.

## Method

The STAR experiment used the Time Projection Chamber (TPC), Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter (BEMC), Time-of-Flight (TOF) detector, Muon Telescope Detector (MTD), and Event-Plane Detectors (EPD) to measure jets, charged hadrons, and heavy-flavor particles in proton-proton, gold-gold, and isobar (ruthenium-ruthenium and zirconium-zirconium) collisions at center-of-mass energies of 200 GeV per nucleon pair. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R = 0.2, 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6, and jet-substructure observables including energy-energy correlators, SoftDrop groomed observables, and generalized angularities were measured and compared to perturbative QCD calculations and Monte Carlo event generators (PYTHIA-6 Perugia tune and PYTHIA-8 Detroit tune). Heavy-flavor measurements focused on J/psi and Upsilon production in various collision systems, with nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow coefficients extracted.

## Key numbers

- Energy-energy correlator (EEC) measured in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for jets with R = 0.4 and 0.6, with jet transverse momentum ranges 15 < pT,jet < 20 GeV/c and 30 < pT,jet < 50 GeV/c; the value of DeltaR * pT,jet at the transition region is between 2-3 GeV, independent of pT,jet range
- SoftDrop and CollinearDrop observables measured for jets with R = 0.4 in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for 10 < pT,jet < 30 GeV/c
- Recoil jet yield ratios between R = 0.2 and 0.5 (Rbrtwofive) measured for direct photon and pi0 triggers with trigger transverse energies 11 < ETtrig < 15 GeV and 15 < ETtrig < 20 GeV in 0-15% central gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Jet acoplanarity measured for semi-inclusive direct photon+jet and pi0+jet with R = 0.5 and 11 < ETtrig < 15 GeV in 0-15% central gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Proton-to-pion ratio (p+anti-p)/(pi+ + pi-) measured within jets with R = 0.3 and reconstructed charged jet pT,jet > 10 GeV/c in proton-proton and 0-20% central gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Inclusive charged hadron nuclear modification factor (R_AA) measured with pT > 5.1 GeV/c in isobar (ruthenium-ruthenium and zirconium-zirconium) collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Jet elliptic flow (v2) measured as a function of jet pT for R = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.5 in isobar collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- J/psi R_AA measured as a function of average number of participating nucleons in isobar collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Upsilon(2S) and Upsilon(3S) states significantly more suppressed than Upsilon(1S) in gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (published in STAR:2022rpk)
- Planned data collection: 20 billion minimum bias and 40 nb^-1 triggered gold-gold collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in 2023 and 2025; 235 pb^-1 of proton-proton collisions and 1.3 pb^-1 of proton-gold collisions in 2024

## Context

This conference summary extends the STAR experiment's long-standing program of hard probes measurements at RHIC, building on prior jet quenching observations by Wang (1994) and heavy-flavor suppression measurements by the STAR collaboration (2022) and Dong (2019). The results on jet-substructure observables in proton-proton collisions provide baselines for future heavy-ion measurements and test perturbative QCD calculations at RHIC energies, while the new measurements of jet acoplanarity and intra-jet broadening in gold-gold collisions represent first observations at RHIC that complement similar studies at the LHC. The planned high-luminosity data taking through 2025 will enable precision measurements of QGP transport properties and the microstructure of the medium through jet and heavy-flavor observables.
