# Quasi-Elastic Neutrino Reactions on Carbon and Lead Nuclei

> We examine neutral-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus reactions on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb targets. We use the relativistic mean field theory approach to describe the nuclear dynamics. We compute the cross sections for the scattering of 150-MeV, 500-MeV and 1000-MeV neutrinos on a $^{12}$C target and study the effect of the strange-quark content of the nucleon which appears in these reactions via the isoscalar weak current. We compare our results with the data of the MiniBooNE experiment for mineral oil (CH$_2$). We also calculate the cross section for the quasi-elastic neutron knockout reaction of 20 to 60-MeV neutrinos on a $^{208}$Pb target which is relevant to plans to use lead as a target material in future supernova neutrino detectors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that relativistic mean-field calculations of neutral-current quasi-elastic neutrino scattering on carbon and lead can reproduce the shape of MiniBooNE data, but the distorted-wave calculations systematically underestimate the measured cross section at low momentum transfer (below about 0.7 GeV²); including a specific set of strange-quark parameters (ρˢ=2, μˢ=-0.5, gˢ_A=-0.1) brings the distorted-wave results closer to the data, though the plane-wave approximation with no strangeness fits the full Q² range within error bars.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) and relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) to compute neutral-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections for ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb targets. Bound nucleon wave functions are obtained from symmetrized Wood-Saxon potentials, and continuum wave functions for the knocked-out nucleon use the energy- and A-dependent optical potential of Cooper et al. The weak nuclear current includes vector form factors (with strangeness contributions F₁ˢ, F₂ˢ) and an axial form factor (with strangeness parameter gˢ_A), using the Weinberg angle sin²θ_W ≈ 0.23143 and axial mass M_A = 1.026 GeV.

## Key numbers

* For ¹²C at E_ν = 500 MeV, the proton-to-neutron cross-section ratio lies between 0.5 and 0.7 as a function of knocked-out nucleon kinetic energy.
* For ²⁰⁸Pb at E_ν = 40 MeV, the plane-wave total cross section for neutral-current neutron knockout is 52.2 × 10⁻⁴⁵ m²; at E_ν = 60 MeV it is 224 × 10⁻⁴⁵ m².
* The total number of quasi-elastic one-neutron-knockout events predicted for HALO-1 (79 tonnes of lead) from a supernova at 10 kpc with total energy 5 × 10⁵² erg is 0.54 events for all three neutrino flavours.
* The fluence of electron neutrinos from a supernova peaks at about 1.7 × 10¹⁰ 1/(MeV·cm²) around E_ν = 8-9 MeV and decreases by one order of magnitude at E_ν = 25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering by Meucci et al. (2004) and the authors of Ref. NPA773 (2006), who used RDWIA for charged- and neutral-current reactions on ¹²C and studied sensitivity to strangeness and final-state interactions. The carbon results are compared with MiniBooNE data from Aguilar-Arevalo et al. (2008). The lead calculations are motivated by the HALO detector at SNOLAB, part of the SuperNova Early Warning System (SNEWS), and are compared with prior calculations by Kolbe and Langanke (2001) and Engel et al. (2003) for neutrino-induced reactions on lead. The quasi-elastic knockout contribution computed here is intended to be added to cross sections based on intermediate excited states of the nucleus.
