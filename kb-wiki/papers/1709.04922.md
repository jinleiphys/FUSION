# The Structure of the Proton in the LHC Precision Era

> We review recent progress in the determination of the parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the proton, with emphasis on the applications for precision phenomenology at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). First of all, we introduce the general theoretical framework underlying the global QCD analysis of the quark and gluon internal structure of protons. We then present a detailed overview of the hard-scattering measurements, and the corresponding theory predictions, that are used in state-of-the-art PDF fits. We emphasize here the role that higher-order QCD and electroweak corrections play in the description of recent high-precision collider data. We present the methodology used to extract PDFs in global analyses, including the PDF parametrization strategy and the definition and propagation of PDF uncertainties. Then we review and compare the most recent releases from the various PDF fitting collaborations, highlighting their differences and similarities. We discuss the role that QED corrections and photon-initiated contributions play in modern PDF analysis. We provide representative examples of the implications of PDF fits for high-precision LHC phenomenological applications, such as Higgs coupling measurements and searches for high-mass New Physics resonances. We conclude this report by discussing some selected topics relevant for the future of PDF determinations, including the treatment of theoretical uncertainties, the connection with lattice QCD calculations, and the role of PDFs at future high-energy colliders beyond the LHC.

# The Structure of the Proton in the LHC Precision Era

## Key claim
The review argues that parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their associated uncertainties now represent one of the dominant theoretical uncertainties for key LHC measurements such as Higgs boson couplings and searches for new high-mass resonances, but that the QCD factorization framework remains in remarkably good shape despite being pushed to unprecedented precision levels where data statistical uncertainties reach the per-mile level and systematic errors are at the few-percent level.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical foundations of global QCD analysis, including QCD factorization theorems for deep-inelastic scattering and hadronic collisions, and the DGLAP evolution equations. They then survey the experimental datasets and state-of-the-art theoretical calculations (including NNLO QCD corrections) used in modern PDF fits, compare the methodologies of different PDF fitting collaborations (CT14, MMHT14, NNPDF3.0, HERAPDF2.0, and others), and present representative phenomenological applications at the LHC including Higgs coupling measurements, high-mass resonance searches, and precision electroweak parameter determinations.

## Key numbers
- PDF uncertainties represent one of the dominant theoretical uncertainties for the determination of Higgs boson couplings (de Florian et al., 2016)
- LHC measurements now achieve statistical uncertainties at the "per-mile level" and systematic errors at the "few-percent level"
- The PDF4LHC15 sets combine CT14, MMHT14, and NNPDF3.0 using the Monte Carlo method (Watt, 2012)
- DGLAP splitting functions are known up to O(alpha_s^3) (NNLO) (Moch, Vogt, and Vermaseren, 2004; Vogt, 2004)
- The charm structure function F_2^c can contribute up to 25% of the total inclusive neutral current structure function F_2^p at HERA at small x and Q^2
- Heavy quark structure function calculations in the GM-VFNS scheme (FONLL) match massive and massless schemes at small and large Q values respectively (Forte et al., 2010)
- Massive coefficient functions for heavy quark production are known up to O(alpha_s^2) for both neutral-current (Laenen et al., 1992) and charged-current (Berger et al., 2016; Gao et al., 2017) scattering

## Context
This review extends and updates earlier PDF review works including Rojo (2015), Butterworth et al. (2015), Ball et al. (2012), Alekhin et al. (2011), Forte (2010, 2013), and Accardi et al. (2016). It covers recent breakthroughs including NNLO QCD calculations for top quark differential distributions (Czakon et al., 2016), inclusive jets (Currie et al., 2016), dijets (Currie et al., 2017), isolated photons (Campbell et al., 2016), and Z boson pT distributions (Boughezal et al., 2015; Ridder et al., 2016). The review enables future work on quantifying theoretical uncertainties from missing higher-order terms, incorporating lattice QCD constraints on PDFs, and applying PDFs to future collider designs such as the Large Hadron electron Collider or a 100 TeV proton-proton collider.
