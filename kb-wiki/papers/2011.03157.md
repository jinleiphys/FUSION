# Variational approach with the superposition of the symmetry-restored quasi-particle vacua for nuclear shell-model calculations

> We propose a variational calculation scheme utilizing the superposition of the angular-momentum, parity, number projected quasiparticle vacua, that is especially suitable for applying to medium-heavy nuclei in shell-model calculations. We derive a formula for the energy variance with quasi-particle vacua and apply the energy-variance extrapolation to the present scheme for further precise estimation of the exact shell-model energy. The validity of the method is presented for the shell-model calculation of $^{132}$Ba in the $50 \leq Z,N \leq 82$ model space. We also discuss the feasibility of this scheme in the case of the $^{150}$Nd in the $50 \leq Z \leq 82$ and $82 \leq Z \leq 126$ model space and demonstrate that its neutrinoless-double-beta-decay matrix element is obtained showing good convergence.

## Key claim

The proposed quasi-particle vacua shell model (QVSM) provides a variational upper limit to the exact shell-model energy that converges much faster than the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) for medium-heavy nuclei, but the computational cost per basis state is about 10 times heavier than the MCSM because of the necessity of number projection.

## Method

The QVSM wave function is constructed as a superposition of angular-momentum, parity, and number projected quasi-particle vacua, where each vacuum is parametrized by complex matrices U and V and determined by minimizing the energy expectation value after projections using the conjugate gradient method. The energy-variance extrapolation method is applied to the QVSM by plotting the energy against the energy variance and fitting with a 2nd-order polynomial to estimate the exact shell-model energy. The method is benchmarked on 132Ba in the 50 ≤ Z,N ≤ 82 model space using the SN100PN interaction, and applied to 150Nd in the 50 ≤ Z ≤ 82 and 82 ≤ N ≤ 126 model space with the Kuo-Herling interaction.

## Key numbers

- The M-scheme dimension of 132Ba is 2.0 × 10^10; the exact shell-model energy was obtained by the conventional Lanczos method with the KSHELL code.
- The QVSM energy of 132Ba with N_b=20 agrees with the exact shell-model energy within 50 keV.
- The MCSM energy of 132Ba with N_b=50 is 440 keV higher than the exact energy and underestimates excitation energies by 100 keV.
- The t=2 truncation energy of 132Ba is 2.3 MeV higher than the exact energy; the t=4 truncation energy is 0.7 MeV higher.
- The extrapolated QVSM energies of 132Ba agree with exact shell-model energies within 10 keV for the 0+1, 2+1, and 4+1 states.
- The extrapolated QVSM energies of the lowest three 0+ and 2+ states of 132Ba agree with exact values within 20 keV.
- The M-scheme dimension of 68Ni is 5.2 × 10^15, beyond the feasibility of the conventional Lanczos method.
- The M-scheme dimension of 150Nd is 2.2 × 10^14.
- The QVSM energy of 150Nd is about 1 MeV lower than the MCSM energy with N_b=100.
- The QVSM 2+ excitation energy of 150Nd is 240 keV, compared to the experimental value of 130 keV.
- The QVSM quadrupole moment of 150Nd is -1.2 eb, compared to the experimental value of -2.0(5) eb.
- The total 0νββ-decay NME of 150Nd obtained by the QVSM is 5.2.
- The effective charges used for 150Nd and 150Sm are (e_p, e_n) = (1.58, 0.85)e.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) approach developed by the same group (Otsuka et al., various years) by replacing Slater determinants with quasi-particle vacua to treat pairing correlations more efficiently in medium-heavy nuclei. The method is related to the VAMPIR approach (Schmid, 1980s) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) applied to the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Yao et al., 2018-2020). The QVSM enables shell-model calculations for nuclei like 150Nd that are beyond the reach of conventional Lanczos diagonalization, and provides converged neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements that can be compared with quasi-particle random phase approximation results and relativistic energy density functional calculations (Yao, 2015; Yao and Engel, 2016).
