# Fermionic Molecular Dynamics: Multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions and in excited nuclei

> Within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics we investigate fragmentation of a compound system which was created in a heavy-ion collision at a beam energy in the Fermi energy domain and the decay of excited iron nuclei. We show that in FMD many-body correlations play an important role in the formation of fragments.

## Key claim

Within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), many-body correlations play an important role in fragment formation; the time set by the expansion is too short to allow the many-body state to develop the special correlations needed to make a cluster with low enough excitation energy that it can survive, and the absence of multifragmentation in other FMD-type calculations may be due to sensitivity to the two-body Hamiltonian employed rather than to restrictions in the FMD trial state.

## Method

The authors used Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) to simulate the decay of excited $^{56}$Fe nuclei, creating initial states by scaling the ground-state density and by randomly displacing the mean positions of wave packets without changing the density. They also simulated central and semi-peripheral collisions of $^{19}$F + $^{27}$Al at 32 $A$MeV and $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca at 35 $A$MeV. The time evolution of the one-body density in coordinate space was analyzed to study fragment formation.

## Key numbers

- Random displacement of mean positions within 2 fm produced $^{56}$Fe excitation energies of 9.7 $A$MeV and 11.2 $A$MeV; at 11.2 $A$MeV some cases left no residue, and above $E^*=12$ $A$MeV no residues were observed (complete vaporization).
- A sharp transition around $E^*=11$ $A$MeV was found where thermal expansion could not be halted by attractive interaction.
- Scaling the density by a factor 2.2 with small random displacements (0.5 fm) gave $E^*=11.3$ $A$MeV for $^{56}$Fe.
- Scaling mean position vectors by 0.6 with random displacements within 0.3 fm gave $E^*=10$ $A$MeV for $^{56}$Fe.
- Random displacement of mean positions within 0.2 fm gave 1.6 MeV excitation energy per particle for $^{56}$Fe.
- Beam energies used for collisions: 32 $A$MeV ($^{19}$F + $^{27}$Al) and 35 $A$MeV ($^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca).

## Context

This work extends FMD studies by Feldmeier (1990), Feldmeier, Bieler, and Schnack (1995), and Feldmeier and Schnack (1995, 1997) to investigate multifragmentation in both heavy-ion collisions and excited nuclei. The results contrast with other FMD-type calculations by Kiederlen and Danielewicz (1996) and Chomaz, Colonna, and Guarnera (1996), which found no multifragmentation; the authors argue this discrepancy may arise from sensitivity to the two-body Hamiltonian rather than limitations of the FMD trial state. The work enables further study of how non-equilibrium systems can cross equilibrium barriers for multifragmentation and how the liquid-gas phase transition can be investigated through non-equilibrium multifragmentation of wounded spectator matter.
