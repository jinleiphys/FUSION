# The Pion Velocity in Dense Skyrmion Matter

> We have developed a field theory formalism to calculate $in$-$medium$ properties of hadrons within a unified approach that exploits a single Lagrangian to describe simultaneously both matter background and meson fluctuations. In this paper we discuss the consequences on physical observables of a possible phase transition of hadronic matter taking place in the chiral limit. We pay special attention to the pion velocity $v_π$, which controls, through a dispersion relation, the pion propagation in the hadronic medium. The $v_π$ is defined in terms of parameters related to the matrix element in matter of the axial-vector current, namely, the in-medium pion decay constants, $f_t$ and $f_s$. Both of the pion decay constants change dramatically with density and even vanish in the chiral limit when chiral symmetry is restored, but the pion velocity does not go to zero, decreasing at most 10% over the whole density range studied. A possible pseudogap structure is indicated.

## Key claim

The pion velocity in dense skyrmion matter does not vanish at chiral restoration, decreasing at most 10% over the whole density range studied, in contrast to the standard linear sigma model prediction of zero velocity at the critical point. This result depends on the specific degrees of freedom included in the model, particularly the presence of vector mesons and the dilaton field.

## Method

The authors use a modified Skyrme model Lagrangian that includes a scalar dilaton field to incorporate the scale anomaly of QCD, with dense baryonic matter described by a static soliton solution having an FCC crystal structure. Pions are incorporated as fluctuating fields on top of this dense skyrmion matter, and the in-medium pion velocity is extracted from the effective Lagrangian by matching propagators calculated from the background potentials up to second order in the interactions.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium pion velocity decreases at most 10% over the whole density range studied (no specific numerical value for the minimum velocity is given in the truncated text)
- At low density from chiral perturbation theory: $f^t_\pi = f_\pi (1-0.26 \rho/\rho_0)$ and $f^s_\pi = f_\pi (1-1.23 \rho/\rho_0)$, where $\rho_0$ is normal nuclear matter density
- The critical density for chiral phase transition depends on $\mu_\chi = m_\chi/ef_\pi$: for $\mu_\chi=1.25$, $\rho_c = 1.32$; for $\mu_\chi=1.63$, $\rho_c = 2.90$; for $\mu_\chi=2.26$, $\rho_c = 8.15$ (in units of $0.015(ef_\pi)^3$)
- The pseudogap phase transition occurs at density $\rho_p \sim 0.78 \times 0.015(ef_\pi)^3 \sim 0.004 m_\rho^3$
- The chiral phase transition occurs at density $\rho_c \sim 0.67\mu_\chi^3 \times 0.015(ef_\pi)^3 \sim 0.01 m_\chi^3$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of in-medium pion properties using the Skyrme model approach (Lee et al., 2003) and contrasts with two competing predictions for the pion velocity at high temperature: the standard linear sigma model prediction that $v_\pi=0$ at $T=T_c$ (Son and Stephanov, 2002) and the hidden local symmetry/vector manifestation prediction that $v_\pi \sim 1$ near $T_c$ (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003). The results suggest a possible pseudogap phase intermediate between chiral symmetry breaking and restoration, and enable bottom-up studies of how the phase transition from normal matter to deconfined QCD matter proceeds from the hadronic side.
