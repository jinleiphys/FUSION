# The charmonium-like exotic hadron productions in $e^+e^-$ collisions at the BESIII energy with the PACIAE model

> Inspired by the BESIII observation of exotic hadron $G$(3900) in $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar D$ process [PRL 133(2024)081901], we use the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE to simulate the productions of charmonium-like exotic hadrons including $X(3872)$, $Z_{c}(3900)^0$ and $G(3900)$ in the $e^+e^-$ annihilations at $\sqrt s$=4.95 GeV. The charmonium-like candidates are recombined by Dynamically Constrained Phase-space Coalescence model using component mesons $D\bar D$ (for $G(3900)$) or $D\bar D^*/\bar DD^*$ (for $X(3872)$, $Z_{c}(3900)^0$ and $G(3900)$) in the PACIAE simulated final hadronic state. We then calculate, for the first time, the charmonium-like candidate's orbital angular momentum quantum number in its rest frame and perform the spectral classification for each of the above candidates. For $G(3900)$, as its $J^{PC}$ is $1^{--}$, it is identified as the $P$-wave $D\bar D$ or $D\bar D^*/\bar DD^*$ state. Meanwhile, for $X(3872)$ and $Z_{c}(3900)^0$, as their $J^{PC}$s are, respectively, $1^{++}$ and $1^{+-}$, they are identified as the $S$-wave $D\bar D^*/\bar DD^*$ candidates. It is observed that the yield of the $P$-wave $G(3900)$ composed of $D\bar D^*/\bar DD^*$ is significantly larger than that composed of $D\bar D$. Moreover, in the $D\bar D^*/\bar DD^*$ channel, the yield of the $P$-wave $G(3900)$ is around three times as large as the yield of the $S$-wave $Z_{c}(3900)^0$, and it is around two orders of magnitude as large as the yield of the $S$-wave $X(3872)$. Finally, significant discrepancies are observed in the transverse momentum spectra and the rapidity distributions among the $X(3872)$, $Z_{c}(3900)^0$ and $G(3900)$ states. These discrepancies are proposed as valuable criteria for identifying the different charmonium-like exotic hadrons from each other.

# The charmonium-like exotic hadron productions in e+e- collisions at the BESIII energy with the PACIAE model

## Key claim
The authors use the PACIAE+DCPC model to simulate charmonium-like exotic hadron production in e+e- collisions at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV and identify G(3900) as a P-wave D\bar D or D\bar D*/\bar D D* state, while X(3872) and Zc(3900)^0 are identified as S-wave D\bar D*/\bar D D* states, based on their assigned JPC quantum numbers from prior experimental determinations rather than from a model-independent extraction.

## Method
The PACIAE 4.0 model simulates e+e- annihilation at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV, producing a final hadronic state via partonic rescattering, LUND string fragmentation, and hadronic rescattering. Charmonium-like candidates are then recombined from component mesons (D\bar D or D\bar D*/\bar D D*) using the Dynamically Constrained Phase-space Coalescence (DCPC) model, which applies coordinate, momentum, and invariant mass constraints. The orbital angular momentum quantum number L of each candidate is calculated in its rest frame using Eq. (6) and the quantization rule l*^2 = L(L+1)hbar^2, enabling spectral classification via the spectroscopic notation ^{2S+1}L_J.

## Key numbers
- Event-averaged yield of P-wave D\bar D state G(3900): 3.87 x 10^{-4} per event at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV
- Event-averaged yield of P-wave D\bar D*/\bar D D* state G(3900): 6.08 x 10^{-4} per event at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV
- Event-averaged yield of S-wave D\bar D*/\bar D D* state X(3872): 5.81 x 10^{-6} per event at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV
- Event-averaged yield of S-wave D\bar D*/\bar D D* state Zc(3900)^0: 1.97 x 10^{-4} per event at sqrt(s)=4.95 GeV
- In the D\bar D*/\bar D D* channel, the yield of P-wave G(3900) is approximately three times the yield of S-wave Zc(3900)^0
- In the D\bar D*/\bar D D* channel, the yield of P-wave G(3900) is approximately two orders of magnitude larger than the yield of S-wave X(3872)
- Input masses: X(3872) m0=3872.9 MeV/c^2, Zc(3900)^0 m0=3893.0 MeV/c^2, G(3900) m0=3872.5 MeV/c^2
- Input mass uncertainties (half decay widths): X(3872) Delta m=1.95 MeV/c^2, Zc(3900)^0 Delta m=22.1 MeV/c^2, G(3900) Delta m=89.85 MeV/c^2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological Monte Carlo simulation method (PACIAE+DCPC) applied to exotic hadron production in e+e- and pp collisions (She 2024, Tai 2023, Ge 2021, Hui 2022, Zhang 2024). It is directly inspired by the BESIII observation of G(3900) in e+e- -> D\bar D (PRL 133 (2024) 081901) and builds on the identification of G(3900) as a P-wave D\bar D*/\bar D D* resonance by Zhu (2024). The calculated discrepancies in transverse momentum spectra and rapidity distributions among the three states are proposed as experimental criteria to distinguish these charmonium-like exotic hadrons, which have very similar masses and are difficult to separate experimentally.
