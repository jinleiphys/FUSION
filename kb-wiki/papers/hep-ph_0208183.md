# QED Radiative Corrections in Processes of Exclusive Pion Electroproduction

> Formalism for radiative correction (RC) calculation in exclusive pion electroproduction on the proton is presented. A FORTRAN code EXCLURAD is developed for the RC procedure. The numerical analysis is done in the kinematics of current Jefferson Lab experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for calculating QED radiative corrections (RC) in exclusive pion electroproduction that avoids the unphysical splitting parameter between soft and hard photon phase space required by the Mo and Tsai (1969) approach, instead using the covariant infrared divergence cancellation procedure of Bardin and Shumeiko. The authors claim their method is necessary because the Mo and Tsai approach cannot be directly applied to exclusive processes due to reduced phase space for the radiated photon and the need for four structure functions rather than two.

## Method

The authors derived exact formulas for the radiative cross section including bremsstrahlung, vertex corrections, and vacuum polarization contributions, then separated the infrared-divergent part using dimensional regularization following the Bardin-Shumeiko covariant procedure. They implemented the calculation in a FORTRAN code called EXCLURAD, which computes the observed cross section as the sum of a factorized infrared-free term and a non-factorized bremsstrahlung contribution, with exponentiation of soft photon effects. The leading-log approximation was extracted from the exact formulas and verified against a generalized leading-log approach.

## Key numbers

- The maximum inelasticity (missing mass) parameter v_m is given by v_m = (W - m_h)^2 - m_u^2, where m_h is the detected hadron mass and m_u is the undetected hadron mass
- The infrared-divergent vertex correction contribution delta_V equals -2(P_IR + log(m/mu))(l_m - 1) - (1/2)l_m^2 + (3/2)l_m - 2 + pi^2/6, where l_m = log(Q^2/m_e^2)
- The soft-photon contribution delta_S yields 2(P_IR + log(v_bar/(mu M)))(l_m - 1) + log(S'X'/(m^2 M^2)) + S_phi, where S_phi contains dilogarithm and logarithmic terms
- The infrared-free sum delta_VR equals (alpha/pi)[(3/2)l_m - 2 - (1/2)log^2(X'_0/S'_0) + Li_2(1 - Q^2 M^2/(S'_0 X'_0)) - pi^2/6]
- The infrared factor delta_inf equals (alpha/pi)(l_m - 1) log(v_m^2/(S'_0 X'_0))

## Context

This work extends the Bardin-Shumeiko covariant approach previously applied to inclusive scattering (Afanasev et al., 1997; Kukhto and Shumeiko, 1982; Akushevich and Shumeiko, 1994), semi-inclusive reactions (Soroko, 1991; Akushevich and Soroko, 1994), and exclusive diffractive reactions (Akushevich, 1995) to the case of exclusive pion electroproduction. The code EXCLURAD enables RC calculations for current Jefferson Lab experiments with the CLAS detector, where new measurements are expected to cover a wide kinematic range in Q^2 and invariant mass W with improved precision. The formalism does not include box-type diagrams or photon emission from hadrons, which are estimated to be smaller by about an order of magnitude and will be addressed in a separate study.
