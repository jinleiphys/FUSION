# Scaling approach to nuclear structure in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> In high-energy heavy-ion collisions, the initial condition of the produced quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and its evolution are sensitive to collective nuclear structure parameters describing the shape and radial profiles of the nuclei. We find a general scaling relation between these parameters and many experimental observables such as elliptic flow, triangular flow, and particle multiplicity distribution. In particular, the ratios of observables between two isobar systems depend only on the differences of these parameters, but not on the details of the final state interactions, hence offering a new way to constrain the QGP initial condition. Using this scaling relation, we show how the structure parameters of $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr conspire to produce the rich centrality dependences of these ratios, as measured by the STAR Collaboration. Our scaling approach demonstrates that isobar collisions are a precision tool to probe the initial condition of heavy-ion collisions, as well as the collective nuclear structures, including the neutron skin, of the atomic nuclei across energy scales.

## Key claim

The paper finds a general scaling relation where ratios of observables (elliptic flow, triangular flow, multiplicity) between two isobar collision systems depend only on the differences in nuclear structure parameters (quadrupole deformation, octupole deformation, half-density radius, surface diffuseness), not on the details of final-state interactions, provided the variations in these parameters are small.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport model (AMPT v2.26t5) in string-melting mode at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with a partonic cross section of 3.0 mb to simulate generic isobar collisions of 96X+96X. They varied the Woods-Saxon parameters (beta_2, beta_3, R_0, a) one at a time over a range of values, calculated v_2, v_3, and charged particle multiplicity distributions, and extracted the response coefficients c_n in the scaling relation R_O = 1 + c_1 Delta(beta_2^2) + c_2 Delta(beta_3^2) + c_3 Delta(R_0) + c_4 Delta(a). They then constructed the ratios for the specific case of 96Ru+96Ru versus 96Zr+96Zr using the default parameter differences and compared with STAR data.

## Key numbers

- Default structure parameters for 96Ru: beta_2 = 0.162, beta_3 = 0, a = 0.46 fm, R_0 = 5.09 fm
- Default structure parameters for 96Zr: beta_2 = 0.06, beta_3 = 0.20, a = 0.52 fm, R_0 = 5.02 fm
- Parameter differences between Ru and Zr: Delta(beta_2^2) = 0.0226, Delta(beta_3^2) = -0.04, Delta(a) = -0.06 fm, Delta(R_0) = 0.07 fm
- The v_n were calculated using hadrons with 0.2 < p_T < 2 GeV and |eta| < 2
- The nuclear crossing time is 2R_0/gamma less than or equal to 0.1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the approach of using isobar collisions to isolate nuclear structure effects, building on earlier studies by Giacalone (2021) and Jia (2021) that showed isobar ratios can cleanly separate structure differences from final-state dynamics. The scaling relation enables a decoupling of density functional theory calculations from heavy-ion collision modeling, allowing direct extraction of neutron skin differences between isobar pairs. The method can be extended to compare collisions along isotopic chains and to probe modifications of nuclear structure at small partonic longitudinal momentum fraction (small-x) by comparing isobar ratios at RHIC and LHC energies or as a function of rapidity.
