# Constraints on the high-density nuclear equation of state from the phenomenology of compact stars and heavy-ion collisions

> A new scheme for testing nuclear matter equations of state (EsoS) at high densities using constraints from neutron star phenomenology and a flow data analysis of heavy-ion collisions is suggested. An acceptable EoS shall not allow the direct Urca process to occur in neutron stars with masses below $1.5~M_{\odot}$, and also shall not contradict flow and kaon production data of heavy-ion collisions. Compact star constraints include the mass measurements of 2.1 +/- 0.2 M_sun (1 sigma level) for PSR J0751+1807, of 2.0 +/- 0.1 M_sun from the innermost stable circular orbit for 4U 1636-536, the baryon mass - gravitational mass relationships from Pulsar B in J0737-3039 and the mass-radius relationships from quasiperiodic brightness oscillations in 4U 0614+09 and from the thermal emission of RX J1856-3754. This scheme is applied to a set of relativistic EsoS constrained otherwise from nuclear matter saturation properties with the result that no EoS can satisfy all constraints simultaneously, but those with density-dependent masses and coupling constants appear most promising.

## Key claim

No single nuclear equation of state (EoS) from the tested set can simultaneously satisfy all constraints from neutron star phenomenology and heavy-ion collision data, but models with density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings and masses appear most promising.

## Method

The authors applied a five-element test scheme to a set of eight relativistic nuclear matter EsoS (NLρ, NLρδ, DBHF, DD, D³C, KVR, KVOR, and a new DD-F parameterization). The scheme combines constraints from neutron star maximum mass measurements, baryon mass-gravitational mass relationships from the double pulsar system J0737-3039, the prohibition of direct Urca cooling in neutron stars below 1.5 M⊙, mass-radius bounds from quasiperiodic brightness oscillations and thermal emission, and constraints from elliptic flow and kaon production data in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0751+1807 mass: 2.1 ± 0.2 M⊙ (1σ confidence), with 2σ range extending to 1.6 M⊙ lower limit
- Pulsar B in J0737-3039 mass: 1.249 ± 0.001 M⊙, with baryon mass constraint 1.366 M⊙ ≤ M_N ≤ 1.375 M⊙ (no mass loss) or 1.356 M⊙ ≤ M_N ≤ 1.375 M⊙ (with 0.01 M⊙ mass loss)
- Maximum masses of tested EsoS range from 1.83 M⊙ (NLρ) to 2.42 M⊙ (DD and D³C)
- Central densities at maximum mass range from 0.82 fm⁻³ (D³C) to 1.24 fm⁻³ (KVR)
- Saturation densities of models range from 0.1459 fm⁻³ (NLρ, NLρδ) to 0.1810 fm⁻³ (DBHF)
- Incompressibility K ranges from 203.3 MeV (NLρ) to 275.0 MeV (KVOR)
- Symmetry energy derivative L ranges from 55.8 MeV (KVR) to 92.3 MeV (NLρδ)
- DD-F model constructed with flow constraint fixing SNM pressure to 50 MeV fm⁻³ at density 0.48 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the approach of Podsiadlowski (2005) on baryon mass constraints from J0737-3039 and the cooling phenomenology of Blaschke, Grigorian, and Voskresensky (2004). It combines for the first time neutron star mass measurements (Nice and Splaver 2005), mass-radius constraints from Miller (2003) and Trumper (2003), and heavy-ion flow constraints from Danielewicz, Lacey, and Lynch (2002) into a unified test scheme. The results enable future refinement of EoS models by identifying which constraints are most restrictive and by sharpening the flow constraint using new mass measurements.
