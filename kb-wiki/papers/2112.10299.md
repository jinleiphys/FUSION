# Specific heat and its high-order moments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from a multiphase transport model

> Energy dependence of specific heat extracted from temperature fluctuation of Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV was investigated by using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. The results were compared with those from other models and some differences at low $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ were found. To explain the above differences and describe the properties of the hot dense matter at low $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$, a new quantity $C_v^{*}$ was derived for describing specific heat in heavy-ion collisions. It was found that, by using $C_v^{*}$ together with its high order moments (skewness and kurtosis), thermal properties of the hot dense matter can be described and different thermal properties with or without parton process can be clearly distinguished. The proposed observable provides a way to learn the property of QCD matter % phase transition %along with critical point in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a newly derived effective specific heat \(C_v^{*}\), computed from event-by-event kinetic quantities (mean transverse velocity and mean transverse momentum derivative with respect to effective temperature), can describe thermal properties of hot dense matter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and distinguish systems with and without parton processes, even though the standard temperature-fluctuation method yields an almost constant specific heat at low collision energies in the AMPT model.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (both String Melting and Default versions) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV for 0–10% centrality (impact parameter 0–4.7 fm). They first extracted the specific heat \(C_V\) from event-by-event temperature fluctuations of the \(\pi^+\) system, fitting the transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) spectra with a Boltzmann distribution below 1 GeV/\(c\) and removing radial flow to obtain kinetic temperature. Then they derived a new formula \(C_v^{*} = \overline{\beta_{p_T}} \, \partial \overline{p_T} / \partial T|_{T=\overline{T_{eff}}}\) using the mean value theorem of integrals and computed its event-by-event distribution, including its mean, skewness, and kurtosis.

## Key numbers

*   The specific heat \(C_V\) from temperature fluctuation in the AMPT model (this work) is almost constant across \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV, in contrast to the STAR data and HRG model which show a rising trend below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 40\) GeV.
*   The variance of \(T_{kin}\) dominates the variance of \(T_{eff}\); the variance from \(\langle\beta_T\rangle^2\) is smaller by about one order of magnitude, and the linear correlation coefficient \(\eta\) between \(T_{kin}\) and \(\langle\beta_T\rangle^2\) is \(\leq 0.2\).
*   The mean value \(\langle C_v^{*}\rangle\) from the AMPT model shows a slightly decreasing trend with increasing \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\), and its values are similar to those from the QGM, HM, and HM-via-QGM models reported by Sa et al. (2007).
*   An inflection point in \(C_v^{*}\) as a function of \(T_{eff}\) appears around \(T_{eff} = 100\) MeV, depending on the \(p_T\) fitting limits.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of specific heat in heavy-ion collisions using temperature fluctuation methods, such as those by Basu et al. (2016) for STAR data and the HRG model, and by Sa et al. (2007) for HM, QGM, and HM-via-QGM models. The AMPT model results show a depression of \(C_V\) at low \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) compared to the STAR data and HRG model, motivating the derivation of \(C_v^{*}\). The new observable, together with its skewness and kurtosis, is proposed as a way to probe the thermal properties of QCD matter and possibly the critical point, and it can be applied to experimental data with improved precision in particle identification and transverse velocity measurement.
