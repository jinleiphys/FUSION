# Alternative Formulation of the Induced Surface and Curvature Tensions Approach

> We develop a novel method to analyze the excluded volume of the multicomponent mixtures of classical hard spheres in the grand canonical ensemble. The method is based on the Laplace-Fourier transform technique and allows one to account for the fluctuations of the particle number density for the induced surface and curvature tensions equation of state. As a result one can go beyond the Van der Waals approximation by obtaining the suppression of the induced surface and curvature tensions coefficients at moderate and high packing fractions. In contrast to the standard induced surface and curvature tensions equation of state the suppression of these coefficients is not an exponential one, but a power-like one. The obtained alternative equation of state is further generalized to account for higher virial coefficients. This result is straightforwardly generalized to the case of quantum statistics.

# Alternative Formulation of the Induced Surface and Curvature Tensions Approach

## Key claim

The paper develops an alternative formulation of the induced surface and curvature tensions (ISCT) equation of state that accounts for fluctuations of particle number density, leading to a power-like suppression of the surface and curvature tension coefficients at moderate and high packing fractions, rather than the exponential suppression found in the standard ISCT approach. This alternative formulation is valid for multicomponent mixtures of classical hard spheres in the grand canonical ensemble and can be generalized to include higher virial coefficients and quantum statistics.

## Method

The author employs a Laplace-Fourier transform technique to evaluate the grand canonical partition function for multicomponent mixtures of Boltzmann particles with hard-core radii, replacing the standard self-consistent mean radius and mean squared radius approximations with new approximations that depend on the fluctuating particle numbers rather than their thermodynamic averages. The resulting system of equations for pressure, induced surface tension coefficient, and induced curvature tension coefficient is solved analytically, yielding closed-form expressions for the suppression factors ξ₁ and ξ₂ that exhibit power-law behavior. The alternative ISCT equation of state is then compared to the standard ISCT equation of state and to the Carnahan-Starling equation of state for the one-component case, and a generalized form with additional adjustable parameters is proposed to extend the validity range.

## Key numbers

- The standard ISCT equation of state is valid up to packing fractions η ≤ 0.45 for hard spheres, while the van der Waals equation of state is valid only for η ≤ 0.1–0.11.
- For the one-component system with parameters A = 0.57 and B = 0.76, the maximal deviation between the alternative and standard ISCT suppression measures ω_Alt and ω_St is less than 20% for z ≤ 0.2.
- The generalized alternative ISCT equation of state with a single adjustable parameter Ã ≈ 0.0215 reproduces the Carnahan-Starling equation of state with a maximal relative deviation not exceeding 1.5% for packing fractions η ≤ 0.42.
- The best set of parameters for the standard ISCT system that reproduces the five virial coefficients of the Carnahan-Starling equation of state is A = 0.57, B = 0.76, α = 1.07, β = 3.76.

## Context

This work extends the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state developed by Bugaev and collaborators (2010–2020), which was successfully applied to describe hadronic multiplicities in heavy ion collisions from √s_NN = 2.7 GeV to 2.76 TeV with χ²/dof = 1.13, and to reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties and the proton flow constraint with only four adjustable parameters. The standard ISCT equation of state (ISCT1, ISCT2) accurately reproduces the Mansoori-Carnahan-Starling-Leland equation of state for hard spheres up to η ≈ 0.45 and hard discs up to η ≈ 0.7. The alternative formulation weakens the assumptions about mean radius and mean squared radius, allowing for particle number density fluctuations that are important for experimental searches for the critical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram at RHIC BNL, NICA JINR, and FAIR GSI. The power-law suppression found here is similar to the density-dependent interaction recently introduced by Dutra et al. (2016, 2018).
