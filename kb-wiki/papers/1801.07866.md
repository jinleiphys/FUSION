# Directed flow of open charm in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV using a quark coalescence model

> The directed flow ($v_{1}$) of open charm meson ($D^{0}$) is studied in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200~GeV using A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model framework with partonic interactions (string melting version). Within this framework, it is found that although the initial spatial eccentricity ($ε_{1}$) of charm quark is smaller than light quarks, the charm quark $v_{1}$ magnitude is found to be approximately 7 times larger than that of the light u quark at large rapidity. This indicates that the charm quarks can retain more information from initial condition than the light quarks. We have studied the directed flow of $D^{0}$ as a function of rapidity and transverse momentum using quark coalescence as the mechanism for hadron production. Like charm quark, the $D^{0}$ $v_{1}$ magnitude is found to be about 7 times larger than that of the light ($π$) hadrons at large rapidity.

## Key claim
The directed flow ($v_1$) of charm quarks and $D^0$ mesons is approximately 7 times larger than that of light u quarks and pions at large rapidity ($2.0 < |y| < 3.0$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, even though the initial spatial eccentricity ($\epsilon_1$) of charm quarks is 2-3 times smaller than that of light quarks. This result is obtained within the specific framework of the AMPT model with string melting and a dynamical coalescence hadronization scheme, and the factor of 7 applies only at large rapidity, not at mid-rapidity.

## Method
The authors used the string melting version (v2.26) of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, with parton-parton cross section set to 3 mb ($\alpha_S = 0.47$, screening mass $\mu = 3.22$ fm$^{-1}$). They extracted parton-level directed flow for charm and up quarks as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum, then applied a dynamical coalescence model using Wigner phase-space functions to form $D^0$ mesons from $c$ and $\bar{u}$ quarks and pions from $u$ and $\bar{d}$ quarks, with meson size parameters $\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.30$ fm$^2$ for $D^0$ and $\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.44$ fm$^2$ for pions.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark $v_1$ magnitude is approximately 7 times larger than u quark $v_1$ within the range $2.0 < |y| < 3.0$ (full $p_T$ integrated): charm quark $v_1 \approx 0.02$, u quark $v_1 \approx 0.003$.
- $D^0$ meson $v_1$ magnitude is approximately 7 times larger than pion $v_1$ within the range $2.0 < |y| < 3.0$ (full $p_T$ integrated).
- Initial spatial eccentricity $\epsilon_1$ for charm quarks is about 2-3 times smaller than for u quarks in all $p_T$ regions studied (0-5 GeV/c, 0-1 GeV/c, 1-5 GeV/c).
- Parton-parton interaction cross section used in the model: $\sigma_{pp} = 3$ mb.
- Root-mean-square radius for $D^0$ meson: $\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.30$ fm$^2$; for pion: $\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.44$ fm$^2$.

## Context
This work extends AMPT model studies of heavy flavor flow, which previously focused on elliptic flow ($v_2$) of $D^0$ mesons at mid-rapidity (Nasim, 2016; Nasim, 2017), to the directed flow ($v_1$) observable at forward rapidity. The results contrast with hydrodynamic calculations (Sandeep, 2017) that suggest heavy flavor $v_1$ slope is sensitive to initial matter distribution, and with predictions (Santosh, 2017) that transient magnetic fields could induce larger $v_1$ for heavy quarks with opposite sign for charm and anti-charm. The finding that charm hadrons retain more initial-state information than light hadrons motivates future experimental measurements of $D^0$ $v_1$ at RHIC and LHC over a large rapidity window.
