# Light Scalar Decay in Diquark Chiral Effective Theory

> We calculate the decay rate of light scalar mesons, using a diquark chiral effective theory, recently proposed to describe exotic hadrons. In the effective theory the light scalar mesons are postulated to be bound states of diquark and anti-diquark. We find our results are in good agreement with experimental data. The axial couplings of diquarks with pions and kaons are found to be quite small and the perturbation is reliable. It shows that the diquark picture captures the correct physics of light scalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper calculates decay widths of light scalar mesons under the assumption that they are bound states of a diquark and an anti-diquark, finding good agreement with experimental data. The authors hedge that the axial couplings of diquarks with pions and kaons are "quite small" and that the perturbation expansion is therefore reliable, but they also note that the binding energy (280 MeV) is not negligible and must be accounted for in the energy-momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors use the diquark chiral effective theory (DχET), which extends the chiral quark effective theory by including scalar diquark fields as degrees of freedom. They treat the scalar mesons as nonrelativistic bound states of a diquark and an anti-diquark, analogous to positronium, and compute the decay width as the product of the annihilation cross section (diquark + anti-diquark → two Nambu-Goldstone bosons) and the square of the 1S wavefunction at the origin. Tree-level and one-loop amplitudes are evaluated, and the axial couplings \(h_A\) and \(h_A'\) are fitted to the dominant decay modes of \(\sigma\), \(f_0\), and \(a_0\); for the subdominant \(f_0 \to K\bar{K}\) mode, a Breit-Wigner broadening is introduced to account for the phase-space suppression.

## Key numbers

- Diquark masses: \(M_{ud} = 450\ \text{MeV}\), \(M_{us} = M_{ds} = 630\ \text{MeV}\) (from random instanton model and scalar nonet mass fit).
- Binding energy of the scalar nonet: \(B = 280\ \text{MeV}\).
- Yukawa coupling squared: \(g^2 = 3.03\) (best fit to scalar nonet masses).
- Pion decay constant: \(f = F_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\).
- Axial coupling of quarks: \(g_A = 0.75\) (experimental value from the quark model).
- Quark mass (SU(3) symmetric): \(m = 400\ \text{MeV}\).
- Fitted axial couplings (set 1): \(h_A = 1.46\), \(h_A' = -1.66\); resulting decay widths: \(\Gamma_{\sigma \to 2\pi} = 317\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{f_0 \to 2\pi} = 67\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{a_0 \to \eta\pi} = 83\ \text{MeV}\).
- Fitted axial couplings (set 2): \(h_A = 1.20\), \(h_A' = -1.38\); resulting decay widths: \(\Gamma_{\sigma \to 2\pi} = 234\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{f_0 \to 2\pi} = 71\ \text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_{a_0 \to \eta\pi} = 57\ \text{MeV}\).
- Calculated \(\Gamma_{f_0 \to 2K}\): 75 MeV (set 1) and 59 MeV (set 2); experimental range: \(5.6\ \text{MeV}\) to \(32\ \text{MeV}\).
- Experimental full width of \(f_0\): \(\Gamma = 40\ \text{MeV}\) to \(100\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the diquark chiral effective theory proposed by Hong, Sohn, and Zahed (2004) for exotic baryons (pentaquarks) to the decay of light scalar mesons, which Jaffe (1976) had suggested might be four-quark states (\(Q^2\bar{Q}^2\)). The authors compare their results to experimental data from Achasov (2000) and the Particle Data Group (Hagiwara, 2002), and they note that the diquark picture of scalar mesons was also discussed by Maiani (2004). The calculation enables further tests of the diquark model for exotic hadrons and suggests that the same effective theory can describe both pentaquark decays and scalar meson decays.
