# Structure of $^{20}$Ne states in the resonance $^{16}$O+$α$ elastic scattering

> Background The nuclear structure of the cluster bands in $^{20}$Ne presents a challenge for different theoretical approaches. It is especially difficult to explain the broad 0$^+$, 2$^+$ states at 9 MeV excitation energy. Simultaneously, it is important to obtain more reliable experimental data for these levels in order to quantitatively assess the theoretical framework. Purpose To obtain new data on $^{20}$Ne $α$ cluster structure. Method Thick target inverse kinematics technique was used to study the $^{16}$O+$α$ resonance elastic scattering and the data were analyzed using an \textit{R} matrix approach. The $^{20}$Ne spectrum, the cluster and nucleon spectroscopic factors were calculated using cluster-nucleon configuration interaction model (CNCIM). Results We determined the parameters of the broad resonances in \textsuperscript{20}Ne: 0$^+$ level at 8.77 $\pm$ 0.150 MeV with a width of 750 (+500/-220) keV; 2$^+$ level at 8.75 $\pm$ 0.100 MeV with the width of 695 $\pm$ 120 keV; the width of 9.48 MeV level of 65 $\pm$ 20 keV and showed that 9.19 MeV, 2$^+$ level (if exists) should have width $\leq$ 10 keV. The detailed comparison of the theoretical CNCIM predictions with the experimental data on cluster states was made. Conclusions Our experimental results by the TTIK method generally confirm the adopted data on $α$ cluster levels in $^{20}$Ne. The CNCIM gives a good description of the $^{20}$Ne positive parity states up to an excitation energy of $\sim$ 7 MeV, predicting reasonably well the excitation energy of the states and their cluster and single particle properties. At higher excitations, the qualitative disagreement with the experimentally observed structure is evident, especially for broad resonances.

## Key claim

The thick-target inverse-kinematics (TTIK) measurement generally confirms the adopted data on alpha-cluster levels in 20Ne, but the cluster-nucleon configuration interaction model (CNCIM) shows qualitative disagreement with the experimentally observed broad resonances above about 7 MeV excitation energy, especially for the 0+4 and 2+4 states.

## Method

The authors used the thick target inverse kinematics (TTIK) technique with a 30 MeV 16O beam incident on a helium gas target to measure 16O+alpha elastic scattering excitation functions in the 20Ne excitation region of 5.5–9.6 MeV over a broad angular interval. The data were analyzed using a multilevel multichannel R-matrix approach. Separately, the 20Ne spectrum and spectroscopic factors were calculated using the cluster-nucleon configuration interaction model (CNCIM) in several model spaces (sd, p-sd, p-sd-pf) with different Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- 0+ level at 8.77 ± 0.150 MeV excitation energy with a width of 750 (+500/-220) keV
- 2+ level at 8.75 ± 0.100 MeV excitation energy with a width of 695 ± 120 keV
- 2+ level at 9.48 MeV with a width of 65 ± 20 keV
- Upper limit on the width of the 9.19 MeV 2+ level (if it exists) of ≤ 10 keV
- The 0+2 state at 6.78 MeV with a width of 20.6 keV
- The 2+2 state at 7.44 MeV with a width of 14.3 keV
- The 2+3 state at 7.85 MeV with a width of 3.68 keV
- The 4+3 state at 9.03 MeV with a width of 1.9 keV
- The 4+4 state at 9.97 MeV with a width of 157 keV
- The 6+1 state at 8.78 MeV with a width of 0.14 keV
- The 8+ state at 11.95 MeV with a width of 0.035 keV
- Experimental energy resolution was approximately 30 keV at zero degrees, deteriorating to about 100 keV at larger angles
- Uncertainties in absolute cross section were less than 6 percent
- The R-matrix fit achieved chi-squared = 1.1

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of alpha-cluster states in 20Ne using the TTIK method, which allows observation of excitation functions at and near 180 degrees where resonance scattering dominates. The results are compared with the adopted data from Tilley et al. (1998) and with a previous R-matrix analysis by Shen et al. (2005) that gave different parameters for broad resonances. The theoretical comparison uses the CNCIM approach developed by Volya et al., which extends classical shell model techniques toward clustering. The work enables future investigation of the coupling mechanism needed to explain broad alpha-cluster states, possibly through the super-radiance mechanism proposed by Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1989, 1992), and highlights the need for improved treatment of cross-shell excitations in shell model Hamiltonians.
