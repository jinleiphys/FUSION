# Radiative Energy-Loss of Heavy Quarks in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We estimate the radiative energy-loss of heavy quarks, produced from the initial fusion of partons, while propagating in a quark-gluon plasma which may be formed in the wake of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find that the radiative energy-loss for heavy quarks is larger than the collisional energy-loss for all energies. We point out the consequences on possible signals of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The radiative energy-loss of heavy quarks in a quark-gluon plasma is larger than the collisional energy-loss at all energies, but this conclusion depends on the specific approximation used: the authors extend a simple approach for massless quarks (Gyulassy, Wang, and Plumer, 1994) to heavy quarks, noting that a more sophisticated treatment (Baier et al., 1997) yields a different energy dependence but quantitatively similar results for typical parameter values.

## Method

The authors start from the Gunion-Bertsch gluon emission probability, factorized into elastic scattering and gluon emission, and adapt it to heavy quarks under the condition that the emitted gluon energy is much smaller than the center-of-mass energy. They compute the average radiative energy-loss per collision by integrating the gluon multiplicity distribution over phase space, including a formation-time restriction to account for the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect, and then multiply by the interaction rate to obtain the energy-loss per unit length. The maximum transverse momentum of the emitted gluon is evaluated using averaged Mandelstam variables that depend on the heavy quark mass, temperature, and energy, while the average momentum transfer is derived from the elastic scattering cross section approximated as proportional to 1/t².

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks at RHIC energies, the drag coefficient gives \(C \simeq 0.4\) for \(E \leq 5\)–6 GeV, leading to a drag of \(\sim 1.6\)/fm at the initial time \(\tau_i = 0.25\) fm/c.
- For charm quarks at LHC energies, the drag coefficient gives \(C \simeq 0.7\) for \(E \leq 5\)–6 GeV, leading to a drag of \(\sim 2.7\)/fm at \(\tau_i = 0.25\) fm/c.
- The final energy of charm quarks after propagation under the combined drag is about 1.5–1.6 GeV, irrespective of initial energy up to 5–6 GeV.
- The radiative energy-loss dominates over collisional energy-loss by an order of magnitude for heavy quark energies above 20 GeV at \(T = 500\) MeV and \(\alpha_s = 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of radiative energy-loss for massless quarks (Gyulassy, Wang, and Plumer, 1994; Baier et al., 1997) to the case of heavy quarks, for which no estimate existed in the literature. It also builds on collisional energy-loss results for heavy quarks obtained using hard thermal loop resummation (Braaten and Thoma, 1991). The finding that radiative energy-loss provides a strong drag force that stops charm quarks in the plasma supports earlier arguments by Shuryak (1997) and Lin et al. (1997) that correlated charm decay backgrounds to dilepton signals may be suppressed, and enables more realistic modeling of heavy quark propagation in chemically equilibrating plasmas at RHIC and LHC.
