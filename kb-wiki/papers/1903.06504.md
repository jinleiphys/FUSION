# $γ$-rigid triaxial nuclei in the presence of a minimal length via a quantum perturbation method

> In this work, we derive a closed solution of the Shr$ \ddot{o} $dinger equation for Bohr Hamiltonien within the minimal length formalism. This formalism is inspired by Heisenberg algebra and a generlized uncertainty principle (GUP), applied to the geometrical collective Bohr- Mottelson model (BMM) of nuclei by means of deformed canonical commutation relation and the Pauli-Podolsky prescription. The problem is solved by means conjointly of asymptotic iteration method (AIM) and a quantum perturbation method (QPM) for transitional nuclei near the critical point symmetry Z(4) corresponding to phase transition from prolate to $γ$-rigid triaxial shape. A scaled Davidson potentiel is used as a restoring potential in order to get physical minimum. The agreement between the obtained theoretical results and the experimental data is very satisfactory.

## Key claim

The paper derives approximate closed-form energy spectra for γ-rigid triaxial nuclei near the Z(4) critical point symmetry by incorporating a minimal length from a generalized uncertainty principle (GUP) into the Bohr-Mottelson model, using a quantum perturbation method (QPM) to handle the Davidson potential which is otherwise not analytically soluble.

## Method

The authors start from the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian with γ frozen at π/6 (the Z(4) model) and introduce a minimal-length deformation via a modified commutation relation, adding a Δ² term to the Schrödinger equation to first order in the deformation parameter α. They separate variables, solve the angular part exactly using Meyer-ter-Vehn's eigenfunctions, and treat the β-vibrational part with a Davidson potential using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) for the unperturbed (α=0) case. The minimal-length correction to the energy is then computed via first-order quantum perturbation theory (QPM), yielding analytical expressions for the energy shifts in terms of expectation values of β powers.

## Key numbers

- The energy spectrum for the unperturbed Z(4)-D model is given by \(E_{n,L}^0 = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar^2}{2B_m} a} \, [3 + 4n_\beta + 2\vartheta_{l,b}]\), where \(\vartheta_{l,b} = -\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{4L(L+1) - 3\alpha^2 + 8b + 4}\).
- The minimal-length energy correction is \(\Delta E_{n,L} = 4B_m\alpha\left[ (E_{n,L}^0)^2 + 2ab - 2E_{n,L}^0 (a\overline{\beta^2}+b\overline{\beta^{-2}}) + (a^2\overline{\beta^4}+b^2\overline{\beta^{-4}}) \right]\), with explicit formulas for \(\overline{\beta^2}, \overline{\beta^{-2}}, \overline{\beta^4}, \overline{\beta^{-4}}\) in terms of \(n, \vartheta_{l,b}, q\).
- Standard deviations σ between theory and experiment for five nuclei: \(^{124}\text{Xe}\) σ_D=0.588, σ_D-ML=0.44; \(^{126}\text{Xe}\) σ_D=0.36, σ_D-ML=0.36; \(^{128}\text{Xe}\) σ_D=0.44, σ_D-ML=0.40; \(^{130}\text{Xe}\) σ_D=0.35, σ_D-ML=0.35; \(^{114}\text{Pd}\) σ_D=0.84, σ_D-ML=0.63.
- The minimum of the Davidson potential \(\beta_0\) for each nucleus: \(^{124}\text{Xe}\) 0.82, \(^{126}\text{Xe}\) 0.61, \(^{128}\text{Xe}\) 0.69, \(^{130}\text{Xe}\) 0.62, \(^{114}\text{Pd}\) 0.78.

## Context

This work extends the Z(4) critical-point symmetry model (Z(4) by Meyer-ter-Vehn and later authors) by adding a minimal-length deformation from generalized uncertainty principle (GUP) to the collective Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian, using a Davidson potential instead of the infinite square well previously treated in the Z(4)-ML model. The quantum perturbation method (QPM) allows analytical treatment of potentials beyond the square well, enabling comparison with experimental data for 32 nuclei in the Xe region (A≈130) and \(^{114}\text{Pd}\), showing improved agreement for the ground-state and β bands. The results enable future studies of minimal-length effects on electromagnetic transition rates and other collective observables in triaxial nuclei.
