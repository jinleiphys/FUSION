# Testing dark decays of baryons in neutron stars

> We demonstrate that the observation of neutron stars with masses greater than one solar mass places severe demands on any exotic neutron decay mode that could explain the discrepancy between beam and bottle measurements of the neutron lifetime. If the neutron can decay to a stable, feebly-interacting dark fermion, the maximum possible mass of a neutron star is 0.7 solar masses, while all well-measured neutron star masses exceed one solar mass. The survival of $2 M_\odot$ neutron stars therefore indicates that any explanation beyond the Standard Model for the neutron lifetime puzzle requires dark matter to be part of a multi-particle dark sector with highly constrained interactions.

## Key claim

The observation of neutron stars with masses greater than one solar mass places severe demands on any exotic neutron decay mode that could explain the discrepancy between beam and bottle measurements of the neutron lifetime; if the neutron can decay to a stable, feebly-interacting dark fermion, the maximum possible mass of a neutron star is 0.7 solar masses, while all well-measured neutron star masses exceed one solar mass.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron star models assuming chemical equilibrium between normal baryonic matter and a non-interacting Fermi gas of dark matter fermions (chi) produced via baryon-number-violating decays such as n -> chi + gamma. They integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation using modern equations of state for nuclear matter, including the quark-hadron crossover QHC18(0.8,1.5) and the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state, to compute the maximum possible neutron star mass as a function of the dark fermion mass.

## Key numbers

- The maximum neutron star mass for baryons coupled to non-interacting dark matter fermions with mass equal to the neutron mass is reduced to approximately 0.7 solar masses.
- The bottle measurement of the neutron lifetime yields tau_bottle = 879.6 +/- 0.6 s, while the beam measurement yields tau_beam = 888.0 +/- 2.0 s, a discrepancy at the 4-sigma level.
- At total fermion density n_F approximately equal to nuclear matter saturation density n_0 (0.16 fm^-3), about 40% of the fermions are dark matter chi particles when m_chi = m_n.
- At n_F = 10 n_0, approximately 70% of the fermions are chi particles when m_chi = m_n.
- The Bullet Cluster allows dark matter self-interaction cross-sections sigma_chi less than about (66/GeV)^2, approximately 10^-24 cm^2, for a particle with the mass of a neutron.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Fornal and Grinstein (2018) that new neutron decay channels to dark matter could explain the neutron lifetime puzzle, and demonstrates that such decays are incompatible with observed neutron star masses unless the dark matter has strong repulsive self-interactions or is part of a multi-particle dark sector with conserved charges. The results enable future work constructing equations of state for self-interacting dark matter within neutron stars, as begun by Cline (2018), and show that neutron stars provide unique probes of GeV-scale dark sectors coupled to the Standard Model via baryon-number-violating interactions, complementing analyses of dark matter capture by neutron stars via elastic scattering (Goldman 1989, Kouvaris 2007, Bertone 2007, and others).
