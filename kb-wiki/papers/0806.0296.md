# Semi-classical equation of state and specific heats for neutron-star inner crust with proton shell corrections

> An approach to the equation of state for the inner crust of neutron stars based on Skyrme-type forces is presented. Working within the Wigner-Seitz picture, the energy is calculated by the TETF (temperature-dependent extended Thomas-Fermi) method, with proton shell corrections added self-consistently by the Strutinsky-integral method. Using a Skyrme force that has been fitted to both neutron matter and to essentially all the nuclear mass data, we find strong proton shell effects: proton numbers $Z$ = 50, 40 and 20 are the only values possible in the inner crust, assuming that nuclear equilibrium is maintained in the cooling neutron star right down to the ambient temperature. Convergence problems with the TETF expansion for the entropy, and our way of handling them, are discussed. Full TETF expressions for the specific heat of inhomogeneous nuclear matter are presented. Our treatment of the electron gas, including its specific heat, is essentially exact, and is described in detail.

## Key claim

Proton shell effects are so strong in the neutron-star inner crust that only proton numbers Z = 50, 40, and 20 are possible, assuming nuclear equilibrium is maintained during cooling down to the ambient temperature. The authors find that the TETF expansion for the proton entropy diverges at low temperatures, forcing them to use the single-particle expression for proton entropy instead.

## Method

The authors model the inner crust using a single spherical Wigner-Seitz cell and calculate the nuclear free energy via the temperature-dependent extended Thomas-Fermi (TETF) method at fourth order, with proton shell corrections added self-consistently through the Strutinsky-integral method (TETFSI). They use the Skyrme force BSk14, which has an effective mass of 0.800M at equilibrium density and was fitted to both neutron matter and essentially all nuclear mass data. The free energy is minimized with respect to six geometrical parameters of the nucleon density distribution, and for equilibrium calculations also with respect to N and Z.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass in symmetric infinite nuclear matter at equilibrium density (0.159 nucleons.fm⁻³) is 0.800M, compared to 0.825M from extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations
- The Skyrme force BSk14 gives an rms deviation of 0.729 MeV from essentially all available nuclear mass data
- The inner crust density range considered is from 2.4 × 10⁻⁴ nucleons.fm⁻³ (neutron drip) up to 0.06 nucleons.fm⁻³
- The transition from droplet phase to homogeneous phase occurs around 0.075 nucleons.fm⁻³ for some interactions
- At mean density 0.06 nucleons.fm⁻³, a test cell with Z=30 and A=1123 is used; at 3 × 10⁻⁴ nucleons.fm⁻³, a test cell with Z=40 and A=150 is used
- Temperatures tested for convergence: 0.1 MeV, 1.0 MeV, and 3.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier TETF approach of Onsi et al. (1997) for core-collapse supernova conditions to the much lower temperatures of neutron-star inner crusts, where proton shell effects become significant and the TETF entropy expansion converges poorly. It improves upon the exploratory study of Dutta et al. (2004) by including shell effects in the entropy and using the more realistic Skyrme force BSk14 (from the HFB-14 mass model of Goriely et al., 2007) rather than forces with effective mass equal to the real mass. The approach contrasts with the compressible liquid-drop model of Lattimer and Swesty (1991), which lacks shell corrections, and with Hartree-Fock calculations such as those of Negele and Vautherin (1973) and Bonche and Vautherin (1981), which introduce spurious neutron shell effects due to discretization in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The results enable calculation of the equation of state and specific heats needed to model decompression of neutron-star crustal material in scenarios such as neutron-star mergers.
