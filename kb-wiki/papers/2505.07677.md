# Nonparametric extensions of nuclear equations of state: probing the breakdown scale of relativistic mean-field theory

> Phenomenological calculations of the properties of dense matter, such as relativistic mean-field theories, represent a pathway to predicting the microscopic and macroscopic properties of neutron stars. However, such theories do not generically have well-controlled uncertainties and may break down within neutron stars. To faithfully represent the uncertainty in this breakdown scale, we develop a hybrid representation of the dense-matter equation of state, which assumes the form of a relativistic mean-field theory at low densities, while remaining agnostic to any nuclear theory at high densities. To achieve this, we use a nonparametric equation of state model to incorporate the correlations of the underlying relativistic mean-field theory equation of state at low pressures and transition to more flexible correlations above some chosen pressure scale. We perform astrophysical inference under various choices of the transition pressure between the theory-informed and theory-agnostic models. We further study whether the chosen relativistic mean-field theory breaks down above some particular pressure and find no such evidence. Using simulated data for future astrophysical observations at about two-to-three times the precision of current constraints, we show that our method can identify the breakdown pressure associated with a potential strong phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper develops a hybrid representation of the dense-matter equation of state that assumes the form of a relativistic mean-field theory at low densities but transitions to a theory-agnostic model at high densities, and finds no evidence that the chosen relativistic mean-field theory breaks down above any particular pressure with current astrophysical data, though the method can identify a breakdown pressure associated with a strong phase transition using simulated data at about two-to-three times the precision of current constraints.

## Method

The authors construct Gaussian Process (GP) emulators from a set of 1109 relativistic mean-field theory (RMFT) equations of state, all derived from the same Lagrangian density with seven coupling constants fit to chiral effective field theory uncertainty bands up to 1.5 times nuclear saturation density. They build hybrid priors that follow the RMFT emulator up to six different transition pressures (ranging from 10^11 to 10^14 g/cm^3) and then transition to model-agnostic GP priors at higher pressures. They perform hierarchical Bayesian inference using real astrophysical data from gravitational-wave observations, X-ray pulse profile measurements, and pulsar timing, as well as simulated data with enhanced precision.

## Context

This work extends the methodology introduced by Essick (2020, 2021) for incorporating theory-informed priors into otherwise model-agnostic equation of state inference, previously applied to chiral effective field theory, to the case of relativistic mean-field theories which lack well-controlled theoretical uncertainties. The approach enables probing whether a specific RMFT framework breaks down at high densities within neutron stars, and can identify breakdown scales associated with strong first-order phase transitions using future observations at approximately three times current precision. The method is generic and can be applied to any nuclear framework expected to become less reliable with increasing density.
