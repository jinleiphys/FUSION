# Dyson-Schwinger equations and the muon g-2

> We present a brief introduction to the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) approach to hadron and high-energy physics. In particular, how this formalism is applied to calculate the electromagnetic form factors $γ^* γ^* \to \textbf{P}^0$ and $γ^* \textbf{P}^\pm \to \textbf{P}^\pm$ (with $\textbf{P}^\pm$ and $\textbf{P}^0$ charged and neutral ground-state pseudoscalar mesons, respectively) is discussed. Subsequently, the corresponding contributions of those form factors to the muon anomalous magnetic moment ($g-2$) are estimated. We look forward to promoting the DSE approach to address theoretical aspects of the muon $g-2$, highlighting some calculations that could be carried out in the future.

# Dyson-Schwinger equations and the muon g-2

## Key claim
The Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) formalism can provide reliable estimates of hadronic light-by-light contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, specifically the charged-pseudoscalar box and neutral-pseudoscalar pole contributions, with uncertainties that are competitive with other approaches; however, the results depend on truncation choices (rainbow-ladder or beyond) and the quark-photon vertex ansatz, and the formalism has not yet been widely applied to this problem.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar meson bound states within the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation using the Qin-Chang effective interaction, and for the eta-eta' system they include an additional anomaly kernel. The electromagnetic elastic form factors (gamma* P^pm -> P^pm) and transition form factors (gamma* gamma* -> P^0) are computed in the impulse approximation, using both direct numerical integration and a perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR) that allows evaluation at arbitrarily large spacelike momenta; the quark-photon vertex is obtained from an inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation or from a gauge-covariant ansatz.

## Key numbers
- Experimental world average: a_mu^Exp = 116592061(41) x 10^{-11}
- Standard Model prediction: a_mu^SM = 116591810(43) x 10^{-11}
- Discrepancy: 4.2 sigma
- Charged pion box contribution: a_mu^{pi^pm-box} = -(15.6 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-11} (weighted average of RL-direct, RL-PTIR, and BRL results)
- Charged kaon box contribution: a_mu^{K^pm-box} = -(0.48 +/- 0.02) x 10^{-11} (weighted average)
- Neutral pion pole contribution: a_mu^{pi^0-pole} = (61.4 +/- 0.21) x 10^{-11}
- Eta pole contribution: a_mu^{eta-pole} = (14.7 +/- 0.19) x 10^{-11}
- Eta' pole contribution: a_mu^{eta'-pole} = (13.6 +/- 0.08) x 10^{-11}
- Eta_c pole contribution: a_mu^{eta_c-pole} = (0.9 +/- 0.1) x 10^{-11}
- Eta_b pole contribution: a_mu^{eta_b-pole} = (0.26 +/- 0.01) x 10^{-13}
- Total pseudoscalar pole sum: a_mu^{P-pole} = (90.6 +/- 4.9) x 10^{-11}
- Pion charge radius: r_pi = 0.676(5) fm
- Kaon charge radius: r_K = 0.595(5) fm

## Context
This work extends the DSE-based calculations of hadronic contributions to the muon g-2 reported by Eichmann (2019) and by the same authors in earlier papers (Raya 2019, Miramontes 2021), using both RL and beyond-RL truncations. The results are compared with data-driven approaches such as those of Colangelo (2017) and Masjuan (2017), and with lattice QCD calculations. The formalism enables future calculations of axial-vector meson contributions and excited pseudoscalar contributions, which are identified as open problems.
