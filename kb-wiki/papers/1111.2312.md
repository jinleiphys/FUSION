# Charged particle multiplicities in pA interactions at the LHC from the Color Glass Condensate

> The forthcoming LHC measurement of hadron multiplicity in proton-nucleus collisions is a crucial test of the k_t factorization and gluon saturation based models. Here, we provide quantitative predictions for the pseudorapidity distribution of charged particles produced in minimum bias proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC based on the idea of gluon saturation in the color-glass condensate framework. Our formulation gives good descriptions of the LHC and RHIC data for the charged-hadron multiplicities in both proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and also the deep inelastic scattering at HERA at small Bjorken-x.

## Key claim
The paper provides quantitative predictions for charged hadron multiplicity in p+Pb collisions at the LHC based on the Color Glass Condensate framework, but notes that the predictions carry uncertainties of about 15% from normalization to RHIC data and about 10% from the choice of mini-jet mass parameter, and that the results depend on which saturation model is used.

## Method
The author uses k_t factorization within the Color Glass Condensate framework to compute gluon jet production in proton-nucleus collisions, then applies the Local Parton-Hadron Duality principle for hadronization. Two saturation models are employed: the b-CGC model (which includes impact-parameter dependence) and the MCrcBK model from Jalilian-Marian and collaborators. The MLLA gluon-decay cascade effect is also incorporated in one variant (CGC-R2). The overall normalization and mini-jet mass parameter are fixed using lower-energy data from RHIC.

## Key numbers
- At midrapidity (eta=0) in p+Pb minimum-bias collisions at sqrt(s)=4.4 TeV, the leading-log k_t factorization prediction (CGC-R1) gives dN/deta = 19.67 ± 1.5
- The MCrcBK model prediction at the same energy and midrapidity gives dN/deta = 16.71 ± 1.3
- Including the MLLA gluon-decay cascade effect (CGC-R2) gives dN/deta = 21.88 ± 1.7 at midrapidity
- The MLLA gluon-decay cascade enhances the multiplicity by about 20% in p+A collisions at the LHC
- The total theoretical uncertainty in the p+p predictions is less than 6%, mainly from fixing the overall normalization and mini-jet mass with lower-energy data

## Context
This work extends the author's previous successful descriptions of charged hadron multiplicities in both p+p and A+A collisions at the LHC and RHIC (Rezaeian 2011) and also the deep inelastic scattering data at HERA. The predictions are intended to be tested by the forthcoming LHC p+Pb run, which the author argues can discriminate between different saturation models such as the b-CGC and the MCrcBK approach of Jalilian-Marian (2010). The paper also addresses the different energy-growth behavior of multiplicities in A+A versus p+p collisions observed by CMS and ALICE, attributing it to MLLA gluon-decay cascade effects.
