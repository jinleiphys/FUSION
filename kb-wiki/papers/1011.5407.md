# Porter-Thomas distribution in unstable many-body systems

> We use the continuum shell model approach to explore the resonance width distribution in unstable many-body systems. The single-particle nature of a decay, the few-body character of the interaction Hamiltonian, and collectivity that emerges in non-stationary systems due to the coupling to the continuum of reaction states are discussed. Correlations between structures of the parent and daughter nuclear systems in the common Fock space are found to result in deviations of decay width statistics from the Porter-Thomas distribution.

# Porter-Thomas distribution in unstable many-body systems

## Key claim

The Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD) is violated in unstable many-body systems when the microscopic physics of decay is taken into account, specifically due to correlations between parent and daughter nuclear structures in the common Fock space; this violation persists even when superradiance effects, rotational symmetry, and the rank of the interaction are considered, contrary to the expectation that the central limit theorem makes the PTD robust against such modifications.

## Method

The continuum shell model approach was used, employing an effective energy-dependent Hamiltonian with a Hermitian part (the traditional shell model Hamiltonian) and an imaginary term representing irreversible decays into the continuum of reaction states via Feshbach projection. Numerical simulations were performed for three types of ensembles: (1) GOE+SR, where the Hermitian part is a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble with superradiance; (2) EGOE+SR, where the Hermitian part is an embedded two-body GOE; and (3) n-body random ensembles (n-BRE) with rotational symmetry for identical fermions in a single-j level (j=19/2), examining decays from 9-particle to 8-particle systems. The distribution of reduced decay widths and amplitudes was compared to the PTD and to a Bessel-function distribution.

## Key numbers

- For the GOE+SR ensemble with Ω=10⁴, average widths Ωγ̅ = 0.76, 0.40, 0.20, and 0.05 for continuum couplings κ=0.5, 1, 2, and 5 respectively (after removing very broad states); for κ>2, Ωγ̅ = κ⁻².
- For the EGOE+SR ensemble with 16 single-particle states (Ω=11440) and decay of a 7-particle system to the ground state of a 6-particle system, Ωγ̅ = 1, 0.19, and 0.06 for κ=0, 1, and 2 respectively.
- For the single-j=19/2 model with 9-particle parent decaying to 8-particle daughter, the fractions of realizations where the daughter ground state has spin F=0 are 42%, 64%, and 83% for two-, three-, and four-body random ensembles respectively.
- The probabilities that the ground state of the odd-particle parent carries single-particle quantum numbers j=I=19/2 are 21%, 47%, and 37% for n=2, 3, and 4 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the validity of the Porter-Thomas distribution, which originates from Porter and Thomas (1956) and is a central prediction of Random Matrix Theory. Prior studies by Brody (1981), Zelevinsky (2004), Papenbrock (2007), and Mitchell (2010) had generally concluded that deviations from PTD are defeated by stochastic components due to the central limit theorem. The paper directly addresses claims of PTD violation by Celardo (2010) and Whitehead (1978), and the experimental observations by PhysRevLett.105.072502, which have been controversial. The continuum shell model approach of Volya (2009) is used to show that parent-daughter correlations in Fock space produce genuine violations that are not explained by data handling issues, which had been suggested as the cause by RevModPhys.81.539. The results enable future work on the energy dependence of the effective Hamiltonian, which is noted as a remaining factor to be examined elsewhere.
