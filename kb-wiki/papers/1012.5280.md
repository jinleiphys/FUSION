# Explanation of Di-jet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> We investigate the medium modification of a partonic jet shower traversing in a hot quark-gluon plasma. We derive and solve a differential equation that governs the evolution of the radiated gluon spectrum as the jet propagates through the medium. Energy contained inside the jet cone is lost by dissipation through elastic collisions with the medium and by scattering of shower partons to larger angles. We find that the jet energy loss at early times is dominated by medium effects on the vacuum radiation, and by medium-induced radiation effects at late times. We compare our numerical results for the nuclear modification of the di-jet asymmetry with that recently reported by the ATLAS collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantitative description of the large di-jet asymmetry observed by ATLAS in Pb+Pb collisions, showing that the effect arises from a combination of collisional energy loss, medium-induced radiation, and scattering of shower partons out of the jet cone, with the transport coefficients consistent with those extracted from RHIC data when extrapolated to LHC densities.

## Method

The authors derive and solve a differential equation for the evolution of the radiated gluon distribution \(f_g(\omega, k_\perp^2, t)\) as a jet shower propagates through a quark-gluon plasma, incorporating collisional energy loss via the transport coefficient \(\hat{e}\), transverse momentum broadening via \(\hat{q}\), and a source term for medium-induced gluon radiation. Vacuum showering is generated from PYTHIA, and the medium evolution is modeled with a one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion starting at \(t_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) with initial temperature \(T_0 = 520\) MeV for central collisions. The energy loss from a jet cone of radius \(R = 0.4\) is computed as the sum of losses from the leading parton and radiated gluons, and the resulting di-jet asymmetry distribution is compared to ATLAS data.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficients are related by \(\hat{q} = 4T \hat{e}\), assuming a medium close to local thermal equilibrium.
- A lower cut-off \(\omega_{\rm min} = 2\) GeV is imposed on radiated gluon energy, about four times the highest medium temperature at the LHC.
- For a static medium at \(T = 250\) MeV with \(\hat{q} = 0.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, a 100 GeV quark jet is studied with cone angle \(R = 0.4\).
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, a 100 GeV quark jet produced at the center loses \(\Delta E \approx 22\) GeV over a medium interaction time of \(t_f - t_0 \approx 5.5\) fm/\(c\).
- The average path-length weighted transport coefficient in central collisions is \(\langle \hat{q} \rangle = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.
- This corresponds to \(\hat{q} = 2.6\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the highest temperature 400 MeV in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- The initial temperature at the hottest point in central Pb+Pb collisions is set to \(T_0 = 520\) MeV, a factor of 1.3 larger than the highest temperature at RHIC.
- The shower evolution terminates when the medium temperature drops to \(T_c = 160\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism of jet quenching developed by Wang (2001) and Majumder (2009) to include the full evolution of the gluon distribution within a jet shower, building on earlier qualitative analysis by Casalderrey-Solana (2010). The results provide the first quantitative description of the ATLAS di-jet asymmetry data from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and the extracted transport coefficients are consistent with those from systematic analyses at RHIC by Bass (2008). The approach enables future studies of jet substructure and energy loss mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
