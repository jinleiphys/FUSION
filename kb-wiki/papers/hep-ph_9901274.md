# Nonlocality and gravity-induced CP violation

> We consider a space-time dependent relative phase between the right- and left-handed spinors and show that it results in a violation of locality in the presence of gravity once the demand of parity covariance is dropped. This violation of locality is such that it readily interprets itself as a gravity-induced CP violation, and at the same time confirms an earlier remark by Wigner that a representation space carries more information than a wave equation. This happens, as Kirchbach has noted, because while the dimensionality of an irreducible representation space does not depend upon the concrete realization of the symmetry generators, Noether currents (Dirac, versus Majorana, versus the CP violating construct presented here) do. The gravity-induced CP violation provides a dynamical reason on how a neutron star carrying its baryon and lepton numbers can collapse into a black hole and loose information on the latter characteristics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a space-time dependent relative phase between right- and left-handed spinors, allowed when parity covariance is not demanded, produces a violation of locality in the presence of gravity that can be interpreted as gravity-induced CP violation; this effect is conditional on dropping the requirement of parity covariance and is presented within a CPT-covariant framework that does not rely on the Morrison-Gold conjecture of opposite gravitational masses for particles and antiparticles.

## Method

The author introduces a space-time dependent phase factor \(\phi(x)\) relating the right- and left-handed spinors at rest, then derives the resulting modified Dirac equation \(\left(\gamma^\mu p_\mu - \xi(x) m\right)\psi(p^\mu)=0\) where \(\xi(x)\) is a \(4\times4\) matrix containing the phase. The field operator is constructed and the equal-time anticommutator \(\left\{\Psi_i(\vec{x},t),\Psi^\dagger_j(\vec{x}',t)\right\}\) is computed to reveal the non-locality. Gravitation is treated in the weak field limit following Sakurai and in the spirit of gravitationally induced quantum interference experiments.

## Key numbers

* The non-locality term in the anticommutator contains a factor \(\sin(\phi(x))\) and vanishes when the phase \(\phi(x)\) is an exact integral multiple of \(\pi\), and/or when the particle is massless or \(m/k_0\) approaches zero.
* The determinant \(\mbox{Det}\,|\gamma^\mu p_\mu - \xi m| = (\vec{p}^2 + m^2 - E^2)^2\) is independent of the phase \(\phi(x)\), yielding the classical energy relation \(E = \pm\sqrt{\vec{p}^2 + m^2}\).
* The spinor normalization constant is \(A = \left[2(m+E)\cos(\phi(x))\right]^{-1/2}\), with the standard norms \(\overline{u}_\sigma u_\sigma = +2m\delta_{\sigma\sigma'}\) and \(\overline{v}_\sigma v_\sigma = -2m\delta_{\sigma\sigma'}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier considerations of gravity-induced CP violation by Good (who built on the Morrison-Gold conjecture of opposite gravitational masses for particles and antiparticles) and by Chardin (who argued for a parameter-free explanation of CP violation in neutral Kaons via gravitational repulsion in wormholes and Kerr geometry). The author claims the present framework drops the Morrison-Gold conjecture while maintaining CPT covariance, offering greater generality than the Chardin-Fischbach scheme. The result enables a dynamical mechanism for how a neutron star carrying baryon and lepton numbers can collapse into a black hole and lose information on those characteristics, and it confirms Wigner's remark that a representation space carries more information than a wave equation.
