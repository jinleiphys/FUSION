# Dark matter scattering off ${}^2$H and ${}^4$He nuclei within chiral effective field theory

> We study dark matter, assumed to be composed by weak interacting massive particles (WIMPs), scattering off ${}^2$H and ${}^4$He nuclei. In order to parameterize the WIMP-nucleon interaction the chiral effective field theory approach is used. Considering only interactions invariant under parity, charge conjugation and time reversal, we examine five interaction types: scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial and tensor. Scattering amplitudes between two nucleons and a WIMP are determined up to second order of chiral perturbation theory. We apply this program to calculate the interaction rate as function of the WIMP mass and of the magnitude of the WIMP-quark coupling constants. From our study, we conclude that the scalar nuclear response functions result much greater than the others due to theirs large combination of low energy constants. We verify that the leading order contributions are dominant in this low energy processes. We also provide an estimate for the background due to atmospheric neutrinos.

## Key claim
The scalar nuclear response functions for dark matter scattering off deuterium and helium-4 are much larger than those for other interaction types (pseudoscalar, vector, axial, tensor) due to large combinations of low-energy constants, and leading-order contributions dominate these low-energy processes; the study also provides an estimate for the atmospheric neutrino background.

## Method
The authors used chiral effective field theory (χEFT) to parameterize WIMP-nucleon interactions for five interaction types (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial, tensor) invariant under parity, charge conjugation, and time reversal. They derived scattering amplitudes for WIMP scattering off two-nucleon systems up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in chiral perturbation theory, then applied these amplitudes to calculate nuclear response functions and interaction rates for deuterium (²H) and helium-4 (⁴He) targets using nuclear wave functions from the Argonne V18 and N4LO500 potentials.

## Key numbers
- The maximal momentum transfer for WIMP-nucleus scattering is q_max ≤ 200 MeV, based on recoil energies of a few keV to tens of keV and nucleus masses up to m_A ≈ 200 GeV.
- The typical WIMP velocity in the galactic halo is |v_χ/c| ≈ 10⁻³, with velocities below about 600 km/s for gravitational binding.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π ≈ 92.4 MeV, and the chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ≈ 4π f_π ≈ 1 GeV.
- For deuteron scattering at q = 0.05 fm⁻¹, the scalar charge reduced matrix element (RME) at LO for l=0 is -0.144×10² (both AV18 and N4LO500 potentials), while the NLO contribution is -0.218×10⁰ (AV18) and -0.806×10⁻¹ (N4LO500).
- The pseudoscalar charge RME for deuteron at N2LO for l=1 is -0.367×10⁻¹ (AV18) and -0.377×10⁻¹ (N4LO500).

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to dark matter scattering previously applied to heavier nuclei like argon and xenon (Aprile 2023) and to light nuclei by Korber (2017), Andreoli (2019), and Vries (2023). It provides a systematic treatment of one-body and two-body currents for five interaction types in light nuclei, enabling more accurate predictions for direct detection experiments targeting deuterium and helium-4, which are sensitive to relatively light dark matter particles with mass ≤ 10 GeV. The results can be compared with alternative approaches such as Pionless effective field theory (Richardson 2022) and Galilean effective field theory (Fitzpatrick 2013, Anand 2014), where constraints on WIMP-nuclei couplings have been reported (Agnes 2020, Akerib 2021, Jeong 2022, Heimsoth 2023).
