# Three-body renormalization group limit cycles based on unsupervised feature learning

> Both the three-body system and the inverse square potential carry a special significance in the study of renormalization group limit cycles. In this work, we pursue an exploratory approach and address the question which two-body interactions lead to limit cycles in the three-body system at low energies, without imposing any restrictions upon the scattering length. For this, we train a boosted ensemble of variational autoencoders, that not only provide a severe dimensionality reduction, but also allow to generate further synthetic potentials, which is an important prerequisite in order to efficiently search for limit cycles in low-dimensional latent space. We do so by applying an elitist genetic algorithm to a population of synthetic potentials that minimizes a specially defined limit-cycle-loss. The resulting fittest individuals suggest that the inverse square potential is the only two-body potential that minimizes this limit cycle loss independent of the hyperangle.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, among all possible two-body potentials, the inverse square potential is the only one that minimizes a specially defined limit-cycle-loss (LCLoss) independent of the hyperangle, suggesting it is the unique two-body interaction leading to renormalization group limit cycles in the three-body system at low energies without restrictions on the scattering length.

## Method

The authors discretize finitely-ranged two-body potentials and solve the low-energy Faddeev equation using a generalized transfer matrix method in hyperspherical coordinates, applying delta-shell regularization to obtain coupling constants as functions of the short-range cutoff. They train a boosted ensemble of convolutional variational autoencoders (VAEs) to encode high-dimensional potential vectors into low-dimensional latent space and generate synthetic potentials, then apply an elitist genetic algorithm to populations of these synthetic potentials to minimize the LCLoss, which measures how closely the renormalization group flow of a coupling constant approaches a log-periodic limit cycle.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov scaling factor is given as λ = exp(π/s₀) = 22.69438, with s₀ = 1.00624 for three identical bosons.
- The spin-singlet np scattering length is a_{¹S₀} = -23.8 fm and the spin-triplet scattering length is a_{³S₁} = 5.4 fm, compared to the spin-triplet effective range r₀ = 1.8 fm and pion Compton wavelength λ_π = 1.4 fm.
- The three-body bound state energy ratio for the Efimov effect is E₃^{(n)}/E₃^{(n+1)} = exp(2π/s₀), with s₀ ≈ 1.0064 from an effective field theory approach.

## Context

This work extends the study of renormalization group limit cycles from the known Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) and the inverse square potential (Hammer 2006) to a general search over two-body potentials using machine learning. It builds on the observation that the Efimov effect produces an effective long-range 1/R² behavior in the hyperradius, and on prior work showing that three identical bosons with pairwise inverse square potentials exhibit an infinite geometric series of three-body bound states (PhysRevLett.108.213202). The approach enables a systematic, data-driven method to identify which two-body interactions produce limit cycles without assuming resonant scattering lengths.
