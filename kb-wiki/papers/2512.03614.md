# Real-time evolution of critical modes in the QCD phase diagram

> A QCD-assisted relaxation dynamic model for the critical mode of the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram is developed, which allows us to investigate the critical slowing down effect quantitatively in the QCD phase diagram, especially in the proximity of the CEP, without any phenomenological parameters. The relaxation time from nonequilibrium to equilibrium in the QCD phase diagram is extracted from the Langevin simulations of the QCD-assisted relaxation dynamic model. It is found that in a narrow region along the phase boundary radiated from the CEP, the relaxation time is enhanced significantly. Outside this narrow region, the relaxation time drops drastically, which implies that the dynamic critical region is small in the QCD phase diagram. We also find that the effects of critical slowing down are mild on the chemical freeze-out curves.

## Key claim

The authors develop a QCD-assisted relaxation dynamic model for the critical mode of the critical end point (CEP) that, for the first time, allows quantitative investigation of the critical slowing down effect in the QCD phase diagram without any phenomenological parameters; however, the relaxation time is normalized by a reference value and the simulations are constrained to baryon chemical potentials up to 630 MeV, not reaching the CEP itself at (107, 635) MeV.

## Method

The authors combine functional renormalization group (fRG) calculations of QCD at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential with a Langevin transport model (Model A dynamics) for the sigma mode. The static effective potential, spatial wave function, and temporal wave function (dissipation coefficient) are all computed from Euclidean and analytically continued Minkowski correlation functions within the fRG approach to QCD. The Langevin equation is then simulated on a 3+1 dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions, lattice spacing 0.1 fm, and 32^3 lattice sites, using adaptive time stepping and Gaussian white noise, from which the relaxation time is extracted by fitting the ensemble-averaged sigma field to an exponential decay.

## Key numbers

* Location of the CEP: (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (107, 635) MeV, from prior fRG work (Fu 2019).
* Maximal relaxation time tau_0 occurs at (T, mu_B) ~ (108, 630) MeV, used to normalize the reduced relaxation time tau/tau_0.
* Lattice parameters: lattice spacing a = 0.1 fm, lattice size N_L = 32, ensemble size N_ens = 256.
* Spatial momentum for temporal wave function: p_L = 10 MeV, corresponding to length scale ~20 fm comparable to fireball size in heavy-ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram using functional QCD (Fu 2019, Gao 2020, Gunkel 2021) and lattice QCD extrapolations via Yang-Lee edge singularities (Clarke 2024, Borsanyi 2025) by adding real-time dynamics. It combines the fRG approach to QCD with mesoscopic dynamic models classified by Hohenberg and Halperin (1977), specifically Model A for purely dissipative relaxation, and builds on earlier fRG studies of dynamic models (Canet 2006, Canet 2011, Roth 2023, Chen 2023, Tan 2024). The results enable quantitative assessment of whether critical slowing down affects observables in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that effects are mild on chemical freeze-out curves and that hydrodynamic simulations with local equilibrium critical fluctuations (Fu 2021, Fu 2023) remain applicable.
