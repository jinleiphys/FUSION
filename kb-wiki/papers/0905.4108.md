# Constraining the nuclear pairing gap with pairing vibrations

> Pairing interactions with various density dependencies (surface/volume mixing) are constrained with the two-neutron separation energy in the Tin isotopic chain. The response associated with pairing vibrations in very neutron-rich nuclei is sensitive to the density dependence of the pairing interaction. Using the same pairing interaction in nuclear matter and in Tin nuclei, the range of densities where the LDA is valid in the pairing channel is also studied.

## Key claim

The two-neutron separation energy in Tin isotopes constrains the pairing gap only at a specific density (0.11 fm$^{-3}$) near the nuclear surface, and pairing vibrations in very neutron-rich nuclei like $^{136}$Sn provide a complementary observable sensitive to the low-density behavior of the pairing interaction, which can distinguish between surface and mixed surface/volume pairing interactions.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme interaction SLy4 for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction with varying surface/volume mixing parameter $\eta$ (0.35, 0.65, and 1.0). For each $\eta$, the strength $V_0$ was adjusted to reproduce experimental two-neutron separation energies in Tin isotopes. They then solved the QRPA equations in coordinate space using a Green's function formalism to compute the strength function for two-neutron addition modes (pairing vibrations) in $^{124}$Sn and $^{136}$Sn, and compared the pairing gap in uniform nuclear matter with the HFB pairing field in finite nuclei to test the validity of the local density approximation (LDA) in the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

- The pairing interactions converge to the same pairing gap in uniform matter at a density of $\rho = 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$, which corresponds to a radius of about 5 fm in Tin nuclei.
- For the mixed pairing interaction ($\eta=0.35$), the LDA in the pairing channel is valid for densities lower than 0.1 fm$^{-3}$, corresponding to radii larger than about 5.1 fm in $^{124}$Sn and 5.2 fm in $^{136}$Sn.
- In $^{124}$Sn with a pure surface pairing ($\eta=1$), the QRPA response shows a peak around 9 MeV, compared to around 10 MeV for mixed interactions.
- In $^{136}$Sn, the unperturbed response for the pure surface interaction starts at about 3.2 MeV, while for $\eta=0.35$ it starts at about 3.8 MeV.
- The numerical cutoff for microscopic calculations was set at $E_{max}=60$ MeV in quasiparticle energies and $j_{max}=15/2$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on pairing in nuclear matter and finite nuclei by Margueron et al. (2007, 2008) and Pillet et al. (2007), who showed that paired neutrons are spatially localized at low density near the nuclear surface. It builds on the first fully microscopic calculations of pairing vibrations by Khan et al. (2004) and aims to use pairing vibrations as a complementary observable to odd-even mass staggering (Dobaczewski et al., Gorgen et al.) for constraining the pairing interaction. The results enable future work using isospin-dependent pairing interactions and experimental measurements of two-neutron transfer angular distributions to further constrain the pairing functional.
