# A study of neutron-deuteron scattering in configuration space

## Key claim
The new computational method for solving configuration-space Faddeev equations for breakup scattering is fully feasible and reliable, producing results of comparable precision to other methods, but the authors note that convergence with respect to maximal values of total angular momentum M, orbital momenta l and lambda is still under study, and agreement with experimental data is good except for angles near 180 degrees.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space by direct integration of the set of partial differential equations, using the MGL basis approach. They employed the charge-independent AV14 potential and included all angular momenta of subsystems with l and lambda up to 4, total angular momentum of a pair of nucleons J up to 3, and total three-body angular momentum M0 up to 9/2, with a cutoff radius of 80 fm.

## Key numbers
- Elastic nd amplitudes at Elab = 1 MeV for M0^pi = 1/2^+: a^{1/2+}_{1/20,1/20} = -0.2903 + i 0.093 (present work), compared to -0.291 + i 0.093 from the Bochum and Pisa group (PhysR)
- Elastic nd amplitudes at Elab = 3 MeV for M0^pi = 1/2^+: a^{1/2+}_{1/20,1/20} = -0.4679 + i 0.324 (present work), compared to -0.4691 + i 0.3272 from PhysR
- Optical theorem check at Elab = 14.1 MeV for M0^pi = 1/2^+, s0=1/2, lambda0=0: Im a = 0.7172, R.H.S. = 0.7182
- Total cross-section for M0 = 1/2 at Elab = 14.1 MeV: sigma_tot = 183.25 mb, sigma_el = 132.47 mb, sigma_in = 50.78 mb
- Total cross-section for M0 = 7/2 at Elab = 14.1 MeV: sigma_tot = 804.25 mb, sigma_el = 648.20 mb, sigma_in = 156.05 mb
- Experimental total cross-section from reference (b): 824 +/- 10 mb

## Context
This work extends the configuration-space Faddeev approach of Suslov to neutron-deuteron scattering both below and above the two-body threshold, using the computational method proposed in MGL. The results are compared with accurate calculations from the Bochum and Pisa groups (PhysR) and with experimental data from references (dif1), (b), (FBS2), and (PRL). The method is intended to be generalized to include Coulomb interaction in proton-deuteron scattering, where other methods face considerable difficulties, especially for the breakup channel.
