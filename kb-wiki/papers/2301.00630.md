# Tribaryons with lattice QCD and one-boson exchange potentials

> Motivated by the existence of two-body hadronic molecules composed of $ΩΩ$, $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ predicted by lattice QCD simulations, we use the Gaussian expansion method to investigate whether three-body systems composed of $ΩΩΩ$, $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ can bind with the two-body $^1S_0$ interactions provided by lattice QCD. Our results show that none of the three-body systems bind. On the other hand, we find that with the one-boson exchange potentials the $ΩΩΩ$ system develops a bound state, for which the $^5S_2$ interaction plays an important role. Our studies support the existence of the $\frac{3}{2}^+$ $ΩΩΩ$ bound state and the nonexistence of the $\frac{3}{2}^+$ $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ bound states, due to the suppressed $^5S_2$ interactions in heavier systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a $\frac{3}{2}^+$ $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ bound state exists when using one-boson exchange potentials that include both the $^1S_0$ and $^5S_2$ partial waves, but that no such bound state forms when using only the $^1S_0$ lattice QCD potentials; the $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ and $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$ systems do not bind in either approach, primarily because the $^5S_2$ interaction is suppressed in heavier systems.

## Method

The authors used the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for systems of three identical baryons ($\Omega\Omega\Omega$, $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$, $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$) in Jacobi coordinates. Two-body interactions were taken from lattice QCD simulations (providing only $^1S_0$ potentials) and from an extended one-boson exchange (OBE) model (providing both $^1S_0$ and $^5S_2$ potentials), with the OBE couplings fitted to reproduce the lattice QCD binding energies of the corresponding dibaryon systems. The three-body wave functions were constructed respecting Fermi-Dirac statistics, and binding energies and root-mean-square radii were computed for each system.

## Key numbers

- $\Omega\Omega$ dibaryon binding energy: $1.41^{+0.89}_{-0.41}$ MeV (both lattice QCD and OBE potentials), with rms radius $3.45^{+0.52}_{-0.62}$ fm (lattice QCD) or $3.33^{+0.51}_{-0.62}$ fm (OBE)
- $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ tribaryon binding energy with OBE potentials (including $^1S_0$ and $^5S_2$): $5.84^{+2.48}_{-1.22}$ MeV, with rms radius $1.86^{+0.13}_{-0.19}$ fm
- $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ tribaryon binding energy including Coulomb interaction: $2.0$ MeV, with rms radius $2.3$ fm
- $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ dibaryon binding energy (no Coulomb): $5.54$ MeV (lattice QCD) or $5.52$ MeV (OBE); dissolves when Coulomb interaction is included
- $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$ dibaryon binding energy (no Coulomb): $88.7$ MeV (lattice QCD) or $88.6$ MeV (OBE); with Coulomb: $79.9$ MeV (lattice QCD) or $78.4$ MeV (OBE)
- Weights of partial waves in the $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ bound state: $^1S_0$ contributes 22%, $^5S_2$ contributes 78%
- Estimated $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ production rate in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and 2.76 TeV: of order $10^{-11}$

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD predictions of dibaryon bound states for $\Omega\Omega$ (Gongyo 2017), $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ (Lyu 2021), and $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$ (Mathur 2022) to the corresponding three-body tribaryon systems, and is the first study of three-body systems composed of fully identical flavored baryons. The results contradict the naive expectation that attractive two-body interactions automatically lead to three-body binding, and demonstrate that spin-spin interactions (particularly the $^5S_2$ partial wave) are crucial for forming such states. The work enables future lattice QCD studies of the $^5S_2$ $\Omega\Omega$ interaction to verify the predicted $\Omega\Omega\Omega$ bound state, and provides production rate estimates for experimental searches at hadron colliders.
