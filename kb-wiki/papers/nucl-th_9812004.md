# Structure in two_pion production in the d d -> alpha X reaction

> A model for the d d -> alpha pi pi reaction, based on two parallel N N -> d pi reactions, is extended to incorporate a complete set of input amplitudes. While low-energy cross sections are underestimated, the rich structure observed in the alpha-particle momentum distributions for 0.8 < T_d < 1.9 GeV (the ABC effect) is extraordinarily well reproduced. In addition, a recent measurement of deuteron analyzing powers agrees quite well with our predictions, both in frequency and magnitude of the oscillations.

# Structure in two-pion production in the dd → αX reaction

## Key claim

The ABC effect observed in the dd → αππ reaction can be explained as a kinematical enhancement arising from two independent, parallel NN → dπ reactions occurring between separate nucleon pairs from the two deuterons, with the α-particle formed by fusing the two final deuterons; however, low-energy cross sections are underestimated by this model, suggesting a different mechanism dominates near threshold.

## Method

The authors model the dd → αππ reaction as two free, parallel, and independent NN → dπ reactions taking place between separate pairs of nucleons from the two deuterons, with the α-particle formed by fusing the two final deuterons. The matrix element incorporates a complete set of nine partial wave amplitudes for the NN → dπ reaction extracted from the SAID database, including both constant and energy-dependent prescriptions (E1 and E2), with the S-state Paris wave function for deuterons and a dd:α cluster function for the α-particle. The unpolarized cross sections and deuteron analyzing powers are calculated by integrating over phase space and summing over spins and polarizations, with a flux damping factor estimated from total cross sections to account for multiple scattering.

## Key numbers

- The ABC peaks appear in α-particle momentum distributions for deuteron beam energies 0.8 < T_d < 1.9 GeV, with missing mass M_X ≈ 300–320 MeV/c²
- At T_d = 570 MeV, the total cross section is σ(570) ∼ 43 nb; at T_d = 650 MeV, σ(650) ∼ 600 nb; the model underestimates these by a factor of about 20
- The α-particle wave function is normalized to N_α = 2.3 pairs of "small" deuterons
- The experimental resolution in α-particle momentum has a relative standard deviation σ(k_α)/k_α = 0.5%
- The η-meson production cross section at 1.95 GeV is ∼ 1 μb/(sr GeV/c), too low to be visible in the plots
- The E1 prescription attains maximal NN → dπ cross section at 940 MeV, while E2 has its maximum at 1250 MeV

## Context

This work extends a preliminary study (Gardestig, Faldt, Wilkin, 1997) that used only the dominant ¹D₂p amplitude, by incorporating a complete set of NN → dπ amplitudes. The model reproduces the ABC effect first observed by Abashian, Booth, and Crowe (1960) in the pd → ³HeX reaction, and later in dd → αX by Banajgs et al. (1976). The approach contrasts with earlier ΔΔ-models (BRS, 1970s) and double-nucleon exchange models (ALS, 1970s) that had limited success, and with chiral bag models (KE) that failed in details. The work enables future investigation of whether the Roper resonance N*(1440) excitation model proposed for lower energies (Luis, 1990s) can explain the smooth behavior near threshold where the present model fails.
