# Mesons in strong magnetic fields: (I) General analyses

> We study properties of neutral and charged mesons in strong magnetic fields |eB|>> Lambda_QCD^2 with Lambda_QCD being the QCD renormalization scale. Assuming long-range interactions, we examine magnetic-field dependences of various quantities such as the constituent quark mass, chiral condensate, meson spectra, and meson wavefunctions by analyzing the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations. Based on the density of states obtained from these analyses, we extend the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to investigate thermodynamics at large B. As B increases the meson energy behaves as a slowly growing function of the meson's transverse momenta, and thus a large number of meson states is accommodated in the low energy domain; the density of states at low temperature is proportional to B^2. This extended transverse phase space in the infrared regime significantly enhances the HRG pressure at finite temperature, so that the system reaches the percolation or chiral restoration regime at lower temperature compared to the case without a magnetic field; this simple picture would offer a gauge invariant and intuitive explanation of the inverse magnetic catalysis.

## Key claim
The authors propose that in strong magnetic fields ($|eB| \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$), the quark mass gap can remain around $\sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ rather than growing with $B$, provided that long-range (infrared-dominated) interactions dominate over short-range (ultraviolet) contributions; this would explain the linear $B$-dependence of the chiral condensate and the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD, without requiring large quark mass gaps that suppress thermal fluctuations.

## Method
The authors analyze the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the lowest Landau level (LLL) quark self-energy and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for neutral and charged mesons, using the Ritus basis to incorporate the background magnetic field exactly. They assume long-range interactions (such as linear rising or harmonic oscillator potentials) and work within the large $N_c$ approximation, restricting both external and intermediate quark states to the LLL in the strong field limit. The analysis yields the transverse momentum dependence of meson spectra, which is then used to extend the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to finite $B$.

## Key numbers
- The density of states at low temperature is proportional to $B^2$.
- The chiral restoration temperature ($T_c$) decreases by $10-20\%$ at $|eB| \sim 1\,{\rm GeV}^2$ according to lattice results cited in the paper.
- The maximal magnetic fields achieved on the lattice are $|eB| \simeq 3.3\,{\rm GeV}^2$.
- The energy cost associated with transverse momenta is suppressed by $\sim B^{-2}$ for neutral mesons and $\sim |B|^{-1}$ for charged mesons when the IR component dominates.
- The LLL form factor is $R_{00}^f(\vec{q}_\perp) = \exp(-\vec{q}_\perp^2 / (4|B_f|))$, which cuts off UV contributions when $\vec{q}_\perp^2 > |B_f|$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kojo 2012) that the quark mass gap stays near $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ in strong magnetic fields, and addresses theoretical paradoxes from lattice QCD studies: the linear $B$-dependence of the chiral condensate (Buividovich 2008) and the inverse magnetic catalysis (Bali 2011, Bornyakov 2013). The analysis contrasts with effective models and chiral perturbation theory that predict a rapidly growing quark mass ($M \sim |eB|^{1/2}$) and an increasing chiral restoration temperature with $B$ (Gatto 2010). The results enable a gauge-invariant explanation of inverse magnetic catalysis via enhanced hadron resonance gas pressure, and may apply to unexplored domains such as cold and dense QCD, where similarities to the low-energy dynamics in strong magnetic fields have been noted (Gusynin 1994, Fukushima 2011, Kojo 2012).
