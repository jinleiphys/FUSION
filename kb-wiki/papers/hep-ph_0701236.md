# The constraint on the spin dependent structure function $g_1$ at low $Q^2$ through the sum rule corresponding to the moment at $n=0$

> The sum rules for the spin dependent structure function $g_1^{ab}$ in the null-plane formalism corresponding to the moment at $n=0$ has been transformed to the sum rule which relates the $g_1^{ab}$ with the cross section of the isovector photon or the real photon. Based on these sum rules, we argue that there is a deep connection among the elastic, the resonance, and the non-resonant contributions, and that it explains why the sign of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum changes at very small $Q^2$.

# The constraint on the spin dependent structure function $g_1$ at low $Q^2$ through the sum rule corresponding to the moment at $n=0$

## Key claim

The sign change of the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum at very small $Q^2$ is explained by a deep connection among elastic, resonance, and non-resonant contributions, where the rapid change of the resonances together with the continuum compensates the rapid change of the elastic contribution to satisfy the sum rule. This behavior is derived from sum rules for the spin dependent structure function $g_1^{ab}$ in the null-plane formalism, transformed to relate $g_1^{ab}$ with the cross section of the isovector photon or the real photon.

## Method

The author derived sum rules from current commutation and anticommutation relations on the null-plane for the spin dependent structure function $g_1^{ab}$, regularizing divergent integrals by analytical continuation from the nonforward direction. By setting $Q_0^2=0$ and using the relation between $G_1^{ab}$ and the polarized photoabsorption cross sections $\sigma_{3/2}^{ab} - \sigma_{1/2}^{ab}$, the sum rules were transformed to forms that relate the integral of $g_1$ over $x$ to the elastic Born term $B(Q^2)$, an integral over the resonance region of the cross section difference, and a high-energy subtraction term $K(E_c,Q^2)$.

## Key numbers

- The integral $\frac{m_p}{8\pi^2\alpha_{em}}\int_{E_0}^{2}dE\{\sigma_{3/2}^{\gamma p} - \sigma_{1/2}^{\gamma p}\}$ is approximately 0.45, with a possible error of about 20%, from experimental data of the GDH collaboration (Dutz, year not specified).
- The integral $\int_{x_c(Q^2)}^1\frac{dx}{x}g_1^{p}(x,Q^2)$ changes sign near $Q^2 \sim 0.1$ (GeV/c)$^2$, from negative to positive.
- The correction term $K(2,Q^2)$ is very small below $Q^2 = 0.5$ (GeV/c)$^2$, using parameters from a simulation (Simula, year not specified).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (Drell and Gerasimov, 1966) and the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974) to low $Q^2$, motivated by the experimental observation at CLAS (Fatemi, 2003) that the sign of $\Gamma^P(Q^2)$ changes in the very small $Q^2$ region. The sum rule derivation follows the null-plane formalism of Dashen, Jaffe, and Treiman (1972) and the regularization method of analytical continuation from nonforward directions. The approach enables a unified understanding of how elastic, resonance, and non-resonant contributions conspire to produce the sign change of the generalized GDH sum at very low $Q^2$, and provides constraints on the spin dependent structure function $g_1$ in this region.
