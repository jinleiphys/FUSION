# Important role of the spin-orbit interaction in forming the 1/2^+ orbital structure in Be isotopes

> The structure of the second 0^+ state of ^{10}Be is investigated using a microscopic $α+α+n+n$ model based on the molecular-orbit (MO) model. The second 0^+ state, which has dominantly the (1/2^+)^2 configuration, is shown to have a particularly enlarged $α-α$ structure. The kinetic energy of the two valence neutrons occupying along the $α-α$ axis is reduced remarkably due to the strong $α$ clustering and, simultaneously, the spin-orbit interaction unexpectedly plays important role to make the energy of this state much lower. The mixing of states with different spin structure is shown to be important in negative-parity states. The experimentally observed small-level spacing between 1^- and 2^- (~ 300 keV) is found to be an evidence of this spin-mixing effect. ^{12}{Be} is also investigated using $α+α+4n$ model, in which four valence neutrons are considered to occupy the (3/2^-)^2(1/2^+)^2 configuration. The energy surface of ^{12}Be is shown to exhibit similar characteristics, that the remarkable $α$ clustering and the contribution of the spin-orbit interaction make the binding of the state with (3/2^-)^2(1/2^+)^2 configuration properly stronger in comparison with the closed p-shell (3/2^-)^2(1/2^-)^2 configuration.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit interaction, which was previously thought to be negligible for the \((1/2^+)^2\) configuration in Be isotopes because the two valence neutrons occupy the same spatial orbit along the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) axis in a spin-singlet state, actually plays an important role when spin-triplet basis states are included by allowing one valence neutron to deviate from the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) axis, lowering the second \(0^+\) state of \(^{10}\)Be by about 3.5 MeV.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic \(\alpha+\alpha+n+n\) model for \(^{10}\)Be and an \(\alpha+\alpha+4n\) model for \(^{12}\)Be based on the molecular-orbit (MO) model, with the Hamiltonian including Volkov No.2 central interaction and G3RS spin-orbit interaction. Valence neutrons were described by linear combinations of local Gaussians centered at variational positions, and the total wave function was fully antisymmetrized and superposed over different \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distances and neutron configurations after angular momentum projection. Spin-triplet basis states were introduced by allowing one valence neutron to occupy a Gaussian centered away from the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) axis, enabling the spin-orbit interaction to act between the spin-singlet and spin-triplet configurations.

## Key numbers

- The second \(0^+\) state of \(^{10}\)Be has an experimental excitation energy of 6.26 MeV; the simple \(\Phi({1/2}^+, {-1/2}^+)\) configuration gives an energy difference of about 11 MeV relative to the ground state, overbinding by about 5 MeV.
- The spin-singlet \(\Phi({1/2}^+, {-1/2}^+)\) configuration at \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distance 4 fm has a \(0^+\) energy of \(-46.3\) MeV; optimizing the linear combination of Gaussians gives \(-47.7\) MeV.
- Including spin-triplet states with \(S_z = 1\) lowers the energy to \(-50.9\) MeV at the optimal Gaussian center \(\vec R = (x,z) = (1.0, 2.0)\) fm, a decrease of more than 3 MeV.
- Superposing 15 spin-triplet states gives \(-51.2\) MeV; further adding 15 \(S_z = 0\) basis states for the tail effect gives \(-52.7\) MeV.
- The expectation value of \(\hat S^2\) in the second \(0^+\) state is 0.26, corresponding to about 13% spin-triplet mixing.
- For negative-parity states in \(^{10}\)Be, the \(S_z = 0\) basis gives \(-47.7\) MeV for \(1^-\) and \(-46.7\) MeV for \(2^-\) at \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distance 3 fm, a splitting of about 1 MeV; including \(S_z = 1\) states gives \(-51.6\) MeV (\(1^-\)) and \(-51.2\) MeV (\(2^-\)), a splitting of about 400 keV, comparable to the experimental 300 keV.
- In \(^{12}\)Be, the coupling with spin-triplet states lowers the \((3/2^-)^2(1/2^+)^2\) configuration by about 4 MeV at \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distance 3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous molecular-orbit model for \(^{10}\)Be (Itagaki, 1999) by including spin-triplet basis states that were absent in traditional MO models (Okabe, 1979; Seya, 1981), and by improving the description of the neutron tail. The study addresses the underbinding of the second \(0^+\) state in \(^{10}\)Be and the breaking of the \(N=8\) magic number in \(^{12}\)Be, which was previously indicated by shell-model calculations (Suzuki, 1995) showing less than 30% closed \(p\)-shell component. The results enable future analysis of pairing mixing among configurations in \(^{12}\)Be and excited cluster states.
