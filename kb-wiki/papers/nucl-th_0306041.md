# Hydrodynamical instabilities in an expanding quark gluon plasma

> We study the mechanism responsible for the onset of instabilities in a chiral phase transition at nonzero temperature and baryon chemical potential. As a low-energy effective model, we consider an expanding relativistic plasma of quarks coupled to a chiral field, and obtain a phenomenological chiral hydrodynamics from a variational principle. Studying the dispersion relation for small fluctuations around equilibrium, we identify the role played by chiral waves and pressure waves in the generation of instabilities. We show that pressure modes become unstable earlier than chiral modes.

# Hydrodynamical instabilities in an expanding quark gluon plasma

## Key claim

Pressure (sound) modes become unstable earlier than chiral modes during the chiral phase transition in an expanding quark-gluon plasma, meaning that mechanical instabilities set in before one would expect from analyzing the thermodynamic potential decoupled from hydrodynamical modes. This shifts the spinodal lines from the values obtained studying only the chiral degrees of freedom, modifying the phase diagram.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological chiral hydrodynamics from a variational principle, coupling a relativistic quark fluid to a chiral field (the linear sigma model with quarks). They expand the coupled equations of motion around equilibrium to first order in perturbations, derive the dispersion relation for small fluctuations, and identify the conditions for instability (negative squared frequency) for both pressure modes and chiral modes. Two illustrative coupling strengths are studied: g=3.3 (smooth crossover at zero chemical potential) and g=5.5 (first-order transition even at zero chemical potential).

## Key numbers

- For g=3.3: constituent quark mass = 307 MeV; critical point E at T_E = 98 MeV and mu_E = 630 MeV; mu_c = 920 MeV at T=0
- For g=5.5: critical temperature at mu=0 is T_c ≈ 123 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV; pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV; sigma mass m_sigma = 600 MeV (from lambda^2 = 20)
- Quark degeneracy factor nu_q = 12 (color-spin-isospin)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinodal decomposition in expanding QGP by Scavenius et al. (1999, 2001), Paech, and others, who used low-energy effective models and numerical simulations. The key advance is showing analytically that hydrodynamical pressure modes become unstable before the chiral modes, which had not been captured by analyses of the thermodynamic potential alone. The results enable more realistic modeling of the hadronization process in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where rapid expansion may bypass nucleation and enter spinodal decomposition, as suggested by the "sudden hadronization" scenario. The variational formulation also provides a framework for coupling chiral fields to fluid dynamics in cosmological applications.
