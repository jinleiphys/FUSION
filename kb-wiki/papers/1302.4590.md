# Pasta phases in neutron star studied with extended relativistic mean field models

> To explain several properties of finite nuclei, infinite matter, and neutron stars in a unified way within the relativistic mean field models, it is important to extend them either with higher order couplings or with density-dependent couplings. These extensions are known to have strong impact in the high-density regime. Here we explore their role on the equation of state at densities lower than the saturation density of finite nuclei which govern the phase transitions associated with pasta structures in the crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The higher-order couplings in extended relativistic mean field models, particularly the fourth-order coupling between the ω and ρ meson fields (Λ_v), can significantly modify the equation of state at subsaturation densities relevant for pasta phases in neutron star crusts, but only at low proton fractions; at higher proton fractions the results from different parameter sets converge.

## Method

The authors employed the Wigner-Seitz approximation to calculate Coulomb and surface energies for five pasta geometries (spheres, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) in neutron star matter. They compared results from six relativistic mean field parameter sets: NL3 (standard nonlinear), DDME2 (density-dependent), FSUGold and IUFSU (with additional higher-order couplings), and G1 and G2 (effective field theory motivated with several higher-order couplings). The energy per particle and phase transitions were calculated for proton fractions of 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5.

## Key numbers

- The different parameter sets converge at a density of approximately 0.11 fm⁻³ for proton fraction x=0.1, which is lower than the nuclear saturation density.
- For symmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction x=0.5), the binding is maximum and results from different parameters show little difference.
- At x=0.1, three clear groups of parameter sets emerge: (i) shallow well in energy (G1, G2, NL3), (ii) saturated energy at low density (FSUGold, DDME2), and (iii) hump in energy at low density (IUFSU).
- The homogeneous matter phase occurs at a density of approximately 0.11 fm⁻³ in most cases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier report on pasta structures studied with higher-order couplings (Gupta and Arumugam, 2011) and complements a comprehensive analysis recently published by Avancini et al. (2012). The study shows that the low-density behavior of the equation of state, especially for neutron-rich matter, should be linked to the symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness rather than solely to high-density effects. The results enable more precise modeling of the neutron star crust-core transition region, which is relevant for understanding thermal evolution, x-ray bursts, and glitches in neutron stars.
