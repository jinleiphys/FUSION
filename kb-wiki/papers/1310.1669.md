# Transverse wobbling-a collective mode in odd-A triaxial nuclei

> The wobbling motion of a triaxial rotor coupled to a high-j quasiparticle is treated semiclassi- cally. Longitudinal and transverse coupling regimes can be distinguished depending on, respectively, whether the quasiparticle angular momentum is oriented parallel or perpendicular to the rotor axis with the largest moment of inertia. Simple analytical expressions for the wobbling frequency and the electromagnetic E2 and M1 transition probabilities are derived assuming rigid alignment of the quasiparticle with one of the rotor axes and harmonic oscillations (HFA). Transverse wobbling is characterized by a decrease of the wobbling frequency with increasing angular momentum. Two examples for transverse wobbling, 163Lu and 135Pr, are studied in the framework of the full triax- ial particle-rotor model and the HFA. The signature of transverse wobbling, decreasing wobbling frequency and enhanced E2 inter-band transitions, is found in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed wobbling bands in odd-A triaxial nuclei, which show a *decrease* of wobbling frequency with increasing spin, can be explained by a "transverse" coupling regime where the odd quasiparticle's angular momentum is aligned perpendicular to the rotor axis with the largest moment of inertia, contrary to the "longitudinal" arrangement assumed in prior QTR model calculations that predicted an increasing frequency.

## Method

The authors performed a semiclassical analysis of the quasiparticle triaxial rotor (QTR) Hamiltonian assuming rigid alignment of the odd quasiparticle's angular momentum with one principal axis (Frozen Alignment approximation). They derived analytical expressions for the wobbling frequency and E2/M1 transition probabilities in the harmonic Frozen Alignment (HFA) approximation, and carried out full numerical QTR calculations using the core-quasiparticle-coupling (CQM) formalism for two nuclei, 163Lu and 135Pr, with moments of inertia fitted to the natural order (medium axis largest, short axis intermediate, long axis smallest) obtained from microscopic cranking calculations.

## Key numbers

- In 163Lu, the QTR calculation with fitted moments of inertia (J_m=64, J_s=56, J_l=13 in units of hbar^2/MeV) reproduces the experimentally observed decrease of the wobbling frequency with spin for the n=1 and n=2 pi i_{13/2} wobbling bands.
- In 135Pr, the QTR calculation with fitted moments of inertia (J_m=21, J_s=13, J_l=4 in units of hbar^2/MeV) reproduces the experimental wobbling bands built on the pi h_{11/2} configuration.
- The critical angular momentum where the wobbling frequency reaches zero is given by J_c = j * J_2 / (J_2 - J_3), where j is the quasiparticle angular momentum and J_2, J_3 are moments of inertia.
- The deformation parameters used are epsilon=0.4, gamma=20 degrees for 163Lu and epsilon=0.16, gamma=26 degrees for 135Pr.

## Context

This work extends the wobbling concept from even-even triaxial rotors (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) to odd-A nuclei, where the presence of a high-j quasiparticle creates two distinct coupling regimes. It contradicts prior QTR calculations for Lu isotopes (Hamamoto, 2002; Hamamoto, 2003; Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe, 2006; Tanabe and Sugawara-Tanabe, 2008) that used a longitudinal arrangement of moments of inertia and predicted increasing wobbling frequency with spin, in disagreement with experiment. The transverse wobbling concept explains the observed decreasing wobbling frequency in 163Lu (Odland, 2001) and 135Pr, and enables the interpretation of wobbling bands in other triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) nuclei such as 161,165,167Lu and 167Ta.
