# Considering non-locality in the optical potentials within eikonal models

> Background: For its simplicity, the eikonal method is the tool of choice to analyze nuclear reactions at high energies ($E>100$ MeV/nucleon), including knockout reactions. However, so far, the effective interactions used in this method are assumed to be fully local. Purpose: Given the recent studies on non-local optical potentials, in this work we assess whether non-locality in the optical potentials is expected to impact reactions at high energies and then explore different avenues for extending the eikonal method to include non-local interactions. Method: We compare angular distributions obtained for non-local interactions (using the exact R-matrix approach for elastic scattering and the adiabatic distorted wave approximation for transfer) with those obtained using their local-equivalent interactions. Results: Our results show that transfer observables are significantly impacted by non-locality in the high-energy regime. Because knockout reactions are dominated by stripping (transfer to inelastic channels), non-locality is expected to have a large effect on knockout observables too. Three approaches are explored for extending the eikonal method to non-local interactions, including an iterative method and a perturbation theory. Conclusions: None of the derived extensions of the eikonal model provide a good description of elastic scattering. This work suggests that non-locality removes the formal simplicity associated with the eikonal model.

## Key claim

Non-locality in optical potentials significantly impacts transfer observables at energies above 100 MeV/nucleon, and therefore is expected to affect knockout observables as well; however, none of the attempted extensions of the eikonal method to include non-local interactions provide a good description of elastic scattering, suggesting that non-locality removes the formal simplicity of the eikonal model.

## Method

The authors compared angular distributions for neutron transfer on 208Pb using the exact R-matrix method for elastic scattering and the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) for transfer, contrasting results from non-local Perey-Buck potentials with their local-equivalent potentials fitted using the code sfresco. They then explored three approaches to extend the eikonal approximation to non-local interactions: an iterative method, an alternative iterative method with non-locality in the transverse coordinate, and a perturbative approach. All extensions were tested against exact non-local solutions for neutron elastic scattering on 208Pb at 69 MeV and 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Transfer cross sections for 208Pb(d,p)209Pb: non-locality increases the integrated cross section by 22% at 100 MeV, diminishes it by 4% at 138 MeV, and reduces it by 20% at 300 MeV
- For elastic scattering of neutrons on 208Pb at 69 MeV, the eikonal approximation underestimates the exact cross section by 30% at 0 degrees and under-predicts the integrated cross section by about 21%
- At 150 MeV, the relative difference between exact and eikonal integrated elastic-scattering cross sections is about 2%
- The relative difference between non-local and local-equivalent integrated elastic-scattering cross sections using exact methods is less than 3% at 69 MeV and 10% at 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of non-locality effects in optical potentials, previously focused on (d,p) transfer reactions below 50 MeV by Titus et al. (2016) and others, into the high-energy regime above 100 MeV/nucleon where knockout reactions are typically measured. The finding that eikonal methods cannot be straightforwardly extended to non-local interactions suggests that alternative approaches providing accurate wave functions over the whole radial range, such as the distorted wave Born approximation, continuum-discretized coupled channel method, or the dynamical eikonal approximation, are better suited for describing high-energy reactions with non-local interactions.
