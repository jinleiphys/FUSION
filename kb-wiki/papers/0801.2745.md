# Scalar form factors and nuclear interactions

> The scalar-isoscalar term in the two-pion exchange $NN$ potential is abnormally large and does not respect the hierarchy of effects predicted by chiral perturbation theory. We argue that this anomaly is associated with non-perturbative effects, which are also present in the $πN$ scalar form factor.

# Scalar Form Factors and Nuclear Interactions

## Key claim

The anomalously large scalar-isoscalar component of the two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential, which violates the chiral perturbation theory hierarchy, is argued to arise from non-perturbative effects that also appear in the pion-nucleon scalar form factor; the range of validity of calculations using nucleon and delta intermediate states is much wider than that predicted by chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon scalar form factor using a leading order tree contribution at O(q^2) from the low-energy constant c_1, supplemented by two triangle diagrams with nucleon and delta intermediate states giving O(q^3) and O(q^4) corrections. They separate contact and loop contributions in configuration space and evaluate the sigma-term by integrating the scalar form factor outside a critical radius R where the quark condensate vanishes, using the expression sigma_N = (4/3) pi R^3 f_pi^2 m^2 + 4 pi integral from R to infinity of r^2 tilde_sigma_N(r) dr.

## Key numbers

- The sigma-term computed from the scalar form factor yields a range of 43 MeV < sigma_N < 49 MeV, depending on the value of the pi N Delta coupling constant
- The empirical value of the sigma-term is 45 +/- 8 MeV
- The critical radius where the chiral angle reaches pi/2 is R ~ 0.6 fm
- The chiral hierarchy is subverted for distances smaller than 1.5 fm, where the delta contribution becomes more important than the nucleon contribution
- The isospin-independent central component V_C^+ is approximately 10 times larger than the isospin-dependent component V_C^-

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the two-pion exchange potential from Robilotta and collaborators (2007), where the isospin-independent central component V_C^+ was found to be dominated by a term proportional to the pion cloud contribution to the nucleon scalar form factor. The authors previously developed a model for the nucleon scalar form factor in a companion paper (2007) that successfully reproduced the empirical sigma-term. The results contradict the hierarchy predicted by chiral perturbation theory, where the isospin-independent central component should begin at O(q^3) and the isospin-dependent component at O(q^2). The findings enable a better understanding of why calculations using nucleon and delta intermediate states can reproduce phenomenological potentials such as the Argonne potential up to 1 fm, despite power counting becoming ineffective already at 2 fm.
