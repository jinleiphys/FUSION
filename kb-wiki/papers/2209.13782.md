# Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation: evidence for detection of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions

> The scaling properties of the $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ correlator and the $Δγ$ correlator are used to investigate a possible chiral-magnetically-driven (CME) charge separation in $p$+Au, $d$+Au, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200$~GeV, and in $p$+Pb ($\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$~TeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ and $2.76$~TeV. The results for $p$+Au, $d$+Au, $p$+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions, show the $1/{\rm N_{ch}}$ scaling for background-driven charge separation. However, the results for Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr collisions show scaling violations which indicate a CME contribution in the presence of a large background. In mid-central collisions, the CME accounts for approximately 27\% of the signal + background in Au+Au and roughly a factor of two smaller for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr, which show similar magnitudes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the scaling properties of the charge-sensitive correlators \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) and \(\Delta\gamma\) can distinguish background-driven charge separation from chiral-magnetic-effect (CME) driven charge separation, with the caveat that the background must first be shown to obey \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) scaling in small collision systems where the CME is negligible.

## Method

The author analyzed published data for the \(\Delta\gamma/v_2\) correlator and the inverse variance \(\sigma^{-2}_{R_{\Psi_2}}\) of the \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) correlator across multiple collision systems at RHIC (\(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV for \(p\)+Au, \(d\)+Au, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, Au+Au) and the LHC (\(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02\) TeV for \(p\)+Pb and Pb+Pb, and 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb). The scaling of these observables with \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) was compared between small systems (where the CME is expected to be negligible) and large systems, using model simulations from AVFD and HIJING to establish the expected background scaling. Deviations from linear \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) scaling were quantified via a power-law fit parameter \(c\) and used to extract the CME fraction \(f_{\rm CME}\).

## Key numbers

* In mid-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV, the CME accounts for approximately 27% of the signal plus background (\(f_{\rm CME} \sim 27\%\)).
* The CME fraction in mid-central Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at \(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV is roughly a factor of two smaller than in Au+Au, with similar magnitudes for the two isobars.
* For \(p\)+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the \(\Delta\gamma/v_2\) data show \(c \approx 0\), consistent with pure \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) scaling and negligible CME contribution.
* For \(d\)+Au collisions at \(\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200\) GeV, the \(\Delta\gamma/v_2\) data also show \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) scaling, confirming negligible CME in that system.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect by using scaling violations of the \(1/N_{\rm ch}\) trend, building on earlier correlator methods introduced by Voloshin (2004) and Magdy (2017). The analysis uses published data from the STAR Collaboration (2009-2021) for RHIC systems and from the ALICE and CMS Collaborations (2012-2020) for LHC systems. The results enable future isobar collision comparisons (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) to isolate the CME signal from background, and provide a methodology to quantify the CME fraction in heavy-ion collisions where the magnetic field is significant.
