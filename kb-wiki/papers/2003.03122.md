# On the robustness of sub-shell closures: A high angular momentum analysis of the titanium isotopes

> The potential sub-shell closures N = 32 and N = 34 are analyzed at high spins in the titanium isotopes within the generalized beyond mean field theory considering triaxial deformations and the angular frequency as generator coordinates together with the particle number and the angular momentum conservation. A good description of bulk properties, high angular momenta spectra and transition probabilities is obtained. The outcome at high spin in these nuclei is consistent with the magic number character of N = 32 but not of N = 34.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that the N=32 sub-shell closure remains robust at high spin in titanium isotopes, but the N=34 sub-shell closure does not satisfy the same criteria, based on the behavior of the 8+₁ state and the associated structural changes in the collective wave functions.

## Method

The authors used a symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) approach within the generalized beyond mean field theory, incorporating triaxial quadrupole deformations (β,γ) and the cranking frequency ω as generator coordinates, together with particle number and angular momentum projection. The intrinsic wave functions were generated by solving constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations with the finite-range density-dependent Gogny D1S interaction, using three values of the angular frequency (ℏω = 0.0, 0.5, and 1.0 MeV) and 70 points in the (β,γ) plane. The Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation was then solved to obtain the energies and wave functions of the eigenstates for different angular momenta in ⁵⁰Ti, ⁵²Ti, ⁵⁴Ti, and ⁵⁶Ti.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle (theory vs. experiment, in MeV): ⁵⁰Ti 8.81 (8.76), ⁵²Ti 8.74 (8.70), ⁵⁴Ti 8.62 (8.60), ⁵⁶Ti 8.48 (8.47)
- Magnetic moment of 2+₁ state (theory vs. experiment, in μ_N): ⁵⁰Ti 2.4 (2.89±0.15), ⁵²Ti 2.1 (1.7±0.4)
- Electric quadrupole moment of 2+₁ state in ⁵⁰Ti: theory 5.7 e fm², experiment 8±16 e fm²
- Mean squared charge radius of ⁵⁰Ti: theory 3.6 fm, experiment 3.57 fm
- Maximum of the 8+₁ collective wave function in (β,γ) coordinates: ⁵⁰Ti at (0.16, 30°), ⁵²Ti at (0.15, -8°), ⁵⁴Ti at (0.21, -17°), ⁵⁶Ti at (0.14, -8°)
- Maximum of the 6+₁ collective wave function in (β,γ) coordinates: ⁵⁰Ti at (0.15, -16°), ⁵²Ti at (0.16, -7°), ⁵⁴Ti at (0.12, -9°), ⁵⁶Ti at (0.16, -7°)
- Quadrupole moment of 6+₁ states: approximately -25 e fm² for all four isotopes

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the N=32 and N=34 sub-shell closures in titanium isotopes, which had been investigated experimentally in calcium (Nature 2013), argon (PRL 2015), potassium (PRL 2015), scandium (PRC 2009), titanium (PLB 2002, PRC 2004), and chromium (PLB 2001, PLB 2005). The N=34 sub-shell closure had been suggested in ⁵⁴Ca (Nature 2013, PRL 2018, PRL 2019) and ⁵²Ar (PRL 2019) but not detected in Ti, Sc, or Cr isotopes. The authors' approach improves upon earlier axially symmetric beyond mean field theories (PRL 2007) that predicted N=32 but not N=34 as a closure, and upon standard symmetry-conserving configuration mixing methods that provided only qualitative agreement with experimental spectra due to spectrum stretching. The results enable future high-spin experimental studies of these isotopes to test the predicted structural changes at the 8+₁ state.
