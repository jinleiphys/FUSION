# Roper-like resonances with various flavor contents and their two-pion emission decays

> We study the three-body decay of the newly observed bottom baryon $Λ_b^*(6072)$ by LHCb; $Λ_b^*(6072) \to Λ_b ππ$. Its mass about 500 MeV above the ground state $Λ_b$ and a broad width imply that the state could be an analogue of the Roper resonance of the nucleon $N(1440)$. In terms of sequential processes going through $Σ_b$ and $Σ_b^*$, we find that the observed invariant mass distribution is reproduced assuming its spin and parity $J^P = 1/2^+$. We discuss that the ratio of the two sequential processes and angular correlation of two pions are useful for the determination of spin and parity. We suggest further studies for the Roper resonance analogue in various flavor contents, raising an interesting and important question in baryon spectroscopy.

# Roper-like resonances with various flavor contents and their two-pion emission decays

## Key claim

The newly observed bottom baryon Λ_b*(6072) is an analogue of the nucleon Roper resonance N(1440) with spin and parity J^P = 1/2^+, and its three-body decay Λ_b*(6072) → Λ_b π π is dominated by sequential processes through Σ_b and Σ_b* intermediate states, provided that the observed invariant mass distribution is reproduced under the assumption of J^P = 1/2^+.

## Method

The authors employed a non-relativistic method to compute amplitudes for sequential decay processes Λ_b* → Σ_b^(*) π followed by Σ_b^(*) → Λ_b π, using heavy quark spin symmetry to constrain the ratios of coupling constants for decays to Σ_b versus Σ_b*. They constructed Dalitz plots and invariant mass distributions, then performed a convolution over the finite mass range of Λ_b*(6072) weighted by a Breit-Wigner function to compare with LHCb data.

## Key numbers

- Mass of Λ_b*(6072): M = 6072.3 MeV, width Γ = 72 MeV (measured by LHCb)
- Excitation energy of Λ_b*(6072) above ground state Λ_b: approximately 500 MeV
- Momentum of pion in Λ_b* → Σ_b π decay: p_1 = 215 MeV
- Momentum of pion in Λ_b* → Σ_b* π decay: p_2 = 192 MeV
- Ratio R = Γ(Λ_b* → Σ_b* π) / Γ(Λ_b* → Σ_b π) = 1.43 for brown muck spin j = 0, and 0.36 for j = 1
- Predicted angular correlation for Λ_b* spin 1/2: W(θ_12) ∝ 1 + 3.3 cos^2 θ_12

## Context

This work extends the study of Roper-like resonances from the nucleon N(1440) (Roper, 1964) to heavy flavor baryons, following dynamical model studies by Suzuki (2009) and Kamano (2010) and transition form factor measurements by Aznauryan (2009) that established N(1440) as a radial excitation. The authors previously applied Dalitz plot analysis to Λ_c*(2765) in the charm sector (Arifi, 2020). The results enable determination of spin and parity for undetermined candidates such as Λ_c*(2765) and suggest searches for analogous states in Ξ baryons, including Ξ_c*(2970) and a predicted Ξ_b* near 6.2 GeV decaying into Ξ_b π π.
