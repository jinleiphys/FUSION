# Averaged transverse momentum correlations of hadrons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We compile experimental data for the averaged transverse momentum ($\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $) of proton, $Λ$, $Ξ^{-}$, $Ω^{-}$ and $ϕ$ at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200, 39, 27, 19.6, 11.5, 7.7 GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 2.76 TeV, and find that experimental data of these hadrons exhibit systematic correlations. We apply a quark combination model with equal-velocity combination approximation to derive analytic formulas of hadronic $\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $ in the case of exponential form of quark $p_{T}$ spectra at hadronization. We use them to successfully explain the systematic correlations exhibited in $\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $ data of $pΛ$, $ΛΞ^{-}$, $Ξ^{-}Ω^{-}$ and $Ξ^{-}ϕ$ pairs. We also use them to successfully explain the regularity observed in $\left\langle p_{T}\right\rangle $ of these hadrons as the function of $(dN_{ch}/dy)/(N_{part}/2)$ at mid-rapidity in central heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. Our results suggest that the constituent quark degrees of freedom and the equal-velocity combination of these constituent quarks at hadronization play important role in understanding the production of baryons and $ϕ$ meson at these RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the systematic correlations observed in the averaged transverse momentum of proton, Λ, Ξ⁻, Ω⁻, and φ hadrons across multiple collision energies and centralities at RHIC and LHC can be explained by a quark combination model with equal-velocity combination approximation, without needing to account for other dynamical effects like system size, geometry, or hadronic rescattering.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data for ⟨pT⟩ of proton, Λ, Ξ⁻, Ω⁻, and φ at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200, 39, 27, 19.6, 11.5, 7.7 GeV and in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. They applied a quark combination model assuming equal-velocity combination of constituent quarks at hadronization, with quark pT spectra parameterized as an exponential form f_q(pT) = N pT^k exp[-√(pT² + m_i²)/T_i]. They derived analytic formulas for hadronic ⟨pT⟩ using constituent quark masses m_u = m_d = 0.3 GeV and m_s = 0.5 GeV, and fitted the slope parameters α_q = m_q/T_q to reproduce the experimental correlations.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV
- Collision energies studied: √sNN = 200, 39, 27, 19.6, 11.5, 7.7 GeV (Au+Au) and 2.76 TeV (Pb+Pb)
- Exponent parameter k explored in range [0, 1] for quark pT spectra
- For k=0.3, extracted coefficients for α_u(α_s) relation: c₀ = -0.11, c₁ = 0.84
- For k=0.3, extracted coefficients for α_ū(α_s) relation: d₀ = -0.11, d₁ = 0.86
- For k=0.3, extracted parameters for α_q vs (dNch/dy)/(Npart/2) relation: u quark g=0.42, h=0.36; ū quark g=0.45, h=0.35; s quark g=0.26, h=0.25
- Numerical approximations for ⟨pT⟩ have relative errors less than about 3% for the physical range studied

## Context

This work extends the equal-velocity combination (EVC) model previously developed by Song (2017, 2020) to explain hadron production at hadronization. The authors show that the EVC mechanism can describe ⟨pT⟩ correlations across multiple hadron species without invoking other dynamical processes, whereas event generators URQMD 3.4 (Bass, 1998) and AMPT 2.26 (Lin, 2004) do not reproduce these correlations with default parameters. The results suggest that constituent quark degrees of freedom and equal-velocity combination at hadronization play an important role in understanding baryon and φ meson production at RHIC and LHC energies, and the findings enable future studies of hadronization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions.
