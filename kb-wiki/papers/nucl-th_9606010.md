# Interplay of Interference Effects in Production of $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ off Nuclei

> Our main observations are: (i) The effective path length of the $c\bar c$ wave packet, which is produced in the nucleus, grows with the energy of the produced charmonium. The variation is controlled by the coherence length $l_c=2E_Ψ/M^2_Ψ$. (ii) A colorless $c\bar c$ wave packet produced in $pp$-interaction is a specific linear combination of the $J/Ψ$ and $Ψ'$ states, and interacts substantially weaker that any of its components. The time evolution of this wave packet is controlled by the formation length, $l_f=2E_Ψ/(M^2_{Ψ'} - M^2_Ψ)$. Exact formulas incorporating with these effects are derived. The interplay of the two phenomena results in a nontrivial energy- and $x_F$-dependence of the nuclear suppression of the charmonium production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions and explains some of the experimentally observed effects.

# Interplay of Interference Effects in Production of J/Ψ and Ψ' off Nuclei

## Key claim

The nuclear suppression of charmonium production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions depends on a nontrivial interplay between two quantum interference effects: the coherence length (which increases suppression with energy) and the formation length (which decreases suppression with energy), but the calculations are restricted to small values of the Feynman variable x_F and assume dominance of the direct production mechanism.

## Method

The authors develop a two-coupled-channel model including the J/Ψ and Ψ' states, solving the evolution of the produced c-cbar wave packet through nuclear matter using a matrix representation of the Schrödinger equation. They derive approximate analytic expressions for the nuclear suppression function S^{hA}_Ψ(E_Ψ) by expanding to first order in the small parameter σ^{ΨN}_{tot}⟨T⟩, incorporating both the coherence length l_c = 2E_Ψ/M^2_Ψ and the formation length l_f = 2E_Ψ/(M^2_{Ψ'} - M^2_Ψ). For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the suppression factorizes into a product of the suppression in pA_1 and pA_2 interactions evaluated at x_F and -x_F respectively.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length: l_c = 2E_Ψ/M^2_Ψ, where M_Ψ is the J/Ψ mass
- Formation length: l_f = 2E_Ψ/(M^2_{Ψ'} - M^2_Ψ)
- Longitudinal momentum transfer difference between Ψ and Ψ': q_f = (M^2_{Ψ'} - M^2_Ψ)/(2E_Ψ)
- Estimated matrix element ratio ε = ⟨Ψ'|f̂|Ψ⟩/⟨Ψ|f̂|Ψ⟩ = -√(2/3)
- Estimated cross section ratio r = σ^{Ψ'N}_{tot}/σ^{ΨN}_{tot} = 7/3
- Experimental value for the initial state composition: |R_ex| = 0.48 ± 0.06
- Proton beam energies considered: 200 GeV and 800 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model treatment of charmonium production in nuclei by incorporating quantum interference effects that were previously neglected in the standard absorption model (which corresponds to l_c = l_f = 0). The authors build on their own earlier work (Hufner and Kopeliovich, 1996) where the two-coupled-channel formalism was first derived, and on the coherence length concept developed by Kopeliovich and Zakharov (1991). The results explain experimental observations from the NA3 Collaboration (Badier et al., 1983) and the NA38 Collaboration (Baglin et al., 1995), specifically: the energy dependence of nuclear suppression in pA collisions, the near-equality of J/Ψ and Ψ' suppression in pA collisions, and the stronger suppression of Ψ' relative to J/Ψ in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The paper identifies that additional suppression from interactions with produced particles is needed to fully account for the observed effects in heavy-ion collisions.
