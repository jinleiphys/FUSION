# Centrality and energy dependence of charged particles in p+A and A+A collisions from running coupling $k_{T}$-factorization

> We extend the numerical analysis of the energy and centrality dependence of particle multiplicities at midrapidity in high-energy p+A and A+A collisions from a running coupling $k_T$-factorization formula made in~\cite{Dumitru:2018gjm} by considering two unintegrated gluon distributions that were left out. While a good agreement with the experimental data in A+A collisions is achieved, improving the description of those observables in p+A collisions calls for a better understanding of the proton unintegrated gluon distribution at larger values of $x$ and also the use of a realistic impact parameter dependence.

# Centrality and energy dependence of charged particles in p+A and A+A collisions from running coupling \(k_{T}\)-factorization

## Key claim

The running coupling \(k_{T}\)-factorization formula, when combined with unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) from the rcBK equation, provides a good description of charged particle multiplicities in A+A collisions across energies, but for p+A collisions all UGDs are only in qualitative agreement with LHC data and fail to describe RHIC data, indicating the need for a better understanding of the proton UGD at larger \(x\) and a realistic impact parameter dependence.

## Method

The authors compute midrapidity charged particle multiplicities in p+A and A+A collisions using a running coupling \(k_{T}\)-factorization formula derived by Horowitz and Kovchegov (2010), which resums one-loop corrections into the running of the coupling. The unintegrated gluon distributions are obtained from solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation using three different initial condition sets (MV, g1.101, g1.119) from the AAMQS fits to HERA data, and the normalization is fixed by matching central Pb+Pb data at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For central (0-6%) Au+Au/Pb+Pb/Xe+Xe collisions, results are shown at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, 2.76 TeV, 5.02 TeV, and 5.44 TeV; curves and data points are scaled by factors 1.0/0.85/1.0/1.35 respectively for visibility.
- For p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, beyond \(N_{\rm part} \simeq 4\) the multiplicity per participant decreases slightly with \(N_{\rm part}\); a fit of the MV result for \(5 \le N_{\rm part} \le 15\) gives \(\sim \ln^{1.25}(N_{\rm part}) / N_{\rm part}\).
- The rcBK evolution is initialized at \(x_0 = 0.01\) and results are shown at evolution rapidity \(Y = 3\) for the UGDs.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dumitru, Giannini, Luzum, and Nara (2018) by including two additional UGD sets (g1.101 and g1.119) beyond the MV set previously considered. It follows the phenomenological approach of earlier works such as Levin and Tuchin (2010), Tribedy and Venugopalan (2010), and Albacete et al. (2012) that applied \(k_{T}\)-factorization to compute multiplicities in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future improvements by incorporating the reweighting procedure of Dumitru et al. (2018) to account for bias in small-\(x\) gluon field configurations, and by extending the CGC framework to higher \(x\) values as suggested by Jalilian-Marian and Rezaeian (2017).
