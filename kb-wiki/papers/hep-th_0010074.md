# Space--Time Symmetry, CPT and Mirror Fermions

> The motivations for the construction of an 8-component representation of fermion fields based on a two dimensional representation of time reversal transformation and CPT invariance are discussed. Some of the elementary properties of the quantum field theory in the 8-component representation are studied. It includes the space-time and charge conjugation symmetries, the implementation of a reality condition, the construction of interaction theories, the field theoretical imaginary- and real-time approach to thermodynamics of fermionic systems, the quantization of fermion fields, their particle content and the Feynman rules for perturbation theories. It is shown that in the new presentation, a CPT violation can be formulated in principle. The construction of interaction theories in the 8-component theory for fermions is shown to be constrained by the CPT invariance. The short distance behavior and relativistic covariance are studied. In the path integral representation of the thermodynamical potential, the conventional imaginary-time approach is shown to be smoothly connected to a real-time thermal field theory in the 8-component representation for fermion fields without any additional subtraction of infinities. The metastability at zero density and the nature of the spontaneous CP violation in color superconducting phases of strong interaction ground states are clarified.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an 8-component representation for fermion fields based on a two-dimensional representation of time reversal transformation, which allows for the formulation of CPT-violating theories in principle, while showing that the construction of interaction theories in this representation is constrained by CPT invariance.

## Method

The author constructs an 8-component spinor from two 4-component Dirac spinors that transform into each other under time reversal, implementing a two-dimensional representation of the time reversal transformation that avoids the conceptual problems of the standard one-dimensional representation. A reality condition based on CPT invariance is imposed to eliminate redundant degrees of freedom at zero density, and the fermion propagator is derived from the constrained eigenvalue problem. The transformation properties of fermion bilinear operators under CPT are classified into two types (type-I and type-II), and the construction of Lorentz-covariant interaction vertices is analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The 8-component spinor Ψ is formed from two 4-component Dirac spinors ψ₁ and ψ₂, satisfying the Dirac equation (i∂̸ - m)Ψ = 0
- The fermion propagator at zero density is S_F(p) = i/(p̸ - mO₃ + iε), where O₃ is the third Pauli matrix acting on the 8-component space
- The fermion propagator at finite density is S_F(p) = i/(p̸ + μγ⁰O₃ - mO₃ + iε), where μ is the chemical potential
- The mass term in the Lagrangian must take the form Σ = mO₃ (a 2×2 block matrix with +m and -m on the diagonal) to satisfy the reality condition

## Context

This work formalizes and extends earlier proposals by Ying and collaborators (ThPap, ThPap2, TMU, 1999-2000) for an 8-component fermion representation, which had been applied to nucleon structure and stability problems. The paper addresses conceptual difficulties with time reversal transformation originally discussed by Wigner (1932) and later by Weinberg, and provides a systematic quantum field theory framework that resolves divergences in finite-density 4-component theories. The formalism enables analysis of spontaneous CP violation in color superconducting phases of the strong interaction vacuum state and establishes connections between imaginary-time and real-time thermal field theories without additional subtractions.
