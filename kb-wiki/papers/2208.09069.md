# Search for the Chiral Effect using isobar collisions and BES-II data from STAR

> In these proceedings we discuss the recent precision measurements of charge separation difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV by STAR collaboration. The measurements indicate that the magnitude of the difference in the charge separation attributable to the magnetic fields between the two systems is smaller than previously expected. We also present charge separation measurements on the Chiral Magnetic Effect search from the RHIC BES-II experiment using the Event Plane Detectors (EPD) from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 27 GeV.

## Key claim

The isobar blind analysis found that the predefined CME criteria are not satisfied by the data, and the measured charge separation difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions is smaller than previously expected, with the results consistent with a new background estimate that includes non-flow contributions.

## Method

The STAR collaboration collected approximately 2 billion minimum-bias events from Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV and performed a blind analysis with five independent teams to minimize unconscious biases. They measured the CME-sensitive \(\Delta\gamma\) observable, which quantifies charge separation correlations with respect to the second-order event plane, and compared the ratio \((\Delta\gamma_{112}/v_2)^{Ru+Ru}/(\Delta\gamma_{112}/v_2)^{Zr+Zr}\) against predefined criteria. Additionally, they analyzed Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=27\) GeV from the BES-II program using the Event Plane Detectors to measure charge separation with respect to both first-order (\(\Psi_1\)) and second-order (\(\Psi_2\)) event planes.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field squared is 15% larger in Ru+Ru collisions compared to Zr+Zr collisions, while the flow-driven background difference approximates 4%.
- Approximately 2 billion minimum-bias events were collected for both isobar species combined.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=27\) GeV, the beam rapidity is \(Y_{\rm beam}=3.4\), which falls in the middle of the EPD acceptance covering \(2.1<|\eta|<5.1\).
- The double ratio \(\Delta\gamma/v_2(\Psi_1)/\Delta\gamma/v_2(\Psi_2)\) measured at 27 GeV is consistent with unity, indicating results consistent with a background-only scenario.

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration's previous CME searches using isobar collisions (STAR 2021) and the Beam Energy Scan I program (STAR 2014) by providing higher-precision measurements with blind analysis techniques and the newly installed Event Plane Detectors. The results contradict the expectation from Huang (2017) that a 15% magnetic field difference would produce a \(5\sigma\) signal difference, and instead show consistency with background estimates incorporating non-flow contributions from the HIJING model (Feng 2021). The BES-II data and event shape engineering techniques (Milton 2021) open new opportunities for CME searches at lower collision energies, where the phenomenon is predicted to have strong energy dependence (Skokov 2009).
