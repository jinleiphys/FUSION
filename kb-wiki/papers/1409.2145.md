# Quasielastic hyperon production in $\barν_μ-$Nucleus interactions

> We have studied quasielastic charged current hyperon production induced by $\barν_μ$ on free nucleon and the nucleons bound inside the nucleus. The calculations are performed for several nuclear targets like $^{12}C$, $^{40}Ar$, $^{56}Fe$ and $^{208}Pb$ which are presently being used in various oscillation experiments using accelerator neutrinos. The inputs are the hyperon-nucleon transition form factors determined from neutrino-nucleon scattering as well as from semileptonic decays of neutron and hyperons using SU(3) symmetry. The calculations for the nuclear targets are done in local density approximation. The nuclear medium effects(NME) due to Fermi motion and final state interaction(FSI) effect due to hyperon-nucleon scattering have been taken into account.

## Key claim

The authors calculate quasielastic charged-current hyperon production in antineutrino-nucleus interactions using SU(3) symmetry to determine transition form factors, but note that SU(3) symmetry breaking effects and possible non-zero second-class currents (g2) remain to be investigated, and that the results for Σ⁰ and Σ⁻ production are sensitive to the neutron electromagnetic form factor parameterization.

## Method

The authors compute total and differential cross sections for the reactions ν̄μ + p → μ⁺ + Λ, ν̄μ + p → μ⁺ + Σ⁰, and ν̄μ + n → μ⁺ + Σ⁻ on free nucleons and on nucleons bound in ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ar, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb targets. Vector form factors are determined from nucleon electromagnetic form factors using SU(3) symmetry, while axial vector form factors use F(0)=0.463 and D(0)=0.804 with a dipole mass MA=1.03 GeV. Nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, Pauli blocking) are treated in a local Fermi gas model, and final state interactions from hyperon-nucleon scattering are included using a Monte Carlo cascade approach with cross sections from earlier parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- For ν̄μ + p → μ⁺ + Λ on free proton at Eν̄μ=2 GeV, the cross section is approximately 0.5 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (from Fig. 1, approximate value)
- For ν̄μ + p → μ⁺ + Σ⁰ at Eν̄μ=2 GeV, using ξ=1 in the neutron form factor yields a cross section of 0.65 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm², compared to 0.5 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² with the standard parameterization
- The axial vector dipole mass used is MA = 1.03 GeV
- The vector dipole mass used is MV = 0.84 GeV
- The Cabibbo angle suppression factor is tan²θC for |ΔS|=1 processes relative to |ΔS|=0
- The ratio σ(ν̄μ p → μ⁺ Σ⁰) = ½ σ(ν̄μ n → μ⁺ Σ⁻) holds for free nucleons
- The form factor parameters used are F(0)=0.463 and D(0)=0.804, giving gA(0)=1.267

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of hyperon production in antineutrino-nucleus interactions by Singh (2006) and Kuzmin and Naumov (2008) to include nuclear medium effects and final state interactions for multiple nuclear targets relevant to current experiments. The calculations are motivated by planned measurements at MINERνA, MicroBooNE, ArgoNeuT, MiniBooNE, and T2K experiments, and provide predictions for total cross sections and Q² distributions that can be compared with older bubble chamber data from Gargamelle (Eichten 1972, Erriquez 1978) and SKAT (Brunner 1989). The results enable experimental searches for flavor-changing neutral currents and studies of SU(3) symmetry breaking in the strangeness sector.
