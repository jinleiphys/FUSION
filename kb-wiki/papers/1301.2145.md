# Recent studies of kaonic atoms and nuclear clusters

> Recent studies of kaonic atoms, few-body kaonic quasibound states and kaonic nuclei are reviewed, with emphasis on implementing the subthreshold energy dependence of the Kbar-N interaction in chiral interaction models that are consistent with the SIDDHARTA K- hydrogen data. Remarks are made on the possible role of the p-wave Sigma(1385) resonance with respect to that of the s-wave Lambda(1405) resonance in searches for strangeness S=-1 dibaryons.

# Recent studies of kaonic atoms and nuclear clusters

## Key claim

The subthreshold energy dependence of the $\bar K N$ interaction, when implemented in chiral interaction models consistent with the SIDDHARTA $K^-$ hydrogen data, produces substantially lower binding energies and widths for kaonic quasibound states than earlier energy-independent calculations, making clear experimental identification of such states difficult.

## Method

The review surveys variational and Faddeev calculations of few-body $\bar K$ and $\bar K\bar K$ quasibound states using energy-dependent chiral interactions, where the subthreshold energy is determined self-consistently from the total binding energy and kinetic energy contributions. For kaonic atoms, the free-space Ikeda-Hyodo-Weise (IHW) NLO chiral $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes are converted to density-dependent in-medium amplitudes via a multiple scattering approach, and a phenomenological two-nucleon absorptive term is added to fit 65 kaonic atom data points across the periodic table.

## Key numbers

- $K^-pp$ binding energy: 16 MeV (Barnea et al. 2012 variational calculation with chiral energy-dependent interaction), compared to 48–95 MeV in non-chiral static calculations
- $K^-pp$ width: 41 MeV (Barnea et al. 2012), compared to 40–110 MeV in non-chiral calculations
- $\bar K\bar KNN$ binding energy: 32 MeV (Barnea et al. 2012), substantially lower than earlier estimates
- $K^-$ nuclear potential depth at center of Ni: −191 MeV (real) and −79 MeV (imaginary) for IHW-based fit with $\chi^2=118$
- Subthreshold energy downward shift at 50% of central nuclear density in Ni and Pb: approximately 40 MeV
- Fitted two-nucleon potential parameters: $b=(-0.34\pm 0.07)$ fm, $B=(1.94\pm 0.16)+i(0.83\pm 0.16)$ fm, $\alpha=1.2$
- $\Sigma(1385)N$ dibaryon candidate: predicted 10–20 MeV below the $\pi\Sigma N$ threshold in relativistic three-body Faddeev calculation

## Context

This review extends the line of work on kaonic atoms and nuclei that began with the SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen (2011) and the development of NLO chiral models by Ikeda, Hyodo, and Weise (2011). The self-consistent treatment of subthreshold energy dependence resolves discrepancies between earlier energy-independent calculations by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and more recent chiral approaches. The results enable experimental searches at GSI and J-PARC for the $K^-pp$ quasibound state and a proposed $I=3/2$, $J^\pi=2^+$ $\Sigma(1385)N$ dibaryon, which would decay via unique $\Sigma p$ channels distinct from those of $K^-pp$.
