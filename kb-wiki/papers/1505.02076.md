# Studying the entropy excess and entropy excess ratio in (105,106,107)Pd within BCS model

> Pairing correlations and their influence on nuclear properties has been studied within BCS model. Using this theoretical model with inclusion of pairing interaction between nucleons, nuclear level density and entropy of Pd(105,106,107) have been extracted. The results well coincide with the empirical values of the nuclear level densities obtained by Oslo group. Then the entropy excess of Pd107 and Pd105 compared to Pd106 as a function of the temperature has been studied. Also the role of neutron and proton system in entropy excess have been investigated by the using of the entropy excess ratio proposed by Razavi et al. [R. Razavi, A.N. Behkami, S. Mohammadi, and M. Gholami, Phys. Rev. C 86, 047303 (2012)].

## Key claim

The BCS model with pairing interactions reproduces the experimental nuclear level densities of 105,106,107Pd obtained by the Oslo group, and the entropy excess in odd-A isotopes (105Pd and 107Pd) relative to the even-A 106Pd can be interpreted as single-hole and single-particle entropy, respectively, with the single-particle entropy exceeding the single-hole entropy after the critical temperature of the pairing phase transition.

## Method

Single-particle energies and spins were calculated using the Nilsson potential with oscillator quantum number 41 A^{-1/3} MeV and standard Nilsson parameters mu and chi. These were used as input to BCS calculations for the neutron and proton systems of 105,106,107Pd, where the pairing gap at zero temperature was obtained by the three-point method to determine the pairing strength G. The grand canonical partition function was then solved to extract excitation energy, entropy, and nuclear level density via inverse Laplace transform, and the entropy excess and entropy excess ratio (Razavi et al., 2012) were computed as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear level density for 106Pd was calculated as a function of excitation energy and shown to agree with experimental data from the Oslo group (figure 1).
- Critical temperature for the pairing phase transition is T = 0.3 MeV, below which single-particle and single-hole entropy excess are equal, and above which single-particle entropy exceeds single-hole entropy.
- For 105Pd and 107Pd, the proton system's role in entropy excess increases at T = 0.3 MeV, compared to 121Sn where this occurs at T = 0.5 MeV (Razavi et al., 2012).
- The oscillator quantum number used was hbar*omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BCS-based entropy excess analysis previously applied to 121Sn by Razavi et al. (2012) to the palladium isotope chain 105,106,107Pd. The results confirm that the entropy excess ratio method can separate neutron and proton contributions to the entropy difference between odd-A and even-A nuclei, and show that the proton system plays a minor role in entropy excess for Pd isotopes (Z=46) compared to Sn (Z=50), where the magic proton number leads to stronger pairing correlations. The agreement with experimental level densities from the Oslo group validates the BCS approach for these nuclei and enables further study of pairing correlations in medium-mass isotopes.
