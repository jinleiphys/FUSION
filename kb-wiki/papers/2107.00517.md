# First observation of high-$K$ isomeric states in $^{249}$Md and $^{251}$Md

> Decay spectroscopy of the odd-proton nuclei $^{249}$Md and $^{251}$Md has been performed. High-$K$ isomeric states were identified for the first time in these two nuclei through the measurement of their electromagnetic decay. An isomeric state with a half-life of $2.8(5)$ ms and an excitation energy $\geq 910$ keV was found in $^{249}$Md. In $^{251}$Md, an isomeric state with a half-life of $1.4(3)$ s and an excitation energy $\geq 844$ keV was found. Similarly to the neighbouring $^{255}$Lr, these two isomeric states are interpreted as 3 quasi-particle high-$K$ states and compared to new theoretical calculations. Excited nuclear configurations were calculated within two scenarios: via blocking nuclear states located in proximity to the Fermi surface or/and using the quasiparticle Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer method. Relevant states were selected on the basis of the microscopic-macroscopic model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential. The most probable candidates for the configurations of $K$-isomeric states in Md nuclei are proposed.

## Key claim

High-K isomeric states have been identified for the first time in the odd-proton nuclei 249Md and 251Md through measurement of their electromagnetic decay, with the interpretation that these are 3-quasiparticle states; however, the excitation energies reported are lower limits (≥910 keV and ≥844 keV respectively) because the full decay path could not be established.

## Method

The nuclei were produced via fusion-evaporation reactions 203Tl(48Ca,2n)249Md and 205Tl(48Ca,2n)251Md at the University of Jyväskylä, using a 48Ca beam at approximately 219 MeV and 218 MeV respectively. Evaporation residues were separated from beam-like products using the RITU gas-filled separator and implanted into the GREAT spectrometer, where recoil-electron-alpha correlations were used to identify isomeric decays via the calorimetric technique, measuring conversion electrons, gamma rays, and alpha particles in coincidence.

## Key numbers

- 249Md isomeric state half-life: 2.8(5) ms (from recoil-electron-alpha correlations)
- 249Md isomeric state excitation energy: ≥910(15) keV (from sum-energy endpoint)
- 251Md isomeric state half-life: 1.4(3) s (from recoil-electron-alpha correlations)
- 251Md isomeric state excitation energy: ≥844(4) keV (from sum-energy endpoint)
- 249Md isomeric state population ratio relative to ground state: 12(2)% (lower limit)
- 251Md ground-state alpha-decay half-life: 4.28(12) min (consistent with prior values of 4.27(26) min and 4.0(5) min)
- Production cross sections: approximately 760 nb for 251Md (Chatillon 2007) and 300 nb for 249Md (Briselet 2019)

## Context

This work extends the known systematics of high-K isomeric states in the A ≈ 250 mass region, where such states have been observed in even-even nuclei like 250Fm (Greenlees 2008) and 252,254No (Sulignano 2007, Tandel 2006), and in the odd-Z nucleus 255Lr (Hauschild 2008, Jeppesen 2009). The new data provide experimental constraints on single-particle configurations and pairing correlations in transfermium nuclei, enabling tests of theoretical calculations using the microscopic-macroscopic model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential and blocking or BCS methods, as performed by Jachimowicz, Kowal, and Skalski in this work.
