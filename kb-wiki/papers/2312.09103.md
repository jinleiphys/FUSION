# Exploring the freeze-out hypersurface of relativistic nuclear collisions with a rapidity-dependent thermal model

> Considering applications to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we develop a rapidity-dependent thermal model that includes thermal smearing effect and longitudinal boost. We calibrate the model with thermal yields obtained from a multistage hydrodynamic simulation. Through Bayesian analysis, we find that our model extracts freeze-out thermodynamics with better precision than rapidity-independent models. The potential of such a model to constrain longitudinal flow from data is also demonstrated.

# Exploring the freeze-out hypersurface of relativistic nuclear collisions with a rapidity-dependent thermal model

## Key claim
The rapidity-dependent thermal model, which includes thermal smearing and longitudinal boost, extracts freeze-out thermodynamics with better precision than rapidity-independent models, but this conclusion is demonstrated only in a closure test using thermal yields from a multistage hydrodynamic simulation rather than directly from experimental data.

## Method
The authors develop a thermal model where freeze-out cells at a given space-time rapidity non-locally contribute hadron yields across the full rapidity range via a thermal smearing kernel, with longitudinal flow parametrized as tau u^eta = alpha eta_s^3. They parametrize the freeze-out thermodynamics as quadratic functions of space-time rapidity for temperature T(eta_s), volume V(eta_s), and fugacity z(eta_s), then perform a Bayesian analysis using Monte Carlo Markov Chain sampling to constrain the eight-parameter set against thermal (Cooper-Frye) yields of pions, kaons, and protons from a multistage hydrodynamic simulation for 0-5% Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- Prior range for mid-rapidity temperature T_0: [0.12, 0.17] GeV
- Prior range for mid-rapidity volume V_0: [600, 6000] fm^3
- Prior range for longitudinal flow strength alpha: [-0.1, 0.1]
- Prior range for maximal space-time rapidity eta_max: [1, 4]
- Bayesian analysis performed at beam energies sqrt(s) = 7.7, 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV; results presented for sqrt(s) = 19.6 GeV
- Estimated error for thermal yields set at 5% of the Cooper-Frye yield at each rapidity point
- Sampled rapidity points y_i in [0, 4], dense for y less than or approximately 1 and sparse at large rapidity

## Context
This work extends rapidity-independent thermal models (Braun-Munzinger 2003, Andronic 2008, Becattini 2003) by incorporating thermal smearing and longitudinal flow, which become important at lower collision energies where boost invariance is violated (Bjorken 1982). The model is tested against a multistage hydrodynamic simulation (Du 2023) as a closure test, and the authors demonstrate that the approach can constrain longitudinal flow from data. Future work will incorporate feed-down effects and apply the model directly to experimental measurements.
