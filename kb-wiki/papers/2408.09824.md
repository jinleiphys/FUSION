# Transport coefficients of the heavy quark in the domain of the non-perturbative and non-eikonal gluon radiation

> Drag and diffusion coefficients of the Heavy Quarks (HQs), such as charm and bottom, are one of the prime tools for discerning the properties of the deconfined QCD medium created in the Heavy Ion Collisions experiments. The innate non-perturbative nature of the QCD medium renders it imperative to estimate the transport coefficients in that domain. The present work evaluates the drag and diffusion coefficients of the moving HQ interacting with the medium particles via two-body collisional and three-body radiative processes to the first order in opacity by employing Gribov mechanism for the non-perturbative regime. We proffer the latest results of the HQ transport coefficients computed for the non-perturbative and non-eikonal gluon radiation off the HQ. The calculations show significant increment of the transport coefficients with the increasing non-eikonality by juxtaposing the results with those of the pertubative and eikonal regions. We hope to shed fresh light towards explaining the experimental data on the nuclear modification facor, $R_{AA}$, the elliptic flow, $v_2$ of the HQ by advocating the importance of the non-eikonality of the gluon radiation off the HQ.

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks are significantly enhanced when both non-perturbative effects (via the Gribov mechanism) and non-eikonal corrections to gluon radiation (measured by the parameter ζ = q⊥/√s) are included, compared to the perturbative and eikonal limits; the authors argue this may help explain experimental data on RAA and v2 at low to intermediate transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors compute drag (η), longitudinal diffusion (κL), and transverse diffusion (κT) coefficients for charm quarks interacting with a thermal QGP medium via two-body collisional processes and three-body radiative gluon emission to first order in opacity. They employ the Gribov-Zwanziger non-perturbative gluon propagator (with the Gribov parameter γG(T) fitted from lattice equation of state data) and relax the eikonal approximation by allowing E > q⊥ rather than E ≫ q⊥, parameterizing non-eikonality by ζ = q⊥/√s. The radiative matrix elements are derived in the soft gluon emission limit and the transport coefficients are obtained from the Fokker-Planck equation formalism.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to 1.3 GeV and momentum 5 GeV for temperature-dependent drag coefficient calculations.
- Drag coefficient η evaluated at temperature 500 MeV for momentum-dependent plots.
- The Gribov parameter γG/T is taken as a temperature-dependent function fitted from lattice QCD equation of state data (Jaiswal 2020, Borsanyi 2012).
- Non-eikonality parameter ζ = q⊥/√s, with ζ = 0 corresponding to the eikonal limit.
- The minimum k⊥ from the LPM condition is k⊥^min = Γ cosh η, and the maximum k⊥ from energy conservation is k⊥^max = E1 / cosh η.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-perturbative heavy quark transport coefficients by Sumit (2024), which used the Gribov mechanism in the eikonal limit of gluon radiation, by relaxing the eikonal approximation. It builds on prior T-matrix approaches (Greco 2008, Fries 2012, Liu 2019) and the Cornell-type potential approach (Cao 2023) for non-perturbative HQ dynamics, as well as earlier perturbative calculations of non-eikonal gluon radiation (Bhattacharyya 2016). The results enable more realistic inputs for Langevin or Fokker-Planck equations used to compute RAA and v2 of heavy quarks, potentially improving agreement with experimental data at low to intermediate pT where perturbative QCD fails.
