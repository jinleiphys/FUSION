# Sterile neutrino searches with reactor antineutrinos using coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments

> We present an analysis on the sensitivity to the active-sterile neutrino mixing with Germanium (Ge) and Silicon (Si) detectors in the context of the proposed coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus experiment in India. The study has been carried out with 3 (active) $+$ 1 (sterile) neutrino oscillation model. It is observed that the measurements that can be carried out with the Ge detector exhibit better sensitivity to the active-sterile neutrino mixing as compared to the Si detector. Both detectors are able to exclude most of the anomaly regions observed by the GALLIUM experiment. The Ge detector with mass 10 kg, can observe the active-sterile neutrino oscillation at 95$\%$ confidence level, provided that $\sin^{2}2θ_{14}\geq 0.09$ at $Δm^{2}_{41}$ = 1.0 eV$^{2}$ for an exposure of 1-yr. At higher values of $Δm^{2}_{41}$, a better sensitivity is obtained at a short baseline. It is also found that the threshold as well as resolution of the detectors play a crucial role on the measurements of active-sterile neutrino mixing parameters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a 10 kg Germanium detector placed 4 m from a compact reactor core can observe active-sterile neutrino oscillations at 95% confidence level for mixing parameters sin^2 2θ14 ≥ 0.09 at Δm^2_41 = 1.0 eV^2 after one year of exposure, but this sensitivity is conditional on achieving sufficiently low detector thresholds and good energy resolution.

## Method

The authors simulated coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) event rates from reactor antineutrinos using a 3+1 sterile neutrino oscillation model, considering Germanium and Silicon detectors with dual ionization and phonon readout. They modeled four reactor types (U-Apsara, DHRUVA, PFBR, VVER) with different thermal powers and fuel compositions, placed the detectors at baselines between 4 m and 30 m, and incorporated detector response including Gaussian energy resolution (σ/T ~ 10%/√T), 80% detection efficiency, 90% fiducial volume, and 70% reactor duty cycle. Sensitivity to the sterile neutrino mixing parameters sin^2 2θ14 and Δm^2_41 was extracted using a χ^2 method with pull variables for systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Ge detector with 10 kg mass can observe active-sterile oscillation at 95% C.L. for sin^2 2θ14 ≥ 0.09 at Δm^2_41 = 1.0 eV^2 with 1-year exposure
- Expected events at 4 m from U-Apsara reactor with 20 eV threshold: 2017 events for Ge detector, 1443 events for Si detector (10 kg mass, 1 year)
- Expected events at 4 m from U-Apsara reactor with 50 eV threshold: 1501 events for Ge detector, 1256 events for Si detector
- Expected events at 4 m from U-Apsara reactor with 100 eV threshold: 982 events for Ge detector, 1038 events for Si detector
- Maximum recoil energy for 1 MeV neutrino: approximately 20 eV for Ge target, 50 eV for Si target
- Reactor thermal powers considered: U-Apsara 3.0 MW_th, DHRUVA 100.0 MW_th, PFBR 1250.0 MW_th, VVER 3000.0 MW_th
- Systematic uncertainties included: 3.0% normalization uncertainty from reactor flux, target atoms, and detector efficiency

## Context

This work extends the search for sterile neutrinos using the CEνNS channel, which offers flavor-blind detection unlike the inverse beta decay (IBD) process used by experiments such as DANSS (2018), STEREO (2018), PROSPECT (2018), and Neutrino-4 (2018). The study is motivated by the reactor antineutrino anomaly (RAA) reported by Mention (2011) and the best-fit sterile oscillation parameters from combined NEOS and DANSS analyses (Δm^2_41 ≈ 1.30 eV^2, sin^2 2θ14 ≈ 0.049). The proposed experiment in India aims to complement existing CEνNS efforts like CONUS, CONNIE, and MINER, and could enable future searches for non-standard interactions, neutrino magnetic moment, and weak mixing angle measurements.
