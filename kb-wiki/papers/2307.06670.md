# Fingerprints of the triaxial deformation from energies and $B(E2)$ transition probabilities of $γ$-bands in transitional and deformed nuclei

> The energies and $B(E2)$ transitions involving the states of the ground- and $γ$-bands in thirty transitional and deformed nuclei are calculated using the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. Systematic good agreement with the existing data substantiates the reliability of the model predictions. The Gamma-rotor version of the collective Bohr Hamiltonian is discussed in order to quantify the classification with respect to the triaxial shape degree of freedom. The pertaining criteria are applied to the TPSM results and the staggering of the energies of the $γ$-bands is analyzed in detail. An analog staggering of the intra-$γ$ $B(E2,I\rightarrow I-2)$ is introduced for the first time. The emergence of the staggering phenomena in the transitions is explained in the terms of interactions between the bands.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) systematically reproduces experimental energies and B(E2) transition probabilities for γ-bands in thirty transitional and deformed nuclei, and introduces a new staggering parameter for intra-γ B(E2) transitions as a signature of triaxiality; however, the classification into γ-rigid versus γ-soft depends on the specific potential parameters of the Gamma-rotor model and the quasiparticle admixtures in the TPSM basis.

## Method

The authors employed the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, diagonalizing a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian in a basis of angular-momentum-projected zero-, two-, and four-quasiparticle configurations generated from a fixed triaxial deformation. They compared the resulting energies and B(E2) transition probabilities for ground- and γ-bands across thirty nuclei with experimental data, and analyzed the staggering of both energies and intra-γ B(E2) values using the Gamma-rotor version of the collective Bohr Hamiltonian to quantify triaxial shape classification.

## Key numbers

- The staggering parameter S(I) is defined as [E(I)-E(I-1)]-[E(I-1)-E(I-2)] divided by E(2⁺₁), and is used to classify γ-rigidity versus γ-softness.
- For the Gamma-rotor model, the ratio E(2⁺₂)/E(2⁺₁) ranges from 26.8 (potential 200-0) to 2.11 (potential 0-200), and E(2⁺₂)/E(4⁺₁) ranges from 8.07 (200-0) to 0.81 (0-200).
- The modified staggering parameter S̄(6) ranges from -2.75 (γ-independent potential 0-0) to 3.87 (potential 0-200).
- For the soft prolate potential 50-0, the staggering parameter S(I) shows even-I-down pattern; for the triaxial potential 0-100, it shows odd-I-down pattern.
- The B(E2, 2⁺₂→2⁺₁) value relative to B(E2, 2⁺₁→0⁺₁) ranges from 0.056 (potential 200-0) to 1.428 (potential 0-0).
- The B(E2, 0⁺₂→2⁺₂) value ranges from 0.102 (potential 0-200) to 2.995 (potential 20-(-50)).

## Context

This work extends previous TPSM studies by Rouoof, Sheikh, and collaborators (2021, 2014) that focused primarily on energy staggering for twenty-three nuclei, now adding comprehensive B(E2) analysis for thirty nuclei including Os and Pt isotopes not previously considered. The study also builds on the Gamma-rotor model of Caprio (2011) and the algebraic collective model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Rowe and Wood (2010) to provide quantitative classification criteria for triaxiality. The results enable future systematic comparisons between microscopic TPSM calculations and phenomenological collective model predictions for γ-band properties across the nuclear chart.
