# Effects of Dynamical Quarks on the Stability of Heavy Quarkonia in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> To study the stability of heavy quarkonia in quark-gluon plasma, we use a color-singlet Q-Qbar potential determined previously to be a well-defined linear combination of the free energy F_1 and the internal energy U_1. Using the lattice gauge results of Kaczmarek et al., the dissociation temperatures of J/psi and chi_b in quenched QCD are found to be 1.62Tc and 1.18Tc respectively, in good agreement with spectral function analyses. The dissociation temperature of J/psi in full QCD with 2 flavors is 1.42Tc. Thus, the presence of dynamical quarks in full QCD lowers the dissociation temperature of J/psi, but J/psi remains bound up to 1.42Tc.

## Key claim

The proper color-singlet Q-Qbar potential for studying heavy quarkonium stability in quark-gluon plasma is a linear combination of the free energy F_1 and the internal energy U_1, with coefficients determined by the equation of state; this potential yields dissociation temperatures consistent with spectral function analyses in quenched QCD, and shows that dynamical quarks lower the J/psi dissociation temperature but J/psi remains bound up to 1.42 T_c.

## Method

The author uses a previously derived color-singlet Q-Qbar potential U_{Q\bar Q}^{(1)} that is a weighted average of the lattice-calculated free energy F_1 and internal energy U_1, with the weight a(T)=3p(T)/epsilon(T) obtained from the quark-gluon plasma equation of state. The potential is parametrized using lattice gauge results from Kaczmarek et al. for both quenched QCD and full QCD with 2 flavors, with a screened Coulomb form at short distances and a constant potential at large distances. Binding energies and dissociation temperatures are then computed for various heavy quarkonium states using quark masses m_c=1.4 GeV and m_b=4.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature of J/psi in quenched QCD: 1.62 T_c (using U_{Q\bar Q}^{(1)} potential)
- Dissociation temperature of chi_b in quenched QCD: 1.18 T_c (using U_{Q\bar Q}^{(1)} potential)
- Dissociation temperature of J/psi in full QCD with 2 flavors: 1.42 T_c (using U_{Q\bar Q}^{(1)} potential)
- Spectral function analyses in quenched QCD give J/psi dissociation temperature range: 1.62-1.70 T_c (Asakawa et al., 2003)
- Spectral function analyses in quenched QCD give chi_b dissociation temperature range: 1.15-1.54 T_c (Petreczky, 2005)
- For comparison, using the F_1 potential alone gives J/psi dissociation temperature 1.40 T_c in quenched QCD and 1.21 T_c in full QCD; using the U_1 potential alone gives 2.60 T_c in quenched QCD and 2.22 T_c in full QCD

## Context

This work extends the potential-model approach to heavy quarkonium dissociation in quark-gluon plasma, building on the original suggestion by Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/psi suppression could diagnose plasma formation. Previous potential-model studies used either the free energy F_1 (Digal et al., 2001; Wong, 2001; Blaschke et al., 2005) or the internal energy U_1 (Kaczmarek et al., 2002; Alberico et al., 2005) as the Q-Qbar potential, but the author shows that neither alone matches spectral function analyses. The results enable reliable predictions for full QCD with dynamical quarks, showing that while dynamical quarks provide additional screening, J/psi remains bound to temperatures well above T_c, supporting its use as a quark-gluon plasma probe.
