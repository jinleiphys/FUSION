# High momentum components in the nuclear symmetry energy

> The short-range and tensor correlations associated to realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions induce a population of high-momentum components in the many-body nuclear wave function. We study the impact of such high-momentum components on bulk observables associated to isospin asymmetric matter. The kinetic part of the symmetry energy is strongly reduced by correlations when compared to the non-interacting case. The origin of this behavior is elucidated using realistic interactions with different short-range and tensor structures.

# High momentum components in the nuclear symmetry energy

## Key claim

Short-range and tensor correlations from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions strongly reduce the kinetic component of the nuclear symmetry energy compared to the non-interacting Fermi gas, but this conclusion is drawn from self-consistent Green's functions calculations at T=5 MeV and ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ using only two-body forces, without three-body forces which would modify the quantitative values.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent ladder approximation calculations within the self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) approach for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature (T=5 MeV). They computed the spectral function and momentum distribution via the Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sum-rule for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) using four realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Argonne v18, CDBonn, Nijmegen 1, and N3LO), including partial waves up to J=4 for dispersive contributions and J=8 for Hartree-Fock contributions. The symmetry energy and its kinetic and potential components were extracted using the parabolic approximation and by direct difference between PNM and SNM energies.

## Key numbers

- For SNM at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and T=5 MeV: zero-momentum occupation n(0) ~ 0.87 for Av18 and CDBonn; for PNM: n(0) ~ 0.96
- Total kinetic energy per particle for SNM: 34.7 MeV (CDBonn) and 41.5 MeV (Av18); for PNM: 42.5 MeV (CDBonn) and 46.2 MeV (Av18); for the free Fermi gas: 23.7 MeV (SNM) and 36.2 MeV (PNM)
- Total symmetry energy at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³: 25.1 MeV (Av18), 27.4 MeV (Nijmegen 1), 28.8 MeV (CDBonn), 29.7 MeV (N3LO)
- Kinetic symmetry energy at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³: 4.9 MeV (Av18), 4.6 MeV (Nijmegen 1), 7.9 MeV (CDBonn), 7.2 MeV (N3LO), compared to the free Fermi gas value of 12 MeV
- Potential symmetry energy at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³: 20.2 MeV (Av18), 22.8 MeV (Nijmegen 1), 20.9 MeV (CDBonn), 22.4 MeV (N3LO)
- Slope parameter L at ρ=0.16 fm⁻³: 37.7 MeV (Av18), 48.5 MeV (Nijmegen 1), 52.6 MeV (CDBonn), 55.2 MeV (N3LO)
- For SNM with Av18: 5.1% of strength in region k > 2k_F; for PNM: 1.8% in same region
- BHF symmetry energy with Av18 at same conditions: 28.4 MeV, about 3 MeV higher than the SCGF result

## Context

This work extends previous SCGF studies of isospin asymmetric matter by Frick (2005) and Rios (2009) to focus specifically on the kinetic symmetry energy and its relation to high-momentum components. The results confirm and quantify the reduction of kinetic symmetry energy found in simplified phenomenological models by Xu (2011) and in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana (2011), as well as in Fermi-Hypernetted-Chain calculations by Lovato (2011). The work enables improved microscopic constraints on the symmetry energy and its density dependence (the L parameter) for neutron star physics, though the authors note that three-body forces, which would increase the symmetry energy by 3-4 MeV at this density based on BHF calculations by Vidana (2009), are needed for quantitative predictions.
