# Exploring two-neutron halo formation in the ground-state of $^{29}$F within a three-body model

> Background$\colon$ The $^{29}$F system is located at the lower-N boundary of the "island of inversion" and is an exotic, weakly bound system. Little is known about this system beyond its two-neutron separation energy ($S_{2n}$) with large uncertainties. A similar situation is found for the low-lying spectrum of its unbound binary subsystem $^{28}$F. Purpose$\colon$ To investigate the configuration mixing, matter radius and neutron-neutron correlations in the ground state of $^{29}$F within a three-body model, exploring the possibility of $^{29}$F to be a two-neutron halo nucleus. Method$\colon$ The $^{29}$F ground-state wave function is built within the hyperspherical formalism by using an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator basis. The Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) nn interaction with central, spin-orbit and tensor terms is employed in the present calculations, together with different core$+n$ potentials constrained by the available experimental information on $^{28}$F. Results$\colon$ The $^{29}$F ground-state configuration mixing and its matter radius are computed for different choices of the $^{28}$F structure and $S_{2n}$ value. The admixture of d-waves with pf components are found to play an important role, favoring the dominance of dineutron configurations in the wave function. Our computed radii show a mild sensitivity to the $^{27}$F$+n$ potential and $S_{2n}$ values. The relative increase of the matter radius with respect to the $^{27}$F core lies in the range 0.1-0.4 fm depending upon these choices. Conclusions$\colon$ Our three-body results for $^{29}$F indicate the presence of a moderate halo structure in its ground state, which is enhanced by larger intruder components. This finding calls for an experimental confirmation.

# Exploring two-neutron halo formation in the ground-state of $^{29}$F within a three-body model

## Key claim
The three-body calculations indicate a **moderate** halo structure in the ground state of $^{29}$F, with the relative increase of the matter radius with respect to the $^{27}$F core lying in the range 0.1-0.4 fm depending on the choice of core+neutron potential and two-neutron separation energy; this halo is enhanced by larger intruder $pf$-shell components, but the conclusion is conditional on the large uncertainties in the experimental $S_{2n}$ value and the unbound $^{28}$F spectrum.

## Method
The ground-state wave function of $^{29}$F was constructed within the hyperspherical formalism using an analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, treating the system as a $^{27}$F core plus two valence neutrons (a Borromean three-body system). The Gogny-Pires-Tourreil (GPT) neutron-neutron interaction with central, spin-orbit and tensor terms was employed, together with four different $^{27}$F+$n$ potentials (Sets A-D) constrained by the limited experimental information on the low-lying resonances of the unbound $^{28}$F subsystem. A phenomenological three-body force was adjusted to reproduce different values of the two-neutron separation energy ($S_{2n}$), and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized with hypermomentum $K_{max}=30$ and $N=20$ hyperradial excitations.

## Key numbers
- The experimental two-neutron separation energy of $^{29}$F is $S_{2n} = 1.443(436)$ MeV (Gaudefroy et al.) and the evaluated value is $1.440(650)$ MeV (Wang et al.).
- The relative increase of the matter radius of $^{29}$F with respect to the $^{27}$F core lies in the range **0.1-0.4 fm** across all potential sets and $S_{2n}$ values considered.
- For the central $S_{2n}=1.440$ MeV, the matter radius $R_m$ ranges from 3.323 fm (Set A) to 3.459 fm (Set D), compared to the adopted core radius $R_c=3.218$ fm.
- The $(p_{3/2})^2$ content in the ground state varies from 6.8% (Set A) to 85.4% (Set D) at $S_{2n}=1.440$ MeV.
- For the shallowest case ($S_{2n}=0.400$ MeV), the matter radius reaches 3.598 fm (Set D) with $\Delta R = 0.380$ fm.
- The low-lying resonance in $^{28}$F is reported at $220\pm50$ keV with a second resonance at 810 keV (Christian et al.).

## Context
This work presents the first three-body calculations for $^{29}$F, extending the hyperspherical formalism previously applied to well-known two-neutron halo nuclei such as $^{6}$He, $^{11}$Li (Tanihata, 2013), and the heaviest known two-neutron halo $^{22}$C (Horiuchi, 2006; Togano, 2016). The study is motivated by the location of $^{29}$F at the lower-$N$ boundary of the "island of inversion" (Warburton, 1991), where intruder $pf$-shell configurations mix with normal $sd$-shell components, as seen in shell-model calculations by Utsuno (2004) and Brown (2006). The results call for experimental confirmation via interaction cross-section measurements and more precise mass measurements to constrain the theoretical models, and they highlight the need for better understanding of the low-lying continuum spectrum of $^{28}$F, where only two resonances have been reported by Christian et al. with unclear spin-parity assignments.
