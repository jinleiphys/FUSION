# Bulk viscosity in quasi particle models

> We discuss transport properties of dynamical fluid composed of quasi-particles whose masses depend on temperature and charge chemical potentials. Based on the relativistic kinetic theory formulated under the relaxation time approximation, we derive a general expression for the bulk viscosity in the quasi-particle medium. We show that dynamically generated particle masses imply an essential modification of the fluid compressibility. As an application of our results we consider a class of quasi-particle models with the chiral phase transition belonging to O(4) and Z(2) universality class. Based on the Ginzburg-Landau and the scaling theory we study the critical properties of the bulk viscosity zeta near the phase transition. We show that under the relaxation time approximation the zeta is not expected to show singular behavior near the O(4) and Z(2) critical point through static critical exponents.

# Bulk viscosity in quasi particle models

## Key claim

Under the relaxation time approximation, the bulk viscosity ζ in quasi-particle models with temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent masses is not expected to show singular or divergent behavior near O(4) or Z(2) critical points through static critical exponents, because singularities from susceptibilities cancel in the expression for ζ.

## Method

The authors derive a general expression for bulk viscosity in a quasi-particle medium using relativistic kinetic theory under the relaxation time approximation, where particle masses depend on temperature T and chemical potential μ. They apply this result to effective chiral models with phase transitions belonging to the O(4) and Z(2) universality classes, analyzing the critical scaling of ζ using Ginzburg-Landau mean-field theory and scaling theory based on the singular part of the free energy.

## Key numbers

- For the O(4) universality class, the singular part of the bulk viscosity scales as ζ^(der) ∼ t^(α + 4β − 1), where α ≃ −0.24 and β ≃ 0.38, yielding a vanishing contribution at the critical point
- For the Z(2) universality class (3D Ising model), the singular part scales as ζ^(der) ∼ h^(γ/βδ + 4/δ − 1), with β ≃ 0.31, δ ≃ 5.2, and γ ≃ 1.25, giving ζ ∼ h^0.54, a non-divergent behavior
- Near the tricritical point (TCP) in the Ginzburg-Landau model, the singular part of ζ vanishes due to the coefficient (∂M/∂T − (α/β) ∂M/∂μ) going to zero

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory results of Arnold (2000) and Hosoya and Kajantie (1985) to include dynamical quasi-particle masses with explicit T and μ dependence. The authors derive a bulk viscosity expression that reduces to these prior results when masses are constant or at vanishing chemical potential. The finding that ζ does not diverge through static critical exponents contradicts the expectation from Karsch (2007) that ζ ∼ C_V and would diverge at the critical end point. The authors note that including dynamical critical phenomena with a dynamical critical exponent z ≃ 3 (as in model H for the QCD critical point) could produce divergence of ζ, but this requires going beyond the relaxation time approximation to incorporate long-range fluctuations of soft modes.
