# Stochastic Semi-Classical Description of Fusion at Near-Barrier Energies

> Fusion reactions of heavy ions are investigated by employing a simple stochastic semi-classical model which includes the coupling between relative motion and low frequency collective surface modes of colliding ions similarly to the quantal coupled-channels description. The quantal effect enters into the calculation through the initial zero-point fluctuations of the surface vibrations. Good agreement with the result of coupled-channels calculations as well as data is obtained for the fusion cross sections of nickel isotopes. The internal excitations in non-fusing events as well as the fusion time are investigated.

## Key claim

The stochastic semi-classical model, which incorporates quantal zero-point fluctuations of surface vibrations via an ensemble of classical trajectories, reproduces quantal coupled-channels fusion cross sections for nickel isotopes at near-barrier energies, but only where barrier fluctuations dominate over quantum tunneling; the model overestimates data above the barrier due to the use of an energy-independent deep folding potential and the neglect of dissipation.

## Method

The authors treat the relative motion of two heavy ions classically while coupling it to low-lying quadrupole (2+) and octupole (3-) surface vibrations of each nucleus, described as harmonic oscillators. Quantal effects enter by sampling the initial deformation and momentum of each vibrational mode from the Wigner distribution of the harmonic-oscillator ground state, generating an ensemble of trajectories. The nuclear interaction is a double-folding potential with a zero-range nucleon-nucleon force, and the fusion cross section is computed by summing partial transmission probabilities over orbital angular momenta, averaging over all relative orientations of the vibration axes.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters: β₂ = 0.215 for ⁶⁴Ni, β₂ = 0.205 for ⁵⁸Ni.
- Octupole deformation parameters: β₃ = 0.263 for ⁶⁴Ni, β₃ = 0.235 for ⁵⁸Ni.
- Excitation energies: E₂* = 1.35 MeV, E₃* = 3.56 MeV for ⁶⁴Ni; E₂* = 1.45 MeV, E₃* = 4.48 MeV for ⁵⁸Ni.
- Zero-range potential strength: V₀ = −456 MeV·fm³; diffuseness parameter a = 0.56 fm.
- Bare s-wave barrier height for ⁶⁴Ni + ⁶⁴Ni: V_B = 96.5 MeV.
- Equivalent sharp radius formula: R₀ᵢ = 1.31 Aᵢ^{1/3} − 0.84 fm.
- Fusion events are defined as trajectories reaching a separation distance of R = 5 fm, starting from an initial relative distance of R = 20 fm.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic semi-classical approach originally proposed by Esbensen (1980s) and reviewed by Dasso, applying it to nickel-isotope fusion for the first time with full orientation averaging. It directly compares with the quantal coupled-channels (CC) calculations and the frozen approximation (FA) of Nobre (2006), using identical input parameters. The model enables the study of observables that are difficult to access in a fully quantal framework, such as the distribution of fusion times and the final kinetic energy of non-fusing events, and it can be extended to include dissipation mechanisms in future work.
