# Dense baryonic matter: constraints from recent neutron star observations

> Updated constraints from neutron star masses and radii impose stronger restrictions on the equation of state for baryonic matter at high densities and low temperatures. The existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars rules out many soft equations of state with prominent "exotic" compositions. The present work reviews the conditions required for the pressure as a function of baryon density in order to satisfy these new constraints. Several scenarios for sufficiently stiff equations of state are evaluated. The common starting point is a realistic description of both nuclear and neutron matter based on a chiral effective field theory approach to the nuclear many-body problem. Possible forms of hybrid matter featuring a quark core in the center of the star are discussed using a three-flavor Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. It is found that a conventional equation of state based on nuclear chiral dynamics meets the astrophysical constraints. Hybrid matter generally turns out to be too soft unless additional strongly repulsive correlations, e.g. through vector current interactions between quarks, are introduced. The extent to which strangeness can accumulate in the equation of state is also discussed.

## Key claim

A conventional equation of state based on nuclear chiral dynamics (in-medium ChEFT with nucleon and pion degrees of freedom plus three-body forces) can satisfy the two-solar-mass neutron star constraint, while hybrid matter with a quark core generally turns out to be too soft unless additional strongly repulsive correlations such as vector current interactions between quarks are introduced.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter, asymmetric nuclear matter, and pure neutron matter using three-loop in-medium chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) with explicit Delta(1232) isobar degrees of freedom, incorporating one- and two-pion exchange dynamics, three-body forces, and contact terms fitted to empirical nuclear matter properties. For the high-density extrapolation, they parametrized the equation of state using three sequential polytropes and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations, accepting only parameter sets that reach or exceed two solar masses and satisfy radius constraints from X-ray bursters and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries. Quark matter was explored using a three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with vector interactions.

- Neutron star J0348+0432 mass: M = (2.01 ± 0.04) solar masses

- Typical radii for 1.4-solar-mass neutron stars: R(1.4) = 12.1 ± 1.1 km (from Steiner-Lattimer-Brown analysis)
- Radius band for neutron stars between 0.8 and 2.0 solar masses: 10.9 to 12.7 km (Lattimer 2013)
- Radius constraints from Trumper analysis: 11.5 to 14.5 km
- Matching energy density between crust and ChEFT EoS: epsilon_0 ≈ 118 MeV/fm^3 at density ≈ 0.75 rho_0
- Polytrope matching points: epsilon_1 = 153 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_2 = 280 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_3 = 560 MeV/fm^3
- Nuclear symmetry energy: E_sym ≈ 33 MeV
- ChEFT cutoff: Lambda = 0.75 GeV (varied between 0.6 and 0.9 GeV)
- Neutron Fermi momentum at 3 rho_0: k_F^n ≈ 2.4 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Hebeler (2010, 2013) and Steiner (2010, 2012) by incorporating the updated two-solar-mass constraints from Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013) to define allowed bands for the equation of state. The ChEFT approach builds on the framework developed by Fritsch (2005), Fiorilla (2011), and Kaiser (2011), and is compared with quantum Monte Carlo computations reviewed by Gandolfi (2014) and the variational APR equation of state from Akmal (1998). The results enable more stringent tests of whether exotic compositions such as hyperons or quark matter can exist in neutron star cores, and provide a benchmark for equations of state used in supernova simulations that currently do not respect neutron matter constraints from many-body theory.
