# Effect of initial-state geometric configurations on the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition

> Within the framework of an extended quantum molecular dynamics model, we simulated $^{40}$Ca + $^{16}$O collisions at beam energies ranging from 60 to 150 MeV/nucleon for $^{16}$O with different $α$-cluster configurations. Results imply that different $α$-cluster configurations lead to different yields of deuteron, triton, $^3$He and $^4$He, but not for proton and neutron. We discuss the effect of geometric fluctuations which are presented by double ratios of light nuclei, namely $\mathcal{O}_\text{p-d-t}$ and $\mathcal{O}_\text{p-d-He}$. It is found that magnitude hierarchy of geometric fluctuations is chain, kite, square and tetrahedron structure of $^{16}$O. $\mathcal{O}_\text{p-d-t}$ has maximum value around 80 -- 100 MeV/nucleon which could be related to liquid-gas phase transition, that is consistent with results from the charge distribution of the heaviest fragments in the collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that different initial α-cluster geometric configurations of ¹⁶O in ⁴⁰Ca + ¹⁶O collisions produce a hierarchy of geometric fluctuations (chain > kite > square > tetrahedron) that can be probed via double ratios of light nuclei, and that the maximum of 𝒪ₚ₋d₋ₜ around 80–100 MeV/nucleon may be related to the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, but this connection is conditional, as the turning point depends on the specific cluster configuration and is cross-checked only by the bimodal charge distribution of the heaviest fragment.

## Method

Central ⁴⁰Ca + ¹⁶O collisions were simulated using the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model at beam energies from 60 to 150 MeV/nucleon, with ¹⁶O initialized in four different α-cluster configurations (chain, kite, square, tetrahedron). After dynamical evolution to 500 fm/c, heavy fragments with excitation energy >0 and mass >4 were de-excited using the GEMINI statistical decay code. For each configuration and energy point, 300,000 events were simulated, and double ratios 𝒪ₚ₋d₋ₜ = NₚNₜ/Nₔ² and 𝒪ₚ₋d₋ₕₑ = NₕₑNₚ²/Nₔ³ were constructed event-by-event to probe nucleonic density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 60 to 150 MeV/nucleon for ⁴⁰Ca + ¹⁶O collisions
- 𝒪ₚ₋d₋ₜ shows maximum value around 80–100 MeV/nucleon, with the peak position varying by cluster configuration
- For chain-like ¹⁶O, the bimodal structure in Z₁/Zₛ distribution appears when incident energy is greater than 80 MeV/nucleon and disappears above 100 MeV/nucleon
- The hierarchy of geometric fluctuations is chain > kite > square > tetrahedron for ¹⁶O α-cluster configurations
- Number of simulated events per configuration and energy point: 300,000
- Skyrme parameters: α = −124.3 MeV, β = 70.5 MeV, γ = 2; symmetry energy coefficient Cₛ = 25 MeV; Pauli potential strength cₚ = 15 MeV, f₀ = 1.0, μ = 1.3

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT) in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, building on the Bertsch-Siemens conjecture (Bertsch and Siemens, 1983) and prior observations of spinodal decomposition (Borderie, 2001) and negative microcanonical heat capacity (Gulminelli, 2002). It connects to recent work using density fluctuations to probe LGPT (Steinheimer, 2012; Steinheimer, 2013; Steinheimer, 2016) and to analytical coalescence frameworks for light nuclei (KJS, 2017; XGD, 2020). The results enable experimental tests at facilities such as HIRFL at CSR and FRIB at MSU, where yields of charged light nuclei and their ratios could be measured to infer geometric fluctuations and LGPT signatures.
