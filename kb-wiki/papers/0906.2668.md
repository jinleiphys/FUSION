# Schrödinger equations for the square root density of an eigenmixture and % the square root of an eigendensity spin matrix

> We generalize a "one eigenstate" theorem of Levy, Perdew and Sahni (LPS) to the case of densities coming from eigenmixture density operators. The generalization is of a special interest for the radial density functional theory (RDFT) for nuclei, a consequence of the rotational invariance of the nuclear Hamiltonian; when nuclear ground states (GSs) have a finite spin, the RDFT uses eigenmixture density operators to simplify predictions of GS energies into one-dimensional, radial calculations. We also study Schrödinger equations governing spin eigendensity matrices.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the Levy-Perdew-Sahni (LPS) theorem, which provides a Schrodinger equation for the square root of a single eigenstate density, to the case of densities derived from eigenmixture density operators (weighted sums over degenerate eigenstates), with the condition that potentials acting on the singled-out particle must be strictly local and spin-independent for the scalar density case.

## Method

The authors start with a Hamiltonian for A particles, split into a part for the first A-1 particles and a part for the A-th particle, and consider a degenerate eigenvalue with multiple orthonormal eigenfunctions. They define a density operator as a weighted sum over these eigenfunctions, take a partial trace over the first A-1 coordinates and all spins to obtain a density tau, and construct auxiliary wave functions in the (A-1)-particle space. By manipulating the eigenstate equation and using properties of the trace over the auxiliary space, they derive a Schrodinger equation for the square root of tau. They then extend the formalism to include spin-dependent interactions by working with spin-density matrices and column vector eigenstates, deriving a matrix Schrodinger equation for the square root of the spin density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalue in the generalized Schrodinger equation for the square root density is (E_A - E_{A-1}), the difference between the A-particle ground state energy and the (A-1)-particle ground state energy.
- The density tau is normalized so that the integral over all space of tau(r) dr equals 1.
- The weights w_n in the eigenmixture density operator sum to 1, with N >= 2 degenerate eigenstates considered.

## Context

This work extends the one-eigenstate theorem of Levy, Perdew and Sahni (1984) to eigenmixture density operators, which is of special interest for radial density functional theory (RDFT) for nuclei as developed by Giraud (2007). The RDFT exploits rotational invariance of the nuclear Hamiltonian to reduce calculations to one-dimensional radial problems, and this generalization allows treatment of nuclear ground states with finite spin, including doubly odd nuclei and odd nuclei with Kramers degeneracy. The results open the possibility of connecting the derived local effective potentials to optical potentials (specifically their real parts), though this remains an open problem.
