# Relativistic calculation of the triton binding energy and its implications

> First results for the triton binding energy obtained from the relativistic spectator or Gross equation are reported. The Dirac structure of the nucleons is taken into account. Numerical results are presented for a family of realistic OBE models with off-shell scalar couplings. It is shown that these off-shell couplings improve both the fits to the two-body data and the predictions for the binding energy.

## Key claim

The triton binding energy calculated from the relativistic spectator (Gross) equation can vary by several MeV depending on the off-shell scalar coupling parameter \(\nu\) in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and a value of \(\nu \simeq 1.5\) yields a binding energy in good agreement with the experimental value of \(-8.48\) MeV, but only if the two-body model also gives a good fit to the scattering data.

## Method

The authors solved the manifestly covariant three-body spectator (Gross) equation for three spin-1/2 particles, using two-body amplitudes obtained from exact solutions of the two-body spectator equation for a family of one-boson exchange (OBE) models. The models include exchange of six bosons (\(\pi, \eta, \sigma, \delta, \omega, \rho\)) and incorporate off-shell scalar-nucleon-nucleon couplings parameterized by \(\nu\), with \(\kappa_s=0\). The three-body equation was solved numerically in a partial-wave expansion including all channels with two-body angular momentum up to \(j_{max}=6\) (148 three-body channels).

## Key numbers

- Experimental triton binding energy: \(-8.48\) MeV.
- For \(\nu=0\), the calculated triton binding energy converges to \(-6.235\) MeV (with \(j_{max}=6\), 148 channels).
- For \(\nu=1.6\), the calculated triton binding energy converges to \(-8.757\) MeV (with \(j_{max}=6\), 148 channels).
- The best fits to two-body data occur for \(\nu\) in the range \(1.5 \le \nu \le 2.0\), with \(\chi^2 \le 2.45\).
- The critical spectator momentum \(q_{\text{crit}} \simeq 4m/3 \simeq 1200\) MeV, above which the two-body subsystem has spacelike four-momenta.
- Convergence of the binding energy is estimated to about 1 keV for \(\nu=0\) and about 5 keV for \(\nu=1.6\).

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic calculations of the triton binding energy (Tjon, Gibson, O'Connell 1970; Glockle et al. 1995) which left a discrepancy of 0.5-1.0 MeV with experiment when three-body forces were omitted. It goes beyond previous relativistic estimates using separable kernels in the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Rupp and Tjon 1992), minimal relativity in the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation (Sammarruca, Xu, Machleidt 1992), and first-order \((v/c)^2\) corrections to the Schrodinger equation (Carlson, Pandharipande, Schiavilla 1993; Forest et al. 1995), none of which treated the full Dirac structure of the nucleons. The results enable systematic study of relativistic dynamics in few-nucleon systems and suggest that off-shell scalar couplings, which are natural in an effective field theory framework, can simultaneously improve two-body fits and three-body binding predictions.
