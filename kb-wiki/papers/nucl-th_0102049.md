# The deuteron: structure and form factors

> A brief review of the history of the discovery of the deuteron in provided. The current status of both experiment and theory for the elastic electron scattering is then presented.

## Key claim

This review paper presents the current status of experimental and theoretical understanding of the deuteron's electromagnetic form factors, but acknowledges that ambiguities remain in the relativistic description of this two-nucleon system and that the simple two-nucleon picture is incomplete, requiring consideration of meson exchange and nucleon excitation into resonances.

## Method

The paper reviews the historical discovery of the deuteron and the development of nonrelativistic potential models, then presents the formalism for elastic electron-deuteron scattering in the one-photon exchange Born approximation, defining the charge monopole (G_C), magnetic dipole (G_M), and charge quadrupole (G_Q) form factors. The unpolarized structure functions A(Q^2) and B(Q^2) are expressed in terms of these form factors, and the tensor polarization observables t_20, t_21, t_22, t_10, and t_11 are derived for polarized targets or recoil polarization measurements.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.22456612 (48) MeV (Kessler 1999)
- Deuteron magnetic dipole moment: 0.8574382284 (94) mu_N (Mohr 2000)
- Deuteron electric quadrupole moment: 0.2859 (3) fm^2 (Ericson 1983, Codling 1971, Bishop 1979)
- Asymptotic D/S ratio eta_d = A_D/A_S: 0.0256 (4) (Rodning 1991)
- Deuteron charge radius r_ch: 2.130 (10) fm (Sick 1998)
- Deuteron matter radius r_m: 1.975 (3) fm (Huber 1998, Martorell 1995)
- Deuteron electric polarizability alpha_E: 0.645 (54) fm^3 (Rodning 1982, Friar 1983)
- Asymptotic S-state normalization A_S: 0.8847 (8) fm^{-1/2} (Stoks 1994)
- Asymptotic ratio from phase-shift analysis: eta_d = 0.0254 (2) (Stoks 1994)
- D-state probability P_D for most modern potentials: between 5.6 and 5.8%; for CD-Bonn potential: 4.83%

## Context

This review extends the earlier reviews of Ericson and Rosa-Clot (1984, 1983) on deuteron static properties and their connection to NN potential models, and of Carlson and Schiavilla (1998) on modern NN potentials. The paper provides the theoretical framework for interpreting elastic electron-deuteron scattering experiments, enabling future measurements of the deuteron form factors at higher momentum transfers to probe short-range nuclear structure and test relativistic and meson-exchange corrections to the simple potential model.
