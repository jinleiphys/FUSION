# Possibility to study a two-proton halo in $^{17}$Ne

> The nuclide $^{17}$Ne is studied theoretically in a three-body $^{15}$O+$p$+$p$ model. We demonstrate that the experimental condition for existence of a proton halo in $^{17}$Ne can be reasonably quantified in terms of $s/d$ configuration mixing. We discuss experimental evidences for a proton halo in $^{17}$Ne. We define which kind of experimental data could elucidate this issue.

## Key claim

The existence of a two-proton halo in $^{17}$Ne can be quantified by the degree of $s/d$ configuration mixing in the ground state wave function, but currently available experimental data are insufficient to determine this mixing reliably; the question can only be resolved by specific exclusive measurements such as invariant mass spectroscopy of $^{16}$F after proton knockout.

## Method

The authors used a three-body $^{15}$O+$p$+$p$ model to generate wave functions for $^{17}$Ne, including a realistic GMZ wave function with approximately 50% $s/d$ mixing, plus artificially constructed "high s" and "low s" wave functions with $W(s^2)\sim 70\%$ and $W(s^2)\sim 7\%$ respectively. They calculated Coulomb displacement energies, $B(E2)$ transition probabilities, momentum distributions in the sudden removal approximation with and without final state interactions, and interaction and two-proton removal cross sections in the eikonal approximation of the Glauber model, comparing all results to experimental data from fragmentation and Coulomb excitation measurements.

## Key numbers

- Ground state $s^2$ configuration weight: 48.1% for GMZ wave function, 73.4% for "high s", 4.8% for "low s"
- Matter radius of $^{17}$Ne: 2.69 fm (GMZ), 2.73 fm ("high s"), 2.65 fm ("low s")
- Coulomb displacement energy for $^{17}$N–$^{17}$Ne isobaric pair: experimental value 7.430 MeV; calculated values 7.424 MeV (GMZ), 7.194 MeV ("high s"), 7.685 MeV ("low s")
- $B(E2,1/2 \rightarrow 3/2)$ for $^{17}$Ne: experimental $66^{+18}_{-25}$ e$^2$fm$^4$; calculated 59.4 e$^2$fm$^4$ (GMZ)
- $B(E2,1/2 \rightarrow 5/2)$ for $^{17}$Ne: experimental $124(18)$ e$^2$fm$^4$; calculated 94.7 e$^2$fm$^4$ (GMZ)
- Longitudinal momentum distribution FWHM for $^{15}$O core from $^{17}$Ne fragmentation: experimental $168(17)$ MeV/c; calculated 184 MeV/c for "low s" wave function
- Two-proton removal cross section on Be target at 66 MeV/amu: experimental $191(48)$ mb; calculated 109 mb (GMZ), 120 mb ("high s"), 82 mb ("low s")
- Proton removal cross section from $^{15}$O core: 53 mb (three-body model), contributing to total $2p$ removal cross section of 162 mb
- Matter radius of $^{15}$O core: 2.53 fm; charge radius of $^{15}$N: 2.615 fm

## Context

This work extends the three-body model studies of $^{17}$Ne by Grigorenko et al. (2003) and addresses the controversy between predictions of $s^2$ dominance by Timofeyuk (1996), Nakamura (1998), and Gupta (2002) versus $d^2$ dominance by Fortune (2001). The authors test their wave functions against experimental data from Chromik (1997, 2002) on Coulomb excitation and Kanungo (2003) and Jeppesen (2004) on fragmentation momentum distributions. They show that inclusive momentum distributions alone cannot resolve the halo question because core proton removal processes contribute significantly, and they propose that exclusive invariant mass measurements of $^{16}$F after proton knockout would provide conclusive information on the $s/d$ mixing and thus the halo properties of $^{17}$Ne.
