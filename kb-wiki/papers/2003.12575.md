# The Q.Q Interaction and Variation of Single Particle Energies

> In this work we make further studies of the quadrupole quadrupole interaction used in shell model calculations.Whereas in a previous work we adjusted the single particle energies so as to obtain the rotatonal spectrum of the Elliott model, we here vary the single particle energies and see the various spectral shapes that evolve.

## Key claim

By varying the single-particle energy splittings in shell-model calculations with a quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q) interaction, the authors show that the rotational J(J+1) spectrum of the Elliott model is only obtained for specific tuned single-particle energies, and that different spectral shapes, including near-degenerate doublets and inverted level orderings, emerge as the splittings are changed.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations using the Q.Q interaction for two protons and two neutrons in the 0p shell (for \(^{20}\)Ne) and in the 1s-0d shell, using the code NUSHELLX@MSU. They systematically varied the single-particle energy differences, \(\Delta = e(p_{1/2}) - e(p_{3/2})\) in the p shell, and the separation of the \(1s_{1/2}\) orbital from the degenerate \(0d\) orbitals in the sd shell, and computed the yrast energy levels for each case.

## Key numbers

- For the p shell with \(\Delta = 0\) (degenerate \(p_{3/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\)), the ratio \(E(4^+)/E(2^+) = 3.3333\), the rotational limit.
- For the p shell with \(\Delta = 100\), the ratio \(E(4^+)/E(2^+) = 2.5\), corresponding to the pure \(p_{3/2}\) single-j shell limit.
- For the sd shell with the rotational tuning (Kingan et al. 2020), the energies are \(E(2^+) = 0.8952\), \(E(4^+) = 2.9840\), \(E(6^+) = 6.2664\), \(E(8^+) = 10.7424\) (arbitrary units).
- For the sd shell with \(\Delta = 9\) (separating \(1s\) from \(0d\)), the \(2^+\) and \(4^+\) states become nearly degenerate at \(E(2^+) = 1.2420\) and \(E(4^+) = 1.2999\), and the \(6^+\) and \(8^+\) states are also nearly degenerate at \(E(6^+) = 3.2486\) and \(E(8^+) = 3.5202\).
- For the sd shell with \(\Delta = 100\), the \(8^+\) state lies below the \(6^+\) state (\(E(8^+) = 2.1115\) vs. \(E(6^+) = 2.9701\)), making the \(8^+\) state isomeric.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kingan et al. 2020) where single-particle energies were adjusted to reproduce the Elliott model rotational spectrum. It also connects to the work of Sharon et al. (2020), who described a similar transition from strong prolate deformation to a gamma-soft rotor (O(6) limit), where the ratio \(E(4^+)/E(2^+) = 2.5\) and the quadrupole moment of the \(2^+\) state vanishes. The results demonstrate how varying single-particle energies within a schematic Q.Q interaction can produce a rich variety of spectral patterns, including band termination and isomeric states, providing insight into more complex nuclear structure behaviors.
