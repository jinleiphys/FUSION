# Nuclear Physics from lattice QCD at strong coupling

> We study numerically the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD with one flavor of massless staggered quarks. We determine the complete phase diagram as a function of temperature and chemical potential, including a tricritical point. We clarify the nature of the low temperature dense phase, which is strongly bound nuclear matter. This strong binding is explained by the nuclear potential, which we measure. Finally, we determine, from this first-principle limiting case of QCD, the masses of atomic nuclei up to A=12 "carbon".

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD with one massless staggered quark flavor, nuclear binding arises from a steric (excluded-volume) effect in the surrounding pion cloud rather than from meson exchange, and that this crude model can reproduce qualitatively plausible nuclear potentials and binding energies for nuclei up to A=12.

## Method

The authors simulate the strong coupling limit (β=0) of lattice QCD with one massless staggered quark flavor on N_s^3 × N_τ lattices, integrating analytically over gauge links first to obtain a dimer-and-baryon-loop representation of the partition function. They use a worm algorithm adapted for SU(3) to preserve the triality constraint at each site, and employ a "snake" algorithm to measure free energy increments when adding baryons one by one. The nuclear potential V_NN(R) is measured by extending the worldline of a second static baryon at distance R from the first in Euclidean time.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass: a m_B = 2.88(1) from the Euclidean correlator
- Critical temperature at μ=0: a T_c = 1.319(2), 1.402(3), 1.417(3) for N_τ = 2, 4, 6 respectively
- Tricritical point location: (a μ_TCP, a T_TCP) = (0.33(3), 0.94(7)) on N_τ=4 lattices
- Critical baryon chemical potential at T=0: a μ_B^crit = 1.78(1) from the snake algorithm
- Depth of nuclear potential minimum: approximately 120 MeV at distance approximately 0.6 fm
- "Deuteron" binding energy for A=2: approximately 120 MeV
- Surface tension of nuclear matter: approximately 200 MeV/fm^2

## Context

This work extends analytic mean-field treatments of strong coupling lattice QCD pioneered by Kawamoto (1981) and others, and provides numerical verification of predictions from Nishida (2003) and Miura (2008) regarding the phase diagram and tricritical point. The authors compare their results to earlier Monte Carlo studies by Karsch (1988) and Boyd (1991), and address ergodicity concerns raised by Aloisio (1999). The approach enables first-principles calculation of nuclear masses and potentials in a simplified QCD model, and the authors suggest future improvements including introducing isospin with a second quark flavor and measuring O(β) corrections as done analytically by Faldt (1985) and Bilic and Karsch (1991).
