# Beyond Mean Field: Fluctuation Diagnostics and Fixed-Point Behavior

> We develop theoretical diagnostics for the breakdown of mean-field theory, demonstrate how spatial structure and finite interaction ranges enter the effective description, and show how these scales qualitatively modify the renormalization-group flow.

## Key claim

The paper develops practical diagnostics for the breakdown of mean-field theory that do not rely solely on universality arguments, showing that the Ginzburg-Landau criterion can quantitatively identify the fluctuation-dominated region in thermodynamic variables, and that finite interaction ranges naturally generate spatial structure in the order parameter and modify renormalization-group flows.

## Method

The author computes the Ginzburg-Landau ratio R_GL = ⟨(Δφ)²⟩/φ̄² in both configuration-space and momentum-space formulations, using a linear sigma model as a test case. A separable interaction kernel with exponential form factor u(r) = e^{-m₁r} is introduced into a Ginzburg-Landau functional to generate spatially varying classical field configurations analytically. One-loop momentum-shell renormalization-group flow equations are derived directly in d=3 dimensions for a φ⁴ model, first with local interactions and then with a Gaussian form factor u(p) = e^{-p²/m₁²} modifying the quartic vertex.

## Key numbers

- In the linear sigma model demonstration, the volume factor is set to V_φ T₀³ = 1, with T₀ as the unit of energy.
- For the Gaussian model with propagator G̃_c(k) = (1/β)/(k² + m²), the regulated momentum-space integral yields (1/(2π²β))(Λ - m tan⁻¹(Λ/m)).
- The Wilson-Fisher fixed point for the local φ⁴ model in d=3 is at (λ̄₂, λ̄₄) = (-1/7, (48π²/49)β̄), with stability matrix eigenvalues v_WFFP = (1/3)(1 ± √22).
- For the separable interaction case, the Wilson-Fisher fixed point shifts to λ̄₂ = -1/(1+6u_Λ²) and λ̄₄ = 48π²β̄(1/(1+6u_Λ²))², where u_Λ = e^{-Λ²/m₁²}.
- The correlation-length exponent ν varies from approximately 0.5 at u_Λ = 1 toward approximately 1 as u_Λ → 0; the 3D Ising value ν ≈ 0.63 is given for reference.
- In the truncated flow scheme, the positive eigenvalue diverges at u_Λ = 1/√6 ≈ 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the standard Ginzburg-Landau framework (Goldenfeld 1992, Cardy 1996) by providing quantitative fluctuation diagnostics applicable to crossover transitions, not just critical points. The analysis of spatial structure from finite-range interactions contrasts with homogeneous mean-field models commonly used in QCD phase diagram studies, and the RG analysis with separable form factors connects to nuclear physics separable interactions (Yamaguchi 1954) while showing how nonlocality can modify effective critical exponents. The framework enables future studies of interaction kernels motivated by QCD and many-body systems where finite-range effects and fluctuation windows are central.
