# Tribaryon configurations and the inevitable three nucleon repulsions at short distance

> We decompose the tribaryon configuration in terms of SU(3) flavor and spin state and analyse their color-spin-flavor wave function following Pauli principle. By comparing the color-color and color-spin interactions of compact tribaryon configuration against the lowest three nucleon threshold within a constituent quark model, we show that the three nucleon forces have to be repulsive at short distance for all possible quantum numbers and all values of the SU(3) symmetry breaking parameter. Our work identifies the origin of the repulsive nuclear three body forces including the hyperons at short distance that are called for from phenomenological considerations starting from nuclear matter to the maximum mass of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The authors show that compact tribaryon configurations (nine quarks in a symmetric spatial state) are always repulsive relative to the lowest three-baryon threshold, for all possible flavor and spin quantum numbers and for all values of the SU(3) symmetry breaking parameter, thereby identifying the microscopic origin of the short-distance repulsive three-nucleon force.

## Method

The authors decompose the color-spin-flavor wave function of a compact nine-quark (tribaryon) configuration using SU(3) flavor and SU(2) spin representations, enforcing antisymmetry via the Pauli principle. They then compute the expectation values of the color-color and color-spin interactions from a constituent quark model, comparing the tribaryon's interaction energy against the sum of the three lowest-lying baryons (the threshold) for each allowed quantum number. The analysis is performed first in the SU(3) flavor symmetric limit using a closed-form formula, and then extended to broken SU(3) by introducing a strange quark mass parameter \(\delta = 1 - m_u/m_s\) and diagonalizing the resulting interaction matrices.

## Key numbers

* The color-spin interaction constant extracted from the nucleon-Delta mass splitting is \(C_{CS}/m_u^2 = 18.125\) MeV, using \(\Delta K = 16\) and \(m_\Delta - m_p \sim 290\) MeV.
* In the SU(3) symmetric limit, the color-spin interaction \(K\) for the least repulsive tribaryon flavor \(\mathbf{\bar{10}}\) with spin \(S=3/2\) is 20, while the lowest three-baryon threshold has \(V = -24\), giving a repulsive difference of 44 units.
* For the strangeness \(-1\) case (one strange quark), the color-spin interaction for the \(\mathbf{\bar{10}}\) state with \(S=3/2\) is \(H_{SS} = 20 + \frac{20}{3}\delta\), and for the \(\mathbf{\bar{35}}\) state with \(S=1/2\) is \(H_{SS} = 40 - \frac{40}{3}\delta\); both are compared against a threshold value of \(-24\).
* For the strangeness \(-3\) case (three strange quarks, isospin zero), the binding energy \(E_B\) is plotted as a function of \(\delta\) (0 to 1) and is repulsive for all spin channels except \(S=9/2\), which does not contribute to octet baryon thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the quark-cluster model approach used for two-nucleon short-range repulsion (Oka 1980, Oka 1981) and later lattice QCD studies (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2012) to the three-nucleon case for the first time. It directly addresses the "Hyperon Puzzle" in neutron star physics, where repulsive three-body forces involving hyperons are required to reconcile observed neutron star masses of \(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) (Demorest 2010) and \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) (Antoniadis 2013) with the otherwise too-soft equation of state from two-body interactions alone (Bombaci 2016). The results provide a microscopic justification for the phenomenological repulsive three-body forces used in nuclear matter and neutron star calculations (Yamamoto 2014).
