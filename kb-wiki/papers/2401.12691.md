# Converging Many-Body Perturbation Theory for Ab Initio Nuclear Structure: II. Brillouin-Wigner Perturbation Series for Open-Shell Nuclei

> Brillouin-Wigner (BW) perturbation theory is developed for both ground and excited states of open-shell nuclei. We show that with optimal partitioning of the many-body Hamiltonian proposed earlier by the authors [Z. Li and N. Smirnova, arXiv:2306.13629], one can redefine the BW perturbation series for a given state of the effective Hamiltonian in a small P-space to be converging under the condition that the energy of this state is below the lowest eigenvalue of the Hamiltonian matrix block belonging to the complement of the P-space, characterized by the same good quantum numbers as the state under consideration. Specifically, the BW perturbative calculations for the lowest $J^π$ states are always converging due to the variational principle. This property does hold for both soft and hard internucleon interactions in the harmonic oscillator basis. To illustrate this method and check the convergence behavior, we present numerical studies of low-energy spectra of $^{5,6,7}$Li using the Daejeon16 and bare N3LO potentials.

## Key claim

The Brillouin-Wigner perturbation series for the effective Hamiltonian in a small P-space converges for a given state if that state's energy lies below the lowest eigenvalue of the Q-space Hamiltonian block with the same quantum numbers; for the lowest J^π states of open-shell nuclei, convergence is always guaranteed by the variational principle, regardless of whether the internucleon interaction is soft or hard and regardless of the harmonic oscillator basis choice.

## Method

The authors generalize their earlier optimal Hamiltonian partitioning (Li and Smirnova, arXiv:2306.13629) to open-shell nuclei, using the 0ħω model space as the P-space and a harmonic oscillator auxiliary potential. They expand the resolvent operator (E − QHQ)^{-1} as an operator-valued geometric series with an adjustable diagonal partitioning parameter ξ in the Q-space, and compute high-order terms iteratively via a K-box vertex function. The method is benchmarked against exact matrix inversion and no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for ⁵,⁶,⁷Li using the Daejeon16 and bare N³LO potentials at ħω = 18 MeV with Nmax = 2, 4, 6 truncations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Brillouin-Wigner formulation for closed-shell ground states (Li and Smirnova, arXiv:2306.13629) to open-shell nuclei, addressing the convergence problems identified in Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory by Roth (2010), Langhammer (2012), and Tichai (2016), who found that perturbative calculations typically diverge in the harmonic oscillator basis and only converge in the Hartree-Fock basis for softened potentials. The method enables systematic order-by-order convergence studies for low-lying states of open-shell nuclei using either soft or hard interactions, with the caveat that intruder states (Q-space dominated states in the energy region of P-space dominated states) may spoil convergence for higher excited states of each J^π.
