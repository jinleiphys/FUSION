# Time reversal invariance violation in neutron-nucleus scattering

> Planning and interpretation of the experiments searching for the time reversal (T) and parity (P) violation in neutron reactions require values of the matrix elements of the T,P-violating nuclear forces between nuclear compound states. We calculate the root mean square values and the ratio of the matrix elements of the T,P-violating and P-violating interactions using statistical theory based on the properties of chaotic compound states and present the results in terms of the fundamental parameters in four different forms: in terms of the constants of the contact nuclear interaction, meson exchange constants, QCD theta-term and quark chromo-EDMs. Using current limits on these parameters, we obtain the upper bounds on the ratio of the matrix elements.

## Key claim

The ratio of time-reversal and parity violating (T,P-odd) to parity violating (P-odd) matrix elements in neutron-nucleus scattering is calculated using a statistical theory of chaotic compound states, and the resulting upper bounds on this ratio are derived from current limits on fundamental CP-violating parameters, confirming that planned experiments with sensitivity below 10^{-6} may improve these limits.

## Method

The authors used a statistical theory based on Gaussian random expansion coefficients for chaotic compound nuclear states to compute the root-mean-square values of the T,P-violating matrix element w and the P-violating matrix element v between s-wave and p-wave compound resonances. They expressed the ratio w/v in terms of five different sets of fundamental parameters: contact nucleon interaction constants, meson exchange constants (specifically pion exchange), the QCD theta-term, quark chromo-electric dipole moments, and axion interaction constants. Upper bounds on w/v were then obtained by inserting current experimental limits on these fundamental parameters.

## Key numbers

* Updated P-violating matrix element v = 1.56 meV, derived from updated weak coupling constants g_p = 2.6 and g_n = 1.5 (Wasem 2012), in agreement with the experimental value 1.39^{+0.55}_{-0.38} meV (Bowman 1990, Frankle 1991).
* T,P-violating matrix element w = 0.15 meV sqrt(eta_n^2 + 0.76 eta_p^2), where eta_n and eta_p are the T,P-violating nucleon interaction constants.
* Ratio w/v = 0.10 sqrt(eta_n^2 + 0.76 eta_p^2), or w/v = 0.13 |eta_n| for |eta_p| = |eta_n|.
* Ratio w/v expressed via QCD theta-term: w/v = 5.7 x 10^4 |theta|, giving w/v < 10^{-5} using the neutron EDM limit |theta| < 10^{-10} (Yamanaka 2017).
* Ratio w/v expressed via quark chromo-EDMs: w/v < 2 x 10^{-5}, using limits |tilde{d}_u - tilde{d}_d| < 6 x 10^{-27} cm and |(1/2)tilde{d}_u + tilde{d}_d| < 3 x 10^{-26} cm (Swallows 2013, Graner 2016).
* Ratio w/v from axion exchange: w/v < 10^{-3} to 10^{-5}, using the limit |g^s g^p| < 1 x 10^{-9} from the proton EDM measurement |d_p| < 2 x 10^{-25} e cm (Graner 2016).
* Upper bound on the ratio of T,P-violating to P-violating cross sections: Delta sigma_{PT} / Delta sigma_P < 2 x 10^{-5}, assuming kappa = 1.
* Expected experimental sensitivity: Delta sigma_{PT} / Delta sigma_P < 10^{-6} (Gudkov 2014, Palos 2018).

## Context

This work extends the statistical theory of chaotic compound states developed by Flambaum and collaborators (Flambaum 1993, Flambaum 1994, Flambaum 1995a, Flambaum 1995b) to calculate the ratio of T,P-violating to P-violating matrix elements in neutron-nucleus scattering, updating earlier numerical calculations with improved values of the weak meson-nucleon coupling constants from lattice QCD (Wasem 2012) and the NPDGamma experiment (2018). The results provide the theoretical interpretation framework for ongoing and planned experiments searching for time-reversal violation in neutron reactions at facilities in Japan and the United States (Snow 2017, Okudaira 2018, Palos 2018), and confirm that these experiments may improve limits on T,P-violating interactions beyond those from electric dipole moment measurements.
