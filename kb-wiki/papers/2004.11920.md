# Effects of the mean field on fluid dynamics in the relaxation time approximation

> In this paper the nonequilibrium correction to the distribution function containing a time and space dependent mass is obtained. Given that, fully consistent fluid dynamic equations are formulated. Then, the physics of the bulk viscosity is elaborated for Boltzmann and Bose-Einstein gases within the relaxation time approximation. It is found that the parametric form of the ratio $ζ/τ_R$ for the quantum gas is affected by the infrared cut-off. This may be an indication that the relaxation time approximation is too crude to obtain a reliable form of bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully consistent formulation of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics that includes a temperature- and space-dependent quasiparticle mass, but finds that the resulting bulk viscosity ratio ζ/τ_R for a Bose-Einstein gas acquires an extra factor T/m_x that depends on the infrared cutoff, suggesting the relaxation time approximation may be too crude to yield a reliable form of bulk viscosity.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann equation for a single-species quasiparticle gas with a space- and time-dependent thermal mass, using the relaxation time approximation (Anderson-Witting model). They derive the nonequilibrium correction to the distribution function that includes both the usual hydrodynamic deviation and a new term arising from the thermal mass variation, then solve for the shear and bulk parts of the distribution function to extract the transport coefficients η/τ_R and ζ/τ_R for both Boltzmann and Bose-Einstein statistics.

## Key numbers

- For the Bose-Einstein gas, ζ/τ_R ≈ T^4 (1/3 - c_s^2)^2 [ (2π^3 T)/(25 m_x) - (4π^2/75)(1 - 9 m_eq^2/(8 m_x^2)) ], where m_x = sqrt(m_0^2 + m_eq^2(x)).
- For the Boltzmann gas, ζ_Boltz/τ_R ≈ T^4 (1/3 - c_s^2)^2 [ 60/π^2 - 36 m_x/(π T) ].
- The shear viscosity ratio is η/τ_R = (ε_0 + P_0)/5 for both statistics.
- The equilibrium thermal mass for the quantum gas is m_eq^2 = λ T^2/24, where λ is the coupling constant.

## Context

This work extends previous kinetic theory attempts to include mean-field effects in hydrodynamics, which the authors state were incomplete or missing the thermal mass correction term in the distribution function (citing Sasaki 2008, Chakraborty 2010, Bluhm 2010, Romatschke 2011, Albright 2015, Chakraborty 2016, Tinti 2016, Alqahtani 2017). The results enable a fully consistent fluid-dynamic description with temperature-dependent mass, but the unexpected infrared-sensitive term in the Bose-Einstein bulk viscosity raises questions about the validity of the relaxation time approximation for bulk transport, motivating future work with more realistic collision kernels.
