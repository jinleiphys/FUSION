# Nuclear matter equation of state including few-nucleon correlations $(A\leq 4)$

> Light clusters (mass number $A \leq 4$) in nuclear matter at subsaturation densities are described using a quantum statistical approach. In addition to self-energy and Pauli-blocking, effects of continuum correlations are taken into account to calculate the quasiparticle properties and abundances of light elements. Medium-modified quasiparticle properties are important ingredients to derive a nuclear matter equation of state applicable in the entire region of warm dense matter below saturation density. Moreover, the contribution of continuum states to the equation of state is considered. The effect of correlations within the nuclear medium on the quasiparticle energies is estimated. The properties of light clusters and continuum correlations in dense matter are of interest for nuclear structure calculations, heavy ion collisions, and for astrophysical applications such as the formation of neutron stars in core-collapse supernovae.

# Nuclear matter equation of state including few-nucleon correlations (A ≤ 4)

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum statistical approach to describe light clusters (A ≤ 4) in nuclear matter at subsaturation densities, claiming that medium-modified quasiparticle properties, including self-energy shifts, Pauli blocking, and continuum correlations, are essential to derive a nuclear matter equation of state applicable in the entire region of warm dense matter below saturation density, but the formalism has not yet been solved in a fully self-consistent way and the author proposes a simple parametrization to improve on the Fermi distribution of the ideal nucleon gas.

## Method

The author uses a quantum statistical approach based on thermodynamic Green functions to derive an in-medium Schrodinger equation for few-nucleon clusters (A = 1 to 4), incorporating single-nucleon quasiparticle energy shifts from a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) parametrization and Pauli blocking terms via occupation numbers. The cluster decomposition of the equation of state is performed by summing over bound state contributions and continuum scattering states, with the effective occupation numbers parametrized by a Fermi distribution using effective temperature and chemical potentials that reproduce the total nucleon densities and momentum moments. The bound state energies of deuteron, triton, helion, and alpha particle are calculated as functions of baryon density, proton fraction, and effective temperature using previously published variational results and fit formulae.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Temperature range considered: T ≤ 20 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy in free space: B_d = 2.225 MeV
- Alpha particle binding energy in free space: B_α = 28.3 MeV
- Alpha particle excited state excitation energy: 20.2 MeV
- Effective mass approximation: m*/m = 1 - 0.17 (n_B / n_sat)
- Baryon thermal wavelength: Λ = (2π ħ² / m T)^{1/2} with nucleon mass m = 939.17 MeV/c²

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach previously applied to two-nucleon correlations by Ropke and collaborators (SRS) to include all light clusters up to A = 4, building on earlier calculations of in-medium cluster binding energies (R, R2011). It contrasts with semi-empirical approaches such as the excluded volume model (Hempel) and the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) approximation, which neglect medium modifications and continuum correlations. The results are intended for applications in nuclear structure calculations, heavy ion collisions, and astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star formation, where the composition of warm dense matter affects neutrino emission and absorption.
