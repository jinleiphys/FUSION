# Triaxiality in 48Cr

> Rotational behavior inducing triaxiality is discussed for 48Cr in the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model, as well as in the spherical shell model. It is shown that the low-spin region up to about I=8, has a prolate well-deformed shape. At higher spins the shape is triaxial with a "negative-gamma" deformation, that is, with rotation around the classically forbidden intermediate axis. By comparing calculated B(E2)-values and spectroscopic quadrupole moments in the CNS with spherical shell model results and experimental data, the triaxial rotation around the intermediate axis is confirmed.

# Triaxiality in 48Cr

## Key claim
The paper claims that 48Cr exhibits triaxial shapes with rotation around the intermediate axis (negative-gamma deformation) at spins above I=8, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) model poorly describes the energy spectrum, and the triaxial interpretation is confirmed primarily through comparison with spherical shell model calculations rather than direct experimental verification.

## Method
The authors performed cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) calculations without pairing, allowing free minimization in the quadrupole degrees of freedom (epsilon and gamma) and one hexadecapole degree of freedom (epsilon_4), and compared these with full fp-shell model calculations using the KB3 interaction and restricted f7/2p3/2 subspace calculations using the modified surface-delta interaction (MSDI). They calculated B(E2) values and spectroscopic quadrupole moments using rotor-model expressions adapted for triaxial shapes, and estimated the effect of quantum fluctuations in the epsilon degree of freedom via a harmonic approximation of the potential-energy surfaces.

## Key numbers
- The ground state deformation is prolate with epsilon approximately 0.26, with four protons and four neutrons in the f7/2 shell occupying deformed orbits with Omega = +/-1/2 and +/-3/2.
- The 8+ state has epsilon approximately 0.20.
- The 12+ state has gamma approximately -15 degrees, corresponding to rotation around the intermediate axis.
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the 12+ state is calculated to be -28 e*fm^2 if the shape is restricted to axial symmetry, and -14 e*fm^2 if the triaxial shape is considered, close to the shell model value.
- A rotational reference of E_rot = 0.051*I(I+1) was subtracted from the energy calculations.

## Context
This work extends earlier discussions of triaxiality in nuclear structure (Ha90) and the prediction that particles in half-filled high-j shells (such as the f7/2 shell in 48Cr) tend to polarize the nucleus toward negative gamma values (An76). The study compares CNS calculations with full fp-shell model calculations by Poves (1998) and CHFB calculations by Poves (1998) using the Gogny force. The results enable future experimental tests of triaxial rotation around the intermediate axis and suggest that wobbling excitations might be possible but were not observed in the shell model calculations, possibly due to the limited number of valence particles in 48Cr.
