# Super Efimov effect for mass-imbalanced systems

> We study two species of particles in two dimensions interacting by isotropic short-range potentials with the interspecies potential fine-tuned to a p-wave resonance. Their universal low-energy physics can be extracted by analyzing a properly constructed low-energy effective field theory with the renormalization group method. Consequently, a three-body system consisting of two particles of one species and one of the other is shown to exhibit the super Efimov effect, the emergence of an infinite tower of three-body bound states with orbital angular momentum $l=\pm1$ whose binding energies obey a doubly exponential scaling, when the two particles are heavier than the other by a mass ratio greater than 4.03404 for identical bosons and 2.41421 for identical fermions. With increasing the mass ratio, the super Efimov spectrum becomes denser which would make its experimental observation easier. We also point out that the Born-Oppenheimer approximation is incapable of reproducing the super Efimov effect, the universal low-energy asymptotic scaling of the spectrum.

## Key claim

A three-body system in two dimensions, consisting of two particles of one species and one of the other with interspecies interactions tuned to a p-wave resonance, exhibits the super Efimov effect (an infinite tower of bound states with doubly exponential binding energy scaling) only when the two heavier particles exceed a critical mass ratio relative to the lighter particle: greater than 4.03404 for identical bosons and greater than 2.41421 for identical fermions.

## Method

The authors constructed a low-energy effective field theory for two species of particles in two dimensions with the interspecies potential fine-tuned to a p-wave resonance, including marginal two-body, three-body, and four-body couplings. They applied the renormalization group method to analyze the running of the three-body coupling \(u_1\) as a function of momentum scale, deriving the universal exponent \(\gamma\) from the renormalization group equation and identifying divergences in the flow that signal the existence of an infinite tower of bound states.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass ratio for the super Efimov effect with two identical bosons: \(m_1/m_2 = 4.03404\).
- Critical mass ratio for the super Efimov effect with two identical fermions: \(m_1/m_2 = 2.41421\).
- For a highly mass-imbalanced mixture of \(^6\)Li and \(^{133}\)Cs, the universal exponent \(\gamma \approx 10.7\), corresponding to a logarithmic energy ratio \(\ln E_{n+1}/\ln E_n \to e^{\pi/\gamma} \approx 1.34\).
- For three identical fermions, the universal scaling factor is \(e^{3\pi/4} \approx 10.55\).
- The super Efimov spectrum obeys \(E_n \propto \exp(-2 e^{\pi n/\gamma + \theta})\) for sufficiently large integer \(n\).

## Context

This work extends the super Efimov effect, previously discovered for three identical fermions in two dimensions at a p-wave resonance (Nishida, 2013), to mass-imbalanced systems. The authors show that increasing the mass ratio makes the super Efimov spectrum denser, which could facilitate experimental observation using ultracold atom mixtures such as \(^6\)Li and \(^{133}\)Cs, where p-wave Feshbach resonances have been observed (Repp, 2013). The paper also demonstrates that the Born-Oppenheimer approximation fails to reproduce the true low-energy asymptotic scaling of the super Efimov spectrum, even at large mass ratios.
