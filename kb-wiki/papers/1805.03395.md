# Role of hexadecapole deformation of projectile $^{28}$Si in heavy-ion fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier

> The vast knowledge of strong influence of quadrupole deformation $β_2$ of colliding nuclei on heavy-ion subbarrier fusion reactions inspires a desire to quest the sensitivity of fusion dynamics to higher order deformations, such as $β_4$ and $β_6$ deformations. However, such studies have rarely been carried out, especially for deformation of projectile nuclei. In this article, we investigated the role of $β_4$ of the projectile nucleus in fusion of the $^{28}$Si + $^{92}$Zr system. We demonstrated that the fusion barrier distribution is sensitive to the sign and the value of the $β_4$ parameter of the projectile, $^{28}$Si, and confirmed that the $^{28}$Si nucleus has a large positive $β_4$. This study opens an indirect way to estimate deformation parameters of radioactive nuclei using fusion reactions, which is otherwise difficult due to experimental constraints.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the fusion barrier distribution for the $^{28}$Si + $^{92}$Zr system is sensitive to the hexadecapole deformation parameter $\beta_4$ of the projectile $^{28}$Si, and that a large positive $\beta_4$ value causes the rotational coupling scheme to produce results nearly identical to a vibrational coupling scheme, confirming that $^{28}$Si is a deformed nucleus with a large positive hexadecapole deformation rather than a spherical vibrational nucleus.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed using the computer code CCFULL with a Woods-Saxon potential (diffuseness parameter $a_0$ = 1.03 fm) to compute fusion cross sections and barrier distributions for the $^{28}$Si + $^{92}$Zr system. The target $^{92}$Zr was treated with a one-quadrupole-phonon coupling at 0.934 MeV with deformation parameter 0.13, while the projectile $^{28}$Si was modeled either as a vibrational nucleus (harmonic oscillator) or as a rotational nucleus with various combinations of $\beta_2$ and $\beta_4$ deformation parameters, including inclusion of the ground-state rotational band up to the $4^+$ state.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{28}$Si used was $\beta_2 = -0.407$ (oblate), with hexadecapole values $\beta_4 = 0.0$, $+0.10$, $+0.25$, and $-0.25$ tested in rotational coupling calculations.
- The value $\beta_4 = +0.25$ for $^{28}$Si, obtained from the finite-range droplet model of Moller and Nix (1995), is consistent with proton scattering experiments giving $+0.25 \pm 0.08$.
- The ratio for perfect cancellation of the reorientation term is $\beta_4/\beta_2 = -\sqrt{5}/3 = -0.745$; with $\beta_2 = -0.407$ and $\beta_4 = +0.25$, the two terms in the reorientation matrix element are $-0.073R_P$ and $+0.060R_P$, respectively.
- The experimental fusion barrier distribution was extracted with a point-difference formula using $\Delta E \sim 2$ MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon diffuseness parameter was fixed at $a_0 = 1.03$ fm, following Newton et al. (2001).

## Context

This work extends the earlier conclusion of Kaur et al. (2016) for the $^{28}$Si + $^{154}$Sm system to the $^{28}$Si + $^{92}$Zr system, confirming that the apparent success of vibrational coupling calculations for $^{28}$Si is due to cancellation of the reorientation term by a large positive $\beta_4$, not because $^{28}$Si is spherical. The study reanalyzes the same system studied by Newton et al. (2001), who had reported that treating $^{28}$Si as a vibrational nucleus gave a better fit than treating it as an oblate rotor. The results suggest that fusion barrier distribution analysis offers an alternative method to extract the magnitude and sign of $\beta_4$ for deformed nuclei, including radioactive nuclei where beam intensity is low, and may be relevant to understanding projectile excitations in superheavy element synthesis.
