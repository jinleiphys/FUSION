# Modified Coherence and the Transverse Extent of Jets

> We present a study of the transverse size of parton showers and their diminishing interaction with the medium in the high virtuality stage of jet evolution. We consider the process of a hard quark produced in deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. Single gluon radiation from this quark, in the absence of scattering, is re-derived using wave-packets. This allows for a derivation of the quantum uncertainty size of the hard quark, at the point of splitting. This uncertainty size is then incorporated within a Monte-Carlo shower routine yielding transverse shower sizes noticeably larger than the classical antenna size of the shower. No clear relation is found between the full uncertainty size of the shower and the virtuality of the originating parton. The single gluon emission from the hard quark is then re-analysed for the case of single rescattering off the remainder of the nucleus. A relation is derived between the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and the gluon Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Function (gTMDPDF). Solving this relation, for a simple case, clearly demonstrates the weakening of $\hat{q}$ with the virtuality of the hard splitting parton.

## Key claim

The transverse size of a parton shower, when quantum uncertainty is included, is noticeably larger than the classical antenna size and shows no clear relation to the virtuality of the originating parton; the weakening of the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ with increasing parton virtuality is derived from a relation between $\hat{q}$ and the gluon Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Function (gTMDPDF), rather than from coherence arguments alone.

## Method

The authors re-derive single gluon radiation from a hard quark produced in deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus using wave-packets, extracting the quantum uncertainty size of the hard quark at the splitting point. This uncertainty size is incorporated into a Monte-Carlo shower routine (the MATTER generator) to compute transverse shower sizes. The single gluon emission is then re-analyzed for the case of single rescattering off the remainder of the nucleus, deriving a relation between $\hat{q}$ and the gTMDPDF, which is solved for a simple case to demonstrate the virtuality dependence of $\hat{q}$.

## Key numbers

- The quantum uncertainty size of a splitting parton is derived as $\delta z_\perp^2 \simeq 10/\mu^2$, where $\mu^2$ is the virtuality of the parton before the split; for a typical gluon momentum fraction $y \sim 1/10$, this gives $\delta z_\perp \approx 1/\ell_\perp$ (in natural units with $\hbar=1$).
- The medium saturation scale is given by $\mu_{\mathrm{med}}^2 = \sqrt{2 \hat{q} E}$, where $\hat{q}$ is the jet transport coefficient and $E$ is the parton energy.
- The formation time for a splitting is $\tau = 2E y(1-y)/\ell_\perp^2 = 2E/\mu^2$, where $y$ is the forward momentum fraction of one offspring and $\ell_\perp$ is the transverse momentum of the radiated parton.
- The integration over the momentum fraction $y$ from $\epsilon$ to $1-\epsilon$ yields a factor $\log(1/\epsilon)$, where $\epsilon$ is an infrared regulator.

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist formalism of Guo (2000), Wang (2001), and Majumder (2009) for jet modification in the quark-gluon plasma, where parton virtualities are much larger than the medium scale. It contradicts the antenna-based coherence picture of Casalderrey-Solana (2012) and Mehtar-Tani (2011), which argued that jets with larger virtuality experience greater quenching due to larger antenna size; instead, the authors find that quantum uncertainty compensates for smaller antenna size at low virtualities, making transverse shower size weakly dependent on originating parton virtuality. The derived weakening of $\hat{q}$ with virtuality provides a theoretical basis for the phenomenological factor $f(\mu^2)$ used in Bayesian analyses by the JETSCAPE collaboration (2022, 2024) and in the effective description of Kumar (2019).
