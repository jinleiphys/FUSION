# Collective flow at SIS energies within a hadronic transport approach: Influence of light nuclei formation and equation of state

> Collective flow observables are known to be a sensitive tool to gain insights on the equation of state of nuclear matter from heavy-ion collision observations. Towards more quantitative constraints one has to carefully assess other influences on the collective behaviour. In this work a hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) is applied to study the first four anisotropic flow coefficients in Au+Au collisions at $E_{\rm lab}=1.23A$ GeV in the context of the recently measured data by the HADES collaboration. In particular, the formation of light nuclei is important in this energy regime. Two different approaches are contrasted to each other: A clustering algorithm inspired by coalescence as well as microscopic formation of deuterons via explicit cross-sections. The sensitivity of directed and elliptic flow observables to the strength of the Skyrme mean field is explored. In addition, it is demonstrated that the rapidity-odd $v_3$ coefficient is practically zero in this energy regime and the ratio of $v_4/v_2^2$ is close to the value of 0.5 expected from hydrodynamic behaviour. This study establishes the current understanding of collective behaviour within the SMASH approach and lays the ground for future more quantitative constraints on the equation of state of nuclear matter within improved mean field calculations.

# Collective flow at SIS energies within a hadronic transport approach: Influence of light nuclei formation and equation of state

## Key claim
The hadronic transport approach SMASH, using a hard equation of state and either a coalescence-based clustering algorithm or explicit microscopic deuteron formation, can reproduce the directed and elliptic flow of protons and deuterons measured by HADES in Au+Au collisions at 1.23A GeV, but the elliptic flow of nucleons at high transverse momentum remains underestimated. The rapidity-odd triangular flow coefficient v3 is practically zero at this energy, and the ratio v4/v2^2 is close to 0.5, indicating near-hydrodynamic behavior.

## Method
The authors used the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport code version 1.8 to solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation via a testparticle ansatz, with hadronic degrees of freedom including resonances described by relativistic Breit-Wigner distributions. Nuclear potentials were implemented using a Skyrme mean field with three parameter sets (soft, default, hard) corresponding to different compressibilities, and each particle was represented by 20 testparticles for density estimation. Light nuclei formation was treated in two ways: a coalescence algorithm that clusters nucleons based on phase-space distance thresholds (r0 = 0.87 fm, p0 = 0.43 GeV) in the final state, and an explicit dynamical treatment where deuterons are produced and propagated via cross sections for processes such as pnN ↔ dN, using a fictional dibaryon resonance d' as an intermediate state.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: Elab = 1.23A GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Nuclear ground state density: ρ0 = 0.168 fm^-3
- Skyrme parameter sets: soft (A = -365 MeV, B = 303 MeV, τ = 1.17, K = 200 MeV), default (A = -209.2 MeV, B = 156.4 MeV, τ = 1.35, K = 240 MeV), hard (A = -124 MeV, B = 71 MeV, τ = 2.0, K = 375 MeV)
- Coalescence thresholds from Bayesian estimation: r0 = 0.87 (+0.03/-0.03) fm, p0 = 0.43 (+0.03/-0.02) GeV
- Centrality selection: 20%-30% most central collisions, impact parameter range 6.6 fm < b < 8.1 fm
- Triangular flow from scalar product method with cuts 0.2 GeV < pT < 2.0 GeV and |η| < 1: v3 = 0.00081 ± 0.00006 for nucleons, v3 = -0.0014 ± 0.0002 for deuterons
- Ratio v4/v2^2 is close to 0.5 for pT above 0.75 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous transport studies of collective flow at SIS energies, where approaches using nucleons and pions with sophisticated potentials (Gale 1987) favored a soft equation of state when compared to FOPI data (Aichelin 1987, Isse 2005, LeFevre 2015), while approaches like UrQMD with many resonance states favored a hard equation of state (Petersen 2006, Hillmann 2018, Hillmann 2019). The study establishes a baseline within the SMASH framework for future quantitative extraction of the nuclear equation of state using improved mean field calculations, including momentum-dependent potentials and relativistic mean fields based on density functional theory (Sorensen 2020). The comparison of coalescence (Sombun 2018) versus dynamical deuteron formation (Oliinychenko 2018) addresses a major systematic uncertainty in transport calculations at these energies, where light nuclei incorporate about half of the nucleons.
