# Lattice QCD Methods for Hadronic Polarizabilities

> Chiral dynamics makes definitive predictions for the electromagnetic polarizabilities of hadrons near the chiral limit; but, agreement with experiment is tenuous in some cases. We provide an overview of lattice QCD methods to compute the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of hadrons. Central to these methods is the lattice simulation of quarks in uniform, classical electromagnetic fields. A long-term goal is the determination of polarizabilities directly from lattice computations, however, in the near term, one may need to rely on partially quenched chiral perturbation theory. Nonetheless the same striking predictions for the pion mass dependence of electric and magnetic polarizabilities can be made from chiral dynamics, and tested with lattice QCD. A particular focus is a novel new method to handle charged particle correlation functions in magnetic fields.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD methods for computing hadronic polarizabilities are feasible in the near term only if one relies on partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to correct for the omission of sea quark electric charges, rather than obtaining polarizabilities directly from lattice computations.

## Method

The paper reviews two lattice QCD approaches to compute electric and magnetic polarizabilities of hadrons. The external field method adds classical electromagnetic fields to QCD computations by appending U(1)-valued links to the SU(3) color gauge links, then studies the field dependence of hadronic correlation functions. For charged particles in magnetic fields, a novel method projects the correlation function onto the lowest Landau level using the coordinate wave-function of that level, rather than summing over all spatial sites, to avoid contamination from higher Landau levels at perturbatively small field strengths.

## Key numbers

- Neutron electric polarizability extraction: the analogue of the Born term (involving the square of the neutron magnetic moment) was shown to affect extraction of the electric polarizability by 50%, using an ensemble of anisotropic clover fermions
- Charged pion energy in the lowest Landau level: \( E_0(B) = m_\pi + \frac{| Q B |}{ 2 m_\pi} + \frac{1}{2} \beta_M B^2 + \cdots \)
- Discretization corrections to the lowest Landau level energy: \( E^2_0 (B,a) = m^2_\pi + | Q B | - \left( \frac{1}{8} a^2 Q^2 + m_\pi \beta_M \right) B^2 \)
- Quantization condition for uniform electric field: \( q \mathcal{E} = 2 \pi n / L \beta \), where \(L\) is the spatial length and \(\beta\) is the temporal length
- Quantization condition for uniform magnetic field: \( q B = 2 \pi n / L^2 \)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory predictions for hadronic polarizabilities near the chiral limit (Holstein 1990, Bernard 1991) by providing a lattice QCD framework to test those predictions. The external field approach builds on earlier lattice studies of hadrons in electromagnetic fields (Detmold 2010, Detmold 2009) and addresses the restrictive quantization condition for magnetic fields that had limited access to perturbatively small field strengths. The method enables future lattice computations of polarizabilities for pions, kaons, nucleons, and potentially the deuteron, though sea quark electric charge effects remain a challenge that may require gauge ensemble re-weighting techniques (Freeman 2012).
