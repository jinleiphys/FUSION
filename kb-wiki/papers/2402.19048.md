# Nuclear medium meson structures from the Schwinger proper-time Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> In this paper, we report the results of our study on the nuclear medium modifications of the meson electromagnetic form factors in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the help of the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme to tame the loop divergence and simulate the effect of QCD confinement. In our current approach, the meson structure and nuclear medium are constructed in the same NJL model at the quark level. We examine the free-space and in-medium charge radii of kaon and pion, the spacelike elastic electromagnetic form factors of kaon and pion, and their quark-sector form factors, which reflect their internal structures. By comparing our result to experimental data, we found that the free-space elastic electromagnetic form factors of the mesons are consistent with the data, while the in-medium elastic electromagnetic form factors of the mesons are found to decrease as the nuclear matter density increases, leading to a rise of meson charge radius, which is consistent with the prediction of other theoretical calculations. We also predict the axial nucleon coupling constant $g_A$ in nuclear medium computed via the Goldberger-Treiman relation (GTR), which is crucial for searching the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$).

## Key claim

The paper reports that in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with Schwinger proper-time regularization, the in-medium elastic electromagnetic form factors of kaons and pions decrease as nuclear matter density increases, while their charge radii increase, but these results depend on the specific regularization scheme and the assumption that the strange quark mass remains unchanged in the medium.

## Method

The authors used a three-flavor NJL model at the quark level to compute both the nuclear medium properties and the meson structure consistently within the same framework, employing the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme to handle loop divergences and simulate QCD confinement. The in-medium dressed quark propagators were modified by introducing a vector mean field and a light quark chemical potential, while the strange quark propagator was kept unchanged. The meson electromagnetic form factors were extracted from the matrix element of the electromagnetic current using dressed quark-photon vertices that satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity, with the nuclear matter properties determined from the NJL model's effective potential and mean-field approximation.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear saturation density \(\rho_B = \rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the enhancement of the pion charge radius is approximately 10.33% compared to free space.
* At \(\rho_B = \rho_0\), the enhancement of the positively charged kaon charge radius is approximately 8.87% compared to free space.
* At \(\rho_B = \rho_0\), the enhancement of the neutral kaon charge radius is approximately 21.69% compared to free space.
* The binding energy per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter is \(E_B/A = -15.7\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The free-space constituent quark mass is \(M_0 = 400\) MeV, and at \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) the effective light quark mass \(M_u^*\) decreases to 328 MeV, a reduction of approximately 18%.
* The free-space pion mass is \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV; at \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) these become \(m_\pi^* = 133\) MeV and \(f_\pi^* = 89\) MeV.
* The free-space kaon mass is \(m_K = 495\) MeV and kaon decay constant \(f_K = 97\) MeV; at \(\rho_B = \rho_0\) these become \(m_K^* = 501\) MeV and \(f_K^* = 102\) MeV.
* The regularization parameters are \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{IR}} = 240\) MeV and \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}} = 645\) MeV.
* The coupling constants are \(G_\pi = 19.04\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(G_\omega = 6.03\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(G_s = 7.49\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and \(G_a = 2.8\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson properties in nuclear medium that used hybrid models, such as the light-front-quark-meson coupling (LF-QMC) model by deMelo (2014) and the NJL-quark meson coupling (NJL-QMC) model by Hutauruk (2018, 2019), by treating both the meson structure and nuclear matter within the same NJL model at the quark level. The results are consistent with predictions from QCD sum rules by Er (2022) and other NJL-based calculations. The predictions for the in-medium axial nucleon coupling constant \(g_A\) via the Goldberger-Treiman relation are relevant for searches of neutrinoless double beta decay and for future experiments at facilities such as the Compressed Baryonic Matter Experiment at FAIR, the Nuclotron Ion Collider Facility at JINR, and J-PARC.
