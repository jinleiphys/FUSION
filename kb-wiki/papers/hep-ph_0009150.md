# Nuclear effects and higher twists in F3 structure function

> We analyze the CCFR collaboration iron target data on the xF3 structure function making particular emphasis on the extraction of the higher twist contributions from data. Corrections for nuclear effects are applied in order to extract data on the structure function of the isoscalar nucleon. Our analysis confirms the observation made earlier, that the higher twist terms depend strongly on the level to which QCD perturbation theory analysis is applied. We discuss the impact of nuclear effects on the higher twist term as well as on the QCD scale parameter Lambda_{\bar{MS}} extracted from the fit to data.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the higher twist term \(h(x)\) in the \(xF_3\) structure function depends strongly on the order of perturbative QCD used for the leading twist part, and applying corrections for nuclear effects (specifically for the iron target) further suppresses the extracted higher twist contribution, especially at large \(x\).

## Method

The authors analyzed CCFR collaboration iron target data on the \(xF_3\) structure function. They first calculated the nuclear EMC ratio \(R_3(x,Q^2) = F_3^A / A F_3^N\) using a phenomenological nuclear spectral function that includes both mean-field and high-momentum (NN-correlation) components, then extracted the isoscalar nucleon structure function by dividing the CCFR data by this ratio. They performed QCD fits to the corrected data using the renormalization group equation in LO, NLO, and NNLO approximations, parameterizing the leading twist part at a starting scale \(Q_0^2 = 20\ \text{GeV}^2\) and fitting the higher twist term \(h(x)\) in each \(x\)-bin along with the QCD scale parameter \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\).

## Key numbers

- The mean kinetic energy of a nucleon in iron from the model spectral function is \(\langle \boldsymbol{p}^2 \rangle / 2M = 31\ \text{MeV}\).
- The high-momentum component of the iron momentum distribution accounts for about 20% of the total normalization, contributing about 20 MeV to the mean kinetic energy.
- The two-nucleon separation energy \(E^{(2)}\) for iron is set to 20 MeV.
- The parameter \(E^{(1)}\) (mean-field separation energy) is fixed at 27 MeV to satisfy the Koltun sum rule with a mean separation energy \(\langle \varepsilon \rangle \approx -50\ \text{MeV}\).
- The fit uses 116 data points with \(Q^2\) between \(1.3\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(200\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The QCD scale parameter from the NNLO fit with nuclear corrections is \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = (394 \pm 55)\ \text{MeV}\) for four quark flavors.
- Nuclear effects cause about a 40 MeV decrease in \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\), corresponding to a shift in \(\alpha_S(M_Z)\) of about \(2 \times 10^{-3}\).
- A test fit with a free mass parameter in the Nachtmann moments yields a value of about \(0.9\ \text{GeV}\) with an error of about \(0.2\ \text{GeV}\), close to the proton mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD analyses of the CCFR/NuTeV data (fit1, fit2) by explicitly applying a realistic nuclear model for the iron target, rather than using a deuteron model as in ST97 or an incorrect assumption about the \(x \to 1\) limit as in fit2. The results confirm the earlier observation that higher twist terms decrease when moving from LO to NLO to NNLO perturbation theory. The analysis enables more reliable extraction of higher twist contributions and the QCD scale parameter from neutrino-nucleus data, and the authors plan to address nuclear shadowing effects at small \(x\) in future work.
