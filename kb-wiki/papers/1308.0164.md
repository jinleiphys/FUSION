# The Effect of Fluctuations on the QCD Critical Point in a Finite Volume

> We investigate the effect of a finite volume on the critical behavior of the theory of the strong interaction (QCD) by means of a quark-meson model for two quark flavors. In particular, we analyze the effect of a finite volume on the location of the critical point in the phase diagram existing in our model. In our analysis, we take into account the effect of long-range fluctuations with the aid of renormalization group techniques. We find that these quantum and thermal fluctuations, absent in mean-field studies, play an import role for the dynamics in a finite volume. We show that the critical point is shifted towards smaller temperatures and larger values of the quark chemical potential if the volume size is decreased. This behavior persists for antiperiodic as well as periodic boundary conditions for the quark fields as used in many lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The critical endpoint (CEP) of the quark-meson model is shifted towards smaller temperatures and larger quark chemical potentials when the system volume is decreased, but this result is obtained within a specific truncation of the renormalization group flow (local potential approximation with no running Yukawa coupling) and for a particular parameter set yielding a pion mass of 75 MeV, chosen because it is the smallest accessible value in lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors employed the functional renormalization group (Wetterich equation) to compute the scale-dependent effective potential of a two-flavor quark-meson model in a finite cubic volume V = L³, using a dimensionally reduced (spatial) regulator and a leading-order derivative expansion (local potential approximation) with constant wave-function renormalizations. They solved the resulting partial differential equation for the order-parameter potential by discretizing the field variable, and located the CEP in finite volume by identifying the point in the (μ, T) plane where the longitudinal susceptibility χ_σ = 1/M_σ² attains its maximum value, requiring continuous connection to the infinite-volume CEP.

## Key numbers

- Infinite-volume CEP coordinates: (μ_CEP^(∞), T_CEP^(∞)) ≈ (298.6 ± 1 MeV, 35.0 ± 1 MeV), for the chosen parameter set with m_π = 75 MeV
- For L = 4 fm with periodic boundary conditions: (μ_CEP^max., T_CEP^max.) = (335.6 ± 1 MeV, 9.0 ± 1 MeV), corresponding to a relative shift Δμ_CEP = +0.12(1) and ΔT_CEP = -0.74(4)
- For L = 6 fm: Δμ_CEP = +0.02(1), ΔT_CEP = -0.26(5)
- For L = 10 fm: Δμ_CEP = 0.00(1), ΔT_CEP = -0.06(6), indicating the infinite-volume position is effectively reached at this box size
- Zero-temperature observables used for parameter fixing: m_q = 318 MeV, m_σ = 461 MeV, m_π = 75 MeV, with UV scale Λ = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of finite-volume effects on the CEP in quark-meson and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Palhares 2009, Fraga 2011, Bhattacharyya 2012) by including bosonic fluctuations via renormalization group techniques, which are essential for correctly capturing Goldstone-mode physics in finite volume. The results are consistent with earlier mean-field findings on the direction of the CEP shift, suggesting fluctuations are subdominant for the shift direction in the studied m_πL regime, and the work provides guidance for lattice QCD simulations (deForcrand 2002, Philipsen 2005) by showing that the CEP is pushed into a difficult-to-access region of the phase diagram at finite volume.
