# Factorization for hadronic heavy quarkonium production

> 8 pages, 12 figures, Talk presented at the 2nd International Conference on Hard and Electromagnetic Probes of High-Energy Nuclear Collisions, Asilomar Conference Grounds, Pacific Grove, California, 9-16 June, 2006

## Key claim

QCD factorization for heavy quarkonium production in hadronic collisions, including the widely used NRQCD model, has not been proven theoretically; a necessary condition for NRQCD factorization fails at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) due to a noncanceling infrared divergence that cannot be absorbed into conventional NRQCD matrix elements, though this divergence can be absorbed if the matrix elements are modified by incorporating Wilson lines.

## Method

The paper reviews three production models (color-singlet model, color evaporation model, and NRQCD model) and examines the status of QCD factorization for each. It tests the NRQCD factorization relation for parton-to-quarkonium fragmentation functions by analyzing representative NNLO contributions to the process of a gluon fragmenting into a heavy quark pair, using an eikonal approximation and summing over cuts and connections to quark and antiquark lines.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark velocity for charmonium production: \(v_c \sim |\vec{q}_c|/m_c \leq \sqrt{(4M_D^2-4m_c^2)/(4m_c^2)} \sim 0.88\), indicating the velocity expansion may not be a good approximation.
- The noncanceling infrared divergence at NNLO for the color-octet-to-singlet fragmentation function is given by \({\mathcal I}^{(8\to 1)}_2(v) = - \alpha_s^2\, \frac{1}{3\varepsilon}\, v^2\).
- The all-orders-in-\(v\) result for the same fragmentation function is \({\mathcal I}^{(8\to 1)}(v) = \frac{\alpha_s^2}{4\varepsilon} \left[ 1-\frac{1}{2f(v)} \ln\left[\frac{1+f(v)}{1-f(v)}\right] \right]\), where \(f(v)=2v/(1+v^2)\).

## Context

This talk extends the long-standing debate on heavy quarkonium production mechanisms, contrasting the color-singlet model (CSM), color evaporation model (CEM), and NRQCD model. The NRQCD model had been most successful in interpreting data but failed to predict quarkonium polarization measured by CDF. The paper identifies a specific infrared divergence in the NRQCD factorization at NNLO, building on earlier work by the author and collaborators (Nayak, Qiu, Sterman, 2005) that proposed gauge-completed matrix elements as a necessary modification. This work enables future efforts to prove a factorization theorem for heavy quarkonium production and suggests that nuclear dependence studies in hadron-nucleus collisions could help distinguish production mechanisms.
