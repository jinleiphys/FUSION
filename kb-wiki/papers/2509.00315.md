# Impacts of isolated nucleon-nucleon correlations in relativistic $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions

> Nucleon-nucleon interactions are fundamental to the nuclear forces operating within the nucleus and play a crucial role in shaping the initial conditions of relativistic ion collisions through two-nucleon correlations. In this paper, we introduce an innovative approach to explore these encoded nucleon-nucleon correlations within advanced \textit{ab-initio} models in the context of relativistic $^{16}O$ collisions. Our methodology successfully reproduces the structural properties of the nucleonic configurations generated by these models, as well as the distance correlations between the nucleon pairs, denoted as $C(Δr)$. By generating nucleon positions that align with authentic configurations and adhering to the constraints imposed by the probability distribution of relative two-nucleon distances, our goal is to better understand nucleon-nucleon interactions within \textit{ab-initio} frameworks.

## Key claim
The paper introduces an acceptance-rejection method (ARM) that can reproduce the two-nucleon distance correlations from *ab-initio* models (NLEFT and VMC) while starting from a fixed 3-parameter Fermi (3pF) one-body density, but the method does not fully capture all angular information, leading to deviations of up to 5% in some initial-state eccentricity observables.

## Method
The authors generate nucleon positions for ¹⁶O using a 3-parameter Fermi (3pF) radial density distribution with parameters R₀=2.608 fm, a₀=0.513 fm, and w=-0.051, then apply the acceptance-rejection method (ARM) to enforce the two-body correlation function C(Δr) obtained from either Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) or Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) configurations. The uncorrelated two-body distribution g'(Δr) serves as the proposal distribution, and samples are accepted when a uniform random number satisfies U ≤ g(Δr')/(M·g'(Δr')) with M = Δr_biggest. The resulting nucleon configurations are used as input to the TRENTo initial-condition model to simulate ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at 200 GeV and 5.36 TeV, and the initial-state eccentricities εₙ and energy-density fluctuations are computed.

## Key numbers
- 3pF density parameters for ¹⁶O: R₀ = 2.608 fm, a₀ = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051
- ARM reproduces the transverse size Rₛ of the fireball with over 98% accuracy; maximum deviation from original configurations is 2% for VMC and about 1% for NLEFT
- For ε₂{2} at 200 GeV, the maximum deviation between NLEFT and 3pF is approximately 6% in the 5-10% centrality range
- Maximum deviation between original configurations and ARM for ε₂{2} reaches up to 5% for both NLEFT and VMC
- For ε₃{2} from NLEFT, ARM captures approximately 97% of the information at 200 GeV and 96% at 5.36 TeV in central collisions
- For VMC, differences between original configurations and ARM for ε₃{2} fall within 2-4%

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon-nucleon correlations in relativistic light-ion collisions, building on prior investigations of α-clustering effects in ¹⁶O and ²⁰Ne by Mehrabpour (2025) and the isolation of multi-nucleon correlations using Skyrme-DFT density profiles by Hu (2025). The method enables systematic comparison of two different *ab-initio* frameworks, NLEFT (Lu, 2018; Elhatisari, 2017) and VMC (Lonardoni, 2017), and their impact on initial-state observables in ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The approach can be extended to other light nuclei such as ⁸Be, ¹²C, and ²⁰Ne, and allows future isolation of short-range versus long-range correlation effects on observables.
