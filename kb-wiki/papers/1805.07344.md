# Thermal corrections to the gluon magnetic Debye mass

> We compute the gluon polarization tensor in a thermo-magnetic environment in the strong magnetic field limit at zero and high temperature. The magnetic field effects are introduced using Schwinger's proper time method. Thermal effects are computed in the HTL approximation. At zero temperature, we reproduce the well-known result whereby for a non-vanishing quark mass, the polarization tensor reduces to the parallel structure and its coefficient develops an imaginary part corresponding to the threshold for quark-antiquark pair production. This coefficient is infrared finite and simplifies considerably when the quark mass vanishes. Keeping always the field strength as the largest energy scale, in the high temperature regime we analyze two complementary hierarchies of scales: $q^2\ll m_f^2\ll T^2$ and $m_f^2\ll q^2\ll T^2$. In the latter, we show that the polarization tensor is infrared finite as $m_f$ goes to zero. In the former, we discuss the thermal corrections to the magnetic Debye mass.

## Key claim

The paper computes thermal corrections to the gluon magnetic Debye mass in the strong magnetic field limit, showing that the Debye mass depends on the order in which the parallel and perpendicular components of the gluon momentum are taken to zero, with the sum of longitudinal and transverse Debye masses remaining constant across different limiting procedures.

## Method

The gluon polarization tensor was computed at one-loop order using Schwinger's proper time method for magnetic field effects and the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation for thermal effects, working in the Lowest Landau Level (LLL) limit where the magnetic field is the largest energy scale. The authors analyzed two complementary hierarchies of scales in the high-temperature regime: \(q^2\ll m_f^2\ll T^2\) and \(m_f^2\ll q^2\ll T^2\), projecting the polarization tensor onto longitudinal and transverse structures relative to the medium rest frame.

## Key numbers

- For the case \(q_\perp\to 0\) with \(q_3\) finite, the longitudinal Debye mass is \((m_D^2)_L = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2}\sum_f |q_fB|\left(\ln\left(\frac{\pi^2T^2}{m_f^2}\right)-2\gamma_E\right)\), while transverse modes are not screened.
- For the case \(q_3\to 0\) with \(q_\perp\) finite, the transverse Debye mass is \((m_D^2)_T = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2}\sum_f |q_fB|\left(\ln\left(\frac{\pi^2T^2}{m_f^2}\right)-2\gamma_E\right)\), while longitudinal modes are not screened.
- For the case where \(q_3\) and \(q_\perp\) vanish at the same rate, both longitudinal and transverse Debye masses equal \((m_D^2)_L = (m_D^2)_T = \frac{g^2}{16\pi^2}\sum_f |q_fB|\left(\ln\left(\frac{\pi^2T^2}{m_f^2}\right)-2\gamma_E\right)\).
- The sum of longitudinal and transverse Debye masses is \((m_D^2)_L+(m_D^2)_T = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2}\sum_f |q_fB|\left(\ln\left(\frac{\pi^2T^2}{m_f^2}\right)-2\gamma_E\right)\) for all three cases.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the gauge field polarization tensor in magnetized media, including the QED studies by Dittrich, Hattori, Fukushima, Ferrer, and Bandyopadhyay, as well as the QCD equation of state calculations by Rath. The results enable further study of gluon dispersion properties in magnetized quark-gluon plasma relevant to heavy-ion collisions and compact astrophysical objects, and provide analytic expressions that can be used to investigate renormalization group evolution and thermo-magnetic effects on screening masses.
