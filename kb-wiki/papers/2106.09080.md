# Diquark properties from full QCD lattice simulations

> We study diquarks on the lattice in the background of a static quark, in a gauge-invariant formalism with quark masses down to almost physical $m_π$. We determine mass differences between diquark channels as well as diquark-quark mass differences. The lightest and next-to-lightest diquarks have "good" scalar, $\bar{3}_F$, $\bar{3}_c$, $J^P=0^+$, and "bad" axial vector, $6_F$, $\bar{3}_c$, $J^P=1^+$, quantum numbers, and a bad-good mass difference for $ud$ flavors, $198(4)~\rm{MeV}$, in excellent agreement with phenomenological determinations. Quark-quark attraction is found only in the "good" diquark channel. We extract a corresponding diquark size of $\sim 0.6~\rm{fm}$ and perform a first exploration of the "good" diquark shape, which is shown to be spherical. Our results provide quantitative support for modeling the low-lying baryon spectrum using good light diquark effective degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper provides quantitative, gauge-invariant lattice QCD evidence that the "good" scalar diquark (with quantum numbers $\bar{3}_F$, $\bar{3}_c$, $J^P=0^+$) is a compact, spherical, and significantly lighter object than other diquark channels, supporting its use as an effective degree of freedom in baryon and multiquark models, but the results are obtained in the presence of a static quark and have not yet been extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors use a gauge-invariant formalism on $n_f=2+1$ dynamical clover-improved Wilson fermion configurations ($32^3 \times 64$, $a \approx 0.090$ fm) from the PACS-CS collaboration, with five ensembles covering pion masses from 164 to 707 MeV. They compute static-light-light baryon correlators to extract diquark mass differences, and fixed-time density-density correlators to extract diquark size and shape from the spatial decay of quark-quark correlations in the background of a static quark.

## Key numbers

- Bad-good ($1^+ - 0^+$) mass difference for $ud$ diquarks: $198(4)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$
- Bad-good mass difference for $\ell s$ diquarks: $145(5)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$
- Bad-good mass difference for $ss'$ diquarks: $118(2)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$
- Good diquark size: $\sim 0.6$ fm, extracted from exponential fits to density-density correlators
- Good diquark-quark mass difference $\delta(Q[ud]_{0^+} - \bar{Q}u)$: $319(1)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$
- Good diquark-quark mass difference $\delta(Q[\ell s]_{0^+} - \bar{Q}s)$: $385(9)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$
- Good diquark-quark mass difference $\delta(Q[\ell s]_{0^+} - \bar{Q}\ell)$: $450(6)$ MeV at physical $m_\pi$

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice studies of diquarks (Alexandrou 2005, Green 2010) to full QCD with dynamical quarks down to near-physical pion masses, confirming the special status of the good scalar diquark channel. The results provide quantitative support for diquark models used in baryon spectroscopy (Jaffe 2004) and have already motivated lattice and phenomenological studies of doubly heavy tetraquarks (Francis 2016, Francis 2018, Bicudo 2015, Junnarkar 2018), including the LHCb discovery of the $T_{cc}$ state. The measured diquark properties can sharpen parameter values in diquark-based models of exotic hadrons.
