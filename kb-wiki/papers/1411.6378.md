# Status of Chiral Meson Physics

> This talk includes a short introduction to Chiral Perturbation Theory in the meson sector concentrating on a number of recent developments. I discuss the latest fit of the low-energy constants. Finite volume corrections are discussed for the case with twisted boundary conditions for form-factors and first results at two-loops for three flavours for masses. The last part discusses the extension to other symmetry breaking patterns relevant for technicolour and related theories as well as the calculation of leading logarithms to high loop orders.

## Key claim

This talk presents an overview of recent developments in mesonic Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT), including the latest fit of low-energy constants (LECs) at two-loop order for three flavours, but the author notes that the fit is "clearly ill-defined" because 17+3 inputs are used to determine 8 LECs and 34 combinations of higher-order constants, requiring many theoretical prejudices and constraints.

## Method

The author performed a global fit of the three-flavour ChPT LECs at order p^6 using experimental data for meson masses, decay constants, scalar radii, pi-pi and pi-K scattering lengths, K_{l4} decay form factors, and lattice input for the quark mass ratio m_s/m_hat, combined with resonance estimates and constraints from large N_c arguments to fix the poorly determined p^6 constants. Finite volume corrections were computed using ChPT with twisted boundary conditions, and the leading logarithms were calculated to high loop orders using a recursive method based on the renormalization group and automated with FORM.

## Context

This talk extends the long line of ChPT development initiated by Weinberg (1978) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984), updating the three-flavour LEC fits from earlier work by Amoros et al. (2001) and Bijnens and Jemos (2012) with the BE14 fit that incorporates more constraints on the p^6 constants. The finite volume calculations with twisted boundary conditions build on the general framework of Luscher (1985) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1987), and the leading logarithm calculations extend the method of Weinberg (1978) and Buchler and Colangelo (2003) to high loop orders using automated techniques. The work enables more precise lattice QCD comparisons, improved extrapolations for technicolour theories, and a systematic understanding of the convergence properties of ChPT.
