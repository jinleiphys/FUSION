# Generalization of the model-independent Laurent-Pietarinen single-channel pole-extraction formalism to multiple channels

> A method to extract resonance pole information from single-channel partial-wave amplitudes based on a Laurent (Mittag-Leffler) expansion and conformal mapping techniques has recently been developed. This method has been applied to a number of reactions and provides a model-independent extraction procedure which is particularly useful in cases where a set of amplitudes is available only at descrete energies. This method has been generalized and applied to the case of a multi-channel fit, where several sets of amplitudes are analysed simultaneously. The importance of unitarity constraints is discussed. The final result provides a powerful, model-independent tool for analyzing partial-wave amplitudes of coupled or connected channels based entirely on the concepts of analyticity and unitarity.

## Key claim

The authors generalize a previously single-channel model-independent pole-extraction method (Laurent+Pietarinen expansion) to simultaneously fit multiple coupled channels, enforcing that pole positions are identical across channels while allowing channel-dependent residues; the method is validated against Bonn-Gatchina amplitudes and reveals that a fourth resonance (N(2100)1/2+) is required for a good multi-channel fit, even though it was considered poorly determined in the original model.

## Method

The multi-channel Laurent+Pietarinen (MC L+P) method represents each channel's partial-wave amplitude as a sum of pole terms (with fixed, shared pole positions across channels) plus three Pietarinen expansions (conformal-mapping power series) for the regular background, each with a specified branch-point. A global discrepancy function sums the chi-squared contributions from all channels, and a penalty function enforces unitarity constraints including subthreshold unitarity for elastic and inelastic channels. The free parameters (residues and Pietarinen coefficients) are minimized simultaneously across all channels to extract unique pole positions and channel-dependent residues.

## Key numbers

- In single-channel fits to BG2011-2 amplitudes, the N(1440)1/2+ pole appears at M=1368 MeV, Gamma=193 MeV in piN elastic, while the N(1710)1/2+ appears at M=1686 MeV, Gamma=204 MeV in piN -> etaN; the N(1880)1/2+ appears at different masses (1857 vs 1861 MeV) in the two channels.
- The MC L+P fit yields N(1440)1/2+ at M=1368(3) MeV, Gamma=191(3) MeV, with piN residue magnitude 49(2) and phase -82(3) degrees.
- The N(1710)1/2+ is found at M=1686(8) MeV, Gamma=153(24) MeV, with etaN branching fraction 14(3)%.
- The N(1880)1/2+ is found at M=1875(9) MeV, Gamma=232(15) MeV, with piN residue magnitude 3(1) and phase 107(16) degrees.
- The N(2100)1/2+ is found at M=2171(24) MeV, Gamma=210(48) MeV, with piN residue magnitude 15(5) and phase -50(8) degrees.

## Context

This work extends the single-channel Laurent+Pietarinen formalism (Svarc et al., 2013) to handle coupled channels, addressing a limitation where correlated multipoles in photoproduction or coupled-channel partial waves could only be treated independently. The method is tested against Bonn-Gatchina BG2011-2 amplitudes (published in 2012), and the multi-channel fit reveals a fourth resonance (N(2100)1/2+) that was poorly determined in the original model but becomes necessary in the simultaneous fit. This enables model-independent extraction of pole parameters directly from experimental partial-wave data for coupled processes, which was previously impossible.
