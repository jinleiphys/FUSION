# Anomaly/Transport in an Ideal Weyl gas

> We study some of the transport processes which are specific to an ideal gas of relativistic Weyl fermions and relate the corresponding transport coefficients to various anomaly coefficients of the system. We propose that these transport processes can be thought of as arising from the continuous injection of chiral states and their subsequent adiabatic flow driven by vorticity. This in turn leads to an elegant expression relating the anomaly induced transport coefficients to the anomaly polynomial of the Ideal Weyl gas.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that anomaly-induced transport in an ideal gas of relativistic Weyl fermions can be understood as arising from continuous injection of chiral states at zero energy by magnetic fields, followed by their adiabatic flow driven by vorticity; this picture yields a simple replacement rule relating the transport coefficients directly to the anomaly polynomial of the system, though the authors emphasize this is a conjecture that may hold beyond free theories.

## Method

The authors study free Weyl fermions in even spacetime dimensions d=2n at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a hydrodynamic description with a fluid velocity u^μ. They introduce a chiral spectral current J^μ_q(x,E_p) that tracks the density and flow of single-particle chiral states as a function of energy, and impose adiabaticity as a conservation equation for this current in the combined (x,E_p) space. By matching the microscopic adiabaticity condition to thermodynamic results from prior work (Loganayagam 2011, Kharzeev 2011), they derive the boundary condition for injection of zero-energy modes and solve for the spectral current, then integrate over energies to obtain the anomaly-induced Gibbs free energy current.

## Key numbers

* In 2d, the anomaly-induced Gibbs free energy current is given by a thermal integral yielding -2π [ μ^2/(2!(2π)^2) (∑_species χ q^2) + T^2/4! (∑_species χ) ] u, where χ is the chirality and q the charge.
* The 2d anomaly polynomial is written as P_anom(F,R) = -2π [ F^2/(2!(2π)^2) (∑_species χ q^2) - p_1(R)/4! (∑_species χ) ]_2d, with p_1(R) the first Pontryagin class.
* The replacement rule in 2d is: G_anom = u P_anom(F→μ, p_1(R)→-T^2).
* For general even dimensions, the chiral spectral current at zero energy is given by J_q|_(E_p=0) = (χ/(2π)) ((qB)/(2π))^(n-1) ∧ u/((n-1)!), where B is the rest-frame magnetic field 2-form.
* The full chiral spectral current solution is J_q = (χ/(2π)) ((qB+2ωE_p)/(2π))^(n-1) ∧ u/((n-1)!), where ω is the vorticity 2-form.

## Context

This work extends thermodynamic/adiabaticity arguments for anomaly-induced transport developed by Son and Surowka (2009), Neiman and Oz (2010), Kharzeev and Yee (2011), and Loganayagam (2011), which derived constraints from the second law of thermodynamics but left the relation to gravitational anomalies mysterious. The paper provides a microscopic derivation for free fermions that generalizes the 4d observation by Landsteiner, Megias, and Pena-Benitez (2011) that T^2 coefficients relate to gravitational anomalies, proposing a universal replacement rule from the anomaly polynomial to transport coefficients that could apply to strongly coupled holographic phases as well. This enables future study of these transport processes away from equilibrium, in the presence of disorder, or in lattice analogues of continuum Weyl fermions.
