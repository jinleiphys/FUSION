# Structure of $^{23}$Al from a multi-channel algebraic scattering model based on mirror symmetry

> The proton-rich nucleus $^{23}$Al has a ground state just 123 keV below the proton drip-line, and as a result comparatively little is known experimentally about its properties, as with many such nuclei. Theoretical investigations have tended to model exclusively the ground and first one to three excited states known. In this paper, we theoretically model most of the known spectrum, and predict what states may as yet be unobserved. We use the multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method to describe states as resonances of a valence proton coupled to a $^{22}$Mg rotor core. Six states with low-excitation energies and defined $J^π$ are matched, and we make the first prediction of the properties of four others and propound the possible existence of several more.

## Key claim

The authors use the multichannel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method to model most of the known spectrum of the proton-rich nucleus $^{23}$Al up to approximately 4 MeV excitation, but all calculated states are overbound relative to experiment, requiring an ad hoc adjustment of the central potential depth to match the ground state binding energy.

## Method

The MCAS method solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space for a valence proton coupled to a $^{22}$Mg rotor core described by a Tamura collective model. The nuclear interaction is first determined by fitting the mirror system $n+^{22}$Ne to reproduce the $^{23}$Ne spectrum, then applied to $p+^{22}$Mg with the addition of a Coulomb potential derived from a relativistic mean-field charge distribution of $^{22}$Mg. Orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials are used to block Pauli-forbidden orbitals, and the coupled-channel potentials include central, spin-orbit, orbital angular momentum, and spin-spin terms expanded to second order in deformation.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of $^{23}$Al lies 123 keV below the one-proton emission threshold
- The MCAS calculation overbinds the $^{23}$Al ground state by approximately 229 keV relative to experiment
- The $^{22}$Mg rms charge radius from the relativistic mean-field model is 3.088 fm
- The $^{23}$Ne ground state energy is matched to three decimal places by adjusting the central well depth to -65.200 MeV (odd parity) and -50.894 MeV (even parity)
- The $^{23}$Al $\frac{1}{2}^+$ state is calculated at 0.722 MeV excitation with a proton width of 1.30$\times 10^{-3}$ MeV, compared to the experimental excitation of 0.55 MeV and width 7.4$\times 10^{-5}$ MeV
- The $\frac{7}{2}^+$ state measured at 1.616 MeV by Gade et al. (2008) is calculated at 1.806 MeV with a width of 8.70$\times 10^{-7}$ MeV
- The $(\frac{3}{2}^+)$ state at 3.00 MeV from He et al. (2007) is matched by a calculated state at 2.732 MeV with width 0.1504 MeV, five times the experimental width of 0.032 MeV
- The deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for the primary rotor band is 0.22, with $\overline{\beta_2}=0.1034$ for the secondary band (43% of the primary)

## Context

This work extends previous MCAS studies of the $n+^{22}$Ne mirror system (Fraser et al., 2014, 2015) to predict the spectrum of the proton-rich $^{23}$Al, which has only one bound state and limited experimental data. The calculation includes five target states of $^{22}$Mg (the $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$, $4^+_1$ primary rotor band states plus the $2^+_2$ and $4^+_2$ states), going beyond earlier shell-model calculations by Yu et al. (2014) that only modeled the four lowest states, and beyond the microscopic cluster model of Timofeyuk et al. (2005) that focused on the Gamow window. The results enable predictions of unobserved states and elastic scattering cross sections for $p+^{22}$Mg that can be tested by future experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities.
