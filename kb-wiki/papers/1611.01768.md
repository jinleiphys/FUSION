# Nuclear symmetry energy and the role of three-body forces

> Density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy as well as its partial wave decomposition is studied within the framework of lowest-order constrained variational (LOCV) method using AV18 two-body interaction supplemented by UIX three-body force. The main focus of the present work is to introduce a revised version of three-body force which is based on an isospin-dependent parametrization of coefficients in the UIX force, in order to overcome the inability to produce correct saturation-point parameters} in the framework of LOCV method. We find that employing the new model of {\ph three-body force} in the LOCV formalism leads to successfully reproducing the semi-empirical parameters of cold nuclear matter, including} $E_{sym}(ρ_0)$, $L$, and $K_{sym}$. All our models of three-body force combined with AV18 two-body force give maximum neutron star mass higher than $2\;M_\odot$. The fraction of protons in the nucleon cores of neutron stars strongly depends on the three-body force parametrization.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a revised version of the UIX three-body force with isospin-dependent parameters \(A\) and \(U\) (expanded to second order in asymmetry parameter \(X\)) to overcome the inability of the standard LOCV method with AV18+UIX to simultaneously reproduce the correct saturation-point parameters and the empirical symmetry energy at normal density.

## Method

The lowest-order constrained variational (LOCV) method was used with the AV18 two-body potential supplemented by the UIX three-body force. The three-body force was included via an effective density-dependent two-body potential obtained by averaging over the third nucleon, weighted by LOCV two-body correlation functions. The standard UIX parameters \(A\) and \(U\) were replaced by isospin-dependent forms \(A = A_{sym} + A'X^2\) and \(U = U_{sym} + U'X^2\), with \(A_{sym} = -0.041\) MeV and \(U_{sym} = 0.000523\) MeV fixed from symmetric nuclear matter; \(A'\) and \(U'\) were varied to reproduce empirical symmetry energy and its slope.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 39.9\) MeV for LOCV with AV18+UIX (isospin-independent 3BF), compared to the empirical range \(31.6 \pm 2.66\) MeV.
* Slope parameter \(L = 115.75\) MeV for LOCV with AV18+UIX (isospin-independent 3BF), compared to the empirical mean \(58.9 \pm 16.0\) MeV.
* With the revised isospin-dependent 3BF, selected \((A',U')\) pairs yield: \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 35.0\) MeV and \(L = 89.91\) MeV for (0.0015, -0.000523); \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 33.0\) MeV and \(L = 74.04\) MeV for (0.0175, -0.000261); \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31.0\) MeV and \(L = 57.43\) MeV for (0.0337, 0).
* All models of three-body force combined with AV18 give maximum neutron star mass higher than \(2\;M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends previous LOCV calculations by Goudarzi et al. (2015) that used AV18+UIX but produced too high symmetry energy and slope. It contrasts with other many-body approaches such as BHF (AV18+UIX) which gave \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 33.6\) MeV and \(L = 66.9\) MeV (Li et al., 2008), and variational calculations (AV8+UIX) giving \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 30.0\) MeV (Akmal et al., 1998). The revised isospin-dependent 3BF enables the LOCV method to simultaneously reproduce empirical saturation properties and symmetry energy parameters, and can be applied to study neutron star structure and beta-stable matter.
