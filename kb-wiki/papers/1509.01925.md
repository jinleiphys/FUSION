# Limits on sterile neutrino contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay

> Nuclear matrix elements (NME) for exchange of arbitrary mass neutrinos are calculated in the interacting boson model (IBM-2). By combining the NME with the phase space factors (PSF), expected half-lives for neutrinos of mass $m_N$ and coupling $U_{eN}$ are estimated. Limits on sterile neutrinos with masses in the eV, keV, MeV-GeV, and TeV range are given.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by sterile neutrinos of arbitrary mass using the interacting boson model (IBM-2), and finds that the contribution to the half-life is enhanced when the sterile neutrino mass is near the nucleon Fermi momentum of approximately 100 MeV, but the limits set on sterile neutrino parameters depend strongly on the assumed value of the axial vector coupling constant g_A, which may be renormalized in nuclei.

## Method

The authors computed the dimensionless IBM-2 nuclear matrix elements M^(0ν)(m_N) * m_N/m_e for 0νββ decay to the ground state and first excited 0+ state, broken down into Gamow-Teller, Fermi, and Tensor contributions, for sterile neutrino masses m_N ranging from 0.00001 GeV to 1000 GeV in three isotopes (76Ge, 130Te, 136Xe). They used the formulation of Simkovic et al. (1999) with Argonne short-range correlations and closure energy, then combined these NMEs with previously tabulated phase space factors to estimate half-lives and set exclusion limits on the coupling |U_eN|^2 versus m_N.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge, the dimensionless matrix element M^(0ν) * m_N/m_e peaks at 340 for m_N = 0.1 GeV (ground state), then falls to 9.14 at m_N = 10 GeV and 0.0911 at m_N = 1000 GeV.
- For 130Te, the peak value is 284 at m_N = 0.1 GeV (ground state), dropping to 8.19 at m_N = 10 GeV and 0.0818 at m_N = 1000 GeV.
- For 136Xe, the peak value is 229 at m_N = 0.1 GeV (ground state), dropping to 6.48 at m_N = 10 GeV and 0.0647 at m_N = 1000 GeV.
- The matrix element M^(0ν) for 76Ge is 4.68(150) and M^(0ν_h) is 104(58), with sqrt(⟨p^2⟩) = 103 MeV.
- For 136Xe, M^(0ν) = 3.05(98) and M^(0ν_h) = 73(41), with sqrt(⟨p^2⟩) = 107 MeV.
- The upper limit on ⟨m_N,light⟩ from GERDA (76Ge) is <0.30 eV, from KamLAND-Zen (136Xe) is <0.20 eV, and from EXO (136Xe) is <0.25 eV.
- The lower limit on ⟨m_N,heavy⟩ from GERDA (76Ge) is >34.8 GeV, from KamLAND-Zen (136Xe) is >58.6 GeV, and from EXO (136Xe) is >44.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IBM-2 calculations for light and heavy neutrino exchange (Barea et al., 2015) to the full range of arbitrary sterile neutrino masses, and provides a comparison with the interacting shell model (ISM) calculations of Barea et al. (2010) and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations of Faessler et al. (2014). The results enable experimental collaborations (GERDA, CUORE-0, KamLAND-Zen, EXO) to set exclusion limits on sterile neutrino parameters in the m_N-|U_eN|^2 plane, and the simple analytical formula provided (similar to that of Kovalenko et al., 2009) allows rapid estimation of half-lives for any sterile neutrino mass.
