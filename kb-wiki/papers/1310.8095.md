# Probing pairing correlations in Sn isotopes using Richardson-Gaudin integrability

> Pairing correlations in the even-even A=102-130 Sn isotopes are discussed, based on the Richardson-Gaudin variables in an exact Woods-Saxon plus reduced BCS pairing framework. The integrability of the model sheds light on the pairing correlations, in particular on the previously reported sub-shell structure.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations in the even-even Sn isotopes from A=102 to 130, modeled with a Woods-Saxon plus reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian, reveal a sub-shell structure that is consistent with previous studies, but the calculated two-neutron separation energies are smoother than experimental values around mid-shell, indicating that the model overestimates pairing gaps in that region.

## Method

The authors solved the reduced BCS Hamiltonian exactly using Richardson-Gaudin (RG) integrability, with single-particle levels from a Woods-Saxon potential using a recent global parametrization. The pairing interaction strength was set to \(g = g_0 / \sqrt{A}\) to reproduce experimental three-point pairing gaps \(\Delta^{(3)}(A)\). The RG variables for the ground state of each even-even Sn isotope were plotted in the complex plane relative to the single-particle poles, and their collective behavior was quantified using a Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA) eigenmode decomposition.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energies \(S_{2n} = [BE(A) - BE(A-2)]\) were calculated for even-even Sn isotopes from A=102 to 130 and compared to experimental data from Audi (2003).
- Three-point pairing gaps \(\Delta^{(3)}(A) = (-)^A[BE(A) - 2BE(A-1) + BE(A-2)]\) were computed and compared to experiment; the model overestimates \(\Delta^{(3)}\) around mid-shell.
- Single-particle energies from the Woods-Saxon potential for \(^{100}\)Sn are: \(d_{5/2}\) at -11.164 MeV, \(g_{7/2}\) at -10.275 MeV, \(s_{1/2}\) at -9.124 MeV, \(d_{3/2}\) at -8.766 MeV, and \(h_{11/2}\) at -7.754 MeV.
- The number of active pairs \(N_p\) in each isotope is given by \(N_p = (A - 50)/2\), ranging from 1 pair for \(^{102}\)Sn to 15 pairs for \(^{130}\)Sn.
- The TDA decomposition shows that for lighter isotopes (e.g., \(^{102}\)Sn to \(^{116}\)Sn), pairs collectively occupy the \(d_{5/2}\) and \(g_{7/2}\) sub-shells; for heavier isotopes (e.g., \(^{118}\)Sn to \(^{130}\)Sn), the \(d_{5/2}\) and \(g_{7/2}\) sub-shells fill normally while additional pairs distribute over \(s_{1/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), and \(h_{11/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the use of Richardson-Gaudin integrability to study pairing correlations in Sn isotopes, building on the reduced BCS Hamiltonian framework of Richardson (1963, 1964) and Gaudin (1976). The results are consistent with the sub-shell structure previously reported by Morales (2011) to explain the decrease in \(B(E2: 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) strength around mid-shell observed by Jungclaus (2011). The authors expect that the sub-shell structure will also be reflected in transition rates, which they plan to investigate in future publications.
