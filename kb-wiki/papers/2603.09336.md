# Extracting freeze-out conditions in beam energy scan via functional QCD

> Fluctuations of conserved charges provide a link between high quality theoretical results and precision measurements of heavy ion collisions. We compare results for ratios of the lowest order baryon number susceptibilities from functional QCD approaches to proton number cumulants extracted from experiments. We find that they meet at a specific temperature and chemical potential for each collision energy. This is indicative of the respective freeze-out point. From this self-consistent determination of the freeze-out parameters we extract a prediction for the kurtosis on the freeze-out line. We find quantitative agreement with experimental data where available, despite comparing apples (baryons) with oranges (protons). At a collision energy around 5 GeV, our kurtosis exhibits a peak structure indicative of the critical end point of QCD.

## Key claim
The authors find that freeze-out temperatures and chemical potentials extracted by comparing functional QCD baryon-number susceptibilities with experimental proton-number cumulants agree with the chiral crossover line at high collision energies, and predict a kurtosis peak at around 5-6 GeV collision energy that may signal the QCD critical end point, but emphasize that the comparison is between baryons (theory) and protons (experiment) and that non-equilibrium effects remain to be included.

## Method
The authors compare ratios of the first two lowest-order baryon number susceptibilities (χ₂ᴮ/χ₁ᴮ and χ₃ᴮ/χ₁ᴮ) computed from functional QCD with corresponding cumulant ratios (C₂/C₁ and C₃/C₁) of net-proton numbers measured by the STAR collaboration in BES-I and BES-II. For each collision energy, the freeze-out temperature T_f and baryon chemical potential μ_{B,f} are determined by solving the matching equations simultaneously. The extracted freeze-out points are then fitted with a parametrized freeze-out line using a Padé form for μ_{B,f}(√s_NN) and a polynomial expansion for T_f(μ_{B,f}), constrained by the chiral crossover parameters T_c^χ(0)=157 MeV and κ₂^χ=0.0153 from functional QCD.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature at zero chemical potential: T₀ = 157 MeV, set equal to the chiral pseudo-critical temperature T_c^χ(0)
- Curvature coefficient of freeze-out line: κ₂^f = 0.0153, set equal to the chiral crossover curvature κ₂^χ
- Fourth-order curvature coefficient κ₄^f: mean value 0.96×10⁻³, with lower bound 1.47×10⁻³ and upper bound 0.73×10⁻³
- Fit parameters for μ_{B,f}(√s_NN) = a/(1+b√s_NN): mean a=953.7 MeV, b=0.2026 GeV⁻¹; lower a=913.9 MeV, b=0.1977 GeV⁻¹; upper a=1027.0 MeV, b=0.2143 GeV⁻¹
- Kurtosis peak location for upper bound: (T_f, μ_{B,f}) = (120, 506) MeV at √s_NN = 5 GeV
- Kurtosis peak location for lower bound: (T_f, μ_{B,f}) = (125, 432) MeV at √s_NN = 6 GeV
- Freeze-out extraction covers collision energies from √s_NN = 200 GeV down to approximately 3 GeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of comparing lattice QCD susceptibility ratios with experimental cumulants (Bazavov 2012, Borsanyi 2013, 2014) to large baryon chemical potential using functional QCD methods, which are the only first-principles approach currently capable of computing thermodynamic quantities at μ_B/T ≲ 4.5 (Fu 2019, Gao 2020, Gunkel 2021, Lu 2025). The resulting freeze-out curve is compared with statistical model extractions from particle yields (Andronic 2017, Sagun 2017) and shows agreement at high energies while revealing discrepancies below √s_NN ≈ 5 GeV that may indicate the onset of new phases such as a moat regime or inhomogeneous phases. The prediction of a kurtosis peak provides an equilibrium baseline that can be compared with future experimental data and non-equilibrium studies (Stephanov 2011, Mukherjee 2015) to further constrain the location of the QCD critical end point.
