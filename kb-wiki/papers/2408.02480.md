# Bayesian analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering data in pionless effective field theory

> We perform Bayesian model calibration of two-nucleon ($NN$) low-energy constants (LECs) appearing in an $NN$ interaction based on pionless effective field theory (EFT). The calibration is carried out for potentials constructed using naive dimensional analysis in $NN$ relative momenta ($p$) up to next-to-leading order [NLO, $O(p^2)$] and next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order [N3LO, $O(p^4)$]. We consider two classes of pionless EFT potential: one that acts in all partial waves and another that is dominated by $s$-wave physics. The two classes produce broadly similar results for calibrations to $NN$ data up to $E_{\rm lab}=5$ MeV. Our analysis accounts for the correlated uncertainties that arise from the truncation of the pionless EFT. We simultaneously estimate both the EFT LECs and the parameters that quantify the truncation error. This permits the first quantitative estimates of the pionless EFT breakdown scale, $Λ_b$: the 95% intervals are $Λ_b \in [50.11,63.03]$ MeV at NLO and $Λ_b \in [72.27, 88.54]$ MeV at N3LO. Invoking naive dimensional analysis for the $NN$ potential, therefore, does not lead to consistent results across orders in pionless EFT. This exemplifies the possible use of Bayesian tools to identify inconsistencies in a proposed EFT power counting.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative Bayesian estimates of the pionless EFT breakdown scale, finding that the 95% credible intervals for Lambda_b are [50.11, 63.03] MeV at NLO and [72.27, 88.54] MeV at N3LO, which are inconsistent with each other and with the expected value of the pion mass, indicating that naive dimensional analysis does not yield consistent results across orders in pionless EFT.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian model calibration of low-energy constants (LECs) for two classes of pionless EFT potentials (full partial-wave and s-wave-dominated) at NLO and N3LO orders, using coordinate-space Gaussian regulators with cutoffs of 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 fm. They simultaneously estimated both the EFT LECs and the parameters quantifying the truncation error by constructing a covariance matrix that accounts for correlated uncertainties from the EFT expansion truncation, using a geometric series model for the neglected higher-order terms. The calibration was carried out against nucleon-nucleon scattering data up to E_lab = 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The 95% credible interval for the pionless EFT breakdown scale Lambda_b at NLO is [50.11, 63.03] MeV
- The 95% credible interval for Lambda_b at N3LO is [72.27, 88.54] MeV
- The calibration was performed for nucleon-nucleon scattering data up to E_lab = 5 MeV
- Three coordinate-space Gaussian regulator cutoffs were used: R_s = 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 fm, corresponding to momentum-scale cutoffs Lambda_s = 2/R_s from about 263 MeV down to 158 MeV

## Context

This work extends the BUQEYE Bayesian uncertainty quantification framework, previously applied to chiral EFT models by Wesolowski (2018) and Wesolowski (2021), to pionless EFT for the first time. The analysis demonstrates that Bayesian tools can identify inconsistencies in a proposed EFT power counting scheme, specifically showing that naive dimensional analysis (Weinberg power counting) does not produce consistent breakdown scale estimates across orders in pionless EFT. This enables future applications of the same Bayesian framework to other power countings, including the standard pionless EFT power counting of Kaplan (1998) and van Kolck (1998), as well as to local chiral interactions developed by Piarulli (2014, 2016).
