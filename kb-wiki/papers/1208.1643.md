# Charm quark transport in Pb+Pb reactions at sqrt{s}_NN=2.76 TeV from a (3+1) dimensional hybrid approach

> We implement a Langevin approach for the transport of charm quarks in the UrQMD (hydrodynamics + Boltzmann) hybrid model. Due to the inclusion of event-by-event fluctuations and a full (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamic evolution, this approach provides a more realistic model for the evolution of the matter produced in heavy ion collisions as compared to simple homogeneous fireball expansions usually employed. As drag and diffusion coefficients we use a resonance approach for elastic heavy-quark scattering and assume a decoupling temperature of the charm quarks from the hot medium of 130 MeV. A coalescence approach at the decoupling temperature for the hadronization of the charm quarks to D-mesons is also included. We present calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_AA as well as the elliptic flow v_2 in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt s_NN=2.76 TeV. The comparison to ALICE measurements shows a very good agreement with our calculations.

## Key claim

The authors implement a Langevin approach for charm quark transport within a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid model (UrQMD + hydrodynamics) and find that their calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 for D-mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_NN}=2.76 TeV are compatible with ALICE measurements, though the agreement is qualified by the statement that new measurements at low p_T would be helpful for more detailed comparison.

## Method

The authors embed a relativistic Langevin equation for charm quark propagation into the UrQMD hybrid model, which combines a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution with a subsequent Boltzmann transport stage for freeze-out. Drag and diffusion coefficients are taken from a resonance approach for elastic heavy-quark scattering, and charm quarks are hadronized to D-mesons via a coalescence mechanism at a decoupling temperature of 130 MeV. Initial charm quark production is based on a Glauber approach with momentum distributions fitted to PYTHIA calculations, and the equation of state includes a smooth crossover between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma degrees of freedom consistent with lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Decoupling temperature for charm quarks from the hot medium: 130 MeV
- Charm quark mass used in the calculation: 1.5 GeV
- Light quark mass assumed for coalescence: 369 MeV, yielding D-meson mass of 1.869 GeV
- D-meson elliptic flow v_2 reaches a maximum of about 15% at p_T = 3 GeV in 30%-50% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_NN}=2.76 TeV
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA shows a maximum at about 2 GeV followed by a decline to about 0.2 at high p_T in 0%-20% centrality
- Rapidity cut used for analysis: |y| < 0.35
- Fitting function coefficients for initial charm quark p_T distribution: A_1 = 0.136, A_2 = 2.055, A_3 = 2.862

## Context

This work extends previous heavy-quark transport studies (e.g., Gossiaux (2012), Uphoff (2011), Rapp (2009), van Hees (2008), He (2011)) by replacing simple homogeneous fireball expansions with a full (3+1)-dimensional hybrid model that includes event-by-event initial-state fluctuations. The approach enables more realistic modeling of the medium evolution in heavy-ion collisions and provides a framework for extracting transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma from comparisons to experimental data from ALICE at the LHC.
