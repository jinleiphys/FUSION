# On Probabilities of E2 Transitions between Positive-Parity States in ^160Dy Nucleus

> Reduced probabilities B(E2) of γtransitions between states of positive parity in the ^160Dy nucleus are calculated within the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM-1). The results are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The interacting boson model (IBM-1) reproduces the measured reduced E2 transition probabilities B(E2) for positive-parity states in ^160Dy, but the agreement is used to infer that transitions with large discrepancies between calculated and experimental B(E2) values are likely not of E2 type, while those with only slight disagreement might be E2, pending experimental confirmation.

## Method
Experimental B(E2) values were extracted from measured γ-ray intensities, level half-lives, and transition energies using the standard formula relating B(E2) to the partial decay width. The IBM-1 Hamiltonian and E2 transition operator were fitted to two measured absolute B(E2) values (for the 2₂⁺→0₁⁺ and 2₁⁺→0₁⁺ transitions) to determine the parameters α and β. For levels with unknown half-lives, theoretical IBM-1 B(E2) values were used as a reference to estimate experimental B(E2) values for competing E2 transitions via intensity and energy scaling.

## Key numbers
- B(E2, 2₂⁺→0₁⁺) = 4.43(34) W.u. (Weisskopf units), used to fix the IBM-1 parameter α = 1.914.
- B(E2, 2₁⁺→0₁⁺) = 192.6(88) W.u., used to fix the IBM-1 parameter β = 1.515.
- The experimental data set includes over 100 new levels and over 500 new γ transitions, with about 150 multipolarities established for the first time.
- Only 0.9% of the total γ intensity from the ^160m,gHo→^160Dy decay remains unplaced.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous theoretical description of energy levels in ^160Dy (Adam et al., 2004) by now calculating transition probabilities within the same IBM-1 framework. It uses new experimental data from the ^160Er→^160Ho→^160Dy decay chain reported in a companion paper (reference [1] in the text). The comparison between calculated and measured B(E2) values provides a test of the IBM-1 for this deformed nucleus and suggests which unassigned transitions may be of E2 character, guiding future experimental multipolarity assignments.
