# Origins of collectivity in small systems

> We review recent developments in the theoretical description and understanding of multi-particle correlation measurements in collisions of small projectiles (p/d/$^3$He) with heavy nuclei (Au, Pb) as well as proton+proton collisions. We focus on whether the physical processes responsible for the observed long range rapidity correlations and their azimuthal structure are the same in small systems as in heavy ion collisions. In the latter they are interpreted as generated by the initial spatial geometry being transformed into momentum correlations by strong final state interactions. However, explicit calculations show that also initial state momentum correlations are present and could contribute to observables in small systems. If strong final state interactions are present in small systems, recent developments show that results are sensitive to the shape of the proton and its fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed long-range azimuthal correlations in small collision systems (p/d/³He with Au/Pb, and p+p) could originate either from strong final-state interactions (hydrodynamic flow) converting initial spatial geometry into momentum correlations, or from intrinsic initial-state momentum correlations produced in the color glass condensate framework; it remains unclear which mechanism dominates and at what multiplicities, with the hedge that "if strong final state interactions are present in small systems, recent developments show that results are sensitive to the shape of the proton and its fluctuations."

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations comparing two frameworks: (1) relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (e.g., IP-Glasma+Music) and transport simulations (e.g., AMPT parton cascade) that convert initial spatial geometry into final-state momentum anisotropies, and (2) color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory calculations (glasma graph approximation, non-linear Gaussian approximation, and fully numerical Yang-Mills solutions) that produce intrinsic initial-state momentum correlations. The review examines how subnucleonic fluctuations of the proton structure, constrained by HERA data on incoherent diffractive vector meson production, affect predictions for anisotropic flow coefficients in p+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- A lower limit of ~0.15 fm for the size of a droplet of fluid nuclear matter was extracted from studies of hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes (Romatschke 2016).
- When fluctuating protons (with hot spots around three valence quarks) are used in IP-Glasma calculations of p+Pb collisions, anisotropic flow coefficients increase by up to a factor of 5, becoming compatible with experimental data (Mantysaari 2017).
- In the IP-Glasma+Music framework, predictions for v₃ in d+Au collisions (significantly smaller than in ³He+Au collisions) showed good agreement with preliminary PHENIX data (McGlinchey 2017).
- For 0-5% central p+Pb collisions, thermal photons from a close-to-thermal medium were predicted to produce up to a factor of two enhancement of the direct photon yield, depending on transverse photon momentum (Shen 2016).
- In minimum bias p+Pb collisions, the nuclear modification factor R_pA was found to be consistent with one even when jet energy loss is included; significant suppression is predicted only for 0-1% central events (Shen 2016).

## Context

This review extends the long-standing interpretation of azimuthal correlations in heavy ion collisions as arising from hydrodynamic response to initial geometry (Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) to the question of whether the same physics applies in small systems. It contrasts the hydrodynamic/final-state interaction picture with initial-state momentum correlations from the color glass condensate framework (Dusling 2015, Schlichting 2016). The work builds on recent developments in understanding subnucleonic fluctuations from HERA data (Mantysaari 2016) and Bayesian analyses of initial conditions (Bernhard 2016, Moreland 2017). The review identifies key observables (mass ordering, sign change of four-particle cumulant, multi-particle cumulants, odd harmonics, different collision systems, jets and electromagnetic probes) that could distinguish between the two scenarios, and notes that no complete calculation yet describes the entire multiplicity dependence of the four-particle cumulant in either framework.
