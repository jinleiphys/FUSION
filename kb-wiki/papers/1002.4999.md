# Directed flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the generation of directed flow in the hydrodynamic expansion of the hot matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at 200GeV. The experimentally observed negative directed flow in a wide range of central pseudorapidities isreproduced assuming that the fireball is tilted away from the collision axis. The tilt of the source is consistent with a preferential emission in the forward/backward hemisphere from forward/backward participating nucleons. The model reproduces the experimentally observed scaling of the directed flow when going from Au-Au to Cu-Cu systems.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed negative directed flow (anti-flow) in a wide range of central pseudorapidities at √s=200 GeV is reproduced by assuming the fireball is tilted away from the collision axis, with the tilt arising from preferential emission in the forward/backward hemisphere from forward/backward participating nucleons; the model reproduces the scaling of directed flow when going from Au-Au to Cu-Cu systems, but the large anti-flow at large pseudorapidities in the fragmentation region is not reproduced.

## Method

The authors perform 3+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations of the fireball expansion at √s=200 GeV, starting from two different initial condition models: shifted initial conditions (Hirano-Tsuda type) and tilted initial conditions where the energy density is constructed as a sum of contributions from forward and backward going participant nucleons with asymmetric emission profiles. The hydrodynamic evolution is continued until freeze-out at T_F=150 MeV, with particle emission and resonance decays handled by the THERMINATOR event generator.

## Key numbers

- Initial time τ0 = 0.25 fm/c for the main calculation, with a comparison calculation using τ0 = 1 fm/c
- Energy density normalization ε0 = 107 GeV/fm⁻³ for Au-Au and ε0 = 65 GeV/fm⁻³ for Cu-Cu collisions
- Freeze-out temperature T_F = 150 MeV
- Initial longitudinal profile uses a plateau of width 2η0 = 2.0 and ση = 1.3
- The range of rapidity correlations ηm ≃ yb − 2 ≃ 3.36 at √s=200 GeV
- Directed flow is built up over the first 3 fm/c of expansion
- Binary collision contribution α = 0.145 in the Glauber model density

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic calculations of collective flow at RHIC energies, addressing the directed flow (v1) which had been measured by PHOBOS (Back 2005) and STAR (Abelev 2008) showing negative values at central pseudorapidities. Prior transport models (Bleicher 2000, Chen 2009) generally underpredicted the anti-flow at RHIC energies, while earlier hydrodynamic calculations (Andrade 2008) gave the correct sign but too small magnitude. The shifted initial conditions of Hirano (2002) produce a positive directed flow at central rapidities, inconsistent with data, whereas the proposed tilted source reproduces the observed negative flow and the system-size scaling between Au-Au and Cu-Cu. The work enables further studies of how initial-state geometry and longitudinal dynamics generate odd-flow harmonics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
