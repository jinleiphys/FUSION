# Mixture of quark and gluon fluids described in terms of anisotropic hydrodynamics

> A system of equations of anisotropic hydrodynamics that describes mixture of quark and gluon fluids is studied. The equations are based on the zeroth, first, and second moments of the RTA kinetic equations. Tests of this formulation are performed by comparing the results of anisotropic hydorodynamics with the exact solutions of the Boltzmann equations for a mixture of fluids in the Bjorken flow limit. One finds a very good agreement between the hydrodynamic and kinetic-theory results.

# Mixture of quark and gluon fluids described in terms of anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Anisotropic hydrodynamics, when formulated using the zeroth, first, and second moments of the relaxation time approximation (RTA) kinetic equations, achieves very good agreement with exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation for a mixture of quark and gluon fluids in the Bjorken flow limit, in contrast to earlier mixture studies that used only the zeroth and first moments and found poorer agreement.

## Method

The author derives a closed system of anisotropic hydrodynamics equations for a mixture of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons by taking the zeroth, first, and second moments of the RTA kinetic equations, using Romatschke-Strickland form distribution functions with separate anisotropy parameters and transverse-momentum scales for quarks and gluons. The equations are solved numerically for (0+1)D Bjorken flow with constant relaxation time, and the results are compared to exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation for the same mixture system obtained in prior work by Florkowski (2014).

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time: τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c; evolution continued to τ = 10 fm/c
- Relaxation time: τ_eq = 0.25 fm/c (constant)
- Initial transverse-momentum parameters for both quarks and gluons: Λ_q(τ₀) = Λ_g(τ₀) = 1 GeV
- Ratio of quark to gluon internal degrees of freedom: r̃ = 2/3
- The ratio of total transverse pressure to total energy density, P_T/ε, approaches 1/3 at late times (thermodynamic equilibrium)

## Context

This work extends anisotropic hydrodynamics from simple single-fluid systems to mixtures of quark and gluon fluids, addressing a shortcoming identified in earlier mixture studies by Florkowski (2012, 2014) that used only the zeroth and first moments and did not achieve good agreement with kinetic theory. The inclusion of the second moment equations, following the method of Tinti (2013) and Florkowski (2014), enables accurate description of systems with both initially oblate-oblate and prolate-prolate momentum-space anisotropies. This enables more realistic modeling of the early-stage evolution of matter produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where large flow gradients and fast longitudinal expansion produce large pressure corrections beyond the regime of traditional viscous hydrodynamics.
