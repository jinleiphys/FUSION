# Heavy-flavor meson and baryon production in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We present a new model for the description of heavy-flavor hadronization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We explore its effect on the charmed hadron yields and kinematic distributions once the latter is applied at the end of transport calculations used to simulate the propagation of heavy quarks in the deconfined fireball produced in the collision. The model is based on the formation of color-singlet clusters through the recombination of charm quarks with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell. This local mechanism of color neutralization leads to a strong space-momentum correlation, which provides a substantial enhancement of charmed baryon production and of the collective flow of all charmed hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hadronization model based on local color-singlet cluster formation via recombination of charm quarks with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell can explain the observed enhancement of charmed baryon production in heavy-ion collisions, but only when space-momentum correlation is preserved; without this correlation, the baryon-to-meson ratio reverts to the value seen in electron-positron collisions.

## Method

The authors simulate charm quark propagation through a deconfined fireball using a transport calculation with hard-thermal-loop (HTL) transport coefficients. At the hadronization hypersurface at temperature T_H = 155 MeV, each charm quark recombines with a light antiquark or diquark sampled from a thermal distribution in the local rest frame of the same fluid cell, forming a color-singlet cluster. Clusters with invariant mass below approximately 4 GeV undergo isotropic two-body decay into a charmed hadron plus a soft particle, while heavier clusters are fragmented as Lund strings.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature: T_H = 155 MeV
- Cluster mass threshold for two-body decay: M_max ≈ 4 GeV
- In central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the model reproduces the Lambda_c+/D0 ratio enhancement at intermediate pT, compared to the e+e- baseline value of Lambda_c+/D0 ≈ 0.1
- Without space-momentum correlation, the Lambda_c+/D0 ratio drops to approximately 0.1, matching the e+e- value
- Results are shown for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV compared to STAR data

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy-flavor hadronization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard approach of using fragmentation fractions tuned to electron-positron data, which cannot explain the charmed baryon enhancement observed by ALICE (2021) and STAR (2019). The model builds on cluster hadronization concepts from Herwig (Webber, 1983) and Lund string fragmentation (Andersson, 1983), but introduces local recombination from the same fluid cell to produce space-momentum correlation. The results enable more accurate extraction of heavy-quark transport coefficients by reducing systematic uncertainties from hadronization, and provide a framework for predicting charmed hadron yields and collective flow in future heavy-ion experiments.
