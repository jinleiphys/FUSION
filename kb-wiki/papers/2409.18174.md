# Tracking Energy Loss in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The main modification to high $p_t$ jets evolving in the quark gluon plasma is the depletion of their energy to the thermal background. Despite the prominent role played by energy loss effects in jet quenching, their theoretical description is still limited, being, to a large extent, driven by phenomenological considerations. As the field moves towards the measurement of multi-scale jet substructure observables, it is necessary to develop new field theoretic techniques to describe energy loss. In this letter, we highlight a formal similarity between energy loss, and the renormalization group based track function formalism for computing jet substructure observables on charged particles. We introduce an energy loss function, $L_i(x)$, tracking the energy fraction $x$ flowing from an initial hard parton $i$ down to the measured hadrons, and show that its renormalization group evolution can be studied in perturbative QCD, and is related to that of the track functions. The features of this new approach are illustrated in the computation of projected energy correlators, opening the window to a better theoretical treatment of these observables in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The authors introduce a new non-perturbative energy loss function (ELF) that formally resembles the track function formalism, arguing that medium-induced energy loss can be studied using perturbative QCD renormalization group evolution rather than relying solely on phenomenological quenching weights. The approach is presented as a proof-of-concept illustration for projected energy correlators, not as a complete medium model.

## Method
The authors define an energy loss function \(L_i(x)\) that gives the probability for an initial parton \(i\) with energy \(p_t\) to produce hadrons carrying \(x p_t\) of the total momentum. They show that this function satisfies the same leading-order renormalization group evolution equations as track functions, involving QCD splitting functions. The formalism is applied to compute the first moments of the energy loss function (giving average energy loss evolution) and to construct a jet energy loss function \(\mathbb{L}_i(x,p_t,R)\) that describes the probability for a jet of radius \(R\) to retain energy fraction \(x\). The approach is illustrated by computing projected energy correlator (PENC) cumulative distributions in an idealized \(e^+e^-\) collision with a thermal background.

## Key numbers
- The leading one-loop terms for the quark jet function in the PENC at order \(n=2\) are \(J_q^{[2],1} = \frac{37}{12} L_g[1]L_q[1]\), where \(L_i[n]\) are moments of the ELF.
- The leading one-loop terms for the quark jet function at order \(n=3\) are \(J_q^{[3],1} = \frac{611}{1200} L_g[2]L_q[1] + \frac{541}{300} L_g[1]L_q[2]\).
- For comparison, the quenching weight approximation gives \(J_q^{Q,[2],1} = \frac{37}{12} Q_g Q_q\) and \(J_q^{Q,[3],1} = \frac{249}{48} Q_g Q_q\), showing disagreement for \(n>2\).
- The mismatch between the ELF and quenching weight approaches appears at order \(\alpha_s^k\) for terms where \(k>n\).

## Context
This work extends the track function formalism developed by Chang (2013) and later advanced by Lee (2023) and Jaarsma (2022) for computing jet substructure observables on charged particles. It contrasts with the traditional quenching weight approach of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller and Schiff (2001), which relates medium-modified cross sections to vacuum ones via a simple energy shift. The ELF formalism enables systematic computation of energy loss effects in multi-scale jet substructure observables such as energy correlators, which have recently been measured in heavy ion collisions by CMS (2024). The authors note that the approach can be extended to higher-twist operators describing correlations in energy loss, analogous to multiparton track functions.
