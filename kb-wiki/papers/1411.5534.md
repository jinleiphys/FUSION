# Impact of the initial size of spatial fluctuations on the collective flow in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV

> The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model is used to study the influence of the initial size of spatial fluctuations of the interacting system on flow observables in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV for different centralities. While the flow coefficients $v_2$, $v_3$, $v_4$ and $v_5$ are reasonably described in comparison to the data from the ALICE Collaboration for different centralities within the default setting, no essential sensitivity is found with respect to the initial size of spatial fluctuations even for very central collisions where the flow coefficients are dominated by the size of initial state fluctuations. We attribute this lack of sensitivity partly to the low interaction rate of the degrees-of-freedom in this very early phase of order $\sim$ 0.3 fm/c which is also in common with the weakly interacting color glass condensate (CGC) or glasma approach. Moreover, since the event shape in the transverse plane is approximately the same for different size of spatial fluctuations very similar eccentricities $ε_n$ are transformed to roughly the same flow coefficients $v_n$ in momentum space.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model finds no essential sensitivity of the flow coefficients \(v_2\), \(v_3\), \(v_4\) and \(v_5\) to the initial size of spatial fluctuations in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 TeV, even for very central collisions where flow is dominated by initial-state fluctuations. This lack of sensitivity is attributed partly to the low interaction rate of the degrees-of-freedom in the very early phase (of order \(\sim\) 0.3 fm/c) and partly to the fact that the event shape in the transverse plane remains approximately the same for different fluctuation sizes, so similar eccentricities \(\epsilon_n\) are transformed to roughly the same flow coefficients \(v_n\).

## Method

The Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport model, upgraded with PYTHIA 6.4 for LHC energies, was used to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 TeV. To probe the effect of initial spatial fluctuations, the authors artificially increased the size of spatial fluctuations by identifying clusters of partons at \(t \approx 3.5 \times 10^{-3}\) fm/c and scaling the transverse position vectors of partons within each cluster by a factor of 1/3, keeping total energy and momentum unchanged. Flow coefficients \(v_n\{2\}\) were computed using the two-particle cumulant method in the pseudorapidity window \(|\eta| < 0.8\) for very central (\(b=0\)) collisions and compared between the default (Monte Carlo Glauber) and enhanced-fluctuation initial conditions.

## Key numbers

*   The partonic interaction rate in PHSD for central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 TeV is very low for about 0.2 fm/c after contact, with the early phase lasting of order \(\sim\) 0.3 fm/c.
*   The default grid resolution used for the study was \(\Delta_x\) = 0.3 fm, with parton distributions smeared by a Gaussian of width \(\sigma\) from 0.2 fm to 1 fm.
*   The flow coefficients \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) as a function of \(p_T\) and \(\eta\) for \(b=0\) collisions showed no apparent sensitivity to the increased size of spatial fluctuations within statistical accuracy up to \(p_T \approx\) 2 GeV/c.
*   Artificially decreasing or increasing the parton formation time \(\tau_f\) by a factor of two did not change the flow coefficients \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) within error bars up to \(p_T \approx\) 2 GeV/c for 30-40% central collisions.

## Context

This study extends the application of the PHSD transport model to LHC energies, following earlier work at RHIC and SPS energies (Cassing and Bratkovskaya, 2009; Konchakovski et al., 2014). It directly addresses the question of whether initial-state fluctuations leave traces in collective flow, a topic previously explored in ideal and viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Schenke et al. (2012, 2013) and Werner et al., which found some sensitivity for higher moments \(v_n\). The PHSD results contradict a strong sensitivity to fluctuation size, suggesting that the low interaction rate in the early phase (common with the color glass condensate or glasma approach) and the preservation of event shape wash out such effects, and that the pre-hydro degrees-of-freedom cannot be disentangled by flow observables alone.
