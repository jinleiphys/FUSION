# J/psi transport in QGP and Pt distribution at SPS and RHIC

> Combining the hydrodynamic equations for the QGP evolution and the transport equation for the primordially produced $J/ψ$ in the QGP, we investigate the $J/ψ$ transverse momentum distribution as well as its suppression in the $\sqrt{s}$=17.3A GeV Pb-Pb collisions at SPS and $\sqrt{s}$=200A GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. The two sets of equations are connected by the $J/ψ$ anomalous suppression induced by its inelastic scattering with gluons in the QGP. The calculated centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression and average transverse momentum square agree well with the SPS data. From the comparison with the coalescence model where charm quark is fully thermalized, our calculated elliptical flow of the primordially produced $J/ψ$ is much smaller. This may be helpful to differentiate the $J/ψ$ production mechanisms in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

By combining hydrodynamic QGP evolution with a transport equation for primordially produced J/psi, the authors find that the leakage effect (where high-pt J/psi escape the QGP faster) and the pt-dependence of the gluon dissociation cross section are essential to explain the experimental pt broadening at SPS, and that the calculated elliptic flow of primordially produced J/psi is much smaller than that predicted by coalescence models with fully thermalized charm quarks.

## Method

The authors coupled 2+1 dimensional boost-invariant relativistic hydrodynamics (Bjorken model) for the QGP evolution with a classical Boltzmann-type transport equation for the transverse phase-space distribution of primordially produced J/psi. The two sets of equations were connected via the anomalous suppression term from inelastic gluon dissociation (g + J/psi -> c + cbar), using perturbative cross sections with non-relativistic Coulomb potential approximation. The initial condition for the transport equation was set by normal suppression from nuclear absorption (sigma_abs = 4.3 mb at SPS, 0 at RHIC) and Cronin pt broadening, and the hydrodynamics was initialized with a critical entropy density parameter s_c = 31.7 fm^{-3} fitted to SPS suppression data.

## Key numbers

- At SPS (sqrt(s) = 17.3A GeV Pb-Pb): thermalization proper time tau_0 = 0.8 fm/c; critical entropy density s_c = 31.7 fm^{-3}; normal suppression cross section sigma_abs = 4.3 mb for J/psi and chi_c; nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section sigma_NN = 32 mb; average transverse momentum square in NN collisions <pt^2>_NN = 1.15 GeV^2/c^2; gluon-nucleon broadening constant a_gN = 0.076 GeV^2/c^2 fm^{-1}; anomalous suppression onset at transverse energy E_t = 23 GeV.
- At RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200A GeV Au-Au): thermalization proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c; sigma_abs = 0; sigma_NN = 41 mb; <pt^2>_NN = 2.70 GeV^2/c^2; entropy per baryon = 250; 75% of entropy density from soft (wounded nucleon) and 25% from hard (binary collision) contributions.
- J/psi mass m_psi = 3.1 GeV; charm quark mass m_c = 1.87 GeV; chi_c mass = 3.5 GeV; psi' mass = 3.7 GeV; critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV at vanishing baryon density; bag parameter B^{1/4} = 236 MeV; mean field repulsion K = 450 MeV fm^3; freeze-out energy density = 60 MeV/fm^3.
- At SPS, the calculated <pt^2> increases steadily with centrality up to E_t = 90 GeV, then saturates and drops slightly in most central collisions.
- At RHIC, the maximal J/psi v_2 (at impact parameter b = 7.8 fm and p_t = 3 GeV/c) is less than 10% of the minimum-bias v_2 predicted by the coalescence model with full charm quark thermalization.

## Context

This work extends the transport approach of Xu and Wang (2002) and Polleri et al. (2002) for J/psi anomalous suppression via gluon dissociation in QGP, and the hydrodynamic framework of Kolb et al. (2001) for RHIC initial conditions. It directly contradicts predictions from Kharzeev et al. (2000) and Chaudhuri (2003) that used pt-independent dissociation without leakage effects. The calculated elliptic flow provides a benchmark to differentiate between primordial pQCD production (this work) and coalescence models (Braun-Munzinger and Stachel, 2000; Gorenstein et al., 2001) where charm quarks are fully thermalized, enabling future experimental discrimination of J/psi production mechanisms at RHIC.
