# Atomic effects in astrophysical nuclear reactions

> Two models are presented for the description of the electron screening effects that appear in laboratory nuclear reactions at astrophysical energies. The two-electron screening energy of the first model agrees very well with the recent LUNA experimental result for the break-up reaction $% He3(He3,2p)He^{4}$, which so far defies all available theoretical models. Moreover, multi-electron effects that enhance laboratory reactions of the CNO cycle and other advanced nuclear burning stages, are also studied by means of the Thomas-Fermi model, deriving analytical formulae that establish a lower and upper limit for the associated screening energy. The results of the second model, which show a very satisfactory compatibility with the adiabatic approximation ones, are expected to be particularly useful in future experiments for a more accurate determination of the CNO astrophysical factors.

## Key claim

The paper presents two models for electron screening in laboratory nuclear reactions at astrophysical energies; the first model reproduces the anomalously high screening energy for the ³He(³He,2p)⁴He reaction measured by LUNA, which had previously defied all theoretical predictions, while the second model provides analytical formulas for multi-electron screening in CNO cycle reactions that bracket the screening energy between a sudden limit and an adiabatic limit.

## Method

The first model treats two-electron screening using a Hartree-Fock approximation, deriving the screened Coulomb potential from Poisson's equation for hydrogen-like atoms and then modifying it for two-electron configurations, with the penetration factor calculated via the WKB method. The second model employs the Thomas-Fermi (TF) model with Tietz's analytic approximation for the screening function, expanding the screened potential to obtain a constant screening energy shift, and derives separate formulas for the sudden limit (SL) and adiabatic limit (AL) of the screening energy.

## Key numbers

- Screening energy for ³He(³He,2p)⁴He: model gives U_e = -338 eV, compared to LUNA experimental result U_e^{ex} = -294 ± 47 eV
- For ²H(d,p)³H with neutral projectile: U_e = -63 eV (compared to -54 eV for bare projectile deuteron)
- Electron-electron repulsion energy for two-electron case: V_ee = +29 eV
- TF sudden limit screening energy formula: U_TF^{SL} = -1.21 Z_1^{4/3} Z_2 e²/a_0
- TF adiabatic limit screening energy formula: U_TF^{AL} = -20.93 [(Z_1+Z_2)^{7/3} - Z_1^{7/3} - Z_2^{7/3}] eV
- For p+¹²C: U_TF^{SL} = 359 eV, U_TF^{AL} = 592 eV; for p+¹⁴N: U_TF^{SL} = 441 eV, U_TF^{AL} = 717 eV
- For ¹²C(p,γ)¹³N at 138 keV: screening enhancement between 2% and 3.4%
- For ¹⁴N(p,γ)¹⁵O at 93 keV: error from neglecting screening up to 9% (AL)
- For ¹⁷O(p,α)¹⁴N at 66 keV resonance: correction of order 12% (AL)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous model for hydrogen-like atoms (Liolios, 2000) to two-electron and multi-electron systems, and directly addresses the discrepancy between the LUNA experimental result for ³He(³He,2p)⁴He (Bonetti et al., 1999) and all prior theoretical models including the adiabatic limit (Assenbaum, 1987; Bracci, 1990). The Thomas-Fermi model provides analytical formulas that bracket the screening energy between the sudden and adiabatic limits, agreeing with the adiabatic approximation of Assenbaum (1987) and enabling more accurate extrapolation of CNO cycle astrophysical factors to zero energy in future low-energy experiments.
