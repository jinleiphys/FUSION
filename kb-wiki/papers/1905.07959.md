# System size and Multiplicity dependence of Chemical freeze-out parameters at the Large Hadron Collider Energies

> The collision system and multiplicity dependence of chemical freeze-out temperature ($T_{\rm ch}$) and strangeness saturation factor ($γ_{s}$) are obtained by studying the particle ratios at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. Here, we consider the new results in pp at 13 TeV, p+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV, Xe+Xe at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV along with the earlier results in pp at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. A statistical thermal model is used to extract the chemical freeze-out parameters in different multiplicity classes. To understand the particle production from small to large collision systems two ensembles namely, canonical and grand canonical have been considered in this study. A clear observation of multiplicity dependence of $T_{\rm ch}$ and $γ_{s}$ is observed. The values obtained in high-multiplicity pp collisions are found to be similar to the peripheral Pb+Pb collisions. A final state midrapidity charged particle multiplicity density of around 20-30 appears to be a threshold below which, the chemical freeze-out temperature is lower than the kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature shows a weak dependence on final-state charged-particle multiplicity across collision systems, while the strangeness saturation factor exhibits a clear evolution with multiplicity; a threshold of midrapidity charged-particle multiplicity density around 20-30 is identified below which the chemical freeze-out temperature is lower than the kinetic freeze-out temperature, contrary to the conventional ordering.

## Method

The authors used the THERMUS statistical thermal model to fit six particle ratios (K/pi, p/pi, Ks0/pi, Lambda/pi, phi/pi, Xi/pi) in both grand-canonical and canonical ensembles, extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature (Tch) and strangeness saturation factor (gamma_s) for each multiplicity or centrality class. The analysis covered pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 and 13 TeV, p+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, Xe+Xe at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.44 TeV, and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, with Xe+Xe using only three ratios due to data availability.

## Key numbers

* Chemical freeze-out temperature in the highest multiplicity class of pp at 13 TeV: Tch = 0.1579 +/- 0.0023 GeV (grand-canonical ensemble, V0M1 class).
* Strangeness saturation factor in the highest multiplicity class of pp at 13 TeV: gamma_s = 0.8209 +/- 0.0231 (grand-canonical ensemble, V0M1 class).
* Chemical freeze-out temperature for 0-5% central Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV: Tch = 0.1491 +/- 0.0021 GeV (grand-canonical ensemble).
* Strangeness saturation factor for 0-5% central Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV: gamma_s = 0.9777 +/- 0.0346 (grand-canonical ensemble).
* Chemical freeze-out temperature for 0-5% central Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV: Tch = 0.1403 +/- 0.0036 GeV (grand-canonical ensemble).
* Strangeness saturation factor for 0-5% central Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV: gamma_s = 0.8341 +/- 0.0639 (grand-canonical ensemble).
* Chemical freeze-out temperature for 0-5% central p+Pb at 5.02 TeV: Tch = 0.1518 +/- 0.0018 GeV (grand-canonical ensemble).
* Threshold multiplicity density for chemical freeze-out temperature to exceed kinetic freeze-out temperature: <dNch/deta>_{|eta|<0.5} approximately 20-30.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis of Sharma (2018) to higher LHC energies and additional collision systems (pp at 13 TeV, p+Pb at 5.02 TeV, Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV, Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV), comparing canonical and grand-canonical ensembles across system sizes. The results confirm the multiplicity-dependent strangeness enhancement observed by ALICE (2017) in pp collisions and show that high-multiplicity pp approaches but does not fully reach the strangeness chemical equilibrium seen in central Pb+Pb collisions. The threshold multiplicity around 20-30 for the ordering of chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures connects to earlier observations of a thermodynamic limit for ensemble equivalence and multipartonic interaction thresholds in pp collisions (Thakur, 2017).
