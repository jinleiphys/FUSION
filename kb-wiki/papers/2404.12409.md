# Exact unified tetraquark equations

> Recently we formulated covariant equations describing the tetraquark in terms of an admixture of two-body states $D\bar D$ (diquark-antidiquark), $MM$ (meson-meson), and three-body-like states where two of the quarks are spectators while the other two are interacting [Phys. Rev. D 107, 094014 (2023)]. A feature of these equations is that they unify descriptions of seemingly unrelated models of the tetraquark, like, for example, the $D\bar D$ model of the Moscow group [Faustov et al., Universe 7, 94 (2021)] and the coupled channel $D \bar D-MM$ model of the Giessen group [Heupel et al., Phys. Lett. B718, 545 (2012)]. Here we extend these equations to the exact case where $q\bar{q}$ annihilation is incorporated explicitly, and all previously neglected terms (three-body forces, non-pole contributions to two-quark t matrices, etc.) are taken into account through the inclusion of a single $q\bar{q}$ potential $Δ$.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously formulated "unified tetraquark equations" to the exact case by incorporating quark-antiquark annihilation explicitly and accounting for all previously neglected terms (three-body forces, non-pole contributions to two-quark t matrices, etc.) through the inclusion of a single quark-antiquark potential Delta, thereby making the equations formally exact rather than approximate.

## Method

The authors apply the Khvedelidze-Kvinikhidze (KK) exact covariant four-body equations to the 2-quark-2-antiquark system, retaining the first two terms of the T_aa' expression that the Giessen group had neglected. They then extend this formalism by introducing a quark-antiquark amplitude Delta that absorbs all corrections needed to restore exactness, including quark-antiquark annihilation, three-body forces, and non-pole contributions to two-body t matrices. The resulting equations are expressed in matrix form as phi = [(V^(0) + V^(1) + ...) + W] D phi, where phi is a rank-3 column of tetraquark form factors (meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and quark-antiquark), and D is a diagonal matrix of the corresponding propagators.

## Key numbers

* The unified tetraquark equations were previously formulated in Phys. Rev. D 107, 094014 (2023), and the present work extends them to the exact case.
* The Giessen group's model (Heupel et al., Phys. Lett. B718, 545 (2012)) corresponds to keeping only the V^(0) term in the kernel: phi = V^(0) D phi.
* The Moscow group's model (Faustov et al., Universe 7, 94 (2021)) corresponds to keeping only the V^(1) term in the kernel: phi = V^(1) D phi, with all but the D-antid-D to D-antid-D elements set to zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unified tetraquark equations (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 2023) which showed that the diquark-antidiquark model of the Moscow group (Faustov et al., 2021) and the coupled channel D-antid-MM model of the Giessen group (Heupel et al., 2012) are two non-overlapping aspects of the same underlying formalism. The present paper makes these equations exact by incorporating quark-antiquark annihilation and all previously neglected contributions through a single correction amplitude Delta, following the same approach the authors demonstrated earlier for the case where the first two terms of T_aa' were neglected (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 2021). This enables future exact numerical treatments of tetraquark bound states that unify both the meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark channels with full field-theoretic rigor.
