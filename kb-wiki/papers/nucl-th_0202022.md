# The role of interaction vertices in bound state calculations

> In recent studies of the one and two-body Greens' function for scalar interactions it was shown that crossed ladder and ``crossed rainbow'' (for the one-body case) exchanges play a crucial role in nonperturbative dynamics. In this letter we use exact analytical and numerical results to show that the contribution of vertex dressings to the two-body bound state mass for scalar QED are cancelled by the self-energy and wavefunction normalization. This proves, for the first time, that the mass of a two-body bound state given by the full theory can in a very good approximation be obtained by summing only ladder and crossed ladder diagrams using a bare vertex and a constant dressed mass. We also discuss the implications of the remarkable cancellation between rainbow and crossed rainbow diagrams that is a feature of one-body calculations.

# The role of interaction vertices in bound state calculations

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for scalar QED (SQED) in the quenched approximation, the exact two-body bound state mass can be reproduced to very good accuracy by using only generalized ladder diagrams with a bare vertex and a constant dressed mass, because vertex dressings are cancelled by self-energy and wavefunction normalization effects. This result is rigorous for SQED but only suggestive for more general theories with spin or internal symmetries.

## Method
The authors use the Feynman-Schwinger Representation (FSR) to compute exact bound state masses for two scalar particles in scalar QED, including all self-energy, vertex, and crossed ladder contributions while neglecting only charge loop contributions (quenched approximation). They first obtain exact analytic results in 0+1 dimensions, then extend the analysis to 3+1 dimensions using numerical path integral methods with Pauli-Villars regularization. The full two-body result \(M_2^{\rm tot}\) is compared to a calculation \(M_2^{\rm exch}\) that uses dressed one-body masses but only sums generalized ladder exchanges with the bare vertex.

## Key numbers
- In 0+1 dimensions, the exact \(n\)-body dressed mass is \(M_n = n m - n(n-2) \frac{e^2}{2\mu^2}\), where \(m\) is the bare mass, \(e\) the coupling, and \(\mu\) the gauge field mass.
- For the two-body case in 0+1 dimensions, the analytic result gives \(M_2/m = 2\) (shown in Fig. 1).
- In 3+1 dimensions, numerical calculations use parameters \(\mu/m = 0.15\), \(\Lambda_1/\mu = 3\), and \(\Lambda_2/\mu = 5\) for the Pauli-Villars regulators.
- The numerical results show that \(M_2^{\rm exch} = M_2^{\rm tot}\) to within errors (Fig. 2).

## Context
This work extends prior studies showing that crossed ladder exchanges make significant contributions to two-body binding energies in scalar theories (Tjon and collaborators, 1990s) and that crossed rainbow diagrams contribute substantially to one-body dressed masses (Savkli, 2000). The paper provides the first demonstration that the full bound state result from a Lagrangian can be approximated by summing only generalized ladder diagrams with a bare vertex and constant dressed mass, reducing the problem to solving bound state equations such as those developed by Gross (1982) or the Mandelzweig-Wallace-Polyzou approach. The results suggest that quasipotential equations, which include crossed exchanges approximately, are more effective than the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-body binding energies.
