# Role of Hyperon Negative Energy Sea in Nuclear Matter

> We have examined the contribution of the filled negative energy sea of hyperons to the energy/particle in nuclear matter at the one and two loop levels. While this has the potential to be significant, we find a strong cancellation between the one and two loop contributions for our chosen parameters so that hyperon effects can be justifiably neglected.

# Role of Hyperon Negative Energy Sea in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The contribution of the filled negative energy sea of hyperons to the energy per particle in nuclear matter shows a strong cancellation between one-loop and two-loop contributions for the chosen parameters, so that hyperon effects can be justifiably neglected, but this conclusion is strongly dependent on the rather uncertain values of the hyperon coupling constants and form factor cutoffs.

## Method
The authors used the Walecka Lagrangian with scalar sigma and vector omega meson exchange to compute the one-loop (Hartree) and two-loop (Fock) contributions from the negative energy sea of hyperons to the energy per particle in symmetric nuclear matter. They employed the Chin-Walecka renormalization prescription and introduced phenomenological monopole form factors at the vertices, with cutoff parameters Lambda_cut. For realistic hyperon species (Lambda, Sigma, Xi), coupling constants were constrained using data from Lambda levels in nuclear matter, Sigma- atomic data from Mares et al., and quark counting arguments.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon parameters used: g_sigmaN^2 = 54.3, m_sigma = 458 MeV, g_omegaN^2 = 102.8, m_omega = 783 MeV, saturating nuclear matter at k_f = 1.3 fm^-1 with binding energy 15.75 MeV.
- For a hypothetical hyperon with vacuum mass 1 GeV and Lambda_cut = infinity, the net two-loop contribution is negative and smaller in magnitude than for nucleons.
- For Lambda_cut = 2 GeV, the omega contribution is reduced by a factor of ~2 for M_H = 1 GeV and ~5 for M_H = 2 GeV.
- For realistic hyperons, the total hyperon one-loop contribution amounts to 3-4% of the pure nucleon energy/particle at k_f = 1.3 and 1.9 fm^-1.
- The lowest Lambda level in nuclear matter at saturation is -28 MeV, with x_omegaLambda = 2/3 and x_sigmaLambda = 0.614.
- For Sigma, x_omegaSigma = 2/3 and x_sigmaSigma = 0.54, giving the lowest Sigma level at -10 MeV.
- For Xi, x_omegaXi = x_sigmaXi = 1/3, giving the lowest Xi level at -21 MeV.
- With x_omegaSigma = 1 and x_sigmaSigma = 0.77, the one plus two loop contribution at saturation is 1.6 - 3.0 = -1.4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends relativistic calculations of nuclear matter beyond purely nucleonic degrees of freedom, following the approach of Furnstahl, Perry and Serot (1990) who first examined two-loop Fock contributions, and subsequent work incorporating form factors. The study addresses whether hyperon vacuum fluctuations introduce significant uncertainty into descriptions of equilibrium nuclear matter, complementing work by Glendenning and Moszkowski on hyperon coupling constants in neutron stars. The conclusion that hyperon negative energy sea effects can be safely neglected supports common practice, but the strong parameter dependence motivates further efforts to constrain hyperon coupling constants and form factor cutoffs.
