# Short-range correlations and the charge density

> Sophisticated high-energy and large momentum-transfer scattering experiments combined with ab-initio calculations can reveal the short-distance behavior of nucleon pairs in nuclei. On an opposite energy and resolution scale, elastic electron scattering experiments are used to extract the charge density and charge radius of different nuclei. We show that even though the charge density has no obvious connection with nuclear short-range correlations, it can be used to extract properties of such correlations. This is accomplished by using the nuclear contact formalism to derive a relation between the charge density and the proton-proton nuclear contacts that describe the probability of two protons being at close proximity. With this relation, the values of the proton-proton contacts are extracted for various nuclei using only the nuclear charge density and a solution of the two-nucleon Schroedinger equation as inputs. For symmetric nuclei, the proton-neutron contacts can also be extracted from the charge density. Good agreement is obtained with previous extractions of the nuclear contacts. These results imply that one can predict (with reasonably good accuracy) the results of high-energy and large momentum-transfer electron-scattering experiments and ab-initio calculations of high momentum tails using only experimental data of elastic scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear charge densities, which are measured in low-energy elastic electron scattering and typically described by mean-field theories without short-range correlations (SRCs), can be used to extract the nuclear contact parameters that quantify the probability of finding two protons (and, for symmetric nuclei, proton-neutron pairs) at close proximity, provided one assumes continuity of the two-body pair density at a matching radius of approximately 0.9 fm.

## Method

The authors use the nuclear contact formalism to relate the short-distance behavior of the proton-proton pair density to the contact parameter via universal two-body wavefunctions from the AV18 potential. They model the large-distance behavior of the same pair density using the uncorrelated product of one-body point-proton densities, corrected for Fermi statistics using a nuclear-matter expression with a local-density-approximation Fermi momentum. By requiring continuity of the pair density at a matching radius r0 = 0.9 fm, they extract the contact values from the charge density alone, and for symmetric nuclei they further extract the deuteron-channel proton-neutron contact using isospin symmetry.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ca, the extracted pp contact is approximately 0.15, and the pn deuteron-channel contact is approximately 2.2, giving a ratio of correlated pn to pp pairs of about 15.
* The pp contacts for various nuclei from 4He to 208Pb scale as Cpp0 = b Z2/A with b = 0.02, obtained from a fit to the extracted values.
* Using a simple Fermi-gas sphere model with R0 = 1.2 fm and the AV18 potential, the pp contact scaling coefficient is predicted as (0.023 ± 0.002) Z2/A, using |φpp0(r0)|2 ≈ 1.8311 fm−3 at r0 = 0.9 fm.
* For symmetric nuclei, the pn deuteron-channel contacts scale as Cpn1(A) = (0.056 ± 0.001) A from a fit to extracted values for A ≤ 40.
* Using the Levinger constant L = 5.5 ± 0.2 and the deuteron contact Cpn1(d) = 0.0475 ± 0.0005, the predicted scaling is Cpn1(A) = (0.065 ± 0.003) A for symmetric nuclei.
* The ratio of correlated pn to pp pairs is predicted to scale as Cpn1/Cpp0 ≈ 13 N/Z.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear contact formalism previously developed by Weiss, Bazak, and Barnea (2015, 2017) and Weiss and Hen (2018), which had established relations between contacts and high-momentum tails, the Coulomb sum-rule, and the Levinger constant, but had not connected contacts to low-energy or thermodynamic properties. The new relation enables extraction of SRC properties for heavy nuclei (e.g., 208Pb) where ab-initio Variational Monte Carlo calculations are not feasible, using only experimentally available charge densities from elastic electron scattering. The results agree with previous contact extractions from VMC calculations by Wiringa, Schiavilla, and Pieper (2014) for A ≤ 40, and with the known dominance of pn over pp SRC pairs observed by Subedi et al. (2008) and Hen et al. (2014).
