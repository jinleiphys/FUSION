# Tetrahedral shape of $^{110}$Zr from covariant density functional theory in 3D lattice space

> Covariant density functional theory is solved in 3D lattice space by implementing the preconditioned conjugate gradient method with a filtering function (PCG-F). It considerably improves the computational efficiency compared to the previous inverse Hamiltonian method (IHM). This new method is then applied to explore the tetrahedral shape of $^{110}$Zr in the full deformation space. The ground state of $^{110}$Zr is found to have a tetrahedral shape, but the deformations $β_{31}$ and $β_{33}$ greatly soften the potential energy surface. This effect is analysed with the microscopic evolution of the single-particle levels near the Fermi surface driven by the deformation.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{110}$Zr is found to have a tetrahedral shape in the full deformation space, but the deformations $\beta_{31}$ and $\beta_{33}$ greatly soften the potential energy surface, meaning the tetrahedral minimum is much less rigid than previously thought when all symmetry restrictions are removed.

## Method

The authors solved covariant density functional theory (CDFT) in a three-dimensional lattice space using the preconditioned conjugate gradient method with a filtering function (PCG-F), which improves computational efficiency over the previous inverse Hamiltonian method (IHM). The point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 was used with a 3D lattice of 30 grid points per axis and 1 fm step size, and pairing correlations were treated with the BCS method. The ground state of $^{110}$Zr was then explored without imposing any symmetry restrictions, allowing all deformation parameters $\beta_{\lambda\mu}$ to vary freely.

## Key numbers

- The PCG-F method required 84 iterations to achieve convergence (energy dispersion $\leq 10^{-8}$ MeV$^2$ and potential difference $\leq 10^{-2}$ MeV), compared to more than 170 iterations for the IHM.
- The total computational time for the PCG-F method was 386 minutes on an Intel Xeon CPU E5-2680, saving 52% of the computational time compared to the IHM.
- The ground state of $^{110}$Zr has deformation parameters $\beta_{20}=0.00$, $\beta_{22}=0.00$, $\beta_{30}=0.00$, $\beta_{31}=0.01$, $\beta_{32}=0.15$, $\beta_{33}=0.01$, $\beta_{40}=-0.02$, with a total energy of -902.49 MeV.
- The barrier between the tetrahedral minimum and the pear-like isomeric state is about 0.5 MeV when $\beta_{31}$ and $\beta_{33}$ are constrained to zero, but this barrier vanishes when all deformations are allowed.
- The neutron and proton pairing strengths were set to $G_n = -330$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ and $G_p = -430$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$, reproducing empirical pairing gaps of $^{102,104}$Zr (e.g., empirical $\Delta_n=1.10$ MeV for $^{102}$Zr versus calculated 1.12 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tetrahedral shapes in $^{110}$Zr using the macroscopic-microscopic model (Schunck 2004), Skyrme density functional theories (Schunck 2004, Olbratowski 2006), and the multidimensionally constrained CDFT (Zhao 2017) by removing all symmetry restrictions for the first time. The key advance is demonstrating that while the tetrahedral ground state persists in the full deformation space, it is greatly softened by $\beta_{31}$ and $\beta_{33}$ deformations, which previous calculations with imposed $V_4$ symmetry could not capture. This enables future searches for well-defined tetrahedral states in other nuclei using calculations in the full deformation space.
