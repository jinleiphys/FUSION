# The $ϕ$ meson width in the medium from proton induced $ϕ$ production in nuclei

> We perform calculations for the production of $ϕ$ mesons in nuclei at energies just above threshold and study the $A$ dependence of the cross section. We use results for the $ϕ$ selfenergy in the medium obtained within a chiral unitary approach. We find a strong $A$ dependence which is tied to the distortion of the incident proton and to the absorption of the $ϕ$ in its way out of the nucleus. The effect of this latter process reduces the cross section in about a factor two in heavy nuclei proving that the $A$ dependence of the cross section bears valuable information on the $ϕ$ width in the nuclear medium. Calculations are done for energies which are accessible in an experimental facility like COSY.

## Key claim

The A-dependence of the ϕ production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions, calculated just above threshold, can reveal the ϕ meson width in nuclear matter because the final-state absorption of the ϕ reduces the cross section by about a factor of two in heavy nuclei, but this conclusion depends on the specific model used for the ϕ selfenergy in the medium and on the treatment of multistep processes involving Δ intermediate states.

## Method

The authors calculated the nuclear cross section for ϕ production in pA collisions using a local Fermi sea model with an eikonal approximation for the distortion of the incoming proton and the outgoing ϕ meson. They used the imaginary part of the ϕ selfenergy in nuclear matter from a chiral unitary approach (Oset 2001, dani) to compute the ϕ absorption probability, and evaluated the ratio R = σ_A/(A σ_free) for various nuclei. They also estimated contributions from two-step processes with nucleon and Δ intermediate states, assuming the Δ N → NN ϕ cross section is 4.5 times larger than the NN → NN ϕ cross section based on coupling constant ratios.

## Key numbers

- The ϕ medium width at rest at normal nuclear density ρ₀ is 24 MeV (from the chiral unitary model used for the selfenergy).
- The total pN cross section for incident proton kinetic energy T_p = 2.5 to 3 GeV is around 40 mb, consisting of 30% elastic and 70% inelastic.
- The estimated energy loss per pN collision is ΔE ≈ 400 MeV or more.
- The ratio f_{πNΔ}/f_{πNN} = 2.13, giving a factor 4.5 in the cross section for Δ-mediated processes relative to nucleon-mediated ones.
- The form factor cutoff used for πNN and πNΔ vertices is Λ = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to ϕ photoproduction in nuclei (Oset 2001, daniluis) to proton-induced reactions, where coherent ϕ production is suppressed by the kinematics just above threshold. The approach is similar to that used for η photoproduction (ref1) to determine the inelastic ηN cross section. The results are intended to guide experiments at COSY and complement studies of ϕ width modification in heavy-ion collisions (Pal 2002, Yokkaichi) and other reactions (Klingl 1998, Mosel).
