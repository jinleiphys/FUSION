# Pseudorapidity profile of transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate pseudorapidity correlations of the average transverse flow of particles emitted in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We employ 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with initial conditions from the quark Glauber Monte Carlo model to confront the recent measurements on the pseudorapidity correlations of the transverse momentum fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760$GeV. We find good agreement between the model predictions and data. Further, we study two other observables build with the covariance of the average transverse momentum in different rapidity bins. These observables have better stability under various systematics, thus allowing for a robust comparison between data and model. The transverse flow-transverse flow correlation coefficient is directly related to correlations of the underlying collective flow at different pseudorapidities. The 3-bin measure of $p_T$ factorization breaking in pseudorapidity gives an estimate of possible decorrelation of the average transverse flow in the longitudinal direction.

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic model with quark Glauber initial conditions reproduces the magnitude and centrality dependence of the preliminary ALICE data on the Pearson correlation coefficient of average transverse momentum in different pseudorapidity bins, but this measured coefficient is dominated by statistical fluctuations from finite multiplicity rather than genuine collective flow correlations.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with shear viscosity to entropy ratio of 0.08, using initial conditions from a Monte Carlo quark Glauber model where each nucleon is composed of three quarks and entropy density is deposited as Gaussians of width 0.3 fm in the transverse plane. The longitudinal profile uses asymmetric deposition from left- and right-going participants with a specific functional form. Particles are emitted at a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV via statistical hadronization, and the resulting events include both collective flow correlations and non-flow correlations from resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- The decorrelation of the collective average transverse momentum is 0.8-1.0 for pseudorapidities |η| < 2.5 in the hydrodynamic model with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions
- The transverse flow-transverse flow correlation coefficient ρ([p_T]_F,[p_T]_B) is 8-10 times larger than the standard Pearson correlation coefficient b([p_T]_F,[p_T]_B)
- The 3-bin decorrelation measure shows 5-10% decorrelation over two units of pseudorapidity η
- The initial entropy density Gaussians have width 0.3 fm in the transverse plane
- The longitudinal profile parameters are σ_η = 1.4 and η_p = 1.15
- The freeze-out temperature is 150 MeV
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio is set to 0.08
- The transverse momentum range for analysis is 0.2-2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of transverse momentum fluctuations (Bozek 2012, Mazeliauskas 2015) to pseudorapidity correlations, confronting preliminary ALICE Collaboration measurements. It builds on prior work on event-plane decorrelation in pseudorapidity (Bozek 2010, Petersen 2011, Jia 2014) and on transverse momentum fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions (Adams 2003, Abelev 2014). The proposed transverse flow-transverse flow correlation coefficient and 3-bin decorrelation measure enable more robust experimental comparisons by removing self-correlation and non-flow contributions, analogous to the 3-bin measure used for azimuthal flow coefficients by the CMS Collaboration (Khachatryan 2015).
