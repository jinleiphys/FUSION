# Threshold scattering of the eta-meson off light nuclei

> The scattering lengths of eta-meson collisions with light nuclei d,t,3He, and 4He are calculated on the basis of few-body equations in coherent approximation. It is found that the eta-nucleus scattering length depends strongly on the number of nucleons and the potential-range parameter. By taking into account the off-shell behavior of the eta-N amplitude, the eta-4He scattering length increases considerably.

## Key claim

The eta-nucleus scattering length depends strongly on the number of nucleons and the potential-range parameter, and taking into account the off-shell behavior of the eta-N amplitude increases the eta-4He scattering length considerably; however, the results are sensitive to the choice of the eta-N scattering length and range parameter, for which three different values each exist in the literature.

## Method

The authors solved few-body equations within the Finite-Rank Hamiltonian Approximation (FRHA) in coherent approximation, which assumes the nucleus remains unexcited during multiple scattering of the eta-meson. They used separable S-wave eta-N amplitudes of Yamaguchi type with a Breit-Wigner propagator for the N*(1535) resonance, and employed Gaussian-type ground-state wave functions for the target nuclei that reproduce experimental mean square radii. The calculations were performed at zero energy for all nine combinations of three available eta-N scattering lengths and three range parameters.

## Key numbers

- Eta-N scattering lengths used as input: (0.27 + i 0.22) fm, (0.28 + i 0.19) fm, and (0.55 + i 0.30) fm
- Range parameters alpha used: 2.357 fm^-1, 3.316 fm^-1, and 7.617 fm^-1
- N*(1535) resonance parameters: resonance energy E0 = 1535 MeV - (M_N + M_eta), width Gamma = 150 MeV
- Nuclear root-mean-square radii used: deuteron 1.956 fm, tritium 1.755 fm, helium-3 1.959 fm, alpha particle 1.671 fm
- For the largest range parameter (alpha = 2.357 fm^-1) and a_etaN = (0.55 + i 0.30) fm, the eta-4He scattering length is (-4.41 + i 2.86) fm
- Prior Wilkin (1995) results for comparison: a(eta-3He) = (-2.31 + i 2.57) fm, a(eta-4He) = (-2.00 + i 0.97) fm

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Wilkin (1995), who used a lowest-order optical-potential method with a constant eta-N amplitude having no energy or off-shell dependence, and by Wilkin (1994), who used a multiple-scattering expansion incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations. The results indicate that the condition A >= 12 previously obtained for the existence of eta-nucleus bound states should be reduced to lower values of A, and the authors note that more accurate nuclear wave functions (e.g., from the Integrodifferential Equation Approach) are needed for improved precision.
