# Nuclear $β^-$-decay half-lives for $fp$ and $fpg$ shell nuclei

> In the present work we calculate the allowed $β^-$-decay half-lives of nuclei with $Z = 20 -30$ and N $\leq$ 50 systematically under the framework of the nuclear shell model. A recent study shows that some nuclei in this region belong to the island of inversion. We perform calculation for $fp$ shell nuclei using KB3G effective interaction. In the case of Ni, Cu, and Zn, we used JUN45 effective interaction. Theoretical results of $Q$ values, half-lives, excitation energies, log$ft$ values, and branching fractions are discussed and compared with the experimental data. In the Ni region, we also compared our calculated results with recent experimental data [Z. Y. Xu {\it et al.}, \emph{Phys. Rev. Lett.} \textbf{113}, 032505, 2014]. Present results agree with the experimental data of half-lives in comparison to QRPA.

## Key claim

The nuclear shell model calculations, using the KB3G effective interaction for $fp$ shell nuclei and the JUN45 effective interaction for Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space, reproduce experimental beta-decay half-lives more accurately than quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations, particularly for nuclei in the island of inversion such as $^{63-65}$Mn and $^{67}$Co, though agreement deteriorates for heavier even-even nuclei near $N=40$ due to the missing $\nu d_{5/2}$ orbital.

## Method

The authors performed systematic nuclear shell model calculations for allowed $\beta^-$-decay of nuclei with $Z = 20-30$ and $N \leq 50$ using the NuShellX@MSU code. For $fp$ shell nuclei they used the KB3G effective interaction, while for Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space they used the JUN45 effective interaction. They determined quenching factors by averaging ratios of experimental to theoretical $R(GT)$ matrix elements, obtaining $q = 0.660 \pm 0.016$ for the $pf$ shell and $q = 0.684 \pm 0.015$ for the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ shell. Theoretical $Q$ values, half-lives, excitation energies, log$ft$ values, and branching fractions were calculated and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor for $pf$ shell nuclei (KB3G interaction): $q = 0.660 \pm 0.016$
- Quenching factor for $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ shell nuclei (JUN45 interaction): $q = 0.684 \pm 0.015$
- For $^{63}$Mn ($5/2^-$): theoretical half-life 216.1 ms vs. experimental 275 $\pm$ 4 ms
- For $^{64}$Mn ($1^+$): theoretical half-life 60.53 ms vs. experimental 90 $\pm$ 4 ms
- For $^{65}$Mn ($5/2^-$): theoretical half-life 73.21 ms vs. experimental 84 $\pm$ 8 ms
- For $^{78}$Ni ($0^+$): theoretical half-life 2.255 ms vs. experimental 122.2 $\pm$ 5.1 ms
- For $^{80}$Zn ($0^+$): theoretical half-life 133.1 ms vs. experimental 562.2 $\pm$ 3.0 ms
- rms deviation between theoretical and experimental $Q$ values: 1162 keV
- For $^{52}$Ca ($0^+$): theoretical half-life 2.519 s vs. experimental 4.6 $\pm$ 0.3 s
- For $^{60}$Cr ($0^+$): theoretical half-life 225.23 ms vs. experimental 490 $\pm$ 10 ms

## Context

This work extends shell model calculations of beta-decay properties to neutron-rich nuclei in the $fp$ and $fpg$ shells, including nuclei identified as belonging to the island of inversion around $N=40$ as noted by previous studies (e.g., Sorlin et al. at GANIL). The results improve upon QRPA calculations from Moller et al. and Bender et al., which underestimate Gamow-Teller strength at low energies. The calculations are compared with recent experimental data from Xu et al. (2014) for Ni isotopes measured at RIKEN, and the work enables future theoretical predictions for nuclei where experimental half-lives remain unknown, such as $^{57-58}$Ca, $^{59-61}$Sc, and $^{62}$Ti.
