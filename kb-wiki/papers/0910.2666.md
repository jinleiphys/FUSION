# Classical Strongly Coupled QGP: VII. Shear Viscosity and Self Diffusion

> We construct the Liouville operator for the SU(2) classical colored Coulomb plasma (cQGP) for arbitrary values of the Coulomb coupling $Γ=V/K$, the ratio of the mean Coulomb to kinetic energy. We show that its resolvent in the classical colored phase space obeys a hierarchy of equations. We use a free streaming approximation to close the hierarchy and derive an integral equation for the time-dependent structure factor. Its reduction by projection yields hydrodynamical equations in the long-wavelength limit. We discuss the character of the hydrodynamical modes at strong coupling. The shear viscosity is shown to exhibit a minimum at $Γ\approx 8$ near the liquid point. This minimum follows from the cross-over between the single particle collisional regime which drops as $1/Γ^{5/2}$ and the hydrodynamical collisional regime which rises as $Γ^{1/2}$. The self-diffusion constant drops as $1/Γ^{3/2}$ irrespective of the regime. We compare our results to molecular dynamics simulations of the SU(2) colored Coulomb plasma. We also discuss the relevance of our results for the quantum and strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (sQGP)

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of the SU(2) classical colored Coulomb plasma (cQGP) exhibits a minimum at Coulomb coupling Γ ≈ 8 near the liquid point, arising from a crossover between a single-particle collisional regime that drops as 1/Γ^(5/2) and a hydrodynamical collisional regime that rises as Γ^(1/2); the abstract states Γ ≈ 8 but the full text later mentions a minimum at Γ ≈ 5.

## Method

The authors constructed the Liouville operator for the SU(2) classical colored Coulomb plasma in phase space and derived a hierarchy of equations for its resolvent. They closed the hierarchy using a free streaming approximation on the 4-point resolvent, introduced a non-local self-energy kernel, and projected the resulting kinetic equation onto the hydrodynamical (long-wavelength) phase space using a projection operator method. The shear viscosity was extracted from the dispersion relation of the transverse hydrodynamical modes.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity minimum occurs at Γ ≈ 8 (abstract) or Γ ≈ 5 (full text, section 7), near the liquid point.
- In the single-particle collisional regime, shear viscosity drops as 1/Γ^(5/2).
- In the hydrodynamical collisional regime, shear viscosity rises as Γ^(1/2).
- The self-diffusion constant drops as 1/Γ^(3/2) irrespective of the regime.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous equilibrium analysis of the static properties of the colored Coulomb plasma (Cho and Zahed, 2009) to transport properties. It builds on the classical model of the colored plasma suggested by Gelman, Shuryak, and Zahed (2006) and uses molecular dynamics simulations of the SU(2) colored Coulomb plasma for comparison. The results are relevant for understanding the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) at temperatures (1-3) Tc, where the plasma behaves more like a liquid than a gas, as suggested by Shuryak and Zahed (2004).
