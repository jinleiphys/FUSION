# Production cross sections of $^{3,4}_Λ$H bound states in $^{3,4}$He($K^-$, $π^0$) reactions at 1 GeV/$c$

> We investigate theoretically productions of $^{3,4}_Λ$H bound states in the exothermic ($K^-$, $π^0$) reactions on $^{3,4}$He targets at $p_{K^-}=$ 1.0 GeV/c in a distorted-wave impulse approximation with the optimal Fermi-averaging $K^-p\toπ^0Λ$ $t$ matrix. We calculate angular distributions of the laboratory differential cross sections $dσ/dΩ_{\rm lab}$ and the integrated cross sections $σ_{\rm lab}$ for a $J^P= 0^+$ ground state and a $J^P=1^+$ excited state of $^4_Λ$H at $π^0$ forward-direction angles of $θ_{\rm lab}=$ 0$^\circ$-20$^\circ$, and those for a $J^P= 1/2^+$ ground state of $^3_Λ$H. The production of a $J^P=3/2^+$ excited state of $^3_Λ$H as a virtual state is also evaluated. The comparison in $dσ/dΩ_{\rm lab}$ and $σ_{\rm lab}$ between $^4_Λ$H and $^3_Λ$H provides examining the mechanism of the production and structure of $^{3,4}_Λ$H, as well as in the endothermic ($π^-$, $K^0$) reactions at $p_{π^-}=$ 1.05 GeV/c. This investigation confirms the feasibility of lifetime measurements of $^3_Λ$H at the J-PARC experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the production cross sections of $^{3,4}_\Lambda$H bound states in the exothermic ($K^-$, $\pi^0$) reactions on $^{3,4}$He targets at $p_{K^-}=$ 1.0 GeV/$c$ are large enough to confirm the feasibility of lifetime measurements of $^3_\Lambda$H at J-PARC experiments, but the absolute cross sections depend sensitively on the weakly-bound nature of the $\Lambda$ hyperon and on distortion effects that reduce the plane-wave results by factors of 0.23–0.67.

## Method

The authors employ a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with the optimal Fermi-averaging $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ $t$ matrix, using eikonal meson distorted waves with absorption cross sections $\sigma_{K^-}=$ 45 mb and $\sigma_\pi=$ 32 mb. They calculate angular distributions of laboratory differential cross sections $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\rm lab}$ and integrated cross sections $\sigma_{\rm lab}$ for the $J^P=0^+$ ground state and $J^P=1^+$ excited state of $^4_\Lambda$H, the $J^P=1/2^+$ ground state of $^3_\Lambda$H, and the $J^P=3/2^+$ excited state of $^3_\Lambda$H treated as a virtual state using continuum-discretized wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For $^4_\Lambda$H($0^+_{\rm g.s.}$) at $p_{K^-}=$ 1.0 GeV/$c$: $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\rm lab}=$ 1184.3 $\mu$b/sr at $\theta_{\rm lab}=0^\circ$, 552.2 $\mu$b/sr at $6^\circ$, 100.8 $\mu$b/sr at $12^\circ$, and 19.2 $\mu$b/sr at $18^\circ$; integrated $\sigma_{\rm lab}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})=$ 63.1 $\mu$b over $0^\circ$–$20^\circ$ in DWIA
- For $^4_\Lambda$H($1^+_{\rm exc}$): $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\rm lab}=$ 0.27 $\mu$b/sr at $0^\circ$, 10.5 $\mu$b/sr at $6^\circ$, 14.4 $\mu$b/sr at $12^\circ$, and 5.68 $\mu$b/sr at $18^\circ$; integrated $\sigma_{\rm lab}(1^+_{\rm exc})=$ 3.8 $\mu$b over $0^\circ$–$20^\circ$ in DWIA
- For $^3_\Lambda$H($1/2^+_{\rm g.s.}$): $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\rm lab}=$ 484.0 $\mu$b/sr at $0^\circ$, 183.4 $\mu$b/sr at $6^\circ$, 57.1 $\mu$b/sr at $12^\circ$, and 7.5 $\mu$b/sr at $18^\circ$; integrated $\sigma_{\rm lab}(1/2^+_{\rm g.s.})=$ 25.9 $\mu$b over $0^\circ$–$20^\circ$ in DWIA
- For $^3_\Lambda$H($3/2^+_{\rm exc}$) virtual state: $d\sigma/d\Omega_{\rm lab}=$ 1.67 $\mu$b/sr at $0^\circ$, 2.94 $\mu$b/sr at $6^\circ$, 2.83 $\mu$b/sr at $12^\circ$, and 1.01 $\mu$b/sr at $18^\circ$; integrated $\sigma_{\rm lab}(3/2^+_{\rm exc})=$ 0.77 $\mu$b over $0^\circ$–$20^\circ$ in DWIA
- Production ratio $R_4=\sigma_{\rm lab}(1^+_{\rm exc})/\sigma_{\rm lab}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})\simeq 0.06$–$0.07$ for $^4_\Lambda$H at $p_{K^-}=$ 1.0 GeV/$c$
- Production ratio $R_3=\sigma_{\rm lab}(3/2^+_{\rm exc})/\sigma_{\rm lab}(1/2^+_{\rm g.s.})\simeq 0.030$–$0.033$ for $^3_\Lambda$H at $p_{K^-}=$ 1.0 GeV/$c$
- $\Lambda$ separation energy $B_\Lambda=$ 0.13 MeV (Juric 1973) or $0.41\pm0.12$ MeV (STAR Collaboration 2020) for $^3_\Lambda$H($1/2^+_{\rm g.s.}$); using $B_\Lambda=$ 0.41 MeV gives $\sigma_{\rm lab}(1/2^+_{\rm g.s.})=$ 41.0 $\mu$b, 60% larger than the $B_\Lambda=$ 0.13 MeV case
- Root-mean-square distance $\langle r^2_\Lambda \rangle^{1/2}=$ 11.2 fm for $^3_\Lambda$H($1/2^+_{\rm g.s.}$), 18.2 fm for $^3_\Lambda$H($3/2^+_{\rm exc}$), 3.68 fm for $^4_\Lambda$H($0^+_{\rm g.s.}$), and 4.60 fm for $^4_\Lambda$H($1^+_{\rm exc}$)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DWIA studies of $^4_\Lambda$He production in ($\pi^-$, $K^0$) reactions (Harada 2019) to the ($K^-$, $\pi^0$) channel on $^{3,4}$He targets, addressing the puzzle of the unexpectedly short $^3_\Lambda$H lifetime observed in heavy-ion collisions (HypHI 2013, Gal 2019). The calculations confirm feasibility for the planned J-PARC experiments P73 and P74, and provide cross section estimates that can be compared with the endothermic ($\pi^-$, $K^0$) reactions at $p_{\pi^-}=$ 1.05 GeV/$c$ studied previously. The treatment of the $J^P=3/2^+$ state as a virtual state near the $d$-$\Lambda$ threshold follows the theoretical discussion by Schafer (2020) regarding a possible $s$-wave virtual state with scattering length $a_{3/2}^{\Lambda d}=$ $-$16.2 fm and effective range $r_{3/2}^{\Lambda d}=$ 3.2 fm.
