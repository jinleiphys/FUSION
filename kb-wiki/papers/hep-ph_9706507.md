# Unified description of light- and strange-baryon spectra

> We present a chiral constituent quark model for light and strange baryons providing a unified description of their ground states and excitation spectra. The model relies on constituent quarks and Goldstone bosons arising as effective degrees of freedom of low-energy QCD from the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. The spectra of the three-quark systems are obtained from a precise variational solution of the Schrödinger equation with a semirelativistic Hamiltonian. The theoretical predictions are found in close agreement with experiment.

# Unified description of light- and strange-baryon spectra

## Key claim
The chiral constituent quark model based on Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) between constituent quarks, solved with a semirelativistic Hamiltonian and a stochastic variational method, reproduces the correct level orderings of positive- and negative-parity states in light and strange baryon spectra, resolving a long-standing problem in baryon spectroscopy. The model achieves this with only a handful of free parameters, though the authors note that the baryon spectra alone do not guarantee a unique determination of the model parameters and that further studies of other observables are necessary to constrain their values.

## Method
The authors construct a semirelativistic three-quark Hamiltonian with a relativistic kinetic-energy operator and a quark-quark interaction consisting of a linear confinement potential plus a chiral potential derived from Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE), including the full pseudoscalar meson octet (pi, K, eta) and the flavor-singlet eta-prime. The GBE potential is parametrized with a zero-volume-integral form involving a Yukawa tail and a short-range term, with meson-quark coupling constants and a linear scaling prescription for the short-range cutoff parameters. The three-quark Schrodinger equation is solved using the stochastic variational method, which the authors state is reliable to an accuracy of better than 1% in the present calculation.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = 340 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV
- Octet meson-quark coupling constant: g_8^2/4pi = 0.67 (extracted from the phenomenological pion-nucleon coupling constant)
- Free parameters from fit to baryon spectra: (g_0/g_8)^2 = 1.34, Lambda_0 = 2.87 fm^-1, kappa = 0.81, V_0 = -416 MeV, C = 2.33 fm^-2
- Nucleon ground state normalized to 939 MeV
- All light- and strange-baryon excitation levels calculated up to M less than or approximately 1850 MeV
- The Roper resonance N(1440) falls well below the negative-parity states N(1535) and N(1520)
- In the Lambda spectrum, Lambda(1600) falls below Lambda(1670)-Lambda(1690), while Lambda(1405)-Lambda(1520) remain the lowest excitations above the Lambda ground state
- In the Sigma spectrum, Sigma(1660) falls below the negative-parity state Sigma(1750)

## Context
This work extends the Goldstone-boson exchange model previously developed by Glozman and Riska (1996) and addresses the failure of the earlier one-gluon exchange (OGE) model of de Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow (1975) to explain correct level orderings in light- and strange-baryon spectra, the spin content of the nucleon, and the flavor asymmetry of the sea in the nucleon. The model enables a unified description of all low-lying light and strange baryons within a chiral constituent quark framework, and the authors note that future work should include coupling to decay channels (QQQpi, QQQK, QQQeta, QQQeta-prime) and test the GBE interaction against observables other than spectra to obtain additional constraints.
