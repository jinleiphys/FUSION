# Baryon Number Conservation and Statistical Production of Antibaryons

> The statistical production of antibaryons is considered within the canonical ensemble formulation. We demonstrate that the antibaryon suppression in small systems due to the exact baryon number conservation is rather different in the baryon-free (B=0) and baryon-rich (B>1) systems. At constant values of temperature and baryon density in the baryon-rich systems the density of the produced antibaryons is only weakly dependent on the size of the system. For realistic hadronization conditions this dependence appears to be close to B/(B+1) which is in agreement with the preliminary data of the NA49 Collaboration for the antiproton/pion ratio in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN SPS energies. However, a consistent picture of antibaryon production within the statistical hadronization model has not yet been achieved. This is because the condition of constant hadronization temperature in the baryon-free systems leads to a contradiction with the data on the antiproton/pion ratio in e+e- interactions.

# Baryon Number Conservation and Statistical Production of Antibaryons

## Key claim

The canonical ensemble suppression of antibaryon production due to exact baryon number conservation is dramatically different in baryon-free (B=0) and baryon-rich (B≥2) systems; for baryon-rich systems at constant temperature and baryon density, the antibaryon density depends only weakly on system size, approaching B/(B+1), which explains the observed antiproton scaling in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the same model with constant hadronization temperature fails to describe the antiproton/pion ratio in e+e- interactions.

## Method

The authors derive analytical expressions for the canonical ensemble partition function of a Boltzmann ideal gas of baryons and antibaryons with exact baryon number conservation, expressing the mean multiplicities in terms of modified Bessel functions I_B(2z). They then compute the canonical suppression factor F_cs, defined as the ratio of antibaryon density in the canonical ensemble to that in the grand canonical ensemble, and evaluate its dependence on baryon number B and the parameter x = 2f/ρ_B (where f is the sum over baryon state densities and ρ_B is baryon density) for both baryon-free and baryon-rich systems.

## Key numbers

- For baryon-rich systems (B≥2) with x << 1, the canonical suppression factor approaches F_cs = B/(B+1), giving a suppression factor of 2/3 for B=2 (nucleon-nucleon interactions)
- For baryon-free systems (B=0) at T=160 MeV and V=5 fm³, the (anti)baryon density increases by a factor of 10 from its small-volume value to the grand canonical limit at infinite volume
- The hadronization temperature for Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV is T = 175 ± 15 MeV, with baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 250 MeV, giving x ≈ 0.5
- The hadronization volume for e+e- interactions at √s = 29 GeV is approximately V = 5 fm³

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model formalism of Hagedorn (1971) and Rafelski and Danos (1980) by providing explicit analytical formulas for antibaryon production with exact baryon number conservation. It addresses the apparent contradiction between the strong canonical suppression expected for antiproton production in small systems and the preliminary NA49 Collaboration data showing approximately equal antiproton/pion ratios in p+p and central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A·GeV. The paper shows that the baryon-rich environment of nuclear collisions naturally produces weak volume dependence, but notes that the same model with constant temperature fails to describe the energy independence of the antiproton/pion ratio in e+e- interactions observed by Becattini (1996, 1997), leaving open the question of whether a consistent statistical hadronization description exists.
