# The nucleon-nucleon interaction and properties of the nucleon in a $πρω$ soliton model including a dilaton field with anomalous dimension

> We investigate an extended chiral soliton model which includes $π, ρ, ω$ and $σ$ mesons as explicit degrees of freedom. The Lagrangian incorporates chiral symmetry and broken scale invariance. A scalar-isoscalar meson $σ$ is associated with a quarkonium dilaton field with a mass $\msig\approx 550 $MeV. We show that the scalar field with anomalous dimension slightly changes the static and electromagnetic properties of the nucleon. In contrast, it plays a significant role in nucleon-nucleon dynamics and gives an opportunity to describe well the two-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a light scalar-isoscalar dilaton field (sigma meson) with anomalous dimension into the pi-rho-omega soliton model only slightly changes the static and electromagnetic properties of the nucleon, but plays a significant role in nucleon-nucleon dynamics by providing the intermediate range attraction in the central potential that was missing in earlier Skyrme-like models.

## Method

The authors extend the pi-rho-omega chiral soliton model by adding a scalar-isoscalar sigma meson field with anomalous scale dimension d=2.6, treating the sigma as a quarkonium dilaton field. They construct classical soliton solutions with baryon number one using spherical symmetric ansatze for the meson fields, then perform adiabatic rotation and collective quantization to obtain nucleon and Delta masses. Meson-nucleon form factors are calculated in the Breit frame and used within a one-boson-exchange (OBE) framework to compute the central nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 550 MeV and m_sigma = 720 MeV (two cases considered)
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV
- Rho and omega meson masses: m_rho = m_omega = 770 MeV
- Rho-pi-pi coupling: g = 5.85 (from KSFR relation)
- Scale dimension of scalar field: d = 2.6
- Vacuum expectation value of scalar field: S_0 = 93 MeV
- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 0.88 for pi-rho-omega model, g_A = 0.95 for pi-rho-omega-sigma model
- Pion-nucleon form factor monopole cutoff: Lambda_pi = 860 MeV (without metric factor), Lambda_pi = 1100 MeV (with metric factor)
- Sigma-nucleon coupling constant and cutoff are smaller than OBE predictions (Lambda_sigma^OBE = 1300-2000 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the pi-rho-omega soliton model of Meissner and collaborators by incorporating a light scalar-isoscalar meson following the approach of Furnstahl, Tang and Serot (1995) for nuclear matter, which realizes QCD symmetries including broken scale invariance. It addresses the long-standing problem of missing intermediate range attraction in the central nucleon-nucleon potential within Skyrme-like models, which was previously tackled by Holzwarth and Machleidt (1990) using form factors from the Skyrme model in OBE calculations. The results enable improved OBE descriptions of nucleon-nucleon observables using microscopically derived meson-nucleon form factors, though the authors note that full NN phase shift calculations would require additional mesons and two-pion exchange contributions.
