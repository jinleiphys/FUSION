# The GDH sum rule for the Delta isobar: A possible anomaly?

> The GDH sum rule is discussed for the Delta(1232) resonance. It is shown that apart from ordinary excitations to higher-energy states, the sum rule contains a large negative contribution due to de-excitation into the nucleon state. Therefore, a fulfillment of the sum rule assumes a strong coupling of Delta^+ and Delta^0 to resonances of spin 5/2 and higher. Calculations performed in quark models suggest that D15(1675) may be such a resonance. However, its strength is found to be not sufficient for bringing the GDH sum rule to a theoretically expected positive magnitude.

# The GDH sum rule for the Delta isobar: A possible anomaly?

## Key claim
The GDH sum rule for the Delta(1232) resonance contains a large negative contribution from de-excitation into the nucleon state, which must be compensated by strong photocouplings to higher-spin resonances (spin ≥ 5/2); however, calculations in quark models and large-Nc QCD suggest that the D15(1675) resonance does not provide sufficient strength to bring the GDH integral to its theoretically expected positive value.

## Method
The author evaluates the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule for the Delta isobar using the Karl-Isgur nonrelativistic quark model (NQM), both with and without one- and two-body spin-orbit electromagnetic interactions. Photocouplings Aλ of the Delta resonance to all lowest L=1 baryons are calculated, and the resonance contribution to the GDH integral is computed via the standard formula I_res^GDH = Σ (2π/ω)(|A_{1+s}|^2 - |A_{1-s}|^2). Results are compared with predictions from large-Nc QCD fits by Carlson and Carone (1998) using experimental nucleon photocoupling data.

## Key numbers
- The M1 contribution to the GDH integral for Δ⁺ or Δ⁰ is approximately -300 μb from the Δ→N de-excitation transition.
- In the NQM without spin-orbit interactions, the total GDH integral for Δ⁺ is -431 μb; with spin-orbit interactions included (NQM+so), it becomes -105 μb.
- The D15(1675) resonance contributes +189 μb (NQM), +332 μb (NQM+so), +309 μb (CC-I), and +532 μb (CC-II) to the GDH integral for Δ⁺.
- The nucleon N(939) contributes -270 μb (NQM) or -468 μb (CC-I/CC-II) to the GDH integral for Δ⁺.
- For Δ⁺⁺, the total GDH integral is -163 μb (NQM) and -90 μb (CC-I).

## Context
This work extends the GDH sum rule analysis beyond the nucleon target, where the sum rule has been experimentally confirmed at MAMI (2000), to the Delta isobar. The author shows that the naive expectation of a small GDH integral for Delta (whose anomalous magnetic moment vanishes in the nonrelativistic quark model) is incorrect due to the large de-excitation contribution. The paper builds on earlier quark model calculations by Li (1993) and De Sanctis et al. (1994), and on large-Nc QCD work by Cohen (2000) and Carlson and Carone (1998). The results indicate that the GDH sum rule for Delta may be anomalous, requiring either stronger photocouplings to higher-spin resonances than currently predicted, or additional contributions from soft-pion photoproduction and finite-width effects that remain to be calculated.
