# Measuring velocity ratios with correlation functions

> We show how to determine the ratio of the transverse velocity of a source to the velocity of emitted particles, using split-bin correlation functions. The technique is to measure $S_2$ and $S_2^ϕ$, subtract the contributions from the single-particle distribution, and take the ratio as the bin size goes to zero. We demonstrate the technique for two cases: each source decays into two particles, and each source emits a large number of particles.

# Measuring velocity ratios with correlation functions

## Key claim

The ratio of the transverse velocity of a source to the velocity of emitted particles can be determined using split-bin correlation functions $S_2$ and $S_2^\phi$, provided the emission is isotropic and the measurement is taken in the limit where the bin size goes to zero; the technique works without requiring particle identification, unlike standard two-particle interferometry.

## Method

The authors define two split-bin correlation functions $S_2(\delta y; \Delta Y)$ (rapidity-based) and $S_2^\phi(\delta y; \Delta Y)$ (azimuthal-angle-based) over a rapidity window of size $\Delta Y$. They subtract the single-particle distribution contributions $\Sigma_2$ and $\Sigma_2^\phi$ from these functions and take the ratio $R = (S_2^\phi - \Sigma_2^\phi) / (S_2 - \Sigma_2)$ in the limit $\delta y \to 0$ and $\Delta Y \to \infty$. The technique is demonstrated analytically for two cases: a resonance decaying into two particles, and a thermal source emitting a large number of particles, both assuming isotropic emission in the source rest frame.

## Key numbers

- For two-particle decay in the non-relativistic limit, $R$ depends only on the velocity ratio $v_s / v_\pi$, where $v_s$ is the source transverse velocity and $v_\pi$ is the velocity of emitted particles in the source rest frame.
- For two-particle decay, $R(0) = 2$ as an identity due to the different topologies of $\phi$ and $y$.
- For two-particle decay in the limit $v_s \to 1$, $R = \frac{2 (1-v_s^2)^{1/2}}{\pi^2} \ln \left[ \frac{v_s + v_\pi}{v_s - v_\pi} \right]$.
- For thermal sources, $R(0) = 1$ and $R(\infty) = 0$, showing different normalization from the two-particle decay case.
- The result $R$ is independent of the number of sources $n_s$ and the background size $N$ in both cases.

## Context

This work extends the use of correlation functions beyond source size measurements (Hanbury-Brown/Twiss interferometry, Pratt 1986, Gong et al. 1990) and source multiplicity measurements (Seibert 1990) to extract source velocities. It addresses the problem that no other source characteristics beyond size were identifiable in high-energy collisions, particularly where sources in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions decay into at least fifteen pions each (Seibert 1989). The technique enables velocity measurements in high-multiplicity collisions where individual sources cannot be resolved, and the authors suggest similar approaches could measure energy distributions of minijets.
