# Nuclear transparencies in relativistic A(e,e'p) models

> Relativistic and unfactorized calculations for the nuclear transparency extracted from exclusive A(e,e'p) reactions for 0.3 \leq Q^2 \leq 10 (GeV/c)^2 are presented for the target nuclei C, Si, Fe and Pb. For Q^2 \geq 0.6 (GeV/c)^2, the transparency results are computed within the framework of the recently developed relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA). The target-mass and Q^2 dependence of the RMSGA predictions are compared with relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations. Despite the very different model assumptions underlying the treatment of the final-state interactions in the RMSGA and RDWIA frameworks, they predict comparable nuclear transparencies for kinematic regimes where both models are applicable.

## Key claim

Despite the very different assumptions underlying the treatment of final-state interactions in the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) and the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), the two frameworks predict comparable nuclear transparencies for kinematic regimes where both models are applicable (0.6 ≤ Q² ≤ 1.8 (GeV/c)²). The RMSGA framework reproduces the ¹²C data but systematically underpredicts transparencies for heavier nuclei, while the RDWIA approach systematically underestimates the data by roughly 5-10%.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear transparencies for exclusive A(e,e'p) reactions on ¹²C, ²⁸Si, ⁵⁶Fe, and ²⁰⁸Pb using two relativistic and unfactorized frameworks: RDWIA (using global S-V optical potential parameterizations from Cooper et al. (1993) for proton kinetic energies up to 1 GeV) and RMSGA (using a Dirac-Glauber phase with profile function parameters interpolated from the Particle Data Group database). Both calculations used identical bound-state wave functions from a Hartree calculation with the W1 parameterization, the CC2 current operator in Coulomb gauge, and identical kinematic cuts (|p_m| ≤ 300 MeV/c, E_m ≤ 80 MeV) to match experimental extraction procedures.

## Key numbers

- RDWIA transparencies were obtained up to Q² ≈ 1.8 (GeV/c)², limited by the highest kinetic energy (1 GeV) in the Cooper et al. (1993) optical potential parameterizations
- RMSGA calculations were performed for Q² ≥ 0.6 (GeV/c)², with the kinematic overlap region 0.6 ≤ Q² ≤ 1.8 (GeV/c)² covered by both frameworks
- The c_A correction factors used to account for short-range correlations were: 0.9 (¹²C), 0.88 (²⁸Si), 0.82 (⁵⁶Fe), and 0.77 (²⁰⁸Pb)
- In the plane-wave limit (no FSI), the RMSGA-Gent and RDWIA-Madrid codes agreed to better than 5% in differential cross sections
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the RMSGA framework predicted 5 to 10 percent more absorption than RDWIA
- The A-dependence parameter α from Abbott et al. (1998) ranged from 0.17 ± 0.04 at Q² = 0.65 (GeV/c)² to 0.25 ± 0.04 at Q² = 3.3 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic transparency calculations by Kelly (1996) and Meucci (2002) by providing the first relativistic calculation covering the wide quasi-elastic kinematic range of existing experiments, bridging the low-energy optical-potential regime (RDWIA) with the high-energy Glauber regime (RMSGA). The results contradict earlier findings by Kelly (1996) of large discrepancies between DWIA and Glauber transparencies, showing instead reasonably good agreement for medium-heavy nuclei like ⁵⁶Fe. The RMSGA framework enables predictions for Q² ≥ 2 (GeV/c)² where optical potentials are unavailable, showing nearly constant transparencies consistent with non-relativistic Glauber calculations by Pandharipande and Pieper (2002) and others.
