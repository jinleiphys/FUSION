# Influence of momentum-dependent interactions on balance energy and mass dependence

> We aim to study the role of momentum-dependent interactions in transverse flow as well as in its disappearance. For the present study, central collisions involving mass between 24 and 394 are considered. We find that momentum-dependent interactions have different impact in lighter colliding nuclei compared to heavier colliding nuclei. In lighter nuclei, the contribution of mean field towards the flow is smaller compared to heavier nuclei where binary nucleon-nucleon collisions dominate the scene. The inclusion of momentum-dependent interactions also explains the energy of vanishing flow in $^{12}C+^{12}C$ reaction which was not possible with the static equation of state. An excellent agreement of our theoretical attempt is found for balance energy with experimental data throughout the periodic table.

## Key claim

Momentum-dependent interactions (MDI) have a non-uniform effect on transverse flow and its disappearance across the mass range: in lighter nuclei MDI increases transverse flow and reduces balance energy, whereas in heavier nuclei MDI suppresses transverse flow and increases balance energy, contrary to the uniform trend assumed in prior work.

## Method

Central heavy-ion collisions for systems with combined mass between 24 and 394 were simulated using the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with both a static hard equation of state and a hard equation of state including momentum-dependent interactions (HMD). The directed transverse momentum \(\langle p_x^{dir}\rangle\) was calculated as a function of incident energy between 30 and 800 MeV/nucleon, and the energy of vanishing flow (balance energy) was extracted for each system using constant nucleon-nucleon cross sections of 40, 50, and 55 mb.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy for \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\): 142.88 MeV/nucleon (hard) vs. 133 MeV/nucleon (HMD), a reduction of 9.9 MeV/nucleon with MDI.
- Balance energy for \(^{40}\text{Ar}+^{45}\text{Sc}\): 70.11 MeV/nucleon (hard) vs. 83.4 MeV/nucleon (HMD), an increase of 13.29 MeV/nucleon with MDI.
- Balance energy for \(^{93}\text{Nb}+^{93}\text{Nb}\): 47.18 MeV/nucleon (hard) vs. 57.29 MeV/nucleon (HMD), an increase of 10.11 MeV/nucleon with MDI.
- Balance energy for \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\): 37.44 MeV/nucleon (hard) vs. 40.1 MeV/nucleon (HMD), an increase of 2.6 MeV/nucleon with MDI.
- Difference in \(\langle p_x^{dir}\rangle\) at 400 MeV/nucleon between HMD and hard: 11 MeV/\(c\) for \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\), 7.3 MeV/\(c\) for \(^{40}\text{Ar}+^{45}\text{Sc}\), 2 MeV/\(c\) for \(^{93}\text{Nb}+^{93}\text{Nb}\), and -3.2 MeV/\(c\) for \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\).
- Power-law exponent \(\tau\) for balance energy vs. combined mass: experimental \(\tau_{expt} = -0.37 \pm 0.031\); hard equation of state with 40 mb cross section \(\tau_{Hard^{40}} = -0.7 \pm 0.06\); HMD with 40 mb \(\tau_{HMD^{40}} = -0.38 \pm 0.029\); HMD with 50 mb \(\tau_{HMD^{50}} = -0.4 \pm 0.003\).
- MDI suppresses total binary nucleon-nucleon collisions by 35-45% across the mass range.

## Context

This work extends prior mass-dependence studies of balance energy by Sood and Puri (2004) that used static equations of state, and addresses contradictions in the literature regarding the effect of MDI on flow (e.g., Zhang (1994) vs. Pei (1989) for different systems). It resolves the failure of static hard equations of state to reproduce the balance energy of the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) system, which had been noted by Westmeier (1993). The results enable a consistent description of balance energy across the periodic table using momentum-dependent interactions with a 50 mb cross section, and provide a benchmark for future studies of the nuclear equation of state in the Fermi energy domain.
