# Shell-model test of the rotational-model relation between static quadrupole moments Q(2^+_1), B(E2)'s, and orbital M1 transitions

> In this work, we examine critically the relation between orbital magnetic dipole (scissors mode) strength and quadrupole deformation properties. Assuming a simple K=0 ground state band in an even-even nucleus, the quantities Q(2^+_1) (i.e., the static quadrupole moment) and B(E2)_{0_1 \to 2_1} both are described by a single parameter--the intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0. In the shell model, we can operationally define Q_0(Static) and Q_0(BE2) and see if they are the same. Following a brief excursion to the sd shell, we perform calculations in the fp shell. The nuclei we consider ({44,46,48}Ti and {48,50}Cr) are far from being perfect rotors, but we find that the calculated ratio Q_0(Static)/Q_0(BE2) is in many cases surprisingly close to one. We also discuss the collectivity of orbital magnetic dipole transitions. We find that the large orbital B(M1) strength in {44}Ti relative to {46}Ti and {48}Ti cannot be explained by simple deformation arguments.

# Shell-model test of the rotational-model relation between static quadrupole moments Q(2⁺₁), B(E2)'s, and orbital M1 transitions

## Key claim

The shell-model ratio Q₀(Static)/Q₀(BE2) is surprisingly close to 1 for several fp-shell nuclei (⁴⁴Ti, ⁴⁶Ti, ⁴⁸Ti, ⁴⁸Cr, ⁵⁰Cr) even though these nuclei are far from perfect rotors, but the large orbital B(M1) strength in ⁴⁴Ti relative to ⁴⁶Ti and ⁴⁸Ti cannot be explained by simple deformation arguments alone.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the complete fp shell using the OXBASH program with the FPD6 interaction, using effective charges of 1.5 for protons and 0.5 for neutrons. The authors computed B(E2)₀₁→₂₁ and Q(2⁺) (laboratory quadrupole moment) and extracted operational values of Q₀(Static) and Q₀(BE2) using the rotational-model relations, then compared their ratio to the rotational-model prediction of unity. Additional calculations were performed with the T0FPD6 interaction (T=0 matrix elements set to zero) to test the role of the T=0 interaction in rotational collectivity.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁴Ti (FPD6): Q(2⁺₁) = -20.156 e fm², B(E2) = 607.24 e² fm⁴, Q₀(S)/Q₀(B) = 0.9029
- For ⁴⁶Ti (FPD6): Q(2⁺₁) = -22.071 e fm², B(E2) = 682.06 e² fm⁴, Q₀(S)/Q₀(B) = 0.9329

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model and rotational-model comparisons by Bender, Flocard, and Heenen (2003) and Bender et al. (2004), who performed angular momentum projections on BCS-Hartree-Fock states mainly in the sd shell and neutron-deficient lead region. The authors also reference earlier Hartree-Fock calculations by Jaqaman and Zamick (1984) that correctly predicted signs of static quadrupole moments in the sd shell. The finding that ⁴⁴Ti has large orbital B(M1) strength despite not being more deformed than ⁴⁶Ti contradicts the proportionality between orbital B(M1) and B(E2) established experimentally in heavy deformed nuclei by Ziegler et al. (1990). The results enable future theory-experiment comparisons of magnetic dipole transitions in ⁴⁶Ti and ⁴⁸Ti, where orbital B(M1) has only been extracted in ⁴⁸Ti so far.
