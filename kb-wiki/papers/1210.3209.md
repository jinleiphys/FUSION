# Charmonium dissociation and recombination at LHC: Revisiting comovers

> We present our results on charmonium production at the Large Hadron Collider energies within the comover interaction model. The formalism includes both comover dissociation of $J/ψ$'s and possible secondary $J/ψ$ production through recombination. The estimation of this effect is made without involving free parameters. The comover interaction model also incorporates an analytic treatment of initial-state nuclear shadowing. With these tools, the model successfully describes the centrality, transverse momentum and rapidity dependence of the experimental data from PbPb collisions at the LHC energy of $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV. We present predictions for PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV.

## Key claim

The comover interaction model, extended to include both dissociation and recombination of $J/\psi$ mesons without free parameters, successfully describes the centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity dependence of PbPb collision data at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV, provided that the recombination term is included; the model's predictions for $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV indicate that recombination effects can dominate over suppression, contrary to earlier versions of the model that used different shadowing treatments.

## Method

The comover interaction model (CIM) was applied to PbPb collisions, incorporating an analytic Glauber-Gribov treatment of initial-state nuclear shadowing, comover dissociation with a fixed cross section $\sigma_{co} = 0.65$ mb from low-energy fits, and a recombination term proportional to the squared density of open charm pairs. The recombination cross section was taken equal to the dissociation cross section, and the open charm density was estimated from $pp$ collision data at the same energy, making the extension parameter-free. The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{J/\psi}$ was computed as a function of centrality, transverse momentum, and rapidity, with the recombination strength controlled by the ratio $C(y) = (d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy)^2 / (\sigma_{pp} \, d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy)$.

## Key numbers

- The comover dissociation cross section used throughout is $\sigma_{co} = 0.65$ mb, fixed from fits to low-energy SPS data (Armesto, 1999).
- At mid-rapidity for PbPb at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV, the $pp$ charmonium cross section is $d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy = 3.73$ $\mu$b (ALICE, Abelev 2012), and the input charm cross section $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy$ is taken in the range $0.6 \div 0.8$ mb, corresponding to $C(y)$ values between 2 and 3.
- At forward rapidity ($2.5 < y < 4$) for the same energy, $d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy = 2.23$ $\mu$b, and $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy$ is taken in the range $0.4 \div 0.6$ mb, with a 20% reduction in $C(y)$ relative to mid-rapidity.
- The minimum-bias Glauber-Gribov shadowing factor for $J/\psi$ at $\sqrt{s} = 2.76$ TeV is approximately 0.66, compared to nDSg shadowing ranging from 0.65 to 0.77 and EKS98 shadowing from 0.70 to 0.84.
- For predictions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV, $C(y)$ at mid-rapidity is varied between 2 and 4, corresponding to $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy \approx 0.8 \div 1.15$ mb, using $BR_{ll} \times d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy |_{y=0} \approx 350$ nb and $\sigma_{pp} = 60$ mb.
- At forward rapidity for $\sqrt{s} = 5.5$ TeV, $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy \approx 0.6 \div 0.9$ mb, with $BR_{ll} \times d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy \approx 250$ nb.

## Context

This work extends the comover interaction model (CIM) of Capella et al. (1997, 2000, 2005) by incorporating a recombination term, following the approach of Capella (2007), to address the $J/\psi$ suppression data from RHIC and LHC that could not be explained by dissociation alone. The results contradict earlier CIM predictions (Capella, 2007) that found recombination effects would be overcome by suppression at 5.5 TeV, due to the use of a different shadowing treatment; the present work uses a Glauber-Gribov shadowing that agrees with EKS98/nDSg calculations (Ferreiro, 2008; Rakotozafindrabe, 2011). The model enables parameter-free predictions for future PbPb runs at higher LHC energies and provides a framework for comparing comover-based approaches with thermal/QGP-based recombination models such as those of Andronic (2011) and Zhao (2011).
