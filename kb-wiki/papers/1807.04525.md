# Eta and Etaprime Photoproduction on the Nucleon with the Isobar Model EtaMAID2018

> The isobar model EtaMAID has been updated with new and high precision data for eta and etaprime photoproduction on protons and neutrons from MAMI, ELSA, GRAAL and CLAS. The background is described in a recently developed Regge-cut model, and for the resonance part the whole list of nucleon resonances has been investigated with 21 N* states contributing to eta photoproduction and 12 N* states contributing to etaprime photoproduction. A new approach is discussed to avoid double counting in the overlap region of Regge and resonances. A comparison is done among four newly updated partial waves analyses for observables and partial waves. Finally, the possibility of a narrow resonance near W=1900 MeV is discussed, that would be able to explain unexpected energy and angular dependence of observables in p(gamma,etaprime)p near etaprime threshold.

## Key claim

The updated isobar model EtaMAID2018 provides a continuous description of η and η′ photoproduction from threshold up to W∼5 GeV by combining a Regge-cut background with Breit-Wigner resonances, but the authors caution that resonance parameters remain model-dependent due to the ambiguity in separating Regge and resonance contributions, and they postpone a detailed study of double counting via finite-energy sum rules to future work.

## Method

The non-resonant background is described using a Regge-cut model with ρ, ω, b₁, h₁ trajectories and Regge cuts from ρℙ, ωℙ, ρf₂, ωf₂ exchanges, with a damping factor applied in the resonance region to partially remove double counting. Nucleon resonance excitations are added as generalized Breit-Wigner functions with energy-dependent widths for up to seven decay channels (πN, ππN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, ωN, η′N), including a unitary phase φ for each resonance. The model is fitted to new high-precision data from MAMI, ELSA, GRAAL, and CLAS for both proton and neutron targets.

## Key numbers

- 21 N* states contribute to η photoproduction and 12 N* states contribute to η′ photoproduction
- Threshold energies for decay channels: πN at 1077.84 MeV, ππN at 1217.41 MeV, ηN at 1486.13 MeV, KΛ at 1609.36 MeV, KΣ at 1686.32 MeV, ωN at 1720.92 MeV, η′N at 1896.05 MeV
- The cut-off parameter X for partial width energy dependence is fixed to 450 MeV
- The possibility of a narrow resonance near W=1900 MeV is discussed, which would explain unexpected energy and angular dependence of observables in p(γ,η′)p near η′ threshold
- The ηNN coupling constant is found to be g²_ηNN/4π ≲ 0.1, consistent with earlier observations and in contradiction to SU(3) symmetry predictions near 1

## Context

This work updates the EtaMAID model (Chiang, 2002) with new data and a Regge-cut background approach (Kashevarov, 2017), extending the energy range from threshold to W∼5 GeV. It provides a comparison with four other recent partial wave analyses from the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich, 2018), the Jülich-Bonn group (Rönchen, 2018), and the Kent State University group (2018), and enables future pole-extraction studies using the Laurent-plus-Pietarinen method to determine fundamental t-matrix pole positions and residues.
