# Multiplicity distributions and H_q moments

> It is argued that a multicomponent structure of $e^+e^-$ and $p\bar p$ high energy collisions determines the shapes of multiplicity distributions and oscillations of $H_q$ moments. QCD and convoluted NBD are used for comparison with experimental data. Predictions at LHC and higher energies are shown. Difference between $e^+e^-$ and $p\bar p$ is discussed.

# Multiplicity distributions and H_q moments

## Key claim

The multicomponent structure of high-energy collisions, specifically, independent pair parton interactions (IPPI) in hadron collisions and subjet formation in QCD for lepton collisions, determines the shapes of multiplicity distributions and the oscillations of H_q moments, but no complete similarity between e+e- and p-pbar processes has been observed, contrary to what a single universal mechanism might suggest.

## Method

The author compares experimental multiplicity distributions from e+e- and p-pbar collisions with predictions from perturbative QCD and from the IPPI model, which represents hadron interactions as a sum of negative binomial distributions (NBDs) for independent binary parton collisions. For p-pbar data at 300, 546, 1000, and 1800 GeV from the E735 collaboration, the H_q moments are fitted using the IPPI model with a single parameter k, and the constancy of the parameter m extracted from factorial moments is used as a validity test. The model is then extrapolated to predict multiplicity distributions at LHC (14 TeV) and higher (100 TeV) energies.

## Key numbers

- At 1.8 TeV, the IPPI model fits H_q moments with parameter k = 4.4, yielding m ≈ 12.94 with constancy better than 1.5% up to q = 16.
- The parameter k is 4.4 at 546 GeV and about 7–8 at 300 and 1000 GeV.
- Oscillation amplitudes of H_q moments for p-pbar are about 10^-2, approximately two orders of magnitude larger than those for e+e- at Z^0 energy (about 10^-4).
- The model assumes 2 parton pairs active above 120 GeV, 3 pairs at 300 and 546 GeV, 4 pairs at 1000 and 1800 GeV, 5 pairs at 14 TeV, and 6 pairs at 100 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of multiplicity distributions and H_q moment oscillations previously studied in gluodynamics (Dremin, 1994) and observed in e+e- data by DELPHI, SLD, and L3 collaborations, as well as in hadron-initiated processes. The IPPI model (Dremin, 2004) provides a parameter-economical alternative to the dual parton model (Capella et al., 1994; Kaidalov, 2001) and the clan model (Giovannini and Van Hove, 1986) for describing hadron collisions. The results enable predictions for LHC energies and highlight the need for separate theoretical treatments of lepton and hadron collision mechanisms, as the asymptotic QCD prediction of 1/q^2 dependence for H_q moments is incompatible with the IPPI model behavior.
