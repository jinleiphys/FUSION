# Approximate symmetries in nuclei and the $2νββ$-decay rate

> A nonstandard method for calculating the nuclear $2νββ$ - decay amplitude is proposed. The method is based on the explicit use of those approximate symmetries of a nuclear hamiltonian, which correspond to the operators of allowed $β$ -- transitions. Within the framework of the proposed method the mentioned amplitude is calculated for a wide range of nuclei. The model parameters used in calculations are taken from independent data. Calculated \bb half-lifes are compared with known experimental data.

# Approximate symmetries in nuclei and the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay rate

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude can be calculated using a perturbation theory based on approximate isospin and spin-isospin symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian, rather than the standard QRPA approach. The method claims to avoid the strong sensitivity to the particle-particle interaction strength that plagues QRPA calculations, but the authors acknowledge that the calculated amplitudes agree with experimental data only within a factor of 2–3, except for Te isotopes.

## Method
The method starts from a zero approximation where the symmetries corresponding to the Fermi and Gamow-Teller $\beta$-transition operators are exact, making the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude zero. The actual amplitude is then obtained by first-order perturbation theory in the symmetry-breaking fields: the nuclear mean Coulomb field for isospin violation and the spin-orbital part of the nuclear mean field for spin-isospin violation. The resulting amplitude formula (Eq. 9) expresses $M_G$ in terms of matrix elements of these symmetry-breaking fields between intermediate states, with the collective isobaric analogue state (IAS) and Gamow-Teller state (GTS) explicitly removed from the sum. Calculations use a phenomenological mean field with Landau-Migdal forces, the BCS model for pairing, and the pair-vibration model for magic $\pm$ two nucleon subsystems.

## Key numbers
- The Gamow-Teller state (GTS) exhausts about 70%–80% of the sum rule $3e_q^2(N-Z)$, where $e_q \simeq 0.8$ is the effective charge.
- The IAS exhausts more than 95% of the Fermi sum rule $N-Z$.
- The Landau-Migdal force constants are $F' = 300\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3$ and $G' = 255\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^3$.
- The relative contribution of nucleon pairing to spin-isospin symmetry violation is estimated as $\Delta_{n,p}/\Delta_{ls} \le 20\%$ for intermediate and heavy mass nuclei.
- Calculated $M_{GT}$ amplitudes are given in units of $m_e^{-1}$ (electron mass inverse) and agree with experimental amplitudes within a factor of 2–3, except for Te isotopes.
- The hindrance factors $h_{GT}$ are found to be sufficiently small to justify the perturbation theory approach.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts by Berdichevsky (1990) and Rumyantsev (1995) to use approximate symmetries for $\beta\beta$-decay calculations. It directly addresses the well-known instability of QRPA calculations to the particle-particle interaction strength, as documented by Vogel (1988), Muto (1995), and Civitarese (1995). The method enables a more robust calculation of $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-lives that does not depend sensitively on the particle-particle interaction, and the authors suggest it could be extended to $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay calculations, which have shown similar instability issues (Hirsch 1995, Pantis 1996).
