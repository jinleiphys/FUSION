# Chirality and Reliability of Baryon QCD Sum Rules

> The QCD sum-rule method has been widely used in studying various baryon properties. For a given problem, there are usually more than one sum rules and they do not work equally well. In this paper, we point out that chirality plays an important role in determining the reliability of a baryon sum rule. The contributions of positive- and negative-parity excited baryon states partially cancel each other in the chiral-odd sum rules, but add up in the chiral-even sum rules. As such, the chiral-odd sum rules are generally more reliable than the chiral-even sum rules. This allows one to identify the more reliable sum rules and use them in extracting the ground-state baryon property of interest. This is illustrated in an explicit example.

## Key claim

Chirality determines the reliability of baryon QCD sum rules: the contributions of positive- and negative-parity excited baryon states partially cancel in chiral-odd sum rules but add up in chiral-even sum rules, making chiral-odd sum rules generally more reliable, provided one checks for a valid Borel window where the pole contribution dominates and higher-order OPE terms are under control.

## Method

The authors analyze the two invariant structures of the baryon correlation function, \(\Pi_s\) (chirality-changing) and \(\Pi_q\) (chirality-conserving), after Borel transformation. They compare the phenomenological side, which includes ground-state and excited-state contributions of both parities, to the operator product expansion (OPE) side truncated at finite order. They apply these criteria to sum rules for the proton matrix element \(H = \langle p|\overline{u}u-\overline{d}d|p\rangle/2M_N\) extracted via linear response to an external isovector-scalar field, using \(\beta = -1.2\) and \(\chi = 2.0\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* The chiral-odd sum rule for \(H\) has a valid Borel window where the continuum contribution is less than 50% of the total phenomenological side and the highest-order OPE term contributes less than 10% of the total QCD side.
* The chiral-even sum rule for \(H\) has no valid Borel window; within the chiral-odd sum rule's valid window, the continuum contribution in the chiral-even sum rule exceeds 100% (due to partial cancellation on the right-hand side).
* The leading Borel mass dependence of the continuum model is \(M^4\) in the chiral-odd sum rule and \(M^8\) in the chiral-even sum rule, causing much faster growth of continuum contamination in the latter.

## Context

This work extends the reliability criteria advocated by Leinweber (1990, 1995) and used in sum-rule calculations by Jin (1995), Iqbal, Jin, and Leinweber (1996), Furnstahl, Jin, and Leinweber (1996), and Lee, Leinweber, and Jin (1996). It directly contradicts the common practice of using the ratio method (which fixes the continuum threshold to be the same for two different sum rules) without checking individual sum-rule validity. The paper enables practitioners to identify chiral-odd sum rules for any baryon property and discard unreliable chiral-even sum rules, preventing inconsistent results.
