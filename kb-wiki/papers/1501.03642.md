# Coulomb breakup of $^{37}$Mg and its ground state structure

> We calculate Coulomb breakup of the neutron rich nucleus $^{37}$Mg on a Pb target at the beam energy of 244 MeV/nucleon within the framework of a finite range distorted wave Born approximation theory that is extended to include the effects of projectile deformation. In this theory, the breakup amplitude involves the full wave function of the projectile ground state. Calculations have been carried out for the total one-neutron removal cross section $(σ_{-1n})$, the neutron-core relative energy spectrum, the parallel momentum distribution of the core fragment, the valence neutron angular, and energy-angular distributions. The calculated $σ_{-1n}$ has been compared with the recently measured data to put constraints on the spin parity, and the one-neutron separation energy ($S_n$) of the $^{37}$Mg ground state ($^{37}$Mg$_{gs}$). The dependence of $σ_{-1n}$ on the deformation of this state has also been investigated. While a spin parity assignment of $7/2^-$ for the $^{37}$Mg$_{gs}$ is ruled out by our study, neither of the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^+$ assignments can be clearly excluded. Using the spectroscopic factor of one for both the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^+$ configurations and ignoring the projectile deformation effects, the $S_n$ values of $0.35 \pm 0.06$ MeV and $0.50 \pm 0.07$ MeV, respectively, are extracted for the two configurations. However, the extracted $S_n$ is strongly dependent on the spectroscopic factor and the deformation effects of the respective configuration. The narrow parallel momentum distribution of the core fragment and the strong forward peaking of the valence neutron angular distribution suggest a one-neutron halo configuration in either of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $2s_{1/2}$ configurations of the $^{37}$Mg ground state.

## Key claim

The study rules out a spin parity of \(7/2^-\) for the ground state of \(^{37}\text{Mg}\), but cannot clearly exclude either the \(3/2^-\) or \(1/2^+\) assignments; the extracted one-neutron separation energy depends strongly on the spectroscopic factor and deformation effects of the respective configuration, so the values of \(0.35 \pm 0.06\) MeV and \(0.50 \pm 0.07\) MeV are only valid under the assumption of a spectroscopic factor of one and ignoring projectile deformation.

## Method

The authors used a finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) theory extended to include projectile deformation, with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential describing the valence neutron-core relative motion. The Coulomb breakup of \(^{37}\text{Mg}\) on a Pb target at 244 MeV/nucleon was calculated, and the total one-neutron removal cross section was compared to experimental data from Kobayashi et al. (2014) to constrain the spin parity and one-neutron separation energy of the ground state.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energy extracted for the \(3/2^-\) configuration (assuming spectroscopic factor of one, no deformation) is \(0.35 \pm 0.06\) MeV.
- The one-neutron separation energy extracted for the \(1/2^+\) configuration (assuming spectroscopic factor of one, no deformation) is \(0.50 \pm 0.07\) MeV.
- The beam energy used in the calculation is 244 MeV/nucleon on a Pb target.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used are radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.24\) fm and diffuseness \(a = 0.62\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the FRDWBA theory previously applied to \(^{31}\text{Ne}\) by Shubhchintak et al. (2014) to the neutron-rich nucleus \(^{37}\text{Mg}\), which lies in the \(N = 20-28\) island of inversion. The study addresses conflicting results about shell evolution in this region reported by Gade et al. (2007) and Doornenbal et al. (2013), and aims to quantify the presence of a one-neutron halo structure in \(^{37}\text{Mg}\) and its correlation with ground state deformation. The results also provide an indirect way to determine the rate of the radiative neutron capture reaction \(^{36}\text{Mg}(n,\gamma)^{37}\text{Mg}\), relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis studies.
