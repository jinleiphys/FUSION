# On the practical interest of one-body overlap functions

> Two types of one-body functions extracted from microscpic models of the 8B ground state are compared with one another and with a potential-model wave function. The one-body overlap function seems to have the strongest physical meaning.

## Key claim

The one-body overlap function \(\phi(\mathbf{r})\) appears to have stronger physical meaning than the auxiliary function \(\bar\phi(\mathbf{r})\) for the \(^8\)B ground state, but this conclusion is preliminary because the microscopic calculation from Descouvemont (1994) shows imperfect numerical convergence (a small oscillation between 5 and 9 fm).

## Method

The authors compared two types of one-body functions, the overlap function \(\phi(\mathbf{r})\) and the auxiliary function \(\bar\phi(\mathbf{r}) = N_\mathrm{p}^{-1/2}\phi(\mathbf{r})\), extracted from two microscopic cluster models (Csoto, 1995; Descouvemont, 1994) and a phenomenological potential model (Barker, 1980) for the \(^7\)Be(\(3/2^-\)) + p component of the \(^8\)B ground state. They also analyzed the behavior of these functions for one-particle decay, showing that both functions yield identical decay widths but can lead to different spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- The microscopic \(\phi(\mathbf{r})\) functions from both cluster models are close to each other and to the phenomenological wave function for the \(^7\)Be(\(3/2^-\)) + p component of the \(^8\)B ground state (spin 2, angular momentum 1).
- The auxiliary function \(\bar\phi(\mathbf{r})\) from the Descouvemont (1994) model is significantly different from \(\phi(\mathbf{r})\); \(\bar\phi(\mathbf{r})\) is not available for the Csoto (1995) model.
- The Descouvemont (1994) microscopic functions display a small oscillation between 5 and 9 fm, indicating imperfect numerical convergence.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of one-body functions from microscopic models to potential-model wave functions, building on the formalism of Escher (2002) which showed that such functions solve Schrödinger equations with simple potentials. The results suggest that the overlap function \(\phi(\mathbf{r})\) is more physically meaningful than the auxiliary function \(\bar\phi(\mathbf{r})\), but the numerical issues in the Descouvemont (1994) calculation prevent a firm conclusion. The authors plan to generalize the formalism to one-cluster functions for deducing nucleus-nucleus potentials from cluster models and baryon-baryon potentials from quark cluster models.
