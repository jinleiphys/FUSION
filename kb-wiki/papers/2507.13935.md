# Strangeon Matter: from Stars to Nuggets

> The fact that strange sea quarks are abundant in the nucleons, but with zero net strangeness, is of great importance for understanding the nature of matter condensed by the strong interaction, particularly in the context of the ``gigantic nucleus'' formed by the gravitational collapse of an evolved massive star. We hypothesize that the basic unit of bulk strong matter with the light-flavor symmetry of valence quarks is ``strangeon'', which is the counterpart of the nucleon found in atomic nuclei. In addition to strangeon stars (SnSs) with large baryon number of $A\approx 10^{57}$, strange nuggets (SnNs) with $A\gtrsim 10^{10}$ could also exist in the Universe. Both the SnS and the SnN are explained, with attention to their observational evidence.

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that the basic unit of bulk strong matter with light-flavor symmetry of valence quarks is the "strangeon" (a hadron-like multi-quark bound state, likely with baryon number B=6), and that both strangeon stars (SnSs) with baryon number A≈10^57 and strangeon nuggets (SnNs) with A≳10^10 could exist in the Universe, with the caveat that the critical baryon number for stable SnNs is uncertain due to the difficulty of computing non-perturbative strong interactions.

## Method

The authors develop the strangeon matter (SnM) model by analogy with nucleons in atomic nuclei, proposing that non-perturbative QCD leads to quark-clustering and core-repulsing, forming massive strangeons that behave like classical particles. They apply the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation with SnM equations of state to calculate mass-radius relations for compact stars, and use Bayesian analysis with Lennard-Jones-type potentials to constrain the interaction between strangeons from observational data. For SnNs, they estimate the critical baryon number from the electron Compton wavelength and calculate penetration depths in Earth based on geometric cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number of strangeon stars: A ≈ 10^57
- Critical baryon number for smallest stable strangeon nugget: A_c ≈ (λ_C / r_nucleon)^3 ≈ 10^10
- Internal energy per baryon in neutron stars: ε ≈ 10^2 MeV (from Equation 4, using R ≈ 10 km and M ≈ 1.4 M_⊙)
- Potential well depth between adjacent strangeons: over 10 MeV (from Bayesian analysis)
- SnS maximum mass can reach 3 M_⊙ (stiffer than neutron-star equations of state)
- SnN geometric cross-section formula: σ_SN = k · (A/10^30)^(2/3) · 8.0 × 10^(-6) cm^2
- Penetration depth of SnNs in Earth can exceed Earth's radius (10^8.8 cm)
- Typical SnN speed in the universe: about 10^(-3) c

## Context

This work extends the strange matter hypothesis originally proposed by Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984), and the MIT bag model of Johnson (1975), by introducing a hadron-like basic unit (strangeon) rather than deconfined quarks as the fundamental constituent of bulk strange matter. The strangeon star model, first proposed in 2003, provides an alternative to conventional neutron-star models and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" (where hyperon introduction softens the equation of state inconsistently with observations of massive pulsars like PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0740+6620). The SnN proposal extends the mass spectrum of strongly interacting matter from atomic nuclei (A<300) and pulsars (A≈10^57) to intermediate scales, with SnNs of baryon number 10^25 < A < 10^35 being candidates for dark matter detectable via acoustic methods.
