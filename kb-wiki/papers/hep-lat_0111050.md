# Baryons in Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We include the lowest-lying octet- and decuplet-baryons into partially quenched chiral perturbation theory. Perturbing about the chiral limit of the graded SU(6|3)_L x SU(6|3)_R flavor group of partially quenched QCD, we compute the leading one-loop contributions to the octet-baryon masses, magnetic moments and matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators. We work in the isospin limit and keep two of the three sea quarks degenerate. The usefulness of the non-unique extension of the electric charge matrix and the isovector twist-2 operators from QCD to partially quenched QCD is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQχPT) to include both octet and decuplet baryons, computing the leading one-loop chiral corrections to octet-baryon masses, magnetic moments, and isovector twist-2 matrix elements, but the extension of the electric charge matrix and isovector twist-2 operators from QCD to PQQCD is non-unique and requires careful discussion.

## Method

The authors construct the graded SU(6|3)_L × SU(6|3)_R flavor group of partially quenched QCD with three valence quarks, three ghost quarks, and three sea quarks, working in the isospin limit with two degenerate sea quarks. They embed the octet and decuplet baryons into the 240-dimensional and 138-dimensional representations of SU(6|3)_V respectively, then compute one-loop contributions using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory: the O(m_Q^{3/2}) contributions to octet-baryon masses, the O(m_Q^{1/2}) contributions to magnetic moments, and the O(m_Q log m_Q) contributions to isovector twist-2 operator matrix elements.

## Context

This work extends the partially quenched chiral perturbation theory framework of Sharpe and Shoresh (2001) and earlier quenched QCD calculations by Labrenz and Sharpe (1996) to include baryons, enabling lattice QCD extrapolations from unphysically heavy quark masses to the physical regime. The results allow lattice practitioners to fit counterterms from partially quenched simulations and then make QCD predictions at physical quark masses, with the non-analytic terms computed here dominating the quark-mass dependence at small masses. The paper also discusses how the non-unique extension of operators from QCD to PQQCD affects the interpretation of magnetic moment and twist-2 operator calculations.
