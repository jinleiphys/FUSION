# Determination of $π^+-$ meson polarizabilities from the $γγ->π^+π^-$ process

> A fit of the experimental data to the total cross section of the process $γγ->π^+π^-$ in the energy region from threshold to 2500 MeV has been carried out using dispersion relations with subtractions for the invariant amplitudes, where the dipole and the quadrupole polarizabilities of the charged pion are free parameters. As a result, the sum and the difference of the electric and magnetic dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the charged pion have been found: $(alpha_1+β_1)=(0.18+0.11-0.02)10^(-4)fm^3$, $(alpha_1-β_1)=(13.0+3.6-1.9)10^(-4)fm^3$, $(alpha_2+β_2)=(0.133+- 0.015)10^(-4)fm^5$, $(alpha_2-β_2)=(25.0+0.8-0.3)10^(-4)fm^5$. These values agree with the dispersion sum rule predictions. The value found for the difference of the dipole polarizabilities is consistent with the results obtained from scattering of high energy $π^-$ mesons off the Coulomb field of heavy nuclei (Serpukhov (1983)) and from radiative $π^+$ photoproduction from the proton at MAMI (2005), while it is at variance with the calculations in the framework of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the charged pion have been extracted from a fit to the total cross section of the process γγ → π⁺π⁻ from threshold to 2500 MeV using dispersion relations with subtractions, where the polarizabilities were free parameters. The resulting difference of the dipole polarizabilities (α₁ − β₁) = (13.0⁺²·⁶₋₁·₉) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ agrees with earlier experimental results from Serpukhov (1983) and MAMI (2005) but is at variance with chiral perturbation theory predictions.

## Method

Dispersion relations at fixed t with one subtraction at s = μ² were constructed for the invariant helicity amplitudes M₊₊ and M₊₋ of the γγ → π⁺π⁻ process. The subtraction functions were determined using dispersion relations at fixed s = μ² with two subtractions at t = 0, where the subtraction constants were expressed in terms of the dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities. The dispersion relations were saturated by contributions from ρ(770), b₁(1235), a₁(1260), a₂(1320) mesons in the s-channel and σ, f₀(980), f₀(1370), f₂(1270), f₂(1525) mesons in the t-channel, and the experimental total cross section data from TPC/2γ, Mark II, CELLO, VENUS, ALEPH, and BELLE were fitted in the energy region from threshold to 2500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- (α₁ + β₁) = (0.18⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·₀₂) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ for the charged pion
- (α₁ − β₁) = (13.0⁺²·⁶₋₁·₉) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ for the charged pion
- (α₂ + β₂) = (0.133 ± 0.015) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵ for the charged pion
- (α₂ − β₂) = (25.0⁺⁰·⁸₋₀·₃) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵ for the charged pion
- The f₂(1270) meson effective interaction radius r_f = 0.96 ± 0.01 fm and its two-photon decay width Γ_{f₂→γγ} = 3.05 ± 0.11 keV, taken from the authors' prior analysis of γγ → π⁰π⁰

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersion relation analysis of the γγ → π⁰π⁰ process (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2004) to the charged pion case, determining quadrupole polarizabilities for the first time. The results agree with dispersion sum rule predictions and with experimental values from radiative π⁺ photoproduction at MAMI (Ahrens et al., 2005) and π⁻ scattering off nuclear Coulomb fields at Serpukhov (Antipov et al., 1983), but disagree with two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bürgi, 1996). The analysis enables improved tests of chiral perturbation theory and suggests that the neglect of the broad σ meson contribution in chiral perturbation theory calculations may be one reason for the discrepancy.
