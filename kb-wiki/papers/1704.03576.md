# Collective flow from AA, pA to pp collisions - Toward a unified paradigm

> I give an overview of the latest development in understanding collective phenomena in high-multiplicity hadronic final state from relativistic nucleus-nucleus, proton-nucleus and proton-proton collisions. Upon reviewing the experimental data and confronting them with theoretical models, a unified paradigm in describing the observed collectivity across all hadronic collision systems is emerging. Potential future paths toward addressing key open questions, especially on collectivity in small systems (pp, pA), are discussed.

## Key claim

A unified paradigm for collective flow across all hadronic collision systems (AA, pA, and pp) is emerging, but the interpretation of collectivity in small systems (pp, pA) remains debated between final-state hydrodynamic interactions and initial-state interaction models, with the key open question being whether the "perfect fluid" paradigm established for large AA systems also applies to the smallest systems.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on two-particle correlation functions and Fourier flow harmonics (v_n) from LHC and RHIC experiments, comparing them to theoretical models including relativistic hydrodynamics, parton transport (AMPT), and color glass condensate (CGC) glasma models. The analysis covers symmetric cumulants for harmonic correlations, non-linear response coefficients for mixed harmonics, and rapidity-dependent flow decorrelation measurements using correlator ratios (r_n and R_n).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for QGP in AA collisions: η/s ≲ 0.2, close to the unitarity bound from the holographic principle
- Long-range azimuthal correlations extend over at least 5 to 10 units of rapidity
- In 0-5% central PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, elliptic flow decorrelations measured via r_n ratio as a function of pseudorapidity
- Event plane twist accounts for about 50% of the decorrelation effect observed in r_n in PbPb collisions from ATLAS
- In He3Au collisions at 200 GeV, v_2 and v_3 data in 0-5% central collisions agree with hydrodynamic calculations
- Normalized symmetric cumulant SC(2,3) shows quantitatively similar magnitude between pPb and PbPb collisions as a function of multiplicity

## Context

This review extends the established paradigm of collective flow in large AA systems, where a nearly perfect QGP fluid has been described by hydrodynamic models (Kovtun 2003), to the emerging study of small systems (pp, pA). It builds on prior experimental observations of the "ridge" structure in two-particle correlations from ATLAS and CMS at the LHC, and addresses the intense debate on whether hydrodynamics applies to systems as small as a few fm in size. The work enables future investigations into subnucleonic-scale quantum fluctuations and the fundamental requirements for creating a microscopic fluid, including potential extension to non-hadronic collisions such as ep, eA, and e+e- systems.
