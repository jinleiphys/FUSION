# Charmonium Absorption in the Meson-exchange Model

> We review the meson-exchange model for charmonium absorption by hadrons. This includes the construction of the interaction Lagrangians, the determination of the coupling constants, the introduction of form factors, and the predicted cross sections for $J/ψ$ absorption by both mesons and nucleons. We further discuss the effects due to anomalous parity interactions, uncertainties in form factors, constraints from chiral symmetry, and the change of charmed meson mass in medium on the cross sections for charmonium absorption in hadronic matter.

# Charmonium Absorption in the Meson-exchange Model

## Key claim

The meson-exchange model predicts that the cross sections for $J/\psi$ absorption by pions and rho mesons are a few mb when form factors with cutoff parameters around 1 GeV are included, but these cross sections depend sensitively on the choice of form factors and are subject to large uncertainties from the composite nature of hadrons.

## Method

The authors construct effective hadronic Lagrangians from SU(4) flavor symmetry by treating vector mesons as gauge particles via minimal substitution, generating three-vector-meson, four-point, and pseudoscalar-vector couplings. Coupling constants are determined from empirical decay widths (e.g., $D^*$ decay) and the vector meson dominance model, while monopole form factors with cutoff parameters of 1 or 2 GeV are introduced at interaction vertices to account for hadron finite size. Cross sections for $J/\psi$ absorption by $\pi$ and $\rho$ mesons are then computed from the scattering amplitudes after averaging over initial spins and summing over final spins.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\pi DD^*} = 5.6$ from the $D^*$ decay width of $\sim 96$ keV measured by CLEO (Ahmed 2001)
- $g_{\rho DD} = g_{\rho D^* D^*} = 2.52$ and $g_{\psi DD} = g_{\psi D^* D^*} = 7.64$ from vector meson dominance relations
- With cutoff $\Lambda = 1$ GeV, $\sigma_{\pi \psi} \approx 3$ mb and $\sigma_{\rho \psi} \approx 2$ mb for $J/\psi$ absorption
- Without form factors, $\sigma_{\pi \psi}$ is about 0.3 mb at kinetic energy 0.8 GeV
- The total $J/\psi$ absorption cross section by a nucleon is at most 5 mb with $\Lambda = 1$ GeV
- The $\pi DD^*$ form factor from QCD sum rules gives a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_\pi = 1.2$ GeV for an off-shell pion and $\Lambda_D = 3.5$ GeV for an off-shell $D$ meson, corresponding to $\Lambda \sim 3$ GeV

## Context

This review extends earlier meson-exchange model calculations by Matinyan (1998) that used only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector couplings and obtained cross sections of about 0.3 mb, and by Haglin (1999), Lin (1999), and Oh (2000) that included three-vector-meson and four-point couplings. The predicted cross sections (a few mb) are comparable to those from the quark-interchange model by Martins (1994) and Wong (1999) but much larger than the perturbative QCD result of about 0.1 mb. The results enable phenomenological studies of $J/\psi$ suppression in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC, and the authors identify the need for an improved approach combining chiral symmetry for light hadrons with heavy quark symmetry for heavy hadrons.
