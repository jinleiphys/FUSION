# Scale Anomaly and "Soft" Pomeron in QCD

> We propose a new non-perturbative approach to hadronic interactions at high energies and small momentum transfer, which is based on the scale anomaly of QCD and emphasizes the role of semi-classical vacuum fields. We find that the hadron scattering amplitudes exhibit Regge behavior and evaluate the intercept alpha(0) of the corresponding trajectory. Both the intercept and the scale for the slope of the trajectory appear to be determined by the energy density of non-perturbative QCD vacuum (the gluon condensate). Numerically, we find Delta = alpha(0) - 1 = 0.08 -0.1, consistent with the values ascribed phenomenologically to the ``soft'' Pomeron. For arbitrary numbers of colors N_c and flavors N_f, Delta is found to be proportional to (N_f/N_c)^2; however, in the large N_c (N_f fixed) limit, Delta \sim N_c^0.

# Scale Anomaly and "Soft" Pomeron in QCD

## Key claim
The authors propose that the "soft" Pomeron intercept arises from a non-perturbative contribution to the BFKL kernel that becomes the leading term (order α_S^0) due to the scale anomaly of QCD, rather than being a perturbative effect. The result depends on the matching scale M_0 extracted from sum rules, which can vary between 4 and 6 GeV^2, leading to a range for the intercept rather than a single precise value.

## Method
The authors isolate a specific class of next-to-leading order corrections to the BFKL kernel corresponding to two-gluon production in the scalar color-singlet channel (J^PC = 0^++). They show that the vertex for this process is proportional to the trace of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, and using low-energy theorems for the correlator of this operator, they demonstrate that the contribution becomes independent of the coupling constant. The non-perturbative spectral density of the scalar gluon operator is estimated using an effective chiral Lagrangian for pions, and the resulting integral is evaluated with a matching scale M_0 that separates perturbative and non-perturbative regimes.

## Key numbers
- Δ = α(0) − 1 = 0.08 to 0.1, consistent with the phenomenological "soft" Pomeron intercept
- For M_0^2 = 4 GeV^2, Δ = 0.082
- The matching scale M_0^2 ≈ 4 to 6 GeV^2, extracted from sum rule analysis of the vacuum energy density
- Vacuum energy density ε_vac ≈ −(0.24 GeV)^4
- For pure gluodynamics (no light quarks), Δ_gluodynamics ≈ 0.01, using scalar glueball mass M_R ≈ 1.65 GeV from lattice QCD
- The slope of the Pomeron trajectory α'_P(0) = 0.25 GeV^−2 is close to 1/M_0^2

## Context
This work extends the BFKL approach (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov, 1970s) to the non-perturbative domain, addressing the problem that next-to-leading order corrections to the BFKL equation are large and drive the intercept below phenomenological values. It builds on the low-energy theorems of Novikov, Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1980s) for the energy-momentum tensor correlator, and on earlier work by Kharzeev and collaborators on low-energy dipole-dipole scattering. The approach provides a systematic framework for the "soft" Pomeron that complements the McLerran-Venugopalan approach to high-energy collisions and the topological expansion ideas of Veneziano. It enables a connection between the vacuum structure of QCD and high-energy scattering phenomenology.
