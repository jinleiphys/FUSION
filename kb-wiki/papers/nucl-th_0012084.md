# p-Shell Nuclei and Two-Frequency Shell Model with a Realistic Effective Interaction

> We have studied p-shell nuclei using a two-frequency shell-model approach with an effective interaction derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential by means of a G-matrix folded-diagram method. First, we briefly describe our derivation of the effective interaction in a model space composed of harmonic hoscillator wave functions with two different length parameters, b_in and b_out, for the core and the valence orbits, respectively. Then we present some selected results of our calculations. We show that a good agreement with experiment is obtained, which is definitely better than that provided by a standard one-frequency calculation. A comparison with results obtained from large-basis shell-model calculations is also made.

## Key claim

The two-frequency shell-model (TFSM) approach, using an effective interaction derived from the Bonn-A potential, yields ground-state binding energies and spectra for p-shell nuclei that agree with experiment as well as or better than large-basis shell-model calculations, provided the valence oscillator length parameter \(b_{\rm out}\) is chosen separately from the core parameter \(b_{\rm in}\) and adjusted per isotopic chain (2.25 fm for He isotopes and their mirrors, 2.0 fm for Li and B isotopes).

## Method

The authors derived an effective interaction \(V_{\rm eff}\) from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential via a \(G\)-matrix folded-diagram method, using harmonic-oscillator wave functions with two different length parameters: \(b_{\rm in}=1.45\) fm for the \({}^{4}\)He core and a variable \(b_{\rm out}\) for the valence \(0p_{3/2}\) and \(0p_{1/2}\) orbits. The \(G\)-matrix was calculated with a Pauli operator defined by boundaries \(n_1=1\), \(n_2=6\), and \(n_3=21\), and the folded-diagram series was summed to all orders via the Lee-Suzuki iteration method, including \(\hat{Q}\)-box diagrams through second order in \(G\). Shell-model calculations were performed with the OXBASH code, using experimental single-particle energies (\(\epsilon_{3/2}=0.886\) MeV, \(\epsilon_{1/2}-\epsilon_{3/2}=4.0\) MeV).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of \({}^{6}\)He: experimental 29.27 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.25\) fm gives 29.81 MeV (overestimate by 0.54 MeV).
- Ground-state binding energy of \({}^{6}\)Li: experimental 31.99 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 32.35 MeV (overestimate by 0.36 MeV).
- Ground-state binding energy of \({}^{7}\)Li: experimental 39.24 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 38.86 MeV (underestimate by 0.38 MeV).
- Ground-state binding energy of \({}^{8}\)Li: experimental 41.28 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 41.23 MeV (within 0.05 MeV).
- Ground-state binding energy of \({}^{8}\)B: experimental 37.74 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 37.67 MeV (within 0.07 MeV).
- For the \({}^{8}\)Li spectrum, TFSM excitation energies deviate from experiment by less than 0.3 MeV for all states except the second \(3^+\) state, where the discrepancy is about 1 MeV; in contrast, one-frequency calculations show discrepancies ranging from 0.3 to 2.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the two-frequency shell-model approach developed by Kuo et al. (1996, 1997) to systematically study p-shell nuclei, using a realistic interaction derived from the Bonn-A potential rather than empirical interactions. The results are compared to large-basis shell-model calculations by Navrátil et al. (1998) and to one-frequency calculations by Kuo et al. (1999); the TFSM achieves comparable or better agreement with experiment despite using a much smaller model space. The approach enables practical calculations for halo nuclei and neutron-rich systems where extended valence orbits are needed, without resorting to very large active spaces.
