# Triangular flow of thermal photons from an event-by-event hydrodynamic model for 2.76A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> We calculate the triangular flow parameter $v_3$ of thermal photons from an event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic model for $0--40\%$ central collisions of Pb nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV at LHC. $v_3$ determined with respect to the participant plane (PP) is found to be non-zero, positive and its $p_T$ dependence is qualitatively similar to the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$(PP) of thermal photons in the range $1 \le p_T \le 6$ GeV/$c$. In the range $p_T \, \le $ 3 GeV/$c$, $v_3$(PP) is found to be about $50--75\%$ of $v_2$(PP) and for $p_T \, >$ 3 GeV/$c$ the two anisotropy parameters become comparable. The local fluctuations in the initial density distribution as well as the initial global geometry of the produced matter in the event-by-event hydrodynamic framework are responsible for this substantial value of $v_3({\rm PP})$. However, as expected, the triangular flow parameter calculated with respect to the reaction plane $v_3$(RP) is found to be close to zero. We show that $v_3$(PP) strongly depends on the value of the fluctuation size scale $σ$ especially in the higher $p_T \, (\ge 3 {\rm GeV}/c)$ region where a larger value of $σ$ results in a smaller $v_3({\rm PP})$. In addition, the $v_3{\rm (PP)}$ is found to increase with the assumed formation time of the thermalized system.

## Key claim
The triangular flow parameter v3 of thermal photons, calculated with respect to the participant plane, is non-zero and positive for 0–40% central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, reaching about 50–75% of the elliptic flow v2 at low pT and becoming comparable to v2 above 3 GeV/c. However, this result depends strongly on the fluctuation size scale σ and the assumed formation time of the thermalized system, and the mechanism is indirect: initial fluctuations modify the radial flow pattern rather than directly mapping initial triangular eccentricity into photon v3.

## Method
The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic model with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, where initial entropy density is distributed around wounded nucleons using a 2D Gaussian with width σ. Thermal photon emission was calculated using complete leading-order plasma rates and hadronic rates, integrated over the space-time evolution of the fireball, for 0–40% central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. The triangular flow v3 was computed with respect to both the participant plane (PP) and the reaction plane (RP) by averaging over 400 random events.

## Key numbers
- v3(PP) at pT = 1 GeV/c is about 50% of v2(PP) for the same centrality bin (0–40%).
- v3(PP) is about 80% of v2(PP) at pT = 3 GeV/c, and the difference is 10–15% at pT = 5 GeV/c.
- For σ = 0.4 fm (default), v3(PP) is positive and significant in the range 1 ≤ pT ≤ 6 GeV/c; for σ = 0.8 fm, v3(PP) is similar below pT ≤ 2 GeV/c but falls sharply at higher pT.
- For τ0 = 0.6 fm/c, v3(PP) rises rapidly for pT ≤ 3 GeV/c and becomes constant above 3 GeV/c, whereas for τ0 = 0.14 fm/c, v3(PP) drops with increasing pT.
- v3(RP) is zero within standard errors for all conditions studied.
- Adding NLO plasma rates to LO rates increases thermal photon production by 10–15% for pT < 2 GeV/c, with negligible difference above 2 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends earlier event-by-event hydrodynamic studies of thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow by Chatterjee et al. (2013, 2014) to the triangular flow harmonic v3, and complements a study by Vujanovic et al. (2013) that investigated triangular flow and higher harmonics of thermal photons using a viscous hydrodynamic model with KLN and Glauber initial conditions. The results show that photon v3 probes initial-state geometry indirectly via generation of additional transverse flow, offering constraints for dynamical modeling of the pre-equilibrium phase and the fluctuation size scale.
