# Shell model study of the isobaric chains A=50, A=51 and A=52

> Shell model calculations in the full pf-shell are carried out for the A=50, 51 and 52 isobars. The most frequently used effective interactions for the pf-shell, KB3 and FPD6 are revisited and their behaviour at the N=28 and Z=28 closures examined. Cures to their -relatively minor- defaults are proposed, and a new mass dependent version called KB3G is released. Energy spectra, electromagnetic transitions and moments as well as beta decay properties are computed and compared with the experiment and with the results of the earlier interactions. A high quality description is achieved. Other miscellaneous topics are addressed; the Coulomb energy differences of the yrast states of the mirror pair 51Mn-51Fe and the systematics of the magnetic moments of the N=28 isotones.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new mass-dependent effective interaction, KB3G, which corrects the overly large quasiparticle gap in \({}^{56}\)Ni produced by the earlier KB3 interaction while maintaining equivalent results for nuclei already studied with KB3; the authors state that "a high quality description is achieved" but note that the monopole adjustments were made via trial and error fitting and that for non-yrast states in several nuclei they used a truncated (t=5) space rather than the full pf-shell space.

## Method

Full pf-shell shell model calculations were performed for the A=50, 51, and 52 isobars using the Lanczos algorithm in the m-scheme (code ANTOINE) or in J-coupled scheme (code NATHAN). The authors revisited the KB3 and FPD6 effective interactions, proposed monopole modifications to correct their behavior at the N=28 and Z=28 closures, and released a new mass-dependent version called KB3G with a (42/A)^{1/3} scaling. Energy spectra, electromagnetic transitions and moments, and beta decay properties were computed and compared with experimental data and with results from the earlier interactions.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle gap for \({}^{56}\)Ni: experimental 6.39 MeV, KB3 gives 8.57 MeV (t=0) and 7.73 MeV (t=3), while KB3G gives 7.12 MeV (t=0) and 6.40 MeV (t=3), correcting the ~1 MeV excess of KB3.
- The quasiparticle gap for \({}^{48}\)Ca: experimental 4.80 MeV, KB3 gives 5.25 MeV (t=0) and 5.17 MeV (full), while KB3G gives 4.73 MeV (t=0) and 4.69 MeV (full).
- For \({}^{50}\)Ti, the B(E2) for \(2^+\rightarrow 0^+\) is 58(9) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) experimentally versus 88 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) theoretically; for \(4^+\rightarrow 2^+\) it is 60(1) versus 86; for \(6^+\rightarrow 4^+\) it is 34(1) versus 41.
- The magnetic moment of the \({}^{51}\)V ground state (\(J=7/2^-\)) is +5.14870573(18) \(\mu_N\) experimentally versus +4.99 \(\mu_N\) theoretically; the quadrupole moment is -5.2(10) \(e\)fm\(^2\) experimentally versus -6.5 \(e\)fm\(^2\) theoretically.
- For \({}^{51}\)Ti, the B(E2) for \(11/2^-\rightarrow 7/2^-\) is 95(17) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) experimentally versus 95 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) theoretically; for \(15/2^-\rightarrow 11/2^-\) it is 62(24) versus 53.
- The magnetic moment of the \({}^{51}\)Cr ground state (\(J=7/2^-\)) is -0.934(5) \(\mu_N\) experimentally versus -0.886 \(\mu_N\) theoretically.

## Context

This work extends the full pf-shell calculations of Poves and collaborators (1999) up to A=52 and provides a systematic comparison of the KB3 and FPD6 effective interactions, which were the most frequently used interactions for the pf-shell. The new KB3G interaction corrects the overly large quasiparticle gap in \({}^{56}\)Ni that was identified in earlier studies of the deformed excited band of \({}^{56}\)Ni by Rudolf and collaborators (1999). The results enable future shell model studies of nuclei approaching and beyond \({}^{56}\)Ni with a more reliable effective interaction, and the analysis of Coulomb energy differences in the mirror pair \({}^{51}\)Mn-\({}^{51}\)Fe and magnetic moments of N=28 isotones provides benchmarks for understanding isospin-symmetry breaking and single-particle structure in this region.
