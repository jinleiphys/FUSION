# Testing semi-local chiral two-nucleon interaction in selected electroweak processes

> The recently developed semi-local improved chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction is used for the first time to study several electromagnetic and weak processes at energies below the pion production threshold. Cross sections and selected polarization observables for deuteron photodisintegration, nucleon-deuteron radiative capture, three-body $^3$He photodisintegration as well as capture rates for decays of the muonic $^2$H and $^3$He atoms are calculated. The Lippmann-Schwinger and Faddeev equations in momentum space are solved to obtain nuclear states. The electromagnetic current operator is taken as a single nucleon current supplemented by many-body contributions induced via the Siegert theorem. For muon capture processes the nonrelativistic weak current together with the dominant relativistic corrections is used. Our results compare well with experimental data, demonstrating the same quality as is observed for the semi-phenomenological AV18 potential. Compared to the older version of the chiral potential with a nonlocal regularization, a much smaller cut-off dependence is found for the state-of-art chiral local interaction employed in this paper. Finally, estimates of errors due to the truncation of the chiral expansion are given.

## Key claim

The semi-local chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction with coordinate-space regularization yields predictions for electroweak processes that match the quality of the semi-phenomenological AV18 potential, while exhibiting a much smaller cut-off dependence than the older chiral potential with nonlocal regularization, though the study omits three-nucleon forces and uses an incomplete model of the electromagnetic current via the Siegert theorem.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for two-nucleon systems and Faddeev equations in momentum space for three-nucleon systems to obtain nuclear states. For electromagnetic processes, the current operator was approximated by a single nucleon current supplemented by many-body contributions via the Siegert theorem; for muon capture, a nonrelativistic single nucleon weak current with leading relativistic corrections was used. Calculations employed partial waves up to two-body total angular momentum j ≤ 3 and three-body total angular momentum J ≤ 15/2, using the semi-local chiral NN interaction at orders up to N⁴LO with regularization parameter R values from 0.8 to 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for γ + d → p + n at photon energies up to 80 MeV: chiral N⁴LO with R=0.9 fm and SNC+Siegert current yields excellent agreement with experimental data, with predictions practically indistinguishable from AV18 below approximately 30 MeV
- Cut-off dependence of differential cross section at Eγ=100 MeV reaches about 20% at small proton c.m. scattering angles for R values 0.8-1.2 fm; omitting R=1.2 fm reduces this to smaller variation
- For deuteron analyzing power T20 at energies up to 140 MeV: difference between N³LO and N⁴LO predictions below 1.6%, difference between predictions with different R values does not exceed 7.3% (2.3% if R=1.2 fm omitted)
- For deuteron analyzing power T22: difference between N³LO and N⁴LO predictions below 1.3%, difference between predictions with different R values does not exceed 3.1% (1.4% if R=1.2 fm omitted)
- Photon asymmetry Σγ at θp=90° measured up to 4.05 MeV: predictions at N²LO, N³LO and N⁴LO practically overlap with excellent agreement to data
- At Eγ=60.8 MeV, the improved chiral interaction yields theoretical uncertainty approximately two times smaller than for the older nonlocal force
- For n + d → ³H + γ at En=9.0 MeV: N³LO and N⁴LO predictions lie about 10% above experimental data
- For p + d → ³He + γ at Ep=95 MeV: LO predictions are relatively close to higher-order predictions, with small truncation errors and cut-off dependence

## Context

This work extends the testing of the semi-local chiral NN interaction developed by Epelbaum et al. (2015) from NN scattering and elastic Nd scattering to electromagnetic and weak reactions below pion production threshold. It contrasts with earlier studies by Rozpedzik et al. (2010) and Skibinski et al. (2011) that used the older nonlocal chiral potential and found strong cut-off dependence (25% at Eγ=30 MeV) that precluded detailed physical conclusions. The results enable future studies with consistent three-nucleon forces and explicit chiral exchange currents, which are under development, and would benefit from precise measurements of deuteron analyzing powers below Eγ=140 MeV.
