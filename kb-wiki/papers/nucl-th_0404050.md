# Intensity interferometry of direct photons emitted in Au+Au collisions

> We calculate the two-body correlation function of direct photons produced in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Our calculation includes contributions from the early pre-equilibrium phase in which photons are produced via hard parton scatterings as well as radiation of photons from a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) and the subsequent expanding hadron gas. We find that high energy photon intensity interferometry provides a faithful probe of the details of the space-time evolution and of the early reaction stages of the system.

# Intensity interferometry of direct photons emitted in Au+Au collisions

## Key claim
High-energy photon intensity interferometry can serve as a faithful probe of the space-time evolution and early reaction stages of the system formed in Au+Au collisions, but only if the transverse momentum is sufficiently high (k_T >= 2 GeV/c) that pre-equilibrium photons dominate over thermal photons from the QGP and hadron gas phases.

## Method
The authors combined a parton cascade model (PCM) to describe the pre-equilibrium phase, where photons are produced via hard parton scatterings, with a boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamic calculation for photon emission from a thermalized QGP and subsequent hadron gas. The two-photon correlation function was computed using the Wigner function scheme implemented in the Hansa code, with emission vertices smeared to incorporate the uncertainty relation, using 125,000 events from the VNI/BMS parton cascade model supplemented by Monte-Carlo sampling of the hydrodynamic calculation.

## Key numbers
- 88% of all photons produced via hard parton scattering in the PCM are emitted within the first 0.3 fm/c of the reaction
- At k_T = 1 GeV/c, pre-equilibrium and thermal photons each contribute roughly 50% to the total photon yield
- At k_T = 2 GeV/c, direct photons from the PCM outweigh thermal photons by one order of magnitude
- For photons at k_T = (2 +/- 0.2) GeV, the sideward radius R_side is approximately 3.8 fm and the outward radius R_out is approximately 3.7 fm
- The longitudinal correlation radius at k_T = 2 GeV is R_long approximately 1.6 fm
- At k_T = 0.2 GeV, the calculation predicts R_side of about 5 fm with R_out/R_side approximately 2

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of photon interferometry of the QGP by Neuhauser, Srivastava (1993), and others, which focused only on photons emitted from a thermalized source. The authors incorporate for the first time contributions from the pre-equilibrium phase using the parton cascade model VNI/BMS, which they had previously used to calculate inclusive photon spectra. The results enable experimental distinction between pre-equilibrium and thermal photon sources at RHIC, with the prediction that high-energy photons (k_T >= 2 GeV/c) will reveal a small source of brief duration characteristic of pre-equilibrium emission, contrasting with the larger source seen at lower momenta from the QGP and hadron gas.
