# Thermalization and isotropization in heavy-ion collisions

> I review our current understanding of the processes driving the thermalization and isotropization of the quark gluon plasma (QGP) created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions (URHICs). I begin by discussing the phenomenological evidence in favor of the creation of a thermal but momentum-space anisotropic QGP in URHICs. I then discuss the degree of isotropization using viscous (dissipative) hydrodynamics, weak-coupling approaches to QGP dynamics, and strong-coupling approaches to QGP dynamics. Finally, I report on recent progress in the area of real-time non-abelian gauge field simulations and non-abelian Boltzmann-Vlasov-based hard-loop simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quark gluon plasma (QGP) created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is thermal but strongly anisotropic in momentum space, possessing two temperatures (transverse and longitudinal), and that this anisotropy persists for multiple fm/c rather than being a brief transient effect.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical approaches to QGP thermalization and isotropization including viscous (dissipative) hydrodynamics, weak-coupling approaches (hard-loop simulations of chromo-Weibel instabilities, classical Yang-Mills simulations), and strong-coupling approaches using AdS/CFT with numerical general relativity. The review synthesizes results from 0+1d boost-invariant systems and 1+1d radially symmetric systems, comparing pressure anisotropies and thermalization timescales across these frameworks.

## Key numbers

- Navier-Stokes viscous hydrodynamics predicts longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio (P_L/P_T) ≈ 0.5 for RHIC-like initial conditions (T₀ = 400 MeV at τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c) with η/S = 1/4π
- For LHC-like initial conditions (T₀ = 600 MeV at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c) with η/S = 1/4π, (P_L/P_T) ≈ 0.35
- Shear relaxation time τ_π ≈ 0.1 fm/c for RHIC-like conditions and τ_π ≈ 0.07 fm/c for LHC-like conditions in the strong coupling limit of N=4 SYM
- AdS/CFT calculations show evolution begins to be well-approximated by viscous hydrodynamics after w = T_eff τ ≈ 0.63, corresponding to τ ≈ 0.2 fm/c for LHC initial conditions
- BMSS parametric estimate gives τ_therm ≈ α_s^(-13/5) Q_s^(-1), yielding τ_therm ≈ 3.2 fm/c at RHIC (Q_s = 1.4 GeV) and 2.3 fm/c at LHC (Q_s = 2 GeV) for α_s = 0.3
- Kurkela-Moore revised estimate gives τ_therm ≈ α_s^(-5/2) Q_s^(-1)
- Hard-loop simulations show longitudinal thermalization (Boltzmann distribution) established within τ̃ ≈ 1 (approximately 1 fm/c for LHC, 1.4 fm/c for RHIC), but large pressure anisotropies persist for at least 5-6 fm/c
- CGC framework with NLO corrections finds early-time pressure anisotropies on the order of 0.01-0.5 depending on g_s = 0.1-0.4

## Context

This review extends the discussion of QGP thermalization beyond early ideal hydrodynamics fits (Huovinen 2001, Hirano 2002) that suggested isotropization at 0.5-1 fm/c, incorporating modern viscous hydrodynamics (Muronga 2001, Baier 2006, Romatschke 2007) and anisotropic hydrodynamics (Martinez 2010, Florkowski 2010). It contrasts the BMSS weak-coupling thermalization estimate (Baier 2000) with the Kurkela-Moore revision (2011) that includes chromo-Weibel instabilities, and compares with strong-coupling AdS/CFT results (Heller 2011, van der Schee 2013). The work enables future phenomenological studies that must incorporate momentum-space anisotropy into calculations of photon production, dilepton production, heavy quarkonium suppression, and jet suppression.
