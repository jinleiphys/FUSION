# Unquenched simulations of four-nucleon interactions

> Exploratory simulations of four-nucleon interactions are performed taking into account the dynamical effects of internal nucleon loops. The four-nucleon interactions in the isoscalar and isovector channels are described by Yukawa interactions with auxiliary scalar fields. The nucleon mass and the average field lengths of the scalar fields are determined as a function of nucleon hopping parameter and Yukawa coupling strengths. There are no problems with "exceptional configurations" at strong couplings which make quenched simulations unreliable.

## Key claim

Unquenched simulations of four-nucleon interactions, including dynamical nucleon loops, eliminate the problem of "exceptional configurations" that made quenched simulations unreliable at strong bare couplings (max(|C0|,|C1|) >= 0.3).

## Method

The author performed Euclidean Monte Carlo simulations of a lattice Yukawa model with Wilson fermions for nucleons and auxiliary scalar fields in the isoscalar and isovector channels, using the two-step Polynomial Hybrid Monte Carlo (TSPHMC) algorithm. The nucleon mass and average squared lengths of the scalar fields were measured on an 8^3 * 32 lattice as functions of the nucleon hopping parameter kappa and the bare Yukawa couplings C0 and C1, with at least 5000 trajectories per simulation sample.

## Key numbers

- Simulations were performed on a lattice of size 8^3 * 32.
- The number of trajectories per simulation sample was at least 5000, in some cases up to 15000.
- The quenched approximation was previously found to be impossible for bare couplings in the range max(|C0|,|C1|) >= 0.3 due to exceptional configurations; the present unquenched simulations showed no such configurations even at stronger couplings.

## Context

This work extends the quenched simulations of Montvay and Urbach (2012) by including dynamical nucleon loops, thereby removing the exceptional configuration problem that plagued the earlier approach. The model is a special case of a renormalizable Higgs-Yukawa model with scalar kinetic terms omitted and quartic self-couplings set to zero; the author notes that the complete model is expected to have a trivial continuum limit. Future work should determine renormalized four-nucleon couplings and tune physical quantities such as the scattering length in the 1S0 channel and the deuterium binding energy, and must eventually introduce pion fields and their Yukawa interactions for a proper description of nuclear interactions.
