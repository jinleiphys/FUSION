# Possible Presence and Properties of Multi Chiral Pair-Bands in Odd-Odd Nuclei with the Same Intrinsic Configuration

> Applying a relatively simple particle-rotor model to odd-odd nuclei, possible presence of multi chiral pair-bands is looked for, where chiral pair-bands are defined not only by near-degeneracy of the levels of two bands but also by almost the same expectation values of squared components of three angular-momenta that define chirality. In the angular-momentum region where two pairs of chiral pair-bands are obtained the possible interband M1/E2 decay from the second-lowest chiral pair-bands to the lowest chiral pair-bands is studied, with the intention of finding how to experimentally identify the multi chiral pair-bands. It is found that up till almost band-head the intraband M1/E2 decay within the second chiral pair-bands is preferred rather than the interband M1/E2 decay to the lowest chiral pair-bands, though the decay possibility depends on the ratio of actual decay energies. It is also found that chiral pair-bands in our model and definition are hardly obtained for $γ$ values outside the range $25^{\circ} < γ< 35^{\circ}$, although either a near-degeneracy or a constant energy-difference of several hundreds keV between the two levels for a given angular-momentum $I$ in "a pair bands" is sometimes obtained in some limited region of $I$. In the present model calculations the energy difference between chiral pair-bands is always one or two orders of magnitude smaller than a few hundreds keV, and no chiral pair-bands are obtained, which have an almost constant energy difference of the order of a few hundreds keV in a reasonable range of $I$.

# Possible Presence and Properties of Multi Chiral Pair-Bands in Odd-Odd Nuclei with the Same Intrinsic Configuration

## Key claim

Using a particle-rotor model with parameters most favorable for producing chiral bands, two lowest ΔI = 1 bands form an almost perfect chiral pair-bands over a range of I varying by as much as 10 units, and a second chiral pair-bands can also be obtained over a range of at least several I units, but only for γ values between 25° and 35°, and with energy differences between chiral partner levels that are always one or two orders of magnitude smaller than a few hundred keV, never approaching the nearly constant energy differences of several hundred keV reported in experimental data for A ≈ 130 nuclei.

## Method

A particle-rotor model Hamiltonian for odd-odd nuclei was numerically diagonalized, consisting of a triaxial collective rotor coupled to one high-j quasiproton and one high-j quasineutron obtained from BCS approximation, using irrotational-flow-like moments of inertia. Chiral pair-bands were identified by requiring both near-degeneracy of two ΔI = 1 bands and very similar (within ±20 percent) expectation values of squared components of the three angular momenta (core rotation R, proton angular momentum j_p, neutron angular momentum j_n) that define chirality. Calculations were performed for two configurations: a proton-particle and neutron-hole in the h_{11/2}-shell, and a proton-hole in the g_{9/2}-shell with a neutron-particle in the h_{11/2}-shell.

## Key numbers

- Chiral pair-bands were obtained for the h_{11/2} proton-particle/neutron-hole configuration in the region 16 ≤ I ≤ 24 for the lowest pair (b1, b2) and 18 ≤ I ≤ 24 for the second pair (b3, b4), at γ = 30°.
- For the g_{9/2} proton-hole / h_{11/2} neutron-particle configuration, chiral pair-bands formed in the region 15 ≤ I ≤ 26 for the lowest pair and 17 ≤ I ≤ 22 for the second pair, at γ = 30°.
- B(M1; I → I-1) values for interband transitions from the second chiral pair-bands to the first were at least a factor of 50 smaller than competing intraband M1 transitions within the second pair.
- Chiral pair-bands were hardly obtained for γ values outside the range 25° < γ < 35°.
- The energy difference between chiral partner levels was always one or two orders of magnitude smaller than a few hundred keV.
- For the g_{9/2}/h_{11/2} configuration at γ = 20°, the energy difference between the third and fourth bands was nearly constant in the region 16 ≤ I ≤ 24, but the R_i values showed these bands were far from being chiral pair-bands.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of nuclear chirality, which was first considered by Frauendorf and Meng (1997), and follows earlier theoretical work by Koike, Starosta, and collaborators (2002) and Dimitrov, Ragnarsson, and Døssing (2009) showing that multiple pair-bands with near-degenerate levels could be obtained for γ ≈ 30°. The study was prompted by the experimental finding by Timar and collaborators (2013) of four or five very similar ΔI = 1 bands with possibly the same parity in ¹⁰⁴Rh. The results challenge interpretations of observed pair-bands in the A ≈ 130 and 110 regions as chiral doublets, since the model cannot reproduce the constant energy differences of several hundred keV reported experimentally, and suggest that such observed pair-bands may arise from other mechanisms. The work also contrasts with claims by Meng and collaborators (2006) of multiple chiral pair-bands for different deformations in ¹⁰⁶Rh, and with interpretations by Ayangeakaa and collaborators (2013) of chiral doublet bands in ¹³³Ce at γ = 11°–15°, which the present model finds too weakly triaxial to support chirality.
