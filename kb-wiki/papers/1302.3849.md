# Clustering in Nuclei from N/Z=1 to N/Z=2

> The clustering of nucleons in nuclei is a widespread but elusive phenomenon for study. Here, we wish to highlight the variety of theoretical approaches, and demonstrate how they are mutually supportive and complementary. On the experimental side, we describe recent advances in the study of the classic cluster nucleus 24Mg. Also, recent studies of clustering in nuclei approaching the neutron drip line are described. In the region near N/Z=2, both theory and experiment now suggest that multi-centre cluster structure is important, in particular for the very neutron rich beryllium isotopes.

## Key claim

Clustering in nuclei is a widespread but elusive phenomenon, and the paper argues that both theory and experiment now suggest multi-centre cluster structure is important for very neutron-rich beryllium isotopes near N/Z=2, though the evidence for many predicted cluster states (such as chain states) remains speculative and not compellingly confirmed experimentally beyond 8Be.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches including the resonating group method (RGM), generator coordinate method (GCM), Brink-Bloch alpha-cluster model, and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), comparing their predictions for cluster structures across the nuclear chart. On the experimental side, the author describes breakup reactions and scattering measurements for 24Mg (populated via 12C(16O,alpha)24Mg and 12C+12C scattering) and inelastic scattering of a radioactive 12Be beam at 378 MeV to study breakup into 6He+6He and alpha+8He channels.

## Key numbers

- The 0_2^+ state in 12C at 7.65 MeV was studied; the ground state of 12C was found to be an almost pure equilateral triangle with sides of 3.0 fm from three-body Fadeev calculations
- Resonances in mutual 3_1^- scattering of 12C+12C were observed at E_cm = 32.5, 37.5 and 43.0 MeV, with dominant spins of 18, 20 and 22 respectively
- For the 10^+ resonance in 12C+12C scattering at E_cm = 16.45 MeV, the measured branching ratio was Gamma_gamma/Gamma = 1.2 x 10^-5, corresponding to a transition strength of order 100 W.u.
- The 12Be beam was produced at 378 MeV with 95% purity at 2 x 10^4 particles per second
- Breakup states in 24Mg have widths of order 100-150 keV

## Context

This review extends the long-standing work on alpha-clustering from the Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, Takigawa, Horiuchi) and earlier cluster models by Brink, Buck, and others, while incorporating recent AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo and collaborators that predict clustering persists in neutron-rich systems beyond the N=Z line. The paper enables future experimental programs using radioactive beams to study clustering in neutron-rich beryllium, boron, and carbon isotopes, and highlights the need for partial decay width measurements to distinguish genuine molecular states from statistical decay in 24Mg breakup studies.
