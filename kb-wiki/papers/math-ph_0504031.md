# Continuum Singularities of a Mean Field Theory of Collisions

> Consider a complex energy $z$ for a $N$-particle Hamiltonian $H$ and let $χ$ be any wave packet accounting for any channel flux. The time independent mean field (TIMF) approximation of the inhomogeneous, linear equation $(z-H)|Ψ>=|χ>$ consists in replacing $Ψ$ by a product or Slater determinant $ϕ$ of single particle states $ϕ_i.$ This results, under the Schwinger variational principle, into self consistent TIMF equations $(η_i-h_i)|ϕ_i>=|χ_i>$ in single particle space. The method is a generalization of the Hartree-Fock (HF) replacement of the $N$-body homogeneous linear equation $(E-H)|Ψ>=0$ by single particle HF diagonalizations $(e_i-h_i)|ϕ_i>=0.$ We show how, despite strong nonlinearities in this mean field method, threshold singularities of the {\it inhomogeneous} TIMF equations are linked to solutions of the {\it homogeneous} HF equations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that threshold singularities of the inhomogeneous time-independent mean field (TIMF) equations for collisions are systematically linked to solutions of the homogeneous Hartree-Fock (HF) equations, despite the strong nonlinearities introduced by the mean-field approximation. This connection is qualified by the presence of additional unphysical "technical" singularities that arise from the nonlinearity of the TIMF approach.

## Method

The authors analyze two soluble models of two one-dimensional particles with kinetic energies only, using the TIMF approximation derived from a Schwinger variational principle for matrix elements of the resolvent. In the first model both particles are free; in the second model one particle experiences a separable attractive potential that supports a bound state. The TIMF equations reduce to polynomial conditions relating the complex energy \(z\), the single-particle energies \(\eta_i\), and the approximate amplitude \(D\), allowing a complete analytic investigation of singularities in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- For the symmetric bare propagation model with \(a_1=a_2=\gamma_1=\gamma_2=1\), the physical two-body threshold occurs at \(z=0\), where the amplitude \(D\) exhibits a cubic branching: \(D = -2 - j\, 2^{2/3}\, 3\, z^{1/3} + \mathcal{O}(z^{2/3})\), with \(j\) a cubic root of unity.
- An unphysical double root singularity occurs at \(z = -27/16\) with \(D = 16\), giving the expansion \(D = 16 - 256/9\, (z+27/16) \pm 8192/243\, [-(z+27/16)^3]^{1/2}\).
- In the second model with a separable potential, a one-body threshold appears at the bound state energy \(\eta_0\) defined by \((\eta_0+\lambda)^2 + a_1 \gamma_1^2 \eta_0 = 0\), in addition to the two-body threshold at \(z=0\).
- The polynomial condition relating \(D\) and \(z\) in the first model has degree 7 in \(D\) and degree 4 in \(z\), yielding 7 approximate amplitudes for each energy and 4 solutions for the inverse problem.

## Context

This work extends the time-independent mean field (TIMF) method introduced by Giraud and collaborators (2003) for calculating collision amplitudes, which itself was developed to overcome conceptual problems of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) and time-dependent mean field (TDMF) approaches. The paper goes beyond a previous study (Giraud and Weiguny, 2004) that established poles of the TIMF amplitude at bound Hartree-Fock energies, by now analyzing continuum singularities including thresholds and cuts. The results enable identification of physical versus unphysical branches of the TIMF solution and show that physical thresholds survive the nonlinear mean-field approximation, while unphysical singularities may be removable by linear admixture of multiple solutions.
