# Saturation and Condensate Fraction Reduction of Cold Alpha Matter

> The ground state energy of ideal alpha-matter at T=0 is analyzed within the framework of variational theory of Bose quantum liquids. Calculations are done for three local alpha-alpha potentials with positive volume integrals and two-body correlation functions obtained from the Pandharipande-Bethe equation. The energy per particle of $\alp$ matter is evaluated in the cluster expansion formalism up to four-body diagrams, and using the HNC/0 and HNC/4 approximation for a Bose liquid. Inclusion of higher order terms in the cluster expansion of the condensate fraction are leading to a stronger depletion of the alpha condensate with the density compared to the two-body approximation prediction.

## Key claim

The ground state energy of ideal alpha matter at zero temperature is analyzed using variational theory, and the inclusion of higher-order terms in the cluster expansion of the condensate fraction leads to a stronger depletion of the alpha condensate with density compared to the two-body approximation prediction, with the effect depending on the specific alpha-alpha potential used.

## Method

The authors calculated the ground-state energy of cold alpha matter using the variational theory of Bose quantum liquids, employing three local alpha-alpha potentials (Ali-Bodmer, Gogny D1, and Gogny D1N) with positive volume integrals. Two-body correlation functions were obtained from the Pandharipande-Bethe equation, and the energy per particle was evaluated using both the cluster expansion formalism (up to four-body diagrams) and the hypernetted chain approximations HNC/0 and HNC/4 for a Bose liquid.

## Key numbers

- For the Gogny-D1N interaction, the saturation point occurs at approximately 0.0325 alpha particles per fm^3 with a binding energy of -16.8 MeV per alpha particle (including the -7.07 MeV binding energy per nucleon of the alpha particle), and an incompressibility K of approximately 265.4 MeV.
- For the Gogny-D1 interaction, the saturation density is approximately 0.015 alpha particles per fm^3 with an incompressibility K of approximately 64 MeV.
- For the Ali-Bodmer potential, the cluster expansion and HNC methods are similar up to a density of approximately 0.0225 alpha particles per fm^3, whereas for the Gogny-D1 potential this interval is limited to densities less than or equal to 0.0125 alpha particles per fm^3.
- At the saturation density of nuclear matter (approximately 0.04 alpha particles per fm^3), the condensate depletion is around 35% for the Ali-Bodmer potential, while for the Gogny-D1 potential only about 10% of alphas remain in the condensate.
- At their respective saturation densities, the condensate fraction is 55% for Gogny D1 and 30% for Gogny D1N.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of alpha matter by Clark and Johnson (1980) who used the HNC/0 approximation with the Ali-Bodmer potential, and by Schuck and collaborators (2007) who estimated the condensate fraction in the lowest approximation. The authors introduce two new alpha-alpha potentials based on realistic alpha-particle densities and effective Gogny nucleon-nucleon interactions, and provide the first detailed investigation of the condensate fraction for alpha matter including higher-order cluster contributions. The results are relevant for theories of low-density nuclear matter composed of neutrons, protons, and alpha particles near the neutrinosphere during supernovae core-collapse, as studied by Horowitz (2007).
