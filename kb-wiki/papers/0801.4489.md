# Multiple scattering effects in quasi free scattering from halo nuclei: a test to Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation

> Full Faddeev-type calculations are performed for $^{11}$Be breakup on proton target at 38.4, 100, and 200 MeV/u incident energies. The convergence of the multiple scattering expansion is investigated. The results are compared with those of other frameworks like Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation that are based on an incomplete and truncated multiple scattering expansion.

## Key claim

Full Faddeev/AGS calculations for \({}^{11}\)Be breakup on a proton target show that the multiple scattering expansion converges at second order at 200 MeV/u, but the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) provides a poor approximation to the exact result at low energies (38.4 MeV/u) even when all second-order terms are included, contrary to the expectation that DWIA becomes accurate at higher energies.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations exactly after partial wave decomposition, including Coulomb interactions via a screening and renormalization method. They compared the full Faddeev/AGS results for the fivefold differential breakup cross section with calculations that truncate the multiple scattering series at single scattering (SS), double scattering (DS), and with the DWIA-full approach that includes only the incomplete set of multiple scattering terms present in the standard DWIA formalism. The calculations used the CD Bonn potential for p-n, an L-dependent potential for n-\({}^{10}\)Be, and a phenomenological optical potential for p-\({}^{10}\)Be.

## Key numbers

- Breakup cross sections calculated at incident energies of 38.4, 100, and 200 MeV/u for the reaction p(\({}^{11}\)Be,\({}^{10}\)Be n)p.
- At 200 MeV/u, the double scattering (DS) approximation reproduces the full Faddeev/AGS calculation, indicating convergence of the multiple scattering series at second order.
- At 38.4 MeV/u, the single scattering (SS) approximation overestimates the exact result in all three kinematic configurations studied.
- At 200 MeV/u, the SS approximation provides a good representation of the full calculation for configuration 1 (neutron at 45°), but increasingly overestimates it for configurations 2 (30°) and 3 (15°).
- The DWIA-full approach to second order provides a poor approximation to Faddeev/AGS at 38.4 MeV in any configuration, but approaches the exact result as energy increases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev/AGS analysis of p-\({}^{11}\)Be elastic scattering (Crespo 2007) to the breakup channel, and tests the validity of the standard DWIA formalism developed by Chant and Roos (1977) for quasi-free scattering from halo nuclei. The results show that at high energies (200 MeV/u), the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) in suitably chosen configurations may be more reliable than DWIA, which contains uncontrolled approximations such as the factorization and on-shell approximations. This enables more accurate extraction of nuclear structure information from knockout reactions on halo nuclei.
