# On the nuclear dependence of the mu-e conversion branching ratio

> The variation of the coherent branching ratio $R_{μe}$ (ratio of the $μ^-\to e^-$ reaction rate divided by the total muon-capture rate) through the periodic table is studied by using exact muon wave functions. It was found that, by using very heavy nuclei (e.g. \nuc{197}Au, the SINDRUM II target) as $μ^-\to e^-$ conversion stopping-targets, the above ratio is favored by a factor of about four to five than by using light ones (e.g. \nuc{48}Ti, chosen as PRIME target).

# On the nuclear dependence of the mu-e conversion branching ratio

## Key claim
The coherent branching ratio Rμe (muon-to-electron conversion rate divided by total muon-capture rate) increases linearly with atomic mass number A when exact muon wave functions are used, favoring very heavy nuclei like 197Au over light ones like 48Ti by a factor of about four to five. This contradicts earlier estimates by Weinberg and Feinberg (1960s) which predicted a maximum in the copper region near A≈60.

## Method
The author computed the coherent muon-to-electron conversion branching ratio Rμe for nuclei across the periodic table using exact muon wave functions and experimental proton densities, focusing on photonic mechanisms where only target protons contribute. The muon-nucleus overlap integrals were evaluated numerically rather than using the common approximation of a factorized average muon wave function. Experimental total muon-capture rates from Suzuki (1987) were used in the denominator to minimize uncertainties from the Primakoff function approximation.

## Key numbers
- For 197Au (SINDRUM II target) versus 48Ti (PRIME target), the branching ratio Rμe is favored by a factor of about four to five when using exact muon wave functions.
- The momentum transfer for coherent conversion is approximately q = 0.534 fm⁻¹ when neglecting muon binding energy, corresponding to an outgoing electron energy Ee ≈ 105.6 MeV.
- The published best upper limit on Rμe for titanium is Rμe(Ti) ≤ 6.1×10⁻¹³ (Wintz, 1998).
- The planned MECO experiment on 27Al aims for sensitivity Rμe(Al) ≤ 2×10⁻¹⁷, an improvement of about three orders of magnitude.
- The planned PRIME experiment on 48Ti aims for Rμe(Ti) ≤ 10⁻¹⁸.
- When using the approximate averaged muon wave function with binding energy neglected, Rμe shows a maximum near A≈130, but exact calculations show linear increase with A.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Weinberg and Feinberg (1960s) who used approximate nuclear form factors and a linear-in-Z approximation for muon capture rates, finding a maximum in the copper region. The exact calculations contradict that result and show that very heavy nuclei are preferred targets, which is relevant for the SINDRUM II experiment at PSI using 197Au, the planned MECO experiment at Brookhaven using 27Al, and the PRIME experiment at KEK using 48Ti. The results enable more reliable target selection for future lepton flavor violation searches and test the validity of the common approximation of factorizing the muon wave function.
