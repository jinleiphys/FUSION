# New aspect of chiral and axial breaking in QCD

> Violation of the $U(1)$ axial symmetry in QCD is stricter than the chiral $SU(2)$ breaking, simply because of the presence of the quantum axial anomaly. If the QCD gauge coupling is sent to zero, the strength of the $U(1)$ axial breaking coincides with that of the chiral $SU(2)$ breaking, which we shall in short call an axial-chiral coincidence. This coincidence is trivial since QCD then becomes a non-interacting theory. Actually, there exists another limit in the QCD parameter space, where an axial-chiral coincidence occurs even with nonzero QCD gauge coupling, that can be dubbed a nontrivial coincidence: it is the case with the massive light quarks $(m_l\neq 0)$ and the massless strange quark ($m_s=0$), due to the flavor-singlet nature of the topological susceptibility. This coincidence is robust and tied to the anomalous chiral Ward-Takahashi identity, which is operative even at hot QCD. This implies that the chiral $SU(2)$ symmetry is restored simultaneously with the $U(1)$ axial symmetry at high temperatures. This simultaneous restoration is independent of $m_l (\neq 0)$, hence is irrespective to the order of the chiral phase transition. In this paper, we discuss how the real-life QCD can be evolved from the nontrivial chiral-axial coincidence limit, by working on a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the $U(1)$ axial anomaly contribution properly incorporated. It is shown that at high temperatures the large differences between the restorations of the chiral $SU(2)$ symmetry and the $U(1)$ axial symmetry for two light quarks and a sufficiently large current mass for the strange quark is induced by a significant interference of the topological susceptibility. Thus the deviation from the nontrivial coincidence, which is monitored by the strange quark mass controlling the topological susceptibility, provides a new way of understanding the chiral $SU(2)$ and $U(1)$ axial breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The authors identify a nontrivial limit in QCD where the strength of $U(1)$ axial symmetry breaking coincides with that of chiral $SU(2)$ breaking even with nonzero QCD gauge coupling: the case of massive light quarks ($m_l \neq 0$) and a massless strange quark ($m_s = 0$), due to the flavor-singlet nature of the topological susceptibility. This coincidence is robust and tied to the anomalous chiral Ward-Takahashi identity, implying that chiral $SU(2)$ symmetry is restored simultaneously with $U(1)$ axial symmetry at high temperatures, independent of the light quark mass and the order of the chiral phase transition.

## Method

The authors employ a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with $2+1$ flavors, incorporating the $U(1)$ axial anomaly contribution properly, to study how real-life QCD evolves from the nontrivial chiral-axial coincidence limit. They first confirm that the NJL model reproduces the nontrivial coincidence at the massless strange quark limit, then introduce a nonzero strange quark mass to monitor the deviation from coincidence through the topological susceptibility. The meson susceptibility functions $\chi_\pi$, $\chi_\eta$, $\chi_\delta$, and $\chi_\sigma$ are used as indicators for chiral $SU(2)$ and $U(1)$ axial symmetry breaking strengths, with their differences serving as well-defined symmetry-breaking indicators.

## Key numbers

* The topological susceptibility $\chi_{\rm top}$ vanishes if either of quarks get massless, reflecting the flavor-singlet nature of the QCD $\theta$-vacuum.
* The anomalous Ward-Takahashi identity relates the meson susceptibility functions as $\chi_\eta - \chi_{\delta/\sigma} = \chi_\pi - \chi_{\delta/\sigma} + 4\chi_{\rm top}/m_l^2$, where $m_l$ is the current mass of up and down quarks.
* The nontrivial coincidence occurs for $m_l \neq 0$ and $m_s = 0$, with the strange quark mass controlling the deviation from coincidence through the topological susceptibility.
* The Veneziano-Witten formula gives $m_\eta^2 f_\eta^2 \sim \chi_{\rm top, g}$, where $\chi_{\rm top, g}$ is the pure gluonic contribution, formulated in the large $N_c$ approximation for massless quarks.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) on how the $U(1)_A$ anomaly affects the order of the chiral phase transition, and addresses the unresolved role of the anomaly in the Columbia plot phase diagram mapped by Brown (1990). The authors contrast their approach with lattice QCD simulations by Buchoff (2013) and Bhattacharya (2014), which showed that chiral symmetry restores faster than $U(1)_A$ symmetry at around the (pseudo)critical temperature for $2+1$ flavors at physical quark masses. The new approach using the strange quark mass as a control parameter provides an alternative to large-$N_c$ QCD a la Veneziano and Witten, using fixed $N_c=3$ and varying current quark masses, enabling a clearer understanding of how the topological susceptibility generates the discrepancy between chiral and axial symmetry restorations.
