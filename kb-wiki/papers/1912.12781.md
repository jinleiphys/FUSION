# The hidden-charm strong decays of the $Z_c$ states

> Inspired by BESIII's measurement of the decay $Z_c(3900)^{\pm}\rightarrow ρ^{\pm}η_c$, we calculate the branching fraction ratio between the $ρη_c$ and $πJ/ψ$ decay modes for the charged states $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$ and $Z_c(4430)$ using a quark interchange model. Our results show that (i) the ratio $R_{Z_c(3900)}=\frac{\mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^{\pm}\rightarrow ρ^{\pm}η_c)}{\mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^{\pm}\rightarrow π^{\pm}J/ψ)}$ is 1.3 and 1.6 in the molecular and tetraquark scenarios respectively, which is roughly consistent with the experimental data $R^{\mathrm{exp}}=2.2\pm0.9$. (ii) The ratios $\frac{Γ[Z_c(3900)\rightarrow ρη_c]}{Γ[Z_c(4020)\rightarrow ρη_c]}$ and $\frac{Γ[Z_c(3900)\rightarrow πJ/ψ]}{Γ[Z_c(4020)\rightarrow πJ/ψ]}$ are about 12.5 and 24.2, respectively in the molecular scenario. In contrast, these ratios are about 1.2 in the tetraquark scenario. The non-observation of the $Z_c(4020)$ signal in the $πJ/ψ$ decay mode strongly indicates that $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ are molecule-like signals which arise from the $D^{(*)}{\bar D}^{(*)}$ hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

The branching fraction ratio \(R_{Z_c(3900)} = \mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^\pm \to \rho^\pm \eta_c) / \mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^\pm \to \pi^\pm J/\psi)\) is predicted to be 1.3 in the molecular scenario and 1.6 in the tetraquark scenario, both consistent within errors with the experimental value of \(2.2 \pm 0.9\). However, the ratios of partial widths between \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_c(4020)\) for the \(\rho\eta_c\) and \(\pi J/\psi\) channels are about 12.5 and 24.2 respectively in the molecular scenario, but only about 1.2 in the tetraquark scenario, making these ratios a sensitive discriminator between the two interpretations.

## Method

The authors used a quark interchange model with a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) potential to calculate the strong decay amplitudes of the \(Z_c(3900)\), \(Z_c(4020)\), and \(Z_c(4430)\) states. In the molecular scenario, the \(Z_c\) states were treated as \(S\)-wave \(D\bar{D}^*\), \(D^*\bar{D}^*\), and \(D(2S)\bar{D}^*\) hadronic molecules; in the tetraquark scenario, they were treated as compact diquark-antidiquark states \([cq][\bar{c}\bar{q}]\). The effective potential was computed as the product of color, flavor, and spin-space overlaps, and the decay widths were obtained by integrating over the relative spatial wave functions of the initial and final states.

## Key numbers

- \(R_{Z_c(3900)} = \mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^\pm \to \rho^\pm \eta_c) / \mathcal{B}(Z_c(3900)^\pm \to \pi^\pm J/\psi)\) is 1.3 in the molecular scenario and 1.6 in the tetraquark scenario, compared to the experimental value \(R^{\mathrm{exp}} = 2.2 \pm 0.9\).
- For the molecular scenario with typical sizes, \(\Gamma[Z_c(3900) \to \eta_c \rho] \sim 2.76\) MeV and \(\Gamma[Z_c(3900) \to J/\psi \pi] \sim 2.18\) MeV; for \(Z_c(4020)\), \(\Gamma[\eta_c \rho] \sim 0.22\) MeV and \(\Gamma[J/\psi \pi] \sim 0.09\) MeV.
- The ratio \(\Gamma[Z_c(3900) \to \rho\eta_c] / \Gamma[Z_c(4020) \to \rho\eta_c]\) is 12.5 in the molecular scenario, while the same ratio is about 1.2 in the tetraquark scenario.
- The ratio \(\Gamma[Z_c(3900) \to \pi J/\psi] / \Gamma[Z_c(4020) \to \pi J/\psi]\) is 24.2 in the molecular scenario, compared to about 1.2 in the tetraquark scenario.
- For \(Z_c(4430)\) as a \(D(2S)\bar{D}^*\) molecule with \(r_{\text{mean}} \simeq 1.00\) fm, \(\Gamma[\eta_c \rho] \sim 1.28\) MeV and \(\Gamma[J/\psi \pi] \sim 0.94\) MeV, giving \(R^{\text{th}}_{Z_c(4430)} \sim 1.4\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of \(Z_c(3900)\) decays using a covariant quark model (Goerke 2016), phenomenological Lagrangians (Patel 2014), nonrelativistic effective field theory (Esposito 2014), the light front model (Ke 2013), and QCD sum rules (Wang 2017, Dias 2013, Agaev 2016), which gave widely varying predictions for the ratio \(R_{Z_c(3900)}\). The present calculation is directly motivated by the BESIII measurement of \(Z_c(3900)^\pm \to \rho^\pm \eta_c\) (Ablikim 2019). The key finding that the relative decay widths of \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_c(4020)\) differ by an order of magnitude in the molecular scenario but are nearly equal in the tetraquark scenario provides a clear experimental test to distinguish between these two interpretations, and suggests that the non-observation of \(Z_c(4020)\) in \(\pi J/\psi\) favors the molecular picture.
