# Density Dependence of the Symmetry Energy in the Post PREX-CREX Era

> The recently published CREX results suggest a rather peculiar picture for the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Whereas PREX favors a large neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb, thereby suggesting a stiff equation of state, CREX suggests instead a much softer equation of state. This discrepancy has caused a large spur in the theoretical community since no model has been able to simultaneously reproduce within $1σ$ the PREX and CREX results. Motivated by a novel correlation between a CREX observable and a combination of bulk symmetry energy parameters, we calibrate three new covariant energy density functionals that reproduce binding energies and charge radii of spherical nuclei - and also accommodate the constraints imposed by PREX and CREX. Given that these models suggest a stiff equation of state at high densities, predictions for neutron star properties are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calibrates three new covariant energy density functionals (DINOa, DINOb, DINOc) that can simultaneously accommodate the PREX-2 and CREX results within 1σ, but only by adopting a specific correlation between the CREX weak skin form factor and the combination Ksym−6L, and only under a "Scenario 1" analysis that excludes non-relativistic Skyrme models; the resulting models require large positive values of Ksym (506–715 MeV) that produce non-physical density oscillations in finite nuclei and overly stiff neutron star equations of state.

## Method

The authors used a Markov Chain Monte Carlo approach with χ² minimization to constrain the slope L and curvature Ksym of the symmetry energy from the PREX-2 and CREX experimental results, considering two scenarios: Scenario 1 using only covariant energy density functionals (EDFs) and the correlation between F_Wskin^48 and Ksym−6L, and Scenario 2 including both covariant and non-relativistic Skyrme EDFs. They then calibrated three new relativistic mean-field models (DINOa, DINOb, DINOc) by fixing L at 50, 70, and 90 MeV respectively, adding a δ-meson Yukawa coupling to the isovector sector, and fitting to binding energies, charge radii, and the PREX-2/CREX constraints on neutron skin and weak skin form factors.

## Key numbers

- PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin^208 = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX neutron skin thickness of 48Ca: R_skin^48 = 0.121 ± 0.035 fm
- CREX weak skin form factor of 48Ca at q = 0.8733 fm⁻¹: F_Wskin^48 = 0.0277 ± 0.0055
- Scenario 1 fit: L = 110 ± 40 MeV, Ksym = 970 ± 320 MeV
- Scenario 2 fit: L = 19 ± 19 MeV, Ksym = −61 ± 280 MeV
- DINOa: L = 50.00 MeV, Ksym = 506.0 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1522 fm⁻³
- DINOb: L = 70.00 MeV, Ksym = 610.0 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1525 fm⁻³
- DINOc: L = 90.00 MeV, Ksym = 715.0 MeV, saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1519 fm⁻³
- DINOa binding energy per nucleon of 208Pb: 7.867 MeV (experiment: 7.867 MeV)
- DINOa charge radius of 208Pb: 5.502 fm (experiment: 5.501 ± 0.001 fm)
- DINOa neutron skin of 208Pb: 0.1748 fm
- DINOa weak skin form factor of 208Pb at PREX momentum transfer: 0.0263 (experiment: 0.041 ± 0.013)
- DINOa neutron skin of 48Ca: 0.0996 fm
- DINOa weak skin form factor of 48Ca at CREX momentum transfer: 0.0333 (experiment: 0.0277 ± 0.0055)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Reed (2021) that used PREX-2 to infer a stiff symmetry energy with L = 106 ± 37 MeV, and attempts to reconcile the tension between PREX-2 and CREX results that previous studies by Hu (2021), Reinhard (2022), Zhang (2022), Mondal (2022), Papakonstantinou (2022), and Yuksel (2022) could not resolve within 1σ. The new DINO models enable predictions for neutron star properties, but their large positive Ksym values produce non-physical density oscillations in 208Pb and 48Ca and overly large neutron star radii, suggesting that current covariant density functional theory may be insufficient to simultaneously describe both experiments without introducing unphysical behavior.
