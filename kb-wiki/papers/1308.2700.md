# Ab initio many-body calculations of nucleon-4He scattering with three-nucleon forces

> We extend the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method to include three-nucleon (3N) interactions for the description of nucleon-nucleus collisions. We outline the formalism, give algebraic expressions for the 3N-force integration kernels, and discuss computational aspects of two alternative implementations. The extended theoretical framework is then applied to nucleon-4He scattering using similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon potentials derived from chiral effective field theory. We analyze the convergence properties of the calculated phase shifts and explore their dependence upon the SRG evolution parameter. We include up to six excited states of the 4He target and find significant effects from the inclusion of the chiral 3N force, e.g., it enhances the spin-orbit splitting between the 3/2- and 1/2- resonances and leads to an improved agreement with the phase shifts obtained from an accurate R-matrix analysis of the five-nucleon experimental data. We find remarkably good agreement with measured differential cross sections at various energies, while analyzing powers manifest larger deviations from experiment for certain energies and angles.

## Key claim

The inclusion of chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces in the no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) significantly enhances the spin-orbit splitting between the 3/2- and 1/2- resonances in nucleon-4He scattering and improves agreement with phase shifts from R-matrix analysis of five-nucleon experimental data, though analyzing powers still show larger deviations from experiment at certain energies and angles.

## Method

The authors extended the ab initio NCSM/RGM formalism to include three-nucleon (3N) interactions for nucleon-nucleus collisions, deriving algebraic expressions for the 3N-force integration kernels and implementing two alternative computational approaches. They applied the framework to nucleon-4He scattering using similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon potentials derived from chiral effective field theory, including up to six excited states of the 4He target and analyzing convergence of phase shifts with respect to model-space parameters and the SRG evolution parameter.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts were calculated for nucleon-4He scattering using SRG-evolved NN+3N Hamiltonians derived from the N3LO NN interaction of Entem and Machleidt (2003) along with the local chiral 3N force at N2LO of Navratil (2007)
- Up to six excited states of the 4He target were included in the calculation
- The 3N force enhanced the spin-orbit splitting between the 3/2- and 1/2- resonances, leading to improved agreement with phase shifts from R-matrix analysis of five-nucleon experimental data
- Remarkably good agreement was found with measured differential cross sections at various energies
- Analyzing powers showed larger deviations from experiment for certain energies and angles

## Context

This work extends the ab initio NCSM/RGM approach previously applied to nucleon scattering (Navratil 2010) and deuteron scattering (Navratil 2011) using only NN potentials, by now including both induced and initial chiral three-nucleon interactions. An earlier ab initio investigation of neutron-4He elastic scattering using realistic NN+3N Hamiltonians was performed within the Green's Function Monte Carlo method (Nollett 2007), but the present study extends to proton-4He scattering, covers a wider range of energies, and is the first to use two-plus three-body Hamiltonians from chiral effective field theory for systems with more than three nucleons. This enables more accurate predictions of nucleon-nucleus scattering observables and reactions such as radiative capture and fusion rates.
