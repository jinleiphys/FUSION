# Heavy-Flavor Fragmentation and Jet Structure from HF-NRevo: Bridging to Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present recent progress on the Heavy-Flavor Non-Relativistic Evolution (HF-NRevo) framework, designed to describe leading-power fragmentation of heavy-flavored hadrons at moderate to large transverse momentum. Starting from NLO NRQCD calculations for all partonic channels into pseudoscalar quarkonia, we construct the NRFF1.0 collinear fragmentation functions via DGLAP evolution in a variable-flavor number scheme. We outline future prospects in the heavy-ion context, where HF-NRevo can serve as a baseline for modeling in-medium modifications of heavy-flavor fragmentation in nuclear collisions. Its accurate modeling of the partonic hierarchy and threshold effects makes it ideally suited to explore jet-quenching sensitivity, energy-loss mechanisms, and the emergence of medium-modified fragmentation functions in the quark-gluon plasma. Moreover, it provides a natural baseline for implementing in-medium hadronization scenarios, including quarkonium regeneration and fragmentation-function apparent-shape distortion. These developments provide new handles for exploring heavy-flavor dynamics at the HL-LHC and future collider facilities.

## Key claim

The HF-NRevo framework provides a theoretically consistent methodology for constructing collinear fragmentation functions (FFs) for heavy quarkonia, using NLO NRQCD inputs at the initial scale and DGLAP evolution with a variable-flavor number scheme, but the presented results are limited to color-singlet pseudoscalar states (ηc and ηb) and do not yet include color-octet channels or vector states.

## Method

The authors construct the NRFF1.0 set of collinear fragmentation functions by starting from NLO NRQCD calculations for all partonic channels into pseudoscalar quarkonia (ηc and ηb) in the color-singlet channel. They perform DGLAP evolution in a variable-flavor number scheme using a two-stage procedure: a symbolic expanded and decoupled evolution step (EDevo) followed by full numerical all-order DGLAP evolution (AOevo), both implemented within the JETHAD framework. Missing higher-order uncertainties are estimated through a scale-variation strategy where renormalization and factorization scales are varied simultaneously by a factor of two up and down from their central values, combined with a replica-based Monte Carlo treatment.

## Key numbers

- Gluon-to-ηc and gluon-to-ηb fragmentation functions are evolved with μF spanning from 30 to 120 GeV (shown in Figures 1 and 2)
- Charm-to-ηc and bottom-to-ηb fragmentation functions are evolved with μF varied from 30 to 120 GeV (shown in Figure 2)
- The scale-variation uncertainty estimation probes sensitivity by varying renormalization and factorization scales by a factor of two up and down from central values

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD-based fragmentation function approach previously used for vector quarkonia in sets such as ZCW19+ and ZCFW22 (Celiberto 2022, Celiberto 2023) and for Bc mesons (Celiberto 2022, Celiberto 2024). The HF-NRevo framework is intended to supersede these earlier sets and provides a vacuum baseline for modeling in-medium modifications of heavy-flavor fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, enabling studies of jet quenching, energy loss, and medium-modified fragmentation functions at the HL-LHC, EIC, and future collider facilities.
