# Production of 92Nb, 92Mo, and 146Sm in the gamma-process in SNIa

> The knowledge of the production of extinct radioactivities like 92Nb and 146Sm by photodisintegration processes in ccSN and SNIa models is essential for interpreting abundances in meteoritic material and for Galactic Chemical Evolution (GCE). The 92Mo/92Nb and 146Sm/144Sm ratios provide constraints for GCE and production sites. We present results for SNIa with emphasis on nuclear uncertainties.

# Production of 92Nb, 92Mo, and 146Sm in the gamma-process in SNIa

## Key claim

The single-degenerate Chandrasekhar-mass SNIa model provides a viable site for producing the extinct radioactivities 92Nb and 146Sm and their abundance ratios as inferred from meteoritic data, but the nuclear uncertainties are specifically large for the Sm isotopes, whereas the 92Nb/92Mo ratio is predicted with smaller nuclear uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used a single-degenerate Chandrasekhar-mass SNIa model (DDT) in which material accreted from a companion AGB star is enriched in s-process seeds before the white dwarf explodes. They followed temperature and density histories of mass elements through the explosion using tracers, scrutinizing specific tracers with maximal production of 92Mo, 92Nb, and 146Sm. They performed manual variations of key reaction rates (multiplying or dividing by a factor of two) and also conducted Monte Carlo variations using the PizBuin framework, which combines a Monte Carlo driver with a fast, parallelized reaction network, applying symmetric uncertainty factors of 1.3 for experimentally determined neutron captures and 2.0 for theoretical neutron captures, with larger factors for proton and alpha reactions.

## Key numbers

- The 92Nb/92Mo abundance ratio after Galactic Chemical Evolution (GCE) ranges from 1.66 x 10^-5 (rate set MIN) to 3.12 x 10^-5 (rate set MAX), based on manual variation of the most important reaction rates by factors of two.
- For the Monte Carlo variations, symmetric uncertainty factors of 1.3 were used for experimentally determined neutron captures and 2.0 for theoretical neutron captures; theory rates for reactions involving protons received an uncertainty factor of 3.0; for predicted rates with alpha particles, an asymmetric uncertainty factor of 2.0 was used for the upper limit and 0.1 for the lower limit.
- The minor production channel for 92Nb via 91Zr(p,gamma) contributes about 3% of the total production.
- For 92Mo production, the main contribution (about 50%) comes through (gamma,n) sequences from stable Mo isotopes with mass numbers A > 94, a second path via 93Nb(p,n)93Mo(gamma,n)92Mo contributes about 35%, and the reaction 91Nb(p,gamma) provides a small additional contribution of about 15%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of gamma-process nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae (ccSN) to the SNIa environment, specifically addressing the production of extinct radioactivities 92Nb and 146Sm whose meteoritic abundances were discussed by Dauphas (2011) and Davis (2013). The authors compare their SNIa results to ccSN models and find that the uncertainty in the 146Sm/144Sm ratio is governed by (gamma,alpha) reactions on unstable, proton-rich nuclei, similar to the ccSN case. The Monte Carlo approach demonstrated in this work, using the PizBuin framework, enables future comprehensive large-scale studies of nuclear uncertainties in abundance predictions across multiple nucleosynthesis processes.
