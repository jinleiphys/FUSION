# Two- and Three-photon Fusion in Relativistic Heavy ion Collisions

> The production of mesons in ultra-peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy ions is re-analyzed using a projection technique to calculate the amplitudes for the appropriate Feynman diagrams. The virtuality of the exchanged photons is fully accounted for in this approach. In the case of two-photon fusion, it is explicitly shown that the inclusion of nuclear form factors validates the equivalent photon approximation. However, this does not apply to three-photon fusion cross sections. The cross section of J/ψproduction in ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC and LHC are shown to be much smaller than the cross sections for the production of C=even mesons of similar masses.

## Key claim

The authors show that the equivalent photon approximation is validated for two-photon fusion cross sections when nuclear form factors are included, but this approximation does **not** apply to three-photon fusion cross sections, which require a full treatment of photon virtuality.

## Method

The authors use a projection technique to calculate Feynman diagram amplitudes for meson production in ultra-peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, fully accounting for the virtuality of exchanged photons. For two-photon fusion, they derive the cross section from the matrix element for quark-antiquark pair production and relate it to the meson decay width, introducing nuclear form factors to impose a cutoff on transverse momenta. For three-photon fusion into vector mesons, they calculate the 12 contributing diagrams and perform the integrations analytically using the sharp-peak approximation for the internal momentum integrals.

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-photon fusion formalism of Brodsky, Kinoshita and Terazawa (1970) and the heavy-ion application of Baur and Bertulani (1988) by providing a full quantum-field-theoretic treatment using the projection method of Novikov et al. (1978). The results agree with the equivalent photon approximation for two-photon fusion and with the positronium production calculation of Serbo (1999). The three-photon fusion calculation enables predictions for vector meson (C=odd) production at RHIC and LHC, showing that J/ψ production cross sections are many orders of magnitude smaller than those for C=even mesons of similar masses, which is relevant for planning ultra-peripheral collision experiments at both facilities.
