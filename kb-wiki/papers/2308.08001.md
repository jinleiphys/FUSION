# Constraints on the Nuclear Symmetry Energy from Experiments, Theory and Observations

> Nuclear mass measurements and neutron matter theory tightly constrain the nuclear symmetry energy parameters $J$, $L$, $K_{sym}$ and $Q_{sym}$. Corroboration of these constraints on $J$ and $L$ can be found from measurements of the neutron skin thicknesses and dipole polarizabilities of neutron-rich nuclei. The experimental constraints on these parameters are compared with those obtained from consideration of astrophysical measurements of the neutron star radius, which we show is highly correlated with $L$. Attention is aimed at the recent PREX and CREX neutron skin measurements from Jefferson Lab, NICER neutron star radius measurements, and a new interpretation of the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement. We find joint satisfaction of PREX and CREX gives $J=32.2\pm1.7$ MeV and $L=52.9\pm13.2$ MeV, in excellent agreement with neutron matter predictions of $J=32.0\pm1.1$ MeV and $L=51.9\pm7.9$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper finds that jointly satisfying the PREX and CREX neutron skin measurements yields symmetry energy parameters \(J=32.2\pm1.7\) MeV and \(L=52.9\pm13.2\) MeV, in excellent agreement with chiral effective field theory neutron matter predictions of \(J=32.0\pm1.1\) MeV and \(L=51.9\pm7.9\) MeV, despite the two experiments individually being barely compatible with each other at the 68% confidence level.

## Method

The author compiles nuclear binding energy data from 2336 nuclei and uses a liquid drop model to extract the symmetry energy parameters \(J\) and the surface symmetry energy \(S_S\), then converts the \(J-S_S\) correlation into a \(J-L\) correlation using Skyrme-like interactions. Neutron skin thicknesses of \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{48}\)Ca from PREX, CREX, and historical measurements are combined with the unitary gas conjecture and unitary gas pressure conjecture constraints, and the resulting two-dimensional Gaussian probabilities in \(r_{np}^{48}-r_{np}^{208}\) space are used to weight interactions from published compilations to produce confidence ellipses for \(J\), \(L\), neutron star radii, and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Joint satisfaction of PREX and CREX gives \(J=32.2\pm1.7\) MeV and \(L=52.9\pm13.2\) MeV
- Neutron matter predictions from chiral EFT give \(J=32.0\pm1.1\) MeV and \(L=51.9\pm7.9\) MeV
- Nuclear binding energy fit to 2336 nuclei yields \(J_0=29\) MeV, \(\sigma_J=2.3\) MeV, \(S_{S0}=50\) MeV, \(\sigma_S=13.2\) MeV, with correlation coefficient \(r=0.997\)
- PREX I+II gives \(r_{np}^{208}=0.283\pm0.071\) fm; CREX gives \(r_{np}^{48}=0.121\pm0.035\) fm
- Weighted mean of all experimental measurements: \(r_{np}^{208}=0.166\pm0.017\) fm; \(r_{np}^{48}=0.137\pm0.015\) fm
- Using PREX/CREX probabilities, predicted neutron star radius \(R_{1.4}=11.6\pm1.0\) km and tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4}=228^{+148}_{-90}\) (68% confidence)
- Using average of other experiments, predicted \(R_{1.4}=11.0\pm0.9\) km and \(\Lambda_{1.4}=177^{+117}_{-70}\)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy parameters \(J\) and \(L\) through multiple independent approaches: nuclear mass measurements (Myers 1966, Audi 2003), neutron matter theory using chiral effective field theory (Weinberg 1967, Drischler 2020), and neutron skin measurements (Adhikari 2021, Adhikari 2022). The paper reconciles the tension between the PREX and CREX results by showing that their joint satisfaction yields parameters consistent with neutron matter predictions and astrophysical observations from NICER (Raaijmakers 2021) and GW170817 (De 2018, Abbott 2019). This enables tighter predictions for neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities, and demonstrates that the unitary gas conjecture (Tews 2017) and unitary gas pressure conjecture (Lattimer 2023) provide effective filters for selecting physically reasonable nuclear interactions.
