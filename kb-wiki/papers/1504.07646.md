# Particle-hole nature of the light high-spin toroidal isomers

> Nuclei under non-collective rotation with a large angular momentum above some threshold can assume a toroidal shape. In our previous work, we showed by using cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach that even-even, N=Z, high-K, toroidal isomeric states may have general occurrences for light nuclei with 28<=A<=52. We present here some additional results and systematics on the particle-hole nature of these high-spin toroidal isomers.

## Key claim

High-spin toroidal isomeric states in light even-even N=Z nuclei (28≤A≤52) arise from specific particle-hole (p-h) excitations above the I=0 toroidal configuration, with the aligned angular momentum determined by simple rules based on the number of p-h pairs and the parameter Λ_max.

## Method

The authors used a three-step method: first, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with quadrupole moment constraints to find oblate toroidal configurations; second, Q20-constrained cranking Skyrme-HF calculations with a Lagrange multiplier to enforce non-collective rotation about the symmetry axis; third, unconstrained cranked HF calculations to locate high-spin toroidal isomeric states in free convergence. The particle-hole nature was analyzed by examining proton-quasiparticle state energies as a function of quadrupole moment for the I=0 toroidal configuration of 52Fe.

## Key numbers

- 18 even-even, N=Z, high-spin toroidal isomeric states were located in the region 16≤A≤52, including 28Si(I=44ℏ), 32S(I=48,66ℏ), 36Ar(I=56,72,92ℏ), 40Ca(I=60,82ℏ), 44Ti(I=68,88,112ℏ), 48Cr(I=72,98,120ℏ), and 52Fe(I=52,80,104,132ℏ)
- For 52Fe, the 2p-2h excitation yields Iz=26ℏ and the 3p-3h excitation yields Iz=40ℏ
- For 52Fe with N=Z=26 and Λ_max=6, the aligned angular momenta are Iz=2×14, 2×26, 2×40, 2×52, 2×66ℏ for 1-, 2-, 3-, 4-, 5-p-h excitations
- For 52Fe toroidal isomers, as I increases from 52 to 132ℏ, the maximum density ρ_max decreases from 0.134 to 0.123 fm⁻³, the major radius R increases from 7.39 to 8.20 fm, and the minor radius d stays constant at d≈1.37 fm
- The ground state density of 52Fe has ρ_max=0.173 fm⁻³, distinctly larger than the toroidal distributions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Staszczak and Wong, 2014) which first showed general occurrences of toroidal high-spin isomers in light nuclei, and confirms findings by Ichikawa et al. (2012, 2014) who found toroidal high-spin isomers in 40Ca and other nuclei in the A=28-52 region. The systematic particle-hole analysis and the simple rules for calculating aligned angular momentum from p-h excitations provide a framework for further theoretical studies and experimental searches for toroidal nuclear shapes.
