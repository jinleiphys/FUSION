# Probing Quark-Gluon Correlation Functions

> We review applicability of QCD factorization theorem to multiple scattering in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering. We show why A^{1/3}-type nuclear enhancement can be calculated consistently in perturbative QCD. We derive the transverse momentum broadening of the leading pions in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus collisions. We argue that the measurement of such transverse momentum broadening can provide direct information on the multiple-parton correlation functions inside a nucleus. We also estimate the numerical values of the broadening at different values of x_B and Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the transverse momentum broadening of leading pions in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering provides a direct measurement of quark-gluon correlation functions inside a nucleus, but this claim is conditional on the assumption that the A^{1/3}-type nuclear enhancement can be isolated from weaker A-dependence in the leading-twist parton distributions, which the authors estimate to be limited to A^α with α ≈ ±0.02 for the relevant x_B range.

## Method

The authors apply the QCD factorization theorem for multiple scattering to derive the first moment of the transverse momentum spectrum of leading pions in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus collisions, separating the nuclear broadening Δ⟨ℓ_T²⟩ into a term with weak A-dependence (a) and a term proportional to A^{1/3} (bA^{1/3}) that directly involves the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function T_{qF}^A. They compute the double-scattering contribution to the hadronic tensor at leading order in α_s using the three Feynman diagrams for final-state interactions, taking pole contributions to fix the momentum fraction integrals, and express the broadening in terms of the correlation function and quark-to-pion fragmentation functions. Numerical estimates are obtained using a model where T_{qF}^A(x,Q²) = λ² A^{1/3} q^A(x,Q²) with λ a free parameter, combined with parameterized fragmentation functions from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear structure function A-dependence is parameterized as F₂^A = A^{α(A,x_B)} F₂^N, with |α| ≤ 0.05 for all accessible x_B at fixed target energies, and α ≈ ±0.02 for the x_B range relevant to pion production (between vertical lines in Fig. 1 at Q² = 4 and 9 GeV²)
- The transverse momentum broadening for π⁰ is given by Δ⟨ℓ_T²⟩_{1/3}^{π⁰} ≈ (4π²α_s(Q²)/3) A^{1/3} λ² D⁰(2,z_min), where D⁰(2,z_min) is the second moment of the π⁰ fragmentation function integrated from z_min to 1
- The fragmentation functions used are D⁺(z) = (1-z²)/(4z), D⁻(z) = (1-z)²/(4z), and D⁰(z) = (1-z)/(4z) for u and d quarks, with D_s^π = D⁻ for strange quarks

## Context

This work extends the generalized QCD factorization theorem developed by Qiu and Sterman (1991) for multiple scattering in nuclear targets, and builds on the observation by Guo (1998) that jet transverse momentum broadening in DIS directly measures quark-gluon correlation functions. The paper adapts this approach to leading pion production, making the measurement accessible to fixed-target facilities like CEBAF rather than requiring a future HERA with nuclear beams. The results enable experimental extraction of the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function T_{qF}^A from pion broadening data, and the predicted ordering Δ⟨ℓ_T²⟩^{π⁺} > Δ⟨ℓ_T²⟩^{π⁰} > Δ⟨ℓ_T²⟩^{π⁻} and the x_B-scaling behavior of π⁰ broadening provide tests of the underlying model for these correlation functions.
