# Weak response of neutron matter at low momentum transfer

> The Landau parameters obtained from the matrix elements of an effective interaction recently derived within the formalism of correlated basis functions have been used to carry out a study of the weak response of neutron matter in the region of low momentum transfer. The proposed approach allows for a consistent description of different interaction effects and can be extended to describe matter at non vanishing temperature. The results show that interactions lead to a sizable enhancement of the neutrino mean free path in cold neutron matter. The dependence of the mean free path on temperature and neutrino energy is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that interactions in neutron matter lead to a sizable enhancement of the neutrino mean free path in cold neutron matter, but this result is conditional on the use of Landau parameters derived from a correlated basis functions (CBF) effective interaction and is strictly applicable only in the regime of low momentum transfer where quasiparticle excitations are well defined.

## Method

The authors derived Landau parameters from the matrix elements of a CBF effective interaction, which was obtained from a truncated Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential including three-nucleon forces. They then used these Landau parameters within the framework of Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids to compute the density and spin-density dynamic structure functions of pure neutron matter in the long wavelength limit. The neutrino mean free path was obtained from these structure functions using the transport equation, assuming non degenerate neutrinos at temperatures up to 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Landau parameters F0, G0, and H0 are tabulated for eight densities from 0.04 fm^-3 to 0.32 fm^-3; for example, at ρ = 0.16 fm^-3 (nuclear matter equilibrium density), F0 = -0.263, G0 = 0.914, and H0 = 0.046.
- The magnetic susceptibility computed from Landau theory differs from the simplified expression neglecting tensor forces by approximately 0.01%.
- The neutrino mean free path was computed for a non degenerate neutrino with energy k0 = 1 MeV in neutron matter at T = 0, showing enhancement relative to the free neutron gas over a broad density range up to 0.32 fm^-3.
- The compressibility and spin susceptibility are normalized to their Fermi gas values and shown as functions of density up to 2ρ0, where ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Benhar and Farina (2009) and Shannon et al. (2010) by using Landau parameters from a CBF effective interaction to study weak responses, rather than computing responses directly from correlated wave functions. The results are compared to Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Fantoni et al. (2004) for spin susceptibility and variational FHNC-SOC calculations by Akmal et al. (1998) for compressibility, showing reasonable agreement. The formalism enables consistent treatment of both short-range and long-range correlations and can be extended to non-zero temperatures, allowing future studies of neutrino emission rates and transport coefficients in neutron star matter.
