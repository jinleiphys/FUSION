# Thermoelectric response of a hot and weakly magnetized anisotropic QCD medium

> We have studied the Seebeck and Nernst coefficients of a weakly magnetized hot QCD medium having a weak momentum anisotropy within the kinetic theory approach. The thermal medium effects have been incorporated in the framework of a quasi-particle model where the medium dependent mass of the quark has been calculated using perturbative thermal QCD in the presence of a weak magnetic field which leads to different masses for the left ($L$) and right ($R$) handed chiral quark modes. We have found that the Seebeck and Nernst coefficient magnitudes for the individual quark flavors as well as for the composite medium are decreasing functions of temperature and decreasing functions of anisotropy strength. The Nernst coefficient magnitudes are about an order of magnitude smaller than their Seebeck counterparts, indicating the Seebeck effect constitutes a stronger response than the Nernst effect. The average percentage change corresponding to switching between quasiparticle modes ($L\to R$ or $R\to L$) is an order of magnitude smaller for Nernst coefficients, compared to the Seebeck coefficients.

## Key claim

The Seebeck and Nernst coefficients of a hot QCD medium with weak momentum anisotropy are decreasing functions of both temperature and anisotropy strength, with the Nernst effect being about an order of magnitude weaker than the Seebeck effect; these results hold within a quasi-particle model where a weak magnetic field lifts the mass degeneracy between left- and right-handed chiral quark modes.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, with the anisotropic distribution function parametrized via the Romatschke-Strickland spheroidal anisotropy parameter ξ (taken small, ξ = 0.3, 0.6). The medium-dependent quark masses for left- and right-handed chiral modes were computed from the poles of the resummed quark propagator using perturbative thermal QCD in a weak magnetic field, and the relaxation time was calculated from 2→2 perturbative QCD scatterings. The Seebeck coefficient was obtained by setting the induced electric current to zero in the presence of a temperature gradient, while the Nernst coefficient was computed similarly but with an additional weak magnetic field perpendicular to both the temperature gradient and the induced transverse electric field.

## Key numbers

- The Nernst coefficient magnitudes are about an order of magnitude smaller than their Seebeck counterparts for the same conditions.
- The average percentage change when switching between left- and right-handed quasiparticle modes (L→R or R→L) is an order of magnitude smaller for Nernst coefficients compared to Seebeck coefficients.
- The anisotropy parameter values used in the study are ξ = 0.3 and ξ = 0.6, representing weak momentum anisotropy.
- The coupling constant α_s(T) is evaluated at the scale Q = 2π√(T² + μ²/π²), with Λ_QCD = 0.176 GeV and Λ_MS̅ = 0.176 GeV.
- The magnetic field-dependent mass splitting term M²(T,μ,B) = |q_iB|/(16π²) × (πT/(2m_i0) − ln2 + 7μ²ζ(3)/(8π²T²)), where ζ is the Riemann zeta function.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermoelectric transport in QCD matter, specifically the Seebeck effect in a hot partonic medium studied by Dey et al. (2020) and the Nernst effect in hadron gas investigated by Bhatt et al. (2018) and Das et al. (2020). It is the first calculation of both Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in an anisotropic QGP medium with a weak magnetic field that incorporates non-degenerate left- and right-handed chiral quasiparticle masses, building on the mass splitting formalism developed by Das et al. (2018). The results enable future phenomenological studies of thermoelectric effects in heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as FAIR and NICA, where baryon-rich plasma with finite chemical potential is expected.
