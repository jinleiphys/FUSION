# Shape phase mixing in critical point nuclei

> Spectral properties of nuclei near the critical point of the quantum phase transition between spherical and axially symmetric shapes are studied in a hybrid collective model which combines the $γ$-stable and $γ$-rigid collective conditions through a rigidity parameter. The model in the lower and upper limits of the rigidity parameter recovers the X(5) and X(3) solutions respectively, while in the equally mixed case it corresponds to the X(4) critical point symmetry. Numerical applications of the model on nuclei from regions known for critical behavior reveal a sizable shape phase mixing and its evolution with neutron or proton numbers. The model also enables a better description of energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions for these nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that nuclei near the critical point of the spherical-to-axially-symmetric shape phase transition exhibit a measurable mixing of γ-stable and γ-rigid collective excitations, quantified by a rigidity parameter χ, rather than being purely described by the limiting X(5) or X(3) symmetries.

## Method
The authors construct a hybrid collective Hamiltonian that interpolates between the γ-stable Bohr Hamiltonian (five-dimensional, associated with X(5)) and the γ-rigid Hamiltonian (three-dimensional, associated with X(3)) via a rigidity parameter χ (0 ≤ χ < 1). The Schrödinger equation is solved using an infinite square well potential in the β variable and a harmonic approximation for the γ variable, yielding analytical Bessel-function solutions for the β wave functions and two-dimensional harmonic oscillator solutions for the γ wave functions. The model is fitted to experimental energy spectra of candidate critical-point nuclei by minimizing a standard error σ, with χ and a γ-band energy shift α as free parameters.

- The rigidity parameter χ fitted for Os isotopes: 0.781 for ¹⁷⁴Os, 0.235 for ¹⁷⁶Os, 0.046 for ¹⁷⁸Os, and 0.079 for ¹⁸⁰Os (Table 2).
- The rigidity parameter χ fitted for Pt isotopes: 0.833 for ¹⁷⁸Pt, 0.448 for ¹⁸⁰Pt, 0.632 for ¹⁸²Pt, and 0.836 for ¹⁸⁴Pt (Table 3).
- The standard error σ for the fits ranges from 0.639 (¹⁵⁸Er) to 1.794 (¹⁵⁶Dy) across all fitted nuclei (Tables 1-3).
- The model predicts that at χ = 0.103, the 0⁺β1 and 6⁺g states become degenerate; at χ = 0.560, the 0⁺β2 and 10⁺g states become degenerate (Section 4).

## Context
This work extends the X(5) critical point symmetry of Iachello (2001) and the X(3) γ-rigid model of Bonatsos et al. (2006) by introducing a continuous interpolation between them, formalized earlier as the X(4) symmetry (Bonatsos et al., 2014). It applies the model to rare-earth nuclei near N=90 (e.g., ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁴Gd, which are known X(5) candidates) and to Os/Pt isotopes near N=100, where X(3) candidates were previously identified. The results enable a systematic study of how shape phase mixing evolves with neutron or proton number, and provide improved descriptions of energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions for these critical-point nuclei.
