# Radiative 3He-alpha reaction in Halo Effective Field Theory

> In this work we study the radiative capture of ${\rm {}^3He}$ on ${\rm {}^4He}$ within the halo effective field theory (EFT) framework. At leading order the capture amplitude comprises the initial state $s$-wave strong and Coulomb interactions summed to all orders. At the same order in the expansion, leading two-body currents contribute as well. We find delicate cancelations between the various contributions, and the two-body current contributions can be replaced by appropriately enhancing the asymptotic normalizations of the $^7$Be ground and first excited state wave functions. The next-to-leading order corrections come from the $s$-wave shape parameter and the pure Coulomb $d$-wave initial state interactions. We fit the EFT parameters to available scattering data and most recent capture data. Our zero-energy astrophysical $S$-factor estimate, $S_{34}\sim 0.55$ keV b, is consistent within error bars with the average in the literature.

## Key claim

The zero-energy astrophysical S-factor for the \(^3\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\text{Be}\) reaction is estimated as \(S_{34} \sim 0.55\) keV b, consistent within error bars with the average in the literature, but this result depends on delicate cancellations between one-body and two-body current contributions that can be absorbed into enhanced asymptotic normalizations of the \(^7\)Be ground and first excited state wave functions.

## Method

The authors apply halo effective field theory (EFT) treating \(^3\)He and \(\alpha\) as point-like clusters, with the \(^7\)Be ground and first excited states as \(p\)-wave bound states. The leading-order capture amplitude includes initial \(s\)-wave strong and Coulomb interactions summed to all orders, plus leading two-body currents; next-to-leading order corrections come from the \(s\)-wave shape parameter and pure Coulomb \(d\)-wave initial state interactions. EFT parameters are fitted to available elastic scattering data and recent capture data, with the Coulomb interaction handled via summation of the Coulomb ladder diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^7\)Be ground state: \(B_0 = 1.5866\) MeV
- Binding energy of \(^7\)Be first excited state: \(B_1 = 1.1575\) MeV
- Zero-energy astrophysical S-factor estimate: \(S_{34} \sim 0.55\) keV b
- Mass of \(^3\)He: \(m_\psi = 2809.41\) MeV; mass of \(\alpha\): \(m_\phi = 3728.4\) MeV
- Sommerfeld parameter defined as \(\eta_p = k_C/p\) with \(k_C = \alpha_e Z_\psi Z_\phi \mu\) and \(\alpha_e \approx 1/137\)

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT approach previously applied to systems such as the \(p\)-wave neutron-alpha resonance (Bertulani 2002, Bedaque 2003) and \(s\)-wave alpha-alpha resonance (Higa 2008, Gelman 2009) to the radiative capture reaction \(^3\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\text{Be}\), which is the main source of uncertainty in solar neutrino flux predictions and contributes to the primordial lithium problem in big bang nucleosynthesis. The result enables a systematic, model-independent extrapolation of capture data to astrophysically relevant energies, improving constraints on solar neutrino physics and BBN lithium abundance calculations.
