# Theoretical description of deformed proton emitters: nonadiabatic coupled-channel method

> The newly developed nonadiabatic method based on the coupled-channel Schroedinger equation with Gamow states is used to study the phenomenon of proton radioactivity. The new method, adopting the weak coupling regime of the particle-plus-rotor model, allows for the inclusion of excitations in the daughter nucleus. This can lead to rather different predictions for lifetimes and branching ratios as compared to the standard adiabatic approximation corresponding to the strong coupling scheme. Calculations are performed for several experimentally seen, non-spherical nuclei beyond the proton dripline. By comparing theory and experiment, we are able to characterize the angular momentum content of the observed narrow resonance.

## Key claim

The nonadiabatic coupled-channel method, based on the weak coupling regime of the particle-plus-rotor model, can yield rather different predictions for lifetimes and branching ratios compared to the standard adiabatic (strong coupling) approximation, but the method's accuracy depends on the proper ordering of spherical shells in the deformed potential, which the Chepurnov parameterization provides as a compromise between good radial properties and correct level ordering.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Schroedinger equation with Gamow (resonance) boundary conditions for a proton interacting with a deformed daughter nucleus, using the particle-plus-rotor model in the weak coupling scheme. The proton-core interaction was modeled with an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential plus spin-orbit and Coulomb terms, with the potential depth adjusted to reproduce the experimental Q_p value for each nucleus. The coupled equations were solved numerically using a piecewise perturbation method with exterior complex scaling for regularization, and partial decay widths were computed from the current expression or from the asymptotic form of the Gamow wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Proton resonance widths for known emitters range between 10^{-16} MeV and 10^{-22} MeV, corresponding to lifetimes from microseconds to seconds.
- For the [422]3/2 Nilsson orbital in 117La at beta_2 = 0.33, the wave function contains 60% g_{7/2} distributed between the 2+ and 4+ daughter states, with contributions from d_{3/2} (9.6%), d_{5/2} (9.0%), g_{9/2} (14.2%), i_{11/2} (3.5%), and s_{1/2} (4.2%) from Coriolis coupling.
- 97% of the total width for the 117La resonance is in the d_{3/2} ⊗ 0+ channel, with the remainder from d_{3/2} ⊗ 2+ (0.6%) and s_{1/2} channels.
- For 131Eu, the K=3/2 component dominates the [411]3/2 band with K=3/2 = 0.9972 for J=3/2, 0.9894 for J=5/2, and 0.9770 for J=7/2, with small admixtures from K=1/2, 5/2, and 7/2 due to Coriolis coupling.
- The asymptotic radius r_as was taken as approximately 40 fm, and the total width computed from the current expression differs by less than 0.1% from the value obtained from the imaginary part of the eigenvalue.
- Multipole expansion of the deformed potential was carried out to order lambda_max = 12, and partial waves up to l < 10-13 were included, corresponding to (J_d)_max = 10.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for deformed proton emitters beyond the strong coupling (adiabatic) approximation used in prior approaches by Kadmenski and collaborators (1973), and by the Gamow-state methods of Maglione, Ferreira, and Liotta (1998-2000). The nonadiabatic method enables calculation of fine structure in proton decay, as observed experimentally by Sonzogni et al. (1999) in 131Eu, and allows prediction of rotational bands built on deformed resonances. The approach enables future studies of branching ratios to excited states in the daughter nucleus and provides a framework for analyzing the angular momentum content of proton resonances in deformed nuclei beyond the proton dripline.
