# Spin Polarization and Chiral Condensation in 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential

> We investigate the ferromagnetic (spin polarization) condensation in (2+1) flavor Nambu Jona-Lasinio(NJL) model with non-zero current quark masses at finite temperature and density which may be relevant in the context of neutron stars. The spin polarization condensation arises due to a tensor type interaction which may be generated due to non-perturbative effects in Quantum Chromodynamics(QCD). In this investigation we have shown the interplay between chiral condensate and spin polarization condensation for different values of tensor coupling. Spin polarization in the case of 2+1 flavor is different from two flavor case because of additional F$_8$ condensate associated with $λ_8^f$ flavor generator. We find a non-zero value of the two spin condensates in the chirally restored phase. Beyond a certain temperature the spin polarization condensates vanish for rather large quark chemical potentials. The spin condensates affect the chiral phase transition, quark masses, and the quark dispersion relation. The spin polarization condensate appears only in the chiral restored phase for light quarks. For large enough tensor couplings, it is observed that the spin polarization condensate acts as a catalyst for chiral symmetry restoration. Thermodynamic behavior of $F_3$ and $F_8$ are found to be different and they affect the quark masses differently.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the (2+1) flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with non-zero current quark masses, spin polarization condensates (F₃ and F₈) appear only in the chirally restored phase for light quarks, and for sufficiently large tensor couplings the spin polarization condensate acts as a catalyst for chiral symmetry restoration, a result that depends on the tensor coupling strength being a free parameter not fixed by perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors use a (2+1) flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model Lagrangian that includes the standard scalar and pseudoscalar four-fermion interactions, the 't Hooft determinant six-fermion interaction, and an additional tensor-type interaction term proportional to coupling G_T. They apply the mean-field approximation to derive the thermodynamic potential in the grand canonical ensemble at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ, then solve the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensates σ_ud, σ_s and the spin polarization condensates F₃, F₈. The divergent vacuum integrals are regularized using a three-momentum cutoff scheme.

## Key numbers

- Three-momentum cutoff: Λ = 602.3 × 10⁻³ GeV
- Light current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5.5 × 10⁻³ GeV
- Strange current quark mass: m_s = 140.7 × 10⁻³ GeV
- Scalar coupling: g = 1.835 / Λ²
- Determinant coupling: K = 12.36 / Λ⁵
- At zero temperature, the chiral phase transition (first order) occurs near μ = 0.360 GeV for the parameter set used
- For tensor coupling G_T = 2g, non-zero spin polarization F₃ appears near μ = 0.480 GeV at T = 0, reaches maximum near μ = 0.510 GeV, and vanishes near μ = 0.600 GeV
- The vacuum constituent quark masses are approximately 368 MeV for u/d quarks and 549 MeV for strange quarks

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in quark matter from single-flavor NJL models (Maedan, 2007) and two-flavor NJL models (Tsue, 2012; Tsue, 2016) to the more realistic (2+1) flavor case with non-zero current quark masses, where two distinct spin polarization condensates (F₃ and F₈) arise from the diagonal generators of SU(3) flavor. The results differ from earlier axial-vector channel studies (Tatsumi, 2003) where spin polarization could appear in the chirally broken phase, whereas here the tensor channel yields spin polarization only in the chirally restored phase. The findings are relevant for understanding possible ferromagnetic phases in dense quark matter inside neutron stars and magnetars.
