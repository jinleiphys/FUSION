# Chiral vortaic effect and neutron asymmetries at NICA

> We study the possibility of testing experimentally signatures of P-odd effects related with the vorticity of the medium. The Chiral Vortaic Effect is generalized to the case of conserved charges different from the electric one. In the case of baryonic charge and chemical potential such effect should manifest itself in neutron asymmetries at the NICA accelerator complex measured by the MPD detector. The required accuracy may be achieved in a few months of accelerator running. We also discuss polarization of the hyperons and P-odd correlations of particle momenta (handedness) as probes of vorticity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Chiral Vortaic Effect (CVE), generalized to baryonic charge, should produce measurable neutron asymmetries at the NICA accelerator complex, with the required accuracy achievable in a few months of running; however, this estimate depends on accumulating approximately 1000 million events, compared to the 15 million events sufficient for the Chiral Magnetic Effect at RHIC.

## Method

The authors generalize the Chiral Vortaic Effect from electromagnetic current to currents of any conserved charge, specifically baryonic charge, by replacing the electromagnetic coupling with chemical potentials in the triangle anomaly diagram. They propose using two- and three-particle azimuthal correlators, analogous to those used for the Chiral Magnetic Effect, to detect baryonic charge separation in neutron production at the MPD detector. The feasibility is estimated using the UrQMD event generator for Au+Au collisions at NICA energies, with the correlator mean value shown to be zero in the absence of the effect.

## Key numbers

- NICA is expected to operate with average luminosity L ~ 10^27 cm^{-2} s^{-1} for Au+Au collisions in the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 4 to 11 GeV/n.
- Neutron multiplicity predicted by UrQMD is about 200 in full solid angle, with 10 to 150 registered neutrons per event depending on centrality and ~60% detection efficiency.
- The proposed interaction rate for MPD is about 6 kHz, allowing accumulation of ~10^9 events in a few months.
- For CVE measurements at NICA, approximately 1000 million events are needed for the same precision as the CME case at RHIC, where ~15 million events were sufficient.
- The chemical potential is estimated at mu ~ 500 MeV in the NICA energy range.

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Vortaic Effect (Kharzeev 2007) from electromagnetic currents to baryonic charge currents, complementing the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Fukushima 2008) which only affects electrically charged particles. The proposed neutron asymmetry measurements at NICA's MPD detector would provide a clean test of vorticity-induced P-odd effects, as neutrons are unaffected by the Chiral Magnetic Effect. The paper also discusses connections to hyperon polarization (Liang 2004) and handedness correlations (Efremov 1992) as additional probes of vorticity, and notes that the large baryonic chemical potential at NICA energies (compared to RHIC) should make these effects more prominent.
