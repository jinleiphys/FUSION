# Di-neutron correlation and soft dipole excitation in medium mass neutron-rich nuclei near drip-line

> The neutron pairing correlation and the soft dipole excitation in medium-mass nuclei near drip-line are investigated from a viewpoint of the di-neutron correlation. Numerical analyses by means of the coordinate-space HFB and the continuum QRPA methods are performed for even-even $^{18-24}$O, $^{50-58}$Ca and $^{80-86}$Ni. A clear signature of the di-neutron correlation is found in the HFB ground state; two neutrons are correlated at short relative distances $\lesim 2$ fm with large probability $\sim 50%$. The soft dipole excitation is influenced strongly by the neutron pairing correlation, and it accompanies a large transition density for pair motion of neutrons. This behavior originates from a coherent superposition of two-quasiparticle configurations $[l\times (l+1)]_{L=1}$ consisting of continuum states with high orbital angular momenta $l$ reaching an order of $l\sim 10$. It raises a picture that the soft dipole excitation under the influence of neutron pairing is characterized by motion of di-neutron in the nuclear exterior against the remaining $A-2$ subsystem. Sensitivity to the density dependence of effective pair force is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that di-neutron correlation (two neutrons correlated at short relative distances ≤2 fm with ~50% probability) is present not only in light halo nuclei but also rather generally in medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei near the drip-line, and that this correlation strongly influences the soft dipole excitation through a coherent superposition of high-angular-momentum continuum two-quasiparticle configurations. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the density dependence of the effective pair force, with the "mixed pairing" choice as the reference case.

## Method

The authors performed coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the ground states and continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (continuum QRPA) calculations for excitations of even-even ¹⁸⁻²⁴O, ⁵⁰⁻⁵⁸Ca, and ⁸⁰⁻⁸⁶Ni. The particle-hole part was replaced by a spherical Woods-Saxon potential, while the particle-particle part used a density-dependent delta interaction with three choices of density dependence (surface, volume, and mixed pairing). The two-body correlation density and pair transition densities were analyzed to probe di-neutron behavior.

## Key numbers

- Di-neutron probability p(r_d) at the surface position (r_d=2 fm): 0.48 for ²²O, 0.53 for ⁵⁸Ca, 0.49 for ⁸⁴Ni
- Di-neutron probability at the external position (r_d=3 fm): 0.47 for ²²O, 0.59 for ⁵⁸Ca, 0.47 for ⁸⁴Ni
- Width of the correlation density peak (full width at half maximum): approximately 2 fm when the reference neutron is at internal or surface positions
- Quasiparticle energy cut-off used: E_max = 50 MeV
- Angular momentum cut-off used: l_max = 12
- Radial mesh size: Δr = 0.2 fm, box size r_max = 20 fm
- Pairing force strength parameter V_0 = -280 MeV fm³ for the mixed pairing case
- Density parameter for surface pairing: ρ_0 = 0.19 fm⁻³; for mixed pairing: ρ_0 = 0.32 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the study of di-neutron correlation from light two-neutron halo nuclei like ¹¹Li (studied by Esbensen, Zhukov, Barranco (2001), and Ikeda) to medium-mass systems near the drip-line. It contradicts earlier RPA calculations by Catara (1997), Hamamoto, Sagawa, and the RRPA group that predicted uncorrelated neutron excitation with little collectivity in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, because those models neglected neutron pair correlations. The results enable future investigations of how the di-neutron correlation affects other excitation modes and how the density dependence of the pairing force can be constrained by experimental data on soft dipole strength.
