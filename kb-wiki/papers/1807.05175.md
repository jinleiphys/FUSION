# Holographic Collisions across a Phase Transition

> We use holography to analyse relativistic collisions in a one-parameter family of strongly coupled gauge theories with thermal phase transitions. For a critical value of the parameter the transition is second order, for subcritical values it is first order, and for super-critical values it is a smooth crossover. We extract the gauge theory stress tensor from collisions of gravitational shock waves on the dual geometries. Regardless of the nature of the transition, for values of the parameter close to the critical value almost all the energy of the projectiles is deposited into a long-lived, quasi-static blob of energy at mid-rapidity. This configuration is well described by the constitutive relations of second-order hydrodynamics that include all second-order gradients that are purely spatial in the local rest frame. In contrast, a Müller-Israel-Stewart-type formulation of hydrodynamics fails to provide a good description. We discuss possible implications for searches of the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

For a one-parameter family of strongly coupled gauge theories with thermal phase transitions, collisions produce a long-lived, quasi-static blob of energy at mid-rapidity regardless of whether the equilibrium transition is first-order, second-order, or a smooth crossover, provided the parameter is close to its critical value. The blob is well described by second-order hydrodynamics that includes all purely spatial gradients in the local rest frame, but a Müller-Israel-Stewart-type formulation fails to describe it.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of gravitational shock wave collisions in a five-dimensional Einstein-scalar gravity model with a potential parameterized by φ_M, which controls the nature of the thermal phase transition in the dual gauge theory. They extracted the gauge theory stress tensor from the collision dynamics for three values of φ_M (2.50, φ_M* ≈ 2.521, and 2.55) corresponding to first-order, second-order, and crossover transitions, respectively. They then compared the exact pressures from gravity to predictions from second-order hydrodynamics in both the purely spatial formulation and the Müller-Israel-Stewart-type formulation, using transport coefficients extracted from static inhomogeneous configurations.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of the parameter is φ_M* ≈ 2.521, at which the theory possesses a second-order phase transition.
- The hydrodynamization time is t_hyd ≈ 19.01/Λ ≈ 4.64/T_hyd, where T_hyd is the temperature at mid-rapidity at that time.
- After t_hyd, 90% of the fluid energy is moving with velocity v_z < 0.1.
- The speed of sound squared in the blob region is |c_s^2| ≈ 10^(-2).
- The minimal energy density values for the three theories are 10^3 ε_mss = {5.7, 5.8, 5.4} for φ_M = {2.50, φ_M*, 2.55}, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of collisions in conformal field theories (Chesler 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2013) and non-conformal theories without phase transitions (Attems 2016, Attems 2017) to theories with thermal phase transitions. It builds on the static, inhomogeneous configurations studied in Attems (2017) to extract second-order transport coefficients. The results suggest that hydrodynamic codes based on Müller-Israel-Stewart-type formulations (Romatschke 2009) may need modification to describe dynamics near a putative QCD critical point, and that the formation of the blob is driven by the small speed of sound near the transition.
