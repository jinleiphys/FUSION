# Impact of off-shell dynamics on the transport properties and the dynamical evolution of Charm Quarks at RHIC and LHC temperatures

> We evaluate drag and diffusion transport coefficients comparing a quasi-particle approximation with on-shell constituents of the QGP medium and a dynamical quasi-particles model with off-shell bulk medium at finite temperature T. We study the effects of the width $γ$ of the particles of the bulk medium on the charm quark transport properties exploring the range where $γ< M_{q,g}$. We find that off-shell effects are in general quite moderate and can induce a reduction of the drag coefficient at low momenta that disappear already at moderate momenta, $p \gtrsim 2-3\, \rm GeV$. We also observe a moderate reduction of the breaking of the Fluctuation-Dissipation theorem (FDT) at finite momenta. Moreover, we have performed a first study of the dynamical evolution of HQ elastic energy loss in a bulk medium at fixed temperature extending the Boltzmann (BM) collision integral to include off-shell dynamics. A comparison among the Langevin dynamics, the BM collisional integral with on-shell and the BM extension to off-shell dynamics shows that the evolution of charm energy when off-shell effects are included remain quite similar to the case of the on-shell BM collision integral.

## Key claim

Off-shell effects from finite-width quasi-particles in the QGP medium produce only moderate changes to charm quark transport coefficients, reducing the drag coefficient at low momenta (p less than about 2-3 GeV) but becoming negligible at higher momenta, especially when normalized to the medium energy density.

## Method

The authors computed drag and diffusion coefficients for charm quarks scattering in a thermal QGP bath using both an on-shell quasi-particle model (QPM) and an off-shell dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) with Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the bulk partons. They solved the Boltzmann collision integral via Monte-Carlo integration for both on-shell and off-shell cases, and also performed time-evolution simulations of the charm quark distribution function comparing the full Boltzmann approach with a Langevin (Fokker-Planck) approximation.

## Key numbers

* Drag coefficient A at fixed charm momentum p = 0.1 GeV decreases by about 30% when off-shell effects are included, for temperatures in the range T ~ 1-2 T_c (T_c = 155 MeV).
* Transverse diffusion coefficient B_T at p = 0.1 GeV decreases by about 35% in the same temperature range when off-shell effects are included.
* At T = 200 MeV, the DQPM widths are gamma*_q ~ 260 MeV and gamma*_g ~ 110 MeV; for the artificially enlarged width case, gamma*/M = 0.75 gives gamma*_g ~ 520 MeV and gamma*_q ~ 330 MeV at the same temperature.
* The ratio A/epsilon shows that after renormalizing to energy density, the off-shell reduction at low p is about 10% for DQPM widths, and up to about 40% at p -> 0 for the larger width case (gamma*/M = 0.75).
* The Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem violation, measured by B_T/(T E A), improves by about 10% for off-shell DQPM widths, and is satisfied within 10% for the larger width case at T = 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the off-shell transport coefficient study of Berrehrah (2013) by exploring larger widths and explicitly testing the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem, and presents a first direct comparison of Langevin dynamics, on-shell Boltzmann, and off-shell Boltzmann evolution for charm quarks in a fixed-temperature medium. The results enable future studies of whether off-shell effects significantly impact observables like R_AA(p_T) in realistic expanding heavy-ion collision simulations, and suggest that for moderate widths (as in DQPM) the on-shell approximation may be adequate for many purposes.
