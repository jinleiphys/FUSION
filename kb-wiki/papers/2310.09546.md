# The extraction of higher-order radial moments of nuclear charge density from muonic atom spectroscopy

> Muonic atom transitions have been measured for almost all stable nuclei to extract nuclear structure properties, including nuclear charge radii and quadrupole moment. To investigate the possibilities of extracting higher-order radial moments of nuclear charge density %what kind of information of nuclear charge distribution can be extracted precisely from muonic atom spectroscopy, a theory-to-theory benchmark analysis based on a model-independent density distribution, i.e., the Fourier-Bessel series expansion instead of two-parameter Fermi distribution, is performed by taking $^{208}$Pb as an example, where nuclear charge density obtained from the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov calculation is used as the benchmark. It is found that the extractions of the higher-order moments, i.e., the fourth and sixth moments in addition to the second moment are feasible with high accuracy. Moreover, the charge form factor in the low-$q$ region can also be well extracted.

# The extraction of higher-order radial moments of nuclear charge density from muonic atom spectroscopy

## Key claim

Using a model-independent Fourier-Bessel series expansion rather than a two-parameter Fermi distribution, the fourth and sixth radial moments of nuclear charge density can be extracted from muonic atom transition energies with high accuracy, at least for the test case of 208Pb. The extraction works only for the low-momentum-transfer region of the charge form factor (q < 0.2 fm⁻¹), and the detailed shape of the charge density cannot be reliably recovered.

## Method

The authors performed a theory-to-theory benchmark analysis using 208Pb as an example. The nuclear charge density from relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations with the PC-PK1 density functional served as the benchmark. Muonic transition energies for twelve transitions (including 2p-1s, 3d-1s, 4f-1s, 3p-2s, 4d-2s, and 5f-2s) were computed from this density and treated as pseudoexperimental data. An iterative inverse-parabolic-interpolation scheme was then used to find the best-fit Fourier-Bessel coefficients (N_FB = 15, cutoff radius R_cut) that minimize the root-mean-square deviation between the FB-based and RCHB-based transition energies.

## Key numbers

* The second moment R_2 of the RCHB benchmark charge density for 208Pb is 30.310279 fm²; the best-fit FB distribution reproduces it with a difference of -3×10⁻⁶ fm².
* The fourth moment R_4 of the benchmark is 1166.040 fm⁴; the best-fit FB distribution differs by 0.025 fm⁴.
* The sixth moment R_6 of the benchmark is 51965.159 fm⁶; the best-fit FB distribution differs by -1.769 fm⁶.
* The root-mean-square deviation χ for transition energies of the best-fit FB distribution is 2.2×10⁻⁵ keV, compared to 0.34 keV for the initial two-parameter Fermi fit.
* The deviation factor ε between the best-fit FB charge density and the benchmark is 0.043, larger than the initial value of 0.030, confirming that the detailed density shape is not reproduced.
* The charge form factor from the best fit agrees with the benchmark better than the direct density fit in the low-q region below 0.2 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Xie et al. (2023) that showed the two-parameter Fermi distribution introduces model dependency limiting extraction of higher-order moments. It builds on the demonstration by Papoulia et al. (PhysRevA.94.042502) that changes in the fourth moment can be extracted from laser spectroscopy field shifts, and applies a similar logic to muonic atom spectroscopy where the muon mass is about 207 times the electron mass. The Fourier-Bessel expansion method was originally introduced by Dreher et al. (1974) for model-independent analysis of electron scattering data. The results enable future extraction of higher-order radial moments from experimental muonic atom data for stable nuclei, complementing the second-moment information already obtained from laser spectroscopy of unstable isotopes.
