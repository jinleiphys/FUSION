# Quantum diffraction grating. A possible new description of nuclear elastic scattering

> The problem of discontinuous functions and their representations in the form of Legendre polynomial series in quantum nuclear scattering theory is presented briefly. The problem is quite old yet not adequately explained in numerous Quantum Theory textbooks and sometimes not correctly understood by physicists. This problem might be quite important in interpretation and analyses of nuclear elastic scattering data and so requires to be stated in a more detailed and clear way.

# Quantum diffraction grating. A possible new description of nuclear elastic scattering

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear elastic scattering amplitude can be split into separate refractive and diffractive components in angular momentum space, revealing a dynamical quantum diffraction grating formed by quantized impact parameters around the nucleus. This grating, rather than the nucleus itself, is claimed to be the dominant source of diffraction patterns observed in elastic scattering differential cross sections.

## Method

The author extends the standard partial-wave formalism of nuclear elastic scattering by treating Legendre polynomial series representations of the plane wave, Coulomb scattering amplitude, and Dirac delta function as generalized functions (distributions) in the sense of Schwartz (1950). This allows the full elastic scattering amplitude for charged particles to be reduced to a single series: f_el(theta) = (1/2ik) * sum_l (2l+1) S_l^Coul S_l P_l(cos theta). The amplitude is then split into low-angular-momentum (l <= l_gr) and high-angular-momentum (l > l_gr) parts, where the high-l series is identified as pure diffraction independent of the nuclear S-matrix elements, and the low-l series as nuclear refraction. A smooth cut-off separation is performed using a Fermi-type function F(l) fitted to the tail of Re S_l.

## Key numbers

- For 28Si + alpha elastic scattering at E_alpha = 26.5 MeV, the ratio of refraction to total integrated differential cross section over the angular range from 10 degrees to 180 degrees is 4.46 x 10^{-3}.
- The sum-of-differences (SOD) formula for total reaction cross section is: 2*pi * integral from 0 to pi of {|f_C(theta)|^2 - |f_el(theta)|^2} sin(theta) d(theta) = pi/k^2 * sum_l (2l+1)(1 - |S_l|^2) = sigma_r.
- The modified SOD formula uses a starting angle theta_st where sigma_el/sigma_Ruth oscillates around one.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Maj and Mrowczynski (unknown year) of mathematical inconsistencies in partial-wave expansions, and the analysis by Taylor (1974) showing that the Coulomb scattering amplitude cannot be uniquely expanded in Legendre series. The author invokes Schwartz's (1950) theory of generalized functions to resolve these issues. The proposed decomposition of the elastic scattering amplitude into refractive and diffractive parts builds on the sharp cut-off Blair diffraction model (Blair, unknown year) and the optical Babinet principle (Barone, unknown year). The paper enables a new interpretation of elastic scattering data where diffraction from the quantum grating dominates over nuclear refraction, and it suggests that standard chi-squared fitting procedures using the approximate Hodgson (unknown year) test function may yield incorrect optical model parameters because they treat two distinct phenomena (diffraction and refraction) as a single process.
