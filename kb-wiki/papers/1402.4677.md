# Massive Hybrid Stars with Strangeness

> How massive the hybrid stars could be is discussed by a "3-window model" proposed from a new strategy to construct the equation of state with hadron-quark transition. It is found that hybrid stars have a strong potentiality to generate a large mass compatible with two-solar-mass neutron star observations.

# Massive Hybrid Stars with Strangeness

## Key claim
Hybrid stars with a hadron-quark transition core can reach masses compatible with two-solar-mass neutron star observations, provided that quark degrees of freedom set in at a rather low density (around 1.5 times nuclear density) and the quark equation of state is sufficiently stiff. The claim is conditional on using a "3-window model" that interpolates between hadronic and quark equations of state rather than applying conventional Gibbs or Maxwell conditions.

## Method
The authors construct the equation of state (EOS) by dividing density into three regions: pure hadronic EOS (TNI2 with hyperons from a G-matrix effective interaction) below ρ_H, pure quark EOS (from the NJL model with vector interaction strength g_v) above ρ_Q, and an interpolated hadron-quark EOS in between using a polynomial function P_HQ(x) = a x^m + b x^n + c with x = ρ/ρ_0. The four coefficients are determined by matching pressure and energy density at the boundaries ρ_H and ρ_Q, and solutions are searched under conditions of thermodynamic stability and subluminal sound velocity.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses of hybrid stars range from 1.99 M_⊙ to 3.08 M_⊙ depending on parameters.
- For fixed hadron-quark transition parameters (x_H=1.5, x_Q=7.0, m=0.2, n=-2.6) and g_v=0.5 G_s, M_max = 2.53 M_⊙ with radius 12.08 km and central density 4.52 ρ_0.
- With stronger vector interaction g_v=1.5 G_s (same other parameters), M_max reaches 3.08 M_⊙ with radius 13.73 km and central density 3.34 ρ_0.
- Without vector interaction (g_v=0), M_max drops to 1.99 M_⊙ with radius 12.30 km and central density 4.85 ρ_0.
- The hadron-quark transition onset density x_H must be as low as 1.5–2.5 ρ_0 (with ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^−3) for solutions to exist.
- The hadronic EOS TNI2 has incompressibility κ = 250 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' preceding studies (Masuda et al. 2013a, 2013b) which used a smooth crossover interpolation with hyperbolic tangent functions, by instead using exact matching at discrete density boundaries. It addresses the longstanding problem that hyperon mixing in pure hadronic neutron stars softens the EOS too much, making maximum masses below 1.44 M_⊙ (Takatsuka 2004) and conflicting with observations of two-solar-mass neutron stars (Demorest et al. 2010; Antoniadis et al. 2013). The 3-window model offers an alternative to the universal three-body force approach (Takatsuka 2008) for resolving this tension, and suggests that quark percolation in nuclear matter (Baym 1979) may begin at densities as low as 1.5 ρ_0.
