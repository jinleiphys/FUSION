# Isospin symmetry breaking at high spins in the mirror pair 67Se and 67As

> Recent experimental data have revealed large mirror energy differences (MED) between high-spin states in the mirror nuclei 67Se and 67As, the heaviest pair where MED have been determined so far. The MED are generally attributed to the isospin symmetry breaking caused by the Coulomb force and by the isospin nonconserving part of the nucleon-nucleon residual interaction. The different contributions of the various terms have been extensively studied in the fp shell. By employing large-scale shell model calculations, we show that the inclusion of the g9/2 orbit causes interference between the electromagnetic spin-orbit and the Coulomb monopole radial terms at high spin. The large MED are attributed to the aligned proton pair excitations from the p3/2 and f5/2 orbits to the g9/2 orbit. The relation of the MED to deformation is discussed.

## Key claim

The large mirror energy differences (MED) observed in the high-spin states of the mirror pair 67Se and 67As are attributed to interference between the electromagnetic spin-orbit term and the Coulomb monopole radial term, driven by aligned proton pair excitations from the p3/2 and f5/2 orbits to the g9/2 orbit; however, the strength of the radial term was fitted to the experimental data, so a strong conclusion about the role of the isospin nonconserving nucleon-nucleon term cannot be made.

## Method

Large-scale spherical shell model calculations were performed in the pf5/2g9/2 model space using the JUN45 effective interaction (based on the Bonn-C potential and adjusted to data in the A=63-96 mass region) with the code MSHELL. The Coulomb interaction was separated into a monopole term (including single-particle correction εll, radial term VCr, and spin-orbit term εls) and a multipole term VCM; the isospin nonconserving nucleon-nucleon term was neglected because the f7/2 orbit is almost not active in this mass region. The strength parameter am for the radial term VCr was fixed to 280 keV to fit the MED of the positive-parity high-spin states and taken as 0.0 keV for negative-parity states.

## Key numbers

- The proton g9/2 orbit in 67Se is lowered by about 95 keV due to the εll term, the f5/2 orbit by about 58 keV, and the p3/2 orbit is raised by about 135 keV.
- The relative energy gap between the proton g9/2 and f5/2 orbits is reduced by only 37 keV from the εll term.
- The εls term lowers the proton g9/2 orbit by about 66 keV and raises the f5/2 orbit by about 66 keV in 67Se, with opposite effects in 67As; the relative energy gap between proton g9/2 and f5/2 decreases by roughly 132 keV.
- The strength parameter am for the Coulomb monopole radial term VCr was fixed to 280 keV for positive-parity high-spin states.
- At spin 25/2+, two protons and one neutron jump from the fp-shell to the g9/2 orbit in 67Se.
- For the 21/2+ state, all four terms (VCM, εll, VCr, εls) give almost the same contribution of about 40 keV each.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mirror energy differences in the upper fp shell, following the formalism introduced by Zuker et al. (2002) and the review by Bentley (2007). It builds on experimental data for 67As from Grzywacz (2001) and Jenkins (2001) and for 67Se from de Angelis (2007), and on earlier shell-model calculations for the A=67 mirror pair by Hasegawa (2005). The results demonstrate that inclusion of the g9/2 orbit is essential for describing MED in this mass region, and they enable future studies of deformation effects on MED using shell models based on deformed single-particle states, as suggested by Sun and collaborators.
