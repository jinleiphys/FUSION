# Strange Particles from Dense Hadronic Matter

> After a brief survey of the remarkable accomplishments of the current heavy ion collision experiments up to 200A GeV, we address in depth the role of strange particle production in the search for new phases of matter in these collisions. In particular, we show that the observed enhancement pattern of otherwise rarely produced multistrange antibaryons can be consistently explained assuming color deconfinement in a localized, rapidly disintegrating hadronic source. We develop the theoretical description of this source, and in particular study QCD based processes of strangeness production in the deconfined, thermal quark-gluon plasma phase, allowing for approach to chemical equilibrium and dynamical evolution. We also address thermal charm production. Using a rapid hadronization model we obtain final state particle yields, providing detailed theoretical predictions about strange particle spectra and yields as function of heavy ion energy. Our presentation is comprehensive and self-contained: we introduce in considerable detail the procedures used in data interpretation, discuss the particular importance of selected experimental results and show how they impact the theoretical developments.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement pattern of multistrange antibaryons in 200A GeV heavy ion collisions can be consistently explained by assuming color deconfinement in a localized, rapidly disintegrating hadronic source, but this interpretation requires that the transition from the deconfined state to the confined final hadronic gas phase occurs sufficiently rapidly to preserve the memory of the high strangeness density from the early phase.

## Method

The authors develop a thermal fireball model to describe the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions, using QCD-based perturbative expressions for strangeness production in the deconfined thermal phase with allowance for approach to chemical equilibrium and dynamical evolution. They apply conservation of energy and baryon number to determine fireball properties at different stages of evolution, and use a rapid hadronization model to obtain final state particle yields. The analysis covers systems studied at CERN SPS (200A GeV) and BNL AGS (15A GeV) facilities, comparing experimental strange antibaryon ratios with theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope temperature T = 232 MeV observed for S--Au collisions at 200A GeV from WA85 and WA80 strange particle spectra in the transverse mass interval 1.5 < m_perp < 2.6 GeV
- Inverse slope temperature T = 210 MeV observed for S--S collisions at 200A GeV
- Central rapidity y_CM = 2.6 +/- 0.1 for 200A GeV S--Au/W/Pb interactions
- Temperatures of approximately 230 MeV observed in S--Au collisions at SPS, compared to approximately 160 MeV noted in p--p collisions (Hagedorn, 1965)
- Dilepton enhancement window between m_phi (approximately 1.02 GeV) and m_Psi (approximately 3.1 GeV), centered around M approximately 2 +/- 0.6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the prediction by Rafelski (1982) that enhanced production of multistrange antibaryons is specifically related to color deconfinement, and builds on experimental results from the WA85, WA80, WA94, and NA35 collaborations at CERN SPS. The analysis shows a profound difference between 15A GeV BNL data and 200A GeV CERN data that precludes interpretation of the lower energy results in terms of a suddenly disintegrating QGP fireball. The findings enable detailed theoretical predictions about strange particle spectra and yields as functions of heavy ion energy for future experiments at RHIC and LHC.
