# $J/ψ$ production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and the nuclear absorption

> It is shown that a QCD based nuclear absorption model, with few parameters fixed to reproduce experimental $J/ψ$ yield in 200 GeV pp/pA and 450 GeV pA collisions can explain the preliminary PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV. However, the model does not give satisfactory description to the preliminary PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions. The analysis suggest that in Au+Au collisions, $J/ψ$ are suppressed in a medium unlike the medium produced in SPS energy nuclear collisions or in RHIC energy Cu+Cu collisions.

# J/ψ production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and the nuclear absorption

## Key claim

The QCD-based nuclear absorption model, with parameters fixed to reproduce J/ψ yields in pp and pA collisions at 200 GeV and 450 GeV, can explain the centrality dependence of J/ψ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC energy (√sNN=200 GeV) but fails to give a satisfactory description for Au+Au collisions at the same energy. The analysis suggests that in Au+Au collisions, J/ψ are suppressed in a medium unlike the medium produced in SPS energy nuclear collisions or in RHIC energy Cu+Cu collisions.

## Method

The authors apply a QCD-based nuclear absorption model where J/ψ production proceeds via two steps: formation of a ccbar pair (calculable in perturbative QCD) and subsequent formation of a J/ψ meson from that pair (parameterized by a transition probability). In nuclear collisions, the ccbar pair interacts with the nuclear medium and gains relative 4-square momentum; pairs that gain enough momentum cross the threshold for open charm meson production, reducing the J/ψ yield. The model's single free parameter, ε² (the relative square momentum gain per unit length), is fitted to preliminary PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor RAA for J/ψ in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV using the MINUIT minimization program.

## Key numbers

- Best fit for Cu+Cu collisions: ε² = 0.173 ± 0.007 GeV²/fm, which is approximately 7% lower than the SPS energy value of 0.187 GeV²/fm
- Best fit for Au+Au collisions: ε² = 0.146 ± 0.014 GeV²/fm, which is approximately 15% lower than the value required for Cu+Cu collisions
- In pp collisions at RHIC energy, the mean square transverse momentum of J/ψ is ⟨p²T⟩J/ψNN = 4.2 ± 0.7 GeV²
- For pT broadening, the combined fit to Cu+Cu and Au+Au data yields σgNδ0 = 0.03 ± 0.51 GeV², indicating no significant pT broadening at RHIC
- At SPS energy, the product σgNδ0 was estimated as 0.442 ± 0.056 GeV² for S+U/Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCD-based nuclear absorption model, which successfully explained anomalous J/ψ suppression in 158 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions (NA50 data) and 200 AGeV S+U collisions at SPS energies (Chaudhuri 2001, 2002, 2003). The model is tested against preliminary PHENIX data on Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The failure to fit Au+Au data suggests that a deconfined medium may be formed in Au+Au collisions, unlike in Cu+Cu collisions where the medium resembles that at SPS energies. The analysis also shows that pT broadening at RHIC is consistent with SPS energy values within large errors, unlike models predicting J/ψ enhancement at RHIC (Thews 2000, Braun-Munzinger 2000) which are inconsistent with the PHENIX data.
