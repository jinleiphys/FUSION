# Closed expressions for the nuclear moments in semi-leptonic processes

> We discuss a general formalism needed for a unified description of the weak and electromagnetic processes in nuclei which is based on the multipole decomposition of the hadronic currents. The use of harmonic oscillator single-particle basis, which is commonly employed in many-body nuclear calculations, simplifies the relevant expressions. We present analytic formulas for the corresponding radial integrals which enter the transition matrix elements of one-body operators in various semi-leptonic nuclear processes. As an example, we apply our formalism in order to simplify previous formalism giving the nuclear moments for the $0νββ$ - decay. Our results for the radial integrals refer to the 1s-0d-1p-0f model space.

## Key claim

The paper provides closed analytic expressions for the radial integrals entering the transition matrix elements of the seven basic one-body operators in semi-leptonic nuclear processes, specifically for the 1s-0d-1p-0f harmonic oscillator model space, and demonstrates that these expressions simplify the previous formalism for nuclear moments in neutrinoless double-beta decay.

## Method

The authors perform a multipole decomposition of the hadronic current into Coulomb, longitudinal, transverse-electric, and transverse-magnetic operators for both vector and axial-vector currents. They then derive analytic formulas for the reduced single-particle matrix elements of the seven basic operators, expressing the radial integrals as products of an exponential times a polynomial in the momentum transfer q, using harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions. The coefficients of these polynomials are given explicitly for the 1s-0d-1p-0f model space.

## Key numbers

- The radial integrals for the operator $\theta_l^0(\rho) = j_l(\rho)$ are expressed as $e^{-y}y^{L/2} \sum_{\mu=0}^{n_{max}}\varepsilon_{\mu}^L y^{\mu}$ where $y=(qb/2)^2$ and $n_{max}=(N_1+N_2-L)/2$
- The radial integrals for the differential operators $\theta_l^{\pm}$ are expressed as $e^{-y}y^{(L-1)/2} \sum_{\mu=0}^{n_{max}}{\zeta}_{\mu}^{\pm}(L) y^{\mu}$ where $n_{max}=(N_1+N_2-L+1)/2$
- The two-body radial integrals for neutrinoless double-beta decay are simplified to ${\cal R}^{\kappa}_{L_1L_2} = \frac{2^{\kappa+2}}{b^{\kappa+3}} \sum_{\mu=0}^{n_{max}}c_{\mu} {\cal I}^{\lambda}(\omega)$ where $\lambda = \mu + (L_1 + L_2 +\kappa +1)/2$
- The coefficients $c_\mu$ are obtained from the $\varepsilon_{\nu}^{L}$ coefficients as $c_{\mu}= \sum_{\lambda=0}^{\mu} \varepsilon_{\nu}^{L_1}\varepsilon_{\mu -\nu}^{L_2}$

## Context

This work extends the method of Kosmas et al. (2001) and Kosmas and Vergados (1997) which provided compact expressions for nuclear form factors in muon-to-electron conversion and dark matter scattering, by now including operators involving the differential operator $\nabla$ (nabla) that had not been studied previously. The formalism simplifies the earlier work of Suhonen and Civitarese (1998) and Barbi (1998, 1999) on nuclear moments for neutrinoless double-beta decay, replacing double summations over integrals with a single summation. This enables easier calculation of transition matrix elements for any value of momentum transfer q in semi-leptonic processes including lepton-nucleus scattering, beta decay, muon capture, and neutrino-nucleus reactions.
