# Are There Tetraquarks at Large $N_c$ in QCD(F)?

> Weinberg recently pointed out a flaw in the standard argument that large $N_c$ QCD with color-fundamental quarks [QCD(F)] cannot yield narrow tetraquark states. In particular, he observed that the argument does not rule out narrow tetraquarks associated with the leading-order connected diagrams; such tetraquarks would have a width scaling as $N_c^{-1}$. It is shown here, however, that while the standard analysis of tetraquarks does not rule them out, a more thorough analysis rules out quantum-number exotic tetraquarks associated with the leading-order connected diagrams. This analysis is based entirely on conventional assumptions used in large $N_c$ physics applied to the analytic properties of meson-meson scattering. Our result implies that one of three possibilities must be true: i) quantum-number exotic tetraquarks do not exist at large $N_c$; ii) quantum-number exotic tetraquarks exist, but are associated with subleading connected diagrams and have anomalously small widths that scale as $N_c^{-2}$ or smaller; or iii) the conventional assumptions used in large $N_c$ analysis are inadequate.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, contrary to a recent observation by Weinberg (2013) that the standard large-\(N_c\) argument does not rule out narrow tetraquarks, a more thorough analysis of meson-meson scattering amplitudes rules out quantum-number exotic tetraquarks associated with the leading-order connected diagrams in QCD with fundamental-representation quarks [QCD(F)], provided the conventional assumptions used in large-\(N_c\) physics are valid.

## Method

The authors analyze the analytic structure of the meson-meson scattering amplitude at leading nontrivial order in the \(1/N_c\) expansion, using fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations and the LSZ reduction formula to relate the four-point correlation function of quark bilinear sources to the scattering amplitude. They classify all leading-order connected Feynman diagrams for the four-point function by their color-flow topology and spacetime momentum flow, then examine all possible \(s\)-channel cuts of these diagrams to determine whether tetraquark intermediate states can contribute spectral strength to the scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order connected diagrams for the four-point function scale as \(O(N_c^1)\), compared to the disconnected diagrams which scale as \(O(N_c^2)\).
* In the scenario considered by Weinberg, a tetraquark coupling to two mesons would scale as \(O(N_c^{-1/2})\), producing a tetraquark decay width of \(O(N_c^{-1})\).
* If tetraquarks exist but arise from subleading connected diagrams, their widths would scale as \(N_c^{-2}\) or smaller.
* The tetraquark decay constant \(f_T\) would scale as \(O(N_c^{1/2})\) if tetraquarks exist at leading order.

## Context

This work extends the recent observation by Weinberg (2013) that the standard argument by Witten (1979) and Coleman against narrow tetraquarks at large \(N_c\) in QCD(F) contains a loophole, since it only applies to disconnected diagrams. The authors show that for quantum-number exotic channels, even the leading-order connected diagrams cannot support tetraquark resonances, contradicting the most natural scenario for narrow tetraquarks. This result contrasts with the case of QCD(AS) (quarks in the two-index antisymmetric representation), where Cohen and Lebed (2014) have shown that tetraquarks necessarily appear as narrow hadrons. The analysis enables future work to distinguish which large-\(N_c\) limit (F or AS) better describes the physical \(N_c=3\) world, depending on whether observably narrow tetraquarks are discovered.
