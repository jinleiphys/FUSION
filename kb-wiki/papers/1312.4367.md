# Non-smooth Chemical Freeze-out and Apparent Width of Wide Resonances and Quark Gluon Bags in a Thermal Environment

> Here we develop the hadron resonance gas model with the Gaussian width of hadron resonances. This model allows us to treat the usual hadrons and the quark gluon bags on the same footing and to study the stability of the results obtained within different formulations of the hadron resonance gas model. In this work we perform a successful fit of 111 independent hadronic multiplicity ratios measured for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} $= 2.7- 200 GeV. We demonstrate that in a narrow range of collision energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 4.3-4.9 GeV there exist peculiar irregularities in various thermodynamic quantities found at chemical freeze-out. The most remarkable irregularity is an unprecedented jump of the number of effective degrees of freedom observed in this narrow energy range which is seen in all realistic versions of the hadron resonance gas model. Therefore, the developed concept is called the non-smooth chemical freeze-out. We are arguing that these irregularities evidence for the possible formation of quark gluon bags. In order to develop other possible signals of their formation here we study the apparent width of wide hadronic resonances and quark gluon bags in a thermal environment. Two new effects generated for the wide resonances and quark gluon bags by a thermal medium are discussed here: the near threshold thermal resonance enhancement and the near threshold thermal resonance sharpening. On the basis of the new effects we argue that the most optimistic chance to find experimentally the quark gluon bags may be related to their sharpening and enhancement in a thermal medium. In this case the wide quark gluon bags may appear directly or in decays as narrow resonances that are absent in the tables of elementary particles and that have the apparent width about 50-120 MeV and the mass about or above 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a narrow range of collision energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV, there exist peculiar irregularities in thermodynamic quantities at chemical freeze-out, most notably an unprecedented jump in the number of effective degrees of freedom, which are seen in all realistic versions of the hadron resonance gas model, and these irregularities may evidence the possible formation of quark gluon bags. The authors also claim that wide quark gluon bags may appear as narrow resonances with apparent width about 50-120 MeV and mass about or above 2.5 GeV due to near-threshold thermal resonance sharpening and enhancement.

## Method

The authors developed a hadron resonance gas model with Gaussian width for hadron resonances (HRGMG), treating usual hadrons and quark gluon bags on the same footing. They performed a fit of 111 independent hadronic multiplicity ratios measured for center-of-mass collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7-200\) GeV, using the same fitting procedure and particle yield ratios as in prior work (Andronic 2005, 2009; Oliinychenko 2012). The model accounts for hard-core repulsion with equal excluded volume \(b \simeq 0.45\) fm\(^3\) (hard-core radius \(R=0.3\) fm) and includes decay contributions from wide resonances using branching ratios from the THERMUS code.

## Key numbers

- The fit covers 111 independent hadronic multiplicity ratios for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7-200\) GeV.
- In the narrow energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV, the chemical freeze-out temperature \(T^{FO}\) increases by about 1.35 times, while the entropy density \(s^{FO}\) jumps by about 4.2 times.
- The ratio \(s^{FO}/(T^{FO})^3\) jumps by 1.67 times, and \(p^{FO}/(T^{FO})^4\) jumps by 1.5 times in that same energy range.
- The chemical freeze-out pressure increases by 5 times and energy density by about 4.75 times from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3\) GeV to 4.9 GeV.
- The apparent width of quark gluon bags is predicted to be about 50-120 MeV with mass about or above 2.5 GeV.
- For the HRGMG, the limiting temperature parameter \(T^{lim}_O\) is about 6 MeV smaller than for the Breit-Wigner version, with \(T^{lim}_O = 161.5\) MeV for the HRGMBW fit.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model framework developed by Andronic (2005, 2009) and Oliinychenko (2012) by introducing Gaussian width for all hadronic resonances, allowing unified treatment with the finite width model of quark gluon bags (FWM, FWMb). The found irregularities in thermodynamic quantities at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.3-4.9\) GeV contradict the traditional assumption of smooth chemical freeze-out (Cleymans 2006) and are argued to signal possible quark gluon bag formation. The predicted narrow resonances from thermal sharpening and enhancement provide a new experimental signature for quark gluon bag searches at collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 4.9-6\) GeV.
