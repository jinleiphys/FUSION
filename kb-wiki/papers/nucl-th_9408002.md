# Loosely bound hyperons in the SU(3) Skyrme model

> Hyperon pairs bound in deuteron like states are obtained within the SU(3) Skyrme model in agreement with general expectations from boson exchange models. The central binding from the flavor symmetry breaking terms increases with the strangeness contents of the interacting baryons whereas the kinetic non-linear $σ$-model term fixes the spin and isospin of the bound pair. We give a complete account of the interactions of octet baryons within the product approximation to baryon number $B=2$ configurations.

## Key claim

Hyperon pairs can form loosely bound deuteron-like states within the SU(3) Skyrme model, with the central binding from flavor symmetry breaking terms increasing with the strangeness content of the interacting baryons, while the kinetic nonlinear sigma-model term fixes the spin and isospin of the bound pair.

## Method

The authors used the product ansatz for baryon number B=2 configurations of two SU(3) skyrmions separated by a distance R, with constant flavor rotations A and C describing the relative orientation. They numerically evaluated the energy functional from the standard SU(3) Skyrme-model lagrangian (including flavor symmetric and flavor symmetry breaking parts) for twelve independent orientations of the relative rotation C, then solved a system of linear equations to extract the seven interaction components. Matrix elements of these components were evaluated between spin-one-half baryon wavefunctions diagonalizing the rotational hamiltonian with symmetry breaking to obtain potentials for various baryon-baryon channels.

## Key numbers

- The flavor symmetry breaking central interaction U_0^(FSB)(R) is proportional to the product of the strangeness contents of the two interacting baryons, where the strangeness content is given by ⟨Y|(1/3)(1 - D_88)|Y⟩
- The asymptotic interaction at large R contains one-pion exchange with strength proportional to (7/30)^2 for nucleons, with g_A^(SU(3)) = (7/10) g_A^(SU(2)) in the Skyrme model
- The most attractive product configuration in SU(3) occurs at the same relative orientation as in SU(2): C = exp(iπ/2 λ_2)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to baryon-baryon interactions (previously applied to the nucleon-nucleon potential, reviewed by Walhout and Wambach (1992)) to hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon systems. It contrasts with earlier studies by Kalbermann and Eisenberg (1990, 1992) which focused on central interactions but reached no conclusive statements about bound states. The results agree with general expectations from boson exchange models such as the Nijmegen potentials (Nijmegen group, 1992) and confirm the existence of an attractive ΛΛ interaction in the ^1S_0 state required from complementary analyses (Dover et al., 1991). The work enables predictions of spin and isospin quantum numbers for bound deuteron-like hyperon states.
