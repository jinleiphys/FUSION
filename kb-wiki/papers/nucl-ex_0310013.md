# Experimental Signals of Phase Transition

> The connection between the thermodynamics of charged finite nuclear systems and the asymptotically measured partitions is presented. Some open questions, concerning in particular equilibrium partitions are discussed. We show a detailed comparison of the decay patterns in Au+ C,Cu,Au central collisions and in Au quasi-projectile events. Observation of abnormally large fluctuations in carefully selected samples of data is reported as an indication of a first order phase transition (negative heat capacity) in the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim
The paper reports that abnormally large fluctuations in carefully selected samples of data indicate a first order phase transition (negative heat capacity) in the nuclear equation of state, but notes that these signals are still circumstantial and require further experimental investigation before the phase transition can be definitively assessed.

## Method
The authors analyzed multifragmentation data from central Au+C, Au+Cu, and Au+Au collisions at 25 and 35 A MeV, and from Au quasi-projectile events in peripheral 35 A MeV Au+Au collisions, measured with the MULTICS-MINIBALL apparatus at the K1200-NSCL Cyclotron. They selected single-source events with constant collected charge (90% of total charge for central collisions, 90% of projectile charge for peripheral events) and verified event isotropy to minimize entrance-channel memory. They then evaluated the microcanonical temperature from kinetic energy thermometers, determined the freeze-out volume from many-body Coulomb trajectory calculations, and computed the heat capacity from partial energy fluctuations using the relation C = C_k^2 T^2 / (C_k T^2 - σ_k^2).

## Key numbers
- Excitation energies for compared data sets: 1.6, 3.1, 4.7, and 7.4 A MeV for central Au+C at 25 A MeV, Au+Cu at 25 and 35 A MeV, and Au+Au at 35 A MeV, respectively.
- Freeze-out volume close to three times the normal volume (3 V_0) reproduces measured kinetic energies for most data sets; for central Au+Au collisions, an uncertainty from radial flow of about 1 A MeV exists.
- For the Au quasi-projectile data, the temperature evaluated from isotope and kinetic energy thermometers strongly deviates from the Fermi gas ansatz of √(8 E*).
- The critical-like energy extracted from scaling analyses of both quasi-projectile and central events points to the same value, though the quality of scaling does not depend on the temperature ansatz adopted.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on nuclear liquid-gas phase transition signals, including caloric curve flattening (Natowitz, 2002; others), negative heat capacity observations (Michela, 2002; Pal, 2002), and critical scaling analyses from the EOS collaboration and Isis data (EOS-Isis, 2002). The paper shows that scaling behavior alone does not distinguish first-order from second-order phase transitions, as similar scaling can arise from first-order transitions in small systems (as shown in statistical models by Campi, 1999). The results enable future systematic studies with higher-resolution detectors (e.g., Garfield apparatus at LNL) to locate multifragmentation in the nuclear phase diagram and investigate isospin distillation effects.
