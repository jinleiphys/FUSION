# Role of isospin dependent mean field in pion production in heavy ion reactions

> The importance of a isospin dependent nuclear mean field (IDMF) in regard to the pion production mechanism is studied for the reaction $Au+Au$ at 1 GeV/nucleon using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model. In particular, the effect of the IDMF on pion spectra and the charged pion ratio are analyzed. It is found that the inclusion of a IDMF considerably suppresses the low$-p_t$ pions, thus, leading to a better agreement with the data on pion spectra. Moreover, the rapidity distribution of the charged pion ratio appears to be sensitive to the isospin dependence of the nuclear mean field.

## Key claim

Including an isospin dependent nuclear mean field (IDMF) in QMD simulations of Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon suppresses low-transverse-momentum pions and modifies the rapidity distribution of the charged pion ratio, but the total charged pion multiplicity remains overestimated compared to experimental data even with the IDMF included.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1 A.GeV, adding an isospin dependent mean field (IDMF) to the standard momentum-dependent Skyrme force (soft equation of state). The IDMF was derived from the potential part of the nuclear symmetry energy with a density dependence F(ρ/ρ₀) = (ρ/ρ₀)^γ, using two values γ=0.3 and γ=2.0. Pions were produced via Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonance decays, with all relevant channels including resonance production, rescattering, decay, and reabsorption treated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at 1 A.GeV without IDMF, the charged pion multiplicity ratios are π⁻:π⁰:π⁺ = 1.37:1:0.71, giving π⁻/π⁺ = 1.95
- With IDMF at zero impact parameter (b=0), the average number of n-n collisions decreases by about 10%, while p-p collisions increase by about 5%
- Total pion multiplicity at midrapidity for b=0 decreases by about 20% when IDMF is included (from approximately 58 to approximately 50 pions)
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio without Coulomb interaction and without IDMF is approximately 1.8 (constant across rapidity)
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio without IDMF but with Coulomb interaction exceeds 1.95 over the midrapidity region

## Context

This work extends prior QMD and BUU transport model studies (Teis 1997, Uma Maheswari 1997) that found these models overestimate low-energy pion yields and charged pion multiplicities, particularly for heavy systems like Au compared to lighter systems like Ni (Pelte 1997). The study builds on earlier findings by Li (1996, 1997) that IDMF strongly affects collective flow and pre-equilibrium p/n ratios at low incident energies. The results suggest that the rapidity distribution of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in central collisions can serve as a signature for the importance of isospin dependent mean fields at SIS energies, with a preliminary analysis of FOPI data (Pelte) indicating a double-peaked structure consistent with the IDMF effect.
