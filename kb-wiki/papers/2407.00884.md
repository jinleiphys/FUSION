# Mechanisms of mirror energy difference for states exhibiting Thomas-Ehrman shift: Gamow shell model case studies of $^{18}$Ne/$^{18}$O and $^{19}$Na/$^{19}$O

> The mirror energy difference (MED) of the mirror state, especially for states bearing the Thomas-Erhman shift, serves as a sensitive probe of isospin symmetry breaking. We employ the Gamow shell model, which includes the inter-nucleon correlation and continuum coupling, to investigate the MED for $sd$-shell nuclei by taking the $^{18}$Ne/$^{18}$O and $^{19}$Na/$^{19}$O as examples. Our GSM provides good descriptions for the excitation energies and MEDs for the $^{18}$Ne/$^{18}$O and $^{19}$Na/$^{19}$O. Moreover, our calculations also reveal that the large MED of the mirror states is caused by the significant occupation of the weakly bound or unbound $s_{1/2}$ waves, giving the radial density distribution of the state in the proton-rich nucleus more extended than that of mirror states in deeply-bound neutron-rich nuclei. Furthermore, our GSM calculation shows that the contribution of Coulomb is different for the low-lying states in proton-rich nuclei, which significantly contributes to MEDs of mirror states. Moreover, the contributions of the nucleon-nucleon interaction are different for the mirror state, especially for the state of proton-rich nuclei bearing the Thomas-Erhman shift, which also contributes to the significant isospin symmetry breaking with large MED.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model (GSM) calculations show that large mirror energy differences (MED) in states exhibiting the Thomas-Ehrman shift arise from the significant occupation of weakly bound or unbound \(s_{1/2}\) partial waves in the proton-rich nucleus, which produces a more extended radial density distribution than in the deeply bound neutron-rich mirror state; both the Coulomb interaction and the difference in nuclear interaction contributions between mirror states are competitive factors in the observed isospin symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow shell model (GSM) in a core-plus-valence-nucleons picture with a \({}^{16}\)O inert core, using a one-body Woods-Saxon potential for the core and a pionless effective field theory interaction (up to next-to-next-to-leading order) for the residual nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Berggren basis was used for the \(s_{1/2}\), \(p_{1/2,3/2}\), and \(d_{3/2,5/2}\) partial waves (40 discretization points for continuum states per partial wave), while the \(f_{5/2,7/2}\) partial waves were treated with a harmonic oscillator basis (6 states per partial wave). The GSM Hamiltonian matrix was diagonalized using the overlap method with the Jacobi-Davidson method extended to complex-symmetric matrices, and the Coulomb force was the only isospin non-conserving part included.

## Key numbers

*   For the \(^{18}\)Ne/\(^{18}\)O mirror pair, the GSM-calculated MED for the \(3_1^+\) state is \(-892\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(-817\) keV.
*   For the \(^{18}\)Ne/\(^{18}\)O mirror pair, the GSM-calculated MED for the \(2_2^+\) state is \(-420\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(-304\) keV.
*   For the \(^{19}\)Na/\(^{19}\)O mirror pair, the GSM-calculated MED for the \(1/2_1^+\) state is \(-607\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(-727\) keV.
*   For the \(^{19}\)Na/\(^{19}\)O mirror pair, the GSM-calculated MED for the \(3/2_1^+\) state is \(-64\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(24\) keV.
*   The GSM-calculated excitation energy of the \(2_1^+\) state in \(^{18}\)Ne is 1.83 MeV (experimental: 1.89 MeV); in \(^{18}\)O it is 1.93 MeV (experimental: 1.98 MeV).
*   The GSM-calculated excitation energy of the \(1/2_1^+\) state in \(^{19}\)Na is 0.69 MeV (experimental: 0.75 MeV); in \(^{19}\)O it is 1.30 MeV (experimental: 1.47 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin symmetry breaking in \(sd\)-shell mirror nuclei, such as those using the standard shell model (Bentley 2007) and the *ab initio* valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (Zhang 2022, Li 2023), by employing the Gamow shell model which explicitly includes continuum coupling and inter-nucleon correlations. The results provide a direct calculation of radial density distributions for mirror states, revealing that the extended density in the proton-rich nucleus (due to \(s_{1/2}\) occupation) is the external mechanism for the Thomas-Ehrman shift, and that both Coulomb and nuclear interaction contributions are significant. This enables future studies to disentangle external (continuum-driven) and internal (configuration-mixing-driven) mechanisms of isospin symmetry breaking in drip-line nuclei.
