# Electron capture cross sections for stellar nucleosynthesis

> In the first stage of this work, we perform detailed calculations for the cross sections of the electron capture on nuclei under laboratory conditions. Towards this aim we exploit the advantages of a refined version of the proton-neutron quasi-particle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) and carry out state-by-state evaluations of the rates of exclusive processes that lead to any of the accessible transitions within the chosen model space. In the second stage of our present study, we translate the above mentioned $e^-$-capture cross sections to the stellar environment ones by inserting the temperature dependence through a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution describing the stellar electron gas. As a concrete nuclear target we use the $^{66}Zn$ isotope, which belongs to the iron group nuclei and plays prominent role in stellar nucleosynthesis at core collapse supernovae environment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pn-QRPA method, tested through muon capture rate calculations, can reliably compute electron capture cross sections for the 66Zn isotope under both laboratory and stellar conditions, but the stellar cross sections require a Maxwell-Boltzmann thermal averaging that introduces temperature dependence and the assumption that the parent nucleus can be in any excited initial state.

## Method

The authors first solve BCS equations to describe the correlated ground state of 66Zn using a Woods-Saxon mean field with pairing from the Bonn C-D potential, then construct excited states of the daughter 66Cu by solving pn-QRPA equations separately for each multipole set up to Jπ ≤ 5±. They test the nuclear model by calculating total muon capture rates for 66Zn and comparing with experimental data, then compute original electron capture cross sections under laboratory conditions (ground-state initial nucleus, no temperature). Finally, they translate these to stellar environment cross sections by inserting temperature dependence through a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for the stellar electron gas and summing over thermally populated initial nuclear states.

## Key numbers

- Experimental neutron pairing gap for 66Zn: Δn_exp = 1.7715 MeV; theoretical value reproduced as 1.7716 MeV
- Experimental proton pairing gap for 66Zn: Δp_exp = 1.2815 MeV; theoretical value reproduced as 1.2814 MeV
- Total muon capture rate for 66Zn calculated with quenched gA = 1.135: Λtot = 5.934 × 10^6 s^-1; experimental value is 5.809 × 10^6 s^-1 (deviation smaller than 7%)
- The 1- multipole transitions contribute 44.21% of the total muon capture rate for 66Zn
- The 1+ multipole transitions contribute 21.29% of the total muon capture rate for 66Zn
- For electron energies up to 30 MeV, the 1+ multipolarity has the largest contribution to total electron capture cross sections; contributions from other multipolarities (1-, 0+, 0-) become noticeable around 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous electron capture rate calculations for iron-group nuclei that used the Independent Particle Model by Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1982), large-scale shell model calculations by Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000, 2001), and RPA studies by Nabi and collaborators (2007). The pn-QRPA approach allows state-by-state evaluation of exclusive transitions, which is important for stellar nucleosynthesis in core-collapse supernovae where 66Zn and similar pf-shell nuclei play a prominent role. The method enables future calculations of electron capture cross sections for other iron-group nuclei under both laboratory and stellar conditions, which are needed for modeling the reduction of electron-to-baryon ratio Ye during the pre-supernova collapse phase.
