# Half-quantized Non-Abelian Vortices in Neutron $^3P_2$ Superfluids inside Magnetars

> We point out that half-quantized non-Abelian vortices exist as the minimum energy states in rotating neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids in the inner cores of magnetars with magnetic field greater than $3 \times 10^{15}$ Gauss, while they do not in ordinary neutron stars with smaller magnetic fields. One integer vortex is split into two half-quantized vortices. The number of vortices is about $10^{19}$ and they are separated at about $μ$m in a vortex lattice for typical parameters, while the vortex core size is about 10-100 fm. They are non-Abelian vortices characterized by non-Abelian first homotopy group, and consequently when two vortices corresponding to non-commutative elements collide, a rung vortex must be created between them, implying the formation of an entangled vortex network inside the cores of magnetars. We find the spontaneous magnetization in the vortex core showing anti-ferromagnetism whose typical magnitude is about $10^{8-9}$ Gauss that is ten times larger than that of integer vortices, when external magnetic fields are present along the vortex line.

## Key claim

Half-quantized non-Abelian vortices exist as the minimum energy states in rotating neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids inside magnetars, but only when the magnetic field exceeds \(3 \times 10^{15}\) Gauss; they do not exist in ordinary neutron stars with weaker magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors use the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy for \(^3P_2\) superfluids, derived in the weak coupling limit, to determine the ground state in the presence of strong magnetic fields. They construct an Ansatz for a half-quantized vortex in the \(D_4\) biaxial nematic phase, solve the resulting equations of motion numerically using Newton's method, and compute the spontaneous magnetization of the vortex core due to the neutron anomalous magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- The threshold magnetic field for the existence of half-quantized non-Abelian vortices is \(3 \times 10^{15}\) Gauss.
- The number of vortices in a typical magnetar is about \(10^{19}\).
- The mean distance between vortices in the lattice is about \(1.7 \times 10^{-6}\) m (approximately \(\mu\)m).
- The vortex core size (coherence length) is about 10–100 fm.
- The spontaneous magnetization in the vortex core is about \(10^{8-9}\) Gauss, which is ten times larger than that of integer vortices.
- The magnetization is anti-ferromagnetic, i.e., anti-parallel to the external magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(^3P_2\) neutron superfluids in magnetars, building on prior determination of the ground state phases by Masuda (2015) and earlier work on integer vortex structures by Richardson (1972), Muzikar (1980), and Sauls (1982). The half-quantized non-Abelian vortices are analogous to those found in spin-2 Bose-Einstein condensates (BECs) studied by Kawaguchi (2012) and others. The existence of these vortices predicts the formation of an entangled vortex network in magnetar cores, which may distinguish the dynamics of magnetars from ordinary neutron stars, and opens the possibility of testing neutron star physics in ultracold atomic gas experiments.
