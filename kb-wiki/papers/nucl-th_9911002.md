# Polarization degrees of freedom in two-nucleon knockout from finite nuclei

> Polarization observables for the A(e,e'NN) and A(γ,NN) reactions are a powerful tool to study nucleon-nucleon correlations in the nuclear medium. In this paper, model calculations for the ^{4}He(e,e'pp), ^{16}O(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}p), ^{16}O(\vec{e},e' \vec{p}n) and ^{12}C(\vecγ,\vec{p}N) reactions are presented. The sensitivity of the differential cross sections and polarization observables to central and spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon correlations is discussed.

# Polarization degrees of freedom in two-nucleon knockout from finite nuclei

## Key claim

Polarization observables in A(e,e'NN) and A(γ,NN) reactions can isolate the longitudinal channel and minimize final-state interaction ambiguities, but the interpretation of two-nucleon knockout data in terms of nucleon-nucleon correlations is complicated by the "background" of meson-exchange and Δ-isobar currents, which are a strong source of two-nucleon knockout strength.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic shell-model calculations for exclusive two-nucleon knockout reactions, implementing central, tensor, and spin-isospin correlation functions in a spectator approximation that retains operators up to the two-body level. They used a distorted-wave description for the two ejected nucleons and included conventional two-body currents from pion-exchange and Δ-isobar contributions. Partial wave expansion and Racah algebra techniques were exploited to reduce the dimensionality of the matrix elements involving three particles in the final state.

## Key numbers

- For the NIKHEF ¹⁶O(e,e'pp) experiment, the average energy transfer was ω = 206 MeV and momentum transfer q ≈ 300 MeV/c; satisfactory agreement with data was obtained using the G-matrix correlation function from Dickhoff and Gearhart.
- The spin-dependent correlation functions in momentum space overshoot the central correlation function for relative momenta in the region 200–400 MeV/c; for prel > 500 MeV/c, the central correlations dominate.
- For ¹²C(γ,pn) in coplanar symmetrical kinematics, the strongest effects of tensor correlations on the photon asymmetry were found in the photon energy range 200–300 MeV, where Δ-isobar currents are heavily contributing.
- For ¹²C(γ,pp) at Eγ = 200 MeV in quasi-deuteron kinematics, tensor correlations induce a substantial global reduction in the differential cross section with a weak proton angle dependence.

## Context

This work extends the factorized approach of Ryckebusch (1996) for A(e,e'pp) reactions to include polarization observables and spin-dependent correlations, and provides model calculations for ⁴He(e,e'pp), ¹⁶O(e,e'pp), ¹⁶O(e,e'pn), and ¹²C(γ,pN). The results contradict the variational calculations of Pieper (using the Argonne v14 potential) for ¹⁶O, which produced a central correlation function too soft to describe the ¹²C(e,e'pp) data from MAMI (Blomqvist, 1998) and the ¹⁶O(e,e'pp) data from NIKHEF (Gerkop). The framework enables future experiments at Mainz to extract central and spin-dependent correlation functions from recoil polarization measurements, particularly in superparallel kinematics where the Pt' observable is a relatively unambiguous measure of tensor correlations.
