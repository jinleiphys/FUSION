# The eta transition form factor from space- and time-like experimental data

> The $η$ transition form factor is analysed for the first time in both space- and time-like regions at low and intermediate energies in a model-independent approach through the use of rational approximants. The $η\rightarrow e^+e^-γ$ experimental data provided by the A2 Collaboration in the very low energy region of the dilelectron invariant mass distribution allows for the extraction of the most precise up-to-date slope and curvature parameters of the form factors as well as their values at zero and infinity. The impact of these new results on the mixing parameters of the $η$-$η^\prime$ system, together with the role played by renormalisation dependent effects, and on the determination of the $VPγ$ couplings from $V\to Pγ$ and $P\to Vγ$ radiative decays are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first model-independent combined analysis of the η transition form factor (TFF) in both space- and time-like regions using rational approximants, yielding the most precise extraction of the slope and curvature parameters to date, but the results depend on the inclusion of the η→γγ decay width as a fitted datum and the asymptotic value remains in tension with the BaBar time-like measurement at 112 GeV².

## Method

The authors fit Padé approximants (rational functions) to all available experimental data on the η TFF: space-like data from CELLO, CLEO, and BaBar collaborations, and time-like data from NA60 and A2 collaborations (η→e⁺e⁻γ and η→μ⁺μ⁻γ decays). They use both P^L_1(Q²) sequences (single-pole type) and P^N_N(Q²) sequences (which incorporate the correct 1/Q² asymptotic behavior) to extract the low-energy parameters (slope b_η, curvature c_η, third derivative d_η) from the Taylor expansion of the TFF at zero momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter: b_η = 0.576(11)_stat(4)_sys
- Curvature parameter: c_η = 0.339(15)_stat(5)_sys
- Third derivative: d_η = 0.200(14)_stat(18)_sys
- Asymptotic limit: lim_{Q²→∞} Q² F_{ηγ*γ}(Q²) = 0.177^{+0.020}_{-0.009} GeV
- Effective single-pole mass: Λ_η = 0.722(7) GeV (or Λ_η⁻² = 1.919(39) GeV⁻²)
- Predicted two-photon decay width from fit without including Γ_{η→γγ}: Γ_{ηγγ}|_{fit} = 0.43(13) keV (P^7_1) and 0.42(10) keV (P^2_2), compared to experimental value Γ_{η→γγ} = 0.516(18) keV

## Context

This work extends the method of rational approximants previously applied to the π⁰ TFF (Masjuan 2012) and to η, η′ TFFs using only space-like data (Escribano 2013). The inclusion of precise time-like data from the A2 Collaboration (Aguar-Bartolome 2013) improves the statistical and systematic uncertainties by an order of magnitude compared to the earlier space-like-only analysis. The results enable better determination of η-η′ mixing parameters and VPγ couplings, and highlight a persistent tension with the BaBar time-like measurement at 112 GeV² (Aubert 2006), motivating future measurements from Belle and BESIII.
