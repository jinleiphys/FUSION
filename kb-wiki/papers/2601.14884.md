# Probing Late-Stage Hadronic Interactions at High Baryon Density via $K^{*0}$ Production in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Program

> A precision measurement of the $K^{*0}$ meson yield is reported in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7,\; 11.5,\; 14.6,\; 19.6,$ and $27~\mathrm{GeV}$ using the high-statistics data sample collected by the STAR experiment during the Beam Energy Scan II (BES-II) program at RHIC. The transeverse momentum ($p_{T}$)-integrated yield ratios $(K^{*0} + \overline{K^{*0}})/(K^{+} + K^{-})$ in central collisions show a suppression relative to peripheral collisions at the $(1.7\text{-}3.6)\,σ$ level, while a thermal model without final-stage rescattering overpredicts this ratio with a deviation of $(6.9\text{-}8.2)\,σ$. These results indicate a loss of the measured $K^{*0}$ signal in central collisions due to re-scattering of its hadronic decay products in the hadronic phase. The $p_{T}$-integrated yield of charged kaons exhibits an approximate scaling with charged-particle multiplicity, independent of collision energy and system size. A similar trend is observed for the short-lived $K^{*0}$ resonance, although significant deviations emerge at lower energies. At BES energies, the $K^{*0}/K$ ratio shows stronger suppression than at the highest RHIC and LHC energies within a given multiplicity bin, particularly in central and mid-central collisions. This behavior is consistent with changes in the effective hadronic interaction cross section and is supported by transport model calculations, which indicate dominant meson-baryon interactions at lower energies and meson-meson interactions at higher energies.

## Key claim
The measured suppression of the $(K^{*0} + \overline{K^{*0}})/(K^{+} + K^{-})$ ratio in central Au+Au collisions at BES-II energies (7.7–27 GeV) is attributed to re-scattering of the hadronic decay products in the hadronic phase, with the thermal model (without final-stage rescattering) overpredicting the ratio by (6.9–8.2)σ, while the central-to-peripheral suppression itself is only at the (1.7–3.6)σ level.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration reconstructed the $K^{*0}$ meson via its hadronic decay channel $K^{*0}(\overline{K^{*0}}) \rightarrow K^{\pm}\pi^{\mp}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 14.6, 19.6,$ and $27$ GeV from the BES-II data sample. The invariant mass distribution of oppositely charged kaon-pion pairs was constructed, the combinatorial background was estimated using the track-rotation method, and the signal peak was fitted with a Breit-Wigner function (width fixed to the vacuum value of 47.4 MeV/$c^{2}$) plus a second-order polynomial residual background. Raw yields were corrected for detector acceptance, reconstruction efficiency (via GEANT3 embedding), and particle identification efficiency.

## Key numbers
- The $(K^{*0} + \overline{K^{*0}})/(K^{+} + K^{-})$ ratio in central collisions is suppressed relative to peripheral collisions at the (1.7–3.6)σ level.
- A thermal model without final-stage rescattering overpredicts this ratio with a deviation of (6.9–8.2)σ.
- The vacuum width of the $K^{*0}$ meson used in the fit is $\Gamma [K^{*0}] = 47.4 \pm 0.6$ MeV/$c^{2}$.
- The combined TPC+TOF PID efficiency for reconstructed tracks is 91%.
- The number of minimum-bias events analyzed ranges from $90 \times 10^{6}$ (at 7.7 GeV) to $775 \times 10^{6}$ (at 19.6 GeV).

## Context
This work extends the STAR BES-I results (STAR 2023) on $K^{*0}$ production, which indicated more suppression at BES energies than at top RHIC and LHC energies but suffered from large uncertainties. The higher-statistics BES-II data confirm the trend and show that transport model calculations incorporating meson-baryon interactions (dominant at lower energies) and meson-meson interactions (dominant at higher energies) qualitatively reproduce the observed suppression pattern. The results enable quantitative constraints on the hadronic interaction cross sections and the duration of the hadronic phase in high-baryon-density matter.
