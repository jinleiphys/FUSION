# Fluctuations, correlations and non-extensivity

> Minireview presented at XXXVI International Symposium on Multiparticle Dynamica, September 2-8, 2006, Paraty (RJ), Brazil; to be published in Braz. J. Phys. (2007). Some misprints corrected

## Key claim
The paper argues that deviations from exponential distributions in high-energy multiparticle production, parameterized by the Tsallis q-exponential, are primarily caused by intrinsic fluctuations in the hadronizing system rather than by long-range correlations, with the non-extensivity parameter q-1 serving as a measure of the strength of those fluctuations.

## Method
The author reviews the connection between nonextensive Tsallis statistics and fluctuations in multiparticle production, showing that when fluctuations of the inverse temperature β follow a gamma distribution, the resulting averaged distribution becomes a q-exponential. The approach is extended by introducing multiplicative noise into the Langevin equation for Brownian motion, which yields Tsallis distributions as exact stationary solutions of the corresponding Fokker-Planck equation. The analysis is applied to experimental data on rapidity distributions dN/dy and transverse momentum distributions dN/dpT from pp, p̄p, and RHIC collisions, fitting them with q-exponentials and comparing with Hagedorn-type models.

## Key numbers
- For rapidity distributions in pp and p̄p collisions, qL ranges from 1.05 at √s = 20 GeV to 1.33 at √s = 1800 GeV, while TL ranges from 1.76 GeV to 55.69 GeV.
- For transverse momentum distributions, qT ranges from 1.095 at √s = 200 GeV to 1.11 at √s = 900 GeV, while TT ranges from 0.134 GeV to 0.14 GeV.
- Fitting RHIC STAR data with the Hagedorn model (including resonance spectrum ρ(m)) requires qT ≈ 1.00015; fitting with a simple q-exponential (ρ(m)=1) gives qT = 1.065.
- Inelasticity distributions χ(K) at √s = 200 GeV and √s = 900 GeV are fitted by gaussian and lorentzian distributions, with the lorentzian corresponding to a q-gaussian with q=2.

## Context
This minireview extends the author's prior work (Wilk and Włodarczyk, 2000) connecting nonextensivity to fluctuations in hadronizing systems, and contrasts with approaches that attribute q≠1 to long-range correlations or memory effects. The work builds on Tsallis (1988) nonextensive entropy and superstatistics (Beck and Cohen, 2003), and shows that the Hagedorn model with resonance spectrum (Hagedorn, 1965) still requires qT>1 to fit RHIC data, suggesting that fluctuations beyond those from resonance decays are present. The results enable event-by-event fluctuation searches and nonextensive hydrodynamical models as next steps.
