# Pionic BEC--BCS crossover at finite isospin chemical potential

> We study the character change of the pionic condensation at finite isospin chemical potential μ_\mathrm{I} by adopting the linear sigma model as a non-local interaction between quarks. At low |μ_\mathrm{I}| the condensation is purely bosonic, then the Cooper pairing around the Fermi surface grows gradually as |μ_\mathrm{I}| increases. This q-\bar q pairing is weakly coupled in comparison with the case of the q-q pairing that leads to color superconductivity.

## Key claim

The pionic condensation at finite isospin chemical potential exhibits a BEC–BCS crossover: at low chemical potential the condensation is purely bosonic, and as the chemical potential increases, Cooper pairing between quarks and antiquarks gradually grows, but this q–qbar pairing is weaker than the q–q pairing that leads to color superconductivity.

## Method

The author adopts the linear sigma model as a non-local interaction between quarks and derives a Gor'kov-type equation for the quark propagator in the pion condensed phase at finite isospin chemical potential. The momentum-dependent pionic gap function and the pair wave function are obtained by solving the coupled equations self-consistently in momentum space, using an instantaneous approximation and a cutoff at the typical hadronic scale. The coherence length is then computed from the Fourier transform of the pair wave function.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.138\) GeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.093\) GeV, current quark mass \(m_q = 0.0055\) GeV, momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.63\) GeV, potential parameter \(\lambda = 4.5\), quark–meson coupling \(G = 3.3\).
* The coherence length at low \(|\mu_\mathrm{I}|\) is consistent with the pion charge radius squared \(\langle r^2\rangle_S^\pi = 0.61 \pm 0.04\) fm\(^2\) from \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) scattering (CGL).
* At \(|\mu_\mathrm{I}| = 0.5\) GeV, the pair wave function shows a peak around the Fermi surface and a bump near \(k=0\) due to the mesonic contribution.
* For \(|\mu_\mathrm{I}| \ge 0.8\) GeV, a gapless pairing (negative quasiparticle energy) occurs.
* The typical inter-pion distance at freeze-out in heavy ion collisions is estimated as \(d \gtrsim (V/N_\mathrm{c})^{1/3} = 0.79\) fm, using charged particle multiplicity \(N_\mathrm{c} = 555\) and source size \(V = (6.48\ \mathrm{fm})^3\).

## Context

This work extends the prediction by Son and Stephanov (2001) that the pion condensed phase evolves to Cooper pairing between \(u\) and \(\bar d\) at high isospin chemical potential, providing the first quantitative study of the spatial structure of the condensation. It contrasts with the diquark pairing in color superconductivity studied by the author (Matsuzaki, 2000) and by Alford, Han, and Iida (2006), showing that the q–qbar pairing is weaker. The results enable lattice QCD simulations (e.g., for SU(2) color systems) to measure the spatial structure of composite pions as a signature of the BEC–BCS crossover, and may affect descriptions of pions in heavy ion collisions.
