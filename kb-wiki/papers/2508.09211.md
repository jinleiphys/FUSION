# On continuum and resonant spectra from exact WKB analysis

> Resonance phenomena are central to many quantum systems, where resonant states are typically characterized by pole singularities of the S-matrix. In this work, we employ the complex scaling method (CSM) in conjunction with exact WKB analysis to elucidate the geometric structure of scattering problems that encompass both bound and resonant states. By analyzing the continuum spectrum via the exact WKB framework, we derive the S-matrix for the inverted Rosen--Morse potential and reveal its underlying complex-geometric features. Furthermore, we reinterpret the Aguilar--Balslev--Combes theorem, the foundation of CSM, from a geometric perspective, and discuss the physical significance of the Siegert boundary condition within a rigorously defined modified Hilbert space. Our analysis bridges scattering cross-sections and spectral theory, offering new geometric insights into quantum resonance and scattering phenomena.

## Key claim
The paper claims that exact WKB analysis, combined with complex scaling, provides a unified geometric framework for treating both continuum scattering states and resonant states in quantum mechanics, but this is demonstrated explicitly only for the inverted Rosen-Morse potential and the Airy-type approximation shows quantitative discrepancies with the exact solution at intermediate energies.

## Method
The authors employ exact WKB analysis (formal WKB series plus Borel resummation) to derive the S-matrix for the inverted Rosen-Morse potential by imposing outgoing boundary conditions and tracking Stokes phenomena across turning points. They then reinterpret the Aguilar-Balslev-Combes theorem from a geometric perspective by showing that complex scaling rotates the continuum spectrum by 2θ while leaving bound and resonant energies invariant, and that the Siegert boundary condition corresponds to requiring the subdominant WKB solution to be dominant at large imaginary coordinate. The transmission coefficient is computed using an Airy-type local model near simple turning points and compared with the exact hypergeometric solution.

## Key numbers
- The transmission coefficient ratio |T_Airy|/|T_exact| is shown as a function of k, with k=0 and k=√2 corresponding to E=0 and E=U_0=1 respectively, where all parameters except E are set to unity.
- The complex scaling rotation angle θ is taken in the range 0 < θ < π/4.
- The resonance energy is expressed as E = E_R - (i/2)Γ with Γ > 0 the decay width.
- The inverted Rosen-Morse potential is given by U_0/cosh²(βx) with U_0 > 0, and the parameter s = (1/2)(-1 + √(1 - 8mU_0/(β²ℏ²))).

## Context
This work extends the exact WKB quantization of resonant states developed by Morikawa and Ogawa (2025) to include continuum scattering states, providing a geometric reinterpretation of the Aguilar-Balslev-Combes theorem (1971) that underlies the complex scaling method. The approach bridges scattering cross-sections and spectral theory, and the authors note that the Airy-type approximation shows discrepancies with the exact solution (Landau, 1991) that could be improved by using a Weber-type (parabolic cylinder) local model. The framework enables a unified treatment of bound, resonant, and continuum spectra within a modified Hilbert space, and the authors draw an equivalence with the Feshbach projection formalism (1958, 1962) where the outgoing boundary condition implements the same radiation condition as the effective Hamiltonian approach.
