# Spin and pseudospin symmetries of the Dirac equation with confining central potentials

> We derive the node structure of the radial functions which are solutions of the Dirac equation with scalar $S$ and vector $V$ confining central potentials, in the conditions of exact spin or pseudospin symmetry, i.e., when one has $V=\pm S+C$, where $C$ is a constant. We show that the node structure for exact spin symmetry is the same as the one for central potentials which go to zero at infinity but for exact pseudospin symmetry the structure is reversed. We obtain the important result that it is possible to have positive energy bound solutions in exact pseudospin symmetry conditions for confining potentials of any shape, including naturally those used in hadron physics, from nuclear to quark models. Since this does not happen for potentials going to zero at large distances, used in nuclear relativistic mean-field potentials or in the atomic nucleus, this shows the decisive importance of the asymptotic behavior of the scalar and vector central potentials on the onset of pseudospin symmetry and on the node structure of the radial functions. Finally, we show that these results are still valid for negative energy bound solutions for anti-fermions.

## Key claim

For confining scalar and vector potentials that go to positive infinity at large distances, positive-energy bound solutions exist under exact pseudospin symmetry conditions for *any* potential shape, contrary to the well-known impossibility for potentials that go to zero at infinity. This result holds independently of the specific radial form of the confining potentials, including harmonic oscillator and linear (Cornell-type) potentials used in hadron physics.

## Method

The authors solve the time-independent Dirac equation with central scalar potential S and vector potential V under the exact symmetry conditions V = ±S + C (with C constant). They analyze the node structure of the upper and lower radial wavefunctions g_κ(r) and f_κ̃(r) by examining their asymptotic behavior at the origin and at infinity, using the product g_κ f_κ̃ and its derivative to determine the relative number of nodes. The analysis extends to anti-fermion bound states via charge conjugation transformations.

## Key numbers

- For spin symmetry (Δ = V - S = 0) with confining potentials: when κ > 0, n_f = n_g + 1; when κ < 0, n_f = n_g (same node structure as for potentials going to zero at infinity).
- For pseudospin symmetry (Σ = V + S = 0) with confining potentials: when κ < 0, n_f = n_g - 1; when κ > 0, n_f = n_g (reversed structure relative to spin symmetry).
- For confining potentials with asymptotic form r^a (a > 0), the radial functions behave asymptotically as exp(-λ r^(a+1)) when both C and D are nonzero, or as exp(-λ r^(a/2+1)) when either C or D is zero (spin or pseudospin symmetry conditions).
- For harmonic oscillator potentials (a = 2) in spin or pseudospin symmetry conditions, the radial functions behave as Gaussians.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Leviatan and Ginocchio (2005) on node structures for potentials vanishing at infinity to the case of confining potentials that diverge at large distances. It generalizes earlier results by Chen and Meng (2004) and the harmonic oscillator studies (2004, 2006) which showed bound solutions exist for pseudospin symmetry with harmonic oscillator potentials. The paper contradicts the established result from Zhou (2003), Pedro (2008), and others that pseudospin symmetry cannot be realized for positive-energy solutions with potentials going to zero at infinity. This enables application to Cornell potentials in particle physics and the Killingbeck potential, as recently studied by Hamzavi (2012) and Benito (2012), where exact pseudospin symmetry can be realized in confining systems.
