# Frame dependence of 3He transverse (e,e') response functions at intermediate momentum transfers

> The transverse electron scattering response function of 3He was recently studied by us in the quasi-elastic peak region for momentum transfers q between 500 and 700 MeV/c. Those results, obtained using the Active Nucleon Breit frame (ANB), are here supplemented by calculations in the laboratory, Breit and ANB frames using the two-fragment model discussed in our earlier work on the frame dependence of the the longitudinal response function R_L(q,omega). We find relatively frame independent results and good agreement with experiment especially for the lower momentum transfers. This agreement occurs when we neglect an omega-dependent piece of the one-body current relativistic correction. An inclusion of this term leads however to a rather pronounced frame dependence at q=700 MeV/c. A discussion of this term is given here. This report also includes a correction to our previous ANB results for R_T(q,omega).

# Frame dependence of 3He transverse (e,e') response functions at intermediate momentum transfers

## Key claim

The transverse response function of 3He shows relatively mild frame dependence (7-10% variation in peak height across frames) when the omega-dependent piece of the one-body current relativistic correction is neglected, but inclusion of this term leads to a pronounced frame dependence at q=700 MeV/c, with the ANB frame giving the smallest relativistic corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the transverse electron scattering response function R_T(q,omega) for 3He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique with AV18 NN plus Urbana IX NNN potentials, including lowest-order relativistic corrections to the one-body current and consistent two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC). Calculations are performed in the laboratory, Breit, and Active Nucleon Breit (ANB) frames using the two-fragment model, which assumes quasi-elastic knockout of a nucleon with relativistically correct kinematics while retaining full three-nucleon dynamics via the LIT method.

## Key numbers

- At q=700 MeV/c, the difference between non-relativistic and relativistic kinetic energy for a single knocked-out nucleon is Delta T_1b = 29 MeV
- For the two-fragment model at q=700 MeV/c, the kinetic energy difference between relativistic and non-relativistic kinematics for the 1-2 pair varies between 16 MeV (p and q orthogonal) and 29 MeV (p and q parallel)
- Non-energy weighted sum rule satisfaction: 98.4% at q=500 MeV/c, 98.5% at q=600 MeV/c, and 98.0% at q=700 MeV/c
- ANB frame peak height increase from two-fragment model: 3.3% at q=500 MeV/c, 4.6% at q=600 MeV/c, and 6.1% at q=700 MeV/c
- Frame dependence in peak height across lab, Breit, and ANB frames: 7% at q=500 MeV/c and 10% at q=700 MeV/c
- Reduction of R_T due to j_omega in lab frame: 37% at q=700 MeV/c
- Reduction of R_T due to j_omega in ANB frame: 5% at q=500 MeV/c, 9% at q=600 MeV/c, and 16% at q=700 MeV/c
- Convergence achieved by final state multipolarity J_f=35/2 at q=700 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the longitudinal response function R_L(q,omega) frame dependence (Efros et al., 2005) to the transverse channel, and corrects earlier ANB results for R_T (Efros et al., 2010). The two-fragment model, previously applied to the longitudinal response, is adapted for the transverse case including MEC contributions. The results enable future improvements by incorporating boost corrections, similar to approaches used in deuteron electrodisintegration (Bosted and Audet, 1980; Raskin and Donnelly, 1989).
