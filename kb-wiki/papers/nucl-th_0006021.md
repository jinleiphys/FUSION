# Anomalous Multiplicity Fluctuations from Phase Transitions in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations and correlations between particles produced in relativistic nuclear collisions are studied. The fluctuations in positive, negative, total and net charge are closely related through correlations. In the event of a phase transitions to a quark-gluon plasma, fluctuations in total and net charge can be enhanced and reduced respectively which, however, is very sensitive to the acceptance and centrality. If the colliding system experiences strong density fluctuations due, e.g., to droplet formation in a first-order phase transition, all fluctuations can be enhanced substantially. The importance of fluctuations and correlations is exemplified by event-by-event measurement of the multiplicities of $J/Ψ$'s and charged particles since these observables should anti-correlate in the presence of co-mover or anomalous absorption.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations in total and net charge can be enhanced or reduced respectively if a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma occurs, but these effects are very sensitive to the experimental acceptance and centrality; if the system experiences strong density fluctuations from droplet formation in a first-order phase transition, all fluctuations can be enhanced substantially.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for event-by-event fluctuations in positive, negative, total, and net charge multiplicities, incorporating correlations from resonance decays, thermal statistics, and total charge conservation. They then compute these fluctuations for a hadron gas, a quark-gluon plasma, and a scenario with droplet formation, and model the acceptance dependence using the measured charged particle rapidity distribution and a diffusion parameter. Finally, they propose a correlation measurement between J/psi and charged particle multiplicities to distinguish co-mover absorption from direct Glauber absorption.

## Key numbers

- In a thermal hadron gas at T=170 MeV, net charge fluctuation omega_Q = 0.70 (Jeon and Koch, 1999) and omega_Q = 0.60 (Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller, 2000).
- In a two-flavor QGP without entropy correction, omega_Q = 0.18 (Jeon and Koch, 2000); with entropy correction, omega_Q = 0.33 (Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller, 2000).
- Lattice results give omega_Q = 0.25 for the QGP.
- In pp collisions, total charge fluctuation omega_Nch = 2.0 at SPS, 6.0 at RHIC, and 10.0 at LHC (Heiselberg, 2001).
- Massless bosons have omega_B = 1.37 and massless fermions have omega_F = 0.91, independent of temperature.
- Pions at T = m_pi have omega_pi = 1.11; rho mesons at T = m_pi have omega_rho = 1.01.
- In a droplet scenario, omega_QGP can be as large as 10^2 to 10^3.
- A sample of 10^4 to 10^5 J/psi events would determine the absorption coefficient gamma with an accuracy of +/- 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies of net charge fluctuations in a QGP by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2000), and on the droplet-formation scenario of Baym and Heiselberg (1999). The analysis provides a systematic treatment of acceptance and diffusion effects that must be understood before anomalous fluctuations can be attributed to QGP formation. The proposed J/psi-charged particle anticorrelation offers a new observable to distinguish co-mover absorption from direct Glauber absorption, complementing elliptic flow measurements of Heiselberg and Mattiello (1999).
