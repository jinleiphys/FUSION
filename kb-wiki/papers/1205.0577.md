# Beyond mean-field calculation for pairing correlation

> The recently proposed Symmetry-Conserving Energy Density Functional approach [G. Hupin, D. Lacroix and M. Bender, Phys. Rev. C84, 014309 (2011)] is applied to perform Variation After Projection onto good particle number using Skyrme interaction, including density dependent terms. We present a systematic study of the Kr and Sn isotopic chains. This approach leads to non-zero pairing in magic nuclei and a global enhancement of the pairing gap compared to the original theory that breaks the particle number symmetry. The need to consistently readjust the pairing effective interaction strength is discussed.

## Key claim

The Symmetry-Conserving Energy Density Functional (SC-EDF) approach, when applied with Variation After Projection (VAP) onto good particle number using Skyrme interactions including density-dependent terms, systematically enhances pairing gaps compared to symmetry-breaking BCS calculations, but the total binding energy gain is partially compensated by a reorganization of the mean field; consequently, the pairing interaction strength must be readjusted when going beyond mean field.

## Method

The authors performed Variation After Projection (VAP) onto good particle number using the SC-EDF formalism of Hupin, Lacroix and Bender (2011), with the SLy4 interaction in the mean-field channel and a density-dependent surface pairing interaction. The EV8 code was updated to minimize the SC-EDF functional by solving mean-field equations via imaginary time step and optimizing occupation probabilities by sequential quadratic programming, using an approximation that factorizes the two-body density matrix of the projected state. Systematic calculations were carried out for the Kr and Sn isotopic chains, including quadrupole deformation constraints.

## Key numbers

- For mid-shell and simply magic nuclei, the energy gain (shift) between SC-EDF and BCS potential energy curves is between 1 and 2 MeV; for the doubly magic nucleus 132Sn, the gain is more than 0.5 MeV.
- In the proton gap of Sn isotopes, SC-EDF yields a mean gap of approximately 0.7 MeV at shell closures, compared to zero MeV at the BCS level.
- In the Kr isotopic chain at the N=50 shell closure, the SC-EDF pairing gap enhancement reaches approximately 1 MeV, while in mid-shell nuclei the enhancement is of order 0.3-0.5 MeV.
- With a reduced pairing strength V0 = 1100 MeV (compared to the original V0 = 1250 MeV), SC-EDF reproduces the original BCS pairing gaps in open-shell nuclei; binding energies for selected Kr isotopes (N=46-54) range from -716.34 to -773.18 MeV in VAP versus -715.64 to -772.3 MeV in BCS.

## Context

This work extends the SC-EDF approach proposed by Hupin, Lacroix and Bender (2011) from Projection After Variation (PAV) to full Variation After Projection (VAP), enabling self-consistent treatment of pairing correlations with particle number restoration. The results contrast with standard BCS/HFB approaches that exhibit the well-known BCS threshold anomaly (sudden disappearance of pairing at shell closures), and the method avoids the pathologies observed when using density-dependent interactions in configuration mixing calculations as reported by Dobaczewski et al. (2007) and Duguet et al. (2009). The study demonstrates that a complete readjustment of both mean-field and pairing channel parameters at the VAP level is needed for future predictive EDF calculations.
