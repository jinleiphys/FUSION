# Beam Fragmentation in Heavy Ion Collisions and its implication for RHIC triggers at low s

> We show that with a realistic treatment of spectator momentum distributions the RHIC detector trigger sensitivity is high even when RHIC is run below injection energies. In particular, a problem region with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=10 to 60 or 80 GeV assuming a simple Fermi step model is not found when using a more realistic one. We argue also that production of fast nucleons (with momenta $> p_F$ in the nucleus rest frame) provides complementary information about the collision process.

## Key claim

Using a realistic spectator momentum distribution that includes a hard component from short-range correlations eliminates the previously claimed problem region at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 10 to 60 or 80 GeV where RHIC trigger sensitivity was thought to be low under a simple Fermi step model.

## Method

The authors calculated the acceptance of the PHENIX Beam-Beam Counters (BBC, covering \(\eta\) = 3.1 to 3.9) for spectator protons using three different momentum distributions: Thomas-Fermi, Feshbach-Huang, and a Frankfurt-Strikman distribution with a short-range correlation (SRC) component. They then combined these single-proton acceptances with NA49 measured spectator proton multiplicities as a function of impact parameter to compute the BBC trigger efficiency at low beam energies.

## Key numbers

- The probability of short-range correlations for heavy nuclei is \(\sim 25\%\) (integral of momentum distribution for \(p \ge p_0 = 300\) MeV/c)
- The hard component of the momentum distribution follows \(n(p) \propto \exp(-\lambda p)\) with \(\lambda \sim 5.6\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for \(p \ge p_0\)
- Fermi momentum \(p_F\) = 270 MeV used for heavy nuclei in the Thomas-Fermi distribution
- The r.m.s. longitudinal momentum spread from Fermi smearing is 120 MeV/c
- The rapidity spread from longitudinal motion is \(\pm 0.12\) and is considered negligible
- The BBC Cerenkov response is fully efficient above approximately 1.5 GeV proton momentum
- At beam momentum \(p_{beam}\) = 6 GeV/u, the acceptance for a single proton using the Fermi distribution is approximately 0.1, giving trigger efficiency \(\varepsilon = 0.5\) for impact parameter \(b = 2\) fm and \(\varepsilon = 0.9\) for \(b = 8\) fm
- The number of nucleons near the surface facing the collision volume is estimated as \(\pi R_A^2 \rho_0 \sim 25\) to 30 nucleons, about 1/4 of spectator nucleons
- The overall trigger efficiency combining ZDC and BBC gives full coverage over the beam energy range from 2 to 100 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by the PHENIX collaboration using Thomas-Fermi and Feshbach-Huang (Feshbach, Huang) distributions for the Zero Degree Calorimeter efficiency at low \(\sqrt{s}\), and incorporates the short-range correlation model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) which was confirmed by experiments at JLAB, BNL AGS, and elsewhere. The results enable RHIC experiments to plan low-energy runs exploring the QCD phase transition critical point (\(\sqrt{s} \sim 5\) to 50 GeV/u) with confidence in trigger performance, and suggest that studying correlations between the reaction plane and proton emission direction could provide a novel probe of heavy ion collision dynamics.
