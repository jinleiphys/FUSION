# Perturbative Gluon Shadowing in Heavy Nuclei

> We study how much gluon shadowing can be perturbatively generated through the modified QCD evolution in heavy nuclei. The evolution of small-$x$ gluons is investigated within the semiclassical approximation. The method of characteristics is used to evaluate the shadowed distributions in low-$Q$ and small-$x$ region. In solving the modified evolution equation, we model in simultaneously fusions from independent constituents and from the same constituent, both in a proton and in a large loosely bound nucleus of $A\sim 200$. In addition to the actual distributions at small $x$, we study the ratios of the distributions at an initial scale $Q_0 = 2$ GeV, and show that a strong nuclear shadowing can follow from the modified QCD evolution.

## Key claim
A strong nuclear shadowing of gluons (approximately 50% depletion at small x) can be generated perturbatively through modified QCD evolution in heavy nuclei, but the result carries a roughly 20% uncertainty from the choice of the boundary x0^A between 0.05 and 0.1, and the saturation of the nuclear shadowing ratio actually reflects the behavior of gluons in a proton rather than in the nucleus itself.

## Method
The authors solved the modified QCD evolution equation for gluon distributions at small x using the semiclassical approximation and the method of characteristics, avoiding the region where the evolution equation itself becomes invalid. They modeled both independent gluon fusions (from different constituents) and non-independent fusions (from the same constituent) simultaneously, for a proton and for a nucleus with A ~ 200. The initial conditions at x0 = 0.01 for a proton and x0^A = 0.05–0.1 for a nucleus were taken from the CTEQ gluon distribution, and the evolution was terminated at Q0 = 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For a proton, the ratio of non-independent to independent fusion strength is λ_II/λ_I ≈ 7.6; for a nucleus of A ~ 200, the ratio is λ_II^A/λ_I^A ≈ 1.0.
- The nuclear gluon distribution at Q0 = 2 GeV shows approximately 50% shadowing (depletion) at small x relative to the nucleon gluon distribution, with a ~20% uncertainty from varying x0^A between 0.05 and 0.1.
- Momentum conservation requires an antishadowing enhancement of about 10% in the region x > x0^A for A ~ 200, with the normalization factor a_A typically ~10%.
- The boundary for the onset of gluon recombination in a nucleus is x0^A ~ 0.05–0.1, compared to x0 ~ 0.01 for a proton.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD approach to gluon shadowing from protons (Collins and Kwiecinski, 1990) to heavy nuclei, using the modified evolution equations of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1981) and Mueller and Qiu (1986). It provides a quantitative, perturbatively-based estimate of nuclear gluon shadowing at Q0 = 2 GeV, which had previously been treated with large theoretical uncertainties. The results enable more precise predictions for semihard processes in heavy ion collisions, such as minijet production, heavy quark production, and total transverse energy, particularly at collider energies in the TeV range where x values below 10^-3 are probed.
