# First Order Phase Transitions in Neutron star Matter: Droplets and Coherent Neutrino Scattering

> A first order phase transition at high baryon density implies that a mixed phase can occupy a significant region of the interior of a neutron star. In this article we investigate the effect of a droplet phase on neutrino transport inside the core. Two specific scenarios of the phase transition are examined, one having a kaon condensate and the other having quark matter in the high density phase. The coherent scattering of neutrinos off the droplets greatly increases the neutrino opacity of the mixed phase. We comment on how the existence of such a phase will affect a supernova neutrino signal.

## Key claim

A first-order phase transition in neutron star matter, leading to a mixed phase with droplets of kaon-condensed or quark matter, can reduce the neutrino mean free path by one to two orders of magnitude compared to uniform neutron matter, but this reduction is energy-dependent and moderated by inter-droplet correlations and form-factor effects.

## Method

The authors model two specific first-order phase transitions: kaon condensation and the quark-hadron transition. For kaon condensation, they use a relativistic mean-field model with meson fields coupled to baryons and kaons, solving the kaon field equation in the Thomas-Fermi approximation within a Wigner-Seitz cell. For the quark-hadron transition, they employ a bag model with a free Fermi gas for quarks and a relativistic mean-field description for nucleons. In both cases, they compute the weak charge density and form factor of the droplets, then calculate the neutrino-droplet scattering cross section and transport mean free path, accounting for inter-droplet correlations by subtracting a uniform background weak charge density.

## Key numbers

* For the kaon condensate scenario at baryon density \(n_B = 0.54\) fm\(^{-3}\), the droplet weak charge is \(N_W \simeq 700\) and the droplet radius is in the range 5-15 fm.
* For the kaon condensate scenario at \(T = 10\) MeV, the energy-averaged neutrino mean free path \(\langle \lambda_E \rangle\) is reduced by a factor of \(\sim 20\) compared to pure neutron matter; at \(T = 30\) MeV the reduction factor is \(\sim 9\).
* For the quark-hadron transition scenario at baryon density \(n_B = 0.7\) fm\(^{-3}\), the droplet weak charge is \(N_W \simeq 860\), the droplet radius is \(r_d = 5\) fm, and the Wigner-Seitz cell radius is \(R_W \sim 11\) fm.
* For the quark-hadron transition scenario at \(T = 10\) MeV, the energy-averaged neutrino mean free path \(\langle \lambda_E \rangle\) is reduced by a factor of \(\sim 10\) compared to pure neutron matter; at \(T = 30\) MeV the reduction factor is \(\sim 17\).
* The Fermi weak coupling constant used is \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends studies of first-order phase transitions in neutron star matter, specifically kaon condensation (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Glendenning and Schaffner, 1998) and the quark-hadron transition (Heiselberg et al., 1993), by focusing on the neutrino opacity of the resulting mixed phase. The authors show that coherent neutrino scattering off droplets greatly increases opacity, which could significantly alter the temporal characteristics of the supernova neutrino signal. This enables future detailed studies of diffusive neutrino transport in models with a droplet phase, potentially linking observable supernova neutrino features to the phases of matter at high density.
