# Kaon electromagnetic form factors in dispersion theory

> The electromagnetic form factors of charged and neutral kaons are strongly constrained by their low-energy singularities, in the isovector part from two-pion intermediate states and in the isoscalar contribution in terms of $ω$ and $ϕ$ residues. The former can be predicted using the respective $ππ\to\bar K K$ partial-wave amplitude and the pion electromagnetic form factor, while the latter parameters need to be determined from electromagnetic reactions involving kaons. We present a global analysis of time- and spacelike data that implements all of these constraints. The results enable manifold applications: kaon charge radii, elastic contributions to the kaon electromagnetic self energies and corrections to Dashen's theorem, kaon boxes in hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) scattering, and the $ϕ$ region in hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP). Our main results are: $\langle r^2\rangle_\text{c}=0.359(3)\,\text{fm}^2$, $\langle r^2\rangle_\text{n}=-0.060(4)\,\text{fm}^2$ for the charged and neutral radii, $ε=0.63(40)$ for the elastic contribution to the violation of Dashen's theorem, $a_μ^{K\text{-box}}=-0.48(1)\times 10^{-11}$ for the charged kaon box in HLbL scattering, and $a_μ^\text{HVP}[K^+K^-, \leq 1.05\,\text{GeV}]=184.5(2.0)\times 10^{-11}$, $a_μ^\text{HVP}[K_SK_L, \leq 1.05\,\text{GeV}]=118.3(1.5)\times 10^{-11}$ for the HVP integrals around the $ϕ$ resonance. The global fit to $\bar K K$ gives $\bar M_ϕ=1019.479(5)\,\text{MeV}$, $\bar Γ_ϕ=4.207(8)\,\text{MeV}$ for the $ϕ$ resonance parameters including vacuum-polarization effects.

### Key claim
The paper presents a global dispersion-theoretical analysis of kaon electromagnetic form factors that simultaneously constrains isovector and isoscalar components, but the isovector part is predicted from pion-pion intermediate states while the isoscalar part requires free parameters for omega and phi residues determined from fits to data; the results depend on the choice of input for the pion-pion to kaon-kaon partial-wave amplitude, with the unconstrained fit to data (UFD) preferred over the constrained fit (CFD) for better agreement with tau decay data.

### Method
The isovector kaon form factor is predicted using an unsubtracted dispersion integral over two-pion intermediate states, combining the pion electromagnetic form factor with the pi-pi to K-Kbar P-wave amplitude from Ref. Pelaez (2020). The isoscalar part is parameterized as a sum of vector-meson-dominance (VMD) poles for omega, phi, and an effective omega-prime, with residues and phi mass/width fitted to timelike e+e- to K+K- and e+e- to K_S K_L cross-section data and spacelike charged-kaon electron scattering data. The fits include vacuum-polarization removal, final-state radiation corrections, and account for binning in the BaBar data, with systematic uncertainties treated as 100% correlated per experiment and an iterative bias-removal procedure.

### Key numbers
- Charged kaon charge radius: <r^2>_c = 0.359(3) fm^2
- Neutral kaon charge radius: <r^2>_n = -0.060(4) fm^2
- Elastic contribution to violation of Dashen's theorem: epsilon = 0.63(40)
- Charged kaon box contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering: a_mu^{K-box} = -0.48(1) x 10^{-11}
- Hadronic vacuum polarization integral for K+K- up to 1.05 GeV: a_mu^{HVP}[K+K-, <= 1.05 GeV] = 184.5(2.0) x 10^{-11}
- Hadronic vacuum polarization integral for K_S K_L up to 1.05 GeV: a_mu^{HVP}[K_S K_L, <= 1.05 GeV] = 118.3(1.5) x 10^{-11}
- Phi resonance mass from global fit to K-Kbar: M_phi = 1019.479(5) MeV
- Phi resonance width from global fit to K-Kbar: Gamma_phi = 4.207(8) MeV

### Context
This work extends the dispersion-theoretical approach previously applied to the pion electromagnetic form factor (Colangelo 2018, Hoferichter 2016) to the kaon case, where both isovector and isoscalar components must be treated. The results enable improved calculations of kaon charge radii, corrections to Dashen's theorem (Dashen 1969), kaon-box contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering for the muon g-2, and hadronic vacuum polarization around the phi resonance, providing cross-checks for lattice QCD and alternative approaches such as vector meson dominance and Dyson-Schwinger equations.
