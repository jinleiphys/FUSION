# Emergence of rotational bands in ab initio no-core configuration interaction calculations of light nuclei

> The emergence of rotational bands is observed in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for the odd-mass Be isotopes (7<=A<=13) with the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, as evidenced by rotational patterns for excitation energies, quadrupole moments, and E2 transitions. Yrast and low-lying excited bands are found. The results demonstrate the possibility of well-developed rotational structure in NCCI calculations using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Rotational band structures emerge in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for odd-mass Be isotopes (7 ≤ A ≤ 13) using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the rotational patterns are clearest for relative properties (ratios of excitation energies or quadrupole matrix elements) rather than for individual observables, and the results are obtained at finite basis truncations (Nmax = 10 for 7Be down to Nmax = 7 for 13Be) without full convergence.

## Method

The authors performed no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations using the JISP16 two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction (without renormalization to the truncated space) in harmonic oscillator bases with truncations Nmax = 10 for 7Be to Nmax = 7 for 13Be, using oscillator parameters ℏΩ = 20–22.5 MeV near the variational minimum. The proton-neutron M-scheme code MFDn was used for the many-body calculations, and the Coulomb interaction was omitted to ensure exact isospin conservation. Excitation energies, quadrupole moments (both proton and neutron), and E2 transition strengths were computed and compared with rotational model predictions for candidate K = 1/2 and K = 3/2 bands.

## Key numbers

- For 13Be unnatural parity space, energies of J = 7/2, 9/2, 11/2, and 13/2 bandmembers agree with rotational values from the K = 1/2 formula to within 0.4 MeV, and a J = 15/2 bandmember is identifiable within 1.5 MeV of the rotational energy.
- For 13Be unnatural parity space, quadrupole moments of J = 3/2 and J = 5/2 bandmembers are in the expected rotational ratio to within 1.1% for protons and 0.4% for neutrons.
- For 11Be natural parity space, the K = 1/2 yrast band terminates at J = 7/2 based on energies; the lowest calculated J = 9/2 state lies 11 MeV too high to be an yrast bandmember.
- The 3/2 → 1/2 transitions in the K = 1/2 bands are enhanced by factors of approximately 1.1–17 relative to the typical Weisskopf single-particle estimate.
- Basis oscillator parameters were ℏΩ = 20–22.5 MeV, with truncations ranging from Nmax = 10 for 7Be to Nmax = 7 for 13Be.

## Context

This work extends prior ab initio NCCI calculations that had shown promising suggestions of collectivity in light nuclei, such as deformation and clustering in calculations by Wiringa (2000), Neff (2008), Cockrell (2012), Kanada-En'yo (2012), and Shimizu (2012). The results demonstrate that rotational band structures can be identified in NCCI calculations using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, enabling future quantitative prediction of collective rotational parameters (such as intrinsic quadrupole moments) for direct comparison with experiment, and motivating further analysis of the origin of rotation through symmetry decompositions (e.g., SU(3) or Sp(3,R) mechanisms) and density distributions.
