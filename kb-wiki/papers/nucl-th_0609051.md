# Gamma-soft Analog of the Confined Beta-soft Rotor Model

> A gamma-soft analog of the confined beta-soft (CBS) rotor model is developed, by using a gamma-independent displaced infinite well beta-potential in the Bohr Hamiltonian, for which exact separation of variables is possible. Level schemes interpolating between the E(5) critical point symmetry (with R(4/2)=E(4)/E(2)= 2.20) and the O(5) gamma-soft rotor (with R(4/2)=2.50) are obtained, exhibiting a crossover of excited 0+ bandheads which leads to agreement with the general trends of first excited 0+ states in this region and is observed experimentally in 128-Xe and 130-Xe.

## Key claim
A gamma-soft analog of the confined beta-soft rotor model is constructed, interpolating between the E(5) critical point symmetry (R4/2=2.20) and the O(5) gamma-soft rotor (R4/2=2.50) using a single free parameter r_beta = beta_m/beta_M, with the caveat that exact separation of variables is possible only because the potential is gamma-independent.

## Method
The Bohr Hamiltonian is solved with a gamma-independent displaced infinite square well potential in the beta coordinate, bounded between beta_m and beta_M. Exact separation of variables is achieved, yielding Bessel equation solutions of order nu = tau + 3/2, with boundary conditions at beta_m and beta_M leading to a discrete spectrum determined by roots of a determinant equation involving Bessel functions J_nu and Y_nu. Energy eigenvalues and B(E2) transition rates are computed analytically as functions of the ratio r_beta = beta_m/beta_M.

## Key numbers
- R4/2 = E(4_1+)/E(2_1+) = 2.20 for the E(5) limit (r_beta = 0)
- R4/2 = 2.50 for the O(5) gamma-soft rotor limit (r_beta -> 1)
- Crossover of excited 0+ bandheads occurs at r_beta_c = 0.171
- For 130-Xe, r_beta = 0.12 (fitted to experimental R4/2)
- For 128-Xe, r_beta = 0.21 (fitted to experimental R4/2)
- Experimental double-ratio Z(0+_3/2) = 52 +/- 30 for 130-Xe
- Experimental double-ratio Z(0+_3/2) = 0.32 +/- 0.17 for 128-Xe
- Contribution of 0+_(xi=2,tau=0) model state to observed 0+_2 state drops from 88(3)% in 130-Xe to 43(3)% in 128-Xe

## Context
This work extends the confined beta-soft (CBS) rotor model of Pietralla and Gorbachenko (2004), which used a displaced infinite well in the X(5) framework, to the gamma-soft (O(5)) case by using the E(5) framework instead. It provides an interpolation between the E(5) critical point symmetry of Iachello (2000) and the O(5) gamma-soft rotor, enabling description of nuclei in the R4/2 range 2.20-2.50. The model successfully reproduces the crossover of excited 0+ bandheads observed experimentally in 128-Xe and 130-Xe, and the results for 128-Xe agree with measured E2 transition data when simple two-state configuration mixing is included.
