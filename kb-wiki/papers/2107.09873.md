# Elastic and inelastic alpha transfer in the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering

> The elastic scattering cross section measured at energies $E\lesssim 10$ MeV/nucleon for some light heavy-ion systems having two identical cores like $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C exhibits an enhanced oscillatory pattern at the backward angles. Such a pattern is known to be due to the transfer of the valence nucleon or cluster between the two identical cores. In particular, the elastic $α$ transfer has been shown to originate directly from the core-exchange symmetry in the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering. Given the strong transition strength of the $2^+_1$ state of $^{12}$C and its large overlap with the $^{16}$O ground state, it is natural to expect a similar $α$ transfer process (or inelastic $α$ transfer) to take place in the inelastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering. The present work provides a realistic coupled channel description of the $α$ transfer in the inelastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering at low energies. Based on the results of the 4 coupled reaction-channels calculation, we show a significant contribution of the $α$ transfer to the inelastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering cross section at the backward angles. These results suggest that the explicit coupling to the $α$ transfer channels is crucial in the studies of the elastic and inelastic scattering of a nucleus-nucleus system with the core-exchange symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the inelastic alpha transfer process contributes significantly to the inelastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering cross section at backward angles, but this contribution becomes well separated from the purely inelastic scattering only at incident energies above 100 MeV, unlike at lower energies where the separation is less clear.

## Method
The authors performed a 4-channel coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculation using the code FRESCO, coupling the elastic scattering, inelastic scattering to the $2^+_1$ state of $^{12}$C, and both direct and indirect alpha transfer channels. The real optical potentials were calculated in the double-folding model using the CDM3Y3 interaction, with Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials, and the alpha spectroscopic factors were taken from large-scale shell-model calculations without adjustment.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies studied: $E_{\rm lab}=100$, 115.9, and 124 MeV for the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C system
- Alpha separation energy of $^{16}$O ground state: $E_\alpha({\rm g.s.})\approx 7.162$ MeV
- Excitation energy of $^{12}$C $2^+_1$ state: $E(^{12}{\rm C}^*)\approx 4.44$ MeV
- Spectroscopic factor for $\alpha + ^{12}{\rm C_{g.s.}}$ configuration: $S_\alpha\approx 0.794$ with $N=3$, $L=0$
- Spectroscopic factor for $\alpha + ^{12}{\rm C}_{2^+_1}$ configuration: $S^*_\alpha\approx 3.9$ with $N=2$, $L=2$
- Renormalization factor of real folded OP: $N_R=0.943$ at 100 MeV, $0.950$ at 115.9 and 124 MeV
- Total reaction cross sections: $\sigma_R=1305$ mb at 100 MeV, 1328 mb at 115.9 MeV, 1410 mb at 124 MeV
- Third Airy minimum (A3) identified at $\theta_{\rm c.m.}\approx 82^\circ$ at 100 MeV
- Second Airy minimum (A2) identified at $\theta_{\rm c.m.}\approx 98^\circ$ at 115.9 MeV and $88^\circ$ at 124 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous CRC studies of elastic alpha transfer in the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C system by Phuc et al. (2018) and Ferretti et al. (2019) to include inelastic alpha transfer to the $2^+_1$ state of $^{12}$C. It provides the first consistent CRC description of both elastic and inelastic scattering data measured by the Strasbourg group (Nicola et al., 2000; Szilner et al., 2006) at refractive energies above 100 MeV. The results enable future studies of alpha cluster structure in $^{16}$O and $^{12}$C using elastic and inelastic alpha transfer at higher incident energies, where the transfer contribution is well separated from the scattering contribution.
