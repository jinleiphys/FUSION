# On the three-body continuum spectrum of He-6

> In recent publications Cobis, Fedorov, and Jensen claim the existence of several previously unknown low-lying narrow resonances in He-6. I show that the distribution of the S-matrix poles corresponding to these states is unphysical. This casts doubt on the results of those works concerning resonances.

# On the three-body continuum spectrum of He-6

## Key claim

The S-matrix poles reported by Cobis, Fedorov, and Jensen (1997–1998) as low-lying narrow resonances in ⁶He are distributed in a clearly unphysical pattern, casting doubt on whether any of these poles correspond to real three-body resonances. The author argues that the claimed states are likely artifacts of an unsatisfactory analytic continuation, possibly due to mishandling of branch cuts arising from two-body subsystem resonances.

## Method

The author analyzes the complex-energy positions of the first four S-matrix poles found by Cobis et al. in the J^π = 1⁻ channel of ⁶He, using an α+n+n cluster model with a structureless α particle. The distribution of these poles is examined against known analytic properties of the three-body S-matrix, including the presence of branch cuts starting at the complex energies of α+n resonances. The author also performs test calculations using the complex scaling method with basis ranges extending beyond 100 fm to check for the claimed 1⁻ state.

## Key numbers

- The α+n resonance poles in the two-body subsystems lie at (0.77 − i0.32) MeV for J^π = 3/2⁻ and (1.97 − i2.61) MeV for J^π = 1/2⁻, as reported by Cobis et al.
- The author’s complex scaling calculations with basis ranges beyond 100 fm found no 1⁻ state, while the position of the known 2⁺ state remained stable.

## Context

This work directly challenges the series of papers by Cobis, Fedorov, and Jensen (1997–1998) claiming numerous previously unknown narrow resonances in ⁶He and ¹¹Li, including the debated soft dipole resonance. It extends earlier calculations by Csótó (1994) which found only known states in ⁶He, ⁶Li, and ⁶Be and found no evidence for a soft dipole resonance, though that method was numerically unstable for broad states. The paper also notes that Danilin et al. (1997) and Aoyama et al. (1995) found different sets of new states with very different widths, and that experiments by Jänecke et al. (1996) do not support new narrow states. The author suggests that a test of the Cobis method on the three-neutron system, where no 1/2⁺ state exists despite attractive interactions in all two-body subsystems (Glockle, 1978; Csótó et al., 1996), would be valuable.
