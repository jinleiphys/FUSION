# First-forbidden mirror beta-decays in A=17 mass region

> The first-forbidden beta-decay of 17Ne into the 'halo' state J=1/2+ of 17F presents one of the largest measured asymmetries for mirror beta-decay feeding bound final states. This asymmetry is studied in the framework of the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC). The spatial extent of single particle orbits is constrained by the proton capture cross-section 16O(p,gamma)17F calculated in SMEC . This allows to estimate the mirror symmetry breaking in 17F/17O and 17Ne/17N nuclei.

## Key claim
The large asymmetry in the mirror first-forbidden beta-decays of 17Ne and 17N is correlated with the increased radius of the weakly bound 1s1/2 single-particle orbit in 17F, but reproducing the observed ratio f+/f- requires an unrealistically large surface diffuseness parameter (a ~ 0.8 fm) that disagrees with proton capture data; when the capture data constraint is applied (a = 0.55 ± 0.05 fm), the remaining discrepancy in f- and f+/f- must be attributed to mirror symmetry breaking in the shell-model effective interaction, estimated at ~30% for the small wave-function component crucial to the decay.

## Method
The authors used the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) framework, which couples bound many-body states (Q subspace) to scattering states (P subspace) via a self-consistent iterative procedure that yields J-dependent potentials and radial wave functions. The Zuker-Buck-McGrory (ZBM) effective interaction in the (0p1/2, 1s1/2, 0d5/2) valence space was employed, with hybrid interactions ZBM-F and ZBM-O adjusted to reproduce experimental energy splittings in 17F and 17O. The first-forbidden beta-decay rates and the 16O(p,γ)17F capture cross-section were calculated using the same SMEC wave functions and input parameters, with the surface diffuseness a of the initial Woods-Saxon potential varied to constrain the radial extent of the 1s1/2 orbit.

## Key numbers
- Experimental ratio f+/f- = 24 ± 4 for the mirror decays 17Ne(β+)17F and 17N(β-)17O (Borge et al.).
- SM analysis assuming strict mirror symmetry yields f+/f- ≃ 9.6, much smaller than the experimental value.
- For a = 0.55 ± 0.05 fm (constrained by proton capture data), f+ agrees perfectly with experiment (f+_exp = 927 ± 95), but f- overshoots the experimental value (f-_exp = 44 ± 7) by a factor of ~1.4.
- The amplitude of the (1s1/2^2 0p1/2^-1) configuration in the ground state of 17Ne (ZBM-F) is ~30% larger than in 17N (ZBM-O*); the dominant 1p-0h component in the 1/2_1+ final state differs by ~12% between 17F and 17O.
- B(E2) for the 1/2_1+ → 5/2_1+ transition in 17F: SMEC/ZBM-F with a = 0.55 fm gives 74.85 e^2fm^4 (experimental: 64.92 e^2fm^4); in 17O: SMEC/ZBM-O gives 3 e^2fm^4, SMEC/ZBM-O* gives 3.2 e^2fm^4 (experimental: 6.2 e^2fm^4).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Borge et al. (1993), who first observed the large asymmetry and attributed it to differences in radial sizes of s1/2 orbits, and contrasts with Millener (1995), who explained the asymmetry via charge-dependent effects altering 1s1/2 occupancy in the initial states. The SMEC approach unifies structure and reaction data, using the 16O(p,γ)17F capture cross-section from Morlock et al. to constrain the radial wave functions, enabling a quantitative estimate of mirror symmetry breaking in the effective interaction for A=17 nuclei. The results enable future studies to disentangle configuration mixing (internal mixing) from exotic radial dependences (external mixing) in first-forbidden beta-decays, though the authors caution that such decays are particularly difficult to exploit as a direct source of information about unstable nuclei.
