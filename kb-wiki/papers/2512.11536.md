# Data-Driven Analysis for the Bottomonium Potential in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We present a data-driven analysis within a quantum evolutionary microscopic framework to constrain the in-medium bottomonium potential. In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, bottomonium bound states serve as invaluable probes of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) owing to their negligible production in the QGP phase. Meanwhile, their non-relativistic nature allows a straightforward theoretical description via effective field theories such as potential models. Recent lattice QCD calculations of the bottomonium interaction potential have yielded qualitatively distinct results. These discrepancies motivate a data-driven extraction of the potential based on heavy-ion experiments. In this work, we perform a Bayesian analysis to constrain the bottomonium interaction potential. The relationship between potential parameters and observables is established by numerically solving the non-relativistic time-dependent Schr"odinger equation. By comparing these simulations with experimental measurements, our Bayesian framework provides the effective potential that is readily testable in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bayesian analysis of bottomonium nuclear modification factors can constrain the in-medium complex potential, but the posterior distribution shows a bimodal structure with two distinct parameter sets comparably favored by the data, with the peak corresponding to weaker screening (a_m less than 1.5) occupying 85.6% of the total likelihood and being 3.04 times more probable than the alternative.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic time-dependent Schrodinger equation with a complex-valued, temperature-dependent potential to evolve bottomonium wave functions through a hydrodynamic background generated by the MUSIC package with s95p equation of state and constant shear viscosity eta/s = 0.08. They parameterize the real part of the potential using a Helmholtz free energy form with a Debye screening mass dressing factor a_m, and the imaginary part with four parameters (f_T, f_1, f_2, f_3) controlling temperature and radial dependence. A Gaussian process emulator trained on 200 Latin Hypercube design points is used as a surrogate for the Schrodinger solver, and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo sampling extracts posterior distributions for the five potential parameters by comparing simulated R_AA values for Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) states against ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass m_b = 4.62 GeV, reduced mass m_mu = m_b/2
- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: Coulomb coupling alpha = pi/12, linear coupling sigma = 0.2 GeV^2
- Vacuum masses: m_1S = 9.47 GeV, m_1P = 9.79 GeV
- Switching temperatures considered: T_d = {160, 180, 200} MeV
- Discretization: Delta t = 0.1 fm, Delta r = 0.03 fm, N = 300 radial points
- Parameter ranges: a_m in [0,3], f_T in [0.5,3], f_1 and f_2 in [0,1.0], f_3 in [1.5,3]
- In pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV: n = 2.5, mean square transverse momentum <p_T^2>_pp = 80.0 GeV^2
- For T_d = 160 MeV: the a_m less than 1.5 peak occupies 85.6% of total likelihood, the a_m greater than 1.5 peak occupies 14.4%; maximal posterior density ratio is 3.04 favoring the weaker screening peak
- Mock test target parameters: (a_m, f_T, f_1, f_2, f_3) = (1.5, 1.75, 0.5, 0.5, 2.25) with T_d = 160 MeV

## Context

This work addresses a discrepancy between two groups of lattice QCD calculations of the bottomonium potential: one group (Rothkopf 2011, Burnier 2014, Burnier 2015) finds strong screening in the real part with modest imaginary potential, while the other (Bala 2019, Bala 2021, Bazavov 2023, Ali 2025) finds mild screening but strong enhancement of the imaginary part. The data-driven Bayesian approach extends prior work on Bayesian inference for heavy-quark transport (Li 2019) and the JETSCAPE collaboration's Bayesian analyses (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2019). The extracted effective potential is readily testable in future experiments, and the bimodal posterior structure suggests that current data cannot uniquely discriminate between the two lattice QCD scenarios.
