# Pion Charge Exchange on Deuterium

> We investigate quantum corrections to a classical intranuclear cascade simulation of pion single charge exchange on the deuteron. In order to separate various effects the orders of scattering need to be distinguished and, to that end, we develop signals for each order of scattering corresponding to quasi-free conditions. Quantum corrections are evaluated for double scattering and are found to be large. Global agreement with the data is good.

## Key claim

The authors investigate quantum corrections to a classical intranuclear cascade simulation of pion single charge exchange on the deuteron, finding that quantum corrections for double scattering are large and that global agreement with experimental data is good, but the classical model's momentum distribution for the deuteron is unrealistic unless a specially constructed potential is used.

## Method

The authors developed an intranuclear cascade (INC) code for the reaction π⁺ + d → ppπ⁰, generating event files with charge exchange events that were then analyzed with threshold cuts (226 MeV/c momentum threshold, 5° counter extension, back-to-back condition cosΔφ ≤ -0.99). They distinguished single, double, triple, and higher-order scattering contributions using quasi-free kinematic predictions for peak positions at incident pion energies of 228 and 294 MeV. Quantum corrections for double scattering were calculated by evaluating the ratio of quantum to classical double scattering amplitudes, treating spin, space, and isospin operators with the deuteron's pure triplet initial state and accounting for the two possible orders of scattering and the isospin structure of the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- Incident pion energies used: 228 MeV and 294 MeV
- Momentum threshold applied to all counters: 226 MeV/c
- Counter angular extension: 5° in the θ sense
- Back-to-back condition: cosΔφ ≤ -0.99
- Beam disk radius for initial pion: 6.4 fm
- Form factor range parameter α: 4 fm⁻¹
- Double scattering peak positions at 294 MeV range from 75 MeV/c (45°,20° pair) to 573 MeV/c (20°,125° pair)
- Double scattering peak positions at 228 MeV range from 54 MeV/c (45°,20° pair) to 488 MeV/c (20°,125° pair)
- Triple scattering Jacobian peak positions at 294 MeV range from 38 MeV/c (45°,-20° pair) to 696 MeV/c (20°,-125° pair)
- Triple scattering Jacobian peak positions at 228 MeV range from 28 MeV/c (45°,-20° pair) to 598 MeV/c (20°,-125° pair)
- Varying absorption cross sections by a factor of two changed charge exchange cross sections by at most 20-30%

## Context

This work extends the classical intranuclear cascade approach originally suggested by Serber (1947) and developed in codes such as RQMD, the Liège code (Cugnon), the Valencia code, and the ARC code (Kahana). The authors previously applied a similar quantum correction for final state interactions in pion double charge exchange on ⁴He (Alqadi et al.). The present study uses data from Tacik et al. on π⁺ + d → ppπ⁰ with coincidence measurements at 228 and 294 MeV, enabling separation of multiple scattering orders and evaluation of quantum corrections to the classical cascade model. The results enable improved treatment of quantum effects in intranuclear cascade simulations for pion-nucleus reactions.
