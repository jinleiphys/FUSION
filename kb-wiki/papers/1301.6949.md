# Nucleon-Nucleon Chiral Two Pion Exchange potential vs Coarse grained interactions

> We analyse the interplay between nucleon-nucleon potentials deduced from chiral perturbation theory and a coarse grained representation of the short distance interactions by delta-shells potentials below a certain cut-off distance. While we find that the number of parameters is greatly reduced when Chiral Two Pion Exchange contributions are included we also observe that discerning the necessity of improvements on the interaction requires a detailed analysis of all error sources. Our points are best illustrated by computing deuteron static properties as well as electromagnetic form factors after error propagation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while including chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) interactions in nucleon-nucleon potentials significantly reduces the number of short-range parameters needed for fits to phase-shift data, discerning whether such improvements are genuinely necessary requires a detailed analysis of all error sources, including statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors constructed a nucleon-nucleon potential combining a short-range coarse-grained delta-shell representation (with spacing determined by the de Broglie wavelength below pion production threshold) with long-range contributions from one-pion exchange (OPE), chiral two-pion exchange at leading order (TPElo), and chiral two-pion exchange including spin-orbit terms (TPEso). They fitted this potential to a pseudodatabase of np phase shifts compiled from the 1993 Partial Wave Analysis and six high-quality potentials, varying the radial cut-off distance \(r_c\) between the short-range and long-range parts. Deuteron static properties and electromagnetic form factors were computed with error propagation from the pseudodata uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The delta-shell spacing is set by \(\Delta r = 1/\sqrt{M_N m_\pi} \sim 0.6\) fm.
- For OPE with \(r_c = 3.0\) fm: 51 parameters, \(\chi^2/\nu = 0.47\) for phase-shift fits.
- For TPEso with \(r_c = 1.8\) fm: 32 parameters, \(\chi^2/\nu = 0.59\) for phase-shift fits.
- For OPE with \(r_c = 1.8\) fm: 37 parameters, \(\chi^2/\nu = 2.14\) (unsatisfactory fit).
- Deuteron binding energy from all potentials: \(-2.2(2)\) to \(-2.2(4)\) MeV, compared to empirical value \(-2.2245(2)\) MeV.
- Deuteron D-state probability from all potentials: \(5.6(4)\) to \(5.7(2)\) percent, compared to empirical value \(5.67(4)\) percent.
- Deuteron quadrupole moment from all potentials: \(0.27(1)\) to \(0.27(2)\) fm\(^2\), compared to empirical value \(0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the coarse-grained delta-shell approach previously introduced by Aviles (1973) and developed by Navarro Perez et al. (2011, 2012) for nuclear binding energies and uncertainty propagation. It directly compares the standard OPE-based potentials (as in the high-quality fits by Stoks et al. (1993), Wiringa et al. (1994), Machleidt (2000), and Gross (2008)) with chiral TPE interactions from Kaiser (1997) and Rentmeester et al. (1999, 2003). The analysis enables a quantitative assessment of whether chiral TPE improvements are statistically significant for nuclear structure applications, particularly for evaluating the role of meson exchange currents in deuteron form factors.
