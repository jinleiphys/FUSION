# Macroscopic Features of Light Heavy-Ion Fission Reactions

> Global macroscopic features observed in the fully-damped binary processes in light di-nuclear systems, such as limiting angular momenta, mean total kinetic energies and energy thresholds for fusion-fission processes (''fission thresholds") are presented. Their deduced systematics are consistent with that obtained for heavier systems and follow a fusion-fission picture which can be described by a realistic rotating liquid drop model considering diffuse-surface and finite-nuclear-range effects.

## Key claim

The global macroscopic features of fully-damped binary processes in light di-nuclear systems (mass region A 竕､ 60) follow a fusion-fission picture consistent with heavier systems, but only when the rotating liquid drop model is modified to include diffuse-surface and finite-nuclear-range effects; the abstract's claim of consistency with heavier systems glosses over the fact that the standard liquid drop model fails for light nuclei without these corrections.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data from multiple light heavy-ion reactions (including systems such as ﾂｹ竅ｹF, ﾂｲ竅ｰ,ﾂｲﾂｹNe, ﾂｲ竅ｷ,ﾂｲ竅ｸAl, 竅ｴ竅ｷV, and 竅ｴ竅ｸCr) on limiting angular momenta, mean total kinetic energies (TKE) of symmetric fission fragments, and fission thresholds. They compared these data to predictions of the standard rotating liquid drop model (LDM) of Cohen, Plasil, and Swiatecki (1974) and to the finite-range rotating liquid drop model (FRLDM) of Sierk (1986), which incorporates a Yukawa-plus-exponential attractive potential and diffuse-surface effects. A new empirical formula for TKE as a function of Zﾂｲ/Aﾂｹ/ﾂｳ was derived by weighted least-squares fitting to the light-system data.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of fusion-fission processes from heavy systems down to very light di-nuclear systems (A 竕､ 60), building on prior statistical model descriptions by Sanders (1991) and Beck et al. (1992, 1993) and on the rotating liquid drop model of Cohen, Plasil, and Swiatecki (1974). It contradicts the applicability of the standard LDM for light nuclei without finite-range corrections, and supports the FRLDM of Sierk (1986) which was previously applied to heavier systems. The new TKE formula enables predictions for fission fragment energies in light systems where the Viola systematics (1985) fails, and the fission threshold systematics motivate further experimental studies in the Businaro-Gallone transition region and theoretical work on sub-threshold excitation functions.
