# $F_K / F_π$ from Möbius domain-wall fermions solved on gradient-flowed HISQ ensembles

> We report the results of a lattice quantum chromodynamics calculation of $F_K/F_π$ using Möbius domain-wall fermions computed on gradient-flowed $N_f=2+1+1$ highly-improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles. The calculation is performed with five values of the pion mass ranging from $130 \lesssim m_π\lesssim 400$ MeV, four lattice spacings of $a\sim 0.15, 0.12, 0.09$ and $0.06$ fm and multiple values of the lattice volume. The interpolation/extrapolation to the physical pion and kaon mass point, the continuum, and infinite volume limits are performed with a variety of different extrapolation functions utilizing both the relevant mixed-action effective field theory expressions as well as discretization-enhanced continuum chiral perturbation theory formulas. We find that the $a\sim0.06$ fm ensemble is helpful, but not necessary to achieve a subpercent determination of $F_K/F_π$. We also include an estimate of the strong isospin breaking corrections and arrive at a final result of $F_{K^\pm}/F_{π^\pm} = 1.1942(45)$ with all sources of statistical and systematic uncertainty included. This is consistent with the Flavour Lattice Averaging Group average value, providing an important benchmark for our lattice action. Combining our result with experimental measurements of the pion and kaon leptonic decays leads to a determination of $|V_{us}|/|V_{ud}| = 0.2311(10)$.

## Key claim
The authors report a lattice QCD calculation of \(F_K/F_\pi\) using Möbius domain-wall fermions on gradient-flowed HISQ ensembles, achieving a subpercent precision result of \(F_{K^\pm}/F_{\pi^\pm} = 1.1942(45)\) that is consistent with the FLAG average. The hedge is that the finest lattice spacing ensemble (a~0.06 fm) is helpful but not necessary for subpercent precision, and the result includes an estimate of strong isospin breaking corrections.

## Method
The calculation uses a mixed-action lattice QCD approach: Möbius domain-wall fermions for valence quarks on gradient-flowed \(N_f=2+1+1\) highly-improved staggered quark (HISQ) sea-quark ensembles generated by the MILC Collaboration. Pseudoscalar decay constants are extracted from two-point correlation functions using a Bayesian 4-state model, with ground-state energies and overlap factors determined from smeared- and point-sink correlators. The interpolation/extrapolation to the physical pion and kaon mass point, continuum, and infinite volume limits is performed using both mixed-action effective field theory expressions and discretization-enhanced continuum chiral perturbation theory formulas up to next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers
- Final result for the charged meson ratio including strong isospin breaking: \(F_{K^\pm}/F_{\pi^\pm} = 1.1942(45)\) (statistical and systematic uncertainties combined).
- Result in the isospin symmetric limit: \(F_K/F_\pi = 1.1964(44)\), with a breakdown of uncertainties: statistical (32), chiral extrapolation (12), continuum (20), volume (01), physical point (15), and model selection (12) in units of \(10^{-4}\).
- Combined with experimental leptonic decay data, this yields \(|V_{us}|/|V_{ud}| = 0.2311(10)\).
- The calculation uses five pion masses from \(130 \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 400\) MeV, four lattice spacings \(a \sim 0.15, 0.12, 0.09, 0.06\) fm, and multiple lattice volumes.

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD determination of \(F_K/F_\pi\) using a mixed-action approach (Möbius domain-wall valence on HISQ sea), providing a test of universality for \(N_f=2+1+1\) actions. The result is consistent with the FLAG average of 1.1932(19) from Aoki et al. (2019) and with prior HISQ calculations by Dowdall et al. (2013) and Bazavov et al. (2017), as well as twisted-mass results by Carrasco et al. (2014). This calculation enables improved precision in extracting \(|V_{us}|/|V_{ud}|\) and tests the control of systematic uncertainties for the MDWF-on-gradient-flowed-HISQ action, which is also used for neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements and the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\).
