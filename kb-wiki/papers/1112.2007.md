# Radiative corrections to anti-neutrino proton scattering at low energies

> For the low-energy anti-neutrino reaction, $\barν_e + p \to e^+ + n$, which is of great current interest in connection with on-going high-precision neutrino-oscillation experiments, we calculate the differential cross section in a model-independent effective field theory (EFT), taking into account radiative corrections of order $α$. In EFT, the short-distance radiative corrections are subsumed into well-defined low-energy constants the values of which can in principle be determined from the available neutron beta-decay data. In our low-energy EFT, the order-$α$ radiative corrections are considered to be of the same order as the nucleon recoil corrections, which include the "weak magnetism" contribution. These recoil corrections have been evaluated as well. We emphasize that EFT allows for a systematic evaluation of higher order corrections, providing estimates of theoretical uncertainties in our results.

## Key claim

The paper calculates radiative corrections of order α and nucleon-recoil corrections (including weak magnetism) for the inverse beta-decay reaction \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \to e^+ + n\) using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), finding that the \(m_N^{-1}\) recoil corrections differ between inverse beta-decay and neutron beta-decay, contrary to the assumption in earlier work by Vogel and Beacom (1999) that these corrections are common between the two processes.

## Method

The authors employed heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) with a \(\bar{Q}/\Lambda_{\chi}\) expansion scheme, where the small expansion parameter \(\bar{Q}/\Lambda_{\chi} \sim 10^{-3}\) is comparable to \(\alpha/(2\pi)\) and \(\bar{Q}/m_N\). They computed the differential cross section including virtual photon-loop diagrams, bremsstrahlung contributions, and counter-term amplitudes involving low-energy constants (LECs) that subsume short-distance radiative physics. The recoil corrections from the next-to-leading-order (NLO) Lagrangian were evaluated separately from kinematic phase-space corrections, and the results were compared with the Sirlin-Marciono (S-M) approach used by Fukugita and Kubota (2004) and with the neutron beta-decay calculation of Ando et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameters are all of order \(10^{-3}\): \(\bar{Q}/\Lambda_{\chi} \sim \alpha/(2\pi) \sim \bar{Q}/m_N \sim 10^{-3}\), where \(\Lambda_{\chi} \simeq 4\pi f_{\pi} \approx 1\) GeV and \(f_{\pi} = 92.4\) MeV.
- The nucleon isovector magnetic moment used is \(\mu_V = \mu_p - \mu_n = 4.706\).
- The axial coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.267\).
- The Fermi coupling constant is \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) and the CKM matrix element is \(|V_{ud}| = 0.97418 \pm 0.00027\).
- The incident anti-neutrino energy range considered is \(E_{\nu} \leq 10\) MeV for reactor anti-neutrinos.
- The outer radiative correction function \(\delta_{out}(\beta)\) contains the term \(3\ln(m_N/m_e) + 23/4\) plus velocity-dependent terms involving the Spence function and logarithms of \((1+\beta)/(1-\beta)\).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to neutron beta-decay by Ando et al. (2004) to the inverse beta-decay reaction relevant for reactor neutrino experiments measuring the mixing angle \(\theta_{13}\). It contradicts the assumption made by Vogel and Beacom (1999) that the \(m_N^{-1}\) recoil corrections are common between neutron beta-decay and inverse beta-decay, finding instead that the weak-magnetism contributions differ in sign between the two processes. The results enable model-independent calculations of the positron yield for the Double-Chooz, Daya Bay, and RENO experiments, with the low-energy constant \(e_V^R(\mu^2)\) determined from neutron beta-decay data providing the short-distance physics input.
