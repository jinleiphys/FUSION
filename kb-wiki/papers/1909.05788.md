# Role of event multiplicity on hadronic phase lifetime and QCD phase boundary in ultrarelativistic collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Hadronic resonances, having very short lifetime, like $\rm{K}^{*0}$, can act as useful probes to understand and estimate lifetime of hadronic phase in ultra-relativistic proton-proton, p--Pb and heavy-ion collisions. Resonances with relatively longer lifetime, like $ϕ$ meson, can serve as a tool to locate the QGP phase boundary. We estimate a lower limit of hadronic phase lifetime in Cu--Cu and Au--Au collisions at RHIC, and in pp, p--Pb and Pb--Pb collisions at different LHC collision energies. Also, we obtain the effective temperature of $ϕ$ meson using Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast-Wave function, which gives an insight to locate the QGP phase boundary. We observe that the hadronic phase lifetime strongly depends on final state charged-particle multiplicity, whereas the QGP phase and hence the QCD phase boundary shows a very weak multiplicity dependence. This suggests that the hadronisation from a QGP state starts at a similar temperature irrespective of charged-particle multiplicity, collision system and collision energy, while the endurance of hadronic phase is strongly dependent on final state charge-particle multiplicity, system size and collision energy.

# Role of event multiplicity on hadronic phase lifetime and QCD phase boundary in ultrarelativistic collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and CERN Large Hadron Collider

## Key claim

The hadronic phase lifetime (estimated as a lower limit using K*0 resonances) depends strongly on final-state charged-particle multiplicity, while the QGP phase boundary (probed via the effective temperature of phi mesons) shows very weak multiplicity dependence. This suggests hadronisation from QGP starts at a similar temperature regardless of multiplicity, system size, or collision energy, but the duration of the hadronic phase is strongly controlled by these factors.

## Method

The authors estimate a lower limit on hadronic phase lifetime using the ratio of K*0 to K mesons, assuming that suppression of this ratio from its chemical freeze-out value (taken as 0.33 from pp collisions) to its kinetic freeze-out value is due to re-scattering of decay daughters in the hadronic phase, with regeneration neglected. They apply the relation [K*0/K]_kinetic = [K*0/K]_chemical * exp(-Delta t / tau), where tau is the K*0 lifetime and Delta t includes the Lorentz factor from the mean pT of K*0. For the QGP phase boundary, they fit Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast-Wave functions to phi meson pT spectra up to 3 GeV/c to extract kinetic freeze-out temperature T_th and average radial flow <beta>, then compute effective temperature T_eff = T_th + (1/2) m <beta>^2, which is argued to preserve information from the QGP phase boundary due to the phi meson's small hadronic interaction cross-section.

## Key numbers

* K*0/K ratio at chemical freeze-out: 0.33 +/- 0.02, obtained from fitting pp collision data across energies with a zeroth-order polynomial.
* Hadronic phase lifetime in high-multiplicity pp collisions: approximately 2 fm/c.
* Hadronic phase lifetime in central Pb-Pb collisions: approximately 6 fm/c.
* Blast-Wave fits to phi meson pT spectra performed up to pT = 3 GeV/c; maximum deviation of fit does not exceed 10% for Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and high-multiplicity pp collisions.
* A threshold in charged-particle multiplicity around 10-20 is observed for pp collisions, where average radial flow shows a sudden increase and kinetic freeze-out temperature drops abruptly.
* The phi N cross-section is cited as 8-12 mb for photoproduction on proton and nuclear targets below 10 GeV, with only about 5% of phi mesons expected to re-scatter in the hadronic phase.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of resonance production and hadronic phase lifetime, notably using the method from Singha (2015), Adams (2004), and Acharya (2019) for K*0-based lifetime estimation. It builds on observations that K*0/K ratios show suppression in central heavy-ion collisions relative to pp (Abelev, 2014), while phi mesons do not show such suppression, consistent with the picture that phi mesons are minimally affected by hadronic re-scattering (Shor, 1984; Ko, 1993; Haglin, 1994). The finding that T_eff is nearly independent of multiplicity aligns with earlier reports that chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch is independent of final-state charged-particle multiplicity (Rath, 2019). The threshold multiplicity around 10-20 for pp collisions is supported by independent observations of multipartonic interactions becoming important (Thakur, 2017) and by thermodynamic limits where statistical ensembles converge (Sharma, 2018), as well as earlier predictions for a cross-over transition between charged-particle multiplicity densities of 6 to 24 (Campanini). The work enables future estimation of exact QGP lifetime as a function of multiplicity, which remains an open problem, and suggests that effective energy responsible for particle production may be the controlling factor (Mishra, 2014; Sarkisyan, 2015, 2016, 2018).
