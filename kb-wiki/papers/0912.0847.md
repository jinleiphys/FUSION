# Direct observation of the glue pairing the halo of the nucleus 11Li

> With the help of a unified description of the nuclear structure and of the direct reaction mechanism we show that a recent 1H(11Li,9Li)3H experiment provides, for the first time in nuclear physics, direct evidence of phonon mediated pairing.

## Key claim

By analyzing the reaction 1H(11Li,9Li)3H with a unified nuclear structure and direct reaction theory, the authors show that the experiment provides direct evidence of phonon-mediated pairing in the halo nucleus 11Li, specifically that the virtual exchange of collective vibrations (the quadrupole vibration of the 9Li core and the dipole pigmy resonance of 11Li) between the two halo neutrons has been forced to become a real final state observable in the two-neutron pickup process.

## Method

The authors performed a Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) analysis of the 1H(11Li,9Li)3H reaction, calculating the differential cross sections for populating the ground state (3/2-) and first excited state (1/2-; 2.69 MeV) of 9Li. They considered nine different reaction channels including multistep transfer (successive, simultaneous, and non-orthogonality terms), breakup channels, and inelastic channels, using wavefunctions from Barranco (2001) and software developed to handle continuum states up to 50 MeV excitation energy. The calculations used second-order perturbation theory to account for interference between different reaction paths, with transition matrix elements computed in the DWBA framework.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of 11Li is S2n ≈ 380 keV (from Bachelet (2008), Smith (2008), and Roger (2009)), while the calculated value from the model is 330 keV.
- The calculated integrated two-neutron transfer cross section to the 9Li ground state is 6.1 mb, compared to the experimental value of 5.7 ± 0.9 mb.
- The calculated integrated cross section to the 1/2- (2.69 MeV) first excited state of 9Li is 0.7 mb, compared to the experimental value of 1.0 ± 0.36 mb.
- When the quadrupole vibration coupling (β parameter) is artificially set to zero, the calculated cross section to the 1/2- state drops to 5 × 10-2 mb, demonstrating the dominance of the phonon-mediated pairing mechanism.
- The wavefunction coefficients for the halo neutron Cooper pair are α ≈ 0.7 (dipole phonon component) and β ≈ 0.1 (quadrupole phonon component), with the bare two-neutron configuration having amplitudes 0.45 for s1/2^2, 0.55 for p1/2^2, and 0.04 for d5/2^2.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Barranco (2001), who predicted that the two halo neutrons in 11Li attract each other primarily through the exchange of collective vibrations rather than the bare nuclear force. The analysis directly tests the predictions of Brink (2005) regarding medium polarization effects in nuclear superfluidity, and provides the first direct experimental evidence for phonon-mediated pairing in nuclei. This enables future studies of pair correlations in exotic nuclei, and the authors suggest that the same experimental setup could check predictions for one-particle pickup processes populating the same final states.
