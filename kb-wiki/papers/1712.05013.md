# Tensor Fermi liquid parameters in nuclear matter from chiral effective field theory

> We compute from chiral two- and three-body forces the complete quasiparticle interaction in symmetric nuclear matter up to twice nuclear matter saturation density. Second-order perturbative contributions that account for Pauli-blocking and medium polarization are included, allowing for an exploration of the full set of central and noncentral operator structures permitted by symmetries and the long-wavelength limit. At the Hartree-Fock level, the next-to-next-to-leading order three-nucleon force contributes to all noncentral interactions, and their strengths grow approximately linearly with the nucleon density up that of saturated nuclear matter. Three-body forces are shown to enhance the already strong proton-neutron effective tensor interaction, while the corresponding like-particle tensor force remains small. We also find a large isovector cross-vector interaction but small center-of-mass tensor interactions in the isoscalar and isovector channels. The convergence of the expansion of the noncentral quasiparticle interaction in Landau parameters and Legendre polynomials is studied in detail.

## Key claim

The authors compute the complete quasiparticle interaction in symmetric nuclear matter from chiral two- and three-body forces up to twice nuclear matter saturation density, finding that three-body forces enhance the already strong proton-neutron effective tensor interaction while the like-particle tensor force remains small; the results are subject to systematic uncertainties estimated from five different chiral nuclear force models and depend on the order in the chiral expansion and the momentum-space cutoff.

## Method

The quasiparticle interaction is derived as the second functional derivative of the ground-state energy with respect to occupation probabilities, computed up to second order in many-body perturbation theory from chiral two-body forces at N2LO and N3LO and three-body forces at N2LO. The noncentral components (relative-momentum tensor, center-of-mass tensor, and cross-vector interactions) are extracted from linear combinations of spin- and isospin-space matrix elements, and the Fermi liquid parameters are obtained by Legendre polynomial decomposition of the scalar functions. Second-order contributions include particle-particle, hole-hole, and particle-hole diagrams, with three-body forces incorporated through a density-dependent two-body force at second order.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the full quasiparticle interaction in neutron matter (Holt et al., 2013) to symmetric nuclear matter, and complements efforts to constrain nuclear energy density functionals from microscopic many-body theory (Pudliner et al., 1996; Brown et al., 2014; Roggero et al., 2015; Buraczynski et al., 2016; Rrapaj et al., 2016; Zhang et al., 2018). The results provide microscopic guidance for tensor forces in modern mean-field effective interactions and nuclear energy density functionals, addressing the ongoing debate about the role of tensor forces in describing single-particle energies (Otsuka et al., 2005, 2006), beta-decay half-lives (Minato et al., 2013), and spin-dependent collective excitations (Cao et al., 2009; Co et al., 2012). The finding of a large isovector cross-vector interaction, not previously studied in phenomenological mean-field modeling, opens the door for improved descriptions of neutrino transport in neutron stars and nuclear collective excitations.
