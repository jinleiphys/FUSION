# Bulk viscosity of two-color superconducting quark matter in neutron star mergers

> We study the bulk viscosity of moderately hot and dense, neutrino-transparent color superconducting quark matter arising from weak-interaction-driven direct URCA processes. The quark matter is modeled using the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model improved to account for vector and 't Hooft interactions as well as antisymmetric pairing among the red/green up and down quarks. The unpaired excitations are the strange quarks and the blue up or down quarks. We compute the relaxation rates associated with $d$ and $s$-quark decay and electron capture processes on $u$ quark for blue color. The resulting bulk viscosity for density oscillations in the 1--10\,kHz range shows a resonant peak at $T\sim 5\,\MeV$, and the damping time may drop below 10 ms. This is short enough to affect the postmerger evolution and is very similar to the damping predicted in nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of two-color superconducting (2SC) quark matter in neutron star merger conditions, arising from direct URCA processes on unpaired blue quarks, peaks at temperatures around 3-6 MeV and produces damping timescales that can drop below 10 ms, which is short enough to affect postmerger evolution. The abstract's claim that this damping is "very similar to the damping predicted in nuclear matter" is qualified by the paper's finding that at low temperatures (below about 4 MeV) the 2SC quark matter bulk viscosity takes intermediate values between two different nuclear matter models (DDME2 and NL3), and that the similarity is strongest at temperatures above 4 MeV.

## Method
The authors model 2SC quark matter using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector interactions, 't Hooft interactions, and diquark pairing among red/green up and down quarks, leaving blue quarks and strange quarks unpaired. They compute the relaxation rates for weak-interaction-driven direct URCA processes (d and s quark decay and electron capture on u quarks for blue color) and the nonleptonic process u+d ↔ u+s, then calculate the bulk viscosity coefficient ζ = (C²/A) × γ/(ω²+γ²) for density oscillations in the 1-10 kHz range, where γ is the β-relaxation rate and C²/A is a susceptibility factor from the equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The 2SC pairing gap varies in the range 130 ≤ Δ ≤ 150 MeV over baryon density range 0.6 ≤ n_B ≤ 1.14 fm⁻³
- The bulk viscosity peaks at temperatures T ≈ 3-4 MeV for oscillation frequency f = 1 kHz and T ≈ 5-6 MeV for f = 10 kHz
- The β-relaxation rate γ follows a power-law scaling γ ∝ T⁴·⁵ in the temperature range 1 ≤ T ≤ 10 MeV
- The damping timescale τ_ζ at its minimum ranges from a few milliseconds to a few hundred milliseconds
- At n_B/n_0 = 7, the maximum bulk viscosity is more than an order of magnitude smaller than at n_B/n_0 = 4
- The difference between Fermi momenta p_Fd - p_Fu - p_Fe is less than 1 MeV for densities 4 ≤ n_B/n_0 ≤ 7
- The s-URCA processes have a momentum mismatch p_Fu + p_Fe - p_Fs ≥ 70 MeV due to large s-quark mass
- The Cabibbo angle is θ_c = 13.04°, giving sin²θ_c = 0.05 for s-quark processes and cos²θ_c = 0.95 for d-quark processes

## Context
This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in cold quark matter (Sawyer 1989; Alford 2006) and in hot unpaired quark matter for neutron star mergers (Rojas 2024; Hernandez 2024) by incorporating color superconductivity at intermediate densities and nonzero temperatures. It complements the authors' recent study of relativistic npeμ matter (Alford 2023) and shows that 2SC quark matter's bulk viscous damping timescales are similar to those of nucleonic matter, making it difficult to distinguish these states via bulk viscous dissipation in the 1-10 MeV temperature range. The results enable more realistic dissipative hydrodynamical simulations of binary neutron star mergers that include quark matter phases.
