# Two- and three-particle nonflow contributions to the chiral magnetic effect measurement by spectator and participant planes in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Correlation measurements with respect to the spectator and participant planes in relativistic heavy ion collisions were proposed to extract the chiral magnetic effect (CME) from background dominated azimuthal correlators. This paper investigates the effects of two- and three-particle nonflow correlations on the extracted CME signal fraction, $f_{\text{CME}}$. It is found, guided by a multiphase transport (AMPT) model and the heavy ion jet interaction generator (HIJING) together with experimental data, that the nonflow effects amount to approximately $(4\pm5)$% and $(-5\pm3)$% without and with pseudorapidity gaps, respectively, in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}= 200 \text{ GeV}$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that nonflow correlations from two- and three-particle sources can produce a spurious chiral magnetic effect signal fraction of approximately (4±5)% without pseudorapidity gaps and (−5±3)% with pseudorapidity gaps in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, meaning the spectator/participant plane method is not entirely free from nonflow contamination as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (v1.25t4cu2/v2.25t4cu2 with string melting) and the HIJING model (v1.411) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, generating 377 million and 592 million minimum-bias events respectively. They calculated elliptic flow v2 and the charge-dependent three-particle correlator Δγ with respect to the spectator plane (approximated by the reaction plane) and the participant plane (approximated by the event plane from final-state hadrons), then extracted the CME signal fraction fCME using the spectator/participant plane method. The nonflow contributions were isolated by comparing results with and without pseudorapidity gaps between the particles of interest and the event plane particles, and by combining AMPT results (which contain flow and two-particle nonflow) with HIJING results (which contain three-particle nonflow from jets).

## Key numbers

- Nonflow effects on the extracted CME signal fraction fCME amount to approximately (4±5)% without pseudorapidity gaps and (−5±3)% with pseudorapidity gaps in 20-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- The two-particle nonflow parameter εnf (nonflow contribution to v2 squared) from AMPT is on the order of 10-20% depending on centrality, with values around 0.10-0.20
- The charge-dependent two-particle correlation strength ε2 from AMPT is approximately 0.3-0.4 in mid-central to central collisions
- The three-particle nonflow correlation strength ε3 from HIJING (N²C3*{EP}) shows only modest increase with centrality, with values significantly larger than the flow-induced contribution C3{RP} which is negligible in HIJING
- The geometry fluctuation factor a* = v2{RP}/v2*{EP} from AMPT is approximately 0.8 in mid-central collisions

## Context

This work extends the spectator/participant plane method developed by Xu et al. (2018, 2017) for extracting the chiral magnetic effect from background-dominated azimuthal correlators in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The method had been applied by the STAR Collaboration (Zhao et al., 2018, 2020) under the assumption of minimal nonflow contamination. This paper shows that two-particle nonflow (from resonance decays and similar sources) and three-particle nonflow (from di-jet correlations) produce opposing contributions to the extracted fCME that partially cancel, with the net effect depending on the relative magnitudes. The results enable more precise interpretation of existing CME measurements and provide guidance for future experimental analyses using pseudorapidity gaps to suppress nonflow.
