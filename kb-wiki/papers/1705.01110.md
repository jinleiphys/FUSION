# Multi moment cancellation of participant fluctuations - MMCP method

> We propose a new way to correct for finite centrality bin width effect i.e. participant fluctuations in fluctuation analysis in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The MMCP method allows to separate participant fluctuations and obtain fluctuations from one participant - a source - from a combination of the experimentally measured first four moments. The EPOS model is used for the numerical check of the MMCP for the net electric charge fluctuations in the forward rapidity region in Ar+Sc reactions at beam momentum 150 GeV/c. We show that using the existing methods - decreasing a centrality bin width, or using the Centrality Bin Width Correction procedure, one may still leave some residual participant fluctuations in the sample. Moreover, we show that the Centrality Bin Width Correction procedure may alter the fluctuation measures. The most important advantage of the MMCP is it's precision even when the amount of measured events does not allow to decrease the centrality bin width, or the experimental determination of participants is difficult, e.g. in collider experiments. Even for the largest centrality bin in the considered case, $c=0-20\%$, the relative error of the MMCP for the scaled variance of a source is below 2\%. It is especially important in determination of the base line of the fluctuations in the search for the QCD Critical Point and the signals of the QCD phase transition.

## Key claim

The MMCP method can extract the true fluctuations from a single participant (source) by algebraically combining the first four measured moments of a multiplicity distribution, under the condition that participant fluctuations are small (parameters α, |β|, |γ| ≪ 1) and that the normalized skewness of the source is much smaller than its normalized kurtosis (|δ| ≪ |ε|).

## Method

The authors derived analytical relations within the Independent Participant Model (IPM) that express the measured scaled variance, normalized skewness, and normalized kurtosis as functions of the corresponding source quantities plus participant fluctuation contributions. They then solved these equations under the assumption of small participant fluctuations to obtain closed-form expressions for the source fluctuations using only the measured first four cumulants. The method was tested on EPOS 1.99 simulations of Ar+Sc collisions at 150 GeV/c beam momentum, comparing the MMCP results to direct calculations of source fluctuations and to a reference with fixed participant number.

## Key numbers

- For the largest centrality bin c = 0–20%, the relative error of the MMCP for the scaled variance of a source is below 2%.
- The average number of particles from one source ⟨n_A⟩ ≈ 0.3, independent of centrality bin width for all δc studied.
- The scaled variance of a source ω_A ≈ 3.3, which is much larger than the participant scaled variance ω_P ≳ 0.5 for all centrality bins.
- The average number of participants ⟨N_P⟩ ranges from 42.82(1) for c = 0–20% to 57.68(2) for c = 0–0.2%.

## Context

This work extends the Independent Participant Model framework (Begun 2016) to provide a new correction method for participant fluctuations, which are a known background in fluctuation analyses searching for the QCD critical point. The MMCP method is compared to the Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) procedure used by STAR and ALICE Collaborations (Luo 2011), and is shown to be more precise even when statistics are insufficient to narrow the centrality bin. The method is particularly relevant for collider experiments where precise participant determination is difficult, and for cases where strongly intensive measures (SIM) may be invalid because different particle species do not share the same volume fluctuations.
