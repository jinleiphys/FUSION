# Signatures of the vortical quark-gluon plasma in hadron yields

> We investigate the hadron production from the vortical quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. Based on the quark-coalescence and statistical hadronization models, we show that total hadron yields summed over the spin components are enhanced by the local vorticity with quadratic dependence. The enhancement factor amounts to be a few percent and may be detectable within current experimental sensitivities. We also show that the effect is stronger for hadrons with larger spin, and thus propose a new signature of the local vorticity, which may be detected by the yield ratio of distinct hadron species having different spins such as $ϕ$ and $η'$. The vorticity dependence of hadron yields seems robust, with consistent predictions in both of the hadron production mechanisms for reasonable values of the vorticity strength estimated for heavy-ion collisions.

# Signatures of the vortical quark-gluon plasma in hadron yields

## Key claim

Total hadron yields summed over spin components are enhanced by local vorticity with a quadratic dependence, with the enhancement factor amounting to a few percent and being stronger for hadrons with larger spin. The authors propose that this effect may be detectable within current experimental sensitivities, but note that the result depends on the assumption that vorticity is weak (ω ≲ m_π) and that the Boltzmann approximation holds.

## Method

The authors extended two hadron production models, the quark-coalescence model and the statistical hadronization model, by including vorticity as a parameter. In the quark-coalescence model, constituent quarks with a local thermal distribution experience a spin-vorticity coupling energy shift δE = −ω s_z, and hadron yields are computed from products of spin-polarized quark distributions with spin combinatorial factors. In the statistical hadronization model, vorticity couples directly to hadron spin via the same energy shift in the thermal distribution. The two models were compared analytically in the Boltzmann approximation and numerically with full momentum integration.

## Key numbers

- Global vorticity strength estimated from Λ hyperon polarization at RHIC: ω ≈ 9 × 10^21 s^−1 = O(1 MeV)
- Local vorticity strength in heavy-ion collisions from phenomenological studies: ω ≈ 0.3 × T ≈ 50 MeV, where T is the temperature
- Enhancement factor for spin-summed hadron yields in the Boltzmann approximation: 1 + s(s+1)(ω/T)^2/6, where s is hadron spin
- For typical local vorticity ω/T = O(0.1), the enhancement is O(1%) (since (ω/T)^2 = O(1%))
- Coalescence and chemical freeze-out temperatures differ by approximately |T − T^ch|/T ≈ 2% at RHIC
- Temperature difference between different centralities at RHIC: |ΔT| ≲ 5 MeV
- Constituent quark mass used in calculations: m_q = 300 MeV for up and down quarks
- Hadron mass used in numerical calculations: m_h = 1000 MeV
- Temperature used in numerical calculations: T = 160 MeV

## Context

This work extends traditional quark-coalescence models (e.g., Fries et al., 2008) and statistical hadronization models (e.g., Braun-Munzinger et al., 2004) by introducing vorticity as a new parameter to characterize the quark-gluon plasma. It is complementary to existing spin polarization measurements of Λ hyperons at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2007, 2017, 2018) and light mesons at LHC and RHIC (Abelev et al., 2008; Acharya et al., 2019), which have observed global spin polarization but face a "sign problem" when comparing local vorticity predictions with data. The proposed hadron yield measurements could provide a new observable for local vorticity that is free from cancellation effects due to fluctuating vorticity directions, and could enable systematic extraction of the local vorticity strength as a function of centrality, collision energy, and rapidity.
