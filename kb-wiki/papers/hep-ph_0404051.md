# Ground-state baryon masses in the perturbative chiral quark model

> Mass differences of the flavor octet and decuplet ground-state baryons are studied in the perturbative chiral quark model. We present a way to understand the nontrivial spin- and flavor dependent mass differences, where both pseudoscalar mesons and gluons play a significant role.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both pseudoscalar meson exchange and gluon exchange are necessary to reproduce the observed spin- and flavor-dependent mass splittings of the ground-state octet and decuplet baryons within the perturbative chiral quark model, with the gluon contribution being suppressed relative to pure-gluon models because mesonic degrees of freedom also contribute in the infrared region.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral quark model where baryons are described as three localized valence quarks in a confining potential, supplemented by a perturbatively evaluated cloud of pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) and quantum gluon fluctuations. They compute one-loop mass shifts from meson-cloud, meson-exchange, gluon-cloud, and gluon-exchange diagrams, using Gaussian ansätze for the quark wave functions with separate parameters for nonstrange and strange quarks, and fit the effective quark-gluon coupling to reproduce the Δ–N mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- The three-quark core mass is fixed to \(E_{3q} = 1612.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from a fit to the nucleon mass including all one-loop effects.
- The nonstrange quark parameters are \(R_q = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(\rho_q = \sqrt{2/13} \simeq 0.392\) (corresponding to \(g_A = 1.25\)).
- The strange quark parameters are fitted as \(\rho_s = 0.354\) and \(R_s = 0.58\ \mathrm{fm}\), yielding \(r_A = 0.77\) for semileptonic hyperon decay ratios.
- The effective gluon coupling parameter is \(D = (0.49\ \mathrm{GeV})^2\), chosen so that the Δ–N mass splitting is reproduced; this is smaller than the value \(D = (0.884\ \mathrm{GeV})^2\) used in Dyson-Schwinger studies of mesons (Maris 1999).
- Current quark masses are taken as \(\hat m = 7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_s/\hat m = 25\), with \(B = 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative chiral quark model, previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the sigma term (Lyubovitskij 2001, 2000), by including gluon degrees of freedom and flavor SU(3) breaking to compute the full octet and decuplet ground-state baryon masses. It addresses the long-standing debate between the Graz group (Glozman 1997), which attributes baryon splittings solely to Goldstone boson exchange, and the Salamanca-Valencia approach (Valcarce 1995), which retains a strong gluon interaction; the present model finds that both mechanisms contribute, with the gluon coupling suppressed by the presence of meson cloud effects. The results enable systematic studies of baryon structure where meson and gluon degrees of freedom are treated on equal footing.
