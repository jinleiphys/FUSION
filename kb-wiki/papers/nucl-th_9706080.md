# Constraints to Coupling Constants of the Omega- and Sigma-Mesons with Dibaryons

> The effect of narrow dibaryon resonances to nuclear matter and structure of neutron stars is investigated in the mean-field theory (MFT) and in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). The existence of massive neutron stars imposes constraints to the coupling constants of the omega- and sigma-mesons with dibaryons. We conclude that the experimental candidates to dibaryons d1(1920) and d'(2060) if exist form in nuclear matter a Bose condensate stable against compression. This proves stability of the ground state for nuclear matter with a Bose condensate of the light dibaryons.

# Constraints to Coupling Constants of the Omega- and Sigma-Mesons with Dibaryons

## Key claim

The existence of neutron stars with mass 1.3 solar masses imposes constraints on the coupling constants of omega- and sigma-mesons with dibaryons such that for the light dibaryon candidates d1(1920) and d'(2060), the dibaryon matter unstable against compression cannot exist, meaning these dibaryons must form a Bose condensate stable against compression if they exist at all.

## Method

The authors extend the Walecka model of quantum hadrodynamics by including dibaryon fields coupled to sigma- and omega-mesons, then compute the equation of state for nuclear and neutron matter in both mean-field theory (MFT) and relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA). They match the resulting equations of state to the BBP and BPS equations of state at subnuclear densities, compute neutron star masses from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and derive constraints on the dibaryon-meson coupling constants by requiring that neutron stars of mass 1.3 solar masses do not contain dibaryon condensates.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3} (equilibrium Fermi wavenumber k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1})
- Nuclear matter binding energy at saturation: E/A - m_N = -15.75 MeV
- Dibaryon candidate masses: d1(1920) at 1920 MeV, d'(2060) at 2063 MeV
- Neutron star mass used for constraints: 1.3 solar masses (M_sun)
- Maximum neutron star masses in MFT with no dibaryons: 3.0 M_sun (for k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1}) or 2.6 M_sun (for k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1})
- Critical ratio h_sigma/(2g_sigma) for breakdown of nuclear matter saturation: approximately 0.8
- Upper limit estimate for h_sigma/(2g_sigma) from dibaryon mass scaling: approximately 0.84 for d1(1920) and d'(2060)
- For the H-particle (2220 MeV), quark cluster model estimates give h_omega/(2g_omega) = 0.89 and h_sigma/(2g_sigma) = 0.80
- Drip density for matching equations of state: rho_drip = 4.3 x 10^{11} g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) and earlier dibaryon studies by Faessler et al. (1996, 1997) to include vacuum fluctuation effects via the relativistic Hartree approximation. The paper addresses the stability condition for dibaryon Bose condensates derived from the sound velocity criterion, building on earlier work by Baldo and Krmpotic (1990) and Krivoruchenko (1995). The constraints are applied to experimental dibaryon candidates from pion double charge exchange reactions (Bilger et al., 1993) and proton-proton bremsstrahlung (Khrykin et al., 1996). The results enable future searches for dibaryons in neutron star observations and constrain the parameter space for the H-particle predicted by Jaffe (1977), which remains a candidate for unstable dibaryon matter that could lead to strange star formation.
