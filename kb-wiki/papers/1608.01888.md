# Comparison of Tsallis statistics with the Tsallis-factorized statistics in the ultrarelativistic $pp$ collisions

> The Tsallis statistics was applied to describe the experimental data on the transverse momentum distributions of hadrons. We considered the energy dependence of the parameters of the Tsallis-factorized statistics, which is now widely used for the description of the experimental transverse momentum distributions of hadrons, and the Tsallis statistics for the charged pions produced in $pp$ collisions at high energies. We found that the results of the Tsallis-factorized statistics deviate from the results of the Tsallis statistics only at low NA61/SHINE energies when the value of the entropic parameter is close to unity. At higher energies, when the value of the entropic parameter deviates essentially from unity, the Tsallis-factorized statistics satisfactorily recovers the results of the Tsallis statistics.

## Key claim

The Tsallis-factorized statistics, widely used to fit transverse momentum distributions of hadrons, deviates from the full Tsallis statistics only at low collision energies (NA61/SHINE range) where the entropic parameter is close to unity; at higher energies (PHENIX and ALICE) the Tsallis-factorized statistics satisfactorily recovers the results of the full Tsallis statistics, but the full Tsallis statistics should be used instead in the expected NICA and FAIR energy range.

## Method

The author fitted the ultrarelativistic transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis statistics (Eq. 7) and the Tsallis-factorized statistics (Eq. 9) to experimental data for charged pions produced in pp collisions at energies from sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV to 7000 GeV, using data from the NA61/SHINE, PHENIX, and ALICE Collaborations. The fits were performed for massless pions in the Maxwell-Boltzmann approximation, with the Tsallis statistics distribution including a cut-off parameter N0 to regularize divergent sums for q<1. The extracted parameters (temperature T, radius R, and entropic parameter q) were compared across the two statistics as a function of collision energy.

## Key numbers

* For NA61/SHINE at sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV (pi-): Tsallis statistics gives T=85.78±10.79 MeV, R=4.047±0.235 fm, q=0.9623±0.0142; Tsallis-factorized gives T=99.59±7.32 MeV, R=4.045±0.234 fm, q=1/q_c=0.9563±0.0190.
* For NA61/SHINE at sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV (pi-): Tsallis statistics gives T=95.83±6.38 MeV, R=4.798±0.246 fm, q=0.9326±0.0166; Tsallis-factorized gives T=95.83±6.38 MeV, R=4.798±0.246 fm, q=0.9326±0.0166.
* For PHENIX at sqrt(s)=200 GeV (pi-): Tsallis statistics gives T=87.20±11.48 MeV, R=3.823±0.714 fm, q=0.8965±0.0081; Tsallis-factorized gives T=87.20±11.49 MeV, R=3.823±0.714 fm, q=0.8965±0.0081.
* For ALICE at sqrt(s)=7000 GeV (pi++pi-): Tsallis statistics gives T=78.75±1.86 MeV, R=4.606±0.093 fm, q=0.8533±0.0024; Tsallis-factorized gives T=78.75±1.86 MeV, R=4.606±0.093 fm, q=0.8533±0.0024.
* The Tsallis statistics temperature shows a jump at sqrt(s)=17.3 GeV (from ~75 MeV at 12.3 GeV to ~96 MeV at 17.3 GeV), attributed to the discrete change in the cut-off parameter N0 from 1 to 0.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the full Tsallis statistics (Tsallis 1988, 1998) and the Tsallis-factorized statistics (Cleymans 2012) for transverse momentum distributions, building on the analytical expression for the Tsallis statistics distribution derived in Parvan (2016b). The paper contradicts the implicit assumption that the Tsallis-factorized statistics is equivalent to the full Tsallis statistics at all energies, showing that deviations occur only at low energies where q is near unity. The results enable the use of the full Tsallis statistics for describing hadron spectra at future NICA and FAIR colliders, where the Tsallis-factorized statistics would be inadequate.
