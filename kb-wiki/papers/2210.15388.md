# Flow of charge and heat in thermal QCD within the weak magnetic field limit: A BGK model approach

> We have computed the charge and heat transport coefficients of hot QCD matter by solving the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation using the BGK model approximation with a modified collision integral in the weak magnetic field regime. This modified collision integral enhances both charge and heat transport phenomena which can be understood by the large values of the above-mentioned coefficients in comparison to the relaxation collision integral. We have also presented a comparative study of coefficients like the electrical conductivity ($σ_{el}$), Hall conductivity ($σ_{H}$), thermal conductivity ($κ$) and Hall-type thermal conductivity($κ_{H}$) in weak and strong magnetic fields in the BGK model approximation. The effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on the transport coefficients have been explored using a quasiparticle model. Moreover, we have also studied the effects of weak magnetic field and finite chemical potential on Lorenz number, Knudsen number, specific heat, elliptic flow and Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key claim

The authors compute charge and heat transport coefficients for hot QCD matter using the BGK model approximation with a modified collision integral in the weak magnetic field regime, finding that this modified collision integral enhances both charge and heat transport compared to the relaxation time approximation (RTA), but only under the condition that the temperature scale dominates over the magnetic field scale so that Landau quantization effects on phase space and scattering processes can be neglected.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation with a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collision kernel, which conserves particle number instantaneously, in the weak magnetic field limit. They derive expressions for electrical conductivity, Hall conductivity, thermal conductivity, and Hall-type thermal conductivity by using an ansatz for the perturbed distribution function and expanding to first order in the electric field and magnetic field. The calculations are performed both for massless quarks with current quark masses (3 MeV, 5 MeV, and 100 MeV for up, down, and strange quarks) and within a quasiparticle model where parton masses are generated by the medium.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time for each quark flavor is given by \(\tau_{f}(T)=\frac{1}{5.1T\alpha^2_s \log(1/\alpha_s)\left[1+0.12(2N_f+1)\right] }\), where \(N_f=3\) and \(\alpha_s\) is the QCD running coupling constant dependent on both temperature and magnetic field.
- The QCD running coupling constant uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}}=0.176\) GeV and \(\Lambda=2\pi\sqrt{T^2+\left(\frac{\mu_f^2}{\pi^2}\right)}\).
- The magnetic field strength at RHIC is given as \(eB=m^2_{\pi}\) and at LHC as \(eB=15m^2_{\pi}\), where \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass.
- Current quark masses used are 3 MeV for up quarks, 5 MeV for down quarks, and 100 MeV for strange quarks.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Rath et al. (2021) which calculated the same transport coefficients using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) in the weak magnetic field regime, and the work by Khan et al. (2021) which explored the BGK model with strong magnetic fields. The BGK model addresses the issue in RTA that charge is not conserved instantaneously, and the authors present a comparative study of results between the two approaches. This enables more accurate modeling of charge and heat transport in the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for understanding the chiral magnetic effect, soft photon emission rates, and the Wiedemann-Franz law in magnetized QCD matter.
