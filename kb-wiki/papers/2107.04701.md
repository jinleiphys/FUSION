# Coulomb field correction due to virtual $e^+e^-$ production in heavy ion collisions

> The correction to the Coulomb energy due to virtual production of $e^+e^-$ pairs, which is on the order of one percent of the Coulomb energy at nuclear scales is discussed. The effects of including a pair-production term in the semi-empirical mass formula and the correction to the Coulomb barrier for a handful of nuclear collisions using the Bass and Coulomb potentials are studied. With an eye toward future work using Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model, we also calculate the correction to the Coulomb energy and force between protons after folding with a Gaussian spatial distribution.

# Coulomb field correction due to virtual $e^+e^-$ production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The correction to the Coulomb energy from virtual electron-positron pair production is on the order of one percent of the Coulomb energy at nuclear scales, but this small correction can noticeably affect the Coulomb barrier height because the nuclear and Coulomb energies roughly cancel there, and the effect reaches about 15% in the modified astrophysical S*-factor for sub-barrier fusion at the lowest energies.

## Method
The authors applied the Uehling vacuum polarization potential (small-r approximation) to compute the correction to the Coulomb self-energy in the liquid-drop model, yielding a modified semi-empirical mass formula. They then added this correction to the Bass nuclear potential to compute the total potential energy for nucleus-nucleus collisions, and used the Feynman path integral method to calculate the sub-barrier fusion probability and astrophysical S*-factor for the 12C+12C system. For future use in Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD), they derived an analytic expression for the vacuum polarization correction folded with a Gaussian nucleon spatial distribution.

## Key numbers
- The vacuum polarization correction to the Coulomb self-energy in the mass formula is (0.0073 - 0.00052 ln A) times the standard Coulomb term a_C Z(Z-1)/A^{1/3}
- For A >= 55, the correction magnitude exceeds 2m_e (the mass of an e+e- pair), where m_e = 0.511 MeV
- In 132Xe-132Xe and 235U-235U collisions, the vacuum polarization energy is 0.1% to 0.3% of the total (Bass + Coulomb) potential for surface separations s less than 5 times the nuclear radius
- The modified astrophysical S*-factor for 12C+12C shows a 15% reduction at the lowest center-of-mass energies when the e+e- correction is included
- For fission fragments, the correction V_{e+e-} = 0.0005 Z^2/A^{1/3} + 0.1 MeV, reaching about 0.5 MeV for A=180 and 1.0 MeV for A=280
- After folding with a Gaussian of width sigma_r = 1.15 fm, the correction to the proton-proton Coulomb energy is never more than about 0.6%

## Context
This work extends the Uehling (1935) vacuum polarization correction to nuclear systems, applying it to the semi-empirical mass formula, the Bass potential (Bass, 1977) for nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the neck model for fission. The sub-barrier fusion calculation for 12C+12C addresses the Gamow energy region relevant to carbon burning in stars, following the Feynman path integral approach of recent work (x2, x4). The folded potential derivation prepares for implementation in the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model (comd1-comd7) and other microscopic models (Aichelin, 1991). Future work will use the Schwinger mechanism to calculate real e+e- production rates in heavy ion collisions.
