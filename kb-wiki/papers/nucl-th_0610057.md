# Constraining the Skyrme effective interactions and the neutron skin thickness of nuclei using isospin diffusion data from heavy ion collisions

> Recent analysis of the isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions based on an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections has led to the extraction of a value of $L=88\pm 25$ MeV for the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density. This imposes stringent constraints on both the parameters in the Skyrme effective interactions and the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei. Among the 21 sets of Skyrme interactions commonly used in nuclear structure studies, the 4 sets SIV, SV, G$_σ$, and R$_σ$ are found to give $L$ values that are consistent with the extracted one. Further study on the correlations between the thickness of the neutron skin in finite nuclei and the nuclear matter symmetry energy in the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach leads to predicted thickness of the neutron skin of $0.22\pm 0.04$ fm for $^{208}$Pb, $0.29\pm 0.04$ fm for $^{132}$Sn, and $0.22\pm 0.04$ fm for $^{124}$Sn.

## Key claim

The slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to \(L=88\pm 25\) MeV from isospin diffusion data, but this value is extracted using a specific transport model (IBUU04) with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and a schematic interpolation between only four MDI interaction parameter sets (\(x=-2,-1,0,1\)).

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent IBUU04 transport model with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections to simulate isospin diffusion in \({}^{124}\)Sn + \({}^{112}\)Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter about 6 fm. They compared the calculated isospin diffusion observable \(1-R_i\) (based on the isospin asymmetry of projectile-like residues) with experimental data from NSCL/MSU for four MDI interactions with different symmetry energy stiffnesses. The value of \(L\) was extracted by linear interpolation between the theoretical results at \(x=-1\) and \(x=0\) for the centroid, and between error bars for the upper and lower limits. They then used 21 Skyrme interaction parameter sets in a Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach to correlate the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei with \(L\), \(K_{\text{sym}}\), and \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0)\).

## Key numbers

- Extracted slope of nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density: \(L = 88 \pm 25\) MeV.
- Among 21 Skyrme interaction sets, only 4 (SIV, SV, G\(_\sigma\), R\(_\sigma\)) give \(L\) values consistent with the extracted \(L = 88 \pm 25\) MeV.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{208}\)Pb: \(0.22 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{132}\)Sn: \(0.29 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Predicted neutron skin thickness for \({}^{124}\)Sn: \(0.22 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Linear correlation coefficient between \(S\) and \(L\) for \({}^{208}\)Pb: 99.25%.
- Linear correlation coefficient between \(S\) and \(L\) for \({}^{132}\)Sn: 98.76%.
- Linear correlation coefficient between \(S\) and \(L\) for \({}^{124}\)Sn: 98.75%.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU (Tsang 2004) using the IBUU04 transport model (Li 2005) to directly constrain the Skyrme interaction parameter space and neutron skin thickness. It builds on the known correlation between neutron skin thickness and the slope parameter \(L\) established by Brown (2000), Horowitz (2001), Typel (2001), Furnstahl (2002), and others. The predicted neutron skin thicknesses agree surprisingly well with those from a relativistic mean-field parametrization by Piekarewicz (2005), and the results enable future parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Laboratory to provide independent cross-checks.
