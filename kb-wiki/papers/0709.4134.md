# Alpha-alpha interaction with chiral two pion exchange and 8Be lifetime

> Assuming that alpha particles are described by a scalar-isoscalar chiral invariant heavy field it is shown how chiral symmetry determines the alpha-alpha interaction at long distances unambiguously from dimensional power counting of an effective chiral Lagrangean. The leading strong contribution is given by a two pion exchange potential which turns out to be attractive and singular at the origin, hence demanding renormalization. When 8Be is treated as a resonance state a model independent correlation between the Q-factor and lifetime 1/Gamma for the decay into two alpha particles arises. For parameters compatible with potential model analyses of low energy pi-alpha scattering it is found a Breit-Wigner width Gamma = 4.3(3) eV very close to the experimental value, Gamma= 5.57 (25)eV.

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry determines the alpha-alpha interaction at long distances unambiguously from dimensional power counting of an effective chiral Lagrangian, and for parameters compatible with potential model analyses of low energy pi-alpha scattering, the predicted Breit-Wigner width Gamma = 4.3(3) eV is close to but does not exactly match the experimental value of 5.57(25) eV, with the discrepancy suggesting further attraction is needed.

## Method

The authors treat the alpha particle as a scalar-isoscalar chiral invariant heavy field and construct an effective chiral Lagrangian with pions and photons. They compute the leading strong contribution to the alpha-alpha potential from two-pion exchange (TPE) using dispersion relations, then solve the Schrodinger equation with the singular attractive TPE potential plus Coulomb potential, implementing renormalization via an energy-independent logarithmic derivative boundary condition at short distances. The 8Be ground state is treated as a resonance state, and the S-matrix pole and Breit-Wigner width are extracted from the scattering solutions.

## Key numbers

- Breit-Wigner width for 8Be decay: Gamma = 4.3(3) eV (theoretical) versus Gamma_exp = 5.57(25) eV (experimental)
- Q-value for 8Be to alpha-alpha decay: Q = 91.84 +/- 0.04 KeV
- Relative CM momentum: p = 19.2 MeV
- Bohr radius for alpha-alpha system: a_B = 3.63 fm
- Outer classical turning point: r_max = 62.6 fm
- Inner classical turning point: r_min approximately 3 fm
- Scattering length: alpha_0^th = -1210(70) fm versus alpha_0 = -1630(150) fm from data
- Effective range: r_0^th = 1.03(1) fm versus r_0 = 1.08(1) fm from data
- WKB approximation width: Gamma_WKB = 8.6(4) eV
- S-matrix pole width: Gamma_pole = 3.4(2) eV
- Pion-alpha scattering length (potential value): A_0^pot = -0.091(17) fm
- Coupling constants from pi-alpha scattering: g_0 = -82(11), g_1 = -5.3(3), g_2 = 77(12)

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory to nuclear reactions involving composite nuclei, building on the Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian approach for pion-nucleon interactions (Weinberg, 1978; Ericson, 1988) and the renormalization program for singular potentials (Valderrama, 2005). Prior treatments of pion exchange between alpha particles include the resonating group method (Shimodaya, 1960), forward dispersion relations (Fang-Landau, 1974), and folding models from NN potentials (Ericson, 1981). The approach enables a model-independent theoretical determination of the 8Be lifetime from chiral symmetry, potentially providing constraints for low-energy nuclear reactions of astrophysical interest where experimental measurements are extremely difficult.
