# Bounds on massive graviton-like particles from searches for axion-like particles coupling to photons

> Limits on spin-0 axion-like-particles (ALPs) coupling to photons are reinterpreted as constraints on massive spin-2 graviton-like-particles (GLPs) with universal coupling $α_\text{G}/M_\text{P}$ (where $M_\text{P}$ is the reduced Planck mass) to the Standard Model fields. A minimally model-dependent recasting is performed, exploiting the formally analogous production and detection mechanisms for both particle types, based on the Primakoff and Gertsenshtein effects, i.e., photon-axion/graviton conversion. Constraints originally derived in the ALP mass vs. photon-coupling plane ($m_\text{a}, g_{\text{a}γ}$) are translated into the corresponding bounds in the GLP ($m_\text{G}, α_\text{G}/M_\text{P}$) parameter space over the full mass range, $m_\text{a,G} \approx 10^{-20}$--$10^{14}$eV probed in current and future experimental setups including cavity-based detectors (haloscopes and resonant upconversion devices), helioscopes, magnetometers, optical interferometers, beam dumps, fixed-target, and collider experiments, as well as astrophysical and cosmological constraints. Generic scenarios are considered in which GLPs are a dark matter candidate and not. Whereas current ALP searches do not set stronger bounds on massive spin-2 particles than fifth-force tests, future magnetometers, two-beam interferometers, and upconversion experiments have the potential to provide very strong sensitivity, down to $α_\text{G}/M_\text{P} \approx 10^{-32} \text{GeV}^{-1}$, for light graviton-like particles with $m_\text{G}\lesssim 10^{-8}$eV. These future detectors exhibit comparatively greater sensitivity to massive gravitons than to axions. For massive gravitons at the TeV scale, exclusive diphoton decay searches, employed in ALP studies, offer a complementary approach to standard searches for spin-2 resonances in other inclusive final states.

## Key claim

The paper reinterprets experimental limits on axion-like particles (ALPs) coupling to photons as constraints on massive spin-2 graviton-like particles (GLPs) with universal coupling to Standard Model fields, exploiting the formal analogy between the Primakoff and Gertsenshtein effects. The authors find that current ALP searches do not set stronger bounds on massive spin-2 particles than existing fifth-force tests, but future magnetometers, two-beam interferometers, and upconversion experiments could provide sensitivity down to \(\alpha_\text{G}/M_\text{P} \approx 10^{-32} \text{GeV}^{-1}\) for light GLPs with \(m_\text{G}\lesssim 10^{-8}\) eV.

## Method

The authors recast constraints from 16 different experimental search methods originally reported in the ALP mass versus photon-coupling plane \((m_\text{a}, g_{\text{a}\gamma})\) into the corresponding GLP parameter space \((m_\text{G}, \alpha_\text{G}/M_\text{P})\) by adjusting production and decay mechanisms to account for the tensor nature of spin-2 particles, different polarization dependencies, and the GLP branching fraction into diphotons (which is \(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{G}\to\gamma\gamma}\approx 0.1\)–0.05 for \(m_\text{G}\gtrsim 270\) MeV, versus \(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{a}\to\gamma\gamma}\approx 1\) for photophilic ALPs). The reinterpretation covers the full mass range \(m_\text{a,G} \approx 10^{-20}\)–\(10^{14}\) eV and includes cavity-based detectors (haloscopes and resonant upconversion devices), helioscopes, magnetometers, optical interferometers, beam dumps, fixed-target experiments, collider experiments, and astrophysical and cosmological constraints, considering both scenarios where GLPs are dark matter candidates and where they are not.

## Key numbers

- Future magnetometers, two-beam interferometers, and upconversion experiments have potential sensitivity down to \(\alpha_\text{G}/M_\text{P} \approx 10^{-32} \text{GeV}^{-1}\) for light GLPs with \(m_\text{G}\lesssim 10^{-8}\) eV.
- The reduced Planck mass is \(M_\text{P} = 1/\sqrt{8\pi G_\text{N}} = 2.4 \times 10^{18}\) GeV.
- For GLPs with masses below \(m_\text{G} \approx 1\) MeV, the diphoton branching fraction is \(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{G}\to\gamma\gamma}\approx 60\%\), with the dineutrino final state accounting for the other 40\%.
- For \(m_\text{G} \approx 1\)–270 MeV, the diphoton branching fraction remains above 40\%; for \(m_\text{G}\gtrsim 270\) MeV, it decreases to about 10–5\%.
- The GLP total decay width scales approximately as \(\propto (m_\text{G})^3 (\alpha_\text{G}/M_\text{P})^2\).
- The current age of the universe is \(t_\text{U} \approx 13.8\) Gyr \(\approx 4.35 \times 10^{17}\) s, corresponding to a decay width \(\Gamma_\text{G} = t_\text{U}^{-1} = 1.5 \times 10^{-33}\) eV.
- Light GLPs with \(m_\text{G} < 1\) eV are effectively stable for any universal coupling \(\alpha_\text{G}/M_\text{P} \gtrsim 10^{-7}\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The local dark matter energy density is \(\rho_\text{DM} \approx 0.4\) GeV cm\(^{-3}\).
- The minimum mass allowed for dark matter particles from dwarf galaxy structure is \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-22} \text{eV})\) to \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-21} \text{eV})\).
- The Higuchi bound for spin-2 particles gives \(m_\text{G} \gtrsim \sqrt{2} H_0 \approx 2.1 \times 10^{-33}\) eV.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of ALP-photon conversion searches (Graham 2015, Jaeckel 2015, Irastorza 2021) to massive spin-2 particles predicted in bimetric theories (Hassan 2011), extra-dimensional models such as ADD (Arkani-Hamed 1998) and RS (Randall 1999), and dRGT massive gravity (de Rham 2016). The reinterpretation provides a minimally model-dependent mapping between ALP and GLP parameter spaces, enabling existing experimental results from haloscopes, helioscopes, beam dumps, fixed-target, and collider experiments to constrain massive graviton-like particles, and identifies future magnetometer, interferometer, and upconversion experiments as particularly promising for probing light GLPs beyond current fifth-force limits.
