# Magnetic moments of the spin-${3\over 2}$ singly heavy baryons

> We calculate the magnetic moments of spin-$3\over 2$ singly charmed baryons in the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). The analytical expressions are given up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$. The heavy quark symmetry is used to reduce the number of low energy constants (LECs). With the lattice QCD simulation data as the magnetic moments of the charmed baryons, the numerical results are given up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ in three scenarios. In the first scenario, we use the results in the quark model as the leading order input. In the second scenario, we use the heavy quark symmetry and neglect the contribution of heavy quark. In the third scenario, the heavy quark contribution is considered on the basis of the scenario II and the magnetic moments of singly bottom baryons are given as a by-product.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of spin-\(3\over 2\) singly charmed baryons are calculated in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\), but the numerical results depend on three different scenarios for inputting the leading-order parameters and treating the heavy quark contribution, with the heavy quark symmetry used to reduce the number of unknown low energy constants.

## Method

The authors construct chiral Lagrangians for the spin-\(3\over 2\) sextet baryons (\(B_6^*\)) up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\), including baryon-photon and meson-baryon interactions. They compute tree-level and one-loop Feynman diagrams (diagrams a-l in the paper) contributing to the magnetic moments order by order up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\). Heavy quark spin symmetry is used to relate the many low energy constants (LECs) among the spin-\(1\over 2\) and spin-\(3\over 2\) sextet baryons, reducing the independent LECs to seven in the heavy quark limit. Numerical results are obtained in three scenarios: (I) using LECs from a previous fit to spin-\(1\over 2\) baryon magnetic moments; (II) using lattice QCD simulation data as input in the heavy quark limit; (III) adding a heavy quark contribution on top of scenario II.

## Key numbers

* \(\mu_{\Sigma_c^{*++}}^{(1)} = -\left({2\over3}f_9 + f_{10}\right)\mu_N\) at leading order (\(\mathcal{O}(p)\)), where \(f_9\) and \(f_{10}\) are LECs and \(\mu_N\) is the nuclear magneton.
* \(\mu_{\Sigma_c^{*0}}^{(1)} = \mu_{\Xi_c^{*0}}^{(1)} = \mu_{\Omega_c^{*0}}^{(1)} = \left({1\over3}f_9 - f_{10}\right)\mu_N\) at leading order.
* The \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) tree-level contributions for \(\Xi_c^{*+}\) and \(\Xi_c^{*0}\) are \(\mu_{\Xi_c^{*+}}^{(3,\text{tree})} = -\left(\frac{1}{2}h_2 + {4\over 3}h_4\right)\mu_N\) and \(\mu_{\Xi_c^{*0}}^{(3,\text{tree})} = -\left(\frac{1}{2}h_2 - {2\over 3}h_4\right)\mu_N\), with LECs \(h_2\) and \(h_4\).
* The \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) loop contribution from diagram (i) is \(\mu^{(3,i)} = 2\beta^{\phi}\frac{f_9 m_{\phi}^2}{128\pi^2 F_{\phi}^2}\ln\frac{m_{\phi}^2}{\lambda^2}\mu_N\), where \(\beta^{\phi}\) is a coefficient depending on the baryon and meson species, and \(F_{\pi}=92.4\) MeV, \(F_K=113\) MeV, \(F_\eta=116\) MeV are used.
* In the heavy quark limit, the axial coupling constants are reduced to two independent LECs: \(g_5 = g_a\), \(g_1 = -\frac{2}{3}g_a\), \(g_3 = -\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}}g_a\), \(g_4 = g_b\), \(g_2 = -\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}}g_b\), and \(g_6 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of magnetic moments for spin-\(1\over 2\) singly charmed baryons (Wang 2018) to the spin-\(3\over 2\) sextet baryons, using the same heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework. It builds on earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of octet and decuplet baryon magnetic moments (Jenkins 1992, Bernard 1995) and on heavy quark symmetry constructions for singly heavy hadrons (Yan 1992, Cho 1992, Cheng 1993). The results provide predictions that can be compared with future lattice QCD simulations (Can 2013, Bahtiyar 2015) and experimental measurements of charmed baryon magnetic moments, which are currently unavailable.
