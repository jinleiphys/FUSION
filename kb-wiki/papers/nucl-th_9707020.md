# Chemical equilibration of strangeness

> Thermal models are very useful in the understanding of particle production in general and especially in the case of strangeness. We summarize the assumptions which go into a thermal model calculation and which differ in the application of various groups. We compare the different results to each other. Using our own calculation we discuss the validity of the thermal model and the amount of strangeness equilibration at CERN-SPS energies. Finally the implications of the thermal analysis on the reaction dynamics are discussed.

## Key claim

Thermal models can describe strange particle production in heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS energies, but the interpretation of strangeness equilibration remains controversial because alternative explanations without quark-gluon plasma formation are also successful, and the thermal fit is not perfect, especially for antibaryon yields.

## Method

The author performed a thermal model calculation including all hadronic states up to 1.7 GeV in mass, using Bose statistics for pions and Boltzmann approximation for all other hadrons, with resonances populated including their width via a Breit-Wigner distribution. The analysis used chi-squared fits to 4pi data from the NA35 collaboration for S+S and S+Ag collisions, and to central particle ratios for S+Au collisions, treating strangeness canonically for S+S and S+Ag but grand canonically for S+Au.

## Key numbers

- For S+S collisions at sqrt(s) = 19.5 GeV: T = 202 +/- 13 MeV, gamma_s = 0.84 +/- 0.07, chi-squared/dof = 11.6/4
- For S+Ag collisions at sqrt(s) = 19.5 GeV: T = 185 +/- 8 MeV, gamma_s = 0.82 +/- 0.07, chi-squared/dof = 6.72/2
- For S+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 19.5 GeV: T = 160.2 +/- 3.5 MeV, mu_B = 158 +/- 4 MeV, gamma_s = 0.66 +/- 0.04
- The error for pions using Boltzmann approximation instead of Bose statistics is 9.4% at T = 150 MeV and 11.3% at T = 200 MeV; for kaons the error is 0.5% and 1.1% respectively
- In p+p collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.5 GeV, grand canonical treatment gives gamma_s = 0.35 compared to gamma_s = 0.51 from canonical treatment (Becattini 1997)

## Context

This paper reviews and compares thermal model calculations from multiple groups (including Becattini 1996, 1997; Braun-Munzinger 1995, 1996; Letessier 1994, 1995; Rafelski 1991, 1996) applied to strange particle production in heavy ion collisions. The work extends earlier proposals that strangeness enhancement signals quark-gluon plasma formation (Rafelski 1981, 1982; Koch 1986) while also addressing alternative hadronic rescattering mechanisms (Sorge 1994; Capella 1996). The analysis enables future studies of whether strangeness reaches chemical equilibrium in heavy ion collisions and what the thermal parameters imply about reaction dynamics, particularly regarding possible enhanced entropy production seen in negative hadron yields.
