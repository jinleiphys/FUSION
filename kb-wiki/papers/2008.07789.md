# Relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem for ideal Magnetohydrodynamics

> We have studied the relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem for ideal Magnetohydrodynamics. The relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem is a conservation equation for the called $T$-vorticity. We have briefly reviewed the ideal magnetohydrodynamics in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The highlight of this work is that we have obtained the general expression of relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem for ideal Magnetohydrodynamics. We have also applied the analytic solutions of ideal magnetohydrodynamics in Bjorken flow to check our results. Our main results can also be implemented to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for the relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem in ideal magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), showing that magnetic fields may modify the conservation of T-vorticity. However, the authors note that for the specific analytic solution of ideal MHD in a Bjorken flow with transverse magnetic fields, the T-vorticity remains conserved despite the general modification.

## Method

The authors start from the energy-momentum conservation equation and Maxwell's equations in the ideal MHD limit, where electric conductivity is infinite and the electric field four-vector vanishes. They rewrite the conservation equations using thermodynamic relations and introduce the antisymmetric T-vorticity tensor. The integral form of the conservation equation is then expressed as a circulation integral along a covariant loop, yielding the relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem for ideal MHD. The result is checked against analytic solutions of ideal MHD in a Bjorken flow with transverse magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields in relativistic heavy ion collisions are of order \(10^{17}-10^{18}\) Gauss (from the introduction, citing prior work)
- The Bjorken flow solution uses magnetic fields decaying as \(B^{\mu} = (0,0,B_{0}\frac{\tau_{0}}{\tau},0)\), where \(B_{0}\) and \(\tau_{0}\) are the initial magnetic field and initial proper time, respectively
- For the Bjorken flow solution, the circulation integral yields \(\frac{d}{d\tau}\oint_{L(\tau)}Tu^{\mu}dx_{\mu}=0\), confirming T-vorticity conservation in this special case

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem, previously established for ideal hydrodynamics without magnetic fields by Gao et al. (2014) and for dissipative hydrodynamics by Yang et al. (2017), to the case of ideal magnetohydrodynamics. The authors had previously derived analytic solutions for ideal MHD in Bjorken flow with transverse magnetic fields (Pu et al. 2016, Roy et al. 2015), which are used to verify the new theorem. The results can be applied to relativistic MHD in heavy ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields are generated, and enable future extensions to normal MHD, anomalous MHD, and magnetization effects.
