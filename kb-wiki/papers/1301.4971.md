# On extraction of the total photoabsorption cross section on the neutron from data on the deuteron

> An improved procedure is suggested for finding the total photoabsorption cross section on the neutron from data on the deuteron at energies < 1.5 GeV. It includes unfolding of smearing effects caused by Fermi motion of nucleons in the deuteron and also takes into account non-additive contributions to the deuteron cross section due to final-state interactions of particles in single and double pion photoproduction. This procedure is applied to analysis of existing data.

# On extraction of the total photoabsorption cross section on the neutron from data on the deuteron

## Key claim
The paper proposes an improved procedure for extracting the total photoabsorption cross section on the neutron from deuteron data at photon energies below 1.5 GeV, which includes proper unfolding of Fermi smearing effects and evaluation of non-additive corrections from final-state interactions. The authors find that the total non-additive correction Δσ_pn is surprisingly small (despite individual contributions of order 10 μb), so the main improvement comes from refining the solution of the integral equation for unfolding rather than from large corrections.

## Method
The authors model the deuteron photoabsorption cross section as σ_d(ω) = F̂[σ_p(ω) + σ_n(ω)] + Δσ_pn(ω), where F̂ is a linear integral operator that smears individual nucleon cross sections according to Fermi motion in the deuteron, using a Hulthén wave function with parameters a=45.7 MeV/c and b=260 MeV/c. They unfold the smearing by fitting the unsmeared cross section σ(ω) = σ_p(ω) + σ_n(ω) as a sum of Breit-Wigner resonances plus a smooth background, with coefficients determined from a fit to deuteron data. Non-additive corrections Δσ_pn are calculated from diagrams including interference of impulse approximation amplitudes, NN final-state interactions, πN final-state interactions, coherent deuteron production, and double-pion production channels, using elementary amplitudes from MAID and SAID analyses.

## Key numbers
- The average longitudinal momentum of nucleons in the deuteron is ⟨p_z²⟩^{1/2} = 53.9 MeV/c (Hulthén) or 54.9 MeV/c (CD-Bonn wave function)
- The spread in effective photon energy due to Fermi motion is Δω^{eff} = 0.057 ω, corresponding to an energy resolution of approximately ±60 MeV at ω ~ 1 GeV
- The Hulthén wave function parameters are a = 45.7 MeV/c, b = 260 MeV/c, with normalization constant k² = 12.588 MeV/c
- The momentum cutoff used in calculations is |p_z| > p_cut = 200 MeV/c
- The deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV
- The Daresbury data step size of 25 MeV is noted as physically unjustified given the ~60 MeV energy resolution

## Context
This work extends the procedure of the Daresbury group (Armstrong 1972) who measured total photoabsorption cross sections on protons and deuterons between 0.265 and 4.215 GeV, using a simple ansatz σ_d(ω) = F(ω)[σ_p(ω) + σ_n(ω)] that assumed identical smearing for proton and neutron and neglected non-additive corrections. The paper is motivated by recent preliminary GRAAL experiment results (Bartalini 2008, Rudnev 2010, Nedorezov 2012, Turinge 2012) that indicate approximately equal strength of the F_15(1680) resonance excitation on proton and neutron, contradicting earlier studies and Particle Data Group branching ratios (Br(N*→γp) = 0.21-0.32%, Br(N*→γn) = 0.021-0.046%). The improved procedure enables more reliable extraction of neutron cross sections from existing deuteron data (Daresbury and Mainz data from McCormick 1996) and will be useful for interpretation of GRAAL data and future experiments.
