# Searching for signatures around 1920 MeV of a $N^*$ state of three hadron nature

> We provide a series of arguments which support the idea that the peak seen in the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ reaction around 1920 MeV should correspond to the recently predicted state of $J^P=1/2^+$ as a bound state of $K \bar{K}N$ with a mixture of $a_0(980)N$ and $f_0(980)N$ components. At the same time we propose polarization experiments in that reaction as a further test of the prediction, as well as a study of the total cross section for $γp \to K^+ K^- p$ at energies close to threshold and of $d σ/d M_{inv}$ for invariant masses close to the two kaon threshold.

## Key claim

The peak observed in the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ reaction around 1920 MeV likely corresponds to a predicted $J^P=1/2^+$ $N^*$ state of $K \bar{K}N$ nature with a mixture of $a_0(980)N$ and $f_0(980)N$ components, rather than a $3/2^+$ resonance as previously claimed. The authors argue that this state naturally explains why the peak appears in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ but not prominently in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$, and why it has not been seen in pion-induced reactions.

## Method

The authors compare the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ and $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ reactions using SU(3) Yukawa couplings to show that the $K^- p \to \Lambda$ vertex is about four times stronger than the $K^- p \to \Sigma^0$ vertex. They analyze the angular dependence of the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ amplitude for both $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ resonance assignments, finding that a $1/2^+$ state produces no angular dependence while a $3/2^+$ state gives a $\sin^2\theta$ dependence. They also calculate the differential and integrated cross sections for $\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p$ close to threshold using the $K^+K^- p \to K^+K^- p$ amplitude from Faddeev equations in coupled channels.

## Key numbers

- The $K^- p \to \Lambda$ coupling is proportional to $-1.26$ and the $K^- p \to \Sigma^0$ coupling to $0.33$, giving an order-of-magnitude difference in signal strength between the two reactions
- The predicted $N^*$ state has mass around 1920 MeV with $J^P=1/2^+$, and the peak in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ has a width of about 100 MeV or less
- The $f_0(980)$ mass is taken as 980 MeV with width 30 MeV; the $N^*$ resonance width from Faddeev calculations is about 20 MeV, but the real state may have width around 60 MeV
- For a $3/2^+$ resonance, the angular distribution gives $\overline{\sum}\sum |t^{(3/2)}|^2 \propto \frac{1}{9}\vec{k}^2\vec{q}^2(3\sin^2\theta+2)$, meaning the signal at 90 degrees is about 5/2 times the size at forward or backward angles
- The peak contribution over background in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ is about $0.5\,\mu b$ for $d\sigma/d\cos\theta$ at all angles
- The asymmetry $\Sigma$ for the $1/2^+$ state is 0; measured values are $\Sigma \simeq 0.25 \pm 0.15$

## Context

This work extends the prediction of a $K \bar{K}N$ bound state with $J^P=1/2^+$ around 1910-1920 MeV from variational methods (Jido, 2008) and Faddeev equation studies (Martinez Torres, 2008) to experimental signatures. It contradicts the claim by Nikonov (2007) of a $3/2^+$ resonance around 1900 MeV in combined analyses, and explains why the PDG and the GWU group (Arndt, 2006) found no evidence for this state in $\pi N$ reactions. The work enables experimental tests using polarization experiments at ELSA, MAMI, or CEBAF, and threshold studies of $\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p$ at Spring8/Osaka.
