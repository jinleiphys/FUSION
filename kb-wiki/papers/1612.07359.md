# Electromagnetic probes of heavy ion collisions: recent developments

> The current status of photon and dilepton emission in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions is reviewed, and recent developments are highlighted. The importance of emissions at early, intermediate and late times is emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-\(p_T\) direct photons in heavy ion collisions are still reasonably assumed to be dominated by thermal radiation from the quark-gluon plasma, despite a quantitative tension where state-of-the-art hydrodynamic calculations underestimate the measured photon spectra by up to a factor of three at RHIC and by smaller amounts at the LHC, with the deviation amounting to approximately two sigma or less at RHIC and one sigma or less at the LHC.

## Method

The author reviews the current status of photon and dilepton measurements in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC (Au-Au, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV) and the LHC (Pb-Pb, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV), focusing on comparisons between experimental data and theoretical calculations based on relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. The review examines thermal photon and dilepton emission computed from hydrodynamic simulations, the role of early-time and late-time emission, and the status of perturbative and non-perturbative calculations of thermal emission rates.

## Key numbers

* Direct photon spectra at RHIC (Au-Au, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV) are underestimated by thermal photon calculations by up to a factor of three, corresponding to a deviation of approximately two sigma or less.
* Direct photon spectra at the LHC (Pb-Pb, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV) show less tension, with a deviation of approximately one sigma or less.
* The hydrodynamic simulation for a central Pb-Pb collision at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2760\) GeV starts at \(\tau_0=0.4\) fm, with the hottest regions reaching temperatures of 500–600 MeV.
* Two dilepton invariant mass windows around 0.3–0.6 GeV and 0.8–1.0 GeV are identified where thermal dileptons are expected to dominate over hadronic and heavy quark decays in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
* Early-time thermal photon emission is estimated to contribute mainly at higher \(p_T^\gamma\) above 1.5–2 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing effort to use electromagnetic probes to constrain the spacetime evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing hadronic observables which are primarily sensitive to later stages. The paper discusses tensions between thermal photon calculations and data from RHIC and the LHC, referencing specific hydrodynamic calculations (Paquet 2015) and earlier work on thermal rates (Ghiglieri 2013, 2014, 2015; Ghisoiu 2014). It highlights the need for simultaneous studies of photon and dilepton production, improved treatment of early-time dynamics and thermalization (Lappi 2015, Kurkela 2016, Gelis 2016), and better understanding of thermal emission rates near the confinement transition, including comparisons with lattice QCD (Ghiglieri 2016) and holographic approaches (Finazzo 2015, Iatrakis 2016).
