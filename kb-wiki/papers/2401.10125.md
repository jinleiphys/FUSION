# A potential approach to the $X(3872)$ thermal behavior

> We study the potential of $X(3872)$ at finite temperature in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation under the assumption that it is a tetraquark. We argue that, at large number of colors, it is a good approximation to assume that the potential consists in a real part plus a constant imaginary term. The real part is then computed adapting an approach by Rothkopf and Lafferty and using as input lattice QCD determinations of the potential for hybrids. This model allows us to qualitatively estimate at which temperature range the formation of a heavy tetraquark is possible, and to propose a qualitative picture for the dissociation of the state in a medium. Our approach can be applied to other suggested internal structures for the $X(3872)$ and to other exotic states.

## Key claim

The authors argue that, under the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and assuming the X(3872) is a tetraquark, the in-medium potential can be approximated by a real part plus a constant imaginary term, allowing a qualitative estimate of the temperature range where formation of a heavy tetraquark is possible, rather than a precise quantitative prediction.

## Method

The authors reduce the four-body tetraquark problem to an effective two-body system using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, treating the heavy c and anti-c quarks as slow degrees of freedom and integrating out the light degrees of freedom. For the real part of the potential at finite temperature, they adapt the approach of Rothkopf and Lafferty (2019), using as input lattice QCD determinations of hybrid potentials from Capitani et al. (2018) and applying a permittivity formalism based on hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation with a Debye mass. For the imaginary part, they argue that at large number of colors it behaves as a constant, with its thermal dependence derived from dimensional analysis and the structure of the HTL permittivity.

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach for conventional quarkonium to exotic tetraquark states at finite temperature, building on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation framework for heavy-light systems discussed by Soto (2020). It adapts the method of Rothkopf and Lafferty (2019) originally developed for the singlet quarkonium potential, and uses lattice QCD results for hybrid potentials from Capitani et al. (2018) as input. The approach enables future studies of how the X(3872) dissociates in a quark-gluon plasma, and can be applied to other proposed internal structures for the X(3872) and to other exotic states.
