# Molecular picture for the $Ω(2012)$ revisited

> We conduct a study of the interaction of the $\bar K Ξ^*$, $ηΩ$($s$-wave) and $\bar K Ξ$($d$-wave) channels within a coupled channel unitary approach where the transition potential between the $\bar K Ξ^*$ and $ηΩ$ channels is obtained from chiral Lagrangians. The transition potential between $\bar K Ξ^*$, $ηΩ$ and $\bar K Ξ$ is taken in terms of free parameters, which together with a cut off to regularize the meson-baryon loops are fitted to the $Ω(2012)$ data. We find that all data including the recent Belle experiment on $Γ_{Ω^* \to π\bar K Ξ}/ Γ_{Ω^* \to \bar K Ξ}$, are compatible with the molecular picture stemming from meson baryon interaction of these channels.

## Key claim

All data, including the recent Belle experiment on the ratio \(\Gamma_{\Omega^* \to \pi \bar K \Xi}/ \Gamma_{\Omega^* \to \bar K \Xi}\), are compatible with the molecular picture of the \(\Omega(2012)\) as a dynamically generated state from meson-baryon interaction, provided that the experimental cut on the \(\pi\Xi\) invariant mass and an energy-dependent \(\Xi^*\) width are properly implemented.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel unitary approach using the Bethe-Salpeter equation with three channels: \(\bar K \Xi^*\) and \(\eta \Omega\) in \(s\)-wave, and \(\bar K \Xi\) in \(d\)-wave. The transition potential between \(\bar K \Xi^*\) and \(\eta \Omega\) was obtained from chiral Lagrangians, while the \(d\)-wave transitions to \(\bar K \Xi\) were parametrized with free parameters \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\). The loop functions were regularized with a cutoff \(q_{\text{max}}\), and all three parameters were fitted to the \(\Omega(2012)\) mass, width, and the Belle ratio. The \(\Xi^*\) width was treated as energy-dependent in the convolution of the \(\bar K \Xi^*\) loop, and the experimental cut \(1.49\ \text{GeV} < M_{\text{inv}}(\pi\Xi) < 1.53\ \text{GeV}\) was applied to the decay calculation.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(\Omega(2012)\): \(2012.4 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.6\ \text{MeV}\) (Belle experiment).
- Total width of \(\Omega(2012)\): \(6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} \pm 1.6\ \text{MeV}\) (Belle experiment).
- Experimental upper limit: \(\Gamma_{\Omega}(\pi \bar K \Xi)/\Gamma_{\Omega,\bar K \Xi} < 11.9\%\) (Belle 2020).
- Best-fit parameters: \(q_{\text{max}} = 704\ \text{MeV}\), \(\alpha = 4.5 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{MeV}^{-3}\), \(\beta = 5.1 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{MeV}^{-3}\).
- With opposite-sign parameters: \(q_{\text{max}} = 735\ \text{MeV}\), \(\alpha = -8.7 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{MeV}^{-3}\), \(\beta = 18.3 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{MeV}^{-3}\).
- Ratio \(\Gamma_{\Omega^* \to \pi \bar K \Xi\ (\text{cut})}/\Gamma_{\Omega^*, \text{non}} = 11\%\) (with opposite-sign parameters), compatible with the experimental upper bound.
- Coupling \(g_{\bar K \Xi^*}\) magnitude: \(1.86 - i0.02\) (opposite-sign solution); probability of \(\bar K \Xi^*\) component: \(0.57 + i0.10\).

## Context

This work revisits the molecular interpretation of the \(\Omega(2012)\) following the Belle 2020 result that set a stringent upper limit on the \(\pi \bar K \Xi\) decay width, which was in tension with earlier molecular predictions by Pavon Valderrama (2018), Xie et al. (2018), and Pavao et al. (2019). The present study extends the approach of Pavao et al. (2019) by implementing an energy-dependent \(\Xi^*\) width and the experimental cut on \(M_{\text{inv}}(\pi\Xi)\), showing that the molecular picture remains viable. It also contrasts with the \(p\)-wave molecular proposal of Lin and Zou (2020) and the quark-model identification of the \(\Omega(2012)\) as a \(3/2^-\) state by Qi et al. (2020). The results enable further refinement of the \(\eta \Omega\) channel's role and motivate improved experimental data to determine the optimal parameters.
