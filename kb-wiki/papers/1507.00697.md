# Ab Initio Derivation of Model Energy Density Functionals

> I propose a simple and manageable method that allows for deriving coupling constants of model energy density functionals (EDFs) directly from ab initio calculations performed for finite fermion systems. A proof-of-principle application allows for linking properties of finite nuclei, determined by using the nuclear nonlocal Gogny functional, to the coupling constants of the quasilocal Skyrme functional. The method does not rely on properties of infinite fermion systems but on the ab initio calculations in finite systems. It also allows for quantifying merits of different model EDFs in describing the ab initio results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to derive coupling constants of model energy density functionals (EDFs) directly from *ab initio* calculations in finite fermion systems, without relying on infinite nuclear matter properties. The method is demonstrated as a proof-of-principle by linking the nonlocal Gogny functional to the quasilocal Skyrme functional, but the author notes that the approach is "simple and manageable" and that the full research program "will probably take some time" given current computational limits.

## Method

The author replaces the standard DFT use of an external one-body potential with a finite set of EDF generators (two-body, three-body, etc. operators) that are added as constraints to the many-body Hamiltonian. For each set of Lagrange multipliers, the constrained *ab initio* calculation yields exact ground-state energies and one-body non-local densities; the coupling constants of the model EDF are then adjusted via linear regression to reproduce these energies. In the proof-of-principle, the Gogny EDF D1S is used as the "exact" reference, and the eight coupling constants of the Skyrme EDF are fitted to 1344 values of Gogny energies obtained for eight doubly magic nuclei with 21 values each of eight Lagrange multipliers.

## Key numbers

- The relative rms deviation between the Gogny energies and the fitted Skyrme EDF S1Sd energies is 0.014% (1344 data points, eight coupling constants).
- The absolute rms deviation of ground-state energies between the Gogny EDF D1S and the Skyrme EDF S1Sd is 1.99 MeV (relative rms 0.28%) across eight doubly magic nuclei (16O, 40,48Ca, 56,78Ni, 100,132Sn, 208Pb).
- For the six-dimensional Skyrme EDF S1Se (with spin-current coupling constants set to zero), the absolute rms deviation increases to 2.34 MeV (relative rms 0.40%).
- The Gogny-equivalent Skyrme coupling constants for S1Sd include: C0^rho = -603.82(22) MeV fm^3, C1^rho = 484(4) MeV fm^3; C0^tau = 77.95(23) MeV fm^5, C1^tau = -78(3) MeV fm^5; C0^J = 24.9(1.2) MeV fm^5, C1^J = 71(3) MeV fm^5.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to link nuclear DFT to *ab initio* approaches (Fur08a, Dru10, Kai10, Sto10, Hol11, Bog11, Gan11, Kai13), which mostly relied on infinite-nuclear-matter properties or the density-matrix expansion (Neg72). The method directly connects *ab initio* calculations in finite nuclei to model EDFs, enabling quantitative testing of different EDF forms (e.g., zero-range higher-order, finite-range regularized, three-body or four-body pseudopotentials) and their adequacy for describing finite nuclear systems. The approach can be extended to time-odd or pairing terms and to matching *ab initio*-derived kernels (Dug15a, Dug15b).
