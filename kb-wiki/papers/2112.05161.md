# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: analytic results for sub-leading (single) logarithms

> Consider the in-medium splitting $g \to gg$ of a very high-energy gluon traversing a QCD medium, accounting for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. It has been known for some time that soft radiative corrections to that splitting generate a double-log correction to the splitting rate, whose effects can be absorbed into running of the medium parameter $\hat q$ describing the rate of transverse momentum kicks to high-energy particles due to small-angle scattering from the medium. Less has been known about sub-leading, single logarithms in this context. In this paper, we find analytic formulas for those single logs (with various caveats and clarifications).

## Key claim

The authors derive analytic formulas for sub-leading (single) logarithms arising from overlapping formation times in sequential gluon bremsstrahlung $g \to gg \to ggg$ in a QCD medium, but only within the $\hat q$ approximation and using a specific infrared regulator (a cut-off on longitudinal momentum fraction $y \sim \delta$) rather than the more physically motivated cut-off on time scales $\Delta t \sim \tau_0$ (the mean free time).

## Method

The authors extract the small-$y$ (soft gluon) limit of previously derived general-$y$ formulas for the nine time-ordered "ABC diagrams" that produce double-logarithmic infrared behavior in the $\hat q$ approximation for an infinite, static, homogeneous QCD medium in the large-$N_c$ limit. They isolate the coefficients of the $y^{-1} \ln y$ and $y^{-1}$ terms in the differential rate $d\Gamma/(dx\,dy)$ for each diagram, sum them, and verify the result against corrected numerical extractions from their prior work.

## Key numbers

* The effective jet quenching parameter including double and single logs is $\hat q_{\rm A}^{\,\rm eff}(\delta,x) = \left[1 + \frac{C_A \alpha_s}{2\pi} \left( \frac12 \ln^2\delta + \bar s(x) \, \ln\delta \right)\right] \hat q_{\rm A}$, where $\delta$ is the infrared cut-off on longitudinal momentum fraction.
* The single-log coefficient is $\bar s(x) = - \ln\bigl(16\,x(1{-}x)(1{-}x{+}x^2)\bigr) + 2 \, \frac{ \bigl[ x^2 \bigl( \ln x - \frac{\pi}{8} \bigr) + (1{-}x)^2 \bigl( \ln(1{-}x) - \frac{\pi}{8} \bigr) \bigr] }{ (1-x+x^2) }$, where $x$ and $1{-}x$ are the energy fractions of the daughters in the underlying single splitting.
* The formation time for the underlying single splitting is $\sqrt{ 2x(1{-}x)E / [(1{-}x{+}x^2) \hat q_{\rm A}] }$.
* The double-log result from earlier literature is $\hat q^{\,\rm eff}(L) = \left[1 + \frac{C_A \alpha_s}{2\pi} \ln^2(L/\tau_0)\right] \hat q$, where $L$ is the medium size and $\tau_0$ is the mean free time.

## Context

This work extends the program of Arnold, Gorda, and Iqbal (2020-2021) on overlapping formation times in medium-induced gluon splitting, which previously extracted the double-log correction numerically and verified it matches the analytic results of Blaizot, Iancu, and Wu (2013) and Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013). The analytic single-log results enable future work on infrared-safe measures of shower development, subtraction of IR divergences in numerical calculations, and investigation of whether single logs can be absorbed into a redefinition of $\hat q$ (addressed in a companion paper). The calculation is restricted to the $\hat q$ approximation and the large-$N_c$ limit, with the caveat that the results depend on the specific choice of infrared regulator (a cut-off on longitudinal momentum rather than the mean free time).
