# Subleading power corrections to heavy quarkonium production in QCD factorization approach

> We report the current understanding of heavy quarkonium production at high transverse momentum ($p_T$) in hadronic collisions in terms of QCD factorization. In this presentation, we highlight the role of subleading power corrections to heavy quarkonium production, which are essential to describe the $p_T$ spectrum of quarkonium at a relatively lower $p_T$. We also introduce prescription to match QCD factorization to fixed-order NRQCD factorization calculations for quarkonium production at low $p_T$.

## Key claim

Subleading power (NLP) corrections to the QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production are essential to describe the $p_T$ spectrum at relatively lower $p_T$ (around $p_T \lesssim 4 m_{J/\psi}$), even though they are formally suppressed by $1/p_T^2$ compared to the leading power (LP) contribution; the NLP contribution can dominate over the LP one when $p_T \lesssim 4 m_{J/\psi}$ because a heavy $Q\bar{Q}$ pair is more likely than a single parton to become a quarkonium.

## Method

The authors employed the QCD factorization approach, expanding the production cross section in powers of $1/p_T$ and factorizing both LP and NLP contributions in terms of perturbatively calculable partonic scattering convoluted with universal parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs). The LP cross section uses twist-2 single-parton-to-quarkonium FFs, while the NLP cross section uses twist-4 double-parton ($Q\bar{Q}$-pair)-to-quarkonium FFs, with coupled DGLAP-like evolution equations for both. The input FFs at the scale $\mu_0 = 6$ GeV were approximated using NRQCD factorization with a parameterized $z$-dependence of the form $z^\alpha (1-z)^\beta / B[1+\alpha,1+\beta]$ with $\alpha=30$, $\beta=0.5$, and the assumption that the heavy quark pair carries equal momentum fractions ($u=v=1/2$) was justified for color-octet channels.

## Key numbers

- The NLP contribution starts to dominate over the LP contribution when $p_T \lesssim 4 m_{J/\psi}$, and specifically at Tevatron energies the NLP contribution becomes predominant around $p_T = 15$ GeV and below.
- The subleading quark pair corrections to the twist-2 evolution equations lead to a 10-30% enhancement to the cross sections even at a probing scale of $p_T = \mathcal{O}(100$ GeV$)$.
- The input FFs were set at $\mu_0 = 6$ GeV with parameters $\alpha=30$, $\beta=0.5$; the NLP contribution was evaluated with a $K$-factor $K_{\rm NLP}=2$ to mimic NLO contributions.
- The LP factorization formula is expected to work for $J/\psi$ production when $p_T \gtrsim 10 m_{J/\psi}$; the NLP contribution provides a better description of data at collider energies even at $p_T \sim 3 m_{J/\psi}$.
- The NLP contribution starts to overshoot data when $p_T \lesssim 2 m_{J/\psi}$, indicating the need for matching to fixed-order NRQCD calculations around $p_T \lesssim 3 m_{J/\psi}$.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization approach for heavy quarkonium production previously developed by the same authors (Lee et al., 2021) which successfully described LHC data on $J/\psi$ production at high $p_T \gg m_{J/\psi}$. The current paper extends the analysis to lower $p_T$ by including NLP corrections and compares with data from both LHC and Tevatron experiments (CDF, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS). The approach contrasts with standard NRQCD factorization (Bodwin, 1994) which lacks systematic treatment of $\ln(p_T^2/m_Q^2)$-type corrections; the authors propose a matching prescription between the QCD-resummed formalism and fixed-order NRQCD calculations to cover the full $p_T$ range, with details left for future work (Lee, Qiu, Sterman, Watanabe, 2022).
