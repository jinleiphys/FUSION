# Pair Winds in Schwarzschild Spacetime with Application to Strange Stars

> We present the results of numerical simulations of stationary, spherically outflowing, electron-positron pair winds, with total luminosities in the range 10^{34}--10^{42} ergs/s. In the concrete example described here, the wind injection source is a hot, bare, strange star, predicted to be a powerful source of pairs created by the Coulomb barrier at the quark surface. We find that photons dominate in the emerging emission, and the emerging photon spectrum is rather hard and differs substantially from the thermal spectrum expected from a neutron star with the same luminosity. This might help distinguish the putative bare strange stars from neutron stars.

## Key claim
Numerical simulations of stationary, spherically outflowing electron-positron pair winds in Schwarzschild spacetime show that for injected pair luminosities above about 10^34 ergs/s, the emerging emission is dominated by photons rather than pairs, and the photon spectrum is hard and non-thermal, which could help distinguish bare strange stars from neutron stars.

## Method
The authors solved the general relativistic kinetic Boltzmann equations for electrons, positrons, and photons in Schwarzschild geometry, including two-body interactions (Møller/Bhabha scattering, Compton scattering, pair annihilation, photon-photon pair production) and their radiative variants (bremsstrahlung, double Compton scattering, three-photon annihilation). They modeled a spherically outflowing pair wind injected from a hot, bare, unmagnetized, non-rotating strange star, with injected pair luminosities ranging from 10^34 to 10^42 ergs/s.

## Key numbers
- Injected pair luminosity range: 10^34 to 10^42 ergs/s
- Upper limit to emerging pair rate: dot N_e^max ~ 10^43 s^-1
- Mean energy of emergent photons decreases from ~400 keV to 40 keV as injected pair luminosity increases from ~10^34 to 10^42 ergs/s
- Maximum pair luminosity for a wind from radius R: L_±^max = 4π m_e c^3 R Γ^2 / σ_T ~ 10^36 (R/10^6 cm) Γ^2 ergs/s, where Γ is the pair bulk Lorentz factor

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous numerical simulations of relativistic pair winds (Aksenov et al. 2003, 2004, 2005) to the specific case of hot bare strange stars, which were predicted by Usov (1998, 2001) to be powerful sources of electron-positron pairs created by the Coulomb barrier at the quark surface. The results enable observational discrimination between bare strange stars and neutron stars, since the predicted hard photon spectrum differs substantially from the thermal spectrum expected from a neutron star with the same luminosity.
