# Analysis of the Energy Spectra of Ground States of Deformed Nuclei in rare-earth region

> The 62Sm, 64Gd, 64Dy, 70Y b, 72Hf and 74W nuclei are classified as deformed nuclei. Low-lying bands are one of the most fundamental excitation modes in the energy spectra of deformed nuclei. In this paper a theoretical analysis of the experimental data within the phenomenological model is presented. The energy spectra of ground states are calculated. It is found the low-lying spectra of ground band states are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The phenomenological model, which accounts for Coriolis mixing of low-lying state bands, reproduces the experimental ground-band energy spectra of even-even deformed rare-earth nuclei (Sm, Gd, Dy, Yb, Hf, W isotopes) with good agreement, but the energy difference between theory and experiment increases with angular momentum due to non-adiabatic effects at high spin.

## Method

The authors used the Harris parametrization to describe the effective moment of inertia as a function of rotational frequency: \(\Im_{eff} = \Im_0 + \Im_1 \omega_{eff}^2(I)\). The parameters \(\Im_0\) and \(\Im_1\) were determined by a least-squares fit to experimental energies for spins \(I \leq 8\hbar\). The rotational energy \(E_{rot}(I)\) was then calculated from these parameters, and the results were compared with experimental data for isotopes of Sm, Gd, Dy, Yb, Hf, and W.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Usmanov (1997, 2010) to a systematic analysis of ground-band spectra across six isotopic chains in the rare-earth region, building on the Bohr-Mottelson adiabatic model. The results show that deviations from the simple \(E(I) \sim I(I+1)\) rotational law become significant at high spin, attributed to Coriolis mixing of the ground band with vibrational bands. The calculated energies agree well with experimental data from Begzhanov (1989) and other compilations (Reich 2003, Helmer 1996, Reich 2005), enabling further study of non-adiabatic effects in deformed nuclei.
