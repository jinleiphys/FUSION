# Magnetic moments of $A = 3$ nuclei with chiral effective field theory operators

> Chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) provides a framework for obtaining internucleon interactions in a systematically improvable fashion from first principles, while also providing for the derivation of consistent electroweak current operators. In this work, we apply consistently derived interactions and currents towards calculating the magnetic dipole moments of the $A=3$ systems Triton and Helium-3. We focus here on LENPIC interactions obtained using semilocal coordinate-space (SCS) regularization. Starting from the momentum-space representation of the LENPIC $χ$EFT vector current, we derive the SCS-regularized magnetic dipole operator up through N2LO. We then carry out no-core shell model calculations for Triton and Helium-3 systems, using the SCS LENPIC interaction at N2LO in $χ$EFT, and evaluate the magnetic dipole moments obtained using the consistently derived one-nucleon and two-nucleon electromagnetic currents. As anticipated by prior results with $χ$EFT currents, the current corrections through N2LO provide improved, but not yet complete, agreement with experiment for the Triton and Helium-3 magnetic dipole moments.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory (χEFT) magnetic dipole operator derived through N2LO, when combined with consistently derived LENPIC semilocal coordinate-space (SCS) regularized interactions, provides improved but not yet complete agreement with experimental magnetic dipole moments for Triton and Helium-3, with the remaining discrepancy indicating that higher-order contributions to the current operator are needed.

## Method

The authors derived the SCS-regularized magnetic dipole operator from the momentum-space representation of the LENPIC χEFT vector current up through N2LO, including one-nucleon (1N) and two-nucleon (2N) electromagnetic currents. They then performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for the A=3 systems Triton and Helium-3 using the SCS LENPIC interaction at N2LO, with wave functions obtained from both 2N-only and 2N+3N potentials, and evaluated the magnetic dipole moments using the consistently derived currents. The calculations were carried out for two regulator parameters (R = 0.9 fm and R = 1.0 fm), and the effects of similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of the potential were also investigated.

## Key numbers

- Triton magnetic dipole moment with LENPIC 2N potential (R = 1.0 fm, no SRG): μ^IA = 2.640 μ_N, μ^MEC = 0.151 μ_N, μ^IA+MEC = 2.791 μ_N (experimental value: 2.979 μ_N)
- Helium-3 magnetic dipole moment with LENPIC 2N potential (R = 1.0 fm, no SRG): μ^IA = -1.801 μ_N, μ^MEC = -0.149 μ_N, μ^IA+MEC = -1.950 μ_N (experimental value: -2.128 μ_N)
- Triton with LENPIC 2N+3N potential (R = 1.0 fm, SRG α = 0.04 fm^4): μ^IA+MEC = 2.814 μ_N
- Helium-3 with LENPIC 2N+3N potential (R = 1.0 fm, SRG α = 0.04 fm^4): μ^IA+MEC = -1.962 μ_N
- Triton with LENPIC 2N potential (R = 0.9 fm, no SRG): μ^IA+MEC = 2.802 μ_N
- Helium-3 with LENPIC 2N potential (R = 0.9 fm, no SRG): μ^IA+MEC = -1.968 μ_N
- The 2N meson-exchange current (MEC) contribution ranges from 0.139 to 0.173 μ_N for Triton and -0.138 to -0.172 μ_N for Helium-3 across the different calculations

## Context

This work extends the program of applying consistently derived χEFT interactions and currents to nuclear observables, following prior calculations using χEFT currents with phenomenological potentials by Song (2009) and Pastore (2013), and with the Norfolk potentials by Schiavilla (2019). The results enable future application of these consistently derived LENPIC currents to NCSM calculations throughout the range of nuclei accessible to the NCSM, while also demonstrating that the 2N MEC corrections improve but do not fully resolve the discrepancy with experiment, motivating the inclusion of higher-order (N2LO and beyond) current contributions.
