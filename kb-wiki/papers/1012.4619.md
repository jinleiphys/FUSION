# Study of the Decoherence of Entangled Kaons by the Interaction with Thermal Photons

> The KLOE-2 detector is a powerful tool to study the temporal evolution of quantum entangled pairs of kaons. The accuracy of such studies may in principle be limited by the interaction of neutral kaons with thermal photons present inside the detector. Therefore, it is crucial to estimate the probability of this effect and its influence on the interference patterns. In this paper we introduce the phenomenology of the interaction of photons with neutral kaons and present and discuss the obtained quantitative results.

## Key claim

The interaction of neutral kaons with thermal photons inside the KLOE-2 detector produces a decoherence probability so small (about \(1.5 \times 10^{-38}\)) that it is negligible compared to the expected statistical uncertainty of decoherence parameters, even though the systematic error on the interaction frequency spans more than an order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors model the inverse Compton scattering of thermal photons (black-body radiation at 300 K) on neutral kaons produced in \(\phi \to K^0 \bar{K}^0\) decay at KLOE-2. They compute the interaction frequency by integrating the differential Compton cross section (derived from the Primakoff effect, using published electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the kaon) over the Planck-distributed photon spectrum, accounting for Lorentz transformations between the laboratory and kaon rest frames. The resulting integral is evaluated numerically, and systematic uncertainties are estimated by varying the kaon polarizabilities and the ambient temperature.

## Key numbers

- Interaction frequency: \(\frac{dN}{dt} = 3.7 \times 10^{-31} \text{ s}^{-1}\) for kaons with kinetic energy \(E = 12\) MeV, Lorentz factor \(\gamma = 1.02412\), and velocity \(\beta = 0.21573\).
- Probability of a single Compton interaction during the kaon flight time of \(4.2 \times 10^{-8}\) s over a distance of 2.5 m: \(P = 1.5 \times 10^{-38}\).
- Systematic variation of \(\frac{dN}{dt}\) from about \(5 \times 10^{-32}\) to \(7.5 \times 10^{-31}\) s\(^{-1}\) depending on the assumed kaon polarizabilities (\(\alpha_K = 2.4 \times 10^{-49}\) m\(^3\), \(\beta_K = 10.3 \times 10^{-49}\) m\(^3\) from Ebert (year not given in text); alternative values from Wilcox (year not given) and Ivanov (year not given)) and temperature variations of \(\pm 20\) K around 300 K.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenology of decoherence in entangled kaon systems studied at the KLOE-2 detector, specifically addressing a potential background from thermal-photon interactions that had not been quantitatively estimated before. The result shows that this background is many orders of magnitude smaller than the statistical sensitivity expected for decoherence parameters as discussed by Amelino-Camelia (year not given in text). The calculation relies on kaon polarizabilities from Ebert (year not given) and cross-checked against values from Wilcox (year not given) and Ivanov (year not given), and it enables future precision tests of quantum mechanics and CPT symmetry with entangled kaons at KLOE-2 by ruling out thermal-photon decoherence as a significant systematic effect.
