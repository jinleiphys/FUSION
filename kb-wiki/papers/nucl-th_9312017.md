# Complex Conjugate Pairs in Stationary Sturmians

> Sturmian eigenstates specified by stationary scattering boundary conditions are particularly useful in contexts such as forming simple separable two nucleon t matrices, and are determined via solution of generalised eigenvalue equation using real and symmetric matrices. In general, the spectrum of such an equation may contain complex eigenvalues. But to each complex eigenvalue there is a corresponding conjugate partner. In studies using realistic nucleon--nucleon potentials, and in certain positive energy intervals, these complex conjugated pairs indeed appear in the Sturmian spectrum. However, as we demonstrate herein, it is possible to recombine the complex conjugate pairs and corresponding states into a new, sign--definite pair of real quantities with which to effect separable expansions of the (real) nucleon--nucleon reactance matrices.

# Complex Conjugate Pairs in Stationary Sturmians

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that complex conjugate pairs appearing in the stationary Sturmian spectrum for certain positive energy intervals can be recombined into a new pair of real, sign-definite quantities, enabling separable expansions of the real nucleon-nucleon reactance matrices even when such complex pairs occur.

## Method
Stationary Sturmian eigenstates were obtained by solving a generalized eigenvalue equation with real, symmetric matrices in momentum space using Gauss-Laguerre quadrature. For realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions where the potential matrix is not sign definite, complex conjugate pairs appear in the spectrum. The authors developed a recombination procedure that transforms each complex conjugate pair and its associated eigenstates into two purely real quantities with opposite signs, which are then used in the Sturmian splitting method to produce separable expansions of the K matrices.

## Key numbers
- For a special analytic interaction, complex conjugate pairs appear when the on-shell momentum k is in the range (0.26–0.29) fm\(^{-1}\)
- For the \(^1S_0\) Reid soft core potential at 140 MeV, a complex conjugate pair appears with value \((-0.33 \pm i 0.02)\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- The complex conjugate pair for the Reid potential disappears between 189 and 190 MeV, being replaced by two real eigenvalues at 190 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Sturmian splitting method (SSM) developed by Pisent, Amos, Dortmans, and Canton (1993) for forming separable expansions of nucleon-nucleon K matrices. It addresses a pathology encountered when using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, where complex conjugate pairs appear in the stationary Sturmian spectrum at positive energies. The recombination technique enables the SSM to handle cases where previous separable expansion methods by Bartnik, Haberzettl, and Sandhas (1986) and others encountered difficulties due to vanishing on-shell phase shifts, as noted by Canton, Pisent, and Rawitscher (1990) and Dortmans and Amos (1991).
