# Hadronic eta and eta-prime decays

> The hadronic decays eta, eta-prime -> 3 pi and eta-prime -> eta pi pi are investigated within the framework of U(3) chiral effective field theory in combination with a relativistic coupled-channels approach. Final state interactions are included by deriving s- and p-wave interaction kernels for meson-meson scattering from the chiral effective Lagrangian and iterating them in a Bethe-Salpeter equation. Very good overall agreement with currently available data on decay widths and spectral shapes is achieved.

## Key claim

The authors achieve very good overall agreement with available data on decay widths and spectral shapes for the hadronic decays η, η′ → 3π and η′ → ηππ by extending a previous chiral unitary approach to include p-wave interactions, but the approach does not guarantee exact unitarity of the resulting S-matrix and neglects three-body interactions, which become significant for η′ → 3π where phase space is about a factor of seven larger than for η → 3π.

## Method

The authors employ U(3) chiral effective field theory up to fourth chiral order combined with a relativistic coupled-channels approach, where s- and p-wave interaction kernels for meson-meson scattering are derived from the chiral effective Lagrangian and iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation to include final state interactions. The decay amplitudes are constructed by summing bubble chains to all orders in each of the three two-particle channels, with the differences between the unitarized T-matrix and the tree-level A-matrix inserted to avoid double-counting of tree graphs. Unknown low-energy constants and subtraction constants in the loop integrals are determined by fitting to experimental decay widths, branching ratios, Dalitz plot parameters, and meson-meson scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The η′ mass is 958 MeV, which is phenomenologically manifested as the pseudoscalar singlet not being a Goldstone boson due to the axial U(1) anomaly.
- The isospin-breaking parameter m_ε^2 = B(m_d - m_u) is expressed via Dashen's theorem as m_ε^2 = m_{K^0}^2 - m_{K^±}^2 + m_{π^±}^2 - m_{π^0}^2, up to corrections of O(e^2 p^2, (m_d - m_u) p^2).
- The widths of η → 3π and η′ → ηππ have been measured roughly at the 10% precision level, while for η′ → 3π^0 the experimental uncertainty is considerably larger and only an upper limit exists for Γ(η′ → π^+ π^- π^0).
- The regularization scale of the loop integrals G_{m\bar{m}} is set to μ = 1 GeV for all channels.
- The coefficient v_0^{(2)} related to the η′ mass in the chiral limit is constrained to the range 0.00183 GeV^4 to 0.00523 GeV^4.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Borasoy and Nissler (2004) by including p-wave interactions, which allows more realistic predictions for η′ → π^+ π^- π^0 where p-waves can yield sizable contributions to the decay width and Dalitz slope parameters. The analysis is timely in view of planned WASA at COSY and MAMI-C facilities that will provide higher statistics, and the authors incorporate new preliminary results from the KLOE Collaboration on η → 3π and the VES Collaboration on η′ → ηπ^+ π^- Dalitz plot distributions. The approach enables tighter constraints on the parameters of the chiral Lagrangian by simultaneously fitting meson-meson scattering phase shifts along with available data on η and η′ hadronic decays.
