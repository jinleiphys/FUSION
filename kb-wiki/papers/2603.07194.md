# Shape phase transition, coexistence and mixing in the $^{98-106}$Ru isotopes

> The deformation properties within the $^{98-106}$Ru even-even isotopic chain, are investigated by means of the Covariant Density Functional Theory with a Density-Dependent Point-Coupling X parametrization. The considered nuclei are found to exhibit very shallow prolate and triaxial ground state deformation. This information is used to ascertain their dynamical behavior within prolate $γ$-stable and $γ$-unstable instances of a phenomenological Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with an octic potential in the axial deformation variable. The comparative study of the low-lying collective states, revealed the presence of a shape phase transition from low to high deformation, as well as evidence of shape coexistence and mixing between spherical vibrator, $γ$-unstable or prolate configurations in ground and excited states. It is also shown that the effect of shape coexistence and mixing on the $γ$-band states can account to some extent for the typical $γ$-unstable staggering even in prolate $γ$-stable conditions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $^{98-106}$Ru isotopic chain exhibits a shape phase transition from low to high deformation, along with shape coexistence and mixing between spherical vibrator, $\gamma$-unstable, and prolate configurations in ground and excited states. The key hedge is that the effect of shape coexistence and mixing on the $\gamma$-band states can account *to some extent* for the typical $\gamma$-unstable staggering even in prolate $\gamma$-stable conditions, meaning the explanation is partial rather than complete.

## Method

The authors used Covariant Density Functional Theory (CDFT) with the Density-Dependent Point-Coupling X (DD-PCX) parametrization to compute potential energy surfaces and ground-state deformations for the even-even $^{98-106}$Ru isotopes. They then solved a Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with an octic potential in the axial deformation variable $\beta$ for both $\gamma$-unstable and prolate $\gamma$-stable cases, using a numerical basis of Bessel functions of the first kind. The $\gamma$-stable prolate solution is an original contribution of this study, employing a $\gamma$-potential with a localized minimum at $\gamma=0^\circ$ and a small-angle approximation.

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ values from CDFT with DD-PCX: $^{98}$Ru (prolate), $^{100}$Ru (prolate), $^{102}$Ru (increasing triaxial), $^{104}$Ru (increasing triaxial), $^{106}$Ru (increasing triaxial); these are compared with BMH predictions and ENSDF database values, with good agreement for $^{98}$Ru, $^{100}$Ru, and $^{104}$Ru, and close agreement for $^{102}$Ru and $^{106}$Ru.
*   The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter used in the DD-PCX parametrization is $\rho_{\mathrm{sat}} = 0.152~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$.
*   The pairing strengths for the separable pairing interaction are $G_n = -800.663126037$ MeV fm$^3$ and $G_p = -773.776776597$ MeV fm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape phase transitions and coexistence in Ru isotopes using quasi-exactly solvable methods for the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian with sextic potential (Budaca 2016, Levai 2018, Lahbas 2020) and the Generalized Collective Model (Troltenier 1994). It also complements CDFT calculations with DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 parametrizations (Bassem 2018) and Interacting Boson Model calculations with configuration mixing (Barbecho 2020). The new numerical solution for the prolate $\gamma$-stable case with an octic potential enables a more general treatment of shape coexistence and mixing than quasi-exact methods, and the concurrent application of $\gamma$-unstable and prolate solutions allows capturing coexistence between triaxial ($\gamma$-unstable) and prolate shapes.
