# Nuclear matter EOS with light clusters within the mean-field approximation

> The crust of a neutron star is essentially determined by the low-density region ($ρ<ρ_0\approx0.15-0.16\unit{fm}^{-3}$) of the equation of state. At the bottom of the inner crust, where the density is $ρ\lesssim0.1ρ_0$, the formation of light clusters in nuclear matter will be energetically favorable at finite temperature. At very low densities and moderate temperatures, the few body correlations are expected to become important and light nuclei like deuterons, tritons, helions and $α$-particles will form. Due to Pauli blocking, these clusters will dissolve at higher densities $ρ\gtrsim 0.1ρ_0$. The presence of these clusters influences the cooling process and quantities, such as the neutrino emissivity and gravitational waves emission. The dissolution density of these light clusters, treated as point-like particles, will be studied within the Relativistic Mean Field approximation. In particular, the dependence of the dissolution density on the clusters-meson couplings is studied.

## Key claim
Light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles) treated as point-like particles within the Relativistic Mean Field approximation dissolve at densities above about 0.1 times nuclear saturation density, and their dissolution density depends primarily on the isoscalar meson-cluster couplings (sigma and omega), not on the rho-meson coupling.

## Method
The authors include light clusters as point-like particles in a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with constant meson-cluster couplings. They solve the mean-field equations of motion at zero temperature to determine the dissolution density of each cluster as a function of the coupling parameters, then constrain the couplings using known dissolution densities from quantum statistical approaches, in-medium binding energies, and experimental Mott points at T=5 MeV. Finally, they calculate particle fractions in chemical equilibrium at finite temperature for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 approximately 0.15-0.16 fm^{-3}
- Cluster formation becomes energetically favorable at densities rho less than about 0.1 rho_0
- Nucleon mass M = 938.918695 MeV
- Triton and helion masses are given by M_{t,h} = A_{t,h} * M - B_{t,h}, where B_{t,h} are binding energies from Ferreira (2012)
- At T=5 MeV and T=10 MeV, particle fractions are calculated for symmetric (Y_p=0.5) and asymmetric (Y_p=0.3) nuclear matter
- For symmetric nuclear matter at T=10 MeV, the deuteron fraction is the largest fraction and the alpha fraction the smallest
- In neutron-rich matter (Y_p=0.3), deuterons have the largest fraction and tritons the second largest at both T=5 and T=10 MeV

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean-field treatment of nuclear matter by including light clusters as explicit degrees of freedom, following the quantum statistical approach of Roepke (2009) and the cluster description of Typel (2010). The results enable improved modeling of the neutron star crust equation of state, particularly for cooling processes, neutrino emissivity, and gravitational wave emission. The authors note that more experimental Mott points at additional temperatures would allow determination of the temperature dependence of the meson-cluster couplings, and that the in-medium binding energies of Typel (2010) could be reproduced with temperature-dependent couplings.
