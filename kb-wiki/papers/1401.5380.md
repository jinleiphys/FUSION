# Heavy hybrid stars from multi-quark interactions

> We explore the possibility of obtaining heavy hybrid stars within the framework of the two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with 8-quark interactions in the scalar and in the vector channel. The main impact of the 8-quark scalar channel is to reduce the onset of quark matter, while the 8-quark vector channel acts to stiff the equation of state at high densities. Within the parameter space where the 4-quark vector channel is small, and the 8-quark vector channel sizeable, stable stars with $2 M_\odot$ and above are found to hold quark matter in their cores.

## Key claim

Heavy hybrid stars with masses of 2 solar masses and above can be obtained within the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model when 8-quark interactions are included, provided the 4-quark vector channel is kept small while the 8-quark vector channel is sizeable; the 8-quark scalar channel reduces the onset density of quark matter, while the 8-quark vector channel stiffens the equation of state at high densities.

## Method

The author uses a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with 4-quark and 8-quark interactions in both scalar and vector channels, solved in the mean-field approximation. The quark matter equation of state is obtained from the thermodynamic potential, and a Maxwell construction is used for the phase transition between the DD2 nuclear matter equation of state and quark matter. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain static, spherically symmetric hybrid star configurations.

## Key numbers

- For the parameter set with $\eta_2 = 0.05$ (small 4-quark vector coupling) and $\eta_4 = 0.0$ (no 8-quark vector coupling), the onset of quark matter occurs at an energy density around $\epsilon \simeq 500$ MeV/fm$^3$.
- With $\eta_2 = 0.05$ and $\eta_4 = 5.0$, the equation of state becomes acausal (speed of sound exceeds the speed of light) already at $\epsilon \sim 2000$ MeV/fm$^3$.
- Values of $\eta_4$ up to $\eta_4 \sim 2.0$ are sufficient to cover the experimental mass windows of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 (both around $2 M_\odot$).
- The extreme case with $\eta_4 = 5.0$ yields a maximum mass of $M \sim 2.25 M_\odot$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, where large vector channels were used for stiffness (Hanauske, 2001) and shifts in vacuum energy or superconductivity were needed for low onset (Pagliara, 2007; Lenzi, 2012; Klahn, 2013). The author proposes an alternative mechanism using higher-dimensional operators, building on the observation by Osipov (2006, 2007) that including higher scalar interactions can reduce the critical temperature in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The results enable future Bayesian inversion of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using precise mass and radius measurements, and motivate further study of vector channel couplings using lattice QCD results at finite temperature and imaginary chemical potential.
