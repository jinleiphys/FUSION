# Measuring differential flow angle fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions

> Recently studies of the differential nature of the flow angle fluctuations, known as event plane angular decorrelation, indicated that measurements that assume a common symmetry plane may need to consider the flow angle fluctuations effect. Using the HIJING and AMPT models, it is shown that the flow angle fluctuations measurements, obtained with the two- and three-subevents correlation method, could have significant non-flow effects associated with long- and short-range non-flow correlations. The current study demonstrates the four-subevents cumulant methods' ability to reduce the non-flow effects. It is further argued that the measurements using the four-subevents correlation method can be used to provide accurate quantification of the differential flow angle fluctuations.

## Key claim

Using HIJING and AMPT model simulations, the paper demonstrates that the four-subevents cumulant method can reduce non-flow effects in differential flow angle fluctuation measurements to less than 0.5%, whereas two- and three-subevents methods retain significant contamination from both short- and long-range non-flow correlations.

## Method

The study used HIJING (which contains only non-flow correlations) and AMPT (v2.26t9b) models to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 5020 GeV. Charged particles with 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 5.0 were analyzed using two-, three-, and four-subevents correlation methods to construct four-particle correlations and cumulants. The differential flow angle fluctuations were quantified using the r2{2} and r2{4} correlators, which compare type-I and type-II four-particle correlations to extract the cosine of the flow angle difference.

## Key numbers

- Non-flow effects in the four-subevents cumulant method are reduced to less than 0.5% in HIJING model simulations (model dependent)
- Charged particles were selected with 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 5.0 for both Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5020 GeV
- AMPT model parameters used: alpha_s = 0.47 and screening mass mu = 3.41 fm^-1 for partonic scattering cross-section
- The two- and three-subevents type-II four-particle correlations show strong centrality and pT dependence in HIJING, indicating non-flow contamination that increases with centrality and mean pT

## Context

This work extends studies of flow angle fluctuations (event plane angular decorrelation) previously investigated by Jia (2014), Bozek (2010, 2021), and the ALICE collaboration (2022). The paper directly addresses the concern that recent ALICE measurements using the two-subevents method with a small eta gap may be contaminated by non-flow effects. The four-subevents method proposed here enables more accurate quantification of the pT dependence of flow angle fluctuations, which can be implemented at RHIC's STAR experiment using the Event Plane Detector (EPD).
