# Direct Photons in Hydrodynamic Modeling of Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> We review direct photons in the phenomenology of nuclear collisions at relativistic energies. Direct photons carry information about the space-time evolution of nuclear collisions because the QCD medium is transparent against colorless particles. After a status summary of theoretical and experimental studies, transverse momentum spectra and azimuthal momentum anisotropies of direct photons are studied in the context of the hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic nuclear collisions with emphasis on pre-equilibrium photons. It is implied that pre-equilibrium photons can be as important as thermal and prompt photons for comprehensive understanding of direct photon production.

## Key claim

Pre-equilibrium photons can be as important as thermal and prompt photons for a comprehensive understanding of direct photon production in relativistic nuclear collisions, though the precise mechanism of thermalization and hydrodynamization in the pre-equilibrium stage is still not quantitatively understood.

## Method

The author reviews direct photon production in relativistic nuclear collisions by integrating thermal photon emission rates over space-time volume using flow and temperature profiles from hydrodynamic model calculations, with the QGP photon emission rate based on the perturbative approach by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe and the hadronic phase rate based on massive Yang-Mills theory. Pre-equilibrium photons are estimated by parametrically scaling the bottom-up thermalization process with turbulent thermalization into three stages, using the Berges-Reygers-Tanji-Venugopalan model with self-similar scaling solutions for parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The quark-hadron crossover transition occurs around T_c ~ 155-160 MeV in the limit of vanishing chemical potentials, from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations
- Effective temperature extracted from direct photon spectra at RHIC in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: T = 239 ± 25 (stat) ± 7 (syst) MeV for 0-20% centrality
- Effective temperature at LHC in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: T = 297 ± 12 (stat) ± 41 (syst) MeV for 0-20% centrality
- The pre-equilibrium stage must end before 1 fm/c for successful application of the hydrodynamic model
- The typical time scale of the hydrodynamic stage is O(1)-O(10) fm/c
- Most hydrodynamic simulations undershoot direct photon elliptic flow experimental data roughly by a factor of two around p_T ~ 2-3 GeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on direct photon phenomenology in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier studies of thermal photon production using ideal hydrodynamic models by Alam (2000), Srivastava (2000), and Turbide (2003), and addressing the "photon puzzle" where measured direct photon elliptic flow exceeds predictions from most hydrodynamic models. The work enables future quantitative comparisons between theoretical estimations and experimental data from RHIC and LHC heavy-ion programs, particularly for understanding pre-equilibrium dynamics and the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
