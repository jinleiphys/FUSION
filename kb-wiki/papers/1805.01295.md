# Exclusive production of pions and the pion distribution amplitude

> Considering, as a limit case, an approximately flat pion distribution amplitude, which is determined from the hardest, in momentum space, solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion wave function, we compute the pion transition form factor $F_{πγγ^{*}}(Q^{2})$ and the pion form factor $F_π(Q^2)$, taking into account the LO as well as NLO form of the hard coefficient function entering the leading-twist factorization formula. We also compute the exclusive photoproduction of pions pairs at high energies, $γγ\to π^{+}π^{-}$, where perturbative QCD can be used to compute the hard scattering matrix elements. We verify that the existent data for exclusive pion production can be reasonably described as a function of such flat distribution amplitude.

## Key claim

An approximately flat pion distribution amplitude (DA), derived from the hardest possible asymptotic solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion wave function in momentum space, can reasonably describe existing data for exclusive pion production processes, provided that dynamical quark and gluon masses are included and that the DA is defined at a very low normalization scale where perturbative evolution is effectively frozen.

## Method

The authors compute the pion transition form factor \(F_{\pi\gamma\gamma^{*}}(Q^{2})\) and the pion electromagnetic form factor \(F_{\pi}(Q^{2})\) using leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) hard-scattering amplitudes within the leading-twist factorization formula, with the flat DA parametrized as \(\varphi_{\pi}(x;\epsilon) \propto x^{\epsilon}(1-x)^{\epsilon}\) where \(\epsilon \approx 0.0248\). They also compute the total and differential cross sections for exclusive \(\gamma\gamma \to \pi^{+}\pi^{-}\) photoproduction at high energies, incorporating dynamical gluon masses into the gluon propagator and running coupling, and applying a smooth cutoff factor to suppress the perturbative QCD contribution at low energies.

## Key numbers

- The flat pion DA parameter is \(\epsilon \approx 0.0248\) (Eq. 13), obtained from the hardest Bethe-Salpeter solution.
- The dynamical quark mass is taken as \(m_q = 250\) MeV and the dynamical gluon mass as \(M_g = 600\) MeV for the form factor calculations.
- The BL limit for the pion transition form factor is \(\sqrt{2}f_{\pi} \approx 0.185\) GeV, with \(f_\pi = 131\) MeV.
- The BaBar data for \(F_{\pi\gamma\gamma^{*}}(Q^{2})\) cover the range \(Q^{2} = 4\) to \(40\) GeV\(^2\).
- The pion form factor changes by about 15% when \(m_q\) varies from 200 to 250 MeV, and by about 19% when \(m_g\) varies from 500 to 700 MeV.
- The optimal normalization scale for the flat DA is \(\mu^2 = e^{-1/0.0248} Q^2 \sim 10^{-8} Q^2\), which for the highest BaBar/Belle \(Q^2\) gives \(\mu^2 \sim 4 \times 10^{-7}\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Fagundes, Luna, Natale, and Pelaez (2017) which first proposed the flat DA from the hardest Bethe-Salpeter solution, and follows the approach of Radyushkin (2009) who argued that a flat DA can describe the BaBar data for the pion transition form factor. The results are contrasted with the asymptotic DA \(\varphi_{\pi}^{as}(x)=6x(1-x)\) of Efremov and Radyushkin (1980) and Lepage and Brodsky (1980), and with the BL limit. The paper enables a consistent description of multiple exclusive pion processes (transition form factor, electromagnetic form factor, and two-photon pair production) using a single flat DA derived from QCD dynamics, and suggests that non-perturbative evolution dominates at currently accessible energies, with perturbative evolution only setting in at far higher scales.
