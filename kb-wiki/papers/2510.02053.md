# The sharpness of the quark-hadron transition and the properties of hybrid stars

> We investigate the effects of the sharpness of the phase transition between hadronic matter and quark matter on various properties of neutron stars. We construct hybrid equations of state by combining a hadronic model with a quark model using a Gaussian function. This approach introduces a smooth transition characterized by two parameters: one representing the overpressure relative to the first-order phase transition point, and the other related to the range over which the hybrid region extends in baryon chemical potential. We find that the sharpness of the phase transition significantly influences the equation of state, which can deviate by several tens of $\text{MeV fm}^{-3}$ from the one with a sharp first-order transition. The speed of sound exhibits diverse behaviors, including drastic drops, pronounced peaks, and oscillatory patterns, depending on the sharpness parameters. In terms of stellar structure, while the maximum neutron star mass remains largely unaffected by the sharpness of the phase transition, the stellar radii can vary significantly. Smoother transitions lead to a leftward shift (up to 1 km) of the mass-radius curve segment corresponding to hybrid stars. The tidal deformability decreases with smoother transitions, especially for higher-mass stars. Our results are quite general and do not qualitatively depend on the specific hadronic and quark matter models employed. In fact, the hybrid equation of state and stellar properties derived from microscopic models of quark-hadron pasta phases display the same behavior as described above.

## Key claim
The sharpness of the quark-hadron phase transition in hybrid stars significantly influences the equation of state and stellar radii, but the maximum neutron star mass remains largely unaffected regardless of whether the transition is sharp or smooth.

## Method
The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining a generalized piecewise polytropic hadronic model with a vector MIT bag model for quark matter, using a Gaussian function to smoothly interpolate between the two phases. The smoothing was controlled by two parameters: δ₀ representing the overpressure relative to the first-order transition point, and σ representing the width of the transition region in baryon chemical potential. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute stellar structure properties and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- Pressure differences between smooth and sharp transition models lie within 2–10 MeV fm⁻³ at the critical chemical potential μc, compared to P(μc) ≈ 200 MeV fm⁻³
- For the smoothest transition (δ₀ = 10 MeV fm⁻³, σ = 150 MeV), the hybrid region extends from 500 to 1500 MeV fm⁻³ in energy density
- Smoother transitions cause a leftward shift of up to 1 km in the mass-radius curve for maximum-mass hybrid stars
- For the (10, 150) parametrization, a star with mass ~1.8 M⊙ has a radius approximately half a kilometer smaller than a purely hadronic star of the same mass
- The speed of sound in quark matter approaches approximately 0.4c from below in the soft hadronic model

## Context
This work extends previous investigations of hybrid star properties by Han (2018) and Pereira (2022), who varied the energy density jump and pressure gap at the transition, respectively. The authors show that their Gaussian smoothing approach produces behavior similar to microscopic models of quark-hadron pasta phases, as studied by Mariani (2023). The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints from pulsar masses, NICER radius measurements, and gravitational wave events like GW170817, while leaving open the question of whether the transition in nature is first-order or a crossover.
