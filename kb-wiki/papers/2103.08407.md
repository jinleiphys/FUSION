# Chiral susceptibility in dense thermo-magnetic QCD medium within HTL approximation

> We have computed the chiral susceptibility in quark-gluon plasma in presence of finite chemical potential and weak magnetic field within hard thermal loop approximation. First we construct the massive effective quark propagator in a thermomagnetic medium. Then we obtain completely analytic expression for the chiral susceptibility in weak magnetic field approximation. In the absence of magnetic field the thermal chiral susceptibility increases in presence of finite chemical potential. The effect of thermomagnetic correction is found to be very marginal as temperature is the dominant scale in weak field approximation.

## Key claim

The authors compute the chiral susceptibility in a quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potential and weak magnetic field using hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, finding that the thermomagnetic correction is "very marginal" because temperature remains the dominant scale in the weak-field regime, and the increase in susceptibility due to the magnetic field is small.

## Method

The authors first construct the massive effective quark propagator in a thermomagnetic medium by computing the fermion self-energy structure functions up to order \((q_f B)^2\) in the presence of chemical potential, using the scale hierarchy \(\sqrt{|q_f B|} < gT < T\). They then obtain a completely analytic expression for the chiral susceptibility by expanding the HTL-resummed propagator in the coupling constant up to order \(g^4\) and performing the sum-integrals, followed by renormalization in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.

## Key numbers

* The thermal chiral susceptibility at zero magnetic field and zero chemical potential is \(\chi_c = \frac{N_c N_f}{6} T^2\) at leading order, with the free value recovered asymptotically at high temperature.
* The thermal mass is \(m_{\text{th}}^2 = \frac{g^2 C_F T^2}{8} \big( 1 + 4 \hat{\mu}^2 \big)\), where \(\hat{\mu} = \mu / (2\pi T)\) and \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2 N_c)\).
* The magnetic-field-dependent effective mass squared is \(m_{\text{eff}}^2 = 4 g^2 C_F \frac{q_f B}{16\pi^2} \big[ -\frac{1}{4} \aleph(z) - \frac{\pi T}{2 m_f} - \frac{\gamma_E}{2} \big]\), with \(\aleph(z)\) defined in the text.
* The renormalized chiral susceptibility includes terms of order \((q_f B)^2\) and \((q_f B)^4\), with the magnetic correction scaled by \(m'^2_{\text{eff}} = \frac{g^2 C_F (q_f B)^2 T}{32 \pi m_f^3}\).
* The plots use magnetic field strength \(|eB| = m_\pi^2 = 0.14^2\) GeV\(^2\) and chemical potentials of 0, 100, and 200 MeV, with temperatures shown from around 0.14 GeV upward.

## Context

This work extends the HTL calculation of chiral susceptibility by Chakraborty (2002) from zero chemical potential to finite chemical potential and adds a weak magnetic field, using the effective quark propagator in a thermomagnetic medium developed by Das (2017). The results are relevant for understanding the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density and weak magnetic fields, such as those encountered in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and they enable comparisons with lattice QCD studies (e.g., Bali 2012) and NJL model calculations (e.g., Das 2019) that address magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis.
