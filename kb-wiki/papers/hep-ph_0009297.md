# Nucleon Transversity Distribution from Azimuthal Spin Asymmetry in Pion Electroproduction

> The azimuthal asymmetry observed by the HERMES collaboration in semi-inclusive pion production in deep inelastic scattering of unpolarized positron on the longitudinally polarized proton target, can provide information of the quark transversity distributions of the nucleon. We show that the quark transversity distributions predicted both by the light-cone quark-spectator-diquark model and by a pQCD inspired model can give consistent descriptions of the available HERMES data for the analyzing powers $A_{UL}^{\sin ϕ}$ and $A_{UL}^{\sin 2 ϕ}$ for $π^+$ and $π^-$ productions. We also show that the two models give similar predictions of $A_{UL}^{\sin ϕ}$ for $π^+$ production, whereas they give very different predictions of $A_{UL}^{\sin ϕ}$ for $π^-$ production at large $x$. Further precision measurement of $A_{UL}^{\sin ϕ}$ for $π^-$ production can provide a decisive test of different models.

# Nucleon Transversity Distribution from Azimuthal Spin Asymmetry in Pion Electroproduction

## Key claim

The quark transversity distributions predicted by both the light-cone quark-spectator-diquark model and a pQCD inspired model can give consistent descriptions of the available HERMES data for the analyzing powers $A_{UL}^{\sin \phi}$ and $A_{UL}^{\sin 2 \phi}$ for $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ production, but the two models give very different predictions of $A_{UL}^{\sin \phi}$ for $\pi^-$ production at large $x$ (above $x \ge 0.3$), which could provide a decisive test of the models. The hedge is that the analysis relies on simplifying assumptions including Wandzura-Wilczek type approximations and the use of only favored fragmentation functions.

## Method

The authors calculated the analyzing powers $A_{UL}^{\sin \phi}$ and $A_{UL}^{\sin 2 \phi}$ for semi-inclusive pion electroproduction using expressions proportional to ratios of quark transversity distributions and fragmentation functions, following the theoretical framework of Efremov et al. (2000). They employed two models for the quark transversity distributions: the light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model with Melosh-Wigner rotation factors, and a pQCD inspired model based on counting rule predictions with parameters constrained by sum rules and the Bjorken sum rule. The unpolarized quark distributions were taken from the CTEQ5 set 1 parametrization at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$, and only valence quark contributions were included in the numerators.

## Key numbers

- The integrated quark transversities for the proton, obtained from sum rules assuming the two model correction factors both equal to 1, are $\delta U = 1.04$ and $\delta D = -0.39$, with $\Delta U = 0.75$ and $\Delta D = -0.45$ from the Bjorken sum rule $\Gamma^p - \Gamma^n = \frac{1}{6}(\Delta U - \Delta D) \approx 0.2$ and the quark helicity sum $\Sigma = \Delta U + \Delta D + \Delta S \approx 0.3$.
- The pQCD inspired model predicts $\delta d(x)/d(x) \to 1$ as $x \to 1$, while the quark-diquark model predicts $\delta d(x)/d(x) \to -1/3$ at $x \to 1$.
- The two models give different predictions for $A_{UL}^{\sin \phi}$ for $\pi^-$ production in the region $x \ge 0.3$, with opposite signs at large $x$.
- The input quark distributions were taken at $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$ from CTEQ5.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of single-spin asymmetries observed by the HERMES collaboration in semi-inclusive pion production, following theoretical proposals by Collins (1993), Kotzinian (1995), Mulders and Tangerman (1996), Jaffe et al. (1998), and Anselmino et al. (1995) to access quark transversity distributions via the Collins effect. The paper directly builds on the analysis of Efremov et al. (2000) for relating the measured analyzing powers to transversity distributions, and compares two specific models: the light-cone quark-spectator-diquark model of Ma (1996) and a pQCD counting rule analysis following Brodsky et al. (1995). The results enable future precision measurements of $A_{UL}^{\sin \phi}$ for $\pi^-$ production at large $x$ to discriminate between these competing theoretical predictions for the $d$ quark transversity distribution.
