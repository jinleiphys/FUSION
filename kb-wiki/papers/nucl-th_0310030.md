# Spin-Isospin Resonances and Neutron Skin of Nuclei

> The Gamow-Teller resonances (GTR) and isobaric analog states (IAS) of a sequence of even-even Sn target nuclei are calculated by using the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model plus proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The calculation reproduces the experimental data on ground-state properties, as well as the excitation energies of the isovector excitations. It is shown that the isotopic dependence of the energy spacings between the GTR and IAS provides direct information on the evolution of neutron skin-thickness along the Sn isotopic chain. A new method is suggested for determining the difference between the radii of the neutron and proton density distributions along an isotopic chain, based on measurement of the excitation energies of the GTR relative to the IAS.

## Key claim

The energy difference between the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and the isobaric analog state (IAS) in Sn isotopes is directly correlated with the neutron-skin thickness, providing a new method to determine the difference between neutron and proton rms radii along an isotopic chain. The method is not completely model independent, but it does not require additional assumptions beyond the theoretical curve relating the energy spacing to the skin thickness.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations for the ground states of even-even Sn isotopes (A=112-124) using the NL3 effective interaction and the Gogny D1S pairing interaction. They then applied the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) in the canonical single-nucleon basis to compute the excitation energies of the Gamow-Teller resonances and isobaric analog states, including the pion-nucleon pseudo-vector coupling and a Landau-Migdal zero-range force with strength parameter g0' = 0.6 in the spin-isospin channel.

## Key numbers

- The calculated energy spacings between GTR and IAS for Sn isotopes with A=112-124 are in very good agreement with experimental data from the (³He,t) charge-exchange reaction (Pham 1995).
- For all Sn isotopes except ¹¹²Sn, the agreement between calculated and experimental IAS excitation energies is better than approximately 0.5 MeV.
- The spin-orbit potential in neutron-rich nuclei is reduced by up to approximately 30% in the surface region, based on RHB calculations with the NL3 parameter set.
- The calculation used the pion mass mπ = 138 MeV and the pion-nucleon coupling constant fπ²/4π = 0.08.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic RPA calculations of Gamow-Teller resonances in doubly closed-shell nuclei (Con. 1998; Ma. 2003) to open-shell Sn isotopes using a fully self-consistent RHB+RQRPA framework. It builds on the method of Krasznahorkay et al. (1999), who used spin-dipole resonance cross sections to extract neutron-skin thickness, and on the relativistic mean-field predictions of a strong isospin dependence of the spin-orbit potential (LVR 1997; LVR 1998). The proposed method enables determination of neutron-skin thickness from charge-exchange reaction measurements, which could complement parity-violating electron scattering experiments (Hor. 1999) and atomic parity-violation studies (VLR 2000) for constraining neutron density distributions.
