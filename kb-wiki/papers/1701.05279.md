# Calculation of Relativistic Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials in Three-Dimensions

> In this paper, we have applied a three-dimensional approach for calculation of the relativistic nucleon-nucleon potential. The quadratic operator relation between the non-relativistic and the relativistic nucleon-nucleon interactions is formulated as a function of relative two-nucleon momentum vectors, which leads to a three-dimensional integral equation. The integral equation is solved by the iteration method, and the matrix elements of the relativistic potential are calculated from non-relativistic ones. Spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon potential is employed in the numerical calculations, and the numerical tests indicate that the two-nucleon observables calculated by the relativistic potential are preserved with high accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the iteration method of Kamada and Glöckle can be extended to a three-dimensional (3D) momentum-vector formulation to construct relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials from non-relativistic ones, without using partial-wave decomposition, and that the resulting relativistic potential preserves two-nucleon observables with high accuracy (relative differences below 0.01% for phase shifts and below 0.008% for total cross sections).

## Method

The quadratic operator relation connecting the non-relativistic and relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials is formulated as a 3D integral equation in momentum space, depending on the magnitudes of the relative momentum vectors and the angle between them. The equation is solved by iteration using the spin-independent Malfliet-Tjon I potential, with Gauss-Legendre quadrature for discretization and a hyperbolic-plus-linear mapping for the momentum integration domain. Convergence is accelerated by redefining the relativistic potential at each iteration as a linear combination of the last two successive iterations, with optimal averaging parameters alpha=4 and beta=1 requiring 8 iterations.

- Deuteron binding energy from non-relativistic MT-I potential: -2.23100 MeV; from relativistic potential: -2.23229 MeV; relative difference: 0.05782%
- Total elastic NN scattering cross sections at on-shell momentum p0=1 MeV: non-relativistic 15274.4 mb, relativistic 15273.3 mb, relative difference 0.00720%
- Total elastic NN scattering cross sections at p0=600 MeV: non-relativistic 56.9070 mb, relativistic 56.9111 mb, relative difference 0.00720%
- s-wave phase shift at p0=1 MeV: non-relativistic 178.399202 degrees, relativistic 178.399259 degrees, relative difference 0.00003%
- s-wave phase shift at p0=400 MeV: non-relativistic 8.220831 degrees, relativistic 8.220032 degrees, relative difference 0.00972%
- p-wave phase shift at p0=1 MeV: non-relativistic 0.000015228 degrees, relativistic 0.000015228 degrees, relative difference 0.0017533%
- p-wave phase shift at p0=600 MeV: non-relativistic 18.01488447 degrees, relativistic 18.01311044 degrees, relative difference 0.0098475%
- Number of iterations to converge: 8 with alpha=4, beta=1; 17 with alpha=1, beta=0; 18 with alpha=1, beta=1
- Momentum mesh: 100 points for momentum, 50 for spherical angle, 10 for azimuthal angle; integration domain up to p_max=60 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the iteration method of Kamada and Glöckle (2007) for constructing relativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials from non-relativistic ones, which was previously implemented only in partial-wave representation, to a fully three-dimensional momentum-vector approach. The 3D approach automatically includes all partial waves and is energy-independent in the number of equations, unlike partial-wave methods that require more partial waves at higher energies. This enables future extension to relativistic three-body scattering calculations with realistic spin-dependent interactions, building on prior 3D bound-state calculations by Hadizadeh and collaborators (2013, 2014) that showed relativistic effects reduce three-body binding energy by about 3%.
