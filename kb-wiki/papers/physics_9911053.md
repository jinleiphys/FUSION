# Low-energy quenching of positronium by helium

> Very low-energy scattering of orthopositronium by helium has been investigated for simultaneous study of elastic cross section and pick-off quenching rate using a model exchange potential. The present calculational scheme, while agrees with the measured cross section of Skalsey et al, reproduces successfully the parameter $^ 1Z_{\makebox{eff}}$, the effective number of electrons per atom in a singlet state relative to the positron. Together with the fact that this model potential also leads to an agreement with measured medium energy cross sections of this system, this study seems to resolve the long-standing discrepancy at low energies among different theoretical calculations and experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The authors use a model exchange potential to simultaneously reproduce the low-energy elastic cross section and the pick-off quenching parameter \(^1Z_{\text{eff}}\) for orthopositronium-helium scattering, claiming to resolve a long-standing discrepancy among theoretical calculations and experimental measurements, though they note the precise agreement of the static-exchange calculation with experiment is expected to be incidental.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for orthopositronium-helium scattering in the static-exchange approximation, using a nonlocal model exchange potential derived from a momentum-space formulation. The wave function is expanded in partial waves, and the resulting integro-differential equation is solved for the scattering phase shifts, from which elastic and momentum-transfer cross sections are computed. The parameter \(^1Z_{\text{eff}}\) is calculated from the short-range part of the Ps-He wave function using the formalism of Fraser and Kraidy, employing a two-term helium wave function.

## Key numbers

- Triplet Ps-He scattering length: 0.87 \(a_0\) (compared to 1.39 \(a_0\) from Drachman and Houston).
- Zero-energy cross sections from prior work vary from 3.3 Å² to 16.54 Å²; measured values range from 2.3 ± 0.4 Å² (at 0.915 eV, Skalsey et al.) to 11 ± 3 Å² (between 0 to 0.3 eV).
- Present calculation yields \(^1Z_{\text{eff}} = 0.11\) at low energy, consistent with the experimental value of 0.108 ± 0.01 from Duff and Heymann.
- Experimental \(^1Z_{\text{eff}}\) values: 0.108 ± 0.01, 0.135 ± 0.068, and 0.25 ± 25%.

## Context

This work extends the model exchange potential approach previously applied to electron-impact and positronium-impact scattering (targets: H, He, H₂, Ar, Ne) by Adhikari and collaborators, and directly addresses the discrepancy between prior static-exchange calculations (Barker and Bransden; Fraser and Kraidy) and experimental measurements (Skalsey et al.; Duff and Heymann). The study enables a consistent description of both cross sections and the pick-off quenching parameter, and suggests that the measurement of Duff and Heymann and the cross section measurements of Skalsey et al. and the medium-energy experiments are mutually consistent.
