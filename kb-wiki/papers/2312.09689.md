# Location of the QCD critical point predicted by holographic Bayesian analysis

> We present results for a Bayesian analysis of the location of the QCD critical point constrained by first-principles lattice QCD results at zero baryon density. We employ a holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton model of the QCD equation of state, capable of reproducing the latest lattice QCD results at zero and finite baryon chemical potential. Our analysis is carried out for two different parametrizations of this model, resulting in confidence intervals for the critical point location that overlap at one sigma. While samples of the prior distribution may not even predict a critical point, or produce critical points spread around a large region of the phase diagram, posterior samples nearly always present a critical point at chemical potentials of $μ_{Bc} \sim 550 - 630$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bayesian analysis of a holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton model, constrained by lattice QCD results at zero baryon density, predicts the QCD critical point to lie at baryon chemical potentials of approximately 550-630 MeV, with posterior samples nearly always producing a critical point even though prior samples may not predict one at all.

## Method

The authors employed a bottom-up holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model of the QCD equation of state, using two different parametrizations of the dilaton potential and Maxwell coupling (a polynomial-hyperbolic ansatz and a parametric ansatz). They performed a Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) Bayesian analysis, using differential evolution MCMC, to sample model parameter sets weighted by a Gaussian likelihood against lattice QCD results for entropy density and baryon susceptibility at vanishing baryon density from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration. For each posterior sample, the critical endpoint location was computed by following the procedure outlined in Hippert (2023).

## Key numbers

* The posterior distribution for the critical baryon chemical potential is mu_Bc ~ 550-630 MeV, representing the range where posterior samples nearly always predict a critical point.
* 20% of the prior distribution predicts no critical point, while a critical point is found in nearly all of the posterior distribution.
* The holographic model reproduces lattice QCD results at intermediate temperatures T ~ 100-500 MeV at both vanishing and finite baryon density.

## Context

This work extends the holographic modeling approach of Critelli (2017) and Rougemont (2023) by adding a systematic Bayesian inference framework to constrain the critical point location using lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2013) and Bellwied (2015). The results enable experimental searches at RHIC and FAIR to target a specific beam energy range derived from the critical chemical potential via the freeze-out line of Vovchenko (2015), and demonstrate that a critical point is statistically favored over models without one.
