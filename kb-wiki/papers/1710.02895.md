# Rotating Dirac fermions in a magnetic field in 1+2,3 dimensions

> We consider the effects of an external magnetic field on rotating fermions in 1+2,3 dimensions. The dual effect of a rotation parallel to the magnetic field causes a net increase in the fermionic density by centrifugation, which follows from the sinking of the particle lowest Landau level in the Dirac sea for free Dirac fermions. In 1+d = 2n dimensions, this effect is related to the chiral magnetic effect in 2n-2 dimensions. This phenomenon is discussed specifically for both weak and strong inter-fermion interactions in 1+2 dimensions. For QCD in 1+3 dimensions with Dirac quarks, we show that in the strongly coupled phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, this mechanism reveals itself in the form of an induced pion condensation by centrifugation. We use this observation to show that this effect causes a shift in the chiral condensate in leading order, and to discuss the possibility for the formation of a novel pion super-fluid phase in present heavy ion collisions at collider energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a rotation parallel to an external magnetic field causes a net increase in fermionic density through centrifugation, even in the absence of a chemical potential, due to the sinking of the particle lowest Landau level into the Dirac sea. This effect is conditional on the rotation being parallel to the magnetic field and is shown to vanish for any finite rotation in the free scalar condensate case in 1+2 dimensions.

## Method

The authors analyze free and interacting Dirac fermions in 1+2 and 1+3 dimensions by implementing a global rotation through a local metric with spin connections, then solving the Dirac equation in the presence of a parallel magnetic field using Landau level quantization with angular momentum eigenstates. For interacting fermions in 1+2 dimensions, they employ a 4-Fermi interaction model with U(2) symmetry and analyze the effective potential in the large N_c approximation using bosonization and loop expansion. For QCD in 1+3 dimensions, they use leading order chiral perturbation theory to study the effect on the chiral condensate and pion condensation.

## Key numbers

- For free Dirac fermions in 1+2 dimensions at zero temperature, the total number of particles from the lowest Landau level is exactly n_0 = sgn(Omega) * N, where N = eBS/(2*pi) is the LLL degeneracy
- In the weak coupling regime of interacting fermions in 1+2 dimensions, the critical rotation for destroying the mass gap is Omega_c = (g_r/(2N+1)) * (eB/Lambda), which for Lambda = 10*sqrt(eB) and N = 100 gives Omega_c = 0.000497*sqrt(eB)
- For the strong coupling regime in 1+2 dimensions with g_r = -4 and Lambda/sqrt(eB) = 5, the critical rotation is Omega_c approximately 0.008*Lambda
- At finite temperature with beta = 80/sqrt(eB) and mu = 0.007/sqrt(eB) in weak coupling, the critical rotation Omega = 0.0003*sqrt(eB) causes loss of the gapped solution, compared to mu_c = 0.02*sqrt(eB) at Omega = 0

## Context

This work extends the analysis of combined rotation and magnetic field effects on Dirac fermions previously studied by Yin (2015), Liao (2015), and Fukuda (2015) in 1+3 dimensions, and by Miransky (2015) for the 1+2 dimensional case. The paper provides a full analysis of free and interacting Dirac fermions in 1+2 dimensions at both weak and strong coupling, establishes a correspondence with anomalies in arbitrary dimensions, and discusses the possibility of pion condensation in off-central heavy ion collisions at collider energies. The results enable further investigation of novel pion super-fluid phases in heavy ion experiments and the rotational inhibition of magnetic catalysis.
