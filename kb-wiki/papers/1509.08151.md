# Emission of neutrino-antineutrino pairs by hadronic bremsstrahlung processes

> We review our recent calculations of neutrino-antineutrino pair production from bremsstrahlung processes in hadronic collisions at temperature and densities relevant for core-collapse supernovae. We focus on neutron-neutron and neutron-alpha collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews calculations showing that neutrino-antineutrino pair production from neutron-alpha bremsstrahlung can dominate over neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung for temperatures below 6 MeV, but only if the alpha-particle number density is comparable to the neutron number density.

## Method

The authors calculated the spin relaxation rate and energy-loss rate for neutrino-antineutrino pair production from bremsstrahlung in neutron-neutron and neutron-alpha collisions. For neutron-neutron collisions, they used chiral effective field theory interactions at different orders (LO through N3LO) and solved the Boltzmann equation in a relaxation time approximation. For neutron-alpha collisions, they constructed the scattering T-matrix from experimental phase shifts, including the resonant P-wave contributions, and computed the energy-loss rate as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- For neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung, calculations at N3LO differ from leading-order (one-pion-exchange) results by a factor of 2-3.
- The Fermi momentum k_F = 1.2 fm^{-1} corresponds to a density of about 1 x 10^{14} g cm^{-3}.
- The nuclear saturation density is rho_0 = 2.8 x 10^{14} g cm^{-3}.
- For neutron-alpha bremsstrahlung, the P-wave resonance enhances the contribution at temperatures in the range of 0.1-4 MeV.
- Neutron-alpha bremsstrahlung dominates over neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung for T < 6 MeV when alpha-particle density is comparable to neutron density.
- For T less than or equal to 2 MeV, the neutron-alpha contribution is over an order of magnitude larger than the neutron-neutron contribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Friman and Maxwell (1979) who used the Born approximation with one-pion-exchange potential, and by Hanhart et al. (2001) who used nucleon-nucleon phase shifts. The authors' previous work (Bacca et al., 2009, 2012) used chiral EFT for neutron-neutron collisions, while the neutron-alpha results (Sharma et al., 2015) are new. The findings enable inclusion of neutron-alpha bremsstrahlung in core-collapse supernova simulations to check whether the enhanced rate affects the predicted neutrino spectrum or the dynamics of dense matter outside the proto-neutron star.
