# Spin diffusive modes and thermal transport in neutron star crusts

> In this contribution we first review a method for obtaining the collective modes of pair-correlated neutron matter as found in a neutron star inner crust. We discuss two classes of modes corresponding to density and spin perturbations with energy spectra $ω= ω_0 + αq^2$, where $ω_0 = 2Δ$ is the threshold frequency and $Δ$ is the gap in the neutron fluid spectrum. For characteristic values of Landau parameters in neutron star crusts the exitonic density modes have $α< 0$, therefore they live below the pair-breaking continuum $ω_0$ and are undamped. For spin diffusive modes $α> 0$ and they exist above $ω_0$ which implies that these modes are damped. As an application of these findings we compute the thermal conductivity due to spin diffusive modes and show that it scales as $T^{1/2} \exp(-2ω_0/T)$ in the case where their two-by-two scattering cross-section is weakly dependent on temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin diffusive modes in neutron star crusts exist above the pair-breaking threshold (ω > ω₀ = 2Δ) and are therefore damped, unlike density excitonic modes which lie below the threshold and are undamped; the thermal conductivity from these spin diffusive modes scales as T^(1/2) exp(-2ω₀/T) under the condition that their two-by-two scattering cross-section is weakly dependent on temperature.

## Method

The authors use the Abrikosov-Gor'kov formalism with Matsubara imaginary-time propagators to compute the density and spin response functions of S-wave paired neutron matter, expanding these functions in small parameters such as q/k_F. They derive the collective mode spectra by solving the quasiparticle equation 1 - f^D_ph Re P(ω,q) = 0, where f^D_ph are Landau parameters for the particle-hole interaction in the density or spin channel. The thermal conductivity is then estimated by solving a linearized kinetic equation for the spin diffusive modes, truncating the expansion in associated Laguerre polynomials at the leading term.

## Key numbers

- The threshold frequency for pair-breaking is ω₀ = 2Δ, where Δ is the pairing gap; for the numerical example, Δ = 1.0 MeV.
- The numerical calculations use a Fermi momentum k_F = 1.0 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a density n = 0.22 n₀ (where n₀ is nuclear saturation density).
- The temperature in the numerical example is T/T_c = 0.5, with T_c = Δ(0)/1.76.
- The perturbative response functions are valid only for small momentum transfers q/k_F ≲ 0.3.
- The thermal conductivity estimate uses a constant cross-section approximation dσ/dΩ ≃ 1/(4k_F²), yielding κ ≃ √(2αT/π) k_F² exp(-2ω₀/T).

## Context

This work extends the Abrikosov-Gor'kov (1962) theory of dirty superconductors and the Fermi-liquid theory extensions by Larkin and Migdal (1967) and Leggett (1966) to neutron star crusts, following the numerical approach of Keller (2012). The paper distinguishes two classes of collective modes in superfluid neutron matter: undamped excitonic density modes (analogous to modes in semiconductors) and damped spin diffusive modes (analogous to rotons in superfluid helium). The results enable future studies of scattering and transport involving these collective modes, and the formalism is relevant to other fermionic systems including ultra-cold atomic vapors, nuclear matter, and deconfined quark matter.
