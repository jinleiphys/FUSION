# Contradicting effective mass scalings within the Skyrme energy density functional method

> The problem of the effective mass scaling in the single particle spectra calculated within the Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) method is studied. It is demonstrated that for specific pairs of orbitals the commonly anticipated isoscalar effective mass (m*) scaling of the single-particle level splittings is almost canceled by an implicit m*-scaling due to other parameters in the Skyrme EDF. This holds in particular for an indirect m*-scaling of the two-body spin-orbit strength making the theory essentially unpredictable with respect to single particle energies. It is argued that this unphysical property of the Skyrme EDF is a mere consequence of the strategies and datasets used to fit these functionals. The inclusion of certain single-particle spin-orbit splittings to fit the two-body spin-orbit and the tensor interaction strengths reinstates the conventional m*-scaling and improves the performance of the Skyrme EDF.

## Key claim

The commonly anticipated isoscalar effective mass scaling of single-particle level splittings in Skyrme energy density functionals is almost canceled for specific orbital pairs by an implicit effective mass scaling of other parameters, particularly the two-body spin-orbit strength, making the theory essentially unpredictable for single-particle energies unless the fitting strategy is changed.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional with eight different parameterizations (SLy4, SLy5, SIII, SkM*, SkXc, MSk1, SkP, SkO) on fully stretched high-spin terminating states in nuclei around A~44. They computed the energy difference between [f7/2^n] and [d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)] configurations and analyzed the correlation between the two-body spin-orbit strength and the effective mass parameter. They then refitted the tensor and two-body spin-orbit coupling constants using empirical f7/2-f5/2 spin-orbit splittings in 40Ca, 56Ni, and 48Ca and repeated the calculations.

## Key numbers

- For standard parameterizations with effective masses ranging from 0.7 to 1.0, the mean deviation of theoretical from experimental energy differences was approximately -550 keV with an rms deviation of about 70 keV, despite effective masses differing by up to 30%
- The experimental average energy difference Delta E_exp was approximately 5.5 MeV
- The anticipated naive effective mass scaling effect on the particle-hole excitation energy was estimated to exceed 1 MeV, more than an order of magnitude larger than the observed rms deviation
- For the refitted SkXc_LT parameterization (effective mass ~1.0), the mean deviation from data was only 33 keV
- A 5% variation in the spin-orbit coupling constant C^nabla J produced the largest change in Delta E, approximately 2.5 times larger than the contribution from the effective mass term
- A 1% variation in the bulk energy parameter C^rho impacted binding energies by approximately 15 MeV while affecting Delta E by only about +/-30 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on high-spin terminating states in the A~44 mass region (Zdu05, Zal07s) and demonstrates that the cancellation of effective mass scaling is an artifact of fitting strategies that prioritize bulk nuclear properties. The paper contradicts the assumption that standard Skyrme parameterizations provide reliable single-particle spectra and shows that including empirical spin-orbit splittings in the fitting procedure, as done in the companion work Zal08s, reinstates the conventional effective mass scaling. This enables future fine-tuning of the two-body spin-orbit and tensor strengths using spectroscopic data rather than bulk properties alone.
