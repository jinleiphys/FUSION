# Strongly Intensive Measures for Multiplicity Fluctuations

> The recently proposed two families of strongly intensive measures of fluctuations and correlations are studied within Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach to nucleus-nucleus collisions. We consider the measures $Δ^{Kπ}$ and $Σ^{Kπ}$ for kaon and pion multiplicities in Au+Au collisions in a wide range of collision energies and centralities. These strongly intensive measures appear to cancel the participant number fluctuations. This allows to enlarge the centrality window in the analysis of event-by-event fluctuations up to at least of 10% most central collisions. We also present a comparison of the HSD results with the data of NA49 and STAR collaborations. The HSD describes $Σ^{Kπ}$ reasonably well. However, the HSD results depend monotonously on collision energy and do not reproduce the bump-deep structure of $Δ^{Kπ}$ observed from the NA49 data in the region of the center of mass energy of nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 8÷12$ GeV. This fact deserves further studies. The origin of this `structure' is not connected with simple geometrical or limited acceptance effects, as these effects are taken into account in the HSD simulations.

# Strongly Intensive Measures for Multiplicity Fluctuations

## Key claim
The strongly intensive measures Δ^(Kπ) and Σ^(Kπ) cancel participant number fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions, allowing centrality windows up to at least 10% most central collisions; however, the HSD transport model reproduces Σ^(Kπ) reasonably well but fails to reproduce the bump-deep structure in Δ^(Kπ) observed by NA49 at √s_NN = 8–12 GeV, indicating this structure is not due to simple geometrical or limited acceptance effects.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event simulations of Au+Au collisions using the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach across a range of collision energies and impact parameters. They computed the scaled variances ω_K and ω_π for charged kaon (K⁺+K⁻) and pion (π⁺+π⁻) multiplicities, then constructed the strongly intensive measures Δ^(Kπ) and Σ^(Kπ) defined in Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2011). The HSD results were compared with the Model of Independent Sources (MIS) using parameters from both p+p collisions and from HSD at zero impact parameter, and with experimental data from NA49 (Pb+Pb) and STAR (Au+Au) collaborations.

## Key numbers
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, the scaled variance ω_π for b ≤ 4 fm is higher than ω_π at b = 0 by approximately a factor of 10.
- The scaled variance ω_part ≈ 100 in a sample of minimum bias Au+Au (or Pb+Pb) collisions.
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, the values of ⟨K⟩/⟨N_part⟩ and ⟨π⟩/⟨N_part⟩ are approximately 5 and 10 times larger, respectively, than the corresponding values at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV.
- For the sample b ≤ 4 fm at √s_NN = 200 GeV, Δ^(Kπ) shows an increase of about 30% compared to its value at b = 0.
- The NA49 data for Δ^(Kπ) show a bump at √s_NN = 8.8 GeV and a deep at √s_NN = 12.3 GeV in 3.5% most central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of strongly intensive measures proposed by Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2011) by testing them within a realistic transport model (HSD) for the first time. It addresses the long-standing problem that conventional scaled variances ω_K and ω_π require very rigid centrality selection (about 1% most central events, as in Lung et al.) due to dominant participant number fluctuations, while the alternative measure ν_dyn (used by the STAR collaboration) depends on the average number of participants. The results enable future experimental studies by NA61 at SPS CERN and the RHIC beam energy scan program to use wider centrality windows (up to 10%) when searching for the QCD critical point, and highlight the need for further investigation of the non-monotonous structure in Δ^(Kπ) observed by NA49 in the energy region where other signals (kink, horn, step) were previously reported.
