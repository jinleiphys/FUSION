# Thermal Charm Production in Quark-Gluon Plasma at LHC

> Charm production from the quark-gluon plasma created in the midrapidity of central heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is studied in the next-to-leading order in QCD. Using a schematic longitudinally boost-invariant and transversally expanding fire-cylinder model, we find that charm production could be appreciably enhanced at LHC as a result of the high temperature that is expected to be reached in the produced quark-gluon plasma. Sensitivities of our results to the number of charm quark pairs produced from initial hard scattering, the initial thermalization time and temperature of the quark-gluon plasma, and the charm quark mass are also studied.

## Key claim

Thermal charm production in the quark-gluon plasma at LHC could be appreciably enhanced compared to direct production from initial hard scattering, but this enhancement is highly sensitive to the initial temperature of the plasma, the charm quark mass, and the number of directly produced charm pairs; under the baseline assumptions (initial temperature 700 MeV, charm mass 1.3 GeV, 20 initial direct pairs) the enhancement is about 30%, but it ranges from roughly 10% to 80% depending on the chosen initial conditions.

## Method

The authors model central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC using a schematic longitudinally boost-invariant and transversally expanding fire-cylinder of quark-gluon plasma, with the temperature evolution determined by entropy conservation. They solve a rate equation for charm pair abundance that includes both leading-order (gluon-gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) processes (real gluon emission and virtual corrections), using thermal-averaged cross sections computed with massive quarks and gluons (thermal masses) and a charm quark mass of 1.3 GeV. The initial number of charm pairs from direct hard scattering is taken as 20 per unit midrapidity from NLO pQCD, and the initial temperature and proper time of the plasma are varied to study sensitivities.

## Key numbers

- At LHC (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), with initial temperature T0=700 MeV, initial proper time tau0=0.2 fm/c, and charm mass m_c=1.3 GeV, the final number of charm pairs at the critical temperature T_C=170 MeV is about 27 in NLO, a 30% enhancement over the 20 directly produced pairs.
- For the same conditions but using only leading-order processes, the final charm pair number is about 22.
- Varying the initial temperature: final charm pairs are about 22 for T0=630 MeV, 27 for T0=700 MeV, and 33 for T0=750 MeV (all with tau0=0.2 fm/c, m_c=1.3 GeV, 20 initial direct pairs).
- With a larger charm mass m_c=1.5 GeV and tau0=0.2 fm/c, final charm pairs are about 20, 22, and 25 for T0=630, 700, and 750 MeV respectively.
- Varying the initial number of directly produced charm pairs (10, 20, 40) with T0=700 MeV, tau0=0.2 fm/c, m_c=1.3 GeV gives final numbers of about 19, 27, and 45 respectively.
- The chemically equilibrium number of charm pairs at T_C=170 MeV is about 4.7, much smaller than the final numbers obtained.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal charm production in quark-gluon plasma, which were based only on leading-order QCD (Matsui 1986, Biro 1990, Levai 1995, Levai 1997, Kampfer 1997), by performing the first calculation at next-to-leading order. It also contrasts with the common assumption in LHC studies that thermal charm production can be neglected compared to direct production from initial hard scattering. The results are relevant for understanding charmonium (J/psi) production at LHC, since regeneration from charm and anticharm quarks in the plasma (Thews 2006) or coalescence during hadronization (Greco 2004) depends on the total charm quark abundance. The sensitivity to initial conditions highlights the need for better constraints on the initial temperature and thermalization time of the quark-gluon plasma at LHC.
