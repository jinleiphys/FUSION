# One-proton emission of $^{102}$Sb and its sensitivity to proton-neutron interaction

> One-proton emission from the $^{102}$Sb nucleus is discussed, assuming an inert $^{100}$Sn core and the valence proton and neutron. There are experimentally measured bound states in the $^{100}$Sn-neutron system, whereas no particle-bound $^{100}$Sn-proton state has been observed. With time-dependent three-body calculations, the $1^+$ ground state of $^{102}$Sb is suggested as a possible proton emitter. This conclusion is reached by assuming a weakening effect on the proton-neutron ($pn$) interaction with respect to a bare deuteron. An analogous phenomenon is necessary to reproduce the empirical binding energies of $^{42}$Sc and $^{18}$F. Continuous shift from the unbound to bound regions by changing the $pn$-interaction strength is demonstrated. The lower limit of lifetime is evaluated as $τ\gtrsim 4.4 \times 10^{-18}$ seconds in the no-$pn$-interaction limit. However, the actual lifetime is expected as longer with a finite $pn$ interaction. Observation of a resonant state in $^{102}$Sb and its decay would provide a benchmark of the $pn$-pairing correlation.

## Key claim

The $1^+$ ground state of $^{102}$Sb is suggested as a possible proton emitter, but this conclusion depends on assuming a weakening of the proton-neutron interaction relative to the bare deuteron; if the full-strength interaction is used, the state becomes bound rather than unbound.

## Method

The authors employed a time-dependent three-body model treating $^{102}$Sb as an inert $^{100}$Sn core with one valence proton and one valence neutron. They solved the three-body Hamiltonian using core-orbital coordinates, with core-nucleon potentials tuned to reproduce experimental single-neutron energies, and a finite-range Gaussian proton-neutron interaction whose strength was modulated by a scaling factor $f_{pn}$. The time evolution of the initial confined state was computed to extract the proton-emission width and lifetime from the survival probability.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energy of $^{101}$Sn is $S_n = 11.2(4)$ MeV (experimental value used for tuning).
- The lowest $d_{5/2}$ proton resonance in $^{101}$Sb exists at $E_p = 3.41$ MeV with width $\Gamma_p \cong 1.5 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV.
- In the no-interaction limit ($f_{pn}=0$), the proton-emitting width is $\Gamma_p = 1.52 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV, corresponding to lifetime $\tau \cong 4.4 \times 10^{-18}$ seconds.
- With $f_{pn}=0.4$, the width narrows to $\Gamma_p \cong 9.4 \times 10^{-7}$ MeV, giving lifetime $7.0 \times 10^{-16}$ seconds.
- With $f_{pn}=1.0$ (bare deuteron strength), the $1^+$ state becomes bound with proton energy $E_p = -0.61$ MeV.
- The threshold for binding the $1^+$ state occurs at $f_{pn} \geq 0.92$.
- To reproduce the experimental $pn$-separation energy of $^{42}$Sc ($12.023$ MeV), a weakening factor $f_{pn}=0.38$ is required; for $^{18}$F ($9.750$ MeV), $f_{pn}=0.58$ is required.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $pn$ pairing in $^{102}$Sb by Taniuchi et al. (2016), which focused on bound-state Gamow-Teller transitions, by instead considering the unbound proton-emission scenario. The finding that the $pn$ interaction must be weakened relative to the bare deuteron to reproduce binding energies of $^{42}$Sc and $^{18}$F parallels known density-dependent effects in effective interactions (Tamagaki, 1968; Suzuki, 1982). Observation of a resonant state in $^{102}$Sb and its decay would provide a benchmark for $pn$-pairing correlations and inform models of proton-rich nuclei near the $A=100$ dripline.
