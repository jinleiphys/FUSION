# On the possibility of fusion reactions in water molecules

> The probability of nuclear transitions $p+p+{}^{16}{O} \, \rightarrow \, {}^{18}{Ne}\, (4.522,1^-)$ in molecular water is estimated. Due to the practically exact agreement of the energy of the $Ne$ resonance and of the $p+p+{}^{16}{O}$ threshold, the transition probability is found to be considerably enhanced. This indicates the possibility of nuclear fusion in rotationally excited $H_2O$ molecules in the state $1^-$.

# On the possibility of fusion reactions in water molecules

## Key claim
The probability of the nuclear transition $p+p+{}^{16}{O} \rightarrow {}^{18}{Ne}\,(4.522,1^-)$ is considerably enhanced in rotationally excited $H_2O$ molecules of angular momentum $1^-$, because the measured energy of the ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ resonance (4.522 MeV) coincides up to the last figure with the threshold energy of the $p+p+{}^{16}{O}$ three-body channel. However, the partial widths of the subsequent decay channels are unknown, making it impossible to estimate the total energy release from this "burning" of molecular water.

## Method
The authors constructed an asymptotic wave function for the water molecule in the $1^-$ rotational state using hyperspherical variables, incorporating Coulomb repulsion between the three particles and the molecular binding energy. They similarly modeled the ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ nuclear resonance state using a Coulombic three-body breakup function normalized to the nuclear volume. The overlap integral between these two wave functions was then evaluated asymptotically, yielding an expression for the transition rate $W = \kappa c |I|^2$ of the process $H_2O \rightarrow {}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$.

## Key numbers
- The ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ resonance energy is $E = 4.522$ MeV, which coincides with the $p+p+{}^{16}{O}$ threshold energy.
- The width of the ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ level for decay into the $p+p+{}^{16}{O}$ channel satisfies $\Gamma_{p} \leq 20$ keV.
- The $Q$-value for the decay ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-) \rightarrow {}^{17}{F}+p$ is $Q_1 \cong 0.6$ MeV.
- The $Q$-value for the decay ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-) \rightarrow {}^{18}{Ne}+\gamma$ is $Q_2 \cong 4.522$ MeV.
- The binding energy of the water molecule is only a few eV, making the rotational $1^-$ state of $H_2O$ and the ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ state degenerate in energy.

## Context
This work extends the known phenomenon of near-threshold resonance enhancement from muon-catalyzed fusion (where the $dt$ threshold and the ${}^{5}{He}\,(3/2^+)$ resonance differ by only about 50 keV, enhancing the transition probability by at least four orders of magnitude compared to molecules without such resonances) to ordinary electronic molecules. The authors draw an analogy to the ground state of ${}^{8}B$, which is separated from the $p+{}^{7}{Be}$ threshold by only 130 keV and requires integrations up to 300 fm. The result suggests that nuclear fusion might occur in rotationally excited water molecules, but quantitative estimates of the energy release await knowledge of the partial widths of the ${}^{18}{Ne}\,(1^-)$ decay channels.
