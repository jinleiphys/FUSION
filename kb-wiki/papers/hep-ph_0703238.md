# Dropping rho and A1 Meson Masses at the Chiral Phase Transition in the Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry

> In this contribution to the proceedings, we present our recent analysis on the chiral symmetry restoration in the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) which incorporates the rho meson, $A_1$ meson and the pion consistently with the chiral symmetry of QCD. It is shown that a set of parameter relations, which ensures the first and second Weinberg sum rules, is invariant under the renormalization group evolution. Then, it is found that the Weinberg sum rules together with the matching of the vector and axial-vector current correlators to the OPE inevitably leads to {\it the dropping masses of both rho and $A_1$ mesons} at the symmetry restoration point, and that the mass ratio flows into one of three fixed points.

# Dropping rho and A1 Meson Masses at the Chiral Phase Transition in the Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry

## Key claim
The chiral symmetry restoration required through matching to QCD can be realized only if both the rho and A1 meson masses vanish at the restoration point, but the mass ratio of the two mesons flows into one of three distinct fixed points (GL-type, VM-type, or Hybrid-type) rather than a single universal behavior.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective field theory based on the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS), which incorporates the rho meson, A1 meson, and pion as dynamical degrees of freedom consistent with QCD chiral symmetry. They derived the renormalization group equations (RGEs) for the GHLS Lagrangian parameters and showed that the parameter relations ensuring the first and second Weinberg sum rules (a = b, d = 0) are invariant under renormalization group evolution. They then imposed matching of the vector and axial-vector current correlators to the operator product expansion (OPE) to study the behavior near the chiral phase transition.

## Key numbers
- The first and second Weinberg sum rules in pole-saturated form require: F_pi^2 + F_A1^2 = F_rho^2 and F_A1^2 M_A1^2 = F_rho^2 M_rho^2
- The matching condition yields: F_rho^2 M_rho^2 (M_A1^2 - M_rho^2) = (32 pi alpha_s / 9) <bar q q>^2
- Three fixed points for the mass ratio zeta = M_rho^2 / M_A1^2 are identified: GL-type at zeta = 1, VM-type at zeta = 0, and Hybrid-type at zeta = 1/3
- The dimensionless parameter W = (N_f / (2 (4 pi)^2)) (mu^2 / F_pi^2(mu)) takes values W = 1/2 at the GL fixed point, W = 1 at the VM fixed point, and W = 3/2 at the Hybrid fixed point

## Context
This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) framework previously developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) in hidden local symmetry, which predicted only the rho meson becoming massless at chiral restoration. The authors incorporate the A1 meson as a dynamical degree of freedom, going beyond earlier analyses such as those by Brown and Rho (1991) that assumed axial-vector mesons decouple near the transition. The results provide a theoretical basis for interpreting dilepton production data from experiments at CERN SPS (CERES, NA60), KEK-PS E325, and STAR, where the violation of vector dominance predicted by the VM plays a crucial role in explaining the observed spectra.
