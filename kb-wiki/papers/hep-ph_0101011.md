# Regularization for effective field theory with two heavy particles

> A regularization for effective field theory with two propagating heavy particles is constructed. This regularization preserves the low-energy analytic structure, implements a low-energy power counting for the one-loop diagrams, and preserves symmetries respected by dimensional regularization.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a regularization scheme for effective field theories with two propagating heavy particles (such as nucleons) that preserves low-energy analytic structure and power counting, while removing fractional powers of the heavy mass M that appear in standard dimensional regularization. The regularization works by decomposing each one-loop integral into a low-energy part (which contains all physical singularities) and a regular part (which contains only fractional powers of M and is discarded).

## Method

The authors start from standard dimensional regularization of one-loop diagrams (bubble, triangle, and box) in the two-heavy-particle sector. They decompose each Feynman integral I into a low-energy part I-bar and a regular part R by extending the Feynman parameter integration domain for the parameter w to the entire real axis, then subtracting contributions from the extended regions to restore the heavy-light thresholds. The low-energy part I-bar is identified as the EFTDR (Effective Field Theory Dimensional Regularization) integral, while the regular part R, which contains all fractional powers of the heavy mass M, is discarded.

## Key numbers

- The bubble diagram low-energy part I-bar_2 = (i/(16 pi)) * alpha, where alpha = sqrt((s_+ - s - i epsilon)/s) and s_+ = 4M^2 is the two-heavy-particle threshold
- The regular part of the triangle diagram R_3 = -(1/(16 pi^2)) * (1/(2 M^2)) * (2/(4-d) - 2 - gamma - log(M^2/(4 pi mu^2))) * (1 + ...) in d -> 4 dimensions
- The low-energy power counting exponent nu = d - 2 n_ell - n_h + n_n, where n_ell and n_h are numbers of light and heavy propagators respectively, and n_n is the number of small numerator factors
- The bubble diagram has leading order nu = 2 by power counting but is actually of order p (more singular) when s - 4M^2 is taken as order p^2

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization developed by Becher and Leutwyler (2000) for single-heavy-particle effective field theories to the case of two propagating heavy particles, where the 1/M expansion cannot be applied due to the two-heavy-particle threshold in the s-channel. The regularization preserves symmetries respected by dimensional regularization and enables consistent power counting in effective field theories with two heavy particles, such as nucleon-nucleon effective field theory and baryon chiral perturbation theory with two baryons. The approach builds on earlier work by Ellis and Tang on dimensional regularization schemes for heavy particles.
