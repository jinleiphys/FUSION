# Gapless CFL and its competition with mixed phases

> We recently argued that as the density of quark matter decreases, there is a continuous transition from the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase to a gapless "gCFL" phase. The reason is the growing importance of the strange quark mass, and the constraint of electric/color neutrality. In this paper we discuss mixed phases that achieve neutrality by charge seperation, and might offer an alternative to the gCFL phase. We find that none of the obvious mixtures is favored relative to gCFL.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the gapless color-flavor-locked (gCFL) phase is the favored homogeneous neutral phase at densities below the CFL phase, and that none of the charge-separated mixed phases investigated can compete with gCFL in free energy, even before including the additional costs of Coulomb and surface energies.

## Method

The authors use an NJL model at quark chemical potential μ = 500 MeV with a coupling chosen so that the CFL gap Δ_CFL = 25 MeV at zero strange quark mass. They compute the free energies of various homogeneous phases (CFL, gCFL, 2SC, 2SCus, unpaired quark matter) and then evaluate whether mixed phases composed of oppositely charged components can achieve neutrality with lower free energy than homogeneous gCFL. The analysis is performed at several values of M_s^2/μ within the gCFL region (47 MeV < M_s^2/μ < 130 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The gCFL region exists for 47 MeV < M_s^2/μ < 130 MeV at μ = 500 MeV
- At M_s^2/μ = 80 MeV, the neutral 2SC+2SCus mixed phase has free energy Ω = -14.93 × 10^6 MeV^4, while neutral gCFL has Ω = -18.01 × 10^6 MeV^4 (both measured relative to neutral unpaired quark matter)
- At M_s^2/μ = 80 MeV, the gCFL+2SC mixed phase has free energy only 0.0012 × 10^6 MeV^4 lower than neutral gCFL, making it unlikely to survive electrostatic and surface costs
- The CFL-to-gCFL transition occurs at a critical value M_s^2/μ = 2Δ, where Δ is the pairing gap

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding (Alford 2003) that the CFL phase undergoes a continuous transition to a gapless gCFL phase as density decreases due to the growing importance of the strange quark mass and neutrality constraints. It directly compares gCFL with mixed phases that achieve neutrality by charge separation, which had been shown to be favored over the gapless 2SC phase in the two-flavor case (Reddy 2004). The paper concludes that gCFL is a strong candidate for the phase below CFL in density, leaving open questions about the role of kaon condensation (Bedaque 2001) and whether the crystalline LOFF phase can compete at sufficiently low density.
