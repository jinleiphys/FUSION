# Modified DGLAP Evolution for Fragmentation Functions in Nuclei and QGP

> Within the framework of generalized factorization of higher-twist contributions, including modification to splitting functions of both quark and gluon, we get and numerically resolve the medium-modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution equations. With Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry and Hirano 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulations of hot medium, we study the medium modified fragmentation functions (mFF) in DIS and Au+Au collisions in RHIC. Our calculation imply that the parton density in hot medium produced in RHIC is about 30 times larger than cold nucleon.

## Key claim

The medium-modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution equations, including modifications to both quark and gluon splitting functions, can describe nuclear modification of fragmentation functions in both cold nuclei and hot QGP, with the calculation implying that the parton density in the hot medium produced at RHIC is about 30 times larger than in cold nucleon matter.

## Method

The authors derived coupled medium-modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution equations within the generalized factorization of higher-twist contributions, including medium modifications to both quark and gluon splitting functions. They numerically solved these equations using a modified HOPPET Fortran 95 package at leading order, employing Woods-Saxon nuclear geometry for DIS and Hirano 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulations for the hot medium in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The jet transport parameter in hot medium was assumed to include contributions from both the QGP phase and hadronic phase, with the path integral over the parton's trajectory through the medium relating the twist-four quark-gluon correlation function to the generalized jet transport parameter.

## Key numbers

- Jet transport parameter in cold nuclear matter: \(\hat q_{N}=0.015\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, extracted from HERMES data on Ne, Kr, and Xe targets
- Jet transport parameter at the center of the bulk medium in the QGP phase at formation time: \(\hat{q}_0=0.5 \pm 0.1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, fitting PHENIX data for top 5% centrality Au+Au collisions
- Parton density in hot medium produced at RHIC is about 30 times larger than in cold nucleon
- Nucleon density in the center of a large nucleus: \(\rho_{N}=n_{0}\approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the framework of Guo and Wang (2000) and Wang (2001) for medium-modified DGLAP evolution in DIS off nuclei to include coupled evolution for both quarks and gluons, and applies it to hot QGP using Hirano 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulations (Hirano, 2005; Hirano, 2007). The results enable quantitative extraction of the jet transport parameter in both cold nuclear matter and hot QGP, and provide a foundation for comparing medium modification of fragmentation functions across different systems, from DIS on nuclei to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
