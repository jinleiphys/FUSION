# Charge symmetry violation in the determination of strangeness form factors

> The strange quark contributions to the electromagnetic form factors of the proton are ideal quantities to study the role of hidden flavor in the properties of the proton. This has motivated intense experimental measurements of these form factors. A major remaining source of systematic uncertainty in these determinations is the assumption that charge symmetry violation (CSV) is negligible. We use recent theoretical determinations of the CSV form factors and reanalyse the available parity-violating electron scattering data, up to $Q^2$ $\sim$ 1 GeV$^2$. Our analysis considers systematic expansions of the strangeness electric and magnetic form factors of the proton. The results provide an update to the determination of strangeness over a range of $Q^2$ where, under certain assumptions about the effective axial form factor, an emergence of non-zero strangeness is revealed in the vicinity of $Q^2$ $\sim$ 0.6 GeV$^2$. Given the recent theoretical calculations, it is found that the current limits on CSV do not have a significant impact on the interpretation of the measurements and hence suggests an opportunity for a next generation of parity-violating measurements to more precisely map the distribution of strange quarks.

# Charge symmetry violation in the determination of strangeness form factors

## Key claim
Under certain assumptions about the effective axial form factor, the global analysis of parity-violating electron scattering data reveals an emergence of non-zero strangeness in the vicinity of Q² ~ 0.6 GeV², but current limits on charge symmetry violation do not have a significant impact on the interpretation of the measurements.

## Method
The authors performed a global analysis of all available parity-violating electron scattering asymmetry data from SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX, G0, and Q_weak experiments up to Q² ~ 1 GeV², using both Taylor expansions and z-expansions of the strange electric and magnetic form factors. They incorporated recent lattice QCD determinations of charge symmetry violation form factors and γZ-box radiative corrections, then reanalysed the data with and without CSV constraints to quantify the impact on strangeness extraction.

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic moment from LO Taylor fit (no CSV): μ_s = -0.12 ± 0.04
- Strange electric radius from LO Taylor fit (no CSV): ρ_s = 0.15 ± 0.04 GeV⁻²
- Reduced χ² for LO Taylor fit: 1.1 with 33 degrees of freedom
- Reduced χ² for NLO Taylor fit: 1.23 with 31 degrees of freedom
- At Q² = 0.63 GeV², G_E^s ~ 0.1, which is on the order of 10% of the proton electric form factor at that momentum transfer
- Best fit for effective axial form factor (z-expansion NLO): \tilde{g}_A^{p} = -0.67 ± 0.25
- Kubis and Lewis CSV magnetic moment: G^{CSV}_M(0) = 0.025 ± 0.020
- Wagman and Miller CSV magnetic moment: G^{CSV}_M(0) = 0.012 ± 0.003
- Lattice QCD CSV: G^{CSV}_M = 0.0 ± 0.001 and ρ^{CSV}_E = 0.0 ± 0.001 GeV⁻²
- Nuclear CSV for ⁴He: F^{CSV} = -0.00157 at Q² = 0.077 and 0.091 GeV²

## Context
This work extends previous global fits by Young et al. (2006, 2007), Liu et al. (2007), and Gonzalez-Canales et al. (2014) by incorporating recent lattice QCD calculations of CSV form factors from Shanahan et al. (2015) and updated γZ-box corrections. The results show excellent agreement with direct lattice QCD calculations of strange form factors by Sufian et al. (2017) and Green et al. (2015). The finding that current CSV limits do not significantly affect strangeness extraction suggests that next-generation parity-violating measurements can more precisely map the distribution of strange quarks, though the sensitivity to the effective axial form factor warrants further investigation of the anapole contribution.
