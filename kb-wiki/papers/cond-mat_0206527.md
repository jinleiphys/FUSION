# Dilute resonating gases and the third virial coefficient

> We study dilute gases with short range interactions and large two-body scattering lengths. At temperatures between the condensation temperature and the scale set by therange of the potential there is a high degree of universality. The first two terms in the expansion of thermodynamic functions in powers of the fugacity $z$, which measures the diluteness of the system, are determined by the scattering length only. The term proportional to $z^3$ depends only on one new parameter describing the three-body physics. We compute the third term of the expansion and show that, for many values of this new parameter, the $z^3$ term may be the dominant one.

## Key claim

The third virial coefficient \(b_3\) for a dilute gas with large two-body scattering length depends on a single new three-body parameter (beyond the scattering length \(a\)), and for many values of this parameter the \(z^3\) term in the virial expansion may dominate over the lower-order terms, contrary to the usual expectation that higher-order terms are smaller.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory with a short-range contact interaction, introducing an auxiliary dimer field to resum the two-body scattering amplitude to all orders. They compute the particle density as a power series in the fugacity \(z\) up to order \(z^3\), extracting the virial coefficients \(b_1\), \(b_2\), and \(b_3\). The three-body contribution \(b_3^{(3)}\) requires solving a Faddeev integral equation for the particle-dimer scattering amplitude, which is renormalized by a three-body force parameter \(\Lambda^*\) that cannot be determined from two-body data.

## Key numbers

* For a gas of \(^4\)He atoms at \(T = 10^{-7}\ \mathrm{eV} \approx 1.16\ \mathrm{mK}\), with scattering length \(a = 104\ \mathrm{\AA}\) and effective range \(r_0 \approx 7\ \mathrm{\AA}\), the two-body bound state energy is \(B_2 = 1/(M a^2)\).
* The three-body parameter \(s_0 \approx 1.006\) is the solution of a trigonometric equation governing the cutoff dependence of the three-body force.
* The cutoff \(\Lambda = 200\ \mathrm{eV}\) is used in the numerical evaluation, with results cutoff-independent up to corrections of order \(1/(\Lambda a)\).

## Context

This work extends the classic virial coefficient formalism of Beth and Uhlenbeck (1937) and the general S-matrix approach of Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969) to systems with unnaturally large scattering lengths, where the two-body scattering amplitude requires non-perturbative resummation. It builds on the effective field theory treatment of three-body systems with large scattering length developed by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) and the renormalization group analysis of the three-body force by the same authors. The results enable computation of the equation of state for dilute atomic gases near Feshbach resonances and for low-density neutron matter, where the three-body parameter \(\Lambda^*\) must be fixed by a single three-body observable (e.g., the trimer binding energy).
