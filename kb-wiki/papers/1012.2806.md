# Hunting resonance poles with Rational Approximants

> Based on the mathematically well defined Padé Theory, a theoretically safe new procedure for the extraction of the pole mass and width of resonances is proposed. In particular, thanks to the Montessus de Ballore's theorem we are able to unfold the Second Riemann sheet of an amplitude to search the position of the resonant pole in the complex plane. The method is systematic and provides a model-independent treatment of the prediction and the corresponding errors of the approximation. This letter partially covers the material presented by the author at the 15th International QCD Conference: QCD 10 (25th anniversary), Montpellier, France, 28 Jun - 3 Jul 2010 and at the Quark Confinement and the Hadron Spectrum IX, 30 August - 3 September 2010, Madrid, Spain.

## Key claim

Based on the mathematically well-defined Pade Theory, a theoretically safe new procedure for extracting the pole mass and width of resonances is proposed, but the method's convergence depends critically on the resonance lying within the disk of applicability of Montessus de Ballore's theorem, bounded by the first and second production thresholds; for resonances with large width (such as a sigma-like model) convergence is slower, and for ultra-fat resonances there is no convergence at all.

## Method

The method uses a sequence of one-pole Pade Approximants \(P_1^N\) centered at a point \(x_0 + i0^+\) on the real axis above the first production threshold but below the second threshold, which unfolds the Second Riemann sheet of the amplitude. The approximants are fitted to experimental data for the modulus and phase-shift of the amplitude, and the pole of each approximant provides a systematic, model-independent estimate of the resonance pole position. The difference between successive orders \(P_1^N\) and \(P_1^{N+1}\) gives an estimate of the systematic error.

## Key numbers

* For the \(\rho\)-meson from ALEPH \(\pi\pi\) vector form factor data: \(M_\rho = 763.7 \pm 1.2\) MeV, \(\Gamma_\rho = 144 \pm 3\) MeV, obtained using a \(P_1^6\) Pade Approximant sequence centered between the pion and kaon production thresholds.
* In a \(\rho\)-like model with a pole at \(q_p^2 = (0.77 - i0.15/2)^2\) GeV\(^2\), the first prediction using \(P_1^0\) has an error below 0.1 MeV, the second below 0.01 MeV.
* For a \(\sigma\)-like model with a pole at \(q_p^2 = (0.48 - i0.53/2)^2\) GeV\(^2\), convergence of the \(P_1^N\) sequence is slower than for the \(\rho\)-like model.
* For an ultra-fat model with a pole at \(q_p^2 = (2.10 - i1.05/2)^2\) GeV\(^2\), there is no convergence at all.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous use of Pade Approximants centered at the origin to study the low-energy behavior of the \(\pi\pi\) vector form factor (Masjuan 2010), which could not access resonance poles due to the branch cut. The new method provides a simpler and systematic alternative to more complex procedures used by Ananthanarayan (2004), Pelaez (2004), Zhou (2005), and Pich and Cillero (2008) for extracting the \(\rho\)-meson pole. The approach enables model-independent extraction of resonance poles from experimental data without relying on a particular Lagrangian or extrapolation model, and can be generalized to multiple resonances using \(P_L^N\) sequences or to functions with branch cuts using Quadratic Approximants.
