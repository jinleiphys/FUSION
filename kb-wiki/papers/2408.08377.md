# Factorized QED Contribution to Lepton-Hadron DIS

> We present the first calculation of next-to-leading order (NLO) factorized QED contributions to the short-distance hard coefficients of inclusive lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in a joint QCD and QED factorization approach. We demonstrate how the joint factorization consistently factorize all perturbative collinear sensitivities of partonic scattering in both QCD and QED into corresponding universal hadron and lepton distribution functions without the need of any parameters other than the standard factorization scale. We discuss the necessary modification to DGLAP-type evolution of the parton and lepton distribution functions in this joint factorization approach. We also discuss the potential impact of this joint factorization approach on the extraction of partonic information from lepton-hadron DIS.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of next-to-leading order (NLO) factorized QED contributions to the short-distance hard coefficients of inclusive lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in a joint QCD and QED factorization approach, demonstrating that all collinear sensitivities can be absorbed into universal lepton distribution functions (LDFs) and lepton fragmentation functions (LFFs) without introducing any parameters beyond the standard factorization scale.

## Method

The authors computed the NLO QED hard coefficients for inclusive lepton-hadron DIS by applying the joint QCD and QED factorization formalism to a quark target state, expanding to order alpha_em^3, and subtracting all collinear divergences using the universal LDFs, LFFs, and PDFs in the MSbar scheme. The partonic cross section at O(alpha_em^3) was calculated from real emission and virtual loop Feynman diagrams using dimensional regularization, with the collinear divergences exactly canceled by subtraction terms involving the leading-order hard parts convoluted with the appropriate distribution functions. The finite, infrared-safe hard coefficient was then obtained and used to evaluate the numerical impact of collision-induced QED radiation on DIS cross sections at different collision energies.

## Key numbers

* At sqrt(s) = 4.7 GeV, the ratio of DIS cross sections with LO QED radiation (using perturbative LDFs and LFFs) to the cross section without QED radiation shows strong dependence on Bjorken x_B, with the ratio deviating significantly from unity across the x_B range.
* At sqrt(s) = 140 GeV, the same ratio shows a different x_B-dependent pattern, with the impact of QED radiation varying substantially between the two collision energies.
* The ratio of NLO over LO QED contributions to the DIS cross section at sqrt(s) = 4.7 GeV shows sizable and nontrivial x_B-dependent corrections, with the shaded uncertainty band from varying the factorization scale between ell'_T/2 and 2 ell'_T.
* The ratio of NLO over LO QED contributions at sqrt(s) = 140 GeV similarly shows x_B-dependent corrections, with the factorization scale uncertainty indicated by the shaded band.

## Context

This work extends the joint QCD and QED factorization approach proposed by Liu et al. (2021) by providing the first explicit NLO QED calculation of the hard coefficients for inclusive lepton-hadron DIS. It contrasts with the traditional approach of calculating QED radiative corrections (RCs) as perturbative corrections to the Born cross section, as done by Mo and Tsai (1969), Bardin et al. (1989), and others, which often requires introducing unknown parameters and cannot fully treat collinear-sensitive contributions. The results enable a systematic joint QCD and QED global analysis to extract universal LDFs and LFFs from data, similar to how PDFs are extracted, and provide a parameter-free framework for improving the precision of DIS calculations and predictions.
