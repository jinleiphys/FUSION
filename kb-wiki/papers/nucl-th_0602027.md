# Effects of non-causal artifacts in a hadronic rescattering model for RHIC collisions

> It has been shown that calculations based on a hadronic rescattering model agree reasonably well with experimental results from RHIC Au+Au collisions. Because of the large particle densities intrinsically present at the early time steps of Monte Carlo calculations attempting to model RHIC collisions undesirable artifacts resulting in non-causality may be present. The effects of such artifacts on observables calculated from the rescattering model are studied in the present work in two ways: 1) varying the time step and 2) using the subdivision method. It is shown that although non-causal artifacts are present in the rescattering model they have no appreciable effects on the calculated observables, thus strengthing the confidence in the results of this rescattering model for RHIC energies.

## Key claim
Although non-causal (superluminal) artifacts are present in the hadronic rescattering model due to high particle densities at early time steps, they have no appreciable effects on the calculated hadronic observables, strengthening confidence in the model's results for RHIC energies. The study tests this by varying the time step and using the subdivision method, finding that observables agree within statistical errors even when artifacts are significantly suppressed.

## Method
A semi-classical Monte Carlo rescattering calculation simulated RHIC-energy Au+Au collisions using isospin-averaged hadrons (pions, kaons, nucleons, lambdas, and resonances) with initial parameters T=300 MeV, σ_y=2.4, and τ_had=1 fm/c. Non-causal artifacts were quantified by calculating the transverse signal propagation velocity (v_sT) for all particle pairs, and their effects on observables were tested by (1) varying the time step (Δt=0.05, 0.1, and 0.2 fm/c) and (2) applying the subdivision method with factors l=1, 5, and 8, which simultaneously reduces cross sections and increases particle density.

## Key numbers
- Nominal time step used in prior RHIC comparisons: Δt=0.1 fm/c; half-step (0.05 fm/c) showed ~10-20% enhancement in superluminal artifacts; double-step (0.2 fm/c) showed ~30% reduction in artifacts over nominal.
- Subdivision factors l=5 and l=8 produced factors of 5-10 reductions in superluminal artifacts compared to l=1.
- Calculations used 1640 events for l=1, 124 events for l=5, and 120 events for l=8; the l=8 sample required 600 CPU-hours on 2.8 GHz processors.
- Central collision freezeout contained approximately 5000 pions, 500 kaons, and 650 nucleons per event.
- Hadronization model parameters: T=300 MeV, σ_y=2.4, τ_had=1 fm/c; projectile radius R=r_0 A^(1/3) with r_0=1.12 fm.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior hadronic rescattering model calculations (Humanic 2002a, 2002b, 2006) that showed reasonable agreement with RHIC Au+Au data at √s=130 and 200 GeV for m_T distributions, elliptic flow, and HBT measurements. It addresses concerns raised by Zhang (1998a) and Molnar (2000a) about non-causal artifacts in high-density Monte Carlo calculations, and applies the subdivision method of Zhang (1998b) to test artifact effects. The results enable continued use of this simple rescattering model for RHIC physics without requiring more complex transport approaches, though the study was performed for non-central collisions (b=8 fm) and the author expects similar conclusions for central collisions.
