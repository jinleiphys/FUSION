# Buda-Lund hydro model and the elliptic flow at RHIC

> The ellipsoidally symmetric Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model describes naturally the transverse momentum and the pseudorapidity dependence of the elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ and 200 GeV. The result confirms the indication of quark deconfinement in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, obtained from Buda-Lund hydro model fits to combined spectra and HBT radii of BRAHMS, PHOBOS, PHENIX and STAR.

## Key claim

The ellipsoidally symmetric Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model can describe both the transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the authors note they have not yet performed a fine-tuned fit with error bars; instead they searched the parameter set by hand and achieved a fit by eye.

## Method

The authors generalized the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model from axial to ellipsoidal symmetry, using a scale-invariant relativistic form with a scaling variable s and four-velocity distribution that satisfy a vanishing co-moving derivative. They calculated observables analytically using a saddle-point approximation, then tuned the model parameters by hand to reproduce the transverse momentum dependence of identified particle elliptic flow at midrapidity from PHENIX and the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow from PHOBOS.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature T0 = 210 MeV, surface temperature Ts ≈ 105 MeV, with T0 > Tc = 170 MeV indicating quark deconfinement
- Transverse Hubble constants: X-dot = 0.57, Y-dot = 0.45, Z-dot = 2.4
- Source sizes: Xf = 8.6 fm, Yf = 10.5 fm, Zf = 17.5 fm
- Mean freeze-out time τ0 = 7 fm/c
- Tilt parameter ϑ = 0.09
- Chemical potentials: μ0,π = 70 MeV, μ0,K = 210 MeV, μ0,p = 315 MeV
- Volume with T > Tc = 170 MeV is about 750 fm³, corresponding to 1/8 of the total volume on the main freeze-out hypersurface
- Non-flow contribution of 0.02 added to match the PHOBOS mid-rapidity v2 data point

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamic model, previously applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Csorgo et al., 2003) and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS, from axial to ellipsoidal symmetry to describe elliptic flow data from PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR. The results confirm the indication of quark deconfinement at RHIC found in earlier Buda-Lund model fits to combined spectra and HBT radii, and enable future extraction of best-fit parameters with error bars from a more systematic analysis.
