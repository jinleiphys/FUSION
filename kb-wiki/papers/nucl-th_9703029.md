# Role of anharmonicities of nuclear vibrations in fusion reactions at subbarrier energies

> We discuss the effects of double octupole and quadrupole phonon excitations in $^{144}$Sm on fusion reactions between $^{16}$O and $^{144}$Sm at subbarrier energies. The effects of anharmonicities of the vibrational states are taken into account by using the $sdf$-interacting boson model. We compare the results with those in the harmonic limit to show that anharmonicities play an essential role in reproducing the experimental fusion barrier distribution. From the analysis of the high quality fusion data available for this system, we deduce negative static quadrupole moments for both the first 2$^{+}$ and 3$^{-}$ states in $^{144}$Sm. This is the first time that the sign of static quadrupole moments of phonon states in a spherical nucleus is determined from the data of subbarrier fusion reactions.

# Role of anharmonicities of nuclear vibrations in fusion reactions at subbarrier energies

## Key claim

Anharmonicities in the quadrupole and octupole vibrational states of 144Sm, rather than the harmonic multi-phonon description, are essential to reproduce the experimental fusion barrier distribution for 16O + 144Sm at subbarrier energies; the sign of static quadrupole moments of the first 2+ and 3- states in a spherical nucleus can be determined from subbarrier fusion data for the first time.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations for the 16O + 144Sm fusion reaction were performed using the sdf-interacting boson model (IBM) to describe quadrupole and octupole vibrations in the target nucleus, with anharmonicities introduced via non-zero χ parameters in the quadrupole and octupole operators. The nuclear coupling was treated to all orders (no linear coupling approximation), finite excitation energies of the target were included, and the constant coupling approximation was avoided. The deformation parameters β2 and β3 and the χ parameters were determined by a χ² fit to the experimental fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of the first 2+ and 3- states in 144Sm: εd = 1.66 MeV and εf = 1.81 MeV
- Deformation parameters from the anharmonic fit: β2 = 0.13, β3 = 0.23 (compared to harmonic-limit estimates β2 = 0.11, β3 = 0.21)
- IBM χ parameters from fit: χ2 = −3.30, χ2f = −2.48, χ3 = 2.87
- Static quadrupole moment of the first 2+ state in 144Sm: −0.28 b
- Static quadrupole moment of the first 3- state in 144Sm: −0.70 b
- Effective boson charge: eB = 0.16 e b (determined from experimental B(E2;0→2+1))
- Nuclear potential parameters: V0 = 105.1 MeV, R0 = 8.54 fm, a = 0.75 fm; target radius RT = 5.56 fm
- Boson number adopted: N = 2

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels studies of subbarrier fusion (reviewed by Balantekin 1988) and addresses a contradiction noted by M. Dasgupta (1995): including double-phonon excitations of 144Sm in the harmonic limit destroyed agreement with experiment, while single-phonon calculations worked. The authors use the sdf-IBM framework developed by Brink et al. (1965) for anharmonic vibrations and the IBM fusion model of Balantekin et al. (1994). The analysis enables the determination of static quadrupole moments in spherical nuclei from fusion barrier distributions, complementing methods such as neutron pick-up reactions on 145Sm (Khoo et al. 1989). The high-quality fusion data for 16O + 144Sm were measured by Leigh et al. (1995).
