# Regulator Artifacts in Uniform Matter for Chiral Interactions

> Regulator functions applied to two- and three-nucleon forces are a necessary ingredient in many-body calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions. These interactions have been developed recently with a variety of different cutoff forms, including regulating both the momentum transfer (local) and the relative momentum (nonlocal). While in principle any regulator that suppresses high momentum modes can be employed, in practice artifacts are inevitable in current power counting schemes. Artifacts from particular regulators may cause significant distortions of the physics or may affect many-body convergence rates, so understanding their nature is important. Here we characterize the differences between cutoff effects using uniform matter at Hartree-Fock and second-order in the interaction as a testbed. This provides a clean laboratory to isolate phase-space effects of various regulators on both two- and three-nucleon interactions. We test the normal-ordering approximation for three-nucleon forces in nuclear matter and find that the relative size of the residual 3N contributions is sensitive to the employed regularization scheme.

## Key claim

Regulator artifacts in chiral effective field theory interactions are inevitable under current power-counting schemes, and the choice of regulator function (local versus nonlocal, momentum-space versus coordinate-space) can significantly distort many-body predictions even at the Hartree-Fock level, with the relative size of residual three-nucleon contributions being sensitive to the regularization scheme employed.

## Method

The authors computed the Hartree-Fock and second-order many-body perturbation theory energies for pure neutron matter (and one case of symmetric nuclear matter) using leading-order nucleon-nucleon and next-to-next-to-leading-order three-nucleon chiral interactions. They compared four regulator schemes: momentum-space nonlocal (MSNL), momentum-space local (MSL), coordinate-space local (CSL), and the semi-local EKM regulator, analyzing how each regulator affects the direct and exchange components of the interaction phase space.

## Key numbers

- Hartree-Fock calculations used soft cutoffs of $\NNcut = 2.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}$ and $R_0 = 1.2\ \text{fm}$ to highlight regulator artifacts at high density
- For the nonlocal regulator (MSNL), typical cutoff values are $\NNcut = 450-600\ \text{MeV}$ with $n=1-3$
- For the EKM regulator, typical cutoffs are $R_0 = 0.8-1.2\ \text{fm}$ with $n\geq 4$
- For three-nucleon nonlocal regulators, common choices include $n=2-3$ and $\TNcut = 400-600\ \text{MeV}$
- The axial coupling constant used was $g_A = 1.267$, with $C_S = 1.0\ \text{MeV}^{-2}$ and $c_i = 1.0\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$, $c_D = 1.0$, $c_E = 1.0$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tews et al. (2016), who found that the Hartree-Fock energy in pure neutron matter for next-to-next-to-leading-order three-nucleon forces has a large dependence on the choice of regulator function. The paper builds on the Weinberg power-counting scheme without explicit Delta-isobar degrees of freedom, as developed by Epelbaum (2008) and Machleidt (2011). The results enable systematic understanding of how regulator artifacts propagate into many-body calculations and motivate further investigation into how these artifacts are absorbed by implicit renormalization when constructing realistic interactions.
