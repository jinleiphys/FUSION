# Chemical Freeze-Out and Higher Order Multiplicity Moments

> We calculate the non-normalized moments of the particle multiplicity within the framework of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. At finite chemical potential $μ$, a non-monotonic behavior is observed in the thermal evolution of third order moment (skewness $S$) and the higher order ones as well. Among others, this observation likely reflects dynamical fluctuations and strong correlations. The signatures of non-monotonicity in the normalized fourth order moment (kurtosis $κ$) and its products get very clear. Based on these findings, we introduce a novel condition characterizing the universal freeze-out curve. The chemical freeze-out parameters $T$ and $μ$ are described by vanishing $κ\, σ^2$ or equivalently $m_4=3\,χ^2$, where $σ$, $χ$ and $m_4$ are the standard deviation, susceptibility and fourth order moment, respectively. The fact that the HRG model is not able to release information about criticality related to the confinement and chiral dynamics should not veil the observations related to the chemical freeze-out. Recent lattice QCD studies strongly advocate the main conclusion of the present paper.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the chemical freeze-out curve in heavy-ion collisions can be characterized by the condition that the product of kurtosis and variance, $\kappa\, \sigma^2$, vanishes, or equivalently that the fourth order moment $m_4$ equals three times the squared susceptibility $\chi^2$ ($m_4 = 3\chi^2$). This condition is presented as a novel universal freeze-out criterion, but the paper explicitly notes that the HRG model cannot provide information about criticality related to confinement and chiral dynamics.

## Method

The authors calculate non-normalized and normalized higher order moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) of particle multiplicity distributions within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, using a grand canonical partition function of an ideal gas of hadron resonances with masses up to 2 GeV. They study the thermal evolution of these moments and their products (ratios) at various baryon chemical potentials $\mu$, and compare the resulting $T$-$\mu$ curve where $\kappa\, \sigma^2$ vanishes against experimentally measured freeze-out parameters from multiple experiments.

## Key numbers

- The HRG model includes hadron resonances with masses up to 2 GeV as constituents of the partition function.
- The constant entropy condition $s/T^3 = 7$ is used for three quark flavors to define the chemical freeze-out boundary in one comparison.
- The condition $m_4 = 3\chi^2$ at freeze-out leads to a naive estimate of the correlation length $\xi \sim 3^{1/3}$ fm.
- The freeze-out curve from vanishing $\kappa\, \sigma^2$ reproduces experimental data over the range $0 < \mu < 0.8$ GeV.
- The paper reports that $\kappa\, \sigma^2$ flips sign at high temperature, with the sign-flip temperature depending on $\mu$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of freeze-out conditions based on constant energy per particle ($\epsilon/n \approx 1$ GeV, from Jean and Redlich), constant baryon density ($n_b + n_{\bar{b}} \approx 0.12$ fm$^{-3}$), and constant normalized entropy ($s/T^3 = 5$ for two flavors, $s/T^3 = 7$ for three flavors, from Tawfik (2005, 2004)). The paper introduces a new condition based on higher order moments, which the authors note is later supported by lattice QCD calculations from Fodor and from Nakamura that connect ratios of higher order fluctuations to freeze-out parameters. The work enables a new method to determine freeze-out parameters from fluctuation observables, though the HRG model cannot address critical endpoint physics.
