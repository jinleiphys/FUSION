# QCD symmetries in excited hadrons

> Recent developments for chiral and $U(1)_A$ restorations in excited hadrons are reviewed. We emphasize predictions of the chiral symmetry restoration scenario for axial charges and couplings to Goldstone bosons. Using very general chiral symmetry arguments it is shown that strict chiral restoration in a given excited nucleon forbids its decay into the $N π$ channel. We confront this prediction with the $N^*Nπ$ coupling constants extracted from the decay widths and observe a 100 % correlation of these data with the spectroscopic parity doublet patterns. These results suggest that the lowest approximate chiral parity doublet is the $N(1440) - N(1535)$ pair. In the meson sector we discuss predictions of the chiral symmetry restoration for still missing states and a signature of the higher symmetry observed in new $\bar p p$ data. We conclude with the exactly solvable chirally symmetric and confining model that can be considered as a generalization of the 1+1 dimensional 't Hooft model to 4 dimensions. Complete spectra of $\bar q q$ mesons demonstrate a fast chiral restoration with increasing $J$ and a slow one with increasing $n$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in highly excited hadrons, chiral and \(U(1)_A\) symmetries become approximately restored even though they remain spontaneously broken in the vacuum; a strict consequence is that an excited nucleon belonging to an exact chiral parity doublet cannot decay into the \(N\pi\) channel, and the observed 100% correlation between parity-doublet patterns and suppressed \(N\pi\) decay widths supports this scenario.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical framework of effective chiral symmetry restoration in excited baryons and mesons, deriving a selection rule from chiral symmetry that forbids \(N\pi\) decay for exact chiral doublets. This prediction is tested by extracting \(N^*N\pi\) coupling constants from experimental decay widths of well-established nucleon resonances and comparing the squared coupling ratios \((f_{BN\pi}/f_{NN\pi})^2\) with the spectroscopic parity-doublet patterns. The analysis also examines meson spectra from PDG and from \(\bar p p\) annihilation data at LEAR, and presents results from the exactly solvable Generalized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (GNJL) model, which generalizes the 1+1 dimensional 't Hooft model to four dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For the \(N(1440) - N(1535)\) parity doublet (spin 1/2), the squared coupling ratio \((f_{B_+N\pi}/f_{NN\pi})^2 - (f_{B_-N\pi}/f_{NN\pi})^2\) is \(0.15 - 0.026\).
- For the \(N(1710) - N(1650)\) doublet (spin 1/2), the same ratio is \(0.0030 - 0.026\).
- For the \(N(1720) - N(1700)\) doublet (spin 3/2), the ratio is \(0.023 - 0.13\).
- For the \(N(1680) - N(1675)\) doublet (spin 5/2), the ratio is \(0.18 - 0.012\).
- For the \(N(2220) - N(2250)\) doublet (spin 9/2), the ratio is \(0.000022 - 0.0000020\).
- For the \(N(2600)\) state (spin 11/2), the ratio is \(0.000000064\) (only the negative-parity member is listed).
- For the \(N(1520)\) state (spin 3/2), which has no chiral partner, the ratio is \(2.5\).
- The axial charge of \(N(1535)\) measured on the lattice by Takahashi and Kunihiro is reported as smaller than 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal of effective chiral restoration in excited baryons (Glozman 2000, Glozman 2001, Cohen and Glozman 2002, Glozman 2003) by providing a model-independent selection rule that links suppressed \(N\pi\) decay to chiral doublet membership. It confronts this prediction with experimental decay data and finds a 100% correlation, suggesting that the lowest approximate chiral parity doublet is the \(N(1440) - N(1535)\) pair. The results enable future lattice studies of axial charges for other excited states and motivate experimental searches for missing high-spin chiral partners in both the nucleon and meson spectra, such as the chiral partners of \(N(2190)\) and \(N(2600)\).
