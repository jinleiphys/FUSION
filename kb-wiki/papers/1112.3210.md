# Phase diagram and heavy-ion collisions: Overview

> The physics of the QCD phase diagram is discussed in view from heavy-ion collisions, compact astrophysical phenomena, lattice QCD and chiral effective models. We find that (T,mu_B) region probed in heavy-ion collisions and the black hole formation processes covers most of the critical point locations predicted in recent lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations and chiral effective models of QCD. We also discuss the consistency of the statistical model results and dynamical model description.

## Key claim

The (T, μB) region probed in heavy-ion collisions and black hole formation processes covers most of the critical point locations predicted in recent lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations and chiral effective models, but the critical point may be close to the chemical freeze-out line in the √sNN = (5–130) GeV range, meaning its existence depends on whether it falls within the accessible experimental region.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD Monte-Carlo simulations (multi-parameter reweighting, canonical ensemble, Taylor expansion) and chiral effective models (NJL, PNJL, PQM) to predict the QCD critical point location. It compares these predictions with chemical freeze-out points extracted from statistical model fits to heavy-ion collision data across AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, and with the (T, μB) trajectory from neutrino-radiation hydrodynamics simulations of black hole formation. The consistency of statistical model results and dynamical model descriptions (hydrodynamics, hadron-string cascade) is also discussed.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD critical temperature from chiral susceptibility: Tc(χσ) = 151(3)(3) MeV with stout-link improved staggered fermions (Borsanyi-Wetterich 2006)
- Lattice QCD critical temperature from O(N) scaling: Tc(O(N)) = 157 ± 6 MeV with asqtad and HISQ/tree actions (HotQCD 2011)
- Polyakov loop critical temperature: Tc(P) = 176(3)(4) MeV with stout action, 25 MeV higher than Tc(χσ)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from statistical model: TFO ∼ 160 MeV (Andronic)
- Direct photon inverse slope parameter from PHENIX: Tphoton = 221 MeV
- Initial QGP temperature estimate: Tinit = (300–600) MeV
- Critical point from multi-parameter reweighting (LR04): (TCP, μCP) = (162 MeV, 360 MeV)
- Critical point from canonical ensemble (LC11): (TCP, μCP) = (0.927 Tc, 2.60 Tc)
- Taylor expansion prediction: μCP ≃ 420 MeV (LT04)
- Chiral effective model predictions (vector-scalar coupling ratio 0.2): NJL (22, 1095) MeV, PNJL (74, 1062) MeV, PNJL8 (129, 708) MeV, PQM (91, 1005) MeV
- Baryon chemical potential in neutron star core: μB ∼ 1650 MeV (ρB = 1.12 fm⁻³) for Mmax = 2.17 M⊙ star
- Transition chemical potential to quark matter in chiral models: μc = (1000–1110) MeV
- Black hole formation temperature: T ∼ 90 MeV at density ρB ∼ 5 ρ0
- Horn structure (K⁺/π⁺ ratio peak) observed at √sNN ≃ 8 GeV
- Quark number scaling of elliptic flow holds at √sNN ≥ 39 GeV but not for φ meson at √sNN = 11.5 GeV
- Kurtosis reduction compared to hadron resonance gas at √sNN ≲ 20 GeV

## Context

This review extends the discussion of the QCD phase diagram from lattice QCD results (Borsanyi-Wetterich 2006, HotQCD 2011, Fodor-Katz 2004) and chiral effective models (Asakawa-Yazaki 1989, NJL, PNJL, PQM) to experimental observables from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, SPS, and AGS. It connects the critical point search to the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and to black hole formation processes in astrophysics (Sumiyoshi). The work enables future experimental searches for the critical point via fluctuation observables (kurtosis, skewness) and systematic comparison of lattice QCD predictions with beam energy scan data, as well as theoretical development of non-equilibrium frameworks that can describe both transport properties and the change of degrees of freedom across the phase boundary.
