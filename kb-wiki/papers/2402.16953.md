# Exploring the Sensitivity to Non-Standard Neutrino Interactions of NaI and Cryogenic CsI Detectors at the Spallation Neutron Source

> After the first observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) by the COHERENT collaboration, many efforts are being made to improve the measurement of this process, making it possible to constrain new physics in the neutrino sector. In this paper, we study the sensitivity to non-standard interactions (NSIs) and generalized neutrino interactions (GNIs) of two experimental setups at the Spallation Neutron Source at Oak Ridge National Laboratory: a NaI detector with characteristics similar to the one that is currently being deployed there, and a cryogenic CsI detector proposed at the same facility. We show that a combined analysis of the data from these detectors, whose target nuclei have significantly different proton-to-neutron ratios, could help to partially break the parameter degeneracies arising from the interference between the Standard Model and NSI contributions to the CE$ν$NS cross section, as well as between different NSI parameters. By contrast, only a slight improvement over the current CsI constraints is expected for parameters that do not interfere with the SM contribution.

## Key claim

A combined analysis of future NaI and cryogenic CsI detectors at the Spallation Neutron Source, whose target nuclei have significantly different proton-to-neutron ratios (0.92 for sodium versus 0.71-0.72 for cesium and iodine), could partially break parameter degeneracies arising from interference between Standard Model and non-standard interaction contributions to coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, but only a slight improvement over current CsI constraints is expected for parameters that do not interfere with the Standard Model contribution.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared statistical analysis of simulated coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data from three detector configurations at the Spallation Neutron Source: the existing COHERENT CsI detector (14.6 kg, 19.3 m from source), a future NaI detector (3395.7 kg, 22 m from source, sensitive only to sodium recoils), and a future cryogenic CsI detector (10 kg, 19.3 m from source). They computed expected event rates using the Klein-Nystrand nuclear form factor parametrization and the standard neutrino flux from pion and muon decays at rest, then derived sensitivity contours for vector, scalar, and tensor non-standard interaction couplings by minimizing over nuisance parameters for signal and background normalizations.

## Key numbers

- The future NaI detector has a total mass of 3395.7 kg, located at 22 m from the neutrino source, with a sodium nuclear recoil energy threshold of 13 keV_nr
- The future cryogenic CsI detector has a mass of 10 kg, located at 19.3 m from the source, with a nuclear recoil energy threshold of 0.533 keV_nr (4 photoelectrons)
- The proton-to-neutron ratios are 0.71 for cesium, 0.72 for iodine, and 0.92 for sodium
- The SNS neutrino beam has a maximum neutrino energy of 52.8 MeV
- The analysis assumes 3 years of data taking with 5.2 x 10^23 protons on target for both future detectors
- The steady-state background for NaI is assumed to be 300 counts per keV_ee per kg per day (400 in the first two bins), reduced by a factor of 8000 due to beam pulsing
- The cryogenic CsI detector assumes a constant quenching factor of 15% and light yield of 50 photoelectrons per keV_ee
- Systematic uncertainties are assumed to be 10% for signal and 5% for background for both future detectors

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-standard neutrino interactions using coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data from the COHERENT collaboration (Akimov 2017, Akimov 2021), which first observed this process with a CsI detector. The analysis builds on the approach of Barranco (2005) and Scholberg (2005) regarding the use of multiple target nuclei with different proton-to-neutron ratios to break parameter degeneracies, and follows the methodology of Chatterjee (2022) for sensitivity projections. The results enable future experimental design decisions at the Spallation Neutron Source and inform strategies for disentangling Standard Model and new physics contributions in neutrino-nucleus scattering measurements.
