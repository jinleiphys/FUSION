# Quenching of hadron and photon spectra in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to LHC

> The generic features of parton energy loss effects on the quenching of single hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions are discussed, paying attention to the expected differences from RHIC to LHC. The need for precise baseline measurements in p-Pb collisions at the LHC is also emphasized. Finally I briefly mention the production of prompt photons in heavy-ion collisions as well as some open questions.

## Key claim

The quenching of single hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC is similar to that observed at RHIC, but the precise value of the nuclear modification factor R_AA depends sensitively on the interpolation procedure used to estimate the proton-proton reference cross section at the LHC energy, with pT-interpolation and xT-interpolation yielding yields that differ by as much as 25%.

## Method

The paper reviews the generic features of parton energy loss on the nuclear modification factor R_AA for single hadrons, using a Taylor expansion of the pp production rate to derive a first-order relation between R_AA, the fractional energy loss, and the power-law exponent n(xT) of the pp spectrum. It compares preliminary CMS data at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV with predictions from several energy loss frameworks (BDMPS-ASW, DGLV, AMY, higher-twist) and introduces the logarithmic derivative of R_AA as a normalization-free observable to test energy loss dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty on the nuclear thickness function T_AA in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC is about 8%, translating into an overall normalization error on R_AA.
- At pT=10 GeV and sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, the yields obtained from pT-interpolation and xT-interpolation differ by as much as 25%, with pT-interpolation giving a smaller yield.
- Nuclear PDF effects (EPS09) are below 20% above pT ~ 10 GeV at LHC.
- Assuming R_AA(pT=20 GeV)=0.4, the predicted R_AA at pT=100 GeV ranges from 0.9 to 0.4 when the exponent alpha in meaneps(pT) ~ pT^alpha is varied from 0 to 1.
- The particle density at LHC is dN/dy|_LHC ~ 2.4 times that at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of parton energy loss from RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) to the first LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, comparing with early ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS measurements. It emphasizes the need for precise baseline measurements in p-Pb collisions at LHC (as advocated by Salgado (2011)) and discusses the role of prompt photons as a reference process, noting that PHENIX and STAR have reported preliminary photon measurements at RHIC while CMS has reported the first isolated photon measurement in heavy-ion collisions at LHC. The paper identifies open questions including the role of collisional vs. radiative energy loss, the heavy-quark dead cone hierarchy, and disentangling energy loss from saturation effects.
