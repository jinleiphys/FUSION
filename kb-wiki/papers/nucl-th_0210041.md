# The Two-Body Momentum Distribution in Finite Nuclei

> The two-body momentum distribution n2(p1,p2) of nuclei is studied. First, a compact analytical expression is derived for Z=N l-closed nuclei, within the context of the independent-particle shell model. Application to the light closed-shell nucleus 16O is included and discussed. Next, the effect of dynamical, short-range correlations is investigated in the case of 4He, by including Jastrow-type correlations in the formalism. The effect is significant for large values of p1 and p2 and for angles between the vectors p1 and p2 close to 180 deg. and 0.

## Key claim
The paper derives an analytical expression for the two-body momentum distribution of Z=N, ℓ-closed nuclei within the independent-particle shell model, and then investigates the effect of short-range correlations using Jastrow-type correlations in a low-order approximation, finding that the effect is significant for large momenta and for angles between the two momentum vectors close to 180 degrees and 0 degrees, but with the caveat that the low-order approximation can produce unphysical negative values in some kinematical regions depending on the correlation function used.

## Method
The authors first derived a compact analytical expression for the two-body momentum distribution of Z=N, ℓ-closed nuclei using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions within the independent-particle shell model, applying it to 16O. For 4He, they included Jastrow-type short-range correlations via the low-order approximation (LOA) of a cluster expansion, using a two-gaussian central correlation function f(r)=1-c1 exp(-r^2/beta1^2)+c2 exp(-r^2/beta2^2) to avoid the negative values that appear with a single-gaussian correlation function.

## Key numbers
- For 16O: harmonic oscillator parameter b=1.7825 fm, reproducing the experimental charge root mean square radius of 2.737 fm
- For 16O: Fermi wave number k_F=1.1 fm^-1, calculated using local density approximation
- For 4He: harmonic oscillator parameter b=1.382 fm, reproducing the experimental charge root mean square radius of 1.67 fm
- For 4He with two-gaussian correlation: c1=0.9, c2=1.7, beta1=0.8 fm, beta2=1.1 fm, b=1.4 fm
- Wound parameter kappa for the two-gaussian correlation function: 0.008; for the single-gaussian correlation function: 0.018
- The quantity xi(p1,p2) for 16O in the independent-particle model lies between 0.75 (=1-1/nu) and 1, with xi=0.75 when p1=p2
- For 4He in the harmonic oscillator model, xi(p1,p2)=0.75 for all momenta

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the generalized momentum distribution in infinite nuclear matter (MPC95) and finite nuclei (PMK00) to the two-body momentum distribution. The method builds on techniques developed for nuclear form factors, charge distributions, and one-body density matrices in closed-shell nuclei (KV90, KV92, Pa95e). For the correlated calculation, the low-order approximation follows the work of Bohigas and Stringari (1980) and Dal Ri, Stringari, and Bohigas (1982) on Jastrow correlations in 4He. The results are relevant for analyzing (e,e'2N) experiments at NIKHEF, MAMI, and Jefferson Lab, where the two-body momentum distribution connects to the two-nucleon spectral function needed for interpreting two-nucleon knockout reactions.
