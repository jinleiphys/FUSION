# Decay rate and asymmetries of $Σ^+\to pμ^+μ^-$

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently reported evidence for the rare hyperon decay $Σ^+\to pμ^+μ^-$ that is consistent with the standard model expectation. Motivated by this new result we revisit the calculation of this mode including both long and short distance contributions. In the standard model this mode is completely dominated by long distance physics and thus subject to large uncertainties. We present a range of predictions for the rate, the dimuon invariant mass spectrum, and a muon polarization asymmetry that covers these uncertainties as well as known constraints. We study the interplay between short and long distance contributions which could result in additional asymmetries, but we find that they are negligible within the standard model. We propose a parameterization of these asymmetries in terms of a couple of constants that can arise from new physics.

## Key claim

The standard model (SM) prediction for the branching fraction of Σ⁺ → pμ⁺μ⁻ is dominated by long-distance physics and therefore subject to large theoretical uncertainties, with the authors presenting a range of predictions that covers these uncertainties and known constraints, rather than a single precise value.

## Method

The authors calculated the decay amplitude for Σ⁺ → pμ⁺μ⁻ including both short-distance (SD) and long-distance (LD) contributions within the SM, using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to evaluate the baryonic matrix elements and form factors. They parameterized the amplitude in a general form that accommodates SM contributions and possible new physics (NP) terms, then derived expressions for the branching fraction, dimuon invariant mass spectrum, forward-backward asymmetry, and three muon polarization asymmetries. The form factors a, b, c, and d were determined using unitarity arguments and constraints from the radiative decay Σ⁺ → pγ, with theoretical uncertainties estimated by comparing relativistic and heavy-baryon χPT approaches.

## Key numbers

- LHCb measured branching fraction: ℬ(Σ⁺ → pμ⁺μ⁻) = (2.2⁺¹·⁸₋₁·₃) × 10⁻⁸
- HyperCP measured branching fraction: ℬ(Σ⁺ → pμ⁺μ⁻) = (8.6⁺⁶·⁶₋₅·₄ ± 5.5) × 10⁻⁸
- SM prediction range for branching fraction: 1.2 × 10⁻⁸ ≤ ℬ ≤ 10.2 × 10⁻⁸ (updated from earlier 1.6 × 10⁻⁸ to 9.0 × 10⁻⁸)
- Imaginary parts of form factors at q²=0: Im(a(0), b(0)) = (2.85, -1.84) MeV (relativistic χPT) and (6.86, -0.54) MeV (heavy-baryon χPT)
- SD contribution alone would produce ℬ ∼ 10⁻¹²
- Integrated forward-backward asymmetry in SM: |Ã_FB| ≤ 3.7 × 10⁻⁵
- Integrated longitudinal polarization asymmetry in SM: |P̃_L⁻| ≤ 7.0 × 10⁻⁵
- Integrated normal polarization asymmetry in SM: |P̃_N⁻| ≤ 1.0 × 10⁻⁵
- Transverse polarization asymmetry in SM: P̃_T⁻ ranges from 17% to 59% depending on the parameter set

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SM calculation (He, 2005) for Σ⁺ → pμ⁺μ⁻, motivated by the recent LHCb evidence (Aaij, 2017) which supersedes the earlier HyperCP observation (Park, 2005) that had hinted at a possible new light particle. The paper provides updated predictions for the rate and dimuon mass spectrum, and proposes several muon polarization asymmetries that are tiny in the SM (below 10⁻⁴) but could be enhanced by new physics to detectable levels, enabling future searches for NP beyond the SM.
