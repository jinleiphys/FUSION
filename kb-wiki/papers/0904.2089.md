# Third moments of conserved charges as probes of QCD phase structure

> The third moments of conserved charges, the baryon and electric charge numbers, and energy, as well as their mixed moments, carry more information on the state around the QCD phase boundary than previously proposed fluctuation observables and higher order moments. In particular, their signs give plenty of information on the location of the state created in relativistic heavy ion collisions in the temperature and baryon chemical potential plane. We demonstrate this with an effective model.

## Key claim

The sign (positive or negative) of third moments of conserved charges, baryon number, electric charge, and energy, can locate the state created in heavy-ion collisions relative to the QCD phase boundary, even if the critical point is at high baryon chemical potential, because the moments change sign where the corresponding susceptibilities peak.

## Method

The authors derive relations between third moments (e.g., \(m_3(\mathrm{BBB})\), \(m_3(\mathrm{QQQ})\), \(m_3(\mathrm{EEE})\)) and derivatives of the thermodynamic potential with respect to chemical potentials and temperature. They then compute these moments in the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in the mean-field approximation, mapping the regions in the temperature–baryon chemical potential plane where each moment becomes negative.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in the NJL model is at \((T, \mu_{\mathrm{B}}) \simeq (48, 980)\) MeV.
- The NJL model parameters used: coupling \(G = 5.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), current quark mass \(m = 5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 631\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For small \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\), lattice QCD indicates \(c_4(T) > 0\) and \(c_6(T) < 0\) in the high-temperature phase, so \(m_3(\mathrm{BBB}) = 24 [c_4(T) \mu_{\mathrm{B}} + 5 c_6(T) \mu_{\mathrm{B}}^3 + \cdots]\) is positive at small \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\) but can become negative at larger \(\mu_{\mathrm{B}}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of conserved-charge fluctuations (Asakawa 2000, Jeon 2000) and higher-order moments (higher 2008) by focusing on the sign rather than the magnitude of third moments. It contradicts the expectation that only the absolute value of fluctuations near the critical point is informative, and it enables experimental searches for the QCD critical point and quark-gluon plasma using sign measurements that are less sensitive to normalization and finite-size effects than previous proposals. The results also connect to lattice QCD Taylor-expansion results (Allton 2005) and to effective-model studies of the phase diagram (Stephanov 2004).
