# Charmonium and open charm production in nuclear collisions at SPS/FAIR energies and the possible influence of a hot hadronic medium

> We provide predictions for charmonium and open charm production in nuclear collisions at SPS/FAIR energies within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. The increasing importance at lower energies of Lambda_c production is demonstrated and provides a challenge for future experiments. We also demonstrate that, because of the large charm quark mass and the different timescales for charm quark and charmed hadron production, possible modifications of charmed hadrons in the hot hadronic medium do not lead to measurable changes in cross sections for D-meson production. A possible influence of medium effects can be seen, however, in yields of charmonium. These effects are visible at all energies and results are presented for the energy range between charm threshold and RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, due to charm conservation and the large charm quark mass, possible in-medium modifications of charmed hadron masses do not lead to measurable changes in D-meson production cross sections, but such medium effects can be seen in charmonium yields at all energies from near threshold to RHIC energy. The authors note that their calculations may somewhat underestimate charmonium production at SPS energies and below, making the calculated medium effects upper limits for energies close to threshold.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical hadronization model (SHM) to predict charmonium and open charm production yields for central heavy-ion collisions in the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 7-200 GeV. They use a charm balance equation incorporating canonical suppression factors to determine a charm fugacity, with input parameters including charm production cross sections in pp collisions (extrapolated to low energies using a threshold-based parameterization) and chemical freeze-out characteristics (temperature, baryochemical potential, volume) from the statistical model. They then consider two scenarios for in-medium mass modifications of charmed hadrons: a common mass decrease for all charmed mesons and baryons, and an asymmetric scenario with mass decrease for particles and increase for antiparticles.

## Key numbers

- Charm production cross section in pp collisions: parameterized as sigma_c-cbar = 1.85 micro-barn * (1 - sqrt(s_thr)/sqrt(s))^4.3 * (sqrt(s_thr)/sqrt(s))^(-1.44), with sqrt(s_thr) = 4.5 GeV for charm quark mass m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Rapidity density of initially produced charm quark pairs rises from 1.1e-3 to 1.7 for sqrt(s_NN) = 7-200 GeV
- Temperature T rises from 151 to 161 MeV from sqrt(s_NN) = 7 to 12 GeV, then stays constant; baryochemical potential mu_b decreases from 434 to 22 MeV
- Volume V at midrapidity rises from 760 to 2400 fm^3 over the energy range
- Canonical suppression factor (I1/I0) decreases from about 1/30 to 1/1.2 for sqrt(s_NN) = 7 to 200 GeV; charm fugacity g_c increases from 0.96 to 8.9
- For in-medium widths of D mesons: yields increase by 11% for a width of 100 MeV, and by 2.7% for a width of 50 MeV
- At LHC energy, the J/psi yield per c-cbar pair approaches 1%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical hadronization model studies (Andronic et al., 2005-2007) from top SPS energy (sqrt(s_NN) ~ 17 GeV) upward to the lower energy range from near threshold (sqrt(s_NN) ~ 6 GeV) to top SPS energy, relevant for the CBM experiment at the future FAIR facility. The paper contradicts expectations (CBM collaboration, Tolos et al.) that D-meson measurements near threshold could provide information on their possible modification near the phase boundary, demonstrating instead that medium effects on D-meson production are likely very small. The results enable future experiments at FAIR to focus on charmonium measurements and charmed baryon production as more sensitive probes of medium effects, while emphasizing the need to measure charmed baryons (particularly Lambda_c) to obtain the total charm production cross section at low energies.
