# Anomalous Transport and Generalized Axial Charge

> In this paper we continue studying the modification of the axial charge in chiral media by macroscopic helicities. Recently it was shown that magnetic reconnections result in a persistent current of zero mode along flux tubes. Here we argue that in general a change in the helical part of the generalized axial charge results in the same phenomenon. Thus one may say that there is a novel realization of chiral effects requiring no initial chiral asymmetry. The transfer of flow helicity to zero modes is analyzed in a toy model based on a vortex reconnection in a chiral superfluid. Then, we discuss the balance between the two competing processes effect of reconnections and the chiral instability on the example of magnetic helicity. We argue that in the general case there is a possibility for the distribution of the axial charge between the magnetic and fermionic forms at the end of the instability.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a change in any helical part of the generalized axial charge (magnetic, flow, or mixed helicity) generates a persistent current of zero modes along flux tubes or vortices, providing a novel realization of chiral effects that requires no initial chiral asymmetry.

## Method

The authors analyze the transfer of flow helicity to zero modes using a toy model of vortex reconnection in a chiral superfluid at zero temperature, where the superfluid velocity field has a clear microscopic counterpart in the Goldstone boson. They then discuss the balance between two competing processes: helicity transfer to fermionic chirality via reconnections and the chiral magnetic instability that converts chiral asymmetry back into magnetic helicity.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic reconnection of two flux tubes yields a change in magnetic helicity of Δℋ_mh = 2Φ₁Φ₂, producing a zero-mode current satisfying ∮ ΔJ_{1+1}·dx = -(1/2π²)Φ₁Φ₂.
- For two linked superfluid vortices with winding numbers n₁ and n₂, the flow helicity is ℋ_sfh = 8π²n₁n₂, and unlinking produces a quantized CVE current satisfying ∫ ΔJ_{1+1}·dx = -2n₁n₂.
- For a linkage between a superfluid vortex and a magnetic flux tube, the mixed helicity is ℋ_mfh = 4πnΦ, and reconnection yields ∫ ΔJ_{1+1}·dx = -(1/π)nΦ.
- The chiral magnetic instability grows modes with eigenvalue K < α, where α is the conductivity coefficient determined by helicity.

## Context

This work extends the recent finding by Hirono (2016) that magnetic reconnections generate persistent currents along flux tubes, showing that the same phenomenon occurs for changes in flow helicity and mixed helicity. The paper connects to the generalized axial charge framework of Avdoshkin (2014) and addresses the competing chiral instability studied by Boyarsky (2012) and Akamatsu (2013). The results enable further study of how the axial charge distributes between magnetic and fermionic forms at the end of instability, with implications for chiral plasmas in systems ranging from quark-gluon plasma to Dirac/Weyl semimetals.
