# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of dark matter scattering off light nuclei

> We compute the matrix elements for elastic scattering of dark matter (DM) particles off light nuclei ($^2$H, $^3$H, $^3$He, $^4$He and $^6$Li) using quantum Monte Carlo methods. We focus on scalar-mediated DM-nucleus interactions and use scalar currents obtained to next-to-leading order in chiral effective theory. The nuclear ground states are obtained from a phenomenological nuclear Hamiltonian that includes the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-body interaction and the three-body Urbana IX interaction. Within this approach, we study the impact of one- and two-body currents and discuss the size of nuclear uncertainties, including for the first time two-body effects in $A=4$ and $A=6$ systems. Our results provide the nuclear structure input needed to assess the sensitivity of future experimental searches of (light) dark matter using light nuclei, such as $^3$He and $^4$He.

## Key claim

The paper computes nuclear matrix elements for scalar-mediated dark matter scattering off light nuclei using quantum Monte Carlo methods, finding that next-to-leading-order corrections (including two-body currents) are at the few percent level for momentum transfers up to 100 MeV, but the two-body contribution shows significant cutoff dependence that represents the largest source of systematic uncertainty in the hybrid approach.

## Method

The authors use variational Monte Carlo with nuclear ground states obtained from a phenomenological Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-body interaction and Urbana IX three-body interaction. Scalar-mediated dark matter-nucleon interactions are derived to next-to-leading order in chiral effective theory, and the resulting one- and two-body currents are Fourier transformed to coordinate space with a Gaussian regulator. The nuclear matrix elements are then evaluated for elastic scattering off 2H, 3H, 3He, 4He, and 6Li, with the cross section expanded in terms of nuclear response functions that factor out the isoscalar coupling and pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon sigma term used is sigma_piN = (59.1 +/- 3.5) MeV, taken from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering
- The strong contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is delta m_N = (2.32 +/- 0.17) MeV
- The nucleon scalar radius correction grows from zero to approximately -2% at q = 60 MeV and approximately -6% at q = 100 MeV
- Two-body corrections to the total cross section range from about 2% up to about 4% depending on momentum transfer and nucleus
- For 4He at q=0, the two-body correction is -2.4% with cutoff Lambda=500 MeV and +2.7% with cutoff Lambda=10000 MeV
- For 2H at q=0, the two-body correction is +0.7% with cutoff Lambda=500 MeV and +3.0% with cutoff Lambda=10000 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter scattering off light nuclei by Korber (2017), which focused on A=2 and A=3 systems with consistent chiral potentials and currents, and by Gazda (2016), which used no-core shell model wave functions with chiral potentials for 3He and 4He but only considered one-body currents. The present paper provides the first results for two-nucleon currents in A=4 and A=6 systems, including the experimentally relevant 4He isotope, and demonstrates that nuclear structure uncertainties are at the few percent level for scalar-mediated interactions. These results enable assessment of future experimental searches for light dark matter using 3He and 4He targets, though further refinements using chiral interactions with quantum Monte Carlo methods are warranted.
