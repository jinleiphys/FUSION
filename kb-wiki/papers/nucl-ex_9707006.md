# A Xenon Solar Neutrino Detector

> The neutrino capture by $^{131}$Xe with a threshold at 352 keV as reaction to detect solar neutrinos is examined. The most important feature of this process is its high sensitivity to beryllium neutrinos, which contribute approximately 40% to the total capture rate (45 SNU) assuming the fluxes predicted by the Standard Solar Model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that neutrino capture by \(^{131}\)Xe (threshold 352 keV) can serve as a solar neutrino detector with high sensitivity to \(^{7}\)Be neutrinos, which contribute approximately 40% of the total capture rate (45 SNU) under the Standard Solar Model fluxes; however, the total capture rate carries an uncertainty of 25–30% and the contribution from excited states is estimated from nuclear model calculations rather than direct measurement.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrino capture cross sections for \(^{131}\)Xe using the experimentally known ground-state-to-ground-state transition strength derived from the electron capture half-life of \(^{131}\)Cs (9.689 days, log(\(f_{EC}t_{EC}\)) = 5.53, giving \(B_F^+ + B_{GT}^+ = 0.018\)). They performed pn-QRPA calculations to estimate contributions from excited states, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental half-lives of cesium isotopes, and supplemented these with phenomenological systematics from beta-decay rates of neighboring nuclei (\(^{127}\)Te, \(^{129}\)Te, \(^{133}\)Xe, \(^{135}\)Xe). The total capture rate was then computed using the Standard Solar Model fluxes of Bahcall and Pinsonneault (1992).

## Key numbers

- Threshold for neutrino capture on \(^{131}\)Xe: 352 keV.
- Half-life of \(^{131}\)Cs daughter: 9.689 days.
- Ground-state-to-ground-state capture rate for \(^{7}\)Be neutrinos: 13.6 SNU; total capture rate including excited states for \(^{7}\)Be neutrinos: 17.8 SNU.
- Total capture rate for \(^{131}\)Xe from all solar neutrino sources: 45.2 \(^{+12}_{-6}\) SNU (uncertainty 25–30%).
- Expected event rate for a 1 kiloton detector: \(\approx\) 1500 events per kiloton per year.
- Abundance of \(^{131}\)Xe in natural xenon: 21.18%.
- Density of liquid xenon: 3.06 g/cm\(^3\); boiling temperature: 165 K.
- Drift mobility of Cs\(^+\) ions in liquid xenon: \(\approx 3 \times 10^{-4}\) cm\(^2\)/V·s, giving collection time over several meters less than half an hour at 1 kV/cm.
- Energy of K-line in \(^{131}\)Cs decay: 35 keV; L-line: 5 keV.

## Context

This work extends the search for a \(^{7}\)Be neutrino detector beyond existing proposals such as BOREXINO (liquid scintillator, 50 counts/day) and the \(^{127}\)I detector (threshold 0.789 MeV, capture rate 20–135 SNU with strong model dependence). The authors argue that the \(^{131}\)Xe detector offers a more reliable capture rate prediction than \(^{127}\)I and complements BOREXINO, which still faces background challenges. The proposed detector would enable determination of the \(pp\), \(^{7}\)Be, and \(^{8}\)B neutrino fluxes when combined with results from gallium (GALLEX, SAGE), chlorine (Homestake), and Superkamiokande experiments, helping to constrain solar models or test vacuum oscillations and the MSW effect.
