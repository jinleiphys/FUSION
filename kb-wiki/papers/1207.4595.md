# Nuclear binding, correlations and the origin of EMC effect

> Recent data for the slope of the EMC-ratio in the intermediate $x$-region for {\em light} nuclei, with $3 \leq A \leq 12$, have the potential to shed new light on the origin of the EMC effect. Here we study the role of nuclear binding using the scaling variable ${\tilde y}$, best suited to take into account this effect, and the understanding of the average nucleon removal energies, $\bar{E}$, provided by state-of-the-art calculations based on nuclear many body theory. We find an excellent correlation between the new EMC data at $x \sim 0.5$ and $\bar{E}$ for nuclei with $A$ from 3 to $\infty$, indicating that in this $x$ region binding is an important ingredient to explain the EMC effect. The role played by nucleon-nucleon correlations in this context is also discussed.

# Nuclear binding, correlations and the origin of EMC effect

## Key claim

The authors find an excellent linear correlation between the slope of the EMC ratio at intermediate x (x ~ 0.5) and the average nucleon removal energy for nuclei from A=3 to infinity, indicating that nuclear binding is an important ingredient, but not necessarily the sole driver, of the EMC effect in this x region. The claim is hedged: binding is described as "important" rather than "dominating," and the authors acknowledge that other mechanisms (pions, final state interactions) contribute at low x.

## Method

The authors analyze deep inelastic scattering data using the scaling variable ỹ (the m→0 limit of the y-scaling variable from quasi-elastic scattering), which accounts for nucleon off-shellness in the lab frame. They compute average nucleon removal energies Ē for light nuclei (A=3,4,9,12) using Koltun's sum rule applied to Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) results for binding energies and momentum distributions, including three-nucleon force contributions. The slope dR/dỹ is obtained from the measured dR/dx values of Seely et al. (2009) at x~0.5, and correlated with Ē across nuclei from ³He to infinite nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Average removal energy for ²H: 2.2 MeV (from Schiavilla 1986)
- Average removal energy for ³He: 14.6 MeV (GFMC, Pieper 2001)
- Average removal energy for ⁴He: 35.8 MeV (VMC, Pieper 2001)
- Average removal energy for ⁹Be: 43.8 MeV (GFMC, Pieper 2007)
- Average removal energy for ¹²C: 52.2 MeV (GFMC, Pieper 2007)
- Average removal energy for nuclear matter: 70.5 MeV (FHNC, Akmal 1998)
- For ¹²C, the mean-field removal energy from (e,e'p) is ~25 MeV, while the full GFMC value including short-range correlations is 52.2 MeV
- For ¹²C, Ē=25.2 MeV and T̄=31.2 MeV, giving E₀/A=6.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the EMC effect to light nuclei (A=3-12) using precise data from Seely et al. (2009), which had previously shown no unambiguous linear correlation with average density, binding energy, or nucleon separation energy. The authors contrast their approach with earlier studies (Ciofi 1991) that found binding effects alone insufficient to explain the EMC data, noting that those studies used much smaller removal energies (~25 MeV for ¹²C) derived from mean-field orbitals rather than the full spectral function including short-range correlations. The correlation with the inclusive quasi-elastic cross section at x>1 found by Weinstein (2011) and Hen (2012) is noted to share the same underlying physics, but the authors emphasize that high-momentum nucleons alone would give EMC ratios >1 and a positive slope, while binding produces the observed R<1 and negative slope. The work enables future theoretical calculations of the EMC slope using spectral functions from GFMC, once the full set of eigenstates of the recoiling nucleus becomes available.
