# Theoretical study of fusion reactions $^{32}$S + $^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca + $^{94,96}$Zr and quadrupole deformation of $^{94}$Zr

> The dynamic coupling effects on fusion cross sections for reactions $^{32}$S + $^{94,96}$Zr and $^{40}$Ca + $^{94,96}$Zr are studied with the universal fusion function formalism and an empirical coupled channel (ECC) model. An examination of the reduced fusion functions shows that the total effect of couplings to inelastic excitations and neutron transfer channels on fusion in $^{32}$S + $^{94}$Zr ($^{40}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr) is almost the same as that in $^{32}$S + $^{96}$Zr ($^{40}$Ca + $^{96}$Zr). The enhancements of the fusion cross section at sub-barrier energies due to inelastic channel coupling and neutron transfer channel coupling are evaluated separately by using the ECC model. The results show that effect of couplings to inelastic excitations channels in the reactions with $^{94}$Zr as target should be similar as that in the reactions with $^{96}$Zr as target. This implies that the quadrupole deformation parameters $β_2$ of $^{94}$Zr and $^{96}$Zr should be similar to each other. However, $β_2$'s predicted from the finite-range droplet model, which are used in the ECC model, are quite different. Experiments on $^{48}$Ca + $^{94}$Zr or $^{36}$S + $^{94}$Zr are suggested to solve the puzzling issue concerning $β_2$ for $^{94}$Zr.

# Theoretical study of fusion reactions \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94,96}\)Zr and \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{94,96}\)Zr and quadrupole deformation of \(^{94}\)Zr

## Key claim

The total effect of couplings to inelastic excitations and neutron transfer channels on fusion in \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr (\(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{94}\)Zr) is almost the same as that in \(^{32}\)S + \(^{96}\)Zr (\(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{96}\)Zr), which implies that the quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) of \(^{94}\)Zr and \(^{96}\)Zr should be similar to each other, contrary to the quite different values predicted by the finite-range droplet model (0.062 for \(^{94}\)Zr versus 0.217 for \(^{96}\)Zr).

## Method

The authors applied the universal fusion function (UFF) formalism to eliminate geometrical factors and static effects, then used an empirical coupled channel (ECC) model to separately evaluate the enhancements due to inelastic excitation couplings and neutron transfer channel couplings. The ECC model employs an asymmetric Gaussian barrier distribution whose parameters are related to quadrupole deformation parameters from the finite-range droplet model, with the effect of positive Q-value neutron pair transfer simulated by broadening the barrier distribution using the \(Q(2n)\) value multiplied by a factor of 0.32.

## Key numbers

- Barrier parameters for \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr: \(V_B = 80.64\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega = 3.95\) MeV, \(R_B = 10.64\) fm (from the Sao Paulo potential)
- Barrier parameters for \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{94}\)Zr: \(V_B = 99.07\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega = 3.97\) MeV, \(R_B = 10.84\) fm
- \(Q(2n)\) values: 5.10 MeV for \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr, 5.74 MeV for \(^{32}\)S + \(^{96}\)Zr, 4.89 MeV for \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{94}\)Zr, 5.53 MeV for \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{96}\)Zr
- FRDM quadrupole deformation parameters: \(\beta_2 = 0.062\) for \(^{94}\)Zr, \(\beta_2 = 0.217\) for \(^{96}\)Zr
- Experimental \(\beta_2\) from \(B(E2)\): 0.09 for \(^{94}\)Zr, 0.08 for \(^{96}\)Zr (Raman 2001)
- RMF (NL3) \(\beta_2\): 0.169 for \(^{94}\)Zr, 0.243 for \(^{96}\)Zr (Bhuyan 2015)
- RMF (NL3*) \(\beta_2\): 0.002 for \(^{94}\)Zr, 0.233 for \(^{96}\)Zr (Bhuyan 2015)

## Context

This work extends the systematic ECC model study of 217 reaction systems by Wang (2015) which found that calculated cross sections underestimated sub-barrier data for \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr and \(^{40}\)Ca + \(^{94}\)Zr. It also follows the quantum coupled channel calculations by Jia (2014) which suggested stronger neutron transfer effects in \(^{32}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr compared to \(^{32}\)S + \(^{96}\)Zr. The authors propose new experiments on \(^{48}\)Ca + \(^{94}\)Zr or \(^{36}\)S + \(^{94}\)Zr, where positive Q-value neutron transfer channels are closed, to resolve the puzzling issue of the quadrupole deformation of \(^{94}\)Zr.
