# $P_c(4457)^+$, $P_c(4440)^+$, and $P_c(4312)^+$: molecules or compact pentaquarks?

> In a chromomagnetic model, we analyse the properties of the newly observed $P_c(4457)^+$, $P_c(4440)^+$, and $P_c(4312)^+$ states. We estimate the masses of the $(uud)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ and $(uds)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ pentaquark states by considering the isospin breaking effects. Their values are determined by calculating mass distances from the $Œ£_c^{++}D^-$ and $Œû_c^{\prime+}D^-$ thresholds, respectively. It is found that the isospin breaking effects on the spectrum are small. From the uncertainty consideration and the rearrangement decay properties in a simple model, we find that it is possible to assign the $P_c(4457)^+$, $P_c(4440)^+$, and $P_c(4312)^+$ as $J^P=3/2^-$, $1/2^-$, and $3/2^-$ pentaquark states, respectively. The assignment in the molecule picture can be different, in particular for the $P_c(4312)^+$. The information from open-charm channels, e.g. ${\cal B}[P_c\toŒ£_c^{++}D^-]/{\cal B}[P_c\to J/œàp]$, will play an important role in distinguishing the inner structures of the $P_c$ states. Discussions and predictions based on the calculations are also given.

## Key claim
The three newly observed $P_c$ states can be assigned as compact pentaquarks in the $(uud)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ configuration, but the authors hedge that this assignment is only possible under the assumption that the chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) coupling parameters derived from conventional hadrons are applicable to multiquark states, and that the mass uncertainties from adjusting these parameters by $\pm 1$ MeV can reach tens of MeV. The molecule picture also explains the masses, and the two pictures cannot be distinguished without further data on open-charm decay branching ratios.

## Method
The authors used a chromomagnetic interaction (CMI) model to calculate mass spectra of $(uud)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ and $(uds)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ pentaquark states, incorporating isospin breaking effects via different $C_{uu}$, $C_{ud}$, and $C_{dd}$ coupling parameters determined from ground hadron mass splittings. They estimated pentaquark masses by calculating mass distances from the $\Sigma_c^{++}D^-$ and $\Xi_c^{\prime+}D^-$ thresholds, respectively, using the formula $M_{penta}=[M_{ref}-\langle H_{CM}\rangle_{ref}]+\langle H_{CM}\rangle_{penta}$. Rearrangement decay widths into open-charm channels were computed with a simple constant-amplitude model, where the squared amplitude is obtained from the overlap of initial and final state wave functions via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers
- Measured masses: $M(P_c(4312)^+)=4311.9\pm0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}$ MeV; $M(P_c(4440)^+)=4440.3\pm1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}$ MeV; $M(P_c(4457)^+)=4457.3\pm0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}$ MeV (LHCb 2019).
- Measured widths: $\Gamma(P_c(4312)^+)=9.8\pm2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5}$ MeV; $\Gamma(P_c(4440)^+)=20.6\pm4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}$ MeV; $\Gamma(P_c(4457)^+)=6.4\pm2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9}$ MeV.
- In the compact pentaquark picture, the $J^P=3/2^-$ state at 4448.4 MeV and the $J^P=1/2^-$ state at 4451.0 MeV (estimated with $\Sigma_c^{++}D^-$ threshold) match $P_c(4457)^+$ and $P_c(4440)^+$, respectively, with a total width ratio $\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4451))/\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4448))\approx 2.6$.
- The lowest $J^P=3/2^-$ state at 4339.4 MeV matches $P_c(4312)^+$, giving $\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4451))/\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4339))\approx 2.4$ and $\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4339))/\Gamma_{tot}(P_c(4448))\approx 1.1$.
- Isospin breaking effects in the pentaquark spectrum are small: $|M_{dduc\bar{c}}-M_{uudc\bar{c}}|<3$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CMI model study of $(qqq)_{8_c}(c\bar{c})_{8_c}$ pentaquarks (Wu 2017) by including isospin breaking effects and decay calculations. It directly addresses the three $P_c$ states newly observed by LHCb (Aaij 2019), which replaced the earlier $P_c(4450)$ seen in 2015 (Aaij 2015). The compact pentaquark interpretation competes with the molecule picture favored by most other studies, including one-boson-exchange models (Chen 2019), chiral EFT (Meng 2019), and QCD sum rules (Chen 2019; Zhang 2019). The authors note that the diquark-diquark-antidiquark picture (Ali 2019) and tightly bound pentaquark picture (Weng 2019) also give masses close to measured values. The key discriminator proposed is the ratio of branching fractions ${\cal B}[P_c\to\Sigma_c^{++}D^-]/{\cal B}[P_c\to J/\psi p]$, which differs between the two pictures; in the compact picture, open-charm decays dominate while hidden-charm decays are suppressed, consistent with the constraint ${\cal B}(P_c\to J/\psi p)<2\%$ from Cao (2019).
