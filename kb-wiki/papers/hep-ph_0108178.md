# Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking with Abnormal Number of Nambu-Goldstone Bosons and Kaon Condensate

> We describe a class of relativistic models incorporating finite density of matter in which spontaneous breakdown of continuous symmetries leads to a lesser number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons than that required by the Goldstone theorem. This class, in particular, describes the dynamics of the kaon condensate in the color-flavor locked phase of high density QCD. We describe the spectrum of low energy excitations in this dynamics and show that, despite the presence of a condensate and gapless excitations, this system is not a superfluid.

## Key claim

In a class of relativistic models with finite density (broken Lorentz symmetry), spontaneous breakdown of continuous symmetries can produce fewer gapless Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons than the number of broken generators, even though the ground-state degeneracy remains conventional; the system with a kaon condensate in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of high-density QCD exhibits this abnormal counting and, despite having gapless excitations, is not a superfluid.

## Method

The authors analyze a toy model with a complex doublet scalar field and a chemical potential, where the Lagrangian density breaks Lorentz symmetry explicitly via the chemical potential term. They compute the tree-level particle spectrum in both the symmetric phase (μ < m) and the broken phase (μ > m), deriving dispersion relations from the quadratic part of the action. They then map this toy model onto the effective low-energy action for the kaon condensate in the CFL phase of dense QCD, using the covariant derivative introduced by Bedaque and Schäfer (2001) that generates effective chemical potentials from quark mass matrices, and derive the dispersion relations for the charged and neutral excitation sets in the condensed ground state.

## Key numbers

- In the toy model broken phase, the vacuum expectation value is φ₀² = (μ² − m²)/(2λ).
- In the toy model, the charged gapless NG boson has dispersion ω₂ ≃ v²q²/(2μ) in the far infrared (q → 0).
- In the toy model, the neutral gapless NG boson has dispersion ω̃₂ ≃ √[(μ² − m²)/(3μ² − m²)] v q in the far infrared.
- In the CFL kaon-condensate phase, the charged gapless NG boson has dispersion ω ≃ q²/[3μ cosα (1 + cosα)] for q → 0, where μ = (m_s² − m_u²)/(2p_F).
- In the CFL kaon-condensate phase, the neutral gapless NG boson has dispersion ω ≃ [sinα/√(2 − 7cos²α + 9cos⁴α)] (q/√3) for q → 0.
- The kaon condensate angle α is determined by cosα = [4c p_F² m_u (m_s + m_u)]/[f_π² (m_s² − m_u²)²] < 1, with c = 3Δ²/(2π²) and f_π² = (21 − 8 ln2)/36 × p_F²/π².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of kaon condensation in the CFL phase of dense QCD, originally suggested by Schäfer (2000) and further developed by Bedaque and Schäfer (2001) and Kaplan and Reddy (2001). The authors show that the abnormal NG boson counting, three broken generators but only two gapless modes, arises from first-order derivative terms in the kinetic part (induced by the chemical potential), which split the energy gaps of charged particle-antiparticle pairs that would otherwise both be NG bosons. The result implies that dense quark matter with a kaon condensate is not a superfluid, which could serve as a distinctive signature for detecting this phase in compact-star cores. The phenomenon is related to analogous behavior in ferromagnets and in superfluid ³He in the A-phase, as discussed by Volovik (1992).
