# Isospin-breaking corrections to the pion-nucleon scattering lengths

> We analyze isospin breaking through quark mass differences and virtual photons in the pion-nucleon scattering lengths in all physical channels in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. The so-called triangle relation is found to be violated by about 1.5 %. We encounter a substantial isospin-breaking correction to neutral-pion-nucleon scattering beyond Weinberg's prediction due to a cusp effect. Finally, the application to hadronic atoms is briefly discussed.

# Isospin-breaking corrections to the pion-nucleon scattering lengths

## Key claim

The triangle relation, which quantifies isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering, is violated by about 1.5%, consistent with earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory results but inconsistent with the 5-7% deviation extracted from experimental data at lowest pion momenta. A substantial isospin-breaking correction to neutral-pion-nucleon scattering beyond Weinberg's prediction arises from a cusp effect, contributing roughly one third of the final result.

## Method

The calculation was performed at third chiral order (Order(p^3)) in manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, at first order in the isospin-breaking parameter delta (order e^2 and m_d - m_u), using the effective Lagrangian for nucleons, pions, and virtual photons. One-loop topologies were evaluated at threshold, with one-photon-reducible diagrams subtracted and threshold divergences regularized via Coulomb phase subtraction. The scattering lengths were extracted from the threshold amplitude T_thr via a = T_thr / (8 pi sqrt(s)).

## Key numbers

- Triangle relation R = (1.5 ± 1.1)%
- Isospin-breaking shift for pi^- p -> pi^- p: (-3.4^{+4.3}_{-6.5} + 5.0 i) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
- Isospin-breaking shift for pi^+ p -> pi^+ p: (-5.3^{+4.3}_{-6.5}) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
- Isospin-breaking shift for pi^- p -> pi^0 n: (0.4 ± 0.9) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
- Isospin-breaking shift for pi^0 p -> pi^0 p: (-5.2 ± 0.2) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
- Isospin-breaking shift for pi^0 n -> pi^0 n: (-1.8 ± 0.2) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}
- Difference a_{pi^0 p} - a_{pi^0 n} = (-3.4 ± 0.4) x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}, with the cusp contribution alone at -1.1 x 10^{-3} M_pi^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Gasser and Rusetsky (2002) at third chiral order for the pi^- p -> pi^- p channel to all physical charge channels in pion-nucleon scattering. It provides the isospin-breaking corrections necessary for extracting the isoscalar and isovector scattering lengths a^+ and a^- from measurements of pionic hydrogen and deuterium, and enables application to threshold pion photoproduction experiments at HIgammaS and MAMI. The results contradict the 5-7% deviation extracted from data by Gibbs (1995) and Matsinos (1997), and show that at full third chiral order the isoscalar scattering length increases compared to previous analyses of hadronic atoms by Meissner, Raha, and Rusetsky (2006).
