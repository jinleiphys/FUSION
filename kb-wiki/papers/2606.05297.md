# Continuous-variable ADAPT-VQE for bosonic lattice models

> We present a continuous-variable adaptive variational quantum eigensolver (CV-ADAPT-VQE). As concrete examples, we consider the ground-state preparation for (i) the Bose-Hubbard model and (ii) the bosonic Kitaev chain, including its extension with an on-site Kerr interaction. The former conserves the total boson number, while the latter conserves global parity. We construct symmetry-preserving operator pools tailored to each case and show, using GPU-based classical simulations, that CV-ADAPT-VQE results in significantly shallower circuits compared to Hamiltonian-based VQE approaches. Our results point toward direct applications in quantum simulations of condensed-matter systems, quantum chemistry, and high-energy physics.

## Key claim
CV-ADAPT-VQE achieves significantly shallower circuits than Hamiltonian-based VQE approaches for ground-state preparation of bosonic lattice models, but only when symmetry-preserving operator pools are tailored to each model's conserved quantities (total boson number for the Bose-Hubbard model; global parity for the bosonic Kitaev chain).

## Method
The authors implement a continuous-variable adaptive variational quantum eigensolver (CV-ADAPT-VQE) that iteratively selects operators from predefined symmetry-preserving pools based on energy gradients, constructing the ansatz dynamically. They benchmark this against a fixed-structure Hamiltonian Variational Ansatz (HVA-CV-VQE) using GPU-based classical simulations in the Fock representation with a local Hilbert space truncation D. The method is applied to three bosonic lattice models: the Bose-Hubbard model (number-conserving), the bosonic Kitaev chain (parity-conserving), and the bosonic Kitaev chain with an additional on-site Kerr interaction.

## Key numbers
- For the Bose-Hubbard model, the local Hilbert space truncation is set to D = N_tot + 1 levels, which introduces no numerical errors due to particle-number conservation.
- The dimensionless parameter quantifying hopping versus interaction strength in the Bose-Hubbard model is defined as Λ_BH = N_tot U / J.
- For the bosonic Kitaev chain, the stability condition ensuring real energy eigenvalues is μ < -2(J + Δ).
- The maximum average boson number observed in the bosonic Kitaev chain ground state is ⟨N_tot⟩ ≈ 10 for a lattice with N_S = 3 sites and local truncation D = 6.
- The HVA circuits use 3 N_S variational gates per layer for both the Bose-Hubbard and bosonic Kitaev chain models, with an additional N_S Kerr gates when including the on-site interaction.

## Context
This work extends the ADAPT-VQE algorithm (Grimsley 2019) from discrete-variable qubit systems to continuous-variable qumode architectures, targeting bosonic lattice models. It builds on earlier Hamiltonian-based CV-VQE results for the Bose-Hubbard model (Yalouz 2021) and general CV-VQE approaches (Kang 2023, Dutta 2024, Chiari 2025), while differing from vibrational-structure ADAPT-VQE (Majland 2025) by acting natively on qumodes with bosonic ladder operators rather than qubit-encoded vibrational modes. The results enable direct applications in quantum simulations of condensed-matter systems, quantum chemistry, and high-energy physics using continuous-variable quantum hardware.
