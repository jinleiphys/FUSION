# Lepton polarization asymmetries for FCNC decays of the $Λ_b$ baryon

> Branching ratios, lepton forward-backward asymmetries, and lepton polarization asymmetries for the flavor-changing neutral current (FCNC) dileptonic decays of the $Λ_b$ baryon to the ground state and a number of excited state $Λ$ baryons are calculated using form factors extracted using wave functions from a constituent quark model. The SM branching ratios for the transition to the ground state calculated using these quark model form factors are consistent with the recent measurement reported by the LHCb collaboration. It is shown that the lepton polarization asymmetries are largely insensitive to the transition form factors and, therefore, to the effects of QCD in the nonperturbative regime. These observables can therefore provide somewhat model independent ways of extracting various combinations of the Wilson coefficients.

## Key claim

The lepton polarization asymmetries for flavor-changing neutral current (FCNC) dileptonic decays of the $\Lambda_b$ baryon to ground and excited state $\Lambda$ baryons are largely insensitive to the transition form factors and therefore to nonperturbative QCD effects, making them somewhat model-independent probes of Wilson coefficients, provided the constituent quark model form factors used here are reliable.

## Method

The authors calculated branching ratios, lepton forward-backward asymmetries, and lepton polarization asymmetries for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda^{(*)} \ell^+ \ell^-$ decays ($\ell = \mu, \tau$) using form factors extracted from two constituent quark model approaches: the simple current approximation (SCA) and the more complete MCN model, with wave functions from a Hamiltonian fit to known baryon masses. They used Standard Model Wilson coefficients evaluated at scale $\mu = 5.0$ GeV with $m_t = 174$ GeV and $\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 225$ MeV, and also considered one supersymmetric scenario (SUSYI) where only $C_7$, $C_9$, and $C_{10}$ are modified. For the ground state $\Lambda(1115)$, they compared their results with predictions from light-cone sum rules (LCSR), QCD sum rules (QCDSR), and a multipole model (PM), as well as with experimental data from CDF and LHCb.

## Key numbers

- SM branching ratio for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \mu^+ \mu^-$ without long-distance contributions: $0.66 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM1, SCA form factors) and $0.59 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM2, MCN form factors); LHCb measurement: $0.96 \pm 0.16 \pm 0.13 \pm 0.21 \times 10^{-6}$
- SM branching ratio for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \tau^+ \tau^-$ without long-distance contributions: $0.24 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM1) and $0.21 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM2)
- SM branching ratio for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1600) \mu^+ \mu^-$ without long-distance contributions: $0.030 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM1) and $0.23 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM2); with long-distance contributions: $2.6 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM1) and $24 \times 10^{-6}$ (SM2)
- Integrated forward-backward asymmetry for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \mu^+ \mu^-$ without long-distance contributions: $-0.1336$ (SM1) and $-0.1216$ (SM2)
- Zero of forward-backward asymmetry for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115) \mu^+ \mu^-$ without long-distance contributions: $\hat s_0 = 0.097$ (SM1) and $0.094$ (SM2)
- Ratio of HQET form factors $\xi_2/\xi_1$ at nonrecoil point for $\Lambda_b \to \Lambda(1115)$: ranges from $-0.064$ to $-0.193$ depending on extraction method and model, consistent with CLEO measurement of $-0.25 \pm 0.14 \pm 0.08$ for $\Lambda_c \to \Lambda(1115)$

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $\Lambda_b$ FCNC decays by using constituent quark model form factors (SCA and MCN) to predict observables for multiple final states, including excited $\Lambda$ baryons up to spin $5/2$. It builds on the formalism of Mott and Roberts (2012) for form factors and compares with predictions from LCSR (Aslam, 2012), QCDSR (Chen, 2007), and PM approaches. The results show that searches for rare $\Lambda_b$ decays at LHCb should include excited final states, as their branching ratios can be comparable to the ground state, analogous to the situation in $B$ meson rare decays where $K$ and $K^*$ modes account for less than half the inclusive dileptonic rate. The lepton polarization asymmetries provide a path to extract Wilson coefficients with reduced hadronic uncertainty.
