# Effect of final state interactions on particle production in $d$+Au collisions at RHIC

> We show that particle species dependence of enhanced hadron production at intermediate transverse momentum (p_{T}) for d+Au collisions at RHIC can be understood in terms of the hadronic rescatterings in the final state. A multiphase transport model (AMPT) with two different hadronization mechanisms: string fragmentation or parton coalescence, is used in our study. When the hadrons are formed from string fragmentation, the subsequent hadronic rescatterings will result in particle mass dependence of nuclear modification factor R_{CP}, which is consistent with the present experimental data. On the other hand, in the framework of parton coalescence, the mass dependence disappears and the strangeness plays an important role. Both mechanisms failed to reproduce the p_{T} dependence of R_{CP} of pion, indicating that initial-state effects might be also important in such collisions.

## Key claim

The particle species dependence of the Cronin effect (enhanced hadron production at intermediate transverse momentum) in d+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by hadronic rescatterings in the final state when hadrons are formed via string fragmentation, but both string fragmentation and parton coalescence mechanisms fail to reproduce the pT dependence of the nuclear modification factor RCP for pions, indicating that initial-state effects are also important.

## Method

The authors used the multiphase transport model (AMPT) with two hadronization mechanisms: the default version (Lund string fragmentation followed by hadronic rescatterings, version 1.11) and the string melting version (parton coalescence, version 2.11). They simulated d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, separating events into centrality bins using the number of participant nucleons, and calculated the central-to-peripheral nuclear modification factor RCP (0-20%/40-100% centrality) for various hadron species with and without final-state hadronic interactions and resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 200 GeV for d+Au collisions at RHIC
- Partonic scattering cross section chosen as 3 mb
- For a momentum p = 2.0 GeV/c pion, formation time is ~9.5 fm/c (for Rh = 0.663 fm); for p = 2.0 GeV/c protons (Rh = 0.88 fm), formation time is ~2.1 fm/c
- The RCP ratio RCP(anti-p)/RCP(pi-) from the default AMPT model agrees very well with experimental data at intermediate pT
- For string melting AMPT, at intermediate pT there is an enhancement of RCP according to the number of anti-s quarks: RCP(anti-Lambda) < RCP(anti-Xi+) < RCP(anti-Omega+)

## Context

This work extends studies of the Cronin effect, first observed by Cronin et al. (1975) at √sNN = 27.4 GeV, to higher collision energies at RHIC. It builds on prior AMPT model studies by Lin and Ko (2002) that showed good agreement with global properties of d+Au collisions up to pT = 2 GeV/c. The paper contrasts with initial-state models (theo1-theo6) that reproduce centrality dependence for pions but cannot predict species-dependent Cronin effects, and with the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) which predicts mass-dependent Cronin effects but requires high initial energy density. The results enable future tests with higher-statistics RHIC data from 2008 and motivate more complete analyses incorporating initial-state effects such as gluon saturation and parton intrinsic pT broadening.
