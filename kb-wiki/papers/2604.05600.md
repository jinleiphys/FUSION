# Channel couplings redirect absorbed flux from peripheral loss to fusion in weakly bound nuclear reactions

> In reactions of weakly bound nuclei, the absorption cross section mixes two physically distinct contributions: inner capture associated with compound-nucleus formation, and peripheral losses from breakup, transfer, and other direct reactions. Within a framework that combines an ingoing-wave boundary condition (IWBC) at an inner radius with a complex potential in the external region, we derive the exact flux identity $σ_{\rm abs}=σ_{\rm fusion}+σ_W$ from the radial continuity equation. The resulting partition is exact within the adopted CC/CDCC model space and provides a practical diagnostic of where absorbed flux is removed. Applied to $^6$Li+$^{209}$Bi, the analysis reveals that channel couplings qualitatively reorganize the absorbed flux: the dominant absorption mechanism shifts from peripheral loss at sub-barrier energies to inner capture above the barrier, whereas the single-channel baseline remains peripheral-loss dominated throughout. The resulting IWBC-defined inner-capture cross section tracks the measured complete-fusion excitation function with only a modest dependence on the chosen boundary radius. Together with the exact identity $σ_{\rm abs}=σ_{\rm fusion}+σ_W$, this agreement supports interpreting the peripheral term $σ_W$ as a major spatial contributor to the well-known CF suppression in weakly bound systems.

# Channel couplings redirect absorbed flux from peripheral loss to fusion in weakly bound nuclear reactions

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact flux identity σ_abs = σ_fusion + σ_W that partitions total absorption into inner capture (fusion) and peripheral loss within a coupled-channels framework, and shows that channel couplings qualitatively reorganize the absorbed flux in ⁶Li+²⁰⁹Bi: the dominant absorption mechanism shifts from peripheral loss at sub-barrier energies to inner capture above the barrier, whereas the single-channel baseline remains peripheral-loss dominated throughout. The claim is conditional on the chosen IWBC radius, the adopted model space, and the specific absorptive operator.

## Method

The authors combine an ingoing-wave boundary condition (IWBC) at an inner radius r_a = 10 fm with a complex potential in the external region, then derive the exact flux identity σ_abs = σ_fusion + σ_W from the radial continuity equation for both single-channel and multichannel systems. They apply the formalism to ⁶Li+²⁰⁹Bi using a single-channel optical-model calculation with the global potential of Maslov as a baseline, and a multichannel CDCC calculation where ⁶Li is described as an α+d cluster with the breakup continuum discretized into bins up to 12 MeV α-d relative kinetic energy and relative orbital angular momentum ℓ ≤ 2, with the surface imaginary part of the deuteron-target potential removed to avoid double counting.

## Key numbers

- For the single-channel calculation at E_lab = 26 MeV: σ_abs = 22.4 mb, σ_fusion = 0.03 mb, σ_W = 22.4 mb; at 52 MeV: σ_abs = 1888.8 mb, σ_fusion = 715.5 mb, σ_W = 1182.0 mb
- For the multichannel CDCC calculation at E_lab = 26 MeV: σ_abs = 39.4 mb, σ_fusion = 1.7 mb, σ_W = 37.6 mb; at 52 MeV: σ_abs = 1770.8 mb, σ_fusion = 1193.7 mb, σ_W = 577.1 mb
- The fusion fraction f_fusion in the multichannel case rises from 0.04 at 26 MeV to 0.51 at 36 MeV and 0.67 at 52 MeV; the two fractions cross near E_lab ≈ 36 MeV
- The closure error |ε|/σ_abs remains below 5×10⁻³ for single-channel and below 10⁻⁴ for multichannel calculations
- The IWBC radius r_a = 10 fm corresponds to the nuclear touching configuration R_P + R_T ≈ 1.3(A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3}) fm; sensitivity is quantified by varying r_a over 9.5–10.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the spatial decomposition approach of Satchler (relating imaginary potentials to total reaction cross section via the optical theorem), Udagawa et al. (separating direct-reaction and compound-nucleus contributions within a local-potential model), and the generalized optical theorem applied in CDCC by Cotanch and others to extract elastic-breakup components. The formalism provides a diagnostic tool for coupled-channel reaction dynamics that enables asking not only how much flux is absorbed but where, offering a sharper basis for discussing fusion suppression and enhancement in weakly bound systems. The authors suggest extending the framework to other weakly bound projectiles such as ⁷Li, ⁹Be, and ¹¹Be to test whether the crossover between peripheral loss and inner capture is a generic feature of complete fusion.
