# Investigating the pion source function in heavy-ion collisions with the EPOS model

> By measuring the momentum correlations of pions created in heavy-ion collisions we can gain information about the space-time geometry of the particle emitting source. Recent experimental results from multiple different collaborations demonstrated that to properly describe the shape of the measured correlation functions, one needs to go beyond the Gaussian approximation. Some studies suggest that the Levy distribution could provide a good description of the source. While there are already many experimental results, there is very little input from the phenomenology side in explanation of the observed non-Gaussian source shapes. The EPOS model is a sophisticated hybrid model where the evolution of the newly-created system is governed by Parton-Based Gribov-Regge theory. It has already proved to be successful in describing many different experimental observations for the systems characterized by baryon chemical potential close to zero, but so far the source shape has not been explored in detail. In this paper we discuss studies of the pion emitting source based on the theoretical approach of the EPOS model.

## Key claim
The EPOS model can produce a pion source function whose shape closely resembles a Lévy distribution when resonance decay pions are included, but the extracted Lévy exponent α shows an increasing trend with centrality and transverse momentum, which contradicts experimental results that show no strong kT dependence and a decreasing trend with centrality.

## Method
The authors used the EPOS359 event generator to simulate √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, keeping only pions from the hydrodynamically expanding core. They measured the angle-averaged radial source distribution D(r1,2) for like-sign pion pairs as a function of pair separation distance, for different centrality and average transverse momentum kT ranges, both with and without including pions from resonance decays. The distributions including resonance decay products were fitted with a Lévy distribution in the range 1 fm to 500 fm to extract the Lévy scale parameter R and the Lévy exponent α.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions
- Single particle transverse momentum cut: 0.15 GeV/c < pT < 2 GeV/c
- The Lévy fits were performed in the range of 1 fm to 500 fm
- The hadronization hyper surface is characterized by constant energy density ε = 0.57 GeV/fm³
- The Lévy scale parameter R shows a geometrical centrality dependence and a decreasing behavior with kT, with magnitudes similar to experimental results from Adare (2017) and Lokos (2018)
- The Lévy exponent α shows an increasing trend with both kT and centrality, with magnitudes similar to experimental results but opposite trends

## Context
This work extends phenomenological studies of non-Gaussian source shapes in heavy-ion collisions, following experimental results from multiple collaborations (Adare 2017, Kincses 2019, Porfy 2019) that showed Lévy-type sources provide statistically acceptable descriptions of femtoscopic correlations. The EPOS model, based on Parton-Based Gribov-Regge theory (Werner 2010), had previously been successful in describing many experimental observations for systems with baryon chemical potential near zero, but the source shape had not been explored in detail. The results enable future investigations of the source distribution in multiple dimensions and different coordinate frames, as well as studies of the pair-source for different particle species such as kaons and protons.
