# Thermal modifications of quarkonia and heavy quark diffusion from a comparison of continuum-extrapolated lattice results to perturbative QCD

> We investigate the in-medium modifications of heavy quarkonia in the vector channel and the heavy quark diffusion coefficient by comparing Euclidean correlators from the lattice to a perturbative spectral function. On the lattice side, we work with continuum extrapolated data from four different large and fine lattices with Clover-improved Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation at five temperatures (0.75, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5 and 2.25$T_c$). On the perturbative side, we use a combination of pNRQCD and vacuum asymptotics to describe the spectral function. After accounting for systematic errors, we obtain a spectral function that is suited to describe the bound state region. This spectral function describes charmonium well without a resonance peak at any of our analyzed temperatures above $T_c$, while we observe a thermally broadened resonance peak for bottomonium that is only melted at our largest temperature, $2.25T_c$. For the transport contribution we assume a Breit-Wigner shaped peak and find that the drag coefficient of charm quarks is larger than that of bottom quarks.

## Key claim

By comparing continuum-extrapolated lattice correlators to a perturbative spectral function constructed from pNRQCD and vacuum asymptotics, the authors find that charmonium in the vector channel shows no resonance peak at any temperature above \(T_c\), while bottomonium exhibits a thermally broadened resonance peak that persists up to approximately \(1.5T_c\) and melts only at \(2.25T_c\); the drag coefficient of charm quarks is larger than that of bottom quarks.

## Method

The authors used continuum-extrapolated lattice data from four quenched lattices with Clover-improved Wilson fermions at five temperatures (0.75, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5, and 2.25 \(T_c\)). They constructed a perturbative spectral function by combining vacuum asymptotics at high energies with pNRQCD calculations in the threshold region, introducing a matching factor and an exponential cutoff. To separate the bound-state and transport contributions, they fitted the perturbative part to the difference of neighboring Euclidean correlators, then analyzed the remaining transport contribution at the midpoint using a Breit-Wigner ansatz for the transport peak.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings used: \(a = 0.018\) fm (11.19 GeV), 0.014 fm (14.24 GeV), 0.012 fm (17.01 GeV), and 0.009 fm (22.78 GeV), determined from the Sommer scale.
- Temperatures studied: 0.75, 1.1, 1.3, 1.5, and 2.25 \(T_c\).
- For charmonium at \(T/T_c = 1.1\): fit parameters \(A = 1.09(2)\) and \(B/T = 0.37(4)\).
- For bottomonium at \(T/T_c = 1.1\): fit parameters \(A = 1.03(2)\) and \(B/T = 0.04(2)\).
- For charmonium at \(T/T_c = 2.25\): fit parameters \(A = 0.99(3)\) and \(B/T = -0.27(9)\).
- For bottomonium at \(T/T_c = 2.25\): fit parameters \(A = 0.99(2)\) and \(B/T = -0.23(4)\).
- The ratio of bottom to charm quark masses is approximately 3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the pseudoscalar channel (Lorenz et al., 2019) to the vector channel, where an additional transport peak from heavy quark diffusion must be included. The method of comparing continuum-extrapolated lattice correlators to perturbative spectral functions built from pNRQCD and vacuum asymptotics follows the approach established in that prior work. The results enable a more detailed determination of heavy quark diffusion coefficients and drag coefficients for charm and bottom quarks, with ongoing work (Lorenz et al., in progress) investigating the transport peak using different ansatze.
