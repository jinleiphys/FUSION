# 6Li direct breakup lifetimes

> alpha-d coincidence data were studied for the 6Li + 59Co reaction at E(lab) = 29.6 MeV. By using a kinematic analysis, it was possible to identify which process, leading to the same final state, has the major contribution for each of the selected angular regions. Contributions of the 6Li sequential and direct breakup to the incomplete fusion/transfer process were discussed by considering the lifetimes obtained by using a semiclassical approach, for both breakup components.

## Key claim

By combining alpha-deuteron coincidence measurements with a semiclassical lifetime analysis, the authors show that direct breakup (DBU) of 6Li into the continuum occurs with lifetimes at least one order of magnitude shorter than the sequential breakup (SBU) lifetime of the 2.186 MeV 3+ resonant state, implying that DBU happens much closer to the target and is therefore the dominant source of flux diverted from complete fusion to incomplete fusion and transfer processes.

## Method

Alpha-deuteron coincidence data were measured for the 6Li + 59Co reaction at Elab = 29.6 MeV using eleven triple telescopes covering angles from -45° to -15° and 15° to 75°. A three-body kinematic analysis identified the dominant reaction mechanism (sequential breakup, direct breakup, or incomplete fusion/transfer) for each angular region. Lifetimes for direct breakup continuum states were estimated using a semiclassical barrier-tunneling model (Tokimoto 2001), while the sequential breakup lifetime was taken from the known width of the 2.186 MeV 3+ state.

## Key numbers

- Sequential breakup lifetime for the 6Li 3+ state at E* = 2.186 MeV: tau_SBU = 2.73 × 10^(-20) s, corresponding to Gamma_SBU = (0.024 ± 0.002) MeV
- Direct breakup lifetimes (tau_DBU) are at least one order of magnitude shorter than the sequential breakup lifetime
- Beam energy: Elab = 29.6 MeV; target thickness: 2.2 mg/cm^2 59Co
- Intermediate nucleus excitation energies: 24.6 MeV for 61Ni (d-incomplete fusion/transfer) and 22.5 MeV for 63Cu (alpha-incomplete fusion/transfer)
- Direct breakup angular distributions summed over 6Li continuum excitation energies from E* = 1.66 MeV to E* = 2.10 MeV; additional DBU ranges: E* = 2.20 to 2.40 MeV and E* = 2.41 to 3.98 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous singles-mode study of the 6Li + 59Co system (Souza 2009) by using coincidence measurements to disentangle sequential and direct breakup contributions from incomplete fusion and transfer processes. The semiclassical lifetime analysis follows the approach of Kanungo (1996) and Tokimoto (2001), and the angular distributions are compared to Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations (Beck 2007, 2008). The finding that direct breakup has much shorter lifetimes than sequential breakup explains why incomplete fusion and transfer, which dominate the total reaction cross section in this system (Souza 2009, Santra 2009), arise primarily from the direct breakup component.
