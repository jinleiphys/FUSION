# Spectrum of low-lying $s^{3}Q\bar{Q}$ configurations with negative parity

> Spectrum of low-lying five-quark configurations with strangeness quantum number $S=-3$ and negative parity is studied in three kinds of constituent quark models, namely the one gluon exchange, Goldstone Boson exchange, and instanton-induced hyperfine interaction models, respectively. Our numerical results show that the lowest energy states in all the three employed models are lying at $\sim$1800 MeV, about 200 MeV lower than predictions of various quenched three-quark models. In addition, it is very interesting that the state with the lowest energy in one gluon exchange model is with spin 3/2, but 1/2 in the other two models.

## Key claim

The lowest-energy five-quark configurations with strangeness -3 and negative parity lie at approximately 1800 MeV across all three constituent quark models studied, which is about 200 MeV lower than predictions from traditional three-quark models; however, the spin of this lowest state depends on the hyperfine interaction model, being 3/2 in the one-gluon-exchange model but 1/2 in the Goldstone-boson-exchange and instanton-induced models.

## Method

The authors constructed nine low-lying \(s^{3}Q\bar{Q}\) configurations (with \(Q\bar{Q}\) being either light \(q\bar{q}\) or \(s\bar{s}\)) using harmonic-oscillator confinement and treated hyperfine interactions as perturbations. They employed three distinct hyperfine interaction models, one-gluon exchange (OGE), Goldstone boson exchange (GBE), and instanton-induced (INS) interactions, and diagonalized the resulting energy matrices for spin 1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 states, including flavor SU(3) symmetry-breaking corrections for the light-quark mass difference.

## Key numbers

- Lowest-energy state across all three models: approximately 1800 MeV, about 200 MeV lower than predictions from quenched three-quark models.
- In the OGE model, the lowest-energy state has spin 3/2; in the GBE and INS models, the lowest-energy state has spin 1/2.
- For configurations with light \(q\bar{q}\) pairs, the unperturbed orbital-plus-mass energies \(E_0\) are 2196 MeV (OGE), 1891 MeV (GBE), and 2171 MeV (INS); for configurations with \(s\bar{s}\) pairs, they are 2436 MeV (OGE), 2131 MeV (GBE), and 2411 MeV (INS).
- After diagonalization, the lowest spin-3/2 state in the OGE model is about 328 MeV lower than the lowest spin-1/2 state.
- In the GBE model, the mixing angle between configurations 2 and 3 for spin 1/2 is about 0.14 radians.
- The oscillator frequency used is \(\omega \approx 196\) MeV, derived from \(\omega_3 = 251\) MeV for three-light-quark systems.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model to include explicit five-quark components in \(\Omega^*\) hyperons, motivated by earlier studies showing notable five-quark components in baryon resonances (Bijker 2009, Santopinto 2010, An 2008-2011). It directly compares three hyperfine interaction models, OGE (Isgur 1977-1979, Capstick 2000), GBE (Glozman and Riska 1996, Helminen 2001), and INS (Blask 1990, Klempt 1995, Koll 2000), applied to the \(s^3 Q\bar{Q}\) system. The results suggest that five-quark components may be energetically favored over traditional three-quark orbital excitations in \(\Omega^*\) states, and that future measurements of \(\Psi^{\prime}\) decay at BESIII could test whether the lowest \(\Omega^*\) lies near 1800 MeV and distinguish between the OGE and the other two models based on the spin of that state.
