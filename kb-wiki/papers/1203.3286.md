# Symmetry breaking and fluctuations within stochastic mean-field dynamics: importance of initial quantum fluctuations

> Dynamics of spontaneous symmetry breaking and fluctuations in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model are investigated in a stochastic mean-field approach. Different from the standard mean-field, in the stochastic approach, initial state fluctuations, are incorporated. In weak coupling, the approach perfectly reproduces the exact quantal dynamics. On the other hand, for increasing coupling strength, above the symmetry breaking threshold, the approach provides description of gross properties (i.e. time averaged behavior) of the exact quantal evolution.

# Symmetry breaking and fluctuations within stochastic mean-field dynamics: importance of initial quantum fluctuations

## Key claim

The stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach, which incorporates initial quantum fluctuations via an ensemble of initial states, can describe spontaneous symmetry breaking and reproduce the gross (time-averaged) properties of exact quantal dynamics in the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, even above the symmetry-breaking threshold where the standard mean-field and the Balian-Veneroni variational approach both fail. However, the approach does not capture detailed quantum mechanical features such as interferences between trajectories or tunneling effects.

## Method

The authors apply the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model of N particles distributed in two N-fold degenerate single-particle states. Instead of a single deterministic initial Hartree-Fock state, an ensemble of initial single-particle density matrices is prepared around the same state |j,-j>, with the x and y quasi-spin components treated as uncorrelated Gaussian random numbers with zero mean and variance 1/(4N). Each trajectory evolves independently according to the mean-field equations of motion for the quasi-spin expectation values, and observables are obtained by averaging over the ensemble of 10^5 trajectories using a second-order Runge-Kutta algorithm with time step 0.01.

## Key numbers

- For N=40 particles, the initial state |j,-j> has j_z(t0) = -1/2 and j_x(t0)=j_y(t0)=0; the SMF initial fluctuations have variance 1/(4N) = 1/160 for the x and y components.
- The critical strength parameter for symmetry breaking is chi = V(N-1)/epsilon = 1; simulations are performed at chi = 0.5 (below threshold), chi = 1.8, and chi = 5.0 (above threshold).
- In the standard TDHF dynamics, dispersions remain constant at their initial values: Delta^2_x = Delta^2_y = N/4 = 10 and Delta^2_z = 0 for N=40.
- The SMF simulations use 10^5 trajectories with a time step of 0.01 in units of hbar/epsilon.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach proposed by Ayik (2008) to the problem of spontaneous symmetry breaking, which standard mean-field (TDHF) and the variational Balian-Veneroni approach (1981, 1992) cannot describe when starting from a symmetry-respecting initial state. The LMG model, exactly solvable both statically (Ring and Schuck, 1980) and dynamically (Kan, 1980; Krieger and Goeke, 1977), serves as a test case because it exhibits spontaneous symmetry breaking in mean-field theory for interaction strength chi > 1. The SMF approach provides a simpler alternative to the time-dependent generator coordinate method (Goeke and Reinhard, 1980, 1981) and enables treatment of fluctuation dynamics and symmetry breaking in complex quantum systems from a quasi-classical perspective, with potential applications to heavy-ion reactions as previously explored by Ayik (2009, 2010), Washiyama (2009), and Yilmaz (2011).
