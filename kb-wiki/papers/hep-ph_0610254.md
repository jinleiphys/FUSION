# Q\bar{Q} modes in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the evolution of heavy quarkonium states with temperature in a Quark-Gluon Plasma by evaluating an in-medium Q\bar{Q} T-matrix within a reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation in S- and P-wave channels. The interaction kernel is extracted from finite-temperature QCD lattice calculations of the singlet free energy of a Q\bar{Q} pair. Quarkonium bound states are found to gradually move across the Q\bar{Q} threshold after which they rapidly dissolve in the hot system. We calculate Euclidean-time correlation functions and compare to results from lattice QCD. We also study finite-width effects in the heavy-quark propagators.

## Key claim

Quarkonium bound states in the Quark-Gluon Plasma do not abruptly disappear at a single temperature but instead gradually move across the \(Q\bar{Q}\) threshold, after which they rapidly dissolve; the survival temperatures depend on the specific state, with the \(J/\psi(1S)\) surviving up to approximately \(3\,T_c\) and the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) beyond \(3.5\,T_c\), under the assumption that the heavy-quark potential is identified with the color-singlet internal energy from lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors solved a reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation (a three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation) for the in-medium \(Q\bar{Q}\) T-matrix in partial-wave basis, using the Haftel-Tabakin algorithm for matrix inversion. The interaction kernel was extracted from finite-temperature lattice QCD calculations of the color-singlet free energy, converted to the internal energy via the thermodynamic relation \(U_1 = F_1 - T\,dF_1/dT\). Heavy-quark masses were fixed to reproduce vacuum ground-state masses at the lowest temperature (\(m_c = 1.7\) GeV, \(m_b = 5.1\) GeV), and a small imaginary part of \(-0.01\) GeV was included in the quark selfenergy for numerical stability.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium ground state (\(J/\psi\), \(\eta_c\)) mass at \(T=1.1\,T_c\): approximately 3.10 GeV.
- Bottomonium ground state (\(\Upsilon(1S)\), \(\eta_b\)) mass at \(T=1.1\,T_c\): approximately 9.45 GeV; first excited state (\(\Upsilon(2S)\), \(\eta_b'\)): approximately 9.95 GeV.
- \(J/\psi(1S)\) bound state survives up to approximately \(3\,T_c\), where it crosses the \(c\bar{c}\) threshold.
- \(\Upsilon(2S)\) moves across the \(b\bar{b}\) threshold at approximately \(2.1\,T_c\); \(\Upsilon(1S)\) survives beyond approximately \(3.5\,T_c\).
- \(P\)-wave charmonium (\(\chi_c\)) bound state at \(T=1.1\,T_c\): mass 3.4 GeV, binding energy approximately 0 GeV.
- \(P\)-wave bottomonium \(\chi_b(1P)\) at \(T=1.1\,T_c\): mass 9.90 GeV, binding energy 0.30 GeV; \(\chi_b(2P)\): mass 10.15 GeV, binding energy 0.05 GeV.
- \(\chi_b(2P)\) moves beyond threshold at approximately \(1.3\,T_c\); \(\chi_b(1P)\) at approximately \(2.3\,T_c\).
- Finite-width effects from a \(c\)-quark width of 0.05 GeV induce \(\Gamma_{\Psi} \approx 0.1\) GeV, producing correlator variations of a few percent.

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix approach of Mannarelli (2005) by calculating Euclidean-time correlation functions for direct comparison with lattice QCD results, specifically those of Datta (2003) for charmonium. The approach treats bound and scattering states on an equal footing using the same interaction, unlike earlier potential-model calculations based on the Schrodinger equation (Shuryak 2003, Wong 2004, Alberico 2005, Mocsy 2005). The results enable more reliable comparisons between model calculations and lattice QCD by providing spectral functions and correlators that account for both bound-state and continuum contributions, and they open the door to studying temperature-dependent heavy-quark masses and widths in future work.
