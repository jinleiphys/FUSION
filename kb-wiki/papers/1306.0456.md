# Gauge symmetry and background independence: Should the proton spin decomposition be path independent?

> Exploring the similarities between the Chen \emph{et al.} approach, where physical and gauge degrees of freedom of the gauge potential are explicitly separated, and the background field method, we provide an alternative point of view to the proton spin decomposition issue. We show in particular that the gauge symmetry can be realized in two different ways, and discuss the relations between the concepts of path dependence, Stueckelberg dependence and background dependence. Finally, we argue that path/Stueckelberg/background-dependent decompositions of the proton spin are in principle measurable and therefore physically meaningful.

## Key claim
The paper argues that path/Stueckelberg/background-dependent decompositions of the proton spin are in principle measurable and therefore physically meaningful, contrary to claims that only local gauge-invariant quantities are observable. The author shows that gauge symmetry can be realized in two different ways (weak and strong forms) within the Chen et al. approach, and that the strong form is not necessary for ensuring measurability.

## Method
The author explores the formal similarities between the Chen et al. approach, which separates gauge potentials into pure-gauge and physical parts, and the background field method of DeWitt and 't Hooft. By comparing the transformation properties of the pure-gauge field under gauge transformations with those of background fields, the author identifies two distinct types of gauge transformations (active/quantum and passive/background) and shows that Stueckelberg transformations correspond to the difference between them. The analysis uses the covariant formulation of the Chen et al. approach and the standard background field method formalism.

## Key numbers
- The Ji decomposition (1996) provides a gauge-invariant split into quark spin, quark orbital angular momentum, and total gluon angular momentum, but does not separate gluon spin from orbital contributions.
- The Jaffe-Manohar decomposition (1989) provides a complete split into quark/gluon and spin/orbital contributions but is not gauge invariant.
- The Chen et al. decomposition (2008) uses the split A_mu(x) = A^pure_mu(x) + A^phys_mu(x) with the condition F^pure_munu = 0, defining a gauge-invariant gluon spin contribution.
- The first x-moment of the quark helicity distribution Delta q(x) coincides with the quark axial charge Delta q, which is a local observable; the first x-moment of the gluon helicity distribution Delta g(x) remains a quasi-observable with no local gauge-invariant expression.

## Context
This work extends the discussion following Chen et al. (2008, 2009) on the proton spin decomposition, which reopened controversies about gauge-invariant decompositions. It addresses criticisms by Ji (2009, 2010) and Ji, Xu, and Zhao (2012) that gauge-invariant extensions of the Jaffe-Manohar decomposition are "not naturally gauge invariant" and "in general unmeasurable." The paper also engages with Wakamatsu (2013) on path dependence and measurability. The analysis enables a clearer classification of proton spin decompositions into observables (path-independent, like the Ji decomposition) and quasi-observables (path-dependent but measurable within expansion frameworks, like the Hatta decomposition based on light-front constraints).
