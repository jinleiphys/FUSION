# Non-instant collisions and two concepts of quasiparticle

> The kinetic theory recently implemented in heavy ion reactions combines a non-local and non-instant picture of binary collisions with quasiparticle features. We show that the non-instant description is compatible with the spectral concept of quasiparticles while the commonly used variational concept is consistent only with instant collisions. The rearrangement energy, by which the variational concept surpasses the spectral one, is shown to be covered by a medium effect on non-instant collisions.

# Non-instant collisions and two concepts of quasiparticles

## Key claim

The spectral concept of quasiparticles is compatible with non-instant binary collisions, while the variational (Landau) concept is consistent only with instant collisions; the rearrangement energy that distinguishes the two concepts can be understood as a medium effect arising from the finite duration of collisions, meaning the variational approach can mimic the spectral one only when collisions are treated as instant and elastic.

## Method

The authors compare two quasiparticle formulations, the variational (Landau) approach, which defines quasiparticle energy as a functional derivative of total energy with respect to distribution, and the spectral (Green's function) approach, which defines quasiparticle energy from the pole of the single-particle spectral function. They analyze a kinetic equation derived from non-equilibrium Green's functions in the Galitskii–Feynman approximation, which includes non-local and non-instant collision terms characterized by a collision delay Δ_t and an energy gain 2Δ_E, and examine the conservation laws for particle number and energy that follow from this equation.

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory developed by Danielewicz and Pratt (1996) for heavy-ion reactions, which introduced non-local and non-instant binary collisions, and connects it to the quasiparticle concepts of Landau (1956) and the Green's function foundations of Nozières and Luttinger (1962). The paper resolves an apparent inconsistency between the variational and spectral quasiparticle formulations by showing that the rearrangement energy, previously noted by Glyde and Hernadi (1983) in the Galitskii–Feynman approximation, corresponds to the mean energy gain from non-instant collisions. This enables the use of simpler instant collision integrals with variational quasiparticle energies in hydrodynamic regimes where global conservation laws dominate over individual particle trajectories.
