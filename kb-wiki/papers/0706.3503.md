# Largely deformed states of $^{13}$B

> The excited states of $^{13}$B were studied with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The theoretical results suggest that the intruder states with large deformations construct the rotational bands, $K^π=3/2^-$ and $K^π=1/2^+$, starting from 5 MeV and 8 MeV, respectively. The neutron structure of the $K^π=3/2^-$ is analogous to the intruder ground state of $^{12}$Be. In the predicted $K^π=1/2^+$ band, we found very exotic structure with a proton intruder configuration. This proton intruder state has a larger deformation than superdeformation. The band-head $1/2^+$ state is assigned to the $1/2^+$(4.83 MeV), which was experimentally suggested to be the proton intruder state because of the strong production via the proton-transfer to the $^{12}$Be($0^+$) state in the $^4$He($^{12}$Be,$^{13}$B$γ$)$X$ experiments.

## Key claim
The paper claims that excited states of $^{13}$B contain intruder configurations with large deformations forming rotational bands, specifically a $K^\pi=3/2^-$ band starting at 5 MeV and a $K^\pi=1/2^+$ band starting at 8 MeV. The key hedge is that the theoretical excitation energy of the $1/2^+_1$ state (8 MeV) is slightly higher than the experimentally observed value (4.83 MeV), though the authors assign them as the same state based on the proton intruder configuration.

## Method
The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to calculate the ground and excited states of $^{13}$B. They used 23 basis AMD wave functions obtained by VAP for states with $J \le 5/2$ and higher, then superposed these wave functions to determine final wave functions for each $J^\pi_n$ state. The effective nuclear interaction consisted of the MV1 central force with Majorana parameter $m=0.65$, spin-orbit force strengths $u_I=-u_{II}=3700$ MeV, and the Coulomb force.

## Key numbers
- Calculated binding energy of $^{13}$B: 76.4 MeV (experimental value: 84.5 MeV)
- Calculated binding energy of $^{12}$Be: 61.9 MeV (experimental value: 68.6 MeV)
- Deformation parameter $\beta = 0.74$ for the $1/2^+_1$ band-head state of the $K^\pi=1/2^+$ band, exceeding the value $\beta=0.6$ for superdeformation
- The $K^\pi=3/2^-$ band starts from excitation energy $E_x = 5$ MeV
- The $K^\pi=1/2^+$ band starts from excitation energy $E_x = 8$ MeV
- The experimental $1/2^+$ state at 4.83 MeV is assigned to the calculated proton intruder state
- Two-center distance $d \sim 5$ fm obtained for the $1/2^+_1$ state
- Width parameter $\nu = 0.18$ fm$^{-2}$ used in the AMD wave functions

## Context
This work extends the molecular orbital and cluster structure studies previously applied to neutron-rich Be isotopes (Kanada-En'yo 2003, 2005) to the $Z=5$ system $^{13}$B, which has a neutron magic number $N=8$ in its ground state. The paper shows that despite the restored $N=8$ magic number in $^{13}$B (unlike $^{12}$Be where the intruder configuration becomes the ground state), intruder states with large deformations appear at low excitation energies. The neutron structure of the $K^\pi=3/2^-$ band is analogous to the intruder ground state of $^{12}$Be, interpreted as $^{12}$Be($0^+_1$)+$p$. The predicted proton intruder $1/2^+$ state is assigned to the state observed by Ota (2007) in $^4$He($^{12}$Be,$^{13}$B$\gamma$)$X$ experiments. This work enables further investigation of exotic cluster structures in more neutron-rich B isotopes like $^{15}$B and $^{17}$B.
