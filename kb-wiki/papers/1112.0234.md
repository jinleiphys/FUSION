# Hyperons and massive neutron stars: vector repulsion and SU(3) symmetry

> With the discovery of massive neutron stars such as PSR J1614-2230, the question has arisen whether exotic matter such as hyperons can exist in the neutron star core. We examine the conditions under which hyperons can exist in massive neutron stars. We consistently investigate the vector meson-hyperon coupling, going from SU(6) quark model to a broader SU(3) symmetry. We propose that the maximum neutron star mass decreases linearly with the strangeness content f_s of the neutron star core as M_max(f_s) = M_max(0) - 0.6 M_solar (f_s/0.1), which seems to be independent of the underlying nuclear equation of state and the vector baryon-meson coupling scheme. Thus, pulsar mass measurements can be used to constrain the hyperon fraction in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the maximum neutron star mass decreases linearly with the strangeness content of the core as M_max(f_s) = M_max(0) - 0.6 M_solar (f_s/0.1), and claims this relation is independent of the underlying nuclear equation of state and the vector baryon-meson coupling scheme. The hedge is that this linear relation holds only for effective nucleon masses m*_N/m_N less than or approximately 0.7, and that the relation is derived from a specific relativistic mean field model with the phi meson included.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model including the full baryon octet (nucleons and hyperons) with meson exchange (sigma, omega, rho, phi). They systematically varied the vector meson-hyperon couplings by moving from the SU(6) quark model to the more general SU(3) flavor symmetry, treating the g_8/g_1 ratio z and the F/(F+D) ratio alpha_V as free parameters while assuming ideal mixing for the omega and phi mesons. The nucleon-meson couplings were fixed to nuclear saturation properties, and the hyperon scalar couplings were fitted to hypernuclear potential depths (U_Lambda = -30 MeV, U_Sigma = +30 MeV, U_Xi = -28 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass ranges from 1.49 M_solar (at z = 0.8) to 2.36 M_solar (at z = 0) for the GM1 parameter set
- The change in maximum mass over the full z range is Delta_M = 0.87 M_solar
- For the SU(6) case (z = 1/sqrt(6) approx 0.4082) with TM1 parameter set, the maximum mass for pure nucleonic stars is 2.18 M_solar
- At alpha_V = 0.0-0.2 with GM1, the maximum mass reaches 2.36 M_solar with radius 11.8 km, matching the purely nucleonic limit
- The linear fit coefficient c is approximately 0.6 M_solar per 0.1 strangeness fraction
- The maximum allowed strangeness fraction in a 2 M_solar star is f_s_max = 0.17, derived from a 3 M_solar upper limit from heavy-ion collisions
- Hyperon threshold densities for GM1 at z=0: Lambda appears at n_b approx 0.73 fm^-3, Xi^- at n_b approx 0.74 fm^-3, Xi^0 at n_b approx 1.38 fm^-3
- For the SU(6) case with GM1: Lambda appears at n_b approx 0.36 fm^-3, Xi^- at n_b approx 0.4 fm^-3, Xi^0 at n_b approx 0.89 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Weissenborn et al. 2012) on how nuclear saturation properties affect hyperon presence in neutron stars, and directly addresses the tension between hyperon softening of the equation of state and the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 +/- 0.04 M_solar, Demorest 2010). The paper systematically explores the parameter space beyond the SU(6) quark model, which had been the standard approach for setting hyperon couplings in RMF models (Glendenning 1985, GM1 parameter set). The finding that the nuclear matter limit can be continuously approached by varying SU(3) parameters, rather than making a discrete on/off decision about hyperons, enables future work to use pulsar mass measurements to constrain the hyperon fraction in neutron star cores. The authors also connect their results to constraints from sub-threshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions (Tolos and Sagert).
