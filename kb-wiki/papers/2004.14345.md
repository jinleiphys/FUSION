# Complete NLO QCD study of single- and double-quarkonium hadroproduction in the colour-evaporation model at the Tevatron and the LHC

> We study the Single-Parton-Scattering (SPS) production of double quarkonia (J/psi+J/psi, J/psi+Upsilon, and Upsilon+Upsilon) in pp and pp(bar) collisions at the LHC and the Tevatron as measured by the CMS, ATLAS, LHCb, and D0 experiments in the Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM), based on the quark-hadron-duality, including Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) QCD corrections up to alpha_s^5. To do so, we also perform the first true NLO --up to alpha_s^4-- study of the p_T-differential cross section for single-quarkonium production. This allows us to fix the non-perturbative CEM parameters at NLO accuracy in the region where quarkonium-pair data are measured. Our results show that the CEM at NLO in general significantly undershoots these experimental data and, in view of the other existing SPS studies, confirm the need for Double Parton Scattering (DPS) to account for the data. Our NLO study of single-quarkonium production at mid and large p_T also confirms the difficulty of the approach to account for the measured p_T spectra; this is reminiscent of the impossibility to fit single-quarkonium data with the sole 3S18 NRQCD contribution from gluon fragmentation. We stress that the discrepancy occurs in a kinematical region where the new features of the improved CEM are not relevant.

## Key claim

The Colour-Evaporation Model (CEM) at Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) significantly undershoots all measured double-quarkonium production data at the LHC and Tevatron, confirming that Double Parton Scattering (DPS) is required to account for the observed yields, even though the CEM was expected to provide an upper limit on Single-Parton-Scattering (SPS) contributions.

## Method

The authors performed the first complete NLO QCD calculation of single- and double-quarkonium hadroproduction in the Colour-Evaporation Model using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, computing cross sections up to alpha_s^4 for single-quarkonium production and up to alpha_s^5 for double-quarkonium production. They fitted the non-perturbative CEM parameters (P_Q) for J/psi, psi(2S), and Upsilon(nS) using experimental p_T-differential cross section data from ATLAS and CMS, then applied these parameters to predict di-J/psi, J/psi+Upsilon, and di-Upsilon production at the LHC and Tevatron.

## Key numbers

- For J/psi pairs at CMS (7 TeV, |y_psi|<1.2, p_T>6.5 GeV): CEM results are at least an order of magnitude below experimental data at both LO and NLO
- The NLO CEM parameter P_J/psi fitted from ATLAS data (8 TeV, |y_psi|<0.5, p_T in [8.5:20] GeV) is 0.009^{+0.004}_{-0.002}
- The NLO CEM parameter P_Upsilon(1S) fitted from CMS data (7 TeV, |y_Upsilon|<1.2, p_T in [10:20] GeV) is 0.02^{+0.01}_{-0.005}
- For J/psi pairs at LHCb (13 TeV, p_T<10 GeV, 2.0<y<4.5): CEM normalisation is off by more than an order of magnitude
- The NLO scale uncertainty in di-J/psi calculations is half that of LO calculations, indicating absence of kinematically-enhanced topologies

## Context

This work extends previous CEM studies by Bedjidian (2004) and Nelson (2012) which were limited to p_T-integrated yields at alpha_s^3, and provides the first NLO p_T-differential CEM study for psi(2S) and Upsilon(nS). The results contradict the expectation that CEM would provide an upper limit on SPS contributions (as found earlier for J/psi+Z and J/psi+W production by Lansberg (2016, 2017)), and instead confirm the need for DPS to explain quarkonium-pair data. The study enables future NLO NRQCD calculations to resolve debates about colour-octet contributions at large rapidity separations, where current LO NRQCD predictions have low predictive power due to large uncertainties in Long Distance Matrix Elements.
