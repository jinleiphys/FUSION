# Chiral condensate and dressed Polyakov loop in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We investigate the chiral condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop or dual chiral condensate at finite temperature and density in two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The dressed Polyakov loop is regarded as an equivalent order parameter of deconfinement phase transition in a confining theory. We find the behavior of dressed Polyakov loop in absence of any confinement mechanism is quite interesting, with only quark degrees of freedom present, it still shows an order parameter like behavior. It is found that in the chiral limit, the critical temperature for chiral phase transition coincides with that of the dressed Polyakov loop in the whole $(T,μ)$ plane. In the case of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, it is found that the transition temperature for chiral restoration $T_c^χ$ is smaller than that of the dressed Polyakov loop $T_c^{\cal D}$ in the low baryon density region where the transition is a crossover. With the increase of current quark mass the difference between the two transition temperatures is found to be increasing. However, the two critical temperatures coincide in the high baryon density region where the phase transition is of first order. We give an explanation on the feature of $T_c^χ=T_c^{\cal D}$ in the case of 1st and 2nd order phase transitions, and $T_c^χ<T_c^{\cal D}$ in the case of crossover, and expect this feature is general and can be extended to full QCD theory. Our result might indicate that in the case of crossover, there exists a small region where chiral symmetry is restored but the color degrees of freedom are still confined.

## Key claim

In the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which lacks any confinement mechanism, the dressed Polyakov loop still exhibits order-parameter-like behavior; the critical temperatures for chiral restoration and the dressed Polyakov loop coincide for first- and second-order phase transitions, but in the crossover region the chiral transition temperature \(T_c^{\chi}\) is smaller than the dressed-Polyakov-loop transition temperature \(T_c^{\cal D}\), suggesting a possible small region where chiral symmetry is restored while color degrees of freedom remain confined.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor NJL model at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a \(U(1)\)-valued temporal boundary condition parameterized by an angle \(\phi\) to define a general chiral condensate \(\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle_\phi\). The dressed Polyakov loop \(\Sigma_1\) is obtained as the Fourier transform (winding number \(n=1\)) of this \(\phi\)-dependent condensate. The conventional chiral condensate is taken at \(\phi=\pi\), and transition temperatures are extracted from slope analysis of both order parameters across the \((T,\mu)\) plane, for the chiral limit (\(m=0\)) and for explicit chiral symmetry breaking (\(m=5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).

## Key numbers

- For \(m=5.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at zero chemical potential, the difference between the chiral transition temperature \(T_c^{\chi}\) and the dressed-Polyakov-loop transition temperature \(T_c^{\cal D}\) is about \(7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with \(T_c^{\chi} < T_c^{\cal D}\).
- For a current quark mass of \(m=200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at zero chemical potential, the difference between \(T_c^{\chi}\) and \(T_c^{\cal D}\) increases to about \(26\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The NJL model parameters used are a three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.6315\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and a scalar coupling constant \(G_S = 5.498\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\).
- In the chiral limit (\(m=0\)), the critical temperatures for the chiral condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop coincide across the entire \((T,\mu)\) plane.

## Context

This work extends studies of the dressed Polyakov loop (dual chiral condensate) from lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger approaches (e.g., Fischer (2009)) to the NJL model, which lacks explicit confinement. It contrasts with the Polyakov-loop-extended NJL (PNJL) model (Fukushima (2003), Ratti (2005)), where the Polyakov-loop potential is inserted by hand, by instead using the dressed Polyakov loop as an equivalent deconfinement order parameter. The qualitative result that \(T_c^{\chi} < T_c^{\cal D}\) in the crossover region agrees with lattice findings from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (Fodor (2009)), and the analysis suggests that this feature may be general and testable in full QCD and other effective models such as Dyson-Schwinger equations.
