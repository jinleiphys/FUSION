# BEC-BCS Crossover in the Epsilon Expansion

> The epsilon expansion (expansion around four spacial dimensions) developed by Nishida and Son for a cold fermi gas with infinite scattering length is extended to finite scattering length to study the BEC-BCS crossover. A resummation of higher order logarithms and a suitable extension of fermion coupling in d-dimensions are developed in order to apply the theory in the BCS regime. The ratio between the chemical potential and the Fermi energy, mu/eF, is computed to next-to-leading order in the epsilon expansion as a function of eta=1/(a kF), where a is the scattering length and kF is the Fermi momentum in a non-interacting system. Near the unitarity limit eta->0, we found mu/eF=0.475-0.707 eta-0.5 eta^2. Near the BEC limit eta->infinity, mu/eF=0.062/eta - eta^2, while near the BCS limit eta->-infinity, mu/eF=1+0.707/eta. Overall good agreement with Quantum Monte Carlo results is found.

## Key claim

The epsilon expansion (expansion around four spatial dimensions) is extended to finite scattering length to study the BEC-BCS crossover, with the key condition that a resummation of higher-order logarithms and a suitable extension of the fermion coupling in d-dimensions are required to apply the theory in the BCS regime; without this resummation, physical quantities would always be even in eta, preventing access to the BCS side.

## Method

The authors compute the effective potential for the condensate field phi_0 in a two-component Fermi gas with zero-range attractive interactions, using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism and a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They perform an epsilon expansion (epsilon = 4 - d) to next-to-leading order, treating the chemical potential mu_B = 2mu + B as O(epsilon) on the BEC side and developing separate power-counting schemes for the BEC, unitarity, and BCS regimes. The ratio mu/epsilon_F is obtained by minimizing the effective potential, computing the fermion number density, and then extrapolating to three dimensions (epsilon = 1).

## Key numbers

* Near the unitarity limit (|eta| -> 0): mu/epsilon_F = 0.475 - 0.707 eta - 0.5 eta^2, where eta = 1/(a k_F).
* Near the BEC limit (eta -> infinity): mu/epsilon_F = 0.062/eta - eta^2.
* Near the BCS limit (eta -> -infinity): mu/epsilon_F = 1 + 0.707/eta.
* The energy per particle relative to a non-interacting Fermi gas near unitarity: xi = 0.475 - 0.884 eta - 0.833 eta^2.
* In the BEC limit, the xi result is xi = 0.052 eta^{-1} - (5/3) eta^2, which agrees with the QMC1 result of xi = 0.055 eta^{-1} - (5/3) eta^2.

## Context

This work extends the epsilon expansion method developed by Nishida and Son (2006) for a cold Fermi gas at infinite scattering length to finite scattering length, enabling a unified analytic treatment of the entire BEC-BCS crossover. The results are compared with Quantum Monte Carlo calculations (QMC1 and QMC2) and show good agreement; the BCS limit result also agrees reasonably well with the low-energy theorem of Kohn and collaborators (2005) and others. This approach enables future analytic calculations of other observables across the crossover, such as the critical temperature or properties of polarized systems.
