# Effective masses of diquarks

> We study meson and diquark bound states using the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. The infrared strength of the rainbow-ladder kernel is described by two parameters. The ultraviolet behavior is fixed by the one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD, which ensures the correct asymptotic behavior of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and brings important qualitative benefits. The diquark with the lowest mass is the scalar, followed by the axialvector and pseudoscalar diquark. This ordering can be anticipated from the meson sector.

## Key claim

Using the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, the scalar diquark is the lightest color-antitriplet diquark bound state, but these diquark bound states are expected to disappear from the spectrum when higher-order corrections beyond ladder truncation are included; the calculated masses should therefore only be considered as an indication for the relevant mass scales of diquark correlations.

## Method

The authors solved the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson and color-antitriplet diquark bound states using the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations. The effective quark-antiquark interaction was modeled with an infrared Gaussian term and an ultraviolet term fixed by the one-loop renormalization group behavior of QCD, with parameters previously fitted to reproduce the pion mass, kaon mass, pion decay constant, and chiral condensate. Bound state masses were determined by solving an eigenvalue problem for the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with diquark bound states corresponding to eigenvalue λ=2 (compared to λ=1 for mesons) due to the different color factor.

## Key numbers

- Scalar diquark mass (up/down quarks): 0.82 GeV
- Axialvector diquark mass (up/down quarks): 1.02 GeV
- Pseudoscalar diquark mass (up/down quarks): 1.03 GeV
- Pion mass (up/down quarks): 0.138 GeV
- Rho meson mass (up/down quarks): 0.741 GeV
- Scalar meson mass (up/down quarks): 0.671 GeV
- Scalar diquark mass (strange quarks): 1.27 GeV
- Axialvector diquark mass (strange quarks): 1.44(4) GeV
- Pseudoscalar diquark mass (strange quarks): 1.50(4) GeV
- Axialvector and pseudoscalar diquarks are about 150 to 250 MeV heavier than the scalar diquark, independent of model parameters

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously applied to pseudoscalar and vector mesons by Maris and Tandy (1999) to diquark bound states. The results are compared with a separable model by Burden (1996) and with quenched lattice QCD simulations by Hess (1998). The calculated diquark masses are intended to guide improvements to quark-diquark models of baryons, such as those by Cahill (1987), Bentz, the Tuebingen group, and the Argonne group, and the authors suggest that pseudoscalar diquark correlations (with mass scales similar to axialvector diquarks) may be worth exploring in such baryon models.
