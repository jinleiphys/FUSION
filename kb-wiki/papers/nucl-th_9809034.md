# Deuteron Compton scattering below pion photoproduction threshold

> Deuteron Compton scattering below pion photoproduction threshold is considered in the framework of the non-relativistic diagrammatic approach with the Bonn OBE potential. The complete gauge-invariant set of diagrams is taken into account which includes resonance diagrams without and with rescattering and diagrams with one- and two-body seagulls. The obtained results are compared with predictions of other models and with experimental data. A possibility of determining isospin-averaged electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon is discussed.

# Deuteron Compton Scattering Below Pion Photoproduction Threshold

## Key claim

The non-relativistic diagrammatic approach with the Bonn OBE potential can describe deuteron Compton scattering data below pion photoproduction threshold, but only if nucleon polarizabilities are included; with polarizabilities set to zero, all model predictions overshoot the experimental data. The isospin-averaged nucleon polarizabilities can in principle be extracted from elastic gamma-d scattering, but only after the large disagreements between different theoretical computations are resolved.

## Method

The total gamma-d scattering amplitude was constructed from a complete gauge-invariant set of diagrams using the non-relativistic Bonn OBE potential. The resonance part was built from two deuteron photodisintegration amplitudes (gamma d -> pn and pn -> gamma' d') with relativistic spin-orbit corrections, combined with the full T-matrix for rescattering of intermediate off-shell nucleons. The seagull operator included one-body terms (Thomson term, nucleon polarizability term, relativistic spin-orbit contribution) and two-body pieces from pi, rho, omega, sigma, and delta-meson exchanges consistent with the NN potential, along with meson-nucleon form factors, retardation effects, and Delta-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers

- Rescattering of intermediate nucleons decreases the differential cross section dσ/dΩ by 7% to 12% at forward angles for energies 50 to 100 MeV.
- Rescattering increases dσ/dΩ by 7% to 3% at backward angles for the same energy range (50 to 100 MeV).
- The diagrammatic calculation agreed with the Gell-Mann-Goldberger-Thirring dispersion relation for the forward spin-averaged amplitude to better than 3% for all energies below 100 MeV.
- Varying α-β from 9 to 15 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) decreases the backward differential cross section by 8% at 50 MeV and by 20% at 100 MeV.
- The fixed sum α+β = 15 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³) was used, as estimated from dispersion relations by LPS79.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Levchuk and L'vov (1995) by including a more sophisticated treatment of mesonic contributions to electromagnetic currents and seagulls, the Delta-isobar, retardation effects, and spin-orbit corrections. It confirms findings of Weyrauch (1990), Wilhelm, Wilbois, and Arenhövel (1995), and Levchuk and L'vov (1995) regarding the magnitude of rescattering effects, but finds significant disagreements with Wilhelm, Wilbois, and Arenhövel (1995) at higher energies and with Weyrauch (1990) and Weller and Arenhövel (1983) at backward angles. The results enable future extraction of neutron polarizabilities from elastic gamma-d scattering, with new experiments at Lund and Saskatoon expected to provide additional data.
