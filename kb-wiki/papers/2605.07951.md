# Quasiparticle properties of a single $Λ$ impurity in symmetric nuclear matter with a regulated $NΛ$ interaction

> We explore the quasiparticle properties of a single $Λ$ hyperon propagating through symmetric nuclear matter using the Green's function formalism. The $NΛ$ interaction is described by a non-local regulated low-momentum contact potential with a leading-order constant term and a next-to-leading-order derivative correction. The two coupling constants in the ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$ channels are fixed by matching the vacuum on-shell $T$ matrix to the scattering length and effective range obtained from modern next-to-next-to-leading-order chiral effective field theory. Using this effective interaction, we calculate the retarded $Λ$ self-energy from the in-medium $NΛ$ ladder $T$ matrix, which sums repeated $NΛ$ scattering in the nucleonic medium. At saturation density, the zero-momentum quasiparticle pole is found at $E_{\rm qp}(0,ρ_{\rm sat})=-29.55~{\rm MeV}$, in good agreement with the empirical depth of the single $Λ$ potential in nuclear matter. The self-energy decomposition gives a static Born contribution $Σ_Λ^{\rm Born}(0)=-26.36~{\rm MeV}$ and a dynamical correlation contribution ${\rm Re}\,Σ_Λ^{\rm corr,R}(0,E_{\rm qp})=-3.19~{\rm MeV}$, showing that repeated in-medium $NΛ$ scattering is needed to reproduce the empirical binding scale. The quasiparticle remains narrow and well defined, with a large residue $Z(0)=0.98$, a small damping width $Γ(0)=0.023~{\rm MeV}$, and a sharp spectral peak near the quasiparticle energy. At finite momentum, the $Λ$ quasiparticle becomes less bound, with $E_{\rm qp}(k,ρ_{\rm sat})$ increasing from $-29.55~{\rm MeV}$ at $k=0$ to $-6.49~{\rm MeV}$ at $k=1~{\rm fm}^{-1}$, while the residue and width change only weakly. A low-momentum fit gives $m_Λ^*/m_Λ=0.747$, consistent with the range obtained in Brueckner calculations with Nijmegen hyperon--nucleon potentials.

## Key claim

Using a regulated low-momentum contact interaction with coupling constants fixed by scattering length and effective range from chiral effective field theory, the in-medium ladder self-energy yields a zero-momentum quasiparticle energy of -29.55 MeV at saturation density, but the abstract's claim of "good agreement with the empirical depth" depends on the fact that the static Born contribution alone (-26.36 MeV) is insufficient and the dynamical correlation contribution (-3.19 MeV) from repeated in-medium NΛ scattering is required to reach the empirical binding scale.

## Method

The authors employed the Green's function formalism for a single Λ impurity in symmetric nuclear matter, using a non-local regulated low-momentum contact potential with a leading-order constant term and a next-to-leading-order derivative correction in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels. The coupling constants were fixed by matching the vacuum on-shell T matrix to the scattering length and effective range from next-to-next-to-leading-order chiral effective field theory, then spin-averaged for spin-saturated matter. The retarded Λ self-energy was calculated from the in-medium NΛ ladder T matrix, which sums repeated NΛ scattering in the nucleonic medium, and the quasiparticle properties were extracted from the Dyson equation.

## Key numbers

- Zero-momentum quasiparticle energy at saturation density: E_qp(0, ρ_sat) = -29.55 MeV
- Static Born contribution to the self-energy: Σ_Λ^Born(0) = -26.36 MeV
- Dynamical correlation contribution at the quasiparticle pole: Re Σ_Λ^corr,R(0, E_qp) = -3.19 MeV
- Quasiparticle residue at zero momentum: Z(0) = 0.98
- Damping width at zero momentum: Γ(0) = 0.023 MeV
- Imaginary part of self-energy at the quasiparticle pole: Im Σ_Λ^R(0, E_qp) = -0.011 MeV
- Quasiparticle energy at k = 1 fm⁻¹: E_qp(1 fm⁻¹, ρ_sat) = -6.49 MeV
- Effective mass ratio: m_Λ*/m_Λ = 0.747, extracted from low-momentum fit with c = 23.341 MeV·fm²
- Spin-averaged coupling constants: g_0,NΛ^av = -1.03 fm², g_2,NΛ^av = 0.34 fm⁴
- Scattering lengths: a_NΛ(¹S₀) = -2.80 fm, a_NΛ(³S₁) = -1.59 fm; effective ranges: r_NΛ(¹S₀) = 2.87 fm, r_NΛ(³S₁) = 3.10 fm

## Context

This work extends previous Green's-function studies of Λ hyperons in nuclear matter by Robertson (2004) and Vidana (2017), who found that the Λ remains less correlated than a nucleon with large quasiparticle strength, and connects to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with Nijmegen hyperon-nucleon potentials by Vidana (2001) that gave m_Λ*/m_Λ between 0.67 and 0.81. The results enable a microscopic connection between modern chiral NΛ interactions from Haidenbauer (2023) and the single-particle spectra of Λ hypernuclei, and provide a benchmark for the in-medium NΛ interaction needed to address the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star matter, where the density dependence of the Λ single-particle potential is critical.
