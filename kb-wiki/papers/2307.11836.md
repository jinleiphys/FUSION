# Hard probes in isobar collisions as a probe of the neutron skin

> We present an estimate of the yield of hard probes expected for collisions of the isobars $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr at collision energies reachable at RHIC and the LHC\@. These yields are proportional to the number of binary nucleon-nucleon interactions, which is characteristically different due to the presence of the large neutron skin in $^{96}_{40}$Zr. This provides an independent opportunity to measure the difference between the neutron skin of $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr, which can provide an important constraint on the Equation of State of cold neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim

The yield of hard probes in isobar collisions of ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr is sensitive to the difference in neutron skin between the two nuclei, with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (Ncoll) being the primary driver; however, the effect of jet quenching in the quark-gluon plasma partially cancels in the ratio, making the total yield ratio a more sensitive observable than the nuclear modification factor RAA alone.

## Method

The authors used the Trajectum 1.2 framework with maximum likelihood settings, which includes a generalized T​R​ENTo initial state with nucleon positions sampled from Woods-Saxon distributions that separately parameterize protons and neutrons with different radii, skin depths, and quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformations. Five different nuclear shape parameterizations were considered, ranging from simple cases without neutron skin to a realistic case (Case 5) with distinct neutron skin parameters for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr and non-equal deformations. Hard probe production was estimated from the number of binary collisions, and energy loss in the QGP was modeled using a temperature-path integral formula δe = κ(pT) ∫ T³ u · dL, with κ fixed by fitting the STAR 0–10% central RuRu RAA data.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon-nucleon cross section σNN is 39.7 mb at √sNN = 0.2 TeV and 68.8 mb at √sNN = 5.67 TeV.
- For Case 5, the inelastic nucleus-nucleus cross section σAA is 4.699 b for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and 4.871 b for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr.
- The Woods-Saxon neutron skin depth σn for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr is 0.564 fm (Case 5), compared to 0.49 fm for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru.
- The hydrodynamic starting time is τ = 1.17 fm/c and the freeze-out temperature is Tswitch = 153.5 MeV.
- The estimated elliptic flow v₂ for high-pT particles reaches up to 1.1%.
- The systematic uncertainty from differing centrality definitions between Ru and Zr changes the Ncoll ratio by up to 0.5% below 40% centrality and by over 3% beyond that.

## Context

This work extends the program of using isobar collisions at RHIC and the LHC to study nuclear structure, building on the STAR isobar paper (STAR 2021) and prior Trajectum studies by Nijs et al. (2021). It complements soft-probe analyses of isobar collisions by focusing on hard probes, which are proportional to the number of binary collisions and thus directly sensitive to the neutron skin difference. The approach enables future measurements of electroweak bosons at the LHC and high-pT hadrons at RHIC to constrain the equation of state of cold neutron-rich matter, and connects to studies using J/ψ mesons by Zhao et al. (2022).
