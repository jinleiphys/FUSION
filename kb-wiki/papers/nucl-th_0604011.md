# Fission modes of 256Fm and 258Fm in a microscopic approach

> A static microscopic study of potential-energy surfaces within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS model is carried out for the 256Fm and 258Fm isotopes with the goal of deducing some properties of spontaneous fission. The calculated fission modes are found to be in agreement with the experimentaly observed asymmetric-to-symmetric transition in the fragment-mass distributions and with the high- and low-total-kinetic-energy modes experimentally observed in 258Fm. Most of the results are similar to those obtained in macroscopic-microscopic models as well as in recent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations with the Gogny interaction, with a few differences in their interpretations. In particular an alternative explanation is proposed for the low-energy fission mode of 258Fm.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS model reproduces the experimentally observed asymmetric-to-symmetric transition in fragment-mass distributions between 256Fm and 258Fm, and the bimodal fission of 258Fm, but the author proposes an alternative interpretation for the low-energy fission mode of 258Fm that differs from previous macroscopic-microscopic and Gogny-HFB calculations.

## Method

The author performed static constrained Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS calculations using the Skyrme SkM* interaction for the mean field and a seniority pairing force, with single-particle states expanded in a cylindrical harmonic-oscillator basis of 17 oscillator shells (N0=16) for most calculations, and up to 21 shells (N0=20) for the symmetric elongated fission valley of 258Fm. The potential-energy surfaces were explored by constraining the axial quadrupole moment Q20 (or the center-of-mass distance Dcm between prefragments), the axial octupole moment Q30 (or the heavy fragment mass AH), and the neck coordinate QN, assuming axial symmetry but releasing left-right reflection symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For 256Fm, the asymmetric fission valley yields nascent fragments of approximately 140Xe (Z=54) and 116Pd (Z=46) at the exit point, with the heavy fragment mass one unit away from the experimental value of AH≈141 from Flynn.
- For 258Fm, the symmetric compact fission (CF) valley corresponds to two nearly spherical 128Sn fragments (Q2f≈0 b) with a neck coordinate QN≈0, while the symmetric elongated fission (EF) valley has fragment quadrupole moments varying from 18 b to 45 b with QN≥6 b.
- The exit point of the symmetric EF valley in 258Fm is at Dcm≈22 fm with Q2f≈45 b.
- The asymmetric fission valley in 258Fm yields nascent fragments of approximately 141Xe+117Pd, similar to 256Fm.
- The two-body center-of-mass correction changes the deformation energy by about 1 MeV relative to the ground state, and the total center-of-mass correction is off by at least 5 MeV at and beyond scission.

## Context

This work extends earlier Skyrme-HFBCS calculations by Staszczak (2004) that imposed left-right symmetry, and provides an alternative interpretation to the macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Moller (1987-1989) and the Gogny-HFB calculations by Warda (2002-2004). The author argues that for 258Fm, the low-energy fission mode corresponds to the symmetric elongated fission valley rather than the asymmetric path proposed by previous authors, while the high-energy mode corresponds to the symmetric compact fission valley leading to two nearly spherical 128Sn fragments. The results enable further dynamical calculations of fragment kinetic-energy and mass distributions for these fermium isotopes.
