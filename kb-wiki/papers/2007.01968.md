# Generalized Rastall's gravity and its effects on compact objects

> We present a generalization of Rastall's gravity in which the conservation law of the energy-momentum tensor is altered, and as a result, the trace of the energy-momentum tensor is taken into account together with the Ricci scalar in the expression for the covariant derivative. Afterwards, we obtain the field equations in this theory and solve them by considering a spherically symmetric space-time. We show that the external solution has two possible classes of solutions with spherical symmetry in the vacuum in generalized Rastall's gravity, and we analyse one of them explicitly. The generalization, in contrast to constant value $k=8πG$ in general relativity, has a gravitational parameter $k$ that depends on the Rastall constant $α$. As an application, we perform a careful analysis of the effects of the theory on neutron stars using realistic equations of state (EoS) as input. Our results show that important differences on the profile of neutron stars are obtained within two representatives EoS.

# Generalized Rastall's gravity and its effects on compact objects

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalization of Rastall's gravity where the non-conservation of the energy-momentum tensor depends on both the Ricci scalar and the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (via a term proportional to RT), and they show that this generalization produces observable differences in neutron star mass-radius profiles compared to general relativity, provided the coupling parameters are restricted so that the Newtonian limit yields a constant gravitational coupling k approximately equal to 8πG.

## Method

The authors modify the conservation law of the energy-momentum tensor to T^μ_{ν;μ} = α R_{,ν} + β (RT)_{,ν}, derive the corresponding field equations, and obtain the Newtonian limit to constrain the coupling parameters α and β such that k ≈ 8πG. They then solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars using two realistic equations of state from relativistic mean field theory (IU-FSU and TM1 parametrizations) with the BPS crust EoS, integrating numerically from the center to the surface where pressure vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The maximum density of white dwarfs used to constrain β is ρ^{max}_{wd} ≈ 10^7 g/cm^3 = 2.8 × 10^{-8} fm^{-4} (in natural units ħ = c = 1).
- The condition for suppressing β contributions in the Newtonian limit is 128π G ρ^{max}_{wd} |β| ≪ 1, which translates to β ≪ 10^{44} fm^2 in SI units or β ≪ 10^5 fm^4 in natural units (c = ħ = G = 1).
- The condition for α is G|α| ≤ 1, which gives α ≤ 3.8 × 10^{-41} fm^{-2} in SI units or α ≤ 1 in natural units (c = ħ = G = 1).
- The NICER mission constraint for the canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star PSR J0030+0451 gives 11.80 km ≤ R_{1.4} ≤ 13.10 km.
- The NICER constraint for the massive neutron star PSR J0740+6620 (mass range 2.01 M⊙ ≤ M ≤ 2.15 M⊙) gives 11.60 km ≤ R ≤ 13.10 km.
- The PREX-2 constraint on the canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star radius gives 13.25 km ≲ R_{1.4} ≲ 14.26 km.
- The TOV solution for the IU-FSU EoS lies in the lower band of the PSR J0030 constraint and fails to reproduce the PSR J0740 and PREX-2 constraints.

## Context

This work extends the original Rastall gravity (Rastall, 1972) by introducing an additional coupling between the Ricci scalar R and the trace of the energy-momentum tensor T, inspired by f(R,T) modifications of gravity (Houndjo, 2011; Odintsov, 2013). Previous studies of neutron stars in Rastall's theory (black2, santos8) found that Rastall's gravity alone affects the neutron star radius considerably but leaves the maximum mass nearly unchanged, while the RT term introduced here also influences the maximum mass. The results enable tests of this generalized theory against current and future neutron star observations from NICER, LIGO-Virgo gravitational wave detections, and PREX-2 terrestrial measurements, potentially distinguishing between modified gravity effects and nuclear physics uncertainties.
