# Positivity domains for pairs of triples of spin observables

> Positivity restrains the allowed domains for pairs or triples of spin observables in polarised reactions. Various domain shapes in ${1\over2}+{1\over2}\to{1\over2}+{1\over2}$ reactions are displayed. Some methods to determine these domains are mentioned and a new one based on the anticommutation between two observables is presented.

## Key claim

Positivity constraints restrict the allowed domains for pairs or triples of spin observables in polarised reactions to subdomains of the square or cube, with shapes that depend on whether the corresponding operators commute or anticommute; the paper presents a new method based on anticommutation between two observables to derive disk-shaped domains.

## Method

The authors consider spin-1/2 reactions of the type A + B → C + D, using Cartesian reaction parameters to express the fully polarised differential cross section. They generate random fictitious helicity or transversity amplitudes, compute the observables, and plot them against each other to reveal the domain contours empirically. A new algebraic method is presented where disk-shaped domains for pairs of observables follow from the theorem that if two operators square to the identity and anticommute, then the sum of squares of their expectation values is bounded by unity.

## Key numbers

* For a single observable, the trivial positivity condition is that the observable lies in the interval [-1, +1].
* For pairs of observables, the allowed domains are either the full square [-1, +1]², the unit disk (⟨O₁⟩² + ⟨O₂⟩² ≤ 1), or a triangle (e.g., |2O₁| ≤ O₂ + 1 or |2O₂| ≤ O₁ + 1).
* For the reaction pp → Λ̄Λ (which has N = 6 independent amplitudes), none of the triple-observable domains reaches more than 6 corners of the cube.
* If N < 8, all triple observables are restricted to domains smaller than the cube.

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical methods by Richard (1996) and Elchikh (1999) for determining positivity domains by generating random amplitudes. The paper presents a new algebraic anticommutation method that provides a straightforward proof for disk-shaped domains, which previously required more involved calculations. The results enable systematic classification of allowed regions for spin observables in polarised reactions, with applications to reactions such as pp → Λ̄Λ and other spin-1/2 processes.
