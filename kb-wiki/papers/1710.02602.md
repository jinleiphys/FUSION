# $^6$Li in a Three-Body Model with Realistic Forces: Separable vs. Non-separable Approach

> {\bf Background:} Deuteron induced reactions are widely used to probe nuclear structure and astrophysical information. Those (d,p) reactions may be viewed as three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. {\bf Purpose:} Faddeev equations in momentum space have a long tradition of utilizing separable interactions in order to arrive at sets of coupled integral equations in one variable. However, it needs to be demonstrated that their solution based on separable interactions agrees exactly with solutions based on non-separable forces. {\bf Results:} The ground state of $^6$Li is calculated via momentum space Faddeev equations using the CD-Bonn neutron-proton force and a Woods-Saxon type neutron(proton)-$^4$He force. For the latter the Pauli-forbidden $S$-wave bound state is projected out. This result is compared to a calculation in which the interactions in the two-body subsystems are represented by separable interactions derived in the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler framework. {\bf Conclusions:} We find that calculations based on the separable representation of the interactions and the original interactions give results that agree to four significant figures for the binding energy, provided an off-shell extension of the EST representation is employed in both subsystems. The momentum distributions computed in both approaches also fully agree with each other.

## Key claim
The ground state binding energy of $^6$Li calculated using separable representations of the two-body interactions agrees with the exact non-separable Faddeev calculation to four significant figures, provided an off-shell extension of the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) representation is employed in both the nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-alpha subsystems.

## Method
The authors solved momentum-space Faddeev equations for the $^6$Li ground state modeled as an $n+p+\alpha$ three-body system. They used the CD-Bonn potential for the neutron-proton interaction and a Woods-Saxon type potential for the neutron(proton)-$^4$He interaction, with the Pauli-forbidden $S$-wave bound state projected out using a Gell-Mann-Goldberger relation. They compared this direct calculation to a second approach where the two-body interactions were represented by separable interactions constructed within the EST framework, using off-shell $t$-matrix elements at negative energies as basis states with independently chosen support energies and momenta.

## Key numbers
- Direct Faddeev calculation yields $E_{3}=-3.787$ MeV for the three-body binding energy of $^6$Li without Coulomb interaction, and $E_{3}=-2.777$ MeV with Coulomb interaction included.
- The experimental binding energy of $^6$Li is $E^{exp}_{3}=-3.699$ MeV from Ajzenberg-Selove (1988).
- For rank-6 separable representations, the uncertainty in the binding energy is $\delta E_{3}\approx 0.5$ keV, corresponding to four significant figures of precision.
- For rank-3 separable representations, the uncertainty is $\delta E_{3}\approx 50$ keV; for rank-5, it reduces to $\delta E_{3}\approx 5$ keV.
- The momentum grids for converged results used $N_p=200$ points for pair momentum and $N_q=200$ points for spectator momentum, with maximum momenta of $p=60$ fm$^{-1}$ and $q=60$ fm$^{-1}$.
- Support energies for the EST expansion were chosen in the range $-100$ MeV $\le E_l \le 0$.

## Context
This work extends the benchmark studies of separable versus non-separable Faddeev calculations previously performed for neutron-deuteron scattering by Cornelius (1990) and Nemoto (1998) to the $A+d$ case, specifically the $^6$Li ground state. It builds on the EST separable representation scheme developed by Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler (1973) and its application to nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering by Hlophe and collaborators (2013-2015). The results enable the application of separable approaches to (d,p) reaction calculations for heavier nuclei where exact Coulomb treatment via screening techniques becomes impractical, as formulated by Mukhamedzhanov (2012).
