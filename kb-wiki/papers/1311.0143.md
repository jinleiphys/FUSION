# Exact Methods for Self Interacting Neutrinos

> The effective many-body Hamiltonian which describes vacuum oscillations and self interactions of neutrinos in a two flavor mixing scheme under the single angle approximation has the same dynamical symmetries as the well known BCS pairing Hamiltonian. These dynamical symmetries manifest themselves in terms of a set of constants of motion and can be useful in formulating the collective oscillation modes in an intuitive way. In particular, we show that a neutrino spectral split can be simply viewed as an avoided level crossing between the eigenstates of a mean field Hamiltonian which includes a Lagrange multiplier in order to fix the value of an exact many-body constant of motion. We show that the same dynamical symmetries also exist in the three neutrino mixing scheme by explicitly writing down the corresponding constants of motion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the effective many-body Hamiltonian for self-interacting neutrinos in a two-flavor mixing scheme under the single-angle approximation has the same dynamical symmetries as the BCS pairing Hamiltonian, and that these symmetries also exist for three-flavor mixing. The authors demonstrate that a neutrino spectral split can be viewed as an avoided level crossing between eigenstates of a mean field Hamiltonian that includes a Lagrange multiplier to fix the value of an exact many-body constant of motion, but this interpretation is presented as an alternative view rather than a definitive explanation.

## Method

The authors formulate the neutrino many-body Hamiltonian using isospin operators obeying SU(2) commutation relations for two flavors, then generalize to SU(3) operators for three flavors. They identify the constants of motion (Gaudin invariants) for both cases and show they commute with the Hamiltonian. For the spectral split analysis, they introduce a Lagrange multiplier into the mean field Hamiltonian, diagonalize it via a transformation to instantaneous non-interacting degrees of freedom, and examine the adiabatic evolution from high to low neutrino density.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum oscillation frequency is defined as ω_p = (m_2^2 - m_1^2)/(2p), where p is the neutrino energy
- The self-interaction strength is μ = √2 G_F / V, where V is the quantization volume
- In the high-density limit (V → 0, μ → ∞), the instantaneous basis α_1, α_2 coincides with the flavor basis
- In the low-density limit (V → ∞, μ → 0), the instantaneous basis α_1, α_2 coincides with the mass basis, with the critical frequency ω_c determining which mass eigenstate each neutrino occupies

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Pehlivan (2011) that the two-flavor neutrino self-interaction model has the same dynamical symmetries as the reduced BCS pairing Hamiltonian, which was first studied by Gaudin (1976) and solved exactly by Richardson (1963) using the Bethe ansatz. The paper provides an alternative perspective on spectral splits, which were previously explained by Raffelt (2007) in terms of instantaneously stable mean field configurations viewed from a rotating frame. The demonstration of dynamical symmetries for three-flavor mixing opens the possibility of explaining multiple spectral splits and other collective behavior modes in future work.
