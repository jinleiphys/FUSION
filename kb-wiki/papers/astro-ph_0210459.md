# Temporal variation of coupling constants and nucleosynthesis

> We investigate the triple-alpha process and the Oklo phenomenon to obtain constraints on possible cosmological time variations of fundamental constants. Specifically we study cosmological temporal constraints for the fine structure constant and nucleon and meson masses.

# Temporal variation of coupling constants and nucleosynthesis

## Key claim
The triple-alpha process and the Oklo phenomenon can constrain cosmological time variations of the fine structure constant and the strong interaction strength (nucleon and meson masses), but the constraints from the triple-alpha process are one to two orders of magnitude weaker than those from quasar absorption lines, while the Oklo constraints are several orders of magnitude tighter, though the authors note that a non-linear or non-monotonic temporal variation could reconcile the different bounds.

## Method
The authors used microscopic nuclear model calculations to compute the energy shift of the 0₂⁺ resonance in ¹²C under fractional variations of the fine structure constant α_em, the nucleon mass m_N, and the meson masses m_B. They then compared the resulting change in the C/O abundance ratio from stellar nucleosynthesis models to observational constraints on C/O in ancient stars. For the Oklo phenomenon, they employed Relativistic Mean-Field Theory (RMFT) to calculate the Coulomb energy difference between ¹⁴⁹Sm and ¹⁵⁰Sm and the total energy difference under variations of α_em and the σ-, ω-, and ρ-meson masses, relating these to the energy of the resonant state in ¹⁵⁰Sm that controls the neutron capture cross section.

## Key numbers
- For the triple-alpha process: a variation of the fine structure constant of Δα_em/α_em ≈ ±6 × 10⁻³ at a mean look-back time of about 10 Gyr (z ≈ 1.5) is consistent with observed C/O abundance ratios not varying by more than a factor of 3.
- For the Oklo phenomenon: the constraint on the fine structure constant is Δα_em/α_em = (−3.6 ± 14.4) × 10⁻⁹ at a mean look-back time of about 2 Gyr (z ≈ 0.1), corresponding to a time variation rate of (−1.8 ± 7.7) × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹.
- For quasar absorption lines (from prior work by Webb and Murphy): Δα_em/α_em = (−0.72 ± 0.18) × 10⁻⁵ at a mean look-back time of about 10 Gyr (z ≈ 1.5), with a time variation rate of (−0.72 ± 0.18) × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹.
- The Coulomb energy difference between ¹⁵⁰Sm and ¹⁴⁹Sm calculated with RMFT is 1.18 MeV.
- The resonant state in ¹⁵⁰Sm relevant to the Oklo analysis lies at E_R = 0.0937 eV above the threshold of the reaction ¹⁴⁹Sm + n → ¹⁵⁰Sm + γ.

## Context
This work extends prior constraints on temporal variations of fundamental constants from the triple-alpha process (Oberhummer 2000, 2001) and the Oklo phenomenon (Damour 1996, Fujii 2000, 2002) by using microscopic nuclear model calculations and Relativistic Mean-Field Theory. The results are compared with the analysis of atomic multiplet spectra in quasar absorption lines (Webb 2000, Murphy 2001), which currently provides the only evidence for a non-zero time variation. The paper enables future work to reconcile the different constraints across cosmological epochs, possibly through non-linear or non-monotonic temporal variations of the fine structure constant.
