# Flavor Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea: Consequences for Dilepton Production

> Parton distributions derived from a chiral quark model that generates an excess of down quarks and antiquarks in the proton's sea satisfactorily describe the measured yields of muon pairs produced in proton-nucleus collisions. Comparison of dilepton yields from hydrogen and deuterium targets promises greater sensitivity to the predicted flavor asymmetry.

# Flavor Asymmetry of the Nucleon Sea: Consequences for Dilepton Production

## Key claim

The chiral quark model's prediction of a small excess of down quarks and antiquarks over up quarks and antiquarks in the proton sea is consistent with existing E772 dilepton data, but the effect on the ratio of yields from tungsten versus isoscalar targets is too small to be clearly distinguished; a more sensitive test using hydrogen and deuterium targets is required to confirm or rule out the predicted flavor asymmetry.

## Method

The authors implemented the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984), in which constituent quarks fluctuate into quarks plus Goldstone bosons (pions), generating a flavor-asymmetric sea. They constructed parton distributions by adjusting the ultraviolet cutoff of the chiral quark model to reproduce the Gottfried-sum-rule defect observed by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC, 1991), then used these distributions to compute Drell-Yan cross sections for muon-pair production in 800 GeV/c proton-nucleus collisions, comparing the ratio of yields from tungsten versus isoscalar targets (E772 data) and predicting the ratio from hydrogen versus deuterium targets.

## Key numbers

- NMC measurement of the Gottfried integral: I_G = 0.240 ± 0.016 at Q^2 = 4 (GeV/c)^2, implying an integrated excess of down over up antiquarks of ∫ dx [ū(x) - d̄(x)] = -0.14 ± 0.024
- Chiral quark model prediction for the Gottfried integral: I_G = 0.278, close to the experimental value
- The flavor-asymmetric sea is a small perturbation on the flavor-symmetric EHLQ Set 1 sea, where xū(x) = xd̄(x) = 0.182(1-x)^8.54
- For the ratio R_W(x) ≈ 1 + 0.195(d̄(x)-ū(x))/(d̄(x)+ū(x)) for tungsten targets, the chiral quark model prediction is consistent with E772 data showing no significant deviation from unity
- The lowest-order Drell-Yan calculation for pd → μ⁺μ⁻ + anything at 800 GeV was normalized to large-x_F data using a K-factor of 1.4

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1992) that explained the Gottfried-sum-rule violation reported by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC, 1991). It shows that the same flavor-asymmetric sea is consistent with the E772 dilepton data, contradicting the more extreme ad hoc modifications of EHLQ structure functions considered by Ellis and Stirling (1991) and by the authors themselves. The paper enables future experiments such as Fermilab proposal P866 to test the predicted small excess of d̄ over ū at low x by comparing dilepton yields from hydrogen and deuterium targets, which would provide greater sensitivity than the existing tungsten-versus-isoscalar comparison.
