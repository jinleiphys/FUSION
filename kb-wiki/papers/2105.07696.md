# Nuclear energy density functionals from machine learning

> Machine learning is employed to build an energy density functional for self-bound nuclear systems for the first time. By learning the kinetic energy as a functional of the nucleon density alone, a robust and accurate orbital-free density functional for nuclei is established. Self-consistent calculations that bypass the Kohn-Sham equations provide the ground-state densities, total energies, and root-mean-square radii with a high accuracy in comparison with the Kohn-Sham solutions. No existing orbital-free density functional theory comes close to this performance for nuclei. Therefore, it provides a new promising way for future developments of nuclear energy density functionals for the whole nuclear chart.

## Key claim
Machine learning (kernel ridge regression) can build an orbital-free nuclear energy density functional that, for the first time, reproduces Kohn-Sham ground-state densities, total energies, and radii to within 0.5% for spherical nuclei, but the proof-of-principle demonstration is limited to three nuclei (4He, 16O, 40Ca) with equal proton and neutron numbers.

## Method
The authors used kernel ridge regression (KRR) to learn the kinetic energy as a functional of the one-dimensional radial nucleon density alone, training on 30,000 samples generated by solving the Schrodinger equation with a combined Gaussian potential for noninteracting systems. The interaction energy was taken from the Skyrme functional SkP. Self-consistent ground-state densities were obtained by gradient descent variation of the total energy functional, bypassing the Kohn-Sham equations, with a local principal component analysis and adaptive functional derivative to handle the functional derivative of the ML kinetic energy term.

## Key numbers
- KRR hyperparameters: regularization lambda = 1.8 x 10^-12, kernel width sigma = 0.58 fm^-1
- Kinetic energy prediction deviations: below 1 keV for most cases in validation and test sets
- Self-consistent density differences from Kohn-Sham: less than 0.01 fm^-1 for 4He, 0.04 fm^-1 for 16O, 0.1 fm^-1 for 40Ca
- Total energy deviations from Kohn-Sham: 4He -26.3931 MeV (KS -26.3700), 16O -127.1622 MeV (KS -127.3781), 40Ca -341.8027 MeV (KS -342.0645)
- Root-mean-square radii from ML: 4He 2.1628 fm, 16O 2.8113 fm, 40Ca 3.4652 fm; all within 0.5% of Kohn-Sham values
- Computational cost scaling: ML orbital-free DFT scales as O(N) versus O(N^3) for Kohn-Sham DFT

## Context
This work extends the application of machine learning to density functional theory from condensed-matter physics (Snyder 2012, Brockherde 2016) and quantum chemistry to nuclear systems for the first time. It directly addresses the long-standing failure of orbital-free DFT for nuclei, where even the Thomas-Fermi approximation with h^4 corrections (Brack 1985, Centelles 2007) shows obvious deviations from Kohn-Sham results. The approach enables future development of orbital-free DFT for the whole nuclear chart and time-dependent orbital-free DFT for fission and fusion problems, potentially replacing expensive 3D Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solvers that require tens of thousands of CPU hours.
