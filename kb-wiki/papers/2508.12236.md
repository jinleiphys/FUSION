# Candidates for three-quasiparticle $K$-isomers in even-odd Fm-Cn nuclei

> Following our study of possible $K$-isomers in odd-even Md-Rg nuclei, here we continue with searching for three-quasiparticle 1$ν$2$π$ and 3$ν$ isomer candidates in even-odd Fm - Cn nuclei. We use the same approach to calculate energies of different nuclear configurations using a microscopic-macroscopic model with the Woods-Saxon potential. We used two versions of pairing: quasi-particle BCS method and particle number projection formalism. The optimal deformations for both ground states and high-$K$ configurations are determined through a four-dimensional energy minimization process. We point out the most promising candidates for high-$K$ isomers and compare them, where possible, with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the most promising candidates for three-quasiparticle high-$K$ isomers in even-odd Fm-Cn nuclei are $1\nu2\pi$ configurations with excitation energies generally above 1.0–1.5 MeV, but the actual existence of these isomers depends on the pairing treatment used (quasiparticle BCS versus particle number projection) and on the specific neutron number relative to the $N=152$ and $N=162$ shell gaps, which cause abrupt changes in configuration energies.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic-macroscopic model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential and Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy to calculate energies of three-quasiparticle ($1\nu2\pi$ and $3\nu$) configurations in even-odd isotopes of Fm, No, Rf, Sg, Hs, Ds, and Cn. They performed four-dimensional energy minimization over $\beta_{20}$, $\beta_{40}$, $\beta_{60}$, and $\beta_{80}$ deformations for both ground states and high-$K$ configurations, using two pairing treatments: the quasiparticle BCS method and the particle number projection (PNP) formalism. The PNP method was applied to selected low-lying configurations to correct for deficiencies of BCS in the weak pairing regime, particularly for three-neutron quasiparticle excitations.

## Key numbers

- The smallest excitation energies for $3\nu$ quasiparticle states were approximately 0.65 MeV with the initial pairing strength, increasing to approximately 1 MeV after adjusting the neutron pairing strength to $G_n = 1.15 \times G_n^{mod}$.
- For Fm isotopes, the lowest $1\nu2\pi$ excitation energies from PNP calculations range from 1.3–1.4 MeV for the lightest isotopes ($N=141$–143) up to approximately 2.8 MeV for heavier isotopes.
- In Rf isotopes, some $1\nu2\pi$ configurations exhibit excitation energies around 1.0–1.2 MeV, particularly in the $N \approx 147$–155 region.
- For Sg isotopes, quasiparticle calculations indicate minima around 1.1 MeV for $N \approx 145$–155.
- For Ds near $N=163$, the lowest quasiparticle excitation energies are predicted around 1.2–1.3 MeV.
- The experimental ground-state configurations in $^{255}$Fm and $^{257}$Fm come out excited in the PNP calculation by 140 and 160 keV, respectively, and in $^{259}$No by 170 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of three-quasiparticle $K$-isomers in odd-even Md-Rg nuclei (Jachimowicz, Kowal, Skalski 2023) to the even-odd Fm-Cn region, using the same microscopic-macroscopic model. The predictions are compared with existing experimental data on high-$K$ isomers in $^{251,253,255}$No and $^{253,255,257}$Rf reported by Hess (2006), Lop (2007, 2022), Streicher (2010), Antalic (2011), Bronis (2022), Kessaci (2024), Mosat (2020), Chakma (2023), Qian (2009), Berr (2010), and Riss (2013). The results enable future experimental searches for $K$-isomers in superheavy nuclei, particularly in isotopes where the predicted excitation energies are low enough to be accessible with current techniques.
