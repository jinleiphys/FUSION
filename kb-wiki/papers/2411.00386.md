# Scale-(in)dependence in quantum 4-body scattering

> We investigate the multi-channel 4-body scattering system using regularized 2- and 3-body contact interactions. The analysis determines the sensitivity of bound-state energies, scattering phase shifts and cross sections on the cutoff parameter ($λ$), and the energy gaps between scattering thresholds. The latter dependency is obtained with a 2-body scale fixed to an unnaturally large value and a floating 3-body parameter. Specifically, we calculate the binding energies of the shallow 3- and 4-body states, dimer-dimer and trimer-atom scattering lengths, and the trimer-atom to dimer-dimer reaction rates. Employing a potential renormalized by a large 2-body scattering length and a 3-body scale, we find all calculated observables to remain practically constant over the range $6\textrm{fm}^{-2}<λ<10\textrm{fm}^{-2}$. Divergences in scattering lengths emerge for critical 3-body parameters at which thresholds are degenerate. Such threshold effects are found to be independent of the regulator cutoff. Furthermore, at those critical points where the dimer-dimer and trimer-atom thresholds overlap, we predict an enhancement of the inelastic over the elastic scattering event. Such an inversion between elastic- and rearrangement-collision probabilities indicates a strong sensitivity of the 4-body reaction dynamics on the 3-body parameter at finite 2-body scale. This phenomenon is absent in earlier studies which differ in the renormalization scheme. As this discrepancy arises for all considered cutoffs, a more comprehensive parametrization of short-distance structure is necessary: sole cutoff variation does not reveal non-perturbative change in reaction rates conjectured to be due to a combined effect of the finite 2-body range and the specific choice for the 3-body parameter.

## Key claim

The authors find that 4-body scattering observables (binding energies, scattering lengths, and reaction cross sections) remain practically constant over a cutoff range of \(6\,\text{fm}^{-2}<\lambda<10\,\text{fm}^{-2}\), but this cutoff insensitivity does not extend to revealing a non-perturbative change in reaction rates that is conjectured to arise from the combined effect of finite 2-body range and the specific choice of the 3-body parameter; the discrepancy with earlier studies indicates that sole cutoff variation is insufficient to capture the full short-distance structure.

## Method

The authors solve the multi-channel 4-body scattering problem using a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian with Gaussian-regularized 2- and 3-body contact interactions, employing a variational method (the refined resonating group method) with a genetic algorithm to optimize trial wavefunction widths. They compute bound-state energies, dimer-dimer and trimer-atom scattering lengths, and elastic and rearrangement (dimer-dimer to trimer-atom) cross sections as functions of the regulator cutoff \(\lambda\) and the energy gap between scattering thresholds, with the 2-body scale fixed to an unnaturally large scattering length and the 3-body parameter varied to control the trimer binding energy.

## Key numbers

* Dimer binding energy fixed at \(B_2 = 0.5\,\text{MeV} = 0.0025\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) throughout the study.
* Cutoff range investigated: \(6\,\text{fm}^{-2} < \lambda < 10\,\text{fm}^{-2}\), corresponding to a ratio of dimer size to interaction range of approximately 50.
* For \(\lambda = 6\,\text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\zeta_1 = 1.2\) (ratio of first excited trimer binding to dimer binding), dimer-dimer scattering length \(a_{22} = 21.0(20)\,\text{fm}\) and trimer-atom scattering length \(a_{31} = -19.0(20)\,\text{fm}\).
* For \(\lambda = 6\,\text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\zeta_1 = 1.9\), dimer-dimer scattering length \(a_{22} = 4.5(5)\,\text{fm}\) and trimer-atom scattering length \(a_{31} = -0.8(16)\,\text{fm}\).
* For \(\lambda = 6\,\text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\zeta_1 = 9.0\), dimer-dimer scattering length \(a_{22} = 4.5(2)\,\text{fm}\) and trimer-atom scattering length \(a_{31} = -12(16)\,\text{fm}\).
* 2-body scattering length fixed at \(a_2 = 10\,\text{fm}\) (vertical line in the Efimov plot).
* Tetramer binding energies for \(\lambda = 6\,\text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\zeta_1 = 1.2\): deepest state at \(84.6\,B_2\); for \(\zeta_1 = 9.0\): states at \(1228.8\,B_2\), \(3706.4\,B_2\), and \(9132.4\,B_2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of universal few-body physics beyond the unitary limit (where the 2-body scattering length diverges) to the regime of large but finite 2-body scale, relevant for nuclear systems. It builds on the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1971) and the development of pionless EFT (van Kolck, 1998; Bedaque, 1999) by examining 4-body scattering observables, which have been less studied than bound-state properties. The results contradict earlier studies (Deltuva, 2010) regarding the inversion of elastic and rearrangement cross sections at threshold degeneracy, suggesting that a more comprehensive parametrization of short-distance structure beyond cutoff variation is needed. The work enables future experimental tests with cold atoms (where the 2-body scattering length can be tuned) and theoretical refinement of contact interactions for nuclear fusion reactions.
