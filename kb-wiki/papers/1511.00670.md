# Analysis of mechanisms that could contribute to the neutrinoless double-beta decay

> Neutrinoless double-beta decay, if observed, would signal physics beyond the Standard Model that could be discovered at energies significantly lower than those at which the relevant degrees of freedom could be excited. Therefore, it could be challenging to further use the neutrinoless double-beta decay observations to distinguish between many beyond Standard Model mechanisms contributing to this process. Accurate nuclear structure calculations of the nuclear matrix elements necessary to analyze the decay rates could be helpful to narrow down the list of contributing mechanisms, and to better identify the more exotic properties of the neutrinos. We investigate the information one can get from the angular and energy distribution of the emitted electron assuming that the right-handed currents exist. For the analysis of these distributions we calculate the necessary nuclear matrix elements using shell model techniques.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether measurements of the angular and energy distributions of the two emitted electrons in neutrinoless double-beta decay can distinguish between the standard light neutrino-exchange mechanism and contributions from right-handed currents (the lambda and eta mechanisms), but the analysis is conditional on the existence of right-handed currents and is performed specifically for the isotope 82Se using shell model nuclear matrix elements.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear matrix elements for 82Se using the interacting shell model with the JUN45 effective Hamiltonian in the jj44 model space, including short-range correlations with CD-Bonn parametrization, finite nucleon size effects, and higher-order corrections of the nucleon current. They then computed the two-electron angular and energy distributions for five scenarios (mass mechanism only, mass plus eta mechanism, mass plus lambda mechanism, lambda plus eta mechanism without mass, and all three mechanisms competing) using the calculated matrix elements and phase-space factors, explicitly including interference terms between the different mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The calculated Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix element for 82Se is M_GT = 2.993 (dimensionless)
- The calculated half-life for the mass mechanism only (Case 0) with neutrino mass parameter <nu> = 2 x 10^-7 is 9.41 x 10^25 years
- The Q-value for 82Se is 2.99 MeV
- The phase-space factor G_1 for 82Se is 2.31 x 10^-14 yr^-1
- The mass mechanism dominates when <nu> is at least 4 times larger than <lambda> and at least 400 times larger than <eta>
- The on-axis limits for neutrino physics parameters corresponding to the same half-life as the 100 meV mass mechanism (9.41 x 10^25 years) are <lambda> = 1.2 x 10^-7 and <eta> = 1.0 x 10^-9
- The ratio max/min in the angular distribution is 15/1 for Case 3 (lambda and eta only) versus 2/1 for Case 2 (mass and lambda)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of right-handed current contributions to neutrinoless double-beta decay by Doi (1985) and the SuperNEMO collaboration (2010), using shell model nuclear matrix elements rather than the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) matrix elements used in those earlier studies. The analysis is specific to 82Se, the baseline isotope for the SuperNEMO experiment, and provides a more detailed treatment of interference effects than the general analysis by Stefanik (2015). The results enable experimentalists to use measured angular and energy distributions to identify which beyond-Standard-Model mechanisms dominate, provided the half-lives are within the SuperNEMO experimental reach of 1.0 x 10^26 years.
