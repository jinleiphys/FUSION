# Nucleus--nucleus interactions in the inner crust of neutron stars

> The interaction between nuclei in the inner crust of neutron stars consists of two contributions, the so-called "direct" interaction and an "induced" one due to density changes in the neutron fluid. For large nuclear separations $r$ the contributions from nuclear forces to each of these terms are shown to be nonzero. In the static limit they are equal in magnitude but have opposite signs and they cancel exactly. We analyze earlier results on effective interactions in the light of this finding. We consider the properties of long-wavelength collective modes and, in particular, calculate the degree of mixing between the lattice phonons and the phonons in the neutron superfluid. Using microscopic theory, we calculate the net non-Coulombic contribution to the nucleus--nucleus interaction and show that, for large $r$, the leading term is due to exchange of two phonons and varies as $1/r^7$: it is an analog of the Casimir--Polder interaction between neutral atoms.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the direct and induced contributions to the nucleus-nucleus interaction in the inner crust of neutron stars cancel exactly in the static limit for large nuclear separations, leaving only a residual non-Coulombic interaction that decays as \(1/r^7\) due to two-phonon exchange; this cancellation is a general thermodynamic result that holds when Coulomb and surface effects are neglected.

## Method

The authors use thermodynamic reasoning, analogous to that applied to dilute solutions of \(^3\)He in superfluid liquid \(^4\)He (BBP1), to derive the effective interaction between nuclei in the inner crust. They analyze the second-order energy density variations in terms of direct and induced interactions, express the induced interaction in terms of the density-density response function of the neutron fluid, and verify the cancellation using a void model and liquid drop models (Lattimer and Swesty, 1991). They then calculate collective mode velocities and hybridization between lattice phonons and neutron superfluid phonons using the two-fluid model, and perform a microscopic calculation of the long-range non-Coulombic interaction.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(\Gamma = E_{np}^2 / (E_{pp}^{\rm nuc} E_{nn})\) is 1.335 at baryon density \(n = 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\), indicating near-cancellation of direct and induced nuclear interactions; values across the inner crust are of the same order.
- The electron contribution to \(E_{pp}\) is \(\partial \mu_e / \partial n_e \approx \frac13 \mu_e / n_e\) for ultrarelativistic electrons.
- The lattice Coulomb contribution to \(E_{pp}\) is approximately \(-0.05337 (Z/40)^{2/3} \partial \mu_e / \partial n_e\).
- The leading non-Coulombic nucleus-nucleus interaction at large separation \(r\) varies as \(1/r^7\), analogous to the Casimir-Polder interaction.
- The hybridization parameter \(\lambda = v_{np}^2 / (v_p^2 - v_n^2)\) is typically of order 10% in amplitude (1% in probability) across most of the inner crust, except near the crust-core boundary where uncoupled mode velocities cross.
- Using Chamel's (2012) neutron superfluid densities, the ratio \(\lambda_C / \lambda\) ranges from 0.6408 at \(n = 3.162 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) to 29.43 at \(n = 5.012 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\), showing strong sensitivity to the superfluid density.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach of Bardeen, Baym, and Pines (1967) for helium mixtures to the inner crust of neutron stars, where nuclei coexist with a neutron fluid and electrons. It corrects and analyzes earlier results on effective interactions from Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and collective mode calculations by Kobyakov and Pethick (2014), showing that the direct and induced nuclear interactions cancel in the static limit. The findings enable more accurate calculations of collective mode velocities and damping in the crust, and the predicted \(1/r^7\) Casimir-Polder-type interaction provides a new ingredient for studies of lattice structure and dynamical phenomena in neutron star crusts.
