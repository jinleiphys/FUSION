# Quenching of hadron spectra in media

> We determine how the yield of large transverse momentum hadrons is modified due to induced gluon radiation off a hard parton traversing a QCD medium. The quenching factor is formally a collinear- and infrared-safe quantity and can be treated perturbatively. In spite of that, in the $p_\perp$ region of practical interest, its value turns out to be extremely sensitive to large distances and can be used to unravel the properties of dense quark-gluon final states produced in heavy ion collisions. We also find that the standard modelling of quenching by shifting $p_\perp$ in the hard parton cross section by the mean energy loss is inadequate.

## Key claim

The quenching of high-transverse-momentum hadron spectra in a QCD medium is not adequately described by shifting the vacuum cross section by the mean energy loss; instead, the steep fall-off of the vacuum spectrum creates a bias that makes the typical energy loss much smaller than the mean, leading to a transverse-momentum-dependent shift proportional to the square root of \(p_\perp\).

## Method

The authors compute the medium-modified inclusive hadron spectrum by convoluting the vacuum production cross section (parameterized as a power law \(1/p_\perp^n\) with large effective exponent \(n\)) with a distribution \(D(\mathcal{E})\) for the additional energy loss due to medium-induced gluon radiation. The energy-loss distribution is derived from the BDMPS gluon spectrum using a Poisson ansatz for independent soft gluon emission, expressed via a Mellin transform that exponentiates the integrated gluon multiplicity. The quenching factor is then evaluated in the large-\(n\) approximation, which replaces the power-law ratio by an exponential, yielding a shift function \(S(p_\perp)\) defined by the Laplace transform of the energy-loss distribution.

## Key numbers

* The transport coefficient for cold nuclear matter is estimated as \(\hat{q}_{\text{cold}} \simeq 0.009\ \text{GeV}^3 \simeq 0.045\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) (from Eq. (4) with \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), \(\alpha_s = 0.5\), and \(xG(x)=1\)).
* For a hot quark-gluon plasma at \(T = 250\ \text{MeV}\), the transport coefficient is \(\hat{q}_{\text{hot}} \simeq 0.2\ \text{GeV}^3 \simeq 1\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) (Eq. (5)).
* The characteristic gluon frequency is \(\omega_c = \hat{q} L^2 / 2\) (Eq. (6)).
* The shift function in the strong-quenching regime is \(S(p_\perp) \simeq \sqrt{2\pi \alpha^2 \omega_c p_\perp / n}\) (Eq. (35)), where \(\alpha = 2\alpha_s C_R/\pi\) and \(n\) is the effective power-law exponent of the vacuum spectrum.
* The minimal transverse momentum for perturbative control is estimated as \(p_\perp > \pi n \omega_{\text{BH}} \approx 10\ \text{GeV}\), using \(n \simeq 12\)–\(13\) and \(\omega_{\text{BH}} \simeq 300\ \text{MeV}\) for a hot medium with \(\hat{q} \simeq 0.2\ \text{GeV}^3\) and \(\lambda \simeq 1/4\ \text{fm}\) (Eq. (42)).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation (Baier et al., 1997; Zakharov, 1996) to the problem of inclusive hadron suppression, showing that the standard practice of modelling quenching by a constant shift equal to the mean energy loss (used in earlier jet-quenching studies such as those by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev, 2000–2001) is inadequate. The analysis reveals that the bias from the steeply falling vacuum spectrum forces the relevant energy loss to be much smaller than the mean, producing a \(p_\perp\)-dependent shift. This result enables a more accurate extraction of the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) from experimental data on high-\(p_\perp\) hadron suppression in heavy-ion collisions, and it clarifies the parametric regimes where perturbative control is maintained versus where sensitivity to non-perturbative infrared scales (the Bethe–Heitler limit) becomes significant.
