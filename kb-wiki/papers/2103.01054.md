# Weakly bound $H$ dibaryon from SU(3)-flavor-symmetric QCD

> We present the first study of baryon-baryon interactions in the continuum limit of lattice QCD, finding unexpectedly large lattice artifacts. Specifically, we determine the binding energy of the $H$ dibaryon at a single quark-mass point. The calculation is performed at six values of the lattice spacing $a$, using O($a$)-improved Wilson fermions at the SU(3)-symmetric point with $m_π=m_K\approx 420$ MeV. Energy levels are extracted by applying a variational method to correlation matrices of bilocal two-baryon interpolating operators computed using the distillation technique. Our analysis employs Lüscher's finite-volume quantization condition to determine the scattering phase shifts from the spectrum and vice versa, both above and below the two-baryon threshold. We perform global fits to the lattice spectra using parametrizations of the phase shift, supplemented by terms describing discretization effects, then extrapolate the lattice spacing to zero. The phase shift and the binding energy determined from it are found to be strongly affected by lattice artifacts. Our estimate of the binding energy in the continuum limit of three-flavor QCD is $B_H^{\text{SU(3)}_{\rm f}}=4.56\pm1.13_{\rm stat}\pm0.63_{\rm syst}$ MeV.

## Key claim

The H dibaryon is weakly bound in SU(3)-flavor-symmetric QCD with a binding energy of 4.56 MeV, but this result is obtained at a single unphysical quark-mass point with degenerate up, down, and strange quarks; the authors note that SU(3) symmetry breaking, which is crucial because the splitting between physical Lambda-Lambda and N-Xi thresholds is larger than this binding energy, may make a bound state at the physical point unlikely.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations at six values of the lattice spacing using O(a)-improved Wilson fermions at the SU(3)-symmetric point with pion and kaon masses of approximately 420 MeV. They extracted finite-volume energy levels using a variational method applied to correlation matrices of bilocal two-baryon interpolating operators computed via the distillation technique, then employed Luscher's finite-volume quantization condition to determine scattering phase shifts from the spectrum. Global fits to the lattice spectra were performed using parametrizations of the phase shift supplemented by terms describing discretization effects, followed by extrapolation to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy in the continuum limit of three-flavor QCD: B_H = 4.56 +/- 1.13 (stat) +/- 0.63 (syst) MeV
- Scattering length: a_0 = 3.30 +/- 0.36 (stat) +/- 0.21 (syst) fm
- Effective range: r_0 = 0.98 +/- 0.04 (stat) +/- 0.05 (syst) fm
- Calculation performed at m_pi = m_K approximately 420 MeV with six different lattice spacings and multiple box sizes satisfying m_pi L >= 4.4

## Context

This work presents the first systematic study of discretization effects in a multibaryon system on the lattice, extending previous calculations by Francis (2018) that found B_H = 19 +/- 10 MeV at a heavier SU(3)-symmetric point with pion mass 960 MeV. The result is substantially lower than earlier lattice calculations by Beane (2010, 2011, 2012) and Inoue (2010, 2011) at nonzero lattice spacing, and adds to evidence against deeply bound hexaquark dark matter. The authors note that previous estimates based on extrapolations of lattice data by Shanahan (2011), Haidenbauer (2011), Inoue (2011), Yamaguchi (2016), and Li (2018) found a bound state at the physical point unlikely, and this smaller binding energy should make it even less likely.
