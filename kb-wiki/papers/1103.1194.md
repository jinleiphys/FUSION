# Magnetic Susceptibility of the Quark Condensate and Polarization from Chiral Models

> We compute the magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate and the polarization of quarks at zero temperature and in a uniform magnetic background. Our theoretical framework consists of two chiral models that allow to treat self-consistently the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry: the linear $σ-$model coupled to quarks, dubbed quark-meson model, and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We also perform analytic estimates of the same quantities within the renormalized quark-meson model, both in the regimes of weak and strong fields. Our numerical results are in agreement with the recent literature; moreover, we confirm previous Lattice findings, related to the saturation of the polarization at large fields.

## Key claim

The magnetic susceptibility of the quark condensate and the polarization of quarks are computed using two chiral models (the quark-meson model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) at zero temperature in a uniform magnetic background, with the hedge that the numerical results agree with recent literature and confirm previous Lattice findings regarding saturation of polarization at large fields, but only within the non-renormalized models where lowest Landau level dominance explains the saturation.

## Method

The authors compute the quantum effective potential at one-loop level using the Leung-Ritus-Wang method to express quark propagators in terms of Landau levels in a uniform magnetic field. They then minimize the effective potential to obtain the chiral condensate and magnetic moment for each quark flavor, using a smooth ultraviolet regulator with N=5. The magnetic susceptibility is extracted from a linear fit of polarization data at small fields (up to 5 m_pi^2), while polarization at large fields is studied numerically and analytically within both the NJL and QM models.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic susceptibility from NJL model: chi ≈ -4.3 GeV^{-2}
- Magnetic susceptibility from QM model: chi ≈ -5.25 GeV^{-2}
- Product chi * ⟨f-bar f⟩ from NJL model: 69 MeV
- Product chi * ⟨f-bar f⟩ from QM model: 65 MeV
- Constituent quark mass in QM model vacuum: m_q = 335 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 700 MeV
- UV cutoff in QM model: Lambda = 560 MeV, giving ⟨u-bar u⟩ = (-231 MeV)^3
- UV cutoff in NJL model: Lambda = 626.76 MeV, with G = 2.02/Lambda^2 and m_0 = 5 MeV
- Asymptotic polarization value at large fields: mu_infinity = 1
- At eB_c ≈ 0.6 GeV^2 (estimated critical value for vector meson condensation), the LLL contributes about 80% of the u-quark condensate

## Context

This work extends the computation of magnetic susceptibility and polarization from Lattice QCD simulations by Buividovich (2009) and from sum rules by Ioffe (1983), Belyaev (1984), and Vainshtein (2002) to effective chiral models that treat chiral symmetry breaking self-consistently. The results enable comparison between model predictions and Lattice data, particularly regarding the saturation of polarization at strong fields, and provide a physical interpretation of saturation as lowest Landau level dominance. The authors note that their NJL result for chi agrees within 3% with the pion dominance estimate of Vainshtein (2002) at renormalization scale 0.5 GeV, while the QM result agrees within 18%.
