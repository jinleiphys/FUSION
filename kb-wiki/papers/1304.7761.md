# Quantum critical behavior in three dimensional lattice Gross-Neveu models

> We study quantum critical behavior in three dimensional lattice Gross-Neveu models containing two massless Dirac fermions. We focus on two models with SU(2) flavor symmetry and either a $Z_2$ or a U(1) chiral symmetry. Both models could not be studied earlier due to sign problems. We use the fermion bag approach which is free of sign problems and compute critical exponents at the phase transitions. We estimate $ν= 0.83(1)$, $η= 0.62(1)$, $η_ψ= 0.38(1)$ in the $Z_2$ and $ν= 0.849(8)$, $η= 0.633(8)$, $η_ψ= 0.373(3)$ in the U(1) model.

## Key claim

The critical exponents at the quantum phase transition in three-dimensional lattice Gross-Neveu models with two massless Dirac fermions are nearly identical for both $Z_2$ and $U(1)$ chiral symmetries, despite the different symmetry breaking patterns, and the authors cannot rule out that the exponents are the same within current precision.

## Method

The authors used the fermion bag approach, a Monte Carlo method free of sign problems, to simulate two lattice Gross-Neveu models with SU(2) flavor symmetry and either $Z_2$ or $U(1)$ chiral symmetry. They computed the chiral condensate susceptibility and a fermion correlation function ratio on lattices ranging from $12^3$ to $36^3$, then performed combined finite-size scaling fits to extract the critical exponents $\nu$, $\eta$, and $\eta_\psi$ at the second-order phase transition from a massless to a massive fermion phase.

## Key numbers

- $Z_2$ model: $\nu = 0.83(1)$, $\eta = 0.62(1)$, $\eta_\psi = 0.38(1)$, critical coupling $U_c = 0.0893(1)$
- $U(1)$ model: $\nu = 0.82(2)$, $\eta = 0.62(2)$, $\eta_\psi = 0.37(1)$, critical coupling $U_c = 0.1560(4)$
- Combined fit across three $SU(2) \times U(1)$ symmetric lattice models: $\nu = 0.849(8)$, $\eta = 0.633(8)$, $\eta_\psi = 0.373(3)$
- At infinite coupling ($U = \infty$), the $U(1)$ model chiral susceptibility fits chiral perturbation theory with $\Sigma^2 = 0.844(1)$ and $\rho_s = 0.381(3)$ for $L \geq 10$; the $Z_2$ model gives a constant $0.971(1)$ for $L \geq 16$

## Context

This work resolves puzzles in the literature: earlier lattice Monte Carlo calculations of Gross-Neveu models by Karkkainen et al. (1993) gave $\nu \approx 1.0$ and $\eta \approx 0.75$, but those results likely suffered from sign problems that the fermion bag approach avoids. The critical exponents found here match those of the lattice Thirring model studied by Chandrasekharan (2011), confirming they belong to the same universality class despite different microscopic formulations. The results enable comparison with continuum RG-flow methods and $\epsilon$-expansion calculations by Rosenstein (1993), which agree for $\nu$ and $\eta$ but not for $\eta_\psi$, and may inform the universality class of the semimetal-insulator transition in graphene.
