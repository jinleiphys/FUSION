# Lowest-order QED radiative corrections in unpolarized elastic electron-deuteron scattering beyond the ultra-relativistic limit for the proposed deuteron charge radius measurement at Jefferson Laboratory

> Analogous to the well-known proton charge radius puzzle, a similar puzzle exists for the deuteron charge radius, $r_{d}$. There are discrepancies observed in the results of $r_{d}$, measured from electron-deuteron ($e-d$) scattering experiments, as well as from atomic spectroscopy. In order to help resolve the charge radius puzzle of the deuteron, the PRad collaboration at Jefferson Lab has proposed an experiment for measuring $r_{d}$, named DRad. This experiment is designed to measure the unpolarized elastic $e-d$ scattering cross section in a low-$Q^{2}$ region. To extract the cross section with a high precision, having reliable knowledge of QED radiative corrections is important. In this paper, we present complete numerical calculations of the lowest-order radiative corrections in $e-d$ scattering for the DRad kinematics. The calculations have been performed within a covariant formalism and beyond the ultra-relativistic approximation ($m_{e}^{2} \ll Q^{2}$). Besides, we present a systematic uncertainty on $r_{d}$ arising from higher-order radiative corrections, estimated based on our cross-section results.

## Key claim

The paper presents complete numerical calculations of the lowest-order QED radiative corrections for unpolarized elastic electron-deuteron scattering, performed within a covariant formalism and beyond the ultra-relativistic approximation (where the electron mass squared is not assumed to be much smaller than the four-momentum transfer squared), specifically for the kinematics of the proposed DRad experiment at Jefferson Lab.

## Method

The authors used the covariant Bardin-Shumeiko formalism for the extraction and cancellation of infrared divergences from virtual and real photon radiation in the Feynman diagrams. They calculated the lowest-order radiative corrections including the lepton vertex correction, vacuum polarization, and bremsstrahlung from the leptonic legs, with the electron mass taken into account. The calculations were performed for the DRad kinematics at beam energies of 1.1 GeV and 2.2 GeV, covering a scattering angle range of 0.7 degrees to 6.0 degrees, corresponding to a Q² range from 2×10⁻⁴ (GeV/c)² to 5×10⁻² (GeV/c)².

## Key numbers

- The DRad experiment is designed to measure the deuteron charge radius with a precision of approximately 0.2 percent.
- The systematic uncertainty on the deuteron charge radius arising from higher-order radiative corrections was estimated based on the cross-section results; the absolute RC systematic uncertainty in PRad for electron-proton scattering due to higher-order RCs was 0.0020 fm, while for the Moller process it was 0.0065 fm, with a total RC systematic uncertainty of Δr_p = 0.0069 fm.
- The deuteron mass used in calculations is 1875.612 MeV.
- The deuteron magnetic dipole moment is μ_M^d = 0.8574 (dimensionless) and the electric quadrupole moment is μ_Q^d = 0.2859 fm².
- The electromagnetic fine-structure constant used is α = 1/137.036.

## Context

This work extends the covariant radiative correction framework previously developed for electron-proton scattering by Akushevich et al. (2015) and Afanasev et al. (2021) to the electron-deuteron case, specifically for the DRad experiment proposed by the PRad collaboration at Jefferson Lab. The calculations are motivated by the deuteron charge radius puzzle, where discrepancies exist between values from electron-deuteron scattering experiments and atomic spectroscopy measurements, similar to the well-known proton charge radius puzzle. The results enable a model-independent extraction of the deuteron charge radius with high precision to help resolve this puzzle, and the framework can be applied to the simultaneous measurement of Moller scattering for systematic uncertainty control.
