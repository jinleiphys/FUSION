# A fresh look at hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon g-2 with the Dyson-Schwinger approach

> We present first results for the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon a_μ in the framework of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We determine the quark loop and pseudoscalar (π^0, η, η') meson exchange diagram using a phenomenological model for the combined strength of the gluon propagator and the quark-gluon interaction as the only input. Our result for meson exchange, a_μ^{LBL;PS}=(84 \pm 13) x 10^{-11}, is commensurate with previous calculations. However, our number for the quark loop contribution, a_μ^{LBL;quarkloop} = (107 \pm 2 \pm 46) x 10^{-11}, is significantly larger due to dressing effects in the quark propagator and the quark-photon vertex. Taken at face value, this then leads to a revised estimate of the total a_μ=116 591 865.0(96.6) x 10^{-11}, which reduces the difference between theory and experiment to about 1.9 σ.

## Key claim
The paper finds that the dressed quark-loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering is significantly larger than previous estimates, leading to a revised total theoretical value for the muon anomalous magnetic moment that reduces the discrepancy with experiment to about 1.9 sigma, but the authors caution that this result is not final and depends on the treatment of the quark-photon vertex.

## Method
The authors use Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) in Landau gauge QCD with the Maris-Tandy model for the combined gluon propagator and quark-gluon interaction strength. They calculate the pseudoscalar meson exchange contribution using fully dressed quark propagators and numerically determined pi-gamma-gamma form factors from the underlying theory, and the quark-loop contribution using dressed quark propagators with either bare or partially dressed (Ball-Chiu 1BC) quark-photon vertices.

## Key numbers
- Pseudoscalar meson exchange total: a_mu^{LBL;PS} = (84.3 ± 12.8) × 10^{-11}, with individual contributions: pion = (57.5 ± 6.9) × 10^{-11}, eta = (15.8 ± 3.5) × 10^{-11}, eta' = (11.0 ± 2.4) × 10^{-11}
- Quark-loop with bare vertex: a_mu^{LBL;quarkloop} = (61 ± 2) × 10^{-11}
- Quark-loop with 1BC vertex dressing: a_mu^{LBL;quarkloop} = (107 ± 2) × 10^{-11}
- Total hadronic LBL contribution (1BC): a_mu^{LBL} = (191 ± 35 ± 46) × 10^{-11}
- Revised theoretical value: a_mu^{theor.} = 116,591,865.0(96.6) × 10^{-11}, reducing the theory-experiment discrepancy to about 1.9 sigma

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering that used effective models such as the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Bijnens 1995), hidden local symmetry (Hayakawa 1995-1997), vector meson dominance (Knecht 2001, Melnikov 2003, Nyffeler 2009-2010), and non-local chiral quark models (Dorokhov 2004-2008). The Dyson-Schwinger approach provides a systematically improvable framework that avoids ambiguities in off-shell meson prescriptions common to earlier methods. The results enable future calculations that include the full quark-photon vertex and additional non-rainbow-ladder diagrams to refine the theoretical prediction.
