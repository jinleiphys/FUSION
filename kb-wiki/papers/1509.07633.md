# Extension and parameterization of high-order density dependence in Skyrme forces

> The three-body force is indispensable in nuclear energy density functionals which leads to a density dependent two-body term in the Hartree-Fock approach. Usually a single factional power of density dependency has been adopted. We consider the possibility of an additional higher-order density dependence in extended Skyrme forces. As a result, new extended Skyrme parametertizations based on the SLy4 force are obtained and the improvements in descriptions of global nuclei have been demonstrated. The higher-order term can also substantially affect nuclear properties in the high density region in general ways.

## Key claim

The authors propose that adding a higher-order density-dependent term (proportional to \(\rho^{\gamma+1/3}\)) to the standard Skyrme energy density functional, on top of the usual single fractional-power term, can improve descriptions of global nuclear properties and substantially affect nuclear matter behavior at supra-saturation densities, but only when the momentum-independent parameters are refitted while keeping other SLy4 parameters fixed.

## Method

The authors extended the standard Skyrme interaction by adding a term \(\frac{1}{6}t_{3E}(1+x_{3E}P_{\sigma})\rho(\mathbf{R})^{\gamma+1/3}\delta(\mathbf{r}_i-\mathbf{r}_j)\) to the density-dependent two-body interaction. They refitted only the momentum-independent parameters (\(t_0, t_3, t_{3E}, x_0, x_3, x_{3E}\)) using the Simulated Annealing Method, keeping all other SLy4 parameters (\(t_1, t_2, x_1, x_2, W_0\)) unchanged. The fitting minimized a \(\chi^2\) quantity that included the saturation energy, neutron matter energies from Wiringa et al., and experimental binding energies and charge radii of selected nuclei, using the axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock+BCS solver Skyax.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density was fixed at 0.1595 fm\(^{-3}\), the symmetry energy coefficient at 32 MeV, and the effective mass \(m^*/m\) at 0.7, unchanged from SLy4.
- The obtained \(t_{3E}\) parameter for the higher-order term ranged from 1334.88 to 2970.72 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3(1+1/2)}\), depending on the fitting group (light, heavy, or global nuclei).
- The incompressibility \(K_{\infty}\) increased from 229.9 MeV (SLy4) to values between 238.58 and 248.84 MeV for the extended parameterizations.
- The root-mean-square deviation in binding energies for 603 even-even nuclei was 2.30 MeV for the extended SLy4 (global), compared to 2.94 MeV for the refitted SLy4' and 4.37 MeV for the original SLy4.
- For neutron droplets in a \(\hbar\omega=10\) MeV trap, the extended SLy4 (light) gave energies of 124.03 MeV for \(N=8\) and 425.50 MeV for \(N=20\), compared to 120.81 MeV and 418.56 MeV for SLy4, and 135.4(2) MeV and 432.8(5) MeV from ab initio calculations.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme force framework, which traditionally uses a single fractional power of density dependence (e.g., \(\gamma=1/6\) in SLy4), by adding a higher-order term inspired by the energy density functional expansion for dilute Fermi gases (Huang-Yang, Lee-Yang, Martin and De Dominicis, 1957). It builds on earlier attempts to include higher-order density dependencies in nuclear matter (Cochet, 2004) and finite nuclei (Agrawal, 2006), but differs by using SLy4 as a base and refitting only momentum-independent terms. The results suggest that such extensions can improve descriptions of charge radii and binding energies, particularly for light nuclei, and provide a path toward better Skyrme functionals by eventually adjusting all parameters including the effective mass and momentum-dependent terms.
