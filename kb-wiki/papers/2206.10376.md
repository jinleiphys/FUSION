# Deuteron yields from LHC: Continuum correlations and in-medium effects

> To explain the production of light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions at extreme energies, we focus on the deuteron case. A Gibbs ensemble at chemical freeze-out is a prerequisite to investigate the non-equilibrium evolution of the expanding fireball. Quantum statistical approaches allow to describe correlations including bound state formation in the strongly interacting and hot system. We consider the virial approach to evaluate proton-neutron correlations. In generalization of the treatment of protons in pionic matter (pion-proton puzzle), the influence of the pion environment on deuteron-like correlations is evaluated using data for the pion-deuteron scattering phase shifts. Calculated yields for deuteron production are compared with the ones observed at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed deuteron yields from LHC heavy-ion collisions can be better explained by including continuum correlations and in-medium effects via a quantum statistical (virial) approach, rather than the simple hadron resonance gas model; the authors show that the HRG overestimates deuteron production by about 20%, and their virial correction reduces the predicted yield significantly, though the uncertainties in the data are still large enough to accommodate the deviation.

## Method

The authors apply a quantum statistical approach using the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula to compute the second virial coefficient for the proton-neutron isoscalar channel, which contains the deuteron bound state and continuum scattering states. They evaluate the intrinsic partition function for deuteron-like correlations using experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts, and separately treat the influence of the pion environment on nucleons using pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts (generalizing the approach previously used to solve the "proton puzzle"). The calculated yields are compared with ALICE data from central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: B_d = 2.225 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fr = 156 MeV
- Measured deuteron yield per rapidity unit: dN_d/dy = 0.098; antideuteron: dN_antid/dy = 0.092
- HRG model prediction for deuteron yield: dN_d^HRG/dy = 0.1171
- Virial approach prediction for deuteron yield: dN_d^virial/dy = 0.0529
- Virial coefficient at freeze-out: b_d^qu(T_fr) = 0.971
- Reduction factor from HRG to virial result: 0.451
- Pion density at freeze-out: about 0.34 fm^{-3}
- Baryon density at freeze-out: about 3 x 10^{-2} fm^{-3}
- Proton yield from HRG: dN_p^HRG/dy = 12.894 (primordial), increased to 35.668 after feed-down; virial approach gives final proton yield of about 30.4

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach previously applied to solve the "proton puzzle" (Andronic 2018) to the case of deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies. The authors build on the Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism (Beth 1937) and the virial expansion method (Ropke 1982) to go beyond the simple hadron resonance gas model (Braun-Munzinger 2003, Andronic 2017). The results enable improved modeling of light nucleus production in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that continuum correlations and in-medium effects must be included for accurate yield predictions, though further work is needed to address transverse momentum spectra and flow observables.
