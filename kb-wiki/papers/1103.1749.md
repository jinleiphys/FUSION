# K*Λ(1116) photoproduction and nucleon resonances

> In this presentation, we report our recent studies on the $K^*Λ(1116)$ photoproduction off the proton target, using the tree-level Born approximation, via the effective Lagrangian approach. In addition, we include the nine (three- or four-star confirmed) nucleon resonances below the threshold $\sqrt{s}_\mathrm{th}\approx2008$ MeV, to interpret the discrepancy between the experiment and previous theoretical studies, in the vicinity of the threshold region. From the numerical studies, we observe that the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ play an important role for the cross-section enhancement near the $\sqrt{s}_\mathrm{th}$. It also turns out that, in order to reproduce the data, we have the vector coupling constants $g_{K^*S_{11}(1535)Λ}=(7.0\sim9.0)$ and $g_{K^*S_{11}(1650)Λ}=(5.0\sim6.0)$.

## Key claim

The tree-level Born approximation plus nine confirmed nucleon resonances below threshold can explain the previously underestimated cross-section for γp → K*+Λ(1116) near √s_th ≈ 2008 MeV, but only the S11(1535) and S11(1650) resonances produce the required enhancement; other resonances are not effective.

## Method

The authors employed a tree-level Born approximation within an effective Lagrangian approach, including Born diagrams, t-channel K and κ(800) meson exchanges, u-channel Σ(1192) and Σ*(1385) hyperon exchanges, and nine s-channel nucleon resonances (P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535), S11(1650), D13(1700), P11(1710), P13(1720), D15(1675), F15(1680)) in a full relativistic manner. The strong vector coupling constants gK*N*Λ for the two dominant S11 resonances were varied to reproduce the experimental total cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- Threshold center-of-mass energy: √s_th ≈ 2008 MeV
- Strong vector coupling constant for S11(1535): gK*S11(1535)Λ = 7.0–9.0
- Strong vector coupling constant for S11(1650): gK*S11(1650)Λ = 5.0–6.0
- Coupling strength range for cross-section enhancement: gK*N*Λ ≈ 4.0–9.0

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Oh (2006) and Oh (2006) on K*Λ(1116) photoproduction, which used Born approximation with Regge contributions but underestimated the cross-section near threshold. By adding nine nucleon resonances, the authors resolve that discrepancy and identify the dominant roles of S11(1535) and S11(1650). The results enable future studies of higher-mass strange meson-baryon photoproduction at facilities such as LEPS2 at SPring-8 and CLAS12 at Jefferson Laboratory, and the authors note that resonances beyond threshold, particularly D13(2080), may also contribute.
