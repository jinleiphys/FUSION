# QCD Reggeon Calculus From KLWMIJ/JIMWLK Evolution: Vertices, Reggeization and All

> We show explicitly how the high energy QCD evolution generated by the KLWMIJ Hamiltonian can be cast in the form of the QCD Reggeon Field Theory. We show how to reduce the KLWMIJ Hamitonian to physical color singlet degrees of freedom. We suggest a natural way of defining the Pomeron and other Reggeons in the framework of the KLWMIJ evolution and derive the QCD Reggeon Field Theory Hamiltonian which includes several lowest Reggeon operators. This Hamiltonian generates evolution equations for all Reggeons in the case of dilute-dense scattering, including the nonlinear Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for the Pomeron. We also find explicit expressions for the Reggeon conjugate operators in terms of QCD operators, and derive their evolution equations. This provides a natural and unambiguous framework for reggeization procedure introduced in \cite{BW, BE}. The Bartels triple Pomeron vertex is inherited directly from the RFT Hamiltonian. For simplicity in the bulk of the paper we work in the large $N_c$ limit.

## Key claim

The KLWMIJ Hamiltonian, which governs high-energy QCD evolution in the Color Glass Condensate framework, can be explicitly rewritten as the Hamiltonian of QCD Reggeon Field Theory (RFT) when projected onto color-singlet degrees of freedom, but this rewriting is performed in the large \(N_c\) limit and is restricted to a finite set of the lowest Reggeon operators (Pomeron, Odderon, and two four-point Reggeons \(B\) and \(C\)), with the truncation not being fully self-consistent because the evolution of the \(B\) and \(C\) operators involves additional operators \(D^\pm\) that are not included in the truncated set.

## Method

The authors start from the KLWMIJ Hamiltonian acting on functionals of unitary matrices \(R(x)\) and color charge densities \(\rho(x)\), and project it onto the subspace of \(SU_L(N_c) \times SU_R(N_c)\) invariant operators. They define composite color-singlet Reggeon fields (Pomeron \(P\), Odderon \(O\), and four-point Reggeons \(B\) and \(C\)) as specific combinations of traces of \(R\) matrices, chosen to interpolate the lightest BKP states and to transform irreducibly under the discrete symmetries. The action of the KLWMIJ Hamiltonian on these Reggeon operators is computed using known evolution equations for the dipole and quadrupole operators, and the resulting expressions are recast as a Hamiltonian \(H_{RFT}\) in terms of the Reggeon fields and their canonical conjugates \(P^\dagger, O^\dagger, B^\dagger, C^\dagger\).

## Key numbers

* The dipole kernel used throughout is \(M_{xy;z} = (x-y)^2 / [(x-z)^2 (y-z)^2]\).
* The linear part of the Pomeron evolution equation (the BFKL equation) is given by \(\frac{d}{dY} P_{x,y} = \frac{\bar \alpha_s}{2\pi} \int_z M_{x,y;z} [P_{x,z} + P_{z,y} - P_{x,y}]\) plus nonlinear terms involving \(P_{x,z} P_{z,y}\) and \(O_{x,z} O_{z,y}\).
* The quadrupole evolution equation contains the kernel \(L_{x,y,u,v;z} = \frac{1}{2}[M_{y,u;z} + M_{x,v;z} - M_{y,v;z} - M_{x,u;z}]\).
* The Hamiltonian \(H_{RFT}\) is given by \(H_{RFT} = H_P + H_O + H_B + H_C\), where each term is proportional to \(\bar \alpha_s / (2\pi)\).
* The evolution equation for the conjugate Pomeron field \(P^\dagger_{12}\) contains terms proportional to \(\bar \alpha_s / (2\pi)\) with kernels \(M_{1,z;2}, M_{2,z;1}, M_{1,2;z}\) and various \(L\)-kernels.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by the authors in earlier papers (reggeon, yinyang, reggegluon) where the JIMWLK/KLWMIJ Hamiltonian was interpreted as a Reggeon Field Theory, but here the connection is made directly at the Hamiltonian level rather than through eigenfunctions. It provides a rigorous derivation of the Bartels triple Pomeron vertex and the reggeization procedure introduced by Bartels and collaborators (BW, BE), removing the arbitrariness of those earlier constructions. The results enable a systematic derivation of evolution equations for higher Reggeon operators and their conjugates, and the authors note that subleading \(1/N_c\) terms (not treated in detail) would introduce merging vertices absent in the leading-order fan-diagram structure.
