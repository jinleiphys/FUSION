# Energetic Light Fragment Production Capability in MCNP6

> The goal of this research is to enable MCNP6 to produce high-energy light fragments. These energetic light fragments may be emitted by our models through three processes: Fermi breakup, preequilibrium, and coalescence. We explore the emission of light fragments through each of these mechanisms and demonstrate an improved agreement with experimental data achieved by extending precompound models to include emission of fragments heavier than $^4$He.

# Energetic Light Fragment Production Capability in MCNP6

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that extending the precompound models in MCNP6 to include emission of fragments heavier than $^4$He, combined with upgrades to the coalescence model, inverse cross sections, and condensation probability, produces improved agreement with experimental data for light fragment spectra; however, the improvements are demonstrated for specific tested reactions and the authors note that fine-tuning of the Fermi breakup cutoff parameter might provide better description of some experimental data, while in some cases the existing $A_{Fermi}=12$ value provided the best agreement.

## Method

The authors implemented four upgrades into the CEM03.03 event generator within MCNP6: extension of the preequilibrium modified exciton model to emit 66 fragment types up to $^{28}$Mg; replacement of the Dostrovsky inverse cross section model with the NASA (Tripathi) total-reaction-cross-section model; development of a new energy-dependent condensation probability model $\gamma_j$ with fitted empirical coefficients $F_j$; and extension of the coalescence model to produce clusters up to $^7$Be in CEM and up to $^{12}$C in LAQGSM. The upgraded code, called CEM03.03F and MCNP6-F, allows users to toggle the preequilibrium and coalescence extensions via the `npreqtyp` variable on the LCA card.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi breakup model in CEM and LAQGSM uses a cutoff of $A_{Fermi} = 12$ for residual nuclei, while GEANT4 and SHIELD-HIT use $A_{Fermi} = 16$.
- The preequilibrium model was extended to emit 66 fragment types up to $^{28}$Mg, compared to the previous 6 particle types (n, p, d, t, $^3$He, $^4$He).
- Coalescence momentum parameters for the extended model at energies below 300 MeV: $p_c(d) = 90$ MeV/c, $p_c(t) = p_c(^3\text{He}) = 108$ MeV/c, $p_c(^4\text{He}) = 130$ MeV/c, $p_c(LF) = 175$ MeV/c.
- Coalescence momentum parameters for the extended model at energies 300 MeV $< T < 1000$ MeV: $p_c(d) = 150$ MeV/c, $p_c(t) = p_c(^3\text{He}) = 175$ MeV/c, $p_c(^4\text{He}) = 205$ MeV/c, $p_c(LF) = 250$ MeV/c.
- Results are shown for 480 MeV p + $^{nat}$Ag $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li at 60 degrees and 200 MeV p + $^{197}$Au $\rightarrow$ $^7$Be at 45 degrees, comparing MCNP6-F to experimental data from Green et al. (1984) and Machner et al. (1980s).

## Context

This work extends the CEM and LAQGSM event generators within MCNP6, which previously described spectra of fragments up to $^4$He well but did not predict high-energy tails of heavier light fragments. The upgrades build on the modified exciton model of Gudima (1983) and address limitations identified in the 03.03 versions of CEM and LAQGSM. The resulting MCNP6-F code enables improved simulations for applications including electronics performance in space, human radiation dosages in space and medical physics, and accelerator shielding design, and the authors anticipate these upgrades will be included in the next official release of MCNP6.
