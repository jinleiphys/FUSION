# Recent developments in antikaon-nucleon dynamics

> Stimulated by various experimental achievements, the study of Kbar N dynamics now enters a new phase. The two-body Kbar N interaction is largely constrained by recent experimental data, and the nature of the Lambda(1405) resonance is being unveiled by several theoretical analyses. These findings provide a basic tool for applications to Kbar-nuclear systems. We summarize the current status of the Kbar N phenomenology and outline the future direction in this field.

## Key claim

The antikaon-nucleon (Kbar N) two-body interaction is now largely constrained by recent experimental data, particularly the SIDDHARTA measurement of kaonic hydrogen, though the subthreshold extrapolation of the amplitude still carries uncertainties that depend on model choices such as the treatment of meson decay constants and the inclusion of next-to-leading order chiral corrections.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral coupled-channel approach for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=-1 sector, where the interaction kernel is derived from chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order and inserted into the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization to obtain the full scattering amplitude. The structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance is analyzed through multiple methods: natural renormalization scheme compositeness analysis, large-Nc scaling of pole trajectories, and finite-volume mass shift calculations of the Kbar N component. Several theoretical models (TW, TWB, NLO) are fitted to experimental data including total cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement.

## Context

This review extends the chiral coupled-channel approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), Lutz (2001), and Hyodo (2011) for describing Kbar N scattering and the Lambda(1405) resonance. The work is stimulated by new experimental results from SIDDHARTA (Bazzi 2011) on kaonic hydrogen, LEPS (Niiyama 2008), CLAS (Moriya 2009), and HADES (Agakishiev 2012) on piSigma spectra. The SIDDHARTA-constrained models enable more reliable applications to Kbar-nuclear systems, particularly the Kbar NN quasi-bound state studied by Shevchenko (2006, 2007), Ikeda (2007), Yamazaki (2007), Dote (2008), and others, where quantitative predictions of binding energy and decay width have not yet converged due to subthreshold uncertainties.
