# Thermonuclear 42Ti(p,gamma)43V rate in type I X-ray bursts

> The thermonuclear rate of the $^{42}$Ti($p$,$γ$)$^{43}$V reaction has been reevaluated based on a recent precise proton separation energy measurement of $S_p$($^{43}$V)=83$\pm$43 keV. The astrophysical impact of our new rates has been investigated through one-zone postprocessing type I x-ray burst calculations. It shows that the new experimental value of $S_p$ significantly affects the yields of species between A$\approx$40--45. As well, the precision of the recent experimental $S_p$ value constrains these yields to better than a factor of three.

## Key claim

The thermonuclear rate of the 42Ti(p,γ)43V reaction has been reevaluated using a recent precise proton separation energy measurement of Sp(43V)=83±43 keV, and the new experimental Sp value significantly affects the yields of species between A≈40–45, with the precision constraining these yields to better than a factor of three.

## Method

The resonant rate was calculated using the narrow resonance formalism with level energies, half-lives, and single-particle spectroscopic factors from the mirror nucleus 43Ca, combined with shell model calculations using the OXBASH code and the isobaric-multiplet-mass-equation (IMME). The direct capture (DC) rate was calculated with the RADCAP code using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and Coulomb potential, with a spectroscopic factor of C2S=0.75. The total reaction rate was obtained by summing the resonant and DC contributions, and the astrophysical impact was investigated through one-zone postprocessing type I X-ray burst calculations using the K04 thermodynamic history.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of 43V: Sp=83±43 keV, experimentally determined for the first time at the HIRFL-CSR facility
- Resonant strength for the 0.436 MeV state (5/2−): ωγ=1.5×10−8 eV, with lower limit 1.4×10−10 eV and upper limit 6.3×10−7 eV
- Resonant strength for the 0.537 MeV state (3/2−): ωγ=6.5×10−6 eV, with lower limit 2.2×10−6 eV and upper limit 1.1×10−5 eV
- Resonant strength for the 2.067 MeV state (7/2−): ωγ=5.4×10−2 eV, with lower limit 2.2×10−2 eV and upper limit 1.1×10−1 eV
- Total reaction rate at 1.0 GK: 5.32×10−3 cm3 mole−1 s−1
- Total reaction rate at 2.0 GK: 6.16×10−2 cm3 mole−1 s−1
- Total reaction rate at 3.0 GK: 9.45×10−1 cm3 mole−1 s−1
- DC contribution to total rate: 10–20% in the temperature region of 2–3 GK
- Mass fraction of 42Ti using IMP ΔSp: (2.3–5.3)×10−5
- Mass fraction of 44Cr using IMP ΔSp: (1.0–1.9)×10−5

## Context

This work extends earlier rate calculations by Wormer et al. (1994) and Herndl et al. (1995), which used theoretical Sp values from the atomic mass evaluations (AME85, AME95, AME03) with uncertainties of 200–230 keV, by incorporating the first experimental mass measurement of 43V from the HIRFL-CSR facility (Tu et al. 2011; Zhang et al. 2012; Yan et al. 2013). The new rate enables more precise predictions of yields for species with A=42–44 in type I X-ray burst models, and demonstrates that statistical model calculations (such as those in the JINA REACLIB database) are not ideally applicable for this reaction due to the low density of low-lying excited states in 43V.
