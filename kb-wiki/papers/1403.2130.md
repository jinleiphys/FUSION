# Thermonuclear 42Ti(p,gamma)43V rate in type I X-ray bursts

> The thermonuclear rate of the $^{42}$Ti($p$,$Œ≥$)$^{43}$V reaction has been reevaluated based on a recent precise proton separation energy measurement of $S_p$($^{43}$V)=83$\pm$43 keV. The astrophysical impact of our new rates has been investigated through one-zone postprocessing type I x-ray burst calculations. It shows that the new experimental value of $S_p$ significantly affects the yields of species between A$\approx$40--45. As well, the precision of the recent experimental $S_p$ value constrains these yields to better than a factor of three.

## Method

The resonant rate was calculated using the narrow resonance formalism with level energies, half-lives, and single-particle spectroscopic factors from the mirror nucleus 43Ca, combined with shell model calculations using the OXBASH code and the isobaric-multiplet-mass-equation (IMME). The direct capture (DC) rate was calculated with the RADCAP code using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and Coulomb potential, with a spectroscopic factor of C2S=0.75. The total reaction rate was obtained by summing the resonant and DC contributions, and the astrophysical impact was investigated through one-zone postprocessing type I X-ray burst calculations using the K04 thermodynamic history.

## Context

This work extends earlier rate calculations by Wormer et al. (1994) and Herndl et al. (1995), which used theoretical Sp values from the atomic mass evaluations (AME85, AME95, AME03) with uncertainties of 200‚Äì230 keV, by incorporating the first experimental mass measurement of 43V from the HIRFL-CSR facility (Tu et al. 2011; Zhang et al. 2012; Yan et al. 2013). The new rate enables more precise predictions of yields for species with A=42‚Äì44 in type I X-ray burst models, and demonstrates that statistical model calculations (such as those in the JINA REACLIB database) are not ideally applicable for this reaction due to the low density of low-lying excited states in 43V.
