# Heavy quark correlations and the effective volume for quarkonia production

> Using the Boltzmann transport approach, we study the effective volume of a correlated heavy quark pair in a partonic medium based on their collision rate. We find that the effective volume is finite and depends sensitively on the momentum of the heavy quark and the temperature of the medium. Generally, it increases linearly with time $t$ at the very beginning and the increase then becomes slower due to multiple scattering, and finally it increases linearly with respect to $t^{3/2}$. We further find that the colliding heavy quark pair has an effective temperature similar to that of the medium even though their initial transverse momentum spectra are far from thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The effective volume of a correlated heavy quark pair in a partonic medium is finite even in an open system, and its time evolution follows three distinct regimes: linear in time at very early times, slower growth at intermediate times due to multiple scattering, and linear in \(t^{3/2}\) at late times. The colliding heavy quark pair acquires an effective temperature close to that of the medium even when their initial transverse momentum spectra are far from thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for the phase-space distribution of a heavy quark pair initially located at the same position with back-to-back momenta, as produced in high-energy heavy ion collisions. The heavy quarks scatter with thermal gluons in the medium using a constant isotropic cross section of 4 mb, and the heavy quark-antiquark collisions are treated by comparing their impact parameter with the scattering cross section. The effective volume is defined from the collision rate of the heavy quark pair relative to the thermal expectation, and its time evolution is extracted by averaging over many independent trajectories of heavy quark pairs.

## Key numbers

* Heavy quark mass used in calculations: \(m = 1.25\) GeV/\(c^2\)
* Heavy quark-gluon scattering cross section: \(\sigma_{gQ} = 4\) mb
* Mean free time for heavy quarks in a medium of temperature \(T = 0.3\) GeV: \(\tau = 0.44\) fm/\(c\)
* For a periodic cubic box of side \(L = 10\) fm, the effective volume approaches the box volume on a time scale of about 30 fm/\(c\)
* For temperature \(T = 0.2\) GeV, the effective volume exceeds 1000 fm\(^3\) at \(t = 0.5\) fm/\(c\); for \(T = 0.4\) GeV, it is less than 10 fm\(^3\) at \(t = 2\) fm/\(c\)
* The effective temperature of the \(\sqrt{s}\) distribution of colliding heavy quark pairs evolves from 0.22 GeV at \(t = 0.5\) fm/\(c\) to 0.29 GeV at \(t = 5\) fm/\(c\) (medium temperature \(T = 0.3\) GeV)
* The effective temperature of the total momentum distribution of colliding charm pairs evolves from 0.36 GeV at \(t = 0.5\) fm/\(c\) to 0.30 GeV at \(t = 5.0\) fm/\(c\)

## Context

This work extends the study of canonical suppression and correlated production of heavy quarks, previously addressed for strangeness by Hagedorn (1984) and for charm by Gorenstein (2000) and Andronic (2006). It provides a dynamical, Boltzmann-equation-based treatment of the effective volume for heavy quark-antiquark collisions, going beyond the simple fireball volume assumption used in earlier quarkonium regeneration models such as Grandchamp (2003). The results enable more realistic calculations of quarkonium regeneration rates in heavy ion collisions where heavy quarks are rare, and highlight that the chance for a heavy quark pair to collide again depends sensitively on their initial momentum and the medium temperature.
