# New insights into the spin structure of the nucleon

> We analyze the low-energy spin structure of the nucleon in a covariant effective field theory with explicit spin-3/2 degrees of freedom to third order in the small scale expansion. Using the available data on the strong and electromagnetic width of the Delta-resonance, we give parameter-free predictions for various spin-polarizabilities and moments of spin structure functions. We find an improved description of the nucleon spin structure at finite photon virtualities for some observables and point out the necessity of a fourth order calculation.

## Key claim

The covariant small-scale expansion (SSE) to third order, with explicit spin-3/2 Delta degrees of freedom and all low-energy constants fixed from the strong and electromagnetic widths of the Delta resonance, yields parameter-free predictions for nucleon spin polarizabilities and moments of spin structure functions; however, the description of the Q^2-dependence of some observables remains incomplete, pointing to the necessity of a fourth-order calculation.

## Method

The authors compute double virtual Compton scattering (V^2CS) amplitudes in a covariant baryon chiral effective field theory that includes explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, using the small-scale expansion (SSE) to order epsilon^3. They evaluate tree-level diagrams with Delta intermediate states and one-loop diagrams involving pions, nucleons, and Deltas, fixing the couplings h_A and b_1 from the measured strong width (118 ± 2 MeV) and electromagnetic branching ratio (0.55–0.65%) of the Delta resonance. The resulting predictions for spin polarizabilities and moments are parameter-free at this order.

## Key numbers

- Forward spin polarizability of the proton at the photon point: gamma_0^p = -1.74 [±0.40] × 10^{-4} fm^4 (central value; experimental value from the GDH collaboration is -1.00 ± 0.08 ± 0.12 × 10^{-4} fm^4)
- Forward spin polarizability of the neutron at the photon point: gamma_0^n = -0.77 [±0.40] × 10^{-4} fm^4
- Longitudinal-transverse spin polarizability of the proton at the photon point: delta_0^p = 2.40 [±0.01] × 10^{-4} fm^4
- Longitudinal-transverse spin polarizability of the neutron at the photon point: delta_0^n = 2.38 [±0.03] × 10^{-4} fm^4
- At Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2: gamma_0^p = -3.25 [±0.48] × 10^{-4} fm^4; gamma_0^n = -2.95 [±0.48] × 10^{-4} fm^4; delta_0^p = 1.29 [±0.03] × 10^{-4} fm^4; delta_0^n = 0.75 [±0.05] × 10^{-4} fm^4
- Delta couplings extracted from widths: h_A = 1.43 ± 0.02; b_1 = -(4.98 ± 0.27)/m_N

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon spin structure, notably the covariant calculation at O(q^4) by Bernard et al. (2002) which added Delta contributions phenomenologically, and the heavy-baryon SSE calculation by Kao et al. (2002). The covariant approach avoids the deformations of analytic structure at higher virtualities introduced by infrared regularization and recovers the heavy-baryon results in the static limit. The parameter-free predictions at O(epsilon^3) provide a baseline for upcoming Jefferson Lab data at low photon virtualities (down to Q^2 ≈ 0.01 GeV^2), but the discrepancies in Q^2-dependence for gamma_0^p and I_A(Q^2) motivate a complete fourth-order calculation to test chiral QCD dynamics.
