# Exclusive production of pi+ pi- pairs in proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions

> We report on a detailed investigation of four-body $p p \to p p π^+ π^-$ and $p \bar{p} \to p \bar{p} π^+ π^-$) reactions which constitute an irreducible background to three-body processes $p p \to p p M$, where $M$ is a broad resonance in the $π^+ π^-$ channel, e.g. $M=σ, ρ^{0}, f_{0}(980), f_{2}(1275), f_{0}(1500)$. We include double-diffractive contribution (both pomeron and reggeon exchanges) as well as the pion-pion rescattering contributions. The first process dominates at higher energies and small pion-pion invariant masses while the second becomes important at lower energies and higher pion-pion invariant masses. We compare our results with the experimental data. We make predictions for future experiments at PANDA, RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies. The two-dimensional distribution in rapidity space of pions $(y_{π^+}, y_{π^-})$ is particularly interesting. The higher the incident energy, the higher preference for the same-hemisphere emission of pions. The processes considered constitute a sizeable contribution to the total nucleon-nucleon cross section as well as to pion inclusive cross section.

## Key claim

The paper provides a detailed calculation of the irreducible four-body background \(pp \to pp \pi^+ \pi^-\) and \(p\bar{p} \to p\bar{p} \pi^+ \pi^-\) to three-body central resonance production, showing that the double-diffractive mechanism (pomeron and reggeon exchanges) dominates at higher energies and low pion-pion invariant masses, while the pion-pion rescattering mechanism becomes important at lower energies and higher pion-pion invariant masses.

## Method

The authors calculated the amplitudes for exclusive \(\pi^+ \pi^-\) pair production using two distinct mechanisms: a central double-diffractive mechanism with pomeron and reggeon exchanges, and a pion-pion rescattering mechanism. The energy dependence of the \(\pi N\) subsystems in the double-diffractive case was parametrized in Regge form using strength parameters and trajectories from the Donnachie-Landshoff analysis of total \(\pi N\) cross sections, with slope parameters adjusted to elastic \(\pi N\) scattering data. The pion-pion amplitude was parametrized using a recent phase shift analysis at low energies and a Regge form of the continuum obtained via Regge factorization.

## Key numbers

- The integrated cross section for \(pp \to pp\pi^+\pi^-\) is more than 1 mb for center-of-mass energies \(2.5 < \sqrt{s} < 10\) GeV.
- The double-diffractive component grows slowly with incident energy, while the pion-pion rescattering contributions are important at lower energies.
- The \(\pi N\) elastic scattering data are well described for \(\sqrt{s} > 2.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of exclusive central production of resonances such as \(\sigma\), \(\rho^0\), \(f_0(980)\), \(f_2(1275)\), and \(f_0(1500)\) by quantifying the irreducible \(\pi^+ \pi^-\) continuum background, which was previously discussed for the \(f_0(1500)\) meson by Szczurek and Lebiedowicz (2009). The calculations enable predictions for future experiments at PANDA, RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies, and show that the two-dimensional rapidity distribution of pions provides a clear way to separate the different production mechanisms experimentally.
