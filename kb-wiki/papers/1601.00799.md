# Charmed meson and charmonium production in PbPb collisions at the LHC

> The phenomenological analysis of various characteristics of $J/ψ$ and $D$ meson production in PbPb collisions at the center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair is presented. The data on momentum spectra and elliptic flow are reproduced by two-component model HYDJET++ including thermal and non-thermal charm production mechanisms. The significant part of $D$-mesons is found to be in a kinetic equilibrium with the created medium, while $J/ψ$-mesons are characterized by earlier (as compared to light hadrons) freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the two-component HYDJET++ model can simultaneously reproduce the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of both D mesons and J/ψ mesons in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but only if D mesons are assumed to freeze out together with light hadrons while J/ψ mesons freeze out earlier (at the chemical freeze-out stage). The abstract's statement that D mesons are "in kinetic equilibrium" is qualified by the finding that this holds only up to transverse momenta of about 4 GeV/c, and that the situation at LHC energies differs from that at RHIC.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ event generator, which combines a soft "thermal" component (parametrized relativistic hydrodynamics with statistical hadronization including charm fugacity) and a hard "non-thermal" component (PYQUEN jet quenching model with radiative and collisional energy loss, based on PYTHIA 6.4 initial parton spectra). They tuned the model's freeze-out parameters separately for D mesons and J/ψ mesons to reproduce ALICE data on pT spectra and elliptic flow v2 in PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, using the same charm fugacity γc = 11.5 fixed from J/ψ yields.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 165 MeV and thermal freeze-out temperature Tth = 105 MeV for inclusive hadrons (and for D mesons at LHC).
- For J/ψ early freeze-out: Tch = Tth = 165 MeV, maximal longitudinal flow rapidity YLmax = 2.3, maximal transverse flow rapidity YTmax = 0.6, minimum transverse momentum transfer pTmin = 3.0 GeV/c.
- Charm fugacity γc = 11.5 fixed from absolute J/ψ yields.
- Initial maximal temperature of quark-gluon fluid T0max = 1 GeV for central PbPb collisions.
- D meson kinetic equilibrium holds up to pT ∼ 4 GeV/c; J/ψ thermalization (without kinetic equilibrium) holds up to pT ∼ 3 GeV/c.
- The model reproduces D-meson nuclear modification factor RAA up to pT = 16 GeV and its centrality dependence for 6 < pT < 12 GeV/c across four centrality classes (0–10%, 10–20%, 20–40%, 40–60%).

## Context

This work extends the HYDJET++ model, previously applied to inclusive charged hadrons, pions, kaons, and protons at LHC (Lokhtin 2012), to open and hidden charm production. It contrasts with the RHIC situation (Lokhtin 2010) where both D and J/ψ mesons required early freeze-out, and addresses the challenge noted by other theoretical approaches (e.g., Andronic 2011, He 2012, Cao 2014) of simultaneously describing D-meson pT spectra and elliptic flow. The results enable further study of heavy-quark transport properties in the quark-gluon plasma and suggest that D-meson interaction cross sections at LHC energies become comparable to those of light hadrons, unlike J/ψ mesons.
