# Relativistic and spin effects in elastic backward p-d scattering

> The elastic backward proton-deuteron scattering is analyzed including relativistic effects in the deuteron and the mechanism of this reaction which includes the graphs corresponding to the emission, rescattering and absorption of the virtual pion by a deuteron nucleon in addition to the one-nucleon exchange graph. It allows one to obtain a rather satisfactory description of all the experimental data on the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power of the deuteron and transfer polarization in this reaction.

## Key claim

The inclusion of triangle Feynman graphs corresponding to virtual pion emission, rescattering, and absorption, in addition to the one-nucleon exchange graph, allows a satisfactory description of the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power, and transfer polarization in elastic backward p-d scattering, but only when the relativistic one-nucleon exchange (RONE) graph is combined with the light-cone dynamics (LCD) calculation of the triangle graphs; the LCD impulse approximation alone fails to describe the tensor analyzing power at deuteron momenta above 1.2 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors analyzed elastic backward p-d scattering using the Weinberg diagrammatic technique in the infinite-momentum frame (light-cone dynamics). They calculated the total amplitude as the sum of the one-nucleon exchange graph (either the relativistic RONE Feynman graph or the LCD impulse approximation) and the triangle graphs for virtual pion emission, π-N elastic scattering, and pion absorption. The deuteron wave function was related to the nonrelativistic Reid soft-core and Argonne v18 wave functions, and the π-N amplitude was taken from phase-shift analysis.

## Key numbers

- The impulse approximations (ONE and LCD) fail to describe the differential cross section at laboratory deuteron momenta \(p_d^{l.s.} > 1.5\) GeV/c.
- The total calculation within both RC and LCD gives approximately the same differential cross section results, which are very close to experimental data.
- The impulse approximations do not describe \(T_{20}\) and \(\kappa_0\) at \(p_d^{l.s.} > 1\) GeV/c.
- The total RC calculation describes \(T_{20}\) better than the LCD calculation, especially at \(p_d^{l.s.} > 5\) GeV/c.
- The total RC and LCD calculations give the same results for \(\kappa_0\) at \(p_d^{l.s.} \leq 4\) GeV/c, with differences appearing only above 4 GeV/c where no data exist.
- The inclusion of triangle graphs allows a satisfactory description of data up to initial deuteron momenta of 7 GeV/c.
- The mean value of the nucleon relative momentum in the deuteron used to remove the integral over the π-N amplitude was \(\bar{|{\vec k}|} \simeq 0.07-0.1\) GeV/c.
- The pion form factor cutoff parameter was taken as \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.7-0.8\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of elastic backward p-d scattering that used the one-nucleon exchange model (ONE) or its relativistic version (RONE), which failed to describe polarization observables at energies above \(T_p > 0.6\) GeV (Ierusalimov and Lykasov, 2001). It also builds on earlier calculations by Nakamura (1985) that included the triangle Feynman graph but could not describe \(T_{20}\) at deuteron momenta above 4 GeV/c. The present approach enables a unified description of both cross section and polarization data up to 7 GeV/c, and suggests that at higher momenta, six-quark admixtures in the deuteron wave function may become important.
