# Determining the $CP$ Property of $ht\bar{t}$ Coupling via a Novel Jet Substructure Observable

> Determining the $CP$ property of the Higgs boson is important for a precision test of the Standard Model as well as for the search for new physics. We propose a novel jet substructure observable based on the azimuthal anisotropy in a linearly polarized gluon jet that is produced in association with a Higgs boson at hadron colliders, and demonstrate that it provides a new $CP$-odd observable for determining the $CP$ property of the Higgs-top interaction. We introduce a factorization formalism to define a polarized gluon jet function with the insertion of an infrared-safe azimuthal observable to capture the linear polarization.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a novel jet substructure observable based on the azimuthal anisotropy of a linearly polarized gluon jet produced in association with a Higgs boson can serve as a new CP-odd observable for determining the CP phase of the Higgs-top coupling, but only in kinematic regions where the CP-odd polarization parameter ξ₂ is appreciable (transition and high-pT regions), while the low-pT region is dominated by the CP-even ξ₁ and is insensitive to the CP phase.

## Method

The authors compute the helicity amplitudes for gg → hg production at leading order via a top quark loop, parametrizing the CP-even and CP-odd contributions with coupling parameters κ and κ̃ (or equivalently κ_t and α). They define a factorization formalism with a polarized gluon jet function that includes an infrared-safe azimuthal observable O(φ, X) = (1/Σ p_{i,T}) Σ p_{i,T} δ(φ - φ_i), which projects out the cos2φ and sin2φ anisotropy structures. The gluon polarization is extracted from the density matrix elements ξ₁ and ξ₂, which are functions of the partonic kinematic variables p_T and y_g and the CP phase α.

## Key numbers

- The CP phase α is currently bounded experimentally to |α| ≤ 35°, 48°, and 63° at 68% C.L. by CMS (2020), CMS (2022), and ATLAS (2023) respectively, and |α| ≤ 43° at 95% C.L. by ATLAS (2020)
- In the transition region (100 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 300 GeV) at the FCC-hh (100 TeV, 20 ab⁻¹), assuming the SM scenario with ξ₂ = 0, the projected constraint on the CP phase is |α| ≤ 8.6°
- The g → b\bar{b} branching fraction in the selected kinematic region is f_{g_{b\bar b}} = 0.013, and g → c\bar{c} is f_{g_{c\bar c}} = 0.019, from Pythia 8.307 simulation
- The estimated number of reconstructed events (with Higgs decaying to diphotons, b/c tagging, and kinematic cuts) is (51 cos²α + 115 sin²α) at the HL-LHC (14 TeV, 3 ab⁻¹) and (8100 cos²α + 18200 sin²α) at the FCC-hh (100 TeV, 20 ab⁻¹)
- The cross section in the transition region (p_T ∈ [100, 300] GeV, |y_g| ≤ 0.8) is κ_t² (0.57 cos²α + 1.3 sin²α) pb for the HL-LHC and κ_t² (13.7 cos²α + 30.7 sin²α) pb for the FCC-hh

## Context

This work extends prior proposals for determining the CP phase of the Higgs-top coupling, which have used either direct associated Higgs and top production or indirect loop effects in Higgs or vector boson production. Unlike previous CP-odd observables that rely on charged particle-antiparticle states (Han, 2009), this observable is constructed purely from kinematic information in a gluon jet. The paper also builds on early theoretical work on linearly polarized gluons in hard scattering (Brodsky, 1978; DeGrand, 1980; Hara, 1988) and modern jet substructure factorization (Berger, 2003; Almeida, 2008). The approach enables a new probe of CP violation that depends only on the htt coupling, unlike associated Higgs-top production or hV/VV production which also depend on hVV couplings.
