# Inelastic quarkonium photoproduction in coherent hadron - hadron interactions at LHC energies

> In this paper we study the inelastic quarkonium photoproduction in coherent $pp/pPb/PbPb$ interactions. Considering the ultra relativistic hadrons as a source of photons, we estimate the total $ h_1 + h_2 \rightarrow h \otimes V + X$ ($V = J/Ψ$ and $Υ$) cross sections and rapidity distributions at LHC energies. Our results demonstrate that the experimental analysis of this process can be used to understand the underlying mechanism governing heavy quarkonium production.

## Key claim

The inelastic quarkonium photoproduction cross section in coherent hadron-hadron collisions is non-negligible compared to the exclusive channel (smaller by a factor of about 3 or more), but the experimental separation of these channels remains an open question due to large event pileup at LHC, and the Color Singlet Model at leading order with parameters constrained by HERA data provides a reasonable first approximation for total cross sections and rapidity distributions.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for inelastic J/Psi and Upsilon photoproduction in coherent pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions by factorizing the process into an equivalent photon flux from one hadron and the photon-hadron inelastic production cross section. The photoproduction cross section is calculated using the Color Singlet Model at leading order, with the partonic differential cross section for gamma + g -> V + g integrated over the quarkonium energy fraction z (0.3 to 0.9) and transverse momentum, using MRSTLO and CTEQ6L parton distributions for protons and EPS09 parametrization for nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for inelastic J/Psi photoproduction in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: 17.93 nb (MRSTLO) and 13.18 nb (CTEQ6L)
- Total cross section for inelastic J/Psi photoproduction in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 25.66 nb (MRSTLO) and 18.40 nb (CTEQ6L)
- Total cross section for inelastic Upsilon photoproduction in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV: 0.30 nb (MRSTLO) and 0.21 nb (CTEQ6L)
- Total cross section for inelastic Upsilon photoproduction in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV: 0.47 nb (MRSTLO) and 0.33 nb (CTEQ6L)
- Total cross section for inelastic J/Psi photoproduction in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: 6122.0 microbarns (CTEQ, no shadowing) and 4647.6 microbarns (CTEQ + EPS09 shadowing)
- Total cross section for inelastic Upsilon photoproduction in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: 71.9 microbarns (CTEQ) and 60.6 microbarns (CTEQ + EPS09)
- Total cross section for inelastic J/Psi photoproduction in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: 16.1 microbarns (CTEQ) and 15.7 microbarns (CTEQ + EPS09)
- Total cross section for inelastic Upsilon photoproduction in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: 0.19 microbarns (CTEQ) and 0.18 microbarns (CTEQ + EPS09)
- MRSTLO and CTEQ6L predictions for pp rapidity distributions differ by approximately 30 percent at Y = 0
- Predictions increase by approximately 23 percent at midrapidity if z_min is lowered from 0.3 to 0.0
- Inclusive channel is smaller than exclusive production by a factor of about 4 for J/Psi and about 3 for Upsilon

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent hadron-hadron interactions at LHC energies, which has been motivated by experimental results from CDF at Tevatron, STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, and ALICE and LHCb at LHC. The authors compute for the first time the inelastic quarkonium photoproduction channel in coherent pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions, complementing previous work on exclusive vector meson production in coherent interactions (Goncalves and Machado, 2010; Klein and Nystrand, 1999; Strikman and collaborators; Schafer and collaborators). The results enable future experimental studies to separate exclusive and inclusive photoproduction channels, potentially using forward detectors for tagging intact hadrons, which could help resolve the ongoing debate about the underlying mechanism of heavy quarkonium production.
