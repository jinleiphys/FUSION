# Landau and Eckart frames for relativistic fluids in nuclear collisions

> The quark matter created in relativistic nuclear collisions is interpreted as a nearly-perfect fluid. The recent efforts to explore its finite-density properties in the beam energy scan programs motivate one to revisit the issue of the local rest frame fixing in off-equilibrium hydrodynamics. I first investigate full second-order relativistic hydrodynamics in the Landau and the Eckart frames. Then numerical hydrodynamic simulations are performed to elucidate the effect of frame choice on flow observables in relativistic nuclear collisions. The results indicate that the flow can differ in the Landau and the Eckart frames but charged particle and net baryon rapidity distributions are mostly frame independent when off-equilibrium kinetic freeze-out is considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while hydrodynamic flow can differ between the Landau and Eckart frames in relativistic nuclear collisions, the charged particle and net baryon rapidity distributions become mostly frame independent when off-equilibrium kinetic freeze-out corrections are properly included, contrary to what might be expected from the frame dependence of the flow itself.

## Method

The author derives full second-order relativistic hydrodynamic equations in both the Landau (energy) frame and the Eckart (conserved charge) frame, including baryon diffusion and energy dissipation currents. The transport coefficients of the two frames are related through matching of entropy production. A non-boost invariant (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model is then solved numerically for Pb+Pb collisions at 17.3 GeV, using an equation of state based on lattice QCD and hadron resonance gas, with the Cooper-Frye formula including off-equilibrium corrections for particle spectra at freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial thermalization time: τ_th = 3 fm/c
- Freeze-out energy density: e_f = 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Model conductivity parameters: c_W = 10 and τ̃_W = 2 in the Eckart frame, giving κ_W = c_W (e+P) and τ_W = τ̃_W κ_W / [(e+P)T]
- Initial energy density parameter: a_1 = 7.19 GeV/fm^3
- Initial net baryon density parameter: b_1 = 0.45 1/fm^3
- Evolution time for comparison: τ = 20 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the decades-long debate over the choice of local rest frame in relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, previously discussed by Landau (fluid dynamics textbook) and Eckart (1940), and more recently by Tsumura (2006-2012), Van (2007, 2011), and Osada (2011). Most numerical hydrodynamic analyses of nuclear collisions have neglected the frame choice because diffusion currents were omitted, but the beam energy scan programs at RHIC and future facilities (FAIR, NICA, SPS, J-PARC) motivate revisiting this issue for finite-density systems. The results enable more reliable hydrodynamic modeling of baryon-rich QCD matter by showing that frame-dependent effects can be controlled through proper treatment of off-equilibrium freeze-out corrections.
