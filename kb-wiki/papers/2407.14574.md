# Superfluid fraction in the rod phase of the inner crust of neutron stars

> The rod phase as it is expected in the bottom layers of neutron-star crusts is analyzed within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. In order to well describe the interplay between band structure and superfluidity, periodicity of the lattice is taken into account using Bloch boundary conditions. A relative flow between the rods and the surrounding neutron gas is introduced in a time-independent way. This induces a non-trivial phase of the complex order parameter, leading to a counterflow between neutrons inside and outside the rods. With the resulting current, we compute the actual neutron superfluid fraction. For the latter our results are significantly larger than previous ones obtained in normal band theory, indicating that the normal band theory overestimates the entrainment effect.

## Key claim
The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculation, which includes both band structure and the dynamics of the superfluid order parameter, yields a neutron superfluid fraction significantly larger than previous normal band theory results, indicating that normal band theory overestimates the entrainment effect in the rod phase of the neutron-star inner crust.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent HFB calculations for neutrons and Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for protons in a periodic lattice, using Bloch boundary conditions to account for the lattice periodicity. A relative stationary flow between the rods (proton clusters) and the surrounding superfluid neutron gas was introduced via a Galilean transformation, inducing a non-trivial phase in the pairing field. The superfluid fraction was extracted from the resulting neutron current using the Andreev-Bashkin two-fluid formalism, with the average superfluid velocity set to zero in the chosen frame.

## Key numbers
- Superfluid fraction \(\rho_S/\bar{\rho}_n = 0.945\) for SLy4 at \(\mu_n = 12.00\) MeV, \(L = 24\) fm, baryon density \(\rho_b = 0.0619\) fm\(^{-3}\), and average gap \(\bar{\Delta}_n = 0.755\) MeV.
- Superfluid fraction \(\rho_S/\bar{\rho}_n = 0.964\) for BSk24 at \(\mu_n = 10.95\) MeV, \(L = 24\) fm, \(\rho_b = 0.0670\) fm\(^{-3}\), and \(\bar{\Delta}_n = 1.506\) MeV.
- For a non-selfconsistent mean field from Oyamatsu (1994) at \(\mu_n = 27.2461\) MeV and \(L = 27.17\) fm, the HFB result is \(\rho_S/\bar{\rho}_n = 0.931\), compared to 0.681 from normal band theory (Carter 2005).
- The superfluid fraction rises quickly with the average gap and approaches a limiting value; at the physical pairing strength the curve is flat, so the precise gap value affects the fraction only weakly.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous HFB study of the slab (lasagna) phase (Almirante 2024) to the rod (spaghetti) phase, and contrasts with normal band theory calculations by Carter and Chamel (2005) which neglected the dynamics of the superfluid order parameter. The results are significantly larger than those from normal band theory, confirming earlier suspicions (Martin 2016, Minami 2022) that band theory underestimates the superfluid fraction. The findings reconcile band-structure and hydrodynamic approaches, and provide microscopic input for modeling pulsar glitches and crustal shear modes.
