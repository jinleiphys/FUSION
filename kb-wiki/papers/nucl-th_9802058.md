# A new Perspective on the Scalar meson Puzzle, from Spontaneous Chiral Symmetry Breaking Beyond BCS

> We introduce coupled channels of Bethe-Salpeter mesons both in the boundstate equation for mesons and in the mass gap equation for chiral symmetry. Consistency is insured by the Ward Identities for axial currents, which preserve the Goldstone boson nature of the pion and prevents a systematic shift of the hadron spectrum. We study the decay of a scalar meson coupled to a pair of pseudoscalars. We also show that coupled channels reduce the breaking of chiral symmetry, with the same Feynman diagrams that appear in the coupling of a scalar meson to a pair of pseudoscalar mesons. Exact calculations are performed in a particular confining quark model, where we find that the groundstate $I=0, ^3P_0 q \bar q$ meson is the f_0(980) with a partial decay width of 40MeV. We also find a 30% reduction of the chiral condensate due to coupled channels.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scalar meson puzzle can be addressed by solving the mass gap equation for chiral symmetry breaking beyond the BCS level, including coupled channels of Bethe-Salpeter mesons, with the condition that Ward Identities for axial currents must be satisfied to preserve the pion as a Goldstone boson and prevent a systematic shift of the hadron spectrum.

## Method

The author introduces coupled channels of Bethe-Salpeter mesons into both the boundstate equation for mesons and the mass gap equation for chiral symmetry, using a particular confining quark model with an instantaneous harmonic oscillator potential. The consistency of the approach is enforced by Ward Identities for axial currents, which ensure the pion remains massless in the chiral limit. Exact calculations are performed for the scalar meson decay into a pair of pseudoscalars and for the effect of coupled channels on the quark condensed vacuum.

## Key numbers

- The groundstate I=0, ^3P_0 q qbar meson is identified as the f_0(980) with a partial decay width of 40 MeV.
- A 30% reduction of the chiral condensate is found due to coupled channels.
- The scalar meson mass is computed as M = 2.94 K_0 = 970 MeV, where K_0 = 330 MeV is the oscillator parameter giving the best overall fit of the meson spectrum.
- The pion decay constant is f_pi^{(t)} = f_pi^{(s)} = 0.21 K_0 = 69 MeV for the parameter a = -0.18 which renders the pion Lorentz invariant.
- The parameter a that makes the pion Lorentz invariant is a = -0.18, while the homogeneous equation for f_1 has a solution at a_0 = -0.195.

## Context

This work extends the BCS-type approach to chiral symmetry breaking pioneered by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) by including coupled channel effects beyond the BCS level, which had previously been neglected in mass gap equations. The paper addresses the scalar meson puzzle, where the observed scalar mesons (f_0(980), a_0(980), K_0*(1430), f_0(1500)) have narrower decay widths than expected and show anomalous SU(3)_f breaking compared to vector mesons and baryons. The results enable a consistent treatment of scalar meson decays and chiral symmetry breaking at the quark level, providing a framework to study the f_0 to pi pi decay and the effect of meson coupled channels on the quark condensed vacuum.
