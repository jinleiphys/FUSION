# Quasi-elastic scattering for the nuclear ground state structure: An intriguing case of $^{30}$Si

> Quasi-elastic (QEL) scattering measurements have been performed using the $^{28, 30}$Si projectiles off the $^{90}$Zr target at energies around the Coulomb barrier. Coupled-channels (CC) calculations were carried out in a large parameter space of quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations for the N=Z, $^{28}$Si and N=Z+2, $^{30}$Si nuclei. $^{28}$Si at the N=Z line is observed to be uniquely oblate shaped in its ground state. In contrast, for $^{30}$Si with just two additional neutrons -- oblate, prolate, and spherical CC descriptions are equally compatible with the measurements. To further investigate the nuclear structure evolution with varying neutron number, shell-model calculations were performed. These calculations reveal a sudden change in the nuclear structure aspects at $^{30}$Si in going from $^{28}$Si to $^{30}$Si. Combined reaction and structure analyses consistently indicate that $^{30}$Si does not possess a well-defined intrinsic shape, and it is a potential candidate for ``shape fluctuations" in its ground state.

# Quasi-elastic scattering for the nuclear ground state structure: An intriguing case of $^{30}$Si

## Key claim
The ground state of $^{30}$Si does not possess a well-defined intrinsic shape; rather, it is a candidate for shape fluctuations (γ-soft character), because oblate, prolate, and spherical coupled-channels descriptions all reproduce the quasi-elastic scattering data equally well, unlike the uniquely oblate $^{28}$Si.

## Method
Quasi-elastic scattering measurements were performed using $^{28,30}$Si beams on a $^{90}$Zr target at beam energies from 70 to 102 MeV in 2-MeV steps, with detectors placed at 140°, 150°, 160°, and 170°. Coupled-channels (CC) calculations using a modified version of CCFULL were carried out over a grid of quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ from -0.50 to +0.50 and hexadecapole deformation $\beta_4$ from -0.40 to +0.40, including rotational couplings for $^{30}$Si and vibrational couplings for $^{90}$Zr; Bayesian analyses with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) were used to extract deformation parameters and uncertainties. Shell-model calculations using the USDB interaction diagonalised with KSHELL were performed for the even-even $^{28-32}$Si isotopes.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit oblate deformation parameters for $^{30}$Si: $\beta_2 = -0.30 \pm 0.08$, $\beta_4 = 0.00 \pm 0.11$, with reduced $\chi^2 \approx 1.9$
- Best-fit prolate deformation parameters for $^{30}$Si: $\beta_2 = +0.31 \pm 0.08$, $\beta_4 = -0.10 \pm 0.06$, with reduced $\chi^2 \approx 1.9$
- Best-fit spherical-vibrational coupling strength for $^{30}$Si: $\beta_{\mathrm{vib}} = 0.26 \pm 0.01$, with reduced $\chi^2 \approx 1.7$
- Adopted $B(E2; 0_1^+ \to 2_1^+)$ for $^{30}$Si: $0.0215(10)~e^2\mathrm{b}^2$, corresponding to $\beta = 0.315(7)$
- $R_{4/2} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ ratios: 2.59 for $^{28}$Si, 2.36 for $^{30}$Si, and approximately 3.0 for $^{32}$Si
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment $Q_s(2^+_1)$ for $^{30}$Si: measured $-0.05(6)~eb$, shell-model prediction $+0.02~eb$
- $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)/B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)$ ratio for $^{30}$Si: experimentally approximately 1.1, shell-model approximately 1.69

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quasi-elastic scattering studies that established $^{24}$Mg as uniquely prolate (Gupta 2020) and $^{28}$Si as uniquely oblate (Gupta 2023) using identical experimental and analysis methods. The results contradict earlier conflicting reports on $^{30}$Si from alpha scattering (Siemaszko 1994) suggesting prolate shape, deuteron scattering (Berg 1972) also suggesting prolate, and Coulomb excitation measurements (Fewell 1979; Pritychenko 2013) suggesting spherical or oblate shapes. The findings also contrast with relativistic mean-field calculations predicting oblate shape (Myaing and Hagino 2008) and are more consistent with beyond-mean-field calculations showing a broad potential energy surface centered around spherical shape (Mei and Hagino 2018) and Skyrme-HF calculations with tensor interactions showing either two minima or a flat minimum (Li and Sagawa 2013). The work enables future coupled-channels approaches that incorporate intrinsic shape softness of the projectile and/or target, and provides experimental constraints for advanced theoretical treatments of shape fluctuations in the sd-shell region.
