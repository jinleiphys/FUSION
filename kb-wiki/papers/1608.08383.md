# Systematic study of favored α-decay half-lives of closed shell odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei related to ground and isomeric states

> In this work, we systematically investigate the favored $α$-decay half-lives and $α$ preformation probabilities of both odd-$A$ and doubly-odd nuclei related to ground and isomeric states around the doubly magic cores at $Z=82$, $N=82$ and at $Z=82$, $N=126$, respectively, within a two-potential approach from the view of the valence nucleon (or hole). The results show that the $α$ preformation probability is linear related to $N_\text{p}N_\text{n}$ or $N_\text{p}N_\text{n}I$, where $N_\text{p}$, $N_\text{n}$, and $I$ are the number of valence protons (or holes), the number of valence neutrons (or holes), and the isospin of the parent nucleus, respectively. Fitting the $α$ preformation probabilities data extracted from the differences between experimental data and calculated half-lives without a shell correction, we give two analytic formulas of the $α$ preformation probabilities and the values of corresponding parameters. Using those formulas and the parameters, we calculate the $α$-decay half-lives for those nuclei. The calculated results can well reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors find that the α preformation probability for odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei around the Z=82, N=82 and Z=82, N=126 shell closures follows a linear relationship with the product of valence proton and neutron numbers (NpNn) or with NpNn times isospin asymmetry (NpNnI), but the linear fit is slightly better for the simpler NpNn/(N0+Z0) expression than for the NpNnI form, and the predictions are better for nuclei around the Z=82, N=126 core than for those around the Z=82, N=82 core.

## Method

The authors used a two-potential approach with a cosh-parametrized nuclear potential to calculate α-decay half-lives, extracting α preformation probabilities by comparing calculated half-lives (assuming constant preformation factors P0=0.35 for odd-A and P0=0.18 for doubly-odd nuclei) to experimental data from NUBASE2012. They then fitted the extracted preformation probabilities to linear functions of NpNn/(N0+Z0) and NpNnI, obtaining separate parameter sets for odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei in three different regions of the valence nucleon coordinate space.

## Context

This work extends the NpNn scheme previously applied to even-even nuclei by Seif et al. (2011) to odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei around the Z=82, N=82 and Z=82, N=126 shell closures, treating both ground and isomeric states in a unified way. The authors find that α preformation probabilities for isomers and ground states show no systematic differences (log10(P*α/Pα) values around 0), enabling a combined analysis. The results enable systematic predictions of α-decay half-lives for odd-A and doubly-odd nuclei in these regions, and the parameter sets provided allow calculation of half-lives for unmeasured cases.
