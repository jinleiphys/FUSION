# Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering on nucleons and nuclei in generalized vector meson dominance model

> We consider Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) on nucleons and nuclei in the framework of generalized vector meson dominance (GVMD) model. We demonstrate that the GVMD model provides a good description of the HERA data on the dependence of the proton DVCS cross section on Q^2, W (at Q^2=4 GeV^2) and t. At Q^2 = 8 GeV^2, the soft W-behavior of the GVMD model somewhat underestimates the W-dependence of the DVCS cross section due to the hard contribution not present in the GVMD model. We estimate 1/Q^2 power-suppressed corrections to the DVCS amplitude and the DVCS cross section and find them large. We also make predictions for the nuclear DVCS amplitude and cross section in the kinematics of the future Electron-Ion Collider. We predict significant nuclear shadowing, which matches well predictions of the leading-twist nuclear shadowing in DIS on nuclei.

## Key claim

The generalized vector meson dominance (GVMD) model provides a good description of HERA data on the proton DVCS cross section dependence on \(Q^2\), \(W\) (at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\)), and \(t\), but at \(Q^2=8\) GeV\(^2\) the soft \(W\)-behavior of the model somewhat underestimates the \(W\)-dependence due to a hard contribution not present in the GVMD model.

## Method

The authors extended the GVMD model to the off-forward case (\(t \neq 0\)) by parameterizing the \(t\)-dependence of the vector meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes with a slope that decreases with increasing vector meson mass index \(n\). They expressed the DVCS amplitude in terms of the total \(\gamma p\) cross section using the ZEUS parameterization, and for nuclear targets they combined the GVMD model with the generalized Glauber formalism to account for multiple vector meson rescatterings inside the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of \(1/Q^2\)-corrections to the DVCS amplitude at \(t=t_{\min}\) is 20% at \(Q^2=2\) GeV\(^2\), 11% at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\), and 6% at \(Q^2=8\) GeV\(^2\).
- The contribution of \(1/Q^2\)-corrections to the \(t\)-integrated DVCS cross section is 56% at \(Q^2=2\) GeV\(^2\), 32% at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\), and 17% at \(Q^2=8\) GeV\(^2\).
- At \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\), the GVMD model predicts \(\sigma_{\text{DVCS}} \sim W^{0.4}\), consistent with HERA data.
- At \(Q^2=8\) GeV\(^2\), the ZEUS data give \(\sigma_{\text{DVCS}} \sim W^{\delta}\) with \(\delta=0.75 \pm 0.15\), steeper than the GVMD prediction.
- The GVMD model reproduces the \(Q^2\)-dependence of \(\sigma_{\text{DVCS}}\) over the range \(3 \leq Q^2 \leq 85\) GeV\(^2\) at fixed \(W=82\) GeV.
- The H1 data for \(d\sigma_{\text{DVCS}}/dt\) at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\) give a slope \(B=(6.66 \pm 0.54 \pm 0.43)\) GeV\(^{-2}\); at \(Q^2=8\) GeV\(^2\), \(B=(5.82 \pm 0.59 \pm 0.60)\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- In Jefferson Lab kinematics (\(E=5.75\) GeV, \(Q^2=2.3\) GeV, \(t=-0.28\) GeV, \(x_B=0.36\)), the DVCS cross section at the lepton level is \(d^4\sigma/(dQ^2 dx_B dt d\phi)=0.0022\) nb/GeV\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the GVMD model, previously applied to inclusive structure functions and exclusive meson production (Fraas 1974, Ditsas 1975, Shaw 1993), to the off-forward case for DVCS on nucleons and nuclei. It provides a bridge between the photoproduction regime (\(Q^2 \approx 0\)) and the deep inelastic regime (\(Q^2 \sim 10\) GeV\(^2\)), complementing the GPD-based factorization approach which requires higher \(Q^2\) for validity. The predictions for nuclear DVCS at the future Electron-Ion Collider show significant nuclear shadowing, matching the leading-twist nuclear shadowing predictions of Frankfurt (2003) for DIS on nuclei.
