# Educing GPDs from amplitudes of hard exclusive processes

> The dual parametrization of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) is considered in details. We discuss which part of information about hadron structure encoded in GPDs [part of total GPD image] can be restored from the known amplitude of a hard exclusive process. The physics content of this partial image is analyzed.

# Educing GPDs from amplitudes of hard exclusive processes

## Key claim
The dual parametrization of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) allows one to restore only a partial "image" of the hadron structure from the known amplitude of a hard exclusive process, specifically, the function N(x,t) and the D-form factor, but the complete GPD cannot be reconstructed from the amplitude alone. The restorable part corresponds to the response of the target hadron to quark-antiquark probes with fixed angular momentum J in the t-channel.

## Method
The author employs the dual parametrization of GPDs, which expresses the GPD H(x,ξ,t) as an infinite series of t-channel exchanges in terms of forward-like functions Q_{2ν}(x,t). The leading-order amplitude of hard exclusive reactions is expressed as a convolution integral of the GPD with a kernel, and the author derives an inversion formula (Eq. 18) that allows the function N(x,t) = Σ x^{2ν} Q_{2ν}(x,t) to be restored directly from the measured imaginary part of the amplitude. The real part of the amplitude is then shown to satisfy a dispersion relation with the D-form factor as the subtraction constant.

## Key numbers
- The inversion formula (Eq. 18) relates N(x,t) to Im A(ξ,t) via: N(x,t) = (2/π) [x(1-x²)/(1+x²)^{3/2}] ∫_{2x/(1+x²)}^{1} dξ/ξ^{3/2} [1/√(ξ - 2x/(1+x²))] {½ Im A(ξ,t) - ξ d/dξ Im A(ξ,t)}
- If Im A(ξ,t) ~ (1-ξ)^β as ξ→1, then N(x,t) ~ [1/(2^{β-1})] [Γ(β+1)Γ(½)/Γ(β+½)] (1-x)^{2β} as x→1
- If Im A(ξ,t) ~ 1/ξ^α as ξ→0, then N(x,t) ~ [1/(2^α)] [Γ(1+α)/(Γ(½)Γ(½+α))] (1/x^α) as x→0
- The D-form factor D(t) is expressed in terms of Q₀(x,t) and N(x,t) via: D(t) = ∫₀¹ dz/z Q₀(z,t)[1/√(1+z²)-1] + ∫₀¹ dz/z [N(z,t)-Q₀(z,t)] [1/√(1+z²)]
- For the "magnetic" nucleon GPD H_M = H+E, the forward limit Q₀^{(M)}(x,t) is normalized to the angular momentum J^Q: ∫₀¹ dx x Q₀^{(M)}(x,t) = (5/3) J^Q(t)

## Context
This work extends the dual parametrization of GPDs originally suggested by Polyakov and Shuvaev (1998) and builds on the tomography approach of Polyakov (2007). It addresses the question of what part of the GPD can be reconstructed from hard exclusive process amplitudes, a problem previously discussed by Mueller et al. (2006), Kumericki et al. (2007), and others. The paper enables a practical methodology for extracting GPD information from experimental data on deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and other hard exclusive processes, and suggests applications to nuclear effects (the "non-forward EMC effect") and baryon resonance spectroscopy via non-diagonal GPDs.
