# Radiative decays of the 1$P$, 1$D$, 2$S$, and 2$P$ $Λ_c$ and 1$D$, 2$S$, and 2$P$ $Ξ_c$ charmed baryons

> We analyze the radiative decays of the the 1$P$, 1$D$, 2$S$, and 2$P$ $Λ_c$ and 1$D$, 2$S$, and 2$P$ $Ξ_c$ charmed baryons, which belong to the flavor anti-triplet ($\bf {\bar 3}_{\rm F}$), using the constituent quark model. We compute electromagnetic transitions from ground and $P$-wave states to ground states, as well as from second-shell states to both ground and $P$-wave final states. Electromagnetic decay widths are especially valuable for identifying resonances when multiple states share the same mass and total decay width. We give branching ratios which can confirm the assignment of the $Ξ_c(3055)$ reported by LHCb. We also give branching ratios that can support the assignment of the $Ξ_c(3080)$, and discuss the possibilities for the $Ξ_c(3080)$ to be the 1$D$ state with $J^P=5/2^{+}$ or the 2$S$ with $J^P=1/2^{+}$. For the first time, this work provides calculations of electromagnetic decays for $D_ρ$-wave states, $ρ-λ$ mixed configurations, and $ρ$-mode radially excited states in singly charmed baryons of the flavor anti-triplet. Both experimental and model-dependent uncertainties are taken into account throughout our analysis.

## Key claim

The authors use a constituent quark model to compute radiative decay widths for excited $\Lambda_c$ and $\Xi_c$ baryons in the flavor antitriplet, claiming that their predicted branching ratios can confirm the assignment of the $\Xi_c(3055)$ reported by LHCb and can support the assignment of the $\Xi_c(3080)$ as either a $1D$ state with $J^P=5/2^{+}$ or a $2S$ state with $J^P=1/2^{+}$, though the abstract's claim of "confirmation" is hedged by the inclusion of both experimental and model-dependent uncertainties throughout the analysis.

## Method

The authors employ a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian plus spin, spin-orbit, isospin, and flavor terms, using Jacobi coordinates $\boldsymbol{\rho}$ and $\boldsymbol{\lambda}$ to describe the three-quark system. Electromagnetic transition amplitudes are computed analytically using the interaction Hamiltonian with magnetic moment, spin-ladder, and momentum-ladder operators for each quark, without relying on the Close-Copley replacement. The masses and state assignments are taken from Garcia-Tecocoatzi (2022), and uncertainties are propagated via a Monte Carlo procedure with $10^3$ samples using Gaussian distributions centered on PDG or theoretical masses.

## Key numbers

* $\Lambda_c(2616)$ with $J^P=1/2^-$ ($^2P_{\lambda,1/2}$) decays to $\Lambda_c^+ \gamma$ with width $3.5_{-2.9}^{+3.2}$ keV.
* $\Lambda_c(2800)$ with $J^P=1/2^-$ ($^2P_{\rho,1/2}$) decays to $\Sigma_c^+ \gamma$ with width $584_{-49}^{+51}$ keV.
* $\Lambda_c(2869)$ with $J^P=3/2^-$ ($^4P_{\rho,3/2}$) decays to $\Sigma_c^{*+} \gamma$ with width $434_{-37}^{+37}$ keV.
* $\Lambda_c(2914)$ with $J^P=5/2^-$ ($^4P_{\rho,5/2}$) decays to $\Sigma_c^{*+} \gamma$ with width $1341_{-249}^{+229}$ keV.
* $\Lambda_c(2981)$ with $J^P=3/2^+$ ($^2D_{3/2}$) decays to $\Lambda_c^+ \gamma$ with width $28_{-2}^{+2}$ keV.
* $\Lambda_c(3375)$ with $J^P=1/2^+$ ($^2S_{1/2}$, $\rho$-mode radially excited) decays to $\Sigma_c^+ \gamma$ with width $240_{-67}^{+57}$ keV and to $\Sigma_c^{*+} \gamma$ with width $100_{-33}^{+30}$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of electromagnetic decays for $\Sigma_c$, $\Xi'_c$, and $\Omega_c$ baryons in the flavor sextet (Davila-Rivera, 2025) to the flavor antitriplet $\Lambda_c$ and $\Xi_c$ states, and provides the first calculations for $D_\rho$-wave states, $\rho$-$\lambda$ mixed configurations, and $\rho$-mode radially excited states in singly charmed baryons of the flavor antitriplet. It builds on prior constituent quark model calculations for $P$-wave $\Xi_c$ states (Garcia-Tecocoatzi, 2025) that agreed with Belle data, and compares with results from Wang (2017), Ortiz-Pacheco (2023), Yao (2018), and Peng (2024). The predictions enable experimental identification of $\Xi_c(3055)$ and $\Xi_c(3080)$ quantum numbers through radiative decay branching ratios.
