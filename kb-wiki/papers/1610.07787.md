# In-medium dispersion relations of charmonia studied by maximum entropy method

> We study in-medium spectral properties of charmonia in the vector and pseudoscalar channels at nonzero momenta on quenched lattices, especially focusing on their dispersion relation and weight of the peak. We measure the lattice Euclidean correlation functions with nonzero momenta on the anisotropic quenched lattices and study the spectral functions with the maximum entropy method. The dispersion relations of charmonia and the momentum dependence of the weight of the peak are analyzed with the maximum entropy method together with the errors estimated probabilistically in this method. We find significant increase of the masses of charmonia in medium. It is also found that the functional form of the charmonium dispersion relations is not changed from that in the vacuum within the error even at $T\simeq1.6T_c$ for all the channels we analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the masses of charmonia (J/ψ and ηc) increase significantly in medium, but the functional form of their dispersion relations remains Lorentz covariant (the same as in vacuum) within error even at T ≃ 1.6Tc, with the caveat that the existence of the ηc peak at this temperature has lower statistical significance than the J/ψ peak.

## Method

The authors measured Euclidean correlation functions for charmonia in the vector and pseudoscalar channels at nonzero momenta on anisotropic quenched lattices using Wilson gauge action and Wilson fermion. They reconstructed spectral functions from these correlators using the maximum entropy method (MEM), analyzing the center of weight of the peak to determine dispersion relations and the integrated spectral weight to determine residues, with errors estimated probabilistically within MEM.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: β = 7.0, bare anisotropy ξ₀ = 3.5, spatial hopping parameter κσ = 0.08285, fermion anisotropy γF = 3.476, resulting in anisotropy ξ = aσ/aτ = 4
- Temporal lattice spacing aτ = 0.00975 fm; spatial length Lσ ≃ 2.5 fm
- Temperatures studied: T/Tc = 0.78 (vacuum), 1.38, 1.49, 1.56, 1.62, 1.70, with lattice sizes Nτ = 96, 54, 50, 48, 46, 44 respectively (all Nσ = 64)
- Number of configurations: 700 for T/Tc = 1.70, 500 for all other temperatures except 500 for T/Tc = 0.78
- Default model parameters: m₀ = 1.15 for pseudoscalar channel, m₀ = 0.40 for vector channel; m₁ = 0 in final analysis
- Lower bound for peak integration: ωmin = 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of charmonia at finite temperature (Asakawa 2004, Datta 2004, Umeda 2004) which had focused primarily on zero momentum, by systematically analyzing nonzero momentum spectral functions and dispersion relations. It also goes beyond earlier momentum-dependent studies (Datta 2004, Nonaka 2011, Oktay 2010, Aarts 2013, Ding 2013) by providing quantitative error estimates from MEM. The results enable future investigations of possible modifications to charmonium dispersion relations at higher temperatures or with dynamical fermions, and may inform phenomenological models of charmonium production in heavy ion collisions.
