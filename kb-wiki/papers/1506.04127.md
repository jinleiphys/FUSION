# Laser-Nucleus Interactions: The Quasiadiabatic Regime

> The interaction between nuclei and a strong zeptosecond laser pulse with coherent MeV photons is investigated theoretically. We provide a first semi-quantitative study of the quasiadiabatic regime where the photon absorption rate is comparable to the nuclear equilibration rate. In that regime, multiple photon absorption leads to the formation of a compound nucleus in the so-far unexplored regime of excitation energies several hundred MeV above the yrast line. The temporal dynamics of the process is investigated by means of a set of master equations that account for dipole absorption, stimulated dipole emission, neutron decay and induced fission in a chain of nuclei. That set is solved numerically by means of state-of-the-art matrix exponential methods also used in nuclear fuel burnup and radioactivity transport calculations. Our quantitative estimates predict the excitation path and range of nuclei reached by neutron decay and provide relevant information for the layout of future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first semi-quantitative study of the quasiadiabatic regime of laser-nucleus interactions, where the photon absorption rate is comparable to the nuclear equilibration rate (N Γ_dip ≈ Γ_sp ≈ 5 MeV), a condition that the abstract presents as the central focus but which depends on the specific values of the effective dipole width and the spreading width.

## Method

The authors constructed a set of time-dependent master equations describing dipole absorption, stimulated dipole emission, neutron evaporation via the Weisskopf estimate, and induced fission via the Bohr-Wheeler formula for a chain of nuclei with mass numbers A - i. They solved these equations numerically using matrix exponential methods, with level densities calculated from single-particle level densities using either linear or quadratic energy dependence for ρ₁(ε). The calculations assumed full equilibration between photon absorption steps and neglected spin, justified by an analytical derivation showing the spin distribution peaks at J = √N₀ for N₀ absorbed photons.

## Key numbers

- Effective dipole width N Γ_dip ≈ 5 MeV, with N ≈ 10³ to 10⁴ coherent photons and Γ_dip in the keV range
- For A = 100: critical energy E_N ≈ 435 MeV where neutron decay width equals dipole absorption width; E_max = 533 MeV (maximum of level density)
- For A = 200: critical energy E_N ≈ 1080 MeV; E_max = 1200 MeV
- Number of absorbed photons to reach E_N: N₀ = 87 photons for A = 100, N₀ = 216 photons for A = 200 (both at E_L = 5 MeV)
- Time to reach E_N: t_N = 12 × 10⁻²¹ s for A = 100, t_N = 3 × 10⁻²⁰ s for A = 200
- Neutron binding energy B_N = 8 MeV (assumed constant across the chain)
- Fission half-life at E = 200 MeV for medium-weight nucleus: approximately 5 zs
- Spreading width Γ_sp ≈ 5 MeV (value taken from low-lying excitations)
- Maximum number of absorbed photons N₀ ≈ 5 × 10²
- Single-particle spectrum range V = 45 MeV, Fermi energy F = 37 MeV

## Context

This work extends the perturbative regime study by Weidenmüller (2011) where N Γ_dip ≪ Γ_sp, and provides the first quantitative treatment of the quasiadiabatic regime (N Γ_dip ≈ Γ_sp). The approach differs from Coulomb excitation (Bertulani 2008) and inelastic electron scattering (Bentz 2008) by forming compound nuclei at very high excitation energies rather than collective modes. The results enable experimental layout for future laser-nucleus experiments at facilities such as ELI and IZEST, and the method is related to precompound reaction theory (Weidenmüller 2008).
