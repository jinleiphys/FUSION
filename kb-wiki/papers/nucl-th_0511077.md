# Baryonic triplet P-wave dominant superfluidity under combined pion condensation with delta-mixing

> We study baryonic superfluidity under the combined pion condensation with delta-mixing, where both condensates of the neutral and charged pions take place in neutron stars. Outline of formulation treating such superfluidity is presented and energy gap calculations show the possibility that critical temperatures are higher than about 1x10^9 K.

## Key claim
Under the combined pion condensation (both neutral and charged pions) with delta-mixing in neutron stars, baryonic superfluidity can occur with critical temperatures higher than about 1×10⁹ K, but this result is obtained from calculations in the dominant quasi-neutron channel only, and the authors note that full coupled-channel solutions are needed for confirmation.

## Method
The authors formulate baryonic superfluidity in the alternating layer spin (ALS) structure that arises from combined neutral and charged pion condensation. They construct quasi-baryon operators (quasi-neutrons and quasi-protons) that include mixing with delta resonances, define pair operators with two-dimensional stretched angular momentum (spin-triplet, 2D P-wave, total m_J = ±2), and derive a coupled gap equation for three pairing channels using Bogoliubov transformation. Energy gap calculations are performed for the dominant quasi-neutron channel using the OPEG-B potential extended to the nucleon-plus-delta space, with pion condensation parameters taken from prior work by the same group.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperatures T_c are higher than about 1×10⁹ K for the combined pion condensation case, for effective mass parameters m_η* ≈ 0.8–0.9
- The attenuation factor from charged pion condensation varies with density: (1−v²)²/(1+y₁²)² ≈ 0.74 at ρ=2ρ₀, 0.50 at ρ=4ρ₀, and 0.63 at ρ=6ρ₀, where v² is the quasi-proton mixing probability and y₁² is the Δ⁻-mixing probability
- The quasi-proton mixing probability v² ≈ 0.10 at ρ=2ρ₀, 0.24 at ρ=4ρ₀, and 0.17 at ρ=6ρ₀
- The Δ⁻-mixing probability y₁² ≈ 0.045 at ρ=2ρ₀, 0.072 at ρ=4ρ₀, and 0.042 at ρ=6ρ₀
- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Calculations are performed at densities ρ = (2–6)ρ₀

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Tamagaki and Takatsuka (1997–1999) on baryonic superfluidity under neutral pion condensation alone, and by Tatsumi (2003) on combined pion condensation with delta-mixing. The authors note that recent experiments on spin-isospin excitation modes of nuclei (Suzuki and Sakai, 1998) suggest neutral and charged pion condensations set in at nearly the same density (not higher than about 2ρ₀), contradicting earlier work by Matsui and Tamagaki (1987) where charged pion condensation was thought to occur first at about 2ρ₀ and neutral pion condensation at higher densities (3–5ρ₀). The results enable studies of neutron star cooling, where such high critical temperatures could suppress the too-rapid cooling from the pion direct Urca process, as discussed by Tsuruta and collaborators (1996–2003) and Yakovlev et al. (2001).
