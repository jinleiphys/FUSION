# Relativistic Approach to Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> Pairing correlations in symmetric nuclear matter are studied within a relativistic mean-field approximation based on a field theory of nucleons coupled to neutral ($σ$ and $ω$) and to charged ($\varrho$) mesons. The Hartree-Fock and the pairing fields are calculated in a self-consistent way. The energy gap is the result of a strong cancellation between the scalar and vector components of the pairing field. We find that the pair amplitude vanishes beyond a certain value of momentum of the paired nucleons. This fact determines an effective cutoff in the gap equation. The value of this cutoff gives an energy gap in agreement with the estimates of non relativistic calculations.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations in symmetric nuclear matter are studied within a relativistic mean-field approximation, showing that the energy gap results from a strong cancellation between scalar and vector components of the pairing field, and that the pair amplitude vanishes beyond a certain momentum of the paired nucleons, providing a natural cutoff in the gap equation without requiring an artificial one.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach based on Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD-I) with nucleons coupled to neutral sigma and omega mesons and charged rho mesons, neglecting pions. They derive self-consistent equations for the one-particle density matrix and pair amplitude using a Gorkov factorization, first neglecting finite-range and retardation effects in meson exchange, then incorporating finite-range effects through an iterative procedure using meson propagators.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants used are: f_S = 2.37 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻², f_V = 1.45 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻², and f_ρ = 1.55 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻², chosen to reproduce a binding energy of 15.75 MeV at a Fermi momentum of 1.42 fm⁻¹
- The effective pairing coupling constants are f = 5.58 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻² and g = -4.05 × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻²
- A superfluid solution exists for Fermi momenta p_F < 1.35 fm⁻¹
- At p_F = 0.9 fm⁻¹, the quasiparticle energies lie slightly below normal-phase excitation energies for momenta above approximately 1.9 fm⁻¹, giving the cutoff
- The difference between the sum fΔ₀ + gΔ_S and the actual energy gap is about 10% near p_F = 0.9 fm⁻¹, where the gap is maximal
- Finite-range corrections reduce the effective coupling constants a_f and a_g at |p| = p_F by about 10% and 2% respectively for 0.6 fm⁻¹ < p_F < 1.0 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of superfluidity in nuclear matter by Kucharek and Ring (1991) and by the authors of a 1996 paper (Gui et al.), who first showed that the pairing field has large scalar and vector components that cancel to give the gap. The present paper improves upon those treatments by avoiding arbitrary cutoffs in the gap equation, instead obtaining a natural cutoff from the relativistic formalism. The results qualitatively agree with nonrelativistic calculations by Baldo et al. (1990), Chen et al. (1986), Kuo et al. (1989), Ainsworth et al. (1989), and others, though the energy gap values are about twice the generally accepted estimates when finite-range effects are neglected. The work enables more consistent relativistic calculations of pairing in nuclear matter without artificial momentum cutoffs.
