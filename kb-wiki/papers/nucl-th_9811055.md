# Parity violating target asymmetry in electron - proton scattering

> We analyze the parity-violating (PV) components of the analyzing power in elastic electron-proton scattering and discuss their sensitivity to the strange quark contributions to the proton weak form factors. We point out that the component of the analyzing power along the momentum transfer is independent of the electric weak form factor and thus compares favorably with the PV beam asymmetry for a determination of the strangeness magnetic moment. We also show that the transverse component could be used for constraining the strangeness radius. Finally, we argue that a measurement of both components could give experimental information on the strangeness axial charge.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parity-violating component of the analyzing power along the momentum transfer in elastic electron-proton scattering is independent of the electric weak form factor, making it a more favorable observable than the beam asymmetry for determining the strangeness magnetic moment, provided that the proton target polarization can be freely chosen and measured with sufficient precision.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the parity-violating target asymmetry components (transverse \( \mathcal{A}_x \) and longitudinal \( \mathcal{A}_z \)) in elastic electron-proton scattering, using the standard electroweak formalism with one-photon and one-\( Z^0 \) boson exchange in the low-\( Q^2 \) limit. They decomposed the hadronic tensor into symmetric and antisymmetric parts, expressed the cross section in terms of electromagnetic and weak form factors (including strange quark contributions), and computed the asymmetry as the difference of cross sections for opposite target spin orientations. The numerical results were obtained using a Galster-type parametrization for the strange form factors and reference values for the axial radiative corrections from Musolf and Holstein (1990).

## Key numbers

- The SAMPLE Collaboration measurement at \( Q^2 = 0.1 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2 \) gave \( G_M^s(0.1 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2) = 0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19 \, \mu_N \) (statistical, systematic, and theoretical uncertainties, respectively).
- The HAPPEX Collaboration measured the beam asymmetry at \( Q^2 = 0.48 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^2 \) as \( \mathcal{A}_{LR} = -14.5 \pm 2.2 \, \text{ppm} \).
- The longitudinal target asymmetry \( \mathcal{A}_z \) is independent of the electric weak form factor \( \tilde{G}_E \), unlike the beam asymmetry \( \mathcal{A}_{LR} \).
- The transverse component \( \mathcal{A}_x \) is sensitive to the strangeness radius \( r_s^2 \), with a reference value of \( r_s^2 = 0.16 \, \text{fm}^2 \) taken from Jaffe (1989).
- The reference value for the strangeness axial charge is \( g_A^{(s)} = -0.15 \), deduced from the neutrino scattering experiment of Ahrens et al. (1987).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of parity-violating electron-proton scattering, correcting errors in the analytic expressions for target asymmetry given by Fayyazuddin and Riazuddin (1990) and by earlier authors from the 1970s (C. N. et al. 1977, R. S. et al. 1974, B. D. C. et al. 1976). It builds on the SAMPLE and HAPPEX experimental results to propose a complementary method for extracting strange quark contributions to the proton weak form factors. The analysis enables future experiments with polarized proton targets to independently determine the strangeness magnetic moment, strangeness radius, and strangeness axial charge, which could resolve discrepancies between theoretical predictions and existing measurements.
