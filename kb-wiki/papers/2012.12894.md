# Quark cluster expansion model for interpreting finite-T lattice QCD thermodynamics

> We present a unified approach to the thermodynamics of hadron-quark-gluon matter at finite temperatures on the basis of a quark cluster expansion in the form of a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach with a generic ansatz for the hadronic phase shifts that fulfills the Levinson theorem. The change in the composition of the system from a hadron resonance gas to a quark-gluon plasma takes place in the narrow temperature interval of $150 - 185$ MeV where the Mott dissociation of hadrons is triggered by the dropping quark mass as a result of the restoration of chiral symmetry. The deconfinement of quark and gluon degrees of freedom is regulated by the Polyakov loop variable that signals the breaking of the $Z(3)$ center symmetry of the color $SU(3)$ group of QCD. We suggest a Polyakov-loop quark-gluon plasma model with $\mathcal{O}(α_s)$ virial correction and solve the stationarity condition of the thermodynamic potential (gap equation) for the Polyakov loop. The resulting pressure is in excellent agreement with lattice QCD simulations up to high temperatures.

## Key claim
The paper presents a unified thermodynamic model for hadron-quark-gluon matter that reproduces lattice QCD results, but the agreement depends on using the lattice QCD chiral condensate as an input rather than deriving it self-consistently, and the model is currently restricted to zero chemical potential.

## Method
The authors construct a thermodynamic potential as a sum of a Polyakov-loop quark-gluon plasma (QGP) part and a Mott-hadron resonance gas (MHRG) part. The QGP part uses a PNJL model with a polynomial Polyakov-loop potential and an O(α_s) perturbative virial correction, while the MHRG part uses a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach with a simple step-function phase shift that satisfies the Levinson theorem. The chiral condensate temperature dependence is taken from lattice QCD fits rather than solved dynamically, and the Polyakov loop is determined by solving the stationarity condition (gap equation) including the perturbative contribution for the first time.

## Key numbers
- The Mott dissociation of hadrons occurs in the narrow temperature interval of 150–190 MeV, where hadrons completely vanish from the system at T ≈ 190 MeV.
- The pseudocritical temperature for chiral restoration is T_c = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV, with width δ_T = 26 MeV for Wuppertal-Budapest data and δ_T = 22.7 MeV for HotQCD data.
- The Polyakov-loop susceptibility peaks at T_φ = 159.0 MeV, while the chiral susceptibility peaks at T_χ = 156.5 MeV, showing strong synchronization.
- The model reproduces lattice QCD pressure up to T = 1960 MeV as reported by Bazavov (2017).
- The strange quark mass is set to m_s = 100 MeV and the light current-quark mass to m_l = 5.5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach previously applied only to low-lying mesons (Hufner 1994, Zhuang 1994, Blaschke 2013) by including all hadronic channels from the PDG and adding O(α_s) perturbative corrections. It improves on earlier versions (Blaschke 2016) by solving the complete gap equation for the Polyakov loop including the perturbative contribution. The model enables predictions for the QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential, including possible critical endpoints, which cannot be addressed by two-phase approaches using Maxwell construction or switch functions between HRG and QGP phases (Plumberg 2018, Parotto 2018).
