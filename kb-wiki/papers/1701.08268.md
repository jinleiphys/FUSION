# Derivative Expansion of Wave Function Equivalent Potentials

> Properties of the wave function equivalent potentials introduced by HAL QCD collaboration are studied in a non-relativistic coupled-channel model. The derivative expansion is generalized, and then applied to the energy-independent and non-local potentials. The expansion coefficients are determined from analytic solutions to the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions. The scattering phase shifts computed from these potentials are compared with the exact values to examine the convergence of the expansion. It is confirmed that the generalized derivative expansion converges in terms of the scattering phase shift rather than the functional structure of the non-local potentials. It is also found that the convergence can be improved by tuning either the choice of interpolating fields or expansion scale in the generalized derivative expansion.

# Derivative Expansion of Wave Function Equivalent Potentials

## Key claim

The generalized derivative expansion of HAL QCD potentials converges in terms of scattering phase shifts rather than in the functional structure of the non-local potentials themselves, and convergence can be improved by tuning either the choice of interpolating fields or the Gaussian expansion scale parameter.

## Method

The authors employed a 1+1 dimensional non-relativistic coupled-channel model introduced by Birse (2012), which provides analytic Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. They generalized the derivative expansion by replacing the delta function kernel with a Gaussian kernel of width rho, allowing application to unsmooth wave functions, and determined expansion coefficients by solving a matrix equation from the Schrodinger equation at multiple energy levels in a finite box under twisted boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: $MV_0 = 1/R^2$, $M\Delta = 6/R^2$, $Mg = 6/R$, with $R=1$ and $M=1$
- A single bound state exists at $E = E_0 \simeq -33.7$ (in model units)
- Box size $L=10$ with twist angle $\theta = \pi/2$
- Field admixture parameter range: $|q| \leq 1.0$
- Gaussian expansion scale range: $0.1 \leq \rho \leq 0.7$
- Reference values: $q = +0.2$, $\rho = 0.5$
- At truncation order $N=5$, extrapolation region extends to $E_5 < E < E_{13}$ (length $0.58\Delta$)
- At $N=1$, extrapolation region is $E_1 < E < E_3$ (length $0.03\Delta$)
- Singular part strengths at $N=5$: $g_0 = -4.20$, $g_1 = 0.54$, $g_2 = -0.05$, $g_3 = -0.03$, $g_4 = 0.04$, $g_5 = 0.09$

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009) for deriving nuclear forces from lattice QCD, where energy-independent non-local potentials are defined from NBS wave functions. The study generalizes the derivative expansion approach previously applied by Murano (2011) to examine energy dependence of HAL QCD potentials, and uses the analytic coupled-channel model of Birse (2012) to enable precise higher-order evaluation. The results enable systematic improvement of derivative expansion convergence in lattice QCD calculations of hadron-hadron interactions without additional computational cost, by tuning either the interpolating fields or the expansion scale parameter.
