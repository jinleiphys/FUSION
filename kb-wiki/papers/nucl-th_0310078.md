# Exclusive $ϕ$ production in proton-proton collisions in the resonance model

> The exclusive $ϕ$ meson production in proton-proton reactions is calculated within the resonance model. The considered model was already successfully applied to the description of $π$, $η$, $ρ$, $ω$, $ππ$ production in proton-proton collisions. The only new parameter entering into the model is the $ω-ϕ$ mixing angle $θ_{mix}$ which is taken equal to $θ_{mix} \approx 3.7^o$.

## Key claim

The resonance model, previously successful for other mesons, reproduces the experimentally observed cross section for exclusive φ production in pp collisions without introducing any new free parameters beyond the ω-φ mixing angle (θ_mix ≈ 3.7°), thereby explaining the order-of-magnitude violation of the naive OZI rule expectation.

## Method

The calculation uses a two-step mechanism: excitation of nucleon resonances (all well-established N* resonances with masses below 2 GeV) in pp → pR, followed by their decay R → pφ. The φNR coupling is derived from the known ωNR coupling via the ω-φ mixing angle, where the ωNR couplings were previously determined within the extended Vector Meson Dominance (eVMD) model using data on electro- and photo-production of nucleon resonances and their mesonic decays. The cross section is obtained by integrating over the running masses of the resonance and the φ meson, using Breit-Wigner distributions and an energy-dependent φ width.

## Key numbers

- The ω-φ mixing angle is taken as θ_mix ≈ 3.7°.
- The naive OZI expectation for the ratio of φ to ω production cross sections is R_φ/ω = tan²(θ_mix) · F ≈ 4.2 × 10⁻³ · F, where F is a phase-space correction factor.
- The experimentally observed ratio R_φ/ω in pp → ppφ(ω) reactions is one order of magnitude larger than this naive expectation.
- Off-shell ω contributions to the total ω cross section are less than 10% at energies above the φ meson production threshold.

## Context

This work extends the resonance model, previously applied to π, η, ρ, ω, and ππ production in NN collisions (Teis; Fuchs03), to φ meson production. It contradicts the naive OZI rule estimate (OZI) and explains the large discrepancy observed experimentally (DISTO; Blo75; Bal77; Are82; Gol97) without additional parameters. The model enables further tests by comparison of invariant mass distributions of the proton-φ system once experimental data become available.
