# Acceptance Dependence of Fluctuation in Particle Multiplicity

> The effect of limiting the acceptance in rapidity on event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions has been investigated. Our analysis shows that the multiplicity fluctuations decrease when the rapidity acceptance is decreased. We explain this trend by assuming that the probability distribution of the particles in the smaller acceptance window follows binomial distribution. Following a simple statistical analysis we conclude that the event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations for full acceptance are likely to be larger than those observed in the experiments, since the experiments usually have detectors with limited acceptance. We discuss the application of our model to simulated data generated using VENUS, a widely used event generator in heavy-ion collisions. We also discuss the results from our calculations in presence of dynamical fluctuations and possible observation of these in the actual data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations (quantified by \(W = \langle\Delta N^2\rangle/\langle N\rangle\)) decrease when the rapidity acceptance window is reduced, contrary to naive expectations; the authors qualify that this trend can be explained by assuming a binomial distribution for particles in the smaller window, and that the "actual" fluctuation for full acceptance is likely larger than what experiments with limited acceptance measure.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the fluctuation \(W\) in a smaller rapidity window \(\Delta\eta\) assuming the particle distribution in that window follows either a binomial or Gaussian distribution, with the total multiplicity in the full rapidity range distributed normally. They then tested the binomial model by analyzing 45,000 VENUS 4.12 simulated Pb+Pb events at 158\(\cdot A\) GeV, selecting events in narrow 5% bins of transverse energy (\(E_T\)) to minimize impact-parameter fluctuations, and comparing the fluctuation \(W\) computed for a 1.0-unit rapidity window to the model prediction using the full 2.0-unit window value \(W_0\).

## Key numbers

* For VENUS simulated Pb+Pb events at 158\(\cdot A\) GeV, the fluctuation \(W\) in a 1.0-unit rapidity window was found to be less than \(W_0\) (the fluctuation for the full 2.0-unit window [2.0, 4.0]) for all transverse energy bins (0–5%, 5–10%, …, 55–60% of minimum-bias cross section).
* The transverse energy resolution used in the fast simulation was 17.9%\(/\sqrt{E}\) for electromagnetic energy and 46.1%\(/\sqrt{E}\) for hadronic energy, where \(E\) is in GeV.
* For a model with \(W_0 = 2\) and probability \(p = 0.4\), the fluctuation in limited acceptance reduces to 1.4; when dynamical fluctuations are present with \(q = 0.8\), the fluctuation increases as the fraction of events with dynamical fluctuation (\(1-\beta\)) increases above 10%.

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously measured by NA49 and WA98, by investigating the previously unexamined dependence on rapidity acceptance. The authors note that thermal models (e.g., the thermal model) and the wounded-nucleon model both predict a value of \(W\) close to 2 but do not address acceptance dependence, and that a recent theoretical calculation on conserved charge fluctuations (charge_fluc) emphasized the importance of acceptance effects. The results enable future experimental analyses to correct for limited acceptance when interpreting fluctuation measurements and provide a method to detect dynamical fluctuations (e.g., from QGP formation) by comparing data to mixed events constructed from the same data.
