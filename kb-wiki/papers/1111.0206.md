# Calculation of the Structure Properties of Asymmetrical Nuclear Matter

> In this paper the structure properties of asymmetrical nuclear matter has been calculated employing AV18 potential for different values of proton to neutron ratio. These calculations have been also made for the case of symmetrical nuclear matter with UV14, AV14 and AV18 potentials. In our calculations, we use the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to compute the correlation function of the system.

# Calculation of the Structure Properties of Asymmetrical Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The LOCV method, applied with the AV18 potential, can reproduce the correlation function, pair distribution function, and structure function of asymmetrical nuclear matter across different proton-to-neutron ratios, with results that agree overall with prior Reid-potential calculations. The authors hedge that the structure function of the non-interacting Fermi gas is reduced by nucleon-nucleon interaction, and that the behavior at the np channel differs substantially from the nn and pp channels.

## Method
The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to compute the two-body correlation function for nuclear matter, minimizing the two-body cluster energy subject to a normalization constraint that introduces a Lagrange multiplier. They solved the resulting coupled and uncoupled Euler-Lagrange differential equations numerically for the AV18 potential (asymmetrical matter) and for UV14, AV14, and AV18 potentials (symmetrical matter). The pair distribution function was obtained from the lowest-order cluster expansion g(r) = f²(r) g_F(r), and the static structure function S(k) was computed via the Fourier transform of g(r) – 1.

## Key numbers
- For symmetrical nuclear matter at density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the correlation function is zero at internucleon distance r < 0.06 fm for all three potentials (UV14, AV14, AV18), representing the hard core of the nucleon-nucleon potential.
- The correlation function approaches unity at approximately r > 3.8 fm for symmetrical nuclear matter at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For asymmetrical nuclear matter with AV18 potential at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the pair distribution function approaches unity at approximately r > 4 fm.
- The pair distribution function for symmetrical nuclear matter at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ shows that for r in the range 1.1 fm to 3.4 fm, the AV18 result is greater than those for UV14 and AV14 potentials.
- The structure function of symmetrical nuclear matter at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is reduced relative to the non-interacting Fermi gas system for all three potentials.

## Context
This work extends the LOCV calculations of Bordbar and Modarres (2010) for asymmetrical nuclear matter with the AV18 potential, and of Bordbar and Bigdeli (2008) for spin-polarized systems, by computing the static structure function and pair distribution function in addition to the correlation function. The results agree overall with earlier calculations using the Reid potential (Modarres, 1993). The method enables future studies of density fluctuations and response functions in asymmetric matter relevant to neutron star crusts and heavy-ion collisions, and can be extended to finite-temperature calculations as done by Modarres (1993, 1995, 1997).
