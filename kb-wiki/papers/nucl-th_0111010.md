# Playing Dice with Nuclei: Pattern out of Randomness?

> We review recent results obtained in numerical studies of the nuclear shell model and the interacting boson model with random interactions, in particular the dominance of ground states with L=0 and the occurrence of vibrational and rotational bands. We discuss some possible explanations of the origin of these regular features.

# Playing Dice with Nuclei: Pattern out of Randomness?

## Key claim
Numerical studies of the nuclear shell model and the interacting boson model with random two-body interactions show a strong statistical preference (over 60%) for ground states with angular momentum and parity J^P = 0^+, and in the IBM case also produce vibrational and rotational band structures, even though the interactions are chosen randomly with no particular physical form. The authors note that no definitive explanation for these regular features is yet available, and the full implications for nuclear structure physics remain to be clarified.

## Method
The authors review numerical experiments using the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE), where two-body matrix elements are drawn from a Gaussian distribution of random numbers with zero mean, satisfying hermiticity, time-reversal, rotational, and reflection invariance. For the shell model, calculations were performed for six valence neutrons in the sd shell (nucleus ²²O); for the interacting boson model (IBM), calculations used N=16 bosons with random one- and two-body interactions. In each case, the many-body Hamiltonian matrices were constructed for each angular momentum, diagonalized, and the resulting spectra analyzed for ground-state angular momenta and energy ratios, repeated for approximately 1,000 random realizations.

## Key numbers
- For six neutrons in the sd shell (²²O), the ground state has J=0 in 67.7% of random realizations, compared to only 9.9% of basis states being J=0 (a seven-fold enhancement).
- For the IBM with N=16 bosons, the ground state has J=0 in 63.4% of cases, compared to only 3.3% of basis states (a twenty-fold enhancement).
- The probability distribution P(R) of the energy ratio R = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) for the IBM shows two pronounced peaks: one at R ~ 1.9 (close to the harmonic vibrator value of 2.0) and one at R ~ 3.3 (close to the rotor value of 10/3 ≈ 3.33).
- For six neutrons in the sd shell, P(R) shows a broad peak between R = 1 and R = 2 with a maximum around R = 1.3, with no evidence for rotational bands.
- Breaking time-reversal invariance (using GUE instead of GOE) increases the dominance of 0⁺ ground states from 67.7% to 76.8% for six identical nucleons in the sd shell.
- The mean-field Hartree-Bose analysis of the IBM shows J=0 ground states dominate for approximately 63-77% of cases, with the sum of J=0 and J=2 ground state percentages nearly independent of boson number N.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the surprising findings of Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (2000) who first showed that random two-body interactions in the shell model produce a strong preference for 0⁺ ground states, contradicting the conventional belief that such regular patterns require specific forms of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (e.g., pairing forces as in Talmi's work, or quadrupole-quadrupole interactions). The results also relate to the tripartite classification of even-even nuclei into seniority, anharmonic vibrator, and rotor regimes identified by Zamfir and collaborators. The review discusses possible explanations including geometric chaoticity (Mulhall, Volya, and Zelevinsky) and mean-field analyses (Bijker and Frank), but concludes that no definitive answer exists. The work opens questions about the emergence of collective behavior from random interactions, the transition from order to chaos, and connections to random matrix theory and quantum chaos.
