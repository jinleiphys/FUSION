# Charge symmetry breaking in mirror nuclei from quarks

> The binding energy differences of the valence proton and neutron of the mirror nuclei, $^{15}$O -- $^{15}$N, $^{17}$F -- $^{17}$O, $^{39}$Ca -- $^{39}$K and $^{41}$Sc -- $^{41}$Ca, are calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The calculation involves nuclear structure and shell effects explicitly. It is shown that binding energy differences of a few hundred keV arise from the strong interaction, even after subtracting all electromagnetic corrections. The origin of these differences may be ascribed to the charge symmetry breaking effects set in the strong interaction through the u and d current quark mass difference.

## Key claim

The binding energy differences of a few hundred keV observed in mirror nuclei (the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly) can be explained by charge symmetry breaking in the strong interaction arising from the u and d current quark mass difference, as calculated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with explicit nuclear structure and shell effects.

## Method

The authors solved a set of coupled, non-linear differential equations for static, spherically symmetric nuclei using the QMC model, where charge symmetry is explicitly broken at the quark level through different u and d current quark masses. The Dirac equations for quarks in proton and neutron bags were solved self-consistently with scalar (σ), vector (ω), and isovector (ρ) mean fields, plus Coulomb potentials, for the mirror pairs ¹⁵O–¹⁵N, ¹⁷F–¹⁷O, ³⁹Ca–³⁹K, and ⁴¹Sc–⁴¹Ca. The ρ-meson central and spin-orbit contributions to valence single-particle energies were evaluated perturbatively using the calculated wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy difference for the valence proton in ¹⁷F and neutron in ¹⁷O: approximately 0.18 MeV for the 1d₅/₂ state, from the CSB calculation.
- ρ-potential contributions to binding energy differences: about –0.11 MeV for ¹⁵O–¹⁵N, –0.011 MeV for ¹⁷F–¹⁷O, –0.17 MeV for ³⁹Ca–³⁹K, and –0.013 MeV for ⁴¹Sc–⁴¹Ca.
- Symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties used: binding energy of 15.7 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³.
- Phenomenological ρ-quark coupling constant used for CSB and SU(2) calculations: g^q_ρ = 4.595 (corresponding to g²_ρNN/4π = 4 × 0.42 at zero three-momentum transfer).
- Empirical isovector magnetic moment used: μ^{emp.}_v = 4.7051.
- Bag radius in free space: R = 0.8 fm.
- Electromagnetic self-energy corrections: +0.63 MeV for the proton, –0.13 MeV for the neutron.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly, where calculated binding energy differences of mirror nuclei disagree with measurements after electromagnetic corrections. It extends earlier quark-based calculations by Wil (1993), Hen (1994), Hatsuda (1994), Adami (1994), Fio (1995), Saito (1997), and Sch (1997), and improves upon an earlier exploratory QMC study by Saito (1997) that used a local density approximation rather than a consistent shell model calculation. The results enable future comparison with the traditional ρ–ω mixing mechanism for charge symmetry breaking, as discussed by Blu (1993), and raise the question of a possible deeper connection between these apparently different sources of charge symmetry violation.
