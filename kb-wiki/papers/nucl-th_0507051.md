# Wavelet Methods in the Relativistic Three-Body Problem

> In this paper we discuss the use of wavelet bases to solve the relativistic three-body problem. Wavelet bases can be used to transform momentum-space scattering integral equations into an approximate system of linear equations with a sparse matrix. This has the potential to reduce the size of realistic three-body calculations with minimal loss of accuracy. The wavelet method leads to a clean, interaction independent treatment of the scattering singularities which does not require any subtractions.

## Key claim

Wavelet bases can transform the momentum-space integral equations of the relativistic three-body problem into an approximate system of linear equations with a sparse matrix, potentially reducing the size of realistic calculations with minimal loss of accuracy, provided that the moving singularities inherent to the three-body problem are handled by a specific change of variables that separates the momentum dependence in the denominator.

## Method

The authors transform the Faddeev-Lovelace equation for three particles of mass m by multiplying numerator and denominator by a non-zero factor to separate the p and q dependence in the singular denominator, then change variables to x and y so that the denominator becomes z' - x - y + i0⁺. They project the smooth kernel and driving term onto an approximation space V_k using Daubechies' K=3 wavelets, evaluate the projected matrix elements at one-point quadrature points, and compute the singular integrals J^k_{m,n}(z) analytically using the scaling equation. The resulting algebraic system is then transformed via the wavelet transform, small matrix elements are discarded to produce a sparse matrix, and the system is solved using sparse matrix iterative techniques.

## Key numbers

- Daubechies' K=3 wavelets are used, with six scaling coefficients h_l given explicitly in Table 1 (e.g., h_0 = (1+√10+√(5+2√10))/16√2).
- The scaling function has support on the interval [0, 2K-1] = [0,5] for K=3.
- A test of the double expansion approximation for x⁴ at resolution k=-5 shows essentially exact agreement except near x=0, where at x=0 the approximation gives 1.757732×10⁻⁶ versus the exact 0.000000×10⁰.
- The one-point quadrature point for φ_{km}(x) is x_m = 2^k(⟨x⟩+m) with ⟨x⟩ = (1/√2)∑_{l=1}^{2K-1} l h_l.

## Context

This paper is the third in a series (Kessler 2003, Kessler 2004) that investigates wavelet numerical analysis for scattering problems, extending the method from the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Malfliet Tjon V potential to the relativistic three-body problem. The key advance over the prior two-body work is the treatment of moving singularities, which are a generic feature of relativistic few-body equations and do not appear in the two-body case. The method enables future calculations at higher energy scales where relativistic treatments are required and where subnuclear degrees of freedom become relevant, and the authors note the formulation is straightforward to extend to systems with explicit mesonic degrees of freedom and stronger forms of cluster properties.
