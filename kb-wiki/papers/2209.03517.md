# Light Curves and Event Rates of Axion Instability Supernovae

> It was recently proposed that exotic particles can trigger a new stellar instability which is analogous to the e-e+ pair instability if they are produced and reach equilibrium in the stellar plasma. In this study, we construct axion instability supernova (AISN) models caused by the new instability to predict their observational signatures. We focus on heavy axion-like particles (ALPs) with masses of ~400 keV--2 MeV and coupling with photons of g_{ag}~10^{-5} GeV^{-1}. It is found that the 56Ni mass and the explosion energy are significantly increased by ALPs for a fixed stellar mass. As a result, the peak times of the light curves of AISNe occur earlier than those of standard pair-instability supernovae by 10--20 days when the ALP mass is equal to the electron mass. Also, the event rate of AISNe is 1.7--2.6 times higher than that of pair-instability supernovae, depending on the high mass cutoff of the initial mass function.

## Key claim

Heavy axion-like particles (ALPs) with masses of approximately 400 keV to 2 MeV and photon couplings of about 10^{-5} GeV^{-1} can trigger a new stellar instability analogous to electron-positron pair instability, producing axion instability supernovae (AISNe) whose light curves peak 10 to 20 days earlier than standard pair-instability supernovae when the ALP mass equals the electron mass.

## Method

The authors constructed stellar evolution and explosion models incorporating ALP production and equilibrium in the stellar plasma, then computed synthetic light curves and event rates for AISNe. They compared these models to standard pair-instability supernova models across a range of progenitor masses to isolate the effects of ALP interactions on nickel-56 yields, explosion energies, and light curve timing.

## Key numbers

- ALP mass range considered: approximately 400 keV to 2 MeV, with photon coupling g_{ag} approximately 10^{-5} GeV^{-1}
- Peak times of AISN light curves occur 10 to 20 days earlier than standard pair-instability supernovae when the ALP mass equals the electron mass
- Event rate of AISNe is 1.7 to 2.6 times higher than that of pair-instability supernovae, depending on the high mass cutoff of the initial mass function

## Context

This work extends the recently proposed idea that exotic particles reaching equilibrium in stellar plasma can trigger a new instability analogous to the electron-positron pair instability. The study provides the first observational predictions for such axion instability supernovae, enabling searches for these events in transient surveys and potentially constraining ALP properties. Future work can compare these predicted light curves and rates to observations from surveys such as the Zwicky Transient Facility or the Vera Rubin Observatory.
