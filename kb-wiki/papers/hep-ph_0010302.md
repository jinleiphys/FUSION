# How Hard Are the Form Factors in Hadronic Vertices with Heavy Mesons?

> The $NDΛ_c$ and $ND^*Λ_c$ form factors are evaluated in a full QCD sum rule calculation. We study the double Borel sum rule for the three point function of one meson one nucleon and one $Λ_c$ current up to order six in the operator product expansion. The double Borel transform is performed with respect to the nucleon and $Λ_c$ momenta, and the form factor is evaluated as a function of the momentum $Q^2$ of the heavy meson. These form factors are relevant to evaluate the charmonium absorption cross section by hadrons. Our results are compatible with constant form factors in these vertices.

## Key claim

The form factors for the $ND\Lambda_c$ and $ND^*\Lambda_c$ vertices are compatible with being constant in the Euclidean momentum region $0 \leq Q^2 \leq 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, implying that the cut-off parameters in these vertices are much larger than the typical $2\ \mathrm{GeV}$ used in phenomenological models, though the authors note that varying the Borel mass can produce a tiny increase rather than a decrease in the form factor over this region.

## Method

The authors performed a full QCD sum rule calculation of the three-point function for the $ND\Lambda_c$ and $ND^*\Lambda_c$ vertices, using currents for the nucleon, $\Lambda_c$, and the heavy meson ($D$ or $D^*$). A double Borel transform was applied with respect to the nucleon and $\Lambda_c$ momenta, and the operator product expansion was carried out up to dimension-six operators (perturbative diagram, quark condensate, and four-quark condensate). The form factors were extracted from specific Dirac structures: $\sigma^{\mu\nu}\gamma_5 p^\prime_\mu p_\nu$ for $g_{ND\Lambda_c}$ and $\rlap{/}{p^\prime}\gamma_\mu\rlap{/}{p}$ for $g_{ND^*\Lambda_c}$, with the Borel parameters related to those of the two-point sum rules for the baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- $g_{ND\Lambda_c}(Q^2) = 7.9 \pm 0.9$, obtained considering 20% variation in continuum thresholds and $f_D = 170 \pm 10\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- $g_{ND^*\Lambda_c}(Q^2) = -7.5 \pm 1.1$, obtained considering 20% variation in continuum thresholds and $f_{D^*} = 240 \pm 20\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The monopole fit for $g_{ND\Lambda_c}(Q^2)$ gives $g_{ND\Lambda_c}(Q^2) = 843/(102 + Q^2)$, corresponding to a cut-off of order $10\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
- The monopole fit for $g_{ND^*\Lambda_c}(Q^2)$ gives $g_{ND^*\Lambda_c}(Q^2) = -204/(26.2 + Q^2)$, corresponding to a cut-off of order $5\ \mathrm{GeV}$.
- Input parameters: $m_c = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $m_D = 1.87\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $m_{D^*} = 2.01\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $M_N = 938\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $M_{\Lambda_c} = 2.285\ \mathrm{GeV}$, $f_D = 170\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $f_{D^*} = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\langle\overline{q}q\rangle = -(0.23)^3\ \mathrm{GeV}^3$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule calculation of the $ND\Lambda_c$ coupling constant (Durães, Navarra, Nielsen, earlier work) by studying the $Q^2$ dependence of the form factor for an off-shell heavy meson, which is the situation relevant for charmonium absorption calculations in meson exchange models such as those of Ko (1997) and Haglin (2000). The results contradict the common assumption of a monopole form factor with a cut-off around $2\ \mathrm{GeV}$ used in those models, and instead support the hypothesis that vertices with a heavy off-shell particle are point-like, consistent with findings for the $D^*D\pi$ vertex with an off-shell $D^*$ by Bel'kov (1993), Belyaev (1995), and Khodjamirian (1999). The constant form factors found here enable more reliable estimates of the $J/\psi$ absorption cross section by hadrons, which is a key ingredient for interpreting $J/\psi$ suppression in heavy ion collisions as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
