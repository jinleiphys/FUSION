# Light-ion production in the interaction of 175 MeV quasi-mono-energetic neutrons with iron and with bismuth

> Nuclear data for neutron-induced reactions in the intermediate energy range of 20 to 200 MeV are of great importance for the development of nuclear reaction codes since little data exist in that range. Also several different applications benefit from such data, notably accelerator-driven incineration of nuclear waste. The Medley setup was used for a series of measurements of p, d, t, $^3$He and $α$-particle production by 175 MeV quasi-mono-energetic neutrons on various target nuclei. The measurements were performed at the The Svedberg Laboratory in Uppsala, Sweden. Eight detector telescopes placed at angles between 20$^\circ$ and 160$^\circ$ were used. Medley uses the $ΔE$-$ΔE$-$E$ technique to discriminate among the particle types and is able to measure double-differential cross sections over a wide range of particle energies. This paper briefly describes the experimental setup, summarizes the data analysis and reports on recent changes in the previously reported preliminary data set on bismuth. Experimental data are compared with INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6 using CEM03.03, TALYS and PHITS model calculations as well as with nuclear data evaluations. The models agree fairly well overall but in some cases systematic differences are found.

# Light-ion production in the interaction of 175 MeV quasi-mono-energetic neutrons with iron and with bismuth

## Key claim
The measured double-differential cross sections for light-ion production (p, d, t, ³He, α) from 175 MeV quasi-mono-energetic neutrons on iron and bismuth agree fairly well overall with four model calculations (INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6 with CEM03.03, TALYS-1.2, and PHITS with MQMD-GEM), but systematic differences exist, notably, TALYS overestimates triton, ³He, and α cross sections, while INCL-ABLA underestimates proton production at forward angles (20°). The previously reported preliminary bismuth data have been revised due to an erroneous thick-target correction.

## Method
The Medley setup at The Svedberg Laboratory (Uppsala, Sweden) used eight ΔE-ΔE-E detector telescopes at angles from 20° to 160° to measure charged particles from thin natural-iron (0.375 mm) and bismuth (0.5 mm) targets irradiated by a quasi-mono-energetic neutron beam with a full-energy peak near 175 MeV. Time-of-flight techniques reduced low-energy tail contributions, but events from neutrons between 80 and 175 MeV could not be separated. Absolute normalization used the (n,p) cross section on a polyethylene target at 20°. Experimental double-differential cross sections were compared to INCL4.5-Abla07, MCNP6 with CEM03.03, TALYS-1.2, and PHITS with MQMD-GEM calculations, all folded with the experimental neutron spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Reference (n,p) cross section used for normalization: σ_H = 19.9 mb sr⁻¹ at 175 MeV and 20°
- Main statistical uncertainty: 5% from (n,p) reference counts; additional uncertainties: 2–3% from beam monitors, 2% from reference cross section
- Thick-target correction systematic uncertainty: up to 20% in low-energy bins, less than 1% at high-energy end
- Low-energy cutoffs: 2 MeV for protons, deuterons, and tritons on Fe; 2 MeV for protons, 3 MeV for deuterons and tritons on Bi; 8 MeV for ³He on both targets; 3 MeV for α on Fe, 14 MeV for α on Bi
- Cross section corrections below cutoff (based on INCL-ABLA): Fe(n,xp) 15 mb, Fe(n,xd) 0.24 mb, Fe(n,xt) 0.18 mb, Fe(n,x³He) 0.40 mb, Fe(n,xα) 0.15 mb; Bi corrections: 0.056 mb (p), 0.0041 mb (d), 0.0031 mb (t), 0.00 mb (³He), 0.36 mb (α)
- Relative magnitude of cutoff corrections: 1–2% for Fe, negligible for Bi except α-production at 0.4%

## Context
This work provides experimental double-differential cross sections for neutron-induced light-ion production in the intermediate energy range (20–200 MeV) where data are scarce, extending earlier measurements at 96 MeV by the same Medley collaboration (Bevilacqua et al., 2011). The data serve as benchmarks for spallation models used in accelerator-driven systems for nuclear waste incineration, and the tritium production measurements specifically address needs of the ANDES project. The results enable validation and improvement of codes such as INCL4.5-Abla07, TALYS, MCNP6 with CEM03.03, and PHITS with MQMD-GEM; ongoing model developments (e.g., extended pre-equilibrium models in CEM and new complex-particle emission models in TALYS by Kalbach, 2005) aim to reduce the systematic discrepancies observed, particularly for composite particle production.
