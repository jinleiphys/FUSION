# Finite-density gauge correlation functions in QC2D

> 2-color QCD is the simplest QCD-like theory which is accessible to lattice simulations at finite density. It therefore plays an important role to test qualitative features and to provide benchmarks to other methods and models, which do not suffer from a sign problem. To this end, we determine the minimal-Landau-gauge propagators and 3-point vertices in this theory over a wide range of densities, the vacuum, and at both finite temperature and density. The results show that there is essentially no modification of the gauge sector in the low-temperature, low-density phase. Even outside this phase only mild modifications appear, mostly in the chromoelectric sector.

## Key claim

The gauge sector of two-color QCD (QC₂D) shows essentially no modification at finite density, even across the silver-blaze phase transition, with only mild changes appearing in the chromoelectric sector at high temperatures; the running coupling remains strong throughout the entire density range studied.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of QC₂D with two flavors of unimproved Wilson quarks and an unimproved Wilson gauge action, using ensembles at multiple lattice spacings (β = 1.6, 1.7, 1.9, 2.1) and volumes. They fixed configurations to minimal Landau gauge and computed the gluon and ghost propagators (both soft and hard modes), the ghost-gluon and three-gluon vertices, and derived the running coupling in the miniMOM scheme, for a range of chemical potentials up to approximately 1.2 GeV at zero and finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 717(25) MeV at β = 1.9 (κ = 0.1680) and 668(6) MeV at β = 1.7 (κ = 0.178)
- Lattice spacings: a = 0.229 fm (β = 1.7), a = 0.186 fm (β = 1.9), a = 0.138 fm (β = 2.1), a = 0.266 fm (β = 1.6, estimated)
- Critical temperature for QC₂D: T_c = 217(23) MeV
- Silver-blaze transition occurs at μ ≈ 375 MeV
- A slow increase in screening masses appears at μ ≈ 750 MeV on the coarser lattice (β = 1.9), but no such increase is seen at β = 2.1
- The Schwinger function zero crossing remains at about the same time scale of 1 fm at all densities

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the gluon propagator alone in QC₂D (Hands 2006, Boz 2013) to include the ghost propagator, three-point vertices, and the running coupling over a wider range of densities and at both finite temperature and density. The results provide benchmarks for functional methods and models (Nickel 2006, Marhauser 2006, Braun 2008, Fischer 2018) that assume the gauge sector is only slightly modified at low temperatures and high densities, and confirm that such approximations are well justified. The findings also agree with simulations using staggered fermions (Astrakhantsev 2018, Bornyakov 2017) that no phase transition occurs at zero temperature in the chemical-potential range studied.
