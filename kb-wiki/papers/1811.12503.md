# Quarkyonic Matter and Neutron Stars

> We consider Quarkyonic Matter to naturally explain the observed properties of neutron stars. We argue that such matter might exist at densities close to that of nuclear matter and at the onset, the pressure and the sound velocity in Quarkyonic matter increase rapidly. In the limit of large number of quark colors $N_c$, this transition is characterized by a discontinuous change in pressure as a function of baryon number density. We make a simple model of Quarkyonic matter and show that generically the sound velocity is a non-monotonic function of density -- it reaches a maximum at relatively low density, decreases, and then increases again to its asymptotic value of $1/\sqrt{3}$.

## Key claim

Quarkyonic matter, where quarks fill the Fermi sea and nucleons emerge as confined triplets only near the Fermi surface, can naturally explain the rapid increase in sound speed required by neutron star observations, but the authors caution that uncertainties in both the neutron matter and Quarkyonic matter equations of state are too large to make discernible predictions for neutron star structure.

## Method

The authors construct a simple phenomenological model of Quarkyonic matter for symmetric nuclear matter and for neutron matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They assume a momentum shell of width Δ near the quark Fermi surface where baryons reside, with Δ parameterized as Δ = Λ³/k_FB² + κΛ/N_c², and compute the baryon density, energy density, pressure, and sound speed from thermodynamic relations. For neutron matter, they incorporate a fit to microscopic calculations of neutron matter interactions from Gandolfi (2013) with an interaction energy V_n(n_n) = ã n_n (n_n/n_0) + b̃ n_n (n_n/n_0)².

## Key numbers

- The transition to Quarkyonic matter occurs at baryon density n_B = 0.24 fm⁻³ for the neutron matter model with Λ = 380 MeV and κ = 0.3.
- The maximum sound speed in Quarkyonic neutron matter is c_s ≃ 0.94, reached at n_B = 0.64 fm⁻³.
- The interaction energy coefficients for neutron matter are ã = -28.6 ± 1.2 MeV and b̃ = 9.9 ± 3.7 MeV, corresponding to a symmetry energy of 32 MeV and pressure P(n_0) = 2.4 MeV/fm³.
- The confinement scale is set to Λ = 300 MeV for symmetric matter and Λ = 380 MeV for neutron matter, with κ = 0.3 in both cases.
- The asymptotic sound velocity in Quarkyonic matter is 1/√3.

## Context

This work extends the Quarkyonic matter concept introduced by McLerran (2007) and Hidaka (2008) to provide a concrete equation of state for neutron stars. It contrasts with traditional approaches such as non-relativistic nuclear Hamiltonians from chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2008) which are only reliable up to about 2n_0, and relativistic mean field models (Serot and Walecka 1997) which require large vector potentials. The model offers an alternative to first-order quark-hadron transitions (Glendenning 1992) that typically soften the equation of state, and instead produces a second-order transition with rapidly increasing pressure. Future work could generalize the model using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio frameworks (Alford 1997) to dynamically generate baryons at the Fermi surface, and investigate whether the baryonic nature of low-energy excitations in Quarkyonic matter leads to transport properties (such as neutrino cooling) that are observationally distinguishable from ordinary nuclear matter.
