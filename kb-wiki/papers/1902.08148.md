# The strong running coupling from the gauge sector of Domain Wall lattice QCD with physical quark masses

> We report on the first computation of the strong running coupling at the physical point (physical pion mass) from the ghost-gluon vertex, computed from lattice simulations with three flavors of Domain Wall fermions. We find $α_{\overline{\rm MS}}(m_Z^2)=0.1172(11)$, in remarkably good agreement with the world-wide average. Our computational bridge to this value is the Taylor-scheme strong coupling, which has been revealed of great interest by itself because it can be directly related to the quark-gluon interaction kernel in continuum approaches to the QCD bound-state problem.

## Key claim

The authors report the first computation of the strong running coupling at the physical point (physical pion mass) from the ghost-gluon vertex using lattice QCD with three flavors of Domain Wall fermions, finding α_MS-bar(m_Z^2)=0.1172(11). The result is qualified by noting that the central value was obtained by taking the condensate value from literature rather than fitting it, and that a systematic uncertainty from higher-order nonperturbative corrections is estimated separately.

## Method

The Taylor-scheme strong coupling α_T(k^2) was computed from lattice simulations of Landau-gauge ghost and gluon propagators using the relation α_T = (g_0^2/4π) F^2 D, where F and D are the ghost and gluon dressing functions. Discretization artifacts were removed by applying H(4)-extrapolation for O(4)-breaking effects and identifying O(a^2)-corrections through comparison of results from two ensembles at the physical point with different lattice spacings. The running coupling data above 3 GeV were then described by a four-loop perturbative expression supplemented by a leading OPE non-perturbative correction from the dimension-two gluon condensate, allowing extraction of Λ_T and conversion to Λ_MS-bar and finally α_MS-bar(m_Z^2).

## Key numbers

- α_MS-bar(m_Z^2) = 0.1172(11), with the error decomposed as 0.1172(3)(9)(5) from lattice uncertainty, condensate uncertainty, and higher-order nonperturbative corrections respectively
- Λ_MS-bar = 320(4)(13) MeV, where the first error propagates the α_T uncertainty at the largest momentum and the second propagates the condensate uncertainty
- The gluon condensate used was g^2_T(q_0^2)⟨A^2⟩_{R,q_0^2} = 4.1(1.1) GeV^2, obtained as a weighted average of N_f=2 and N_f=4 results from prior work
- The lattice spacings were a^{-1} = 1.7295(38) GeV (β=2.13) and a^{-1} = 2.3586(70) GeV (β=2.25) for the physical point ensembles
- The physical pion masses were 139.4 MeV and 139.2 MeV for the two physical point ensembles

## Context

This work extends a long-term program that previously determined Λ_MS-bar for N_f=0 (Boucaud et al., 2008), N_f=2 (Blossier et al., 2010), and N_f=2+1+1 (Blossier et al., 2011, 2012, 2013) using the same Taylor-scheme ghost-gluon method but with twisted-mass fermions at heavier pion masses. The new result at the physical point with Domain Wall fermions (which have better chiral properties) updates the earlier N_f=2+1+1 value of 0.1200(14) and lies closer to the FLAG lattice average of 0.1182(12) and the PDG non-lattice average of 0.1174(16). The Taylor coupling itself can be directly related to the quark-gluon interaction kernel in continuum approaches to the QCD bound-state problem, as established by Binosi et al. (2014, 2016, 2018) and Rodriguez-Quintero et al. (2018).
