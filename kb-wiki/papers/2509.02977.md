# The role of the soft scale for $J/ψ$ production in the transverse momentum dependent framework

> We use vNRQCD to study power corrections in the ${\rm v}$ expansion due to soft gluon radiation during $J/ψ$ production at small transverse momentum. We categorize four new $J/ψ$ production operators that mediate the transition of perturbatively produced color-octet charm quark/anti-quark pairs to charm quarks in a $^3S_1^{[1]}$ state via soft gluon emission. We then use Soft Collinear Effective Theory and vNRQCD to derive a factorization theorem for $J/ψ$ production in SIDIS in terms of the gluon transverse momentum dependent (TMD) PDFs in the proton and new objects which we call TMD soft transition functions. We show that the TMD soft transition function leads in the ${\rm v}$ power-counting with respect to the color-octet TMD shape functions that have been used in previous studies of $J/ψ$ production at small transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the vNRQCD framework, new TMD soft transition functions (TMDSTFs) describing the transition of color-octet charm quark pairs to a \(^3S_1^{[1]}\) state via soft gluon emission are leading in the \(\vv\) power-counting compared to the color-octet TMD shape functions used in prior studies, but only in the regime where the \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum is small and TMD factorization applies.

## Method

The authors expand tree-level QCD amplitudes for a \(c\bar{c}\) pair radiating an arbitrary number of soft gluons to next-to-leading order in the \(\vv\) expansion, then match these expressions onto vNRQCD operators. They use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) and vNRQCD to derive a factorization theorem for \(J/\psi\) production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) at small transverse momentum, expressing the hadronic tensor in terms of gluon transverse momentum dependent (TMD) PDFs in the proton and the new TMD soft transition functions.

## Key numbers

* The soft scale for charmonium is \(m_Q \vv \sim 0.75 - 1.5\) GeV, where \(\vv^2 \approx 0.3\) for the \(J/\psi\).
* The ultrasoft scale is \(m_Q \vv^2 \sim 300 - 500\) MeV \(\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\).
* The color-singlet LDME \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[1]}) \rangle\) scales as \(\vv^3\).
* The color-octet LDMEs \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3S_1^{[8]}) \rangle\), \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^1S_0^{[8]}) \rangle\), and \(\langle \mathcal{O}^{J/\psi}(^3P_0^{[8]}) \rangle\) each scale as \(\vv^7\) in traditional NRQCD.
* The color-singlet LDME fit from Butenschoen and Kniehl (2011, 2012) is \(1.32 \pm 0.20\) GeV\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends the vNRQCD approach of Fleming et al. (2019) and the TMD shape function framework of Echevarria et al. (2024) by deriving new subleading-power production operators that account for soft gluon radiation during \(J/\psi\) production. It addresses the long-standing confusion in NRQCD about the role of soft versus ultrasoft gluons, which was previously tackled by pNRQCD (Brambilla et al., 1999, 2004) and vNRQCD (Luke et al., 1999; Rothstein, 2018). The new TMDSTFs enable a consistent treatment of quarkonium production at small transverse momentum, potentially resolving discrepancies in color-octet LDME extractions and the \(J/\psi\) polarization puzzle.
