# Surface tension of dense matter at the chiral phase transition

> If a first-order phase transition separates nuclear and quark matter at large baryon density, an interface between these two phases has a nonzero surface tension. We calculate this surface tension within a nucleon-meson model for domain walls and bubbles. Various methods and approximations are discussed and compared, including a numerical evaluation of the spatial profile of the interface. We also compute the surface tension at the other first-order phase transitions of the model: the nuclear liquid-gas transition and, in the parameter regime where it exists, the direct transition from the vacuum to the (approximately) chirally symmetric phase. Identifying the chirally symmetric phase with quark matter - our model does not contain explicit quark degrees of freedom - we find maximal surface tensions of the vacuum-quark transition $Σ_{\rm VQ}\sim 15 \, {\rm MeV}/{\rm fm}^2$, relevant for the surface of quark stars, and of the nuclear-quark transition $Σ_{\rm NQ}\sim 10 \, {\rm MeV}/{\rm fm}^2$, relevant for hybrid stars and for quark matter nucleation in supernovae and neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The surface tension of the interface between nuclear matter and chirally symmetric matter (identified with quark matter) is calculated within a nucleon-meson model that does not contain explicit quark degrees of freedom, so the chirally restored phase is only a rough approximation of dense quark matter; the maximal values found are approximately 10 MeV/fm² for the nuclear-quark transition and 15 MeV/fm² for the vacuum-quark transition.

## Method

The authors employ a nucleon-meson model in the mean-field approximation with the no-sea approximation and the Thomas-Fermi approximation, integrating out fermionic fields to obtain the free energy density. They solve the Euler-Lagrange equations for the sigma and omega condensates numerically via successive over-relaxation to obtain domain wall and bubble profiles, and compare these full numerical results with several approximate methods that require only numerical integration rather than solving differential equations. The model parameters are matched to nuclear matter properties at saturation density, including the saturation density n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, binding energy E_bind = -16.3 MeV, effective Dirac mass M₀ in the range (0.7-0.8) m_N, and incompressibility K in the range (200-300) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximal surface tension of the vacuum-quark transition: Σ_VQ ∼ 15 MeV/fm²
- Maximal surface tension of the nuclear-quark transition: Σ_NQ ∼ 10 MeV/fm²
- Saturation density used for parameter matching: n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Binding energy at saturation: E_bind = -16.3 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π ≃ 93 MeV
- Omega meson mass: m_ω ≃ 782 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 139 MeV
- Vacuum nucleon mass: m_N = 939 MeV
- Surface tension of nuclear matter at saturation (used as constraint): Σ₀ ≃ (1.0 - 1.2) MeV fm⁻²

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of the surface tension at the chiral phase transition, which were either performed in chiral models lacking nuclear matter or employed two different models for nuclear and quark matter glued together at the phase transition. The authors use a nucleon-meson model following Boguta (1982, 1986), Floerchinger (2012), and Drews (2013) that contains realistic nuclear matter matched to saturation properties. The results are relevant for bubble nucleation of quark matter in supernovae and neutron star mergers, for the possible quark-hadron mixed phase in neutron star interiors as studied by Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg (1992), and for the surface of quark stars and strangelet dwarfs as discussed by Alcock (1986) and Alford (2008, 2011).
