# Hoyle-analog state in ${}^{13}$N

> We investigate the cluster structure of $\rm {}^{13}N$ using a microscopic $α+α+α+p$ four-body cluster model. The calculated spectra agree well with the observed spectra in the low-lying states. We calculate the reduced width amplitudes and spectroscopic factors to investigate the Hoyle-analog state in $\rm {}^{13}N$. Our calculations show that the $3/2_3^-$ state at $E_x=10.8$ MeV is primarily constituted by $\rm {}^{12}C(0^+_2)+\mathit{p}$ and $\rm {}^{9}B(3/2^-)+α$ components. This finding is generally consistent with the newly observed $3/2^-$ state at $E_x=11.3$ MeV via the $3α+ \mathit{p}$ decay channel. Moreover, considering the calculated root-mean-square radius and isoscalar monopole transition strengths, the $3/2_3^-$ state emerges as a candidate for the Hoyle-analog state with the $\rm {}^{12}C(0^+_2)+\mathit{p}$ cluster structure.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the $3/2_3^-$ state in ${}^{13}$N at an excitation energy of 10.8 MeV is a candidate for the Hoyle-analog state, based on its calculated root-mean-square radius, isoscalar monopole transition strength, and cluster structure composition. This theoretical identification is generally consistent with a newly observed $3/2^-$ state at 11.3 MeV, but the paper's calculated energy is 0.5 MeV lower than the experimental value.

## Method
The authors performed a microscopic generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation using an $\alpha+\alpha+\alpha+p$ four-body cluster model for ${}^{13}$N. They superposed 1000 basis wave functions with randomly generated coordinates for the three alpha clusters and the valence proton, using the Volkov No.2 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and the G3RS spin-orbit interaction. Reduced width amplitudes (RWAs) and spectroscopic factors were calculated for the ${}^{12}$C + $p$ and ${}^{9}$B + $\alpha$ channels to analyze the cluster structure of each state.

## Key numbers
- The $3/2_3^-$ state is calculated at an excitation energy $E_x = 10.8$ MeV, compared to a newly observed $3/2^-$ state at $E_x = 11.3$ MeV.
- The $3/2_3^-$ state has a spectroscopic factor of $S^2 = 0.31$ in the $[{}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_2^+) \otimes p_{1/2}]_{1/2} \otimes 1$ channel and $S^2 = 0.73$ in the $[{}^{9}\mathrm{B}(3/2^-) \otimes \alpha]_{3/2} \otimes 0$ channel.
- The ground state ($1/2_1^-$) has an r.m.s. radius of 2.46 fm, while the $3/2_3^-$ state shows a significantly larger r.m.s. radius exceeding 3.0 fm.
- The $1/2_2^-$ state has a spectroscopic factor of $S^2 = 0.91$ in the $[{}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+) \otimes p_{1/2}]_{1/2} \otimes 1$ channel with one node.
- The $3/2_2^-$ state has its largest spectroscopic factor of $S^2 = 0.88$ in the $[{}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+) \otimes p_{1/2}]_{1/2} \otimes 1$ channel.
- The calculated $B(E1, 3/2_1^- \to 1/2_1^+) = 0.016$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ (experiment: 0.036 $e^2$ fm$^2$).
- The calculated $B(E1, 1/2_1^+ \to 1/2_1^-) = 0.0007$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ (experiment: 0.036 $\pm$ 0.004 $e^2$ fm$^2$).

## Context
This work extends the search for Hoyle-analog states from self-conjugate $4N$ nuclei like ${}^{16}$O and ${}^{20}$Ne into non-self-conjugate nuclei, specifically ${}^{13}$N. It builds on previous studies of ${}^{13}$C by Yamada (2006) and Chiba (2020), and responds to recent experimental observations by Bishop (2024) of new states in ${}^{13}$N via the $3\alpha + p$ decay channel. The calculations use the GCM approach, similar to earlier work by Zhou (2018) on ${}^{11}$B, and provide a theoretical candidate for the Hoyle-analog state that can guide future experimental searches for dilute cluster structures in neutron-deficient nuclei.
