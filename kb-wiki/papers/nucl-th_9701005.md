# Recent Calculations of Electromagnetic and Strong Decays of N*

> We report on recent calculations of electromagnetic elastic form factors, helicity amplitudes and strong decay widths of N* resonances. The calculations are done in a collective constituent model for the nucleon, in which the resonances are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of an oblate top with a prescribed distribution of charges and magnetization.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a collective constituent model, in which nucleon resonances are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of an oblate top with a prescribed charge and magnetization distribution, can simultaneously describe electromagnetic elastic form factors, helicity amplitudes, and strong decay widths of N* resonances, but the model fails to reproduce the large observed eta decay width of the N(1535)S11 resonance, suggesting the need for additional configurations beyond the conventional q^3 state.

## Method

The authors used a collective model of baryons where resonances are treated as rotations and vibrations of a string configuration (oblate top) with wave functions spread over many oscillator shells. Electromagnetic form factors and helicity amplitudes were calculated by folding matrix elements of transition operators with a probability distribution g(beta) = beta^2 * exp(-beta/a) / 2a^3, with flavor-dependent scale parameters a_u and a_d introduced to break SU_sf(6) symmetry. Strong decay widths for B -> B' + M (with M = pi or eta) were computed using algebraic expressions involving the same collective form factors, with two parameters g and h determined from a least square fit to N pi partial widths excluding the S11 resonances.

## Key numbers

- Elastic electric form factor of the proton: G_E^p = 1/(1 + k^2 a^2)^2, with a = 0.232 fm when SU_sf(6) symmetry is satisfied
- Magnetic form factor ratio: G_M^n / G_M^p = -2/3 under SU_sf(6) symmetry
- With SU_sf(6) symmetry breaking: a_u = 0.230 fm, a_d = 0.257 fm, mu_u = 2.777 mu_N, mu_d = 2.915 mu_N
- Strong decay fit parameters: g = 1.164 GeV^{-1}, h = -0.094 GeV^{-1}, a = 0.232 fm
- N(1520)D13 N pi decay width: theoretical 115 MeV vs. experimental 67 +/- 9 MeV
- N(1535)S11 N pi decay width: theoretical 85 MeV vs. experimental 79 +/- 38 MeV
- N(1535)S11 N eta decay width: theoretical 0.1 MeV vs. experimental 74 +/- 39 MeV
- N(1650)S11 N eta decay width: theoretical 8 MeV vs. experimental 11 +/- 6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the collective model approach of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) for baryon structure, contrasting with nonrelativistic quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1978) and relativized quark models (Capstick and Roberts, 1993) that emphasize single-particle aspects of quark dynamics. The calculations enable systematic analysis of electromagnetic and strong couplings to distinguish between single-particle and collective forms of dynamics, and suggest that future data from Jefferson Lab and MAMI could be used to study the effects of hadron swelling on helicity amplitudes. The failure to reproduce the N(1535)S11 eta width points to the need for exotic configurations such as q^4 q-bar states or quasi-bound meson-baryon resonances (Kaiser, 1995).
