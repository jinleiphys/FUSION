# SU(3) breaking in hyperon transition vector form factors

> We present a calculation of the SU(3)-breaking corrections to the hyperon transition vector form factors to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with finite-range regularisation. Both octet and decuplet degrees of freedom are included. We formulate a chiral expansion at the kinematic point $Q^2=-(M_{B_1}-M_{B_2})^2$, which can be conveniently accessed in lattice QCD. The two unknown low-energy constants at this point are constrained by lattice QCD simulation results for the $ÎŁ^-\rightarrow n$ and $Îž^0\rightarrow ÎŁ^+$ transition form factors. Hence we determine lattice-informed values of $f_1$ at the physical point. This work constitutes progress towards the precise determination of $|V_{us}|$ from hyperon semileptonic decays.

# SU(3) breaking in hyperon transition vector form factors

## Key claim

The SU(3)-breaking corrections to hyperon transition vector form factors are calculated to O(p^4) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with finite-range regularisation, using lattice QCD results at the kinematic point Q^2 = -(M_B1 - M_B2)^2 rather than at Q^2 = 0. The sign of the SU(3)-breaking corrections is found to be consistent with quark models, in contrast to earlier chiral perturbation theory and 1/N_c expansion results.

## Method

The authors formulate a chiral expansion at Q^2 = -(M_B1 - M_B2)^2 (corresponding to zero three-momentum transfer in lattice simulations) and fit the two unknown low-energy constants b_D and b_F to lattice QCD simulation results for the Sigma- -> n and Xi0 -> Sigma+ transition form factors. The calculation includes octet and decuplet baryon intermediate states, tadpole loops, and 1/M_0 relativistic corrections, with loop integrals evaluated using finite-range regularisation with a dipole regulator. Finite-volume corrections are applied to the lattice data before chiral extrapolation to physical pseudoscalar masses.

## Key numbers

- Lattice simulation at beta = 5.5, a = 0.074(2) fm, volume 32^3 x 64, with pion masses 310-360 MeV and kaon masses 435-520 MeV across three ensembles
- Sigma- -> n transition: f_1/f_1^SU(3) - 1 = -1.5(11)(+5/-8)(2) percent at Q^2 = -0.065 GeV^2
- Xi0 -> Sigma+ transition: f_1/f_1^SU(3) - 1 = -4.8(7)(+3/-10)(4) percent at Q^2 = -0.016 GeV^2
- Lambda -> p transition: f_1/f_1^SU(3) - 1 = -4.5(7)(+5/-16)(3) percent at Q^2 = -0.031 GeV^2
- Xi- -> Lambda transition: f_1/f_1^SU(3) - 1 = -5.4(7)(+12/-32)(0) percent at Q^2 = -0.041 GeV^2
- Finite-volume corrections range from 0.1 to 0.6 percent of the form factors
- Regulator mass Lambda = 0.8 +/- 0.2 GeV (dipole form)
- Delta_phys = 0.292 GeV for decuplet baryon mass splitting

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Villadoro (2006), Lacour (2007), and Geng (2014) by using finite-range regularisation instead of dimensional regularisation, working at nonzero Q^2, and incorporating the full meson-mass dependence of octet baryon mass splittings from lattice fits. The results resolve a long-standing sign discrepancy between lattice QCD/quark model predictions and earlier chiral effective field theory/1/N_c expansion results for SU(3)-breaking corrections. This enables progress toward a precise determination of |V_us| from hyperon semileptonic decays, providing an independent cross-check on kaon decay determinations that have shown tension with CKM unitarity.
