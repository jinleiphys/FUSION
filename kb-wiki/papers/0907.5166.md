# Influence of low energy scattering on loosely bound states

> Compact algebraic equations are derived, which connect the binding energy and the asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) of a subthreshold bound state with the effective-range expansion of the corresponding partial wave. These relations are established for positively-charged and neutral particles, using the analytic continuation of the scattering (S) matrix in the complex wave-number plane. Their accuracy is checked on simple local potential models for the 16O+n, 16O+p and 12C+alpha nuclear systems, with exotic nuclei and nuclear astrophysics applications in mind.

# Influence of low energy scattering on loosely bound states

## Key claim

Compact algebraic equations connecting the binding energy and asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) of a subthreshold bound state to the effective-range expansion of the corresponding partial wave are derived for both positively-charged and neutral particles, but their accuracy depends on the binding energy being small and on the availability of higher-order terms in the effective-range expansion when the binding is not extremely weak.

## Method

The authors use analytic continuation of the scattering (S) matrix in the complex wave-number plane, defining an effective-range function that is holomorphic in the physical energy sheet. For charged particles, they introduce a modified effective-range function using the digamma function to handle Coulomb complications, then expand this function in a Taylor series around zero energy. They relate the bound-state pole location and the S-matrix residue (which gives the ANC) to the coefficients of this expansion (scattering length, effective range, and higher-order shape parameters), truncating the expansion at various orders and testing the resulting formulas on Woods-Saxon and Gaussian potential models for the 16O+n, 16O+p, and 12C+alpha systems.

## Key numbers

- For the 17F nucleus (16O+p), the s-wave bound state at 107.5 keV below threshold: Eq. (ere1C) gives the ANC with accuracy better than 0.1%; Eq. (ere1E) predicts the scattering length a0 with 5% error, while a second-order generalization reduces this error to 0.4%.
- For the 12C+alpha system, the first 2+ excited state of 16O lies 245 keV below threshold, with numerically computed ANC Cb = 1.384 x 10^5 fm^{-1/2}. Eq. (ere1C) gives Cb ≈ 1.23 x 10^5 fm^{-1/2} (11% error); adding the second-order term gives Cb ≈ 1.414 x 10^5 fm^{-1/2} (3% error); the third-order formula (ere3C) gives Cb ≈ 1.379 x 10^5 fm^{-1/2} (0.4% error).
- For the 12C+alpha potential, the effective-range coefficients are a2 = 5.891 x 10^4 fm^5, r2 = 0.1580 fm^{-3}, P2 = -65.96 fm^8, and Q2 = 0.1411(2) fm. With a third-order expansion, phase shifts are fitted up to 700 keV within 1% precision (without the third term, only up to 80 keV).
- For the 16O+p system, the nuclear Bohr radius aB = 3.825 fm; for the 12C+alpha system, aB = 0.8060 fm.

## Context

This work extends the effective-range formalism, originally developed for nucleon-nucleon scattering (Bethe 1949) and for the deuteron, to charged-particle systems with arbitrary partial waves, generalizing Eq. (45) of Mukhamedzhanov (1999) to l >= 0. It provides a model-independent alternative to the reaction-matrix analyses used by Angulo (2000) and Sparenberg (2004) for extracting ANCs from elastic scattering data. The method is directly applicable to the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O capture reaction, where the subthreshold 2+ and 1- states of 16O (Azuma 1994, Brune 1999) strongly influence the carbon-to-oxygen ratio in red giant stars; the authors plan to apply their approach to the experimental phase shifts of Tischhauser (2002).
