# Comment on "A phenomenological $π^- p$ scattering length from pionic hydrogen''

> This short technical note addresses a number of issues regarding the estimates of the 2004 paper by Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech for the corrections $δ_ε$ and $δ_Γ$, which aim at the removal of the effects of electromagnetic origin from the measurements of the strong-interaction shift $ε_{1s}$ and of the total decay width $Γ_{1s}$ of the ground state in pionic hydrogen.

# Comment on "A phenomenological π⁻ p scattering length from pionic hydrogen"

## Key claim

The corrections δ_ε and δ_Γ, which remove electromagnetic effects from the strong-interaction shift and decay width of pionic hydrogen to obtain hadronic scattering lengths, were computed in Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech (2004) using outdated input values for the range parameters imported from the Karlsruhe analyses; updated input yields substantially different gauge-term contributions, though the author does not assess the correctness or applicability of the original approach itself.

## Method

The author re-evaluates the four contributions to δ_ε (extended charge distribution, vacuum polarisation, renormalisation correction, and gauge term) and the analogous contributions to δ_Γ using the same analytical framework as Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech (2004), but with updated scattering lengths and range parameters obtained from fits to πN data for laboratory kinetic energies T ≤ 100 MeV using a polynomial parameterisation of spin-isospin K-matrix elements, with explicit resonance contributions from Δ(1232) and N(1440) added in the P₃₃ and P₁₁ partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum-polarisation correction to the ground-state wavefunction at the origin for pionic hydrogen is given as α⁻¹ (δψ(0))^vp/ψ(0) = 0.33064, from Eiras (2000).
- The Euler constant γ ≈ 0.577216 appears in the renormalisation-correction contribution δ^c.
- The updated δ_Γ correction matches the result of Zemp (2004), obtained within Chiral-Perturbation Theory, though the author notes this agreement is likely coincidental given the disparate approaches.
- The range parameters b⁺ and b⁻ given in the first paragraph of Section 3 of Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech (2004) are stated to be one order of magnitude smaller than the actual values, described as an obvious mistype.

## Context

This note updates the electromagnetic corrections of Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech (2004) for extracting hadronic π⁻p scattering lengths from pionic hydrogen data, using modern πN data from the three meson factories (LAMPF, PSI, TRIUMF) rather than the Karlsruhe analyses (Hoehler, 1983) which relied on fewer than one hundred low-energy measurements, almost exclusively π⁺p differential cross sections with no analysing power data. The work enables improved determination of the purely hadronic scattering lengths ã_cc and ã_c0, but the author notes that criticism of the original approach has already been expressed, e.g., in Oades (2007), while Ericson, Loiseau, and Wycech have expressed concerns about the incompatibility of the coupled-channel formalism with low-energy K-matrix expansion.
