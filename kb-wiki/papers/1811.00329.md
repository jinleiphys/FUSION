# Effect of the Pauli exclusion principle in the electric dipole moment of $^9$Be with $|ΔS|=1$ interactions

> We calculate the contribution of the $|ΔS|=1$ $K$ meson exchange process generated by the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the $^9$Be nucleus by considering the $αn - αΛ$ channel coupling. It is found that the effect of the Pauli exclusion principle is not important intermediate $S=-1$ state, and that the result is consistent with the EDM of $^9$Be calculated with the $|ΔS|=1$ interactions as a perturbation without considering the nucleus-hypernucleus mixing. Our result suggests that the effect of the $|ΔS|=1$ interactions is neither suppressed nor enhanced in nuclei, if the difference of binding energies between the nucleus and the hypernucleus is small compared to the hyperon-nucleon mass difference.

## Key claim

The Pauli exclusion principle does not significantly affect the electric dipole moment (EDM) of \(^9\)Be generated by \(|\Delta S|=1\) interactions through nucleus-hypernucleus mixing, provided the binding energy difference between the nucleus and hypernucleus is small compared to the hyperon-nucleon mass difference.

## Method

The authors employed an \(\alpha\)-cluster model with Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to calculate the wave functions of \(^9\)Be and \(^9_\Lambda\)Be, treating the \(\alpha n - \alpha\Lambda\) channel coupling. They modeled the \(|\Delta S|=1\) weak interaction via one-kaon exchange with CP-violating couplings derived from the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix, using the effective Hamiltonian renormalized to the hadronic scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV and the factorization approach for meson-baryon interactions.

## Key numbers

* \(d_{^9\mathrm{Be}} = 5.47 \times 10^{-32} \, e \, \mathrm{cm}\) for the EDM of \(^9\)Be including the full \(\alpha n - \alpha\Lambda\) channel coupling with the \(|\Delta S|=1\) kaon exchange interaction.
* \(d_{^9\mathrm{Be}} = 5.8 \times 10^{-32} \, e \, \mathrm{cm}\) for the EDM of \(^9\)Be calculated without considering the intermediate hypernuclear contribution (using effective CP-odd \(NN\) interaction from Yamanaka (2015)).
* The ratio of the matrix element with channel coupling to that without is 0.99, indicating the Pauli blocking effect is negligible.
* The first term of the EDM (via \(\alpha\alpha n\) intermediate states) contributes \(5.16 \times 10^{-32} \, e \, \mathrm{cm}\), while the second term (via \(\alpha\alpha\Lambda\) intermediate states) contributes \(0.31 \times 10^{-32} \, e \, \mathrm{cm}\).
* The \(N\alpha\) distance for \(^9\mathrm{Be}(3/2^-)\) is 3.6 fm and the \(\Lambda\alpha\) distance for \(^9_{\Lambda}\mathrm{Be}(3/2^+)\) is 4.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the evaluation of CKM-driven nuclear EDMs from the deuteron (Yamanaka, 2016) to the \(^9\)Be nucleus, which serves as a prototype for heavier odd nuclei with core-plus-valence configurations. The result contradicts the expectation that Pauli blocking in hypernuclear intermediate states might significantly suppress the EDM, and suggests that CP-odd electron-nucleon interactions and \(T\)-odd angular correlations in nuclear beta decay are not suppressed in heavier nuclei. The calculation enables improved estimates of standard model backgrounds for searches of CP violation beyond the standard model in nuclear and atomic systems.
