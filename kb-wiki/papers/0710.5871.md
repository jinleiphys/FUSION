# Hydrodynamics of transversally thermalized partons in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The hydrodynamic description of transversally thermalized matter, possibly formed at the early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, is developed. The formalism is based on the thermodynamically consistent approach with all thermodynamic variables referring to two-dimensional objects, the so-called transverse clusters, which are identified with the particles having the same rapidity. The resulting hydrodynamic equations for a single cluster have the form of the two-dimensional hydrodynamic equations of the perfect fluid. Since the clusters do not perform any work in the longitudinal direction, their energy is completely transformed and used to generate strong radial and elliptic flows that turn out to be compatible with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors propose that matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions undergoes hydrodynamic expansion only in the transverse direction, with longitudinal expansion treated as free-streaming of independent clusters; they claim this picture is compatible with experimental data on hadron spectra and elliptic flow, in contrast to the earlier conclusion of Heinz and Wong (2002) that such a scenario cannot produce large elliptic flow.

## Method

The formalism factorizes the phase-space distribution function into a longitudinal part describing free-streaming clusters at fixed rapidity and a transverse part described by two-dimensional (2D) perfect-fluid hydrodynamics. The authors solve the resulting 2D hydrodynamic equations for the temperature, transverse flow velocity, and flow angle using a numerical method adapted from Baym et al. (1983) and Chojnacki et al. (2006), with initial conditions based on the wounded-nucleon density from the Glauber model. Transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow coefficients are computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at constant final temperature.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: \(T_i = 250\) MeV used for all calculations.
- Final temperatures considered: \(T_f = 200, 180, 160, 140\) MeV.
- Impact parameters used: \(b = 7.25\) fm for centrality 20-30%, \(b = 7.94\) fm for 20-40%, \(b = 10.26\) fm for 40-60%, \(b = 11.69\) fm for 60-70%.
- Sound velocity in the 2D system: \(c_s^2 = 1/2\).
- Degeneracy factor for gluons: \(\nu_g = 16\).
- Woods-Saxon parameters for gold nucleus: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(r_0 = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3})\) fm, \(a = 0.54\) fm, \(A = 197\).
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: \(\sigma = 40\) mb.

## Context

This work extends the concept of purely transverse thermalization previously analyzed by Heinz and Wong (2002), who concluded it could not produce large elliptic flow; the authors argue their approach differs by using thermodynamically consistent 2D densities and conserving entropy, yielding a perfect-fluid description rather than viscous dynamics. The model aims to address the early thermalization problem and the HBT puzzle in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors note that further development (e.g., a hadronization scheme) is needed to connect parton-level results to observed hadrons.
