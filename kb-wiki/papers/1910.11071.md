# Virtual Compton Scattering and Nucleon Generalized Polarizabilities

> This review gives an update on virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off the nucleon, $γ^* N \to N γ$, in the low-energy regime. We recall the theoretical formalism related to the generalized polarizabilities (GPs) and model predictions for these observables. We present the GP extraction methods that are used in the experiments: the approach based on the low-energy theorem for VCS and the formalism of Dispersion Relations. We then review the experimental results, with a focus on the progress brought by recent experimental data on proton generalized polarizabilities, and we conclude by some perspectives in the field of VCS at low energy.

## Key claim

This review synthesizes the theoretical formalism and experimental status of nucleon generalized polarizabilities (GPs) extracted from virtual Compton scattering (VCS), noting that a consistent picture of the proton scalar GPs is only now starting to emerge from recent experimental data, rather than being firmly established.

## Method

The authors review the low-energy theorem (LET) and low-energy expansion (LEX) for VCS, which parametrizes the non-Born amplitude in terms of six independent dipole GPs at finite momentum transfer \(Q^2\). They describe the extraction methods used in experiments, including the LET-based approach and the Dispersion Relation (DR) formalism, and compare model predictions from non-relativistic constituent quark models, effective Lagrangian models, the linear sigma model, and chiral perturbation theory against experimental results for the proton.

## Key numbers

- Static proton electric polarizability \(\alpha_{E1} = 11.2 \pm 0.4\) and magnetic polarizability \(\beta_{M1} = 2.5 \pm 0.4\) in units of \(10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) (Particle Data Group 2018)
- The fine-structure constant used in GP definitions: \(e^2 / 4\pi = \alpha_{\mathrm{QED}} = 1/137\)
- At \(Q^2 = 0\), the scalar GPs \(P^{(L1,L1)0}\) and \(P^{(M1,M1)0}\) coincide with the RCS polarizabilities \(\alpha_{E1}\) and \(\beta_{M1}\) via the relations: \(P^{(L1,L1)0}(0) = -\frac{4\pi}{e^2}\sqrt{\frac{2}{3}}\,\alpha_{E1}\) and \(P^{(M1,M1)0}(0) = -\frac{4\pi}{e^2}\sqrt{\frac{8}{3}}\,\beta_{M1}\)

## Context

This review updates the earlier comprehensive treatments by Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (1998) and Drechsel et al. (2002), and complements other reviews by d'Hose (2000), Hyde-Wright and de Jager (2004), and Hagelstein et al. (2015). It focuses on the recent application of the Dispersion Relation model to VCS experiments and the resulting progress in extracting proton GPs, which enables future measurements of the separate \(P_{LL}\) and \(P_{TT}\) structure functions and extension to neutron GPs.
