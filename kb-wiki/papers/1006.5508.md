# The effect of spin-orbit nuclear charge density corrections due to the anomalous magnetic moment on halonuclei

> In this paper we consider the contribution of the anomalous magnetic moments of protons and neutrons to the nuclear charge density. We show that the spin-orbit contribution to the mean-square charge radius, which has been neglected in recent nuclear calculations, can be important in light halonuclei. We estimate the size of the effect in helium, lithium, and beryllium nuclei. It is found that the spin-orbit contribution represents a ~2% correction to the charge density at the center of the Be-7 nucleus. We derive a simple expression for the correction to the mean-square charge radius due to the spin-orbit term and find that in light halonuclei it may be larger than the Darwin-Foldy term and comparable to finite size corrections. A comparison of experimental and theoretical mean-square radii including the spin-orbit contribution is presented.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit contribution to the nuclear charge density, arising from the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons and previously neglected in nuclear calculations, can be comparable to the Darwin-Foldy term and finite-size corrections in light halonuclei, although the effect is a maximal estimate that may be reduced by configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors derived a nonrelativistic expression for the spin-orbit charge density from the 4-current, accurate to order \(q^2/M^2\), using the anomalous magnetic moments \(\mu_n = -1.91\) and \(\mu_p = 1.79\). They then obtained a simple, general formula for the correction to the mean-square charge radius that is independent of the radial wavefunction, and applied it to helium, lithium, and beryllium isotopes using the shell model with a harmonic oscillator potential (\(\alpha = 0.25\) fm\(^{-2}\)) to estimate the charge density distribution in \(^7\)Be.

## Key numbers

- In \(^7\)Be, the spin-orbit contribution represents a \(\sim 2\%\) correction to the charge density at the center of the nucleus.
- The spin-orbit correction to the mean-square charge radius for \(^7\)Be is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{so} = 0.02\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^8\)He, the spin-orbit correction to the mean-square charge radius is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{so} = -0.17\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^6\)He, the spin-orbit correction is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{so} = -0.08\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^9\)Li, the spin-orbit correction is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{so} = -0.09\) fm\(^2\); for the unpaired \(1p_{3/2}\) proton in lithium, the correction is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{so,p} = 0.03\) fm\(^2\).
- The Darwin-Foldy term is \(\frac{3}{4M^2} = 0.033\) fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of spin-orbit charge density effects, first discussed by Bertozzi (1972) for neutron contributions in \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca, to light halonuclei where the effect had been neglected in recent calculations. The authors compare their results with experimental charge radii and theoretical calculations from the no-core shell model (NCSM) by Navratil (2004) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) by Pieper (2007), finding that the spin-orbit correction can be larger than the Darwin-Foldy term and comparable to finite-size corrections, though the spread between different theoretical values is often larger than the correction itself. This work enables more complete comparisons between theory and high-precision experiments on halonuclei such as \(^6\)He, \(^8\)He, and \(^{11}\)Li.
