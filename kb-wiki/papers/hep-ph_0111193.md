# Renormalization of Self-consistent Approximation schemes Finite Temperature II: Applications to the Sunset Diagram

> The theoretical concepts for the renormalization of self-consistent Dyson resummations, deviced in the first paper of this series, are applied to first example cases for the $ϕ^4$-theory. Besides the tadpole (Hartree) approximation as a novel part the numerical solutions are presented which includes the sunset self-energy diagram into the self-consistent scheme based on the $Φ$-derivable approximation or 2PI effective action concept.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the renormalization of self-consistent Dyson resummations in the \(\Phi\)-derivable (2PI effective action) framework can be achieved with counter terms defined solely on the vacuum level of the self-consistent scheme, provided the underlying theory is renormalizable and the approximation is \(\Phi\)-derivable; this procedure avoids the uncompensated divergences encountered by Braaten and Petitgirard (2001) when they used a \(\beta\)-function not pertaining to the self-consistent scheme.

## Method

The authors apply the BPHZ renormalization scheme combined with dimensional regularization to the \(\phi^4\) theory in the symmetric phase, splitting the self-energy into vacuum, \(\Sigma^{(0)}\) (linear in matter propagator parts), and \(\Sigma^{(\text{r})}\) (remainder) components. For the sunset approximation, they solve the renormalized Dyson equation for the vacuum propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter ladder equation for the four-point function \(\Lambda\) using spectral (Lehmann) representations, then compute the finite-temperature self-energy via the renormalized expression for \(\Sigma^{(0)}\) that involves the four-point function and the matter propagator.

## Key numbers

* For the tadpole (Hartree) approximation with \(m = 0.14\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\lambda = 30\), the self-consistent gap equation yields an effective mass \(M\) that is reduced relative to the perturbative one-loop result (Fig. 1).
* For the massless case in the tadpole approximation, the perturbative thermal mass is \(M_{\text{pert}}^2 = \frac{\lambda}{24} T^2\).
* For the sunset approximation, the physical mass is set to \(m = m_\pi = 140\ \text{MeV}\) with \(\lambda = 30\); at this coupling there is no visible difference between the perturbative and self-consistent vacuum self-energy results (Fig. 2).
* The renormalized loop function \(L(s)\) has an imaginary part determined by the two-body phase space opening at \(s = 4m^2\), with threshold behavior given by \(8\pi^2 L^{(\text{pert})}(s,m^2) \approx \frac{i\pi}{2}\sqrt{(s-4m^2)/s} - 4m^2/s\) for \(0 < s-4m^2 \ll s\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization concepts developed in the first paper of the series (van Hees and Knoll, 2001) to explicit numerical solutions for the \(\phi^4\) theory, covering both the tadpole (Hartree) approximation and the more sophisticated sunset diagram. The approach resolves the hidden and nested vacuum subdivergences that caused problems in the work of Braaten and Petitgirard (2001), who used a different technique with a restricted ansatz for the propagator. The results enable future calculations of the thermodynamic potential and spectral functions within self-consistent schemes, and a forthcoming paper (van Hees and Knoll, 2002) will address symmetry violations such as crossing symmetry and conservation laws that arise beyond the order included in \(\Phi\).
