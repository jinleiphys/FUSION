# The study of participant-spectator matter and collision dynamics in heavy-ion collisions

> We present the simulations of heavy-ion collisions in terms of participant-spectator matter. We find that this matter depends crucially on the collision dynamics and history of the nucleons. The important changes in the momentum space are due to the binary nucleon-nucleon collisions experienced during the high dense phase. This was otherwise not possible with mean field alone. The collisions push the colliding nucleons into midrapidity region responsible for the formation of participant matter. This ultimately leads to thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The formation of participant-spectator matter in heavy-ion collisions depends crucially on the collision dynamics and history of individual nucleons, with binary nucleon-nucleon collisions during the high-density phase being the primary driver of changes in momentum space. However, beyond a certain limit, additional binary collisions do not improve thermalization further and instead lead to transparency.

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model for reactions of \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{131}\)Xe+\(^{131}\)Xe at incident energies between 200 and 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters from central (\(b=0\) fm) to peripheral (\(b=R_1+R_2\)). They used a hard equation of state with a nucleon-nucleon cross section of \(\sigma=40\) mb, and defined participant matter as nucleons experiencing at least one binary collision, with the remainder classified as spectator matter. The time evolution of the system was tracked in both coordinate and momentum space, and the anisotropy ratio \(<R_a>\) and relative momentum \(<K_R>\) were used to quantify global and local thermalization.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy range: 200 to 400 MeV/nucleon for both \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{131}\)Xe+\(^{131}\)Xe reactions.
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section used: \(\sigma=40\) mb.
- Maximum density reached in central \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca collisions at 200 MeV/nucleon: \(<\rho^{max}>/\rho_0\) rises from 0.9 to 1.7 between 10-20 fm/\(c\), then falls back between 20-30 fm/\(c\).
- Time of maximal density for central and semicentral collisions: around 20 fm/\(c\) for \(b/b_{max}=0.2\), 0.65, and 0.95.
- Anisotropy ratio \(<R_a>\) = 1 corresponds to full global equilibrium averaged over many events.

## Context
This work extends the participant-spectator matter concept previously studied by Sood et al. (2004) and Sood et al. (2004a), where participant-spectator matter was found to be independent of system size at the energy of vanishing flow. The study uses the QMD model framework developed by Aich et al. (1991) and Stoc et al. (1986). The results enable a better understanding of thermalization mechanisms and the conditions for intermediate mass fragment emission, showing that semicentral collisions are optimal for such studies, consistent with the rise-and-fall behavior reported by Tsan et al. (1993) and Stoc et al. (1989). The finding that binary collisions beyond a certain limit do not improve thermalization contradicts the naive expectation that more collisions always lead to better equilibrium.
