# The hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter and neutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars (NS's). We calculate the equation of state (EOS) of hadronic matter using the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formalism with realistic two-body and three-body forces, as well as a relativistic mean field model. For quark matter we employ the MIT bag model constraining the bag constant by using the indications coming from the recent experimental results obtained at the CERN SPS on the formation of a quark-gluon plasma. We find necessary to introduce a density dependent bag parameter, and the corresponding consistent thermodynamical formalism. We calculate the structure of NS interiors with the EOS comprising both phases, and we find that the NS maximum masses fall in a relatively narrow interval, $1.4 M_\odot \leq M_{\rm max} \leq 1.7 M_\odot$. The precise value of the maximum mass turns out to be only weakly correlated with the value of the energy density at the assumed transition point in nearly symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of neutron stars containing a hadron-quark phase transition falls in a narrow interval of 1.4 to 1.7 solar masses, but this maximum mass is only weakly correlated with the value of the energy density at the assumed transition point in nearly symmetric nuclear matter, contrary to what one might expect if the transition point strongly constrained the stellar structure.

## Method

The authors calculated the hadronic equation of state using two approaches: the microscopic Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formalism with realistic two-body (Paris potential) and three-body (Urbana model) forces including hyperons (Sigma- and Lambda), and a relativistic mean field (nonlinear Walecka) model. For quark matter they employed the MIT bag model with a density-dependent bag parameter, constrained by the transition energy density of about 1 GeV/fm^3 inferred from CERN SPS heavy-ion collision data. They then performed the Glendenning construction for the mixed phase and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star maximum masses fall in the interval 1.4 solar masses ≤ M_max ≤ 1.7 solar masses
- Normal nuclear matter density is approximately 0.17 fm^-3
- The transition energy density values considered are 0.8, 1.1, and 1.5 GeV fm^-3
- The bag constant B_0 at zero density was set to 400 MeV fm^-3
- The strange quark mass values used are 150 MeV and 200 MeV
- The QCD coupling constant alpha_s values used are 0 and 0.1
- The renormalization point sigma_ren is 313 MeV
- The energy density of normal nuclear matter epsilon_0 is approximately 156 MeV fm^-3

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by the same group (Burgio et al., 2001) that first constrained the bag constant using CERN SPS data, and it builds on the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body framework for nuclear matter. The study systematically explores the sensitivity of neutron star properties to the transition energy density, finding that the maximum mass is only weakly correlated with this parameter. This enables future work to focus on other observables, such as radii or cooling properties, that might better constrain the hadron-quark transition point in dense matter.
