# Thermal Mass Spectra of Vector and Axial-Vector Mesons in Predictive Soft-Wall AdS/QCD Model

> We extend the predictive soft-wall AdS/QCD model to a thermodynamic model by considering the black hole metric. A modified bulk vacuum expectation value and a modified coefficient for the quartic term in the bulk scalar potential are introduced to obtain a smooth dilaton solution for computing the spectral functions of the vector and axial-vector mesons. It is demonstrated that the peaks appearing in the spectral functions characterize the thermal mass spectra of vector and axial-vector mesons, where the location of the peak moves to a lower value and the width of the peak becomes wider when increasing the temperature. We observe that the peak disappears completely at the critical temperature around $T_c=200$ MeV, which implies the deconfinement of quark and the restoration of chiral symmetry breaking. A numerical study by fitting the spectral function in terms of the Breit-Wigner form has been made to show how the peak dissolves quantitatively when the temperature is increased to the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper extends the predictive soft-wall AdS/QCD model to finite temperature by introducing a black hole metric, but only after modifying the bulk vacuum expectation value (bVEV) and the quartic coupling coefficient to avoid a divergent dilaton solution at the black hole horizon; the resulting spectral functions show that the peaks for vector and axial-vector mesons disappear completely at a critical temperature around \(T_c = 200\) MeV, which the authors interpret as indicating deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors replace the zero-temperature AdS metric with a black hole metric \(ds^2 = a^2(z)(f(z) dt^2 - d\mathbf{x}^2 - dz^2/f(z))\), where \(f(z)=1-(z/z_0)^4\) and the Hawking temperature is \(T = 1/(\pi z_0)\). To obtain a smooth dilaton solution, they modify the zero-temperature bVEV \(v_0(z)\) by adding a term \(v_1(z)\ln f(z)\) and, for the case with a quartic term (\(\lambda_0=9\)), also modify the quartic coupling to \(\lambda = \lambda_0/(1 + c_\lambda (\ln f(z))^3)\). They then solve the equations of motion for the vector and axial-vector gauge fields in the bulk, impose in-falling boundary conditions at the horizon, and compute the retarded Green function via the shooting method to obtain the spectral function \(\rho(\omega,q)\) at zero spatial momentum.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature at which the spectral peaks disappear is \(T_c \approx 200\) MeV.
* For the case without the quartic term (\(\lambda_0=0\)), the regulator parameters are set to \(c_v = -10^{-3}\) (constant).
* For the case with the quartic term (\(\lambda_0=9\)), the regulator parameters are set to \(c_v = c_\lambda = -10^{-4}\) (constant).
* The zero-temperature mass predictions for the lowest vector meson (\(\rho\)) range from 603 MeV (Model Ib) to 777 MeV (Model IIa), compared to the experimental value \(775.5 \pm 1\) MeV.
* The zero-temperature mass predictions for the lowest axial-vector meson (\(a_1\)) range from 714 MeV (Model Ib) to 940 MeV (Model IIa), compared to the experimental value \(1230 \pm 40\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the predictive soft-wall AdS/QCD model of Cui, Takeuchi, and Wu (2011) to finite temperature by incorporating a black hole metric, following the approach used for other soft-wall models by Colangelo (2009) and Misumi. It contrasts with earlier hard-wall AdS/QCD studies at finite temperature (e.g., Herzog, 2006) and with D3/D7 setups (e.g., Mas, 2008) by focusing on the thermal dissolution of meson peaks as a signal of chiral symmetry restoration. The results enable future quantitative studies of the temperature dependence of meson widths and masses in holographic QCD, and the authors note that the regulator parameters \(c_v\) and \(c_\lambda\) cannot be determined theoretically and must be fixed phenomenologically.
