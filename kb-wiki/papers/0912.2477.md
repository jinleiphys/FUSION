# D mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures

> We study the in-medium properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures using an effective chiral SU(4) model. The interactions of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons with nucleons, scalar isoscalar meson $σ$, and scalar iso-vector meson $δ$ are taken into consideration. It is found that as compared to the $\bar{D}$ mesons, the properties of the $D$ mesons are observed to be quite sensitive to the isospin-asymmetry at high densities. At finite densities, the masses of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons observed at finite temperatures are seen to be higher in comparison to the zero temperature case. The present study of the in-medium properties of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons will be of relevance for the experiments in the future facility of the FAIR, GSI, where the compressed baryonic matter at high densities and moderate temperatures is expected to be produced. The mass modifications of $D$ and $\bar{D}$ mesons in hot nuclear medium can lead to decay of the charmonium states ($Ψ^{'}, χ_{c}, J/Ψ$) to $D\bar{D}$ pairs in dense hadronic matter. The isospin-asymmetric effects on the properties of the open charm mesons for the doublets $D=(D^0,D^+)$ and $\bar D=(\bar D^0,D^-)$ should show in observables like their production and collective flow in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions. The small attractive potentials observed for the $\bar{D}$ mesons may also lead to formation of the $\bar{D}$ mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of D and anti-D mesons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures are calculated using an effective chiral SU(4) model, with the hedge that the D meson properties are quite sensitive to isospin asymmetry at high densities, while the anti-D mesons show only small attractive potentials that may allow formation of anti-D mesic nuclei, contrary to some coupled-channel approaches that predict repulsive mass shifts for anti-D mesons.

## Method

The authors generalize a chiral SU(3) model to SU(4) flavor symmetry to derive the interactions of D and anti-D mesons with nucleons and scalar mesons (sigma and delta) in the mean-field approximation. The self-energies of the D and anti-D mesons are computed from the Weinberg-Tomozawa vectorial interaction, scalar exchange terms, and range terms, using the scalar field expectation values obtained by solving the coupled equations of motion at finite temperature and density. The dispersion relations are then solved to obtain the momentum-dependent energies and optical potentials for each member of the D and anti-D doublets.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear saturation density rho_0 and zero temperature in isospin-symmetric matter, the D+ meson mass drops by 81 MeV from its vacuum value of 1869 MeV.
* At density 4 rho_0 and zero temperature in isospin-symmetric matter, the D+ meson mass drops by about 364 MeV.
* At rho_0 and T = 150 MeV in isospin-symmetric matter, the D+ mass drop is reduced to 63 MeV.
* At rho_0 and zero temperature in isospin-symmetric matter, the D0 meson mass drops by 80 MeV from its vacuum value of 1864.5 MeV.
* At rho_0 and zero temperature in isospin-symmetric matter, the D- meson mass drops by 30 MeV from its vacuum value.
* At 4 rho_0 and zero temperature in isospin-symmetric matter, the D- meson mass drops by 184 MeV.
* At rho_0 and T = 150 MeV in isospin-symmetric matter, the D- mass drop is reduced to 12 MeV.
* For D0 mesons at zero temperature and rho_0, moving from isospin-symmetric (eta=0) to isospin-asymmetric (eta=0.5) medium increases the mass by 21 MeV; at 4 rho_0 this increase is 91 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of D mesons in symmetric hot nuclear matter (Kumar and Mishra, 2009) and in asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature (Kumar and Mishra, 2009) to include finite temperature effects. It contrasts with coupled-channel approaches (Mizutani and others, 2008) that found repulsive mass shifts for anti-D mesons and ruled out charmed mesic nuclei, whereas the present model obtains small attractive potentials for anti-D mesons that may allow formation of anti-D mesic nuclei. The results are relevant for interpreting future experiments at FAIR, GSI, where compressed baryonic matter at high densities and moderate temperatures is expected to be produced, and for understanding charmonium suppression mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions.
