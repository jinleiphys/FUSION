# Local and global $Λ$ polarization in a vortical fluid

> We compute the fermion spin distribution in the vortical fluid created in off-central high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We employ the event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model. The spin polarization density is proportional to the local fluid vorticity in quantum kinetic theory. As a result of strong collectivity, the spatial distribution of the local vorticity on the freeze-out hyper-surface strongly correlates to the rapidity and azimuthal angle distribution of fermion spins. We investigate the sensitivity of the local polarization to the initial fluid velocity in the hydrodynamic model and compute the global polarization of Λ hyperons by the AMPT model. The energy dependence of the global polarization agrees with the STAR data.

## Key claim
The local polarization of Λ hyperons around mid-rapidity is highly sensitive to the initial transverse flow in the hydrodynamic model, and the global polarization computed from the AMPT model agrees semi-quantitatively with STAR data, though the paper notes that the self-coupling term of the vorticity tensor with an undetermined coefficient leads to a breakdown of the program for fluctuating initial conditions and is therefore not included.

## Method
The authors employed the CLVisc (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model with extended second-order Israel-Stewart equations to solve energy-momentum conservation, using initial conditions from the AMPT model. They computed the local spin polarization density on the freeze-out hyper-surface (at T_f = 0.137 GeV) from the thermal vorticity, and calculated the global Λ polarization by integrating the product of the vorticity field Ω_zx and the Λ distribution f_Λ over the hyper-surface for two impact parameters (b = 7 fm and 9 fm) across multiple beam energies.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 0.137 GeV used for the hyper-surface determination in the local polarization calculation.
- The maximal magnitude of polarization at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV is about 5 times that at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions.
- Two impact parameters considered for global polarization: b = 7 fm and 9 fm (approximating 20-50% centrality collisions).
- Initial fluid evolution started at τ_0 = 0.4 fm from AMPT model initial conditions.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic predictions of Λ polarization from earlier studies by Becattini (2016), Liang (2004), and Csernai (2013) by using event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics with realistic initial conditions from the AMPT model. The results enable quantitative constraints on the fluid vorticity of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and the magnitude of the magnetic field created in heavy-ion collisions through the spin-vorticity and spin-magnetic coupling, as the difference between Λ and anti-Λ polarization provides a unique opportunity to determine the magnetic field.
