# Analysis of the quadrupole deformation of $Δ$(1232) within an effective Lagrangian model for pion photoproduction from the nucleon

> We present an extraction of the E2/M1 ratio of the $Δ$(1232) from experimental data applying an effective Lagrangian model. We compare the result obtained with different nucleonic models and we reconcile the experimental results with the Lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The intrinsic (bare) E2/M1 ratio of the Δ(1232) extracted from experimental data using an effective Lagrangian model reconciles experimental results with Lattice QCD calculations, but only after removing final-state interaction effects and using a consistent treatment of spin-3/2 particles that avoids pathologies present in previous models.

## Method

The authors developed a pion photoproduction model up to 1 GeV photon energy based on effective Lagrangians, including Born terms, vector meson exchanges (ρ and ω), and all four-star resonances up to 1.7 GeV mass and spin-3/2. They fitted the model parameters to 1526 data points from the SAID energy-independent multipole analysis for the γp → π⁰p process using a genetic algorithm combined with a NAG library routine, and included final-state interactions phenomenologically by adding a phase to the electromagnetic multipoles determined from the SAID energy-dependent solution.

## Key numbers

- Physical (dressed) EMR = (−3.9 ± 1.1)%, obtained as the ratio of imaginary parts of E₁₊³/² and M₁₊³/² multipoles at the resonance energy
- Bare (intrinsic) EMR = (−1.30 ± 0.52)%, defined as the ratio G_E^Δ(1232)/G_M^Δ(1232) for comparison with nucleonic models
- The fit used m = 1526 data points (763 real parts and 763 imaginary parts of multipoles) from SAID up to 1 GeV
- Lattice QCD result from Alexandrou et al. at Q² = 0.1 GeV² and m_π = 0 gives EMR = (−1.93 ± 0.94)%
- Lattice QCD result from Alexandrou et al. at Q² = 0.1 GeV² and m_π = 370 MeV gives EMR = (−1.40 ± 0.60)%

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach of Garcilazo and Moya de Guerra and shares similarities with models by Sato and Lee, Pascalutsa and Tjon, and others based on the seminal work of Peccei. The extracted bare EMR of −1.30% reconciles experimental data with Lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou et al. (2006) and Leinweber et al., while indicating that non-relativistic quark models (predicting 0%) and constituent quark models (predicting −3.5%) need improvement. The analysis enables direct comparison between reaction-model extractions and nucleonic structure calculations by isolating the intrinsic properties of the Δ(1232) from final-state interactions and dressing effects.
