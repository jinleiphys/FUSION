# Vacuum Effects and Compressional Properties of Nuclear Matter in Cutoff Field Theory

> Including the vacuum effects, the compressional properties of nuclear matter are studied in the cutoff field theory. Under the Hartree approximation, the low-energy effective Lagrangian is derived in the framework of the renormalization group methods. The coefficients are determined in a way where the physical results hardly depend on the value of the cutoff which is conveniently introduced into the theory. It is shown that, to reproduce the empirical data of the nucleus incompressibility, the compressibility of the nuclear matter is favorable to be 250$\sim$350MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, when vacuum effects are included in a cutoff field theory of nuclear matter, the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K\) is favorable to be 250–350 MeV to reproduce empirical data on nucleus incompressibility, but this range depends on the stability condition of the \(\sigma\)-meson equation of motion and on the inclusion of higher-order \(\Phi^5\) and \(\Phi^6\) terms in the effective Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors start from a renormalizable \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model with a four‑dimensional cutoff \(\Lambda\) and derive a low‑energy effective Lagrangian under the Hartree approximation using renormalization‑group methods. They introduce a convenient cutoff \(\Lambda'\) (set to 1.5 GeV in most calculations) and add \(\Phi^5\) and \(\Phi^6\) terms to the Lagrangian to remove the \(\Lambda'\)‑dependence of physical results to order \((1/\Lambda')^4\). The parameters \(C_s\), \(C_v\), \(C_5\), and \(C_6\) are determined phenomenologically by requiring saturation conditions, a given effective nucleon mass \(M^*_0\) at normal density, and a chosen incompressibility \(K\); the skewness coefficient \(K'\) and the Coulomb coefficient \(K_c\) are then computed and compared with empirical data from giant monopole resonances.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter incompressibility \(K\) is favorable to be 250–350 MeV to reproduce empirical \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\) simultaneously (Section 4, point 6).
* With the condition \(C_6 > 0\) (stability at \(\Phi \to \infty\)), only \(K = 250 \pm 50\) MeV is allowed, and in all allowed cases \(M^*_0 > 0.85M\) (Section 3, table II(a) discussion).
* For parameter set PS11 (negative \(C_6\), stable for \(\Phi < M\)), \(K = 350.0\) MeV, \(K_c = -7.274 + 2.06\) MeV, \(a_4 = 30.0\) MeV, \(M^*_0 = 0.5442M\), and \(K_{vs} = -357.8\) (Section 3, PS11 description).
* Binding energy at normal density is taken as \(E_{b0} = -15.75\) MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Section 3).
* The upper limit of the effective nucleon mass at normal density is \(M^*_0 = 0.944M\) (from eq. (14) with \(C_v^2 = 0\)).

## Context

This work extends the cutoff field‑theory approach of Cohen (1990s) and the authors’ own earlier paper (Kouno et al., 1997, ref. [7]) by focusing on compressional properties and the skewness coefficient. It contrasts with the renormalization‑procedure results of Chin (1977), which gave smaller incompressibilities, and with the scaling‑model analyses of Pearson (1991) and Shlomo and Youngblood (1990), which provided empirical correlations between \(K\), \(K_c\), and \(K_{vs}\). The paper enables future studies of meson self‑energies in the random‑phase approximation using the parameter sets obtained here.
