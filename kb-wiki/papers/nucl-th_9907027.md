# Pair creation: back-reactions and damping

> We solve the quantum Vlasov equation for fermions and bosons, incorporating spontaneous pair creation in the presence of back-reactions and collisions. Pair creation is initiated by an external impulse field and the source term is non-Markovian. A simultaneous solution of Maxwell's equation in the presence of feedback yields an internal current and electric field that exhibit plasma oscillations with a period tau_pl. Allowing for collisions, these oscillations are damped on a time-scale, tau_r, determined by the collision frequency. Plasma oscillations cannot affect the early stages of the formation of a quark-gluon plasma unless tau_r >> tau_pl and tau_pl approx. 1/Lambda_QCD approx 1 fm/c.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that plasma oscillations arising from back-reactions in pair creation are unlikely to affect the early stages of quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy ion collisions, because the plasma oscillation period is large (approximately 5 fm/c) compared to the formation and decay timescale of the plasma, unless the collision relaxation time is much larger than the plasma oscillation period.

## Method
The authors solved the quantum Vlasov equation for both bosons and fermions with a non-Markovian source term, coupled self-consistently to Maxwell's equation for the electric field including back-reactions. They used a relaxation-time approximation for collisions and solved the resulting system of integro-differential equations numerically on a momentum grid with 200 transverse and 400 longitudinal points, using a time step of 0.005 in units of the parton mass m.

## Key numbers
- Plasma oscillation period for bosons with initial field E(t=0)=10 (in units of m) and e^2=4: oscillations are evident with frequency increasing with field magnitude
- For the impulse configuration with A_0=10 and e^2=5, corresponding to initial field strength eE ~ 15σ (where σ is the QCD string tension) and energy density ½E² ~ 20σ², the plasma oscillation period is τ_pl = 5 fm/c
- The relaxation time τ_r is treated as a parameter; oscillations are unaffected when τ_r >> τ_pl, but are damped when τ_r ~ τ_pl
- The momentum grid used 200 transverse and 400 longitudinal points with time step dt = 0.005 and cutoff Λ = 50 in the regularisation integral
- The equilibrium temperature was set to T_eq = 1.0, maximum temperature T_m = 2.0, with profile width t_0² = 10 ~ τ_pl

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the Schwinger mechanism for pair creation in strong fields, particularly the back-reaction studies of Back (1993), BackA (1994), and Rau (1994), and the kinetic equation formulations of KM (1992) and GKM (1995). It builds on the non-Markovian source term derived by kme (1997) and the authors' own previous work (PRD, basti, basti1). The paper enables future studies of quark-gluon plasma formation to incorporate more realistic collision terms beyond the relaxation-time approximation and to account for finite interaction volumes and spatial inhomogeneity, as begun by mel (1998).
