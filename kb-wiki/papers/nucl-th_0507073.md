# STAR $ρ^0/π^-$ Ratio in Au-Au Peripheral Collisions at RHIC and the Vector Manifestation of Hidden Local Symmetry

> The hidden local symmetry effective theory, Wilsonian matched to QCD at a matching scale near the chiral scale $\sim 4πf_π$ as formulated by Harada and Yamawaki, presents a scenario drastically different from the standard scenario of heavy-ion collisions in that as the temperature approaches the critical temperature $T_c$ of chiral restoration from below, the mass of the vector mesons and their widths go to zero in the chiral limit or nearly zero otherwise, proportionally to some power of the quark condensate at what is called "vector manifestation (VM) fixed point". This scenario makes a striking prediction that as the fire-ball expands and cools from $T_c$ down toward the freezeout temperature $T_{freezeout}$ in heavy ion collisions, what we call "hadronic freedom" sets in in which hadrons do not interact or barely interact. We suggest that this scenario is testable at RHIC and as a specific example, we provide an astonishingly simple explanation in terms of the vector manifestation suitably extended to include axial-vector mesons for the anomalously large $ρ^0/π^-$ ratio measured in peripheral collisions by STAR.

## Key claim

The anomalously large ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio measured by STAR in peripheral Au-Au collisions can be explained by the "vector manifestation" (VM) fixed point scenario, where hadronic interactions become negligible near the critical temperature T_c, allowing ρ mesons to decay only at a "flash temperature" T_flash ≈ 120 MeV that coincides with the freezeout temperature in peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors apply the Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective theory, Wilsonian matched to QCD at a matching scale near 4πf_π, which predicts that the ρ meson mass and gauge coupling g vanish proportionally to the quark condensate as the critical temperature T_c is approached. They extend this VM scenario to include axial-vector a_1 mesons via the generalized vector manifestation (GVM), and calculate the ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio by counting the pions produced from the decay of the 32 degrees of freedom in an SU(4) multiplet (ρ, π, σ, a_1) at the flash temperature, assuming only one generation of decays occurs before freezeout.

## Key numbers

- STAR measured ρ⁰/π⁻ = 0.169 ± 0.003 (stat) ± 0.037 (syst) in Au+Au peripheral collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The predicted range from the VM scenario is 0.16 ≤ ρ⁰/π⁻ ≤ 0.21
- The lower bound (0.16) assumes all vector and axial-vector mesons decay at the flash temperature, yielding 3 ρ⁰ pions from 22 total π⁻ minus 3 reconstructed ρ⁰ pions
- The upper bound (0.21) assumes only 42 of 66 total pions are emitted at T ≈ 120 MeV
- The flash temperature is T_flash ≈ 120 MeV, extracted from Miller's lattice calculation of the gluon condensate
- The critical temperature is T_c ≈ 175 MeV
- At T = 120 MeV, the ρ meson is 90% on-shell with m_ρ^*/m_ρ = 0.90 and Γ^*/Γ = 0.67
- The standard equilibrium scenario would predict ρ⁰/π⁻ ∼ 4 × 10⁻⁴, a factor of ∼400 smaller than the STAR measurement
- The ρ meson width in free space is Γ ∼ 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (HY) and the Brown-Rho scaling framework to explain the STAR collaboration's unexpected finding that the ρ⁰/π⁻ ratio in peripheral Au-Au collisions is nearly as large as in proton-proton scattering (0.183 ± 0.027). The VM scenario contradicts the standard heavy-ion collision scenario (e.g., Rapp at SPS) where multiple generations of rescattering would reduce the ratio to equilibrium values. The calculation enables experimental tests of the VM fixed point near the chiral restoration temperature, and the authors note that lattice measurements of the vector meson pole mass at T_c would provide a crucial test.
