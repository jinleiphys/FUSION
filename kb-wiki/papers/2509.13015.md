# Nuclear Suppression in Diffractive Vector Meson Production within the Color Glass Condensate Framework

> We extend a recent global Bayesian analysis of diffractive $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production in $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework to investigate potential modifications of the nucleon structure inside nuclei. To this end, we perform fits that allow the effective nucleon structure parameters in Pb nuclei to differ from those of free protons. This approach directly addresses the question of whether the proton's spatial gluon distribution at intermediate to large $x$ is modified in the nuclear environment. We compare results obtained with shared and independent nucleon structure parameters and assess the impact on the simultaneous description of $γ+p$ data from HERA and the LHC, as well as $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ data from the LHC. Our findings show that there is no hint of difference in the nucleon structure beyond those already present in the CGC when embedding nucleons inside a nuclear environment.

## Key claim

The paper finds no evidence that the effective nucleon structure inside a lead nucleus differs from that of a free proton, beyond the modifications already present in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework when embedding nucleons in a nuclear environment. This conclusion is conditional on fixing the phenomenological K-factor to unity, meaning the analysis tests only whether separate nucleon structure parameters can resolve the tension between proton and lead data without absorbing other model uncertainties.

## Method

The authors perform a global Bayesian analysis of diffractive J/psi production in gamma+p collisions (HERA and LHC data) and gamma+Pb collisions (LHC data) within the CGC framework. They extend a previous analysis by allowing the effective nucleon size parameters B_G and hot spot size parameters B_{G,q} to differ between free protons and nucleons inside lead nuclei, while keeping the K-factor fixed to unity. The dipole amplitude is calculated from Wilson lines sampled using the McLerran-Venugopalan model at x_P = 0.01 and evolved to smaller x_P with the JIMWLK equation on an event-by-event basis, with 25 parameter sets sampled from the posterior distribution to propagate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The prior range for the proton size parameter B_G is [1, 10] GeV^{-2}, and for the hot spot size B_q is [0.05, 3] GeV^{-2}
- The prior range for the infrared regulator m is [0.02, 1.2] GeV, and for the JIMWLK infrared regulator m_JIMWLK is [0.02, 1.2] GeV
- The prior range for Lambda_QCD is [0.0001, 0.28] GeV
- The prior range for the magnitude of Q_s fluctuations sigma is [0, 1.5]
- The prior range for the ratio Q_s/(g^2 mu) is [0.05, 1.5]

## Context

This work extends a previous global Bayesian analysis by Mantysaari et al. (2025) that combined gamma+p and gamma+Pb diffractive J/psi data with an overall K-factor to absorb model uncertainties. The study addresses the persistent tension between CGC calculations constrained by HERA data and LHC measurements of coherent J/psi production in gamma+Pb collisions (ALICE 2023, CMS 2023), which show stronger nuclear suppression than predicted. The results enable future work to focus on other sources of nuclear suppression, such as the K-factor or higher-order corrections, rather than modifications to the nucleon structure parameters.
