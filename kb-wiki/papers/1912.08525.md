# Bohr Hamiltonian with Pöschl Teller potential in γ unstable and γ stable pictures

> In this paper, we present an analytical solution for the Bohr Hamiltonian with the trigonometric Pöschl Teller (P.T) potential in the cases of γ unstable nuclei and γ stable axially symmetric prolate deformed ones with γ = 0. The energy spectra and corresponding wave functions are derived by means of the asymptotic iteration method. In addition, B(E2) transition rates are calculated and compared with experimental data. Overall good agreement is obtained for inter and intra band transitions within ground state and \b{eta} bands. Our numerical results, particularly for transition rates are much closer to experimental ones in comparison with those obtained by Davidson and Kratzer potentials which are widely used in the literature.

## Key claim

The Bohr Hamiltonian with a trigonometric Poschl Teller potential provides analytical energy spectra and B(E2) transition rates for gamma-unstable and gamma-stable nuclei that are in good agreement with experiment, and the transition rates are "much closer to experimental ones" than those obtained with the widely used Davidson and Kratzer potentials, particularly because the Poschl Teller potential is flatter beyond its minimum.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian by separating variables for gamma-unstable nuclei (potential independent of gamma) and for gamma-stable axially symmetric prolate deformed nuclei (gamma approximately 0). The radial Schrodinger equation is solved using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) with an approximation to the centrifugal term. Energy spectra and wave functions are derived analytically, and B(E2) transition rates are computed from the resulting wave functions for inter-band and intra-band transitions within ground state and beta bands.

## Key numbers

* For the gamma-unstable case, the energy spectrum formula is epsilon^(gamma-uns) = (alpha^2/24)(L(L+1)/3 + 2) + 2 alpha^2 (n_beta + mu + nu)^2, where mu and nu are defined in terms of the potential parameters V1, V2, alpha, and the seniority quantum number tau.
* For the gamma-stable case, the energy spectrum formula is epsilon^(gamma-sta) = (alpha^2/24)(L(L+1)/3 + Lambda' + 2) + 2 alpha^2 (n_beta + mu + nu)^2, with Lambda' = 6c(n_gamma+1) - K^2/3.
* Over 27 studied nuclei in the gamma-unstable case, 78.9% are well reproduced with the Poschl Teller potential versus 10.5% for Davidson and 10.5% for Kratzer potentials.
* Over 24 studied nuclei in the gamma-stable case, 70.6% are well reproduced with the Poschl Teller potential versus 11.8% for Davidson and 17.6% for Kratzer potentials.
* The r.m.s. deviation sigma_r.m.s. for energy spectra is reported for each nucleus; for example, for 98Ru the values are 0.27 (Davidson), 0.81 (Kratzer), and 0.61 (Poschl Teller).

## Context

This work extends the analytical solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian beyond the critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) by introducing a trigonometric Poschl Teller potential, which has a similar shape to the Davidson potential for small deformations but is flatter for larger beta values. The results are compared with the Davidson potential calculations of Bonatsos (2004) and the Kratzer potential calculations of Bonatsos (2006), both of which used a Deformation Dependent Mass formalism. The Poschl Teller potential's double-well structure, arising from the periodicity of trigonometric functions, opens the possibility for future studies of shape coexistence in nuclei within the Bohr Mottelson model.
