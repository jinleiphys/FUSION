# Elliptic flow from a parton cascade

> The dependence of elliptic flow at RHIC energies on the effective parton scattering cross section is calculated using the ZPC parton cascade model. We show that the v_2 measure of elliptic flow saturates early in the evolution before the hadronization transition to a rather large value ~0.05-0.15 as σ_g varies from 2-10 mb and thus is a sensitive probe of the dynamics in the plasma phase.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) saturates early (before hadronization) to values of 0.05–0.15 when the effective gluon-gluon scattering cross section \(\sigma_g\) varies from 2–10 mb, but this strong sensitivity to the cross section means that dissipative corrections are large and the system is far from the ideal fluid limit.

## Method

The authors used the ZPC parton cascade model, which includes only elastic gluon-gluon scattering with a cross section regulated by an effective screening mass \(\mu\). Initial conditions were idealized: partons uniformly distributed in the overlapping region of two 5 fm radius disks at impact parameter \(b=7.5\) fm, with a uniform rapidity distribution from \(-5\) to \(+5\), a thermal momentum distribution at temperature 500 MeV, and a formation proper time \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) was computed as the single-particle average of \(\cos(2\phi) = (p_x^2-p_y^2)/(p_x^2+p_y^2)\) over partons.

## Key numbers

- For a gluon-gluon cross section of 3 mb at impact parameter \(b=7.5\) fm, the final \(v_2\) after the partonic stage is around 0.1.
- As \(\sigma_g\) varies from 2–10 mb, \(v_2\) ranges from approximately 0.05 to 0.15.
- The initial gluon rapidity density from HIJING for \(b=0\) is \(\mathrm{d}N_g/\mathrm{d}y \approx 300\).
- The formation proper time used is \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm.
- The strong coupling constant \(\alpha\) is taken as 0.47.
- The transverse radius of each nucleus is 5 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow at lower energies (Sorge 1997, Jolli 1994) by focusing on the partonic stage at RHIC energies, where copious mini-jet production creates a high-density parton system before hadronization. The results show that elliptic flow is a sensitive probe of the parton scattering cross section and thus of plasma dynamics, but the authors note that hadronic rescattering (studied by Sorge 1997) and different initial conditions (e.g., from the HIJING model by Wang and Gyulassy 1991) could modify the signal; a comprehensive transport model combining ZPC with the ART hadron cascade is planned to address these effects.
