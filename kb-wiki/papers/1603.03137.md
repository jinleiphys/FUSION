# Low-lying Resonances and Relativistic Screening in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We explore effects of the screening due to the relativistic electron-positron plasma and presence of resonances in the secondary reactions leading to A=7 nuclei during the Big Bang Nucleosynthesis. In particular, we investigate and examine possible low-lying resonances in the $^7$Be($^3$He, $γ$)$^{10}$C reaction and examine the resultant destruction of $^7$Be for various resonance locations and strengths. While a resonance in the $^{10}$C compound nucleus is thought to have negligible effects we explore the possibility of an enhancement from plasma screening that may adjust the final $^7$Be abundance. We find the effects of relativistic screening and possible low-lying resonances to be relatively small in the standard Early Universe models.

## Key claim

The paper finds that both relativistic electron-positron plasma screening and possible low-lying resonances in the $^7$Be($^3$He,$\gamma$)$^{10}$C reaction have relatively small effects on the final $^7$Be abundance in standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis models, with the caveat that this conclusion holds for the standard Early Universe scenario and that a higher density at lower temperatures could potentially alter the outcome.

## Method

The authors incorporated relativistic electron-positron plasma screening into a BBN nuclear reaction network by computing the Debye length from the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the photon propagator and applying the resulting enhancement factor to reaction rates. They also assumed a resonance in the $^7$Be($^3$He,$\gamma$)$^{10}$C reaction with an energy-dependent entrance channel width derived from Coulomb functions, and scanned across resonance energies and strengths to map the relative reduction of $^7$Be mass fractions.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic Debye length at T=1 MeV for $Z_1=Z_2=2$ is $\lambda_D \approx 10^4$ fm, giving a Salpeter correction $f_D-1 \approx 6 \times 10^{-4}$.
- The energy shift from relativistic screening for $Z_1=2$ and $Z_2=4$ at T$\sim$2 MeV is approximately 20 keV.
- The relative difference between the exact screening correction and the Salpeter correction factor is on the order of $10^{-4}$ (0.01%) for $\Gamma_\gamma=110$ keV, $\Gamma_p=2$ keV at $E_R\approx 500$ keV.
- For decay widths $\Gamma_x \lesssim 50$ keV, the resonant rates for the $^7$Be($^3$He,$\gamma$)$^{10}$C reaction are less than the non-resonant rate.
- The decay width of the 17.79 MeV state in the $^{10}$Be mirror nucleus (0.53 MeV above threshold) is $110 \pm 35$ keV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Coulomb screening in BBN by Wang (2010), which considered only non-relativistic screening, and incorporates the relativistic electron-positron plasma screening recently shown to be crucial for neutrino interactions by Vassh (2015). The paper also revisits the possibility of $^7$Be destruction via a resonant reaction through $^{10}$C, previously explored by Hammache (2013) and Broggini (2012), finding that even with relativistic screening included, the effects remain negligible in the standard BBN scenario. The results confirm the robustness of standard BBN predictions ahead of future high-precision measurements from instruments like the Thirty Meter Telescope.
