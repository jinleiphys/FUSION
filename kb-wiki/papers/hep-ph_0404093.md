# Nuclear parton distribution functions and their uncertainties

> We analyze experimental data of nuclear structure-function ratios F_2^A/F_2^{A'} and Drell-Yan cross section ratios for obtaining optimum parton distribution functions (PDFs) in nuclei. Then, uncertainties of the nuclear PDFs are estimated by the Hessian method. Valence-quark distributions are determined by the F_2 data at large x; however, the small-x part is not obvious from the data. On the other hand, the antiquark distributions are determined well at x~0.01 from the F_2 data and at x~0.1 by the Drell-Yan data; however, the large-x behavior is not clear. Gluon distributions cannot be fixed by the present data and they have large uncertainties in the whole x region. Parametrization results are shown in comparison with the data. We provide a useful code for calculating nuclear PDFs at given x and Q^2.

## Key claim

The authors determine optimum nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) by a χ² analysis of nuclear structure-function ratios and Drell-Yan cross section ratios, but they note that the gluon distributions cannot be fixed by the present data and have large uncertainties in the whole x region, while valence-quark distributions at small x and antiquark distributions at large x also remain poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors parametrize nuclear modifications as weight functions multiplying nucleonic PDFs (MRST01-LO) at Q₀² = 1 GeV², assuming a simple 1 − 1/A^α dependence for the A-dependence. They perform a leading-order χ² fit using 951 data points from F₂^A/F₂^D ratios (606 points), F₂^A/F₂^A' ratios with A' ≠ D (293 points), and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios (52 points), evolving the NPDFs via DGLAP equations to experimental Q² values. Uncertainties are estimated using the Hessian method with a confidence level corresponding to Δχ² = 10.427 for the nine free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Total χ² = 1489.8 for 951 data points, giving χ² per degree of freedom = 1.58
- Valence-quark parameters: b = 2.894 ± 0.395, c = −9.390 ± 1.068, d = 7.308 ± 0.866
- Antiquark parameters: a = −0.3794 ± 0.0461, b = 8.626 ± 1.551, c = −56.64 ± 11.84, d = 94.11 ± 27.57
- Gluon parameters: b = 2.165 ± 3.126, d = 1.349 ± 44.56 (c_g fixed to 0.000)
- Smallest x value with Q² ≥ 1 GeV² in the data set is 0.0055
- The initial scale is chosen as Q₀² = 1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the earlier NPDF analysis of Hirai, Kumano, and Miyama (2001) by adding Drell-Yan data from Fermilab-E772 and E866/NuSea collaborations, HERMES data for nitrogen and krypton, NMC data for F₂^A/F₂^A' ratios, and including charm-quark distributions. The Hessian uncertainty estimation follows the methodology used for unpolarized PDFs by the CTEQ collaboration and for polarized PDFs by the Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC, 2003). The results enable more reliable calculations of cross sections for heavy-ion reactions searching for quark-gluon plasma signatures and for neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments, though future data from neutrino facilities (NuMI, neutrino factories) and large-x Drell-Yan experiments are needed to reduce the remaining uncertainties.
