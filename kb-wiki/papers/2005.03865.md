# Static quadrupole moments of nuclear chiral doublet bands

> The static quadrupole moments (SQMs) of nuclear chiral doublet bands are investigated for the first time taking the particle-hole configuration $π(1h_{11/2}) \otimes ν(1h_{11/2})^{-1}$ with triaxial deformation parameters in the range $260^\circ \leq γ\leq 270^\circ$ as examples. The behavior of the SQM as a function of spin $I$ is illustrated by analyzing the components of the total angular momentum. It is found that in the region of chiral vibrations the SQMs of doublet bands are strongly varying with $I$, whereas in the region of static chirality the SQMs of doublet bands are almost constant. Hence, the measurement of SQMs provides a new criterion for distinguishing the modes of nuclear chirality. Moreover, in the high-spin region the SQMs can be approximated by an analytic formula with a proportionality to $\cosγ$ for both doublet bands. This provides a way to extract experimentally the triaxial deformation parameter $γ$ for chiral bands from the measured SQMs.

## Key claim

The static quadrupole moment (SQM) provides a new criterion for distinguishing between chiral vibration and static chirality in nuclear chiral doublet bands, but this distinction is conditional on the triaxial deformation parameter \(\gamma\) deviating from \(270^\circ\); at \(\gamma = 270^\circ\) the SQMs vanish identically for both bands.

## Method

The particle-rotor model (PRM) was used to calculate static quadrupole moments for the particle-hole configuration \(\pi(1h_{11/2}) \otimes \nu(1h_{11/2})^{-1}\) coupled to a triaxial rigid rotor. The PRM Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the strong-coupling basis, and the SQM for each band was computed from the resulting wave functions using the quadrupole operator in the laboratory frame. The calculations were performed for six values of the triaxial deformation parameter \(\gamma\) in the range \(260^\circ \leq \gamma \leq 270^\circ\), with fixed deformation parameter \(\beta = 0.23\) and moments of inertia given by the irrotational flow formula.

## Key numbers

* For \(\gamma = 270^\circ\), the SQMs of both yrast and yrare bands are zero over the entire spin region.
* The empirical electric quadrupole moment was set to \(Q_0^\prime = 3.5\;e\text{b}\).
* The deformation parameter was fixed at \(\beta = 0.23\).
* The coupling coefficients were \(C_p = 0.32\;\text{MeV}\) and \(C_n = -0.32\;\text{MeV}\).
* The moment of inertia parameter was \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 30\;\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\).
* For the yrast band at high spin, the SQM is approximated by \(Q(I) = \frac{3I^2 - I(I+1)}{(I+1)(2I+3)} Q_0^\prime \cos\gamma\).
* For the yrare band at high spin, the SQM is approximated by \(Q(I) = \frac{3(I+3/2)^2 - I(I+1)}{(I+1)(2I+3)} Q_0^\prime \cos\gamma\).
* Static chirality occurs in the spin region \(15 \leq I \leq 18\hbar\) for the studied configuration.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear chirality, previously focused on energy spectra and electromagnetic transition strengths (Frauendorf 1997, Koike 2004, Grodner 2006, Tonev 2006, Mukhopadhyay 2007, Qi 2009), to the static quadrupole moment as a new observable. It follows recent measurements of the \(g\)-factor in chiral bands (Grodner 2018) and provides a method to extract the triaxial deformation parameter \(\gamma\) from experimentally measured SQMs, enabling a new experimental test of chiral symmetry breaking in nuclei.
