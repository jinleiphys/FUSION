# Calculation of stellar electron-capture cross sections on nuclei based on microscopic Skyrme functionals

> A fully self-consistent microscopic framework for evaluation of nuclear weak-interaction rates at finite temperature is introduced, based on Skyrme functionals. The single-nucleon basis and the corresponding thermal occupation factors of the initial nuclear state are determined in the finite-temperature Skyrme Hartree-Fock model, and charge-exchange transitions to excited states are computed using the finite-temperature RPA. Effective interactions are implemented self-consistently: both the finite-temperature single-nucleon Hartree-Fock equations and the matrix equations of RPA are based on the same Skyrme energy density functional. Using a representative set of Skyrme functionals, the model is applied in the calculation of stellar electron-capture cross sections for selected nuclei in the iron mass group and for neutron-rich Ge isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a fully self-consistent microscopic framework for calculating stellar electron-capture cross sections based on Skyrme energy density functionals, but the authors explicitly state that the RPA approach reproduces only the centroid and total GT strength, not the detailed fragmentation of strength distributions that requires shell-model calculations including higher-order correlations.

## Method

The single-nucleon basis and thermal occupation factors of the initial nuclear state were determined using finite-temperature Skyrme Hartree-Fock (FTSHF) with Fermi-Dirac occupation factors, and charge-exchange transitions to excited states were computed using finite-temperature random-phase approximation (FTRPA). Both the FTSHF equations and the matrix equations of RPA were based on the same Skyrme energy density functional, ensuring self-consistency. The model was applied to calculate stellar electron-capture cross sections for selected nuclei in the iron mass group (A ≈ 45-65) and for neutron-rich Ge isotopes, using a representative set of Skyrme functionals.

## Key numbers

- Electron capture cross sections were calculated for temperatures in the range T = 0.5-1.5 MeV, relevant for stellar electron capture processes
- For ⁷⁴Ge, the GT⁻ resonance appears at approximately 14 MeV excitation energy
- The Ikeda sum rule (S_GT⁻ - S_GT⁺) = 3(N-Z) was verified to be satisfied at each temperature
- The weak interaction form factors used include: F₁^V(q²) = [1 + (q/840 MeV)²]⁻², μ^V(q²) = 4.706[1 + (q/840 MeV)²]⁻², and F_A(q²) = -1.262[1 + (q/1032 MeV)²]⁻²
- The phase transition from superfluid to normal state in nuclei occurs at temperatures T ≈ 0.5-1 MeV, while contributions from continuum states become large above T ≈ 4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid SMMC/RPA model introduced by Langanke, Dean, and collaborators (2001) by providing a fully self-consistent microscopic alternative based on Skyrme functionals, which can be applied over arbitrary mass regions without additional parameter adjustments. The approach contrasts with large-scale shell-model diagonalization calculations by Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000) for the pf-shell, which provide detailed spectroscopy but cannot be systematically extended to heavier nuclei beyond A ≈ 65 due to huge configuration spaces. The framework enables future systematic calculations of weak-interaction rates for neutron-rich nuclei far from stability, where experimental data on ground-state properties and excitations are not available.
