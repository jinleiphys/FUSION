# Probing strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas from higher curvature gravity

> We consider five-dimensional AdS-axion-dilaton gravity with a Gauss-Bonnet term and use a black brane solution displaying spatial anisotropy as the gravity dual of a strongly coupled anisotropic plasma. We compute several observables relevant to the study of the plasma, namely, the drag force, the jet quenching parameter, the quarkonium potential and the thermal photon production. The effects of higher derivative corrections and of the anisotropy are discussed and compared with previous results.

## Key claim

The paper computes several QGP observables (drag force, jet quenching parameter, quarkonium potential, and thermal photon production) in a holographic model that simultaneously incorporates spatial anisotropy (via an axion field linear in one spatial direction) and higher-curvature corrections (via a Gauss-Bonnet term), finding that the combined effects are qualitatively consistent with interpreting the Gauss-Bonnet coupling as mimicking finite-'t Hooft coupling corrections, but the authors caution that the dual field theory is not precisely known and the approach is "bottom-up" rather than a precise top-down derivation.

## Method

The authors use a five-dimensional AdS-axion-dilaton gravity action with a Gauss-Bonnet term and a black brane solution that is spatially anisotropic due to an axion field linear in the z-direction, χ = a z. They work in the small-anisotropy limit (expanding to O(a²)) and compute the drag force, jet quenching parameter, quarkonium static potential, and thermal photon production rate using standard holographic prescriptions (trailing string, light-like Wilson loop, U-shaped string, and retarded correlator of the electromagnetic current, respectively). The results are expressed as functions of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling λ_GB, the anisotropy parameter a, and the temperature T.

## Key numbers

* Drag force: For motion parallel to the anisotropic direction, the normalized drag force F_drag^|| / F_drag^iso is enhanced for λ_GB > 0 and reduced for λ_GB < 0, with the isotropic N=4 SYM result F_drag^iso = π² T² v / √(1-v²) recovered when a → 0 and λ_GB → 0.
* Jet quenching parameter: The normalized jet quenching parameter q̂/q̂_iso is increased for λ_GB > 0 and decreased for λ_GB < 0, where the isotropic result is q̂_iso = π^(3/2) Γ(3/4)/Γ(5/4) √λ T³; the anisotropy increases q̂, with the largest increase when the quark moves in the anisotropic direction.
* Quarkonium screening length: For λ_GB = 0.1 and a/T ≈ 0.3, the screening length L_s decreases relative to the isotropic case; for λ_GB = -0.1, L_s increases. The screening length for a pair oriented along the anisotropic direction (θ=0) is always smaller than for a pair oriented transversely (θ=π/2).
* Temperature and entropy: The temperature is T = √B₀ [1/(π u_h) - (2B₀ - 6λ_GB + √λ_GB log((1+2√λ_GB)/(1-2√λ_GB)) - log(4B₀/√(1-4λ_GB)))/(48π(1-4λ_GB)) u_h a² + O(a⁴)], and the entropy density is s = (π/(4G B₀^(3/2))) (π² T³ + (1/8) T B₀ a² + O(a⁴)).

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic holographic plasma model of Mateos and Trancanelli (2011) by adding a Gauss-Bonnet term, which simulates finite-'t Hooft coupling corrections in a simpler framework than the α'³R⁴ corrections of type IIB superstring theory. It generalizes prior computations of the drag force (Chernicoff et al., 2012 for anisotropy; Fadafan, 2008 for Gauss-Bonnet), jet quenching parameter (Giataganas, 2012; Chernicoff et al., 2012), quarkonium potential (Fadafan et al., 2013; Giataganas, 2012), and photon production (various authors) to the combined case. The results enable qualitative understanding of how both anisotropy and higher-curvature corrections affect QGP observables, and the authors note that the decreasing effect of negative λ_GB on the jet quenching parameter is interesting because weak-coupling models give lower values than the strong-coupling N=4 SYM result.
