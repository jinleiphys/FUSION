# Momentum dependent mean-field dynamics of compressed nuclear matter and neutron stars

> Nuclear matter and compact neutron stars are studied in the framework of the non-linear derivative (NLD) model which accounts for the momentum dependence of relativistic mean-fields. The generalized form of the energy-momentum tensor is derived which allows to consider different forms of the regulator functions in the NLD Lagrangian. The thermodynamic consistency of the NLD model is demonstrated for arbitrary choice of the regulator functions. The NLD approach describes the bulk properties of the nuclear matter and compares well with microscopic calculations and Dirac phenomenology. We further study the high density domain of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) relevant for the matter in $β$-equilibrium inside neutron stars. It is shown that the low density constraints imposed on the nuclear EoS and by the momentum dependence of the Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential lead to a maximum mass of the neutron stars around $M \simeq 2 M_{\odot}$ which accommodates the observed mass of the J1614-2230 millisecond radio pulsar.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the non-linear derivative (NLD) model, which incorporates momentum-dependent relativistic mean-fields, can simultaneously satisfy low-density constraints on the nuclear equation of state and the empirical energy dependence of the Schrodinger-equivalent optical potential, leading to a maximum neutron star mass around 2 solar masses that accommodates the observed mass of the J1614-2230 pulsar. The hedge is that this result depends on the specific choice of regulator functions and the low-density constraints imposed on the nuclear EoS.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with non-linear derivative (NLD) interactions, where higher-order derivative operators in the meson-nucleon vertices act as cut-off form factors to regulate high-momentum components. They derived the generalized Euler-Lagrange equations, Noether current, and energy-momentum tensor for arbitrary regulator functions, then applied the model to infinite nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state. The high-density domain of the EoS was studied for matter in beta-equilibrium inside neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass around M ≃ 2 M⊙, which accommodates the observed mass of the J1614-2230 millisecond radio pulsar
- The proton-nucleus optical potential starts to level off at incident energies of about 300 MeV, based on analyses of proton-nucleus scattering data from Cooper (1993) and Hama (1990)
- The NLD model describes bulk properties of nuclear matter around saturation density and compares well with microscopic calculations and Dirac phenomenology

## Context

This work extends the non-linear derivative (NLD) approach previously introduced by Gaitanos (2011) and Gaitanos (2009), which used a particular exponential form of the regulators, to a generalized formalism allowing arbitrary regulator functions. The model addresses the well-known limitation of standard RHD models (Walecka, 1974; Serot, 1997) where nucleon selfenergies depend only on density and produce a divergent energy dependence of the optical potential at high energies, contradicting Dirac phenomenology (Cooper, 1993; Hama, 1990). The NLD approach provides an alternative to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations (Haar, 1986; Brockmann, 1996) that is more tractable for applications in transport theory and heavy-ion collisions, while maintaining thermodynamic consistency.
