# On the definition of the nucleon axial charge density

> We work out the spatial density distributions corresponding to the axial-vector charge density operator for spin-1/2 systems using states described by sharply localized wave packets in arbitrary Lorentz-frames. The static approximation, leading to the frequently assumed Breit-frame distributions, is also considered. We discuss the interpretation of the resulting spatial densities in terms of the axial charge density.

# On the definition of the nucleon axial charge density

## Key claim
The spatial distributions obtained from the axial-vector charge density operator using sharply localized wave packets vanish identically in the zero average momentum frame (ZAMF), but a physically meaningful axial charge density can be defined by dropping the overall spin-dependent prefactor that does not encode information about the internal structure of the system. The resulting axial charge density in the ZAMF and infinite-momentum frame (IMF) coincides with the corresponding expressions for the electric charge density upon replacing the electric form factor by the axial one.

## Method
The authors compute matrix elements of the zeroth component of the axial-vector current operator for spin-1/2 systems using spherically symmetric, sharply localized wave packet states in the ZAMF and in moving frames, applying the method of dimensional counting to evaluate the R→0 limit. They also consider the static approximation (naive definition) by expanding the integrand in powers of 1/m before localizing the wave packet, which leads to the traditional Breit-frame distributions. The resulting spatial distributions are interpreted by separating structure-independent spin-velocity prefactors from the frame-independent factors containing the axial form factor information.

## Key numbers
- The axial charge density in the ZAMF yields a mean square radius ⟨r²⟩_A = 4 G_A'(0), where G_A'(0) is the derivative of the axial form factor at q²=0.
- The conventional (naive/Breit-frame) definition yields a mean square radius ⟨r²⟩_{A,naive} = 6 G_A'(0).
- In the IMF, the axial charge density reduces to a delta-function δ(r_∥) in the longitudinal direction multiplied by a two-dimensional Fourier transform of G_A(-q_⊥²) in the transverse plane.
- For the naive definition in moving frames, the leading-order 1/m expansion gives J^0_{A,naive,v}(r) = (v·σ) ∫ d³q/(2π)³ G_A(-q²) e^{-iq·r}.

## Context
This work extends the novel definition of three-dimensional spatial densities using sharply localized wave packets developed by Epelbaum et al. (2022) and Panteleeva et al. (2022-2023) from the electromagnetic case to the axial-vector current. It addresses the claim by Chen et al. (2024) that the axial charge density vanishes identically in the Breit frame, confirming that result but showing that a proper definition exists after removing the spin-dependent prefactor. The paper also contrasts with the traditional Breit-frame interpretation originating from Hofstadter (1958), Ernst, Sachs, and Wali (1960), and Sachs (1962), which is appropriate for heavy systems but problematic for light systems whose Compton wavelength is comparable to their characteristic radii. The results enable consistent definitions of axial charge densities for systems of any mass, including light hadrons.
