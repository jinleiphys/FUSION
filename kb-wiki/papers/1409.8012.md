# Examination of the stability of a rod-shaped structure in $^{24}$Mg

> The stable existence of a six-$α$ linear structure in highly excited states of $^{24}$Mg is studied based on a systematic Cranked Hartree-Fock calculation with various Skyrme-type interactions. Its stability is examined by allowing the transition of the cluster structure to the shell-model like structure. Especially, the six-$α$ linear state is exposed to two major instabilities: the bending motion, which is the main path for the transition to low-lying states, and the spin-orbit interaction, which is the driving force to break the $α$ clusters and enhance the independent motion of the nucleons. The linear structure with large angular momentum is obtained as a meta-stable stationary state.

## Key claim

The six-alpha linear (rod-shaped) structure in highly excited states of $^{24}$Mg is obtained as a meta-stable stationary state under rotation, but its stability is conditional: it exists only within a specific range of rotational frequencies and depends on the initial configuration, with the rod shape surviving even when three-dimensional distortions are introduced in the initial state.

## Method

The authors performed systematic cranked Hartree-Fock calculations using nine different Skyrme-type interactions (SV-bas, SV-min, SkI3, SkI4, SLy4, SLy6, SkM*, SkP, SkT6) for $^{24}$Mg. They prepared initial states with six alpha clusters in a linear rod configuration and bent configurations with opening angles of $2\pi/3$, $\pi/3$, and $\pi/6$, as well as a three-dimensionally distorted initial state, and evolved them under the variational principle $\delta H' = 0$ with $H' = H - \omega J$ for rotational frequencies $\omega$ from 0.0 to 1.5 MeV/$\hbar$. The final states were classified as compact shape, rod shape, or fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The rod-shaped structure is obtained as a meta-stable stationary state even at $\omega = 0.0$ MeV/$\hbar$ when starting from the pure rod initial state (i), independent of the Skyrme interaction parameter set.
- The lowest excitation energy for the rod shape (SV-bas, initial state (i), $\omega = 0$ MeV/$\hbar$) is $E_x = 78.9$ MeV.
- The rod shape appears in the region of $E_x = 86.7$ MeV measured from the ground state of $^{24}$Mg, corresponding to 75.1 MeV above the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C threshold.
- The rod shapes give large angular momenta around $J = 19$ to $37 \hbar$ for the SV-bas parameter set.
- The angular momentum for rod shape formation must be between 19.2 and 36.9 $\hbar$, and the incident energy must be larger than 75.1 MeV for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C reaction.
- The impact parameter must be smaller than 5.49 fm (twice the $^{12}$C radius) for rod shape formation.
- Without the spin-orbit interaction, the region of rod shapes increases to $\omega = 0.0$ to 1.3 MeV/$\hbar$, showing enhanced stability of alpha clusters.
- With the spin-orbit interaction, the rod shape exists for $\omega$ between 0.8 and 1.3 MeV/$\hbar$ for the SV-bas parameter set starting from initial state (ii).

## Context

This work extends the earlier cranked Hartree-Fock study by Flocard et al. (1984) on four-alpha linear configurations in $^{16}$O, which could not treat the rod-shaped structure in $^{24}$Mg due to restricted model space. It also builds on the finding by Ichikawa et al. that four-alpha rod-shaped configurations can be stabilized despite bending and spin-orbit instabilities. The paper addresses the long-standing search for six-alpha cluster states in $^{24}$Mg, following experimental suggestions by Wuosmaa et al. and Rae et al. of a candidate at $E_x = 32.5$ MeV above the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C threshold, which was later explained by Hirabayashi et al. without requiring a specific rod shape. The results enable experimental searches for the rod-shaped state in $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C collisions at specific incident energies and angular momenta.
