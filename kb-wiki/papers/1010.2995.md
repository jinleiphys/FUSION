# Production of light nuclei, hypernuclei and their antiparticles in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We present, using the statistical model, an analysis of the production of light nuclei, hypernuclei and their antiparticles in central collisions of heavy nuclei. Based on these studies we provide predictions for the production yields of multiply-strange light nuclei.

## Key claim

The thermal (hadro-chemical equilibrium) model, using only temperature T and baryo-chemical potential μb as parameters, can describe the production yields of light nuclei, hypernuclei, and their antiparticles in central relativistic nuclear collisions; however, the measured ratio of hypertritium to helium-3 at RHIC energy (200 GeV) is underpredicted by about two standard deviations, suggesting either a new production mechanism or the existence of an excited 3/2+ state of (anti)hypertritium not yet included in the model.

## Method

The authors performed a thermal model analysis of hadron yields measured in central heavy-ion collisions from AGS to RHIC energies, fitting the chemical freeze-out temperature T and baryo-chemical potential μb (plus fireball volume V for absolute yields) to experimental data including the newly measured ³Hē/³He ratio from STAR. They then extended the same thermal model parameters to predict yields of light nuclei, hypernuclei, and their antiparticles, incorporating canonical strangeness suppression at lower energies and feed-down corrections from strong decays of excited baryonic states.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV): T = 164 MeV, V = 1960 fm³, μb = 24 ± 2 MeV (including ³Hē/³He ratio); without that ratio, μb = 30 ± 4 MeV
- Measured ³Hē/³He ratio at RHIC: 0.45 ± 0.02 ± 0.04; model prediction: 0.42 ± 0.03
- Measured ³Λ̄H̄/³ΛH ratio at RHIC: 0.49 ± 0.18 ± 0.07; model prediction: 0.45 ± 0.03
- Measured ³ΛH/³He ratio at RHIC: 0.82 ± 0.16 ± 0.12; model prediction: 0.35 ± 0.003
- For T = 5 MeV (binding energy scale), the calculated ³Hē/³He ratio would be 3.1 × 10⁻¹³, versus 0.415 at T = 164 MeV
- At LHC energy (2.76 TeV): predicted ³He/⁴He ratio = 2.76 × 10⁻³, ³Hē/⁴Hē ratio = 2.70 × 10⁻³; at RHIC (200 GeV): 3.13 × 10⁻³ and 2.37 × 10⁻³ respectively
- At FAIR energy, predicted production rate for ⁷ΛΛΞHe is about 60 per month per 10⁶ central Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses previously established by Andronic et al. (2005, 2008, 2009) for hadron production to include light nuclei and hypernuclei, building on earlier thermal model studies of light nuclei by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995, 2001) and coalescence model work by Baltz and Dover. The paper confronts model predictions with the first measurement of (anti)hypertritium by the STAR collaboration at RHIC, and provides predictions for the FAIR and LHC energy regimes. The results enable future experimental tests of thermal production mechanisms for multi-strange hypernuclei and can constrain the QCD phase boundary through precision measurements of baryo-chemical potential.
