# Interplay between kaon condensation and hyperons in highly dense matter

> Possible coexistence and/or competition of kaon condensation with hyperons are investigated in hyperonic matter, where hyperons are mixed in the ground state of neutron-star matter. The formulation is based on the effective chiral Lagrangian for the kaon-baryon interaction and the nonrelativistic baryon-baryon interaction model. First, the onset condition of the s-wave kaon condensation realized from hyperonic matter is reexamined. It is shown that the usual assumption of the continuous phase transition is not always kept valid in the presence of the negatively charged hyperons. Second, the equation of state (EOS) of the kaon-condensed phase in hyperonic matter is discussed. In the case of the stronger kaon-baryon attractive interaction, it is shown that a local energy minimum with respect to the baryon number density appears as a result of considerable softening of the EOS due to both kaon condensation and hyperon-mixing and recovering of the stiffness of the EOS at very high densities. This result implies a possible existence of self-bound objects with kaon condensates on any scale from an atomic nucleus to a neutron star.

## Key claim

The usual assumption of a continuous phase transition for kaon condensation (where kaon condensates develop smoothly from zero amplitude above a critical density) is not always valid in hyperonic matter when negatively charged hyperons ($\Sigma^-$) are already present; instead, there can be a discontinuous transition between a noncondensed state with $\Sigma^-$ mixing and a condensed state without $\Sigma^-$ mixing over a small density interval, particularly for stronger kaon-baryon attractive interactions.

## Method

The author uses an effective chiral SU(3)$_L \times$ SU(3)$_R$ Lagrangian for the kaon-baryon interaction combined with a nonrelativistic baryon-baryon interaction model parameterized to reproduce nuclear saturation properties and hyperon potential depths from hypernuclear experiments. The total effective energy density is constructed under charge neutrality and baryon number conservation, and the ground state is obtained variationally with respect to the chiral angle $\theta$, charge chemical potential $\mu$, and baryon number densities. Two parameter sets for the baryon-baryon interaction (H-EOS A and H-EOS B) are used, with different incompressibilities and density dependencies of the repulsive interactions.

## Key numbers

- For H-EOS (A), the $\Lambda$ hyperon starts to mix at $\rho_{\rm B} = 0.340$ fm$^{-3}$ (2.13 $\rho_0$) and the $\Sigma^-$ hyperon at $\rho_{\rm B} = 0.525$ fm$^{-3}$ (3.28 $\rho_0$), where $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is nuclear saturation density.
- For H-EOS (B), the $\Lambda$ hyperon starts to mix at $\rho_{\rm B} \sim 0.44$ fm$^{-3}$ (2.69 $\rho_0$) and the $\Sigma^-$ hyperon at $\rho_{\rm B} \sim 0.59$ fm$^{-3}$ (3.69 $\rho_0$).
- The $K^-$ optical potential at nuclear saturation density is estimated as $-$115 MeV for the stronger kaon-baryon attraction ($a_3 = -0.9$, giving $\Sigma_{Kn} = 305$ MeV) and $-$95 MeV for the weaker attraction ($a_3 = -0.7$, giving $\Sigma_{Kn} = 207$ MeV).
- The onset density for kaon condensation from the continuous phase transition condition is $\rho_{\rm B}^{c(2)}(K^-) = 0.6433$ fm$^{-3}$ (4.02 $\rho_0$) for $\Sigma_{Kn} = 305$ MeV with H-EOS (A).
- At high densities, the $\Sigma^-$ and proton fractions reach 20-30%, the $\Lambda$ fraction reaches 30-40%, and the total negative strangeness fraction reaches 50-60%.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of kaon condensation in neutron-star matter (Kaplan and Nelson 1986; Muto, Tatsumi, and Tamagaki 1993; Thorsson, Prakash, and Lattimer 1994; Lee, Brown, and Min 1995; Fujii, Muto, Tatsumi, and Tamagaki 1996) by reexamining the onset condition in hyperonic matter where hyperons are already mixed. It contradicts the common assumption of continuous phase transition used in earlier works (Pons, Baldo, and Palacios 1997; Ellis, Kapusta, and Olive 1995; Schaffner and Mishustin 1996) and shows that the interplay between kaon condensation and $\Sigma^-$ hyperons can produce a local energy minimum (density isomer state), implying possible self-bound objects with kaon condensates on scales from atomic nuclei to neutron stars. The results enable further investigation of first-order phase transitions in neutron-star interiors and the existence of self-bound kaon-condensed objects.
