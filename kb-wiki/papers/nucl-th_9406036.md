# Isolated States

> We show that a quantum system with nonlocal interaction can have bound states of unusual type (isolated states (IS)). IS is a bound state that do not generate a $S$-matrix pole. IS can have positive as well as negative energy and can be treated as a generalization of bound states embedded in continuum on the case of discrete spectrum states. Formation of IS in the spectrum of quantum system is studied using a simple rank--2 separable potential with harmonic oscillator formfactors. Some physical applications are discussed, in particular, we propose separable $NN$ potential that describes not only most important two-nucleon data (deuteron binding energy and s-wave triplet and singlet scattering phases) but also the trinucleon binding energy without making use of three-body forces.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a quantum system with a nonlocal interaction can have bound states (isolated states, IS) that do not generate an S-matrix pole, including states with negative energy; this generalizes the concept of bound states embedded in the continuum (BSEC) to discrete spectrum states, but only under the specific condition that the truncated Hamiltonian matrix and its principal minor share common eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors use the J-matrix formulation of scattering theory, expanding continuum wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis, and study a rank-2 separable potential with harmonic oscillator formfactors. They analyze the condition for isolated states by requiring that the truncated Hamiltonian matrix \(H^N\) and its principal minor \(H^{N-1}\) have common eigenvalues, and demonstrate the formation of IS through an analytically solvable model where the off-diagonal potential matrix elements cancel the kinetic energy matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy \(E_d\) and deuteron rms radius \(\langle r_0^2 \rangle^{1/2}\) are reproduced by the fitted potential.
- Singlet \(s\)-wave potential parameter: \(V^s_{11} = -365.75\) MeV.
- Triplet \(s\)-wave potential parameter: \(V^t_{11} = -407.56\) MeV.
- Oscillator energy parameter: \(\hbar \omega = 500\) MeV.
- The triton binding energy \(E_t\) depends on \(V^s_{00}\) and \(V^t_{00}\) and is fitted simultaneously with two-body data without three-body forces.

## Context

This work extends the study of bound states embedded in the continuum (BSEC) previously investigated for both local and nonlocal interactions, and generalizes the concept to discrete spectrum states with negative energy. The authors connect their results to the Dubovoy-Flores theorem (Dubovoy and Flores, year not given) regarding variational calculations in the harmonic oscillator basis, and to the quark resonating group model of NN interaction (Author, year not given) where a similar cancellation of matrix elements occurs. The proposed separable NN potential enables simultaneous description of two-nucleon data and the trinucleon binding energy without three-body forces, offering an alternative to deep attractive potentials with forbidden states (Author, year not given).
