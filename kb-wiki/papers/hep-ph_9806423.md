# How Isospin Violation Mocks ``New'' Physics: $π^0-η,η'$ Mixing in $B\to ππ$ Decays

> An isospin analysis of $B\toππ$ decays yields $\sin 2α$, where $α$ is the usual CKM angle $α\equiv arg [-V_{td}V_{tb}^\ast /(V_{ud}V_{ub}^\ast)]$, without hadronic uncertainty if isospin is a perfect symmetry. Isospin, however, is broken not only by electroweak effects but also by the u and d quark mass difference. The latter generates $π^0-η, η'$ mixing and converts the isospin-perfect triangle relation between the $B\toππ$ amplitudes to a quadrilateral. The combined isospin-violating effects impact the extracted value of $\sin 2α$ in a significant manner, particularly if the latter is small.

## Key claim

Isospin-violating effects from π⁰-η,η' mixing, driven by the u and d quark mass difference, convert the isospin-perfect triangle relation between B→ππ amplitudes into a quadrilateral, and the resulting error in the extracted value of sin 2α can be 50% or more for small values of sin 2α, though it decreases to less than 10% if sin 2α is near unity.

## Method

The author computed B→ππ decay amplitudes using the ΔB=1 effective Hamiltonian from the operator product expansion in QCD at next-to-leading logarithmic order, employing the factorization approximation for hadronic matrix elements with Nc = 2, 3, ∞ and k²/m_b² = 0.3, 0.5. The π⁰-η,η' mixing was incorporated by expressing the physical π⁰ state in terms of SU(3)f perfect states with mixing parameters ε and ε' derived from a low-energy effective Lagrangian expanded to leading order in 1/Nc, momenta, and quark masses. The resulting quadrilateral amplitude relations were used to extract sin 2α from Im r_{π⁺π⁻} and Im r_{π⁰π⁰} and compare with input values.

## Key numbers

- ε = 1.4 × 10⁻² and ε' = 7.7 × 10⁻³ for π⁰-η,η' mixing parameters, computed using m_q(μ=2.5 GeV)
- Ratio of penguin to tree amplitudes in B⁻→π⁻π⁰: |P|/|T| ∼ (2.2-2.7)% |V_tb V*_td| / |V_ub V*_ud| for Nc=2,3 with k²/m_b²=0.3,0.5
- For electroweak penguins alone: |P|/|T| ∼ (1.4-1.5)% |V_tb V*_td| / |V_ub V*_ud|
- For input sin 2α = 0.0432 (case a), extracted (sin 2α)_GL from Im r_{π⁺π⁻}/Im r_{π⁰π⁰} ranged from -0.0926/0.107 (Nc=3) to 0.0451/0.394 (Nc=∞)
- For input sin 2α = 0.959 (case c), extracted (sin 2α)_GL ranged from 0.905/0.952 (Nc=3) to 0.917/0.915 (Nc=2,∞)
- η-η' mixing angle θ ≈ -22°

## Context

This work extends the isospin analysis method of Gronau and London (1990) for extracting sin 2α from B→ππ decays without hadronic uncertainty, by incorporating isospin violation from π⁰-η,η' mixing as formulated by Leutwyler (1996). Prior studies by Deshpande and He (1995), Gronau et al. (1995), and Fleischer (1996) had estimated electroweak penguin effects to be small, but this paper shows that π⁰-η,η' mixing introduces additional ΔI=1 amplitudes that break the triangle relations and can cause significant errors in sin 2α extraction, particularly for the small values favored by contemporary phenomenology. The results also demonstrate that the bounds on strong phases proposed by Grossman and Quinn (1998) are typically broken in the presence of isospin violation.
