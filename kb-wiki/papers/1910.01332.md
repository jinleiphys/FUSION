# Vorticity and Particle Polarization in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We review studies of vortical motion and the resulting global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons in heavy-ion collisions, in particular, within 3FD model. 3FD predictions for the global midrapidity polarization in the FAIR-NICA energy range are presented. The 3FD simulations indicate that energy dependence of the observed global polarization of hyperons in the midrapidity region is a consequence of the decrease of the vorticity in the central region with the collision energy rise because of pushing out the vorticity field into the fragmentation regions. At high collision energies this pushing-out results in a peculiar vortical structure consisting of two vortex rings: one ring in the target fragmentation region and another one in the projectile fragmentation region with matter rotation being opposite in these two rings.

# Vorticity and Particle Polarization in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the observed global midrapidity polarization of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons is a consequence of the decrease of thermal vorticity in the central region as collision energy rises, because the vorticity field is pushed out into the fragmentation regions. At high collision energies (above approximately 8 GeV), this pushing-out produces a peculiar vortical structure consisting of two vortex rings with opposite matter rotation in the target and projectile fragmentation regions.

## Method

The authors review studies of vortical motion and global hyperon polarization within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the collision as two counterstreaming baryon-rich fluids (projectile and target) plus a fireball fluid of newly produced particles, coupled by friction terms. The thermal vorticity is computed separately for the baryon-rich and fireball fluids, then combined as a proper-energy-density weighted average. The global midrapidity Λ polarization is estimated from the thermal vorticity component in the reaction plane, using an approximate decoupling of the vorticity averaging from the kinematic factor involving the Λ transverse mass.

## Key numbers

- Total angular momentum in Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 8 fm exceeds 10⁵ ħ at √s_NN > 25 GeV, with 25–30% deposited into the participant region.
- The global midrapidity Λ polarization is predicted to reach values of 5% at √s_NN = 3.8 GeV in the FAIR-NICA energy range.
- The estimate of global midrapidity polarization reproduces STAR data in the energy range √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV, with uncertainties of approximately 20%.
- The difference between Λ and anti-Λ midrapidity polarization is quantitatively estimated to not exceed 5% of the Λ polarization value.
- The counterstreaming of projectile and target fluids lasts from approximately 5 fm/c at √s_NN = 5 GeV to approximately 1 fm/c at 39 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior 3FD model studies of vorticity and polarization (Ivanov 2017, Ivanov 2018, Ivanov 2019) and compares with the thermodynamic approach of Becattini (2013, 2016) implemented in models such as UrQMD+vHLLE (Karpenko 2016), AMPT (Li 2017, Sun 2017), and PHSD (Kolomeitsev 2018). The work addresses the puzzle of why observed midrapidity polarization drops with increasing collision energy while the angular momentum in the participant region rises. The predictions for the FAIR-NICA energy range enable future experimental tests at lower collision energies, and the identified vortex-ring structure provides a target for experimental verification through polarization measurements in fragmentation regions.
