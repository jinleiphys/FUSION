# Production of $Ξ^-$-hypernuclei via the ($K^-,K^+$) reaction in a quark-meson coupling model

> We study the production of $Ξ^-$-hypernuclei, $^{12}_{Ξ^{-}}$Be and $^{28}_{Ξ^{-}}$Mg, via the ($K^-,K^+$) reaction within a covariant effective Lagrangian model, employing the bound $Ξ^-$ and proton spinors calculated by the latest quark-meson coupling model. The present treatment yields the $0^\circ$ differential cross sections for the formation of simple s-state $Ξ^-$ particle-hole states peak at a beam momentum around 1.0 GeV/c with a value in excess of 1 $μ$b.

## Key claim

The 0° differential cross sections for producing simple s-state Ξ⁻ particle-hole states in ¹²Ξ⁻Be and ²⁸Ξ⁻Mg via the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction peak at a beam momentum around 1.0 GeV/c with a value in excess of 1 μb, but this peak position disagrees with earlier work by Gal (1983) which found peaks near 1.8 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors calculated bound Ξ⁻ and proton spinors using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, then employed a covariant effective Lagrangian model with only s-channel production diagrams to compute differential cross sections for the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)¹²Ξ⁻Be and ²⁸Si(K⁻,K⁺)²⁸Ξ⁻Mg reactions. They used pure single-particle-single-hole (Ξ p⁻¹) wave functions for the nuclear structure and a plane wave approximation for the K⁻ and K⁺ mesons, with distortion effects partially accounted for by reduction factors of 2.8 for ¹²C and 5.0 for ²⁸Si taken from Ikeda (1994).

## Key numbers

- The QMC model predicts a Ξ⁻-1s₁/₂ state binding energy of 3.038 MeV for the ¹²Ξ⁻Be system.
- For ²⁸Ξ⁻Mg, the QMC model predicts three bound Ξ⁻ states: 1s₁/₂ (binding energy 8.982 MeV), 1p₃/₂ (4.079 MeV), and 1p₁/₂ (4.414 MeV).
- The thresholds for the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)¹²Ξ⁻Be and ²⁸Si(K⁻,K⁺)²⁸Ξ⁻Mg reactions are about 0.761 GeV/c and 0.750 GeV/c, respectively.
- The momentum transfers involved at 0° vary between 1.8 and 2.9 fm⁻¹.
- The cross sections peak at pK⁻ around 1.0 GeV/c, which is about 0.25-0.26 GeV/c above the corresponding production thresholds.
- The peak cross section values are in excess of 1 μb.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to the elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction by Shyam (2011) to the production of Ξ⁻-hypernuclei, using bound spinors from the QMC model developed by Guichon (1988, 2008) and Tsushima (1998). The results differ from earlier calculations by Gal (1983), which predicted cross section peaks near 1.8 GeV/c, and the authors suggest this discrepancy warrants re-examination of the beam momentum dependence. The study enables future experimental searches at JPARC, which will have energy resolution of a few MeV and large statistics, to compare with these predictions.
