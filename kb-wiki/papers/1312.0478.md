# No-Core Shell Model for Nuclear Systems with Strangeness

> We report on a novel ab initio approach for nuclear few- and many-body systems with strangeness. Recently, we developed a relevant no-core shell model technique which we successfully applied in first calculations of lightest $Λ$ hypernuclei. The use of a translationally invariant finite harmonic oscillator basis allows us to employ large model spaces, compared to traditional shell model calculations, and use realistic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon interactions (such as those derived from EFT). We discuss formal aspects of the methodology, show first demonstrative results for ${}_Λ^3$H, ${}_Λ^4$H and ${}^4_Λ$He, and give outlook.

# No-Core Shell Model for Nuclear Systems with Strangeness

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM) can be extended to incorporate strangeness degrees of freedom and applied to light hypernuclei, but the convergence with model space size is very slow for weakly bound systems like hypertriton, while it is much faster for more bound systems such as ${}^4_\Lambda$H and ${}^4_\Lambda$He. The approach uses bare chiral interactions without constructing any effective interaction tailored to a specific model space, making the results variational with respect to the model space truncation.

## Method

The authors extended the no-core shell model technique to include strangeness, diagonalizing the total Hamiltonian in a finite A-body harmonic oscillator basis truncated by a maximal HO excitation energy $N_{\rm max}\hbar\Omega$ relative to the unperturbed ground state. They used a relative Jacobi-coordinate HO basis that explicitly removes center-of-mass degrees of freedom and couples to total angular momentum and isospin, allowing larger model spaces than conventional Slater-determinant bases. Calculations employed the chiral N${}^3$LO nucleon-nucleon interaction and the chiral leading-order nucleon-hyperon interaction with cutoff $\Lambda=600$ MeV, treating all particles as active.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton ${}^3_\Lambda$H ground state energy: preliminary extrapolation to infinite model space gives $E = -2.31 \pm 0.01$ MeV, compared to the experimental value $E^{\rm (exp.)} = -2.35 \pm 0.05$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation energy in the $0^+$ state of ${}^4_\Lambda$H: experimental value $E_\Lambda^{\rm (exp.)} = 2.04 \pm 0.04$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation energy in the $1^+$ state of ${}^4_\Lambda$H: experimental value $E_\Lambda^{\rm (exp.)} = 1.00 \pm 0.06$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation energy in the $0^+$ state of ${}^4_\Lambda$He: experimental value $E_\Lambda^{\rm (exp.)} = 2.39 \pm 0.03$ MeV
- $\Lambda$ separation energy in the $1^+$ state of ${}^4_\Lambda$He: experimental value $E_\Lambda^{\rm (exp.)} = 1.24 \pm 0.05$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model technique of Navratil (2009) to hypernuclear systems, providing an ab initio alternative to the few existing exact calculations for 3- and 4-body hypernuclei by Nogga (2002), Filikhin (2002), and others. The approach enables future calculations for heavier s- and p-shell hypernuclei with $A \ge 4$, and the authors plan to extend the methodology to study other exotic systems such as $\bar{K}$- and $\eta$-nuclei, as well as to incorporate possible $NNY$ three-body forces.
