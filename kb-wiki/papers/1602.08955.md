# Dipole oscillation modes in light $α$-clustering nuclei

> The $α$ cluster states are discussed in a model frame of extended quantum molecular dynamics. Different alpha cluster structures are studied in details, such as $^8$Be two-$α$ cluster structure, $^{12}$C triangle structure, $^{12}$C chain structure, $^{16}$O chain structure, $^{16}$O kite structure, and $^{16}$O square structure. The properties studied, include as the width of wave packets for different $α$ clusters, momentum distribution, and the binding energy among $α$ clusters. It is also discussed how the $α$ cluster degree of freedom affects nuclear collective vibrations. The cluster configurations in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O are found to have corresponding characteristic spectra of giant dipole resonance (GDR), and the coherences of different $α$ clusters's dipole oscillation are described in details. The geometrical and dynamical symmetries of $α$-clustering configurations are responsible for the number and centroid energies of peaks of GDR spectra. Therefore, the GDR can be regarded as an effective probe to diagnose different $α$ cluster configurations in light nuclei.

# Dipole oscillation modes in light α-clustering nuclei

## Key claim

The giant dipole resonance (GDR) spectra of α-clustering light nuclei exhibit multifragmented peaks whose number and centroid energies are determined by the geometrical and dynamical symmetries of the α-cluster configurations; however, the authors qualify that the GDR peak positions shift by about 2 MeV depending on excitation energy due to nonlinearity of the system response, and a normalization procedure is introduced to account for this width.

## Method

The authors used an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model with dynamical Gaussian wave-packet widths for each nucleon, incorporating Skyrme, Coulomb, symmetry, and Pauli potentials, to simulate α-cluster configurations in ⁸Be, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O. GDR spectra were calculated via the linear response function method by applying a dipole boost to the system and Fourier-transforming the time evolution of the dipole moment, with a smoothing parameter of 2 MeV and an integration time of 600 fm/c. For coherence analysis of different α clusters, Coulomb excitation simulations were performed with cluster-nuclei as projectiles hitting a ⁴⁰Ca target at 100 MeV incident energy and 20 fm impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- ⁸Be ground state binding energy: 7.06 A MeV (experimental binding energy for ¹⁶O ground state is 7.98 A MeV for comparison)
- ¹²C triangle structure binding energy: 7.12 A MeV; chain structure binding energy: 7.17 A MeV
- ¹⁶O square structure binding energy: 7.29 A MeV; kite structure: 7.17 A MeV; chain structure: 7.21 A MeV
- ⁸Be GDR peaks at 21 MeV (long axis) and 31 MeV (short axis)
- ¹²C triangle structure GDR peaks at 21, 26.5, and 31 MeV
- ¹²C chain structure GDR peaks at 15, 23.5, and 31.5 MeV
- ¹⁶O chain structure short-axis coherent peak at 31 MeV; end α clusters show a frequency at 28.5 MeV
- Internal binding energy of α cluster in ⁸Be: 27.2 MeV (experimental free α particle binding energy: 28.3 MeV)
- Binding energy between two α clusters in ⁸Be: 1.02 MeV/α
- Wave-packet width for nucleons in cluster states: 1.9–2.1 fm; for non-cluster states: 4.2–4.3 fm (¹⁶O) and 3.5–3.6 fm (¹²C)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of α clustering in light nuclei using the EQMD model, building on the authors' prior publication (He 2014) on collective motion in α-clustering systems. The results support the tetrahedral 4-α configuration in ¹⁶O ground state, consistent with ab initio calculations by Epelbaum (2014) using nuclear chiral effective field theory and covariant density functional theory calculations by Liu (2012). For ¹²C, the authors infer a multi-configuration mixing of shell-model-like and α-cluster configurations, consistent with AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo (1998) and FMD calculations by Chernykh (2007). The work enables using GDR spectra as an experimental probe to distinguish different α-cluster configurations in light nuclei, particularly around the cluster decay threshold energies predicted in the Ikeda diagram (Ikeda 1968).
