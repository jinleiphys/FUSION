# Partial decay widths of baryons in the spin-momentum operator expansion method

> The covariant operator expansion method used by the Bonn-Gatchina group for the analysis of the meson photoproduction data is extended on the case of meson electro-production reactions. The angular dependence of the partial waves is deduced and the obtained amplitudes are compared with those used in other analyses of the electro-production reactions

## Key claim

The covariant operator expansion method previously used by the Bonn-Gatchina group for meson photoproduction analysis is extended to meson electro-production reactions, with the angular dependence of partial waves deduced and compared to other electro-production analyses, though the extension requires careful handling of gauge invariance and the additional longitudinal degree of freedom of the virtual photon.

## Method

The author constructs covariant vertex functions for baryon resonance decays into a nucleon and a virtual photon, using spin-momentum operators for orbital angular momentum up to L=3 and fermion propagators with projection operators. The formalism is developed separately for "+" states (J = L + 1/2) and "-" states (J = L - 1/2), with three independent vertices identified for each sector, and gauge-invariant combinations are derived. The resulting amplitudes are expressed in terms of electric (E), magnetic (M), and longitudinal (L) multipoles, and the angular dependence is given by Legendre polynomials and their derivatives, with explicit formulas for the CGLN (Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu) functions provided.

## Key numbers

- The orbital angular momentum operators for L ≤ 3 are explicitly given, with the L=2 operator containing a factor 3/2 and the L=3 operator containing a factor 5/2.
- The recurrence expression for the orbital momentum operators uses the factor (2L-1)/L^2.
- The fermion propagator includes the factor 1/(2√s) to remove divergency at large energies.
- For "+" states with n ≥ 1, three vertices are constructed: one with S=1/2 and L=n, one with S=3/2 and L=n+2, and one with S=3/2 and L=n.
- For "-" states, vertices are constructed with (S=1/2, L=n+1), (S=3/2, L=n+1), and (S=3/2, L=n-1).
- The coefficient α^(n) is defined as the product from j=1 to n of (2j-1)/j, with α^(0)=1.
- For the "+" sector spin-1/2 amplitude, the multipoles satisfy E_n^(1+) = M_n^(1+) = L_n^(1+) = λ_n/(n+1), where λ_n = α^(n)/(2n+1) (|k||q|)^n χ_i χ_f.
- For the "+" sector spin-3/2 amplitude (third vertex), E_n^(3+) = -n M_n^(3+) = λ_n/(n+1).
- For the "-" sector spin-1/2 amplitude, -M_(n+1)^(1-) = E_(n+1)^(1-) = L_(n+1)^(1-) = ζ_(n+1)/(n+1), where ζ_(n+1) = α^(n+1)/(n+1) (|k||q|)^(n+1) χ_i χ_f.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn-Gatchina covariant formalism (Anisovich, 2007) from meson photoproduction to electro-production, enabling the study of resonance production couplings as a function of virtual photon mass, which provides information about resonance size and internal structure. The formalism is compared with the covariant approach of Krivoruchenko (2001), and explicit relations between the two sets of amplitudes are derived, showing how the gauge-invariant vertices in the present work connect to the three independent amplitudes defined in that earlier work. This enables combined analyses of photoproduction and electro-production data from collaborations such as CB-ELSA, CLAS, MAMI, and HADES.
