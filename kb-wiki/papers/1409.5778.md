# Experimental results and phenomenology of quarkonium production in relativistic nuclear collisions

> An overview of recent measurements of quarkonium production in nucleus-nucleus collisions and their understanding in theoretical models is given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed increase in J/ψ nuclear modification factor (R_AA) from RHIC to LHC energies, contrary to expectations from Debye screening alone, demonstrates that novel production mechanisms beyond screening, namely statistical hadronization or kinetic (re)combination, are at work, though the data cannot yet definitively distinguish between these two competing models.

## Method

The author reviews experimental measurements of charmonium (J/ψ, ψ(2S)) and bottomonium (Υ(1S), Υ(2S)) production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC (Au–Au, Cu–Cu, Cu–Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV) and the LHC (Pb–Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV), comparing the nuclear modification factor R_AA, transverse momentum dependence, elliptic flow v_2, and centrality dependence against predictions from the statistical hadronization model and transport (kinetic recombination) models. The analysis also incorporates data from p–Pb and d–Au collisions to assess cold nuclear matter effects.

## Key numbers

- In central Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, transport models indicate about 60–80% of the J/ψ yield originates from (re)combination of c and c̅ quarks, while at RHIC (Au–Au, √s_NN = 200 GeV) that fraction is about 30–50%.
- The input c̅c production cross section in statistical and transport models differs at the LHC by almost a factor of 2.
- The statistical hadronization model prediction for the Υ(2S)/Υ(1S) yield ratio in central Pb–Pb collisions is compatible with data at a temperature of T = 159 MeV.
- J/ψ R_AA values at the LHC are significantly larger than at RHIC when plotted against charged-particle pseudorapidity density dN_ch/dη, contrary to the Debye screening expectation.
- Prompt J/ψ mesons at the LHC exhibit elliptic flow for p_T as large as 10 GeV/c.

## Context

This review extends the earlier summary of charmonium data at SPS and RHIC by Kluberg (2009) and the statistical hadronization model framework of Braun-Munzinger (2000) and Andronic (2007, 2011). The results contradict the simple sequential suppression picture of Karsch (1990, 2005) and Digal (2001) by showing that (re)generation dominates at LHC energies. The data enable future discrimination between the statistical hadronization model and kinetic transport models (TAMU group of Zhao and Rapp; Tsinghua group of Zhou and Zhuang), and open the possibility of using bottomonium to further constrain the QCD phase boundary temperature.
