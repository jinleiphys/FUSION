# Coupled-Channels Approach for Dissipative Quantum Dynamics in Near-Barrier Collisions

> A novel quantum dynamical model based on the dissipative quantum dynamics of open quantum systems is presented. It allows the treatment of both deep-inelastic processes and quantum tunneling (fusion) within a fully quantum mechanical coupled-channels approach. Model calculations show the transition from pure state (coherent) to mixed state (decoherent and dissipative) dynamics during a near-barrier nuclear collision. Energy dissipation, due to irreversible decay of giant-dipole excitations of the interacting nuclei, results in hindrance of quantum tunneling.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully quantum mechanical coupled-channels density matrix approach that can treat both deep-inelastic processes and quantum tunneling (fusion) simultaneously, showing that energy dissipation from irreversible decay of giant-dipole excitations results in hindrance of quantum tunneling at near-barrier energies.

## Method
The authors formulate a time-dependent coupled-channels problem using Lindblad's equation for a reduced density matrix, describing the dynamical evolution of the relative motion of nuclei plus selected intrinsic collective excitations that irreversibly interact with two model environments. The first environment represents capture inside the fusion barrier (compound nucleus formation), while the second represents irreversible decay out of intrinsic excitations, specifically the giant dipole resonance (GDR). Test calculations are performed for a head-on collision of 16O + 144Sm at energies below the nominal Coulomb barrier, using a Gaussian wave-packet for the relative motion and numerically propagating the density matrix with a time step of 10^-22 s over a radial grid of 0-250 fm with 512 points.

## Key numbers
- Nominal Coulomb barrier for 16O + 144Sm: V_B = 61.1 MeV
- GDR excitation energy: E_{1^-} = 15 MeV
- GDR spreading width: 6 MeV
- Deformation parameter for GDR coupling: β_1 = 0.2
- Woods-Saxon nuclear potential parameters: V_0 = -105.1 MeV, r_0 = 1.1 fm, a_0 = 0.75 fm
- Absorption rate inside pocket: depth 10 MeV, diffuseness 0.1 fm, located at pocket radius ≈ 7 fm
- Initial wave-packet width: σ_0 = 20 fm, initially centered at r = 150 fm
- FWHM energy spread of wave-packet: ∼ 3%
- Time step for propagation: Δt = 10^-22 s, with typically 700 time steps for full collision duration
- For E_0 = 45 MeV with only state |X⟩: purity Tr[ρ̂²] = 1.0000, energy loss ΔE = 0.0004 MeV
- For E_0 = 45 MeV with both states |X⟩ and |Y⟩: purity Tr[ρ̂²] = 0.9196, energy loss ΔE = 1.8718 MeV
- For E_0 = 60 MeV with only state |X⟩: purity Tr[ρ̂²] = 0.6067, energy loss ΔE = 14.862 MeV
- For E_0 = 60 MeV with both states |X⟩ and |Y⟩: purity Tr[ρ̂²] = 0.5127, energy loss ΔE = 18.908 MeV

## Context
This work extends stationary state coupled-channels approaches (Thompson, Hagino) that have been successful for many collision observables but cannot simultaneously describe elastic scattering and fusion measurements (Newton, Angeli) or below-barrier quantum tunneling and above-barrier fusion yields (Nanda2). The approach also addresses limitations of classical treatments of relative motion in direct damped collisions (Schroeder). The model enables future investigation of the sub-barrier fusion hindrance phenomenon (Jiang) and can be extended to include multi-nucleon or cluster transfer reactions (Rehm) to confront experimental measurements.
