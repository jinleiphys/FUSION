# Thermalization of overpopulated systems in the 2PI formalism

> Based on the two-particle irreducible (2PI) formalism to next-to-leading order in the $1/N$ expansion, we study the thermalization of overpopulated systems in scalar $O(N)$ theories with moderate coupling. We focus in particular on the growth of soft modes, and examine whether this can lead to the formation of a transient Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) when the initial occupancy is high enough. For the value of the coupling constant used in our simulations, we find that while the system rapidly approaches the condensation threshold, the formation of a BEC is eventually hindered by particle number changing processes.

## Key claim

Using the 2PI formalism at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion with moderate coupling (λ=10), the authors find that while overpopulated systems in scalar O(N) theory rapidly approach the condensation threshold, the formation of a transient Bose-Einstein condensate is eventually hindered by particle-number-changing inelastic processes, contrary to what would be expected from number-conserving kinetic theory.

## Method

The authors solved the nonequilibrium evolution equations for the statistical and spectral functions in the 2PI formalism at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion for scalar O(N) theory with N=4 and coupling λ=10. They initialized the system with a step-function momentum distribution f_in(p)=A θ(Q-p) for three values of A (0.1, 1, and 5) and evolved the equations on a momentum-space lattice with memory integrals truncated at t_c=50/Q, tracking the quasiparticle distribution, effective chemical potential, and particle number over time.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant λ=10 and N=4 used throughout all simulations
- For A=0.1 (underpopulated case): particle number changed by less than 2% during the simulation; zero-mode occupancy grew monotonically without reaching equilibrium
- For A=1 (overpopulated case): zero-mode occupancy rose sharply, peaked, then decreased; system lost about 20% of particles by end of simulation; effective chemical potential μ_soft approached within less than 2% of the quasiparticle mass m_qp at closest approach
- For A=5 (strongly overpopulated case): total particle number dropped by about 40% by end of simulation; zero-mode occupancy peaked earlier than in A=1 case
- Critical initial occupancy estimate: A_c ≈ 0.15 for massless noninteracting theory
- Memory integral cutoff: t_c = 50/Q (results unchanged with t_c=25/Q)
- Lattice spacing: a_p=0.2 m; time step: a_t=0.01/m
- Momentum cutoff: Λ=2Q=10 m (50 grid points) for A=0.1 and A=1; Λ=7.5Q=15 m (75 grid points) for A=5
- Total energy conserved to better than 1% accuracy

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BEC formation in overpopulated systems that used kinetic theory with only elastic 2→2 scattering (Semikoz 1994, Blaizot 2013, Epelbaum 2014, Meistrenko 2015) or classical statistical field theory (Berges 2012). It contrasts with the kinetic theory analysis of Blaizot (2011) and Kurkela (2011) which suggested that inelastic processes allow only transient BEC formation, and with Blaizot (2016) who found complete hindrance of BEC from 2→3 processes in QCD. The 2PI approach used here goes beyond the Boltzmann equation's regime of validity (f(p) << 1/λ) and allows exploration of the deep infrared where f(p) >> 1/λ, while also naturally including inelastic processes. The results enable comparison with the recent large-volume 2PI simulation of Berges (2016) which focused on non-thermal fixed points at small coupling, whereas this work examines moderate coupling where thermalization occurs in finite computer time.
