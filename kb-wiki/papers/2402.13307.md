# Renormalization of beta decay at three loops and beyond

> The anomalous dimension for heavy-heavy-light effective theory operators describing nuclear beta decay is computed through three-loop order in the static limit. The result at order $Z^2α^3$ corrects a previous result in the literature. An all-orders symmetry is shown to relate the anomalous dimensions at leading and subleading powers of $Z$ at a given order of $α$. The first unknown coefficient for the anomalous dimension now appears at $O(Z^2α^4)$.

## Key claim
The anomalous dimension for heavy-heavy-light effective theory operators describing nuclear beta decay is computed through three-loop order in the static limit, correcting a previous result in the literature at order \(Z^2\alpha^3\); an all-orders symmetry relating leading and subleading powers of \(Z\) at a given order of \(\alpha\) is shown, so that the first unknown coefficient now appears at \(O(Z^2\alpha^4)\).

## Method
The authors compute the amputated amplitude for the process \([Z+1] \to [Z] e^+\) in QED with one massless electron, using heavy-particle effective theory Feynman rules in the static limit (neglecting nuclear recoil). They evaluate one-, two-, and three-loop diagrams in arbitrary covariant gauge with dimensional regularization and a photon mass \(\lambda\) to regulate infrared divergences, then extract the operator renormalization constant \(Z_{\cal O}\) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme and the anomalous dimension \(\gamma_{\cal O}\) from the coefficient of the \(1/\epsilon\) pole.

## Key numbers
- The one-loop anomalous dimension coefficient at order \(Z^0\alpha\) is \(\gamma_0^{(1)} = \xi\) (gauge parameter \(\xi\)).
- The two-loop anomalous dimension coefficient at order \(Z^2\alpha^2\) is \(A_{2,-1} = 2\pi^2\).
- The two-loop anomalous dimension coefficient at order \(Z^1\alpha^2\) is \(B_{2,-1} = 2\pi^2\).
- The three-loop result at order \(Z^2\alpha^3\) corrects a previous result in the literature; the first unknown coefficient now appears at \(O(Z^2\alpha^4)\).

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory framework for nuclear beta decay developed by Hill (2023) and others, computing the anomalous dimension to higher loop order than previously available. The all-orders symmetry relating different powers of \(Z\) at the same order in \(\alpha\) is a new result that reduces the number of undetermined coefficients; for example, at three loops the symmetry forces \(\gamma_2^{(0)} = 0\) and \(\gamma_2^{(2)} = \gamma_1^{(1)}\), leaving only one unknown coefficient. The corrected three-loop result enables more precise resummation of logarithmic QED radiative corrections in extractions of \(|V_{ud}|\) from super-allowed beta decays and in searches for Fierz interference terms.
