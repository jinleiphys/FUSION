# Nucleon-to-Delta transition form factors in chiral effective field theory using the complex-mass scheme

> We calculate the form factors of the electromagnetic nucleon-to-$Δ$-resonance transition to third chiral order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory. For the purpose of generating a systematic power counting, the complex-mass scheme is applied in combination with the small-scale expansion. We fit the results to available empirical data.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the electromagnetic nucleon-to-Delta transition form factors to third chiral order in manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory, but the results only poorly describe experimental data unless a semi-phenomenological contribution from the rho meson is included at tree level. The complex-mass scheme is applied to treat the Delta(1232) as an unstable particle with a complex pole mass, enabling a systematic power counting.

## Method
The calculation uses manifestly Lorentz-invariant chiral effective field theory combined with the complex-mass scheme and small-scale expansion to generate a systematic power counting. The effective Lagrangian includes pion, pion-nucleon, pion-Delta, and pi-N-Delta terms up to third chiral order, with tree-level diagrams at orders two and three and one-loop diagrams at order three. The free parameters from tree-level diagrams are fitted to experimental data for the magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, and Coulomb quadrupole form factors at the Breit-Wigner position for Q^2 up to 0.3 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- The Delta(1232) pole position used is z_Delta = (1210 - i 50) MeV, with nucleon mass m_N = 938 MeV.
- The leading-order prediction for the pole ratio R_EM^(pole (2))(0) = (-5.98 + i 0.90)% using z_Delta = (1210 - i 50) MeV and m_N = 938 MeV.
- The coupling constants from the best fit including the rho meson (Fit III) are: C_1^gamma = (-1.69 +/- 0.20) GeV^{-1}, C_2^gamma = (2.91 +/- 0.21) GeV^{-2}, C_1^rho = (-21.3 +/- 1.6) GeV^{-1}, C_2^rho = (10.6 +/- 1.7) GeV^{-2}.
- The combination tilde{C}_1 = C_1^gamma - C_1^rho/g = (1.91 +/- 0.34) GeV^{-1} and tilde{C}_2 = (1.12 +/- 0.36) GeV^{-2}, with g = 5.91 from the Kawarabayashi-Suzuki-Riazuddin-Fayyazuddin relation.
- The pseudo-threshold for the Siegert limit is Q^2_pt = -(m_Delta - m_N)^2 = -0.087 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends previous chiral effective field theory calculations of the gamma N to Delta transition by treating the Delta(1232) as an unstable particle using the complex-mass scheme, which was originally developed for W, Z, and Higgs bosons and later applied to strong-interaction effective field theories by Djukanovic (2009), Bauer (2012), and others. The results enable extraction of complex transition form factors at the Delta pole position, as recently determined from partial wave analyses by Tiator (2016). The calculation improves upon earlier ChEFT approaches by providing a consistent power counting for the unstable resonance and demonstrates the necessity of including rho-meson contributions to describe data up to Q^2 = 0.6 GeV^2.
