# Competition of isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pairing in nuclei

> We use shell model techniques in the complete pf shell to study pair correlations in nuclei. Particular attention is paid to the competition of isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pairing modes which is investigated in the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 46V and in the chain of even Fe-isotopes. We confirm the dominance of isovector pairing in the ground states. An inspection of the level density and pair correlation strength in 46V, however, shows the increasing relative importance of isoscalar correlations with increasing excitation energy. In the Fe-isotopes we find the expected strong dependence of the isovector pairing strength on the neutron excess, while the dominant J=1 isoscalar pair correlations scale much more gently with neutron number. We demonstrate that the isoscalar pair correlations depend strongly on the spin-orbit splitting.

## Key claim

Using shell-model calculations in the complete pf shell, the authors confirm that isovector pairing dominates the ground states of odd-odd N=Z nuclei like 46V, but show that isoscalar pair correlations become increasingly important with excitation energy and can exceed the total isovector pairing strength above about 15 MeV. The isoscalar pairing is strongly suppressed by the spin-orbit splitting; if the spin-orbit force were removed, isoscalar correlations would prevail even in the ground state.

## Method

The authors performed full pf-shell diagonalization with the realistic KB3 interaction for 46V, computing spectra, level densities, and pair correlation strengths state-by-state. For the Fe isotopes 50-58Fe, they used the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach with the same KB3 interaction, employing the g-extrapolation procedure to circumvent the sign problem. They defined pair correlation strength operators in LS-coupling for L=0 pairs and calculated expectation values as functions of excitation energy (diagonalization) or temperature (SMMC).

## Key numbers

* In 46V, the calculated T=0 spectrum lies about 400 keV too low in excitation energy compared to experiment.
* The specific heat of 46V shows a peak at T=0.2 MeV, signaling the transition from the T=1 ground state to low-lying T=0 levels.
* The 46V level density is described by a backshifted Fermi gas with parameters Delta = -2.0 MeV and a = 3.5 MeV^-1 for excitation energies above 2.5 MeV.
* In the lowest T=0 state of 46V with the realistic KB3 interaction, the isovector pairing Hamiltonian expectation value is 2.18 MeV and the isoscalar is 1.41 MeV; removing the monopole terms inverts these to 2.09 MeV (isovector) and 4.62 MeV (isoscalar).
* The isovector pairing coupling constant is G_p01 = 0.37 MeV and the isoscalar pairing coupling constant is G_p10 = 0.57 MeV.
* Reducing the spin-orbit splitting by a factor of 2 in 50Fe and 56Fe increases the f7/2-f5/2 isoscalar correlations by about a factor of 2.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of proton-neutron pairing by Engel, Langanke, and Vogel (1996) and Langanke et al. (1997), confirming the rapid decrease of isovector pn pairing with neutron excess predicted in those works. It also relates to the Wigner energy studies by Satula et al. (1998) and the explanation of ground-state isospin changes in odd-odd N=Z nuclei by Vogel (1998). The results enable future investigations of pairing phase transitions in rotating nuclei, as observed experimentally in 74Rb by Rudolph et al. (1996), and provide a benchmark for testing beyond-mean-field theories of nuclear pairing.
