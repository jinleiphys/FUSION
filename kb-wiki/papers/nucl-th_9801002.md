# Instanton tunneling and multiphonon giant resonances

## Key claim

The sine-Gordon model, with its instanton tunneling solutions, can describe the centroid energies and widths of multiphonon giant resonances in heavy nuclei, provided the ratio of successive widths is taken as unity and the ratio of successive energies follows the harmonic relation \(E_n/E_{n-1} = n/(n-1)\).

## Method

The authors map the multiphonon giant resonance system onto the sine-Gordon model, where the potential \(V(q) = (a/b)(1 - \cos bq)\) depends on a generalized coordinate \(q\) and imaginary time \(\tau\). They compute the transition amplitude between adjacent multiphonon states \(|q_{n-1}\rangle\) and \(|q_n\rangle\) using a Feynman path integral evaluated in the stationary-phase approximation, which yields a classical action \(S[q_{cl}] = 8\omega/b^2\) and a resonance width \(\Gamma_n = (\omega/\pi) \exp(-16\omega/b^2)\). The model parameters \(\omega = \sqrt{ab}\) (phonon frequency) and \(\lambda = ab^3\) (anharmonicity constant) are extracted from the Taylor expansion of the potential.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{208}\)Pb: ratio of centroid energies \(E_2/E_1 = 1.93 \pm 0.07\); ratio of widths \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 = 1.4 \pm 0.4\).
- For \(^{136}\)Xe: ratio of centroid energies \(E_2/E_1 = 1.86 \pm 0.05\); ratio of widths \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 = 1.3 \pm 0.4\).
- The model predicts \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_{n-1} = 1\) and \(E_n/E_{n-1} = n/(n-1)\), which the authors state agree with the experimental data within errors.

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of multiphonon giant resonances in \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{136}\)Xe reported by the GSI/SIS Darmstadt collaboration (references 1 and 2 in the paper). The sine-Gordon model provides a theoretical framework for describing the tunneling between adjacent multiphonon states, complementing earlier anharmonic oscillator treatments. The approach enables future calculations of higher-order multiphonon excitations and their decay widths using instanton methods.
