# Search for three alpha states around an $^{16}$O core in $^{28}$Si

> We investigate the existence of weakly coupled gas-like states comprised of three $α$ particles around an $^{16}$O core in $^{28}$Si. We calculate the excited states in $^{28}$Si using the multi-configuration mixing method based on the $^{16}$O + 3$α$ cluster model. We also include the $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C and $^{24}$Mg + $α$ basis wave functions prepared by the generator coordinate method. To identify the gas-like states, we calculate the isoscalar monopole transition strengths and the overlap of the obtained states with the geometrical cluster wave function and the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function. The results show that the obtained fourth and twelfth states significantly overlap with the THSR wave function. These two states clearly coexist with the $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C cluster states, emerging at similar energies. The calculated isoscalar monopole strengths between those two states are significantly large, indicating that the states are members of the excitation mode. Furthermore, the calculated root-mean-squared (RMS) radii for these states also suggest that a layer of gas-like three $α$ particles could exist around the surface of the $^{16}$O core, which can be described as a "two-dimensional gas" in the intermediate state before the Hoyle-like three $α$ states emerge.

## Key claim

The fourth and twelfth excited 0+ states in 28Si significantly overlap with the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke (THSR) wave function, suggesting they are members of a new excitation mode featuring a "two-dimensional gas" of three alpha particles around a 16O core, which serves as an intermediate state before a fully three-dimensional Hoyle-like gas state emerges.

## Method

The authors used a multi-configuration mixing (MCM) method based on the 16O + 3α cluster model, superposing 1000 randomly generated Slater determinants for the 16O + 3α configuration. They additionally included 16O + 12C and 24Mg + α basis wave functions prepared by the generator coordinate method (GCM), totaling 1120 basis wave functions, and performed parity and angular-momentum projections to the 0+ state. To identify gas-like states, they calculated overlaps with the THSR wave function, isoscalar monopole transition strengths, and root-mean-squared (RMS) radii.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy is calculated at -24.64 MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value of -24.03 MeV.
- The fourth state overlaps with the THSR wave function by 16.7% (σ=3 fm), 9.8% (σ=4 fm), and 3.3% (σ=5 fm); the twelfth state overlaps by 20.0% (σ=3 fm), 13.7% (σ=4 fm), and 4.9% (σ=5 fm).
- The isoscalar monopole transition strength from the fourth to the twelfth state is B(IS0)/B0(IS0) = 3.20, and from the first to the fourth state is B(IS0)/B0(IS0) = 2.21.
- The RMS radii for the first, fourth, and twelfth states are 2.80 fm, 2.92 fm, and 3.02 fm, respectively.
- The RMS radii of the three α particles (core subtracted) for the first, fourth, and twelfth states are 3.45 fm, 3.67 fm, and 3.86 fm, respectively.
- The ground state overlaps maximally with the 16O + 12C⊥ configuration at 78.5% with a=2.0 fm and d(16O-12C)=3.0 fm.
- The sixth state has the strongest transition strength from the ground state at B(IS0)/B0(IS0)=3.67.

## Context

This work extends the search for gas-like alpha cluster states, previously established for the Hoyle state in 12C by Tohsaki et al. (2001) and for four-alpha states in 16O by Funaki et al. (2003, 2006), to the heavier system 28Si with a 16O core. It builds on theoretical investigations of gas-like two-alpha states around 16O in 24Mg by Yamada et al. (2008), Ichikawa et al. (2011), and others, as well as experimental studies by Kawabata et al. (2007) and Wakasa et al. (2007). The results enable future experimental searches for these predicted 2D gas-like states in 28Si via isoscalar monopole transitions and suggest the need for advanced theoretical methods such as complex scaling to identify the fully developed 3D gas-like states at higher excitation energies.
