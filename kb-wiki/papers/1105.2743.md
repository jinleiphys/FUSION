# System-size scan of dihadron azimuthal correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> System-size dependence of dihadron azimuthal correlations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision is simulated by a multi-phase transport model. The structure of correlation functions and yields of associated particles show clear participant path-length dependences in collision systems with a partonic phase. The splitting parameter and root-mean-square width of away-side correlation functions increase with collision system size from $^{14}$N+$^{14}$N to $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions. The double-peak structure of away-side correlation functions can only be formed in sufficient "large" collision systems under partonic phase. The contrast between the results with partonic phase and with hadron gas could suggest some hints to study onset of deconfinment.

## Key claim

The double-peak structure of away-side dihadron azimuthal correlation functions can only be formed in sufficiently "large" collision systems that undergo a partonic phase, with an onset occurring near the mass range of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV; the authors hedge that these observations do not assume any specific dynamical mechanism for the double-peak formation, and that the AMPT model includes collective Mach-like effects as well as eccentricity fluctuations and triangular flow components.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with string melting (Melt AMPT version, which includes a partonic phase) and the default AMPT version (pure hadron gas) to simulate dihadron azimuthal correlations for seven collision systems from ¹⁴N+¹⁴N to ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at √sNN = 200 GeV in the most central collisions (0-10%). They analyzed the correlation functions in the kinetic windows 1 < pTassoc < 3 GeV/c, 2.5 < pTtrig < 6 GeV/c, and |η| < 1, subtracting the mixed-event background using the Zero Yield At Minimum (ZYAM) assumption.

## Key numbers

- Participant path-length ν ranges from 1.89 (¹⁴N+¹⁴N) to 5.33 (¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) for central collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- Average parton collision number ncolparton increases from 1.31 (¹⁴N+¹⁴N) to 7.24 (¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) in the Melt AMPT version.
- The onset of the double-peak structure in the away-side correlation function occurs near ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions, corresponding to ν = 2.76 and ncolparton = 2.79.
- The parton interaction cross section used in the AMPT model is 10 mb, which reproduces elliptic flow and dihadron azimuthal correlations.
- The emission angle relative to jet in the shock wave model is about 1.23 rad for QGP, 1.11 rad for hadronic gas, and zero for mixed phase (cited from prior work Casalderrey).

## Context

This work extends the system-size scan approach proposed by the NA61 and NA49-future collaborations to study the QCD phase diagram, and builds on prior AMPT simulations of dihadron correlations by Ma et al. (2010) and the path-length studies by Jia et al. and Ma et al. The results show that the double-peak structure and jet modification are sensitive to the effective degrees of freedom of the dense medium, providing hints for the onset of deconfinement from a system-size perspective; the authors note that the AMPT model includes collective Mach-like effects as well as eccentricity fluctuations and triangular flow components discussed by Alver and collaborators (2010) and Ma et al. (2010).
