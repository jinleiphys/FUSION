# Predicting parton energy loss in small collision systems

> Medium induced parton energy loss is not conclusively established neither in very peripheral heavy-ion collisions nor in proton-ion collisions. However, the standard interpretation of azimuthal momentum anisotropies in theses systems implies some partonic rescattering. The upcoming light-ion runs at the LHC provide a unique opportunity to search for parton energy loss in different systems of similar size. Here, we make predictions for the expected parton energy loss signal in the charged hadron spectra in a system size scan at LHC. We test a large set of model assumptions against the transverse momentum and centrality dependence of the charged hadron nuclear modification factor in lead-lead and xenon-xenon collisions at the LHC. We then attempt to make a model agnostic prediction for the charged hadron nuclear modification factor in oxygen-oxygen collisions.

## Key claim
Medium induced parton energy loss is not conclusively established in very peripheral heavy-ion collisions or proton-ion collisions, but the standard interpretation of azimuthal momentum anisotropies in these systems implies some partonic rescattering. The authors test a large set of model assumptions against transverse momentum and centrality dependence of the charged hadron nuclear modification factor in lead-lead and xenon-xenon collisions, then attempt a model agnostic prediction for oxygen-oxygen collisions.

## Method
The authors use a reformulation of the BDMPS-Z formalism due to Arnold (2008) to compute the probability distribution of parton energy loss (quenching weight) from medium-induced gluon emission rates. They embed hard partons on eikonal trajectories through a background temperature profile obtained from a one-parameter conformal kinetic theory solution that interpolates between free-streaming and perfect fluidity, with the quenching parameter proportional to T^3. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed by averaging over parton production vertices and directions, then combining nPDF effects from EPPS16 with quark and gluon quenching factors weighted by their fragmentation contributions.

## Key numbers
- The temperature at the origin in 0-10% PbPb collisions at time tau_ref = 0.6 fm/c is set to T(tau_ref, 0) = 485 MeV
- The freeze-out temperature is T_F = 175 MeV and the initial time for medium interactions is tau_0 = 0.05 fm/c
- The crossover frequency omega_kink between LPM and opacity regimes is approximately omega_kink^PbPb = 6.6 GeV, omega_kink^OO = 1.0 GeV, and omega_kink^pPb = 0.7 GeV
- The hadron spectrum is dominated by quark fragmentation at p_T > 70 GeV
- The spectral index n is greater than 5 in the kinetic regime 20 GeV < p_T < 1000 GeV
- The reduced exponent for fragmented hadrons is typically <z n> = 3

## Context
This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) and the reformulation by Arnold (2008) to predict parton energy loss in small collision systems like oxygen-oxygen collisions, motivated by the LHC discovery of strong collectivity in small systems (Abelev 2014, Khachatryan 2015). It builds on prior model comparisons to quenched hadron spectra (Chien 2015, Andres 2016, Noronha-Hostler 2016) and responds to the community-wide study by Citron (2018) that called for modeling of light-ion collisions. The companion paper by Huss (2020) addresses whether a high accuracy baseline can detect the predicted small nuclear modifications in oxygen-oxygen collisions.
