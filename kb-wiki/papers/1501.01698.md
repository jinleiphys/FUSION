# Constraining the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy from an X-ray bursting neutron star

> Neutrons stars lighter than the Sun are basically composed of nuclear matter of density up to around twice normal nuclear density. In our recent analyses, we showed that possible simultaneous observations of masses and radii of such neutron stars could constrain $η\equiv(K_0L^2)^{1/3}$, a combination of the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter $K_0$ and the density derivative of the nuclear symmetry energy $L$ that characterizes the theoretical mass-radius relation. In this paper, we focus on the mass-radius constraint of the X-ray burster 4U 1724-307 given by Suleimanov et al. (2011). We therefrom obtain the constraint that $η$ should be larger than around 130 MeV, which in turn leads to $L$ larger than around 110, 98, 89, and 78 MeV for $K_0=180$, 230, 280, and 360 MeV. Such a constraint on $L$ is more or less consistent with that obtained from the frequencies of quasi-periodic oscillations in giant flares observed in soft-gamma repeaters.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the combination of nuclear matter parameters \(\eta \equiv (K_0 L^2)^{1/3}\) to be larger than about 130 MeV by comparing theoretical mass-radius relations for low-mass neutron stars (central density \(\rho_c \le 2\rho_0\)) with the observational mass-radius constraint for the X-ray burster 4U 1724-307 from Suleimanov et al. (2011); this result is conditional on the assumption that the star has a canonical mass of order \(1.4 M_\odot\) and that the equation of state is universal.

## Method

The authors used 247 phenomenological equations of state (the OI-EOSs) based on a simplified Thomas-Fermi method that reproduces masses and charge radii of stable nuclei, covering a range of the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter \(K_0\) (180–360 MeV) and the density derivative of the symmetry energy \(L\) (0–180 MeV). They constructed stellar models for low-mass neutron stars with central density up to \(2\rho_0\) and compared the resulting mass-radius curves to the 90% confidence-level allowed region for 4U 1724-307 derived by Suleimanov et al. (2011) using three different atmosphere models (pure hydrogen, pure helium, and solar H/He with sub-solar metal abundance).

## Key numbers

- \(\eta \equiv (K_0 L^2)^{1/3}\) is constrained to be larger than around 130 MeV from the mass-radius constraint of 4U 1724-307.
- For \(K_0 = 180\) MeV, this implies \(L \gtrsim 110\) MeV; for \(K_0 = 230\) MeV, \(L \gtrsim 98\) MeV; for \(K_0 = 280\) MeV, \(L \gtrsim 89\) MeV; for \(K_0 = 360\) MeV, \(L \gtrsim 78\) MeV.
- The 247 OI-EOSs cover \(K_0\) from 180 to 360 MeV in steps of 10 MeV (19 values) and \(y\) from \(-200\) to \(-1800\) MeV fm\(^3\) (13 values), producing \(L\) in the range \(0 < L < 180\) MeV.
- A fit to the OI-EOSs gives \(\eta = 6.59 (L / 1\ \mathrm{MeV})^{2/3}\) MeV, corresponding to \(K_0 = 286.8\) MeV.
- The constraint from the neutron star radius lower limit for 4U 1608-52 (Poutanen, 2014) gives \(\eta \gtrsim 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding (Sotani et al., 2014) that the mass-radius relation of low-mass neutron stars is characterized by the single parameter \(\eta\). The resulting lower bound on \(L\) is consistent with the constraint from quasi-periodic oscillations in giant flares of soft-gamma repeaters (Sotani et al., 2013b), which gave \(101.1 \le L \le 131.0\) MeV (if all observed frequencies below 100 Hz are crustal torsional oscillations) or \(58.0 \le L \le 85.3\) MeV (if the second lowest frequency in SGR 1806-20 has a different origin). The result enables future missions such as NICER and LOFT to further constrain \(\eta\) via simultaneous mass and radius measurements of low-mass neutron stars.
