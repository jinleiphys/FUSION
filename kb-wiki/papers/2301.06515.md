# Gravitational Waves from a Core g-Mode in Supernovae as Probes of the High-Density Equation of State

> Using relativistic supernova simulations of massive progenitor stars with a quark-hadron equation of state (EoS) and a purely hadronic EoS, we identify a distinctive feature in the gravitational-wave signal that originates from a buoyancy-driven mode (g-mode) below the proto-neutron star convection zone. The mode frequency lies in the range $200\lesssim f\lesssim 800\,\text{Hz}$ and decreases with time. As the mode lives in the core of the proto-neutron star, its frequency and power are highly sensitive to the EoS, in particular the sound speed around twice saturation density.

## Key claim
The paper identifies a buoyancy-driven core g-mode (the \({}^2\!g_1\) mode) in the gravitational-wave signal from core-collapse supernovae that is highly sensitive to the equation of state (EoS) at densities around twice nuclear saturation density, but the authors explicitly note that quark formation is not directly responsible for the distinctive signal in their quark-hadron EoS models, the effect arises from differences in sound speed and stratification at lower densities rather than from a direct phase transition.

## Method
The authors performed axisymmetric (2D) general-relativistic neutrino hydrodynamics simulations using the CoCoNuT-FMT code, simulating two zero-metallicity progenitors of 35 \(M_\odot\) and 85 \(M_\odot\). They compared the purely hadronic SFHx EoS with the chiral mean field (CMF) EoS that includes a smooth crossover to quark matter, and analyzed gravitational-wave spectrograms using Morlet wavelet transforms alongside spatially resolved Fourier analysis of the quadrupole perturbation integrand to identify the radial location and nature of the oscillation modes.

## Key numbers
- The core g-mode frequency lies in the range \(200\lesssim f\lesssim 800\,\text{Hz}\) and decreases with time.
- For the 85 \(M_\odot\) progenitor with the CMF EoS, the mode frequency decreases from \(\sim 600\,\text{Hz}\) at \(0.2\,\text{s}\) to \(\sim 220\,\text{Hz}\) at \(0.32\,\text{s}\) (when black hole collapse occurs).
- For the 85 \(M_\odot\) progenitor with the SFHx EoS, the mode frequency evolves from \(\sim 800\,\text{Hz}\) down to \(\sim 560\,\text{Hz}\) at \(0.58\,\text{s}\).
- For the 35 \(M_\odot\) progenitor with the CMF EoS, the mode lives in the range \(220\text{-}600\,\text{Hz}\).
- The turbulent kinetic energy in the PNS convection zone is about an order of magnitude larger in the 35 \(M_\odot\) CMF model (several \(10^{50}\,\text{erg}\)) than in the corresponding SFHx model.
- The CMF EoS has a maximum Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff mass of \(2.10\,M_\odot\); the SFHx EoS has \(2.13\,M_\odot\).
- Nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_0 \geq 2.6\times 10^{14}\,\text{g}\,\text{cm}^{-3}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of gravitational-wave signatures from proto-neutron star oscillations (e.g., Mueller 2013, Morozova 2018, Torres 2018, Andersen 2021) by identifying a core g-mode that probes the EoS at densities around twice saturation density, in contrast to the dominant surface f/g-mode which is sensitive only to bulk PNS parameters. The results complement constraints from neutron star merger gravitational waves (GW170817, Abbott 2017), pulsar mass measurements (Demorest 2010), NICER radius measurements (Riley 2021, Miller 2021), and heavy-ion collision experiments (Huth 2022). The signal has been found in 3D models by Vartanyan (2023) after submission of this paper, suggesting observational potential for next-generation detectors like the Einstein Telescope.
