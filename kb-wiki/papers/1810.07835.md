# QCD Processes in Few Nucleon Systems

> One of the important issues of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) - the fundamental theory of strong interaction, is the understanding of the role of the quark-gluon interactions in the processes involving nuclear targets. One direction in such studies is to explore the onset of the quark gluon degrees of freedom in nuclear dynamics. The other direction is using the nuclear targets as a `micro-labs' in studies of the QCD processes involving protons and neutrons bound in the nucleus. In the proposed research, we work in both directions considering high energy photo- and electro-production reactions involving deuteron and $^3$He nuclei. In the first half of the research, we study the high energy break-up of the $^3$He nucleus, caused by a incoming photon, into a proton-deuteron pair at the large center of mass scattering angle. The main motivation of the research is the theoretical interpretation of recent experimental data which revealed the unprecedentedly large exponent $s^{-17}$, for the energy dependence of the differential cross section. In the present research, we extend the theoretical formalism of the hard QCD rescattering model to calculate energy and angular dependences of the absolute cross section of the $γ^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ reaction in high momentum transfer limit. The second half of the research explores the deep-inelastic scattering of a polarized electron off the polarized deuteron and $^3$He nuclei, to explore the quark-gluon structure of polarized neutron. In this work, we developed a comprehensive theoretical framework for calculation of the all relevant nuclear effects that will allow the accurate extraction of the neutron data from deep-inelastic scattering involving deuteron and $^3$He targets.

## Key claim

The paper extends the hard QCD rescattering model to calculate absolute cross sections for the high-energy photodisintegration of $^3$He into a proton-deuteron pair at large center-of-mass scattering angles, aiming to theoretically interpret recent experimental data that revealed an unprecedentedly large exponent $s^{-17}$ for the energy dependence of the differential cross section. The work also develops a comprehensive theoretical framework for calculating all relevant nuclear effects to enable accurate extraction of neutron spin structure function $g_1^n$ from deep-inelastic scattering off deuteron and $^3$He targets, with the caveat that the extraction relies on assuming the smearing correction factor for the neutron asymmetry equals that of the proton.

## Method

For the photodisintegration reaction, the author developed the hard rescattering model (HRM) formalism for $\gamma + ^3\text{He} \rightarrow p + d$, calculating the nuclear amplitude, transition spectral function, and parametrizing the hard elastic $pd \rightarrow pd$ differential cross section. For the deep-inelastic scattering part, the author developed a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) framework for polarized electron scattering off polarized deuteron and $^3$He targets, including polarization terms and Fermi motion corrections, then applied a smearing correction procedure to extract the neutron asymmetry $A_1^n$ and subsequently $g_1^n$ from deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- The experimental data for $\gamma^3\text{He}\rightarrow pd$ revealed an energy dependence exponent of $s^{-17}$ for the differential cross section at large center-of-mass scattering angles
- Proton smearing correction $S_p$ ranges from 0.843 (at $x=0.883$, $Q^2=6.574$ GeV$^2$, $E_k=10.0$ GeV) to 0.990 (at $x=0.413$, $Q^2=18.794$ GeV$^2$, $E_k=30.0$ GeV) for a scattering angle of $19^\circ$
- Proton asymmetry smearing ratio $S_{A1,p}$ ranges from 0.9901 (at $x=0.128$, $Q^2=0.48$ GeV$^2$) to 1.1015 (at $x=0.75$, $Q^2=15.73$ GeV$^2$)
- The deep-inelastic scattering regime is established at $Q^2 \geq 4$ GeV$^2$
- COMPASS data covers $1$ GeV$^2 < Q^2 < 100$ GeV$^2$ and $0.004 < x < 0.7$
- HERMES data covers $0.18$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 20$ GeV$^2$ and $0.0041 \leq x \leq 0.9$ with 27.6 GeV polarized positrons
- E143 data covers $0.5$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10$ GeV$^2$ and $0.024 \leq x \leq 0.75$ at incident energies of 9.7, 16.2 and 29.1 GeV
- E155 data covers $1.0$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 40$ GeV$^2$ and $0.010 \leq x \leq 0.9$ at incident energy of 48.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hard rescattering model formalism previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration to the $^3$He nucleus, addressing the theoretical interpretation of recent experimental data showing an $s^{-17}$ scaling. For the deep-inelastic scattering part, the work builds upon the impulse approximation method for extracting neutron structure functions from deuteron data developed by Bodek (parametrization) and Atwood and West (smearing corrections), extending it to spin-dependent observables. The framework enables future extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_1^n$ from polarized $^3$He and deuteron experiments at facilities such as COMPASS, HERMES, E143, and E155, with the next step being application of the same tools to calculate asymmetries for $^3$He targets.
