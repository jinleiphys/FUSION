# Supersymmetric inversion of effective-range expansions

> A complete and consistent inversion technique is proposed to derive an accurate interaction potential from an effective-range function for a given partial wave in the neutral case. First, the effective-range function is Taylor or Padé expanded, which allows high precision fitting of the experimental scattering phase shifts with a minimal number of parameters on a large energy range. Second, the corresponding poles of the scattering matrix are extracted in the complex wave-number plane. Third, the interaction potential is constructed with supersymmetric transformations of the radial Schrödinger equation. As an illustration, the method is applied to the experimental phase shifts of the neutron-proton elastic scattering in the $^1S_0$ and $^1D_2$ channels on the $[0-350]$ MeV laboratory energy interval.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a complete inversion technique that derives an accurate interaction potential from an effective-range function for a given partial wave in the neutral case, but the method's success depends on constraining scattering-matrix poles to the imaginary axis to avoid oscillating potentials, and the resulting potentials are local and partial-wave dependent, unlike standard realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The method first expands the effective-range function as a Taylor or Padé series to fit experimental scattering phase shifts with minimal parameters over a large energy range. Second, the corresponding poles of the scattering matrix are extracted in the complex wave-number plane from this rational expansion. Third, the interaction potential is constructed by applying a chain of supersymmetric transformations to the radial Schrödinger equation, starting from a purely centrifugal initial potential, with the final potential given by a Crum-Krein formula involving Wronskians of the factorization solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron-proton singlet S-wave, a six-pole fit with poles at κ = -0.0401, -0.7540, 0.6152, 2.0424, 4.1650, 4.6 fm⁻¹ provides a satisfactory fit on the [0-350] MeV laboratory energy interval.
- The S-wave Padé expansion of order [3/2] is K_S(k²) = (0.0422 + 1.3793 k² + 2.0105 k⁴ - 0.058 k⁶)/(1 + 1.5986 k² - 0.6164 k⁴).
- The S-wave 3-term Taylor expansion gives scattering length a = -23.7 fm and effective range r = 2.608 fm, fitting phase shifts only up to 30 MeV laboratory energy.
- For the singlet D-wave, the 3-term Taylor expansion gives a = 0.88762 fm, r = 15.33061 fm, P = -0.00246, with poles at κ = -0.4294, -0.8827, -8.7653, 0.7750, 0.4376 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier inversion methods based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics (Sparenberg 1997, Baye 2004, 2014) and Padé expansions of the effective-range function (Hartt 1981, Kievsky 1997, Babenko 2005, Pupasov 2011). The method produces local potentials that are partial-wave dependent, unlike standard realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions; the S-wave potential is shallow while the D-wave potential is deep, reopening the deep/shallow ambiguity discussion. Future developments could include linking different partial waves, generalizing to coupled-channel cases, and applying the method to atomic physics collisions.
