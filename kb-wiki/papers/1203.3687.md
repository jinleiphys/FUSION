# Role of momentum transfer in the quenching of Gamow-Teller strength

> The relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) is applied in the calculation of the L=0 strength in charge-exchange reactions on $^{48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{208}$Pb and nuclei in the Sn isotopic chain. The microscopic theoretical framework is based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model for the nuclear ground state. The calculation is fully self-consistent, i.e. the same interaction is used both in the RHB equations that determine the quasiparticle basis, and in the matrix equations of the pn-RQRPA. The inclusion of the higher-order terms that include the effect of finite momentum transfer, primarily the isovector spin monopole (IVSM) term, in the transition operator shifts a portion of the strength to the high-energy region above the Gamow-Teller (GT) resonance. The total strength is slightly enhanced in nuclei with small neutron-to-proton ratio but remains unchanged with increasing neutron excess. Based on the strength obtained using the full L=0 transition operator in the pn-RQRPA calculation, we have estimated the impact of the IVSM on the strength measured in the charge-exchagne reactions on $^{90}$Zr and found that the data are consistent with the Ikeda sum rule.

## Key claim

The inclusion of higher-order terms in the transition operator that account for finite momentum transfer, primarily the isovector spin monopole (IVSM) term, shifts a portion of the L=0 strength to high excitation energies above the Gamow-Teller resonance, but the total strength is only slightly enhanced in nuclei with small neutron-to-proton ratio and remains unchanged with increasing neutron excess; based on this calculation, the charge-exchange reaction data on 90Zr are consistent with the Ikeda sum rule.

## Method

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model was used to determine the nuclear ground state, and the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) was applied to calculate L=0 transition strength in charge-exchange reactions. The calculation was fully self-consistent, using the same interaction (the DD-ME2 density-dependent relativistic mean-field functional) in both the RHB equations and the pn-RQRPA matrix equations, with the Gogny D1S force for pairing correlations. The transition operator included the spherical Bessel function j0(qr) expanded to account for finite momentum transfer, incorporating both the Gamow-Teller term and the isovector spin monopole term.

## Key numbers

- For 90Zr, the IVSM strength distribution shows a strong peak at the Gamow-Teller resonance position, with 2-hbar-omega transitions contributing significant strength at excitation energies above the giant resonance
- The non-energy-weighted sum rule for IVSM strength in 90Zr gives S_minus minus S_plus = 17001 fm^4, consistent with the value 3[N<r^4>_n - Z<r^4>_p] = 16654 fm^4
- Energy centroids for IVSM strength (excluding 0-hbar-omega configurations) in 90Zr are 40.0 MeV in the beta- channel and 24.2 MeV in the beta+ channel using DD-ME2
- For 208Pb, the IVSM energy centroids are 37.4 MeV (beta-) and 18.3 MeV (beta+), compared to 39.9 MeV and 14.3 MeV from the Skyrme SGII functional
- For Sn isotopes, the ratio eta of strength below E_95% to total strength reaches 0.8 for 116Sn and 0.9 for 150Sn when using the GT+IVSM operator
- The momentum transfer for a (p,n) reaction at 300 MeV proton kinetic energy depends linearly on excitation energy and has a 1/(1+T/m)^2 dependence on incoming proton kinetic energy

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of isovector spin excitations by Auerbach (1984) using Skyrme SIII Hartree-Fock with schematic RPA, and by Hamamoto (2000) using Skyrme SGII and SIII functionals in a self-consistent HF+TDA approach, which identified the IVSM mode at excitation energies between 20 and 60 MeV. The present study is the first to investigate the effect of the IVSM mode and higher-order momentum transfer terms on the quenching of Gamow-Teller strength, using a fully self-consistent relativistic framework. The results enable more accurate extraction of Gamow-Teller strength from charge-exchange reaction data, particularly for unstable nuclei where new measurements are being performed, and confirm that the measured strength in 90Zr is consistent with the Ikeda sum rule when IVSM contributions are properly accounted for.
