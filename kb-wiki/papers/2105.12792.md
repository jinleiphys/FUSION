# Predictions for flow harmonic distributions and flow factorization ratios at RHIC

> Data obtained at RHIC can be reproduced with relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations by adjusting the viscosity and initial conditions but it is difficult to disentangle these quantities. It is therefore important to find orthogonal observables to constrain the initial conditions separately from the viscosity. New observables have been measured at the LHC and shown to be sensitive to initial conditions and less to medium properties, specifically factorization breaking ratios appears to be promising. Here we consider two initial condition models, NeXus and TRENTO. While both models yield similar results for the scaled flow harmonic distributions both at the LHC and RHIC, they lead to quantitatively much more different patterns for the factorization breaking ratios at RHIC than at LHC, due to the shorter lifetime. Additionally, not only final state interactions but also initial free streaming matter in these ratio predictions and differences between these are enhanced at RHIC compared to LHC. Therefore experimental factorization breaking ratios at RHIC top energy (for transverse momentum) would be interesting to get.

## Key claim

While both NeXus and TRENTO initial condition models yield similar results for scaled flow harmonic distributions at both LHC and RHIC energies, they produce quantitatively much more different patterns for factorization breaking ratios at RHIC than at LHC due to the shorter lifetime of the system at RHIC, and these differences are enhanced by the effects of free streaming and final state interactions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using two initial condition models: NeXus (with the 3+1 ideal hydrodynamic code NeXSPheRIO) and TRENTO (with the 2+1 viscous hydrodynamic code v-USPhydro and separately with VISHNU including free streaming and UrQMD hadronic cascade). They computed scaled flow harmonic distributions P(v_n/<v_n>) and flow factorization ratios r_n(p_T1,p_T2) for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, comparing results across different centralities and transverse momentum bins.

## Key numbers

- For TRENTO initial conditions without free streaming: p=0, k=1.6, nucleon width w=0.51 fm, shear viscosity eta/s=0.05, freeze-out temperature T_FO=150 MeV
- For TRENTO with free streaming (fTRENTO): free streaming time tau_fs=1.16 fm, p=0, k=1.2, nucleon width w=0.956 fm, switching temperature T_sw=151 MeV
- Differences between NeXus and TRENTO predictions for r_2 at RHIC can be as large as approximately 25% for 0-10% centrality at high p_T
- At LHC, differences between NeXus and TRENTO for r_2 in the 0-5% bin were smaller than 10%
- For r_3 at RHIC, fTRENTO and fTRENTO+UrQMD results differ from 1 and can be distinguished for larger p_T and more central collisions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of flow harmonic distributions and factorization breaking ratios that were measured at LHC by ATLAS (2012, 2013), ALICE (2013), and CMS (2015). Prior work by Giacalone (2017) established that scaled harmonic flow distributions are fairly independent of fluid transport properties and reflect initial state fluctuations. The authors build on comparisons by Zhao (2017) showing that AMPT, TRENTO, and IP-Glasma predict a drop in r_3 for large p_T differences stronger than data, and by McDonald (2016) showing that hadronic cascade pushes r_n values toward 1. The paper demonstrates that factorization breaking measurements at RHIC would help distinguish between initial condition models and constrain pre-hydrodynamic stages, which is important for the Beam Energy Scan program exploring the QCD phase diagram.
