# On the critical condition in gravitational shock wave collision and heavy ion collisions

> In this paper, we derived a critical condition for matter equilibration in heavy ion collisions using a holographic approach. A gravitational shock waves with infinite transverse extension is used to model infinite nucleus. We constructed the trapped surface in the collision of two asymmetric planar shock waves with sources at different depth in the bulk AdS and formulated a critical condition for matter equilibration in collision of "nucleus" in the dual gauge theory. We found the critical condition is insensitive to the depth of the source closer to the AdS boundary. To understand the origin of the critical condition, we computed the Next to Leading Order stress tensor in the boundary field theory due to the interaction of the nucleus and found the critical condition corresponds to the breaking down of the perturbative expansion. We indeed expect non-perturbative effects be needed to describe black hole formation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a critical condition for trapped surface formation (dual to matter equilibration) in collisions of asymmetric planar gravitational shock waves in AdS, finding that the condition depends on the product of the two saturation scales and becomes insensitive to the depth of the source closer to the AdS boundary in the highly asymmetric limit.

## Method

The authors model infinite nuclei as gravitational shock waves with infinite transverse extension and sources at different depths \(z_1, z_2\) in the bulk AdS, corresponding to different saturation scales. They construct the marginally trapped surface at the collision moment by solving the Laplace-type master equation with boundary conditions, then compute the Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) stress tensor in the dual boundary field theory from the interaction of the two shock waves, using a bulk-to-boundary propagator in lightcone coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Critical condition for symmetric collision (\(z_1 = z_2 \equiv z_0\)): \(\frac{2\pi^2}{N_c^2} \mu z_0^3 \ge 1\) (equation 4.13 in the text).
- Critical condition for asymmetric collision: \(\frac{4\pi^2}{N_c^2} \mu (z_1 z_2)^{3/2} \ge \sqrt{G(r)}\), where \(G(r)\) is the minimum of function \(A(r,F)\) at given asymmetry ratio \(r = (z_1^4 + z_2^4)^2 / (z_1^4 z_2^4)\).
- In the highly asymmetric limit \(z_1 \gg z_2\), the critical condition simplifies to \(\frac{16\pi^2}{N_c^2} \mu z_2^3 \ge 1\), showing insensitivity to the larger scale \(z_1\).
- The asymptotic power law for \(\sqrt{G(r)}\) as a function of \(z_1/z_2\) has slope approximately 1.5 in a log-log plot, analytically derived as \(\sqrt{G(r)} \sim (z_1/z_2)^{3/2}\).
- Entropy per transverse area for the outermost trapped surface: \(s = (2\pi N_c^2 \mu)^{1/3} + \cdots\) in the high-energy limit.

## Context

This work extends the trapped surface approach of Gubser, Pufu, and Yarom (2009) from point-like black hole collisions to planar wall shock waves with finite extent in the holographic direction, following the wall-on-wall model introduced by Lin and Shuryak (2009). It generalizes the symmetric wall collision to asymmetric collisions with different saturation scales, finding a critical condition analogous to the critical impact parameter in non-central collisions. The NLO stress tensor calculation provides initial conditions for hydrodynamical treatment of heavy ion collisions, and the results suggest that collisions of objects with sufficiently different intrinsic scales (e.g., deuteron-Au versus Au-Au at RHIC) may fail to produce thermal equilibration even at the same energy per nucleon.
