# Simulation of Time-dependent Heisenberg Models in 1D

> In this paper, we provide a theoretical analysis of strongly interacting quantum systems confined by a time-dependent external potential in one spatial dimension. We show that such systems can be used to simulate spin chains described by Heisenberg Hamiltonians in which the exchange coupling constants can be manipulated by time-dependent driving of the shape of the external confinement. As illustrative examples, we consider a harmonic trapping potential with a variable frequency and an infinite square well potential with a time-dependent barrier in the middle.

## Key claim

The paper shows that strongly interacting one-dimensional multicomponent fermionic systems in a time-dependent external potential can be mapped onto a Heisenberg spin chain with time-dependent exchange coupling constants, but only up to corrections suppressed by the inverse interaction strength (1/g_f). The mapping holds provided the interaction strength remains the largest energy scale in the problem.

## Method

The authors start with a 1D system of spinless fermions of one kind (spin up) and one fermion of another kind (spin down) with strong interspecies repulsion, described by Bethe-Peierls boundary conditions. They insert an ansatz wave function into the time-dependent Schrodinger equation, project onto each spatial ordering of the particles, and eliminate the subleading term using the boundary conditions and orthogonality constraints. This yields a system of linear equations for the coefficients a_j(t) that is equivalent to the dynamics of a Heisenberg spin chain Hamiltonian with site- and time-dependent exchange coefficients J_j(t), which are expressed as integrals over the time-dependent wave function of spinless fermions.

## Key numbers

- The exchange coupling coefficients J_j(t) scale as 1/g_f, where g_f is the final interaction strength taken to be very large
- For a harmonic oscillator potential with time-dependent frequency, all coupling constants scale as 1/lambda^3(t), where lambda(t) is the time-dependent scale parameter satisfying lambda^3*ddot_lambda = 1 - omega^2(t)*lambda^4(t)
- For the infinite square well with a delta-barrier of strength alpha, as alpha goes to infinity, J_2/J_1(0) approaches 0 and J_1/J_1(0) approaches 4/3
- For a four-body system in the square well with a time-dependent barrier, driving with parameter A=0.8 yields a 99.6% probability of ending in the down-down-up-up configuration after one cycle

## Context

This work extends the time-independent mapping of strongly interacting 1D systems onto spin chains, previously developed by Volosniev (2014), Deuret (2014), and Volosniev (2015), to the time-dependent case. It contrasts with lattice-based approaches such as those by Itin (2014) by not requiring a lattice approximation, allowing arbitrary trapping potentials. The results enable the study of driven quantum magnetism and state preparation in cold atomic gases, complementing experimental work on superexchange by Trotzky (2008) and offering a route to multiparticle entanglement and quantum gates.
