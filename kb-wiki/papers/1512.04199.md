# Towards Lattice QCD Baryon Forces at the Physical Point: First Results

> Lattice QCD calculations of baryon forces are performed for the first time with (almost) physical quark masses. $N_f = 2+1$ dynamical clover fermion gauge configurations are generated at the lattice spacing of $a \simeq 0.085$ fm on a $(96 a)^4 \simeq (8.2 {\rm fm})^4$ lattice with quark masses corresponding to $(m_π, m_K) \simeq (146, 525)$ MeV. Baryon forces are calculated using the time-dependent HAL QCD method. In this report, we study $ΞΞ$ and $NN$ systems both in $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ channels, and the results for the central and tensor forces as well as phase shifts in the $ΞΞ$ $(^1S_0)$ channel are presented.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of baryon forces are performed for the first time with (almost) physical quark masses, but the results are preliminary and require larger statistics for quantitative conclusions; the paper explicitly states that the term corresponding to relativistic effects is omitted for simplicity in this analysis.

## Method
The authors generated \(N_f = 2+1\) dynamical clover fermion gauge configurations on a \(96^4\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.085\) fm and quark masses corresponding to \((m_\pi, m_K) \simeq (146, 525)\) MeV. Baryon forces were extracted from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlators using the time-dependent HAL QCD method, with a velocity expansion to obtain leading-order central and tensor potentials. The study examined \(\Xi\Xi\) and \(NN\) systems in both \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels, using 203 configurations with 4 rotations and 20 wall sources each.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: \(a \simeq 0.085\) fm on a \((96 a)^4 \simeq (8.2\ \text{fm})^4\) lattice.
- Quark masses correspond to \((m_\pi, m_K) \simeq (146, 525)\) MeV.
- Total statistics: 203 configurations \(\times\) 4 rotations \(\times\) 20 wall sources.
- Computational performance: \(\sim 17\%\) efficiency on 2048 nodes (8 cores/node) of the K computer, corresponding to \(\sim 45\) TFlops sustained.
- For \(\Xi\Xi\) (\(^1S_0\)) channel, results are consistent across Euclidean time \(t = 10, 11, 12, 13, 14\) (shown in figures).
- For \(NN\) (\(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\)) channel, results are shown at \(t = 7, 8, 9\) (smaller than \(\Xi\Xi\) due to larger statistical errors).

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of baryon forces that were limited to unphysically heavy quark masses, using the HAL QCD method developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2012). It enables first-principles studies of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions at physical quark masses, complementing experimental efforts where precision is better for larger strangeness values. The results for \(\Xi\Xi\) (\(^1S_0\)) serve as a doorway to probe the \(NN\) (\(^1S_0\)) interaction via flavor SU(3) classification, and the observed \(\Xi\Xi\) tensor force with opposite sign and weaker magnitude compared to the \(NN\) tensor force can be understood through phenomenological one-boson exchange potentials with SU(3) meson-baryon couplings.
