# Spectroscopy of quadrupole and octupole states in rare-earth nuclei from a Gogny force

> Collective quadrupole and octupole states are described in a series of Sm and Gd isotopes within the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM), whose Hamiltonian parameters are deduced from mean field calculations with the Gogny energy density functional. The link between both frameworks is the ($β_2β_3$) potential energy surface computed within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework in the case of the Gogny force. The diagonalization of the IBM Hamiltonian provides excitation energies and transition strengths of an assorted set of states including both positive and negative parity states. The resultant spectroscopic properties are compared with the available experimental data and also with the results of the configuration mixing calculations with the Gogny force within the generator coordinate method (GCM). The structure of excited $0^{+}$ states and its connection with double octupole phonons is also addressed. The model is shown to describe the empirical trend of the low-energy quadrupole and octupole collective structure fairly well, and turns out to be consistent with GCM results obtained with the Gogny force.

## Key claim

The mapped interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian, derived from Gogny-HFB potential energy surfaces, describes the empirical trend of low-energy quadrupole and octupole collective structure fairly well in Sm and Gd isotopes, but the agreement is limited to a 5 MeV window around the absolute minimum of the mean-field potential energy surface, and the IBM surfaces are systematically flatter than the HFB ones.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric (Q20, Q30)-constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to obtain mean-field potential energy surfaces for 146-156Sm and 148-158Gd. They then mapped these fermionic surfaces onto bosonic potential energy surfaces by fitting the parameters of an sdf interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian, using coherent boson states to compute the IBM expectation value. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized without truncation on the number of f bosons to obtain excitation energies, wave functions, and reduced transition probabilities for positive- and negative-parity states.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R4/2 = E(4+1)/E(2+1) for 146,148Sm is 2.33 and 2.38, both close to the vibrational limit
- For the transitional nuclei 150,152Sm, the theoretical R4/2 values are 2.82 and 2.91, respectively (experimental: 2.31 and 3.01)
- For 154,156Sm, the calculated R4/2 ratios are 3.21 and 3.25, indicating well-developed rotational bands
- The 152Sm R4/2 value of 2.91 is exactly the same as the X(5) critical point symmetry value
- The boson effective charges used are e1 = 0.01 e b1/2, e2 = 0.13 e b, and e3 = 0.099 e b3/2, taken from previous phenomenological IBM studies
- The total boson number NB runs from 6 to 12 for Sm isotopes and 7 to 13 for Gd isotopes, with 132Sn as the inert core

## Context

This work extends the HFB-to-IBM mapping methodology, previously applied to quadrupole states by Nomura et al. (2010, 2011, 2012) and to quadrupole-octupole correlations in light actinides using relativistic EDFs by Nomura et al. (2013, 2014), to rare-earth nuclei using the Gogny energy density functional. The results are compared with two-dimensional generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations by Rayner et al. (2012) using the same Gogny-D1M parametrization, and the consistency between IBM and GCM results for 1-1 state excitation energies in the N=84-88 range validates the mapping procedure. The study enables systematic IBM calculations of quadrupole-octupole collectivity across isotopic chains, with the structure of excited 0+ states and their connection to double octupole phonons identified as a key open question for future work.
