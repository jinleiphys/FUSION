# Azimuthal harmonics in small and large collision systems at RHIC top energies

> The first ($v_1^{\text{even}}$), second ($v_2$) and third ($v_3$) harmonic coefficients of the azimuthal particle distribution at mid-rapidity, are extracted for charged hadrons and studied as a function of transverse momentum ($p_T$) and mean charged particle multiplicity density $\langle \mathrm{N_{ch}} \rangle$ in U+U ($\roots =193$~GeV), Au+Au, Cu+Au, Cu+Cu, $d$+Au and $p$+Au collisions at $\roots = 200$~GeV with the STAR Detector. For the same $\langle \mathrm{N_{ch}} \rangle$, the $v_1^{\text{even}}$ and $v_3$ coefficients are observed to be independent of collision system, while $v_2$ exhibits such a scaling only when normalized by the initial-state eccentricity ($\varepsilon_2$). The data also show that $\ln(v_2/\varepsilon_2)$ scales linearly with $\langle \mathrm{N_{ch}} \rangle^{-1/3}$. These measurements provide insight into initial-geometry fluctuations and the role of viscous hydrodynamic attenuation on $v_n$ from small to large collision systems.

## Key claim

The first, second, and third harmonic coefficients of the azimuthal particle distribution at mid-rapidity are extracted for charged hadrons and studied as a function of transverse momentum and mean charged particle multiplicity density in U+U, Au+Au, Cu+Au, Cu+Cu, d+Au, and p+Au collisions at RHIC top energies. The claim that v1^even and v3 are independent of collision system for the same mean multiplicity density, while v2 exhibits such scaling only when normalized by the initial-state eccentricity, is conditional on the specific range of multiplicities and transverse momenta studied, and on the use of the event-plane method for extracting the harmonics.

## Method

The STAR detector was used to measure charged hadrons at mid-rapidity in six collision systems at center-of-mass energies of 193 GeV (U+U) and 200 GeV (all others). Azimuthal harmonic coefficients v1^even, v2, and v3 were extracted using the event-plane method as a function of transverse momentum and mean charged particle multiplicity density. The v2 values were normalized by the initial-state eccentricity epsilon_2, and the scaling of ln(v2/epsilon_2) with mean multiplicity density was examined.

## Key numbers

- v1^even and v3 coefficients are observed to be independent of collision system for the same mean charged particle multiplicity density at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (and 193 GeV for U+U).
- v2 exhibits scaling with collision system only when normalized by the initial-state eccentricity epsilon_2.
- ln(v2/epsilon_2) scales linearly with mean charged particle multiplicity density to the power of -1/3, i.e., ln(v2/epsilon_2) proportional to <N_ch>^{-1/3}.
- Measurements were performed in U+U at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV and in Au+Au, Cu+Au, Cu+Cu, d+Au, and p+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, such as those by the STAR Collaboration (2014) and the PHENIX Collaboration (2015), by systematically comparing small systems (p+Au, d+Au) with large systems (Au+Au, U+U) at the same collision energy. The results provide constraints on initial-geometry fluctuations and the role of viscous hydrodynamic attenuation on flow harmonics from small to large collision systems, enabling more precise tests of hydrodynamic models and the extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the quark-gluon plasma.
