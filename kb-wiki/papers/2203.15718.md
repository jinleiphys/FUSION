# $Λ$ spin polarization in event-by-event relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present a systematic study of $Λ$ hyperon's polarization observables using event-by-event (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamics. The effects of initial hot spot size and QGP's specific shear viscosity on the polarization observables are quantified. We examine the effects of the two formulations of the thermal shear tensor on the polarization observables using the same hydrodynamic background. With event-by-event simulations, we make predictions for the Fourier coefficients of $Λ$'s longitudinal polarization $P^z$ with respect to the event planes of different orders of anisotropic flow. We propose new correlations among the Fourier coefficients of $P^z$ and charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients to further test the mapping from fluid velocity gradients to hyperon's polarization. Finally, we present a system size scan with Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and O+O collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV to study the system size dependence of polarization observables at the Relativistic Heavy-ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies how the $\Lambda$ hyperon's polarization observables depend on initial hot spot size and QGP specific shear viscosity in event-by-event (3+1)D relativistic hydrodynamics, while also comparing two different formulations of the thermal shear tensor contribution to polarization. The abstract glosses over the fact that the two shear-induced polarization formulations give substantially different results, and that the sign and magnitude of the longitudinal polarization's azimuthal dependence can flip depending on which formulation is used.

## Method

The authors employ a geometric-based 3D initial condition model with Glauber geometry and participant nucleon hot spots, evolved using the (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code MUSIC with a lattice QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-BQS). The system is converted to hadrons via the Cooper-Frye prescription at a switching energy density, followed by hadronic transport with UrQMD. The $\Lambda$ spin polarization vector is computed from the Cooper-Frye hyper-surface using two different axial vector formulations: one from Becattini et al. (2021) (SIP(BBP)) and one from Liu et al. (2021) and Yi et al. (2021) (SIP(LY)), which differ in their treatment of the thermal shear tensor and include a net baryon chemical potential induced polarization term.

## Key numbers

- Initial hot spot width $w$ values studied: 0.4 fm, 0.8 fm, and 1.2 fm
- Specific shear viscosity $\eta T/(e+P)$ values studied: 0, 0.08, and 0.16
- Switching energy density $e_{\rm sw}$ values studied: 0.25 GeV/fm$^3$ and 0.5 GeV/fm$^3$
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV for Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and O+O collisions
- The thermal shear tensor from Becattini et al. (2021) suppresses the magnitude of global polarization $P^y$ by 10% in semi-peripheral centrality bins
- The second-order Fourier coefficient of longitudinal polarization $p_2^z\{\mathrm{SP}\}$ increases with specific shear viscosity; ideal hydro gives almost zero $p_2^z\{\mathrm{SP}\}$
- The STAR measurement of fluid vorticity is quoted as $\omega \approx (9 \pm 1) \times 10^{21} s^{-1}$ in semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\Lambda$ hyperon polarization using (3+1)D hydrodynamics (Karpenko 2016, Ryu 2021) by systematically studying sensitivity to initial-state fluctuations and QGP viscosity, which had been lacking. It directly compares the two competing formulations of thermal shear tensor contributions to polarization from Becattini et al. (2021) and Liu et al. (2021)/Yi et al. (2021) using the same hydrodynamic background, and proposes new Pearson correlations between Fourier coefficients of $P^z$ and charged hadron anisotropic flow coefficients. The results enable tighter constraints on initial condition modeling and QGP transport properties by combining polarization observables with traditional hadronic flow observables.
