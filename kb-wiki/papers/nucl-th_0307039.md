# On meson resonances and chiral symmetry

> We study meson resonances with quantum numbers J^P=1^+ in terms of the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. At leading order a parameter-free prediction is obtained for the scattering of Goldstone bosons off vector mesons with J^P=1^- once we insist on approximate crossing symmetry of the unitarized scattering amplitude. A resonance spectrum arises that is remarkably close to the empirical pattern. In particular, we find that the strangeness-zero resonances h_1(1380), f_(1285) and b_1(1235) are formed due to strong K \bar K_μand \bar K K_μchannels. This leads to large coupling constants of those resonances to the latter states.

## Key claim

The authors claim that axial-vector meson resonances with quantum numbers J^P = 1^+ are dynamically generated by coupled-channel interactions of Goldstone bosons with vector mesons, rather than being part of the large-N_c ground state of QCD. This claim is conditional on insisting on approximate crossing symmetry of the unitarized scattering amplitude, which makes the leading-order prediction parameter-free.

## Method

The authors use the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian to describe the scattering of Goldstone bosons (π, K, η) off vector mesons (ρ, ω, K*, φ). They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an on-shell reduction scheme and apply a renormalization condition that enforces approximate crossing symmetry, with subtraction scales set to the vector meson masses (M_ρ = 770 MeV for S=0, M_K* = 892 MeV for S=±1). The scattering problem decouples into thirteen orthogonal channels specified by isospin, G-parity, and strangeness, and the resulting amplitudes are analyzed for poles corresponding to J^P = 1^+ resonances.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction scale for S=0 channels is set to μ(I,0) = M_ρ(770) MeV, and for S=±1 channels to μ(I,±1) = M_K*(892) MeV.
- In the heavy SU(3) limit (m_π,K,η ≈ 500 MeV, m_ρ,ω,K*,φ ≈ 900 MeV), the resonance states turn into bound states forming two degenerate octets and one singlet with masses 1360 MeV and 1280 MeV respectively.
- In the light SU(3) limit (m_π,K,η ≈ 140 MeV, m_ρ,ω,K*,φ ≈ 700 MeV), no bound-state nor resonance signals are observed.
- The leading-order interaction kernel scales with N_c^{-1}, causing resonances to disappear in the large-N_c limit.
- The pion decay constant used throughout is f = 90 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings that baryon resonances with J^P = 1/2^- and J^P = 3/2^- are dynamically generated by chiral coupled-channel dynamics (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2000-2002; Granada and Copenhagen works). It contrasts with approaches where axial-vector mesons are considered part of the large-N_c ground state of QCD (Prades, Pich, and Rafel, 2001; de Rafael, 2001). The results enable a unified description of meson and baryon resonances as dynamically generated states, and provide parameter-free predictions for axial-vector meson properties that can be tested against empirical data.
