# Parity Violation and the Nucleon-Nucleon System

> The status of the field of nuclear parity violation---both experimental and theoretical---is reviewed, with special emphasis on those results which have been obtained since the 1985 review article by Adelberger and Haxton.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of nuclear parity violation, emphasizing that while the weak interaction at the quark level is simple, making a convincing connection between experimental signals and the fundamental Hamiltonian has proven extraordinarily difficult, and deep unresolved questions remain despite more than a quarter-century of work since the first convincing evidence in 1967.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for parity violation in the nucleon-nucleon system, describing the parity-violating NN potential derived from meson exchange (π, ρ, ω) with weak parity-violating coupling constants calculated using the valence quark model and the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) approach. They survey experimental signals including photon circular polarization, gamma-decay asymmetries, longitudinal analyzing power in polarized proton scattering, neutron spin rotation, and parity-forbidden decay widths, with emphasis on measurements in the pp system, few-body nuclei, and complex nuclei where nuclear enhancement can amplify signals.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of parity-violating to parity-conserving NN potentials is estimated as V_NN^(-)/V_NN^(+) ~ G m_π^2 ~ 10^(-7), where G = 1.01 × 10^(-5) M_N^(-2) is the weak coupling constant
- The longitudinal analyzing power A_z for 45 MeV pp scattering was measured as A_z(45.0 MeV) = -(1.50 ± 0.22) × 10^(-7), with the total nuclear PNC analyzing power A_z^tot(45.0 MeV) = -(1.57 ± 0.23) × 10^(-7)
- The photon asymmetry in the decay of the 8^- isomer of ^180Hf is A_γ = -(1.66 ± 0.18) × 10^(-2)
- The longitudinal asymmetry in low energy neutron scattering from ^139La is A_L = (9.55 ± 0.35) × 10^(-2)
- DDH "best" values for weak NNM couplings (in units of 3.8 × 10^(-8)): f_π = 12, h_ρ^0 = -30, h_ρ^1 = -0.5, h_ρ^2 = -25, h_ω^0 = -5, h_ω^1 = -3

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive review by Adelberger and Haxton (1985), summarizing progress in experiments and interpretation since that time. The paper describes the DDH calculation (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980) as the comprehensive theoretical framework that includes both charged and neutral current contributions, with subsequent calculations by Dubach (1986) and Feldman et al. (1991) yielding qualitatively similar results. The work enables future experiments to determine the six weak NNM coupling constants through measurements in the NN system and complex nuclei, though the authors note that the number of feasible NN experiments is not sufficient to determine these couplings separately.
