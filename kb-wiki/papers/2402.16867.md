# Microscopic Origin of Criticality at Macroscale in QCD Chiral Phase Transition

> We reveal that the criticality of the chiral phase transition in QCD at the macroscale arises from the microscopic energy levels of its fundamental constituents, the quarks. We establish a novel relation between cumulants of the chiral order parameter (i.e., chiral condensate) and correlations among the energy levels of quarks (i.e., eigenspectra of the massless Dirac operator), which naturally leads to a generalization of the Banks-Casher relation. Based on this novel relation and through (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action with varying light quark masses in the vicinity of the chiral phase transition, we demonstrate that the correlations among the infrared part of the Dirac eigenspectra exhibit same universal scaling behaviors as expected of the cumulants of the chiral condensate. We find that these universal scaling behaviors extend up to the physical values of the up and down quark masses.

## Key claim
The paper establishes that the universal scaling behavior of macroscopic cumulants of the chiral condensate near the QCD chiral phase transition originates from correlations among the infrared eigenvalues of the massless Dirac operator, and demonstrates that this scaling extends down to physical light quark masses (pion mass 140 MeV) for temperatures between 135 and 145 MeV.

## Method
The authors derived a theoretical relation connecting cumulants of the chiral condensate to n-point correlation functions of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum, generalizing the Banks-Casher relation. They performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action with physical strange quark mass and varying light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 140, 110, 80, and 55 MeV, at temperatures from 135 to 176 MeV. They computed the first three cumulants of the chiral condensate from the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectra and tested their scaling against the predictions of the 3-d O(2) universality class.

## Key numbers
- The chiral transition temperature was determined as Tc = 144.2(6) MeV, with scale parameter z0 = 1.83(9), taken from prior O(2) scaling fits to static quark free energy by Clarke (2020).
- Light quark masses were varied as ml = ms/27, ms/40, ms/80, ms/160, corresponding to Goldstone pion masses m_pi ≈ 140, 110, 80, and 55 MeV respectively.
- The universal critical exponents used were beta = 0.349 and delta = 4.78, belonging to the 3-d O(2) universality class.
- The temperature range where scaling was observed was T in [135, 145] MeV; outside this window no scaling was seen.

## Context
This work extends the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1979) to higher-order cumulants of the chiral condensate, providing a direct microscopic origin for the critical scaling previously observed in macroscopic observables. It builds on lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition by the HotQCD collaboration (2019) and on O(2) scaling analyses by Engels (2001) and Ejiri (2009). The result enables future studies to probe criticality in heavy-ion collisions through connections between Dirac eigenvalue correlations and experimental observables, and opens the possibility of extracting universal scaling functions directly from the microscopic quark energy level correlations.
