# Nucleon Resonances Near 2 GeV

> The nucleon resonances near 2 GeV are investigated through the $Σ$(1385) and $Λ(1520)$ photoproductions within a Regge-plus-resonance approach based on the new experimental data released by the CLAS Collaboration. The $Δ(2000)$ and the $N(2120)$ are found essential to reproduce the experimental data and should be assigned as second $[Δ5/2^+]$ and third $[N3/2^-]$ in the constituent quark model, respectively. A calculation of the binding energy and decay pattern supports that the $N(1875)$, which is listed in the PDG as the third $N3/2^-$ nucleon resonance instead of the $N(2120)$, is from the $Σ(1385)K$ interaction rather than a three quark state.

# Nucleon Resonances Near 2 GeV

## Key claim
The nucleon resonances Delta(2000) and N(2120) are essential to reproduce the new CLAS photoproduction data for Sigma(1385) and Lambda(1520), and should be assigned as the second [Delta 5/2+] and third [N 3/2-] states in the constituent quark model, respectively. However, the N(1875), which the PDG lists as the third N 3/2- resonance, is argued to arise from a Sigma(1385)K molecular interaction rather than a three-quark state, based on binding energy and decay pattern calculations.

## Method
The author analyzed the new CLAS experimental data for Sigma(1385) and Lambda(1520) photoproduction off protons using a Regge-plus-resonance approach, fitting differential cross sections across a range of energies and angles. Nucleon resonances near 2 GeV were included in the model only if they had large predicted decay widths in the gamma p and meson-baryon channels from constituent quark model calculations. The binding energy of the N(1875) was calculated using a Bethe-Salpeter equation approach for the Sigma(1385)K system, and the resulting wave function was used to study the decay pattern through a hadronic loop mechanism.

## Key numbers
- The Delta(2000) resonance produced a chi-squared increase of 4.5 when turned off in the Sigma(1385) photoproduction fit, with the full model achieving chi-squared = 2.5 per degree of freedom.
- The N(2120) resonance produced a chi-squared increase of 0.5 when turned off in the Lambda(1520) photoproduction fit, with the full model achieving chi-squared = 3.1 per degree of freedom.
- For the N(1875) as a Sigma(1385)K molecular state, binding energies of 8 MeV, 28 MeV, and 67 MeV were calculated for cutoff values Lambda = 1.72 GeV, 1.80 GeV, and 1.88 GeV, respectively.
- The N(1875) decay branching ratio to N sigma was approximately 55% across all cutoff values, consistent with the BnGa analysis value of 60 +/- 12% and the PDG value of 24 +/- 24%.
- The N(1875) total decay width was calculated as 73 MeV, 155 MeV, and 257 MeV for the three cutoff values, compared to the PDG value of 30 +25/-25 MeV and the BnGa value of 200 +/- 20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous constituent quark model studies of nucleon resonance radiative and strong decays to Sigma(1385) and Lambda(1520) by Capstick (1998, 1992), and builds on theoretical analyses of Lambda(1520) photoproduction by Xie (2010) and He (2012). The new high-precision CLAS data from Moriya (2013) provide the opportunity to test the role of high-mass nucleon resonances near 2 GeV. The molecular interpretation of N(1875) as a Sigma(1385)K state contradicts the constituent quark model assignment and the current PDG listing, and enables future studies of hadronic molecule formation in the nucleon resonance spectrum.
