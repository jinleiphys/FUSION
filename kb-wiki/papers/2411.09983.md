# Effect of Coriolis Force on Diffusion of D Meson

> We have attempted to calculate and estimate the spatial diffusion coefficients of D meson through rotating hadron resonance gas, which can be produced in the late stage of peripheral heavy ion collisions. Employing the framework of kinetic theory in relaxation time approximation, and using Einstein's diffusion relation, one can express the spatial diffusion coefficients of D meson as a ratio of its conductivity to its susceptibility. Here, we have tuned D meson relaxation time from the knowledge of earlier works on its spatial diffusion estimations, and then we have extended the framework for the finite rotation picture of hadronic matter, where only the effect of Coriolis force is considered. Our study also revealed the anisotropic nature of diffusion in the presence of rotation with future possibilities of phenomenological signature.

## Key claim

The paper calculates spatial diffusion coefficients of D mesons in a rotating hadron resonance gas, finding that the Coriolis force introduces anisotropy in the diffusion tensor, splitting it into parallel, perpendicular, and Hall components. The authors note that this is an initial step that leaves the actual evolution of the D meson distribution via Fokker-Planck or Langevin equations as future work.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within a rotating frame, using the covariant formulation with connection coefficients derived from the rotating-frame metric. The D meson relaxation time is obtained from a hard-sphere scattering model against the background hadron resonance gas, where the hadronic medium is described by the ideal hadron resonance gas model including all hadrons and resonances up to mass 2.6 GeV. The spatial diffusion coefficients are then expressed as the ratio of the anisotropic conductivity tensor to the susceptibility, following Einstein's diffusion relation.

## Key numbers

*   The orbital angular momentum of the medium formed in off-central collisions at RHIC can range up to \(10^7\hbar\) (STAR 2017).
*   The hadron resonance gas model includes all hadrons up to mass 2.6 GeV as listed by the Particle Data Group (2008).
*   The relaxation time of the D meson is defined as \(\tau_c = 1/(n_{\text{HRG}} v_{\text{av}} \pi a^2)\), where \(n_{\text{HRG}}\) is the hadron resonance gas number density, \(v_{\text{av}}\) is the average D meson velocity, and \(\pi a^2\) is the hard-sphere cross section.
*   The effective relaxation time in the perpendicular direction is \(\tau_c^{\perp} \equiv \tau_c / [1 + (\tau_c / \tau_{\Omega})^2]\), and in the Hall direction is \(\tau_c^{\times} \equiv \tau_c (\tau_c / \tau_{\Omega}) / [1 + (\tau_c / \tau_{\Omega})^2]\), where \(\tau_{\Omega} \equiv 1/(2\Omega)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on anisotropic transport coefficients in rotating nuclear matter, which had explored shear viscosity and electrical conductivity in the presence of the Coriolis force (Aung 2023, Dwibedi 2023, Padhan 2024). It draws a mathematical analogy between the Coriolis force in a rotating frame and the Lorentz force in a magnetic field, which had previously been used to study anisotropic diffusion of heavy quarks and mesons in magnetic fields (Satapathy 2022, Dudal 2018). The paper enables future phenomenological studies of D meson diffusion in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, where the rotating hadronic matter produced in the late stage could imprint anisotropic signatures on heavy-flavor observables.
