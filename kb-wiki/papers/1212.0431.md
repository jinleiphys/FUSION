# Complex scaling method for three- and four-body scattering above the break-up thresholds

> A formalism based on the complex-scaling method is presented to solve the few particle scattering problem in configuration space using bound state techniques with trivial boundary conditions. Several applications to A=3,4 systems are presented to demonstrate the efficiency of the method in computing elastic as well as break-up reactions with Hamiltonians including both short and long-range interaction.

## Key claim

The complex-scaling method, when combined with Faddeev-Yakubovski equations, can solve three- and four-body scattering problems above break-up thresholds using bound-state techniques with trivial boundary conditions, provided the complex scaling angle \(\theta\) satisfies specific inequalities involving cluster binding energies and center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The authors separate the incoming plane wave from the Faddeev-Yakubovski components for four identical particles, then apply the complex scaling operator \(\widehat{S}=e^{i\theta r\partial/\partial r}\) to the outgoing wave components, which makes them exponentially decreasing for \(0<\theta<\pi/2\). The resulting driven equations for the scaled components are solved by expanding the radial dependence in cubic spline basis functions on a three-dimensional grid, with vanishing boundary conditions. Scattering amplitudes are extracted from these solutions using integral relations derived from Green's theorem.

## Key numbers

- For S-wave nucleon-deuteron scattering at \(E_{lab}=14.1\) MeV using MT I-III potential: \(^2S_{1/2}\) n-d phase shift \(Re(\delta)=105.5^\circ\) with inelasticity \(\eta=0.465\); \(^4S_{3/2}\) n-d phase shift \(Re(\delta)=69.0^\circ\) with \(\eta=0.978\)
- For S-wave nucleon-deuteron scattering at \(E_{lab}=42.0\) MeV: \(^2S_{1/2}\) n-d phase shift \(Re(\delta)=41.5^\circ\) with \(\eta=0.502\); \(^4S_{3/2}\) n-d phase shift \(Re(\delta)=37.7^\circ\) with \(\eta=0.903\)
- For n-\(^3\)H collisions at \(E_{lab}=14.4\) MeV using MT I-III: elastic cross section \(\sigma_e=922\) mb, break-up cross section \(\sigma_b=11\) mb, total \(\sigma_t=933\) mb (experimental total \(978\pm70\) mb)
- For n-\(^3\)H collisions at \(E_{lab}=22.1\) MeV: elastic \(\sigma_e=512\) mb, break-up \(\sigma_b=38\) mb, total \(\sigma_t=550\) mb (experimental total \(620\pm24\) mb)
- The complex scaling angle must satisfy \(\tan\theta < \sqrt{2B_3/E_{cm}}\) for 3+1 incoming waves and \(\tan\theta < \sqrt{2B_2/E_{cm}}\) for 2+2 incoming waves, where \(B_3\) and \(B_2\) are cluster binding energies

## Context

This work extends the complex-scaling approach originally proposed by Nuttal and Cohen (late 1960s) for short-range potentials, and builds on the authors' previous demonstration for three-nucleon scattering (Lazauskas 2011). It provides an alternative to the Lorentz integral transform method (Barnea 2001), the complex energy method (Uzu 2003), momentum lattice techniques (Rubtsova 2009), and discretized continuum solutions (Pisa 2012), with the advantage of handling both elastic and break-up reactions for up to four particles including Coulomb repulsion and optical potentials. The method enables accurate treatment of many-body scattering and breakup reactions in nuclear and molecular physics.
