# Quantum anholonomies in time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm rings

> Anholonomies in eigenstates are studied through time-dependent variations of a magnetic flux in an Aharonov-Bohm ring. The anholonomies in the eigenenergy and the expectation values of eigenstates are shown to persist beyond the adiabatic regime. The choice of the gauge of the magnetic flux is shown to be crucial to clarify the relationship of these anholonomies to the eigenspace anholonomy, which is described by a non-Abelian connection in the adiabatic limit.

# Quantum anholonomies in time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm rings

## Key claim

Eigenvalue and eigenspace anholonomies (exotic quantum holonomies) persist in a time-dependent Aharonov-Bohm ring beyond the adiabatic regime, but the appearance of the eigenspace anholonomy depends critically on the choice of gauge for the magnetic vector potential; under the periodic gauge the eigenfunctions show no anholonomy despite the eigenenergy anholonomy, while under the Byers-Yang gauge both anholonomies are synchronized.

## Method

The authors study a charged particle on a one-dimensional ring threaded by a time-dependent magnetic flux, using two different gauge choices: the periodic gauge (where wavefunctions satisfy periodic boundary conditions) and the Byers-Yang gauge (where wavefunctions satisfy a quasi-periodic boundary condition). They solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation exactly for arbitrary time dependence of the flux, exploiting the fact that eigenfunctions in the periodic gauge are independent of the flux. The eigenspace anholonomy is characterized through a holonomy matrix derived from a non-Abelian gauge connection in the parameter space of the magnetic flux.

## Key numbers

- The eigenenergy of the k-th state is $E_k(\Phi) = \frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{2\pi(k-\Phi)}{L}\right]^2$, where $\Phi$ is the magnetic flux normalized by the flux quantum $2\pi/q$ and $L$ is the ring circumference.
- Under a cyclic increment of $\Phi$ by 1, the eigenenergy satisfies $E_k(\Phi+1) = E_{k-1}(\Phi)$, demonstrating eigenenergy anholonomy.
- The expectation value of the velocity operator for the k-th eigenstate is $v_k(\Phi) = \frac{2\pi(k-\Phi)}{L}$, with $v_k(\Phi+1) = v_{k-1}(\Phi)$.
- The probability current density for the k-th eigenstate is $j_k(\Phi) = \frac{2\pi(k-\Phi)}{mL^2}$, with $j_k(\Phi+1) = j_{k-1}(\Phi)$.
- Under the Byers-Yang gauge, the eigenfunctions satisfy $\tilde{\psi}_k(x; \Phi'+1) = e^{i\pi}\tilde{\psi}_{k-1}(x; \Phi')$, showing eigenspace anholonomy.
- The holonomy matrix for a cyclic increment of $\Phi$ by 1 is $M_{k'', k'}(C) = \delta_{k'', k'-1}$, a permutation matrix.
- The non-Abelian gauge connection in the Byers-Yang gauge is $\tilde{A}_{k'',k'}(\Phi) = \frac{i}{k''-k'}(1-\delta_{k'',k'})$, independent of $\Phi$.
- The W-matrix for a path from $\Phi'$ to $\Phi''$ is $W_{k'',k'}(C) = e^{-i(k''-k')\pi}\frac{\sin[\pi(\Phi''-\Phi'+k''-k')]}{\pi(\Phi''-\Phi'+k''-k')}$.
- The correction to the dynamical phase in the Byers-Yang gauge is $\pi(\Phi'' - \Phi')$, which does not vanish even in the adiabatic limit.

## Context

This work extends the concept of exotic quantum holonomy, previously studied only in the strictly adiabatic limit by Cheon (1998) and Tanaka (2008, 2009), into the non-adiabatic regime using the Aharonov-Bohm ring as a testbed. The system is related to the persistent current studied by Buettiker (1983) and the Aharonov-Bohm effect (Aharonov and Bohm, 1959). The Byers-Yang gauge (Byers and Yang, 1961) is employed to reconcile the eigenspace anholonomy with the standard non-Abelian connection formalism of Cheon (2009). The results enable experimental investigation of exotic quantum holonomies in mesoscopic rings and suggest the need for a gauge-invariant formulation of anholonomies in expectation values of observables.
