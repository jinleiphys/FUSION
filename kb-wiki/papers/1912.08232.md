# Effective charge from lattice QCD

> Using lattice configurations for quantum chromodynamics (QCD) generated with three domain-wall fermions at a physical pion mass, we obtain a parameter-free prediction of QCD's renormalisation-group-invariant process-independent effective charge, $\hatα(k^2)$. Owing to the dynamical breaking of scale invariance, evident in the emergence of a gluon mass-scale, this coupling saturates at infrared momenta: $\hatα(0)/π=0.97(4)$. Amongst other things: $\hatα(k^2)$ is almost identical to the process-dependent (PD) effective charge defined via the Bjorken sum rule; and also that PD charge which, employed in the one-loop evolution equations, delivers agreement between pion parton distribution functions computed at the hadronic scale and experiment. The diversity of unifying roles played by $\hatα(k^2)$ suggests that it is a strong candidate for that object which represents the interaction strength in QCD at any given momentum scale; and its properties support a conclusion that QCD is a mathematically well-defined quantum field theory in four dimensions.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD configurations with three domain-wall fermions at a physical pion mass, the authors obtain a parameter-free prediction of QCD's renormalisation-group-invariant process-independent effective charge, which saturates at infrared momenta due to dynamical gluon mass generation; the abstract's claim of a "strong candidate" for the universal interaction strength is hedged by the observation that the charge is almost identical to two specific process-dependent charges rather than proven to be universal.

## Method

The authors compute the renormalisation-group-invariant process-independent effective charge from lattice QCD gauge-sector two-point functions (gluon propagator and ghost dressing function) obtained using domain-wall fermion configurations at physical pion mass, combined with the longitudinal part of the gluon-ghost vacuum polarisation computed via the associated Dyson-Schwinger equation. The gluon propagator is fitted with an analytic form informed by the dynamical gluon mass scale, and the ghost dressing function is obtained both from lattice inversion of the Faddeev-Popov operator and from solving the ghost gap equation. The effective charge is then constructed from these inputs using the pinch technique and background field method formalism.

## Key numbers

- Infrared value of the renormalisation-group-invariant interaction: $\hat d(k^2=0) = 16.6(4)$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Infrared saturation value of the effective charge: $\hat\alpha(0)/\pi = 0.97(4)$
- Dynamical gluon mass scale: $m_0 = 0.428$ GeV (from fit parameters) or $m_0 = 0.43(1)$ GeV (from abstract)
- Canonical gluon mass at zero momentum: $m_g(0;\zeta^2) = 0.445$ GeV at renormalisation scale $\zeta = 3.6$ GeV
- The effective charge saturates at a value $\alpha_0 = 0.97(4)\,\pi$, which is above the threshold $\alpha_0 \gtrsim 0.3\,\pi$ required for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the process-independent effective charge by Binosi et al. (2016) and Rodriguez-Quintero et al. (2018), which used older lattice configurations with heavier pion masses (twisted-mass fermions at $m_\pi \gtrsim 0.3$ GeV) or quenched approximations. The new result uses state-of-the-art domain-wall fermion configurations at the physical pion mass, yielding a refined prediction that is almost identical to the process-dependent charge $\alpha_{g_1}$ defined via the Bjorken sum rule, and also to the process-dependent charge that, when used in one-loop DGLAP evolution, reproduces experimental pion parton distribution functions. This enables a unified description of hadron observables and supports the view that QCD may be a mathematically well-defined quantum field theory in four dimensions.
