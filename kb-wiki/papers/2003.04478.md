# Fundamental Properties of the Proton in Light-Front Zero Modes

> For a proton in the infinite momentum frame, its wave function contains a zero-momentum part (light-front zero-modes) originated from the modification of the QCD vacuum in the presence of the valence quarks, exhibiting a light-front long-range order in the quantum state. This ``non-travelling'' component of the proton contributes to its fundamental properties, including the mass and spin, as well as the naive-time-reversal-odd correlations between transverse momentum and spin. The large momentum effective theory (LaMET) provides a theoretical approach to study the physical properties of the proton's zero-mode ``condensate'' through lattice simulations.

# Fundamental Properties of the Proton in Light-Front Zero Modes

## Key claim
The proton's light-front wave function contains a zero-momentum component (light-front zero-modes) that differs from the QCD vacuum and contributes to fundamental properties including mass, spin, and naive-time-reversal-odd correlations; however, this component is not directly calculable in standard light-front quantization and requires matching through large momentum effective theory (LaMET) to be studied via lattice simulations.

## Method
The paper develops a theoretical framework separating the proton's light-front wave function into a normal component (finite longitudinal momentum partons) and a zero-mode component (k⁺=0 quanta). It derives light-cone sum rules for twist-three and twist-four observables, scalar charge, axial charge, tensor charge, and proton mass, and shows that these sum rules contain δ(x) contributions at x=0 arising from the zero-mode component. The paper then argues that LaMET provides a matching formalism to compute these zero-mode effects from finite-momentum Euclidean lattice calculations.

## Key numbers
- Proton mass decomposition: M = M_q^LF + M_g^LF + M_a^LF, where M_q^LF = (a+b)M/2, M_g^LF = (1-a)M/2, M_a^LF = (1-b)M/2, with a being the quark momentum fraction and b = Σ_q σ_q (m_q/M)
- In the massless limit, quark kinetic motion contributes a/4 of the proton mass, gluon kinetic motion contributes (1-a)/4, and the anomaly contributes 1/2
- The scalar charge sum rule: σ_q = ∫_{-1}^{1} dx e_q(x,μ), where e_q(x,μ) contains a δ(x) term: e_q(x,μ) = δσ_q δ(x) + e_qn(x,μ)

## Context
This work extends the light-front quantization approach of Brodsky (1997) and the light-cone sum rule formalism of Jaffe (1991) by explicitly identifying the zero-mode component of the proton state. It builds on the observation by Aslan (2018) that twist-three distributions like e(x) and g₂(x) contain δ-function contributions at x=0. The paper connects to the LaMET framework of Ji (2013, 2014) as a practical path to compute these zero-mode effects from lattice QCD, and relates the Sivers function mechanism to zero-mode gluons as discussed by Ji (2002) and Belitsky (2002). This enables future lattice calculations of higher-twist proton structure including mass decomposition and single-spin asymmetries.
