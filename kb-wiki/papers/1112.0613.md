# The effect of initial fluctuations on bottomonia suppression in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using the screened Cornell potential and the next-to-leading order perturbative QCD to determine, respectively, the properties of bottomonia and their dissociation cross sections in a quark-gluon plasma, we study in a 2+1 ideal hydrodynamics the effect of initial fluctuations on bottomonia production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that while initial fluctuations hardly affect the yield of the 1S ground state bottomonium, their effect on that of excited bottomonium states is not small. Compared to the case with smooth initial conditions, the survival probability of excited bottomonia is reduced at low transverse momentum and increased at high transverse momentum. The observed suppression of the excited bottomonia relative to the ground state bottomonium by the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) collaborations at an average transverse momentum can, however, be described at present with both smooth and fluctuating initial conditions.

## Key claim

Initial fluctuations in the collision geometry hardly affect the yield of the 1S ground state bottomonium, but their effect on excited bottomonium states is not small; the observed suppression of excited bottomonia relative to the ground state by the CMS collaboration at an average transverse momentum can be described with both smooth and fluctuating initial conditions.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 ideal hydrodynamic model with both smooth and Monte Carlo-generated fluctuating initial conditions to describe the expansion of the quark-gluon plasma in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. Bottomonia properties were determined from the screened Cornell potential, and their dissociation cross sections with thermal partons were calculated up to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. The survival probability of each bottomonium state was computed by integrating its thermal decay width along its trajectory through the evolving medium.

## Key numbers

* Dissociation temperatures for bottomonium states: 681 MeV (1S), 285 MeV (2S), 190 MeV (3S), 257 MeV (1P), and 185 MeV (2P).
* The screening mass used in the Cornell potential is \(\mu(T)=(N_c/3+N_f/6)^{1/2}gT\) with coupling constant \(g=1.87\).
* The initial thermalization time \(\tau_0\) is 1.05 fm/c, and the smearing parameter for fluctuating initial conditions is \(\sigma=0.4\) fm.
* The double ratio of (2S+3S) to 1S yield in Pb+Pb divided by that in p+p is \(0.31\pm 0.03\) from CMS data, which both smooth and fluctuating initial conditions can describe.
* The parameters \(C=24.8\) and \(\alpha=0.15\) in the initial entropy density formula were obtained from fitting charged-particle multiplicity data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Song, 2011) that used a schematic hydrodynamics with uniform temperature, by incorporating a 2+1 ideal hydrodynamic model with both smooth and fluctuating initial conditions. The study focuses on bottomonia rather than charmonia because ground state bottomonium has smaller contributions from excited state decays and regeneration in the QGP (Zhao, 2011; Song, 2011). The results enable more realistic comparisons with CMS experimental data on bottomonia suppression at LHC energies, though the current data cannot distinguish between smooth and fluctuating initial conditions.
