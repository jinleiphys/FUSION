# What do we (not) know theoretically about solar neutrino fluxes?

> Solar model predictions of 8B and p-p neutrinos agree with the experimentally-determined fluxes (including oscillations): phi(pp)_{measured} = (1.02 +- 0.02 +- 0.01)phi(pp)_{theory}, and phi(8B)_{measured} =(0.88 +- .04 +- 0.23)phi(8B)_{theory}, 1 sigma experimental and theoretical uncertainties, respectively. We use improved input data for nuclear fusion reactions, the equation of state, and the chemical composition of the Sun. The solar composition is the dominant uncertainty in calculating the 8B and CNO neutrino fluxes; the cross section for the 3He(4He, gamma)7Be reaction is the largest uncertainty for the calculated 7Be neutrino flux.

## Key claim

Solar model predictions of the p-p, 7Be, and 8B neutrino fluxes agree with experimentally-determined values (including oscillations) within their combined 1-sigma uncertainties, but this agreement is "accidentally better" than the uncertainties would suggest; the authors caution that the preferred model BP04 deliberately excludes the most recent low heavy-element abundance measurements because those abundances conflict with helioseismological measurements of the convective zone depth.

## Method

The authors constructed standard solar models (BP04 and BP04+) using improved input data for nuclear fusion cross sections (including updated S-factors for 7Be(p,gamma)8B, p-p, and hep reactions), a refined equation of state with relativistic corrections, and new surface chemical composition analyses from three-dimensional atmospheric models. They compared the predicted neutrino fluxes for each source (pp, pep, hep, 7Be, 8B, 13N, 15O, 17F) against empirical fluxes derived from a global analysis of solar neutrino experiments and KamLAND reactor data, allowing all fluxes to vary subject only to the solar luminosity constraint.

## Key numbers

- Measured pp neutrino flux: phi(pp)_measured = (1.02 ± 0.02 ± 0.01) × phi(pp)_theory (1-sigma experimental and theoretical uncertainties, respectively)
- Measured 8B neutrino flux: phi(8B)_measured = (0.88 ± 0.04 ± 0.23) × phi(8B)_theory
- Measured 7Be neutrino flux: phi(7Be)_measured = (0.91 +0.24 -0.62 ± 0.11) × phi(7Be)_theory
- BP04 predicted 8B flux: 5.79 × 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1 with 23% fractional uncertainty
- BP04 predicted pp flux: 5.94 × 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1 with 1% fractional uncertainty
- BP04 predicted 7Be flux: 4.86 × 10^9 cm^-2 s^-1 with 12% fractional uncertainty
- BP04+ convective zone base at 0.726 R_sun, conflicting with helioseismological measurement of 0.713 ± 0.001 R_sun
- BP04 surface heavy element to hydrogen ratio Z/X = 0.0229; BP04+ uses Z/X = 0.0176
- 3He(4He,gamma)7Be cross section uncertainty contributes 8% uncertainty to both 7Be and 8B neutrino flux predictions
- Surface composition (Z/X) uncertainty contributes 20% uncertainty to 8B flux and ~35% to CNO neutrino fluxes

## Context

This paper extends the authors' series of solar model calculations spanning over 40 years, updating their previous best model BP00 (Bahcall and Pinsonneault 2001) with improved nuclear reaction data, equation of state, and composition analyses. The work highlights a tension between the new low heavy-element abundances derived from three-dimensional atmospheric models (Asplund et al. 2004) and helioseismological measurements of the convective zone depth, leading the authors to prefer BP04 over BP04+. The results enable more precise tests of stellar evolution theory via solar neutrino experiments and identify the 3He(4He,gamma)7Be reaction rate as the highest priority for nuclear astrophysics measurements, as it has not been remeasured in 15 years.
