# $η^\prime$ meson under partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium

> In-medium modification of the eta' mass is discussed in the context of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium. We emphasize that the U_A(1) anomaly effects causes the eta'-eta mass difference necessarily through the chiral symmetry breaking. As a consequence, the eta' mass is expected to be reduced by order of 100 MeV in nuclear matter where about 30% reduction of chiral symmetry takes place. The strong attraction relating to the eta' mass generation eventually implies that there should be also a strong attractive interaction in the scalar channel of the eta'-N two-body system. We find that the attraction can be strong enough to form a bound state.

## Key claim

The eta' mass reduction in nuclear matter is expected to be of order 100-150 MeV at saturation density, but this prediction depends on the assumption that spontaneous SU(3) chiral symmetry breaking predominates over explicit breaking by the strange quark mass, and that about 30-35% reduction of the chiral condensate occurs at saturation density.

## Method

The authors use an SU(3) linear sigma model as a chiral effective theory to describe the eta' meson and its interactions. Nuclear medium effects are introduced through a linear density approximation, where the in-medium modification of meson masses comes from two contributions: the shift of the vacuum induced by the first term of the mean-field Lagrangian, and particle-hole excitations expressed by the second term. The coupling constant g is determined to produce 35% reduction of the quark condensate at saturation density, and the eta'-N two-body interaction is extracted from the model at tree level, with the sigma meson exchange term providing the dominant attractive contribution.

## Key numbers

- The eta'-eta mass difference reduction is found to be 135 MeV at saturation density in the linear sigma model with 35% reduction of the quark condensate
- The eta'-N interaction from sigma exchange is estimated to be -0.053 MeV^{-1} in the parameter set producing 35% reduction of the quark condensate at saturation density
- The eta' and N form a bound state with 6 MeV binding energy
- The scattering length is of order 2 fm with repulsive sign
- For comparison, the I=0 bar-K N interaction from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term is -0.086 MeV^{-1} at threshold, which dynamically generates the Lambda(1405) with 10-15 MeV binding energy

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Jido (2011) on eta' mass reduction under partial chiral symmetry restoration, and connects to experimental efforts by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Nanova, 2012) which measured the in-medium eta' width to be 15-25 MeV at saturation density. The prediction of a possible eta'-nucleon bound state motivates planned experiments at GSI (Itahashi, 2012) to observe eta'-nucleus bound states via (p,d) reactions on carbon-12 targets, and provides a theoretical basis for interpreting the transparency ratio measurements and the planned bound state searches.
