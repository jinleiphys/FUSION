# The $ππ$ S-Wave in the 1 to 2 GeV Region from a $ππ$, $\bar{K}K$ and $ρρ$($ωω$) Coupled Channel Model

> A simple $ππ$, $\bar{K}K$, and $ρρ$($ωω$) fully coupled channel model is proposed to predict the isoscalar S-wave phase shifts and inelasticities for $ππ$ scattering in the 1.0 to 2.0 GeV region. The S-matrix is required to exhibit poles corresponding to the established isoscalar J$^π$ = 0$^+$ resonances f$_0$(975), f$_0$(1400), and f$_0$(1710). A dominant feature of the experimental $ππ$ inelasticity is the clear opening of the $\bar{K}K$ channel near 1 GeV, and the opening of another channel in the 1.4 - 1.5 GeV region. The success of our model in predicting this observed dramatic energy dependence indicates that the effect of multi-pion channels is adequately described by the $ππ$ coupling to the $\bar{K}K$ channel, the $ρρ$(4$π$) and $ωω$(6$π$) channels.

## Key claim

A simple three-channel separable potential model coupling ππ, \(\bar{K}K\), and ρρ (with ωω treated as an effective ρρ channel) can reproduce the observed isoscalar S-wave ππ phase shifts and inelasticities from 1.0 to 2.0 GeV, provided the S-matrix is constrained to have poles at the complex energies of the f₀(975), f₀(1400), and f₀(1710) resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully coupled three-channel model using separable S-wave potentials with exponential form factors \(g_i(r) = e^{-\beta_i r}/r\) for the ππ, \(\bar{K}K\), and ρρ channels. They solved the coupled Schrodinger equations analytically to obtain the S-matrix element S₁₁, then imposed the condition that S₁₁ have poles at the known complex energies of the three isoscalar J\(^{\pi}\) = 0\(^+\) resonances, providing six constraints on the nine model parameters (three range parameters βᵢ and six couplings λᵢⱼ). The remaining parameter freedom was narrowed by requiring all off-diagonal couplings to be real.

## Key numbers

*   The three resonance pole positions used as constraints were (Re M\(_{\pi\pi}\), Im M\(_{\pi\pi}\)) = (0.975, -0.0165) GeV for f₀(975), (1.400, -0.075) GeV for f₀(1400), and (1.709, -0.350) GeV for f₀(1710).
*   The channel masses used were m₁ = 0.1396 GeV (π), m₂ = 0.4937 GeV (K), and m₃ = 0.7680 GeV (ρ).
*   The ρρ branch cut begins at M\(_{\pi\pi}\) = 2 m\(_{\rho}\) = 1.536 GeV, and the ωω branch cut at M\(_{\pi\pi}\) = 2 m\(_{\omega}\) = 1.564 GeV.
*   The phase shift is approximately 180 degrees at the opening of the \(\bar{K}K\) channel and approximately 400 degrees at the opening of the ρρ channel.

## Context

This work extends earlier two-channel ππ and \(\bar{K}K\) separable potential models by Cannata, Dedonder, and Lesniak (1988, 1989, 1992), which failed to reproduce data above 1.4 GeV, by adding a third effective ρρ(ωω) channel. The model was motivated by the need for ππ scattering amplitudes up to 2 GeV for studying final state interactions in \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi\pi\) annihilation, as measured by Bathas and Kloet (1993). The results enable extrapolation of S-wave ππ phase shifts and inelasticities to 2 GeV, where no experimental data exist, and demonstrate that multi-pion channels (4π, 6π) are effectively described as ρρ and ωω formation rather than through direct multi-pion branch cuts.
