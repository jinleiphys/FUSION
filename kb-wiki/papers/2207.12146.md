# Microscopic nuclear equation of state at finite temperature and stellar stability

> A microscopic nuclear equation of state compatible with all current astrophysical constraints constructed within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism is presented and extended in a consistent way to finite temperature. The effects of finite temperature on the properties of neutron stars are studied in detail and a universal relation regarding stellar stability is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic nuclear equation of state (EOS) within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) formalism that is extended to finite temperature, and claims that the temperature dependence of the three-body forces (TBF) causes a softening of the EOS at high density, leading to a non-monotonic temperature profile and negative thermal pressure at certain densities, which is not captured by the Frozen Correlations Approximation (FCA).

## Method

The authors solve the finite-temperature Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented by compatible three-body forces, going beyond the Frozen Correlations Approximation (FCA) that assumes correlations are unchanged from T=0. They compute the free energy density for symmetric and pure neutron matter, then use a parabolic approximation to obtain the free energy for asymmetric matter, and determine the composition of beta-stable charge-neutral matter by imposing chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations are solved for isentropic temperature profiles at constant specific entropy S/A to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- The free symmetry energy at saturation density is about 32 MeV, with a slope parameter L ≈ 67 MeV.
- The cold BHF V18 EOS yields a maximum gravitational mass M_max ≈ 2.36 solar masses.
- For S/A=2.5, temperatures reach about 50 MeV at high density in the BHF case and up to 70 MeV for the FCA.
- The proton fraction at T=0 exceeds the direct Urca threshold x_DU ≈ 0.13 at a relatively low density of about 0.37 fm^-3.
- The relative change of the maximum gravitational mass for the hottest S/A=2.5 configuration is limited to less than about 5 percent.
- The combined NICER+GW170817 analysis yields R_1.4 = 12.45 ± 0.65 km (Miller21), 11.94^{+0.76}_{-0.87} km (Pang21), and 12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81} km or 12.18^{+0.56}_{-0.79} km (Raaijmakers21).

## Context

This work extends previous BHF calculations using the Frozen Correlations Approximation (Zhou 2004, Li 2008a, Li 2008b, Burgio 2018, Lu 2019, Wei 2020, Figura 2020, Figura 2021, Li 2021, Wei 2021) by fully incorporating temperature effects into the K-matrix equation. It contrasts with the FCA results and with recent finite-temperature BHF calculations using chiral N2LOΔ TBF (Logoteta 2021), which produce a much softer EOS. The proposed universal linear correlation between the scaled relative change of maximum mass and the scaled thermal pressure ratio at the stellar center, when normalized by the squared core temperature, enables predictions for hot neutron star stability across different EOSs and thermal conditions.
