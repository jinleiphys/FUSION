# Pion absorption from the lowest atomic orbital in 2H, 3H and 3He

> The pi- + 2H -> n + n, pi- + 3H -> n + n + n, pi- + 3He -> n + d and pi- + 3He -> p + n + n capture reactions from the lowest atomic orbitals are studied under full inclusion of final state interactions. Our results are obtained with the single-nucleon and two-nucleon transition operators derived at leading order in chiral effective field theory. The initial and final three-nucleon states are calculated with the chiral nucleon-nucleon SMS potential up to N4LO+ augmented by the consistently regularized chiral N2LO three-nucleon potential. We found that absorption rates depend strongly on the nuclear pion absorption operator used, and its two-body parts change the rates by a few orders of magnitude. The final state interactions between nucleons generated by the two-nucleon forces are also important, while the three-nucleon interaction plays a visible role only in the pi- + 3He -> n + d reaction. Our absorption rate for the pi- + 2H -> n + n process is in good agreement with the experimental data from the hadronic ground-state broadening in pionic deuterium. The capture rates on 3He are also generally consistent with the spectroscopic data within error bars, though our central values are found to be systematically below the data. We show that for the three-body breakup processes the dominant contributions to the absorption rates arise from the quasi-free scattering and final-state interaction kinematical configurations.

## Key claim

The authors find that absorption rates for pion capture on light nuclei depend strongly on the nuclear pion absorption operator used, with two-body parts changing the rates by a few orders of magnitude; while the absorption rate for the pi- + 2H -> n + n process agrees well with experimental data, the central values for capture rates on 3He are systematically below the spectroscopic data, though still consistent within error bars.

## Method

The authors employed the Faddeev equations formalism to calculate pion absorption reactions from the lowest atomic orbitals in 2H, 3H, and 3He, using single-nucleon and two-nucleon transition operators derived at leading order in the momentum counting scheme of chiral effective field theory. The initial and final three-nucleon states were computed using the chiral nucleon-nucleon SMS potential up to N4LO+ augmented by the consistently regularized chiral N2LO three-nucleon potential, with full inclusion of final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron momentum magnitude in pi- + 2H -> n + n: p0 = 357.534 MeV
- Neutron momentum magnitude in pi- + 3He -> n + d: q0 = 407.726 MeV
- The two-body parts of the pion absorption operator change the absorption rates by a few orders of magnitude compared to using only the single-nucleon operator
- Relativistic corrections to the neutron momenta are approximately 2 percent larger than the nonrelativistic values
- The full results (single-nucleon plus two-nucleon operator) calculated with the N4LO+ chiral NN wave functions show very good agreement with experimental data for the pi- + 2H -> n + n reaction

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions (Lensky 2005, Baru 2016) and muon capture on 2H, 3He, and 3H (Golak 2014, 2016) as well as pion radiative capture (Skibinski 2018) to the inverse process of stopped pion absorption. The authors provide the first chiral EFT-based predictions for pion absorption rates on 3He and 3H, enabling future comparisons with spectroscopic data from pionic atom experiments (Schwanner 1984, Gotta 1995) and establishing a framework for studying three-nucleon force effects in these reactions.
