# Study of relativistic nuclear collisions at AGS energies from p+Be to Au+Au with hadronic cascade model

> A hadronic cascade model based on resonances and strings is used to study mass dependence of relativistic nuclear collisions from p+Be to Au+Au at AGS energies ($\sim 10\AGeV$) systematically. Hadron transverse momentum and rapidity distributions obtained with both cascade calculations and Glauber type calculations are compared with experimental data to perform detailed discussion about the importance of rescattering among hadrons. We find good agreement with the experimental data without any change of model parameters with the cascade model. It is found that rescattering is of importance both for the explanation of high transverse momentum tail and for the multiplicity of produced particles.

## Key claim

The hadronic cascade model JAM1.0, using only free cross sections and free masses as inputs with no medium modifications, reproduces experimental data for hadron transverse momentum and rapidity distributions across systems from p+Be to Au+Au at AGS energies (approximately 10 A GeV) without any change of model parameters, demonstrating that rescattering among hadrons is essential for explaining both the high transverse momentum tail and the multiplicity of produced particles.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic cascade model (JAM1.0) that treats nuclear collisions as the sum of independent binary hadron-hadron collisions via closest distance approach, with inelastic collisions modeled by resonance production at low center-of-mass energies (below 4 GeV for baryon-baryon collisions) and by string formation and fragmentation via the Lund string model at higher energies. They compared results from the full cascade (including rescattering among hadrons) with Glauber type calculations (without rescattering among produced particles) for p+Be, p+Al, p+Cu, p+Au, Si+Al, Si+Cu, Si+Au, and Au+Au collisions at laboratory incident momenta of 14.6 A GeV/c and 11.6 A GeV/c, using experimental data from the E802 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Incident laboratory momentum for p+A and Si+A collisions: 14.6 A GeV/c
- Incident laboratory momentum for Au+Au collisions: 11.6 A GeV/c
- For central Au+Au collisions, impact parameter distributed from 0 to 3.338 fm
- For Si+Al collisions, impact parameter b ≤ 1.79 fm; for Si+Cu, b ≤ 2.2 fm; for Si+Au, b ≤ 2.9 fm
- Rapidity bin width of 0.2 used for all collision systems in the cascade calculations
- String formation threshold: approximately 4 GeV in baryon-baryon collisions, approximately 3 GeV in meson-baryon collisions, and approximately 2 GeV in meson-meson collisions
- Formation time of roughly 1 fm/c with string tension κ = 1 GeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of relativistic nuclear collisions at AGS energies by applying a unified hadronic cascade model (JAM1.0) across a wide range of system sizes from p+Be to Au+Au, building on the approach of earlier transport models such as RQMD (Sorge et al.), UrQMD (Bass et al.), and ARC (Pang et al.). The comparison between cascade and Glauber type calculations enables a detailed assessment of rescattering effects, and the model's ability to describe data without medium modifications provides a baseline for future studies that might include partonic degrees of freedom, which will become necessary at collider energies such as RHIC and LHC.
