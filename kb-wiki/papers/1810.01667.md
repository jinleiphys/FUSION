# The pole structure of low energy $πN$ scattering amplitudes

> This report presents an investigation of the pion-nucleon elastic scattering in low energy region using a production representation of the partial wave $S$ matrix. The phase shifts are separated into contributions from poles and branch cuts, where the left-hand cut term can be evaluated by tree-level covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. A comparison between the sum of known contributions and the data in $S$- and $P$- wave channels is made. It is found that the known components in $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ channels are far from enough to saturate the corresponding experimental data, indicating the existence of low-lying hidden poles. The positions of those hidden poles are figured out and the physics behind them are explored.

# The pole structure of low energy πN scattering amplitudes

## Key claim
Using the model-independent PKU representation of the partial wave S-matrix, the known contributions from observed resonances and left-hand cuts evaluated at tree-level covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) are insufficient to saturate the experimental phase shift data in the S11 and P11 channels, indicating the existence of low-lying hidden poles whose positions are determined by fitting.

## Method
The PKU representation factorizes the partial wave S-matrix into pole factors (from bound states, virtual states, and resonances on the first and second Riemann sheets) and a background term carrying left-hand cut and right-hand inelastic cut information. The left-hand cut term was evaluated using tree-level covariant BChPT up to O(p²), with a cutoff parameter s_c set to -0.08 GeV², while known resonance poles were taken from experimental results using a narrow width approximation to obtain second-sheet shadow poles. The sum of these known contributions was compared to phase shift data from the SAID database in six S- and P-wave channels, and hidden pole positions were determined by fitting the residual discrepancies.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff parameter for the left-hand cut integral was set to s_c = -0.08 GeV², determined according to the N*(1440) shadow pole position.
- In the P11 channel, a near-threshold virtual state was found at 980 MeV when using s_c = -9.00 GeV².
- In the S11 channel, the hidden pole position varied with cutoff: at s_c = -0.08 GeV² the pole was at 0.808 - 0.055i GeV; at s_c = -1.00 GeV² at 0.822 - 0.139i GeV; at s_c = -9.00 GeV² at 0.883 - 0.195i GeV; and at s_c = ∞ at 0.914 - 0.205i GeV.
- A square-well potential toy model with range L = 0.829 fm and depth V₀ = 144 MeV produced a pole at 0.872 - 0.316i GeV, close to the PKU representation fit results.

## Context
This work extends the PKU representation method previously applied to ππ scattering by Xiao et al. (2000) and Zheng et al. (2003) to the πN system, where similar hidden pole signatures were found in the S11 and P11 channels analogous to the σ (f₀(500)) discovery in ππ scattering. The analysis uses resonance parameters from Anisovich et al. (2011) and BChPT calculations from Alarcon et al. (2011) and Chen et al. (2012). The results enable further investigation of the nature of these hidden states, with follow-up O(p³) BChPT calculations already showing no qualitative changes from the O(p²) results presented here.
