# A Model of Charmonium Absorption by Light Mesons

> We calculate the cross sections for dissociation of $J/ψ$ by pions and $ρ$-mesons within the framework of a meson exchange model. We find that these cross sections are small at center-of-mass energies less than 1 GeV above threshold, and that dissociation rates are less than 0.01 $c$/fm in a thermal meson gas at temperatures where such a description makes sense.

# A Model of Charmonium Absorption by Light Mesons

## Key claim
The dissociation cross sections for $J/\psi$ by pions and $\rho$-mesons are small at center-of-mass energies less than 1 GeV above threshold, and the resulting thermal dissociation rates are less than 0.01 $c$/fm in a meson gas at temperatures where a hadronic description remains physically meaningful. The authors present their result as an upper limit for the hadronic dissociation rate, noting that inclusion of form factors would further reduce the rates.

## Method
The authors construct an effective hadronic Lagrangian based on a strongly broken local U(4) flavor symmetry, treating vector mesons as quasi-gauge bosons, to describe interactions among $\pi$, $\rho$, $J/\psi$, $D$, and $D^*$ mesons. They compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for the charm-exchange reactions $\pi + J/\psi \to D + \bar D^*$ and $\rho + J/\psi \to D + \bar D$, using coupling constants derived from vector meson dominance and from the $D^*$ decay width. The cross sections are then thermally averaged assuming a thermal meson gas to obtain dissociation rates as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers
- The kinematic threshold for $\pi + J/\psi \to D + \bar D^*$ is 640 MeV; for $\rho + J/\psi \to D + \bar D$ the threshold is -135 MeV (exothermic).
- The coupling constants derived are $g_{\rho DD} \approx 5.6$ and $g_{\psi DD} \approx 7.7$, with an estimated error of about 20%.
- The $\pi$-meson coupling $g_{\pi DD^*} \approx 8.8$ is adopted from a QCD sum rule estimate, as only an experimental upper bound $g_{\pi DD^*} < 15$ is known.
- The effective density of pions and $\rho$-mesons with sufficient energy to dissociate $J/\psi$ does not exceed 0.1 fm$^{-3}$ even at $T = 200$ MeV.
- At $T = 300$ MeV, the thermal dissociation rates are so small that a $J/\psi$ has a lifetime around 10 fm/$c$; for $T \le 200$ MeV, dissociation by hadrons is completely negligible on the time scale of a hot hadronic gas state in nuclear collisions.
- A Gaussian form factor with $Q_0 = 1.5$ GeV would reduce the absorption rates by slightly more than one order of magnitude.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing debate over whether $J/\psi$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions can be explained by absorption on comoving hadrons (``comovers'') or requires quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors note that prior predictions for $J/\psi$ absorption cross sections on light mesons differ by orders of magnitude: the Bhanot-Peskin approach (Peskin 1979, Bhanot and Peskin 1979) applied by Kharzeev and Satz (1994) found thermally averaged cross sections below 0.1 mb, while the nonrelativistic quark model calculation by Martins, Blaschke, and Quack found cross sections peaking around 7 mb. The present meson exchange model yields results consistent with the smaller Bhanot-Peskin estimates and with other recent calculations for different absorption channels, such as $\pi + J/\psi \to \pi + \psi'$ and $\rho + J/\psi \to \pi + \eta_c$, collectively indicating that a thermal hadronic environment cannot cause significant $J/\psi$ absorption.
