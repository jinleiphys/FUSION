# Viscosity-entropy ratio of the unitary Fermi gas from zero-temperature elementary excitations

> We investigate the low-temperature behavior of the ratio between the shear viscosity ηand the entropy density s in the unitary Fermi gas by using a model based on the zero-temperature spectra of both bosonic collective modes and fermonic single-particle excitations. Our theoretical curve of η/s as a function of the temperature T is in qualitative agreement with the experimental data of trapped ultracold 6Li atomic gases. We find the minimum value η/s \simeq 0.44 (in units of \hbar/k_B) at the temperature T/T_F \simeq 0.27, with T_F the Fermi temperature.

# Viscosity-entropy ratio of the unitary Fermi gas from zero-temperature elementary excitations

## Key claim

The authors find that the viscosity-entropy ratio η/s of the unitary Fermi gas reaches a minimum value of approximately 0.44 ħ/k_B at T/T_F ≈ 0.27, but this result is obtained from a model that uses zero-temperature elementary excitation spectra and heuristic scaling arguments rather than a first-principles calculation of shear viscosity. The theoretical curve shows only qualitative agreement with experimental data from trapped ultracold ⁶Li atomic gases, and the model does not capture a phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian for the uniform unitary Fermi gas with two spin components, treating bosonic collective excitations (density waves with a dispersion relation including a gradient term parameter λ) and fermionic single-particle excitations (with a BCS-like gap Δ₀ = γ ε_F). They compute the Helmholtz free energy from the partition function of these non-interacting elementary excitations, then derive the shear viscosity using the heuristic formula of How and LeClair (2010) that relates η to the internal energy per particle via kinetic theory, and obtain η/s as a function of scaled temperature T/T_F from the free energy and its derivative.

## Key numbers

- Minimum viscosity-entropy ratio: η/s ≈ 0.44 (in units of ħ/k_B) at T/T_F ≈ 0.27
- Zero-temperature Bertsch parameter: ξ = 0.42 (from Monte Carlo predictions of Astrakharchik et al.)
- Gradient term parameter: λ = 0.25 (best fit to Monte Carlo energy data)
- Zero-temperature gap parameter: γ = 0.45, giving Δ₀ = γ ε_F
- Single-particle excitation parameter: ζ ≈ 0.9 (from Monte Carlo results of Magierski et al.)
- First sound velocity at zero temperature: c₁ = √(ξ/3) v_F, with v_F the Fermi velocity
- Ground-state energy: E₀ = (3/5) ξ N ε_F

## Context

This work extends the heuristic analysis of shear viscosity by How and LeClair (2010) and the thermodynamical model of Salasnich and Toigo (2010) for the unitary Fermi gas. The authors compare their results to experimental data from the Thomas group (2007, 2010) obtained from damping of the radial breathing mode of trapped ⁶Li atomic clouds, to the low-temperature prediction of Rupak and Schäfer (2007), and to the high-temperature prediction of Bruun et al. (2007). The model shows better agreement with experiment than these prior theoretical approaches, particularly up to T/T_F ≈ 0.4, and enables further study of transport properties in strongly interacting Fermi systems near the unitary limit.
