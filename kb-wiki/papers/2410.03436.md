# Light Nuclei Femtoscopy and Baryon Interactions in 3 GeV Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> We report the measurements of proton-deuteron ($p$-$d$) and deuteron-deuteron ($d$-$d$) correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 3 GeV using fixed-target mode with the STAR experiment at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). For the first time, the source size ($R_{G}$), scattering length ($f_{0}$), and effective range ($d_{0}$) are extracted from the measured correlation functions with a simultaneous fit. The spin-averaged $f_0$ for $p$-$d$ and $d$-$d$ interactions are determined to be -5.28 $\pm$ 0.11(stat.) $\pm$ 0.82(syst.) fm and -2.62 $\pm$ 0.02(stat.) $\pm$ 0.24(syst.) fm, respectively. The measured $p$-$d$ interaction is consistent with theoretical calculations and low-energy scattering experiment results, demonstrating the feasibility of extracting interaction parameters using the femtoscopy technique. The reasonable agreement between the experimental data and the calculations from the transport model indicates that deuteron production in these collisions is primarily governed by nucleon coalescence.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports the first simultaneous extraction of source size, scattering length, and effective range from proton-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at 3 GeV, demonstrating that femtoscopy can extract baryon interaction parameters for light nuclei. The measured spin-averaged scattering lengths are consistent with theoretical calculations and low-energy scattering experiments, but the quoted values carry systematic uncertainties that are several times larger than the statistical uncertainties.

## Method

The STAR experiment used fixed-target mode at RHIC to measure proton-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 3 GeV. A simultaneous fit to the measured correlation functions was performed to extract the Gaussian source size (R_G), scattering length (f_0), and effective range (d_0) for both particle pairs. The experimental results were compared with theoretical calculations and predictions from a transport model based on nucleon coalescence.

## Key numbers

- Spin-averaged scattering length for proton-deuteron interactions: f_0 = -5.28 +/- 0.11 (stat.) +/- 0.82 (syst.) fm
- Spin-averaged scattering length for deuteron-deuteron interactions: f_0 = -2.62 +/- 0.02 (stat.) +/- 0.24 (syst.) fm
- Measurements performed in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV using fixed-target mode at RHIC

## Context

This work extends femtoscopy techniques, previously applied to baryon pairs such as proton-proton and proton-Lambda, to light nuclei (proton-deuteron and deuteron-deuteron) for the first time in heavy-ion collisions. The results are consistent with low-energy scattering experiments and theoretical calculations, demonstrating that femtoscopy can serve as a complementary tool to traditional scattering experiments for extracting nuclear interaction parameters. The agreement with transport model calculations supports the nucleon coalescence picture for deuteron production in these collisions, and the technique enables future studies of three-body nuclear forces and hyperon-nucleon interactions using light nuclei.
