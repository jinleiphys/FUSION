# Do charged pions condense in a magnetic field with rotation?

> We revisit the condensation scenario of charged pions in external magnetic field and rotation, which was first considered by Y. Liu and I. Zahed. Based on the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, we find that the charged-pion condensation takes place only when both a strong coupling constant and negatively large baryon chemical potential are applied. Besides, our numerical calculation shows that the chiral restoration induced by the interplay between magnetic field and rotation (i.e., the rotational magnetic inhibition) interrupts the formation of the charged-pion condensate. This suggests that the analysis of such condensation requires a careful treatment of the inner structure of pions, which was not taken into account before. We also discuss the underlying physical mechanism of our finding and the indication of charged-rho condensation.

## Key claim

The authors find that charged-pion condensation in a combined magnetic field and rotation occurs only when both a strong coupling constant and a negatively large baryon chemical potential are applied, contradicting earlier claims that such condensation could occur under more general conditions. The chiral restoration induced by the interplay between magnetic field and rotation (rotational magnetic inhibition) interrupts the formation of the charged-pion condensate, suggesting that the inner structure of pions must be carefully treated.

## Method

The authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau analysis of the charged-pion fields in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, treating pions as composite quark-antiquark states rather than point-like particles. They solve the Dirac equation in a rotating frame with a constant magnetic field along the rotation axis, construct the fermion propagator including the Schwinger phase, and compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation. The chiral condensate is determined from the gap equation, and the stability against charged-pion fluctuations is analyzed through a second-order expansion of the effective potential.

## Context

This work extends and contradicts the earlier analysis by Liu and Zahed (2017), who argued that charged-pion condensation could occur in a combined magnetic field and rotation based on a point-like pion picture. The authors use the NJL model approach previously employed by Chen, Huang, and Mameda (2015) to study rotational magnetic inhibition of the chiral condensate. The results indicate that a proper treatment of the quark substructure of pions is essential, and the findings enable future studies of charged-rho meson condensation and the role of the Meissner effect in rotating magnetized systems.
