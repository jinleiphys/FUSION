# Recent Developments in the Bethe-Salpeter Description of Light Mesons

> Results for the light meson mass spectrum from a Bethe-Salpeter approach are presented. The results obtained in the standard framework are Poincaré covariant and compare favourably with lattice results. Using a more sophisticated scheme, the pseudoscalar, vector and $1^{++}$ ($a_1/f_1$) axialvector charge eigenstate masses are unaltered whereas the $1^{+-}$ ($b_1/h_1$) axialvector meson mass is raised.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter approach in the standard rainbow-ladder truncation yields light meson masses that compare favourably with lattice results, but the axialvector mesons are approximately 300 MeV too light; a more sophisticated scheme that includes a one-loop vertex correction raises the \(1^{+-}\) (\(b_1/h_1\)) axialvector mass without altering the pseudoscalar, vector, or \(1^{++}\) axialvector masses.

## Method

The homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in ladder truncation was solved for quark-antiquark mesons using dressed quark propagators from the rainbow truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equation (DSE), with both kernels constrained by the flavor non-singlet axialvector Ward-Takahashi identity (AXWTI). The effective interaction was a transverse Gaussian form with parameters \(\omega\) and \(D\). To improve axialvector masses, an abelian one-loop correction to the quark-gluon vertex was introduced, with the interaction in the vertex correction reduced to an algebraic expression via a delta-function approximation, and the BSE kernel was constructed to preserve the AXWTI and charge conjugation symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar \(\bar{u}u\) mass: 0.137 GeV (BSE) vs. 0.135 GeV (experiment), using \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV, \(D = 16\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV.
- Vector \(\bar{u}u\) mass: 0.758 GeV (BSE) vs. 0.770 GeV (experiment), same parameters.
- \(1^{+-}\) (\(\bar{u}u\)) axialvector mass: 0.915 GeV (BSE) vs. 1.230 GeV (experiment), same parameters; the vertex correction raises this mass by approximately 300 MeV.
- \(1^{++}\) (\(\bar{u}u\)) axialvector mass: 0.936 GeV (BSE) vs. 1.230 GeV (experiment), same parameters; unaltered by the vertex correction.
- Comparison of vector meson mass \(M_V\) vs. pseudoscalar mass \(M_{PS}\) using BSE with \(\omega = 0.45\) GeV, \(D = 24.4\) GeV\(^{-2}\) shows good agreement with unquenched CP-PACS lattice data over a wide range of \(M_{PS}\).

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder BSE description of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons (Jain 1993, Maris 1997) to axialvector mesons, where the standard truncation fails. The improved vertex correction scheme (Watson 2004) raises the \(1^{+-}\) axialvector mass toward experiment, while leaving other states unchanged. The results enable further studies of more sophisticated truncations and the inclusion of dynamical meson decay and multiquark states (Watson 2004).
