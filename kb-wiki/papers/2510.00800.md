# Next highest weight and other lower $SU(3)$ irreducible representations with proxy-$SU(4)$ symmetry for nuclei with $32 \le \mbox{Z,N} \le 46$

> In the applications of proxy-SU(3) model in the context of determining $(β,γ)$ values for nuclei across the periodic table, for understanding the preponderance of triaxial shapes in nuclei with $Z \ge 30$, it is seen that one needs not only the highest weight (hw) or leading $SU(3)$ irreducible representation (irrep) $(λ_H, μ_H)$ but also the lower $SU(3)$ irreps $(λ,μ)$ such that $2λ+ μ=2λ_H + μ_H-3r$ with $r=0,1$ and $2$ [Bonatsos et al., Symmetry {\bf 16}, 1625 (2024)]. These give the next highest weight (nhw) irrep, next-to-next highest irrep (nnhw) and so on. Recently, it is shown that for nuclei with $32 \le \mbox{Z,N} \le 46$, there will be not only proxy-$SU(3)$ but also proxy-$SU(4)$ symmetry [Kota and Sahu, Physica Scripta {\bf 99}, 065306 (2024)]. Following these developments, presented in this paper are the $SU(3)$ irreps $(λ,μ)$ with $2λ+ μ=2λ_H + μ_H-3r$, $r=0,1,2$ for various isotopes of Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Zr, Mo, Ru and Pd (with $32 \le \mbox{N} \le 46$) assuming good proxy-$SU(4)$ symmetry. A simple method for obtaining the SU(3) irreps is described and applied. The tabulations for proxy-$SU(3)$ irreps provided in this paper will be useful in further investigations of triaxial shapes in these nuclei.

## Key claim

For nuclei in the Ge to Pd isotopic chain (32 ≤ Z,N ≤ 46), the lower SU(3) irreducible representations beyond the highest weight irrep, specifically those with 2λ+μ = 2λ_H+μ_H−3r for r=0,1,2, must be computed under the assumption of proxy-SU(4) symmetry rather than the simpler proton-neutron proxy-SU(3) scheme, because valence protons and neutrons occupy the same proxy oscillator shell (η=3) in this region.

## Method

The author computes the SU(3) irreducible representations (λ,μ) for U(10) → SU(3) reductions (η=3, Ω=10) by first determining the lowest U(4) irrep for given nucleon number m and isospin T, then converting this to a four-column U(10) irrep {f} = {4^a 3^b 2^c 1^d}. The reduction to SU(3) irreps is performed by expanding the four-column U(10) irrep as a 4×4 determinant of antisymmetric irreps {1^X}, multiplying these via the Chew-Sharp rule, and summing the 24 determinant terms. A FORTRAN code implements this procedure, and the results are tabulated for Ge, Se, Kr, Sr, Zr, Mo, Ru, and Pd isotopes with 32 ≤ N ≤ 46.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Ge (m=8, T=0): hw irrep (16,4) with multiplicity 1; nhw irrep (15,3) with multiplicity 1; nnhw irrep (16,1) with multiplicity 1.
- For ⁷²Kr (m=16, T=0): hw irrep (20,8) with multiplicity 1; nhw irrep (22,4) with multiplicity 1; nnhw irrep (24,0) with multiplicity 1.
- For ⁷⁶Sr (m=20, T=0): hw irrep (20,8) with multiplicity 1; nhw irrep (22,4) with multiplicity 1; nnhw irrep (24,0) with multiplicity 1.
- For ⁸²Se (m=26, T=7): hw irrep (12,0) with multiplicity 1; nhw irrep (9,3) with multiplicity 1; nnhw irrep (6,6) with multiplicity 1.
- For ⁸⁴Sr (m=28, T=4): hw irrep (10,10) with multiplicity 1; nhw irrep (11,8) with multiplicity 1; nnhw irrep (12,6) with multiplicity 3.
- The shell quantum number η=3 gives Ω=(η+1)(η+2)/2=10 for the U(Ω) algebra.

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) model of Bonatsos et al. (2017-2024) by incorporating the proxy-SU(4) symmetry recently established by Kota and Sahu (2024) for nuclei in the 32 ≤ Z,N ≤ 46 region. The tabulations of lower SU(3) irreps (nhw, nnhw, etc.) are needed for calculating deformation parameters (β,γ) and understanding triaxial shape preponderance in Ge to Pd isotopes, as previously emphasized by Bonatsos et al. (Symmetry, 2024). The results differ from those of Bonatsos et al. (2024) because the present work uses the full four-column U(Ω) irreps required by SU(4) symmetry rather than the two-column irreps appropriate for identical nucleons, and it reports multiplicities that were omitted in earlier tabulations.
