# Efimov effect in nuclear three-body resonance decays

> We investigate the effects of the nearly fulfilled Efimov conditions on the properties of three-body resonances. Using the hyper-spheric adiabatic expansion method we compute energy distributions of fragments in a three-body decay of a nuclear resonance. As a realistic example we investigate the 1- state in the halo nucleus 11Li within a three-body 9Li+n+n model. Characteristic features appear as sharp peaks in the energy distributions. Their origin, as in the Efimov effect, is in the large two-body s-wave scattering lengths between the pairs of fragments.

# Efimov effect in nuclear three-body resonance decays

## Key claim

The nearly fulfilled Efimov conditions in nuclear three-body systems (where two-body s-wave scattering lengths are large but mass ratios are unfavorable for bound Efimov states) can still produce characteristic sharp peaks in the energy distributions of fragments from three-body resonance decays, serving as fingerprints of the Efimov effect in the continuum rather than in the discrete spectrum.

## Method

The authors used the hyper-spheric adiabatic expansion method, solving the Faddeev equations for the fast angular coordinates at each fixed hyper-radius, then solving the coupled hyper-radial equations with complex scaling to obtain resonance wave-functions with outgoing-wave boundary conditions. From the asymptotic normalization coefficients of the hyper-radial wave-functions, they computed the momentum distributions of the decay fragments using the expression relating the asymptotic wave-function to the probability distribution over the hyper-spheric momentum angles.

## Key numbers

- The n-core and n-n scattering lengths in the realistic $^{11}$Li model are approximately 20 fm, with effective ranges of about 5 fm
- For the model systems, the scattering lengths were increased to approximately 50 fm to amplify the Efimov signatures
- The realistic calculation required up to $\rho_{max}=2.5a_{nn}$ and N=12 adiabatic channels, including about 960 different hyper-spheric partial waves, achieving convergence within about 5% accuracy
- The resonance energy for the realistic $^{11}$Li(1$^-$) state is approximately $0.4-0.1i$ MeV (from the complex scaling angle $\theta_R=0.12$)
- The core energy distribution shows a peak at $E_c/E_c^{\rm(max)}\approx 0.5$ in the s-wave-only model, and an additional peak at $E_c/E_c^{\rm(max)}\approx 1$ when n-n s-wave interaction is included
- The neutron energy distribution shows peaks at $E_n/E_n^{\rm(max)}\approx 0$, $E_n/E_n^{\rm(max)}\approx 1$, and an additional peak at $E_n/E_n^{\rm(max)}\approx 1/2$ when n-n s-wave interaction is included

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) from bound states into the continuum region for nuclear three-body systems, specifically addressing the lack of calculations for resonance decays under Efimov conditions noted by previous authors. The paper builds on the hyper-spheric adiabatic method developed by Nielsen et al. (2001) and the formalism for computing momentum distributions from asymptotic wave-functions by Fedorov et al. (2004). It enables experimental verification of Efimov fingerprints in three-body decays of halo nuclei such as $^{11}$Li, complementing the rapidly increasing number of kinematically complete experiments for three-body decays (Fynbo et al. 2000, 2003; Blazhev et al. 2003) and providing predictions that can be tested against measurements of fragment energy distributions.
