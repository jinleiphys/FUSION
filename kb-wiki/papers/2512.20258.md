# Proton-neutron correlations in baryon-number fluctuations near the liquid-gas transition

> We study net-baryon number density fluctuations in isospin-symmetric matter near the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition using the parity doublet model. We analyze second-order susceptibilities of net-proton and net-neutron numbers and their correlations. We show that proton-neutron correlations are nontrivial and lead to qualitative differences between net-proton and net-baryon fluctuations. We further investigate factorial cumulants and demonstrate that the differences between baryon- and proton-number factorial cumulants are governed by proton-neutron correlations. Our results highlight the importance of interaction-driven correlations for interpreting fluctuation measurements near the liquid-gas critical endpoint.

# Proton-neutron correlations in baryon-number fluctuations near the liquid-gas transition

## Key claim
Proton-neutron correlations are nontrivial near the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition and lead to qualitative differences between net-proton and net-baryon fluctuations, meaning net-proton observables cannot be directly identified with net-baryon fluctuations near the liquid-gas critical endpoint, contrary to the assumption commonly made in experimental analyses.

## Method
The author used the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation to study a two-flavor isospin-symmetric system at low temperatures and small densities, where the system is predominantly composed of positive-parity nucleons. Second-order susceptibilities of net-proton and net-neutron numbers and their correlations were analyzed, along with factorial cumulants, to quantify the difference between baryon- and proton-number fluctuations near the liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers
- At T=30 MeV, the net-baryon susceptibility exhibits a clear maximum across the crossover transition and decreases at larger baryon chemical potential; the proton-neutron correlator vanishes as mu_B approaches 0, reflecting free-gas behavior
- The difference between baryon and proton factorial cumulants is directly given by the proton-neutron correlator: C_2^{pn} = chi_2^{pn}, which is nonzero near the critical region
- For isospin-symmetric matter with complete isospin randomization, n_B = 2n_p = 2n_n, but the scaling relation C_k^B = 2^k C_k^p is broken by nonzero C_k^{pn}

## Context
This work extends studies of baryon number fluctuations near the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition using effective models, building on prior work by Marczenko (2024) on the parity doublet model. The results contradict the common experimental practice of using net-proton fluctuations as a proxy for net-baryon fluctuations, which is valid in an ideal gas framework but breaks down due to interaction-induced correlations near the critical region. The findings are relevant for interpreting fluctuation measurements from low-energy heavy-ion experiments such as HADES at GSI and the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, and are consistent with nonzero off-diagonal susceptibilities observed in lattice QCD studies by Gavai (2005).
