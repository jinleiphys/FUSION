# Structure evolution of ground and excited states in the exotic nucleus $^{22}$Al

> Recent experimental studies on proton-rich nuclei in the $sd$ shell have revealed intriguing near-threshold phenomena, including exotic structures associated with mirror-symmetry breaking. In particular, a halo-like structure has been suggested for the $1^+$ state of $^{22}$Al based on the large isospin asymmetry observed in the $^{22}$Si/$^{22}$O mirror Gamow-Teller transitions. Recent mass measurements further indicate that the ground state of $^{22}$Al is weakly bound, with a single-proton separation energy of about 100 keV. To investigate how the continuum affects the structure and decay properties of this proton-dripline nucleus, we employ the state-of-the-art Gamow shell model. This approach utilizes valence-space effective interactions and operators derived from chiral forces. Our calculations identify the ground state of $^{22}$Al as a $4^+$ state, with a $3^+$ state as the first excitation. Despite their diffuse nature under weak binding, the Thomas-Ehrman shift for these states is found to be negligible due to their small $s$-wave components. In contrast, the excited $1_1^+$ state possesses a significantly larger $s$-wave component, resulting in a more pronounced halo-like structure.

# Structure evolution of ground and excited states in the exotic nucleus $^{22}$Al

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{22}$Al is a $4^+$ state (not a $3^+$ state as previously speculated), and despite its weak binding with a single-proton separation energy of about 100 keV, neither the $4^+$ ground state nor the $3^+$ first excited state exhibits a halo structure due to their small $s$-wave components; only the excited $1_1^+$ state possesses a significantly larger $s$-wave component resulting in a more pronounced halo-like structure.

## Method

The authors employ the Gamow shell model (GSM) using valence-space effective interactions and operators derived from chiral effective field theory (the EM1.8/2.0 interaction), with the Berggren basis generated via the Gamow-Hartree-Fock method to treat bound, resonant, and non-resonant continuum states on equal footing. The valence space is defined with $^{16}$O as the core, and the effective Hamiltonian and operators are constructed within many-body perturbation theory, including third-order two-body matrix elements for all states including non-resonant continuum configurations. For comparison, standard shell-model calculations are performed using the same chiral interaction but in a localized real-space Hartree-Fock basis that excludes explicit continuum effects.

## Key numbers

- The single-proton separation energy of $^{22}$Al is calculated as 0.200 MeV (GSM) and 0.339 MeV (SM), compared to the experimental value of 0.1004(8) MeV from Campbell (2024) or 0.090(10) MeV from Sun (2024).
- The $3^+$ first excited state lies 60 keV (GSM) or 111 keV (SM) above the $4^+$ ground state in $^{22}$Al.
- The $s_{1/2}$ occupations in $^{22}$Al are 0.31 for the $4^+$ state and 0.41 for the $3^+$ state from GSM calculations.
- The two-proton separation energy of $^{22}$Si is calculated as 0.004 MeV (GSM) and 0.089 MeV (SM), compared to the experimental value of 0.229(54) MeV from Xing (2025).
- The $\log ft$ value for the $^{22}$Al $\beta$ decay to the $4^+$ state of $^{22}$Mg is 4.86 (GSM) and 4.89 (SM), compared to the experimental value of 4.93(7).
- The $\log ft$ value for the $^{22}$F $\beta$ decay to the $4^+$ state of $^{22}$Ne is 5.20 (GSM) and 5.37 (SM), compared to the experimental value of 4.802(16).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mirror-symmetry breaking and near-threshold phenomena in proton-rich $sd$-shell nuclei, particularly following the suggestion by Lee (2020) of a halo-like $1^+$ state in $^{22}$Al based on large isospin asymmetry in $^{22}$Si/$^{22}$O Gamow-Teller transitions, and the mass measurements by Campbell (2024) and Sun (2024) showing weak binding. The calculations contradict earlier proposals by Blank (1997) and Czajkowski (1997) that $^{22}$Al might have a $3^+$ ground state with enhanced $s_{1/2}$ occupation, and are consistent with the recent $\beta$-delayed $\alpha$-emission measurement by Jensen (2026) suggesting a $4^+$ ground state dominated by $d$-wave components. The results enable quantitative predictions for the structure of $^{22}$Al and clarify the role of continuum coupling in shaping the low-lying states of this proton-dripline nucleus.
