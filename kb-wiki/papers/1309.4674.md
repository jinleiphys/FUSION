# Quasi-elastic scattering in the 20Ne + 90,92Zr reactions: role of non-collective excitations

> Conventional coupled-channels analyses, that take account of only the collective excitations of the colliding nuclei, have failed to reproduce the different behavior of the experimental quasi-elastic barrier distributions for the $^{20}$Ne + $^{90,92}$Zr systems. To clarify the origins of this difference, we investigate the effect of non-collective excitations of the Zr isotopes. Describing these excitations in a random-matrix model, we explicitly take them into account in our coupled-channels calculations. The non-collective excitations are capable of reproducing the observed smearing of the peak structure in the barrier distribution for $^{20}$Ne + $^{92}$Zr, while not significantly altering the structure observed in the $^{20}$Ne + $^{90}$Zr system. The difference is essentially related to the closed neutron shell in $^{90}$Zr.

# Quasi-elastic scattering in the 20Ne + 90,92Zr reactions: role of non-collective excitations

## Key claim
Non-collective excitations of the Zr isotopes, modeled with a random-matrix approach, can reproduce the observed smearing of the quasi-elastic barrier distribution for 20Ne + 92Zr while leaving the 20Ne + 90Zr barrier distribution largely unchanged, but only when the same random-matrix parameters (w = 200 MeV^3/2, Δ = 7 MeV, σ = 4 fm) are used for both systems and the energy shift from adiabatic renormalization (+0.6 MeV for 92Zr, +0.3 MeV for 90Zr) is applied.

## Method
Coupled-channels calculations were performed including collective rotational states of 20Ne (up to 6+ with β2 = 0.46 and β4 = 0.27, plus octupole at 5.62 MeV with β3 = 0.39) and vibrational states of the Zr isotopes (up to two-phonon states). Non-collective excitations of the Zr targets were described using a random-matrix model, with coupling matrix elements generated from experimental level energies and spins, using a form factor proportional to the derivative of the Woods-Saxon potential. The calculations used a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential with a = 0.65 fm and r0 = 1.15 fm, with depths V0 = 55.0 MeV for 90Zr and 62.3 MeV for 92Zr.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier heights: V_B = 54.0 MeV for 20Ne + 90Zr, V_B = 53.3 MeV for 20Ne + 92Zr
- Number of known states up to 5 MeV: 35 for 90Zr, 87 for 92Zr
- Non-collective states included up to 5.7 MeV: 38 levels in 90Zr, 75 levels in 92Zr
- Energy shifts applied to compensate for adiabatic renormalization: +0.3 MeV for 20Ne + 90Zr, +0.6 MeV for 20Ne + 92Zr
- Random-matrix model parameters: w = 200 MeV^3/2, Δ = 7 MeV, σ = 4 fm
- Surface diffuseness parameter: a = 0.65 fm for both systems
- Deformation parameters for 90Zr: β2 = 0.089 (2+ at 2.18 MeV), β3 = 0.211 (3- at 2.75 MeV)
- Deformation parameters for 92Zr: β2 = 0.103 (2+ at 0.93 MeV), β3 = 0.17 (3- at 2.34 MeV); nuclear coupling β2^(N) = 0.144

## Context
This work addresses a failure of conventional coupled-channels calculations (which include only collective excitations) to reproduce the different quasi-elastic barrier distributions observed by Piasecki et al. for the 20Ne + 90,92Zr systems. The random-matrix model was originally introduced by Weidenmüller et al. in the 1970s for deep-inelastic collisions, and was previously applied by Yusa, Hagino, and Rowley (2013) to the 16O + 208Pb system. The results demonstrate that differences in non-collective level densities between the closed-shell 90Zr (N=50) and the two-neutron-richer 92Zr can explain the experimental barrier distribution differences, and suggest that similar random-matrix treatments could be extended to quantum-mechanical studies of deep-inelastic collisions at higher energies.
