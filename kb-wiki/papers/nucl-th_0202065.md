# Comparison of various models of particle multiplicity distributions using a general form of the grand canonical partition function

> Various phenomenological models of particle multiplicity distributions are discussed using a general form of the grand canonical partition function. These phenomenological models include a wide range of varied processes such as coherent emission or Poisson processes, chaotic emission resulting in a negative binomial distribution, combinations of coherent and chaotic processes called signal/noise distributions, and models based on field emission from Lorentzian line shapes leading to Lorentz/Catalan distributions. These specific cases can be written as special cases of a more general distribution. Using this grand canonical approach moments and cumulants, combinants, hierarchical structure, void scaling relations, KNO scaling features, clan variables and branching laws associated with stochastic or ancestral variables are discussed. It is shown that just looking at the mean and fluctuation of data is not enough to distinguish these distributions or the underlying mechanism. A generalization of the Poisson transform of a distribution and the Poissonian decomposition of it into a compound or sequential process is also given.

## Key claim

The paper shows that many phenomenological models of particle multiplicity distributions (Poisson, negative binomial, signal/noise, and Lorentz/Catalan) can be derived as special cases of a single general grand canonical partition function, but that examining only the mean and fluctuation of data is insufficient to distinguish between these distributions or their underlying physical mechanisms.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized grand canonical partition function based on a weight factor \(W(\vec x, \vec n) = \prod_k x_k^{n_k}/n_k!\) where \(n_k\) is the number of species of type \(k\) (e.g., clusters of size \(k\) or cycles of length \(k\) in a permutation). They then derive expressions for moments, cumulants, combinants, factorial cumulants, clan variables, and void scaling relations within this framework. Specific distributions (Poisson, negative binomial, Lorentz/Catalan) are obtained by particular choices of the weight parameters \(x_k\) and the quantity \(\alpha_k\) that defines the conserved quantity \(A = \sum_k \alpha_k n_k\).

## Key numbers

- For a negative binomial (NB) distribution, the reduced factorial cumulants satisfy \(\kappa_m = (m-1)! \kappa_2^{m-1}\), demonstrating an hierarchical structure.
- For the Lorentz/Catalan (LC) model with evolutionary variables, the void parameter \(\chi = (\sqrt{2\xi\langle A\rangle+1} - 1)/(\xi\langle A\rangle)\), while for the NB distribution \(\chi = \ln(1 + \xi\langle A\rangle)/(\xi\langle A\rangle)\).
- In the LC model with branching probability \(p\), the clan variable \(n_c\) ranges from \(n_c = 1\) at \(p = 0\) and \(p = 1\) to \(n_c = \infty\) at \(p = 1/2\).
- For the LC model, the mean number of clans \(N_c = \beta (1 - |1-2p|)/(2p)\) and the mean total members \(\langle A \rangle = \beta (1-p)/|1-2p|\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical models of nuclear multifragmentation (Lee and Mekjian, 1990s) to multiparticle production in high-energy collisions, including pion distributions studied at CERN SPS and RHIC. The paper unifies several phenomenological approaches: the negative binomial distribution used by Van Hove and Giovannini (1980s) with its clan structure, the Lorentz/Catalan distribution from field emission models (Mekjian, 1986), and Poisson/coherent emission models. The general framework enables systematic comparison of void scaling relations, KNO scaling features, and branching laws across these models, and provides tools for distinguishing mechanisms in future LHC data.
