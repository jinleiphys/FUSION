# Is a soft nuclear equation of state extracted from heavy-ion data incompatible with pulsar data?

> We discuss the recent constraints on the nuclear equation of state from pulsar mass measurements and from subthreshold production of kaons in heavy-ion collisions. While recent pulsar data points towards a hard equation of state, the analysis of the heavy-ion data allows only for soft equations of state. We resolve the apparent contradiction by considering the different density regimes probed. We argue that future measurements of global properties of low-mass pulsars can serve as an excellent cross-check to heavy-ion data.

## Key claim

The apparent contradiction between a soft nuclear equation of state (EoS) required by heavy-ion kaon data and a hard EoS suggested by high pulsar masses is resolved by noting that the two probes access different density regimes: heavy-ion kaon production is sensitive to densities around twice saturation density (2n₀), while pulsar maximum masses are determined by the EoS above 2n₀, where unknown high-density physics (hyperons, kaon condensation, quark matter) dominates.

## Method

The authors used a Skyrme-type parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, fixing parameters by nuclear matter properties (saturation density n₀, binding energy, compression modulus K, and symmetry energy S₀), to compute neutron star maximum masses as a function of compression modulus. They compared these results to constraints from the KaoS collaboration's subthreshold kaon production data in Au+Au and C+C collisions, which were analyzed via transport models (IQMD, RQMD) using soft (K=200 MeV) and hard (K=380 MeV) EoS. They also examined relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, requiring the Schrödinger equivalent potential to match that of the soft Skyrme parameterization, and considered the effect of including hyperons.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of a neutron star can exceed 2 M⊙ for a compression modulus K ≥ 190 MeV, even for "soft" EoS.
- For K=200 MeV, the maximum central density reached is n_c ≈ 5n₀.
- The symmetry energy variation S₀ = 28–32 MeV changes the maximum mass by only ΔM = ±0.05 M⊙ at low compression moduli.
- The pulsar J0751+1807 has mass M = (2.1 ± 0.2) M⊙ (1σ) and M = (1.6–2.5) M⊙ (2σ) (Nice et al., 2005).
- The pulsar J1756–2251 has mass (1.18 ± 0.02) M⊙ (Faulkner et al., 2005).
- The X-ray burster EXO 0748–676 gives M ≥ (2.10 ± 0.28) M⊙ and R ≥ (13.8 ± 1.8) km (Ozel, 2006).
- The isolated neutron star RXJ 1856 has a conservative lower limit for the radiation radius of R∞ = 16.5 km (d/117 pc) (Trumper et al., 2003).
- Including hyperons in RMF models reduces maximum masses to about 1.75 M⊙ and below; for parameter sets compatible with KaoS data, masses below 1.6 M⊙ are found, though masses above 1.44 M⊙ are possible.
- A 1.2 M⊙ neutron star probes the EoS up to n ≈ 2n₀.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent conflict between pulsar mass measurements (Nice et al., 2005; Ozel, 2006) pointing to a hard EoS and subthreshold kaon production data from the KaoS collaboration (Sturm et al., 2000; Senger, 2007) favoring a soft EoS, as analyzed in transport models by Fuchs et al. (2000) and Hartnack et al. (2005). The authors show that the two probes access different density regimes, enabling future cross-checks via radius measurements of low-mass pulsars like J1756–2251 (Faulkner et al., 2005). The work extends earlier combined analyses by Klahn et al. (2006) and highlights the role of hyperons (Schaffner-Bielich, 2002) and quark matter (Alford et al., 2006) at high densities.
