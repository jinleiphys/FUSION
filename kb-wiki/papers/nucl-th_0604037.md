# New developments in nuclear supersymmetry

> In this contribution, I discuss the role of symmetries and algebraic methods in nuclear structure physics. In particular, I review some recent developments in nuclear supersymmetry and indicate possible applications for light nuclei in the sd- and pf-shell.

## Key claim

Nuclear supersymmetry (SUSY) provides parameter-independent predictions for one- and two-nucleon transfer reaction intensities between members of a supermultiplet, but these predictions are only valid when the even-even nucleus can be described by one of the dynamical symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model and the odd nucleon occupies specific single-particle orbits close to the Fermi surface.

## Method

The paper reviews the algebraic framework of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and its extension to odd-mass nuclei via the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM), which are unified into a supersymmetry characterized by the total number of bosons and fermions. For the Pt-Au region, the author presents a simultaneous fit of excitation energies for the supersymmetric quartet of nuclei \(^{194,195}\)Pt and \(^{195,196}\)Au using a single energy formula with six fitted coefficients, and derives analytic expressions for one-proton and two-nucleon transfer reaction strengths. For light nuclei in the \(sd\)- and \(pf\)-shells, the paper proposes a \(U(36/24)\) supersymmetry that combines the orbital group \(U_L(6)\) with the (pseudo)spin-isospin group \(SU_{ST}(4)\).

## Key numbers

* The supersymmetric quartet in the Pt-Au region is characterized by \(\aleph_{\pi}=2\) and \(\aleph_{\nu}=5\) (total boson plus fermion numbers for protons and neutrons, respectively).
* For two-nucleon transfer reactions in the \(U_{\nu}(6/12)\otimes U_{\pi}(6/4)\) supersymmetry, the predicted ratios of spectroscopic strengths for different final states are \(R_{2,LJ} = (N+4)/(15N) = 0.12\) and \(R_{3,LJ} = 2(N+4)(N+6)/[15N(N+3)] = 0.33\) for \(N=5\).
* The one-proton transfer operator \(P_{2}^{\dagger}\) yields a parameter-independent ratio \(R_2 = 9(N+1)(N+5)/[4(N+6)^2]\) for the intensity of transfer to an excited state relative to the ground state.
* The one-proton transfer reaction intensities between Pt and Au nuclei are related by \(I(^{195}\text{Pt} \rightarrow ^{196}\text{Au}) = (2L+1)/4 \cdot I(^{194}\text{Pt} \rightarrow ^{195}\text{Au})\).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical supersymmetry framework previously applied to the \(A \sim 190\) mass region, where the even-even nucleus \(^{196}\)Pt is the standard example of the \(SO(6)\) limit of the IBM (Castaños et al., 1979) and the odd-odd nucleus \(^{196}\)Au was shown experimentally to match the supersymmetry prediction (Metz et al., 1999). The paper proposes new applications for light nuclei in the \(sd\)-shell (Szpikowski, 1980) and the beginning of the \(pf\)-shell, where pseudo-\(SU(4)\) symmetry has been identified in \(^{58}\)Cu, \(^{60}\)Zn and \(^{60}\)Ni (Bijker et al., 2003). The results enable experimental tests of supersymmetry predictions using one-proton transfer reactions on \(^{194}\)Pt and \(^{195}\)Pt targets, and open the possibility to generalize nuclear SUSY to transitional regions of the nuclear mass table beyond the limited cases of exact dynamical symmetries.
