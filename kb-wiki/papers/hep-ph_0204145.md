# Enhanced thermal production of hard dileptons by $3\to 2$ processes

> In the framework of the Hard Thermal Loop effective theory, we calculate the two-loop contributions to hard lepton pair production in a quark-gluon plasma. We show that the result is free of any infrared and collinear singularity. We also recover the known fact that perturbation theory leads to integrable singularities at the location of the threshold for $q\bar{q}\toγ^*$. It appears that the process calculated here significantly enhances the rate of low mass hard dileptons.

## Key claim

The authors calculate two-loop contributions to hard dilepton production in a quark-gluon plasma using Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) effective theory, and find that these processes significantly enhance the rate of low-mass hard dileptons, but only when the dilepton invariant mass squared satisfies \(Q^2 > 4 M_\infty^2\) (where \(M_\infty\) is the thermal quark mass), which allows the tree-level \(q\bar{q}\to\gamma^*\) process to contribute at order zero in the strong coupling.

## Method

The authors compute the imaginary part of the retarded photon polarization tensor \(\mathrm{Im}\,\Pi_R{}^\mu_\mu(Q)\) at two-loop order in the HTL resummed theory, using the retarded/advanced (R/A) formalism. They evaluate the vertex correction and self-energy insertion diagrams (figure 1 in the paper), keeping only terms enhanced by the Bose-Einstein factor for soft gluons (approximated as \(n_B(l_0)\approx T/l_0\)), and perform the quark momentum integration in the complex plane using the residue theorem, focusing on the pinching pole contributions that give collinear enhancement. The integration over the gluon momentum uses the HTL gluon spectral functions for transverse and longitudinal modes.

## Key numbers

- The thermal quark mass squared is \(M_\infty^2 \equiv g^2 C_F T^2/4\), where \(C_F \equiv (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\) for gauge group \(SU(N_c)\).
- The gluon thermal mass squared is \(m_g^2 \equiv g^2 T^2 [N_c + N_F/2]/9\) for \(SU(N_c)\) with \(N_F\) flavors.
- The effective mass that regulates collinear singularities is \(M_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 \equiv M_\infty^2 + (Q^2/q_0^2) p_0 r_0\), which can vanish or become negative when \(Q^2 > 4 M_\infty^2\).
- The threshold for the tree-level \(q\bar{q}\to\gamma^*\) process is at \(Q^2 = 4 M_\infty^2\), above which the cuts (a) and (c) in figure 1 contribute.

## Context

This work extends the off-shell annihilation mechanism for real photon production (Aurenche et al., 2000) to the case of virtual photons (dileptons), and complements earlier calculations of \(q\bar{q}\to g\gamma^*\) and \(qg\to q\gamma^*\) processes (Altherr and Ruuskanen, 1992; Thoma and Traxler, 1997). The results enable phenomenological studies of thermal dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, where the off-shell annihilation process may dominate over one-loop processes (which only grow logarithmically with photon energy). The paper also discusses the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect from resummation of ladder diagrams (Arnold et al., 2001-2002), which reduces the photon rate by about 25% but is not included in this two-loop calculation.
