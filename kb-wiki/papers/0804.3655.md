# Reexamination for the calculation of elliptic flow and other fourier harmonics

> We have argued that the azimuthal symmetry and asymmetry components in fourier expansion of particle momentum azimuthal distribution, $v_n$ (n=0, 1, 2, ...), should be calculated as an average of $cos(nϕ)$ first over particles in an event and then over events (event-wise average) rather than "an average over all particles in all events" (particle-wise average). In case of large centrality (multiplicity) bin the particle-wise average is not accurate because the influence (fluctuation) of particle multiplicity was not taken into account.

# Reexamination for the calculation of elliptic flow and other fourier harmonics

## Key claim

The Fourier harmonic coefficients $v_n$ should be calculated as an event-wise average (average of $\cos(n\phi)$ first over particles in an event, then over events) rather than the conventional particle-wise average (average over all particles in all events). The particle-wise average is inaccurate for large centrality (multiplicity) bins because it does not account for the influence of event multiplicity fluctuations.

## Method

The authors rederive the Fourier expansion of the particle momentum azimuthal distribution starting from the particle number (multiplicity) distribution in momentum space. They then apply the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE to calculate charged hadron $v_2(\eta)$ in 0-40% most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV using both event-wise and particle-wise averaging methods for comparison.

## Key numbers

- In 0-40% most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the $v_2^e(\eta)$ calculated by event-wise average is about 10% larger than $v_2^p(\eta)$ calculated by particle-wise average.
- The reaction plane angle $\Psi_r$ is set to zero in the theoretical derivation, with the particle azimuthal angle $\phi$ measured with respect to the reaction plane.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering formalism of Poskanzer and Voloshin (1998) and Zhang (1998) for elliptic flow and Fourier harmonics in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The standard definition of $v_n = \langle \cos[n(\phi-\Psi_r)]\rangle$ as an average over all particles in all events (particle-wise average) is shown to be problematic for wide centrality bins. The paper argues that experimental extraction methods (event plane method, cumulant method, Lee-Yang zeroes method) already use event-wise averaging, and this theoretical reexamination brings the calculation into consistency with experimental practice. The work enables more accurate comparisons between hydrodynamic and transport model predictions and experimental data, particularly for minimum bias collisions or broad centrality bins where multiplicity fluctuations are significant.
