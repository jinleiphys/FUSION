# Structure of Bound States with Coulomb plus Short-range Interaction

> We study the structure of bound states appearing in systems governed by the Coulomb and short-range interactions. We analyze the binding energies and wave functions of the bound states generated by the Coulomb plus short-range potential. We demonstrate that Coulomb-induced shifts of the binding energy are closely correlated with the spatial distribution of the wave function. Furthermore, we show that the asymptotic behavior of wave functions of weakly bound states is qualitatively altered by Coulomb repulsion, leading to a modification of the near-threshold mass scaling that is otherwise universal for short-range interactions.

# Structure of Bound States with Coulomb plus Short-range Interaction

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Coulomb repulsion qualitatively alters the asymptotic behavior of wave functions for weakly bound states, leading to a modification of the near-threshold mass scaling that is otherwise universal for short-range interactions. The authors show that the Coulomb-induced shift in binding energy is governed by a competition between enhancement of density near the origin due to nodal wave functions and the spread of the wave function caused by small binding energies.

## Method
The authors solve the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation for s-wave two-body systems using a potential model consisting of an attractive square-well short-range interaction (strength V0, range b = 1.5 fm) combined with a Coulomb potential (αZ1Z2/r). The reduced mass is fixed to 1863 MeV, modeling the αα system analogous to 8Be, and the binding energies and radial wave functions are computed for various combinations of short-range potential depth and Coulomb charge product.

## Key numbers
- For V0 = -25 MeV with Z1Z2 = 0: ground state binding energy B = 5.3 MeV
- For V0 = -25 MeV with Z1Z2 = +4 (repulsive Coulomb): ground state binding energy B = 0.45 MeV
- For V0 = -25 MeV with Z1Z2 = -4 (attractive Coulomb): ground state binding energy B = 11 MeV
- For V0 = -125 MeV, excited state: B0 = 9.55 MeV (Z1Z2 = 0), B = 2.06 MeV (Z1Z2 = +4, ΔB = -7.5 MeV), B = 17.1 MeV (Z1Z2 = -4, ΔB = 7.6 MeV)
- For V0 = -125 MeV, ground state: B0 = 93.3 MeV (Z1Z2 = 0), B = 85.72 MeV (Z1Z2 = +4, ΔB = -7.6 MeV), B = 101 MeV (Z1Z2 = -4, ΔB = 7.7 MeV)
- For V0 = -250 MeV, excited state: B0 = 112.5 MeV (Z1Z2 = 0), B = 102.1 MeV (Z1Z2 = +4, ΔB = -10.0 MeV), B = 121.7 MeV (Z1Z2 = -4, ΔB = 9.2 MeV)
- For V0 = -250 MeV, ground state: B0 = 214.8 MeV (Z1Z2 = 0), B = 206.7 MeV (Z1Z2 = +4, ΔB = -8.1 MeV), B = 222.9 MeV (Z1Z2 = -4, ΔB = 8.1 MeV)
- Pure Coulomb (Z1Z2 = -4, V0 = 0): ground state binding energy = 0.794 MeV
- Excited state for V0 = -125 MeV with Z1Z2 = 0: root-mean-square radius = 1.5 fm (all other states have ~1.0 fm)
- For V0 = -20 MeV with Z1Z2 = +4: bound state disappears (transition to resonance)

## Context
This work extends studies of Coulomb plus short-range interactions by Domcke, Kong (1998), Higa (2008), Mochizuki (2024), and Kinugawa (2025), and applies insights to near-threshold exotic hadrons such as X(3872) (binding energy ~40 keV) and Tcc (~360 keV) discovered by LHCb (2021). The results connect to lattice QCD findings by Lyu (2021) showing that Coulomb repulsion dissolves the Ωccc++Ωccc++ bound state into a resonance, and to the known behavior of 8Be where Coulomb repulsion makes the αα system unbound (Braaten 2004, Higa 2008). The paper enables understanding of how Coulomb effects modify low-energy universality (Braaten 2004, Naidon 2016) and compositeness (Hyodo 2014, Kinugawa 2024) for near-threshold states, with implications for distinguishing hadronic molecules from other exotic hadron configurations.
