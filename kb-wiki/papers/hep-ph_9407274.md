## Key claim

This review argues that color coherent phenomena in QCD, including color transparency, color opacity, and color fluctuations, can be understood through analogies with QED coherence effects, but the authors emphasize that the non-Abelian nature of QCD and the interplay between perturbative and non-perturbative effects mean that complete elimination of interactions requires both decreasing the size of the configuration and increasing the energy, with soft non-perturbative physics playing an important role at fixed Q² and high energy.

## Method

The paper is a comprehensive review that synthesizes theoretical developments in QCD coherence phenomena, using the two-gluon exchange model and its extensions to derive color screening effects, the scattering eigenstates formalism (P(σ) probability distribution) to describe color fluctuations in hadrons, and the coherence length formalism (l_c = 2p_lab/(M²-m²)) to determine when configurations can be treated as frozen during nuclear passage. The authors compare QED analogs (charge transparency, filtering, opacity) with QCD predictions for deep inelastic scattering at small x, hard diffractive processes, nuclear quasielastic reactions, and heavy ion collisions, referencing experimental data from BNL, FNAL, CERN, and HERA.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of single diffraction to elastic cross section at t=0 for pp scattering is 0.2-0.3 (Eq. e:frozen6)
- The probability for a nucleon (pion) to be in a configuration with small cross section is P_N(σ ≤ 5 mb) ≈ 2% for the pion
- The coherence length in deep inelastic scattering is l_c = 1/(2 M_N x) ≈ (0.1/x) fm, where M_N is the nucleon mass
- For positronium traversing a foil, the survival probability for the ground state follows P(positronium → positronium) = 16/(σ_tot ρ L)² for high energies where l_c > L (Eq. ptop)
- The ratio of inelastic to elastic diffraction for positronium is P(positronium → e⁺e⁻)/P(positronium → positronium) = (σ_tot ρ L)/8 (Eq. ratiopos)
- The expansion time for a point-like configuration is τ ≤ (E/1 GeV) fm, giving about 5 fm for E ≈ 5 GeV
- Small configurations can be produced for momentum transfers as low as about 1-2 (GeV/c)²
- Diffractive events at HERA account for at least 5% of events with Q² ≥ 10 GeV²

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on color transparency by Mueller (1982) and Brodsky (1982), and the two-gluon exchange model of Low (1975) and Gunion and Soper (1977), by providing a unified framework connecting QED coherence phenomena (Chudakov 1955, Perkins 1955) to QCD color coherent effects. The paper synthesizes developments in hard diffractive processes (Frankfurt, Miller, Strikman 1993; Brodsky 1994), color fluctuations in hadrons (Gribov 1965, 1969), and nuclear quasielastic reactions (Frankfurt, Strikman 1988; Jennings, Miller 1990), enabling future experimental tests at CEBAF, BNL, FNAL, and HERA to probe the transition between non-perturbative and perturbative QCD, as well as predictions for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
