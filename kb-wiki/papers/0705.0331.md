# Center-of-Mass Correction in a Relativistic Hartree Approximation Including the Meson Degrees of Freedom

> The Peierls-Yoccoz projection method is used to study the motion of a relativistic system of nucleons interacting with sigma and omega mesons. The nuclear system is described in a mean-field Hartree approach, including explicitly the meson contribution and the formalism is applied to some selected N=Z spherical nuclei. The center-of-mass motion correction makes the system too much bounded, but we show that an appropriate refitting of the model brings the radii and binding energies close to the experimental values.

## Key claim

The Peierls-Yoccoz projection method, when applied to a relativistic Hartree approximation that explicitly includes meson degrees of freedom, makes the system too strongly bound; however, a refitting of the model parameters (coupling constants) can bring the binding energies and charge radii close to experimental values for \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca, though not for \(^{4}\)He.

## Method

The authors start from a Walecka-type \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model Lagrangian and solve the mean-field Hartree approximation by expanding nucleon Dirac spinors and meson fields in three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis functions. They then apply the Peierls-Yoccoz projection operator to project the product mean-field state (nucleon Slater determinant plus coherent meson states for \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) fields) onto a zero-momentum state, computing the projected energy and charge root-mean-square radius via norm and energy kernels that include both nucleonic and mesonic contributions.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{16}\)O, the total binding energy without CM correction is 127.6 MeV; with the full CM projection including mesons it becomes 175.2 MeV (too bound), and after refitting parameters it becomes 127.8 MeV (experimental: 127.6 MeV).
* For \(^{40}\)Ca, the total binding energy without CM correction is 341.7 MeV; with the full CM projection including mesons it becomes 462.1 MeV, and after refitting it becomes 341.8 MeV (experimental: 342.1 MeV).
* For \(^{16}\)O, the charge RMS radius without CM correction is 2.73 fm; with the full CM projection including mesons it becomes 2.65 fm, and after refitting it becomes 2.73 fm (experimental: 2.73 fm).
* For \(^{40}\)Ca, the charge RMS radius without CM correction is 3.48 fm; with the full CM projection including mesons it becomes 3.39 fm, and after refitting it becomes 3.48 fm (experimental: 3.48 fm).
* For \(^{4}\)He, the total binding energy without CM correction is 27.9 MeV; with the full CM projection including mesons it becomes 46.2 MeV, and after refitting it becomes 33.0 MeV (experimental: 28.3 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the Peierls-Yoccoz projection method previously applied to the alpha particle (Alberto, 2005) to heavier \(N=Z\) spherical nuclei (\(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca), and generalizes earlier CM corrections that considered only nucleonic degrees of freedom (BJP, 2005) or used the harmonic approximation or the simple \(\langle \hat{P}_A^2 \rangle / (2AM)\) subtraction (Ring, 1996). The results show that including meson degrees of freedom in the CM correction makes the system too bound with standard parameters, necessitating a refit; this enables future calculations of electromagnetic form factors and spectroscopic factors within a translationally invariant framework.
