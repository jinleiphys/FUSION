# Deformation Projected RMF Calculation for Cr and Fe nuclei in Hybrid Derivative Coupling Model

> The ground state properties of even mass Cr and Fe isotopes are studied using the generalized hybrid derivative coupling model. The energy surface of each isotope is plotted as a function of the mass quadrupole moment. The neutron numbers N=20 and N=40 are seen to remain magic numbers but N= 28 and 50 are predicted to be non-magic. The neutron number N=70 turns out to be a magic number according to the present calculation. In all the isotopes studied the calculated binding energy values are less than those obtained from experiment while the deformation is in better agreement.

## Key claim

The generalized hybrid derivative coupling model predicts that neutron numbers N=28 and N=50 are not magic numbers in Cr and Fe isotopes, while N=70 emerges as a new magic number, contrary to the conventional magic numbers N=28 and N=50 that are expected from standard shell structure.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the generalized hybrid derivative coupling model with a Yukawa-to-derivative coupling strength ratio α/(1-α) where α=0.25. They assumed axial symmetry, employed BCS formalism with a constant gap approach (gap parameters taken from odd-even mass differences or calculated as Δ=11.2/√N(Z)), and used 12 oscillator shells for both fermions and bosons. The energy surface for each even-mass Cr isotope (A=44-92) and Fe isotope (A=48-96) was plotted as a function of the mass quadrupole moment to identify ground state deformations.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵²Cr, the calculated quadrupole deformation β=0.345 is closer to the experimental value 0.224 than the spherical prediction from conventional RMF calculations (Lalazissis et al., 1998).
- The spin-orbit splitting between neutron 1f₇/₂ and 1f₅/₂ levels in the present model is 4 MeV, compared to approximately 8 MeV in conventional RMF calculations with the NL3 parameter set.
- For ⁸²Cr, increasing the number of oscillator shells from 12 to 14 changes the total binding energy by only 0.3 MeV with no change in the shape of the energy surface.
- The calculated binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁴Cr is 7.92 MeV (theoretical) versus 7.95 MeV (experimental); for ⁵⁶Fe it is 8.55 MeV (theoretical) versus 8.79 MeV (experimental).
- The neutron number N=70 nucleus ⁹⁶Fe is spherical with β=0.000 in its ground state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generalized hybrid derivative coupling model study of Ne, Mg, Si, and S isotopes (Mitra, Gangopadhyay, and Malakar, 2002) to heavier Cr and Fe nuclei. The model addresses the problem that conventional RMF models using NL3 or NL-SH parameter sets often fail to accurately predict deformation in lighter nuclei, and that the Zimanyi-Moszkowsky derivative coupling model predicts high effective nucleon mass and consequently low spin-orbit splitting. The results enable further investigation of shape coexistence and triaxiality in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, and challenge the traditional magic numbers N=28 and N=50 in this mass region.
