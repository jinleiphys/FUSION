# Isotropic and anisotropic neutron star structure in 4D Einstein Gauss Bonnet Gravity

> With regard to the coupling constant and the strong magnetic field of neutron stars, we have studied these stars in the 4D Einstein Gauss Bonnet (4D EGB) gravity model in order to grasp a better understanding of these objects. In this paper, we have shown that the neutron star properties are considerably affected by the coupling constant and magnetic field. We have found that as a consequence of the strong magnetic field and the coupling constant, the maximum mass and radius of a neutron star are increasing functions of the coupling constant, while Schwarzschild radius, compactness, surface gravitational redshift, and Kretschmann scalar are decreasing functions. Additionally, our study has shown that the physical properties of a magnetized neutron star are greatly influenced not only by the strong magnetic field, but also by the anisotropy. Moreover, we have shown that to obtain the hydrostatic equilibrium configuration of the magnetized material, both the local anisotropy effect and the anisotropy due to the magnetic field should be considered. Finally, we have found that in the anisotropic magnetized neutron stars, the maximum mass and radius do not always increase with increasing the internal magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in 4D Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity, the maximum mass and radius of a neutron star are increasing functions of the coupling constant, while the Schwarzschild radius, compactness, surface gravitational redshift, and Kretschmann scalar are decreasing functions; however, for anisotropic magnetized neutron stars, the maximum mass and radius do not always increase with increasing internal magnetic field.

## Method

The authors used the lowest-order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the AV18 two-body interaction potential to calculate the equation of state for pure neutron matter, both without a magnetic field and with a Gaussian magnetic field profile parameterized by central field strength B0 and decay parameters. They then solved the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for 4D EGB gravity, integrating from the star center to the surface where pressure vanishes, for various values of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant alpha. For magnetized stars, they distinguished radial and tangential pressure components and considered both fast-decay and slow-decay magnetic field profiles.

## Key numbers

* For isotropic non-magnetized neutron stars in 4D EGB gravity with alpha = 0 (Einstein gravity), maximum mass = 1.68 solar masses, radius = 8.73 km.
* For alpha = +3.0 km^2, maximum mass = 1.86 solar masses, radius = 9.18 km; for alpha = -3.0 km^2, maximum mass = 1.46 solar masses, radius = 8.18 km.
* For alpha = +2.0 km^2, maximum mass = 1.81 solar masses, radius = 9.04 km; Schwarzschild radius = 5.84 km, compactness = 0.646, surface gravitational redshift = 0.680, Kretschmann scalar = 2.74 x 10^(-8) m^(-2).
* For alpha = +1.0 km^2, maximum mass = 1.75 solar masses, radius = 8.89 km; for alpha = -1.0 km^2, maximum mass = 1.61 solar masses, radius = 8.56 km.
* To match the observed mass of PSR J0740+6620 (2.10 solar masses, radius 12 +/- 2 km), the model requires alpha = 7.7 km^2, giving radius 9.76 km.
* To match PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses), the model requires alpha = 5.9 km^2, giving radius 9.55 km.
* To match PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 solar masses), the model requires alpha = 5 km^2, giving radius 9.44 km.
* To match Vela X-1 (1.80 solar masses), the model requires alpha = 2 km^2, giving radius 9.04 km.
* To match 4U 1608-52 (1.74 solar masses), the model requires alpha = 1 km^2, giving radius 8.89 km.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutron star structure using the LOCV method with the AV18 potential (Bordbar 1998) and their earlier investigations of neutron stars in Einstein gravity, rainbow gravity, and spin-2 massive graviton theories (Bordbar 2011-2019). It applies the recently proposed 4D EGB gravity theory of Glavan and Lin (2020) to neutron star modeling, building on prior work by Doneva (2020) and Tangphati (2021) on compact stars in modified gravity. The results enable constraints on the Gauss-Bonnet coupling constant by comparing predicted mass-radius relations with observations of pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J1614-2230, and they show that the longstanding open question of whether magnetic fields increase or decrease maximum mass (Bandyopadhyay 1997, Casali 2014) depends on anisotropy effects.
