# Quark-Gluon Plasma as a Quantum Channel: Entanglement, Decoherence, and Hadronization

> We propose a quantum information framework to model the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as a composite quantum channel acting on a multi-qubit or multi-qutrit color-entangled system. The QGP's effects are represented by amplitude damping (jet quenching), $SU(3)$ depolarizing noise (decoherence), and a thermal hadronization channel projecting onto color-singlet states. This construction captures energy loss, decoherence, and confinement dynamics in a unified open quantum system framework. We analyze the evolution of entanglement entropy and purity under this composite channel. Amplitude damping reduces entropy by driving subsystems toward pure states, while decoherence increases mixedness. Hadronization further modifies correlations via thermal projections weighted by hadron masses and freeze-out temperature ($T \sim 156 \,\text{MeV}$). Numerical simulations show monotonic entropy and purity loss, consistent with entanglement degradation and confinement. Our results support interpreting the QGP as a noisy quantum channel that progressively erases color entanglement. This framework bridges quantum information theory and QCD, offering new insights into hadronization and non-perturbative dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.

# Quark-Gluon Plasma as a Quantum Channel: Entanglement, Decoherence, and Hadronization

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can be modeled as a composite quantum channel acting on a multi-qubit or multi-qutrit color-entangled system, where the combined effects of amplitude damping (jet quenching), SU(3) depolarizing noise (decoherence), and a thermal hadronization channel produce monotonic entropy and purity loss consistent with entanglement degradation and confinement. The framework is presented as a theoretical model rather than a direct experimental test, with the hadronization channel using a smooth crossover behavior rather than a sharp phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct a composite quantum channel acting on a multi-qubit or multi-qutrit system, where a generalized color-singlet entangled state represents a quark-antiquark pair with additional partonic degrees of freedom. Three sequential channels are applied: an amplitude damping channel parametrized by gamma_AD to model jet quenching energy loss, an SU(3) depolarizing channel parametrized by gamma_dep to model decoherence from medium interactions, and a stochastic hadronization channel with Kraus operators weighted by thermal Boltzmann factors at freeze-out temperature T ~ 156 MeV to project onto color-singlet states. The evolution of entanglement entropy S(rho_A) and purity of the reduced density matrix is analyzed through numerical simulations across varying damping strengths, time, and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T ~ 156 MeV, used as the scale for thermal Boltzmann weights in the hadronization channel
- Maximum entanglement entropy S = log2(3) ~ 1.585 for a pure Bell-like two-qutrit state at gamma_SD = 0 (amplitude damping strength zero)
- Entanglement entropy decreases from S ~ 1.585 at gamma_SD = 0 to near zero at gamma_SD = 1 for the two-qutrit color-singlet state under amplitude damping
- Pion mass m_pi ~ 140 MeV used as the energy scale for hadron states in thermal probability calculations
- Temperature range for hadron yield crossover between pion and kaon production occurs around T ~ 150-200 MeV

## Context

This work extends quantum information approaches to QCD by modeling the QGP as an open quantum system environment, building on prior statistical hadronization models (Andronic 2018, Braun-Munzinger 2003, Letessier and Rafelski 2002) and jet quenching theory (Wang and Gyulassy 1991, Baier 1995, Wiedemann 2009). The framework bridges quantum information theory and non-perturbative QCD by replacing simplistic phase damping models with a stochastic hadronization channel that enforces SU(3) color neutrality, enabling future studies of how confinement reshapes quantum correlations in strongly coupled gauge theories. The results support interpreting the QGP as a noisy quantum channel that progressively erases color entanglement, offering a new theoretical lens for understanding hadronization dynamics in heavy-ion collisions.
