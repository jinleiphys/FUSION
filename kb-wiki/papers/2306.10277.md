# Core-corona procedure and microcanonical hadronization to understand strangeness enhancement in proton-proton and heavy ion collisions in the EPOS4 framework

> The multiplicity dependence of multistrange hadron yields in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions at several TeV allows one to study the transition from very big to very small systems, in particular, concerning collective effects. I investigate this, employing a core-corona approach based on new microcanonical hadronization procedures in the EPOS4 framework, as well as new methods allowing one to transform energy-momentum flow through freeze-out surfaces into invariant-mass elements. I try to disentangle effects due to ``canonical suppression'' and ``core-corona separation'', which will both lead to a reduction of the yields at low multiplicity.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed strangeness enhancement in both proton-proton and lead-lead collisions can be explained within the EPOS4 framework by a combination of core-corona separation and microcanonical hadronization, but the author explicitly states that the results are only a "very small fraction of simulation results" and that "no attempt has been made to fit one particular curve for one particular system."

## Method

The author employs the EPOS4 framework, which uses a parallel scattering approach based on S-matrix theory (Gribov-Regge approach) with Pomerons representing elementary parton-parton scatterings, incorporating DGLAP parton evolutions and saturation scales. After primary scatterings produce prehadrons, a core-corona procedure identifies which prehadrons form bulk matter (core) treated via viscous hydrodynamics with eta/s=0.08, while corona prehadrons become hadrons directly. The core matter hadronizes via microcanonical hadronization on a hyper-surface defined by constant energy density, replacing the earlier Cooper-Frye procedure.

## Key numbers

- The initial proper time tau_0 is 0.4 fm/c for proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV in EPOS4.0.0
- The freeze-out energy density epsilon_H is 0.57 GeV/fm^3
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s is 0.08
- Core fraction (ratio of dn/deta_s of core contribution over all) in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV: 0.97 for 0-10% centrality, 0.95 for 30-40%, 0.85 for 70-80%, and 0.77 for 80-100%
- Energy density of about 2 GeV/fm^3 for 70-80% centrality PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV
- Jet cone radii used: 0.3 for proton-proton and 0.2 for PbPb collisions
- The smoothing kernel parameters d_bot are given as 0.3+0.3Z for PbPb and as 0.3-0.02*min(N_Pom-1,12) for pp, where Z is centrality based on number of participants

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4 project (Werner 2023) which was developed to describe relativistic collisions from proton-proton to nucleus-nucleus at energies from several GeV per nucleon up to several TeV. The core-corona procedure was introduced in earlier work (Werner 2007) and updated (Werner 2013), and the importance of the core-corona picture is also emphasized by Becattini (2008), Aichelin (2010), and Yuuka (2021). The microcanonical hadronization replaces the earlier Cooper-Frye procedure (Werner 2011) which was problematic for small systems, particularly regarding energy and flavor conservation and issues with heavy baryons at large baryon chemical potential.
