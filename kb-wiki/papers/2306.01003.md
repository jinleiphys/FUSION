# Hadron transverse momentum distributions in the Tsallis statistics with escort probabilities

> The exact and approximate hadron transverse momentum distributions for the Fermi-Dirac, Bose-Einstein and Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics of particles in the framework of the Tsallis statistics with escort probabilities (the Tsallis-3 statistics) have been derived. The classical and quantum transverse momentum distributions in the zeroth term approximation and the quantum transverse momentum distributions in the factorization approximation introduced in the zeroth term approximation were found. The transverse momentum distributions in the zeroth term approximation and in the factorization approximation of the zeroth term approximation are the same in the Tsallis-3, Tsallis-2 and $q$-dual statistics. The well-known classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution exactly coincides with the classical transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-3 statistics in the zeroth term approximation for which the entropy of system is zero in the whole range of state variables. However, the quantum phenomenological Tsallis distribution does not coincide with either the exact or approximate transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis-3 statistics. The exact Tsallis-3 classical distribution and the classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution were applied to describe the experimental spectra of the charged pions produced in the proton-proton collisions at high energies. The values of the parameters $(T,q)$ for both these model distributions differ in the whole energy range. Thus, the classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution is an unsatisfactory approximation for the exact classical transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-3 statistics.

## Key claim

The classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution, widely used in high-energy physics to describe hadron spectra, exactly coincides with the classical transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-3 statistics only in the zeroth term approximation, for which the entropy of the system is zero in the whole range of state variables; however, the quantum phenomenological Tsallis distribution does not coincide with either the exact or approximate transverse momentum distributions of the Tsallis-3 statistics, and the classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution is an unsatisfactory approximation for the exact classical transverse momentum distribution of the Tsallis-3 statistics.

## Method

The author derived exact and approximate hadron transverse momentum distributions for Fermi-Dirac, Bose-Einstein, and Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics within the Tsallis-3 statistics (Tsallis statistics with escort probabilities) using the grand canonical ensemble formalism with two normalization functions. The classical and quantum transverse momentum distributions were obtained in the zeroth term approximation, and quantum distributions were also derived in the factorization approximation of the zeroth term approximation, then compared to the phenomenological Tsallis distributions and applied to experimental charged pion spectra from proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

- The exact Tsallis-3 classical distribution and the classical phenomenological Tsallis distribution were applied to describe experimental spectra of charged pions produced in proton-proton collisions at high energies; the values of the parameters (T, q) for both model distributions differ in the whole energy range.
- The transverse momentum distribution formula for the Tsallis-3 statistics includes the factor gV/(2π)² p_T m_T cosh y, where m_T = √(p_T² + m²) is the transverse mass, g is the spin degeneracy factor, and V is the volume.
- The entropic parameter q takes values in the range 0 < q < ∞, and in the Boltzmann-Gibbs limit q → 1, the Tsallis-3 statistics reduces to the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivations of exact transverse momentum distributions in the Tsallis-1 statistics (Parvan 2017, 2020a) and the q-dual statistics (Parvan 2020b) to the Tsallis-3 statistics, which is frequently considered by the scientific community to be the most correct version of Tsallis statistics (Kapusta). The paper demonstrates that the widely used Tsallis-like distributions (Alberico 2009, Wong 2015, Shen 2018, PHENIX, CMS) and phenomenological Tsallis distributions (Cleymans 2012, Bediaga 2000, Beck 2000) do not correspond to the exact distributions of the Tsallis-3 statistics, enabling future work to use the correct exact distributions for describing experimental hadron spectra from relativistic heavy-ion collisions and proton-proton reactions at LHC and RHIC energies.
