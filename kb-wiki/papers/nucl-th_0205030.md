# Nucleus-nucleus collisions at high baryon densities

> We study central collision of Pb+Pb at 20, 40, 80 and 160 AGeV within the UrQMD transport approach and compare rapidity distributions of pi-, K+, K- and Lambda with the recent measurements from the NA49 Collaboration at 40, 80 and 160 AGeV. It is found that the UrQMD model reasonably describes the data, however, systematically overpredicts the pi- yield by about 20%, whereas the K+ yield is underestimated by about 15%. The K- yields are in a good agreement with the experimental data, the Lambda yields are also in a reasonable correspondence with the data for all energies. We find that hadronic flavour exchange reactions largely distort the information about the initial strangeness production mechanism at all energies considered.

# Nucleus-nucleus collisions at high baryon densities

## Key claim
The UrQMD transport model, using hadronic and string degrees of freedom, reasonably describes NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at 40, 80, and 160 AGeV, but systematically overpredicts pion yields by about 20% and underpredicts K+ yields by about 15%, while the authors caution that these discrepancies may be due to uncertainties in hadronic cross sections or string fragmentation rather than necessarily indicating partonic degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors performed UrQMD (version 1.3) transport calculations for central Pb+Pb collisions at 20, 40, 80, and 160 AGeV, comparing the resulting rapidity distributions of pi-, K+, K-, and Lambda (including Sigma0) with experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration at 40, 80, and 160 AGeV. They also carried out a channel decomposition analysis to trace the final kaon and antikaon yields back to their production mechanisms, including string decays, resonance decays, and secondary meson-baryon interactions.

## Key numbers
- pi- yield overpredicted by ~20% at 80 AGeV and ~25% at 40 AGeV for central Pb+Pb collisions
- K+ yield underestimated by ~15% at 160 AGeV, ~17% at 80 AGeV, and ~15% at 40 AGeV
- K- yield underestimated by ~6% at 160 AGeV, with good agreement at 40 and 80 AGeV
- The experimental K+/pi+ ratio is underestimated by ~30% from UrQMD calculations at 80 AGeV
- About 15-20% of final K+ and ~20% of final K- stem from meson-baryon string decays at SPS energies
- Approximately 2% of final K+ and ~5% of K- appear from phi(1020) meson decays

## Context
This work extends earlier transport studies using RQMD (Sorge et al.) and HSD (Ehehalt et al.) approaches that failed to reproduce the non-monotonic K+/pi+ ratio observed by NA49, which had been interpreted as a possible signature of quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors find that hadronic flavor exchange reactions during the expansion phase largely distort information about the initial strangeness production mechanism, which may explain why statistical models (Braun-Munzinger et al.) appear to work well despite being based on non-interacting hadronic degrees of freedom. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming data at 20 AGeV from the SPS and the proposed GSI facility, while leaving open the question of whether the systematic discrepancies indicate partonic degrees of freedom or simply incomplete knowledge of hadronic cross sections.
