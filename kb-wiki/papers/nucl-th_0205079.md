# Dipole excited states in $^{11}$Li with complex scaling

> The 1$^-$ excitations of the three--body halo nucleus $^{11}$Li are investigated. We use adiabatic hyperspherical expansion and solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space. The method of complex scaling is used to compute the resonance states. The Pauli forbidden states occupied by core neutrons are excluded by constructing corresponding complex scaled phase equivalent two-body potentials. We use a recently derived neutron--core interaction consistent with known structure and reaction properties of $^{10}$Li and $^{11}$Li. The computed dipole excited states with $J^π=1/2^+$, $J^π=3/2^+$, and $J^π=5/2^+$ have energies ranging from 0.6 MeV to 1.0 MeV and widths between 0.15 MeV and 0.65 MeV. We investigate the dependence of the complex energies of these states on the $^{10}$Li spectrum. The finite spin 3/2 of the core and the resulting core-neutron spin-spin interaction are important. The connection with Coulomb dissociation experiments is discussed and a need for better measurements is pointed out.

## Key claim

The computed dipole excited states (1/2+, 3/2+, and 5/2+) of the three-body halo nucleus 11Li have energies ranging from 0.6 MeV to 1.0 MeV and widths between 0.15 MeV and 0.65 MeV, but these results depend on the assumed spectrum of the unbound two-body subsystem 10Li, specifically on which of the two possible virtual s-states (1- or 2-) is placed at low energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method, combined with complex scaling to compute resonance states. They constructed complex-scaled phase-equivalent two-body potentials to exclude Pauli forbidden states occupied by core neutrons in 9Li. The neutron-core interaction was taken from a previous study (Garrido 2002) that reproduced known properties of both 10Li and 11Li, including a 2+ p-resonance at 0.54 MeV and a virtual s-state at 50 keV in 10Li, with the spin-spin interaction between the neutron and the 9Li core (spin 3/2) explicitly included.

## Key numbers

- The three dipole excited states (1/2+, 3/2+, 5/2+) have energies between 0.6 MeV and 1.0 MeV and widths between 0.15 MeV and 0.65 MeV.
- The 10Li spectrum used includes a 2+ p-resonance at 0.54 MeV and a 1+ p-resonance at 0.25 MeV (consistent with the 0.24 +/- 0.06 MeV level observed by Bohlen (1997)).
- One of the two virtual s-states in 10Li is placed at about 50 keV, while the second virtual state is at 0.80 +/- 0.30 MeV.
- The 11Li ground state binding energy is about 0.300 keV (likely a typo for 0.300 MeV in the text), with a p-wave content of about 40%.

## Context

This work extends previous three-body calculations of 11Li ground-state properties (Garrido 2001, Fedorov 1995) into the continuum spectrum, which had been computed only for the 0+ excitations (3/2- states) using complex scaling by Aoyama (2002). The results are relevant to interpreting Coulomb dissociation experiments on 11Li (Sackett 1993, Shimoura 1995, Zinser 1997) and proton-induced reactions (Korsheninnikov 1996, 1997), which show a prominent peak at about 1 MeV in the B(E1) distribution but with significant experimental discrepancies. The authors point out that better measurements are needed to resolve the spectrum.
