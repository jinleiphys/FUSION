# A phenomenological negatively charged pion-proton scattering length from pionic hydrogen

> We derive a closed, model independent, expression for the electromagnetic correction factor to a phenomenological hadronic scattering length extracted from a hydrogenic atom. It is obtained in a non-relativistic approach and in the limit of a short ranged hadronic interaction to terms of order ((alpha)**2)(log(alpha)) using an extended charge distribution. A hadronic negatively charged pion-proton scattering length of 0.0870(5), in units of inverse charged pion-mass, is deduced, leading to a pion-nucleon coupling constant from the GMO relation equals to 14.04(17).

## Key claim

The authors derive a closed, model-independent expression for the electromagnetic correction factor to a phenomenological hadronic scattering length extracted from pionic hydrogen, obtaining the correction to terms of order α² log α using an extended charge distribution. The resulting hadronic π⁻p scattering length is 0.0870(5) m_π⁻¹, which is 1.5% smaller than the value deduced in the prior Sigg analysis (Schröder 2001), and the authors note they have not corrected for electromagnetic terms outside their Coulomb potential approach.

## Method

The authors solve the π⁻p atomic system exactly to all orders in α in the limit of a short-ranged hadronic interaction with the charge concentrated on a spherical shell, then generalize perturbatively to an arbitrary extended charge distribution using the observed π⁻ and proton form factors. They match the logarithmic derivative of the wave function at the shell radius R to obtain the energy shift, include vacuum polarization as a separate correction, and extend the single-channel formalism to the coupled π⁻p, π⁰n, and γn channels using Coulomb-corrected K-matrices. The hadronic scattering length is extracted from the experimental 1s strong interaction shift and width measured by Schröder (2001).

## Key numbers

- Strong interaction shift ε₁s = [-7.108 ± 0.013(stat) ± 0.034(syst)] eV
- Total decay width Γ₁s = [0.868 ± 0.040(stat) ± 0.038(syst)] eV
- Hadronic π⁻p scattering length a^h_π⁻p = 0.0870(5) m_π⁻¹
- Hadronic charge-exchange scattering length a^h_π⁻p→π⁰n = -0.125(4) m_π⁻¹
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant from GMO relation g_c²/(4π) = 14.04(17)
- Electromagnetic expectation values: ⟨r⟩_em = 0.95(1) fm; ⟨1/r⟩_em = 1.48(1) fm⁻¹; V_C(0) = 2.13(2) MeV
- Total correction δ₁s = -0.62(29)% for the energy shift
- Total correction δ_Γ = 1.02(23)% for the width
- Vacuum polarization contribution δ^vp = 0.48%
- Panofsky ratio P = 1.546(9)

## Context

This work extends the analytical Coulomb-correction approaches of Deser (1954), Trueman (1961), and others by explicitly including the effect of the extended charge distribution, which previous analytical treatments did not explore. The result differs from the potential-based analysis of Sigg (1996), which gave δ₁s = (-2.1 ± 0.5)% and a scattering length 1.5% larger, and from the effective field theory calculations of Lyubovitskij (2000) and Gasser (2002), which gave larger corrections of (-4.3 ± 2.8)% and (-7.2 ± 2.9)% respectively. The extracted hadronic scattering length enables improved determination of the isovector scattering length when combined with π⁻D scattering data following the procedure of Ericson (2002).
