# Using world charged pion--nucleus scattering data to constrain an intranuclear cascade model

> The NEUT intranuclear cascade model is described and fit to a large body of \pipm--nucleus scattering data. Methods are developed to deal with deficiencies in the available historical data, and robust uncertainty estimates are produced. The results are compared to a variety of simulation packages, and the data itself. This work provides a method for tuning Final State Interaction models, which are of particular interest to neutrino experiments that operate in the few-GeV energy region, and provides results which can be used directly by the T2K and Super-Kamiokande collaborations, for whom NEUT is the primary simulation package.

## Key claim
The NEUT intranuclear cascade model's final-state interaction (FSI) parameters were tuned to a large body of historical charged pion-nucleus scattering data, but the resulting uncertainty bands from the fit alone did not cover 68% of the data at 1σ; an ad-hoc error inflation procedure (scaling uncertainties by a factor of 57) was required to achieve proper coverage, making the final uncertainties conservative.

## Method
The NEUT semi-classical intranuclear cascade model was fit to world data on π±–nucleus scattering cross sections for carbon, oxygen, aluminium, iron, copper, and lead targets over a pion momentum range of 60–2000 MeV/c. The fit minimized a χ² statistic that included normalization penalty terms (40% prior uncertainty) for each dataset except the DUET collaboration data, which contributed a full covariance matrix. Two interpolation methods (TMultiDimFit polynomial and GNU-Octave spline) were used to smooth the pre-computed grid of NEUT cross section predictions as functions of six FSI rescaling parameters; the GNU-Octave spline results were adopted as final. Error inflation scaled the post-fit uncertainties by a factor ξ = 57 so that the distribution of residuals had an RMS of 1, enforcing 68% coverage at 1σ.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit FSI parameters (with error scaling, GNU-Octave method): f_QE = 1.07 ± 0.31, f_ABS = 1.40 ± 0.43, f_CX = 0.70 ± 0.30, f_INEL = 1.00 ± 1.10, f_QEH = 1.82 ± 0.86.
- Minimum χ²/n.d.o.f for fixed-normalization fit: 149.03/59 (GNU-Octave) and 150.74/59 (TMultiDimFit).
- Error inflation scaling factor ξ = 57.0, determined by requiring the RMS of the Ψ distribution to equal 1.
- For fits with floating normalization parameters on all nuclei: f_ABS = 1.26 ± 0.05, f_CX = 0.80 ± 0.04, χ²/n.d.o.f = 53.48/59.
- The DUET collaboration data provided ABS and CX cross sections on ¹²C at pion momenta 206–295 MeV/c, with a full covariance matrix.

## Context
This work tunes the NEUT cascade model, the primary simulation package for T2K and Super-Kamiokande, to the world dataset of charged pion–nucleus scattering, updating earlier tuning efforts by Long (2015) and de Perio (2015). The fit uses historical data from the 1950s–1990s (Allardyce, Saunders, Gelderloos, Ashery, and others) plus the modern DUET measurement. The results provide directly usable FSI parameter uncertainties and correlations for neutrino oscillation analyses at T2K and Super-Kamiokande, and the methodology (including error inflation following Pumplin (2000) and the NIWG paper) is applicable to other cascade models such as GEANT4, FLUKA, GENIE, and NuWro.
