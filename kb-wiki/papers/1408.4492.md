# From hypernuclei to the Inner Core of Neutron Stars: A Quantum Monte Carlo Study

> Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations have been employed to revise the interaction between $Λ$-hyperons and nucleons in hypernuclei. The scheme used to describe the interaction, inspired by the phenomenological Argonne-Urbana forces, is the $ΛN+ΛNN$ potential firstly introduced by Bodmer, Usmani et al.. Within this framework, we performed calculations on light and medium mass hypernuclei in order to assess the extent of the repulsive contribution of the three-body part. By tuning this contribution in order to reproduce the $Λ$ separation energy in $^5_Λ$He and $^{17}_{~Λ}$O, experimental findings are reproduced over a wide range of masses. Calculations have then been extended to $Λ$-neutron matter in order to derive an analogous of the symmetry energy to be used in determining the equation of state of matter in the typical conditions found in the inner core of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a repulsive three-body ΛNN interaction, tuned to reproduce the Λ separation energy in just two hypernuclei (⁵_ΛHe and ¹⁷_ΛO), can accurately reproduce experimental binding energies across a wide range of hypernuclei and provides the necessary repulsion to prevent excessive softening of the neutron star equation of state at high densities.

## Method

The authors employed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations to solve the non-relativistic many-body Hamiltonian for hypernuclei and Λ-neutron matter. The hyperon-nucleon interaction used a phenomenological ΛN+ΛNN potential inspired by the Argonne-Urbana forces, with the two-body ΛN parameters fixed from scattering data and the three-body ΛNN parameters (W_D and C_P) refitted to reproduce the experimental Λ separation energies in ⁵_ΛHe and ¹⁷_ΛO. The nucleon-nucleon interaction was described by the Argonne V4' potential, with the assumption that systematic errors largely cancel in the Λ separation energy difference.

## Key numbers

- Λ separation energy in ⁵_ΛHe: B_Λ = 3.12(2) MeV (experimental value from Juric (1973)), used as a fitting target
- Refitted three-body ΛNN parameters: W_D = 0.035 MeV (dispersive term coefficient) and C_P = 1.00 MeV (P-wave term coefficient)
- Simulations performed for closed-shell nucleus/hypernucleus pairs up to ⁹¹_ΛZr
- Pion reduced mass inverse: 1/μ_π ≈ 1.4 fm
- The S-wave three-body coefficient C_S was fixed to 1.5 MeV (from Usmani (2008))

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological ΛN+ΛNN potential framework originally introduced by Bodmer and Usmani (1988, 1995, 2006, 2008) by refitting the three-body parameters using AFDMC calculations. The results contradict earlier calculations (Dapo (2010), Schulze (2011), Vidana (2011), Massot (2012)) that predicted excessive softening of the neutron star equation of state upon hyperon appearance, which would be incompatible with the observed 2 M_⊙ neutron stars (Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013)). The derived S_Λn coefficient (analog of symmetry energy for Λ-neutron matter) enables future determination of the equation of state and mass-radius relation for neutron stars with hyperonic cores.
