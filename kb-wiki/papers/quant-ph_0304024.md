# Quantum mechanics and low energy nucleon dynamics

> We discuss the problem of consistency of quantum mechanics as applied to low energy nucleon dynamics with the symmetries of QCD. It is shown that the dynamics consistent with these symmetries is not governed by the Schrodinger equation. We present a new way to formulate the effective theory of nuclear forces as an inevitable consequence of the basic principles of quantum mechanics and the symmetries of strong interactions. We show that being formulated in this way the effective theory of nuclear forces can be put on the same firm theoretical grounds as the quantum mechanics of atomic phenomena. In this case the effective theory allows one to describe with a given accuracy not only two-nucleon scattering, but also the evolution of nucleon systems, and places the constraints on the off-shell behavior of the two-nucleon interaction. In this way we predict the off-shell behavior of the S wave two-nucleon T-matrix at very low energies when the pionless theory is applicable. Further extensions and applications of this approach are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy nucleon dynamics consistent with QCD symmetries is not governed by the Schrödinger equation but by a generalized dynamical equation with a nonlocal-in-time interaction operator; the authors claim that the pionless effective theory of nuclear forces can be formulated as an inevitable consequence of the basic principles of quantum mechanics and QCD symmetries, without requiring regularization or renormalization, provided the principles are entered via this generalized equation.

## Method

The authors apply the formalism of generalized quantum dynamics (GQD), in which the evolution operator is constructed from contributions of processes with finite interaction duration, governed by an integro-differential equation derived from the superposition principle and unitarity. They analyze the time-ordered diagrams of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for the \(^1S_0\) two-nucleon channel in the pionless regime, requiring the resulting \(T\)-matrix to satisfy the GQD equation of motion. This yields a closed-form expression for the \(T\)-matrix whose off-shell behavior is determined by the symmetries, and they show that expanding this amplitude reproduces the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) expansion of effective field theory without regularization.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order \(T\)-matrix for the \(^1S_0\) channel is \(\langle{\bf p}_2|T^{(0)}(z)|{\bf p}_1\rangle = \left(C_0^{-1} - \frac{m\sqrt{-zm}}{4\pi}\right)^{-1}\), where \(m\) is the nucleon mass and \(C_0\) is a parameter.
* The generalized interaction operator for this leading-order case is \(\langle{\bf p}_2| H_{int}^{(s)}(\tau)|{\bf p}_1\rangle = 4\pi i m^{-3/2}\left(\tau^{-1/2}+i\gamma\right)\) with \(\gamma=4\pi\exp(-i\pi/4)m^{-3/2}C_0^{-1}\).
* The full \(T\)-matrix including all orders is \(\langle{\bf p}_2| T(z)|{\bf p}_1\rangle = \frac{\psi^*(p_2/\Lambda)\psi(p_1/\Lambda)}{C_0^{-1}-\frac{m}{4\pi}\sqrt{-zm}-M(z)}\), where \(M(z)\) is defined via integrals over \(k\) with cutoff scale \(\Lambda\).
* The couplings to scattering data are \(C_0 = \frac{4\pi a}{m}\), \(C_2 = \frac{2\pi}{m} a^2 r_0\), and \(C_4 = \frac{4\pi a^3}{m}\left(\frac{r_0^2}{4} + \frac{r_1}{2a}\right)\), where \(a\) is the scattering length and \(r_0, r_1\) are effective range parameters.

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics formalism developed by Gainutdinov (1999) and applies it to the low-energy nucleon-nucleon problem, directly addressing the ultraviolet divergence issues encountered in the Weinberg (1990) approach to chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces. The authors show that the GQD framework reproduces all results of the standard pionless EFT (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998; van Kolck 1999) for scattering amplitudes while providing a consistent, divergence-free formulation that also predicts the off-shell behavior of the two-nucleon \(T\)-matrix. This off-shell prediction is argued to be crucial for resolving the \(A_y\) puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering and for constraining three-nucleon forces, which previous realistic potentials (Machleidt; Huber) and chiral potentials (Entem) could not adequately address.
