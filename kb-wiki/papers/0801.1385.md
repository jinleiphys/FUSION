# Microscopic calculation and LDA of the spatial dependence of the pairing field with bare and induced interactions

> The bare nucleon-nucleon interaction is essential for the production of pair correlations in nuclei, but an important contribution also arises from the induced interaction resulting from the exchange of collective vibrations between nucleons moving in time reversal states close to the Fermi energy. The pairing field resulting from the summed interaction is strongly peaked at the nuclear surface. It is possible to reproduce the detailed spatial dependence of this field using a Local Density Approximation (LDA) and a contact interaction, with parameters which are quite different from those commonly used in more phenomenological approaches.

## Key claim

The pairing field resulting from the sum of the bare Argonne $v_{14}$ interaction and the induced interaction from collective vibrations is strongly peaked at the nuclear surface, and this spatial dependence can be reproduced by a local approximation or a contact interaction, but with parameters "quite different from those commonly used in more phenomenological approaches", specifically, the authors do not claim to achieve a precise estimate of the pairing gap or condensation energy, focusing instead on the spatial dependence of the pairing field.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations using the SLy4 interaction for neutrons in $^{120}$Sn, then solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in the pairing channel using three different interactions: the bare Argonne $v_{14}$ interaction, the induced interaction from exchange of QRPA collective vibrations (multipolarities $J=0$ to $5$, energies up to 30 MeV), and their sum renormalized by a quasiparticle strength factor $Z=0.7$. They constructed the spatial pairing field $\Delta(R_{c.m.}, r_{12})$ and its Fourier transform $\Delta(R_{c.m.}, k)$, then developed a local approximation $\Delta_{loc}(R_{c.m.})$ using the Thomas-Fermi prescription with the local Fermi momentum, extended into the classically forbidden region using the zero-momentum component.

## Key numbers

- The average pairing gap at the Fermi energy for the Argonne plus induced interaction $v_{Arg+ind}$ is $\Delta_F = 1.47$ MeV, "very close to the value derived from the experimental binding energies through the usual three-point formula."
- For the Argonne interaction alone, $\Delta_F = 1.04$ MeV; for the induced interaction alone, $\Delta_F = 1.11$ MeV.
- The coherence length in uniform matter is estimated as $\xi \sim 19$ fm inside the nucleus (with $m^* = 0.7$, $k_F = 1.3$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Delta_F = 1$ MeV) and $\xi \sim 6$ fm on the surface (with $m^* = 1$, $k_F = 0.9$ fm$^{-1}$, $\Delta_F = 2$ MeV).
- The local pairing field for the Argonne plus induced interaction reaches a peak of about 3 MeV at the surface ($R_{c.m.} \approx 6$ fm), compared to about 1.5 MeV for the Argonne interaction alone.
- The turning point for the classically allowed region is at $R_t = 7.1$ fm.
- Most quasiparticle energies from the local approximation are reproduced within 200 keV, and occupation probabilities larger than 0.1 are reproduced within 15%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach of Barranco et al. (2004) and Gori et al. (2004) that used the Nambu-Gorkov formalism with renormalized QRPA phonons to calculate pairing gaps in stable and halo nuclei, now focusing specifically on the spatial dependence of the pairing field in $^{120}$Sn. The authors show that a simple Local Density Approximation (LDA) using uniform neutron matter results overestimates the surface-to-interior difference of the pairing gap, an effect previously observed in neutron star crusts by Pizzochero (1998). The local approximation developed here enables comparison with phenomenological forces like the Gogny interaction and zero-range density-dependent interactions, and the authors provide fitted parameters for such contact interactions that reproduce the microscopic spatial dependence.
