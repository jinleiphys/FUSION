# Twelve-quark hypernuclei with A=4 in relativistic quark-gluon model

> Hypernuclei $ ^4_Y He$, $ ^4_Y H$, $ ^4_{YY} He$, $ ^4_{YY} H$, where $Y=Λ$, $Σ_0$, $Σ_+$, $Σ_-$, A=4 are considered using the relativistic twelve-quark equations in the framework of the dispersion relation technique. Hypernuclei as the systems of interacting quarks and gluons are considered. The relativistic twelve-quark amplitudes of hypernuclei, including $u$, $d$, $s$ quarks are constructed. The approximate solutions of these equations are obtained using a method based on the extraction of leading singularities of the amplitudes. The poles of the multiquark amplitudes allow us to determine the masses of hypernuclei with the atomic (baryon) number $A=B=4$. The mass of state $ ^4_ΛHe$ with the isospin projection $I_3=1/2$ and the spin-parity $J^P=0^+$ is equal to $M=3922\, MeV$. The mass of $ ^4_{ΛΛ}H$ $M=4118\, MeV$ with the isospin projection $I_3=0$ and the spin-parity $J^P=0^+$ is calculated. We predict the mass spectrum of hypernuclei with A=4, which is valuable to further experimental study of the hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The authors use a relativistic twelve-quark model, solved via dispersion relations and the extraction of leading singularities, to predict the masses of A=4 hypernuclei; the model's three parameters are fixed by fitting to the known mass of the \(^4_{\Lambda}\text{He}\) hypernucleus (3922 MeV), and the resulting predictions include states with negative binding energies (unbound) that lie near threshold.

## Method

The authors construct relativistic twelve-quark amplitudes for hypernuclei with baryon number A=4, including u, d, and s quarks, using a dispersion relation technique and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach. The pairwise quark-quark interactions are calculated via an N/D method with a four-fermion interaction carrying gluon quantum numbers, and the coupled-channel equations are solved approximately by extracting the leading singularities of the amplitudes. The poles of the resulting reduced amplitudes determine the masses of the hypernuclei, with the model parameters (cutoff \(\Lambda=6.355\), gluon coupling \(g=0.2122\), u/d quark mass 410 MeV, strange quark mass 594 MeV) fixed by the masses of \(^4_{\Lambda}\text{He}\) and other inputs.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic six-quark and nine-quark models for dibaryons and \(^3\text{He}\) (Gerasyuta and Matskevich, 2010, 2013) to twelve-quark systems describing A=4 hypernuclei. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD calculations by the NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane et al., 2013) and the HAL QCD Collaboration (Inoue et al., 2012), which compute binding energies in the limit of flavor SU(3) symmetry. The predictions for states like \(^4_{\Lambda\Lambda}\text{H}\) (mass 4118 MeV, near threshold) are relevant for experimental programs at DA\(\Phi\)NE, FAIR, Jefferson Lab, and J-PARC, and the authors suggest that such multiquark systems with small decay widths are analogous to tetraquarks, pentaquarks, and hexaquarks.
