# The non-linear evolution of jet quenching

> We construct a generalization of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian, going beyond the eikonal approximation, which governs the high-energy evolution of the scattering between a dilute projectile and a dense target with an arbitrary longitudinal extent (a nucleus, or a slice of quark-gluon plasma). Different physical regimes refer to the ratio $L/τ$ between the longitudinal size $L$ of the target and the lifetime $τ$ of the gluon fluctuations. When $L/τ\ll 1$, meaning that the target can be effectively treated as a shockwave, we recover the JIMWLK Hamiltonian, as expected. When $L/τ\gg 1$, meaning that the fluctuations live inside the target, the new Hamiltonian governs phenomena like the transverse momentum broadening and the radiative energy loss, which accompany the propagation of an energetic parton through a dense QCD medium. Using this Hamiltonian, we derive a non-linear equation for the dipole amplitude (a generalization of the BK equation), which describes the high-energy evolution of jet quenching. As compared to the original BK-JIMWLK evolution, the new evolution is remarkably different: the plasma saturation momentum evolves much faster with increasing energy (or decreasing Bjorken's $x$) than the corresponding scale for a shockwave (nucleus). This widely opens the transverse phase-space for the evolution and implies the existence of large radiative corrections, enhanced by the double logarithm $\ln^2(LT)$, with $T$ the temperature of the medium. This confirms and explains from a physical perspective a recent result by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (arXiv:1304.7677). The dominant corrections are smooth enough to be absorbed into a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$. This renormalization is controlled by a linear equation supplemented with a saturation boundary, which emerges via controlled approximations from the generalized BK equation alluded to above.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a generalization of the JIMWLK Hamiltonian beyond the eikonal approximation for scattering between a dilute projectile and a dense target with arbitrary longitudinal extent, and shows that when the target is an extended medium (L/τ ≫ 1), the resulting non-linear evolution of jet quenching is dominated by double-logarithmic corrections that can be absorbed into a renormalization of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\), confirming and explaining a recent result by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013).

## Method

The author derives a generalized JIMWLK Hamiltonian that includes non-eikonal effects by allowing for transverse motion of gluon fluctuations during their lifetime inside the medium, using light-cone gauge and path-integral methods. From this Hamiltonian, a non-linear equation for the dipole S-matrix (a generalization of the BK equation) is obtained under the assumption of Gaussian and local-in-light-cone-time medium correlations. The dominant double-logarithmic corrections are isolated by solving a simplified linear equation with a saturation boundary, which emerges from the generalized BK equation in the regime where the medium size L is much larger than the thermal wavelength λ = 1/T.

## Key numbers

- The double-logarithmic corrections are enhanced by \(\ln^2(LT)\), where T is the temperature of the medium.
- The plasma saturation momentum evolves much faster with energy than the corresponding scale for a shockwave (nucleus), with tree-level behavior \(Q_s^2(x) \propto 1/x\) where \(x \equiv \lambda/\tau\).
- The validity of the high-energy approximations for a weakly-coupled QGP requires the stronger constraint \(L/\lambda \gg 1/(\alpha_s^2 \ln(1/\alpha_s))\), equivalently \(Q_s^2 \gg T^2\).
- The transverse momentum broadening satisfies \(\langle p_\perp^2\rangle \simeq \hat q L\) at tree level, with \(\hat q^{(0)}(L)\) logarithmically dependent on L via the saturation momentum \(Q_s^2 \equiv \hat q L\).

## Context

This work extends the BK-JIMWLK formalism (Balitsky, 1996; Kovchegov, 1999; Jalilian-Marian et al., 1997-1998) from shockwave targets (proton-nucleus collisions) to extended media relevant for jet quenching in quark-gluon plasma. It confirms and provides a first-principles framework for the double-logarithmic resummation proposed by Liou, Mueller, and Wu (2013), and enables systematic calculation of radiative corrections to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) and the BDMPSZ radiative energy loss (Baier et al., 1996-1998; Zakharov, 1996-1997; Wiedemann, 2000; Arnold et al., 2001-2002).
