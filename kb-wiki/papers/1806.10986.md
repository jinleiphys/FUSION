# Relativistic tidal properties of superfluid neutron stars

> We investigate the tidal deformability of a superfluid neutron star. We calculate the equilibrium structure in the general relativistic two-fluid formalism with entrainment effect where we take neutron superfluid as one fluid and the other fluid is comprised of protons and electrons, making it a charge neutral fluid. We use a relativistic mean field model for the equation of state of matter where the interaction between baryons is mediated by the exchange $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons. Then, we study the linear, static $l=2$ perturbation on the star to compute the electric-type Love number following Hinderer's prescription.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the tidal deformability of a superfluid neutron star, calculated using a general relativistic two-fluid formalism with entrainment, is larger than that of a non-superfluid single-fluid star, with the percentage change in the dimensionless tidal deformability ranging from approximately 4% to 11% for stars with masses between 1 and 2 solar masses, depending on the equation of state used.

## Method

The authors adopt a general relativistic two-fluid model where the neutron superfluid is one fluid and the combined proton-electron charge-neutral fluid is the other, including the entrainment effect. They use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange (using NL3 and GM1 parameter sets) to compute the master function and matter coefficients, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for the equilibrium structure. Finally, they apply linear, static l=2 even-parity perturbations in the Regge-Wheeler gauge following Hinderer's prescription to compute the electric-type Love number k2 and the tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass for NL3 EOS: 2.793 solar masses, with corresponding radius 13.19 km.
* Maximum mass for GM1 EOS: 2.384 solar masses, with corresponding radius 11.9 km.
* For a 1.4 solar mass star with NL3 EOS: dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda_T (two-fluid) = 1382.9, compared to Lambda_T (one-fluid) = 1276.7, giving a percentage change of 8.32%.
* For a 1.4 solar mass star with GM1 EOS: dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda_T (two-fluid) = 971, compared to Lambda_T (one-fluid) = 903.9, giving a percentage change of 7.42%.
* Percentage change in dimensionless tidal deformability ranges from 5.29% to 8.61% for NL3 EOS across masses 1.1 to 1.9 solar masses.
* Percentage change in dimensionless tidal deformability ranges from 4.09% to 11.36% for GM1 EOS across masses 1.0 to 2.0 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic tidal deformability formalism of Hinderer (2008) to superfluid neutron stars, which had previously been studied only in the Newtonian context by Yu and Weinberg (2017). It uses the two-fluid formalism developed by Carter, Comer, and collaborators, and employs the RMF model with isospin dependence updated by Kheto and Bandyopadhyay (2014, 2015). The results show that including superfluidity increases the tidal deformability, which could impose stricter constraints on the dense matter equation of state when compared with gravitational wave data from events like GW170817.
