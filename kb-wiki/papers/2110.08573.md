# Insight from the elliptic flow of identified hadrons measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In this paper, we have discussed the transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The transverse momentum dependence of the number of constituent quarks ($n_{q}$) scaled $v_{2}$ is obtained for all the measured identified hadrons. The $n_{q}$ scaled $ϕ$ $v_{2}$ is found to be similar to $n_{q}$ scaled $Ω$ $v_{2}$. This indicates that both $ϕ$ and $Ω$ are produced through quark recombination at RHIC energies. We also find $n_{q}$ scaled proton $v_{2}$ ($v_{2}$ of light quarks) is higher than $n_{q}$ scaled $ϕ$ and $Ω$ $v_{2}$ ($v_{2}$ of strange quarks) at $p_{T}/n_{q}$ $<$ 1.0 GeV/c. This can be explained by considering quark $v_{2}$ is proportional to its transverse kinetic energy considering mass of deconfined light and strange quarks equal to $\sim $4 MeV and $\sim$ 140 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the elliptic flow ($v_2$) of light and strange quarks, extracted from identified hadron data under the quark recombination model, scales as a function of the quarks' transverse kinetic energy ($KE_T^q$) when using bare quark masses, but this scaling is presented as a purely empirical observation that "needs more theoretical input to understand the physics behind it."

## Method

The authors compiled published data on the transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2$) for identified hadrons (protons, $\phi$, $\Omega$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$) from Au+Au collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5, 19.6, 39, 200 GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV). Within the quark recombination model, they extracted $v_2$ of light quarks (from proton $v_2$) and strange quarks (from $\phi$ and $\Omega$ $v_2$) and tested scaling by plotting $v_2/n_q$ versus $p_T/n_q$ and versus $KE_T^q = \sqrt{(p_T/n_q)^2 + m_q^2} - m_q$, using bare quark masses of 4 MeV/$c^2$ for light quarks and 100 or 140 MeV/$c^2$ for strange quarks. They quantified the scaling quality via $\chi^2/ndf$ for different strange quark masses and extended the test to triangular flow ($v_3$) and lower beam energies.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, the $n_q$-scaled $v_2$ of $\phi$ and $\Omega$ are found to be similar, consistent with production through quark recombination.
- The best scaling between light and strange quark $v_2$ as a function of $KE_T^q$ is obtained for a strange quark mass $m_s$ = 130-140 MeV/$c^2$, based on $\chi^2/ndf$ analysis.
- The scaling holds for $v_2$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 39 and 19.6 GeV (0-80% minimum bias), but for 11.5 GeV the authors state "we need high statistics measurements to make any conclusion."
- The scaling also holds for $v_2$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV (20-30% centrality), where LHC energy is "a factor 100 times higher than RHIC BES energies."
- For triangular flow ($v_3$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV, $v_3/n_q^{3/2}$ as a function of $KE_T^q$ shows consistency between proton and $\phi$, similar to the $v_2$ case.

## Context

This work extends the established quark recombination/coalescence model (Coal_Fries, Coal_Greco, Coal_Voloshin, 2000s) and the earlier observation by the PHENIX experiment (Scaling_Phenix) that $v_2/n_q$ versus $KE_T/n_q$ shows better scaling than versus $p_T/n_q$. The authors propose a new empirical scaling using the transverse kinetic energy of individual quarks ($KE_T^q$) rather than hadrons, which works across a wide range of collision energies from RHIC Beam Energy Scan (11.5-200 GeV) to LHC (5.02 TeV). The finding enables future tests with higher-statistics data from RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase-II at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 19.6$ GeV and calls for theoretical work to explain the underlying mechanism.
