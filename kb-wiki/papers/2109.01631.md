# Evidence of quadrupole and octupole deformations in $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr and $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru collisions at ultra-relativistic energies

> In the hydrodynamic model description of heavy ion collisions, the elliptic flow $v_2$ and triangular flow $v_3$ are sensitive to the quadrupole deformation $β_2$ and octupole deformation $β_3$ of the colliding nuclei. The relations between $v_n$ and $β_n$ have recently been clarified and were found to follow a simple parametric form. The STAR Collaboration have just published precision $v_n$ data from isobaric $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions, where they observe large differences in central collisions $v_{2,\mathrm{Ru}}>v_{2,\mathrm{Zr}}$ and $v_{3,\mathrm{Ru}}<v_{3,\mathrm{Zr}}$. Using a transport model simulation, we show that these orderings are a natural consequence of $β_{2,\mathrm{Ru}}\ggβ_{2,\mathrm{Zr}}$ and $β_{3,\mathrm{Ru}}\llβ_{3,\mathrm{Zr}}$. We reproduce the centrality dependence of the $v_2$ ratio qualitatively and $v_3$ ratio quantitatively, and extract values of $β_2$ and $β_3$ that are consistent with those measured at low energy nuclear structure experiments. STAR data provide the first direct evidence of strong octupole correlations in the ground state of $^{96}$Zr in heavy ion collisions. Our analysis demonstrates that flow measurements in high-energy heavy ion collisions, especially using isobaric systems, are a new precision tool to study nuclear structure physics.

## Key claim
Using AMPT transport model simulations, the authors show that the observed ordering of elliptic flow (v2,Ru > v2,Zr) and triangular flow (v3,Ru < v3,Zr) in isobaric collisions at 200 GeV is a natural consequence of quadrupole deformation β2,Ru ≫ β2,Zr and octupole deformation β3,Ru ≪ β3,Zr, with the caveat that additional physics such as neutron skin differences are required to quantitatively describe the v2 ratio.

## Method
The authors performed AMPT v2.26t5 simulations in string-melting mode at √sNN = 200 GeV with a partonic cross section of 3.0 mb for generic 96X+96X collisions. They scanned β2 values (0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2) with β3=0 and β3 values (0, 0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2) with β2=0.06, calculating eccentricities εn from participating nucleons and flow harmonics vn using two-particle correlations for hadrons with 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV and |η| < 2. The ratios vn,Ru/vn,Zr were then predicted using the known β2 and β3 values from low-energy nuclear structure measurements and compared to STAR data.

## Key numbers
- β2,Ru = 0.154 and β2,Zr = 0.062, deduced from B(E2;0+1 → 2+1) transition measurements
- β3,Zr values from three B(E3;0+1 → 3-1) measurements: 0.202, 0.235, and 0.27; β3,Ru assumed to be 0
- In 0-1% most central collisions, predicted v2,Ru/v2,Zr ≈ 1.08 (from v2,Ru2/v2,Zr2 ≈ 1.16) assuming β2,Ru=0.154 and β2,Zr=0.062
- For β3,Zr = 0.202, 0.235, 0.27, predicted v3,Ru/v3,Zr = 0.90, 0.87, 0.83 respectively (from v3,Zr2/v3,Ru2 = 1.24, 1.33, 1.44)
- The β3 contribution to v2 ratio reaches maximum reduction at Npart ≈ 146 (about 8% centrality)

## Context
This work extends the framework developed by Giacalone (2021), Jia (2021), and Bally (2021) relating nuclear deformation to collective flow in heavy ion collisions. It applies the parametric form vn2 = an + bnβn2 to the recent STAR isobar data (2021), providing the first direct evidence of strong octupole correlations in the ground state of 96Zr from high-energy collisions. The analysis demonstrates that isobaric collisions can complement low-energy nuclear structure spectroscopy methods (such as those of Stautberg (1967), Mach (1990), and Hofer (1993)) for constraining nuclear deformation parameters, and motivates extended system scans of different isobaric systems at existing collider facilities.
