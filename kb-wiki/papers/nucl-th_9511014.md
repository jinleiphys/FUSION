# An Analysis of the Use of the Cluster Separability in Scattering Theory

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard Adiabatic Cluster Separability (ACS) introduces serious limitations for representing scattering processes involving bound states, particularly for deconfinement and hadronization formalisms, and proposes a less rigid alternative called Disturbative Adiabatic Cluster Separability (DACS) that includes intermediate disturbed phases between the initial, interaction, and final propagation regions.

## Method
The authors analyze three forms of cluster separability, Cluster Separability (CS), Adiabatic Cluster Separability (ACS), and Disturbative Adiabatic Cluster Separability (DACS), within the framework of time-dependent propagator theory in Quantum Field Theory. They derive the factorization of propagators and S-matrices for each approach, with DACS introducing additional disturbance amplitudes \(K_\alpha^d\) and \(\bar{K}_\beta^d\) that describe intermediate disturbed regions. The mathematical basis for the DACS formalism is developed using wave (Møller) operator methods in quantum mechanical S-matrix theory.

## Key numbers
- No specific numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal analysis of theoretical principles.

## Context
This work extends the standard formulation of cluster separability as discussed in Foldy (year not given) and other references [3]-[4], and the Adiabatic Hypothesis from Kato and others [5]. The authors introduce DACS as a refinement of ACS to address limitations in describing intermediate objects such as transition operators and form factors, particularly in formalisms involving deconfinement and hadronization. The analysis enables future applications to off-mass-shell effects in nuclear reactions, formation of new particle states, and transition amplitudes at the quark level, with the general scheme being projectable onto various scattering formalisms.
