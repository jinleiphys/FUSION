# Screening Mass from Chiral Perturbation Theory, Virial Expansion, and the Lattice

> We calculate the electric screening mass in hot hadronic matter using two different approaches, chiral perturbation theory and the relativistic virial expansion with empirical phase shifts, and compare the results to each other and to a gas of free pions and $ρ$ mesons. We also compute the electric screening mass for noninteracting, charged bosons with mass $m$ on a lattice to study likely finite size effects in lattice gauge theory simulations of continuum QCD. For a lattice of given size, the continuum can be properly represented only for a window in the ratio $T/m$.

## Key claim

The electric screening mass in hot hadronic matter, calculated via chiral perturbation theory and the relativistic virial expansion with empirical phase shifts, agrees exactly in the low temperature Boltzmann limit (T << mπ) to order (T/mπ)^(3/2), but the two approaches diverge at temperatures above approximately 100 MeV where the chiral expansion ceases to converge; furthermore, the screening mass for an interacting pion gas is not simply the sum of screening masses of an ideal gas of pions and rho mesons, contrary to what might be expected from the behavior of the pressure.

## Method

The electric screening mass squared was computed from the relation m_el^2 = e^2 (∂^2P/∂μ^2)_{μ=0}, where μ is the electric charge chemical potential and P is the pressure. Three approaches were used: (1) a relativistic virial expansion truncated at the second virial coefficient, using empirical ππ scattering phase shifts in the spin-isospin channels δ_0^0, δ_0^2, and δ_1^1; (2) finite-temperature chiral perturbation theory extended to nonzero chemical potential, with the effective Lagrangian expanded to order F^(-4) and including two-loop thermal diagrams; (3) an exact analytic calculation of the electric susceptibility for noninteracting charged bosons on a Euclidean lattice of size N_s^3 × N_t with lattice spacing a.

## Key numbers

- The virial expansion and chiral perturbation theory results agree to relative order (T/mπ)^(3/2) in the low-temperature Boltzmann limit T << mπ.
- Chiral perturbation theory shows reasonable agreement with the virial calculation up to temperatures of around 80 MeV; above 100 MeV the chiral perturbation expansion does not appear convergent.
- The order F^(-4) result in chiral perturbation theory "starts to blow-up" above 100 MeV.
- For the lattice calculation, the continuum can be properly represented only for a window in the ratio T/m, where m is the boson mass.
- Lattice QCD calculations on a 10^3 × 6 lattice cover a temperature range of about 120 to 190 MeV.
- The critical chemical potential on the lattice for Bose-Einstein condensation is determined by sinh(aμ_crit/2) = am/2.

## Context

This work extends the study of electric screening mass in hot QCD initiated by Irvin et al. (1993) and connects to the broader program of understanding hot hadronic matter properties relevant to heavy ion experiments. The virial expansion approach builds on the relativistic virial expansion formalism of Dashen et al. (1969) and its recent applications to hadronic thermodynamics by Venugopalan and Prakash (1992) and by Welke et al. (1990). The chiral perturbation theory calculation generalizes the zero-chemical-potential pressure calculation of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) to finite chemical potential. The lattice results provide guidance for interpreting lattice QCD simulations of quark number susceptibilities, such as those by Gottlieb et al. (1987) on 8^3 × 4 lattices and subsequent work on 10^3 × 6 lattices, and are intended to inform the design of upcoming Teraflop-scale lattice calculations.
