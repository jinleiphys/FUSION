# Deuteron -- $α$ interaction by inversion of RGM S-matrix: determination of spin-orbit potential for spin-1 projectile

> The iterative-perturbative (IP) procedure for S-matrix to potential inversion is applied to spin-one projectiles for the restricted case of vector spin-orbit interaction only. In order to evaluate this extension of IP inversion we have inverted the multi-channel RGM $S_{lj}$ of Kanada et al for deuterons scattering from $^4$He with deuteron distortion and then compared the central components with those derived from RGM with spin set to zero. Attention is given to the question of how well the resulting potentials are established. Reliable spin-1 inversion is demonstrated. Results relating to inversion, to deuteron-nucleus interactions and to RGM are presented and suggest the range of nuclear interaction information which the procedure makes possible. Unusual non-locality and parity dependence effects are found; these are of possible relevance to generic properties of nuclear potentials.

## Key claim

The iterative-perturbative (IP) inversion procedure can be extended to spin-one projectiles for the restricted case of vector spin-orbit interaction only, provided there is no coupling between partial wave channels with different orbital angular momentum for a given total angular momentum; the resulting potentials are reliable despite the fact that three sets of amplitudes determine only two potential components, which is not _a priori_ guaranteed to yield smooth potentials.

## Method

The IP inversion method was applied to multi-channel resonating group model (MCRGM) S-matrix elements \(S_{lj}\) from Kanada et al. (KKST) for deuteron scattering from \(^4\)He at center-of-mass energies of 19.6 and 37.33 MeV, where the MCRGM included deuteron distortion via coupling to bound excited quasi-states but no tensor forces and no coupling between \(l=J-1\) and \(l=J+1\) channels. The inversion yielded local potentials of the form \(V(r) + \mathbf{l}\cdot\mathbf{S} V_{\rm so}(r)\) with parity-dependent components \(V_1(r)\) and \(V_2(r)\) defined by \(V(r) = V_1(r) + (-1)^l V_2(r)\), and the uniqueness of the potentials was tested by varying the starting potential and the inversion basis. Additional inversions were performed on S-matrices from spin-zero deuteron RGM calculations and from the no-distortion RGM of Lemere, Tang and Thompson at 16.58, 24.87 and 53.9 MeV to disentangle the effects of deuteron distortion and spin-orbit interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the 37.33 MeV KKST inversion (pot3), the volume integral of the real central \(V_1\) component was \(J_{\rm R} = 479.86\) MeV fm\(^3\) per projectile nucleon, with an RMS radius of 2.962 fm; the imaginary volume integral was \(J_{\rm I} = 136.85\) MeV fm\(^3\) with RMS radius 3.463 fm; the convergence parameter was \(\sigma = 0.00173\).
- For the 19.6 MeV KKST inversion (pot-a), the real central \(V_1\) volume integral was \(J_{\rm R} = 499.72\) MeV fm\(^3\) per projectile nucleon with RMS radius 2.905 fm; the imaginary volume integral was \(J_{\rm I} = 101.75\) MeV fm\(^3\) with RMS radius 3.448 fm; \(\sigma = 0.000903\).
- For the spin-zero RGM at 20 MeV CM, the parity-dependent \(V_2\) term was attractive such that at 4 fm, where \(V_1 \sim 5\) MeV, the ratio of even to odd potential was about 2:1.
- For the spin-zero RGM at 60 MeV CM, the even parity potential was some 2.5 MeV deeper than the odd parity potential at about 2.5 fm.
- The energy-dependent inversion of spin-zero RGM phase shifts from 20 to 80 MeV CM was well represented by \((1 - 0.00237 E) V_1(r) + (-1)^l (1 - 0.00919 E) V_2(r)\), where \(V_2(r)\) peaked at \(r=3\) fm with value \(-2.2\) MeV.
- For the 55 MeV CM spin-zero complex RGM of Thompson and Tang, the volume integral of the real \(V_1\) component was 470 MeV fm\(^3\) per projectile nucleon at a laboratory energy of 82.5 MeV; the real \(V_2\) term had a maximum magnitude of 2.5 MeV at about 1.8 fm; the imaginary \(V_2\) term peaked near 1.5 fm with a maximum of about 1 MeV.
- For the Lemere et al. RGM at 24.87 MeV CM, the real central \(V_1\) volume integral was \(J_{\rm R} = 552\) MeV fm\(^3\) with RMS radius 3.111 fm; at 53.9 MeV CM, \(J_{\rm R} = 557\) MeV fm\(^3\) with RMS radius 3.137 fm.

## Context

This work extends the IP inversion method, previously applied to spin-half projectiles (Mackintosh and Cooper, 1996), to spin-one projectiles for the first time, using the multi-channel RGM S-matrices of Kanada et al. (KKST) for deuteron-alpha scattering. The results reveal an "anti-Perey effect" in the imaginary potential at the nuclear center, opposite to the Perey-Buck non-locality effect seen in nucleon scattering (Perey and Buck, 1962), and demonstrate substantial parity dependence in the local equivalent potentials, consistent with earlier spin-zero RGM studies. The findings enable systematic studies linking inversion to RGM and coupled-channels calculations to disentangle the effects of antisymmetrization, channel coupling, and different nucleon-nucleon interactions on deuteron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus potentials.
