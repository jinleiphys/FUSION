# Direct Observation of Proton-Neutron Short-Range Correlation Dominance in Heavy Nuclei

> We measured the triple coincidence A(e,e'np) and A(e,e'pp) reactions on carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead targets at Q2 > 1.5 (GeV/c)2, xB > 1.1 and missing momentum > 400 MeV/c. This was the first direct measurement of both proton-proton (pp) and neutron-proton (np) short-range correlated (SRC) pair knockout from heavy asymmetric nuclei. For all measured nuclei, the average proton-proton (pp) to neutron-proton (np) reduced cross-section ratio is about 6%, in agreement with previous indirect measurements. Correcting for Single-Charge Exchange effects decreased the SRC pairs ratio to ~ 3%, which is lower than previous results. Comparisons to theoretical Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) cross-section calculations show good agreement using both phenomenological and chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials, favoring a lower pp to np pair ratio. The ability of the GCF calculation to describe the experimental data using either phenomenological or chiral potentials suggests possible reduction of scale- and scheme-dependence in cross section ratios. Our results also support the high-resolution description of high-momentum states being predominantly due to nucleons in SRC pairs.

## Key claim

The first direct measurement of both proton-proton and neutron-proton short-range correlated pair knockout from heavy asymmetric nuclei (carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead) confirms that np pairs dominate over pp pairs by a factor of about 15-20, with the measured pp/np reduced cross-section ratio of about 6% representing an upper limit that decreases to approximately 3% after correcting for single-charge exchange effects.

## Method

The CLAS collaboration measured triple-coincidence A(e,e'np) and A(e,e'pp) reactions on carbon, aluminum, iron, and lead targets using a 5.01 GeV electron beam at Jefferson Lab Hall B, selecting events with Q2 > 1.5 (GeV/c)2, xB > 1.1, and missing momentum > 400 MeV/c. The scattered electron, knocked-out leading nucleon (proton or neutron), and recoil proton were detected simultaneously, with event yields weighted by inverse detection efficiencies and elementary electron-nucleon cross sections to extract reduced cross-section ratios. Glauber-based calculations were applied to correct for single-charge exchange effects, and results were compared to Generalized Contact Formalism calculations using both phenomenological (AV18) and chiral (N2LO and N3LO) nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- Average pp/np reduced cross-section ratio across all measured nuclei: about 6% (before SCX correction)
- After correcting for single-charge exchange effects, the SRC pair ratio decreased to approximately 3%
- Reduced cross-section ratio in lower recoil momentum bin (0.3-0.6 GeV/c): average 5.5 ± 0.4% across all nuclei
- Reduced cross-section ratio in higher recoil momentum bin (0.6-1.0 GeV/c): average 7.0 ± 0.9% across all nuclei
- For carbon specifically: 5.33 ± 0.65 ± 0.35% in the low recoil bin and 7.87 ± 1.68 ± 0.70% in the high recoil bin
- Previously determined pp/np contact ratios from Weiss (2018): 7.1% ± 1.5% (AV18), 5.2% ± 1.1% (local N2LO), 4.0% ± 0.8% (non-local N3LO)
- New contact ratios fitted to this data: 3.0% ± 0.8% (AV18), 3.6% ± 1.0% (local N2LO), 1.9% ± 0.5% (non-local N3LO)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of SRC pairs in light symmetric nuclei (4He and 12C) by Subedi (2008) and Korover (2014) to heavy asymmetric nuclei, providing the first direct confirmation of np-SRC dominance without the assumptions required by the indirect extraction method of Hen (2014). The agreement between data and Generalized Contact Formalism calculations using both phenomenological and chiral potentials supports the high-resolution description of high-momentum nucleons as predominantly members of SRC pairs, and enables improved constraints on theoretical models of nuclear structure relevant to neutron star properties and the EMC effect.
