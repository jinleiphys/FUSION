# Low-energy QCD and hadronic structure

> Recent developments and selected topics in low-energy QCD are summarized, from chiral effective field theory to systems with strange and charm quarks, from lattice QCD to precision experiments.

## Key claim

Low-energy QCD with light quarks has become a "quantitative science" through the joint efforts of chiral effective field theory, lattice QCD, and precision experiments, but this quantitative success is primarily established in the meson sector; the baryon sector and systems with strange or charm quarks remain less precise or more challenging.

## Method

The paper reviews recent developments in low-energy QCD by summarizing results from chiral effective field theory (both SU(2) and SU(3) versions), lattice QCD simulations, and precision experiments (including NA48/2, KLOE, KEK, and DEAR). It covers the meson sector (pion properties, pi-pi scattering, kaon decays), the baryon sector (nucleon mass, spin structure), the interface with nuclear physics (in-medium chiral perturbation theory for the chiral condensate), strangeness physics (coupled-channel dynamics for the Kbar-N system), and charmonium physics (new states above open-charm thresholds).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 0.09 GeV (approximately)
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: 4*pi*f_pi = 1 GeV (approximately)
- Light quark masses: m_d = 3-7 MeV, m_u/m_d = 0.3-0.6, m_s = 70-120 MeV (at renormalization scale mu = 2 GeV)
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.2 GeV
- Top quark mass: m_t = 174 GeV
- Critical temperature for chiral and deconfinement transitions: T_c = 190 MeV (from N_f = 2+1 lattice QCD)
- Pion-pion scattering lengths from NA48/2: a_0 = (0.218 +/- 0.013) m_pi^{-1}, a_2 = (-0.046 +/- 0.009) m_pi^{-1}
- Theoretical pi-pi scattering lengths: a_0 = (0.220 +/- 0.005) m_pi^{-1}, a_2 = (-0.044 +/- 0.001) m_pi^{-1}
- Sigma pole mass and width: M_sigma = 441^{+16}_{-8} MeV, Gamma_sigma = 544^{+18}_{-25} MeV
- Branching ratio for K_S -> gamma gamma from KLOE: (2.26 +/- 0.12 +/- 0.06) x 10^{-6}
- Nucleon sigma term: sigma_N = 0.05 GeV (approximately)
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} = 0.45 m_pi^3
- Fermi momentum at saturation: p_F = 0.26 GeV
- K^-pp binding energy range from theory: B = 20-70 MeV, width Gamma = 40-110 MeV
- Kaonic hydrogen energy shift and width from KEK and DEAR experiments (values given in figures, not as explicit numbers in text)

## Context

This review extends the line of work establishing chiral effective field theory as the low-energy limit of QCD, building on foundational papers by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for chiral perturbation theory and by Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1995) for the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel approach to Kbar-N interactions. It enables future work on clarifying the relationship between chiral and deconfinement transitions (where lattice results from Cheng et al. (2008) and Aoki et al. (2006) disagree by about 20 MeV), on resolving the kaonic hydrogen puzzle with the upcoming SIDDHARTA experiment, and on understanding the new charmonium states discovered since 2002 by Belle, BaBar, CLEO-c, CDF, and D0.
