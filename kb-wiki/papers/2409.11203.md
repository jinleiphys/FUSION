# Ultra-low $Q_β$ value for the allowed decay of $^{110}$Ag$^m$ confirmed via mass measurements

> The mass of the electron-antineutrino can be determined in dedicated measurements of the $β$ spectral shape near the $β$ endpoint of a $β^-$ transition, with a low $Q$ value enhancing the sensitivity of the measurement. One such low-$Q$-value candidate is the transition between the $6^+$ isomer of $^{110}$Ag and the $5^+_2$ state in $^{110}$Cd ($Q^{\ast}_{β,m}=-0.12(131)$ keV). To reduce the uncertainty of the $Q$ value, we have used the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance technique with the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap and performed a high-precision atomic-mass measurement of $^{109}$Ag with $^{110}$Cd as a reference. Combined with the known spectroscopic data, we obtain a re-evaluated value $Q^{\ast}_{β,m}=405(135)$ eV, for the $^{110}\text{Ag}(6^+_\text{m}) \rightarrow {^{110}\text{Cd}}(5^+_2)$ transition. This represents the lowest $Q_β$ value for any allowed transition observed to date. In order to estimate the partial half-life ($t_{1/2}$) and branching ratio (Br) of the transition, nuclear shell-model calculations were performed using the $jj45pnb$ Hamiltonian in combination with state-of-the-art atomic calculations. The computed values $t_{1/2} = 2.23^{+5.24}_{-1.28} \times 10^7$ years and $\textrm{Br} = 3.07^{+4.16}_{-2.15} \times 10^{-8}$, along with the thermal-neutron capture on stable $^{109}$Ag as a viable production method, make $^{110}\textrm{Ag}^m$ a promising candidate for future antineutrino-mass measurements.

# Ultra-low $Q_\beta$ value for the allowed decay of $^{110}$Ag$^m$ confirmed via mass measurements

## Key claim

The $^{110}$Ag$(6^+_\text{m}) \rightarrow {^{110}\text{Cd}}(5^+_2)$ transition has a re-evaluated $Q_{\beta,m}^*$ value of 405(135) eV, making it the lowest $Q_\beta$ value for any allowed transition observed to date, but the result is only at a $3\sigma$ confidence level ($Q/\delta Q = 3$) and the transition has not yet been experimentally confirmed to populate the $5^+_2$ state.

## Method

The phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique with the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap at the IGISOL facility was used to measure the atomic mass of $^{109}$Ag relative to $^{110}$Cd as a reference, yielding a frequency ratio $r = \nu_c(^{110}\text{Cd}^+)/\nu_c(^{109}\text{Ag}^+)$. This measurement determined the single-proton separation energy of $^{110}$Cd, which was combined with known spectroscopic data (single-neutron separation energy, isomer excitation energy, and daughter state excitation energy) to re-evaluate the $Q_{\beta,m}^*$ value. Nuclear shell-model calculations using the KSHELL code with the $jj45pnb$ Hamiltonian, combined with atomic corrections including screening, radiative, and exchange effects, were performed to estimate the partial half-life and branching ratio of the transition.

## Key numbers

- $Q_{\beta,m}^* = 405(135)$ eV for the $^{110}\text{Ag}(6^+_\text{m}) \rightarrow {^{110}\text{Cd}}(5^+_2)$ transition, representing the lowest $Q_\beta$ value for any allowed transition observed to date
- Single-proton separation energy of $^{110}$Cd: $S_p = 8918.07(5)$ keV, a factor of 26 more precise than the AME20 value of 8917.5(13) keV
- Mass excess of $^{109}$Ag: ME = $-88718.8(4)$ keV, three times more precise than the AME20 value
- Frequency ratio $r = \nu_c(^{110}\text{Cd}^+)/\nu_c(^{109}\text{Ag}^+) = 0.99091693576(44)$
- Theoretical partial half-life for the $6^+_\text{m} \rightarrow 5^+_2$ transition: $t_{1/2} = 2.23^{+5.24}_{-1.28} \times 10^7$ years (using $g_{\rm A}^{\rm eff} = 0.5$)
- Branching ratio: Br $= 3.07^{+4.16}_{-2.15} \times 10^{-8}$
- $Q_{\beta,m}^*$ for the $6^+_\text{m} \rightarrow 0^+_{\text{gs}}$ transition: 3008.81(13) keV
- $Q_{\beta}$ for the $1^+_{\text{gs}} \rightarrow 0^+_{\text{gs}}$ transition: 2891.22(12) keV

## Context

This work extends the search for ultra-low-$Q_\beta$ transitions beyond ground-state-to-excited-state decays, which have been the focus of studies by Ge (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024) and Ramalho (2022), to isomeric-state-to-excited-state (iso-ex) transitions. The $^{110}$Ag$^m$ candidate joins a small group of ultra-low-$Q^*$ decays including $^{115}$In (second-forbidden unique, $Q_\beta^* \approx 200$ eV, Wieslander 2009), $^{135}$Cs ($Q_\beta^* = 0.44(31)$ keV, deRoubin 2020), $^{131}$I ($Q_\beta^* = 1.03(23)$ keV, Eronen 2022), and $^{159}$Dy ($Q_{EC}^* = 1.18(19)$ keV, Ge 2021a). The work enables future antineutrino mass measurement experiments using $^{110}$Ag$^m$, which can be produced via thermal-neutron capture on stable $^{109}$Ag with a cross section of approximately 4.1 barns, though a new decay study is required to confirm the population of the $5^+_2$ state.
