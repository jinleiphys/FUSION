# Quadrupole dominance in light Cd and Sn isotopes

> Shell model calculations with the neutron effective charge as single free parameter describe well the \bet and \bef rates for $N\le 64$ in the Cd and Sn isotopes. The former exhibit weak permanent deformation corroborating the prediction of a pseudo SU3 symmetry, which remains of heuristic value in the latter, though the pairing force erodes the quadrupole dominance. Calculations in $10^7$ and $10^{10}$-dimensional spaces exhibit almost identical patterns: A vindication of the shell model. For $N\ge 64$ quadrupole dominance is accentuated in the Cd isotopes and gives way to seniority dominance for the Sn isotopes.

## Key claim

Shell model calculations with the neutron effective charge as the single free parameter describe well the B(E2: 2+→0+) and B(E2: 4+→2+) rates for N ≤ 64 in Cd and Sn isotopes, but the pseudo SU3 symmetry that holds heuristically in Sn is eroded by the pairing force, while in Cd the quadrupole dominance is stronger and more clear-cut.

## Method

Shell model diagonalizations were performed in spaces defined by (g^{X-u}r_4^u)_π(g^{10-t}r_4^{n+t})_ν with proton (u) and neutron (t) excitations restricted to u+t ≤ M, using Vlow-k variants of the N3LO precision interaction with oscillator parameter ħω = 8.4 MeV and cutoff λ = 2 fm^{-1}. The monopole part of the interaction was removed and replaced by single-particle energies for 100Sn from the GEMO (General Monopole) description, then the interaction was scaled by 1.1 and renormalized by increasing the quadrupole and JT=01 pairing components by q×10% and p×10% respectively, yielding interactions called I.q.p. The neutron effective charge e_ν was varied while e_π was fixed at 1.40.

## Key numbers

- For 104Cd: calculated B(E2: 2+→0+) = 809 e^2fm^4 (B20SP) compared to experimental 779(80) e^2fm^4
- For 106Cd: calculated B(E2: 2+→0+) = 838 e^2fm^4 (B20SP) compared to experimental 814(24) e^2fm^4
- For 108Cd: calculated B(E2: 2+→0+) = 833 e^2fm^4 (B20SP) compared to experimental 838(28) e^2fm^4
- For 110Cd: calculated B(E2: 2+→0+) = 827 e^2fm^4 (B20SP) compared to experimental 852(42) e^2fm^4
- The Alaga rule ratio B(E2: 4+→2+)/B(E2: 2+→0+) ≈ 1.43 holds for Cd isotopes
- The quadrupole quotient rule yields average Q/q = 0.96 for 106-110Cd
- For 112Cd: experimental B(E2: 2_1→0_1) = 1000(100) e^2fm^4 or 31(3) W.u., compared to calculated 41 W.u. from quasi-SU3 mechanism
- For Sn isotopes: calculations in 10^7 and 10^10-dimensional spaces exhibit almost identical patterns

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU3 symmetry scheme of Arima (1969) and Hecht (1969) and Elliott's SU3 scheme (1958) to explain quadrupole dominance in light Cd and Sn isotopes. The calculations corroborate the prediction of weak permanent deformation in Cd isotopes anticipated in PhysRevC.96.014302 (2017) and extend the analysis of B(E2) trends in Sn from Banu et al. (2005) and subsequent work by Vaman (2007), Cederkall (2007), Ekstrom (2008), Kumar (2010, 2017), Bader (2013), and Doornenbal (2014). The results enable future neutron-only calculations for the transition region 116-120Sn and demonstrate that severely truncated spaces can represent exact results, vindicating the shell model approach.
