# Chiral Light Front Perturbation Theory and the Flavor Dependence of the Light-Quark Nucleon Sea

> The light-quark flavor dependence of the proton sea has been of great interest for many years because of its close connection with non-perturbative effects. One hypothesis is that this dependence arises from the pion cloud of the proton. We apply light cone perturbation theory and experimental constraints to a chiral Lagrangian to compute the relevant Fock-space components of the nucleon wave function with well-defined uncertainties. Existing experimental information regarding the light flavor sea is studied, and predictions for future experimental results are provided. Future experiments have the ability to rule out this hypothesis and have profound implications for understanding the nucleon-nucleon force.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the light-quark flavor dependence of the proton sea can be fully explained by the pion cloud of the proton, but this hypothesis can only be definitively tested by future experiments such as SeaQuest; the current E866 data show disagreement with the model at higher x values, which may rule out the pion cloud hypothesis if confirmed.

## Method

The authors apply light-front perturbation theory to a leading-order chiral Lagrangian to compute the Fock-space components of the nucleon wave function, including pion-nucleon and pion-Delta intermediate states. They constrain the pion-baryon vertex function using the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation, which relates the pion-nucleon form factor to the experimentally measured axial form factor, thereby reducing the major uncertainty in previous calculations. The resulting splitting functions are then used in a convolution formalism to compute the antiquark distributions of the proton sea.

## Key numbers

- Integrated asymmetry: predicted range 0.098 ≤ D̄−Ū ≤ 0.131, compared to E866 experimental value 0.118 ± 0.012
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant range: lower limit 12.8 from Goldberger-Treiman relation with g_A = 1.267 ± 0.04, M = 0.939 GeV, f_π = 92.6 MeV; upper limit 13.2 from scattering data
- Axial form factor dipole mass: M_A = 1.03 ± 0.04 GeV, with cutoff Λ = √3/2 M_A increased to ±10% uncertainty
- Pion-Delta to pion-nucleon coupling ratio: g_{πΔ}/g_{πN} = 1.61 from lattice calculations, with range 1.5 ≤ r ≤ 1.7 from large N_c and quark model limits
- Transverse spatial distribution peaks at b ≈ 0.6 fm for the πN fluctuation
- Ratio d̄/ū approaches 1/2 as x → 1 due to the πΔ contribution becoming relatively more important

## Context

This work extends the long-standing pion cloud model of the nucleon sea, originally proposed by Thomas (1983) and Henley (1990), and addresses the controversy between Alberg (2012) and Salamu (2014), who argue the pion cloud accounts completely for the measured asymmetry, versus Strikman (2009), who finds only about half can be accounted for. The paper resolves the discrepancy by using a four-dimensional formalism with experimentally constrained vertex functions, showing that soft form factors produce long-range pionic effects (∼1 fm) that justify including contributions at small transverse distances. The results enable definitive tests with the SeaQuest experiment, which will cover the range 0.1 ≤ x ≤ 0.6 and can rule out the pion cloud hypothesis.
