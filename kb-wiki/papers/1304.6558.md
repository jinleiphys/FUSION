# Eta-nuclear bound states revisited

> The strong energy dependence of the s-wave eta-N scattering amplitude at and below threshold, as evident in coupled-channels K-matrix fits and chiral models that incorporate the S11 N*(1535) resonance, is included self-consistently in eta-nuclear bound-state calculations. This approach, applied recently in calculations of kaonic atoms and Kbar-nuclear bound states, is found to impose stronger constraints than ever on the onset of eta-nuclear binding, with a minimum value of Re a_{eta N} approximately 0.9 fm required to accommodate an eta-4He bound state. Binding energies and widths of eta-nuclear states are calculated within several underlying eta-N models for nuclei across the periodic table, including eta-25Mg for which some evidence was proposed in a recent COSY experiment.

# Eta-nuclear bound states revisited

## Key claim
The strong energy dependence of the s-wave eta-N scattering amplitude below threshold, when included self-consistently in eta-nuclear bound-state calculations, imposes stronger constraints than previously recognized on the onset of eta-nuclear binding. A minimum value of Re a_{eta N} approximately 0.9 fm is required to accommodate an eta-4He bound state, which is substantially larger than the values used in earlier calculations.

## Method
The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently with a density- and energy-dependent optical potential derived from the in-medium eta-N scattering amplitude, incorporating Pauli correlations via the Ericson-Ericson multiple-scattering approach. The subthreshold energy shift was calculated including contributions from nucleon binding energy, eta binding energy, nucleon kinetic energy (approximated by the Fermi gas model), and the eta kinetic energy (approximated via the local density approximation). The self-consistent procedure iterated between the optical potential and the subthreshold energy argument until convergence, typically requiring about 5 cycles, and was embedded in a dynamical Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) scheme allowing for core polarization.

## Key numbers
- Minimum Re a_{eta N} approximately 0.9 fm required to accommodate an eta-4He bound state
- GW amplitude model: Re a_{eta N} = 0.96 fm, Im a_{eta N} = 0.26 fm (threshold values)
- M1 amplitude model: Re a_{eta N} = 0.22 fm, Im a_{eta N} = 0.24 fm (threshold values)
- M2 amplitude model: Re a_{eta N} = 0.38 fm, Im a_{eta N} = 0.20 fm (threshold values)
- GW model yields a 1s_eta bound state in 4He with binding energy 1.2 MeV and width 2.3 MeV
- Downward subthreshold energy shifts of up to approximately 55 MeV involved in the self-consistent calculations
- Experimental values for the proposed eta-25Mg bound state: B^{exp} = 13.1 +/- 1.6 MeV, Gamma^{exp} = 10.2 +/- 3.0 MeV
- Average bound-nucleon kinetic energy T_N = 23.0 MeV used in the Fermi gas approximation
- Average nucleon binding energy B_N approximately 8.5 MeV used in the subthreshold shift formula

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent methodology previously applied to K- nuclear bound states (Friedman and Gal, 2013) to the eta-nuclear system, and contrasts with earlier eta-nuclear calculations by Haider and Liu (1986, 2002) who used a fixed 30 MeV downward shift and weaker eta-N attraction with Re a_{eta N} less than 0.3 fm. The results contradict the earlier prediction that eta-nuclear binding begins around A=12, showing instead that models with threshold Re a_{eta N} less than 0.5 fm are unlikely to produce bound states in 12C. The findings enable more stringent tests of the proposed eta-25Mg bound state from the COSY-GEM experiment (Budzanowski et al., 2009) and motivate few-body calculations for light systems such as eta-3He and eta-4He.
