# Towards factorization with emergent scales for jets in dense media

> Employing the recently developed open quantum system Effective Field Theory framework, we investigate jet production and evolution in a dense nuclear medium in electron-ion/heavy-ion collisions. We confirm that the frequent monitoring of the jet by the medium leads to the emergence of a perturbative transverse momentum scale, often referred to as the saturation scale that necessitates further factorization to completely isolate the non-perturbative physics of the medium. A part of this goal is achieved in this paper by providing an operator definition for the broadening probability of a gluon in the medium within the Markovian approximations. We show that this distribution is (semi)universal; it depends on the angular measurement on the jet and probes both the large and small $x$ dynamics of the medium. We further elucidate all other contributions to non-perturbative physics suggesting that the parameterization of non-perturbative physics is more complex than previously assumed and outline steps required for a complete factorization of the jet production cross section.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that frequent monitoring of a jet by a dense nuclear medium leads to the emergence of a perturbative transverse momentum scale (the saturation scale), which requires further factorization to isolate non-perturbative medium physics; however, the parameterization of non-perturbative physics is more complex than previously assumed, and the current jet broadening parameterization in terms of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) is only an approximation recoverable in a certain limit.

## Method

The authors employ an open quantum system Effective Field Theory framework based on Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) with Glauber extension, previously formulated by Mehtar-Tani and Vaidya (2024), to study jet production and evolution in a dense nuclear medium. They compute the single subjet collinear-soft function for multiple interactions of the jet with the medium, working in the Markovian limit where the gluon formation time is much smaller than the medium size \(L\), and derive an operator definition for the broadening probability of a gluon in the medium. The analysis focuses on the unresolved jet regime where the jet acts as a single coherent color source for collinear-soft radiation.

## Key numbers

* The emergent medium scale \(Q_{\rm med} \equiv \langle k_\perp^2\rangle \sim \hat q L\) varies in the range 1–3 GeV for typical parameters \(\hat q \sim 1\)–2 GeV\(^2\)/fm and medium length \(L \sim 2\)–5 fm.
* The hierarchy considered is \(p_T \gg p_T R \gg Q_{\rm med} \gtrsim T \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), with jet \(p_T R \sim 10\)–100 GeV and medium temperature \(T \sim 0.5\) GeV.
* The Debye mass \(m_D \sim gT\) is treated as comparable to \(T\) since \(g \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\).
* For a dilute medium with single interaction, the transverse momentum transfer \(\bfq \sim m_D\) and \(q^- \sim m_D/R\).
* The BFKL resummation exponent involves \(\chi(n,r)\) with evolution from rapidity scale \(\nu_0 \sim k_\perp^2/m_D \sim m_D\) to \(\nu_f \sim \epsilon_L\).

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework for jet production in heavy-ion collisions developed by Mehtar-Tani and Vaidya (2024), which was previously limited to dilute media with single jet-medium interactions. The paper generalizes this to dense media with multiple interactions, confirming the emergence of a saturation scale previously observed by Casalderrey-Solana and collaborators (2011). The results enable a systematic separation of perturbative and non-perturbative physics in jet quenching, though the authors note that complete factorization requires further work to isolate all non-perturbative contributions beyond the broadening probability.
