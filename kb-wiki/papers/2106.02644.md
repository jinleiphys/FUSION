# Bayesian model-selection of neutron star equation of state using multi-messenger observations

> Measurement of macroscopic properties of neutron stars, whether in binary or in an isolated system, provides us a key opportunity to place a stringent constraint on its equation of state. In this paper, we perform Bayesian model-selection on a wide variety of neutron star equation of state using multi-messenger observations. In particular, (i) we use the mass and tidal deformability measurement from two binary neutron star merger event, GW170817 and GW190425; (ii) simultaneous mass-radius measurement of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 by NICER collaboration, while the latter has been analyzed by joint NICER/radio/XMM-Newton collaboration. Among the 31 equations of state considered in this analysis, we are able to rule out different variants of MS1 family, SKI5, H4, and WFF1 EoSs decisively, which are either extremely stiff or soft equations of state. The most preferred equation of state model turns out to be AP3 (or MPA1), which predicts the radius and dimensionless tidal deformability of a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ neutron star to be 12.10 (12.50) km and 393 (513) respectively.

# Bayesian model-selection of neutron star equation of state using multi-messenger observations

## Key claim
Among 31 nuclear-physics motivated equations of state, the AP3 (or MPA1) model is the most preferred by current multi-messenger data, but the result depends on which NICER analysis is used: AP3 is preferred when using Riley et al. (2019, 2021) data, while MPA1 is preferred when using Miller et al. (2019, 2021) data, reflecting a 0.4 km systematic difference in the inferred radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.

## Method
The author computed Bayesian evidences for each of 31 equations of state by marginalizing over neutron star masses with a flat prior between 1 solar mass and the EoS-specific maximum mass, using nested sampling (Pymultinest). Likelihoods were modeled as Gaussian kernel density estimates from the posterior samples of: (i) mass and tidal deformability from GW170817 and GW190425; (ii) simultaneous mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER; and (iii) the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (already incorporated in the NICER analysis). The odds ratio between any two EoSs was taken as the product of Bayes factors across datasets, assuming equal prior probability for each EoS.

## Key numbers
- The most preferred EoS (AP3) predicts a radius of 12.10 km and dimensionless tidal deformability of 393 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.
- The alternative most preferred EoS (MPA1) predicts a radius of 12.50 km and dimensionless tidal deformability of 513 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.
- EoSs with radius >= 13.69 km and tidal deformability >= 897 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star are decisively ruled out (log10 Bayes factor <= -2 relative to AP3/MPA1).
- The softest EoS WFF1, with radius 10.42 km and tidal deformability 153 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, is also decisively ruled out.
- The 31 EoSs span radii from 10.42 km to 15.07 km and tidal deformabilities from 153 to 1622 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.
- The heaviest pulsar mass used is 2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses at 1 sigma confidence.

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian model-selection studies (LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2019; Ghosh 2021; Pacilio 2021) that used only gravitational-wave observations, by adding NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. The inclusion of multi-messenger data decisively rules out WFF1, which was among the most preferred EoSs in the GW-only analyses, demonstrating the power of combining electromagnetic and gravitational-wave constraints. The results provide a benchmark for future EoS model building and numerical simulations, and the methodology can incorporate any future measurements of neutron star properties from gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations.
