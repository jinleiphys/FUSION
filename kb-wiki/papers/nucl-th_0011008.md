# $σ$ Exchange in the NN Interaction within the Chiral Unitary Approach

> We study the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the isoscalar-scalar channel using t he chiral unitary approach. The $t$-matrix of the pion-pion scattering in this c hannel is summed up to all orders using the B-S equation. We find that the calcu lated results at long distances are close to those of the $σ$-exchange inte raction. In addition, there appears a shorter range repulsion in this channel.

# σ Exchange in the NN Interaction within the Chiral Unitary Approach

## Key claim

The authors show that a nonperturbative (unitary) treatment of the two-pion exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel produces an intermediate-range attraction in the NN potential that resembles conventional σ exchange, but also generates a shorter-range repulsion. This result differs qualitatively from the perturbative chiral approach of Ref. [9], which found only a very weak repulsion in this channel.

## Method

The authors use the Bethe-Salpeter equation to sum the ππ scattering t-matrix in the isoscalar-scalar channel to all orders, starting from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian. The resulting unitary ππ amplitude, which produces a σ pole around 450 MeV, is then used to construct the NN potential via two-pion exchange diagrams with intermediate nucleon and Δ baryon states. The vertex functions are regularized with monopole form factors (Λ = 1.1 GeV), and the potential is Fourier-transformed to coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- The σ pole from the unitary ππ amplitude appears at approximately 450 − i225 MeV, consistent with results from the inverse amplitude method (442 − i225 MeV in Ref. [17]) and the N/D method (445 − i221 MeV in Ref. [18]).
- The empirical σNN coupling used for comparison is g²_σNN/4π = 5.69 (g_σNN = 8.46) from Ref. [1], with a σ mass of about 450 MeV.
- The vertex function Ṽ(0) is approximately 0.10 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹, leading to a predicted σNN coupling of order g_σNN ≈ 5 from the qualitative analysis.
- The NN potential shows moderate attraction beyond r = 0.9 fm and a repulsion of about 200 MeV at r = 0.5 fm, with the repulsion reaching order 1 GeV at very short distances.
- Changing the form factor cutoff Λ from 1.1 GeV to 1.2 GeV changes the attraction from 10 to 15 MeV.
- The function t_NN(q) develops a spurious pole around 2060 MeV, outside the model's region of validity.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the NN interaction, following the line of Ref. [8] and Ref. [9] which studied peripheral NN partial waves using perturbative chiral methods. The authors find that the perturbative treatment of Ref. [9] (which gave only a very weak repulsion in the σ channel) is insufficient, and that the nonperturbative summation of ππ interactions is necessary even at large distances. The results contradict the conventional σ-exchange picture (Ref. [1]), which always gives attraction, and instead produce a short-range repulsion in the scalar-isoscalar channel. This work enables future chiral approaches to the NN interaction to incorporate the unitary treatment of the ππ subsystem, which the authors argue is essential for obtaining proper results across all distance ranges.
