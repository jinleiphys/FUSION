# Single Inclusive Hadron Production in DIS at Small $x$: Next to Leading Order Corrections

> We calculate the one-loop corrections to single inclusive hadron production in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small $x$ in the forward rapidity region using the Color Glass Condensate formalism. We show that the divergent parts of the next to leading order (NLO) corrections either cancel among each other or lead to $x$ (rapidity) evolution of the leading order (LO) dipole cross section according to the JIMWLK evolution equation and DGLAP evolution of the parton-hadron fragmentation function. The remaining finite parts constitute the NLO ($α_s$) corrections to the LO single inclusive hadron production cross section in DIS at small $x$.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the one-loop (NLO) corrections to single inclusive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering at small $x\) in the forward rapidity region, showing that all divergent parts either cancel or are absorbed into JIMWLK evolution of the dipole cross section and DGLAP evolution of the fragmentation function, leaving finite \(\alpha_s\) corrections to the leading-order cross section.

## Method
The calculation starts from the authors' previous NLO results for dihadron production in DIS (Bergabo and Jalilian-Marian, 2022) and integrates out one final-state parton (the quark) to obtain single inclusive hadron production. The real corrections come from squaring four diagrams (Fig. 1) and the virtual corrections from multiplying ten diagrams (Fig. 2) with the leading-order amplitude; the resulting expressions are integrated over the quark's transverse momentum and rapidity. Cancellations among UV, soft, and collinear divergences are demonstrated, with collinear divergences absorbed into the parton-hadron fragmentation function and rapidity divergences absorbed into JIMWLK evolution of the dipole.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculation for dihadron production in DIS (Bergabo and Jalilian-Marian, 2022) to the single inclusive hadron case, which is more directly comparable to experimental measurements at a future Electron-Ion Collider. It builds on leading-order CGC calculations for SIDIS by Marquet (2009) and Kovchegov (2001), and on the broader program of NLO corrections in the CGC framework including work by Chirilli (2011), Balitsky (2013), and Boussarie (2017). The result enables quantitative studies of hadron transverse momentum spectra at small \(x\) with NLO precision, which is needed to distinguish saturation dynamics from other QCD effects.
