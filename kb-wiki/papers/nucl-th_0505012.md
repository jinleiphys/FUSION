# Landau-Ginzburg method applied to finite fermion systems: Pairing in Nuclei

> Given the spectrum of a Hamiltonian, a methodology is developed which employs the Landau-Ginsburg method for characterizing phase transitions in infinite systems to identify phase transition remnants in finite fermion systems. As a first application of our appproach we discuss pairing in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper develops a methodology that uses the Landau-Ginzburg theory, originally designed for phase transitions in infinite systems, to identify remnants of phase transitions in finite fermion systems. The authors explicitly acknowledge that "strictly speaking it is incorrect to speak of phase transitions in finite systems," but argue that remnants appear as peaked structure in the specific heat rather than discontinuities.

## Method

The authors empirically construct the canonical partition function for four even-even nuclei ($^{20}$Ne, $^{48}$Ca, $^{88}$Sr, $^{208}$Pb) using measured bound-state energy levels and a continuum level density parameterized by the Gilbert-Cameron prescription. From the partition function they compute the specific heat as a function of temperature, then apply Landau-Ginzburg theory to fit the specific heat in both the condensed (low-temperature) and uncondensed (high-temperature) phases. The magnitude of the discontinuity remnant at the critical temperature is compared with a simple analytical pairing calculation corrected for finite-size effects using Dirichlet boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb: empirical discontinuity $\Delta = 7.15$, Landau-Ginzburg prediction $\Delta_L = 10.22$, finite-size corrected $\Delta_L^{finite} = 10.27$, critical temperature $T_c = 0.66$ MeV
- For $^{88}$Sr: empirical discontinuity $\Delta = 22.67$, $\Delta_L = 14.81$, $\Delta_L^{finite} = 14.92$, $T_c = 0.72$ MeV
- For $^{48}$Ca: empirical discontinuity $\Delta = 21.59$, $\Delta_L = 18.02$, $\Delta_L^{finite} = 18.27$, $T_c = 1.0$ MeV
- For $^{20}$Ne: empirical discontinuity $\Delta = 12.95$, $\Delta_L = 20.88$, $\Delta_L^{finite} = 21.46$, $T_c = 2.11$ MeV
- The specific heat coefficients from Landau-Ginzburg fits: $^{208}$Pb $k_1 = 10.83$, $k_2 = 6.75$; $^{88}$Sr $k_1 = 22.94$, $k_2 = 10.79$; $^{48}$Ca $k_1 = 17.10$, $k_2 = 6.80$; $^{20}$Ne $k_1 = 6.53$, $k_2 = 3.74$
- For an infinite system, the analytical discontinuity ratio is $\frac{12}{7\zeta(3)} = 1.43$
- The matching point parameterization: $U_x = 2.5 + \frac{150}{A}$ on average, with upper limit $U_x = 2.7 + \frac{200}{A}$ and lower limit $U_x = 2.1 + \frac{120}{A}$

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical evidence for a pairing phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter at $T_c \approx 8$ MeV from finite-temperature extensions of the semi-empirical mass formula (Dieperink et al., 1993; Rijken et al., 2001) and Landau-Ginzburg treatments of pairing (Lifshitz and Pitaevskii, 1980). It addresses the question of whether remnants of such transitions survive in finite nuclei, a topic previously studied in SU(2)xSU(2) model systems (Ferrer et al., 1979; Plastino and Rios, 1985) and in the context of collective-to-non-collective transitions (Meng et al., 1989). The methodology enables identification of pairing transitions in finite nuclei where exact shell model calculations have found them difficult to identify (Dean and Hjorth-Jensen, 2003), and can be applied to any finite fermion system with a known spectrum.
