# Magnetized strange quark matter in a quasiparticle description

> The quasiparticle model is extended to investigate the properties of strange quark matter in a strong magnetic field at finite densities. For the density-dependent quark mass, self-consistent thermodynamic treatment is obtained with an additional effective bag parameter, which depends not only on the density but also on the magnetic field strength. The magnetic field makes strange quark matter more stable energetically when the magnetic field strength is less than a critical value of the order $10^7$ Gauss depending on the QCD scale $Λ$. Instead of being a monotonic function of the density for the QCD scale parameter $Λ>126$ MeV, the effective bag function has a maximum near $0.3\sim 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$. The influence of the magnetic field and the QCD scale parameter on the stiffness of the equation of state of the magnetized strange quark matter and the possible maximum mass of strange stars are discussed

## Key claim

The quasiparticle model is extended to show that a strong magnetic field makes strange quark matter more stable energetically only when the field strength is below a critical value of order 10^17 Gauss (depending on the QCD scale parameter Lambda); above that critical field, the magnetic field energy itself raises the energy per baryon.

## Method

The authors extend the quasiparticle model, where effective quark masses depend on chemical potential via a hard-dense-loop inspired formula, to include a constant magnetic field along the z-axis. Landau quantization is introduced for charged quarks, and a self-consistent thermodynamic treatment is obtained by introducing an effective bag function B* that depends on both baryon density and magnetic field strength, derived from the requirement that pressure be stationary with respect to changes in the effective mass. The equation of state is then computed for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated strange quark matter using both constant and running coupling constants, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations for strange quark stars.

## Key numbers

* For QCD scale parameter Lambda = 120 MeV, the critical magnetic field strength B_m^c equals 2.15 x 10^17 G; for Lambda = 180 MeV, B_m^c equals 2.34 x 10^17 G.
* When the magnetic energy scale sqrt(e B_m) approaches about 76.9 MeV, the field energy contribution becomes considerable and the energy per baryon increases with field strength.
* For QCD scale parameter Lambda > 126 MeV, the effective bag function B* is non-monotonic and reaches a maximum at a baryon density in the range 0.3 to 0.4 fm^{-3}.
* For a polarization parameter xi = 0.1, the free energy per baryon is enlarged by 0.8% at baryon densities n_B > 0.2 fm^{-3}.
* The maximum masses obtained from the mass-radius relation do not reach the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (about two solar masses).

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model previously applied to non-magnetized strange quark matter by Schertler (1997) and Wen (2010) to include strong magnetic fields, complementing studies using the MIT bag model (Chakrabarty, 1996), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Ebert, 2003; Frol, 2010; Faya, 2011; Avan, 2011), and the linear sigma model with Polyakov loops (Mizher, 2010). The finding that the maximum mass of magnetized strange quark stars in the ordinary phase cannot explain the observed two-solar-mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230 suggests that additional phases such as superconductivity or mixed phases may be necessary for future studies.
