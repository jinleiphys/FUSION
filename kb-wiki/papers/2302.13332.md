# Practicing carpe diem in the journey of studying physics: A brief review of the scientific contribution of Ru-Keng Su

> We briefly review the scientific contributions of the late Prof. Ru-Keng Su in his academic life. In the area of intermediate and high-energy nuclear physics, Su explored various topics in high-energy nuclear physics and particle physics, inclusively about the finite temperature field theory, effective models for nuclear and quark matter, soliton, and quasiparticle models, among others. In gravity and cosmology, Su's research primarily embraces black hole thermodynamics, quasinormal modes, cosmological microwave background radiation, modified theories of gravity, and AdS/CFT correspondence and its applications. Besides, many aspects of Su's distinguished impact on the Chinese academic physics community are discussed. We also summarize the biographical and academic career of Su. This article is an elaborated version of the memorial article that will be published in \href{https://www.mdpi.com/journal/symmetry}{\it symmetry}.

## Key claim

This paper reviews the scientific contributions of Ru-Keng Su across intermediate/high-energy nuclear physics and general relativity/cosmology, but as a memorial review it does not present a single new result; instead it surveys Su's published work and his role in developing the Chinese physics community.

## Method

The authors compiled a narrative review of Su's approximately 200 publications, organized by subfield (finite-temperature field theory, effective models for nuclear and quark matter, black hole thermodynamics, quasinormal modes, cosmology). They also summarize his textbooks, teaching awards, and biographical details, drawing on personal recollections as his former students.

## Key numbers

- Su published more than 200 academic papers in physics journals.
- He received the second prize for Scientific and Technological Development from the Ministry of Education three times: in 1988, 1992, and 1996.
- He won the second prize in the Natural Science Award from the Chinese Academy of Sciences in 1999.
- He received the second prize in the Shanghai Science and Technology Achievement Award in 2003.
- He cultivated 11 Ph.D. students and dozens of master's students.
- His textbook "Quantum Mechanics" was selected as a national premier course in 2004 and included in the first batch of "Public Courses of National Excellent Resource" in 2016.

## Context

This review memorializes the career of Ru-Keng Su, whose work extended finite-temperature field theory (e.g., using generalized Bogoliubov transformations for soliton solutions in phi^4 theory) and applied effective models such as the Skyrme model and quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model to nuclear matter phase transitions. In gravity, Su's group built on black hole thermodynamics studies by Cai (1995) and Wang (1998) and later contributed to observational constraints on holographic dark energy models using supernova, WMAP, and baryon acoustic oscillation data. The review enables future researchers to trace the lineage of Chinese theoretical physics groups working at the intersection of nuclear physics and cosmology.
