# Neutron/Proton Structure Function Ratio at Large x

> We re-examine the large-x neutron/proton structure function ratio extracted from the latest deuteron data, taking into account the most recent developments in the treatment of Fermi motion, binding and nucleon off-shell effects in the deuteron. Our findings suggest that as x->1 the ratio of the neutron to proton structure functions (F_2^n/F_2^p) is consistent with the perturbative QCD expectation of 3/7, but larger than the value of 1/4 obtained in earlier analyses.

## Key claim

The neutron-to-proton structure function ratio (F2^n/F2^p) as x approaches 1 is consistent with the perturbative QCD expectation of 3/7, but only when nuclear binding effects in the deuteron are properly included in the analysis; earlier analyses that ignored or approximated binding effects gave the lower value of 1/4.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing SLAC and EMC deuteron and proton deep-inelastic scattering data using a convolution formalism that includes Fermi motion, nucleon binding, and off-mass-shell effects in the deuteron, following the approach of Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1994). They extracted the neutron structure function by subtracting the smeared proton contribution from the deuteron data, iteratively correcting for the neutron smearing factor, and compared results obtained with off-shell (binding-included) versus on-shell (Fermi-motion-only) deuteron models. The Paris nucleon-nucleon potential was used for the deuteron wave function in both cases.

## Key numbers

- The combined effects of binding, Fermi motion, and nucleon off-shellness on the calculated ratio F2^D/F2^N were about 4-5% at x ~ 0.7.
- The non-convolution correction term delta^(off) F2^D was found to be less than 1-2% for most x in realistic potential models.
- The reanalyzed SLAC data points were at an average Q^2 of approximately 12 GeV^2.
- The perturbative QCD expectation for F2^n/F2^p as x -> 1 is 3/7, compared to the SU(6) symmetry value of 2/3 and the scalar diquark dominance value of 1/4.
- The d/u ratio as x -> 1 approaches approximately 1/5 in the perturbative QCD model, compared to zero in the scalar diquark dominance model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Close (1973) and Carlitz (1975), who predicted F2^n/F2^p -> 1/4 based on scalar diquark dominance, and the perturbative QCD approach of Farrar and Jackson (1975) and Brodsky, Burkardt, and Schmidt (1995), who predicted 3/7. The results contradict the extraction of Liuti and Gross (1995), who used a non-relativistic expansion formula that overestimates the convolution results above x ~ 0.7. The findings enable more reliable extraction of neutron structure functions from deuteron data and have implications for spin-dependent structure functions, where the same nuclear corrections must be applied to g1^n and g1^3He data to discriminate between competing models.
