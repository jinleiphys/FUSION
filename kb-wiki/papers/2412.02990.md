# General Relativistic Hartree-Fock Calculations for Neutron Star

> We investigate the global structures of neutron stars within the framework of general relativity, treating the entire star as a quantum-degenerate system. Rather than relying on the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation, we solve the Einstein-Cartan (EC) field equations self-consistently, incorporating the energy-momentum tensor contributions from neutrons. Neutron wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation in a curved spacetime with both torsion and curvature effects. Given that neutron stars contain about 10^57 particles, we adopt a scaled h-bar approach to efficiently describe the quantum state of highly degenerate system.

## Key claim
The paper claims that neutron stars can be modeled as fully quantum-degenerate systems using a General Relativistic Hartree-Fock (GRHF) method, rather than the classical fluid approach of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation. The hedge is that this first trial calculation focuses only on neutrons and neglects electromagnetic and nuclear interactions, as well as the contributions of protons and electrons.

## Method
The authors solve the Einstein-Cartan field equations self-consistently with the Dirac equation for neutrons in a curved spacetime that includes both torsion and curvature. To handle the approximately 10^57 particles in a neutron star, they employ a scaled Planck constant (S\(\hbar\)M) approach, where \(\hbar_{*} = \gamma \hbar_{\star}\) with \(\hbar_{\star}=10^{18}\hbar\), allowing a single Slater determinant to represent an assemblage of \(\gamma^{3} N_{\star}\) fermions. The iterative procedure involves: (1) solving the Dirac equation in a gravitational field using the scaled \(\hbar\), (2) calculating the energy-momentum tensor and spin current from the wave functions, and (3) solving the Einstein-Cartan field equations to obtain updated gravitational potentials for the next cycle.

## Key numbers
- Neutron star mass and radius used for illustration: 1.5 \(M_{\odot}\) and \(R=12\) km.
- Number of nucleons in a typical neutron star: about \(1700 \times N_{\star}\), where \(N_{\star} = 10^{54}\).
- Average nucleon density: 0.25 fm\(^{-3}\), which is greater than the nuclear density of 0.17 fm\(^{-3}\).
- Fermi energy of neutrons: about 75 MeV.
- Time scale of gravitational field variation: approximately 10 seconds to 10 milliseconds, which is significantly longer than the inverse of the average energy spacing of approximately \(5 \times 10^{-20} / \gamma^{3}\) seconds.

## Context
This work extends the traditional TOV formalism (To39, OV39), which treats neutron stars as classical fluids or Fermi gases, by incorporating quantum features such as phase relations from the anti-symmetrization of fermion wave functions. The GRHF method with scaled \(\hbar\) enables the study of global neutron star structure without relying on a local equation of state, potentially avoiding the singularities predicted by TOV models. Future work will include protons, electrons, and electromagnetic and nuclear interactions to build a more complete model.
