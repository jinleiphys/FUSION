# Photoproduction of hidden-bottom pentaquark and related topics

> Due to the discovery of the hidden-charm pentaquark $P_c$ states by the LHCb collaboration, the interests on the candidates of hidden-bottom pentaquark $P_b$ states are increasing. They are anticipated to exist as the analogues of the $P_c$ states in the bottom sector and predicted by many models. We give an exploration of searching for a typical $P_b$ in the $γp \to Υp$ reaction, which shows a promising potential to observe it at an electron-ion collider. The possibility of searching for $P_b$ in open-bottom channels are also briefly discussed. Meanwhile, the $t$-channel non-resonant contribution, which in fact covers several interesting topics at low energies, is systematically investigated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a hidden-bottom pentaquark \(P_b\) state, the bottom-sector analogue of the LHCb \(P_c\) states, can be observed in the \(\gamma p \to \Upsilon p\) reaction at a future electron-ion collider, provided the branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(P_b \to \Upsilon p)\) is larger than about 1.0%; if the branching fraction is smaller, the \(P_b\) may still be detectable through open-bottom channels such as \(B^{(*)}\Lambda_b\).

## Method

The authors model the non-resonant \(t\)-channel background for \(\gamma p \to \Upsilon p\) using several approaches: an empirical deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) formula, a two-gluon exchange model (Brodsky, Chudakov, Hoyer, Laget), a parametrization by Gryniuk and Vanderhaeghen (GV), and the soft dipole Pomeron model (Martynov, Predazzi, Prokudin). They then add a resonant \(s\)-channel \(P_b\) contribution using a Breit-Wigner formula, assuming a mass of 11.12 GeV, widths of 30 MeV (narrow) and 300 MeV (wide), and a spin \(J=1/2\). The branching ratio \(\mathcal{B}(P_b \to \gamma p)\) is related to \(\mathcal{B}(P_b \to \Upsilon p)\) via vector-meson dominance, and the signal is compared to the non-resonant background over the energy range of the proposed Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC).

## Key numbers

* The non-resonant \(\gamma p \to \Upsilon p\) cross section at EicC energies is around 0.01 to 0.02 nb (from the DVMP empirical formula) or varies rapidly near threshold (from the soft dipole Pomeron model).
* The peak resonant cross section for a narrow \(P_b\) (width 30 MeV) with \(\mathcal{B}(P_b \to \Upsilon p) = 5\%\) is around 0.1 nb.
* The DVMP fit parameters are \(\alpha = 0.31 \pm 0.02\) and \(\beta = 0.13 \pm 0.01\), giving \(\delta(Q^2=0) = 0.89 \pm 0.05\).
* The normalization for the DVMP formula is \(\mathcal{N} = 2.62 \pm 0.38\); for the GV parametrization it is \(\mathcal{N} = 0.014 \pm 0.002\).
* The slope parameter for the two-gluon exchange model is \(b = 1.13\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The dilepton width \(\Gamma(\Upsilon \to e^+e^-) = 1.34\) keV.
* The relative uncertainty of the bottom quark mass is about 1.0%, and of \(\alpha_s\) at the \(\Upsilon\) scale is about 3.7%.
* For the EicC with an integrated luminosity of 50 fb\(^{-1}\), about \(5 \times 10^4\) signal events of \(ep \to e P_b \to e \Upsilon p\) are expected.

## Context

This work extends the search for hidden-charm pentaquarks \(P_c\) (discovered by LHCb, Aaij et al. 2015, 2019) to the bottom sector, motivated by heavy-quark flavor symmetry. It builds on prior photoproduction proposals for \(P_c\) by Wang et al. (2015), Karliner and Rosner (2015), and Kubarovsky and Voloshin (2015), and on the GlueX upper limit for \(P_c\) photoproduction (Ali et al. 2019). The paper enables future experimental searches at the proposed EicC and other electron-ion colliders (US-EIC, LHeC), and also connects to studies of the \(\Upsilon p\) scattering length and the trace-anomaly contribution to the proton mass (Hatta and Yang 2018).
