# Meson-hyperon couplings in the bound-state approach to the Skyrme model

> Strong pion and kaon couplings to hyperons are given in the bound-state approach to the Skyrme model.

## Key claim
The bound-state approach to the Skyrme model can reproduce empirical meson-hyperon coupling constants, but the quality of the agreement depends on the choice of model parameters: the parameter set with the physical pion decay constant (Set II) yields better agreement than the set tuned only to nucleon and Delta masses (Set I).

## Method
The authors calculate kaon and pion coupling constants to hyperons by defining them as matrix elements of the meson source terms sandwiched between single-baryon states, following the LSZ reduction formalism. The hyperons are treated as bound states of an antikaon to an SU(2) Skyrme soliton: positive-parity hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Sigma(1385)) arise from P-wave kaon bound states, while Lambda(1405) arises from an S-wave bound state. The coupling constants are extracted from the residues at the poles of the Born terms in the scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- For the kaon coupling to Lambda: |f_{Lambda p K-}/sqrt(4pi)| = 1.35 (Set I) and 0.92 (Set II), compared to empirical values 0.89 +/- 0.10 and 0.94 +/- 0.03
- For the kaon coupling to Sigma: |f_{Sigma0 p K-}/sqrt(4pi)| = 0.45 (Set I) and 0.31 (Set II), compared to empirical values <0.43 +/- 0.07 and 0.25 +/- 0.05
- For the pion coupling to hyperons: f_{Sigma Lambda pi}/sqrt(4pi) = 0.20 +/- 0.01 and f_{Sigma Sigma pi}/sqrt(4pi) = 0.21 +/- 0.02 (empirical values)
- For the Lambda(1405) kaon coupling: G_{Lambda* p K-}/sqrt(4pi) = -1.45 (Set I) and -0.72 (Set II), compared to phenomenological values 0.75 and 0.58
- Model parameters: Set I uses f_pi = 54 MeV and e = 4.84; Set II uses f_pi = 93 MeV and e = 4.0

## Context
This work extends the bound-state approach to strangeness proposed by Callan and Klebanov (1985), which had previously been applied to hyperon masses and magnetic moments by various authors including Cohen, Kaplan, and collaborators. The authors improve upon earlier attempts to obtain kaon coupling constants in this framework by Gobbi (1994) and Schat (1995) by providing a simpler and more transparent definition of the vertices. The results enable future calculations of meson-baryon scattering amplitudes involving strangeness exchange processes, which had not been fully developed within this approach. The method is also applicable to the SU(3) collective-coordinate method, though that would be more involved.
