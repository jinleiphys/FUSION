# omega -> pi pi decay in nuclear medium

> We calculate the width for the omega -> pi pi decay in nuclear medium. Chiral dynamics and low-density approximation are used. At densities around twice the nuclear saturation density we estimate the partial width for the decay of the longitudinal mode to be of the order of a few tens of MeV, and for the transverse mode a few times less.

## Key claim

The in-medium partial width for ω → ππ decay is of the order of a few tens of MeV for the longitudinal mode and a few times less for the transverse mode at densities around twice nuclear saturation density, which is much smaller than the value of a few hundred MeV previously estimated by Wolf, Friman, and Soyeur (1998) because additional diagrams beyond the ω-σ mixing mechanism provide strong cancellations.

## Method

The authors used chiral dynamics and a low-density expansion to calculate the ω → ππ amplitude in nuclear matter, evaluating one-nucleon-loop diagrams with both pseudovector and pseudoscalar πN couplings. The in-medium nucleon propagator was decomposed into free and density-dependent parts, and the amplitude was decomposed into Lorentz-invariant coefficients A, B, and C. The decay width was computed separately for longitudinally and transversely polarized ω mesons using projection tensors, with the nucleon mass reduced to 70% of its vacuum value to account for typical in-medium effects.

## Key numbers

- At ρ_B = 2ρ_0 (twice nuclear saturation density) and |q| around 400 MeV: Γ^T,PV ≈ 5 MeV for the transverse mode and Γ^L,PV ≈ 25 MeV for the longitudinal mode
- The ratio of longitudinal to transverse width is approximately 5 for physical parameter values (Γ^L/Γ^T ≈ 5)
- The ωN coupling constant used: g_ω = 10
- The pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_πN ≈ 45 MeV
- The expressions scale approximately as M^(-8), making the 70% reduction of nucleon mass a very important effect

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Wolf, Friman, and Soyeur (1998), who found a huge partial width of a few hundred MeV from ω-σ mixing alone, by showing that additional triangle diagrams provide cancellations that reduce the width to a few tens of MeV. The results are relevant for understanding in-medium modifications of vector mesons observed in relativistic heavy-ion experiments such as CERES and HELIOS, and the authors note that final-state interactions could further modify the results, leaving this extension for future work.
