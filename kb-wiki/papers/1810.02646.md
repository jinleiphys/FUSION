# How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory

> We discuss the connection between the perturbative and non-perturbative renormalization and related conceptual issues in the few-nucleon sector of the low-energy effective field theory of the strong interactions. General arguments are supported by examples from effective theories with and without pions as dynamical degrees of freedom. A quantum mechanical potential with explicitly specified short- and long-range parts is considered as an "underlying fundamental theory" and the corresponding effective field theory potential is constructed. Further, the problem of the effective field theoretical renormalization of the Skornyakov-Ter-Martyrosian equation is revisited.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the common "non-perturbative renormalization" procedure of making cutoff-regularized integral equations finite by tuning a finite number of contact interactions does **not** constitute proper effective field theory renormalization, because the resulting non-perturbative expression, when expanded in powers of \(\hbar\), fails to reproduce the renormalized perturbative series of Feynman diagrams.

## Method

The authors analyze the renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering in pionless EFT and in EFT with one-pion-exchange, using cutoff regularization. They compare the properly renormalized amplitude (obtained via BPHZ subtraction or counterterm technique) with the "non-perturbatively renormalized" amplitude obtained by fixing a finite number of contact interactions to reproduce low-energy observables while taking the cutoff to infinity. They also construct a toy model with a non-singular underlying potential whose long-range part behaves like \(1/r^3\) and compare its exact phase shifts to those from the corresponding LO EFT potential with a single contact interaction.

## Key numbers

- The toy model underlying potential uses parameters \(\alpha = -36\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(M = 0.1385\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_1 = 0.75\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and \(m_2 = 1.15\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- For the pionless EFT contact potential at NLO, the properly renormalized amplitude is given by \(T_{\mathrm{NLO}}(q) = (c_R + 2 q^2 c_{2R}) / [1 - \hbar (I(q^2) - I(-\mu^2)) (c_R + 2 q^2 c_{2R})]\).
- The "non-perturbatively renormalized" amplitude for the same potential, when expanded in \(\hbar\), contains terms of order \(\hbar\) with positive powers of the cutoff \(\Lambda\), e.g., a term \(2 a^4 \Lambda q^4 r_e^2 / m\).
- For the toy model, LO EFT phase shifts are reasonably well described for cutoffs around the optimal value \(\sim 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}\), but the description degrades as the cutoff increases, and the removed-cutoff limit of the singular long-range potential alone strongly deviates from the exact phase shifts.

## Context

This work extends the debate on renormalization in few-nucleon chiral EFT, following the foundational papers by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and the subsequent controversies reviewed by Bedaque and van Kolck (2002), Epelbaum et al. (2009), and Machleidt and Entem (2011). The authors directly critique the "non-perturbative renormalization" approach applied to the one-pion-exchange potential by Nogga et al. (2005) and to the Skornyakov-Ter-Martyrosian equation by Bedaque et al. (1999). The paper clarifies that proper EFT renormalization requires that the non-perturbative sum of diagrams, when expanded in \(\hbar\), reproduces the renormalized perturbative series, and that power counting should be applied to renormalized couplings, not to bare ones. This enables a consistent framework for treating singular potentials in few-body EFT without introducing spurious bound states or violating the Wigner bound.
