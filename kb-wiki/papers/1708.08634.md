# Analysis of corrections to the eikonal approximation

> Various corrections to the eikonal approximations are studied for two- and three-body nuclear collisions with the goal to extend the range of validity of this approximation to beam energies of 10 MeV/nucleon. Wallace's correction does not improve much the elastic-scattering cross sections obtained at the usual eikonal approximation. On the contrary, a semiclassical approximation that substitutes the impact parameter by a complex distance of closest approach computed with the projectile-target optical potential efficiently corrects the eikonal approximation. This opens the possibility to analyze data measured down to 10 MeV/nucleon within eikonal-like reaction models.

## Key claim

Wallace's correction to the eikonal approximation does not significantly improve elastic-scattering cross sections at low energies, whereas a semiclassical correction that replaces the impact parameter with a complex distance of closest approach computed from the projectile-target optical potential efficiently extends the eikonal approximation's validity down to 10 MeV/nucleon, provided a systematic impact-parameter cutoff is applied to address convergence issues.

## Method

The authors compared several corrections to the standard eikonal approximation for elastic scattering of 10Be and 11Be projectiles off a 12C target at 20 and 10 MeV/nucleon. They tested Wallace's first-order perturbative correction, a semiclassical correction using a complex distance of closest approach derived from the optical potential, and a hybrid combining Wallace's correction with a Coulomb-only semiclassical shift. For the three-body case (11Be as a 10Be core plus valence neutron), they implemented the semiclassical correction in two ways: shifting the projectile center-of-mass impact parameter, and shifting each fragment's impact parameter separately. Results were benchmarked against exact partial-wave calculations for the two-body case and against Continuum-Discretised Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations for the three-body case.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 20 MeV/nucleon and 10 MeV/nucleon for both 10Be+12C and 11Be+12C systems
- The 10Be-12C optical potential parameters: V_R = 250.0 MeV, R_R = 3.053 fm, a_R = 0.788 fm; W_I = 247.9 MeV, R_I = 2.982 fm, a_I = 0.709 fm (from SMC86, rescaled for mass number)
- The n-12C Koning-Delaroche potential parameters: V_R = 46.9395 MeV, R_R = 2.5798 fm, a_R = 0.676 fm; W_I = 1.8256 MeV, R_I = 2.5798 fm, a_I = 0.676 fm; W_D = 28.6339 MeV, R_D = 2.9903 fm, a_D = 0.5426 fm
- 11Be ground-state binding energy: 0.5 MeV (s-wave valence neutron)
- Coulomb radius for 10Be-12C: R_C = 5.777 fm
- For two-body 10Be+12C at 20 MeV/nucleon: the complex semiclassical correction (b'') is accurate up to 25 degrees; at 10 MeV/nucleon, accurate up to 20 degrees
- For three-body 11Be+12C at 20 MeV/nucleon: the fragment-by-fragment semiclassical correction (b''_cT and b''_nT) is as precise as CDCC up to 25 degrees; at 10 MeV/nucleon, up to 20 degrees

## Context

This work extends the eikonal approximation's range of validity to beam energies around 10 MeV/nucleon, where facilities like HIE-ISOLDE at CERN and ReA12 at MSU will deliver radioactive-ion beams. It builds on prior studies of Wallace's correction (Wallace 1971, 1973) and semiclassical corrections (Baur and Weber 1981; Bertulani 1985; Alder and Winther 1975). The results confirm findings by Alvarez-Rodriguez et al. (1997) for structureless nuclei and extend them to halo-nucleus projectiles. The complex-distance semiclassical correction, particularly when applied separately to each fragment's impact parameter, enables eikonal-like reaction models to analyze data down to 10 MeV/nucleon, a regime where CDCC faces convergence issues and high computational cost.
