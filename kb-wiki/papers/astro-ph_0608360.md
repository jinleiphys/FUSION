# Dense Matter in Compact Stars: Theoretical Developments and Observational Constraints

> We review theoretical developments in studies of dense matter and its phase structure of relevance to compact stars. Observational data on compact stars, which can constrain the properties of dense matter, are presented critically and interpreted.

## Key claim

This review argues that observational data from compact stars can constrain dense matter properties, but the interpretation of these constraints is heavily qualified by uncertainties in atmospheric composition, magnetic field effects, and the need for self-consistent spectral modeling that accounts for surface gravity variations.

## Method

The authors review theoretical models of dense matter phases relevant to compact stars, including nuclear matter, Bose-condensed hadronic matter, hyperonic matter, and deconfined quark matter. They critically present observational data from mass measurements of neutron stars in binary systems, thermal emission from isolated cooling neutron stars, and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries, and interpret these observations in terms of the equation of state and low-energy response functions such as specific heat and neutrino emission.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_\mathrm{nuclear}=2.5 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$, corresponding to baryon number density $n_B \simeq 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- Typical Fermi momentum in neutron star interiors: $k_F \sim 300$--$600$ MeV
- Fermi degeneracy energy: $E_F = k_F^2/2m \sim 60$--$150$ MeV
- Baryon chemical potential at the star center: $500$--$1000$ MeV larger than nucleon rest mass
- Eddington accretion rate: $\dot{M}_\mathrm{Edd} \approx 10^{-8} M_\odot$ yr$^{-1}$
- Heat released in crust per accreted baryon: $Q_\mathrm{nuc} \approx 1.5$ MeV
- Quiescent luminosity relation: $L_\mathrm{q} \simeq 6 \times 10^{32} \; f \; (Q_\mathrm{nuc}/1.5 \, \mathrm{MeV}) \; (\langle\dot{M}\rangle/10^{-11} M_\odot \mathrm{yr}^{-1})$ erg s$^{-1}$
- Radius of qLMXB in $\omega$ Centauri: $R_\infty = 13.6\pm0.3$ km
- Radius of qLMXB in M13: $R_\infty = 12.8\pm0.44$ km
- Surface gravity used in atmosphere models: $g_s=2.43\times10^{14}$ cm s$^{-2}$

## Context

This review extends earlier theoretical work on neutron star structure and dense matter phases, including studies of the nuclear pasta phase transition and the initial mass function predictions from stellar evolution theory by Timmes, Woosley, and Weaver (1996) and Fryer and Kalogera (2001). It critically assesses observational constraints from mass measurements compiled by Thorsett and Chakrabarty (1999) and Stairs (2004), and temperature measurements from cooling neutron stars. The work enables future self-consistent analyses of neutron star radii using atmosphere models that account for varying surface gravity, as demonstrated by Heinke et al. (2006) for the quiescent LMXB X7 in 46 Tucanae.
