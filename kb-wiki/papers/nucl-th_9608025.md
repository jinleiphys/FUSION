# Isospin Lattice-Gas Model and Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> An isospin lattice-gas model, which is a spin-1 Ising model, is employed to investigate the liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter. We consider nuclear matter as a lattice where each lattice site can be either empty or occupied by a proton or a neutron, with a nearest-neighbor interaction among the nucleons. With the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation, we calculate various thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter for different densities and different proton-neutron asymmetry parameter $s$. Our model exhibits liquid-gas phase transition below a critical temperature $T_c$, and predicts a monotonic decreasing of $T_c$ as the magnitude of $s$ is increased. The dependence of the nuclear matter isotherms on the asymmetry parameter $s$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple isospin lattice-gas model, treated with the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation, exhibits a liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter with a critical temperature that decreases monotonically as the magnitude of the proton-neutron asymmetry parameter increases; however, this result depends on a specific choice of the ratio alpha = 5 kappa / 6 C_2 that forces a smooth phase boundary, and a different choice would produce an unconventional cusp-shaped phase diagram.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as a three-dimensional simple cubic lattice where each site can be empty, occupied by a proton, or occupied by a neutron, with nearest-neighbor interactions (J_s for symmetric pp/nn pairs and J_d for asymmetric pn pairs). They add a Fermi-gas kinetic energy term proportional to density^(2/3) and use the Bragg-Williams mean field approximation to evaluate the grand partition function in the thermodynamic limit. The equation of state is derived from the grand potential, and the critical temperature is found from the condition that the functions k_B T * f(r) and g(r) become tangent at their common zero point.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature T_c decreases monotonically with increasing asymmetry parameter s; for typical values of s, T_c is around 17-20 MeV.
* The interaction parameter C_2 is approximately 125 MeV, estimated from an average potential energy of about -40 MeV in nuclear matter.
* With C_2 = 125 MeV and the chosen alpha = (2/3)^(1/3), the kinetic energy parameter kappa is 131.037 MeV.
* For a three-dimensional simple cubic lattice, the number of nearest neighbors gamma = 6.
* The critical vacancy r_0 for symmetric matter (s=0) occurs at r = 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the lattice-gas model approach previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Kuo et al. (1990, 1996) to the asymmetric case, where the proton-neutron asymmetry parameter s is nonzero. The results are compared with earlier many-body calculations using Skyrme interactions (Jaqaman et al., 1983; Sulaksono et al., 1986), Gogny interactions (Sébille et al., 1990), and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Bombaci and Lombardo, 1991; Baldo et al., 1994; Sammarruca et al., 1992). The model produces p-v isotherms similar to van der Waals theory but without metastable states in the coexistence region, eliminating the need for a Maxwell construction. The approach enables further study of the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter relevant to supernovae explosions and neutron star evolution.
