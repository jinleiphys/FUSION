# Investigating the inclusive $D^0$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> The inclusive $D^0$ photoproduction in $PbPb$ collisions at the center - of - mass energies of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) is investigated considering the color dipole $S$ - matrix approach. The analytical expressions for the differential distributions are derived in the impact parameter and transverse momentum spaces and predictions for the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions are presented considering three distinct models for the unintegrated gluon distribution of the nuclear target. In particular, we compare the predictions derived assuming a linear dynamics, with and without the inclusion of nuclear effects, with those obtained by solving the running coupling Balitsky - Kovchegov equation. A comparison of these predictions with the recent (preliminary) CMS data is also performed. Our results indicate that a detailed analysis of this observable will be very useful to improve our understanding of the strong interaction theory at high energies and in a nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The inclusive $D^0$ photoproduction cross-section in ultraperipheral $PbPb$ collisions is sensitive to the description of QCD dynamics and nuclear effects, and a comparison with preliminary CMS data indicates that models including nuclear effects or nonlinear QCD dynamics (via the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation) provide a better description than linear dynamics without nuclear effects.

## Method

The authors derive the differential cross-section for inclusive $D^0$ photoproduction using the color dipole $S$-matrix approach, expressing the charm quark momentum spectrum in terms of the unintegrated gluon distribution (UGD) of the nuclear target. They consider three models for the nuclear UGD: the CCFM-setA1 parametrization (linear dynamics, no nuclear effects), the PB-EPPS16 parametrization (linear dynamics with nuclear effects from EPPS16), and the solution of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation (nonlinear dynamics). The cross-section is convoluted with the Peterson fragmentation function for $c \to D^0$, and the effective photon flux includes survival probabilities against strong interactions and electromagnetic dissociation.

## Key numbers

- Charm mass assumed: $m_c = 1.4$ GeV
- Center-of-mass energy: $\sqrt{s} = 5.36$ TeV for $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC
- Peterson fragmentation parameter: $\epsilon_c = 0.05$
- Nuclear radius parameterization: $R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}$ (in fm)
- Electromagnetic dissociation parameter $S$: values of $(10.4\ \mathrm{fm})^2$ and $(17.4\ \mathrm{fm})^2$ from Baur (1998), compared with the authors' own calculation yielding $S \approx (11.4\ \mathrm{fm})^2$ for giant dipole resonance excitation and $S \approx (14.9\ \mathrm{fm})^2$ integrating to $E = 10$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the color dipole $S$-matrix approach, previously applied to exclusive processes and gauge boson production (Bandeira 2024), to inclusive $D^0$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the first predictions for inclusive heavy meson photoproduction in UPHICs by Goncalves (2017) and compares with recent preliminary CMS data and ongoing ALICE analyses. The study enables future precise experimental measurements to discriminate between linear QCD evolution (CCFM, DGLAP-based approaches) and nonlinear dynamics (rcBK equation), and to constrain nuclear effects in the small-$x$ gluon distribution.
