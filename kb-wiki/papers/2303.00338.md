# Low-Energy Deuteron-Alpha Elastic Scattering in Cluster Effective Field Theory

> In this paper, we study the low-energy $d-α$ elastic scattering within the two-body cluster effective field theory (EFT) framework. The importance of the $d(α,α) d$ scattering in the $^6 \textrm{Li} $ production reaction leads us to study this system in an effective way. In the beginning, the scattering amplitudes of each channel are written in a cluster EFT with two-body formalism. Using the effective range expansion analysis for the elastic scattering phase shift of $S$, $P$ and $D$ partial waves, the unknown EFT low-energy coupling constants are determined and the leading and next-to-leading orders EFT results for the phase shift in each channel are presented. To verify the accuracy of the results, we compare experimental phase shift and differential cross section data with obtained results. The accuracy of the EFT results and consistency with the experimental data indicate that the EFT is an effective approach for describing low-energy systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that low-energy deuteron-alpha elastic scattering can be described by a two-body cluster effective field theory (EFT) with controlled precision up to center-of-mass energies of about 3.3 MeV, provided the Coulomb interaction is treated non-perturbatively and the deuteron and alpha are treated as point-like, structureless particles.

## Method

The authors constructed a cluster EFT Lagrangian with dimeron auxiliary fields for each partial-wave channel (³S₁, ³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂, ³D₁, ³D₂, ³D₃), treating the Coulomb interaction non-perturbatively via Coulomb Green's functions and wave functions. They matched the EFT scattering amplitudes to the effective range expansion (ERE) form, determined the low-energy coupling constants (scattering length, effective range, shape parameter) by fitting to experimental phase shift data from Gruebler (1975), Jenny, and Schmelzbach (1972), and computed phase shifts and differential cross sections at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers

- The high-momentum scale Lambda is chosen between the pion mass (140 MeV) and the momentum corresponding to the deuteron binding energy (approximately 90 MeV); the expansion parameter is estimated as about 1/5.
- The inverse Bohr radius of the d-alpha system is k_C approximately 18 MeV; the EFT is reliable for CM energies below 3.3 MeV (p less than or equal to k_C).
- For the ³S₁ channel at LO EFT: scattering length a = -2.060 x 10⁻² MeV⁻¹, effective range r = 3.533 x 10⁻³ MeV, chi-squared = 2.6187.
- For the ³S₁ channel at NLO EFT: a = -2.780 x 10⁻² MeV⁻¹, r = 3.830 x 10⁻³ MeV, shape parameter s = -8.345 x 10⁻⁷ MeV³, chi-squared = 0.0775.
- For the ³P₀ channel at LO EFT: a = -8.029 x 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³, r = 2.163 x 10² MeV, chi-squared = 0.0069.
- For the ³P₁ channel at LO EFT: a = -2.161 x 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻³, r = -6.166 x 10³ MeV, chi-squared = 0.0433.
- For the ³P₂ channel at LO EFT: a = 1.297 x 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻³, r = 1.124 x 10⁵ MeV, chi-squared = 1.4657.
- For the ³D₁ channel (LO EFT/ERE): a = -1.375 x 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻⁵, r = 1.012 x 10⁶ MeV³, s = -1.905 x 10³ MeV⁵, chi-squared = 0.5597.
- For the ³D₂ channel (LO EFT/ERE): a = -1.716 x 10⁻¹⁰ MeV⁻⁵, r = -7.086 x 10⁵ MeV³, s = -10.958 MeV⁵, chi-squared = 0.0033.
- For the ³D₃ channel (LO EFT/ERE): a = -4.500 x 10⁻⁸ MeV⁻⁵, r = -1.554 x 10⁶ MeV³, s = 1.303 x 10³ MeV⁵, chi-squared = 0.0028.

## Context

This work extends the halo/cluster EFT approach, previously applied to systems such as n-alpha scattering (Bertulani et al., Bedaque et al.), alpha-alpha scattering (Higa and Hammer), and p-⁷Li scattering (Lensky and Birse), to the d-alpha system as a six-nucleon problem reduced to a two-body cluster. The results enable future application of the same EFT framework to the astrophysically important radiative capture process d + alpha → ⁶Li + gamma, which the authors identify as the next step.
