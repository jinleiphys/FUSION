# Light fragment and neutron emission in high-energy proton induced spallation reactions

> The dynamics of high-energy proton-induced spallation reactions on target nuclides of $^{136}$Xe, $^{59}$Ni, $^{56}$Fe, $^{208}$Pb, $^{184}$W, $^{181}$Ta, $^{197}$Au and $^{112}$Cd, are investigated with the quantum molecular dynamics transport model. The production mechanism of light nuclides and fission fragments is thoroughly analyzed. The statistical code GEMINI is employed in conjunction to the model for managing the decay of primary fragments. For the treatment of cluster emission during the preequilibrium stage, a surface coalescence model is implemented into the model. It is found that the available data of total cross sections are well reproduced with the combined approach for the spallation reactions on both the heavy and light targets, i.e., $^{56}$Fe and $^{208}$Pb, while it is underestimated in the intermediate-mass-fragment region for the medium-mass target $^{136}$Xe. The energetic clusters are mainly contributed from the preequilibrium recognition, in which the quantum tunneling is taken into account. On the other hand, a fairly well overall description of light cluster and neutron emission is obtained and detailed discrepancies with respect to the experimental results are discussed. Possible modifications on the description of spallation reactions are stressed and compared with both recent experimental and theoretical results in the literature.

## Key claim
The combined LQMD transport model plus GEMINI statistical decay code, augmented with a surface coalescence model for preequilibrium cluster emission, reproduces total spallation cross sections well for light (⁵⁶Fe) and heavy (²⁰⁸Pb) targets at 1 GeV, but underestimates intermediate-mass-fragment yields for the medium-mass target ¹³⁶Xe, indicating that the model's success is target-dependent and that the Phase-Space-Density-Constraint method's effectiveness depends on mean-field parameter choices.

## Method
The Lanzhou-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate the dynamical stage of proton-induced spallation reactions on eight target nuclides (¹³⁶Xe, ⁵⁹Ni, ⁵⁶Fe, ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹⁸⁴W, ¹⁸¹Ta, ¹⁹⁷Au, ¹¹²Cd) at incident energies of 1.0 and 1.2 GeV. After the dynamical evolution, hot primary fragments were identified via a Minimum Spanning Tree algorithm and their statistical decay was handled by the GEMINI code using Hauser-Feshbach formalism for light particle evaporation and Moretto's formalism for asymmetric fission. A surface coalescence model was implemented into LQMD to treat preequilibrium emission of deuterons, tritons, ³He, and ⁴He clusters, with cluster formation governed by phase-space conditions and emission tested against Coulomb plus Woods-Saxon barrier penetration including quantum tunneling.

## Key numbers
- For p+¹³⁶Xe at 1 GeV: the model without Phase-Space-Density-Constraint (PSDC) seriously underestimates intermediate-mass-fragment yields in the range 5 ≤ Z ≤ 40; introducing PSDC only negligibly increases these cross sections.
- For p+⁵⁶Fe at 1 GeV: the experimental total cross sections as a function of mass number are well reproduced under both momentum-dependent and momentum-independent mean fields.
- For p+²⁰⁸Pb at 1 GeV: the calculation without momentum-dependent interaction gives a very much better overall fit to experimental data than the case with MDI, which peaks too early at the target-like end.
- For p+⁵⁸Ni at 1.2 GeV: double differential cross sections of d, t, ³He, and ⁴He are reproduced well for high-energy tails except at very forward angles for deuterons and large angles for tritons and ³He.
- The switching times from dynamical to statistical stage were set to 65 fm/c for p+⁵⁶Fe and ⁵⁸Ni, 85 fm/c for ¹¹²Cd and ¹³⁶Xe, and 115 fm/c for ¹⁸¹Ta, ¹⁸⁴W, and ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The surface coalescence parameter h₀ values for cluster construction range from 300 to 387 fm MeV/c depending on the cluster type and parameter set.

## Context
This work extends the application of the LQMD transport model to spallation reactions across a wide range of target masses, building on earlier QMD studies by Fan and Zhang and on intranuclear-cascade-plus-statistical-multifragmentation models (INC+SMM) used to explain the triple-humped kinematics of intermediate-mass-fragments. The surface coalescence approach follows the method implemented in the INCL model by Boudard et al. (2002) and later refinements, and the results for ²⁰⁸Pb confirm the fission delay prescription given by Ou and Li (2019). The study enables further refinement of preequilibrium cluster emission mechanisms in transport models and identifies the need for improved treatment of intermediate-mass-fragment production for medium-mass targets.
