# Quark Polarization in a Viscous Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Quarks produced in the early stage of non-central heavy-ion collisions could develop a global spin polarization along the opposite direction of the reaction plane due to the spin-orbital coupling via parton interaction in a medium that has finite longitudinal flow shear along the direction of the impact parameter. We study how such polarization evolves via multiple scattering in a viscous quark-gluon plasma with an initial laminar flow. The final polarization is found to be sensitive to the viscosity and the initial shear of local longitudinal flow.

## Key claim
Quarks produced in noncentral heavy-ion collisions can develop a global spin polarization opposite to the reaction plane via spin-orbital coupling in a medium with finite longitudinal flow shear, but the final polarization is sensitive to both the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma and the initial shear of local longitudinal flow, with the polarization rate being inversely proportional to viscosity.

## Method
The authors calculated the quark-quark scattering cross section for initially polarized quarks using a screened static potential model within perturbative QCD, then derived a rate equation for the time evolution of quark polarization via multiple scattering. They modeled the hydrodynamic evolution of a relativistic laminar flow between two frictionless impenetrable walls using Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics, with initial sine-type longitudinal flow velocity profiles and uniform initial temperature of 355 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial orbital angular momentum for Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=200 GeV: roughly 10^5 to 10^6 for impact parameter 1 fm < b < 10 fm
- Initial orbital angular momentum for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV: roughly 3x10^6 to 3x10^7
- Experimental limit on Lambda hyperon polarization: |P_Lambda| less than or about 0.02
- Wall separation distance in laminar flow model: 2h = 10 fm (h=5 fm)
- Initial temperature: 355 MeV
- Initial time for expanding case: tau_0 = 1 fm
- Relaxation time for shear stress: about 0.27 to 1.35 fm for eta/s = 1/(4pi) to 5/(4pi) at T=350 MeV
- Initial longitudinal velocity at boundaries: v_0 = 0.7 and 0.9 considered

## Context
This work extends the original proposal by Liang and Wang (2004) that global quark polarization could occur in QGP formed in noncentral heavy-ion collisions, where earlier estimates considered only single scattering between quarks and thermal partons. The authors improve upon those studies by incorporating multiple scattering effects and the time evolution of the longitudinal flow shear via viscous hydrodynamics, showing that the final polarization could serve as a viscometer of the QGP. The results enable future studies using full 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics with initial conditions from Monte Carlo models such as HIJING, and extension beyond the small angle approximation.
