# Ab initio folding potentials for proton-nucleus scattering with NCSM nonlocal one-body densities

> Based on the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series we employ a nonlocal translationally invariant nuclear density derived from a chiral next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and the very same interaction for consistent full-folding calculations of the effective (optical) potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering for light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent *ab initio* folding potential for proton-nucleus scattering can be constructed by using the same chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interaction for both the nonlocal translationally invariant one-body density (from the no-core shell model) and the NN t-matrix, but the first-order term does not explicitly contain any three-nucleon force (3NF) contributions, so the choice of interaction is limited to those where 3NF effects are small.

## Method

The authors employ the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering series to compute the first-order effective (optical) potential for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering. They use the chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interaction to generate both the nonlocal translationally invariant one-body density matrices (via the no-core shell model) and the NN t-matrix, then evaluate the full-folding integral for the effective potential. Calculations are performed for proton scattering on \(^4\)He (with \(N_{\rm max}=18\)) and \(^{16}\)O (with \(N_{\rm max}=10\)) at laboratory kinetic energies between 100 and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The chiral NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) interaction is fitted to NN data up to 125 MeV laboratory kinetic energy with \(\chi^2 \approx 1\) per degree of freedom.
- For \(^4\)He, calculations are carried out with \(N_{\rm max}=18\); for \(^{16}\)O, with \(N_{\rm max}=10\).
- The angular distributions for elastic proton scattering are shown for energies between 100 and 200 MeV laboratory kinetic energy for both \(^4\)He and \(^{16}\)O.
- The difference between using the full \(U\) versus the driving term \(\hat{U}\) is clearly visible for \(^4\)He at 200 MeV, but very small for \(^{16}\)O at the same energy.

## Context

This work extends the spectator expansion approach pioneered by Watson (1953) and formalized by Kerman, McManus, and Thaler (KMT), as further developed by Siciliano (1977) and Chinn (1995). It builds on prior work by Burrows (2017) on translationally invariant nonlocal one-body densities and Burrows (2018) on the full-folding effective potential. The key advance is the consistent use of the same chiral NN interaction (NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) from Ekstrom, 2013) for both the structure and reaction parts, enabling fully *ab initio* optical potentials for light nuclei. Future work with different interactions will be needed to study the effect of including three-nucleon forces in the one-body density for the first-order effective potential.
