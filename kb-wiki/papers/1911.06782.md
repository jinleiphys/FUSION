# Lifetimes and moments measurements to investigate the structure of midheavy nuclei

> Mid-heavy nuclei offer unique opportunities to study the collective and single-particle aspects of nuclear structure. This mass regime is a dynamic area where protons and neutrons generally occupy different orbitals, giving rise to complex structures with a wide variety of shapes, shape evolution and shape coexistence. To that end, measurements of nuclear lifetimes and electromagnetic moments ($μ$,$Q$) can be invaluable. Recent experimental activities of the NuSTRAP group in Athens have focused on $γ$-spectroscopy studies employing the RoSPHERE array in Magurele, Romania. In recent studies [1,2], the neutron-rich $^{144-146}$Ba isotopes have exhibited octupole degrees of freedom. Similar questions exist for the lighter $^{140}$Ba isotope, which is located at the onset of octupole collectivity. In addition, understanding the structure of heavier, even-even hafnium isotopes requires more data regarding shape coexistence and shape evolution. Preliminary results on lifetimes in this area pave the way to understand dynamical phenomena prior to studying more exotic species in the future.

## Key claim

The paper reports preliminary results from two test experiments on mid-heavy nuclei, but the suspected octupole collectivity in \(^{140}\)Ba could not be confirmed due to very low statistics, and the analysis for \(^{180}\)Hf is ongoing and not yet conclusive.

## Method

Two separate three-day experiments were performed at the 9 MV Tandem Accelerator Laboratory IFIN-HH in Magurele, Romania using the RoSPHERE array. The first experiment used the 2n-transfer reaction \(^{138}\)Ba(\(^{18}\)O,\(^{16}\)O)\(^{140}\)Ba at four energies just below the Coulomb barrier (61, 63, 65 and 67 MeV) with 15 HPGe detectors; the second used the charge-pickup reaction \(^{181}\)Ta(\(^{11}\)B,\(^{12}\)C)\(^{180}\)Hf at 47 MeV with 25 HPGe detectors. Lifetimes were attempted via the Doppler-Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM), and \(\gamma\)-\(\gamma\) angular correlations were used for spin/parity assignments.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{140}\)Ba, a lower lifetime limit of \(\tau > 1\) ps was set based on DSAM limitations.
- The \(^{140}\)Ba target had a thickness of 2 mg/cm\(^2\) of barium between a 4.88 mg/cm\(^2\) Au backing and a 0.5 mg/cm\(^2\) Au front layer.
- The \(^{140}\)Ba experiment used beam energies of 61, 63, 65, and 67 MeV; only the 65 MeV setting produced usable data.
- The \(^{180}\)Hf experiment used a thick 5 mg/cm\(^2\) \(^{\text{nat}}\)Ta foil and a beam energy of 47 MeV.
- In \(^{180}\)Hf, a K=4 8\(^-\) isomer at 1141.6 MeV has a half-life of 5.53 h, and a 4\(^-\) isomer has \(t_{1/2} = 0.57\ \mu\)s.
- The 1821 keV non-band state in \(^{180}\)Hf is depopulated by a 447 keV \(\gamma\) transition.

## Context

This work extends studies of octupole collectivity in the barium isotopic chain, following measurements of \(B(E3)\) values in neutron-rich \(^{144-146}\)Ba by Bucher (2016, 2017). For \(^{180}\)Hf, the work aims to test predictions of shape coexistence from the proxy-SU(3) model of Assimakis (2018) and Martinou (2018). The results enable future experiments using radioactive beams for \(^{140}\)Ba and plunger measurements for \(^{180}\)Hf to obtain precise lifetimes and transition matrix elements.
