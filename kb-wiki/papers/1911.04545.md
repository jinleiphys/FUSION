# Unruh effect universality: emergent conical geometry from density operator

> The Unruh effect has been investigated from the point of view of the quantum statistical Zubarev density operator in space with the Minkowski metric. Quantum corrections of the fourth order in acceleration to the energy-momentum tensor of real and complex scalar fields, and Dirac field are calculated. Both massless and massive fields are considered. The method for regularization of discovered infrared divergences for scalar fields is proposed. The calculated corrections make it possible to substantiate the Unruh effect from the point of view of the statistical approach, and to explicitly show its universality for various quantum field theories of massless and massive fields. The obtained results exactly coincide with the ones obtained earlier by calculation of the vacuum average of energy-momentum tensor in a space with a conical singularity. Thus, the duality of two methods for describing an accelerated medium is substantiated. One may also speak about the emergence of geometry with conical singularity from thermodynamics. In particular, the polynomiality of the energy-momentum tensor and the absence of higher-order corrections in acceleration can be explicitly demonstrated.

## Key claim

The Unruh effect is shown to be universal for real scalar, complex scalar, and Dirac fields (both massless and massive) using the Zubarev quantum statistical density operator in Minkowski space, with the condition that the energy-momentum tensor vanishes exactly at the Unruh temperature ¥(T_U = |a|/(2¥pi)¥); the paper explicitly demonstrates that fourth-order corrections in acceleration coincide with results from field theory in a space with a conical singularity, establishing a duality between the two approaches.

## Method

The authors calculate quantum corrections to the energy-momentum tensor up to fourth order in acceleration by expanding the Zubarev density operator (containing the boost operator) perturbatively and evaluating five-point connected thermal correlators of the energy-momentum tensor in the imaginary-time formalism. For scalar fields, infrared divergences appear in the fourth-order coefficients and are regularized using Veltman's formula (setting ¥(¥int d|p|/|p|^2 = 0¥) for massless fields) and by summing the series of divergences in the massive case. The resulting expressions are compared term-by-term with the energy-momentum tensor computed on a Rindler space with a conical singularity, as derived by Dowker (1994).

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach to the Unruh effect previously demonstrated for massless Dirac fields by Prokhorov et al. (2019) to scalar fields (both real and complex) and massive fields, showing universality across different quantum field theories. The results exactly match those obtained by Dowker (1994) and Frolov (1987) using quantum field theory on a space with a conical singularity (Rindler space), thereby establishing a duality between the Zubarev statistical operator method and the geometric approach. This duality enables the use of known results for cosmic strings to predict properties of accelerated media, and vice versa, and also demonstrates the polynomiality of the energy-momentum tensor (absence of higher-order corrections beyond fourth order in acceleration).
