# Projection on particle number and angular momentum: Example of triaxial Bogoliubov quasiparticle states

> Many quantal many-body methods that aim at the description of self-bound nuclear or mesoscopic electronic systems make use of auxiliary wave functions that break one or several of the symmetries of the Hamiltonian in order to include correlations associated with the geometrical arrangement of the system's constituents. Such reference states have been used already for a long time within self-consistent methods that are either based on effective valence-space Hamiltonians or energy density functionals, and they are presently also gaining popularity in the design of novel ab-initio methods. A fully quantal treatment of a self-bound many-body system, however, requires the restoration of the broken symmetries through the projection of the many-body wave functions of interest onto good quantum numbers. The goal of this work is three-fold. First, we want to give a general presentation of the formalism of the projection method starting from the underlying principles of group representation theory. Second, we want to investigate formal and practical aspects of the numerical implementation of particle-number and angular-momentum projection of Bogoliubov quasiparticle vacua, in particular with regard of obtaining accurate results at minimal computational cost. Third, we want to analyze the numerical, computational and physical consequences of intrinsic symmetries of the symmetry-breaking states when projecting them.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general group-theoretical formalism for particle-number and angular-momentum projection of Bogoliubov quasiparticle vacua, demonstrating that high numerical accuracy can be achieved with limited computational cost and that intrinsic point-group symmetries of the reference states can be exploited to further reduce the cost, with the simplifications valid for any number parity of the quasiparticle states.

## Method

The authors derive projection operators from the Great Orthogonality Theorem for both finite groups and compact Lie groups, then apply them to particle-number projection (Abelian U(1) gauge group) and angular-momentum projection (non-Abelian SU(2) group) of triaxial Bogoliubov quasiparticle vacua. They study convergence and accuracy of quadrature rules for the multi-dimensional integrals appearing in projected matrix elements, using realistic examples built with either a pseudo-potential-based energy density functional or a valence-space shell-model interaction. They also provide group-theoretical derivations of relations between intermediate matrix elements that arise from conserved subgroups of the broken symmetry groups, specifically the double point symmetry group D2h^TD.

## Key numbers

* The paper demonstrates how to achieve high accuracy of discretizations for multi-dimensional integrals while limiting the order of employed quadrature rules (no specific numerical precision values given in the truncated text).
* The dimension d_lambda of an irrep lambda is used throughout; for Abelian groups all irreps are one-dimensional, while for SU(2) the dimension is 2J+1 for angular momentum J.
* The volume of a compact Lie group G is defined as v_G = integral over G of the invariant measure dv_G(g).
* The order of a finite group G is denoted n_G.

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of symmetry-breaking-plus-symmetry-restoration methods in nuclear physics, from early work by Peierls and Yoccoz (1957) and the Copenhagen school (Bohr, Mottelson) through modern energy density functional approaches (Bender 2004, Niksic 2006, Egido 2016) and emerging ab-initio methods (Soma 2013, Hergert 2014, Duguet 2017). The paper clarifies formal properties of projection operators rarely addressed systematically in the nuclear physics literature, and provides practical guidance for reducing computational cost through exploitation of intrinsic symmetries, enabling more efficient variation-after-projection calculations and applications to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei.
