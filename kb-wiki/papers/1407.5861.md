# Systematic study of breakup effects on complete fusion at energies above the Coulomb barrier

> A large number of complete fusion excitation functions of reactions including the breakup channel were measured in recent decades, especially in the last few years. It allows us to investigate the systematic behavior of the breakup effects on the complete fusion cross sections. To this end, we perform a systematic study of the breakup effects on the complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier. The reduced fusion functions F(x) are compared with the universal fusion functions which are used as a uniform standard reference. The complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by the breakup of projectiles. This suppression effect for reactions induced by the same projectile is independent of the target and mainly determined by the lowest energy breakup channel of the projectile. There holds a good exponential relation between the suppression factor and the energy corresponding to the lowest breakup threshold.

# Systematic study of breakup effects on complete fusion at energies above the Coulomb barrier

## Key claim
Complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier are suppressed by projectile breakup, and for reactions induced by the same projectile this suppression is independent of the target nucleus, being determined primarily by the projectile's lowest-energy breakup channel. A good exponential relation holds between the suppression factor and the energy corresponding to that lowest breakup threshold.

## Method
The authors collected complete fusion excitation functions for reactions induced by ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹⁰B, ¹¹B, ¹²C, ¹³C, and ¹⁶O projectiles on various targets. They reduced the data to dimensionless fusion functions F(x) using the Sao Paulo potential to obtain barrier parameters, then compared these with the universal fusion function (UFF) derived from Wong's formula. Suppression factors were extracted by fitting the ratio F(x)/F₀(x) for x>0, where F₀(x) is the UFF.

## Key numbers
- ⁶Li: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.60, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 1.474 MeV (breakup into α + d)
- ⁷Li: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.67, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 2.467 MeV (breakup into α + t)
- ⁹Be: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.68, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 1.573 MeV (breakup into n + α + α)
- ¹⁰B: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.80, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 4.461 MeV (breakup into ⁶Li + α)
- ¹¹B: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.91, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 8.665 MeV (breakup into ⁷Li + α)
- ¹²C: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.88, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 7.367 MeV (breakup into ⁸Be + α)
- ¹³C: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.94, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 10.648 MeV (breakup into ⁹Be + α)
- ¹⁶O: suppression factor F_B.U. = 0.87, lowest breakup threshold E_B.U. = 7.162 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Gasques (2009) and Dasgupta (2010) who found CF suppression for ⁶Li, ⁷Li, and ¹⁰B projectiles was target-independent using CC or SBPM comparisons, and by Gomes (2011) who found no clear systematic behavior for ⁹Be using the UFF method. The present study covers a wider range of projectiles including tightly bound ones (¹¹B, ¹²C, ¹³C, ¹⁶O) where breakup evidence was recently found by Singh (2008), Palshetkar (2010), and others. The exponential relation between suppression factor and breakup threshold energy enables prediction of CF suppression for other projectiles and provides a systematic framework for understanding breakup effects on fusion.
