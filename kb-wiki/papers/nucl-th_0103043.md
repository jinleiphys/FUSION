# Selected topics on Low Energy Antiproton Physics

> 10 pages, 11 figures Invited talk to the Biennial Conference on Low Energy Antiproton Physics (LEAP 2000), Venice 20-26 August (2000). To appear in Nuclear Physics B

## Key claim

The paper reviews selected low-energy antiproton physics results from LEAR, arguing that the measured \(^3P_0\) protonium level shift provides a strong indication of a near-threshold \(\bar{N}N\) state in the isospin-zero channel, though the observed energy shift is underestimated by a factor of two in existing optical models.

## Method

The authors present Faddeev calculations for the \(\bar{n}d\) scattering length, solving the coupled Faddeev equations in configuration space for the \(\bar{n}d\) system with the KW optical model. They also analyze protonium P-level shifts and \(\bar{p}p\) scattering volumes using coupled-channel calculations that include Coulomb interactions and the proton-neutron mass difference, comparing results from the KW, DR1, and DR2 optical models.

## Key numbers

- \(\Delta E_R(^3P_0) = -139 \pm 28\) meV and \(\Gamma(^3P_0) = 120 \pm 25\) meV for the protonium \(^3P_0\) level shift (experimental result from G_NPA_99).
- The \(\bar{p}p\) scattering volume for the \(^3P_0\) state is \(a_{\bar{p}p} = (-5.68 \pm 1.14) - (2.45 \pm 0.53)i\) fm\(^3\).
- The extracted isospin-zero \(^3P_0\) scattering volume is \(a_0 \approx (-15.7 \pm 2.6) - (5.8 \pm 1.2)i\) fm\(^3\).
- The \(\bar{n}d\) spin-averaged scattering length from Faddeev calculations is \(\bar{a}_{\bar{n}d} = 1.61 - 0.84i\) fm.
- The doublet and quartet \(\bar{n}d\) scattering lengths are \(a^{(2)}_{\bar{n}d} = 1.59 - 0.88i\) fm and \(a^{(4)}_{\bar{n}d} = 1.62 - 0.82i\) fm, respectively.
- The \(\bar{n}n\) threshold corresponds to \(\bar{p}p\) laboratory momentum \(p_{\bar{p}p} = 98.6\) MeV/c and \(\bar{n}p\) laboratory momentum \(p_{\bar{n}p} = 69.7\) MeV/c, with \(\Delta = m_n - m_p = 1.293\) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the analysis of low-energy antiproton physics following the shutdown of LEAR, building on prior work by the OBELIX group (Iazzi et al., 2000) on \(\bar{n}p\) cross sections and the PS207 experiment (G_NPA_99) on protonium level shifts. The Faddeev calculations for \(\bar{n}d\) provide the first exact solution of the three-body problem with \(\bar{N}N\) interactions, enabling future comparisons with experimental data from the AD facility and with earlier model calculations by Wycech et al. (1985) and by PBLZ (2000) for \(\bar{p}d\).
