# The cluster structure of the inner crust of neutron stars in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach

> We analyse how the structure of the inner curst is influenced by the pairing correlations. The inner-crust matter, formed by nuclear clusters immersed in a superfluid neutron gas and ultra-relativistic electrons, is treated in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The properties of the Wigner-Seitz cells, i.e., their neutron to proton ratio and their radius at a given baryonic density, are obtained from the energy minimization at beta equilibrium. To obtain the binding energy of baryonic matter we perform Skyrme-HFB calculations with zero-range density-dependent pairing forces of various intensities. We find that the Wigner-Seitz cells have much smaller numbers of protons compared to previous calculations. For the dense cells the binding energy of the configurations with small proton numbers do not converge to a well-defined minimum value which precludes the determination of their structure. We show that for these cells there is a significant underestimation of the binding energy due to the boundary conditions at the border of the cells imposed through the Wigner-Seitz approximation.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations have a weak influence on the structure of Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust, but for high-density cells with small radii the binding energies do not converge to a well-defined minimum, and the Wigner-Seitz approximation itself is not accurate enough to predict the structure in that region.

## Method

The inner-crust matter was treated in the Wigner-Seitz approximation, with spherical cells containing nuclear clusters immersed in a superfluid neutron gas and ultra-relativistic electrons. The nuclear binding energy was calculated using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with a Skyrme SLy4 interaction for the particle-hole channel and three different zero-range density-dependent pairing forces (two isoscalar forces of different strengths and one isovector-isoscalar force) for the particle-particle channel. The properties of the cells (neutron-to-proton ratio and radius) were determined by minimizing the total energy under beta equilibrium conditions for baryonic densities between about 4 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³ and 1.4 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- The inner crust extends from the neutron drip density ρ_d ≈ 4 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³ up to a density of about ρ ≈ 1.4 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³.
- For the isoscalar pairing forces, the two values of the pairing strength used were V₀ = {-570, -430} MeV fm⁻³, corresponding to a maximum gap of about 3 MeV (strong pairing) and about 1 MeV (weak pairing).
- For the isovector-isoscalar pairing force, the parameters used were V₀ = -703.86 MeV fm⁻³, η_s = 0.7115, α_s = 0.3865, η_n = 0.9727, α_n = 0.3906.
- For the cells where a minimum could be identified, the difference between the binding energy of the minimum and other local minima was of the order of 10 keV.
- For cells 6-9, the numbers of protons in HF and HFB calculations differed by about 2 units; the largest difference was 10 units for cell 5.
- The finite-size energy correction for the Wigner-Seitz approximation was parametrized as f(ρ_{n_g}, R_WS) = 89.05 (ρ_{n_g}/ρ_0)^0.1425 R_WS^{-2}, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the earlier microscopic calculation of the inner crust by Negele and Vautherin (1973), which used the Hartree-Fock approximation with Density Matrix Expansion and found cells with magic or semi-magic proton numbers (Z=40,50). It also builds on the HF+BCS study by Baldo et al., which found that pairing can change the cell structure significantly. The present HFB calculations show that pairing has only a weak influence on cell structure, and that the Wigner-Seitz approximation fails for high-density cells with small radii, requiring empirical energy corrections as proposed by Margueron et al. (2007). The results enable future work to explore more accurate treatments of the inner crust beyond the Wigner-Seitz approximation.
