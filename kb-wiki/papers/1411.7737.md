# Energy and mass-number dependence of hadron-nucleus total reaction cross sections

> We systematically analyze nuclear reaction data that are sensitive to nuclear size, namely, proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections and differential elastic cross sections, using a phenomenological black-sphere approximation of nuclei that we are developing. In this framework, the radius of the black sphere is found to be a useful length scale that simultaneously accounts for the observed proton-nucleus total reaction cross section and first diffraction peak in the proton elastic differential cross section. This framework is expected to be applicable to any kind of projectile that is strongly attenuated in the nucleus. On the basis of a cross-section formula constructed within this framework, we find that a less familiar $A^{1/6}$ dependence plays a crucial role in describing the energy dependence of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors find that a less familiar $A^{1/6}$ dependence plays a crucial role in describing the energy dependence of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections, but this result is conditional on the validity of their black-sphere approximation framework, which assumes the target nucleus acts as a strongly absorptive black sphere for incident protons.

## Method

The authors systematically analyzed proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections and differential elastic cross sections using a phenomenological black-sphere approximation, where the nucleus is treated as a black disk of radius $a$. They determined $a$ by fitting the calculated first diffraction peak angle to measured values from elastic scattering data, then used this radius to compute the total reaction cross section as $\sigma_{\rm BS} = \pi a^2$. They constructed a cross-section formula that expresses the energy dependence of $\sigma_R$ through the nuclear optical depth, using the energy dependence of the average proton-nucleon total cross section $\bar{\sigma}_{pN}^{\rm total}$.

## Key numbers

- The black-sphere scaling for stable nuclei at $T_p \gtrsim 800$ MeV gives $a \simeq 1.2135 A^{1/3}$ fm, $r_{\rm BS} \simeq 0.9400 A^{1/3}$ fm, and $\sigma_{\rm BS} \simeq 46.263 A^{2/3}$ mb.
- BS-fit1 parametrization: $a = 1.2671A^{1/3} - 0.152$ fm, with standard deviation around 0.096 fm.
- BS-fit2 parametrization: $a = 1.33A^{1/3} - 0.35$ fm.
- The path length $L'$ in the optical depth expression scales as $L' \propto A^{1/6} D^{1/2}$, where $D = 2.2$ fm is a constant.
- The coefficient $\rho_0 a_0/(D n_{c0})$ scales as $D^{-1/2} A^{1/2}$, with $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The BS cross-section formula reproduces empirical $\sigma_R$ data for stable nuclei at $T_p = 100$--1000 MeV remarkably well.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous black-sphere approximation studies (Kohama, Iida, Oyamatsu, 2005-2008) by systematically analyzing the mass-number and energy dependence of proton-nucleus total reaction cross sections. The framework is similar in concept to the sharp cutoff model developed by Blair for low-energy alpha-particle scattering, but differs in how the cutoff radius is defined. The BS cross-section formula has been incorporated into the Particle and Heavy Ion Transport code System (PHITS) for practical applications in accelerator technology, particle therapy, and space radiation fields. The work enables systematic predictions of reaction cross sections for nuclides where elastic scattering data exist but reaction cross section data are unavailable.
