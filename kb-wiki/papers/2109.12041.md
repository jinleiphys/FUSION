# Anomalous diffusion in QCD matter

> We study the effects of quantum corrections on transverse momentum broadening of a fast parton passing through dense QCD matter. We show that, at leading logarithmic accuracy the broadening distribution tends at late times or equivalently for large system sizes $L$ to a universal distribution that only depends on a single scaling variable $k^2_\perp/Q^2_s$ where the typical transverse momentum scale increases with time as $\ln Q_s^2 \simeq (1+2 β) \ln L - \frac{3}{2}(1+β)\,\ln\ln L$ up to non-universal terms, with an anomalous dimension $β\sim \sqrt{α_s} $. This property is analogous to geometric scaling of gluon distributions in the saturation regime and traveling waves solutions to reaction-diffusion processes. We note that since $β>0$ the process is super-diffusive, which is also reflected at large transverse momentum where the scaling distribution exhibits a heavy tail $k_\perp^{4-2β}$ akin to Lévy random walks.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, at leading logarithmic accuracy, the transverse momentum broadening distribution of a fast parton in dense QCD matter becomes a universal scaling function of \(k_\perp^2/Q_s^2\) at large system sizes \(L\), with the saturation scale growing as \(\ln Q_s^2 \simeq (1+2\beta)\ln L - \frac{3}{2}(1+\beta)\ln\ln L\) and an anomalous dimension \(\beta \sim \sqrt{\alpha_s}\). The process is super-diffusive because \(\beta > 0\), and the distribution exhibits a heavy tail \(k_\perp^{4-2\beta}\) akin to Levy random walks, a condition the abstract presents as a finding rather than a proven result for all regimes.

## Method

The authors resum radiative corrections to the quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) to double logarithmic accuracy (DLA) via an evolution equation ordered in the gluon fluctuation lifetime \(\tau\), incorporating a non-linear saturation boundary \(Q_s^2(\tau) = \hat{q}(\tau, Q_s^2(\tau))\tau\). They solve this non-linear integro-differential system analytically by mapping it to a traveling wave (TW) problem analogous to the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, obtaining the scaling function and sub-asymptotic corrections. Numerical solutions of the full non-linear system are compared to the analytic results.

## Key numbers

* The anomalous dimension is \(\beta \sim \sqrt{\alpha_s}\), with the traveling wave speed \(c = 1 + 2\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/\pi} + 2\alpha_s N_c/\pi\) (using \(\bar{\alpha} = \alpha_s N_c/\pi\)).
* The saturation scale grows as \(\ln Q_s^2 \simeq (1+2\beta)\ln L - \frac{3}{2}(1+\beta)\ln\ln L\) for large \(L\).
* The scaling distribution has a heavy tail \(\mathcal{P}(k_\perp) \propto (Q_s^2/k_\perp^2)^{\nu}\) with \(\nu = 2 - \beta\) in the strict scaling limit, deviating from the tree-level Rutherford \(\nu=2\) by \(\sim -2\sqrt{\alpha_s}\).
* The median transverse momentum scales as \(\langle k_T\rangle_{\text{med}} \propto L^{c/2}\) with \(c/2 > 1/2\), demonstrating super-diffusive behavior with deviation from standard diffusion of order \(\sqrt{\alpha_s}\).
* The sub-asymptotic correction to the saturation line is \(b = -3/[2(1-\beta)]\), which is parametrically of order \(\sqrt{\alpha_s}\ln Y\) relative to single logarithmic corrections.

## Context

This work extends the double-logarithmic resummation of radiative corrections to transverse momentum broadening initiated by Liou et al. (2013) and Blaizot et al. (2013, 2014) by incorporating the non-linear saturation boundary that restricts the phase space for quantum fluctuations. It establishes a formal analogy between transverse momentum broadening and traveling wave solutions to reaction-diffusion processes, previously exploited for the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation by Munier (2003). The results provide a model-independent functional form for the initial condition of non-linear small-\(x\) evolution equations (e.g., BK equation) and predict a new Levy flight phenomenon in QCD transport, enabling experimental searches in heavy ion collisions and studies of nuclear structure at high energy.
