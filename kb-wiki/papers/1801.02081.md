# Nuclear structure investigation of even-even and odd Pb isotopes by using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method

> The nuclear structure of even-even and odd lead isotopes (178-236 Pb) is investigated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Calculations are performed for a wide range of neutron numbers, starting from the proton-rich side up to the neutron-rich side, by using the SLy4 Skyrme interaction and a new proposed formula for the pairing strength which is more precise for this region of nuclei as we did in previous works in the regions of Neodymium (Nd, Z=60) [Int. J. Mod. Phys. E 24, 1550073 (2015)] and Molybdenum (Mo, Z=42) [Nuc. Phys. A 957 22-32 (2017)]. Such a new pairing strength formula allows reaching exotic nuclei region where the experimental data are not available. Calculated values of various physical quantities such as binding energy, two-neutron separation energy, quadrupole deformation, and rms-radii for protons and neutrons are discussed and compared with experimental data and some estimates of other nuclear models like Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with NL3 functional (NL3), Density-Dependent Meson-Exchange Relativistic Energy Functional (DD-ME2) and results of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations based on the D1S Gogny effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (Gogny D1S).

## Key claim

The authors claim that their new pairing-strength formula \(V_0^{n,p} = 7.28\,A^{3/4}\) (in MeV), used with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, reproduces ground-state properties of lead isotopes (\({}^{178-236}\)Pb) with improved accuracy compared to default constant pairing strengths, though the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) remains more accurate for binding energies.

## Method

The authors performed axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFBTHO (v2.00d) code with the SLy4 Skyrme functional and a zero-range mixed surface-volume pairing interaction. For each lead isotope (both even-even and odd), they iteratively adjusted the pairing strength \(V_0^{n,p}\) (identical for protons and neutrons) until the calculated total binding energy matched the experimental value, then fitted these optimal values to obtain the mass-dependent formula \(V_0^{n,p} = 7.28\,A^{3/4}\). Odd-N isotopes were treated using the equal-filling approximation for blocking of quasi-particle states.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon: the maximum is observed at the magic neutron number \(N = 126\) for lead isotopes.
- Root-mean-square deviation of total binding energy from experiment for even Pb isotopes: SLy4 with new formula = 1.8564 MeV; FRDM = 0.5482 MeV; Gogny D1S = 4.0746 MeV; NL3 = 2.5795 MeV; DD-ME2 = 2.2670 MeV; Direct Calculation (default pairing) = 6.4609 MeV.
- Root-mean-square deviation of total binding energy from experiment for odd Pb isotopes: SLy4 with new formula = 1.7023 MeV; FRDM = 0.6035 MeV; DD-ME2 = 2.0616 MeV; Direct Calculation = 6.9293 MeV.
- Root-mean-square deviation of two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\) from experiment for all Pb isotopes: SLy4 = 0.9679 MeV; NL3 = 1.1343 MeV; FRDM = 0.2394 MeV; Gogny D1S = 1.1948 MeV; DD-ME2 = 0.9847 MeV.
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R = R_n - R_p\) for \({}^{236}\)Pb: SLy4 = 0.308 fm; DD-ME2 = 0.370 fm; Gogny D1S = 0.260 fm.
- Quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\): for neutron numbers above \(N = 142\), the prolate deformation saturates near \(\beta_2 = 0.05\) in the SLy4 calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB studies with generalized pairing-strength formulas for Nd (Z=60) in El Bassem and Oulne (2015) and Mo (Z=42) in El Bassem and Oulne (2017) to the lead region (Z=82), a typical proton magic number. The results are compared against experimental data and several theoretical models: Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) by Moller et al. (1997), Relativistic Mean Field with NL3 functional by Lalazissis et al., Density-Dependent Meson-Exchange Relativistic Energy Functional (DD-ME2) by Lalazissis et al. (2005), and HFB with Gogny D1S force by Amedee. The new formula enables calculations for exotic lead isotopes where experimental data are unavailable, and the authors note that the NL3 parameter set has difficulties reproducing deformations in light Pb and Hg isotopes, leading to the introduction of NL3* by Lalazissis et al. (2009).
