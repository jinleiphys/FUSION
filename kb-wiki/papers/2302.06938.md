# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock study of quasifission trajectories in reactions forming $^{294}$Og

> Background: Fission modes in superheavy nuclei are expected to be impacted by quantum shell effects. Similar shell effects may be present in quasifission reactions, acting to hinder the mass equilibration process in heavy-ion collisions. Purpose: To investigate quasifission mechanisms in five different reactions forming $^{294}$Og as a compound nucleus and compare quasifission trajectories with predicted fission modes. Methods: The potential energy surface (PES) of $^{294}$Og is calculated using the static Hartree-Fock approach with BCS pairing correlations. Quasifission trajectories for central collisions at various energies are studied with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory. Results: The exit channel strongly depends on initial mass asymmetry and orientation, but it only exhibits small dependences in the reaction energy. The $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf reaction is affected by the PES topology, leading to either fusion or asymmetric fission. Spherical shell effects associated with the $Z=50$ magic gap hinder charge and mass equilibrations in $^{126}$Sn$+^{168}$Er, resulting in large total kinetic energies and compact scission configurations. Conclusions: Quasifission trajectories can be interpreted in terms of the underlying PES for low excitation energies. Future investigations of quasifission with temperature and angular momentum dependent PES could be considered.

# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock study of quasifission trajectories in reactions forming $^{294}$Og

## Key claim

Quasifission trajectories in reactions forming $^{294}$Og can be interpreted in terms of the underlying zero-temperature potential energy surface (PES) for low excitation energies, but this interpretation breaks down for more symmetric reactions where higher excitation energies wash out shell effects. The exit channel strongly depends on initial mass asymmetry and orientation, with only small dependence on reaction energy.

## Method

The potential energy surface (PES) of $^{294}$Og was calculated using the static Hartree-Fock approach with BCS pairing correlations and constraints on quadrupole ($Q_{20}$) and octupole ($Q_{30}$) moments, using the Sky3D code with the SLy4$d$ Skyrme energy-density functional. Quasifission trajectories for central collisions at various energies were studied with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory for five different reactions forming $^{294}$Og as a compound nucleus: $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf, $^{86}$Kr$+^{208}$Pb, and $^{126}$Sn$+^{168}$Er, with both tip and side orientations considered for deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The PES grid spans $Q_{20}$ from 0 to 270 b with 2 b spacing and $Q_{30}$ from 0 to 110 b$^{3/2}$ with 2 b$^{3/2}$ spacing
- Scission is defined when neck density falls below 0.08 nucleons/fm$^3$ (half nuclear saturation density)
- The ground state energy of $^{294}$Og is -2071.4 MeV
- The second barrier is located at $Q_{20} \simeq 35$ b
- Coulomb barriers: $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf at 235.5 MeV, $^{86}$Kr$+^{208}$Pb at 342.6 MeV, $^{126}$Sn$+^{168}$Er at 387.4 MeV
- Contact times for $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf reach approximately 10-15 zs for tip orientation and greater than 15 zs for side orientation
- The longest contact time found is approximately 27 zs for $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf side orientation at $E_{c.m.} \simeq 220$ MeV
- The heavy fragment in the asymmetric fission valley is near-doubly-magic $^{208}$Pb with $\beta_2 \simeq 0.02$ and $\beta_3 = 0.1$; the light fragment ($^{86}$Kr) has $\beta_2 \simeq 0.3$ and $\beta_3 \simeq 0.12$
- Quasifission in $^{48}$Ca$+^{246}$Cf at higher energy produces heavy fragments with $Z_H \sim 86$ and $N_H \sim 131$, light fragments with $Z_L \sim 31$ and $N_L \sim 44$
- $^{86}$Kr$+^{208}$Pb at higher energy produces heavy fragments with $Z_H \sim 76$ and $N_H \sim 117$, light fragments with $Z_L \sim 42$ and $N_L \sim 60$

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies that predicted strong similarities between quasifission fragments in $^{50}$Ca$+^{176}$Yb and fission fragments of $^{226}$Th (Simenel 2021), and connects to experimental work on quasifission reviewed by Hinde (2021). The study compares quasifission trajectories with predicted fission modes from earlier PES calculations for superheavy nuclei (Poenaru 2011, Zhang 2018, Santhosh 2018, Warda 2018, Matheson 2019, Ishizuka 2020). The results enable future investigations of quasifission with temperature and angular momentum dependent PES, which could clarify the role of excitation energy in washing out shell effects observed here.
