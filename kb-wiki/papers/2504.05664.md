# Assessment of effect of local approximation on single folding potential at low and intermediate incident energies

> To the single folding potentials (SFPs) for the nucleon-nucleus ($N$-$A$) elastic scatterings, local approximations (LAs) have customarily been applied. The LA discussed by Brieva and Rook has been well-known, which only needs the density profile as the structure information of the target nucleus. By applying the M3Y-P6 interaction both to the target wave functions and the real part of SFP, supplemented with the Koning-Delaroche phenomenological imaginary potential, the precision of the Brieva-Rook LA on the SFP is investigated for the proton-nucleus elastic scatterings at $ε_p=16\,-\,80\,\mathrm{MeV}$ incident energies. The analyzing powers as well as the differential cross sections are in reasonable agreement with the available data. The precision of the LA for the central and LS channels is distinctly examined. Although the LA works well at small angles ($θ_\mathrm{c.m.}\lesssim 30^\circ$), it gives rise to sizable deviation from the results of the non-local SFP (\textit{i.e.}, without the LA) at larger angles. The results of the non-local SFP are always in better agreement with the data. The LA for the LS channel influences the differential cross-sections, and the LA for the central channel does the spin observables. It is found that the precision of the LA well correlates to the momentum transfer $q$, and the discrepancy becomes sizable at $q\gtrsim 1.5\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$. The LA is also examined for a halo nucleus, by taking $^{86}$Ni as an example. The precision is slightly worse than in stable nuclei. Difference from the prediction of the empirical potential in the observables of the $p$-$^{86}$Ni scattering is discussed.

## Key claim

The Brieva-Rook local approximation (LA) on the single folding potential (SFP) works well at small scattering angles (θ_c.m. ≲ 30°) but produces sizable deviations from the non-local SFP at larger angles, with the non-local SFP always agreeing better with experimental data; the precision correlates with momentum transfer q, becoming sizable at q ≳ 1.5 fm⁻¹.

## Method

The authors applied the M3Y-P6 effective interaction consistently to both the target wave functions (via spherical Hartree-Fock calculations) and the real part of the single folding potential for proton-nucleus elastic scatterings, supplemented by the Koning-Delaroche phenomenological imaginary potential. They solved the integro-differential Schrödinger equation using the SIDES code to compute differential cross sections and analyzing powers for p-¹⁶O, p-⁴⁰Ca, p-⁹⁰Zr, and p-²⁰⁸Pb at incident energies ε_p = 16–80 MeV, comparing results with and without the local approximation and separately examining the LA for central and LS channels.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: ε_p = 16–80 MeV for proton-nucleus elastic scatterings
- LA works well at θ_c.m. ≲ 30°; deviation becomes sizable at θ_c.m. ≳ 50°
- Discrepancy between LA and non-local SFP becomes sizable at q ≳ 1.5 fm⁻¹
- For p-²⁰⁸Pb at ε_p = 16 MeV, the LA precision is notably better than at higher energies
- For the halo nucleus ⁸⁶Ni, the LA precision is slightly worse than for stable nuclei, with deviation starting at a smaller angle at ε_p = 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ishida and Nakada, 2024) where the M3Y-P6 interaction was applied to N-A elastic scatterings with consistent wave functions, now specifically assessing the Brieva-Rook local approximation (Brieva and Rook, 1977a, 1977b, 1978) that has been widely used in practical folding potential calculations. The study goes beyond earlier work by Lovell, Kravvaris, and Potel (2020) which limited non-locality to the central channel below 50 MeV, and by Arellano, Brieva, and Love (1990) which examined the LA at higher energies (200–400 MeV) using t-matrix interactions. The results enable future assessment of the LA in nucleus-nucleus scatterings and provide a benchmark for non-local double folding potential calculations.
