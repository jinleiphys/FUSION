# Nuclear EMC Effect in a Statistical Model

> A simple statistical model in terms of light-front kinematic variables is used to explain the nuclear EMC effect in the range $x \in [0.2,~0.7]$, which was constructed by us previously to calculate the parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the nucleon. Here, we treat the temperature $T$ as a parameter of the atomic number $A$, and get reasonable results in agreement with the experimental data. Our results show that the larger $A$, the lower $T$ thus the bigger volume $V$, and these features are consistent with other models. Moreover, we give the predictions of the quark distribution ratios, \emph{i.e.}, $q^A(x) / q^D(x)$, $\bar{q}^A(x) / \bar{q}^D(x)$, and $s^A(x) / s^D(x)$, and also the gluon ratio $g^A(x) / g^D(x)$ for iron as an example. The predictions are different from those by other models, thus experiments aiming at measuring the parton ratios of antiquarks, strange quarks, and gluons can provide a discrimination of different models.

# Nuclear EMC Effect in a Statistical Model

## Key claim
A statistical model with temperature as a function of atomic number A can explain the nuclear EMC effect in the range x ∈ [0.2, 0.7], but the model fails outside this region because it does not include Fermi motion at high x or shadowing at low x.

## Method
The authors applied a previously developed statistical model using light-front kinematic variables, treating the nucleon as a thermal system of free partons (quarks, antiquarks, gluons) obeying Fermi-Dirac or Bose-Einstein distributions. They determined the temperature T for nucleons in deuterium by matching the Gottfried sum to the experimental value, then fitted the ratio F₂^A/F₂^D to experimental data for various nuclei in the EMC region x ∈ [0.2, 0.7] to extract T for each nucleus. The calculations were performed under three conditions: with only u, d flavors and gluons; with s flavor included using m_s = 130 MeV; and with s flavor using m_s = 70 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of nucleon in deuterium T^D = 46.84 MeV when only u, d and gluons are considered; T^D = 42.53 MeV with s flavor and m_s = 130 MeV; T^D = 40.08 MeV with s flavor and m_s = 70 MeV
- Temperature difference between free and bound nucleons is about 1–2 MeV lower in bound nucleons
- Volume increase for bound nucleons is about 5%–10% larger than in free nucleons
- Experimental Gottfried sum used as constraint: S_G^exp = 0.235 ± 0.026
- The temperature difference T^D − T^Fe = 1–2 MeV, close to the 3 ± 1 MeV given in Angelini and Pazzi (1985)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous statistical model for free nucleon PDFs (Zhang, Shao, Ma, 2009) to the nuclear EMC effect, following earlier statistical approaches by Angelini and Pazzi (1985) and Li and Peng (1988). The results are compared with three other models: the 6-quark cluster model (Carlson and Havens, 1983; Szwed, 1992), the pion excess model (Smith, 1983; Ericson and Thomas, 1983), and the Q²-rescaling model (Close, Roberts, Ross, 1983; Jaffe, 1984; Close, 1985). The predictions for antiquark, strange quark, and gluon ratios in iron differ significantly between models, suggesting that future experiments measuring these parton ratios in nuclei could discriminate between competing explanations of the EMC effect.
