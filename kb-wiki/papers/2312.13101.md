# Complete fusion of $^6$Li with $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni nuclei in the framework of the continuum discretized coupled channel method

> The complete fusion of $^6$Li with $^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni nuclei, ranging from subbarrier energies up to values well above the Coulomb barrier, is studied using the continuum discretized coupled channels method. We investigate the sensitivity of the results to the largest energy used in the discretization of the continuum, including closed channels. Our results reveal that, as long as the states in the continuum are not limited to too small energies, the specific upper bound adopted is not relevant as the complete fusion cross-section is fairly insensitive to it above a threshold. Convergence with respect to this parameter is rapidly reached, so that closed channels play a role only at low collision energies. A good agreement with the available experimental data is obtained.

## Key claim

The complete fusion cross-section for $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si and $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni is fairly insensitive to the upper energy bound used in discretizing the continuum states, provided that bound is above approximately 5 MeV; convergence is reached for values between 10 and 20 MeV, contrary to earlier suggestions that closed channels might require much higher cutoffs.

## Method

The authors solved the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) equations using an R-matrix formalism with a Lagrange-Laguerre basis for the projectile internal states and a Lagrange-mesh representation for the radial wavefunctions. The complete fusion cross-section was obtained via the CF-ICF model of Rangel et al. (2020), which separates direct complete fusion (DCF) from sequential complete fusion (SCF) by assuming the SCF probability equals the product of the inclusive fusion probabilities of the two fragments. The nuclear interactions between fragments and target were described by the Akyuz-Winther potential, and the $^6$Li ground state cluster structure (deuteron + $^4$He) was included with a spectroscopic amplitude of 0.7.

## Key numbers

- For $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni, the Coulomb barrier of the folded potential $V_{00}(R)$ is 11.6 MeV, compared to 12.4 MeV for the projectile-target potential $V_{PT}(R)$ that neglects cluster structure (a reduction of 0.8 MeV).
- For $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si, the folded barrier $V_{00}^B$ is 6.4 MeV, compared to $V_{PT}^B$ = 7.0 MeV (a reduction of 0.6 MeV).
- Varying the maximum orbital angular momentum $l_{\rm max}$ from 3 to 4 changes the CF cross-section by approximately 10% at sub-barrier energies, with negligible differences a few MeV above the barrier.
- For $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni, differences between $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=10$ MeV and $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=20$ MeV are typically of order 5% across the energy range 10–27 MeV.
- At $V_{00}^B - 2$ MeV for $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni, excluding all closed channels (about 24% of total channels) changes the CF cross-section by about 20%.
- The suppression factor at the highest energies is 0.77 for $^6$Li + $^{64}$Ni and 0.84 for $^6$Li + $^{28}$Si.

## Context

This work extends the CF-ICF model previously applied by Rangel et al. (2020), Canto et al. (2020), and Lopes et al. (2022) to heavier targets, now testing it on lighter targets ($^{28}$Si and $^{64}$Ni). The results contradict the findings of Canto et al. (2023), who suggested that closed channels might require much higher continuum energy cutoffs; instead, the present work shows convergence is reached with $\varepsilon_{\rm max}$ between 10 and 15 MeV. The good agreement with experimental data from M. R. D. Rodrigues et al. (2014) for $^{64}$Ni and from S. K. Singh et al. (2017) and S. B. Moraes et al. (2010) for $^{28}$Si validates the approach for medium-mass and light targets, enabling future studies of incomplete fusion channels and systematic comparisons across target masses.
