# Pseudo-Gauge Stabilizers and Fibration Structure of the Cooper--Frye Map at Freeze-Out

> We study the pseudo-gauge transformation (PGT) freedom at freeze-out in relativistic spin hydrodynamics. The Cooper--Frye map is shown to factor through the quotient of freeze-out data by a universal stabilizer, yielding a stratified fibration over the space of thermodynamic Lagrange multipliers. This classifies observables into base and fiber types, bounds the number of independent PGT-sensitive observables by the family-restricted fiber dimension, and implies cross-observable consistency relations. Applied to heavy-ion polarization data, the fibration structure provides a structural interpretation of the tension between $Λ$ polarization and $ϕ$-meson spin alignment as evidence that the vorticity-dominated response sector may need to be enlarged with local field-correlation data. We show that Weyl-anomaly-induced currents studied recently are classified as base observables and recover the known Belinfante--canonical obstruction $Ω_{ab}\neq\varpi_{ab}$ from the stabilizer condition.

## Key claim

The Cooper-Frye map at freeze-out in relativistic spin hydrodynamics factorizes through a quotient of freeze-out data by a universal stabilizer subgroup of pseudo-gauge transformations, which classifies observables into base and fiber types and bounds the number of independent pseudo-gauge-sensitive observables by the family-restricted fiber dimension; this holds under the fixed-multiplier, fundamental-\(u\) local-equilibrium scheme, and the stabilizer is state-dependent, saturating the full pseudo-gauge group only at global thermodynamic equilibrium where the spin potential equals thermal vorticity.

## Method

The paper constructs the local-equilibrium density operator \(\hat\rho_{\mathrm{LE}}\) on a freeze-out hypersurface \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{FO}}\) using the Bogoliubov-Kubo-Mori (BKM) formalism, with the generator \(\hat K_{\mathrm{LE}}\) built from stress-energy and spin tensors in a given pseudo-gauge. The pseudo-gauge transformation (PGT) shift \(\Delta_\Phi \hat K_{\mathrm{LE}}\) is computed via an insertion formula, and the universal stabilizer \(H_{\mathrm{univ}}\) is defined as the set of PGT generators for which this shift is a c-number multiple of the identity, ensuring invariance of all expectation values. The quotient space \(\mathcal{Q}_{\mathrm{FO}} = R_{\mathrm{FO}}/H_{\mathrm{univ}}\) is then fibered over the space of thermodynamic Lagrange multipliers \((\beta,\xi,\Omega)\), and the Belinfante-canonical test for a Dirac spin fluid is performed by linearizing away from global equilibrium along the one-parameter family \(\Phi_\tau^{\lambda,\mu\nu} = \tau S_{\mathrm{can}}^{\lambda,\mu\nu}\).

## Key numbers

- At global equilibrium, the universal stabilizer saturates the full pseudo-gauge group: \(H_{\mathrm{univ}}[ \beta_{\mathrm{Killing}}, \Omega = \varpi ] = G_{\mathrm{PGT}}\), so the fiber \(G_{\mathrm{PGT}}/H_{\mathrm{univ}}\) degenerates to a point.
- The Belinfante-canonical test recovers the known obstruction \(\Omega_{ab} \neq \varpi_{ab}\) from the stabilizer condition, where \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}\) is thermal vorticity and \(\Omega_{\mu\nu}\) is the spin potential.
- The number of independent PGT-sensitive observables is bounded by the family-restricted fiber dimension, though no explicit numerical bound is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pseudo-gauge ambiguity in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, previously studied by Buzzegoli (2021) who showed that \(\Lambda\) polarization and axial vortical conductivity depend on the pseudo-gauge choice, and by Becattini and collaborators (2014, 2020, 2022) on the local-equilibrium density operator and freeze-out. The fibration structure provides a geometric interpretation of the tension between \(\Lambda\) polarization and \(\phi\)-meson spin alignment observed in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that the vorticity-dominated response sector must be enlarged with local field-correlation data as proposed by Sheng (2022). The framework enables cross-observable consistency relations and classifies Weyl-anomaly-induced currents as base observables.
