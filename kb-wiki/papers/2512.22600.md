# Bound States of $Ω$ Baryons in Light Nuclei

> We investigate bound states of light $Ω_{3x}$-clusters ($x = s, c$), motivated by the $Ω_{3s}N$ potential recently developed by the HAL QCD collaboration. To regularize this potential, we remove the deeply attractive core at $r < 0.4~\mathrm{fm}$ and parametrize the long-range component ($r > 0.4~\mathrm{fm}$) using a two-range Gaussian form. This procedure preserves the relevant two-body bound state energy while having a negligible effect on the $Ω_{3s}NN$ and $Ω_{3s}Ω_{3s}N$ systems. An effective $Ω_{3s}α$ potential is then constructed by fitting a two-range Gaussian function to the long-range component of the folding potential, enabling calculations of the bound state energies of the $Ω_{3s}α$, $Ω_{3s}αα$, and $Ω_{3s}Ω_{3s}α$ systems. The regularization procedure leads to a substantial reduction in bound state energies compared to those obtained with the original potential. We further extend the analysis to $Ω_{3c}$-cluster systems by introducing an $Ω_{3c}N$ interaction, derived by comparing the existing $Ω_{3s}Ω_{3s}$ and $Ω_{3c}Ω_{3c}$ potentials. Our results suggest that several parametrizations predict bound states in $Ω_{3c}$-containing clusters. Finally, the $Ω_{3s}Ω_{3s}$ interaction is described using a contact-like potential approach, motivated by the effective field theory.

## Key claim
The paper claims that bound states of $\Omega_{3s}$-clusters (strange) and $\Omega_{3c}$-clusters (charm) in light nuclei exist for several parametrizations, but this conclusion is conditional on a regularization procedure that removes the deeply attractive core of the HAL QCD $\Omega N$ potential at $r < 0.4$ fm, and the results for $\Omega_{3c}$-containing clusters are explicitly described as "hypothetical" due to the lack of experimental data.

## Method
The authors solve the non-relativistic three-body eigenvalue problem using configuration-space Faddeev equations in the $s$-wave approximation, treating particles as structureless point-like entities. They regularize the HAL QCD $\Omega N$ potential by removing the short-range attraction below $r = 0.4$ fm and fitting the long-range component ($r > 0.4$ fm) with a two-range Gaussian form, then construct an effective $\Omega_{3s}\alpha$ potential via a single-folding method using the $\Omega N$ potential and a Gaussian matter density for the $\alpha$ particle. For $\Omega_{3c}$ systems, they derive an $\Omega_{3c}N$ interaction by scaling the $\Omega_{3s}N$ potential using parameters $c_1$ and $c_2$ obtained from comparing the $\Omega_{3s}\Omega_{3s}$ and $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}$ potentials from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers
- The HAL QCD $\Omega N$ potential (P$_1$ set) gives a two-body binding energy $E_2 = -1.29$ MeV, scattering length $a_{\Omega N} = 6.4$ fm, and effective range $r_{\Omega N} = 1.3$ fm
- The regularized potential $\widetilde{V}_{\Omega N}$ gives $E_2 = -1.29$ MeV, $a_{\Omega N} = 6.2$ fm, and $r_{\Omega N} = 1.2$ fm
- The $\Omega NN$ system with the regularized potential has three-body ground state energy $E_3 = -19.92$ MeV
- The $\Omega\Omega N$ system with the regularized potential has $E_3 = -6.15$ MeV
- The $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}$ potential gives a two-body binding energy $E_2 = -5.68$ MeV, scattering length $a = 1.57$ fm, and effective range $r = 0.57$ fm
- For the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ system with parameters $c_1=1.35$, $c_2=1.3$: $E_3 = -18.26$ MeV, $a_{\Omega_{3c}N} = 7.61$ fm, $r_{\Omega_{3c}N} = 1.00$ fm
- For the $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}N$ system with parameters $c_1=1.1$, $c_2=1.3$: $E_3 = -35.35$ MeV, $a_{\Omega_{3c}N} = 2.29$ fm, $r_{\Omega_{3c}N} = 0.89$ fm
- The $\Omega\Omega$ potential gives $E_2 = -1.41$ MeV, scattering length $a = 6.4$ fm, effective range $r = 1.3$ fm

## Context
This work extends previous studies of $\Omega N$ bound states from the HAL QCD collaboration (Iritani 2019) and Faddeev calculations of $\Omega NN$ and $\Omega\Omega N$ systems by GV (2020) using momentum-space methods. It addresses the overbinding problem identified in earlier hyperspherical harmonics calculations (Zhang 2022, ESE 2023) by introducing a short-range cutoff motivated by the breakdown of the point-particle approximation at distances below the sum of baryon radii. The results enable future experimental searches for $\Omega$-hypernuclei and provide a framework for comparing strange and charmed baryon clusters, though the authors note that experimental data for the $^5_{\Omega}$He bound state are insufficient to constrain the potential depth.
