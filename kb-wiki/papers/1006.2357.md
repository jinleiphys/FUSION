# Droplets in the cold and dense linear sigma model with quarks

> The linear sigma model with quarks at very low temperatures provides an effective description for the thermodynamics of the strong interaction in cold and dense matter, being especially useful at densities found in compact stars and protoneutron star matter. Using the MSbar one-loop effective potential, we compute quantities that are relevant in the process of nucleation of droplets of quark matter in this scenario. In particular, we show that the model predicts a surface tension of Σ~ 5-15 MeV/fm^2, rendering nucleation of quark matter possible during the early post-bounce stage of core collapse supernovae. Including temperature effects and vacuum logarithmic corrections, we find a clear competition between these features in characterizing the dynamics of the chiral phase conversion, so that if the temperature is low enough the consistent inclusion of vacuum corrections could help preventing the nucleation of quark matter during the collapse process. We also discuss the first interaction corrections that come about at two-loop order.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model with quarks predicts a surface tension of Σ ~ 5–15 MeV/fm² for the chiral transition in cold and dense matter, which renders nucleation of quark matter possible during the early post-bounce stage of core collapse supernovae; however, if the temperature is low enough, the consistent inclusion of vacuum logarithmic corrections could help prevent nucleation during the collapse process.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop effective potential for the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) at finite quark chemical potential and very low temperatures, using the MSbar renormalization scheme. They integrated over quark fields while keeping quadratic fluctuations of the sigma field around the condensate, incorporating both medium contributions and vacuum logarithmic corrections. Nucleation parameters (surface tension, critical radius, correlation length, and nucleation rate) were extracted by fitting the effective potential to a quartic polynomial and applying the thin-wall approximation for bubble nucleation.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension Σ ~ 4–13 MeV/fm² across the metastable region (with values up to ~15 MeV/fm² near criticality)
- Critical chemical potential μ_crit = 305.03 MeV for the chiral transition in the LSMq without vacuum corrections
- Critical radius R_c < 10 fm over approximately 90% of the metastable regions
- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV used to fix the chiral condensate in vacuum
- Yukawa coupling g = 3.32, fixed from constituent quark mass M_q^vac = 1/3 × 938 MeV
- Sigma meson mass M_σ^vac ≈ 600 MeV used for parameter fixing
- With one-loop vacuum corrections included: λ² ≈ 16.65, v² ≈ 3296.89 MeV², Λ ≈ 16.48 MeV (MSbar scale)
- Without vacuum corrections: λ² ≈ 20, v² ≈ 7696.8 MeV²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the LSMq that focused primarily on thermal effects (Baym 1977, Scavenius 2000) by instead considering the cold and dense regime relevant for compact stars and protoneutron star matter. The authors build on the analysis of Mintz (2009) and Sagert (2008) regarding the possibility of quark matter nucleation in supernova explosions, providing the first systematic calculation of surface tension and nucleation parameters within the LSMq framework. The results enable more quantitative assessments of whether homogeneous nucleation of quark droplets can occur during core collapse supernovae and in neutron star interiors, and highlight the need to include vacuum logarithmic corrections that were often discarded in previous LSMq studies.
