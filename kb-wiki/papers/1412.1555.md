# Electromagnetic production of $KΣ$ on the nucleon near threshold

> Photo- and electroproduction of $KΣ$ have been investigated near their production thresholds by using an effective Lagrangian approach. For this purpose, the background amplitude is constructed from suitable Feynman diagrams, whereas the resonance terms are calculated by means of the Breit-Wigner form of multipoles. Experimental data available in the proton channels ($K^+Σ^0$ and $K^0Σ^+$) with energies up to 50 MeV above the thresholds have been utilized to extract the unknown parameters. In these proton channels the calculated observables fit nicely the experimental data, whereas in the neutron channels ($K^+Σ^-$ and $K^0Σ^0$) the predicted observables contain some uncertainties due to the the uncertainties in the values of helicity photon couplings. To this end, new $K^0Σ^+$ photoproduction data are urgently required. The present analysis indicates the validity of the $P_Λ= -\frac{1}{3} P_Σ$ relation derived a long time ago. In the electroproduction sector the present analysis confirms the smooth transition between photoproduction and low $Q^2$ electroproduction data. The effect of new Crystal Ball data is shown to be mild at the backward angles. It is also found that the electroproductions of $K^0Σ^+$ and $K^0Σ^0$ are practically not the suitable reactions for investigating the $K^0$ charge form factor, since the effect is small.

## Key claim

The effective Lagrangian approach with Breit-Wigner multipoles can describe photo- and electroproduction of KΣ on the proton near threshold (up to 50 MeV above threshold) using only four resonances, but predictions for neutron channels carry large uncertainties due to poorly known neutron helicity amplitudes.

## Method

The background amplitude was constructed from Feynman diagrams using an effective Lagrangian, while resonance terms were calculated using the Breit-Wigner form of multipoles for the N(1700)D13, Δ(1700)D33, N(1710)P11, and N(1720)P13 resonances. A total of 331 experimental data points from the K+Σ0 and K0Σ+ channels were fitted to extract unknown coupling constants and resonance parameters, with energies limited to 50 MeV above the production thresholds (W ≈ 1690-1740 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Threshold energies for the four channels: γ+p→K+Σ0 at Eγ = 1046 MeV (W = 1686 MeV), γ+p→K0Σ+ at 1048 MeV (1687 MeV), γ+n→K+Σ- at 1052 MeV (1691 MeV), γ+n→K0Σ0 at 1051 MeV (1690 MeV)
- Fit to 331 data points yielded χ²/N = 1.07 without the Λ(1800)S01 resonance and χ²/N = 1.05 with it
- Extracted KΣ branching ratios: β = 3.0×10⁻² for N(1700)D13, 6.4×10⁻⁵ for Δ(1700)D33, 5.5×10⁻³ for N(1710)P11, and 1.2×10⁻⁴ for N(1720)P13
- Extracted ratio r_{K1Kγ} = 2.99 (without Λ(1800)S01) and 2.07 (with Λ(1800)S01), compared to the Kaon-Maid value of -0.45
- Extracted resonance masses: N(1700)D13 at 1716 MeV, Δ(1700)D33 at 1692 MeV, N(1710)P11 at 1727 MeV, N(1720)P13 at 1700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analyses of K+Λ and K0Λ photoproduction near threshold (Mart, 2013) to all four isospin channels of KΣ production, and is part of the program to upgrade the Kaon-Maid phenomenological model. The analysis confirms the validity of the PΛ = -1/3 PΣ relation and the smooth transition between photoproduction and low Q² electroproduction, while showing that K0Σ+ and K0Σ0 electroproduction are not suitable for investigating the K0 charge form factor. New high-statistics K0Σ+ data from CLAS are needed to constrain the K1(1270) transition moment ratio and reduce uncertainties in neutron channel predictions.
