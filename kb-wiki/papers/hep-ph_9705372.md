# Meson properties in an extended nonlocal NJL model

> We consider a nonlocal version of the NJL model, based on a separable quark-quark interaction. The interaction is extended to include terms that bind vector and axial-vector mesons. The nonlocality means that no further regulator is required. Moreover the model is able to confine the quarks by generating a quark propagator without poles at real energies. Working in the ladder approximation, we calculate amplitudes in Euclidean space and discuss features of their continuation to Minkowski energies. Conserved currents are constructed and we demonstrate their consistency with various Ward identities. Various meson masses are calculated, along with their strong and electromagnetic decay amplitudes. We also calculate the electromagnetic form factor of the pion, as well as form factors associated with the processes gamma gamma^* to pi^0 and omega to pi^0 gamma^*. The results are found to lead to a satisfactory phenomenology and lend some dynamical support to the idea of vector-meson dominance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an extended nonlocal NJL model with a separable quark-quark interaction can provide a satisfactory phenomenological description of meson properties, including vector and axial-vector mesons, while also generating quark confinement through a propagator without poles at real energies. The authors note that the results "lend some dynamical support to the idea of vector-meson dominance," but this support is conditional on the specific choices of form factor and the prescription for analytically continuing Euclidean amplitudes to Minkowski energies.

## Method

The authors work in the ladder approximation at leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, using a nonlocal separable four-quark interaction with a Gaussian form factor f(p_E) = exp(-p_E^2 / Lambda^2) in Euclidean space. They solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, constructing conserved vector and axial currents with nonlocal terms to satisfy Ward identities. Amplitudes are calculated in Euclidean space and continued to Minkowski energies using the Cutkosky prescription to handle the complex poles of the confining quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV (used as input to fix parameters, along with m_pi = 138 MeV)
* Quark condensate in chiral limit: approximately -(250 MeV)^3 (from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation)
* Rho meson mass: m_rho = 770 MeV (calculated from the Bethe-Salpeter equation)
* Omega meson mass: m_omega = 782 MeV (calculated)
* a_1 meson mass: m_a1 = 1260 MeV (calculated)
* Pion-quark coupling constant: g_piqq = 3.0 (at the pion pole)
* Rho meson decay constant: f_rho = 216 MeV (from the rho to e+e- decay amplitude)

## Context

This work extends the nonlocal NJL model of Birse and Banerjee (1995), which previously only studied pions and their scalar partner, to include vector and axial-vector mesons. The model is motivated by the instanton-liquid approach of Dyakonov and Petrov, and differs from local NJL models (such as those reviewed by Klevansky, 1992) by providing a natural regularization and quark confinement. The results enable further studies of electromagnetic processes and tests of vector-meson dominance within a confining framework, and the authors note that next-to-leading order corrections in 1/N_c (as studied by Bijnens and Prades, 1997) could be significant, especially in the scalar-isoscalar channel.
