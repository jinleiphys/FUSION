# Pion transverse-momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss interferometry in a viscous granular source model

> We examine the evolution of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) droplets with viscous hydrodynamics and analyze the pion transverse-momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry in a granular source model consisting of viscous QGP droplets. The shear viscosity of the QGP droplet speeds up and slows down the droplet evolution in the central and peripheral regions of the droplet, respectively. The effect of the bulk viscosity on the evolution is negligible. Although there are viscous effects on the droplet evolution, the pion momentum spectrum and elliptic flow change little for granular sources with and without viscosity. On the other hand, the influence of viscosity on HBT radius $R_{\rm out}$ is considerable. It makes $R_{\rm out}$ decrease in the granular source model. We determine the model parameters of granular sources using the experimental data of pion transverse-momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and HBT radii together, and investigate the effects of viscosity on the model parameters. The results indicate that the granular source model may reproduce the experimental data of pion transverse-momentum spectrum, elliptic flow, and HBT radii in heavy-ion collisions of Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV in different centrality intervals. The viscosity of the droplet leads to an increase in the initial droplet radius and a decrease of the source shell parameter in the granular source model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that while shear viscosity has a dual effect on the evolution of QGP droplets (speeding up the central region and slowing down the periphery), its influence on the final pion transverse-momentum spectrum and elliptic flow is small in the granular source model due to the outward motion of whole droplets partially compensating for the viscous effects; however, viscosity significantly reduces the HBT radius R_out.

## Method

The authors model heavy-ion collisions using a granular source composed of spherical QGP droplets whose evolution is described by second-order Muller-Israel-Stewart viscous relativistic hydrodynamics in spherical geometry. They solve the hydrodynamic equations numerically using the HLLE algorithm with the s95p-PCE equation of state, taking shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at 3 times the AdS/CFT minimum (0.24) and bulk viscosity similarly scaled. The pion transverse-momentum spectrum, elliptic flow v2, and HBT correlation radii are calculated using the Cooper-Frye formula with viscous corrections to the distribution function, and the model parameters are determined by simultaneously fitting experimental data from Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV across multiple centrality intervals.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio taken as eta/s = 0.24 (3 times the minimum 1/(4pi)) for investigating viscous effects
- Initial energy density at droplet center taken as epsilon_0 = 2.2 GeV/fm^3
- Freeze-out temperature range used: 80 MeV < T_f < T_c = 165 MeV
- Woods-Saxon width parameter a = 0.1 r_0 for droplet initial energy density distribution
- Bulk relaxation time parametrization: tau_Pi = max[120 * xi/s, 0.1] fm/c
- Direct emission fraction f_dir = 0.75, with temperature widths Delta T_dir = 10 MeV and Delta T_dec = 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the granular source model previously developed by Zhang et al. (2004-2011) and Yang and Zhang (2014-2015), which used ideal hydrodynamics for droplet evolution and successfully reproduced pion spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii at RHIC and LHC energies. The present paper introduces viscous hydrodynamics to examine how shear and bulk viscosity modify the droplet evolution and the extracted model parameters. The results enable more realistic constraints on the initial droplet radius and source shell parameter when fitting experimental data, and suggest that the granular source model with viscosity can still reproduce the multi-observable data from Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies.
