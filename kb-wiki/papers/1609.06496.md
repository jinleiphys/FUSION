# Initial Temperature and Extent of Chemical Equilibration of Partons in Relativistic Collision of Heavy Nuclei

> We emphasize that a knowledge of energy and entropy densities of quark gluon plasma - a thermalized de-confined matter, formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions fixes the formation temperature and the product of gluon fugacity and formation time uniquely, {\em provided} we know the relative fugacities of quarks and gluons. This also provides that a smaller formation time would imply larger fugacities for partons. Next we explore the limits of chemical equilibration of partons during the initial stages in relativistic collision of heavy nuclei. The experimentally measured rapidity densities of transverse energy and charged particle multiplicity at RHIC and LHC energies are used to estimate the energy and number densities with the assumption of formation of a thermally equilibrated quark gluon plasma which may be chemically equilibrated to the same or differing extents for quarks and gluons. The estimates are found to be very sensitive to the correction factor used for the Björken energy density for identifying it with the initial energy density. The extent of chemical equilibration near the end of the QGP phase is inferred by solving master equations by including the processes $gg \leftrightarrow ggg$ and $gg \leftrightarrow q\overline{q}$ along with expansion and cooling of the plasma. The possible consequences for invariant mass distribution of intermediate mass dileptons radiated from the plasma are discussed which could distinguish between different scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the initial temperature and the product of gluon fugacity and formation time of a quark-gluon plasma can be uniquely determined from measured rapidity densities of transverse energy and charged particle multiplicity, provided the relative fugacities of quarks and gluons are known; however, these estimates are extremely sensitive to the correction factor applied to the Bjorken energy density, with a factor of 2 change in this correction altering the initial temperature by a factor of 2 and the fugacity by a factor of 8.

## Method

The authors use experimentally measured rapidity densities of transverse energy and charged particle multiplicity at RHIC and LHC energies to estimate initial energy and number densities, assuming formation of a thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma that may be chemically equilibrated to different extents for quarks and gluons. They solve master equations for the chemical reactions gg ↔ ggg and gg ↔ qq̅, including longitudinal expansion and cooling of the plasma, to infer the extent of chemical equilibration near the end of the QGP phase. The Bjorken energy density estimate is corrected by a factor f_Bj (taken as 1 or 2) to account for work done against pressure and viscous effects.

## Key numbers

- For a thermally and chemically equilibrated plasma with f_Bj = 1, the initial temperature at RHIC (200 AGeV) is approximately 0.196 GeV and at LHC (2.76 ATeV) is approximately 0.280 GeV.
- For a thermally and chemically equilibrated plasma with f_Bj = 1, the formation time at RHIC (200 AGeV) is about 1.0 fm/c and at LHC (2.76 ATeV) is about 1.3-1.4 fm/c.
- When f_Bj is increased from 1 to 2, the initial temperature increases by a factor of 2 and the initial fugacities λ_g(τ_0) = λ_q(τ_0) decrease by a factor of 8 for a formation time of 1 fm/c.
- For a thermally equilibrated plasma with λ_g(τ_0) = λ_q(τ_0) and τ_0 = 1 fm/c, the product λ_g(τ_0)τ_0 at RHIC (0.2 ATeV) is 2.046 fm for f_Bj = 1.
- For a thermally equilibrated plasma with λ_g(τ_0) = 5λ_q(τ_0) and τ_0 = 1 fm/c, the product λ_g(τ_0)τ_0 at RHIC (0.2 ATeV) is 3.239 fm for f_Bj = 1.
- The initial temperature for the case λ_g(τ_0) = 5λ_q(τ_0) with α = 0.2 is about 6% larger than for the equal fugacity case, with the fraction (a_1 + 2α b_1)/(a_2 + 2α b_2) ≈ 0.357.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chemical equilibration in quark-gluon plasma by Biro et al. (1993), Munshi et al. (1995), and Duncan et al. (1997), who used initial conditions from self-screened parton cascade models or HIJING. The paper uses the more recent expression for the rate R_3 from Wang et al. (2015) for the gg ↔ ggg process, which avoids unphysical behavior for large gluon fugacities. The results enable future work to distinguish between different chemical equilibration scenarios using intermediate mass dilepton radiation, and suggest that a correction factor f_Bj ≈ 2 with a formation time of 0.2 fm/c would support the common assumption of a chemically equilibrated plasma in electromagnetic radiation calculations.
