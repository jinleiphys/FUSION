# Pentaquark Theta^+(1540) production in gamma N --> K Anti-K N

> The recent developments in the search of exotic pentaquark hadrons are briefly reviewed. We then focus on investigating how the exotic pentaquark $풎(1540)$ baryon production can be identified in the $풥N \to K \bar{K} N$ reactions, focusing on the influence of the background (non-$풎$ production) mechanisms. By imposing the SU(3) symmetry and using various quark model predictions, we are able to fix the coupling constants for evaluating the kaon backgrounds, the $K\bar{K}$ production through the intermediate vector meson and tensor meson photoproduction, and the mechanisms involving intermediate $풑(1116)$, $풑(1405)$, $풑(1520)$, $풖(1193)$, $풖(1385)$, and $풊(1232)$ states. The total cross section data of $풥p \to K^+K^- p$ is then used to fix the form factors which regularize the background amplitudes so that the signal of $풎(1540)$ in $풥n \to K^+K^-n$ and $풥p \to K^0\bar{K}^0p$ cross sections can be predicted. We find that the predicted $K^+K^-$ and $K^+ n$ invariant mass distributions of the $풥n \to K^+ K^- n$ reaction can qualitatively reproduce the shapes of the JLab data. However, the predicted $풎(1540)$ peak can not be identified unambiguously with the data. High statistics experiments are needed to resolve the problem. We also find that an even-parity $풎$ is more likely to be detected, while it will be difficult to identify an odd-parity $풎$, even if it exists, from the background continuum, if its coupling constants are small as in the present quark model predictions.

## Key claim

The authors model the $\gamma N \to K \bar{K} N$ reaction to test whether the $\Theta(1540)$ pentaquark signal can be unambiguously identified above the non-resonant background, and find that the predicted $\Theta(1540)$ peak cannot be identified unambiguously with existing JLab data; an even-parity $\Theta$ is more likely to be detected, while an odd-parity $\Theta$ would be difficult to identify from the background continuum if its coupling constants are small as in present quark model predictions.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level amplitudes for $\gamma N \to K \bar{K} N$ including four classes of diagrams: $t$-channel Drell-type diagrams, vector meson and tensor meson photoproduction backgrounds, hyperon backgrounds (involving $\Lambda(1116)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1520)$, $\Sigma(1193)$, $\Sigma(1385)$, and $\Delta(1232)$ states), and $\Theta(1540)$ production. Coupling constants were fixed using SU(3) symmetry and quark model predictions, and form factors were regularized by fitting to total cross section data of $\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p$. The model was then used to predict observables for $\gamma n \to K^+ K^- n$ and $\gamma p \to K^0 \bar{K}^0 p$.

## Key numbers

- The $\rho NN$ coupling constants used: $g_{\rho NN} = 3.1$, $\kappa_\rho = 2.0$; the $\omega NN$ coupling constants: $g_{\omega NN} = 10.3$, $\kappa_\omega = 0.0$.
- The $\rho KK$ coupling constant: $g_{\rho KK} = g_{\omega KK} = 3.02$, derived from SU(3) symmetry as $g_{\rho\pi\pi}/2$.
- The $\phi KK$ coupling constant: $g_{\phi KK} = -4.49$, deduced from the experimental $\Gamma(\phi \to K^+ K^-)$ decay width.
- The $K^* K \pi$ coupling constant: $g_{K^* K \pi} = 6.56$, determined from the experimental $\Gamma(K^* \to K \pi)$ width.
- The $\omega\rho\pi$ coupling constant: $g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 14.9$ GeV$^{-1}$, from the hidden gauge approach with $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.
- The $\gamma K^* K$ coupling constants: $g_{\gamma K^* K}^0 = -0.388$ GeV$^{-1}$ (neutral) and $g_{\gamma K^* K}^c = 0.254$ GeV$^{-1}$ (charged), from radiative decay widths of $K^*$ vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the earlier investigation by Nakayama and Tsushima (2003) by including all four classes of background amplitudes (Drell diagrams, vector meson, tensor meson, and hyperon backgrounds) in a single model, unlike prior studies such as Dzierba et al. (2004) which omitted Drell and hyperon contributions, or Titov et al. (2004) which omitted Drell and hyperon backgrounds. The authors also improve on the tensor meson photoproduction model of Dzierba et al. (2004) by including all possible vector meson exchanges rather than $\pi^0$ exchange, which is forbidden by $C$ parity for neutral tensor mesons. The results enable future high-statistics experiments to resolve whether the $\Theta(1540)$ signal is genuine or an artifact of background mechanisms.
