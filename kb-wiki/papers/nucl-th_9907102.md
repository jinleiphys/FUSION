# Chiral symmetry in electromagnetically induced resonant two hadron production

> We report on recent progress on the chiral unitary approach, analogous to the effective range expansion in Quantum Mechanics, which is shown to have a much larger convergence radius than ordinary chiral perturbation theory, allowing one to reproduce data for meson meson interaction up to 1.2 GeV. Applications to physical processes so far unsuited for a standard chiral perturbative approach are presented, in which two hadrons, either two mesons or a meson and a baryon, in a resonant state are produced in photoproduction processes.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach, which implements exact unitarity and a chiral expansion of Re \(T^{-1}\) rather than of \(T\) itself, extends the convergence radius of chiral perturbation theory to reproduce meson-meson interaction data up to 1.2 GeV, whereas ordinary chiral perturbation theory is limited to about \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 400\) MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel \(K\)-matrix formalism where unitarity is enforced exactly, and expand Re \(T^{-1}\) in powers of the external four-momentum of the lightest pseudoscalars. They then apply this framework to photoproduction reactions, using a Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest-order chiral amplitude \(T_2\) as the potential and the loop function \(G\) regularized with a cutoff, to generate resonant two-hadron final states such as \(\Lambda(1405)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\).

## Key numbers

- The chiral unitary approach reproduces meson-meson interaction data up to 1.2 GeV, while ordinary chiral perturbation theory is limited to about \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 400\) MeV.
- For the \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda(1405)\) reaction, the photon energy chosen is \(E_\gamma = 1.7\) GeV.
- The coupled-channel treatment increases the \(K^-p \rightarrow \gamma \Sigma^0\) rate by about a factor of 16 relative to the Born approximation.
- For \(\gamma p \rightarrow p \pi^+ \pi^-\), the estimated background cross section is around 45 \(\mu\)b at \(E_\gamma = 1.7\) GeV, providing a background of about 55 \(\mu\)b/GeV, while the resonant \(f_0(980)\) peak has about 2.5 \(\mu\)b/GeV strength (a 5% signal-to-background ratio assuming no interference).
- The \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 \pi^0 p\) cross section is about eight times smaller than the \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- p\) cross section, giving a signal-to-background ratio of 20% for the \(f_0(980)\) in the \(\pi^0 \pi^0\) channel.
- The \(\pi^0 \pi^0\) cross section is \(\frac{1}{2}\) of the \(\pi^+ \pi^-\) one due to the symmetry factor.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by the authors and collaborators (Oset et al., 1998; Oller et al., 1998) to photoproduction processes, which were previously unsuited for standard chiral perturbation theory. It enables experimental tests of dynamically generated resonances such as the \(\Lambda(1405)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\) at facilities like SPring8/RCNP and TJNAF, and opens the door to studying the modification of these resonances in nuclear media, with implications for \(K^-\) atoms and possible kaon condensation in neutron stars.
