# On the influence of the magnetic field of the GSI experimental storage ring on the time-modulation of the EC-decay rates of the H-like mother ions

> We investigate the influence of the magnetic field of the Experimental storage ring (ESR) at GSI on the periodic time-dependence of the orbital K-shell electron capture decay $(EC$) rates of the H--like heavy ions. We approximate the magnetic field of the ESR by a uniform magnetic field. Unlike the assertion by Lambiase et al., arXiv: 0811.2302 [nucl-th], we show that a motion of the H-like heavy ion in a uniform magnetic field cannot be the origin of the periodic time-dependence of the EC-decay rates of the H-like heavy ions.

## Key claim

The authors show that a motion of H-like heavy ions in a uniform magnetic field, approximating the field of the GSI experimental storage ring (ESR), cannot be the origin of the periodic time-dependence of the electron-capture (EC) decay rates, contrary to the assertion by Lambiase et al. (2008).

## Method

The authors model the magnetic field of the ESR as a constant uniform field and calculate the EC-decay rate using the standard weak interaction Hamiltonian, including the interaction of the electron and nuclear spins with the magnetic field via the Bohr and nuclear magnetons, as well as Thomas precession corrections. They compute the time-dependent probability for the mother H-like ion to survive, using the wave function for the ground state (1s) with total angular momentum F = 1/2, and then average this probability over the neutrino angular distribution in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- The calculated frequency from the spin-rotation coupling in the uniform magnetic field is ω_B = 1.34 × 10^11 s^{-1}.
- The corresponding period of time modulation from the magnetic field is T_B = 4.70 × 10^{-11} s.
- The magnetic field strength used is B_0 = 1.19703 T, and the Lorentz factor is γ = 1.43.
- For the H-like ion 140Pr58+, the nuclear g-factor is g_I = 2.5 and the nuclear mass is M_I = 130324.46 MeV.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the proposal by Lambiase et al. (2008) that the GSI oscillations observed by Litvinov et al. (2008) could originate from the magnetic field of the storage ring. The authors instead support their own earlier explanation (Ivanov et al., 2008) that the periodic time-dependence arises from an interference of two neutrino mass eigenstates, with periods obeying an A-scaling (T_EC = A/20 s) that the magnetic-field mechanism cannot reproduce. The result rules out a simple magnetic-field origin for the GSI oscillations and reinforces the need for a neutrino-mass-based interpretation.
