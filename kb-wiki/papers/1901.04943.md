# Directed flow of photons in Cu+Au collisions at RHIC

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the positions of nucleons in two colliding identical nuclei can lead to non-uniform initial energy density distribution on the transverse plane. In addition to initial state fluctuations, the difference in the number of participating nucleons in collision of two non-identical nuclei can also result in significant anisotropy in the initial geometry and energy density distributions. Thus, Cu+Au collisions are expected to provide interesting new aspects in the understanding of anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions. We calculate directed flow co-efficient $v_1$ of thermal photons using a hydrodynamic model with fluctuating initial conditions at 200A GeV Cu+Au collisions at RHIC and compare it with the elliptic and triangular flow parameters obtained at same initial conditions. The photon $v_1$ as a function of transverse momentum is found to be non-zero and significantly large. However, it shows a different nature compared to the elliptic and triangular flow parameters. The $v_1$ is found to be completely dominated by QGP radiation in the region $1 < p_T < 6$ GeV and contribution from the hadronic phase to photon $v_1$ is only marginal. At $p_T < 2$ GeV, it is negative and it decreases further with smaller values of $p_T$. However, at $p_T > 2 $ GeV, $v_1$ is positive and rises slowly with $p_T$. In addition, the photon $v_1$ is found to be more sensitive to the initial formation time of the plasma compared to the elliptic and triangular flow parameters. We suggest that a simultaneous measurements of photon $v_n$ co-efficients, (n=1, 2, 3) can provide valuable information about the initial state produced in heavy ion collisions as well as help us understanding the direct photon puzzle.

## Key claim

The directed flow coefficient \(v_1\) of thermal photons in Cu+Au collisions at 200A GeV is non-zero and significantly large, but its sign changes with transverse momentum: negative below about 2.5 GeV and positive above that value. This behavior is entirely dominated by QGP radiation for \(1 < p_T < 6\) GeV, with only marginal contribution from the hadronic phase, unlike the elliptic and triangular flow coefficients where hadronic contributions are significant.

## Method

The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant event-by-event ideal relativistic hydrodynamic model with Monte Carlo Glauber fluctuating initial conditions to simulate Cu+Au collisions at 200A GeV at RHIC. Photon production rates from the QGP phase were taken from complete leading-order rates, and from the hadronic phase using parameterized rates; the space-time integration of these rates over 400 fluctuating events yielded the thermal photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_1\), \(v_2\), and \(v_3\) for the 20–30% centrality bin.

## Key numbers

- Photon \(v_1\) is negative for \(p_T < 2.5\) GeV and positive for \(p_T > 2.5\) GeV, rising slowly with \(p_T\) at higher momenta.
- Photon \(v_1\) is completely dominated by QGP radiation in the region \(1 < p_T < 6\) GeV.
- The initial formation time \(\tau_0\) was taken as 0.17 fm (default) and also as 0.51 fm (three times larger) to test sensitivity; larger \(\tau_0\) increases \(v_1\) at high \(p_T\).
- The charged particle multiplicity used to fix the normalization constant K was \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta \approx 140\) for the 20–30% centrality bin.
- The constant K in the initial entropy density distribution was taken as 81; the Gaussian smearing parameter \(\sigma\) was 0.4 fm; the freeze-out temperature \(T_F\) was 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of photon anisotropic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, where experimental data for \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) exceed hydrodynamic predictions (the "direct photon puzzle"). It also builds on prior calculations of photon \(v_1\) in Au+Au collisions by Shen (2017), which found the largest \(v_1\) signal near the phase transition temperature. The use of asymmetric Cu+Au collisions, as proposed by Bozek (2012), provides a new probe of initial-state geometry and fluctuations; the authors suggest that simultaneous measurement of photon \(v_1\), \(v_2\), and \(v_3\) in such collisions could help resolve the direct photon puzzle and constrain the initial formation time of the QGP.
