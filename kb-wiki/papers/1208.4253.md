# Gauge invariant definition of the jet quenching parameter

> In the framework of Soft-Collinear Effective Theory, the jet quenching parameter, $\hat{q}$, has been evaluated by adding the effect of Glauber gluon interactions to the propagation of a highly-energetic collinear parton in a medium. The result, which holds in covariant gauges, has been expressed in terms of the expectation value of two Wilson lines stretching along the direction of the four-momentum of the parton. In this paper, we show how that expression can be generalized to an arbitrary gauge by the addition of transverse Wilson lines. The transverse Wilson lines are explicitly computed by resumming interactions of the parton with Glauber gluons that appear only in non-covariant gauges. As an application of our result, we discuss the contribution to $\hat{q}$ coming from transverse momenta of order $g^2T$ in a medium that is a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper derives a gauge-invariant expression for the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ by adding transverse Wilson lines to the previously known covariant-gauge expression, which only holds in covariant gauges; the result is valid for arbitrary gauges, provided the transverse Wilson lines are included to account for Glauber gluon interactions that appear only in non-covariant gauges.

## Method

The authors work within Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) extended to include Glauber gluons, computing the transverse momentum broadening of a highly-energetic collinear quark propagating through a medium. They first review the calculation in covariant gauges, then perform the calculation explicitly in the light-cone gauge $A^+(x)=0$, where the singular part of the transverse gluon field requires new interaction vertices and the introduction of transverse Wilson lines. The result is generalized to arbitrary gauges by adding transverse Wilson lines to the covariant-gauge expression, and the contribution from transverse momenta of order $g^2T$ is discussed for a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ is defined as $\hat{q} = \frac{1}{L}\langle k_\perp^2\rangle$, where $L$ is the distance traveled through the medium and $k_\perp$ is the transverse momentum picked up.
- The collinear quark initial momentum is $q_0 = (0, Q, 0)$ with $Q$ the large scale; the small dimensionless ratio is $\lambda = T/Q \ll 1$, where $T$ is the medium temperature.
- Glauber gluon momenta scale as $Q(\lambda^2, \lambda, \lambda)$; all components of such Glauber fields scale like $Q\lambda^2$.
- In light-cone gauge, the singular part of the transverse Glauber field scales as $A^{\mathrm{sin},i}_\perp(x) \sim Q\lambda$, enhanced by one power of $1/\lambda$ compared to the covariant gauge case.
- The contribution to $\hat{q}$ from transverse momenta of order $g^2T$ is discussed for a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma of temperature $T$.

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based calculation of the jet quenching parameter by D'Eramo, Liu, and Rajagopal (2010) from covariant gauges to arbitrary gauges, resolving a gauge-invariance issue noted in that earlier work. The result enables connections to thermal field theory and lattice gauge theory calculations of $\hat{q}$ following the approach of Caron-Huot (2008) and Laine (2012), and generalizes the gauge-invariant expression for $\hat{q}$ that was previously limited to second order in the opacity expansion by Ovanesyan and Vitev (2011).
