# Exciting pions in nuclei: DCX and Electroproduction in the resonance region

> We describe the double charge exchange (DCX) reaction of pions with different nuclear targets at incident kinetic energies of 120-180 MeV within the GiBUU transport framework. The DCX process is highly sensitive to details of the interactions of pions with the nuclear medium and, therefore, represents a major benchmark for any model of pion scattering off nuclei at low and intermediate energies. We achieve a good quantitative agreement with the extensive data set obtained at LAMPF. Furthermore, we present first results on electron scattering off nuclei for beam energies of 700-1500 MeV and virtualities of Q^2<700 MeV^2. Including quasi-elastic and pion- production processes and employing in-medium kinematics and realistic form factors, we evaluate inclusive cross sections and compare to data obtained at the ADONE storage ring (Frascati).

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model achieves good quantitative agreement with LAMPF data for pion double charge exchange (DCX) on nuclear targets at incident kinetic energies of 120-180 MeV, but the scaling law of Gram et al. (1989) is only approximately reproduced because two opposing effects, neutron skins and Coulomb forces, compensate each other.

## Method

The authors used the semi-classical Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model, which evolves single-particle phase-space densities for pions, nucleons, and Delta(1232) resonances via coupled Boltzmann equations with collision terms based on resonance contributions from Manley et al. and background terms. For DCX, they simulated sequential single charge-exchange reactions on O-16, Ca-40, and Pb-208 targets at pion kinetic energies of 120, 150, and 180 MeV, including Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Coulomb forces, collisional broadening of the Delta resonance, and mean-field potentials for nucleons and Deltas. For electron scattering, they employed impulse approximation with in-medium kinematics and form factors from Budd et al. (2003) and Krutov et al. (2002) for quasi-elastic scattering, and MAID parametrizations for pion production, comparing inclusive cross sections to ADONE data from Anghinolfi et al. (1996).

## Key numbers

- DCX total cross sections on O-16, Ca-40, and Pb-208 at pion kinetic energies of 120, 150, and 180 MeV show good quantitative agreement with LAMPF data from Wood et al. (1992)
- For Pb-208 at 180 MeV, a neutron skin of R_n - R_p = 0.266 fm enhances the (pi+, pi-) DCX total cross section by roughly 35% compared to the case with equal neutron and proton radii
- Electron scattering calculations cover beam energies of 700-1500 MeV and virtualities Q^2 up to 632 MeV^2, compared to data from the ADONE storage ring (Frascati)
- The scaling law sigma_tot ~ A^(2/3) * Q/(A-Q) * (Q-1)/(A-1) is approximately reproduced in GiBUU simulations at 180 MeV across targets

## Context

This work extends prior GiBUU studies of pion re-scattering in photon-induced double-pion production by Muhlich et al. (2004) and Buss et al. (2006), and builds on earlier Boltzmann-equation approaches to pion-nucleus scattering by Huefner and Thies and cascade-model work by Vicente et al. (1988). The DCX results demonstrate that the semi-classical transport approach successfully passes a demanding benchmark for pion dynamics in nuclei, while the electron scattering calculations provide a testbed for neutrino-nucleus calculations by Leitner et al. (2006) and enable future studies of exclusive channels and neutron skin determination via forward-angle DCX measurements.
