# In Search of Truth: In memory of Balraj Singh

> Born in Punjab (India) in December 1941, Balraj Singh is not only the single most prolific nuclear data evaluator and disseminator of nuclear structure and decay data with 148 evaluations in Nuclear Data Sheets -- 85 as the first and often only author -- plus other journals, but his upmost curiosity and dedication brought him to be one of the finest nuclear physicists, with an everlasting influence on many of us. Balraj passed away about a year ago on 9 October 2023 in Ottawa, Ontario (Canada) at the age of 81, and at Atomic Data and Nuclear Data Tables we would like to commemorate some of his scientific achievements.

## Key claim

This memorial article honors Balraj Singh as the single most prolific nuclear data evaluator in the history of Nuclear Data Sheets, with 148 evaluations (85 as first and often only author), but emphasizes that his deeper contribution was resolving discrepancies between experimental measurements and theoretical predictions through a combination of evaluation, experimental participation, and theoretical understanding.

## Method

The paper reviews Singh's career contributions to nuclear structure and decay data evaluation, including his work on resolving specific discrepancies such as those between Coulomb-excitation and lifetime measurements in nickel and tin isotopes, the anomalous behavior of the first 2+ excited states in 94Zr, and his development of evaluation codes such as AlphaHF for alpha-decay hindrance factors and KeynunberCheck for NSR keynumber format verification. It also describes his mentorship of students at McMaster University and participation in experiments at the University of Kentucky, Chalk River, and TRIUMF.

## Key numbers

- 148 evaluations in Nuclear Data Sheets, with 85 as first and often only author
- 81 years old at time of death on 9 October 2023 in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada
- Born in December 1941 in Punjab, India
- 80 published mass chains out of 300 in ENSDF have his name (27% of all mass chains)

## Context

This memorial extends the tribute published in Nuclear Data Sheets (Dimitriou, 2024) by providing additional personal and scientific details about Singh's career. It highlights his role in resolving specific nuclear physics controversies, including the lower E2 transition probabilities in neutron-deficient nickel isotopes that led to new JUN45 shell-model interactions (Honma, 2009) and new Coulomb-excitation (Allmond, 2014) and lifetime measurements (Orce, 2008; Chakraborty, 2011), as well as the asymmetric behavior of E2 transition probabilities in tin isotopes (Pritychenko, 2016; Maheshwari, 2016) and the anomalous collectivity in 94Zr (Elhami, 2007; Chakraborty, 2013). The article enables future evaluators to continue Singh's legacy of critical data assessment and discrepancy resolution.
