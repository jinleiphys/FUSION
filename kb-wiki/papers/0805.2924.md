# Atmospheric, long baseline, and reactor neutrino data constraints on $θ_{13}$

> A new atmospheric neutrino oscillation tool which utilizes full three neutrino oscillation probabilities and a full three neutrino treatment of the MSW effect is combined with a standard analysis of the K2K, MINOS, and CHOOZ data to examine the bounds on $θ_{13}$ implied by existing data, including the recent, more finely binned, Super-K atmospheric data. In the region $L/E_ν\gtrsim 10^4$ km/GeV, we have previously found that the sub-dominant expansion does not converge and that terms linear in $θ_{13}$ can be significant. The current analysis confirms this and leads to the conclusion that $θ_{13}$ is bounded from above by the atmospheric data while CHOOZ provides the lower bound. We trace the origin of this result to fully contained data in the previously mentioned very long baseline region, to a combination of a quadratic in $θ_{13}$ term in ${\mathcal P}_{ee}$ and a linear term in ${\mathcal P}_{eμ}$ and their contribution to $R_e$, a broad MSW resonance for the solar mass-squared difference at 180 MeV, and an increase in $θ_{12}$ due to this resonant matter effect which alters the sign of the linear in $θ_{13}$ term. Assuming CP is conserved in the lepton sector, we find $θ_{13}=-0.07^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$, the asymmetry being a reflection of the importance of the linear in $θ_{13}$ terms.

## Key claim

The paper finds that atmospheric neutrino data, when analyzed with full three-neutrino oscillation probabilities and the full MSW effect, bound θ₁₃ from above, while CHOOZ provides the lower bound, yielding an asymmetric allowed region θ₁₃ = -0.07⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀.₁₁ (90% C.L.) under the assumption of CP conservation.

## Method

The authors constructed a new atmospheric neutrino oscillation analysis tool that uses exact three-neutrino oscillation probabilities and a full three-neutrino treatment of the MSW effect through a two-density Earth model (mantle 4.5 g/cm³, core 11.5 g/cm³). They combined this with standard analyses of the K2K, MINOS, and CHOOZ experiments, using the more finely binned Super-Kamiokande atmospheric data. Statistical errors were handled via chi-square, systematic errors via the pull method with 43 pulls, and the sub-dominant approximation (setting Δ₂₁=0) was used as a comparison.

## Key numbers

- θ₁₃ = -0.07⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀.₁₁ at 90% confidence level (Δχ²=6.25 for a three-parameter analysis)
- In the sub-dominant approximation, sin²θ₁₃ < 0.14 (|θ₁₃| < 0.38) for Δχ² < 4.6 from atmospheric data alone
- Solar parameters fixed at best-fit values: θ₁₂ = 0.58 and Δ₂₁ = 8.0 × 10⁻⁵ eV²
- Earth mantle density: 4.5 g/cm³; core density: 11.5 g/cm³ with radius 3486 km
- MSW resonance occurs near E_ν = 100 MeV for mantle density
- The very long baseline region where linear terms become significant: L/E_ν ≳ 10⁴ km/GeV
- For θ₁₃ = ±0.15, Δχ² ∼ 9; sub-GeV fully contained events contribute two-thirds of this, with half (4.5) coming from a single angular bin (-0.8 < cosϑ < -0.6) for fully contained e-like events at the two lowest energy bins (p_ℓ < 400 MeV/c)

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Roa, Latimer, and Ernst (2005) that identified significant linear-in-θ₁₃ terms for very long baselines, and by Gonzalez-Garcia and Maltoni (2007) who gave |θ₁₃| ≤ 0.15. It contradicts the assumption that the sub-dominant approximation is sufficient for extracting θ₁₃, showing that full three-neutrino MSW effects produce asymmetric bounds. The results enable future reactor experiments (Daya Bay, Double CHOOZ, RENO) and long-baseline experiments (T2K, NOvA) to understand how their measurements of θ₁₃² and linear-in-θ₁₃ effects will interplay with atmospheric data.
