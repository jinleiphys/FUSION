# Neutron electromagnetic form factors and inclusive scattering of polarized electrons by polarized $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H targets

> The electromagnetic inclusive responses of polarized $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H are thoroughly investigated at the quasielastic peak for squared momentum transfers up to $2 (GeV/c)^2$, within the plane wave impulse approximation. Great emphasys is put on the effects in the bound-state due to different two- and three-body nuclear forces, and to the Coulomb interaction as well. A careful analysis of the polarized responses allows to select possible experiments for minimizing the model dependence in the extraction of the neutron electromagnetic form factors. In particular, the relevant role played by the proton in the transverse-longitudinal response of polarized $^{3}$He, at low momentum transfer, can be utilized for obtaining valuable information on the proton contribution to the total polarized response and eventually on the neutron charge form factor.

# Neutron electromagnetic form factors and inclusive scattering of polarized electrons by polarized $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H targets

## Key claim

Within the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), the model dependence of the neutron electromagnetic form factors extracted from polarized $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H inclusive responses can be minimized by a suitable choice of kinematics and target, but the proton contribution to the transverse-longitudinal response of $^{3}$He remains a significant obstacle, especially at low momentum transfer. The authors propose that a measurement of the polarization angle where the proton contribution vanishes could yield the ratio of proton contributions to the transverse and transverse-longitudinal responses, and that future experiments with polarized $^{3}$H could enable an almost model-independent extraction of both neutron electric and magnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors calculated the spin-dependent spectral functions of $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H from three-body wave functions obtained using the Pair correlated Hyperspherical-Harmonic (PHH) basis, with different realistic two-body nuclear forces (Argonne Av14 and Reid soft-core RSCv8) and three-nucleon interactions (Brazil and Tucson-Melbourne). The electromagnetic inclusive polarized responses at the quasielastic peak were evaluated within the PWIA for squared momentum transfers up to $2~(GeV/c)^2$, using the CC1 prescription for the off-energy-shell nucleon electromagnetic current and including the Coulomb interaction for $^{3}$He.

## Key numbers

- Neutron effective polarization in $^{3}$He: ${\cal{P}}^n \approx 86-87\%$ across all interactions considered (e.g., 87.37% for Av14 without Coulomb, 86.65% for Av14+Cou+BR, 86.19% for RSCv8+Cou)
- Proton effective polarization in $^{3}$He: small and negative, ranging from $-2.49\%$ (Av14+Cou) to $-2.77\%$ (Av14+Cou+BR)
- Triton experimental binding energy used to adjust three-body force cut-off: $B(^{3}{\rm H}) = 8.48$ MeV
- $^{3}$H binding energies from calculations: 7.683 MeV (Av14), 7.600 MeV (RSCv8), 8.485 MeV (Av14+BR and Av14+TM)
- $^{3}$He binding energies: 7.032 MeV (Av14), 6.958 MeV (RSCv8), 7.809 MeV (Av14+BR and Av14+TM)
- Asymmetry variations due to initial state interaction at the quasielastic peak: less than 2% for kinematics where $A_{peak} \propto R^{^{3}He}_{T'}$ (Gao kinematics), less than 4% for kinematics where $A_{peak} \propto R^{^{3}He}_{TL'}$ (Han kinematics), at $Q^2 \le 0.2~(GeV/c)^2$
- Variations in $R_{T'}/G_D^2$ and $R_{TL'}/G_D^2$ due to different nuclear forces and Coulomb interaction: less than 5-6% over the whole range $Q^2$ up to $2~(GeV/c)^2$
- Proposed momentum transfer range for transverse-longitudinal polarized response measurement in $^{3}$He: $0.1 \le Q^2 \le 0.3~(GeV/c)^2$
- Differences between CC1 and CC2 prescriptions for off-energy-shell nucleon tensor: less than 2% for proton and neutron contributions to $R_{T'}$, except 3% variation for the negligible neutron contribution in $^{3}$H

## Context

This work extends earlier PWIA calculations of polarized $^{3}$He responses by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (CPS) and Sauer, using more accurate variational wave functions from the PHH method and including both Coulomb interaction and three-body forces. The authors compare their asymmetry predictions with experimental data from Gao (1994) and Han (1995), finding that initial state interaction effects on the asymmetry are small (3-4% at most) at low momentum transfer. The paper enables future experiments at TJNAF by identifying specific kinematics where the proton contribution can be minimized or separately measured, and proposes that polarized $^{3}$H measurements could complement $^{3}$He data to achieve an almost model-independent extraction of both neutron electric and magnetic form factors.
