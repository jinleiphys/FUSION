# Quadrupole Oscillations as Paradigm of the Chaotic Motion in Nuclei. Part 1

> We have presented a complete description of classical dynamics generated by the Hamiltonian of quadrupole nuclear oscillations and identified those peculiarities of quantum dynamics that can be interpreted as quantum manifestations of classical stochasticity. Particular attention has been given to investigation of classical dynamics in the potential energy surface with a few local minima. A new technique is suggested for determination of the critical energy of the transition to chaos. It is simpler than criteria of transition to chaos connected with one or another version of overlap resonances criterion. We have numerically demonstrated that for potential with a localized unstable region motion becomes regular at the high energy again, i.e. transition regularity-chaos-regularity {R - C - R} takes place for these potentials. The variations of statistical properties of energy spectrum in the process of {R - C - R) transition have been studied in detail. We proved that the type of the classical motion is correlated with the structure of the eigenfunctions of highly excited states in the (R - C - R) transition. Shell structure destruction induced by the increase of nonintegrable perturbation was analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for quadrupole nuclear oscillations with a potential containing a localized unstable region, the classical motion exhibits a regularity-chaos-regularity (R-C-R) transition as energy increases, meaning motion becomes regular again at high energies rather than remaining chaotic. The authors propose a new technique for determining the critical energy of transition to chaos based on the sign of Gaussian curvature of the potential energy surface, which they claim is simpler than overlap resonance criteria.

## Method

The authors study classical dynamics generated by a C3v-symmetric Hamiltonian for quadrupole nuclear oscillations, which generalizes the Henon-Heiles potential but ensures finite motion at all energies. They perform scaling analysis to reduce the Hamiltonian to a one-parameter form depending only on the dimensionless parameter W = b^2/(ac), then investigate the geometry of the potential energy surface across different parameter regimes (0<W<16, W>16, W<0). The critical energy for transition to chaos is estimated using the negative curvature criterion (NCC), where Ecr equals the lowest energy on the zero Gaussian curvature line of the potential energy surface.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter W = b^2/(ac) uniquely characterizes the reduced Hamiltonian and corresponding equations of motion
- The separatrix in parameter space occurs at W = 16, dividing structurally stable regions of the potential
- For 0<W<16, the potential has 1 critical point (1 minimum, 0 saddles, 0 maxima)
- For W>16, the potential has 7 critical points (4 minima, 3 saddles, 0 maxima)
- For W<0, the potential has 7 critical points (3 minima, 3 saddles, 1 maximum)
- The boundary between subregions for W<0 occurs at W = -1/2, where both eigenvalues of the stability matrix vanish simultaneously
- The critical energy of transition to chaos is given by Ecr = Umin(K=0), where K is the Gaussian curvature

## Context

This work extends the study of chaotic dynamics in nuclear systems, building on prior investigations of quadrupole oscillations and the Henon-Heiles potential (Henon and Heiles, 1964). The paper applies the negative curvature criterion previously suggested in the literature (references 34, 35) to nuclear quadrupole oscillations, and compares it with Chirikov's overlap resonance criterion (Chirikov, 1959). The results enable further investigation of quantum manifestations of classical stochasticity in nuclear spectra and wave functions, particularly the destruction and reconstruction of shell structure during the R-C-R transition.
