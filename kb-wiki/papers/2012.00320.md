# Scalar field quasinormal modes of noncommutative high dimensional Schwarzschild-Tangherlini black hole spacetime with smeared matter sources

> We investigate the massless scalar quasinormal modes (QNMs) of the noncommutative $D$-dimensional Schwarzschild-Tangherlini black hole spacetime in this paper. By using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation method, the asymptotic iterative method (AIM) and the inverted potential method (IPM) method, we made a detail analysis of the massless scalar QNM frequencies by varying the general smeared matter distribution and the allowable characteristic parameters ($k$ and $θ$) corresponding to different dimensions. It is found that the nonconvergence of the high order WKB approximation exists in the QNMs frequencies of scalar perturbation around the noncommutative $D$-dimensional Schwarzschild black holes. We conclude that the 3rd WKB result should be more reliable than those of the high order WKB method since our numerical results are also verified by the AIM method and the IPM method. In the dimensional range of $4\leq D \leq7$, the scalar QNMs as a function of the different papameters (the noncommutative parameter $θ$, the smeared matter distribution parameter $k$, the multipole number $l$ and the main node number $n$) are obtained. Moreover, we study the dynamical evolution of a scalar field in the background of the noncommutative high dimensional Schwarzschild-Tangherlini black hole.

## Key claim

The 3rd-order WKB approximation is more reliable than higher-order (4th, 5th, 6th) WKB approximations for computing scalar quasinormal mode frequencies of noncommutative Schwarzschild-Tangherlini black holes, because higher-order WKB results show nonconvergence and spurious oscillations that are not present in results verified by the asymptotic iterative method (AIM) and the inverted potential method (IPM).

## Method

The authors solved the massless Klein-Gordon equation on a noncommutative D-dimensional Schwarzschild-Tangherlini background with a general smeared matter density parameterized by integer k (k=0 for Gaussian, k=1 for Rayleigh, k=2 for Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions). They computed quasinormal mode frequencies using three independent numerical methods: the 6th-order Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation, the asymptotic iterative method (AIM), and the inverted potential method (IPM) based on the Poschl-Teller potential approximation, for dimensions D=4 through D=7 and varying noncommutative parameter θ and multipole number l.

## Context

This work extends the study of quasinormal modes in noncommutative geometry-inspired black holes, originally developed by Nicolini (2005) for four dimensions and by Rizzo (2006) for higher dimensions, to include general smeared matter distributions parameterized by k. The authors find that the nonconvergence of high-order WKB methods, previously noted by Batic (2019) for the D=4, k=0 case, persists in higher dimensions and for non-Gaussian distributions. The results enable more reliable computation of QNMs for noncommutative black holes across dimensions 4-7, and the time-domain evolution analysis provides a check on the frequency-domain methods.
