# Incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the first resonance region

> Incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron is studied in the first resonance region. The unpolarized cross section, the beam asymmetry, and the vector and tensor target asymmetries are calculated in the framework of a diagrammatic approach. Pole diagrams and one-loop diagrams with $NN$ scattering in the final state are taken into account. An elementary operator for pion photoproduction on the nucleon is taken in various on-shell forms and calculated using the SAID and MAID multipole analyses. Model dependence of the obtained results is discussed in some detail. A comparison with predictions of other works is given. Although a reasonable description of many available experimental data on the unpolarized total and differential cross sections and photon asymmetry has been achieved, in some cases a significant disagreement between the theory and experiment has been found. Invoking known information on the reactions $γd\toπ^0 d$ and $γd\to np$ we predict the total photoabsorption cross section for deuterium. We find that our values strongly overestimate experimental data in the vicinity of the $Δ$ peak.

## Key claim

The authors calculate incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron using a diagrammatic approach that includes pole diagrams and one-loop diagrams with nucleon-nucleon final-state interaction, finding that while a reasonable description of many unpolarized cross section and photon asymmetry data is achieved, the predicted total photoabsorption cross section for deuterium strongly overestimates experimental data in the vicinity of the Delta peak, and significant disagreement between theory and experiment remains in some cases.

## Method

The amplitude for the reaction γd → πNN was computed using a diagrammatic approach retaining pole diagrams (Fig. 1a) and one-loop diagrams with NN final-state interaction (Fig. 1b), with the elementary pion photoproduction operator on the nucleon taken in various on-shell forms parametrized via the SAID and MAID multipole analyses. The deuteron wave function was taken from the CD-Bonn potential, and half-off-shell NN scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the same potential, with Coulomb interaction handled via the Vincent-Phatak method for the ¹S₀ partial wave. All summations over polarizations and the three-dimensional integration in the FSI loop were carried out numerically with accuracy better than 2%.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections from the SAID SM04K solution at total energy 1232 MeV: 267 μb (π⁰p), 270 μb (π⁰n), 212 μb (π⁺n), and 240 μb (π⁻p); corresponding MAID03 values: 279 μb, 281 μb, 215 μb, and 244 μb.
- The threshold energy Eγ^max for π⁰, π⁺, and π⁻ channels is 142.6 MeV, 149.0 MeV, and 146.2 MeV respectively (using m = 938.9 MeV).
- The deuteron binding energy Δ = 2.2 MeV is used to justify on-shell parametrization near quasifree peaks.
- The total cross section for π⁰ production from the present calculation is about 5% higher than that from FixAr05 in the vicinity of the peak, with the peak position shifted about 10 MeV to lower energies compared to Darwish (2003).
- At 208 MeV, the differential cross section shows noticeable sensitivity to both the choice of analysis (SAID vs MAID) and the on-shell form of the operator; this sensitivity diminishes with increasing photon energy for the analysis choice but remains for the operator form.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron beyond earlier approaches by Laget (1981) and Schmidt (1996), which had significantly overestimated experimental data in the Delta region, and by Darwish et al. (2003-2005) and Fix and Arenhövel (2005), where model dependencies from the elementary production operator were not systematically studied. The authors provide an independent check of these models and estimate uncertainties from different on-shell parametrizations of the elementary operator (SAID vs MAID, and different invariant amplitude sets A_i vs A_i'), which is essential for extracting information on pion photoproduction on the neutron from deuteron data.
