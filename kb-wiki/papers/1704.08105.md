# Relative mass distributions of neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei within statistical model

> We study the fission yield of recently predicted thermally fissile neutron-rich uranium and thorium nuclei using statistical model. The level density parameters needed for the study are evaluated from the excitation energies of temperature dependent relativistic mean field formalism. The excitation energy and the level density parameter for a given temperature are employed in the convolution integral method to obtain the probability of the particular fragmentation. As representative case, we present the results for the binary fission yield of 250 U and 254 Th. The relative yields are presented for three different temperatures T = 1, 2 and 3 MeV.

## Key claim
The authors apply a statistical model to predict binary fission yields for the neutron-rich thermally fissile nuclei ²⁵⁰U and ²⁵⁴Th, but the results represent relative fragmentation probabilities from a prompt democratic breakup, explicitly excluding dynamics, secondary emissions, and competing decay modes such as neutron emission or alpha decay.

## Method
The authors used temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) formalism to compute excitation energies and level density parameters for fission fragments, then applied the convolution integral method from Fong's statistical model to obtain relative yields. The level density parameter a was calculated from the excitation energy and temperature as a = E*/T², and the relative yield was normalized to the sum over all possible binary fragmentations. Calculations were performed at three temperatures T = 1, 2, and 3 MeV for the parent nuclei ²⁵⁰U and ²⁵⁴Th, with comparisons to the finite range droplet model (FRDM) using single-particle energies from the Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL-3).

## Key numbers
- The level density parameter a values from TRMF are close to the empirical estimate a = A/K with K = 10, while FRDM level density parameters are "appreciably lower" than this reference value.
- The inverse level density parameters K_E and K_S for ²³⁶U, ²⁵⁰U, ²³²Th, and ²⁵⁴Th show maximum fluctuations up to 30 MeV at T = 1 MeV, reducing to 10–13 MeV at T = 2 MeV or above.
- For ²⁵⁰U at T = 1 MeV in TRMF, the most favorable fragment combination ¹⁴¹Te + ¹⁰⁹Zr has a relative yield of 1.454 (normalized to 2).
- For ²⁵⁴Th at T = 3 MeV in TRMF, the symmetric binary combination ¹²⁷Rh + ¹²⁷Rh has the largest yield.
- The basis space used for the axially symmetric harmonic oscillator expansion was N_F = 12 and N_B = 20 for fragments up to mass number A ~ 182.

## Context
This work extends the statistical model approach of Fong (1956) and Rajasekaran and Devanathan (1981), who used Nilsson model single-particle energies, by replacing those with excitation energies from the temperature-dependent relativistic mean field formalism. The study focuses on the recently predicted thermally fissile neutron-rich uranium and thorium nuclei (²⁴⁶–²⁶⁴U and ²⁴⁴–²⁶²Th), which are of interest for achieving critical conditions in controlled fission reactions more readily than normal fissile nuclei. The results are compared with FRDM predictions and with the experimental work of Chaudhuri et al. (2015) on symmetric mass distributions at large excitation energy, and the authors note that symmetric or asymmetric mass distribution depends on the proton and neutron combination of the parent nucleus.
