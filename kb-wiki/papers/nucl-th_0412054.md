# Power-law behavior observed in $p_{T}$-distributions and its implications in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Preconception-free analyses of the inclusive invariant transverse-momentum distribution data taken from the measurements of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV have been performed. It is observed that the distributions exhibit for $p_{T}\geq 2$ GeV/c remarkably good power-law behavior ($p_{T}$-scaling) with general regularities. This power-law behavior leads us in particular to recognize that the concept of centrality, albeit its simple appearance, is rather complex; its underlying geometrical structure has to be understood in terms of fractal dimensions. Experimental evidences and theoretical arguments are given which show that the observed striking features are mainly due to geometry and self-organized criticality. A simple model is proposed which approximately reproduces the above-mentioned data for the ``suppression'' without any adjustable parameter. Further heavy-ion collision experiments are suggested.

## Key claim

The observed power-law behavior of inclusive invariant transverse-momentum distributions for charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ and $200$ GeV, for $p_{T}\geq 2$ GeV/c, is interpreted as evidence that the underlying geometrical structure of centrality must be understood in terms of fractal dimensions, and that the striking features are mainly due to geometry and self-organized criticality (SOC), not necessarily to quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors performed preconception-free analyses of published STAR and PHENIX data on inclusive invariant $p_{T}$-distributions for charged hadrons and neutral pions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ and $200$ GeV, across a broad range of centrality bins. They extracted power-law exponents $\lambda_{AuAu}(p_c)$ and $\lambda_{pp}$ from log-log plots of the distributions for $p_{T}\geq 2$ GeV/c, and examined the centrality-dependent quotient $Q(p_T,p_c)$ between Au+Au and p+p yields. They then constructed a model based on geometry and SOC, incorporating phase-space factors from the uncertainty principle and size distributions of gluon clusters from inelastic diffractive scattering, to reproduce the observed power-law exponents.

## Key numbers

* The power-law exponent for p+p collisions is $\lambda_{pp} \approx 7.66$, derived from the model using the lower limit of the SOC size-distribution exponent $\mu = 1.95 \pm 0.12$.
* For Au+Au collisions, the power-law exponent $\lambda_{AuAu}(p_c)$ increases monotonically from the most peripheral value ($p_c \rightarrow 100\%$) where $\lambda_{AuAu} \approx \lambda_{pp}$, to the most central value ($p_c \rightarrow 0\%$).
* The exponent $\lambda(p_c) = \lambda_{AuAu}(p_c) - \lambda_{pp}$ is identified as the fractal dimension of the geometrical support (the set of impact parameter points) for each centrality bin.
* The power-law behavior is observed for $p_{T} \geq 2$ GeV/c in the measured kinematic region $0 < p_T < 12$ GeV/c at both $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of high-$p_T$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, previously interpreted as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, by proposing an alternative explanation based on geometry and self-organized criticality (SOC) as developed by Bak, Tang, and Wiesenfeld (1990). The authors build on their own prior work (Meng and Liu, 2004) on SOC fingerprints in gluon systems and inelastic diffractive scattering, and they invoke the classical observations of Rutherford (1911) and Williams (1935) on large-momentum-transfer scattering. The proposed model enables a quantitative description of the centrality-dependent power-law exponents without adjustable parameters, and suggests that further experiments at higher $p_T$, higher energies, and with other nuclei can test whether fractal geometry and SOC concepts are generally applicable in relativistic heavy-ion physics.
