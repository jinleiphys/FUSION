# Electromagnetic strength of neutron and proton single-particle halo nuclei

> Electromagnetic strength functions of halo nuclei exhibit universal features that can be described in terms of characteristic scale parameters. For a nucleus with nucleon+core structure the reduced transition probability, as determined, e.g., by Coulomb dissociation experiments, shows a typical shape that depends on the nucleon separation energy and the orbital angular momenta in the initial and final states. The sensitivity to the final-state interaction (FSI) between the nucleon and the core can be studied systematically by varying the strength of the interaction in the continuum. In the case of neutron+core nuclei analytical results for the reduced transition probabilities are obtained by introducing the effective-range expansion. The scaling with the relevant parameters is found explicitly. General trends are observed by studying several examples of neutron+core and proton+core nuclei in a single-particle model assuming Woods-Saxon potentials. Many important features of the neutron halo case can be obtained from a square-well model. Rather simple analytical formulas are found. The nucleon-core interaction in the continuum affects the determination of astrophysical S factors at zero energy in the method of asymptotic normalisation coefficients (ANC). It is also relevant for the extrapolation of radiative capture cross sections to low energies.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic strength functions of single-particle halo nuclei exhibit universal features determined by characteristic scale parameters (nucleon separation energy, orbital angular momenta, and potential range), but the final-state interaction between the nucleon and core systematically affects the reduced transition probabilities and must be accounted for when extracting astrophysical S factors via the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method.

## Method

The authors employed a single-particle model with Woods-Saxon and square-well potentials to calculate reduced transition probabilities for electric multipole transitions (primarily E1) in neutron+core and proton+core halo nuclei. For neutron+core systems, analytical results were obtained using the effective-range expansion to parametrize the final-state interaction, with the scattering length and effective range as the main parameters. The radial integrals were separated into interior and exterior contributions to demonstrate that for halo nuclei with small binding energy, the total transition strength is dominated by the asymptotic region outside the nuclear potential.

## Key numbers

- The inverse decay length of the bound state is given by \( q = \sqrt{2\mu S_b}/\hbar \), where \( S_b \) is the nucleon separation energy and \( \mu \) is the reduced mass.
- The dimensionless parameter \( \gamma = qR \) (with \( R \) the potential range) characterizes the halo nature; for small \( \gamma \) the wave function extends much beyond the potential range.
- The ratio \( x = k/q = \sqrt{E/S_b} \) relates the continuum energy \( E \) to the separation energy; the relevant range of \( x \) extends from zero to about one.
- For the square-well model, the probability of finding the neutron inside the potential in the extreme halo limit \( \gamma \to 0 \) approaches finite values of 0, 1/3, and 3/5 for s, p, and d waves, respectively.
- The root-mean-square radius scales as \( \langle r^2 \rangle_l \to R^2/\gamma^2 \) for \( l=0 \) and \( \langle r^2 \rangle_l \to 5R^2/(6\gamma) \) for \( l=1 \) as \( \gamma \to 0 \).
- For the ratio of interior to exterior dipole integrals in the limit \( x \to 0 \), the scaling laws are \( R_0^1(1) \to \gamma^4/[2(2n-1)^2\pi^2] \) for \( s \to p \) transitions and \( R_1^0(1) \to -\gamma^2/(4n^2\pi^2) \) for \( p \to s \) transitions, where \( n \) is the principal quantum number.

## Context

This work extends the familiar textbook case of the deuteron (Blatt and Weisskopf, 1979) to arbitrary nucleon+core halo systems, building on earlier studies of scaling laws in exotic nuclei (Otsuka et al., 1994; Meng and Ring, 1995; Kalassa et al., 1996; Typel and Baur, 2001). The effective-range approach for final-state interactions was introduced in Typel and Baur (2004) and applied to 11Be breakup, and later to 23O (Nociforo et al., 2004). The results enable systematic extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients from Coulomb dissociation experiments, and clarify how the continuum nucleon-core interaction affects extrapolations of radiative capture cross sections to astrophysically relevant low energies.
