# Investigating nuclear effects in lepton-ion DIS at the LHC

> Recent studies have demonstrated that the far-forward physics program of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) can be useful to probe the hadron structure with GeV-TeV neutrinos and muons. In particular, these studies indicate that the measurement of the muon-ion and neutrino-ion cross-sections by the same experiment is feasible. In this paper, we investigate the impact of nuclear effects on the muon-tungsten ($μW$) and neutrino-tungsten ($νW$) deep inelastic scattering (DIS) events at FASER$ν$ and its proposed upgrade FASER$ν2$. We estimate the rates associated with the inclusive cross-sections and for events with a charm tagged in the final state considering different parameterizations for the nuclear parton distribution functions. These results point out that muon and neutrino-induced interactions probe complementary kinematical ranges and that a simultaneous analysis of associated events will allow to test the universality (or not) of the nuclear effects. Moreover, we propose the study of the ratio between the charm tagged and inclusive events in order to discriminate between the distinct modeling of the nuclear effects at small-$x$. Our results indicate that a future experimental reconstruction of $μW$ and $νW$ DIS events at the LHC is a promising way to improve our understanding of nuclear effects and decrease the current uncertainties in parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that simultaneous measurement of muon-tungsten and neutrino-tungsten deep inelastic scattering events at the LHC's far-forward detectors FASERν and FASERν2 will allow testing the universality of nuclear parton distribution functions, but only if the ratio between charm-tagged and inclusive events is used to discriminate between different nuclear effect models at small Bjorken-x.

## Method

The authors used the POWHEG-BOX-RES event generator at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, interfaced with PYTHIA8 for hadronization, to simulate muon-tungsten and neutrino-tungsten deep inelastic scattering. They considered three nuclear parton distribution function parameterizations (EPPS21, nCTEQ15HQ, nNNPDF 3.0) and two free-nucleon baselines (CT18ANLO and NNPDF 3.0), applying detector acceptance cuts including final lepton energy above 100 GeV and at least two charged tracks with momentum above 1 GeV. Event rates were estimated for both inclusive and charm-tagged final states at FASERν (250 fb⁻¹ integrated luminosity) and FASERν2 (3 ab⁻¹ integrated luminosity).

## Key numbers

- Inclusive muon-tungsten events at FASERν: 1.75×10⁵ (CT18ANLO), 1.54×10⁵ (nCTEQ15HQ), 1.60×10⁵ (EPPS21), 1.71×10⁵ (nNNPDF 3.0(W))
- Inclusive neutrino-tungsten events at FASERν: 5.66×10³ (CT18ANLO), 5.66×10³ (nCTEQ15HQ), 5.55×10³ (EPPS21), 6.12×10³ (nNNPDF 3.0(W))
- Charm-tagged muon-tungsten events at FASERν: 9.85×10³ (CT18ANLO), 9.91×10³ (nCTEQ15HQ), 7.37×10³ (EPPS21), 7.14×10³ (nNNPDF 3.0(W))
- Charm-tagged neutrino-tungsten events at FASERν: 281 (CT18ANLO), 263 (nCTEQ15HQ), 263 (EPPS21), 381 (nNNPDF 3.0(W))
- FASERν2 event rates are approximately 100 times larger than FASERν rates across all channels
- Charm tag efficiency assumed: ε = 0.7
- Minimum Q² cut: Q ≥ 1.65 GeV
- Detector target length for muon DIS: 50 cm
- FASERν detector mass: 1.1 metric tons of tungsten; FASERν2: approximately 20 metric tons of tungsten

## Context

This work extends the feasibility studies of muon-ion deep inelastic scattering at FASERν by Francener (2025) and neutrino-ion deep inelastic scattering by Cruz-Martinez (2023), which demonstrated that both processes cover a large Bjorken-x range (3×10⁻⁴ to 0.9). The paper addresses the known tension between charged lepton-ion and neutrino-ion deep inelastic scattering data in global nuclear PDF analyses, as reported by Muzakka (2022), and the debate about possible breakdown of nuclear PDF universality discussed by Schienbein (2007), Paukkunen (2010), and Kovarik (2010). The proposed ratio between charm-tagged and inclusive events enables discrimination between the EPPS21, nCTEQ15HQ, and nNNPDF 3.0 parameterizations, particularly at small-x where shadowing effects differ significantly.
