# Importance of Mixing for Exotic Baryons

> Exotic antidecuplet baryons are predicted to be not only surprisingly light but also very narrow. First we explain how small decay width arises in the quark soliton model. Next, we study possible mixing of exotic antidecuplet with Roper octet and discuss its phenomenological consequences.

# Importance of Mixing for Exotic Baryons

## Key claim
Exotic antidecuplet baryons can be both light and narrow, but only if mixing with the Roper octet is included; without such mixing, the observed decay constraints for the candidate nucleon resonance N(1685) cannot be satisfied with realistic mixing angles, because the required mixing angle that suppresses the πN decay is an order of magnitude too small to account for baryon masses.

## Method
The authors use the Chiral Soliton Quark Model (χQSM) to compute decay widths via a nonrelativistic formula relating the width to the cube of the outgoing meson momentum and a coupling constant derived from the Goldberger-Treiman relation. They then introduce Gell-Mann–Okubo (GMO) mixing between the antidecuplet and the ground-state octet, and subsequently consider additional mixing between the antidecuplet and the Roper octet, parameterized by mixing angles α and φ, to satisfy experimental bounds on the N(1685) resonance.

## Key numbers
- The Θ⁺(1540) is assumed to have a mass of 1540 MeV and a total width Γ < 1 MeV.
- The N(1685) candidate must satisfy: Γ(N_10̅ → πN) < 0.5 MeV, Br(N_10̅ → ηN) > 0.2, and 5 MeV < Γ_total(N_10̅) < 25 MeV.
- Without mixing, the naive estimate for the Θ⁺ → NK decay width is Γ ≈ 150 MeV, using g_ΔNπ = g_ΘNK ≈ 17.6.
- With mixing included, the Θ⁺ → NK coupling is g_θNK ≈ 1.4, assuming Γ_ΘNK ≈ 1 MeV.
- The mixing angle α that satisfies the N(1685) constraints lies in the range 0.079 < α < 0.159, along the line φ(α) = 0.0508 − 2.207α.
- Predicted masses for the remaining antidecuplet members: 1795 MeV < M(Σ_10̅) < 1830 MeV; 1900 MeV < M(Ξ_10̅) < 1970 MeV.
- The total decay width of Ξ_10̅ is of order 10 MeV; the total width of Σ_10̅ is between 10 and 30 MeV, with branching ratios to KN ≈ 60% and to πΛ ≈ 20%.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Goeke (2009) by emphasizing that mixing with the Roper octet is necessary to reconcile the narrow width and decay patterns of the putative N(1685) with SU(3) symmetry relations. It contradicts the earlier diquark model of Jaffe and Wilczek, which predicted ideal mixing leading to comparable widths for the Roper and N*(1710) states, in disagreement with experiment. The results enable predictions for the masses and decay channels of the Σ_10̅ and Ξ_10̅ baryons, which can be tested by future experiments; the predicted Ξ_10̅ mass range (1900–1970 MeV) conflicts with the NA49 claim of M(Ξ_10̅) ≈ 1860 MeV.
