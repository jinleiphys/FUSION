# Pairing in finite nuclei from low-momentum two- and three-nucleon interactions

> The present contribution reviews recent advances made toward a microscopic understanding of superfluidity in nuclei using many-body methods based on the BCS ansatz and low-momentum inter-nucleon interactions, themselves based on chiral effective field theory and renormalization group techniques.

## Key claim

The paper reviews progress toward a microscopic understanding of nuclear superfluidity using BCS-based many-body methods and low-momentum interactions, but emphasizes that first-order calculations from two- and three-nucleon forces leave about 30% room for higher-order contributions such as coupling to collective fluctuations, and that fully ab-initio calculations remain a mid-term objective.

## Method

The author reviews two complementary approaches: (1) a semi-empirical single-reference energy density functional (SR-EDF) method where the pairing field is built at first order from low-momentum two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, while the normal self-energy is taken from an empirical Skyrme parametrization constrained by Hartree-Fock calculations of nuclear matter; (2) ab-initio self-consistent Gorkov Green's function (SCGGF) calculations at second order in the self-energies, using low-momentum 2N interactions evolved via the Vlowk renormalization group procedure, applied to calcium isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Neutron and proton pairing gaps from the semi-empirical EDF approach are close to experimental values for semi-magic spherical nuclei, but the addition of first-order 3N contributions lowers pairing gaps systematically by about 30%.
* Theoretical uncertainty from the Lambda dependence of pairing gaps in the EDF approach is of order approximately 100-250 keV across all semi-magic chains investigated.
* In the SCGGF calculations for Ca isotopes, first- and second-order gaps account for about half of experimental values; the second-order contribution generates a slight decrease of the three-point mass difference.
* The low-momentum cutoff used in the SCGGF calculations is Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1, with a restricted model space of 7 major harmonic oscillator shells.

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological EDF approaches (e.g., Skyrme and Gogny functionals) by building the pairing kernel from realistic low-momentum interactions derived from chiral effective field theory and renormalization group techniques, as developed by Bogner (2003, 2009) and Epelbaum (2008). It also reports on the first-ever ab-initio Gorkov Green's function calculations in finite nuclei, following the formalism of Gorkov (1958) and Nambu (1960). The results enable future fully microscopic calculations of pairing in mid- and heavy-mass nuclei, with the mid-term objectives of including 3N interactions and coupling to collective fluctuations.
