# Boosted saturation bound in colliding nuclei

> Interaction with a nucleus in pA collisions enhances the higher Fock components in the projectile proton. Effectively, this corresponds to an increase of the hard scale of the process by the saturation momentum Q^2 -> Q^2+Q_{sA}^2, which leads to an increased gluon distribution function at small x (but suppressed at x -> 1) compared to that in pp collisions. In the case of AA collisions the gluon distributions of bound nucleons in both nuclei turn out to be enhanced, i.e. to be boosted to higher saturation scales compared to pA collisions. A set of bootstrap equations relating the saturation scales in the colliding nuclei is derived and solved. The boosting effect has a moderate magnitude at the energies of RHIC, but becomes significant at LHC.

## Key claim

In proton-nucleus (pA) collisions, multiple interactions enhance the higher Fock components of the projectile proton, effectively increasing the hard scale by the saturation momentum \(Q^2 \rightarrow Q^2 + Q_{sA}^2\); in nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions, the gluon distributions of bound nucleons in both nuclei are mutually boosted to higher saturation scales via a set of bootstrap equations, with the effect being moderate at RHIC energies but significant at LHC energies.

## Method

The author derives and solves bootstrap equations that relate the saturation scales in colliding nuclei, where the saturation scale in one nucleus depends on the gluon distribution of the other nucleus evaluated at the boosted scale. The equations are solved for central and non-central lead-lead collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, using the MSTW2008 gluon distribution function and a fixed infrared cutoff \(Q_0^2 = 1.7\ \text{GeV}^2\). The boosting effect is then applied to calculate observables such as charmonium suppression by initial-state interactions and the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) for \(J/\Psi\) production.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum squared in pA collisions: \(Q_{sA}^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) used for calculations of the gluon density ratio \(g(x, Q^2 + Q_{sA}^2) / g(x, Q^2)\) at hard scales \(Q^2 = 2,\ 3,\ 5,\ 10\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Infrared cutoff: \(Q_0^2 = 1.7\ \text{GeV}^2\) chosen to obtain correct infrared behavior in the bootstrap equations.
- Boosting effect magnitude: about 20% at RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s} = 200\ \text{GeV}\)) for central lead-lead collisions, becoming significant at LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\ \text{TeV}\)).
- Transport coefficient for final-state interactions: \(\hat q_0 = 0.6\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) adjusted to describe RHIC \(J/\Psi\) data; \(\hat q_0 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) used for LHC predictions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.7\ \text{TeV}\).
- Nuclear thickness values used in non-central collision calculations: \(T_A\) fixed at \(0.5\), \(1.0\), and \(2.0\ \text{fm}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of saturation-scale boosting introduced by the same author (Kopeliovich, 2011) from pA collisions to AA collisions, where the mutual enhancement of saturation scales in both nuclei is described by bootstrap equations. The paper builds on prior work on gluon distribution modification in nuclei (Kopeliovich, 2011) and on charmonium suppression by initial-state interactions (Kopeliovich, 2010). The results enable predictions for observables at LHC energies, such as enhanced \(J/\Psi\) suppression and a more pronounced Cronin effect, which could be tested with future LHC data.
