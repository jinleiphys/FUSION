# The effect of causality constraints on Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion collisions

> There have long been questions about the limits to the validity of relativistic fluid dynamics, and whether it is being used outside its regime of validity in modern simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. An important new tool for answering this question is a causality analysis in the nonlinear regime -- if the solutions of the evolution equations do not respect relativistic causality, they are not a faithful representation of the underlying relativistic theory (in this case, quantum chromodynamics). Using this non-linear criterion, it has recently been shown that hydrodynamics is indeed being used outside its regime of validity in simulations, at least sometimes. Here we explore the phenomenological implications, particularly the quantitative effects of demanding limits on acausality in modern Bayesian parameter estimation. We find that, while typically only a small fraction of the system's energy is initially in an acausal regime, placing strict limits on the allowed energy fraction significantly changes the preferred properties of the initial condition, which in turn alters the extracted medium properties such as bulk viscosity, where large values are no longer favored. These findings highlight the importance of developing better theoretical descriptions of the early-time, out-of-equilibrium dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

While only a small fraction of the system's energy is initially in an acausal regime, imposing strict limits on the allowed energy fraction in acausal regions significantly changes the preferred properties of the initial condition and alters extracted medium properties such as bulk viscosity, where large values are no longer favored.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE simulation framework, which includes T_RENTo initial conditions, free-streaming, MUSIC viscous hydrodynamic evolution, Cooper-Frye particlization, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner, to perform Bayesian parameter estimation for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. They imposed the necessary conditions for nonlinear causality derived by Bemfica et al. (2020) on the posterior distribution of model parameters, evaluating how these constraints affect the extraction of QGP transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The linear causality condition requires \( n_\text{static} \equiv c_s^2 + \frac{4}{3} \frac{\eta}{\tau_\pi (\epsilon + P)} + \frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi (\epsilon + P)} \leq 1 \)
- The shear relaxation time factor \( b_{\pi} \) has a prior range of [2, 8]
- The nucleon width \( w \) has a prior range of [0.5, 1.5] fm
- The particlization temperature \( T_{\text{sw}} \) has a prior range of [0.135, 0.165] GeV
- The maximum of \( (\zeta/s) \) has a prior range of [0.01, 0.25]
- The temperature of the \( (\zeta/s) \) peak \( T_{\zeta} \) has a prior range of [0.12, 0.3] GeV

## Context

This work extends the causality analysis of Chiu and Shen (2021) and the ExTrEMe Collaboration (2023), which showed that current numerical simulations of QGP space-time evolution violate causality in the nonlinear regime. It uses as a baseline the Bayesian parameter estimations performed by the JETSCAPE Collaboration (2020). The findings highlight the importance of developing better theoretical descriptions of early-time, out-of-equilibrium dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and demonstrate that causality constraints can bias the extraction of QCD properties from experimental data.
