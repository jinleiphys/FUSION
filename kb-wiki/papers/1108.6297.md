# Jet momentum balance independent of shear viscosity

> Jet momentum balance measurements, such as those recently performed by the CMS collaboration, provide an opportunity to quantify the energy transferred from a parton shower to the underlying medium in heavy-ion collisions. Specifically, I argue that the Cooper-Frye freezeout distribution associated with the energy and momentum deposited by the parton shower is controlled to a significant extent by the distribution of the underlying bulk matter and independent of the details of how deposited energy is redistributed in the medium, which is largely determined by transport coefficients such as shear viscosity. Thus by matching the distribution of momentum associated with the secondary jet in such measurements to the thermal distribution of the underlying medium, one can obtain a model independent estimate on the amount of parton shower energy deposited.

## Key claim

The Cooper-Frye freezeout spectrum associated with energy and momentum deposited by a parton shower is largely controlled by the distribution of the underlying bulk matter and independent of the details of how deposited energy is redistributed in the medium, which is largely determined by transport coefficients such as shear viscosity; this holds provided the disturbance is not too strong and the linearized approximation is relatively accurate when integrating over the entire volume.

## Method

The author uses linearized hydrodynamics coupled to a parton shower source term, with the source representing energy and momentum flow between the parton shower and medium as a point source weighted by the energy loss rate. The parton shower energy deposition rate is calculated using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) radiative energy loss formalism for 120 GeV gluon-initiated parton showers, with collisional energy losses from the Neufeld-Vitev formalism including finite time effects and quantum color interference. The final particle distribution is obtained via the Cooper-Frye freezeout prescription, and the momentum deposition spectrum is evaluated for varying shear viscosity to entropy density ratios and varying gap times between the end of energy deposition and freezeout.

## Key numbers

- For a 120 GeV gluon propagating in a QGP produced at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the most likely number of medium-induced gluons is 14 with average energy omega_g approximately 4.8 GeV and average emission angle theta approximately 0.7
- For a 120 GeV quark under the same conditions, the most likely number of gluons is 6 with average energy omega_g approximately 6 GeV
- The total integrated energy deposition amounts to about 51 GeV for the primary gluon and about 25.5 GeV for the primary quark
- The momentum deposition curves for varying shear viscosity each integrate to about 50 GeV, slightly less than the 51 GeV obtained from the energy deposition calculation
- The parton shower pathlength is taken as 8 fm, based on analysis of He (2011)
- The medium parameters used are g = 2, T = 0.35 GeV, and m_D = g T, based on average values for LHC collision energies in a Bjorken expanding plasma

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dijet momentum balance measurements performed by the CMS collaboration at the LHC, providing a theoretical framework to interpret the observed dijet asymmetry in PbPb collisions relative to pp collisions. The author argues that the results of the CMS dijet momentum balance measurement have a natural interpretation in terms of parton shower energy deposition, and that by matching the distribution of momentum associated with the secondary jet to the thermal distribution of the underlying medium, one can obtain a model-independent estimate of parton shower energy deposition. This enables quantification of energy transfer from parton showers to the quark-gluon plasma without requiring detailed knowledge of transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, and the approach can be extended to jets tagged with electroweak bosons for model-independent single parton shower energy deposition estimates.
