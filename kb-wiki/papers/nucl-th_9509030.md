# The Role of Color Neutrality in Nuclear Physics--Modifications of Nucleonic Wave Functions

> The influence of the nuclear medium upon the internal structure of a composite nucleon is examined. The interaction with the medium is assumed to depend on the relative distances between the quarks in the nucleon consistent with the notion of color neutrality, and to be proportional to the nucleon density. In the resulting description the nucleon in matter is a superposition of the ground state (free nucleon) and radial excitations. The effects of the nuclear medium on the electromagnetic and weak nucleon form factors, and the nucleon structure function are computed using a light-front constituent quark model. Further experimental consequences are examined by considering the electromagnetic nuclear response functions. The effects of color neutrality supply small but significant corrections to predictions of observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that color neutrality, the principle that color-singlet systems have reduced interactions with surrounding matter when their quarks are close together, leads to small but significant modifications of nucleon properties in the nuclear medium, with the effects depending on the nucleon's position within the nucleus and being suppressed by an excitation energy of about 500 MeV (the Roper resonance energy).

## Method

The authors model the interaction of a nucleon with the nuclear medium using a perturbing Hamiltonian proportional to the nuclear density and to the squared inter-quark separation, consistent with color neutrality. They employ a light-front constituent quark model (based on Schlumpf's wave functions) to compute medium-modified electromagnetic and weak form factors, using a closure approximation that replaces the sum over excited states by an average excitation energy ΔE. The formalism is applied to evaluate changes in the proton electric and magnetic form factors, the axial form factor, and the nuclear electromagnetic response functions.

## Key numbers

- The central shell model potential parameter V₀ is approximately -50 MeV, with nuclear density ρ(R) normalized so that ρ(R=0)=1.
- The factor 2V₀ρ(0)/ΔE is about +0.2, indicating the effects are small but not insignificant.
- For the harmonic oscillator toy model at nuclear center, the mean square radius increases by a factor of 1.17 (17% increase) with ΔE = -500 MeV and V₀ = -50 MeV.
- The expectation value of r⁻¹ decreases by a factor of 0.93 in the same harmonic oscillator model.
- The wave function at the origin is reduced by about 20% in the medium, in agreement with a QCD sum rule calculation by Jin et al.
- For the harmonic oscillator plus Coulomb model, the form factor is reduced by a factor of 0.79 when ω = 0.86 ω₀.
- The harmonic oscillator model predicts a "deconfinement" phase transition at densities about 3.75 times nuclear matter density (ρ(0) = 3.75).

## Context

This work extends the original concept of point-like configurations (PLC) introduced by Frankfurt and Strikman (1985) to explain the EMC effect, but avoids their "all or nothing" assumption that PLC suppression turns on abruptly at a certain Q². The authors compare their approach to the quark-meson coupling model and to Desplanques' non-relativistic treatment including spatial variation of nuclear mean fields. The formalism enables calculations of medium modifications to nucleon form factors and structure functions that can be tested against data from (e,e') and (e,e'p) reactions, and provides a framework applicable to other mechanisms for medium modifications beyond color neutrality.
