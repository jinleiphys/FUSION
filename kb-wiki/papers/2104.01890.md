# Manipulating strong electromagnetic fields with the average transverse momentum of relativistic nuclear collisions

> We show that an event-shape engineering based on the mean transverse momentum of charged hadrons, $[p_t]$, provides an optimal handle on the strength of the magnetic field created in central heavy-ion collisions at high energy. This is established through quantitative evaluations of the correlation existing between the event-by-event magnetic field produced by the spectator protons in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions and the event-by-event $[p_t]$ at a given collision centrality. We argue that the event selection based on $[p_t]$ provides a better handle on the magnetic field than the more traditional selection based on the event ellipticities. Advantages brought by this new method for the experimental search of the chiral magnetic effect are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that event-by-event selection based on the average transverse momentum of charged hadrons, [pt], provides a stronger handle on the magnetic field strength in central Pb+Pb collisions than the traditional selection based on elliptic flow, with the correlation being most effective only in central collisions (below approximately 20% centrality) where the correlation coefficient reaches about 0.3.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions using the TRENTo model, generating 10^5 minimum bias events. They calculated the magnetic field induced by spectator protons at midrapidity using semi-analytical solutions assuming a constant electric conductivity of 0.023 fm^-1, and evaluated the event-by-event average magnetic field weighted by the initial energy density. The average transverse momentum was estimated from initial-state quantities using two proxies: the ratio of total energy to total entropy (E/S) and the ratio of entropy to elliptical area (S/Ae), and Pearson correlation coefficients were computed between [pt] and the magnetic field in narrow centrality bins.

## Key numbers

- The correlation between [pt] and the average magnetic field ⟨By⟩ in central Pb+Pb collisions is ρ([pt],⟨By⟩) ≈ 0.3
- The average magnetic field ⟨By⟩ reaches a maximum of about 1 mπ^2 in peripheral collisions
- The correlation between [pt] and spectator number Ns is stronger than the correlation between v2^2 and Ns across all centralities
- In an extreme example from the 2-3% centrality class, varying [pt] by about 5% (from 0.976 to 1.028 of the mean) changed the number of spectators from 6 to 72 and ⟨By⟩ from 0.013 mπ^2 to 0.151 mπ^2

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Giacalone (2020) by providing quantitative validation that [pt] correlates with the magnetic field from spectator protons, and contradicts the assumption that elliptic flow-based event selection is optimal for studying magnetic field effects. The method enables improved experimental searches for the chiral magnetic effect by allowing enhancement of the magnetic field signal relative to the hydrodynamic flow background, similar in spirit to the isobaric collision approach (Ru+Ru vs Zr+Zr at RHIC) but applicable within a single collision system. The approach avoids contamination from 1/N effects such as global transverse momentum conservation that plague traditional CME observables.
