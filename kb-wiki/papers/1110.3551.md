# Role of the $K_1$ meson in $K^0$ photoproduction off the deuteron

> Neutral kaon photoproduction off the nucleon and deuteron has been reinvestigated by utilizing the new experimental data on both targets. An isobar model for elementary operator and impulse approximation for the reaction on the deuteron have been used. The available free parameters in the elementary model have been extracted from both elementary and deuteron data. In contrast to the elementary reaction, fitting the deuteron data requires an inclusion of weighting factor. The result indicates that the angular distribution of the elementary $K^0Λ$ process does not show backward peaking behavior.

## Key claim

The paper finds that fitting the isobar model to new elementary reaction data alone still overpredicts the inclusive deuteron cross section, and that only by introducing an ad hoc weighting factor in the fit to emphasize deuteron data can agreement be achieved; the resulting angular distribution of the elementary \(n(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda\) process does not show backward peaking behavior.

## Method

The authors use an isobar model for the elementary kaon photoproduction operator, including Born terms and resonance terms, and apply the impulse approximation for the reaction on the deuteron. The free parameters in the elementary model are extracted by fitting to new experimental data on elementary reactions only, and then separately by fitting simultaneously to both elementary and deuteron data using a weighting factor \(w\) in the \(\chi^2\) definition. The deuteron wave function is taken from Nijmegen models.

## Key numbers

- The inclusive cross section for \(d(\gamma,K^0)YN\) is calculated at photon energies around reaction threshold with forward kaon angles, using data from the LNS Sendai experiment (Tsu08).
- Fitting with weighting factors \(w = 1, 10, 100, 1000\) is shown; the solid line (elementary-only fit) overpredicts the deuteron data, while increasing \(w\) improves agreement (Fig. 3).
- The differential cross section for the \(p(\gamma,K^0)\Sigma^+\) channel is shown at several energies, with improvement over KAON-MAID especially at higher energies and forward angles (Fig. 2).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon and deuteron, particularly the KAON-MAID model (Mart and Bennhold, 2000) and earlier work by Salam and Mart (2009) that showed KAON-MAID overpredicts the \(n(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda\) channel. The new analysis uses recent experimental data from LNS Sendai (Tsu08) and from Lawall (2005). The results indicate that more deuteron data are needed to avoid the weighting factor and to better understand the reaction mechanism, and that the \(n(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda\) angular distribution lacks backward peaking, contrary to some earlier expectations.
