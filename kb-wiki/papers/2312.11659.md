# First constraint on the dissipative tidal deformability of neutron stars

> The gravitational waves (GWs) emitted by neutron star binaries probe the physics of matter at supra nuclear densities. During the late inspiral, tidal deformations raised on each star by the gravitational field of its companion depend crucially on the star's internal properties. The misalignment of a star's tidal bulge with its companion's gravitational field encodes the strength of internal dissipative processes, which imprint onto the phase of the gravitational waves emitted. We here analyze GW data from the GW170817 (binary neutron star) event detected by LIGO and Virgo and find the first constraint on the dissipative tidal deformability of a neutron star. From this constraint, \emph{assuming} a temperature profile for each star in the binary, we obtain an order of magnitude bound on the averaged bulk ($ζ$) and shear ($η$) viscosity of each star during the inspiral.: $ζ\lesssim 10^{31} \mathrm{g}\;\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\mathrm{s}^{-1}$ and $η\lesssim 10^{28} \mathrm{g}\;\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\mathrm{s}^{-1} $. We forecast that these bounds could be improved by two orders of magnitude with third-generation detectors, like Cosmic Explorer, using inspiral data. These constraints already inform nuclear physics models and motivate further theoretical work to better understand the interplay between viscosity and temperature in the late inspiral of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first constraint on the dissipative tidal deformability of neutron stars from gravitational wave data, but this constraint can only be translated into bounds on bulk and shear viscosity by *assuming* a specific temperature profile for each star in the binary.

## Method

The authors analyzed 128 seconds of publicly available GW170817 strain data from LIGO and Virgo using Bayesian parameter estimation with an enhanced version of the IMRPhenomPv2_NRTidal waveform model that includes the leading post-Newtonian contribution of the dissipative tidal deformability to the gravitational wave phase. They sampled over 18 parameters including the binary dissipative chirp tidal deformability, using nested sampling with the Bilby library and DYNESTY sampler, and performed consistency checks by varying the base waveform model and prior distributions.

## Key numbers

- Dissipative tidal deformability constraint: $\tilde{\Xi} < 1200$ at 90% confidence from GW170817 data
- Averaged bulk viscosity bound: $\zeta \lesssim 4.57 \times 10^{31} \mathrm{g}\;\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (assuming a temperature profile)
- Averaged shear viscosity bound: $\eta \lesssim 9.15 \times 10^{28} \mathrm{g}\;\mathrm{cm}^{-1}\mathrm{s}^{-1}$ (assuming a temperature profile)
- Dissipative tidal effects become important at gravitational wave frequency $f \approx 253$ Hz and orbital separation $r \approx 84$ km for $\tilde{\Xi}=1200$ and total mass $2.8 M_{\odot}$
- Forecast improvement: bounds could improve by two orders of magnitude with third-generation detectors like Cosmic Explorer
- Bias in maximum likelihood of conservative tidal deformability when neglecting dissipative effects: $\Delta \Lambda_{s}^{\mathrm{ML}} \approx 46.78$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Ripley (2023) showing that dissipative tidal effects enter the gravitational wave phase at lower post-Newtonian order than conservative tidal effects, making them potentially measurable with current detectors. The constraint on $\tilde{\Xi}$ overlaps with the upper end of predictions for bulk viscosity driven by Urca processes (Most 2021, Yang 2023) and hyperon processes (Jones 2001, Lindblom 2001, Gusakov 2008, Alford 2020). The results enable future work to break degeneracies between individual dissipative tidal deformabilities, marginalize over equations of state, and connect viscosity measurements to temperature evolution during the inspiral.
