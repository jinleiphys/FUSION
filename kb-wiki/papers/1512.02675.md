# Effects of pairing correlation on low-lying quasi-particle resonance in neutron drip-line nuclei

> We discuss effects of pairing correlation on quasi-particle resonance. We analyze in detail how the width of low-lying quasi-particle resonance is governed by the pairing correlation in the neutron drip-line nuclei. We consider the 46Si + n system to discuss low-lying p wave quasi-particle resonance. Solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation in the coordinate space with scattering boundary condition, we calculate the phase shift, the elastic cross section, the resonance width and the resonance energy. We found that the pairing correlation has an effect to reduce the width of quasi-particle resonance which originates from a particle-like orbit in weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

The pairing correlation reduces the width of low-lying quasi-particle resonances originating from particle-like orbits in weakly bound neutron drip-line nuclei, when compared at the same resonance energy; this effect is non-perturbative and contrasts with the perturbative expectation that the width increases with the pair gap.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation in coordinate space with a scattering boundary condition for the 46Si + n system, using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and a Woods-Saxon pair potential. They calculated the phase shift and elastic cross section for neutron scattering, extracting the resonance width and resonance energy of the 2p1/2 quasi-particle resonance by fitting the phase shift. The pairing strength (average pair gap) was varied from 0.0 to 3.0 MeV, and the single-particle energy of the 2p1/2 orbit was varied by adjusting the Woods-Saxon potential depth.

## Key numbers

*   The 2p1/2 single-particle energy in the Woods-Saxon potential of 46Si is -0.056 MeV, with the Fermi energy fixed at λ = -0.269 MeV.
*   The average pair gap was varied from 0.0 to 3.0 MeV, with the empirical systematics for 46Si giving Δ ~ 1.7 MeV.
*   For a particle-like quasi-particle resonance at e_R = 0.45 MeV, the resonance width Γ is smaller for Δ̅ = 1.634 MeV than for Δ̅ = 0.0 MeV (single-particle resonance), and smallest for Δ̅ = 1.897 MeV.
*   The perturbative evaluation of the resonance width (Fermi's golden rule) is applicable when the single-particle energy satisfies e_sp ≲ λ - 0.5Δ̅, or equivalently when the ratio of integrated probabilities inside the nuclear surface is u̅²/v̅² ≲ 0.1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasi-particle resonances in neutron drip-line nuclei (Hamamoto 2003, Hamamoto 2004, Zhang 2011, Grasso 2000, Fayans 2000, Michel 2008, Pei 2011, Zhang 2012, Oba 2009, Zhang 2013, Sandulescu 2000, Betan 2006, Sandulescu 2005) by focusing on the non-perturbative effect of pairing on the resonance width for low-lying p-wave resonances. The finding that pairing can reduce the width for particle-like orbits contradicts the perturbative prediction (Belyaev 1987, Bulgac 1980) that the width is proportional to the square of the pair gap. This work enables future studies of s-wave quasi-particle resonances (involving virtual states) and more realistic self-consistent HFB calculations for drip-line nuclei.
