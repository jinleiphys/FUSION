# New results for the missing quantum numbers labeling the quadrupole and octupole boson basis

> The many $2^k$-pole boson states, $|N_kv_kα_k I_kM_k>$ with $k=2,3$, realize the irreducible representation (IR) for the group reduction chains $SU(2k+1)\supset R_{2k+1}\supset R_3\supset R_2$. They have been analytically studied and widely used for the description of nuclear systems. However, no analytical expression for the degeneracy $d_v(I)$ of the $R_{2k+1}$'s IR, determined by the reduction $R_{2k+1}\supset R_3$, is available. Thus, the number of distinct values taken by $α_k$ has been so far obtained by solving some complex equations. Here we derive analytical expressions for the degeneracy $d_v(I)$ characterizing the octupole and quadrupole boson states, respectively. The merit of this work consists of the fact that it completes the analytical expressions for the $2^k$-pole boson basis.

# New results for the missing quantum numbers labeling the quadrupole and octupole boson basis

## Key claim

The paper derives closed-form analytical expressions for the degeneracy d_v(I) of irreducible representations of R_5 and R_7 under reduction to R_3, which had previously only been obtainable numerically or via contour integration. The authors claim these expressions complete the analytical description of the 2^k-pole boson basis for k=2 and k=3, but the derivation relies on series expansions whose coefficients are themselves sums of floor functions, so the "analytical" result is a finite sum of integer-arithmetic terms rather than a simple closed formula.

## Method

The authors start from the known contour-integral representation of the degeneracy d_v(I) for the group reductions R_5 → R_3 (quadrupole bosons) and R_7 → R_3 (octupole bosons). They expand the integrand's denominator factor 1/[(1-z^2)(1-z^3)(1-z^4)(1-z^5)] as a power series in z, whose coefficients N(n) count the number of nonnegative integer solutions to a linear Diophantine equation. By isolating the singular part of the integrand at z=0 and applying the residue theorem, they obtain d_v(I) as a sum of these coefficient functions evaluated at specific arguments determined by v and I. The same procedure is then generalized to the group R_{2l+1} for arbitrary l.

## Key numbers

* For octupole bosons (R_7), the degeneracy d^{(3)}_v(I) is given by Eq. (2.21): d^{(3)}_v(I) = A(v-I-3) - A(v+I-2) + A(I-v-7) + B(2v+I) - B(2v-I-1) - B(I-2v-10) + C(3v-I) - C(3v+I+1) + C(I-3v-14), where A, B, C are sums of floor-function terms defined via Eqs. (2.19) and (2.18).

* For quadrupole bosons (R_5), the degeneracy d^{(2)}_v(I) is given by Eq. (3.8): d^{(2)}_v(I) = P(I-2v-5) + P(2v-I) - P(2v+I+1) + Q(v+I) - Q(v-I-1) - Q(I-v-3), where P(n) = (1/2)θ(n)N_{23}(n) and Q(n) = (1/2)θ(n)N_{13}(n), with N_{23}(n) = [n/2] - [n/3] + χ(n/3) and N_{13}(n) = [n/3] + 1.

* For the general case R_{2l+1}, the degeneracy d^{(l)}_v(I) is given by Eq. (4.12): d^{(l)}_v(I) = Σ_{n≥0} N_l(n)[θ(lv-I-n)D_{lv}(lv-I-n) - θ(lv+I-n+1)D_{lv}(lv+I-n+1)], where N_l(n) counts solutions to Σ_{k=1}^{2l-2} (k+1)n_k = n.

## Context

This work extends the authors' own prior analytical studies of the quadrupole and octupole boson basis (Gheorghe, Raduta, Ceausescu 1978; Raduta, Ceausescu, Gheorghe 1978) by providing the missing degeneracy formulas that had previously required numerical solution of inequalities or contour integration. The octupole degeneracy was first encountered in microscopic quadrupole-octupole boson calculations by Ceausescu and Raduta (1974, 1976), who gave a contour-integral expression; a recursion equation was later derived by Shi (1976) and solved numerically by Rohozinski (1978). The present analytical results enable direct construction of the complete boson basis without numerical root-finding, facilitating calculations of octupole collective states in nuclei where static octupole deformation breaks reflection symmetry. The general R_{2l+1} formula provides a framework for treating higher-multipole boson systems.
