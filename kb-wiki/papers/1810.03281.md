# Quadrupole moments of spin-1 systems: the rho meson, the S-wave deuteron and some general constraints

> We costruct the relativistic operator of the quadrupole moment of two-particle composite spin one systems with zero orbital moment of the relative motion and derive explicit analytical expression for the quadrupole moment using the approach to relativistic composite systems based on our version of the instant-form relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM). We calculate the quadrupole moments of the rho meson and of the S-wave deuteron without any free parameters, using our unified pi&rho model (Phys. Rev.D 93, 036007 (2016); 97, 033007 (2018)) and our previous results on deuteron. Our calculation gives Q_rho=-0.158+-0.04 GeV^-2 and Q_d=-1.4*10^-4 GeV^-2. Having in our disposition the rather general form of the quadrupole-moment operator we for the first time formulate the problem of the upper and lower bounds for possible values of the quadrupole moment of a two-particle system with indicated quantum numbers for a large range of constituent masses, and partially solve it.

## Key claim

The quadrupole moment of a spin-1 two-particle system in the S-state of relative motion is a purely relativistic effect arising from spin rotation of the constituents, and the authors derive an explicit analytical expression for this moment and calculate it for the rho meson and the S-wave deuteron without free parameters, while also formulating, for the first time, upper and lower bounds on possible quadrupole moment values across a wide range of constituent masses.

## Method

The authors construct the relativistic quadrupole-moment operator for two-particle composite spin-1 systems with zero orbital angular momentum using their modified instant-form relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM). They compute the quadrupole moment for the rho meson using the unified pi&rho model with a power-law wave function and parameters fixed in prior work (Krutov and Troitsky 2016, 2018), and for the S-wave deuteron using the MT wave function from the inverse scattering problem (Mukhtarov and Troitsky 1981). The quadrupole moment is obtained as the static limit of the quadrupole form factor via an integral over the wave function and the c-number operator Q(s).

## Key numbers

*   Rho meson quadrupole moment: \( Q_{\rho} = -0.158 \pm 0.04 \) GeV\(^{-2}\), calculated with constituent quark masses \( M_u = M_{\bar d} = 0.22 \) GeV, sum of anomalous magnetic moments \( a = 0.0268 \) in quark magnetons, and wave function parameter \( b = 0.385 \pm 0.019 \) GeV.
*   S-wave deuteron quadrupole moment: \( Q_d = -1.4 \times 10^{-4} \) GeV\(^{-2}\), calculated with nucleon mass \( M = 0.93891870 \) GeV and sum of anomalous magnetic moments \( a = \kappa_p + \kappa_n = -0.1201953 \pm 0.0000005 \) in nuclear magnetons.
*   The quadrupole-moment operator Q(s) vanishes in the limits \( s \to 4M^2 \) and \( s \to \infty \), and the quadrupole moment is a linear decreasing function of the sum of anomalous magnetic moments \( a \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' unified pi&rho model (Krutov and Troitsky 2016, 2018) and their prior deuteron calculations (Krutov and Troitsky 2007) to compute quadrupole moments without free parameters, contrasting with earlier calculations of the rho meson quadrupole moment that differ widely in sign and magnitude (e.g., Bakamjian and Thomas 1953, Cardarelli et al. 1995, Melosh 1974, Jaus 2003). The derived general constraints on quadrupole moment values for spin-1 S-state systems, based on the operator's dependence on constituent mass, anomalous magnetic moments, and interaction strength, provide a framework for testing future experimental or lattice results and for understanding the relativistic spin-rotation effect across systems from the rho meson to the deuteron.
