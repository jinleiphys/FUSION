# Primordial nucleosynthesis and hadronic decay of a massive particle with a relatively short lifetime

> In this paper we consider the effects on big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) of the hadronic decay of a long-lived massive particle. If high-energy hadrons are emitted near the BBN epoch ($t \sim 10^{-2}$ -- $10^2 \sec$), they extraordinarily inter-convert the background nucleons each other even after the freeze-out time of the neutron to proton ratio. Then, produced light element abundances are changed, and that may result in a significant discrepancy between standard BBN and observations. Especially on the theoretical side, now we can obtain a lot of experimental data of hadrons and simulate the hadronic decay process executing the numerical code of the hadron fragmentation even in the high energy region where we have no experimental data. Using the light element abundances computed in the hadron-injection scenario, we derive a constraint on properties of such a particle by comparing our theoretical results with observations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hadronic decay of a massive particle (mass ~100 GeV–10 TeV) with a relatively short lifetime (τ_X ~ 10⁻²–10² sec) can significantly alter primordial light element abundances, particularly increasing ⁴He, by inter-converting background protons and neutrons after the standard freeze-out of the neutron-to-proton ratio. The constraint on such particles depends on which observational deuterium abundance ("low D" vs. "high D") is adopted, with the baryon-to-photon ratio η differing by a factor of ~3 between the two cases.

## Method

The author used the JETSET 7.4 Monte Carlo event generator to simulate hadron fragmentation from quark/gluon decay of a massive parent particle, producing energy distributions of mesons (π⁺, π⁻, K⁺, K⁻, K_L⁰) and baryons (n, p, n̄, p̄). The thermalization of these hadrons in the electromagnetic plasma was computed via energy loss rates (Coulomb scattering, ionization), and thermal-averaged strong interaction cross sections for hadron-nucleon scattering were applied to compute the inter-conversion of background neutrons and protons. The resulting light element abundances were compared to observational data using a Maximum Likelihood analysis with Monte Carlo error propagation, including both theoretical and observational uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering investigation by Reno and Seckel (1988) on hadronic decay effects in BBN, incorporating updated experimental data on hadron fragmentation from e⁺e⁻ collider experiments (LEPII at √s = 130–183 GeV) and the JETSET 7.4 Monte Carlo code. The paper also uses the NACRE compilation of low-energy nuclear reaction cross sections and the reanalysis by Cyburt, Fields, and Olive (2001) for Monte Carlo error propagation. The results enable constraints on hypothetical massive particles (gravitino, Polonyi field, moduli) predicted by supergravity and superstring theories, with lifetimes in the range 10⁻²–10² sec, complementing earlier constraints from electromagnetic cascade processes for longer lifetimes (τ_X ≳ 10⁴ sec) studied by Ellis (1992), Kawasaki (1995), and Holtmann (1999).
