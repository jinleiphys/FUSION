# Role of Delta exchange for proton-antiproton annihilation into two-pion and three-pion channels

> Proton-antiproton annihilation into two pions and three pions is studied in a baryon-exchange model. Annihilation diagrams involving nucleon as well as Delta-resonance exchanges are included consistently in the two- and three-pion channels. Effects from the initial-state interaction are fully taken into account. A comparison of the influence of the Delta exchange on the considered annihilation channels is made and reveals that its importance for three-pion annihilation is strongly reduced as compared to two-pion annihilation. It is found that annihilation into three uncorrelated pions can yield up to 10 % of the total experimentally observed three-pion annihilation cross section.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a baryon-exchange model of proton-antiproton annihilation, the contribution of Δ-resonance exchange to three-pion annihilation is strongly reduced compared to its contribution to two-pion annihilation, and that annihilation into three uncorrelated pions can yield up to 10% of the total experimentally observed three-pion annihilation cross section, but this result depends sensitively on the choice of vertex form factors and the relative strength of nucleon versus Δ exchange.

## Method

The authors calculate proton-antiproton annihilation into two and three pions using Feynman-type baryon-exchange diagrams involving nucleon and Δ-resonance exchanges, with the initial-state interaction treated in distorted-wave Born approximation using the Jülich N̄N model. They determine the cutoff masses in the NNπ and NΔπ vertex form factors by fitting to experimental data on p̄p → π⁺π⁻ total and differential cross sections, then use these parameters to predict the uncorrelated three-pion annihilation cross section. Three different form factor parametrizations (monopole, Gaussian, and Pearce) are tested, and two parameter sets are prepared: one where nucleon exchange dominates (set A) and one where Δ exchange is strong (set B).

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: f_{NNπ}²/4π = 0.0778, f_{ΔNπ}²/4π = 0.224, f_{ΔΔπ}²/4π = 0.252 (from SU(2)×SU(2) quark-model relation f_{ΔΔπ} = (9/5) f_{NNπ})
- The total experimental p̄p → π⁺π⁻π⁰ cross section ranges from approximately 7 mb to 3 mb for energies from 65 MeV to 220 MeV
- For the Gaussian form factor with parameter set A, the calculated uncorrelated three-pion contribution reaches about 10% of the measured total three-pion cross section
- For parameter set A with the Pearce form factor, turning off Δ exchange reduces the two-pion cross section by up to 30%; for the monopole form factor the reduction is 10%; for the Gaussian form factor the reduction is about 20%
- For parameter set B with the Pearce form factor, adding Δ-exchange contributions enhances the three-pion cross section by almost a factor of two

## Context

This work extends the baryon-exchange model of N̄N annihilation previously developed by the Jülich group (Mull et al., 1995) from two-meson channels to three-meson channels, and addresses an inconsistency in earlier studies by the same authors (Betz et al., 1997, 2000, 2001) where two-pion and three-pion amplitudes were treated with different perturbation theories. The paper enables future precise analyses of annihilation into three neutral pions, which has been studied experimentally by the Crystal Barrel Collaboration (Amsler, 1998; Abele, 1999; Anisovich, 1999), and provides a framework for comparing baryon-exchange and quark-model descriptions of N̄N annihilation.
