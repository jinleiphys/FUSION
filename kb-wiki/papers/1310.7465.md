# Pion electromagnetic form factor in the Covariant Spectator Theory

> The pion electromagnetic form factor at spacelike momentum transfer is calculated in relativistic impulse approximation using the Covariant Spectator Theory. The same dressed quark mass function and the equation for the pion bound-state vertex function as discussed in the companion paper are used for the calculation, together with a dressed quark current that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. The results obtained for the pion form factor are in agreement with experimental data, they exhibit the typical monopole behavior at high-momentum transfer, and they satisfy some remarkable scaling relations.

## Key claim

The pion electromagnetic form factor calculated in the Covariant Spectator Theory using the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) agrees with experimental data and exhibits monopole behavior at high momentum transfer, but the RIA approximation cannot correctly describe the form factor in the limit where both the pion mass and momentum transfer are small, because neglected contributions from negative-energy poles become important.

## Method

The authors calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor at spacelike momentum transfer using triangle diagrams in the Covariant Spectator Theory, where one quark is constrained to be on-shell. They use the same dressed quark mass function and pion bound-state vertex function from their companion paper, together with a dressed quark current that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. The calculation employs the relativistic impulse approximation, retaining only the spectator pole contribution, and uses an approximate pion vertex function that is an off-shell extension near the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The dressed quark mass is \(m = 0.308\) GeV, obtained from the condition \(M(m^2)=m\)
- The physical pion mass used is \(\mu = 0.14\) GeV
- The form factor falls like \(Q^{-2}\) at large momentum transfer, with the coefficient independent of the detailed structure of the strong form factor \(h\)
- The integrand peaks at \(k_{z0} \simeq Q E_\perp/(2\mu)\) for large \(Q\), where \(E_\perp = \sqrt{m_\chi^2 + k_\perp^2}\)

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) framework, previously applied to nuclear form factors by Gross (2006) and Pinto (2009), to the calculation of the pion electromagnetic form factor. The model uses a simple interaction kernel combining a vector delta-function interaction with a confining interaction, and the results are compared with experimental data and previous calculations by Maris and Tandy (2000) and others. The paper demonstrates that the model can be applied to physics problems beyond the bound-state equation, and the authors note that future work will include the full four-channel CST equation and extend calculations to the time-like region where vector meson states contribute.
