# Resonance lifetime in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck theory: observable consequences

> Within the transport BUU theory we study the influence of the choice for the $Δ$ resonance lifetime on pion-nucleus reactions and on heavy-ion collisions at 1 A GeV. A quite small effect of these modifications on the pion absorption on nuclei is found provided that the absorption probability of the $Δ$ resonance is modified consistently. Observable effects in the case of heavy-ion collisions are demonstrated.

## Key claim

The choice of the Δ resonance lifetime in BUU transport theory, whether the physically correct phase-shift derivative or the standard inverse width, has only a weak effect on observable pionic quantities once the absorption and rescattering probabilities of the Δ resonance are modified consistently, despite the strong difference in the energy dependence of the two lifetime prescriptions.

## Method

The authors implemented two different prescriptions for the Δ resonance lifetime in the BUU transport code: the standard inverse width (1/Γ) and the physically correct derivative of the πN scattering phase shift (dδ/dE). They consistently modified the Δ absorption and rescattering cross sections by the factor (Γτ)⁻¹ to keep branching ratios constant. The calculations were performed for pion-nucleus reactions (π⁺ on C and Fe at beam energies 85–315 MeV) and for central and semi-central Au+Au collisions at 1 A GeV, using a soft momentum-dependent mean field with incompressibility K=220 MeV and resonance production/absorption quenching to reproduce experimental pion multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Δ resonance pole mass: mΔ = 1.232 GeV; vacuum decay width parameter: Γ₀ = 0.118 GeV; β₀ = 0.2 GeV
- Pion absorption peak on nuclei occurs at beam energy Eπ = 192 MeV, corresponding to Δ excitation at pole mass
- For Au+Au at 1 A GeV and impact parameter b=6 fm, the modified lifetime with modified cross sections produces less negative π⁺ flow (antiflow) than the standard calculation
- In central Au+Au at 1.06 A GeV, the correlated proton-pion invariant mass spectrum peak is overpredicted by about 50 MeV compared to data from Eskef (1998); calculations with modified lifetime produce sharper peaks near 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Morawetz and coauthors (2001) on nonlocal and quasiparticle corrections to nucleon-nucleon scattering in the Fermi energy domain, applying similar lifetime considerations to the Δ resonance at higher energies. The authors build on the generalization of the lifetime formula by Leupold (2001) for resonances with multiple decay channels, and use the Δ width parameterization from Effenberger (1998). The study enables future tests of the correct Δ lifetime prescription through more precise measurements of pion flow and proton-pion correlations in heavy-ion collisions, as the effects are small but potentially observable with improved data.
