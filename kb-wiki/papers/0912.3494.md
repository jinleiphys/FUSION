# Bethe-Salpeter studies of mesons beyond rainbow-ladder

> We investigate the masses of light mesons from a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The dominant non-Abelian and sub-leading Abelian contributions to the dressed quark-gluon vertex are explicitly taken into account. We also include unquenching effects in the form of hadronic resonance contributions via the back-reaction of pions. We construct the corresponding Bethe--Salpeter kernel that satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. Our numerical treatment fully includes all momentum dependencies with all equations solved completely in the complex plane. This approach goes well beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation and permits us to investigate the relative impact of different corrections beyond rainbow-ladder on the properties of mesons. We find that sub-leading Abelian corrections are further suppressed dynamically, and that our results supersede early qualitative predictions with significantly simpler truncation schemes.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that sub-leading Abelian corrections to the quark-gluon vertex are dynamically suppressed in meson bound-state calculations, while the dominant non-Abelian corrections and pion back-reaction produce the most significant effects; however, the study is explicitly qualitative rather than quantitatively reliable, serving to assess relative impacts of different corrections rather than to provide a definitive truncation scheme.

## Method
The authors solved a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the quark propagator and quark-gluon vertex, incorporating the dominant non-Abelian diagram (involving the three-gluon vertex) and sub-leading Abelian diagram, plus hadronic unquenching effects via pion back-reaction. They then constructed the corresponding Bethe-Salpeter kernel that satisfies the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity and solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for light meson masses in Euclidean space, with all momentum dependencies treated fully in the complex plane using a Gaussian effective gluon interaction with parameters D=16 and ω=0.5.

## Key numbers
- The difference in the quark mass function at zero momentum between the full non-Abelian+Abelian calculation and the non-Abelian-only calculation is approximately 1 MeV, demonstrating suppression of the Abelian contribution.
- The effective gluon interaction parameters used are D=16 and ω=0.5 (in appropriate units), providing the scale and strength of the Gaussian interaction.
- The dynamical quark mass is approximately M ≈ 350 MeV, which suppresses physics on scales p ≪ M.
- The pion decay constant is set to f_π = 93 MeV for normalization purposes.

## Context
This work extends the earlier exploratory study by Fischer et al. (2009) which considered only the dominant non-Abelian corrections to the quark-gluon vertex in a symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter truncation. It goes beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation used extensively in prior DSE-BSE studies by Maris and collaborators (1997-2006) and addresses truncation artefacts present in earlier studies that employed the Munczek-Nemirovsky delta-function ansatz (1983) for gluon exchange. The results enable future development of a quantitatively reliable truncation scheme that includes both Yang-Mills corrections and hadronic resonance contributions, with the full quantitative calculation reported elsewhere.
