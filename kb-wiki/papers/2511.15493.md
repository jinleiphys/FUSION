# First observation of deuteron-Λ correlations at RHIC

> Precise experimental information on hyperon-nucleon interactions is scarce but of paramount importance to our understanding of the inner structure of compact stars. In this letter, we report the first experimental results of correlation functions between deuterons (d) and Λ hyperons in Au+Au collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN} = 3.0 GeV measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC. A clear enhancement at small relative momenta has been observed in the correlation function. Through a Bayesian inference analysis, the source size parameters as a function of collision centrality and the spin- dependent strong interaction parameters (scattering length f0 and effective range d0) are extracted using the Lednický-Lyuboshitz formalism. The derived doublet spin state parameters (f0, d0) lead to a novel method to precisely determine Λ separation energy for the weakly bounded hypertriton ^{3}_{Λ}H.

# First observation of deuteron-Λ correlations at RHIC

## Key claim
The paper reports the first experimental measurement of deuteron-Λ correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3.0 GeV, and through Bayesian inference analysis extracts spin-dependent strong interaction parameters (scattering length f0 and effective range d0) separately for the doublet (spin-1/2) and quartet (spin-3/2) states. The derived doublet-state parameters provide a novel method to determine the Λ separation energy of the weakly bound hypertriton, though the constrained fit yields a value (0.04_{-0.03}^{+0.12} MeV at 95% CL) that is lower than but consistent with the previous world average of 0.19 ± 0.06 MeV.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured deuteron-Λ correlation functions in fixed-target Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3.0 GeV using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors, with approximately 250 million events analyzed across three centrality bins (0-10%, 10-20%, 20-60%). The correlation functions were modeled using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism with a spherical Gaussian source, treating both particles as point-like and considering only two-body S-wave interactions with spin-dependent scattering parameters. A Bayesian inference analysis was performed simultaneously on all three centrality bins to extract source radii and the spin-dependent strong interaction parameters, with contamination from hypertriton three-body decay and Λ feed-down from Σ and Ξ decays subtracted.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length for doublet (spin-1/2) state: f0(D) = -26.1 ± 5.6 fm (constrained fit, negative value required for bound state)
- Scattering length for quartet (spin-3/2) state: f0(Q) = 18.7 ± 2.8 fm (constrained fit)
- Effective range for quartet state: d0(Q) = 6.5 ± 1.8 fm (constrained fit)
- Effective range for doublet state: d0(D) = 8 fm (at fit limit, with little constraining power from data)
- Source radii: 2.69 ± 0.20 fm (0-10% centrality), 2.44 ± 0.17 fm (10-20%), 2.08 ± 0.19 fm (20-60%)
- Λ separation energy of hypertriton: BΛ = 0.04_{-0.03}^{+0.12} MeV at 95% CL (constrained fit)
- Corresponding hypertriton radius: √⟨r²⟩ = 18^{+12}_{-9} fm at 95% CL

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic correlation techniques previously applied to proton-Λ pairs by STAR (2005) and ALICE (2018), and to proton-deuteron correlations by STAR (2024), to the first measurement of deuteron-Λ correlations. The extracted doublet-state scattering parameters are compared with predictions from pionless effective field theory by Hammer (2001) and potential model calculations by Cobis (1996), while quartet-state predictions come from Alexander (1968), Rijken (2010), and Haidenbauer (2013). The derived Λ separation energy is compared with previous worldwide measurements from K⁻ beam experiments by Juric (1973), Keyes (1970), Gajewski (1967), and Bohm (1968), as well as recent STAR (2020) and ALICE (2023) results using invariant mass methods. This technique enables a new approach to determine hypertriton binding energy and provides inputs for understanding hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron star equations of state.
