# Dispersive Evaluation of the Inner Radiative Correction in Neutron and Nuclear $β$-decay

> We propose a novel dispersive treatment of the so-called inner radiative correction to the neutron and nuclear $β$-decay. We show that it requires knowledge of the parity-violating structure function $F_3^{(0)}$ that arises from the interference of the axial vector charged current and the isoscalar part of the electromagnetic current. By isospin symmetry, we relate this structure function to the charged current inelastic scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos. Applying this new data-driven analysis we obtain a new, more precise evaluation for the universal radiative correction $Δ_{R}^{V,\,new}=0.02467(22)$ that supersedes the previous estimate by Marciano and Sirlin, $Δ_R^V=0.02361(38)$. The substantial shift in the central value of $Δ_R^V$ reflects in a respective shift of $V_{ud}$ and a considerable tension in the unitarity constraint on the first row of the CKM matrix which is used as one of the most stringent constraints on New Physics contributions in the charged current sector. We also point out that dispersion relations offer a unifying tool for treating hadronic and nuclear corrections within the same framework. We explore the potential of the dispersion relations for addressing the nuclear structure corrections absorbed in the ${\cal F}t$ values, a crucial ingredient alongside $Δ_R^V$ in extracting $V_{ud}$ from superallowed nuclear decays.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a dispersive treatment of the inner radiative correction to neutron and nuclear beta decay, obtaining a new central value for the universal radiative correction Δ_R^V that is substantially larger than the previous estimate by Marciano and Sirlin, which creates tension with the unitarity constraint on the first row of the CKM matrix.

## Method

The authors express the vector-axial interference contribution to the γW box diagram in terms of a Q²-integral over the first Nachtmann moment of the parity-odd structure function F₃^(0), which arises from the interference of the axial vector charged current and the isoscalar part of the electromagnetic current. They relate this structure function via isospin symmetry to charged current inelastic scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos, and separate the integral into a high-Q² deep inelastic scattering piece treated with perturbative QCD, a Born elastic contribution evaluated using modern nucleon form factor data, and a Regge contribution extracted from neutrino scattering data. For nuclear corrections, they employ the plane wave impulse approximation with a Fermi gas model to account for binding effects and Fermi motion.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Marciano and Sirlin (2005) for the inner radiative correction in beta decay by providing a dispersive framework that treats hadronic and nuclear corrections within the same formalism. The new data-driven analysis yields a substantially shifted central value for Δ_R^V, creating considerable tension with the unitarity constraint on the first row of the CKM matrix, which serves as one of the most stringent constraints on New Physics in the charged current sector. The dispersive treatment enables future improvements as more precise data on nucleon form factors and neutrino scattering become available, and offers a unifying tool for addressing nuclear structure corrections absorbed in the ℱt values used to extract V_ud from superallowed nuclear decays.
