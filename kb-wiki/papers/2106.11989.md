# Classicalization and unitarization of wee partons in QCD and Gravity: The CGC-Black Hole correspondence

> We discuss a remarkable correspondence between the description of Black Holes as highly occupied condensates of $N$ weakly interacting gravitons and that of Color Glass Condensates (CGCs) as highly occupied gluon states. In both cases, the dynamics of "wee partons" in Regge asymptotics is controlled by emergent semi-hard scales that lead to perturbative unitarization and classicalization of $2\rightarrow N$ particle amplitudes at weak coupling. In particular, they attain a maximal entropy permitted by unitarity, bounded by the inverse coupling $α$ of the respective constituents. Strikingly, this entropy is equal to the area measured in units of the Goldstone constant corresponding to the spontaneous breaking of Poincar{é} symmetry by the corresponding graviton or gluon condensate. In gravity, the Goldstone constant is the Planck scale, and gives rise to the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy. Likewise, in the CGC, the corresponding Goldstone scale is determined by the onset of gluon screening. We point to further similarities in Black Hole formation, thermalization and decay, to that of the Glasma matter formed from colliding CGCs in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions, which decays into a Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Color Glass Condensates (CGCs) in QCD and Black Holes described as graviton condensates (the Black Hole N-portrait, BHNP) are deeply analogous saturated states, with the correspondence holding specifically at the saturation scale where both systems satisfy the critical packing condition \(N = 1/\alpha(Q_S)\) and attain a maximal entropy bounded by the inverse coupling.

## Method

The authors establish a dictionary between the BHNP and CGC by comparing their respective saturation equations, emergent scales, and entropy bounds. They analyze the unitarization of \(2\rightarrow N\) scattering amplitudes in multi-Regge kinematics for both gravity and QCD, showing that in both cases the saturation scale \(Q_S\) emerges from the condition that occupation number \(N\) and coupling \(\alpha\) satisfy \(N\alpha = 1\). They further connect the micro-state entropy to an area law via a Goldstone decay constant \(f\) arising from spontaneous breaking of Poincaré symmetry by the condensate.

## Key numbers

- For the BHNP: the graviton-graviton coupling at the saturation scale is \(\alpha_{\rm gr}(Q_S) = Q_S^2 / M_P^2 = (L_P Q_S)^2\), where \(M_P\) is the Planck mass and \(L_P\) the Planck length.
- For the BHNP: the saturation condition is \(\alpha_{\rm gr}(Q_S) N = 1\), and the Schwarzschild radius is \(R_S = M_{\rm BH} / M_P^2\).
- For the CGC: the saturation condition is \(\alpha_S(Q_S) N = 1\), with the gluon occupancy given by \(N = xG_A(x,Q_S^2) / [2(N_c^2-1)\pi R_A^2 Q_S^2]\).
- The maximal entropy bound is \(S_{\rm max} = 1/\alpha(Q_S)\), and equivalently \(S_{\rm max} = {\rm Area} \times f^2\), where \(f = \sqrt{N} Q_S\).
- For the BHNP: the Black Hole entropy is \(S_{\rm BH} = 1/\alpha_{\rm gr} = 4\pi R_S^2 / (4 L_P^2)\).
- The \(2\rightarrow N\) scattering rate for Black Hole formation is \(\Gamma_{2\rightarrow {\rm BH}} \sim e^{-1/\alpha_{\rm gr} + S_{\rm BH}}\).
- The saturation scale satisfies: for BHNP, \(Q_S^{-1} \gg M_P^{-1}\); for CGC, \(Q_S \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).

## Context

This work extends the Black Hole N-portrait (BHNP) framework of Dvali (2011, 2012) and the entropy bound arguments of Dvali (2019, 2020) to establish a concrete correspondence with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) description of high-energy QCD, as developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1993, 1994) and others. The correspondence builds on earlier insights by Lipatov (1982, 1991) regarding multi-Regge kinematics and by 't Hooft (1984), Gross and Mende (1987), and Amati, Ciafaloni, and Veneziano (1987) on Black Hole formation in trans-Planckian scattering. The paper enables a unified understanding of unitarization and classicalization in both QCD and gravity, and suggests that the Glasma formed in nuclear collisions and Black Hole decay share similar thermalization and information-scrambling dynamics.
