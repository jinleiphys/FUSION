# Nuclear matter with a Bose condensate of dibaryons in relativistic Hartree approximation

> The Green's functions are constructed and a one-loop calculation is given for the scalar and vector densities and for the equation of state of nuclear matter with a Bose condensate of dibaryons. This is the lowest approximation in the loop expansion of quantum hadrodynamics, sufficient to account for the presence of dibaryons not in the condensate in the heterophase nucleon-dibaryon matter. It leads to a finite effective nucleon mass and remains consistent with increasing the density.

# Nuclear matter with a Bose condensate of dibaryons in relativistic Hartree approximation

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree approximation (one-loop level) for nuclear matter containing a Bose condensate of dibaryons yields a finite effective nucleon mass at high densities, unlike mean-field theory where it vanishes, and accounts for the presence of non-condensate dibaryons that become dominant at high total baryon number density.

## Method

The authors constructed Green's functions for heterophase nucleon-dibaryon matter within quantum hadrodynamics, including sigma-omega-dibaryon mixing via a system of Gorkov-Dyson equations. They performed a one-loop calculation of the scalar and vector densities for nucleons and dibaryons, and derived the equation of state from the chemical potential, using renormalization counterterms to handle divergences. The dibaryon chemical potential was fixed by the relativistic Hugenholtz-Pines relation, yielding mu_D* = m_D*.

## Key numbers

- The d' dibaryon mass used in numerical calculations is m_D = 2060 MeV.
- The sigma-meson mass is m_sigma = 520 MeV and the omega-meson mass is m_omega = 783 MeV, with equilibrium Fermi wave number k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1}.
- For the parameter set h_sigma/(2g_sigma) = 0.6 and h_omega/(2g_omega) = 0.8, three critical densities rho_1, rho_2, rho_3 separate four regimes: normal nuclear matter, mixed nucleon-dibaryon matter, pure dibaryon matter, and dibaryon-antinucleon matter.
- The vacuum contribution to the dibaryon scalar density is given by 2m_D* rho_DS' = m_D^3 zeta(m_D*/m_D), where zeta(x) = (1/(4pi^2))[x^3 ln x + 1 - x - (5/2)(1-x)^2 + (11/6)(1-x)^3].
- Dibaryon effects become large when h_sigma/(2g_sigma) is approximately 0.84, derived from the condition 4g_sigma^4/m_N approx h_sigma^4/m_D.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field theory (MFT) treatment of dibaryon condensation by Faessler et al. (1997) and earlier studies by Baldin et al. and Chizov et al. that used van der Waals volume corrections. It goes beyond the assumption of previous works (Baldin et al. through Faessler et al.) that at zero temperature all dibaryons are in the condensate, by incorporating the Bogoliubov model of a dilute interacting Bose gas where a fraction of bosons is not in the condensate. The one-loop calculation enables the study of neutron star interiors with dibaryons without density restrictions and allows construction of the phase diagram for the transition from normal nuclear matter to dibaryon matter and quark matter.
