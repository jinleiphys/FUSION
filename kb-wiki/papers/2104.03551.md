# Interacting quark matter effects on the structure of compact stars

> In this work, we study the effects that interacting quark matter has on the stellar structure of strange and charm quark stars. Additionally, their stability against radial pulsations is analyzed using a first-order formalism for adiabatic general relativistic oscillations. Besides, the early stage of stellar evolution of neutron stars after the supernovae explosion, i.e. protoneutron stars, is investigated by considering the possibility of a first-order phase transition to quark matter rich in leptons, where the dynamics of conversion between phases is studied within a thermal nucleation model. For each kind of compact star mentioned we use for the quark phase the equation of state calculated within cold perturbative QCD (pQCD), parametrized only by its renormalization scale. We note that the original pQCD framework is manipulated appropriately to include neutrinos and extended to add heavy quarks to the original system composed only by up, down and strange quark flavors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that perturbative QCD (pQCD) equations of state, parametrized only by the renormalization scale, can be extended to include leptons and heavy quarks for studying compact star structure, but the reliability of these calculations is limited to high densities and carries inherent uncertainty from the unphysical renormalization scale dependence.

## Method

The author extends the cold pQCD equation of state of Kurkela et al. (2009) to include trapped electrons and neutrinos for protoneutron star conditions, and develops a novel method to incorporate heavy quarks (specifically charm) into the QCD system. The thermodynamic potential is computed using the KRV prescription, where the quark number density is treated as the fundamental quantity and integrated to obtain pressure, ensuring thermodynamic consistency. The stellar structure is solved using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations, and stability against radial pulsations is analyzed using a first-order formalism for adiabatic general relativistic oscillations. For protoneutron stars, a thermal nucleation model is used to study the dynamics of first-order phase transition to quark matter.

## Context

This work extends the cold pQCD equation of state of Kurkela et al. (2009) to include leptons for protoneutron star conditions, building on earlier bag-model studies of phase transitions in supernovae by Migdal (1979), Nakazato (2008), Sagert (2008), and Fischer (2010). The thermal nucleation framework extends previous work by Mintz (2009, 2010) that used simplified quark matter descriptions. The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in compact stars using first-principles QCD calculations rather than the MIT bag model, and can be constrained by upcoming observations from NICER, NICA/MPD, and multimessenger gravitational wave detections.
