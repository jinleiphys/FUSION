# The parity-violating asymmetry including QED corrections in high-energy electron-nucleus collisions

> The parity-violating asymmetry, accounting for the vector and axial-vector vertex plus self-energy correction as well as for vacuum polarization, is calculated nonperturbatively by solving the corresponding Dirac equation for the electronic scattering states. Investigating the nuclei $^{27}$Al, $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb at collision energies in the GeV region and at forward scattering angles matching the experimental geometries, it is found that the combined QED effects change the parity-violating asymmetry by less than one percent. The same is true for $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb at an energy of 150 MeV.

## Key claim

The combined QED corrections (vector and axial-vector vertex plus self-energy, plus vacuum polarization) change the parity-violating asymmetry by less than one percent for all investigated nuclei and energies, but the authors note that for the lower energy of 150 MeV these corrections must be taken into account if the experimental error is below 0.3%.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation nonperturbatively for electronic scattering states, incorporating the vector and axial-vector vertex plus self-energy corrections and vacuum polarization as effective potentials. The axial-vector vs potential is constructed from the inverse Fourier transform of the weak Born amplitude multiplied by the weak vs form factor, using the large-q approximation of Reed and Horowitz (2026). The resulting Dirac equation is solved via partial-wave analysis to obtain the QED-modified parity-violating asymmetry for each target and geometry.

## Key numbers

- For 150 MeV electrons on 12C at 30 degrees: total QED correction dA_pv^QED = -6.28 × 10^{-3} (0.628% decrease); A_pv = 5.131 × 10^{-7}, A_pv^QED = 5.099 × 10^{-7}
- For 1157 MeV electrons on 27Al at 7.61 degrees: dA_pv^QED = -6.45 × 10^{-3} (0.645% decrease); A_pv = 2.046 × 10^{-6}, A_pv^QED = 2.033 × 10^{-6}
- For 953 MeV electrons on 208Pb at 4.7 degrees: dA_pv^QED = 8.79 × 10^{-4} (0.088% increase); A_pv = 5.898 × 10^{-7}, A_pv^QED = 5.903 × 10^{-7}
- For 150 MeV electrons on 208Pb at 30 degrees: dA_pv^QED = 1.04 × 10^{-3} (0.104% increase); A_pv = 6.364 × 10^{-7}, A_pv^QED = 6.371 × 10^{-7}
- For 1063 MeV electrons on 208Pb at 5 degrees: dA_pv^QED = 2.47 × 10^{-3} (0.247% increase); A_pv = 7.178 × 10^{-7}, A_pv^QED = 7.196 × 10^{-7}
- For 150 MeV on 12C at 30 degrees: dispersive correction = 7.57 × 10^{-3}; total radiative correction (QED plus dispersion) = 1.86 × 10^{-3}
- For 150 MeV on 208Pb at 30 degrees: dispersive correction = 8.98 × 10^{-4}; total radiative correction = 2.02 × 10^{-3}
- No convergence obtained for dA_pv^QED from 48Ca at 2.18 GeV

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative QED correction calculations of Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2025) by including the axial-vector vertex plus self-energy correction, following the conjecture of Reed and Horowitz (2026) that this contribution is important. The results confirm the counteraction between vector and axial-vector vs corrections found by Reed and Horowitz (2026) using the semiclassical Eikonal approximation, though deviations occur for the combined QED corrections. The findings are directly relevant for the analysis of PREx and CREx experimental data at GeV energies, where QED corrections are negligible given experimental uncertainties, and for the planned MREx experiment at the MESA facility at 150 MeV, where the corrections must be considered if precision below 0.3% is achieved.
