# Statistical error propagation in ab initio no-core full configuration calculations of light nuclei

> We propagate the statistical uncertainty of experimental NN scattering data into the binding energy of $^3$H and $^4$He. We also study the sensitivity of the magnetic moment and proton radius of the $^3$H to changes in the NN interaction. The calculations are made with the no-core full configuration method in a sufficiently large harmonic oscillator basis. For those light nuclei we obtain $ΔE$($^3$H) = 0.015 MeV and $ΔE$($^4$He) = 0.055 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper propagates the statistical uncertainty from experimental NN scattering data into the binding energies of 3H and 4He using a Monte-Carlo sampling of the Gauss-OPE potential parameters, but the authors note that the systematic uncertainty from the choice of potential representation is expected to be about an order of magnitude larger than the statistical uncertainty reported here.

## Method

The authors used the no-core full configuration (NCFC) method with a harmonic oscillator basis to solve the many-body Schrodinger equation for 3H and 4He, employing the Gauss-OPE potential which has 44 independent fitting parameters. A Monte-Carlo approach generated 33 sets of potential parameters drawn from a multivariate normal distribution based on the covariance matrix from the chi-square fit to the Granada scattering database. Ground state energies were calculated at multiple basis truncation values (Nmax = 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20) and extrapolated to infinite basis using an exponential fit, with the statistical uncertainty taken as the standard deviation of the 33 Monte-Carlo extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- Statistical uncertainty in 3H binding energy: Delta E_stat(3H) = 0.015 MeV (from extrapolation using 7 Nmax points)
- Statistical uncertainty in 4He binding energy: Delta E_stat(4He) = 0.055 MeV (from extrapolation using 7 Nmax points)
- 3H extrapolated binding energy: E_infinity = -7.424 MeV (mean of 33 Monte-Carlo samples, Np=7)
- 4He extrapolated binding energy: E_infinity = -24.934 MeV (mean of 33 Monte-Carlo samples, Np=7)
- 3H magnetic moment: mu = 2.610(1) mu_N
- 3H proton radius: r_p = 1.4668(8) fm
- Variational minimum for 3H found at hbar Omega = 60 MeV
- Variational minimum for 4He found at hbar Omega = 65 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Perez (2014) that estimated the statistical uncertainty of the triton ground state energy using a similar Monte-Carlo approach with 205 samples, and it applies the same methodology to 4He for the first time. The results enable future work that includes three-body forces and systematic uncertainties from different potential representations, which the authors note from Perez (2014) are expected to be an order of magnitude larger than the statistical uncertainties reported here. The paper also demonstrates that the Gauss-OPE potential converges faster in NCSM calculations than the Reid93, Argonne V18, and NijmII potentials due to its softer core.
