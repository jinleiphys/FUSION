# Hunting for tetraquarks in ultra-pheripheral heavy ion collisions

> Ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions constitute an ideal setup to look for exotic hadrons because of their low event multiplicity and the possibility of an efficient background rejection. We propose to look for four-quark states produced by photon-photon fusion in these collisions at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.5~{\rm TeV}$. In particular, we focus on those states that would represent a definite smoking gun for the compact tetraquark model. We show that the $X(6900)$, a likely $cc\bar{c}\bar{c}$ compact state, is a perfect candidate for this search, and estimate a production cross section ranging from around $250$ nb to $1150$ nb, depending on its quantum numbers. Furthermore, we discuss the importance of ultra-peripheral collisions to the search for the scalar and tensor partners of the $X(3872)$ predicted by the diquarkonium model, and not yet observed. The completion of such a flavor-spin multiplet would strongly speak in favor of the compact tetraquark model.

## Key claim

Ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions (UPCs) at the LHC can produce compact tetraquark states via photon-photon fusion with cross sections large enough to be detectable, provided the states have quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) or \(2^{++}\) and couple strongly to vector mesons; the actual production cross sections depend on the unknown branching ratios of the states into vector meson pairs.

## Method

The authors compute the two-photon luminosity for lead-lead UPCs at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.5\) TeV using the STARlight code, which implements the equivalent-photon (Weizsacker-Williams) approximation with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density and a probability for no hadronic interaction. They estimate the partial width into two photons for each tetraquark candidate using vector meson dominance, relating the two-photon width to the decay width into vector mesons (e.g., \(J/\psi J/\psi\) for the \(X(6900)\) or \(J/\psi\rho/\omega\) for the \(X(3872)\) multiplet). The production cross section is then obtained by convolving the two-photon luminosity with the narrow-width approximation for the resonance.

## Key numbers

*   \(X(6900)\) with \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\): partial width into two photons divided by the di-\(J/\psi\) branching ratio \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}/\mathcal{B}_\psi \sim 104\) eV; production cross section in PbPb UPCs at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.5\) TeV divided by \(\mathcal{B}_\psi\) is \(\sim 282\) nb.
*   \(X(6900)\) with \(J^{PC}=2^{++}\): \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}/\mathcal{B}_\psi \sim 86\) eV; production cross section divided by \(\mathcal{B}_\psi\) is \(\sim 1165\) nb.
*   Scalar state \(X_0\) (mass \(\sim 3770\) MeV, \(0^{++}\)): \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} \sim 6.3\) eV; production cross section \(\sim 185\) nb.
*   Scalar state \(X_0'\) (mass \(\sim 4000\) MeV, \(0^{++}\)): \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} \sim 6.7\) eV; production cross section \(\sim 156\) nb.
*   Tensor state \(X_2\) (mass \(\sim 4000\) MeV, \(2^{++}\)): \(\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma} \sim 1.6\) eV; production cross section \(\sim 187\) nb.
*   The \(X(3872)\) mass is \(m_1 = 3872\) MeV; its total width is \(\Gamma_X = 1.39 \pm 0.34\) MeV (LHCb 2020).

## Context

This work proposes UPCs as a new production mechanism for compact tetraquarks, extending the search strategies for exotic hadrons beyond proton-proton collisions. It directly builds on the LHCb discovery of the \(X(6900)\) (Aaij 2020) and the diquarkonium model predictions for a full flavor-spin multiplet including scalar and tensor partners of the \(X(3872)\) (Maiani 2014). The estimated cross sections are large enough to motivate experimental searches at the LHC, potentially discriminating between the compact tetraquark and hadronic molecule interpretations of exotic states.
