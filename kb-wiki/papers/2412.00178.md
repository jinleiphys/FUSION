# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: subleading contributions

> In this work, we present an evaluation of subleading effects in the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. Using a recently derived optimized basis, we first study the matching of axial-vector contributions to short-distance constraints at the level of the scalar basis functions, finding that also the tails of the pseudoscalar poles and tensor mesons play a role. We then develop a matching strategy that allows for a combined evaluation of axial-vector and short-distance constraints, supplemented by an estimate of tensor-meson contributions based on simplified assumptions for their transition form factors. Uncertainties are primarily propagated from the axial-vector transition form factors and the variation of the matching scale, but we also consider estimates of the low-energy effect of hadronic states not explicitly included. In total, we obtain $a_μ^\text{HLbL}\big|_\text{subleading}=33.2(7.2)\times 10^{-11}$, which in combination with previously evaluated contributions in the dispersive approach leads to $a_μ^\text{HLbL}\big|_\text{total}=101.9(7.9)\times 10^{-11}$.

# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: subleading contributions

## Key claim

The subleading hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is evaluated at \(a_\mu^\text{HLbL}\big|_\text{subleading}=33.2(7.2)\times 10^{-11}\), but this result depends on a matching scale \(Q_0\) varied between 1.2 and 2.0 GeV and on simplified assumptions for tensor-meson transition form factors, with the uncertainty dominated by axial-vector transition form factors and the variation of the matching scale rather than by a fully model-independent dispersive reconstruction.

## Method

The authors evaluate subleading HLbL contributions using a narrow-resonance approximation in an optimized basis for the HLbL tensor, computing axial-vector contributions from \(f_1(1285)\), \(f_1'(1420)\), and \(a_1(1260)\) with transition form factors from a global data analysis, and estimating scalar (\(f_0(1370)\), \(a_0(1450)\)) and tensor (\(f_2(1270)\), \(a_2(1320)\), \(f_2'(1525)\)) contributions with vector-meson-dominance parameterizations. Below a matching scale \(Q_0\) the hadronic description is used, above \(Q_0\) perturbative QCD results are applied, and in mixed regions an operator product expansion relating to the vector-vector-axial-vector correlator is employed, with effective poles introduced to improve the matching between hadronic states and short-distance constraints.

## Key numbers

* Subleading HLbL contribution: \(a_\mu^\text{HLbL}\big|_\text{subleading}=33.2(7.2)\times 10^{-11}\), with decomposition \(a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{1,2}]=26.9(5.4)\times 10^{-11}\) and \(a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{3\text{--}12}]=6.3(3.0)\times 10^{-11}\)
* Total HLbL contribution combining subleading with previously evaluated dispersive contributions and charm-loop correction: \(a_\mu^\text{HLbL}\big|_\text{total}=101.9(7.9)\times 10^{-11}\)
* Axial-vector sum at \(Q_0=1.5\) GeV (without \(f_1\to\phi\gamma\) constraint): \(12.2(2.3)\times 10^{-11}\) for \(a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{1,2}]+a_\mu[\bar\Pi_{3\text{--}12}]\)
* Tensor-meson sum at \(Q_0=1.5\) GeV: \(-2.5(3)\times 10^{-11}\) (from \(f_2(1270)\), \(a_2(1320)\), \(f_2'(1525)\))
* Heavy scalar sum at \(Q_0=1.5\) GeV: \(-0.7(3)\times 10^{-11}\) (from \(f_0(1370)\), \(a_0(1450)\))
* Quark loop (LO+NLO) at \(Q_0=1.5\) GeV: \(6.9^{+0.2}_{-0.3}\times 10^{-11}\) with \(\alpha_s(Q_0)=0.35^{+0.11}_{-0.06}\)
* Previously evaluated dispersive contributions sum: \(65.7^{+3.1}_{-2.6}\times 10^{-11}\) (from \(\pi^0,\eta,\eta'\) poles, \(\pi^\pm\) and \(K^\pm\) boxes, \(S\)-wave rescattering)
* Charm-loop correction: \(3(1)\times 10^{-11}\)

## Context

This work completes the dispersive approach to HLbL scattering developed by Colangelo, Hoferichter, Stoffer, and collaborators over the past decade, extending previous evaluations of pseudoscalar poles (Hoferichter 2018, Holz 2024), pion and kaon boxes (Colangelo 2017, Stamen 2022), and \(S\)-wave rescattering (Colangelo 2017, Danilkin 2021, Deineka 2024) to include axial-vector, tensor, and heavy scalar contributions with matching to short-distance constraints. The result agrees with the white-paper evaluation (Aoyama 2020) and lattice-QCD calculations by the Mainz group (Chao 2021, 2022), while suggesting a slightly lower value than other lattice results (Blum 2023, Fodor 2024). Future improvements require additional experimental input for axial-vector transition form factors (possible at BESIII and Belle II) and a dispersive evaluation of tensor contributions in triangle kinematics (Ludtke 2023) to replace the simplified narrow-resonance approximation used here.
