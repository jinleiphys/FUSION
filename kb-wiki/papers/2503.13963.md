# Influence of the effective mass on the properties of nuclear matter at finite density and temperature

> Significance of the chiral symmetry restoration is studied by considering the role of the modification of the nucleon mass in nuclear medium at finite density and temperature. Using the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU density functional theory, we can create models that have an identical nuclear matter equation of state but different isoscalar and isovector effective masses at zero temperature. Effect of the effective mass becomes transparent at non-zero temperatures, and it becomes more important as temperature increases. Role of the effective mass is examined thoroughly by calculating the dependence of thermodynamic variables such as free energy, internal energy, entropy, pressure and chemical potential on density, temperature and proton fraction. We find that sensitivity to the isoscalar effective mass is several times larger than that of the isovector effective mass, so the uncertainties arising from the effective mass are dominated by the isoscalar effective mass. In the analysis of the relative uncertainty, we obtain that the maximum uncertainty is less than 2% for free energy, internal energy and chemical potential, but it amounts to 20% for pressure. Entropy shows a behavior completely different from the other four variables that the uncertainty is about 40% at the saturation density and increases monotonically as density increases. Effect of the uncertainty to properties of physical systems is investigated with the proto-neutron star. It is shown that temperature depends strongly on the effective mass at a given density and substantial swelling of the radius occurs due to the finite temperature. Equation of state is stiffer with smaller isoscalar effective mass, so the effect of the effective mass appears clearly in the mass-radius relation of the proto-neutron star, larger radius corresponding to smaller effective mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isoscalar effective mass dominates uncertainties in thermodynamic properties of nuclear matter at finite temperature, with sensitivity several times larger than that of the isovector effective mass, but this conclusion is conditional on using the KIDS density functional framework where the zero-temperature equation of state is held fixed across all models.

## Method

The authors used the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) density functional theory to construct ten Skyrme-type models that share an identical nuclear matter equation of state at zero temperature but differ in their isoscalar and isovector effective masses at saturation density, with values ranging from 0.7 to 1.0 times the free nucleon mass. They then calculated thermodynamic variables (free energy, internal energy, entropy, pressure, and chemical potential) as functions of density, temperature, and proton fraction for these models, and applied the resulting equations of state to compute proto-neutron star properties under isentropic, neutrino-trapped conditions using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar effective mass ratios at saturation density: 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, and 1.0 times the free nucleon mass
- Isovector effective mass ratios at saturation density: 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, and 1.0 times the free nucleon mass
- Maximum relative uncertainty less than 2% for free energy, internal energy, and chemical potential
- Maximum relative uncertainty amounts to 20% for pressure
- Entropy uncertainty is about 40% at saturation density (0.16 fm^-3) and increases monotonically with density
- Saturation density fixed at 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy per particle at 16.0 MeV, and incompressibility at 240 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter
- Proto-neutron star radius for the canonical 1.4 solar mass star constrained to 11.8-13.1 km by the KIDS0 model

## Context

This work extends the KIDS density functional framework previously developed by the group (Togashi et al., various years) to systematically study the role of effective mass in hot nuclear matter, building on earlier studies that examined effective mass effects in electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering. The results are relevant for numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and proto-neutron star evolution, where thermodynamic quantities are essential inputs, and complement prior work by Constantinou (2014, 2015), Schneider (2019), and Raithel (2021) on thermal effects in dense matter. The finding that smaller isoscalar effective mass produces stiffer equations of state and larger proto-neutron star radii provides a testable prediction for future neutron star observations.
