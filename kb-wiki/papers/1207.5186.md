# The Surface Tension of Quark Matter in a Geometrical Approach

> The surface tension of quark matter plays a crucial role for the possibility of quark matter nucleation during the formation of compact stellar objects, because it determines the nucleation rate and the associated critical size. However, this quantity is not well known and the theoretical estimates fall within a wide range, $γ_0 \approx 5-300 MeV/fm^2$. We show here that once the equation of state is available one may use a geometrical approach to obtain a numerical value for the surface tension that is consistent with the model approximations adopted. We illustrate this method within the two-flavor linear σmodel and the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with two and three flavors. Treating these models in the mean-field approximation, we find $γ_0 \approx 7-30 MeV/fm^2$. Such a relatively small surface tension would favor the formation of quark stars and may thus have significant astrophysical implications. We also investigate how the surface tension decreases towards zero as the temperature is raised from zero to its critical value.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the surface tension of quark matter can be extracted from the equation of state using a geometrical approach, yielding values in the range gamma_0 approximately 7-30 MeV/fm^2 for the linear sigma model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in mean-field approximation, which is significantly lower than the upper bound of previous estimates (5-300 MeV/fm^2) and would favor quark star formation.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy density f_T(rho) for uniform matter across the full density range, including the thermodynamically unstable region between the two coexistence densities, using the mean-field approximation for both the two-flavor linear sigma model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with two and three flavors. The surface tension gamma_T is then obtained from a formula involving an integral of the square root of the free energy deficit Delta f_T(rho) over the density interval between the coexistence densities, with an effective interaction range a = 0.33 fm fixed by the sigma meson mass. For the linear sigma model, the authors also solve the Euler-Lagrange equation to obtain the spatial profile of the order parameter sigma(z) and the density profile across the interface.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension at zero temperature for the linear sigma model: gamma_0 approximately 12.98 MeV/fm^2 (from the thin-wall approximation in prior work) and approximately 17 MeV/fm^2 when vacuum terms are included
- Surface tension range from the present geometrical approach across all models: gamma_0 approximately 7-30 MeV/fm^2
- For the linear sigma model: phase coexistence starts at (mu = 918 MeV, T = 0) and ends at critical point (mu_c = 621 MeV, T_c = 99 MeV)
- For the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with parameter set I: phase coexistence starts at (mu = 1045.5 MeV, T = 0) and ends at (T_c = 46 MeV, mu_c = 996 MeV); constituent quark mass in vacuum M_vac = 337 MeV
- For the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with parameter set II: phase coexistence starts at (mu = 1146.3 MeV, T = 0) and ends at (T_c = 81 MeV, mu_c = 990 MeV); constituent quark mass in vacuum M_vac = 400 MeV
- Interface width from the linear sigma model profile: b = 1.85 a, where a = 0.33 fm
- For the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at T = 0: phase coexistence occurs at mu = 1083 MeV; the strange quark mass decreases from 549 MeV to 464.4 MeV across the interface, while the light quark mass decreases from 367.6 MeV to 52.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the geometrical approach developed by Randrup (2009) for evaluating interface tension from the equation of state to quark matter models, and directly compares with the thin-wall approximation results of Palhares and Fraga (2010) who found gamma_0 approximately 5-15 MeV/fm^2 for the linear sigma model. The results contradict the much larger estimates of Alford et al. (2001) who found gamma_0 approximately 300 MeV/fm^2 for color-flavor locked interfaces, and are consistent with the lower range of estimates by Heiselberg and Sato (1995) of gamma_0 approximately 10-50 MeV/fm^2. The relatively small surface tension values enable further studies of quark matter nucleation during core-collapse supernovae and the possible existence of quark stars, as well as investigations of mixed phases in compact stars as discussed by Kurkela (2010).
