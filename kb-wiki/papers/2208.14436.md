# Hyperon bulk viscosity and $r$-modes of neutron stars

> We propose and apply a new parameterization of the modified chiral effective model to study rotating neutron stars with hyperon cores in the framework of the relativistic mean-field theory. The inclusion of mesonic cross couplings in the model has improved the density content of the symmetry energy slope parameters, which are in agreement with the findings from recent terrestrial experiments. The bulk viscosity of the hyperonic medium is analyzed to investigate its role in the suppression of gravitationally driven $r$-modes. The hyperonic bulk viscosity coefficient caused by non-leptonic weak interactions and the corresponding damping timescales are calculated and the $r$-mode instability windows are obtained. The present model predicts a significant reduction of the unstable region due to a more effective damping of oscillations. We find that from $\sim 10^8$ K to $\sim 10^{9}$ K, hyperonic bulk viscosity completely suppresses the $r$-modes leading to a stable region between the instability windows. Our analysis indicates that the instability can reduce the angular velocity of the star up to $\sim$0.3~$Ω_K$, where $Ω_K$ is the Kepler frequency of the star.

## Key claim

The paper's new parameterization of the modified chiral effective model, which includes mesonic cross couplings, predicts that hyperonic bulk viscosity completely suppresses r-modes in neutron stars over the temperature range from approximately 10^8 K to 10^9 K, creating a stable region between instability windows; the authors qualify that this suppression depends on the specific model parameterization and the inclusion of sigma-rho meson cross coupling.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field model with a modified chiral effective Lagrangian that includes mesonic cross couplings (sigma-rho coupling with parameter eta_1) and applied it to charge-neutral hyperon-rich neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static stars and used the RNS code for rotating configurations, then calculated the hyperonic bulk viscosity coefficient from non-leptonic weak interaction processes (involving Lambda and Sigma hyperons) using a relaxation time approximation, and computed the corresponding r-mode damping timescales to determine instability windows.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for a non-rotating configuration: 2.05 solar masses at central density 1.55 x 10^15 g cm^-3, with radius 11.86 km
- Kepler frequency for the rotating star: Omega_K = 1420 Hz, with corresponding mass 2.57 solar masses and equatorial radius 16.5 km
- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.151 fm^-3; symmetry energy J_0 = 32.5 MeV; symmetry energy slope L_0 = 61 MeV; incompressibility K = 211 MeV
- Lambda hyperons appear at approximately 1.7 rho_0; Sigma hyperons appear in the range 2.22 < rho_B/rho_0 < 3.8; Xi hyperons appear at 3.3 rho_0
- Hyperonic bulk viscosity is maximum for temperatures around 10^8 K for the relevant densities
- The instability can reduce the angular velocity of the star up to approximately 0.3 Omega_K

## Context

This work extends previous studies of r-mode damping in neutron stars with hyperonic matter, particularly building on the framework of Lindblom (1998) and the hyperonic bulk viscosity calculations of Jones (2001) and Lindblom and Owen (2002). The new parameterization improves upon earlier chiral model applications by Jha (2004, 2006, 2007, 2008) by including sigma-rho mesonic cross couplings, which brings the symmetry energy slope parameters into agreement with terrestrial experiments such as heavy ion collision flow data and neutron skin thickness measurements. The results enable more precise predictions of gravitational wave signatures from rotating neutron stars and constrain the possible rotation rates of massive neutron stars with hyperon cores.
