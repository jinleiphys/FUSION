# A new form of three-body Faddeev equations in the continuum

> We propose a novel approach to solve the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev equation which avoids the complicated singularity pattern going with the moving logarithmic singularities of the standard approach. In this new approach the treatment of the 3N Faddeev equation becomes essentially as simple as the treatment of the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Very good agreement of the new and old approaches in the application to nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and the breakup reaction is found.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a reformulation of the three-nucleon Faddeev equation that completely avoids the moving logarithmic singularities of the standard approach, making the numerical treatment of the kernel as simple as solving the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation; the authors demonstrate very good agreement between the new and old approaches for nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and breakup observables at 13 MeV.

## Method

The authors start from the partial-wave decomposed three-nucleon Faddeev equation for the breakup amplitude and examine all six possible choices for analytically integrating over the internal angular and momentum variables in the integral kernel. They identify one choice (case 2, analytical integration over x and p'') that yields only simple poles in a single integration variable, cleanly separating the free three-nucleon propagator pole from the deuteron pole. The new approach is applied for q'' ≤ q_max (where the old approach had moving singularities) and combined with the standard approach for q'' > q_max, using the CD Bonn potential restricted to two-nucleon partial waves with total angular momentum j ≤ 1.

## Key numbers

- Incoming neutron laboratory energy: E^n_lab = 13 MeV for the nd elastic scattering and breakup comparisons
- The free propagator pole occurs at p_0 = sqrt(3/4 (q_max^2 - q^2)), which is of concern only for q ≤ q_max = sqrt(4/3 m E)
- The deuteron pole occurs at q'' = q_0 = sqrt(4m/3 (E - E_d))
- For the finite-rank force demonstration, the coupling constants are G_11 = sqrt(2)/8 = G_22 and G_12 = -3 sqrt(2)/8 = G_21

## Context

This work extends the standard three-nucleon Faddeev approach developed by Gloeckle et al. (1980s-1990s), which handled moving logarithmic singularities by direct integration on the real momentum axis using Spline interpolations. The new formulation avoids the contour deformation method used for separable forces (Schmid and Ziegelmann) and the problems encountered by Huber (1994) with the virtual state pole of the 2N t-matrix in the 1S0 state. The simplification should enable easier treatment of initial and final state interactions in electromagnetic processes on the three-nucleon system and can be extended to include three-nucleon forces without changing the singularity structure.
