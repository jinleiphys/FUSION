# Exact 4He Spectral Function in a Semirealistic NN Potential Model

> The spectral function of 4He is calculated with the Lorentz integral transform method in a large energy and momentum range. The excitation spectrum of the residual 3N-system is fully taken into account. The obtained spectral function is used to calculate the quasi elastic longitudinal (e,e') response R_l of 4He for q=300, 400, and 500 MeV/c. Comparison with the exact R_l shows a rather sizeable disagreement except in the quasi elastic peak, where the differences reduce to about 10% at q=500 MeV/c. It is shown as well that very simple momentum distribution approximations for the spectral function provide practically the same results for R_l as the exact spectral function.

# Exact 4He Spectral Function in a Semirealistic NN Potential Model

## Key claim

The full spectral function of 4He is calculated for the first time using the Lorentz integral transform method with a semirealistic central NN potential (Trento model), but the resulting plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) longitudinal response shows sizeable disagreement with the exact response except near the quasi-elastic peak, where differences reduce to about 10% at q=500 MeV/c.

## Method

The spectral function S(k,E) for 4He is obtained by solving a three-body inhomogeneous equation via expansion in hyperspherical harmonics, using the Lorentz integral transform method to handle the continuum of the residual 3N-system. The resulting proton spectral function is then used within the PWIA to compute the longitudinal (e,e') response R_L at momentum transfers q=300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, and compared to the exact R_L from the same NN potential model from Ref. ELO97.

## Key numbers

- At q=500 MeV/c, the PWIA quasi-elastic peak height overestimates the exact R_L by 13%, and the peak is shifted by 12 MeV
- At q=400 MeV/c, the PWIA quasi-elastic peak height overestimation is 25%
- At q=300 MeV/c, the PWIA quasi-elastic peak is shifted by 15 MeV and the peak height is overestimated by more than 40%
- The spectral function sum rules are satisfied with relative differences of 0.9% (norm), 0.2% (mean kinetic energy), and 0.8% (Koltun sum rule)
- The spectral factor for the TN potential is 0.89, compared to 0.84 for the Argonne v18 + Urbana IX realistic potential

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz integral transform method from response functions (ELO94, ELO97) to the full spectral function of 4He, which previously had only approximate evaluations neglecting final state interactions in the residual 3N-system (MS91). The results enable quantitative assessment of the spectral function ansatz in inclusive (e,e') scattering for a tightly bound nucleus, and show that simple momentum distribution approximations (CS92) yield R_L results very close to those from the full spectral function. The authors note similar findings for 3H and 3He at q=300 and 400 MeV/c (Gl94).
