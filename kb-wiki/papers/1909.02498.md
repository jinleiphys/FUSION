# A simple family of solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for fireballs with Hubble flow and ellipsoidal symmetry

> New, analytic solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics are presented, describing expanding fireballs with Hubble-like velocity profile and ellipsoidal symmetry, similar to fireballs created in heavy ion collisions. We find that with these specifications, one obtains solutions where the shear viscosity essentially does not influence the time evolution of the system, thus these solutions are particularly adept tools to study the effect of bulk viscosity alone, which always results in a slower decrease of energy density as well as temperature compared to the case of perfect fluid. We investigate different scenarios for the bulk viscosity and find qualitatively different effects on the time evolution which suggests that there is a possibility to infer the value of bulk viscosity from energy density and temperature measurements in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents new analytic solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for fireballs with Hubble-flow velocity profiles and ellipsoidal symmetry, where shear viscosity and thermal conductivity effects cancel identically, allowing the study of bulk viscosity in isolation. The authors find that bulk viscosity always results in a slower decrease of energy density and temperature compared to the perfect fluid case, and that different assumptions about the bulk viscosity's functional form produce qualitatively different time evolutions.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamic equations in the Eckart frame, assuming a Hubble-type velocity field \(u^\mu = x^\mu/\tau\) and an equation of state \(\varepsilon = \kappa p\) with constant \(\kappa\). They reduce the partial differential equations to ordinary differential equations for pressure as a function of proper time \(\tau\) by exploiting the vanishing shear stress tensor \(\sigma^{\mu\nu}=0\) for Hubble flow, then solve these ODEs analytically for five distinct cases of bulk viscosity dependence (constant \(\zeta\), \(\zeta \propto s\), \(\zeta \propto n\), \(\zeta \propto T^\kappa\), and \(\zeta \propto \Pi\)) under both Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart frameworks.

## Key numbers

- For Case A (no conserved \(n\), constant \(\zeta\)): pressure solution is \(p(\tau) = \left[p_0 - \frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)d - \kappa}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau_0}\right]\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{d\frac{\kappa+1}{\kappa}} + \frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)d - \kappa}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau}\), where \(d=3\) is the spatial dimension.
- For Case C (no conserved \(n\), \(\zeta \propto T^\kappa\)): when \(\kappa \neq d\), \(p(\tau) = p_0\left\{\left(1+\frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)(\kappa-d)}\frac{\zeta_0}{p_0\tau_0}\right)\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\frac d\kappa} - \frac{d^2}{(\kappa+1)(\kappa-d)}\frac{\zeta_0}{p_0}\frac 1\tau\right\}^{\kappa+1}\).
- For Case D (conserved \(n\), \(\zeta \propto n\)): when \(\kappa \neq d\), \(p(\tau) = \left[p_0+\frac{d^2}{\kappa-d}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau_0}\right]\left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\frac{\kappa+1}{\kappa} d} - \frac{d^2}{\kappa-d}\frac{\zeta_0}{\tau_0}\frac{\tau_0^{d+1}}{\tau^{d+1}}\).
- For Case F (Israel-Stewart, \(\zeta \propto \Pi\)): \(\Pi(\tau) = \Pi_0 \left(\frac{\tau_0}{\tau}\right)^{\frac{d \zeta_0}{\Pi_0 \tau_{_\Pi}}} \exp\left[-\frac{\tau-\tau_0}{\tau_{_\Pi}}\right]\), with \(\Pi_0 < 0\) required by the second law of thermodynamics.
- In Case B (conserved \(n\), constant \(\zeta\)), the temperature diverges asymptotically as \(T \propto \tau^{d-1}\) for \(d>1\), making the solution unphysical.

## Context

This work extends the family of analytic perfect-fluid solutions with Hubble flow and ellipsoidal symmetry (the CCHK solutions of Csorgo et al. (2003, 2004) and the CNC solutions of Csorgo et al. (2006, 2008)) to include viscous effects, specifically bulk viscosity. The solutions complement the Gubser solution (Gubser, 2010) and the CKCJ solutions (Csorgo et al., 2018) by providing a framework where shear viscosity and heat conduction are exactly canceled, enabling clean extraction of bulk viscosity effects. The results suggest that measurements of energy density and temperature evolution in heavy-ion collisions could be used to infer the value of bulk viscosity, and the solutions are valid for arbitrary shear viscosity coefficients and thermal conductivity when temperature depends only on proper time.
