# Investigation of collective radial expansion and stopping in heavy ion collisions at Fermi energies

> We present an analysis of multifragmentation events observed in central Xe+Sn reactions at Fermi energies. Performing a comparison between the predictions of the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model and experimental data, we investigate the impact of the compression-expansion dynamics on the properties of the final reaction products. We show that the amount of radial collective expansion, which characterizes the dynamical stage of the reaction, influences directly the onset of multifragmentation and the kinematic properties of multifragmentation events. For the same set of events we also undertake a shape analysis in momentum space, looking at the degree of stopping reached in the collision, as proposed in recent experimental studies. We show that full stopping is achieved for the most central collisions at Fermi energies. However, considering the same central event selection as in the experimental data, we observe a similar behavior of the stopping power with the beam energy, which can be associated with a change of the fragmentation mechanism, from statistical to prompt fragment emission.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the amount of radial collective expansion directly influences the onset of multifragmentation and the kinematic properties of multifragmentation events in central Xe+Sn reactions at Fermi energies, but the authors note that the multifragmentation threshold in their Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model is shifted to higher beam energies compared to experimental data, indicating an underestimation of the radial expansion in the simulations.

## Method

The authors performed Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model calculations for central and mid-central collisions of 129Xe+119Sn at beam energies of 25, 32, 35, 39, 45, and 50 MeV/nucleon, using impact parameters from 0 to 4 fm (and up to 6.5 fm for lower energies). They extracted the radial expansion energy from the velocity profile of the composite source, applied a coalescence procedure with density cut-offs of 0.03 and 0.09 fm−3 to identify fragments, and then fed the primary fragments into the SIMON statistical de-excitation code to obtain final reaction products. The simulated events were compared to INDRA experimental data using the same flow angle selection criterion (θflow > 60°) to isolate central collisions.

## Key numbers

- The radial expansion energy reaches 30% of the available kinetic energy in the center of mass for the highest beam energy (50 MeV/nucleon), in agreement with experimental estimates.
- The multifragmentation regime in SMF calculations is achieved above Eproj = 39 MeV/nucleon, while experimentally multifragmentation occurs above a beam energy of 25 MeV/nucleon for Xe+Sn central collisions.
- For the reaction at 45 MeV/nucleon, the cross section of selected events (θflow > 60°) is 1.9 mb in both SMF+SIMON and INDRA data after normalization.
- At 50 MeV/nucleon, the mean fragment multiplicity reaches approximately 6 around t = 240 fm/c for the standard density cut-off of 0.03 fm−3.
- The probability that events have fragment multiplicity ≥ 3 is 0.98 for SMF+SIMON at 45 MeV/nucleon and 1.00 at 50 MeV/nucleon, compared to 0.99 and 0.98 in INDRA data respectively.
- The maximum density reached by the composite source shows an almost linear correlation with the radial expansion energy across beam energies from 25 to 50 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective expansion in multifragmentation by the FOPI collaboration (Jeong, Hsi, Poggi, Kunde) and statistical model analyses (Marie, Hudan, Bellaize, Lefevre, Piantelli, Beaulieu) by using a dynamical transport model (SMF) instead of treating fragment production and collective expansion separately. The paper also addresses the stopping power measurements first investigated by the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf) and later applied at Fermi energies by Lehaut (2012), showing that full stopping is achieved for the most central collisions but that the stopping power trend with beam energy can be associated with a change from statistical to prompt fragment emission. The results enable future combined studies of collective expansion and stopping to extract information on nuclear matter properties such as compressibility and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
