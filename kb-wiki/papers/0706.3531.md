# On the Origin of the "Ridge" phenomenon induced by Jets in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We argue that "ridge" in 2-particle correlation function associated with hard trigger at RHIC heavy ion collisions is naturally explained by an interrelation of jet quenching and hydrodynamical transverse flow. The excess particles forming the ridge are produced by QCD bremsstrahlung along the beam (and thus have wide rapidity distribution) and then boosted by transverse flow. Nontrivial correlation between directions of the jet and the radial flow is provided by jet quenching: our straightforward and basically parameter-independent calculation reproduces the angular shape, width and other properties of the "ridge".

## Key claim

The "ridge" in two-particle correlations at RHIC is explained by QCD bremsstrahlung gluons radiated along the beam direction, which are then boosted by transverse radial flow; the crucial correlation between the jet direction and the flow direction is induced by jet quenching, but the author notes the model is not quantitatively accurate as the calculated width is larger than observed.

## Method

The author calculates the distribution of hard collision points in the transverse plane using a simple production probability proportional to (R² − r²) and an exponential quenching probability exp(−L/l_abs), where L is the distance the triggered jet travels through nuclear matter. The resulting distribution in the r−φ₁ plane is then convolved with a boosted Boltzmann thermal distribution at freezeout temperature T_f = 100 MeV, using a linear "Hubble law" v(r) = r/(10 fm) for the radial flow velocity. The final correlation function C(p_t, φ) is obtained by averaging over the jet origination point r and the jet emission angle φ₁, with the observable angle φ = φ₁ − φ₂ between the trigger particle and the associated particle.

## Key numbers

- The observed ridge has a width of about 1 radian in relative azimuthal angle φ = φ₁ − φ₂, approximately twice that of the jet.
- For p_t ≈ 2.25 GeV and T_f ≈ 100 MeV, at the fireball edge where v ≈ 0.7, the flow-boosted distribution gives F ≈ exp(−11 φ₂²), which is extremely well collimated.
- The calculation uses a linear radial flow profile v(r) = r/(10 fm).
- For absorption lengths l_abs = 1, 0.5, and 0.25 fm, the resulting correlation functions are shown; for l_abs > 3 fm the ridge correlation disappears catastrophically.
- The ridge is observed in the region p_t ∼ 2 GeV, where the p/π⁺ ratio crosses 1.
- The data shown are from AuAu collisions at √s = 200 GeV in the 0–10% central collision bin.

## Context

This work extends the idea of Voloshin (2003) that the location of the hard collision point can be correlated with transverse flow, providing the first quantitative estimates based on that mechanism. The paper contrasts with the alternative mechanism of Armesto (2004) which proposed a longitudinal extension of a jet due to longitudinal flow; the author notes that model would predict a ridge near the away-side jet, which has not been observed. The calculation enables further experimental tests using three-particle correlations, specifically (2+1) correlations where two hard particles in the trigger should produce a ridge at a completely different location, orthogonal to both jets.
