# Nonlinear amplification of instabilities with longitudinal expansion

> We study the dynamics of nonequilibrium instabilities in anisotropically expanding systems. The most prominent example of such a system is the 'Glasma' in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, where the expansion is a consequence of approximately boost-invariant initial conditions. Here we consider the problem of parametric resonance in scalar $N$-component quantum field theories with boost-invariant initial conditions, which is similar in spirit. We find that many aspects of the dynamics can be treated analytically by introducing a generalized conformal time. Primary instabilities, which are described by the linearized evolution equations, are seen to lead to a secondary regime of amplifications with strongly enhanced growth rates due to nonlinear corrections. For the secondary instabilities we present a power-counting scheme for weak coupling, and discuss their role for the question of isotropization and the establishment of an equation of state.

## Key claim

The authors show that in anisotropically expanding scalar field theories with boost-invariant initial conditions, primary instabilities from linearized evolution lead to a secondary regime of amplification with strongly enhanced growth rates due to nonlinear corrections, which can be treated analytically by introducing a generalized conformal time and a power-counting scheme for weak coupling.

## Method

The authors study parametric resonance in a scalar N-component quantum field theory with quartic self-interaction and boost-invariant initial conditions, using comoving coordinates (proper time τ and rapidity η). They introduce a generalized conformal time variable θ ∝ τ^(2/3) to map the expanding system onto an equivalent problem in Minkowski space-time, solve the linearized evolution equations for the macroscopic field and fluctuations analytically using Jacobi elliptic functions and Lamé equations, and then incorporate nonlinear corrections via the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action at next-to-leading order in a 1/N expansion.

## Key numbers

- Initial field amplitude: φ(τ₀) = √(6N/λ) σ₀ with σ₀τ₀ = 5, N = 4, λ = 10⁻⁴
- Maximum linear growth rate: γ₀ ≈ 2π e⁻π / K(1/2) for dimensionless momentum p̃₀² ≈ 1/4
- Resonance band condition for exponential growth: 0 ≤ p̃² ≤ 1/2
- Set-in time for modes with p_T = 0: (τ_Start/τ₀)^(2/3) = √(2ν²)/(σ₀τ₀)
- Freeze-out time for modes with ν = 0: (τ_End/τ₀)^(2/3) = σ₀²/(2 p_T²)
- Condition for unstable modes: p_T⁴ ν² / (σ₀⁶ τ₀²) ≤ 1/54

## Context

This work extends the study of nonlinear amplification of instabilities from isotropic expansion in early-universe inflaton preheating (Berges 2002, Berges 2004) to anisotropically expanding systems relevant for the Glasma in heavy-ion collisions. It provides analytic understanding for secondary growth rates previously observed in fixed-box studies of SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theory (Berges 2007, Fujii 2009) and in classical simulations with longitudinal expansion (Romatschke 2005, Fukushima 2011). The results enable quantitative predictions for isotropization timescales and the establishment of an equation of state in heavy-ion collisions, and provide a framework for comparing 2PI 1/N expansion results with classical-statistical simulations.
