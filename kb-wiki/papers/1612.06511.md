# Jet and heavy flavor production in heavy-ion collisions

> We review recent progress in the theoretical description of hard probes in heavy-ion collisions within the framework of Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET). Firstly, we consider the inclusive production of heavy flavor mesons and jets with a small radius parameter in proton-proton collisions. Secondly, we describe how in-medium effects can be incorporated consistently with the proton-proton baseline calculations. The in-medium interactions are described by Glauber gluon interactions. We present results for the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ which is most commonly used to study the quenching of hadron or jet production yields in heavy-ion collisions and we compare to recent data from the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper reviews a theoretical framework (SCET$_{\mathrm{M,G}}$) that consistently incorporates in-medium Glauber gluon interactions into next-to-leading-order (NLO) proton-proton baseline calculations for heavy flavor and jet production, achieving agreement with LHC data for $D^0$ mesons and non-prompt $J/\psi$ suppression, but notes that cold nuclear matter effects are not included in the presented results.

## Method
The authors calculate the proton-proton baseline for open heavy flavor production using the Zero Mass Variable Flavor Number Scheme at NLO with fragmentation functions from Kneesch (2007) and Kniehl (2008). In-medium effects are incorporated by adding collinear in-medium splitting functions (obtained from SCET$_{\mathrm{M,G}}$) to the vacuum splitting functions, computed to first order in opacity. For inclusive jets, semi-inclusive jet functions are evolved via timelike DGLAP equations in Mellin moment space to resum single logarithms in the jet radius parameter $R$ at NLO+NLL$_R$ accuracy.

## Key numbers
- $D^0$ meson $R_{AA}$ results compared to CMS data at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, $|\eta|<1$, $0-10\%$ centrality
- Non-prompt $J/\psi$ (from $B$-hadrons) $R_{AA}$ results compared to CMS data at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, $|\eta|<2.4$ for minimum bias events
- Jet cross section calculations for $R=0.2, 0.3, 0.4$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, $|\eta|<2$ using anti-$k_T$ reclustering algorithm
- The $\ln R$ resummation is significant even for relatively large $R\sim 0.6$

## Context
This work extends the Soft Collinear Effective Theory framework for hard probes in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating Glauber gluon interactions (Idilbi 2008, Ovanesyan 2011) and finite quark masses (Leibovich 2003) into a unified SCET$_{\mathrm{M,G}}$ approach. It goes beyond traditional parton energy loss models (Gyulassy 2000, Djordjevic 2003) by computing full collinear in-medium splitting functions. The results enable future calculations of inclusive jet modification and jet substructure observables in heavy-ion collisions, building on the small-$R$ jet resummation framework (Kang 2016).
