# Charm and bottom production in inclusive double Pomeron exchange in heavy ion collisions at the LHC

> The inclusive double Pomeron exchange cross section for heavy quark pair production is calculated for nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC. The present estimate is based on hard diffractive factorization, corrected by absorptive corrections and nuclear effects. The theoretical uncertainties for nuclear collisions are investigated and a comparison to other approaches is presented. The production channels producing a similar final state configuration are discussed as well.

# Charm and bottom production in inclusive double Pomeron exchange in heavy ion collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The inclusive double Pomeron exchange cross section for heavy quark pair production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC is calculated using hard diffractive factorization with absorptive corrections and nuclear effects, but the predictions carry substantial theoretical uncertainties from the gap survival probability in nuclear collisions, which remains an open question. The diffractive channel dominates over exclusive photoproduction in proton-proton collisions but is two or more orders of magnitude smaller than the photon-Pomeron contribution in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors compute the inclusive double Pomeron exchange cross section for charm and bottom quark pair production using two approaches: the Ingelman-Schlein partonic Pomeron model with H1 diffractive parton distribution functions, and the Bialas-Landshoff soft Pomeron model. Both are corrected by a gap survival probability factor from the KMR parametrization, and nuclear coherent diffractive cross sections for CaCa and PbPb collisions are obtained using a Glauber-based optical approximation with Woods-Saxon nuclear densities. The results are compared to competing channels including photon-Pomeron ultra-peripheral collisions and two-photon production.

## Key numbers
- In pp collisions at LHC: inclusive DPE cross section for charm is 13.6–0.53 microbarn (IS approach vs BL approach); for bottom is 0.053–0.027 microbarn
- Diffractive ratio in pp collisions: 0.17% to 7×10^{-3}% for charm; 0.01% to 0.007% for bottom
- Gap survival probability used: S_gap^2 = 0.026 at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV; S_gap^2 = 0.032 at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV; S_gap^2 = 0.031 at sqrt(s) = 6.3 TeV
- Coherent DPE cross section for PbPb collisions: 31.1–4.2 microbarn for charm; 0.32–0.2 microbarn for bottom
- Coherent DPE cross section for CaCa collisions: 22.8–2.8 microbarn for charm; 0.25–0.14 microbarn for bottom
- Photon-Pomeron channel in PbPb: 59 mb for charm; 10 microbarn for bottom
- Two-photon channel in PbPb: 1.81–1.95 mb for charm; 2–2.2 microbarn for bottom
- Muller-Schramm model for PbPb after gap survival correction: 3.8 microbarn for charm; 0.02 nb for bottom
- Inelastic pp cross section at sqrt(s) = 6 TeV: 73 mb

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study on single diffractive heavy quark production (Ducati, Machado, Machado, 2009) and provides complementary predictions for the double Pomeron exchange channel. The calculations are compared to the Bialas-Landshoff approach (Bialas and Landshoff, 1991) and the Muller-Schramm formalism (Muller and Schramm, 1995) for meson production in double Pomeron exchange. The results enable experimental searches at the LHC (particularly ALICE) for central heavy flavor production with large rapidity gaps, and highlight the need for kinematic cuts to separate diffractive, two-photon, and photon-Pomeron channels in heavy-ion collisions.
