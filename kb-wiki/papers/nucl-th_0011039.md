# Finite temperature amplitudes and reaction rates in Thermofield dynamics

> We propose a method for calculating the reaction rates and transition amplitudes of generic process taking place in a many body system in equilibrium. The relationship of the scattering and decay amplitudes as calculated in Thermo Field Dynamics the conventional techniques is established. It is shown that in many cases the calculations are relatively easy in TFD.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that reaction rates and transition amplitudes for generic processes in a many-body system at finite temperature can be calculated directly using the S-matrix formalism of Thermo Field Dynamics (TFD), with the same phase-space factors as at zero temperature, and that this direct method yields results equivalent to those obtained from the generalized Cutkosky rules (GCR). The hedge is that the equivalence is demonstrated only for tree-level processes and for specific decay and scattering examples, not for all possible loop orders or interactions.

## Method

The authors construct the thermal S-matrix operator in TFD using the doubled Hilbert space and the thermal Lagrangian consisting of the difference between the physical and tilde interaction Lagrangians. They compute transition amplitudes by evaluating matrix elements of this S-matrix between temperature-dependent initial and final states built from thermal creation operators acting on the thermal vacuum, using Bogoliubov transformations to express physical fields in terms of thermal operators. The decay rates and cross sections are then obtained from these amplitudes using the standard zero-temperature formulas for phase space and flux factors, without introducing additional thermal factors beyond those already contained in the amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- For sigma meson decay into two pions: the ratio of decay rates is Gamma(T not equal 0)/Gamma(T=0) = (1 + 2 n_B(M/2)), where n_B is the Bose-Einstein distribution and M is the sigma mass.
- For Higgs boson decay into electron-positron pair: the ratio of decay rates is Gamma(T not equal 0)/Gamma(T=0) = (1 - n_F(M/2))(1 - bar n_F(M/2))/(1 + n_B(M)), where n_F and bar n_F are Fermi-Dirac distributions for particles and antiparticles.
- For boson decay into three bosons: the squared amplitude on the mass shell yields the factor [(1+n_1)(1+n_2)(1+n_3) - n_1 n_2 n_3] times the energy-conserving delta function, where n_i are Bose-Einstein distributions.
- The detailed balance relation for boson decay into m particles is Gamma_d/Gamma_i = exp(beta omega), where omega is the energy of the decaying particle.

## Context

This work extends the real-time finite-temperature formalism of Thermo Field Dynamics, originally developed by Umezawa and collaborators, to direct perturbative calculation of scattering and decay amplitudes. It establishes equivalence with the generalized Cutkosky rules developed by Kobes and Semenoff for finite-temperature discontinuities, and with the imaginary-time Matsubara formalism as applied by Dolan and Jackiw. The method enables practical calculation of reaction rates in hot and dense nuclear matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and to early universe and stellar interior physics, without requiring the analytic continuation needed in imaginary-time approaches.
