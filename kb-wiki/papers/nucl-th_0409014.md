# Neutron stars with isovector scalar correlations

> Neutron stars with the isovector scalar $δ$-field are studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field ($RMF$) approach in a pure nucleon plus lepton scheme. The $δ$-field leads to a larger repulsion in dense neutron-rich matter and to a definite splitting of proton and neutron effective masses. Both features are influencing the stability conditions of the neutron stars. Two parametrizations for the effective nonlinear Lagrangian density are used to calculate the nuclear equation of state ($EOS$) and the neutron star properties, and compared to correlated Dirac-Brueckner results. We conclude that in order to reproduce reasonable nuclear structure and neutron star properties within a $RMF$ approach a density dependence of the coupling constants is required.

## Key claim

The isovector scalar δ-field, when included in a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach, produces a larger repulsion in dense neutron-rich matter and a definite splitting of proton and neutron effective masses, both of which significantly influence neutron star stability; however, to reproduce reasonable nuclear structure and neutron star properties within an RMF approach, a density dependence of the coupling constants is required.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using equations of state (EOS) derived from two RMF parametrizations (Set A and Set B, the latter based on NL3) for nuclear matter including the isovector scalar δ-meson field. They considered two compositions for the neutron star: pure neutron matter and β-stable (n,p,e⁻) matter, with the proton fraction determined by charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions. The results were compared to correlated Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations, specifically the DBBM, DBT, and DBHM approaches.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ for Set A and 0.148 fm⁻³ for Set B.
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.0 MeV (Set A) and -16.299 MeV (Set B).
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 31.3 MeV (Set A) and 33.7 MeV (Set B).
- Incompressibility K: 240.0 MeV (Set A) and 271.7 MeV (Set B).
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M*/M at saturation: 0.75 (Set A) and 0.60 (Set B).
- Maximum neutron star mass for pure neutron matter with Set A: 2.24 M☉ (NLρ) and 2.56 M☉ (NLρδ); with Set B: 2.88 M☉ (NLρ) and 3.12 M☉ (NLρδ).
- Maximum neutron star mass for (npe⁻) matter with Set A: 2.14 M☉ (NLρ) and 2.30 M☉ (NLρδ); with Set B: 2.83 M☉ (NLρ), with no solution for NLρδ.
- Corresponding central densities for pure neutron matter maximum mass: 5.75 ρ₀ (Set A, NLρ), 4.65 ρ₀ (Set A, NLρδ), 4.12 ρ₀ (Set B, NLρ), 3.55 ρ₀ (Set B, NLρδ).
- Corresponding radii for pure neutron matter maximum mass: 11.44 km (Set A, NLρ), 12.56 km (Set A, NLρδ), 13.70 km (Set B, NLρ), 14.69 km (Set B, NLρδ).

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of the δ-field in asymmetric nuclear matter at low densities (s1, s2, s3) and in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies (s4, s5, s6) to the regime of neutron star properties. The results contradict the applicability of the NL3 parametrization (s11) at high densities, showing it is too stiff and fails to produce β-equilibrium solutions when the δ-field is included, while Set A is consistent with softer DBHF predictions (DBT from s13) and with heavy ion collision data (s18, s19). The findings enable more realistic neutron star structure calculations using density-dependent RMF parametrizations, as subsequently performed by Menezes (2004) using the Set A Lagrangian.
