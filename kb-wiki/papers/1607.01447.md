# Direct virtual photon production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV

> We report the direct virtual photon invariant yields in the transverse momentum ranges $1\!<\!p_{T}\!<\!3$ GeV/$c$ and $5\!<\!p_T\!<\!10$ GeV/$c$ at mid-rapidity derived from the dielectron invariant mass continuum region $0.10<M_{ee}<0.28$ GeV/$c^{2}$ for 0-80\% minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. A clear excess in the invariant yield compared to the number-of-binary-collisions ($N_{bin}$) scaled $p+p$ reference is observed in the $p_T$ range $1\!<\!p_{T}\!<\!3$ GeV/$c$. For $p_T\!>6$ GeV/$c$ the production follows $N_{bin}$ scaling. Model calculations with contributions from thermal radiation and initial hard parton scattering are consistent within uncertainties with the direct virtual photon invariant yield.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports a clear excess in direct virtual photon invariant yield relative to the binary-collision-scaled p+p reference in the transverse momentum range 1 < pT < 3 GeV/c for 0-80% minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV, while for pT > 6 GeV/c the production follows Nbin scaling; the excess is interpreted as consistent with thermal radiation from the quark-gluon plasma, but the authors note that model calculations including both thermal radiation and initial hard parton scattering are consistent within uncertainties, meaning the data do not uniquely isolate a thermal component.

## Method

The direct virtual photon invariant yield was derived from the dielectron invariant mass continuum region 0.10 < Mee < 0.28 GeV/c2 at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV. The measurement used the STAR detector to reconstruct electron-positron pairs and subtracted the hadronic decay background (mainly from pi0, eta, and omega Dalitz decays and vector meson decays) to isolate the direct virtual photon signal. The yield was obtained in two transverse momentum ranges: 1 < pT < 3 GeV/c and 5 < pT < 10 GeV/c, and compared to a p+p reference scaled by the number of binary collisions (Nbin).

## Key numbers

* Direct virtual photon invariant yield in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV shows a clear excess over Nbin-scaled p+p reference in the pT range 1 < pT < 3 GeV/c.
* For pT > 6 GeV/c, the direct virtual photon production follows Nbin scaling, consistent with hard scattering processes.
* The measurement covers the dielectron invariant mass continuum region 0.10 < Mee < 0.28 GeV/c2.
* The analysis uses 0-80% minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of direct photon production in heavy-ion collisions by using the virtual photon method (via internal conversion to e+e- pairs), which provides access to lower pT regions than real photon measurements. The observed excess at low pT (1-3 GeV/c) is consistent with expectations for thermal radiation from a quark-gluon plasma, as modeled by calculations such as those from Turbide et al. (2004) and Rapp (2013), while the high-pT region (above 6 GeV/c) confirms the dominance of hard scattering processes as seen in p+p collisions. The results enable further constraints on the initial temperature and evolution of the hot QCD medium created in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
