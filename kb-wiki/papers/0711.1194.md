# On $pp \to p K Î›, N K ÎŁ, pp Ď•$ -- the basic ingredients for strangeness production in heavy ion collisions

> The strangeness production in heavy ion collisions was proposed to be probes of the nuclear equation of state, Kaon potential in nuclear medium, strange quark matter and quark-gluon plasma, etc. However, to act as reliable probes, proper understanding of the basic ingredients for the strangeness production, such as $pp \to pK^+Î›$, $pp \to pp Ď•$ and $pp \to nK^+ÎŁ^+$ is necessary. Recent study of these reactions clearly shows that previously ignored contributions from the spin-parity $1/2^-$ resonances, $N^*(1535)$ and $Î”^*(1620)$, are in fact very important for these reactions, especially for near-threshold energies. It is necessary to include these contributions for getting reliable calculation for the strangeness production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the previously ignored subthreshold spin-parity \(1/2^-\) resonances \(N^*(1535)\) and \(\Delta^*(1620)\) are in fact crucial for reproducing near-threshold experimental data for the basic strangeness-production reactions \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\), \(pp \to pp\phi\), and \(pp \to nK^+\Sigma^+\), and therefore must be included in calculations for heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors review effective Lagrangian calculations for each reaction, including Feynman diagrams with t-channel meson exchanges (\(\pi^0, \eta, \rho^0\) for \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\) and \(pp \to pp\phi\); \(\pi^+, \rho^+\) for \(pp \to nK^+\Sigma^+\)) and the excitation of the subthreshold resonances \(N^*(1535)\) or \(\Delta^*(1620)\). Coupling constants for the \(N^*(1535)\) to \(K\Lambda\) and \(N\phi\) are deduced from BES data on \(J/\psi\) decays and from \(\pi^- p \to n\phi\) data, respectively. Final-state interactions (FSI) are included where relevant.

## Key numbers

- For \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\): the ratio of effective coupling constants \(g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda}/g_{N^*(1535)p\eta} = 1.3 \pm 0.3\), deduced from BES branching ratios.
- For \(pp \to pp\phi\): the coupling constant \(g_{N^*(1535)N\phi} = 0.13\) is determined from fitting \(\pi^- p \to n\phi\) data.
- For \(pp \to nK^+\Sigma^+\): the \(\Delta^*(1620)\) contribution via \(\rho^+\) exchange is found to be very important for the two lowest data points close to threshold; the FSI enhances the total cross section by a factor of about 3 for these points.
- The \(\Delta^*(1620)\) resonance is about 85 MeV below the \(N\rho\) threshold; the \(\Sigma^*(1750)1/2^-\) is about 70 MeV below the \(NK^*\) threshold; the \(\Xi^*(1950)\) is about 60 MeV below the \(\Lambda K^*\) threshold.

## Context

This work extends previous resonance models (Sibirtsev 1999, Tsushima 1999) that fit older high-energy data but underestimated recent COSY near-threshold data for \(pp \to nK^+\Sigma^+\) and \(pp \to pK^+\Lambda\) by orders of magnitude. It also addresses the OZI-rule suppressed \(pp \to pp\phi\) reaction, where earlier calculations (Sibirtsev 2006, Titov, Nakayama) assumed t-channel \(\rho\) exchange or nucleon-pole dominance. The results enable more reliable calculations of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that subthreshold resonances, rather than the naive OZI rule, explain the enhanced \(\phi\) production, complicating attempts to extract nucleon strangeness content (Ellis). The work also motivates searches for predicted \(1/2^-\) SU(3) decuplet partners, such as an \(\Omega^* 1/2^-\) around 2160 MeV, at future facilities like BEPC2, CEBAF, and JPARC.
