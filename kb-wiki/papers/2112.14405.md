# Single-state or low-lying-states dominance mechanism of $2־½־²־²$-decay nuclear matrix elements

> The $2־½־²־²$-decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for 11 nuclei are studied with the self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) based on Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Skyrme HFB) model. As a common feature pointed out in https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.98.064325 Phys. Rev. C 98, 064325 (2018), negative contributions in the running sums of NMEs are found, and play important roles in the fulfillment of the single-state dominance or low-lying-states dominance hypothesis. By comparing the results of QRPA model and quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approximation (QTDA) model, we find that the negative contributions are due to the enhanced ground-state correlations, which are brought by the backward amplitude in QRPA model and tuned by strong isoscalar pairing interaction. The enhancement of ground-state correlations will change the signs of GT$^{+}$ transition amplitudes of higher-lying states and leads to the negative contributions in the running sum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that negative contributions in the running sums of $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) are caused by enhanced ground-state correlations introduced through the backward amplitude in the QRPA model, which are tuned by strong isoscalar pairing interaction; these negative contributions enable the fulfillment of the single-state dominance (SSD) or low-lying-states dominance (LLD) hypothesis, but only for specific nuclei and depending on the value of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$.

## Method

The authors performed spherical self-consistent QRPA calculations based on Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Skyrme HFB) mean-field calculations using the SkO' interaction, with density-dependent delta pairing forces. They computed the Gamow-Teller $2\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs for 11 nuclei by summing over all virtual intermediate $1^+$ states of the intermediate nucleus, including the overlap between initial and final HFB ground states and the overlap of intermediate states constructed from mother and daughter nuclei. The isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength $f_{\rm IS}$ was adjusted to reproduce experimental NMEs, and results were compared with quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approximation (QTDA) calculations that lack ground-state correlations.

## Key numbers

* For $^{48}$Ca with $g_A=1.27$, the experimental NME is $0.046 \pm 0.004$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the theoretical NME is $0.046$ MeV$^{-1}$, with SSD contribution $0.036$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $f_{\rm IS}=0.7570$.
* For $^{76}$Ge with $g_A=1.00$, the experimental NME is $0.221 \pm 0.012$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the theoretical NME is $0.221$ MeV$^{-1}$, with LLD contribution $0.256$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $f_{\rm IS}=1.1612$.
* For $^{128}$Te with $g_A=1.27$, the experimental NME is $0.056 \pm 0.007$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the theoretical NME is $0.057$ MeV$^{-1}$, with SSD contribution $0.043$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $f_{\rm IS}=1.1928$.
* For $^{136}$Xe with $g_A=1.27$, the experimental NME is $0.022 \pm 0.001$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the theoretical NME is $0.022$ MeV$^{-1}$, with SSD contribution $0.003$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $f_{\rm IS}=0.9968$.
* For $^{238}$U with $g_A=1.27$, the experimental NME is $0.158^{+0.109}_{-0.085}$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the theoretical NME is $0.157$ MeV$^{-1}$, with SSD contribution $0.185$ MeV$^{-1}$ and $f_{\rm IS}=1.1787$.
* Most determined $f_{\rm IS}$ values are around 1.2, except for magic nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{116}$Cd, and $^{136}$Xe which have smaller values.
* When $g_A=1.27$, the first $1^+$ state contributes more than 75% of the total NME for $^{48}$Ca, $^{82}$Se, $^{116}$Cd, $^{128}$Te, and $^{238}$U, fulfilling the SSD hypothesis.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Simkovic (2018) who first identified negative contributions in running sums of $2\nu\beta\beta$ NMEs as a common feature across different nuclear models. The paper systematically analyzes 11 nuclei with experimental NMEs, building on the earlier observation by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) that isoscalar pairing suppresses $M^{2\nu}_{\rm GT}$ and on the work of Muto (1989) linking ground-state correlations to NME suppression. The results enable future studies to understand which nuclei satisfy SSD or LLD hypotheses under different $g_A$ values, and provide a mechanism for the cancellation among higher-lying $1^+$ states that makes these hypotheses valid.
