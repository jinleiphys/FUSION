# Screening length in plasma winds

> We study the screening length L_s of a heavy quark-antiquark pair in strongly coupled gauge theory plasmas flowing at velocity v. Using the AdS/CFT correspondence we investigate, analytically, the screening length in the ultra-relativistic limit. We develop a procedure that allows us to find the scaling exponent for a large class of backgrounds. We find that for conformal theories the screening length is (boosted energy density)^{-1/d}. As examples of conformal backgrounds we study R-charged black holes and Schwarzschild-anti-deSitter black holes in (d+1)-dimensions. For non-conformal theories, we find that the exponent deviates from -1/d and as examples we study the non-extremal Klebanov-Tseytlin and Dp-brane geometries. We find an interesting relation between the deviation of the scaling exponent from the conformal value and the speed of sound.

## Key claim

For conformal gauge theories, the screening length of a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled plasma wind scales as (boosted energy density)^{-1/d} in the ultra-relativistic limit, while for non-conformal theories the exponent deviates from -1/d; the authors develop an analytic procedure to find this scaling exponent for a large class of backgrounds, with the caveat that the analysis is restricted to the ultra-relativistic limit where analytic computations become tractable.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a heavy quark-antiquark pair as a fundamental string in a boosted black hole background, where the boost creates a "plasma wind." They derive the Nambu-Goto action for the string in a general metric with power-law fall-off behavior near the boundary, then analyze the equations of motion in the ultra-relativistic limit (large boost parameter η) to extract the scaling of the maximum screening length L_s with the boosted energy density. The procedure is applied to conformal backgrounds (Schwarzschild-AdS in d+1 dimensions and R-charged black holes) and non-conformal backgrounds (non-extremal Klebanov-Tseytlin geometry and Dp-brane geometries).

## Key numbers

- For SAdS_5 (dual to 4d N=4 SYM) at θ=π/2 (wind perpendicular to dipole): L_s ∼ 0.743 / (√(cosh η) π T) or equivalently L_s ∼ 0.328 √N_c / (ε_0 cosh η)^{1/4}
- For SAdS_5 at θ=0 (wind parallel to dipole): L_s ∼ 0.838 / (√(cosh η)) (R²/r_0) or equivalently L_s ∼ 0.370 √N_c / (ε_0 cosh η)^{1/4}
- For SAdS_5 at θ=π/2, the turning point parameter ℏq_m = √2 cosh η at maximum screening length
- For SAdS_5 at θ=0, the turning point parameter ℏp_m = 1.38 cosh η at maximum screening length
- For conformal theories in d dimensions: ν = 1/d, giving L_s ∝ (boosted energy density)^{-1/d}
- For SAdS_d: ν = 1/d, with L_s ∝ 1/(T (cosh η)^{1/d})

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT study of screening lengths in moving plasmas initiated by Liu, Rajagopal and Wiedemann (2006), who computed the screening length numerically for the five-dimensional Schwarzschild-AdS black hole and found it proportional to (boosted energy density)^{-1/4}. The present paper provides an analytic treatment in the ultra-relativistic limit, generalizing to R-charged black holes (finite chemical potential), higher-dimensional SAdS, and non-conformal backgrounds such as the Klebanov-Tseytlin geometry and Dp-brane solutions. The results enable systematic comparison of screening length scaling across different holographic duals and suggest a relation between the deviation of the scaling exponent from the conformal value and the speed of sound in the plasma.
