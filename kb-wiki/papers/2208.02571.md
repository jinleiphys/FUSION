# Proton number cumulants and correlation functions from hydrodynamics and the QCD phase diagram

> We analyze the behavior of (net-)proton number cumulants in central collisions of heavy ions across a broad collision energy range by utilizing hydrodynamic simulations. The calculations incorporate essential non-critical contributions to proton fluctuations such as repulsive baryonic core and exact baryon number conservation. The experimental data are consistent with non-critical physics at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \geq 20$ GeV. The data from STAR and HADES Collaborations at lower collision energies indicate an excess of (multi-)proton correlations over the non-critical reference. This observation is discussed in the context of different mechanisms, including the possibility of a critical point in the baryon-rich region of the QCD phase diagram.

# Proton number cumulants and correlation functions from hydrodynamics and the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim

The experimental data on (net-)proton cumulants in central heavy-ion collisions are consistent with non-critical physics (baryon number conservation and short-range repulsion) at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \geq 20$ GeV, but data from STAR and HADES at lower energies show an excess of (multi-)proton correlations over this non-critical reference, which could indicate a QCD critical point in the baryon-rich region but also could arise from other mechanisms such as volume fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC framework for 0-5% central Au-Au collisions across a broad range of collision energies, incorporating non-critical contributions to proton fluctuations through analytic approximations and Monte Carlo sampling at the Cooper-Frye particlization stage. The calculations included repulsive baryonic core effects via excluded volume and exact baryon number conservation using the SAM-2.0 method. For the HADES data at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.4$ GeV, a Siemens-Rasmussen like fireball model was used with chemical and kinetic freeze-out parameters from Harabasz (2020) and Motornenko (2021), accounting for protons bound into light nuclei.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \geq 20$ GeV, experimental data on net-proton cumulant ratio $\kappa_3/\kappa_1$ and scaled variance $\kappa_2/\kappa_1$ are quantitatively described by the non-critical hydrodynamic calculation incorporating both baryon repulsion and baryon conservation
- At $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 3$ GeV (STAR-FXT) and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.4$ GeV (SIS-HADES), the non-critical calculations show no change in trend for $\kappa_2/\kappa_1$ while experimental data show a dramatic enhancement
- The HADES data at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.4$ GeV on rapidity acceptance of proton cumulant ratios up to fourth order can be described by thermal emission from a grand-canonical heat bath with baryon number susceptibilities exhibiting the hierarchy $\chi_4^B \gg -\chi_3^B \gg \chi_2^B \gg \chi_1^B$, with chemical freeze-out at $T \sim 70$ MeV and $\mu_B \sim 850-900$ MeV
- For the ALICE data at 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, the normalized variance $\kappa_2[p-\bar{p}]/\langle p+\bar{p} \rangle$ shows suppression relative to the baseline of unity, consistent with global baryon number conservation when hadronic phase dynamics are neglected

## Context

This work extends the approach of Vovchenko (2021) by providing a systematic comparison of non-critical hydrodynamic calculations with experimental data from ALICE (2019), STAR (2020, 2021), and HADES (2020) across a broad energy range. It contrasts with efforts to incorporate critical fluctuations directly into hydrodynamic frameworks (Bluhm 2020, An 2021) or molecular dynamics approaches with a critical point (Sorensen 2020, Kuznietsov 2022), which are not yet ready for expanding systems. The results enable future experimental constraints from the RHIC BES-II program and combined measurements of $\kappa_2[p-\bar{p}]/\langle p+\bar{p} \rangle$ and $\kappa_2[p+\bar{p}]/\langle p+\bar{p} \rangle$ to distinguish between local and global baryon conservation scenarios, as well as fluctuation measurements involving light nuclei (ALICE 2022).
