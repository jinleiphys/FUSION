# Deep inelastic scattering from light nuclei

> We review recent developments in the study of deep inelastic scattering from light nuclei, focusing in particular on deuterium, helium, and lithium. Understanding the nuclear effects in these systems is essential for the extraction of information on the neutron structure function.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical status of deep inelastic scattering from light nuclei (deuterium, helium, and lithium) and argues that nuclear corrections, particularly off-shell effects and Fermi motion, are essential for extracting the free neutron structure function, even in loosely bound systems like the deuteron.

## Method

The authors use a covariant, relativistic impulse approximation framework to compute nuclear structure functions. For deuterium, they include off-shell corrections evaluated from relativistic wave functions and the \(p^2\) dependence of truncated nucleon structure functions. For \(A=3\) nuclei, they employ convolution models with nucleon momentum distributions from Faddeev equations (Paris potential) and variational approaches, while noting that relativistic wave functions for \(A=3\) are not yet available.

## Key numbers

- Off-shell corrections to the deuteron \(F_2\) structure function are small: \(|\delta^{\rm (off)} F_2^D| \lesssim 1\%\) for all \(x\).
- For the deuteron spin-dependent \(g_1\) structure function, the convolution approximation deviates from the full result by \(\lesssim 1-2\%\) for \(x < 0.8\).
- In \(^3\)He, the effective neutron polarization is \(\rho_n \approx 88\%\), and the proton polarization is \(\rho_p \approx -2\%\).
- For \(^4\)He quasi-elastic scattering, binding effects modify the intrinsic nucleon structure by \(< 5\%\) for \(x < 0.8\).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the nuclear EMC effect, which has been studied for heavier nuclei at CERN, SLAC, DESY (Hermes), Fermilab, and Jefferson Lab. The paper specifically addresses the lack of data on the EMC effect in deuterium and helium, which prevents unambiguous extraction of the free neutron structure function. It builds on prior theoretical approaches such as the on-shell ansatz of Frankfurt and Strikman (1980s) and the relativistic off-shell model of Melnitchouk, Schreiber, and Thomas (1990s). The work enables future extraction of neutron structure functions from \(A=3\) mirror nuclei and suggests that lithium isotopes at radioactive beam facilities (e.g., RIKEN or GSI) could provide new tests of nuclear quark structure.
