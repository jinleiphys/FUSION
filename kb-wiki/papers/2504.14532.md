# Correlations and fluctuations in a magnetized three-flavor PNJL model with and without inverse magnetic catalysis effect

> The correlations $χ^{BQ}_{11},\ χ^{BS}_{11} ,\ χ^{QS}_{11}$ and quadratic (quartic) fluctuations $χ^{B,\ Q,\ S}_{2,4}$ of baryon number $B$, electric charge $Q$ and strangeness $S$ are investigated in a three-flavor PNJL model at finite temperature and magnetic field. The inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is introduced through the magnetic field dependent parameters $G(eB)$ or $T_0(eB)$, and we make comparison of the results in the cases with and without IMC effect. Since including IMC effect does not change the strength of phase transition under external magnetic field, it does not lead to qualitative difference in the correlations and fluctuations, but modifies their values. Under vanishing and nonvanishing magnetic field, the correlations and fluctuations increase with temperature, and then show the peak around the pseudocritical temperatures of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions. The peak structure in $χ^{BQ}_{11}$, $χ^B_{4}$ and $χ^Q_{4}$ are much more apparent than in others. The correlations and fluctuations along the phase transition line under external magnetic field are characterized by the scaled correlations ${\hat χ}_{11}^{XY}=\frac{χ_{11}^{XY}(eB,T_{pc}^c(eB))}{χ_{11}^{XY}(eB=0,T_{pc}^c(eB=0))}$ and scaled quadratic (quartic) fluctuations ${\hat χ}_{2,4}^{X}=\frac{χ_{2,4}^{X}(eB,T_{pc}^c(eB))}{χ_{2,4}^{X}(eB=0,T_{pc}^c(eB=0))}$, with $X,\ Y=B,\ Q,\ S$ and $X \neq Y$ at the pseudocritical temperature $T_{pc}^c$ of chiral restoration phase transition. They increase with magnetic fields due to the increase of phase transition strength under magnetic fields. Among them, ${\hat χ}_{11}^{BQ}$ increases fastest, which may serve as the magnetometer of QCD.

## Key claim
The inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect, introduced via magnetic-field-dependent parameters \(G(eB)\) or \(T_0(eB)\), does not change the strength of the chiral restoration phase transition under external magnetic field, and therefore does not produce qualitative differences in the correlations and fluctuations of conserved charges, only modifying their numerical values.

## Method
The authors employed a three-flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at finite temperature and magnetic field, with the IMC effect implemented through two separate schemes: a magnetic-field-dependent quark coupling \(G(eB)\) and a magnetic-field-dependent Polyakov loop scale parameter \(T_0(eB)\), both fitted to Lattice QCD results for the decreasing pseudocritical temperature of chiral restoration. The correlations \(\chi^{BQ}_{11}, \chi^{BS}_{11}, \chi^{QS}_{11}\) and quadratic (quartic) fluctuations \(\chi^{B,Q,S}_{2,4}\) were computed from derivatives of the thermodynamic potential with respect to chemical potentials at zero chemical potential, using covariant Pauli-Villars regularization.

- Current quark masses \(m_0^{u}=m_0^{d}=5.5\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_0^s=154.7\ \text{MeV}\); coupling parameters \(G\Lambda^2=3.627\), \(K\Lambda^5=92.835\), cutoff \(\Lambda=1101\ \text{MeV}\).
- Polyakov potential parameters: \(a_0=6.75\), \(a_1=-1.95\), \(a_2=2.625\), \(a_3=-7.44\), \(b_3=0.75\), \(b_4=7.5\), \(T_0=270\ \text{MeV}\).
- Magnetic field values studied: \(eB/m^2_{\pi}=0,\ 10,\ 20\).
- Scaled correlation \(\hat{\chi}^{BQ}_{11}\) increases fastest with magnetic field among all scaled correlations and fluctuations, proposed as a magnetometer of QCD.

## Context
This work extends analytical studies of conserved-charge correlations and fluctuations in magnetized QCD matter, previously conducted in the hadron resonance gas model, PNJL model, and Polyakov loop extended chiral quark model (e.g., Ding et al. (2022, 2025) and Ding et al. (2024) for Lattice QCD; Ref. [dingref35] for earlier PNJL calculations). It specifically addresses the three-flavor case with IMC effect, which was not well considered in prior analytical work. The results enable comparison with Lattice QCD simulations (e.g., Lattice QCD collaborations (2012-2025)) and suggest that \(\hat{\chi}^{BQ}_{11}\) could serve as a practical magnetometer for detecting magnetic fields in QCD matter produced in heavy-ion collisions or present in compact stars.
