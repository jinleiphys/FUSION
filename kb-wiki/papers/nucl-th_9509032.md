# The Low-Energy Neutron-Proton Scattering Parameters and the Deuteron

> The low-energy parameters describing the np scattering in the 3S1 + 3D1 partial waves, the deuteron parameters, and their relations are discussed. These parameters can be determined quite accurately in the energy-dependent Nijmegen partial-wave analyses of the np data and are also given by the high-quality Nijmegen potentials. The newest values for these parameters are presented.

## Key claim

The low-energy neutron-proton scattering parameters and deuteron properties can be determined with high precision from the energy-dependent Nijmegen partial-wave analyses and from high-quality Nijmegen potentials, but the effective-range expansion is not very accurate unless many terms are included, limiting the usefulness of the extracted effective-range parameters.

## Method

The authors performed energy-dependent partial-wave analyses (Nijm PWA93) of np scattering data below T_L = 350 MeV, including the deuteron binding energy as an experimental datum, and constructed three high-quality (HQ) Nijmegen potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Reid93) that fit the data with chi^2/N_d = 1.03. They extracted low-energy scattering parameters from the effective-range expansion of the Blatt-Biedenharn eigenphase shifts and computed deuteron parameters from the pole of the S-matrix in the coupled ^3S_1 + ^3D_1 partial waves.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length a = 5.4194(20) fm
- Effective range r = 1.7536(25) fm
- Shape parameters: v_2 = 0.040(7) fm^3, v_3 = 0.673(2) fm^5, v_4 = 3.95(5) fm^7, v_5 = 27.0(3) fm^9
- Deuteron binding energy B = 2.224575(9) MeV
- Deuteron radius (relativistic) R = 4.318946 fm
- d/s ratio eta = 0.0253(2)
- Deuteron effective range rho_d = 1.765(4) fm
- Residue N_p^2 = 0.7830(15) fm^{-1}
- Asymptotic normalizations: A_S = 0.8845(8) fm^{-1/2}, A_D = 0.0223(2) fm^{-1/2}
- d-state probability p_d = 5.67(4)%
- Mean-square radius <r^2>^{1/2} = 1.9676(10) fm
- Electric quadrupole moment Q_0 = 0.271(1) fm^2

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen partial-wave analyses (Nijm PWA93) and high-quality potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Reid93) that describe np scattering data below 350 MeV. The recommended parameters supersede earlier determinations and provide benchmarks for comparison with experimental measurements, such as the atomic physics determination of the deuteron mean-square radius by Pachucki (1994) and the experimental quadrupole moment by Bishop (1979). The results enable improved calculations of deuteron properties and serve as input for nuclear structure and reaction models, while the discrepancy between Q_0 and the experimental quadrupole moment highlights the need for meson exchange current, relativistic, and Q^6 state contributions.
