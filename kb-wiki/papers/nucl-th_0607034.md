# $^1S_0$ superfluid phase--transition in neutron matter with realistic nuclear potentials and modern many--body theories

> The $^1S_0$ pairing in neutron matter has been investigated in presence of realistic two-- and three--nucleon interactions. We have adopted the Argonne $v_{8^\prime}$ NN and the Urbana IX 3N potentials. Quantum Monte Carlo theory, specifically the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method, and Correlated Basis Function theory are employed in order to get quantitative and reliable estimates of the gap. They both fully take into account the medium modifications due to the interaction induced correlations. The two methods are in good agreement up to the maximum gap density and both point to a slight reduction with respect to the standard BCS value. In fact, the maximum gap is about $2.5 \text{MeV}$ at $k_F \sim 0.8 \text{fm}^{-1}$ in BCS and 2.3--$2.4 \text{MeV}$ at $k_F \sim 0.6 \text{fm}^{-1}$ in correlated matter. At higher densities the Quantum Monte Carlo gap becomes close to BCS. In general, the computed medium polarization effects are much smaller than those previously estimated within \emph{all theories}. Truncations of Argonne $v_{8^\prime}$ to simpler forms give the same gaps in BCS, provided the truncated potentials have been refitted to the same NN data set. Differences among the models appear in the correlated theories, most of the reduction being attributable to the tensor force. The three--nucleon interaction provides an additional increase of the gap of about 0.35 MeV.

## Key claim

The medium polarization effects on the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in neutron matter are much smaller than previously estimated within all theories, with the maximum gap reduced only slightly from the standard BCS value of about 2.5 MeV to 2.3–2.4 MeV in correlated matter, and the Quantum Monte Carlo gap becoming close to BCS at higher densities.

## Method

The authors employed Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) and Correlated Basis Function (CBF) theories to compute the $^1S_0$ superfluid pairing gap in neutron matter at zero temperature. They used the Argonne $v_{8^\prime}$ nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, with the AFDMC simulations performed for 12–18 neutrons in a periodic box and the CBF calculations using a cluster expansion of the correlated BCS wave function.

## Key numbers

- Maximum BCS gap: about 2.5 MeV at Fermi momentum $k_F \sim 0.8 \text{ fm}^{-1}$
- Maximum correlated gap: 2.3–2.4 MeV at $k_F \sim 0.6 \text{ fm}^{-1}$
- AFDMC gap with Argonne $v_{8^\prime}$ at $k_F = 0.6 \text{ fm}^{-1}$: 2.457(76) MeV (average from N=13, 15, 17)
- AFDMC gap with Argonne $v_{8^\prime}$ plus Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction at $k_F = 0.6 \text{ fm}^{-1}$: 2.810(146) MeV
- Three-nucleon interaction provides an additional increase of the gap of about 0.35 MeV
- Normal phase energy per particle at $k_F = 0.6 \text{ fm}^{-1}$ for N=14: 2.548(3) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous BCS calculations of neutron matter pairing by incorporating realistic nuclear potentials and modern many-body methods that fully account for medium modifications due to interaction-induced correlations. The results contradict earlier estimates by Martino (Review of Modern Physics) which predicted gap suppressions of a factor of two or more, and differ from the Bruckner G-matrix approach of Zuo (2002) which found a small decrease of the gap from three-nucleon interactions. The findings enable more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling rates and post-glitch relaxation times, and the authors suggest extending these methods to $^3P_2$–$^3F_2$ neutron pairing and $^1S_0$ proton pairing in highly asymmetrical nuclear matter.
