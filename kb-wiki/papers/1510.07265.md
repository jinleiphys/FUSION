# Strange meson-baryon interaction in hot and dense medium: recent progress for a road to GSI/FAIR

> We report recent results on the dynamics of strange hadrons in two-body reactions relevant for near-threshold production in heavy-ion collisions at GSI/FAIR and NICA-Dubna. In particular, $\bar K N$ scattering in hot and dense nuclear matter is studied within a chiral unitary framework in coupled channels, setting up the starting point for implementations in microscopic off-shell transport approaches. We focus on the calculation of transition rates with special attention to the excitation of hyperon resonances and isospin effects. Additionally, we explore "unconventional" strangeness generation by meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions in connection with recent HADES observations of deep sub-threshold $ϕ$ and $Ξ$ production.

# Strange meson-baryon interaction in hot and dense medium: recent progress for a road to GSI/FAIR

## Key claim

The chiral unitary framework for $\bar K N$ scattering in coupled channels, extended to finite density and temperature with both $s$- and $p$-wave contributions, provides full off-shell in-medium scattering amplitudes that can serve as input for microscopic off-shell transport approaches; however, the cross sections for $\eta N \to \phi N$ remain below the mbarn scale despite the presence of resonant states above 2 GeV, limiting the role of this channel in explaining deep sub-threshold $\phi$ production observed by HADES.

## Method

The authors study $\bar K N$ scattering in hot and dense nuclear matter within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach based on the SU(3) meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian, implementing unitarization of scattering amplitudes in both $s$ and $p$ waves at finite density and temperature. Transition probabilities are defined fully off-shell as the angular integrated modulus-squared scattering amplitude (including all partial waves $L=0,1$), averaged over total angular momentum ($J=1/2, 3/2$). For $\phi$ production, the authors employ an extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to couple pseudoscalar and vector mesons with baryons from the $1/2^+$ octet and $3/2^+$ decuplet.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar K$ potential at nuclear matter density is shallow attractive at 40-60 MeV, with a broad spectral function.
- For $\eta N \to \phi N$ (total isospin $I=1/2$), the cross section remains below the mbarn scale.
- For $K^*Y \to \phi N$ reactions (with $Y=\Lambda,\Sigma,\Sigma^*$), cross sections are in the range of 10-100 mbarn close to threshold energies.
- For $\rho N(\Delta)\to\phi N$ reactions without net strangeness, cross sections are typically below 5 mbarn.
- The HADES Collaboration observed deep sub-threshold $\phi$ and $\Xi$ production in 1.23 AGeV Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent coupled-channel studies of $\bar K$ meson interactions in nuclear matter by Tolos et al. (2006, 2008), which described the $\bar K$ spectral function and nuclear optical potential, by now implementing full off-shell in-medium scattering amplitudes in both $s$ and $p$ waves. The results are complementary to the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model analysis by Cassing et al. (2003) and are intended for implementation in transport approaches such as PHSD and IQMD. The study of $\phi$ and $\Xi$ production addresses recent HADES observations that challenge existing transport simulations, with the $\eta \pi \to \phi \pi$ reaction found to be suppressed due to $C$-parity and $G$-parity mismatch, while $K^*Y \to \phi N$ reactions show promise for explaining $\phi$ production at SPS energies and beyond.
