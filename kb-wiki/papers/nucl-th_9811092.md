# $J/ψ$-Nucleon Scattering Length and In-medium Mass Shift of $J/ψ$ in QCD Sum Rule Analysis

> We calculate the spin-averaged $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering length $a_{J/ψ}$ by directly applying QCD sum rule to $J/ψ$-$N$ forward scattering amplitude. Our result $a_{J/ψ} = - 0.10 \pm 0.02$ fm, predicts the possibility of bound states with nuclei, though the force is weaker than that of the light vector mesons ($ρ,ω,ϕ$)-$N$ cases. Up to dimension-4 gluonic operators, we evaluate the scattering length with twist-2 contribution, which increases the absolute value of the scattering length about 30%. If we apply $a_{J/ψ}$ to the effective mass of $J/ψ$ in nuclear matter on the basis of the linear density approximation, it shows very slight decrease ($4 \sim 7$ MeV) at normal matter density.

# J/ψ-Nucleon Scattering Length and In-medium Mass Shift of J/ψ in QCD Sum Rule Analysis

## Key claim

The J/ψ-nucleon scattering length is negative (attractive) at about -0.10 ± 0.02 fm, suggesting that J/ψ could form bound states with nuclei, but the force is weaker than that of light vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) with nucleons. The result depends on including twist-2 gluonic operator contributions up to dimension-4, which increase the absolute value of the scattering length by about 30% compared to the scalar-only calculation.

## Method

The author applies QCD sum rules (moment sum rule method) directly to the forward J/ψ-nucleon scattering amplitude, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-4 gluonic operators including both scalar and twist-2 contributions. The Wilson coefficients for twist-2 operators are calculated explicitly with finite charmed quark mass using the fixed-point gauge method. The scattering length is extracted by fitting the OPE side to a phenomenological spectral function parametrized with a double pole term (related to the scattering length), a single pole term, and a continuum term, with a constraint from the low-energy theorem.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ-nucleon scattering length: a_J/ψ = -0.10 ± 0.02 fm (spin-averaged, including twist-2 contribution)
- Scalar-only contribution gives a_J/ψ ≈ -0.07 fm (average across ξ values from 0.0 to 3.0)
- Including twist-2 contribution gives a_J/ψ ≈ -0.09 fm (average across ξ values from 0.0 to 3.0)
- Mass shift of J/ψ in nuclear matter at normal density (ρ_N = 0.17 fm⁻³): δm_J/ψ = -4 to -7 MeV (about 0.1 to 0.2% of the J/ψ mass)
- Total cross section at threshold: σ_J/ψ = 4π a_J/ψ² ≈ 1.26 mb
- Comparison: nuclear absorption cross section from experimental data is σ_nab = 7.3 mb, with elastic channel contributing about 20%
- Comparison with light vector meson-nucleon scattering lengths: a_ρ ≈ -0.47 fm, a_ω ≈ -0.41 fm, a_φ ≈ -0.15 fm
- Nucleon matrix element of scalar gluon operator: ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩_N = -(1.222 ± 0.282) GeV²
- Nucleon matrix element of twist-2 gluon operator: ⟨(α_s/π) ST(G⁰_σ G⁰_σ)⟩_N = -(0.094 ± 0.010) GeV²
- J/ψ coupling from leptonic decay: f_J/ψ² = 1.7 × 10⁻²

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule formalism developed by Koike and Hayashigaki (1997) for light vector mesons to the heavy quarkonium system J/ψ, where the interaction is purely gluonic without quark exchange to first order. The results are smaller in magnitude than those obtained by Brodsky et al. and de Teramond et al. using on-shell QCD sum rule approaches at q²=0. The author notes that Klingl et al. reported a larger mass shift of about -20 MeV (0.6%) for J/ψ in nuclear matter, with a derivation pattern similar to the light vector meson cases of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Koike and Hayashigaki (1997). The work enables future experimental searches for J/ψ-nucleus bound states and provides a baseline for separating nuclear absorption effects from quark-gluon plasma signals in heavy-ion collisions.
