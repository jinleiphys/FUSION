# Baryonic mixing and production of hypernuclei

> We address mixing of different baryonic states in hypernuclei and formation of strange nuclei of various kinds. First, production of neutron-rich Lambda hypernuclei by the (pi-,K+) reaction is considered, and one-step production via baryonic admixtures in the final as well as in the initial state is discussed. Then, the Lambda Lambda - Xi N mixing in p shell Lambda Lambda hypernuclei exemplified by $^{12}_{ΛΛ}$Be is studied. The last topic is devoted to production of Theta pentaquark nuclei (pentanuclei). We suggest a type of reactions with recoilless Theta production.

# Baryonic mixing and production of hypernuclei

## Key claim
The paper suggests that baryonic mixing mechanisms (including Δ-admixtures in initial nuclei and ΛΛ-ΞN coupling) can contribute to hypernucleus production, but the quantitative agreement with experimental data remains relatively poor due to the complicated nature of the processes and many uncertainties in input information.

## Method
The author calculates cross sections for neutron-rich Λ hypernucleus production via the (π⁻,K⁺) reaction using two-step meson charge-exchange mechanisms and one-step production through Σ⁻ doorway states from Λn-Σ⁻p coupling, then extends the analysis to a new mechanism involving Δ⁺⁺ admixtures in the initial nuclear state. For double-Λ hypernuclei, a coupled-channel model treating ΛΛ and ΞN channels with phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials and Nijmegen meson-exchange interactions is applied to ¹²_ΛΛBe. For pentaquark (Θ) nuclei, the author proposes reactions on nucleon pairs (γNN→ΘY, πNN→ΘY, KNN→ΘN) to achieve recoilless kinematics.

## Key numbers
- Cross sections for the (π⁻,K⁺) reaction are typically smaller by about three orders of magnitude than those of the usual (π⁺,K⁺) reaction.
- For ¹⁰B(π⁻,K⁺)¹⁰_ΛLi at p_π=1.2 GeV/c: theory gives 22 nb/sr, experiment gives 12±2 nb/sr.
- For ¹²C(π⁻,K⁺)¹²_ΛBe at p_π=1.2 GeV/c: theory gives 2.5 nb/sr, experiment gives 7 nb/sr.
- The Δ⁺⁺ admixture probability in ¹²C is taken as N_Δ⁺⁺=0.4%, giving an upper bound estimate of dσ/dΩ∼10-15 nb/sr for ¹²C(π⁻,K⁺)¹²_ΛBe.
- For ¹²_ΛΛBe, ΔB_ΛΛ values for the ground (1s_Λ²) state differ by about 2 MeV between the weakest (NHC-D) and strongest (NHC-F) coupling models, reaching almost 4 MeV for NHC-F.
- For the 1p_Λ²(L=0) state in ¹²_ΛΛBe, ΔB_ΛΛ reaches about 6 MeV for the strongest coupling.
- Ξ admixture probabilities in ¹²_ΛΛBe are not substantially greater than 1% for the ground state.
- Minimal momentum transfer for Θ production from a single nucleon is 635 MeV/c in the (K,π) reaction at 900 MeV/c.
- Estimated summed cross section for ¹²C(γ,Λ)¹¹_ΘC: 29 nb/sr at E_γ=1.2 GeV, 10 nb/sr at 1.6 GeV, and 2 nb/sr at 2.0 GeV (assuming Θ width of 5 MeV).

## Context
This work extends studies of neutron-rich Λ hypernuclei production observed in the KEK experiment (Saha, 2004) and previous theoretical work by Lanskoy and Tretyakova on two-step mechanisms. The ΛΛ-ΞN mixing analysis follows the coupled-channel model previously applied to s-shell hypernuclei ⁵_ΛΛH and ⁵_ΛΛHe by Lanskoy and Yamamoto. The pentaquark production proposal builds on theoretical studies of Θ-nucleus interaction (various groups, 2003-2004) and the experimental discovery of the Θ⁺ pentaquark (Nakano, 2003). The recoilless production mechanism using nucleon pairs is analogous to the well-established (K⁻,π⁻) reaction for hypernuclei production (Boyer, 1970s). The work enables future experimental searches for pentanuclei at facilities such as TJNAF (g10 experiment studying γd→ΛΘ).
