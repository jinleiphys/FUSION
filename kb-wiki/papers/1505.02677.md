# Event-by-event fluctuations in perturbative QCD + saturation + hydro model: pinning down QCD matter shear viscosity in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We introduce an event-by-event perturbative-QCD + saturation + hydro ("EKRT") framework for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where we compute the produced fluctuating QCD-matter energy densities from next-to-leading order perturbative QCD using a saturation conjecture to control soft particle production, and describe the space-time evolution of the QCD matter with dissipative fluid dynamics, event by event. We perform a simultaneous comparison of the centrality dependence of hadronic multiplicities, transverse momentum spectra, and flow coefficients of the azimuth-angle asymmetries, against the LHC and RHIC measurements. We compare also the computed event-by-event probability distributions of relative fluctuations of elliptic flow, and event-plane angle correlations, with the experimental data from Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. We show how such a systematic multi-energy and multi-observable analysis tests the initial state calculation and the applicability region of hydrodynamics, and in particular how it constrains the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio of QCD matter in its different phases in a remarkably consistent manner.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an event-by-event framework combining next-to-leading order perturbative QCD, a saturation conjecture for soft particle production, and dissipative fluid dynamics (the "EKRT" model) to simultaneously compare multiple observables against LHC and RHIC data, showing that the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s(T)\) can be constrained in a remarkably consistent manner, though the authors note they are not yet capable of performing a full statistical global analysis to extract \(\eta/s(T)\) and its uncertainty limits.

## Method

The authors compute fluctuating QCD-matter energy densities event-by-event from next-to-leading order perturbative QCD using a saturation conjecture to control soft particle production, obtaining the saturation momentum \(p_{\rm sat}\) locally in each transverse location from the product of nuclear thickness functions of the colliding nuclei. They describe the space-time evolution of the QCD matter with 2+1 dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant dissipative Israel-Stewart type transient fluid dynamics, using the \(s95p\)-PCE-v1 parametrization of lattice QCD results at zero net-baryon density and the Cooper-Frye procedure with a constant-temperature freeze-out surface at \(T_{\rm dec}=100\) MeV to compute hadron spectra.

## Key numbers

- \(\eta/s(T) = param1\): minimum at \(T_{\rm min}=150\) MeV with \((\eta/s)_{\rm min}=0.12\), rising to \(\eta/s(500\,{\rm MeV})=0.65\) in the QGP and to \(\eta/s(100\,{\rm MeV})=0.24\) in the hadron gas phase.
- \(\eta/s(T) = param2\): minimum at \(T_{\rm min}=180\) MeV with \((\eta/s)_{\rm min}=0.16\), \(\eta/s(500\,{\rm MeV})=0.16\) and \(\eta/s(100\,{\rm MeV})=0.36\).
- \(\eta/s(T) = param4\): minimum at \(T_{\rm min}=180\) MeV with \((\eta/s)_{\rm min}=0.12\), \(\eta/s(500\,{\rm MeV})=0.30\) and \(\eta/s(100\,{\rm MeV})=0.76\).
- Constant \(\eta/s=0.2\) also gives equally good overall agreement with data.
- Initial proper time for fluid dynamical simulation: \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm.
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_{\rm dec}=100\) MeV.
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: \(T_{\mathrm{chem}}=175\) MeV.
- Nuclear radius parametrization: \(R_A = (1.12A^{1/3} - 0.86A^{-1/3})\) fm, with diffuseness \(d=0.54\) fm and central density \(n_0\approx 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO-improved pQCD + saturation model (EKRT) from earlier studies by Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, and Tuominen (1999) and Paatelainen, Eskola, and Niemi (2012, 2013) by introducing event-by-event fluctuations and comparing with higher Fourier flow coefficients and event-plane angle correlations. It contrasts with the IP-Glasma initial conditions approach (Schenke, 2012) which uses a constant \(\eta/s=0.12\) at RHIC and 0.2 at the LHC, and with the MC-KLN model (Hirano, 2005; Song, 2013) which gives \(\eta/s=0.16\) in the QGP; the present work enables simultaneous multi-energy and multi-observable constraints on the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) across both QGP and hadron gas phases.
