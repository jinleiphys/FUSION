# Kinetic freeze out

> Freeze out of particles across a space-time hypersurface is discussed in kinetic models. The calculation of final momentum distribution of emitted particles is described for freeze out surfaces, with spacelike normals. The resulting non-equilibrium distribution does not resemble, the previously proposed, cut Juttner distribution, and shows non-exponential p_t-spectra similar to the ones observed in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the commonly assumed "cut Jüttner distribution" for particles emitted from a freeze-out hypersurface with a spacelike normal is not a realistic choice; instead, kinetic models with rescattering or volume emission produce a non-equilibrium post-freeze-out distribution that does not tend to the cut Jüttner form even at infinite freeze-out layer thickness, and yields non-exponential transverse momentum spectra similar to experimental observations.

## Method

The authors construct a stationary one-dimensional kinetic model of a freeze-out layer, where the interacting component of the momentum distribution evolves according to coupled differential equations with a drain term (particles leaving the system) and, in the improved model, a relaxation-time approximation for rescattering and re-thermalization. They also develop a volume emission model based on an escape probability derived from a Poisson distribution for collision-free propagation. Conservation laws for baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and entropy are enforced across the freeze-out front, and the parameters of the re-thermalized Jüttner distribution (temperature, flow velocity, density) are updated stepwise using covariant expressions for the changes due to particle drain.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of the interacting component: \(T_0 = 130\) MeV, chosen to fit the situation in refs. Csernai and Mishustin (1994) and Csernai and Mishustin (1995).
- In the immediate re-thermalization limit (\(\lambda' \ll \lambda\)), the interacting component approaches a spherical Jüttner distribution at each position \(x\).
- For the massless, baryon-free gas, the change of Eckart flow velocity and Landau flow velocity in the rest frame of the freeze-out front (RFG) are related by \(du^x_{i,E} = \frac{4}{3} du^x_{i,L}\).
- The freeze-out distribution \(f_{free}(x,\vec{p})\) is shown at positions \(x = 0.2\lambda\), \(3\lambda\), and \(100\lambda\) for initial flow velocities \(u_{RFG}^\mu|_{x=0} = (1,0,0,0)\) and \(u_{RFG}^\mu|_{x=0} = \gamma(1,0.5,0,0)\).
- The local transverse momentum (\(p_x\)) distribution at \(x = 100\lambda\), \(p_y = 0\), and \(T_0 = 130\) MeV shows a curved (non-exponential) spectrum, with the apparent slope parameter increasing with transverse momentum.

## Context

This work extends the earlier kinetic freeze-out model of Anderlik et al. (1998), which used only a drain term and produced incomplete freeze-out and a cut Jüttner distribution at infinite layer thickness. The present paper solves both problems by introducing rescattering (relaxation-time approximation) and a volume emission model with escape probability. The results contradict the cut Jüttner ansatz used in earlier surface freeze-out models by Bugaev (1996) and Anderlik et al. (1998), and the non-exponential transverse momentum spectra resemble experimental pion spectra from the NA44 and NA49 collaborations at SPS. The approach enables more realistic evaluation of freeze-out distributions for fluid dynamical models of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems involving quark-gluon plasma with rapid hadronization as in Csernai and Mishustin (1994, 1995).
