# First extraction of the proton mass radius and scattering length $\left|α_{ρ^0 p}\right| $ from $ρ^0$ photoproduction

> As it involves the lightest physical states excited from the vacuum by the vector quark current, near-threshold $ρ^0$ photoproduction is considered a possible way to research the proton radius and the absolute value of the scattering lengths of the $ρ^0$--proton interaction. In this work, under the assumption of a scalar form factor of dipole form, the value of the proton mass radius is calculated as $0.85\pm 0.06 \text{ fm }$ by fitting the differential cross section of the $γp \rightarrow ρ^0 p$ reaction at near-threshold energy. For light vector meson photoproduction, because the exchange of a scalar quark--antiquark pair is not suppressed and should dominate the scalar gluon exchange, the radius we extract from $ρ^0$ photoproduction is likely to represent the quark radius of the proton. This fact may explain why the value obtained in this work is very near the proton charge radius. Moreover, the absolute value of the $ρ^0$--proton scattering length $|α_{ρ^0 p}|= 0.31 \pm 0.06 \text{ fm}$ is obtained for the first time within the vector meson dominance model. This result disobeys the rule that the absolute value of the vector meson and proton scattering length $|α_{V p}|$ increases with the meson's mass, which can be attributed to treating the $ρ^0$ meson as a point in the analysis. These results provide useful theoretical information for an in-depth understanding of proton structure and the proton--vector meson interaction.

# First extraction of the proton mass radius and scattering length |α_ρ⁰p| from ρ⁰ photoproduction

## Key claim

Under the assumption of a scalar form factor of dipole form, the proton mass radius extracted from near-threshold ρ⁰ photoproduction is 0.85 ± 0.06 fm, which is argued to represent the quark radius of the proton rather than the gluonic mass radius, because scalar quark–antiquark exchange dominates over scalar gluon exchange for light vector mesons. The extracted scattering length |α_ρ⁰p| = 0.31 ± 0.06 fm disobeys the previously observed trend that |α_Vp| increases with vector meson mass, which the authors attribute to treating the ρ⁰ meson as a point particle in the analysis.

## Method

The differential cross section of the γp → ρ⁰p reaction was fitted using the SAPHIR experimental data at six center-of-mass energies (W = 1.92, 2.02, 2.11, 2.20, 2.28, and 2.36 GeV) in the incident photon energy range E_γ = [1.4 GeV, 2.6 GeV]. The amplitude was expressed in terms of scalar gravitational form factors (GFFs) parameterized in dipole form, with free parameters c₂ (short-distance coefficient) and m_s (dipole mass parameter) determined by the fit. The scattering length was extracted using the vector meson dominance (VMD) model, connecting the near-threshold photoproduction cross section to the elastic ρ⁰p scattering length, using both differential and total cross section data.

## Key numbers

* Proton mass radius: 0.85 ± 0.06 fm, extracted from fitting differential cross section data of γp → ρ⁰p at near-threshold energies (W = 1.92–2.36 GeV)
* Short-distance coefficient c₂ = 1.36 fm² from the ρ⁰ fit (compared to c₂ = 0.207 fm² from J/ψ GlueX data)
* Absolute value of ρ⁰–proton scattering length: |α_ρ⁰p| = 0.31 ± 0.06 fm, obtained by combining differential and total cross section extractions
* From differential cross section alone: |α_ρ⁰p| = 0.32 ± 0.03 fm
* From total cross section alone (SAPHIR data, W ∈ [1.72 GeV, 2.00 GeV]): |α_ρ⁰p| = 0.29 ± 0.02 fm
* VMD coupling constant g_ρ⁰ = 2.47, derived from leptonic decay width Γ_ρ⁰_e⁺e⁻ = 7.04 keV
* Quark mass used: m_q = m_u = m_d = 0.33 GeV, giving r_q\bar{q} ≈ 1/(2m_q) and π r_q\bar{q}² = 0.28 fm²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev (2021) for extracting the proton mass radius from J/ψ photoproduction (which gave 0.55 ± 0.03 fm) to the lighter ρ⁰ meson, where the |t|_min effect is much smaller. The authors compare their result with previous extractions from ω photoproduction (0.68 ± 0.03 fm) and φ photoproduction (0.67 ± 0.10 fm) by Wang (2021). The scattering length extraction follows the VMD methodology applied by Strakovsky (2014, 2020, 2021) for ω, φ, J/ψ, ψ(2S), and Υ scattering lengths, and the result is compared with a QCD sum rule prediction of 0.47 ± 0.05 fm by Koike (1996). The work enables future high-precision measurements at JLab to refine both the mass radius and scattering length determinations.
