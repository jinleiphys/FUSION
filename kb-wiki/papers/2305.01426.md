# On-Shell Calculation of Mixed Electromagnetic and Gravitational Scattering

> The study of long-range effects arising from the higher order exchange of massless particles via summation of Feynman diagrams is well known, but recently it has been shown that the use of on-shell methods can provide a streamlined route to the calculation of both electromagnetic and gravitational effects. In this note we demonstrate that the use of on-shell methods yields a similar simplification in the evaluation of higher order effects in the case of mixed electromagnetic and gravitational scattering.

## Key claim

On-shell methods can simplify the evaluation of higher-order mixed electromagnetic and gravitational scattering of two spinless charged particles, reducing the calculation from twenty Feynman diagrams (each requiring four-dimensional integration) to only two separate contributions (each requiring only two-dimensional solid angle integration). The paper demonstrates that this streamlined approach reproduces previous results obtained via more tedious Feynman diagram methods.

## Method

The author applies on-shell unitarity methods to compute the t-channel discontinuity of the mixed electromagnetic-gravitational scattering amplitude for two spinless charged scalars. The calculation uses physical on-shell amplitudes for graviton photoproduction (obtained via the double copy theorem from Compton scattering) and for two-photon exchange involving a graviton pole diagram, then performs polarization sums and solid angle averages to extract the leading nonanalytic terms in momentum transfer. The resulting amplitude is subtracted of the second-order Born iteration to obtain the long-range effective potential.

## Context

This work extends the on-shell calculation methods previously applied to purely electromagnetic scattering (Feinberg and Sucher, 1988) and purely gravitational scattering (Holstein, 2017; Bjerrum-Bohr, 2014) to the mixed electromagnetic-gravitational case. The results agree with earlier Feynman diagram calculations by Holstein (2008), Bjerrum-Bohr (2002), and others, but demonstrate significant simplification: the on-shell approach requires only two contributions instead of twenty diagrams, avoids ghost terms, and uses the double copy theorem to relate graviton amplitudes to simpler electromagnetic counterparts. This enables more efficient computation of long-range corrections to scattering processes involving both electromagnetic and gravitational interactions.
