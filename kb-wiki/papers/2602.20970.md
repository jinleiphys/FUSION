# Spatial confinement-deconfinement transition in accelerated gluodynamics within lattice simulation

> In this work we investigate the influence of weak acceleration on the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in gluodynamics. Our study is carried out within lattice simulation in the comoving reference frame of accelerated observer which is parameterized by the Rindler coordinates. We find that finite temperature confinement-deconfinement phase transition turns into spatial crossover in the Rindler spacetime. In other words, spatially separated confinement and deconfinement phases can coexist in the Rindler spacetime within certain intervals of temperature and acceleration. We determine the position of the boundary between the phases as a function of temperature for several accelerations and find that it can be described by the Tolman-Ehrenfest law with rather good accuracy although a minor deviation takes place. Moreover, the critical temperature of the system in the weak acceleration regime is found to remain unchanged as that of the standard homogeneous gluodynamics. Our results imply that the spatial confinement-deconfinement transition might take place in the vicinity of the Schwarzschild black hole horizon.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature confinement-deconfinement phase transition in gluodynamics becomes a spatial crossover in Rindler spacetime, meaning that spatially separated confinement and deconfinement phases can coexist for certain intervals of temperature and acceleration. This conclusion is drawn for weak accelerations (α ≪ Λ, where Λ ∼ 200 MeV is the characteristic gluodynamics scale), and the critical temperature of the system remains unchanged from standard homogeneous gluodynamics.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(3) gluodynamics directly in the Rindler coordinates that parameterize the comoving reference frame of a uniformly accelerated observer, using a tree-level improved Symanzik action modified by metric factors (1+αz) multiplying chromoelectric and chromomagnetic plaquettes. Simulations were carried out on lattices with temporal extent Nt = 4, 5, 6, 8 and spatial sizes up to Ns = 60 and Nz = 181, using a heatbath algorithm with overrelaxation updates and accumulating 100,000 to 400,000 sweeps per parameter set. The acceleration values studied ranged from α = 1 to 18 MeV, all satisfying the weak-acceleration condition α ≪ Λ.

## Key numbers

- Accelerations used in simulations: α = 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18 MeV, all satisfying α ≪ Λ where Λ ∼ 200 MeV.
- Base lattice size: Nt × Ns² × Nz = 5 × 40² × 121; additional temporal extents Nt = 4, 6, 8 with Ns/Nt = 8 and (Nz−1)/Nt = 24.
- The Unruh temperature for the largest acceleration studied is TU = α/2π ≈ 2.9 MeV, much smaller than any temperature studied in the paper.
- The horizon in Rindler spacetime is located at zh = −1/α, and the lattice volume is adjusted to remain distant from this horizon.

## Context

This work extends the study of confinement-deconfinement transitions in non-inertial frames, following prior lattice investigations of rotating gluodynamics by Braguta et al. (2023, 2024) which discovered a mixed inhomogeneous phase with spatially separated confinement and deconfinement. The approach differs from Chernodub (2024), who used the Tolman-Ehrenfest law to model acceleration as a temperature gradient; the present work shows that for gluodynamics the two methods are not fully equivalent, differing by corrections of order α/Λ or α²/Λ² depending on the observable. The results suggest that a spatial confinement-deconfinement transition might occur near Schwarzschild black hole horizons, where the Rindler metric approximates the near-horizon geometry.
