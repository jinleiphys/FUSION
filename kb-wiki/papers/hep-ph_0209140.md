# Extracting the Pomeron-Pomeron Cross Section from Diffractive Mass Spectra

> We have calculated the mass distribution ($dσ/dM_X$) as observed by UA8 Collaboration in the inclusive reaction $p \bar p \to p X \bar p$ at $\sqrt s = 630 GeV$, using the Interacting Gluon Model (IGM) with Double Pomeron Exchange included. The only new parameter is the $IP IP$ cross section, which we can extract from fitting experimental data. We compare our results with the values obtained in the UA8 study. Assuming a constant Pomeron-Pomeron total cross section ($σ_{IP IP} = 1 mb$), we make predictions for $dσ/dM_X$ at Tevatron and LHC energies.

# Extracting the Pomeron-Pomeron Cross Section from Diffractive Mass Spectra

## Key claim

The authors claim that the low-mass excess in the UA8 diffractive mass spectra can be explained with a constant (M_X-independent) Pomeron-Pomeron total cross section of approximately 0.5 mb, contrary to the UA8 analysis which found that a constant cross section could not fit the data and required either an M_X-dependent cross section or invoked glueball formation. The authors hedge that this conclusion depends on the specific choice of Pomeron gluon distribution and that one can "trade the hardness of the Pomeron with its interaction cross section."

## Method

The authors used the Interacting Gluon Model (IGM), which treats nucleon-nucleon collisions as an incoherent sum of multiple gluon-gluon collisions, to calculate the double Pomeron exchange (DPE) mass distribution dσ/dM_X for the reaction p p̅ → p X p̅ at √s = 630 GeV. They introduced the Pomeron-Pomeron cross section σ_{IP IP} as the only new parameter, fitting it to the UA8 data using a gluon distribution G_{IP}(y) = a (1-y)^5 / y with momentum sum rule parameter p_d = 0.05, and compared results using hard and superhard Pomeron gluon distributions from HERA analyses.

## Key numbers

- σ_{IP IP} ≈ 0.5 mb is favored by the UA8 data when using the gluon distribution G_{IP}(y) = a (1-y)^5 / y with p_d = 0.05
- σ_{IP IP} < 1.0 mb is the upper bound extracted from the analysis
- The calculation was performed at √s = 630 GeV (UA8 energy), with predictions made for √s = 2 TeV (Tevatron) and √s = 14 TeV (LHC)
- The momentum sum rule parameter for gluons in the Pomeron is p_d = 0.05 (compared to p = 0.5 for all gluons in the proton)
- The soft gluon correlation length from lattice QCD is cited as 0.3 fm, used to justify treating soft gluon fields as uncorrelated

## Context

This work extends the Interacting Gluon Model, previously applied to single diffractive dissociation (Durães et al., 1997; 1998), to double Pomeron exchange processes. It directly confronts the UA8 analysis (Brandt et al., 1992) which concluded that a constant σ_{IP IP} could not fit the low-mass excess and suggested glueball formation as a possible explanation. The authors also compare their Pomeron gluon distributions with those used by H1 and ZEUS collaborations at HERA, finding that the "soft" Pomeron distribution (6 (1-β)^5 / β) is incompatible with HERA single diffractive mass spectra (H1, 1997). The results enable predictions for DPE mass spectra at Tevatron and LHC energies and suggest that the ratio of normalized DPE to single diffractive mass distributions grows with M_X.
