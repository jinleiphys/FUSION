# Charge exchange operators sum rules and proton-neutron T=0 and T=1 pairing interactions

> We study sum rules for charge exchange operators of the Gamow-Teller and Fermi type and explore what can be learned from them about proton-neutron pairing in T=0 and T=1 channels. We consider both energy weighted (EWSR) and non-energy weighted (NEWSR) sum rules. We use a schematic Hamiltonian to stress the distinctive roles of T=1 and T=0 pair interactions and correlations in the sum rules. Numerical results for 44Ti are presented for both schematic and realistic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the non-energy-weighted sum rules (NEWSR) and energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR) for Gamow-Teller and Fermi charge-exchange operators are sensitive to proton-neutron pairing in the T=0 and T=1 channels, but the Ikeda sum rule (B⁺_GT − B⁻_GT = 3(N−Z)) is exactly independent of such pairing correlations. The authors use a schematic Hamiltonian to demonstrate that increasing the number of T=0 or T=1 proton-neutron pairs reduces the individual β⁻ and β⁺ Gamow-Teller strengths, while the EWSR for Gamow-Teller transitions depends on the difference between T=0 and T=1 pairing strengths and on the numbers of like-nucleon pairs.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the non-energy-weighted and energy-weighted sum rules for Gamow-Teller and Fermi charge-exchange operators, using a schematic Hamiltonian with isoscalar pairing interactions in the T=0 and T=1 channels. They evaluate the two-body correlation terms in the sum rules for a simple model of independent T=0 and T=1 proton-neutron pairs. Numerical calculations are performed for the N=Z nucleus ⁴⁴Ti within a single j = f₇/₂ shell, using several schematic pairing Hamiltonians: isopairing (T=0 pairing), standard J=0, T=1 pairing, T=0, J=1 pairing, and a degenerate case.

- For ⁴⁴Ti in the single f₇/₂ shell with the isopairing Hamiltonian (Case I), the total Gamow-Teller β⁻ strength B⁺_GT averaged over the three T=0 initial 0⁺ states is 2.56.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Gamow-Teller sum rules by Ikeda (1963) and Cha (1987), who noted sensitivity of β⁺ strength to the particle-particle channel, and by Engel et al. (1997) who gave analytic expressions in SU(4) and SO(5) limits. The paper also relates to debates on the existence of T=0 proton-neutron pairing in N≈Z nuclei, as discussed by Goodman (1979) and Machleidt (2001). The results enable future work to use measured Gamow-Teller strengths from charge-exchange reactions to extract information about the numbers of T=0 and T=1 proton-neutron pairs in nuclear ground states, and to test whether T=0 pairing is present in nuclei near the N=Z line.
