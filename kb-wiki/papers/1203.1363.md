# Odd systems in deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> In order to describe the exotic nuclear structure in unstable odd-$A$ or odd-odd nuclei, the deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in continuum has been extended to incorporate the blocking effect due to the odd nucleon. For a microscopic and self-consistent description of pairing correlations, continuum, deformation, blocking effects, and the extended spatial density distribution in exotic nuclei, the deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov equations are solved in a Woods-Saxon basis in which the radial wave functions have a proper asymptotic behavior at large $r$. The formalism and numerical details are provided. The code is checked by comparing the results with those of spherical relativistic continuum Hartree Bogoliubov theory in the nucleus $^{19}$O. The prolate deformed nucleus $^{15}$C is studied by examining the neutron levels and density distributions.

# Odd systems in deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theory in continuum

## Key claim
The deformed relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory in continuum, previously developed for even-even nuclei, is extended to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei by incorporating the blocking effect of an odd nucleon. The equal filling approximation is used to average over the two time-reversal configurations, thereby neglecting currents and preserving time reversal symmetry in each iteration step.

## Method
The deformed RHB equations are solved in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis, which provides proper asymptotic behavior of radial wave functions at large distances. For odd systems, the blocking effect is implemented by exchanging the creator and annihilator of the quasi-particle state with the lowest quasi-particle energy, and the density and abnormal density matrices are averaged and symmetrized between the +Ω and −Ω subspaces using the equal filling approximation. The code is validated by comparing results for the spherical nucleus ¹⁹O with those from spherical relativistic continuum Hartree Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, and then applied to the prolate deformed nucleus ¹⁵C.

## Key numbers
- Total binding energy of ¹⁹O: −144.80 MeV from deformed RHB versus −144.78 MeV from spherical RCHB, a difference of about 0.02 MeV (0.01% accuracy).
- Neutron root mean square radius of ¹⁹O: 2.83 fm from both deformed RHB and RCHB calculations.
- Proton root mean square radius of ¹⁹O: 2.57 fm from both calculations.
- Total root mean square radius of ¹⁹O: 2.72 fm from both calculations.
- Pairing energy of ¹⁹O: −3.82 MeV from both deformed RHB and RCHB.
- Deformation parameter β = 0.25 predicted for ¹⁵C.
- Neutron root mean square radius of ¹⁴C: 2.56 fm.
- Neutron root mean square radius of ¹⁵C: 2.79 fm.
- The valence level in ¹⁵C with Ω^π = 1/2⁺ has occupation probability v² = 0.5 and is a mixture of spherical orbits 1d₅/₂ (62%) and 2s₁/₂ (36%).

## Context
This work extends the deformed RHB theory in continuum previously developed for even-even nuclei by Zhou et al. (2010) and Li et al. (2012) to odd-A and odd-odd systems, enabling microscopic and self-consistent description of pairing correlations, continuum, deformation, blocking effects, and extended spatial density distributions in exotic nuclei. The equal filling approximation follows the approach used by Schunck (2010) and Perez-Martin (2008). The method enables future studies of halo phenomena in deformed odd nuclei near the neutron drip line, building on earlier predictions of shape decoupling between core and halo in deformed even-even nuclei such as ⁴²,⁴⁴Mg by Zhou et al. (2010) and Li et al. (2012).
