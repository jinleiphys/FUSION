# A periodic table for the excited Nucleon and Delta spectrum in a relativistic chiral quark model

> A possibility of the construction of a periodic table for the excited baryon spectrum is shown in the frame of a relativistic chiral quark model based on selection rules derived from the one-pion exchange mechanism. It is shown that all the $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances appearing in the $πN$ scattering data and strongly coupling to the $πN$ channel are identified with the orbital configurations $(1S_{1/2})^2(nlj)$. Baryon resonances corresponding to the orbital configuration with two valence quarks in excited states couple strongly to the $ππN$-channel, but not to the $πN$ channel. At low energy scale up to 2000 MeV, the obtained numerical estimations for the SU(2) baryon states (up to and including F-wave $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances) within the schematic periodic table are mostly consistent with the experimental data. It is argued that due-to the overestimation of the ground state N(939) and Roper resonance N(1440) almost by the same amount and that the Roper resonance is a radial excitation of the N(939), the "lowering mechanism" for the both baryon states must be the same. The same mechanism is expected in the $Δ$ sector. At higher energies, where the experimental data are poor, we can extend our model schematically and predict seven new $N^*$ and four $Δ^*$ resonances with larger spin values.

## Key claim

The paper argues that all excited nucleon (N*) and Delta (Δ*) resonances that appear in πN scattering data and couple strongly to the πN channel can be identified with the specific orbital configuration (1S₁/₂)²(nlj), where two valence quarks remain in the ground state and only one quark is excited. The author hedges that this construction works "mostly consistent" with experimental data only up to 2000 MeV and for resonances up to and including F-wave, while at higher energies the predictions are schematic and based on poor experimental data.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic chiral quark model with a Lagrangian containing quark core, quark-pion interaction, and pion kinetic terms, solving the Dirac equation with scalar plus vector confinement potentials. Second-order perturbative corrections from one-pion exchange are calculated using the Gell-Mann and Low theorem, including self-energy and exchange diagrams. Selection rules for baryon quantum numbers are derived from the one-pion exchange mechanism coupling upper and lower Dirac components of valence quarks, and the resulting mass spectrum is compared with experimental data from the Review of Particle Physics and other compilations.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is fixed at fπ = 93 MeV; a renormalized value f'π(ex) = 65.22 MeV is used for exchange diagrams in some calculations.
- The ground state N(939) and Roper resonance N(1440) are both overestimated by "almost the same amount" in the model.
- Seven new N* resonances are predicted at higher energies: Jπ = 7/2⁻ (~2000 MeV), 9/2⁺ (~2100-2300 MeV), 11/2⁺ (~2100-2300 MeV), 11/2⁻ (~2500-2700 MeV), 13/2⁻ (~2500-2700 MeV), 13/2⁺ (~2600-2800 MeV), 15/2⁺ (~2600-2800 MeV).
- Four new Δ* resonances are predicted: Jπ = 5/2⁻ (~1700 MeV), 3/2⁻ (~2350 MeV), 11/2⁻ (~2750 MeV), 13/2⁺ (~2950 MeV).
- The Δ*(1700)(3/2⁻) is underestimated by 70-170 MeV in the model.
- The orbital configuration (1S₁/₂)²(1P₃/₂) with n=1 yields three N* resonances: 3/2⁻(1520), 5/2⁻(1675), and 3/2⁻(1700).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous relativistic chiral quark model studies (Tursunov 2005, 2009, 2010) which first estimated the Roper resonance and lowest negative parity nucleon and delta resonances in this framework. The paper addresses the long-standing "missing resonances" problem in Constituent Quark Models (Capstick and Isgur 1986, Glozman et al. 1998, Metch et al. 2001) by restricting the configuration space to (1S₁/₂)²(nlj), which strongly suppresses the number of predicted states. The approach is similar in spirit to diquark-quark models where some degrees of freedom are "frozen." The work enables future experimental searches for the predicted seven new N* and four new Δ* resonances at current facilities, and suggests that the remaining "missing resonances" from Constituent Quark Models should appear in the ππN strong coupling sector rather than the πN channel.
