# Larger Domains of Disoriented Chiral Condensate Through Annealing

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions can generate metastable domains in which the chiral condensate is disoriented. Nucleus-sized domains can yield measurable fluctuations in the number of neutral and charged pions. We propose a scenario in which domains are `annealed' by a dynamically evolving effective potential in the heavy ion system. Domains of sizes exceeding 3~fm are possible in this scenario.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that disoriented chiral condensate domains in relativistic heavy ion collisions can grow to sizes exceeding 3 fm through an "annealing" scenario where the effective potential evolves dynamically, rather than through the previously assumed "quench" scenario where the potential is fixed; the actual domain size depends on the annealing parameter epsilon and the time at which chiral symmetry is broken.

## Method

The authors model the condensate fields (sigma and pion) using relativistic Ginzburg-Landau equations with a Hartree-like semiclassical approach that includes quasiparticle fluctuations. They assume a homologous three-dimensional expansion of the system and solve the equations of motion for the condensate as a function of proper time tau, using collisionless Boltzmann equations for the quasiparticle distributions. Domain sizes are estimated by analyzing the growth of fluctuations about the unstable Phi=0 state using a WKB approximation and saddle-point integration of the correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for disappearance of the wine-bottle shape of the effective potential: T_c = sqrt(2)*f_pi ≈ 132 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.5 MeV
- Zero-temperature sigma mass: m_sigma = 600 MeV
- Zero-temperature pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV
- Standard model parameters: lambda = 20, v = 87.4 MeV, H = (119 MeV)^3
- Spinodal time scale at zero temperature: tau_sp^0 = (lambda*v^2)^(-1/2) ≈ 0.5 fm
- Time for chiral symmetry breaking in RHIC-energy collisions: tau_c ~ 5-20 fm
- For epsilon = 1 (critical system), domain size can reach R_D ≈ 7 fm
- For epsilon = 0.5 (conservative estimate), domain size R_D ~ 3-4 fm
- Initial fluctuation spread: <(dPhi/dtau)^2> ≈ (95 MeV/fm)^2, <Phi^2> ≈ (48 MeV)^2

## Context

This work extends the "quench" scenario proposed by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) for disoriented chiral condensate formation, where the condensate evolves in a fixed zero-temperature potential. Numerical simulations by Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1993) showed that the quench scenario yields domains roughly pion-sized (too small for experimental detection). The annealing scenario presented here accounts for finite-temperature effects and the slow expansion of the heavy ion system, producing significantly larger domains that could be observable at RHIC and LHC. The paper enables experimental searches for centauro-like fluctuations in neutral-to-charged pion ratios at these colliders.
