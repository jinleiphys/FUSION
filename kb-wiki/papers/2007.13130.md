# Determination of the masses and decay widths of the scalar-isoscalar and vector-isovector mesons below $2$ GeV

> This study revisits the issue of the extraction of estimates for the masses and for the partial decay widths to two pions of the scalar-isoscalar $I^G\,(J^{PC}) = 0^+\,(0^{++})$ and vector-isovector $I^G\,(J^{PC}) = 1^+\,(1^{--})$ mesons below $2$ GeV, from the data contained in the recent compilation by the Particle Data Group.

## Key claim

The paper extracts improved estimates for the masses and partial decay widths to two pions of scalar-isoscalar and vector-isovector mesons below 2 GeV from the Particle Data Group (PDG) compilation, using robust statistical methods that are less sensitive to outliers than the standard weighted averaging used by the PDG; the author notes that the main weakness is the assumption that all listed values in the PDG are independent observations, which is not always true.

## Method

The author applies four statistical methods to the PDG data: standard chi-squared minimization, two robust optimization methods (Andrews and Tukey weights) that reduce contributions from outliers, and a non-parametric method based on the Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) obtained by averaging Gaussian distributions from each input datapoint. The robust methods of Andrews and Tukey yield the main recommended values, while the trimmed dataset (after removing outliers identified by both robust methods) is analyzed with standard chi-squared and CDF methods as supplementary checks.

## Key numbers

- Mass of f0(500): 497 +28 -33 MeV (Andrews weights), compared to PDG estimate of 400-550 MeV
- Total decay width of f0(500): 381 +74 -75 MeV (Andrews weights), compared to PDG estimate of 400-700 MeV
- Mass of f0(980): 979.4 +4.0 -3.8 MeV (this work), compared to PDG estimate of 990 ± 20 MeV
- Total decay width of f0(980): 49.2 +22.6 -5.1 MeV (this work), compared to PDG estimate of 10-100 MeV
- Branching fraction of f0(980) to pi pi: 81.0 +3.0 -3.2 percent (this work); PDG gives no value
- Mass of f0(1370): 1320 ± 12 MeV (this work), compared to PDG estimate of 1200-1500 MeV
- Total decay width of f0(1370): 233 +44 -54 MeV (this work), compared to PDG estimate of 200-500 MeV
- Mass of f0(1500): 1498.5 +4.4 -4.2 MeV (this work), compared to PDG average of 1506 ± 6 MeV
- Total decay width of f0(1500): 117.2 ± 4.1 MeV (this work), compared to PDG average of 112 ± 9 MeV
- Branching fraction of f0(1500) to pi pi: 28.2 +5.3 -2.8 percent (this work), compared to PDG fit value of 34.5 ± 2.2 percent
- Mass of f0(1710): 1715.1 +10.5 -9.2 MeV (this work), compared to PDG average of 1732 +9 -7 MeV
- Total decay width of f0(1710): 144 ± 13 MeV (this work), compared to PDG average of 147 +12 -10 MeV
- Branching fraction of f0(1710) to pi pi: 8.9 +3.2 -3.0 percent (this work); PDG gives no value

## Context

This work extends the author's previous pion-nucleon interaction model (Matsinos 2017) which requires t-channel exchanges of scalar-isoscalar and vector-isovector mesons below 2 GeV, and was motivated by the need for estimates of the f0(1710) properties not provided by the PDG. The results contradict the PDG's practice of providing broad ranges (estimates) for several resonances such as f0(500), f0(980), and f0(1370), instead offering specific values with uncertainties from robust statistical treatment. The work enables improved modeling of pion-nucleon scattering by providing self-consistent resonance parameters from a single analysis of the PDG data, rather than relying on disparate PDG recommendations.
