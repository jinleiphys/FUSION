# Molecular effects in Charmonium Spectrum

> We study the influence of possible molecular structures in the charmonium spectrum. We focus on the $0^{++}$ and $1^{--}$ sectors. In the second one we include the $3^3S_1$ and $2^3D_1$ charmonium states coupled to $DD$, $DD^*$, $D^*D^*$, $D_sD_s$, $D_sD_s^*$ and $D_s^*D_s^*$. ## Key claim

The paper claims that coupling bare charmonium states with meson-antimeson molecular channels can produce additional states and resolve discrepancies in decay branching ratios, but the identification of the new molecular state with either the G(3900) or the controversial Y(4008) remains ambiguous, and the experimental situation for the 0++ sector is not clear because two states (X(3945) and Y(3940)) may actually be a single state (X(3915)).

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel calculation using a constituent quark model, where the T-matrix is decomposed into resonant and non-resonant contributions. They solved the Schrodinger equation for the q-qbar states using the interaction of Vijande (2005), obtained the meson-antimeson interaction via the Resonating Group Method, and coupled the two sectors using the microscopic 3P0 model. Poles of the T-matrix were found in the second Riemann sheet, and partial widths were evaluated from the residues of the poles.

- In the 1-- sector: a state at 3994.6 - i 11.60 MeV with 31.56% 3^3S1 and 3.00% 2^3D1 probability; a state at 4048.4 - i 7.54 MeV with 0.92% 3^3S1 and 36.15% 2^3D1 probability; a state at 4123.9 - i 71.11 MeV with 59.01% 3^3S1 and 0.98% 2^3D1 probability.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the X(3872) as a DD* molecule coupled to the chi_c1(2P) state (Ortega, 2010), using the same constituent quark model framework (Vijande, 2005). It generalizes the formalism to study resonances above thresholds and addresses the strong disagreement of decay branching ratios measured by BABAR (Aubert, 2008) for the psi(4040) and psi(4160). The results are compared with the updated Cornell model (C3) of Eichten (2005), which finds only one state in the 0++ sector and two states in the 1-- sector, whereas the present calculation finds an additional molecular state in each sector.
