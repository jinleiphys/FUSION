# Nonextensive effects on the viscous properties of hot and magnetized QCD matter

> We have studied the effect of the nonextensive Tsallis mechanism on the viscous properties of hot QCD matter in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The results are compared to the case of absence of magnetic field. The viscous coefficients, such as the shear viscosity ($η$) and the bulk viscosity ($ζ$) are determined in the similar environment by utilizing the nonextensive Tsallis mechanism within the relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory. We have observed that, when the nonextensive parameter $q$ is just above unity, both shear and bulk viscosities get increased as compared to their counterparts at $q=1$. This enhancement in viscosities is more evident in the additional presence of a strong magnetic field. Furthermore, some observables pertaining to the flow characteristic, fluid behavior and conformal symmetry of the medium are also explored.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a hot QCD medium slightly out of equilibrium (described by the Tsallis nonextensive parameter \(q\) just above unity), both shear viscosity (\(\eta\)) and bulk viscosity (\(\zeta\)) are enhanced compared to the equilibrium (\(q=1\)) case, and this enhancement is more pronounced when a strong magnetic field (\(|q_fB| \gg T^2\)) is present.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation within the relaxation time approximation, using the Tsallis nonextensive form of the quark, antiquark, and gluon distribution functions. They compute the shear and bulk viscosities from the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor, both in the absence of a magnetic field and in the strong magnetic field limit where quarks occupy only the lowest Landau levels. The relaxation times for quarks and gluons are taken from standard perturbative QCD expressions.

## Key numbers

- The estimated magnitude of the magnetic field at RHIC is \(m_\pi^2\) (\(\simeq 10^{18}\) Gauss) and at LHC is \(15 m_\pi^2\) (from cited references).
- For hadronic matter, the nonextensive parameter \(q\) varies in the range 1.08 – 1.2 based on fits to RHIC and LHC spectra (from cited references).
- For quark matter, the nonextensive parameter \(q\) deviates up to 1.22 (from cited references).
- The lower bound of \(\eta/s\) from AdS/CFT is \(1/(4\pi)\) (from cited reference).
- The value of \(\eta/s\) near \(T_c\) from first-principle approaches is about 0.2, increasing with temperature (from cited references).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in hot QCD matter (e.g., shear and bulk viscosities computed via perturbation theory by Arnold et al. (2000, 2003, 2006) and via kinetic theory by Danielewicz (1985) and Sasaki (2009)) by incorporating the nonextensive Tsallis mechanism. It also builds on the authors' own recent work on charge and heat conductivities in a magnetized nonextensive medium (Rath and Dash, 2023). The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma to account for nonextensive effects and strong magnetic fields, particularly for observables like elliptic flow and the specific shear viscosity.
