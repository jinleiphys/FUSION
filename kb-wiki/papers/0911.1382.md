# Diffraction on Nuclei: Effects of Nucleon Correlations

> The cross sections for a variety of diffractive processes in proton-nucleus scattering, associated with large gaps in rapidity, are calculated within an improved Glauber-Gribov theory, where the inelastic shadowing corrections are summed to all orders by employing the dipole representation. The effects of nucleon correlations, leading to a modification of the nuclear thickness function, are also taken into account. Numerical calculations are performed for the energies of the HERA-B experiment, and the RHIC and LHC colliders, and for several nuclei. It is found that whereas the Gribov corrections generally make nuclear matter more transparent, nucleon correlations act in the opposite direction and have important effects in various diffractive processes.

## Key claim

Nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) increase the nuclear thickness function and make nuclear matter more opaque, counteracting the transparency induced by Gribov (inelastic shadowing) corrections; the effect is small but non-negligible, with the correction to the total cross section rising slowly as \(A^{1/3}\) and coming mainly from peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors sum Gribov inelastic shadowing corrections to all orders using the dipole representation, with the dipole-nucleon cross section of Kopeliovich et al. (2002) fitted to data. They incorporate nucleon correlations by expanding the square of the nuclear wave function in terms of one- and two-body density matrices, using a fully correlated wave function generated by the Argonne \(V8^\prime\) interaction (Alvioli et al., 2005, 2006) with no free parameters. Numerical calculations are performed for proton-nucleus scattering on several nuclei at HERA-B, RHIC, and LHC energies, comparing two models for the proton wave function (a \(q\)-\(2q\) diquark model and a symmetric \(3q\) model).

## Key numbers

- Total \(p\)-\(^{12}\)C cross section at HERA-B energy: Glauber + SRC gives 364.11 mb, compared to 353.71 mb for Glauber alone; the \(q\)-\(2q\) model + SRC gives 344.16 mb, and the \(3q\) model + SRC gives 349.37 mb.
- Elastic \(p\)-\(^{12}\)C cross section at HERA-B: Glauber + SRC gives 92.96 mb, versus 86.90 mb for Glauber alone; \(q\)-\(2q\) + SRC gives 82.39 mb, \(3q\) + SRC gives 85.42 mb.
- Single diffraction cross section \(p\)-\(^{12}\)C at HERA-B: 5.43 mb in the \(q\)-\(2q\) model + SRC, 2.40 mb in the \(3q\) model + SRC.
- Quasielastic cross section \(p\)-\(^{12}\)C at HERA-B: Glauber + SRC gives 19.62 mb, compared to 22.85 mb for Glauber alone; \(q\)-\(2q\) + SRC gives 21.12 mb, \(3q\) + SRC gives 22.05 mb.
- Double diffraction cross section \(p\)-\(^{12}\)C at HERA-B: 0.61 mb in the \(q\)-\(2q\) model + SRC, 0.26 mb in the \(3q\) model + SRC.
- The correction \(\Delta T_A^h(b)\) to the nuclear thickness function is mostly negative, increasing the effective thickness; for carbon and lead at HERA-B energy, \(\Delta T_A^h(b) \ll T_A^h(b)\).
- The single diffraction cross section used in the dipole model is \(\sigma^{pN}_{sd} \approx 4\) mb, taken as nearly constant at high energies.

## Context

This work extends the dipole-approach calculations of Kopeliovich et al. (2005) and Kopeliovich et al. (2007) by improving the nuclear wave function model to include short-range nucleon correlations, which were previously ignored in nuclear shadowing studies. It builds on the finding of Alvioli et al. (2008) that SRC affect total neutron-nucleus cross sections at Fermilab energies, and on experimental evidence for SRC from lepton and hadron scattering (Science, 2003). The results enable more accurate predictions for diffractive processes at RHIC and LHC, and demonstrate that SRC must be included in Glauber-Gribov calculations for heavy nuclei.
