# Glauber-theory calculations of high-energy nuclear scattering observables using variational Monte Carlo wave functions

> Experiments using intermediate- to high-energy radioactive nuclear beams present numerous findings. Extracting important properties of physical observables relies on a firm theoretical analysis. Though Glauber theory is believed to work well, no convincing calculation has so far been done. We perform ab initio Glauber theory calculations of both elastic differential cross sections and total reaction cross sections for p+12C, 12C+12C, and 6He+12C systems. The wave functions of both 6He and 12C are generated by variational Monte Carlo calculations with spatial and spin-isospin correlations induced by realistic two- and three-nucleon potentials. Glauber's phase-shift function is computed by Monte Carlo integration up to all orders of nucleon-nucleon multiple scatterings. We show an excellent performance of the Glauber description to the selected data on the above systems. We also find that the cumulant expansion of the phase-shift function converges rapidly up to the second order for the above systems. This finding will open up interesting applications for the analysis of high-energy nuclear experiments.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that ab initio Glauber theory calculations using variational Monte Carlo wave functions can reproduce experimental elastic differential cross sections and total reaction cross sections for p+12C, 12C+12C, and 6He+12C systems, but only within the eikonal and adiabatic approximations; the adiabatic assumption becomes questionable below about 100 MeV/nucleon.

## Method
The authors compute Glauber's phase-shift function by Monte Carlo integration up to all orders of nucleon-nucleon multiple scatterings, using variational Monte Carlo wave functions for 6He and 12C generated with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana X three-nucleon potentials. The Coulomb breakup effect is accounted for by subtracting the point-Coulomb phase and adding a correction term in the profile function with a cut-off parameter based on the root-mean-square point-proton radii. The cumulant expansion of the phase-shift function is evaluated up to second order and compared against the full Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers
- p+12C elastic differential cross sections are reproduced up to q ≈ 3 fm−1 at 300 MeV, where K = 3.7 fm−1
- 12C+12C total reaction cross sections are reproduced above 300 MeV/nucleon; the cumu-1 approximation overestimates σR by 30–50 mb beyond 300 MeV/nucleon
- 12C+12C elastic differential cross sections at 120 MeV/nucleon show agreement with data; at 80 MeV/nucleon the calculation reproduces experiment only at q ≲ 1 fm−1
- 6He+12C total reaction cross section at 800 MeV/nucleon is reproduced by the full calculation, with σR ≈ σI for this system

## Context
This work extends the Glauber theory calculations of Varga (2002) by using realistic variational Monte Carlo wave functions for both projectile and target nuclei, rather than simpler density models. The 12C wave function has been used successfully in studies of (p,pN) reactions by Crespo (2020) and Cravo (2024), electron scattering by Andreoli (2024), and neutrinoless double-beta decay by Weiss (2022). The finding that the cumulant expansion converges rapidly up to second order enables future applications such as determining the neutron skin of 208Pb via p+208Pb scattering, revisiting the analysis of Horiuchi (2016) with improved Coulomb breakup treatment.
