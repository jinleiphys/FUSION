# Surmounting the sign problem in non-relativistic calculations: a case study with mass-imbalanced fermions

> The calculation of the ground state and thermodynamics of mass-imbalanced Fermi systems is a challenging many-body problem. Even in one spatial dimension, analytic solutions are limited to special configurations and numerical progress with standard Monte Carlo approaches is hindered by the sign problem. The focus of the present work is on the further development of methods to study imbalanced systems in a fully non-perturbative fashion. We report our calculations of the ground-state energy of mass-imbalanced fermions using two different approaches which are also very popular in the context of the theory of the strong interaction (Quantum Chromodynamics, QCD): (a) the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with imaginary mass imbalance, followed by an analytic continuation to the real axis; and (b) the Complex Langevin algorithm. We cover a range of on-site interaction strengths that includes strongly attractive as well as strongly repulsive cases which we verify with non-perturbative renormalization group methods and perturbation theory. Our findings indicate that, for strong repulsive couplings, the energy starts to flatten out, implying interesting consequences for short-range and high-frequency correlation functions. Overall, our results clearly indicate that the Complex Langevin approach is very versatile and works very well for imbalanced Fermi gases with both attractive and repulsive interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Complex Langevin (CL) method can reliably compute the ground-state energy of mass-imbalanced fermions across both attractive and repulsive interaction strengths, while the hybrid Monte Carlo with imaginary mass imbalance (iHMC) followed by analytic continuation works only up to moderate mass imbalances (approximately \(\bar{m} \sim 0.5-0.6\)) before uncertainties grow prohibitively.

## Method

The authors study one-dimensional fermions with contact interaction using two non-perturbative approaches: (a) hybrid Monte Carlo with imaginary mass imbalance (iHMC), where masses are taken complex conjugate to ensure a positive measure, followed by analytic continuation via Pade approximants to real mass imbalance; and (b) Complex Langevin (CL) dynamics, which complexifies the auxiliary Hubbard-Stratonovich field and includes a regulator term with strength \(\xi\) that is extrapolated to zero. Both methods are applied to systems of \(N=5+5\) and \(N=3+3\) fermions on a spatial lattice of \(N_x = 40\) sites with temporal lattice spacing \(\tau = 0.05\), using up to \(N_\tau \sim 1500\) time slices and averaging 5000 decorrelated samples.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of \(N=5+5\) mass-balanced fermions (\(\bar{m}=0\)) agrees among iHMC, CL, and DFT-RG methods for attractive couplings \(-2 \lesssim \gamma < 0\) and for repulsive couplings \(0 < \gamma \lesssim 2\).
* The iHMC method yields reliable results for real mass imbalance up to \(\bar{m} \sim 0.5-0.6\); beyond \(\bar{m} \sim 0.6\) uncertainties grow rapidly and quantitative predictions are not guaranteed.
* CL results for the ground-state energy are obtained with statistical uncertainties at the \(1\%\) level using regulator strength \(\xi = 0.1\) and target CL integration step \(h_0 = 0.01\).
* The noninteracting energy on the lattice as a function of mass imbalance follows \(E_{\bar{m}} = E_0 [1/(1-\bar{m}^2)]\), where \(E_0\) is the mass-balanced noninteracting energy on the lattice.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mass-imbalanced Fermi systems that were limited to special configurations (Harshman 2017, Olshanii 2015) or infinite interaction strength (Dehkharghani 2016), and builds on the imaginary chemical potential technique used in QCD (deForcrand 2002) and earlier nonrelativistic applications (Braun 2013, Loheac 2015). The CL method had been applied to relativistic theories (Aarts 2009, 2010, 2011) and nonrelativistic systems (Loheac 2017), but this paper provides the first determination of the full equation of state for mass-imbalanced fermions with contact interaction in one dimension, enabling future studies of correlation functions and extensions to higher dimensions.
