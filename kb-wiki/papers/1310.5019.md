# Asymptotic description of finite lifetime effects on the photon emission from a quark-gluon plasma

> Direct photons play an important role as electromagnetic probes from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) which occurs during ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this context, it is of particular interest how the finite lifetime of the QGP affects the resulting photon production. Earlier investigations on this question were accompanied by a divergent contribution from the vacuum polarization and by the remaining contributions not being integrable in the ultraviolet (UV) domain. In this work, we provide a different approach in which we do not consider the photon number density at finite times, but for free asymptotic states obtained by switching the electromagnetic interaction according to the Gell-Mann and Low theorem. This procedure eliminates a possible unphysical contribution from the vacuum polarization and, moreover, renders the photon number density UV integrable. It is emphasized that the consideration of free asymptotic states is, indeed, crucial to obtain such physically reasonable results.

# Asymptotic description of finite lifetime effects on the photon emission from a quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The photon number density from a finite-lifetime quark-gluon plasma (QGP) becomes ultraviolet (UV) integrable and free of vacuum-polarization divergences only when defined for free asymptotic states via Gell-Mann and Low adiabatic switching, rather than at finite times; the artifacts of earlier approaches (Wang 2000, Wang 2001, Boyanovsky 2003) reappear if the photon number density is considered at finite times instead.

## Method

The authors model the time evolution of the QGP by introducing time-dependent quark/antiquark occupation numbers in the photon self-energy at one-loop order, using three different switching functions (instantaneous step function, once-differentiable exponential, and infinitely differentiable hyperbolic tangent). They compute the asymptotic photon number density in the limit t -> infinity with an adiabatic switching factor exp(-epsilon|t|) on the electromagnetic interaction, then take epsilon -> 0. The photon self-energy is summed over up and down quark flavors (sum of squared charges = 5/9 e^2) and three colors, with quark masses set to 0.01 GeV to avoid infrared singularities.

## Key numbers

- For instantaneous formation (step function), the photon number density scales as 1/omega^3_k for large photon momenta, making the total number density logarithmically divergent and the total energy density linearly divergent.
- For the once-differentiable switching function f_2(t) with formation time tau = 1.0 fm/c, the photon number density is suppressed to proportional to 1/omega^7_k, rendering both total number and energy densities UV finite.
- For the infinitely differentiable switching function f_3(t) with tau = 1.0 fm/c, the photon number density decays exponentially in omega_k, with the slope (energy scale for a factor of 1/e decrease) coinciding with beta = 1/T.
- Quark masses are set to m_u = m_d = 0.01 GeV.
- The expected phenomenological formation time of the QGP is tau_QGP approximately 1.0 fm/c (Heinz 2001).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Michler 2009) which used time-dependent occupation numbers but considered photon number density at finite times, and their subsequent approach (Michler 2012) using a Yukawa-like source term with asymptotic states. It directly addresses the divergent vacuum polarization and non-integrable UV behavior found in earlier investigations by Wang (2000, 2001) and Boyanovsky (2003). The results enable consistent calculation of first-order QED photon emission from a finite-lifetime QGP and raise the question of whether the artifacts in prior work stem from an inconsistent definition of photon number density at finite times.
