# Studying the production mechanisms of light meson resonances in two-pion photoproduction

> A theoretical model of two-pion photoproduction is presented. The model encodes the prominent $ρ(770)$ resonance and the expected leading background contribution coming from the Deck mechanism. To validate the model, angular moments are computed and compared with the CLAS dataset. After fitting a number of free parameters, the model provides a good description of the data.

## Key claim

The model provides a good description of the CLAS angular-moment data for two-pion photoproduction, but only after fitting 18 free parameters that include relative weights of resonant partial waves and polynomial non-resonant contributions; the agreement is preliminary and limited to fixed values of the Mandelstam variables s and t.

## Method

The authors construct a theoretical model for the reaction γ p → π⁺ π⁻ p by combining resonant amplitudes for the ρ(770), f₀(500)/σ, and f₀(980) mesons (described by Breit-Wigner line shapes and Regge production amplitudes) with a gauge-invariant Deck mechanism for the continuum background, where the πN subprocess is described using the SAID partial-wave parameterization at low energies interpolated to a Regge description at higher energies. The model is partial-wave projected, and the relative weights of each partial wave are reweighted by complex free parameters g_{LM} that are fit to the CLAS angular-moment data at fixed s and t. Angular moments ⟨Y_{LM}⟩ are computed as integral moments of the cross section and compared with experimental results.

## Key numbers

- Photon energies for the CLAS dataset are of order E_γ ∼ 3 GeV, corresponding to s ∼ 7 GeV².
- Momentum transfer values are as large as t ∼ −1 GeV².
- The hadronic form factor cutoff is Λ_π = 0.9 GeV.
- The model contains 18 free parameters: 2+2+2 for the relative weights of two resonant S-wave contributions and one resonant P-wave contribution, plus 6+6 for non-resonant S- and P-wave contributions modelled as polynomials in s₁₂.
- Fits are performed at fixed t values with −t ∈ {0.55, 0.65, 0.75, 0.85, 0.95} GeV².
- The Regge scale parameter is s₀ = 1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of two-pion photoproduction by combining resonant contributions from known light mesons (ρ(770), f₀(500), f₀(980)) with a gauge-invariant Deck mechanism, building on prior work by Bibrzycki (2018, 2019, 2020) and Hammoud (2022) on the Deck model, and using the πN scattering amplitudes of Mathieu (2015) that incorporate the SAID partial-wave parameterization of Workman (2012). The model is validated against the CLAS dataset (CLAS, 2009) and is intended to enable predictions for the upcoming higher-precision data from CLAS12 and GlueX experiments.
