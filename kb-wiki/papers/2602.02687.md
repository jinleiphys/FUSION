# Anisotropic time evolution of sound modes in Bjorken expanding holographic plasma

> The speed of sound is a key parameter for characterizing equilibrium states. However, sound waves change their properties when propagating through rapidly evolving anisotropic media, such as the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. This paper uses $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super-Yang-Mills theory to numerically study the time evolution of the speed and attenuation of sound modes along with the relaxation time in a plasma undergoing Bjorken expansion from various initial states in a quasi-static approximation. The longitudinal Bjorken expansion breaks the isotropy, resulting in two distinct sound speeds that range from just below the conformal value to the speed of light. An anisotropic hydrodynamic description is constructed and its applicability is discussed. Implications for the analysis of heavy ion data are considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a Bjorken-expanding strongly coupled plasma, the longitudinal expansion breaks isotropy and produces two distinct speeds of sound (transverse and longitudinal) that can range from just below the conformal value of \(1/\sqrt{3}\) up to the speed of light, with the quasi-static approximation breaking down at early times \(\tau T \lesssim 1.2\) for transverse modes and \(\tau T \lesssim 1.4\) for longitudinal modes.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality to numerically compute the time evolution of sound modes in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super-Yang-Mills theory undergoing Bjorken expansion from various initial states. At each instant of the Bjorken time evolution, a sound perturbation is introduced and Fourier transformed under a quasi-static approximation, assuming sound perturbations occur on time scales much shorter than the Bjorken expansion. The speed of sound, sound attenuation coefficient, and relaxation time are extracted from the dispersion relation of the eigenfrequencies of that sound perturbation at that instant, and an anisotropic hydrodynamic description is constructed to analyze the numerical data.

## Key numbers

- The two speeds of sound range from just below the conformal isotropic equilibrium value \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\) up to the speed of light \(c_s \approx 1\)
- The deviation of perturbatively computed sound speeds from thermodynamically computed values can be as large as a factor of two
- Agreement between thermodynamically and perturbatively computed longitudinal (transverse) sound speed is good for times later than 2.5 fm/c (3.5 fm/c), where dimensionless \(\tau T = 1\) corresponds to 1 fm/c
- Both sound attenuation coefficients deviate from their isotropic equilibrium value by at most approximately 10%
- Around \(\tau T \approx 2.5\), the relaxation becomes instantaneous
- The quasi-static approximation and fit procedure break down for the transverse speed of sound including time-derivatives around \(\tau T \lesssim 1.2\) and for longitudinal around \(\tau T \lesssim 1.4\)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of anisotropic hydrodynamic transport in holographic systems, such as those by Ammon et al. (2020) and Garbiso and Sedrakian (2020), where each isotropic transport coefficient splits into longitudinal and transverse components relative to the anisotropy direction. The results provide a strong motivation for considering anisotropic plasma properties in experimental analyses of heavy-ion collisions, particularly challenging the assumption that local thermodynamic equilibrium implies the equilibrium definition of the speed of sound \(c_s^2 = \partial P / \partial \varepsilon\) is valid. The paper also discusses implications for the CMS analysis by CMS (2024) extracting a speed of sound from LHC data, and the subsequent critique by Gavassino et al. (2025).
