# Chiral extrapolation of the magnetic polarizability of the neutral pion

> The magnetic polarizability of the neutral pion has been calculated in the background magnetic-field formalism of Lattice QCD. In this investigation, the chiral extrapolation of these lattice results is considered in a formalism preserving the exact leading nonanalytic terms of chiral perturbation theory. The $n_f = 2 + 1$ numerical simulations are electro-quenched, such that the virtual sea-quarks of the QCD vacuum do not interact with the background field. To understand the impact of this, we draw on partially quenched chiral perturbation theory and identify the leading contributions of quark-flow connected and disconnected diagrams. While electro-quenching does not impact the leading-loop contribution to the magnetic polarizability, the loops which generate the leading term have yet to be considered in lattice QCD simulations. Lattice QCD results are used to constrain the analytic terms in the chiral expansion and supplementing those with the two-loop result from chiral perturbation theory enables an evaluation of the polarizability at the physical quark mass. The resulting magnetic polarizability of the neutral pion is $β_{π^0}=3.44(19)^{\rm stat}(37)^{\rm syst}\times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, which lies just above the $1 σ$ error bound of the experimental measurement.

## Key claim

The magnetic polarizability of the neutral pion is determined to be \(\beta_{\pi^0}=3.44(19)^{\rm stat}(37)^{\rm syst}\times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\), which lies just above the \(1\sigma\) error bound of the experimental measurement; this result relies on combining lattice QCD results with two-loop chiral perturbation theory to account for quark-annihilation loop contributions that are absent in the lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors perform a chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results for the neutral pion magnetic polarizability from Ref. [Bignell (2020)], using a tree-level expansion in powers of the pion mass \(M_\pi\) to fit the lattice data. They then correct the fit by adding the leading one-loop contribution from chiral perturbation theory (which is missing in the electro-quenched lattice simulations) and the two-loop contributions from chiral perturbation theory, including chiral logarithms, while replacing the low-energy coupling (LEC) contributions at order \(M_\pi\) with those from the two-loop calculation. The analysis uses partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to show that sea-quark loop diagrams do not contribute at one loop, so the electro-quenched approximation does not affect the leading contribution.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic polarizability of the neutral pion at the physical pion mass is \(\beta_{\pi^0}=3.44(19)^{\rm stat}(37)^{\rm syst}\times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
* The leading term of the full QCD prediction contributes \(2.38 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\), of which the leading one-loop contribution is \(0.50 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) and the tree-level fit coefficient \(a_{-1}\) contributes \(1.88 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
* The order \(M_\pi\) term of the full QCD prediction contributes \(1.06 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\), and the order \(M_\pi^3\) term contributes \(-0.004 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\).
* The experimental value is \(\beta_{\pi^0}^{\rm Expt} = 1.29(1.10) \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^3\) from Filkov (1998).
* The tree-level fit parameters from lattice QCD results are \(a_{-1}=+1.34\times10^{-4}\) fm\(^2\), \(a_1=+6.85\times10^{-5}\) fm\(^4\), and \(a_3=-1.22\times10^{-6}\) fm\(^6\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results for the neutral pion magnetic polarizability from Bignell (2020), which had not been performed previously, and builds on earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bellucci (1994) and Gasser (2005). The analysis uses partially quenched chiral perturbation theory, following the diagrammatic formalism of Leinweber (2002) and Hall (2013), to identify that the missing quark-annihilation loop contributions (Figs. 2e–2h) are the only one-loop contributions to the magnetic polarizability. The result enables future lattice QCD calculations to include these loop contributions directly, and it motivates two-loop partially quenched chiral perturbation theory calculations to further clarify the role of sea-quark loops.
