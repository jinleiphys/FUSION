# Light cluster production in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei

> The coalescence model based on nucleon distribution functions from an isospin-dependent transport model is used to study the production of light clusters such as deuteron, triton, and $^{3}$He from heavy-ion collisions induced by neutron-rich nuclei at intermediate energies. It is found that the emission time of light clusters depends on their masses. For clusters with the same momentum per nucleon, heavier ones are emitted earlier. Both the yield and energy spectrum of light clusters are sensitive to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, with more light clusters produced in the case of a stiff symmetry energy. On the other hand, effects due to the stiffness of the isoscalar part of nuclear equation of state and the medium dependence of nucleon-nucleon cross sections on light cluster production are unimportant. We have also studied the correlation functions of clusters, and they are affected by the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy as well, with the stiff symmetry energy giving a stronger anti-correlation of light clusters, particularly for those with large kinetic energies. Dependence of light cluster production on the centrality and incident energy of heavy ion collisions as well as the mass of the reaction system is also investigated.

## Key claim

Light cluster production in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, with a stiff symmetry energy producing more clusters than a soft one; however, the effect is strongest for low-energy clusters and the isobaric yield ratio t/³He shows opposite energy dependence for soft versus stiff symmetry energies.

## Method

The authors used a coalescence model based on nucleon phase-space distributions from an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. The Wigner phase-space densities for deuteron (from a 15-Gaussian fit to the Hulthén wave function), triton, and ³He (from harmonic oscillator wave functions) were computed from nucleon distributions at freeze-out, defined when local density falls below ρ₀/8. The IBUU model was run with 20,000 events using 100 test particles per physical nucleon, comparing soft (γ=0.5) and stiff (γ=2) symmetry energy parameterizations E_sym(ρ)=30 MeV·u^γ.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron multiplicity from ⁵²Ca+⁴⁸Ca at 80 MeV/nucleon is 51% larger for stiff symmetry energy than for soft symmetry energy
- Triton multiplicity is 73% larger for stiff symmetry energy than for soft symmetry energy
- ³He multiplicity is 100% larger for stiff symmetry energy than for soft symmetry energy
- The final isobaric yield ratio t/³He is about 17% less for stiff symmetry energy than for soft symmetry energy
- At 70 fm/c, the t/³He ratio is increased by 23% from stiff to soft symmetry energy
- For clusters with kinetic energy around 10 MeV, symmetry energy effect on yield is about 60% (deuteron), 100% (triton), and 120% (³He)
- For clusters with kinetic energy around 100 MeV, symmetry energy effect on yield is about 30% (deuteron), 40% (triton), and 85% (³He)
- Changing nuclear incompressibility from K₀=380 to 201 MeV changes t/³He ratio by less than 4%
- Using in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections instead of free-space cross sections changes t/³He ratio by less than 4%
- Deuteron multiplicity from ³⁶Ar+⁵⁸Ni at E/A=95 MeV for impact parameter 4-5 fm is 1.15 (calculated) versus 0.814 (experimental), with average kinetic energy per nucleon 15.7 MeV (calculated) versus 18.2 MeV (experimental)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Chen, Ko, and Li, 2003) on light cluster production using the IBUU model, providing additional results on cluster correlation functions and dependence on centrality, incident energy, and system mass. It builds on prior findings that the strength of nucleon-pair correlation functions is sensitive to symmetry energy (CorShort) and that pre-equilibrium neutron/proton ratios (Li, 1997), isospin fractionation (Xu, 2000; Tan, 2001; Baran, 2002), and π⁻/π⁺ ratios (Li, 2002) are also symmetry-energy sensitive. The results enable using light cluster yields and the t/³He ratio as experimental probes of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy at future Rare Isotope Accelerator facilities, with the advantage that these observables are insensitive to the isoscalar equation of state and medium-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
