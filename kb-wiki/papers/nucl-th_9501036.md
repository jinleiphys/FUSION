# Nuclear Medium Effects in the Relativistic Treatment of Quasifree Electron Scattering

> Non-relativistic reduction of the S-matrix for the quasifree electron scattering process $A\left(~e, e'p~\right)A-1$ is studied in order to understand the source of differences between non-relativistic and relativistic models. We perform an effective Pauli reduction on the relativistic expression for the S-matrix in the one-photon exchange approximation. The reduction is applied to the nucleon current only; the electrons are treated fully relativistically. An expansion of the amplitude results in a power series in the nuclear potentials. The series is found to converge rapidly only if the nuclear potentials are included in the nuclear current operator. The results can be cast in a form which reproduces the non-relativistic amplitudes in the limit that the potentials are removed from the nuclear current operator. Large differences can be found between calculations which do and do not include the nuclear potentials in the different orders of the nuclear current operator. In the high missing momentum region we find that the non-relativistic calculations with potentials included in the nuclear current up to second order give results which are close to those of the fully relativistic calculation. This behavior is an indication of the importance of the medium modifications of the nuclear currents in this model, which are naturally built into the relativistic treatment of the reaction.

# Nuclear Medium Effects in the Relativistic Treatment of Quasifree Electron Scattering

## Key claim
The essential difference between relativistic and non-relativistic approaches to quasifree electron scattering is not merely in the choice of optical potentials, but in the appearance of nuclear potentials in the nuclear current operators when the relativistic amplitude is reduced to non-relativistic form. Non-relativistic calculations that include nuclear potentials in the nuclear current up to second order give results close to the fully relativistic calculation in the high missing momentum region, while expansions based on free vertices (without nuclear potentials) do not converge to the relativistic results even at higher orders.

## Method
The authors perform an effective Pauli reduction on the relativistic expression for the S-matrix for the A(e, e'p)A-1 reaction in the one-photon exchange approximation, applying the reduction only to the nucleon current while treating electrons fully relativistically. The amplitude is expanded as a power series in (E+M)^{-1}, and the resulting nuclear current operators are expressed in terms of Schr\"odinger-equivalent wave functions with explicit dependence on the Dirac scalar and vector potentials through functions Q(r) = (S(r) - V(r))/(E+M). Calculations are performed for parallel kinematics using Hartree bound state wave functions and energy-dependent optical potentials for the continuum proton.

## Key numbers
- Incident electron energy: 456 MeV, with detected proton kinetic energy fixed at 90 MeV in parallel kinematics for the reaction $^{16}O(e, e'p)^{15}N$
- The expansion in (E+M)^{-1} converges rapidly only when nuclear potentials are included in the nuclear current operator; second-order calculations with potentials give results very close to the fully relativistic calculation over the range of missing momenta shown
- Different optical potentials can change results by as much as 14%, as found by Jin and Onley (1994)
- The analysis is restricted to nuclei no heavier than $^{90}Zr$ because Coulomb interactions in the leptonic part are not included

## Context
This work extends the analysis of differences between relativistic and non-relativistic treatments of quasifree electron scattering, following studies by Boffi et al. (1987), Udias et al. (1993), and Jin and Onley (1994). Previous work had focused on the sensitivity to different optical potentials and renormalizations of continuum wave functions, with Boffi et al. multiplying non-relativistic continuum wave functions by a potential-dependent factor and Udias et al. finding little difference between relativistic and "non-relativistic" calculations when using upper components of Dirac wave functions. The present paper demonstrates that nuclear potentials must appear explicitly in the nuclear current operators to achieve convergence to relativistic results, explaining why relativistic and non-relativistic approaches can give different spectroscopic factors even when equivalent optical potentials are used. This enables a clearer understanding of medium modifications of nuclear currents that are naturally built into relativistic treatments.
