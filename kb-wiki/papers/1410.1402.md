# Charge-dependent directed flow in asymmetric nuclear collisions

> The directed flow of identified hadrons is studied within the parton-hadron-string-dynamics (PHSD) approach for the asymmetric system Cu+Au in non-central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. It is emphasized that due to the difference in the number of protons of the colliding nuclei an electric field emerges which is directed from the heavy to the light nucleus. This strong electric field is only present for about 0.25 fm/c at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and leads to a splitting of the directed flow $v_1$ for particles with the same mass but opposite electric charges in case of an early presence of charged quarks and antiquarks. The microscopic calculations of the directed flow for $π^\pm, K^\pm, p$ and $\bar{p}$ are carried out in the PHSD by taking into account the electromagnetic field induced by the spectators as well as its influence on the hadronic and partonic quasiparticle trajectories. It is shown that the splitting of the directed flow as a function of pseudorapidity $η$ and in particular as a function of the transverse momentum $p_t$ provides a direct access to the electromagnetic response of the very early (nonequilibrium) phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and allows to shed light on the presence (and number) of electric charges in this phase.

## Key claim

The directed flow splitting between particles of the same mass but opposite electric charge in asymmetric Cu+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is predicted to arise from a strong early-time electric field (present for about 0.25 fm/c) that acts only if charged quarks and antiquarks are already present during that brief window; if charges appear only later (at \(t \ge 0.5\) fm/c), no such splitting occurs.

## Method

The parton-hadron-string-dynamics (PHSD) transport approach was used to simulate non-central Cu+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV for impact parameters \(4.7 \le b \le 9.5\) fm (10–40% centrality). Retarded Liénard-Wiechert potentials from spectator protons provided the electromagnetic fields, which were then applied to the trajectories of partonic and hadronic quasiparticles. The directed flow \(v_1\) was computed for \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(p\), and \(\bar{p}\) under two scenarios: one where electric charges are present immediately after string dissolution (early charges) and one where charge formation is delayed to the parton formation time.

## Key numbers

- The strong electric field \(eE_x\) in Cu+Au collisions is present for about 0.25 fm/c at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The \(\langle eE_x\rangle\) component in Cu+Au is larger by a factor of \(\sim 5\) compared to symmetric Au+Au collisions at the same energy.
- At mid-pseudorapidity (\(\eta = 0\)), the directed flow values with electromagnetic field (PHSD+EMF) for \(\pi^+\) and \(\pi^-\) are \((-3.87 \pm 0.133) \times 10^{-3}\) and \((1.71 \pm 0.129) \times 10^{-3}\), respectively; without the field (PHSD only) they are \((-0.68 \pm 0.140) \times 10^{-3}\) and \((-1.09 \pm 0.136) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The slope \(dv_1/d\eta\) at \(\eta = 0\) for \(\pi^+\) with electromagnetic field is \((-6.39 \pm 0.299) \times 10^{-3}\) and for \(\pi^-\) is \((-6.18 \pm 0.291) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The critical energy density for the parton-hadron transition in PHSD is \(\varepsilon_c \approx 0.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The maximal magnetic field component \(\langle eB_y\rangle\) is on the level of a few \(m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior PHSD studies of directed flow in symmetric Au+Au collisions (Voronyuk et al. 2011; Konchakovski et al. 2012) to the asymmetric Cu+Au system, where a charge-dependent splitting was anticipated by Hirono et al. (2012) and Gürsoy et al. (2014). The predictions enable experimental tests at RHIC (PHENIX Collaboration) to distinguish whether electric charges are present immediately after string dissolution or only after parton formation, and to probe the electric conductivity of the early quark-gluon plasma phase.
