# Quark-mass dependence in $ω\to3π$ decays

> We study the quark-mass dependence of $ω\to3π$ decays, based on a dispersion-theoretical framework. We rely on the quark-mass-dependent scattering phase shift for the pion-pion $P$-wave extracted from unitarized chiral perturbation theory. The dispersive representation then takes into account the final-state rescattering among all three pions. The described formalism may be used as an extrapolation tool for lattice QCD calculations of three-pion decays, for which $ω\to3π$ can serve as a paradigm case.

## Key claim

The quark-mass dependence of the ω→3π decay width can be predicted using a dispersion-theoretical framework that combines unitarized chiral perturbation theory for the pion-pion P-wave phase shift with Khuri-Treiman equations, provided that the ω mass dependence on the pion mass is assumed to follow that of the ρ meson and the subtraction constant (related to the ωρπ coupling) is taken as pion-mass independent.

## Method

The authors construct the ω→3π decay amplitude using Khuri-Treiman equations, which incorporate elastic final-state rescattering among all three pions via the pion-pion P-wave scattering phase shift. The phase shift is extracted from a one-loop unitarized chiral perturbation theory (inverse amplitude method, IAM) amplitude for ππ→ππ scattering, which provides the quark-mass-dependent ρ meson pole position. The dispersive representation requires one subtraction constant α, which is fixed to the physical ω→3π decay width and assumed to be independent of the pion mass. The ω mass dependence on the pion mass is taken to be linear in Mπ², with the same slope as the ρ mass, based on an effective Lagrangian analysis of vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- ρ meson pole position at the physical point: √spole = 0.7620(15) GeV − i 0.0778(11) GeV
- ρ meson mass dependence on Mπ²: Mρ(0) = 0.7480(16) GeV, slope a = 0.719(9) GeV⁻¹
- ρππ coupling extracted from IAM: |gρππ| = 6.12(4), found to be essentially pion-mass independent
- ω meson mass at the chiral limit: Mω(0) = 0.7686(20) GeV, with slope a = 0.719(9) GeV⁻¹ (same as ρ)
- Low-energy constant combination from IAM fit: l̄ = 5.73(8)
- Pion decay constant at physical point: Fπ = 92.28(9) MeV; ratio Fπ/F = 1.064(7)
- Isospin-breaking correction to Γω from charged/neutral pion mass difference is less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical approach previously applied to ω→3π (Niecknig 2012, Danilkin 2014) and related processes such as γ(*)→3π (Hoferichter 2012, 2014, 2017, 2018) by incorporating quark-mass dependence. The pion-pion phase shift input relies on the inverse amplitude method (IAM) as developed by Hanhart (2008) and Peláez (2010) for studying the ρ meson's quark-mass dependence. The formalism is intended as an extrapolation tool for lattice QCD calculations of three-pion decays, where ω→3π serves as a paradigm case; the results can be compared with lattice studies of the ρ meson (Alexandrou 2017) and ongoing investigations of three-body decays on the lattice (Hansen 2014, 2015, Mai 2017).
