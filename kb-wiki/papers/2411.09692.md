# Reggeization in Color

> In the high energy limit, $s\gg -t$, amplitudes in planar gauge theories Reggeize, with power law behavior $\big( \frac{s}{-t} \big)^{α(t)}$ governed by the Regge trajectory $α(t)$. Beyond the planar limit this simplicity is violated by "Regge cuts", for which practical organizational principles are still being developed. We use a top-down effective field theory organization based on color projection in the $t$ channel and rapidity evolution equations for collinear impact factors, to sum large $s\gg -t$ logarithms for Regge cut contributions. The results are matrix equations which are closed within a given color channel. To illustrate the method we derive in QCD with $SU(N_c)$ for the first time a closed 6$\times$6 evolution equation for the "decupletons" in the $\text{10}\oplus\overline{\text{10}}$ Regge color channel, a 2$\times$2 evolution equation for the "triantapentons" in the $\text{35}\oplus\overline{\text{35}}$ color channel, and a scalar evolution equation for the "tetrahexaconton" in the 64 color channel. More broadly, our approach allows us to describe generic Reggeization phenomena in non-planar gauge theories, providing valuable data for the all loop structure of amplitudes beyond the planar limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents a top-down effective field theory (EFT) organization based on color projection in the t-channel and rapidity evolution equations for collinear impact factors, which enables the summation of large s >> -t logarithms for Regge cut contributions in non-planar gauge theories, with the method illustrated by deriving closed evolution equations for specific color channels that only receive contributions from triple Glauber exchange.

## Method

The authors use Glauber SCET (Soft-Collinear Effective Theory) to factorize forward scattering amplitudes into collinear impact factors (jet functions J and anti-jet functions J-bar) and a central rapidity soft function S, with the amplitude expressed as a sum over terms with different numbers of Glauber operator exchanges. They perform internal color projections onto irreducible representations of SU(N_c) in the t-channel, rather than the standard approach of projecting using external particles, to derive rapidity renormalization group equations (RRGEs) that take the form of BFKL equations but with an additional matrix structure accounting for the multiplicity of identical irreps. The anomalous dimension Gamma, which governs the RRGEs, is independent of the scattering states and enables resummation of ln(s/-t) logarithms at any order in perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* A closed 6x6 evolution equation is derived for the "decupletons" in the 10 ⊕ 10-bar Regge color channel in QCD with SU(N_c)
* A 2x2 evolution equation is derived for the "triantapentons" in the 35 ⊕ 35-bar color channel
* A scalar (1x1) evolution equation is derived for the "tetrahexaconton" in the 64 color channel
* The decomposition of 8 ⊗ 8 = 1 ⊕ 8^A ⊕ 8^S ⊕ 10 ⊕ 10-bar ⊕ 27 ⊕ 0 for two-Reggeon exchange, where the 0 representation only exists for N_c > 3
* For three-Reggeon exchange with N_c > 3: 8 ⊗ 8 ⊗ 8 = 1^2 ⊕ 8^9 ⊕ 10^6 ⊕ 10-bar^6 ⊕ 27^6 ⊕ 35^2 ⊕ 35-bar^2 ⊕ 64 ⊕ 0^17
* For three-Reggeon exchange with N_c = 3: 8 ⊗ 8 ⊗ 8 = 1^2 ⊕ 8^8 ⊕ 10^4 ⊕ 10-bar^4 ⊕ 27^6 ⊕ 35^2 ⊕ 35-bar^2 ⊕ 64

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous EFT approach to Regge scattering (Gao 2024) from two-Glauber exchange to three-Glauber exchange, addressing the problem of organizing color representations with high multiplicity that appears starting at three-Reggeon exchange. The approach provides a systematic framework for describing Reggeization phenomena in non-planar gauge theories, complementing the Wilson line approach used by Caron-Huot (2013, 2017) and others for extracting the three-loop Regge trajectory in QCD, and enables future work on the mixing of cut and pole solutions for the remaining color channels, most importantly the octet.
