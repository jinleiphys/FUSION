# Effects of $β_{6}$ deformation and low-lying vibrational bands on heavy-ion fusion reactions at sub-barrier energies

> We study fusion reactions of $^{16}$O with $^{154}$Sm, $^{186}$W and $^{238}$U at sub-barrier energies by a coupled-channels framework. We focus especially on the effects of $β_{6}$ deformation and low-lying vibrational excitations of the target nucleus. It is shown that the inclusion of $β_{6}$ deformation leads to a considerable improvement of the fit to the experimental data for all of these reactions. For the $^{154}$Sm and $^{238}$U targets, the octupole vibration significantly affects the fusion barrier distribution. The effect of $β$ band is negligible in all the three reactions, while the $γ$ band causes a non-negligible effect on the barrier distribution at energies above the main fusion barrier. We compare the optimum values of the deformation parameters obtained by fitting the fusion data with those obtained from inelastic scatterings and the ground state mass calculations. We show that the channel coupling of high multipolarity beyond the quadrupole coupling is dominated by the nuclear coupling and hence higher order Coulomb coupling does not much influence the optimum values of $β_4$ and $β_6$ parameters. We also discuss the effect of two neutron transfer reactions on the fusion of $^{16}$O with $^{238}$U.

## Key claim

The inclusion of β₆ deformation significantly improves the fit to experimental fusion data for all three reactions studied, but the sign of β₆ obtained from fusion-only analysis is opposite to that from inelastic scattering and mass calculations for ¹⁵⁴Sm and ²³⁸U; this sign discrepancy is resolved only when the coupling to low-lying octupole vibrations is also included.

## Method

Coupled-channels calculations were performed for ¹⁶O + ¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁶O + ¹⁸⁶W, and ¹⁶O + ²³⁸U fusion reactions at sub-barrier energies, treating the projectile as inert and the target as axially symmetric with deformation expanded up to β₆. The no-Coriolis approximation and sudden tunneling approximation were employed, reducing the problem to decoupled eigen-channels for fixed target orientations; fusion cross sections were obtained by averaging tunneling probabilities over orientation angles and summing over partial waves. Deformation parameters and nuclear potential parameters were determined by χ² fitting to experimental fusion excitation functions and barrier distributions.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵⁴Sm, optimum deformation parameters from fusion-only analysis: β₂ = 0.322, β₄ = 0.027, β₆ = 0.027; after including octupole vibration: β₂ = 0.314, β₄ = 0.011, β₆ = −0.016
- For ¹⁸⁶W, optimum deformation parameters from fusion-only analysis: β₂ = 0.285, β₄ = −0.031, β₆ = 0.027
- For ²³⁸U, optimum deformation parameters from fusion-only analysis: β₂ = 0.289, β₄ = 0.01, β₆ = 0.044; after including octupole vibration: β₂ = 0.279, β₄ = 0.0007, β₆ = −0.024
- E3 transition strength from ground state to 3⁻ state: 24 W.u. for ²³⁸U and 11 W.u. for ¹⁵⁴Sm
- B(E3₀↑) values: 0.100 e²b³ for ¹⁵⁴Sm, 0.575 e²b³ for ²³⁸U
- B(E2↑) for γ band: 4.4 W.u. in ¹⁵⁴Sm, 1.4 W.u. in ¹⁸⁶W, 1.5 W.u. in ²³⁸U; for β band: 1.0 W.u. in ¹⁵⁴Sm, 8.9 W.u. in ¹⁸⁶W, 3.0 W.u. in ²³⁸U
- Q-value for two neutron pick-up from ¹⁶O + ²³⁸U to ¹⁸O + ²³⁶U: 0.826 MeV
- Fusion barrier distribution calculated with point difference formula of ΔE = 2 MeV in laboratory energy

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels analyses by Lemasson et al. (1993, 1995) that included only up to β₄ deformation, where noticeable discrepancies remained between experimental and theoretical barrier distributions. The study demonstrates that heavy-ion fusion reactions can serve as a spectroscopic tool for determining higher-order deformation parameters, complementing methods such as inelastic alpha scattering (Aponick et al., 1970), proton scattering (Palladino et al., 1983), neutron scattering (Delaroche et al., 1982), and ground state mass calculations (Atomi et al., 1995). The results enable future work that allows different Coulomb and nuclear deformation parameters to probe differences between charge and matter distributions, particularly relevant for exotic unstable nuclei.
