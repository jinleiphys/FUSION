# Zeros of 6j Symbols:Atoms:Nuclei and Bosons

> The same 6j symbols that explains the absence of a certain atomic state in LS coupling also explain the absence of certain states for a system of bosons and also for certain nuclear states in jj coupling.

## Key claim

The same vanishing 6j symbols that explain the absence of certain atomic LS-coupling states (e.g., no quartet \(^{4}\)D state for three g electrons) also explain the absence of specific states for three spinless bosons and for three identical fermions in jj coupling, but the conditions on the angular momentum (even L, odd L, or half-integer j) differ across these systems.

## Method

The authors analyze the non-existence of certain states for three identical fermions in LS coupling (electrons or nucleons), three spinless bosons in a single L shell, and three identical fermions in jj coupling by constructing antisymmetric (fermions) or symmetric (bosons) wave functions via Racah algebra. They show that the vanishing of specific 6j symbols (e.g., Eq. 2 and Eq. 3) is equivalent to the absence of these states, and they also provide alternative m-state counting arguments that do not rely on 6j symbols. Regge symmetries are used to connect different vanishing 6j relations, and the work extends to vanishing 9j symbols for four-fermion systems and partial dynamical symmetries.

## Key numbers

* For three electrons in the g\(^{3}\) configuration (LS coupling), there is no quartet state \(^{4}\)D; this corresponds to vanishing 6j symbols \(\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} 4 & 2 & 5\\ 4 & 4 & 3 \end{array}\right\} =0\) and \(\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} 4 & 8 & 7\\ 4 & 4 & 5 \end{array}\right\} =0\).
* For three spinless bosons with odd L in an L\(^{3}\) configuration, there is no J=2 state; this follows from the vanishing 6j \(\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} L & L & L-1\\ L & 2 & L+1 \end{array}\right\} =0\) (Eq. 2 for odd L).
* For three identical fermions in a single j shell (jj coupling), there is no state with J = J\(_{\text{max}}\) - 1, where J\(_{\text{max}} = 3j-3\); this leads to the vanishing 6j \(\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} j & (3j-4) & (2j-1)\\ j & j & (2j-3) \end{array}\right\} =0\) for half-integer j > 3/2 (Eq. 3).
* For three identical fermions in a single j shell, there are no J=1/2 states; this yields the relations \(1+2(2j+2)\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} j & j & (j+1/2)\\ j & 1/2 & (j+1/2) \end{array}\right\} =0\) (if j+1/2 even) and \(1+4j\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} j & j & (j-1/2)\\ j & 1/2 & (j-1/2) \end{array}\right\} =0\) (if j+1/2 odd).
* For four identical fermions in a j shell, a vanishing 9j symbol is found: \(\left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} j & j & (2j-1)\\ j & j & (2j-1)\\ (2j-1) & (2j-3) & (4j-4) \end{array}\right\} =0\).
* For three nucleons in the g\(_{9/2}\) shell with two protons and one neutron, degenerate states with J\(_{p}\)=9, J\(_{n}\)=9/2 and total J = 19/2, 23/2, 25/2 (all T=1/2) are predicted.
* For four nucleons in the g\(_{9/2}\) shell with two protons and two neutrons, degenerate sets include (J\(_{p}\)=8, J\(_{n}\)=8) with J=14,16 (T=0) and (J\(_{p}\)=8, J\(_{n}\)=6) with J=11,13,14 (T=0).

## Context

This work unifies and extends prior results on missing states across atomic, nuclear, and bosonic systems. It builds on the atomic work of Judd and Li (1989) for three electrons in LS coupling, the nuclear jj-coupling results of Talmi (1993) for three identical fermions, and the boson studies of Bayman and Lande (1966). The paper shows that the same 6j vanishing conditions (Eqs. 2 and 3) apply to all three systems with appropriate angular momentum parity, and it uses Regge symmetries (Regge, 1959) to connect seemingly different vanishings. The results enable the identification of partial dynamical symmetries in nuclear systems with mixed proton-neutron configurations, as previously explored by Robinson and Zamick, and provide a unified framework for understanding missing states in many-body systems.
