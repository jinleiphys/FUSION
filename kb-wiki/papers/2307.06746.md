# Chiral magnetovortical instability

> We demonstrate that in a chiral plasma subject to an external magnetic field, the chiral vortical effect can induce a new type of magnetohydrodynamic instability which we refer to as the {\it chiral magnetovortical instability}. This instability arises from the mutual evolution of the magnetic and vortical fields. It can cause a rapid amplification of the magnetic fields by transferring the chirality of the constituent particles to the cross helicity of the plasma.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the chiral vortical effect (CVE) can induce a new magnetohydrodynamic instability, the chiral magnetovortical instability (CMVI), in a chiral plasma subject to an external magnetic field, but only when the CVE conductivity parameter satisfies the condition \(|\xi_\omega| > \sqrt{\rho}\), where \(\rho\) is the mass density.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of the non-relativistic chiral magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) equations, which include the CVE current \(\mathbf{j}_\omega = \xi_\omega \boldsymbol{\omega}\) (where \(\boldsymbol{\omega} = \nabla \times \mathbf{v}\) is the vorticity) alongside the standard Ohmic current. They consider small fluctuations of velocity and magnetic field around a static equilibrium with a uniform background magnetic field \(\mathbf{B}_0\), derive the dispersion relation for plane-wave perturbations, and analyze the conditions for exponential growth of the modes. The evolution of the instability and its saturation are studied using a helicity basis expansion and the chiral anomaly equation.

## Key numbers

* The instability condition is \(|\xi_\omega| > \sqrt{\rho}\), where \(\xi_\omega\) is the CVE conductivity and \(\rho\) is the mass density.
* In the small-wave-number limit (\(|k_z|, |k_\perp| \ll |\mathbf{B}_0|/(\eta|\xi_\omega|)\) and \(|\mathbf{B}_0|/(\eta\sqrt{\rho})\)), the dispersion relation is \(\omega \approx \pm \mathbf{B}_0 \cdot \mathbf{k}/\sqrt{\rho} - i(\eta/2)[1 \pm \xi_\omega/\sqrt{\rho}] k^2 + O(k^4)\).
* In the presence of both CME and CVE, when \(\xi'_\omega < 1\), the unstable wave-number region is enlarged from \(|\mathbf{k}| < \xi_B\) to \(|\mathbf{k}| < \xi_B/(1 - \xi'_\omega)\), where \(\xi_B\) is the CME conductivity and \(\xi'_\omega = \xi_\omega/\sqrt{\rho}\).
* Numerical simulations use a background magnetic field \(|\mathbf{B}'_0| = 5\) (in units of \(1/\eta\)) and initial velocity amplitude \(v_0 = 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral plasma instabilities, previously focused on the chiral magnetic effect (CME) as in the chiral plasma instability or chiral dynamo instability (Joyce 1997, Akamatsu 2013), by demonstrating that the chiral vortical effect can also drive an instability. The CMVI provides a new dynamo mechanism that transfers chirality of constituent particles to cross helicity of the plasma, and it can act together with the previously known cross-helicity dynamo (Yokoi 2013) in turbulent chiral plasmas. The results are relevant for magnetic field evolution in the early Universe, supernovae, neutron stars, quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, and Weyl/Dirac semimetals.
