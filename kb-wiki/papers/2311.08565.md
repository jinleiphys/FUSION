# Pion distribution functions from low-order Mellin moments

> Exploiting an evolution scheme for parton distribution functions (DFs) that is all-orders exact, contemporary lattice-QCD (lQCD) results for low-order Mellin moments of the pion valence quark DF are shown to be mutually consistent. The analysis introduces a means by which key odd moments can be obtained from the even moments in circumstances where only the latter are available. Combining these elements, one arrives at parameter-free lQCD-based predictions for the pointwise behaviour of pion valence, glue, and sea DFs, with sound uncertainty estimates. The behaviour of the pion DFs at large light-front momentum fraction, $x> 0.85$, is found to be consistent with QCD expectations and continuum analyses of pion structure functions, i.e., damping like $(1 -x)^{β_{\rm parton}}$, with $β_{\rm valence} \approx 2.4$, $β_{\rm glue} \approx 3.6$, $β_{\rm sea} \approx 4.6$. It may be possible to test these predictions using data from forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

Using an all-orders exact evolution scheme, the authors demonstrate that contemporary lattice-QCD results for low-order Mellin moments of the pion valence quark distribution function are mutually consistent, and they introduce a method to obtain odd moments from even moments when only the latter are available, enabling parameter-free predictions for the pointwise behavior of pion valence, glue, and sea distribution functions.

## Method

The authors employ an all-orders exact evolution scheme based on an effective charge that, when used to integrate one-loop DGLAP equations, defines a scheme that is renormalization-group consistent and scheme independent. They use this scheme to evolve lattice-QCD Mellin moments from four different studies (Alexandrou 2021, Joo 2019, Sufian 2019, Gao 2022) to a common hadron scale, then exploit a recursion relation that determines odd-order moments from lower-order even moments to complete the Gao 2022 dataset, and finally perform a chi-squared minimization to the moments using a one-parameter functional form for the hadron-scale valence quark distribution function.

## Key numbers

- First moment of the pion valence quark DF from Gao 2022 at 2 GeV: 0.271 (obtained via chi-squared minimization, with chi-squared/degree-of-freedom = 0.95/4 = 0.24)
- Hadron scale values from each lattice study: 0.379 GeV (Alexandrou 2021), 0.351 GeV (Joo 2019), 0.287 GeV (Sufian 2019), 0.381 GeV (Gao 2022); mean 0.350(44) GeV
- Best-fit parameter rho = 0.061 for combined lattice moments, with chi-squared/degree-of-freedom = 4.8/13 = 0.37
- Valence quark DF at 5.2 GeV: exponent alpha = -0.134(62), beta = 2.55(36), gamma = 1.62(77) in the parametrization n0 x^alpha (1-x)^beta (1+gamma x^2)
- Effective large-x exponents over x in [0.85,1.0] at 5.2 GeV: beta_valence = 2.36(31), beta_glue = 3.56(23), beta_sea = 4.62(26)
- Momentum fractions at 5.2 GeV: valence 2*M^1 = 0.438(5), glue M^1 = 0.436(1), sea M^1 = 0.125(1)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cui (2022) which showed that moments from Alexandrou 2021, Joo 2019, and Sufian 2019 are mutually consistent, by incorporating the Gao 2022 dataset which only provides even moments. The results enable parameter-free lattice-QCD-based predictions for the full x-dependence of pion valence, glue, and sea distribution functions that can be tested by forthcoming experiments such as those at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and can also be used via crossing symmetry to develop predictions for pion fragmentation functions as in Xing (2023).
