# Histograms in heavy-quark QCD at finite temperature and density

> We study the phase structure of lattice QCD with heavy quarks at finite temperature and density by a histogram method. We determine the location of the critical point at which the first-order deconfining transition in the heavy-quark limit turns into a crossover at intermediate quark masses through a change of the shape of the histogram under variation of coupling parameters. We estimate the effect of the complex phase factor which causes the sign problem at finite density, and show that, in heavy-quark QCD, the effect is small around the critical point. We determine the critical surface in 2+1 flavor QCD in the heavy-quark region at all values of the chemical potential mu including mu=infty.

## Key claim

The paper determines the critical surface in 2+1 flavor QCD in the heavy-quark region at all values of the chemical potential μ, including μ=∞, by showing that the effect of the complex phase factor (which causes the sign problem at finite density) is small around the critical point, so the critical point location is well approximated by that in the phase-quenched theory.

## Method

The authors use a histogram method combined with multi-point reweighting to study the phase structure of lattice QCD with heavy quarks at finite temperature and density. They evaluate the quark determinant via a hopping parameter expansion to leading order in κ, which introduces dependence on the Polyakov line and its complex phase. The effective potential for the Polyakov line is computed from histograms, and the critical point where the first-order deconfining transition turns into a crossover is identified by the change in shape of the histogram under variation of coupling parameters. The effect of the complex phase factor is estimated using the cumulant expansion method.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in Nf=2 QCD at μ=0 is found at κcp^4 ≈ 0.00002 (κcp ≈ 0.0669) from the Polyakov line effective potential, consistent with the previous result κcp = 0.0658(3)(+4−11) from plaquette analysis.
- The shift of κcp due to next-to-leading order κ^6 terms is about 3% at Nt=4.
- The critical point in phase-quenched Nf=2 QCD is given by κcp^I(μ) = κcp(0) / [cosh(μ/T)]^(1/Nt) with Nt=4.
- The exponent of the average phase factor, -ln⟨e^{iθ̂}⟩, is evaluated at β*=5.69 and κ^4 sinh(μ/T) ≈ 0.00002 and 0.00005, showing that the second-order cumulant dominates.

## Context

This work extends the histogram method previously applied at μ=0 by the WHOT-QCD Collaboration (Ejiri et al., 2007; PRD84_054502) to finite chemical potential in the heavy-quark region. It provides a testing ground for methods to locate the QCD critical point, which is relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments. The results enable determination of the critical surface in 2+1 flavor QCD at all μ, though the authors note this heavy-quark region is different from the light-quark region studied by other groups (e.g., de Forcrand and Philipsen, 2003) and thus not directly relevant to the real-world transition.
