# The propagation of statistical errors in covariant density functional theory: ground state observables and single-particle properties

> Statistical errors in ground state observables and single-particle properties of spherical even-even nuclei and their propagation to the limits of nuclear landscape have been investigated in covariant density functional theory (CDFT) for the first time. In this study we consider only covariant energy density functionals with non-linear density dependency. Statistical errors for binding energies and neutron skins significantly increase on approaching two-neutron drip line. On the contrary, such a trend does not exist for statistical errors in charge radii and two-neutron separation energies. The absolute and relative energies of the single-particle states in the vicinity of the Fermi level are characterized by low statistical errors ($σ(e_i)\sim 0.1$ MeV). Statistical errors in the predictions of spin-orbit splittings are rather small. Statistical errors in physical observables are substantially smaller than related systematic uncertainties. Thus, at the present level of the development of theory, theoretical uncertainties at nuclear limits are dominated by systematic ones. Statistical errors in the description of physical observables related to the ground state and single-particle degrees of freedom are typically substantially lower in CDFT as compared with Skyrme density functional theory. The correlations between the model parameters are studied in detail. The parametric correlations are especially pronounced for the $g_2$ and $g_3$ parameters which are responsible for the density dependence of the model. The accounting of this fact potentially allows to reduce the number of free parameters of non-linear meson coupling model from six to five.

# The propagation of statistical errors in covariant density functional theory: ground state observables and single-particle properties

## Key claim

Statistical errors in ground state observables and single-particle properties of spherical even-even nuclei are substantially smaller than related systematic uncertainties in covariant density functional theory (CDFT), meaning that at the present level of theory development, theoretical uncertainties at nuclear limits are dominated by systematic ones. This conclusion is restricted to covariant energy density functionals with non-linear density dependency and to spherical nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte-Carlo based analysis of statistical errors for non-linear meson coupling covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs), randomly generating parametrizations in the six-dimensional parameter hyperspace and accepting those satisfying the condition χ²_norm(p) ≤ χ²_norm(p₀) + 1. Using the set of accepted functional variations, they calculated mean values and standard deviations of ground state observables and single-particle properties for spherical even-even nuclei in the Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotope chains from proton to neutron drip lines, employing a spherical relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov code with separable finite-range Gogny pairing.

## Key numbers

- Statistical errors for single-particle energies in the vicinity of the Fermi level: σ(e_i) ~ 0.1 MeV
- The NL5(A) functional yields nuclear matter incompressibility K₀ = 318.42 MeV, which is outside the recommended range of 190-270 MeV
- The NL5(C) functional yields K₀ = 260.673 MeV, within the recommended range, with symmetry energy J = 35.925 MeV and slope of symmetry energy L₀ = 112.31 MeV
- The NL5(D) functional (neutron skins removed from fitting) reduces the error in reproduction of binding energies by approximately a factor of 2 compared to NL5(C)
- The NL5(E) functional, using experimental errors for neutron skins, yields χ²_total = 85.049 with χ²_total/degree = 3.698

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of systematic uncertainties in CDFT by Agbemava, Afanasjev, and collaborators (2013-2016) to include statistical errors for the first time in this framework. It contrasts with earlier covariance analysis approaches used in density functional theory by employing a Monte-Carlo method that accounts for non-linear dependencies between parameters, similar to the approach applied to Woods-Saxon potentials by Dobaczewski and collaborators (2017). The finding that statistical errors in CDFT are substantially lower than in Skyrme density functional theory enables more reliable extrapolations to the limits of the nuclear landscape, while the identification of strong correlations between the g₂ and g₃ parameters potentially allows reduction of the non-linear meson coupling model from six to five free parameters.
