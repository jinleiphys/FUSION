# Mixed Harmonic Correlations: Hydrodynamics Predictions at RHIC using Experimental Analysis Techniques

> Correlations of different azimuthal flow harmonics $v_n$ and symmetry planes $Ψ_n$ can add constraints to theoretical models, and probe aspects of the system that are independent of the traditional single-harmonic measurements. Using NeXSPheRIO, a hydrodynamical model which has accurately reproduced a large set of single-harmonic correlations at RHIC, we make predictions of these new observables for 200 A GeV Au+Au Collisions, providing an important baseline for comparison to correlations of flow harmonics, which contain non-trivial information about the initial state. We also point out how to properly compare theoretical calculations to measurements using wide centrality bins and non-trivial event weighting.

## Key claim

The paper provides predictions for mixed-harmonic correlations at RHIC top energy (200 A GeV Au+Au) using the NeXSPheRIO hydrodynamic model, which has already reproduced single-harmonic correlations at RHIC; these predictions serve as a baseline for comparison, but the authors emphasize that proper comparison to data requires careful treatment of experimental analysis techniques such as centrality rebinning and event weighting, which can significantly affect the measured values.

## Method

The authors used NeXSPheRIO, a 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic model with event-by-event initial conditions from NeXus (including initial flow and longitudinal fluctuations at finite baryon chemical potential), to simulate 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions. They computed normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(n,m) and event-plane correlations for charged particles with transverse momentum above 200 MeV and pseudorapidity |η| < 1, using 6 centrality bins from 0-60% and applying the same event-weighting scheme (W = M!/(M-m)!) used by LHC experiments, with centrality rebinning from 1% sub-bins recombined into 10% bins.

## Key numbers

* Predictions are made for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, for charged particles with pT > 200 MeV and |η| < 1
* 6 centrality bins between 0-60% were used in the analysis
* Centrality rebinning was performed by recombining 1% sub-bins into 10% bins using event weighting
* The normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3,2) shows very small dependence on collision energy, as noted from comparison across models
* A strong correlation between v2 and v4 is predicted for peripheral collisions, attributed to non-linear response where v4 ∝ kε4 + k'ε2^2

## Context

This work extends the study of mixed-harmonic correlations, which have been performed at LHC by ALICE (2016) but not yet at RHIC, providing predictions for RHIC's top energy using NeXSPheRIO, a model that has accurately reproduced single-harmonic correlations at RHIC (Gardim 2012). The results are especially relevant because these observables are sensitive to the temperature dependence of η/s(T) (Niemi 2015) and can help discriminate between different initial-state models; the authors also show that the normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(3,2) can be approximated by the corresponding eccentricity correlation εSC(3,2), allowing study without full hydrodynamic simulations.
