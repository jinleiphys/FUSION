# Circumventing the odd particle-number sign problem in the shell model Monte Carlo

> The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method is a powerful method for calculating exactly (up to statistical errors) thermal observables and statistical properties of atomic nuclei. However, its application has been limited by a sign problem at low temperatures that arises from the projection onto odd particle number even for good-sign interactions. Here, we develop a technique - the partition function extrapolation method (PFEM) - to extract the ground-state energy of an odd-mass nucleus from the excitation partition function calculated at temperatures at which this sign problem is moderate. We validate the PFEM in heavy even-mass nuclei and systematically calculate ground-state energies for isotopic chains of heavy odd-mass nuclei. The PFEM can be extended to other finite-size quantum many-body systems.

# Circumventing the odd particle-number sign problem in the shell model Monte Carlo

## Key claim

The partition function extrapolation method (PFEM) can extract ground-state energies of odd-mass nuclei from shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations performed at temperatures where the odd-particle sign problem is moderate, without requiring experimental data or computationally expensive Green's function calculations. The method relies on fitting a parameterized state-density model (the back-shifted Bethe formula or Gilbert-Cameron composite formula) to the excitation partition function, and its accuracy depends on extending SMMC calculations to as large inverse temperatures as possible.

## Method

The PFEM expresses the excitation partition function as the Laplace transform of a parameterized state density model (the back-shifted Bethe formula or, when the back-shift parameter is positive, the Gilbert-Cameron composite formula). In a first step, the saddle-point approximation to this Laplace transform is fitted to SMMC data at moderate temperatures (0.3 to 0.6 MeV) to determine the single-particle level density parameter a and a combined parameter S = E0 - Eref + Delta. In a second step, keeping a and S fixed, the full Laplace transform integral is fitted numerically to SMMC data at lower temperatures (0.2 to 0.4 MeV) to extract the ground-state energy E0, with the back-shift parameter Delta determined from the relation Delta = S - (E0 - Eref).

## Key numbers

- For 154Sm, the PFEM ground-state energy is -295.43 (+0.08, -0.03) MeV, compared to the direct SMMC value of -295.45 ± 0.01 MeV; with restricted temperature range (beta ≈ 3.5-5 MeV-1) the PFEM gives -295.21 (+0.11, -0.11) MeV.
- For 152Sm, the PFEM ground-state energy is -275.77 (+0.05, -0.02) MeV, compared to the direct SMMC value of -275.85 ± 0.02 MeV; with restricted temperature range the PFEM gives -275.55 (+0.10, -0.11) MeV.
- For 150Sm, the PFEM ground-state energy is -255.99 ± 0.14 MeV, compared to the direct SMMC value of -255.77 ± 0.02 MeV; with restricted temperature range the PFEM gives -256.08 ± 0.47 MeV.
- For 148Sm, the PFEM ground-state energy is -235.95 ± 0.27 MeV, compared to the direct SMMC value of -235.66 ± 0.02 MeV; with restricted temperature range the PFEM gives -235.92 ± 0.40 MeV.
- For odd-mass neodymium isotopes, PFEM ground-state energies range from -191.71 (+0.06, -0.03) MeV for 143Nd to -262.92 (+0.11, -0.12) MeV for 151Nd.
- For odd-mass samarium isotopes, PFEM ground-state energies range from -244.83 (+0.18, -0.15) MeV for 149Sm to -304.57 (+0.16, -0.18) MeV for 155Sm.
- The single-particle level density parameter a extracted from PFEM for odd-mass isotopes ranges from 12.97 ± 0.12 MeV-1 (143Nd) to 20.13 ± 0.08 MeV-1 (153Sm).
- The back-shift parameter Delta extracted from PFEM for odd-mass isotopes ranges from -0.03 (+0.02, -0.06) MeV (145Nd) to -0.69 (+0.10, -0.10) MeV (153Sm) and -0.69 (+0.18, -0.16) MeV (155Sm).

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method, previously limited by the odd-particle sign problem at low temperatures that prevented direct determination of ground-state energies in odd-mass nuclei. It provides an alternative to the Green's function method of Mukherjee (2012), which is computationally expensive for heavy nuclei, and to the approach of Ozen (2015) that combined SMMC with experimental data. The PFEM enables systematic calculation of ground-state energies for isotopic chains of heavy odd-mass nuclei without external data, and can be extended to other auxiliary-field Monte Carlo studies of finite-size quantum many-body systems, such as spin-imbalanced cold atomic Fermi gases studied by Gilbreth (2013) and Jensen (2020).
