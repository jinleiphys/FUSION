# $Υ$ and $η_b$ nuclear bound states

> $Υ$ and $η_b$ nuclear bound state energies are calculated for various nuclei neglecting any possible effects of the widths. Essential input for the calculations, namely the medium-modified $B$ and $B^{*}$ meson masses, as well as the density distributions in nuclei, are calculated within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The attractive potentials for the $Υ$ and $η_b$ mesons in nuclei are calculated from the mass shifts of these mesons in nuclear matter in the local density approximation. These potentials originate from the in-medium enhanced $B\overline{B}$ and $BB^{*}$ loops in their respective self energy. After an extensive analysis we conclude that our results suggest that the $Υ$ and $η_b$ mesons should form bound states with all the nuclei considered.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that both the Upsilon and eta_b mesons should form bound states with all nuclei considered (from He-4 to Pb-208), but the specific binding energies depend strongly on the cutoff parameter Lambda_B (varied from 2000 to 6000 MeV), which is an unknown input to the calculation and represents a significant uncertainty.

## Method

The authors first calculated the in-medium masses of B and B* mesons in symmetric nuclear matter using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, then computed the Upsilon and eta_b mass shifts in nuclear matter from the in-medium enhanced BB and BB* loop contributions to their self-energies, respectively, using SU(5)-flavor symmetric effective Lagrangians. These mass shifts were converted into nucleus-dependent potentials via the local density approximation using QMC-calculated nuclear density distributions for eight nuclei, and the Klein-Gordon equation was solved in momentum space to obtain bound state energies.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, the B meson mass shift is (m*_B - m_B) = -61 MeV and the B* meson mass shift is (m*_{B*} - m_{B*}) = -61 MeV
- The Upsilon mass shift at rho_0 varies from -16 to -22 MeV as the cutoff Lambda_B varies from 2000 to 6000 MeV
- The eta_b mass shift at rho_0 varies from -75 to -82 MeV as the cutoff Lambda_B varies from 2000 to 6000 MeV
- The Upsilon-4He 1s bound state energy ranges from -5.6 MeV (Lambda_B=2000) to -10.8 MeV (Lambda_B=6000)
- The eta_b-4He 1s bound state energy ranges from -63.1 MeV (Lambda_B=2000) to -71.5 MeV (Lambda_B=6000)
- The Upsilon-208Pb 1s bound state energy ranges from -15.7 MeV (Lambda_B=2000) to -22.1 MeV (Lambda_B=6000)
- The eta_b-208Pb 1s bound state energy ranges from -74.7 MeV (Lambda_B=2000) to -82.6 MeV (Lambda_B=6000)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nuclear matter study (Zeminiani 2020) to finite nuclei, applying the same mechanism previously used for charmonium (J/Psi and eta_c) binding to nuclei by Krein (2010) and Tsushima (2011), where intermediate state hadrons containing light quarks (B and B* mesons) provide the attractive interaction. The results suggest that experimental searches for bottomonium-nucleus bound states could be feasible at facilities like FAIR or the Electron-Ion Collider, and the authors note that SU(5) flavor symmetry is likely broken in nature, meaning the actual coupling constants may differ from the assumed values.
