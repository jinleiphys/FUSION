# Separation of the monopole contribution to the nuclear Hamiltonian

> It is shown that the nuclear Hamiltonian can be separated rigorously as ${\cal H}={\cal H}_m+{\cal H}_M.$ For sufficiently smooth forces the monopole part ${\cal H}_m $is entirely responsible for Hartree-Fock selfconsistency and hence saturation properties. The multipole part ${\cal H}_M$ contains the ``residual'' terms - pairing, quadrupole, etc. - that will be analyzed in a companion paper. We include a review of the basic results often needed when working with multipole decompositions and average monopole properties.

## Key claim

The nuclear Hamiltonian can be rigorously separated into a monopole part \( \mathcal{H}_m \) and a multipole part \( \mathcal{H}_M \), where only the monopole part is affected by spherical Hartree-Fock variation and is therefore entirely responsible for global saturation properties and single-particle behavior. The authors emphasize that as long as realistic interactions cannot simultaneously satisfy nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, saturation properties, and good spectroscopy, the monopole part must be treated phenomenologically.

## Method

The authors prove the separation property using angular momentum and isospin coupling techniques following the formalism of French (1966). They introduce both the normal (V) representation and the multipole (\(\omega\)) representation of the Hamiltonian, and derive the transformations between them using recoupling through 6j and 9j symbols. The monopole field \(\mathcal{H}_m\) is defined by extracting all \(\lambda=0\) contributions from the multipole representation, which yields operators expressed in terms of number operators \(n_r\) and isospin operators \(T_r\), while \(\mathcal{H}_M\) contains the residual pairing, quadrupole, and other multipole terms.

## Key numbers

- Observed nuclear radii are given as \( r \cong 1.2\,A^{1/3} \) fm, implying average interparticle distances of approximately 2.4 fm.
- Typical monopole matrix elements \( V_{rs} \approx 300 \) keV in the pf shell.
- For a single shell with constant interaction \( V_{rrrr}^\Gamma = 1 \), the correct result \(\sum Z_{rr\Gamma}^+ \cdot Z_{rr\Gamma} = n_r(n_r-1)/2\) is obtained; the naive extraction from \(\omega^{00}\) gives \((D_r-1)/2D_r\) times this value, missing by over a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of French (1966) on multipole decompositions and provides a rigorous foundation for separating the nuclear Hamiltonian into monopole and multipole parts. The monopole part is shown to govern Hartree-Fock self-consistency and saturation, while the multipole part contains the residual interactions (pairing, quadrupole, etc.) to be analyzed in a companion paper (Dufour and Zuker, 1995). The separation enables a phenomenological treatment of the monopole field to enforce saturation properties, while the multipole part can be extracted from realistic interactions, as demonstrated in earlier work by Abzouzi, Caurier, and Zuker (1991) and Caurier, Poves, Zuker, and Martinez-Pinedo (1994). The approach points toward a simple parametrization of the monopole field for nuclear mass studies, taken up in Dufour and Zuker (1995).
