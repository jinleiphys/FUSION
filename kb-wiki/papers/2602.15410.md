# pion-rho Mixing as a mechanism for non-monotonic charged pion behavior in magnetic fields

> We investigate whether magnetic field induced $π-ρ$ mixing can explain the non-monotonic behavior of the charged pion reported in lattice QCD. Using a near-pole effective action derived from the SU(2)$_f$ Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, we show that the lowest Landau level charged pion mixes with the longitudinally polarized charged rho meson, which shares the same quantum numbers in a magnetic background. The resulting level repulsion is strongly amplified by the suppression of the rho wave-function renormalization near the pole. As a consequence, the lowest mixed mode develops a turnover as the magnetic field increases, reproducing the qualitative trend seen on the lattice. Comparison with the direct determinant solution of the Landau-projected kernel shows that the mechanism is robust, although the quantitative location of the maximum remains scheme dependent. These results support $π-ρ$ mixing as an important candidate mechanism for charged meson spectra in strong magnetic fields.

# pion-rho Mixing as a mechanism for non-monotonic charged pion behavior in magnetic fields

## Key claim

Magnetic field induced mixing between the lowest Landau level charged pion and the longitudinally polarized charged rho meson, amplified by suppression of the rho wave-function renormalization near the pole, can explain the non-monotonic magnetic field dependence of the charged pion mass seen in lattice QCD, although the quantitative location of the maximum remains scheme dependent.

## Method

The author uses a near-pole effective action derived from the SU(2)_f Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with quarks minimally coupled to an external magnetic field, employing Landau-level representations for quark propagators and projecting the bosonic action onto lowest Landau level (LLL) meson eigenstates. The diagonal and off-diagonal polarization functions for the charged pion and longitudinal rho channels are computed in the Random Phase Approximation, and the tree-level mixing operator is fixed by matching to the experimental vacuum decay width of rho to pi gamma. The pole condition of the canonically normalized near-pole kernel is solved to obtain mixed eigenmodes, and the results are compared with a direct determinant solution of the full Landau-projected kernel.

## Key numbers

* Unmixed LLL pion energy E_pi(B) and rho energy E_rho(B) both increase monotonically with magnetic field, exhibiting the expected sqrt(eB) behavior at large fields.
* Vacuum wave-function renormalizations: Z_pi(0) = 0.109, Z_rho(0) = 0.413.
* Experimental partial width Gamma_exp(rho^+ -> pi^+ gamma) ≈ 0.068 MeV yields kappa_exp ≈ 0.222 GeV^{-1}.
* Vacuum loop contribution g^loop,vac_{rho pi} = 0.064556 GeV^{-1} at quark mass m_f = 0.4 GeV and pion pole mass q_0 = 0.135 GeV.
* Tree-level mixing contribution fixed by matching: g^tree_{rho pi} = 0.09104 GeV^{-1}.
* The direct determinant solution places the turnover near eB ~ 0.6-0.7 GeV^2, closer to the lattice-indicated region than the near-pole truncation.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy between lattice QCD results (Ding 2020, Ding 2026) showing non-monotonic charged pion mass in magnetic fields and previous model descriptions that could not reproduce this trend. It extends prior studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using NJL-type models (Inagaki 2003, Fayazbakhsh 2014, Coppola 2018), vector meson studies (Liu 2014, Ghosh 2016, Kawaguchi 2015), and recent attempts incorporating magnetic field dependent quark couplings (Avancini 2021), meson mixing (Carlomagno 2022, Coppola 2023), wave-function renormalization (Wen 2023), or inverse magnetic catalysis (Li 2023). The mechanism enables future investigation of mixing with axial-vector modes in the light sector and analogous effects in strange meson systems.
