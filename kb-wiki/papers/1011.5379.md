# Non-equilibrium fluctuations at the QCD phase transition

> We study the chiral phase transition by the non-equilibrium propagation of the sigma field. A quark fluid acts as a heat bath in local thermal equilibrium and evolves fluid dynamically. We allow for dissipative processes and fluctuations since the sigma field is propagated according to a Langevin-type equation of motion. Non-equilibrium fluctuations at the first order phase transition lead to an increase in the intensity of sigma excitations.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium fluctuations at a first-order QCD phase transition produce a much larger increase in the intensity of sigma excitations than at a critical point, but this enhancement depends on the specific dynamics of supercooling and reheating during the expansion of the hot and dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors couple a Langevin-type equation of motion for the sigma field (the chiral order parameter) to a fluid-dynamically expanding quark fluid that acts as a heat bath in local thermal equilibrium. The linear sigma model with constituent quarks is used as the effective theory, with a damping coefficient η = 2.2/fm and white noise satisfying the dissipation-fluctuation theorem. A stochastic source term is introduced into the relativistic fluid-dynamic equations to ensure total energy-momentum conservation of the coupled field-fluid system. The fluid-dynamic equations are solved with a SHASTA code and the field equations with a staggered leap-frog algorithm on a lattice with spacing Δ = 0.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Sigma mass m_σ ≈ 604 MeV, derived from λ² = 20, f_π = 93 MeV, and m_π = 138 MeV.
- Damping coefficient η = 2.2/fm, chosen as independent of temperature and mass.
- Lattice spacing Δ = 0.2 fm used in the numerical solution.
- Initial temperature of the quark fluid: T = 160 MeV.
- For a first-order phase transition, the standard deviation of sigma fluctuations increases to almost 70 MeV around t = 5 fm.
- The average volume over which fluctuations are averaged: V = 0.24 fm³.
- At the first-order transition, the sigma field fluctuates between ⟨σ⟩_{T>T_C} ≈ 10 MeV and ⟨σ⟩_{vac} = f_π = 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-equilibrium models of the chiral phase transition by Mishustin (1998) and Paech (2003) by including a complete treatment of fluctuations and dissipation of the chiral order parameter coupled to a fluid-dynamic expansion. The results show that non-equilibrium effects such as supercooling and reheating produce a second rise in sigma fluctuations at a first-order phase transition, which is absent at a critical point. This suggests that experimental searches for the QCD critical point, as proposed by Stephanov (1998, 1999), must account for the stronger fluctuation signals from a first-order transition and the weakening of critical signals due to non-equilibrium dynamics noted by Berdnikov (1999).
