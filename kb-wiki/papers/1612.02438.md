# Hunting the Gluon Orbital Angular Momentum at the Electron-Ion Collider

> Applying the connection between the parton Wigner distribution and orbital angular momentum (OAM), we investigate the probe of the gluon OAM in hard scattering processes at the planned electron-ion collider. We show that the single longitudinal target-spin asymmetry in the hard diffractive dijet production is very sensitive to the gluon OAM distribution. The associated spin asymmetry leads to a characteristic azimuthal angular correlation of $\sin(ϕ_q -ϕ_Δ)$, where $ϕ_Δ$ and $ϕ_q$ are the azimuthal angles of the proton momentum transfer and the relative transverse momentum between the quark-antiquark pair. This study will enable a first measurement of the gluon OAM in the proton spin sum rule.

## Key claim

The single longitudinal target-spin asymmetry in hard exclusive dijet production at the Electron-Ion Collider is sensitive to the gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distribution, but the asymmetry is a twist-three (power-suppressed) effect of order 1/Q, and its extraction requires measuring the azimuthal correlation sin(φ_q - φ_Δ) where φ_Δ and φ_q are the azimuthal angles of the proton momentum transfer and the relative transverse momentum of the quark-antiquark pair.

## Method

The authors performed a collinear factorization calculation at next-to-leading power (twist-three) for the process ℓ + p → ℓ' + q₁ + q₂ + p', expanding the scattering amplitude in the gluon transverse momentum k_⟂ relative to the hard scale Q. They derived the spin-average cross section from the leading-twist gluon generalized parton distribution (GPD) and the single longitudinal target-spin dependent cross section from the twist-three gluon OAM distribution, using the relation between the gluon Wigner distribution and OAM. The spin asymmetry arises from the interference between leading-twist and twist-three amplitudes, producing a characteristic azimuthal angular correlation sin(φ_q - φ_Δ) proportional to (q_⟂ × Δ_⟂).

## Key numbers

- The spin-average cross section is given by dσ/(dy dQ² dΩ) = σ₀ [ (1-y)|A_L|² + (1+(1-y)²)/2 |A_T|² ], where σ₀ = α_em² α_s² e_q² / (16π² Q² y N_c) × 4ξ² z(1-z) / [(1-ξ²)(q_⟂² + μ²)³] and μ² = z(1-z) Q².
- The spin-dependent cross section is dΔσ/(dy dQ² dΩ) = σ₀ λ_p × 2(1-2z)(q_⟂ × Δ_⟂)/(q_⟂² + μ²) × [ (1-y)A_{fL} + (1+(1-y)²)/2 A_{fT} ], where λ_p is the nucleon longitudinal polarization.
- The asymmetry A_{sin(φ_q-φ_Δ)} is proportional to (1-2z) |q_⟂| |Δ_⟂| / (q_⟂² + μ²), making it a twist-three effect of order 1/Q.
- The gluon helicity contributes about 40% of the proton spin within the kinematic range 0.05 ≤ x ≤ 0.2, based on prior RHIC experiments cited in the paper.

## Context

This work extends the program of measuring parton orbital angular momentum via generalized parton distributions (GPDs) proposed by Ji (1996) and the Wigner distribution formalism developed by Belitsky (2003), Lorce (2011), Hatta (2011), and Ji (2012). It differs from earlier proposals by Courtoy (2013, 2014) by focusing on hard exclusive dijet production at the planned Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) rather than other processes. The results enable a first direct measurement of the gluon OAM contribution to the proton spin sum rule, complementing existing measurements of quark spin (about 30%) and gluon helicity (about 40%) from SLAC, CERN, DESY, JLab, and RHIC. The authors note that future work must include twist-three multi-parton GPDs (three-gluon correlations) and that at small x a dipole framework by Hatta (2016) is more appropriate.
