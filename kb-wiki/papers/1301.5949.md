# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering and Large N(c) QCD

> Nucleon-nucleon scattering observables are discussed in the context of large Nc QCD. As is well known, the baryon spectrum in the large Nc limit exhibits contracted SU(2Nf) spin-flavor sym- metry. This symmetry can be used to derive model-independent relations between proton-proton and proton-neutron total cross sections. These relations are valid in the kinematic regime in which the relative momentum of two nucleons is of order of Nc. In this semiclassical regime the nucleon-nucleon scattering can be described in the time-dependent mean field approximation. These model-independent results are compared to experimental data for spin-independent and polarized total nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key claim

The large \(N_c\) QCD contracted \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor symmetry predicts that the spin-averaged total proton-proton and proton-neutron cross sections are equal at leading order in \(1/N_c\), but this relation is valid only in the kinematic regime where the center-of-mass momentum is of order \(N_c\) (semiclassical regime) and up to corrections of order \(1/N_c\).

## Method

The author uses the time-dependent mean-field theory (TDMFT) framework, valid in the large \(N_c\) limit when the relative momentum of two nucleons is of order \(N_c\), to derive the spin-isospin structure of the total nucleon-nucleon cross section. The derivation employs the Skyrme model as a concrete realization of the contracted \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor symmetry, but the resulting form of the cross section is argued to be model-independent. The inclusive differential cross section is obtained by integrating the baryon current over impact parameters and time, then projecting onto initial two-nucleon states using Wigner D-matrices, yielding a general expression with three unknown functions of velocity. The total cross section is then compared to experimental data for spin-averaged and polarized proton-proton and proton-neutron scattering.

## Key numbers

- Spin-averaged total proton-proton and proton-neutron cross sections are predicted to be equal at leading order in \(1/N_c\); experimentally, these cross sections are approximately \(40\ \text{mb}\) at beam momenta above \(1\ \text{GeV}/c\).
- The predicted relation \(\Delta\sigma_T^{(pp)} = -\Delta\sigma_T^{(np)}\) (transverse polarization) and \(\Delta\sigma_L^{(pp)} = -\Delta\sigma_L^{(np)}\) (longitudinal polarization) are not quantitatively satisfied by data, but the experimental values of \(\Delta\sigma_T\) and \(\Delta\sigma_L\) are consistent with zero (much smaller than the \(40\ \text{mb}\) spin-averaged cross sections), which is qualitatively consistent with the large \(N_c\) prediction that these polarized observables are of the same order as the spin-averaged ones.
- The forward-angle contribution to the total cross section, where the semiclassical approximation breaks down, vanishes as \(1/N_c\) in the large \(N_c\) limit, provided the scattering is not anomalously peaked in the forward direction.

## Context

This work extends the large \(N_c\) QCD analysis of baryon-baryon interactions, building on Witten's (1979) demonstration that baryons in the large \(N_c\) limit are soliton-like configurations and on earlier work by Cohen and Gelman (2012) that derived the spin-flavor structure of the nucleon-nucleon potential. The results enable model-independent tests of large \(N_c\) scaling in nucleon-nucleon scattering, showing that the contracted \(SU(4)\) symmetry relations are well satisfied for spin-averaged cross sections but only qualitatively for polarized observables, suggesting additional dynamical suppression mechanisms beyond the large \(N_c\) expansion.
