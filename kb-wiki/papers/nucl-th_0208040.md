# Further comments on nuclear forces in the chiral limit

> We substantiate our statement that the deuteron remains bound in the chiral limit. We critically discuss recent claims that effective field theory cannot give a definite answer to this question.

## Key claim

The deuteron remains bound in the chiral limit (where the pion mass is zero), and this conclusion is robust under parameter variations; the authors substantiate their original statement against recent claims that effective field theory cannot give a definite answer to this question.

## Method

The authors use chiral effective field theory (EFT) with a modified Weinberg power counting that successfully describes two-, three-, and four-nucleon systems. They critically examine the error analysis and parameter choices of Beane and Savage (2002), recalculating the deuteron binding energy and scattering length in the \(^3S_1\) channel as functions of the pion mass, using the same or larger ranges for the low-energy constants (LECs) \(\bar{D}_{^3S_1}\), \(\bar{d}_{16}\), and \(\bar{d}_{18}\) as those claimed by Beane and Savage (2002). They also check the consistency of the power counting and the treatment of two-pion exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(\eta_{^3S_1} \equiv \bar{D}_{^3S_1} M_\pi^2 / C_0^{^3S_1} \leq 1/8.6\) (not \(1/19\) as claimed by Beane and Savage (2002)); the ratio \(\eta_{^1S_0} \leq 1/4.2\).
* The LECs for the exponential regulator with cutoff \(\Lambda = 560\) MeV: \(C_0^{^1S_0} = -0.143 \times 10^4\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(C_0^{^3S_1} = -0.146 \times 10^4\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The LECs \(C_S = -0.979 \, F_\pi^{-2}\) and \(C_T = -0.003 \, F_\pi^{-2}\).
* The deuteron binding energy in the chiral limit, using the Beane and Savage (2002) ranges for \(\bar{D}_{^3S_1}\) and \(\bar{d}_{16}\) (with \(\eta_{^3S_1} = 1/4.3\) and \(-2.61\) GeV\(^{-2} < \bar{d}_{16} < -0.17\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), is \(B_D^{\rm CL} = 9.6 { {+ 4.4} \atop {-3.2}} { {+ 5.7} \atop {-2.4}}\) MeV.
* Including variation of \(\bar{d}_{18}\) in the range \(-1.54\) GeV\(^{-2} < \bar{d}_{18} < -0.51\) GeV\(^{-2}\) adds an additional uncertainty of \({ {+ 0.6} \atop {-0.4}}\) MeV, giving \(B_D^{\rm CL} = 9.6 { {+ 4.4} \atop {-3.2}} { {+ 5.7} \atop {-2.4}} { {+ 0.6} \atop {-0.4}}\) MeV.
* With \(\eta_{^3S_1} = 1/4.3\) (a factor of two larger than the authors' original value), the deuteron binding energy in the chiral limit varies only between 4 and 20 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' earlier work (Epelbaum, Meissner, Gloeckle, 2002) which first claimed the deuteron is bound in the chiral limit, and directly contradicts the claims of Beane and Savage (2002) that a definite statement cannot be made due to unknown low-energy constants. The authors also critique the coordinate-space power counting and error analysis of Beane and Savage (2002) and of earlier work by Beane, Bedaque, Savage, and van Kolck (2001). This work enables future studies of the quark-mass dependence of nuclear forces using chiral EFT, and provides a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron at unphysical pion masses.
