# The Effect of the Short-Range Correlations on the Generalized Momentum Distribution in Finite Nuclei

> The effect of dynamical short-range correlations on the generalized momentum distribution $n(\vec{p},\vec{Q})$ in the case of $Z=N$, $\ell$-closed shell nuclei is investigated by introducing Jastrow-type correlations in the harmonic-oscillator model. First, a low order approximation is considered and applied to the nucleus $^4$He. Compact analytical expressions are derived and numerical results are presented and the effect of center-of-mass corrections is estimated. Next, an approximation is proposed for $n(\vec{p}, \vec{Q})$ of heavier nuclei, that uses the above correlated $n(\vec{p},\vec{Q})$ of $^4$He. Results are presented for the nucleus $^{16}$O. It is found that the effect of short-range correlations is significant for rather large values of the momenta $p$ and/or $Q$ and should be included, along with center of mass corrections for light nuclei, in a reliable evaluation of $n(\vec{p},\vec{Q})$ in the whole domain of $p$ and $Q$.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) significantly affect the generalized momentum distribution (GMD) of finite nuclei for large values of the momenta \(p\) and/or \(Q\), and must be included along with center-of-mass corrections for light nuclei to obtain a reliable evaluation of \(n(\vec{p},\vec{Q})\) over its full domain; the authors present an exploratory approach using Jastrow-type correlations within a low-order approximation (LOA) rather than a full correlation operator.

## Method

The authors introduce Jastrow-type central correlation functions \(f(r)=1-c e^{-r^2/\beta^2}\) into the harmonic-oscillator independent-particle model for \(Z=N\), \(\ell\)-closed shell nuclei. They apply the low-order approximation (LOA) of the cluster expansion to compute the correlated GMD for \(^4\)He, deriving compact analytical expressions and estimating center-of-mass corrections via a scaling approximation. For heavier nuclei such as \(^{16}\)O, they propose an approximate scheme that uses the correlated GMD of \(^4\)He as a building block.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)He, the harmonic-oscillator parameter \(b = 1.382\) fm reproduces the experimental charge root mean square radius \(\langle r_{\mathrm{ch,exp}}^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 1.67\) fm.
- The correlation function 1G uses parameters \(c = 0.76\) and \(\beta = 0.83\) fm, yielding a wound parameter \(\kappa = 0.018\).
- The correlation function \(g_2\) uses parameters \(c = 1\) and \(\beta = 0.6765\) fm, yielding a wound parameter \(\kappa = 0.016\).
- For \(^{16}\)O, the center-of-mass correction factors are \((1 - 1/A)^{-1} = 16/15\) and \((1 - 1/(A-1)) = 14/15\), indicating smaller CM effects than in \(^4\)He.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the GMD in the independent-particle model for closed-shell nuclei by Papa (2000) and of the GMD in Jastrow-correlated infinite nuclear matter using Fermi-hypernetted-chain procedures by Mavrommatis (1995) and Petraki (2001). The LOA approach follows the methodology of Bohigas and Stringari (1980) and Dal Ri, Stringari, and Bohigas (1982) for one- and two-body density matrices. The results enable more reliable evaluation of final-state interactions in inclusive and exclusive electron- and proton-induced reactions on finite nuclei, and provide a basis for future calculations with realistic correlation operators.
