# Cross-over between different symmetries

## Key claim
The yrast states of even-even vibrational and transitional nuclei are interpreted as a rotating condensate of interacting d-bosons, and the strong octupole correlations in light rare earth and actinide nuclides are interpreted as rotation-induced condensation of interacting f-bosons; however, the paper argues that most nuclei previously described as "octupole deformed" are actually situated in a cross-over region between reflection symmetry and asymmetry, where they behave more like soft anharmonic vibrators than static octupole rotors.

## Method
The author uses the semi-classical tidal wave concept, which describes a condensate of aligned quadrupole phonons (d-bosons) as a quadrupole wave traveling over the nuclear surface with constant angular velocity. Microscopic calculations are performed using the Shell Correction Tilted Axis Cranking (SCTAC) version of the Cranking Model, which calculates energy for a given expectation value of angular momentum by the micro-macro method using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, minimizing energy with respect to deformation parameters β and γ. For octupole correlations, the author analyzes experimental energy differences and angular momentum functions of yrast sequences of both parities to identify the pattern of rotation-induced condensation of octupole phonons (f-bosons).

## Key numbers
- For Nd isotopes: the semi-magic N=82 isotope shows an irregular yrast sequence, while the open shell N=94 isotope shows a rotational sequence, with the cross-over center around N=86 where yrast energies increase approximately linearly over an extended range of I.
- For Ru isotopes: the experimental moment of inertia J(I)=2I/(E(I)-E(I-2)) shows a gradual transition from vibrational behavior for N=54 (center of cross-over) to rotational behavior for N=64 (broken symmetry).
- In 102Pd: B(E2) values increase with I up to the seven-boson yrast state, confirmed by lifetime measurements by Ayangeakaa et al.; the ratio B(E2)/J is independent of I within experimental uncertainties.
- For 220Ra: the energy difference S(I)=E-(I)-(E+(I-1)+E+(I+1)) changes sign at the one-phonon/zero-phonon crossing, then again at the two-phonon/one-phonon crossing near I=24.
- For octupole phonons: the π=- one-phonon band starts with additional 3ħ relative to the π=+ zero-phonon band at the same ω; the π=+ two-phonon band carries additional 6ħ.

## Context
This work extends the tidal wave concept introduced by Frauendorf, Gu, and Sun (2010) for describing quadrupole collectivity in transitional nuclei, and applies it to octupole correlations in light actinides and rare earths. The interpretation contrasts with the conventional view of static octupole deformation (pear-shaped) discussed by Butler and collaborators, instead proposing that nuclei such as 220Ra, Th isotopes, and Ba isotopes are in a cross-over region where rotation-induced condensation of octupole phonons better describes the data. The approach enables future microscopic calculations using parity-projected cranking of a non-axial, reflection-asymmetric mean field, and a Tamm-Dankoff method in the rotating frame currently under development with F. Dönau.
