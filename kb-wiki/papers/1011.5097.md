# Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Description of the Dipole Strength in Neutron-Rich Tin Isotopes

> Low-energy E1 strength in neutron-rich $^{132-164}$Sn isotopes is analyzed in the framework of the Skyrme random phase approximation (RPA) with different Skyrme forces. A double folding procedure is applied to take into account the energy-dependent width effects beyond RPA. All the considered Skyrme forces indicate a soft prolate deformation in the open shell isotopes $^{142-164}$Sn. The integrated E1 strength in the energy region of the pygmy resonance grows with the neutron number. The influence of deformation on the integrated strength near the particle emission thresholds (which is of a keen astrophysical interest) is strictly suppressed by the mutual compensation effect for the branches of the giant dipole resonance. The results obtained are in a good agreement with the previous findings of the relativistic mean field model.

## Key claim

All four Skyrme forces predict a region of prolate-soft isotopes in the open-shell range 142-162Sn, but the deformation effect on the integrated E1 strength near particle emission thresholds is strictly suppressed by mutual compensation between the mu=0 and mu=1 branches of the giant dipole resonance, resulting in a weak negative (depleting) effect rather than a strong positive one.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) calculations based on the Skyrme energy functional, using four representative Skyrme forces (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) for the tin isotope chain 132-166Sn. A double folding procedure was applied to incorporate an energy-dependent width for smoothing effects beyond RPA, with a linear energy dependence above the first emission threshold (Delta_0 = 0.1 MeV, slope a = 1/6). Equilibrium quadrupole deformations were found by minimizing the total HFB energy on a cylindrical coordinate-space grid with 0.7 fm mesh.

## Key numbers

- The integrated E1 strength in the pygmy resonance energy region (5-10 MeV) grows with neutron number across the 132-164Sn chain.
- For 154Sn at equilibrium deformation beta = 0.316, deformation leads to larger cumulative strength only above 10 MeV; below 10 MeV (the astrophysically relevant near-threshold region) deformation causes a weak depletion of strength.
- The double folding procedure gives a width Delta ~ 1 MeV at the GDR peak.
- The photoabsorption cross section is related to the strength function as sigma(E) ~ 0.402 S_E1(E) with S_E1 in e^2 fm^2 and sigma in fm^2.
- The TRK sum rule used for normalization is Sigma_TRK = 60NZ/A mb MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field (RMF) study of Pena-Arteaga (2009) on 132-166Sn to the Skyrme mean-field framework, confirming the predicted region of deformed isotopes 142-162Sn and the growth of pygmy strength with neutron number. The key new finding is that deformation effects on near-threshold E1 strength are suppressed by compensation between GDR branches, contradicting the assertion of a strong positive deformation effect made by Donau (2007) and Rusev (2009). The results are relevant for astrophysical applications involving neutron capture rates in neutron-rich nuclei.
