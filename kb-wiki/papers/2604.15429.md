# Systematic Analytic Regularization in $φ^4$ and Yukawa Theories

> We introduce a novel regularization scheme: Systematic Analytic Regularization (SAR). SAR regularizes a theory at the level of the action by analytically continuing the power of the kinetic operator, ensuring that the theory is formally finite before any terms in the Dyson series are evaluated. We demonstrate that SAR fully and self-consistently regularizes $φ^4$ and Yukawa theories at NLO.

## Key claim
The paper introduces Systematic Analytic Regularization (SAR), which regularizes a quantum field theory at the level of the action by analytically continuing the power of the kinetic operator, ensuring the theory is formally finite before any Dyson series terms are evaluated. The authors demonstrate that SAR fully and self-consistently regularizes φ⁴ and Yukawa theories at next-to-leading order (NLO), but note that confirming the preservation of gauge invariance and supersymmetry will be the subject of future work.

## Method
The authors replace the kinetic operator in the action with a fractional differential operator (−□−M²+iε)^(1+ε/2), defined via its Fourier transform, introducing a UV regulator ε > 0, a renormalization scale μ, and a phase factor e^(−iπε/2) required by unitarity. They compute the superficial degree of divergence for the regularized theory and evaluate the one-loop scalar self-energy and four-point vertex in φ⁴ theory, and the analogous fermion self-energy, scalar self-energy, and Yukawa vertex in Yukawa theory, using the regularized Feynman rules derived from the modified action.

## Key numbers
- In φ⁴ theory, the superficial degree of divergence in the unregularized theory is D = 4 − N_φ, where N_φ is the number of external scalar legs; for N_φ = 0, 2, 4 the diagrams are superficially divergent.
- In the SAR-regularized φ⁴ theory, the superficial degree of divergence becomes D_SAR = 4 − N_φ − ε(2V − ½N_φ), where V is the number of vertices, so that D_SAR ≤ D for ε > 0.
- The regularized scalar propagator in SAR is i μ^ε e^(iπε/2) / (p² − M_r² + iε)^(1+ε/2).
- The one-loop scalar self-energy in SAR is given by (i λ_r μ^ε / 32π²) Γ(ε/2) (M_r²)^(−ε/2) + finite terms, where Γ is the gamma function and the pole at ε = 0 appears as 1/ε.

## Context
This work extends analytic regularization (Speer 1968, Bollini 1964) by applying the analytic continuation of kinetic operators systematically at the level of the action, building on a provisional strategy from Kroll (1966) for modifying vertices to maintain Ward identities. The approach differs from dimensional regularization (t Hooft and Veltman 1972) by keeping spacetime dimensions fixed at 4, avoiding ambiguities with γ⁵ and preserving supersymmetry at leading order, and from denominator regularization (Horowitz 2022) by ensuring the theory is finite before evaluating Feynman diagrams. The method enables a mathematically rigorous, symmetry-preserving regularization scheme that can be applied to gauge theories and supersymmetric theories in future work.
