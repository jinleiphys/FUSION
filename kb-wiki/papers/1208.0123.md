# Phase diagram of dilute nuclear matter: Unconventional pairing and the BCS-BEC crossover

> We report on a comprehensive study of the phase structure of cold, dilute nuclear matter featuring a SD condensate at non-zero isospin asymmetry, within wide ranges of temperatures and densities. We find a rich phase diagram comprising three superfluid phases, namely a Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell phase, the ordinary BCS phase, and a heterogeneous, phase-separated BCS phase, with associated crossovers from the latter two phases to a homogeneous or phase-separated Bose-Einstein condensate of deuterons. The phase diagram contains two tricritical points (one a Lifshitz point), which may degenerate into a single tetracritical point for some degree of isospin asymmetry.

# Phase diagram of dilute nuclear matter: Unconventional pairing and the BCS-BEC crossover

## Key claim
The phase diagram of cold, dilute nuclear matter with isospin asymmetry contains three superfluid phases (BCS, LOFF, and phase-separated BCS) that undergo crossovers to Bose-Einstein condensates of deuterons, with the specific topology of the phase diagram and the location of tricritical points depending on the value of the isospin asymmetry parameter.

## Method
The authors solved the Dyson equation for the Nambu-Gorkov Green's functions self-consistently with the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction in the coupled $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ partial wave, using the Paris potential. The nuclear mean field was modeled by a Skyrme density functional (SkIII and SLy4 parameterizations). The free energy was minimized to determine the ground state among homogeneous BCS, LOFF with nonzero center-of-mass momentum, phase-separated (heterogeneous) BCS, and unpaired phases, with the densities of neutrons and protons obtained from the retarded Green's functions.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Tetracritical point occurs at $\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.22$, $T = 2.85$ MeV, and asymmetry $\alpha_4 = 0.255$
- LOFF phase disappears at $\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.65$ and $T = 0$ for asymmetry $\alpha = 0.62$
- Effective mass mismatch parameter $\delta_m$ lies in the range $0 \le |\delta_m| \le 0.06$, with the upper bound attained for largest asymmetries and densities
- $^1S_0$ Cooper pairing may mix with and eventually dominate the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ pairing at asymptotically small temperatures ($T \le 0.5$ MeV) and large asymmetries

## Context
This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover studies in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter by Lombardo et al. (2001) to include phases with broken spatial symmetry (LOFF) and spatially symmetric but heterogeneous phases. It also extends earlier studies of the nuclear LOFF phase by Sedrakian et al. (2001, 2003, 2009) to the low-density regime, showing that the LOFF phase is succeeded by a heterogeneous phase before the BEC transition. The results are relevant to supernova and hot proto-neutron-star matter at sub-saturation densities, where deuteron fluid is a substantial constituent as shown by Typel et al. (2010) and Hempel et al. (2009).
