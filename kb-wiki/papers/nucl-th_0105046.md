# Dissipative Relativistic Fluid Dynamics for Nuclear Collisions

> In the context of the Müller-Israel-Stewart second-order theory for dissipative fluids due to Grad, we analyze the effects of thermal conduction and viscosity in heavy ion collisions. We contrast the results to those of the first-order theory due to Eckart and to Landau and Lifshitz and to those of perfect (ideal) fluid due to Euler. We study the energy density and entropy density evolution of a pion gas produced in the heavy ion collisions. The truncated version of the second-order theory is used to find the dissipative quantities.

## Key claim

The Müller-Israel-Stewart second-order (causal) theory of dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics, applied to a massless pion gas in a 1+1 Bjorken expansion, predicts a monotonic decrease in energy density and entropy density at early times, whereas the first-order (Eckart/Landau-Lifshitz) theory produces an unphysical peak in those quantities; the paper argues that the second-order theory is therefore the appropriate choice for modeling the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, but notes that the results are based on a truncated version of the transport equations and a simple equation of state.

## Method

The author solves the energy density evolution equation for a massless pion gas in the 1+1 Bjorken hydrodynamic limit, comparing three cases: perfect (Euler) fluid, first-order (Eckart) dissipative theory, and second-order (Müller-Israel-Stewart) dissipative theory with the Maxwell-Cattaneo form of transport equations. The shear viscosity coefficient is taken as η = b T⁻¹ with b = π/8 fπ⁴ (fπ = 93 MeV), and the relaxation time τπ is obtained from β₂ = 3/(4p). The equations are solved analytically for the perfect fluid and first-order cases (assuming constant η) and numerically for the second-order case, with an initial temperature T₀ = 200 MeV and initial proper times τ₀ = 0.7 fm/c and 1.0 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T₀ = 200 MeV used for all calculations.
- Initial proper times considered: τ₀ = 0.7 fm/c and τ₀ = 1.0 fm/c.
- Pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV used in the shear viscosity coefficient η = b T⁻¹ with b = π/8 fπ⁴.
- For a massless pion gas, the equation of state parameters: pressure p = a T⁴ with a = g_h π²/90, g_h = 3 degrees of freedom; energy density ε = 3a T⁴; entropy density s = 4a T³.
- Perfect fluid energy density evolution: ε(τ) = ε(τ₀) [τ/τ₀]⁻⁴/³.
- First-order theory energy density evolution: ε(τ) = {ε(τ₀) - 4η/τ₀} [τ/τ₀]⁻⁴/³ + 4η/τ.

## Context

This work extends the causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics framework of Müller (1967) and Israel and Stewart, based on Grad's 14-moment approximation, to the specific context of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. It contrasts the second-order theory with the first-order theories of Eckart (1940) and Landau and Lifshitz (1959), which are known to produce parabolic, acausal equations (Cattaneo, 1958). The results enable future studies of dissipative effects on observables such as dileptons and photons, and provide a foundation for constructing hydro-molecular dynamic schemes as proposed by Bass and collaborators, as well as comparisons with multi-fluid dynamics approaches.
