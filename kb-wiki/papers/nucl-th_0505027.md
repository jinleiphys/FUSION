# Resonance Absorption and Regeneration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The regeneration of hadronic resonances is discussed for heavy ion collisions at SPS and SIS-300 energies. The time evolutions of Delta, rho and phi resonances are investigated. Special emphasize is put on resonance regeneration after chemical freeze-out. The emission time spectra of experimentally detectable resonances are explored.

# Resonance Absorption and Regeneration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The regeneration of hadronic resonances after chemical freeze-out can significantly alter observable resonance yields, with the Delta resonance fully regaining its abundance within about 6 fm/c after forced decay, while the rho meson does not fully recover and the phi meson falls short by 40% even after 30 fm/c, depending on the specific hadronic cross sections and densities.

## Method
The authors employed the UrQMD transport model, which propagates hadrons, resonances, and strings covariantly, to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies of 30 AGeV and 158 AGeV. They tracked the time evolution of Delta, rho, and phi resonances, and performed a controlled scenario where all resonances were forced to decay at chemical freeze-out (6 fm/c) to isolate the regeneration component. They also tracked direct regeneration events where decay products of a resonance immediately reform the same resonance type in the next interaction.

## Key numbers
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, the number of reconstructable rho mesons (via hadronic decay channels) is suppressed by a factor of 6 compared to all decayed rho mesons (reconstructable via dileptonic decay); for the Delta resonance, the suppression factor is 3.
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at 30 AGeV, the suppression factor for the rho is 7 and for the Delta is 6.
- Most reconstructable Delta and rho resonances at 158 AGeV decay at times greater than or equal to 10 fm/c.
- The direct regeneration probability for the Delta peaks around 10-12 fm/c at 25% (158 AGeV) and 30% (30 AGeV); for the rho, direct regeneration is only 10-15% at both energies and becomes negligible after 12 fm/c.
- When all resonances are forced to decay at 6 fm/c, the phi meson multiplicity falls short of the full calculation by 40% even after an additional 30 fm/c of propagation.

## Context
This work extends earlier transport studies by Bleicher (2002, 2003) that showed chemical freeze-out around 6 fm/c for Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV, and addresses the failure of thermal absorption models by Torrieri (2001) that neglected regeneration. The results help explain experimental observations by the STAR Collaboration (Markert, 2004) of centrality-dependent resonance-to-groundstate ratios, such as the slight increase of the Delta++/p ratio and suppression of K*/K ratios. The findings enable improved modeling of the hadronic stage after chemical freeze-out and suggest that regeneration dynamics must be included to correctly interpret resonance yields from heavy ion collisions.
