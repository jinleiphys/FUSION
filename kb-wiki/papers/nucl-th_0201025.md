# Neutral-current neutrino reactions in the supernova environment

> We study the neutral-current neutrino scattering for four nuclei in the iron region. We evaluate the cross sections for the relevant temperatures during the supernova core collapse and derive Gamow-Teller distributions from large-scale shell-model calculations. We show that the thermal population of the excited states significantly enhances the cross sections at low neutrino energies. Calculations of the outgoing neutrino spectra indicate the prospect of neutrino upscattering at finite temperatures. Both results are particularly notable in even-even nuclei.

# Neutral-current neutrino reactions in the supernova environment

## Key claim
Thermal population of excited states in nuclei significantly enhances neutral-current neutrino scattering cross sections at low neutrino energies (below about 10 MeV), with the effect being most dramatic for even-even nuclei like 56Fe; however, this enhancement is restricted to neutrino energies that cannot excite the Gamow-Teller resonance, and for neutrinos above approximately 15 MeV the temperature effects become negligible.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell-model diagonalization calculations in the complete pf-shell using a modified KB3 residual interaction to derive Gamow-Teller (GT0) strength distributions for four nuclei (56Fe, 59Fe, 59Co, 56Co). They computed inelastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections at finite temperatures by splitting the cross section into a downscattering part (using the Brink hypothesis, where GT distributions built on excited states are the same as for the ground state but shifted by excitation energy) and an upscattering part (backresonances) that accounts for low-lying transitions with large phase space, with thermal weights given by the nuclear partition function.

## Key numbers
- For 56Fe, the cross section averaged between 0 and 5 MeV increases by about a factor of 30 as temperature increases from T=0 to T=1.29 MeV.
- At T=0, the averaged cross section for 59Fe, 59Co, and 56Co is nearly 200 times larger than the one for 56Fe.
- At T=1.29 MeV, the cross section for 59Fe, 59Co, and 56Co increases by about 30% due to upscattering contributions.
- At T=1.29 MeV and E_nu=10 MeV, the cross sections for the two cobalt isotopes increase by about a factor of 5-6 compared to the 56Fe T=0 cross section; at E_nu=15 MeV this increase is reduced to about 30%.
- The GT0 resonance resides around E=10 MeV for all four nuclei studied.
- The threshold for inelastic neutrino scattering on the ground state of even-even 56Fe is approximately 3 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Haxton (1988) and Bruenn and Haxton (1991), who included inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering in supernova simulations using only 56Fe with ground-state cross sections, and by Fuller and Meyer (1991), who studied finite-temperature effects using the independent particle model (which sets the GT threshold too high at about 7 MeV due to spin-orbit splitting). The present calculations use modern shell-model diagonalization (Caurier et al., 1999; Langanke et al., 2000) that accurately reproduces both spectroscopy and GT strength distributions for iron-group nuclei. The results enable improved neutrino transport simulations by suggesting that inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering rates at low energies may be comparable to inelastic neutrino-electron scattering, and that an "average nucleus" approximation may be feasible for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei in collapse simulations.
