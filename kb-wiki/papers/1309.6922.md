# Two-body and Three-body Contacts for Identical Bosons near Unitarity

> In a recent experiment with ultracold trapped Rb-85 atoms, Makotyn et al. have studied a quantum-degenerate Bose gas in the unitary limit where its scattering length is infinitely large. We show that the observed momentum distributions are compatible with a universal relation that expresses the high-momentum tail in terms of the 2-body and the 3-body contacts. We determine the 2- and 3-body contact densities for the unitary Bose gas with number density n to be approximately 20n^{4/3} and 2n^{5/3}, respectively. We also show that the observed atom loss rate is compatible with that from 3-atom inelastic collisions, which gives a contribution proportional to the 3-body contact, but the loss rate is not compatible with that from 2-atom inelastic collisions, which gives a contribution proportional to the 2-body contact. We point out that the contacts could be measured independently by using the virial theorem near and at unitarity.

## Key claim

The observed momentum distributions from the JILA experiment with ultracold trapped Rb-85 atoms are compatible with a universal relation that expresses the high-momentum tail in terms of the 2-body and 3-body contacts, assuming the unitary Bose gas is in a locally equilibrated metastable state and ignoring possible transient or turbulent phenomena.

## Method

The authors fit the universal relation for the tail of the momentum distribution, which includes a log-periodic term from the 3-body contact, to the JILA experimental data for the higher-density cloud (average density 5.5 × 10^12 /cm^3) over the range k = 1.5 k_F to k = 3.0 k_F. They then used the extracted contact densities to predict the momentum distribution for the lower-density cloud (average density 1.6 × 10^12 /cm^3) without adjustable parameters. The atom loss rate was compared separately to the contributions from 2-atom inelastic collisions (proportional to C_2) and 3-atom inelastic collisions (proportional to C_3).

## Key numbers

* 2-body contact density for the unitary Bose gas: C_2 ≈ 20 n^(4/3), with the fitted numerical constant α = 22(1) from the momentum distribution analysis.
* 3-body contact density for the unitary Bose gas: C_3 ≈ 2 n^(5/3), with the fitted numerical constant β = 2.1(1) from the momentum distribution analysis.
* The atom loss time constant τ was 0.63 ± 0.03 ms for the cloud with average density ⟨n⟩ = 5.5 × 10^12 /cm^3.
* The Efimov resonance for Rb-85 was observed at a_- = -759(6) a_0 with inelasticity parameter η_* = 0.057(2) (Wild et al., 2012).
* The minimum in the momentum tail from the 3-body contact is predicted at 0.71 κ_*, which corresponds to 3.9 k_F for the higher-density cloud and 5.8 k_F for the lower-density cloud.

## Context

This work extends the universal relations framework developed by Braaten, Kang, and Platter (2012) for identical bosons, which introduced both the 2-body and 3-body contacts, to experimental data from the JILA group (Makotyn et al., 2013). The analysis demonstrates that the observed scaling violations in the momentum distribution tails are explained by the log-periodic dependence of the 3-body contact term, and that the atom loss rate is consistent with 3-atom inelastic collisions rather than 2-atom inelastic collisions. The results enable future independent measurements of the contacts using the virial theorem (Werner, 2008) or rf spectroscopy, and provide evidence that the JILA experiment studied a locally equilibrated metastable state of the unitary Bose gas.
