# Dilepton and Photon Emission Rates from a Hadronic Gas III

> We extend our early analyses of the dilepton and photon emission rates from a hadronic gas to account for strange mesons using a density expansion. The emission rates are reduced to vacuum correlation functions using three-flavor chiral reduction formulas, and the latters are assessed in terms of empirical data. Using a fire-ball, we compare our results to the low and intermediate mass dilepton data available from CERN. Our results suggest that a baryon free hadronic gas does not account for the excess of low mass dielectrons observed at CERES but do well in accounting for the intermediate dimuons at HELIOS. The same observations apply to the recent low and high $p_t$ dielectron rates from CERES.

## Key claim

A baryon-free hadronic gas (including strange mesons) does not account for the low-mass dielectron excess observed by CERES, but it does account for the intermediate-mass dimuon data from HELIOS-3; the same pattern holds for both low and high transverse momentum dielectron rates from CERES.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier two-flavor chiral reduction formalism to three flavors (SU(3)) to compute dilepton and photon emission rates from a thermalized hadronic gas. The rates are expressed in terms of vacuum correlation functions using three-flavor chiral reduction formulas, with the spectral functions constrained by empirical data from e+e- annihilation, electro-production, and photon-fusion reactions. The thermal emission rates are then integrated over a parametrized fireball evolution (volume expansion and temperature cooling) with detector acceptance cuts to compare with CERES and HELIOS-3 data.

## Key numbers

- The ρ(770) resonance parameters used: mass = 768.5 MeV, decay width = 150.7 MeV, decay constant = 130.67 MeV
- The a1(1260) resonance parameters used: mass = 1230 MeV, decay width = 400 MeV, decay constant = 190 MeV
- The φ(1020) resonance parameters used: mass = 1020 MeV, decay width = 4.43 MeV, decay constant = 79 MeV
- Meson decay constants relative to fπ: fK ≈ 1.24 fπ, fη ≈ 1.32 fπ, fη′ ≈ 0.74 fπ
- Fireball parameters: initial temperature Ti = 170 MeV, final temperature T∞ = 110 MeV, expansion time scale τ = 8 fm/c, freeze-out time tf.o. = 10 fm/c, t0 = 10 fm/c
- For the CERES Pb-Au pT analysis: Ti = 160 MeV, T∞ = 105 MeV, τ = 10 fm/c, tf.o. = 20 fm/c, t0 = 10.8 fm/c
- The three- and four-point correlation function contributions to the rates are negligible (2% correction)
- The hadronic tail parametrization uses q1 = 2 GeV and q̃ = 0.4 GeV for the high-energy vector spectral weight

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor analyses (Lee, Yamagishi, Zahed, 1995-1997) to include strange mesons (kaons, etas, phis) in the hadronic gas emission rates. It builds on the three-flavor chiral reduction formulas developed by Lee and Zahed. The results are compared to the recent analysis by Li and Gale (1998) using effective Lagrangians, finding overall agreement. The paper shows that a baryon-free hadronic gas cannot explain the CERES low-mass dilepton excess, consistent with the authors' earlier finding that sizable nucleon densities are required. The work enables future studies of nonperturbative effects in the partonic phase at the temperatures considered.
