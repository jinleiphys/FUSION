# Pion-Nucleon Scattering and the $π$NN Coupling Constant in the Chiral Color Dielectric Model

> We apply the chiral color dielectric model to low- and medium-energy scattering within the coupled $π$N and $πΔ$ system. Dynamic baryon states in which quarks are confined by the scalar color-dielectric field are constructed in Fock space. Spurious motion of the center of mass is eliminated by constructing momentum eigenstates via a Peierls-Yoccoz projection. The relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved for the complex energies of the T matrix poles, and the pole positions of the nucleon and delta are used to fix the few parameters of the model. The $S$- and $P$-wave phase shifts, the bare N and $Δ$ masses, the renormalized $π$NN and $π$N$Δ$ coupling constants, and the $π$NN and $π$N$Δ$ vertex functions are predicted. nucleon and delta are used to fix the few parameters of the model. The $S$- and $P$-wave phase shifts, the bare N and $Δ$ masses, the renormalized $π$NN and $π$N$Δ$ coupling constants, and the $π$NN and $π$N$Δ$ vertex functions are predicted.

# Pion-Nucleon Scattering and the πNN Coupling Constant in the Chiral Color Dielectric Model

## Key claim

The chiral color dielectric model (CDM) can reproduce low- and medium-energy πN scattering phase shifts and predict renormalized coupling constants, but only after the model's few parameters are fixed by fitting the pole positions of the nucleon and delta; the P11 channel remains problematic and likely requires inclusion of three-body ππN channels and higher-order 1/f^4 diagrams not present in this calculation.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled quark and dielectric field equations in the mean field approximation, then quantize the classical dielectric field using a coherent state and include one-gluon exchange corrections perturbatively to split the nucleon and delta masses. They construct momentum eigenstates via Peierls-Yoccoz projection to remove spurious center-of-mass motion, derive πN and πΔ potentials from the pseudovector quark-pion interaction expanded to order 1/f^2, and iterate these potentials in a relativistic coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation to ensure unitarity and obtain dressed T-matrix poles.

## Key numbers

- Glueball mass fitted to m_gb ≈ 1153 MeV
- Quark-gluon coupling constant α_s ≈ 0.12, much smaller than the MIT bag result α_s ≈ 2.2
- Bag constant B^{1/4} varied between 100 MeV and 150 MeV; quark mass varied between 40 MeV and 120 MeV
- Pion decay constant f = 93 MeV (accepted value) or f = 90 MeV
- Renormalization shifts bare nucleon and delta masses downward by approximately 250 MeV
- Ratio of renormalized coupling constants f_{πNΔ} / f_{πNN} ≈ 1.87, compared to the SU(6) prediction of 1.70 and experimental value of approximately 2
- πNN form factor range R ≈ 0.47 fm for f = 90 MeV and R ≈ 0.48 fm for f = 93 MeV, determined from the k → 0 limit assuming a monopole form

## Context

This work extends the cloudy bag model approach of Thomas, Theberge and Miller (1981) and subsequent studies at TRIUMF by replacing the artificial bag confinement with the more microscopic color dielectric mechanism, which provides smoother wave functions and avoids unphysical oscillations in form factors. The calculation also improves upon earlier CDM studies by including baryon recoil via Peierls-Yoccoz projection and coupling the πN and πΔ channels explicitly. The results enable future inclusion of three-body ππN channels and higher-order 1/f^4 diagrams, which the authors identify as necessary to resolve the persistent difficulty with the P11 channel and the Roper resonance.
