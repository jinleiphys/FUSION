# Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions on the Lattice

> We consider the nucleon-nucleon potential in quenched and partially-quenched QCD. The leading one-meson exchange contribution to the potential is found to fall off exponentially at long-distances, in contrast with the Yukawa-type behaviour found in QCD. This unphysical component of the two-nucleon potential has important implications for the extraction of nuclear properties from lattice simulations.

## Key claim

In quenched and partially-quenched QCD, the leading one-meson exchange contribution to the nucleon-nucleon potential falls off exponentially at long distances rather than with the Yukawa-type behavior of full QCD, which disrupts the fine-tuning between long- and short-distance parts of the potential that is essential for nuclear phenomena such as the shallowly bound deuteron.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order (LO) nucleon-nucleon potential in quenched chiral perturbation theory (Q$\chi$PT) and partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQ$\chi$PT) by evaluating one-meson exchange diagrams including pion and eta exchange, where the eta propagator acquires a double-pole (hairpin) structure due to quenching or partial quenching. They work in the isospin limit for both valence and sea quarks and derive coordinate-space expressions for the potential in both QQCD and PQQCD, analyzing the long-distance asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- In QQCD, the long-distance potential asymptotically behaves as $V(r) \rightarrow -\frac{g_0^2}{16\pi f^2} \mathbf{\sigma}_1\cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}} \mathbf{\sigma}_2\cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}} \, m_\pi e^{-m_\pi r} (M_0^2 - \alpha_\Phi m_\pi^2)$, where $M_0$ and $\alpha_\Phi$ are hairpin parameters characterizing the singlet field dynamics.
- In PQQCD, the long-distance potential asymptotically behaves as $V(r) \rightarrow -\frac{g_0^2}{16\pi f^2} \mathbf{\sigma}_1\cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}} \mathbf{\sigma}_2\cdot \hat{\mathbf{r}} \, m_\pi e^{-m_\pi r} (m_{\eta_j}^2 - m_\pi^2)$, where $m_{\eta_j}$ is the mass of pseudo-Goldstone bosons composed of sea quarks.
- Typical lattice simulations use pion masses $m_\pi^{\rm latt.} \sim 500$ MeV, significantly larger than the physical value.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions (Weinberg 1990, Kaplan-Savage-Wise 1998) into the quenched and partially-quenched regimes relevant for lattice QCD simulations. It builds upon the development of quenched chiral perturbation theory (Sharpe 1990, 1992; Bernard and Golterman 1992) and partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (Sharpe and Shoresh 2000, 2001). The results show that extracting nuclear properties from lattice simulations requires accounting for the unphysical exponential tail of the potential, which complicates the determination of quantities like the deuteron binding energy that depend on the fine-tuning between OPE and short-range contact interactions.
