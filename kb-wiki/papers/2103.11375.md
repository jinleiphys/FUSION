# Functional-renormalization-group approach to classical liquids with short-range repulsion: a scheme without repulsive reference system

> The renormalization-group approaches for classical liquids in previous works require a repulsive reference such as a hard-core one when applied to systems with short-range repulsion. The need for the reference is circumvented here by using a functional renormalization group approach for integrating the hierarchical flow of correlation functions along a path of variable interatomic coupling. We introduce the cavity distribution functions to avoid the appearance of divergent terms and choose a path to reduce the error caused by the decomposition of higher order correlation functions. We demonstrate using an exactly solvable one-dimensional models that the resulting scheme yields accurate thermodynamic properties and interatomic distribution at various densities when compared to integral-equation methods such as the hypernetted chain and the Percus-Yevick equation, even in the case where our hierarchical equations are truncated with the Kirkwood superposition approximation, which is valid for low-density cases.

## Key claim
The authors claim that their functional renormalization group (FRG) approach for classical liquids can treat short-range repulsive forces without requiring a repulsive reference system (such as a hard-core reference), by introducing cavity distribution functions and choosing an appropriate path for the interatomic coupling evolution. The method yields accurate thermodynamic properties and interatomic distributions even when truncated with the Kirkwood superposition approximation (KSA), which is normally valid only at low densities.

## Method
The authors derive a flow equation for the free-energy density functional along a path of variable interatomic coupling \(v_\lambda(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')\), where \(\lambda\) runs from 0 to 1. They perform a vertex expansion to convert the functional flow equation into a hierarchy of flow equations for density correlation functions, then rewrite these in terms of cavity distribution functions \(y^{(m)}_\lambda\) to eliminate divergences from strong short-range repulsion. The hierarchy is truncated at second order using the Kirkwood superposition approximation for the three- and four-particle cavity distribution functions, and the resulting equations are solved numerically for one-dimensional exactly solvable models.

## Key numbers
* For the one-dimensional pure hard-rod system, the FRG approach with KSA truncation yields more accurate predictions for the free energy per particle and peak structure of the pair distribution function than the Percus-Yevick equation, even though PY gives exact solutions for this system.
* The method reproduces the leading-order density expansion for \(\omega_\lambda(\mathbf{r}_1) = \ln y_\lambda(\mathbf{r}_1)\) as \(\omega_\lambda(\mathbf{r}_1) = \rho \int_{\mathbf{r}} f_\lambda(\mathbf{r}_1-\mathbf{r}) f_\lambda(\mathbf{r})\), where \(f_\lambda(\mathbf{r}) = e^{-\beta v_\lambda(\mathbf{r})} - 1\) is the Mayer function.
* The flow equations are demonstrated to work at various densities, with the scheme formulated at the level of KSA already superior to integral-equation methods such as hypernetted chain (HNC) and Percus-Yevick (PY) for one-dimensional models.

## Context
This work extends the functional renormalization group approach to classical liquids, which previously required a repulsive reference system (as in the hierarchical reference theory of Parola and Reatto (1985-2012) and the FRG works of Caillol (2006, 2011) and Lue (2015)). The method avoids the empirical choice of a reference system and its associated dependence on that choice. It enables systematic improvement beyond the Kirkwood superposition approximation by including higher-order flow equations or more accurate closures, and can treat short-range repulsion and long-range attraction on the same footing.
