# The Imaginary Part of the Static Potential in Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Plasma

> Using the gauge/gravity duality we study the imaginary part of the static potential associated to the thermal width in finite temperature strongly coupled anisotropic plasma. We firstly derive the potential for a generic anisotropic background. Then we apply our formulas to a theory where the anisotropy has been generated by a space dependent axion term. We find that using our method there exist a peculiar turning point in the imaginary part of the potential, similar to the one appearing in the real part. The presence of anisotropy leads to decrease of the imaginary potential, where larger decrease happens along the anisotropic direction when the temperature is kept fixed. When the entropy density is fixed, increase happens along the parallel direction while along the transverse plane we observe a decrease. To estimate the thermal width we use an approximate extrapolation beyond the turning point and we find a decrease in presence of the anisotropy, independently of the comparison scheme used.

## Key claim

Using gauge/gravity duality, the authors derive a generic formula for the imaginary part of the static potential in anisotropic strongly coupled plasma and find that anisotropy decreases the imaginary potential when temperature is fixed, with larger decrease along the anisotropic direction; however, when entropy density is fixed, the imaginary potential increases along the parallel direction while decreasing in the transverse plane, and the thermal width decreases in presence of anisotropy regardless of the comparison scheme used.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to study a U-shaped string configuration in a deformed anisotropic finite-temperature N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma, where anisotropy is generated by a space-dependent axion term. The imaginary part of the static potential is obtained by considering fluctuations around the turning point of the string worldsheet near the black hole horizon, using a saddle-point approximation. The analysis is performed separately for small anisotropy (where the background is known analytically to order a^2) and large anisotropy (where numerical solutions of the supergravity equations are required), with the dipole oriented at various angles relative to the anisotropic direction.

## Key numbers

- For a/T=0.3, the critical lengths where the imaginary potential starts to be generated are: L_{0,parallel}T approx 0.26618, L_{0,perp}T approx 0.26626, L_{0,iso}T approx 0.26634
- The maximum lengths before the turning point for a/T=0.3 are: L_{m,parallel}T approx 0.27646, L_{m,perp}T approx 0.27654, L_{m,iso}T approx 0.27664
- The temperature to order a^2 is given by T = u_h/pi + (5 log 2 - 2)/(48 pi u_h) a^2 + O(a^4)
- The entropy density to order a^2 is s = (pi^2 N_c^2 T^3)/2 + (N_c^2 T)/16 a^2 + O(a^4)
- The pressures at small anisotropy satisfy P_parallel less than P_perp, with P_perp = e_1 + a^2 e_2 + O(a^4) and P_parallel = e_1 - a^2 e_2 + O(a^4), where e_1 = (pi^2 N_c^2 T^4)/8 and e_2 = (N_c^2 T^2)/32
- The transition between small and large anisotropy scaling occurs around a/T approx 3.7

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of the imaginary part of the static potential, previously developed for isotropic plasmas by Noronha (2009), to anisotropic strongly coupled plasmas using the top-down anisotropic background of Mateos (2011). The results complement weak-coupling calculations of the imaginary potential in anisotropic plasmas by Dumitru (2009, 2010) and provide a strong-coupling counterpart. The findings enable estimation of the thermal width of heavy quarkonium in anisotropic QGP, relevant for understanding J/psi suppression at RHIC and LHC, and connect to studies of the real part of the static potential in anisotropic plasmas by Giataganas (2012) and Rebhan (2012).
