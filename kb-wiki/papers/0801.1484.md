# Reply to Comment on ``Ab Initio Study of 40-Ca with an Importance Truncated No-Core Shell Model''

> We respond to Comment on our recent letter (Phys.Rev.Lett.99:092501,2007) by Dean et al (arXiv:0709.0449).

# Reply to Comment on “Ab Initio Study of 40-Ca with an Importance Truncated No-Core Shell Model”

## Key claim
The authors defend their importance-truncated no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for ⁴⁰Ca against criticism from Dean et al., asserting that the restriction to 3p3h configurations for large model spaces is not inherent to the method and that explicit inclusion of 4p4h configurations changes the ground-state energy by only about 2%, supporting the reliability of their variational approach.

## Method
The authors performed full 4p4h importance-truncated NCSM calculations for ⁴⁰Ca in a 14ħΩ no-core model space at ħΩ = 24 MeV using the Vlowk interaction. They compare this result to their earlier 3p3h calculation reported in Fig. 5(b) of the original Letter, with an extrapolation uncertainty of typically 1 MeV due to κmin → 0. They also discuss the development of perturbative methods for including up to 6p6h configurations and multi-reference Davidson corrections.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy with 4p4h configurations: E₄ₚ₄ₕ = −471.0 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca in a 14ħΩ model space at ħΩ = 24 MeV using Vlowk.
- Ground-state energy with 3p3h configurations: E₃ₚ₃ₕ = −461.2 MeV for the same system, with an extrapolation uncertainty of typically 1 MeV.
- The difference between 3p3h and 4p4h results is approximately 2%.
- The CCSD(T) ground-state energy for ⁴⁰Ca with Vlowk reported by Hagen et al. is about 30 MeV lower than the authors’ 4p4h result; CCSD is about 20 MeV lower.
- The coupled-cluster method binding energy results for ¹⁶O with the chiral N³LO NN potential from two different references differ by more than 5 MeV.

## Context
This reply addresses a comment by Dean et al. (2007) criticizing the original Letter by Roth and Navratil (2007) on importance-truncated NCSM calculations for ⁴⁰Ca. The authors contrast their variational, translationally invariant approach with the coupled-cluster method (CCM), noting that CCM violates translational invariance and the variational principle. They cite specific discrepancies in CCM results for ⁴He (binding energy uncertainty of several MeV versus 10 keV accuracy in NCSM from Navratil and collaborators, 2004 and 2007) and for ¹⁶O (more than 5 MeV difference between two CCM calculations). The work enables systematic, controlled improvements for medium-heavy nuclei while preserving translational invariance.
