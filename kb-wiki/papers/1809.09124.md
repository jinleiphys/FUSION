# P-wave heavy quarkonium spectrum with next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy

> We compute the heavy quarkonium mass of $l\not= 0$ (angular momentum) states, with otherwise arbitrary quantum numbers, with next-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N$^3$LL) accuracy. This constitutes the first observable in heavy quarkonium for which two orders of the weak-coupling expansion sensitive to the ultrasoft scale are known and the resummation of ultrasoft logarithms is made. We also obtain, for the first time, resummed N$^3$LL expressions for the different fine and hyperfine energy splittings of these states, which are not sensitive to the ultrasoft scale but still require resummation of (hard) logarithms. We do this analysis for the equal and non-equal mass cases. We also study an alternative computational scheme that treats the static potential exactly. We then perform a comprehensive phenomenological analysis: we apply these results to the $n=2$, $l=1$ bottomonium, $B_c$ and charmonium systems and study their convergence.

## Key claim

The authors compute the heavy quarkonium mass of P-wave (l≠0) states with next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N³LL) accuracy, which is the first observable in heavy quarkonium for which two orders of the weak-coupling expansion sensitive to the ultrasoft scale are known and the resummation of ultrasoft logarithms is performed; they also obtain resummed N³LL expressions for fine and hyperfine energy splittings of these states, which are not sensitive to the ultrasoft scale but still require resummation of hard logarithms.

## Method

The authors work within potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), an effective field theory derived from NRQCD, to compute the heavy quarkonium spectrum. They solve the renormalization group equations for the static, 1/m, and 1/m² potentials to obtain resummed expressions at N³LL accuracy, using the known ultrasoft renormalization group analysis of Pineda (2011) and the N³LO fixed-order results of Peset, Pineda, and Segovia (2015). The spin-dependent momentum-dependent potentials are obtained with N³LL accuracy by incorporating the NLL running of the NRQCD Wilson coefficient c_F, while the spin-independent V_r and V_{S²} potentials are included at the leading nonvanishing order for P-wave states. The authors then apply these results to n=2, l=1 bottomonium, B_c, and charmonium systems, and also study an alternative computational scheme that treats the static potential exactly in the Schrödinger equation.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-order N³LO computations of Kiyo et al. (2013, 2014) for equal masses and Peset et al. (2015) for unequal masses by resumming large logarithms to N³LL accuracy for P-wave states. It builds on the ultrasoft renormalization group analysis of Pineda (2011) for the potentials and the NNLO phenomenological analyses of Brambilla et al. (2001, 2002) and Recksiegel and Sumino (2002, 2003) that used Upsilon counting. The results enable precision tests of weak-coupling descriptions for bottomonium, charmonium, and B_c P-wave states, and allow quantification of the impact of logarithmic resummation in the heavy quarkonium spectrum for the first time at this order.
