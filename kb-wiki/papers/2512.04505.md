# A Quarkyonic Quark-Meson Coupling Model for Nuclear and Neutron Matter

> We unite the dual quarkyonic model with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to construct a novel nuclear model based on the quark degrees of freedom, which can cover a wide range of nuclear density from low density to the crossover region. In the model, the relativistic, gaussian quark wavefunction is used to describe the nucleon structure. We first evaluate the energy density, chemical potential, pressure and sound velocity within the ideal Fermi gas picture. In this case, those physical quantities are discontinuous or divergent at the quark saturation density, where quarkyonic phase emerges. To remove such singular behavior, we next introduce an infrared regulator, and combine the dual quarkyonic model and the QMC model to include the nuclear interaction -- we call it the quarkyonic quark-meson coupling (QQMC) model. We then find that the quark saturation density depends strongly on the nucleon size. For example, when $r_p = 0.6\, (0.8)$ fm, where $r_p$ is the root-mean-square radius of proton, the quark saturation density is about $3.6\,(1.5) \times ρ_0$ in symmetric nuclear matter, where $ρ_0$ is the nuclear saturation density. It is notable that the nuclear interaction is quite important to consider physical quantities quantitatively. In fact, the QQMC model can produce the sound velocity which is consistent with that inferred from the observed data of several neutron stars. Furthermore, pressure in symmetric or pure neutron matter deduced from the experiments of heavy-ion collisions at high energy can be explained by the QQMC model as well. We discuss in detail the formulation for the QQMC model and the physical quantities calculated by the model.

## Key claim

The paper unifies the dual quarkyonic (IdylliQ) model with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to construct a new nuclear model (QQMC) that covers densities from low density to the crossover region, but the quark saturation density depends strongly on the nucleon size and the model requires an infrared regulator to remove singular behavior at the quark saturation density that appears in the ideal Fermi gas treatment.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic Gaussian quark wavefunction derived from a scalar-vector harmonic oscillator confinement potential to describe the nucleon structure, then combine the dual quarkyonic model (which implements Pauli blocking at the quark level) with the QMC model (which includes nuclear interactions via scalar and vector meson mean fields). They first evaluate the ideal Fermi gas case (Gaussian quarkyonic or GQ model) where physical quantities become discontinuous at the quark saturation density, then introduce an infrared regulator and incorporate nuclear interactions to construct the full QQMC model. The nucleon momentum distribution in the postsaturation region is separated into a bulk Fermi sea (suppressed by factor 1/N_c^3) and a nucleon shell structure.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter with proton root-mean-square radius r_p = 0.6 fm, the quark saturation density is about 3.6 × ρ_0 (where ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}); for r_p = 0.8 fm, it is about 1.5 × ρ_0
- For case 2 (quark mass m = 300 MeV) with r_p = 0.8 fm, the relativistic calculation gives ρ_sat/ρ_0 ≈ 1.63 in symmetric nuclear matter, compared to about 1.27 in the nonrelativistic limit
- In pure neutron matter with m = 300 MeV and r_p = 0.8 fm, ρ_sat/ρ_0 ≈ 1.26; for r_p = 0.9 fm, ρ_sat/ρ_0 ≈ 0.859 (below nuclear saturation density)
- The quark saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter is larger than in pure neutron matter by about 30%
- The nucleon mass in vacuum is fixed to M_N = 939 MeV, and the experimental proton charge radius is 0.841 fm

## Context

This work extends the ideal dual quarkyonic (IdylliQ) model of Fujimoto et al. (2024) by replacing the Yukawa-type quark momentum distribution with a relativistic Gaussian wavefunction from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model framework developed by Guichon (1988) and Saito (1995, 2007). It also builds on the QMC model's application to neutron stars and the hyperon puzzle by Katayama (2012) and Miyatsu (2011, 2015). The model enables future studies of the equation of state for neutron star matter that include both quark Pauli blocking and nuclear interactions, potentially addressing the hyperon puzzle by shifting hyperon onset to higher densities.
