# Theory of the n=2 levels in muonic deuterium

> The present knowledge of Lamb shift, fine- and hyperfine structure of the $\mathrm{2S}$ and $\mathrm{2P}$ states in muonic deuterium is reviewed in anticipation of the results of a first measurement of several $\mathrm{2S-2P}$ transition frequencies in muonic deuterium ($μ\mathrm{d}$). A term-by-term comparison of all available sources reveals reliable values and uncertainties of the QED and nuclear structure-dependent contributions to the Lamb shift, which are essential for a determination of the deuteron rms charge radius from $μ\mathrm{d}$. Apparent discrepancies between different sources are resolved, in particular for the difficult two-photon exchange contributions. Problematic single-sourced terms are identified which require independent recalculation.

## Key claim
This review compiles and reconciles all available theoretical calculations of the 2S and 2P energy levels in muonic deuterium, resolving discrepancies between different sources for the two-photon exchange contributions, but identifies that the deuteron polarizability contribution (uncertainty 0.020 meV) remains the main theoretical limitation for determining the deuteron rms charge radius from upcoming muonic deuterium measurements.

## Method
The authors performed a term-by-term comparison of all published QED and nuclear-structure-dependent contributions to the Lamb shift, fine structure, and hyperfine structure of the n=2 states in muonic deuterium, using sources from Borie (2014), the Martynenko group (Krutov et al. 2011, Faustov et al. 2014), Jentschura (2011), and Karshenboim et al. (2012). For each contribution, they identified the range of published values, calculated an average with uncertainty as half the spread between maximum and minimum values, and resolved an error in Martynenko's relativistic correction term (item #2) through private communication.

## Key numbers
- Leading-order one-loop electron vacuum polarization (Uehling term): 227.65658 ± 0.00020 meV (average of three sources, accounts for 99.5% of nuclear-structure-independent Lamb shift)
- Deuteron polarizability contribution to Lamb shift: 1.709 ± 0.020 meV (the main theoretical uncertainty, corresponding to 1% relative uncertainty)
- Deuteron rms charge radius from CODATA: 2.1424(21) fm
- Deuteron rms charge radius from electron scattering: 2.130(10) fm
- Deuteron rms charge radius inferred from muonic hydrogen proton radius: 2.12771(22) fm
- Proton rms charge radius from muonic hydrogen: 0.84087(39) fm
- Squared deuteron-proton charge radius difference from 1S-2S isotope shift: 3.82007(65) fm²
- Anticipated experimental accuracy of muonic deuterium 2S-2P transitions: ~1 GHz (≈0.004 meV)
- Item #2 relativistic correction discrepancy: Martynenko value 0.0177 meV differs from other three sources (0.02178 meV) due to an error confirmed by Martynenko

## Context
This work extends the compilation methodology of Antognini et al. (2013) from muonic hydrogen to muonic deuterium, in anticipation of the CREMA collaboration's first measurement of muonic deuterium 2S-2P transitions (Pohl et al. 2010, Antognini et al. 2013). The review resolves discrepancies between Borie (2014), the Martynenko group (Krutov et al. 2011), Jentschura (2011), and Karshenboim et al. (2012), particularly for the two-photon exchange contributions. The resulting theoretical framework will enable a determination of the deuteron rms charge radius independent of the proton radius, providing new data to address the proton radius puzzle.
