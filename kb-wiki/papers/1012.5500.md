# Role of different model ingredients in the exotic cluster-decay of $^{56}Ni^*$

> We present cluster decay studies of $^{56}$Ni$^*$ formed in heavy-ion collisions using different Fermi density and nuclear radius parameters proposed by various authors. Our study reveals that different technical parameters do not alter the transfer structure of fractional yields significantly. The cluster decay half-lives of different clusters lies within $\pm$10% for different Fermi density parameters and nuclear radius, therefore, justify the current set of parameters used in the literature for the calculations of cluster decay.

## Key claim

The cluster decay half-lives of $^{56}$Ni$^*$ vary by only about ±10% across different Fermi density parameters and nuclear radius prescriptions, justifying the current set of parameters used in the literature, though the SM density parameter set produces larger deviations (up to ±13% in PCM and ±9% in UFM).

## Method

The author used the Skyrme Energy Density Model (SEDM) with the SIII Skyrme force to calculate the nuclear interaction potential for cluster decay of $^{56}$Ni$^*$ formed in heavy-ion collisions. Five different Fermi density parameter sets (DV, Ngo, SM, EW, HS) and seven different nuclear radius formulas (Prox77, AW, Prox00, Royer, Ngo, Bass, CW) were tested within both the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) and the Unified Fission Model (UFM). The decay half-lives and fractional yields were computed for clusters ≥ $^{16}$O at a nuclear temperature of 3.0 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cluster decay half-lives for different Fermi density parameters lie within ±10% of each other for most parameter sets; excluding SM parameters, PCM variation is within ±5%, and UFM variation is within ±1.5%.
- For SM Fermi density parameters, PCM half-life variation reaches ±13% and UFM variation reaches ±9%.
- For different nuclear radius formulas, PCM half-life variation lies within ±7% excluding the Bass radius, which gives variation within ±10%.
- The cluster decay constant for $^{16}$O varies by an order of magnitude ten across different Fermi density parameters; SM parameters give decay constants larger by an order of 14.
- The Bass nuclear radius gives cluster decay constant variation of order $10^2$; other radii give the same order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends studies of model parameter sensitivity in nuclear reactions, following earlier work by Puri et al. (1992) on SEDM and by Gupta et al. on the Preformed Cluster Model. It directly addresses the lack of systematic study of Fermi density parameters in cluster decay, complementing prior work by Rashmi et al. on fusion processes where radius effects were found to be more than marginal. The results enable confident use of standard parameter sets in cluster decay calculations for $^{56}$Ni$^*$ and similar compound systems.
