# In-medium modification of the isovector pion-nucleon amplitude

> We study the in-medium modification of the isovector pi N amplitude using a non-linear representation of the sigma model but keeping the scalar degree of freedom. We check that our result does not depend on the representation. We discuss the connection with other approaches based on chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The in-medium enhancement of the isovector pion-nucleon scattering length \(b_1\) is more moderate than earlier proposals, with an increase of about 18% at normal nuclear density, because the pion-loop correction to the Weinberg-Tomozawa amplitude partly cancels the effect of the pion-nucleon sigma term; the result is representation-independent only when both the residue of the pion propagator and the pion-loop correction to the vertex are included.

## Method

The authors use a non-linear representation of the linear sigma model that retains the scalar degree of freedom (the sigma meson) while coupling pions derivatively to nucleons and the scalar field. They compute the s-wave pion self-energy in infinite nuclear matter to first order in density, extract the wave-function renormalization (residue) of the in-medium pion, and separately evaluate the one-pion-loop correction to the Weinberg-Tomozawa (isovector) vertex. The in-medium isovector scattering length \(b_1^*\) is then obtained as the product of the residue and the vertex correction, and the result is checked for independence of the field-representation parameter \(\alpha\).

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density \(\rho_0\), the scalar-field contribution to the sigma term gives \(\Sigma_N^{(s)}\rho/(f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2) \simeq 0.21\,\rho/\rho_0\) and the pion-cloud contribution gives \(\Sigma_N^{(\pi)}\rho/(f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2) \simeq 0.18\,\rho/\rho_0\).
- The resulting in-medium enhancement of the isovector scattering length is \(b_1^*/b_1 \simeq 1 + 0.18\,\rho/\rho_0\) (i.e., 18% at \(\rho_0\)).
- The leading non-analytical contribution to the pion-cloud sigma term is \((\Sigma_N^{(\pi)})^{(LNAC)} = -22\) MeV.
- The full pion-nucleon sigma term is \(\sigma_N \simeq +45\)–\(50\) MeV.
- The scalar-field contribution to the sigma term is \(\Sigma_N^{(s)} \simeq 28\) MeV for a sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 800\) MeV.
- The double-scattering correction to \(b_1\) from two correlated nucleons is a decrease of \(\simeq 5\%\) at normal density.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium pion-nucleon amplitudes motivated by experiments on deeply bound pionic states, particularly the charge-exchange scattering length \(b_1\) fitted by Geissmann (2002) and others. It contrasts with the original proposal by Weise (2001), which predicted a larger enhancement (coefficient \(\sim 0.35\)–\(0.40\)) by ignoring the pion-loop correction to the vertex, and with chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kolomeitsev, Kaiser, and Weise (2002) and by Park, Jung, and Min (2001), which the authors argue use only the leading non-analytical part of the pion loop rather than the full pion density. The result enables a more complete comparison with pionic-atom data and raises the question of whether higher-order density effects or many-body dressing of the pion cloud can reconcile theory with the larger enhancement suggested by fits.
