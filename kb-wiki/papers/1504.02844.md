# Isospin breaking decay $η(1405) \to f_0(980)π^0 \to 3π$

> There are attempts in the literature to theoretically explain the large breaking of isotopic invariance in the decay $η(1405)$\,$\to$\,$f_0(980)π^0$\,$\to$\,$ 3π$ by the mechanism containing the logarithmic (triangle) singularity, i.e., as being due to the transition $η(1405)\to(K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)π^0\to f_0(980)π^0\to3π$. The corresponding calculations were fulfilled for a hypothetic case of the stable $K^*$ meson. Here, we show that the account of the finite width of the $K^*$ ($Γ_{K^*\to Kπ}\approx50$ MeV) smoothes the logarithmic singularities in the amplitude and results in the suppression of the calculated decay width $η(1405)\to f_0(980) π^0\to3π$ by the factor of $6-8$ as compared with the case of $Γ_{K^*\to Kπ}$\,=\,0. We also analyze the difficulties related with the assumption of the dominance of the $η(1405)\to(K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)\to K\bar Kπ$ decay mechanism and discuss the possible dynamics of the decay $η(1405)\toηππ$. The decisive improvement of the experimental data on the $K\bar K$, $ Kπ$, $ηπ$, and $ππ$ mass spectra in the decay of the resonance structure $η(1405/1475)$ to $K\bar Kπ$ and $ηππ$, and on the shape of the resonance peaks themselves in the $K\bar Kπ$ and $ηππ$ decay channels is necessary for the further establishing the $η(1405)\to3 π$ decay mechanism.

## Key claim
The large isospin breaking in the decay η(1405) → f₀(980)π⁰ → 3π, previously attributed to a mechanism with logarithmic (triangle) singularities assuming a stable K* meson, is suppressed by a factor of 6–8 when the finite width of the K* (Γ_{K*→Kπ} ≈ 50 MeV) is taken into account, smoothing the singularities and reducing the calculated decay width.

## Method
The authors computed the triangle diagram for the decay chain η(1405) → (K*K̅ + K̅*K) → (K⁺K⁻ + K⁰K̅⁰)π⁰ → f₀(980)π⁰ → 3π, using the spectral Källén-Lehmann representation for the unstable K* meson propagator to incorporate its finite width. They evaluated the amplitude numerically, integrating over the K* spectral density, and compared the resulting decay width and π⁺π⁻ mass spectra to the case of a stable K*. The ratio of the averaged widths for the isospin-breaking decay to the allowed K̅Kπ decay was then compared to BESIII data.

## Key numbers
- The finite width of the K* meson, Γ_{K*→Kπ} ≈ 50 MeV, suppresses the calculated decay width η(1405) → f₀(980)π⁰ → 3π by a factor of 6–8 compared to the case of Γ_{K*→Kπ} = 0.
- The ratio of the averaged widths, R = ⟨Γ_{ι→π⁺π⁻π⁰}⟩ / ⟨Γ_{ι→K̅Kπ}⟩, is approximately 4 × 10⁻³ over the region 1.400 GeV < √s₁ < 1.425 GeV.
- Using this ratio, the predicted branching ratio BR(J/ψ → γι → γf₀(980)π⁰ → γπ⁺π⁻π⁰) ≈ 1.12 × 10⁻⁵, which agrees with the BESIII measured value of (1.50 ± 0.11 ± 0.11) × 10⁻⁵.
- The BESIII mass and width of the η(1405) peak in the π⁺π⁻π⁰ channel are 1409.0 ± 1.7 MeV and 48.3 ± 5.2 MeV, respectively.
- The branching ratio ratio BR(J/ψ → γη(1405) → γf₀(980)π⁰ → γπ⁺π⁻π⁰) / BR(J/ψ → γη(1405/1475) → γK̅Kπ) = (0.53 ± 0.13)%.
- The mixing parameter ξ_{af} = Γ_{a⁰₀→f₀→π⁺π⁻} / Γ_{a⁰₀→ηπ⁰} = (0.31 ± 0.16 ± 0.143)% from BESIII.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical analysis of isospin breaking in η(1405) decays, previously studied by Wu et al. (2012), Albaladejo et al. (2012), and Wang et al. (2013), who used a stable K* approximation leading to logarithmic singularities. The authors show that including the K* width (Γ_{K*→Kπ} ≈ 50 MeV) reduces the predicted width by a factor of 6–8, bringing it into agreement with BESIII data (Ab1, Ab2). The paper also discusses the difficulties of assuming dominance of the K*K̅ mechanism for η(1405/1475) → K̅Kπ and the possible role of a⁰₀(980)–f₀(980) mixing (Achasov et al., 1979), which alone underestimates the observed rate by about a factor of 30. Improved experimental data on mass spectra in the η(1405/1475) decays are needed to further establish the decay mechanism.
