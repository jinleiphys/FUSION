# A Problem in the Statistical Description of Beta-Delayed Neutron Emission

> Reaction measurements on fission products are being planned at both Argonne National Lab and at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams. These indirect experiments produce specific short-lived nuclei via beta decay, and the subsequent neutron and gamma emission are studied. Some initial experiments found a surprising overabundance of gamma emission, which theory has yet to explain. To remedy this, we are developing an integrated nuclear data workflow that connects advanced nuclear shell model codes for describing the beta decay with a contemporary nuclear reaction model code.

# A Problem in the Statistical Description of Beta-Delayed Neutron Emission

## Key claim
The statistical Hauser-Feshbach description of beta-delayed neutron emission significantly under-predicts the intensity of emitted photons in some total absorption gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments, and this discrepancy may be explained by one of three hypotheses: an enhanced photon-decay strength function, stronger forbidden beta decay blocking high-angular-momentum neutron emission, or non-statistical selectivity of states populated by beta decay that prevents compound nucleus formation.

## Method
The authors develop an integrated nuclear data workflow connecting advanced nuclear shell model calculations with Hauser-Feshbach reaction modeling. They implement an importance truncation scheme in a code called PANASH (proton and neutron approximate shell-model), which constructs basis states by coupling eigenstates of the proton-only and neutron-only Hamiltonians and truncates based on excitation energy thresholds. The method computes energy level spectra, beta transition strengths, photon strength functions, and nuclear level densities from the same wave functions for use as input to statistical reaction codes.

## Key numbers
- For $^{70}$Ge in the $fpg$ model space with the JUN45 interaction, the PANASH truncation with $(E^{(p)})^2+(E^{(n)})^2 < 8.5$ MeV yields a maximum model space dimension of $10^4$, using 217 of 701 proton components and 1190 of 47722 neutron components.
- For $^{78}$Ge in the $pf$-shell with the JUN45 interaction, taking 20 percent of proton and neutron eigenstates yields a maximum basis dimension of 7,000, which is only 4 percent relative to the untruncated dimension of 170,000.
- The Gamow-Teller quenching factor used for beta-decay log$ft$ values is $q=0.684$, taken from Kumar (2016).
- For the M1 strength function calculation, the standard magnetic moment operator uses proton spin and orbital couplings $g_s=5.586$, $g_l=1$; neutron couplings $g_s=-3.826$, $g_l=0$; and a quenching factor of $q=0.7$.
- The untruncated full configuration interaction dimension for $^{94}$Rb is estimated to be $O(10^{13})$, while the proton/neutron subspaces are $O(10^{7})$.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing challenge of describing beta-delayed neutron emission statistically, which has been debated since the 1970s with the proposal of selectivity by Hardy (1974) and the Pandemonium effect identified by Hardy (1977). The authors aim to resolve discrepancies found in total absorption gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments such as those reported by PhysRevC.95.024320, where statistical models under-predict gamma emission. The approach builds on prior efforts combining quasiparticle random-phase approximation descriptions of beta decay with Hauser-Feshbach calculations (PhysRevC.78.054601, Hall 2021) and recent shell model work by Grzywacz (2022), and enables improved indirect cross section measurements such as the beta-Oslo method of PhysRevLett.113.232502.
