# Dibaryons as carriers of strong internucleon interactions and a basis for nuclear physics based on QCD

> New concept of intermediate- and short-range nuclear force proposed by the authors a few years ago is discussed briefly. The general concept is based on an assumption on generation of the dressed dibaryon in intermediate state in $NN$ interaction. This new mechanism has been shown to lead not only to numerous new predictions in hadronic physics but also should be responsible for a large portion of nuclear binding energy and properties of nuclear wavefunctions at high momenta.

## Key claim
The authors argue that the intermediate- and short-range nuclear force is dominated by an s-channel mechanism involving a dressed dibaryon (a six-quark bag surrounded by mesonic fields), rather than the traditional t-channel meson-exchange picture. They claim this dibaryon-based approach can resolve fundamental inconsistencies in conventional one-boson-exchange (OBE) models, particularly the failure to reproduce intermediate-range attraction and the mismatch of cutoff parameters between elastic and inelastic scattering.

## Method
The authors developed a microscopic quark-meson model for a dressed six-quark bag as an intermediate state in NN interactions, replacing conventional Yukawa-like meson exchange at ranges below 1 fm. They constructed a potential model for NN interaction that fits phase shifts at lower partial waves up to 1 GeV and higher, and reformulated the approach using a fully covariant effective field theory (EFT) with non-local Lagrangian densities describing NN to dibaryon transitions and dibaryon dressing by meson clouds. The Dyson equation for dibaryon propagators is solved using a basis of relativistic (Dirac) oscillators, yielding an analytical expression for the relativistic NN potential that includes an imaginary part for meson production.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff parameters in OBE-based NN models (Λ_mNN ~ 1.5–1.7 GeV/c) are 2–3 times higher than values needed to describe π± production in pp collisions or π-meson absorption in d, ³He, etc.
- The renormalized σ-meson mass around the dibaryon bag is estimated at m_σ ~ 350–380 MeV, compared to the free σ-meson mass of ~550 MeV.
- In 3N calculations, the new three-body force gives at least half of the total nuclear binding energy.
- The total weight of the dibaryon component in the 3N wavefunction is as large as 10% or higher.
- The potential model fits NN phase shifts at lower partial waves until 1 GeV and higher.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kukulin and Pomerantsev, ~2000) that dibaryons are fundamental degrees of freedom in nuclear physics, contradicting the traditional OBE-based approach that relies on scalar σ-meson exchange or t-channel two-pion exchange. The approach is motivated by Jaffe's earlier QCD-based argument that multi-quark bags are not forbidden by QCD, and by cumulative experiments showing short-range correlations incompatible with traditional force models. The dibaryon model enables new predictions for hadronic processes including meson production in pp, pd collisions at 1–5 GeV, electro- and photoproduction processes like d(γ,2π⁰), and two-nucleon disintegration reactions such as ³He(e,e'pp) and A(γ,pp).
