# Analytic Bjorken flow in one-dimensional relativistic magnetohydrodynamics

> In the initial stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, strong magnetic fields appear due to the large velocity of the colliding charges. The evolution of these fields appears as a novel and intriguing feature in the fluid-dynamical description of heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we study analytically the one-dimensional, longitudinally boost-invariant motion of an ideal fluid in the presence of a transverse magnetic field. Interestingly, we find that, in the limit of ideal magnetohydrodynamics, i.e., for infinite conductivity, and irrespective of the strength of the initial magnetization, the decay of the fluid energy density $e$ with proper time $τ$ is the same as for the time-honored "Bjorken flow" without magnetic field. Furthermore, when the magnetic field is assumed to decay $\sim τ^{-a}$, where $a$ is an arbitrary number, two classes of analytic solutions can be found depending on whether $a$ is larger or smaller than one. In summary, the analytic solutions presented here highlight that the Bjorken flow is far more general than formerly thought. These solutions can serve both to gain insight on the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions in the presence of strong magnetic fields and as testbeds for numerical codes.

## Key claim

In the limit of ideal magnetohydrodynamics (infinite electrical conductivity), the decay of the fluid energy density with proper time follows the same power law as the standard Bjorken flow without magnetic field, regardless of the initial magnetization strength; this result holds specifically for one-dimensional, longitudinally boost-invariant flow with a transverse magnetic field and an ultrarelativistic equation of state.

## Method

The authors solved the energy-conservation equation derived from the energy-momentum tensor of an ideal magnetized relativistic fluid in Milne coordinates, assuming longitudinal boost-invariance and a transverse magnetic field. For the ideal-MHD case, they combined the energy equation with the flux-freezing condition expressed in terms of entropy density (or energy density) to obtain an evolution equation for the fluid energy density. For the non-ideal case, they imposed a power-law decay of the magnetic field as B ~ tau^{-a} and solved the resulting differential equation analytically for arbitrary a.

## Key numbers

- For ideal MHD (a=1) with an ultrarelativistic equation of state (c_s^2 = 1/3), the fluid energy density decays as e ~ tau^{-4/3}, independent of the initial magnetization sigma_0.
- For a power-law decay with exponent a = 2/3 and c_s^2 = 1/3, the energy density solution is e(tau) = (tau_0/tau)^{4/3} + (sigma_0/3)(tau_0/tau)^{4/3} ln(tau_0/tau).
- For a = 2/3 and sigma_0 = 2, the energy density reaches zero at tau approximately 2.7 fm.
- For a = (1 + c_s^2)/2 (which equals 2/3 for c_s^2 = 1/3), the energy density reaches zero at a finite proper time bar-tau = tau_0 exp(2/[(1 - c_s^2) sigma_0]).
- Typical magnetic fields in Au-Au collisions at top RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) are approximately 10 m_pi^2 ~ 10^19 G, corresponding to an energy density of about 5 GeV/fm^3.
- The initial fluid energy density for such collisions is approximately 10 GeV/fm^3, giving sigma ~ 1.

## Context

This work extends the classic Bjorken flow solution (Bjorken 1983) to include transverse magnetic fields in relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, showing that the ideal-MHD case preserves the original tau^{-4/3} scaling. The power-law decay solutions provide analytic testbeds for numerical MHD codes and offer insight into resistive heating effects when the magnetic field decays faster than in ideal MHD (a > 1), which could correspond to regimes with finite electrical conductivity as studied by Gursoy (2014), Zakharov (2014), and Tuchin (2013). The results also connect to studies of magnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions by Bzdak (2011) and Deng (2012).
