# Generalized Polarizabilities in a Constituent Quark Model

> We discuss low-energy virtual Compton scattering off the proton within the framework of a nonrelativistic constituent quark model. A simple interpretation of the spin-averaged generalized polarizabilities is given in terms of the induced electric polarization (and magnetization). Explicit predictions for the generalized polarizabilities obtained from a multipole expansion are presented for the Isgur-Karl model and are compared with results of various models.

# Generalized Polarizabilities in a Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim
The nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) of Isgur and Karl provides order-of-magnitude estimates for the generalized polarizabilities (GPs) of the proton in low-energy virtual Compton scattering, but the model does not satisfy the relations among the ten GPs that arise from nucleon crossing symmetry combined with charge conjugation, and it generates much smaller values for spin GPs than chiral perturbation theory due to missing pionic degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors calculate the virtual Compton scattering tensor using a nonrelativistic interaction Hamiltonian with seagull and resonance-excitation contributions, employing the Isgur-Karl constituent quark model that includes the Delta(1232) resonance and low-lying negative-parity baryons (D13(1520), S11(1535), S31(1620), S11(1650), S13(1700), D33(1700)). The generalized polarizabilities are extracted via a multipole expansion restricted to first order in the outgoing real photon momentum, using a scheme from Liu et al. (1996) that divides crossed-channel contributions into leading and recoil parts with relativistic energies for intermediate states.

## Key numbers
- At |q| = 0 MeV (real Compton scattering limit): P_LL = 37.0 GeV^-2, P_TT = -0.1 GeV^-2, P_LT = -11.2 GeV^-2
- At |q| = 240 MeV (MIT-Bates kinematics): P_LL = 28.7 GeV^-2, P_TT = -2.8 GeV^-2, P_LT = -8.8 GeV^-2
- At |q| = 600 MeV (MAMI kinematics): P_LL = 9.9 GeV^-2, P_TT = -5.8 GeV^-2, P_LT = -3.2 GeV^-2
- For |q| = 600 MeV and epsilon = 0.62: calculated P_LL - P_TT/epsilon = 19.2 GeV^-2 (experimental value 23.7 ± 2.2 ± 0.6 ± 4.3 GeV^-2); calculated P_LT = -3.2 GeV^-2 (experimental value -5.0 ± 0.8 ± 1.1 ± 1.4 GeV^-2)
- The ordinary electric polarizability alpha = e^2/(4 pi m omega_0^2) for a simple harmonic oscillator model

## Context
This work extends the generalized low-energy theorem (GLET) framework of Guichon et al. (1995) and Scherer (1996) for virtual Compton scattering off the proton, providing explicit predictions from the Isgur-Karl constituent quark model (Isgur, 1978). The results are compared with the linear sigma model (Metz, 1996), heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (Hemmert, 1997), an effective Lagrangian model (Korchin, 1998), and dispersion-relation calculations (Pasquini, 2001). The NRCQM predictions are complementary to chiral approaches that emphasize pionic degrees of freedom, and the calculated structure functions P_LL - P_TT/epsilon and P_LT at MAMI kinematics (|q| = 600 MeV) are compared with experimental data from Roche (2000). The work enables future improvements incorporating relativity, gauge invariance, and chiral symmetry.
