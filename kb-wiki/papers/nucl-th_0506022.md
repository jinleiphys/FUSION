# Giant resonances in exotic spherical nuclei within the RPA approach with the Gogny force

> Theoretical results for giant resonances in the three doubly magic exotic nuclei $^{78}$Ni, $^{100}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn are obtained from Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using the D1S parametrization of the Gogny two-body effective interaction. Special attention is paid to full consistency between the HF field and the RPA particle-hole residual interaction. The results for the exotic nuclei, on average, appear similar to those of stable ones, especially for quadrupole and octupole states. More exotic systems have to be studied in order to confirm such a trend. The low energy of the monopole resonance in $^{78}$Ni suggests that the compression modulus in this neutron rich nucleus is lower than the one of stable ones.

# Giant resonances in exotic spherical nuclei within the RPA approach with the Gogny force

## Key claim
Giant resonance properties in the exotic doubly magic nuclei ⁷⁸Ni, ¹⁰⁰Sn and ¹³²Sn appear similar on average to those of stable nuclei, especially for quadrupole and octupole states, but the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in ⁷⁸Ni is significantly lower than systematics, suggesting a reduced compression modulus in this neutron-rich nucleus. The authors caution that more exotic systems must be studied to confirm this trend.

## Method
Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations were performed using the D1S parametrization of the Gogny two-body effective interaction, with full consistency between the HF mean field and the RPA particle-hole residual interaction. Spherical symmetry was imposed, single-particle wave functions were expanded on harmonic oscillator bases of 15 major shells, and the spin-orbit strength of D1S was reduced from 130 MeV to 115 MeV (denoted D1S') to obtain equivalent binding energies and radii. The RPA equations were solved in matrix form in the particle-hole representation.

## Key numbers
- The N=50 gap in ⁷⁸Ni and ¹⁰⁰Sn and the N=82 gap in ¹³²Sn are of the order of 5 MeV, less than 20% smaller than gaps in stable spherical nuclei with the same neutron numbers.
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energy in ⁷⁸Ni is lowered by approximately 1.5 MeV relative to the empirical 80A⁻¹/³ law.
- The compression modulus in infinite nuclear matter with D1S' is K_nm = 209 MeV, slightly outside the interval 220-235 MeV that explains bulk experimental data.
- The mean isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) energy in ¹⁰⁰Sn is M₁/M₀ = 19.98 MeV, compared to the systematic 79A⁻¹/³ value of 17.02 MeV.
- The mean IVGDR energy in ¹³²Sn is 18.33 MeV, compared to the systematic 79A⁻¹/³ value of 15.52 MeV.
- The mean IVGDR energy in ⁷⁸Ni is 20.31 MeV, compared to the systematic 79A⁻¹/³ value of 18.49 MeV.
- The mean IVGDR energy in ²⁰⁸Pb is 16.50 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 13.43 MeV.
- The EWSR value for the IVGDR in TRK units is 1.59 for ¹⁰⁰Sn, 1.58 for ¹³²Sn, and 1.57 for ⁷⁸Ni.
- The high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) mean energies are 28.16 MeV in ¹⁰⁰Sn, 26.06 MeV in ¹³²Sn, 29.51 MeV in ⁷⁸Ni, and 23.20 MeV in ²⁰⁸Pb, giving E₀ values of 130, 132, 126, and 137 in the systematic E₀A⁻¹/³, compared to the usual estimate of 110A⁻¹/³.
- The spin-orbit part of the residual interaction contributes 8% to ISGMR energies in ⁷⁸Ni and 5% in ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The Coulomb contribution to ISGMR energies is 3% in ²⁰⁸Pb and almost negligible in Ni.
- The first 2⁺ state in ¹³²Sn shows fair agreement with experimental data from OR (2003), with B(E2) values of the same order of magnitude.
- The first 2⁺ state in ⁷⁸Ni is predicted at a comparatively low value, but still higher than the experimental 2.7 MeV in ⁵⁶Ni.

## Context
This work extends previous HF+RPA calculations with the Gogny force for giant resonances in stable doubly closed shell nuclei (Ref. ref2, ref6, ref3) to three exotic doubly magic nuclei. It builds on the D1S parametrization and emphasizes full consistency between the HF field and RPA residual interaction, including spin-orbit and Coulomb terms often omitted in other approaches. The results enable comparisons with empirical A⁻¹/³ systematics and with experimental data for ¹³²Sn from OR (2003) and for ²⁰⁸Pb from Zie (1990) and Youn (2004). The study also relates to work by Colo (2004) on the role of Coulomb and spin-orbit forces in constrained HF calculations with the Skyrme interaction, and to relativistic RPA calculations by Vre (2001) for pygmy dipole resonances. The authors note that coupling to 2-particle-2-hole states, as discussed in npa371 and rmp, would be needed before reliable predictions for the ISGQR in exotic nuclei can be made.
