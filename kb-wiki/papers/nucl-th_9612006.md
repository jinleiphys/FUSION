# A New Nonlinear Liquid Drop Model. Clusters as Solitons on The Nuclear Surface

> By introducing in the hydrodynamic model, i.e. in the hydrodynamic equations and the corresponding boundary conditions, the higher order terms in the deviation of the shape, we obtain in the second order the Korteweg de Vries equation (KdV). The same equation is obtained by introducing in the liquid drop model (LDM), i.e. in the kinetic, surface and Coulomb terms, the higher terms in the second order. The KdV equation has the cnoidal waves as steady-state solutions. These waves could describe the small anharmonic vibrations of spherical nuclei up to the solitary waves. The solitons could describe the preformation of clusters on the nuclear surface. We apply this nonlinear liquid drop model to the alpha formation in heavy nuclei. We find an additional minimum in the total energy of such systems, corresponding to the solitons as clusters on the nuclear surface. By introducing the shell effects we choose this minimum to be degenerated with the ground state. The spectroscopic factor is given by the ratio of the square amplitudes in the two minima.

## Key claim

By including higher-order terms in the deviation of the nuclear shape within the liquid drop model, the authors derive a Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation whose cnoidal wave solutions can describe both small anharmonic vibrations and solitary waves; these solitons are interpreted as preformed clusters on the nuclear surface, and the model is applied to alpha formation in heavy nuclei, where an additional energy minimum corresponding to such solitons is found.

## Method

The authors start from the hydrodynamic description of the nucleus as an incompressible, irrotational, inviscid fluid layer with a free surface and a rigid core, expanding the velocity potential in a power series in the radial deviation from the spherical shape. They introduce the shape function as a traveling wave factorized into a transversal profile and a longitudinal traveling part, then derive the KdV equation from the Euler equation and boundary conditions kept to second order in the deformation. Separately, they expand the liquid drop model Hamiltonian (kinetic, surface, and Coulomb energies) to third order in the shape function and show that the same KdV equation emerges, with soliton solutions given by a sech-squared profile whose amplitude, width, and velocity are determined by the model coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The soliton amplitude is given by \(\eta_0 = \epsilon R_0\), where \(\epsilon\) is the maximum relative deformation and \(R_0\) is the spherical nuclear radius.
* The soliton half-width is \(L(\theta) = \sqrt{12D / (\eta_0 C)}\), where \(D\) and \(C\) are coefficients of the KdV equation depending on the surface tension \(\sigma\), the charge density \(\rho_{el}\), and the layer depth \(h\).
* The tangential velocity of the flow in the direction of motion is \(v_\phi = (2 V R_0 \sin\theta / h)\,\xi + \mathcal{O}_2(\xi)\), with \(V\) the soliton angular velocity and \(h\) the fluid layer depth.
* The surface pressure on the free surface is expanded to second order: \(P|_\Sigma = (\sigma/R_0)(-2\xi - 4\xi^2 - \triangle_\Omega \xi + 3\xi \xi_\theta^2 \cot\theta) + \text{const}\).

## Context

This work extends the traditional liquid drop model of Bohr and Mottelson (linear harmonic vibrations) by incorporating nonlinear terms that yield anharmonic oscillations and solitary waves, addressing the long-standing question of how nucleons spontaneously form isolated clusters on the nuclear surface. It builds on earlier solitonic models for cluster preformation by Ludu, Sandulescu, and Greiner (1995) and provides a collective, hydrodynamic alternative to shell-model approaches with many-body correlations. The results enable a unified description of nuclear surface dynamics from small vibrations to cluster formation, with the spectroscopic factor given by the ratio of squared amplitudes in the two minima (ground state and soliton state).
