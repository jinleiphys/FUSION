# Magnetic Polarisability of Octet Baryons via Lattice QCD

> Drawing on recent advances in lattice-QCD background-field techniques, the magnetic polarisability of octet baryons is calculated from the first principles of QCD. The results are presented in the context of new constituent quark-model calculations providing a framework for understanding the lattice results and a direct comparison with simulation results at unphysical quark masses. Using smeared quark sources, low-lying Laplacian eigenmode projection and final-state Landau mode projection, considerable attention is devoted to ensuring single-state isolation in the lattice correlation functions. We also introduce new weighting methods to reduce the sensitivity to correlation-function fits, averaging over many fits based on merit drawn from the full correlated $χ^2$ of the fits. The techniques are implemented on the $32^3 \times 64$, 2+1-flavour dynamical-fermion lattices provided by the PACS-CS collaboration following the introduction of uniform magnetic fields quantised to the lowest nontrivial values available. After some fine tuning of the constituent quark model parameters, we find the model captures the patterns observed in the lattice QCD results very well, providing important insights into the physics underpinning the magnetic polarisabilities. Finally, comparison with the most recent results from experiment proceeds through an effective field theory formalism which incorporates estimates of finite-volume corrections and small electro-quenching corrections as the results are brought to the physical point. We find excellent agreement with experiment where available, including the proton and neutron polarisabilities.

# Magnetic Polarisability of Octet Baryons via Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The magnetic polarisabilities of all octet baryons are calculated from first-principles lattice QCD, and after fine-tuning constituent quark model parameters, the model captures the patterns observed in the lattice results; excellent agreement with experiment is found for the proton and neutron polarisabilities after applying finite-volume and electro-quenching corrections through an effective field theory formalism.

## Method

The calculation uses the uniform background magnetic-field method on 2+1-flavour dynamical-fermion lattices of size 32^3 x 64 provided by the PACS-CS collaboration, with magnetic fields quantised to the lowest nontrivial values. Smeared quark sources, low-lying Laplacian eigenmode projection at the quark level, and final-state Landau-mode projection at the sink are employed to ensure single-state isolation in the correlation functions. A new weighting method averaging over many fits based on the full correlated chi-squared of the fits is introduced to reduce sensitivity to correlation-function fitting choices.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume: 32^3 x 64, with 2+1-flavour dynamical fermions from the PACS-CS collaboration
- Magnetic fields quantised to the lowest nontrivial values available on the lattice
- Constituent quark model parameters are fine-tuned to match the lattice QCD results at unphysical quark masses
- Proton and neutron polarisabilities show excellent agreement with experimental values after finite-volume and electro-quenching corrections are applied

## Context

This work extends previous lattice-QCD background-field analyses of nucleon polarisabilities (Bignell 2020) to the full octet of baryons, including hyperons. The constituent quark model calculations extend earlier work on nucleon polarisabilities (Capstick 1992, Lipkin 1992, Bhaduri 1998) to the hyperon sector. The results enable future comparisons with experimental measurements of hyperon magnetic polarisabilities and provide a framework for understanding the competing physical effects that generate the pattern of polarisabilities across the octet.
