# Relativistic effects in electromagnetic nuclear responses in the quasi-elastic delta region

> A new non-relativistic expansion in terms of the nucleon's momentum inside nuclear matter of the current for isobar electro-excitation from the nucleon is performed. Being exact with respect to the transferred energy and momentum, this yields new current operators which retain important aspects of relativity not taken into account in the traditional non-relativistic reductions. The transition current thus obtained differs from the leading order of the traditional expansion by simple multiplicative factors. These depend on the momentum and energy transfer and can be easily included together with relativistic kinematics in non-relativistic, many-body models of isobar electro-excitation in nuclei. The merits of the new current are tested by comparing with the unexpanded electromagnetic nuclear responses in the isobar peak computed in a relativistic Fermi gas framework. The sensitivity of the relativistic responses to the isobar's magnetic, electric and Coulomb form factors and the finite width of the isobar is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new non-relativistic expansion of the nucleon-to-delta electro-excitation current that retains the full dependence on energy and momentum transfer, unlike traditional expansions which also expand in those quantities. The resulting current operators differ from the leading-order traditional expansion only by simple multiplicative factors depending on momentum and energy transfer, making them easy to incorporate into existing non-relativistic many-body models.

## Method

The authors perform a systematic expansion of the relativistic gamma-N-delta vertex in powers of the bound nucleon momentum (eta = p/m_N) while keeping the exact dependence on the transferred energy (omega) and momentum (q). They apply this expansion to the magnetic dipole (M1) transition current, which dominates both longitudinal and transverse nuclear responses, and test the resulting "relativized" current by comparing the exact relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) response functions with those computed using the new non-relativistic current combined with relativistic kinematics. The RFG framework uses a delta-hole approach with the full Jones and Scadron vertex including M1, E2, and C2 amplitudes, and the finite width of the delta is included via convolution with a Lorentz distribution.

## Key numbers

- The scaling variable psi_delta is defined as psi_delta^2 = (epsilon_0 - 1)/xi_F, where xi_F = epsilon_F - 1, and the delta response region corresponds to -1 <= psi_delta <= 1
- The energy position of the delta peak occurs at lambda_DeltaP = tau*rho, where tau = kappa^2 - lambda^2 and rho = 1 + (mu_Delta^2 - 1)/(4*tau)
- The E2/M1 ratio at resonance is approximately -3% from polarized photon measurements (Bec97)
- The C2/M1 ratio around -13% has been reported in H(e,e'pi^0)p experiments at Q^2 = 0.13 (GeV/c)^2 (Kal97)
- For a pure M1 transition, the longitudinal response satisfies (1+tau*rho^2)*w_2 - w_1 = 0, so only the w_2 structure function contributes

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-relativistic expansion of the electromagnetic nucleon current (Amaro 1996) and meson-exchange currents (Amaro 1998) to the delta electro-excitation current. It addresses limitations of earlier relativistic calculations by Moniz (1969), which used the Peccei Lagrangian only appropriate for low momentum transfer, and by Wehrberger (1989) and Chanfray (1993), which included only the M1 magnetic transition current without E2 and C2 amplitudes. The new current operators enable non-relativistic many-body calculations to incorporate relativistic effects at high momentum transfers (q ~ 1 GeV/c) where traditional expansions fail, and provide a framework for studying the sensitivity of nuclear responses to the delta's magnetic, electric, and Coulomb form factors as well as its finite width.
