# Light Hadron Production in Proton-Proton Collisions at Different LHC Energies: Measured Data versus a Model

> Experiments involving proton-proton collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 0.9, 2.76 and 7 TeV in Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have produced a vast amount of high-precision data. Here, in this work, we have chosen to analyse the two aspects of the measured data, viz., (i) the $p_T$ -spectra of pions, kaons, proton-antiproton at above-mentioned energies, and (ii) some of their very important ratio-behaviours, in the light of a version of the Sequential Chain Model (SCM). The agreements between the measured data and model-based results are generally found to be modestly satisfactory.

# Light Hadron Production in Proton-Proton Collisions at Different LHC Energies: Measured Data versus a Model

## Key claim

The Sequential Chain Model (SCM) provides a "modestly satisfactory" description of transverse momentum spectra and particle ratios for pions, kaons, and (anti)protons in pp collisions at LHC energies, but the model shows disagreements in the low-pT region and the authors acknowledge that the model "has turned essentially into a mixed one" and that "the power-law part of the equation might not be the only factor for this type of discrepancy."

## Method

The authors apply the Sequential Chain Model (SCM), in which high-energy hadronic interactions are reduced to pion-pion interactions, with protons conceived as p = (π⁺π⁰ϑ) where ϑ is a spectator particle. Inclusive cross-section expressions for π⁻, K⁻, and antiprotons are derived from field-theoretic Feynman diagram techniques using infinite momentum frame tools and impulse approximation, with positive particle distributions obtained by applying multiplicative factors (1+γ p_T) to the negative particle distributions. The model calculations are compared to CMS data at √s_NN = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV and also to PYTHIA-based calculations.

## Key numbers

- The average pion multiplicity is given by ⟨n_π⁺⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_π⁻⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_π⁰⟩_pp ≈ 1.1 s^{1/5}, where s is the square of the center-of-mass energy.
- The average kaon multiplicity is ⟨n_K⁺⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_K⁻⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_K⁰⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_K̅⁰⟩_pp ≈ 5×10⁻² s^{1/4}.
- The average antiproton multiplicity at ultrahigh energies is ⟨n_p̅⟩_pp ≈ ⟨n_p⟩_pp ≈ 2×10⁻² s^{1/4}.
- The K⁻/K⁺ ratio is calculated to be approximately 0.98 at all three energies (√s_NN = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV).
- The γ parameter for π⁺ production is γ^{π⁺} ≈ 0.44, with ⟨p_T⟩_{π⁺} ≈ 0.31 GeV/c.
- The γ parameter for K⁺ production is γ^{K⁺} ≈ 0.082, with ⟨p_T⟩_{K⁺} ≈ 0.36 GeV/c.
- The γ parameter for proton production is γ^p ≈ 0.32, with ⟨p_T⟩_p ≈ 0.50 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Sequential Chain Model approach previously developed by the same authors and collaborators (Guptaroy et al., earlier works) to LHC pp collision data at 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV from the CMS collaboration (CMS, 2012). The model is presented as an alternative to perturbative QCD, which the authors note "is not able to provide detailed predictions of particle production" at low pT, and is compared with PYTHIA-based calculations. The authors acknowledge that the model differs from thermal models and the blast-wave model (Ma, recent paper) and cannot incorporate factors like radial flow or thermal equilibrium, suggesting these as limitations to be addressed in future work.
