# Dibaryons in a constituent quark model

> We investigate the properties of dibaryons containing u and d quarks in the constituent quark model. In constructing the ground state wave function, we choose the spatial part to be fully symmetric and the remaining color, isospin and spin part to be antisymmetric so as to satisfy the the Pauli principle. By adapting the IS coupling scheme that combine the isospin basis function with the spin basis function, and subsequently coupling this to the color singlet basis function, we construct the color $\otimes$ isospin $\otimes$ spin states compatible with the physical states of the dibaryon. By using the variational method, we then calculate the mass of the dibaryon in a nonrelativistic potential model, involving Coulomb, color confinement and color-spin hyperfine interaction. In particular, to asses the stability for different types of the confinement potential, we introduce one that is linearly proportional to the interquark distance and another to its square root. For all cases considered, we find that there are no compact bound states against the strong decay.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether dibaryons composed only of up and down quarks can form compact bound states, and finds that for all considered confinement potentials (linear and square-root), no such bound states exist against strong decay into two baryons.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian including Coulomb, color confinement, and color-spin hyperfine interactions. They construct fully antisymmetric six-quark wave functions by taking a fully symmetric spatial part (built from 15 Gaussian functions with three variational parameters) and an antisymmetric color-isospin-spin part, using the IS coupling scheme and Young-Yamanouchi representation. The variational method is then applied to calculate dibaryon masses, which are compared to the relevant two-baryon thresholds to assess stability against strong decay.

## Key numbers

- For Type 1 confinement (linear), the fitted up quark mass is 340 MeV and strange quark mass is 610 MeV; for Type 2 (square-root), the fitted up quark mass is 348 MeV and strange quark mass is 612 MeV.
- The calculated nucleon mass is 977.1 MeV (Type 1) and 976.3 MeV (Type 2), compared to the experimental range of 938.2-939.5 MeV.
- The calculated Delta mass is 1267.5 MeV (Type 1) and 1237.2 MeV (Type 2), compared to the experimental range of 1230-1234 MeV.
- The calculated Lambda mass is 1115.6 MeV for both potential types, matching the experimental value of 1115.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dibaryons, particularly following the observation of d*(2380) by the WASA detector at COSY (Bashkanov 2008, Adlarson 2011-2014). It builds on the classification of dibaryon states within SU(3)_F representation by Silvestre-Brac and Leandri (1992), and uses the color-spin hyperfine potential introduced by Bhaduri (1981). The negative result for compact bound states in the light-quark sector contrasts with predictions for the H-dibaryon (Jaffe 1976) and Omega-Omega dibaryon, and suggests that any stable dibaryons would require heavier quarks, as studied in tetraquark configurations (Zouzou 1986, Silvestre-Brac 1993, Brink 1998, Park 2013).
