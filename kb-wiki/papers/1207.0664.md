# Self-consistent calculations of the electric giant dipole resonances in light and heavy mass nuclei

> While bulk properties of stable nuclei are successfully reproduced by mean-field theories employing effective interactions, the dependence of the centroid energy of the electric giant dipole resonance on the nucleon number A is not. This problem is cured by considering many-particle correlations beyond mean-field theory, which we do within the "Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation". The electric giant dipole resonances in $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb are calculated using two new Skyrme interactions.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including many-particle correlations beyond mean-field theory, specifically through the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA), can resolve the long-standing failure of self-consistent mean-field theories to simultaneously reproduce the centroid energy of the electric giant dipole resonance (GDR) in both light and heavy nuclei with the same effective interaction. The key qualifier is that the phonon contribution is small in light nuclei but large in heavy nuclei, providing a mass-dependent mechanism for damping and energy shift.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent calculations of the electric GDR in 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb using the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA), which generalizes the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) by explicitly including the coupling of one-particle one-hole (1p1h) configurations with phonons. Two new Skyrme interactions (SV-m56-O and SV-m64-O) were fitted specifically to reproduce the GDR in 16O within the mean-field (RPA) approach, then applied to all three nuclei using both RPA and QTBA. The single-particle continuum was treated exactly, and phonons were calculated within a discretized RPA (DRPA) with transition probabilities of at least 1/5 of the strongest state of each multipolarity.

## Key numbers
- Two new Skyrme forces were created: SV-m56-O with effective mass m*/m = 0.56 and SV-m64-O with m*/m = 0.64, both having symmetry energy a_sym = 27 MeV and TRK sum-rule enhancement factor kappa_TRK = 0.6.
- For 208Pb with SV-m56-O: RPA centroid energy = 14.30 MeV, QTBA centroid energy = 13.37 MeV, experimental centroid = 13.43 MeV; RPA width = 4.96 MeV, QTBA width = 5.99 MeV, experimental width = 5.08 MeV.
- For 40Ca with SV-m56-O: RPA centroid energy = 21.61 MeV, QTBA centroid energy = 21.14 MeV, experimental centroid = 20.00 MeV.
- For 16O with SV-m56-O: RPA centroid energy = 25.31 MeV, QTBA centroid energy = 24.49 MeV, experimental centroid = 23.76 MeV.
- Dipole polarizability alpha_D = 20.2 fm^3 for SV-m56-O and 19.4 fm^3 for SV-m64-O; neutron skin thickness = 0.156 fm for SV-m56-O and 0.134 fm for SV-m64-O.

## Context
This work extends the systematic variation of Skyrme forces described in Klupfel (2008) and Erler (2010, 2011), which showed that mean-field theories could not simultaneously describe ground-state properties and GDR centroid energies in light and heavy nuclei. The paper demonstrates that the QTBA, previously developed within Landau-Migdal theory and generalized to Skyrme interactions in Avdeenkov (2007) and Lyutorovich (2008), provides a mass-dependent mechanism that corrects the GDR energy in heavy nuclei while preserving the good description in light nuclei. This enables more reliable predictions of neutron skin thickness and pygmy dipole strength, which are relevant for understanding the isotope abundance produced in supernova explosions.
