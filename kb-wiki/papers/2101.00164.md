# Passage of heavy quarks through the fluctuating hot QCD medium

> The change in the energy of the moving heavy (charm and bottom) quarks due to field fluctuations present in the hot QCD medium has been studied. A finite quark chemical potential has been considered while modeling the hot QCD medium counting the fact that the upcoming experimental facilities such as Anti-proton and Ion Research (FAIR) and Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA) are expected to operate at finite baryon density and moderate temperature. The effective kinetic theory approach has been adopted where the collisions have been incorporated using the well defined collisional kernel, known as Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK). To incorporate the non-ideal equations of state (EoSs) effects/ medium interaction effects, an extended effective fugacity model has been adopted. The momentum dependence of the energy change due to fluctuation for the charm and bottom quark has been investigated at different values of collision frequency and chemical potential. The results are exciting as the heavy quarks are found to gain energy due to fluctuations while moving through the produced medium at finite chemical potential and collision frequency.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks (charm and bottom) are found to gain energy due to field fluctuations while moving through the hot QCD medium, rather than losing it, under the specific conditions of finite quark chemical potential and non-zero collision frequency; the gain saturates at high momentum and is suppressed by non-ideal medium interaction effects.

## Method

The authors employ an effective kinetic theory approach using the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel to incorporate collisions, and the extended effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to incorporate non-ideal equations of state (EoSs) and finite quark chemical potential. The change in heavy-quark energy due to fluctuations is derived from Wong's equations, with the longitudinal and transverse components of the medium dielectric permittivity computed at finite chemical potential and collision frequency. The energy change is then evaluated numerically for charm and bottom quarks as a function of momentum, chemical potential, and collision frequency.

## Key numbers

- At temperature \(T = 2 T_c\) with \(T_c = 0.17\) GeV, the energy gain due to fluctuations for both charm and bottom quarks is suppressed up to **50%** when using non-ideal EoSs (lattice and 3-loop HTL perturbative) compared to the leading-order (LO) ideal case.
- At a finite quark chemical potential of \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV, the energy gain is reduced by a maximum of **5%** compared to the case with vanishing chemical potential (\(\mu = 0\)).
- The collision frequency is taken in the order of the screening mass, with values up to \(\nu = 0.62 m_D\), and results are shown for \(\nu = 0\), \(0.2 m_D^{\text{LO}}\), and \(0.4 m_D^{\text{LO}}\).
- The upper limit of the momentum integration is set to the Debye mass, \(k_{\infty}^{\text{EoSs}} \sim m_D^{\text{EoSs}}(T)\), for each equation of state.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy-quark energy loss in the hot QCD medium (e.g., Bjorken (1982), Thoma (1990), Braaten (1991), Chakraborty (2006)) by focusing on the energy change due to fluctuations rather than polarization, and by incorporating finite quark chemical potential relevant for upcoming facilities like FAIR and NICA. The authors previously studied the polarization contribution (Jamal, 2020) and found that both charm and bottom quarks lose energy via that mechanism. The present results on fluctuation-induced energy gain, combined with future inclusion of magnetic field effects, momentum anisotropy, and viscous effects, could impact calculations of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for heavy mesons in nucleus-nucleus and p-nucleus collisions.
