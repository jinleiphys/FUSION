# Tracing masses of ground-state light-quark mesons

> We describe a symmetry-preserving calculation of the meson spectrum, which combines a description of pion properties with reasonable estimates of the masses of heavier light-quark mesons, including axial-vector states. The kernels used in formulating the problem are essentially nonperturbative. They incorporate effects of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) that were not previously possible to express. Our analysis clarifies a causal connection between DCSB and the splitting between vector and axial-vector mesons, and exposes a key role played by the anomalous chromomagnetic moment of dressed-quarks in forming the spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper provides a symmetry-preserving calculation of the light-quark meson spectrum that, for the first time, reproduces the large splitting between vector and axial-vector mesons (e.g., the rho and a1), but only when the dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment is included in the quark-gluon vertex; the widely-used Ball-Chiu vertex alone is inadequate.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the dressed-quark propagator and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound-state masses, using a symmetry-preserving kernel constructed from a novel Ansatz for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex. The vertex combines the Ball-Chiu (BC) component with an anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM) term, both of which are driven by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB). Meson masses are extracted by locating zeros in the inverse of the leading amplitude at spacelike total momenta via Padé approximants.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass: 0.138 GeV (input, matches experiment).
*   Rho meson mass: 0.84 ± 0.03 GeV (computed; experimental value 0.777 GeV).
*   Sigma meson mass: 1.13 ± 0.01 GeV (computed; experimental range 0.4–1.2 GeV).
*   a1 meson mass: 1.28 ± 0.01 GeV (computed; experimental value 1.24 ± 0.04 GeV).
*   b1 meson mass: 1.24 ± 0.10 GeV (computed; experimental value 1.21 ± 0.02 GeV).
*   a1–rho mass splitting: 0.44 ± 0.04 GeV (computed; experimental value 0.46 ± 0.04 GeV).
*   b1–rho mass splitting: 0.40 ± 0.14 GeV (computed; experimental value 0.43 ± 0.02 GeV).
*   Pion decay constant: 0.091 GeV (computed).
*   Dressed-quark mass function at zero momentum: approximately 0.5 GeV (in the chiral limit, from prior work cited).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to meson spectroscopy beyond the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation, which had previously failed to describe axial-vector meson masses (e.g., Alkofer (2002) and Watson (2004)). The authors show that the Ball-Chiu vertex alone (used in earlier DSE studies) is insufficient, and that the DCSB-induced anomalous chromomagnetic moment is essential for the vector–axial-vector mass splitting. The framework enables future reliable, Poincaré-invariant computations of light-quark hybrid and exotic meson spectra, providing predictions for next-generation hadron physics facilities.
