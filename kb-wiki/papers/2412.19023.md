# Nuclear matter properties from chiral-scale effective theory including a dilatonic scalar meson

> Chiral effective theory has become a powerful tool for studying the low-energy properties of QCD. In this work, we apply an extended chiral effective theory -- chiral-scale effective theory -- including a dilatonic scalar meson to study nuclear matter and find that the properties around saturation density can be well reproduced. Compared to the traditionally used Walecka-type models in nuclear matter studies, our approach improves the behavior of symmetry energy and the incompressibility coefficient in describing empirical data without introducing additional freedoms. Moreover, the predicted neutron star structures fall within the constraints of GW170817, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0030+0451, while the maximum neutron star mass can reach about $~3M_{\odot}$ with a pure hadronic phase. Additionally, we find that symmetry patterns of the effective theory significantly impact neutron star structures. %In chiral-scale effective theory, effective operators are well organized by chiral-scale orders and freedoms induced by QCD symmetry patterns. We believe that introducing this type of theory into nuclear matter studies can lead to a deeper understanding of QCD, nuclear matter, and compact astrophysical objects.

## Key claim
The chiral-scale effective theory (bsHLS) can reproduce nuclear matter properties around saturation density and predict neutron star structures consistent with astrophysical constraints, but only when the anomalous dimension parameter β' is approximately 1.15 and the product m_σ f_χ is about 2.3 × 10^5 MeV^2, conditions that align with the pseudo-conformal limit at high densities.

## Method
The authors applied the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation to the bsHLS Lagrangian, which includes nucleons, pions, omega, rho, and a dilatonic scalar meson (sigma) as a Nambu-Goldstone boson of scale symmetry. Free parameters (M_σ = m_σ f_χ, β', r, g_ωNN, g_ρNN, g^SSB_ωNN, g^SSB_ρNN) were fixed by fitting nuclear matter properties around saturation density n_0. Neutron star mass-radius relations were computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation using the resulting equations of state for pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density n_0 = 0.159 fm^{-3} for both bsHLS-L and bsHLS-H
- Binding energy e_0 = -16.0 MeV for both bsHLS-L and bsHLS-H
- Incompressibility K_0 = 232 MeV (bsHLS-L) and 284 MeV (bsHLS-H), compared to empirical 230 ± 30 MeV
- Symmetry energy at n_0: E_sym(n_0) = 30.5 MeV (bsHLS-L) and 29.2 MeV (bsHLS-H), compared to empirical 30.9 ± 1.9 MeV
- Symmetry energy at 2n_0: E_sym(2n_0) = 51.5 MeV (bsHLS-L) and 50.2 MeV (bsHLS-H), compared to empirical 46.9 ± 10.1 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass for bsHLS-H: ~2.8 M_⊙
- Tidal deformability Λ_1.4 = 910 for bsHLS-H, compared to 878 for FSU-δ6.7
- Product M_σ = m_σ f_χ ≈ 2.3 × 10^5 MeV^2 for bsHLS-H
- Anomalous dimension β' = 1.15 for bsHLS-H
- Scaling parameter r ≈ 0.19 for bsHLS-H

## Context
This work extends the chiral-scale effective theory (bsHLS) previously developed by Ma (2019) and others, which had been applied to nuclear matter using the low-momentum potential V_low k renormalization group approach. The present study uses the RMF method instead, enabling direct comparison with traditional Walecka-type models such as L-HS (Horowitz 1981), NL1 (Reinhard 1986), TM1 (Sugahara 1993), and FSU-δ6.7 (Li 2022). The key improvement over these models is that bsHLS reproduces a stiff symmetry energy at subsaturation density and a soft symmetry energy at intermediate densities without introducing additional mesons like the delta meson. The predicted maximum neutron star mass near 3 M_⊙ with a pure hadronic phase could help address the mass gap problem in supernova remnants and the gravitational wave event GW190814.
