# Collisional Effects in Isovector Response Function of Nuclear Matter at Finite Temperature

> The dipole response function of nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures is investigated by employing the linearized version of the extended TDHF theory with a non-Markovian binary collision term. Calculations are carried out for nuclear dipole vibrations by employing the Steinwedel-Jensen model and compared with experimental results for $^{120}Sn$ and $^{208}Pb$.

## Key claim

The collisional damping mechanism, included via a non-Markovian binary collision term in the linearized extended TDHF theory, introduces a spread in the isovector response function that becomes more pronounced with increasing temperature, but the simple description does not produce a good description of the experimental strength functions as a function of temperature because the coherent damping mechanism due to coupling with low-frequency collective surface modes is missing.

## Method

The authors linearize the extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with a non-Markovian collision term around a finite-temperature equilibrium state, obtaining a retarded response function that includes both the unperturbed Lindhard function and a collisional response function. They apply the Steinwedel-Jensen model for nuclear dipole oscillations to finite nuclei, using a simplified Skyrme SLy4 force, and evaluate the five-dimensional integrals for the collisional response numerically after reducing the twelve-dimensional integrals using conservation laws and Fermi liquid theory methods.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}Pb$, the RPA-level (without collision term) peak is at $\omega = 12.3$ MeV for experimental temperatures $T^* = 1.34, 1.62, 1.85, 2.05$ MeV
- For $^{120}Sn$, the RPA-level peak is at $\omega = 14.3$ MeV for $T^* = 1.36, 2.13$ MeV and at $\omega = 14.2$ MeV for $T^* = 2.67, 3.1$ MeV
- The experimental peak values are $\omega = 15.4$ MeV for $^{120}Sn$ and $\omega = 13.4$ MeV for $^{208}Pb$
- The coupling constant $V_0$ for dipole vibrations using SLy4 parameters is $V_0 = 85$ MeV
- The wavenumber $k = \pi/2R$ is $k = 0.28$ fm$^{-1}$ for $^{120}Sn$ ($R = 5.6$ fm) and $k = 0.23$ fm$^{-1}$ for $^{208}Pb$ ($R = 6.7$ fm), using $R = 1.13A^{1/3}$
- The spin-averaged proton-neutron scattering cross section used is $\sigma_{pn} = 40$ mb

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations (Ring and Schuck, 1980) that successfully describe mean resonance energies but cannot explain the temperature dependence of the GDR width, and builds on the extended TDHF approach of Ayik et al. (1998) and Lacroix et al. (2001). The results are compared with experimental data from Baumann et al. (1998) for $^{120}Sn$ and $^{208}Pb$, and the authors identify the missing coherent damping mechanism due to coupling with thermal surface deformations (Bertsch et al., 1983; Ormand et al., 1996) as the next element to incorporate, which they plan to do in Thomas-Fermi approximation.
