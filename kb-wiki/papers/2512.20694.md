# Isoscalar Giant Resonances in Highly-Deformed $^{172}$Yb

> To study the isoscalar giant resonances in a deformed case, background-free $α$-particle inelastic scattering measurements using a 386 MeV $α$ beam were performed on the highly-deformed $^{172}$Yb nucleus using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP) at very forward angles, including $0^\circ$. The strength distributions for the isoscalar giant resonances up to $L \leq 3$ were obtained using multipole decomposition analysis. The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) strength exhibits a splitting into two components, interpreted as the coupling of the ISGMR with the $K=0$ component of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR). A \textit{bimodal} structure is observed in the strength distribution of the isoscalar giant dipole resonance. The ISGQR strength shows an enhancement near 25 MeV, attributed to the excitation of an overtone mode, while the broadening of the main-tone peak is associated with nuclear deformation. The experimental results are well reproduced by theoretical strength distributions calculated using the quasiparticle finite amplitude method for $L \leq 3$.

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) strength in the highly-deformed nucleus $^{172}$Yb splits into two components, interpreted as coupling with the $K=0$ component of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), and the experimental results are well reproduced by quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) calculations for multipolarities $L \leq 3$, though the theoretical strength distributions use phenomenological damping parameters adjusted to match the data.

## Method

Background-free $\alpha$-particle inelastic scattering measurements were performed on a $^{172}$Yb target (areal density 2.95 $\pm$ 0.08 mg/cm$^2$) using a 386 MeV $\alpha$ beam at the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka University, at very forward angles including $0^\circ$. Strength distributions for isoscalar giant resonances up to $L \leq 3$ were obtained via multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) using distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations with optical model parameters extracted from elastic scattering data on the same nucleus. Theoretical strength distributions were calculated using the QFAM approach on top of a ground state from the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-PC1 functional.

## Key numbers

- The $^{172}$Yb target had an areal density of 2.95 $\pm$ 0.08 mg/cm$^2$; the beam energy was 386 MeV.
- The energy resolution of the spectrometer was approximately 175 keV.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{172}$Yb is $\beta_2 = 0.330 (5)$ from nuclear data center values; the RHB calculation predicts $\beta_2 = 0.337$.
- The ISGQR strength shows an enhancement near 25 MeV, attributed to excitation of an overtone mode.
- The RHB calculation predicts a binding energy of 1392.485 MeV for $^{172}$Yb, compared to the experimental value of 1392.758 MeV.
- The QFAM calculations used a Lorentzian smearing width of 2$\gamma$ with $\gamma$ set to 1 MeV for ISGMR, 0.5 MeV for ISGDR, 1.5 MeV for ISGQR, and 0.5 MeV for ISGOR.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isoscalar giant resonances in deformed nuclei, including investigations in Sm isotopes (Itoh 2002, Itoh 2003, Youngblood 2004), Nd isotopes (Garg 1984, Abdullah 2024), and lighter deformed nuclei such as $^{24}$Mg (Gupta 2015) and $^{28}$Si (Peach 2016). Unlike prior work on $^{154}$Sm where optical model parameters were extracted using the spherical isotope $^{144}$Sm, this analysis derives optical model parameters directly from elastic scattering on the deformed $^{172}$Yb nucleus. The observation of an ISGQR overtone mode near 25 MeV follows the recent identification of such modes in Nd isotopes (Abdullah 2024) and earlier evidence in $^{208}$Pb (Hunyadi 2003, Hunyadi 2007). The results enable systematic comparison of deformation effects on compressional modes across the rare-earth region and provide a benchmark for QFAM calculations in highly-deformed systems.
