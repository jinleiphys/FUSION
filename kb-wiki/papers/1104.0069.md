# Phase-space analysis of fragments formed in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effect of momentum-dependent interactions and a broader Gaussian on multifragmentation. We also look into the details of the fragment structure for a broader Gaussian and momentum-dependent interactions. We find that nucleons forming the fragments belong to same region of the phase space.

## Key claim

The authors find that nucleons forming fragments (both intermediate mass fragments and the largest fragment) originate from the same region of phase space, regardless of whether momentum-dependent interactions or a broader Gaussian width are used. This conclusion is drawn from simulations using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model, and the abstract's claim that "nucleons forming the fragments belong to same region of the phase space" is confirmed for both static and momentum-dependent equations of state, as well as for both normal and broad Gaussian widths.

## Method

The authors performed QMD simulations of \({}^{58}\)Ni+\({}^{58}\)Ni and \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon for central (\(\hat{b}=0.2\)) and peripheral (\(\hat{b}=0.8\)) impact parameters. They used stiff (Hard), soft (Soft), and soft with momentum-dependent interactions (SMD) equations of state, along with two Gaussian widths: L = 4.33 fm\({}^{2}\) (L\({}^{\text{norm}}\)) and L = 8.66 fm\({}^{2}\) (L\({}^{\text{broad}}\)). The phase space of nucleons was stored at several time steps and clustered using the minimum spanning tree method with a binding distance of 4 fm.

## Key numbers

*   For \({}^{58}\)Ni+\({}^{58}\)Ni at 100 MeV/nucleon and central collision (\(\hat{b}=0.2\)), the largest fragment (A\({}^{\text{max}}\)) reaches a maximum of about 116 (the total mass of the system) at 20-40 fm/c.
*   For \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au at 100 MeV/nucleon and central collision (\(\hat{b}=0.2\)), A\({}^{\text{max}}\) reaches a maximum value of 394 (the total mass of the system).
*   The normal Gaussian width used was L = 4.33 fm\({}^{2}\) and the broad Gaussian width was L = 8.66 fm\({}^{2}\).
*   The Yukawa interaction parameters were \(t_3 = -6.66\) MeV and \(\mu = 1.5\) fm.
*   The momentum-dependent interaction parameters were \(t_4 = 1.57\) MeV and \(t_5 = 5 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\({}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies on multifragmentation that examined the effect of momentum-dependent interactions (Peilert, year not given) and interaction range (Hartnack, year not given; Gautam, year not given) on fragment production, but which did not analyze the internal phase-space structure of the fragments. The authors use the QMD model (Aich, year not given) to show that fragments are not formed by random coalescence but rather emerge from nucleons that are already close in phase space. This finding enables future studies to focus on the pre-selection of nucleons in phase space as the mechanism for fragment formation, rather than relying solely on spatial clustering algorithms.
