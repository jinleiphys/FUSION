# Theory of the n=2 levels in muonic helium-3 ions

> The present knowledge of Lamb shift, fine-, and hyperfine structure of the 2S and 2P states in muonic helium-3 ions is reviewed in anticipation of the results of a first measurement of several $\mathrm{2S\rightarrow2P}$ transition frequencies in the muonic helium-3 ion, $\mathrm{μ^3He^+}$. This ion is the bound state of a single negative muon $μ^-$ and a bare helium-3 nucleus (helion), $\mathrm{^3He^{++}}$. A term-by-term comparison of all available sources, including new, updated, and so far unpublished calculations, reveals reliable values and uncertainties of the QED and nuclear structure-dependent contributions to the Lamb shift and the hyperfine splitting. These values are essential for the determination of the helion rms charge radius and the nuclear structure effects to the hyperfine splitting in $\mathrm{μ^3He^+}$. With this review we continue our series of theory summaries in light muonic atoms; see Antognini et al., Ann. Phys. 331, 127 (2013), Krauth et al., Ann.Phys. 366, 168 (2016), and Diepold et al., ArXiv 1606.05231 (2016).

## Key claim
This review compiles and compares all available calculations of QED and nuclear-structure contributions to the 2S and 2P energy levels in muonic helium-3 ions, providing reliable values and uncertainties that are essential for extracting the helion rms charge radius from upcoming laser spectroscopy measurements, though the total theoretical uncertainty (0.52 meV) is dominated by the two-photon exchange contributions which remain difficult to calculate.

## Method
The authors performed a term-by-term comparison of all available sources for the Lamb shift, fine structure, and hyperfine structure of the 2S and 2P states in muonic helium-3 ions, including new, updated, and previously unpublished calculations from groups including Borie, the Martynenko group, the Karshenboim group, Jentschura, the TRIUMF-Hebrew group, and Carlson et al. For each contribution, they took the average of values from different groups when agreement was found, with an uncertainty of half the spread, and adopted specific values from individual groups when only one calculation existed.

## Key numbers
- Lamb shift (2S-2P energy difference) nuclear structure-independent contributions: 1642.3962 ± 0.0018 meV for the Uehling term with relativistic correction (items #1+#2 or #3+#19)
- Total theoretical uncertainty of the Lamb shift calculation (excluding charge radius contribution): 0.52 meV, corresponding to a relative uncertainty of approximately 0.03%
- Pure QED contributions uncertainty (without two-photon exchange): 0.04 meV
- Expected experimental accuracy: approximately 20 GHz, which corresponds to about 0.08 meV
- Helion rms charge radius from electron scattering: 1.973(14) fm (Sick, 2014)
- Two-loop electron vacuum polarization (Kallen-Sabry term, item #4): value from averaging Borie and Martynenko group calculations, agreeing within 0.0037 meV
- Third-order electron vacuum polarization (item #6+7): Martynenko group and Karshenboim group differ by 0.004 meV, with Martynenko group giving uncertainty of 0.003 meV

## Context
This review continues the CREMA collaboration's series of theory summaries for light muonic atoms, following Antognini et al. (2013) for muonic hydrogen, Krauth et al. (2016) for muonic deuterium, and Diepold et al. (2016) for muonic helium-4 ions. The work enables determination of the helion rms charge radius with nearly hundred-fold better accuracy than the electron-scattering value from Sick (2014), and will help resolve the 4-sigma discrepancy between electronic helium isotope shift measurements by Shiner et al. (1995) and van Rooij et al. (2011).
