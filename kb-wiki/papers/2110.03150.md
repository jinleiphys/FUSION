# Recent results and future prospects of kaonic nuclei at J-PARC

> $\bar K$-nuclear bound systems, kaonic nuclei, have been widely discussed as products of the strongly attractive $\bar K N$ interaction in $I = 0$ channels. Recently, we demonstrated that kaonic nuclei can be produced via in-flight $(K^-,N)$ reactions using the low-momentum DC kaon beam at the J-PARC E15 experiment. We observed the simplest kaonic nuclei, $K^-pp$, having a much deeper binding energy than normal nuclei. For further studies, we have proposed a series of experimental programs for the systematic investigation of light kaonic nuclei, from $\bar K N$ ($Λ(1405)$) to $\bar K NNNN$. In the new experiment approved as J-PARC E80, we will measure the $\bar K NNN$ ($A=3$) system as a first step toward a comprehensive study.

## Key claim

The J-PARC E15 experiment confirmed the existence of the $K^-pp$ bound state via in-flight $^3$He$(K^-,N)$ reactions, with a binding energy of approximately 50 MeV, but the authors note that the simplest and most natural interpretation is a kaon-nuclear bound state, while acknowledging that the observed structure has also been interpreted as a $\bar K NN$ quasi-bound system in theoretical calculations.

## Method

The experiment used a 1.0 GeV/$c$ kaon beam incident on a $^3$He target, employing the in-flight $(K^-,N)$ reaction where the back-scattered kaon acts as an off-shell particle that is absorbed by spectator nucleons to form the $K^-pp$ state. The $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum was measured exclusively from the $^3$He$(K^-,\Lambda p)n$ final state, and the momentum transfer $q$ between the incident kaon and outgoing neutron was used to distinguish bound-state production from quasi-free processes. For the upcoming J-PARC E80 experiment, the same technique will be applied to $^4$He targets to study the $\bar K NNN$ ($K^-ppn$) system via $\Lambda d$ and $\Lambda pn$ decay channels.

## Key numbers

- $K^-pp$ binding energy of approximately 50 MeV, observed below the $M(Kpp)$ mass threshold of 2.37 GeV/$c^2$
- $K^-pp$ signal cross section of approximately 10 $\mu$b, compared to quasi-free process cross sections of approximately 10 mb
- Form factor of approximately 400 MeV/$c$ for the $K^-pp$ state, based on a plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) analysis
- Momentum transfer region $0.3 < q < 0.6$ GeV/$c$ where the $K^-pp$ bound state is dominant
- For the E80 experiment, expected data taking of 100-G kaons on target, corresponding to three weeks under 90-kW beam power
- Assumed $K^-ppn$ binding energy of approximately 50 MeV and width of approximately 100 MeV for the expected spectra
- Charged particle momentum resolution of $5.3\% \times p_t \oplus 0.5\%$ and neutron momentum resolution of approximately $10\% \times p \oplus 7\%$

## Context

This work extends the search for kaonic nuclei beyond the $K^-pp$ system observed at J-PARC E15, following earlier candidate observations by FINUDA (Agnello 2007) reporting a $K^-ppn$ candidate with binding energy of approximately 60 MeV and width of approximately 40 MeV, and FOPI (Herrmann 2005) reporting a deeper binding energy of approximately 150 MeV. The new J-PARC E80 experiment aims to provide the first systematic mass-number dependence of kaonic nuclei properties from $A=2$ to $A=4$, enabling comparison with theoretical models such as Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), Wycech (2008), Barnea (2012), and Ohnishi (2017), which predict varying binding energies and widths depending on the $\bar K N$ interaction model used.
