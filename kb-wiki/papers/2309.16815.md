# Diquarks and $Œõ^0/œÄ^+$, $Œû^-/œÄ^+$ ratios in the framework of the EPNJL model

> The applicability of the effective models to the description of baryons and the behaviour of ratios of strange baryons to pions is discussed. In the framework of the EPNJL model, the Bethe - Salpeter equation is used to find masses of baryons, which are considered as diquark-quark state. Baryon melting is discussed at a finite chemical potential and a flavor dependence of the hadronic deconfinement temperature is pointed. It is shown that the description of the diquark-quark state at finite chemical potential is limited due to the occurrence of the Bose condensate. This effect is strongly manifested in the description of light diquarks and baryons. Both $Œõ^0/œÄ^+$ and $Œû^-/œÄ^+$ ratios show a sharp behaviour as functions of $T/Œº_B$ variable, where T and $Œº_B$ are calculated along the melting lines.

# Diquarks and $\Lambda^0/\pi^+$, $\Xi^-/\pi^+$ ratios in the framework of the EPNJL model

## Key claim

The EPNJL model reproduces a peak-like structure for both $\Lambda^0/\pi^+$ and $\Xi^-/\pi^+$ ratios as functions of $T/\mu_B$, but the authors state that most of their analysis "must primarily be taken as qualitative hints" due to limitations in describing baryons as diquark-quark bound states at finite chemical potential, particularly the onset of Bose condensate formation in light diquarks.

## Method

The authors use the extended Polyakov-loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model with SU(3) flavor symmetry, including vector interaction and $U_A(1)$ anomaly. Baryons are treated as diquark-quark bound states via the Bethe-Salpeter equation derived from the static approximation of the Faddeev equation. The baryon dissociation temperature is defined as the minimum of the baryon Mott temperature and the diquark Mott temperature, with an alternative three-quark dissociation temperature considered for borromean states. The $\Lambda^0/\pi^+$ and $\Xi^-/\pi^+$ ratios are calculated along the baryon melting lines in the $T-\mu_B$ plane using number densities with degeneracy factors.

## Key numbers

* The EPNJL model crossover temperature is $T^{\rm EPNJL}_c = 0.18$ GeV, reduced from the PNJL value of $T_c = 0.27$ GeV to better match the Lattice prediction of $T_c \sim 0.17$ GeV.
* The $\Lambda$ baryon is described up to $\mu_q \sim 0.27$ GeV, while the $\Xi^-$ baryon is described up to $\mu_q \sim 0.37$ GeV, reflecting the greater stability of diquarks containing heavier quarks.
* The parameter set uses $m_{u0}=m_{d0} = 4.75$ MeV, $m_{s0}= 0.147$ GeV, $\Lambda = 0.708$ GeV, $g_{S}\Lambda^{2} = 1.922$, $g_{D}\Lambda^5 = 10.0$, $g_{\rm V} = 0$, and $g_{\rm diq} = 0.725 g_{\rm S}$.
* The difference in chemical freeze-out temperature between proton and $\Xi$ is about 20 MeV in the PNJL model and 10 MeV in the EPNJL model, compared to approximately 30 MeV from experiment and statistical models (Torres-Rincon, 2015; Preghenella, 2011).
* The pion chemical potential parameters for fitting experimental data include $\mu_\pi^{\mathrm{max}}= 107\pm10$ MeV, $\mu_\pi^{\mathrm{min}}=92$ MeV, $x_\pi^{\mathrm{th}}=0.409$, $\Delta x_\pi = 0.00685$; for the strange quark chemical potential: $\mu_s^{\mathrm{max}}/\mu_u^{\mathrm{crit}}= 0.205$, $x_s^{\mathrm{th}}=0.223$, $\Delta x_s = 0.06$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of $K^+/\pi^+$ and $K^-/\pi^-$ ratios in PNJL-like models (Friesen, 2019; Blaschke, 2021; Blaschke, 2020) to baryon-to-meson ratios, motivated by the observation from thermal model calculations (Oeschler, 2017) that the $\Lambda^0/\pi^+$ peak does not disappear with reducing system size, unlike the $K^+/\pi^+$ peak. The results are limited by the neglect of axial-vector diquark contributions to baryon octet masses (Blanquier, 2011; Torres-Rincon, 2015) and by the difficulty of describing baryons as diquark-quark states at high chemical potential where color superconductivity may occur. The work enables future improvements including the axial-vector diquark channel and description of baryons above critical densities.
