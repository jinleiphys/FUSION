# Isospin Violation and the Proton's Strange Form Factors

> The strange form factors of the proton are basic to an understanding of proton structure, and are presently the focus of many experiments. Before the strangeness effects can be extracted from data, it is necessary to calculate and remove effects due to isospin violation, which exist independently of the strange quark but which contribute nevertheless to the experimentally measured ``strange'' form factors. A discussion of the isospin violating contributions to vector form factors is given here in the context of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

# Isospin Violation and the Proton's Strange Form Factors

## Key claim

The experimentally measured "strange" form factors of the proton contain contributions from isospin violation that exist even in a world with no strange quarks, and these isospin-violating contributions must be calculated and subtracted before the true strange quark effects can be extracted from data. In heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, the isospin-violating electric form factor is parameter-free at leading order, while the magnetic form factor has only a single additive constant at leading order and no new parameters at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute the isospin-violating contributions to the proton's vector form factors, denoted \(G_E^{u,d}(q^2)\) and \(G_M^{u,d}(q^2)\), in a strange-free world. They perform a systematic expansion in small momenta, pion masses, and QED coupling, working through Lagrangians up to order \({\cal L}^{(4)}\) for leading order and including next-to-leading order corrections from one-loop diagrams and \({\cal L}^{(5)}\) counterterms. The calculation yields explicit analytic expressions for the form factors, with the electric form factor containing no unknown parameters and the magnetic form factor having only a single \(q^2\)-independent constant at leading order.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-violating contribution to the proton's strange electric charge radius is \(\left<r_s^2\right>_E \equiv 6{\rm d}G_E^{u,d}(0)/{\rm d}q^2 \approx +0.013\,{\rm fm}^2\).
- The next-to-leading order correction to the isospin-violating magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer is \(\left.G_M^{u,d}(0)\right|_{LO+NLO} - \left.G_M^{u,d}(0)\right|_{LO} \approx 0.013\).
- The SAMPLE Collaboration measured \(G_M^s(0.1\,{\rm GeV}^2) = 0.23 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.19\).
- The HAPPEX Collaboration measured \([G_E^s+0.39G_M^s](0.48\,{\rm GeV}^2) = 0.023 \pm 0.034 \pm 0.022 \pm 0.026\).
- A later SAMPLE measurement found \(G_M^s(0.1\,{\rm GeV}^2) = +0.61 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.21\).
- Lattice QCD results give \(\left<r_s^2\right>_E = -0.06 \rightarrow -0.16\,{\rm fm}^2\) and \(G_M^s(0) = -0.36 \pm 0.20\).
- Most model predictions for \(G_M^s(0)\) lie in the range \(-0.5 \lesssim G_M^s(0) \lesssim +0.05\).
- Constituent quark model calculations give \(-0.001 < G_M^{u,d}(-0.25\,{\rm GeV}^2) < 0\).
- A light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model gives \(G_M^{u,d}(0) = 0.006 \rightarrow 0.088\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin violation in the strange form factors by Dmitrašinović and Pollock (1996) and Miller (1996) using the nonrelativistic constituent quark model, and by Ma (1998) using a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model. The present HBChPT calculation provides a model-independent framework for computing these isospin-violating effects, which is necessary for interpreting experimental results from the SAMPLE and HAPPEX collaborations. The results enable future experiments to extract the true strange quark contributions to the proton's form factors by subtracting the isospin-violating component computed here.
