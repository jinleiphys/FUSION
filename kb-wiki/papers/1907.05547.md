# Predictions of $╬ź(4S) \to h_b(1P,2P) ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ transitions

> In this work, we study the contributions of the intermediate bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states and the bottom meson loops in the heavy quark spin flip transitions $╬ź(4S) \to h_b(1P,2P) ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$. Depending on the constructive or destructive interferences between the $Z_b$-exchange and the bottom meson loops mechanisms, we predict two possible branching ratios for each process: BR$_{╬ź(4S) \to h_b(1P)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-}\simeq\big(1.2^{+0.8}_{-0.4}\times10^{-6}\big)$ or $\big( 0.5^{+0.5}_{-0.2}\times10^{-6}\big)$, and BR$_{╬ź(4S) \to h_b(2P)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-}\simeq \big(7.1^{+1.7}_{-1.1}\times10^{-10}\big)$ or $\big( 2.4^{+0.2}_{-0.1}\times10^{-10}\big)$. The bottom meson loops contribution is found to be much larger than the $Z_b$-exchange contribution in the $╬ź(4S) \to h_b(1P) ŽĆŽĆ$ transitions, while it can not produce decay rates comparable to the heavy quark spin conserved $╬ź(4S) \to ╬ź(1S,2S) ŽĆŽĆ$ processes. We also predict the branch fractions of $Žł(3S,4S) \to h_c(1P)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ contributed from the charm meson loops.

## Key claim

The bottom meson loops mechanism dominates over the $Z_b$-exchange mechanism in the heavy quark spin flip transition $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$, but it still cannot produce decay rates comparable to the heavy quark spin conserved $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(1S,2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ processes; the predictions are order-of-magnitude estimates due to the neglect of triangle diagrams and unknown gluon hadronization contributions.

## Method

The author calculated the contributions of intermediate $Z_b$ states (tree-level exchange) and bottom meson box diagrams (within nonrelativistic effective field theory, NREFT) to the dipion transitions $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(1P,2P)\pi^+\pi^-$. The box diagrams were computed using effective Lagrangians for $\Upsilon B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$, $h_b B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$, and $B^{(*)}B^{(*)}\pi$ couplings, with coupling constants extracted from experimental open-bottom decay widths of $\Upsilon(4S)$ and from previous analyses of $Z_b$ decays. The three-dimensional loop integrals were evaluated numerically, and the total amplitude was summed coherently with the $Z_b$-exchange amplitude to obtain branching ratios for constructive and destructive interference scenarios.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio for $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$: either $(1.2^{+0.8}_{-0.4}\times10^{-6})$ or $(0.5^{+0.5}_{-0.2}\times10^{-6})$, depending on interference sign.
* Branching ratio for $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(2P)\pi^+\pi^-$: either $(7.1^{+1.7}_{-1.1}\times10^{-10})$ or $(2.4^{+0.2}_{-0.1}\times10^{-10})$, depending on interference sign.
* Box-diagram-only branching ratio for $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$: $(0.8^{+0.7}_{-0.3}\times10^{-6})$.
* $Z_b$-exchange-only branching ratio for $\Upsilon(4S) \to h_b(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$: $(0.6^{+0.1}_{-0.1}\times10^{-7})$.
* Predicted box-diagram-only branching ratio for $\psi(3S) \to h_c(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$: $2.9\times10^{-5}$.
* Predicted box-diagram-only branching ratio for $\psi(4S) \to h_c(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$: $4.5\times10^{-3}$.
* Coupling constant $|C_{Z_{b}\Upsilon(4S)\pi}| = (3.3 \pm 0.1)\times 10^{-3}$, extracted from $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(mS)\pi\pi$ data.
* Coupling $g_{JHH(4S)} = 1.43 \pm 0.01\ \text{GeV}^{-3/2}$, extracted from $\Upsilon(4S)$ open-bottom decay widths.
* Coupling $g_1 = 0.53^{+0.19}_{-0.13}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}$ for $h_b B^{*}\bar{B}^{*}$ interaction, derived from Guo (2011).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous NREFT analysis of $\Upsilon(4S) \to \Upsilon(1S,2S)\pi\pi$ (Chen 2016, Chen 2019) to the heavy quark spin flip channels $h_b(1P,2P)\pi\pi$. It contrasts with the QCD multipole expansion (QCDME) predictions by Kuang (2006) and Voloshin (1980) that the $\Upsilon(3S) \to h_b(1P)\pi\pi$ branching fraction is suppressed by 2-3 orders of magnitude relative to $\Upsilon(3S) \to \Upsilon(1S)\pi\pi$, a suppression supported by experiment (Lees 2011). The work also provides a comparison to the $\Upsilon(5S)$ case, where the $Z_b$ states are on-shell and the $\Upsilon(5S) \to h_b(nP)\pi\pi$ rates are comparable to the spin-conserved transitions (Belle 2011, Belle 2012). The predictions for $\psi(3S,4S) \to h_c(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$ are preliminary estimates using charm meson loops, extending the method to charmonium.
