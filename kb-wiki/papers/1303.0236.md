# Charmonium production in antiproton-nucleus reactions at low energies

> The J/Ψ(1S) and Ψ'(2S) production near threshold in antiproton-nucleus reactions is calculated on the basis of the Glauber model. The model takes into account the antiproton (pre)absorption, proton Fermi motion, and charmonium formation length. We confirm earlier prediction that the charmonium production in $\bar pA$ collisions at $p_{lab}=3-10$ GeV/c is not influenced by formation length effects and is very well suited to determine the genuine charmonium-nucleon dissociation cross sections. The comparison is performed with the J/Ψphotoproduction at high energies, where formation length effects play a very important role. However, we demonstrate that the detailed structure of the proton and neutron density profiles has to be taken into account, if one wants to extract information on the J/ΨN dissociation cross section from J/Ψtransparency ratios. These studies are relevant for the upcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR.

# Charmonium production in antiproton-nucleus reactions at low energies

## Key claim

The authors confirm that charmonium production in antiproton-nucleus collisions at beam momenta of 3-10 GeV/c is not influenced by formation length effects and is well suited to determine genuine charmonium-nucleon dissociation cross sections, but they demonstrate that the detailed structure of proton and neutron density profiles must be taken into account when extracting the J/ΨN dissociation cross section from J/Ψ transparency ratios.

## Method

The authors apply a Glauber model to calculate J/Ψ(1S) and Ψ'(2S) production near threshold in antiproton-nucleus reactions, incorporating antiproton (pre)absorption, proton Fermi motion, and charmonium formation length. The model uses relativistic Breit-Wigner formulas for the elementary cross sections, empirical nucleon density profiles from harmonic oscillator and two-parameter Fermi distributions, and a formation-length-dependent effective charmonium-nucleon cross section. For comparison, the authors also calculate J/Ψ transparency ratios in photon-induced reactions and present results from the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model to evaluate multistep processes.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range for the study: p_lab = 3-10 GeV/c
- Threshold beam momentum for J/Ψ production in p̅p interactions: p_thr = 4.07 GeV/c
- J/ΨN dissociation cross section values used: 3.5 mb (motivated by SLAC photoproduction experiment at E_γ=20 GeV) and 6 mb (from global Glauber fit by Gerschel (1993))
- J/Ψ formation length at p_lab=4 GeV/c: l_J/Ψ ≈ 0.4 fm
- Ψ'(2S) formation length estimate: l_Ψ' ≈ 6 fm × (p_Ψ'/30 GeV)
- Ψ' dissociation cross section used: σ_Ψ'N = 20 mb (from Gerland (1998))
- Threshold beam momentum for Ψ'(2S) production in vacuum: p_lab = 6.23 GeV/c
- Ratio estimate for on-shell production: σ_p̅A→R(A-1)*/(Zσ_p̅p→R) ∼ 10^(-4) for J/Ψ with Γ_R ≈ 93 keV
- Coherence lengths for photoproduction: l_c = 0.8 fm at E_γ=20 GeV, l_c = 4.8 fm at E_γ=120 GeV
- J/Ψ formation lengths in photoproduction: l_J/Ψ = 2 fm at p_J/Ψ=20 GeV/c, l_J/Ψ = 12 fm at p_J/Ψ=120 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical study by Farrar (1989) within the color diffusion and Glauber models, which first proposed measuring color transparency effects for J/Ψ production in p̅-nucleus interactions and found high sensitivity to the charmonium-nucleon dissociation cross section. The present analysis improves upon the work by Gerland (2005) by including Fermi motion effects, which were neglected in that earlier study. The results are directly relevant for the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR, and the comparison with photoproduction data from SLAC (Anderson, 1976) and Fermilab (Sokoloff, 1986) demonstrates that those high-energy measurements cannot constrain the J/ΨN dissociation cross section due to formation length effects and large experimental errors.
