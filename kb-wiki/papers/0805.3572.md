# Nonextensive/dissipative correspondence in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We argue that there is profound correspondence (the nonextensive/dissipative correspondence - NexDC) between the perfect nonextensive hydrodynamics and the usual dissipative hydrodynamics which leads to simple expression for dissipative entropy current.

## Key claim

The paper argues that perfect nonextensive hydrodynamics (based on Tsallis statistics with parameter $q$) is equivalent to usual dissipative hydrodynamics, provided the nonextensive/dissipative correspondence (NexDC) relations hold; this equivalence is conditional on the existence of a temperature $T$ and velocity field $\delta u_q^{\mu}$ satisfying the NexDC relations, and the resulting dissipative entropy current can be expressed in a full-order form.

## Method

The authors start from the perfect $q$-hydrodynamical equations for a non-viscous $q$-fluid, then decompose the energy-momentum tensor using a different four-velocity field $u^{\mu}(x)$ to extract terms interpretable as energy density, pressure, heat flow, shear pressure, and bulk pressure. They impose the NexDC relations linking the nonextensive energy density and pressure to their Boltzmann-Gibbs counterparts, transforming the $q$-hydrodynamic equation into the standard dissipative hydrodynamic equation. The entropy current is then derived from the $q$-entropy current under the NexDC, and compared to the second-order Israel-Stewart form to extract thermodynamic coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The bulk pressure $\Pi$ is given by $\Pi \equiv \frac{1}{3} w_q [\gamma^2+2\gamma]$, where $w_q \equiv \varepsilon_q + P_q$ and $\gamma \equiv u_{\mu}\delta u_q^{\mu}$.
- The $q$-enthalpy relation: $\varepsilon_q(T_q)+P_q(T_q) =\frac{\varepsilon(T)+P(T)}{[1+\gamma]^2}$, with $w\equiv Ts=\varepsilon+P$ and $1/(\gamma+1)= \sqrt{1-3\Pi/w}$.
- The tensor relations: $W^{\mu} W_{\mu} = -3\Pi w$, $\pi^{\mu\nu}W_{\nu} = -2 \Pi W^{\mu}$, $\pi_{\mu\nu}\pi^{\mu\nu} = 6\Pi^2$.
- In the second-order expansion, the thermodynamic coefficients satisfy $\beta_1 = \frac{2}{w}$, $\beta_0+6\beta_2 = \frac{9}{2w}$, and $\alpha_0+2\alpha_1=0$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed $q$-hydrodynamics (Osada, 2008) based on nonextensive statistics (Tsallis, 1988) and relativistic nonextensive kinetic theory (Lavagno, 2002; Lima, 2001). It establishes a formal correspondence between perfect $q$-hydrodynamics and standard dissipative hydrodynamics (Eckart, 1940; Israel, 1976; Muronga, 2004; Heinz, 2006), enabling the interpretation of nonextensive effects as dissipative terms. The NexDC provides a full-order expression for the dissipative entropy current, which can be compared to the second-order Israel-Stewart theory, and suggests that relaxation times are proportional to the inverse of enthalpy.
