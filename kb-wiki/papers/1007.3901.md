# Rho-meson, Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the far infrared

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation in QCD connects the gauge-dependent gluon and quark degrees of freedom with the gauge-invariant properties of mesons. We study the rho meson mass and decay constant for various versions of the gauge-dependent input functions discussed in the literature, which start to differ generically below the hadronic scale, and show qualitative different infrared behavior. We find that, once the gauge-dependent quark-gluon vertex is permitted to vary as well, the rho mass and decay constant is reproduced equally well for all forms investigated. A possible conclusion from this is that these $ρ$-meson properties are only sensitive to changes in the input at scales above a few hundred MeV.

## Key claim

The rho meson mass and decay constant are reproduced equally well for both scaling-type and decoupling-type gluon inputs, provided the quark-gluon vertex is permitted to vary accordingly; this suggests that these rho-meson properties are only sensitive to changes in the input at scales above a few hundred MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in rainbow truncation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder truncation for the rho meson, using four different versions of the Landau-gauge Yang-Mills running coupling (one scaling-type and three decoupling-type). The parameters of the effective quark-gluon vertex are first fixed to reproduce pion properties for the scaling case, then separately re-optimized for each input set to again fit the pion mass and decay constant.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass from scaling solution: 0.1417 GeV; pion decay constant: 0.0932 GeV (after fitting vertex parameters D=0.8 GeV^4, omega=0.8 GeV).
- Rho mass from scaling solution: 0.7443 GeV; rho decay constant: 0.1492 GeV.
- With separate vertex optimization for each input, all four cases yield rho masses between 0.7443 and 0.7485 GeV and rho decay constants between 0.1492 and 0.1514 GeV, agreeing at the percent level.
- Without vertex re-optimization, the decoupling-type inputs produce rho masses ranging from 0.6321 to 0.7641 GeV and rho decay constants from 0.1239 to 0.1533 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of meson properties from Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations (Alkofer 2000, Fischer 2006, Roberts 2007, Krassnigg 2009) by directly comparing scaling-type and decoupling-type Yang-Mills solutions. It complements investigations by Luecker (2009), Fischer (2009), and Natale (2009) that focused on different observables. The results support the idea from Glozman (2009, 2010) that the rho meson's gauge-invariant structure is dominated by scales around 0.5 fm, and they demonstrate that reproducing meson properties alone cannot distinguish between different infrared behaviors of gauge-dependent correlation functions.
