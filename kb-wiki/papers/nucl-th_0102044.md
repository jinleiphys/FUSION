# Exact Conservation Laws of the Gradient Expanded Kadanoff-Baym Equations

> It is shown that the Kadanoff-Baym equations at consistent first-order gradient approximation reveal exact rather than approximate conservation laws related to global symmetries of the system. The conserved currents and energy-momentum tensor coincide with corresponding Noether quantities in the local approximation. These exact conservations are valid, provided a Phi-derivable approximation is used to describe the system, and possible memory effects in the collision term are also consistently evaluated up to first-order gradients.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the Kadanoff-Baym equations, when expanded to first-order gradients and combined with a Phi-derivable approximation for self-energies, yield exact conservation laws for Noether currents and the energy-momentum tensor, not merely approximate ones as previously assumed. This exactness holds only when all memory effects from internal vertices within self-energy diagrams are also consistently expanded to first-order gradients.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equations in the closed time contour formulation and perform a complete first-order gradient expansion of all two-point functions, including those with internal vertices. They introduce a diagrammatic representation of the gradient expansion using a diamond operator, which acts on local diagram expressions to produce terms linear in space-time gradients. The proof of exact conservation laws proceeds by showing that the local parts of the charge and energy-momentum leakage terms vanish identically, while the gradient terms compile into a complete divergence, provided the self-energies are generated from a Phi-functional through functional variation.

## Key numbers

- The conserved current is defined as J^mu(X) = sum_a integral (e_a v^mu (MP i) Gr_a^{-+}(X,p)) d^4p/(2pi)^4, where v^mu = partial G_0^{-1}(p)/partial p_mu
- The local energy-momentum tensor is defined as Theta_loc^{munu}(X) = sum_a integral v^mu p^nu (MP i) Gr_a^{-+}(X,p) d^4p/(2pi)^4 + g^{munu}(E^{int}_loc(X) - E^{pot}_loc(X))
- The gradient expansion is carried out to first order in derivatives, with the diamond operator producing terms linear in space-time gradients
- The Phi-functional is constructed from two-particle irreducible closed diagrams in terms of full Green functions, with the interaction strength lambda(x) set to its physical value lambda=1

## Context

This work extends the foundational results of Baym (1962) and Kadanoff and Baym (1962) on conserving approximations for non-equilibrium Green functions, specifically addressing the problem that earlier quantum transport schemes based on the Botermans-Malfliet substitution (Bot90) did not yield strict conservation laws for Noether currents. The paper provides a rigorous diagrammatic proof that the original Kadanoff-Baym form of the quantum kinetic equations, when combined with Phi-derivable self-energies and consistent first-order gradient expansion, possesses exact conservation laws. This enables a generalized quantum transport scheme capable of describing particles with broad damping width (such as resonances) without the usual quasi-particle approximation, formulated in four-dimensional phase-space.
