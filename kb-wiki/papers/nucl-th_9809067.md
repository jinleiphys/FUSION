# Invariant amplitudes for coherent electromagnetic pseudoscalar production from a spin-one target

> A set of 13 linearly independent invariant amplitudes for the electromagnetic production of a pseudoscalar particle from a spin-one particle is derived which respect Lorentz and gauge invariance. The $T$-matrix can be represented by a linear superposition of these amplitudes with invariant functions as coefficients, which depend on the Mandelstam variables only. Their explicit form is determined by the underlying dynamics. Nine of these amplitudes are purely transverse and describe photoproduction. The remaining four appear in electroproduction in addition, describing charge and longitudinal current contributions. Furthermore, a reduction of these amplitudes to operators acting in non-relativistic spin space is given.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete set of 13 linearly independent invariant amplitudes for coherent electromagnetic pseudoscalar production from a spin-one target that respect both Lorentz and gauge invariance, with the caveat that the explicit form of the invariant functions multiplying these amplitudes is determined by the underlying dynamics and is not provided by the formal framework itself.

## Method

The author constructs all possible covariant amplitudes for the transition operator \(O_{\mu\nu\rho}\) using the available momenta, the metric tensor, and the Levi-Civita tensor, respecting Lorentz covariance and parity conservation. From an initial set of 60 candidate amplitudes, the transversality conditions for the photon and target polarization vectors and two linear relations for the Levi-Civita tensor are used to reduce the set to 13 linearly independent, gauge-invariant amplitudes. Nine of these are purely transverse and describe photoproduction, while the remaining four describe charge and longitudinal current contributions in electroproduction; these four are further transformed into equivalent purely longitudinal amplitudes. The amplitudes are then expressed in helicity form and reduced to nonrelativistic spin-one operators.

## Key numbers

- The total number of \(T\)-matrix elements for electroproduction is 27, which reduces to 13 independent elements when parity conservation is invoked; for photoproduction, the numbers are 18 and 9, respectively.
- The paper identifies exactly 13 linearly independent invariant amplitudes that respect gauge invariance, with 9 being purely transverse (sufficient for photoproduction) and 4 additional ones for electroproduction.
- The invariant functions \(F_\alpha(s,t,u)\) depend on the Mandelstam variables \(s\), \(t\), and \(u\), which satisfy \(s+t+u = 2M^2 - K^2 + m^2\), where \(M\) is the target mass, \(m\) the meson mass, and \(K^2 = -k_\mu^2 > 0\) for virtual photons.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of CGLN amplitudes for pion photo- and electroproduction on a nucleon (Chew, Goldberger, Low, and Nambu, 1957; Donnachie, 1972) to the case of a spin-one target, motivated by coherent pseudoscalar meson production on the deuteron. It provides a general, model-independent framework into which any specific reaction model must fit, enabling future construction of explicit invariant functions for models such as impulse approximation and subsequent inclusion of rescattering and two-body contributions. The paper references prior work on coherent pion and eta photo- and electroproduction on the deuteron (Blomqvist and Bystricky, 1994; Wilhelm and Arenhoevel, 1995; Kamalov and Tiator, 1997; Bracher and Arenhoevel, 1997).
