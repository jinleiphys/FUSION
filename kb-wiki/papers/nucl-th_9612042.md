# Monopole giant resonances and nuclear compressibility in relativistic mean field theory

> Isoscalar and isovector monopole oscillations that correspond to giant resonances in spherical nuclei are described in the framework of time-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. Excitation energies and the structure of eigenmodes are determined from a Fourier analysis of dynamical monopole moments and densities. The generator coordinate method, with generating functions that are solutions of constrained RMF calculations, is also used to calculate excitation energies and transition densities of giant monopole states. Calculations are performed with effective interactions which differ in their prediction of the nuclear matter compression modulus K_nm. Both time-dependent and constrained RMF results indicate that empirical GMR energies are best reproduced by an effective force with K_nm \approx 270 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that empirical giant monopole resonance (GMR) energies in spherical nuclei are best reproduced by a relativistic mean-field effective force with nuclear matter compression modulus \(K_{\rm nm} \approx 270\) MeV, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific set of effective interactions tested and the assumption that the time-dependent and generator coordinate methods provide a reliable microscopic description.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for isoscalar and isovector monopole oscillations in spherical doubly closed-shell nuclei, exciting the ground state by scaling the radial coordinate of the Dirac spinors and extracting eigenmode frequencies from Fourier analysis of the time-dependent monopole moment. They also applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) using constrained RMF solutions as generating functions, including explicit coherent states for the meson fields, to compute excitation energies and transition densities. Calculations were carried out with six effective interactions (NL1, NL3, NL-SH, NL2, HS, L1) that differ in their nuclear matter compression modulus.

## Key numbers

- The experimental GMR energy in \(^{208}\)Pb is \(13.7 \pm 0.3\) MeV; the NL3 parameter set (\(K_{\rm nm} = 271.8\) MeV) yields 14.1 MeV.
- The experimental GMR energy in \(^{90}\)Zr is around 16.5 MeV; NL1 (\(K_{\rm nm} = 211.7\) MeV) gives 15.6 MeV, NL3 gives 19.4 MeV.
- The experimental GMR energy in \(^{40}\)Ca is \(18 \pm 1\) MeV; the NL1 parameter set produces a frequency very close to this value.
- The nuclear matter compression modulus for NL1 is 211.7 MeV, for NL3 is 271.8 MeV, for NL-SH is 355.0 MeV, and for NL2 is 399.2 MeV.
- The isovector GMR in \(^{208}\)Pb is experimentally \(26 \pm 3\) MeV; the four non-linear parameter sets place most strength between 25 and 30 MeV.
- The isovector GMR in \(^{40}\)Ca is \(31.1 \pm 2.2\) MeV and in \(^{90}\)Zr is \(28.5 \pm 2.6\) MeV; the time-dependent calculations produce Fourier spectra generally in agreement with these values.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic GCM calculations by SRS (1994) and parallels non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA studies using Skyrme and Gogny interactions (Blaizot 1980, BBD 1995a), which suggested \(K_{\rm nm}\) in the range 210–220 MeV. The authors also reference the macroscopic analysis of Shlomo and Youngblood (1993), which found that the experimental GMR data do not constrain \(K_{\rm nm}\) better than 200–350 MeV. The results enable a more precise determination of nuclear matter compressibility from microscopic relativistic calculations, with the NL3 interaction providing a consistent description of GMR energies across the periodic table.
