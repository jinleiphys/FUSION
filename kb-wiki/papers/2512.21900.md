# Nucleon momentum distributions of complex nuclei from inclusive electron scattering

> Nucleon momentum distributions (NMDs) reveal essential information about Fermi motion and short-range correlations (SRCs). In extracting NMDs from inclusive electron scattering data, theoretical analyses, such as the scaling analysis, are typically employed. For complex nuclei, consistently treating the excitation energy of the residual system is a complicated task, leading to discrepancies between existing extracted NMDs and ab initio calculations, particularly around the Fermi momentum $k_F$. To address this issue, we introduce an improved description of the excitation energy in the framework of the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model. With this treatment, the extracted NMDs of complex nuclei show better agreement with ab initio calculations across the low- and high-momentum range, especially around $k_F$, successfully reproducing both the behaviors of Fermi motion and SRCs. These results provide a new experimental perspective on the interplay between Fermi motion and SRCs in complex nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an improved, momentum-dependent description of the excitation energy of the residual nuclear system within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, which yields extracted nucleon momentum distributions (NMDs) that show better agreement with ab initio calculations across both low- and high-momentum ranges, especially around the Fermi momentum \(k_F\), than previous extractions that used different approximations for the excitation energy.

## Method

The authors employ the \(y\)-scaling analysis within the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) to extract NMDs from inclusive \((e,e')\) cross sections for complex nuclei. They propose a unified, momentum-dependent expression for the average excitation energy \(E^{*\text{RFG}}_{A-1}\) of the residual \(A-1\) system: for \(k<k_F\) it is given by \(\sqrt{k_F^2+m_N^2}-\sqrt{k^2+m_N^2}\), and for \(k>k_F\) it is \(\sqrt{k^2+m_N^2}-\sqrt{k_F^2+m_N^2}-E^{R}_{A-1}\), where \(E^{R}_{A-1}\) is the recoil kinetic energy of the \(A-1\) system; a softplus function blends the two branches to ensure continuity at \(k=k_F\). The scaling function \(F(y)\) is extracted from experimental inclusive cross sections via Eq.~(1), and the NMD \(n(k)\) is obtained by differentiating \(F(y)\) as \(n(k)=-(1/(2\pi y))\,dF(y)/dy|_{|y|=k}\).

## Key numbers

* For \(^{12}\)C with \(k_F=285\) MeV/c, the excitation energy above \(k_F\) differs from that below \(k_F\) by only \(\sim 4\) MeV after subtracting the recoil energy.
* The extracted NMDs for \(^{12}\)C are shown for five kinematic settings with \(Q^2\) values ranging from 1.6 to 3.3 \((\text{GeV}/c)^2\): (3.356 GeV, \(25^\circ\)), (4.045 GeV, \(23^\circ\)), (4.045 GeV, \(30^\circ\)), (5.766 GeV, \(18^\circ\)), and (5.766 GeV, \(22^\circ\)).
* The values of \(k_F\) used in the model range from 240 to 300 MeV/c for nuclei from \(^3\)He to nuclear matter.
* For the deuteron, the minimal separation energy is \(E_{\min}=2.225\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(y\)-scaling extraction of NMDs from inclusive electron scattering, previously applied to the deuteron by Liang (2024), to complex nuclei by addressing the long-standing challenge of treating the excitation energy of the residual system. It improves upon earlier approaches such as the \(y_{\text{CW}}\) scaling of Ciofi degli Atti (2009), which used the kinetic energy of the correlated nucleon as the excitation energy and showed discrepancies with quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations in the Fermi-motion region (\(|y|<0.4\) GeV/c). The extracted NMDs for \(^{12}\)C and \(^{4}\)He show good agreement with QMC calculations by Wiringa (2014) using various \(NN+3N\) interactions (AV18+UX, NV2+3-Ia*, NV2+3-IIb), enabling a consistent experimental perspective on the interplay between Fermi motion and short-range correlations across a broad range of nuclei.
