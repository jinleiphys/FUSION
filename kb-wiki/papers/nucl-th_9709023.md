# $πY$ Phenomenology from $\bar KN$ Scattering

> The dispersive determination of $π{Y}Σ$ coupling constants ($Y = Λ, Σ$) and the implications for low-energy $\bar{K}N$ analysis are discussed. (Presented by G. Violini at the "7th Int. Symp. on Meson-Nucleon Physics and the Structure of the Nucleon, MENU '97", Vancouver, B.C., July 28th - August 1st, 1977).

# πY Phenomenology from KN Scattering

## Key claim

The dispersive determination of πYΣ coupling constants (Y = Λ, Σ) from current KN low-energy analyses is less reliable than previously claimed, because the P-wave contributions, particularly the J = 1/2 P-waves, are poorly determined in existing K- or M-matrix parametrizations, leading to strong energy-dependent variations in the extracted coupling constants that likely reflect inadequacies in the input amplitudes rather than physical structure.

## Method

The authors applied unsubtracted forward dispersion relations to three reactions, elastic πΛ scattering, elastic πΣ scattering, and the inelastic πΛ → πΣ process, using the B/ω amplitude for the elastic channels and the C/ω amplitude for the inelastic channel. The dispersive integrals were evaluated using Kim's K-matrix parametrization (1971) for the low-energy region from threshold to 550 MeV/c, resonance saturation in a narrow-width approximation for the intermediate region (550–2000 MeV/c), and a Regge-pole model extended via SU(3) symmetry for the high-energy region, with the contributions from each partial wave separated to assess their individual impact on the extracted couplings.

## Key numbers

- The πΛΣ coupling squared from πΛ elastic scattering at the KN threshold: G²_{πΛΣ}/4π = 17.5 (including all partial waves) versus 5.0 (with P_{11} wave subtracted)
- The πΣΣ coupling squared from πΣ elastic scattering at the KN threshold: G²_{πΣΣ}/4π = 11.2 (including all partial waves) versus 3.9 (with P_{01} and P_{11} waves subtracted)
- The product G_{πΛΣ}G_{πΣΣ}/4π from πΛ → πΣ at the KN threshold: approximately 5.0 after P_{11} wave subtraction, consistent with the individual squared couplings after the same subtraction
- The asymptotic total cross sections used: σ_{tot}^{πΛ(Σ)} = 12.42 mb · (s/s₀)^{0.0808} + 25.13 mb (17.25 mb) · (s/s₀)^{-0.525} with s₀^{1/2} = 1 GeV
- The Λ pole in the πΣ amplitude lies at ω_Σ = 71.35 MeV, below the πΣ threshold

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersive determinations of πYΣ couplings by Kim (1971) and others (Refs. 5–10 in the paper), updating the inputs and systematically testing the stability of results against variations in the energy at which dispersion relations are evaluated. The authors find that the strong energy dependence of the extracted couplings, particularly the variation of G²_{πΛΣ}/4π with energy, mirrors a similar energy dependence found in KN scattering by a previous study (Ref. 18), where the slope d(G²_{KNΛ}/4π)/dν ≈ 30 GeV⁻² at the KN threshold was observed. The results suggest that the low-energy S = −1 meson-nucleon sector is less well known than currently believed, and that the forthcoming DAΦNE facility could provide the improved experimental data needed to resolve these ambiguities.
