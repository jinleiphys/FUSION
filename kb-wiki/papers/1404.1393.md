# The Effect of Sterile States on the Magnetic Moments of Neutrinos

> We briefly review recent work exploring the effect of light sterile neutrino states on the neutrino magnetic moment as explored by the reactor and solar neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while light sterile neutrino states could in principle affect the measured neutrino magnetic moment, the Standard Model prediction for this quantity is too small to be experimentally accessible in the foreseeable future, and any measured value larger than the Standard Model prediction would require unfolding contributions from active and sterile states.

## Method

The authors calculate the folded differential cross section for neutrino-electron scattering including massive neutrino states, using the antineutrino spectrum from fissioning 235U for reactor neutrinos and considering three mass scenarios: light-to-heavy, equal-mass, and heavy-to-light transitions. They then compute the Standard Model prediction for the effective neutrino magnetic moment for both reactor and solar neutrinos, incorporating oscillations and the mixing matrix for three active flavors plus one sterile state, for both Dirac and Majorana neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- For reactor experiments, the experimental limit from GEMMA and TEXONO is compared to Standard Model predictions that are well below this limit
- For solar neutrinos, Super-Kamiokande sets a limit of mu_nu <= 3.6 x 10^-10 mu_B at 90% C.L.
- The folded differential cross section does not appreciably change from the massless neutrino limit even for neutrino masses up to ~10 keV
- For neutrino masses in the range 10 keV < m_nu <= m_e, the folded differential cross section is reduced for light-to-heavy and heavy-to-light transitions, while equal-mass cases show enhancement that does not change significantly from zero mass
- The Standard Model prediction for Dirac neutrinos shows identical effective magnetic moments for solar and reactor neutrinos when m_min is in the range 0.1 to 1 eV
- For Majorana neutrinos, the solar neutrino effective magnetic moment is always slightly higher than the reactor case but within the same order of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Balantekin and Vassh, 2013) on the effects of light sterile neutrinos on reactor neutrino magnetic moments by including solar neutrino measurements and providing more detailed calculations. The paper builds on the anomalous experimental results that suggest possible light sterile neutrino states, and it enables future interpretation of neutrino magnetic moment measurements if sterile states are discovered, providing a framework to separate active and sterile contributions. The calculations use mixing parameters from the Particle Data Group compilation (Beringer et al., 2012) and compare to experimental limits from GEMMA (Beda et al., 2013), TEXONO (Deniz et al., 2009), and Super-Kamiokande (Liu et al., 2004).
