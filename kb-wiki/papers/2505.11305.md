# Systematic analysis of double Gamow-Teller sum rules

> Sum rules are important bulk properties of transition strength functions for atomic nuclei. Unlike the Ikeda sum rule for single Gamow-Teller transition, double Gamow-Teller transition sum rules rely on the details of many-body wavefunctions. We approximate the shell model ground state with nucleon-pair condensates, by projection after variation, and compute double Gamow-Teller (DGT) transition sum rules from both $β+$ and $β-$ directions. By systematic investigation of DGT sum rules of even-even nuclei in the $1s0d$, $1p0f$ major shells, we quantitatively estimate the model-dependent fractions in the sum rules, and analyze the importance of double isospin-analogue state in the DGT strength function.

## Key claim

The double Gamow-Teller (DGT) sum rules contain both model-independent and model-dependent terms, and the authors quantitatively estimate that for nuclei with neutron excess N-Z >= 4, the model-independent component exhausts more than 80% of the sum rule, but this fraction depends on the specific nucleus and valence space.

## Method

The authors approximate the shell model ground state using angular-momentum projected nucleon-pair condensates (PVPC) in the 1s0d and 1p0f major shells, optimizing pair structure coefficients variationally against the shell-model Hamiltonian. They compute DGT sum rules S_DGT+ and S_DGT- directly as expectation values of four-body operators, and benchmark the one-body spin-isospin sum rules S_GT+ and S_sigma against exact shell-model results from the BIGSTICK code using the USDB interaction (1s0d shell) and GX1A interaction (1p0f shell).

## Key numbers

- For even-even nuclei in the 1s0d shell, PVPC overestimates S_GT+ by up to a factor of 2 compared to shell-model results
- For even-even nuclei in the 1p0f shell, PVPC overestimates S_GT+ by a factor of 1.2 to 1.5 compared to shell-model results
- The PVPC ground state energy lies above the exact shell-model ground state by typically 0.5 MeV for semi-magic nuclei and 1-2 MeV for open-shell nuclei
- When N-Z >= 4, the model-independent component of the DGT sum rule exhausts more than 80% of the total sum rule (based on results for even-A Neon isotopes from prior work)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of DGT sum rules developed by Muto (1992) and Sagawa (2016), who derived the J=0 and J=2 DGT sum rule formulas and studied semi-magic nuclei. The authors benchmark the PVPC approximation against full configuration-interaction shell model calculations using the BIGSTICK code (Johnson, 2013), finding that while S_GT+ is overestimated, S_sigma is remarkably faithful. This enables systematic surveys of DGT sum rules across multiple major shells, which is computationally prohibitive with exact shell-model diagonalization. The results are relevant to interpreting heavy-ion double charge-exchange experiments (Cappuzzello, 2023) and to empirical correlations between DGT strengths and neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements (Shimizu, 2018).
