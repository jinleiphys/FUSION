# Error Estimation for Moments Analysis in Heavy-Ion Collision Experiments

> Fluctuations of conserved quantities are predicted to be sensitive to the correlation length and connected to the thermodynamic susceptibility. Thus, moments of net-baryon, net-charge and net-strangeness have been extensively studied theoretically and experimentally to explore phase structure and bulk properties of QCD matters created in heavy ion collision experiment. As the moments analysis is statistics hungry study, the error estimation is crucial to extract physics information from the limited experimental data. In this paper, we will derive the limit distributions and error formula based on Delta theorem in statistics for various order moments used in the experimental data analysis. The Monte Carlo simulation is also applied to test the error formula.

# Error Estimation for Moments Analysis in Heavy-Ion Collision Experiments

## Key claim
The Delta theorem from statistics provides a rigorous method to derive error formulas for higher-order moments (variance, skewness, kurtosis) and moment products (Sσ, κσ², κσ/S) used in heavy-ion collision data analysis, but the reliability of these error estimates depends on the distribution being approximately normal and the sample size being sufficiently large. The paper explicitly notes that for the eighth-to-second order cumulant ratio, reliable results require statistics that may exceed the number of events accumulated by experiments.

## Method
The author applies the multi-dimensional Delta theorem to derive joint limiting distributions and covariance matrices for sample moments (μ₂, μ₃, μ₄) and for moment products (Sσ, κσ², κσ/S). The error formulas are expressed in terms of normalized central moments mᵣ = μᵣ/σʳ and the number of events n. Monte Carlo simulations using Skellam distributions (the difference of two independent Poisson distributions) with parameters μ₁=4.11 and μ₂=2.99, matching mean proton and anti-proton numbers in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV from STAR, are performed to validate the error formulas by comparing expected values and one-standard-deviation limits across 30 independent samples.

## Key numbers
- For a normal distribution, the variance of the sample standard deviation is Var(σ̂) = σ²/(2n), where n is the number of events.
- For a normal distribution, Var(Ŝ) = 6/n and Var(κ̂) = 24/n.
- For a normal distribution, Var(Ŝσ̂) = 6σ²/n and Var(κ̂σ̂²) = 24σ⁴/n.
- For a normal distribution, Var(Ĉ₆/Ĉ₂) = 720σ⁸/n and Var(Ĉ₈/Ĉ₂) = 40320σ¹²/n.
- Monte Carlo simulation parameters: μ₁=4.11, μ₂=2.99 for the Skellam distribution, corresponding to mean proton and anti-proton numbers in most central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- The eighth-to-second order cumulant ratio shows large error bars even with 250 million events.

## Context
This work provides the statistical foundation for error estimation in higher-moment analyses of conserved quantities (net-baryon, net-charge, net-strangeness) that are used to probe the QCD phase diagram, including the chiral phase transition and critical point. The method extends prior theoretical connections between experimental moments and thermodynamic susceptibilities from Lattice QCD (Lattice, 2009; MCheng, 2009) and the Hadron Resonance Gas model (HRG). The error formulas enable quantitative comparisons between experimental data from the STAR experiment and theoretical predictions, particularly for moment products such as κσ² = χ⁽⁴⁾_B/χ⁽²⁾_B and Sσ = χ⁽³⁾_B/χ⁽²⁾_B, and for higher-order cumulant ratios (Ĉ₆/Ĉ₂ and Ĉ₈/Ĉ₂) that are predicted to become negative near the chiral phase transition temperature.
