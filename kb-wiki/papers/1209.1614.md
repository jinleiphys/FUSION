# High-Energy QCD factorization from DIS to pA collisions

> The high-energy QCD factorization for Deep Inelastic Scattering and for proton-nucleus collisions using Wilson line formalism and factorization in rapidity is discussed. We show that in DIS the factorization in rapidity reduces to the $k_{\rm T}$-factorization when the 2-gluon approximation is applied, provided that the composite Wilson line operator is used in the high-energy Operator Product Expansion. We then show that the inclusive forward cross-section in proton-nucleus collisions factorizes in parton distribution functions, fragmentation functions and dipole gluon distribution function at one-loop level.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the high-energy QCD factorization in rapidity for Deep Inelastic Scattering reduces to the standard $k_{\rm T}$-factorization when the 2-gluon approximation is applied, but only if the composite Wilson line operator is used in the high-energy Operator Product Expansion. For proton-nucleus collisions, the inclusive forward cross-section factorizes into parton distribution functions, fragmentation functions, and dipole gluon distribution function at one-loop level, with the hedge that this factorization structure is shown explicitly only for the quark channel.

## Method

The author applies the background field technique to the T-product of two electromagnetic currents in the presence of a gluon field, which in the spectator frame reduces to a shock wave. For DIS, the high-energy Operator Product Expansion is used to express the T-product in terms of Wilson line operators and impact factors, with conformal symmetry restored by constructing composite operators that absorb rapidity-divergence counterterms. For pA collisions, one-loop Feynman diagrams (real and virtual) for the quark channel are calculated, and collinear divergences are absorbed into DGLAP evolution of parton distributions and fragmentation functions while rapidity divergences are absorbed into the Balitsky-BK evolution of the dipole gluon distribution.

## Context

This work extends the Balitsky hierarchy (1996) and the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation by providing the NLO impact factor for DIS in momentum space, which was previously available only as a combination of numerical and analytical expressions (Bartels and collaborators). The result for pA collisions establishes a QCD factorization formula in the saturation formalism at one-loop level, showing that the dipole gluon distribution follows the Balitsky-JIMWLK evolution equation while parton distributions follow DGLAP evolution. This enables future NNLO calculations where the $S^{(2)}$ distribution would follow NLO B-JIMWLK evolution, $S^{(4)}$ would follow LO B-JIMWLK, and a new $S^{(6)}$ operator would appear with no evolution at that order.
