# Quenching of vector boson-tagged jets at the LHC

> Electroweak boson-tagged jet measurements provide a promising experimental channel to accurately study the physics of jet production and propagation in dense QCD medium. In this talk, we present theoretical predictions for the nuclear-induced attenuation of the differential cross section for isolated photon-tagged and $\rm Z^0$-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions, and provide theoretical interpretations to the recent LHC data. We demonstrate quantitatively the significance of collisional and radiative energy losses, as revealed in the strong momentum asymmetry $dσ/dx_{VJ}$ and nuclear modification $\rm I_{AA}$ in central lead-lead reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents theoretical predictions for the nuclear-induced attenuation of vector boson-tagged jet production in heavy ion collisions, demonstrating that the observed momentum imbalance and nuclear modification can be explained by a combination of collisional and radiative energy losses, with the caveat that the results depend on the assumed jet-medium coupling strength (g = 2.0 or 2.2) and whether collisional energy loss is included.

## Method

The authors used Pythia 8 to evaluate the isolated photon-tagged and Z0-tagged jet differential cross sections in proton-proton collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, validating against CMS measurements at √s = 7 TeV. For lead-lead collisions, they implemented medium-induced parton shower effects using a soft gluon emission radiative energy loss approximation, incorporating both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms, with a factor f_loss(R) accounting for energy lost outside the jet cone. They then calculated the transverse momentum imbalance x_JV distribution and the nuclear modification factor I_AA for comparison to LHC data from ATLAS and CMS.

## Key numbers

- The fractional contribution from Compton scattering (q(bar q) + g → V + q) dominates Z0+jet and γ+jet production at approximately 80% over a wide pT range at √s = 5.02 TeV
- Two jet-medium coupling strengths were tested: g = 2.0 and g = 2.2, with the stronger coupling producing greater downshift in the x_JV distribution
- The nuclear modification factor I_AA shows strongest suppression along the diagonal region where pT^γ ≈ pT^J, with suppression in the region pT^J > pT^γ and enhancement in pT^J < pT^γ

## Context

This work extends theoretical calculations of jet quenching to the specific channel of vector boson-tagged jets, motivated by recent measurements from ATLAS and CMS collaborations at the LHC (Sirunyan 2017, CMS 2016, ATLAS 2016). The analysis builds on prior work on collisional energy loss (Neufeld 2011, Neufeld 2014) and radiative energy loss (Gyulassy 2000, Vitev 2005) to provide theoretical interpretation of the LHC data, demonstrating that this channel can constrain quark energy loss mechanisms in hot dense QCD medium. The results enable future precision studies of the flavor dependence of parton energy loss and properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
