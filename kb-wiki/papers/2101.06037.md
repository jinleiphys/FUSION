# Production of super-heavy nuclei in cold fusion reactions

> The model for the cold-fusion reactions related to the synthesis of super-heavy nuclei in collisions of heavy projectile-nuclei with $^{208}$Pb target nucleus is discussed. In the framework of this model the production of the compound nucleus by two paths through, the di-nuclear system and the fusion way, are taken into account simultaneously. The formation of the compound nucleus in the framework of the di-nuclear system is related to the transfer of nucleons from the light nucleus to the heavy one. The fusion way is linked to the sequential evolution of the nuclear shape from the system of contacting nuclei to the compound nucleus. It is shown that the compound nucleus is mainly formed by the fusion way in the cold-fusion reactions. The landscape of the potential energy related to the fusion path is discussed in detail. This landscape for very heavy nucleus-nucleus systems has the intermediate state, which is linked to the formation of both the compound nucleus and the quasi-fission fragments. The decay of the intermediate state is taken into account in the calculation of the compound nucleus production cross sections and the quasi-fission cross sections. The values of the cold-fusion cross sections obtained in the model are well agreed with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present a model for super-heavy nucleus synthesis in cold fusion reactions that simultaneously accounts for both the di-nuclear system (multi-nucleon transfer) and the fusion (smooth shape evolution) pathways to compound nucleus formation, and they show that the compound nucleus is mainly formed by the fusion way, contrary to models that rely solely on the DNS mechanism.

## Method

The model calculates the cross section for super-heavy nucleus production as the product of the transmission coefficient through the fusion barrier, the probability of compound nucleus formation, and the survival probability of the compound nucleus against fission. The probability of compound nucleus formation is determined by the ratio of decay widths for the fusion path and the DNS (multi-nucleon transfer) path to the total decay width of the di-nuclear system, which also includes widths for deep-inelastic collisions and quasi-elastic decay modes. The potential energy landscape for the fusion path is evaluated using a new two-parameter axial-symmetric shape parametrization that describes the evolution from two touching spherical nuclei to a deformed one-body compound nucleus, with macroscopic and shell-correction contributions to the potential energy calculated using the Yukawa-plus-exponential model and the WSBETA code.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical description of super-heavy nucleus synthesis by unifying the previously competing fusion and DNS mechanisms, which were treated separately in prior models such as those by Swiatecki (1990s) and by Volkov (1970s) and the Adamian group (2000s). The model enables more consistent calculations of cold-fusion cross sections for elements up to Z=118 and provides a framework for predicting production rates for future experiments aimed at elements 119 and 120, where no decay chains have yet been observed.
