# Conventional nuclear effects on generalized parton distributions of trinucleons

> The measurement of nuclear Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) will represent a valuable tool to understand the structure of bound nucleons in the nuclear medium, as well as the role of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the phenomenology of hard scattering off nuclei. By using a realistic microscopic approach for the evaluation of GPDs of 3He, it will be shown that conventional nuclear effects, such as isospin and binding ones, or the uncertainty related to the use of a given nucleon-nucleon potential, are rather bigger than in the forward case. These findings suggest that, if great attention is not paid to infer the properties of nuclear GPDs from those of nuclear parton distributions, conventional nuclear effects can be easily mistaken for exotic ones. It is stressed therefore that 3He, for which the best realistic calculations are possible, represents a unique target to discriminate between conventional and exotic effects. The complementary information which could be obtained by using a 3H target is also addressed.

## Key claim

Conventional nuclear effects (isospin, binding, and nucleon-nucleon potential dependence) on generalized parton distributions of trinucleons are significantly larger than in the forward (parton distribution) case, and if not properly accounted for, these conventional effects can be mistaken for exotic non-nucleonic degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author evaluates the unpolarized quark GPD \(H_q^3\) of \(^3He\) in the Impulse Approximation using a realistic non-diagonal spectral function calculated with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, convoluted with a model nucleon GPD based on the Double Distribution representation. The calculation is performed in the nuclear Breit Frame for low momentum transfers (\(-\Delta^2 \leq 0.25\) GeV\(^2\)) and small skewedness, and the results are compared to simpler approximations that neglect binding, use momentum distributions instead of spectral functions, or employ variable rescaling prescriptions.

## Key numbers

* The difference between the full calculation and a prescription using variable rescaling (Eq. app1) exceeds 10% for the \(d\) flavor at \(\Delta^2 = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\xi_3 = 0.2\) across all \(x_3\) values.
* The flavor dependence of nuclear effects between \(u\) and \(d\) quarks reaches approximately 10% at \(\Delta^2 = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\xi_3 = 0.2\).
* Using a momentum distribution instead of the full spectral function produces differences of 5% to 10% between \(x = 0.4\) and \(x = 0.7\) in the non-forward case.
* The super-ratio \(S_{du}\) for \(^3He\) and \(^3H\) targets deviates from unity by only a few percent for \(\Delta^2 \leq 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and low \(\xi\).
* The AV18 and AV14 potentials show no difference in the forward limit but produce a sizable difference in the non-forward case.

## Context

This work extends the Impulse Approximation calculation of GPDs for \(^3He\) presented in Scopetta (2004) (Ref. io) by studying flavor dependence, binding effects, and dependence on the nucleon-nucleon potential. It contrasts with prescriptions proposed by Kirchner and Mueller (2003) (Ref. km) for finite nuclei, which assume factorized \(\Delta^2\) dependence and do not capture the full nuclear effects. The analysis enables future experiments at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade to use simultaneous measurements on \(^3He\) and \(^3H\) targets to discriminate between conventional nuclear effects and exotic phenomena, extending the method proposed by Thomas (1999) (Ref. thom1) for parton distributions to the GPD case.
