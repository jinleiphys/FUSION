# Spin-polarized low-density neutron matter

> Low-density neutron matter is relevant to the study of neutron-rich nuclei and neutron star crusts. Unpolarized neutron matter has been studied extensively over a number of decades, while experimental guidance has recently started to emerge from the field of ultracold atomic gases. In this work, we study population-imbalanced neutron matter (possibly relevant to magnetars and to density functionals of nuclei) applying a Quantum Monte Carlo method that has proven successful in the field of cold atoms. We report on the first ab initio simulations of superfluid low-density polarized neutron matter. For systems with small imbalances, we find a linear dependence of the energy on the polarization, the proportionality coefficient being dependent on the density. We also present results for the momentum and pair distributions of the two fermionic components.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first *ab initio* simulations of superfluid low-density polarized neutron matter, finding a linear dependence of the energy on polarization at small imbalances (P < 0.1), with the proportionality coefficient depending on density. The abstract's claim of "first ab initio simulations" is hedged by the restriction to small polarizations and the use of the fixed-node approximation, which yields an upper bound to the true ground-state energy.

## Method

The author used a Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method with a fixed-node approximation, starting from a Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) trial wave function. The Hamiltonian included the Argonne v4' (AV4') potential for neutron-neutron interactions, with opposite-spin pairs interacting through the 1S0 channel and same-spin pairs including p-wave interactions via a perturbative correction. The trial wave function was a BCS-type wave function with Jastrow correlation terms, optimized to minimize the fixed-node energy for systems of 66 to 76 particles in a periodic cubic box at three densities.

## Key numbers

- Three total densities studied: ρ₁ = 6.65 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, ρ₂ = 2.16 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³, and ρ₃ = 5.32 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³, corresponding to 0.41%, 1.35%, and 3.32% of nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).
- For the unpolarized 33+33 system at ρ₃, the ground-state energy per particle is 2.133(1) MeV.
- For the polarized 39+33 system at ρ₃, the ground-state energy per particle is 2.286(2) MeV.
- The linear coefficient α(ρ) relating energy per particle to polarization is 0.37(5) MeV at ρ₁, 1.01(5) MeV at ρ₂, and 1.84(9) MeV at ρ₃.
- The pairing gap at ρ₃ is Δ = 1.05(11) MeV, which is 0.17(2) of the Fermi energy.
- The same-spin contribution to the total energy is at most 1% of the total energy; at ρ₃ the maximum perturbative correction is 9% of the total energy.

## Context

This work extends Quantum Monte Carlo methods previously applied to unpolarized neutron matter (Gezerlis 2008, 2010) and to polarized cold atomic gases (Carlson 2005, Pilati 2008) into the regime of superfluid spin-polarized neutron matter. Prior calculations of polarized neutron matter (Fantoni 2001, Vidana 2002, Margueron 2002, Rios 2005, Bordbar 2007, Sammarruca 2007, Perez 2009) focused on dense normal matter with a quadratic energy-polarization dependence, whereas this work finds a linear dependence in the low-density superfluid regime. The results could serve as input to density-functional theory approaches for nuclei (analogous to Bulgac 2008, 2011 for cold atoms) and are relevant to understanding magnetar crusts and neutron star observations.
