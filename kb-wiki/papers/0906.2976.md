# Rapidity and energy dependence of average transverse momentum and particle density in saturation models

> Saturation models -- colour glass condensate and string percolation -- impose a strict relation between the average transverse momentum, <P_T>, and the rapidity particle densities, dn/dy. By combining this relation with an appropriate evolution equation for dn/dy, and imposing energy-momentum conservation, we obtain a fair description of data, for generic AB collisions (hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus) at all rapidities and (high) energies. Predictions are given for the LHC.

# Rapidity and energy dependence of average transverse momentum and particle density in saturation models

## Key claim

Saturation models (colour glass condensate and string percolation) impose a strict relation between average transverse momentum and rapidity particle density; by combining this relation with a logistic evolution equation for particle density and imposing energy-momentum conservation, the authors obtain a fair description of data for generic AB collisions at all rapidities and high energies, but the work is carried out explicitly within percolation theory and several assumptions (rescaling corrections, kinematic corrections to transverse momentum) remain phenomenological without full theoretical justification.

## Method

The authors start from the string percolation model (SPM), where the transverse density parameter xi determines both the particle density dn/dy and the average transverse momentum via a colour suppression factor F(xi). They combine this with a logistic evolution equation for dn/dy that describes the generation of lower-rapidity strings from higher-rapidity ones, and impose energy-momentum conservation through integrals of the transverse effective mass over rapidity. The model parameters (alpha, delta, lambda, gamma) are fixed by fitting RHIC data for Au-Au and d-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, then used to make predictions for LHC Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters fixed from RHIC data: alpha = 0.347, delta = 0.851, lambda = 0.270, gamma = 0.21
- Single string average transverse momentum parameter: m = 0.23 GeV (used as effective mass)
- Ratio of string radius to proton radius: r/R_P = 1/4
- For d-Au 0-20% centrality: model predicts N_d = 1.9 (experiment 2.0 +/- 0.1), N_Au = 14.5 (experiment 13.5 +/- 1.0), N_col = 18.0 (experiment 14.7 +/- 0.9), charged multiplicity N^ch_{|eta|<5.4} = 143 (experiment 157 +/- 10)
- For d-Au 80-100% centrality: model predicts N_d = 1.0 (experiment 1.1 +/- 0.2), N_Au = 1.2 (experiment 1.6 +/- 0.4), N_col = 1.2 (experiment 1.7 +/- 0.5), charged multiplicity N^ch_{|eta|<5.4} = 30 (experiment 28 +/- 3)
- Energy-momentum conservation ratio |P|/E for d-Au 0-20%: LHS = 0.76, RHS = 0.77
- Energy-momentum conservation ratio |P|/E for d-Au 80-100%: LHS = 0.09, RHS = 0.09
- Predictions made for LHC Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV and p-Pb at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV

## Context

This work extends the string percolation model framework developed by Dias de Deus (2007) that used a logistic evolution equation to describe the Glasma, and builds on the clarified relationship between SPM and CGC approaches established by Kharzeev (2006), Dias de Deus (2006), and Armesto (2006). The authors previously made predictions for LHC observables using RHIC data to fix parameters (Abreu 2007, Dias de Deus 2007, Brogueira 2007), and found agreement with CGC/Glasma results for forward-backward correlations (Armesto 2006). The present work adds average transverse momentum to the model via the saturation relation from McLerran (2001) and Schaffner-Bielich (2001), and imposes energy-momentum conservation following Dias de Deus (2007). The results enable predictions for LHC Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions, but the authors note that explicit dependence on participant numbers and energy-momentum conservation have not been worked out in detail within the CGC framework.
