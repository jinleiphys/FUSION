# $Λ$ polarization in peripheral collisions at moderate relativistic energies

> The polarization of $Λ$ hyperons from relativistic flow vorticity is studied in peripheral heavy ion reactions at FAIR and NICA energies, just above the threshold of the transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Previous calculations at higher energies with larger initial angular momentum, predicted significant $Λ$ polarization based on the classical vorticity term in the polarization, while relativistic modifications decreased the polarization and changed its structure in the momentum space. At the lower energies studied here, we see the same effect namely that the relativistic modifications decrease the polarization arising from the initial shear flow vorticity.

# Λ Polarization in Peripheral Collisions at Moderate Relativistic Energies

## Key claim

At FAIR and NICA energies just above the Quark-Gluon Plasma transition threshold, relativistic modifications decrease the Λ polarization arising from initial shear flow vorticity, just as they do at higher energies, but the net average polarization in the y-direction remains non-zero due to strong initial shear flow and vorticity from the initial orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D hydrodynamic simulations using the PICR method with an initial Yang-Mills flux-tube model describing the first 2.5 fm/c of the collision, followed by hydrodynamical evolution for another 4.75 fm/c to a freeze-out time of 7.25 fm/c. The thermal vorticity was computed from the inverse temperature four-vector field, and the Λ polarization was calculated using the Fermi-Juttner distribution with two terms: a classical vorticity term and a relativistic modification term. The Bag Model equation of state was applied throughout the hydro simulation.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at NICA energy (√sNN = 9.3 GeV), the averaged y-component of Λ polarization is 1.82% at freeze-out time 2.5+4.75 fm/c
- For U+U collisions at FAIR energy (√sNN = 8.0 GeV) at the same freeze-out time, the averaged y-component of Λ polarization is 1.85%
- For U+U collisions at 8 GeV/A at an earlier freeze-out time of 2.5+1.7 fm/c = 4.2 fm/c, the averaged y-component of Λ polarization is 2.02%
- The classical vorticity term (first term) of the y-component of polarization has a maximum of -26% for FAIR U+U at √sNN = 8.0 GeV
- The relativistic modification term (second term) of the y-component has a maximum of +22% for the same reaction
- The x-component of the first term is about ±6%, about four times smaller than the y-component
- The x-component of the second term is about ±17%, about half of the y-component
- The z-component of both terms has a maximum of ±2%
- The average thermal vorticity Ωzx for Au+Au at √sNN = 4.65+4.65 GeV was 0.0847 at t=0.17 fm/c and 0.0739 at t=3.56 fm/c
- The average standard vorticity Ωzx for Au+Au at √sNN = 4.65+4.65 GeV was 0.1297 at t=0.17 fm/c and 0.0736 at t=3.56 fm/c
- For Au+Au at √sNN = 4.0+4.0 GeV, the relativistic weighted thermal vorticity Ωzx was 0.0856 at t=0.34 fm/c and 0.0658 at t=3.72 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous calculations at higher RHIC and LHC energies (Becattini 2013, Becattini et al. 2013) that predicted significant Λ polarization from classical vorticity, with relativistic modifications decreasing the polarization and changing its momentum-space structure. The results are consistent with the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program's measurements of significant Λ and anti-Lambda polarizations with largest values at lowest energies (Lisa 2016). The study enables predictions for the upcoming FAIR CBM and NICA experiments, where fixed-target setups can determine the event-by-event center-of-mass frame more accurately than collider experiments, allowing high-resolution measurement of the momentum-space dependence of Λ polarization to distinguish between competing polarization mechanisms.
