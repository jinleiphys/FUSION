# Emulating multi-channel scattering based on the eigenvector continuation in the discrete basis formalism

> We construct an emulator for a multi-channel scattering problem based on the eigenvector continuation. To this end, we employ the Kohn variational principle formulated in the discrete basis formalism. We apply this to one-dimensional scattering problems with a Gaussian barrier. Both for a single-channel and two-channel problems, we demonstrate that the penetration probability as well as the wave functions are well reproduced by the emulator. In particular, the energy dependence of the penetrability in a wider range of energy from well below the barrier to well above the barrier is successfully reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an emulator for multi-channel scattering using eigenvector continuation combined with the Kohn variational principle in a discrete basis formalism, and demonstrates that it reproduces exact penetrabilities and wave functions for one-dimensional Gaussian barrier problems; however, the emulator's accuracy depends on the number of basis states (N_EC) and the relative error increases to O(10^{-1}) at energies well below the barrier even when N_EC=6.

## Method

The authors discretize the one-dimensional coordinate on a mesh and represent the Hamiltonian as a matrix using a three-point formula for the second derivative. They solve the scattering problem via the Kohn variational principle in the discrete basis formalism, then construct an eigenvector continuation emulator by linearly superposing solutions obtained with different potential parameters (varying the barrier height V_0 for single-channel, or the coupling strength F_0 for two-channel). The emulator reduces the matrix inversion dimension from N+1 (602 mesh points) to N_EC+1 (as few as 7), achieving a computational speedup factor of about 10 for single-channel and about 20 for two-channel problems.

## Key numbers

- For the single-channel Gaussian barrier (V_0=100 MeV, s=3 fm, m=29 m_N), the eigenvector continuation with N_EC=6 reproduces the exact penetrability with a relative error of about 10^{-4} at energies above the barrier, increasing to O(10^{-1}) at energies below the barrier.
- The wave function at the barrier-top energy (E=100 MeV) is reproduced within O(10^{-3}) for both real and imaginary parts with N_EC=6.
- The dimension of the matrix to be inverted is reduced from N+1=602 in the exact calculation to N_EC+1=7 in the eigenvector continuation with N_EC=6.
- The computation time for 1000 repeated calculations is reduced by a factor of about 10 with the eigenvector continuation for the single-channel case.
- For the two-channel problem (V_0=100 MeV, F_0=3 MeV, s=s_f=3 fm, epsilon=1 MeV), the eigenvector continuation with N_EC=5 reproduces the exact result, with a computational speedup factor of about 20.

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation method, previously applied to bound state problems (Ekstrom 2019, Konig 2020) and nuclear reactions (Furnstahl 2020, Drischler 2021), to multi-channel scattering using the discrete basis formalism developed by Fanto (2018) and Bertsch (2019) for fission reactions. The emulator enables efficient parameter variation for uncertainty quantification and could be extended to realistic three-dimensional scattering problems (Hagino 1999, Hagino 2012) for fitting experimental data to determine deformation parameters of colliding nuclei (Gupta 2020, Gupta 2023).
