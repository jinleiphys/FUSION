# Electroweak axial structure functions and improved extraction of the $V_{ud}$ CKM matrix element

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the $γW$ interference radiative correction to the neutron $β$-decay matrix element. Within a dispersion relations approach, we compute the axial-vector part of the $γW$ box amplitude $\Box^{γW}_{A}$ in terms of the isoscalar part of the $F_3^{γW}$ interference structure function. Using the latest available phenomenology for $F_3^{γW}$ from the nucleon elastic, resonance, deep-inelastic, and Regge regions, we find the real part of the box correction to be $\Box^{γW}_A = 3.90(9) \times 10^{-3}$. This improved correction gives a theoretical estimate of the CKM matrix element $|V_{ud}|^2=0.94805(26)$, which represents a 4$σ$ violation of unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved calculation of the axial-vector part of the gamma-W box radiative correction to neutron beta decay, finding a value of 3.90(9) x 10^-3 that is higher than recent results from Czarnecki et al. (2019) and Seng et al. (2018, 2019), leading to a 4-sigma violation of CKM unitarity rather than the 2.5-sigma discrepancy reported by Czarnecki et al. (2019).

## Method

The authors compute the axial-vector gamma-W box amplitude using a dispersion relations approach, expressing the correction in terms of the isoscalar part of the F3^(gamma-W) interference structure function. They separate the structure function into four kinematic regions: nucleon elastic, deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) at high Q^2 and high W^2, Regge region at high W^2 but low Q^2, and resonance plus background region at low W^2. For the DIS region they use leading-twist parton distribution functions from global QCD analyses, for the Regge region they fit a hybrid model to neutrino scattering data from Bosetti et al. (1982), and for the elastic region they use parametrizations of the nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors.

## Key numbers

- Box correction: Box^(gamma-W)_A = 3.90(9) x 10^-3
- CKM matrix element: |V_ud|^2 = 0.94805(26), representing a 4-sigma violation of unitarity
- Elastic contribution: Box^(gamma-W)_A(el) = 1.05(4) x 10^-3
- DIS contribution: Box^(gamma-W)_A(DIS) = 2.29(3) x 10^-3
- Regge contribution: Box^(gamma-W)_A(Reg) = 0.37(7) x 10^-3
- Nucleon axial coupling: g_A = 1.2756(13)
- Axial meson mass: m_A = 1.23(4) GeV
- Boundary between DIS and Regge regions: Q_0^2 = 2 GeV^2 (varied between 1 and 2 GeV^2)
- Boundary between resonance and high-W regions: W_0^2 = 4 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relations approach of Seng et al. (2018, 2019) for the gamma-W box correction, using improved phenomenological input and a more systematic assessment of uncertainties from nonperturbative QCD. The result is higher than the earlier calculation by Marciano and Sirlin (2006) and the updated analysis by Czarnecki et al. (2019), leading to a larger discrepancy with Standard Model unitarity. The improved precision enables more stringent tests of CKM unitarity and motivates further experimental and theoretical work on the F_3^(gamma-W) structure function, particularly in the Regge region where data from Bosetti et al. (1982) provide the main constraints.
