# R-Matrix theory with level-dependent boundary condition parameters

> I present a new formalism of the R-matrix theory where the formal parameters for the resonance energies and widths are identical to the observed values. By allowing the boundary condition parameters to vary from level to level, the freedom required to adjust the formal parameters for the pole positions to the observed values is obtained. The basis of the resulting theory becomes nonorthogonal, and I describe the procedure to construct a consistent R-matrix theory with such a nonorthogonal basis. And by adjusting the normalization of the states that form the basis, the formal parameters for the reduced decay widths also become the same as those observed, leaving no formal parameters that are different from the observed ones. A demonstration of the developed theory to the elastic 12C+p scattering data is presented.

# R-Matrix theory with level-dependent boundary condition parameters

## Key claim
By allowing boundary condition parameters to vary from level to level (rather than being level-independent as in conventional R-matrix theory), the formal parameters for resonance energies, widths, and reduced decay widths can be made identical to the observed values, provided the basis states are allowed to be nonorthogonal.

## Method
The author constructs a new R-matrix formalism in which the orthogonality condition for the basis states in the internal region is released, permitting level-dependent boundary condition parameters B_{λc}. The boundary condition parameters are set to the shift factor S_c(E_λ) at each level's pole position, and the diagonal inner products J_{λλ} are chosen according to Eq. (Kll) involving the energy derivative of the shift factor. The resulting collision matrix is constructed using a nonorthogonal basis, and a Mathematica code implementing the formalism is applied to elastic 12C+p scattering data.

## Key numbers
- For 12C+p elastic scattering, three low-lying resonances of 13N are considered: J^π = 1/2+ at 0.421 MeV, 3/2- at 1.559 MeV, and 5/2+ at 1.604 MeV (center-of-mass energies relative to the 12C+p threshold).
- The observed parameters from Descouvemont are used directly as the formal parameters in the new formalism, with no conversion needed.
- The relation between formal and observed reduced widths in the conventional theory is given by γ_{λc}^{obs} = \check{γ}_{λc} / sqrt(1 + Σ_{c'} \check{γ}_{λc'}^2 S_{λc'}'), where S_{λc'}' is the energy derivative of the shift factor at E_λ.

## Context
This work extends the R-matrix theory originally developed by Wigner and Eisenbud (1937) and Kapur and Peierls (1938), where formal parameters differ from observed values via a nonlinear conversion. It shares features with the level-dependent boundary condition schemes of Barker (1971) and Azuma et al. (2010), and with the single-channel formalism of Angulo and Descouvemont (2000) that eliminates level shifts. The work is most similar to Brune (2002), who introduced an alternative parametrization using observed pole locations as inputs; however, in the present formalism the theory is constructed so that all formal parameters directly equal observed values without requiring a transformation. The new approach enables simplified calculations where no conversion between formal and observed parameters is needed, as demonstrated for 12C+p elastic scattering.
