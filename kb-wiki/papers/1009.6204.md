# Chiral Perturbation Theory, the ${1/N_c}$ expansion and Regge behaviour determine the structure of the lightest scalar meson

> The leading $1/N_c$ behaviour of Unitarised Chiral Perturbation Theory distinguishes the nature of the $ρ$ and the $σ$. At one loop order the $ρ$ is a ${\bar q}q$ meson, while the $σ$ is not. However, semi-local duality between resonances and Regge behaviour cannot be satisfied for larger $N_c$, if such a distinction holds. While the $σ$ at $N_c=3$ is inevitably dominated by its di-pion component, Unitarised Chiral Perturbation Theory beyond one loop order reveals that as $N_c$ increases above 6-8, the $σ$ has a sub-dominant ${\bar q}q$ fraction up at 1.2 GeV. Remarkably this ensures semi-local duality is fulfilled for the range of $N_c \lesssim 15$, where the unitarisation procedure adopted applies.

## Key claim
The sigma meson (σ) at Nc=3 is dominated by its di-pion component and is not a pure quark-antiquark (qq̅) state, but Unitarised Chiral Perturbation Theory beyond one loop reveals that as Nc increases above 6-8, the σ develops a sub-dominant qq̅ fraction near 1.2 GeV, which ensures semi-local duality between resonances and Regge behaviour is fulfilled for Nc ≲ 15-30.

## Method
The authors used the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to unitarise one-loop and two-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory (χPT) for ππ scattering, incorporating the leading 1/Nc dependence of the low-energy constants (LECs). They computed the Nc evolution of the ρ and σ pole trajectories in the complex energy plane and evaluated Finite Energy Sum Rules (FESRs) for t-channel isospin amplitudes to test semi-local duality as a function of Nc.

## Key numbers
- The ρ pole at Nc=3 has a width of approximately 150 MeV and becomes narrower as Nc increases, consistent with a qq̅ state.
- The σ pole at Nc=3 has a width of at least 500 MeV and its shape is not Breit-Wigner-like.
- For Nc above 6-8, the σ pole trajectory turns around and moves back toward the real axis above 1 GeV, indicating a sub-dominant qq̅ component near 1.2 GeV.
- Semi-local duality is fulfilled for Nc ≲ 15-30, the range where the unitarisation procedure applies.
- The P-wave scattering length from Regge analysis gives mπ² a₁¹ = (3.4 ± 0.5)·10⁻², compared to the dispersive value (3.79 ± 0.06)·10⁻² from Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler.
- The D-wave scattering length from Regge analysis gives mπ⁴ a₂⁰ = (1.67 ± 0.19)·10⁻³, compared to the dispersive value (1.75 ± 0.03)·10⁻³ from Colangelo, Gasser and Leutwyler.
- The Regge slope α' is approximately 0.9 GeV⁻², with the ρ trajectory having α₀ = 0.467 and α' = 0.889 GeV⁻².
- The Pomeron trajectory is α_P(t) = 1.083 + 0.25 t (with t in GeV² units).

## Context
This work extends the earlier one-loop UChPT study by Pelaez (2003) which showed the σ becomes wider with increasing Nc, and the two-loop analysis by Pelaez and Rios (2006) which hinted at a turnaround in the σ pole trajectory. It resolves a paradox: if the σ were purely non-qq̅, semi-local duality would fail for larger Nc because the ρ would become delta-function-like with nothing to cancel. The result enables future studies of the σ's internal structure and constrains higher-order LECs in χPT, with the FESR ratios providing a test that the LECs must satisfy.
