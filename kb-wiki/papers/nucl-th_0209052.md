# Neutron Densities from a Global Analysis of Medium Energy Proton Nucleus Elastic Scattering

> A new method for extracting neutron densities from intermediate energy elastic proton-nucleus scattering observables uses a global Dirac phenomenological (DP) approach based on the Relativistic Impulse Approximation (RIA). Data sets for Ca40, Ca48 and Pb208 in the energy range from 500 MeV to 1040 MeV are considered. The global fits are successful in reproducing the data and in predicting data sets not included in the analysis. Using this global approach, energy independent neutron densities are obtained. The vector point proton density distribution is determined from the empirical charge density after unfolding the proton form factor. The other densities are parametrized. This work provides energy independent values for the RMS neutron radius, R_n and the neutron skin thickness, S_n, in contrast to the energy dependent values obtained by previous studies. In addition, the results presented in paper show that the expected rms neutron radius and skin thickness for Ca40 is accurately reproduced. The values of R_n and S_n obtained from the global fits that we consider to be the most reliable are given as follows: for Ca40 R_n is 3.314 > R_n > 3.310 fm and S_n is -0.063 > S_n > -0.067 fm; for Ca48 R_n is 3.459 > R_n > 3.413 fm and S_n is 0.102 > S_n > 0.056 fm; and for Pb208 R_n is 5.550 > R_n > 5.522 and S_n is 0.111 > S_n > 0.083 fm. These values are in reasonable agreement with nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models and with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The results from the global fits for Ca48 and Pb208 are generally not in agreement with the usual relativistic mean-field models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new global Dirac phenomenological approach based on the Relativistic Impulse Approximation (RIA) can extract energy-independent neutron densities and neutron skin thicknesses from medium-energy proton-nucleus elastic scattering data, in contrast to previous studies that produced energy-dependent values. The authors hedge that the most reliable results come from a specific subset of their sensitivity tests (using Arndt NN amplitude set FA00 and form factor G1(q) with the SOG charge distribution), and that the extracted values for Ca-48 and Pb-208 are generally not in agreement with usual relativistic mean-field models.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of elastic proton-nucleus scattering data for Ca-40, Ca-48, and Pb-208 at energies between 500 MeV and 1040 MeV using a Dirac phenomenological approach based on the Relativistic Impulse Approximation (RIA). The vector point proton density was fixed from empirical charge densities after unfolding the proton form factor, while the vector neutron density and scalar proton and neutron densities were parametrized using a cosh functional form. The fits were performed over multiple momentum transfer ranges (from 0.0 fm^-1 to 1.5 fm^-1 up to 0.0 fm^-1 to 3.5 fm^-1) and tested for sensitivity to different effective field theory models, proton form factors, Arndt NN amplitude sets, charge distribution models, and data set selections.

## Key numbers

* For Ca-40: RMS neutron radius R_n between 3.314 fm and 3.310 fm, neutron skin thickness S_n between -0.063 fm and -0.067 fm (from the most reliable fits using four momentum transfer ranges, Arndt FA00 amplitudes, G1(q) form factor, and SOG charge distribution)
* For Ca-48: R_n between 3.459 fm and 3.413 fm, S_n between 0.102 fm and 0.056 fm (same most reliable fit conditions)
* For Pb-208: R_n between 5.550 fm and 5.522 fm, S_n between 0.111 fm and 0.083 fm (same most reliable fit conditions)
* For the most conservative final case combining all tests with five momentum transfer ranges: Ca-40 R_n between 3.350 fm and 3.300 fm, S_n between -0.008 fm and -0.080 fm; Ca-48 R_n between 3.505 fm and 3.421 fm, S_n between 0.148 fm and 0.058 fm; Pb-208 R_n between 5.589 fm and 5.513 fm, S_n between 0.156 fm and 0.076 fm
* For the most conservative final case with four momentum transfer ranges: Ca-40 R_n between 3.350 fm and 3.304 fm, S_n between -0.006 fm and -0.080 fm; Ca-48 R_n between 3.485 fm and 3.417 fm, S_n between 0.129 fm and 0.053 fm; Pb-208 R_n between 5.561 fm and 5.513 fm, S_n between 0.130 fm and 0.074 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier RIA and non-relativistic KMT analyses by Ray and Hoffmann, which did not produce energy-independent neutron densities and gave negative skin thicknesses for Ca-48 and Pb-208. The results are in reasonable agreement with nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models (Brown and collaborators) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov models with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (Ring and collaborators), but generally disagree with usual relativistic mean-field models. The extracted neutron densities and skin thicknesses are relevant for atomic parity violation experiments, antiprotonic atom analysis, neutron star crust studies, and nuclear astrophysics extrapolations to proton-rich or neutron-rich nuclei.
