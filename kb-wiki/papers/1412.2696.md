# Ab initio Bogoliubov coupled cluster theory for open-shell nuclei

> Ab initio many-body methods address closed-shell nuclei up to mass A ~ 130 on the basis of realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions. Several routes to address open-shell nuclei are currently under investigation, including ideas which exploit spontaneous symmetry breaking. Singly open-shell nuclei can be efficiently described via the sole breaking of $U(1)$ gauge symmetry associated with particle number conservation, to account for their superfluid character. The present work formulates and applies Bogoliubov coupled cluster (BCC) theory, which consists of representing the exact ground-state wavefunction of the system as the exponential of a quasiparticle excitation cluster operator acting on a Bogoliubov reference state. Equations for the ground-state energy and cluster amplitudes are derived at the singles and doubles level (BCCSD) both algebraically and diagrammatically. The formalism includes three-nucleon forces at the normal-ordered two-body level. The first BCC code is implemented in $m$-scheme, which will eventually permit the treatment of doubly open-shell nuclei. Proof-of-principle calculations in an $N_{\text{max}}=6$ spherical harmonic oscillator basis are performed for $^{16,18,20}$O, $^{18}$Ne, $^{20}$Mg in the BCCD approximation with a chiral two-nucleon interaction, comparing to results obtained in standard coupled cluster theory when applicable. The breaking of $U(1)$ symmetry is monitored by computing the variance associated with the particle-number operator. The newly developed many-body formalism increases the potential span of ab initio calculations based on single-reference coupled cluster techniques tremendously, i.e. potentially to reach several hundred additional mid-mass nuclei. The new formalism offers a wealth of potential applications and further extensions dedicated to the description of ground and excited states of open-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper formulates Bogoliubov coupled cluster (BCC) theory for open-shell nuclei by representing the exact ground-state wavefunction as the exponential of a quasiparticle excitation cluster operator acting on a Bogoliubov reference state that breaks U(1) gauge symmetry; the authors present proof-of-principle calculations but note that the formalism is not yet fully implemented with three-nucleon forces at the normal-ordered two-body level in the actual BCC code, and that the center-of-mass separation remains to be verified in the BCC framework.

## Method

The authors derived equations for the ground-state energy and cluster amplitudes at the singles and doubles level (BCCSD) both algebraically and diagrammatically, including three-nucleon forces at the normal-ordered two-body level. They implemented the first BCC code in m-scheme and performed calculations in an Nmax=6 spherical harmonic oscillator basis for 16,18,20O, 18Ne, and 20Mg using the BCCD approximation with a chiral two-nucleon interaction. The breaking of U(1) symmetry was monitored by computing the variance associated with the particle-number operator.

## Key numbers

- Calculations were performed in an Nmax=6 spherical harmonic oscillator basis for nuclei 16,18,20O, 18Ne, and 20Mg
- The formalism is designed to extend ab initio calculations based on single-reference coupled cluster techniques to potentially reach several hundred additional mid-mass nuclei
- The Hamiltonian includes two- and three-nucleon interactions, with the three-nucleon force truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level (Omega[k] with k less than or equal to 4)
- The particle-number variance Delta A^2 is computed via the formula involving the operators A and A^2 to monitor symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends single-reference coupled cluster theory, which previously addressed closed-shell nuclei up to mass A ~ 130 (Binder 2013), by breaking U(1) gauge symmetry to capture the superfluid character of singly open-shell nuclei. It follows a similar route recently exploited within self-consistent Gorkov-Green's function theory (Soma 2011, 2012, 2013) and complements multi-reference IMSRG techniques (Hergert 2013) and CC-based configuration interaction methods (Jansen 2014). The formalism enables future extensions to doubly open-shell nuclei via further breaking of SU(2) symmetry, exact restoration of U(1) and SU(2) symmetries, treatment of excited states through equation-of-motion methodology, and inclusion of triple corrections.
