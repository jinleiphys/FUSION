# Laser-coolable AcOH$^+$ ion for $\mathcal{CP}$-violation searches

> The AcOH${}^+$ molecular ion is identified as a prospective system to search for $\mathcal{CP}$-violation effects. According to our study AcOH${}^+$ belongs to the class of laser-coolable polyatomic molecular cations implying the large coherence time in the experiments to study symmetry violating effects of fundamental interactions. We perform both nuclear and high level relativistic coupled cluster electronic structure calculations to express experimentally measurable $\mathcal{T}$,$\mathcal{P}$-violating energy shift in terms of fundamental quantities such as the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM), electron electric dipole moment ($e$EDM) and dimensionless scalar-pseudoscalar nuclear-electron interaction constant. We further express nuclear MQM in terms of the strength constants of $\mathcal{CP}$-violating nuclear forces: quantum chromodynamics vacuum angle $\barθ$ and quark chromo-EDMs. The equilibrium geometry of AcOH${}^+$ in the ground and the four lowest excited electronic states was found to be linear. The calculated Franck-Condon factors and transition dipole moments indicate that the laser cooling using optical cycle involving the first excited state is possible for the trapped AcOH${}^+$ ions with the Doppler limit estimated to be~$\sim 4$~nK. The lifetime of the (0,1$^1$,0) excited vibrational state considered as a working one for MQM and $e$EDM search experiments is estimated to be $\sim 0.4$ sec.

## Key claim

The AcOH⁺ molecular ion is identified as a prospective system for CP-violation searches, with the hedge that the laser cooling feasibility and the closed optical cycle depend on the nearly diagonal Franck-Condon matrix for the (1)1/2 → (1)3/2 transition, which is predicted theoretically but requires experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors performed high-level relativistic coupled cluster calculations (FS-RCCSD and CCSD(T)) with relativistic pseudopotentials to compute potential energy surfaces, Franck-Condon factors, and transition dipole moments for the ground and four lowest excited electronic states of AcOH⁺. They also carried out nuclear structure calculations to express the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of ²²⁵Ac and ²²⁷Ac isotopes in terms of fundamental CP-violating parameters including the QCD vacuum angle θ̄ and quark chromo-EDMs.

## Key numbers

- Doppler limit for laser-cooled trapped AcOH⁺ ions estimated to be ~4 nK
- Lifetime of the (0,1¹,0) excited vibrational state (working state for MQM and eEDM searches) estimated to be ~0.4 sec
- Lifetime of the (1)3/2 electronic excited state estimated to be τ ~ 110 μs
- Franck-Condon factor for the (0,0,0) → (0,0,0) band of the (1)3/2→(1)1/2 transition: 0.8892, with vibrational branching ratio 91.6%
- Sum of first three Franck-Condon factors equals 0.9999, indicating a closed optical cycle
- Equilibrium Ac-O bond distance in ground state (1)1/2: 2.077 Å; O-H bond distance: 0.960 Å
- Term energy of (1)3/2 state: 7294 cm⁻¹; (1)5/2 state: 9469 cm⁻¹
- Ground state vibrational frequencies for ²²⁷AcOH⁺: ν₁(σ⁺) = 656 cm⁻¹, ν₂(π) = 423 cm⁻¹, ν₃(σ⁺) = 3868 cm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the search for CP-violating effects from diatomic molecules (such as ThO studied by the ACME collaboration, and ThF⁺) to polyatomic molecular ions, following proposals for linear triatomic systems like YbOH (Kozyryev 2017) and LuOH⁺ (Maison 2020). The study builds on previous calculations for actinium compounds including AcF, AcO⁺, and AcN (Flambaum 2019, Skripnikov 2020), and on the recent calculation of effective electric fields for AcOH⁺ and AcOCH₃⁺ (Kyuberis 2021). The results enable future experiments using trapped, laser-cooled AcOH⁺ ions to probe CP-violation parameters including the electron EDM, nuclear MQM, and scalar-pseudoscalar nucleus-electron interaction, with sensitivity to physics beyond the Standard Model.
