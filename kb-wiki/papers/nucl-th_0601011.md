# Structure and decay width of Θ^+ in a one-gluon exchange model

> The mass and decay width of the $Θ^+(1540)$ with isospin 0 are investigated in a constituent quark model comprising $uudd\bar{s}$ quarks. The resonance state for the $Θ^+$ is identified as a stable solution in correlated basis calculations. With the use of a one-gluon exchange quark-quark interaction, the mass is calculated to be larger than 2 GeV, increasing in order of the spin-parity, ${1/2}^-, {3/2}^-$ and ${1/2}^+ ({3/2}^+)$, and only the ${3/2}^-$ state has a small width to the $nK^{*+}$ decay. If the calculated mass is shifted to around 100 MeV above the $N+K$ threshold, the $Θ^+(1540)$ is possibly ${1/2}^+ ({3/2}^+)$ or ${3/2}^-$, though in the latter case it cannot decay to the $nK^{+}$ channel. In addition it is conjectured that other pentaquark state with different spin-parity exists below the $Θ^+(1540)$. The structure of the $Θ^+$ is discussed through the densities and two-particle correlation functions of the quarks and through the wave function decomposition to a baryon-meson model and a diquark-pair model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark mass calculated with a one-gluon exchange quark-quark interaction is larger than 2 GeV for all spin-parity states considered, and that only the 3/2⁻ state has a small decay width to the nK*⁺ channel; however, if the calculated mass is artificially shifted to around 100 MeV above the N+K threshold, the Θ⁺(1540) could possibly be 1/2⁺ (3/2⁺) or 3/2⁻, though in the latter case it cannot decay to the nK⁺ channel.

## Method

The authors performed a nonrelativistic constituent quark model calculation for the uudd‾s pentaquark system using a correlated Gaussian basis with a stochastic variational method. They employed the AL1 and TS one-gluon exchange potentials including color Coulomb, color magnetic, and confinement terms, and used a real stabilization method to identify resonance states and calculate decay widths from square-integrable basis functions. The wave function included all possible spin-isospin-color configurations with full antisymmetrization of the four quarks, and the structure was analyzed through densities, two-particle correlation functions, and decomposition into baryon-meson and diquark-pair models.

## Key numbers

- The calculated mass of the Θ⁺ is larger than 2 GeV for all spin-parity states, increasing in order: 1/2⁻, 3/2⁻, then 1/2⁺ (3/2⁺)
- The N+K threshold is 1486 MeV for the AL1 potential and 1451 MeV for the TS potential
- The calculated mass of the nucleon N(939) is 995 MeV (AL1) and 937 MeV (TS), with rms radii of 0.49 fm and 0.50 fm respectively
- The calculated mass of the K(496) meson is 491 MeV (AL1) and 514 MeV (TS), with rms radii of 0.31 fm and 0.33 fm respectively
- The calculated mass of the K*(892) meson is 903 MeV (AL1) and 813 MeV (TS), with rms radii of 0.42 fm and 0.41 fm respectively
- The experimental decay width of Θ⁺(1540) is reported as less than 25 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model calculations of the Θ⁺ pentaquark by Takeuchi (2003), Enyo (2004), Paris (2004), and Hiyama (2005), but differs by using a real stabilization method to handle the unbound nature of the resonance and by including all possible hidden color components. The calculation tests the validity of the diquark model of Jaffe (2003) and the diquark-triquark model of Karliner and Lipkin (2003) through dynamical calculation and wave function decomposition. The results enable future studies of whether the Θ⁺(1540) can be described as a compact pentaquark state or requires alternative explanations such as the KπN heptaquark system discussed by Bicudo (2003).
