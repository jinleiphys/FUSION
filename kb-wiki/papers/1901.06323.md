# Nuclear Charge Radii of $^{10,11}$B

> The first determination of the nuclear charge radius by laser spectroscopy for a five-electron system is reported. This is achieved by combining high-accuracy ab initio mass-shift calculations and a high-resolution measurement of the isotope shift in the $2s^2 2p\, ^2\mathrm{P}_{1/2} \rightarrow 2s^2 3s\, ^2\mathrm{S}_{1/2}$ ground state transition in boron atoms. Accuracy is increased by orders of magnitude for the stable isotopes $^{10,11}$B and the results are used to extract their difference in the mean-square charge radius $\langle r^2_\mathrm{c}\rangle^{11} - \langle r^2_\mathrm{c}\rangle^{10} = -0.49\,(12)\,\mathrm{fm}^2$. The result is qualitatively explained by a possible cluster structure of the boron nuclei and quantitatively used as a benchmark for new ab initio nuclear structure calculations using the no-core shell model and Green's function Monte Carlo approaches.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first determination of a nuclear charge radius using laser spectroscopy on a five-electron system (boron), achieving an improvement in accuracy by orders of magnitude for the stable isotopes 10B and 11B. The extracted difference in mean-square charge radius is -0.49(12) fm2, which is qualitatively explained by a possible cluster structure of the boron nuclei and used as a benchmark for ab initio nuclear structure calculations.

## Method

High-accuracy ab initio mass-shift calculations were performed for the 2s2 2p 2P1/2 → 2s2 3s 2S1/2 transition in boron using explicitly correlated Gaussian basis functions with up to K=8192 terms, treating finite nuclear mass effects up to (m/M)2 and relativistic/QED contributions up to m/M. A high-resolution measurement of the isotope shift was carried out using resonance ionization mass spectrometry on a thermal atomic beam of boron, with a tunable continuous-wave laser system generating 249.75 nm light via frequency doubling, and a frequency comb for long-term drift monitoring. The isotope shift was extracted from single-pass and double-pass spectra using Voigt profile fitting, and the field shift factor C = 16.91(9) MHz/fm2 was calculated to convert the measured field shift into the difference in mean-square charge radius.

## Key numbers

- Measured isotope shift δνIS = ν(10B) - ν(11B) = -5031.3(2.0) MHz in the 2p 2P1/2 → 3s 2S1/2 transition
- Calculated mass shift = -5023.00(8) MHz
- Extracted field shift = 8.8(2.0) MHz
- Field shift factor C = 16.91(9) MHz/fm2
- Difference in mean-square charge radius ⟨rc2⟩11 - ⟨rc2⟩10 = -0.49(12) fm2
- Natural Lorentzian linewidth of the transition: 40.1 MHz
- The result improves experimental precision from electron scattering by at least a factor of five

## Context

This work extends the technique of extracting nuclear charge radii from isotope shift measurements, previously applied to two- and three-electron systems (He, Li, Be) by Wang (2004), Mueller (2007), Ewald (2005), Sanchez (2006), Noertershaeuser (2009), and Krieger (2012), to the five-electron system of boron. The result is compared to new ab initio nuclear structure calculations using the no-core shell model (NCSM) with various chiral interactions and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) with the AV18+IL7 Hamiltonian, showing good agreement within uncertainties. The advances enable future high-precision investigations of a wider range of light nuclei, providing benchmarks for refining nuclear models.
