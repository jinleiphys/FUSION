# Final state interaction effects in semi-inclusive DIS off the deuteron

> The effects of the final state interaction (FSI) in semi-exclusive deep-inelastic scattering of electrons off the deuteron are analyzed paying particular attention to two extreme kinematical regions: i) the one where FSI effects are minimized, so that the quark distribution of bound nucleons could be investigated, and ii) the one where the re-interaction of the produced hadrons with the spectator nucleon is maximized, which would allow one to study the mechanism of hadronization of highly virtual quark. It is demonstrated that when the recoiling spectator nucleon is detected in the backward hemisphere with low momentum the effects from FSI are negligible, whereas at large transverse momenta of the spectator the FSI effects are rather large. Numerical estimates show that the FSI corrections are sensitive to the theoretical models of the hadronization mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final state interaction (FSI) effects in semi-exclusive deep-inelastic scattering off the deuteron are negligible when the recoiling spectator nucleon is detected in the backward hemisphere with low momentum, but become large at large transverse momenta of the spectator; the FSI corrections are sensitive to the theoretical model of the hadronization mechanism, meaning that the utility of the process for studying bound nucleon structure functions versus hadronization depends critically on the chosen kinematics.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for the process e + D → e' + X + N within the spectator mechanism, where the deep-inelastic scattering occurs on one nucleon (the active nucleon) and the other nucleon (the spectator) is detected in coincidence. They compare the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), where FSI are neglected, with a treatment that includes FSI via an eikonal S-matrix describing the rescattering of the nucleon debris off the spectator nucleon, characterized by an effective cross section σ_eff(z, Q², x) that depends on the hadronization model. The distorted momentum distribution n_D^FSI is computed using the Reid Soft Core and Bonn potentials for the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

* The ratio n_D^FSI/n_D shows significant differences between two models of the effective cross section at spectator momentum p_s ~ 0.2 GeV/c and emission angle θ_s ~ 90° (perpendicular to the momentum transfer).
* For quasi-elastic scattering, the effective cross section used is σ_eff = 44 mb (nucleon-nucleon total cross section).
* For the time- and Q²-dependent model of σ_eff, the constant cross section comparison case uses σ_eff = 20 mb.
* The two deuteron potentials (Reid Soft Core and Bonn) give essentially the same results at relatively small spectator momenta but very different results at |p_s| ≥ 0.25 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of semi-exclusive DIS on the deuteron, originally proposed for studying bound nucleon structure functions (tagging method, see e.g. Simula, Wally, and tagging references), by quantitatively including FSI effects. It builds on the hadronization model of Kopeliovich (kope1) and the FSI treatment for heavier nuclei by Boccalero et al. (bocla), applying it specifically to the deuteron case in preparation for planned experiments at JLab (Kuhn). The paper enables experimentalists to identify two distinct kinematic regimes: backward/low-momentum kinematics for studying the neutron structure function, and perpendicular/large-momentum kinematics for investigating the hadronization mechanism.
