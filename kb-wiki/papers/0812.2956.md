# Chiral susceptibility and the scalar Ward identity

> The chiral susceptibility is given by the scalar vacuum polarisation at zero total momentum. This follows directly from the expression for the vacuum quark condensate so long as a nonperturbative symmetry preserving truncation scheme is employed. For QCD in-vacuum the susceptibility can rigorously be defined via a Pauli-Villars regularisation procedure. Owing to the scalar Ward identity, irrespective of the form or Ansatz for the kernel of the gap equation, the consistent scalar vertex at zero total momentum can automatically be obtained and hence the consistent susceptibility. This enables calculation of the chiral susceptibility for markedly different vertex Ansaetze. For the two cases considered, the results were consistent and the minor quantitative differences easily understood. The susceptibility can be used to demarcate the domain of coupling strength within a theory upon which chiral symmetry is dynamically broken. Degenerate massless scalar and pseudoscalar bound-states appear at the critical coupling for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The chiral susceptibility can be rigorously defined via a Pauli-Villars regularisation procedure for QCD in-vacuum, and the scalar Ward identity ensures that the consistent scalar vertex at zero total momentum can be obtained automatically regardless of the form or Ansatz for the gap equation kernel, enabling calculation of the susceptibility for markedly different vertex Ansätze.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral susceptibility from the scalar vacuum polarisation at zero total momentum, using the scalar Ward identity to relate the scalar vertex to derivatives of the dressed-quark propagator functions. They employ two different Ansätze for the quark-gluon vertex, the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation and the Ball-Chiu (BC) vertex, within a simplified renormalisation-group-improved effective interaction that is ultraviolet finite. The gap equation is solved for the dressed-quark propagator, and the scalar vertex at zero momentum is obtained either by solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation or by direct differentiation of the propagator functions with respect to current-quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Critical interaction strength for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking: \( \mathcal{I}_c = 1.93 \) for the rainbow-ladder (RL) vertex and \( \mathcal{I}_c = 1.41 \) for the BC vertex, where \( \mathcal{I} = D/\omega^2 \).
- At the real-world reference interaction strength with \( \omega = 0.5 \) GeV: for RL vertex (\( D = 1.0 \) GeV\(^2\)), \( A(0) = 1.3 \), \( M(0) = 0.40 \) GeV, \( -(\langle \bar q q \rangle^0)^{1/3} = 0.25 \) GeV, \( \sqrt{\chi} = 0.39 \) GeV; for BC vertex (\( D = 0.5 \) GeV\(^2\)), \( A(0) = 1.1 \), \( M(0) = 0.28 \) GeV, \( -(\langle \bar q q \rangle^0)^{1/3} = 0.26 \) GeV, \( \sqrt{\chi} = 0.28 \) GeV.
- The product \( \omega D = (0.8\ \text{GeV})^3 \) defines the trajectory along which fitted in-vacuum low-energy observables are approximately constant for the RL interaction.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to QCD, building on prior studies of vacuum polarisations and susceptibilities by Zong (2005, 2006) and Shi (2006), and on the symmetry-preserving truncation scheme developed by Bhagwat (2004) and Munczek (1994). The results enable the chiral susceptibility to be used as a tool to demarcate the domain of coupling strength within which dynamical chiral symmetry breaking occurs, and to identify the critical coupling where degenerate massless scalar and pseudoscalar bound-states appear. The method can be applied to analyse nonzero temperature behaviour of QCD and to determine critical temperatures and exponents for chiral symmetry restoration, as previously studied by Blaschke (1998), Holl (1998), Aoki (2006), and Fu (2007).
