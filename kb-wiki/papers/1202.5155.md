# Transport properties of anisotropically expanding quark-gluon plasma within a quasi-particle model

> The bulk and shear viscosities ($η$ and $ζ$) have been studied for quark-gluon-plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions within semi-classical transport theory, in a recently proposed quasi-particle model of (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state. These transport parameters have been found to be highly sensitive to the interactions present in hot QCD. Contributions to the transport coefficients from both the gluonic sector and the matter sector have been investigated. The matter sector is found to be significantly dominating over the gluonic sector, in both the cases of $η$ and $ζ$. The temperature dependences of the quantities, $ζ/{\mathcal S}$, and $ζ/η$ indicate a sharply rising trend for the $ζ$, closer to the QCD transition temperature. Both $η$, and $ζ$ are shown to be equally significant for the temperatures that are accessible in the relativistic heavy ion collision experiments, and hence play crucial role while investigating the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma are computed using a semi-classical transport theory combined with a quasi-particle model of (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD, but the analysis is restricted to the Vlasov (anomalous) contribution from chromo-Weibel instabilities in the weak coupling limit, assumed valid only beyond 1.2 times the QCD transition temperature.

## Method
The author employed a linearized transport equation with a Vlasov-Dupree diffusive term that accounts for ensemble-averaged turbulent color fields, using equilibrium distribution functions from a quasi-particle model with effective fugacities for gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks. The perturbations to the distribution functions were solved analytically in the absence of collision terms, and the resulting shear and bulk viscosities were expressed in terms of the parton energy loss parameter \(\hat{q}\) and the speed of sound extracted from lattice data. Contributions from the gluonic sector and the matter sector (light quarks and strange quarks) were computed separately.

## Key numbers
- The strange quark mass was set to \(0.1\ \text{GeV}\) in the quasi-particle model.
- The QCD transition temperature from lattice data is cited as approximately \(175\ \text{MeV}\).
- The shear viscosity components are expressed analytically, e.g., \(\eta_g = \frac{T^6}{\hat{q}} \frac{64(N_c^2-1)}{3\pi^2} \text{PolyLog}[6, z_g]\) for gluons, with \(N_c=3\).
- The bulk viscosity components involve integrals over transverse momentum \(p_T\) and longitudinal momentum \(p_z\) with a logarithmic factor \(\ln(p_T / (\sqrt{6} T))\).

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of shear and bulk viscosities for pure SU(3) gauge theory by Chandra and collaborators (2011, 2012) to the case of (2+1)-flavor QCD, incorporating the matter sector (quarks and antiquarks) which had been largely ignored. It builds on the mechanism proposed by Asakawa, Bass, and Muller (2006) for explaining small viscosity via chromo-Weibel instabilities in an anisotropically expanding plasma, and uses the quasi-particle model of Chandra and collaborators (2011) for the lattice QCD equation of state. The results enable future studies of the interplay between anomalous and collisional contributions to transport coefficients and their impact on hydrodynamic modeling at RHIC and LHC.
