# Enhanced nuclear Schiff moment and time reversal violation in 229Th-containing molecules

> Octupole deformation results in a strongly enhanced collective Schiff moment in 229Th nucleus. An additional enhancement of time reversal (T) and parity (P) violating effects (such as T,P-violating electric dipole moments) appears in the ground 1Sigma state and in the metastable 3Delta_1 state of diatomic molecule 229ThO. Similar enhancements exist in molecular ions 229ThOH+, 229ThF+ and 225,223RaOH+. Corresponding experiments may be used to test CP-violation theories predicting T,P-violating nuclear forces and to search for axions.

# Enhanced nuclear Schiff moment and time reversal violation in 229Th-containing molecules

## Key claim
Octupole deformation in the 229Th nucleus produces a collective Schiff moment approximately twice that of 225Ra, and when combined with molecular enhancement in 229ThO, ThOH+, or ThF+, the resulting T,P-violating effects are 2-3 orders of magnitude larger than in TlF molecules, provided the molecule can be fully polarized and the theoretical uncertainties from cancellation effects in spherical nuclei are avoided.

## Method
The author calculates the collective Schiff moment for 229Th by scaling from numerical calculations for 225Ra using an analytical formula that depends on the octupole deformation parameter β3, quadrupole deformation parameter β2, nuclear spin I, and the energy interval between opposite-parity rotational states. The molecular enhancement is estimated by comparing the interaction constant WS for ThO, ThOH+, and ThF+ with previously calculated values for RaO, using the Z-dependence of atomic EDM enhancement and the known electronic structure similarities between Th and Ra.

## Key numbers
- Schiff moment of 229Th: S = 600 × 10⁻⁸ e η fm³ (scaled from S(225Ra) = 300 × 10⁻⁸ e η fm³)
- Energy interval between opposite parity levels in 229Th: E(5/2⁻) - E(5/2⁺) = 133.3 KeV
- Octupole deformation parameters for 229Th: β₃ = 0.115, β₂ = 0.240, I = 5/2
- Schiff moment expressed via QCD θ-term: S(229Th) = 2.0 θ̄ e fm³
- Schiff moment expressed via quark chromo-EDMs: S(229Th) = 10⁴ (1.0 d̃u - 1.1 d̃d) e fm²
- Atomic EDM of Th from Schiff moment: dₐ(Th) ≈ -9 × 10⁻¹⁷ (S/|e| fm³) |e| cm = -2 × 10⁻¹⁶ θ̄ |e| cm
- Energy shift for fully polarized 229ThO: 2 WS S = 1 × 10⁷ θ̄ Hz
- WS estimates: WS(ThO) ≈ 50000 a.u., WS(ThOH⁺) ≈ 30000 a.u., WS(RaOH⁺) ≈ 30000 a.u.
- 229Th half-life: 7917 years

## Context
This work extends the study of collective Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei, originally developed by Auerbach, Spevak, and collaborators for nuclei such as 225Ra, 223Rn, and 221Fr. The Schiff moment of 225Ra exceeds that of 199Hg (where the most accurate measurements have been performed by the Hg EDM collaboration) by a factor of 200. The key advance is proposing 229Th, which is far more stable (7917 year half-life versus 15 days for 225Ra) and can be produced in macroscopic quantities from 233U decay for medical isotope production. The molecular enhancement mechanism builds on the Sandars proposal for polar diatomic molecules and the Sushkov-Flambaum interpretation of T,P-violating EDM generation, and the ³Δ₁ state of ThO has already been used in the ACME electron EDM experiment. The proposed experiments could improve limits on the QCD θ-term (currently |θ̄| < 10⁻¹⁰) and other CP-violation parameters, and may also be used to search for axion dark matter through oscillating Schiff moments.
