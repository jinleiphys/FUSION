# Particle Ratios, Equilibration, and the QCD Phase Boundary

> We discuss the status of thermal model descriptions of particle ratios in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra-relativistic energy. An alternative to the ``Cleymans-Redlich'' interpretation of the freeze-out trajectory is given in terms of the total baryon density. Emphasis is placed on the relation between the chemical equilibration parameters and the QCD phase boundary. Furthermore, we trace the essential difference between thermal model analyses of data from collisions between elementary particles and from heavy ion collisions as due to a transition from local strangeness conservation to percolation of strangeness over large volumes, as occurs naturally in a deconfined medium. We also discuss predictions of the thermal model for composite particle production.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out trajectory in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is better interpreted as occurring at constant total baryon density (n_b = 0.12/fm^3) rather than at constant energy per particle as in the Cleymans-Redlich interpretation, and the close approach of freeze-out points to the QCD phase boundary supports the interpretation that matter at SPS and RHIC energies was first thermalized in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Method

The authors performed thermal model fits to hadron multiplicity ratios measured in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions across SIS, AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, extracting freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_b for each beam energy. They compared the resulting freeze-out trajectory to lattice QCD and bag model calculations of the QCD phase boundary, and analyzed the transition from canonical to grand-canonical strangeness conservation by examining the volume dependence of the canonical suppression factor F_c = I_n(x_1)/I_0(x_1). They also computed thermal model predictions for composite particle yields including light nuclei and strange clusters.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC data at T=174 MeV and μ_b=46 MeV, all available particle ratios near central rapidity are reproduced with χ²=5.7 for 7 effective degrees of freedom.
- The freeze-out line corresponds to constant total baryon plus anti-baryon density n_b = 0.12/fm³.
- At 40 GeV/nucleon beam energy, freeze-out parameters are T≈140 MeV and μ_b≈400 MeV, close to the value of about 420 MeV from a phenomenological parameterization.
- The low-mass di-electron enhancement observed by CERES increases from 2.7 ± 0.65 at 160 GeV/nucleon to 4.5 ± 1.2 at 40 GeV/nucleon.
- For elementary particle collisions, the canonical strangeness conservation volume is V₀ ≈ 7 fm³.
- The penalty factor for each additional nucleon in composite particle production at AGS energy is about 48 from data, reducing to about 26 after extrapolation to large transverse momentum; the thermal model predicts R_p ≈ 23 using T=125 MeV and μ_b=540 MeV.
- Anti-matter penalty factors from E864 data are about 2×10⁵, compared to a predicted value of 1.3×10⁵.
- Thermal model predictions for exotic objects include: d yield 15-19 per central Au+Au collision at AGS energy; _ΛΛ⁵H yield 3.5×10⁻⁵ to 2.3×10⁻⁴; _Ξ⁰ΛΛ⁷He yield 4.0×10⁻¹⁰ to 9.6×10⁻⁹; ¹⁰₁St⁻⁸ yield 1.6×10⁻¹⁴ to 7.3×10⁻¹³.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses of Cleymans and Redlich (2000) and Becattini (2000) by proposing an alternative freeze-out criterion based on constant baryon density rather than constant energy per particle. It connects the chemical freeze-out parameters to lattice QCD calculations of the phase boundary by Fodor (2001) and bag model calculations by Gerry (1970). The paper identifies a region of dense hadronic matter between the phase boundary and chemical freeze-out accessible at beam energies between 10 and 40 GeV/nucleon, which the planned GSI facility is designed to explore, and it enables predictions for exotic multi-strange hadron production in future high-statistics experiments.
