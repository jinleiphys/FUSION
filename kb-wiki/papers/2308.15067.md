# Probing Quarkyonic Matter in Neutron Stars with the Bayesian Nuclear-Physics Multi-Messenger Astrophysics Framework

> The interior of neutron stars contains matter at the highest densities realized in our Universe. Interestingly, theoretical studies of dense matter, in combination with the existence of two solar mass neutron stars, indicate that the speed of sound $c_s$ has to increase to values well above the conformal limit ($c_s^2\sim 1/3$) before decreasing again at higher densities. The decrease could be explained by either a strong first-order phase transition or a cross-over transition from hadronic to quark matter. The latter scenario leads to a pronounced peak in the speed of sound reaching values above the conformal limit, naturally explaining the inferred behavior. In this work, we use the Nuclear-Physics Multi-Messenger Astrophysics framework \textsc{NMMA} to compare predictions of the quarkyonic matter model with astrophysical observations of neutron stars, with the goal of constraining model parameters. Assuming quarkyonic matter to be realized within neutron stars, we find that there can be a significant amount of quarks inside the core of neutron stars with masses in the two solar mass range, amounting to up to $\sim 0.13M_\odot$, contributing $\sim 5.9\%$ of the total mass. Furthermore, for the quarkyonic matter model investigated here, the radius of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star would be $13.44^{+1.69}_{-1.54} (13.54^{+1.02}_{-1.04})$ km, at $95\%$ credibility, without (with) the inclusion of AT2017gfo.

## Key claim

Assuming quarkyonic matter is realized within neutron stars, the quarkyonic model can produce a significant quark core in stars near two solar masses, but the inferred radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star depends on whether the kilonova AT2017gfo is included in the analysis, with the central value shifting from 13.44 km to 13.54 km at 95% credibility.

## Method

The authors used the Nuclear-Physics Multi-Messenger Astrophysics framework (NMMA) to perform Bayesian inference on the parameters of a dynamic quarkyonic excluded-volume model, which treats neutrons as hard spheres and quarks as nearly free point particles with a density-dependent regulator. They compared model predictions against gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and GW190425, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, radio pulsar mass lower bounds from PSR J0348+4032 and PSR J1614-2230, and electromagnetic observations of the kilonova AT2017gfo. The equilibrium configuration of the quarkyonic Fermi distribution was found by minimizing the total energy density functional at each baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Quark content in neutron stars with masses in the two solar mass range: up to approximately 0.13 solar masses, contributing about 5.9% of the total mass.
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star under the quarkyonic matter model: 13.44 (+1.69, -1.54) km at 95% credibility without including AT2017gfo.
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star under the quarkyonic matter model: 13.54 (+1.02, -1.04) km at 95% credibility when including AT2017gfo.

## Context

This work extends the dynamic quarkyonic matter model of Jeong (2019) and Sen (2020) by performing the first Bayesian parameter inference against a comprehensive set of astrophysical observations, rather than only comparing to mass-radius data qualitatively. It contrasts with models featuring a strong first-order phase transition to quark matter, which produce a different speed-of-sound behavior and lower pressures at high density. The results enable future studies to distinguish between crossover and first-order transitions in neutron star interiors and to quantify the quark fraction in massive neutron stars.
