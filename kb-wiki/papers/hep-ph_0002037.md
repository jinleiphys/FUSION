# Hadronization in the Chromodielectric Model

> We study the dynamics of quarks and gluons in the Chromodielectric Model (CDM), which contains a dynamical description of confinement. Results are presented for hadronization out of an equilibrated quark--gluon plasma. It is shown that hadronization is complete and hadrons are classified within the scheme of CDM.

## Key claim

The Chromodielectric Model (CDM) provides a dynamical, classical molecular-dynamical description of hadronization from an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma, but only within an Abelian approximation to SU(3) color that neglects magnetic fields and non-Abelian gluon contributions.

## Method

The authors simulate a classical system of spinless, extended quarks and massive gluons coupled to a color-electric field and a scalar sigma field, using the Gauss law in an Abelian subgroup of SU(3) with only the 3 and 8 color directions. The system is initialized as a homogeneous sphere of radius 4 fm containing quarks and gluons in a Boltzmann distribution at temperature 160 MeV, then evolved via classical equations of motion until only irreducible white clusters (IWCs) remain, which are identified as hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of the quark-gluon plasma: \( T_0 = 160 \) MeV
- Radius of the initial spherical droplet: \( R = 4 \) fm
- Constituent quark masses: \( m_{u,d} = 400 \) MeV, \( m_s = 550 \) MeV, \( m_c = 1500 \) MeV
- Gluon mass: \( m_g = 700 \) MeV (to reproduce a 1400 MeV glueball)
- Scalar potential parameters: \( B = (150 \text{ MeV})^4 \), \( a = (489.9 \text{ MeV})^2 \), \( b = -15901 \) MeV, \( c = 163.1 \), vacuum expectation value \( \sigma_v = 61.1 \) MeV
- Strong coupling constant: \( \alpha_S = g/(4\pi) = 2 \)
- Hadronic temperature from Hagedorn fit with \( a = -3 \): \( T = 146 \) MeV; with \( a = -3/2 \): \( T = 125 \) MeV (assuming \( T_h = 160 \) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Friedberg-Lee model (1977) and earlier CDM studies by Traxler (1998), Loh (1996), Kalmbach (1993), and Vetter (1995) by demonstrating complete hadronization from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma into irreducible white clusters. The approach enables dynamical studies of the hadronization transition that complement lattice QCD results, though it relies on an Abelian approximation and neglects pions as Goldstone bosons of chiral symmetry breaking.
