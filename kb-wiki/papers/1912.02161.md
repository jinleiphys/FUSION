# Multiple charm and hidden charm mesons with strangeness

> In this talk we summarize our latest results on the study of three-body systems with explicit/hidden charm and with strangeness. In particular, we focus on the $K D D $ and $K D\bar D^*$ systems, where a charm $+2$, isospin $1/2$ and strangeness $+1$ state is found with a mass around 4140 MeV in the former and a $K^*$ state, with hidden charm, at a mass around 4307 MeV is obtained in the latter. Both states are predictions of our model and the experimental confirmation of them would be important to understand the properties of the strong interaction in the presence of heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The authors predict two three-body bound states: a charm +2, isospin 1/2, strangeness +1 state near 4140 MeV in the KDD system, and a hidden-charm K* vector meson near 4307 MeV in the KD\bar D* system. Both states are predictions of their Faddeev-equation model and require experimental confirmation; the 4140 MeV state has a calculated width of only 2-3 MeV, while the 4307 MeV state acquires a width of about 18 MeV when the finite width of the Z_c(3900) component is included.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the three-body KDD and KD\bar D* systems, using as input two-body t-matrices obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in its on-shell factorization form. The kernel v was constructed from effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy quark symmetries, and the coupled-channel dynamics included the D*_{s0}(2317) from the KD system and the X(3872) and Z_c(3900) from the D\bar D* system. For the KDD system, the coupled channels considered were DDK, DD\pi, and D_s\eta; for the KD\bar D* system, the calculation incorporated the finite width of the Z_c(3900) via its decay to J/\psi\pi.

## Key numbers
- A bound state in the KDD system with mass around 4140 MeV, charm +2, isospin 1/2, strangeness +1, and a total width of 2-3 MeV from triangular-loop decays to D_s D, D_s D*, and D D*_s.
- Root mean square (RMS) distance of the 4140 MeV state estimated as 1.0-1.4 fm, larger than that of the D*_{s0}(2317).
- A K* vector meson in the KD\bar D* system with mass around 4307 MeV, which acquires a width of about 18 MeV when the Z_c(3900) width of approximately 30 MeV is included.
- Partial decay widths of the 4307 MeV state: approximately 7 MeV to J/\psi K*(892), approximately 0.5 MeV to \bar D D*_s and \bar D* D*_s, and approximately 1 MeV to \bar D D_s.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of three-body systems with charm, such as the DDK system studied by Sanchez Sanchez et al. (2017) using an effective two-body potential, and the KD\bar D* system studied by Ma et al. (2017). The results agree with the findings of Sanchez Sanchez et al. (2017) for the 4140 MeV state, and the RMS distance estimate was confirmed by Wu et al. (2019). The predicted states are molecular in nature, arising from the same coupled-channel dynamics that generate the D*_{s0}(2317), X(3872), and Z_c(3900). Experimental confirmation of these states would open new research lines, such as searching for excited kaons and K* mesons at energies around 4-5 GeV with hidden charm or bottom quantum numbers, and could be achieved by reconstructing the J/\psi\pi K invariant mass in final-state reactions.
