# Impact of collective vibrations on quasiparticle states of open-shell odd-mass nuclei and possible interference with the tensor force

> The energy splittings between the proton $1h_{11/2}$ and $1g_{7/2}$ states in the Sb (Z=51) isotopes and the neutron $1i_{13/2}$ and $1h_{9/2}$ states in the ${N=83}$ isotones are analyzed within relativistic quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) model. Although a common viewpoint is that an effective tensor interaction is needed to reproduce these splittings, we show, for the first time, that quasiparticle-vibration coupling also improves their description considerably. The remaining differences between the experiment and the QVC calculations point to a complex scenario in which both tensor interaction and quasiparticle-vibration coupling are needed to reproduce accurately the observed energy splittings. This scenario, however, requires a considerably weaker tensor interaction as compared with the previous estimates.

## Key claim

The paper shows for the first time that quasiparticle-vibration coupling (QVC) considerably improves the description of energy splittings between specific single-quasiparticle states in odd-mass nuclei, which were previously thought to require an effective tensor interaction. The authors argue that both QVC and tensor interaction act in the same direction, meaning the effective tensor force must be weaker than earlier estimates, and that fitting tensor strength without including QVC is flawed.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic quasiparticle-vibration coupling (RQVC) model, which solves the Dyson equation for the single-quasiparticle propagator with a frequency-dependent self-energy that includes both pairing correlations and quasiparticle-phonon coupling on equal footing. Calculations were performed with the NL3* covariant energy density functional (which contains no tensor interaction) and two different pairing schemes (empirical gaps \(\Delta_{emp}=12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV and five-point indicators \(\Delta^{(5)}\)). The phonon space was truncated at angular momenta \(J^\pi=6^+\) and frequencies up to 15 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The energy splitting \(\Delta \epsilon_{\pi} = \epsilon(\pi h_{11/2}) - \epsilon(\pi g_{7/2})\) in Sb isotopes changes by approximately 2.5 MeV between \(N=62\) and \(N=82\) in experiment; mean-field CDFT gives \(\Delta \epsilon_{\pi}\sim 4\) MeV, smoothly increasing with neutron number.
- The QVC correction to some states (e.g., neutron \(1i_{13/2}\), \(3p_{1/2}\), \(3p_{3/2}\) in \(^{148}\)Dy and proton \(2d_{5/2}\), \(2d_{3/2}\), \(1h_{11/2}\), \(3s_{1/2}\) in \(^{116}\)Sn) is substantial, reaching 2–3 MeV.
- Spectroscopic factors \(S\) of the dominant states near magic shell gaps are \(\sim 0.8\), decreasing away from magic numbers; for example, the \(\pi h_{11/2}\) state in mid-shell Sb isotopes has \(S\sim 0.5\) in both older experimental data and RQVC calculations.
- Pairing gaps used: \(\Delta_{emp}\) close to 1.1 MeV for Sn isotopes and \(N=82\) isotones; \(\Delta^{(5)}\) gaps are larger by roughly 0.2 MeV in Sn isotopes and by up to \(\sim 0.5\) MeV at \(Z=70\) in \(N=82\) isotones.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic PVC studies of Litvinova and Afanasjev (2011) on single-particle spectra near doubly magic nuclei to open-shell odd-mass nuclei with pairing correlations, specifically targeting the Sb isotopes and \(N=83\) isotones. It directly contradicts the conclusions of Skyrme DFT studies (e.g., Colò et al. 2007) and Gogny DFT studies (Otsuka et al. 2006) which claimed that tensor interaction alone is needed to reproduce the observed energy splittings. The results enable future calculations that incorporate both QVC and tensor interaction simultaneously to quantitatively disentangle their contributions, and they caution against using single-quasiparticle energies to fit tensor strength without beyond-mean-field effects.
