# Initial- and final-state temperatures of emission source from differential cross-section in squared momentum transfer in high energy collisions

> The differential cross-section in squared momentum transfer of $ρ$, $ρ^0$, $ω$, $ϕ$, $f_{0}(980)$, $f_{1}(1285)$, $f_{0}(1370)$, $f_{1}(1420)$, $f_{0}(1500)$, and $J/ψ$ produced in high energy virtual photon-proton ($γ$$^{*} p$), photon-proton ($γp$), and proton-proton ($pp$) collisions measured by the H1, ZEUS, and WA102 Collaborations are analyzed by the Monte Carlo calculations. In the calculations, the Erlang distribution, Tsallis distribution, and Hagedorn function are separately used to describe the transverse momentum spectra of the emitted particles. Our results show that the initial- and final-state temperatures increase from lower squared photon virtuality to higher one, and decrease with increasing of center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim

The initial- and final-state temperatures of the emission source in high energy virtual photon-proton, photon-proton, and proton-proton collisions increase with higher squared photon virtuality and decrease with increasing center-of-mass energy, though the authors note that for the small systems studied the final-state temperature is not a freeze-out temperature in the sense used for heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo calculations to fit the differential cross-section in squared momentum transfer for ten meson species produced in gamma* p, gamma p, and pp collisions measured by the H1, ZEUS, and WA102 Collaborations. Transverse momentum spectra were generated using the Erlang distribution, Tsallis distribution, and Hagedorn function, then transformed into squared momentum transfer via the Mandelstam variable |t| under an isotropic emission assumption in the center-of-mass frame. The initial-state temperature was extracted from the root-mean-square of transverse momentum, while the final-state temperature was approximated by the effective temperature parameter from the Tsallis distribution, neglecting flow effects.

## Key numbers

- For gamma* p -> rho p at W=75 GeV, initial temperature T_i increases from 0.216 +/- 0.008 GeV at Q^2=3.3 GeV^2 to 0.291 +/- 0.012 GeV at Q^2=33.0 GeV^2 (Erlang distribution)
- For gamma* p -> rho^0 p at W=90 GeV, T_i increases from 0.206 +/- 0.003 GeV at Q^2=2.7 GeV^2 to 0.243 +/- 0.009 GeV at Q^2=41.0 GeV^2 (Erlang distribution)
- For gamma p -> J/psi p at Q^2=0.05 GeV^2, T_i decreases from 0.288 +/- 0.006 GeV at W=45 GeV to 0.240 +/- 0.003 GeV at W=251 GeV (Erlang distribution)
- For gamma* p -> rho p at W=75 GeV, final-state temperature T (from Tsallis distribution) increases from 0.036 +/- 0.001 GeV at Q^2=3.3 GeV^2 to 0.060 +/- 0.004 GeV at Q^2=33.0 GeV^2
- For gamma p -> omega p at 70 GeV < W < 90 GeV, T_i = 0.145 +/- 0.006 GeV and T = 0.021 +/- 0.001 GeV (Erlang and Tsallis distributions respectively)
- For pp -> pp f_0(980) at sqrt(s_NN)=29.1 GeV, T_i = 0.250 +/- 0.003 GeV (Erlang distribution)

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model approach of Liu and collaborators (2008-2019) by applying the Erlang, Tsallis, and Hagedorn distributions to squared momentum transfer spectra rather than transverse momentum spectra alone. The results enable systematic comparison of initial- and final-state temperatures across different collision systems and meson species, and the method can be applied to other two-body and two-body-like reactions in future analyses of small collision systems where flow effects are negligible.
