# Study of charged particle production in $U$-$U$ collisions in a Wounded Quark Model

> Recently, there has been a growing interest in the study of deformed uranium-uranium ($U$-$U$) collisions in its various geometrical configurations due to their usefulness in understanding the different aspects of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In this paper we have studied the particle production in deformed $U$-$U$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = $193$ GeV using modified wounded quark model (WQM). At first, we have shown the variation of quark-nucleus inelastic scattering cross-section ($σ_{qA}^{in}$) with respect to centralities for various geometrical orientations of $U$-$U$ collisions in WQM. After that we have calculated the pseudorapidity density ($dn_{ch}/dη$) within WQM using two-component prescription. Further we have calculated the transverse energy density distribution ($dE_{T}/dη$) along with the ratio of transverse energy to charged hadron multiplicity ($E_{T}/N_{ch}$) for $U$-$U$ collisions and compared them with the corresponding experimental data. We have shown the scaling behavior of $dn_{ch}/dη$ and $dE_{T}/dη$ for different initial geometry of $U$-$U$ collision with respect to $p$-$p$ data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. Furthermore we have shown the Bjorken energy density achieved in $U$-$U$ collisions for various configurations and compared them with experimental data of $Au$-$Au$ at 200 GeV. We observe that the present model suitably describes the experimental data for minimum bias geometrical configuration of $U$-$U$ collisions. An estimate for various observables in different initial geometries of $U$-$U$ collisions is also presented which will be tested in future by experimental data.

# Study of charged particle production in U-U collisions in a Wounded Quark Model

## Key claim
The modified wounded quark model (WQM) with a two-component prescription using a very small hard-process fraction (x = 0.005) suitably describes the experimental pseudorapidity density data for minimum bias U-U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV, but the model shows a different behavior than the small correlation between multiplicity and initial orientation observed in recent experimental data, suggesting the need for additional observables to disentangle geometrical configurations.

## Method
The authors extended a modified wounded quark model (WQM) based on the additive quark model (AQM) to deformed U-U collisions, using a deformed Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution with deformation parameters β2 and β4 for uranium nuclei. They calculated the quark-nucleus inelastic scattering cross-section using Glauber's approximation, then computed the pseudorapidity density at mid-rapidity using a two-component formula involving the number of participating quarks (Nq) and the mean number of quark collisions (νq), with a small hard-process fraction x = 0.005. The transverse energy density was obtained from the pseudorapidity density using average transverse momentum from the AMPT model, and the Bjorken energy density was calculated for different geometrical configurations (tip-tip, body-body, body-tip, and minimum bias).

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √sNN = 193 GeV for U-U collisions
- Pseudorapidity density for tip-tip configuration in most central collisions is about 36% higher than body-body and 27% higher than minimum bias configuration
- Number of participating quarks (Nq) for 0-5% centrality: tip-tip = 444.28, body-tip = 364.18, min-bias = 331.12, body-body = 284.09
- Number of participating quarks (Nq) for 45-50% centrality: all configurations give Nq = 32.66
- Ratio (dET/dη)/(dnch/dη) is approximately 0.9 in experimental data for minimum bias, varying between 0.8 to 0.85 in HYDJET++ model and 0.8 to 0.7 in AMPT model
- Charged hadron production in tip-tip central U-U collisions is about 1.5 times larger than in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV
- Bjorken energy density in tip-tip configuration is about 30% higher than the energy density of the fireball created in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV
- Variation of σqA with polar angle is about 40% depending on the polar angle
- Parameters for the p-p parametrization: a1' = 1.15, b1' = 0.16, c1' = 0.05, leading particle effect α = 0.85

## Context
This work extends the wounded quark model previously applied by the same authors to symmetric collisions (Au-Au, Pb-Pb, Cu-Cu) and asymmetric collisions (Cu-Au, d-Au) as reported in Ashwini (2013) and Chaturvedi (2016). It builds on the additive quark model framework of Bialas (1982) and Anisovich (1984), and uses the deformed Woods-Saxon parameterization from Loizides (2014) and Shou (2015). The study enables predictions for various observables in different initial geometries of U-U collisions that can be tested by future experimental data, and provides a comparison with the Monte Carlo Glauber model results of Sandeep (2016) and Moreland (2015), which showed contradictory behavior regarding multiplicity-orientation correlations.
