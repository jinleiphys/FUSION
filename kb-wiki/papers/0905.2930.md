# Memory effects in radiative jet energy loss

> In heavy-ion collisions the created quark-gluon plasma forms a quickly evolving background, leading to a time dependent radiative behavior of high momentum partons traversing the medium. We use the Schwinger Keldysh formalism to describe the jet evolution as a non-equilibrium process including the Landau-Pomeranschuk-Migdal effect. Concentrating on photon emission, a comparison of our results to a quasistatic calculation shows good agreement, leading to the conclusion that the radiative behavior follows the changes in the medium almost instantaneously.

## Key claim

The radiative behavior of a high-momentum parton traversing a dynamically evolving quark-gluon plasma adjusts almost instantaneously to changes in the medium, with memory times for photon emission being significantly shorter than the formation time of the photon, especially in the Landau-Pomeranschuk-Migdal (LPM) regime.

## Method

The authors used the Schwinger-Keldysh non-equilibrium Green's function formalism to derive a photon production rate expression that is non-local in time, accounting for memory effects. They modeled the fermion jet as a single high-energy particle with momentum p = 20 GeV, described by a Breit-Wigner spectral function with a time-dependent scattering width Γ(T), and computed the photon emission rate by evaluating the current-current correlator at one-loop order. The time evolution of the medium was simulated by linearly varying Γ from 1.0 GeV to 0.3 GeV over different time intervals Δt, and memory times were extracted by fitting the relative difference between final and intermediate photon rates.

## Key numbers

- Memory times for photon emission are below 1 fm/c when Γ depends on the center-of-mass time (t+u)/2, indicating near-instantaneous adjustment.
- For k⊥ ≪ k∥ (LPM region, e.g., k∥ = 5.0 GeV, k⊥ = 0.5 GeV), the relative difference r(Δt) decays exponentially as r(Δt) ∼ e^{-Δt/τ}, with τ ∼ 1/Γ₂ for k⊥ → 0.
- For larger k⊥ (Bethe-Heitler region, e.g., k∥ = 5.0 GeV, k⊥ = 4.0 GeV), r(Δt) follows r(Δt) ∼ (Δt/τ) e^{-Δt/τ}.
- The characteristic timescale contributing to the memory integral is ⟨τ⟩ = 2Γ/(ω_k² + 4Γ²), where ω_k = p - √(p² + k∥² + k⊥² - 2pk∥) - √(k∥² + k⊥²).
- The initial and final scattering rates used were Γ₁ = 1.0 GeV and Γ₂ = 0.3 GeV, with a fermion jet momentum p = 20 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of radiative energy loss in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating non-equilibrium dynamics, building on earlier studies of memory effects in quantum transport equations by Greiner (1994) and Kohler (1995), and in dilepton production from non-equilibrated hadronic matter by Schenke (2006). The results contradict the expectation that memory times would be comparable to the photon formation time τ_F (which diverges as k⊥ → 0 in Dokshitzer's approximation), showing instead that the decorrelation time τ_S = 1/(2Γ) sets an upper bound. This enables future studies of jet energy loss in expanding QGP using time-dependent scattering rates without needing to account for significant memory effects, and suggests that quasistatic calculations are adequate for describing radiative energy loss in dynamic media.
