# Probing configuration of $α$ clusters with spectator particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We propose to use spectator particle yield ratios to probe the configuration of $α$ clusters in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O by their collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The idea is illustrated based on initial density distributions with various $α$-cluster configurations generated by a microscopic cluster model, and without $α$ clusters from mean-field calculations. The multifragmentation of the spectator matter produces more spectator light nuclei including $α$ clusters in collisions of nuclei with chain structure of $α$ clusters, compared to those of nuclei with a more compact structure. The yield ratio of free spectator neutrons to spectator particles with mass-to-charge ratio $A/Z=2$ scaled by their masses can be practically measured by the zero-degree calorimeter (ZDC) at RHIC and LHC, serving as a clean probe free from modeling the complicated dynamics at midrapidities.

## Key claim

The yield ratio of free spectator neutrons to spectator particles with mass-to-charge ratio A/Z=2, scaled by their constituent nucleon numbers, can serve as a clean probe of alpha-cluster configurations in 12C and 16O nuclei in ultracentral relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided that the ratio is measured by zero-degree calorimeters at RHIC and LHC, thus avoiding uncertainties from modeling midrapidity dynamics.

## Method

The authors generated initial density distributions for various alpha-cluster configurations (triangle, chain, square, Y-shape, tetrahedron) using a microscopic cluster model with Brink wave functions, and a spherical distribution without alpha clusters from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations. They simulated 12C+12C and 16O+16O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and 7 TeV using a Monte-Carlo Glauber model to determine spectator nucleons, then applied a multifragmentation framework including a minimum spanning tree algorithm for heavy cluster formation, a Wigner function approach for light cluster coalescence (A <= 3), and the GEMINI model for deexcitation of heavy clusters (A >= 4).

## Key numbers

- RMS radii: 12C sphere 2.49 fm, triangle 2.45 fm, chain 3.29 fm; 16O sphere 2.69 fm, square 2.77 fm, chain 4.06 fm, Y-shape 3.09 fm, tetrahedron 2.45 fm (experimental charge radii: 2.47 fm for 12C, 2.70 fm for 16O)
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross sections used: 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, 71 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV
- Coalescence parameters: Delta_r_max=3 fm, Delta_p_max=300 MeV/c
- Size parameter b=1.46 fm for each nucleon in the Brink wave function
- The N_n/N_{A/Z=2} ratio is smallest for chain configurations and largest for sphere configurations (no alpha clusters) at both energies, with the separation being clearer at 7 TeV than at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal to use free spectator nucleons as a probe of neutron-skin thickness in ultracentral collisions (Liu 2022, Liu 2023) to the domain of alpha-cluster structure. It contrasts with prior probes of alpha clustering such as deexcitation spectra of isovector giant dipole resonances (He 2014), nucleon emissions in photonuclear reactions (Huang 2017), direct photon production (Shi 2021), and anisotropic flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (PhysRevC.95.064904, PhysRevC.97.034912, Behera 2023, PhysRevC.104.L041901), which all suffer from uncertainties in modeling midrapidity dynamics. The proposed spectator ratio enables future experimental tests at RHIC and LHC to distinguish between chain, triangle, square, Y-shape, tetrahedron, and spherical configurations in 12C and 16O.
