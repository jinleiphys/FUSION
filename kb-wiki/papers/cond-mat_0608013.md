# Reduction of surface coverage of finite systems due to geometrical steps

> The coverage of vicinal, stepped surfaces with molecules is simulated with the help of a two-dimensional Ising model including local distortions and an Ehrlich-Schwoebel barrier term at the steps. An effective two-spin model is capable to describe the main properties of this distorted Ising model. It is employed to analyze the behavior of the system close to the critical points. Within a well-defined regime of bonding strengths and Ehrlich-Schwoebel barriers we find a reduction of coverage (magnetization) at low temperatures due to the presence of the surface step. This results in a second, low-temperature transition besides the standard Ising order-disorder transition. The additional transition is characterized by a divergence of the susceptibility as a finite-size effect. Due to the surface step the mean-field specific heat diverges with a power law.

## Key claim

Within a well-defined regime of bonding strengths and Ehrlich-Schwoebel barriers, the presence of a surface step causes a reduction of coverage (magnetization) at low temperatures, resulting in a second, low-temperature transition besides the standard Ising order-disorder transition; this additional transition is characterized by a divergence of the susceptibility as a finite-size effect.

## Method

The authors simulated coverage of vicinal, stepped surfaces with molecules using a two-dimensional Ising model on an N×N square lattice with a straight step across the middle, where the coupling constant across the step is set to J' = J/√2 and an Ehrlich-Schwoebel barrier term H_s is added to the magnetic field at step sites. They solved the model numerically with the standard Metropolis Monte Carlo scheme and constructed an effective two-spin mean-field model that distinguishes normal spins on terrace sites from fictitious spins at repulsive step sites, deriving self-consistent mean-field equations for the magnetization. The critical exponents of the new transition were then analyzed analytically from the mean-field equations.

## Key numbers

- The coupling across the step is set to J' = J/√2, where J is the normal coupling constant.
- For a 50×50 lattice with J=1, J'=1/√2, and μ₀H_s=5, the reduction of magnetization at low temperatures is 1-2/50 = 0.96.
- The condition for the reduction of magnetization is μ₀H + J + J' < μ₀H_s (effective two-spin model) or μ₀H + 3J + J' < μ₀H_s (three-spin model).
- The second transition occurs at a critical temperature T_c' ≈ 0.35 T_c for the parameters shown, where T_c = jJ/k_B.
- The susceptibility at the second transition diverges as χ ∼ 1/(T - T_c') for finite lattices, giving critical exponent γ' = 1.
- The specific heat at the second transition diverges with exponent α' = 2 for finite lattices (α' = 0 for infinite lattices).
- For the standard Ising transition at T_c, the mean-field exponents are α=3/2, β=0, γ=1, δ=0 for finite lattices, and α=0, β=1/2, γ=1, δ=3 for infinite lattices.

## Context

This work extends studies of surface defects in Ising models, such as those by Barber (1979, 1982, 2000) who derived exact critical exponents for line defects, and by Fisher and Ferdinand (1967) who studied specific heat divergences for boundaries. The paper shows that divergent heat capacities can arise from geometrical distortion rather than from a genuine phase transition, which may inform interpretation of signals in nuclear liquid-gas phase transition searches (Chomaz et al., 2004). The model enables application to fabrication of nanowires created near surface steps, as described in the companion paper by Lozovoi et al. (2006).
