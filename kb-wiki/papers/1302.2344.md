# Quark Transverse Momentum Distributions inside a nucleon : a Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics study

> Through an impulse approximation analysis of single spin Sivers and Collins asymmetries in the Bjorken limit, the possibility to extract the quark transverse-momentum distributions in the neutron from semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off polarized 3He is illustrated. The analysis is generalized to finite momentum transfers in a light-front Poincare' covariant framework, defining the light-front spin-dependent spectral function of a J=1/2 system. The definition of the light-front spin-dependent spectral function for constituent quarks in the nucleon allows us to show that, within the light-front dynamics, only three of the six leading twist T-even transverse-momentum distributions are independent.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within a light-front Hamiltonian dynamics framework with a fixed number of on-mass-shell constituents, only three of the six leading-twist T-even transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs) are independent, and that the extraction of neutron Sivers and Collins asymmetries from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off polarized 3He remains reliable at finite momentum transfers, with only small relativistic corrections compared to the non-relativistic impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors develop a light-front Poincaré covariant framework for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off polarized 3He, defining a light-front spin-dependent spectral function for a J=1/2 system. They compute single-spin asymmetries (SSAs) in impulse approximation using a realistic spin-dependent spectral function for 3He obtained with the AV18 interaction and wave functions from the Pisa group, then generalize the analysis to finite momentum transfers using light-front dynamics. For the nucleon case, they express the quark correlation function in terms of the light-front nucleon spectral function, which depends on three independent scalar functions, and derive relations among the six T-even TMDs.

## Key numbers

- Effective polarizations for 3He using AV18 interaction: proton effective polarization p_p = -0.023, neutron effective polarization p_n = 0.878
- Light-front longitudinal polarization for proton: -0.02231 (compared to non-relativistic -0.02263)
- Light-front longitudinal polarization for neutron: 0.87248 (compared to non-relativistic 0.87805)
- Light-front transverse polarization for proton: -0.02268 (compared to non-relativistic -0.02263)
- Light-front transverse polarization for neutron: 0.87494 (compared to non-relativistic 0.87805)

## Context

This work extends the impulse approximation analysis of Sivers and Collins asymmetries from the Bjorken limit (Scopetta, 2007) to finite momentum transfers using light-front dynamics, building on the proposal by Cates et al. to extract neutron TMDs from polarized 3He experiments. The light-front spectral function approach incorporates relativistic effects through Melosh rotations and provides a framework for testing the validity of the standard extraction formula used by experimental collaborations. The finding that only three of six T-even TMDs are independent within light-front dynamics offers a testable prediction for future experiments at Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV, and the authors plan to include final-state interactions through a Glauber approach (Kaptari).
