# Is there a Pronounced Giant Dipole Resonance in ^4He?

> A four-nucleon calculation of the total ^4He photodisintegration cross section is performed. The full final-state interaction is taken into account for the first time. This is achieved via the method of the Lorentz integral transform. Semi-realistic NN interactions are employed. Different from the known partial two-body ^4He(γ,n)^3He and ^4He(γ,p)^3H cross sections our total cross section exhibits a pronounced giant resonance. Thus, in contrast to older $(γ,np)$ data, we predict quite a strong contribution of the $(γ,np)$ channel at the giant resonance peak energy.

## Key claim

The total photodisintegration cross section of ^4He, calculated for the first time with full final-state interaction, exhibits a pronounced giant dipole resonance peak, in contrast to the suppressed peak seen in the two-body breakup channels alone; the authors predict a strong contribution from the (γ,np) channel at the giant resonance peak energy, which contradicts older (γ,np) data.

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross section for ^4He was calculated using the Lorentz integral transform method, which avoids direct continuum calculations by solving a Schrödinger-like equation with a localized source term. The calculation employed semi-realistic NN interactions (Trento and Malfliet-Tjon I+III potentials) and considered E1 transitions in the long-wavelength limit via the unretarded dipole operator, with full final-state interaction included for the first time across the entire energy range below the pion threshold.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ^4He: 30.5 MeV for the TN potential, 29.2 MeV for the MT potential (experimental value: 28.3 MeV)
- Matter r.m.s. radius: 1.41 fm for TN potential, 1.43 fm for MT potential (experimental value: 1.45 fm)
- ^1S_0 scattering length: -17.9 fm for TN potential, -23.3 fm for MT potential
- Bremsstrahlung weighted sum rule σ_b: 2.41 mb (TN) and 2.48 mb (MT) from sum rule calculation; 2.40 mb (TN) and 2.48 mb (MT) from explicit integration of cross sections
- TRK sum enhancement factor κ: 0.727 (TN) and 0.684 (MT) from sum rule; 0.754 (TN) and 0.712 (MT) from integration, with relative deviations of about 1.5% from the sum rule
- TRK sum σ_TRK = 59.74(1+κ) MeV mb; the κ values are lower than those from realistic NN interactions (1.0 to 1.3), leading to an underestimate of σ_TRK by 15-25%

## Context

This work extends previous four-nucleon calculations of the two-body breakup channels (Hofmann 1992, Sandhas 1996) which found a suppressed giant dipole resonance peak, by computing the total cross section including the (γ,np) channel. The results contradict the older experimental data of Gorbunov (1976) and Arkhangelsky (1978) which showed very small cross sections for channels beyond two-body breakup. The calculation enables future experimental work to measure the (γ,np) cross section in the energy range between the three-body breakup threshold (26.1 MeV) and 80 MeV, where high-statistics data are currently lacking.
