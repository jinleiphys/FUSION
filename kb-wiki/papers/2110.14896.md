# Large and massive neutron stars: Implications for the sound speed in dense QCD

> The NASA telescope NICER has recently measured x-ray emissions from the heaviest of the precisely known two-solar mass neutron stars, PSR J0740+6620. Analysis of the data [Miller et al., Astrophys. J. Lett. 918, L28 (2021); Riley et al., Astrophys. J. Lett. 918, L27 (2021)] suggests that PSR J0740+6620 has a radius in the range of $R_{2.0} \approx (11.4-16.1)$ km at the $68\%$ credibility level. In this article, we study the implications of this analysis for the sound speed in the high-density inner cores by using recent chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) calculations of the equation of state at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order to describe outer regions of the star at modest density. We find that the lower bound on the maximum speed of sound in the inner core, $\textbf{min}\{c^2_{s, {\rm max}}\}$, increases rapidly with the radius of massive neutron stars. If $χ$EFT remains an efficient expansion for nuclear interactions up to about twice the nuclear saturation density, $R_{2.0}\geqslant 13$ km requires $\textbf{min}\{c^2_{s, {\rm max}}\} \geqslant 0.562$ and $0.442$ at the $68\%$ and $95\%$ credibility level, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper finds that if chiral effective field theory (χEFT) calculations remain valid up to about twice nuclear saturation density, then a radius of 13 km or larger for a 2.08 solar mass neutron star (PSR J0740+6620) requires the minimum maximum sound speed squared in the inner core to be at least 0.562 at the 68% credibility level and 0.442 at the 95% credibility level, with the caveat that these bounds depend on the matching density between the outer and inner core and on whether phase transitions are present.

## Method

The authors construct neutron star equations of state by matching three density regions: a low-density crust EOS, an intermediate-density outer core described by χEFT calculations at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) with quantified truncation errors, and a high-density inner core parametrized by a constant sound speed squared (c²_s,mat ≤ 1). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating neutron stars to map the minimum core sound speed required to support a given radius for a 2.0 solar mass star, and incorporate constraints from the binary tidal deformability measured in GW170817.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0740+6620 has mass M = 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses and radius R₂.₀ ≈ (11.4–16.1) km at the 68% credibility level from NICER and XMM-Newton data
- If χEFT is valid up to n_c = 2.0 n_sat, achieving R₂.₀ ≥ 13 km requires min{c²_s,max} ≥ 0.562 at 68% credibility and ≥ 0.442 at 95% credibility
- For the central χEFT-N3LO EOS with n_c = 2.0 n_sat, increasing R₂.₀ from 12.6 km to 13.1 km requires min{c²_s,max} to increase from ≈ 0.5 to ≈ 1.0
- The maximum radius for a 2.0 solar mass neutron star compatible with both causality and GW170817 is R₂.₀^max ≈ 13.7 km
- For the stiffest χEFT-N3LO EOS at the 2σ level, the largest possible R₂.₀ limited by causality is ≈ 13.53 km for n_c = 2.0 n_sat and ≈ 13.87 km for n_c = 1.8 n_sat
- The binary tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 is Λ̃₁.₁₈₆ ≤ 720 at the 90% credibility level

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Drischler et al., 2020) that derived upper and lower bounds on neutron star radii from microscopic χEFT calculations, now applying the framework to the specific NICER measurement of PSR J0740+6620 by Miller et al. (2021) and Riley et al. (2021). The results show tension between the soft equation of state predicted by χEFT at densities up to twice nuclear saturation and the large radii suggested by NICER data, requiring sound speeds exceeding the conformal bound of c²_s = 1/3. The analysis enables future multi-messenger constraints by showing how combined radius and tidal deformability measurements can distinguish between different high-density phases of QCD matter.
