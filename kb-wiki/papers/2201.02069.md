# Analyzing transverse momentum spectra by a new method in high-energy collisions

> We analyzed the transverse momentum spectra of positively and negatively charged pions ($π^+$ and $π^-$), positively and negatively charged kaons ($K^+$ and $K^-$), protons and antiprotons ($p$ and $\bar p$), as well as $ϕ$ produced in mid-(pseudo)rapidity region in central nucleus--nucleus (AA) collisions over a center-of-mass energy range from 2.16 to 2760 GeV per nucleon pair. The transverse momentum of the considered particle is regarded as the joint contribution of two participant partons which obey the modified Tsallis-like transverse momentum distribution and have random azimuths in superposition. The calculation of transverse momentum distribution of particles is performed by the Monte Carlo method and compared with the experimental data measured by international collaborations. The excitation functions of effective temperature and other parameters are obtained in the considered energy range. With the increase of collision energy, the effective temperature parameter increases quickly and then slowly. The boundary appears at around 5 GeV, which means the change of reaction mechanism and/or generated matter.

## Key claim

The effective temperature parameter extracted from transverse momentum spectra in central nucleus-nucleus collisions increases quickly with collision energy up to around 5 GeV per nucleon pair, after which the increase slows, indicating a change in reaction mechanism or generated matter; this boundary is identified from fits using a modified Tsallis-like distribution combined with a two-parton superposition model.

## Method

The authors model each particle's transverse momentum as the vector sum of two participant partons (from projectile and target nuclei) whose transverse momenta individually follow a modified Tsallis-like distribution with an additional shape parameter a0. Monte Carlo sampling generates the resulting particle pT distribution, which is then fitted to experimental invariant yield data for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and phi mesons from central Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions across 2.16 to 2760 GeV center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair. The fit extracts the effective temperature T, entropy index q, and shape parameter a0 for each particle species at each energy.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature T for pi+ at 2.4 GeV (HADES, 0-10% central): 0.062 +/- 0.002 GeV; at 2760 GeV (ALICE, 0-5% central): 0.227 +/- 0.002 GeV
- Effective temperature T for K+ at 2.16 GeV (KaoS, 0-40% central): 0.020 +/- 0.002 GeV; at 2760 GeV (ALICE, 0-5% central): 0.242 +/- 0.002 GeV
- Effective temperature T for protons at 2.7 GeV (E895, 0-5% central): 0.180 +/- 0.003 GeV; at 2760 GeV (ALICE, 0-5% central): 0.263 +/- 0.001 GeV
- Effective temperature T for phi meson at 2.4 GeV (HADES, 0-40% central): 0.124 +/- 0.001 GeV; at 200 GeV (STAR, 0-5% central): 0.261 +/- 0.001 GeV
- Boundary in excitation function of effective temperature appears at around 5 GeV per nucleon pair
- Chemical potential for baryons given by empirical formula: mu_B = 1.3075 / (1 + 0.288 * sqrt(s_NN)) with units in GeV

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model approach of Liu and collaborators (recent work, 2021) by introducing a modified Tsallis-like distribution with an extra shape parameter a0 to better describe low-pT spectra. It provides systematic excitation functions of effective temperature across a wider energy range (2.16 to 2760 GeV) than previous studies, which had shown indefinite trends for the kinetic freeze-out temperature T0 due to different treatments of flow effects. The results enable future comparisons with chemical freeze-out temperatures from statistical models and may help constrain the energy dependence of the QCD phase transition boundary.
