# Role of $־›(1670)$ in the $־³p \to K^+ ־·־›$ reaction near threshold

> The role of the $־›(1670)$ resonance in the $־³p \to K^+ ־·־›$ reaction near threshold is studied within an effective Lagrangian approach. We perform a calculation for the total and differential cross section of the $־³p \to K^+ ־·־›$ reaction by including the contributions from the $־›(1670)$ intermediate state decaying into $־·־›$ dominated by $K^-$ and $K^{*-}$ mesons exchanges, the nucleon pole and $N^*(1535)$ resonance decaying into $K^+ ־›$ dominated by exchanges of $ֿ‰$ and $K^-$ mesons. Besides, the non-resonance process and contact terms to keep the total scattering amplitude gauge invariant are also considered. With our model parameters, the total cross section of this reaction is of the order of $1$ nanobarn at photon beam energy $E_־³ \sim 2.5$ GeV. It is expected that our model predictions could be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance, via \(K^-\) and \(K^{*-}\) meson exchanges, provides the dominant contribution to the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \eta \Lambda\) reaction near threshold, while contributions from the nucleon pole and \(N^*(1535)\) resonance are negligibly small; however, the predicted cross sections have a strong dependence on the form-factor cutoff parameter, with uncertainties of 100ג€“200% for a 20% variation in the cutoff.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model to compute the total and differential cross sections of the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \eta \Lambda\) reaction. They included Feynman diagrams for the \(\Lambda(1670)\) intermediate state decaying into \(\eta \Lambda\) via \(K^-\) and \(K^{*-}\) exchanges, the nucleon pole and \(N^*(1535)\) resonance decaying into \(K^+ \Lambda\) via \(\omega\) and \(K^-\) exchanges, plus non-resonant processes and contact terms to maintain gauge invariance. Coupling constants were taken from partial decay widths, \(SU(3)\) flavor symmetry relations, and previous literature, with a common cutoff parameter \(\Lambda_c = 1.5\) GeV for all form factors.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section of the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \eta \Lambda\) reaction is of the order of 1 nanobarn at photon beam energy \(E_\gamma \sim 2.5\) GeV.
* At \(E_\gamma = 2.5\) GeV, the cross section for the \(K^-\) exchange contribution varies from 0.5 nb to 0.8 nb when the form-factor exponent \(n\) is changed from 2 to 1.
* At \(E_\gamma = 2.5\) GeV, the cross section for the \(K^{*-}\) exchange contribution varies from 0.3 nb to 0.7 nb when the form-factor exponent \(n\) is changed from 2 to 1.
* The \(\eta \Lambda\) invariant mass spectrum shows a peak at \(M_{\eta\Lambda} \approx 1.675\) GeV, attributed to the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance.
* The \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance has a total width of 35 MeV and a branching ratio of 0.18 to \(\Lambda\eta\).
* The \(N(1535)\) resonance has a total width of 150 MeV and a branching ratio of 0.42 to \(N\eta\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical analyses of the \(K^- p \to \eta \Lambda\) reaction, where the \(\Lambda(1670)\) was found to dominate near threshold (Liu 2012, Xiao 2013, Kamano 2014), to the photoproduction process \(\gamma p \to K^+ \eta \Lambda\). The study provides model predictions for total and differential cross sections that can be tested by future experiments, and it highlights the need for unitary treatments and coupled-channel effects, as noted in prior work by Kamano (2009, 2011) and Suzuki (2009).
