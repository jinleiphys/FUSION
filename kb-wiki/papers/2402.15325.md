# Perturbative computations of neutron-proton scattering observables using renormalization-group invariant $χ$EFT up to N$^3$LO

> We predict neutron-proton scattering cross-sections and polarization observables up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order in a renormalization-group invariant description of the strong nucleon-nucleon interaction. Low-energy constants are calibrated to phase shifts, sub-leading corrections are computed in distorted-wave perturbation theory, and we employ momentum-cutoff values 500 and 2500 MeV. We find a steady order-by-order convergence and realistic descriptions of scattering observables up to a laboratory scattering energy of approximately 100 MeV. We also compare perturbative and non-perturbative calculations for phase shifts and cross sections and quantify how unitarity is gradually restored at higher orders. The perturbative approach offers an important diagnostic tool for any power counting and our results suggest that the breakdown scale in chiral effective field theory might be significantly lower than estimates obtained in non-perturbative calculations.

## Key claim

The authors predict neutron-proton scattering observables using a renormalization-group invariant formulation of chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, finding that the breakdown scale of the theory might be significantly lower than estimates from non-perturbative calculations, with order-by-order convergence up to approximately 100 MeV laboratory energy but deterioration at higher energies.

## Method

The authors construct nucleon-nucleon potentials following the modified Weinberg power counting of Long and Yang, where one-pion exchange is treated non-perturbatively at leading order in six low partial waves, while all sub-leading corrections are included using distorted-wave perturbation theory. Low-energy constants are calibrated to reproduce Nijmegen phase shifts at selected scattering energies, and calculations are performed for two momentum-cutoff values of 500 MeV and 2500 MeV. Scattering observables are computed from the spin-scattering matrix constructed from perturbatively summed partial-wave amplitudes up to N$^3$LO.

## Key numbers

- Momentum cutoff values used: 500 MeV and 2500 MeV
- Laboratory scattering energy range for reliable predictions: up to approximately 100 MeV
- Calibration data: 33 single-energy Nijmegen phase shifts used to determine the total number of contact low-energy constants up to N$^3$LO
- Calibration energies for $^1S_0$ channel: 5 MeV at LO; 5 and 25 MeV at NLO; 5, 25, and 50 MeV at NNLO; 5, 25, 50, and 75 MeV at N$^3$LO
- Total cross sections computed up to $T_\mathrm{lab} = 150$ MeV, with deterioration in agreement above approximately 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the modified Weinberg power counting approach of Long and Yang (2012, 2013) by providing the first predictions of neutron-proton scattering observables beyond leading order in a renormalization-group invariant chiral effective field theory framework. The perturbative treatment offers a diagnostic tool for power counting schemes and enables future uncertainty quantification studies, while also suggesting that the breakdown scale may be lower than estimates from non-perturbative calculations such as those by Reinert (2017) and Entem (2017). The results call for further investigation of the role of the $\Delta(1232)$-isobar in two-pion exchange and for Bayesian inference methods to quantify posterior probability distributions for low-energy constants.
