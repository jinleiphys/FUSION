# Examining the influence of hadronic interactions on the directed flow of identified particles in RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies using UrQMD model

> The directed flow of identified particles can serve as a sensitive tool for investigating the interactions during initial and final states in heavy ion collisions. This study examines the rapidity-odd directed flow ($v_{1}$) and its slope ($dv_{1}/dy$) for $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, p, and $\bar{\mathrm p}$ in Au+Au collisions at different collision centralities and beam energies ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV) using the UrQMD model. We investigate the impact of late-stage hadronic interactions on charge dependent $v_{1}(y)$ and its slope by modifying the duration of the hadronic cascade lifetime ($τ$). The energy dependence of $dv_{1}/dy$ for p ($\bar{\mathrm p}$) exhibits distinct pattern compared to $π^{\pm}$ and $K^{\pm}$. Notably, we observe a change in the sign reversal position of proton $dv_{1}/dy$ at different beam energies with varying $τ$ in central and mid-central collisions. Moreover, the difference in $dv_{1}/dy$ between positively and negatively charged hadrons ($Δdv_{1}/dy$) demonstrates a stark centrality dependence for different particle species. The deuteron displays a significant increase in $dv_{1}/dy$ with increasing $τ$ compared to p and n. This investigation underscores the importance of considering the temporal evolution and duration of the hadronic phase when interpreting the sign reversal, charge splitting of $v_{1}$ and light nuclei formation at lower RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The directed flow slope of protons and its sign reversal position shift with the duration of the hadronic cascade lifetime in the UrQMD model, indicating that the hadronic phase duration must be accounted for when interpreting sign reversal as a signature of the QCD phase transition, rather than attributing it solely to softening of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) model in its default CASCADE mode (no potentials, EoS=0) to simulate Au+Au collisions at six beam energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV. They varied the hadronic cascade lifetime parameter tau from 5 to 20 fm/c to control the duration of late-stage hadronic interactions, and extracted the odd-component of directed flow v1 and its slope dv1/dy for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and deuterons (the latter formed via a dynamical coalescence model using the phase-space distributions of protons and neutrons at freeze-out).

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV
- Hadronic cascade lifetime parameter tau varied over 5, 10, and 20 fm/c
- Centrality ranges: 0-10%, 10-40%, and 40-80%
- For deuteron coalescence: sigma_d = 3.2 fm (corresponding to RMS charge radius of 1.96 fm); statistical factor g_d = 3/8
- Proton dv1/dy sign change in UrQMD occurs around 19 GeV, while STAR data shows sign change at approximately 10 GeV
- At 7.7 GeV, Delta dv1/dy exhibits marked centrality dependence across all particle species, with magnitude increasing as tau increases from 5 to 20 fm/c in central and mid-central collisions
- Deuteron dv1/dy shows a larger relative increase with increasing tau compared to protons and neutrons at the same energy

## Context

This work extends studies of directed flow at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (STAR Collaboration, 2014, 2017, 2020) by using the UrQMD model to isolate the effect of hadronic phase duration, which is often neglected. Previous work by Singha (2016) noted inconsistencies among models treating the equation of state similarly, and Nara (2016) found that hadronic mean field interactions alone cannot account for the negative proton directed flow slope. The study enables future investigations that must disentangle the roles of the equation of state, mean field potentials, baryon stopping, and electromagnetic fields from hadronic phase effects when interpreting directed flow measurements and light nuclei formation at lower RHIC energies.
