# Density dependence of symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness revisited using relativistic mean field models with nonlinear couplings

> The correlation between neutron skin-thickness of a neutron-rich nucleus and slope parameter of symmetry energy is assessed as a function of density using relativistic mean field models containing non-linear couplings among different mesons. Models with larger skin were found to probe the slope parameter even at suprasaturation densities, whereas models with smaller skin were observed to be sensitive only at specific subsaturation density, connected to the average density of nuclei. Possible reasons behind this density dependence are explored systematically. These results might be model specific, which need to be reassessed in other type of interactions existing in the literature. Nevertheless, extrapolating the predictions at high densities from models, which are optimized by data at saturation or subsaturation densities, needs to be handled with care.

## Key claim

The correlation between neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich nuclei and the density slope parameter of symmetry energy is density-dependent: models with larger neutron skin thickness probe the slope parameter even at suprasaturation densities, while models with smaller skin are sensitive only at a specific subsaturation density. The author notes these results might be model specific and need reassessment in other types of interactions.

## Method

Four relativistic mean field models (MOD16, MOD18, MOD19, MOD23) with nonlinear couplings among sigma, omega, and rho mesons were fitted to binding energies and charge radii of 12 closed-shell spherical nuclei, with the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb fixed to four different values (0.16, 0.18, 0.19, and 0.23 fm). The correlation coefficient between neutron skin thickness and the density slope parameter L was then calculated as a function of density using covariance analysis, and the density dependence of the Jacobians for L with respect to model parameters was examined.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness in 208Pb fixed to four values: 0.16 fm (MOD16), 0.18 fm (MOD18), 0.19 fm (MOD19), and 0.23 fm (MOD23)
- All four models have a crossover in symmetry energy at around rho = 0.1 fm^-3, with C_sym(rho=0.1) approximately 24 MeV
- Maximum correlation coefficient between Delta r_np of 208Pb and L parameter appears at densities: 0.09 fm^-3 (MOD16, coefficient 0.967), 0.1 fm^-3 (MOD18, coefficient 0.972), 0.105 fm^-3 (MOD19, coefficient 0.972), and 0.15 fm^-3 (MOD23, coefficient 0.986)
- Correlation coefficient between Delta r_np of 48Ca and 208Pb ranges from minimum -0.79 (MOD19) to maximum -0.92 (MOD18)
- Chi-squared values for all four models lie between 0.7 and 0.8
- All models marginally satisfy the pulsar mass constraint of 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses for PSR J0348+0432

## Context

This work extends the analytic relation between symmetry energy parameters and neutron skin thickness established by Centelles et al. (2009) using the Droplet model, and addresses the debate raised by Zhang et al. (2013, 2014, 2015) on whether the correlation between L and neutron skin thickness should be at saturation or subsaturation densities. The results suggest that extrapolating high-density predictions from models optimized by data at saturation or subsaturation densities needs careful handling, and the findings should be explored in other relativistic and non-relativistic interactions.
