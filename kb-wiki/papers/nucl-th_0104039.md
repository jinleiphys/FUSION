# Extended Gari-Krumpelmann model fits to nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> Nucleon electromagnetic form factor data (including recent data) is fitted with models that respect the confinement and asymptotic freedom properties of QCD. Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) type models, which include the major vector meson pole contributions and at high momentum transfer conform to the predictions of perturbative QCD, are combined with Hohler-Pietarinen (HP) models, which also include the width of the rho meson and the addition of higher mass vector meson exchanges, but do not evolve into the explicit form of PQCD at high momentum transfer. Different parameterizations of the GK model's hadronic form factors, the effect of including the width of the rho meson and the addition of the next (in mass) isospin 1 vector meson are considered. The quality of fit and the consistency of the parameters select three of the combined HP/GK type models. Projections are made to the higher momentum transfers which are relevant to electron-deuteron experiments. The projections vary little for the preferred models, removing much of the ambiguity in electron-nucleus scattering predictions.

## Key claim

By combining the Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) model's vector meson dominance and perturbative QCD constraints with the Hohler-Pietarinen (HP) type dispersion relation treatment of the rho meson width and an additional rho-prime (1450) pole, three specific parameterizations yield nucleon electromagnetic form factor predictions that vary little from each other even when extrapolated to higher momentum transfers, removing much of the ambiguity in electron-nucleus scattering predictions.

## Method

The author fitted seven variations of the Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) model to the world data set for the proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factors (G_Ep, G_Mp, G_En, G_Mn) and the ratio R_p = mu_p G_Ep/G_Mp. The models combined the original GK pole terms for rho, omega, and phi mesons with hadronic form factors that enforce the asymptotic scaling behavior of perturbative QCD, and in four models replaced the simple rho pole with a dispersion relation treatment of the rho meson width (following Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996)) plus an explicit rho-prime (1450) pole. The 11 to 12 free parameters were optimized by minimizing chi-squared using a Levenberg-Marquardt method.

## Key numbers

- The total chi-squared values for the GK type models ranged from 348.6 to 352.7, while for the DR-GK type models (including the rho width and rho-prime pole) the range was 322.6 to 327.1, indicating significantly better fits for the dispersion-relation-based models.
- The slope dG_En/dQ^2 at Q^2=0 was fixed to 0.0199 +/- 0.0003 fm^2, as determined by thermal neutron scattering (Kopecky, 1995).
- For the three favored models (DRN-GK(3), DR-GK(1), and DR-GK'(1)), the extrapolated values of R_p at Q^2 = 8 GeV^2/c^2 differed by as much as 0.1, and the extrapolated G_Mn values differed by almost 0.2.
- The best fit value of the rho normalization factor N for model DRN-GK(3) was 0.7838, with chi-squared increasing to 375.1 when N was fixed to 1.0.
- The original GK parameters from 1992 produced chi-squared values 2.4 to 3.0 times larger than the best fits to the present data set.

## Context

This work extends the Gari-Krumpelmann model (Gari and Krumpelmann, 1992) by incorporating the dispersion relation treatment of the rho meson width from Mergell, Meissner, and Drechsel (1996) and the Hohler-Pietarinen approach (Hohler et al., 1976), which includes higher mass vector meson exchanges. The analysis shows that the three models with parameters most consistent with independent evaluations (Lambda_QCD in the 100-300 MeV range and reasonable kappa_rho values) produce nearly identical predictions, enabling more reliable extraction of few-nucleon wave function and meson exchange current effects from electron-deuteron scattering data up to Q^2 = 6 GeV^2/c^2.
