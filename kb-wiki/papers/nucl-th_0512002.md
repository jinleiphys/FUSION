# Origin of three-body resonances

> We expose the relation between the properties of the three-body continuum states and their two-body subsystems. These properties refer to their bound and virtual states and resonances, all defined as poles of the $S$-matrix. For one infinitely heavy core and two non-interacting light particles, the complex energies of the three-body poles are the sum of the two two-body complex pole-energies. These generic relations are modified by center-of-mass effects which alone can produce a Borromean system. We show how the three-body states evolve in $^6$He, $^6$Li, and $^6$Be when the nucleon-nucleon interaction is continuously switched on. The schematic model is able to reproduce the main properties in their spectra. Realistic calculations for these nuclei are shown in detail for comparison. The implications of a core with non-zero spin are investigated and illustrated for $^{17}$Ne ($^{15}$O+p+p). Dimensionless units allow predictions for systems of different scales.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that three-body resonances arise only when two-body poles from two different subsystems coincide simultaneously, with the three-body pole energy being the sum of the two two-body pole energies. This relation holds strictly only for the schematic case of an infinitely heavy core with two non-interacting light particles; center-of-mass effects and spin-dependent interactions modify this simple additivity.

## Method
The authors employed the complex-scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to solve the Faddeev equations for three-body systems. They first derived analytic expressions for the three-body S-matrix in the schematic limit of an infinitely heavy core and two non-interacting light particles, then numerically studied how three-body states evolve in the complex energy plane as realistic features (finite masses, nucleon-nucleon interactions, core spin) are progressively incorporated. The method was applied to the A=6 isobars (⁶He, ⁶Li, ⁶Be) and ¹⁷Ne.

## Key numbers
- For the schematic system with two gaussian p-wave interactions producing two-body resonances at 2mb²E₁₂/ℏ² = 0.073 − i 0.033 and 2mb²E₁₃/ℏ² = 0.024 − i 0.007, the three-body resonance appears at 2mb²E/ℏ² = 0.097 − i 0.040, precisely the sum of the two two-body energies.
- The three-body resonance energy equals E₁₂ + E₁₃ = (0.073 + 0.024) − i(0.033 + 0.007) = 0.097 − i 0.040 in dimensionless units.
- For the spin-1/2 core with two spin-1/2 light particles and identical interactions, the three-body eigenvalues become (1/2)E⁽⁰⁾ + (3/2)E⁽¹⁾ and (3/2)E⁽⁰⁾ + (1/2)E⁽¹⁾, where E⁽⁰⁾ and E⁽¹⁾ are the two-body energies for j=0 and j=1 channels respectively.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of three-body continuum structure beyond bound-state halo systems, which were previously well-characterized by Jensen (2004) and others. The paper provides a systematic framework connecting two-body S-matrix poles to three-body resonances, complementing earlier Faddeev-based studies by Glöckle (1996) and the complex-scaling approach of Ho (1983). The results enable interpretation of continuum spectra in Borromean nuclei like ⁶He and ¹⁷Ne, and provide a foundation for predicting three-body resonances in other systems through the dimensionless units developed.
