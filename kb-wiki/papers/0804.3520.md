# Kinetic description of particle emission from expanding source

> The freeze out of the expanding systems, created in relativistic heavy ion collisions, is discussed. We combine kinetic freeze out equations with Bjorken type system expansion into a unified model. The important feature of the proposed scenario is that physical freeze out is completely finished in a finite time, which can be varied from 0 (freeze out hypersurface) to infinity. The dependence of the post freeze out distribution function on the freeze out time will be studied. As an example, model is completely solved and analyzed for the gas of pions. We shall see that the basic freeze out features, pointed out in the earlier works, are not smeared out by the expansion of the system. The entropy evolution in such a scenario is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a gradual freeze-out (FO) layer of finite thickness can be combined with Bjorken-type longitudinal expansion into a unified kinetic model, and that the basic features of the freeze-out process identified in earlier non-expanding models are not smeared out by the expansion. The hedge is that this conclusion is demonstrated only for a gas of pions in the fast rethermalization limit, and the model is best justified for the reaction region near midrapidity.

## Method

The authors combine the gradual freeze-out kinetic equations of Molnar and Csernai (2005) with the Bjorken one-dimensional expansion model. The system is divided into an interacting component and a frozen-out component; the interacting component follows Bjorken hydrodynamics plus a loss term due to freeze-out, while the free component accumulates particles via the escape probability \(P_{esc}\) and also expands. The equations are solved semi-analytically in the fast rethermalization limit for a massive pion gas with a Jüttner equilibrium distribution, using a finite FO layer of thickness \(L\) between two parallel hypersurfaces at proper times \(\tau_1\) and \(\tau_1+L\).

## Key numbers

- The FO layer thickness \(L\) can be varied from \(0\) (freeze-out hypersurface) to \(\infty\).
- The initial temperature is \(T_i(\tau_0=0.05\ \text{fm}) = 820\ \text{MeV}\); the FO begins at \(\tau_1 = 5\ \text{fm}\) where \(T_i(\tau_1) = T_{FO} = 180\ \text{MeV}\).
- The characteristic FO length scale is \(\tau_{FO} = 0.5\ \text{fm}\).
- For a FO layer as thin as \(2\tau_{FO}\) (i.e., \(L = 1.0\ \text{fm}\)), the entropy production is negligible, around \(1\%\).
- The post-FO distribution for layers of thickness several \(\tau_{FO}\) approaches a universal form; in the medium momentum region \(0.5\ \text{GeV} < |p| < 2.5\ \text{GeV}\) it can be fitted by a thermal distribution with temperature \(T = 160\ \text{MeV}\), but this fit fails at low and high momenta.

## Context

This work extends the gradual freeze-out models of Molnar and Csernai (2005) and earlier studies of time-like and space-like FO layers (Csernai et al., 2005; Molnar et al., 2005) by incorporating Bjorken expansion, which was missing in those oversimplified models. It contrasts with the approach of Grassi et al. (2004), who used a longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamical model with continuous emission and a different escape probability based on the Glauber formula, and found entropy increases up to \(70\%\). The present model enables future quantitative calculations of post-FO spectra and two-particle correlations for comparison with experimental data, particularly at midrapidity.
