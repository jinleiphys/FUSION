# Formation time effect on J/ψdynamical nuclear suppression

> The proposed hadronic and string cascade model, JPCIAE, for ultrarelativistic nucleus - nucleus collisions based on the LUND model and the PYTHIA event generator especially, is used to investigate the $J/ψ$ suppression due to the nuclear absorption of a $J/ψ$ in minimum bias pA and BA collisions at 200 A GeV energy. With the different sets of reasonable formation time for hadron and $J/ψ$ the results of $J/ψ$ suppression factor from both the usual scenario and the Glauber - like simulations are comparable with all the NA38 pA and BA data, except the NA50 data of Pb + Pb collisions. However, the difference between the usual scenario and the Glauber - like simulation, hence the difference between the dynamical simulation and Glauber theory, can not be ignored. The sensitive effect of the hadron formation time on the $J/ψ$ suppression is studied in detail. The results seem to denote that for the $J/ψ$ suppression the meson absorption plays role in pA as well as in BA collisions.

# Formation time effect on J/ψ dynamical nuclear suppression

## Key claim
The JPCIAE hadronic and string cascade model can describe the NA38 data on J/ψ suppression in pA and BA collisions at 200 A GeV using reasonable formation time parameters, but it cannot reproduce the NA50 Pb+Pb data. The difference between the full dynamical simulation (usual scenario with complete hadron rescattering) and the Glauber-like simulation (rescattering only between J/ψ and other hadrons) is significant and formation-time dependent.

## Method
The authors constructed the JPCIAE model based on the LUND model and PYTHIA event generator, simulating nucleus-nucleus collisions in the laboratory system with Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions and Lorentz contraction. For each hadron-hadron collision with center-of-mass energy above 4 GeV, PYTHIA was called to produce hadrons including J/ψ via the process g + g → J/ψ + g; otherwise conventional scattering was executed. The J/ψ suppression factor was calculated as the ratio of final J/ψ multiplicity after interactions to primary J/ψ multiplicity, using absorption cross sections of 6 mb for J/ψ-baryon and 3 mb for J/ψ-meson interactions.

## Key numbers
- Meson formation time τ_M = 1.2 fm/c and J/ψ formation time τ_{J/ψ} = 0.5 × τ_M (parameter set 1) used in usual scenario calculations
- Meson formation time τ_M = 0.8 fm/c and J/ψ formation time τ_{J/ψ} = 0.5 × τ_M (parameter set 2) used in Glauber-like calculations
- J/ψ-baryon absorption cross section σ_{J/ψ-B}^{Abs} = 6 mb; J/ψ-meson absorption cross section σ_{J/ψ-M}^{Abs} = 3 mb
- Total cross sections used: σ_{J/ψ-B}^{tot} = 7.2 mb and σ_{J/ψ-M}^{tot} = 4.0 mb
- J/ψ production cross section parametrized as σ_{NN→J/ψ+X} = d(1 − c/√s)^{12} with c = 3.097 GeV and d = 2.37/B_{μμ} nb (B_{μμ} = 0.0597)
- All calculations performed at scaled energy of 200 A GeV for minimum bias pA and BA collisions

## Context
This work extends the Glauber theory absorption model studies by Capella et al. (various years) and the covariant transport approach of Cassing and Bratkovskaya (1996-1997) by introducing a full dynamical cascade with formation time effects. The model successfully reproduces NA38 data for p+Al, p+Cu, p+Ag, P+U, O+Cu, and O+U collisions but fails to describe the anomalous suppression in Pb+Pb observed by NA50, leaving the mechanism for that system as an open question. The finding that meson absorption plays a role in both pA and BA collisions contradicts earlier conclusions by several authors (various years) who argued meson effects were negligible in pA collisions.
