# Correcting event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions for exact global conservation laws with the generalized subensemble acceptance method

> We introduce the subensemble acceptance method 2.0 (SAM-2.0) -- a procedure to correct cumulants of a random number distribution inside a subsystem for the effect of exact global conservation of a conserved quantity to which this number is correlated, with applications to measurements of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. The method expresses the corrected cumulants in terms of the cumulants inside and outside the subsystem that are not subject to the exact conservation. The derivation assumes that all probability distributions associated with the cumulants are peaked at the mean values but are otherwise of arbitrary shape. The formalism reduces to the original SAM [V. Vovchenko et al., Phys.Lett.B 811 (2020) 135868 [arXiv:2003.13905]] when applied to a coordinate space subvolume of a uniform thermal system. As the new method is restricted neither to the uniform systems nor to the coordinate space, it is applicable to fluctuations measured in heavy-ion collisions at various collision energies in different momentum space acceptances. The SAM-2.0 thus brings the experimental measurements and theoretical calculations of event-by-event fluctuations closer together, as the latter are typically performed without the account of exact global conservation.

## Key claim

The subensemble acceptance method 2.0 (SAM-2.0) corrects cumulants of event-by-event fluctuations measured in a subsystem for the effect of exact global conservation laws, under the assumption that all relevant probability distributions are highly peaked at their mean values but otherwise of arbitrary shape; unlike the original SAM, the new method is not restricted to uniform systems or coordinate-space partitions, making it applicable to momentum-space acceptances at various collision energies.

## Method

The method partitions the system into an in-system (acceptance) and an out-system (complement), assumes the unconstrained (grand-canonical) distributions of the conserved quantity in each subsystem are independent and highly peaked at their means, and then imposes exact global conservation via a Kronecker delta constraint. The corrected cumulants inside the acceptance are expressed recursively in terms of the unconstrained cumulants inside and outside the acceptance using Faà di Bruno's formula applied to the cumulant generating function. For non-conserved quantities correlated to a conserved charge, the procedure is extended to joint cumulants by solving a system of linear equations derived from multivariate Faà di Bruno expansions.

## Key numbers

- For a uniform thermal system partitioned into volumes \(V_1 = \alpha V\) and \(V_2 = (1-\alpha)V\), the corrected variance is \(\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm ce} = V T^3 \alpha (1-\alpha) \chi_2^B\) and the corrected third cumulant is \(\kappa_{3,\rm in}^{\rm ce} = V T^3 \alpha (1-\alpha) (1-2\alpha) \chi_3^B\), reproducing the original SAM results.
- The second-order cumulant of a conserved quantity after correction is \(\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm ce} = (\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm gce} \kappa_{2,\rm out}^{\rm gce}) / (\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm gce} + \kappa_{2,\rm out}^{\rm gce})\).
- The third-order cumulant after correction is \(\kappa_{3,\rm in}^{\rm ce} = [\kappa_{3,\rm in}^{\rm gce} (\kappa_{2,\rm out}^{\rm gce})^3 - \kappa_{3,\rm out}^{\rm gce} (\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm gce})^3] / (\kappa_{2,\rm in}^{\rm gce} + \kappa_{2,\rm out}^{\rm gce})^3\).
- For a non-conserved quantity \(N\) correlated to conserved charge \(B\), the corrected covariance is \(\kappa_{1,1;\rm in}^{\rm ce} = (\kappa_{1,1;\rm in}^{\rm gce} \kappa_{2,0;\rm out}^{\rm gce}) / (\kappa_{2,0;\rm in}^{\rm gce} + \kappa_{2,0;\rm out}^{\rm gce})\).
- The application to Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV uses a binomial probability \(q \approx 0.33\) for a baryon to end up as a proton.

## Context

This work extends the original subensemble acceptance method (SAM) of Vovchenko et al. (2020), which was restricted to coordinate-space subvolumes of uniform thermal systems, to arbitrary subsystems including momentum-space acceptances and non-uniform systems. The SAM-2.0 enables direct correction of theoretical calculations from dynamical models (e.g., hydrodynamics) for exact global conservation laws, bringing them closer to experimental measurements where only a subset of particles (e.g., protons rather than all baryons) can be detected. The method generalizes earlier treatments of baryon number conservation effects on net proton fluctuations by Bzdak (2012) to arbitrary orders and arbitrary peaked distributions.
