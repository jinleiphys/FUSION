# First proton-pair breaking in semi-magic nuclei beyond 132Sn and 208Pb: Configuration of the long-lived isomer of 217Pa

> The close similarity between the shell structures in the 132Sn and 208Pb regions is a well known phenomenon. Thus, using the correspondence between the high-j orbits located above the Z=50 and Z=82 shell gaps, we discuss the evolutions of the fully aligned states with one broken proton pair in the N=82 and N=126 isotones. A long-lived isomeric state was discovered in 217Pa more than thirty years ago and despite two other experiments giving new experimental results, the discussions on its main properties (spin, parity, configuration) remained inconclusive. Then, using the comparison with the I^π=17/2^+ isomeric state recently measured in 139La, the isomeric state of 217Pa is assigned as the fully aligned state of the (πh_{9/2})^2(πf_{7/2})^1 configuration.

# First proton-pair breaking in semi-magic nuclei beyond 132Sn and 208Pb: Configuration of the long-lived isomer of 217Pa

## Key claim

The long-lived isomeric state of 217Pa, previously assigned as a 29/2+ state from the (πh9/2)2(πi13/2)1 configuration, is instead the fully-aligned 23/2- state of the (πh9/2)2(πf7/2)1 configuration, based on systematic comparison with the 17/2+ isomeric state recently measured in 139La and the close similarity of shell structures between the 132Sn and 208Pb regions.

## Method

The authors compare the excitation-energy systematics of fully-aligned states with one broken proton pair across N=82 isotones (Z=52-58) and N=126 isotones (Z=84-92), using the known correspondence between high-j orbits above the Z=50 and Z=82 shell gaps (πg7/2 → πh9/2, πd5/2 → πf7/2, πh11/2 → πi13/2). They evaluate the previously measured α-decay and γ-decay properties of the 217Pa isomer against the systematic trends of the 23/2- and 29/2+ states in neighboring isotones, and compare with the recently characterized 17/2+ isomer in 139La.

## Key numbers

- The isomeric state in 217Pa is located at ~1854(7) keV, measured by its α decay.
- The isomeric state in 139La has a half-life T1/2 = 315 ns, decaying via an E2 transition of 89 keV with B(E2) = 1.9 W.u.
- The isomeric state in 217Pa has T1/2 = 1.2(2) ms, with an internal transition branch of 27(4)% giving a partial γ half-life T1/2^γ ~ 4.4 ms.
- The proposed E3 transition in 217Pa would have an energy of about 20 keV, with B(E3) ~ 30 W.u., comparable to the 29/2+ → 23/2- transitions in lighter isotones (211At, 213Fr, 215Ac) which have B(E3) ~ 25 W.u.
- The 8+ isomeric state in 210Pb has T1/2 = 201 ns; the 8+ isomeric state in 210Po has T1/2 = 99 ns.
- In 133Sb, the πd5/2 orbit lies 962 keV above the πg7/2 orbit.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of fully-aligned states from broken proton pairs in N=82 isotones (Astier 2012) to the analogous N=126 region, resolving a thirty-year ambiguity about the 217Pa isomer first observed by Schmidt-Ott (1979). The assignment contradicts the earlier interpretation by Ikuta (1998) and Heßberger (2002), who proposed a 29/2+ state, by showing that such an assignment would require an implausible 650 keV drop in excitation energy from 215Ac to 217Pa. The result enables predictions for similar three-nucleon isomeric states in heavy Pb isotopes (Lane 2005) and potentially in super-heavy nuclei near N=184, where a (νj13/2)2(νh11/2)1 configuration with Iπ=35/2- could produce very long-lived isomers with highly hindered α decay.
