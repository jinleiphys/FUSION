# Inclusive Quasielastic and Deep Inelastic Scattering of Polarized Electrons by Polarized $^{3}$He}

> A comprehensive treatement of the theoretical approach for describing nuclear effects in inclusive scattering of polarized electrons by polarized $^3$He is presented.

# Inclusive Quasielastic and Deep Inelastic Scattering of Polarized Electrons by Polarized $^{3}$He

## Key claim
The proton contribution to the measured asymmetry in polarized electron-$^{3}$He scattering is sizeable in both the quasielastic and deep inelastic regions, hindering the direct extraction of neutron properties; however, a specific polarization angle can be chosen to make the proton contribution vanish at the quasielastic peak, and a factorized formula with effective nucleon polarizations reliably extracts the neutron spin structure function $g_1^n$ from the $^{3}$He data, with residual nuclear effects of at most 4%.

## Method
The authors develop a theoretical framework within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) to compute the polarized inclusive cross section and asymmetry for scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons by polarized $^{3}$He. The nuclear structure is described using a spin-dependent spectral function that incorporates the $S'$ and $D$ wave components of the $^{3}$He ground state, and the nucleon structure functions are related to Sachs form factors in the quasielastic region and to spin structure functions in the deep inelastic region. The asymmetry is calculated from the polarized structure functions $G_1^A$ and $G_2^A$ of the $^{3}$He target, and the proton and neutron contributions are separated to assess their relative importance.

## Key numbers
- Experimental asymmetry at $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV and $\theta_e = 44^\circ$, averaged over three polarization angles around $\theta^* \approx 90^\circ$ and over a 100 MeV energy transfer interval: $A^{exp}_{qe} = 2.41 \mp 1.29 \mp 0.51\%$; theoretical value $A^{th} = 1.65\%$ (without averaging: $3.74\%$).
- Experimental asymmetry at $\epsilon_1 = 574$ MeV and $\theta_e = 51.1^\circ$, averaged over three polarization angles around $\theta^* \approx 0^\circ$ and over a 50 MeV interval: $A^{exp}_{qe} = -3.79 \mp 1.37 \mp 0.67\%$; theoretical value $A^{th} = -4.30\%$ (without averaging: $-3.43\%$).
- Effective nucleon polarizations in $^{3}$He: $p_p = -0.030$ and $p_n = 0.88$, consistent with world values $p_p = -0.028 \pm 0.004$ and $p_n = 0.86 \pm 0.02$ from Friar et al. (1990).
- Difference between the free neutron structure function $g_1^n$ and the neutron contribution extracted from $^{3}$He is at most $4\%$ due to binding and Fermi motion effects.
- The quasielastic analysis covers the range $0.3 \leq Q^2 \leq 2$ (GeV/c)$^2$ for investigating sensitivity to $G_E^n$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ciofi degli Atti, Pace, and Salmè, 1992) on spin-dependent spectral functions for $^{3}$He, and provides a unified treatment of both quasielastic and deep inelastic scattering. The results are compared with experimental data from the MIT-Caltech collaboration (Jones-Woodward et al., 1993) and with calculations using Faddeev spectral functions by Schultze and Sauer (1993). The analysis enables the extraction of the neutron electric form factor $G_E^n$ in the quasielastic region by selecting a kinematic configuration where the proton contribution vanishes, and provides a validated factorized formula for extracting the neutron spin structure function $g_1^n$ from $^{3}$He data, which is essential for tests of the Bjorken Sum Rule (Bjorken, 1971) and for interpreting results from experiments such as E142 (Anthony et al., 1993).
