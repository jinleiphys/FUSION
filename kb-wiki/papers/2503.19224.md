# Quark antenna in early stage anisotropic QCD matter

> The states of matter produced in the early stage of heavy ion collisions can be highly anisotropic. If such a feature is sufficiently pronounced, one should expect the final particle distribution inside jets to reflect it in the form of non-trivial angle correlations. In this talk, we discuss a first step in exploring such correlations by studying how a $q\bar q$ state produced from an initial unpolarized gluon couples to the anisotropies of an underlying static QCD medium. The medium anisotropy is captured by allowing the jet quenching parameter to take different magnitudes in two orthogonal directions in the plane transverse to the jet axis. We find that the final particle distribution is sensitive to the medium anisotropy in the form of an azimuthal angle modulation, and more importantly, that this effect couples directly to the helicity/spin of the final states, offering a novel way to extract the details of the underlying matter which is not accessible with standard jet observables. We further show how such features can be extracted from the Fourier decomposition of the distribution and from final state transverse spin polarization measurements.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a quark-antiquark pair produced from an unpolarized gluon inside an anisotropic QCD medium develops an azimuthal angle modulation in its final particle distribution that couples directly to the helicity and spin of the final states, but this effect is derived under the assumptions of a static medium of fixed length, the harmonic approximation for the dipole cross-section, and the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order cross-section for the \(g\rightarrow q\bar q\) process (a quark antenna) inside a static QCD medium using the BDMPS-Z path integral formalism, resumming all gluon exchanges with the medium at leading order in eikonality. The medium anisotropy is introduced by allowing the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) to take different values along two orthogonal directions in the transverse plane to the jet axis, denoted \(\hat q_x\) and \(\hat q_y\). The resulting spectrum is derived analytically in the harmonic approximation and large-\(N_c\) limit, and the final particle distribution is analyzed through its Fourier decomposition and through transverse spin polarization measurements for massive quarks.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless anisotropy parameter is defined as \(\zeta = (\sqrt{\hat q_y} - \sqrt{\hat q_x})/(\sqrt{\hat q_y} + \sqrt{\hat q_x})\), with values explored in the range \(0.10 \leq \zeta \leq 0.95\).
- The dimensionless medium length parameter is \(r = L^+ (\sqrt{\hat q_y} + \sqrt{\hat q_x})/(2\sqrt{2q_0^+})\), with a typical value \(r \sim 2\) estimated from \(L^+/\sqrt{2} \sim 5\) fm and \(q_0^+/\sqrt{2} \sim 100\) GeV.
- The dimensionless mass parameter is \(\mu = 2 m^2 / [(\sqrt{\hat q_y} + \sqrt{\hat q_x})\sqrt{q_0^+}]\), with a typical value \(\mu \sim 0.16\) estimated using the charm quark mass \(m = 1.27\) GeV/\(c^2\) and \((\sqrt{\hat q_x} + \sqrt{\hat q_y})/2 \sim 2\) GeV fm\(^{-1/2}\).
- For massless quarks, the \(v_2\) Fourier harmonic (elliptic-type deformation) monotonically increases with \(\zeta\), while the \(w_2^{(+1)}\) harmonic (helicity-dependent) is non-monotonic with both \(\zeta\) and \(z\), vanishing at \(z=0\) and \(z=0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic medium effects on jet observables, following the approach of Hauksson (2023) for pure gluonic systems, by considering the quark-antiquark antenna. It builds on the BDMPS-Z formalism (Zakharov, 1996; Baier, 1996) and uses the harmonic approximation to model the dipole cross-section. The results enable new experimental probes of early-stage QCD matter anisotropy through Fourier azimuthal harmonics inside jets and through transverse spin polarization measurements, which are not accessible with standard jet observables.
