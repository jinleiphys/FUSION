# The Solar hep Process in Effective Field Theory

> Using effective field theory, we calculate the S-factor for the hep process in a totally parameter-free formulation. The transition operators are organized according to chiral counting, and their matrix elements are evaluated using the realistic nuclear wave functions obtained in the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics method. Terms of up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory are considered. Fixing the only parameter in the theory by fitting the tritium β-decay rate, we predict the hep S-factor with accuracy better than \sim 20 %.

## Key claim

Using a hybrid method that combines effective field theory (EFT) with realistic nuclear wave functions from the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), the authors predict the hep S-factor with an accuracy better than about 20 percent, but only after fixing the single free parameter by fitting the tritium beta-decay rate and noting that the result shows a moderate dependence on the cutoff parameter Lambda in the range 500-800 MeV.

## Method

The authors organize transition operators according to chiral counting in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), and evaluate their matrix elements using correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics wave functions obtained with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions. The single unknown parameter d^R in the axial-current two-body operator is fixed by reproducing the experimental tritium beta-decay rate for each of three cutoff values (500, 600, and 800 MeV). The resulting currents are regulated with a Gaussian cutoff in Fourier transformation before being applied to the hep process.

## Key numbers

* The predicted hep S-factor is S = (8.6 ± 1.3) × 10^{-20} keV-b, where the error spans the range of Lambda-dependence for cutoffs 500-800 MeV.
* The one-body contribution to the reduced matrix element L1(q;A) at q=19.2 MeV is -0.081 fm^{3/2} for all cutoffs.
* The total two-body contribution to L1(q;A) varies from 0.049 fm^{3/2} (Lambda=500 MeV) to 0.059 fm^{3/2} (Lambda=800 MeV).
* The total S-factor from individual channels ranges from 7.32 × 10^{-20} keV-b (Lambda=800 MeV) to 9.95 × 10^{-20} keV-b (Lambda=500 MeV).
* The tritium beta-decay parameter d^R takes values 1.00 ± 0.07 (Lambda=500 MeV), 1.78 ± 0.08 (Lambda=600 MeV), and 3.90 ± 0.10 (Lambda=800 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to solar pp fusion by Park et al. (2001) and to thermal np capture by Park, Kubodera, Min, and Rho (2000). It directly compares with the SNPA calculation of Marcucci et al. (2001), which gave S=9.64 × 10^{-20} keV-b using the same AV18/UIX interactions, and with earlier studies by Carlson et al. (1991) and Schiavilla et al. (1992) that used less accurate variational wave functions and omitted P-wave capture contributions. The result enables more precise interpretation of Super-Kamiokande solar neutrino data, where the current upper limit on the hep neutrino flux is 40 × 10^3 cm^{-2}s^{-1}, compared to the standard solar model prediction of 9.4 × 10^3 cm^{-2}s^{-1} using the Marcucci et al. S-factor.
