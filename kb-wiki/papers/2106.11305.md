# Variational coupled cluster for ground and excited states

> In single-reference coupled-cluster (CC) methods, one has to solve a set of non-linear polynomial equations in order to determine the so-called amplitudes which are then used to compute the energy and other properties. Although it is of common practice to converge to the (lowest-energy) ground-state solution, it is also possible, thanks to tailored algorithms, to access higher-energy roots of these equations which may or may not correspond to genuine excited states. Here, we explore the structure of the energy landscape of variational CC (VCC) and we compare it with its (projected) traditional version (TCC) in the case where the excitation operator is restricted to paired double excitations (pCCD). By investigating two model systems (the symmetric stretching of the linear \ce{H4} molecule and the continuous deformation of the square \ce{H4} molecule into a rectangular arrangement) in the presence of weak and strong correlations, the performance of VpCCD and TpCCD are gauged against their configuration interaction (CI) equivalent, known as doubly-occupied CI (DOCI), for reference Slater determinants made of ground- or excited-state Hartree-Fock orbitals or state-specific orbitals optimized directly at the VpCCD level. The influence of spatial symmetry breaking is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether stationary points (including saddle points and maxima) of the variational coupled cluster (VCC) energy functional can correspond to genuine excited electronic states, comparing this landscape with traditional projected CC (TCC) for the specific case of paired double excitations (pCCD). The authors find that the agreement between pCCD and DOCI holds for excited states only when state-specific optimized orbitals are employed.

## Method

The authors restrict the cluster operator to paired double excitations (pCCD) and compare variational pCCD (VpCCD) with traditional projected pCCD (TpCCD) for two model systems: symmetric stretching of linear H4 and continuous deformation of square H4 into a rectangular arrangement. They locate stationary points of the VpCCD energy functional using Newton-Raphson algorithms with exact Jacobian matrices, and perform orbital optimization at the VpCCD level using second-order Newton-Raphson methods. Energies are compared against doubly-occupied configuration interaction (DOCI) for reference Slater determinants made from ground-state Hartree-Fock orbitals, excited-state Hartree-Fock orbitals obtained via initial maximum overlap method (IMOM), and state-specific orbitals optimized at the VpCCD level.

## Key numbers

- For linear H4 in STO-6G basis, convergence thresholds for cluster amplitude and orbital optimization procedures were both set to 10^{-6} a.u.
- The convergence threshold for restricted HF calculations was set to 10^{-10} a.u.
- The paper reports energy differences between DOCI and TpCCD/VpCCD as functions of bond length R (in bohr) for linear H4, with energies plotted in hartree

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kossoski 2021) on non-standard solutions of pCCD equations for excited states, and builds on earlier investigations of CC solution structure by Jankowski et al. (1994, 1995) and the homotopy method studies by Kowalski et al. (1998). The paper addresses an open question raised by Mayhall (2010) regarding the structure of CC solutions for realistic systems, and demonstrates that Δoo-pCCD provides double excitation energies comparable to the more expensive EOM-CCSDT method. The work enables further development of variational CC approaches for excited states while highlighting the computational challenges due to the exponential scaling of VCC.
