# Coulomb exchange functional with generalized gradient approximation for self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations

> We perform the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation with the Coulomb exchange functional in the form of generalized gradient approximation (GGA). It is found that the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof GGA (PBE-GGA) Coulomb exchange functional is able to reproduce the exact-Fock energy for the nuclei in a large region of the nuclear chart with one adjustable parameter. The remaining error of Coulomb exchange energy by the GGA with respect to the exact-Fock energy dominantly comes from the functional-driven error.

# Coulomb exchange functional with generalized gradient approximation for self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations

## Key claim
The Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof generalized gradient approximation (PBE-GGA) Coulomb exchange functional, with one adjustable parameter λ multiplying the standard μ coefficient, reproduces the exact-Fock Coulomb exchange energy for nuclei across a wide region of the nuclear chart; the remaining error is dominantly functional-driven rather than density-driven, but the optimal λ value shows some dependence on mass region and remains uncertain for calcium isotopes and light or superheavy nuclei.

## Method
Self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations were performed using the SAMi nuclear energy density functional, replacing the exact-Fock Coulomb exchange term with the PBE-GGA Coulomb exchange functional. The free parameter μ in the PBE-GGA enhancement factor was scaled by a factor λ (tested values 1.00, 1.25, and 1.50), and the resulting Coulomb exchange energies were compared to exact-Fock energies computed via first-order perturbation theory for doubly magic nuclei and isotopic chains of oxygen, calcium, and tin. Spherical symmetry was assumed with a 15 fm box and 0.1 fm mesh, and protons were treated as point particles.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴He, the LDA Coulomb exchange energy is -0.627 MeV, exact-Fock is -0.732 MeV, and PBE-GGA with λ=1.25 gives -0.712 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the LDA Coulomb exchange energy is -31.265 MeV, exact-Fock is -32.090 MeV, and PBE-GGA with λ=1.25 gives -32.140 MeV.
- For the superheavy nucleus ³¹⁰126, the LDA Coulomb exchange energy is -48.304 MeV, exact-Fock is -49.305 MeV, and PBE-GGA with λ=1.00 gives -49.266 MeV.
- The deviation ΔE_Cx^(LDA) between PBE-GGA and LDA ranges from approximately 12% in ⁴He to 2% in ³¹⁰126.
- The deviation ΔE_Cx^(exact) between PBE-GGA (λ=1.25) and exact-Fock ranges from -3% in ⁴He to less than 1% in ³¹⁰126, corresponding to approximately 100 keV accuracy.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, proton single-particle energy differences between LDA and PBE-GGA are less than 10 keV, while differences between LDA and exact-Fock exceed 100 keV.
- The total energy per particle for ²⁰⁸Pb is -7.872 MeV (exact-Fock), -7.868 MeV (LDA), and -7.873 MeV (PBE-GGA with λ=1.25).
- The numerical cost of self-consistent calculations with PBE-GGA is O(N³), compared to O(N⁴) for the exact-Fock term.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Naito 2018) which showed that GGA Coulomb functionals give accurate total energies when using experimental charge densities as input, by now performing fully self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations. The PBE-GGA functional was originally developed for electronic systems by Perdew, Burke, and Ernzerhof (1996) with μ=0.21951, while the PBEsol variant (Perdew 2008) uses μ=0.1235 for solids. The present study finds that for nuclear systems, scaling μ by λ=1.25 gives optimal results across a wide mass range, differing from the values used in atomic and solid-state physics. This enables efficient O(N³) calculations of Coulomb exchange in nuclear structure, with potential applications to isobaric analog state energies (Roca-Maza 2018) and superallowed Fermi beta decay (Liang 2009, Hardy 2015). The finite-size effect of protons, which is difficult to incorporate in exact-Fock calculations but feasible in the GGA framework, is identified as an important future direction.
