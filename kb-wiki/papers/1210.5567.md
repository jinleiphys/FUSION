# Emission of Low Momentum Particles at Large Angles from Jet

> We study dynamics of a QGP fluid induced by energetic partons propagating through it. We construct a (3+1)-dimensional QGP-fluid+Jet model. When a jet traverses a uniform fluid, it induces a Mach cone structure of energy density distribution and a vortex ring surrounding a path of the jet. When a pair of jets travels through a radially expanding fluid, low momentum particles are dominantly induced at large angles from the quenched jet. This result is qualitatively consistent with observation of the CMS Collaboration at LHC.

## Key claim
When a pair of jets traverses a radially expanding QGP fluid, low momentum particles are dominantly induced at large angles from the quenched (sub-leading) jet, which is qualitatively consistent with CMS Collaboration observations at LHC, but the result is a qualitative, not quantitative, match.

## Method
The authors constructed a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic model with source terms representing energy and momentum deposited by traversing jets, assuming instantaneous thermalization and collisional energy loss only. They solved the non-linear hydrodynamic equations numerically without linearization, using a massless ideal gas equation of state (P = e/3). Particle distributions were calculated via the Cooper-Frye formula on an isochronous hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density of the uniform fluid: 0.019 GeV⁴.
- For the expanding fluid case, initial temperature T₀ = 0.5 GeV, Gaussian width σ = 1.5 fm, and radius R = 6 fm.
- A pair of jets is created at (x, y, z) = (3 fm, 0 fm, 0 fm) with common initial energy 150 GeV.
- Momentum regions defined as: "high-p" (p > 8 GeV), "mid-p" (4 GeV < p < 8 GeV), and "low-p" (0 GeV < p < 4 GeV).
- At large angles from the sub-leading jet (cosθ > -0.94), low momentum particles are dominant.

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of jet-medium interactions beyond linearized treatments, following earlier studies of jet quenching (Bjorken 1982, Gyulassy 1990, Gyulassy 1993) and the CMS observation of low-momentum hadrons at large angles from a quenched jet (Chatrchyan 2011). The results demonstrate that medium responses to jet propagation are important for understanding modifications of jet structure, and the model enables future quantitative comparisons with LHC data.
