# Spontaneously broken color

> The vacuum of QCD is characterized by the Higgs mechanism. Color is ``spontaneously broken'' by a quark-antiquark condensate in the octet representation. The massive gluons carry integer electric charges and are identified with the vector mesons. The fermionic excitations consist of the low mass baryon octet and a singlet. The interactions between these particles and the light pseudoscalar octet are largely determined by chiral symmetry and a nonlinear local symmetry. A consistent phenomenological picture of strong interactions at long distances arises from a simple effective action.

## Key claim

The QCD vacuum can be described by a Higgs mechanism where color is spontaneously broken by a quark-antiquark condensate in the octet representation, with the caveat that local symmetries cannot be spontaneously broken in the vacuum, so the confinement and Higgs descriptions are complementary facets of the same physical state rather than mutually exclusive phases.

## Method

The author constructs an effective action for long-distance QCD by adding scalar fields representing quark-antiquark bound states (color singlet and color octet) to the quark and gluon fields, keeping only terms with mass dimension four or less. The effective action contains seven real parameters (g, h, tilde h, sigma_0, chi_0, nu, nu') plus explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms from current quark masses, and the author performs a phenomenological analysis treating these couplings as free parameters rather than computing them from QCD.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposals (Wetterich, 1999, 2000) that color symmetry is spontaneously broken by a dynamical quark-antiquark condensate, and draws on the concept of complementarity between Higgs and confinement descriptions established by Banks and others. The approach differs from previous treatments of chiral symmetry breaking by assuming that both color singlet and octet composite fields acquire vacuum expectation values, and it offers analogies to color-flavor locking at high baryon density proposed for neutron star interiors, though with a different pattern of symmetry breaking (quark-antiquark vs. quark-quark condensate).
