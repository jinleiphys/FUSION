# Antibound and Resonant States in Halo Nuclei

> An unified shell model scheme to evaluate simultaneously the contribution of antibound states and Gamow resonances to the nuclear halos is presented. The calculations, performed in the complex energy plane, are applied to the case of $^{11}$Li. It is found that $^{11}$Li may develop a resonant state excitation near the breakup threshold.

# Antibound and Resonant States in Halo Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors present a unified shell model formalism in the complex energy plane that treats antibound states on the same footing as bound states and Gamow resonances, and apply it to $^{11}$Li. They find that $^{11}$Li *may* develop a low-lying resonant state excitation near the breakup threshold, but caution that the structure of this excited state changes drastically when the interaction strength approaches the value used for the ground state, making it difficult to conclude whether this state is a physical resonance or not.

## Method
The authors extend the complex-energy shell model (CXSM) to include antibound states as discrete poles of the single-particle Green function, alongside Gamow resonances and bound states, within a Berggren space. They construct a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential for $^{10}$Li, identify the relevant poles (including the $1s_{1/2}$ antibound state at -0.050 MeV and the $0p_{1/2}$ resonance), and then use a separable two-body interaction with strength adjusted to reproduce the $^{11}$Li ground state energy of -295 keV. Two-particle resonant states are found by solving the dispersion relation in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers
- The $1s_{1/2}$ antibound state in $^{10}$Li is found at -0.050 MeV.
- The $0p_{1/2}$ resonance in $^{10}$Li is studied at two energies: (0.195, -0.047) MeV (200 keV case) and (0.470, -0.197) MeV (500 keV case).
- The $^{11}$Li ground state energy is -0.295 MeV, reproduced with interaction strength G = 0.00194 MeV (200 keV case) or G = 0.00694 MeV (500 keV case).
- For the 200 keV case, the ground state wave function of $^{11}$Li consists of 44% $s$-states, 48% $p$-states, and 8% $d$-states.
- For the 500 keV case, the ground state wave function consists of 49% $s$-states, 39% $p$-states, and 12% $d$-states.
- The first excited $0^+$ state in $^{11}$Li (200 keV case) appears at complex energy (0.202, -0.137) MeV.
- For the $^{72}$Ca candidate system, the antibound $2s_{1/2}$ state appears at -0.050 MeV, and a low-lying two-particle resonance is found at about (0.550, -0.350) MeV with interaction strength G = 0.00174 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the complex-energy shell model (CXSM) developed in earlier work (Refs. cited as "rsm" and "cxsm") by incorporating antibound states directly into the single-particle basis, whereas previous studies of $^{11}$Li had only treated the antibound $s$-state indirectly through the scattering length. The paper addresses the suggestion by Zhukov (1994) that a low-lying narrow resonance around 0.2-0.4 MeV in $^{11}$Li would be consistent with fragment momentum distributions, and provides the first microscopic calculation of such a state. The results enable future experimental searches for low-lying resonant excitations in $^{11}$Li and suggest that other neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, such as $^{72}$Ca, may also exhibit halo structure with antibound states and low-lying resonances.
