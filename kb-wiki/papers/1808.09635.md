# Two-neutron correlations in a Borromean $^{20}{\rm C}+n+n$ system: Sensitivity of unbound subsystems

> The structure of $^{22}$C plays a vital role in the new physics at subshell closure of $N=16$ in the neutron-rich region. We study the two-neutron correlations in the ground state of the weakly-bound Borromean nucleus $^{22}$C sitting at the edge of the neutron-drip line and its sensitivity to ${\rm core}$-$n$ potential. For the present study, we employ a three-body (${\rm core}+n+n$) structure model designed for describing the Borromean system by explicit coupling of unbound continuum states of the subsystem (${\rm core}+n$). We use a density-independent contact-delta interaction to describe the neutron-neutron interaction and its strength is varied to fix the binding energy. Along with the ground-state properties of $^{22}$C, we investigate its electric-dipole and monopole responses, discussing the contribution of various configurations. Our results indicate more configuration mixing as compared to the previous studies in the ground state of $^{22}$C. However, they strongly depend upon the choice of the $^{20}{\rm C}$-$n$ potential as well as the binding energy of $^{22}$C, which call for new precise measurements for the low-lying continuum structure of the binary system ($^{20}{\rm C}+n$) and the mass of $^{22}$C. These measurements will be essential to understand the Borromean three-body system $^{22}$C with more accuracy.

## Key claim
The ground-state configuration mixing in the Borromean nucleus 22C is more significant than previously reported, but the results are strongly sensitive to both the choice of the 20C-n potential and the binding energy of 22C, requiring new precise measurements of the 21C continuum structure and the 22C mass.

## Method
The authors employ a three-body (core+n+n) structure model that explicitly couples unbound continuum states of the 20C+n subsystem (21C). Single-particle continuum wave functions for s-, p-, d-, and f-waves are calculated on a radial grid from 0.1 to 100.0 fm using four different sets of core-n potentials. A density-independent contact-delta pairing interaction is used for the neutron-neutron interaction, with its strength varied to reproduce the ground-state energy of 22C, and the resulting pairing matrix is diagonalized to obtain the ground-state wave function.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of 22C fixed at -0.140 MeV (shallow case) and -0.500 MeV (deep case)
- For the shallow case with Set 1, the s-wave component is 0.923 (92.3%); for Set 4, it drops to 0.553 (55.3%)
- For the deep case with Set 4, the f-wave component (0.475) exceeds the s-wave component (0.358), showing an inversion
- Matter radius for the shallow case ranges from 3.31 fm (Set 3) to 3.51 fm (Set 4), compared to the experimental value of 3.44 ± 0.08 fm from Togano (2016)
- For the deep case, matter radius ranges from 3.14 fm (Set 2) to 3.32 fm (Set 4)
- The d3/2 resonance in 21C is placed at 0.83 MeV with decay width 0.09 MeV (Set 2) and at 0.80 MeV with decay width 0.08 MeV (Set 4)
- Scattering length for s-wave is -2.8 fm for Sets 1, 2, and 4; -47.6 fm for Set 3

## Context
This work extends previous three-body calculations of 22C by Horiuchi (2006), Ershov (2012), and Pineda (2016), but focuses on the previously underexplored sensitivity of configuration mixing to the choice of core-n potential. The authors find that using a single potential depth for all l>0 waves (Set 4) produces strong f-wave mixing, in contrast to earlier studies that found s-wave dominance. The results call for new measurements of the 21C low-lying continuum structure and the 22C mass to resolve the uncertainties, and the approach is validated against the known Borromean system 6He before being applied to the heavier 22C.
