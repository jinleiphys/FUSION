# Summary of theoretical contributions

> Results from various theoretical approaches and ideas presented at this exciting meeting are reviewed. I also point towards future directions, in particular hydrodynamic behaviour induced by jets traveling through the quark-gluon plasma, which might be worth looking at in more detail.

# Summary of theoretical contributions

## Key claim
This conference summary reviews approximately 30 theory talks and argues that Mach shock waves induced by jets traveling through the quark-gluon plasma can provide a direct measurement of the speed of sound in the medium, but the author notes that this interpretation remains qualitative and that the reasons why hydrodynamics fits both radial and elliptic flow for many hadron species are "still not fully settled."

## Method
The author synthesizes presentations from the conference on topics including the QCD phase diagram, lattice QCD results, color glass condensate, fluctuations, strangeness production, and collective dynamics. A detailed analytical derivation is given for Mach cone angles in the presence of collective flow, showing how the Mach angle in the center-of-mass frame depends on sound velocity and flow velocity via Lorentz transformations.

## Key numbers
- Critical endpoint from Swansea-Bielefeld and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations: μ_B ≈ 400 MeV, T ≈ 160 MeV
- Gavai and friends' alternative critical endpoint: T = 0.95 T_c, μ_B/T = 1.1-1.3 (μ_B ≈ 190-220 MeV)
- Speed of sound from Gavai: c_s² = 0.15 at T = 1.1 T_c, compared to noninteracting gas value c_s² = 1/3
- Bombarding energies E_LAB < 40 A·GeV needed to probe a first order phase transition according to UrQMD calculations
- Thermal width of J/Ψ: ~1 GeV at T = 600 MeV
- Characteristic equilibration time: τ_eq ∼ T^{-60}
- Mach angle for ultrarelativistic jet in static QGP: Δθ ≈ 0.96 rad = 61°
- Sound velocity for non-interacting massless plasma: c_s ≈ c/√3 ≈ 57% c
- Hadronic rescattering models explain < 30% of observed elliptic flow v₂ for p_T > 2 GeV/c
- Initial pressure at RHIC: p > 1 GeV/fm³
- Away-side jet suppression due to pre-hadronic or hadronic rescattering: < 50%

## Context
This summary extends the line of work on Mach shock waves in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier studies of Mach shocks in cold hadronic matter (Hofmann 1974, Stoecker 1980) and nuclear Fermi liquids (Glas 1959, Kho 1980). The author connects the recent observation of away-side jet suppression by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations to predictions of Mach cone formation (Stoecker 2004), and proposes that precise measurements of emission angles can distinguish between weakly coupled QGP (c_s² = 1/3), hadronic matter (c_s² = 1/5), and strongly coupled QGP (c_s² = 2/3). The collapse of proton flow v₁ and v₂ observed by NA49 at 40 A·GeV is presented as first evidence for a first order phase transition in baryon-rich matter, which could be further studied at RHIC's fragmentation region and at the future FAIR facility.
