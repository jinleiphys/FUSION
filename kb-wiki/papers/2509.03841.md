# Exploring the fluid behavior in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13 \mathrm{TeV}$ with viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics

> The applicability of hydrodynamics in small collision systems remains controversial due to the small size and short lifetime of the system. In this letter, we employ viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (VAH), which incorporates large pressure anisotropies, to study the collectivity in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13 \mathrm{TeV}$.VAH provides a good description for $v_{2}\{2\}$ and $v_{3}\{2\}$ over a wide range of multiplicities and correctly reproduces the experimentally observed negative $c_{2}\{4\}$. Traditional second-order viscous hydrodynamics (VH), on the other hand, can describe the measurements, in particular the negative $c_{2}\{4\}$, only with model parameters for which the bulk of the evolution is characterized by large values of the shear Knudsen number. It also can not capture the large longitudinal/transverse pressure anisotropy during the early evolution. These demonstrate the failure of traditional viscous hydrodynamics in small collision systems and establish viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics as a more reliable framework to describe the bulk evolution and the observed anisotropic flow in p-p collisions at the LHC.

# Exploring the fluid behavior in p+p collisions at √s = 13 TeV with viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (VAH) can describe the measured anisotropic flow coefficients v₂{2} and v₃{2} and reproduce the negative sign of the four-particle cumulant c₂{4} in high-multiplicity p+p collisions at √s = 13 TeV, whereas traditional second-order viscous hydrodynamics (VH) can only achieve this by operating in regimes with large Knudsen numbers (⟨Knθ,π⟩ > 1) where its results are unreliable and strongly dependent on numerical regulation schemes.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional event-by-event hybrid simulations coupling a fluid dynamical description of the quark-gluon plasma (using either VAH or VH) with the SMASH hadronic cascade for late-stage rescattering and freeze-out, starting from initial conditions generated by the parameterized 3D TRENTo model with sub-nucleon fluctuations. For VAH, the energy-momentum tensor is decomposed into longitudinal pressure P_L and transverse pressure P_⊥ which evolve independently, with the anisotropic particle distribution determined through generalized Landau matching; for VH, standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics is used. Both models employ the same temperature-dependent parametrizations for specific shear viscosity η/s and bulk viscosity ζ/s, with the hydrodynamic evolution starting at τ₀ = 0.12 fm/c for VAH and τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c for VH, and particlization at T_sw = 146 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The VAH simulation (para I) uses (η/s)_min = 0.08, with initial pressure anisotropy (P_L/P_⊥)₀ = 0.4 and starting proper time τ₀ = 0.12 fm/c
- The VH simulation (para IV) uses (η/s)_min = 0.08, with starting proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- VAH correctly reproduces negative c₂{4} in the multiplicity range 3 < N_ch/⟨N_ch⟩ < 8, with magnitude roughly agreeing with ATLAS two-subevent data
- For VH simulations that fit v₂{2} and v₃{2} and produce negative c₂{4}, the average Knudsen number ⟨Knθ,π⟩ exceeds 1 during early time evolution
- The particlization temperature is set to T_sw = 146 MeV for both VAH and VH
- The kinematic cuts used for model predictions are |η| < 2.0 and 0.2 < p_T < 3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to describe collectivity in small collision systems using traditional viscous hydrodynamics, which failed to reproduce the negative sign of c₂{4} in high-multiplicity p+p collisions, the so-called "sign puzzle" identified by ALICE (2014), CMS (2015), and ATLAS (2013). Prior studies using HIJING, AMPT, TRENTo, or IP-Glasma initial conditions (Zhao 2017, Zhao 2020, Schenke 2019) all failed to produce negative c₂{4}. The VAH approach builds on earlier developments by Bazow (2013, 2015), Tinti (2015), Molnar (2016), and McNelis (2018, 2021) for treating large pressure anisotropies non-perturbatively. This work enables quantitative hydrodynamic analysis of flow and collectivity in p+p and other small collision systems at the LHC, where traditional viscous hydrodynamics is unreliable.
