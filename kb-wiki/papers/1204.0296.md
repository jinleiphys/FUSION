# Scalar and Pseudoscalar Glueball Masses within a Gaussian Wavefunctional Approximation

> The lowest scalar and pseudoscalar glueball masses are evaluated by means of the time-dependent variational approach to the Yang-Mills gauge theory without fermions in the Hamiltonian formalism within a Gaussian wavefunctional approximation. The glueball mass is calculated as a pole of the propagator for a composite glueball field which consists of two massless gluons. The glueball propagator is here evaluated by using the linear response theory for the composite external glueball field. As a result, a finite glueball mass is obtained through the interaction between two massless gluons, in which the glueball mass depends on the QCD coupling constant g in the nonperturbative form.

## Key claim

The scalar and pseudoscalar glueball masses are obtained as finite values through the interaction between two massless gluons within a Gaussian wavefunctional approximation, with the masses depending on the QCD coupling constant \(g\) in a nonperturbative form; the calculation uses the pole of the propagator for a composite glueball field evaluated via linear response theory, and the results are compared with lattice QCD simulations.

## Method

The author applies a time-dependent variational approach to pure Yang-Mills gauge theory in the Hamiltonian formalism, using a Gaussian wavefunctional as a trial state that includes a mean field and quantum fluctuations. The glueball propagator is evaluated via linear response theory for a composite external glueball field consisting of two massless gluons, and the glueball mass is extracted as the pole of this propagator. The scalar (\(0^{+}\)) and pseudoscalar (\(0^{-}\)) glueball masses are calculated in the modified minimal subtraction scheme, with the gluon mass verified to be zero under the approximation used.

## Key numbers

- The scalar glueball mass (\(0^{+}\)) is obtained as a finite value depending on the QCD coupling constant \(g\) in a nonperturbative form; the specific numerical result is not given in the truncated text.
- The pseudoscalar glueball mass (\(0^{-}\)) is also obtained as a finite value with the same dependence on \(g\); no explicit numerical value is provided in the truncated text.
- The gluon mass is verified to be zero under the Gaussian wavefunctional approximation (Appendix C).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent variational method previously formulated for Yang-Mills theory in the author's earlier paper (Tsue, 2012, referred to as (I)), and applies the linear response theory approach used in the linear sigma model by Tsue and Matsuda (2012) to calculate glueball masses. The results are intended to be compared with lattice QCD simulations of glueball masses, which have provided estimates for states with certain spins and parities, and with other theoretical approaches such as the bag model, flux tube model, and QCD sum rules.
