# Two-gluon rapidity correlations of strong colour field in $pp$, $pA$ and $AA$ collisions

> Using the CGC formalism, we calculate the two-gluon rapidity correlations of strong colour fields in $pp$, $pA$ and $AA$ collisions, respectively. If one trigger gluon is fixed at central rapidity, a ridge-like correlation pattern is obtained in symmetry $pp$ and $AA$ collisions, and a huge bump-like correlation pattern is presented in asymmetry $pA$ collisions. It is demonstrated that the ridge-like correlation patterns are caused by the stronger correlation with the gluon of colour source. The transverse momentum and incident energy dependence of the ridge-like correlation pattern is also systematically studied. The ridge is more likely observed at higher incident energy and lower transverse momentum of trigger gluon.

## Key claim

Using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, the authors calculate two-gluon rapidity correlations and find that ridge-like patterns in symmetric $pp$ and $AA$ collisions, and bump-like patterns in asymmetric $pA$ collisions, are caused by stronger correlation with the gluon of colour source at large-$x$ degrees of freedom, rather than by small-$x$ Glasma flux tube dynamics alone.

## Method

The authors compute two-gluon and single-gluon distributions using the CGC effective field theory at leading logarithmic accuracy in $x$, with the unintegrated gluon distribution (uGD) obtained from the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution equation using the AAMQS parametrization. The correlation function $C(\mathbf{p}_\perp, y_p; \mathbf{q}_\perp, y_q)$ is evaluated for minimum bias samples of $pp$, $pA$, and $AA$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV, with the trigger gluon fixed at central rapidity $y_p=0$ and the transverse momenta chosen near the sum of saturation scales of the colliding particles.

## Key numbers

- Initial saturation scale for proton: $Q^2_{s0p}=0.168\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ for minimum bias samples.
- Initial saturation scale for nucleus: $Q^2_{s0A}=0.504\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ (three times that of the proton) for minimum bias samples.
- In central rapidity region at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV, the maximum correlation for $pp$ collisions appears at $p_\perp \sim 2Q_{sp}=1.6$ GeV; for $AA$ collisions at $p_\perp \sim 2Q_{sA}=2.8$ GeV; for $pA$ collisions at $p_\perp = Q_{sp}+Q_{sA} \sim 2.2$ GeV.
- Infrared scale in AAMQS parametrization: $\Lambda=0.241\ \mathrm{GeV}$; anomalous dimension $\gamma=1.119$ for both proton and nucleus.
- Parameter $\beta=4$ used for large-$x$ ($x>10^{-2}$) extrapolation of uGD.

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based phenomenological studies of ridge-like dihadron correlations observed at RHIC and LHC (STAR 2005, PHENIX 2008, CMS 2010, ALICE 2013) by focusing on gluon-level correlations rather than hadron-level observables. It builds on the formalism of Dumitru (2008), Dusling (2010), and Gelis (2009) for two-gluon correlations, and clarifies that the ridge originates from correlations with colour source fields at large $x$ rather than from small-$x$ Glasma flux tube fluctuations alone. The results enable systematic study of how the ridge pattern depends on transverse momentum and incident energy, and show that the ridge should be more easily observed in $pp$ collisions than in $AA$ collisions at the same energy.
