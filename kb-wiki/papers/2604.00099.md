# Light-by-light scattering: asymptotic expansions, Coulomb resummation and NLO corrections

> Light-by-light (LbL) scattering is one of the earliest predictions of quantum electrodynamics (QED). Interest in this process has been renewed following its experimental observation at the LHC and the prospects of future measurements at free-electron laser facilities. In this paper, we refine theoretical predictions for LbL scattering by improving the full fermion-mass-dependent two-loop QCD and QED helicity amplitudes using high- and low-energy asymptotic expansions, and by performing Coulomb resummation in the threshold region. We present state-of-the-art predictions for LbL cross sections in the Standard Model and provide a new event generator, LbLatNLO, for Monte Carlo simulations of LbL scattering.

## Key claim
The paper refines theoretical predictions for light-by-light scattering by improving the full fermion-mass-dependent two-loop QCD and QED helicity amplitudes using high- and low-energy asymptotic expansions and by performing Coulomb resummation in the threshold region, providing state-of-the-art Standard Model cross sections and a new event generator for Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method
The authors derived analytic low-energy and high-energy asymptotic expansions of the one- and two-loop helicity amplitudes for $\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma$ using the expansion-by-region method and tensor decomposition of two-loop vacuum integrals. They performed Coulomb resummation of gluon and photon exchanges to cure logarithmic Coulomb divergences appearing in the two-loop amplitudes at threshold. The results were implemented in a new event generator, LbLatNLO, for Monte Carlo simulations of light-by-light scattering.

## Key numbers
- The cross section in PbPb ultraperipheral collisions scales as $Z^4 \approx 4.5 \times 10^7$ relative to proton-proton or lepton-lepton collisions
- The leading-order contribution to the cross section is $\mathcal{O}(\alpha^4)$ from one-loop Feynman diagrams
- The low-energy expansion of the one-loop all-plus helicity amplitude for a fermion $f$ begins as $-i\mathcal{M}_{++++}^{(0,0,f),\mathrm{LE}} = N_{c,f}Q_f^4\alpha^2[-\frac{1}{15}(x_s^2+x_t^2+x_u^2) + \mathcal{O}(x_s^4)]$ where $x_s = s/m_f^2$
- The two-loop low-energy expansion of the all-plus amplitude for a fermion $f$ begins as $i\mathcal{M}_{++++}^{(1,0,f),\mathrm{LE}} = N_{c,f}Q_f^4\alpha^2\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}C_{F,f}[\frac{5}{12}(x_s^2+x_t^2+x_u^2) + \mathcal{O}(x_s^4)]$
- The two-loop vacuum master integrals are $I_{1,1,0}^{V,(2)} = m_f^4 e^{2\epsilon\gamma_E}(\Gamma(-1+\epsilon))^2$ and $I_{1,1,1}^{V,(2)} = m_f^2 e^{2\epsilon\gamma_E}2^{1-2\epsilon}\sqrt{\pi}\frac{\Gamma(-1+\epsilon)\Gamma(-1+2\epsilon)}{\Gamma(\frac{1}{2}+\epsilon)}$

## Context
This work extends the complete two-loop QCD and QED corrections with full fermion-mass dependence recently computed by AH (2023) and provides analytic asymptotic expansions that improve numerical stability. The low-energy expansions are relevant for vacuum birefringence experiments such as the BIREF@HIBEF programme at the European XFEL and the PVLAS experiment, while the high-energy expansions are relevant for understanding subleading-power logarithmic structures in loop-induced processes studied by Kotsky (1997), Fadin (1997), and others. The new event generator LbLatNLO enables Monte Carlo simulations for future measurements at the LHC and free-electron laser facilities.
