# Semiclassical study on Proton and Neutron

> Starting from the existing semiclassical studies on hydrogenoid atoms, we propose a similar intuitive exercise for the three-body quark systems corresponding to protons and neutrons. In the frame of this toy model we try to explain both the stabilities of proton and neutron with respect to the nuclear interaction, and the spectrum of nucleonic resonances with J=1/2. Our choice is motivated also by a good agreement obtained for the up and down quark rest masses report. Taking into account the deterministic chaotic behavior of many-body systems, the discussed exercise could be understood as an interesting particular case of a quantum three-body problem which admits a semiclassical treatment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a semiclassical toy model for the three-quark systems of protons and neutrons, claiming that the closing-trajectory condition for harmonic interactions can explain nucleon stability and the spectrum of nucleonic resonances with J=1/2, but only under the less restrictive condition that the Lissajous condition is satisfied exactly for the proton and only approximately for the neutron.

## Method

The authors model the proton (uud) and neutron (udd) as three-body systems with harmonic quark-quark interactions, assuming two constituents have equal mass. They decompose the motion into harmonic components with frequencies ω1 (common to all three quarks) and ω2 (additional non-parallel oscillation for the two equal-mass quarks). Using the Lissajous condition for closed trajectories and treating the system as a collection of five quantum harmonic oscillators (three with ω1, two with ω2), they set the ground-state total energy equal to the nucleon rest mass and solve for the frequencies. Excited states are then computed from integer multiples of these frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Up-to-down quark mass ratio r = mu/md = 13/24 = 0.541(6), which is close to the known theoretical value 0.56
- For the proton: ω1p ≈ 347 MeV, ω2p ≈ 417 MeV
- For the neutron: ω1n ≈ 408 MeV, ω2n ≈ 326 MeV
- All computed nucleonic resonance masses (except one) differ from known PDG values by less than 6.5%
- Predicted masses for a possible new nucleonic resonance with J=1/2: 2467 MeV (proton channel) and 2407 MeV (neutron channel)

## Context

This work extends semiclassical studies on hydrogenoid atoms (Bohr model) to the three-quark systems of nucleons, building on the Isgur-Karl quark model (Isgur, 1978-1985) and nuclear billiard models. The authors note that the deterministic chaotic behavior of many-body systems normally precludes semiclassical treatment, but the harmonic potential case is exactly solvable via Lissajous conditions. The model enables predictions of nucleonic resonance masses and suggests a possible new J=1/2 resonance, though it ignores spin and electric charge.
