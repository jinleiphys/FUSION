# Applicability of shape parameterizations for giant dipole resonance in warm and rapidly rotating nuclei

> We investigate how well the shape parameterizations are applicable for studying the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in nuclei, in the low temperature and/or high spin regime. The shape fluctuations due to thermal effects in the GDR observables are calculated using the actual free energies evaluated at fixed spin and temperature. The results obtained are compared with Landau theory calculations done by parameterizing the free energy. We exemplify that the Landau theory could be inadequate where shell effects are dominating. This discrepancy at low temperatures and high spins are well reflected in GDR observables and hence insists on exact calculations in such cases.

## Key claim

The paper argues that shape parameterizations such as Landau theory are inadequate for calculating giant dipole resonance (GDR) observables in nuclei when shell effects are strong, specifically at low temperatures (T < 1 MeV) and at moderate temperatures with high spin (T ~ 1 MeV, I > 30 hbar), and that exact calculations without parameter fitting are necessary in these regimes.

## Method

The authors performed exact thermal fluctuation calculations for GDR observables using Nilsson-Strutinsky free energies evaluated at fixed spin and temperature, integrating numerically over the deformation parameters beta and gamma without any parameterization. They compared these exact results with calculations using the Landau theory parameterization of the free energy and with liquid-drop model calculations, for nuclei including 208Pb, 179Au, 147Eu, and 84Zr at various temperatures and spins.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb at omega=0, strong shell corrections for spherical shape lead to GDR widths much lower than liquid-drop model results at low temperature
- For 179Au, ground state deformation calculated as beta_2 = -0.22, compared to beta_2 = -0.17 from relativistic mean field theory and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations
- For 147Eu at T=1.3 MeV, Landau theory and exact calculations show little difference, with widths similar to liquid-drop model results even at spins up to 60 hbar
- For 147Eu at T=0.5 MeV, shell correction is of order 2 MeV at omega=0, and at 40 hbar the equilibrium shape acquires deformation (beta,gamma) = (0.3, -180 degrees), changing to (0.3, -130 degrees) at 60 hbar
- For 84Zr at T=1.0 MeV and I=60 hbar, spin-driven shell effects play a vital role even at T ~ 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous exact thermal fluctuation calculations by Arumugam et al. (2004) and Ansari (2001) to test the validity of the Landau theory parameterization developed by Alhassid and collaborators. The authors show that while Landau theory works well for nuclei like 120Sn at high temperature (T=1.8 MeV) and for 179Au at T=0.7 MeV where spin-dependent shell effects are insignificant, it fails for nuclei like 147Eu at low temperature where spin-driven shell effects are crucial. The paper enables future exact calculations for GDR observables in regimes where shape parameterizations are unreliable, particularly at low temperatures and high spins where recent experiments by Heckman et al. (2003), Camera et al. (2003), and Bracco et al. (2003) have focused.
