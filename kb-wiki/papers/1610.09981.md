# Susceptibilities from a black hole engineered EoS with a critical point

> Currently at the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC experimental efforts are being made to find the QCD critical point. On the theoretical side, the behavior of higher-order susceptibilities of the net-baryon charge from Lattice QCD at $μ_{_B}\!=\!0$ may allow us to estimate the position of the critical point in the QCD phase diagram. However, even if the series expansion continues to higher-orders, there is always the possibility to miss the critical point behavior due to truncation errors. An alternative approach is to use a black hole engineered holographic model, which displays a critical point at large densities and matches lattice susceptibilities at $μ_{_B}\!=\!0$. Using the thermodynamic data from this black hole model, we obtain the freeze-out points extracted from the net-protons distribution measured at STAR and explore higher order fluctuations at the lowest energies at the beam energy scan to investigate signatures of the critical point.

# Susceptibilities from a black hole engineered EoS with a critical point

## Key claim
A black hole engineered holographic model that displays a critical point at large densities and matches lattice susceptibilities at zero baryon chemical potential can reproduce the freeze-out points extracted from net-proton distributions measured at STAR, but the model's critical point appears at much larger densities than the freeze-out values, so the observed increase in the chi_4/chi_2 ratio at low collision energies does not necessarily signal proximity to the critical point.

## Method
The authors used a revised black hole engineered holographic model whose parameters were tuned to match lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential, then extended to nonzero baryon chemical potential. They computed baryon number susceptibilities (chi_1 through chi_4) from the model as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential, and extracted freeze-out temperatures and chemical potentials by requiring that the model's chi_1/chi_2 and chi_3/chi_2 ratios match the corresponding net-proton moments measured by STAR at each collision energy from 7.7 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out points were extracted for collision energies sqrt(s) = 7.7 to 200 GeV by matching model chi_1/chi_2 and chi_3/chi_2 to STAR data from Adamczyk (2013).
- Susceptibility ratios were computed at mu_B = 0, 100, 200, 300, and 400 MeV (black, cyan, red, magenta, and blue lines respectively in the left panel of Figure 1).
- The chi_4/chi_2 ratio computed along the extracted freeze-out line increases with decreasing sqrt(s), even though the model's critical point is located at much larger densities than the freeze-out values.
- Preliminary STAR data with higher p_T cuts (0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV) from Luo (2015) show larger enhancement for chi_4/chi_2 at low sqrt(s).

## Context
This work extends the black hole engineered holographic approach of Gubser (2008), DeWolfe (2010), and DeWolfe (2011), which constructed gravitational duals with a critical point at large baryon densities. The model parameters were recently revised by Finazzo (2016) to better match current lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential. The results enable future comparisons with higher-statistics STAR data using different p_T cuts and accounting for acceptance cuts and decays, as well as providing a holographic benchmark for the freeze-out line that can be contrasted with lattice-based Taylor expansion approaches (Allton 2002, Gavai 2008) and analytic continuation methods (D'Elia 2002, de Forcrand 2002, Gunther 2016).
