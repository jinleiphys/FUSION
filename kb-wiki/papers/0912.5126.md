# Microscopic approach to the spectator matter fragmentation from 400 to 1000 AMeV

> A study of multifragmentation of gold nuclei is reported at incident energies of 400, 600 and 1000 MeV/nucleon using microscopic theory. The present calculations are done within the framework of quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model. The clusterization is performed with advanced sophisticated algorithm namely \emph{simulated annealing clusterization algorithm} (SACA) along with conventional spatial correlation method. A quantitative comparison of mean multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments with experimental findings of ALADiN group gives excellent agreement showing the ability of SACA method to reproduce the fragment yields. It also emphasizes the importance of clustering criterion in describing the fragmentation process within semi-classical model.

# Microscopic approach to the spectator matter fragmentation from 400 to 1000 AMeV

## Key claim
The quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model combined with the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA) can reproduce the universal, energy-invariant nature of spectator multifragmentation in gold-gold collisions across 400 to 1000 MeV/nucleon, but only when the SACA clustering criterion is used; the conventional minimum spanning tree (MST) method fails entirely, resolving a prior discrepancy where QMD models underestimated fragment yields at large impact parameters.

## Method
The authors simulated 197Au+197Au collisions at 400, 600, and 1000 MeV/nucleon using the QMD model with a soft equation of state and standard energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section. The phase space generated at each time step was clusterized using two methods: the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA), which searches for the most bound configuration via energy minimization with a Metropolis algorithm, and the conventional minimum spanning tree (MST) spatial correlation method with a 4 fm cutoff. Mean multiplicities of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) were computed as a function of impact parameter and compared to ALADiN experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Mean IMF multiplicity ⟨N_IMF⟩ was calculated for 197Au+197Au at incident energies of 400, 600, and 1000 MeV/nucleon and compared to ALADiN experimental data.
- The binding energy threshold used in SACA was E_bind = -4.0 MeV for fragments with N_f ≥ 3, and E_bind = 0 otherwise.
- The Gaussian width L in the QMD model was fixed at 1.08 fm.
- Average nuclear density reached its maximum around 25 fm/c for all three incident energies at an impact parameter of 6 fm.
- The density profile showed insignificant change when increasing incident energy by a factor of 2.5 (from 400 to 1000 MeV/nucleon).
- Fragment configuration after 60 fm/c was identified as the stage where fragments leave the reaction zone without further nucleon-nucleon correlations being destroyed.

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Puri and Kumar (2009) on the rise-and-fall of multiplicity in 40Ca+40Ca reactions and by the ALADiN collaboration (Schut, 1997; Tsang, 1995) which established the universal behavior of spectator fragmentation in 197Au+197Au from 150 to 600 MeV/nucleon. The SACA method had previously been tested against INDRA data at 50 MeV/nucleon for Xe+Sn (Hirsch, 2005) and against O+Ag/Br reactions from 25 to 200 AMeV (Jain, 2007), but this is the first demonstration that QMD+SACA reproduces the entire energy domain up to 1000 MeV/nucleon. The results enable future studies of spectator fragmentation at even higher bombarding energies and resolve the long-standing discrepancy where QMD models (Tsang, 1995; Blaich, 1995) underestimated fragment yields at large impact parameters.
