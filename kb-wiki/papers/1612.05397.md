# First estimation of the fission dynamics of the spectator created in heavy-ion collisions

> In peripheral high-energy heavy-ion collisions only parts of colliding nuclei interact leading to the production of a fireball. The remnants of such nuclei are called spectators. We estimated the excitation energy as a function of impact parameter. Their excitation energy is of the order of 100 MeV. The dynamical evolution of hot nuclei is described by solving a set of Langevin equations in four-dimensional collective coordinate space. The range of nuclear masses and excitation energies suits very well to ability of our model. Thus for the first time we investigate dynamically the fission and evaporation channels in de-excitation of the spectators produced in heavy-ions collision.

### Key claim
The paper presents the first dynamical investigation of fission and evaporation channels in the de-excitation of spectator nuclei produced in peripheral high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but the results are explicitly preliminary and rely on a rough assumption that the time to change from the initial exotic deformation to a spherical shape is small compared to the rest of the dynamics.

### Method
The excitation energy of spectator remnants from Pb+Pb collisions was estimated as a function of impact parameter using three geometric scenarios ("sphere-plane", "sphere-sphere", "sphere-cylinder") to compute deformation energy via the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop formula. The dynamical evolution of five selected hot nuclei (⁶⁴Mn, ¹⁰⁰Y, ¹⁴²Ba, ¹⁶⁸Dy, ¹⁹⁴Os) was then simulated by solving four-dimensional Langevin equations in collective coordinate space (elongation, neck, octupole shape, and K-projection of angular momentum), with transport coefficients derived from hydrodynamical friction and Gaussian white noise.

### Key numbers
- Excitation energies of spectators are of the order of 100 MeV (from abstract).
- The highest deformation energies reach almost 500 MeV for the "sphere-sphere" grazing scenario (from Fig. 1 description).
- The 4D Langevin method is applied for excitation energies E* = 50–250 MeV.
- Five nuclei were studied: ⁶⁴Mn, ¹⁰⁰Y, ¹⁴²Ba, ¹⁶⁸Dy, and ¹⁹⁴Os.
- The maximal angular momentum was taken as 2 ħ to avoid technical problems.
- Approximately 300,000 trajectories were calculated for each nucleus.

### Context
This work extends the stochastic approach to fission dynamics (Kramers 1940, Krappe 1995, Abe 1996, Frobrich 1998) to the novel application of spectator remnants in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, which have previously been studied mainly in the context of fireball and quark-gluon plasma physics (Teaney 2001, Florkowski 2010). The results are preliminary and enable future comparisons with new detection techniques (Staszewski 2016) that could identify fission/evaporation reactions in spectators, but the authors note that no direct measurements currently exist to constrain the spectator evolution.
