# Pseudoscalar transition form factors: $(g-2)$ of the muon, pseudoscalar decays into lepton pairs, and the $η-η'$ mixing

> We present our model-independent and data-driven method to describe pseudoscalar meson transition form factors in the space- and (low-energy) time-like regions. The method is general and conforms a toolkit applicable to any other form factor, of one and two variables, with the potential to include both high- and low-energy QCD constraints altogether. The method makes use of analyticity and unitary properties of form factors, it is simple, systematic and can be improved upon by including new data. In the present discussion, the method is used to show the impact of experimental data for precision calculations in the low-energy sector of the Standard Model. In particular, due to its relevance for New Physics searches, we have considered the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon (the pseudoscalar exchange contribution), the pseudoscalar decays into lepton pairs, and the determination of the mixing parameters of the $η$ and $η'$ system. For all of them we provide the most updated results in a data-driven manner.

# Pseudoscalar transition form factors: (g-2) of the muon, pseudoscalar decays into lepton pairs, and the η-η' mixing

## Key claim

The authors present a model-independent, data-driven method based on Padé approximant theory to describe pseudoscalar meson transition form factors, claiming it provides the most robust calculation of the η and η' contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering to date, though the results for the pseudoscalar pole contribution to the muon (g-2) span a range of (7.33 to 9.94) × 10^{-10} depending on the unknown doubly-virtual slope parameter a_{1,1}, which cannot be determined from existing experimental data.

## Method

The method uses sequences of Padé approximants (P^L_1 and P^N_N) fitted to space-like transition form factor data from CELLO, CLEO, BABAR, and BELLE collaborations to extract the low-energy Taylor expansion coefficients (slope b_P and curvature c_P) for π^0, η, and η' mesons. For the doubly-virtual form factor needed in hadronic light-by-light and lepton-pair decay calculations, the method extends to Canterbury approximants, which incorporate both the low-energy expansion and high-energy perturbative QCD constraints, with the unknown doubly-virtual slope a_{1,1} varied between the factorization limit (a_{1,1} = b_P^2) and the operator product expansion limit (a_{1,1} = 2b_P^2).

## Key numbers

- π^0 slope: b_π = 0.0324(22); curvature: c_π = 1.06(27) × 10^{-3}
- η slope: b_η = 0.60(7); curvature: c_η = 0.37(12); asymptotic limit: 0.160(24) GeV
- η' slope: b_η' = 1.30(17); curvature: c_η' = 1.72(58); asymptotic limit: 0.255(4) GeV
- Pseudoscalar pole contribution to muon (g-2): total range (7.33 to 9.94) × 10^{-10} depending on a_{1,1} choice, with individual contributions: π^0 (5.10 to 6.64) × 10^{-10}, η (1.16 to 1.69) × 10^{-10}, η' (1.07 to 1.61) × 10^{-10}
- Predicted branching ratio for π^0 → e^+e^-: (6.20 to 6.35) × 10^{-8}, compared to experimental value 7.48(38) × 10^{-8} from KTeV
- Predicted branching ratio for η → μ^+μ^-: (4.52 to 4.72) × 10^{-6}, compared to experimental value 5.8(8) × 10^{-6}
- Predicted branching ratio for η' → μ^+μ^-: (1.37 to 1.49) × 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Padé approximant methodology (Masjuan 2012, Escribano and Masjuan) for single-virtual transition form factors to the doubly-virtual case using Canterbury approximants, enabling data-driven calculations of hadronic light-by-light scattering and pseudoscalar decays into lepton pairs. The method contrasts with model-dependent approaches such as Vector Meson Dominance, Lowest Meson Dominance, and holographic QCD models, and provides a systematic way to include both experimental data and high-energy QCD constraints. The results enable improved precision for Standard Model predictions relevant to New Physics searches, particularly for the muon (g-2) where the new Fermilab experiment aims for 10% uncertainty, and for resolving the 2.6-1.4 sigma discrepancy between the predicted and measured π^0 → e^+e^- branching ratio.
