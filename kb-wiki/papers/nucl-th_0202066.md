# Evaluation of QCD sum rules for HADES

> QCD sum rules are evaluated at finite nucleon densities and temperatures to determine the change of pole mass parameters for the lightest vector mesons $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ in a strongly interacting medium at conditions relevant for the starting experiments at HADES. The role of the four-quark condensate is highlighted. A few estimates (within a fire ball model and BUU calculations) of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions at 1 AGeV are presented.

## Key claim

The pole mass parameters of the light vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) in a strongly interacting medium at finite nucleon density and temperature are evaluated using QCD sum rules, but the actual mass shifts depend critically on the poorly known density dependence of the four-quark condensate, parameterized by the factor κ_N; for the ω meson, even the qualitative sign of the mass shift remains unsettled.

## Method

The retarded current-current correlation function for vector mesons at rest was evaluated using the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) at large spacelike momentum, truncated at mass dimension 6, and matched via a Borel-transformed dispersion relation to a hadronic spectral ansatz consisting of a single delta-function pole, a continuum threshold, and Landau damping terms from nucleon and pion scattering. The four-quark condensate was treated with an extended parameterization that includes a linear density dependence beyond the ground-state saturation assumption, introducing the parameter κ_N. The pole mass parameter m_V was then extracted by requiring maximum flatness of the Borel curve within a window determined by the "10% + 50%" rule.

## Key numbers

* The chiral condensate in vacuum is taken as ⟨ūu⟩₀ = (−0.245 GeV)³, and the strange condensate as ⟨s̄s⟩₀ = 0.8 ⟨ūu⟩₀.
* The gluon condensate in vacuum is ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩₀ = (0.33 GeV)⁴.
* The vacuum four-quark condensate parameter κ₀ is set to 2.36 (following Klingl, Weise, Kaiser) or 3.3 (following Hatsuda, Lee, Shiomi); the density-dependent parameter κ_N is varied between 0 and 4.
* For the ρ meson, a maximum mass shift of Δm_ρ = 300 MeV is considered in the fire ball model.
* For the ω meson in the BUU calculations, a density-dependent mass shift δm_ω = 70 MeV and width change δΓ_ω = 50 MeV are assumed.
* The initial fire ball density is n_i = 3 n₀, with initial temperature T_i = 90 MeV and freeze-out at n_f = 0.3 n₀, T_f = 50 MeV.
* The vector meson broadening factor used in the dilepton spectra is b = 3.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (1991) to finite baryon density and temperature simultaneously, specifically for conditions relevant to the HADES experiment at 1 AGeV. It builds on prior sum rule studies by Hatsuda (1993) and Klingl, Weise, and Kaiser (1997) but goes beyond them by parameterizing the density dependence of the four-quark condensate and by including both hydrodynamical (fire ball model) and transport (BUU) calculations of dilepton spectra. The results enable experimental strategies for HADES to extract in-medium vector meson properties, particularly by measuring double-differential dilepton spectra in selected transverse mass bins to isolate the ρ peak from the large background.
