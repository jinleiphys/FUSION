# Density-dependent NN-interaction from subsubleading chiral 3N-force: Intermediate-range contributions

> From the subsubleading chiral three-nucleon force [intermediate-range contributions, published in Phys. Rev. C\,87, 054007 (2013)] a density-dependent NN-interaction $V_\text{med}$ is derived in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. Following the division of the pertinent 3N-diagrams into two-pion-one-pion exchange topology and ring topology, one evaluates for these all selfclosings and concatenations of nucleon-lines to an in-medium loop. In the case of the $2π1π$-exchange topology, the momentum- and $k_f$-dependent potentials associated with the isospin-operators ($1$ and $\vecτ_1 \!\cdot\! \vecτ_2$) and five independent spin-structures require at most one numerical integration. For the more challenging (concatenations of the) ring diagrams proportional to $c_{1,2,3,4}$, one ends up with regularized double-integrals $\int_0^λdr\,r \int_0^{π/2} dψ$ from which the $λ^2$-divergence has been subtracted and the logarithmic piece $\sim \ln (m_π/λ)$ is isolated. The derived semi-analytical results are most helpful to implement the subsubleading chiral 3N-forces into nuclear many-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives semi-analytical expressions for a density-dependent two-nucleon interaction \(V_\text{med}\) from the subsubleading (N\(^4\)LO) chiral three-nucleon force of intermediate range, but the results are limited to isospin-symmetric spin-saturated nuclear matter and require eventual partial-wave projection before implementation in many-body calculations.

## Method

The author divides the pertinent 3N diagrams into two-pion-one-pion (\(2\pi1\pi\)) exchange topology and ring topology, then evaluates all selfclosings and concatenations of nucleon lines to an in-medium loop. For the \(2\pi1\pi\)-exchange topology, the momentum- and Fermi-momentum-dependent potentials associated with isospin operators (1 and \(\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2\)) and five independent spin structures require at most one numerical integration. For the ring diagrams proportional to \(c_{1,2,3,4}\), the calculations yield regularized double-integrals \(\int_0^\lambda dr\,r \int_0^{\pi/2} d\psi\) from which the \(\lambda^2\)-divergence is subtracted and the logarithmic piece \(\sim \ln(m_\pi/\lambda)\) is isolated.

## Key numbers

* The selfclosing contribution from the \(2\pi1\pi\)-exchange topology gives a \(1\pi\)-exchange-like potential with a factor linear in density: \(V_\text{med}^{(0)} = \frac{g_A^4 m_\pi^2 k_f^3}{24 \pi^4 f_\pi^6} \frac{\vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{m_\pi^2+q^2} \vec\sigma_1\!\cdot\!\vec q\, \vec\sigma_2\!\cdot\!\vec q\,(6c_1-c_2-5c_3)\).
* For the \(g_A^0\) ring interaction, the selfclosing contribution yields an isovector central potential with a logarithmic term: \(\frac{k_f^3 \vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{48 \pi^4 f_\pi^6} \big[ (2c_1-\frac{3c_2}{2}-3c_3)m_\pi^2 - (\frac{c_2}{4}+\frac{c_3}{3})q^2 \big] \ln\frac{m_\pi}{\lambda}\).
* The total \(\lambda^2\)-divergence from the \(g_A^0\) ring interaction sums to \(\frac{k_f^3 \lambda^2}{128 \pi^4 f_\pi^6}(c_2+2c_3)\), which is isoscalar central and can be absorbed on the 3N short-distance parameter \(c_E\).
* For the \(g_A^2\) ring interaction, the selfclosing contribution gives an isovector central potential with logarithmic term: \(\frac{g_A^2 k_f^3 \vec\tau_1\!\cdot\!\vec\tau_2}{48 \pi^4 f_\pi^6} \big[ 3m_\pi^2(4c_1-c_2-6c_3) - \frac{5q^2}{6}(c_2+4c_3) \big] \ln\frac{m_\pi}{\lambda}\).
* The low-energy constant \(\bar e_{14}\) extracted from \(\pi N\)-scattering is given as \(\bar e_{14}= 1.52\) GeV\(^{-3}\) or \(\bar e_{14}= 1.18\) GeV\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of density-dependent NN-interactions from chiral three-nucleon forces to subsubleading order N\(^4\)LO, following previous calculations for the leading N\(^2\)LO terms by Holt (2010) and for the subleading N\(^3\)LO short-range and long-range terms by the same author in two recent works (2019). The semi-analytical results enable approximate implementation of subsubleading chiral 3N-forces into nuclear many-body calculations, such as those for nuclear matter thermodynamics or medium-mass nuclei, where direct inclusion of 3N forces remains technically challenging.
