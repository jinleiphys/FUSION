# Nonextensive perfect hydrodynamics - a model of dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics?

> We demonstrate that nonextensive perfect relativistic hydrodynamics ($q$-hydrodynamics) can serve as a model of the usual relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics ($d$-hydrodynamics) facilitating therefore considerably its applications. As illustration we show how using $q$-hydrodynamics one gets the $q$-dependent expressions for the dissipative entropy current and the corresponding ratios of the bulk and shear viscosities to entropy density, $ζ/s$ and $η/s$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that nonextensive perfect hydrodynamics (q-hydrodynamics), which is formally a first-order theory, can serve as a model for the usual relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics (d-hydrodynamics), which is a second-order theory, provided the nonextensivity parameter q is close to unity and a specific nonextensive/dissipative conjecture (NexDC) relating the q-temperature and q-flow to the usual temperature and flow is assumed.

## Method

The authors start from a 1+1 dimensional baryon-free version of q-hydrodynamics derived from a nonextensive Boltzmann equation using the Tsallis formalism. They formally decompose the q-hydrodynamic energy-momentum tensor into an ideal part and a dissipative part, then impose the NexDC relations that equate the q-pressure with the usual pressure and relate the q-energy density to the usual energy density plus a bulk pressure term. This transformation converts the perfect q-hydrodynamic equation into the standard form of dissipative hydrodynamic equations, allowing identification of the dissipative entropy current and transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The parameter q = 1.08 was used in calculations for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Two types of initial conditions were considered: fixed maximal initial energy density epsilon^(in) = 22.3 GeV/fm^3 (with sigma = 1.28) and fixed width sigma = 1.25 (with epsilon^(in) = 27.8 GeV/fm^3)
- The total multiplicity of produced secondaries follows N = 11 * (7 + 10q)
- The inequality |1-q|(p·u/T)^2 < 2 must hold for the linear expansion in (q-1) to be valid in the whole phase space

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of perfect q-hydrodynamics (Osada 2007) and connects it to the established second-order dissipative hydrodynamic theories of Israel (1976) and Muronga (2004). It provides a practical alternative to standard viscous hydrodynamics, which suffers from acausality and instability in its first-order Navier-Stokes form and complexity in second-order formulations. The approach enables calculation of dissipative quantities like entropy production and viscosity-to-entropy ratios using the simpler q-hydrodynamic framework, with potential applications to heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC.
