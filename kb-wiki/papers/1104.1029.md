# Another mean field treatment in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD

> We discuss the QCD phase diagram in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD by using a new type of mean field coming from the next-to-leading order of the large dimensional expansion. The QCD phase diagram in the strong coupling limit recently obtained by using the monomer-dimer-polymer (MDP) algorithm has some differences in the phase boundary shape from that in the mean field results. As one of the origin to explain the difference, we consider another type of auxiliary field, which corresponds to the point-splitting mesonic composite. Fermion determinant with this mean field under the anti-periodic boundary condition gives rise to a term which interpolates the effective potentials in the previously proposed zero and finite temperature mean field treatments. While the shift of the transition temperature at zero chemical potential is in the desirable direction and the phase boundary shape is improved, we find that the effects are too large to be compatible with the MDP simulation results.

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a new type of mean field corresponding to a point-splitting mesonic composite, derived from the next-to-leading order of the large dimensional expansion, can improve the shape of the QCD phase boundary in the strong coupling limit; however, the shifts in transition temperature and chemical potential are too large to be compatible with the monomer-dimer-polymer (MDP) simulation results.

## Method

The authors start from the lattice QCD action with one species of unrooted staggered fermion for color SU(3) in the strong coupling limit (g → ∞). They use the zero-temperature treatment where both spatial and temporal link variables are integrated out first, then introduce an auxiliary field for the point-splitting mesonic composite V±ν,x = ην,x χ̄x χx+ν̂. After applying an extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and assuming constant auxiliary fields, they evaluate the effective potential under the anti-periodic boundary condition for quarks, obtaining a momentum-integral form with a constituent quark mass and wave function renormalization factors.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature at zero chemical potential in the present treatment: Tc = 0.92 (in lattice units), compared to MDP simulation Tc ≈ 1.4 and standard mean field Tc = 5/3
- Critical chemical potential at zero temperature in the present treatment: μc = 1.08 (in lattice units), compared to MDP simulation μc ≈ 0.59 and standard mean field μc = 0.55
- Standard mean field overestimates the MDP transition temperature at μ=0 by 10-20%
- Standard mean field overestimates the transition temperature by about 50-60% at β = 2Nc/g² ∼ 4

## Context

This work extends the mean field treatment of strong coupling lattice QCD (SC-LQCD) to address discrepancies with the MDP simulation results of de Forcrand and Fromm (2010). The standard mean field treatment (SCL-Std, 1985) overestimates the transition temperature and produces a phase boundary shape significantly different from MDP results. The new mean field improves the phase boundary shape, making it similar to the finite-temperature treatment with baryonic composite effects (KMOO2007), but the magnitude of the shifts is too large. The approach enables analytical study of the phase diagram in a regime where MDP simulations are currently the only available method, and future work should examine combinations with baryonic effects and chiral condensate fluctuations.
