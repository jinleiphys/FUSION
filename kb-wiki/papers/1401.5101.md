# An operator definition and derivation of collisional energy and momentum loss in relativistic plasmas

> We present an operator definition of the collisional energy and momentum loss suffered by an energetic charged particle in the presence of a medium. Our approach uses the energy-momentum tensor of the medium to evaluate the energy and momentum transfer rates. We apply this formalism to an energetic lepton or quark propagating in thermal electron-positron or quark-gluon plasmas, respectively. By using two different approaches to describe the energetic charged particle, an external current approach and a diagrammatic approach, we show explicitly that the operator method reproduces the known results for collisional energy loss from the scattering rate formalism. We further use our results to evaluate the collisional energy and momentum loss for the cases of heavy quark propagation through a quark-gluon plasma and energetic muon propagation in an electron-positron plasma produced in a high-intensity laser field.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that collisional energy and momentum loss of an energetic charged particle in a relativistic plasma can be derived from the medium's energy-momentum tensor rather than from the scattering rate formalism, and that the operator method exactly reproduces known results from the scattering rate approach for both the leading logarithmic term and the constant term.

## Method

The authors compute the divergence of the medium's energy-momentum tensor (the source term) using real-time thermal field theory, separating the calculation into hard and soft momentum exchange regions separated by an arbitrary scale q*. For the hard region, tree-level photon propagators are used; for the soft region, hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed propagators are employed. The formalism is applied to an energetic lepton in an electron-positron plasma using an external current approach, and to an energetic quark in a quark-gluon plasma using a diagrammatic approach with two-gluon exchange.

## Key numbers

- The complete collisional energy loss rate for a charged fermion in a QED plasma is dE/dt = (e^4 T^2 / 24π) [1 - (1-u^2)/u tanh^{-1}[u]] (ln(T/(3m_γ)) + ln(1/√(1-u^2)) + C(u)), where m_γ = eT/3 is the photon screening mass
- In the ultrarelativistic limit u→1, the collisional energy loss rate becomes dE/dt = (e^4 T^2 / 48π) [ln(E/(e^2 T)) + 2.725]
- The collisional momentum loss is related to energy loss by dp^z/dt = (1/u) dE/dt, with dp^x/dt = dp^y/dt = 0 for propagation in the z-direction
- The hard contribution in the u→1 limit is dE/dt|_hard = (e^4 T^2 / 48π) [ln(q_m T/(q^*)^2) + 8/3 - 12 ln(A) + ln(4π)], where A ≈ 1.282 is Glaisher's constant
- The soft contribution in the u→1 limit is dE/dt|_soft = (e^4 T^2 / 24π) [ln(q^*/(3m_γ)) + 0.256]

## Context

This work extends the operator definition of collisional energy loss from the medium perspective, building on earlier calculations by Neufeld (2010, 2011) that used the energy-momentum tensor to leading logarithmic accuracy. The results reproduce the classic collisional energy loss calculations by Braaten and Thoma (1991) using the scattering rate formalism, confirming that the two approaches are equivalent. The formalism enables future applications to more complex systems, including heavy quark propagation through a quark-gluon plasma and energetic muon propagation in electron-positron plasmas produced by high-intensity laser fields, and can potentially be extended to parton shower problems where the external source is more general than a single classical current.
