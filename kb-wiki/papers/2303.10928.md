# Origin of octupole deformation softness in atomic nuclei

> Recent high-energy heavy ion collision experiments have revealed that some atomic nuclei exhibit unusual softness and significant shape fluctuations. In this work, we use the fully self-consistent mean-field theory to identify all even-even nuclei that are unstable or soft against octupole deformation. All exceptional cases of enhanced octupole transition strengths in stable even-even nuclei throughout the nuclide chart are resolved and the origin is found in basic shell structure. The presence of atomic nuclei exhibiting significant softness to quadrupole-octupole deformation is suggested. These results represent a significant advance in our understanding of the underlying mechanisms of nuclear octupole deformation and have implications for further experimental and theoretical studies.

# Origin of octupole deformation softness in atomic nuclei

## Key claim

The authors use fully self-consistent mean-field theory (Skyrme HF-RPA/QRPA) to diagnose which even-even nuclei are unstable or soft against octupole deformation, finding that the origin lies in basic shell structure and the spin-orbit splitting that creates small energy gaps between opposite-parity single-particle pairs near the Fermi surface. The claim is hedged by the fact that results are extremely sensitive to the choice of Skyrme force, particularly its spin-orbit component, and that the spherical QRPA framework sometimes predicts "collapse" (imaginary energies) rather than a definitive ground-state shape.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent spherical Skyrme HF-BCS (or HF) calculations followed by RPA (or QRPA) for all stable even-even nuclei with experimental data available, using the SkM*, SLy4, SLy5, and SIII Skyrme forces. The octupole polarizability alpha_3 was computed from the inverse energy-weighted sum rule m_{-1}(3) to quantify softness, and the presence of imaginary RPA solutions (collapse) was taken as a diagnostic of octupole instability or deformation. Pairing correlations were included via the BCS method for open-shell nuclei, extending HF to HF-BCS and RPA to QRPA while maintaining self-consistency.

## Key numbers

- For 96Zr with SLy5 QRPA: E(3^-_1) = 1.592 MeV, B(E3) = 56.1 W.u., alpha_3 = 4.5 W.u./MeV; experimental values are E(3^-_1) = 1.897 MeV and B(E3) = 53 +/- 6 W.u.
- For 96Ru with SLy5 QRPA: E(3^-_1) = 3.685 MeV, B(E3) = 23.3 W.u., alpha_3 = 0.9 W.u./MeV; experimental E(3^-_1) = 2.650 MeV
- For 208Pb (stiff reference): alpha_3 is approximately 1 W.u./MeV
- For 32S with SLy5 QRPA: B(E3) = 20.1 W.u.; experimental B(E3) = 30 +/- 5 W.u. (Kibedi 2002) or 16 +/- 3 W.u. (Ouellet 2011)
- For 72Se with SLy5 QRPA: E(3^-_1) = 2.312 MeV, B(E3) = 50.0 W.u.; experimental E(3^-_1) = 2.406 MeV, B(E3) = 32 +/- 11 W.u.
- For 146Ba with SLy5 QRPA: E(3^-_1) = 1.444 MeV, B(E3) = 48.7 W.u.; experimental E(3^-_1) = 0.821 MeV, B(E3) = 48^{+21}_{-29} W.u.
- For 226Ra with SLy5 QRPA: E(3^-_1) = 1.162 MeV, B(E3) = 61.4 W.u.; experimental E(3^-_1) = 0.322 MeV, B(E3) = 54 +/- 3 W.u.
- The SLy5 QRPA calculation found octupole collapse in 38 even-even nuclei in the lanthanide and actinide regions, including 152-156Sm, 152-160Gd, 156-170Dy, 162-170Er, 166-172Yb, 234,238,240U, 236-244Pu, 246,248Cm, 248-252Cf, and 256Fm
- Simultaneous quadrupole and octupole collapse was found for 38 nuclei, identified as candidates for quadrupole-octupole deformation

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RPA approach of Blaizot (1976) and the early octupole studies of Abbas (1981) to a global survey of all stable even-even nuclei, resolving the previously unexplained exceptional octupole transition strengths. It directly addresses the recent STAR Collaboration data (2021) showing strong octupole correlation in 96Zr in high-energy heavy ion collisions, as analyzed by Zhang (2022) and Nijs (2023). The results confirm the octupole-magic numbers (16, 34, 56, 88, 134) proposed by Nazarewicz (1984) and Butler (1996), and identify 38 nuclei as candidates for quadrupole-octupole deformation that could enhance violations of fundamental symmetries as discussed by Auerbach (1996) and Spevak (1997). The work enables future beyond-RPA studies of quadrupole-octupole coupling and constrains energy density functionals through experimental octupole data.
