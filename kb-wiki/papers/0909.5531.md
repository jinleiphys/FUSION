# Origin of resonances in chiral dynamics

> The nature of baryon resonances is studied in the dynamical chiral coupled-channel approach for meson-baryon scattering. In general, origin of resonances in two-body scattering can be classified into two categories: dynamically generated states and genuine elementary particles. We demonstrate that the genuine contribution in the loop function can be excluded by adopting a natural renormalization scheme. The origin of resonances can be studied by looking at the effective interaction in the natural renormalization scheme, which is deduced from the phenomenological amplitude fitted to experimental data. Applying this method to the baryon resonances, we find that the dominant component for the Lambda(1405) resonance is dynamical, while a genuine contribution plays a substantial role for the structure of the N(1535).

# Origin of resonances in chiral dynamics

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the origin of baryon resonances can be classified by separating the CDD (Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson) pole contribution from the dynamical meson-baryon component using a "natural renormalization scheme," finding that the Lambda(1405) is predominantly a dynamical meson-baryon molecule while the N(1535) contains a substantial genuine (elementary) component beyond the two-body scattering space.

## Method
The authors apply the N/D method to s-wave meson-baryon scattering, using the Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) term as the leading-order chiral interaction kernel. They define a "natural renormalization scheme" by fixing the subtraction constant such that the loop function vanishes at the baryon mass threshold, thereby excluding any CDD pole contribution from the loop function. By equating the phenomenological amplitude (fitted to data with a free subtraction constant) to the amplitude in the natural scheme, they extract an effective interaction kernel whose pole structure reveals whether a resonance is dynamically generated or requires a genuine (CDD) component.

## Key numbers
- Effective pole in the natural interaction kernel for the N(1535): z_eff^{N*} = 1693 ± 37i MeV
- Effective pole for the Lambda(1405): z_eff^{Lambda*} ~ 7.9 GeV (far above the resonance region)
- Purely dynamical (WT + natural subtraction) pole for N(1535): z^{N*} = 1582 - 61i MeV
- Purely dynamical poles for Lambda(1405): z_1^{Lambda*} = 1417 - 19i MeV, z_2^{Lambda*} = 1402 - 72i MeV
- Phenomenological (fitted) pole for N(1535): z^{N*} = 1493 - 31i MeV
- Phenomenological poles for Lambda(1405): z_1^{Lambda*} = 1429 - 14i MeV, z_2^{Lambda*} = 1397 - 73i MeV
- Typical energy scale for natural renormalization in pi-Sigma channel: ~200 MeV (for pion mass ~138 MeV, baryon mass ~1193 MeV)
- Typical energy scale for natural renormalization in Kbar-N channel: ~630 MeV (for kaon mass ~496 MeV, baryon mass ~939 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), Oller (2000), and Lutz (2001) for describing baryon resonances in meson-baryon scattering. It builds on the N/D method framework of Oller (2000) and the CDD pole concept from Castillejo (1956). The method enables a systematic distinction between dynamically generated resonances (meson-baryon molecules) and genuine elementary states, complementing analyses based on N_c scaling by Hyodo (2007) and Roca (2008) and electromagnetic size estimates by Sekihara (2008). The results for the Lambda(1405) confirm its predominantly dynamical nature, while the N(1535) requires further investigation into possible components such as three-quark states, five-quark states, chiral partners, or vector-meson-baryon channels.
