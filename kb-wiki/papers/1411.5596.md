# Quarkonia Disintegration due to time dependence of the $q \bar{q}$ potential in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Rapid thermalization in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions leads to fast changing potential between a heavy quark and antiquark from zero temperature potential to the finite temperature one. Time dependent perturbation theory can then be used to calculate the survival probability of the initial quarkonium state. In view of very short time scales of thermalization at RHIC and LHC energies, we calculate the survival probability of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ using sudden approximation. Our results show that quarkonium decay may be significant even when temperature of QGP remains low enough so that the conventional quarkonium melting due to Debye screening is ineffective.

# Quarkonia Disintegration due to time dependence of the $q \bar{q}$ potential in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Quarkonium suppression can occur significantly even when the QGP temperature remains below the Debye screening temperature $T_D$, provided the thermalization time scale is short enough that the sudden approximation applies. The authors argue that for thermalization times of order 0.1–0.25 fm at LHC and RHIC, the change in the $q\bar{q}$ potential is too fast for adiabatic evolution, leading to transitions from the initial quarkonium state to excited states that subsequently melt.

## Method
The authors use the sudden (quench) approximation to calculate survival probabilities of $J/\psi$ and $\Upsilon$ states. They solve the Schrodinger equation with a zero-temperature Cornell potential $V(r) = -\alpha_s/r + \sigma r$ for the initial state, and a Debye-screened finite-temperature potential $V(r) = -(\alpha_s/r)\exp(-\omega_D r) + (\sigma/\omega_D) r (1 - \exp(-\omega_D r))$ for the final state, using the Numerov method and energy minimization. The survival probability is computed as the squared overlap of the zero-temperature wave function with the finite-temperature wave function at a given QGP temperature.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass $m_c = 1.28$ GeV, string tension $\sigma = 0.16$ GeV$^2$, $\alpha_s = \pi/12$ for $J/\psi$ calculations.
- Bottom quark mass $m_b = 4.67$ GeV for $\Upsilon$ calculations.
- Thermalization time scale: 0.25 fm for RHIC, 0.1 fm for LHC; conservatively within 1 fm from elliptic flow measurements.
- Energy gap between $J/\psi$ and its next excited state ($\chi$) is about 300 MeV, leading to transition time scale of about 0.7 fm; for $\Upsilon$ states the gap is about 400 MeV with transition time scale about 0.5 fm.
- Survival probability for $J/\psi$ drops to about 10% as temperature increases to 270 MeV.
- Survival probability for $\Upsilon$ drops to about 10% as temperature increases to 560 MeV.
- Transition probability of an initial $\Upsilon$ to the $\Upsilon(2S)$ excited state at $T = 200$ MeV is about 10%.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of quarkonium suppression beyond the conventional Debye screening picture proposed by Matsui and Satz. While prior work by N. Dutta and collaborators had considered adiabatic evolution during the cooling stage of QGP for sequential suppression, the authors note that the validity of adiabatic evolution during the thermalization stage had not been previously discussed. The mechanism proposed here operates even when QGP temperature remains below $T_D$, where conventional screening would predict no suppression, and should be incorporated alongside standard mechanisms when calculating net quarkonium suppression in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
