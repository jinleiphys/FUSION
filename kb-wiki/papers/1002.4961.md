# $π$-$η$ mixing and charge symmetry violating NN potential in matter

> We construct density dependent Class $III$ charge symmetry violating (CSV) potential due to mixing of $π$-$η$ meson with off-shell corrections. The density dependence enters through the non-vanishing $π$-$η$ mixing driven by both the neutron-proton mass difference and their asymmetric density distribution. The contribution of density dependent CSV potential is found to be appreciably larger than the contribution of vacuum CSV potential.

## Key claim

The density-dependent Class III charge symmetry violating (CSV) potential due to π-η mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter is appreciably larger than the vacuum CSV potential, but the overall contribution of π-η mixing to CSV observables remains smaller than that from ρ-ω mixing.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-body CSV nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential by evaluating Feynman diagrams with mixed π-η intermediate states, using both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) coupling for the meson-nucleon vertices. The π-η mixing amplitude was calculated from one-loop nucleon-antinucleon self-energy diagrams, separating vacuum contributions (driven by the neutron-proton mass difference) from medium contributions (driven by asymmetric Fermi distributions of neutrons and protons in asymmetric nuclear matter). The momentum-space potential was then Fourier-transformed to coordinate space, with and without phenomenological monopole form factors at the meson-nucleon vertices.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum π-η mixing amplitude at the η mass shell: Π^{PS}_{vac}(q²=m²_η) = -1197 MeV⁻² (PS coupling), compared to the experimental value of -4200 MeV⁻².
- Nuclear matter density used for medium calculations: ρ_B = 0.148 fm⁻³ with asymmetry parameter α = (ρ_n - ρ_p)/ρ_B = 1/3.
- Difference between nn and pp scattering lengths from vacuum CSV potential: Δa = 0.00082 fm without form factors and Δa = -0.0001 fm with form factors.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of charge symmetry violation in nuclear interactions, particularly the construction of CSV potentials from meson mixing. Previous work on π-η mixing (Coon 1987, Kim 1993, Piekarewicz 1993) treated the mixing amplitude as constant or on-shell, while this paper incorporates the full momentum dependence and adds medium effects from asymmetric nuclear matter. The density-dependent CSV potential due to ρ-ω mixing was recently constructed by Biswas (2010), and the present paper provides the analogous calculation for π-η mixing. The results enable more complete calculations of CSV observables such as the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and the difference between pp and nn scattering lengths, though the π-η contribution is found to be smaller than the ρ-ω contribution.
