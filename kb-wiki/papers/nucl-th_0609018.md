# Self-Consistent Separable Rpa for Skyrme Forces: Giant Resonances in Axial Nuclei

> We formulate the self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) method and specify it for Skyrme forces with pairing for the case of axially symmetric deformed nuclei. The factorization of the residual interaction allows to avoid diagonalization of high-rank RPA matrices, which dramatically reduces the computational expense. This advantage is crucial for the systems with a huge configuration space, first of all for deformed nuclei. SRPA takes self-consistently into account the contributions of both time-even and time-odd Skyrme terms as well as of the Coulomb force and pairing. The method is implemented to description of isovector E1 and isoscalar E2 giant resonances in a representative set of deformed nuclei: $^{154}$Sm, $^{238}$U, and $^{254}$No. Four different Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3) are employed to explore dependence of the strength distributions on some basic characteristics of the Skyrme functional and nuclear matter. In particular, we discuss the role of isoscalar and isovector effective masses and their relation to time-odd contributions. High sensitivity of the right flank of E1 resonance to different Skyrme forces and the related artificial structure effects are analyzed.

## Key claim

The self-consistent separable RPA (SRPA) method, when applied to axially deformed nuclei with Skyrme forces and pairing, reproduces the full RPA spectra accurately using only a small number (K ~ 2-6) of separable terms, dramatically reducing computational expense compared to diagonalizing high-rank RPA matrices, but the success depends critically on an appropriate choice of the basic operators $\hat{Q}_{sk}$ and the method cannot describe Landau fragmentation without its microscopic extension.

## Method

The authors formulate SRPA by factorizing the residual two-body Skyrme interaction into a separable form using time-even and time-odd hermitian one-body operators derived self-consistently from the energy-density functional. The macroscopic part uses scaling transformations to generate collective operators and strength constants, while the microscopic part solves the eigenvalue problem or computes the strength function directly using the Cauchy residue theorem to avoid diagonalization of high-rank RPA matrices. The method is implemented for isovector E1 and isoscalar E2 giant resonances in the deformed nuclei $^{154}$Sm, $^{238}$U, and $^{254}$No using four Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3).

## Key numbers

- The rank of the SRPA matrix is reduced to 4K, where K is the number of separable operators (typically K ~ 2-6), compared to the full RPA matrix rank equal to the size of the 1ph basis (which can reach $10^4$-$10^5$ in heavy deformed nuclei).
- The method is applied to three deformed nuclei: $^{154}$Sm, $^{238}$U, and $^{254}$No, covering a range from rare-earth to super-heavy elements.
- Four different Skyrme parameterizations are employed: SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3, which represent different values of isoscalar and isovector effective masses and asymmetry energy.
- The Lorentz averaging parameter $\Delta$ is used in the strength function calculation via the Cauchy residue theorem.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent separable RPA approach previously developed for spherical nuclei (Nesterenko et al., 2002; 2005) to axially deformed systems with pairing, building on earlier separable expansion schemes that were either limited to analytical estimates (Rowe, 1970; Bertsch and Tsai, 1975) or not fully self-consistent (Nesterenko, 1993). The method enables large-scale studies of giant resonances in deformed nuclei where the 1ph configuration space grows huge, and is particularly relevant for describing spectra and reaction rates of exotic nuclei in astrophysics (Langanke, 2001; Stone, 2006).
