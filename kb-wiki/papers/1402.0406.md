# Isospin properties of electric dipole excitations in 48Ca

> Two different experimental approaches were combined to study the electric dipole strength in the doubly-magic nucleus 48Ca below the neutron threshold. Real-photon scattering experiments using bremsstrahlung up to 9.9 MeV and nearly mono-energetic linearly polarized photons with energies between 6.6 and 9.51 MeV provided strength distribution and parities, and an (α,α'γ) experiment at E_α=136 MeV gave cross sections for an isoscalar probe. The unexpected difference observed in the dipole response is compared to calculations using the first-order random-phase approximation and points to an energy-dependent isospin character. A strong isoscalar state at 7.6 MeV was identified for the first time supporting a recent theoretical prediction.

# Isospin properties of electric dipole excitations in 48Ca

## Key claim

The dipole response of 48Ca shows a state-to-state difference in isospin character that is not observed in 40Ca: a strong isovector state at 7.3 MeV and a strong isoscalar state at 7.6 MeV coexist, with the latter identified for the first time, supporting a recent theoretical prediction of an energy-dependent isospin character rather than a simple isospin splitting into lower-energy mixed states and higher-energy isovector states as seen in heavier neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The study combined three experimental techniques: real-photon scattering using bremsstrahlung up to 9.9 MeV, nearly mono-energetic linearly polarized photons between 6.6 and 9.51 MeV at the HIgammaS facility for parity determination, and an (alpha,alpha'gamma) coincidence experiment at E_alpha = 136 MeV using the AGOR cyclotron and Big-Bite Spectrometer at KVI Groningen. Singles alpha-scattering cross sections were converted into fractions of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule using the coupled-channels program CHUCK and the code BEL with a global optical potential and the Harakeh-Dieperink dipole form factor. Results were compared to first-order random-phase approximation calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction.

## Key numbers

- The state at 7.3 MeV has the strongest B(E1)upward in the (gamma,gamma') experiment but was not excited by alpha particles within an experimental sensitivity limit of 0.15(4) mb/sr
- The state at 7.6 MeV has one of the weakest B(E1)upward in (gamma,gamma') but the largest alpha-particle excitation cross section of 1.61(9) mb/sr
- The total B(E1)upward strength in 48Ca was corrected to 80(8) x 10^{-3} e^2 fm^2 from a reanalysis of (gamma,gamma') data
- The isospin-mixing matrix element between the 7.3 and 7.6 MeV 1- states was determined to be 85(3) keV, with a squared mixing amplitude beta^2 = 0.061(6) and a perturbed level-energy difference of 357 keV
- The excitation-energy resolution in singles alpha-scattering was 236(1) keV at 3831 keV
- The absolute photopeak efficiency of the HPGe array was 0.504(1)% at 1238 keV
- The 48Ca target had thickness 1.7 mg/cm^2 enriched to 99%

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of low-lying E1 strength in the calcium chain by Hartmann et al. (2000-2004) and earlier (alpha,alpha'gamma) experiments on neutron-rich nuclei by Savran et al. (2006), Endres et al. (2009-2012), and Derya et al. (2013). It contradicts the prediction by Chambers et al. (1994) of a linear dependence of low-lying E1 strength on excess neutrons, since 44Ca and 48Ca show similar strength. The results enable the first experimental determination of isospin mixing between 1- states in the pygmy dipole resonance energy region, and support the theoretical prediction by Papakonstantinou et al. (2012) of a transition from isoscalar to neutron-skin oscillation modes at N=28.
