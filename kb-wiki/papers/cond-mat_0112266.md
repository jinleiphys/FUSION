# Comment on "Negative specific heat for a cluster of 147 Sodium atoms" by Schmidt et al

> Schmidt et al (Phys Rev Lett 86, 1191 (2001)) observe and analyze a negative specific heat in Na clusters. The analysis is done within the framework of Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium statistical mechanics. We argue that this is not the only physical possibility to be taken into account. A possible connection with nonextensive statistical mechanics is considered as well.

## Key claim

The negative specific heat observed by Schmidt et al. (2001) in Na₁₄₇⁺ clusters, which they interpret within Boltzmann-Gibbs microcanonical equilibrium statistical mechanics, could alternatively arise from a dynamic metastable state requiring a nonextensive statistical mechanics framework; the authors argue that without further experimental evidence, the Boltzmann-Gibbs interpretation is not necessarily the only valid one.

## Method

The authors present a theoretical argument contrasting two possible interpretations of negative specific heat in isolated, nonextensive systems: (1) the system is in a thermodynamically stable state, justifying the Boltzmann-Gibbs microcanonical ensemble used by Schmidt et al. (2001); (2) the system is in a dynamic metastable state, which may require nonextensive statistical mechanics. To illustrate the second possibility, they report numerical simulations of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, a system of long-range coupled planar rotators, showing two plateaux in the relaxation of average kinetic energy per particle, where the early plateau exhibits a negative specific heat and a non-Maxwellian velocity distribution consistent with nonextensive statistical mechanics.

## Key numbers

- In the HMF model numerical simulation, two plateaux are observed for the average kinetic energy per particle as a function of time; the early plateau shows a region of negative specific heat (panel c of fig. 1), while the late plateau is described by a standard Maxwellian velocity distribution (panel b).
- The Na₁₄₇⁺ cluster studied by Schmidt et al. (2001) contains 147 sodium atoms, and the negative specific heat is observed in a specific energy range (no numerical energy values given in this comment).

## Context

This comment extends the debate on negative specific heat in small systems, previously discussed for self-gravitating systems, melting atomic clusters, and fragmenting nuclei (references in Schmidt et al., 2001). The authors specifically cite Gross (reference in text) for the Boltzmann-Gibbs microcanonical approach, and their own prior work (Tsallis, 1988) for the nonextensive statistical mechanics alternative. The comment suggests that metastable states analogous to those seen in long-range coupled planar rotators (the HMF model and the α-XY model) and in colloidal glasses (reference to colloid work) could occur in sodium clusters, implying that the theoretical interpretation of Schmidt et al. (2001) is not uniquely determined without further experimental evidence.
