# $Ξ$-deuteron low-energy $s$-wave phase shifts and momentum correlation functions in Faddeev formulation

> The low-energy $Ξ$-deuteron scattering is investigated through the solution of Faddeev equations, employing three sets of the currently available parametrization of the $Ξ$-nucleon interactions. One of these is the chiral NLO interaction parametrized by the Jülich group, and the other two are based on the calculations by the HAL-QCD method. The $s$-wave phase shifts in the $J=3/2$ and $J=1/2$ states are presented. Three-body wave functions in coordinate space are constructed from the Faddeev amplitudes in momentum space. These functions are used in the calculation of $Ξd$ momentum correlation functions. The effects of the deuteron breakup are significant in the $J=3/2$ channel. The differences in the magnitude of the calculated correlation function show the quantitative difference of the $ΞN$ interactions in the spin-isospin channels. The prospective experimental data on the $Ξd$ momentum correlation function could contribute to a better description of the $ΞN$ interactions.

# Ξ-deuteron low-energy s-wave phase shifts and momentum correlation functions in Faddeev formulation

## Key claim
The low-energy Ξ-deuteron s-wave scattering phase shifts and momentum correlation functions are calculated using three parametrizations of the Ξ-nucleon interaction, but the calculations omit the Coulomb force and use averaged baryon masses, so the results are not fully realistic yet provide basic information on how three-body dynamics influence the Ξd system.

## Method
Faddeev equations for the Ξd system were solved in an isospin basis using three sets of ΞN interactions: a chiral NLO interaction from the Jülich group (cutoff Λc=550 MeV) and two local potentials based on HAL-QCD calculations by Inoue et al. and Sasaki et al. Three-body wave functions in coordinate space were constructed from the Faddeev amplitudes in momentum space, including both incident-channel and full three-body wave functions, and used to evaluate Ξd momentum correlation functions with Gaussian source functions of varying radius.

## Key numbers
- Averaged masses used: 938.919 MeV for N, 1115.68 MeV for Λ, 1193.12 MeV for Σ, and 1318.29 MeV for Ξ.
- Source radii selected for correlation function calculations: Rs = 1.2, 2.5, and 5.0 fm.
- A cusp structure in the J=1/2 elastic correlation functions appears at q ~ 60 MeV/c, attributed to opening of the 1S0 NN decay at the deuteron threshold.
- The spin-averaged correlation function is defined as C^av(q) = (C^{J=1/2} + 2C^{J=3/2})/3.
- The statistical spin-isospin factor for deuteron formation is g_s g_I = 3/4.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev calculations of Λd low-energy scattering and correlation functions (Kohno and Kamada, 2024, 2025) to the Ξd system. It employs three ΞN interaction models: the chiral NLO interaction from the Jülich-Bonn-München group (Haidenbauer et al., 2016, 2019) and two HAL-QCD parametrizations by Inoue et al. (2019) and Sasaki et al. (2020). The results are intended to guide interpretation of prospective experimental data on Ξd momentum correlation functions from heavy-ion collision experiments, such as those being pursued by the STAR collaboration (2025), and to help constrain the spin-isospin structure of ΞN interactions beyond what can be inferred from Ξ bound-state energies in light nuclei observed in emulsion experiments (Nakazawa et al., 2015; Hayakawa et al., 2021; Yoshimoto et al., 2021; Ichikawa et al., 2024).
