# Quenching of pairing gap at finite temperature in 184W

> We extract pairing gap in $^{184}$W at finite temperature for the first time from the experimental level densities of $^{183}$W, $^{184}$W, and $^{185}$W using "thermal" odd-even mass difference. We found the quenching of pairing gap near the critical temperature $T_c = 0.47$ MeV in the BCS calculations. It is shown that the monopole pairing model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential explains the reduction of the pairing correlation using the partition function with the number parity projection in the static path approximation plus random-phase approximation.

# Quenching of pairing gap at finite temperature in 184W

## Key claim

The pairing gap in 184W extracted from experimental level densities using a "thermal" odd-even mass difference shows a rapid decrease around T = 0.5 MeV, which is interpreted as a signature of pairing transition; however, the authors note that fluctuations beyond the mean field wash out the sharp phase transition, so the pairing gap does not become quickly zero at the BCS critical temperature.

## Method

The authors extracted the pairing gap at finite temperature from experimental level densities of 183W, 184W, and 185W using a thermal odd-even mass difference defined by a three-point indicator involving shifted thermal energies. They calculated the canonical partition function from the measured level densities, extrapolated to approximately 40 MeV using a back-shifted Fermi gas model, and compared the results to a monopole pairing model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential treated in the static path approximation plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) with number parity projection.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in BCS calculations: T_c = 0.47 MeV
- Deformation parameter for 184W: beta = 0.23, estimated from experimental B(E2) = 119.3 W.u.
- Pairing force strength fixed at G = 20/A to reproduce the experimental odd-even mass difference Delta = 0.83 MeV for 184W at zero temperature
- Neutron pairing energies used for back-shifted parameters: 0.61 MeV for 183W, 0.72 MeV for 185W, and 1.60 MeV for 184W
- Level density parameters: a = 0.21A^0.87 MeV^-1, with back-shift parameters E1 = C1 + Delta where C1 = -6.6A^-0.32
- Adjustment factors f for back-shifted level density: 0.3 for 183W, 0.4 for 184W, and 0.7 for 185W

## Context

This work extends previous studies of S-shaped heat capacities in 162Dy, 166Er, and 172Yb by Schiller (2001) and Melby (2001), which were interpreted as signatures of Cooper pair breaking near T ≈ 0.5 MeV. The authors build on their own earlier suggestion (Kaneko, 2004) that thermal odd-even mass differences reveal pairing suppression more directly than heat capacity S-shapes. The results enable future extraction of pairing gaps from level densities of other triplet nuclei and call for further theoretical work incorporating quadrupole-quadrupole interaction fluctuations beyond the SPA+RPA treatment used here.
