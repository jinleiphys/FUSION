# Photon Scattering with the Lorentz Integral Transform Method

> The application of the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method to photon scattering off nuclei is presented in general. As an example, elastic photon scattering off the deuteron in the unretarded dipole approximation is considered using the LIT method. The inversion of the integral transform is discussed in detail paying particular attention to the high-energy contributions in the resonance term. The obtained E1-polarizabilities are compared to results from the literature. The corresponding theoretical cross section is confronted with experimental results confirming, as already known from previous studies, that the E1-contribution is the most important one at lower energies.

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method can be applied to photon scattering off nuclei, not just to photoabsorption, by reformulating the resonance amplitude in terms of a polarizability strength function that is independent of photon momentum in the unretarded dipole approximation for the deuteron; the inversion of the transform requires careful treatment of high-energy contributions to obtain reliable polarizabilities.

## Method

The authors applied the LIT method to elastic photon scattering off the deuteron in the unretarded dipole (E1) approximation, using the Argonne AV18 potential as the interaction model. They solved radial Schrödinger-like equations with a complex energy and a source term to obtain Lorentz states, then inverted the integral transforms using two different basis sets to extract the strength functions \(F_j(E)\) for \(j=0,1,2\). The real and imaginary parts of the E1 polarizabilities were computed from these strength functions via principal-value integrals and dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- Lorentz integral transforms \(L_j(\sigma)\) were computed with \(\sigma_I = 5\) MeV for \(\sigma_R\) up to 1000 MeV, showing an approximate \(\sigma_R^{-2}\) fall-off at high energies.
- Relative differences \(\Delta_j(E)\) between inversions with the two basis sets were less than about 1% up to 50 MeV for all \(j\), with the largest difference of about 7% for \(F_1\) at very low energies.
- The combined inversion results used \(E_1 = 30\) MeV for \(j=0\), \(E_1 = 18\) MeV for \(j=1\), and \(E_1 = 33\) MeV for \(j=2\) as the transition points between the two basis sets.
- The strength functions \(F_j(E)\) showed pronounced low-energy peaks with a strong subsequent fall-off, becoming weaker at higher energies, with a stronger fall-off again beyond about 200 MeV for \(F_0\) and beyond about 300 MeV for \(F_1\) and \(F_2\).

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to total photoabsorption cross sections for systems up to A=7 (Bacca et al., 2002, 2007), to the exclusive process of photon scattering. It uses the deuteron as a test case to compare with conventional approaches such as the dispersion-theoretic calculation of Weyrauch and Arenhövel (1983) and more recent chiral effective field theory calculations (Hildebrandt et al., 2005, 2010). The method enables future calculations of photon scattering for larger nuclei, such as the recently measured \(^6\)Li at HIGS (Feldman et al., 2011), where full continuum calculations are currently out of reach.
