# Probing an extended region of Delta m^2 with rapidly oscillating 7Be solar neutrinos

> If 7Be solar neutrinos can be observed in real time experiments, then an extended region of Delta m^2 can be probed by a proper analysis of the rapidly changing phase of vacuum oscillations due to the eccentricity of the earth's orbit about the sun. For the case of maximal vacuum mixing, a kind of Fourier analysis of expected data for one year's time could uniquely pick out Delta m^2 if it lies in the region ~10^{-10} - 6 X 10^{-9} (eV)^2.

## Key claim

If the solar neutrino puzzle is solved by maximal vacuum mixing in the mass range $\Delta m^2 \sim 10^{-10} - 6 \times 10^{-9}$ (eV)$^2$, then a Fourier-type analysis of one year of real-time $^7$Be neutrino data from an experiment like Borexino could uniquely determine $\Delta m^2$, provided that thermal broadening and MSW damping do not completely wash out the oscillatory signal.

## Method

The author derived an expression for the detected $^7$Be neutrino rate as a function of orbital phase $\phi$, incorporating vacuum oscillations, thermal broadening from the solar temperature (using the published energy profile of Bahcall (1994)), and MSW damping modeled numerically with a Gaussian factor $\exp[-1.583(\Delta m^2)^2]$. Monte Carlo data sets of approximately 9000 events (corresponding to one year of Borexino statistics) were generated using this rate function, and a Fourier-type analysis was performed by integrating the data with a phase factor $\exp[i 4396 \Delta m_v^2 / (1 + 0.0167 \cos \phi)]$, subtracting the Bessel-function-like background $J(\Delta m_v^2)$ to extract the signal $K(\Delta m^2, \Delta m_v^2)$.

## Key numbers

- The observable range of $\Delta m^2$ via $^7$Be neutrinos is from about $10^{-10}$ up to about $6 \times 10^{-9}$ (eV)$^2$.
- The Earth's orbital eccentricity is $\epsilon = 0.0167$, producing a $\pm 1.67\%$ yearly variation in the Earth-Sun distance.
- The $^7$Be neutrino line is at 0.862 MeV; the oscillation phase factor contains the constant 4396 when $\Delta m^2$ is expressed in units of $10^{-8}$ (eV)$^2$.
- The MSW damping factor is well represented by $\exp[-1.583(\Delta m^2)^2]$ for maximal mixing, with $\Delta m^2$ in units of $10^{-8}$ (eV)$^2$.
- The overall phase of the cosine factor changes by $180^\circ$ when $\Delta m^2$ changes by $\pi / 4396 \cong 0.000714$ in units of $10^{-8}$ (eV)$^2$.
- Monte Carlo simulations used 15000 attempts generating about 9000 data points, corresponding roughly to one year's running time at Borexino.
- An unambiguous signal was obtained for $\Delta m^2$ in the range 0.1--0.5 (in units of $10^{-8}$ (eV)$^2$); the analysis becomes ambiguous below $\Delta m^2 = 0.04$ (in the same units).

## Context

This work extends the "just-so" vacuum oscillation region for $^8$B neutrinos (Hata and Langacker (1997); Gelb and Rosen (1999)) to lower $\Delta m^2$ values accessible via $^7$Be neutrinos, and is in approximate agreement with a previous analysis by de Gouvea, Friedland, and Murayama (1999). The method enables a determination of $\Delta m^2$ in the range $10^{-10} - 6 \times 10^{-9}$ (eV)$^2$ for maximal mixing, a region not excluded by global analyses of SNO, Super-Kamiokande, chlorine, and gallium experiments (Bahcall, Gonzalez-Garcia, and Pena-Garay (2001); Fogli et al. (2001)). Not seeing a signal would eliminate this $\Delta m^2$ region for large mixing angle solutions.
