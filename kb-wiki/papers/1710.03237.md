# Evolution of the Mean Jet Shape and Dijet Asymmetry Distribution of an Ensemble of Holographic Jets in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> Some of the most important probes of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy ion collisions come from the analysis of how the shape and energy of jets are modified by passage through QGP. We model an ensemble of back-to-back dijets to gain a qualitative understanding of how the shapes of the individual jets and the asymmetry in the energy of the pairs of jets are modified by passage through an expanding droplet of strongly coupled plasma, as modeled in a holographic gauge theory. We do so by constructing an ensemble of strings in the gravitational description of the gauge theory. We model QCD jets in vacuum using strings whose endpoints move "downward" into the gravitational bulk spacetime with some fixed small angle that represents the opening angle (ratio of jet mass to jet energy) that the QCD jet would have in vacuum. Such strings must be moving through the gravitational bulk at (close to) the speed of light; they must be (close to) null. This condition does not specify the energy distribution along the string, meaning that it does not specify the shape of the jet being modeled. We study the dynamics of strings that are initially not null and show that strings with a wide range of initial conditions rapidly accelerate and become null and, as they do, develop a similar distribution of their energy density. We use this distribution of the energy density along the string, choose an ensemble of strings whose opening angles and energies are distributed as in perturbative QCD, and show that we can then fix one model parameter such that the mean jet shape in our ensemble matches that measured in p-p collisions reasonably well. We send our strings through the plasma, choosing the second model parameter to get a reasonable suppression in the number of jets, and study how the mean jet shape and the dijet asymmetry are modified, comparing both to data from LHC heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

By constructing an ensemble of holographic strings whose initial opening angles and energies are distributed as in perturbative QCD, the authors show that the mean jet shape in their ensemble can match proton-proton collision data after fixing one model parameter, and that after passage through strongly coupled plasma the ensemble reproduces both the modification of the mean jet shape and the dijet asymmetry distribution measured in LHC heavy ion collisions, provided a second parameter is chosen to match the jet suppression factor R_AA^jet.

## Method

The authors model each jet as a null string in the gravitational dual of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, where the string endpoint moves downward into the bulk with a fixed small angle sigma_0 proportional to the jet opening angle. They construct an ensemble of back-to-back dijets by sampling initial jet energies and opening angles from perturbative QCD distributions, then evolve each string through an expanding cooling droplet of strongly coupled plasma described by a 4+1-dimensional black hole spacetime, using the analytic energy loss rate dE/dx = -4x^2/(pi x_therm^2 sqrt(x_therm^2 - x^2)) where x_therm is the thermalization distance related to sigma_0. The initial energy distribution along each string is determined by studying the dynamics of initially non-null strings, which rapidly accelerate to become null and develop a universal scaling form for their energy density.

## Key numbers

- The thermalization distance x_therm is related to the initial downward angle sigma_0 by T x_therm = Gamma(1/4)^2/(4 pi^(3/2)) * 1/sqrt(sigma_0)
- The energy loss rate for small distances is dE_jet/dx = -E_jet * 256 pi^(7/2)/Gamma(1/4)^6 * T^3 x^2 sigma_0^(3/2) + O(x^4)
- The initial jet energy distribution in the ensemble follows approximately E_jet^(-6) scaling
- The jet suppression factor R_AA^jet is used as the second model parameter to calibrate the overall quenching strength

## Context

This work extends the holographic jet ensemble approach introduced by Rajagopal, Sadofyev, and van der Schee (2016), which first demonstrated that analyzing an ensemble of jets is crucial because wider jets lose more energy, potentially causing the average opening angle of surviving jets to narrow even though each individual jet widens. The present study adds two key improvements: analyzing the full jet shape rather than just the opening angle, and studying the dijet asymmetry distribution. The authors also build on the null string framework developed by Chesler and collaborators (2014, 2015) for modeling individual jets in strongly coupled plasma. This approach complements the hybrid strong/weak coupling model developed by Casalderrey-Solana and collaborators (2014-2017), which uses perturbative QCD parton showers from Pythia modified by holographic energy loss rates.
