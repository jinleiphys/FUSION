# In search of the QCD-Gravity correspondence

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is the fundamental theory of strong interactions. It describes the behavior of quarks and gluons which are the smallest known constituents of nuclear matter. The difficulties in solving the theory at low energies in the strongly interacting, non-perturbative regime have left unanswered many important questions in QCD, such as the nature of confinement or the mechanism of hadronization. In these lectures oriented towards the students we introduce two classes of dualities that attempt to reproduce many of the features of QCD, while making the treatment at strong coupling more tractable: (1) the AdS/CFT correspondence between a specific class of string theories and a conformal field theory and (2) an effective low-energy theory of QCD dual to classical QCD on a curved conformal gravitational background. The hope is that by applying these dualities to the evaluation of various properties of the strongly-interacting matter produced in heavy ion collisions one can understand how QCD behaves at strong coupling. We give an outline of the applications, with emphasis on two transport coefficients of QCD matter -- shear and bulk viscosities.

## Key claim

These lecture notes introduce two classes of dualities that attempt to reproduce features of QCD at strong coupling, making the treatment more tractable: the AdS/CFT correspondence and an effective low-energy theory of QCD dual to classical QCD on a curved conformal gravitational background. The authors present these approaches as pedagogical tools for students, with the hope that applying these dualities to strongly-interacting matter produced in heavy ion collisions can help understand how QCD behaves at strong coupling, but they acknowledge that the AdS/CFT correspondence relies on conformal symmetry in a very essential way, which is currently the main obstacle to applying these ideas to QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews two dual approaches to strongly-coupled QCD: (1) the AdS/CFT correspondence, which maps conformal strongly-coupled SU(N_c) gauge theories with large 't Hooft coupling onto a weakly-coupled dual gravity theory in AdS_5 x S^5 space; and (2) an effective low-energy Lagrangian of QCD that satisfies Renormalization Group constraints, is scale and conformally invariant in the limit of vanishing vacuum energy density, and matches perturbative behavior at short distances. The second approach has a dual description as classical gluodynamics on a curved conformal space-time background on one hand, and gluodynamics in flat space-time coupled to scalar glueballs (dilatons) on the other.

## Key numbers

* The QCD beta function at leading order: β(g) = -b g^3/(4π)^2 + O(g^5), where b = 11 N_c - 2 N_f, with N_c = 3 colors and N_f flavors
* The running coupling constant expression: α_s(Q^2) = α_s(Q_0^2) / [1 + (α_s(Q_0^2)/(12π)) (11 N_c - 2 N_f) log(Q^2/Q_0^2)]
* The quark-antiquark potential: V(R) = -(4/3) α_s(R)/R + σ R, with string tension σ ≈ 1 GeV/fm
* The AdS/CFT relation: L^4 = g_YM^2 N α'^2, where L is the common radius of curvature of AdS_5 and S^5, requiring strong 't Hooft coupling g_YM^2 N ≫ 1 for L ≫ √α'
* Black hole temperature: T_BH = ħ c^3 / (8π G M k_B)
* Black hole entropy: S_BH = k_B A c^3 / (4 G ħ)

## Context

These lectures extend the pedagogical literature on applying gauge-gravity dualities to QCD, building on the original AdS/CFT correspondence introduced by Maldacena (1997) and developed by Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998) and Witten (1998). The effective low-energy QCD approach follows work by Kharzeev, Levin, and Tuchin (2004) and Kharzeev (2008) on scale-invariant Lagrangians, and relates to studies of confinement as an event horizon for colored particles by Kharzeev (2005) and Casalderrey-Solana, Kharzeev, and Shuryak (2007). The paper enables future applications of these dualities to compute transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly shear and bulk viscosities, with connections to entropy production from scale invariance breaking studied by Kharzeev and Tuchin (2005) and Kharzeev, Krasnitz, and Tuchin (2007).
