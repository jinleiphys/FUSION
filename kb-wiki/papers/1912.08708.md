# Multiple quantum phase transitions in the Zr isotopes

> We present a detailed analysis of spectra and other observables for the entire chain of Zr isotopes, from neutron number 52 to 70, in the framework of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing. The results suggest a remarkable interplay of multiple quantum phase transitions (QPTs). One type of QPT involves an abrupt crossing of normal and intruder configurations, superimposed on a second type of QPT involving gradual shape-changes within each configuration.

## Key claim
The Zr isotopes exhibit an interplay of two distinct types of quantum phase transitions: a Type-II abrupt crossing of normal and intruder configurations, superimposed on a Type-I gradual shape change within each configuration. The configurations retain high purity before and after the crossing, making the Zr chain exceptional compared to other nuclei where such separate QPTs are masked by strong mixing.

## Method
The authors employed the Interacting Boson Model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using a core of \(^{90}_{40}\)Zr\(_{50}\) with valence neutrons in the 50-82 major shell. The normal A-configuration has no active protons above the Z=40 subshell gap, while the intruder B-configuration involves two-proton excitations creating 2p-2h states. Hamiltonian parameters were obtained by a global fit to energy and E2 data for the entire chain from neutron number 52 to 70, with bosons replaced by boson holes beyond neutron number 66.

## Key numbers
- Ratio \(R_{4/2}\) for configuration A at neutron numbers 52-56: \(R^{(A)}_{4/2} \cong 1.6\); for configuration B: \(R^{(B)}_{4/2} \cong 2.3\)
- In \(^{102}\)Zr: \(E_{2^+_1} = 151.78\) keV, \(R^{(B)}_{4/2} = 3.15\)
- In \(^{104}\)Zr: \(E_{2^+_1} = 139.3\) keV, \(R^{(B)}_{4/2} = 3.24\)
- In \(^{106}\)Zr: \(E_{2^+_2} = 607.0\) keV, \(E_{4^+_1} = 476.5\) keV
- In \(^{110}\)Zr: \(E_{4^+_1} = 565\) keV, \(E_{2^+_2} = 485\) keV
- Wavefunction probability in intruder configuration for \(0^+_1\) state: \(b^2 = 1.8\%\) in \(^{98}\)Zr, jumping to \(b^2 = 87.2\%\) in \(^{100}\)Zr
- For \(2^+_1\) state: \(b^2 = 97.1\%\) already in \(^{98}\)Zr
- Boson effective charges: \(e^{(A)} = 0.9\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\), \(e^{(B)} = 2.24\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\)
- Isotope shift parameters: \(\alpha = 0.235\) fm\(^2\), \(\eta = 0.264\) fm\(^2\)
- Two-neutron separation energy parameters: \(\tilde{A} = -16.5\) MeV, \(\tilde{B} = 0.758\) MeV, \(\Delta_n = 0\) for neutron numbers 50-56 and \(\Delta_n = 2\) MeV for 58-70

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in nuclei beyond single-configuration shape transitions observed in the neutron number 90 region (e.g., Nd-Sm-Gd isotopes, as noted by Cejnar and Jolie) and configuration-coexistence transitions seen in light Pb-Hg isotopes (Heyde, 2011). The results contrast with mean-field based calculations (Delaroche, Nomura, Mei) which smooth out the phase transitional behavior, and differ from Monte-Carlo shell-model calculations (Taka) which find triaxial rather than gamma-unstable shapes and identify the spherical state in \(^{100}\)Zr as \(0^+_4\) rather than \(0^+_2\). The findings enable detailed study of intertwined QPTs in a single isotopic chain and provide benchmarks for microscopic theories.
