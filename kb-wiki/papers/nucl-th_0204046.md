# Pions in the Pionless Effective Field Theory

> We show that processes involving pions that remain very near their mass-shell can be reliably computed in the pionless effective field theory, with the pion integrated in as a heavy field. As an application, we compute the pion-deuteron scattering amplitude near threshold to next-to-leading order in the momentum expansion. This amplitude is formally dominated by an infrared logarithm of the form log(gamma/mpi), where gamma is the deuteron binding momentum, and mpi is the mass of the pion. The coefficient of this logarithm is determined by the S-wave pion-nucleon scattering lengths.

## Key claim

Processes involving pions that remain very near their mass-shell can be reliably computed in the pionless effective field theory by integrating the pion in as a heavy field, but only when the number of pions entering and leaving each interaction vertex is equal; the formal EFT expansion parameters are gamma/m_pi and p_pi/m_pi, where gamma is the deuteron binding momentum and p_pi is the pion momentum.

## Method

The authors constructed a variant of the dibaryon pionless effective field theory (dEFT) in which non-relativistic nucleons interact via contact operators and pions near their mass-shell are included as heavy fields, analogous to heavy-quark effective theory. They performed field redefinitions to remove the classical trajectory of a pion and used nonrelativistic kinematics, treating the residual energy operator in perturbation theory. The pion-deuteron scattering amplitude was computed to next-to-leading order in the momentum expansion, with the leading-order amplitude coming from a one-loop diagram with a single insertion of the pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes and a local pion-deuteron operator, and the next-to-leading-order amplitude from a two-loop rescattering diagram involving a heavy-pion propagating between nucleons and a local counterterm.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding momentum gamma = sqrt(M_N B) appears as the key infrared scale, with gamma/m_pi ~ 1/3
* The isovector pion-nucleon scattering length a- = (-0.0905 +/- 0.0042) m_pi^-1 from the Neuchatel-PSI-ETHZ pionic hydrogen measurement
* The isoscalar pion-nucleon scattering length a+ = (-0.0022 +/- 0.0043) m_pi^-1, consistent with zero
* The predicted pion-deuteron scattering length a_pi d = -0.019 m_pi^-1, compared to the experimental value Re(a_pi d^exp) = (-0.0261 +/- 0.0005) m_pi^-1
* The experimental imaginary part Im(a_pi d^exp) = (-0.0063 +/- 0.0007) m_pi^-1
* At T_pi = 20 MeV, the amplitude at zero momentum transfer is A_pi d(20,0) = (-0.30 - i 0.00013) fm
* At T_pi = 20 MeV and momentum transfer |k| = 150 MeV, the amplitude is A_pi d(20,150) = (-0.27 - i 0.038) fm

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon pionless effective field theory developed by Beane and Savage (2001) to include pions as heavy fields, analogous to the treatment of heavy quarks in heavy-quark effective theory and non-relativistic QCD. The infrared logarithm log(gamma/m_pi) found in the two-loop diagram was previously identified by Borasoy and Griesshammer (2001). The approach enables systematic computation of low-energy pion-deuteron scattering, with the leading non-analytic contribution providing the dominant contribution, and the formalism can be extended to other processes involving pions near their mass-shell in multi-nucleon systems.
