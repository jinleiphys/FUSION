# $D^*Ď€$ interaction from the lineshape of $D_1(2420)$ in $B$-decays

> We present a model calculation to reproduce the differential mass distribution for the $D^*Ď€$ system in the reactions $B^- \to D^{*+} Ď€^- Ď€^-$ and $B^{+}\to D_s^+D^{*-}Ď€^{+}$ analyzed by the LHCb Collaboration, which shows a dominant signal for $D_1(2420)$. %\textbf{We} (The idea is to) We consider a model based on coupled channel meson-meson interactions that can describe the properties of $D_1(2420)$ in terms of the underlying dynamics, use it to determine the invariant mass distribution of the $D^*Ď€$ system, and compare the results with the experimental data. We also determine the $D^*Ď€$ scattering length, for which different values are available from different sources, leading to a controversy. To our knowledge, this is the first attempt to reproduce the mentioned data using model calculations. Our formalism relies on the hadronization of different mesons through a weak decay, allowing for the final-state (strong) interactions among the relevant constituents. We benefit from our previous work when obtaining the amplitudes corresponding to the strong interactions. We hope that our findings can be useful in further investigations of the properties of $D_1(2420)$.

## Key claim

The authors present a model calculation that reproduces the LHCb data on the $D^*\pi$ invariant mass distribution in two $B$-decay processes, finding that the $S$-wave contribution from coupled-channel meson-meson interactions is larger than estimated in the experimental analyses, and that the $D^*\pi$ scattering length in isospin 1/2 agrees with the findings of Liu (2012) rather than with the smaller value reported by the ALICE Collaboration (2024).

## Method

The authors construct amplitudes for $B^- \to D^{*+} \pi^- \pi^-$ and $B^{-}\to D_s^- D^{*+}\pi^{-}$ by first describing the weak decay at the quark level with external and internal $W$-boson emission, then hadronizing the resulting quark pairs into intermediate vector-pseudoscalar ($VP$) meson pairs using SU(4) flavor matrices. The final-state strong interactions among these $VP$ pairs are treated via a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in $S$-wave, using interaction kernels from an effective Lagrangian based on hidden local symmetry and heavy-quark symmetry, with the $D_1(2420)$ resonance generated dynamically from the $VP$ rescattering. The full amplitude combines tree-level and rescattering contributions, and the $D^{*+}\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution is computed by integrating over the Dalitz plot phase space.

## Key numbers

* The $D^*\pi$ scattering length in isospin 1/2 is found to be more in agreement with the findings of Liu (2012) than with the ALICE Collaboration (2024) value, which has an absolute value smaller than 0.1 fm.
* The constant $C^\text{ext}_0$ for $B^-\to D^-_s D^{*+}\pi^-$ is found to be 0.27 MeV$^{-1}$ (Gaussian form factor, $\Lambda = 900$ MeV) and 0.19 MeV$^{-1}$ (Gaussian form factor, $\Lambda = 1100$ MeV), when normalizing to 100% of the experimental area.
* The constant $C^\text{ext}_0$ for $B^-\to D^{*+}\pi^-\pi^-$ is found to be 2.58 MeV$^{-1}$ (Gaussian form factor, $\Lambda = 900$ MeV) and 2.07 MeV$^{-1}$ (Gaussian form factor, $\Lambda = 1100$ MeV), when normalizing to 100% of the experimental area.
* For the monopole form factor with $\Lambda = 600$ MeV, $C^\text{ext}_0$ is 0.21 MeV$^{-1}$ for $B^-\to D^-_s D^{*+}\pi^-$ and 2.32 MeV$^{-1}$ for $B^-\to D^{*+}\pi^-\pi^-$.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel formalism developed by Malabarba (2022), Gamermann (2007), and Khemchandani (2023), which describes the $D_1(2420)$ and $D_1(2430)$ axial mesons through vector-pseudoscalar interactions, by applying it to reproduce LHCb data on $B$-decay lineshapes (LHCb 2019, LHCb 2024). The results address the controversy between the small $D^*\pi$ scattering length reported by the ALICE Collaboration (2024) and the larger values from unitarized chiral perturbation theory (Guo 2009, Guo 2018, Abreu 2011, Geng 2010), and provide a framework for extracting resonance properties from weak decay data without relying on $D$-wave contributions or additional quark-model poles.
