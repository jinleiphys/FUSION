# Dynamical evolution of fermion-boson stars with realistic equations of state

> Fermion-boson stars are mixtures of the ordinary nuclear matter of a neutron star and bosonic dark matter. We dynamically evolve fermion-boson stars for the first time using a realistic equation of state for nuclear matter. We use our dynamical solutions to make a detailed study of the evolution of weakly and strongly perturbed static solutions. As examples of our findings, we identify a region of parameter space where weakly perturbed unstable static solutions migrate to a stable configuration and we determine the criteria under which strongly perturbed stable static solutions will always move to a stable configuration instead of collapsing to a black hole.

## Key claim

The authors dynamically evolve fermion-boson stars for the first time using a realistic equation of state for nuclear matter, finding that weakly perturbed unstable static solutions can migrate to a stable configuration in a specific region of parameter space, while strongly perturbed stable static solutions will always move to a stable configuration instead of collapsing to a black hole under certain criteria.

## Method

The authors solve the Einstein field equations coupled to a perfect fluid (fermion sector) and a complex scalar field (boson sector) using finite volume and high-resolution shock-capturing methods for the hydrodynamical system, with the scalar field evolved via second-order finite differencing and third-order Runge-Kutta time integration. Static solutions are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the fermion sector and the boson star ansatz equations, using analytical fits of the FPS, SLy, BSk19, BSk20, and BSk21 unified equations of state. The linear stability of static solutions is determined by computing critical curves from the condition that mass and particle numbers are stationary with respect to parameter variations.

## Key numbers

* For the SLy equation of state, the critical solution (maximum mass neutron star) has mass M = 2.048 solar masses, radius R = 9.979 km, and central pressure P(0) = 860.3 MeV/fm^3.
* For boson stars with mu = 10^{-10} eV and lambda = 0, the critical solution has central scalar field phi(0)/m_P = 0.0541 and mass M = 0.846 solar masses.
* The five representative linearly stable static solutions for SLy have masses ranging from 0.733 to 1.946 solar masses, with central pressures from 10^{1.5} to 10^{2.8} MeV/fm^3 and central scalar field values from 10^{-1.9} to 10^{-1.2} in units of m_P.
* The dimensionless parameter beta = (mu_N/m_P)^2 / (mu/m_P) is order one when mu is approximately 10^{-10} eV, with mu_N = 938 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior dynamical studies of mixed stars with bosonic dark matter by Valdez-Alvarado (2012), Brito (2015), Bezares (2019), and DiGiovanni (2020), which used simple polytropic equations of state, by incorporating realistic unified equations of state (FPS, SLy, BSk19-21) with analytical fits from Haensel (2004) and Potekhin (2013). The framework follows the seminal perturbation study of boson stars by Seidel and Suen (1990) and enables future investigations of nonlinear stability and migration phenomena in mixed dark matter-neutron star systems with realistic nuclear physics.
