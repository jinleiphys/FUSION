# Quantum Interactions Between Non-Perturbative Vacuum Fields

> We develop an approach to investigate the non-perturbative dynamics of quantum field theories, in which specific vacuum field fluctuations are treated as the low-energy dynamical degrees of freedom, while all other vacuum field configurations are explicitly integrated out from the path integral. We show how to compute the effective interaction between the vacuum field degrees of freedom both perturbatively (using stochastic perturbation theory) and fully non-perturbatively (using lattice field theory simulations). The present approach holds to all orders in the couplings and does not rely on the semi-classical approximation.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism to compute the effective interaction between selected non-perturbative vacuum field configurations (such as instantons and antiinstantons) by projecting lattice-generated field configurations onto a manifold of those configurations, holding to all orders in the couplings and without relying on the semiclassical approximation; the method is demonstrated on a one-dimensional double-well quantum mechanical system as a toy model.

## Method

The authors decompose a generic Euclidean path integral configuration \(x(t)\) into a chosen vacuum field manifold configuration \(\tilde{x}(t;\gamma)\) (parametrized by collective coordinates \(\gamma\)) plus a fluctuation field \(y(t)\), imposing orthogonality conditions between \(y(t)\) and the tangent vectors of the manifold. They then rewrite the path integral as an integral over the collective coordinates \(\gamma\) with an effective interaction \(F(\gamma)\) defined by the logarithm of the projected path integral over fluctuations. For the double-well potential, they use a sum-ansatz of instanton and antiinstanton fields as the vacuum manifold and extract the two-body effective interaction \(F_2(\xi)\) from the pair correlation function \(g_2(\xi)\) obtained by projecting lattice Monte Carlo configurations onto the manifold.

## Key numbers

* The double-well potential is defined as \(U(x) = m \alpha (x^2 - \beta^2)^2\), with parameters \(m\), \(\alpha\), and \(\beta\).
* The one-instanton measure is given by \(\kappa \simeq 4 \sqrt{\frac{2 (2 \alpha)^{3/2} \beta^2}{\pi \hbar}}\).
* The number of instanton-antiinstanton pairs is estimated as \(2N \simeq \kappa T\) for a total Euclidean time \(T\).
* The tangent vectors for the instanton-antiinstanton manifold are \(g_{t_1}(t) = -\sqrt{2 \alpha} \beta^2 \operatorname{sech}^2[\sqrt{2 \alpha} \beta (t - \bar{t}_1)]\) and \(g_{t_2}(t) = \sqrt{2 \alpha} \beta^2 \operatorname{sech}^2[\sqrt{2 \alpha} \beta (t - \bar{t}_2)]\).

## Context

This work extends the program of identifying relevant low-energy vacuum field configurations in non-perturbative quantum field theories, following studies of instantons (Shuryak, various years), monopoles, center-vortices, and merons in QCD. The formalism provides a way to compute effective interactions between such configurations beyond the dilute instanton gas approximation, enabling the study of interacting instanton liquids and the breakdown of semiclassical descriptions. The method is illustrated on the double-well system but is designed to be applicable to lattice formulations of quantum field theories in any number of dimensions.
