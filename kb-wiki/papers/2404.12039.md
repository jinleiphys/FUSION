# Constraints on the finite volume two-nucleon spectrum at $m_π\approx 806$ MeV

> The low-energy finite-volume spectrum of the two-nucleon system at a quark mass corresponding to a pion mass of $m_π\approx 806$ MeV is studied with lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) using variational methods. The interpolating-operator sets used in [Phys.Rev.D 107 (2023) 9, 094508] are extended by including a complete basis of local hexaquark operators, as well as plane-wave dibaryon operators built from products of both positive- and negative-parity nucleon operators. Results are presented for the isosinglet and isotriplet two-nucleon channels. In both channels, noticably weaker variational bounds on the lowest few energy eigenvalues are obtained from operator sets which contain only hexaquark operators or operators constructed from the product of two negative-parity nucleons, while other operator sets produce low-energy variational bounds which are consistent within statistical uncertainties. The consequences of these studies for the LQCD understanding of the two-nucleon spectrum are investigated.

## Key claim
The variational bounds on the lowest few two-nucleon energy eigenvalues obtained from operator sets containing only hexaquark operators or operators built from products of two negative-parity nucleons are noticeably weaker than those from other operator sets, while all other operator sets produce low-energy variational bounds consistent within statistical uncertainties. The results do not support the presence of bound two-nucleon states at this quark mass, but the variational method cannot definitively rule out bound dibaryons.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using a variational method with a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) on a cubic spatial volume with side-length L ≈ 3.4 fm (L/a = 24) at a pion mass of mπ ≈ 806 MeV. They constructed interpolating-operator sets containing up to 46 operators in the isosinglet channel and 31 operators in the isotriplet channel, including momentum-projected dibaryon operators, local hexaquark operators, quasi-local operators inspired by nuclear EFTs, and new operators built from products of negative-parity nucleon operators. The correlation-function matrices were computed and the GEVP was solved to extract variational bounds on energy eigenvalues in multiple irreducible representations of the cubic group.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: mπ ≈ 806 MeV
- Lattice volume: L ≈ 3.4 fm (L/a = 24)
- In the isosinglet T1+ channel, the ground state energy shift ΔE0 = −6.2(1.7) MeV (n=0) and first excited state ΔE1 = 59.6(2.3) MeV (n=1)
- In the isotriplet A1+ channel, the ground state energy shift ΔE0 = −4.6(1.5) MeV (n=0) and first excited state ΔE1 = 63.3(2.3) MeV (n=1)
- In the isosinglet G1+ channel, the ground state energy E0 = 1.636(23) GeV (n=0) and first excited state E1 = 2.601(53) GeV (n=1)
- In the isosinglet G1- channel, the ground state energy E0 = 2.227(33) GeV (n=0)
- In the isotriplet A1+ channel, the fifth excited state (n=5) energy shift ΔE5 = 438.5(12.6) MeV

## Context
This work extends the variational LQCD study of Amarasinghe et al. (2021) by including a complete basis of local hexaquark operators and plane-wave dibaryon operators built from both positive- and negative-parity nucleon operators, in a smaller spatial volume (L ≈ 3.4 fm versus L ≈ 4.5 fm). The results are consistent with earlier variational studies by Francis et al. (2018), Horz et al. (2020), and Amarasinghe et al. (2021) that found no definitive evidence for bound two-nucleon states, but contrast with earlier asymmetric correlation-function studies that implied bound states at similar quark masses. The new hexaquark and negative-parity nucleon operators probe "hidden color" components of dibaryon states but do not produce large differences in the observed low-energy variational bounds.
