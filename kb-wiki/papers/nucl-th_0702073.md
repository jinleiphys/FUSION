# Model dependence of the neutrino-deuteron disintegration cross sections at low energies

> Model dependence of the reaction rates for the weak breakup of deuterons by low energy neutrinos is studied starting from the cross sections derived from potential models and also from pionless effective field theory. Choosing the spread of the reaction yields, caused basically by the different ways the two-body currents are treated, as a measure of the model dependent uncertainty, we conclude that the breakup reactions are $\sim$ 2 - 3 % uncertain, and that even the ratio of the charged to neutral current reaction rates is also $\sim$ 2 % uncertain.

## Key claim

The model-dependent uncertainty in the neutrino-deuteron breakup cross sections at low energies is assessed by taking the spread of reaction yields caused by different treatments of two-body currents; the authors conclude that the breakup reactions are approximately 2-3% uncertain, and even the ratio of charged to neutral current reaction rates is about 2% uncertain, but these uncertainties are "smaller, but basically comparable, to the full effect of the two-body currents."

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for the weak breakup of deuterons by low-energy neutrinos using two approaches: potential models (Nijmegen I, Nijmegen 93, QG, and AV18) with one- and two-nucleon currents, and pionless effective field theory (EFT) at next-to-next-to-leading order. Two models of the axial two-body exchange current were employed, differing in how the Delta excitation currents were treated (model I with standard Lagrangians, model II with suppressed Delta strength). The low-energy constant L1,A was extracted by comparing potential-model cross sections to the EFT form, and the integral yield Y for 8B solar neutrinos was computed to quantify the spread.

## Key numbers

- Neutral current reaction yield uncertainty: approximately 2.3% (from Table 1, maximum deviation Delta S/S = 0.6% for Y_I ncd and 0.4% for Y_II ncd, but text states "uncertainty of approximately 2.3%")
- Charged current reaction yield uncertainty: approximately 3.3% (from Table 1, maximum deviation Delta S/S = 1.3% for Y_I ccd and 1.8% for Y_II ccd, but text states "uncertainty of approximately 3.3%")
- Ratio of charged to neutral current reaction rates uncertainty: approximately 2% (from Table 1, Delta S/S = 2.0% for both R_I and R_II)
- L1,A values extracted from potential models: 3.8 to 5.7 fm^3 for neutral current, 3.9 to 6.4 fm^3 for charged current
- L1,A from fitting yields to 8B neutrino spectrum: 4.2 to 5.6 fm^3 for neutral current, 4.4 to 6.7 fm^3 for charged current
- Effect of meson exchange currents: 4.6% to 5.7% for model I, 2.4% to 3.8% for model II
- Radiative corrections enhance reaction rates by approximately 1.5% (neutral channel) and 2% (charged channel)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of neutrino-deuteron breakup cross sections by Ying, Haxton, and Henley (1990s), Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera (2001), and Mosconi, Ricci, and Truhlik (2005), and compares with the pionless EFT approach of Butler, Chen, and Kong (2001). The results provide the theoretical uncertainty estimates needed for interpreting SNO solar neutrino data, showing that the model dependence is smaller than the statistical and systematic uncertainties of the SNO experiment but still comparable to the full two-body current contribution.
