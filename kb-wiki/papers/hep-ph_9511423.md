# Second quantization approach to composite hadron interactions in quark models

> Starting from the Fock space representation of hadron bound states in a quark model, a change of representation is implemented by a unitary transformation such that the composite hadrons are redescribed by elementary-particle field operators. Application of the unitary transformation to the microscopic quark Hamiltonian gives rise to effective hadron-hadron, hadron-quark, and quark-quark Hamiltonians. An effective baryon Hamiltonian is derived using a simple quark model. The baryon Hamiltonian is free of the post-prior discrepancy which usually plagues composite-particle effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Fock-Tani (FT) unitary transformation that maps composite baryon states in a quark model onto elementary-particle field operators satisfying canonical anticommutation relations, thereby enabling standard field-theoretic techniques for hadron-hadron interactions. The derived effective baryon-baryon Hamiltonian is free of the post-prior discrepancy that typically plagues composite-particle effective interactions, but the transformation is constructed such that only a single real-hadron state is mapped to a single ideal-hadron state, not many-body states.

## Method

Starting from a Fock-space representation where baryons are three-quark bound states with noncanonical anticommutation relations, the authors implement a unitary transformation (the Fock-Tani transformation) that maps real baryon operators to ideal baryon operators satisfying canonical anticommutation relations. The transformation is constructed iteratively as a power series in the baryon wave functions, and when applied to a microscopic quark Hamiltonian with two-body quark-quark interactions, it yields effective Hamiltonians for baryon-baryon, baryon-quark, and quark-quark processes. The effective baryon-baryon potential is then derived explicitly for a nonrelativistic quark model with s-wave Gaussian wave functions and a spin-spin interaction from one-gluon exchange, and the resulting nonlocal nucleon-nucleon potential is used to compute s-wave phase shifts via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass parameter: \( m_q = 330 \) MeV
- RMS nucleon radius: \( a = 0.5 \) fm
- Coupling parameter: \( \alpha_s / m_q^2 = 0.6 / (330)^2 \) MeV\(^{-2}\)
- The effective NN potential contains five operator terms \( \mathcal{O}_i \) with associated momentum-space form factors \( v_i(\mathbf{p}, \mathbf{p}') \), where \( v_1 = v_3 = \exp[-(a^2/3)(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}')^2] \), \( v_2 = (3/4)^{3/2} \exp[-(a^2/6)(\mathbf{p}^2 + \mathbf{p}'^2)] \), and \( v_4 = v_5 = (12/11)^{3/2} \exp[-(2a^2/11)(\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}')^2 - (a^2/33)(\mathbf{p}^2 + 7\mathbf{p}'^2)] \)
- The direct term \( V^{dir} \) vanishes (\( \mathcal{O}_1 = 0 \)) due to color considerations

## Context

This work extends the Fock-Tani formalism, originally developed by Girardeau and by Vorob'ev and Khomkin for atomic physics, to hadronic physics with three-quark baryons. The approach shares characteristics with Weinberg's quasi-particle method and the quark Born diagram (QBD) approach of Barnes and collaborators, and the derived NN potential is similar to that obtained by Barnes et al. using QBD techniques. The formalism enables the use of standard many-body field-theoretic methods (Feynman diagrams, Green's functions) for composite hadron interactions, and is particularly suited for studying hadronic matter at high temperature or density where hadrons and quarks can coexist, as well as for application to relativistic light-cone quark models.
