# Weak Pion and Photon Production off Nucleons in a Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Neutrino-induced pion and photon production from nucleons and nuclei are important for the interpretation of neutrino-oscillation experiments, and these processes are potential backgrounds in the MiniBooNE experiment [A. A. Aquilar-Arevalo \textit{et al.} (MiniBooNE Collaboration), Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ {\bf 100}, 032301 (2008)]. Pion and photon production are investigated at intermediate energies, where the $Δ$ resonance becomes important. The Lorentz-covariant effective field theory contains nucleons, pions, Deltas, isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and vector ($ω$) fields, and isovector vector ($ρ$) fields. The lagrangian exhibits a nonlinear realization of (approximate) $SU(2)_L \otimes SU(2)_R$ chiral symmetry and incorporates vector meson dominance. Power counting for vertices and Feynman diagrams involving the $Δ$ is explained. Because of the built-in symmetries, the vector currents are automatically conserved, and the axial-vector currents satisfy PCAC. The irrelevance of so-called off-shell $Δ$ couplings and the structure of the dressed $Δ$ propagator, which has a pole only in the spin-3/2 channel, are discussed. To calibrate the axial-vector transition current $(N\! \leftrightarrow Δ)$, pion production from the nucleon is used as a benchmark and compared to bubble-chamber data from Argonne and Brookhaven National Laboratories. At low energies, the convergence of our power-counting scheme is investigated, and next-to-leading-order tree-level corrections are found to be very small.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Lorentz-covariant effective field theory (EFT) for neutrino-induced pion and photon production off nucleons that incorporates the Δ(1232) resonance, chiral symmetry, and vector meson dominance, but the calibration of the axial-vector N↔Δ transition current relies on fitting to bubble-chamber data from Argonne and Brookhaven National Laboratories, which have large error bars and introduce significant model dependence.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective field theory Lagrangian containing nucleons, pions, Deltas, and isoscalar scalar (σ) and vector (ω) fields plus isovector vector (ρ) fields, with a nonlinear realization of approximate SU(2)L ⊗ SU(2)R chiral symmetry and vector meson dominance. They compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for charged-current and neutral-current pion production and neutral-current photon production from nucleons, using power counting to organize contributions through next-to-leading order. The axial-vector N↔Δ transition current is calibrated by fitting to ANL and BNL bubble-chamber pion production data, and the vector part is constrained by electromagnetic interactions.

- Neutrino energy region studied: EνLab < 1 GeV, with low-energy region defined as EνLab ≤ 0.5 GeV
- ρ meson mass: mρ = 0.776 GeV; ω meson mass: mv = 0.783 GeV; a1 meson mass: ma1 = 1.26 GeV
- Axial coupling constant: gA = 1.26; βA(1) = 2.27; ca1ga1 = 3.85
- Vector form factor parameters: β(1) = −1.35, gρ/gγ = 2.48, λ(1) = 1.85, fρ = 3.04
- Isoscalar form factor parameters: β(0) = −1.40, gv/gγ = 3.95, λ(0) = −0.06, fv = −0.19
- Pion vector form factor: gρππ/gγ = 1.20

## Context

This work extends the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) effective field theory framework (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1997) to consistently include the Δ(1232) resonance for weak pion and photon production, addressing a need identified in neutrino-oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2008) and K2K. The approach contrasts with earlier studies (Adler, 1968; Rein and Sehgal, 1981; Hernandez et al., 2007; Graczyk et al., 2008, 2009) by using a chiral EFT with built-in current conservation and vector meson dominance rather than phenomenological form factors, and it enables future calculations of pion production from nuclei for neutrino-oscillation background analyses.
