# Mesic nuclei with a heavy antiquark

> The binding system of a hadron and a nucleus is a topic of great interest for investigating the hadron properties. In the heavy-flavor region, the attraction between a $P(=\bar{D},B)$ meson and a nucleon $N$ can appear, where the $PN-P^\ast N$ mixing plays an important role in relation to the heavy-quark spin symmetry. The attraction can produce exotic heavy mesic nuclei that are stable against the strong decay. We study an exotic system where the $\bar{D}$ ($B$) meson and nucleus are bound. The meson-nucleus interaction is given by a folding potential with single-channel $PN$ interaction and the nucleon number distribution function. By solving the Schrödinger equations of the heavy meson and the nucleus, we obtain several bound and resonant states for nucleon number $A=16,\dots,208$. The results indicate the possible existence of exotic mesic nuclei with a heavy antiquark.

## Key claim

The paper claims that exotic mesic nuclei containing a heavy antiquark ($\bar{D}$ or $B$ meson) can exist as bound or resonant states stable against strong decay, but this result depends on the assumption that the projected single-channel $PN$ potential, derived from a coupled-channel $PN-P^{\ast}N$ interaction, does not qualitatively change for different binding energies at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors constructed a meson exchange potential between a $P(=\bar{D},B)$ meson and a nucleon using pion and vector-meson ($\rho$, $\omega$) exchange, incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry and the $PN-P^{\ast}N$ mixing. They projected the coupled-channel $I(J^P)=0(1/2^-)$ potential onto a single-channel $PN$ potential, then built a folding potential by convolving this with a Woods-Saxon nucleon number distribution function for nuclei with $A=16$ to $208$. The Schrödinger equation for the $P$-nucleus system was solved numerically using the renormalized Numerov method to obtain bound and resonant states for $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$-waves.

## Key numbers

- For the $\bar{D}N$ two-body system, the coupled-channel calculation yields a bound state with energy $E=2.14$ MeV.
- For the $BN$ two-body system, the coupled-channel calculation yields a bound state with energy $E=23.04$ MeV.
- For the $\bar{D}$-nucleus system with $A=16$, three bound states are found for the $S$-wave; for $A=208$, seven bound states are found for the $S$-wave.
- For the $\bar{D}$-nucleus system, a $P$-wave resonance at $A=20$ has $E_{\rm re}=8.90\times 10^{-2}$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma/2=3.47\times 10^{-2}$ MeV.
- For the $B$-nucleus system, a $P$-wave resonance at $A=208$ has $E_{\rm re}=6.94\times 10^{-1}$ MeV with half-width $\Gamma/2=1.60\times 10^{-4}$ MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon distribution parameters are $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ and $a=0.54$ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy meson-nucleon interactions by Yasui and Yamaguchi (2009) and Yamaguchi et al. (2011), which established the $PN-P^{\ast}N$ mixing potential and found bound states in the $I=0$ channel. It contrasts with the wide scatter of binding energies (from a few MeV to a hundred MeV) predicted by other approaches such as the quark-meson coupling model of Tsushima (1998), the flavor SU(4) symmetry approach of Lutz (2005), and the QCD sum rule calculations of Hayashigaki (2000). The results enable future experimental searches for heavy mesic nuclei and provide a framework for studying the properties of heavy hadrons in finite nuclear systems, though the authors note that the $t\rho$ approximation used for the folding potential may break down if the $t$-matrix has a pole, as discussed by Garcia-Recio (2011).
