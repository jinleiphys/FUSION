# Confinement-induced p-wave resonances from s-wave interactions

> We show that a purely s-wave interaction in three dimensions (3D) can induce higher partial-wave resonances in mixed dimensions. We develop two-body scattering theories in all three cases of 0D-3D, 1D-3D, and 2D-3D mixtures and determine the positions of higher partial-wave resonances in terms of the 3D s-wave scattering length assuming a harmonic confinement potential. We also compute the low-energy scattering parameters in the p-wave channel (scattering volume and effective momentum) that are necessary for the low-energy effective theory of the p-wave resonance. We point out that some of the resonances observed in the Florence group experiment [Phys. Rev. Lett. 104, 153202 (2010)] can be interpreted as the p-wave resonances in the 2D-3D mixed dimensions. Our study paves the way for a variety of physics, such as Anderson localization of matter waves under p-wave resonant scatterers.

## Key claim
A purely s-wave interaction in three dimensions can induce higher partial-wave resonances (including p-wave) in mixed dimensions (0D-3D, 1D-3D, and 2D-3D) when one atomic species is confined by a harmonic potential, with the resonance positions well described by a simple approximate formula for mass ratios m_A/m_B ≳ 1.

## Method
The authors developed two-body scattering theories for all three mixed-dimension cases (0D-3D, 1D-3D, and 2D-3D) using the Schrodinger equation with a generalized Bethe-Peierls boundary condition characterized by the 3D s-wave scattering length and effective range. They solved integral equations numerically to determine resonance positions and low-energy scattering parameters (scattering volume and effective momentum) in each partial-wave channel, assuming a harmonic confinement potential on the lower-dimensional species and setting the effective range to zero for qualitative results.

## Key numbers
- For the 0D-3D mixture in the weak-coupling limit, p-wave (ℓ=1) resonances occur at critical values of (m_B/m_AB)(a_3D/l_ho) = -5.36, -15.5, -31.2, ...
- For the 0D-3D mixture in the weak-coupling limit, ℓ=2 resonances occur at (m_B/m_AB)(a_3D/l_ho) = -11.9, -25.6, -44.6, ...
- For the 0D-3D mixture in the weak-coupling limit, ℓ=3 resonances occur at (m_B/m_AB)(a_3D/l_ho) = -20.9, -37.9, -60.4, ...
- The binding energy of shallow p-wave molecules in the 0D-3D mixture near resonance is given by ε_AB = 1/(m_B a_eff r_eff) for ℓ ≥ 1.

## Context
This work extends the confinement-induced resonance concept from s-wave scattering in mixed dimensions, previously studied by Massignan and Nishida (2006) and Nishida (2008), to higher partial waves including p-wave, d-wave, and f-wave channels. The authors show that some resonances observed in the Florence group experiment (Lamporesi, 2010) can be interpreted as p-wave resonances in 2D-3D mixed dimensions. This enables the study of p-wave resonant phenomena such as Anderson localization of matter waves and provides a pathway to realize p-wave superfluids without the instability problems that plague conventional p-wave Feshbach resonances in 3D.
