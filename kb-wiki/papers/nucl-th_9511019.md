# Nuclear Compton scattering in the $Δ$-resonance region with polarized photons

> Nuclear Compton scattering in the $Δ$-resonance region is reconsidered within the framework of the $Δ$-hole model. The different role of the resonant and non-resonant contributions to the transition amplitudes is discussed and their effect is investigated by comparing the results of calculation with recent data also taken with polarized photons.

## Key claim

The paper reconsiders nuclear Compton scattering in the Δ-resonance region using a local-density approximation to the Δ-hole model, finding that the model reproduces data satisfactorily up to about 350 MeV but fails at backward angles and lower energies, where non-resonant background contributions are not correctly treated.

## Method

The authors adopt a local-density approximation to the Δ-hole Green's function, splitting the transition operator into a resonant part (direct Δ-hole excitation plus crossed Δ-hole excitation) and a non-resonant background part (s-wave pion production via Kroll-Ruderman term and a two-photon contact interaction). The model is applied to zero-spin nuclei (⁴He and ¹²C) using nuclear-matter densities derived from charge form factors, and the resulting Compton scattering amplitudes are compared with unpolarized and polarized photon data.

## Key numbers

- Δ mass M_Δ = 1238 MeV used in the Δ-hole Green's function
- γNΔ coupling constant f_γ = 0.122 (dimensionless, with pion mass m_π)
- Pion-nucleon coupling f²/4π = 0.08
- Fine-structure constant α = 1/137
- Form factor cutoff parameter β = 300 MeV for the off-shell pion form factor
- Data for ¹²C at scattering angle θ = 40° in photon energy range 200–500 MeV, with momentum transfer reaching q ≈ 1.7 fm⁻¹ at the highest energy
- Data for ⁴He from the LEGS collaboration at Brookhaven in energy range 200–310 MeV at five incoming energies

## Context

This work extends the Δ-hole model approach previously applied to pion scattering and coherent π⁰ photoproduction (Hirata 1979, Lenz 1979, Kara 1985) to Compton scattering with polarized photons, following earlier calculations by Koch, Moniz, and Oset (1984) and Osterfeld (1992). The local-density approximation used here was developed by Salcedo (1988) and Garcia (1990) for photon absorption and pion-nucleus scattering. The new polarized photon data from the LEGS collaboration (Schaerf 1995) allow separation of the two structure functions W_T and W_TT, which provides a more detailed test of resonant versus background contributions than unpolarized measurements alone. The results enable future improvements in modeling the non-resonant background, particularly at backward angles and energies below the Δ peak.
