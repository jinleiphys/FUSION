# Flavor content of the nucleon in an unquenched quark model

> We discuss the flavor content of the nucleon in an unquenched quark model in which the effects of quark-antiquark pairs (up, down and strange) are taken into account in an explicit form. It is shown that the inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs leads to an excess of anti-d over anti-u quarks in the proton and to a large contribution of orbital angular momentum to the spin of the proton.

# Flavor content of the nucleon in an unquenched quark model

## Key claim
The inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs (u u-bar, d d-bar, and s s-bar) in an unquenched quark model produces an excess of anti-d over anti-u quarks in the proton and a large orbital angular momentum contribution to the proton spin, but all parameters were taken from the literature with no attempt to optimize them to improve agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The authors extended the unquenched quark model of Geiger and Isgur to baryons by solving the permutation symmetry problem for identical quarks using group-theoretical techniques and constructing an algorithm to generate a complete set of intermediate states for any baryon or meson model expressed in the harmonic oscillator basis. Quark-antiquark pairs were created with the 3P0 quantum numbers of the vacuum, and the model was applied to calculate the integrated flavor asymmetry and the spin decomposition of the proton.

## Key numbers
- The Gottfried integral measured by NMC is 0.2281 ± 0.0065 at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² over the range 0.004 ≤ x ≤ 0.8, which implies a flavor-asymmetric sea.
- The unquenched quark model calculation reduces the Gottfried sum rule value of 1/3 by about one third, in qualitative agreement with the NMC result.
- The most recent experimental value for the quark contribution to the proton spin is 33.0 ± 3.9 %.
- In the unquenched quark model, q q-bar pairs contribute about half of the proton spin, of which one quarter is due to sea quark spin and three quarters to orbital angular momentum.

## Context
This work extends the unquenched quark model approach of Geiger and Isgur (1990s), who studied s s-bar loops in the proton and emphasized the need to include u u-bar and d d-bar loops to address flavor asymmetry and the spin crisis. The results are in qualitative agreement with the NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule violation and with the EMC finding that quarks carry only a small fraction of the proton spin. The model enables systematic future studies of baryon electromagnetic and strong couplings, elastic and transition form factors, and sea quark content, as noted by the authors for planned work.
