# Collective transition densities in neutron-rich nuclei

> Quadrupole transition densities in neutron-rich nuclei in the vicinity of the neutron drip-line are calculated in the framework of the Random Phase Approximation. The continuum is treated by expansion in oscillator functions. We focus on the states which contribute to the usual Giant Quadrupole Resonance, and not on the low-lying strength which is also expected in such nuclei and whose collective character is still under debate. We find that, due to the large neutron skin in these nuclei, the isoscalar and isovector modes are in general strongly mixed. We further show that the transition densities corresponding to the GQR states can be reasonably well described by the collective model in terms of in phase and out of phase oscillations of neutron and proton densities which have different radii.

## Key claim
The authors find that in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line (but not so weakly bound as to produce a halo), the isoscalar and isovector quadrupole modes are strongly mixed due to the neutron skin, and the transition densities of the Giant Quadrupole Resonance (GQR) states can be reasonably described by a collective model with in-phase and out-of-phase oscillations of neutron and proton densities having different radii. However, the collective description is only qualitative for the isovector-dominated states, which exhaust very small fractions of the energy-weighted sum rule.

## Method
Quadrupole transition densities and strength distributions were calculated using the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) with a spherical Hartree-Fock basis employing the Skyrme SGII interaction. The continuum was treated by expanding in oscillator functions, and the adequacy of this expansion was tested by varying the number of oscillator states. The calculations were performed for the neutron-rich nuclei ²⁸O and ⁶⁰Ca, and the resulting transition densities were compared with two collective model prescriptions: the Bohr-Mottelson model and the Tassie model.

## Key numbers
- The last neutron in ²⁸O is bound by 3.25 MeV; in ⁶⁰Ca it is bound by 5.1 MeV.
- The neutron skin (difference in root-mean-square radii) is about 0.6 fm for ²⁸O and 0.4 fm for ⁶⁰Ca.
- In ²⁸O, the state at 17.417 MeV exhausts 48.9% of the isoscalar EWSR and 14.4% of the isovector EWSR, with B(E2)ₚ = 28.39 e²fm⁴ and B(E2)ₙ = 399.32 e²fm⁴.
- In ⁶⁰Ca, the state at 15.315 MeV exhausts 44% of the isoscalar EWSR and 4% of the isovector EWSR, with B(E2)ₚ = 241.11 e²fm⁴ and B(E2)ₙ = 912.03 e²fm⁴.
- For the isoscalar GQR state in ²⁸O at 17.417 MeV, the Tassie model gives β₂ᵖ = 0.096 and β₂ⁿ = 0.099; the Bohr-Mottelson model gives β₂ᵖ = 0.098 and β₂ⁿ = 0.103.
- For the isovector-dominated state in ²⁸O at 30.968 MeV, the Tassie model gives β₂ᵖ = 0.088 and β₂ⁿ = -0.027; the Bohr-Mottelson model gives β₂ᵖ = 0.091 and β₂ⁿ = -0.029.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the isovector strength at the isoscalar GQR energy (around 11 MeV) is about 3%, close to the ratio ((N-Z)/A)² = 4%.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of collective excitations in neutron-rich nuclei, motivated by the prospects of radioactive beam facilities. It contrasts with the case of β-stable nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb (where isoscalar and isovector strengths are well separated) and with N=Z nuclei like ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca (where the modes are pure). The authors note that different choices of Skyrme interaction (as in earlier work by other authors) can lead to lower binding energies and significant low-lying strength, which they do not observe here. The findings enable future studies of Coulomb and nuclear inelastic excitation of neutron-rich nuclei, where the surface behavior of transition densities will be important.
