# Shell model description of normal parity bands in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei

> The low-energy spectra and B(E2) electromagnetic transition strengths of 159Eu, 159Tb and 159Dy are described using the pseudo SU(3) model. Normal parity bands are built as linear combinations of SU(3) states, which are the direct product of SU(3) proton and neutron states with pseudo spin zero (for even number of nucleons) and pseudo spin 1/2 (for odd number of nucleons). Each of the many-particle states have a well-defined particle number and total angular momentum. The Hamiltonian includes spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, the quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, as well as three rotor terms which are diagonal in the SU(3) basis. The pseudo SU(3) model is shown to be a powerful tool to describe odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei.

## Key claim
The pseudo SU(3) model can quantitatively describe normal parity bands in odd-mass heavy deformed nuclei using only a few SU(3) representations with the largest Casimir operator values and pseudo spin 0 or 1/2, but only when pairing interactions are included in the Hamiltonian; without pairing, the calculated spectra are strongly compressed and fail to reproduce the correct moments of inertia.

## Method
The authors constructed many-particle states for protons and neutrons in normal parity shells using the pseudo SU(3) coupling scheme, retaining only configurations with highest spatial symmetry (pseudo spin zero for even nucleon numbers, 1/2 for odd numbers). They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole and monopole pairing interactions with strengths taken from systematics, plus three rotor-like terms (K_J^2, J^2, and an asymmetry term) diagonal in the SU(3) basis, with only two parameters (a and b) fitted. The model was applied to 159Eu, 159Tb, and 159Dy, using 15 pseudo SU(3) irreps for 159Tb.

## Key numbers
- For 159Tb: 15 protons and 12 neutrons in the 50-82 and 82-126 shells, with 9 protons and 8 neutrons in normal parity orbitals (n^N_pi = 9, n^N_nu = 8).
- Effective charges used for B(E2) calculations: e_pi = 2.3, e_nu = 1.3.
- The ground state in 159Tb is 3/2^+, while in 159Eu it is 5/2^+, reproduced by the model with realistic single-particle energies.
- For 159Dy, the 17/2^- state in the K = 3/2 ground state band is predicted higher than experimentally observed, suggesting a possible band crossing.

## Context
This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model, previously applied to even-even nuclei (Draayer 1982, Beuschel 1998), to odd-mass rare-earth nuclei, building on early applications to odd-mass nuclei from nearly thirty years prior (Ratna Raju 1973). It demonstrates that pairing is essential even in the strongly truncated Hilbert space, and that the approach can describe both energy spectra and B(E2) transition strengths. The results open the possibility for microscopic descriptions of other properties in heavy deformed nuclei, including g-factors, M1 transitions, and beta decays.
