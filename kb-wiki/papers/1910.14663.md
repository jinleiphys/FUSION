# Searching for three-nucleon short-range correlations

> Three nucleon short range correlations~(SRCs) are one of the most elusive structures in nuclei. Their observation and the subsequent study of their internal makeup will have a significant impact on our understanding of the dynamics of super-dense nuclear matter which exists at the cores of neutron stars. We discuss the kinematic conditions and observables that are most favorable for probing 3N-SRCs in inclusive electro-nuclear processes and make a prediction for a quadratic dependence of the probabilities of finding a nucleon in 2N- and 3N- SRCs. We demonstrate that this prediction is consistent with the limited high energy experimental data available, suggesting that we have observed, for the first time, 3N-SRCs in electro-nuclear processes. Our analysis enables us to extract $a_3(A,Z)$, the probability of finding 3N-SRCs in nuclei relative to the A=3 system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three-nucleon short-range correlations (3N-SRCs) have been observed for the first time in inclusive electro-nuclear processes, but this claim is hedged by the fact that the experimental data at high momentum transfer (Q²) are limited and required a substitution of problematic ³He data points using a y-scaling fit from older SLAC measurements; the authors state the data are "consistent with" their prediction rather than providing a definitive confirmation.

## Method

The authors derived a kinematic condition for the onset of 3N-SRCs in inclusive A(e,e')X scattering using light-cone momentum fractions, identifying a threshold α₃N ≥ 1.6 above which 3N-SRCs should dominate over 2N-SRCs. They analyzed existing inclusive cross section ratios of ⁴He, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ⁶⁴Cu, and ¹⁹⁷Au to ³He from the JLab E02-109 experiment, replacing problematic ³He data points between x = 2.68 and x = 2.85 with a y-scaling function fit to SLAC data. They then compared the measured ratios in the 3N-SRC region (α₃N > 1.6) to the prediction that R₃ ≈ R₂², where R₂ is the ratio in the 2N-SRC plateau region.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for 3N-SRC dominance is set at α₃N ≥ 1.6, corresponding to internal longitudinal momenta ≥ 700 MeV/c.
- For ⁴He, the extracted a₃ parameter is 3.20 ± 0.28, compared to a₂ = 3.57 ± 0.09.
- For ¹²C, the extracted a₃ parameter is 5.71 ± 0.50, compared to a₂ = 4.65 ± 0.14.
- For ¹⁹⁷Au, the extracted a₃ parameter is 7.18 ± 0.64, compared to a₂ = 5.13 ± 0.21.
- The experimental R₃ for ⁴He is 2.74 ± 0.24, consistent with the prediction (a₂(⁴He)/a₂(³He))² = (1.68)² ≈ 2.82.
- The analysis uses data at Q² ≈ 2.5-2.7 GeV²; final state interactions are estimated to alter α₃N by no more than 8% at Q² ~ 3 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations from the well-established 2N-SRCs, first observed in inclusive ratios at SLAC by Frankfurt, Strikman, and collaborators (1993) and later confirmed at JLab by Fomin et al. (2011), to the more elusive 3N-SRCs. The prediction of a quadratic relation R₃ ≈ R₂² follows from the observed dominance of proton-neutron pairs in 2N-SRCs (Egiyan et al., 2006; Subedi et al., 2008) and the sequential two-step mechanism for 3N-SRCs. The extracted a₃ parameters provide input for modeling super-dense nuclear matter in neutron stars, and the authors call for confirmation by the forthcoming JLab 12 GeV experiment E12-06-105.
