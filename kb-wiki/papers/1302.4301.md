# Interaction for the trapped fermi gas from a unitary transformation of the exact two-body spectrum

> We study systems of few two-component fermions interacting in a Harmonic Oscillator trap. The fermion-fermion interaction is generated in a finite basis with a unitary transformation of the exact two-body spectrum given by the Busch formula. The few-body Schrödinger equation is solved with the formalism of the No-Core Shell Model. We present results for a system of three fermions interacting at unitarity as well as for finite values of the S-wave scattering length $a_2$ and effective range $r_2$. Unitary systems with four and five fermions are also considered. We show that the many-body energies obtained in this approach are in excellent agreement with exact solutions for the three-body problem, and results obtained by other methods in the other cases.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-body effective interaction constructed via a unitary transformation of the exact two-body spectrum (Busch formula) reproduces few-fermion energies in a harmonic oscillator trap with excellent agreement to exact solutions and other methods, but the convergence of the many-body energies depends on the size of both the two-body model space (cutoff \(N_2^{\rm{max}}\)) and the many-body model space (cutoff \(N_A^{\rm{max}}\)), with the method limited to small particle numbers (A=3,4,5) due to increasing antisymmetrization difficulty in Jacobi coordinates.

## Method

The two-body effective interaction was generated by applying a unitary transformation (Lee-Suzuki method) directly to the exact analytical two-body spectrum given by the Busch formula, which depends on the S-wave scattering length \(a_2\) and effective range \(r_2\). The few-body Schrödinger equation was solved using the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) formalism in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis constructed with Jacobi coordinates, with the model space truncated by a maximum number of HO quanta \(N_A^{\rm{max}}\). The effective Hamiltonian \(H^{\rm{eff}}_P\) was constructed from the lowest eigenenergies of the two-particle system in the trap, and the two-body effective interaction \(V^{\rm{eff}}_P\) was obtained by subtracting the HO potential.

## Key numbers

* Three-fermion ground state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 1^-\)): energy = \(2.7712\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=20\), compared to exact value \(2.7727\ \hbar\omega\) (Werner, 2006); at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=4\), energy = \(2.7657\ \hbar\omega\) (difference < 0.3%).
* Three-fermion first excited state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 1^-\)): energy = \(4.7732\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=20\), compared to exact value \(4.7727\ \hbar\omega\) (Werner, 2006); at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=6\), energy = \(4.7769\ \hbar\omega\) (difference < 0.1%).
* Three-fermion ground state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 0^+\)): energy = \(3.1640\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=22\), compared to exact value \(3.1662\ \hbar\omega\) (Werner, 2006).
* Three-fermion first excited state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 0^+\)): energy = \(5.1622\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=22\), compared to exact value \(5.1662\ \hbar\omega\) (Werner, 2006).
* Three-fermion ground state for \(b/a_2=1,\ r_2/b=0.1\) (\(L^\pi = 1^-\)): energy = \(1.8458\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=20\), compared to \(1.8443\ \hbar\omega\) from EFT at N\(^2\)LO (Rotureau et al., 2012).
* Three-fermion first excited state for \(b/a_2=1,\ r_2/b=0.1\) (\(L^\pi = 1^-\)): energy = \(3.8739\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=20\), compared to \(3.8680\ \hbar\omega\) from EFT at N\(^2\)LO (Rotureau et al., 2012).
* Four-fermion ground state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 0^+\)): energy = \(3.537\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=10\), compared to \(3.52\ \hbar\omega\) from EFT (Rotureau et al., 2012), \(3.509\ \hbar\omega\) (Stecher et al., 2007), \(3.6 \pm 0.1\ \hbar\omega\) from GFMC (Chang et al., 2004), \(3.551 \pm 0.009\ \hbar\omega\) (Blume et al., 2007), and \(3.545 \pm 0.003\ \hbar\omega\) (Alhassid et al., 2008).
* Four-fermion first excited state at unitarity (\(L^\pi = 0^+\)): energy = \(5.085\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=10\), compared to \(5.07\ \hbar\omega\) from EFT (Rotureau et al., 2012).
* Five-fermion ground state at unitarity: energy = \(6.036\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=4,\ N_5^{\rm{max}}=13\) and \(6.068\ \hbar\omega\) at \(N_2^{\rm{max}}=6,\ N_5^{\rm{max}}=13\), compared to \(5.9565(15)\ \hbar\omega\) from stochastic variational method (Blume et al., 2007) and \(6.1(1)\ \hbar\omega\) from GFMC (Chang et al., 2004).

## Context

This work extends the application of unitary transformation methods (Lee-Suzuki method) from nuclear physics to ultracold atomic Fermi gases, where the interaction is constructed directly from the model-independent Busch formula rather than from a bare potential. The approach is similar to the Configuration Interaction method of Alhassid (2008) but does not assume a separable form for the two-body interaction, and it complements earlier NCSM studies using Effective Field Theory interactions (Rotureau et al., 2012) by providing an alternative construction that reproduces the full two-body spectrum. The results enable systematic studies of few-fermion systems with finite scattering length and effective range, and the method could be extended to larger systems using Slater determinant bases, though the current Jacobi-coordinate approach is limited to A \(\sim\) 5-10.
