# The origin of correlation between mass and angle in quasi-fission

> Mass-angle distribution (MAD) measurement of heavy and superheavy element fragmentation reactions is one of the powerful tools for investigating the mechanism of fission and fusion process. MAD shows a strong correlation between mass and angle when the quasi-fission event is dominant. It has characteristic that appears diagonal correlation as long as the quasi-fission event is dominant. This diagonal correlation could not be reproduced in previous our model before the introduction of the parameters. In this study, we systematically evaluate the unknown model parameters contained in our model and clarify those model parameters to reproduce the diagonal correlation that appears in MAD. Using a dynamical model based on the fluctuation dissipation theorem that employs Langevin equations, we calculate MADs of two reaction systems $^{48}$Ti+$^{186}$W and $^{34}$S+$^{232}$Th which are dominated by quasi-fission. We were able to clarify the effects of unknown model parameters on the MAD. In addition, we identified the values of model parameters that can reproduce the correlation between mass and angle. As a result, it was found that the balance of tangential friction and moment of inertia values is important for the correlation between mass and angle.

## Key claim

The diagonal correlation between mass and angle in mass-angle distributions (MADs) for quasi-fission reactions can be reproduced by tuning two unknown model parameters, the tangential friction correction factor and the moment-of-inertia correction factor, but the specific parameter values that work depend on the initial mass asymmetry of the reaction system, meaning no single universal parameter set fits all systems.

## Method

The authors used a multidimensional Langevin equation based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to simulate trajectory calculations for two reaction systems: ⁴⁸Ti+¹⁸⁶W and ³⁴S+²³²Th. They employed a time-dependent potential that evolves from a diabatic to an adiabatic form, with three collective coordinates (distance between centers, fragment deformation, and mass asymmetry) plus rotational degrees of freedom. They systematically varied two correction factors, the tangential friction strength γₜ⁰ and the moment-of-inertia scaling factor f_ina, and computed MADs for each parameter combination to identify which values reproduce the experimental diagonal correlation.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ti+¹⁸⁶W at E_c.m. = 187.87 MeV, the diagonal correlation was reproduced with small values of both parameters (e.g., f_ina = 1.5 and γₜ⁰ = 0.5 or 1.0), while large γₜ⁰ (e.g., 10) caused loss of correlation.
- For ³⁴S+²³²Th at E_c.m. = 168.75 MeV, the diagonal correlation was reproduced only when both parameters were large (e.g., f_ina = 5.0 and γₜ⁰ = 10), unlike the ⁴⁸Ti+¹⁸⁶W case.
- The parameter scan covered f_ina from 1.5 to 5.0 and γₜ⁰ from 0.5 to 10 for both systems.
- The relaxation time τ used in the potential weighting function was 10⁻²¹ s, following prior work (Bertsch 1978, Cassing 1983, Diaz-Torres 2004).
- The shell damping energy E_d was set to 20 MeV, following Ignatyuk (1975).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Aritomo (2004) and the unified model of Zagrebaev (2005, 2007) by systematically evaluating two previously unknown model parameters that control tangential friction and the moment of inertia. The study directly addresses the failure of earlier versions of the model to reproduce the diagonal MAD correlation observed experimentally by Rietz (2013) for quasi-fission reactions. The results enable future systematic calculations to determine how these parameters depend on physical quantities such as initial mass asymmetry, potentially allowing predictive modeling of quasi-fission in unexplored reaction systems relevant to superheavy element synthesis.
