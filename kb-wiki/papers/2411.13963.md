# Proton-neutron pair correlations in neutron-rich nuclei

> [Background] Nuclear pairing is a well-established many-body correlation, particularly among like particles in a spin-singlet state. However, the strength of spin-triplet proton-neutron (pn) pairing in nuclei has remained a long-standing and unresolved issue. [Purpose] The relative strength of spin-triplet pn pairing compared to spin-singlet one is investigated by introducing and analyzing the polarizability of the response to pn pair transfers. [Method] The nuclear energy-density functional method is employed. The ground state of the target nucleus is described using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation, which accounts for the conventional superfluidity of like-particle pairs. The response to pn pair transfers is then analyzed using the pn quasiparticle random-phase approximation. [Results] The spin-singlet pn-pair correlation is strongest at $N=Z$ and decreases monotonically with the increasing number of excess neutrons, whereas the spin-triplet pn-pair correlation is shown to depend non-monotonically on the neutron number and can be enhanced in cases where the pn-pair transfers involving the $πj_> \otimes νj_<$ configuration occur at low energy. [Conclusions] The shell effect, which uniquely appears in spin-triplet pn-pair correlation, serves as a key indicator of the strength of pn-pair correlations.

## Key claim

The relative strength of spin-triplet proton-neutron (pn) pairing compared to spin-singlet pairing is investigated by introducing the static polarizability of the response to pn-pair transfers; the spin-triplet correlation depends non-monotonically on neutron number and can be enhanced in neutron-rich isotopes when the pn-pair removal modes involving the πj_> ⊗ νj_< configuration occur at low energy, but the paper does not claim definitive experimental evidence for spin-triplet pair condensation.

## Method

The nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) method was employed, with ground states of target nuclei described using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation accounting for like-particle superfluidity. The response to pn-pair transfers was analyzed using the pn quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) to compute the static polarizability α_ST for spin-singlet (S=0,T=1) and spin-triplet (S=1,T=0) pn pairs. The ratio R_01 = 3 α_01 / α_10 was introduced to compare the relative strength of the two pairing channels, with calculations performed for Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes using the Skyrme-type SGII functional and a density-dependent pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca with f=1.3, the lowest 1⁺ state is lower than the 0⁺ state by 0.6 MeV for pn-pair addition and 0.2 MeV for pn-pair removal, and the R_01 value is about 0.5, indicating the system is close to the critical point of S=1 pn-pair condensation.
- In Ca isotopes, the S=1 pn-pair polarizability shows a peak at N=Z and drops with increasing neutron number, but persists around N=30 due to the νf_5/2 orbital near the Fermi level.
- In Ni isotopes, the S=1 pn-pair polarizability increases toward N=40, and in Sn isotopes it keeps almost constant up to N=64, with R_01 showing a minimum at N=40 and N=64 respectively.
- More than 90% of the polarizability for ⁴⁰Ca is given by states up to 25 MeV, with low-lying states below 15 MeV being dominant.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that focused primarily on N≈Z nuclei (Goodman, 1999; Gezerlis, 2011) by exploring pn-pair correlations across a wide mass region from calcium to tin isotopes, extending from the N=Z line to the neutron-rich side. The approach builds on the concept of pair polarizability recently introduced for two-neutron transfers by Takahashi (2023), and the shell effect uniquely appearing in spin-triplet pn-pairing serves as a key indicator of pn-pair correlation strength. The results enable future experimental studies using pn-transfer reactions such as (p,³He) and (³He,p) to probe the relative strength of spin-triplet versus spin-singlet pairing in neutron-rich nuclei.
