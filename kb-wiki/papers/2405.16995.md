# Electron form factors in Basis Light-front Quantization

> In this paper, we evaluate the electromagnetic and gravitational form factors as well as the corresponding generalized parton distributions of the electron using the Basis Light-front Quantization approach to QED. We compare our results with those from light-front perturbation theory. We adopt a novel basis with its scale depending on the constituents' longitudinal momentum fraction. We show that this basis improves convergence of the form factors with increasing basis dimension, compared to that calculated in the original basis with fixed scale. These results both validate the BLFQ approach and provide guidance for its efficient implementation in solving light-front Hamiltonian mass eigenstates for more complex systems in QED and QCD.

# Electron form factors in Basis Light-front Quantization

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a basis with a scale parameter that depends on the constituents' longitudinal momentum fraction (x-dependent b basis) improves convergence of electromagnetic and gravitational form factors compared to the original fixed-scale basis, but this advantage is most pronounced at low momentum transfer (-t) and the results from both bases converge to the same infinite-basis limit.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem for the physical electron in QED using Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) with Fock space truncated to the |e⟩ and |eγ⟩ sectors. They compute electromagnetic form factors (Dirac F₁ and Pauli F₂) and gravitational form factors (A and B) from the light-front wave functions obtained after diagonalization, applying mass renormalization to fix the physical electron mass at 0.511 MeV and wave function renormalization to correct for Ward identity violation from the Fock sector truncation. Results are compared with light-front perturbation theory using matching ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs estimated from the basis parameters.

## Key numbers

- Physical electron mass set to M_e = 0.511 MeV, used as the constant b' in the x-dependent basis scale parameter b_i = M_e √x_i
- Coupling strength α = 1/137.036
- To achieve a_e ≈ 0.00102 (about 88% of the Schwinger value α²/(2π)), the fixed-b basis requires N_max = 80 (basis dimension 5,182,401) while the x-dependent b basis requires only N_max = 32 (basis dimension 134,913)
- The anomalous magnetic moment a_e ≡ F₂(t→0) extrapolated to infinite basis agrees with the Schwinger result from perturbation theory
- B(t→0) from BLFQ is always zero independent of N_max

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ calculations of the electron system in QED (Zhao 2012, PhysRevD.89.116004, PhysRevD.91.105009) by introducing the x-dependent basis and computing gravitational form factors alongside electromagnetic ones. The electron serves as a benchmark for generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that are key goals of upcoming electron-ion colliders (Accardi 2016, Kalantarians 2014). The improved convergence with the x-dependent basis is particularly relevant for extending BLFQ to QCD systems with more than two constituents, such as the proton, where the Talmi-Moshinsky transformation for separating center-of-mass motion becomes impractical with the fixed-b basis.
