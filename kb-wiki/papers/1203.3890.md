# Asymptotic normalization coefficients and continuum coupling in mirror nuclei

> Background: An asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) characterizes the asymptotic form of a one-nucleon overlap integral required for description of nucleon-removal reactions. Purpose: We investigate the impact of the particle continuum on proton and neutron ANCs for mirror systems from $p$- and $sd$-shell regions. Method: We use the real-energy and complex-energy continuum shell model approaches. Results: We studied the general structure of the single-particle ANCs as a function of the binding energy and orbital angular momentum. We computed ANCs in mirror nuclei for different physical situations, including capture reactions to weakly-bound and unbound states. Conclusions: We demonstrated that the single-particle ANCs exhibit generic behavior that is different for charged and neutral particles. We verified the previously proposed relation [N.K. Timofeyuk, R.C. Johnson, and A.M. Mukhamedzhanov, Phys. Rev. Lett. 91, 232501 (2003); Phys. Rev. Lett. 97, 069904(E) (2006); N. K. Timofeyuk and P. Descouvemont, Phys. Rev. C 72, 064324 (2005)] between proton and neutron mirror ANCs. We find minor modifications if the spectroscopic strength is either localized in a single state or broadly distributed. For cases when several states couple strongly to the decay channel, these modifications may reach 30%.

## Key claim

The previously proposed relation between proton and neutron mirror asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) is verified to be largely approach-independent and interaction-independent, but modifications reaching 30% can occur when several states couple strongly to the decay channel, rather than when spectroscopic strength is localized in a single state or broadly distributed.

## Method

The authors employed two continuum shell model approaches: the real-energy Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) and the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model (GSM). They computed single-particle ANCs (SPANCs) for bound proton and neutron states in Woods-Saxon potentials, and calculated many-body ANCs for mirror pairs in the p-shell (systems around ⁶Li/⁷Be, ⁷Be/⁸B) and sd-shell (¹⁶O/¹⁷F, ¹⁶O/¹⁷O) regions using the Berggren ensemble for the single-particle basis.

## Key numbers

- For the 1s₁/₂ proton state in ¹⁷F, the asymptotic behavior of the SPANC ratio β/β̃ is reached at η ≈ 1; for the 0d₅/₂ state, it is reached at η ≈ 10.
- The 5/2⁺ ground state of ²³Al has a proton separation energy of S_p = 141 keV and a very large value of β_{ℓ,j}.
- For neutron SPANCs with ℓ=0, the coefficient a_{ℓ=0,1/2}^{(0)} becomes √2 in the limit of zero binding.
- For ℓ=1, j=1/2 neutron states, the coefficient a_{ℓ,j}^{(0)} takes values 1.549, 1.612, and 1.681 for Woods-Saxon diffuseness d = 0.46 fm, 0.58 fm, and 0.7 fm, respectively.
- Modifications to the mirror ANC ratio ℛ can reach 30% when several states couple strongly to the decay channel.

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation proposed by Timofeyuk, Johnson, and Mukhamedzhanov (2003) and Timofeyuk and Descouvemont (2005) linking proton and neutron mirror ANCs via charge symmetry of the nuclear force. The study uses continuum shell model frameworks (GSM and SMEC) to test the robustness of this relation under realistic treatment of configuration mixing and continuum coupling, particularly for near-threshold states where binding energy effects and different asymptotic behavior of neutron and proton wave functions become important. The results enable more reliable extraction of information about unbound proton-rich states from transfer reaction studies in their bound mirror partners.
