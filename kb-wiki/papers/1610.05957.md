# Two-photon-exchange effects in the unpolarized $μp$ scattering within the hadronic model

> In this work, the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effects in the unpolarized $μp$ scattering are discussed within the hadronic model where the intermediate states $N,Δ$ and $σ$ are considered. The contribution from the $N$ intermediate is close to the results given by Ref. \cite{Tomalak2014} at the small $Q$ and there is a sizeable difference when $Q>0.25$GeV (where $Q^2$ is the four momentum transfer). The contributions from the $Δ$ and the $σ$ intermediate states are much smaller than that from the $N$ intermediate at the small $Q$. In the kinematic region with $k_i\subseteq [0.01,0.3]$ GeV and $Q \leq0.4$GeV (where $k_i$ is the three momentum of initial muon at Lab frame), a naive expression for the TPE contributions is given, which can be used directly for other analysis.

## Key claim
The two-photon-exchange (TPE) corrections to unpolarized muon-proton scattering are dominated by the nucleon (N) intermediate state at low momentum transfer, with contributions from the Delta and sigma intermediate states being much smaller (below 0.05%); however, the N-intermediate contribution shows a sizeable dependence on the choice of nucleon form factors when Q > 0.25 GeV, and the sigma-meson contribution estimated via a loop-based method decreases more quickly with Q² than previous estimates by Koshchii (2016).

## Method
The TPE amplitudes were calculated within a hadronic model using effective vertices for the N, Δ, and σ intermediate states. For the N and Δ states in the s- and u-channels, the amplitudes were evaluated using one-loop diagrams with improved form factors for the γNN vertex and standard parametrizations for the γNΔ vertex. For the σ meson exchange in the t-channel, the contribution was estimated via two-loop diagrams involving pion and proton loops, using effective σππ and σNN couplings matched to baryon chiral perturbation theory, with numerical integration performed using LoopTools and FIESTA4.

## Key numbers
- The TPE correction from the N intermediate state, δ^(N)_μp, was fitted to a naive formula valid for k_i in [0.01, 0.3] GeV and Q ≤ 0.4 GeV, with fitted coefficients c1^(N)=15.2205, c4^(N)=52.5231, c7^(N)=91.8465 (units: GeV powers as appropriate).
- The TPE correction from the Δ intermediate state, δ^(Δ)_μp, is about 2% of the N-intermediate correction at k_i=0.21 GeV and Q~0.22 GeV.
- The effective coupling g_σμμ^(π) at small Q² is parametrized with coefficients c0^(π)=5.2770, c1^(π)=-28.7494, c2^(π)=67.1914, c3^(π)=-64.4362 (Q in GeV).
- The effective coupling g_σμμ^(N) at small Q² is parametrized with coefficients c0^(N)=1.0755, c1^(N)=-4.7336, c2^(N)=12.3169, c3^(N)=-14.7901 (Q in GeV).
- At Q²=0.16 GeV², the g_σμμ value from this work is about half of that given by Koshchii (2016) (shaded region).

## Context
This work extends the hadronic-model calculations of TPE effects in μp scattering previously performed by Tomalak (2014) and Dian-Yong Chen (2013) for the N intermediate state, and by Koshchii (2016) for the σ meson exchange. It improves upon prior work by using more realistic form factors for the γNN vertex and by calculating the σ contribution via a loop-based method that accounts for the Q² dependence of the σγγ coupling, rather than using a constant coupling from the decay width. The results provide a naive fitting formula for the TPE corrections that can be directly used in the analysis of the upcoming MUSE experiment, which aims to measure the proton electromagnetic form factors via elastic unpolarized μp scattering at low momentum transfer.
