# Generalised Planar Black Holes and the Holography of Hydrodynamic Shear

> AdS black holes with planar event horizon topology play a central role in AdS/CFT holography, and particularly in its applications. Generalisations of the known planar black holes can be found by considering the Plebanski--Demianski metrics, a very general family of exactly specified solutions of the Einstein equations. These generalised planar black holes may be useful in applications. We give a concrete example of this in the context of the holographic description of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). We argue that our generalised planar black holes allow us to construct a model of the internal shearing motion generated when the QGP is produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. When embedded in string theory, the bulk physics is in fact unstable. We find however that this instability may develop too slowly to affect the evolution of the plasma, except possibly for high values of the quark chemical potential, such as will be studied in experimental scans of the quark matter phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper argues that generalised planar black holes derived from the Plebanski–Demianski family can model the internal shearing motion of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, but the bulk physics is unstable due to string-theoretic effects; however, this instability may develop too slowly to affect the plasma evolution except possibly at high quark chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors start from the Plebanski–Demianski family of Petrov Type D metrics and impose conditions to obtain a planar black hole geometry (the ℓQKMV₀ metric) that induces a conformally flat boundary metric with a velocity profile matching the shape expected for shearing QGP far from the collision axis. They then analyse the stability of the bulk geometry by considering brane-production instabilities characteristic of string theory, and estimate the timescale for this instability to develop relative to the QGP lifetime.

## Key numbers

- The velocity profile at infinity is given by \(v(x) = \omega_\infty(x)L\), with the maximal velocity \(V\) defined by \(\ell^2 = V|a|L\), where \(\ell\) is a new length-scale parameter.
- The second derivative of the velocity profile is \(\frac{d^2v}{dx^2} = -\frac{2|a|}{L^3}\left[3\left(v - \frac{V}{3}\right)^2 + \left(1 - \frac{V^2}{3}\right)\right]\), which does not vanish, confirming classical stability.
- The asymptotic AdS curvature scale \(L\) is chosen to be of order 10 femtometres, consistent with nuclear physics magnitudes.
- The angular velocity of the event horizon is \(\omega(r_{\rm H}) = \frac{a}{r_{\rm H}^2 + \ell^2}\), independent of position on the horizon.

## Context

This work extends the KMV₀ and QKMV₀ planar black hole geometries of Klemm, Moretti and Vanzo (2001) by introducing a new parameter \(\ell\) that gives a velocity profile of the type shown in Figure 3, complementing the profiles from the earlier metrics. The authors connect their holographic model to the classical hydrodynamic approach of Kelvin–Helmholtz instability in the QGP studied by KelvinHelm and viscous (2013), and note that the string-theoretic instability they find may become important at high chemical potentials relevant for beam energy scan programmes at RHIC, SHINE, FAIR, and NICA.
