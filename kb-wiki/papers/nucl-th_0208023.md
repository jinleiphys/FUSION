# Three-Nucleon Forces from Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We perform the first complete analysis of nd scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory including the corresponding three-nucleon force and extending our previous work, where only the two-nucleon interaction has been taken into account. The three-nucleon force appears first at this order in the chiral expansion and depends on two unknown parameters. These two parameters are determined from the triton binding energy and the nd doublet scattering length. We find an improved description of various scattering observables in relation to the next-to-leading order results especially at moderate energies (E_lab = 65 MeV). It is demonstrated that the long-standing A_y-problem in nd elastic scattering is still not solved by the leading 3NF, although some visible improvement is observed. We discuss possibilities of solving this puzzle. The predicted binding energy for the alpha-particle agrees with the empirical value.

## Key claim

The leading three-nucleon force (3NF) at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, which depends on two unknown low-energy constants, improves the description of nd scattering observables compared to next-to-leading order results, but does not solve the long-standing A_y-problem in nd elastic scattering, although some visible improvement is observed.

## Method

The authors performed a complete analysis of nd scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, including the corresponding three-nucleon force derived from chiral Lagrangians. The two unknown low-energy constants c_D and c_E were determined by fitting to the triton binding energy and the nd doublet scattering length, using np-corrected pseudo-experimental values. The Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state and scattering were solved in momentum space with a partial wave decomposition, and the alpha-particle binding energy was computed using Yakubovsky equations.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy was fixed to the np-corrected pseudo-experimental value of 8.68 MeV
- The nd doublet scattering length was fixed to the np-corrected pseudo-experimental value of 0.45 ± 0.04 fm
- For cut-off Λ = 500 MeV: c_D = 3.6, c_E = 0.37
- For cut-off Λ = 600 MeV: c_D = 1.8, c_E = -0.11
- The alpha-particle binding energy was predicted to agree with the empirical value of 28.3 MeV, after applying a Coulomb and np correction of 1.5 ± 0.1 MeV to give a pseudo-experimental value of 29.8 ± 0.1 MeV
- Improved description of scattering observables was found especially at E_lab = 65 MeV
- The 3N binding energy calculations were converged to 2 keV accuracy, and 4N calculations to 20 keV accuracy

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous next-to-leading order analysis (Epelbaum et al., 2001) which included only the two-nucleon interaction and found significant deviations from data at 65 MeV. The chiral 3NF at next-to-next-to-leading order was first derived qualitatively by Weinberg (1991) and later by van Kolck (1994), who showed the leading contribution cancels at next-to-leading order. The present complete analysis demonstrates that the Phillips line correlation between triton binding energy and nd doublet scattering length is broken by the chiral 3NF, enabling determination of both low-energy constants. The predicted alpha-particle binding energy agrees with the empirical value, and the results enable future work on solving the A_y-puzzle, possibly through inclusion of delta-resonance degrees of freedom or higher-order contributions.
