# Heavy Flavor at the Large Hadron Collider in a Strong Coupling Approach

> Employing nonperturbative transport coefficients for heavy-flavor (HF) diffusion through quark-gluon plasma (QGP), hadronization and hadronic matter, we compute $D$- and $B$-meson observables in Pb+Pb ($\sqrt{s}$=2.76\,TeV) collisions at the LHC. Elastic heavy-quark scattering in the QGP is evaluated within a thermodynamic $T$-matrix approach, generating resonances close to the critical temperature which are utilized for recombination into $D$ and $B$ mesons, followed by hadronic diffusion using effective hadronic scattering amplitudes. The transport coefficients are implemented via Fokker-Planck Langevin dynamics within hydrodynamic simulations of the bulk medium in nuclear collisions. The hydro expansion is quantitatively constrained by transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow of light hadrons. Our approach thus incorporates the paradigm of a strongly coupled medium in both bulk and HF dynamics throughout the thermal evolution of the system.

# Heavy Flavor at the Large Hadron Collider in a Strong Coupling Approach

## Key claim

The paper claims that a nonperturbative transport model incorporating strong coupling in both bulk medium evolution and heavy-flavor dynamics can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of D and B mesons at LHC energies, but only if resonance recombination and hadronic diffusion near the critical temperature are included; the purely elastic treatment of heavy-flavor interactions appears to lack sufficient path-length dependence at high transverse momentum, suggesting radiative processes may be needed.

## Method

The authors compute heavy-quark diffusion through quark-gluon plasma using Fokker-Planck Langevin dynamics with transport coefficients from a thermodynamic T-matrix approach, which generates resonance structures near the critical temperature. Heavy quarks are hadronized via resonance recombination with light quarks, with the remaining quarks undergoing fragmentation using FONLL calculations. The hadronic diffusion of D and B mesons is continued using effective scattering amplitudes off pions, kaons, and other hadrons, all embedded in a hydrodynamically expanding medium (AZHYDRO code) tuned to reproduce charged-hadron spectra and elliptic flow at LHC.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark relaxation rates from the T-matrix approach are enhanced over leading-order perturbative calculations (with alpha_s=0.4) by up to a factor of approximately 5 at low momenta and temperatures close to T_c, reduced to a factor of 2.5-3 at 2 T_c
- The heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient D_s is approximately 3-4/(2 pi T) around the pseudo-critical temperature T_pc=170 MeV
- D-meson R_AA shows a flow bump at p_T approximately 1.5 GeV due to resonance recombination
- B-meson R_AA is approximately 0.5 for p_T greater than 10 GeV, with a flat suppression
- B-meson v_2 reaches up to 7.5% at intermediate p_T
- Bottom-to-charm cross section ratio of approximately 0.05 reproduces the ALICE data for the bottom fraction of single electrons
- Bottom contribution exceeds charm contribution for electron momenta above p_T approximately 6 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier transport approach (He 2011, He 2012) from RHIC to LHC energies, implementing a strong-coupling scheme in both micro- and macrophysics of QGP and hadronic matter. It contrasts with perturbative approaches (Svetitsky 1987) and other transport models (Moore 2004, Gossiaux 2009, Uphoff 2011) by using nonperturbative T-matrix interactions for both heavy-light quark and heavy quark-gluon scattering. The approach enables quantitative tests of heavy-flavor transport properties at low and intermediate p_T, while pointing toward the need for radiative energy loss mechanisms at high p_T, which remains a challenge for implementation in transport frameworks.
