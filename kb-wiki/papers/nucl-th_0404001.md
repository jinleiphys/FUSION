# New generation of neutron decay experiments

> New generation of neutron decay experiments, which can be done with high accuracy at new Spallation Neutron Sources, are very important for solution of current problems of fundamental physics and for tests of the Standard Model. The open problems of theory related to these experiments and possible approaches to their solutions are discussed.

# New generation of neutron decay experiments

## Key claim

Precise neutron decay measurements at new Spallation Neutron Sources can test the Standard Model and resolve the CKM unitarity problem, but the standard approach to radiative corrections cannot extract the hadronic model-dependent parts from any set of neutron decay experiments alone, because those corrections renormalize the axial-vector and vector coupling constants in a way that leaves all measurable correlation coefficients unchanged in first-order approximation.

## Method

The paper analyzes the theoretical framework for extracting the CKM matrix element \(V_{ud}\) and the axial-vector coupling ratio \(\lambda\) from neutron beta-decay measurements, including the total decay rate and the angular correlation coefficients \(a\), \(A\), \(B\), and \(D\). It examines the standard separation of radiative corrections into "outer" (energy-dependent, calculable) and "inner" (hadron structure-dependent) parts, and demonstrates that in first-order approximation the strong-interaction contributions renormalize the vector and axial-vector couplings by energy-independent parameters \(a_V\) and \(a_A\), making it impossible to determine the unrenormalized \(\lambda\) or to experimentally constrain the model-dependent corrections from any combination of neutron decay observables.

## Key numbers

- Current \(V_{ud}\) from nuclear \(0^+ \rightarrow 0^+\) beta-decay: \(0.9740 \pm 0.0005\)
- Current \(V_{ud}\) from neutron beta-decay: \(0.9713 \pm 0.0014\)
- \(V_{ud}\) from unitarity constraint (using \(V_{us}=0.2196\pm0.0023\) and \(V_{ub}=0.0036\pm0.0007\)): \(0.9756 \pm 0.0004\)
- Energy dependence of hadronic structure corrections in second-order approximation: extremely small, about \(10^{-5}\) to \(10^{-6}\)
- Possible new physics contributions to unitarity equation: at the level of \(10^{-2}\) to \(10^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of radiative corrections for neutron decay beyond the well-studied case of nuclear Fermi transitions (Towner and Hardy, 2003) and addresses the unitarity problem of the CKM matrix. The paper shows that the standard QCD-based approach cannot resolve the model-dependent parts of radiative corrections from neutron decay data alone, motivating alternative theoretical approaches such as effective field theory (EFT), which has been applied to neutron decay radiative corrections in a recent paper (Ando et al., 2004). The results are directly relevant to planned experiments at Spallation Neutron Sources and to neutrino astrophysics, particularly the analysis of SNO data requiring precise neutrino-deuteron cross sections dominated by Gamow-Teller transitions.
