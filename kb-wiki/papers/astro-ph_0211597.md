# Strangelets as Cosmic Rays beyond the Greisen-Zatsepin-Kuzmin Cutoff

> Strangelets (stable lumps of quark matter) can have masses and charges much higher than those of nuclei, but have very low charge-to-mass ratios. This is confirmed in a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model. The high charge allows astrophysical strangelet acceleration to energies orders of magnitude higher than for protons. In addition, strangelets are much less susceptible to the interactions with the cosmic microwave background that suppress the flux of cosmic ray protons and nuclei above energies of $10^{19}$--$10^{20}$ eV (the GZK-cutoff). This makes strangelets an interesting possibility for explaining ultra-high energy cosmic rays.

# Strangelets as Cosmic Rays beyond the Greisen-Zatsepin-Kuzmin Cutoff

## Key claim
Strangelets (stable lumps of quark matter) are presented as a candidate for ultra-high energy cosmic rays above the GZK-cutoff, but the authors hedge that this possibility depends on the robustness of phenomenological models like the MIT bag model, and that many details of strangelet stability, charge-mass relationships, and disintegration remain at a very crude level.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model to calculate the net screened charge of strangelets, following a procedure similar to that adopted for nuclei in earlier work. They compared the charge-to-mass ratios, acceleration limits, and energy loss mechanisms (photo-pion production, photo-disintegration, and photo-pair production) for ordinary strangelets and color-flavor locked strangelets against those for protons and nuclei. The analysis also considered Larmor radius in the galactic magnetic field and atmospheric fragmentation column densities.

## Key numbers
- For protons, the GZK-cutoff from photo-pion production sets in at energies above approximately $10^{20}$ eV (with detailed calculations showing the drop sets in at energies more than an order of magnitude smaller).
- For nuclei, the photo-disintegration cutoff energy is approximately $10^{19}A$ eV, and photo-pair production sets in at approximately $10^{18}A$ eV.
- Ordinary strangelets have charge $Z \approx 0.1A$ for $A \ll 150$ and $Z \approx 8A^{1/3}$ for $A \gg 150$.
- Color-flavor locked strangelets have $Z \approx 0.3A^{2/3}$.
- The maximum baryon number for a gravitationally unstable strange star is $A_{\max} \approx 2 \times 10^{57}$.
- The maximum unscreened point charge in quantum electrodynamics is $1/\alpha \approx 137$.
- The Larmor radius in the galactic magnetic field ($B \approx 3 \mu G$) is $r_{\mathrm{gyro}} = 36 \mathrm{kpc} (E/10^{20} \mathrm{eV}) (3 \mu G/B) Z^{-1}$, compared to a galactic radius of approximately 10 kpc.
- The critical column density for strangelet fragmentation is $x \approx 4 \times 10^{-12} E_{B10} E_{20}^{-1} (\rho_s / \rho_n)^{2/3} A^{4/3}$ grams per cm$^2$, where $E_{B10}$ is binding energy per baryon in units of 10 MeV and $E_{20}$ is kinetic energy in units of $10^{20}$ eV.
- A non-relativistic strangelet moving at 300 km/sec would have kinetic energy in the interesting range if its baryon number were of order $10^{17}$ (mass of 0.1 microgram).

## Context
This work extends the discussion of strange quark matter stability, which was revived by Bodmer (1971) and reviewed by Madsen, and by recent Chandra X-ray observations of pulsars and predictions of increased stability in a color-flavor locked phase by Alford (2001). The paper contradicts the standard GZK-cutoff explanation for ultra-high energy cosmic rays, which was originally suggested by Greisen, Zatsepin and Kuzmin, and addresses the puzzle that observed cosmic ray energies reach as high as $3 \times 10^{20}$ eV. The work enables future searches for cosmic ray strangelets with the AMS-02 experiment on the International Space Station starting in 2005, and calls for detailed simulations of strangelet air showers and direct measurements of charge and mass of ultra-high energy cosmic ray primaries.
