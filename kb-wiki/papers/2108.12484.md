# Energy dependent ratios of level-density parameters in superheavy nuclei

> The nuclear level densities and level-density parameters in fissioning nuclei at their saddle points of fission barriers - $a_{f}$, as well as those for neutron - $a_{n}$, proton - $a_{p}$ , and $α$-particle - $a_α$ emission residues at the ground states are calculated for isotopic chains of superheavy nuclei with $Z$=112-120. The calculations are performed with the superfluid formalism using the single-particle energies obtained from the diagonalization of the deformed Woods-Saxon potential. Spectra were generated at global minima of the adiabatic potential energy surfaces, found by the multidimensional minimization method, and at the proper saddle points, found by the "immersion water flow" technique on multidimensional energy grids, with allowed the reflection and axial symmetry breaking. The influence of shell effects on the energy dependence of the ratios of level-density parameters corresponding to residues of the considered decay modes to those of neutron emission is studied. We have shown that, in contrast to the $a_{f}/a_{n}$ ratio, the $a_{p}/a_{n}$ and $a_α/a_{n}$ ratios do not show characteristic maxima depending on the excitation energy of the compound nucleus being formed. In the case of alpha decay, we identified the collective enhancement caused by cluster degrees of freedom to play quite an important role. The energetic course of the variability of the level density parameters before reaching the asymptotic value, not taken into account so far, will be of great importance for the estimation of the probabilities of de-excitation cascades via light particles emission in competition with splitting and thus for the determination of the survival probabilities and finally for the total production cross-sections of superheavy nuclei in channels with their (light particles) participation.

# Energy dependent ratios of level-density parameters in superheavy nuclei

## Key claim

The ratios of level-density parameters for proton and alpha-particle emission residues to those for neutron emission residues do not show characteristic maxima as a function of excitation energy, in contrast to the ratio for fission saddle-point to neutron-emission residues. However, when collective enhancement from cluster degrees of freedom is included for alpha decay, the ratio a_alpha/a_n can develop a maximum in the 20-30 MeV energy range and reach asymptotic values larger than unity.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear level densities and level-density parameters for superheavy nuclei with Z=112-120 using the thermodynamic superfluid formalism. Single-particle energies were obtained from diagonalization of a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with ground-state minima found by multidimensional minimization and saddle points found by the immersion water flow technique on multidimensional energy grids allowing reflection and axial symmetry breaking. The calculated intrinsic level densities were fitted with the back-shifted Fermi gas expression to extract energy-dependent level-density parameters a(U) for fissioning nuclei at saddle points (a_f) and for residues after neutron (a_n), proton (a_p), and alpha-particle (a_alpha) emission at ground states.

## Key numbers

- Level-density parameter ratios a_f/a_n reach asymptotic values less than 1.1 for all studied isotopes
- Level-density parameter ratios a_p/a_n and a_alpha/a_n reach asymptotic values between 0.95 and 1 without collective enhancement
- For the Og isotopic chain, the maximum of a_f/a_n occurs at approximately 15 MeV for 296Og, with the excitation function maximum expected at U approximately 15 MeV for the 1n-channel and U approximately 25 MeV for the 2n-channel
- For 292Fl, a variation of the proton emission barrier by plus or minus 1.5 MeV produces about plus or minus 10 percent deviation in a_p/a_n at 20 MeV excitation energy
- For 292Fl, a variation of the alpha-particle emission barrier by plus or minus 1.5 MeV produces about plus or minus 8 percent difference in a_alpha/a_n at 20 MeV excitation energy
- With collective enhancement included, the enhancement factor K_alpha(beta) varies between 10 and 50, with an effective value of approximately 20 in the energy range of interest, using hbar*omega_ma = 0.5 MeV and hbar*omega_b = 0.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of level densities in superheavy nuclei by Rahmatinejad et al. (2021) using the same mass tables from Jachimowicz et al. (2021), now focusing on the energy dependence of ratios between different decay channels. The superfluid formalism was previously validated against experimental level densities for Dy and Mo isotopes by Rahmatinejad et al. (2020). The results enable improved estimation of survival probabilities and total production cross-sections for superheavy nuclei, particularly for 1n and 2n evaporation channels where the system is far from asymptotic level-density parameter values and where the difference between a_f and a_n is most important.
