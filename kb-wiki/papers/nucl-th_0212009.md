# Three-Nucleon Electroweak Capture Reactions

> Recent advances in the study of the p-d radiative and mu-3he weak capture processes are presented and discussed. The three-nucleon bound and scattering states are obtained using the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics method, with realistic Hamiltonians consisting of the Argonne v14 or Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Tucson-Melbourne or Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The electromagnetic and weak transition operators include one- and two-body contributions. The theoretical accuracy achieved in these calculations allows for interesting comparisons with experimental data.

# Three-Nucleon Electroweak Capture Reactions

## Key claim

The correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics method with realistic Hamiltonians (AV18/UIX or AV14/TM) and one- plus two-body transition operators can reproduce measured p-d radiative capture and muon-³He weak capture observables to within a few percent, but persistent discrepancies remain in the deuteron tensor analyzing powers (T₂₀, T₂₁, A_xx, A_yy, A_zz) across multiple energies, indicating that the spin-flip E1 reduced matrix elements are overpredicted by the full calculation.

## Method

Bound and scattering states of the three-nucleon system were obtained by expanding on a basis of pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonic (PHH) functions, using the Argonne v₁₈ (AV18) or Argonne v₁₄ (AV14) two-nucleon potentials combined with the Urbana IX (UIX) or Tucson-Melbourne (TM) three-nucleon interactions. The electromagnetic and weak transition operators included one-body terms from the non-relativistic limit of the covariant single-nucleon currents and two-body meson-exchange and Δ-isobar contributions, with the NΔ axial coupling constant g_A* adjusted to reproduce the tritium β-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element. For muon capture, the 1s muon wave function was obtained by solving the Dirac equation in the electrostatic potential derived from the calculated ³He charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- p-d radiative capture differential cross sections at E_c.m. = 3.33, 6.67, and 9.87 MeV are well reproduced by the full calculation (AV18/UIX with one- and two-body currents), but T₂₀ and T₂₁ at 3.33 MeV show significant discrepancies from the data of Knu (1992).
- At E_c.m. = 5.83 MeV, the deuteron tensor analyzing powers A_xx, A_yy, and A_zz also show discrepancies, while A_y(d) (proportional to iT₁₁) is well reproduced.
- Muon capture rate Γ₀ on ³He: AV18/UIX gives 1484(8) s⁻¹, AV14/TM gives 1486(8) s⁻¹, compared to the experimental value 1496(4) s⁻¹ from Ack (1998); the AV18-only result is 1441(7) s⁻¹, about 4% lower.
- Angular correlation parameters for muon capture (AV18/UIX): A_v = 0.5350(14), A_t = –0.3650(9), A_Δ = –0.1000(16); the experimental A_v is 0.63(15) from Sou (1998).
- The one-body-only muon capture rate (AV18/UIX) is 1316 s⁻¹; adding meson-exchange contributions gives 1384 s⁻¹; full calculation including Δ-excitation gives 1484 s⁻¹.
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor parameter R_PS, determined by enforcing agreement with the experimental Γ₀, is R_PS = 0.94 ± 0.06.
- The factor ℛ correcting the muon 1s wave function from the point-charge value is 0.979 (AV18), 0.980 (AV18/UIX), and 0.980 (AV14/TM).

## Context

This work extends the PHH-based calculations of p-d capture below the deuteron breakup threshold reported by Viviani et al. (1996, 1999) to energies above breakup (E_c.m. = 3.33–9.87 MeV), and provides the first consistent treatment of muon capture on ³He with the same Hamiltonian and current models. The muon capture results agree with the experimental rate of Ack (1998) to within 1% when three-nucleon forces are included, and the weak model dependence (AV18/UIX vs. AV14/TM results differ by <0.5%) contrasts with earlier studies by Congleton (1992, 1995) and Govaerts (2000) that found larger uncertainties. The persistent discrepancies in deuteron tensor analyzing powers, also seen at 2 MeV by Viviani et al. (1999), motivate future work on alternative short-range two-body electromagnetic currents and extension to the muon capture breakup channels ³He(μ⁻,ν_μ)nd and ³He(μ⁻,ν_μ)nnp.
