# Possibility of $ρ$ Meson Condensation in Neutron Star: Unified Approach of Chiral SU(3) Model and QCD Sum Rules

> In the present work the conjunction of chiral SU(3) model with QCD sum rules is employed to explore the possibility of $ρ$ meson condensation in neutron stars. The quark and gluon condensates in terms of which the in-medium masses of $ρ$ mesons can be expressed are calculated using the chiral SU(3) model in the charge neutral matter which is relevant for neutron stars. It is observed that condition of $ρ$ meson condensation is satisfied for the density of about 7$ρ_{0}$, where $ρ_{0}$ is the nuclear saturation density. In the end, a brief qualitative discussion of the magnetic field is also involved to check out for the further possibility of $ρ$ meson condensation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the condition for rho meson condensation in neutron star matter is satisfied at a baryonic density of about 7 times nuclear saturation density, based on a unified approach combining the chiral SU(3) model with QCD sum rules, but this result is conditional on the specific model-dependent calculation of quark and gluon condensates in charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion of the chiral SU(3) model in relativistic mean-field approximation for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated neutron star matter to obtain density-dependent values of scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta, chi) and vector fields (omega, rho). These fields are then used to calculate the in-medium scalar quark condensates (up, down, strange) and the scalar gluon condensate via the trace anomaly relation. These condensates are inserted into the Wilson coefficients of QCD finite-energy sum rules for the rho meson vector current correlator, and the resulting set of three sum rule equations is solved to extract the in-medium rho meson mass, pole residue, and continuum threshold as functions of baryonic density.

## Key numbers

* The condition for rho meson condensation is satisfied at a baryonic density of about 7 rho_0, where rho_0 is the nuclear saturation density.
* The vacuum masses of up and down quarks used in the QCD sum rule Wilson coefficients are 4 MeV and 7 MeV, respectively.
* The factorization parameter k_q for the four-quark condensate is set to 2.0850.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hatsuda and Lee (1992), who used QCD sum rules with linear density approximation to calculate in-medium rho meson masses, by employing a non-linear chiral SU(3) model to compute the density dependence of condensates beyond the linear regime. The study is motivated by earlier work on kaon condensation by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and by experimental observations of rho meson mass shifts in heavy-ion collisions at CERES and RHIC. The results enable future calculations of the equation of state of neutron star matter including rho meson condensates, which would affect predicted neutron star masses and radii.
