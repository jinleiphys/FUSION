# Multipole analysis for pion photoproduction with MAID and a dynamical models

> Results of new analysis of pion photoproduction data obtained with Dynamical and MAID models are presented

## Key claim

The paper presents a new multipole analysis of pion photoproduction data using two models (DMT and MAID) that differ in how they separate background and resonance contributions, with the key difference being that MAID omits pion off-shell rescattering contributions from the background, instead absorbing them into the resonance sector.

## Method

The authors performed a multipole analysis of pion photoproduction data using two theoretical frameworks: the Dynamical Model (DMT) and the Unitary Isobar Model (MAID). The DMT model includes a principal value integral for pion off-shell rescattering in the background amplitude, while MAID uses a K-matrix approximation without this term. Both models parameterize resonance contributions using a Breit-Wigner form for eight nucleon resonances, with the electromagnetic transition strengths treated as free parameters extracted from fits to benchmark data bases.

## Key numbers

- Low-energy (LE) fit used 1287 data points in photon energy range 180 < E_gamma < 450 MeV; high-energy (HE) fit used 3270 data points in range 180 < E_gamma < 1200 MeV
- For the P33(1232) resonance, MAID HE fit gives A_{1/2} = -143 and A_{3/2} = -264 (in 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), compared to PDG2000 values of -135 +/- 6 and -255 +/- 8
- For the S11(1535) resonance, DMT HE fit gives A_{1/2} = 102 (in 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}) with a total width of 120 MeV, compared to MAID HE fit value of 81
- REM (E2/M1 ratio) for P33(1232) from LE fit: MAID = -1.9%, DMT = -2.1%, PDG2000 = -2.5 +/- 0.5%
- Total chi^2/d.o.f. for HE fit: MAID = 6.10, DMT = 6.10; for LE fit: MAID = 4.56, DMT = 3.59
- PV-PS mixing parameter Lambda_m from HE fit: MAID = 406 MeV, DMT = 302 MeV
- Low-energy correction parameter Delta E from HE fit: MAID = 1.73 (in 10^{-3}/m_pi+)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dynamical Model (Kamalov and Yang, 1999) and Unitary Isobar Model (MAID98) analyses of pion photo- and electroproduction. The analysis uses benchmark data bases created and distributed among theoretical groups for testing purposes, and the results are compared with the SAID group's multipole analysis (VPI97) and PDG2000 values. The study enables improved extraction of N* resonance properties by clarifying how different treatments of background-resonance separation affect the extracted helicity amplitudes, particularly for the S11(1535) resonance where the DMT result is more consistent with eta photoproduction analyses than previous pion photoproduction results.
