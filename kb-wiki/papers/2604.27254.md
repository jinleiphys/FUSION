# Species-Resolved Scaling of Azimuthal Anisotropy: Constraining Attenuation, Collective Expansion, and Hadronic Dynamics in Hydrodynamic Simulations

> Species-resolved azimuthal anisotropy scaling functions are constructed from identified particle $v_2$ and $v_3$ obtained from event-by-event iEBE-VISHNU simulations for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ and $5.02$~TeV. The scaling functions exhibit a robust collapse across transverse momentum, centrality, particle species, and beam energy, indicating a common and tightly constrained scaling structure. High scaling fidelity yields quantitative agreement with the data-defined reference through an energy-dependent attenuation baseline $β_0$ in central to mid-central collisions and a centrality-dependent modification of the effective attenuation in more peripheral collisions, with only a weak dependence on $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. The multiplicity dependence of the extracted scaling parameters reflects the interplay of EOS-driven collective expansion, finite system lifetime, and hadronic re-scattering. These results demonstrate that the scaling framework provides a quantitative, constraint-driven probe of the hydrodynamic response, enabling the disentanglement and constraint of the coupled contributions to azimuthal anisotropy.

## Key claim

The species-resolved scaling framework, when applied to iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic simulations, yields a robust collapse of azimuthal anisotropy across transverse momentum, centrality, particle species, and beam energy, but the quantitative agreement with the data-defined reference requires an energy-dependent attenuation baseline in central to mid-central collisions and a centrality-dependent modification of the effective attenuation in more peripheral collisions, with only a weak dependence on center-of-mass energy.

## Method

Event-by-event iEBE-VISHNU hybrid hydrodynamic simulations with AMPT initial conditions and UrQMD hadronic afterburner were used to compute identified particle v2 and v3 for pions, kaons, and protons in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. The species-resolved scaling framework was applied, expressing anisotropic flow in terms of reduced response v_n/epsilon_n with scaling variables based on transverse kinetic energy KE_T = m_T - m_0, incorporating parameters for viscous attenuation (beta_0, k_beta), hadronic re-scattering (zeta_hs), and radial flow (zeta_rf). The scaling functions from hydrodynamic calculations were compared to data-defined reference scaling functions from ALICE measurements.

## Key numbers

- Attenuation baseline for data-defined reference system (ultra-central Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV): beta_0 = 0.88 with k_beta = 1 and zeta_hs approximately 0
- Hydrodynamic attenuation baseline at 5.02 TeV: beta_0^hydro approximately 0.85 times beta_0 (i.e., approximately 0.75)
- Hydrodynamic attenuation baseline at 2.76 TeV: beta_0^hydro approximately 0.80 times beta_0 (i.e., approximately 0.70)
- In mid-peripheral collisions (40-50% centrality) at 5.02 TeV: k_beta^hydro approximately 0.80, indicating reduced effective attenuation
- Switching temperature for hadronic phase transition: T_sw approximately 148 MeV

## Context

This work extends the species-resolved scaling framework developed by Lacey (2024) and Lacey (2024) from experimental data to hydrodynamic simulations, testing whether the scaling structure is realized consistently within the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid framework of Song (2010) and Shen (2015) with AMPT initial conditions from Zhao (2017). The analysis enables quantitative disentanglement of viscous attenuation, EOS-driven collective expansion, and hadronic re-scattering contributions to azimuthal anisotropy, providing mechanistic constraints that go beyond reproducing v_n(p_T, centrality) alone. The framework can be applied to other hybrid models such as MUSIC coupled to UrQMD or SMASH, and accommodates the full p_T range including jet-quenching contributions at higher transverse momenta.
