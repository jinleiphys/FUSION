# Can Hyperfine Excitation explain the Observed Oscillation-Puzzle of Nuclear Orbital Electron Capture of Hydrogen-like Ions?

> Modulated in time orbital electron capture (EC) decays have been observed recently in stored H-like $^{140}$Pr$^{58+}$ and $^{142}$Pm$^{60+}$ ions. Although, the experimental results are extensively discussed in literature, a firm interpretation has still to be established. Periodic transitions between the hyperfine states could possible lead to the observed effect. Both selected nuclides decay to stable daughter nuclei via allowed Gamow-Teller transitions. Due to the conservation of total angular momentum, the allowed EC decay can only proceed from the hyperfine ground state of parent ions. In this work we argue that periodic transitions to the excited hyperfine state (sterile) in respect to the allowed EC decay ground state cannot explain the observed decay pattern.

## Key claim

The authors argue that periodic transitions between hyperfine states of H-like ions cannot explain the observed time-modulated orbital electron capture decays, because the required adjustment to the ratio of EC decay constants in H-like versus He-like ions would disagree with both theoretical predictions and experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-coupled-differential-equation model describing periodic excitation between the hyperfine ground state (EC-active) and excited state (EC-sterile) of H-like \(^{140}\)Pr\(^{58+}\) and \(^{142}\)Pm\(^{60+}\) ions, with spontaneous hyperfine decay back to the ground state. They solved these equations numerically using the Euler method with a time step of 0.00056 s, and fitted the solutions to the decay curves obtained from many-ion (MI) spectroscopy experiments at GSI. They compared the resulting effective EC decay constant ratios \(\lambda_{EC}^{\rm H-like}/\lambda_{EC}^{\rm He-like}\) to the theoretical value of approximately 3/2.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{140}\)Pr\(^{58+}\): hyperfine splitting \(\delta E = 1.26\) eV, hyperfine decay constant \(\lambda_{hf} = 38.2\) s\(^{-1}\), excitation constant \(b = 7.8\) s\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{142}\)Pm\(^{60+}\): hyperfine splitting \(\delta E = 1.12\) eV, hyperfine decay constant \(\lambda_{hf} = 26.2\) s\(^{-1}\), excitation constant \(b = 5.4\) s\(^{-1}\).
- The observed modulation amplitude from single-ion (SI) experiments is \(a \approx 0.2\), corresponding to an average occupancy of the excited hyperfine state \(x \approx 0.17\).
- The readjusted experimental ratios \(\lambda_{EC}^{\rm H-like}/\lambda_{EC}^{\rm He-like}\) are 1.77(7) for \(^{142}\)Pm and 1.83(10) for \(^{140}\)Pr, which disagree with the theoretical ratio of 1.5 by about 4 and 3 standard deviations, respectively.
- The correction factor required for the ratio due to hyperfine excitation is approximately 1.23, derived from numerical fits to MI experimental data from Litvinov et al. (2008).

## Context

This work addresses the oscillation puzzle observed in EC decays of stored H-like ions at GSI, first reported by Litvinov et al. (2008) and Winckler et al. (2009). The authors test the hyperfine excitation hypothesis against the well-established theoretical result from Patyk et al. (2001) and Kienle et al. (2001) that \(\lambda_{EC}^{\rm H-like} \approx 3/2 \cdot \lambda_{EC}^{\rm He-like}\). The paper demonstrates that the hyperfine mechanism cannot simultaneously explain the single-ion modulation data and the many-ion decay constant ratios, and recommends performing single-ion experiments on He-like ions to resolve the puzzle.
