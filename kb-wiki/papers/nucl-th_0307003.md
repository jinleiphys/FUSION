# NN final-state interaction in two-nucleon knockout from $^{16}O$

> The influence of the mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons (NN-FSI) in electro- and photoinduced two-nucleon knockout from $^{16}O$ has been investigated perturbatively. It turns out that the effect of NN-FSI depends on the kinematics and on the type of reaction considered. The effect is generally larger in pp- than in pn-knockout and in electron induced than in photoinduced reactions. In superparallel kinematics NN-FSI leads in the $(e,e'pp)$ channel to a strong increase of the cross section, that is mainly due to a strong enhancement of the $Δ$-current contribution. In pn-emission, however, this effect is partially cancelled by a destructive interference with the seagull current. For photoreactions NN-FSI is considerably reduced in superparallel kinematics and can be practically negligible in specific kinematics.

## Key claim

The mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons (NN-FSI) in two-nucleon knockout from $^{16}O$ can produce a strong enhancement of the cross section in superparallel kinematics for $(e,e'pp)$ reactions, but this effect is partially cancelled in pn-emission by destructive interference with the seagull current, and is considerably reduced in photoreactions.

## Method

The authors used a perturbative treatment of the NN final-state interaction, adding the first-order NN scattering amplitude to the existing distorted-wave (DW) model that already included optical potential interactions between each outgoing nucleon and the residual nucleus. The calculations were performed for $^{16}O$ targets, using the Bonn OBEPQ-A potential for pp-knockout and the AV14 potential for pn-knockout, with partial wave decompositions up to $l' = 3$ for the NN scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- In superparallel kinematics for $^{16}O(e,e'pp)^{14}C$ to the $0^+$ ground state, NN-FSI gives a strong increase of the cross section at recoil momenta above $p_{\rm B} \simeq 150$ MeV/$c$, with the enhancement increasing with $p_{\rm B}$
- For $^{16}O(e,e'pn)^{14}N$ to the $1^+$ ground state in the same kinematics, the NN-FSI enhancement is partially cancelled by destructive interference with the seagull current
- The optical potential gives the dominant contribution of FSI for recoil-momentum values up to $p_{\rm B} \simeq 150$ MeV/$c$ in both pp- and pn-knockout
- The NN-FSI effect is generally larger in pp-knockout than in pn-knockout, and larger in electron-induced than in photoinduced reactions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for $^{16}O(e,e'pp)$ (Schwamb et al. 2003) to include $(e,e'pn)$, $(\gamma,pp)$, and $(\gamma,pn)$ channels, and provides more detailed numerical results. It contradicts the earlier assumption that NN-FSI could be neglected in superparallel kinematics, a guess that had been adopted in prior models (Boffi et al. 1996, Giusti et al. 1997-2001). The results confirm a first indication from nuclear matter calculations (Knoll 2000) that NN-FSI can be important in superparallel kinematics, and the work enables future full three-body calculations that the authors state are in progress.
