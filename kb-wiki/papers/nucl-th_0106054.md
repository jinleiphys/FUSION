# Novel Weak Decays in Doubly Strange Systems

> The strangeness-changing ($ΔS = 1$) weak baryon-baryon interaction is studied through the nonmesonic weak decay of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei. Besides the usual nucleon-induced decay $ΛN \to N N$ we discuss novel hyperon-induced decay modes $ΛΛ\to ΛN$ and $ΛΛ\to ΣN$. These reactions provide unique access to the exotic $ΛΛ$K and $ΛΣ$K vertices which place new constraints on Chiral Pertubation Theory ($χ$PT) in the weak SU(3) sector. Within a meson-exchange framework, we use the pseudoscalar $π,η,K$ octet for the long-range part while parametrizing the short-range part through the vector mesons $ρ, ω, K^*$. Realistic baryon-baryon forces for the $S=0,-1$ and -2 sectors account for the strong interaction in the initial and final states. For $^6_{ΛΛ}$He the new hyperon-induced decay modes account for up to 4% of the total nonmesonic decay rate. Predictions are made for all possible nonmesonic decay modes.

# Novel Weak Decays in Doubly Strange Systems

## Key claim
The nonmesonic weak decay of double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei can proceed via hyperon-induced modes ($\Lambda\Lambda \to \Lambda N$ and $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Sigma N$) in addition to the usual nucleon-induced mode ($\Lambda N \to NN$), and these novel channels provide access to the exotic $\Lambda\Lambda$K and $\Lambda\Sigma$K weak vertices. However, the total hyperon-induced rate is only up to 4% of the total nonmesonic decay rate, and the extraction of weak coupling constants from these channels requires careful treatment of final-state interactions and is sensitive to whether tree-level or one-loop corrected chiral perturbation theory couplings are used.

## Method
The authors employ a one-meson-exchange framework using the pseudoscalar octet ($\pi$, $\eta$, $K$) for long-range contributions and the vector meson octet ($\rho$, $\omega$, $K^*$) for short-range contributions, with strong baryon-baryon-meson couplings taken from the Nijmegen NSC97f potential. Correlated wave functions for the initial $\Lambda$N and $\Lambda\Lambda$ states are obtained from $G$-matrix calculations, while final-state interactions for the outgoing NN and YN pairs are treated via $T$-matrix scattering equations using the same Nijmegen interaction models. The calculations are performed for the specific double-$\Lambda$ hypernucleus $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He using harmonic oscillator single-particle orbitals with $b_N=1.4$ fm and $b_\Lambda=1.6$ fm.

## Key numbers
- The total nucleon-induced rate for $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He is $\Gamma_{\rm NN} = 0.96 \, \Gamma_\Lambda$, where $\Gamma_\Lambda = 3.8 \times 10^8$ s$^{-1}$ is the free $\Lambda$ decay rate.
- The hyperon-induced $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Lambda n$ rate is $\Gamma_{\Lambda {\rm n}} = 0.025 \, \Gamma_\Lambda$ with tree-level couplings, and the $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Sigma N$ rate is $\Gamma_{\Sigma {\rm N}} = 0.0012 \, \Gamma_\Lambda$ with tree-level couplings.
- The ratio of rates is $\Gamma_{\rm NN} : \Gamma_{\Lambda {\rm N}} : \Gamma_{\Sigma {\rm N}} \simeq 320 : 12 : 1$ when correlations are included.
- The $\Lambda$ single-particle separation energy increases from 3.12 MeV in $^5_\Lambda$He to approximately 5 MeV in $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, corresponding to a change in the harmonic oscillator parameter from $b_\Lambda=1.87$ fm to $b_\Lambda=1.6$ fm.
- Using one-loop corrected chiral perturbation theory BBK couplings changes the $\Lambda N \to NN$ rate by +40%, the $\Gamma_{\rm n}/\Gamma_{\rm p}$ ratio by a factor of two smaller, the $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Lambda n$ rate by roughly a factor of two smaller, and the $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Sigma N$ rate by almost a factor of two larger compared to tree-level values.

## Context
This work extends previous weak decay calculations of single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei (Parreno, Ramos, Bennhold 1997; Parreno, Ramos, Bennhold 2001) into the $S=-2$ sector, exploring the novel $\Lambda\Lambda \to \Lambda(\Sigma)N$ processes. The study addresses a known failure of SU(3) chiral perturbation theory in predicting the four parity-conserving $P$-wave amplitudes in weak nonleptonic decays of octet baryons (Jenkins 1992), and the new vertices provide additional tests of $\chi$PT in the weak SU(3) sector. The results are compared with those of Itonaga et al. (2001), who used $\pi$, $\rho$, and correlated two-pion exchange but omitted kaon exchange and used a $\Lambda$ wave function neglecting the stronger binding in double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei. The work enables future experimental programs at KEK, BNL, and FINUDA at DA$\Phi$NE to extract weak coupling constants from double-$\Lambda$ hypernucleus decay measurements.
