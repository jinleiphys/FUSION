# Photon production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions

> We calculate the photon production cross-section in $pA$ collisions under the assumption that the nucleus has reached the saturation regime, while the proton can be described by the standard parton distribution functions. We show that due to the strong classical field $O(1/g)$ of the nucleus, bremsstrahlung diagrams become dominant over the direct photon diagrams. In particular, we show that $γ-$jet transverse momentum spectrum and correlations are very sensitive to gluon saturation effects in the nucleus.

## Key claim

In proton-nucleus collisions where the nucleus is treated as a saturated color glass condensate (with a strong classical field of order \(O(1/g)\)), bremsstrahlung diagrams for photon production become dominant over direct photon diagrams, and the photon-jet transverse momentum spectrum and correlations are highly sensitive to gluon saturation effects in the nucleus.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon production cross-section in \(pA\) collisions by treating the nucleus as a classical color field (color glass condensate) while describing the proton with standard parton distribution functions. They derive the amplitude for the bremsstrahlung process \(q \to q\gamma\) in the background of this classical color field, resumming interactions to all orders, and then perform color averaging over the nuclear color sources to obtain the inclusive differential cross-section.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is defined as \(Q_s^2 \equiv g^4 (t_a t^a) \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} dz^- \mu^2(z^-)/2\), where the integral of \(\mu^2\) over \(z^-\) is the number density of color sources per unit transverse area in the target nucleus.
- The quantity \(B_1\) is given by \(B_1({\bf x}_\perp) \equiv Q_s^2 \int d^2{\bf z}_\perp G_0^2({\bf x}_\perp-{\bf z}_\perp) \sim Q_s^2 / \Lambda_{QCD}^2\), and since \(B_1 \gg 1\), the term \(\exp(-B_1)\) is neglected in the inclusive cross-section.
- In the perturbative limit (transverse momentum transfer \(l_\perp \gg Q_s\)), the correlator \(C({\bf l}_\perp) \approx 2 Q_s^2 / l_\perp^4\).
- The inclusive cross-section per unit transverse area is given by \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2} \frac{d\sigma^{q\to q\gamma}_{\rm incl}}{d^2{\bf k}_\perp} = \frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^5 {\bf k}_\perp^2} \int_0^1 dz \frac{[1+(1-z)^2]}{z} \int d^2{\bf l}_\perp \frac{{\bf l}_\perp^2 C({\bf l}_\perp)}{[{\bf l}_\perp - {\bf k}_\perp/z]^2}\), where \(z \equiv k^-/p^-\).
- In the perturbative limit, the cross-section becomes \(\frac{d\sigma^{q\to q\gamma}_{\rm incl}}{d^2{\bf k}_\perp}\big|_{\rm pert.} = \frac{2 N_h e_q^2 \alpha_{\rm em} \alpha_S^2}{\pi^2} \frac{C_F}{{\bf k}_\perp^2} \int_0^1 dz \frac{1+(1-z)^2}{z} \int \frac{d^2{\bf l}_\perp}{{\bf l}_\perp^2 [{\bf l}_\perp - {\bf k}_\perp/z]^2}\), where \(N_h \equiv \pi R^2 \int dz^- \mu^2(z^-)\) is the total number of hard color sources in the target nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the color glass condensate framework, previously applied to deep inelastic scattering at HERA (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and to heavy ion collisions at RHIC (Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi, 2001), to the cleaner environment of proton-nucleus collisions. It builds on earlier studies of saturation effects in \(pA\) collisions (Dumitru and Jalilian-Marian, 2002) and provides a specific observable, photon-jet correlations, that can directly probe the correlator \(C({\bf l}_\perp)\) and thus the saturation scale \(Q_s\). The result enables experimental tests of saturation in forward region measurements at RHIC and the LHC, and can be extended to dilepton and di-photon production.
