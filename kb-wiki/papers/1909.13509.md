# Glauber model for small system using anisotropic and inhomogeneous density profile of proton

> Recent studies reveal that at high energies, collisions of small system like $p+p$ gives signatures similar to that widely observed in heavy ion collisions hinting towards a possibility of forming a medium with collective behaviour. With this motivation, we have used the Glauber model, which is traditionally applied to heavy ion collisions, in small system using anisotropic and inhomogeneous density profile of proton and found that the proposed model reproduces the charged particle multiplicity distribution of $p+p$ collisions at LHC energies very well. Collision geometric properties like mean impact parameter, mean number of binary collisions ($\langle N_{coll} \rangle$) and mean number of participants ($\langle N_{part} \rangle$) at different multiplicities are determined. Having estimated $\langle N_{coll} \rangle$, we have calculated nuclear modification-like factor ($R_{HL}$) in $p+p$ collisions. We also estimated eccentricity and elliptic flow as a function of charged particle multiplicity using the linear response to initial geometry.

## Key claim

The authors apply a Glauber model, traditionally used for heavy-ion collisions, to proton-proton collisions by using an anisotropic and inhomogeneous density profile of the proton (composed of three effective quarks and gluonic flux tubes), and find that this model reproduces the charged-particle multiplicity distribution at LHC energies "very well" in the mid-multiplicity region (15 < Nch < 90), but with a 5-10% discrepancy and an inability to describe the extreme low and high multiplicity regions.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo Glauber model for p+p collisions using a proton density profile with three effective quarks (Gaussian distributions) and gluonic flux tubes (cylindrical Gaussian distributions) connecting them, parameterized by varying azimuthal and polar angles to account for all possible configurations. They calculated the thickness function and overlap function for quark-quark, quark-gluon, gluon-quark, and gluon-gluon interactions, then used a two-component model (soft interactions proportional to Npart, hard interactions proportional to Ncoll) with a Negative Binomial Distribution to estimate charged-particle multiplicity, fitting parameters to ALICE data at √s = 7 TeV.

## Key numbers

* The effective partonic cross section was fixed at σeff = 4.3 ± 0.6 mb with Ng = 10 partons to reproduce the experimental inelastic cross section σpp = 60 mb for p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV.
* The best agreement for the Nch distribution with ALICE data was found with parameters f = 0.85, n̄ = 8, and k = 0.13.
* The model yields ⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 7.47 for minimum-bias (0-100%) collisions, which is higher than the experimental value of 6.01 ± 0.01+0.20−0.12.
* For the 0-1% multiplicity class, the model gives ⟨dNch/dη⟩glauber = 19.69 compared to the experimental value of 28.82+0.86−0.84, with ⟨Npart⟩ = 13.142 and ⟨Ncoll⟩ = 31.156.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kubiczek (2015), who first considered an azimuthally asymmetric and inhomogeneous density distribution of the proton, by using a more comprehensive parameterization of quark positions (varying azimuthal and polar angles) and by including a two-component model for charged-particle multiplicity (Npart + Ncoll) rather than linear scaling with Ncoll alone. The results enable the estimation of collision geometric properties (mean impact parameter, Ncoll, Npart) and flow harmonics (eccentricity, elliptic flow) in p+p collisions, which is relevant for understanding whether a collective medium can form in high-multiplicity small systems.
