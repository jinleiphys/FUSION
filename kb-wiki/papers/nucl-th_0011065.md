# Peripheral Collisions of Relativistic Heavy Ions

## Key claim

The paper reviews the physics of peripheral collisions of relativistic heavy ions (PCRHI), asserting that the equivalent photon method (Weizsäcker-Williams) is valid for all multipolarities in extreme relativistic collisions, but that many processes, especially electron-positron pair production, remain poorly understood because no theory has fully accounted for Coulomb distortion of leptonic wavefunctions with respect to both nuclei simultaneously.

## Method

The review surveys one- and two-photon processes in PCRHI using the equivalent photon approximation, where the electromagnetic field of a fast charged particle is replaced by a flux of virtual photons. Cross sections are expressed as convolutions of equivalent photon numbers with photon-induced or two-photon cross sections. The author compares perturbative and non-perturbative calculations for specific processes (atomic ionization, pair production, Coulomb excitation, meson production) and contrasts them with experimental data from fixed-target and collider experiments.

## Key numbers

- Bremsstrahlung cross section for 10 MeV photons: central collisions deliver 10^6 more photons than peripheral collisions (no absolute cross section given for peripheral case).
- Delbrück scattering cross section at LHC: about 50 barns.
- Atomic ionization cross section for Pb^{81+} (33 TeV) beams: of order kilobarns, increasing slowly with the logarithm of the RHI energy.
- Pair production cross section (free e+e-): to leading order in gamma, σ = (28/27π) Z_P^2 Z_T^2 r_e^2 ln^3(γ/4).
- Pair production with electron capture cross section: σ = [3.3π Z^8 α^6 r_e^2 / (exp(2πZα) - 1)] [ln(0.681 γ_c^2) - 5/3].
- DGDR cross section: σ ~ S^2 / (A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3})^2, where S = 5.45 × 10^{-4} Z_P^2 Z_T N_T / A_T^{2/3} mb.
- Single meson production at RHIC: π^0 cross section = 7.1 mb (γγ fusion), 0.05 mb (pomeron-pomeron); η cross section = 1.5 mb (γγ), 0.038 mb (PP); η' cross section = 1.1 mb (γγ), 0.04 mb (PP).
- Single meson production at LHC: π^0 = 40 mb (γγ), 0.367 mb (PP); η = 17 mb (γγ), 0.355 mb (PP); η' = 22 mb (γγ), 0.405 mb (PP).
- Higgs boson production via γγ fusion at LHC: cross section of order 1 nanobarn.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Fermi (1924) and Weizsäcker and Williams (1934-1935) on the equivalent photon method, and summarizes developments since the earlier review by Bertulani and Baur (1988). It covers applications including Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics (e.g., the ^7Be(p,γ)^8B reaction studied via Coulomb breakup at GSI/Darmstadt), double giant dipole resonance studies, and meson production at RHIC and LHC. The paper identifies open questions in the treatment of Coulomb distortion in pair production, noting that perturbative calculations by Landau and others (1933-1937) may underestimate cross sections by a factor of about two, and that non-perturbative approaches remain controversial.
