# Spin-orbit correlations in the nucleon

> We investigate the correlations between the quark spin and orbital angular momentum inside the nucleon. Similarly to the Ji relation, we show that these correlations can be expressed in terms of specific moments of measurable parton distributions. This provides a whole new piece of information about the partonic structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the quark spin-orbit correlation inside the nucleon can be expressed in terms of specific moments of measurable parton distributions, analogous to the Ji relation for orbital angular momentum. The derivation relies on the asymmetric gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor rather than the symmetric Belinfante tensor, and the resulting expressions involve the second moment of the quark helicity distribution and the Dirac form factor at zero momentum transfer.

## Method

The author defines a local gauge-invariant light-front operator for the quark longitudinal spin-orbit correlation as the difference between right- and left-handed quark orbital angular momentum operators. The non-forward matrix elements of the parity-odd partner of the quark energy-momentum tensor are parametrized in terms of five form factors, and these form factors are related to specific moments of twist-2 axial-vector generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and to standard electromagnetic form factors using QCD identities. The quark spin-orbit correlation is then expressed in terms of the second moment of the helicity GPD \(\tilde H_q\) and the Dirac and tensor form factors at \(t=0\).

## Key numbers

*   For \(u\) quarks, the second moment of the helicity distribution \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\,x\tilde H_u(x,0,0) \approx 0.19\) at scale \(\mu^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) from the Leader-Sidorov-Stamenov (LSS) analysis, leading to an estimated spin-orbit correlation \(C^u_z \approx -0.9\).
*   For \(d\) quarks, \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\,x\tilde H_d(x,0,0) \approx -0.06\) at the same scale, leading to \(C^d_z \approx -0.53\).
*   The naive quark model predicts \(\Delta^{(1)}u = 4/9\) and \(\Delta^{(1)}d = -1/9\) for the second axial moments at scale \(\mu^2 \sim 0.26\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The light-front constituent quark model predicts \(\Delta^{(1)}u = 0.345\) and \(\Delta^{(1)}d = -0.086\) at the same scale.
*   The light-front chiral quark-soliton model predicts \(\Delta^{(1)}u = 0.392\) and \(\Delta^{(1)}d = -0.098\) at the same scale.
*   Lattice calculations by the LHPC collaboration at scale \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and pion mass \(m_\pi = 293\) MeV give \(\Delta^{(1)}u \approx 0.20\) and \(\Delta^{(1)}d \approx -0.05\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ji (1996) for quark orbital angular momentum to the parity-odd sector, providing a new quantitative relation between the quark spin-orbit correlation and measurable parton distributions. It complements earlier work by Lorce (2011) which studied spin-orbit correlations at the level of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs), which are not yet experimentally accessible. The results enable experimental extraction of the quark spin-orbit correlation from combined fits to inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data, and provide new sum rules connecting twist-2 and twist-3 GPDs.
