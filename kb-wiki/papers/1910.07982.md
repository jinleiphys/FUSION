# Coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering off He nuclei

> The status of realistic calculations of nuclear generalized parton distributions, entering the theoretical description of coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering off nuclei, is reviewed for trinucleons and for $^4$He, also in view of forthcoming measurements at the Jefferson Laboratory and at the future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

Realistic calculations of nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off trinucleons and $^4$He are reviewed, with the hedge that for $^4$He a rigorous realistic evaluation using state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon potentials and exact three-body scattering wave functions is still missing and should be done.

## Method

The authors review plane wave impulse approximation (IA) analyses for $^3$He and $^4$He, where the struck quark belongs to one nucleon in the target and final state interactions are disregarded. For $^3$He, a complete analysis uses the Av18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential with realistic non-diagonal spectral functions. For $^4$He, a model of the nuclear non-diagonal spectral function based on the Av18 NN interaction momentum distribution is used, with the spectral function exact in its ground state part (remnant as a bound three-body system) and modelled in the excited sector.

## Key numbers

- For $^3$He, nuclear effects on GPDs are found to grow with the momentum transfer to the target, $\Delta^2$, and with the longitudinal momentum asymmetry (skewness variable, $\xi$).
- For $^3$He, nuclear effects are bigger for the $d$ flavor than for the $u$ flavor, as shown in Figure 2 (no explicit numerical values given in text).
- For $^4$He, the beam-spin asymmetry $A_{LU}(\phi)$ calculated in Fucini (2018) agrees with data from Hattawy (2017) (Figure 3; no explicit numerical values given in text).
- The first data for coherent DVCS off $^4$He were collected at JLab in the 6 GeV setup and published by Hattawy (2017).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations for $^4$He by Guzey (2003) and Liuti (2005), updating them with modern NN potentials. For $^3$He, it builds on prior IA analyses by Scopetta (2004) and Rinaldi (2012, 2014). The review enables interpretation of forthcoming measurements at Jefferson Lab (JLab) at 12 GeV and at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC), particularly for disentangling conventional nuclear effects from exotic ones responsible for the EMC effect.
