# Coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering off He nuclei

> The status of realistic calculations of nuclear generalized parton distributions, entering the theoretical description of coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering off nuclei, is reviewed for trinucleons and for $^4$He, also in view of forthcoming measurements at the Jefferson Laboratory and at the future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

Realistic calculations of nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) off trinucleons and $^4$He are reviewed, with the hedge that for $^4$He a fully rigorous calculation using state-of-the-art spectral functions is still missing and should be done, while the existing model-based approach already reproduces current experimental data without requiring exotic nuclear effects.

## Method

The authors review calculations of nuclear GPDs within a plane wave impulse approximation (IA), where the struck quark belongs to one nucleon in the target and final state interactions are disregarded. For $^3$He, a complete analysis uses the Av18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and realistic non-diagonal spectral functions. For $^4$He, a model of the non-diagonal spectral function is constructed from the Av18 momentum distribution, exact in the ground state part and modeled in the excited sector, and the nucleonic GPD input is taken from the Goloskokov-Kroll model.

## Context

This review extends earlier calculations of nuclear GPDs for few-body systems, specifically updating the work on $^4$He by Guzey (2003) and Liuti (2005) with modern NN potentials. For $^3$He, it builds on prior IA analyses by Scopetta (2004) and Rinaldi (2012-2014) using the Av18 potential. The results enable interpretation of upcoming coherent DVCS measurements at Jefferson Lab (JLab) at 12 GeV with the ALERT detector project (Armstrong 2017) and at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC), allowing separation of conventional nuclear effects from exotic ones possibly responsible for the EMC effect.
