# Masses and Decay widths of Charmonium states in presence of strong magnetic fields

> The masses and decay widths of charmonium states are studied in the presence of strong magnetic fields. The mixing between the pseudoscalar and vector charmonium states at rest is observed to lead to appreciable negative (positive) shifts in the masses of the pseudoscalar (longitudinal component of the vector) charmonium states in vacuum/hadronic medium in the presence of high magnetic fields. The pseudoscalar and vector charmonium masses in the hadronic medium, calculated in an effective chiral model from the medium changes of a scalar dilaton field, have additional significant modifications due to the mixing effects. The masses of the $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons in the magnetized hadronic matter are calculated within the chiral effective model. The partial decay widths of the vector charmonium state to $D\bar D$ are computed using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with quark/antiquark constituents, and are compared to the decay widths calculated using an effective hadronic Lagrangian. The effects of the mixing are observed to lead to significant contributions to the masses of the pseusoscalar and vector charmonium states, and an appreciable increase in the decay width $ψ(3770) \rightarrow D\bar D$ at large values of the magnetic fields. These studies of the charmonium states in strong magnetic fields should have observable consequences on the dilepton spectra, as well as on the production of the open charm mesons and the charmonium states in ultra relativistic heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The mixing between pseudoscalar and vector charmonium states at rest in strong magnetic fields leads to appreciable negative (positive) shifts in the masses of the pseudoscalar (longitudinal component of the vector) charmonium states, with these mixing effects being the dominant contribution to mass shifts beyond the medium modifications from the scalar dilaton field.

## Method

The masses of pseudoscalar (eta_c, eta'_c) and vector (J/psi, psi(2S), psi(1D)) charmonium states in magnetized hadronic matter were computed using a chiral effective model where the scalar dilaton field simulates the gluon condensate, with additional mixing effects incorporated through an effective Lagrangian interaction term coupling pseudoscalar and vector mesons via the dual electromagnetic field. The partial decay widths of vector charmonium states to D and anti-D mesons were calculated using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with quark/antiquark constituents, assuming harmonic oscillator wave functions, and compared to results from an effective hadronic Lagrangian. The D and anti-D meson masses in magnetized nuclear matter were computed within the same chiral effective model, retaining lowest Landau level contributions for charged mesons.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields at RHIC estimated as eB ~ 2 m_pi^2 and at LHC as eB ~ 15 m_pi^2
- The coupling parameter g_PV for the pseudoscalar-vector mixing is fitted from the observed radiative decay width Gamma(V -> P + gamma) using the formula Gamma(V -> P gamma) = (e^2/12) * (g_PV^2 * p_cm^3) / (pi * m_av^2), where p_cm = (m_V^2 - m_P^2)/(2 m_V)
- The harmonic oscillator strength parameter for D(anti-D) meson is taken as R_D = (310 MeV)^{-1} to give experimental vacuum decay widths of psi(3770) -> D anti-D
- The decay width formula for the composite hadron model without mixing is Gamma(Psi -> D(p) anti-D(-p)) = gamma_Psi^2 * (8 pi^2/3) * |p|^3 * (p^0_D(|p|) * p^0_anti-D(|p|) / m_Psi) * A^Psi(|p|)^2
- The effective hadronic model decay width is Gamma_had(Psi -> D anti-D) = (g_Psi D anti-D^2) / (6 pi m_Psi^2) * |p|^3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium properties in magnetic fields using QCD sum rule approaches (charmonium_mag_QSR, charmonium_mag_lee) and Schrodinger equation methods (Alford and Strickland, 2013), which showed dominant contributions from mixing effects. It also builds on earlier calculations of charmonium masses in hadronic matter using the chiral effective model (Mishra et al., various years) and studies of open charm mesons in magnetized matter (dmeson_mag). The results enable predictions for observable consequences in dilepton spectra and open charm meson production in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, particularly regarding the "anomalous" decay modes such as eta_c, eta'_c -> l+ l- that could serve as probes of strong magnetic fields at early stages of collisions.
