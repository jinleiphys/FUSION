# Nuclear phenomena at HERA energies

> We argue that current data on nuclear shadowing confirm expectations of the color singlet models of nuclear shadowing. We demonstrate that unitarity constrains require nuclear shadowing for gluons to be very significant already for $x \le 10^{-3}$ . Physics of coherent diffraction production of vector mesons off light nuclei is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors argue that current data on nuclear shadowing confirm expectations of color singlet models, but they qualify that unitarity constraints require nuclear shadowing for gluons to be very significant already for x ≤ 10⁻³, and that at very small x and moderate Q² ≤ 10 GeV², hard processes sensitive to gluon densities in nuclei should be strongly shadowed.

## Method

The authors analyze nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei using the color singlet (color screening) mechanism, where shadowing arises from soft color singlet interactions of qqbar pairs with nucleons. They apply unitarity constraints to derive bounds on gluon densities in nuclei and examine coherent diffraction production of vector mesons off light nuclei, including deuterons, using QCD-based expressions for the interaction of small transverse size qqbar configurations. The analysis compares predictions of the color singlet mechanism with the alternative parton fusion model.

## Key numbers

- The average cross section for interaction of color singlet qqbar pairs with nucleons is about 15-18 mb for ν ~ 200 GeV.
- The ratio of coherent diffractive to total cross section for nuclei with A between 12 and 200 is approximately 0.1 × (A/10)^0.25.
- For σ_L(A) at Q² = 10 GeV², the effective gluon density is bounded by xG_A^eff(x,Q²) ≤ 50 / A^(1/3) due to unitarity constraints.
- The unitarity condition for heavy nuclei requires α_s(Q²) xG_A(x,Q²) ≤ 1.9 Q² / A^(1/3).
- For ep collisions, nonlinear effects become significant for x ≤ 3 × 10⁻⁵, which is beyond HERA kinematics.
- At large t (|t| ≥ 0.5 GeV²), the slope parameter B' for the differential cross section is predicted to be ~ 2 GeV⁻², compared to ~ 3 GeV⁻² in the Glauber model.
- The real part of the production amplitude is estimated as Re f/Im f ~ π n/2 ~ 0.5, where n characterizes the increase of gluon density at small x.

## Context

This work extends the color singlet (color screening) model of nuclear shadowing developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and Frankfurt, Strikman, and Liuti (1990), and contrasts with the parton fusion model of Mueller and Qiu (1986) and Qiu (1987). The analysis builds on the aligned jet model of Bjorken (1971) and uses unitarity constraints from Abramowicz, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1995) to predict strong gluon shadowing at small x. The results enable predictions for coherent vector meson production off nuclei at HERA and have implications for understanding nonlinear QCD effects and color transparency phenomena, with relevance to heavy ion collisions at LHC.
