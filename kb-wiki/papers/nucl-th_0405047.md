# Description of rotating $N=Z$ nuclei in terms of isovector pairing

> A systematic investigation of the rotating $N=Z$ even-even nuclei in the mass $A=58-80$ region has been performed within the frameworks of the Cranked Relativistic Mean field, Cranked Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov theories and cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky approach. Most of the experimental data is well accounted for in the calculations. The present study suggests that there is strong isovector $np$-pair field at low spin, the strength of which is defined by the isospin symmetry. At high spin, the isovector pair field is destroyed and the data are well described by the calculations assuming zero pairing. No clear evidence for the existence of the isoscalar $t=0$ $np$-pairing has been obtained in the present investigation.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that strong isovector (t=1) neutron-proton pairing exists at low spin in rotating N=Z nuclei, with its strength defined by isospin symmetry, but finds no clear evidence for isoscalar (t=0) neutron-proton pairing; the authors hedge that discrepancies between calculations and experiment should not necessarily be attributed to neglect of t=0 pairing, as they may arise from deficiencies in single-particle spectra or incomplete experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic investigation of rotating even-even N=Z nuclei in the mass A=58-80 region using three theoretical frameworks: Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory, Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory with the Lipkin-Nogami method, and the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approach. At high spin (I >= 15 hbar), they neglected pair correlations using CRMF or CNS, while CRHB+LN calculations were used for low and intermediate spin states. The calculations used the NL3 parameterization for the RMF Lagrangian and the D1S Gogny force for the pairing channel, with no adjustable parameters specific to the N=Z mass region.

## Key numbers

- Mass range studied: A=58-80 for rotating N=Z even-even nuclei
- For 74Kr, the CRHB+LN calculations reproduced experimental kinematic moments of inertia excellently except for the lowest frequency point and two points after band crossing; no convergence was obtained for rotational frequency omega >= 1.21 MeV due to very weak pairing
- In 78Sr, the CRHB+LN calculations predicted a back-bend at I ~ 16 hbar for the ground-state band crossing with the S-band
- The calculated equilibrium deformation of the S-band in 78Sr was Q_t ~ 2.37 eb, beta_2 ~ 0.28, gamma ~ -20 degrees, while the g-band had Q_t ~ 3.6 eb, beta_2 ~ 0.47, gamma ~ -1 degree
- In 74Kr, the CNS calculations with standard Nilsson parameters placed bands involving a hole in pi f_{7/2} more than 1 MeV above the yrast line
- Band crossings in the studied nuclei occurred at rotational frequencies omega = 0.6-1.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of N=Z nuclei using shell model calculations (Engel 1997, Dean et al. 1997) that showed strong isovector np-pairing suppressed by increasing angular momentum, and mean-field studies of binding energies (Vogel 2000, Macchiavelli et al. 2000) that supported an isovector pair gap. The paper contradicts suggestions in earlier publications that rotational properties of N=Z nuclei could provide evidence for isoscalar t=0 np-pairing, arguing that shape softness and band termination effects must be accounted for. The results enable future experimental studies to search for the unobserved S-band in 78Sr and to test whether discrepancies in N=Z nuclei arise from missing t=0 pairing or from other model deficiencies.
