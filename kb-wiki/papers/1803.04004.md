# Target-normal single-spin asymmetry in elastic electron-nucleon scattering

> We estimate the target-normal single-spin asymmetry at nearly forward angles in elastic electron-nucleon scattering. In the leading-order approximation, this asymmetry is proportional to the imaginary part of the two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitude, which can be expressed as an integral over the doubly virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) tensor. We develop a model that parametrizes the VVCS tensor for the case of nearly forward scattering angles. Our parametrization ensures a proper normalization of the imaginary part of the TPE amplitude on the well-known forward limit expression, which is given in terms of nucleon structure functions measurable in inelastic electron-nucleon scattering experiments. We discuss applicability limits of our theory and provide target-normal single-spin asymmetry predictions for both elastic electron-proton and electron-neutron scattering.

## Key claim

The target-normal single-spin asymmetry in elastic electron-nucleon scattering at nearly forward angles is proportional to the imaginary part of the two-photon exchange amplitude, which the authors model by parametrizing the doubly virtual Compton scattering tensor using only the four structures that survive in the forward limit, under the assumption that off-forward contributions are negligible for small momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors express the target-normal single-spin asymmetry in terms of an integral over the doubly virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) tensor, splitting the intermediate hadronic state into elastic (nucleon) and inelastic (pion production and higher) contributions. For the elastic part, they use the nucleon electromagnetic form factors with Kelly's parametrization for the proton and fits from Wojtsekhowski (2010) and Qattan (2012) for the neutron. For the inelastic part, they model the VVCS tensor using only the four tensor structures that survive in the forward scattering limit, normalizing their imaginary parts to nucleon structure functions measurable in deep inelastic scattering, and they compute the asymmetry numerically using the integration variables transformed to a Lorentz-invariant form.

## Key numbers

- The target-normal single-spin asymmetry is expected to be of order α ≈ 1/137.
- The model is applicable for momentum transfers Q² ≲ 1 GeV² and for nearly forward scattering angles where Q² ≪ s.
- The elastic intermediate state contribution corresponds to the invariant mass range M² ≤ W² ≤ (M + mπ)², where mπ is the pion mass.
- The inelastic intermediate state contribution covers the range (M + mπ)² ≤ W² ≤ s.
- The authors reference the experimental data point from Zhang (2015) taken at electron beam energy ε₁ = 3.605 GeV and Q² = 0.967 GeV² for the neutron-normal SSA.
- The electroproduction amplitudes calculation of Pasquini and Vanderhaeghen (2004) is constrained by W ≲ 2 GeV, corresponding to ε₁ ≲ 1.66 GeV, and their neutron-normal SSA prediction is given only for ε₁ = 0.57 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical calculations of target-normal single-spin asymmetries by De Rujula et al. (1971) and Afanasev et al. (2002) for the proton, and provides new model predictions for the neutron that cover kinematical regions inaccessible by the generalized parton distributions calculation of Chen et al. (2004) (which agrees with the Zhang (2015) datapoint at Q² = 0.967 GeV² but cannot describe lower Q² data) and the electroproduction amplitudes model of Pasquini and Vanderhaeghen (2004) (limited to W ≲ 2 GeV). The results are relevant for upcoming experiments such as MUSE, which will study two-photon exchange in the low-Q² region (0.0016 < Q² < 0.082 GeV²) using both electron and muon beams.
