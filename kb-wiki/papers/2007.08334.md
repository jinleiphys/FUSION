# Field of a moving locked charge in classical electrodynamics

> The paradox of a field of a moving locked charge (confined in a closed space) is considered and solved with the use of the integral Maxwell equations. While known formulas obtained for instantaneous fields of charges moving along straight and curved lines are fully correct, measurable quantities are average electric and magnetic fields of locked charges. It is shown that the average electric field of locked charges does not depend on their motion. The average electric field of protons moving in nuclei coincides with that of protons being at rest and having the same spatial distribution of the charge density. The electric field of a twisted electron is equivalent to the field of a centroid with immobile charges which spatial distribution is defined by the wave function of the twisted electron.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the paradox of a moving locked charge by showing that while instantaneous electric fields depend on velocity via Lorentz contraction, the *average* electric field of charges confined in a closed space (such as protons in nuclei or electrons in atoms) does not depend on their motion and coincides with the field of the same charge distribution at rest.

## Method

The author applies the integral Maxwell equations (Gauss's law and Ampère's law with displacement current) to the average charge and current densities of locked charges. By noting that the average charge density of particles moving along a closed trajectory is independent of velocity due to charge conservation, and that the average linear charge density of a filament in a storage ring equals the total charge divided by the circumference, the average electric and magnetic fields are derived directly from the integral equations without Lorentz transformation of instantaneous fields.

## Key numbers

- For an infinite moving filament, the instantaneous electric field is \(E = 2\tau\gamma / r\) (where \(\tau\) is charge per unit length, \(\gamma\) is the Lorentz factor, and \(r\) is distance), but the average electric field of a closed filament is \(\overline{E} = 2\overline{\tau} / r\) with \(\overline{\tau} = Q/C\) (total charge divided by circumference).
- The average magnetic field of a closed filament is \(\overline{\bm B} = 2\overline{\tau}\bm\beta \times \bm r / r^2\), where \(\bm\beta = \bm v / c\).
- Twisted electron beams with intrinsic orbital angular momentum up to \(1000\hbar\) have been obtained (citing VGRILLO).
- The orbital magnetic moment of a free pointlike particle is \(\bm\mu_L = e\bm L / (2E)\) in any frame, and the total magnetic moment including spin is \(\bm\mu = e(\bm L + 2\bm s) / (2E)\).

## Context

This work extends and definitively resolves a paradox first stated in JPhysG2015locked, which noted that naive Lorentz transformation of potentials for moving locked charges leads to contradictions with atomic and nuclear physics. The analysis differs from the Schiff paradox (explained in VanBladel) which concerns Maxwell equations in rotating frames. The results are directly applicable to beam-beam interactions in electric-dipole-moment experiments in storage rings (dEDM, RevSciInstrum) and to the modeling of twisted electrons (BliokhSOI, ResonanceTwistedElectrons, PhysRevLettEQM2019). The paper enables the use of static Coulomb fields for calculating electric fields of moving nuclear charges and provides a classical foundation for treating twisted electron fields via immobile charge distributions.
