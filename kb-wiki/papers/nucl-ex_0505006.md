# Recent Physics At COSY--A Review

> The COSY accelerator in Jülich is presented together with its internal and external detectors. The physics program performed recently is discussed with emphasis on strangeness physics and precision experiments.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the recent physics program at the COSY accelerator in Jülich, with emphasis on strangeness production and precision experiments; the abstract glosses over the fact that many of the presented results are preliminary or show discrepancies with theoretical models, such as the ratio of associated strangeness production cross sections which none of the existing models fully accounts for.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the COSY accelerator, which provides proton and deuteron beams up to 3700 MeV/c momentum with electron and stochastic cooling, using internal detectors (COSY-11, ANKE, EDDA) and external detectors (TOF, BIG KARL, MOMO, Germanium Wall). The physics program covers associated strangeness production in pp, pd, and pA interactions, η-meson production, and precision measurements of meson masses and isospin symmetry breaking, with data taken near threshold where possible.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R = σ(pp → ppφ)/σ(pp → ppω) was measured as (3 ± 1) × 10⁻², while the OZI-rule predicts 4 × 10⁻³.
- The η-meson rest mass was measured by GEM as 547.311 ± 0.028 (stat.) ± 0.032 (syst.) MeV/c².
- The η-³He scattering length was determined as a = (−4.3 ± 0.3) + i(0.5 ± 0.5) fm.
- Evidence for a pentaquark Θ⁺ in pp → K⁰Σ⁺p was reported at a 4σ significance level.
- The pp → dK⁺K̄⁰ reaction was studied at an energy of 2.65 GeV, above the design value of 2.5 GeV.
- The extracted beam emittance is ε = 0.4π mm mrad.

## Context

This review extends the body of work on meson production near threshold at hadron accelerators, complementing earlier studies at facilities like CELSIUS and SATURNE. The results on associated strangeness production and the OZI-rule violation connect to theoretical work by Gasparian (2000), Sibirtsev (1999, 2000), and Shyam (2001, 2004), while the η-mass measurement challenges the NA48 result from Lai (2002). The isospin symmetry breaking studies enable future searches for η-nucleus bound states and further investigations of π⁰-η mixing with the WASA detector at COSY.
