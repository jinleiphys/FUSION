# Evolution of single-particle structure of silicon isotopes

> New data on proton and neutron single-particle energies $E_{nlj}$ of Si isotopes with neutron number $N$ from 12 to 28 as well as occupation probabilities $N_{nlj}$ of single particle states of stable isotopes $^{28,30}$Si near the Fermi energy were obtained by the joint evaluation of the stripping and pick-up reaction data and excited state decay schemes of neighboring nuclei. The evaluated data indicate following features of single-particle structure evolution: persistence of $Z = 14$ subshell closure with $N$ increase, the new magicity of the number $N = 16$, and the conservation of the magic properties of the number $N = 20$ in Si isotopic chain. The features were described by the dispersive optical model. The calculation also predicts the weakening of $N = 28$ shell closure and demonstrates evolution of bubble-like structure of the proton density distributions in neutron-rich Si isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the joint evaluation of stripping and pickup reaction data, combined with the dispersive optical model, reveals the persistence of the Z=14 subshell closure with increasing neutron number N, the new magicity of N=16, and the conservation of magic properties of N=20 in the silicon isotopic chain, while also predicting the weakening of the N=28 shell closure. The hedge is that these features are described within the spherical approximation despite the known stable deformation of silicon isotopes (e.g., β₂ = -0.42 ± 0.02 for ²⁸Si), and the evaluation for unstable isotopes relies on extrapolation from stable isotopes with assumed occupation probabilities.

## Method
The authors performed a joint numerical analysis of stripping and pickup reaction data on ²⁸Si and ³⁰Si targets, applying a penalty function to select complementary experimental pairs based on criteria including total spectroscopic strength, sum rules, and BCS approximation consistency. Single-particle energies and occupation probabilities were extracted using centroid methods from spectroscopic strengths, then used as input for the dispersive optical model (DOM) to calculate single-particle structure evolution across the silicon isotopic chain from A=26 to A=42. For unstable isotopes where spectroscopic data are unavailable, single-particle energies were evaluated from the spectra of neighboring odd nuclei, using occupation probabilities assumed from the stable isotope trends.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive optical model approach previously applied to magic nuclei like ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb (Mahaux and Sartor, 1991) to the silicon isotopic chain, which exhibits stable deformation. The results support earlier findings on the persistence of N=20 magic properties in the chain from ⁴⁰Ca to ³⁴Si and the gradual weakening of N=28 from ⁴⁸Ca to ⁴²Si (Bastin, Tarpanov). The evaluated single-particle energies and occupation probabilities enable future calculations of proton density distributions, including the predicted bubble-like structure in neutron-rich silicon isotopes, and provide a basis for testing shell evolution predictions in exotic nuclei near the neutron drip line.
