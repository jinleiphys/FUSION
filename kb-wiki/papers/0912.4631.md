# Dynamics of strong and radiative decays of Ds-mesons in the hadrogenesis conjecture

> The positive parity scalar D$_{s0}^*$(2317) and axial-vector D$_{s1}^*$(2460) charmed strange mesons are generated by coupled-channel dynamics through the s-wave scattering of Goldstone bosons off the pseudoscalar and vector D(D$_s$)-meson ground states. The specific masses of these states are obtained as a consequence of the attraction arising from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in the chiral Lagrangian. Chiral corrections to order Q$_χ^2$ are calculated and found to be small. The D$_{s0}^*$(2317) and D$_{s1}^*$(2460) mesons decay either strongly into the isospin-violating $π^0$D$_s$ and $π^0$D$_s^*$ channels or electromagnetically. We show that the $π^0$-$η$ and (K$^0$D$^+$-K$^+$D$^0$) mixings act constructively to generate strong widths of the order of 140 keV and emphasize the sensitivity of this value to the $KD$ component of the states. The one-loop contribution to the radiative decay amplitudes of scalar and axial-vector states is calculated using the electromagnetic Lagrangian to chiral order Q$_χ^2$. We show the importance of taking into account processes involving light vector mesons explicitly in the dynamics of electromagnetic decays. The radiative width are sensitive to both $ηD_s$ and $KD$ components, hence providing information complementary to the strong widths on the positive parity $D_s$-meson structure.

## Key claim

The D$_{s0}^*$(2317) and D$_{s1}^*$(2460) mesons are generated as molecular states by coupled-channel dynamics from s-wave scattering of Goldstone bosons off D-meson ground states, with the strong widths of order 140 keV arising from constructive interference between $\pi^0$-$\eta$ and ($K^0$D$^+$-K$^+$D$^0$) mixings, but the authors cannot make definite predictions for radiative decay ratios due to large cancellations between graphs involving $\eta$D$_s$ and KD channels.

## Method

The authors apply the hadrogenesis conjecture using leading-order chiral Lagrangians with Weinberg-Tomozawa interactions to generate the D$_{s0}^*$(2317) and D$_{s1}^*$(2460) as SU(3) anti-triplet bound states from coupled channels including $\eta$D$_s$, K$^0$D$^+$, K$^+$D$^0$ and $\pi^0$D$_s$ (for the scalar) and analogous channels with vector mesons (for the axial-vector). Chiral corrections to order Q$_\chi^2$ are included, and the strong decay amplitudes are computed from isospin-violating mixings while the radiative decay amplitudes are computed from one-loop diagrams using an electromagnetic Lagrangian to chiral order Q$_\chi^2$ that incorporates light vector mesons explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Strong widths of both D$_{s0}^*$(2317) and D$_{s1}^*$(2460) are of order 140 keV
- The $\pi^0$-$\eta$ mixing angle $\epsilon$ is determined to be approximately 0.01
- For the ratio $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_s$(1968) $\gamma$] / $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_s^*$(2112) $\pi^0$], the experimental value is 0.31 $\pm$ 0.06
- For the ratio $\Gamma$[D$_{s0}^*$(2317) $\rightarrow$ D$_s^*$(2112) $\gamma$] / $\Gamma$[D$_{s0}^*$(2317) $\rightarrow$ D$_s$(1968) $\pi^0$], the experimental upper limit is less than 0.059
- For the ratio $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_s^*$(2112) $\gamma$] / $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_s^*$(2112) $\pi^0$], the experimental upper limit is less than 0.16
- For the ratio $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_{s0}^*$(2317) $\gamma$] / $\Gamma$[D$_{s1}^*$(2460) $\rightarrow$ D$_s^*$(2112) $\pi^0$], the experimental upper limit is less than 0.22
- The calculated ranges for the radiative ratios using natural parameter sets are: 0.014-0.046 for the D$_{s0}^*$(2317) ratio, 0.08-0.15 and 0.001-0.004 for the D$_{s1}^*$(2460) ratios
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale $f$ is taken as 90 MeV
- An exotic axial state is predicted in the $\eta$D$^*$ invariant mass distribution at 2568 MeV with a width of about 18 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Kolomeitsev and Lutz (2004) and Hofmann and Lutz (2004) that were restricted to studying the masses of these states, now incorporating both strong and radiative decays within the same hadrogenesis framework. The calculated strong widths of 140 keV are smaller than the experimental upper limits of 3.8 MeV (D$_{s0}^*$(2317)) and 3.5 MeV (D$_{s1}^*$(2460)) from Aubert (2003) by a factor of about 20, and the results emphasize that strong decays probe the KD components while radiative decays probe both $\eta$D$_s$ and KD components, providing complementary information on the meson structure. The work enables future progress through more accurate experimental data, further coupled-channel studies, and improved knowledge of couplings from measurements or lattice studies.
