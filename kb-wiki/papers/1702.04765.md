# Hot magnetized nuclear matter: Thermodynamic and Saturation Properties

> We have used a realistic nuclear potential, AV18, and a many body technique, the lowest order constraint variational (LOCV) approach, to calculate the properties of hot magnetized nuclear matter. By investigating the free energy, spin polarization parameter, and symmetry energy, we have studied the temperature and magnetic field dependence of the saturation properties of magnetized nuclear matter. In addition, we have calculated the equation of state of magnetized nuclear matter at different temperatures and magnetic fields. It was found that the flashing temperature of nuclear matter decreases by increasing the magnetic field. In addition, we have studied the effect of the magnetic field on liquid gas phase transition of nuclear matter. The liquid gas coexistence curves, the order parameter of the liquid gas phase transition, and the properties of critical point at different magnetic fields have been calculated.

# Hot magnetized nuclear matter: Thermodynamic and Saturation Properties

## Key claim

Using the AV18 potential and lowest order constraint variational (LOCV) approach, the authors find that the flashing temperature of symmetric nuclear matter decreases with increasing magnetic field, and that the effect of the magnetic field on saturation density reverses sign depending on temperature: at low temperatures (below about 15 MeV) the magnetic field increases saturation density, while at higher temperatures it decreases saturation density.

## Method

The authors apply the lowest order constraint variational (LOCV) many-body method with the realistic AV18 nuclear potential to symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature in a uniform magnetic field. They compute the free energy per nucleon by minimizing with respect to effective masses and chemical potentials, using Fermi-Dirac distributions for spin-up and spin-down nucleons with Landau level quantization for protons. The two-body energy is obtained by minimizing the cluster-expanded energy functional under a normalization constraint, yielding correlation functions and the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Flashing temperature decreases from between 17.0 and 17.5 MeV at the given magnetic fields as the magnetic field increases; flashing density is 0.19 fm^{-3} and insensitive to magnetic field.
- Critical temperature decreases from 22.9 MeV at B = 10^{18} G to 21.8 MeV at B = 10^{19} G.
- Critical pressure decreases from 0.93 MeV fm^{-3} at B = 10^{18} G to 0.84 MeV fm^{-3} at B = 10^{19} G.
- Critical densities are nearly identical for different magnetic fields; the ratio gamma_c = P_c/(rho_c T_c) is 0.29, 0.28, and 0.28 for B = 10^{18}, 5x10^{18}, and 10^{19} G respectively, close to the value 0.28 from prior work.
- Saturation density at zero temperature and zero magnetic field is 0.310 fm^{-3} (from the authors' prior work).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of polarized nuclear matter at finite temperature without magnetic fields (Bordbar et al., various years) and of cold magnetized nuclear matter (Bordbar et al., 2016) to the combined case of finite temperature and strong magnetic fields. It uses the same LOCV approach with AV18 potential that has been applied to hot neutron matter and asymmetric nuclear matter by Modarres et al. (various years). The results for critical temperature (21.8-22.9 MeV depending on field) are higher than the experimental values of 16.6-17.9 MeV from heavy ion collision data (Natowitz et al.; Elliott et al.) and the theoretical values of 11.6-19.1 MeV from other approaches such as Self-Consistent Green's Functions with AV18 (Rios et al., 2008) and chiral effective field theory (Wellenhofer et al.). The work enables predictions for protoneutron star and heavy ion collision phenomenology where both high temperatures (20-50 MeV) and strong magnetic fields (up to 10^{19} G) are present.
