# Hard pion and prompt photon at RHIC, from single to double inclusive production

> Single pion and prompt photon large transverse momentum spectra in p-p and Au-Au collisions are computed in perturbative QCD at RHIC energy, s^1/2 = 200 GeV. Next-to-leading order calculations are discussed and compared with p-p scattering data. Subsequently, quenching factors are computed to leading order for both pions and photons within the same energy loss model. The good agreement with PHENIX preliminary data allows for a lower estimate of the energy density reached in central Au-Au collisions, epsilon > 10 GeV/fm^3. Double inclusive photon-pion production in p-p and Au-Au collisions is then addressed. Next-to-leading order corrections prove rather small in p-p scattering. In Au-Au collisions, the quenching of momentum-correlation spectra is seen to be sensitive to parton energy loss processes, which would help to understand how the fragmentation dynamics is modified in nuclear collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of high-transverse-momentum pions in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by parton energy loss in a dense medium, and that this same model predicts a smaller but measurable suppression of prompt photons. The key hedge is that the energy loss parameter ω_c = 20–25 GeV is extracted from fitting the pion quenching data, not predicted from first principles.

## Method

Single-inclusive π⁰ and prompt photon production cross sections in p-p collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV were computed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD using the CTEQ6M parton distribution functions, KKP pion fragmentation functions, and BFGW photon fragmentation functions. For Au-Au collisions, leading-order calculations were performed including isospin corrections, nuclear shadowing via the EKS98 parametrization, and parton energy loss modeled using the BDMPS framework with a Poisson approximation for the medium-induced gluon spectrum, where the fragmentation variable is shifted from z to z* = z/(1 − ε/k⊥). Double inclusive γ–π⁰ production was also computed at leading order.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Optimal scale for pions: μ_opt^π ≈ p⊥/√2; for photons: μ_opt^γ = p⊥/2
- Energy density lower bound from central Au-Au collisions: ε_RHIC ≳ 10 GeV/fm³
- Mean path length used in energy loss model: ⟨L⟩ = 5 fm
- Energy loss parameter range: ω_c = 20–25 GeV
- Number of binary collisions in central Au-Au (≤20% centrality): ⟨N_coll⟩ = 779
- Geometric cross section for Au-Au: σ_geo_AA = 6900 mb; nucleon-nucleon cross section: σ_NN = 42 mb
- Pion quenching factor: ~0.1 at p⊥π = 4 GeV, rising to ~0.3 at p⊥π = 20 GeV
- Photon quenching factor (including isospin and energy loss): ~0.6 at p⊥γ = 4 GeV, ~0.7 at p⊥γ = 20 GeV
- Isospin effect alone suppresses photon production by ~20% at p⊥γ = 20 GeV
- Scale uncertainty: ~30% for pions, ~15% for photons

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS parton energy loss framework (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff, 1997) to simultaneously describe single-pion and single-photon suppression in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, following earlier phenomenological analyses by Vitev (2002), Eskola et al. (2004), Dainese (2004), and Turbide et al. (2005). The paper also proposes double inclusive γ–π⁰ momentum correlations as a more differential probe of medium-modified fragmentation, building on the photon-tagging idea of Wang (1996) and earlier LHC-focused work by Arleo (2004). The results enable future experimental tests of whether the fragmentation dynamics is modified in nuclear collisions, and provide a lower bound on the energy density of the medium produced at RHIC.
