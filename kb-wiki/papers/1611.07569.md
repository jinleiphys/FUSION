# Geometrical Clusterization in SU(2) gluodynamics and Liquid-gas Phase Transition

> The liquid droplet formula is applied to an analysis of the properties of geometrical (anti)clusters formed in SU(2) gluodynamics by the Polyakov loops of the same sign. Using this approach, we explain the phase transition in SU(2) gluodynamics as a transition between two liquids during which one of the liquid droplets (the largest cluster of a certain Polyakov loop sign) experiences a condensation, while another droplet (the next to the largest cluster of the opposite sign of Polyakov loop) evaporates. The clusters of smaller sizes form two accompanying gases, which behave oppositely to their liquids. The liquid droplet formula is used to analyze the size distributions of the gas (anti)clusters. The fit of these distributions allows us to extract the temperature dependence of surface tension and the value of Fisher topological exponent $τ$ for both kinds of gaseous clusters. It is shown that the surface tension coeficient of gaseous (anti)clusters can serve as an order parameter of the deconfinement phase transition in SU(2) gluodynamics. The Fisher topological exponent $τ$ of (anti)clusters is found to have the same value $1.806 \pm 0.008$. This value disagrees with the famous Fisher droplet model, but it agrees well with an exactly solvable model of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. This finding may evidence for the fact that the SU(2) gluodynamics and this exactly solvable model of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition are in the same universality class.

# Geometrical Clusterization in SU(2) gluodynamics and Liquid-gas Phase Transition

## Key claim

The deconfinement phase transition in SU(2) gluodynamics can be explained as a transition between two liquids, where the largest cluster of one Polyakov loop sign condenses while the next-to-largest cluster of the opposite sign evaporates, but this interpretation relies on a specific cut-off parameter $L_{cut}$ for the Polyakov loop absolute value and applies only to the gaseous (anti)clusters, not to the droplets themselves.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of 3+1 dimensional SU(2) gluodynamics on a $24^3 \times 8$ lattice for 13 values of the inverse lattice coupling $\beta$ in the interval $[2.31, 3]$, identifying geometrical clusters of Polyakov loops of the same sign with a cut-off $L_{cut}=0.1$ or $0.2$ to exclude points with $|L(\vec x)| \le L_{cut}$ as auxiliary vacuum. They fitted the size distributions of gaseous (anti)clusters to the Fisher liquid droplet formula $n_A^{th}(k)=C_A\exp(\mu_A k - \sigma_A k^{2/3} - \tau \ln k)$ using a four-parameter iterative gradient search minimization of $\chi^2/dof$, then fixed $\tau$ and performed three-parameter fits to extract the reduced chemical potential $\mu_A$ and reduced surface tension $\sigma_A$ for both clusters and anticlusters.

## Key numbers

- Fisher topological exponent $\tau = 1.806 \pm 0.008$, identical for both clusters and anticlusters, determined from four-parameter fits with $k_{min}=2$
- Critical inverse lattice coupling $\beta_c = 2.52$ (compared to $\beta_c^\infty = 2.5115$ for an infinite system from Fingberg et al. (1993))
- For $L_{cut}=0.2$, surface tension exponent for clusters $B_{cl} = 0.2891 \pm 0.0016$ and for anticlusters $B_{acl} = 0.4483 \pm 0.0021$
- For $L_{cut}=0.2$, mean droplet size exponent for clusters $b_{cl} = 0.3359 \pm 0.0289$ and for anticlusters $b_{acl} = 0.3750 \pm 0.0129$
- For $L_{cut}=0.1$, surface tension exponent for clusters $B_{cl} = 0.2920 \pm 0.0012$ and for anticlusters $B_{acl} = 0.4129 \pm 0.0077$
- For $L_{cut}=0.1$, mean droplet size exponent for clusters $b_{cl} = 0.2964 \pm 0.0284$ and for anticlusters $b_{acl} = 0.3315 \pm 0.0269$
- The droplet size exponents $b_A$ are close to the 3D Ising model critical exponent $\beta_{Ising} = 0.3265 \pm 0.0001$ from Campostrini et al. (2002) and to the simple liquids exponent $\beta_{liquids} = 0.335 \pm 0.015$ from Huang (1987)

## Context

This work extends the percolation approach to deconfinement in SU(2) gluodynamics previously studied by Gattringer (2010) and Gattringer and Schmidt (2011), who focused only on the largest and next-to-largest clusters, by analyzing the full size distribution of all clusters using the Fisher droplet model formalism from Fisher (1967, 1969). The finding that $\tau = 1.806 \pm 0.008$ disagrees with the Fisher droplet model prediction $\tau > 2$ but agrees with the exactly solvable model of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition from Sagun et al. (2014), suggesting that SU(2) gluodynamics and that nuclear model may belong to the same universality class. The results enable using the reduced surface tension coefficient and mean size of the largest (anti)cluster as new order parameters for the deconfinement phase transition, and open the question of whether the power law for size distributions is found only for the gas of clusters at $\beta=3$ rather than at the phase transition point itself.
