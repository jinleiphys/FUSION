# Centrality and transverse momentum dependence of hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> The transverse momentum spectra of seven identified hadrons produced in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ and 5.02 TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have been investigated in the framework of the recombination model (RM). Soft, semihard and hard partons all play important roles in our model and are uniformly treated for all hadrons produced.The investigation has been extended to non-central collisions and the parameters controlling the momentum degradation of semihard partons have been tuned. Our study shows good agreement between theoretical results and experimental data. To be able to provide a coherent explanation for the production of all identified hadrons ($π$, p, K, $Λ$, $ϕ$, $Ξ$, $Ω$) for transverse momenta as high as 14 GeV/c (for $π$, p and K), and for all centralities, is an unprecedented achievement that supports the RM as a sensible model to combine various mechanisms of parton production with a universal scheme of hadronization.

## Key claim

The recombination model (RM) can provide a coherent explanation for the production of seven identified hadrons (π, p, K, Λ, φ, Ξ, Ω) across transverse momenta up to 14 GeV/c and for all centralities in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, but only after tuning the parameters controlling momentum degradation of semihard partons (γ0 and q0) and the normalization factors (C and Cs) for thermal parton distributions at each centrality.

## Method

The authors applied the Hwa-Yang recombination model, which treats hadron formation as the recombination of thermal (soft) partons and shower partons from semihard and hard partons. The shower parton distributions were computed from minijet distributions after momentum degradation through the medium, parametrized by a dynamical path length factor γi and a momentum degradation function G(k,q,ξ). The model includes contributions from thermal-thermal, thermal-shower, and shower-shower recombination channels for mesons and baryons, with the shower-shower channel from one jet equivalent to fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope parameters for thermal partons: Tq = 0.39 GeV (2.76 TeV) and 0.415 GeV (5.02 TeV); Ts = 0.51 GeV (2.76 TeV) and 0.545 GeV (5.02 TeV)
- Momentum degradation parameters for 2.76 TeV: γ0 = 2.8, q0 = 7 GeV/c; for 5.02 TeV: γ0 = 4.5, q0 = 7 GeV/c
- Normalization factors for thermal partons at 2.76 TeV, 0-5% centrality: C = 23.2 (GeV/c)^(-1), Cs = 11.0 (GeV/c)^(-1)
- Normalization factors for thermal partons at 5.02 TeV, 0-5% centrality: C = 22.0 (GeV/c)^(-1), Cs = 10.0 (GeV/c)^(-1)
- Nuclear modification factor RAA ~ 0.13 at pT = 6-7 GeV/c for 0-5% central collisions at both energies
- Resonance decay contribution parametrized as u(pT, Npart) = (2.8 + 0.003 Npart) e^(-pT/0.45)
- Probability Γ for two-jet shower recombination estimated as 0.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (Zhu, 2014) and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV at RHIC (Hwa and Zheng, 2012) to non-central collisions at both LHC energies. The recombination model contrasts with hydrodynamical models (valid for pT < 2 GeV/c) and jet fragmentation approaches (valid for pT > 8 GeV/c), filling the intermediate pT region where the baryon-to-meson ratio is large. The results enable predictions for Λ, φ, Ξ, and Ω spectra at √sNN = 5.02 TeV that can be tested against future experimental data from ALICE.
