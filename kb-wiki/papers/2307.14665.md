# Possible formation of Quark-Gluon Plasma in small collision systems at the Large Hadron Collider: Observations and Challenges

> With the advent of unprecedented collision energy at the Large Hadron Collider, CERN, Geneva, a new domain of particle production and possible formation of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions and the collisions of light nuclei has been a much-discussed topic recently. In this review, I discuss some of the recent observations leading to such a possibility, associated challenges, and some predictions for the upcoming light-nuclei collisions at the LHC.

# Possible formation of Quark-Gluon Plasma in small collision systems at the Large Hadron Collider: Observations and Challenges

## Key claim
High-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies exhibit several signatures traditionally associated with Quark-Gluon Plasma formation, including strangeness enhancement, collective flow, and ridge-like correlations, but the absence of jet-quenching and J/ψ suppression means the search for QGP droplets in small collision systems remains elusive.

## Method
This review examines experimental measurements from ALICE and CMS at the LHC, comparing observables across pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collision systems. The author discusses strangeness enhancement ratios, kinetic freeze-out temperatures and radial flow velocities extracted from Blastwave fits to identified particle spectra, and two-particle correlation functions showing ridge structures. Theoretical approaches including Glauber model extensions with anisotropic proton density profiles, multi-partonic interactions in PYTHIA8, and the UMQS model for charmonium production are reviewed alongside predictions for upcoming O-O and p-O collisions.

## Key numbers
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV: T_kin = 163 ± 10 MeV, extracted from simultaneous Blastwave fit to π, K, and p spectra
- Mean collective radial flow velocity in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV: <β_T> = 0.49 ± 0.02
- Bjorken energy densities in O-O collisions at √s_NN = 7 TeV exceed the lattice QCD predicted value of approximately 1 GeV/fm³ required for deconfinement transition, across all centrality classes
- Ridge structure observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV for charged particle pairs with 1 < p_T < 3 GeV/c, at Δφ ~ 0

## Context
This review extends the discussion of QGP-like signatures from heavy-ion collisions at SPS (WA97, 1999), RHIC (STAR, 2011), and LHC (ALICE, 2013) into the domain of small collision systems. The observations of strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity pp collisions by ALICE (2016, 2020) and the ridge structure reported by CMS (2015) challenge the traditional use of pp collisions as a baseline for heavy-ion measurements. The work enables future investigations with proposed p-O and O-O collisions at LHC energies, which may bridge the multiplicity gap between hadronic and heavy-ion collisions and test predictions for α-clustered nuclear density profiles in oxygen, building on low-energy nuclear physics work by Wheeler (1937) and Kanungo (2009).
