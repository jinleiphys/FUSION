# Chiral magnetic plasmons in anomalous relativistic matter

> The chiral plasmon modes of relativistic matter in background magnetic and strain-induced pseudomagnetic fields are studied in detail using the consistent chiral kinetic theory. The results reveal a number of anomalous features of these chiral magnetic and pseudomagnetic plasmons that could be used to identify them in experiment. In a system with nonzero electric (chiral) chemical potential, the background magnetic (pseudomagnetic) fields not only modify the values of the plasmon frequencies in the long-wavelength limit, but also affect the qualitative dependence on the wave vector. Similar modifications can be also induced by the chiral shift parameter in Weyl materials. Interestingly, even in the absence of the chiral shift and external fields, the chiral chemical potential alone leads to a splitting of plasmon energies at linear order in the wave vector.

## Key claim

The paper claims that chiral magnetic and pseudomagnetic plasmons in relativistic matter exhibit anomalous features, such as modified plasmon frequencies and qualitative changes in wave-vector dependence, that could be used for experimental identification, but these results rely on the consistent chiral kinetic theory (which includes a topological Chern-Simons correction to enforce local electric charge conservation) and assume weak background fields and the collisionless limit.

## Method

The authors used the consistent chiral kinetic theory, which incorporates Berry curvature and a topological Chern-Simons correction to the electric current, to derive the susceptibility tensor and solve the Maxwell equations for collective plasmon modes in a Weyl or Dirac material with constant background magnetic field \(\mathbf{B}_0\) and strain-induced pseudomagnetic field \(\mathbf{B}_{0,5}\). They assumed \(\mathbf{B}_0 \parallel \mathbf{B}_{0,5}\), considered weak oscillating electromagnetic fields as plane waves, and solved the linearized kinetic equation in the collisionless limit using cylindrical coordinates aligned with the magnetic field. The dispersion relations were obtained from the determinant of the wave equation, with numerical calculations using parameters for Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\).

## Key numbers

- Fermi velocity of Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\): \(v_F \approx 9.8~\text{eV\AA} \approx 1.5 \times 10^{8}~\text{cm/s}\) (used in numerical calculations).
- Refractive index of Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\): \(n_0 \approx 6\) (from Freyland).
- Characteristic pseudomagnetic field strengths in Dirac and Weyl materials range from \(B_5 \approx 0.3~\text{T}\) (static torsion on a nanowire of Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\)) to approximately \(B_5 \approx 15~\text{T}\) (bent thin film of Cd\(_3\)As\(_2\)).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chiral collective modes, such as the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) proposed by Kharzeev (2008) and the splitting of plasmon frequencies by chiral chemical potential studied by Akamatsu (2013) and Stephanov (2015). It also builds on the consistent chiral kinetic theory advocated by Gorbar (2016) to resolve the local electric charge nonconservation issue raised by Grushin-Vishwanath (2016) and Pikulin (2016) in strained Weyl materials. The results enable experimental probes of strain-induced pseudomagnetic fields and the chiral shift parameter in Weyl semimetals via plasmon spectroscopy, and could help distinguish between consistent and covariant implementations of the chiral anomaly.
