# The neutron star inner crust: an empirical essay

> In this work we study how the small contribution of the inner crust to the total equation of state (EoS) of a neutron star affects its mass-radius relation, focusing on the canonical mass of 1.4$M_\odot$. We build empirical EoS of the kind - $p = Kε^γ + b$ - in the range of 0.003 fm$^{-3}~<~n~<~$0.08 fm$^{-3}$ and also calculate the speed of sound in this region. We see that different behaviours of the speed of sound can affect the radius of the canonical star by more than 1.1 km. This result can help us understand extreme results as GW170817, where some studies indicate that the radius of the canonical star cannot exceed 11.9 km.

## Key claim

Different behaviors of the speed of sound in the neutron star inner crust, modeled via an empirical polytropic equation of state, can affect the radius of a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star by more than 1.1 km, even though the inner crust contributes only a small fraction of the total equation of state.

## Method

The author constructs an empirical polytropic equation of state of the form \(p = K\epsilon^{\gamma} + b\) for the inner crust in the density range 0.003 fm\(^{-3}\) < \(n\) < 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\), with parameters \(K\) and \(b\) determined by requiring continuous pressure at the outer crust-inner crust and inner crust-core boundaries. The outer crust uses the BPS equation of state, the inner crust below 0.003 fm\(^{-3}\) uses the BBP equation of state, and the core uses the Quantum Hadrodynamics model NL\(\rho\). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved for five values of \(\gamma\) (1/3, 2/3, 7/3, 4, and 6) to obtain mass-radius relations and gravitational surface redshifts.

## Key numbers

- For \(\gamma = 1/3\), the radius of the canonical 1.4\(M_\odot\) star is 13.87 km; for \(\gamma = 6\), the radius is 12.75 km, a difference of 1.1 km.
- The maximum mass for all models is 2.11\(M_\odot\), with a radius ranging from 10.81 km (\(\gamma = 6\)) to 11.13 km (\(\gamma = 1/3\)).
- The gravitational redshift for the canonical mass, \(z_{1.4}\), varies from 0.195 (\(\gamma = 1/3\)) to 0.218 (\(\gamma = 6\)), a difference of about 10%.
- The central density for the canonical 1.4\(M_\odot\) star is 0.42–0.43 fm\(^{-3}\) for all models.
- The pressure at the outer crust-inner crust boundary is \(1.509 \times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-4}\) at energy density \(1.402 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-4}\); at the inner crust-core boundary the pressure is \(2.409 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-4}\) at energy density \(3.838 \times 10^{-1}\) fm\(^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends studies that vary the core equation of state while fixing the crust, such as Rafa (2014) and Lopes (2014), which found that varying the symmetry energy slope \(L\) changes the canonical radius by about 0.9–1.1 km. The author shows that varying only the inner crust equation of state produces radius variations of the same magnitude, which is notable because the inner crust constitutes less than 20% of the total equation of state. The results are compared to the GW170817 constraint from Capano (2020) that the canonical star radius cannot exceed 11.9 km, and to Patra (2020) who found inner crust effects up to 0.7 km using Thomas-Fermi approximation but could not reproduce the 11.9 km constraint.
