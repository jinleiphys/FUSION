# Global Description of Beta Decay with the Axially-Deformed Skyrme Finite Amplitude Method: Extension to Odd-Mass and Odd-Odd Nuclei

> We use the finite amplitude method (FAM), an efficient implementation of the quasiparticle random phase approximation, to compute beta-decay rates with Skyrme energy-density functionals for 3983 nuclei, essentially all the medium-mass and heavy isotopes on the neutron rich side of stability. We employ an extension of the FAM that treats odd-mass and odd-odd nuclear ground states in the equal filling approximation. Our rates are in reasonable agreement both with experimental data where available and with rates from other global calculations.

# Global Description of Beta Decay with the Axially-Deformed Skyrme Finite Amplitude Method: Extension to Odd-Mass and Odd-Odd Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors extend the finite amplitude method (FAM) implementation of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) to compute beta-decay half-lives for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei using the equal filling approximation (EFA), achieving reasonable agreement with experiment and other global calculations, but with the important caveat that approximately 6% of unexamined odd nuclei require simple contour adjustments and about 15% may require more intricate corrections to avoid artificially inflated half-lives.

## Method

The authors use the Skyrme energy-density functional SKO' with the finite amplitude method (FAM) implementation of the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnFAM) to compute beta-decay strength functions, extending the method to odd nuclei via the equal filling approximation (EFA) which treats blocked quasiparticle states as statistical ensembles. They compute HFB ground states using the hfbtho code with three initial quadrupole constraints (beta2 = -0.2, 0.0, +0.2) and select the lowest-energy configuration, then calculate beta-decay half-lives by integrating phase-space-weighted strength along a circular complex energy contour, using a rational function interpolation of phase-space integrals on a 20-point Chebychev grid to avoid the Runge phenomenon.

## Key numbers

- 3983 total nuclei computed, of which 2998 are odd isotopes (odd-mass or odd-odd), from Z=20 to Z=110 out to the one-neutron drip line
- 985 even-even ground states obtained versus 1387 in the prior even-even calculation of Mustonen (2016), using the same functional
- Computation consumed approximately 270,000 Xeon core hours
- From a random sample of 100 odd nuclei, approximately 6% require simple contour adjustment and about 15% may require more intricate corrections; only one half-life in the random sample changed by more than 5% (it changed by 30%)
- 60 nuclei identified requiring simple contour adjustment (54 in suspicious set, 6 in random sample) and 41 requiring more careful corrections (26 in suspicious set, 15 in random sample), of which 33 have an imaginary pole in at least one multipole
- Bayesian error analysis yields most likely parameter values: b_c = 0.049, b_q = -0.082, sigma_q = 1.807, indicating hardly any systematic bias

## Context

This work extends the global even-even beta-decay calculation of Mustonen (2016), which used the same Skyrme functional SKO' and was restricted to even-even nuclei, to all isotopes that play a role in the r-process. The method builds on the EFA-FAM development of Shafer (2016), who applied it to rare-earth nuclei, and the pnFAM contour-integral approach of Mustonen (2014). The results enable more complete r-process nucleosynthesis simulations by providing beta-decay half-lives for odd nuclei that were previously treated with less consistent approximations, such as the perturbative treatment of Homma (1996) or the zero-quasiparticle approximation of Marketin (2016).
