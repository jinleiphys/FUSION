# Energy Level Displacement of Excited np State of Kaonic Deuterium In Faddeev Equation Approach

> We calculate the energy level displacement of the excited $np$ state of kaonic deuterium in terms of the P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering. We solve the Faddeev equations for the amplitude of $K^-d$ scattering in the fixed centre approximation and derive the complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering in terms of the S-wave and P-wave scattering lengths of $\bar{K}N$ scattering. The estimated uncertainty of the complex P-wave scattering length is of about $15\,%$. For the calculated width $Γ_{2p} = 10.203\,{\rm meV}$ of the excited $2p$ state of kaonic deuterium we evaluate the yield $Y_{K^-d} = 0.27\,%$ of $X$-rays for the $K_α$ emission line of kaonic deuterium. Using the complex S-wave and P-wave scattering lengths of $\bar{K}N$ scattering, calculated in \cite{ECL1,Weise1}, we get the width $Γ_{2p} = 2.675\,{\rm meV}$ of the excited $2p$ state and the yield $Y_{K^-d} = 1.90 \,%$ of $X$-rays for the $K_α$ emission line of kaonic deuterium. The results, obtained in this paper, can be used for the planning of experiments on the measurements of the energy level displacement of the ground state of kaonic deuterium, caused by strong low-energy interactions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the energy level displacement of the excited $np$ state of kaonic deuterium using the complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering derived from Faddeev equations in the fixed centre approximation, with an estimated uncertainty of about 15% for the P-wave scattering length; the results depend significantly on which set of $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths is used, yielding two distinct predictions for the width of the $2p$ state.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the P-wave amplitude of $K^-d$ scattering in the fixed centre approximation, expressing the complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering in terms of the S-wave and P-wave scattering lengths of $\bar{K}N$ scattering. The $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths were calculated within an $SU(3)$ coupled-channel approach using chiral Lagrangians with derivative meson-baryon couplings invariant under chiral $SU(3)\times SU(3)$ symmetry, including contributions from baryon resonances and scalar meson resonances. The energy level shift and width of the excited $np$ state were then expressed in terms of the complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering, and the yield of $X$-rays for the $K_{\alpha}$ emission line was evaluated using a quantum-classical Monte Carlo cascade model.

## Key numbers

- Complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering (their calculation): $\tilde{a}^{(1)}_{K^-d} = -0.352 + i\,0.432$ fm$^3$
- Width of excited $2p$ state of kaonic deuterium (their calculation): $\Gamma_{2p} = 10.203$ meV
- Yield of $X$-rays for $K_{\alpha}$ emission line (their calculation): $Y_{K^-d} = 0.27\%$
- Complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering (using $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths from ECL1 and Weise1): $\tilde{a}^{(1)}_{K^-d} = -0.174 + i\,0.113$ fm$^3$
- Width of excited $2p$ state (using $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths from ECL1 and Weise1): $\Gamma_{2p} = 2.675$ meV
- Yield of $X$-rays (using $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths from ECL1 and Weise1): $Y_{K^-d} = 1.90\%$
- Energy level shift of ground $1s$ state (their calculation): $\epsilon_{1s} = 0.766$ keV, $\Gamma_{1s} = 2.933$ keV
- Energy level shift of excited $2p$ state (their calculation): $\epsilon_{2p} = 4.158$ meV
- Complex S-wave scattering length of $K^-d$ scattering (their calculation): $\tilde{a}^{(0)}_{K^-d} = -1.273 + i\,2.435$ fm
- Complex S-wave scattering length of $K^-p$ scattering: $\tilde{a}^{(0)}_{K^-p}(K^-p) = -0.680 + i\,0.639$ fm
- Complex P-wave scattering length of $K^-p$ scattering: $\tilde{a}^{(1)}_{K^-p}(K^-p) = -0.069 + i\,0.179$ fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev equation approach of Oset (1981) from S-wave to P-wave $K^-d$ scattering, enabling predictions for the excited $np$ state of kaonic deuterium. The results can be used by the SIDDHARTA Collaboration for planning experiments on the ground state energy level displacement of kaonic deuterium. The paper also compares with the chiral dynamics calculations of ECL1 and Weise1, noting that the real part of the P-wave scattering length differs in sign from the earlier calculation of IV5 due to different chiral Lagrangian realizations.
