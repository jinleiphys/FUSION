# Medium modification of averaged jet charge in heavy-ion collisions

> Jet charge characterizes the electric charge distribution inside a jet. In this talk we make the first theoretical study of jet charge in high-energy nuclear collisions and calculate numerically the medium alternations of jet charge due to parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The parton multiple scattering in hot/dense QCD medium is simulated by a modified version of PYQUEN Monte Carlo model with 3+1D ideal hydrodynamical evolution of the fireball. Our preliminary results show that the averaged jet charge is significant modified in A+A collisions relative to that in p+p. The different features of quark jet charge and gluon jet charge in heavy-ion collisions, and the sensitivity of jet charge modifications to flavour dependence of energy loss are observed, which could then be used to discriminate quark and gluon jet as well as their energy loss patterns in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical study of jet charge in heavy-ion collisions, showing that the averaged jet charge is significantly modified in A+A collisions relative to p+p. The authors hedge that these are "preliminary results" and that the medium enhancement results mainly from the suppressed gluon fraction due to jet quenching, not from intrinsic changes to quark jet charge.

## Method
The authors used PYTHIA6 with the Perugia 2012 tune to simulate p+p collisions and a modified version of the PYQUEN Monte Carlo model to simulate parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma. The modified PYQUEN replaced the original 1+1D Bjorken expansion with an event-by-event 3+1D ideal hydrodynamical evolution of the fireball. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with radius R=0.4 in FastJet, with candidate particles required to have pT > 500 MeV and jet rapidity |eta| < 2.1.

## Key numbers
- Jet charge calculations were performed for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV at the LHC
- The power parameter kappa was varied between 0.3 and 1 to adjust the bias of hadrons with different transverse momentum
- Two scenarios of flavour dependence of parton energy loss were compared: Scenario I with f = Delta_E_g / Delta_E_q = 9/4 and Scenario II with f = 1
- The initial conditions for the 3+1D hydrodynamical model were tau_0 = 0.4 fm and T_0 = 0.39 GeV, compared to the original PYQUEN values of tau_0 = 0.6 fm and T_0 = 1 GeV
- Jet charge enhancement from medium effects is more pronounced with smaller kappa

## Context
This work extends the study of jet charge, originally proposed by Field and Feynman (1977) and recently measured by the ATLAS Collaboration (2015) in p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=8 TeV, to heavy-ion collisions for the first time. The authors use the PYQUEN model developed by Lokhtin (2005) and compare two scenarios of flavour-dependent energy loss (f=9/4 versus f=1) to address the puzzle of proton over pion ratios in Au+Au collisions at RHIC raised by Liu (2006) and Chen (2008). The results suggest jet charge can be used to discriminate quark and gluon jets and their energy loss patterns in heavy-ion collisions.
