# Status and challenges of neutrino cross sections

> Neutrino oscillations physics entered in the precision era. In this context accelerator-based neutrino experiments need a reduction of systematic errors to the level of a few percent. Today one of the most important sources of systematic errors are the neutrino-nucleus cross sections. The status of our knowledge of these cross sections in the different open channels in the few-GeV region, i.e. the quasielastic, the pion production and the multinucleon emission, is reviewed. Special emphasis is devoted to the multinucleon emission channel, which attracted a lot of attention in the last few years. It is crucial to properly reconstruct the neutrino energy which enters the expression of the oscillation probability. This channel was not included in the generators used for the analyses of the neutrino cross sections and oscillations experiments.

## Key claim

The multinucleon emission (np-nh) channel, which was not included in the generators used for neutrino cross section and oscillation analyses, is crucial for properly reconstructing the neutrino energy that enters the oscillation probability, and its inclusion resolves the apparent discrepancy between MiniBooNE quasielastic-like data and theoretical predictions without requiring an increase of the axial mass.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the few-GeV region for quasielastic, pion production, and multinucleon emission channels, with special emphasis on the multinucleon emission channel. It compares theoretical calculations from different groups (Martini et al., Nieves et al., Megias et al.) against flux-integrated double-differential cross section data from MiniBooNE, T2K, and MINERvA, focusing on CC0π and CCQE-like topologies.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the hundreds-MeV to few-GeV region are known with a precision not exceeding 20%, representing one of the most important sources of systematic uncertainties.
- The MiniBooNE CCQE-like measurement used a high-statistics sample of νμ events on ¹²C, and the comparison with a relativistic Fermi gas model using the standard axial cut-off mass MA = 1.03 GeV/c² revealed a substantial discrepancy.
- The T2K CC0π flux-integrated double-differential cross section on carbon, compared with calculations from Martini et al. and Nieves et al., shows that both models agree with data, and the data suggests the presence of np-nh contributions relative to pure CCQE RPA predictions.
- Selecting CC0π1p events (one charged lepton, 0 pions, exactly 1 proton) instead of CC0π events loses a factor of 3 in the number of events but allows true and reconstructed energy results to be quite close to each other.

## Context

This work extends the discussion initiated by Martini et al. (2009), who first suggested that multinucleon excitations (2p-2h states from NN correlations and meson exchange currents) could explain the MiniBooNE anomaly without increasing the axial mass. It contrasts with earlier analyses that used only the relativistic Fermi gas model with the standard axial mass MA = 1.03 GeV/c² from bubble chamber experiments. The review enables future neutrino oscillation experiments (T2K, NOvA, Hyper-K, DUNE) to reduce systematic uncertainties by properly including multinucleon emission in event generators and by exploring more restrictive final-state topologies such as CC0π1p for improved neutrino energy reconstruction.
