# Anomalous diffusion and quasistationarity in the HMF model

> We explore the quasistationary regime of the Hamiltonian Mean Field Model (HMF) showing that at least three different classes of events exist, with a different diffusive behavior and with a relative frequency which depends on the size of the system. Along the same line of a recent work \cite{epl}, these results indicate that one must be very careful in exchanging time averages with ensemble averages during the non-ergodic metastable regime and at the same time they emphasize the role of finite size effects in the evaluation of the diffusive properties of the system.

## Key claim

The quasistationary regime of the Hamiltonian Mean Field Model contains at least three distinct classes of dynamical events with different diffusive behavior, and the relative frequency of these event types depends on system size, implying that time averages and ensemble averages cannot be freely exchanged during the non-ergodic metastable regime.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of the HMF model for systems of N rotators with water-bag initial conditions (initial magnetization M0=1) at energy density U=0.69, integrating the equations of motion and tracking the temperature evolution and mean square displacement of phases for individual realizations. They classified single-run trajectories into three event types based on the number and temperature of quasistationary plateaux observed before final relaxation to equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N=15000 rotators at U=0.69, the equilibrium temperature is Teq ~ 0.476.
- Type 1 events show a single quasistationary plateau at T ~ 0.41, with relaxation beginning after t ~ 10^5.
- Type 2 events show a double plateau: first around T ~ 0.40, then relaxing to T ~ 0.38, with final relaxation after t ~ 10^5.
- Type 3 events show a single plateau at T ~ 0.38 (the limiting T_{N->infinity} for U=0.69), persisting up to t ~ 10^6 before abrupt relaxation.
- The three event classes become clearly distinguishable only for large system sizes N >= 10000.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Pluchino and Rapisarda, 2007) on ergodicity breaking in the HMF model, and addresses ongoing controversies about superdiffusion in the quasistationary regime reported by earlier studies (such as those by Latora, Rapisarda, and Tsallis). The results challenge the assumption that ensemble-averaged diffusion exponents (gamma ~ 1.4-1.5) are representative of individual trajectories, and suggest that claims of normal diffusion in the thermodynamic limit (e.g., by Yamaguchi and Bouchet) must be re-evaluated in light of finite-size effects on the relative frequency of event types.
