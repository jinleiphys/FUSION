# Decay of low-lying 12C resonances within a 3alpha cluster model

> We compute energy distributions of three $α$-particles emerging from the decay of $^{12}$C resonances by means of the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method combined with complex scaling. The large distance continuum properties of the wave functions are crucial and must be accurately calculated. The substantial changes from small to large distances determine the decay mechanisms. We illustrate by computing the energy distributions from decays of the $1^{+}$ and $3^-$-resonances in $^{12}$C. These states are dominated by direct and sequential decays into the three-body continuum respectively.

# Decay of low-lying 12C resonances within a 3alpha cluster model

## Key claim

The energy distributions of three alpha particles emerging from 12C resonance decays can be computed from a three-alpha cluster model using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with complex scaling, under the conjecture that the decay mechanism is independent of how the initial state was formed and that the final-state distributions are insensitive to the initial many-body structure.

## Method

The authors solve Faddeev equations in coordinate space using the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method, combined with complex scaling to obtain resonances. They use an Ali-Bodmer potential modified to reproduce the s-wave resonance of 8Be, plus a three-body potential chosen independently for each Jπ. The energy distributions are computed from the asymptotic large-distance behavior of the resonance wave function at a large value of the hyperradius ρ, separating components with two-body and three-body asymptotics to distinguish sequential and direct decays.

## Key numbers

- The 1+ resonance of 12C is located at 5.43 MeV above the 3α threshold (excitation energy 12.7 MeV); its energy distribution shows direct decay into the three-body continuum.
- The 3− resonance of 12C is located at 2.33 MeV above the 3α threshold (excitation energy 9.6 MeV); its decay is 96% sequential via 8Be(0+) and 4% direct.
- The sequential decay component for the 3− resonance produces a narrow high-energy peak at 2/3 of the resonance energy, with width given by the sum of the three-body decaying resonance width and the intermediate two-body resonance width.
- The 1+ resonance energy distribution shows "impressive agreement" with experimental data from Diget (2006); the 3− resonance shows "nice agreement" with preliminary data from Kirsebom et al. (unpublished, CMAM Madrid).

## Context

This work extends the three-body cluster model approach previously applied by the same group (Alvarez-Rodriguez 2007) to compute decay mechanisms of 12C resonances. It directly compares with experimental data from Diget (2006) for the 1+ state populated in beta-decay and with preliminary data from the 10B(3He,pααα) reaction studied by Kirsebom and collaborators. The method enables predictions for other astrophysically relevant three-body processes such as the triple-alpha process (3α → 12C + γ), the formation of 9Be (2α + n → 9Be + γ), and two-proton capture in the rp-process.
