# Minijet and Transverse Energy Production in the BFKL Regime

> Minijet production in hadronic and nuclear collisions through a BFKL pomeron ladder is studied for the energies of the future LHC heavy-ion collisions. We use unintegrated gluon densities compatible with the small-$x$ increase of parton distributions observed at HERA. We show that at LHC energies the BFKL minijet and transverse energy production is at most of the same order of magnitude as that in the collinear factorization approach.

## Key claim
At LHC heavy-ion energies, minijet and transverse energy production via the BFKL pomeron ladder mechanism is at most of the same order of magnitude as that from the conventional collinear factorization approach, but the BFKL mechanism may extend into the region below p_T ~ 2 GeV where collinear factorization breaks down.

## Method
The authors compute inclusive minijet production from a cut BFKL pomeron ladder between two colliding hadrons or nuclei, using unintegrated gluon densities f(x,q_T^2) obtained from the homogeneous BFKL equation and normalized to integrated gluon densities from global parton distribution analyses. The minijet cross section is derived by extracting one rung from the ladder, giving a factor α_s N_c/(π^2 p_T^2) times a convolution of two unintegrated gluon distributions. The normalization constant K_N cannot be determined from first principles but is argued to be at most ~1 based on comparison with measured jet cross sections and collinear factorization calculations.

## Key numbers
- At √s = 5.5 A TeV for central Pb-Pb collisions, the average transverse energy at y=0 from BFKL minijets with p_T ≥ 1 GeV is 3060 GeV (with α_s = 0.2) and 4940 GeV (with running α_s(p_T)), compared to 3060 GeV and 4870 GeV respectively from collinear factorization with p_T ≥ √5 GeV.
- The BFKL slope parameter λ = 4 ᾱ_s ln 2, with ᾱ_s = 3α_s/π; for α_s = 0.2 this gives a small-x rise ~ x^{-0.5}, matching the MRSD-' parton distributions.
- The nuclear overlap function for central Pb-Pb collisions is T_PbPb(0) = 32/mb.
- The BFKL calculation uses p_0 = 1 GeV as the lower cutoff, while the collinear factorization calculation uses p_0 = √5 GeV due to the MRSD-' parton distribution scale limitations.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of minijet production via collinear factorization (Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, 1994) by considering the alternative BFKL mechanism first suggested by Gribov, Levin, Ryskin (1994) and Levin, Levin (1994). The unintegrated gluon densities follow the analysis of Andersson, Kharzeev, Mankiewicz, Samuelsson (1995). The results enable comparison of two perturbative QCD approaches to semihard parton production in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies, and suggest that the BFKL mechanism may provide a controlled way to extend calculations into the p_T < 2 GeV region where collinear factorization fails. The work also connects conceptually to the McLerran-Venugopalan model of color charge distributions in nuclear collisions.
