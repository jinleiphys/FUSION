# A composite bosons minimal basis for the pairing Hamiltonian

> The pairing interaction among identical nucleons in a single-particle level is treated in the hamiltonian formalism using even Grassmann variables. A minimal (irreducible) basis having a remarkable symmetry property is set up using composite, commuting variables with a finite index of nilpotency. Eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of given energy, seniority and zero third component of the angular momentum of the pairing hamiltonian are then found. The eigenvectors, which cannot be cast solely in terms of composite bosons with angular momentum zero and two, are expanded in the minimal basis with coefficients analytically expressed in terms of a generalized hypergeometric function.

# A composite bosons minimal basis for the pairing Hamiltonian

## Key claim

The authors construct a minimal (irreducible) basis for the pairing Hamiltonian using even Grassmann variables, showing that the eigenstates of the pairing Hamiltonian for identical nucleons in a single j-shell can be expanded in terms of composite bosons with a finite index of nilpotency. However, contrary to the expectation that only two composite bosons (angular momentum zero and two) would suffice, the eigenvectors with seniority v=4 and above cannot be expressed solely in terms of s and d composite bosons.

## Method

The authors employ the Hamiltonian formalism using even Grassmann variables, exploiting the isomorphism between Fock space and the Grassmann algebra. They map the fermionic creation operators onto anticommuting Grassmann variables, then construct even, commuting, nilpotent variables φ_m = (-1)^{j-m} λ_{-m} λ_m from pairs of Grassmann variables. The pairing Hamiltonian H_P = -g_P B_0^† B_0 is expressed solely in terms of these φ variables. The authors then partition the Ω single-particle levels into two subsets (I and II) and construct a minimal orthonormal basis of n+1 composite bosons Φ_k^*, where each Φ_k^* represents a coherent superposition of all configurations with n-k pairs in set I and k pairs in set II. The eigenvalue problem reduces from a matrix of dimension Ω choose n to a tridiagonal (n+1)×(n+1) matrix, whose eigenvalues and eigenvectors are solved analytically.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the natural Fock basis in terms of fermionic degrees of freedom is {2Ω choose 2n}, while the basis in terms of φ variables has dimension {Ω choose n}, representing a major reduction.
- For n=1 pair, the eigenvalues are E_0 = -Ω (seniority v=0, degeneracy δ=1) and E_2 = 0 (seniority v=2, degeneracy δ=Ω-1).
- For n=2 pairs with Ω=4, the eigenvalues are E_0 = -6 (degeneracy δ=1), E_2 = -2 (degeneracy δ=3), and E_4 = 0 (degeneracy δ=2).
- The general eigenvalue formula recovered is E = -(n-p)(Ω-n-p+1), where p = v/2 and v is the seniority quantum number.
- The seniority degeneracy is given by δ_v = {Ω choose v/2} - {Ω choose v/2 - 1}.
- In the limit Ω >> n, the eigenvalues become y ≈ (v/2)Ω, and the eigenvector components for seniority v have v/2 vanishing components out of n+1 total components.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous path-integral approach (Barbaro et al., 1997) that used a nonlinear change of variables in the Berezin integral to define composite bosons for the pairing Hamiltonian. The present paper addresses the problem of excited states with nonzero seniority without resorting to the change of variables in the Berezin integral. The approach is related to the Arima and Iachello model (Iachello, 1974) for selecting relevant degrees of freedom, but provides a systematic procedure starting from a fermionic Hamiltonian. The results enable future extension to encompass the long-range quadrupole-quadrupole force alongside the pairing interaction, and the techniques developed have already been applied to set up a new perturbative expansion in QCD (Palumbo, 1998; Palumbo, 1999).
