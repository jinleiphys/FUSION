# Radiative decay of rho^0 and phi mesons in a chiral unitary approach

> We study the rho^0 and phi decays into pi^+ pi^- gamma, pi^0 pi^0 gamma and phi into pi^0 eta gamma using a chiral unitary approach to deal with the final state interaction of the M M system. The final state interaction modifies only moderately the large momenta tail of the photon spectrum of the rho^0 --> pi^+ pi^- gamma decay. In the case of phi decay the contribution to pi^+ pi^- gamma and pi^0 pi^0 gamma decay proceeds via kaonic loops and gives a distribution of pi pi invariant masses in which the f_0(980) resonance shows up with a very distinct peak. The spectrum found for phi --> pi^0 pi^0 gamma decay agrees with the recent experimental results obtained at Novosibirsk. The branching ratio for phi --> pi^0 eta gamma, dominated by the a_0(980), is also in agreement with recent Novosibirsk results.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach, which incorporates final-state interactions of the meson-meson system via unitarized amplitudes, reproduces the experimental photon spectrum and branching ratios for radiative decays of the rho and phi mesons, but the agreement for phi -> pi+ pi- gamma is qualified by the need to account for interference with the rho contribution in the experimental reaction e+ e- -> phi -> pi+ pi- gamma.

## Method

The authors compute the invariant mass distributions for the decays rho^0 -> pi+ pi- gamma, rho^0 -> pi0 pi0 gamma, phi -> pi+ pi- gamma, phi -> pi0 pi0 gamma, and phi -> pi0 eta gamma using a chiral unitary approach. The strong final-state interaction of the two mesons is described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian as the potential, regularized with a cutoff of about 1 GeV, following the method of Oller and Oset (1997). The radiative vertices are obtained from the chiral Lagrangian for vector mesons of Ecker et al. (1989), with couplings G_V and F_V determined from rho -> pi+ pi- and rho -> e+ e- decays, and the phi meson is introduced via ideal mixing between the SU(3) octet and singlet.

## Key numbers

* Branching ratio for rho^0 -> pi+ pi- gamma (K > 50 MeV): 1.18 x 10^-2, compared to the experimental value (0.99 +/- 0.04 +/- 0.15) x 10^-2 from Dolinsky et al.
* Branching ratio for rho^0 -> pi0 pi0 gamma: 1.4 x 10^-5 (with F_V G_V > 0) or 1.0 x 10^-4 (with F_V G_V < 0).
* Branching ratio for phi -> pi+ pi- gamma: 1.6 x 10^-4; for phi -> pi0 pi0 gamma: 0.8 x 10^-4, compared to the Novosibirsk result (1.14 +/- 0.10 +/- 0.12) x 10^-4.
* Branching ratio for phi -> pi0 eta gamma: 0.87 x 10^-4, compared to the Novosibirsk result (0.83 +/- 0.23) x 10^-4.
* Branching ratio for phi -> f_0(pi+ pi-) gamma (from integrating the f_0 peak): 0.44 x 10^-4.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Oset (1997) and Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1998) to radiative decays of vector mesons, going beyond the lowest-order chiral perturbation theory used by Bramon et al. (1992) for the same decays. The results agree with the Novosibirsk experimental data for phi -> pi0 pi0 gamma and phi -> pi0 eta gamma, and provide a test for the sign of the F_V G_V product. The approach enables further study of the nature of the f_0(980) and a_0(980) scalar resonances, with implications for planned experiments at Frascati.
