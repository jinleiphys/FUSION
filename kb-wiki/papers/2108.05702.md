# Study of invariance of nonextensive statistics under the uniform energy spectrum translation

> The general formalisms of the $q$-dual statistics, the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, and three versions of the Tsallis statistics known as Tsallis-1, Tsallis-2, and Tsallis-3 statistics have been considered in the canonical ensemble. We have rigorously proved that the probability distribution of the Tsallis-1 statistics is invariant under the uniform energy spectrum translation at a fixed temperature. This invariance demonstrates that the formalism of the Tsallis-1 statistics is consistent with the fundamentals of the equilibrium statistical mechanics. The same results we have obtained for the probability distributions of the Tsallis-3 statistics, Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, and $q$-dual statistics. However, we have found that the probability distribution of the Tsallis-2 statistics, the expectation values of which are not consistent with the normalization condition of probabilities, is indeed not invariant under the overall shift in energy as expected.

## Key claim

The paper rigorously proves that the probability distribution of the Tsallis-1 statistics is invariant under a uniform energy spectrum translation at a fixed temperature, contradicting the widespread belief that only the Tsallis-3 statistics possesses this property. The invariance holds specifically when the probability distribution is expressed in terms of the thermodynamic temperature rather than the Lagrange multiplier beta.

## Method

The author analytically derived equilibrium probability distributions for five statistical formalisms (Boltzmann-Gibbs, Tsallis-1, Tsallis-2, Tsallis-3, and q-dual statistics) in the canonical ensemble using the method of Lagrange multipliers to extremize the thermodynamic potential. For each formalism, the probability distribution was transformed under a uniform energy shift E'_i = E_i + E_0, and the invariance condition p'_i = p_i was checked by examining how the entropy, mean energy, free energy, and norm functions transform under the shift. The canonical ensemble for the q-dual statistics was studied for the first time in this work.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis-1 probability distribution is given by p_i = [1 + (q-1)/q * (Lambda - E_i)/T]^(1/(q-1)), where q is a real parameter with 0 < q < infinity
- The Tsallis-2 probability distribution p_i = (1/Z) * [1 - (1-q) * E_i/T]^(1/(1-q)) was found to be not invariant under the uniform energy shift
- The Tsallis-3 probability distribution p_i = (1/Z) * [1 + (1-q) * (E - E_i)/(T * Z^(1-q))]^(1/(1-q)) was proven invariant under the energy shift
- The q-dual statistics probability distribution p_i = [1 + (1-q) * (Lambda - E_i)/T]^(q/(1-q)) was proven invariant under the energy shift
- In the Gibbs limit q approaches 1, all Tsallis-type distributions recover the Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution p_i = exp((F - E_i)/T)

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Sisto (year not specified) that the Tsallis-1 statistics is invariant under energy translation, using a more general and explicit method. It directly contradicts the statements in Tsallis (1998) and the textbook by Tsallis (year not specified) that only the Tsallis-3 statistics is consistent with equilibrium statistical mechanics fundamentals. The paper also addresses uncertainties in high-energy physics regarding which variant of Tsallis statistics is correct for describing transverse momentum distributions in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies, where phenomenological Tsallis distributions are commonly used by Cleymans (2012), Bediaga (2000), and Beck (2000). The results enable the consistent use of Tsallis-1 statistics for single-particle distribution functions in high-energy physics, as previously found by Parvan (2017, 2020).
