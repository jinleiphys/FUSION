# Empirical pairing gaps and neutron-proton correlations

> Analysis of various mass relations connected with neutron-proton correlations in atomic nuclei is carried out. On the example of $N = Z$ chain it is shown that for self-adjoint nuclei various formulas proposed in literature for $ np $ pairing energy estimations lead to similar results. Significant differences between the calculation methods arise when nuclei with $N \ne Z$ are considered, which allows to show the complexity of neutron-proton correlations in different types of atomic nuclei and to make some assumptions on the correspondence of a mass ratio to the real effect of $ np $ pairing. The Shell Model parametrization of binding energy makes it possible to arrive to additional conclusions on the structure of mass formulas and their interrelationships with one another.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for self-adjoint nuclei with N=Z, various formulas for np-pairing energy estimation yield similar results, but significant differences arise for nuclei with N≠Z, revealing the complexity of neutron-proton correlations; the authors hedge that the correspondence between mass ratios and the real effect of np-pairing remains an assumption rather than a proven equivalence.

## Method

The authors systematically analyzed multiple mass relations from the literature for estimating neutron-proton correlations, including indicators based on binding energy differences (Δnp, Δnp^(7), δVnp), separation energy differences (Δnp^(6n), Δnp^(6p)), deuteron separation energy (Δnp^(3), Δnp^(4)), and the Madland-Nix OES approach (Δnp^MN). They applied these indicators to chains of nuclei with N-Z = Const (specifically N=Z, N=Z+1, and N=Z+2) using experimental masses from the AME2016 database, and fitted the A-dependence of each indicator to a power law Δ = C·A^(-b). The shell model parametrization of binding energy was used to interpret the structure of the mass formulas.

## Key numbers

- For the N=Z chain, Δnp(A) fits to C = 29.4 ± 1.8 MeV, b = 0.60 ± 0.02
- For the N=Z chain, Δnp^(7)(A) fits to C = 23.3 ± 1.6 MeV, b = 0.53 ± 0.03
- For the N=Z chain, Δnp^(3)(A) fits to C = 25.9 ± 1.3 MeV, b = 0.53 ± 0.02
- For the N=Z chain, Δnp^(4)(A) fits to C = 32.7 ± 2.1 MeV, b = 0.59 ± 0.02
- For the N=Z chain, Δnp^MN(A) fits to C = 19.9 ± 1.6 MeV, b = 0.48 ± 0.02
- For the N=Z+2 chain, Δnp(A) fits to C = 5.8 ± 0.8 MeV, b = 0.37 ± 0.04
- For the N=Z+2 chain, Δnp^(3)(A) fits to C = 10.8 ± 0.8 MeV, b = 0.33 ± 0.02
- For the N=Z+2 chain, Δnp^MN(A) fits to C = 15.2 ± 0.6 MeV, b = 0.41 ± 0.01
- The Wigner term in the droplet model was adopted as W = 30 MeV with a d-term for odd-odd N=Z nuclei
- Analysis of experimental masses gave d/W = 0.56 ± 0.27 (from earlier work by Janecke and collaborators)
- The correction δ from residual np interaction was approximated as δ = 20/A MeV (from Bohr and Mottelson)

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of mass relations for identical nucleon pairing previously performed by the same group (Ishkhanov et al., Chinese Physics C) to the case of neutron-proton correlations. It builds on the foundational work of Wigner (1937) on SU(4) symmetry and the Wigner term, the Garvey-Kelson mass relations (Garvey and Kelson, 1966), and the Madland-Nix formulation of odd-even staggering (Madland and Nix, 1988). The analysis enables future studies to select appropriate mass indicators for extracting np-pairing energies in different nuclear regions, particularly for nuclei far from stability where experimental masses are becoming available from the AME2016 database.
