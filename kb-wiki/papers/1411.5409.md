# Correlation between elliptic flow and shear viscosity in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The correlation between the elliptic flow $v_2$ scaled by the impact parameter $b$ and the shear viscosity $η$ as well as the specific viscosity $η/s$, defined as the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density $s$, is investigated for the first time in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions based on an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamic model. The elliptic flow is calculated at balance energies to exclude the geometric influence such as the blocking effect from the spectators. Our study shows that $v_{2}/b$ decreases almost linearly with increasing $η$, consistent with that observed in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. On the other hand, $v_{2}/b$ is found to increase with increasing $η/s$.

## Key claim

The paper claims a correlation between the impact-parameter-scaled elliptic flow \(v_2/b\) and both shear viscosity \(\eta\) and specific viscosity \(\eta/s\) in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, but only under the condition that collisions are selected at the balance energy where the directed flow vanishes, thereby removing spectator blocking effects.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamic (IQMD) model to simulate \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}+^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) collisions at beam energies of 40–60 MeV/u and impact parameters of 2–8 fm. They selected five specific combinations of beam energy and impact parameter where the slope parameter of directed flow \(F_d\) is closest to zero (balance energies), then calculated the average shear viscosity \(\langle \eta \rangle\), entropy density \(\langle s \rangle\), and specific viscosity \(\langle \eta/s \rangle\) from the participant zone using the Danielewicz parameterization and a hot Thomas-Fermi formalism. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) was computed from light fragments (charge number \(Z \le 3\)) at mid-rapidity and scaled by the impact parameter \(b\) to remove geometric effects.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter \(F_d\) of directed flow is closest to zero at five combinations: [50 MeV/u, 2 fm], [50 MeV/u, 4 fm], [50 MeV/u, 6 fm], [45 MeV/u, 6 fm], and [45 MeV/u, 8 fm].
- The \(v_2\) at [45 MeV/u, 8 fm] is about 10 times that at [50 MeV/u, 2 fm]; after scaling by impact parameter \(b\), this difference is reduced to about 2 times.
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\) is calculated using the Danielewicz parameterization: \(\eta(\rho/\rho_0, T) = \frac{1700}{T^2}(\rho/\rho_0)^2 + \frac{22}{1+T^2 10^{-3}}(\rho/\rho_0)^{0.7} + \frac{5.8\sqrt{T}}{1+160T^{-2}}\), where \(\eta\) is in MeV/fm\(^2 c\) and \(T\) in MeV.
- The saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is used, with Skyrme parameters \(\alpha = -356\) MeV, \(\beta = 303\) MeV, and \(\gamma = 7/6\) for a soft equation of state.

## Context

This work extends the study of the correlation between elliptic flow and shear viscosity from ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (where viscous hydrodynamic models are used, e.g., by Romatschke (2007), Song (2008), and Huovinen (2009)) to the intermediate-energy regime for the first time. It builds on previous studies of the liquid-gas phase transition via \(\eta/s\) using IQMD and BUU models by Zhou et al. (2013, 2012) and Li et al. (2011). The findings enable experimental extraction of shear viscosity and specific viscosity from elliptic flow measurements at balance energies in intermediate-energy collisions, where hydrodynamic models fail due to hadronic degrees of freedom.
