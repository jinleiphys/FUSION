# Relativistic Correction to Charmonium Dissociation Temperature

> By solving the covariant relativistic Schrödinger equations for a pair of heavy quarks, we obtained the wave functions for the ground and excited quarkonium states at finite temperature. In comparison with the non-relativistic calculation, the $J/ψ$ dissociation temperature determined by the infinity size or zero binding energy of the system increases $7%-13%$, when the central potential varies between the free energy and internal energy.

# Relativistic Correction to Charmonium Dissociation Temperature

## Key claim
By solving the covariant relativistic Schrödinger equations for a charm-anticharm pair, the J/ψ dissociation temperature increases by 7% to 13% compared to non-relativistic calculations, depending on whether the central potential is taken as the free energy or the internal energy. The relativistic effect makes the potential well deeper, allowing charmonia to survive in a hotter medium than predicted by non-relativistic treatments.

## Method
The authors used the two-body Dirac equation (TBDE) of constrained dynamics, reduced to a covariant relativistic Schrödinger equation for four-component spinors, to compute charmonium wave functions at finite temperature. The radial equations for spin triplet states (with coupled S-wave and D-wave components) and spin singlet states were solved numerically using the inverse power method, with the temperature-dependent potential taken either as the free energy F or the internal energy U extracted from lattice QCD simulations. The dissociation temperature was defined by the condition of infinite average size or zero binding energy of the c-cbar system.

## Key numbers
- J/ψ dissociation temperature with potential V = F: increases from non-relativistic 1.26 Tc to relativistic 1.35 Tc, a relativistic correction of 7%
- J/ψ dissociation temperature with potential V = U: increases from non-relativistic 2.1 Tc to relativistic 2.38 Tc, a relativistic correction of 13%
- Critical temperature for deconfinement: Tc = 165 MeV (taken from Digal et al. and Satz et al.)
- Charm quark mass: mc = 1.422 GeV; Coulomb coupling: α = 0.492; string tension: σ = 0.186 GeV²
- Fitted charmonium masses in vacuum: mJ/ψ = 3.113 GeV (experimental 3.097 GeV), mψ' = 3.692 GeV (experimental 3.686 GeV), mχ0 = 3.404 GeV (experimental 3.415 GeV), mχ1 = 3.504 GeV (experimental 3.511 GeV)

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation calculations of charmonium dissociation by Digal et al. and Satz et al., who found J/ψ dissociation temperatures between 1.2 Tc and 2 Tc using free energy and internal energy potentials from lattice QCD. The relativistic correction is expected to be negligible for bottomonium but significant for the lighter charmonium states. The results enable more accurate predictions for J/ψ suppression as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and the method can be applied to other charmonium states and to study the effect on excited states like χ0, which shows a smaller relativistic correction than the ground state.
