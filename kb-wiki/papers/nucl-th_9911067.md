# Band structure from random interactions

> The anharmonic vibrator and rotor regions in nuclei are investigated in the framework of the interacting boson model using an ensemble of random one- and two-body interactions. We find a predominance of L(P)=0(+) ground states, as well as strong evidence for the occurrence of both vibrational and rotational band structures. This remarkable result suggests that such band structures represent a far more general (robust) property of the collective model space than is generally thought.

## Key claim

Using an ensemble of random one- and two-body interactions in the interacting boson model (IBM), the authors find that vibrational and rotational band structures emerge in a significant fraction of cases, suggesting these collective features are a robust property of the model space rather than requiring fine-tuned realistic interactions. The hedge is that this result holds specifically for the subset of cases (63.4%) that have an L^P = 0^+ ground state, and the band structures are not universal but appear as two pronounced peaks in the probability distribution of energy ratios.

## Method

The authors used the most general one- and two-body IBM Hamiltonian with N=16 bosons, selecting the one- and two-body matrix elements independently from a Gaussian distribution with zero mean and specified variances. For 1000 random realizations, they calculated the full energy spectrum and B(E2) values between yrast states using a quadrupole operator with fixed parameter chi = -sqrt(7)/2. They then analyzed the probability distribution of the energy ratio R = [E(4^+)-E(0^+)]/[E(2^+)-E(0^+)] and the correlation between this ratio and the B(E2) ratio for 4^+ -> 2^+ versus 2^+ -> 0^+ transitions.

## Key numbers

- 63.4% of random Hamiltonians produce an L^P = 0^+ ground state
- 13.8% of cases have an L^P = 2^+ ground state, and 16.7% have the maximum angular momentum L^P = 32^+
- The probability distribution P(R) shows a peak at R ~ 1.95 (close to the harmonic vibrator value of 2.00) and a narrower peak at R ~ 3.35 (close to the rotor value of 10/3 ~ 3.33)
- About 50% of all cases with a 0^+ ground state concentrate around the vibrator peak, and about 25% around the rotor peak
- For the B(E2) ratio, the vibrator region gives a value of 2 (in the large N limit) and the rotor region gives 10/7 ~ 1.43

## Context

This work extends the finding of Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) that random two-body interactions in the nuclear shell model produce pairing-like features and 0^+ ground states, but contrasts with that work by showing that rotational band structures also emerge in the IBM framework, which were not found in the shell model study. The results are in qualitative agreement with the empirical observations of Casten, Zamfir, and Brenner (1993) and Zamfir, Casten, and Brenner (1994) showing robust tripartite classification of nuclear structure into seniority, anharmonic vibrator, and rotor regions. The paper enables future investigation of whether rotational features can arise from random interactions in the nuclear shell model with appropriate restrictions on the parameter space.
