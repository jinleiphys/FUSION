# Improved Thermometer from Intermediate Mass Fragments in Heavy-Ion Collisions with Isobaric Yield Ratio Difference

> \item[Background] Temperature is an important parameter in studying many important questions in heavy-ion collisions. A thermometer based on the isobaric yield ratio (IYR) has been proposed [Ma \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{86}, 054611 (2012) and Ma \textit{et al.}, \textit{ibid.}, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{88}, 014609 (2013)]. \item[Purpose] An improved thermometer ($T_{IB}$) is proposed based on the difference between IYRs. $T_{IB}$ obtained from isobars in different reactions will be compared. \item[Methods] The yields of three isobars are employed in $T_{IB}$. The residual free energy of the three isobars are replaced by that of the binding energy. No secondary decay modification for odd $A$ fragment is used in $T_{IB}$. \item[Results] The measured fragment yields in the 140$A$ MeV $^{40, 48}$Ca + $^{9}$Be ($^{181}$Ta) and $^{58, 64}$Ni + $^9$Be ($^{181}$Ta), the 1$A$ GeV $^{124, 136}$Xe + Pb, and the $^{112,124}$Sn + $^{112,124}$Sn reactions have been analyzed to obtain $T_{IB}$ from IMFs. $T_{IB}$ from most of the fragments in the $^{40, 48}$Ca and $^{58, 64}$Ni reactions is in the range of 0.6 MeV $ < T_{IB} < $ 3.5 MeV. $T_{IB}$ from most of the fragments in the $^{124}$Xe and $^{112,124}$Sn reactions is in the range of 0.5 MeV $ < T_{IB} < $ 2.5 MeV, while the range is 0.5 MeV $ < T_{IB} <$ 4 MeV from most of the fragments in the $^{136}$Xe reaction. In general, for most of the fragments $T_{IB}$ in the $^{40, 48}$Ca and $^{58, 64}$Ni reactions are very similar (except in the very neutron-rich fragments), and $T_{IB}$ from IMFs in the $^{124, 136}$Xe and $^{112,124}$Sn reactions is also similar. A slightly dependence of $T_{IB}$ on $A$ is found. \item[Conclusions] Using the binding energy of the nucleus, $T_{IB}$ can be obtained without the knowledge of the free energies of fragments. In the investigated reactions, $T_{IB}$ from most of the IMFs is low.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an improved thermometer (T_IB) based on the difference between isobaric yield ratios that uses binding energies instead of free energies, but the extracted temperatures are low (mostly below 4 MeV) and apply only to cold intermediate mass fragments, not primary hot fragments.

## Method

The authors use the canonical ensemble model to derive T_IB from the yields of three isobars with neutron-excess values I, I+2, and I-2, replacing the residual free energy with the residual binding energy from the AME12 mass evaluation. They analyze measured fragment yields from 140A MeV 40,48Ca + 9Be (181Ta) and 58,64Ni + 9Be (181Ta) reactions, 1A GeV 124,136Xe + Pb reactions, and 112,124Sn + 112,124Sn reactions, calculating T_IB for intermediate mass fragments without applying secondary decay modifications for odd-A fragments.

## Key numbers

- T_IB from most fragments in the 40,48Ca and 58,64Ni reactions lies in the range 0.6 MeV < T_IB < 3.5 MeV
- T_IB from most fragments in the 124Xe and 112,124Sn reactions lies in the range 0.5 MeV < T_IB < 2.5 MeV
- T_IB from most fragments in the 136Xe reaction lies in the range 0.5 MeV < T_IB < 4 MeV
- For I=1 fragments in the Ca and Ni reactions, T_IB is almost constant around 1.5 MeV
- The fitted mass dependence parameter C for T_IB in Xe reactions is close to 5.5 MeV (the value for hot primary fragments from Liu (2014)), while the slope parameter k is larger than 0.007

## Context

This work extends the isobaric yield ratio thermometer method previously developed by Ma et al. (2012, 2013) by using the difference between two isobaric yield ratios to cancel the chemical potential difference term, avoiding the fitting procedure required in the earlier IYR thermometer. The T_IB thermometer enables separation of the temperature term in isoscaling, isobaric yield ratio, and isobaric yield ratio difference methods, and the results support the assumption that temperatures can be treated as equal in similar reactions for the isoscaling and IBD methods.
