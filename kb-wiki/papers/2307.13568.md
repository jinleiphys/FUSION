# Optimized nuclear energy density functionals including long-range pion contributions

> Nuclear energy density functionals successfully reproduce properties of nuclei across almost the entire nuclear chart. However, nearly all available functionals are phenomenological in nature and lack a rigorous connection to systematically improvable nuclear forces. This issue might be solved with an energy density functional obtained from first principles. As an intermediate step towards this goal we construct the GUDE family of functionals that is obtained from a hybrid scheme consisting of long-range pion-exchange contributions derived from chiral effective field theory at the Hartree-Fock level and a phenomenological Skyrme part. When including pion contributions beyond next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, we find significant improvements over a reference Skyrme functional constructed following the same protocol. We analyze the importance of different pion contributions and identify which terms drive the observed improvements. Since pions are incorporated without adding further optimization parameters to the functionals, the improvements can be attributed to the functional form of these terms. Our work therefore suggests that the considered chiral contributions constitute useful ingredients for true ab initio energy density functionals.

## Key claim

The GUDE family of functionals, which adds long-range pion-exchange contributions from chiral effective field theory at the Hartree-Fock level to a phenomenological Skyrme part without increasing the number of adjustable parameters, yields significant improvements over a reference Skyrme functional when pion contributions beyond next-to-leading order are included, suggesting that these chiral terms are useful ingredients for future ab initio energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid energy density functional by adding Hartree and Fock terms from chiral pion exchanges (computed at the Hartree-Fock level using a density-matrix expansion) to a standard Skyrme functional plus pairing, Coulomb, and kinetic energy terms. The Skyrme and pairing parameters (15 total) are optimized to nuclear ground-state observables, while the chiral pion contributions contain no adjustable parameters; the Hartree terms are evaluated exactly via Gaussian expansions of the chiral potentials, and the Fock terms are approximated using the phase-space averaging density-matrix expansion. The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations are solved with the code HFBTHO using a harmonic-oscillator basis of 20 shells.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density bounds: 0.15 to 0.17 fm^-3; saturation energy bounds: -16.2 to -15.8 MeV; incompressibility bounds: 180 to 260 MeV; symmetry energy bounds: 28 to 36 MeV; slope parameter bounds: 30 to 80 MeV.
- The difference between exact and Gaussian-approximated Hartree energy in 208Pb at N2LO is about 5 MeV (on a total Hartree energy of about 4000 MeV), compared to 37 MeV with previous Gaussian parameters.
- The pairing cutoff energy is 60 MeV; the mixed-pairing density parameter rho_s = 0.16 fm^-3.
- The kinetic energy prefactor is hbar^2/(2m) = 20.73553 MeV fm^2.
- The regulator for chiral interactions uses R_c = 1.0 fm and n = 6.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid strategy of Gebr10DME, Gebr10DME3N, Stoi10DMEEDF, Gebr11DME, Dyhd16DME, and Nava18DMEEDF, which added chiral pion-exchange terms to Skyrme functionals, but corrects several implementation errors in Nava18DMEEDF (incorrect Gaussian fits, missing pairing cutoff, incorrect 3N Fock terms, and inconsistent axial coupling constant). The GUDE functionals enable a cleaner order-by-order assessment of chiral contributions and identify which pion terms drive improvements, providing a path toward true ab initio energy density functionals that are systematically improvable via chiral effective field theory.
