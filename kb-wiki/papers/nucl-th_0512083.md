# Superfluidity within Exact Renormalisation Group approach

> The application of the exact renormalisation group to a many-fermion system with a short-range attractive force is studied. We assume a simple ansatz for the effective action with effective bosons, describing pairing effects and derive a set of approximate flow equations for the effective coupling including boson and fermionic fluctuations. The phase transition to a phase with broken symmetry is found at a critical value of the running scale. The mean-field results are recovered if boson-loop effects are omitted. The calculations with two different forms of the regulator was shown to lead to similar results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the exact renormalisation group (ERG) approach, using a simple ansatz for the effective action with effective bosons describing pairing, can describe the phase transition to superfluidity in a many-fermion system with short-range attractive forces, but the results depend on the truncation scheme and regulator choice, with the chemical potential showing approximately 20% sensitivity to the regulator form even when the gap itself is more robust.

## Method

The authors construct an ERG flow based on the average effective action (AEA) for a many-fermion system with a contact interaction, introducing a bosonic Hubbard-Stratonovich field to describe pairing. They derive approximate flow equations for the effective potential parameters (u0, u1, u2), the boson wave-function renormalisation Z_phi, and the chemical potential, truncating the potential at quartic order in the boson field and using fermion-loop-only results to approximate higher-order coefficients. The evolution equations are solved numerically from a large starting scale K down to k=0 using two different regulator functions: a smooth step-function type (R1) and a sharp cutoff (R2), with parameters relevant to neutron matter (M=4.76 fm^-1, p_F=1.37 fm^-1).

## Key numbers

- The critical scale for the phase transition to the broken (superfluid) phase is k_crit ≈ 1.2 fm^-1 with the smooth regulator R1, and k_crit ≈ 0.7 fm^-1 with the sharp regulator R2.
- The physical gap in neutron matter is found to be comparable to the Fermi energy epsilon_F, of the order of 30 MeV.
- Boson-loop contributions to the gap are very small, at the level of 1%.
- The starting scale K must be greater than 5 fm^-1 to achieve scale-independent results for both regulator types.
- The chemical potential obtained with different regulators differs by approximately 20%, indicating greater sensitivity to the truncation than the gap.
- The gap becomes scale-independent at k ≈ 0.5 fm^-1 for the smooth regulator and at k ≈ 0.2 fm^-1 for the sharp regulator.

## Context

This work extends the ERG formalism to many-fermion systems with pairing, building on earlier ERG applications to few- and many-body systems by Birse, Krippa, and Friar, and on the average effective action approach of Wetterich. The fermion-loop-only results reproduce the gap equation and number density of Papenbrock and Bertsch (1999), while the full calculation including boson loops enables study of the BCS-BEC crossover by varying the scattering length from large negative values (BCS phase with positive chemical potential) to large positive values (BEC phase with negative chemical potential). The approach enables future calculations of superfluid properties in neutron matter and fermionic atoms in traps, though the 20% regulator dependence of the chemical potential indicates the need for improved truncations or inclusion of additional running couplings.
