# Chiral quarks, chiral limit, nonanalytic terms and baryon spectroscopy

## Key claim
The principal pattern in baryon spectroscopy associated with flavor-spin dependent hyperfine interaction is due to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and persists in the chiral limit; corrections from finite quark (Goldstone boson) mass are suppressed by the factor (mu/Lambda_chi)^2 and higher, contrary to claims that heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory invalidates the chiral constituent quark model.

## Method
The author analyzes the pion-exchange interaction between constituent quarks within the chiral constituent quark model, distinguishing between quark self-energy loop diagrams and pion-exchange interaction diagrams. The short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange interaction is shown to survive in the chiral limit, while the long-range Yukawa tail vanishes, and the volume integral of the interaction is shown to be zero for finite pion mass but discontinuous in the chiral limit. The analysis compares the chiral constituent quark model predictions with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) and large N_c approaches.

## Key numbers
- The constituent quark mass is approximately 300-400 MeV
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV
- Corrections from finite meson mass are suppressed by the factor (mu/Lambda_chi)^2, where mu is the Goldstone boson mass
- The nucleon axial coupling constant and quark axial coupling constant are related as g_A^q = 3/5 g_A^N within exact SU(6)
- The leading nonvanishing order of the pion-exchange interaction in the 1/m expansion is 1/m^2
- The Delta-N mass splitting is given by M_N = M_0 - 15 P_00^pi and M_Delta = M_0 - 3 P_00^pi, where P_00^pi is positive

## Context
This paper responds to a critique by Thomas and Krein (1999) who argued that the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska (1996) fails to implement correct chiral behavior of QCD. The author argues that HBChPT (Jenkins and Manohar, 1991) has no bearing on the short-range Goldstone boson exchange interaction because HBChPT deals with structureless baryons while the quark model includes explicit quark substructure. The work extends the chiral constituent quark model framework (Glozman, Plessas, Varga, Wagenbrunn, 1998) and is consistent with large N_c analysis (Dashen, Jenkins, Manohar, 1994) and combined ChPT analysis (Oh and Weise, 1999). The paper enables future development of a constituent quark chiral perturbation theory for systematic treatment of finite mass corrections.
