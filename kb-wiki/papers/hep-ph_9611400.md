# Non-equilibrium QCD: Interplay of hard and soft dynamics in high-energy multi-gluon beams

> A quantum-kinetic formulation of the dynamical evolution of a high-energy non-equilibrium gluon system at finite density is developed, to study the interplay between quantum fluctuations of high-momentum (hard) gluons and the low-momentum (soft) mean color-field that is induced by the collective motion of the hard particles. From the exact field-equations of motion of QCD, a self-consistent set of approximate quantum-kinetic equations are derived by separating hard and soft dynamics and choosing a convenient axial-type gauge. This set of master equations describes the momentum space evolution of the individual hard quanta, the space-time development of the ensemble of hard gluons, and the generation of the soft mean-field by the current of the hard particles. The quantum-kinetic equations are approximately solved to order g^2 (1+gA) for a specific example, namely the scenario of a high-energy gluon beam along the lightcone, demonstrating the practical applicability of the approach.

## Key claim

The paper develops a self-consistent quantum-kinetic description for a highly non-equilibrium, non-uniform, and anisotropic gluon system (specifically a gluon beam along the lightcone), where the separation of hard and soft dynamics is achieved by a momentum scale \(\mu\) that is at this point arbitrary and idealizing, rather than uniquely determined.

## Method

The author starts from the exact QCD field equations and uses the Closed-Time-Path (CTP) formalism to derive an infinite BBGKY-like hierarchy of equations for non-equilibrium Green functions. This hierarchy is truncated to a system involving only the 1-point function (identified with the soft mean color field \(\overline{A}_\mu\)) and the 2-point function (the hard gluon propagator \(\widehat{\Delta}_{\mu\nu}\)), by assuming higher-order correlators are small and that soft quantum excitations can be treated classically. The resulting field equations are then transformed into a set of three master equations using the Wigner-function technique and a gradient expansion, assuming the soft field varies slowly on the scale of hard quantum fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The hard-soft separation scale \(\mu\) is taken to be \(\mu \sim 1-2\) GeV, below which non-perturbative dynamics is expected to take over.
* The initial hard gluons are characterized by a very large momentum scale \(Q\), with typical energy and longitudinal momentum \(\sim Q\).
* The transverse extent of hard gluons is \(\lambda \sim 1/k_\perp \le 0.2\) fm for \(\mu = 1\) GeV.
* The quantum-kinetic equations are solved approximately to order \(g^2 (1+g\overline{A})\) for the specific example of a high-energy gluon beam along the lightcone.

## Context

This work extends previous developments in "hot QCD" (Blaizot and Iancu, 1990s) to the opposite extreme of a highly non-equilibrium, anisotropic gluon system, incorporating the aspect of quantum evolution and renormalization that was absent in that thermal context. It builds on the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1990s) and work by Makhlin (1990s) by adding the space-time development of the evolution, and it also relates to the non-equilibrium scalar field theory studies of Boyanovski et al. (1990s) using the CTP formalism. The framework enables a practical calculation scheme for the interplay between hard quantum fluctuations and the soft mean field in high-energy multi-gluon beams, relevant to small-\(x\) physics and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
