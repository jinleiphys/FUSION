# Comment on ``Relativistic cluster dynamics of nucleons and mesons. II. Formalism and examples''

> In a recent paper [Phys.\ Rev.\ C{\bf 49}, 2142 (1994)], Haberzettl presented cluster N-body equations for arbitrarily large systems of nucleons and mesons. Application to the three-nucleon system is claimed to yield a new kind of three-nucleon force. We demonstrate that these three-nucleon equations contain double counting.

# Comment on "Relativistic cluster dynamics of nucleons and mesons. II. Formalism and examples"

## Key claim
The three-nucleon equations proposed by Haberzettl (1994) contain double counting: the claimed new one-pion exchange three-nucleon force is identical to part of the iterated nucleon-exchange diagram that arises from the pionic component of the two-nucleon interaction, so including it as a separate three-nucleon force counts the same field-theoretical graph twice.

## Method
The authors evaluated the RCD cluster graphs B1 and B2 from Haberzettl (1994) using the RCD rules specified in that paper's appendix, assuming point-like pion-nucleon vertices. They compared these expressions to the lowest-order pionic contributions obtained from iterating the one-nucleon exchange diagram A once, which involves the two-nucleon t-matrix built from one-pion exchange. The algebraic forms of the two sets of diagrams were found to be identical, demonstrating that the same Feynman graph contribution appears in both the driving term and the iterated term of the RCD Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers
- The paper contains no new numerical results; it is a comment demonstrating a formal inconsistency in an existing formalism.

## Context
This comment addresses the relativistic cluster dynamics (RCD) formalism of Haberzettl (1992, 1994), which proposed cluster N-body equations for systems of nucleons and mesons. Haberzettl (1994) claimed that application to the three-nucleon system yields a new kind of three-nucleon force not included in previous calculations. Stadler and Tjon show that the claimed three-nucleon force double-counts contributions already present from iterating the two-nucleon interaction, and that the RCD equations contain this double counting generally because the structure of vertex functions depends on the underlying interaction. This work clarifies that care is needed when constructing relativistic few-body equations to avoid overcounting field-theoretical graphs.
