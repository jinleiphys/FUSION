# Duality condition for s- and t-channel exchange in nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We specify conditions under which the nucleon-nucleon interaction, based on the t-channel meson-exchange mechanism, is equivalent to an interaction generated via an s-channel exchange of six-quark bags. The duality is possible provided the alternation of zeros and poles of the non-dispersive part of D function takes place in the normalization where the imaginary part of D is non-negative and the CDD poles are the only poles of D.

## Key claim

The paper specifies that the duality between the t-channel meson-exchange description of nucleon-nucleon scattering and an s-channel exchange of six-quark primitives holds provided the non-dispersive part of the D function has alternating zeros and poles, in the normalization where the imaginary part of D is non-negative and the only poles of D are CDD poles.

## Method

The authors analyze the N/D representation of the scattering amplitude, starting from a hybrid Lee model with compound states and contact four-fermion vertices. They show that by multiplying the N and D functions by a rational function constructed from the zeros of the numerator function on the unitary cut, one obtains a D function with non-negative imaginary part, CDD poles, and zeros that correspond to primitives. This reduction procedure is then applied to the one-boson exchange (OBE) models and to the inverse scattering problem for s-channel exchange.

## Key numbers

* In the Benjamins-van Dijk model for the 3S1 channel, the zero of the numerator function with negative slope (primitive) occurs at T_lab = 354 MeV, and the zero with positive slope (CDD pole) at about 700 MeV.
* In the 1S0 channel, the primitive zero occurs at T_lab = 265 MeV, and the CDD pole zero at about 1 GeV.
* After reduction of the Benjamins-van Dijk model for the 3S1 channel, the compound state masses are M1 = 2167 MeV and M2 ~ 10^7 MeV, the physical state masses are M1 = 2047 MeV and M2 (high), and the CDD pole is at M = 2204 MeV.
* After reduction for the 1S0 channel, the compound state masses are M1 = 2310 MeV and M2 ~ 10^5 MeV, the physical state masses are M1 = 2006 MeV and M2 (high), and the CDD pole is at M = 2321 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Simonov-Dyson model (Simonov, 1981) and the earlier hybrid Lee model of Benjamins and van Dijk (1986) by establishing formal conditions for duality between s-channel and t-channel exchange mechanisms in nucleon-nucleon scattering. The paper builds on the concept of primitives introduced by Jaffe and Low (1979) and the Dyson model (1957). The results enable a systematic reduction of OBE models to s-channel exchange models and provide a framework for solving the inverse scattering problem for s-channel exchange interactions, with applications to nucleon-nucleon scattering in the 3S1, 1S0, 3P1, 1P1, and 3P0 channels.
