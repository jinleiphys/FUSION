# $r$-process Heating Feedback on Disk Outflows from Neutron Star Mergers

> Neutron star mergers produce $r$-process elements, with yields that are sensitive to the kinematic and thermodynamic properties of the ejecta. These ejecta properties are potentially affected by dynamically-important feedback from $r$-process heating, which is usually not coupled to the hydrodynamics in post-merger simulations modeling the ejecta launching and expansion. The multi-messenger detection of GW170817 showed the importance of producing reliable ejecta predictions, to maximize the diagnostic potential of future events. In this paper, we develop a prescription for including $r$-process heating as a source term in the hydrodynamic equations. This prescription depends on local fluid properties and on the $Y_{e}$ history as recorded by dedicated tracer particles, which exchange information with the grid using the Cloud-in-Cell method. The method is implemented in long-term viscous hydrodynamic simulations of accretion disk outflows to investigate its feedback on ejecta properties. We find that $r$-process heating can increase the unbound disk ejecta mass by $\sim 10\%$ relative to a baseline case that only considers alpha particle recombination. Nuclear heating also enhances the radial velocity of the ejecta with $Y_e < 0.25$ by up to a factor of two, while concurrently suppressing marginally-bound convective ejecta.

## Key claim

The authors find that including composition-dependent $r$-process heating in viscous hydrodynamic simulations of post-merger black hole-disk outflows increases the unbound ejecta mass by approximately 10% relative to a baseline that only includes alpha particle recombination, and enhances the radial velocity of low-electron-fraction ejecta ($Y_e < 0.25$) by up to a factor of two, while suppressing marginally-bound convective ejecta.

## Method

The authors implemented a prescription for $r$-process heating as a source term in the hydrodynamic equations using the FLASH code in two-dimensional axisymmetric spherical coordinates, with a pseudo-Newtonian potential for a spinning black hole of 2.65 $M_\odot$ and spin 0.8. The heating rate depends on local fluid properties and on the electron fraction history recorded by dedicated "memory" tracer particles (9.9 × 10^5 additional tracers), which exchange information with the grid using the Cloud-in-Cell method; the heating rates are interpolated from tabulated nucleosynthesis calculations for 50 initial $Y_e$ values from 0.01 to 0.50, assuming 50% of nuclear energy is carried away by neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Unbound disk ejecta mass increases by ~10% relative to baseline: from 0.027 $M_\odot$ (model al03nH, no heating) to 0.030 $M_\odot$ (model al03T4, with heating) for viscosity $\alpha = 0.03$
- Radial velocity of ejecta with $Y_e < 0.25$ enhanced by up to a factor of 2.09 (model al03T4) relative to the no-heating case, measured as the ratio $\mathcal{R}_{\langle v_r \rangle;< 0.25}$ at $T = 1$ GK
- Mass-averaged radial velocity at $T = 1$ GK for all unbound tracers: 2.85 × 10$^{-2}$ c (al03nH) versus 5.49 × 10$^{-2}$ c (al03T4)
- Total $r$-process heating energy $Q_r$ = 3.20 × 10$^{50}$ erg for model al03T4
- For higher viscosity $\alpha = 0.06$, unbound mass increases from 0.030 $M_\odot$ (al06nH) to 0.033 $M_\odot$ (al06T4)
- For higher threshold temperature $T_{\rm heat} = 6$ GK (model al03T6): unbound mass 0.031 $M_\odot$, $\mathcal{R}_{\langle v_r \rangle;< 0.25} = 2.78$, $Q_r = 4.18 \times 10^{50}$ erg

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to include $r$-process heating in post-merger simulations, which had adopted temperature-dependent but composition-independent heating rates (Just 2015, Wu 2016) or simple time-dependent parametrizations (Klion 2021), finding varying impacts on ejecta properties. The new composition-dependent prescription, using memory tracers and Cloud-in-Cell mapping, enables more accurate modeling of how heating varies with neutron richness, and the results can improve kilonova light curve predictions and nucleosynthesis yield calculations for future multi-messenger events like GW170817.
