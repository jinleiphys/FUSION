# Masses of the doubly heavy tetraquarks in a constituent quark model

> We perform a constituent quark model analysis for the masses of the doubly heavy tetraquark states $T_{QQ}$ after we fix the parameters to fit the masses of the newly observed $Ξ_{cc}^{++}$ and hadrons involving heavy quarks relevant to the stability of these states. We investigate in detail how the relative distances between quark pairs vary as we change the quark content and how they affect the various contributions to the total tetraquark masses. We also find that our full calculations give in general less binding compared to simplified quark model calculations that treat quark dynamics inside the tetraquark the same as that inside a baryon. We trace the main origin to be the differences in the number of relative kinetic energies which increases as one goes from meson, baryon and tetraquarks. We also compare our new results with previous works using less constrained parameters and find that the tetraquark state $T_{bb}(ud\bar{b}\bar{b})$ and $T_{bb}(us\bar{b}\bar{b})$ are bound by 120.56 MeV and 7.3 MeV respectively.

## Key claim

The authors find that the doubly heavy tetraquark states \(T_{bb}(ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) and \(T_{bb}(us\bar{b}\bar{b})\) are bound by 120.56 MeV and 7.3 MeV respectively, but their full constituent quark model calculations give in general less binding compared to simplified quark model calculations that treat quark dynamics inside the tetraquark the same as that inside a baryon.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian with Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential and a Gaussian-smeared spin-spin hyperfine interaction. They fix the model parameters by fitting to the masses of mesons and baryons containing heavy quarks, including the newly observed \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) baryon, then perform a variational calculation for the tetraquark states using a Gaussian spatial wave function with three independent variational parameters in the Jacobi coordinate set where the two light quarks and two heavy antiquarks are paired.

## Key numbers

* \(T_{bb}(ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S)=(0,1)\): mass = 10517.67 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = -120.56\) MeV (bound)
* \(T_{bb}(us\bar{b}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S)=(1/2,1)\): mass = 10684.4 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = -7.3\) MeV (bound)
* \(T_{bb}(ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S)=(1,0)\): mass = 10710.15 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +121.15\) MeV (unbound)
* \(T_{bb}(ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S)=(1,1)\): mass = 10722.23 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +84.0\) MeV (unbound)
* \(T_{cc}(ud\bar{c}\bar{c})\) with \((I,S)=(0,1)\): mass = 3964.90 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +98.62\) MeV (unbound)
* Fitted constituent quark masses: \(m_u = 326\) MeV, \(m_s = 607\) MeV, \(m_c = 1918\) MeV, \(m_b = 5323\) MeV
* Fitted \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) mass: 3612.37 MeV (experimental value 3621.40 MeV, error 0.25%)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model analysis of doubly heavy tetraquarks by Park, Noh, and Lee (2013) and Brink (1998), using the newly measured \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\) mass from LHCb (Aaij 2017, 2018) to constrain the model parameters. The authors find that their full calculation gives less binding than the simplified quark model of Karliner and Rosner (2014, 2017), and they trace this to the increased number of relative kinetic energy terms when going from mesons to baryons to tetraquarks. The results enable future experimental searches for bound doubly bottom tetraquark states and provide a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations of these systems.
