# Baryon inhomogeneities driven charge dependent directed flow in heavy ion collisions

> Electromagnetic field in heavy ion collisions are expected to cause charge dependent directed flow splitting ($Δv_1$). Such charge dependent $Δv_1$ has been observed by the STAR collaboration. We demonstrate that relativistic dissipative fluid dynamic simulations with baryon diffusion that include realistic model of baryon stopping in the initial condition and no contribution from electromagnetic field describe the measured $Δv_1$ for observables involving baryons and anti-baryons. This suggests strong background contribution from baryon current as a response to initial state baryon inhomogeneities to such charge dependent $Δv_1$ involving baryons and anti-baryons. Our current model calculations that only account for the evolution of the baryon charge and not electric charge and strangeness miss the observed $Δv_1$ of mesons leaving their interpretation open.

# Baryon inhomogeneities driven charge dependent directed flow in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Relativistic dissipative fluid dynamic simulations with baryon diffusion that include a realistic model of baryon stopping in the initial condition and no contribution from electromagnetic fields can describe the measured charge-dependent directed flow splitting ($\Delta v_1$) for observables involving baryons and anti-baryons at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27$ GeV. However, the model calculations that only account for the evolution of baryon charge and not electric charge and strangeness miss the observed $\Delta v_1$ of mesons, leaving their interpretation open.

## Method

The authors performed event-averaged smooth initial conditions from a Monte Carlo Glauber model over 25,000 configurations at each centrality class, with a tilted energy density profile and a separate tilted baryon density profile using a baryon deposition ansatz. These initial conditions were evolved using the MUSIC code for viscous hydrodynamic evolution with baryon diffusion, using a temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent baryon diffusion coefficient derived from the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation, followed by particlization on a constant energy density hypersurface and hadronic transport with UrQMD.

## Key numbers

* Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 27$ GeV were simulated; the initial proper time $\tau_0 = 1.2$ fm, initial energy density $\epsilon_0 = 2.4$ GeV/fm$^3$, and particlization energy density $\epsilon_f = 0.26$ GeV/fm$^3$.
* The specific shear viscosity was set to $C_\eta = \eta T / (\epsilon + P) = 0.08$; the baryon diffusion coefficient free parameter $C_B$ was set to unity.
* The model parameters included $\eta_0 = 1.3$, $\sigma_\eta = 0.7$, $\eta_0^{n_B} = 2.3$, $\sigma_{B,-} = 1.1$, $\sigma_{B,+} = 0.2$, $\omega = 0.11$, and $\eta_m = 1.1$.
* The model reproduces the sign change of $\Delta (dv_1/dy)$ for protons and anti-protons from positive in central collisions to negative in peripheral collisions, matching STAR data without any electromagnetic field contribution.
* The slope $F_\Delta = d\Delta v_1/dy$ for hadron combinations with non-zero net baryon difference ($\Delta B$) shows increasing trend with $\Delta B$ in agreement with STAR measurements.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Parida 2022) on baryon stopping and directed flow, and directly addresses the interpretation of STAR collaboration measurements (STAR 2023) that attributed charge-dependent directed flow splitting primarily to electromagnetic fields. The results contradict the assumption that the observed $\Delta v_1$ signals are clean signatures of the electromagnetic field, showing instead that baryon inhomogeneities and subsequent baryon diffusion produce a strong background contribution. The work builds on earlier hydrodynamic frameworks with tilted initial profiles (Bozek 2010) and baryon diffusion implementations (Denicol 2018). The inability to describe meson splitting points to the need for future hydrodynamic frameworks that independently evolve all three conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) rather than imposing local constraints like $n_S = 0$ and $n_Q = 0.4 n_B$.
