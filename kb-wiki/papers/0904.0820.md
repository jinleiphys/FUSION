# Concerning gauge field fluctuations around classical configurations

> We treat the fluctuations of non-Abelian gauge fields around a classical configuration by means of a transformation from the Yang--Mills gauge field to a homogeneously transforming field variable. We use the formalism to compute the effective action induced by these fluctuations in a static background without Wu--Yang ambiguity.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for treating non-Abelian gauge field fluctuations around a classical background using a transformation to a homogeneously transforming antisymmetric tensor field variable, which avoids explicit gauge fixing and ghost terms; the approach is demonstrated for a static background without Wu–Yang ambiguity, but the formalism breaks down when the determinant of the field-strength matrix in the adjoint representation vanishes, which includes the zero-field vacuum and Coulomb-type fields.

## Method

The author replaces the Yang–Mills vector gauge field \(A^a_\mu\) with an antisymmetric tensor field \(B^a_{\mu\nu}\) via a first-order formalism, then splits the gauge field into a classical background \(\mathcal{A}^a_\mu\) and fluctuations \(a^a_\mu\). After integrating out the fluctuation field \(a^a_\mu\), the generating functional is expressed in terms of \(B^a_{\mu\nu}\) and its fluctuations \(b^a_{\mu\nu}\), with a determinant factor \(\Det^{-1/2}\mathbbm{B}\) arising from the integration. The effective action induced by fluctuations is then computed for a three-dimensional Euclidean \(SU(2)\) system with a constant background field configuration that satisfies \(\det\mathbbm{F}\neq 0\).

## Key numbers

* For the three-dimensional Euclidean \(SU(2)\) example with background \(\mathcal{A}^1_1=\mathcal{A}^2_2=\mathcal{A}^3_3=\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{F}=g\mathcal{AA}\), the effective Lagrangian after renormalisation is \(\mathcal{L}^{(1)} = |g\mathcal{F}|^{3/2}/(3\pi)\).
* The determinant of the fluctuation propagator for this background is \(\det(G^{-1}G_\varepsilon/\varepsilon^2) = \varepsilon^{-10} (p^4+|2g\mathcal{F}|^2) (p^4+|2g\varepsilon^2\mathcal{F}|^2)^{-1}\).
* In \(d\) dimensions, the effective action exhibits a \(\sim |g\mathcal{F}|^{d/2}\) behaviour.

## Context

This work extends the background field method (BFM) for non-Abelian gauge theories by providing an alternative formulation that avoids gauge fixing and ghost fields, unlike the standard Faddeev–Popov approach. The formalism connects to earlier work on first-order formulations by Deser (1976) and to studies of constant-field effective actions by Blau (1988). The criterion \(\det\mathbbm{F}=0\) links background field configurations with instabilities (such as Coulomb fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions, as studied by McLerran and Venugopalan) to Wu–Yang ambiguities, and the paper suggests that in the presence of such ambiguities not all observables can be expressed in terms of invariants constructed solely from the field tensor.
