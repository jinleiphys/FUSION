# Initial conditions for hydrodynamics: implications for phenomenology

> It is shown how the initial azimuthally asymmetric flow develops from the free-streaming + sudden equilibration approximation to the early dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Consequences for the hydrodynamics and physical results are discussed.

# Initial conditions for hydrodynamics: implications for phenomenology

## Key claim
The free-streaming plus sudden equilibration (FS+SE) approximation to early-stage dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions generates an initial azimuthally asymmetric flow at the moment of equilibration, contrary to the previous assumption that FS+SE unavoidably reduces elliptic flow. The sudden equilibration (Landau matching) causes the momentum anisotropy \(\epsilon_p\) to jump to a nonzero value, compensating for the spatial anisotropy reduction during free streaming.

## Method
The authors model the early stage of a boost-invariant system with an initial Gaussian transverse energy profile, where massless partons free-stream from initial proper time \(\tau_0\) to final proper time \(\tau\), then undergo sudden equilibration via Landau matching to a perfect hydrodynamic form (isotropic or transverse). The energy-momentum tensor is computed from the free-streaming phase-space evolution, and the resulting initial flow and anisotropy are used as initial conditions for subsequent perfect hydrodynamics, with results compared to hydrodynamics-only simulations.

## Key numbers
- Initial free-streaming time \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm, final free-streaming time \(\tau = 1.0\) fm for the comparison case.
- The transverse velocity near the origin for low free-streaming times is given by \(\mathbf{v} = -\frac{1}{3}(\tau - \tau_0) \nabla n / n\).
- The momentum anisotropy \(\epsilon_p = (\langle T_{xx}\rangle - \langle T_{yy}\rangle)/(\langle T_{xx}\rangle + \langle T_{yy}\rangle)\) jumps from zero during free streaming to a nonzero value at sudden equilibration.
- The pion HBT radii \(R_{\text{side}}\), \(R_{\text{out}}\), \(R_{\text{long}}\), and the ratio \(R_{\text{out}}/R_{\text{side}}\) from the FS+SE model match data from Adams (2004) and are strikingly similar to hydrodynamics-only results.

## Context
This work extends the FS+SE approximation proposed by Kolb, Sollfrank, and Heinz (2000) and further discussed by Jas and Mrowczynski (2007) and Sinyukov, Gyulassy, Karpenko, and Nazarenko (2006-2008). It contradicts the earlier assumption that FS+SE reduces elliptic flow, showing instead that the sudden equilibration generates initial azimuthally asymmetric flow. The approach enables delaying the start of perfect hydrodynamics to "comfortable" times of about 1 fm/c while preserving physical results, in contrast to studies using hydro with initialization times of 0.1 fm/c (Pratt, 2008). The phenomenological relevance of initial flow was first examined in hydrodynamics by Chojnacki (2004) and emphasized by Pratt (2008) and Lisa (2008).
