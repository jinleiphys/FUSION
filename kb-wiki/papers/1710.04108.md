# Collective Neutrino Oscillations and Nucleosynthesis

> The emergent phenomenon of collective neutrino oscillations arises from neutrino-neutrino interactions in environments with very large number of neutrinos. Since such environments are likely sites of the heavy-element synthesis, understanding all aspects of collective neutrino oscillations seems to be necessary for a complete accounting of nucleosynthesis. I briefly summarize some of the salient features along with recent work on the properties and astrophysical applications of the collective neutrino oscillations.

# Collective Neutrino Oscillations and Nucleosynthesis

## Key claim
Collective neutrino oscillations, driven by neutrino-neutrino interactions in dense astrophysical environments, can significantly influence nucleosynthesis yields in the νp process, but the magnitude of this effect depends on the specific wind trajectory and is subject to uncertainties in the initial neutrino fluxes and hydrodynamic quantities.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical framework of collective neutrino oscillations using the neutrino flavor isospin algebra, presenting the many-body Hamiltonian with one-body (mass and matter) and two-body (neutrino-neutrino) terms. Exact solutions are obtained via the Bethe ansatz for the single-angle approximation, and the spectral split phenomenon is analyzed through both mean-field and full many-body calculations. Multi-angle three-flavor calculations are coupled with nucleosynthesis network calculations for two proton-rich neutrino-driven wind trajectories from a one-dimensional supernova explosion simulation.

## Key numbers
- A proto-neutron star emits neutrinos with total energy of ∼10^59 MeV; if average neutrino energy is ∼10 MeV, this corresponds to 10^58 neutrinos streaming out of the core.
- In a toy model with two neutrinos (j1 = j2 = 1/2), the Bethe ansatz solutions for N=1 are x± = (ω1 + ω2)/2 + (1/2)μ ± (1/2)√((ω1−ω2)^2 + μ^2), and for N=2 are x1,2 = (ω1 + ω2)/2 + (1/2)μ ± (1/2)√((ω1−ω2)^2 − μ^2).
- In Ref. Pehlivan (2016), the adiabatic evolution of a full many-body state of 250 electron neutrinos with inverted hierarchy was followed as μ decreased from a very high value to zero.
- For the later wind trajectory at t=1.1 s after core bounce, collective oscillations increase energetic ν̄e leading to an enhancement of the νp process about up to 20 times larger than that obtained in Ref. MartinezPinedo (2011).

## Context
This work extends the study of collective neutrino oscillations from core-collapse supernovae, merging neutron stars, and gamma-ray burst environments, building on the formalism developed by Balantekin (2006) and Pehlivan (2011) for exact many-body solutions via Bethe ansatz. The paper contrasts single-angle approximations, which produce complete spectral swaps as described by Raffelt (2007) and Duan (2008), with multi-angle calculations that delay the onset of collective oscillations and yield drastically different nucleosynthesis results, as shown by Duan (2010) for the supernova r-process. The application to νp process nucleosynthesis in proton-rich winds, following the work of MartinezPinedo (2011), demonstrates that collective oscillations can enhance p-nuclei abundances, but the results depend on the wind trajectory and require further systematic studies of neutrino physics and hydrodynamics input.
