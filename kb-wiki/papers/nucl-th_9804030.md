# Inclusive quasielastic scattering of polarized electrons from polarized nuclei

> The inclusive quasielastic response functions that appear in the scattering of polarized electrons from polarized nuclei are computed and analyzed for several closed-shell-minus-one nuclei with special attention paid to 39K. Results are presented using two models for the ejected nucleon --- when described by a distorted wave in the continuum shell model or by a plane wave in PWIA with on- and off-shell nucleons. Relativistic effects in kinematics and in the electromagnetic current have been incorporated throughout. Specifically, the recently obtained expansion of the electromagnetic current in powers only of the struck nucleon's momentum is employed for the on-shell current and the effects of the first-order terms (spin-orbit and convection) are compared with the zeroth-order (charge and magnetization) contributions. The use of polarized inclusive quasielastic electron scattering as a tool for determining near-valence nucleon momentum distributions is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper computes and analyzes inclusive quasielastic response functions for polarized electrons scattering from polarized nuclei, focusing on one-proton-hole nuclei (especially 39K). The authors show that polarization observables in inclusive scattering can serve as a tool for determining near-valence nucleon momentum distributions, but the results depend on the model used for the ejected nucleon (distorted wave in the continuum shell model versus plane wave in PWIA) and on whether on-shell or off-shell single-nucleon currents are employed.

## Method

The authors computed the six nuclear response functions (L, T, TL, TT, T', TL') for inclusive quasielastic scattering of polarized electrons from polarized one-hole nuclei using two models: the continuum shell model (CSM) with a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential for both bound and continuum states, and the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) with both on-shell and off-shell single-nucleon currents. Relativistic kinematics and a recently developed expansion of the electromagnetic current in powers of the struck nucleon's momentum (η = p/M) were incorporated, with first-order terms (spin-orbit and convection) compared to zeroth-order (charge and magnetization) contributions. The multipole expansion was used for the CSM calculations, while the PWIA allowed factorization of responses into single-nucleon responses and the spectral function of the polarized nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer values of q ∼ 500 MeV/c were considered in the quasielastic region.
- The energy of the outgoing nucleon in the quasielastic region is very high compared to bound nucleon energies, especially for polarization-dependent responses where only the outer shell contributes.
- The number of multipoles needed for convergence grows with mass number A and with momentum transfer, as detailed in table 2 of Amaro (1996).
- For the longitudinal response, the unpolarized closed-shell contribution is given by 4π Σ_{phJ} δ(ε_p − ε_h − ω) t_{CJ}^2.
- The Fano tensor for 100% polarization is f^i_𝒥 = ⟨J_i J_i J_i − J_i|𝒥 0⟩.
- The unpolarized momentum distribution of a complete shell is given by n^{(h)}_{rr'}(p)_{unpol} = δ_{rr'} (1/(8π)) [j_h]^2 |\tilde{R}_h(p)|^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of polarization in quasielastic electron scattering, which had focused primarily on exclusive coincidence observables (Raskin and Donnelly, 1989; Boffi et al., 1993; Caballero et al., 1993; Garrido et al., 1995) or on light nuclei like ²H and ³He (Gao et al., 1994; Hansen et al., 1995). The authors apply the formalism of Donnelly (1986) for polarized nuclear responses to medium and heavy nuclei, using the continuum shell model approach of Amaro (1994) and the on-shell current expansion of Amaro (1996). The results enable future experimental extraction of near-valence nucleon momentum distributions using polarized inclusive quasielastic scattering, and provide a baseline for comparing distorted-wave and plane-wave approximations at high momentum transfers where final-state interactions become less important.
