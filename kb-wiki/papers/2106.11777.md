# Beyond the relaxation time approximation

> The relaxation time approximation (RTA) is a well known method of describing the time evolution of a statistical ensemble by linking distributions of the variables of interest at different stages of their temporal evolution. We show that if all the distributions occurring in the RTA have the same functional form of a quasi-power Tsallis distribution the time evolution of which depends on the time evolution of its control parameter, nonextensivity $q(t)$, then it is more convenient to consider only the time evolution of this control parameter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that if all distributions in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) have the same functional form of a quasi-power Tsallis distribution, the time evolution of the system can be described solely by the time evolution of the nonextensivity parameter \(q(t)\), replacing the two-component final distribution from standard RTA with a single smoothly evolving distribution.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann transport equation with the RTA collision term and assume all probability distributions are Tsallis distributions characterized by a time-dependent nonextensivity parameter \(q(t)\). They derive an equation of motion for \(q(t)\) analogous to the RTA relaxation equation, \(dq/dt = (q_{eq} - q)/\tau\), with \(q_{eq}=1\) corresponding to the Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium distribution. They then compare the relaxation times \(\tau_f\) (for the full distribution in standard RTA) and \(\tau_q\) (for the control parameter in their approach) and relate the time evolution of \(q(t)\) to fluctuations of the scale parameter \(x_0\).

## Key numbers

* For the Tsallis distribution, the mean value \(\langle z(t) \rangle = 1/(3-2q(t))\) and variance \(Var(z) = (2-q(t))/[(3-2q(t))^2(4-3q(t))]\).
* The ratio of relaxation times \(\tau_f/\tau_q\) changes from \(\tau_f/\tau_q = 1/\langle z\rangle_{in}\) for \(t\rightarrow 0\) to \(\tau_f/\tau_q = 1\) for \(t\rightarrow \infty\), shown for an initial mean value \(\langle z\rangle_{in}=2\) (corresponding to \(q_{in}=1.25\)).
* The scale parameter evolves as \(x_0(t) = x_0^* [1-2(q_{in}-1)\exp(-t/\tau)]\), where \(x_0^*\) is the equilibrium value.
* The variance of the inverse scale parameter is \(Var(1/x_0) = (q_{in}-1)\langle 1/x_0\rangle^2 \exp(-t/\tau)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard relaxation time approximation (RTA) used in Boltzmann transport equations, as applied in recent analyses of nucleus-nucleus collisions by BGSS, YTTS, ZLD, TKTS, TBGKSC, and QCGZZ (all around 2020). The authors build on the Tsallis distribution formalism from Tsallis (1988) and earlier work by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (WW1, WWCT, WW2) showing its effectiveness in describing transverse momentum spectra over 14 orders of magnitude. The approach enables modeling time evolution of non-equilibrium systems without invoking complicated collision integrals, using the internal variable concept from IP and VP.
