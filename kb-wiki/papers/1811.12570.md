# Light vector mesons ($ω$, $ρ$ and $ϕ$) in strong magnetic fields: A QCD sum rule approach

> The mass modifications of the light vector mesons ($ω$, $ρ$ and $ϕ$) are investigated in asymmetric nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic fields, using a QCD sum rule approach. These are computed from the medium modifications of the non-strange and strange light quark condensates as well as scalar gluon condensate. The quark and gluon condensates are calculated from the medium changes of the scalar fields (non-strange and strange) and a scalar dilaton field in the magnetized nuclear matter, within a chiral SU(3) model. The scalar dilaton field within the model breaks the scale invariance of QCD and simulates the gluon condensate. The anomalous magnetic moments for the nucleons are taken into account in the present study.

## Key claim

The masses of light vector mesons (omega, rho, phi) in asymmetric nuclear matter under strong magnetic fields are computed using QCD sum rules, with the key condition that the medium modifications arise entirely from changes in quark and gluon condensates calculated within a chiral SU(3) model, rather than from direct magnetic field effects on the meson currents themselves.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled mean-field equations of motion for the scalar fields (non-strange sigma and delta, strange zeta, and dilaton chi) within a chiral SU(3) model to obtain the in-medium quark and gluon condensates in magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter, including the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. These condensates are then used as inputs to the operator product expansion coefficients in the QCD sum rule approach for the vector meson correlation functions. The finite energy sum rules (FESR) are solved to extract the medium-dependent masses, continuum thresholds, and residues for the omega, rho, and phi mesons.

## Key numbers

- For the omega meson at density rho_0, isospin asymmetry eta=0, and magnetic field eB=4 m_pi^2: mass = 773.4 MeV (without AMM) and 775.7 MeV (with AMM); at density 2rho_0: mass = 950 MeV (without AMM) and 953.4 MeV (with AMM)
- For the omega meson at density rho_0, eta=0.5, and eB=4 m_pi^2: mass = 787.24 MeV (without AMM) and 792.5 MeV (with AMM); at density 2rho_0: mass = 964.2 MeV (without AMM) and 967.56 MeV (with AMM)
- For the rho meson at density rho_0, eta=0, and eB=4 m_pi^2: mass = 618 MeV (without AMM) and 620.7 MeV (with AMM); at density 4rho_0: mass = 374.9 MeV (without AMM) and 398 MeV (with AMM)
- For the rho meson at density rho_0, eta=0.5, and eB=4 m_pi^2: mass = 634.5 MeV (without AMM) and 640.85 MeV (with AMM); at density 4rho_0: mass = 468.5 MeV (without AMM) and 477.5 MeV (with AMM)
- For the phi meson at density rho_0, eta=0, and eB=4 m_pi^2: mass = 1001.11 MeV (without AMM) and 1001.28 MeV (with AMM); at density 4rho_0: mass = 998.4 MeV (without AMM) and 998.08 MeV (with AMM)
- Vacuum masses used: rho = 770 MeV, omega = 783 MeV, phi = 1020 MeV; current quark masses: m_u=4 MeV, m_d=7 MeV, m_s=150 MeV
- The kappa_q coefficients from vacuum FESR: 7.788 for omega, 7.236 for rho; kappa_s for phi: -1.21

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule study of light vector mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter previously performed by the same group (Mishra et al., 2018) to include strong magnetic fields, building on the chiral SU(3) model framework used for nuclear matter (Papazoglou et al., 1999) and finite nuclei (Papazoglou et al., 1998). The approach enables future comparisons with experimental observables from non-central ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions where large magnetic fields are expected, and complements studies of heavy flavor mesons (D, B, charmonium, bottomonium) in magnetized matter by the same authors.
