# Multifragmentation calculated with relativistic force

> A saturating hamiltonian is presented in a relativistically covariant formalism. The interaction is described by scalar and vector mesons, with coupling strengths adjusted to the nuclear matter. No explicit density depe ndence is assumed. The hamiltonian is applied in a QMD calculation to determine the fragment distribution in O + Br collision at different energies (50 -- 200 MeV/u) to test the applicability of the model at low energies. The results are compared with experiment and with previous non-relativistic calculations. PACS: 25.70Mn, 25.75.+r

## Key claim

A relativistically covariant Hamiltonian derived from scalar-vector meson interactions (Walecka-type and Zimanyi-Moszkowski-type Lagrangians) can reproduce nuclear saturation without explicit density dependence, and when applied in Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations for O + Br collisions at 50–200 MeV/u, it yields fragment distributions comparable to experiment and to previous non-relativistic calculations, provided the small-acceleration approximation holds for mean-field interactions.

## Method

The authors derived an instantaneous action-at-a-distance from a relativistic Lagrangian with scalar and vector meson fields, using the Wheeler-Feynman symmetric Green's function and a small-acceleration approximation to eliminate radiation fields. They then constructed a Hamiltonian in terms of positions and canonical momenta, folded the Yukawa forces with Gaussian density distributions (width parameter α = 0.5 fm⁻¹), and implemented it in a QMD simulation for O + Br collisions at beam energies from 50 to 200 MeV/u. Two parameter sets were used: the Walecka (W) and the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) couplings, with the ZM set including a nonlinear scalar coupling to reduce compressibility; the ρ meson was added for symmetry energy, and nucleon-nucleon cross sections were fitted from experimental data with screening radii of 1.6 fm and 2.4 fm.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants for Walecka forces: g_s = 11.04, g_v = 13.74, g_ρ = 7.0; for Zimanyi-Moszkowski forces: g_s = 7.84, g_v = 6.67, g_ρ = 7.0.
- Scalar meson mass μ_s c² = 550 MeV; vector meson mass μ_v c² = μ_ρ c² = 783 MeV.
- Gaussian folding width parameter α = 0.5 fm⁻¹ for nucleon density distributions.
- Screening lengths for nucleon-nucleon collisions: r_coll = 1.6 fm and r_coll = 2.4 fm.
- Beam energies studied: 50 to 200 MeV/u for O + Br collisions.
- The vast majority of nucleon-nucleon collisions in Ca + Ca central collisions at 200–800 MeV/u occur at relative momenta below 0.2 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic QMD calculations using Skyrme forces (Aichelin 1991, Frankfurt group) by introducing a relativistic mean-field Hamiltonian that naturally yields saturation without an explicit density-dependent term. It builds on the Walecka model (Serot and Walecka 1986) and the Zimanyi-Moszkowski Lagrangian (Zimanyi and Moszkowski 1990) to improve the compressibility. The results enable future applications of relativistic QMD at intermediate energies (300 MeV/u – 2 GeV/u), where non-relativistic treatments become inadequate, and provide a framework for comparing relativistic versus non-relativistic mean-field effects in multifragmentation.
