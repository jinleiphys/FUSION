# High-p_T Pion Quenching versus anti+Baryon Enhancement in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Jet quenching of quarks and gluons in central A+A collisions suppresses the high-p_T meson production and exposes novel baryon dynamics that we attribute to (gluonic) baryon junctions. This mechanism predicts baryon enhancement in a finite moderate-p_T window that decreases with increasing impact parameter. We also extend recent calculations of the transverse momentum behavior of p/pi^+ and p-bar/pi^- to other baryon species and show that a similar pattern is expected for the K^-/Lambda and K^+/bar{Lambda} ratios. Within the framework of the model constant p-bar/p and bar{Lambda}/Lambda ratios are found in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies up to p_T ~= 4 - 5 GeV.

## Key claim

Jet quenching of quarks and gluons in central A+A collisions suppresses high-p_T meson production and exposes novel baryon dynamics attributed to (gluonic) baryon junctions, predicting baryon enhancement in a finite moderate-p_T window that decreases with increasing impact parameter; the model finds constant p-bar/p and bar-Lambda/Lambda ratios in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies up to p_T approximately 4-5 GeV.

## Method

The authors combine a non-perturbative baryon production and transport mechanism based on Regge theory (baryon junction) for the moderate-p_T region with leading-order perturbative QCD augmented by initial multiple scattering (Cronin effect), nuclear shadowing, and parton energy loss computed via the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism for the high-p_T region. They compute baryon/meson ratios (p/pi^+, p-bar/pi^-, K^-/Lambda, K^+/bar-Lambda) and baryon/antibaryon ratios (p-bar/p, bar-Lambda/Lambda) as functions of p_T for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV and 200 GeV, using centrality classes based on N_part and N_bin geometry.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV, central Au+Au collisions yield dN^{B-bar-B}/dy approximately 18; at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the corresponding value is approximately 13.5.
- The mean transverse momentum relation among species is given as <p_T>_pi : <p_T>_K : <p_T>_B approximately 1 : (1 to sqrt(2)) : sqrt(3), corresponding to 220 MeV : 275 MeV : 380 MeV.
- For p_T > 5 GeV, baryon/meson ratios reduce below unity and follow perturbative calculations; the suppression factor R_AA(p_T) is approximately 0.3 and almost independent of p_T up to approximately 10 GeV at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV.
- The baryon enhancement window is found for 2 GeV <= p_T <= 5 GeV, with predicted weak centrality dependence for K^-/Lambda and K^+/bar-Lambda ratios.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Gyulassy and Vitev (2002) on the p_T-differential p/pi^+ and p-bar/pi^- ratios to other baryon species (K^-/Lambda, K^+/bar-Lambda), and builds on the baryon junction mechanism originally proposed by Veneziano and Rossi for elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions and later generalized to nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results explain the difference in suppression factors R_AA(p_T) between inclusive charged hadrons and neutral pions observed by PHENIX and STAR at RHIC, and predict that at p_T >= 5 GeV the suppression becomes approximately equal for all hadron species, enabling tests of parton energy loss models at higher transverse momenta.
