# Medium-heavy nuclei from nucleon-nucleon interactions in lattice QCD

> On the basis of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with the nucleon-nucleon forces obtained from lattice QCD simulations, the properties of the medium-heavy doubly-magic nuclei such as 16^O and 40^Ca are investigated. We found that those nuclei are bound for the pseudo-scalar meson mass M_PS ~ 470 MeV. The mass number dependence of the binding energies, single-particle spectra and density distributions are qualitatively consistent with those expected from empirical data at the physical point, although these hypothetical nuclei at heavy quark mass have smaller binding energies than the real nuclei.

## Key claim

Using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with nucleon-nucleon forces from lattice QCD, the authors find that the medium-heavy doubly-magic nuclei 16O and 40Ca are bound at a pseudo-scalar meson mass of approximately 470 MeV, but these hypothetical nuclei have smaller binding energies than real nuclei at the physical point.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory for finite nuclei, using nucleon-nucleon potentials previously obtained from lattice QCD simulations via the HAL QCD method. The bare NN potentials were limited to the 1S0, 3S1, and 3D1 channels, and the Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved using a harmonic-oscillator basis with the Talmi-Moshinsky coefficient. The single-particle potentials, density matrix, and wave functions were determined self-consistently, and the ground state energy was calculated with a center-of-mass correction.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 16O at M_PS ≈ 470 MeV: E0 = -34.7 MeV, with a binding energy per nucleon of -2.17 MeV.
- Ground state energy of 40Ca at M_PS ≈ 470 MeV: E0 = -112.7 MeV, with a binding energy per nucleon of -2.82 MeV.
- Root-mean-square matter radius of 16O: 2.35 fm; of 40Ca: 2.78 fm.
- Single-particle energies for 16O: 1S at -35.8 MeV, 1P at -13.8 MeV.
- Single-particle energies for 40Ca: 1S at -59.0 MeV, 1P at -36.0 MeV, 2S at -14.7 MeV, 1D at -14.3 MeV.
- Extrapolated binding energies per nucleon to infinite basis: 16O at -2.86 MeV, 40Ca at -3.64 MeV.
- Bethe-Weizsacker volume coefficient a_V = 5.46 MeV and surface coefficient a_S = -6.56 MeV at M_PS ≈ 470 MeV.
- Statistical error on ground state energies is about ±10 percent.

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration approach from the earlier study of 4He using the stochastic variational method (Inoue 2011) and nuclear matter using the BHF method (Inoue 2013) to medium-heavy finite nuclei. It demonstrates that combining lattice QCD nuclear forces with many-body techniques can produce bound nuclei with shell structure and density distributions qualitatively consistent with empirical data, despite the unphysical quark mass. The results enable future studies of the quark mass dependence of nuclear binding and the inclusion of spin-orbit forces (Murano 2013) and three-nucleon forces (Doi 2011) from lattice QCD, with the goal of reaching physical quark masses using larger lattice volumes on the K-computer.
