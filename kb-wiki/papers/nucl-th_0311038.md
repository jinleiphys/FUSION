# Gluon condensation and deconfinement critical density in nuclear matter

> An upper limit to the critical density for the transition to the deconfined phase, at zero temperature, has been evaluated by analyzing the behavior of the gluon condensate in nuclear matter. Due to the non linear baryon density effects, the upper limit to the critical density, ρ_c turns out about nine times the saturation density, rho_0 for the value of the gluon condensate in vacuum <(α_s/π) G^2>=0.012 GeV^4. For neutron matter ρ_c \simeq 8.5 ρ_0. The dependence of the critical density on the value of the gluon condensate in vacuum is studied.

## Key claim

The vanishing of the gluon condensate at high baryon density provides only an upper limit to the critical density for deconfinement, not a precise transition point, because the condensate is not an order parameter and only the "electric" component vanishes at the phase transition while the "magnetic" component remains finite.

## Method

The authors computed the density dependence of the QCD gluon condensate in nuclear matter using the relation g(ρ) = g(0) - (8ρ/9)(m + ε(ρ) - Σ m_i ⟨N|q̄_i q_i|N⟩), where ε(ρ) is the binding energy per nucleon from realistic equations of state. They used the microscopic nuclear matter EoS from Maruyama et al. (2002) for two different potentials (AV14 and Paris) including three-body forces, for both symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, and solved for the density at which the condensate vanishes.

## Key numbers

- For the central vacuum gluon condensate value g(0) = 0.012 GeV⁴, the upper limit to the critical density in symmetric nuclear matter is ρ_c ≈ 9.1 ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.
- For neutron matter with the same vacuum condensate, the upper limit is ρ_c ≈ 8.5 ρ₀.
- The vacuum gluon condensate range is 0.003 GeV⁴ < g(0) < 0.021 GeV⁴, with the central value taken as 0.012 GeV⁴.
- A toy calculation using screened potential and Debye screening gives a critical screening parameter μ_c ≈ 0.24 GeV, corresponding to ρ ≈ 3 ρ₀ for glueball disappearance.
- The strange quark condensate contribution uses m_s = 0.1 GeV and ⟨N|s̄ s|N⟩ = 1, with overall effect of only a few percent on g(ρ).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cohen et al. (1992) on the gluon condensate in nuclear matter by including nonlinear density effects from the binding energy per nucleon, which reduces the critical density by almost a factor two compared to linear extrapolation. The results are compatible with critical densities obtained from phenomenological quark matter models using the Gibbs construction (e.g., Schertler et al., 1998), but provide an independent estimate based on QCD condensate behavior rather than matching equations of state. The analysis enables future comparison with heavy-ion collision constraints and neutron star observations, and the authors note that lower vacuum condensate values (0.008-0.01 GeV⁴) would bring the critical density closer to phenomenological estimates.
