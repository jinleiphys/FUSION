# Open charm and charmonium states in strong magnetic fields

> The mass modifications of the open charm ($D$ and $D^*$) mesons, and their effects on the decay widths $D^*\rightarrow Dπ$ as well as of the charmonium state, $Ψ(3770)$ to open charm mesons ($Ψ(3770)\rightarrow D\bar D$), are investigated in the presence of strong magnetic fields. These are studied accounting for the mixing of the pseudoscalar ($P$) and vector ($V$) mesons ($D-D^*$, $η_c'-Ψ(3770)$ mixings), with the mixing parameter, $g_{PV}$ of a phenomenological three-point ($PVγ$) vertex interaction determined from the observed radiative decay width of $V\rightarrow Pγ$. For charged $D-D^*$ mixing, this parameter is dependent on the magnetic field, because of the Landau level contributions to the vacuum masses of these mesons. The masses of the charged $D$ and $D^*$ mesons modified due to $PV$ mixing, in addition, have contributions from the lowest Landau levels in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The effects of the magnetic field on the decay widths are studied using a field theoretic model of composite hadrons with quark (and antiquark) consittuents. The parameter for the charged $D-D^*$ mixing is observed to increase appreciably with increase in the magnetic field. This leads to dominant modifications to their masses, and hence the decay widths of charged $D^*\rightarrow Dπ$ as well as $Ψ(3770)\rightarrow D^+D^-$ at large values of the magnetic field. The modifications of the masses and decay widths of the open and hidden charm mesons in the presence of strong magnetic fields should have observable consequences on the production of the open charm ($D$ and $D^*$) mesons as well as of the charmonium states resulting from non-central ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The mass modifications of open charm mesons (D and D*) and the charmonium state Ψ(3770) due to pseudoscalar-vector meson mixing in strong magnetic fields lead to significant changes in the decay widths D* → Dπ and Ψ(3770) → D\(\bar D\), with the charged D-D* mixing parameter increasing appreciably with the magnetic field because of Landau level contributions to the vacuum masses, producing dominant modifications at large field values.

## Method

The authors compute mass modifications of D and D* mesons in a uniform magnetic field by incorporating pseudoscalar-vector (PV) mixing via a phenomenological three-point PVγ vertex interaction, with the coupling parameter gPV determined from the observed radiative decay width V → Pγ. For charged mesons, the mixing parameter becomes magnetic-field-dependent due to lowest Landau level contributions to the vacuum masses, and the modified masses are obtained from the mixing formula using these field-dependent masses. The decay widths D* → Dπ and Ψ(3770) → D\(\bar D\) are then calculated using a field-theoretic model of composite hadrons with quark and antiquark constituents, where the matrix element for the decay is evaluated using the light quark-antiquark pair creation term of the free Dirac Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength considered ranges up to eB ∼ 15 mπ², corresponding to values at LHC, CERN.
* The mixing parameter gPV for charged D-D* mixing is observed to increase appreciably with the magnetic field, leading to dominant modifications to the masses of charged open charm mesons at large fields.
* The decay width for D* → Dπ is computed in KeV, with the logarithm of the decay width plotted as a function of eB/mπ² for charged D*+ and neutral D*0 mesons.
* The partial decay widths for Ψ(3770) → D\(\bar D\) are computed in MeV, plotted as functions of eB/mπ² for the final states D+D− and D0\(\bar D\)0, and their total.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy flavor mesons in magnetic fields using QCD sum rule approaches (Gubler et al., 2016) and effective potential methods (Alford and Strickland, 2013), which showed that pseudoscalar-vector mixing gives dominant contributions to meson masses. It also builds on earlier work by the same authors (Mishra and Misra, 2019) that studied Ψ(3770) → D\(\bar D\) decay from mass modifications due to PV mixing and Landau levels, now additionally accounting for D-D* mixing effects on the final-state open charm mesons. The results enable predictions for observable consequences in non-central ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, where strong magnetic fields are produced.
