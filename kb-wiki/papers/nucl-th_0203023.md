# Dileptons and Photons from Coarse-Grained Microscopic Dynamics and Hydrodynamics Compared to Experimental Data

> Radiation of dileptons and photons from high energy nuclear collisions provides information on the space-time evolution of the hot dense matter produced therein. We compute this radiation using relativistic hydrodynamics and a coarse-grained version of the microscopic event generator UrQMD, both of which provide a good description of the hadron spectra. The currently most accurate dilepton and photon emission rates from perturbative QCD and from experimentally-based hadronic calculations are used. Comparisons are made to data on central Pb-Pb and Pb-Au collisions taken at the CERN SPS at a beam energy of 158 A GeV. Both hydrodynamics and UrQMD provide very good descriptions of the photon transverse momentum spectrum measured between 1 and 4 GeV, but slightly underestimate the low mass spectrum of e+e- pairs, even with greatly broadened rho and omega vector mesons. Predictions are given for the transverse momentum distribution of dileptons.

# Dileptons and Photons from Coarse-Grained Microscopic Dynamics and Hydrodynamics Compared to Experimental Data

## Key claim
Both relativistic hydrodynamics and a coarse-grained version of the microscopic event generator UrQMD provide very good descriptions of the photon transverse momentum spectrum measured between 1 and 4 GeV, but slightly underestimate the low mass spectrum of e+e- pairs, even with greatly broadened rho and omega vector mesons; the authors suggest that further data are needed to decide whether a significant discrepancy with theory exists.

## Method
The authors computed dilepton and photon emission using two dynamical models: (1) relativistic non-viscous perfect fluid dynamics with an equation of state including a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at Tc = 200 MeV, and (2) a coarse-grained version of the UrQMD event generator, where 100 central Pb-Pb collisions were averaged over small space-time cells to extract local temperature, baryon chemical potential, and flow velocity fields. The most accurate available emission rates from perturbative QCD and experimentally-based hadronic calculations were applied, and results were compared to experimental data from the CERN SPS at 158 A GeV beam energy.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 158 A GeV (center-of-mass energy 17 A GeV) for central Pb-Pb and Pb-Au collisions at the CERN SPS
- Critical temperature for phase transition: Tc = 200 MeV in net baryon-free matter
- Photon transverse momentum spectrum measured between 1 and 4 GeV is well described by both models
- Freeze-out temperature of about 120 +/- 10 MeV reproduces experimental hadronic data from NA49 reasonably well
- Local temperature between 120 and 250 MeV for a period of about 15 fm/c with transverse extent of about 10 fm
- Dilepton yields from both models are within one standard deviation of the 1996 CERES data when Tf = 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier comparisons of hydrodynamics to SPS data (Sollfrank, 1999; Huovinen, 1999) by introducing a coarse-graining procedure for UrQMD that allows direct comparison of space-time evolution between microscopic and fluid-dynamic approaches. The thermal emission rates used are from Eletsky et al. (based on scattering amplitudes from experimental data) and Rapp et al. (based on self-consistent one-loop self-energy diagrams), which give very similar results. The paper enables future studies of viscous hydrodynamics by showing that differences between UrQMD and perfect fluid dynamics are likely attributable to shear and bulk viscosity and heat conduction not accounted for in the perfect fluid model.
