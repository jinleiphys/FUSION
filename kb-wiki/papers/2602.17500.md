# Hyperon longitudinal polarization and vector meson spin alignment in a thermal model for heavy-ion collisions

> The concept of a common local spin equilibrium for both spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles is incorporated into a thermal model of particle production in heavy-ion collisions at the top RHIC energies. We show that an effective spin polarization tensor leading to a correct description of the longitudinal spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons simultaneously yields a positive alignment of vector mesons ($ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$) that grows monotonically with transverse momentum and centrality. Similar trends can be seen in the data, suggesting a possible common mechanism for longitudinal spin polarization and alignment. However, model calculations are insufficient to explain the data in a fully quantitative way. The correlation found between the magnitude of the $Λ$ longitudinal polarization and vector meson alignment suggests further more elaborate investigations of this issue.

# Hyperon longitudinal polarization and vector meson spin alignment in a thermal model for heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

A common local spin equilibrium for spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles, implemented via a projected thermal vorticity rescaled by a factor λ, can simultaneously describe the longitudinal polarization of Λ hyperons and yield positive spin alignment of φ and K*0 vector mesons that grows with transverse momentum and centrality; however, the model calculations are insufficient to explain the data in a fully quantitative way.

## Method

The authors employ a single freeze-out thermal model with anisotropic transverse flow, previously fitted to PHENIX data at √sNN = 130 GeV, and compute the spin polarization tensor as the thermal vorticity with its electric-like components set to zero, rescaled by a parameter λ. The spin density matrix element ρ00 for vector mesons is then calculated from the equilibrium spin density matrix in the adjoint representation, averaging over the freeze-out hypersurface using a Boltzmann distribution for φ and K*0 mesons.

## Key numbers

- For φ mesons in the 30–60% centrality class, the model with λ = 1 gives ρ00 values ranging from 0.333361 at pT = 1.4339 GeV to 0.333747 at pT = 4.5640 GeV; with λ = 3, values range from 0.333581 to 0.336982 over the same pT range (STAR data show ρ00 between 0.3251 and 0.3384)
- For K*0 mesons in the 30–60% centrality class, the model with λ = 1 gives ρ00 from 0.333356 at pT = 1.25 GeV to 0.333845 at pT = 4.50 GeV; with λ = 3, values range from 0.333538 to 0.337830 (STAR data show ρ00 between 0.320 and 0.381)
- The pT-integrated ρ00 for φ mesons (mass 1.019 GeV) is larger than for K*0 mesons (mass 0.892 GeV) in the 30–60% centrality class, showing a mass ordering
- Centrality dependence: for φ mesons with λ = 1, ρ00 = 0.333335 in 0–15% centrality, 0.333345 in 15–30%, and 0.333380 in 30–60%; with λ = 3, values are 0.333350, 0.333438, and 0.333755 respectively
- The freeze-out temperature is Tf = 0.165 GeV, with baryon chemical potential μB = 0
- The parameters λ = 1 and λ = 3 both describe the longitudinal polarization of Λ hyperons with χ2 per degree of freedom below 2

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach previously used for Λ polarization analysis by Banerjee et al. (2024) and Florkowski et al. (2019, 2021), incorporating a recently proposed uniform description for local equilibrium of spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles by Florkowski et al. (2026). It addresses the longstanding difficulty of reproducing longitudinal Λ polarization (Becattini et al., 2017) and the negative spin alignment predicted by many theoretical frameworks (Liang et al., 2004; Yang et al., 2017; Sheng et al., 2019; Kumar et al., 2023), which contradicts experimental data. The correlation found between Λ longitudinal polarization magnitude and vector meson alignment motivates future more elaborate investigations using more realistic spin polarization tensor calculations.
