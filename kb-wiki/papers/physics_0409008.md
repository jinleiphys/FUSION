# Constrained Molecular Dynamics Simulations of Atomic Ground-States

> Constrained molecular dynamics(CoMD) model, previously introduced for nuclear dynamics, has been extended to the atomic structure and collision calculations. Quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principle are enforced by constraints, in a parameter-free way. Our calculations for small atomic system, H, He, Li, Be, F reproduce the ground-state binding energies within 3%, compared with the results of quantum mechanical Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Key claim

The Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model, previously used for nuclear systems, can reproduce atomic ground-state binding energies for H, He, Li, Be, and F within 3% of quantum mechanical Hartree-Fock results, but only when the binding energies are computed as ensemble averages over many events and the constraints act as a dissipative term that can either increase or decrease the total energy depending on phase-space occupancy.

## Method

The authors extend the CoMD approach by enforcing the Pauli exclusion principle through a constraint on the phase-space occupancy \(\bar{f}_i \le 1\), implemented via Lagrange multipliers that restrict the product \(r_{ij} p_{ij}\) for electron pairs with the same spin. The Heisenberg uncertainty principle is similarly enforced by a constraint on \(r_{ij} p_{ij}\) between electrons and the nucleus. The equations of motion are integrated using a Hermite integration scheme with variable time-steps, starting from an ensemble of initial configurations where electrons are placed pairwise opposite to the nucleus in phase space, and the ground-state binding energies and radial coordinates are computed as averages over the ensemble and over time.

## Key numbers

- Hydrogen ground-state binding energy: \(-13.3 \pm 1.8\) eV (CoMD), compared to \(-13.61\) eV (Hartree-Fock) and \(-13.6\) eV (experimental).
- Helium ground-state binding energy: \(-79.1 \pm 6.9\) eV (CoMD), compared to \(-77.87\) eV (Hartree-Fock) and \(-79.1\) eV (experimental).
- Lithium ground-state binding energy: \(-196.8 \pm 15\) eV (CoMD), compared to \(-202.26\) eV (Hartree-Fock) and \(-203.6\) eV (experimental).
- Beryllium ground-state binding energy: \(-371.2 \pm 27\) eV (CoMD), compared to \(-396.56\) eV (Hartree-Fock) and \(-399.4\) eV (experimental); with forced symmetry: \(-385.3 \pm 29\) eV.
- Fluorine ground-state binding energy: \(-2560.3 \pm 192\) eV (CoMD), compared to \(-2705.1\) eV (Hartree-Fock) and \(-2717.4\) eV (experimental).
- Hydrogen radial coordinate: \(1.0 \pm 0.2\) atomic units (CoMD), compared to 1.0 atomic units (Hartree-Fock, 1s).
- Helium radial coordinates: \(0.55 \pm 0.09\) and \(0.60 \pm 0.1\) atomic units (CoMD), compared to 0.9273 atomic units (Hartree-Fock, 1s).

## Context

This work extends the CoMD approach, previously applied to nuclear dynamics by Papa, Maruyama, and Bonasera (2001) and to quark systems by Terranova and Bonasera (2004), to atomic structure as an alternative to Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) methods that use pseudo-potentials as introduced by Kirschbaum and Wilets (1980) and Cohen (1994). The approach enables collision calculations such as the D+d fusion enhancement factor studied by Kimura and Bonasera (2004), and is intended for determining the Equation of State of matter at very low temperatures where quantum effects dominate.
