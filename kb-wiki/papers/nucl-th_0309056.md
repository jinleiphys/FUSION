# Shear Viscosity Coefficient from Microscopic Models

> The transport coefficient of shear viscosity is studied for a hadron matter through microscopic transport model, the Ultra--relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD), using the Green--Kubo formulas. Molecular--dynamical simulations are performed for a system of light mesons in a box with periodic boundary conditions. Starting from an initial state composed of $π, η,ω,ρ,ϕ$ with a uniform phase--space distribution, the evolution takes place through elastic collisions, production and annihilation. The system approaches a stationary state of mesons and their resonances, which is characterized by common temperature. After equilibration, thermodynamic quantities such as the energy density, particle density, and pressure are calculated. From such an equilibrated state the shear viscosity coefficient is calculated from the fluctuations of stress tensor around equilibrium using Green--Kubo relations. We do our simulations here at zero net baryon density so that the equilibration times depend on the energy density. We do not include hadron strings as degrees of freedom so as to maintain detailed balance. Hence we do not get the saturation of temperature but this leads to longer equilibration times.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity coefficient of a hadron gas of mesons and their resonances is extracted from the microscopic transport model UrQMD using Green-Kubo relations, but only for a system that excludes hadron strings and multi-particle production/absorption processes in order to maintain detailed balance, which leads to longer equilibration times than would occur in a more complete treatment.

## Method

Molecular-dynamical simulations were performed for a system of light mesons (π, η, ω, ρ, φ) in a cubic box of volume 1000 fm³ with periodic boundary conditions and zero net baryon density. The system evolved through elastic collisions, two-body resonance production and annihilation, and strong decays, until reaching a stationary state characterized by a common temperature. From the equilibrated state, the shear viscosity coefficient was calculated from the fluctuations of the stress tensor around equilibrium using the Green-Kubo relation η = (V/T) ∫₀^∞ ⟨π_{ij}(t)·π_{ij}(t+t')⟩ dt'.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity coefficient of the meson gas grows with temperature; UrQMD results are about twice those from the variational method (which only included pions).
- Relaxation time for shear flux in the hot pion gas from UrQMD is about a factor of two less than variational method results.
- Thermal equilibrium was established at about t = 350 fm/c for the system with V = 1000 fm³, n_B = 0.0 fm⁻³, and ε = 0.9 GeV/fm³.
- The study focused on the hadronic scale temperature range 100 MeV < T < 200 MeV.
- Simulations were performed at zero net baryon density (n_B = 0.0 fm⁻³).
- A statistical ensemble of 200 events was generated for each set of input parameters.
- The box volume used was V = 1000 fm³; results were insensitive to box length greater than 6 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hadron gas transport coefficients using the variational method (Prakash, Davesne) and relaxation time approximation (Gavin), which were limited to binary mixtures such as ππ systems. It also builds on earlier UrQMD box calculations by Belkacem (1998) that showed temperature saturation due to broken detailed balance from multi-particle production without reverse processes. By maintaining detailed balance through only two-body processes, this work avoids temperature saturation but obtains longer equilibration times, and it enables future calculation of shear viscosity for quark-gluon plasma using parton cascade models such as VNI/BMS.
