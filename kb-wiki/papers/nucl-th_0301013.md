# Helicity Amplitudes In the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> We report on the recent results of the hypercentral constituent quark model. The model contains a spin independent three-quark interaction which is inspired by QCD lattice calculations and reproduces the average energy values of the SU(6) multiplets. The splittings are obtained with a residual generalized SU(6)-breaking interaction, including an isospin dependent term. The long standing problem of the Roper resonance is absent and all the 3- and 4-star states are well reproduced. The model has also been used for predictions concerning the electromagnetic transition form factors giving a good description of the medium $Q^2$ -behaviour. In particular the calculated $S_{11} A_{1/2}$ helicity amplitude agrees very well with the recent CLAS data. Finally the ratio of the elastic form factors of the proton, calculated including kinematic relativistic corrections, exhibits a substantial decreasing with $Q^2$ in agreement with the recent TJNAF experiment.

# Helicity Amplitudes In the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM) reproduces all 3- and 4-star baryon resonances and resolves the long-standing Roper resonance problem, with the calculated $S_{11} A_{1/2}$ helicity amplitude agreeing very well with recent CLAS data, though discrepancies remain at small $Q^2$ that may be due to the non-relativistic character of the model and the lack of explicit quark-antiquark configurations.

## Method

The authors use a hypercentral three-quark interaction consisting of a linear plus Coulomb-like term inspired by lattice QCD calculations, with a hyperfine term treated as a perturbation. After fixing the quark mass to 1/3 of the nucleon mass and determining parameters from the spectrum, the resulting wave functions are used to calculate helicity amplitudes for electroexcitation of baryon resonances using a non-relativistic current for point quarks. Kinematic relativistic corrections from boosting three-quark states to the Breit frame are applied to the elastic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass fixed to $m = 1/3$ of the nucleon mass
- Hypercentral potential parameters: $\tau = 4.5$ and $\alpha = 1.61\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}$
- The $N-\Delta$ mass difference arises from: hyperfine interaction (35%), spin-isospin term (50%), and isospin term (15%)
- The ratio $R = \mu_p G_E/G_M$ is reproduced up to $1.5\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2$ by the hCQM calculation
- Non-relativistic calculation gives $R = 1$ and remains within 1% even with hyperfine mixing included

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model (Giannini, Santopinto, Vassallo, prior work) by applying it to electromagnetic transition form factors and elastic form factors, building on earlier spectrum calculations. The results are compared with the Capstick and Keister model (Capstick, 1990s) for the $S_{11}(1535)$ helicity amplitude and with the Isgur-Capstick model for the spectrum. The model enables systematic predictions for all 3-4 star and 1-2 star resonance electroexcitation amplitudes, and the relativistic correction framework can be applied to any constituent quark model.
