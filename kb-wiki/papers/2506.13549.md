# Connecting dilaton thermal fluctuation with the Polyakov loop at finite temperature

> Understanding the character of the deconfinement phase transition is one of the fundamental challenges in particle physics. In this work, we derive a formula for the expectation value of the Polyakov loop -- the order parameter of the deconfinement phase transition -- in pure $\mathrm{SU(N_{\mathrm{c}})}$ gauge systems at finite temperatures starting from the Coleman\textendash Weinberg-type effective potential encoding the trace anomaly of QCD. Our results are in good agreement with the Lattice QCD data and can effectively describe the large-$N_{\mathrm{c}}$ behaviors of the expectation value of the Polyakov loop. Notably, our findings predict the strongest first-order deconfinement phase transition as $N_{\mathrm{c}} \to +\infty$. Furthermore, to establish a relation between the dilaton field and the Polyakov loop, we also derive the scale transformation rule for temperature based on quantum statistical mechanics. The results of this work may shed a light on the connection between deconfinement phase transition and evolution of scale symmetry in the thermal system.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical formula for the expectation value of the Polyakov loop in pure SU(N_c) gauge systems by incorporating thermal fluctuations of the dilaton field into a Coleman-Weinberg-type effective potential, claiming that the thermal fluctuation, rather than the mean-field approximation used in their prior work, resolves the discrepancy with Lattice QCD data for the critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition.

## Method

The authors decompose the dilaton field into a temperature-dependent expectation value and a thermal fluctuation, where the fluctuation is further split into a Polyakov-loop-independent part and a part expressed as a power series in the Polyakov loop and its conjugate. They derive scale transformation rules for temperature and chemical potentials from the covariance of the Liouville equation under scale transformations, then minimize the temperature-modified Coleman-Weinberg-type potential at next-leading order and next-next-leading order to obtain the expectation value of the Polyakov loop as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

* For N_c=3, the predicted critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition is T_c ≈ 270 MeV, consistent with the Lattice QCD result T_c,LQCD ≈ 270 MeV (the authors' prior mean-field approximation gave T_c ≈ 356 MeV).
* The dilaton vacuum expectation value at zero temperature is f_σ ≈ 150 MeV.
* The Coleman-Weinberg-type potential minimum at zero temperature is V(χ=f_σ) = -m_σ² f_σ²/16.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior mean-field approach (Sheng 2023) which connected the dilaton potential to the Polyakov loop but yielded a critical temperature 356 MeV, much higher than the Lattice QCD result of 270 MeV. The current paper incorporates thermal fluctuations to resolve this discrepancy and generalizes the analysis to pure SU(3), SU(4), and SU(5) gauge systems, comparing with Lattice QCD data from Kaczmarek (2002), Ratti (2005), and Mykkanen (2012), as well as with large-N_c results from Panero (2009) and Kang (2021). The work enables a field-theoretic description of the deconfinement phase transition linked to scale symmetry restoration, with implications for cosmology and the QCD phase diagram.
