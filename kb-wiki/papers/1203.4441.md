# Novel Bose-Einstein Interference in the Passage of a Fast Particle in a Dense Medium

> When an energetic particle collides coherently with many medium particles at high energies, the Bose-Einstein symmetry with respect to the interchange of the exchanged virtual bosons leads to a destructive interference of the Feynman amplitudes in most regions of the phase space but a constructive interference in some other regions of the phase space. As a consequence, the recoiling medium particles have a tendency to come out collectively along the direction of the incident fast particle, each carrying a substantial fraction of the incident longitudinal momentum. Such an interference appearing as collective recoils of scatterers along the incident particle direction may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with a high-$p_T$ trigger in high-energy AuAu collisions at RHIC.

# Novel Bose-Einstein Interference in the Passage of a Fast Particle in a Dense Medium

## Key claim

When a fast particle collides coherently with many medium particles such that the longitudinal coherence length greatly exceeds the mean free path, Bose-Einstein symmetry among exchanged virtual bosons produces destructive interference in most phase-space regions but constructive interference in others, forcing the recoiling medium particles to emerge collectively along the incident direction with substantial longitudinal momentum fractions. The effect may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with a high-$p_T$ trigger in central AuAu collisions at RHIC, but the author notes that large error bars and scarce data points in threshold regions preclude a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The author calculates Feynman amplitudes for coherent (1+$n$)-body collisions of a fast fermion with $n$ fermion scatterers in an Abelian gauge theory at high energy, then sums over all $n!$ permutations of exchanged virtual bosons to enforce Bose-Einstein symmetry. The sum is shown to reduce to a product of delta functions $\Delta(2p\cdot q_i)$, which constrains each scatterer's longitudinal momentum transfer. The analysis is extended to non-Abelian QCD by including color matrix factors, where the space-time symmetric and color-index symmetric component behaves identically to the Abelian case.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal coherence length $\Delta z_{\rm coh} \sim 25$ fm for a jet of momentum $p_0 \sim 10$ GeV/c with transverse momentum transfer $q_T \sim 0.4$ GeV/c at RHIC.
- For central AuAu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, the momentum kick model analysis gives $\langle n \rangle \sim 6$ and $\langle q_z \rangle \sim 1$ GeV.
- For the highest multiplicity $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7$ TeV at LHC, the analysis gives $\langle n \rangle \sim 2.4$ and $\langle q_z \rangle \sim 2$ GeV.
- The average longitudinal momentum kick per scatterer is $\langle q_{iz}\rangle \sim p_z/(2n)$, where $n$ is the number of scatterers.
- The transverse momentum distribution for each scatterer scales as $1/|{\bb q}_T|^4$.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier momentum kick model (Wong 2007-2011) by providing a quantum-field-theoretic mechanism for the longitudinal momentum kicks that model had postulated phenomenologically. The Bose-Einstein interference mechanism is consistent with earlier results on bremsstrahlung of real photons and gluons (Chew 1969, Lam 1997) and with the sum of ladder and cross-ladder diagrams in two-fermion collisions. The predicted collective recoil signatures are compared to ridge-particle correlations observed by the STAR, PHENIX, and PHOBOS Collaborations in AuAu collisions at RHIC. The threshold behavior for $n \ge 2$ is compared to centrality-dependent ridge yields measured by STAR with low-$p_T$ triggers (Daub 2008, Ray 2008, Kettler 2009, STAR 2011).
