# 2D Azimuthal Space for Au + Au Mid-Central Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV

> In this paper we will show that one can summarize the major two particle reaction plane azimuthal correlations for Au + Au mid-central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV by defining a 2D azimuthal space which is a summary of the event by event average.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the event-averaged distribution of two-particle azimuthal angles (phi_1, phi_2) with respect to the reaction plane can summarize the major two-particle reaction-plane azimuthal correlations for Au + Au mid-central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, but this summary depends on fixing the quadrupole flow axis (psi_2) to zero and orienting the hexapole flow axis (psi_3) to -90 degrees based on a mono-jet squeeze-out effect from a prior model.

## Method

The author defines a 2D azimuthal space (phi_1, phi_2) for particle pairs, with the quadrupole flow axis (v_2) fixed to the 0-180 degree axis and the hexapole flow axis (v_3) oriented to -90 degrees based on a mono-jet squeeze-out effect from a prior model (Longacre, 2019). Using a modified version of that prior model, the author generates event-averaged phi_1-phi_2 plane distributions for all, unlike-sign, and like-sign particle pairs, and calculates four three-particle correlators (C_112, C_123, C_132, C_143) along with the in-plane and out-of-plane components of the two-particle correlation.

## Key numbers

*   For unlike-sign pairs: C_112 = -0.0002, C_132 = 0.0010, C_123 = 0.0003, C_143 = 0.0000, <cos(phi_1)cos(phi_2)> = 0.0030, <sin(phi_1)sin(phi_2)> = 0.0032 (Table I).
*   For like-sign pairs: C_112 = -0.0024, C_132 = 0.0000, C_123 = 0.0004, C_143 = 0.0000, <cos(phi_1)cos(phi_2)> = -0.0021, <sin(phi_1)sin(phi_2)> = 0.0003 (Table II).
*   The two-particle correlation delta = <cos(phi_1 - phi_2)> for all particle pairs shows a dipole and a quadrupole; for unlike-sign pairs it shows a dipole, quadrupole, and hexapole; for like-sign pairs it shows a negative dipole and a quadrupole (Figures 6-8).
*   The simulation is for mid-central Au + Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200.0 GeV (Figure captions).

## Context

This work extends the mono-jet model of Longacre (2019) by adding a negative momentum-conservation correlation for like-sign pairs to better match the results of the Csepvsplane analysis (likely a prior experimental or model comparison). The paper introduces a 2D azimuthal space as a new summary tool for two-particle reaction-plane correlations in heavy-ion collisions, and it enables future comparisons of the predicted correlators (C_112, C_123, C_132, C_143) with experimental data from Au + Au collisions at RHIC. The appendix shows that a simple model with only v_2, v_3, and a two-particle correlation delta cannot reproduce the observed results for particles at central rapidity (Case II), indicating the need for the more complex structure captured by the 2D azimuthal plane.
