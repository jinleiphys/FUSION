# First-Order Chiral Phase Transition in High-Energy Collisions: Can Nucleation Prevent Spinodal Decomposition?

> We discuss homogeneous nucleation in a first-order chiral phase transition within an effective field theory approach to low-energy QCD. Exact decay rates and bubble profiles are obtained numerically and compared to analytic results obtained with the thin-wall approximation. The thin-wall approximation overestimates the nucleation rate for any degree of supercooling. The time scale for critical thermal fluctuations is calculated and compared to typical expansion times for high-energy hadronic or heavy-ion collisions. We find that significant supercooling is possible, and the relevant mechanism for phase conversion might be that of spinodal decomposition. Some potential experimental signatures of supercooling, such as an increase in the correlation length of the scalar condensate, are also discussed.

# First-Order Chiral Phase Transition in High-Energy Collisions: Can Nucleation Prevent Spinodal Decomposition?

## Key claim

Significant supercooling is possible in a first-order chiral phase transition during high-energy heavy-ion collisions, and the relevant mechanism for phase conversion might be spinodal decomposition rather than homogeneous nucleation, because the thin-wall approximation systematically overestimates nucleation rates and underestimates the degree of supercooling required for the metastable state to decay.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model coupled to quarks as an effective field theory for low-energy QCD, computing the one-loop finite-temperature effective potential for the chiral order parameter. They obtained exact numerical solutions for critical bubble profiles and nucleation rates by solving the Euler-Lagrange field equation for radially symmetric bubbles, and compared these to analytic results from the thin-wall approximation using a Landau-Ginzburg polynomial expansion of the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the standard parameter choice (g=5.5, λ²=20): T_c = 123.7 MeV
- Spinodal temperature for the standard parameter choice: T_sp = 108 MeV
- For g=10, λ²=20: spinodal temperature decreases to approximately 78 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV; pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV; sigma mass: m_σ = 600 MeV
- The exact numerical results show the system is likely to supercool for approximately two-thirds of the way from T_c to T_sp (x ≈ 0.67), while the thin-wall approximation gives x ≈ 0.4
- For a modified effective potential with a higher barrier, the metastable state remains stable for all x < 0.83
- The correlation length in the metastable state (l_f) can exceed that in the true minimum (l_t) by a factor of 2-3 depending on the degree of supercooling
- Expansion rates considered: H ≈ 0.1-1 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous studies of homogeneous nucleation in the QCD phase transition using bag model calculations (Ignatius 1994, Enqvist 1992, Kapusta) by employing a linear sigma model approach that yields a weaker first-order transition, which likely overestimates tunneling rates and underestimates supercooling effects. The results contradict the assumption that the Maxwell construction provides a reliable description of the phase transition dynamics in rapidly expanding systems. The paper enables future investigation of non-equilibrium signatures such as entropy production up to 30%, coherent pion emission from chiral field realignment, and event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
