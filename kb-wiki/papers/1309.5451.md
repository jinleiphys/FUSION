# Comment on "The low lying modes of triplet-condensed neutron matter and their effective theory"

> Contrary to what is claimed in the article by P. F. Bedaque and A. N. Nicholson [Phys. Rev. C 87, 055807 (2013)], their result do not contradict but rather complement the conclusion of the paper by L. B. Leinson [Phys. Rev. C 85, 0655021 (2012)] with respect to the low lying nonunitary excitations in the anisotropic neutron superfluid. In fact, the corresponding Goldstone modes (angulons), existing at zero temperature, can be found from Eq. (59) of the work by Leinson. Being massless at absolute zero these modes acquire a mass gap at finite temperature.

# Comment on "The low lying modes of triplet-condensed neutron matter and their effective theory"

## Key claim

The massless Goldstone modes (angulons) found by Bedaque and Nicholson (2013) for the anisotropic neutron superfluid at zero temperature are not contradictory to, but rather complement, the earlier work by Leinson (2012), because the massless solutions are contained in Eq. (59) of Leinson's paper and become gapped at finite temperature, violating the Nambu-Goldstone theorem at T > 0.

## Method

The author solves the general dispersion equation (Eq. 59 of Leinson (2012)) for collective excitations of the \(^{3}\)P\(_{2}\)(m\(_{j}\)=0) condensate in the low-temperature limit where \(y \equiv \Delta(T)/T \gg 1\) and \(\omega, qV_F \ll 2\Delta\). Angular averages over the Fermi surface are performed analytically to obtain explicit dispersion relations for the two nonunitary modes with m\(_{j}\) = \(\pm 1\), and the temperature-dependent mass gap is extracted.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the propagation velocities (in units of \(V_F\)) for the two angulon modes are: \(s_z^{(1,2)} = 0.5198\) along the \(z\)-axis; \(s_\perp^{(1)} = 0.4757\) and \(s_\perp^{(2)} = 0.7096\) in the orthogonal direction.
- The mass gap at low temperature is given by \(\tilde{\omega}_1(T \ll \Delta) = \frac{6 \varkappa^{1/2}}{\sqrt{2\pi\sqrt{3}+9}} \Delta(T) \langle \mathbf{b}_1^* \mathbf{b}_1 e^{-y\bar{b}} \rangle^{1/2}\), where \(\varkappa \simeq 0.989667\).
- The mass gap is exponentially small at low temperatures, passes through a maximum, and tends to zero both as \(T \rightarrow 0\) and as \(T \rightarrow T_c\).

## Context

This comment resolves an apparent contradiction between Bedaque and Nicholson (2013), who derived massless Goldstone bosons for the \(^{3}\)P\(_{2}\) condensate at T=0, and Leinson (2012), who found gapped nonunitary excitations at finite temperature. The author shows that the massless modes at T=0 numerically recover the dispersion law of Bedaque and Nicholson (2013), while at finite temperature the modes acquire a mass gap due to violation of the Nambu-Goldstone theorem, a phenomenon known from prior work (e.g., W. 1980s). The gapped nature of these waves (\(\omega > qV_F\)) means they avoid Landau damping and can propagate at finite temperature, which has implications for transport in neutron star interiors.
