# Stiffening of matter in quark-hadron continuity: a mini-review

> Recent observations of neutron stars, combined with causality, thermodynamic stability, and nuclear constraints, indicate a rapid stiffening of QCD matter at densities slightly above nuclear saturation density ($n_0 \simeq 0.16\,{\rm fm}^{-3}$). The evolution of the stiffening is faster than expected from purely nucleonic models with many-body repulsion. Taking into account the quark substructure of baryons, we argue that the saturation of quark states occurs at $\sim$ 2-3$n_0$, driving quark matter formation even before baryonic cores of radius $\sim$0.5 fm spatially overlap. We describe the continuous transitions from hadronic to quark matter within a quarkyonic matter model in which gluons are assumed to remain confining at densities of interest. To obtain analytic insight into the transient regime, we construct an ideal model of quarkyonic matter, the {\it IdylliQ} model, in which one can freely switch from baryonic to quark descriptions and vice versa.

## Key claim

Recent neutron star observations combined with causality, thermodynamic stability, and nuclear constraints indicate a rapid stiffening of QCD matter at densities slightly above nuclear saturation density (n0 ≈ 0.16 fm−3), and the evolution of this stiffening is faster than expected from purely nucleonic models with many-body repulsion; the paper argues that quark saturation at ∼2-3n0 drives quark matter formation even before baryonic cores of radius ∼0.5 fm spatially overlap, within a quarkyonic matter model where gluons remain confining at the densities of interest.

## Method

The author constructs an idealized model of quarkyonic matter called the IdylliQ model, which incorporates a sum rule relating baryon and quark momentum distributions to enforce the Pauli principle at the quark level. The model assumes confinement (quarks exist only as baryon constituents), neglects all other interactions, and uses a specific Yukawa form for the internal quark momentum distribution φ that allows analytic inversion of the sum rule. The energy density is minimized by optimizing the baryon occupation probability fB at each momentum subject to fixed baryon density, yielding a momentum shell solution where baryons occupy low-momentum states with probability 1/Nc3 (dual to saturated quark states) and higher-momentum states with probability 1.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n0 ≈ 0.16 fm−3
- Quark saturation occurs at ∼2-3n0
- Baryonic core radius: ∼0.5 fm
- Constituent quark mass Mq ≈ 300 MeV
- The kinetic energy coefficient in quark descriptions is approximately a factor of Nc2 ∼ 10 larger than in nuclear descriptions
- Maximum mass relation for free massless quark gas: Mmax/M⊙ = 2.03 M⊙ × (56 MeV/fm3 / B)1/2, with corresponding radius R|Mmax = 10.7 km × (Mmax / 2.0 M⊙)
- Relativistic baryons emerge around nB ∼ M_B3/Nc3 ∼ Λ3 ∼ 5n0
- The width of the momentum distribution φ is ∼Λ ∼ 0.2-0.4 GeV
- Baryon overlap density estimated around ∼5-6n0, with quark saturation at roughly half that density (∼2-3n0)

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept originally proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and the momentum shell picture conjectured by McLerran (2018) as a mechanism for rapid stiffening of the equation of state. The IdylliQ model provides a concrete realization of continuous hadron-to-quark matter transitions, complementing earlier phenomenological interpolation models by Masuda (2012) and Kojo (2014) that extracted general trends such as sound speed peaks and the weaker role of strangeness in quark matter. The results enable future studies of how baryonic excitations near the Fermi surface might organize into more complex paired states, and connect to lattice QCD studies of two-color and isospin QCD by Brandt (2022), Abbott (2023), and Iida (2024) that estimate overlap densities of diquarks or pions.
