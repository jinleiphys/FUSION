# Influences of statistics and initial size fluctuation on high-order cumulants of conserved quantities in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> By the generator of the UrQMD model, event statistics for the products of kurtosis ($κ$) and variance ($σ^2$) of net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions are carefully studied. It is shown that the statistics at RHIC/BES below $\sqrt {s_{NN}} < 19.6$ GeV are not sufficient for using the method of Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC). Corresponding results are systematically underestimated. A way to improve the CBWC method is proposed. It can remove the statistics dependence of the data and reduce the initial size fluctuation as well.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the statistics available at RHIC/BES for collision energies below 19.6 GeV are insufficient for the Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method, causing the product of kurtosis and variance (κσ²) of net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions to be systematically underestimated. The authors propose an improved CBWC method using a wider centrality bin width of approximately 1.0% to remove this statistics dependence while still reducing initial size fluctuations.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model to generate 250 million minimum bias Au+Au collision events at 11.5 GeV, then randomly divided these into sub-samples of varying sizes (1M, 2M, 5M, 10M, 25M, and 50M events). They calculated κσ² for net-proton and net-charge multiplicity distributions using the standard CBWC method (calculating cumulants in each multiplicity bin Nch) and compared results across different statistics sizes. They then tested an improved method where the centrality bin width was varied from δ0.2% to δ5.0% instead of using each individual Nch bin, examining how the mean κσ² changed with statistics for each bin width.

## Key numbers

- For net-proton at 0-5% centrality and 11.5 GeV, the difference from the saturated κσ² value (at 250M events) is 7.0% at 10M events and 2.8% at 25M events; the required statistics for less than 5% difference is about 15M events
- For net-charge at 0-5% centrality and 11.5 GeV, the differences from the saturated value are 7.4% at 25M events and 4.1% at 50M events; the required statistics for less than 5% difference is about 50M events
- At the lowest RHIC/BES energy of 7.7 GeV, the total available events for net-proton analysis is 3 Million (0-80% centrality), with approximately 0.19 Million events in the 0-5% centrality bin
- The proper centrality bin width for the improved CBWC method is found to be approximately δ1.0% for both net-proton and net-charge κσ² measurements

## Context

This work extends the discussion of non-critical effects on high-order cumulant measurements in heavy ion collisions, specifically addressing the Centrality Bin Width Correction method introduced by Luo (2011) and used by the STAR Collaboration. The paper builds on studies by Heiselberg (2001) and Skokov, Friman, and Redlich (2013) regarding volume fluctuation effects, and on the centrality resolution work by Luo, Xu, Mohanty, and Xu (2013). The proposed improved CBWC method enables more reliable extraction of κσ² at low RHIC/BES energies (below 19.6 GeV) where current statistics are limited, allowing better comparison with lattice QCD predictions for the QCD phase transition.
