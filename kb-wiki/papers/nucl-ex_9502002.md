# High-K Isomers in 176W and Mechanisms of K-Violation

> An isomer, with t1/2 = 35 +- 10 ns and J, Kpi = 14, 14+, has been observed in the nucleus 176W using the reaction 150Nd(30Si,4n) at a beam energy of 133 MeV. The isomer exhibits an unusual pattern of decay in which the _majority_ of the flux proceeds directly to states with <K>=0, bypassing available levels of intermediate K. This severe breakdown of normal K-selection rules in 176W is compared with recent observations of K-violation in neighboring nuclei, within the framework of proposed theoretical approaches. The available data on these K-violating decays seem to have a consistent explanation in models of K-mixing which include large-amplitude fluctuations of the nuclear shape.

## Key claim

The paper reports the observation of a K=14+ isomer in 176W with a half-life of 35 ± 10 ns that exhibits an unusual decay pattern where the *majority* of the decay flux proceeds directly to states with K=0, bypassing available intermediate-K states, a severe breakdown of normal K-selection rules that the abstract presents as potentially explainable by models of K-mixing involving large-amplitude shape fluctuations, though the paper does not definitively prove this mechanism.

## Method

The isomer was produced using the reaction 150Nd(30Si,4n) at a beam energy of 133 MeV, with gamma rays detected by the Argonne-Notre Dame BGO gamma-ray facility consisting of 12 Compton-suppressed Ge detectors and an inner array of 50 BGO elements. A delayed BGO fold parameter was created to isolate decays of high-spin isomers, and timing parameters for both BGO and Ge detectors were adjusted off-line using an empirical energy-dependent correction formula. The level scheme was constructed from 45 × 10^6 events, with spins deduced by Directional Correlations from Oriented states (DCO) and rotational band assignments confirmed by regular M1 and E2 cascades.

## Key numbers

- Isomer half-life: t1/2 = 35 ± 10 ns for the Kπ = 14+ isomer in 176W
- Beam energy: 133 MeV for the 150Nd(30Si,4n) reaction
- Total events recorded: 45 × 10^6
- Weakest gamma ray intensity: about 0.025% of total population of 176W, corresponding to an absolute cross-section of ~5 μb
- Interaction matrix element between g-band and s-band: |Vgs| ≈ 30–33 keV, with an upper limit of |Vgs| < 33 keV from the 66-keV difference between the 16g+ and 16s+ levels
- Deformation of g band: increases gradually from ε2 = 0.24 in the ground-state to ε2 = 0.29 at spin 18, then remains approximately constant
- Deformation of s band: ε2 ≈ 0.22–0.23 for the range of spins observed
- Interaction matrix element between 02+ band and s band: |V02+,s| ~ 30–110 keV
- Second isomer: t1/2 ≈ 10 ns (no error bar given in text)
- B(M1) for transitions from the 02+ band to the g band: ~0.02 W.u. (assuming mixing ratio δ=0)

## Context

This work extends the study of K-violating decays in the A~180 region, following observations of anomalously low hindrance factors in neighboring nuclei such as 174Hf (Chowdhury 1988) and 179W (Walker 1994). The 176W isomer provides a particularly striking case because, unlike previous examples where anomalous decays constituted only a small fraction of the total decay, the majority of the decay here proceeds through highly K-violating ΔK=14 transitions. The comparison of hindrance factors for isomers with the same quasiparticle configuration in 176W and the isotone 174Hf enables systematic tests of γ-tunneling models (Chowdhury 1988, Bengtsson 1989) versus Coriolis mixing as the dominant mechanism for K-violation, and the measured interaction matrix elements between rotational bands provide constraints for future theoretical descriptions.
