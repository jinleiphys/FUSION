# Quasi-elastic scattering of 6He, 7Be, and 8B nuclei by 12C nuclei

> The observed cross sections of quasi-elastic scattering of 6He, 7Be, and 8B nuclei by 12C nuclei are described within the framework of the diffraction nuclear model and the model of nucleus-nucleus scattering in the high-energy approximation with a double folding potential, for intermediate energies of the incident particles. The calculations make use of realistic distributions of nucleon densities and take account of the Coulomb interaction and inelastic scattering with excitation of low-lying collective states of the target.

## Key claim
Both the diffraction nuclear model and the high-energy approximation (HEA) with a double folding potential can satisfactorily describe the observed quasi-elastic scattering cross sections of 6He, 7Be, and 8B on 12C at intermediate energies, but the diffraction model offers greater flexibility through cluster-specific fitting parameters, while the HEA model overestimates cross sections at scattering angles below 10 degrees for some systems.

## Method
The author calculated quasi-elastic scattering cross sections using two theoretical approaches: a diffraction model treating the projectile as a two-cluster system (4He+2n for 6He, 4He+3He for 7Be, 7Be+p for 8B) with single and double scattering amplitudes including nuclear and Coulomb contributions, and a high-energy approximation (HEA) model using a double folding potential with the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential in CDM3Y6 form and a Woods-Saxon imaginary potential. Both models incorporated realistic nucleon density distributions from large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations or density functional methods, accounted for Coulomb interaction, and included inelastic scattering contributions from excitation of the 2+ (4.44 MeV) and 3- (9.64 MeV) collective states of the 12C target.

## Key numbers
- 6He on 12C at T=494 MeV: diffraction model parameters r0=0.8 fm, δ1=0.7, γ1=0.49, δ2=0.080, γ2=0.072; HEA model parameters NV=1.0, NW=1.0, rW=0.91 fm, aW=0.61 fm.
- 7Be on 12C at T=280 MeV: diffraction model parameters r0=1.1 fm, δ1=0.9, γ1=0.90, δ2=0.011, γ2=0.026; HEA model parameters NV=1.0, NW=1.0, rW=0.95 fm, aW=0.58 fm.
- 8B on 12C at T=320 MeV: diffraction model parameters r0=0.8 fm, δ1=0.9, γ1=0.90, δ2=0.220, γ2=0.044; HEA model parameters NV=1.0, NW=1.0, rW=0.89 fm, aW=0.75 fm.
- Target deformation parameters used: β2=0.582 for the 2+ state and β3=0.365 for the 3- state of 12C.
- Inelastic scattering contributions become substantial only for scattering angles θ > (6 to 8) degrees.

## Context
This work extends the diffraction scattering model developed in Kovalchuk (earlier works) and the HEA with double folding potential from Satchler and Love (1979) to describe quasi-elastic scattering of halo nuclei 6He, 7Be, and 8B on 12C, where experimental data from previous studies (reported in references 7 and 8) include an admixture of inelastic channels. The results enable future generalization to a three-cluster model for 8B (4He+3He+p) as suggested by the author, and address the known open question of theory-experiment divergence at scattering angles below 10 degrees noted in prior work (reference 7).
