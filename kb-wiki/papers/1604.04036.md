# Polarization of massive fermions in a vortical fluid

> Fermions become polarized in a vorticular fluid due to spin-vorticity coupling. Such a polarization can be calculated from the Wigner function in a quantum kinetic approach. Extending previous results for chiral fermions, we derive the Wigner function for massive fermions up to the next-to-leading order in spatial gradient expansion. The polarization density of fermions can be calculated from the axial vector component of the Wigner function and is found to be proportional to the local vorticity $ω$. The polarizations per particle for fermions and anti-fermions decrease with the chemical potential and increase with energy (mass). Both quantities approach the asymptotic value $\hbarω/4$ in the large energy (mass) limit. The polarization per particle for fermions is always smaller than that for anti-fermions, whose ratio of fermions to anti-fermions also decreases with the chemical potential. The polarization per particle on the Cooper-Frye freeze-out hyper-surface can also be formulated and is consistent with the previous result of Becattini et al..

## Key claim

Massive fermions in a vortical fluid become polarized due to spin-vorticity coupling, with the polarization per particle approaching the asymptotic value \(\hbar\omega/4\) in the large energy (mass) limit; the polarization per particle for fermions is always smaller than that for anti-fermions, and both decrease with chemical potential and increase with energy (mass).

## Method

The authors extend a quantum kinetic approach based on the Wigner function, previously developed for chiral (massless) fermions, to massive fermions up to next-to-leading order in a spatial gradient expansion. They derive the Wigner function components for massive fermions from the Dirac equation, solve the kinetic equations perturbatively in powers of \(\hbar\), and extract the axial vector component which gives the spin density. The polarization density is obtained by integrating the axial vector component over momentum, and the polarization per particle on the Cooper-Frye freeze-out hypersurface is formulated.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic value of polarization per particle for both fermions and anti-fermions in the large energy (mass) limit is \(\hbar\omega/4\).
- At zero temperature, the integrated polarization per particle for fermions is \(\frac{3}{4} \hbar\beta^{-1} \boldsymbol{\omega} \frac{\mu}{\mu^2-m^2} \theta (\mu-m)\).
- At zero temperature, the spin polarization density for fermions is \(\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\hbar \beta^{-1} \boldsymbol{\omega} \mu\sqrt{\mu^2-m^2}\theta (\mu-m)\).
- At zero temperature, the particle number density for fermions is \(\frac{1}{3\pi^2}(\mu^2-m^2)^{3/2}\theta (\mu-m)\).
- At zero temperature, the polarization density from electromagnetic fields is \(\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\hbar Q \mathbf{B}\sqrt{\mu^2-m^2}\theta (\mu-m)\).

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function method for chiral fermions from Gao et al. (2012) and Chen et al. (2012) to massive fermions, enabling calculation of polarization induced by vorticity in heavy-ion collisions. The results are consistent with the previous work of Becattini et al. on the Cooper-Frye freeze-out hypersurface. The formalism enables future calculations of hyperon polarization in non-central heavy-ion collisions, connecting to measurements of global polarization through parity-violating hyperon decays as proposed by Liang and Wang (2004).
