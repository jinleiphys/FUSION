# Effective restoration of chiral symmetry in excited mesons

> A fast restoration of chiral symmetry in excited mesons is demonstrated. A minimal "realistic" chirally symmetric confining model is used, where the only interaction between quarks is the linear instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential. Chiral symmetry breaking is generated via the nonperturbative resummation of valence quarks self-energy loops and the meson bound states are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The excited mesons fall into approximate chiral multiplets and lie on the approximately linear radial and angular Regge trajectories, though a significant deviation from the linearity of the angular trajectory is observed.

## Key claim

Using a minimal chirally symmetric confining model with only a linear instantaneous Lorentz-vector potential, the authors demonstrate that excited mesons exhibit a fast effective restoration of chiral symmetry, with states falling into approximate chiral multiplets at large radial excitation number \(n\) and large spin \(J\), though the restoration is slower with increasing \(n\) than with increasing \(J\).

## Method

The authors employ a generalized Nambu and Jona-Lasinio model (GNJL) in 3+1 dimensions with an instantaneous linear Lorentz-vector confining potential. Chiral symmetry breaking is generated via the nonperturbative resummation of valence quark self-energy loops in the rainbow approximation (Schwinger-Dyson equation), and meson bound states are obtained from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation. The Salpeter amplitude is decomposed into components according to \(J^{PC}\) quantum numbers, and the resulting coupled integral equations are solved by expansion into basis functions, yielding a matrix eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate: \(\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = (-0.231\sqrt{\sigma})^3\); with \(\sqrt{\sigma} \approx 300\)–\(400\) MeV, this gives a condensate between \((-70\ \text{MeV})^3\) and \((-90\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- Mass splittings within chiral multiplets decrease asymptotically as \(1/\sqrt{n}\) for fixed \(J\), with the fitted line \(0.7\sqrt{\sigma/n}\) shown for \(J=0\) and \(J=1\).
- For the lowest-lying states: the \(0^{-+}\) meson mass is \(0.00\sqrt{\sigma}\) (the pion in the chiral limit), the \(0^{++}\) meson mass is \(1.49\sqrt{\sigma}\), and the \(1^{--}\) meson from the \((0,1)\oplus(1,0)\) multiplet has mass \(1.55\sqrt{\sigma}\).
- For \(J=1\) states at \(n=0\): the \(1^{+-}\) mass is \(2.68\sqrt{\sigma}\), the \(1^{--}\) from \((1/2,1/2)_b\) is \(2.78\sqrt{\sigma}\), and the \(1^{++}\) mass is \(2.20\sqrt{\sigma}\).
- For \(J=6\) states at \(n=0\): the \(6^{-+}\) mass is \(6.88\sqrt{\sigma}\) and the \(6^{++}\) from \((0,1)\oplus(1,0)\) is \(6.83\sqrt{\sigma}\), showing near degeneracy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chiral symmetry restoration in heavy-light mesons with a quadratic confining potential by KNR (2004) and in models with more complicated instantaneous potentials by SW (2004). It provides the first explicit demonstration of effective chiral symmetry restoration in light-light mesons within a solvable field-theoretical model in 3+1 dimensions using a linear potential, building on the GNJL model framework of Finger (1981), Orsay (1984), and BR (1985). The results enable future lattice studies of high-lying states and motivate inclusion of additional gluonic interactions to improve the quantitative agreement with phenomenological values of the quark condensate and pion decay constant.
