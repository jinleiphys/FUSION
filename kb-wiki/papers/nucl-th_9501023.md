# Analysis of Boltzmann-Langevin Dynamics in Nuclear Matter

> The Boltzmann-Langevin dynamics of harmonic modes in nuclear matter is analyzed within linear-response theory, both with an elementary treatment and by using the frequency-dependent response function. It is shown how the source terms agitating the modes can be obtained from the basic BL correlation kernel by a simple projection onto the associated dual basis states, which are proportional to the RPA amplitudes and can be expressed explicitly. The source terms for the correlated agitation of any two such modes can then be extracted directly, without consideration of the other modes. This facilitates the analysis of collective modes in unstable matter and makes it possible to asses the accuracy of an approximate projection technique employed previously.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the source terms agitating collective modes in Boltzmann-Langevin dynamics can be obtained directly from the basic correlation kernel by projecting onto dual basis states proportional to RPA amplitudes, without needing to consider other modes; this exact projection method improves upon an earlier approximate technique that only retained the two collective modes and inverted a 2x2 overlap matrix.

## Method

The authors analyze the Boltzmann-Langevin dynamics of harmonic modes in nuclear matter within linear-response theory, using both an elementary treatment and a frequency-dependent response function approach. They expand the phase-space density on the eigenfunctions of the Vlasov equation, introduce the dual basis states that are orthogonal to these eigenfunctions, and show that the source terms for mode agitation are obtained by projecting the microscopic diffusion coefficient onto these dual basis states. The explicit form of the dual basis is derived, proportional to the RPA amplitudes \(Q_\k^\nu(\p) = 1/(\k\cdot\bfv - \omega_\nu^*)\).

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for symmetric and uncharged nuclear matter is about 16 MeV.
* The fastest amplification time for unstable modes is about \(0.7 \cdot 10^{-22}\ \rm s\).
* For a typical temperature \(T = 4\ \rm MeV\) and density \(\rho = 0.3\rho_0\), the predicted average magnitude of the density fluctuation equals the average density at \(t \approx 3.5 \cdot 10^{-22}\ {\rm s}\), so results are not meaningful beyond \(t \approx 3 \cdot 10^{-22}\ {\rm s}\).
* The approximate projection method (using only the 2x2 collective overlap matrix) underestimates the exact source term by about 30% for the most rapidly amplified modes.

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Langevin model developed by Ayik (1988) and the linear-response framework for unstable nuclear matter introduced by Chomaz, Colonna, and Randrup (1993). The earlier approximate projection technique (Chomaz et al., 1993) retained only the two collective modes and inverted a 2x2 overlap matrix; the present paper provides the exact dual basis states, enabling direct calculation of source terms for any pair of modes. This enables more accurate studies of spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter and may facilitate the development of simplified stochastic mean-field approaches where the complicated Langevin term is replaced by a simpler approximate term leading to the same agitation rates.
