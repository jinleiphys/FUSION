# Chebyshev Based Spectral Representations of Neutron-Star Equations of State

> Causal parametric representations of neutron-star equations of state are constructed here using Chebyshev polynomial based spectral expansions. The accuracies of these representations are evaluated for a collection of model equations of state from a variety of nuclear-theory models and also a collection of equations of state with first- or second-order phase transitions of various sizes. These tests show that the Chebyshev based representations are convergent (even for equations of state with phase transitions) as the number of spectral basis functions is increased. This study finds that the Chebyshev based representations are generally more accurate than a previously studied power-law based spectral representation, and that pressure-based representations are generally more accurate than those based on enthalpy.

## Key claim
Chebyshev polynomial based spectral expansions provide convergent parametric representations of neutron-star equations of state even for cases with first- or second-order phase transitions, whereas a previously studied power-law based spectral representation was non-convergent for equations of state with larger discontinuities. The Chebyshev based representations are generally more accurate than the power-law based ones, and pressure-based Chebyshev representations are generally more accurate than enthalpy-based Chebyshev representations.

## Method
The authors constructed causal parametric representations of the neutron-star equation of state by expressing the velocity function Upsilon (related to sound speed) as a spectral expansion using Chebyshev polynomial basis functions, in both pressure-based and enthalpy-based formulations. They performed best-fit minimizations of a chi-squared error measure comparing the spectral model energy densities to tabulated reference equations of state, using a Levenburg-Marquardt algorithm implemented in Fortran and Python. Tests were conducted on 26 nuclear-theory based equations of state and on collections of equations of state with first- or second-order phase transitions of varying sizes, with the phase transitions inserted into the GM1L nuclear-theory based equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The pressure-based Chebyshev representations have average modeling errors roughly half those of the enthalpy-based Chebyshev representations for the collection of 26 nuclear-theory based equations of state, for each value of the number of spectral parameters N_parms.
- For equations of state with first-order phase transitions, the fitting errors decrease with increasing N_parms as chi(N_parms) proportional to N_parms^{-1/2} (approximately) for large N_parms.
- For equations of state with second-order phase transitions, the fitting errors decrease as chi(N_parms) proportional to N_parms^{-3/2} (approximately).
- The lower pressure bound of the spectral domain is p_0 = 1.20788 x 10^32 erg/cm^3.
- The enthalpy-based Chebyshev and power-law representations have almost identical errors for N_parms less than or equal to 5, but Chebyshev based models are more accurate for larger N_parms.

## Context
This work extends the line of causal parametric representations of neutron-star equations of state developed by Lindblom (2010, 2018, 2022) and addresses the non-convergence problem identified by Lindblom (2024) for power-law based spectral representations when applied to equations of state with strong phase transitions. The new Chebyshev based representations enable more accurate and convergent modeling of neutron-star equations of state, including those with phase transitions, which is important for determining equation of state parameters by matching astronomical observations of neutron-star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.
