# One particle distribution function and shear viscosity in magnetic field: a relaxation time approach

> We calculate the $δf$ correction to the one particle distribution function in presence of magnetic field and non-zero shear viscosity within the relaxation time approximation. The $δf$ correction is found to be electric charge dependent. Subsequently, we also calculate one longitudinal and four transverse shear viscous coefficients as a function of dimensionless Hall parameter $χ_{H}$ in presence of the magnetic field. We find that a proper linear combination of the shear viscous coefficients calculated in this work scales with the result obtained from Grad's moment method in \cite{Denicol:2018rbw}. Calculation of invariant yield of $π^{-}$ in a simple Bjorken expansion with cylindrical symmetry shows no noticeable change in spectra due to the $δf$ correction for realistic values of the magnetic field and relaxation time. However, when transverse expansion is taken into account using a blast wave type flow field we found noticeable change in spectra and elliptic flow coefficients due to the $δf$ correction. The $δf$ is also found to be very sensitive on the magnitude of magnetic field. Hence we think it is important to take into account the $δf$ correction in more realistic numerical magnetohydrodynamics simulations.

## Key claim

The δf correction to the one-particle distribution function in the presence of a magnetic field is electric charge dependent and very sensitive to the magnitude of the magnetic field, but in a simple Bjorken expansion with cylindrical symmetry it produces no noticeable change in pion spectra for realistic values of the magnetic field and relaxation time; only when transverse expansion is included via a blast wave flow field do the spectra and elliptic flow coefficients show noticeable changes due to the δf correction.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, including a magnetic force term and a non-zero velocity gradient, to obtain the δf correction to the equilibrium distribution function. They expressed the fourth-rank tensor C_{ijkl}(ε) as a linear combination of eight independent basis tensors constructed from δ_{ij}, b_i, and ε_{ijk}, and solved for the coefficients c^{(n)} as functions of the dimensionless Hall parameter χ_H = qBτ_c/ε. The five shear viscosity coefficients η_{(0)} through η_{(4)} were then derived from the stress tensor definition in kinetic theory and compared with results from Grad's 14-moment method.

## Key numbers

* The longitudinal shear viscosity for massless bosons at μ=0 is η_{(0)} = (2/225) g τ_c π² T⁴.
* The longitudinal shear viscosity for massless fermions at μ=0 is η_{(0)} = (7/900) g τ_c π² T⁴.
* The transverse shear viscosity coefficients satisfy η_{(1)} = η_{(0)}/(1+4χ_H²), η_{(2)} = η_{(0)}/(1+χ_H²), η_{(3)} = 2χ_H η_{(1)}, and η_{(4)} = χ_H η_{(2)}.
* The coefficients c^{(7)} and c^{(8)} in the δf expansion depend linearly and cubically on χ_H respectively, making δf electric charge dependent.
* For the Bjorken expansion with transverse flow, visible corrections to the invariant yield of π⁻ were found for magnetic field values up to qB = 100 m_π², with relaxation time τ_c = 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of shear viscous coefficients in a magnetic field using the relaxation time approximation, complementing the Grad's 14-moment method of Denicol (2018) which the authors show yields identical results after appropriate scaling of the Hall parameter. The study addresses the previously neglected effect of magnetic field on the freezeout distribution function in magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, where prior work by Gursoy (2014), Zakharov (2014), Pang (2016), Inghirami (2016), and others focused only on the magnetic field's effect on fluid evolution. The results enable future implementation of the δf correction in numerical magnetohydrodynamics codes to study charge-dependent elliptic flow and the chiral magnetic effect.
