# A statistical method to estimate low-energy hadronic cross sections

> In this article we propose a model based on the Statistical Bootstrap approach to estimate the cross sections of different hadronic reactions up to a few GeV in c.m.s energy. The method is based on the idea, when two particles collide a so called fireball is formed, which after a short time period decays statistically into a specific final state. To calculate the probabilities we use a phase space description extended with quark combinatorial factors and the possibility of more than one fireball formation. In a few simple cases the probability of a specific final state can be calculated analytically, where we show that the model is able to reproduce the ratios of the considered cross sections. We also show that the model is able to describe proton\,-\,antiproton annihilation at rest. In the latter case we used a numerical method to calculate the more complicated final state probabilities. Additionally, we examined the formation of strange and charmed mesons as well, where we used existing data to fit the relevant model parameters.

# A statistical method to estimate low-energy hadronic cross sections

## Key claim

The authors propose a statistical bootstrap model that can estimate hadronic cross sections up to a few GeV in center-of-mass energy, but the method relies on calculating ratios of formation probabilities relative to a reference channel rather than absolute cross sections, and the model parameters for strange and charm quark production must be fitted to existing experimental data.

## Method

The model assumes that colliding particles form a fireball that decays statistically into final states, with the probability of each channel calculated from phase space integrals extended by quark combinatorial factors and a multi-fireball decay scheme. The fireball can undergo chain-like decays into two or three hadrons per step, following Frautschi's energy-independent decay probabilities (0.69 for two-body and 0.24 for three-body decays). The total formation probability for a given final state is computed from the fireball formation probabilities, hadronization probabilities using the Hagedorn density of states, and quark combinatorial factors based on a multinomial distribution of quark-antiquark pairs, with the number of pairs given by N(M) = (1 + sqrt(1 + M^2/T_0^2))/2.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature parameter T_0 = 160 MeV, determined by fitting to experimental ratios of cross sections
- M_0 = 500 MeV in the density of states formula rho(M) = a sqrt(M)/(M_0 + M)^3.5 exp(M/T_0)
- Fireball decay probabilities: P^d_2 = 0.69 for two-body decays, P^d_3 = 0.24 for three-body decays
- Interaction volume set to V = 4 pi/(3 m_pi^3)
- Minimum invariant mass for a fireball to produce a real final state: m_c = 2 m_pi
- Energy range for strange quark probability fitting: E in {1.876, 2.602} GeV for the ratio sigma(p anti-p -> pi+ pi-)/sigma(p anti-p -> K+ K-)
- Nine nucleon and delta resonances considered with branching ratios above 15% for the p pi^0 channel, with masses ranging from 1.232 GeV (Delta_1232) to 1.930 GeV (Delta_1950)

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap approach of Hagedorn (1965, 1968) and Frautschi (1971) to calculate specific hadronic cross sections rather than just particle multiplicities, incorporating multi-fireball decay schemes similar to Sitte (2012) and Bediaga (1990) but with a different decay scheme, and adding quark combinatorial factors not previously used with the bootstrap idea. The model is intended to provide elementary cross sections for improving BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport code simulations of heavy-ion collisions as developed by Wolf (1990, 1993, 1997), and can be compared with the one-fireball model of Ferroni (2011) which showed agreement with data in the 2.1 to 2.6 GeV range.
