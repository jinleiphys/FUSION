# Coexistence of single particle and collective excitation in $^{61}$Ni

> The high spin states in 61 Ni have been studied using the fusion evaporation reaction, Ti( $^{14}$C,3n) $^{61}$Ni at an incident beam energy of 40 MeV. A Compton suppressed multi-HPGe detector setup, consisting of six Clover detectors and three single crystal HPGe detectors was used to detect the de-exciting $γ$ rays from the excited states. The level scheme has been extended up to an excitation energy of 12.8 MeV and a tentative J$_π$ = 35/2$^+$ . The low-lying negative parity levels are found to be generated by single particle excitation within the f p shell and also excitations to the g$_{9/2}$ orbitals as explained well with shell model calculations using the GXPF1Br+V M U (modified) interaction. Two rotational structure of regular E2 sequences with small to moderate axial deformation have been established at higher excitation energy. Most interestingly, two sequences of M1 transitions are reported for the first time and described as magnetic rotational bands. The shears mechanism for both the bands can be described satisfactorily by the geometrical model. The shell model calculation involving the cross shell excitation beyond the fp shell well reproduce the M1 and E2 sequences. The shell model predicted B(M1) values for the magnetic rotational band B1 show the decreasing trend with spin as expected with closing of the shears.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of collective rotational structures in $^{61}$Ni, including two magnetic rotational bands (B1 and B3) composed of M1 transitions, which are described by the shears mechanism; the level scheme is extended up to 12.8 MeV excitation energy and a tentative spin-parity of 35/2$^+$, though the highest-spin assignments remain tentative due to limited statistics.

## Method

High-spin states in $^{61}$Ni were populated via the fusion-evaporation reaction $^{50}$Ti($^{14}$C,3n)$^{61}$Ni at a beam energy of 40 MeV, using a Compton-suppressed multi-HPGe detector array of six Clover and three single-crystal detectors at the John D. Fox Superconducting Accelerator Facility at Florida State University. Gamma-gamma coincidence data were collected, and multipolarities and parities of transitions were determined using DCO ratio and IPDCO polarization asymmetry measurements. Shell model calculations using the GXPF1Br+$V_{MU}$(modified) interaction, including cross-shell excitations to the $\nu$g$_{9/2}$ and $\nu$d$_{5/2}$ orbitals, were performed to interpret the low-lying states and collective bands.

## Key numbers

* Level scheme extended to an excitation energy of 12.8 MeV and a tentative spin-parity of 35/2$^+$.
* A total of 76 new transitions and 28 new levels were observed compared to the last published work.
* The ground state of $^{61}$Ni has $J^\pi = 3/2^-$; the lowest positive parity state $9/2^+$ is at 2121 keV.
* Band B2 consists of E2 transitions (1179, 1502, 1540, 1588, 1822, and 1945 keV) extending from 3298 keV up to 12874 keV.
* Band B1 M1 transitions: 384, 562, 779, and 1295 keV; a crossover E2 transition of 1341 keV was tentatively placed from the 21/2$^+$ to the 17/2$^+$ level.
* Band B3 M1 transitions: 349, 525, 784, 863, and 938 keV, extending up to 11138 keV.
* Band B4 E2 transitions: 1721, 1314, 2111, 1684, and 2042 keV, extending up to 10993 keV.
* A total of $9.4 \times 10^4$ $\gamma$-$\gamma$ coincidence events were accumulated.

## Context

This work extends the high-spin study of $^{61}$Ni beyond the previous work by Saradindu et al. (2020), which used a $^{59}$Co($^{7}$Li,$\alpha$n) reaction and identified states up to a possible spin of 17/2$^+$ at 6734 keV. The observation of magnetic rotational bands in $^{61}$Ni follows similar findings in $^{60}$Ni (Torres et al., 2008) and $^{62}$Co (Niru et al.), where the shears mechanism involves proton holes in the $\pi$f$_{7/2}$ orbital and neutron particles in the $\nu$g$_{9/2}$ orbital. The results enable further investigation of wobbling motions predicted by CDFT calculations for $^{59-63}$Ni (Hu et al.), particularly for $^{61}$Ni as an intermediate odd-mass nucleus in this region.
