# Renormalization of One-Pion Exchange in Higher Partial Waves in Chiral Effective Field Theory for Antinucleon-Nucleon System

> The renormalization of iterated one-pion exchange (OPE) has been studied in Chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) for the antinucleon-nucleon ($\overline{N}\!N$) scattering in some partial waves (Phys. Rev. C 105, 054005 (2022)). We go further for the other higher partial waves but with total angular momenta $J\leq 3$ in this paper. Contact interactions are represented by a complex spherical well in coordinate space. Changing the radius of the spherical well means changing the cutoff. We check the cutoff dependence of the phase shifts, inelasticities, and mixing angles for the partial waves, and show that contact interactions are needed at leading order in channels where the singular tensor potentials of OPE are attractive. Results are compared with the energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of $\overline{N}\!N$ scattering data. Comparisons between our conclusions and applications of $χ$EFT to the nucleon-nucleon system are discussed as well.

## Key claim

Contact interactions (counterterms) must be promoted to leading order in antinucleon-nucleon partial waves where the singular tensor part of one-pion exchange is attractive, even when naive dimensional analysis would place those counterterms at higher orders; this promotion is required to achieve cutoff independence of phase shifts, inelasticities, and mixing angles for partial waves with total angular momentum J ≤ 3.

## Method

The author solved the partial-wave Schrödinger equation with a nonperturbative one-pion exchange potential in coordinate space, using a complex spherical well of variable radius b (with cutoff Λ ≡ 1/b) to represent short-range contact interactions. For each partial wave, the cutoff dependence of phase shifts, inelasticities, and mixing angles was examined before and after including counterterms; where cutoff dependence was found, the real and imaginary parts of the spherical well strength were fitted to the Groningen energy-dependent partial-wave analysis at a laboratory kinetic energy of 20 MeV, and the resulting observables were compared with the partial-wave analysis over laboratory momenta from 0 to 450 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The boundary radius separating short-range and long-range interactions is taken as b = 1.2 fm in the Groningen partial-wave analysis, but is allowed to vary in this work with Λ ≡ 1/b.
- For the spin-triplet uncoupled ³³D₂ wave, the fitted short-range potential at b = 0.2 fm has V_c = -86.96 fm⁻¹ and W_c = -0.05 fm⁻¹.
- For the spin-triplet uncoupled ³³F₃ wave, the fitted short-range potential at b = 0.2 fm has V_c = -162.73 fm⁻¹ and W_c = -0.00 fm⁻¹.
- The cutoff range examined for most partial waves is between 2 fm⁻¹ and 8 fm⁻¹; for isospin-triplet waves the range extends to higher cutoffs to reveal cutoff dependence.
- The results for the ³³D₂ wave agree with the partial-wave analysis for laboratory momenta p_lab ≲ 230 MeV/c, but show dents in phase shift and inelasticity near p_lab ≈ 250 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization study of one-pion exchange in chiral effective field theory for the antinucleon-nucleon system from lower partial waves (Zhou 2022) to higher partial waves with J ≤ 3, specifically the uncoupled D and F waves and the coupled D-G waves. The analysis follows the methodology of Nogga et al. (2005) for the nucleon-nucleon system and uses the Groningen energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (Zhou et al. 2012) as empirical reference. The results enable future perturbative treatment of one-pion exchange in some antinucleon-nucleon partial waves and inclusion of higher-order interactions such as two-pion exchange.
