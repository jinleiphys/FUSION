# Impact of strong magnetic fields on the inner crust of neutron stars

> We study the impact of strong magnetic fields on the pasta phases that are expected to exist in the inner crust of neutron stars. We employ the relativistic mean field model to describe the nucleon interaction and use the self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approximation to calculate the nonuniform matter in neutron star crust. The properties of pasta phases and crust-core transition are examined. It is found that as the magnetic field strength $B$ is less than $10^{17}$ G, the effects of magnetic field are not evident comparing with the results without magnetic field. As $B$ is stronger than $10^{18}$ G, the onset densities of pasta phases and crust-core transition density decrease significantly, and the density distributions of nucleons and electrons are also changed obviously.

## Key claim

The paper finds that magnetic field effects on the inner crust pasta phases become evident only when the field strength exceeds approximately $10^{17}$ G, with significant changes occurring at $B \geq 10^{18}$ G; the abstract's claim that effects are "not evident" below $10^{17}$ G and "significant" above $10^{18}$ G is qualified by the finding that for the TM1 model, nonspherical pasta phases (rod, tube, bubble) appear only at $B = 10^{18}$ G and are absent at lower fields, while for the IUFSU model all five pasta shapes exist regardless of field strength.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with TM1 and IUFSU parametrizations to describe nucleon interactions, and used the self-consistent Thomas-Fermi (TF) approximation within the Wigner-Seitz (WS) cell approach to calculate nonuniform matter in the inner crust. They considered five pasta configurations (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) and assumed a homogeneous magnetic field along the $z$ direction, including the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. The binding energy per nucleon was minimized with respect to cell size for each configuration at a given average baryon density, and the most stable shape was determined by comparing energies across configurations.

## Key numbers

- For TM1 model with $B = 10^{18}$ G, the onset density for rod phase is $0.0429$ fm$^{-3}$, tube phase $0.0514$ fm$^{-3}$, bubble phase $0.0546$ fm$^{-3}$, and homogeneous matter $0.0594$ fm$^{-3}$; with $B = 0$ only homogeneous matter appears at $0.0618$ fm$^{-3}$.
- For IUFSU model with $B = 10^{18}$ G, the onset densities are: rod $0.0400$ fm$^{-3}$, slab $0.0556$ fm$^{-3}$, tube $0.0738$ fm$^{-3}$, bubble $0.0807$ fm$^{-3}$, and homogeneous matter $0.0850$ fm$^{-3}$; with $B = 0$ these are $0.0476$, $0.0620$, $0.0794$, $0.0851$, and $0.0916$ fm$^{-3}$ respectively.
- For IUFSU model with $B = 10^{18}$ G and neglecting anomalous magnetic moments ($\kappa_{\rm{p,n}} = 0$), the onset densities are: rod $0.0421$ fm$^{-3}$, slab $0.0608$ fm$^{-3}$, tube $0.0762$ fm$^{-3}$, bubble $0.0817$ fm$^{-3}$, and homogeneous matter $0.0859$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The symmetry energy slope $L$ is $110.8$ MeV for TM1 and $47.2$ MeV for IUFSU (Table 2); the saturation density $n_0$ is $0.145$ fm$^{-3}$ for TM1 and $0.155$ fm$^{-3}$ for IUFSU.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pasta phases under strong magnetic fields by Lima et al. (2013), which used NL3 and TM1 parametrizations with fixed proton fraction and neglected anomalous magnetic moments, and by Nandi et al. (2011), which considered only the droplet phase with SkM interaction. The present study uses self-consistent TF approximation with $\beta$ equilibrium and includes anomalous magnetic moments, comparing TM1 and IUFSU models which have different symmetry energy slopes. The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar crust structure and could inform interpretations of quasiperiodic oscillations and glitch phenomena in magnetars.
