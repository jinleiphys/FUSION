# Using spin alignment of inelastically-excited fast beams to make spin assignments: the spectroscopy of 13O as a test case

> Excited states in 13O were investigated using inelastic scattering of an E/A=69.5-MeV 13O beam off of a 9Be target. The excited states were identified in the invariant-mass spectra of the decay products. Both single proton and sequential two-proton decays of the excited states were examined. For a number of the excited states, the protons were emitted with strong anisotropy where emissions transverse to the beam axis are favored. The measured proton-decay angular distributions were compared to predictions from distorted-wave born-approximation (DWBA) calculations of the spin alignment which was shown to be largely independent of the excitation mechanism. The deduced $^{13}$O level scheme is compared to ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) predictions. The lowest-energy excited states decay isotropically consistent with predictions of strong proton 1s1/2 structure. Above these states in the level scheme, we observed a number of higher-spin states not predicted within the model. Possibly these are associated with rotational bands built on deformed cluster configurations predicted by antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations. The spin alignment mechanism is shown to be useful for making spin assignments and may have widespread use.

## Key claim
The spin alignment of inelastically-excited fast beams can be used to make spin assignments for newly observed states, provided the target nucleus remains in its ground state after the reaction; this alignment is largely independent of the excitation mechanism and is determined by general angular-momentum matching conditions in grazing collisions.

## Method
The authors produced a 69.5-MeV/A 13O secondary beam via fragmentation of a 150-MeV/A 16O primary beam on a 9Be target, purified it with a magnetic separator and radio-frequency filter, and directed it onto a 1-mm-thick 9Be target. Excited states in 13O were identified via invariant-mass reconstruction of the p+12N and 2p+11C decay channels using the High Resolution Array (HiRA) of Si-CsI(Tl) telescopes. Proton-decay angular distributions were measured for events where the target remained in its ground state and compared to distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of the spin alignment using the code FRESCO.

## Context
This work extends the spin-alignment technique previously demonstrated for inelastic excitations of 7Li, 7Be, and 17Ne projectiles (Charity 2015, Hoff 2017, Hoff 2018, Charity 2018) to the proton-rich nucleus 13O, providing a test case for the method's general applicability. The experimental level scheme is compared to ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) predictions, which reproduce the lowest-energy states but fail to predict several higher-spin states that may correspond to rotational bands built on deformed cluster configurations from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations. The results enable future spin assignments for newly observed states in exotic nuclei produced via inelastic scattering of fast beams.
