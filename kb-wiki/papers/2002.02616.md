# Magnetic Field in the Charged Subatomic Swirl

> We report a novel relation between rotation and magnetic field in a charged fluid system: there is naturally a magnetic field along the direction of fluid vorticity due to the currents associated with the swirling charges. This general connection is demonstrated using a fluid vortex. Applying the idea to heavy ion collisions we propose a new mechanism for generating in-medium magnetic field with a relatively long lifetime. We estimate the magnitude of this new magnetic field in the Au-Au colliding systems across a wide span of collisional beam energy. Such a magnetic field is found to increase rapidly toward lower beam energy and could account for a significant amount of the experimentally observed global polarization difference between hyperons and anti-hyperons.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a magnetic field naturally arises along the direction of fluid vorticity in a charged fluid system, and that this mechanism can generate a long-lived in-medium magnetic field in heavy ion collisions. The authors hedge that this field is smaller in peak magnitude than the initial spectator-generated magnetic field but has a much longer lifetime, and that it could account for a significant amount, but not all, of the observed global polarization difference between hyperons and anti-hyperons.

## Method
The authors derive a relation between average magnetic field and average vorticity for a charged fluid vortex by solving Maxwell's equations, obtaining \( e\bar{\mathbf{B}} = (e^2/4\pi) \, n \, (\pi R_0^2) \, \bar{\boldsymbol{\omega}} \). They then extract average vorticity and charge density from AMPT simulations for (20-50)% centrality Au-Au collisions at RHIC across beam energies from 10 to 200 GeV, using early (τ=0.5 fm) and late (τ=5 fm) time estimates. The magnetic field is computed from these inputs and used to estimate the induced polarization splitting between Λ and anti-Λ hyperons via their opposite magnetic moments.

## Key numbers
- Average fluid vorticity at early time (τ=0.5 fm) and late time (τ=5 fm) is shown as a function of beam energy, with values increasing rapidly toward lower beam energy (Fig. 1 left panel).
- Charge density at early time (τ=0.5 fm) and late time (τ=5 fm) is shown as a function of beam energy, also increasing toward lower beam energy (Fig. 1 middle panel).
- The generated magnetic field \( e\bar{B} \) reaches an order of magnitude of approximately \( 0.01 \, m_\pi^2 \) (Fig. 1 right panel).
- The transverse area estimate uses \( R_0 \sim 4 \, \text{fm} \), giving \( A \sim \pi (4 \, \text{fm})^2 \).
- The freeze-out temperature used is \( T_{fo} = 155 \, \text{MeV} \).
- The Λ magnetic moment magnitude is \( |\mu_\Lambda| = 0.613 \, \mu_N = 0.613 \, e / (2 M_N) \) with \( M_N = 938 \, \text{MeV} \).
- The induced polarization difference \( \Delta P = P_{\bar{\Lambda}} - P_\Lambda \) is compared with STAR data (Fig. 2), showing consistency in magnitude and beam-energy dependence.

## Context
This work extends the study of magnetic fields in heavy ion collisions beyond the initial vacuum field from spectators, which is short-lived (Bloczynski 2012, McLerran 2013, Tuchin 2015, Inghirami 2016, Gursoy 2018). It also addresses the puzzle of the polarization difference between Λ and anti-Λ hyperons observed by STAR (2017), which previous attempts (Becattini 2016, Csernai 2018, Muller 2018) could not resolve. The proposed mechanism enables future quantitative computations using state-of-the-art modeling (Jiang 2016, Shi 2017, Guo 2017) and provides a new connection between rotation and magnetic fields relevant to the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Vortical Effect.
