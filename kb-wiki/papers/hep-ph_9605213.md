# Jet Quenching in the Opposite Direction of a Tagged Photon in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We point out that events associated with large $E_T$ direct photons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be used to study jet energy loss in dense matter. In such events, the $p_T$ spectrum of charged hadrons from jet fragmentation in the opposite direction of the tagged photon is estimated to be well above the background which can be reliably subtracted at moderately large $p_T$. We demonstrate that comparison between the extracted fragmentation function in $AA$ and $pp$ collisions can be used to determine the jet energy loss and the interaction mean-free-path in the dense matter produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that measuring the charged-hadron pT distribution opposite a tagged direct photon in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can extract the jet fragmentation function and thereby determine both the jet energy loss and the interaction mean-free-path in dense matter, provided the background is reliably subtracted at moderately large pT.

## Method

The authors use perturbative QCD to calculate the pT spectrum of charged hadrons from jet fragmentation in the opposite direction of a tagged direct photon, with the photon restricted to central rapidity (|y| ≤ 0.5) and hadrons collected in a kinematic window (Δy=1, Δφ=2). They compare these calculations with HIJING Monte Carlo simulations for Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, and model jet quenching by assuming a Poisson-distributed number of inelastic scatterings with average energy loss per scattering, modifying the vacuum fragmentation functions from Binnewies, Kniehl and Kramer (1994) accordingly.

## Key numbers

- The acceptance factor C(Δy,Δφ) ≈ 0.5 at √s=200 GeV, independent of photon energy ETγ, from HIJING simulations with kinematic cuts Δy=1, Δφ=2.
- The background in pp collisions is about 1200 times smaller than in central Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV.
- For the modified fragmentation functions, the authors use dEq/dx = 1 GeV/fm and λq = 1 fm as example parameters, with gluon mean-free-path half that of a quark and gluon energy loss twice that of a quark.
- The suppression factor at large z > 0.9 is given by ⟨exp(-ΔL/λa)⟩, independent of jet energy ET and energy loss dEa/dx.
- At intermediate z = 0.2–0.5, the suppression factor scales with dEa/dx and depends weakly on the mean-free-path in the "soft emission" scenario where εa ≪ ET.

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals to use jet acoplanarity and energy imbalance (Appel, 1986; Blaizot and McLerran, 1986) and single-particle inclusive pT spectra (Wang and Gyulassy, 1992) as probes of dense matter, by introducing a photon-tagging method that avoids uncertainties from the jet production cross section. The approach enables direct measurement of the modified fragmentation function, which can be compared between pp, pA, peripheral AA, and central AA collisions to extract the energy loss and mean-free-path, with the authors noting that the energy loss may depend on path length as indicated by Baier, Dokshitser, Peigne and Schiff (1995).
