# Microscopic mass formulae

> By assuming the existence of a pseudopotential smooth enough to do Hartree-Fock variations and good enough to describe nuclear structure, we construct mass formulae that rely on general scaling arguments and on a schematic reading of shell model calculations. Fits to 1751 known binding energies for N,Z$\geq 8$ lead to rms errors of 375 keV with 28 parameters. Tests of the extrapolation properties are passed successfully. The Bethe-Weizsäcker formula is shown to be the asymptotic limit of the present one(s). The surface energy of nuclear matter turns out to be probably smaller than currently accepted.

# Microscopic mass formulae

## Key claim

By constructing a mass formula from a schematic reading of shell model calculations and general scaling arguments, the authors achieve an rms error of 375 keV for 1751 known binding energies with 28 parameters, but the fit is described as exploratory and the treatment of the monopole Hamiltonian is acknowledged to be too crude because it forces an artificial degeneracy on the r-orbits.

## Method

The authors separate the nuclear Hamiltonian into a monopole part H_m, which contains quadratic forms in number and isospin operators for each orbit and is treated phenomenologically, and a multipole part H_M which is mocked by algebraic expressions for configuration mixing. The monopole terms are constructed as symmetric quadratic forms of properly scaled operators derived from geometric properties of realistic forces, with the dominant master term producing harmonic oscillator closures and spin-orbit terms producing extruder-intruder closures. Spherical nuclei are described by H_s operators that smooth subshell effects, while deformed nuclei are described by H_d operators that simulate the promotion of JU protons and neutrons to configurations of the next harmonic oscillator shell, with the spherical-deformed boundary determined by taking the maximum of the two energies.

## Key numbers

- RMS error of 375 keV for the 28-parameter fit (28p) to 1751 binding energies for N, Z ≥ 8
- RMS error of 485 keV for the 28p* fit where the FM+ master term is replaced by A^{4/3}
- RMS error of 673 keV for the droplet model (FRDM) with 30 parameters for N, Z ≥ 8
- RMS error of 730 keV for the Thomas-Fermi model (EFTSI) with 9 parameters for A ≥ 36
- RMS error of 254 keV for the 396 deformed nuclei in the 28p fit
- RMS error of 313 keV for the 393 deformed nuclei in the 28p* fit
- When fitted to 1503 nuclei from the 1983 compilation, the 28p fit yields an rms error of 357 keV; when those same parameters are applied to the 248 new nuclei in the 1993 tables, the rms error is 470 keV
- The 6-parameter liquid drop fit yields an rms error of 2499 keV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Duflo (1994), which achieved 385 keV rms error with 12 parameters for N ≥ 28, Z ≥ 20 but did not extrapolate well and required a-priori knowledge of closures and spherical-deformed boundaries. The present formula improves upon the droplet model (FRDM) of Moller, Nix, Myers and Swiatecki (1993) and the Thomas-Fermi model (EFTSI) of Aboussir, Pearson, Dutta and Tondeur (1992) by achieving significantly lower rms errors while maintaining good extrapolation properties. The Bethe-Weizsacker formula is shown to be the asymptotic limit of the present mass formulae, and the surface energy of nuclear matter is suggested to be smaller than the currently accepted value. The authors note that the fits are exploratory and that a more refined treatment of the spin-orbit operators could yield better results.
