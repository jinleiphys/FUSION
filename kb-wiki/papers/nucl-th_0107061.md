# Unified description of three positive and three negative parity interacting bands

> The coherent state model (CSM) is extended so that three negative parity bands are treated on equal footing with three positive parity bands. The six rotational bands are generated by projecting out angular momenta and parities from three intrisic orthogonal states which exhibit both quadrupole and octupole deformations. The projected states are, by construction, mutually orthogonal. In the space of projected states, a sub-space of a quadrupole and octupole multi-boson states, an effective quadrupole and octupole boson Hamiltonian is solved. The eigenstates of the model Hamiltonian are linked by multipole transition operators for which lowest order boson expressions are considered. The calculations involve 6 structure coefficients and two deformation parameters. All of them are fixed by a least square fit of the known experimental energies. Applications are made to Gd158, Yb172, Ra218, Ra226, Th232, U238, Pu238. Very good agreement is obtained for both excitation energies and transition probabilities. New signatures for octupole deformation, manifested in excited bands, are pointed out.

## Key claim
The coherent state model (CSM) is extended to describe three positive and three negative parity bands simultaneously, using intrinsic states with both quadrupole and octupole deformations. The model claims very good agreement for both excitation energies and transition probabilities across seven nuclei, but the authors note that for some nuclei (e.g., \(^{172}\)Yb) the negative parity bands are highly excited and interpreted as vibrational rather than static octupole deformation, and that for \(^{238}\)U and \(^{238}\)Pu the \(\beta^-\) band is predicted lower than the \(\beta^+\) band, which awaits experimental confirmation.

## Method
The authors generalize the coherent state model (CSM) by adding an octupole boson coherent state to the intrinsic ground, beta, and gamma band generating functions. Angular momentum and parity are projected from three orthogonal intrinsic states, yielding six bands (three positive, three negative). An effective boson Hamiltonian with six structure coefficients and two deformation parameters (quadrupole \(d\) and octupole \(f\)) is solved in the restricted projected space, with parameters fixed by a least-squares fit to experimental energies. Electric multipole transition operators (E1, E2, E3) are used in their lowest-order boson expressions to compute transition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{226}\)Ra, the octupole deformation parameter is \(f = 0.65\); for \(^{232}\)Th, \(f = 0.30\).
- For \(^{226}\)Ra, the ground bands show an excellent octupole deformed structure in the angular momentum interval \(11^- - 27^-\).
- For \(^{232}\)Th, 55 experimental excitation energies were described by the model with high accuracy.
- The state \(2^+\) in the ground band of \(^{238}\)U is identified at 0.352 MeV, close to the model prediction.
- For \(^{172}\)Yb, the coefficient \({\cal B}_3\) is 8711.1 keV, reflecting a large energy shift for negative parity bands.
- For \(^{226}\)Ra, the ground band \(2^+\) energy is 0.068 MeV (experimental) vs. 0.060 MeV (predicted); for \(^{232}\)Th, \(2^+\) is 0.049 MeV (experimental) vs. 0.049 MeV (predicted).

## Context
This work extends the coherent state model (CSM) previously developed by Raduta and Faessler (Rad8, Rad9, Rad10) for ground and \(K^\pi = 0^-\) bands to include beta and gamma bands of both parities. It builds on earlier suggestions of octupole deformation by Chassman (Cha1, Cha2) and Moller and Nix (Mol), and on the phenomenological linkage of bands by Sheline (She) and Sakai (Saka). The model enables a unified description of octupole-deformed nuclei (e.g., \(^{218}\)Ra, \(^{226}\)Ra) and octupole-vibrational nuclei (e.g., \(^{158}\)Gd, \(^{172}\)Yb, \(^{232}\)Th, \(^{238}\)U, \(^{238}\)Pu), and points to new signatures of octupole deformation in excited bands, such as parity doublet structures in beta and gamma bands.
