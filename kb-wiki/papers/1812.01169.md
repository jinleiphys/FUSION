# Shape evolution of Zr nuclei and roles of tensor force

> Shape evolution of Zr nuclei are investigated by the axial Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the semi-realistic interaction M3Y-P6, with focusing on roles of the tensor force. Deformation at $N\approx 40$ is reproduced, which has not been easy to describe within the self-consistent mean-field calculations. The spherical shape is obtained in $46\leq N\leq 56$, and the prolate deformation is predicted in $58\leq N\leq 72$, while the shape switches to oblate at $N=74$. The sphericity returns at $N=80$ and $82$. The deformation in $60\lesssim N\lesssim 70$ resolves the discrepancy in the previous magic-number prediction based on the spherical mean-field calculations [Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys. \textbf{2014}, 033D02]. It is found that the deformation at $N\approx 40$ takes place owing to the tensor force with a good balance. The tensor-force effects significantly depend on the configurations, and are pointed out to be conspicuous when the unique-parity orbit (e.g. $n0h_{11/2}$) is present near the Fermi energy, delaying deformation. These effects are crucial for the magicity at $N=56$ and for the predicted shape change at $N=74$ and $80$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the semi-realistic interaction M3Y-P6, which includes a realistic tensor force, can reproduce deformation at N≈40 in Zr nuclei within axial Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations, a result that has not been easy to achieve with self-consistent mean-field calculations that lack the tensor force. The tensor force effects are configuration-dependent and are particularly conspicuous when the unique-parity orbit (e.g., n0h11/2) is near the Fermi energy, where it delays deformation.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent axial Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations for even-N Zr isotopes from 80Zr to 122Zr using the semi-realistic interaction M3Y-P6, which contains a realistic tensor force derived from the G-matrix. Constrained HF (CHF) calculations were carried out to obtain energy curves E(q0) as functions of the intrinsic mass quadrupole moment q0. For comparison, axial HF calculations were also performed using the Gogny-D1M interaction, which does not include a tensor force, and the tensor force contribution was evaluated by subtracting E(TN)(q0) from the total energy.

## Key numbers

- Deformation at N=40 and N=42: M3Y-P6 predicts prolate shape, while D1M gives spherical shape at N=40 at the HF level.
- Spherical shape obtained for 46 ≤ N ≤ 56; prolate deformation predicted for 58 ≤ N ≤ 72; shape switches to oblate at N=74; sphericity returns at N=80 and 82.
- For 80Zr, the lowest energy is given by a prolate minimum with q0 ≈ 800 fm², corresponding to β ≈ 0.5.
- For 100Zr, the lowest energy is obtained by a well-deformed prolate state with q0 ≈ 1100 fm².
- Energy difference between prolate and oblate minima in 98Zr is 0.5 MeV, compared to 1.5 MeV in 100Zr.
- For 96Zr, the spherical HFB energy difference between HF and HFB at q0=0 is tiny but visible, and the spherical minimum is distinctly lower than deformed local minima.

## Context

This work extends the spherical mean-field calculations of Nakada (2014) that predicted magic numbers using M3Y-P6 but could not reproduce the Z=40 magicity in neutron-rich Zr nuclei. The present axial HF calculations resolve the discrepancy in the previous magic-number prediction for 60 ≲ N ≲ 70 by explicitly including quadrupole deformation. The results enable systematic comparison with other self-consistent mean-field approaches, including those using Gogny-D1M, D1S, SkM*, SLy4, NL3*, and DD-PC1 interactions, and provide a basis for future beyond-mean-field calculations that include pairing and rotational correlations.
