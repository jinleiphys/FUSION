# $η$ photoproduction on the quasi-free nucleons in the chiral quark model

> A chiral quark-model approach is adopted to study the $η$ photoproduction off the quasi-free neutron and proton from a deuteron target. Good descriptions of the differential cross sections, total cross sections and beam asymmetries for these two processes are obtained in the low energy region. For $γp\rightarrow ηp$, the dominant resonances are $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{13}(1700)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$. While for the $γn\rightarrow ηn$ process, the dominant resonances are $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{15}(1675)$ and $P_{13}(1720)$. Furthermore, the $u$ channel backgrounds have significant contributions to the $η$ photoproduction processes. The configuration mixings in the $S_{11}(1535,1650)$ and $D_{13}(1520,1700)$ can be extracted, i.e. $θ_S\simeq 26^\circ$ and $θ_D\simeq 21^\circ$. It shows that the narrow bump-like structure around $W= 1.68$ GeV observed in $γn\rightarrow ηn$ can be naturally explained by the constructive interferences between $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$. In contrast, the destructive interference between $S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$ produces the shallow dip around $W= 1.67$ GeV in $γp\rightarrow ηp$. The $S$ wave interfering behaviors in the proton and neutron reactions are correlated with each other in the quark model framework, and no new exotic nucleon resonances are needed in these two reactions.

## Key claim

The narrow bump-like structure around W = 1.68 GeV observed in γn → ηn can be naturally explained by constructive interferences between S11(1535) and S11(1650) within the chiral quark model, without requiring any new exotic nucleon resonances; the corresponding shallow dip around W = 1.67 GeV in γp → ηp arises from destructive interference between the same two resonances.

## Method

The authors adopt a chiral quark-model approach with an effective Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling and electromagnetic interactions at tree level, calculating s-channel and u-channel transition amplitudes in the harmonic oscillator basis. For quasi-free nucleons from a deuteron target, the Fermi motion of the bound nucleon is folded into the cross sections using the Paris or CD-Bonn deuteron wave functions. Configuration mixings between SU(6)⊗O(3) representations [70,²8] and [70,⁴8] are accommodated by introducing coupling strength parameters CR determined by fitting experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle θ_S ≃ 26° for S11(1535) and S11(1650), extracted from the ratio of coupling coefficients C^{[70,²8]}_{S11(1535)} ≃ 1.10 and C^{[70,²8]}_{S11(1650)} ≃ -0.22 in γp → ηp
- Mixing angle θ_D ≃ 21° for D13(1520) and D13(1700), extracted from C_{D13(1520)} ≃ 1.80 and C_{D13(1700)} ≃ 0.50 in γp → ηp
- ηNN coupling g_{ηNN} ≃ 2.13 (α_η ≡ g²_{ηNN}/4π = 0.36)
- Constituent quark mass m_q = 330 MeV and harmonic oscillator strength α² = 0.16 GeV²
- Favorable widths: S11(1535) Γ = 119 MeV, S11(1650) Γ = 105 MeV (both slightly below PDG lower limits)
- χ²/N = 2.9 for γp → ηp (560 data points, 0.748 GeV ≤ E_γ ≤ 1.3 GeV); χ²/N = 1.6 for γn → ηn (quasi-free)
- Bump structure in γn → ηn at W = 1.68 GeV; dip structure in γp → ηp at W = 1.67 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral quark model studies of γp → ηp by Li (1995, 1997, 1998), Saghai (2001), and He (2008, 2009), and addresses the narrow structure around W = 1.68 GeV observed in γn → ηn by Kuznetsov (2004), the GRAAL Collaboration (2008), and the CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration (2008). The results contradict interpretations requiring a pentaquark state as proposed by Azimov (2005), Fix (2007), and Choi (2005), and instead support the interference explanation suggested by Anisovich (2008) and Shyam (2008). The extracted mixing angles enable future tests of SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking and provide helicity amplitudes for S11(1535), S11(1650), D13(1520), D13(1700), and D15(1675) that can be compared with partial-wave analyses.
