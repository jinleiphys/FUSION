# Thermal Equilibration and Expansion in Nucleus-Nucleus Collision at the AGS

> The rather complete data set of hadron yields from central Si + A collisions at the Brookhaven AGS is used to test whether the system at freeze-out is in thermal and hadro-chemical equilibrium. Rapidity and transverse momentum distributions are discussed with regards to the information they provide on hydrodynamic flow.

# Thermal Equilibration and Expansion in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at the AGS

## Key claim

The system formed in central Si + A collisions at the AGS reaches thermal and hadro-chemical equilibrium at freeze-out, but only when a collective longitudinal and transverse flow velocity is superimposed on the isotropic thermal source; the isotropic thermal model alone fails to reproduce the measured rapidity distributions.

## Method

The authors used a grand canonical ensemble of non-interacting fermions and bosons at freeze-out temperature T and baryon chemical potential μb = 0.54 GeV, with strangeness chemical potential fixed by strangeness conservation, to compute primary particle densities including finite-size and excluded-volume corrections. They compared calculated particle ratios (after accounting for resonance decays and feeding) to experimental data from central 14.6 A GeV/c Si + Au(Pb) collisions, and then modeled rapidity and transverse momentum distributions by superimposing isotropic thermal sources with a common collective flow velocity in longitudinal and transverse directions.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature range: T = 0.12 – 0.14 GeV, with baryon chemical potential μb = 0.54 GeV
- Mean longitudinal flow velocity: ⟨β_l⟩ = 0.52, corresponding to y'_max = 1.15 and ⟨y'⟩ = 0.58
- Mean transverse flow velocity: ⟨β_t⟩ = 0.39 at T = 0.12 GeV, and ⟨β_t⟩ = 0.33 at T = 0.14 GeV
- Calculated nucleon density at freeze-out: ρn = 0.070 /fm³ at T = 0.12 GeV and 0.13 /fm³ at T = 0.14 GeV; experimental estimate from E814 HBT: ρn_exp = 0.058 /fm³
- Calculated pion density at freeze-out: ρπ = 0.09 /fm³ at T = 0.12 GeV and 0.17 /fm³ at T = 0.14 GeV; experimental estimate: ρπ_exp = 0.063 /fm³
- Transverse freeze-out radius: R = 6.7 fm (from E814 HBT data for Si + Pb)
- All particle ratios reproduced to better than a factor of two, with yields varying over three orders of magnitude
- For the Si + Al system, the isotropic model fails; longitudinal flow with ⟨β_l⟩ = 0.52 gives good agreement with rapidity distributions
- Normalization factors from the thermal model for Si + Al would require upward shifts of 20% (protons), 37% (π⁺), and 60% (K⁺) relative to data

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Stachel and Braun-Munzinger (1989) that thermalization and hydrodynamic expansion occur in AGS and SPS collisions, using the much larger data set now available from central Si + A collisions. It builds on thermodynamic calculations by Cleymans et al. (1990, 1991, 1993) and Letessier, Rafelski, and Tounsi (1994), but differs by requiring chemical equilibrium throughout combined with strangeness conservation, and by using the Δ(1232) abundance to constrain the temperature range. The results enable future determination of the equation of state during the high-density phase by probing earlier times with different observables, and are independently supported by flow analyses of azimuthal distributions in Au + Au collisions at AGS energies (E877 Collaboration, 1994).
