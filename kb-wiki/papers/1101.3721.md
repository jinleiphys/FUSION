# Still Few-Nucleon Systems - After So Many Years

> Three- and four-nucleon reactions are discussed. The focus is on the notion of nuclear potentials, on conceptual and technical issues of calculations and on unresolved problems between existing theoretical predictions and experimental data. The special focus is on some historic aspects of the evolution of the field. The views presented have a strong personal bias.

## Key claim

The paper argues that after decades of research, few-nucleon systems still present unresolved discrepancies between theory and experiment, and that the choice of nuclear dynamics (potentials) is inherently model-dependent, with no single approach yet providing a complete and consistent description across all observables.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of three- and four-nucleon bound states and scattering reactions using a coupled-channel potential (CD Bonn + Δ) that includes explicit Δ-isobar degrees of freedom, generating consistent effective two-, three-, and four-nucleon forces from meson exchanges. The Coulomb interaction between protons is treated via a screening and renormalization technique within momentum-space integral equations (AGS formalism), allowing standard short-range scattering theory to be applied. Results are compared with experimental data for elastic pd scattering, pd break-up, elastic p³He scattering, dd → p³H and dd → n³He reactions, and total elastic n-³H cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The 4N force contributes only about 1% to the total binding energy of ⁴He, while the 3N force contributes about 10% (based on the CD Bonn + Δ potential).
- Elastic pd scattering data are shown at proton energy E_p = 5.00 MeV (top panels) and at E_p = 10.0 MeV and E_p = 13.0 MeV for break-up in space-star kinematics.
- Elastic p³He scattering is shown at E_p = 5.54 MeV.
- The reactions dd → p³H and dd → n³He are shown at deuteron energy E_d = 3.00 MeV.
- Elastic pd scattering at higher energies is shown at 135 MeV proton energy (inconsistent data sets noted).
- Break-up in pd scattering is shown at E_d = 130 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to test nuclear dynamics in few-nucleon systems, following the tradition of Brueckner (1972) and the Bethe-Faddeev equations. It directly builds on the coupled-channel approach with Δ-isobar excitation developed by Sauer (1986) and Deltuva (2003), and addresses the Coulomb problem previously tackled by Hackenbroich (1970) using the Kohn variational principle and by the Pisa group (Kievsky, 2001) in configuration space. The paper identifies persistent discrepancies (the A_y puzzle, the space-star anomaly, the total elastic n-³H cross section) that remain unresolved, and notes that EFT-based potentials (Entem, 2003; Navratil, 2007) offer new hope for settling some of these issues.
