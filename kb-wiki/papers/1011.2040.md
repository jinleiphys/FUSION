# Hamiltonian formulation of the effective kinetic theory for superfluid Fermi liquids

> We present in a local form the time dependent effective description of a superfluid Fermi liquid which includes Landau damping effects at $T\neq 0$. This is achieved by the introduction of an additional variable, the quasiparticle distribution function, which obeys a simple kinetic equation. The transport equation is coupled with first order equations for the Goldstone mode and the particle density. We prove that a main feature of this formulation is its Hamiltonian structure relative to a certain Poisson bracket. We construct the Hamiltonian to quadratic order.

## Key claim

The paper presents a local, Hamiltonian formulation of the effective low-energy dynamics of a superfluid Fermi liquid at finite temperature, including Landau damping effects, but only within the linear response approximation and neglecting Fermi liquid effects (i.e., quasiparticle interactions beyond the mean-field level).

## Method

The author starts from the BCS action for a balanced two-component Fermi gas and introduces a Hubbard-Stratonovich field for the pairing interaction. After separating the Goldstone mode from the heavy fields, the fermions are integrated out in the one-loop approximation, yielding nonlocal response functions due to Landau damping. To obtain local equations, a quasiparticle distribution function \(w(X,\bm{k})\) is introduced, which obeys a linearized Vlasov-type kinetic equation without collision term. This transport equation is coupled to first-order equations for the Goldstone mode \(\theta\) and the particle density \(n_1\), and the complete set is shown to be Hamiltonian with respect to a Poisson bracket that combines a frozen Lie-Poisson bracket for the distribution function and a canonical bracket for density and phase.

## Key numbers

* The regular part of the current-current response function is \(\bar{\chi}_{JJ}^{kl} = \frac{\langle n\rangle}{m} \hat{q}^k \hat{q}^l\), where \(\langle n\rangle\) is the equilibrium particle density.
* The regular density-density response is \(\bar{\chi}_{nn} = -\int \frac{d^3 k}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{\Delta_0^2}{E_k^3} \tanh \frac{\beta E_k}{2}\).
* The regular mixed response is \(\bar{\chi}_{n1} = -\int \frac{d^3 k}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{\Delta_0 \xi_k}{E_k^3} \tanh \frac{\beta E_k}{2}\).
* The factor \(P\) in the Poisson bracket is fixed to \(P = (2\pi)^3/2\) to match the kinetic equation.
* In the normal phase limit \(\Delta_0 \to 0\), the Hamiltonian reduces to that of a non-interacting Fermi gas in an external potential, with the constraint \(n_1 = 2 \int_k \mathrm{sign}(\xi_k) w\).

## Context

This work extends the analogy between the effective theory of superfluid Fermi liquids and the hard-thermal-loop effective theory of high-temperature QCD, where local formulations using auxiliary fields were developed by Blaizot and Iancu (1990s) and Nair (1990s). The kinetic equation derived here was obtained earlier by Betbeder-Matibet and Nozieres (1969) and later by Urban and Schuck (2000s) from a matrix distribution function approach. The Hamiltonian structure enables numerical simulation of real-time non-equilibrium processes, such as oscillations of trapped Fermi gases, which was previously difficult due to the nonlocal nature of the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau theory. The paper explicitly neglects Fermi liquid effects (quasiparticle interactions parametrized by Landau parameters \(f_{\bm{k}\bm{k}'}\)), which are left for future extension.
