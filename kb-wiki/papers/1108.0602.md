# Kubo formulas for relativistic fluids in strong magnetic fields

> Magnetohydrodynamics of strongly magnetized relativistic fluids is derived in the ideal and dissipative cases, taking into account the breaking of spatial symmetries by a quantizing magnetic field. A complete set of transport coefficients, consistent with the Curie and Onsager principles, is derived for thermal conduction, as well as shear and bulk viscosities. It is shown that in the most general case the dissipative function contains five shear viscosities, two bulk viscosities, and three thermal conductivity coefficients. We use Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method to relate these transport coefficients to correlation functions of equilibrium theory. The desired relations emerge at linear order in the expansion of the non-equilibrium statistical operator with respect to the gradients of relevant statistical parameters (temperature, chemical potential, and velocity.) The transport coefficients are cast in a form that can be conveniently computed using equilibrium (imaginary-time) infrared Green's functions defined with respect to the equilibrium statistical operator.

## Key claim

The paper derives Kubo formulas for the transport coefficients of relativistic fluids in strong magnetic fields using Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method, under the condition that the system is in the hydrodynamic regime where thermodynamic parameters can be defined locally and the thermodynamic forces are sufficiently small for linear response theory to apply.

## Method

The authors first develop the magnetohydrodynamic theory for relativistic fluids in strong magnetic fields, identifying the complete set of transport coefficients consistent with Curie and Onsager principles: five shear viscosities, two bulk viscosities, and three thermal conductivity coefficients. They then apply Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator method, expanding the non-equilibrium statistical operator to linear order in gradients of temperature, chemical potential, and velocity, to relate these transport coefficients to equilibrium correlation functions expressed as imaginary-time Green's functions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths in neutron stars: 10^12-10^13 G on average, with magnetars having fields of order 10^15-10^16 G
- Upper limit on magnetic field magnitude from virial theorem: B ≤ 10^18 G
- The dissipative function contains five shear viscosities, two bulk viscosities, and three thermal conductivity coefficients
- The viscosity tensor w^{μναβ} is decomposed into seven independent coefficients: w_0, w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4, ζ_perp, and ζ_para
- The thermal conductivity tensor κ^{μν} is decomposed into three independent coefficients: κ_perp, κ_parallel, and κ_cross

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier anisotropic hydrodynamic theory for strongly magnetized strange matter (Huang, 2009) by providing Kubo formulas that relate transport coefficients to equilibrium correlation functions, enabling computation using equilibrium statistical mechanics methods. The approach is the relativistic counterpart of Braginskii's non-relativistic plasma theory in strong magnetic fields, and differs from other relativistic magnetohydrodynamic treatments (Gedalin, 1991) by relating transport coefficients to retarded correlation functions of the microscopic theory. The results enable future computation of transport coefficients for dense matter in neutron stars and magnetars using equilibrium Green's functions.
