# Possibility of time reversal symmetry violation at proton deuteron forward elastic scattering

> We consider T-violating P-conserving proton-deuteron forward elastic scattering amplitude in a frame of the Glauber multiscattering theory, proceeding from T-odd, P-even N-N interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a T-violating, P-conserving term in the proton-deuteron forward elastic scattering amplitude can arise from double scattering of the proton by deuteron nucleons, even though the nucleon-nucleon forward amplitude itself contains no such term. The effect is generated by combining a T-odd, P-even N-N interaction at a small angle with a T-even N-N interaction at the opposite angle, and the calculation includes the deuteron's non-sphericity and possible T-odd impurities in the deuteron density matrix.

## Method

The author uses the Glauber multiscattering theory generalized to particles with spin, treating the proton-deuteron scattering as a sequence of eikonal collisions with the two nucleons in the deuteron. The T-odd, P-even N-N amplitudes are derived from one-meson exchange (ρ and A₁ mesons) using standard diagram techniques, and the deuteron is described by a spin-density matrix that includes both T-even and T-odd terms. The forward p-d amplitude is then computed by integrating over the deuteron form factors and the N-N profile functions, with the T-odd constants taken from existing limits.

## Key numbers

- The T-odd coupling constant for ρ-meson exchange is bounded as |ḡ_ρ| < 6.7 × 10⁻³.
- The T-odd coupling constant for A₁-meson exchange is bounded as |f_A| < 3 × 10⁻⁵.
- For the A₁-meson exchange mechanism, the required experimental accuracy to detect a T-odd amplitude is 10⁻⁹–10⁻¹⁰ fm² for the amplitude or 10⁻⁷–10⁻⁸ mb for the cross section, assuming f_A = 10⁻⁵.
- For the ρ-meson exchange mechanism, the required accuracy is 10⁻⁶–10⁻⁷ fm² for the amplitude or 10⁻⁴–10⁻⁵ mb for the cross section, assuming ḡ_ρ = 10⁻³.
- For a T-odd impurity in the deuteron density at the level T₂ = 10⁻⁴ A₉, the required accuracy is 10⁻⁶–10⁻⁷ fm² for the amplitude and 10⁻⁴–10⁻⁵ mb for the cross section.
- The planned TRV collaboration experiment at COSY has an accuracy of 10⁻⁶ mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Beyer (1993) by including the deuteron's non-sphericity and T-odd impurities in the deuteron density matrix. It provides a theoretical framework for the planned TRV collaboration experiment at COSY (Hinterberger, 1998), which aims to measure T-odd, P-even effects in proton-deuteron scattering. The results suggest that deuteron non-sphericity makes the experimental search more optimistic, and that the COSY experiment's accuracy of 10⁻⁶ mb will allow new constraints on the T-odd coupling constants f_A and ḡ_ρ.
