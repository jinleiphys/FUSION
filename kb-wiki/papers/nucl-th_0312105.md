# Non-extensive resonant reaction rates in astrophysical plasmas

> We study two different physical scenarios of thermonuclear reactions in stellar plasmas proceeding through a narrow resonance at low energy or through the low energy wing of a wide resonance at high energy. Correspondingly, we derive two approximate analytical formulae in order to calculate thermonuclear resonant reaction rates inside very coupled and non ideal astrophysical plasmas in which non-extensive effects are likely to arise. Our results are presented as simple first order corrective factors that generalize the well known classical rates obtained in the framework of Maxwell-Boltzmann statistical mechanics. As a possible application of our results, we calculate the dependence of the total corrective factor with respect to the energy at which the resonance is located, in an extremely dense and non ideal carbon plasma.

## Key claim

Non-extensive statistical mechanics corrections to thermonuclear resonant reaction rates can be expressed as simple first-order multiplicative factors to the classical Maxwell-Boltzmann rates, provided the deformation parameter \(|\delta|\) is very small (typically \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-2}\) in astrophysical plasmas). The sign of \(\delta\) determines whether the high-energy tail of the energy distribution is enhanced (super-extensivity, \(\delta<0\)) or depleted (sub-extensivity, \(\delta>0\)), which in turn increases or decreases the reaction rate relative to the classical value.

## Method

The authors derived two approximate analytical formulae for thermonuclear resonant reaction rates in non-ideal, strongly coupled astrophysical plasmas, starting from a first-order non-extensive energy distribution function \(\phi_{\mathrm{NE}}(E)\) that deviates from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution by a factor \(\exp[-\delta(E/k_B T)^2]\). For narrow resonances at low energy (conditions: \(E_R \approx E_0\), \(\Gamma_T \ll E_R\)), they integrated the cross section over the resonance region to obtain a corrective factor \(1 + (15/4)\delta - (E_R/k_B T)^2\delta\). For wide resonances (conditions: \(E_R > E_0\) or \(E_R \gg E_0\), \(\Gamma_T > E_0\)), they expanded the deformed Gamow energy \(\tilde{E}_0\) and the astrophysical S-factor ratio to first order in \(\delta\), yielding a more complex corrective factor involving \(E_0\), \(E_R\), \(\Gamma_T\), and \(k_B T\). As an application, they calculated the total corrective factor \(F = f_{\mathrm{NE}} \cdot f_{\mathrm{S}} \cdot f_{\mathrm{RS}}\) for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}\) reaction in a carbon white dwarf plasma, using the screening factors \(f_{\mathrm{S}}\) and \(f_{\mathrm{RS}}\) from Itoh et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter \(|\delta| = 10^{-3}\) used in the application to a carbon white dwarf plasma.
- White dwarf plasma conditions: temperature \(T = 8 \times 10^8\,\mathrm{K}\), mass density \(\rho = 2 \times 10^9\,\mathrm{g\cdot cm^{-3}}\), plasma parameter \(\Gamma \simeq 5.6\).
- For a narrow resonance at \(E_R = 2.4\,\mathrm{MeV}\) in the carbon plasma, the total effective factor \(F\) is approximately 136 for sub-extensivity (\(\delta > 0\)) and approximately 1484 for super-extensivity (\(\delta < 0\)); the classical (\(\delta = 0\)) result is shown in the figure but its numerical value is not given in the text.
- In the Sun's core, the thermal energy is \(k_B T \simeq 1.36\,\mathrm{keV}\); the Gamow energy \(E_0\) for CNO cycle reactions lies in the interval \(24\,\mathrm{keV} < E_0 < 29\,\mathrm{keV}\), and for the \(p+p\) reaction \(E_0 \approx 6\,\mathrm{keV}\).
- The condition for the narrow-resonance coefficient \(C_1\) to be negative is \(E_R/k_B T > \sqrt{15/4} \simeq 1.936\), which is satisfied in the Sun's core and many other stellar plasmas.
- The \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(p,\gamma)^{13}\mathrm{N}\) reaction has a wide resonance at \(E_R = 424\,\mathrm{keV}\) with total width \(\Gamma_T \simeq 40\,\mathrm{keV}\) and electromagnetic channel width \(\Gamma_\gamma \simeq 0.77\,\mathrm{eV}\); its Gamow energy in the Sun is \(E_0 = 24.68\,\mathrm{keV}\).
- The \(^{17}\mathrm{O}(p,\alpha)^{14}\mathrm{N}\) reaction has a narrow resonance at an energy between \(65\,\mathrm{keV}\) and \(75\,\mathrm{keV}\), with proton partial width \(\Gamma_p \simeq 22\,\mathrm{neV}\).

## Context

This work extends the classical resonant reaction rate formalism of Clayton (1968) to include non-extensive statistical mechanics corrections, motivated by the possibility that long-range many-body nuclear correlations and memory effects in dense stellar plasmas (such as white dwarfs) may cause deviations from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution. The authors build on prior studies of non-extensive effects in astrophysical plasmas by Lavagno and Quarati (2000) and by Kaniadakis, Lavagno, Lissia, and Quarati (1998), and they apply their results to the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}\) reaction in carbon white dwarfs, where previous work by Cussons, Langanke, and Liolios (2002) and by Itoh et al. (2003) had already identified resonant screening corrections that can alter the reaction rate by many orders of magnitude. The derived formulae enable future calculations of non-extensive corrections for other resonant reactions, such as those in the CNO cycle, and can be combined with existing screening factors to obtain a total effective rate enhancement factor.
