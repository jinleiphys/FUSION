# Constraining $η/s$ through high-p$_\perp$ theory and data

> We study whether it is possible to use high-$p_\perp$ data/theory to constrain the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$ of the matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We use two approaches: i) We calculate high-$p_\perp$ $R_{AA}$ and flow coefficients $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$ assuming different $(η/s)(T)$ of the fluid-dynamically evolving medium. ii) We calculate the quenching strength ($\hat{q}/T^3$) from our dynamical energy loss model and convert it to $η/s$ as a function of temperature. It turned out that the first approach can not distinguish between different $(η/s)(T)$ assumptions when the evolution is constrained to reproduce the low-$p_\perp$ data. In distinction, $(η/s)(T)$ calculated using the second approach agrees surprisingly well with the $(η/s)(T)$ inferred through state-of-the-art Bayesian analyses of the low-$p_\perp$ data even in the vicinity of $T_c$, while providing much smaller uncertainties at high temperatures.

## Key claim
High-pT observables (R_AA and flow coefficients v2, v3, v4) cannot distinguish between different temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s)(T) parametrizations when the hydrodynamic evolution is constrained to reproduce low-pT data; however, eta/s(T) derived theoretically from the dynamical energy loss model via the jet quenching parameter qhat agrees surprisingly well with Bayesian analyses of low-pT data near Tc and provides much smaller uncertainties at high temperatures.

## Method
The authors used two complementary approaches. First, they performed 2+1-dimensional dissipative fluid-dynamical simulations (VISHNew) with event-by-event T_R_ENTo initial conditions, assuming three different (eta/s)(T) parametrizations (constant, "Nature," and "LHHQ") adjusted to reproduce low-pT data at RHIC and LHC, then computed high-pT R_AA and flow harmonics v2, v3, v4 using the DREENA-A framework based on dynamical energy loss formalism. Second, they derived the jet quenching parameter qhat/T^3 from the dynamical energy loss model using hard thermal loop perturbation theory with running coupling and finite magnetic mass, then converted it to eta/s via the relation eta/s approximately 1.25 T^3/qhat.

## Key numbers
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, the constant eta/s parametrization used eta/s = 0.15; the "Nature" parametrization used (eta/s)_min = 0.1, (eta/s)_slope = 1.11, (eta/s)_crv = -0.48; the "LHHQ" parametrization used (eta/s)_min = 0.04, (eta/s)_slope = 3.30, (eta/s)_crv = 0.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the constant eta/s parametrization used eta/s = 0.12.
- The pseudocritical temperature was fixed to Tc = 154 MeV from the HotQCD collaboration.
- The average initial temperature in the (10-20)% centrality class for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV was 370 MeV, while the maximal temperature experienced by jets could reach up to 600 MeV.
- The jet-perceived temperatures differed by less than 4% at early stages and less than 2% for most of the evolution across the three (eta/s)(T) parametrizations.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section was 70 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The initial time for fluid-dynamical evolution was tau_0 = 1 fm/c.
- The bulk viscosity parametrization used (zeta/s)_max = 0.03, width = 0.022, and T_0 = 0.183 GeV.

## Context
This work extends efforts to constrain the temperature dependence of eta/s in quark-gluon plasma, which has been well constrained only near Tc (up to about 1.5 Tc) by Bayesian analyses such as Bernhard (2019) and Auvinen (2020), while remaining poorly constrained at higher temperatures. The study demonstrates that high-pT observables alone cannot distinguish between different (eta/s)(T) scenarios, contradicting naive expectations that jet energy loss would be sensitive to high-temperature viscosity. The theoretical derivation of eta/s from the dynamical energy loss model provides a new method to constrain eta/s at high temperatures with smaller uncertainties than Bayesian analyses, enabling future combined constraints from both low-pT and high-pT approaches.
