# Jet Mass Spectra in Higgs + One Jet at NNLL

> The invariant mass of a jet is a benchmark variable describing the structure of jets at the LHC. We calculate the jet mass spectrum for Higgs plus one jet at the LHC at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) order using a factorization formula. At this order, the cross section becomes sensitive to perturbation theory at the soft m_jet^2/p_T^jet scale. Our calculation is exclusive and uses the 1-jettiness global event shape to implement a veto on additional jets. The dominant dependence on the jet veto is removed by normalizing the spectrum, leaving residual dependence from non-global logarithms depending on the ratio of the jet mass and jet veto variables. For our exclusive jet cross section these non-global logarithms are parametrically smaller than in the inclusive case, allowing us to obtain a complete NNLL result. Results for the dependence of the jet mass spectrum on the kinematics, jet algorithm, and jet size R are given. Using individual partonic channels we illustrate the difference between the jet mass spectra for quark and gluon jets. We also study the effect of hadronization and underlying event on the jet mass in PYTHIA. To highlight the similarity of inclusive and exclusive jet mass spectra, a comparison to LHC data is presented.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the jet mass spectrum for Higgs plus one jet at the LHC at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) order, but only for an *exclusive* jet cross section that uses a 1-jettiness event shape to veto additional jets, and the authors note that non-global logarithms are parametrically smaller in this exclusive case than in the inclusive case, allowing a complete NNLL result without resumming those logarithms.

## Method

The authors use a factorization formula based on Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the process $pp\to H+1$ jet, differential in the 1-jettiness variables $\Tau_J$, $\Tau_a$, and $\Tau_b$. The jet mass $m_J$ is obtained from $\Tau_J$ via $m_J^2 = Q_J \Tau_J$, and a common cut $\Tau_{a,b} \leq \Tau^{\cut}$ implements the jet veto. The hard, beam, jet, and soft functions are each evaluated at their natural scales and evolved via renormalization group equations to a common scale, resumming logarithms of $m_J^2/p_T^{J\,2}$ and $Q_i\Tau_i/p_T^{J\,2}$. The normalized spectrum is obtained by dividing the differential cross section by the cumulant cross section integrated up to $m_J^\cut$.

## Key numbers

* For a jet with $p_T^J \sim 300$ GeV, the jet mass peaks at $m_J \sim 50$ GeV, leading to large logarithms of $p_T^{J\,2}/m_J^2 \sim 36$.
* The non-global logarithms are not large when $1/8 \le m_J^{\cut\,2}/p_{\rm cut}^2 \le 8$, where $p_{\rm cut}^2 \simeq p_T^J \Tau^\cut$.
* For the geometric $R$ measure with $R=0.5$, the parameter $\rho$ is given by $\rho(R=0.5,\eta_J) = 0.164 + 0.037 \eta_J^2 - 0.009 \eta_J^4 + 0.0008 \eta_J^6$ for $|\eta_J| \le 2$.
* For the geometric $R$ measure with $R=1$, the parameter $\rho$ is given by $\rho(R=1,\eta_J) = 0.834 - 0.233 \eta_J^2 + 0.077 \eta_J^4 - 0.008 \eta_J^6$ for $|\eta_J| \le 2$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of jet mass spectra that were carried out at NLL for inclusive jets in $pp\to 2$ jets and $Z+1$ jet by Dasgupta (2012) and for $e^+e^-\to 2$ jets by Banfi (2010). It also builds on the $N$-jettiness factorization framework developed by Stewart (2010) and Jouttenus (2011). The exclusive NNLL result enables comparisons with LHC data on jet mass spectra and provides a benchmark for testing Monte Carlo programs like PYTHIA, while the normalized spectrum removes the dominant dependence on the jet veto variable.
