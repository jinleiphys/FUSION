# Computations of scattering lengths in nnpp system within cluster reduction method for Yakubovsky equations

> Scattering lengths for d-d, n-$^3$He and p-$^3$H systems are computed via Cluster Reduction Method for Yakubovsky equations in configuration space taking into account Coulomb interaction between protons. MT I-III potential model was used to describe the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Results of calculations are in a good agreement with existing experimental data and results of calculations of other authors.

## Key claim

The cluster reduction method (CRM) for Yakubovsky differential equations yields scattering lengths for the four-nucleon system that are in good agreement with existing experimental data and other calculations, but the agreement is qualified by the use of the MT I-III potential model and by the fact that the method is an approximation that reduces the full Yakubovsky equations to equations for relative cluster motion.

## Method

The authors applied the cluster reduction method (CRM) to the Yakubovsky differential equations in configuration space, expanding the four-body wave function components along a basis of Faddeev equation solutions for the three-body subsystems. They then projected onto a biorthogonal basis to obtain reduced equations for amplitudes describing the relative motion of two clusters. For charged-particle systems (nnpp), they included the Coulomb interaction between protons using a modified version of the reduced equations.

## Key numbers

- n-³He scattering length for S=0 state: 7.5 - 4.2i fm (this work), compared to experimental value 6.53 (±0.32) - 4.445 (±0.003) i fm from Exp. (1997)
- p-³H scattering length for S=0 state: -22.6 fm (this work), compared to -21 fm from other authors (1997)
- d-d scattering length for S=0 state: 10.2 - 0.2i fm (this work)
- d-d scattering length for S=2 state: 7.5 fm (this work)
- n-³He scattering length for S=1 state: 2.9 - 0.0i fm (this work)
- p-³H scattering length for S=1 state: 0.5 fm (this work)
- A₁₀ (total spin S=1, total isospin T=0) scattering length: 2.8 fm (this work)
- A₀₀ (total spin S=0, total isospin T=0) scattering length: 14.7 fm (this work)
- 0⁺ resonance state of ⁴He: resonance energy E_r = 0.15 MeV, width Γ = 0.3 MeV (this work), compared to experimental values E_r = 0.25 MeV, Γ = 0.3 MeV from Exp. (1997)

## Context

This work extends the cluster reduction method previously applied to n-³H scattering by Yakovlev and Filikhin (1997) to the nnpp system including Coulomb interactions. The results are compared with calculations from other authors (1997) using different methods and with experimental data from Exp. (1997). The CRM enables practical four-nucleon scattering calculations on modest computational resources, as the authors note that exact four-nucleon calculations of n-³H scattering were performed on a personal computer.
