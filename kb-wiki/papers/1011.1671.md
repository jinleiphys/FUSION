# Color Decoherence in In-Medium QCD Cascades

## Key claim
The talk analyzes the consequences of the assumption that quantum coherence effects, specifically angular ordering in QCD cascades, are disrupted within the hot fireball created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, based on the fact that color-spin rotation of the hard mode is faster than the rate of change of its energy-momentum. The authors do not take into account energy-momentum exchange with the medium accompanying color randomization, and explicitly consider the finite size of the hot zone, unlike prior work.

## Method
The authors model in-medium QCD cascades by associating a spatiotemporal pattern with the cascade using the lifetime τ of a virtual parton, defined as τ = E (1/Q² − 1/Q²_par) for a parton with energy E and virtuality Q² created from a parent with virtuality Q²_par. For decays occurring inside the medium, angular ordering is switched off to represent the destruction of quantum coherence. The resulting rapidity distributions of final prehadrons are computed for gluon jets with initial energy E₀ = 100 GeV and various medium sizes L.

## Key numbers
- For gluon jets with initial energy E₀ = 100 GeV, the rapidity distribution P(y) of final prehadrons is computed for medium sizes L = 0.5 fm, 1 fm, and 5 fm.
- At L = 0.5 fm, the rapidity distribution is already significantly different from the vacuum case (L = 0 fm, full angular ordering).
- At larger L (e.g., 5 fm), the distribution practically saturates.
- The dominant effect of decoherence is a softening of the rapidity distribution, accompanied by an increase in the yield of hard final particles and an overall relative flattening compared to the in-vacuum case.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of medium-induced modifications to intrajet properties in heavy ion collisions, building on Monte Carlo generators such as PYQUEN, JEWEL, QPYTHIA, and QHERWIG, which take into account medium-induced radiative energy loss. It differs from JEWEL by not including energy-momentum exchange with the medium, and from the earlier comparison of angular ordered and non-angular ordered cases by Borghini and Schürmann (1987) by explicitly accounting for the finite size of the hot zone. The results enable further quantitative study of in-medium QCD and the role of color decoherence in jet quenching.
