# Origin of the Three-body Parameter Universality in Efimov Physics

> In recent years extensive theoretical and experimental studies of universal few-body physics have led to advances in our understanding of universal Efimov physics [1]. The Efimov effect, once considered a mysterious and esoteric effect, is today a reality that many experiments in ultracold quantum gases have successfully observed and continued to explore [2-14]. Whereas theory was the driving force behind our understanding of Efimov physics for decades, recent experiments have contributed an unexpected discovery. Specifically, measurements have found that the so-called three-body parameter determining several properties of the system is universal, even though fundamental assumptions in the theory of the Efimov effect suggest that it should be a variable property that depends on the precise details of the short-range two- and three-body interactions. The present Letter resolves this apparent contradiction by elucidating unanticipated implications of the two-body interactions. Our study shows that the three-body parameter universality emerges because a universal effective barrier in the three-body potentials prevents the three particles from simultaneously getting close to each other. Our results also show limitations on this universality, as it is more likely to occur for neutral atoms and less likely to extend to light nuclei.

## Key claim

The three-body parameter in Efimov physics can be universal for neutral atoms because a universal effective repulsive barrier in the three-body potentials at hyperradius R ≈ 2 r_vdW prevents the three particles from simultaneously getting close together, but this universality is limited and less likely to extend to light nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic hyperspherical representation to solve the hyperradial Schrödinger equation for three-body systems, employing a recently developed methodology that can treat two-body model interactions supporting up to 100 ro-vibrational bound states. They analyzed several model potentials (v_sch, v^a_λ, v^b_λ, and v_vdW^hs) with different numbers of bound states, including a hard-sphere-plus-van-der-Waals model that replaces the deep potential well with a hard wall. The effective hyperradial potentials W_ν and channel functions Φ_ν were computed to study the suppression of probability density within the two-body potential wells.

## Key numbers

- The universal repulsive barrier in the effective three-body potentials occurs at hyperradius R ≈ 2 r_vdW, where r_vdW is the van der Waals length.
- For the hard-sphere-plus-van-der-Waals model (v_vdW^hs), the three-body parameter values are κ_* = 0.226(2)/r_vdW and a_3b^- = -9.73(3) r_vdW.
- Experimental Efimov resonance positions for 133Cs, 39K, 7Li, 6Li, and 85Rb all lie within about 15% of the v_vdW^hs result for a_3b^-.
- The probability to find atoms at distances r < r_vdW was calculated to be in the range 2%–4% for the v_λ^a model.
- For 133Cs, Efimov resonances were found at a = a_3b^- = -9.1(2) r_vdW.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between the fundamental assumption of Efimov theory that the three-body parameter should be nonuniversal and depend on short-range interaction details (Braaten 2006), and experimental observations by Berninger et al. (2011) and others showing that the three-body parameter is approximately the same across different atomic species. The paper extends prior theoretical work on universal three-body parameters in dipolar systems (Wang 2011) and near narrow Feshbach resonances (Petrov 2004, Stoof 2009), but identifies a broader class of systems with universal three-body parameters: those with two-body interactions that efficiently suppress the probability of finding particles at short distances. The results enable future exploration of heteronuclear systems and the relationship between a<0 and a>0 Efimov features.
