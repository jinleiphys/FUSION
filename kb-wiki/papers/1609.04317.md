# Applying the relativistic quantization condition to a three-particle bound state in a periodic box

> Using our recently developed relativistic three-particle quantization condition, we study the finite-volume energy shift of a spin-zero three-particle bound state. We reproduce the result obtained using non-relativistic quantum mechanics by Meissner, Rios and Rusetsky, and generalize the result to a moving frame.

## Key claim

Using their relativistic three-particle quantization condition, the authors reproduce the non-relativistic quantum mechanics result of Meissner, Rios and Rusetsky (MRR) for the finite-volume energy shift of a spin-zero three-particle bound state, and generalize it to a moving frame. The derivation assumes a shallow bound state (binding momentum κ much smaller than the particle mass m), the unitary limit where the two-particle s-wave scattering length diverges, and that s-wave two-particle channels dominate with zero total angular momentum of the bound state.

## Method

The authors apply their previously developed relativistic quantization condition for three identical spinless particles in a cubic box of side length L, which relates finite-volume energies to the two-particle scattering amplitude M and an infinite-volume three-particle K-matrix Kdf. They expand the finite-volume three-particle scattering amplitude in powers of the two-particle amplitude and the three-particle propagator, then extract the leading volume dependence by decomposing momentum sums into integrals plus sum-integral differences. The resulting expression for the energy shift is evaluated using the pole structure of the infinite-volume scattering amplitude and the unsymmetrized residue factors Gamma(u) and Gamma-bar(u), which are related to the Faddeev wavefunction components from the NRQM analysis.

## Key numbers

- The binding momentum κ is defined by E_B = 3m - κ²/m, with the hierarchy 1/L ≪ κ ≪ m for large L
- The leading energy shift is ΔE(L) = c |A|² (κ²/m) (1/(κL)^(3/2)) e^(-2κL/√3) + ...
- The numerical coefficient c ≈ -96.351, determined from the solution to the Faddeev equation
- The parameter s₀ ≈ 1.00624, defined by s₀ cosh(πs₀/2) = (8/√3) sinh(πs₀/6)
- For comparison, the two-particle bound state shift is ΔE₂(L) = -12 (κ₂²/m) (1/(κ₂L)) e^(-κ₂L) + ...

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic quantum mechanics result of Meissner, Rios and Rusetsky (MRR) to a relativistic framework and generalizes it to nonzero total momentum, providing a cross-check of the authors' previously developed three-particle quantization condition (Hansen and Sharpe, 2014, 2015). The result enables lattice QCD calculations of three-particle bound states (Efimov states) by providing the leading finite-volume correction, and the generalization to moving frames allows cancellation of volume dependence by combining results at different total momenta, as was done for two-particle bound states by Davoudi and Savage (2011).
