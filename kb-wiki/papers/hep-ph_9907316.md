# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay in Gauge Theories

> Neutrinoless double beta decay is a very important process both from the particle and nuclear physics point of view. Its observation will severely constrain the existing models and signal that the neutrinos are massive Majorana particles. From the elementary particle point of view it pops up in almost every model. In addition to the traditional mechanisms, like the neutrino mass, the admixture of right handed currents etc, it may occur due to the R-parity violating supersymmetric (SUSY) interactions. From the nuclear physics point of view it is challenging, because: 1) The relevant nuclei have complicated nuclear structure. 2) The energetically allowed transitions are exhaust a small part of all the strength. 3) One must cope with the short distance behavior of the transition operators, especially when the intermediate particles are heavy (eg in SUSY models). Thus novel effects, like the double beta decay of pions in flight between nucleons, have to be considered. 4) The intermediate momenta involved are about 100 MeV. Thus one has to take into account possible momentum dependent terms in the nucleon current. We find that, for the mass mechanism, such modifications of the nucleon current for light neutrinos reduce the nuclear matrix elements by about 25 per cent, almost regardless of the nuclear model. In the case of heavy neutrinos the effect is much larger and model dependent. Taking the above effects into account, the available nuclear matrix elements for the experimentally interesting nuclei A = 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136 and 150 and the experimental limits on the life times we have extracted new stringent limits on the average neutrino mass and on the R-parity violating coupling for various SUSY models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that momentum-dependent terms in the nucleon current, previously neglected for the neutrino mass mechanism, reduce the nuclear matrix elements for light neutrino exchange by about 25 percent almost regardless of the nuclear model, and that this reduction must be accounted for when extracting limits on lepton-number violating parameters from experimental half-life limits.

## Method

The author computed nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double beta decay using the renormalized proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) for nine experimentally interesting nuclei (A = 76, 82, 96, 100, 116, 128, 130, 136, 150). The nucleon current was expanded to include weak magnetism and induced pseudoscalar terms beyond the standard vector and axial-vector contributions, with form factors parameterized using empirical cutoff values (Lambda_V^2 = 0.71 GeV^2, Lambda_A = 1.09 GeV). The resulting two-body transition operators in momentum space included Fermi, Gamow-Teller, and a new tensor component, and the matrix elements were combined with experimental half-life limits to extract upper bounds on the effective neutrino mass and on the R-parity violating SUSY coupling lambda'_111.

## Key numbers

- For light neutrino exchange, the inclusion of momentum-dependent nucleon current terms reduces the nuclear matrix element by about 25 percent, almost independently of the nuclear model; for A=76 the reduction is 29 percent, for A=130 it is 38 percent.
- The extracted upper limit on the effective neutrino mass is <m_nu> < 0.3 eV/c^2, based on the best experimental limits across the nine nuclei.
- The extracted upper limit on the R-parity violating coupling is lambda'_111 < 4.0 x 10^{-4} for reasonable choices of SUSY model parameters.
- For the light neutrino mass mechanism, the nuclear matrix element M^I_<m_nu> (dimensionless) ranges from 0.66 (for Xe-136) to 3.33 (for Nd-150) in the QRPA calculation.
- For the heavy neutrino exchange mechanism, the nuclear matrix element M^I_eta_N ranges from 14.1 (for Xe-136) to 35.6 (for Nd-150).
- The present experimental half-life lower limit for Ge-76 is 1.1 x 10^25 years, yielding an upper limit on <m_nu> of 0.62 eV from that isotope alone.

## Context

This work extends the long history of neutrinoless double beta decay calculations, beginning with Furry (1939) and Primakoff and Rosen (1952), and continuing through the gauge theory era with Kotani and his group, by incorporating previously neglected momentum-dependent terms in the nucleon current. The author shows that these terms, which were earlier considered only for the lambda and eta parameters in left-right interference mechanisms by Tomoda (1985) and by Suhonen, Civitarese, and collaborators, also significantly affect the mass mechanism. The results enable more reliable extraction of limits on neutrino mass and SUSY parameters from experimental data, and the paper provides updated nuclear matrix elements for all nine experimentally relevant isotopes using both shell model and QRPA approaches.
