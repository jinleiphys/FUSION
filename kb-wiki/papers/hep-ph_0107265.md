# Novel phases and transitions in Color Flavor Locked matter

> We analyze the phases of dense quark matter at nonzero quark masses. We map out the phase diagrams for charged quark matter as well as charge neutral matter containing leptons. We find that in Color Flavor Locked (CFL) quark matter, the symmetric phase with equal numbers of $u$, $d$ and $s$ quarks is unlikely to occur in nature. Various less symmetric phases with additional meson condensates may play a role in neutron star cores, however, in agreement with the analysis of Bedaque and Schäfer. We discuss the implications of these novel color superconducting quark matter phases and their transitions for neutron stars.

## Key claim

The symmetric Color Flavor Locked (CFL) phase with equal numbers of u, d, and s quarks is unstable with respect to meson condensation at all densities below approximately 10^21 times those expected in neutron star cores, meaning that less symmetric meson-condensed phases are the relevant ground states for realistic quark matter in neutron stars.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective chiral Lagrangian for the Goldstone bosons of the CFL phase, including the Bedaque-Schäfer term (X_L = -M M^dagger / 2mu) which acts as an effective chemical potential for flavor symmetries. They solved the mean-field free energy minimization for single-meson condensates (pions and kaons) and for mixed condensates, then mapped the phase diagram in the plane of (m_s^2/2mu) versus mu_Q. They further imposed electric charge neutrality with leptons to determine the ground state for neutron star conditions.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric CFL phase is disfavored until quark densities greater than approximately 10^21 times those expected in neutron star cores.
- For typical neutron star conditions, the quark chemical potential mu is approximately 400 MeV, and (m_s^2/2mu) is approximately 25 MeV (with m_s = 150 MeV).
- The color superconducting gap Delta is taken as approximately 100 MeV for mu approximately 500 MeV, based on phenomenological models.
- The effective chemical potential for neutral kaons is given by (m_s^2 - m_d^2)/(2mu).
- For charge neutral matter, electrons appear in the ground state when the neutrino chemical potential mu_nu_e is greater than approximately 6 MeV (depending on the electromagnetic parameter b).
- The electric charge chemical potential mu_Q in the charge neutral K^+ phase is only a few MeV even when mu_nu_e is approximately 200 MeV.
- Pseudo-Goldstone boson masses are approximately 10-20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bedaque and Schäfer (2001), who first pointed out that nonzero quark masses induce effective chemical potentials that favor meson condensation in CFL matter. It contradicts the earlier expectation that the symmetric CFL phase (with equal numbers of u, d, s quarks) would be the ground state at densities relevant for neutron stars. The results enable future studies of neutrino transport, magnetic field evolution, and lepton number emission in neutron stars, and suggest that the K^0 phase (which is electrically neutral without electrons) may be the relevant ground state for mature neutron star cores.
