# Dilepton production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies

> Dilepton production in proton- and nucleus-induced reactions is studied in relativistic transport model using initial conditions determined by the string dynamics from RQMD. It is found that both the CERES and HELIOS-3 data for dilepton spectra in proton-nucleus reactions can be well described by the `conventional' mechanism of Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay. However, to provide a quantitative explanation of the observed dilepton spectra in central S+Au and S+W collisions requires contributions other than these direct decays. Introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot and dense medium, we find that these heavy-ion data can also be satisfactorily explained. This agrees with our earlier conclusions based on a fire cylinder model. We also give predictions for Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon using current CERES mass resolution and acceptance.

# Dilepton production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons in central S+Au and S+W collisions at SPS energies can be quantitatively explained by introducing a decrease of vector meson masses in hot and dense medium, but only after conventional mechanisms (Dalitz decay and direct vector meson decay) fail to reproduce the heavy-ion data; the same conventional mechanisms are sufficient for proton-nucleus reactions.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model with initial hadron phase-space distributions generated by string fragmentation from the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) model version 2.1. Hadronic rescattering was treated with the relativistic transport model including processes such as pion-pion annihilation through rho meson formation, kaon-antikaon annihilation through phi meson formation, and pi-rho annihilation through a1 meson formation, with in-medium vector meson masses determined from an extended Walecka model using constituent quark model relations.

## Key numbers

- For central S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm: final charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη ≈ 125 in rapidity range 2.1 < η < 2.65; initial slope parameter of pion transverse mass distribution ≈ 165 MeV; average baryon density in central region ≈ 2.3 ρ_0; average energy density ≈ 2.5 GeV/fm^3
- For the CERES S+Au data around 0.4 GeV invariant mass, theoretical results with free meson masses underpredicted the data by about a factor of 4; for the HELIOS-3 S+W data around 0.5 GeV, underprediction was less than a factor of two
- With in-medium meson masses, the dilepton yield in the mass range 0.2 to 0.6 GeV was enhanced by about a factor of 2-3 compared to free meson mass results
- Freeze-out time was found to be about 20 fm/c
- For rho meson decay: Γ_{ρ^0→e+e-} ≈ 5 keV from vector dominance, compared to measured width of 6.5 keV; coefficients C_{e+e-} = 8.814×10^{-6} (ρ^0), 0.767×10^{-6} (ω), and 1.344×10^{-6} (φ) determined from measured widths

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier fire cylinder model (Li et al., 1995) by using a fully microscopic transport model with initial string dynamics from RQMD, rather than assuming thermal equilibrium. It supports the findings of Cassing et al. (1995) using Hadron-String Dynamics that conventional mechanisms suffice for proton-nucleus reactions but medium effects are needed for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results contradict the possibility that pion-pion annihilation alone (without medium modifications) could explain the CERES and HELIOS-3 data, and enable predictions for upcoming Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/nucleon.
