# Effective Field Theory and the Nuclear Many Body Problem

> To appear in the proceedings of 4th International Conference On Quarks And Nuclear Physics (QNP06), 5-10 June 2006, Madrid, Spain. European Physical Journal A, in press

# Effective Field Theory and the Nuclear Many Body Problem

## Key claim
Dilute neutron matter at densities where the inter-particle spacing is large compared to the effective range but small compared to the scattering length exhibits universal behavior, with the energy per particle proportional to that of a free Fermi gas by a universal constant ξ. The paper reviews multiple analytical and numerical approaches to computing ξ, but the extracted values span a wide range (0.09–0.44) depending on the method, and the convergence of expansions in dimension or number of fermion species remains uncertain.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice field theory simulations of an effective lagrangian of point-like fermions with a short-range four-fermion interaction, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to render the action real for Monte Carlo simulations. Analytical approaches include a large N expansion with SU(N) and Sp(2N) symmetric interactions, a large d (spatial dimension) expansion summing particle-particle ladder diagrams, and epsilon expansions near d=2 and d=4 spatial dimensions where the scattering amplitude vanishes linearly in the deviation from integer dimension.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18 fm; effective range: r_nn = 2.8 fm
- Universal parameter ξ from lattice simulations at non-zero temperature: ξ = 0.09–0.42
- ξ from canonical T=0 lattice simulations (Lee 2005): ξ = 0.25
- ξ from Green Function Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson 2003): ξ = 0.44
- ξ from large N expansion with Sp(2N) interaction in unitarity limit: ξ = 0.59
- ξ from large d expansion in the strong coupling limit (d→∞): ξ = 1/2
- ξ from epsilon expansion near d=4 (setting ε=1): ξ = 0.475
- ξ from epsilon expansion near d=2 (setting ε̄=1): ξ = 0
- Pairing gap from large d expansion: Δ = (2e⁻γ E_F / d) exp(-1/(dλ)) (1 + O(1/d))
- Pairing gap from epsilon expansion near d=2: φ₀ = (2μ/e) exp(-1/ε̄) (1 + O(ε̄))
- From Sp(2N) large N expansion near T_c: (μ/T)(T_c) = 1.50 + 2.79/N + O(1/N²)

## Context
This review extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear two- and three-body bound states (Weinberg 1990, Kaplan 1998, Bedaque 1998) into the many-body domain, specifically for dilute neutron matter. The lattice results agree well with variational calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) using phenomenological potentials. The analytical expansions in dimension (Nussinov and Nussinov 2004, Nishida and Son 2006) and number of fermion species (Furnstahl 2002, Nikolic and Sachdev 2006) provide complementary frameworks, though the epsilon expansions show poor convergence when extrapolated to d=3. The work enables future extension to nuclear systems near saturation density, including finite range effects, explicit pions, isospin, and three-body forces, with the goal of understanding whether effective field theories can explain saturation and the Coester line (Coester 1970).
