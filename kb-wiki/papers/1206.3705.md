# Antibound poles in cutoff Woods-Saxon and in Salamon-Vertse potentials

> The motion of l=0 antibound poles of the S-matrix with varying potential strength is calculated in a cutoff Woods-Saxon (WS) potential and in the Salamon-Vertse (SV) potential, which goes to zero smoothly at a finite distance. The pole position of the antibound states as well as of the resonances depend on the cutoff radius, especially for higher node numbers. The starting points (at potential zero) of the pole trajectories correlate well with the range of the potential. The normalized antibound radial wave functions on the imaginary k-axis below and above the coalescence point have been found to be real and imaginary, respectively.

# Antibound poles in cutoff Woods-Saxon and in Salamon-Vertse potentials

## Key claim

The motion of l=0 antibound S-matrix poles depends on the cutoff radius in a cutoff Woods-Saxon potential, especially for higher node numbers, and this dependence is a caution against indiscriminate use of the WS potential to represent broad resonances or antibound states. The normalized antibound radial wave functions on the imaginary k-axis below the coalescence point are real, while those above are imaginary.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrodinger equation numerically for l=0 using a modified version of the computer code GAMOW, with the pole positions defined by matching left and right logarithmic derivatives at an intermediate distance. They compared a cutoff Woods-Saxon potential (cut off at R_max) with the Salamon-Vertse potential, which goes to zero smoothly with all derivatives zero at a finite distance, fitting the SV parameters to match the WS shape for a neutron potential representing 208Pb. The accuracy was checked using the more accurate program ANTI with Ixaru's CP method.

## Key numbers

- WS potential parameters: R = 1.27 × 208^(1/3) fm = 7.525 fm, a = 0.7 fm, with R_max = 15 fm
- SV potential best-fit parameters: c1 = 0.997, ρ0 = 10.963 fm, ρ1 = 8.328 fm
- Coalescence depths V_n for WS (R_max = 15 fm): V_1 = 6.969 MeV, V_2 = 18.995 MeV, V_3 = 36.286 MeV
- Coalescence depths for SV: V_1 = 6.978 MeV, V_2 = 19.378 MeV, V_3 = 37.347 MeV
- Threshold depths V_{0,n} for WS: V_{0,0} = 0.897 MeV, V_{0,1} = 7.727 MeV, V_{0,2} = 20.562 MeV, V_{0,3} = 39.122 MeV
- Threshold depths for SV: V_{0,0} = 0.893 MeV, V_{0,1} = 7.634 MeV, V_{0,2} = 20.519 MeV, V_{0,3} = 39.072 MeV
- For WS, the slope a1 of Re(k_n) versus n gives R = 14.82 fm, in good agreement with R_max = 15 fm
- For SV, the slope a1 = 0.32 fm^(-1) gives R = 9.778 fm
- For n=7 WS trajectories, as R_max varies from 11 fm to 20 fm, the intersection with the Im(k)-axis moves from -i0.40 fm^(-1) to -i0.61 fm^(-1)
- For n=7, the product β_7 = R_max × γ̄_7 is approximated by β_7(R_max) = -5.05 + 0.864 R_max
- Wave numbers for n=1 antibound states in WS at V_0 = 6.9692 MeV: k_2 = -0.183 i fm^(-1), k_3 = -0.188 i fm^(-1)
- Wave numbers for n=2 antibound states in WS at V_0 = 19.5 MeV: k_4 = -0.410 i fm^(-1), k_5 = -0.422 i fm^(-1)

## Context

This work extends recent studies by Salamon and Vertse (2008) and Racz et al. (2011) on the dependence of S-matrix poles on the tail behavior of the potential, now specifically examining antibound poles. It contrasts with earlier work by Newton (1959) and Michel et al. (2009) by also exploring the wave functions of antibound states. The results enable inclusion of antibound states in orthonormal bases for describing weakly bound or unbound nuclei, as previously found meaningful for 11,12Li by Lovas et al. (2004), Betan et al. (2004, 2005), and Xu et al. (2011).
