# A model for high energy rho meson leptoproduction based on collinear factorization and dipole models

> We present a phenomenological model for the helicity amplitudes T11 and T00 of the rho meson exclusive diffractive leptoproduction in the forward limit. This model leads to a very good description of the polarized cross-sections sigmaT and sigmaL when compared to HERA data. This model is based on the impact factor representation of the helicity amplitudes. The gamma* -> rho impact factor is computed within the light-cone collinear factorization scheme, the impact parameter space representation allowing to factorize out the dipole-target amplitude. Finally our description combines a model for the dipole-target amplitude that includes the saturation effects with the results for the impact factor where the twist 2 and twist 3 distribution amplitudes of the rho meson are involved.

# High energy rho meson leptoproduction

## Key claim
The model provides a very good description of the polarized cross-sections σ_T and σ_L compared to HERA data, but only for Q² ≳ 5 GeV²; the discrepancy at lower Q² is attributed to missing higher twist corrections in the γ* → ρ impact factor.

## Method
The helicity amplitudes T_11 and T_00 for exclusive diffractive rho meson leptoproduction in the forward limit (t → 0) are computed using an impact factor representation, where the γ* → ρ impact factor is calculated within light-cone collinear factorization up to twist 3. The impact parameter space representation allows factorization of the dipole-target amplitude, and the model combines a dipole cross-section that includes saturation effects with rho meson distribution amplitudes (twist 2 and twist 3) from Ball, Braun, Koike and Tanaka (1998). The polarized cross-sections σ_L and σ_T are obtained from the forward helicity amplitudes using an exponential t-dependence with slope b(Q²) extracted from H1 data.

## Key numbers
- The model predictions are compared to HERA data for Q² ≳ 5 GeV², where agreement is very good; below 5 GeV² discrepancies appear.
- The renormalization scale is set to μ² = (Q² + m_ρ²)/4 for the full twist 3 result.
- The asymptotic (AS) result corresponds to the limit μ² → ∞.
- The dipole cross-section model used is from Albacete (2010).

## Context
This work extends the first-principles approach to the γ*_T → ρ_T impact factor involving twist 3 operators, developed by Anikin, Ivanov, Pire, Szymanowski and Wallon (2009, 2010), by incorporating saturation effects in the dipole-target amplitude. A previous model by the same authors (Anikin et al., 2011) used the Gunion and Soper (1977) nucleon impact factor but gave sizable contributions from soft gluons with k² < 1 GeV², motivating the inclusion of saturation. The results enable future extension to the non-forward limit to probe the impact parameter dependence of the gluon density profile of the proton.
