# Baryon fluctuation signatures of the onset of deconfinement

## Key claim

An anomalous collision-energy dependence of proton number fluctuations is predicted as a consequence of the onset of deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energy of the nucleon pair around 10 GeV, arising from changes in the effective degrees of freedom between confined and deconfined matter. The prediction is conditional on the assumption that equilibrium hadron resonance gas (HRG) is created at energies below 8 GeV and equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is formed at energies above 12 GeV, with the transition region between these energies treated as a changeover that is not explicitly modeled.

## Method

The authors model confined matter using an ideal-gas classical hadron resonance gas (HRG) and deconfined matter using an ideal-gas QGP equation of state with massless u, d, and s quarks, both within the grand canonical ensemble (GCE). Net-baryon number cumulant ratios are first calculated in GCE, then corrected for limited momentum acceptance using the sub-ensemble acceptance method (SAM) with a baryon acceptance fraction alpha = 0.26, and finally converted to proton-number cumulant ratios via binomial thinning with a proton fraction beta = 0.4. The collision energy dependence of temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B is taken from a parametrization fitted to mean hadron multiplicities in central Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions (Cleymans 2005).

## Key numbers

- The onset of deconfinement is predicted to occur at sqrt(s_NN) approximately 8-12 GeV, with HRG assumed for sqrt(s_NN) <= 8 GeV and QGP for sqrt(s_NN) >= 12 GeV.
- For the HRG phase at sqrt(s_NN) <= 8 GeV, all net-baryon number cumulant ratios in GCE equal unity: kappa_2/kappa_1 = 1, kappa_3/kappa_2 = 1, kappa_4/kappa_2 = 1.
- For the QGP phase, the net-baryon number cumulant ratios are given by analytic expressions involving z = mu_B/(pi T), yielding values substantially below unity; for example, kappa_2/kappa_1 approximately 1/3 in the toy model limit.
- The antibaryon-to-baryon ratio at sqrt(s_NN) <= 8 GeV is below 1% (Becattini 2005).
- The parametrization of T and mu_B uses coefficients: a_1 = 0.152 GeV, a_2 = 0.026 GeV^{-1}, a_3 = 0.219 GeV^{-3}, b_1 = 1.310 GeV, b_2 = 0.278 GeV^{-1}.
- The acceptance fraction alpha = 0.26 and binomial thinning parameter beta = 0.4 are used as typical experimental values for STAR.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing idea from Asakawa (2000) and Jeon (2000) that net-baryon and electric charge fluctuations should be suppressed in deconfined matter due to fractional baryon charges of quarks (1/3). It provides a framework to interpret the beam-energy-scan results on proton number fluctuations from STAR at RHIC (STAR 2021) and earlier data from NA49 at CERN SPS (NA49 2002, 2007), offering an alternative or complementary explanation to critical point searches (Stephanov 2008) and baryon-baryon interaction transition proposals (Friman 2025). The results enable a consistent interpretation of fluctuation data from GSI SIS through CERN SPS to BNL RHIC and CERN LHC, though quantitative comparison requires improved modeling of acceptance parameters, nuclear cluster production, and possible critical point effects (Poberezhnyuk 2019).
