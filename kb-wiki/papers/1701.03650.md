# Triaxial shape fluctuations and quasiparticle excitations in heavy nuclei

> The deformation parameters beta and gamma together with the two-quasiparticle excitations are taken into account, for the first time, as coordinates within a symmetry conserving (angular momentum and particle number) generator coordinate method. The simultaneous consideration of collective as well as single particle degrees of freedom allows us to describe soft and rigid nuclei as well as the transition region in between. We apply the new theory to the study of the spectra and transition probabilities of the 156-172Er isotopes with a Pairing plus Quadrupole residual interaction. Good agreement with the experimental results is obtained for most of the observables studied and with the same quality for the very soft and the strongly deformed nuclei.

# Triaxial shape fluctuations and quasiparticle excitations in heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The simultaneous inclusion of both collective shape fluctuations in the \((\beta,\gamma)\) plane and two-quasiparticle excitations within a symmetry-conserving generator coordinate method allows the description of soft, rigid, and transitional nuclei with comparable quality, though the \(\gamma\)-band head energies remain systematically too high by about 25% relative to experiment, a common limitation shared with other theoretical approaches such as the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation with Skyrme forces or the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with Gogny forces.

## Method

The authors solve constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations on a triangular mesh of 49 points in the interval \(0 \leq \beta \leq 0.6\), \(0^\circ \leq \gamma \leq 60^\circ\) to generate intrinsic wave functions with different quadrupole shapes. For each HFB vacuum, two-quasiparticle states are constructed and both the vacua and the 2qp states are projected onto good angular momentum and particle number; the full ansatz mixes all these states via the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. The calculation uses a separable pairing plus quadrupole Hamiltonian fitted to reproduce experimental deformations of the Erbium isotopes, with three major shells for neutrons (\(N=4,5,6\)) and protons (\(N=3,4,5\)).

## Key numbers

- Energy minimum of \(^{156}\)Er is at \((\beta = 0.179, \gamma = 21.53^\circ)\) for the \(0_1\) state and at \((\beta = 0.200, \gamma = 31.29^\circ)\) for the \(2_2\) (\(\gamma\)-band) state.
- Energy minimum of \(^{172}\)Er is at \((\beta = 0.305, \gamma = 8.07^\circ)\) for the \(0_1\) state and at \((\beta = 0.305, \gamma = 12.43^\circ)\) for the \(2_2\) state.
- The two-quasiparticle energy cutoff condition is set at \(E_i(\beta,\gamma) + E_j(\beta,\gamma) + E_0(\beta,\gamma) \leq E(\beta_{\text{min}},\gamma_{\text{min}}) + 3.0\) MeV.
- Including shape mixing (theo2) lowers the \(\gamma\)-band head energy by approximately 500 keV for most isotopes, and by about 850 keV for \(^{156}\)Er and 750 keV for \(^{158}\)Er.
- The \(\beta\)-band head energies show a maximum at neutron number \(N = 98\) and a marked decrease toward \(N = 102\).

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method framework by adding two-quasiparticle excitations as explicit coordinates alongside the traditional \((\beta,\gamma)\) shape parameters, which had previously been ignored in GCM calculations with effective interactions such as Skyrme (PRC 78 024309, 2008), Gogny (PRC 81 064323, 2010), and relativistic forces (PRC 81 044311, 2010). It also contrasts with the projected shell model (PSM), which uses multi-quasiparticle states but only a single shape and does not always describe collective \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) bands properly. The results enable systematic studies of how collective and single-particle degrees of freedom couple across a chain of isotopes from soft to rigid, and the authors note that breaking time-reversal symmetry, as done in recent work by Borrajo, Rodriguez, and Egido (2015) and Egido, Borrajo, and Rodriguez (2016), may further improve the description of \(\gamma\)-bands.
