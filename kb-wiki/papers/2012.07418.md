# Multifarious roles of hidden chiral-scale symmetry:"Quenching" ${g_A}$ in nuclei

> I discuss how the axial current coupling constant $g_A$ renormalized in scale symmetric chiral EFT defined at a chiral matching scale impacts on the axial current matrix elements on beta decays in nuclei with and without neutrinos. The "quenched" $g_A$ observed in nuclear superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions, a long-standing puzzle in nuclear physics, is shown to encode the emergence of chiral-scale symmetry hidden in QCD in the vacuum. This enables one to explore how trace-anomaly-induced scale symmetry breaking enters in the renormalized $g_A$ in nuclei applicable to certain non-unique forbidden processes involved in neutrinoless double beta decays. A parallel is made between the roles of chiral-scale symmetry in quenching $g_A$ in highly dense medium and in hadron-quark continuity in the EoS of dense matter in massive compact stars. A systematic chiral-scale EFT, presently lacking in nuclear theory and potentially crucial for the future progress, is suggested as a challenge in the field.

# Multifarious roles of hidden chiral-scale symmetry: "Quenching" \(g_A\) in nuclei

## Key claim
The "quenched" \(g_A\) observed in nuclear superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions is not a fundamental quenching of the axial coupling constant itself, but rather encodes the emergence of chiral-scale symmetry hidden in QCD, with the quenching factor arising from strong nuclear correlations captured by a Landau Fermi-liquid fixed-point approximation combined with scale symmetry breaking effects from the trace anomaly.

## Method
The author employs an effective field theory incorporating both hidden local symmetry and hidden scale symmetry in a chiral Lagrangian, using large \(N_c\) and large \(\bar{N}\) approximations in the Fermi-liquid approach to calculate the superallowed Gamow-Teller matrix element. The total quenching factor \(q\) is decomposed into a product of two components: \(q_{\rm snc}^{\rm Landau}\) representing pure nuclear correlation effects (computed via Landau Fermi-liquid theory with soft-pion and soft-dilaton theorems) and \(q_{\rm ssb}\) representing anomaly-induced scale symmetry breaking (AISB) effects parameterized by the ratio \(f_\pi^\ast/f_\pi\) and the anomalous dimension \(\beta^\prime\) of the gluon stress tensor.

## Key numbers
- Free-space axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.276(4)\)
- Landau Fermi-liquid quenching factor from nuclear correlations at \(n \approx n_0\): \(q_{\rm snc}^{\rm Landau} \approx 0.79\)
- Scale-symmetric effective \(g_A\) from nuclear correlations alone: \(g_A^{\rm ss} \approx g_A \times q_{\rm snc}^{\rm Landau} = 1.276 \times 0.79 \approx 1.0\)
- Experimental quenching factor from RIKEN data for \(^{100}\)Sn superallowed decay: \(q_{\rm riken}^{\rm ESPSM} \approx 0.50\)
- Extracted AISB quenching factor from RIKEN data: \(q_{\rm ssb}^{\rm RIKEN} \approx 0.63\)
- Predicted "genuinely quenched" axial coupling constant from RIKEN data: \(\bar{g}_A^{\rm RIKEN} = 1.276 \times 0.63 \approx 0.80\)
- COBRA analysis results for \(\bar{g}_A\) in \(^{113}\)Cd \(\beta\) decay: \(0.809 \pm 0.122\), \(0.893 \pm 0.054\), \(0.968 \pm 0.056\)
- Range of \(c_A\) values consistent with COBRA data for \(\beta^\prime \approx 2.5\) and \(\Phi \approx 0.79\): \(0.15 \lesssim c_A^{\rm exp} \lesssim 0.48\)

## Context
This work extends the author's previous resolution of the quenched \(g_A\) puzzle (Rho, 2020; Rho and collaborators) by connecting it to the broader role of hidden chiral-scale symmetry in QCD. The approach contrasts with standard shell-model calculations that treat quenching phenomenologically (Wilkinson, 1970s) and with chiral effective field theory approaches that include higher-order many-body currents (Park et al., 1990s; King et al., 2020). The paper enables exploration of how trace-anomaly-induced scale symmetry breaking affects neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements (relevant to the work of Ejiri and collaborators) and draws parallels to hadron-quark continuity in dense matter equations of state for compact stars. The author suggests that a systematic chiral-scale EFT, currently lacking in nuclear theory, is needed for future progress.
