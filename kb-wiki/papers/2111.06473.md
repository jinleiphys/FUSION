# Semileptonic transitions: $B_{(s)} \to π(K)$; $D_s \to K$; $D\to π, K$; and $K\to π$

> Continuum Schwinger function methods for the strong-interaction bound-state problem are used to arrive at a unified set of parameter-free predictions for the semileptonic $K\to π$, $D\to π, K$ and $D_s \to K$, $B_{(s)} \to π(K)$ transition form factors and the associated branching fractions. The form factors are a leading source of uncertainty in all such calculations: our results agree quantitatively with available data and provide benchmarks for the hitherto unmeasured $D_s\to K^0$, $\bar B_s \to K^+$ form factors. The analysis delivers a value of $|V_{cs}| = 0.974(10)$ and also predictions for all branching fraction ratios in the pseudoscalar meson sector that can be used to test lepton flavour universality. Quantitative comparisons are provided between extant theory and the recent measurement of ${\cal B}_{B_s^0\to K^- μ^+ ν_μ}$. Here, further, refined measurements would be useful in moving toward a more accurate value of $|V_{ub}|$.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified, parameter-free set of predictions for semileptonic transition form factors across multiple meson systems using continuum Schwinger function methods, with the hedge that the rainbow-ladder truncation is the leading-order approximation and that the statistical Schlessinger point method (SPM) is used to extrapolate beyond the domain where direct numerical integration is possible due to singularities in the complex integration domain.

## Method

The authors employ the rainbow-ladder truncation of the continuum Schwinger function methods, solving coupled integral equations for dressed-quark propagators, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for initial- and final-state mesons, and the dressed vector weak transition vertex. For systems where the valence quark mass difference becomes large enough that singularities enter the complex integration domain (occurring at renormalisation-group-invariant current masses of 0.38 GeV for Q-u-bar and 0.45 GeV for Q-s-bar systems), they use the statistical Schlessinger point method (SPM) to extrapolate from calculations on lighter systems, building continued-fraction interpolations from 20 randomly chosen points out of 40 calculated values and selecting 100,000 singularity-free interpolants to obtain central values and 1-sigma uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Renormalisation-group-invariant light-quark current masses: $\hat m_{u=d} = 0.0068$ GeV, $\hat m_s = 0.168$ GeV
- Renormalisation-group-invariant heavy-quark current masses: $\hat m_c = 1.45$ GeV, $\hat m_b = 6.30$ GeV
- $K^+ \to \pi^0$ form factor at zero momentum transfer: $f_+^{K_u^u}(0) = 0.964$, with average from continuum methods of $0.971(9)$
- $D^0 \to \pi^-$ branching fractions: $2.70(12) \times 10^{-3}$ for $e^+ \nu_e$, $2.66(12) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\mu^+ \nu_\mu$, ratio $0.987(02)$
- $D_s^+ \to K^0$ branching fractions: $2.73(12) \times 10^{-3}$ for $e^+ \nu_e$, $2.68(12) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\mu^+ \nu_\mu$, ratio $0.982(01)$
- $D^0 \to K^-$ branching fractions: $39.0(1.7) \times 10^{-3}$ for $e^+ \nu_e$, $38.1(1.7) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\mu^+ \nu_\mu$, ratio $0.977(01)$
- $\bar B^0 \to \pi^+$ branching fractions: $0.162(44) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\mu^- \bar\nu_\mu$, $0.120(35) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\tau^- \bar\nu_\tau$, ratio $0.733(02)$
- $\bar B_s^0 \to K^+$ branching fractions: $0.186(53) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\mu^- \bar\nu_\mu$, $0.125(37) \times 10^{-3}$ for $\tau^- \bar\nu_\tau$, ratio $0.667(09)$
- Extracted CKM matrix element: $|V_{cs}| = 0.974(10)$

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function methods previously applied to hadron properties from light pseudoscalar mesons to triply heavy baryons (Ding 2018, Binosi 2018, Qin 2019, Yao 2020, Yao 2021), now providing a unified treatment of semileptonic transitions across the full pseudoscalar meson sector. The results provide benchmarks for the hitherto unmeasured $D_s \to K^0$ and $\bar B_s \to K^+$ form factors, and the predictions for branching fraction ratios can be used to test lepton flavour universality. The analysis also enables refined determinations of CKM matrix elements, particularly noting that further refined measurements of $B_s^0 \to K^- \mu^+ \nu_\mu$ would be useful for a more accurate value of $|V_{ub}|$.
