# Neutrino propagation in binary neutron star mergers in presence of nonstandard interactions

> We explore the impact of nonstandard interactions on neutrino propagation in accretion disks around binary neutron star mergers remnants. We show flavor evolution can be significantly modified even for values of the nonstandard couplings well below current bounds. We demonstrate the occurrence of I resonances as synchronized MSW phenomena and show that intricate conversion patterns might appear depending on the nonstandard interaction parameters. We discuss the possible implications for nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nonstandard neutrino-matter interactions (NSI) can significantly modify flavor evolution in binary neutron star merger accretion disks even for coupling values well below current experimental bounds, and demonstrates that the I resonance can occur as a synchronized MSW phenomenon when neutrino self-interactions dominate, contrary to previous assumptions that self-interactions have negligible effects on the I resonance location.

## Method

The authors performed two-flavor neutrino evolution simulations using matter density profiles and electron fractions from detailed three-dimensional Newtonian simulations of binary neutron star merger remnants (Perego 2014), with neutrinos emitted as Fermi-Dirac distributions from flavor-dependent neutrinospheres. They employed the single-angle approximation for neutrino self-interactions and varied the diagonal NSI parameter δϵⁿ in the range [-0.9, -0.7] and the off-diagonal parameter ε₀ below 10⁻³, using both normal and inverted mass ordering with fixed oscillation parameters from Patrignani (2016). The analysis used the SU(2) isospin formalism to describe collective neutrino modes and identify synchronized MSW effects.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mass-squared differences used: δm² = 2.43 × 10⁻³ eV² (normal ordering) and δm² = -2.38 × 10⁻³ eV² (inverted ordering)
- Mixing angles: sin²2θ = 0.087 (normal ordering) and sin²2θ = 0.092 (inverted ordering)
- Neutrinosphere radii: R(νₑ) = 84 km, R(ν̄ₑ) = 60 km, R(νₓ) = 58 km, taken from Frensel (2016) at 100 ms after merger
- Average neutrino energies: ⟨E(νₑ)⟩ = 10.6 MeV, ⟨E(ν̄ₑ)⟩ = 15.3 MeV, ⟨E(νₓ)⟩ = 17.3 MeV
- Neutrino luminosities: L(νₑ) = 15 × 10⁵¹ erg/s, L(ν̄ₑ) = 30 × 10⁵¹ erg/s, L(νₓ) = 8 × 10⁵¹ erg/s, from Perego (2014)
- NSI parameter bounds used: |δϵⁿ| ≲ O(10) and |ε₀| ≲ O(1), with ε₀ restricted to values smaller than 10⁻³ in simulations
- I resonance observed at approximately 1 km from the neutrinosphere in one example with δϵⁿ = -0.88

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino flavor evolution in compact object mergers (Malkus 2012, Malkus 2015, Zhu 2016, Frensel 2016) by incorporating nonstandard neutrino-matter interactions, and builds on the I resonance concept first identified in supernova contexts (EstebanPretel 2007, EstebanPretel 2009, Stapleford 2016). The paper demonstrates that NSI can shift the location and adiabaticity of matter-neutrino resonances (MNRs) and produce I resonances as synchronized MSW phenomena even when self-interactions dominate, which was not previously recognized. The results enable future studies of how NSI-modified flavor conversion affects the electron fraction Ye and r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds from binary neutron star mergers, following the nucleosynthesis framework of Martin (2015).
