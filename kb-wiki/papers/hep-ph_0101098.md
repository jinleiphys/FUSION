# Large-Nc QCD meets Regge theory: the example of spin-one two-point functions

> We discuss the phenomenological implications of assuming a Veneziano-type spectrum for the vector and axial-vector two-point functions in QCD at large Nc. We also compare the phenomenological results with those of Lowest-Meson Dominance, and find that they agree.

## Key claim

The authors propose that assuming a Veneziano-type (equal-spacing) spectrum for the vector and axial-vector meson towers in large-Nc QCD, while treating the rho meson and pion as separate states outside these towers, yields phenomenological predictions consistent with experiment and with the Lowest-Meson Dominance (LMD) approximation, provided the rho decay constant is taken around 130 MeV rather than its experimental value of 153 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the vector and axial-vector two-point functions in the large-Nc and chiral limits using an infinite tower of resonances with masses obeying M^2_V,A(n) = m^2_V,A + n Lambda^2_V,A, plus separate contributions from the rho meson and the pion. They match the large-Q^2 expansion of these spectral functions to the operator product expansion (OPE), imposing constraints from the parton-model logarithm, the absence of dimension-two operators, the gluon condensate, and the four-quark condensate, then solve for the tower parameters and decay constants using input values for F_pi, M_rho, and a range for F_rho.

## Key numbers

- F_pi = 93 MeV (also considered 87 MeV as the chiral-limit value)
- M_rho = 770 MeV (input)
- F_rho = 130 MeV (preferred value; experimental value is 153 +/- 4 MeV)
- For F_rho = 130 MeV and F_pi = 93 MeV: F_V = 130 MeV, m_V = 1.42 GeV, Lambda_V = 1.17 GeV
- For F_rho = 130 MeV and F_pi = 93 MeV: F_A = 170 MeV, m_A = 1.35 GeV, Lambda_A = 1.50 GeV
- Predicted gluon condensate alpha_s <G^2> = 0.02 GeV^4 (for F_pi = 93 MeV)
- Predicted four-quark condensate pi alpha_s <psi-bar psi>^2 = 9 x 10^{-4} GeV^6 (for F_pi = 93 MeV)
- Predicted pion mass difference m_pi+ - m_pi0 = 4.7 MeV (experimental: 4.5936 +/- 0.0005 MeV)
- Predicted L_10(mu = M_rho) = -5.6 x 10^{-3} (experimental: -5.13 +/- 0.19 x 10^{-3})
- Predicted rho' mass = 1.4 GeV (experimental: 1.47 GeV), rho'' = 1.8 GeV (experimental: 1.70 GeV), rho''' = 2.2 GeV (experimental: 2.15 GeV)
- Predicted a_1 mass = 1.3 GeV (experimental: 1.23 GeV), a_1' = 2.0 GeV (experimental: 1.64 or 1.8 GeV)
- Daughter trajectory slope a = 0.7 in M^2_V(n) = M^2_rho' (1 + a n)
- Q_7^{3/2}(mu = 2 GeV) = -0.021 (GeV)^4 for F_pi = 93 MeV (ES); -0.024 (GeV)^4 (LMD)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bramon, Etim and Greco (1970s) and Sakurai (1970s), who used a Veneziano-type spectrum including the rho as part of the tower, and of Geshkenbein (1980s), who considered infinite resonances matching the OPE. It differs by treating the rho meson as separate from the equally spaced tower, avoiding the negative gluon condensate that arises when the rho is included. The results are compared with the Lowest-Meson Dominance (LMD) approximation of previous work by the same authors, finding agreement within large-Nc uncertainties. The equal-spacing ansatz enables predictions for the resonance spacing parameters Lambda_V and Lambda_A and for the weak matrix element Q_7^{3/2}, lending support to the robustness of such sum-rule approaches.
