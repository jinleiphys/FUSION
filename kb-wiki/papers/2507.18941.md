# Effect of eigenstates on spectra in coupled-channel scattering with the chiral unitary model

> In recent years, as experimental data on the excited $Ξ(1620)$ and $Ξ(1690)$ states have accumulated, theoretical analyses based on chiral dynamics have also been actively pursued. In this study, we construct theoretical models within the chiral unitary approach to reproduce recent experimental data, and perform a model interpolation to examine the relationship between the poles appearing in different models. We then calculate the invariant mass spectra of the $π^+Ξ^-$ system in the $Ξ_c \to ππΞ$ decay, using the scattering amplitudes obtained from each model, to investigate how the distinct pole structures influence the observable spectrum.

## Key claim

The poles corresponding to the $\Xi(1620)$ resonance in two different chiral unitary models (Model 1 based on Belle data and Model 2 based on ALICE data) originate from different physical mechanisms, as continuous interpolation between the models shows the poles remain on distinct Riemann sheets and cannot be smoothly connected.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral unitary approach, solving the coupled-channel scattering equation with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel and dimensionally regularized loop functions. They construct two models by adjusting subtraction constants: Model 1 reproduces the Belle-reported mass and decay width of $\Xi(1620)$, while Model 2 reproduces the ALICE-determined $K^-\Lambda$ scattering length. A linear interpolation between the subtraction constants of the two models is performed, and the resulting scattering amplitudes are used to calculate the $\pi^+\Xi^-$ invariant mass distribution in the three-body decay $\Xi_c \to \pi\pi\Xi$.

## Key numbers

- Pole $z_1$ (Model 1) on the [bbtttt] Riemann sheet at $1610 - 30i$ MeV
- Pole $z_2$ (Model 2) on the [ttbttt] Riemann sheet at $1726 + 80i$ MeV
- Peak position in the $\pi^+\Xi^-$ invariant mass distribution for Model 1 ($x=0$) is $1599$ MeV, lower than the pole real part of $1610$ MeV
- Peak position in the two-body scattering amplitude for Model 1 is $1606$ MeV
- The real part of the $\bar{K}^0\Lambda$ scattering length changes sign from positive to negative around $x \sim 0.375$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nishibuchi 2023) which constructed two models for $\Xi(1620)$ based on Belle and ALICE data but only calculated two-body scattering amplitudes. The present analysis enables direct comparison with the experimentally observed $\Xi_c \to \pi\pi\Xi$ decay spectrum from Belle (2019). The finding that threshold effects are stronger in three-body decays than in two-body scattering indicates that future theoretical analyses of the Belle data must properly account for these effects in the three-body decay amplitude.
