# Nuclear Pairing from Two-body Microscopic Forces: Analysis of the Cooper Pair Wavefunctions

> In a recent paper we studied the behavior of the pairing gaps $Δ_F$ as a function of the Fermi momentum $k_F$ for neutron and nuclear matter in all relevant angular momentum channels where superfluidity is believed to naturally emerge. The calculations employed realistic chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials with the inclusion of three-body forces and self-energy effects. In this contribution, after a detailed description of the numerical method we employed in the solution of the BCS equations, we will show a preliminary analysis of the Cooper pair wavefunctions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a preliminary analysis of Cooper pair wavefunctions in neutron and nuclear matter, showing that the BCS-BEC crossover behavior depends strongly on the angular momentum channel and is ruled out for the triplet \(^3\)PF\(_2\) channel, while it is supported for the singlet \(^1\)S\(_0\) and triplet \(^3\)SD\(_1\) channels, but only at densities below a certain Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation using Khodel’s method with partial-wave decomposition, employing a chiral nucleon-nucleon potential at N3LO. They computed the pairing gap \(\Delta_F\) as a function of Fermi momentum \(k_F\) for neutron and symmetric nuclear matter in the \(^1\)S\(_0\), \(^3\)SD\(_1\), and \(^3\)PF\(_2\) channels, then calculated the Cooper pair wavefunction and probability density \(\rho(r)\) from the gap solutions. The numerical method was benchmarked against the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel in neutron matter and tested for stability against variations in Gaussian integration points, momentum cutoff, and a scale factor \(\delta\).

## Key numbers

*   For the singlet \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel in neutron matter, the crossover region is found at densities below \(k_F \simeq 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), while higher densities belong to the pure BCS region.
*   The numerical method was tested with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_k = 4.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), a scale factor \(\delta = 1\times10^{-10}\ \text{MeV}\), and \(n_{gauss} = 200\) Gaussian integration points.
*   The method is stable when using at least 100 Gaussian points and a momentum cutoff of at least \(4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pairing gaps from the authors’ previous paper (Finelli, Maurizio, and Holt, 2014) by adding a preliminary study of Cooper pair wavefunctions. It confirms the spatial correlation behavior reported by Matsuo (2006) for the singlet channel using phenomenological interactions, now with microscopic chiral forces. The results enable future studies of the BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear systems, including the effects of three-body forces and self-energy corrections.
