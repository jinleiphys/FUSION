# Quarkonium production via recombination

> The contrast between model predictions for the transverse momentum spectra of J/Psi observed in Au-Au collisions at RHIC is extended to include effects of nuclear absorption. We find that the difference between initial production and recombination is enhanced in the most central collisions. Models utilizing a combination of these sources may eventually be able to place constraints on their relative magnitudes.

## Key claim

The difference between model predictions for J/ψ production via initial production versus in-medium recombination is enhanced in the most central Au-Au collisions at RHIC when nuclear absorption effects are included, but the lack of independent information on the initial temperature parameter prevents a definitive statement about which mechanism dominates.

## Method

The author calculates J/ψ transverse momentum spectra using a kinetic model that includes both dissociation and recombination of charm quark pairs in a deconfined medium, with charm quark momentum distributions from pQCD. The model incorporates initial-state transverse momentum broadening via a random-walk picture parameterized by ⟨k_T²⟩, and extends previous work by including nuclear absorption effects on directly-produced J/ψ using an effective absorption cross section in a path-length geometry.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T₀ = 500 MeV and N_ccbar = 10 initially-produced ccbar pairs for central collisions used in the recombination model
- ⟨p_T²⟩ for diagonal ccbar pairs = 0.8 + 4⟨k_T²⟩ GeV²
- ⟨p_T²⟩ for off-diagonal ccbar pairs = 4.9 + 2⟨k_T²⟩ GeV²
- ⟨p_T²⟩ for in-medium J/ψ formation = 2.4 + ⟨k_T²⟩ GeV²
- ⟨k_T²⟩_pp = 0.5 ± 0.1 GeV² extracted from pp J/ψ production data
- λ² = 0.56 ± 0.08 GeV² extracted from nuclear broadening measurements in d-Au interactions
- For finite nuclear absorption, N_n ≈ (1 + 0.2β)(n-1)/2 for 0 ≤ β ≤ 1.5, where β ≡ ρσL_max

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier recombination model (Thews 2000) and compares with PHENIX measurements of J/ψ R_AA and transverse momentum spectra at RHIC. The results contrast with the sequential suppression scenario proposed by Satz (2005) and Karsch (2005) in which observed suppression is entirely due to dissociation of ψ′ and χ_c states. The centrality dependence of ⟨p_T²⟩ provides a potential observable to distinguish between initial production and recombination mechanisms, though the author notes that preliminary PHENIX data may already be difficult to reconcile with the nuclear absorption predictions.
