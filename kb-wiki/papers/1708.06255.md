# Causality and existence of solutions of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics with gravity

> A new approach is described to help improve the foundations of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics and its coupling to general relativity. Focusing on neutral conformal fluids constructed solely in terms of hydrodynamic variables, we derive the most general viscous energy-momentum tensor yielding equations of motion of second order in the derivatives, which is shown to provide a novel type of generalization of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations for which causality holds. We show how this energy-momentum tensor may be derived from conformal kinetic theory. We rigorously prove existence, uniqueness, and causality of solutions of this theory (in the full nonlinear regime) both in a Minkowski background and also when the fluid is dynamically coupled to Einstein's equations. Linearized disturbances around equilibrium in Minkowski spacetime are stable in this causal theory. A numerical study reveals the presence of an out-of-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractor for a rapidly expanding fluid. Further properties are also studied and a brief discussion of how this approach can be generalized to non-conformal fluids is presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims to present the first theory of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics that simultaneously satisfies causality, local well-posedness (existence and uniqueness of solutions in the full nonlinear regime), linear stability around equilibrium, derivability from kinetic theory, and the second law of thermodynamics, but only for neutral conformal fluids constructed solely from hydrodynamic variables and with equations of motion truncated at second order in derivatives.

## Method

The authors derive the most general viscous energy-momentum tensor for a neutral conformal fluid that yields equations of motion of second order in derivatives, using only hydrodynamic variables and imposing conformal symmetry. They then prove local well-posedness and causality of the resulting system of partial differential equations both in Minkowski background and when dynamically coupled to Einstein's equations, using standard techniques for symmetric hyperbolic systems. The energy-momentum tensor is also shown to be derivable from conformal kinetic theory, and a numerical study of rapidly expanding fluid is performed to investigate the presence of an out-of-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractor.

## Key numbers

* The theory in Minkowski background is determined by four evolution equations of second order, which can be rewritten as eight first order evolution equations; for comparison, conformal Israel-Stewart theory has nine equations of motion.
* From a computational point of view, the complexity of the new theory is reduced to four first order equations, hence simpler than Israel-Stewart theory.
* Linearized disturbances around equilibrium in Minkowski spacetime are stable in this causal theory.

## Context

This work extends and improves upon the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations of Eckart (1940) and Landau and Lifshitz (1959), which were shown to be acausal by Hiscock and Lindblom (1985). It also addresses open questions in the Israel-Stewart theory (1976), the resummed BRSSS theory of Baier et al. (2008), and the DNMR equations of Denicol et al. (2012), where causality had only been established for linear disturbances around equilibrium and for certain values of dynamic variables, and where existence and uniqueness of solutions (including coupling to gravity) remained unproven. The new theory enables rigorous numerical simulations of relativistic viscous fluids in contexts such as heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers, where previous theories lacked guarantees of well-posedness.
