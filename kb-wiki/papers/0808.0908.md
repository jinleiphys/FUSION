# Systematic Comparison of Jet Energy-Loss Schemes in a realistic hydrodynamic medium

> We perform a systematic comparison of three different jet energy-loss approaches. These include the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann scheme based on the approach of Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff and Zakharov (BDMPS-Z/ASW), the Higher Twist approach (HT) and a scheme based on the approach of Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY). In this comparison, an identical medium evolution will be utilized for all three approaches: not only does this entail the use of the same realistic three-dimensional relativistic fluid dynamics (RFD) simulation, but also includes the use of identical initial parton-distribution functions and final fragmentation functions. We are, thus, in a unique position, not only to isolate fundamental differences between the various approaches, but also to make rigorous calculations for different experimental measurements using "state of the art" components. All three approaches are reduced to a version which contains only one free tunable parameter, this is then related to the well known transport parameter $\hat{q}$. We find that the parameters of all three calculations can be adjusted to provide a good description of inclusive data on $R_{AA}$ versus transverse momentum. However, we do observe slight differences in their predictions for the centrality and azimuthal angular dependence of $R_{AA}$ vs. $p_T$. We also note that the value of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in the three approaches to describe the data differs significantly.

## Key claim

All three jet energy-loss schemes (BDMPS-Z/ASW, HT, and AMY) can be tuned to reproduce inclusive nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) data versus transverse momentum, but they yield significantly different values of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and show slight differences in predictions for the centrality and azimuthal angular dependence of $R_{AA}$.

## Method

The authors coupled three distinct jet energy-loss formalisms (BDMPS-Z/ASW, HT, and AMY) to an identical three-dimensional relativistic fluid dynamics (RFD) simulation of the medium produced at RHIC, using the same initial parton-distribution functions and final fragmentation functions for all three approaches. Each formalism was reduced to a version containing only one free tunable parameter, which was then related to the transport parameter $\hat{q}$. The calculations were compared to experimental data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ versus transverse momentum $p_T$ for pions produced in gold-gold collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=200$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic expansion was assumed to start at $\tau_0=0.6$ fm, with initial energy density and baryon number density parametrized by a combination of wounded nuclear model and binary collision model.
- The analysis focused on hadrons with $p_T$ above 6 GeV, where vacuum fragmentation functions are applicable and recombination effects are negligible.
- The characteristic frequency of radiation in the ASW scheme is given by $\omega_c = \int d\zeta \zeta \hat{q}(\zeta)$, and for a static medium with large length, the radiation distribution scales as $\sqrt{\omega_c/(2\omega)}$ for $\omega < \omega_c$ and as $(1/12)(\omega/\omega_c)^2$ for $\omega > \omega_c$.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Hirano and Nara (2002) and Hirano et al. (2003) that first incorporated jet energy loss in three-dimensional RFD simulations but used simplified energy-loss formalisms. The paper enables direct comparison of the BDMPS-Z/ASW, HT, and AMY approaches under identical medium conditions, isolating differences due to the energy-loss schemes themselves rather than differing treatments of the medium evolution. This comparison allows future work to determine which formalism best describes experimental data on the centrality and azimuthal angular dependence of $R_{AA}$, and to extract consistent values of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ from RHIC measurements.
