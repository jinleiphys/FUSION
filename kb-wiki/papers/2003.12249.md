# Light Meson Parton Distribution Functions from Basis Light-Front Quantization and QCD Evolution

> We investigate the parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the pion and kaon from the eigenstates of a light-front effective Hamiltonian in the constituent quark-antiquark representation suitable for low-momentum scale applications. By taking these scales as the only free parameters, the valence quark distribution functions of the pion, after QCD evolving, are consistent with the E615 experiment at Fermilab. In addition, the ratio of the up quark distribution in the kaon to that in the pion also agrees with the NA3 experimental result at CERN.

## Key claim
The valence quark distribution functions of the pion and kaon, computed from basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) eigenstates and evolved via DGLAP equations, agree with experimental data from E615 (Fermilab) and NA3 (CERN) only when the initial low-momentum scales are treated as free parameters; the agreement improves at higher perturbative orders (NNLO) as quantified by reduced chi-squared values.

## Method
The authors solved the light-front Schrodinger equation using an effective Hamiltonian that includes kinetic terms, transverse and longitudinal confining potentials from light-front holographic QCD, and a color-singlet Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interaction for chiral dynamics. Valence parton distribution functions (PDFs) were extracted from the resulting light-front wave functions at a low model scale, then evolved to experimental scales (16 GeV^2 for pion, 20 GeV^2 for kaon) using the DGLAP equations at LO, NLO, and NNLO via the HOPPET toolkit.

## Key numbers
- Initial scale for pion PDF at NNLO: mu^2_{0,pi} = 0.240 +/- 0.024 GeV^2
- Initial scale for kaon PDF at NNLO: mu^2_{0,K} = 0.246 +/- 0.024 GeV^2
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for pion PDF compared to E615 experiment: 6.71 (LO), 4.67 (NLO), 3.64 (NNLO)
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for kaon-to-pion u-quark ratio compared to NA3 experiment: 0.88 (LO), 0.56 (NLO), 0.50 (NNLO)
- Pion valence PDF behavior at large x: (1-x)^1.44
- First moment contributions for pion at 4 GeV^2: valence 0.489, gluon 0.398, sea 0.113

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to light mesons by Jia (2018) and Lan (2019) by computing PDFs and performing QCD evolution. The results are compared with the E615 experiment at Fermilab (Conway, 1989) and the NA3 experiment at CERN (Badier, 1983), as well as with the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) model of Ding (2019) and the GRS model of Gluck (1997). The approach enables first-principles nonperturbative calculations of meson structure that can be tested against experimental Drell-Yan data.
