# Speed of sound exceeding the conformal bound in dense 2-color QCD

> We review recent works on the Monte Carlo simulations of dense two-color QCD (QC$_2$D) by focusing on the phase diagram, the equation of state, and the sound velocity at nonzero quark chemical potential. A possible upper bound of the sound velocity is known as the conformal bound, namely, $c_s^2/c^2 \leq 1/3$. The sound velocity is below the bound at least in the case of finite-temperature QCD. However, our recent work~\cite{Iida:2022hyy} shows the breaking of this bound in dense QC$_2$D. This phenomenon was previously unknown from any lattice calculations. We also discuss recent related works including lattice studies on QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential, some effective model analyses, and an analysis based on recent neutron star observations. These works also suggest the breaking of the conformal bound.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the speed of sound squared in dense two-color QCD (QC₂D) exceeds the conformal bound of \(c_s^2/c^2 \leq 1/3\), a phenomenon previously unknown from any lattice calculations, but this result is obtained at a fixed temperature of \(T \approx 79\) MeV rather than at zero temperature, and the authors note that the standard thermodynamic definition of sound velocity requires evaluation at constant entropy rather than constant temperature.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of two-color QCD using the Rational Hybrid Monte Carlo (RHMC) algorithm with an extended Dirac matrix that includes a diquark source term to overcome the onset problem in the superfluid phase. They used Iwasaki gauge action with parameters \((\beta, \kappa, N_s, N_\tau) = (0.80, 0.159, 16, 16)\) corresponding to lattice spacing \(a \approx 0.17\) fm and temperature \(T \approx 79\) MeV, with a pseudo-scalar meson mass \(m_{PS} \approx 750\) MeV. The pressure was obtained by integrating the quark number density, and the trace anomaly was computed using non-perturbative beta-functions evaluated at \(\mu = T = 0\) along the line of constant physics, from which the energy density and sound velocity were derived.

## Key numbers

* Sound velocity squared exceeds the conformal bound \(c_s^2/c^2 = 1/3\) in dense QC₂D at \(T \approx 79\) MeV, with the excess occurring at \(\mu/m_{PS}\) values where the ChPT prediction fails.
* The pressure at the highest density studied reaches \(p/p_{SB} = 0.84\) of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, where \(p_{SB}(\mu) = N_f N_c \mu^4/(12\pi^2)\) in the continuum.
* The simulation parameters correspond to \(a \approx 0.17\) fm, \(T \approx 79\) MeV, and \(m_{PS} \approx 750\) MeV, with \(a\mu \leq 0.8\) and \(a\Delta\mu = 0.05\) between simulation points.
* The non-perturbative beta-function coefficients at \((\beta, \kappa) = (0.80, 0.159)\) are \(a d\beta/da = -0.352\) and \(a d\kappa/da = 0.0282\).

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of dense two-color QCD by Itou and collaborators (2019-2022) that established the phase diagram including hadronic, BEC, and BCS phases, and provides the first lattice evidence for violation of the conformal bound conjectured by Cherman et al. (2009). The results are consistent with Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions by Son (2001) and Hands et al. (2006) near the phase transition, but show deviation at higher densities. The finding parallels recent lattice results for three-color QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potential by Brandt et al. (2022) and Abbott et al. (2023), and is consistent with neutron star observational analyses by Brandes et al. (2023) that suggest stiff equations of state. Future work requires finer lattices or lighter quark masses to probe higher densities, and investigation of the temperature-dependent difference between constant-temperature and constant-entropy definitions of sound velocity.
