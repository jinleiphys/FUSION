# The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model: 'quasi-local' quantum criticality in nuclear physics

> Motivated by recent work on local quantum criticality in condensed matter systems, we study the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model of nuclear physics as a simple model of a kind of 'quasi-local' quantum criticality. We identify a new crossover temperature, T*(V,W), between linear and nonlinear dynamics, which is analogous to the crossover between the renormalized classical and quantum critical regimes in the condensed-matter case. This temperature T* typically vanishes logarithmically as the quantum phase transition is approached, except near the quantum tricritical point where it becomes linear. We also note a further analogy with condensed-matter quantum criticality: the LMG model exhibits quantum order-by-disorder phenomena, of the type often associated with phase reconstruction near quantum critical points.

## Key claim

The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model exhibits a crossover temperature \(T^{*}(V,W)\) between linear and nonlinear dynamics that vanishes logarithmically as the quantum phase transition is approached, except near the quantum tricritical point where it becomes linear; this is analogous to the crossover between renormalized classical and quantum critical regimes in condensed-matter systems, but the model's "quasi-local" nature means the coherence length \(\xi\) is large but does not diverge at the quantum critical point.

## Method

The authors studied the LMG model in the large-\(N_p\) (finite-size but infinite-range) limit using a Holstein-Primakoff boson representation of the pseudospin operators, with the spin fixed at \(J = N_p/2\). They derived the boson Hamiltonian, performed a Bogolyubov transformation to eliminate anomalous terms, and computed the thermal average \(\langle b^\dagger b \rangle_T\) from the Bose-Einstein distribution. The crossover temperature \(T^{*}\) was obtained by solving the condition \(\langle b^\dagger b \rangle_T = N_p\), i.e., when the number of thermal excitations equals the size of the coherent patch.

## Key numbers

- The crossover temperature \(T^{*}\) is given by \(T^{*} = \frac{\varepsilon}{k_B} \frac{\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}{2} \left[ \atanh \left( \frac{\sqrt{\gamma^2-1}}{\gamma} \frac{y}{\sqrt{y^2-x^2}} \right) \right]^{-1}\), where \(x \equiv V/\varepsilon\), \(y \equiv 1 + W/\varepsilon\), and \(\gamma \equiv \left( 1 - 1/(2N_p+1)^2 \right)^{-1/2}\).
- Near the renormalized transition line \(y = \gamma x\) (with \(y = \gamma x + \delta\), \(0 < \delta \ll 1\), \(x \gg \delta\)), \(T^{*} \approx \frac{\varepsilon}{k_B} \frac{x \sqrt{\gamma^2 - 1}}{\ln (2\gamma(\gamma^2-1)x) - \ln \delta} \sim -1/\ln \delta\) as \(\delta \to 0^{+}\).
- Near the tricritical point \((x,y) = (0,0)\) (setting \(x=0\), \(0 < y \ll 1\)), \(T^{*} \approx \frac{\varepsilon}{k_B} \frac{y}{2} \left[ \atanh \left( \frac{\sqrt{\gamma^2-1}}{\gamma} \right) \right]^{-1} \sim y\) as \(y \to 0^{+}\).
- The transition from phase I to the singlet phase at \(V=0\) occurs when \(W/\varepsilon = N_p/2\); for non-zero \(V\), the critical value shifts to \(W_c = \frac{\varepsilon N_p}{2} + \frac{V^2}{\varepsilon N_p} \left( 1 - \frac{1}{N_p} \right)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of the LMG model, originally introduced by Fallieros (1959) and developed by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965), to the full \((V,W)\)-plane at non-zero temperature with large but finite \(N_p\). It draws analogies to local quantum criticality in condensed matter, specifically the neutron-scattering measurements on CeCu\(_{6-x}\)Au\(_x\) by Schroeder (1998) and the theories of Si (1999) and Coleman (1999), as well as the tricriticality and phase reconstruction near ferromagnetic quantum critical points discussed by Belitz, Kirkpatrick, and Vojta (1997). The results enable controlled analytic study of quasi-local quantum criticality and identify a new crossover scale in the Dicke model (equivalent to the \(V=0\) line).
