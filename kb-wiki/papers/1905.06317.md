# Extrapolation of nuclear structure observables with artificial neural networks

> Calculations of nuclei are often carried out in finite model spaces. Thus, finite-size corrections enter, and it is necessary to extrapolate the computed observables to infinite model spaces. In this work, we employ extrapolation methods based on artificial neural networks for observables such as the ground-state energy and the point-proton radius. We extrapolate results from no-core shell model and coupled-cluster calculations to very large model spaces and estimate uncertainties. Training the network on different data typically yields extrapolation results that cluster around distinct values. We show that a preprocessing of input data, and the inclusion of correlations among the input data, reduces the problem of multiple solutions and yields more stable extrapolated results and consistent uncertainty estimates. We perform extrapolations for ground-state energies and radii in $^{4}$He, $^{6}$Li, and $^{16}$O, and compare the predictions from neural networks with results from infrared extrapolations.

## Key claim

Artificial neural networks can reliably extrapolate no-core shell model and coupled-cluster results for ground-state energies and point-proton radii to infinite model spaces, but only when data preprocessing (Gaussian weighting near variational minima) and a correlated loss function are used to suppress the multi-modal solution distributions that otherwise arise from imbalanced training data.

## Method

The authors constructed feed-forward neural networks with a single hidden layer (8 nodes for energies, 16 nodes for radii) using a sigmoid activation function. They trained on NCSM and CC results for \(^{4}\)He, \(^{6}\)Li, and \(^{16}\)O at finite \(N_{\rm max}\) and \(\hbar\omega\) values, augmented by quadratic spline interpolation in \(\hbar\omega\) at fixed \(N_{\rm max}\). A correlated loss function (tridiagonal block matrix for points interpolated at the same \(N_{\rm max}\)) and Gaussian sample weights centered on the variational minima were employed to address overfitting and imbalanced data. Extrapolations to \(N_{\rm max}=100\) were obtained from ensembles of 100 independently initialized networks, with the mean and standard deviation of the resulting Gaussian-fitted distribution taken as the central value and uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{4}\)He, neural network extrapolations converged after \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=16\) and agreed with infrared extrapolations from Forssen (2018) for both ground-state energy and point-proton radius.
- For \(^{6}\)Li ground-state energy with the largest dataset (\(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=22\)): \(E_{\rm{g.s.}} = -30.743 \pm 0.061\) MeV.
- For \(^{6}\)Li point-proton radius with \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=22\): \(r_{\rm{p}} = 2.471 \pm 0.028\) fm.
- For \(^{6}\)Li, neural network energy predictions began to converge at \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=16\); radius predictions began to converge at \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=20\).
- Uncertainty analysis for \(^{6}\)Li with loss doubled (\(c=2\)): standard deviation of energy predictions ranged from 0.006 MeV (at \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=22\)) to 0.013 MeV (at \(\rm{max}(N_{\rm max})=12\)); for radius, from 0.0034 fm to 0.0061 fm.
- Uncertainty analysis for \(^{6}\)Li with loss increased tenfold (\(c=10\)): standard deviation of energy predictions ranged from 0.016 MeV to 0.037 MeV; for radius, from 0.0034 fm to 0.0061 fm (values not explicitly given for radius at \(c=10\) in the truncated text, but the text states variances and maximal deviations increase by about \(\sqrt{5}\) relative to \(c=2\)).

## Context

This work extends the neural-network extrapolation approach introduced by Negoita (2018) for NCSM results, addressing the multi-mode solution problem that arose from random network initialization. The authors compare their neural network predictions to the infrared extrapolation formulas derived by Furnstahl (2012), Coon (2012), More (2013), and Forssen (2018), which are based on harmonic-oscillator basis cutoffs. The method enables more robust extrapolations for weakly bound nuclei like \(^{6}\)Li, where convergence is slow, and provides uncertainty estimates that complement existing empirical and IR-based schemes.
