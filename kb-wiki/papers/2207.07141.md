# Jet Broadening in Flowing Matter -- Resummation

> In this work, we obtain the leading corrections to the jet momentum broadening distribution in a QCD medium arising from the transverse flow of the matter. We first derive the single-particle propagator of a highly energetic parton resumming its multiple interactions with the homogeneous flowing matter, explicitly keeping the leading subeikonal flow terms. Then, we use this propagator to obtain the jet broadening distribution and its leading moments. We show that this distribution becomes anisotropic in the presence of transverse flow, since its odd moments are generally non-zero and proportional to the transverse velocity of the medium. Finally, we evaluate several odd moments, which we compare to the corresponding results at first order in opacity, showing that accounting for multiple in-medium scatterings is essential to describe some observables in dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading subeikonal corrections to the jet momentum broadening distribution in a QCD medium arising from transverse flow, showing that the distribution becomes anisotropic because odd moments become non-zero and proportional to the transverse velocity of the medium, but only when multiple in-medium scatterings are resummed (beyond first order in opacity).

## Method

The authors first derive the single-particle propagator of a highly energetic parton in homogeneous flowing matter by solving a differential equation that resums multiple interactions with the medium, keeping leading subeikonal flow terms of order \(\bot/E\). They then use this propagator to compute the jet broadening distribution by averaging over scattering centers using a Gaussian distribution for the background color field, following the Gyulassy-Wang model. The propagator is expanded into eikonal and subeikonal parts, where the eikonal part satisfies a differential equation with a velocity-dependent shift in transverse coordinates, and the subeikonal correction is expressed as an integral involving the eikonal propagator and flow-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

* The leading subeikonal corrections scale as \(\mathcal{O}(\bot/E)\), where \(\bot\) represents any relevant transverse scale and \(E\) is the parton energy.
* The interaction potential in the Gyulassy-Wang model is \(v(q) = g^2/(q^2 - \mu^2 + i\epsilon)\), where \(\mu\) is the Debye mass of the QGP.
* The color factor for the medium average is \(\mathcal{C} = C_{proj}/(2C_{\bar{R}})\), with \(C_{\bar{R}} = N_c\) for quarks and \(C_{\bar{R}} = C_F\) for gluons.
* The eikonal propagator in coordinate space includes a shift \(\vec{x} - (L - z_0)\vec{u}\) due to transverse flow velocity \(\vec{u}\).

## Context

This work extends the first-order-in-opacity calculation of flow effects on jet broadening by Sadofyev et al. (2021) to all orders in opacity using the BDMPS-Z framework, which resums multiple in-medium scatterings. It also builds on the treatment of transverse gradients of matter properties by Barata et al. (2022). The results enable future calculations of jet observables in dense nuclear matter where multiple scatterings are essential, and could be extended to cold nuclear matter with collective nucleon motion.
