# Combined Rastall and Rainbow theories of gravity with applications to neutron stars

> The possibility of modifications on general relativity is investigated. We propose an alternative theory of gravity constructed with the combination of Rastall and Rainbow theories. The hydrostatic equilibrium equations are obtained in order to test the new theory in neutron stars, whose mass-radius diagrams are obtained using modern equations of state of nuclear matter derived from relativistic mean field models and compared with the ones computed by the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. We conclude that substantial modifications are obtained even for very small alterations on the two free parameters, making the reproduction of astrophysical observations an easy task.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that combining Rastall and Rainbow modifications to general relativity allows neutron star models to simultaneously increase maximum mass and decrease canonical star radius, making it "an easy task" to reproduce astrophysical observations, but only for very small deviations from general relativity (within approximately 0.1% for the Rastall parameter λ and with the Rainbow parameter Σ > 1).

## Method

The authors derive modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equilibrium equations by incorporating an energy-dependent metric from Rainbow gravity into the Rastall-modified Einstein field equations, where the energy-momentum tensor's covariant derivative depends on the curvature scalar. They then solve these equations numerically using three relativistic mean-field equations of state (IU-FSU, QMC, and QMCωρ) to compute mass-radius relations for neutron stars, varying the Rastall parameter λ and the Rainbow function Σ independently and in combination.

## Key numbers

- IU-FSU model: maximum mass 1.94 M⊙ and radius 12.55 km for the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star under general relativity; with RR theory (Σ=1.05, λ=0.999) maximum mass increases to 2.03 M⊙ while canonical radius becomes 12.76 km
- QMC model: maximum mass 2.14 M⊙ and canonical radius 13.61 km under general relativity; with RR theory (Σ=1.05, λ=0.999) maximum mass reaches 2.24 M⊙ and canonical radius 13.80 km
- QMCωρ model: maximum mass 2.07 M⊙ and canonical radius 10.96 km under general relativity; with RR theory (Σ=1.05, λ=0.999) maximum mass becomes 2.17 M⊙ and canonical radius 11.36 km
- The Rastall parameter λ cannot assume values in the interval 1/2 < λ < 2/3 because stellar mass becomes negative
- For λ = 1.006 with the QMC EoS, the canonical 1.4 M⊙ star radius becomes 20.84 km, deemed unreasonable

## Context

This work extends the separate investigations of Rastall gravity applied to neutron stars by Fabris (who found only small deviations from GR are consistent) and Rainbow gravity by Garattini and Hendi (who found maximum masses above 2 M⊙ are easily attainable). The combined Rastall-Rainbow theory enables all three tested equations of state (IU-FSU, QMC, QMCωρ) to simultaneously satisfy the observational constraints of maximum mass above 2 M⊙ and canonical star radii within the 10.5–13.4 km range from the GW170817 observation, which individual models could not achieve alone. The authors suggest this framework could also address the hyperon puzzle, where including hyperons softens the equation of state and reduces maximum masses below 2 M⊙.
