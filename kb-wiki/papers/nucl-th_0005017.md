# Pion Production Model - Connection between Dynamics and Quark Models

> We discuss the difficulties in testing the hadron models by using the N^* parameters extracted from the empirical amplitude analyses of the pi-N and gamma-N reaction data. As an alternative or perhaps a more advantageous approach, we present a Hamiltonian formulation that can relate the pion production dynamics and the constituent quark models of N^* structure. The application of the approach in investigating the Delta and N^*(S_{11}) excitations is reviewed. It is found that while the Delta excitation can be described satisfactory, the pi-N scattering in S_{11} channel can not be described by the constituent quark models based on either the one-gluon-exchange or one-meson-exchange mechanisms. A phenomenological quark-quark potential has been constructed to reproduce the S_{11} amplitude.

## Key claim

The authors argue that comparing resonance parameters extracted from empirical amplitude analyses (e.g., Particle Data Group values) with perturbative decay widths from constituent quark models is misleading because the extracted parameters inherently contain non-resonant contributions; they present a Hamiltonian formulation that nonperturbatively relates pion production dynamics to quark-model predictions, finding that while the Delta excitation can be described satisfactorily, the S11 channel cannot be described by either one-gluon-exchange or one-meson-exchange constituent quark models.

## Method

The authors develop a Hamiltonian formulation where baryon wavefunctions and masses are obtained by solving a three-quark bound state problem with a constituent quark Hamiltonian (kinetic energy, linear confinement, and residual quark-quark interaction). They then define meson-quark and photon-quark vertex operators from the nonrelativistic limit of Feynman amplitudes and construct a hadronic Hamiltonian for pi-N and gamma-N reactions. Using unitary transformation up to second order, they derive an effective Hamiltonian with non-resonant potentials and dressed vertices, leading to coupled equations (Eqs. 18-24) that relate the full scattering amplitude nonperturbatively to the quark wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The predicted helicity amplitudes for the Delta -> N gamma transition are A_{3/2} ~ -160 x 10^{-3} (GeV)^{-1/2} (with A_{1/2} = A_{3/2}/sqrt(3)), about 40% lower than PDG values.
- The bare mass of the Delta required to fit the SL model is m_Delta = 1300 MeV.
- The pi qq coupling constant is fixed as f_{pi qq}/m_pi = (3/5)(g_{pi NN}/2M_N) using the empirical value g_{pi NN}^2/4pi = 14.
- The MIT-Bates data for asymmetry T_{TL} of the p(e,e'pi^0) reaction were measured at Q^2 = 0.126 (GeV/c)^2.
- Basis states up to 11 hbar omega were included in the diagonalization of the three-quark bound state problem.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical SL model developed by the same authors (Sato and Lee, prior work) for Delta excitation to a general Hamiltonian formulation that can test constituent quark models against pi-N and gamma-N reaction data. It contradicts the common practice of comparing perturbative quark model decay widths with PDG values, noting that such comparisons are misleading due to non-resonant contributions. The approach enables direct testing of specific quark-quark interaction mechanisms (one-gluon-exchange models from Isgur and Capstick, and one-meson-exchange models from Glozman) by comparing predicted scattering amplitudes with data, and it provides a dynamical interpretation for the multi-channel multi-resonance parameterization originally developed by Cutkosky.
