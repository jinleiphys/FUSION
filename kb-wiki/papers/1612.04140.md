# A microscopically derived formula for alpha-decay half-lives

> Although Geiger-Nuttall (GN) law gives a single straight line, if we consider the experimental data of alpha particle emitters including heavy and super heavy nuclei with proton numbers as large as 118, instead of getting a single linear path we observe several linear segments with different slopes and intercepts. This problem is overcome when the experimental results in logarithm form are plotted as a function of Viola-Seaborg (VS) parameters with values of parameters set by hand. By using the fundamental principle of decay, we derive a formula of logarithm of half-lives in terms of well-defined parameters or coefficients and this replaces the empirical VS rule.

## Key claim

The authors derive a closed-form analytical formula for the logarithm of alpha-decay half-lives from first principles, replacing the empirical Viola-Seaborg (VS) rule. The derived formula reproduces the linear relationship between log half-life and the VS parameter, but with coefficients that are well-defined physical parameters rather than constants set by hand.

## Method

The authors calculate the alpha-decay width using the Gell-Mann-Goldberger transformation, which expresses the width in terms of an integral over the regular Coulomb wave function, the resonant wave function of the alpha-nucleus system, and the difference between the effective potential and the point-charge Coulomb potential. They solve the Schrodinger equation for the alpha-nucleus system using a parametrized effective potential that reproduces the results of relativistic mean-field theory, then apply approximations to the Coulomb wave function to condense the integral into a closed-form expression for log half-life. The final formula is log T_{1/2} = a*chi + c + d + b_l, where chi = Z_alpha*Z_D * sqrt(A_alpha*A_D/((A_alpha+A_D)*Q_alpha)).

## Key numbers

- The coefficient a in the derived formula equals 0.96 (dimensionless).
- The constant d in the derived formula equals -45.262 (dimensionless).
- For l=0 decays, the factor c_f (used in the condensed integral) ranges from 0.4 to 1.5 across different nuclei, with a representative value of 0.8.
- For l>0 decays, the factor c_f is assigned the value 0.6.
- The derived formula reproduces experimental half-lives ranging from log10 T_{1/2} = -7.39 s to 14.4 s for nuclei with Z=52 to 118.
- For the specific system alpha + 88^220Ra, the Q-value is 7.298 MeV and the Coulomb barrier radius is r_B = 10.11 fm.

## Context

This work extends the Geiger-Nuttall (GN) law (1911) and the empirical Viola-Seaborg (VS) rule, which the authors note gives a single straight line for log half-life versus the VS parameter but uses constants set by hand (a'=1.478, b'=8.714, c'=-0.183, d'=-34.699). The derivation builds on the R-matrix theory approach of Qi et al. and the S-matrix theory of resonance scattering. The resulting formula enables prediction of alpha-decay half-lives for any nucleus given only its Q-value, mass number, and charge number, without requiring empirical fitting parameters.
