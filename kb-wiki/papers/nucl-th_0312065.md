# Transverse radial expansion in nuclear collisions and two particle correlations

> At the very first stage of an ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collision new particles are produced in individual nucleon-nucleon collisions. In the transverse plane, all particles from a single $NN$ collision are initially located at the same position. The subsequent thermalization and transverse radial expansion of the system create strong position-momentum correlations and lead to characteristic rapidity, transverse momentum, and azimuthal correlations among the produced particles.

## Key claim

Transverse radial expansion in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions creates strong position-momentum correlations that lead to characteristic two-particle correlations in rapidity, transverse momentum, and azimuthal angle, with the caveat that the analysis neglects azimuthal dependence in the transverse expansion velocity field (which is most important for anisotropic flow studies).

## Method

The paper develops a simple picture where particles produced in each independent nucleon-nucleon collision are initially at the same transverse position and then receive a collective radial boost depending on that position and the transverse expansion velocity profile. The author uses a blast-wave model with a boosted Boltzmann distribution at freeze-out temperature T=110 MeV, assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, freeze-out at constant proper time, uniform matter density within a cylinder of radius R, and a power-law transverse rapidity flow profile ρ_t = ρ_t,max (r/R)^n. Numerical calculations are performed for two velocity profiles (n=2 and n=0.5) to compute mean transverse momentum and two-particle transverse momentum correlations.

## Key numbers

- The increase in average transverse momentum due to radial expansion is of the order of 20-30%.
- The relative correlation from transverse expansion, α²σ²_v²/⟨p_t⟩², could be of order 1-3%, similar to the value of ⟨δp_t,1 δp_t,2⟩_NN/⟨p_t⟩² ≈ (0.12)² measured at ISR.
- For central Au+Au collisions, the dilution factor D is approximately 1/150.
- The thermalization time of the system is estimated to be smaller than 1 fm/c.
- For a linear velocity profile, σ²_v² = ⟨v²⟩²/3.
- Numerical calculations use freeze-out temperature T = 110 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse radial expansion beyond single-particle spectra (typically analyzed using thermal parameterizations as in the blast-wave model of Schnedermann, Sollfrank, and Heinz) and transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii. The paper introduces a new type of long-range two-particle correlation (as opposed to HBT-scale correlations) arising from the common spatial origin of particles from the same nucleon-nucleon collision. The results enable new approaches to studying initial conditions and system evolution in heavy-ion collisions, including potential applications to jet tomography by correlating high-p_t hadrons with the mean transverse momentum of associated soft particles.
