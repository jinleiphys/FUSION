# Exact solution of the (0+1)-dimensional Boltzmann equation for a massive gas

> We solve the one-dimensional boost-invariant kinetic equation for a relativistic massive system with the collision term treated in the relaxation time approximation. The result is an exact integral equation which can be solved numerically by the method of iteration to arbitrary precision. We compare predictions for the shear and bulk viscosities of a massive system with those obtained from the exact solution. Finally, we compare the time evolution of the bulk pressure obtained from our exact solution with results obtained from the dynamical equations of second-order viscous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim
The paper presents an exact solution of the (0+1)-dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for a massive gas, generalizing prior massless results. The solution is an integral equation for the effective temperature that must be solved numerically by iteration, meaning the "exact" solution is exact in the sense of the kinetic equation but requires numerical evaluation.

## Method
The authors solved the boost-invariant Boltzmann equation with a collision term in the relaxation time approximation for a classical massive gas. They expressed the distribution function as a sum of an initial Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution and an integral over the equilibrium distribution weighted by a damping function, then imposed dynamical Landau matching to obtain an integral equation for the effective temperature T(τ). The integral equation was solved iteratively, and the resulting T(τ) was used to compute transverse and longitudinal pressures via momentum integrals of the distribution function.

## Key numbers
- Mass m = 300 MeV, relaxation time τ_eq = 0.5 fm/c, initial time τ_0 = 0.5 fm/c, evolution to τ = 10 fm/c.
- Initial effective temperatures T_0 = 600 MeV and T_0 = 300 MeV were used.
- Initial anisotropy parameter ξ_0 ∈ {0, 100} (isotropic or oblate initial configuration).
- Degeneracy factor g_0 = 16.
- In the large-mass limit (γ → ∞), the shear viscosity η → τ_eq P_eq + O(γ⁻¹) and the bulk viscosity ζ → (2/3) τ_eq P_eq + O(γ⁻¹).

## Context
This work extends the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation for massless particles (Florkowski 2013) to massive systems, enabling direct tests of viscous hydrodynamic approximations including bulk viscous effects. The results are compared with second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations from Muronga (2003), Heinz (2005), and Jaiswal (2013). The shear and bulk viscosity formulas are taken from Anderson and Witting (1974) and from Bozek (2009), Sasaki (2008), and Romatschke (2011). The exact solution provides a benchmark for assessing the accuracy of these hydrodynamic approaches for massive gases.
