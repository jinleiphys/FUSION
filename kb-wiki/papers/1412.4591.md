# Use of the Husimi distribution for nucleon tomography

> In the context of nucleon structure, the Wigner distribution has been commonly used to visualize the phase-space distribution of quarks and gluons inside the nucleon. However, the Wigner distribution does not allow for a probabilistic interpretation because it takes negative values. In pursuit of a positive phase-space distribution in QCD, we introduce the Husimi distribution and demonstrate its advantages via a simple one-loop example. We also comment on a possible connection to the semiclassical approach to saturation physics at small-$x$.

## Key claim

The Husimi distribution, obtained by Gaussian smearing of the Wigner distribution, provides a positive-semidefinite phase-space distribution for quarks and gluons inside the nucleon, overcoming the Wigner distribution's lack of a probabilistic interpretation; however, the standard proof of positivity from nonrelativistic quantum mechanics does not directly apply in QCD because the initial and final nucleon states differ due to momentum recoil.

## Method

The authors define the QCD Husimi distribution as a Gaussian convolution of the Wigner distribution in transverse position \(\vec{b}_\perp\) and transverse momentum \(\vec{k}_\perp\), with a smearing parameter \(\ell\) that sets the resolution scale. They compute the Wigner and Husimi distributions analytically at one-loop order for a single quark dressed by a gluon in light-cone gauge \(A^+=0\), using the splitting function \(P_{qq}(x)=\frac{1+x^2}{1-x}\) and a quark mass \(m\). Numerical evaluations of both distributions are then compared for various values of the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\), the transverse position \(\vec{b}_\perp\), and the transverse momentum \(\vec{k}_\perp\).

## Key numbers

- The Wigner distribution at one-loop order contains a term proportional to \(\vec{q}_+ \cdot \vec{q}_- P_{qq}(x) + m^2(1-x)^3\) where \(\vec{q}_\pm = \vec{k}_\perp \pm \frac{\vec{\Delta}_\perp}{2}(1-x)\); the coefficient of \(P_{qq}\) becomes negative when \(|\vec{k}_\perp| < (1-x)|\vec{\Delta}_\perp|/2\).
- The smearing parameter \(\ell\) is chosen as \(\ell = 1\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) for the numerical demonstration.
- The numerical calculations use \(m^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\Delta_\perp^{max} = 5\ \text{GeV}\), and fixed \(\vec{k}_\perp = (0.5\ \text{GeV}, 0)\) or fixed \(\vec{b}_\perp = (0.5\ \text{GeV}^{-1}, 0)\).
- The Husimi distribution is shown to be positive for all tested parameter sets, in contrast to the Wigner distribution which exhibits negative peaks and oscillations.

## Context

This work extends the use of the Husimi distribution, well-known in quantum mechanics and statistical physics (Husimi, 1940), to QCD nucleon tomography as an alternative to the Wigner distribution defined by Ji (2003), Belitsky (2003), and Lorce (2011). The paper addresses the negativity problem observed in the QCD Wigner distribution, for example in the one-loop calculation by Mukherjee (2014). The authors suggest that the Husimi distribution with \(\ell = 1/Q_s(x)\) may connect to the semiclassical approach to saturation physics at small-\(x\) (McLerran, 1994), where the Gaussian smearing factor becomes identical to the forward dipole amplitude.
