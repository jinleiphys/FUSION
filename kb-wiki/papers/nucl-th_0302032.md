# Study of Proton Magic Even-Even Isotopes and Giant Halos of Ca Isotopes with Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory

> We study the proton magic O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotope chains from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line with the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory. Particulary, we study in detail the properties of even-even Ca isotopes due to the appearance of giant halos in neutron rich Ca nuclei near the neutron drip line. The RCHB theory is able to reproduce the experimental binding energies $E_b$ and two neutron separation energies $S_{2n}$ very well. The predicted neutron drip line nuclei are $^{28}$O, $^{72}$Ca, $^{98}$Ni, $^{136}$Zr, $^{176}$Sn, and $^{266}$Pb, respectively. Halo and giant halo properties predicted in Ca isotopes with $A>60$ are investigated in detail from the analysis of two neutron separation energies, nucleon density distributions, single particle energy levels, the occupation probabilities of energy levels including continuum states. The spin-orbit splitting and the diffuseness of nuclear potential in these Ca isotopes are studied also. Furthermore, we study the neighboring lighter isotopes in the drip line Ca region and find some possibility of giant halo nuclei in the Ne-Na-Mg drip line nuclei.

## Key claim

The relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory predicts that giant halos appear in neutron-rich Ca isotopes with mass number A > 60 near the neutron drip line, specifically for nuclei such as \({}^{72}\)Ca, where the halo is formed by more than two neutrons scattered as Cooper pairs into the continuum region.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) equations in coordinate space within a spherical box of radius 20 fm and step size 0.1 fm, using the NL-SH parameter set and a density-dependent delta-force pairing interaction with strength V0 = 650 MeV·fm\(^{-3}\) and \(\rho_0 = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\). They calculated ground-state properties for all even-even isotopes of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line, and analyzed two-neutron separation energies, nucleon density distributions, single-particle energy levels, occupation probabilities including continuum states, spin-orbit splitting, and nuclear potential diffuseness for Ca isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Predicted neutron drip line nuclei: \({}^{28}\)O, \({}^{72}\)Ca, \({}^{98}\)Ni, \({}^{136}\)Zr, \({}^{176}\)Sn, and \({}^{266}\)Pb.
- For Ca isotopes, the binding energy of \({}^{72}\)Ca is -467.993 MeV (RCHB calculation), and the neutron drip line is reached at \({}^{72}\)Ca, with \({}^{74}\)Ca having a binding energy of -467.645 MeV (less bound).
- The difference between experimental and calculated binding energies is less than 3.5 MeV for most nuclei (less than 1% of experimental values), except for some Zr isotopes where differences up to ~10 MeV are attributed to deformation.
- The neutron root-mean-square radius for \({}^{72}\)Ca is 4.636 fm, compared to 3.311 fm for \({}^{40}\)Ca, indicating a dramatic increase in neutron radius near the drip line.
- The two-neutron separation energy S\(_{2n}\) becomes negative for nuclei beyond the predicted drip line, e.g., for \({}^{74}\)Ca the binding energy is lower than for \({}^{72}\)Ca.

## Context

This work extends the RCHB theory, previously successful in describing the halo in \({}^{11}\)Li (Meng, 1996) and predicting giant halos in Zr isotopes with A > 122 (Meng, 1998), to lighter Ca isotopes that are more accessible with current radioactive ion beam facilities. The study enables experimental searches for giant halo phenomena in Ca isotopes near the neutron drip line and suggests possible giant halo nuclei in the Ne-Na-Mg drip line region, building on prior mean-field calculations for Ca isotopes using Skyrme Hartree-Fock (Im, 2000) and non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov methods (Fayans et al., 2000).
