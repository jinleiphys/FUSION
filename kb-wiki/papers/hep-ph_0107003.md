# Critical couplings in Crystalline Color superconductivity

> Solving the Schwinger-Dyson equations, we analyze the pairing of quarks in asymmetric quark matter where quarks have different chemical potentials. We show that in the asymmetric quark matter a crystalline color-superconducting gap opens when the quark coupling is stronger than a critical value. The critical coupling is nonzero, since the infrared divergence is lessened when the momenta of pairing quarks are not opposite. The superconducting gaps and the critical couplings are calculated both at high and intermediate densities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a crystalline color-superconducting (LOFF) gap in asymmetric quark matter opens only when the quark coupling exceeds a nonzero critical value, in sharp contrast to ordinary BCS Cooper pairing which occurs for arbitrarily weak attraction. This nonzero critical coupling arises because the infrared divergence is lessened when the pairing quarks have non-opposite momenta.

## Method

The authors solve Schwinger-Dyson equations for the LOFF gap in asymmetric quark matter where up and down quarks have different chemical potentials. They analyze the gap equations both at intermediate density using effective four-Fermi interactions with an ultraviolet cutoff, and at high density using hard-dense loop (HDL) approximated gluon propagators with Landau-damped magnetic gluons. The gap equation is reduced from (3+1) to (2+1) dimensions by integrating over the perpendicular momentum component that labels degeneracy in LOFF pairing.

## Key numbers

- At intermediate density with $\mu = 400$ MeV and $\Lambda = 200$ MeV, the critical four-Fermi coupling is $g_c^2 \simeq 11.6 \sin\beta \, (\sin\alpha_d)^{-1}$, where $\beta$ is the angle between $\vec{v}_F^d$ and $-\vec{v}_F^u$ and $\alpha_d$ is the angle between $\vec{q}$ and $\vec{v}_F^d$.
- At high density, the critical strong coupling is $g_c \simeq 13 \, (\tan\beta)^{3/4}$.
- Near the critical coupling at intermediate density, the gap scales as $\Delta \simeq 2\Lambda \, (1 - g_c^2/g^2) / [\ln(1 - g_c^2/g^2)]^2$.
- Near the critical coupling at high density, the gap scales as $\Delta \simeq (2\sqrt{2}\bar\Lambda / 27 g_s^2) \, (g_s^{4/3} - g_c^{4/3})^{3/2} / |\ln(g_s^{4/3} - g_c^{4/3})|^3$.

## Context

This work extends the study of the LOFF (Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell) phase in quark matter, previously proposed by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2001) and others, by demonstrating that a critical coupling exists for LOFF pairing, unlike BCS pairing. The result contrasts with Cooper pairing analyzed by Son (1999) and Hong (2000) where the gap opens for any attraction due to (1+1) dimensional infrared divergence. The findings explain why crystalline superconductivity is hard to realize in ordinary BCS superconductors with weak couplings but may be observable in high-Tc or heavy fermion superconductors where effective couplings are large.
