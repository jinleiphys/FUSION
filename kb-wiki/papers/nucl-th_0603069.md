# Beyond mean-field study of excited states: Analysis within the Lipkin model

> Beyond mean-field methods based on restoration of symmetries and configuration mixing by the generator coordinate method (GCM) enable to calculate on the same footing correlations in the ground state and the properties of excited states. Excitation energies are often largely overestimated, especially in nuclei close to magicity, even when transition probabilities are well-described. We analyse here the origin of this failure. The first part of the paper compares realistic projected GCM and QRPA calculations for selected Sn isotopes performed with the same effective Skyrme interaction. Although it is difficult to perform RPA and GCM calculations under exactly the same conditions, this comparison shows that the projected GCM overestimates the RPA results. In the second part of this paper, we compare GCM and RPA in the framework of the exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model. We show that the discretized GCM works quite well and permits to obtain nearly exact results with a small number of discretization points. This analysis indicates also that to break more symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian in the construction of the GCM basis is probably the best way to improve the description of excited states by the GCM.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the overestimation of excitation energies in beyond-mean-field generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations, particularly for spherical nuclei near magicity, likely originates from the insufficient richness of the GCM variational subspace, specifically, the absence of two-quasi-particle excitations that break time-reversal invariance, rather than solely from inadequacies of the effective interaction.

## Method

The authors first compare projected GCM and (quasiparticle) random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations for the first excited 2⁺ states in ¹³²Sn and ¹²⁰Sn using the same Skyrme SLy4 effective interaction, noting unavoidable technical differences between the two methods. They then analyze the GCM and RPA within the exactly solvable Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model for N=30 and N=50 particles, solving the discretized Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation using the deformation-like variable α as the generator coordinate, with meshes of 7 discretization points chosen to mimic realistic applications.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³²Sn: GCM gives excitation energy 5.69 MeV and B(E2↑) = 630 e²fm⁴; particle-hole RPA gives 5.13 MeV and 1370 e²fm⁴; experiment gives 4.04 MeV and 1100±300 e²fm⁴.
- For ¹²⁰Sn: GCM gives excitation energy 2.40 MeV and B(E2↑) = 1350 e²fm⁴; quasiparticle RPA gives 1.44 MeV and 440 e²fm⁴; experiment gives 1.17 MeV and 2020±40 e²fm⁴.
- The GCM energy centroid of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in ¹³²Sn is 12.45 MeV, close to the empirical systematics 63 A⁻¹/³ MeV.
- In the LMG model with N=30 particles, the discretized GCM becomes more accurate than HF only for χ larger than 0.25 for the second excited state and than 0.45 for the first excited state.
- The RPA excitation energy in the LMG model is ε√(1−χ²), becoming zero at the phase transition χ=1.

## Context

This work extends earlier GCM studies of ²⁰⁸Pb and its isotopes by Heenen et al. (2001), which found excitation energies overestimated by more than 1 MeV for the first 3⁻ and 2⁺ states, and addresses the open question of whether the problem lies in the effective interactions or the GCM methodology itself. The comparison with RPA calculations by Colo et al. (2003, 2004) and the use of the exactly solvable LMG model introduced by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965) enables the authors to isolate the role of the variational space; the analysis suggests that breaking more symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian in constructing the GCM basis is the most promising path to improve excited-state descriptions.
