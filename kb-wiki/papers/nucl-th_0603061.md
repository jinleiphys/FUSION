# Semileptonic Decays of Heavy Omega Baryons in a Quark Model

> The semileptonic decays of $Ω_c$ and $Ω_b$ are treated in the framework of a constituent quark model developed in a previous paper on the semileptonic decays of heavy $Λ$ baryons. Analytic results for the form factors for the decays to ground states and a number of excited states are evaluated. For $Ω_b$ to $Ω_c$ the form factors obtained are shown to satisfy the relations predicted at leading order in the heavy-quark effective theory at the non-recoil point. A modified fit of nonrelativistic and semirelativistic Hamiltonians generates configuration-mixed baryon wave functions from the known masses and the measured $\lcle$ rate, with wave functions expanded in both harmonic oscillator and Sturmian bases. Decay rates of $\ob$ to pairs of ground and excited $\omc$ states related by heavy-quark symmetry calculated using these configuration-mixed wave functions are in the ratios expected from heavy-quark effective theory, to a good approximation. Our predictions for the semileptonic elastic branching fraction of $Ω_Q$ vary minimally within the models we use. We obtain an average value of (84$\pm$ 2%) for the fraction of $Ω_c\to Ξ^{(*)}$ decays to ground states, and 91% for the fraction of $Ω_c\to Ω^{(*)}$ decays to the ground state $Ω$. The elastic fraction of $\ob \to \omc$ ranges from about 50% calculated with the two harmonic-oscillator models, to about 67% calculated with the two Sturmian models.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the semileptonic decay form factors and rates for heavy Omega baryons (Omega_c and Omega_b) can be reliably calculated using a constituent quark model with configuration-mixed wave functions, and that the results satisfy heavy-quark effective theory (HQET) relations at the non-recoil point, but only to a good approximation and with model-dependent variation in the elastic branching fractions.

## Method
The authors used a nonrelativistic and semirelativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian including linear confinement, Coulomb, and hyperfine interactions, fitting parameters to known baryon masses and the measured Lambda_c+ to Lambda e+ nu rate. Baryon wave functions were expanded in both harmonic oscillator and Sturmian bases up to N=2 oscillator shells, with configuration mixing determined by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian. Form factors for semileptonic decays of Omega_b and Omega_c to ground and excited states were computed analytically in the spectator approximation, and decay rates were obtained by integrating differential rates over phase space.

## Key numbers
- Average branching fraction for Omega_c to Xi^{(*)} decays to ground states: (84 +/- 2)%.
- Branching fraction for Omega_c to Omega^{(*)} decays to the ground state Omega: 91%.
- Elastic fraction of Omega_b to Omega_c ranges from about 50% (harmonic oscillator models) to about 67% (Sturmian models).
- CLEO-c measured product B(Omega_c0 -> Omega- e+ nu) * sigma(e+e- -> Omega_c X) = 42.2 +/- 14.1 +/- 11.9 fb.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quark-model treatment of semileptonic decays of heavy Lambda baryons (Pervin, Roberts, Capstick, 2005) to the Omega_Q sector, where the light diquark is symmetric rather than antisymmetric. It provides the first non-HQET calculation of Omega_Q semileptonic form factors, complementing earlier HQET analyses by Boyd and Brahm (1991), Sutherland (1995), and Xu (2002). The results enable future experimental comparisons with CLEO-c, ARGUS, and BELLE data, and can be used to test HQET predictions beyond leading order.
