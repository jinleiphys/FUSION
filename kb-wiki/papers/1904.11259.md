# Toroidal modes in nuclei by inelastic electron scattering

> Electron scattering is a tool that can provide relatively clean view of the nuclear structure in both ground and excited states, as it depends on the well-known electromagnetic interaction. But since the common expressions for its cross section were derived with certain assumptions, in this paper we describe several nontrivial steps necessary for a proper theoretical calculation within the current density-functional framework, namely with Skyrme QRPA for axial nuclei, with aim to enable comparison of the theoretically predicted low-lying $1^-$ toroidal modes with future (e,e') experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that inelastic electron scattering can distinguish low-lying toroidal (vortical) 1⁻ modes from compression-type pygmy dipole modes in nuclei, but only if several nontrivial theoretical corrections are applied to the standard plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) cross-section formulas within the Skyrme density-functional framework.

## Method

The authors formulate the inelastic electron scattering cross section within the axial quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) on top of a Skyrme density functional, deriving an effective current operator that satisfies the continuity equation. They then implement five specific corrections: (i) removal of spurious modes from the QRPA spectrum, (ii) use of the effective current instead of the bare convective current, (iii) exact relativistic kinematics for the electron, (iv) adaptation of the form-factor formalism from spherical to axially deformed nuclei, and (v) inclusion of the recoil term and effective-momentum approximation to mimic distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) effects.

## Key numbers

- The effective current correction affects the transversal cross-section of isovector transitions by an overall scaling of around 25%.
- The effective-momentum correction uses \( k_{\mathrm{eff}} = k_{\mathrm{exp}}\big(1+\frac{3}{2}\frac{Z\alpha\hbar}{p_i R}\big) \) with \( R=1.12A^{1/3}\ \mathrm{fm} \).
- The recoil factor is approximated as \( f_{\mathrm{rec}} \approx \frac{Mc^2 + \hbar\omega_\nu}{Mc^2+2E_i\sin^2\tfrac{\theta}{2}} \).
- The paper shows results for nuclei \( ^{20}\mathrm{Ne} \), \( ^{24}\mathrm{Mg} \), and \( ^{208}\mathrm{Pb} \), with a smoothing parameter \( \Delta=1\ \mathrm{MeV} \) used in the photoabsorption strength function.
- Back-scattering geometry at \( \theta=178^\circ \) is identified as the regime where transversal form-factors become dominant.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of \( ^{208}\mathrm{Pb} \) (Repko 2013), which questioned the usual interpretation of the pygmy E1 mode in favor of an isoscalar toroidal mode. It also builds on predictions of low-lying 1⁻ states with vortical character in \( ^{10}\mathrm{Be} \) (Kvasil 2017), \( ^{24}\mathrm{Mg} \) (Nesterenko 2018), and \( ^{20}\mathrm{Ne} \) (Burgas). The paper enables future comparison of theoretically predicted toroidal modes with experimental (e,e') data, particularly in light nuclei where the density of excitations is low and where the toroidal current, decoupled from density, is suppressed in alpha scattering.
