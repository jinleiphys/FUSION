# A Data-Guided Coalescence Model for Light Nuclei and Hypernuclei Production in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 3$--200 GeV

> The production of light hypernuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides a unique opportunity to probe hyperon--nucleon interactions and possible three-body forces, which are central to the resolution of the hyperon puzzle in neutron star matter. In this work, we develop a data-guided coalescence framework in which the source size is extracted from proton and deuteron yields and used, together with measured proton and $Λ$ spectra, to predict the production of $A=3$ nuclei $(t,{}^{3}\rm{He})$ and hypernuclei $({}^{3}_Λ\rm{H})$ in $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=3$--200~GeV Au+Au collisions. For tritons, calculations with a Gaussian wave function reproduce experimental spectra and yield ratios across this energy range. For the hypertriton, the model predictions are highly sensitive to the assumed wave function. This sensitivity is strongest at low collision energies and in low-multiplicity environments, implying that such conditions are particularly valuable for probing hypernuclear structure.

## Key claim

The paper develops a data-guided coalescence framework that uses experimentally measured proton and deuteron yields to extract the source size, which is then used to predict triton, helium-3, and hypertriton production in Au+Au collisions from 3 to 200 GeV. The hypertriton predictions are highly sensitive to the assumed wave function, with the strongest sensitivity at low collision energies and in low-multiplicity environments, implying these conditions are particularly valuable for probing hypernuclear structure.

## Method

The authors extract the nucleon source radius R_inv from measured deuteron coalescence parameter B_2 data using the coalescence-correlation framework of Bellini (2020), which relates the coalescence probability to an overlap integral between the nuclear wave function and a Gaussian source function. Neutron spectra are estimated from measured proton spectra combined with neutron-to-proton ratios from Thermal-FIST calculations, and Lambda spectra are scaled by a factor of 1 minus the Sigma-zero fraction to account for feed-down contributions. The extracted source radius is then used with measured proton and Lambda spectra to predict yields of A=3 nuclei and hypernuclei via the same coalescence formalism.

## Key numbers

- Source radius R_inv extracted from deuteron B_2 data at p_T/A = 0.75 GeV/c increases from sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV to around 11.5 GeV, stays level until 62.4 GeV, then rises steadily beyond
- For triton, Gaussian wave function with RMS charge radius b_t = 1.77 fm reproduces experimental spectra and yield ratios across the energy range 3-200 GeV
- For helium-3, Gaussian wave function with RMS charge radius b_He3 = 1.97 fm is employed
- The Congleton wave function parameters for hypertriton include Q_Lambda = 2.5 fm^-1 and alpha_Lambda = 0.068 fm^-1 (3-body EFT) and Q_Lambda = 1.17 fm^-1 with alpha_Lambda = 0.068 fm^-1 (2-body closure approximation)
- RMS deuteron-Lambda separation for all hypertriton wave functions is approximately 10.8 fm, consistent with the value 9.8^{+1.1}_{-0.7} fm inferred from the hypertriton binding energy 0.163 +/- 0.036 MeV
- Source radius as a function of charged-particle multiplicity follows R = A x (dN_ch/d_eta)^(1/3) + B with A = 0.501 +/- 0.011 and B = 0.36 +/- 0.06, with chi^2/NDF = 62/40
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, radii extracted from B_2 are consistently larger than those from correlation functions by approximately 8%

## Context

This work extends the coalescence-correlation framework of Bellini (2020) to lower collision energies (3-200 GeV) where baryon correlation measurements are limited, using a data-driven approach that avoids the uncertainties of transport models that are strongly influenced by the poorly constrained equation of state at low energies. The method enables predictions for the STAR Beam Energy Scan II program and future facilities like CHNS at HIAF and CBM at FAIR, and the sensitivity of hypertriton yields to wave function shape suggests that yield measurements can provide complementary constraints on hyperon-nucleon interactions and three-body forces beyond traditional binding energy measurements.
