# Viscous evolution of the rapidity distribution of matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Longitudinal hydrodynamic expansion of the fluid created in relativistic heavy-collisions is considered taking into account shear viscosity. Both a on-vanishing viscosity and a soft equation of state make particle distributions in rapidity narrower. The presence of viscosity has dramatic consequence on the value of the initial energy density. The reduction of the longitudinal work and dissipative processes due to the shear viscosity, increase the total entropy and the particle multiplicity at central rapidities. The total energy in the collision, dominated by the longitudinal motion, is conserved. Viscous corrections make the longitudinal velocity of the fluid to stay close to the Bjorken scaling v_z = z/t through the evolution. At the freeze-out viscous corrections are the strongest for non-central rapidities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shear viscosity significantly reduces the initial energy density required to reproduce observed meson rapidity distributions, with the effect being conditional on the specific value of the viscosity-to-entropy ratio and the equation of state used.

## Method

The author solves 1+1 dimensional second-order viscous relativistic hydrodynamic equations for longitudinal expansion of a baryon-free fluid, using a realistic equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD data and a hadron gas. The initial energy density is taken as a Gaussian in space-time rapidity, and the fluid rapidity is initially set to the Bjorken scaling value. The Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription is used with viscous corrections to the distribution function, and the parameters of the initial energy density distribution are adjusted to reproduce BRAHMS data for pion rapidity distributions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density for ideal fluid evolution: epsilon_0 = 16.9 GeV/fm^3 with sigma = 1.05
- Initial energy density for eta/s = 0.2: epsilon_0 = 5.6 GeV/fm^3 with sigma = 1.8
- Initial energy density for eta/s = 0.3: epsilon_0 = 4.0 GeV/fm^3 with sigma = 1.86
- Lifetime until freeze-out for ideal fluid: 14.8 fm/c; for eta/s = 0.2: 13.1 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 165 MeV
- Initial proper time: tau_0 = 1.0 fm/c
- For eta/s = 0.2, the flow stays Bjorken-like for |theta| < 1.8 at the freeze-out hypersurface

## Context

This work extends the 1+1 dimensional longitudinal hydrodynamic analysis of Satarov (2006) by incorporating shear viscosity, which had previously been studied only in the context of transverse expansion and elliptic flow by Song (2007) and Romatschke (2007). The author notes that only one earlier work by Chu (1986) considered viscous effects on longitudinal expansion, finding no strong effect at lower energies. The results enable more realistic initial conditions for full three-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations at RHIC and LHC energies, and show that the boost-invariant Bjorken scaling solution is not realized at RHIC energies.
