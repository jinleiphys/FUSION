# Thermal Radiation from Nucleons and Mesons

> Thermal photon emission rates due to meson-nucleon interactions have been evaluated. An exhaustive set of reactions involving p(\bar p), n(\bar n), rho, omega, a_1, pi and eta is seen to provide a sizeable contribution to the emission rate from hot hadronic matter. Contributions from baryonic resonances are found to be negligibly small.

## Key claim

Thermal photon emission from hot hadronic matter receives a sizeable contribution from meson-nucleon interactions (involving p, n, rho, omega, a1, pi, and eta), but this contribution is suppressed by a factor of about 3 around E_gamma ~ 2 GeV when monopole form factors are included at the meson-nucleon vertices; contributions from baryonic resonance decays are negligibly small.

## Method

The authors evaluated thermal photon emission rates by computing Feynman diagrams for all relevant scattering and annihilation processes involving nucleons (and antinucleons) with pseudoscalar mesons (pi, eta), vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), and the axial-vector a1 meson. They used standard phenomenological interaction Lagrangians with monopole form factors at the strong vertices, taking coupling constants and cutoff parameters from the literature (Roper, cited as Ref. [roper]), and included the decay of baryonic resonances (N(1440) through N(1700) and Delta(1232) through Delta(1950)) using partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- At T = 170 MeV and zero baryonic chemical potential, reactions of type P (pseudoscalar meson + nucleon -> gamma + nucleon, and nucleon-antinucleon -> gamma + pseudoscalar) give the highest contribution at lower photon energies; type V (vector meson + nucleon) reactions dominate above about 1.5 GeV.
- At T = 140 MeV, inclusion of monopole form factors at meson-nucleon vertices reduces the photon production rate by a factor of about 3 around E_gamma ~ 2 GeV.
- The coupling constants used include g_piNN^2/(4pi) = 0.079, g_etaNN^2/(4pi) = 0.148, g_rhoNN^2/(4pi) = 0.84 with kappa = 6.1, g_omegaNN^2/(4pi) = 20.0, and g_a1_pi_gamma = 0.743; cutoff parameters Lambda range from 0.6 GeV to 2.5 GeV depending on the vertex.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of thermal photon emission from hadronic matter that considered only mesonic reactions (pi, rho, omega, eta, a1) as in the work of the authors' previous paper (Alam et al., 2003, cited as Ref. [our_npa]) and earlier studies (Refs. [old1, old2]). The authors note that baryonic contributions had been neglected in all prior work, and they find that meson-nucleon interactions provide a non-negligible contribution to the photon rate, especially when form factor effects are accounted for. This enables more accurate background estimates for electromagnetic signals from quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, and suggests that at non-zero baryonic chemical potential the nucleonic contributions could be further enhanced.
