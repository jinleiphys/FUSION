# Quark Model Calculations Of Symmetry Breaking in Parton Distributions

> Using a quark model, we calculate symmetry breaking effects in the valence quark distributions of the nucleon. In particular, we examine the breaking of the quark model SU(4) symmetry by color magnetic effects, and find that color magnetism provides an explanation for deviation of the ratio $d_V(x)/u_V(x)$ from $1/2$. Additionally, we calculate the effect of charge symmetry breaking in the valence quark distributions of the proton and neutron and find, in contrast to other authors, that the effect is too small to be seen experimentally.

## Key claim

Color magnetic interactions within a quark model provide a natural mechanism for producing the observed deviation of the ratio \(d_V(x)/u_V(x)\) from 1/2, but the quantitative agreement with data is less satisfactory and is expected to improve in models where SU(4) symmetric valence distributions carry less momentum. Charge symmetry breaking effects in valence quark distributions are calculated to be too small to be seen experimentally, in contrast to other authors who found effects on the order of 10-15%.

## Method

The authors evaluate twist-two contributions to valence quark distributions using quark model wavefunctions at a low renormalization scale \(\mu_{bag}^2\), then evolve the distributions to high \(Q^2\) using next-to-leading order QCD perturbation theory. They use the Los Alamos Potential Model (LAMP) to calculate symmetry breaking effects, incorporating color magnetic interactions for SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry breaking, and including a \(u-d\) quark mass difference of 4 MeV and Coulomb effects for charge symmetry breaking. The Peierls-Yoccoz projection method is used to construct momentum eigenstates from the quark model wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The SU(4) symmetric prediction gives \(u_V(x) = 2d_V(x)\) for all values of \(x\).
- For the MIT Bag model, the area under the valence quark distribution curve is nearly \(1/3\), indicating each valence quark carries nearly one-third of the nucleon momentum; for the soliton bag, each valence quark carries \(1/4\) of the momentum.
- The ratio \(d_V(x)/u_V(x)\) is calculated at the bag scale and evolved to 15 GeV\(^2\), assuming \(\mu_{BAG}^2 = 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).
- For charge symmetry breaking, the minority quark ratio \(R_{min}(x)\) is calculated after evolution from a bag scale of 0.4 GeV\(^2\) to 10 GeV\(^2\).
- For \(x < 0.7\), no individual contribution to charge symmetry breaking is greater than about 2%, and the total CSB effect for minority quarks is less than 4%; for majority quarks, the total CSB effect is less than 2%.

## Context

This work extends the Jaffe and Ross (1980) renormalization group approach to relate quark model wavefunctions to parton distributions, and applies it to symmetry breaking effects previously studied in the context of the nucleon-Delta mass splitting. The charge symmetry breaking results contrast markedly with those of the Adelaide group (Rodionov et al., 1994), who found CSB effects on the order of 10-15% for \(x\) near 0.7 due to sensitivity to a diquark mass parameter. The calculations enable experimental tests of charge symmetry breaking in Drell-Yan experiments, which could provide new insight into strong interaction dynamics.
