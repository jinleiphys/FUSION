# Lifetime Effects in Color Superconductivity at Weak Coupling

> Present computations of the gap of color superconductivity in weak coupling assume that the quarks which participate in the condensation process are infinitely long-lived. However, the quasiparticles in a plasma are characterized by having a finite lifetime. In this article we take into account this fact to evaluate its effect in the computation of the color gap. By first considering the Schwinger-Dyson equations in weak coupling, when one-loop self-energy corrections are included, a general gap equation is written in terms of the spectral densities of the quasiparticles. To evaluate lifetime effects, we then model the spectral density by a Lorentzian function. We argue that the decay of the quasiparticles limits their efficiency to condense. The value of the gap at the Fermi surface is then reduced. To leading order, these lifetime effects can be taken into account by replacing the coupling constant of the gap equation by a reduced effective one.

## Key claim

The finite lifetime of quasiparticles in a dense QCD plasma reduces their efficiency to participate in Cooper pairing, and to leading order this effect can be accounted for by replacing the coupling constant in the gap equation with a reduced effective coupling constant, rather than by introducing a physical ultraviolet cutoff as in BCS superconductivity.

## Method

The author derived the Schwinger-Dyson equations for color superconductivity including one-loop fermion self-energy corrections, then expressed the gap equation in terms of spectral densities of the quasiparticles. To evaluate lifetime effects at zero temperature, the spectral density was modeled by a Lorentzian function with a damping rate that depends linearly on the quasiparticle energy near the Fermi surface. The resulting frequency integrals were approximated analytically to obtain a modified gap equation with an effective coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The damping rate for quarks near the Fermi surface is given by \(\Gamma_q = \frac{g^2 C_F}{24 \pi} ||{\bf q}| - \mu| + \frac{g^2 C_F}{64 m_D} (|{\bf q}| - \mu)^2 + \mathcal{O}(((|{\bf q}|-\mu)/m_D)^3)\), where \(C_F = 4/3\) for quarks in the fundamental representation.
- The effective coupling constant including lifetime effects is \(\bar{g}_{eff}^2 = \bar{g}^2 (1 - 2 \bar{g}^2)\), where \(\bar{g} = g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)\).
- The gap at the Fermi surface including damping effects is \(\phi_0^{damp} \sim 2 \, b_0 g^{-5} \mu \exp(-\pi/(2\bar{g}_{eff}))\).
- For coupling \(g \sim 0.5\), the reduction of the gap value due to lifetime effects is of order 5%.

## Context

This work extends the computation of the color superconductivity gap in weak coupling, previously carried out by Son (1999), Schafer (1999b), Pisarski (2000bf, 2000tv), and Hong (2000tn, 2000fh), by including the one-loop fermion self-energy corrections that were neglected in those earlier treatments. The approach parallels the treatment of lifetime effects in BCS superconductivity by Morel, but with the crucial difference that the damping rate in QCD depends linearly on quasiparticle energy rather than quadratically. The results enable more accurate predictions of the gap at moderate coupling strengths and suggest that lifetime effects could be more dramatic if extrapolated to the strong coupling region.
