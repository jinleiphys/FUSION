# Non-Extremality, Chemical Potential and the Infrared limit of Large N Thermal QCD

> Non-extremal solution with warped resolved-deformed conifold background is important to study the infrared limit of large N thermal QCD. Earlier works in this direction have not taken into account all the back-reactions on the geometry, namely from the branes, fluxes, and black-hole carefully. In the present work we make some progress in this direction by solving explicitly the supergravity equations of motions in the presence of the backreaction from the black-hole. The backreactions from the branes and the fluxes on the other hand and to the order that we study, are comparatively suppressed. Our analysis reveal, among other things, how the resolution parameter would depend on the horizon radius and how the RG flows of the coupling constants should be understood in these scenarios, including their effects on the background three-form fluxes. We also study the effect of switching on a chemical potential in the background and, in a particularly simplified scenario, compute the actual value of the chemical potential for our case.

## Key claim

The authors claim to make progress toward a UV-complete holographic dual of large N thermal QCD by explicitly solving the supergravity equations of motion including backreaction from the black hole, while the backreactions from branes and fluxes are argued to be comparatively suppressed to the order studied; the resolution parameter is shown to depend on the horizon radius, and the chemical potential is computed in a simplified scenario.

## Method

The authors start from a warped resolved-deformed conifold background and solve the supergravity equations of motion including the backreaction from the black hole, while neglecting the backreactions from branes and fluxes to lowest order in string coupling and color-to-flavor ratio. They then compute the detailed three-form fluxes incorporating backreactions from the black hole and flavor seven-branes, showing how these backreactions make the fluxes non-ISD (non-imaginary self-dual). Finally, they compute the chemical potential in a simplified scenario and argue that an alternative method via duality chasing may be flawed.

## Key numbers

* The warp factor for the Klebanov-Tseytlin solution is given by \(h_{\rm KT} = \frac{L^4}{r^4}\left[1 + \frac{3g_s M^2}{2\pi N}\log r\right]\), where the geometry becomes ill-defined for small \(r\).
* The Klebanov-Strassler warp factor is \(h_{\rm KS}(\rho) = \alpha \frac{2^{2/3}}{4}\int_{\rho}^\infty dx \frac{x\coth x - 1}{\sinh^2 x}\left(\sinh(2x)-2x\right)^{1/3}\) with \(\alpha = \mathcal{O}(g_s^2 M^2)\).
* The gauge theory cascades from \(SU(N+M)\times SU(N)\) down to \(SU(M)\) when \(N = kM\) with \(k\) a natural number.

## Context

This work extends the Klebanov-Strassler (KS) model (Klebanov, 2000) and the Klebanov-Tseytlin (KT) solution (Klebanov, 2000) by incorporating black hole backreaction, building on earlier efforts by the same authors (Mia et al., 2009-2011) to construct a UV-complete dual of large N thermal QCD. The paper also references the Ouyang extension (Ouyang, 2003) for fundamental matter and earlier non-extremal extensions of the cascading picture (KT-non-ex, thorimal, cotrone). The results enable future work to fill in missing steps toward a fully backreacted holographic model of thermal QCD with chemical potential.
