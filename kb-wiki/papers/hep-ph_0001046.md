# Polarized Parton Distribution Functions in the Nucleon

> Polarized parton distribution functions are determined by using world data from the longitudinally polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments. A new parametrization of the parton distribution functions is adopted by taking into account the positivity and the counting rule. From the fit to the asymmetry data A_1, the polarized distribution functions of u and d valence quarks, sea quarks, and gluon are obtained. The results indicate that the quark spin content is ΔΣ=0.20 and 0.05 in the leading order (LO) and the next-to-leading-order (NLO) MS-bar scheme, respectively. However, if x dependence of the sea-quark distribution is fixed at small x by "perturbative QCD" and Regge theory, it becomes ΔΣ=0.24 ~ 0.28 in the NLO. The small-x behavior cannot be uniquely determined by the existing data, which indicates the importance of future experiments. From our analysis, we propose one set of LO distributions and two sets of NLO ones as the longitudinally-polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The quark spin content ΔΣ extracted from a global fit to polarized deep inelastic scattering asymmetry data depends strongly on the order of the QCD evolution and on assumptions about the small‑x behavior of sea‑quark distributions, ranging from 0.05 (NLO with free sea) to 0.24–0.28 (NLO with sea fixed by perturbative QCD and Regge theory), indicating that the existing data cannot uniquely determine the small‑x shape.

## Method

The Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC) performed a χ² fit to 375 world data points of the longitudinal asymmetry A₁(x,Q²) for proton, neutron, and deuteron targets, using a new parametrization of polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) at an initial scale Q₀² = 1.0 GeV² that enforces the positivity condition and the quark counting rule. The polarized PDFs were evolved to the experimental Q² points by LO and NLO DGLAP equations using a brute‑force numerical method with cubic Spline interpolation, and the asymmetry A₁ was constructed as g₁/F₁ using the GRV98 unpolarized PDFs and the SLAC 1990 parametrization of R = σ_L/σ_T.

## Key numbers

- Quark spin content ΔΣ = 0.20 in LO and ΔΣ = 0.05 in NLO (MS‑bar scheme) from the fit with free sea‑quark distributions.
- With the small‑x sea fixed by “perturbative QCD” and Regge theory, ΔΣ = 0.24–0.28 in NLO.
- Total number of data points used in the fit: 375, all with Q² > 1.0 GeV².
- The initial scale for the parametrization is Q₀² = 1.0 GeV².
- Differences between GRV98, MRST, and CTEQ unpolarized F₂ at Q² = 5 GeV² in the range 0.001 < x < 0.7 are less than about 3%.

## Context

This work extends the post‑EMC effort to determine polarized PDFs from world DIS data, following earlier analyses such as those by Gehrmann and Stirling (1996), Altarelli et al. (1997), and the SMC fit (1998). The AAC provides one LO and two NLO sets of longitudinally polarized PDFs for use in predictions for polarized hadron‑hadron collisions (e.g., at RHIC) and for future experiments at SLAC, CERN (COMPASS), and DESY (HERMES). The strong sensitivity of ΔΣ to the small‑x sea assumption highlights the need for new data at low x to resolve the proton spin puzzle.
