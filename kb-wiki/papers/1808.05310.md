# Bayesian extraction of jet energy loss distributions in heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the factorization in perturbative QCD, a jet cross sections in heavy-ion collisions can be expressed as a convolution of the jet cross section in $p+p$ collisions and a jet energy loss distribution. Using this simple expression and the Markov Chain Monte Carlo method, we carry out Bayesian analyses of experimental data on jet spectra to extract energy loss distributions for both single inclusive and $γ$-triggered jets in $Pb+Pb$ collisions with different centralities at two colliding energies at the Large Hadron Collider. The average jet energy loss has a dependence on the initial jet energy that is slightly stronger than a logarithmic form and decreases from central to peripheral collisions. The extracted jet energy loss distributions with a scaling behavior in $x=Δp_T /\langle Δp_T\rangle$ have a large width. These are consistent with the linear Boltzmann transport model simulations, in which the observed jet quenching is caused on the average by only a few out-of-cone scatterings.

## Key claim
The paper uses Bayesian analyses of experimental jet spectra to extract jet energy loss distributions, finding that the average jet energy loss depends on initial jet energy slightly stronger than logarithmic and that the distributions scale in \(x = \Delta p_T / \langle \Delta p_T \rangle\) with a large width; the extracted distributions are consistent with linear Boltzmann transport model simulations in which jet quenching is caused on average by only a few out-of-cone scatterings.

## Method
The authors express the jet cross section in heavy-ion collisions as a convolution of the \(p+p\) jet cross section and a flavor-averaged jet energy loss distribution, based on perturbative QCD factorization. They assume the energy loss distribution is a \(\Gamma\) function of the scaled variable \(x = \Delta p_T / \langle \Delta p_T \rangle\) and the average energy loss follows \(\langle \Delta p_T \rangle = \beta p_T^\gamma \log(p_T)\). Using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) with uniform priors on parameters \([\alpha, \beta, \gamma]\), they perform Bayesian fits to ATLAS single inclusive jet \(R_{AA}\) data and CMS \(\gamma\)-triggered jet spectra in \(Pb+Pb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) and 5.02 TeV for different centralities.

## Key numbers
- For single inclusive jets in 0-10% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: \(\alpha = 3.87 \pm 2.93\), \(\beta = 1.40 \pm 1.12\), \(\gamma = 0.21 \pm 0.09\).
- For single inclusive jets in 20-30% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: \(\alpha = 4.47 \pm 2.83\), \(\beta = 1.12 \pm 0.47\), \(\gamma = 0.15 \pm 0.07\).
- For single inclusive jets in 0-10% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV: \(\alpha = 4.41 \pm 2.86\), \(\beta = 1.06 \pm 0.97\), \(\gamma = 0.26 \pm 0.06\).
- For \(\gamma\)-triggered jets in 0-30% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: \(\alpha = 2.13 \pm 1.28\), \(\beta = 2.68 \pm 1.40\), \(\gamma = 0.16 \pm 0.14\).
- For \(\gamma\)-triggered jets in 30-100% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV: \(\alpha = 3.75 \pm 2.81\), \(\beta = 0.55 \pm 0.44\), \(\gamma = 0.13 \pm 0.18\).
- For \(\gamma\)-triggered jets in 0-30% central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV: \(\alpha = 0.90 \pm 0.09\), \(\beta = 1.50 \pm 0.85\), \(\gamma = 0.21 \pm 0.12\).

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to extract jet transport coefficients from single inclusive hadron suppression, such as the systematic study by the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013) which narrowed uncertainties to within 40%, and earlier extractions of average jet energy loss from single inclusive jet data by Spousta (2015). The Bayesian extraction of full energy loss distributions, rather than just averages, provides a data-driven constraint that can be compared to linear Boltzmann transport model simulations (He 2018) and can help reduce model uncertainties in studies of the jet transport coefficient and jet-medium interaction properties in heavy-ion collisions.
