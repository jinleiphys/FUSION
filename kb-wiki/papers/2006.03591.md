# Zilch Vortical Effect, Berry Phase, and Kinetic Theory

> Rotating photon gas exhibits a chirality separation along the angular velocity which is manifested through a generation of helicity and zilch currents. In this paper we study this system using the corresponding Wigner function and construct elements of the covariant chiral kinetic theory for photons from first principles. The Wigner function is solved order-by-order in $\hbar$ and the unconstrained terms are fixed by matching with quantum field theory results. We further consider the zilch and helicity currents and show that both manifestations of the chirality transport originate in the Berry phase of photons similarly to other chiral effects. Constructing the kinetic description from the Wigner function we find that the frame vector needed to fix the definition of spin of a massless particle is, in fact, the vector of the residual gauge freedom for the free Maxwell theory. We also briefly comment on the possible relation between vortical responses in rotating systems of massless particles and the anomalies of underlying quantum field theory.

# Zilch Vortical Effect, Berry Phase, and Kinetic Theory

## Key claim
The zilch vortical effect (ZVE) in a rotating photon gas originates from the same topological Berry phase mechanism as the chiral vortical effect (CVE), and the frame vector needed to define photon spin in chiral kinetic theory corresponds to the residual gauge freedom of free Maxwell theory. The authors show this by constructing the Wigner function for photons order-by-order in ℏ and matching unconstrained terms with quantum field theory results, but note that the relation between vortical responses and anomalies of the underlying quantum field theory remains a comment rather than a proven result.

## Method
The authors compute the expectation value of the general symmetric zilch current in a rotating thermal photon gas using quantum field theory in cylindrical coordinates with Coulomb gauge, solving Maxwell equations for circularly polarized harmonics. They then construct a covariant chiral kinetic theory for photons using the Wigner-function formalism, expanding the Wigner function in powers of ℏ and fixing the free function U by requiring gauge invariance of the field-strength Wigner function and matching to the QFT zilch current results. The CKT current is expressed in terms of the spin tensor S^{μν}_n defined with a frame vector n^μ, and the zilch current is built from this CKT current.

## Key numbers
- The symmetric zilch current on the rotation axis at leading order in Ω is ⟨:Z̄^{(s)}_{30...0}:⟩|_{r→0} = (-1)^{(s-1)/2} ((s+2)(s+1)/(3s)) (Ω/π²) ∫₀^∞ dω ω^s f_B(βω) + O(Ω²), where s is the rank of the zilch tensor and f_B is the Bose-Einstein distribution.
- For the non-symmetrized zilch current (s=3), the result on the rotation axis is ⟨:Z^{(3)3}_{00}:⟩|_{r→0} = (8π² T⁴)/(45 ℏ²) Ω.
- The CKT calculation reproduces the QFT result: ⟨:Z̄^{(s)}_{30...0}:⟩|_{r→0} = (-1)^{(s-1)/2} ((s+2)(s+1)/(3s ℏ^{s-1})) (Ω/π²) ∫ dω ω^s f_B(βω) + O(Ω²).
- The equilibrium distribution function for the rotating ensemble is f_B(ξ) with ξ = β(ω - mΩ), where m is the angular quantum number and Ω is the angular velocity.

## Context
This work extends the study of chiral effects in rotating systems from fermions to photons, building on prior calculations of the photonic chiral vortical effect by Avkhadiev (2017) and Yamamoto (2017), and on the zilch vortical effect by Chernodub (2018) and Copetti (2018). The authors use the Wigner-function formalism previously applied to fermionic chiral kinetic theory by Chen (2012), Gao (2012), Hidaka (2016), and others, and generalize it to photons following the early works of Vasak (1987) and Elze (1986, 1989). The result enables further studies of gauge field contributions to spin polarization phenomena and connects the Berry-phase origin of chiral transport to the underlying quantum field theory, while leaving open the question of whether new anomalies exist for zilch currents in gravitational backgrounds.
