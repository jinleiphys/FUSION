# Coupled-Channel Model for $\bar{K}N$ Scattering in the Resonant Region

> We present a unitary multichannel model for $\bar{K}N$ scattering in the resonance region that fulfills unitarity. It has the correct analytical properties for the amplitudes once they are extended to the complex-$s$ plane and the partial waves have the right threshold behavior. To determine the parameters of the model, we have fitted single-energy partial waves up to $J=7/2$ and up to 2.15 GeV of energy in the center-of-mass reference frame obtaining the poles of the $螞^*$ and $危^*$ resonances, which are compared to previous analyses. We provide the most comprehensive picture of the $S=-1$ hyperon spectrum to date. Important differences are found between the available analyses making the gathering of further experimental information on $\bar{K}N$ scattering mandatory to make progress in the assessment of the hyperon spectrum.

# Coupled-Channel Model for $\bar{K}N$ Scattering in the Resonant Region

## Key claim
The authors present a unitary multichannel $K$-matrix model for $\bar{K}N$ scattering that correctly incorporates analytic properties and threshold behavior, enabling continuation to the complex-$s$ plane for pole extraction. However, the abstract's claim of providing "the most comprehensive picture of the $S=-1$ hyperon spectrum to date" is qualified by the finding that important differences exist between available analyses, making further experimental data on $\bar{K}N$ scattering mandatory before progress can be made.

## Method
The model uses an analytic $K$-matrix formalism with Chew-Mandelstam representation for the phase-space matrix $\rho(s,\ell)$, ensuring correct square-root branch points at thresholds and enabling continuation to the complex-$s$ plane. Single-energy partial waves up to $J=7/2$ and up to 2.15 GeV center-of-mass energy were fitted, with the $K$-matrix containing both pole terms (representing resonances) and background terms (parameterizing left-hand cuts from exchange processes). The $T$-matrix poles were extracted from the condition $\mathcal{D}(s)=0$ in unphysical Riemann sheets, using up to six pole/background components and up to 13 coupled channels per partial wave.

## Key numbers
- Fitted single-energy partial waves up to $J=7/2$ and up to 2.15 GeV center-of-mass energy
- Database contains approximately 8000 data points for $\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N$, 4500 for $\bar{K}N\to\pi\Lambda$, and 5000 for $\bar{K}N\to\pi\Sigma$ channels
- Energy range: $2.19 < s < 4.70$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to kaon lab momentum $0.288 < p_\text{lab} < 1.820$ GeV/$c$
- Interaction range parameter $r = 1$ fm; normalization factor $q_0 = 2$ GeV
- $\chi^2/\text{dof}$ values range from 0.10 ($G_{17}$ partial wave) to 9.70 ($S_{11}$ partial wave)
- Number of channels per partial wave ranges from 4 ($F_{07}$) to 13 ($D_{13}$)
- Statistical errors computed using 50 bootstrap resamplings; systematic errors estimated by pruning 20% of data points with 20 repetitions

## Context
This work extends the KSU model of Manley et al. (2013) by implementing unitarity analytically rather than only on the real axis, enabling unique continuation to unphysical Riemann sheets for pole extraction. It provides a comprehensive comparison with the dynamical coupled-channel model of Kamano et al. (2014, 2015) and the Review of Particle Physics listings. The model enables future partial-wave analyses of reactions such as $\gamma p \to K\bar{K}p$ at Jefferson Lab (CLAS12 and GluEx) and provides systematic uncertainty studies for pentaquark searches in $\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi K^- p$ decays.
