# Shadowing in the nuclear photoabsorption above the resonance region

> A model based on the hadronic fluctuations of the real photon is developed to describe the total photonucleon and photonuclear cross sections in the energy region above the nucleon resonances. The hadronic spectral function of the photon is derived including the finite width of vector-meson resonances and the quark-antiquark continuum. The shadowing effect is evaluated considering the effective interaction of the hadronic component with the bound nucleons within a Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering theory. The low energy onset of the shadowing effect is interpreted as a possible signature of a modification of the hadronic spectral function in the nuclear medium. A decrease of the $ρ$-meson mass in nuclei is suggested for a better explanation of the experimental data.

# Shadowing in the nuclear photoabsorption above the resonance region

## Key claim

The low-energy onset of the nuclear shadowing effect in photonuclear absorption (below 2 GeV) can be explained by a modification of the hadronic spectral function of the photon inside the nuclear medium, specifically a decrease of the ρ-meson mass in nuclei. The authors suggest this mass shift as a possible explanation for the experimental data, but note that the extracted mass shifts for light nuclei are significantly larger than most theoretical expectations and cannot be accounted for by mean field considerations alone.

## Method

The authors develop a model based on the hadronic fluctuations of the real photon, where the hadronic spectral function is derived from e+e- → hadrons data including the finite width of vector-meson resonances (ρ, ω, φ, J/ψ, ψ') and the quark-antiquark continuum. The shadowing effect is evaluated using a Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering theory up to fifth order, with energy-dependent vector-meson nucleon cross sections derived from photoproduction data. The ρ-meson mass shift in the nuclear medium is treated as a free parameter fitted to photonuclear absorption data for five nuclei (C, Al, Cu, Sn, Pb) in the energy range 1.65-30 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson nucleon cross section is parameterized as σρp(k) = 18 mb + 27 mb·GeV^(1/2)/√k
- The ρ-meson accounts for about 85% of the resonance contribution to the photonucleon cross section, the ω-meson for 9%, the φ-meson for 4%
- Extracted ρ-meson mass shifts: C: -163 ± 14 ± 50 MeV; Al: -133 ± 11 ± 40 MeV; Cu: -104 ± 14 ± 57 MeV; Sn: -115 ± 17 ± 53 MeV; Pb: -63 ± 20 ± 62 MeV
- The model covers photon energies k ≥ 1.65 GeV up to 30 GeV
- The χ² improves by about a factor of two when the ρ-meson mass shift is included compared to calculations with δmρ = 0

## Context

This work extends earlier Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) models by Bianchi et al. (1995) and Boreskov et al. (1996), which used δ-function vector-meson mass distributions and energy-independent vector-meson nucleon cross sections and failed to predict the nuclear damping observed below 2 GeV. It also improves upon a Generalized VMD calculation by Engel et al. (1997) that showed better agreement with the shadowing ratio but could not reproduce the absolute photonuclear cross section. The extracted mass shifts are compared with theoretical predictions from effective chiral Lagrangians, QCD sum rules, and quark bag models reviewed by Cassing et al. (1999), and with a measurement via the ³He(γ,π⁺π⁻)X reaction by Lolos et al. (1998) and Huber et al. (1998) suggesting a ~160 MeV ρ-mass reduction in ³He. The results enable further investigation of in-medium modifications of vector meson properties and suggest that local density distributions, rather than mean field approximations, may be needed to explain the large mass shifts observed in light nuclei.
