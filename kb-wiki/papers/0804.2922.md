# Pairing correlations and effective mass

> We study the effect of effective mass on pairing correlations in the ground states of superfluid nuclei $^{124}$Sn and $^{136}$Sn. Various parameter sets of Skyrme interactions and relativistic Lagrangians are adopted to study pairing correlations across a wide range of effective mass. It is shown that surface-type pairing interaction gives an almost constant pairing gap as a function of the effective mass, while volume-type pairing interaction shows rather strong dependence of the pairing gap upon the effective mass. The local pair potentials of various effective interactions are also examined in relation to the effective mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the effective mass dependence of the pairing gap in superfluid nuclei is almost entirely determined by the choice of pairing interaction (surface-type versus volume-type), not by the mean-field model (Skyrme Hartree-Fock or relativistic mean field), and that the surface-type pairing interaction yields a nearly constant pairing gap across a wide range of effective mass values (0.4 to 1.0).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) calculations for the spherical superfluid nuclei 124Sn and 136Sn using 13 different Skyrme interaction parameter sets and 3 relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian parameter sets, covering effective masses from 0.471 to 0.949. They employed two zero-range pairing interactions: a volume-type (β=0) and a surface-type (β=1) with fixed pairing strengths (323 MeV·fm⁻³ for volume-type, 999 MeV·fm⁻³ for surface-type), and computed the averaged neutron pairing gap energies using two different weighting schemes (v²-weighted and uv-weighted).

## Key numbers

- The effective mass m*/m of the adopted Skyrme interactions ranges from 0.471 (SIV) to 0.949 (SVI), with MSkA at 0.794.
- For 124Sn with surface-type pairing, the difference in the local pair potential at r=5.7 fm between SVI (m*/m=0.949) and SIV (m*/m=0.471) is only 19%, while for volume-type pairing it is 33%.
- For 124Sn with surface-type pairing, the difference in the uv-weighted averaged pairing gap between SIV and SVI is only 9%, while for volume-type pairing it is 39%.
- For 136Sn with surface-type pairing, the difference in the local pair potential at r=5.9 fm between SVI and SIV is 36%, while for volume-type pairing it is 65%.
- For 136Sn with surface-type pairing, the difference in the uv-weighted averaged pairing gap between SIV and SVI is 32%, while for volume-type pairing it is 72%.
- The RMF Lagrangians NL3 and NLSH have effective masses of 0.634 and 0.635, respectively.
- The neutron densities of 124Sn and 136Sn reach half their central densities at r=5.7 fm and r=5.9 fm, respectively, independent of the Skyrme interaction.

## Context

This work systematically extends earlier studies by Dobaczewski (1995) and Bender (2000) on pairing interactions in mean-field calculations by explicitly examining the role of effective mass across a wide parameter range. The results enable future studies to choose pairing interaction types based on the desired sensitivity to effective mass, and show that the surface-type pairing interaction is more robust against changes in the mean-field model. The findings are consistent with the known relationship between effective mass and level density near the Fermi energy discussed by Mahaux (1985).
