# Constraining nucleon strangeness

> Determining the nonperturbative $s\bar{s}$ content of the nucleon has attracted considerable interest and been the subject of numerous experimental searches. These measurements used a variety of reactions and place important limits on the vector form factors observed in parity-violating (PV) elastic scattering and the parton distributions determined by deep inelastic scattering (DIS). In spite of this progress, attempts to relate information obtained from elastic and DIS experiments have been sparse. To ameliorate this situation, we develop an interpolating model using light-front wave functions capable of computing both DIS and elastic observables. This framework is used to show that existing knowledge of DIS places significant restrictions on our wave functions. The result is that the predicted effects of nucleon strangeness on elastic observables are much smaller than those tolerated by direct fits to PV elastic scattering data alone. Using our model, we find $-0.024 \le μ_s \le 0.035$, and $-0.137 \le ρ^D_s \le 0.081$ for the strange contributions to the nucleon magnetic moment and charge radius. The model we develop also independently predicts the nucleon's strange spin content $Δs$ and scalar density $\langle N| \bar{s}s | N \rangle$, and for these we find agreement with previous determinations.

## Key claim
Existing constraints from deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on the strange quark content of the nucleon, when applied to a common light-front wave function framework, force the predicted strange contributions to elastic observables (magnetic moment and charge radius) to be much smaller than the ranges tolerated by direct fits to parity-violating elastic scattering data alone.

## Method
The authors developed a light-front wave function (LFWF) model describing the nucleon fluctuating into a strange (or antistrange) quark plus a scalar tetraquark spectator. They used this framework to compute both the strange quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) \(s(x)\) and \(\bar{s}(x)\) from DIS and the strange contributions to the nucleon's elastic Sachs form factors \(G^{s\bar{s}}_{E,M}(Q^2)\). The model parameters were constrained by requiring consistency with the CTEQ6.5S global analysis limits on the strange sea momentum asymmetry \(xS^-\) and total strange momentum \(xS^+\), and the resulting parameter space was scanned to find the extremal predictions for the strange magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) and strange charge radius \(\rho^D_s\).

## Key numbers
* The strange magnetic moment is constrained to \(-0.024 \le \mu_s \le 0.035\).
* The strange contribution to the nucleon charge radius (in units of \(\rho^D_s\)) is constrained to \(-0.137 \le \rho^D_s \le 0.081\).
* The CTEQ6.5S constraints used as input are \(0.018 \le xS^+ \le 0.040\) and \(-0.001 \le xS^- \le 0.005\) at \(Q^2 = 1.69\) GeV\(^2\).
* The total strange quark multiplicity \(P_s\) for the extremal Gaussian models is \(8.05\%\) (G1) and \(6.16\%\) (G2).

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing disconnect between elastic parity-violating scattering experiments (SAMPLE, HAPPEX I–III, Mainz, G0) and DIS global analyses (CTEQ, NuTeV) regarding nucleon strangeness. Prior attempts to relate these channels, such as those by Diehl (2007, 2013) using generalized parton distributions, required additional input from lattice QCD and vector meson dominance. The present model extends the quark-diquark LFWF approach of Cloët and Miller (2012), which successfully described the light-quark sector and predicted the nucleon spin content, to the strange sector. This enables a unified, parameter-constrained prediction that shows DIS data severely limit the allowed range for elastic strange form factors, guiding future experimental searches.
