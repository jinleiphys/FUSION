# Transverse structure of the pion beyond leading twist with basis light-front quantization

> We investigate the twist-$3$ transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) of the pion with basis light-front quantization. The twist-3 TMDs are not independent and can be decomposed into twist-$2$ and genuine twist-$3$ terms from the equations of motion (EOM). We compute the TMDs from the resulting light-front wave functions obtained by diagonalizing the light-front QCD Hamiltonian, determined for pion's constituent quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors, with three-dimensional confinement. We also obtain the twist-3 parton distribution functions (PDFs) and show that they preserve the sum rule, which affirms the robustness of our approach. This is the first time that theoretical predictions are made for subleading twist structures of the pion containing interference terms between two light-front Fock sectors.

## Key claim

The authors present the first theoretical predictions for subleading twist (twist-3) transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) of the pion that include interference terms between the quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock sectors, computed using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) at a model scale without TMD evolution.

## Method

The authors diagonalize an effective light-front QCD Hamiltonian for the pion, retaining the leading two Fock sectors (quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon) with a three-dimensional confining interaction, using basis light-front quantization with 2D harmonic oscillator basis functions for transverse degrees of freedom and plane-wave basis for longitudinal motion. They obtain light-front wave functions as eigenvectors of this Hamiltonian, then compute the twist-3 TMDs by decomposing them into twist-2 and genuine twist-3 terms using the equations of motion (EOM) in QCD, constructing overlap representations for the genuine twist-3 TMDs \(\tilde{e}\) and \(\tilde{f}^\perp\).

## Key numbers

- Model parameters for truncation \(\{N_{\text{max}}, K\} = \{14, 15\}\): quark mass \(m_q = 0.39\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.60\) GeV, harmonic oscillator basis scale \(b = 0.29\) GeV, confinement strength \(\kappa = 0.65\) GeV, vertex quark mass \(m_f = 5.69\) GeV, coupling constant \(g = 1.92\) (dimensionless).
- The genuine twist-3 TMD \(\tilde{e}\) is shown to preserve the sum rule, affirming the robustness of the approach.
- The IR cutoff is \(\lambda_{\text{IR}} \simeq b/\sqrt{N_{\text{max}}}\) and the UV cutoff is \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} \simeq b\sqrt{N_{\text{max}}}\) in momentum space.

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ calculations of leading-twist TMDs for the electron (Hu 2020), photon (Nair 2022), and proton (Hu 2022) to subleading twist for the pion, and complements model calculations of twist-3 TMDs using diquark spectator models (Jakob 1997), the MIT bag model (Jaffe 1991), and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (Ydrefors 2023). The results provide nonperturbative initial-scale components for twist-3 TMDs that can serve as input for TMD evolution calculations relevant to future experimental programs at JLab 12 and the Electron-Ion Collider.
