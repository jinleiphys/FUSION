# Correlation energies in the random phase approximation using realistic interactions

> The self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) based on a correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction is used to evaluate correlation energies in closed-shell nuclei beyond the Hartree-Fock level. The relevance of contributions associated with charge exchange excitations as well as the necessity to correct for the double counting of the second order contribution to the RPA ring summation are emphasized. Once these effects are properly accounted for, the RPA ring summation provides an efficient tool to assess the impact of long-range correlations on binding energies throughout the whole nuclear chart, which is of particular importance when starting from realistic interactions.

## Key claim

The RPA ring summation provides an efficient tool for evaluating correlation energies throughout the nuclear chart when starting from realistic interactions, but only after explicitly correcting for the double counting of the second-order contribution and including charge-exchange excitations; without these corrections, the naive application of standard RPA energy formulas leads to strong overbinding.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction V_UCOM derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the unitary correlation operator method. The RPA equations were projected onto good angular momentum J and parity, including all available multipolarities up to J=13, and the correlation energy was evaluated using two different formulations: a direct expectation value approach (Eq. A) and the quasi-boson approximation (Eq. B). The double counting of the second-order MBPT contribution was corrected by subtracting E^(2) from the RPA correlation energy.

## Key numbers

- The range of the tensor correlator in the triplet-even channel was fixed to I_theta = 0.09 fm^3 to reproduce binding energies of A ≤ 4 nuclei in no-core shell model calculations.
- All calculations were performed using 13 major harmonic oscillator shells.
- The RPA correlation energy contributions were evaluated for multipolarities J^pi = 0^± to 13^± for nuclei ^48Ca, ^90Zr, and ^208Pb.
- The RPA correlation energy increases with J, reaches a maximum for J = 3-4, and slowly decreases towards higher multipolarities.
- Beyond ^48Ca, the binding energies per nucleon including the corrected RPA correlation energy (HF+RPA^(B)-MBPT) are in excellent agreement with experimental values.

## Context

This work extends previous HF and many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) studies using V_lowk and V_UCOM interactions (Cor. 2005, Rot. 2006) by evaluating correlation energies through the RPA ring summation rather than low-order perturbation theory. The authors emphasize that while phenomenological models like Skyrme or Gogny functionals already mimic many-body correlations through fitting, realistic interactions require explicit treatment of long-range correlations. The results confirm that second-order MBPT provides a good approximation to the full RPA ring summation, with higher-order ring diagrams having a relatively small net effect, enabling efficient nuclear structure calculations throughout the nuclear chart.
