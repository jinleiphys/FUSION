# Deuteron electrodisintegration with unitarily evolved potentials

> Renormalization group (RG) methods used to soften Hamiltonians for nuclear many-body calculations change the effective resolution of the interaction. For nucleon knock-out processes, these RG transformations leave cross sections invariant, but initial-state wave functions, interaction currents, and final-state interactions are individually altered. This has implications for the factorization of nuclear structure and reactions. We use deuteron electrodisintegration as a controlled laboratory for studying how structure and reaction components are modified under RG evolution, without the complication of three-body forces or currents. The dependence of these changes on kinematics is explored.

## Key claim

Renormalization group (RG) transformations that soften nuclear Hamiltonians leave cross sections for nucleon knock-out processes invariant, but the individual components (initial-state wave functions, interaction currents, and final-state interactions) are altered in a resolution-dependent way; the paper uses deuteron electrodisintegration as a controlled laboratory to study how structure and reaction components are modified under SRG evolution without the complication of three-body forces or currents.

## Method

The authors calculate the longitudinal structure function f_L for deuteron electrodisintegration in the center-of-mass frame of the outgoing proton-neutron pair, using the formalism of Yang and Phillips (2013). They apply similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformations to evolve the deuteron wave function, the one-body current operator (which develops two-body components), and the final-state t-matrix, then decompose the unitary transformation matrix U into the identity plus a residual smooth matrix to track how each component contributes to the invariant cross section. The calculations are performed numerically in C++11 using momentum-space discretization, two-dimensional cubic spline interpolation for the potential, and nested Gaussian quadrature for integrations.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function normalization condition is (2/pi) * integral dp p^2 (psi_0^2(p) + psi_2^2(p)) = 1, where psi_0 and psi_2 are the S-wave (L=0) and D-wave (L=2) components
- The differential cross section formula includes the fine structure constant alpha and the four-momentum-squared Q^2 of the virtual photon
- The SRG decoupling scale lambda sets the division between low and high momentum in the Hamiltonian and between one-body and two-body current contributions
- The outgoing nucleon energy is E' = p^2 / M, where M is the average of proton and neutron mass

## Context

This work extends previous SRG operator evolution studies for the deuteron by Anderson et al. (2010) and builds on the chiral EFT approach to deuteron electrodisintegration by Yang and Phillips (2013). The paper addresses the puzzle that short-range correlations (SRCs) appear essentially absent from wave functions evolved with low-momentum Hamiltonians, yet cross sections remain invariant because the physics described by SRCs shifts to other components such as two-body currents. This enables future studies of how factorization of nuclear structure and reactions depends on resolution scale, with implications for interpreting high-momentum-transfer electron scattering experiments and extracting quantities like nuclear contacts.
