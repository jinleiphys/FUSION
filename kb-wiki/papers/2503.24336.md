# Leading qT/Q correction for Drell-Yan in TMD factorization

> The transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization theorem accommodates various types of power corrections. Among them, the least studied are qT/Q corrections, which become significant at large values of transverse momentum. These corrections partially originate from higher-twist TMD distributions, which exhibit singularity at small transverse distances. We propose a decomposition that reveals this singularity explicitly, and makes the qT/Q correction manifest. As a concrete application, we consider the next-to-leading power correction for the angular distributions in Drell-Yan, and determine the leading qT/Q corrections. These corrections are significant for the angular distributions A1 and A3, in complete agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the leading $q_T/Q$ correction to Drell-Yan angular distributions in TMD factorization originates from a previously overlooked singularity in twist-three TMD distributions at small transverse distances, and that after isolating this singularity via a decomposition into regular and singular parts, the resulting $q_T/Q$ term resolves a known discrepancy between theoretical predictions and LHC data for the $A_1$ angular coefficient.

## Method

The authors decompose twist-three TMD distributions into a regular part plus a singular part expressed as a perturbative kernel convolved with twist-two TMD distributions, making the $q_T/Q$ behavior explicit. They apply this decomposition to the next-to-leading power (NLP) hadron tensor for unpolarized Drell-Yan, compute the angular coefficients $A_0$ through $A_7$ in the Collins-Soper frame, and compare the resulting $q_T/Q$ corrections to experimental data from ATLAS, CMS, and LHCb.

## Key numbers

* The NLP cross-section decomposition yields three distinct power correction types: $k_T/Q$ (kinematic), $\Lambda/Q$ (genuine twist-three), and $q_T/Q$ (leading TMD approximation), with the $q_T/Q$ term scaling as $q_T/Q$ rather than the naive $\Lambda/Q$ expected from twist-three distributions.
* The hard coefficient function for the LP term at NLO is $\mathbb{C}_{\text{LP}}(\tau^2/\mu^2)=1+2a_s(\mu)C_F(-\mathbf{L}^2+3\mathbf{L}-8+7\pi^2/6)+\mathcal{O}(a_s^2)$, where $\mathbf{L}=\ln(\tau^2/\mu^2)$ and $C_F=(N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)$.
* The NLO coefficient functions for the genuine NLP part are given explicitly for $\mathbb{C}_R$ and $\mathbb{C}_I$ in terms of plus-distributions, with $\mathbf{L}=\ln(\tau^2/\mu^2)$, $C_F$, $C_A=N_c$, and the plus-distribution defined as $\int du f(u)/(u)_+=\int du (f(u)-f(0))/u$.
* The $q_T/Q$ correction is shown to be significant for the angular distributions $A_1$ and $A_3$, bringing theoretical predictions into agreement with LHC data where previous kinematic power corrections alone (from Piloneta (2024)) overestimated $A_1$ by approximately a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization power correction program of Vladimirov (2021), Balitsky (2020), and Rodini (2022) by identifying and isolating the $q_T/Q$ contribution hidden within genuine power corrections. Previous studies by Piloneta (2024) showed that kinematic power corrections (KPCs) described most Drell-Yan angular distributions well but failed for $A_1$, where predictions were roughly twice the measured value. The decomposition enables a systematic treatment of all three power correction types ($k_T/Q$, $\Lambda/Q$, $q_T/Q$) and provides a framework for extending the analysis to higher powers and other processes.
