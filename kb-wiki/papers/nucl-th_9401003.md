# Properties of the phi meson at finite temperature

> We calculate the $ϕ$-meson propagator at finite temperature at the one--loop order. The real and imaginary parts are studied separately in full kinematic ranges. From this activity we investigate how temperature affects such things as decay widths and dispersion relations. {}From here we estimate the thermal rate of lepton pair radiation in a hadron gas proceeding through $K^{+}K^{-}\to ϕ\to \ell^{+}\ell^{-}$ and $πρ\to ϕ\to \ell^{+}\ell^{-}$. We find several interesting things. From the dispersion relations we learn the effective mass calculated this way increases with temperature as does the partial width, but only slightly. At $T=200$ MeV, the mass increases by $\sim$ 4 MeV and the partial width increases by 34\%. Polarizations are indistinguishable for practical purposes.

## Key claim

The effective mass of the phi meson increases with temperature (by approximately 4 MeV at T=200 MeV) and the partial width increases by 34% at T=200 MeV, but these modifications are quite modest; the abstract's claim of "slight" increase is confirmed, and the authors note that polarization effects are indistinguishable for practical purposes.

## Method

The authors calculated the phi-meson self-energy at finite temperature at one-loop order using finite-temperature field theory in the imaginary-time formalism, including contributions from both kaon-loop diagrams and a new pi-rho loop diagram. They decomposed the self-energy into longitudinal and transverse scalar functions, then extracted the full propagator to study effective widths, masses, dispersion relations, and thermal dilepton emission rates. The coupling constants were fixed by matching vacuum partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- At T=200 MeV, the phi meson effective mass increases by approximately 4 MeV relative to its vacuum mass of 1019.4 MeV.
- At T=200 MeV, the partial width increases by 34% compared to its vacuum value.
- The total width (sum of both decay channels) goes from 2.74 MeV at T=0 to 2.90 MeV at T=100 MeV, 3.22 MeV at T=150 MeV, and 3.67 MeV at T=200 MeV.
- The effective mass rises by 0.42 MeV at T=100 MeV, 1.40 MeV at T=150 MeV, and 4.32 MeV at T=200 MeV.
- Input masses used: m_pi = 139.6 MeV, m_K = 493.6 MeV, m_rho = 768.1 MeV, m_phi = 1019.4 MeV; vacuum full width Gamma_phi = 4.43 MeV with partial width fractions 49.1% (KK) and 12.9% (rho pi).
- Coupling constants determined: m_phi^2 * g_{phi rho pi}^2 / (4 pi) = 0.19, g_{phi KK}^2 / (4 pi) = 1.65.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-temperature calculations of the phi meson by Haglin, Gale, and Kapusta (1991) by including the pi-rho decay channel contribution to the self-energy, which was previously omitted. The results contrast with QCD sum-rule calculations by Ko and collaborators that predicted a large decrease of the phi mass at finite temperature (approximately 100 MeV reduction at T=175 MeV). The authors argue their self-consistent approach has theoretical appeal because thermal modifications of both real and imaginary parts of the self-energy are computed simultaneously. This work enables improved estimates of thermal dilepton production rates in heavy-ion collisions and raises open questions about incorporating collisional broadening effects and modified pion dispersion relations at high temperature.
