# Hyperons in neutron stars

> Using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, the properties of neutron-star matter including hyperons are investigated. In the calculation, we consider both time and space components of the vector self-energies of baryons as well as the scalar ones. Furthermore, the effect of negative-energy states of baryons is partly taken into account. We obtain the maximum neutron-star mass of $2.08\,M_{\odot}$, which is consistent with the recently observed, massive neutron stars. We discuss a universal, repulsive three-body force for hyperons in matter.

## Key claim

Using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, the authors obtain a maximum neutron-star mass of 2.08 solar masses when hyperons are included, which is consistent with the observed massive neutron stars J1614-2230 and J0348+0432, but they note that the hyperon-meson couplings were determined by SU(6) symmetry and should be improved in future calculations.

## Method

The authors performed DBHF calculations for neutron-star matter including hyperons, considering both time and space components of the vector self-energies of baryons as well as the scalar ones, and partly including the effect of negative-energy states of baryons in the Bethe-Salpeter equation. They used the Bonn potentials for nucleon-nucleon interactions, determined hyperon-meson coupling constants using SU(6) symmetry and hypernuclei data, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions to obtain neutron-star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass of 2.08 solar masses (M_sun) for the B5M8 calculation (five baryons, eight mesons)
- Maximum neutron-star mass of 2.03 M_sun for B5M6 (five baryons, six mesons)
- Maximum neutron-star mass of 2.04 M_sun for B8M6 (eight baryons, six mesons)
- Maximum neutron-star mass of 2.44 M_sun for NM6 (nucleons and leptons only, no hyperons)
- Radius at maximum mass for B5M8: 13.1 km, with central density 0.74 fm^{-3}
- Sigma-minus hyperon first appears around baryon density 0.37 fm^{-3}
- Space component of vector self-energy reaches about 0.7 at baryon density 1.0 fm^{-3} in symmetric nuclear matter
- Saturation properties for Bonn A with modified coupling (A*): saturation density 0.168 fm^{-3}, binding energy -15.3 MeV, incompressibility 233 MeV, symmetry energy 33.6 MeV, slope parameter 95.0 MeV

## Context

This work extends DBHF calculations, previously applied only to nucleonic matter by groups such as Poschenrieder and Weigel, to include hyperon degrees of freedom for the first time in a relativistic framework. The results contrast with non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, which require explicit three-body forces to explain massive neutron stars when hyperons are included, as noted by Schulze and others. The authors argue that the DBHF approach inherently contains a universal repulsive three-body force through its density dependence, enabling the prediction of neutron-star masses consistent with the observed 1.97 +/- 0.04 M_sun (J1614-2230) and 2.01 +/- 0.04 M_sun (J0348+0432) without additional three-body forces for hyperons.
