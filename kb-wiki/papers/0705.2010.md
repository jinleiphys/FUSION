# Photoproduction of phi meson off deuteron near threshold

> We discuss coherent and incoherent $ϕ$ meson photoproduction off the deuteron at low energy and small momentum transfer with the aim to check whether the recent experimental data need for their interpretation the inclusion of exotic channels. Our analysis of the differential cross section and spin-density matrix elements shows that the existing data may be understood on the base of conventional dynamics. For a firm conclusion about a possible manifestation of exotic channels one has to improve the resolution of the data with providing additional information on channels with spin- and double-spin flip transitions being sensitive to the properties of the photoproduction amplitude in $γp$ and $γD$ reactions.

## Key claim

The existing LEPS and CLAS data on coherent and incoherent phi meson photoproduction off the deuteron near threshold can be understood within conventional dynamics (Pomeron and pseudoscalar meson exchange) without invoking exotic channels, but a firm conclusion about exotic channels requires improved data resolution and additional information on spin- and double-spin flip transitions.

## Method

The authors model the gamma p -> phi p amplitude using a modified Donnachie-Landshoff Pomeron exchange plus pseudoscalar (pi, eta) meson exchange, with parameters adjusted to reproduce the nucleon form factor of the two-gluon exchange model. For coherent gamma D -> phi D, they use a non-relativistic deuteron wave function from the Paris potential and a single-scattering approximation, where the isovector pion exchange cancels due to the isoscalar target. For incoherent gamma D -> phi np, they compute the missing mass distribution using the deuteron wave function and off-shell struck nucleon amplitudes, folding with a Gaussian resolution of sigma = 10 MeV to compare with data.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range studied: E_gamma = 1.6 - 3 GeV, with momentum transfer |t| less than or about 0.4 GeV^2
- Pomeron trajectory: alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25 t; strength factor C_P = 6 e g^2 / gamma_phi with gamma_phi about 6.7
- Pseudoscalar coupling constants: g_pi NN = 13.26, g_gamma phi pi = -0.14, g_gamma phi eta = -0.71, g_eta NN = 1.94; cutoff Lambda_pi = Lambda_eta = 1.05 GeV
- Structure factor Z(t) relates gamma D cross section to isoscalar nucleon cross section via d sigma(gamma D)/dt = 4 Z(t) d sigma(gamma <N>)/dt
- The model describes LEPS data for gamma D -> phi D at E_gamma = 2.07 - 2.17 GeV and |t_D| up to about 0.4 GeV^2, but underestimates data at higher |t|
- No bump-like structure is seen in the gamma D -> phi D energy dependence at E_gamma about 2 GeV, unlike the gamma p case
- The coherent contribution amounts to more than 30% of the total cross section at E_gamma about 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model for phi photoproduction off the proton (Titov and Kampfer, 2003) to the deuteron target, and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting recent LEPS data (Mibe, 2005; Wen Chen, 2007) and CLAS data (Mibe, 2007). The analysis shows that the bump-like structure seen in gamma p -> phi p around E_gamma = 2 GeV is absent in the coherent gamma D -> phi D reaction, consistent with the cancellation of isovector pion exchange. The results enable extraction of gamma n -> phi n observables from inclusive gamma D -> phi X measurements, which can be combined with gamma p data to search for exotic channels in future experiments with improved statistics and polarization measurements.
