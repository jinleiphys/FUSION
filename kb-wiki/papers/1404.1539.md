# Pion transverse charge density and the edge of hadrons

> We use the world data on the pion form factor for space-like kinematics and a technique previously used to extract the proton transverse densities to extract the transverse pion charge density and its uncertainty due the incomplete knowledge of the pion form factor at large values of Q2 and the experimental uncertainties. The pion charge density at small values of impact parameter b<0.1 fm is dominated by this incompleteness error while the range between 0.1-0.3 fm is relatively well constrained. A comparison of pion and proton transverse charge densities shows that the pion is denser than the proton for values of b<0.2 fm. The pion and proton transverse charge densities seem to be the same for values of b=0.3-0.6 fm. Future data from Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab) 12 GeV and the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will increase the dynamic extent of the form factor data to higher values of Q2 and thus reduce the uncertainties in the extracted pion transverse charge density.

## Key claim

The pion transverse charge density is extracted from world space-like form factor data using a finite-radius Bessel-series technique, with the result that the pion is denser than the proton for impact parameters \(b < 0.2\) fm, but the two densities coincide within uncertainties for \(b = 0.3\)–\(0.6\) fm; however, the extraction is dominated by incompleteness error from the lack of form factor data at \(Q^2 > 9.8\) GeV\(^2\), especially for \(b < 0.1\) fm.

## Method

The authors compiled world data on the pion elastic form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) from pion-electron scattering and pion electroproduction up to \(Q^2 = 9.8\) GeV\(^2\), then performed a global fit using a three-parameter empirical function (a sum of a monopole and a dipole term). They applied the finite-radius approximation (with \(R = 2\) fm) to expand the transverse charge density \(\rho_\pi(b)\) as a Bessel-function series, truncating at the term corresponding to the highest measured \(Q^2\) (\(n_{\text{max}} = 10\)). The experimental uncertainty was propagated from the fit, and the incompleteness error from the unmeasured high-\(Q^2\) region was estimated by taking the difference between the series contributions from a monopole model (upper bound) and a light-front constituent quark model (lower bound) for \(Q^2 > 10\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

* Pion form factor fit parameters: \(A = 0.384 \pm 0.071\), \(B = 1.203 \pm 0.101\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(C = 1.054 \pm 0.080\) GeV\(^{-2}\), with \(\chi^2 = 1.64\) (99% probability).
* Extracted pion charge radius from the fit: \(r_\pi = 0.641 \pm 0.025\) fm, consistent with the world-data value \(0.672 \pm 0.008\) fm.
* The largest experimental uncertainty on \(\rho_\pi(b)\) is \(0.1\) fm\(^{-2}\) at \(b = 0\).
* The series truncation uses \(n_{\text{max}} = 10\) terms, with the last term evaluated at \(Q_n^2 = 9.14\) GeV\(^2\), below the highest data point at \(Q^2 = 9.8\) GeV\(^2\).
* The pion and proton transverse charge densities coalesce within uncertainties for \(b = 0.3\)–\(0.6\) fm; the pion is denser for \(b < 0.2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the transverse-density technique previously applied to the proton by Venkat et al. (2011) to the pion, using the same finite-radius Bessel-series method. It improves on earlier pion analyses by Miller (2009, 2011) by using only space-like data, avoiding dispersion-relation models, and providing a quantitative uncertainty budget that separates experimental and incompleteness errors. The comparison of pion and proton densities probes the spatial structure of the proton’s pion cloud, following the work of Strikman and Weiss (2010) on the proton’s non-chiral core. Future data from JLab 12 GeV and the Electron-Ion Collider are expected to extend the form factor measurements to \(Q^2\) up to 25 GeV\(^2\), reducing the incompleteness error and enabling studies of the “edge” of hadrons.
