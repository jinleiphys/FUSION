# Blurred femtoscopy in two-proton decay

> We study the effects of final state interactions in two-proton emission by nuclei. Our approach is based on the solution the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. We show that the final relative energy between the protons is substantially influenced by the final state interactions. We also show that alternative correlation functions can be constructed showing large sensitivity to the spin of the diproton system.

## Key claim

Final-state interactions (FSI) in two-proton decay substantially alter the relative energy between the emitted protons, and specially constructed correlation functions can resolve the spin state (singlet vs. triplet) of the diproton system, but the calculations neglect the tunneling process and treat only the external motion after the protons emerge from the Coulomb barrier.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of two protons using a finite-difference method on a grid of 5000 spatial mesh points (0.1 fm spacing) and 2000 time mesh points (0.5 fm/c spacing). The center-of-mass motion is treated classically along a radial trajectory, while the relative motion evolves under the combined influence of the mutual proton-proton Coulomb and nuclear interaction and the tidal Coulomb field from the daughter nucleus. The continuum wavefunction is obtained by removing the bound-state component from the time-evolved wavefunction, and the probability amplitude for final relative momentum is computed via projection onto asymptotic Coulomb waves.

## Key numbers

- Two-proton decay of ⁴⁵Fe with a decay energy of 1.1 MeV is used as the test case.
- The proton-proton potential parameters are \(v_0 = -46.124\) MeV and \(b = 1.1809\) fm, yielding scattering length \(a_p = -7.8196\) fm and effective range \(\rho_0 = 2.790\) fm.
- The average initial separation \(r_0\) and binding energy \(B\) are approximately related by \(r_0 = \hbar (4 B m_p)^{-1/2}\).
- For initial separations \(r_0 < 6\) fm, FSI cannot be neglected; for \(r_0 < 4\) fm, the strong nuclear interaction noticeably reduces the relative energy.
- The correlation function for the triplet state equals \(-1\) at \(\Delta p = 0\), while for the singlet state it is close to zero at \(\Delta p = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of two-proton radioactivity, motivated by the observation of 19 events assigned to simultaneous correlated two-proton emission from the \(21^+\) isomeric state in ⁹⁴Ag (Mukha et al., 2006). It contrasts with Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) studies in heavy-ion collisions, where protons are emitted chaotically and FSI are the primary signal; here the protons retain memory of their initial spin admixture and wavefunction from the parent nucleus. The method enables future tests of Bell's inequalities using proton-proton spin entanglement in nuclear decay, complementing earlier tests in low-energy proton-proton scattering (Lamehi-Rachti and Mittig, 1976).
