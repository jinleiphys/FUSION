# Reaction mechanism and characteristics of T_{20} in d + ^3He backward elastic scattering at intermediate energies

> For backward elastic scattering of deuterons by ^3He, cross sections σand tensor analyzing power T_{20} are measured at E_d=140-270 MeV. The data are analyzed by the PWIA and by the general formula which includes virtual excitations of other channels, with the assumption of the proton transfer from ^3He to the deuteron. Using ^3He wave functions calculated by the Faddeev equation, the PWIA describes global features of the experimental data, while the virtual excitation effects are important for quantitative fits to the T_{20} data. Theoretical predictions on T_{20}, K_y^y (polarization transfer coefficient) and C_{yy} (spin correlation coefficient) are provided up to GeV energies.

## Key claim

The proton-transfer mechanism from ^3He to the deuteron is the dominant reaction process for d + ^3He backward elastic scattering at intermediate energies, but the quantitative description of the tensor analyzing power T20 requires including virtual excitations of other reaction channels through adjustable phase parameters, with the PWIA alone only capturing global features.

## Method

Cross sections and tensor analyzing powers were measured for d + ^3He backward elastic scattering at deuteron incident energies of 140, 200, and 270 MeV using a polarized deuteron beam from the RIKEN Accelerator Research Facility and a cryogenic ^3He gas target, with scattered ^3He particles detected by the magnetic spectrograph SMART. The data were analyzed using the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) assuming proton transfer from ^3He to the deuteron, with ^3He wave functions obtained from Faddeev calculations using the AV14 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Brazil three-nucleon force. A more general formula was also applied that includes virtual excitations of other reaction channels by introducing adjustable phase parameters Theta and Theta-prime between the scalar and tensor amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections measured at three energies: 16.3 ± 0.1 mb/sr at 140 MeV, 8.56 ± 0.03 mb/sr at 200 MeV, and 0.97 ± 0.08 mb/sr at 270 MeV
- Tensor analyzing power T20 measured at three energies: 0.07 ± 0.02 at 140 MeV, 0.15 ± 0.01 at 200 MeV, and 0.17 ± 0.03 at 270 MeV
- Systematic errors: ±10% for cross sections, ±2% for T20
- ^3He gas target density: 6.6 × 10^20 cm^-3
- The zero point of the S-state wave function u(k) for both ^3He and deuteron occurs at k = 0.40 GeV/c
- The zero point of the D-state wave function w(k) for ^3He occurs at k = 0.79 GeV/c with the three-nucleon force and at 0.96 GeV/c without it
- The PWIA calculation with Theta = 60 degrees reproduces the measured T20 data in the small-k region

## Context

This work extends the neutron-transfer mechanism successfully applied to d + p backward scattering by Arvieux (1984) and Punjabi (1995) to the d + ^3He system, where proton transfer from ^3He to the deuteron is the analogous mechanism. The opposite sign of the D-state to S-state ratio in ^3He compared to the deuteron, consistent with the asymptotic D/S ratio calculations by Eiro and Santos (1990), explains the opposite sign of T20 between the two scattering systems. The results enable future measurements of T20 at higher energies (particularly near k = 0.4 GeV/c where the observable is nearly independent of the phase parameters) and measurements of the polarization transfer coefficient K_y^y and spin correlation coefficient C_yy to provide additional constraints on the reaction mechanism and three-nucleon forces.
