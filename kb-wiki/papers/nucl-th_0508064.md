# Helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections on the nucleon

> We examine the energy dependence of single-meson photoproduction as it contributes to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule. For photon energies above approximately 1 GeV, through the full resonance region, this contribution dominates the proton sum rule integral. Over the same energy region, our single-pion contribution to the neutron sum rule also qualitatively follows a recent set of GDH data. The predicted neutral-pion contribution to the neutron sum rule is nearly zero above 1 GeV in this result. The SAID and Mainz (MAID) results are very different for a number of observables over this energy region.

## Key claim

Single-meson photoproduction, particularly single-pion production, dominates the proton Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule integral for photon energies above approximately 1 GeV, but the authors caution that conclusions regarding the neutron sum rule are much less solid because the underlying single-pion photoproduction database for neutron targets is less complete and precise than for proton targets.

## Method

The authors used phenomenological single-pion photoproduction amplitudes from the SAID and MAID analyses to compute the helicity-dependent cross section difference (sigma_3/2 minus sigma_1/2) and its contribution to the GDH sum rule integral for both proton and neutron targets. They compared these single-pion predictions to measured GDH data and to total photoabsorption cross sections, and also examined contributions from eta-meson and kaon photoproduction. The analysis covered photon energies from threshold through the full resonance region, with particular focus on energies above 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The left-hand side of the GDH sum rule is (2 pi^2 alpha / M^2) times (kappa_p,n)^2, where kappa_p and kappa_n are the proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments, M is the nucleon mass, and alpha is the fine structure constant
- The single-pion contribution to the neutron sum rule shows a dip near 900 MeV in both SAID and MAID analyses
- The SAID solution predicts a negligible n pi^0 contribution to the neutron sum rule above about 1 GeV
- The p pi^- component is mainly responsible for the qualitative agreement between SAID predictions and GDH data for the neutron sum rule
- Both the SAID solution and data suggest an upward trend near 2 GeV, though the authors note this may be an artifact

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of the GDH integral that used phenomenological single-pion photoproduction amplitudes and crude estimates for two-pion and other-meson production, as referenced in the paper's discussion of early results (Old, 1990s). The analysis compares SAID and MAID (Mainz) analyses, which show very different predictions for many observables above 1 GeV. The results enable further investigation of the apparent cancellation of large multi-pion contributions to the proton GDH sum rule, and motivate additional measurements of two-pion production and single-meson photoproduction off the neutron to resolve discrepancies between SAID and MAID predictions and to search for states coupling more strongly to gamma n than gamma p.
