# The stress-energy tensor of a quark moving through a strongly-coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma: comparing hydrodynamics and AdS/CFT

> The stress-energy tensor of a quark moving through a strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma is evaluated using gauge/string duality. The accuracy with which the resulting wake, in position space, is reproduced by hydrodynamics is examined. Remarkable agreement is found between hydrodynamics and the complete result down to distances less than 2/T away from the quark. In performing the gravitational analysis, we use a relatively simple formulation of the bulk to boundary problem in which the linearized Einstein field equations are fully decoupled. Our analysis easily generalizes to other sources in the bulk.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that hydrodynamics reproduces the exact stress-energy tensor of a quark moving through a strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma with remarkable accuracy down to distances less than 2/T from the quark, despite the fact that gradients in the hydrodynamic variables become large at such short distances.

## Method

The authors use gauge/string duality (AdS/CFT) to compute the perturbation in the stress-energy tensor caused by a heavy quark moving at constant velocity through a strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma at temperature T. They formulate the linearized Einstein field equations in the AdS-Schwarzschild background using gauge-invariant variables that fully decouple the equations, then solve these equations numerically by constructing Green's functions from homogeneous solutions. The resulting stress-energy tensor in position space is compared to predictions from relativistic hydrodynamics with effective sources matched to the microscopic drag force.

## Key numbers

- The drag force on the quark is f = (pi/2) * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 * v / sqrt(1-v^2), where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling
- The speed of sound in the plasma is c_s^2 = 1/3
- The sound attenuation constant is gamma = 1/(3 pi T)
- The transverse momentum diffusion constant is D = 1/(4 pi T)
- Hydrodynamics agrees with the exact result down to distances less than 2/T away from the quark

## Context

This work extends previous studies that calculated the energy density (Chesler 2007, Gubser 2007) and energy flux (Gubser 2007) associated with a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled SYM plasma, which had shown qualitative features like Mach cone formation but lacked quantitative comparison with hydrodynamics. The paper also builds on the hydrodynamic analysis of Casalderrey-Solana (2006) that did not fully specify the effective sources. The results support the use of hydrodynamics to model energy and momentum transport from high-energy particles traversing a real quark-gluon plasma, despite large gradients in hydrodynamic variables.
