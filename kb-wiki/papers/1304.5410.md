# Calculation of proton-${}^3$He elastic scattering between 7 and 35 MeV

> Background: Theoretical calculations of the four-particle scattering above the four-cluster breakup threshold are technically very difficult due to nontrivial singularities or boundary conditions. Further complications arise when the long-range Coulomb force is present. Purpose: We aim at calculating proton-${}^3$He elastic scattering observables above three- and four-cluster breakup threshold. Methods: We employ Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for the four-nucleon transition operators and solve them in the momentum-space framework using the complex-energy method whose accuracy and practical applicability is improved by a special integration method. Results: Using realistic nuclear interaction models we obtain fully converged results for the proton-${}^3$He elastic scattering. The differential cross section, proton and ${}^3$He analyzing powers, spin correlation and spin transfer coefficients are calculated at proton energies ranging from 7 to 35 MeV. Effective three- and four-nucleon forces are included via the explicit excitation of a nucleon to a $Δ$ isobar. Conclusions: Realistic proton-${}^3$He scattering calculations above the four-nucleon breakup threshold are feasible. There is quite good agreement between the theoretical predictions and experimental data for the proton-${}^3$He scattering in the considered energy regime. The most remarkable disagreements are the peak of the proton analyzing power at lower energies and the minimum of the differential cross section at higher energies. Inclusion of the $Δ$ isobar reduces the latter discrepancy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that fully converged calculations of proton-${}^3$He elastic scattering above the four-nucleon breakup threshold are feasible using the AGS equations with realistic nuclear interactions, but the agreement with experimental data is qualified by two persistent discrepancies: the peak of the proton analyzing power at lower energies and the minimum of the differential cross section at higher energies, with the latter only partially reduced by including $\Delta$-isobar effects.

## Method

The authors solved the symmetrized Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) four-particle equations in momentum-space partial-wave framework, using the complex-energy method with a special integration technique to handle quasi-singularities. The $pp$ Coulomb interaction was treated via screening and renormalization, with screening radii of 8 to 10 fm and a sharp screening exponent $n=6$. Calculations employed realistic high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, INOY04, CD Bonn, CD Bonn+$\Delta$) including up to partial waves with $l_x,l_y,l_z,j_x,j_y = 7$, $J_y = 13/2$, and $\mathcal{J} = 6$.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section, analyzing powers, spin correlations, and spin transfer coefficients calculated at proton energies from 7 to 35 MeV
- The ${}^3$He binding energy calculated with INOY04 is 7.73 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 7.72 MeV; AV18 gives 6.92 MeV, CD Bonn gives 7.26 MeV, CD Bonn+$\Delta$ gives 7.54 MeV
- Screening radii of 8 to 10 fm were sufficient for convergence in the energy regime studied
- Grid points for momentum discretization ranged from 30 (at lower energies) to 35 (at higher energies)
- The $\varepsilon$ parameter for the complex-energy method ranged from 1 to 2 MeV at lowest energies and from 2 to 4 MeV at highest energies
- The bound state energy of the asymptotic $p$-${}^3$He state is $\epsilon_1 = -7.72$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AGS calculations for neutron-${}^3$H elastic scattering above breakup threshold (Deltuva 2012, 2013) to the more challenging proton-${}^3$He system with long-range Coulomb forces. It builds on benchmark comparisons between the AGS method, hyperspherical harmonics expansion (Viviani 2001, Kievsky 2008), and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations (Lazauskas 2004, 2009) that were performed below three-cluster breakup threshold (Viviani 2011). The results enable future calculations of other four-nucleon reactions and provide a testing ground for effective field theory generated three-nucleon forces (Epelbaum 2000, Machleidt 2011).
