# Heavy-ion collisions described by a new QMD code interfaced to FLUKA: model validation by comparisons with experimental data concerning neutron and charged fragment production

> A new code, based on the Quantum Molecular Dynamics theoretical approach, has been developed and interfaced to the FLUKA evaporation/fission/Fermi break-up module. At present, this code is undergoing a series of validation tests. In this paper its predictions are compared to measured charged fragment yields and double differential neutron spectra in thin target heavy-ion reactions, at bombarding energies of about 100 MeV/A. The comparisons with the predictions of a modified version of RQMD 2.4 originally developed in Frankfurt, already available in the FLUKA code, are presented and potential improvements are briefly sketched.

## Key claim
A new Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code, interfaced to the FLUKA evaporation/fission/Fermi break-up module, reproduces measured double-differential neutron spectra and charged fragment yields in thin-target heavy-ion reactions at bombarding energies around 100 MeV/A, with overall good agreement; however, the code underestimates neutron emission in the very forward direction and shows discrepancies with the modified RQMD 2.4 code for intermediate-mass fragment yields in asymmetric systems.

## Method
The authors developed a QMD code where each nucleon is represented as a Gaussian wave-packet, with the Hamiltonian including Skyrme-II (attractive), Skyrme-III (repulsive), symmetry, surface, and Coulomb terms. Nuclear ground states were initialized by sampling spatial coordinates and momenta constrained to reproduce experimental binding energies, then evolved for 250-300 fm/c to ensure stability. Ion-ion collisions were simulated by boosting stored nuclear states toward each other, and hot fragments formed during the fast stage were de-excited using the FLUKA module (including gamma emission, evaporation, Fermi break-up, and fission). Predictions were compared to experimental data from Chiba et al. and to simulations with a modified version of the RQMD 2.4 code.

## Key numbers
- Bombarding energies: 135 MeV/A for He, C, Ne ions; 95 MeV/A for Ar ions, on C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets.
- Nuclear ground states evolved for 250-300 fm/c to check stability before use in collision simulations.
- Reaction mass asymmetry eta = |A_proj - A_targ| / (A_proj + A_targ) values: 0.88 for He + Cu, 0.22 for Ar + Cu.
- QMD code predicted higher He fragment yield than RQMD for He + Cu at 135 MeV/A; discrepancies increased with reaction mass asymmetry.
- For neutron spectra at intermediate emission angles (30 degrees to 80 degrees), QMD predicted the high-energy end better than RQMD; both models showed comparable agreement at backward angles (>= 90 degrees).

## Context
This work extends the application of QMD models to heavy-ion collisions at non-relativistic energies (around 100 MeV/A), building on the RQMD 2.4 code originally developed by Sorge (1990s) and modified by the group for fragment production. The new QMD code improves upon RQMD by distinguishing neutrons and protons, including Coulomb and surface terms in the Hamiltonian, and using mass-dependent parameters for asymmetric systems as suggested by China (2005). The code enables future studies of multifragmentation and can be extended to higher energies by incorporating pion production via resonance formation and angular-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
