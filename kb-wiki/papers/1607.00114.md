# Microscopic Description of Diffractive Deuteron Breakup by He-3 Nuclei

> A microscopic formalism for describing observed cross sections for deuteron breakup by three-nucleon nuclei was developed on the basis of the diffraction nuclear model. A general formula that describes the amplitude for the reaction d+3He->3He+p+n and which involves only one adjustable parameter was obtained by using expansions of the integrands involved in terms of a Gaussian basis. This formula was used to analyze experimental data on the exclusive cross sections for deuteron breakup by He-3 nuclei at the projectile energy of 89.4 MeV. The importance of employing, in calculations, a deuteron wave function that has a correct asymptotic behavior at large nucleon-nucleon distances was demonstrated.

# Microscopic Description of Diffractive Deuteron Breakup by He-3 Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper claims that a microscopic diffraction model with only one adjustable parameter can describe the exclusive cross sections for deuteron breakup by He-3 nuclei at 89.4 MeV, but only when the deuteron wave function has correct asymptotic behavior at large nucleon-nucleon distances; the model's agreement with data degrades at larger proton emission angles because the diffraction approximation condition (momentum transfer and relative momentum much less than 1) is violated.

## Method

The author developed a formalism based on the diffraction nuclear model for the reaction d+3He→3He+p+n, expanding all integrands (nucleon density distributions, deuteron wave functions, and nucleon-nucleon profile functions) in Gaussian basis sets to evaluate the eight-dimensional scattering amplitude analytically. The amplitude was reduced to triple and quadruple sums involving only one adjustable parameter, the normalization N_W of the imaginary part of the high-energy nucleon-nucleon potential, and was used to compute exclusive cross sections via equation (19) for comparison with experimental data from a coplanar geometry experiment at 89.4 MeV projectile energy.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energy: 89.4 MeV for 3He nuclei incident on deuteron targets.
- Coulomb barrier height approximately 1.5 MeV, neglected because emission angles exceed 20 degrees.
- The normalization parameter N_W varied from 0.13 (at θ2=20°) to 1.0 (at θ2=50° and 55°) to fit data.
- The simple Gaussian deuteron wave function (21) with adjustable parameter α ranging from 0.12 (20°) to 1.0 (55°) described data only in the proton-energy range E2 ≤ 20–30 MeV.
- Experimental cross sections show two peaks: a small peak in E2 = 10–20 MeV and a large peak in E2 = 30–50 MeV.
- Root-mean-square range of nucleon-nucleon interaction: R²_NN ≈ 0.65 fm².

## Context

This work extends the diffraction model approach previously applied to the similar breakup reaction 3H(d,p)n3H by Sitenko and collaborators (referenced as [9]), which used a simple Gaussian deuteron wave function and profile functions with free parameters. The present study improves upon that work by using a realistic deuteron wave function with correct asymptotic behavior (from variational calculations with the K2 nucleon-nucleon potential) and by reducing the number of adjustable parameters to one. The results are compared with experimental data from [11] and with four-body scattering model calculations from the same reference; the present formalism reproduces both peaks in the proton energy spectra, whereas the earlier four-body model described only the second peak quantitatively. The formalism is intended to be applicable to other few-body breakup problems such as deuteron breakup by deuterons or breakup of tritons and 3He by nucleons.
