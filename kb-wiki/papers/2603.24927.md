# Curvature Corrections to the Yukawa Potential in Tolman Metrics

> This work investigates curvature-induced modifications to the Yukawa potential in static, spherically symmetric spacetimes described by Tolman metrics, focusing on their implications for compact stellar objects, with particular application to solutions IV and VI. Motivated by the interplay of quantum interactions and strong gravitational fields in systems like neutron stars, we derive explicit corrections to the Yukawa potential for these metrics. Contrary to previous findings suggesting that curvature corrections break the radial symmetry of the interacting potential near a highly charged black hole, we demonstrate that Tolman metric corrections preserve this symmetry in the local inertial frame. Numerical estimates for astrophysical objects reveal energy shifts of the order of $10^{-34}$ MeV for solution IV. The Tolman VI solution, while singular at the center, yields comparable corrections for most of the fluid sphere radius. A detailed analysis of the repulsive or attractive nature of these curvature corrections for a local observer is provided for each scenario. These results highlight the role of spacetime geometry in shaping quantum interactions and provide a foundation for future studies of nuclear interactions within the context of relativistic stars.

# Curvature Corrections to the Yukawa Potential in Tolman Metrics

## Key claim

Contrary to previous findings that curvature corrections break the radial symmetry of the interacting potential near a highly charged black hole, the authors demonstrate that Tolman metric corrections preserve this symmetry in the local inertial frame. The energy shifts from curvature corrections are extremely small, of order 10^{-34} MeV for Tolman IV solutions, but the qualitative nature (attractive or repulsive) depends on the local frame position and the specific Tolman solution.

## Method

The authors compute the Feynman propagator for a scalar field in curved spacetime using Riemann normal coordinates, expanding to first order in the Ricci tensor. They apply this to a phi^3-like theory to derive curvature corrections to the Yukawa potential in the Born approximation, then specialize to static, spherically symmetric Tolman IV and Tolman VI metrics. The Ricci tensor components and curvature scalar are computed in the local inertial frame using spatial tetrads (dreibein), and the corrected potential is expressed in terms of the compactness parameters of the stellar objects.

## Key numbers

- Energy shifts of order 10^{-34} MeV for Tolman IV solution applied to astrophysical objects
- For Tolman IV at the center (r' -> 0), the correction coefficients are R_1 = 3 r_s (r_star - 3 r_s) / [r_star^3 (2 r_star - 3 r_s)] and R_2 = 3 r_s (r_star - r_s) / [r_star^3 (2 r_star - 3 r_s)], where r_s = 2GM is the Schwarzschild radius
- Tolman VI solution fixes the mass-radius ratio as M / r_star = 3 / (14 G)
- For Tolman VI, the metric component e^g = 7/4 (constant), and the Kretschmann scalar at n=1/2 is K = 24 (3 A^2 - 10 A B r + 15 B^2 r^2) / [49 r^4 (A^2 - 2 A B r + B^2 r^2)]
- Curvature corrections become relevant when spacetime curvature quantities are approximately 10 fm^{-2} (~ (20 MeV)^2 in natural units)
- Newton's constant in natural units: G = m_P^{-2} ≈ 6.709 × 10^{-45} MeV^{-2}
- Numerical examples use Pulsar J0740+6620 (M = 2.1 M_sun, r_star = 12.32 km) and Compact Object HESS J1731-347 (M = 0.8 M_sun, r_star = 10.42 km), showing corrections on order 10^{-34} to 10^{-32} MeV^2

## Context

This work extends the curvature-induced modifications to the Yukawa potential derived in Zamperlini (2025) by applying them to Tolman metrics (Tolman, 1939), which are static, spherically symmetric perfect fluid solutions used as first approximations for compact stars in General Relativity. The formalism builds on the Feynman propagator representation in Riemann normal coordinates developed by Bunch and Parker (1979) and Bunch (1981). The results contradict earlier findings that curvature corrections break radial symmetry near highly charged black holes, showing instead that Tolman metric corrections preserve symmetry due to the isotropic nature of the fluid's energy-momentum tensor. This work enables future studies of nuclear interactions within relativistic stars, particularly for neutron stars as reviewed by Ozel (2016), Hamel (2008), Lattimer (2000), and Menezes (2021).
