# Density Functional Theory: Methods and Problems

> The application of density functional theory to nuclear structure is discussed, highlighting the current status of the effective action approach using effective field theory, and outlining future challenges.

## Key claim

The paper argues that density functional theory (DFT) for nuclear structure can be systematically constructed from microscopic effective field theory (EFT) using the effective action formalism and inversion method, but this program faces significant challenges including long-range effects, broken symmetries, and the need for quantitative connection to microscopic interactions.

## Method

The author develops DFT for nuclear systems by starting from the partition function with external sources coupled to densities, then performing Legendre transformations to obtain effective actions that serve as energy functionals. The inversion method of Fukuda is used to expand quantities in a hierarchy ordered by EFT power counting, with zeroth order corresponding to the non-interacting Kohn-Sham system that yields the exact density. The approach is extended to include kinetic energy density (meta-GGA), pairing via Nambu-Gorkov formalism, and time-dependent sources for collective excitations.

## Key numbers

- For trapped fermions with scattering lengths a_p = a_s, the rho-only functional gives E/A = 7.66 and sqrt(mean square radius) = 2.87, while the rho-tau functional gives E/A = 7.65 and sqrt(mean square radius) = 2.87
- For a_p = 2 a_s, the rho-only functional gives E/A = 8.33 and sqrt(mean square radius) = 3.10, while the rho-tau functional gives E/A = 8.30 and sqrt(mean square radius) = 3.09
- The pairing gap in a dilute Fermi gas with two spin states is reduced approximately fifty percent by induced interactions in the weak-coupling limit
- EFT coupling constants scale as C_{2i} ~ (4 pi / M) R^{2i+1} and D_{2i} ~ (4 pi / M) R^{2i+4}, where R is the interaction range

## Context

This work extends the effective action approach to DFT developed by Fukuda and collaborators, and connects to conventional nuclear structure methods such as Skyrme Hartree-Fock (reviewed by Ring and Schuck, and Bender et al.) and relativistic mean-field models. The paper outlines a program to derive nuclear energy functionals from chiral effective field theory and low-momentum interactions (as pursued by Schwenk and collaborators), aiming for model-independent functionals with error estimates, analogous to how Coulomb DFT achieves ab initio accuracy from uniform electron gas calculations.
