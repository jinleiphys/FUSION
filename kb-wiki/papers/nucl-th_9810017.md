# Exchange currents in radiative hyperon decays and hyperon charge radii

> Radiative decays of decuplet hyperons and octet hyperon charge radii are evaluated in a chiral constituent quark model emphasizing the role of exchange currents. Exchange currents largely cancel for the M1 decay amplitudes, while they dominate the E2 amplitude. Due to the pseudoscalar meson cloud the charge radii of Sigma^- and Xi^- are almost as large as the proton radius, in agreement with recent experimental results from SELEX. Strangeness suppression is weakened by exchange currents for several observables.

## Key claim

Exchange currents largely cancel for the M1 decay amplitudes in radiative hyperon decays, while they dominate the E2 amplitude; due to the pseudoscalar meson cloud, the charge radii of Σ⁻ and Ξ⁻ are almost as large as the proton radius, in agreement with recent experimental results from SELEX, but this agreement is conditional on the inclusion of exchange currents which weaken strangeness suppression.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic chiral constituent quark model with spherical (0s)³ oscillator wave functions and SU_SF(6) spin-flavor states, incorporating a two-body confinement potential, pseudoscalar meson octet exchange (π, K, η) for intermediate-range interactions, and residual one-gluon exchange at short range. Electromagnetic currents were constructed via non-relativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams to satisfy the continuity equation, including impulse approximation, PS-meson pair and in-flight currents, gluon-pair current, and scalar exchange current. Transition magnetic and quadrupole moments for decuplet hyperon radiative decays and charge radii for octet hyperons were then computed.

## Key numbers

- Transition magnetic moment for γp ↔ Δ⁺: μ_Tot = 2.533 μ_N, with impulse contribution 2.828 μ_N, PS-meson contribution 0.312 μ_N, and gluon+scalar contribution -0.609 μ_N
- Transition quadrupole moment for γp ↔ Δ⁺: Q_PS = -0.031 fm², Q_G = -0.058 fm²
- Decay width for γp ↔ Δ⁺: Γ = 350 keV; E2/M1 ratio = -3.65%
- Decay width for γΣ⁻ ↔ Σ*⁻: Γ = 3.61 keV; E2/M1 ratio = -5.5%
- Decay width for γΞ⁻ ↔ Ξ*⁻: Γ = 6.18 keV; E2/M1 ratio = -2.8%
- Total charge radius squared for proton: r²_Tot = 0.651 fm², corresponding to √|r²_Tot| = 0.807 fm
- Total charge radius squared for Σ⁻: r²_Tot = 0.643 fm², corresponding to √|r²_Tot| = 0.802 fm
- Total charge radius squared for Ξ⁻: r²_Tot = 0.587 fm², corresponding to √|r²_Tot| = 0.766 fm
- SELEX experimental result for Σ⁻ charge radius: r²_Σ⁻ = 0.60 ± 0.08 (stat) ± 0.08 (syst) fm²

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral constituent quark model calculations by Wagner (1995) for hyperon masses and magnetic moments, now applying the same framework to radiative decuplet hyperon decays and octet hyperon charge radii. The results contradict the SU_F(3) flavor-symmetric expectation that charge radii decrease with increasing strangeness, showing instead that exchange currents from the pseudoscalar meson cloud nearly eliminate strangeness suppression. The calculations enable comparison with ongoing experiments at new facilities measuring radiative decays of Σ* and Ξ* hyperons (Russ, 1995) and with SELEX measurements of hyperon charge radii (Eschrich, 1998), potentially pinning down non-valence degrees of freedom in the effective quark-quark interaction.
