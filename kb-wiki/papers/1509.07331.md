# Violations of discrete space-time symmetries in chiral effective field theory

> We review recent progress in the theoretical description of the violation of discrete space-time symmetries in hadronic and nuclear systems. We focus on parity-violating and time-reversal-conserving interactions which are induced by the Standard Model weak interaction, and on parity- and time-reversal-violating interactions which can be caused by a nonzero QCD theta term or by beyond-the-Standard Model physics. We discuss the origins of such interactions and review the development of the chiral effective field theory extension that includes discrete symmetry violations. We discuss the construction of symmetry-violating chiral Lagrangians and nucleon-nucleon potentials and their applications in few-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how chiral effective field theory (χEFT) provides a systematic framework for describing parity-violating (PVTC) and parity- and time-reversal-violating (PVTV) interactions in hadronic and nuclear systems, but emphasizes that the size of the weak pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) remains unknown despite significant experimental and theoretical effort, and that the isovector four-quark operator's suppression by \(\sin^2\theta_W \sim 1/5\) may be further enhanced by large-\(N_c\) arguments, making formally higher-order corrections potentially larger than expected.

## Method

The authors construct chiral Lagrangians and nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials for PVTC and PVTV interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) by matching microscopic four-quark operators from the Standard Model weak interaction and from beyond-the-Standard-Model sources (QCD \(\bar\theta\) term, quark (chromo-)EDMs, gluon CEDM, and four-quark left-right operators) onto hadronic degrees of freedom (pions and nucleons) using chiral symmetry and Weinberg power counting. They derive the PVTC potential including one-pion-exchange (OPE) at leading order, two-pion-exchange (TPE) diagrams and five NN contact interactions at NLO, and additional TPE terms proportional to \(h_\pi c_4\) at N²LO, while the PVTV potential is constructed analogously from the \(\bar\theta\) term and dimension-six CPV operators.

## Key numbers

* The weak pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) has a DDH "best" value of \(0.46 \times 10^{-6}\) and a DDH range of \((0.0 \rightarrow 1.2) \times 10^{-6}\); the KMW estimate gives \(0.10 \times 10^{-6}\).
* Fermi's constant \(G_F \simeq 1.16 \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\) sets the scale of PVTC interactions; the weak mixing angle \(\sin^2\theta_W \simeq 0.23\) suppresses the isovector and isotensor operators by factors of about 5 and 2, respectively.
* The QCD \(\bar\theta\) term is constrained to \(\bar\theta < 10^{-10}\) from the neutron EDM limit (Crewther et al., 1979).
* The chiral-symmetry-breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 1~\text{GeV}\); the pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92.4~\text{MeV}\).
* The LO PVTC potential involves the product \(g_A h_\pi / (2\sqrt{2} F_\pi)\); the NLO TPE contribution includes a term proportional to \(g_A^3 h_\pi / (2\sqrt{2} F_\pi) \cdot 4/(4\pi F_\pi)^2\).
* The LEC \(c_4 \simeq 3.4~\text{GeV}^{-1}\) appears at N²LO and is enhanced by underlying \(\Delta\) and \(\rho\)-meson resonances.

## Context

This review extends the traditional one-boson-exchange (OBE) models for discrete symmetry violations in nuclear systems, which lack a direct link to QCD, by providing a systematic χEFT framework that connects microscopic quark-gluon operators to hadronic observables. It builds on prior work by Kaplan et al. (1992) on chiral symmetry properties of PVTC operators, by Zhu et al. (2004) on NLO PVTC potentials, and by de Vries et al. (2012, 2014) on PVTV Lagrangians and EDMs in light nuclei. The approach enables consistent calculations of PVTC observables in few-body systems (e.g., pp scattering, the anapole moment of \(^{133}\text{Cs}\)) and PVTV observables (e.g., EDMs of light nuclei) that can be improved order by order, and it provides a framework to interpret current EDM limits and future nonzero measurements in terms of BSM physics at scales comparable to the LHC.
