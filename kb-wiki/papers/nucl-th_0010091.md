# Elastic p-3He and n-3H scattering with two- and three-body forces

> We report on a microscopic calculation of n-3H and p-3He scattering employing the Argonne v_{18} and v_8' nucleon-nucleon potentials with and without additional three-nucleon force. An R-matrix analysis of the p-3He and n-3H scattering data is presented. Comparisons are made for the phase shifts and a selection of measurements in both scattering systems. Differences between our calculation and the R-matrix results or the experimental data can be attributed to only two partial waves (3P0 and 3P2). We find the effect of the Urbana IX and the Texas-Los Alamos three-nucleon forces on the phase shifts to be negligible.

## Key claim

Microscopic RGM calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18 and v8') reproduce most p-3He and n-3H scattering phase shifts well, but fail to reproduce the 3P2 and 3P0 phase shifts; the calculated splitting between these two channels is much too small, and neither the Urbana IX nor the Texas-Los Alamos three-nucleon force improves this splitting significantly.

## Method

The authors performed a Resonating Group Model (RGM) calculation of elastic p-3He and n-3H scattering using Gaussian-parametrized versions of the Argonne v18 and v8' nucleon-nucleon potentials, with and without the Urbana IX and Texas-Los Alamos three-nucleon forces. They compared these microscopic calculations to an R-matrix analysis of 1447 experimental data points covering proton energies from 1.01 to 19.7 MeV and neutron energies from 0.06 to 20.06 MeV, using the Kohn-Hulthén variational principle with a model space including positive and negative parity states of the three-nucleon subsystem as distortion channels.

## Key numbers

- R-matrix analysis achieved chi-squared per degree-of-freedom of 1.27 for p-3He data alone, and 1.59 when fitting both p-3He and n-t total cross sections simultaneously
- The energy shift of R-matrix eigenenergies between 4Li and 4H systems was Delta E_lambda = -0.86 MeV
- 3He binding energy in the 29-dimensional model space using Av18 alone: -6.37 MeV; with Urbana IX TNI: -6.88 MeV; in a larger model space: -7.72 MeV
- 3He rms radius: 1.78 fm; D-state probability: 7.7% (Av18 alone) and 8.2% (Av18 + Urbana IX)
- n-t total cross section uncertainties on the order of 0.2%
- The J3- distortion channels reduced by half the difference for 3P2 phase shift compared to R-matrix values
- Urbana IX and V3* together achieved an analyzing power Ay of 0.35 at E_cm = 4.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous R-matrix analyses of the A=4 system (Hale, unpublished) and RGM calculations of 4He (Pfitzinger et al., earlier work) to the simpler p-3He and n-3H systems. The finding that realistic NN forces fail to reproduce the 3P2-3P0 splitting, and that standard three-nucleon forces (Urbana IX, TLA) cannot fix it, motivates the need for new LS-type three-nucleon force operators as proposed by Kievsky (1997) for N-d analyzing powers, or the V3* operators of Schadow (1999). The results enable future work on three-nucleon force structure by providing precise phase shift benchmarks from the R-matrix analysis.
