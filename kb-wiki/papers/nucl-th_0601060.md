# IBM-2 configuration mixing and its geometric interpretation for germanium isotopes

> The low-energy spectra, electric quadrupole transitions and moments for the germanium isotopes are studied in the formalism of the IBM-2 with configuration mixing. The calculated observables reproduce well the available experimental information, including the newly obtained data for radioactive neutron-rich 78, 80, 82 Ge isotopes. A geometric interpretation of the model was established using a matrix formulation. The two energy surfaces determined after mixing carry information about the deformation parameters of the nucleus. Tor the even-even Ge isotopes the results are consistent with the shape transition that takes place around neutron number N=40.

## Key claim

The IBM-2 with configuration mixing, assuming the intruder configuration arises from a two-proton two-hole excitation across the Z=28 shell gap, reproduces the low-energy structure of even-even germanium isotopes from A=68 to A=82, including newly measured radioactive neutron-rich isotopes, with the caveat that the extrapolation to heavier isotopes (A=78-82) suggests configuration mixing is less important, but a definitive conclusion requires more experimental information.

## Method

The authors applied the standard two-particle two-hole IBM-2 with configuration mixing to the germanium isotopes, using 11 independent parameters per nucleus. They diagonalized separate Hamiltonians for the normal configuration (two proton-bosons) and intruder configuration (four proton-bosons) independently, then combined them using a mixing Hamiltonian that does not conserve boson number. A geometric interpretation was obtained by constructing energy surfaces from coherent states and diagonalizing a 2x2 matrix energy surface to yield two energy surfaces for each isotope.

## Key numbers

- For $^{68}$Ge: calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 27.2 \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$ (experimental: $29(3) \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$)
- For $^{72}$Ge: calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 39.0 \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$ (experimental: $40(3) \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$)
- For $^{74}$Ge: calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 62.2 \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$ (experimental: $60(3) \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$)
- For $^{82}$Ge: calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 27.6 \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$ (experimental: $25(5) \times 10^{-3} e^2 b^2$)
- Quadrupole moment for $^{74}$Ge $2_1^+$ state: calculated $-15 \times 10^{-2} e b$ (experimental: $-19(2) \times 10^{-2} e b$)
- The mixing parameter $\Delta$ varies linearly from 3.73 MeV for $^{68}$Ge to -3.00 MeV for $^{82}$Ge
- The effective charge $e_2$ ranges from 0.052 $e b$ for $^{68}$Ge to 0.032 $e b$ for $^{76-80}$Ge, with $e_4 = 2 e_2$ for all isotopes

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-2 configuration mixing calculations by Duval and Goutte (1981) for stable germanium nuclei to the newly explored radioactive neutron-rich isotopes $^{78,80,82}$Ge, using data from Coulomb excitation experiments at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility. The results are consistent with the shape transition around neutron number N=40 previously suggested by studies of $0_2^+$ excitation energies (Vergnes 1978) and electric quadrupole moments (Lecomte 1980, Toh 2000), and the geometric interpretation using coherent states provides a connection to the IBM-1 projection formalism of Scholten (1985). The work enables future experimental tests of the predicted configuration mixing in neutron-rich germanium isotopes.
