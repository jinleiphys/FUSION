# Hadrons in Medium - Observables

> The observable consequences of a lowering of the restmasses of vector mesons on dilepton spectra in the SIS energy regime (HADES) and on photoabsorption cross sections on nuclei are studied in a transporttheoretical framework. We also investigate the consequences of a mass-shift of kaons in dense matter and compare with recent SIS data. The need for a detailed understanding of the elementary processes is underlined.

## Key claim

The observable consequences of predicted in-medium mass shifts of vector mesons and kaons on dilepton spectra and photoabsorption cross sections are studied in a transport-theoretical framework, but the results depend critically on the magnitude of elementary production cross sections in energy regions where no data exist, and the disappearance of nucleon resonances in photoabsorption requires further investigation of the \(2\pi\) channel.

## Method

The author uses a semiclassical transport code that propagates all baryonic resonances up to 2 GeV mass, along with \(\pi\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), and \(\sigma\) mesons, including momentum-dependent baryon self-energies but no meson self-energies. For dileptons, the code calculates invariant mass spectra for Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, comparing free and medium-dependent \(\rho\)-meson masses. For photoabsorption, collisional broadening and Doppler broadening from Fermi motion are computed, and for kaons, the transport model includes all relevant kaon channels using microscopic cross sections from an effective boson exchange model constrained by SU(2) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The surplus in CERES dilepton data compared to cocktail plots is reduced from a factor of 5–7 to about a factor of 3 when secondary \(\Delta\) decays are included.
- The in-medium shift of the \(\rho\) peak leads to a change in the dilepton spectrum by a factor of 3 at 1 AGeV for Au+Au collisions.
- The N(1520) resonance lies about 130 MeV below the energetic threshold for \(N\rho\) decay based on pole masses.
- The predicted lowering of the \(\rho\) meson mass in nuclear medium is about 18% according to QCD sum rule predictions.
- For kaons, the \(K^+\) mass increases slightly with density, while the \(K^-\) mass is predicted to be lowered significantly; the relevant energy range for elementary kaon production cross sections is 100–400 MeV above threshold.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport-theoretical studies by the same group (e.g., Batko, Ko, Wolf) that explained the DLS dilepton surplus via secondary \(\pi\Delta\) decays, and contrasts with the CERES data where a factor of 3 surplus remains even after accounting for such effects. The photoabsorption analysis builds on the work of Alberico (1994) and Bianchi (1996) who used Breit-Wigner parametrizations, while the kaon self-energy analysis follows the suggestion of Nelson and Kaplan (1987) and the calculations of Weise (1995). The results enable future measurements by the HADES spectrometer at SIS and highlight the need for precise elementary cross-section measurements in the 100–400 MeV above-threshold region.
