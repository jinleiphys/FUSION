# Determination of nuclear parton distributions

> Parametrization of nuclear parton distributions is investigated in the leading order of alpha_s. The parton distributions are provided at Q^2=1 GeV^2 with a number of parameters, which are determined by a chi^2 analysis of the data on nuclear structure functions. Quadratic or cubic functional form is assumed for the initial distributions. Although valence quark distributions in the medium x region are relatively well determined, the small x distributions depend slightly on the assumed functional form. It is difficult to determine the antiquark distributions at medium x and gluon distributions. From the analysis, we propose parton distributions at Q^2=1 GeV^2 for nuclei from deuteron to heavy ones with the mass number A~208. They are provided either analytical expressions or computer subroutines for practical usage. Our studies should be important for understanding the physics mechanism of the nuclear modification and also for applications to heavy-ion reactions. This kind of nuclear parametrization should also affect existing parametrization studies in the nucleon because "nuclear" data are partially used for obtaining the optimum distributions in the "nucleon".

## Key claim

This paper presents the first \(\chi^2\) analysis of nuclear parton distributions, providing parametrizations at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) for nuclei from deuteron to heavy nuclei with mass number \(A \sim 208\). The authors hedge that while valence quark distributions in the medium \(x\) region are relatively well determined, the small \(x\) distributions depend slightly on the assumed functional form, and it is difficult to determine the antiquark distributions at medium \(x\) and gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors performed a leading-order \(\chi^2\) analysis of nuclear structure function \(F_2^A\) data from electron and muon deep inelastic scattering experiments (EMC, SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, E665) for 14 nuclear species, using 309 data points with \(Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\). They assumed nuclear parton distributions at \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) as the product of nucleon parton distributions (MRST-LO) and a weight function \(w_i(x,A,Z)\) with a \(1 - 1/A^{1/3}\) dependence, using either quadratic (\(a_i + b_i x + c_i x^2\)) or cubic (\(a_i + b_i x + c_i x^2 + d_i x^3\)) functional forms in \(x\), with parameters determined by minimizing \(\chi^2\) using the CERN subroutine Minuit.

## Key numbers

*   The analysis used 309 experimental data points from 14 nuclear species (He, Li, Be, C, N, Al, Ca, Fe, Cu, Ag, Sn, Xe, Au, Pb) with \(Q^2 \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The initial parton distributions are provided at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The nuclear modification is assumed to be proportional to \(1 - 1/A^{1/3}\), with the \(A\) dependence justified by measured \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) data at \(x = 0.5\).
*   The quadratic fit has 7 free parameters: \(b_v, c_v, \beta_v, a_{\bar q}, b_{\bar q}, c_{\bar q}, c_g\).
*   The cubic fit has 9 free parameters: \(b_v, c_v, d_v, \beta_v, a_{\bar q}, b_{\bar q}, c_{\bar q}, d_{\bar q}, c_g\).

## Context

This work extends prior model-dependent studies of nuclear parton distributions (Saga group) and model-independent studies by Eskola, Kolhinen, and Ruuskanen (Helsinki group) by performing the first \(\chi^2\) analysis rather than relying on model assumptions. The parametrization enables accurate calculations of cross sections for high-energy nuclear reactions, particularly heavy-ion collisions where precise initial conditions are needed to search for quark-gluon plasma signatures, and also improves nucleon parton distribution determinations that currently use nuclear data (e.g., neutrino \(F_3\) data from iron targets) without accounting for nuclear modifications.
