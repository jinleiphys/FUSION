# Measuring anomalous ``spin'' in elastic e-p or ν-p and deep inelastic e-p scattering

> We obtain a general rule that the O(1/log m_h) term due to the current \bar{h}γ_μγ_5h of a mass-m_h quark h in f-flavour theory is -3\bar{g}^2_f(m_h)/{2π^2(33-2f)} times the flavour singlet current of the residual (f-1)-flavour theory, where \bar{g}_f is the f-flavour running coupling constant in a mass-independent renormalization scheme. The rule is applied to the Ellis-Jaffe moment below and well above charm threshold, and to low-energy Z^0-exchange amplitudes. The singlet axial charge of the proton common to these experiments is both scale and gauge invariant, but is related to the axial anomaly and the ``gluon spin'' by a non-perturbative renormalization factor.

# Measuring anomalous "spin" in elastic e-p or ν-p and deep inelastic e-p scattering

## Key claim

The singlet axial charge of the proton extracted from deep inelastic scattering and from elastic Z⁰-exchange processes is the same scale- and gauge-invariant quantity, despite the presence of a non-perturbative renormalization factor E(g) arising from the axial anomaly; this equivalence holds for the leading terms in the heavy-quark expansion and includes a calculable O(1/log m_h) correction.

## Method

The authors work in a mass-independent renormalization scheme and perform a sequential heavy-quark expansion (m_t → ∞, m_b → ∞, m_c → ∞) of the six-flavour neutral current amplitude, matching onto three-flavour amplitudes via operator product expansions. They derive a general rule for the O(1/log m_h) term from the mixing of the heavy-quark axial current with the flavour-singlet current of the residual theory, using the matrix Callan-Symanzik function for the two-component operator vector. The rule is applied to the Ellis-Jaffe moment below and above charm threshold and to low-energy Z⁰-exchange amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The three-flavour singlet axial charge (scale- and gauge-invariant) has experimental value 0.28 ± 0.07.
- The coefficient C(m_h) in the neutral current expansion is C = 1/6 + (3/4π²)[(1/21)ḡ²₆(m_t) - (1/23)ḡ²₅(m_b) + (1/25)ḡ²₄(m_c)] + O((log m_h)⁻²).
- With μ = 0.5 GeV, the numerical estimate gives C ≈ (1/6)(1.11), corresponding to a correction of 0.01 to Δs_inv compared with the experimental value -0.11 ± 0.03.
- The difference between the Ellis-Jaffe moment above and below charm threshold is predicted to be approximately (1/9) g⁰_A_inv × (-0.10).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Collins, Wilczek, and Zee (1978) and Kaplan and Manohar (1988) on heavy-quark decoupling in axial currents, but uses a mass-independent renormalization scheme to obtain explicit asymptotic expansions in 1/m_h. The authors resolve ambiguities in the flavour dependence of the O(1/log m_h) corrections compared to Chetyrkin and Kühn (1994). The results enable a precise comparison between polarised deep inelastic scattering experiments (below charm threshold) and parity-violating elastic ep and νp scattering experiments that measure strange-quark contributions to the proton's axial charge.
