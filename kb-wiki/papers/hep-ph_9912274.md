# Slowing Out of Equilibrium Near the QCD Critical Point

> The QCD phase diagram may feature a critical end point at a temperature T and baryon chemical potential $μ$ which is accessible in heavy ion collisions. The universal long wavelength fluctuations which develop near this Ising critical point result in experimental signatures which can be used to find the critical point. The magnitude of the observed effects depends on how large the correlation length $ξ$ becomes. Because the matter created in a heavy ion collision cools through the critical region of the phase diagram in a finite time, critical slowing down limits the growth of $ξ$, preventing it from staying in equilibrium. This is the fundamental nonequilibrium effect which must be calculated in order to make quantitative predictions for experiment. We use universal nonequilibrium dynamics and phenomenologically motivated values for the necessary nonuniversal quantities to estimate how much the growth of $ξ$ is slowed.

## Key claim

Critical slowing down prevents the correlation length ξ from growing larger than about 2/T_E ~ 3 fm in heavy ion collisions that pass near the QCD critical point, even though an infinite system held at the critical point would have infinite ξ; this bound is more stringent than the finite-size limit of about 6 fm estimated in prior work.

## Method

The authors model the universal nonequilibrium dynamics of the order parameter near the critical point using the three-dimensional Ising model in dynamical universality class Model C, with dynamical exponent z = 2.17. They solve a differential equation for the inverse correlation length m_σ = 1/ξ as the system cools along the h-axis of the Ising model, using a cooling rate dT/dt ≈ -4 MeV/fm estimated from hydrodynamic and cascade models of Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, and assuming the system begins in equilibrium at T_0 = 180 MeV with ξ_0 = 1.4 fm.

## Key numbers

- The maximum correlation length achieved is 2.1 ξ_0 ≈ 3.0 fm for the central estimate a = 50, with a range of 2.6–3.4 fm for a = 25–100
- The equilibrium correlation length at T_0 = 180 MeV is defined as ξ_0 = 1.4 fm, corresponding to 1/T_E with T_E = 140 MeV
- The cooling rate estimate is dT/dt ≈ -4 MeV/fm at T = 140 MeV, with a plausible range of -2 to -6 MeV/fm
- The dynamical exponent is z = 2 + α/ν ≈ 2.17, using α = 0.11 and ν = 0.630 from the three-dimensional Ising model
- The equilibrium correlation length along the h-axis scales as ξ_eq ~ |h|^{-ν/βδ} with ν/βδ ≈ 0.215, using β = 0.326 and δ = 4.80

## Context

This work extends the predictions of Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998, 1999) for experimental signatures of the QCD critical point by quantifying the nonequilibrium limitation on correlation length growth, which was previously only estimated from finite-size effects. The authors use the dynamical universality class classification of Halperin and Hohenberg (1977) and the Ising model exponents from Guida and Zinn-Justin (1998). The result enables more quantitative predictions for observables such as event-by-event fluctuations of mean transverse momentum and soft pion multiplicity, which depend on ξ_freezeout^2.
