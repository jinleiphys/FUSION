# Gravitational Wave Signatures of Highly Magnetized Neutron Stars

> Motivated by the recent gravitational wave detection by the LIGO-VIRGO observatories, we study the Love number and dimensionless tidal polarizability of highly magnetized stars. We also investigate the fundamental quasi-normal mode of neutron stars subject to high magnetic fields. To perform our calculations we use the chaotic field approximation and consider both nucleonic and hyperonic stars. As far as the fundamental mode is concerned, we conclude that the role played by the constitution of the stars is far more relevant than the intensity of the magnetic field and if massive stars are considered, the ones constituted by nucleons only present frequencies somewhat lower than the ones with hyperonic cores, a feature that can be used to point out the real internal structure of neutron stars. Moreover, our studies clearly indicate that strong magnetic fields play a crucial role in the deformability of low mass neutron stars, with possible consequences on the interpretation of the detected gravitational waves signatures.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the internal composition (nucleonic vs. hyperonic) of neutron stars has a far more significant effect on the fundamental quasi-normal mode frequency than the magnetic field intensity, and that strong magnetic fields play a crucial role in the deformability of low-mass neutron stars, with possible consequences for interpreting gravitational wave signatures.

## Method

The authors used the chaotic field approximation (monopole approximation) to incorporate magnetic fields into the equation of state, employing a density-dependent magnetic field profile with a maximum central value B0. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for equilibrium configurations and then computed the tidal Love number k2 and dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda by solving the associated differential equation for the metric perturbation, considering both nucleonic-only and hyperonic compositions using a modified GM1 relativistic mean-field parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for nucleonic stars: 2.37 solar masses (radius 11.91 km); for hyperonic stars: 1.95 solar masses (radius 12.46 km)
- Canonical 1.4 solar mass star radius: 13.63 km for both non-magnetized nucleonic and hyperonic cases
- For the canonical 1.4 solar mass star, dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda decreases from 684 (non-magnetized) to 559 (NB318, B0 = 3.0 x 10^18 G), a reduction of about 18%
- For low-mass stars (1.04 solar masses), Lambda decreases from 3361 (non-magnetized) to 2552 (NB318), a reduction of about 24%
- Central magnetic field for maximum mass hyperonic star with B0 = 3.0 x 10^18 G: 2.7 x 10^18 G
- Central magnetic field for canonical 1.4 solar mass star with B0 = 3.0 x 10^18 G: 1.0 x 10^17 G (nucleonic) and 7.4 x 10^16 G (hyperonic)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tidal deformability and neutron star oscillations by incorporating strong magnetic fields via the chaotic field approximation, building on the approach of Zel'dovich and later applications by Lopes et al. (2015, 2016). The results are compared with constraints from the GW170817 event observed by LIGO-Virgo, and with the 35 relativistic mean-field models previously tested against nuclear bulk properties by Dutra et al. (2014). The study enables future gravitational wave asteroseismology with third-generation detectors like the Einstein Telescope, where the fundamental mode frequency could help distinguish between nucleonic and hyperonic compositions in massive neutron stars.
