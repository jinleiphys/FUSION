# The nuclear scissors mode within two approaches (Wigner function moments versus RPA)

> Two complementary methods to describe the collective motion, RPA and Wigner function moments method, are compared on an example of a simple model - harmonic oscillator with quadrupole-quadrupole residual interaction. It is shown that they give identical formulae for eigenfrequencies and transition probabilities of all collective excitations of the model including the scissors mode, which here is the subject of our special attention. The exact relation between the variables of the two methods and the respective dynamical equations is established. The normalization factor of the "synthetic" scissors state and its overlap with physical states are calculated analytically. The orthogonality of the spurious state to all physical states is proved rigorously.

## Key claim

The Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) produce identical analytical formulae for eigenfrequencies and transition probabilities of all collective excitations in the harmonic oscillator plus quadrupole-quadrupole (HO+QQ) model, including the scissors mode, but only under the approximation that equilibrium deformation and mean square radius of neutrons equal those of protons (approximation Apr4).

## Method

The authors compare two complementary approaches to collective nuclear motion: the Wigner Function Moments method, which derives dynamical equations for multipole phase space moments of the nucleus from the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equation, and standard RPA with a schematic separable residual interaction. Both methods are applied to the exactly solvable HO+QQ model for axially symmetric, non-rotating nuclei in the small-amplitude approximation, with the simplifying assumption that neutron and proton equilibrium deformations and mean square radii are equal. The authors derive analytical expressions for eigenfrequencies and B(E2) and B(M1) transition probabilities for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR), isovector GQR, and scissors mode, then establish the exact relation between the variables of the two methods.

## Key numbers

- Scissors mode frequency: \(\Omega_{sc}^2 = 4\bar\omega^2 \left(1+\frac{\delta}{3}-\sqrt{(1+\frac{\delta}{3})^2-\frac{3}{4}\delta^2}\right)\), where \(\bar\omega\) is the self-consistent oscillator frequency and \(\delta\) is the deformation parameter
- Isovector GQR frequency: \(\Omega_{iv}^2 = 4\bar\omega^2 \left(1+\frac{\delta}{3}+\sqrt{(1+\frac{\delta}{3})^2-\frac{3}{4}\delta^2}\right)\) for \(\alpha=-2\) (isovector/isoscalar strength ratio)
- Isoscalar GQR frequency: \(\Omega_{is}^2 = 2\bar\omega^2(1+\delta/3)\)
- B(E2) transition probability for all three modes: \(B(E2)_{\nu} = \frac{5}{16\pi}\frac{e^2\hbar}{m\bar\omega^2}Q_{00}\frac{(1+\delta/3)\Omega_{\nu}^2-2(\bar\omega\delta)^2}{\Omega_{\nu}[\Omega_{\nu}^2-\bar{\omega}^2(2-\alpha)(1+\delta/3)]}\)
- B(M1) transition probability for scissors and IVGQR: \(B(M1)_{\nu} = \frac{1-\alpha}{8\pi}\frac{m\bar\omega^2}{\hbar}Q_{00}\delta^2\frac{\Omega_{\nu}^2-2(1+\delta/3)\bar\omega^2}{\Omega_{\nu}[\Omega_{\nu}^2-\bar{\omega}^2(2-\alpha)(1+\delta/3)]}\,\mu_N^2\)
- The sums of squared matrix elements: \(D_0 = \frac{Q_{00}}{m\bar\omega^2}\epsilon_0\) and \(D_2 = \frac{Q_{00}}{m\bar\omega^2}\epsilon_2\), where \(\epsilon_0 = \hbar(\omega_x-\omega_z)\) and \(\epsilon_2 = \hbar(\omega_x+\omega_z)\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the scissors mode in the HO+QQ model previously performed by Balbutsev and Schuck (BaSc2) using the WFM method, and establishes its equivalence with the RPA treatment of Hamamoto and Nazarewicz (Hamam). The authors prove that the two methods give identical formulae for eigenfrequencies and transition probabilities, and they derive the exact relation between WFM collective variables and RPA amplitudes. The paper provides analytical expressions for the normalization factor of the "synthetic" scissors state and proves rigorously the orthogonality of the spurious state to all physical states. This enables future work to use either method interchangeably for studying collective modes in deformed nuclei, with the WFM method offering better physical interpretation through its collective variables while RPA provides the standard microscopic framework.
