# Magnetic moment of the deuteron as probe of relativistic corrections

> The calculation of the magnetic moment of the deuteron in the framework of the Bethe--Salpeter approach is performed. The relativistic corrections are calculated analytically and estimated numerically. It is shown that the main contributions are due to partial waves with positive energies and $P$--waves. A comparison with the non-relativistic schemes of calculations including mesonic exchange currents is made.

## Key claim

The magnetic moment of the deuteron calculated in the Bethe-Salpeter approach can be expressed in a form close to non-relativistic calculations, with additional terms interpreted as relativistic corrections; the dominant relativistic contributions come from P-wave states rather than from negative-energy S- and D-waves, and the magnitude of these corrections is comparable to the contribution from mesonic exchange currents in non-relativistic schemes.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron magnetic moment from the matrix element of the electromagnetic current in the Bethe-Salpeter framework, expanding the integrand in powers of \(\sqrt{\eta}\) (where \(\eta = -q^2/4M^2\)) in the Breit system. The vertex function is decomposed into eight partial-wave states (classified by orbital momentum \(L\), spin \(S\), and \(\rho\)-spin \(\rho\)), and the magnetic moment is separated into contributions from positive-energy states (\(\mu_+\)), negative-energy states (\(\mu_{2-}\)), and P-wave states (\(\mu_{1-}\)), each with associated relativistic correction terms \(R_a\). Numerical estimates are performed using a separable model for the vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- The D-state pseudo-probability \(P_{^3D_1^{++}}\) is 4.8% (from Tjon) or 5.1% (from Kazakov).
- The negative-energy D-state pseudo-probability \(P_{^3D_1^{--}}\) is \(-6 \times 10^{-4}\%\) (Tjon) or \(-3.4 \times 10^{-4}\%\) (Kazakov).
- The combined P-state pseudo-probabilities \(P_{^3P_1^{e}}+P_{^3P_1^{o}}\) are \(-0.88 \times 10^{-2}\%\) (Tjon) or \(-9 \times 10^{-2}\%\) (Kazakov).
- The combined P-state pseudo-probabilities \(P_{^1P_1^{e}}+P_{^1P_1^{o}}\) are \(-2.5 \times 10^{-2}\%\) (Tjon) or \(-2.4 \times 10^{-2}\%\) (Kazakov).
- The relativistic correction from positive-energy states relative to the non-relativistic value is \(R_+ / \mu_{NR} = -(5.8 - 11) \times 10^{-2}\; \%\).
- The relativistic correction from P-states relative to the non-relativistic value is \(\Delta \mu_{1-} / \mu_{NR} = 0.2\; \%\).
- The experimental deuteron magnetic moment is \(\mu_{exp} = 0.857406 (1)\) (in nuclear magnetons).
- The mesonic exchange current correction from the pair term, extracted from Burov (1992) for the Bonn potential, gives \(\Delta \mu / \mu_{NR} = 0.21 - 0.22\%\).

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter treatment of the deuteron by Tjon (1980) and Kazakov (private communication) to the magnetic moment, providing an analytic separation of relativistic corrections from the non-relativistic formula. The results show that the relativistic corrections from P-waves are of the same order as the mesonic exchange current contributions calculated in non-relativistic frameworks by Burov (1992), suggesting a common physical origin. This enables a more direct comparison between relativistic Bethe-Salpeter calculations and non-relativistic approaches with mesonic exchange currents for electromagnetic properties of the deuteron.
