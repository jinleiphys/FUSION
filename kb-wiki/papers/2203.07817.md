# Search for the QCD Critical Point in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> QCD critical point is a landmark region in the QCD phase diagram outlined by temperature as a function of baryon chemical potential. To the right of this second-order phase transition point, one expects first order quark-hadron phase transition boundary, towards the left a crossover region, top of it lies the quark gluon plasma phase and below it the hadronic phase. Hence locating the QCD critical point through relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments is an active area of research. Cumulants of conserved quantities in strong interaction, such as net-baryon, net-charge, and net-strangeness, are suggested to be sensitive to the physics of QCD critical point and are therefore useful observables in the study of the phase transition between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter. We review the experimental status of the search for the QCD critical point via the measurements of cumulants of net-particle distributions in heavy ion collisions. We discuss various experimental challenges and associated corrections in such fluctuation measurements. We also comment on the physics implications of the measurements by comparing them with theoretical calculations. This is followed by a discussion on future experiments and measurements related to high baryonic density QCD matter.

## Key claim

The QCD critical point, if it exists, should be located at baryon chemical potential $\mu_{B} \geq 300$ MeV according to recent lattice-QCD estimates, but its exact location remains highly uncertain and theoretical predictions scatter over $\mu_{B} = 200$–1100 MeV and $T = 40$–180 MeV.

## Method

The authors review experimental searches for the QCD critical point via measurements of cumulants (up to fourth order) of net-particle multiplicity distributions (net-proton, net-kaon, net-charge) from the STAR Collaboration's Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. They discuss how cumulants of conserved charges (net-baryon, net-charge, net-strangeness) relate to thermodynamic susceptibilities and correlation length, and compare experimental results to baselines from the Skellam distribution, hadron resonance gas model, and UrQMD model, as well as to theoretical predictions from lattice-QCD and QCD-based models.

## Key numbers

* Crossover transition temperature at $\mu_{B} = 0$: 156.5 $\pm$ 1.5 MeV (lattice-QCD, HotQCD Collaboration)
* Chiral phase transition temperature at $\mu_{B} = 0$: $136^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV for two light quark flavors; $98^{+3}_{-6}$ MeV for three-flavor QCD
* Lattice-QCD estimate: critical point, if it exists, should be in region $\mu_{B} \geq 300$ MeV
* Finite size and time effects in heavy-ion collisions limit correlation length $\xi \approx$ 2–3 fm
* Theoretical predictions for critical point location scatter over $\mu_{B} = 200$–1100 MeV and $T = 40$–180 MeV

## Context

This review extends the experimental search program initiated by the STAR Collaboration's Beam Energy Scan phase I, which measured cumulants of net-charge, net-kaon, and net-proton distributions. The work builds on theoretical foundations from Stephanov (1998, 1999, 2008) on critical point signatures, lattice-QCD results from Aoki (2006) establishing crossover at $\mu_{B}=0$, and model calculations from Fodor (2001, 2004), Fischer (2014), and others. The review enables future experimental programs at FAIR, NICA, CSR, and J-PARC to target the high baryon density region of the QCD phase diagram.
