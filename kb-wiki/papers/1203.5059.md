# Anisotropic Optical Response of Dense Quark Matter under Rotation: Compact Stars as Cosmic Polarizers

> Quantum vortices in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD have bosonic degrees of freedom, called the orientational zero modes, localized on them. We show that the orientational zero modes are electromagnetically charged. As a result, a vortex in the CFL phase nontrivially interacts with photons. We show that a lattice of vortices acts as a polarizer of photons with wavelengths larger than some critical length.

## Key claim

A lattice of non-Abelian vortices in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD acts as a polarizer for photons with wavelengths larger than a critical value, but only if the CFL core extends to the stellar surface; if covered by a nuclear mantle, optical probes cannot penetrate.

## Method

The authors model the low-energy effective theory of a single non-Abelian vortex in the CFL phase as a gauged \(\mathbb{C}P^2\) nonlinear sigma model, where the orientational zero modes become electromagnetically charged under the \(U(1)_{\rm EM}\) subgroup of the flavor symmetry. They derive the equation of motion for a photon scattering off a vortex, analogous to Witten's superconducting string formalism, and compute the scattering cross section per unit length. They then average this cross section over an ensemble of vortices in a rotating star, estimate the intervortex spacing from the total vortex number, and derive an exponential attenuation law for photons with electric fields parallel to the vortices.

## Key numbers

- Total vortex number in a rotating CFL core: \(N_v \simeq 1.9 \times 10^{19}\) for rotation period \(P_{\rm rot} = 1\ {\rm ms}\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu/3 = 300\ {\rm MeV}\), and core radius \(R = 10\ {\rm km}\).
- Intervortex spacing: \(\ell \simeq 4.0 \times 10^{-6}\ {\rm m}\) for \(P_{\rm rot} = 1\ {\rm ms}\) and \(\mu/3 = 300\ {\rm MeV}\).
- Characteristic decay length for photons with electric field parallel to vortices: \(L \simeq 6.5 \times 10^{-12}\ {\rm m}^2 / \lambda\), assuming random \(\mathbb{C}P^2\) orientation, \(\mu = 900\ {\rm MeV}\), \(\Delta = 100\ {\rm MeV}\), and \(\eta = 1\).
- Critical wavelength for significant attenuation within a 1 km CFL core: \(\lambda_{\rm c} = 6.5 \times 10^{-15}\ {\rm m}\).
- With finite strange quark mass (\(m_s \sim 10^2\ {\rm MeV}\)) and all vortices in the \(\phi_0^T = (0,1,0)\) orientation: \(L \sim 1.2 \times 10^{-11}\ {\rm m}^2 / \lambda\) and \(\lambda_{\rm c} = 1.2 \times 10^{-14}\ {\rm m}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of non-Abelian vortices in the CFL phase, previously analyzed using Ginzburg-Landau theory by Nakano (2007), Eto (2009), and others, and the Bogoliubov–de Gennes equation by Yasui (2010). It builds on the observation that these vortices carry orientational zero modes (Nakano 2007, Eto 2009, Hirono 2010) and shows for the first time that these modes are electromagnetically charged, enabling photon-vortex interactions. The polarizer effect provides a potential observational signature for CFL matter in compact stars, though the authors note that a nuclear mantle would block optical probes; future work could explore interactions with strong magnetic fields in neutron stars.
