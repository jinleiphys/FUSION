# Quantization of Differences Between Atomic and Nuclear Rest Masses and Selforganization of Atoms and Nuclei

> We come to the conclusion that all atomic models based on either the Newton equation and the Kepler laws, or the Maxwell equations, or the Schrodinger and Dirac equations are in reasonable agreement with experimental data. We can only suspect that these equations are grounded on the same fundamental principle(s) which is (are) not known or these equations can be transformed into each other. We proposed a new mechanism of LENR: cooperative processes in the whole system - nuclei+atoms+condensed matter - nuclear reactions in plasma - can occur at smaller threshold energies than the corresponding ones on free constituents. We were able to quantize phenomenologically the first time the differences between atomic and nuclear rest masses by the formula (in MeV/$c^{2}$) $ΔM=\frac{n_{1}}{n_{2}}*0.0076294, n_{i}=1,2,3,...$ Note that this quantization rule is justified for atoms and nuclei with different $A, N$ and $Z$ and the nuclei and atoms represent a coherent synchronized systems - a complex of coupled oscillators (resonators). The cooperative resonance synchronization mechanisms are responsible for explanation of how the electron volt world can influence the nuclear mega electron volt world. It means that we created new possibilities for inducing and controlling nuclear reactions by atomic processes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that differences between atomic and nuclear rest masses can be quantized phenomenologically for the first time using the formula \(\Delta M = \frac{n_1}{n_2} \times 0.0076294\) MeV/\(c^2\), where \(n_i = 1,2,3,\dots\); this quantization rule is presented as justified for atoms and nuclei with different mass number \(A\), neutron number \(N\), and proton number \(Z\), but the paper provides no explicit numerical validation or statistical comparison to measured mass differences for any specific nuclide.

## Method

The authors review multiple historical atomic models (Bohr, Sukhorukov, Parson, Lucas, Bergman-Lucas, Gareev, Gryzinski, Gudim-Andreeeva, Huang, Mills) to argue that classical and quantum approaches yield equivalent results and that atoms and nuclei form coherent synchronized systems of coupled oscillators. They then propose a phenomenological quantization formula for the difference between atomic and nuclear rest masses, based on a constant factor of 0.0076294 MeV/\(c^2\) multiplied by a rational fraction of integers, and discuss cooperative resonance synchronization as a mechanism for low-energy nuclear reactions (LENR) in condensed matter and plasma.

## Key numbers

- The proposed quantization formula for mass differences is \(\Delta M = \frac{n_1}{n_2} \times 0.0076294\) MeV/\(c^2\), with \(n_i = 1,2,3,\dots\).
- The rest mass difference between a neutron and (proton + electron) is given as 0.7823 MeV/\(c^2\).
- The neutron is about 0.2% more massive than a proton, corresponding to a mass difference of 1.29 MeV.
- The measured half-life for bare \(^{187}\text{Re}^{75+}\) is \(T_{1/2} = (32.9 \pm 2.0)\) years, stated to be a billion times shorter than that for neutral \(^{187}\text{Re}\).
- The kinetic energy ratio of electron to proton in a hydrogen atom is given as \(E_k(e)/E_k(p) = 4.46 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The ratio of maximum mountain height \(H\) to Bohr radius \(a\) is given as \(H/a = 2.6 \times 10^{14}\).
- The ratio of water wavelength \(\lambda\) on a lake surface to Bohr radius \(a\) is given as \(\lambda/a \approx 2\pi \times 10^{7}\).

## Context

This paper extends the authors' prior work on cooperative resonance synchronization mechanisms (Gareev and Zhidkova, earlier publications) and reviews alternative atomic models including those of Mills (2005), Huang (2006), and Gryzinski (2004) to argue that classical and quantum descriptions of atoms are equivalent. The work aims to provide a phenomenological basis for inducing and controlling nuclear reactions via atomic processes, particularly in the context of low-energy nuclear reactions (LENR) in condensed matter and plasma, and claims to enable new interpretations of self-organization in hierarchical systems from nuclei to living cells.
