# Constraining mean-field models of the nuclear matter equation of state at low densities

> An extension of the generalized relativistic mean-field (gRMF) model with density dependent couplings is introduced in order to describe thermodynamical properties and the composition of dense nuclear matter for astrophysical applications. Bound states of light nuclei and two-nucleon scattering correlations are considered as explicit degrees of freedom in the thermodynamical potential. They are represented by quasiparticles with medium-dependent properties. The model describes the correct low-density limit given by the virial equation of state (VEoS) and reproduces RMF results around nuclear saturation density where clusters are dissolved. A comparison between the fugacity expansions of the VEoS and the gRMF model provides consistency relations between the quasiparticles properties, the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and the meson-nucleon couplings of the gRMF model at zero density. Relativistic effects are found to be important at temperatures that are typical in astrophysical applications. Neutron matter and symmetric matter are studied in detail.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an extension of the generalized relativistic mean-field (gRMF) model that, by including bound states of light nuclei and two-nucleon scattering correlations as quasiparticles with medium-dependent properties, can describe the correct low-density limit given by the virial equation of state (VEoS) while still reproducing RMF results around nuclear saturation density; however, the model does not exactly reproduce the VEoS thermodynamical properties without introducing consistency relations derived from comparing fugacity expansions of both approaches.

## Method

The authors compare the fugacity expansion of the grand canonical potential in the VEoS (up to second order in nucleon fugacities) with that of the gRMF model, deriving consistency relations that connect quasiparticle properties, nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and meson-nucleon couplings at zero density. They then introduce temperature-dependent resonance energies and modified degeneracy factors for scattering states to satisfy these relations, enabling a smooth interpolation between the VEoS and gRMF descriptions. Neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter are studied in detail using this extended framework.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(B_d = 2.225\) MeV (used in the isospin-zero np channel virial coefficient)
- Alpha-particle binding energy: \(B_\alpha = 28.296\) MeV (used in chemical equilibrium relation)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: \(a_{nn} = -18.818\) fm; effective range: \(r_{nn} = 2.834\) fm (from Ref. Wir01, used in effective-range approximation)
- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_{\rm sat} \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) (reference density where clusters dissolve and RMF results are reproduced)
- In the unitary limit (\(a_{ij} \to -\infty\), \(r_{ij}=0\)), the effective resonance energy is \(E_{ij} = T \ln 2\) and the virial integral \(I_0^{(ij)}(T) = 1/2\)

## Context

This work extends the generalized relativistic mean-field (gRMF) model introduced by Typel (2010), which itself built on the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model from Typel (1999). The approach addresses a known deficiency of mean-field models: at low densities (\(n \ll n_{\rm sat}\)) they fail to include correlations that give rise to cluster formation, unlike the model-independent virial equation of state (VEoS) developed by Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) and O'Connor and others (2007). The results enable improved equation of state tables for astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star structure, complementing recent EoS tables from Hempel and others (2010), Shen and others (2011), and others.
