# Positive and negative parity hyperons in nuclear medium

> The effects of nuclear medium on the residue, mass and self energy of the positive and negative parity $Σ$, $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons are investigated using the QCD sum rule method. In the calculations, the general interpolating currents of hyperons with an arbitrary mixing parameter are used. We compare the results obtained in medium with those of the vacuum and calculate the shifts in the corresponding parameters. It is found that the shifts on the residues in nuclear matter are over all positive for both the positive and negative parity hyperons, except for the positive parity $Σ$ hyperon that the shift is negative. The shifts on the masses of these baryons are obtained to be negative. The shifts on the residues and masses of negative parity states are large compared to those of positive parities. The maximum shift belongs to the residue of the negative parity $Λ$ hyperon. The vector self-energies gained by the positive parity baryons are large compared to the negative parities' vector self-energies. The maximum value of the vector self-energy belongs to the positive parity $Σ$ hyperon. The numerical values are compared with the existing predictions in the literature.

## Key claim

The nuclear medium induces shifts in the residues, masses, and self-energies of both positive and negative parity hyperons (Sigma, Lambda, Xi), with the sign and magnitude of these shifts depending on the specific hyperon and parity state; the abstract's claim of "overall positive" residue shifts is qualified by the finding that the positive parity Sigma hyperon exhibits a negative shift.

## Method

The authors employed the QCD sum rule method, calculating the two-point correlation function of interpolating currents for Sigma, Lambda, and Xi hyperons in nuclear matter. General interpolating currents with an arbitrary mixing parameter beta were used, and the correlation function was decomposed into three Lorentz structures (p-slash, u-slash, and unit matrix). The hadronic side was expressed in terms of positive and negative parity hyperon states, while the OPE side was computed up to dimension-six condensates, including in-medium quark and gluon condensates at nuclear matter density rho_N = (0.11 GeV)^3. Eight coupled equations were solved simultaneously to extract the residues, masses, and vector self-energies for both parities.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear matter density used is rho_N = (0.11)^3 GeV^3.
* The vacuum masses used as inputs: m_Sigma+ = 1192.642 +/- 0.024 MeV; m_Lambda+ = 1115.683 +/- 0.006 MeV; m_Xi+ = 1314.86 +/- 0.2 MeV.
* The negative parity vacuum masses used: m_Sigma- approximately 1620 MeV; m_Lambda- = 1405.13 MeV (with errors -1.0/+1.3 MeV); m_Xi- = 1.8 GeV.
* The continuum threshold sqrt(s_0) - m_H was taken in the interval [0.3, 0.5] GeV.
* The quark condensate in vacuum: <q-bar q>_0 = (-0.241)^3 GeV^3; the strange quark condensate in vacuum: <s-bar s>_0 = 0.8 <q-bar q>_0.
* The sigma term sigma_N = 0.045 GeV; the strangeness content parameter y = 0.04 +/- 0.02.
* The parameter m_0^2 = 0.8 GeV^2 was used for mixed condensates.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-density QCD sum rule studies of hyperons in nuclear matter, specifically the calculations for Lambda hyperon self-energies by Jin (1994) and for Sigma hyperon self-energies by Jin (1995), by including negative parity states for all three hyperons (Sigma, Lambda, Xi). It also relates to chiral perturbation theory calculations of baryon octet mass shifts by Savage (1996) and the chiral quark-meson coupling model applied to hyperons by Miyatsu (2009). The results enable comparison of medium modifications across the strange baryon octet and provide input for models of dense astrophysical objects and heavy ion collision phenomenology.
