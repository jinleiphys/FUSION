# Pseudorapidity dependent hydrodynamic response in heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a differential hydrodynamic response relation, $V_2(ζ)=\int dξG(ζ-ξ) \mathcal{E}_2(ξ)$, to describe the formation of a pseudorapidity dependent elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, in response to a fluctuating three-dimensional initial density profile. By analyzing the medium expansion using event-by-event simulations of 3+1D MUSIC, with initial conditions generated via the AMPT model, the differential response relation is verified. Given the response relation, we are able to separate the two-point correlation of elliptic flow in pseudorapidity into fluid response and two-point correlation of initial eccentricity. The fluid response contains information of the speed of sound and shear viscosity of the medium. From the pseudorapidity dependent response relation, a finite radius of convergence of hydrodynamic gradient expansion is obtained with respect to realistic fluids in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the pseudorapidity-dependent elliptic flow \(V_2(\zeta)\) can be described by a non-local, differential hydrodynamic response relation \(V_2(\zeta)=\int d\xi G(\zeta-\xi) \mathcal{E}_2(\xi)\), where the response function \(G\) is determined by fluid properties and the relation is verified only under the assumption of a boost-invariant background on top of which longitudinal fluctuations are treated as perturbations.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code, with initial conditions generated by the AMPT model for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV in the 30-40% centrality class. They decomposed the initial eccentricity and final elliptic flow into Fourier modes in pseudorapidity and space-time rapidity, then extracted the response coefficients \(G_n\) by solving linear relations between moments of these distributions. The analysis used approximately 5000 events for the main results and 1000 events for the convergence study, with a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\).

## Key numbers

* For ideal hydrodynamics (\(\eta/s=0.001\)), the radius of convergence of the gradient expansion is \(k^*=1.209\) (from both slope and Pade methods).
* For \(\eta/s=0.2\), the radius of convergence is \(k^*=1.192\) (slope) and \(1.189\) (Pade).
* The initial two-point correlation length of eccentricity is \(\bra (\Delta \xi)^2\ket=12.81 \pm 0.08\) for the 30-40% centrality class.
* The pole closest to the origin in the complex \(k^2\)-plane is at \((1.4615,0)\) for the ideal case and \((1.4126,0)\) for \(\eta/s=0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard linear response relation \(V_2=G_0 \mathcal{E}_2\) (Ollitrault 1992, Qiu 2011, Niemi 2012) to include pseudorapidity dependence, enabling differential study of the QGP medium properties. The formulation enables extraction of the shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) from two-point correlations of elliptic flow, and provides a framework to analyze the convergence of hydrodynamic gradient expansion in realistic 3+1D expanding systems, building on studies of one-dimensional Bjorken expansion (Heller 2015, Heller 2013, Basar 2015) and dispersion relations (Withers 2018, Grozdanov 2019).
