# Spin Dependent Parton Distributions in a Bound Nucleon

> The determination of the spin distribution functions for the neutron requires an understanding of the changes induced by a nuclear medium. We present the first estimates of the changes induced in the spin-dependent, valence parton distributions of the proton and neutron bound in $^3$He and $^6$Li. For $^3$He the result is quite reassuring, in that the off-shell correction to $g_{1n}$ is very small. On the other hand, in $^6$Li the corrections to the spin distributions in the bound proton are sufficiently large that they should be taken into account if one aims for a precision of better than 10%. We provide a simple parametrization of the off-shell changes in the valence spin distributions of $^3$He and $^6$Li, which can be used with any conventional estimate of nuclear binding and Fermi motion corrections.

## Key claim

The off-shell correction to the neutron spin structure function \(g_{1n}\) in \(^3\)He is very small, validating the use of \(^3\)He as an effective neutron target; however, for \(^6\)Li the corrections to the spin distributions in the bound proton are sufficiently large that they must be accounted for if one aims for a precision better than 10%.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model to calculate free nucleon valence parton distributions and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to describe the nuclear medium, applying the same quark model for both the nucleon and the nucleus. For \(^3\)He, empirical density distributions were used with local density approximation to compute mean-field potentials, while for \(^6\)Li the mean-field potentials and nucleon densities were calculated self-consistently within QMC. The off-shell corrections were isolated by computing the ratio of free to bound polarized valence quark distributions, averaged over the nuclear density, and parametrized for general use with any conventional nuclear binding and Fermi motion corrections.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of off-shell corrections in the deuteron (Deut, year not given) to heavier nuclei, providing the first consistent calculations for \(^3\)He and \(^6\)Li using the same quark model (MIT bag) for both the nucleon and nuclear structure (QMC model). The results enable more precise extraction of the neutron spin structure function \(g_{1n}\) from future experiments using \(^6\)Li targets, and confirm that \(^3\)He remains a reliable effective neutron target without significant off-shell corrections. The parametrizations provided can be combined with standard nuclear structure calculations (e.g., from the EMC effect literature and convolution approaches by Bickerstaff and Thomas) to account for binding and Fermi motion.
