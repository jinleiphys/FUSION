# Baryon structure from Lattice QCD

> We present recent lattice results on the baryon spectrum, nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors, nucleon to $Δ$ transition form factors as well as the $Δ$ electromagnetic form factors. The masses of the low lying baryons and the nucleon form factors are calculated using two degenerate flavors of twisted mass fermions down to pion mass of about 270 MeV. We compare to the results of other collaborations. The nucleon to $Δ$ transition and $Δ$ form factors are calculated in a hybrid scheme, which uses staggered sea quarks and domain wall valence quarks. The dominant dipole nucleon to $Δ$ transition form factor is also evaluated using dynamical domain wall fermions. The transverse density distributions of the $Δ$ in the infinite momentum frame are extracted using the form factors determined from lattice QCD.

# Baryon structure from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD results for baryon masses and nucleon form factors from different fermion discretization schemes show agreement before taking the continuum limit, but the nucleon isovector electric form factor falls off slower than experimental parametrizations, and the axial mass extracted from lattice data is larger than the experimental value, indicating that lattice results at pion masses down to about 270 MeV do not yet fully reproduce the physical regime.

## Method

The authors compute baryon masses from two-point correlation functions using twisted mass fermions (TMF) with two degenerate flavors, clover fermions, and a hybrid approach combining staggered sea quarks with domain wall valence quarks. Nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors are extracted from three-point functions using sequential inversions through the sink, with an overcomplete set of lattice momentum vectors solved via singular value decomposition to obtain electric and magnetic Sachs form factors. The nucleon-to-Delta transition and Delta electromagnetic form factors are calculated using the hybrid scheme and dynamical domain wall fermions, with optimized sinks constructed to isolate subdominant form factors.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass reached: about 270 MeV for the TMF calculations of baryon masses and nucleon form factors
- Lattice spacings used: 0.089 fm and 0.070 fm for TMF, 0.124 fm for the hybrid action, 0.0907 fm for clover fermions
- Spatial lengths: 2.1 fm and 2.7 fm for TMF calculations
- Nucleon axial charge g_A: extrapolated to the physical point yields a value lower than experiment by slightly more than one standard deviation
- Axial mass m_A from dipole fit to lattice G_A(Q^2): larger than the experimental value of about 1.1 GeV
- Delta electric quadrupole form factor G_E2 at Q^2=0: -0.810 ± 291 (quenched), -0.87 ± 67 (N_F=2 Wilson), -2.06^{+1.27}_{-2.35} (hybrid), in units of e/(2m_Delta^2)

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of baryon structure by the European Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) and compares with results from QCDSF, PACS-CS, BMW, CERN, LHPC, RBC-UKQCD, and JLQCD collaborations. The results for nucleon form factors are consistent with recent high-accuracy results from the LHP Collaboration (Bratt 2008) and RBC-UKQCD Collaboration (Ohta 2008). The extraction of transverse charge densities for the Delta baryon enables a field-theoretic interpretation of hadron shape, showing a prolate deformation for the Delta^+ polarized along the x-axis. The method for extracting excited baryon states using chi-squared minimization with unbiased error evaluation (AMIAS method) is applied to the Roper resonance question, which remains unresolved regarding its ordering relative to the negative parity partner of the nucleon.
