# Particle production in quantum transport theories

> The particle production in the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions is discussed in the framework of the nonequilibrium Green's functions formalism. The evolution equations of the Green's functions for fermions allows for the discussion of the off-shell fermion propagator and of the large momentum component in the initial state. For the case of a homogeneous system numerical calculations of the meson production rate are performed and compared with the semiclassical production rate.

## Key claim

The quantum transport (Kadanoff-Baym) treatment of meson production in intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions yields production rates that differ significantly from semiclassical (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) rates, particularly due to off-shell fermion propagator effects and the proper treatment of the initial-state time contour, but the comparison is made for a simplified homogeneous system with a scalar meson-nucleon coupling rather than a realistic vertex.

## Method

The author solves the Kadanoff-Baym equations for a homogeneous system of nonrelativistic fermions interacting via a two-body potential, using a direct Born approximation for the fermion self-energy and a one-loop approximation for the meson self-energy. The fermion evolution is initialized with two counter-streaming Fermi spheres (p_f = 255 MeV/c) at collision energies of 180 MeV and 110 MeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame, and the meson production rate is computed perturbatively from the fermion Green's functions. For comparison, a semiclassical BUU-like evolution is performed using the same fermion self-energy in the on-shell approximation, with the meson production rate calculated from the on-shell two-nucleon collision process.

## Key numbers

- Initial Fermi sphere radius: p_f = 255 MeV/c
- Collision energies studied: 180 MeV and 110 MeV per nucleon in the center-of-mass frame
- Momentum grid spacing: 46 MeV/c, with grid size |p| < 920 MeV/c
- Imaginary time evolution: tau_0 = 3 fm/c; real time evolution: 20 fm/c
- Fermion width: approximately 50 MeV, giving a damping time of about 4 fm/c
- Time steps used for large meson mass/momentum: 0.067 fm/c (real) and 0.15 fm/c (imaginary)
- Meson masses studied: 20, 140, 300, and 500 MeV
- Semiclassical production rate calculated with gamma = 50 MeV
- Average kinetic energy in the correlated initial state is about 10 MeV/nucleon larger than in the Hartree state; the quantum evolution from the Hartree state increases kinetic energy by 30 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the numerical solution of the Kadanoff-Baym equations for homogeneous nuclear matter, first presented by Danielewicz (1984), to the calculation of meson production rates. It contrasts the quantum transport approach with the standard semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) simulations used in heavy-ion collision modeling, showing that off-shell effects and the proper treatment of the initial time contour (including imaginary-time evolution) produce systematically different production rates. The results enable future comparisons with experimental particle production yields per participant, provided realistic meson-nucleon vertices and delta degrees of freedom are included.
