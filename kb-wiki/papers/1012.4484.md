# Chiral Aspects of Improved Staggered Fermions with 2+1-Flavors from the HotQCD Collaboration

> We present recent results from lattice simulations of 2+1 flavors of improved staggered fermions at zero baryon number density near the high temperature crossover. Included are new results from simulations of asqtad fermions at Nt = 12 and a nearly physical Goldstone pion mass and from simulations of HISQ fermions at Nt = 6 and 8. We focus on observables sensitive to chiral symmetry and confinement. A companion HotQCD talk discusses the effects of staggered-fermion taste-symmetry breaking on thermodynamic quantities.

## Key claim

The HotQCD collaboration presents a preliminary continuum-extrapolated pseudocritical temperature for the chiral transition in 2+1-flavor QCD, but this estimate is derived from asqtad fermion data only and carries a combined statistical and systematic uncertainty of ±6 MeV; the analysis uses a scaling ansatz motivated by O(N) models that works "reasonably well" but omits Nτ = 4 data for stability.

## Method

The collaboration performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of improved staggered fermions at zero baryon density, using the asqtad action at Nτ = 12 with a light quark mass m_l = 0.05 m_s and the HISQ/tree action at Nτ = 6 and 8 at the same mass ratio. They computed the disconnected chiral susceptibility χ_l,disc from the light quark Dirac operator and extracted pseudocritical temperatures T_p by fitting the peak positions; these were then extrapolated to the continuum and chiral limits using an ansatz T_p(m_l,Nτ) = T_c + b (m_l/m_s)^d + c/Nτ^2 with the O(N) critical exponent d ≈ 0.54.

## Key numbers

- Continuum-extrapolated pseudocritical temperature at the physical point (m_l/m_s ≈ 1/27): T_p = (164 ± 6) MeV, from asqtad data at Nτ = 6, 8, 12.
- The transition region for asqtad at Nτ = 12 and m_l = 0.05 m_s is around T ≈ 170 MeV.
- The scale is set using r1 = 0.3106(8)(18)(4) fm for asqtad calculations and r0 = 0.469 fm for HISQ/tree calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier HotQCD calculations (HotQCD Collaboration, 2009) by adding asqtad data at finer lattice spacing (Nτ = 12) and introducing HISQ/tree results with intrinsically smaller cutoff effects, addressing the need for continuum extrapolations identified in prior studies of cutoff effects in thermodynamic quantities (Bazavov et al., 2009; Borsanyi et al., 2010). The preliminary T_p = 164 ± 6 MeV provides a benchmark for future continuum extrapolations using HISQ/tree data at Nτ = 12, and the scaling analysis of the chiral condensate enables a more precise determination of the chiral critical temperature T_c in the massless limit.
