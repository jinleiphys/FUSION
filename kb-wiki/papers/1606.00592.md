# Precise Determination of Charge Dependent Pion-Nucleon-Nucleon Coupling Constants

> We undertake a covariance error analysis of the pion-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants from the Granada-2013 np and pp database comprising a total of 6720 scattering data below LAB energy of 350 MeV. Assuming a unique pion-nucleon coupling constant in the One Pion Exchange potential above a boundary radius $r_c=3 {\rm fm}$ we obtain $f^2=0.0763(1)$. The effects of charge symmetry breaking on the $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$ and $^3P_2$ partial waves are analyzed and we find $f_{p}^2 = 0.0761(4)$, $f_{0}^2 = 0.0790(9)$ and $f_{c}^2 = 0.0772(6)$ with a strong anti-correlation between $f_c^2$ and $f_0^2$. We successfully test normality for the residuals of the fit. Potential tails in terms of different boundary radii as well as chiral Two-Pion-Exchange contributions as sources of systematic uncertainty are also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper determines the pion-nucleon-nucleon coupling constants from the Granada-2013 np and pp database, finding that a charge-independent fit yields \(f^2 = 0.0763(1)\), but when charge symmetry breaking is allowed in the \(^3P_0\), \(^3P_1\), and \(^3P_2\) partial waves, the resulting values \(f_p^2 = 0.0761(4)\), \(f_0^2 = 0.0790(9)\), and \(f_c^2 = 0.0772(6)\) become incompatible with charge independence due to a strong anti-correlation between \(f_c^2\) and \(f_0^2\).

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis (PWA) of the Granada-2013 database, which contains 6720 np and pp scattering data below a laboratory energy of 350 MeV, using a coarse-grained representation of the nuclear force with delta-shell potentials below a boundary radius \(r_c = 3\) fm and a charge-dependent one-pion exchange (CD-OPE) potential above that radius. They carried out a covariance error analysis, fitting the pion-nucleon coupling constants as free parameters alongside the delta-shell parameters, and tested for charge symmetry breaking by allowing separate parameters for pp and np in the \(^1S_0\), \(^3P_0\), \(^3P_1\), and \(^3P_2\) partial waves. The statistical quality of the fits was verified using quantile-quantile plots to test normality of the residuals.

## Key numbers

- \(f^2 = 0.0763(1)\) from a charge-independent fit to the full Granada-2013 database (6720 data, 47 parameters, \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.041\)).
- \(f_p^2 = 0.0761(4)\), \(f_0^2 = 0.0790(9)\), \(f_c^2 = 0.0772(6)\) from a fit with charge symmetry breaking on the \(^1S_0\) and \(P\) waves (6741 data, 55 parameters, \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.025\)).
- \(g_p^2/(4\pi) = 13.774(75)\), \(g_0^2/(4\pi) = 14.30(16)\), \(g_c^2/(4\pi) = 13.984(82)\) from the full covariance matrix analysis.
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation yields a lower bound \(f_{\pi NN}^2 > 0.07324(4)\) using PDG values \(F_{\pi^+} = 92.21(14)\) MeV and \(g_A = 1.2723(23)\).

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's analyses (Klomp 1991, Stoks 1993, de Swart 1997), which used 4313 data and found \(f^2 = 0.0749(4)\), by employing a larger database (6720 data) and a more rigorous statistical treatment including normality tests. The results are compared with \(\pi N\) scattering determinations, such as those by Baru (2010, 2011) yielding \(g_c^2/(4\pi) = 13.69(20)\), and the paper enables future searches for isospin breaking in the nuclear force by providing precise, correlated coupling constants and demonstrating that charge dependence in P-waves is statistically significant.
