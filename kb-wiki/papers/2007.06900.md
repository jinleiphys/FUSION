# Spinodal instabilities of spin polarized asymmetric nuclear matter

> We analyze the spinodal instabilities of spin polarized asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature for several configurations of the neutron and proton spins. The calculations are performed with the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock (BHF) approach using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus a three-nucleon force of Urbana type. An analytical parametrization of the energy density, which reproduces with good accuracy the BHF results, is employed to determine the spinodal instability region. We find that, independently of the of the orientation of the neutron and proton spins, the spinodal instability region shinks when the system is polarized, being its size smaller smaller when neutron and proton spins are antiparallel than when they are oriented in a parallel way. We find also that the spinodal instability is always dominated by total density fluctuation independently of the degree of polarization of the system, and that restoration of the isospin symmetry in the liquid phase, {\it i.e.,} the so-called isospin distillation or fragmentation effect, becomes less efficient with the polarization of the system.

## Key claim

The spinodal instability region of asymmetric nuclear matter shrinks when the neutron and proton spins are polarized, and the size of this region is smaller when the spins are antiparallel than when they are parallel; however, the instability remains dominated by total density fluctuations regardless of the degree of polarization, and the isospin distillation effect becomes less efficient with polarization.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner–Hartree–Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus a three-nucleon force of Urbana type to compute the energy density of spin polarized asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature. An analytical parametrization of the energy density, fitted to reproduce the BHF results for four specific combinations of isospin asymmetry and spin polarizations, was then employed to determine the spinodal instability region by analyzing the convexity of the energy density with respect to neutron and proton densities at fixed spin polarizations.

## Key numbers

- The spinodal instability region disappears completely when matter is totally polarized, i.e., for \(S_n=S_p=1\) and \(S_n=-S_p=1\).
- For \(S_n=-S_p=0.75\) the instability region is extremely small, almost completely suppressed, whereas for \(S_n=S_p=0.75\) it is much larger but still clearly reduced compared to the non-polarized case.
- At a fixed density \(\rho=0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) and proton fraction \(x_p=0.25\), the ratio \(\delta\rho^-_p/\delta\rho^-_n\) is positive in all cases, indicating dominance of total density fluctuations; for symmetric matter (\(x_p=0.5\)) this ratio equals 1.
- The coefficients \(v_i(\rho)\) in the energy density parametrization are given by \(v_i(\rho)=a\rho^{\gamma}+b\rho^{\delta}\); for example, \(v_0\) has \(a=-118.92\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3\gamma-3}\), \(\gamma=1.60\), \(b=484.31\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3\delta-3}\), \(\delta=3.99\).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of spinodal instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter, which had been performed using mean-field models with Skyrme or Gogny forces (Margueron 2003, Ducoin 2006, 2007) and BHF approaches (Vidana 2008), but only for spin-saturated (non-polarized) matter. It also complements studies using relativistic mean-field models (Rabhi 2009, Fang 2017, Avancini 2018) that examined the effect of strong magnetic fields on spinodal instabilities. The results enable future exploration of the nuclear matter phase diagram in systems with polarized spins, such as neutron stars with strong magnetic fields or multifragmentation experiments with polarized targets and beams.
