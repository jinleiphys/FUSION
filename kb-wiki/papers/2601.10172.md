# Quadrupole transitions of $^{10}$C and their isospin symmetry with $^{10}$Be

> We investigate the structures of $^{10}$C focusing on the quadrupole properties in comparison with the mirror nucleus $^{10}$Be. We describe $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be in the variation of the multiple bases of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), in which the multiple AMD bases are optimized simultaneously in the total-energy variation. In the monopole transitions, we confirm the isospin symmetry between $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be by exchanging protons and neutrons. In the quadrupole transitions, most cases show larger values in $^{10}$C than those of $^{10}$Be, except for the transition of $2^+_1\to 0^+_1$. The transition of $2^+_1\to 0^+_1$ shows similar values in the two nuclei in spite of the different proton numbers, which agrees with the experimental situation as an anomaly. This relation comes from the small proton deformation in $^{10}$C due to its subclosed nature and the large proton deformation in $^{10}$Be due to two-$α$ clustering. This property can also be seen in the quadrupole moments of the two nuclei. In the neutron deformations of $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be, the opposite tendency of protons is confirmed and these results ensure the isospin symmetry between the two nuclei. We also confirm the large quadrupole transitions between the elongated linear-chain states. It would be desirable for future experiments to investigate the present characteristics of the transitions in the two nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anomalous near-equality of the $B(E2,2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ values between the mirror nuclei $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be, contrary to the naive expectation that proton-rich nuclei have larger $B(E2)$ values, originates from the small proton deformation in $^{10}$C due to its subclosed nature and the large proton deformation in $^{10}$Be due to two-$\alpha$ clustering.

## Method

The authors employed the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework with a newly developed "multicool method" that simultaneously optimizes multiple AMD basis states in a total-energy variation without imposing physical constraints. For excited states, they introduced a pseudopotential to enforce orthogonality to the ground-state configurations, then superposed all configurations with angular-momentum and parity projections to solve the generalized eigenvalue problem. The Hamiltonian used the Volkov No.2 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force, with the same parameters as previous studies of $^{10}$Be.

## Key numbers

- Experimental $B(E2,2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ for $^{10}$C is 8.8(3) $e^2$fm$^4$, slightly smaller than that of $^{10}$Be at 9.2(3) $e^2$fm$^4$
- Total energy of $^{10}$C ground state: $-59.32$ MeV (experimental: $-60.32$ MeV)
- Total energy of $^{10}$Be ground state: $-63.75$ MeV (experimental: $-64.98$ MeV)
- Energy difference $\Delta E$ between $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be: 4.43 MeV (experimental: 4.66 MeV)
- Matter radius of $^{10}$C ground state: 2.36 fm (experimental: 2.27(3) fm)
- Matter radius of $^{10}$Be ground state: 2.33 fm (experimental: 2.30(2) fm)
- Charge radius of $^{10}$Be: 2.35 fm (experimental: 2.357(18) fm)
- Excitation energy of $0^+_2$ state in $^{10}$C: 7.22 MeV (experimental: 5.38 MeV)
- Excitation energy of $0^+_2$ state in $^{10}$Be: 7.72 MeV (experimental: 6.18 MeV)
- Expectation value $\langle N \rangle$ for $^{10}$C ground state: 7.51 (lowest quanta is 6)
- $\langle N \rangle$ for $0^+_2$, $2^+_3$, and $4^+_2$ states in $^{10}$C: around 13-14
- Matter radii of $0^+_2$, $2^+_3$, and $4^+_2$ states in $^{10}$C: around 2.9 fm
- Energy gain from single AMD to multicool for $^{10}$C intrinsic state: 5.8 MeV
- Radius increase from single AMD to multicool for $^{10}$C intrinsic state: 0.11 fm

## Context

This work extends the multicool AMD method previously applied to $^{10}$Be by Myo et al. (2023) and other light nuclei, now focusing on the mirror nucleus $^{10}$C. The study addresses the anomalous $B(E2)$ systematics between $^{10}$C and $^{10}$Be that contradicts the typical mirror-nucleus trend observed in many experimental data from Tunl and NNDC databases. The results enable future experimental investigations of the predicted large quadrupole transitions between elongated linear-chain states in both nuclei, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding isospin symmetry breaking in quadrupole properties of light mirror pairs.
