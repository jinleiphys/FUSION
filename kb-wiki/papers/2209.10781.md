# Preparations for Quantum Simulations of Quantum Chromodynamics in 1+1 Dimensions: (II) Single-Baryon $β$-Decay in Real Time

> A framework for quantum simulations of real-time weak decays of hadrons and nuclei in a 2-flavor lattice theory in one spatial dimension is presented. A single generation of the Standard Model is found to require 16 qubits per spatial lattice site after mapping to spin operators via the Jordan-Wigner transformation. Both quantum chromodynamics and flavor-changing weak interactions are included in the dynamics, the latter through four-Fermi effective operators. Quantum circuits which implement time evolution in this lattice theory are developed and run on Quantinuum's H1-1 20-qubit trapped ion system to simulate the $β$-decay of a single baryon on one lattice site. These simulations include the initial state preparation and are performed for both one and two Trotter time steps. The potential intrinsic error-correction properties of this type of lattice theory are discussed and the leading lattice Hamiltonian required to simulate $0νββ$-decay of nuclei induced by a neutrino Majorana mass term is provided.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that real-time quantum simulations of single-baryon beta decay in 1+1 dimensional lattice QCD with weak interactions are feasible on current trapped-ion quantum hardware, achieving results at the approximately 5% level with minimal error mitigation, but only for a simplified vector-like four-Fermi operator (not the full V-A structure of the Standard Model) and for a single spatial lattice site with one or two Trotter steps.

## Method

The authors constructed a Kogut-Susskind lattice Hamiltonian for 1+1 dimensional QCD with two quark flavors (up and down) plus electrons and neutrinos, including a four-Fermi effective operator for beta decay. They mapped the Hamiltonian to 16 qubits per spatial lattice site using the Jordan-Wigner transformation, developed quantum circuits for Trotterized time evolution, and implemented these circuits on Quantinuum's H1-1 20-qubit trapped ion system to simulate the decay of a Delta-minus baryon into a Delta-zero baryon plus an electron and antineutrino on a single lattice site.

## Key numbers

- A single generation of the Standard Model requires 16 qubits per spatial lattice site after Jordan-Wigner mapping (12 quark and 4 lepton qubits)
- State preparation for the Delta-minus baryon requires 9 CNOT gates
- Each Trotter step of the strong Hamiltonian (quarks and glue) uses 114 CNOTs with an ancilla
- The simplified valence beta-decay operator Trotterization requires 50 CNOTs
- Results were obtained at the approximately 5% level of uncertainty
- Fermi's coupling constant is given as G_F = 1.1663787(6) x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- The chosen simulation parameters were m_u = 0.9, m_d = 2.1, g = 2, and m_e = m_nu = 0 (in lattice units)
- The Delta-minus baryon energy gap above vacuum is 6.409 (in lattice units)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum simulations of SU(3) gauge theory with N_f=1,2 flavors in 1+1 dimensions (Farrell 2022, Atas 2022) by adding flavor-changing weak interactions via a four-Fermi operator. It builds on foundational work in lattice gauge theory quantum simulations by Klco (2019), Ciavarella (2021), and others. The framework enables future real-time studies of nuclear beta decay and neutrinoless double-beta decay processes that are inaccessible to Euclidean-space lattice QCD calculations, and the authors provide the leading lattice Hamiltonian required to simulate 0νββ-decay induced by a neutrino Majorana mass term.
