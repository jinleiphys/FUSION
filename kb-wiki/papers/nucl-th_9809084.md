# Effective theory for the two-nucleon system

> We apply the method of unitary transformations to a model two-nucleon potential and construct from it an effective potential in a subspace of momenta below a given cut-off $Λ$. The S-matrices in the full space and in the subspace are shown to be identical. We solve numerically the Schrödinger equation in the small momentum space and recover exactly the bound and scattering states of the full theory. We then expand the heavy repulsive meson exchange of the effective potential in a series of local contact terms and discuss the question of naturalness of the corresponding coupling constants. Using our exact effective theory we address further issues related to the chiral perturbation theory approach of the two-nucleon system. The coordinate space representation of the effective potential is also considered.

## Key claim

The authors construct an exact effective theory for the two-nucleon system by applying unitary transformations to a model potential, demonstrating that the S-matrices in the full momentum space and in the low-momentum subspace are identical. However, this equivalence holds only for scattering states with initial asymptotic momenta belonging to the low-momentum subspace, and the transformed bound states reside in the low-momentum subspace only for physically reasonable choices of the cutoff.

## Method

The authors start with a model nucleon-nucleon potential consisting of attractive light-meson exchange (mass μ_L) and repulsive heavy-meson exchange (mass μ_H) and divide momentum space into low (|p| ≤ Λ) and high (|p| > Λ) subspaces using projection operators. They apply the Okubo unitary transformation to block-diagonalize the Hamiltonian, solving the resulting nonlinear decoupling equation for the operator A by iteration with a regularized potential to avoid singularities at the cutoff. The effective potential in the low-momentum subspace is then constructed from the transformed Hamiltonian, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved numerically using only momenta below Λ.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.23 MeV for the model potential
- Heavy meson mass μ_H = 613.7 MeV; light meson mass μ_L = 305.9 MeV
- Coupling strengths: V_H = 7.291, V_L = 3.177
- Cutoff Λ = 400 MeV (primary value) and Λ = 200 MeV (secondary value)
- With Λ = 400 MeV and regularization parameters a = 20 keV, b = 10 keV, the shift in binding energy due to regularization is 0.012 keV (0.01 permille)
- Using 40 quadrature points, the effective binding energy agrees within 9 digits with the full-space result
- For Λ = 400 MeV, phase shifts go to zero at T_lab = 341 MeV; for Λ = 200 MeV, at T_lab = 85 MeV
- For Λ = 2 GeV, the relative difference between effective and original potential is [V(q,q') - V'(q,q')]/V(0,0) ∼ 0.02

## Context

This work extends the method of unitary transformations (Okubo 1954; FST) to construct an exact effective theory for the two-nucleon system, serving as a controlled model to test concepts from chiral perturbation theory approaches to nuclear forces (Weinberg; various authors 1990s). The authors use their exact effective theory to study the naturalness of coupling constants when expanding the heavy meson exchange in local contact terms, and to investigate the convergence properties of such expansions, which is directly relevant for systematic effective field theory treatments of the two-nucleon system. The coordinate-space representation of the effective potential is also examined, revealing strong non-localities induced by the projection.
