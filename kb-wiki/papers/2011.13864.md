# Transseries for causal diffusive systems

> The large proper-time behaviour of expanding boost-invariant fluids has provided many crucial insights into quark-gluon plasma dynamics. Here we formulate and explore the late-time behaviour of nonequilibrium dynamics at the level of linearized perturbations of equilibrium, but without any special symmetry assumptions. We introduce a useful quantitative approximation scheme in which hydrodynamic modes appear as perturbative contributions while transients are nonperturbative. In this way, solutions are naturally organized into transseries as they are in the case of boost-invariant flows. We focus our attention on the ubiquitous telegrapher's equation, the simplest example of a causal theory with a hydrodynamic sector. In position space we uncover novel transient contributions as well as Stokes phenomena which change the structure of the transseries based on the spacetime region or the choice of initial data.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a transseries description of linearized nonequilibrium dynamics that separates hydrodynamic modes (perturbative) from transient nonhydrodynamic modes (nonperturbative), using the telegrapher's equation as a minimal causal model. The key hedge is that this transseries structure in position space depends on the spacetime region and the choice of initial data, and that the perturbative series generically diverges factorially for initial data with unbounded momentum-space support.

## Method

The authors introduce a formal small parameter epsilon by rescaling time as t -> t/epsilon^alpha and space as x -> x/epsilon, choosing alpha = z (the Lifshitz exponent) to preserve hydrodynamic scaling. They apply this to the telegrapher's equation (tau partial_t^2 rho + partial_t rho - D partial_x^2 rho = 0) and construct a transseries ansatz in momentum space: rho_hat(t,k) = sum_n a_hat_n(t,k) epsilon^{2n} + exp(-t/(tau epsilon^2)) sum_n b_hat_n(t,k) epsilon^{2n}. The coefficients are solved from nested ODE systems with initial conditions that couple the perturbative and nonperturbative sectors. The position-space coefficients are obtained by inverse Fourier transform, and the large-order behavior is analyzed via ratio tests and Borel transforms for various initial data (compact support, delta function, Gaussian).

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum for mode collision in the telegrapher's equation is k_c^2 = 1/(4 D tau); with the chosen values tau = D = 1/2, this gives k_c^2 = 1.
- For compactly supported initial data v(k) = (1/(2pi)) Theta(A^2 - k^2), the perturbative series at x=0 converges only when A < 1 (i.e., momentum support below k_c = 1), with ratio test giving q = A^2.
- For delta-function initial data v(x) = delta(x), the ratio between successive coefficients at x=0 grows as q_n ~ (tau/t) n, implying factorial divergence.
- For Gaussian initial data with width s, the factorial growth prefactor r satisfies q_n ~ r^{-1} n, where r = (s^2/(8 D tau)) mu(2Dt/s^2), with mu(eta) = (1+eta)^2 for eta < 1 and mu(eta) = 4 eta for eta > 1, and t_c = s^2/(2D).

## Context

This work extends the transseries framework previously developed for boost-invariant Bjorken flow (Heller 2013, Heller 2015, Basar 2015, Aniceto 2015) to linearized perturbations without special symmetry assumptions, building on the authors' recent analysis of hydrodynamic gradient expansion convergence in linearized conformal hydrodynamics (Heller 2020). The telegrapher's equation arises in Muller-Israel-Stewart relativistic hydrodynamics and quasihydrodynamics (Grozdanov 2018), and the results enable systematic study of how transient nonhydrodynamic modes affect late-time behavior in causal diffusive systems, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and condensed matter systems featuring a k-gap phenomenon.
