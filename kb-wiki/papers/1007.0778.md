# Covariant spectator theory of np scattering: Effective range expansions and relativistic deuteron wave functions

> We present the effective range expansions for the 1S_0 and 3S_1 scattering phase shifts, and the relativistic deuteron wave functions that accompany our recent high precision fits (with chi^2/N{data} approx 1) to the 2007 world np data below 350 MeV. The wave functions are expanded in a series of analytical functions (with the correct asymptotic behavior at both large and small arguments) that can be Fourier-transformed from momentum to coordinate space and are convenient to use in any application. A fortran subroutine to compute these wave functions can be obtained from the authors.

# Covariant spectator theory of np scattering: Effective range expansions and relativistic deuteron wave functions

## Key claim
The covariant spectator theory (CST) yields high-precision fits to the 2007 world np data below 350 MeV with chi^2/N_data approximately 1, but the effective range expansions derived from these fits are only qualitatively accurate up to about 150 MeV lab kinetic energy, and the deuteron wave functions include small P-state components (0.3% or less) that are required by manifest covariance but absent in some other relativistic approaches.

## Method
The authors solved the CST bound-state and scattering equations for np interactions using two one-boson-exchange models (WJC-1 with 27 parameters and WJC-2 with 15 parameters) fitted to 3788 np data points below 350 MeV. They extracted effective range parameters for the 1S0 and 3S1 channels by evaluating phase shifts at four energies (0.0001, 10, 25, and 50 MeV) and fitting a four-term expansion. The relativistic deuteron wave functions were decomposed into four independent amplitudes (S-state u, D-state w, and two P-state components v_t and v_s) and expanded in analytic functions with correct asymptotic behavior at large and small arguments.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length for 1S0: a = -23.7494 fm (WJC-1) and -23.7496 fm (WJC-2)
- Scattering length for 3S1: a = 5.4295 fm (WJC-1) and 5.4342 fm (WJC-2)
- Effective range for 1S0: r0 = 2.6261 fm (WJC-1) and 2.6623 fm (WJC-2)
- Effective range for 3S1: r0 = 1.7601 fm (WJC-1) and 1.7666 fm (WJC-2)
- Fractional error Delta at 1 MeV for 1S0: 0.0044 (WJC-1) and 0.0038 (WJC-2)
- Fractional error Delta at 1 MeV for 3S1: -0.0011 (WJC-1) and -0.0009 (WJC-2)
- S-state probability (scaled): 92.3330% (WJC-1) and 93.5985% (WJC-2)
- D-state probability (scaled): 7.3432% (WJC-1) and 6.3827% (WJC-2)
- P-state v_t probability (scaled): 0.1119% (WJC-1) and 0.0101% (WJC-2)
- P-state v_s probability (scaled): 0.2118% (WJC-1) and 0.0088% (WJC-2)
- Derivative term in normalization: -5.4743% (WJC-1) and -2.3101% (WJC-2)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous high-precision CST fits (Gross, 2008) by providing the effective range expansions and explicit deuteron wave functions for the WJC-1 and WJC-2 models. The wave functions include P-state components of purely relativistic origin first discussed by Buck and Gross (1979) and Blankenbecler and Cook, which are absent in some other relativistic approaches such as Keister (1991). The results enable future calculations of deuteron electromagnetic properties (magnetic and quadrupole moments) that require inclusion of interaction currents, which the authors defer to later work.
