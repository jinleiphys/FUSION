# QCD Thermodynamics on the Lattice from the Gradient Flow

> To obtain the precise values of the bulk quantities and transport coefficients in quark-gluon-plasma phase, we propose that a direct calculation of the renormalized energy-momentum tensor (EMT) on the lattice using the gradient flow. From one-point function of EMT, authors in Ref.[1] obtained the interaction measure and thermal entropy. The results are consistent with the one obtained by the integral method. Based on the success, we try to measure the two-point function of EMT, which is related to the transport coefficients. Advantages of our method are (1) a clear signal because of the smearing effects of the gradient flow and (2) no need to calculate the wave function renormalization of EMT. In addition, we give a short remark on a comparison of the numerical cost between the positive- and adjoint-flow methods for fermions, needed to obtain the EMT in the (2+1) flavor QCD.

# QCD Thermodynamics on the Lattice from the Gradient Flow

## Key claim
The gradient flow method can directly compute the renormalized energy-momentum tensor (EMT) on the lattice, yielding bulk thermodynamic quantities consistent with the integral method, and enabling two-point function measurements for transport coefficients with improved signal quality. The method avoids wave function renormalization of the EMT operator due to UV finiteness of flowed composite operators, but the flow-time dependence of the two-point function is stronger than for the one-point function, requiring careful extrapolation.

## Method
The authors use the small flow-time expansion of the Yang-Mills gradient flow to define a correctly-normalized conserved EMT on the lattice, with coefficients calculated perturbatively. For the quenched (pure gauge) case, they perform lattice simulations using the Wilson plaquette gauge action on lattices with fixed physical temperature T=1.65Tc, varying Nτ = 8, 10, 12, 16 with Ns/Nτ = 4, and take the continuum limit. For the two-point function, they measure the T12 correlator at finite flow-time and compare results across different lattice spacings and flow-times. For the (2+1)-flavor QCD extension, they compare the computational costs of the positive-flow and adjoint-flow methods for solving the fermion gradient flow equation.

## Key numbers
- Continuum limit results for (ε - 3P)/T4 and (ε + P)/T4 are obtained at T/Tc = 1.65, 1.24, and 0.99, consistent with results from Borsanyi (2012) using the integral method
- For the two-point function, gauge configurations number 3000, 5000, 2500, and 800 for Nτ = 8, 10, 12, and 16 respectively, with 200 Sweeps between measurements
- The flow-time parameter used for the two-point function is rsmearT = 0.25, where rsmear = √(8t)
- For the (2+1)-flavor QCD cost comparison on a 32³ × 8 lattice: positive-flow total cost = 21,104 seconds; adjoint-flow total cost = 196,180 seconds for 20 data points between 0 ≤ t/a² ≤ 2.0 with interval Δt/a² = 0.1

## Context
This work extends the gradient flow approach of Asakawa (2013) from one-point function calculations of bulk thermodynamic quantities to two-point function measurements needed for transport coefficients like shear viscosity. The results confirm consistency between the microscopic quantum field theory picture and the macroscopic integral method of Borsanyi (2012) for quenched QCD. The method addresses difficulties in prior lattice calculations of EMT correlators by Nakamura (2004), Meyer (2007), and Mages (2015), specifically the need for renormalization factors and poor signal-to-noise ratios. The comparison of positive-flow versus adjoint-flow methods for fermions provides practical guidance for extending these calculations to full (2+1)-flavor QCD, with the positive-flow method being approximately nine times cheaper for the parameters studied.
