# Jet charge modification in dense QCD matter

> Jet production and jet substructure modification in heavy-ion collisions have played an essential role in revealing the in-medium evolution of parton showers and the determination of the properties of strongly-interacting matter under extreme conditions. It is imperative to extend these studies to include flavor tagging and to devise observables that are sensitive to the partonic origin of jets. The average jet charge, defined as the momentum-weighted sum of the electric charges of particles inside the jet, is a proxy of the electric charge of the quark or gluon that initiates the jet. We demonstrate how the factorization framework of soft-collinear effective theory can be generalized to evaluate the jet charge in a dense strongly-interacting matter environment, such as the one produced in nuclear reactions at collider energies. Observables that can separate the contribution of in-medium branching from the trivial isospin effects are identified and their connection to established jet quenching effects is elucidated. We present predictions for the transverse momentum dependence of the jet charge distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions and its modification relative to the proton case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) factorization framework can be generalized to compute the average jet charge in heavy-ion collisions, and that the medium-induced modifications to the jet charge can be disentangled from trivial isospin effects by studying the ratio of jet charges in nucleus-nucleus versus proton-proton collisions. The authors hedge that their calculations are performed to first order in opacity and next-to-leading order in QCD, and that the accuracy of this order has been confirmed by experimental measurements for other observables.

## Method

The authors generalize the SCET factorization framework for jet charge in vacuum, developed by Krohn et al. (2012) and Waalewijn (2012), to include medium-induced corrections from the quark-gluon plasma. The medium modifications are incorporated by replacing vacuum splitting kernels with in-medium splitting functions calculated to first order in opacity, and by evolving the charge-weighted fragmentation functions using medium-modified DGLAP equations with the full splitting function \(P_{ij} \to P_{ij}+P_{ij}^{\rm med}\). Initial-state nuclear effects are included using the nCTEQ15 nuclear parton distribution functions, and the jet function and jet matching coefficient are constructed from the medium-induced splitting functions with an upper integration limit set by the jet radius \(R\).

## Key numbers

- Average Debye mass \(\mu_D = 0.75\) GeV, corresponding to an average temperature \(T \approx 340\) MeV for coupling \(g=1.9\) with \(N_f=2\) active quark flavors.
- The coupling between jet and QCD medium is set to \(g=1.9 \pm 0.1\) for Pb+Pb predictions.
- The nonperturbative fragmentation function evolution starts from \(\mu_0 = 1\) GeV in \(p+p\) collisions and from \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=0.2\) GeV in heavy-ion collisions.
- The jet scale is \(\mu = 2E \tan(R/2) \approx p_T R\).
- The scale of \(\alpha_s\) in medium-induced splitting functions is chosen as \(\sqrt{k_\perp^2 + \mu_D^2}\).
- The dijet event selection uses \(p_T > 50\) GeV and \(|\eta| > 2.1\) for both jets.

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based jet charge formalism developed for proton-proton collisions by Krohn et al. (2012) and Waalewijn (2012) to the heavy-ion environment, building upon the medium-induced splitting functions calculated by Ovanesyan and Vitev (2011) and the medium-modified DGLAP evolution framework of Kang et al. (2014) and Chien et al. (2015). It complements recent Monte Carlo simulations of jet charge in nucleus-nucleus reactions by Chen et al. (2019) by providing an analytic perturbative framework. The results enable extraction of the \((\kappa+1)\)th Mellin moment of the medium-induced splitting function from measurements of individual flavor jet charges, and can be used to test the evolution of energetic partons in QCD matter.
