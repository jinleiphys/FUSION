# Partial-wave analysis of $K^+$ nucleon scattering

> We have performed a partial-wave analysis of K$^+$-nucleon scattering in the momentum range from 0 to 1.5 GeV/c addressing the uncertainties of the results and comparing them with several previous analyses. It is found that the treatment of the reaction threshold behavior is particularly important. We find a T=0 scattering length which is not consistent with zero, as has been claimed by other analyses. The T=0 phase shifts for $\ell>0$ are consistent with a pure spin-orbit potential. Some indications for the production of a T=0 pentaquark with spin-parity D5/2+ are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find a T=0 scattering length that is not consistent with zero, contrary to claims by other analyses, and they discuss indications for a T=0 pentaquark with spin-parity D5/2+, but these are presented as tentative findings, with the pentaquark possibility described only as "some indications" that are "discussed" rather than claimed definitively.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis of K⁺-nucleon scattering data in the momentum range from 0 to 1.5 GeV/c, fitting phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for partial waves through F7/2 using a χ² minimization procedure. For T=1, they used 1880 data points (including angular distributions, total cross sections, polarization, and reaction cross sections) and parameterized S- and P-wave phase shifts with a tangent form, D-waves with a polynomial, and F-waves with a single term; inelasticities were parameterized with threshold-dependent forms. For T=0, they extracted amplitudes from K⁺-deuteron scattering data using their best T=1 fit as input, with 488 data points including total cross sections, elastic cross sections, polarization, and pion production data.

## Key numbers

- T=1 s-wave scattering length: −0.308 ± 0.003 fm (best fit central value, with error estimated from variation among minima and from fixing the scattering length)
- T=1 P1/2 scattering volume: −0.092 fm³ (best fit); T=1 P3/2 scattering volume: 0.103 fm³ (best fit)
- Total χ² for T=1 best fit: 2031.05, giving χ² per data point of 1.080 for 1880 data points
- Standard deviation of normalization factors for T=1 data sets: 2.2%, with mean normalization 1.0004
- T=1 P3/2 phase shift parameters: a = 13.357 (GeV/c)⁻³, b₁ = 126.676 (GeV/c)⁻², b₂ = −666.951 (GeV/c)⁻⁴, b₃ = 1276.123 (GeV/c)⁻⁶
- T=0 data set: 92 total cross section points, 17 pion production points, 336 elastic cross section points, 43 polarization points

## Context

This work extends the line of partial-wave analyses of K⁺-nucleon scattering by Hashimoto (energy-independent analysis from 0.6 to 1.5 GeV/c), the VPI group (Arndt et al., who used K-matrix parameterization and suggested a resonance in the P3/2 wave at masses around 1.78–1.80 GeV/c²), and Martin (who parameterized amplitudes directly with a single reaction threshold for all partial waves). The authors emphasize that treatment of reaction threshold behavior is particularly important, and they find that the T=0 phase shifts for ℓ>0 are consistent with a pure spin-orbit potential. The analysis enables more reliable phase shifts for calculations of multiple scattering corrections in nuclear medium studies and for testing theoretical models of hadronic interactions, and it provides partial-wave decomposition of the reaction cross section that may help identify production mechanisms near threshold.
