# A Co-moving Coordinate System for Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The equations of relativistic hydrodynamics are transformed so that steps forward in time preserves local simultaneity. In these variables, the space-time coordinates of neighboring points on the mesh are simultaneous according to co-moving observers. Aside from the time step varying as a function of the location on the mesh, the local velocity gradient and the local density then evolve according to non-relativistic equations of motion. Analytic solutions are found for two one-dimensional cases with constant speed of sound. One solution has a Gaussian density profile when mapped into the new coordinates. That solution is analyzed for the effects of longitudinal acceleration in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, especially in regards to two-particle correlation measurements of the longitudinal size.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic hydrodynamics can be reformulated in a co-moving coordinate system where local simultaneity is preserved by allowing different points on the mesh to propagate forward by different proper-time steps, and that this approach yields analytic solutions for one-dimensional cases with constant speed of sound, though the method is limited to irrotational flow and the analytic solutions assume complete stopping at initial time, which experiment suggests is not fully realized at RHIC.

## Method

The author transforms the relativistic hydrodynamic equations into a co-moving frame where the hyper-surface satisfies local simultaneity (u·dx=0), requiring that each mesh point advances by a different proper-time step δτ = α δτ_R, where α = exp(-∫ a·dx') depends on the acceleration. For one-dimensional flow with constant speed of sound, the equations are mapped to non-relativistic form using a scaled coordinate dℓ̃ = α dℓ, and analytic solutions are found for Gaussian and exponential entropy profiles. The Gaussian solution is compared to numerical solutions with Hwa-Bjorken initial conditions to assess the effects of longitudinal acceleration on lifetime estimates from two-particle correlations.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound used in the analytic Gaussian solution is c_s² = 0.2.
- For the Gaussian solution at RHIC, the source rapidity width σ_y is estimated to be ~1.7, giving x ≈ 2.4 in the Dawson's integral correction factor.
- The Hwa-Bjorken estimate underestimates the lifetime by approximately 10% when longitudinal acceleration is included, based on the correction factor [2xD(x)] in Eq. (13).
- For the numerical comparison, the initial time τ₀ = 0.5 fm/c and the initial size R₀ = 0.55 fm were chosen so that σ_y = 1.7 at breakup time τ = 10 fm/c.
- The rapidity width σ_y grew by more than 60% between τ = 0.5 fm/c and 10 fm/c in the numerical solution.
- The analytic Gaussian solution at t=0 has an entropy profile that develops a power-law tail s(x→∞, t=0) ≈ (u/2√(ln u))^{-1/c²} with u ≡ c_s x/(R₀√2).

## Context

This work extends the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant picture (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) by incorporating longitudinal acceleration, which is neglected in the standard Bjorken ansatz where dv/dz = 1/τ. The analytic Gaussian solution provides a correction to lifetime estimates from two-particle correlation measurements of R_long, showing that the Bjorken formula underestimates the breakup time by ~10%, consistent with findings from three-dimensional numerical hydrodynamics by Renk (2005). The results also address part of the "HBT puzzle" by suggesting that breakup times may be 11-12 fm/c rather than the 9 fm/c inferred from boost-invariant blast-wave models (Retiere 2003). The method is limited to irrotational flow, which is approximately valid at RHIC and LHC energies where rotational effects from non-zero impact parameters are expected to be small.
