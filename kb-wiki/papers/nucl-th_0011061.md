# Electromagnetic Dissociation as a Tool for Nuclear Structure and Astrophysics

> Coulomb dissociation is an especially simple and important reaction mechanism. Since the perturbation due to the electric field of the nucleus is exactly known, firm conclusions can be drawn from such measurements. Electromagnetic matrix elements and astrophysical S-factors for radiative capture processes can be extracted from experiments. We describe the basic theory, new results concerning higher order effects in the dissociation of neutron halo nuclei, and briefly review the experimental results obtained up to now. Some new applications of Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics and nuclear structure physics are discussed.

# Electromagnetic Dissociation as a Tool for Nuclear Structure and Astrophysics

## Key claim
Coulomb dissociation can reliably extract electromagnetic matrix elements and astrophysical S-factors for radiative capture processes, provided that first-order electromagnetic excitation dominates; higher-order effects in neutron halo nuclei are found to be "reassuringly small" (e.g., a -10% next-to-leading-order correction for 19C dissociation), though discrepancies with other calculations (which found -35%) remain to be resolved.

## Method
The authors review the theory of Coulomb dissociation using the equivalent photon approximation, where the cross section is expressed as an integral over the photon spectrum n(ω) times the photo-induced cross section σγ(ω). They analyze higher-order effects in a zero-range model for neutron-core interactions, obtaining analytical results for first- and second-order electromagnetic excitation for small adiabaticity parameter ξ, and compare these to a sudden-approximation solution that sums higher orders to all orders. Experimental results from Coulomb dissociation of halo nuclei (11Be, 19C, 11Li) and astrophysically relevant reactions (7Be(p,γ)8B, d(α,γ)6Li) are reviewed.

## Key numbers
- Cross sections for giant dipole resonance excitation at RHIC and LHC (Pb-Pb) are of the order of 100 b for heavy systems (Au-Au or Pb-Pb).
- The neutron separation energy of 19C was determined to be 530 ± 130 keV from Coulomb dissociation.
- The ratio of the next-to-leading-order (NLO) contribution to the leading-order (LO) contribution for Coulomb dissociation of 19C is -10%, compared to a value of about -35% found in Tos99.
- The angle-integrated breakup probability in leading order is dP_LO/dq = (16/3πη) y² x⁴/(1+x²)⁴, where y is the strength parameter and x = q/η.
- The NLO expression is dP_NLO/dq = (16/3πη) y⁴ x²(5-55x²+28x⁴)/[15(1+x²)⁶].

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework of Coulomb excitation (Bertulani 2001) and applies it to neutron halo nuclei and astrophysical radiative capture reactions. It addresses discrepancies with higher-order calculations by Tos99 for 19C dissociation and discusses postacceleration effects, finding them to be small and consistent with the 11Be experiment by Bush. The work enables future studies of two-nucleon capture reactions relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis (Rolfs 2001, Cowan 2001) and rp-process waiting points (Goriely 2002, Schatz 2002), including two-proton capture on nuclei such as 40Ti and 68Se.
