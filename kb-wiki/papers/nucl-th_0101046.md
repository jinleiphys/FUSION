# Variational separable expansion scheme for two-body Coulomb-scattering problems

> We present a separable expansion approximation method for Coulomb-like potentials which is based on Schwinger variational principle and uses Coulomb-Sturmian functions as basis states. The new scheme provides faster convergence with respect to our formerly used non-variational approach.

# Variational separable expansion scheme for two-body Coulomb-scattering problems

## Key claim
The authors present a separable expansion method for Coulomb-like potentials based on the Schwinger variational principle using Coulomb-Sturmian basis states, which converges faster in terms of number of basis states than their previously used non-variational approach, though the new scheme is more complicated numerically so the overall numerical efficiency of both methods is roughly comparable.

## Method
The method generalizes a separable expansion scheme originally proposed by Adhikari and Tomio for short-range potentials to Coulomb-like potentials by splitting the potential into a pure Coulomb part and a short-range part, keeping the Coulomb interaction in the Green's operator. The short-range part of the potential is approximated via a twofold separable expansion using Coulomb-Sturmian functions as basis states with two different scaling parameters b1 and b2, allowing exact analytical calculation of the Coulomb Green's operator matrix elements. The resulting finite-rank approximation of the Coulomb-modified transition operator is then solved from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers
- For p-p scattering with a Malfliet-Tjon short-range potential (v0 = -626.885 MeV, beta0 = 1.55 fm, v1 = 1438.720 MeV, beta1 = 3.11 fm), the variational method (Eq. potapp) needed 10-13 basis states to reach 6-digit accuracy, while the non-variational method (Eq. orig) needed 20-23 states.
- At E = 0.1 MeV, the converged Coulomb-modified nuclear phase shift delta^{sC}_0 was -0.119162 radians for both methods.
- At E = 1 MeV, the converged phase shift was -0.701504 radians for both methods.
- At E = 10 MeV, the converged phase shift was 1.480957 radians for both methods.
- At E = 100 MeV, the converged phase shift was 0.407499 radians for both methods.
- The CS basis parameters used were b = 3 fm^{-1} for the non-variational method, and b1 = 3.8 fm^{-1} and b2 = 2.5 fm^{-1} for the variational method.

## Context
This work combines two separable expansion approaches: the Schwinger variational principle method of Adhikari and Tomio (1991) which was not applied to Coulomb potentials, and the non-variational Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method of Papp (1991) which was designed for Coulomb-like potentials. The new variational scheme converges considerably faster in terms of basis states than the non-variational method, which could be useful in three-body Faddeev calculations where the rank of the expansion is crucial; however, whether the method can be extended to three-body Coulombic systems remains an open question.
