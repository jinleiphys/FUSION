# Fine and hyperfine structure in different bound systems

> We demonstrate that the generalized Gell-Mann-Low theorem permits for a systematic expansion around the nonrelativistic limit when applied to bound states in the Wick-Cutkosky model, Yukawa theory, and QED (in Coulomb gauge). We apply this expansion to obtain new results for the fine and hyperfine structure of bound states in the cases of the Wick-Cutkosky model and Yukawa theory, and reproduce correctly the fine and hyperfine structure of hydrogenic systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the generalized Gell-Mann-Low theorem can be systematically expanded around the nonrelativistic limit to obtain fine and hyperfine structure of bound states, but only for the Wick-Cutkosky model and Yukawa theory does this produce genuinely new results; for QED the method merely reproduces the known hydrogenic structure, and the antihermitian term in the Bloch-Wilson Hamiltonian leads to different fine-structure predictions than the hermitian Okubo Hamiltonian for the scalar and Yukawa cases.

## Method

The author applies the generalized Gell-Mann-Low theorem to construct the Bloch-Wilson Hamiltonian (a nonhermitian effective Hamiltonian) and the Okubo Hamiltonian (its hermitian counterpart) for two-particle bound states in three field theories: the Wick-Cutkosky model (scalar fields with cubic coupling), Yukawa theory (two Dirac fields coupled to a massless scalar), and QED in Coulomb gauge (two Dirac fields with opposite charges). The effective Hamiltonians are expanded in powers of relative momentum around the nonrelativistic limit, and second-order time-independent Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory is used to extract the lowest-order fine and hyperfine structure from the next-to-leading terms.

## Key numbers

* Wick-Cutkosky model: the expansion of the retardation term in the effective potential yields an antihermitian correction of order \((p/m_A)^1\) in Eq. (retard), contributing to the same order as the hermitian second-order term of order \((p/m_A)^2\)
* Yukawa theory: the spin-dependent term in the momentum-space Schrodinger equation contains Pauli matrix contributions \(-(\mathbf{p}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_A)(\mathbf{p}'\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_A)/(4 m_A^2) - (\mathbf{p}\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_B)(\mathbf{p}'\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_B)/(4 m_B^2)\) in Eq. (Yspin)
* QED: the transverse-photon exchange carries additional powers of momentum so that retardation only contributes to higher orders, and the spin-orbit coupling appears as \([(\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{p}')\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_A][(\mathbf{p}-\mathbf{p}')\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}_B]/(4 m_A m_B)\) in Eq. (QEDspin)
* Reduced mass defined as \(m_r = m_A m_B/(m_A + m_B)\) appears in the kinetic expansion for all three theories

## Context

This work extends the Bloch-Wilson Hamiltonian approach previously used for numerical bound-state calculations by Weber and collaborators (WL02, WL05) to analytic fine-structure calculations. It contrasts with the historical Breit-equation approach (Bre29) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation method (Sal52), noting that the ladder approximation in the Wick-Cutkosky model (WC54) produces a "curious" term of order \(\alpha^3 \ln \alpha\) (FH73) that the present method avoids. The results enable systematic analytic calculation of fine and hyperfine structure for scalar and Yukawa bound systems where such structure had not previously been determined, and clarify the role of the antihermitian term in the Bloch-Wilson Hamiltonian versus the hermitian Okubo Hamiltonian.
