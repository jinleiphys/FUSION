# Quark and Gluon Production from a Boost-invariantly Expanding Color Electric Field

> Particle production from an expanding classical color electromagnetic field is extensively studied, motivated by the early stage dynamics of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We develop a formalism at one-loop order to compute the particle spectra by canonically quantizing quark, gluon and ghost fluctuations under the presence of such an expanding classical color background field; the canonical quantization is done in the $τ$-$η$ coordinates in order to take into account manifestly the expanding geometry. As a demonstration, we model the expanding classical color background field by a boost-invariantly expanding homogeneous color electric field with lifetime $T$, for which we obtain analytically the quark and gluon production spectra by solving the equations of motion of QCD non-perturbatively with respect to the color electric field. In this paper we study (i) finite lifetime effect which is found to modify significantly the particle spectra from those expected from the Schwinger formula; (ii) the difference between the quark and gluon production; and (iii) the quark mass dependence of the production spectra. Implications of these results to ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the finite lifetime of a boost-invariantly expanding color electric field significantly modifies quark and gluon production spectra from the static Schwinger formula, and that the production rates for quarks and gluons differ due to their distinct color charges, with the gluon production rate being enhanced relative to quarks by a factor related to the ratio of squared color charges in the adjoint versus fundamental representations.

## Method

The author develops a one-loop formalism for particle production by canonically quantizing quark, gluon, and ghost fluctuations in the presence of a classical color background field, using tau-eta coordinates to manifestly incorporate the boost-invariant expanding geometry. The classical field is modeled as a boost-invariantly expanding homogeneous color electric field with a finite lifetime T, and the equations of motion for QCD fluctuations are solved analytically and non-perturbatively with respect to the color electric field under an Abelian dominance assumption. The Bogoliubov coefficients between creation and annihilation operators at asymptotic times are computed to obtain the particle spectra.

## Key numbers

* The trace of the squared effective color charge for quarks in the fundamental representation is \( \sum_{i=1}^{N_c} |q_i^{(q)}|^2 = g^2/2 \), independent of the color direction and gauge choice.
* The trace of the squared effective color charge for gluons (and ghosts) in the adjoint representation is \( \sum_{A=1}^{N_c(N_c-1)/2} |q_A^{(g)}|^2 = g^2 N_c/2 \), which is \( N_c \) times larger than that for quarks.
* The typical lifetime of the color fields is estimated to be of order \( 1/Q_s \), where \( Q_s \) is the saturation scale of the Color Glass Condensate, with \( gA_\mu \sim Q_s \sim \text{a few GeV} \).
* The formation time of the Quark-Gluon Plasma assumed in hydrodynamical models is extremely short: \( \tau_{\text{form}} \lesssim 1\ \text{fm}/c \).

## Context

This work extends the study of particle production from expanding electromagnetic fields in scalar QED by Tanji (2011) to the case of quark and gluon production in QCD, and generalizes earlier studies of finite lifetime effects in non-expanding electric fields by Brezin and Itzykson (1970) and Taya (2014). The results enable a more realistic treatment of the early stage dynamics of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the color fields have a short lifetime and expanding geometry, and provide a foundation for future studies including backreaction and scattering effects that are necessary for understanding thermalization.
