# Jets as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma

> The suppression and modification of high-energy objects, like jets, in heavy-ion collisions provide an important window to access the degrees of freedom of the quark-gluon plasma on different length scales. Despite increasingly precise and differential measurements of the properties of jets in heavy-ion collisions, however, it has remained challenging to use jets to make unambiguous and model-independent statements about the quark-gluon plasma. Here I will give a personal take on some origins of these challenges, including the difficulty of modelling and biases from jet selection that obfuscate the direct interpretation of jet modification measurements. I will discuss a few model studies that have helped to disentangle the source of non-intuitive effects in measurements, and finally highlight data-driven approaches as an interesting opportunity toward studying the quark-gluon plasma in a model-independent way using jets.

# Jets as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Despite increasingly precise and differential measurements of jet properties in heavy-ion collisions, it has remained challenging to use jets to make unambiguous and model-independent statements about the quark-gluon plasma, due to difficulties in modelling and biases from jet selection that obfuscate the direct interpretation of jet modification measurements.

## Method

This is a mini-review that surveys theoretical frameworks for studying jet modification in heavy-ion collisions, including Soft Collinear Effective Theory extended to medium interactions and Monte Carlo generators (such as Pythia) modified to describe parton interactions with the quark-gluon plasma. The author discusses how jet selection biases, particularly the bias introduced by selecting jets based on their transverse momentum after energy loss, complicate the interpretation of jet modification observables, and reviews model studies that disentangle competing physical effects.

## Key numbers

- The measured jet shape in heavy-ion collisions is narrower at intermediate radii than for jets in proton-proton collisions in the same pT range, but this narrowing can occur even if every individual jet widens due to its interaction with the plasma.
- The jet RAA does not depend on collision energy, despite the larger energy loss anticipated in the hotter plasma produced at higher energies.
- Gluon jets will lose more energy by a factor of their larger color charge compared to quark jets, with deviations from this scaling suggesting the quark-gluon plasma resolves finer scales within the jet.
- The quenching of jets initiated by a b-quark shows no significant difference compared to the quenching of inclusive jets, which also contain gluon-initiated jets.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of jet modification measurements reviewed in Connors (2017) and Busza (2018), and addresses challenges raised by prior studies including Chien (2015) on jet shape narrowing from quark/gluon fraction changes, Rajagopal (2016) and Brewer (2017) showing narrowing can occur even with individual jet widening, and Milhano (2015) on the role of energy loss fluctuations in back-to-back jet asymmetry. The review enables future data-driven approaches to studying the quark-gluon plasma, including methods proposed by Brewer (2018) for correcting selection biases and Metodiev (2018) for extracting quark and gluon fractions without templates.
