# High-density Skyrmion matter and Neutron Stars

> We examine neutron star properties based on a model of dense matter composed of B=1 skyrmions immersed in a mesonic mean field background. The model realizes spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking non-linearly and incorporates scale-breaking of QCD through a dilaton VEV that also affects the mean fields. Quartic self-interactions among the vector mesons are introduced on grounds of naturalness in the corresponding effective field theory. Within a plausible range of the quartic couplings, the model generates neutron star masses and radii that are consistent with a preponderance of observational constraints, including recent ones that point to the existence of relatively massive neutron stars with mass M 1.7 Msun and radius R (12-14) km. If the existence of neutron stars with such dimensions is confirmed, matter at supra-nuclear density is stiffer than extrapolations of most microscopic models suggest.

# High-density Skyrmion matter and Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The model generates neutron star masses and radii consistent with observational constraints, including relatively massive neutron stars with M ~ 1.7 M_sun and R ~ 12-14 km, but only within a plausible range of quartic couplings (0.1 ≤ ξ ≤ 0.3 and 1.0 ≤ χ ≤ 2.0) that are required by naturalness in the effective field theory; without these couplings, the equation of state is too stiff to satisfy laboratory constraints from collective flow data and isospin diffusion studies.

## Method

The authors model dense matter as a fluid of B=1 skyrmions immersed in mesonic mean fields (dilaton σ, isoscalar ω, and isovector ρ), with spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking realized non-linearly and QCD scale-breaking incorporated through a dilaton potential. Quartic self-interactions of the form (ξ/4!)g_ω^4 ω^4 and (χ/4!)g_ρ^4 ρ^4 are added to the Lagrangian on grounds of naturalness, and the mean-field equations are solved for symmetric and neutron-rich matter in the density range n_0 ≤ n ≤ 5n_0, with the equation of state then used to compute neutron star mass-radius curves via the RNS code for both static and rotating configurations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV at saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} (used as a fitting constraint)
- Effective mass M = 600 MeV at saturation density, with bare nucleon mass M_0 = 900 MeV and e^{σ_0} = 2/3
- Quartic coupling range: 0.1 ≤ ξ ≤ 0.3 for the ω field and 1.0 ≤ χ ≤ 2.0 for the ρ field
- Compressibility K = 240 MeV at saturation (chosen value), rising to K ~ 2000 MeV for a 10% increase in baryon density when ξ = 0
- Symmetry energy density dependence: a_2(n) ∝ (n/n_0)^{1.26} for ξ = χ = 0, softening to a_2(n) ∝ (n/n_0)^{0.71} for natural couplings
- Proton fraction in beta-equilibrated matter: 0.045 ≤ x_0 ≤ 0.11 for n/n_0 = [1..5] with non-zero couplings
- Maximum mass from JOM EoS reaches densities larger than 5n_0 (extrapolated regime)
- Rotation frequency ν ~ 600 Hz (period 1.6 ms) used for rotating star models

## Context

This work extends the Ouyed-Butler-Jaikumar (OBJ) skyrmion fluid model (2006) by adding quartic vector meson self-interactions, motivated by the naturalness arguments used in relativistic mean field models such as Müller & Serot (1996) (MS0 EoS). The OBJ model produced a very stiff equation of state that was inconsistent with experimental constraints from collective flow data (Danielewicz et al., 2002) and isospin diffusion studies (Tsang et al., 2004), as well as neutron-skin thickness measurements (Li & Steiner, 2006). The extended JOM model softens the equation of state sufficiently to satisfy these laboratory constraints while remaining consistent with observational bounds from RXJ 1856.5-3754, Vela pulsar glitches, and the EXO 0748-676 redshift measurement (z=0.35). The work enables future studies of skyrmion overlap at densities above 5n_0 and application to nuclei and sub-saturation density phases.
