# Magnetic moments of the hidden-charm pentaquark states

> The magnetic moment of a baryon state is an equally important dynamical observable as its mass, which encodes crucial information of its underlying structure. According to the different color-flavor structure, we have calculated the magnetic moments of the hidden-charm pentaquark states with $J^P={\frac{1}{2}}^{\pm}$, ${\frac{3}{2}}^{\pm}$, ${\frac{5}{2}}^{\pm}$ and ${\frac{7}{2}}^{+}$ in the molecular model, the diquark-triquark model and the diquark-diquark-antiquark model respectively. Although a good description for the pentaquark mass spectrum and decay patterns has been obtained in all the three models, different color-flavor structures lead to different magnetic moments, which can be used to pin down their inner structures and distinguish various models.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of hidden-charm pentaquark states, calculated in the molecular, diquark-diquark-antiquark, and diquark-triquark models, differ significantly across models even though all three models can describe the observed mass spectrum and decay patterns; these differences can therefore be used to distinguish the internal color-flavor structure of the states, provided the nonrelativistic quark model framework is valid.

## Method

The authors constructed color, flavor, spin, and orbital wave functions for hidden-charm pentaquark states with isospin (1/2, 1/2) and quantum numbers J^P = 1/2^±, 3/2^±, 5/2^±, and 7/2^+ in three distinct configurations: the molecular model (a loosely bound state of a heavy meson and a heavy baryon), the diquark-diquark-antiquark model, and the diquark-triquark model. Using the nonrelativistic quark model, they computed the magnetic moments by summing the contributions of the constituent quarks and orbital angular momenta, employing standard constituent quark masses (m_u = m_d = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV, m_c = 1.55 GeV) and diquark masses from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.33 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV, m_c = 1.55 GeV.
- For the molecular state composed of Σ_c and D̅* with J^P = 3/2^+, the magnetic moment is -0.740 μ_p for the [1/2^+ ⊗ 1^-]_{1/2} ⊗ 1^- configuration and 1.041 μ_p for the [1/2^+ ⊗ 1^-]_{3/2} ⊗ 1^- configuration.
- For the Σ_c* D̅* molecular state with J^P = 3/2^+, the three configurations yield magnetic moments of 2.040 μ_p, 1.668 μ_p, and 2.326 μ_p.
- For the molecular state with J^P = 1/2^- and configuration 1/2^+ ⊗ 0^- ⊗ 0^+, the magnetic moment is 1.760 μ_p.
- For the molecular state with J^P = 3/2^- and configuration 3/2^+ ⊗ 1^- ⊗ 0^+, the magnetic moment is 2.219 μ_p.
- Diquark masses used: m_{[qq]} = 0.710 GeV, m_{[cq]} = 1.937 GeV, m_{[cs]} = 2.091 GeV, m_{ {qq} } = 0.909 GeV, m_{ {cq} } = 2.036 GeV, m_{ {cs} } = 2.158 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the hidden-charm pentaquark states P_c(4380) and P_c(4450) discovered by LHCb (Aaij 2015), which have been interpreted in multiple models including the molecular scheme (Chen 2015, Karliner 2015, Huang 2015), the diquark-diquark-antiquark scheme (Maiani 2015, Anisovich 2015), and the diquark-triquark scheme (Lebed 2015, Zhu 2015). While previous studies focused on mass spectra and decay widths, the authors note that those observables alone cannot distinguish the models; the calculated magnetic moments provide a new observable to discriminate between the different color-flavor configurations. The results enable future experimental measurements of pentaquark magnetic moments to test which internal structure is realized in nature.
