# Revisiting an extended-mean-field approach in heavy-ion collisions around the Fermi energy

> Static and dynamical aspects of nuclear systems are described through an extended time-dependent mean-field approach. The foundations of the formalism are presented, with highlights on the estimation of average values and their corresponding dispersions. In contrast to semiclassical transport models, the particular interest of this description lies on its intrinsic quantal character. The reliability of this approach is discussed by means of stopping-sensitive observables analysis in heavy-ion collisions in the range of 20 to 120 MeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper revisits and extends a quantal extended-time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ETDHF) approach for heavy-ion collisions around the Fermi energy, but explicitly states that this work is intended to illustrate the immanent features of the model and not to carry out a quantitative comparison with experimental results. The authors acknowledge that the model currently lacks a fluctuating term of the Langevin type, which is foreseen for future publications.

## Method

The authors solve the ETDHF equation by expanding single-particle wave functions in a basis of squeezed coherent states (CSs) derived from spline wavelets, with the mean-field evolution governed by equations of motion for the first and second moments of the CSs in phase space. The collision term is implemented as a master equation for CS occupation numbers using a test-particles method with a nucleon-nucleon cross section, and the Pauli principle is enforced by suppressing diffusions inside the same phase-space elementary volume of size ℏ³. To estimate dispersions of observables, the authors construct a many-body density matrix from Slater determinants of coherent states (CSSDs) and sample 1,000 CSSDs for the isotropy ratio or 100 CSSDs for the linear momentum transfer calculations.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon and mean square radius for ground-state nuclei show relative errors less than 5% and 10%, respectively, compared to experimental values over a wide range of mass number A.
- For the in-medium NN cross section with ν=0.85, σ_med varies from 27.7 to 25.6 mb at incident energies between 17 and 115 MeV/nucleon; for ν=0.6 it varies from 19.55 to 19.15 mb; for ν=0.4 it goes from 13.03 to 13.01 mb (all at ρ=0.17 fm⁻³).
- The typical time step in the dynamical calculation is 1 fm/c.
- The collision rate for Au+Au at 100 MeV/nucleon and b=7 fm is shown as a function of center-of-mass energy √s, integrated over 140 fm/c.
- For the ¹²⁹Xe+¹²⁰Sn collision at 50 MeV/nucleon and b=3 fm, the isotropy ratio E_iso is shown with dispersion over 1,000 CSSDs.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier DYWAN (dynamical wavelets in nuclei) model from 1998, which used biorthogonal spline bases to span single-particle wave functions. The approach contrasts with semiclassical transport models such as IQMD, AMD, SMF, UrQMD, LV, and BUU, which have been used to study stopping observables in heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables future inclusion of a fluctuating term of the Langevin type to account for density fluctuations needed to describe the manifold of exit channels observed experimentally, and it provides a framework for estimating dispersions of observables around their mean values in a quantal many-body description.
