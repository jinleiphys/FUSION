# A Framework for Systematic Study of QCD Vacuum Structure II: Coherent Lattice QCD

> We propose the formulation of lattice QCD wherein all elements of the theory (gauge action, fermionic action, theta-term, and all operators) are constructed from a single object, namely the lattice Dirac operator D with exact chiral symmetry. Several regularizations of this type are suggested via constructing scalar densities (gauge actions) that are explicit functions of D. The simplest of these is based on the proposition that classical limit of density associated with Tr D is (up to an additive constant) proportional to FF, while the corresponding operator is local. The possibilities of explicit interrelations between gauge and fermionic aspects of the theory are emphasized together with the utility of such formulations for exploring the QCD vacuum structure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that lattice QCD can be formulated entirely from a single object, the lattice Dirac operator D with exact chiral symmetry, such that the gauge action, fermionic action, theta-term, and all operators are explicit functions of D. The hedge is that this construction relies on conjectures about the classical limit and locality of the resulting gauge action, which have not been rigorously proved even for simpler cases such as the overlap Dirac operator.

## Method

The author constructs coherent lattice QCD by replacing the standard gauge action with a function of the lattice Dirac operator D, specifically using the trace of D (or functions of D such as ln(D+m0)) as the gauge action density. The simplest version uses Tr(D(U) - D(identity)) as the gauge action, with the coupling constants barbeta and bartheta chosen so that the classical limit reproduces the continuum gauge action and theta-term. The full theory is defined by the path integral weight that includes both the gauge part and the fermionic part, both expressed in terms of the same Dirac operator D.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar constant c^P(rho) for standard overlap Dirac operators based on Wilson-Dirac operator with mass -rho is given by c^P(rho)/rho = 1/(8 pi^2) for 0 < rho < 2
- The gauge coupling barbeta is related to the standard beta by barbeta = beta/(12 c^S), where beta = 6/g^2
- The theta parameter bartheta is related to the continuum theta by bartheta = theta/(16 pi^2 c^P), with theta in (-pi, pi]
- For the pseudoscalar constant of Tr(gamma5 f(D)) with an overlap operator at rho=1, c^P(f(D)) = (f(2)-f(0))/2 * c^P(D)

## Context

This paper extends the author's previous work (Horvath 2006) on a systematic framework for studying QCD vacuum structure, specifically the "Bottom-Up" approach using lattice QCD. It proposes explicit actions that realize the chiral ordering transformations suggested in that earlier work, where gauge configurations are replaced by effective SU(3) phases from fermion hopping. The formulation enables a natural definition of effective lattice QCD at a fermionic response scale Lambda_F, which can be written down and directly simulated, unlike the generic case. The work also relates to the use of Ginsparg-Wilson fermions and overlap operators (Neuberger 1998) for exact chiral symmetry on the lattice.
