# Steady electric currents in magnetized QCD and their use for the equation of state

> In this paper we study the emergence of steady electric currents in QCD as a response to a non-uniform magnetic background using lattice simulations with 2 + 1 quark flavors at the physical point, as well as leading-order chiral perturbation theory. Using these currents, we develop a novel method to determine the leading-order coefficient of the equation of state in a magnetic field expansion: the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD medium. We decompose the current expectation value into valence- and sea-quark contributions and demonstrate that the dominant contribution to the electric current is captured by the valence term alone, allowing for a comparably cheap determination of the susceptibility. Our continuum extrapolated lattice results for the equation of state confirm the findings of some of the existing studies in the literature, namely that the QCD medium behaves diamagnetically at low and paramagnetically at high temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that steady electric currents emerge in QCD as a response to a non-uniform magnetic background, and these currents can be used to determine the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD medium; the key hedge is that the method relies on a weak-field expansion and extrapolation to zero magnetic field, and the valence approximation is shown to be dominant only empirically for the range of temperatures and field strengths studied.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of stout-improved rooted staggered quarks at physical masses, using four lattice spacings for continuum extrapolation, with a spatially inhomogeneous magnetic field profile of the form B(x) = B cosh^{-2}(x/epsilon). They computed the electric current density induced by this non-uniform field and extracted the magnetic susceptibility chi_m by integrating the current and the magnetic field gradient using Ampere's law, then extrapolating to zero magnetic field with a quadratic ansatz and subtracting the zero-temperature value to remove logarithmic divergences.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic susceptibility chi_m changes sign across the QCD crossover temperature T_c, with the medium behaving diamagnetically below T_c and paramagnetically above T_c (no explicit numerical values for chi_m given in the truncated text).
* The magnetic field profile width was fixed to epsilon approximately 0.6 fm, compatible with model simulations of off-central heavy-ion collisions.
* Temperatures studied range from 113 MeV to 300 MeV across multiple lattice volumes (24^3 x 6, 24^3 x 8, 28^3 x 10, 36^3 x 12).
* The valence approximation was validated by comparing full and valence currents across a broad range of temperatures and up to rather strong magnetic fields (no explicit numerical comparison given in truncated text).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of magnetic susceptibility by Levkova (2013), Bali (2013, 2014, 2020), Bonati (2013), and Buividovich (2021), confirming the finding of Bali (2020) that the QCD medium transitions from diamagnetic to paramagnetic behavior across the crossover. The new method using inhomogeneous fields and Ampere's law offers a computationally cheaper alternative to existing techniques, particularly because the valence approximation requires only B=0 gauge configurations. The approach enables future calculations of magnetic susceptibility using different fermion discretizations beyond the staggered formulation, and the results are relevant for understanding magnetized neutron stars, heavy-ion collisions, and the early universe.
