# Direct mass measurements of Cd isotopes show strong shell gap at N=82

> A $^{238}$U projectile beam was used to create cadmium isotopes via abrasion-fission at 410 MeV/u in a beryllium target at the entrance of the in-flight separator FRS at GSI. The fission fragments were separated with the FRS and injected into the isochronous storage ring ESR for mass measurements. The Isochronous Mass Spectrometry (IMS) was performed under two different experimental conditions, with and without B$ρ$-tagging at the dispersive central focal plane of the FRS. In the experiment with B$ρ$-tagging the magnetic rigidity of the injected fragments was determined by an accuracy of $2\times 10^{-4}$. A new method of data analysis, using a correlation matrix for the combined data set from both experiments, has provided mass values for 25 different isotopes for the first time. The high selectivity and sensitivity of the experiment and analysis has given access even to rare isotopes detected with a few atoms per week. In this letter we present for the $^{129,130,131}$Cd isotopes mass values directly measured for the first time. The Cd results clearly show a very pronounced shell effect at $N=82$ which is in agreement with the conclusion from $γ$-ray spectroscopy of $^{130}$Cd and confirms the assumptions of modern shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The direct mass measurements of 129,130,131Cd show a strong shell effect at N=82 for cadmium isotopes, with a shell gap magnitude within experimental uncertainties equal to that of tin isotopes at 132Sn, contradicting earlier claims of shell quenching for Z≈40 at N=82.

## Method

A 410 MeV/u 238U beam impinged on a beryllium target at the FRS fragment separator at GSI, producing neutron-rich cadmium isotopes via abrasion-fission. The fission fragments were separated in flight using pure magnetic rigidity (Bρ) selection and injected into the isochronous storage ring ESR for Isochronous Mass Spectrometry (IMS) measurements under two conditions: with and without Bρ-tagging at the dispersive central focal plane of the FRS. A new correlation-matrix analysis method combined data from both experiments, providing mass values even for isotopes detected with only a few atoms per week.

## Key numbers

* Mass excess of 129Cd: -63145 keV with a total error of 173 keV (statistical 17 keV, systematic 172 keV), based on 18 counts.
* Mass excess of 130Cd: -62131 keV with a total error of 177 keV (statistical 41 keV, systematic 172 keV), based on 5 counts; this value is 600 keV lower than the value deduced from Qβ measurements by Dillmann (2003).
* Mass excess of 131Cd: -55583 keV with a total error of 941 keV (statistical 925 keV, systematic 172 keV), based on 2 counts.
* Systematic error of the measurement: 172 keV (standard deviation), determined from reanalysis of 47 reference masses.
* Bρ-tagging accuracy: 2×10−4, achieved using mechanical slits with ±0.5 mm opening at the dispersive central focal plane of the FRS.
* Extra binding energy at N=82 for cadmium isotopes: approximately -14 MeV relative to the liquid-drop model.

## Context

This work extends direct mass measurements to the neutron-rich cadmium isotopes 129,130,131Cd for the first time, providing experimental evidence that contradicts earlier claims of shell quenching at N=82 for Z≈40 based on Qβ measurements by Dillmann (2003) and low 2+ excitation energies in 128Cd reported by Kautzsch, Walters, et al. (2000). The results agree with conclusions from γ-ray spectroscopy of 130Cd by Jungclaus (2007) and support modern shell-model calculations. The new data enable improved r-process nucleosynthesis calculations and motivate future IMS experiments at next-generation facilities (FAIR, RIBF, FRIB, EURISOL) to measure isotones with Z≤42, where theoretical models predict substantial differences in the shell gap evolution.
