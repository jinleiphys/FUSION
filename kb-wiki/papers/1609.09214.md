# New dynamic critical phenomena in nuclear and quark superfluids

> We study the dynamic critical phenomena near the possible high-density QCD critical point inside the superfluid phase of nuclear and quark matter. We find that this critical point belongs to a new dynamic universality class beyond the conventional classification by Hohenberg and Halperin. We show that the speed of the superfluid phonon vanishes at the critical point and that the dynamic critical index is $z \approx 2$.

# New dynamic critical phenomena in nuclear and quark superfluids

## Key claim
The high-density QCD critical point inside the superfluid phase belongs to a new dynamic universality class beyond the conventional Hohenberg-Halperin classification, with the dynamic critical index z ≈ 2, but only under the condition that the critical point lies within a phase where U(1) baryon number symmetry is spontaneously broken (superfluid phase), not in phases where this symmetry remains unbroken.

## Method
The authors constructed a Ginzburg-Landau potential and Langevin equations for the hydrodynamic variables near the high-density QCD critical point, including the superfluid phonon as an additional hydrodynamic variable associated with spontaneous U(1) baryon number symmetry breaking. They solved the linearized Langevin equations in momentum space to obtain the hydrodynamic modes and their dispersion relations, then extracted the dynamic critical exponent from the scaling of the phonon speed with correlation length.

## Key numbers
- Dynamic critical index: z = 2 - η/2, where η ≈ 0.04, giving z ≈ 2
- The baryon number susceptibility scales as χ_B ∼ ξ^(2-η) with η ≈ 0.04
- The phonon speed vanishes as c_s ∼ ξ^(-1+η/2) near the critical point
- The correlation length diverges as ξ ∼ Δ^(-1/2) where Δ = AC - B^2

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD critical phenomena beyond the high-temperature critical point, which was previously classified as model H in the Hohenberg-Halperin scheme by Son (2004) and others. The high-density critical point was proposed earlier by Hatsuda (2006), Abuki (2010), and Schmitt (2010) between the hadron phase and the color-flavor locking phase. The new dynamic universality class arises from the interplay between chiral criticality and the superfluid phonon, a feature absent in previous classifications. This prediction could be tested in beam energy scan programs at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC.
