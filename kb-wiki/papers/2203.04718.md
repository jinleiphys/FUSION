# Snowmass White Paper: New ideas for many-body quantum systems from string theory and black holes

> During the last two decades many new insights into the dynamics of strongly coupled quantum many-body systems have been obtained using gauge/gravity duality, with black holes often playing a universal role. In this white paper we summarize the results obtained and offer some outlook for future developments, including the ongoing mutually beneficial feedback loop with the study of more general, not necessarily holographic, quantum many-body systems.

## Key claim

This white paper summarizes how gauge/gravity duality has provided new insights into strongly coupled quantum many-body systems over the past two decades, with black holes playing a universal role in characterizing dynamics at finite temperature and density. The authors emphasize an ongoing mutually beneficial feedback loop between holographic and non-holographic studies of quantum many-body systems, but note that many of the most striking results (such as the chaos bound and Planckian transport bounds) remain conjectural or have been demonstrated only in specific models.

## Method

The paper reviews results obtained using gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT correspondence) to study strongly coupled quantum many-body systems, focusing on hydrodynamic transport, holographic superconductors and strange metals, quantum chaos and scrambling, spectral statistics, quantum information theory, the SYK model, and the quark-gluon plasma. It synthesizes findings from gravitational computations of black hole dynamics, out-of-time ordered correlation functions, spectral form factors, tensor network models, and solvable large N theories, and discusses open questions and future directions for each area.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta = (hbar / (4 pi k_B)) s, derived for all holographic models with Einstein gravity duals (Kovtun, Son, Starinets 2004)
- Chaos bound: lambda <= 2 pi k_B T / hbar, discovered by Maldacena, Shenker, and Stanford (2015)
- Planckian bound conjecture: 1/tau <= 1/tau_Pl ~ k_B T / hbar, proposed as a possible bound on many-body timescales
- Diffusion constant relation: D ~ v_B^2 / lambda, linking diffusion constants to butterfly velocity and Lyapunov exponent

## Context

This white paper extends and synthesizes two decades of work on gauge/gravity duality applied to many-body physics, building on foundational results such as the Maldacena duality (1997), the Ryu-Takayanagi conjecture for holographic entanglement entropy, and the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev model. It connects holographic insights to experimental observations in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, cold atoms (Cao 2010), charge-neutral graphene (Muller 2009, Crossno 2016, Ku 2019), and high-temperature superconductors. The paper enables future work on unifying chaos bounds with transport bounds, developing effective field theories of scrambling, constructing improved holographic toy models beyond SYK, and realizing traversable wormholes in table-top experiments.
