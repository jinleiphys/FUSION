# Van der Waals Excluded Volume Model of Multicomponent Hadron Gas

> A generalization of the Van der Waals excluded volume procedure for the multicomponent hadron gas is proposed. The derivation is based on the grand canonical partition function for the system of particles of several species interacting by hard core potentials. The obtained formulae for thermodynamical quantities are consistent with underlying principles of statistical mechanics as well as with thermodynamical identities. The model can be applied to the analysis of experimental data for particle number ratios in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Van der Waals Excluded Volume Model of Multicomponent Hadron Gas

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of the Van der Waals excluded volume procedure to a multicomponent hadron gas where different hadron species have different hard-core radii, showing that the simple substitution of total volume by volume minus the sum of proper volumes of all particles is incorrect when radii are unequal; instead, the excluded volume seen by each species depends on the radii of both the particle and its neighbors.

## Method

The authors start from the grand canonical partition function for a system of particles of several species interacting via hard-core potentials, using the Mayer function expansion in the rarefied gas approximation. They derive the canonical partition function for the multicomponent case, then obtain the pressure and particle number densities as solutions of a set of coupled transcendental equations. The key innovation is the introduction of species-dependent excluded volume parameters $\tilde{b}_{pq}$ that satisfy the constraint $\tilde{b}_{12} + \tilde{b}_{21} = 2 b_{12}$ and reduce to the correct limits when radii are equal or when one radius vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The excluded volume parameter for two particles of the same species is $b_{pp} = \frac{16}{3} \pi R_p^3$, where $R_p$ is the hard-core radius of that species.
- For two particles of different species, the excluded volume parameter is $b_{pq} = \frac{2}{3} \pi (R_p + R_q)^3$.
- The effective excluded volume parameters are defined as $\tilde{b}_{12} = 2 b_{11} b_{12} / (b_{11} + b_{22})$ and $\tilde{b}_{21} = 2 b_{22} b_{12} / (b_{11} + b_{22})$.
- The pressure in the grand canonical ensemble is given by $p = T \sum_{p=1}^{K} \xi_p$, where $\xi_p$ satisfy $\xi_p = A_p \exp(-\sum_{q=1}^{K} \tilde{b}_{pq} \xi_q)$.
- Particle number densities are obtained from $n_p = \xi_p / (1 + \sum_{q=1}^{K} n_q \tilde{b}_{qp})$.

## Context

This work extends the Van der Waals excluded volume approach previously applied to hadron gas models by Cleymans, Redlich, Satz, and Suhonen (1993-1994) and by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, Wessels, and Xu (1995), which used the simple substitution $V \rightarrow V - \sum_i b_{ii} N_i$ for all species. The paper shows this earlier ansatz is incorrect for unequal hard-core radii. The model enables more accurate fitting of particle number ratios in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS and SPS energies, where ideal hadron gas models produce artificially large particle and energy densities as noted by Yen, Gorenstein, Stocker, Yang, and Greiner (1998).
