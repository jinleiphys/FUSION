# Chiral dynamics and the reactions pp \to d K^+ \bar{K}^0 and pp \to d π^+ η

> We perform a study of the final state interactions of the K^+ \bar{K^0} and the \bar{K^0}d systems in the reactions pp \to d K^+ \bar{K^0} and pp \to d π^+ η. Since the two-meson system couples strongly to the a_0(980) resonance, these reactions are expected to be an additional source of information about the controversial scalar sector. We also show that these reactions present peculiar features which can shed additional light on the much debated meson-baryon scalar sector with strangeness -1. We deduce the general structure of the amplitudes close to the d K^+\bar{K}^0 threshold, allowing for primary K^+ \bar{K^0} as well as π^+ηproduction with the two mesons in relative S- or P-wave. The interactions of the mesons are accounted for by using chiral unitary techniques, which generate dynamically the a_0(980) resonance, and the \bar{K^0}d interaction is also taken into account. General formulae are derived that allow to incorporate the final state interactions in these systems for any model of the production mechanism. We illustrate this approach by considering two specific production mechanisms based on three flavor meson-baryon chiral perturbation theory. It is demonstrated that in this scenario the \bar{K^0}d interactions are very important and can change the cross section by as much as one order of magnitude. The amount of π^+ηversus K^+ \bar{K^0} production is shown to depend critically on the primary mixture of the two mechanisms, with large interference effects due to final state interactions. These effects are also shown to occur in the event distributions of invariant masses which are drastically modified by the final state interactions of the two-meson or the \bar{K}d system.

## Key claim

The reactions \(pp \to d K^+ \bar{K}^0\) and \(pp \to d \pi^+ \eta\) can provide novel information about the controversial scalar meson sector (specifically the \(a_0(980)\) resonance) and the meson-baryon scalar sector with strangeness \(-1\) (the \(\Lambda(1405)\)), but only if the final state interactions of both the two-meson system and the \(\bar{K}^0 d\) system are properly included, as these interactions can change the cross section by as much as one order of magnitude.

## Method

The authors derived the general structure of the production amplitudes close to the \(d K^+ \bar{K}^0\) threshold, allowing for primary \(K^+ \bar{K}^0\) or \(\pi^+ \eta\) production with the two mesons in relative S-wave or P-wave. They accounted for meson-meson final state interactions using chiral unitary techniques (Bethe-Salpeter equation with lowest-order chiral perturbation theory kernels) which dynamically generate the \(a_0(980)\) resonance, and included the \(\bar{K}^0 d\) final state interactions using a parameterization of the \(\bar{K} d\) scattering amplitude derived from chiral dynamics. Two specific production mechanisms based on three-flavor meson-baryon chiral perturbation theory were used to illustrate the approach.

## Key numbers

- The ANKE experiment energy is fixed at \(\sqrt{s} = 2912.88\) MeV, about 45 MeV above the \(d K^+ \bar{K}^0\) threshold.
- The \(\bar{K} d\) scattering length obtained from chiral dynamics is approximately \((-1.6 + i 1.9)\) fm.
- The \(\bar{K} d\) scattering amplitude parameterization uses a threshold invariant mass \(\widetilde{M}_{B0} = 1432\) MeV, with coefficients \(a = 4.32 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-3}\), \(b = -1.55 \times 10^{-2}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(c = 0.13\) MeV\(^{-1}\) for the real part, and \(b' = 1.1 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(c' = -1.5 \times 10^{-1}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) for the imaginary part.
- The \(\bar{K}^0 d\) interactions can change the cross section by as much as one order of magnitude.
- The deuteron mass is taken as \(M_d = 1875.61\) MeV and the average kaon mass as \(m_K = 495.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to meson-meson scattering developed by the authors and collaborators (Oset et al., 1998; Oller et al., 1999) and the meson-baryon chiral dynamics that dynamically generates the \(\Lambda(1405)\) (Weise et al., 1995; Oller et al., 2000) to the production reactions \(pp \to d K^+ \bar{K}^0\) and \(pp \to d \pi^+ \eta\). It is motivated by the ANKE experiment at COSY and prior theoretical work by Cassing et al. (2000) that used direct \(a_0(980)\) production models. The paper enables future experimental comparisons with ANKE data to test whether the \(a_0(980)\) is a dynamically generated meson-meson resonance or a preexisting quark-model state, and whether the \(\Lambda(1405)\) is of dynamical origin.
