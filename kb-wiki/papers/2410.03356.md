# Mean-field approximation on steroids: exact description of the deuteron

> The present article demonstrates that the deuteron, i.e. the lightest bound nuclear system made of a single proton and a single neutron, can be accurately described within a mean-field-based framework. Although paradoxical at first glance, the deuteron ground-state binding energy, magnetic dipole moment, electric quadrupole moment and root-mean-square proton radius are indeed reproduced with sub-percent accuracy via a low-dimensional linear combination of non-orthogonal Bogoliubov states, i.e. with a method whose numerical cost scales as $n_{\text{dim}}^4$, where $n_{\text{dim}}$ is the dimension of the basis of the one-body Hilbert space. By further putting the system into a harmonic trap, the neutron-proton scattering length and effective range in the ${}^{3}S_1$ channel are also accurately reproduced. To achieve this task, (i) the inclusion of proton-neutron pairing through the mixing of proton and neutron single-particle states in the Bogoliubov transformation and (ii) the restoration of proton and neutron numbers before variation are shown to be mandatory ingredients. This unexpected result has implications regarding the most efficient way to capture necessary correlations as a function of nuclear mass and regarding the possibility to ensure order-by-order renormalizability of many-body calculations based on chiral or pionless effective field theories beyond light nuclei. In this context, the present study will be extended to $^{3}$H and $^{3,4}$He in the near future as well as to the leading order of pionless effective field theory.

## Key claim

The deuteron's ground-state properties can be reproduced with sub-percent accuracy using a mean-field-based framework, contradicting the common expectation that the mean-field approximation is poorly suited for very light nuclear systems; this success requires both the inclusion of proton-neutron pairing through mixing of single-particle states in the Bogoliubov transformation and the restoration of proton and neutron numbers before variation.

## Method

The authors employed a hierarchy of variational methods based on symmetry-broken and symmetry-restored mean-field states, ranging from spherical Hartree-Fock (sHF) through deformed Hartree-Fock (dHF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with proton-neutron mixing (d\(np\)HFB) to variation-after-projection on particle number (d\(np\)VAPNP). The most sophisticated approach, termed "mean field on steroids" (MFS), combines d\(np\)VAPNP with subsequent projection onto good angular momentum and parity (d\(np\)VAPNP+PAV). Calculations used the SRG-evolved EM500 chiral interaction (flow parameter 1.8 fm\(^{-1}\)) expanded over a spherical harmonic oscillator basis characterized by oscillator spacing \(\hbar\omega\) and maximum energy quanta \(e_{\text{max}}\).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: MFS result \(-2.2225(12)\) MeV versus reference \(-2.2246\) MeV, relative error +0.09%
- Electric quadrupole moment: MFS result +0.2643 \(e\,\text{fm}^2\) versus reference +0.2663 \(e\,\text{fm}^2\), relative error \(-0.75\%\)
- Magnetic dipole moment: MFS result +0.8649 \(\mu_N\) versus reference +0.8651 \(\mu_N\), relative error \(-0.02\%\)
- Point-proton root-mean-square radius: MFS result 1.975 fm versus reference 1.982 fm, relative error \(-0.35\%\)
- Neutron-proton scattering length in \({}^{3}S_1\) channel: MFS result 5.44(3) fm versus reference 5.417 fm, relative error +0.42%
- Effective range in \({}^{3}S_1\) channel: MFS result 1.71(5) fm versus reference 1.752 fm, relative error \(-2.40\%\)

## Context

This work extends the investigation of how many-body correlations split between a zeroth-order mean-field state and dynamical corrections, previously studied by Frosini et al. (2022) for the \(A=16\)–\(20\) mass region using PGCM-PT, to the lightest bound nuclear system. The results challenge the conventional belief that the mean-field approximation is poorly justified for very light nuclei, and the authors note that the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approximation with neutron-proton pairing (Baldo et al., 1995) already hinted at this possibility in the zero-density limit of symmetric nuclear matter. The study will be extended to \({}^{3}\)H and \({}^{3,4}\)He in the near future, as well as to the leading order of pionless effective field theory to investigate whether such boosted mean-field states can satisfy renormalization invariance.
