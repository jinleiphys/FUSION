# Quark Delocalization, Color Screening, and Nuclear Intermediate Range Attraction

> We consider the effect of including quark delocalization and color screening, in the nonrelativistic quark cluster model, on baryon-baryon potentials and phase shifts. We find that the inclusion of these additional effects allows a good qualitative description of both.

## Key claim

Including both quark delocalization and color screening in a nonrelativistic quark cluster model can produce a good qualitative description of baryon-baryon potentials and phase shifts, including the intermediate range attraction that previous quark model calculations had failed to obtain.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic quark cluster model with a Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, confining potential (harmonic oscillator form), and one-gluon exchange terms. Quark delocalization was introduced by allowing single-quark orbital wavefunctions to be linear combinations of left and right cluster orbitals with a variational parameter epsilon(R). Color screening was implemented by modifying the inter-cluster confinement interaction with a Gaussian screening factor exp(-mu r^2). The authors calculated adiabatic baryon-baryon potentials and then used the Canto-Brink variational method (equivalent to the resonating group method) to compute phase shifts, varying the screening parameter mu to best fit the NN 1S0 phase shift.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: m = 313 MeV (set to 1/3 of nucleon mass)
- Quark cluster size parameter: b = 0.603 fm
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_S = 1.54
- Confinement strength: a = 25.13 MeV/fm^2
- Optimum color screening constant: mu = 0.46 fm^(-2)
- Quark delocalization parameter epsilon at R = 0.375 fm: approximately 1.0 for both NN 1S0 and NN 3S1 channels
- Quark delocalization parameter epsilon at R = 1.5 fm: 0.123 for NN 1S0, 0.155 for NN 3S1
- Quark delocalization parameter epsilon at R = 3.0 fm: 0.005 for NN 1S0, 0.005 for NN 3S1

## Context

This work extends earlier quark cluster model calculations (Isgur and Karl, 1979) that could only produce a repulsive core in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, by adding quark delocalization (suggested by Goldman et al., 1988) and color screening motivated by lattice gauge calculations (Svetitsky, 1986). The model provides a quark-level description of nuclear intermediate range attraction that may serve as an alternative to meson exchange models (Machleidt, Holinde and Elster, 1987), and enables future studies of dibaryon resonances, the H-particle, and the deuteron bound state.
