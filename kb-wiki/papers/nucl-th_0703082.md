# Boost-invariant mean field approximation and the nuclear Landau-Zener effect

> We investigate the relation between time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) states and the adiabatic eigenstates by constructing a boost-invariant single-particle Hamiltonian. The method is numerically realized within a full three-dimensional TDHF which includes all the terms of the Skyrme energy functional and without any symmetry restrictions. The study of a free translational motion of a nucleus demonstrates the validity of the concept of boost-invariant and adiabatic TDHF states. The interpretation is further corroborated by the test case of fusion of $^{16}{\textrm O}$+$^{16}{\textrm O}$. As a first application, we present a study of the nuclear Landau-Zener effect on a collision of $^{4}{\textrm {He}}$+$^{16}{\textrm O}$.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a boost-invariant single-particle Hamiltonian to extract meaningful single-particle energies and adiabatic states from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations, enabling the first self-consistent mean-field study of the nuclear Landau-Zener effect in heavy-ion collisions. The method works exactly for free translational motion and approximately for collisions, where the boost-invariant states acquire small variances that serve as a measure of intrinsic excitation.

## Method

The authors define a boost-invariant Hamiltonian by replacing the kinetic energy density with the Galilean-invariant combination τ − j²/ρ, which removes trivial flow contributions from the single-particle energies. They solve the full three-dimensional TDHF equations with the Skyrme SLy6 force on a 24×24×24 Cartesian grid with 1 fm spacing, including all terms of the Skyrme energy functional and no symmetry restrictions. For each time step, they diagonalize the boost-invariant Hamiltonian to obtain adiabatic single-particle energies and wave functions, and compute adiabatic occupation probabilities to quantify intrinsic excitation.

## Key numbers

- For free translation of ¹⁶O, the boost-invariant Hamiltonian yields variances of about 0.02–0.05 MeV and expectation values within 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ MeV of the static single-particle energies.
- The central ¹⁶O+¹⁶O collision was studied at a center-of-mass energy of 25 MeV, with the nuclei initially placed 5 fm off the box center.
- The ⁴He+¹⁶O collisions were studied at center-of-mass energies of 75 MeV and 125 MeV, both resulting in deep-inelastic scattering.
- The time step used for dynamical propagation was 0.2 fm/c, with a Taylor series expansion of the unitary mean-field propagator up to sixth order.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to define adiabatic states from TDHF using density-constrained Hartree-Fock (Cusson 1985, Dendooven 1985), which lacked exact treatment of flow contributions. The Landau-Zener effect was previously studied in nuclear physics using the asymmetric two-center shell model (Maruhn 1972) and applied to heavy-ion collisions (Park 1980, Park 1982, Cusson 1985). The boost-invariant Hamiltonian enables the first self-consistent mean-field analysis of level crossings and Landau-Zener transitions in TDHF, providing a tool to connect TDHF simulations to adiabatic basis expansions and cranking models.
