# Nuclear Charge Radii of the Nickel Isotopes $^{58-68,70}$Ni

> Collinear laser spectroscopy is performed on the nickel isotopes $^{58-68,70}$Ni, using a time-resolved photon counting system. From the measured isotope shifts, nuclear charge radii $R_c$ are extracted and compared to theoretical results. Three ab initio approaches all employ, among others, the chiral interaction NNLO$_{\rm sat}$, which allows an assessment of their accuracy. We find agreement with experiment in differential radii $δ\left\langle r_\mathrm{c}^2 \right\rangle$ for all employed ab initio methods and interactions, while the absolute radii are consistent with data only for NNLO$_{\rm sat}$. Within nuclear density functional theory, the Skyrme functional SV-min matches experiment more closely than the Fayans functional Fy($Δr$,HFB).

## Key claim
Collinear laser spectroscopy on nickel isotopes $^{58-68,70}$Ni yields charge radii that agree with three ab initio methods for differential radii regardless of the chiral interaction used, but absolute radii are consistent with experiment only when using the NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction, not with the 1.8/2.0(EM) or $NN$+$3N$(lnl) interactions.

## Method
Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on bunched ion beams of nickel isotopes produced at ISOLDE/CERN, using a frequency-doubled continuous-wave titanium-sapphire laser to excite the $3d^9\,4s\,^{3}\!D_3 \rightarrow 3d^9\,4p\,^{3}\!P_2$ transition at 352.45 nm. A time-resolved photon counting system (TILDA) recorded fluorescence photons with time stamps relative to the RFQ extraction trigger, and isotope shifts were extracted from the measured resonance spectra relative to the reference isotope $^{60}$Ni. Changes in mean-square nuclear charge radii were obtained using the field-shift factor $F=-783(94)$ MHz/fm$^2$ and mass-shift factor $M_{\alpha=396}=950(5)$ GHz u, and compared to three ab initio methods (self-consistent Green's function, valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group, and coupled-cluster) as well as two nuclear density functional theory approaches (Skyrme functional SV-min and Fayans functional Fy($\Delta r$, HFB)).

## Key numbers
- Absolute charge radius of $^{60}$Ni: $R_c = 3.806(2)$ fm (used as reference)
- Absolute charge radius of $^{70}$Ni: $R_c = 3.910(3)$ fm
- Change in mean-square charge radius from $^{60}$Ni to $^{70}$Ni: $\delta\langle r_c^2\rangle^{60,70} = 0.806(24)$ fm$^2$
- Change in mean-square charge radius from $^{60}$Ni to $^{58}$Ni: $\delta\langle r_c^2\rangle^{60,58} = -0.275(8)$ fm$^2$
- Field-shift factor: $F = -783(94)$ MHz/fm$^2$
- Mass-shift factor: $M_{\alpha=396} = 950(5)$ GHz u
- Coupled-cluster uncertainty on $R_c$ estimated as +2%/-1%

## Context
This work provides the first complete charge radius measurements across the nickel isotopic chain ($Z=28$), which was the last unexplored magic isotopic chain in this mass region for charge radii. The experimental data extend previous work on $^{68}$Ni by Kaufmann (2020) and are compared to three independent ab initio methods (SCGF, VS-IMSRG, coupled-cluster) using chiral EFT interactions, as well as DFT with the SV-min and Fy($\Delta r$, HFB) functionals. The results enable stringent benchmarking of theoretical approaches: the Skyrme functional SV-min matches experiment more closely than the Fayans functional Fy($\Delta r$, HFB), while among ab initio methods only NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ reproduces absolute radii, extending similar comparisons previously made for calcium (Garcia Ruiz, 2016; Miller, 2019) and copper (de Groote, 2020) isotopes.
