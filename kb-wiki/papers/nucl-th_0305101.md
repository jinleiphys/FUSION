# On baryon resonances and chiral symmetry

> We study J^P=(3/2)^- baryon resonances as generated by chiral coupled-channel dynamics. Parameter free results are obtained in terms of the Weinberg-Tomozawa term predicting the leading s-wave interaction strength of Goldstone bosons with baryon-decuplet states. In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with m_π= m_K \sim 500 MeV the resonances turn into bound states forming a decuplet and octet representation of the SU(3) group. Using physical masses the mass splitting are remarkably close to the empirical pattern.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, which predicts the leading s-wave interaction strength of Goldstone bosons with baryon-decuplet states, can dynamically generate \(J^P=(3/2)^-\) baryon resonances in a parameter-free manner, but only under the assumption that the dominant coupling is to s-wave meson baryon-decuplet channels; the predicted bound states in the 'heavy' SU(3) limit disappear once the current quark masses are sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors use the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach, a chiral coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter framework, to compute meson-baryon scattering amplitudes for the decuplet sector. The interaction kernel is derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved after decomposing the amplitudes into covariant projectors with definite total angular momentum \(J\) and parity \(P\). The resulting amplitudes are analyzed via speed plots to identify resonance structures across various isospin and strangeness channels.

## Key numbers

* In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with \(m_\pi = m_K \sim 500\) MeV and baryon-octet mass \(M_{[8]} \simeq 1275\) MeV, the \(J^P=(3/2)^-\) sector produces 72 bound states forming an octet at 1620 MeV and a decuplet at 1710 MeV.
* Using physical masses and \(f=90\) MeV, the scheme predicts the four-star hyperon resonances \(\Xi(1820)\), \(\Lambda(1520)\), and \(\Sigma(1670)\) with masses close to empirical values.
* A bound state in the \((0,-3)\) channel is predicted at 1950 MeV, below all thresholds, associated with a decuplet resonance not yet observed.
* The \((1,-1)\)-speed shows a decuplet state at 1920 MeV, identified with the three-star \(\Xi(1940)\) resonance.
* The \((0,-1)\)-speeds show signals of two resonance states consistent with the four-star \(\Lambda(1520)\) and \(\Lambda(1690)\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel dynamics previously applied to s-wave baryon resonances (Kolomeitsev and Lutz 2003, Jido et al. 2003) to the d-wave sector involving baryon-decuplet states. It complements the finding that chiral dynamics predicts two degenerate octet and one singlet bound states for s-wave resonances in the 'heavy' SU(3) limit, which also disappear for small current quark masses. The results enable future lattice QCD tests of the predicted bound-state pattern and suggest that vector-meson baryon-octet channels (Lutz et al. 2002) may improve the mass and width predictions.
