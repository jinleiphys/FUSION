# Elliptic flow of thermal dileptons in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulation

> The elliptic flows of thermal di-electrons are investigated within a (2+1)-dimension event-by-event hydrodynamic model for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The fluctuating initial conditions are given by the Monte Carlo Glauber model. We find in our event-by-event calculation rather weak correlation between the dilepton emission angles and the event plane angles of charged hadrons. We observe strong fluctuation effects in dilepton elliptic flows when using the event plane angles of dileptons at specific invariant mass $M$. The correlation between the dilepton event plane angle and charged hadron one becomes stronger with decreasing $M$. This provides a possible measure of the interplay between the effect of geometric deformation and that of fluctuating "hot spots" in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations, the elliptic flow of thermal dileptons shows only a weak correlation with the event plane of charged hadrons, contrary to expectations that fluctuations would strongly affect dilepton flow. The correlation between dilepton and hadron event planes becomes stronger only at lower dilepton invariant mass, providing a potential measure of the interplay between geometric deformation and fluctuating "hot spots."

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions generated by the Monte Carlo Glauber model for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. They calculated thermal dilepton production rates from both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) phase, using in-medium \(\rho\) meson spectral functions for the hadronic contribution. The elliptic flow of dileptons was computed both with respect to the charged hadron event plane and with respect to a dilepton event plane defined at specific invariant mass \(M\).

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, with initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c and kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature was set to \(T_{chem} = 165\) MeV.
- The initial entropy density parameters were \(C = 16\) and \(\delta = 0.14\).
- 400 events were used for each centrality bin for hadron spectra; 1000 events were used for event plane distribution studies.
- The dilepton elliptic flow was studied at invariant masses \(M = 0.77\) GeV/\(c^2\) (hadronic dominated region) and \(M = 2.0\) GeV/\(c^2\) (partonic dominated region).
- The correlation between dilepton event plane angle \(\varphi_2(M)\) and charged hadron event plane angle \(\Psi_2\) was found to be stronger at lower \(M\) and weaker at higher \(M\).

## Context

This work extends previous smooth (one-shot) hydrodynamic calculations of dilepton elliptic flow by Mohanty (2011), Deng (2010), and Chatterjee (2007), which used smooth initial conditions and did not account for event-by-event fluctuations. The authors note that similar fluctuation effects on thermal photon elliptic flows were studied by Shen (2013) using viscous hydrodynamics. The results enable experimental searches for the interplay between geometric deformation and fluctuating hot spots in relativistic heavy ion collisions, though the authors caution that identifying dilepton event planes experimentally is difficult due to low production rates.
