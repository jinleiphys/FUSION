# Non-renormalizability of the classical statistical approximation

> In this paper, we discuss questions related to the renormalizability of the classical statistical approximation, an approximation scheme that has been used recently in several studies of out-of-equilibrium problems in Quantum Field Theory. Although the ultraviolet power counting in this approximation scheme is identical to that of the unapproximated quantum field theory, this approximation is not renormalizable. The leading cause of this non-renormalizability is the breakdown of Weinberg's theorem in this approximation. We also discuss some practical implications of this negative result for simulations that employ this approximation scheme, and we speculate about a possible modification of the classical statistical approximation in order to systematically subtract the leading residual divergences.

## Key claim

The classical statistical approximation (CSA), despite having the same ultraviolet power counting as the full quantum field theory, is not renormalizable because the omission of the vertex \(\Gamma_{1112}\) causes a breakdown of Weinberg's theorem, leading to ultraviolet divergences that are stronger than expected from power counting alone.

## Method

The authors analyze the CSA in a massless real scalar \(\phi^4\) theory in four dimensions, using the retarded-advanced formalism where the CSA is defined by discarding all vertices with three indices of type 1 (the \(\Gamma_{1112}\) vertex). They examine all one-loop 2-point and 4-point functions in the CSA, compute their ultraviolet behavior, and test whether the divergences can be absorbed by redefining parameters of the original Lagrangian. They also compare the analytic structure of loop integrals in the CSA to the requirements of Weinberg's theorem for renormalizability.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop self-energy \(\Sigma_{12}\) in the CSA has a quadratic divergence \(\frac{g^2 \Lambda_{\rm UV}^2}{16\pi^2}\), which can be removed by a mass counterterm \(\delta m^2 = -\frac{g^2 \Lambda_{\rm UV}^2}{16\pi^2}\).
* The one-loop 4-point function \(\Gamma_{1222}\) in the CSA contains a logarithmic divergence that cannot be subtracted by redefining the coupling constant or any other operator present in the original Lagrangian.
* The superficial degree of divergence for a graph in the CSA is \(\omega({\cal G}) = 4 - E - N_\varphi\), where \(E\) is the number of external legs and \(N_\varphi\) is the number of external classical field insertions, identical to the full theory.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of classical approximations in quantum field theory at finite temperature, such as those by Bodeker (1998), Arnold, Son, and Yaffe (1999), and Aarts and Smit (1999), which found that classical approximations at high temperature are renormalizable. The present paper addresses the zero-temperature case where the classical approximation replaces vacuum fluctuations rather than thermal ones, leading to fundamentally different ultraviolet behavior. The results explain the strong cutoff dependence observed in numerical CSA simulations by Berges et al. (2011) and imply that existing CSA computations must be interpreted with caution. The authors suggest that adding a complex noise term to the classical equations of motion might allow systematic subtraction of the leading non-renormalizable divergences.
