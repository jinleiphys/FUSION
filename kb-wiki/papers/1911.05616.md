# Lambda-nucleon scattering in baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the lambda-nucleon scattering phase shifts and mixing angles by applying time-ordered perturbation theory to the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory. Scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the corresponding coupled-channel integral equations that have a milder ultraviolet behavior compared to their non-relativistic analogs. This allows us to consider the removed cutoff limit in our leading-order calculations also in the $^3P_0$ and $^3P_1$ partial waves. We find that, in the framework we are using, at least some part of the higher-order contributions to the baryon-baryon potential in these channels needs to be treated nonperturbatively and demonstrate how this can be achieved in a way consistent with quantum field theoretical renormalization for the leading contact interactions. We compare our results with the ones of the non-relativistic approach and lattice QCD phase shifts obtained for non-physical pion masses.

## Key claim

The authors calculate lambda-nucleon scattering phase shifts using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory with time-ordered perturbation theory, finding that at least some higher-order contributions to the baryon-baryon potential in the \(^3P_0\) and \(^3P_1\) partial waves must be treated nonperturbatively to achieve renormalization, contrary to the standard Weinberg power counting assumption that leading-order contact interactions suffice.

## Method

The authors apply time-ordered perturbation theory to the manifestly Lorentz-invariant SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian, constructing the effective potential from two-baryon irreducible diagrams at leading order (LO) including one-meson exchange and contact interactions. Scattering amplitudes for the coupled \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) system are obtained by solving the coupled-channel Kadyshevsky integral equations, which have milder ultraviolet behavior than the non-relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation, allowing the cutoff to be removed. For the \(^3P_0\) and \(^3P_1\) partial waves, next-to-leading order contact interactions are included nonperturbatively using subtractive (BPHZ-type) renormalization to achieve cutoff-independent results.

## Key numbers

- Low-energy constants (LECs) for the \(^1S_0\) partial wave: \(C^{^1S_0}_{\Lambda N,\Lambda N} = -0.0186 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), \(C^{^1S_0}_{\Lambda N,\Sigma N} = 0.00361 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), \(C^{^1S_0}_{\Sigma N,\Sigma N} = -0.00849 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), fitted with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 20\) GeV.
- LECs for the \(^3S_1\) partial wave: \(C^{^3S_1}_{\Lambda N,\Lambda N} = -0.0124 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), \(C^{^3S_1}_{\Lambda N,\Sigma N} = -0.00499 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), \(C^{^3S_1}_{\Sigma N,\Sigma N} = -0.0129 \times 10^4 \text{ GeV}^{-2}\), fitted with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 20\) GeV.
- Scattering length for \(\Lambda N\) \(^1S_0\): \(a_{^1S_0} = -2.94\) fm (relativistic LO, \(\Lambda=20\) GeV), compared to \(-1.91\) fm (non-relativistic LO) and \(-2.91\) fm (non-relativistic NLO).
- Effective range for \(\Lambda N\) \(^1S_0\): \(r_{^1S_0} = -1.44\) fm (relativistic LO), compared to \(1.40\) fm (non-relativistic LO) and \(2.78\) fm (non-relativistic NLO).
- Scattering length for \(\Lambda N\) \(^3S_1\): \(a_{^3S_1} = -1.41\) fm (relativistic LO), compared to \(-1.23\) fm (non-relativistic LO) and \(-1.41\) fm (non-relativistic NLO).
- Effective range for \(\Lambda N\) \(^3S_1\): \(r_{^3S_1} = 1.61\) fm (relativistic LO), compared to \(2.13\) fm (non-relativistic LO) and \(2.53\) fm (non-relativistic NLO).

## Context

This work extends the modified Weinberg approach using time-ordered perturbation theory, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Epelbaum (2012) and to the strange sector by Li (2016) and Ren (2018), to the \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) coupled-channel system. The results are compared to non-relativistic chiral EFT calculations by Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2019) and to lattice QCD phase shifts from the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane, 2006) and HAL QCD collaboration (Miyamoto, 2016) at non-physical pion masses. The demonstration that higher-order contact interactions in P-waves can be renormalized nonperturbatively enables future calculations of hyperon-nucleon scattering with controlled chiral extrapolations and provides a framework for treating the \(^1S_0\) channel similarly.
