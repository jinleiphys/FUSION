# $Λ$(1405) in the flavor SU(3) limit using a separable potential in the HAL QCD method

> Using the HAL QCD method, we investigate S-wave meson-baryon interactions in singlet and two octet channels in the flavor SU(3) limit, where the chiral unitary model predicts that a combination of bound-state poles in these channels corresponds to $Λ(1405)$. To avoid the singular behavior of the leading-order potentials of these channels in the derivative expansion, we instead employ a separable potential in the time-dependent HAL QCD method. To calculate all-to-all propagators in the three-point correlation functions including $Λ$-baryon source operators with zero momentum, we employ the conventional stochastic estimation combined with the covariant approximation averaging. Separable potentials both in the singlet and octet channels show attraction without singular behavior. Our results of the corresponding phase shifts indicate that the attractive interaction in the singlet channel is stronger than that in the octet. Binding energies are consistent with the estimates from the two-point correlation function within one (two) sigma for the singlet (octet) channel, and this ordering of binding energies is consistent with the mass hierarchy suggested by the chiral unitary model.

## Key claim

Using a separable potential in the time-dependent HAL QCD method avoids the singular behavior that plagued leading-order derivative-expansion potentials for meson-baryon systems in the flavor SU(3) limit, and the resulting binding energies are consistent with the mass hierarchy predicted by the chiral unitary model, though the octet-channel results agree with two-point correlation function estimates only within two sigma.

## Method

The authors employ the time-dependent HAL QCD method with a separable potential \(U(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}') \simeq \eta v(\mathbf{r})v^*(\mathbf{r}')\) instead of the usual leading-order local potential, to avoid singularities from zeros of the R-correlator. They compute three-point correlation functions using all-to-all propagators via conventional stochastic estimation with covariant approximation averaging, on gauge configurations with Iwasaki gauge action and \(\mathcal{O}(a)\)-improved Wilson quarks at \(\beta=1.83\). The S-wave separable potential is extracted from the R-correlator and its time derivative, then the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved to obtain phase shifts and binding energies for the singlet and two octet (\(8_s\) and \(8_a\)) channels.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-scalar meson mass \(m_{8,M} = 455.6(1.4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and octet baryon mass \(m_{8,B} = 1163.4(4.1)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from two-point correlation functions.
- Binding energy from two-point correlation functions: \(E^{(8)}_{\mathrm{bind}} = 24.6(15.9)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the octet channel and \(E^{(1)}_{\mathrm{bind}} = 88.6(5.8)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the singlet channel.
- Binding energy from separable potential analysis: \(E^{(8_s)}_{\mathrm{bind}} = 57.9(5.5)(^{+5.0}_{-2.9})\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(E^{(8_a)}_{\mathrm{bind}} = 59.8(3.6)(^{+1.1}_{-11.3})\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(E^{(1)}_{\mathrm{bind}} = 77.0(6.3)(^{+6.8}_{-7.0})\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Lattice spacing \(a = 0.121(2)\ \mathrm{fm}\) and volume \(V = (32a)^4 \approx (3.87\ \mathrm{fm})^4\).
- The coefficient \(\eta = -1\) for all three channels, indicating attractive interactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Murakami 2023) where leading-order derivative-expansion potentials showed singular behavior due to zeros of the R-correlator, preventing reliable extraction of observables. The results are compared to predictions of the chiral unitary model (Jido 2003, Bruns 2021, Guo 2023), which suggests that \(\Lambda(1405)\) corresponds to a combination of bound-state poles with the singlet channel more bound than the octet. The present analysis enables future studies including coupled-channel effects between \(8_s\) and \(8_a\) channels, finer lattice simulations to reduce sensitivity to short-distance potential behavior, and extension to more general separable forms for resonance extraction.
