# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Gribov Inelastic Shadowing in High Energy Collisions

> 4 pages, 2 figures. Talk given at DIFFRACTION 2010, International Workshop on Diffraction in High Energy Physics, Otranto, Italy, September 10-15, 2010

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) and Gribov inelastic shadowing (IS) have opposite effects on nuclear thickness functions and cross sections at high energies: SRC increase nuclear opacity while Gribov IS increases nuclear transparency, and their combined net effect on total and inelastic cross sections amounts to only a few percent.

## Method

The author calculates hadron-nucleus cross sections using a Glauber-like approach that includes both SRC (via two-body density contractions from realistic NN interactions) and Gribov IS (summed to all orders via the dipole approach). The formalism is applied to p-12C, p-208Pb, and 208Pb-208Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, using realistic one- and two-body densities and correlation functions from Ref. ACMprl.

## Key numbers

- Total p-208Pb cross section at LHC energies: 3850.63 mb (Glauber only), 3885.77 mb (Glauber+SRC), 3833.26 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov with q-2q model)
- Elastic p-208Pb cross section at LHC: 1664.76 mb (Glauber), 1690.48 mb (Glauber+SRC), 1655.70 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov q-2q)
- Quasi-elastic p-208Pb cross section at LHC: 120.92 mb (Glauber), 112.65 mb (Glauber+SRC), 113.37 mb (Glauber+SRC+Gribov q-2q)
- Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at LHC: 6.88 (Glauber), 6.82 (Glauber+SRC), 6.88 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov q-2q)
- Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at RHIC: 4.70 (Glauber), 4.65 (Glauber+SRC), 4.75 (Glauber+SRC+Gribov q-2q)

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber approach by incorporating both short-range correlations and Gribov inelastic shadowing, which are usually neglected in simpler treatments. The calculations use realistic two-body densities from Panda (2005) and correlation functions from Alvioli, Ciofi degli Atti, and Morita (2005). The results enable more accurate predictions for hard reaction cross section normalization in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, and are consistent with earlier deuteron-gold scattering calculations by Kopeliovich (2007).
