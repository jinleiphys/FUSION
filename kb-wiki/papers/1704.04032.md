# How brightly does the Glasma shine? Photon production off-equilibrium

> We present a parametric estimate of photon production at early times in heavy-ion collisions based on a consistent weak coupling thermalization scenario. We quantify the contribution of the off-equilibrium Glasma phase relative to that of a thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma. Taking into account the constraints from charged hadron multiplicity data, the Glasma contribution is found to be significant especially for large values of the saturation scale.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium Glasma phase contributes significantly to photon production in heavy-ion collisions, with its contribution being comparable to that of the thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma, especially for large values of the saturation scale and for lower collision energies or less central collisions.

## Method

The authors performed a parametric estimate of photon production in the bottom-up thermalization scenario, dividing the pre-equilibrium evolution into three temporal stages based on the saturation scale Q_s and the QCD coupling alpha_s. They computed photon yields from Compton scattering and annihilation processes using a small-angle approximation formula that integrates single-particle distribution functions for quarks and gluons, with the quark distribution assumed to be smaller than the gluon distribution by a factor of alpha_s due to Fermi-Dirac statistics.

## Key numbers

- The thermalization time tau_th is parametrized as tau_th = c_eq * alpha_s^{-13/5} * Q_s^{-1}, with the coefficient c_T estimated to be 0.18 up to logarithmic accuracy and varied in the range 0.1 to 0.4
- The hadronization time tau_c is defined as the time when temperature falls below the crossover temperature T_c = 154 MeV
- The value of Q_s^2 at RHIC most central collision (N_part = 353) is set to 2 GeV^2
- For RHIC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, if Q_s is larger than approximately 1.5 GeV, the bottom-up thermalization produces more photons than the hydro model extended to early times
- For LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, if Q_s is larger than approximately 2 GeV, the bottom-up thermalization produces more photons than the hydro model extended to early times
- The Glasma photon yield is approximately proportional to Q_s^2

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier (2000) by quantifying photon production from the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase, which is not included in standard hydrodynamic models for photon production. The authors use results from classical Yang-Mills simulations with color glass condensate initial conditions by Krasnitz (2000) and Lappi (2007), and the IP-Glasma model by Schenke (2012) for the N_part-dependence of Q_s. This parametric estimate motivates future ab-initio calculations using kinetic theory or classical-statistical simulations that could reduce uncertainties and address the photon v_2 puzzle.
