# Calculating the Jet Quenching Parameter from AdS/CFT

> Models of medium-induced radiative parton energy loss account for the strong suppression of high-pT hadron spectra in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC in terms of a single "jet quenching parameter'' $\hat q$. The available suite of jet quenching measurements make $\hat q$ one of the experimentally best constrained properties of the hot fluid produced in RHIC collisions. We observe that $\hat q$ can be given a model-independent, nonperturbative, quantum field theoretic definition in terms of the short-distance behavior of a particular light-like Wilson loop. We then use the AdS/CFT correspondence to obtain a strong-coupling calculation of $\hat q$ in hot N=4 supersymmetric QCD, finding $\hat{q}_{SYM} = 26.69 \sqrt{α_{SYM} N_c} T^3$ in the limit in which both $N_c$ and $4πα_{SYM} N_c$ are large. We thus learn that at strong coupling $\hat q$ is not proportional to the entropy density $s$, or to some "number density of scatterers'' since, unlike the number of degrees of freedom, $\hat q$ does not grow like $N_c^2$.

## Key claim

The jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ can be given a nonperturbative, model-independent definition via the short-distance behavior of a light-like Wilson loop, and in the strongly coupled limit of $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory it scales as $\sqrt{\lambda}\,T^3$ rather than as the number of degrees of freedom $N_c^2$, contradicting expectations from perturbative models.

## Method

The authors define $\hat q$ as the coefficient of the $L^- L^2$ term in the logarithm of the thermal expectation value of a light-like adjoint Wilson loop at small transverse separation $L$. They then compute this Wilson loop using the AdS/CFT correspondence by evaluating the Nambu-Goto action of the extremal string worldsheet in the AdS black hole metric dual to the hot $\mathcal{N}=4$ theory, subtract the self-energy of two disconnected worldsheets, and extract $\hat q$ from the small-$L$ expansion of the resulting interaction action.

## Key numbers

- $\hat{q}_{\mathrm{SYM}} = 26.69 \sqrt{\alpha_{\mathrm{SYM}} N_c} \, T^3$ in the large-$N_c$, large-$\lambda$ limit of $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory.
- For $N_c=3$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{SYM}}=1/2$ (so $\lambda=6\pi$), the result gives $\hat q = 4.5$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=300$ MeV, $10.6$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=400$ MeV, and $20.7$ GeV$^2$/fm at $T=500$ MeV.
- The time-averaged $\overline{\hat q}$ extracted from RHIC data is around 5-15 GeV$^2$/fm; using a Bjorken expansion with $\tau_0=0.5$ fm and $L^-/\sqrt{2}=2$ fm, the authors find that to obtain $\overline{\hat q}=5$ GeV$^2$/fm requires $T(1\ \mathrm{fm})\approx 310$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/CFT methodology previously applied to shear viscosity (Policastro 2001, Buchel 2003, Kovtun 2003) and diffusion constants (Policastro 2002) to the jet quenching parameter, which had only been defined perturbatively (Baier 1996). The result shows that at strong coupling $\hat q$ is not proportional to entropy density or gluon number density, unlike the shear viscosity to entropy ratio $\eta/s$ which is universal. The calculation enables future work on $\hat q$ in other gauge theories with gravity duals and on $1/\lambda$ corrections, and provides a benchmark for comparing strongly coupled plasma predictions to RHIC data.
