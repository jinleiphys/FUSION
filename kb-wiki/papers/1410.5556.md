# Changes in rotational characters of one- and two-phonon $γ$-vibrational bands in $^{105}$Mo

> The $γ$ vibration is the most typical low-lying collective motion prevailing the nuclear chart. But only few one-phonon rotational bands in odd-$A$ nuclei have been known. Furthermore, two-phonon states, even the band head, have been observed in a very limited number of nuclides not only of odd-$A$ but even-even. Among them, that in $^{105}$Mo is unique in that Coriolis effects are expected to be stronger than in $^{103}$Nb and $^{105}$Nb on which theoretical studies were reported. Then the purpose of the present work is to study $^{105}$Mo paying attention to rotational character change of the one-phonon and two-phonon bands. The particle-vibration coupling model based on the cranking model and the random-phase approximation is used to calculate the vibrational states in rotating odd-$A$ nuclei. The present model reproduces the observed yrast zero-phonon and one-phonon bands well. Emerging general features of the rotational character change from low spin to high spin are elucidated. In particular, the reason why the one-phonon band does not exhibit signature splitting is clarified. The calculated collectivity of the two-phonon states, however, is located higher than observed.

## Key claim

The particle-vibration coupling model reproduces the observed yrast zero-phonon and one-phonon γ-vibrational bands in ¹⁰⁵Mo well, but the calculated collectivity of the two-phonon states is located higher than observed, indicating an unknown mechanism for anharmonicity in high-j odd-A nuclei that the model does not capture.

## Method

The author used a particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model based on the cranking model and the random-phase approximation (RPA) to calculate vibrational states in rotating odd-A nuclei. First, a one-dimensionally cranked Nilsson plus BCS Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain quasiparticle states, then RPA was applied to the residual two-body pairing plus doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. Finally, the PVC Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a model space spanned by 0–4γ basis states (1qp ⊗ (γ or γ̄)ⁿ states) for ¹⁰⁵Mo, with calculations performed in signature-classified bases as continuous functions of rotational frequency.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps: Δₙ = 1.05 MeV and Δₚ = 0.85 MeV, widely used for both even- and odd-A nuclides in this mass region
- Quadrupole deformation: ε₂ = 0.3254, same as adopted for the isobar ¹⁰⁵Nb
- Triaxial deformation: γ = -10°, chosen to reproduce overall features of observed signature splitting
- The γ-vibrational energy ω_γ was set to 1.0 MeV in the RPA calculation for the even-even core to reproduce the observed situation that the γ-vibrational energy of ¹⁰⁵Mo is higher than those of ¹⁰⁴Mo (0.8121 MeV) and ¹⁰⁶Mo (0.7104 MeV)
- Harris parameters to fit the ground band of ¹⁰⁶Mo: 𝒥₀ = 18.08 MeV⁻¹ and 𝒥₁ = 43.21 MeV⁻³
- Calculated Routhians of zero-phonon, upper one-phonon, and upper two-phonon states at ω_rot = 0: (0.972, 1.890, 2.808) MeV with PVC; without PVC they would be (1.050, 2.050, 3.050) MeV
- Experimental anharmonicity: E₂γ/E₁γ = 1.76, in contrast to harmonic spectra in adjacent even-even isotopes ¹⁰⁴Mo and ¹⁰⁶Mo

## Context

This work extends the author's previous theoretical studies on ¹⁰³Nb and ¹⁰⁵Nb (Matsuzaki, 2014) using the same particle-vibration coupling model, but applied to ¹⁰⁵Mo where the odd neutron occupies the ν[532]5/2⁻ orbital from the h₁₁/₂ subshell with stronger Coriolis effects than the πg₉/₂ orbitals in the Nb isotopes. The calculation reproduces the observed zero- and one-phonon bands well and explains why the one-phonon band does not exhibit signature splitting, but the two-phonon states remain harmonic in the calculation while the data show anharmonicity (E₂γ/E₁γ = 1.76), suggesting an unknown mechanism proper to high-j odd-A nuclei. This enables future work to identify the mechanism producing anharmonicity in odd-A systems, different from the one discussed for even-even nuclei by Matsuo and Matsuyanagi.
