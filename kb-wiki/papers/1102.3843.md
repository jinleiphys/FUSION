# Domain Growth and Ordering Kinetics in Dense Quark Matter

> The kinetics of chiral transitions in quark matter is studied in a two flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We focus on the phase ordering dynamics subsequent to a temperature quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase. We study the dynamics by considering a phenomenological model (Ginzburg-Landau free-energy functional). The morphology of the ordering system is characterized by the scaling of the order-parameter correlation function.

# Domain Growth and Ordering Kinetics in Dense Quark Matter

## Key claim
The kinetics of chiral transitions in quark matter, modeled via a Ginzburg-Landau free-energy functional derived from the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, exhibits universal dynamical scaling and a growth law of $L(t) \sim t^{1/2}$ for both first-order and second-order temperature quenches, but only in the asymptotic late-stage regime where memory of the initial nucleation dynamics is lost.

## Method
The authors derived a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free-energy functional up to $M^6$ order from the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor NJL model. They then solved the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation numerically on a $d=2$ lattice of size $4096^2$ with periodic boundary conditions, using an Euler-discretization scheme with an isotropic Laplacian, to simulate the ordering dynamics following a sudden temperature quench from the massless quark phase to the massive quark phase.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum mass of quarks: $M \simeq 312$ MeV, obtained with cutoff $\Lambda = 653.30$ MeV and coupling $G = 5.0163 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$.
- At $T=0$, a first-order transition occurs at chemical potential $\mu \simeq 326.321$ MeV.
- For $\mu=0$, a second-order transition occurs at temperature $T \simeq 190$ MeV.
- Tricritical point coordinates: $(\mu_{tcp}, T_{tcp}) \simeq (282.58, 78)$ MeV.
- First-order transition occurs at $a_c = 3b^2/(16d)$, with the order parameter jumping discontinuously to $M = \pm (3|b|/4d)^{1/2}$.
- Asymptotic domain growth law: $L(t) \sim t^{1/2}$ (Allen-Cahn growth law).

## Context
This work extends the study of chiral transition kinetics in dense quark matter using the NJL model, building on prior mean-field analyses by the same authors (Singh, Puri, Mishra, earlier work) and on the closed time path formalism applied to the NJL model by Das (year not specified). The results are consistent with the Ohta-Jasnow-Kawasaki (OJK) scaling function for ordering ferromagnets and the Allen-Cahn growth law from condensed matter phase ordering. The findings enable predictions for non-equilibrium structures in heavy-ion collision fireballs, suggesting that equilibrium thermodynamic models may overestimate the transition temperature if the system's equilibration time exceeds the fireball lifetime.
