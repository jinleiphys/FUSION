# On gauge invariance of transverse momentum dependent distributions at small-x

> Transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions at small x exhibit a rich infinite twist structure that encompasses the leading twist (partonic) distributions as well as the physics of gluon saturation. Progress to further the connection between the standard TMD framework at moderate x and small x has been recently made. In this context, we show that light cone Wilson line operators at small-x can be formulated in terms of transverse gauge links. This new formulation of small x operators allows a direct matching with the standard leading twist gluon TMD distributions and provides an efficient and general prescription for computing TMD distributions at small x beyond leading twist.

## Key claim

The authors show that light-cone Wilson line operators at small-x can be reformulated in terms of transverse gauge links, which provides a direct matching with standard leading-twist gluon TMD distributions and a general prescription for computing TMD distributions at small-x beyond leading twist. The key condition is that this equivalence holds when the transverse field is a pure gauge, as occurs in the light-cone gauge A^- = 0.

## Method

The authors demonstrate that pairs of Wilson line operators, such as the dipole operator appearing in dijet production in eA collisions, can be expressed as transverse gauge links by performing a gauge rotation of the A^- field and using the property that the target gauge field in the A^- = 0 gauge is a transverse pure gauge A^i = (i/g) Omega partial^i Omega^{-1}. They then show that the dipole operator reduces to a transverse string built from rotated transverse gluon fields, and by iterating the transverse link expansion they recover the Weizsacker-Williams (WW) gluon TMD distribution with its characteristic staple-shaped gauge link structure.

## Key numbers

- The transverse gauge link is defined via the equation [x_1, x_2]_{x^+} = 1 - ig integral from x_2 to x_1 of dz dot A(x^+, z) [z, x_2]_{x^+}, where the integration runs over a straight-line trajectory parameterized by s from 0 to 1.
- The dipole operator M_{ij} ~ (U_{x_1}(infinity, xi) U_{x_2}^dagger(xi, infinity))_{ij} is transformed into the manifestly gauge-covariant form U_{x_1}(infinity, xi) [x_1, x_2]_xi U_{x_2}^dagger(xi, infinity).
- The expansion of the dipole operator yields terms at order (ig)^1 and (ig)^2 corresponding to one-body and two-body amplitudes, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the connection between small-x physics and TMD distributions established by Dominguez et al. (2011), who extracted the small-x limit of TMD distributions via a transverse gradient expansion of Wilson line operators in forward dijet production. It builds on recent attempts to unify small-x and moderate-x evolution equations for leading-twist TMDs by Balitsky (2015, 2019) and on the infinite-twist TMD framework developed by Altinoluk et al. (2019). The new formulation in terms of transverse gauge links provides a geometric interpretation of hadronic operators at small-x and enables straightforward generalization to other observables beyond the dipole case, with implications for extending the TMD/small-x equivalence beyond tree level and connecting to the Bjorken limit.
