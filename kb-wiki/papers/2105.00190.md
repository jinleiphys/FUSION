# Coulomb effects in the decays $Υ(4S) \rightarrow B\bar B$

> A simple exactly solvable model is proposed for describing the decays $Υ(4S) \rightarrow B^0\bar B^0$ and $Υ(4S) \rightarrow B^+B^-$. Our predictions agree with available experimental data. Using this model, we analyze the Coulomb effects in the spectra of these decays. It is shown that the frequently used assumption of factorization of Coulomb effects is not fulfilled. The Coulomb interaction leads to the difference in the positions and heights of the peaks corresponding to the charged and neutral modes. As a result, the ratio of probability of $Υ(4S)\rightarrow B^+B^-$ decay and $Υ(4S) \rightarrow B^0\bar B^0$ decay is a nontrivial function of energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple exactly solvable model for the decays Υ(4S) → B⁺B⁻ and Υ(4S) → B⁰B̄⁰ that agrees with available experimental data, but the key hedge is that the model is a phenomenological one with parameters chosen to match data, and the authors state they are "confident that, at least qualitatively, our predictions correspond to the actual experimental situation."

## Method

The authors model the B-meson pair production using a coupled-channel Schrödinger equation with a square-well strong interaction potential V(r) = -V₀ θ(a-r) and a delta-function transition potential V_ex(r) = g δ(r-a) between charged and neutral channels, plus the Coulomb potential -α/r for charged mesons. They solve the radial wave functions analytically for the p-wave (l=1) state, matching solutions at the boundary r=a, and compute the decay probabilities W_c and W_n from the derivatives of the wave functions at the origin. The parameter g is found to be very small and set to zero, and the potential depth V₀ is chosen to produce a virtual state near threshold.

## Key numbers

- Υ(4S) mass M_R = 10579.4 ± 1.4 MeV, with M_R - M_th = 20.1 ± 1.4 MeV for neutral mesons and 20.7 ± 1.4 MeV for charged mesons
- Υ(4S) width Γ_R = 20.5 ± 2.5 MeV
- The Coulomb parameter t = πα/v ≈ 0.37, giving a naive Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov factor W_c/W_n ≈ 1.2, while the PDG value is 1.058 ± 0.024
- Model parameters used: V₀ = 269 MeV, a = 2.5 fm, b = 23, g = 0 (corresponding to n = 5)
- The distance between the peaks of W_c and W_n is approximately 2 MeV
- The width of each peak is approximately 17 MeV, while the width of W_tot = W_c + W_n is about 20 MeV
- The ratio W_c/W_n can reach 1.4 at E ∼ 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach developed for e⁺e⁻ annihilation into nucleon-antinucleon pairs near threshold by Milstein and Salnikov (2018), adapting it to the Υ(4S) → BB̄ decays. It contradicts the frequently used assumption of factorization of Coulomb effects (the Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov factor) and shows that the ratio W_c/W_n is a nontrivial function of energy, not a constant. The predictions enable experimental tests of the energy dependence of the charged-to-neutral ratio and the existence of two separate peaks, which could be measured at B-factories like Belle and BaBar.
