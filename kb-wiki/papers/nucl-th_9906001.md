# Proton-neutron interactions in N \approx Z nuclei

> Proton-neutron (p-n) interactions and its various aspects in $N\approx Z$ nuclei of $g_{9/2}$- and $f_{7/2}$ subshell are studied using a schematic model interaction with four force parameters proposed recently. It is shown that the model interaction well reproduces observed physical quantities: the double differences of binding energies, symmetry energy, Wigner energy, odd-even mass difference and separation energy, which testifies the reliability of the model interaction and its p-n interactions. First of all, the double differences of binding energies are used for probing the p-n interactions. The analysis reveals different contributions of the isoscalar and isovector p-n pairing interactions to two types of double difference of binding energies, and also indicates the importance of a unique form of isoscalar p-n pairing force with all $J$ components. Next, it is shown that this p-n force is closely related to the symmetry energy and the Wigner energy. Other calculations demonstrate significant roles of p-n interactions in the odd-even mass difference and in the separation energy at $N=Z$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a schematic model interaction with four force parameters, including a unique isoscalar proton-neutron pairing force with all J components, reproduces observed binding-energy-related quantities in N ≈ Z nuclei of the g9/2 and f7/2 subshells, with the caveat that the single-j shell model is only semiquantitative for binding energies and insufficient for detailed energy spectra.

## Method

The authors employed a shell model Hamiltonian consisting of a single-particle term plus four isospin-invariant forces: isovector monopole pairing (P0), isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ), isovector quadrupole pairing (P2), and a J-independent isoscalar proton-neutron force (Vτ=0πν). They applied this model in a single-j shell approximation using the g9/2 shell for A=82-100 nuclei (with Z=N=40 core) and the f7/2 shell for A=42-50 nuclei (with Z=N=20 core), calculating ground-state energies and comparing with experimental binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Force parameters for the g9/2 shell region: g0 = 0.26 MeV, X = 1.50 MeV, G2 = 0.35 MeV, k0 = 0.925 MeV
- Force parameters for the f7/2 shell region: g0 = 0.59 MeV, X = 1.20 MeV, G2 = 0.90 MeV, k0 = 1.90 MeV
- The double difference δV(1)(Z,N) follows the approximate form I0 = 40/A with staggering (−1)A I1
- The double difference δV(2)(Z,N) follows the smooth systematic curve 40/A for A > 80
- The isoscalar p-n force strength k0 has approximate global dependence k0 ≈ 80/A (80/A = 0.93 for A=86, 80/A = 1.74 for A=46)
- The symmetry energy coefficient from the simplified Hamiltonian is ã(A) = 1.245A in the f7/2 region, with 76% contribution from Vτ=0πν and 24% from the isovector J=0 pairing force
- The Wigner energy with Vτ=0πν becomes EW = 37.4|N-Z|/A MeV for A=60, consistent with the empirical formula
- The odd-even mass difference Δ3 and Δ4 show peaks at N=Z, with Δ3 reaching 2.710 MeV for 48Cr (N=Z=24)

## Context

This work extends the schematic P+QQ model proposed by Hasegawa (1997) to study proton-neutron interactions in N ≈ Z nuclei, building on earlier analyses of double differences of binding energies by Janecke (1965), Jensen (1984), and Brenner (1990). The paper addresses the debate about the origin of the Wigner energy, showing that the isoscalar p-n pairing force with all J components (not just J=1 as considered by Satula and Wyss (1997)) is crucial for reproducing both the symmetry energy and Wigner energy, contrary to the shell model calculation by Poves and Martinez-Pinedo (1998) which used only J=0 isovector and J=1 isoscalar pairing forces. The results enable further study of α-like four-nucleon correlations in N=Z nuclei and provide a framework for understanding separation energies in proton-rich nuclei.
