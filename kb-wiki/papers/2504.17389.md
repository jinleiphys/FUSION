# Directed and elliptic flow of light nuclei and hypernuclei in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3$ GeV: Coalescence vs. Statistical Fragmentation

> The harmonic flow coefficients of light nuclei and hypernuclei in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3$ GeV are investigated using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics transport model. For the Equation-of-State we employ a density and momentum dependent potential from the Chiral-Mean-Field model. Light nuclei and hypernuclei production is described at kinetic freeze-out via a coalescence mechanism or with a statistical multi-fragmentation calculation. The directed flow $v_1$ of p, d, t, $^3$He, $^4$He as well as the $Λ$, $^3_Λ$H and $^4_Λ$H is shown to approximately scale with mass number $A$ of the light cluster in both calculations. This is in agreement with the experimental results for the directed flow measured by STAR. Predictions for the directed and elliptic flow of (hyper)nuclei at further RHIC-FXT and FAIR energies show that the scaling properties should improve as the beam energy is increased.

## Key claim

The directed flow \(v_1\) of light nuclei and hypernuclei in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3\) GeV approximately scales with mass number \(A\) in both the coalescence and statistical multi-fragmentation approaches, but this scaling is not perfect at this energy and improves significantly only at higher beam energies; the elliptic flow \(v_2\) at midrapidity shows approximate mass scaling in the simulations, in contrast to the STAR data which do not show such scaling.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.4) transport model with a density- and momentum-dependent potential from the Chiral-Mean-Field (CMF) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3\) GeV. Light nuclei and hypernuclei were produced at kinetic freeze-out either via a phase-space coalescence mechanism (with refitted coalescence parameters \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}\) and \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}\)) or via a statistical multi-fragmentation model (SMM) applied to primary hot clusters selected from the UrQMD freeze-out configuration. Flow coefficients were extracted with respect to the known reaction plane in the simulation.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow slope \(\mathrm{d}v_1/\mathrm{d}y|_{y=0}\) for protons and \(\Lambda\) is essentially equal in both coalescence and SMM calculations at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=3\) GeV.
- Coalescence parameters for deuteron: \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}=4.0\) fm, \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}=0.33\) GeV; for \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He: \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}=3.5\) fm, \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}=0.45\) GeV; for \(^4\)He: \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}=3.5\) fm, \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}=0.55\) GeV; for \(^3_\Lambda\)H: \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}=9.5\) fm, \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}=0.15\) GeV; for \(^4_\Lambda\)H: \(\Delta r_\mathrm{max}=9.5\) fm, \(\Delta p_\mathrm{max}=0.25\) GeV.
- Transverse momentum ranges used for flow extraction: \(0.4 \leq p_\mathrm{T}/A \leq 1.0\) GeV for light nuclei; for \(\Lambda\): \(0.4 \leq p_\mathrm{T} \leq 0.8\) GeV; for \(^3_\Lambda\)H: \(1.0 \leq p_\mathrm{T} \leq 2.5\) GeV; for \(^4_\Lambda\)H: \(1.2 \leq p_\mathrm{T} \leq 3.0\) GeV.
- The SMM model uses a coalescence parameter \(v_c=0.22\) for primary hot cluster recognition and a fixed stopping time of \(t=40\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD studies of cluster and hypernuclei production (Reichert 2022, Buyukcizmeci 2023) and flow coefficients (Reichert 2024) to a direct comparison of two production mechanisms against STAR data (STAR 2022). It provides predictions for the energy dependence of mass-number scaling of directed and elliptic flow across the STAR fixed-target program and the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, enabling future constraints on the hyperon-nucleon interaction and the nuclear equation of state.
