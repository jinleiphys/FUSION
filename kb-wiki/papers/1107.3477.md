# Two-pion interferometry for the granular sources in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the two-pion interferometry in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions in the granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets. The pion transverse momentum spectra and HBT radii of the granular sources agree well with the experimental data of the $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au-Au and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb most central collisions. In the granular source model the larger initial system breakup time may lead to the larger HBT radii $R_{\rm out}$, $R_{\rm side}$, and $R_{\rm long}$. However, the large droplet transverse expansion and limited average relative emitting time of particles in the granular source lead to small ratios of the transverse HBT radii $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$.

## Key claim

The granular source model of quark-gluon plasma droplets can simultaneously reproduce the pion transverse momentum spectra and HBT radii for both RHIC Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and LHC Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, provided the LHC source uses the same initial droplet temperature and velocity formula as the RHIC source but with a larger initial system breakup time.

## Method

The authors model the particle-emitting source as a collection of QGP droplets that form at a breakup time t0, with droplet positions distributed within a cylinder and droplet radii following a Gaussian distribution. Each droplet evolves via relativistic hydrodynamics with a cross-over equation of state, and pions freeze out over a temperature range that includes both direct emission and resonance decay contributions. The two-pion correlation functions are calculated in the longitudinal comoving system and fitted with a Gaussian parametrization to extract HBT radii Rout, Rside, and Rlong as functions of transverse pair momentum kT.

## Key numbers

- Initial droplet temperature T0 = 200 MeV, corresponding to initial energy density ε0 = 2.24 GeV/fm³
- Standard deviation of droplet radius distribution σd = 2.5 fm
- For RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV Au-Au): τ0 = 0.8 fm/c, breakup time t0 = 4.3 fm/c, source radius R⊥ = 6.5 fm, shell parameter ΔR⊥ = 3.4 fm
- For LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb): τ0 = 0.4 fm/c, breakup time t0 = 8.0 fm/c, source radius R⊥ = 7.8 fm, shell parameter ΔR⊥ = 5.5 fm
- Droplet transverse velocity parameters aT = 0.65 and bT = 1.40, same for both energies, with R⊥0 = 8.0 fm
- Freeze-out temperature range: Tc = 170 MeV down to 80 MeV, with direct emission fraction fdir = 0.85, ΔTdir = 10 MeV, and ΔTdec = 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends the granular source model developed by Zhang (2006, 2009) to explain RHIC HBT data, now applying it to LHC energies. The model addresses the long-standing "HBT puzzle" where simple evolution models failed to describe source space-time from RHIC measurements (STAR 2005, PHENIX 2004). The consistent description of both RHIC and LHC data provides constraints on the initial condition, source evolution, and particle freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, and enables further study of the formation and evolution of pion-emitting sources.
