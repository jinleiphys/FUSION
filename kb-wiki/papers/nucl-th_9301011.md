# Extracting Nuclear Transparency from p-A Cross sections

> We study nuclear structure effects on the transparency in high transverse momentum $(p,2p)$ and $(e,e'p)$ reactions. We show that in the DWIA-eikonal approximation, even when correlations are included, one can get a factorized expression for the transparency. This depends only on the average nucleon density $ρ(r)$ and a correlation function. We develop a technique to include correlations in a Monte-Carlo Glauber type calculation. We compare calculations of $T$ using the eikonal formalism and a continuous density, with a Monte Carlo method based on discrete nucleons.

## Key claim
The paper shows that, within the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) using the eikonal approximation and closure, the nuclear transparency \(T\) for high-transverse-momentum \((p,2p)\) and \((e,e'p)\) reactions can be expressed in a factorized form that depends only on the average nucleon density \(\rho(r)\) and a correlation function, not on detailed single-particle wave functions. The authors caution that the definition of \(T\) used by Lee and Miller (1991) differs from the experimental definition, which can affect comparisons with data.

## Method
The authors derive a factorized expression for the transparency \(T = \int P_0 P_1 P_2 \rho(r) d^3 r\) using the DWIA-eikonal approximation, where the \(P_i\) are probabilities of no interaction along each proton leg. They develop a Monte-Carlo Glauber method that treats the nucleus as composed of discrete nucleons, including nucleon-nucleon correlations via a two-body correlation factor \(g(r)\); for finite nuclei, they adjust a parent distribution \(\zeta(r)\) to reproduce a Woods-Saxon density. They compare calculations of \(T\) for \((p,2p)\) and \((e,e'p)\) reactions using both the eikonal formalism and the Monte-Carlo method, with and without correlations.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{12}\)C in the \((p,2p)\) reaction at 10 GeV lab energy with \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} = 37\) mb, the uncorrelated eikonal calculation gives \(T = 0.24\); the correlated eikonal (Benhar-type correlation) gives \(T = 0.33\); the uncorrelated Glauber Monte Carlo gives \(T = 0.29\); the correlated Glauber Monte Carlo gives \(T = 0.44\).
- For \(^{12}\)C in the \((e,e'p)\) reaction under the same conditions, the uncorrelated eikonal gives \(T = 0.60\); the correlated eikonal (Benhar-type) gives \(T = 0.68\); the uncorrelated Glauber gives \(T = 0.63\); the correlated Glauber gives \(T = 0.72\).
- For \(^{208}\)Pb in the \((p,2p)\) reaction, the uncorrelated eikonal gives \(T = 0.0243\); the correlated eikonal gives \(T = 0.0319\); the uncorrelated Glauber gives \(T = 0.0254\); the correlated Glauber gives \(T = 0.0298\).
- The correlation function used is a Gaussian \(g(r) = 1 - \exp(-(r/R)^2)\) with \(R = 0.92\) fm, which yields a two-body correlation function \(C\) similar to that used by Benhar et al. (1993).
- The Woods-Saxon density parameters are \(R_0 = 1.14 A^{1/3}\) fm and \(\sigma = 0.545\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the DWIA-eikonal formalism of Lee and Miller (1991) and the Glauber Monte Carlo approach of Farrar et al. (1988) by explicitly including nucleon-nucleon correlations and comparing continuous-density eikonal calculations with discrete-nucleon Monte Carlo methods. The authors find that correlations increase the transparency by about 30% in the eikonal calculation and by 17-22% in the Glauber calculation, correcting an earlier overestimate by Frati et al. (1992). The results enable more reliable extraction of nuclear transparency from experimental \((p,2p)\) and \((e,e'p)\) data, which is relevant for testing color transparency predictions.
