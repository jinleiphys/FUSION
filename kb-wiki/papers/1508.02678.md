# Exclusive decays $B \to \ell^-\barν$ and $B \to D^{(\ast)}\ell^-\barν$ in the covariant quark model

> We study the exclusive leptonic and semileptonic $B$ decays $B \to \ell^- \barν$ and $B \to D^{(\ast)} \ell^-\barν$ in the framework of the covariant quark model with built-in infrared confinement. We compute the relevant form factors in the full kinematical momentum transfer region. The calculated form factors are used to evaluate branching fractions and polarization observables of the above transitions. We compare our results with experimental data and results from other theoretical studies.

## Key claim

The covariant quark model (CQM) with built-in infrared confinement can describe the full kinematic range of form factors for exclusive leptonic and semileptonic B decays without relying on Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET), and the resulting predictions for branching fractions and polarization observables agree with experimental data within uncertainties, though the model's zero-recoil value for the form factor A+ exceeds the leading-order HQET result by about 13%.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant constituent quark model where meson-quark coupling is determined by the compositeness condition Z_H = 0, and infrared confinement is implemented by cutting the Fock-Schwinger parameter integration at an upper limit 1/lambda^2. They compute the leptonic decay constants and semileptonic form factors for B -> D and B -> D* transitions using a Gaussian vertex function, with model parameters (constituent quark masses, size parameters, and infrared cutoff lambda) fixed by a least-squares fit to experimental data and lattice simulations. The form factors are then used to evaluate branching fractions, forward-backward asymmetries, and ratios R(D) and R(D*).

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses (in GeV): m_u = 0.241, m_s = 0.428, m_c = 1.67, m_b = 5.04; infrared cutoff lambda = 0.181 GeV
- Leptonic decay constant f_B = 193.1 MeV (compared to PDG value 190.6 ± 4.7 MeV)
- Leptonic decay constant f_D = 206.1 MeV (compared to PDG value 204.6 ± 5.0 MeV)
- Branching fraction B(B- -> tau- nu_tau) = 1.10 x 10^{-4} (compared to PDG value (1.14 ± 0.27) x 10^{-4})
- Branching fraction B(B- -> mu- nu_mu) = 0.49 x 10^{-6} (compared to PDG upper limit < 1.0 x 10^{-6})
- Branching fraction B(B- -> e- nu_e) = 1.16 x 10^{-11} (compared to PDG upper limit < 9.8 x 10^{-7})
- Form factor F_+(0) = 0.78 for B -> D transition; slope parameter a = 0.74, b = 0.038 in double-pole parametrization
- Form factor V(0) = 0.77 for B -> D* transition; slope parameter a = 0.90, b = 0.075
- Ratio F_0(q^2)/F_+(q^2) slope alpha = 0.019 GeV^{-2} (compared to lattice result 0.020(1) GeV^{-2})
- Zero-recoil values: F_+(q^2_max) = 1.12, A_0(q^2_max) = 1.91, A_+(q^2_max) = 0.99, V(q^2_max) = 1.16

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark model approach previously developed by the group (Branz 2009, Ivanov 2011) to the specific case of B -> l nu and B -> D(*) l nu decays, providing an independent calculation that does not rely on HQET. The results are compared with experimental data from BABAR, Belle, and LHCb collaborations, as well as with lattice QCD results and other theoretical approaches such as the relativistic quark model based on the quasipotential method (Ebert 2006, Faustov 2012). The computed form factors and branching fractions enable further studies of the observed discrepancies in R(D) and R(D*) (1.8 sigma for R(D) and 3.3 sigma for R(D*)) and can be used to constrain New Physics scenarios such as two-Higgs-doublet models, supersymmetry, and leptoquark models.
