# Unbound spectra of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes predicted by the Gamow shell model

> The Gamow shell model has shown to efficiently describe weakly bound and unbound nuclear systems, as internucleon correlations and continuum coupling are both taken into account in this model. In the present work, we study neutron-dripline oxygen isotopes. It is hereby demonstrated that the presence of continuum coupling is important for the description of oxygen isotopes at dripline, and especially to assess the eventual bound or unbound character of $^{28}$O. Our results suggest that the ground state of $^{28}$O is weakly unbound and is similar to the narrow resonant $^{26}$O ground state. Predictions of weakly bound and resonance excited states in $^{24\text-26}$O are also provided. The asymptotes of the studied many-body states are analyzed via one-body densities, whereby the different radial properties of well bound, loosely bound, resonance states are clearly depicted.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the ground state of $^{28}$O is weakly unbound and similar to the narrow resonant $^{26}$O ground state, but this conclusion depends on the specific effective field theory (EFT) interaction used and requires either A-dependent or A-independent interactions with appropriate momentum cutoffs to reproduce experimental binding energies within about 100 keV.

## Method

The Gamow shell model (GSM) was applied using a $^{22}$O core with valence neutrons in the sdpf model space, employing a Woods-Saxon potential for the one-body mean field and a leading-order pionless EFT nucleon-nucleon interaction. The EFT interaction strength was optimized by fitting the binding energies of $^{24-26}$O, and the Berggren basis was used to include continuum coupling via bound, resonance, and scattering one-body states discretized along a complex-momentum contour. Natural orbitals were employed to reduce the model space dimension, allowing calculations with up to 3p-3h truncations.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of $^{28}$O is located about 700 keV above the ground state of $^{24}$O across all three successful interaction sets (A-dependent EFT(318) and A-independent EFT(356), EFT(390), EFT(436))
- The two-neutron separation energy of $^{26}$O is about 20 keV, reproducing the experimental value of 18 keV
- The optimized EFT interaction strength $C_{S0}$ ranges from $-0.0449$ to $-0.0572 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV$^{-2}$ for the sdpf space depending on the momentum cutoff (318 to 436 MeV)
- The exponent parameter for the A-dependent two-body factor is $e = 0.4$
- The momentum truncation for the Berggren contour is $k_{\text{max}} = 5$ fm$^{-1}$
- The largest theory-experiment differences in calculated energies are about 350 keV for $^{23-26}$O nuclei
- Calculated widths of unbound states are of order 10 to 100 keV, not exceeding 240 keV

## Context

This work extends previous GSM calculations on neutron-rich oxygen isotopes that used the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) interaction (PhysRevC.96.024308), which overbound $^{26}$O and gave theoretical uncertainties of about 500 keV. The use of EFT interactions improves agreement with experimental data compared to the FHT framework, as was also found for proton-rich isotones of $^{16}$O (PhysRevC.100.064303). The results are consistent with continuum shell model (CSM) calculations using the USDB interaction (PhysRevLett.94.052501) and with coupled-cluster (CC) calculations (PhysRevLett.108.242501), but provide better agreement for the two-neutron separation energy of $^{26}$O than ab initio IM-SRG calculations (Holt 2019, PhysRevLett.118.032502) which show theory-experiment differences of about 2 MeV. The predictions enable further experimental searches for the unbound $^{28}$O ground state and its four-neutron decay via $2n$-$2n$ emission through the $^{26}$O ground state.
