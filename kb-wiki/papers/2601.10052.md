# Revisiting the nuclear island of negative hexadecapole deformations in A$\approx$180 mass region: focusing on moments of inertia and quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling

> For even-even nuclei $^{180-184}$Yb, $^{182-186}$Hf and $^{184-188}$W located on an island of hexadecapole-deformation archipelago, the structure properties, especially under rotation, are reinvestigated by using the Hartree-Fock-Bogliubov-Cranking (HFBC) calculation with a fixed shape (e.g., the ground-state equilibrium shape). The equilibrium deformations, extracted from the potential energy surface, are calculated based on the phenomenological Woods-Saxon mean-field Hamiltonian within the framework of macroscopic-microscopic (MM) model. The impact of different deformation degrees of freedom on, e.g., single-particle levels, total energy, and moment of inertia, is revealed, especially concentrating on the hexadecapole-deformation effects and the quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling. Considering the axially hexadecapole deformation, the present calculations can well reproduce available experimental data, including the quadrupole deformations and moments of inertia. Interestingly, it is found that the impact of different deformation degrees of freedom on moment of inertia exhibits a similar trend in the HFBC and rigid-body calculations though the latter ignores the pairing effects. Before starting or constructing a complex theory-model, to some extent, such a similarity can provide an alternative way of understanding the effect of, e.g., exotic deformations, on moment of inertia by the calculation of a simple rigid-body approximation. The present findings could offer insights into the static and dynamic effects of hexadecapole deformations, contributing valuable information for the corresponding research in nuclear structure and reaction.

## Key claim

The paper revisits the nuclear "island" of negative hexadecapole deformations in the A≈180 mass region and finds that the impact of different deformation degrees of freedom on the moment of inertia exhibits a similar trend in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov-Cranking (HFBC) calculations and rigid-body calculations, even though the latter ignores pairing effects; this similarity suggests that a simple rigid-body approximation can provide an alternative way to understand the effect of exotic deformations on the moment of inertia before constructing a more complex theory model.

## Method

The authors performed macroscopic-microscopic (MM) model calculations using a Woods-Saxon mean-field Hamiltonian to extract equilibrium deformations from potential energy surfaces for even-even nuclei in the A≈180 region. They then carried out Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov-Cranking (HFBC) calculations with fixed ground-state equilibrium shapes, incorporating the Lipkin-Nogami pairing treatment with approximate particle number projection, to study the effects of quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations on single-particle levels, total energy, and moment of inertia. The rigid-body moment of inertia was computed numerically using Gauss-Legendre quadrature from the nuclear shape defined by spherical harmonic multipole expansion.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zhang 2022) which identified an archipelago of at least 7 islands of hexadecapole-deformation nuclei on the nuclear chart, and follows their earlier investigations of positive hexadecapole-deformation islands in the A≈150 and A≈230 mass regions (Song 2023, Wei 2024). The calculations are compared with theoretical predictions by Möller et al. (1995, 2016) for ground-state deformations, and the findings could offer insights into static and dynamic effects of hexadecapole deformations relevant to nuclear structure and reaction studies, including applications to superheavy element production and neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements as discussed by Engel (2017).
