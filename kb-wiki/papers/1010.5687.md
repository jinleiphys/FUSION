# Effective Potential and Phase Diagram in the Strong-Coupling Lattice QCD with Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order and Polyakov Loop Effects

> We investigate chiral and deconfinement transitions in the strong coupling lattice QCD for color SU(3). We combine the leading order Polyakov loop effective action of the strong coupling expansion and the next-to-next-to-leading order (1/g^4) fermionic effective action with one species of unrooted staggered fermion. Two approximation schemes are adopted to evaluate the Polyakov loop effects; a Haar measure method (no fluctuation from the mean field) and a Weiss mean-field method (with fluctuations). The Polyakov loop is found to suppress the chiral condensate and to reduce the chiral transition temperature at mu = 0. The chiral transition temperature roughly reproduces the Monte Carlo results in the region beta = 2N_c /g^2 < 4.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop suppresses the chiral condensate and reduces the chiral transition temperature at zero chemical potential, and the resulting chiral transition temperature roughly reproduces Monte Carlo results in the region β = 2N_c/g^2 ≲ 4, but only when both Polyakov loop effects and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) finite coupling corrections are included.

## Method

The authors combine the leading order Polyakov loop effective action from the strong coupling expansion with the next-to-next-to-leading order (1/g^4) fermionic effective action for one species of unrooted staggered fermion in color SU(3) lattice QCD. They evaluate the temporal link integral using two approximation schemes: a Haar measure method (no fluctuation from the mean field) and a Weiss mean-field method (with fluctuations), then derive an analytic expression for the effective potential at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Chiral transition temperature at μ=0 is reduced when Polyakov loop effects are included, compared to SC-LQCD without Polyakov loop effects
- The chiral transition temperature roughly reproduces Monte Carlo results in the region β = 2N_c/g^2 ≲ 4
- The Polyakov loop effective action is of order O(1/g^{2N_τ}), where N_τ is the temporal lattice size
- The NNLO quark effective action is of order O(1/g^4)
- The leading order Polyakov loop action contains a factor (1/g^2 N_c)^{N_τ} N_c^2 d in the coupling β_p

## Context

This work extends previous strong coupling lattice QCD studies at NLO and NNLO (Nakano et al., 2010) by incorporating Polyakov loop effects to describe both chiral and deconfinement transitions simultaneously. It builds on the approach of Fukushima (2003) and the Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and aims to improve upon Monte Carlo results from deForcrand (2009) and other MC studies. The framework enables future investigation of the full QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and chemical potential.
