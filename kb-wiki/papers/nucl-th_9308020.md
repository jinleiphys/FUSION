# Self-Consistent Relativistic Calculation of Nucleon Mean Free Path

> We present a fully self-consistent and relativistic calculation of the nucleon mean free path in nuclear matter and finite nuclei. Starting from the Bonn potential, the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results for nuclear matter are parametrized in terms of an effective $σ$-$ω$ Lagrangian suitable for the relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) approximation. The nucleon mean free path in nuclear matter is derived from this effective Lagrangian taking diagrams up to fourth-order into account. For the nucleon mean free path in finite nuclei, we make use of the density determined by the RDHF calculation in the local density approximation. Our microscopic results are in good agreement with the empirical data and predictions by Dirac phenomenology.

## Key claim
The paper presents a fully self-consistent relativistic calculation of the nucleon mean free path in nuclear matter and finite nuclei, starting from the Bonn potential as the realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The authors hedge that their results are in "reasonable agreement" with empirical data given the "large ambiguities" in the empirical determination, rather than the abstract's stronger claim of "good agreement."

## Method
The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) results for nuclear matter, obtained from the Bonn potential, were parametrized into an effective σ-ω Lagrangian with density-dependent coupling constants suitable for the relativistic density-dependent Hartree-Fock (RDHF) approximation. The nucleon self-energy (optical potential) was derived from this effective Lagrangian including diagrams up to fourth order (Hartree, Fock, and two-particle-one-hole contributions). The nucleon mean free path was then calculated from the complex optical potential via a dispersion relation, using the local density approximation for finite nuclei with densities from RDHF calculations of ⁴⁰Ca.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation from DBHF with Bonn A: 0.185 fm⁻³ density with energy per nucleon ℰ/A = -15.6 MeV.
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- In the center of ⁴⁰Ca, the nucleon mean free path is about 2-5 fm, depending on nucleon energy (T_lab = 150, 300, and 450 MeV).
- The effective coupling constants for sigma and omega mesons drop with increasing density; those based on Bonn C decrease more with increasing density than those from Bonn A or B.

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent relativistic approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Brockmann and Toki (1992) by applying the effective Lagrangian derived from DBHF to calculate the nucleon mean free path, rather than only nuclear matter properties. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy in earlier non-relativistic calculations (Negele and Yazaki, 1981) that underestimated the mean free path by up to a factor of two, and provides a microscopic foundation for results previously obtained only through Dirac phenomenology (Cooper et al., 1990; Clark et al., 1990). The approach enables future studies of temperature-dependent mean free paths relevant to heavy-ion reactions.
