# Novel halos in light kaonic nuclei as an indicator of nuclear equation of state at supra-normal densities

> The sensitive correlations between the low-density halo structure and the high-density properties of the nuclear equation of state (EOS) are constructed in light kaonic nuclei with the relativistic mean-field theory. More specifically, the $1p_{1/2}$ halo spreads out linearly with increasing the pressure and sound velocity square at supra-normal densities and quadratically with decreasing the incompressibility at saturation density. These results suggest that the novel halo in light kaonic nuclei can serve as a sensitive indicator of the nuclear EOS of symmetric matter at supra-normal densities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the radius of the $1p_{1/2}$ neutron halo in light kaonic nuclei (specifically $^{13}_{K^-}$C) correlates linearly with the pressure and sound velocity square at supra-normal densities (2-2.5$\rho_0$) and quadratically with the incompressibility at saturation density, but only under the condition that the $K^-$ optical potential depth is set to -100 MeV at saturation density, consistent with heavy-ion collision extractions.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set as a starting point, modifying the strengths of meson self-interacting terms to generate different nuclear equations of state (EOS) in two schemes: Scheme A varies the high-density stiffness (sound velocity square $v_s^2$ at 2.5$\rho_0$) while keeping saturation properties fixed, and Scheme B varies the incompressibility $\kappa$ at saturation density while keeping similar high-density behavior. The $K^-$ meson is introduced via a Lagrangian with meson-$K^-$ couplings, where $g_{\omega K}$ and $g_{\rho K}$ are fixed by SU(3) relations and $g_{\sigma K}$ is adjusted to produce a $K^-$ optical potential depth of -100 MeV at saturation density. The Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equation for $K^-$ are solved self-consistently for the system $^{13}_{K^-}$C, with $^{13}$C as the seed nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The $1p_{1/2}$ neutron radius in $^{13}_{K^-}$C ranges from 3.30 fm (for $v_s^2$=0.38 at 2.5$\rho_0$) to 4.30 fm (for $v_s^2$=0.63 at 2.5$\rho_0$), compared to 3.16 fm in normal $^{13}$C without $K^-$.
- The core radius of $^{13}_{K^-}$C shrinks to between 1.97 fm and 2.08 fm depending on the EOS, compared to 2.34 fm in normal $^{13}$C.
- The maximum nuclear density in $^{13}_{K^-}$C reaches up to 2.62$\rho_0$ (for $\kappa$=210.0 MeV) and as low as 2.35$\rho_0$ (for $v_s^2$=0.38), where $\rho_0 = 0.148$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The $1p_{1/2}$ single-neutron binding energy in $^{13}_{K^-}$C varies from 1.41 MeV (for $v_s^2$=0.63) to 5.05 MeV (for $v_s^2$=0.38), compared to 8.45 MeV in normal $^{13}$C.
- The incompressibility $\kappa$ at saturation density ranges from 210.0 MeV to 290.0 MeV in Scheme B, with corresponding $1p_{1/2}$ halo radii from 3.68 fm to 3.21 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of exotic nuclear structures by demonstrating that kaonic nuclei, previously investigated for their high-density cores (e.g., by Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002) and by Dote et al. (2007)), can also exhibit novel halo formation due to the strongly attractive $K^-$N interaction. The results contradict the conventional understanding that halos are restricted to nuclei near the drip lines (e.g., as reviewed by Jensen and Riisager (1998) and by Tanihata (2005)), showing instead that halos can form in $\beta$-stable nuclei when a $K^-$ meson is implanted. The work enables a new method to constrain the high-density nuclear EOS through measurements of low-density halo properties in kaonic nuclei, potentially avoiding the large uncertainties (up to 50% relative error) from heavy-ion reactions and neutron star observations.
