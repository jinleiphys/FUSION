# Non-equilibrium evolution of quarkonium in medium in the open quantum system approach

> In this proceedings contribution, I review recent work that aims to provide a more comprehensive and systematic understanding of bottomonium dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma using an open quantum system (OQS) approach that is applied in the framework of the potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) effective field theory and coupled to realistic hydrodynamical backgrounds that have been tuned to soft hadron observables. I review how the computation of bottomonium suppression can be reduced to solving a Gorini-Kossakowski-Sudarshan-Lindblad (GKSL) equation for the evolution of the b-bbar reduced density matrix, which includes both singlet and octet states plus medium-induced transitions between them at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the binding energy over temperature. Finally, I present comparisons of phenomenological predictions of the NLO OQS+pNRQCD approach and experimental data for bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow in LHC 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions obtained using both smooth and fluctuating hydrodynamic initial conditions.

# Non-equilibrium evolution of quarkonium in medium in the open quantum system approach

## Key claim
The NLO OQS+pNRQCD framework provides a good description of existing experimental data for bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow in LHC 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, but the amount of $\Upsilon(2S)$ suppression is slightly overestimated regardless of hydrodynamic initial conditions used, which could indicate a breakdown of the pNRQCD framework for excited states that are larger in radius.

## Method
The authors derived a Gorini-Kossakowski-Sudarshan-Lindblad (GKSL) equation accurate to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the binding energy over temperature for the evolution of the $b\bar{b}$ reduced density matrix, including both singlet and octet states plus medium-induced transitions between them. They solved this GKSL equation using the Monte Carlo quantum trajectories algorithm implemented in the QTraj code, coupled to 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics with both smooth optical Glauber and fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions. The evolution was performed on a one-dimensional lattice with 2048 points and a time step of 0.001 GeV$^{-1}$, starting in-medium evolution at $\tau_{\rm med} = 0.6$ fm and terminating when the local temperature dropped below $T_F = 190$ MeV.

## Key numbers
- Bottom quark mass taken as $M = m_b = m_{\Upsilon(1S)}/2 = 4.73$ GeV
- Strong coupling evaluated at inverse Bohr radius: $\alpha_{\rm s}(1/a_0) = 0.468$ (1-loop running with $N_f=3$ and $\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}}^{N_f=3}=332$ MeV)
- Heavy-quark momentum-diffusion coefficient $\hat{\kappa}(T) = \kappa/T^3$ used in three parameterizations: $\hat{\kappa}_L(T)$, $\hat{\kappa}_C(T)$, and $\hat{\kappa}_U(T)$ from lattice data
- Dispersive coefficient $\hat{\gamma} = \gamma/T^3$ constrained in range $-3.5 \leq \hat\gamma \leq 0$, with best fit value $\hat\gamma = -2.6$
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ elliptic flow integrated in 10-90% centrality and $p_T < 50$ GeV: $v_2[1S] = 0.005 \pm 0.002 \pm 0.001$ (statistical $\pm$ systematic)
- Lattice parameters: NUM = 2048 points, L = 40 GeV$^{-1}$, dt = 0.001 GeV$^{-1}$
- In-medium evolution started at $\tau_{\rm med} = 0.6$ fm, terminated at $T_F = 190$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the OQS+pNRQCD framework from leading order to next-to-leading order in binding energy over temperature (Brambilla 2022), building on earlier derivations of the GKSL equation for heavy-quarkonium reduced density matrices (Brambilla 2017, Brambilla 2019). The approach enables phenomenological comparisons with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations for both the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Future work will assess the full impact of dynamical quantum jumps, compute higher anisotropic flow coefficients with fluctuating hydrodynamical backgrounds, and go beyond the underlying Coulomb potentials to study effects on excited states.
