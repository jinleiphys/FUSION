# Evaluation of the resonance enhancement effect in neutrinoless double-electron capture in 152Gd, 164Er and 180W atoms

> We study the resonant neutrinoless double-electron capture (0nECEC) in 152Gd, 164Er and 180W atoms, associated with the ground-state to ground-state nuclear transitions. The corresponding matrix elements are calculated within the deformed QRPA using the realistic Bonn-CD nucleon-nucleon interaction. The half-lives are estimated with the use of the most recent precision data on the Q-values of these processes. Perspectives of experimental search for the 0nECEC with the isotopes 152Gd, 164Er and 180W are discussed.

## Key claim

The resonance enhancement effect in neutrinoless double-electron capture (0νECEC) for 152Gd, 164Er, and 180W is evaluated using deformed QRPA calculations, which show that nuclear deformation suppresses the nuclear matrix elements by a factor of 2-3 compared to the spherical approximation; consequently, the half-life of 152Gd is 2-3 orders of magnitude longer than that of 0νββ decay of 76Ge for the same Majorana neutrino mass, and 164Er and 180W are excluded from the list of prospective isotopes.

## Method

The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for ground-state to ground-state 0νECEC transitions in 152Gd, 164Er, and 180W were calculated within the proton-neutron deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (deformed QRPA) using the realistic Bonn-CD nucleon-nucleon interaction. Single-particle states were described by an axially symmetric Woods-Saxon mean field, and the BCS equations were solved to obtain occupation amplitudes and quasiparticle energies. The electron wave functions in the atoms were treated in the relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock approximation, and the half-lives were estimated using recent precision Penning-trap mass spectrometry data on the Q-values.

## Key numbers

- For 152Gd with the capture of electrons from K and L shells (1s1/2 and 2s1/2 holes), the 0νECEC half-life range is 4.7 × 10^28 to 4.8 × 10^29 years for an effective Majorana neutrino mass mββ = 50 meV.
- For 164Er with 2s1/2 and 2s1/2 electron holes, the half-life range is 7.5 × 10^32 to 8.4 × 10^32 years for mββ = 50 meV.
- For 180W with 1s1/2 and 1s1/2 electron holes, the half-life range is 1.3 × 10^31 to 1.8 × 10^31 years for mββ = 50 meV.
- The mass difference Δ for 152Gd (1s1/2, 2s1/2 capture) is -0.83 ± 0.18 keV; for 164Er (2s1/2, 2s1/2 capture) is -6.82 ± 0.12 keV; for 180W (1s1/2, 1s1/2 capture) is -11.24 ± 0.27 keV.
- The deformed QRPA NME M0ν for 152Gd is 3.23 (for M2νGT = 0.10 MeV-1) compared to 7.59 in spherical QRPA; for 164Er it is 2.64 compared to 6.12; for 180W it is 2.05 compared to 5.79.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Simkovic and Krivoruchenko (2008) and Krivoruchenko et al. (2011) by incorporating deformed QRPA calculations for the nuclear matrix elements, going beyond the spherical approximation used in earlier estimates. The study uses recent precision Q-value measurements from Penning-trap mass spectrometry by Eliseev et al. (2011) for 152Gd, Eliseev et al. (2011) for 164Er, and Droese et al. (2011) for 180W. The results enable experimentalists to focus on 152Gd as the only viable candidate among the three isotopes for further 0νECEC searches, while excluding 164Er and 180W from the list of promising isotopes.
