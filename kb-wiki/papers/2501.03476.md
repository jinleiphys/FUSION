# Strange mesons with one dynamical gluon: A light-front approach

> We obtain the mass spectra of strange mesons using a light-front quantized Hamiltonian with Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) input, incorporating quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock components, along with a three-dimensional confinement. We work within the basis light-front quantization framework. The resulting eigenvectors can simultaneously describe the kaon's electromagnetic form factor, decay constant, distribution amplitude, and quark and gluon distribution functions under QCD scale evolution. Using the obtained kaon parton distribution functions (PDFs), supplemented by established nuclear PDFs, we also predict the kaon-nucleus-induced Drell-Yan cross section, which is expected to be measured soon by COMPASS++/AMBER at CERN.

## Key claim

The authors obtain mass spectra and parton distributions of strange mesons from a light-front Hamiltonian that includes both quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-gluon Fock components, but the results depend on fitting two additional parameters (the strange quark mass and the vertex interaction mass) to the experimental masses of just two strangeonium states, \(\phi(1020)\) and \(f_1(1420)\).

## Method

The authors solve the light-front stationary Schrodinger equation within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, using a Hamiltonian that combines the light-front QCD Hamiltonian with a three-dimensional confining potential in the \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) sector. The Fock space is truncated to include only \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) and \(|q\bar{q}g\rangle\) components, with basis states discretized in longitudinal momentum (via antiperiodic/periodic boundary conditions) and expanded in two-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions for transverse degrees of freedom. The strange quark masses \(m_s\) and \(m_{f_s}\) are fitted to the \(\phi(1020)\) and \(f_1(1420)\) masses, while all other parameters are taken from a prior study of unflavored mesons (Lan 2021).

## Key numbers

*   Kaon decay constant: \(f_K^{\rm{BLFQ}} = 156.9\) MeV, compared to the experimental value \(f_K^{\rm{Exp.}} = 155.6 \pm 0.4\) MeV.
*   Model scale for kaon PDF evolution: \(\mu_{0K}^2 = 0.42 \pm 0.04\) GeV\(^2\), determined by fitting the evolved \(u_v^K/u_v^{\pi}\) ratio to CERN-NA3 data.
*   At \(\mu^2 = 20\) GeV\(^2\), the valence up quark PDF falls as \((1-x)^{2.24}\) and the valence strange antiquark PDF as \((1-x)^{1.72}\).
*   First moments of kaon PDFs at \(\mu^2 = 20\) GeV\(^2\): \(\langle x\rangle_u = 0.21 \pm 0.01\), \(\langle x\rangle_{\bar{s}} = 0.26 \pm 0.01\), \(\langle x\rangle_{\rm sea} = 0.11 \pm 0.01\), \(\langle x\rangle_{\rm gluon} = 0.42 \pm 0.02\).

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach previously applied to the pion (Lan 2021) to strange mesons by introducing two additional parameters for the strange quark. It provides a unified description of kaon electromagnetic form factor, decay constant, distribution amplitudes, and parton distribution functions, with predictions for the kaon-nucleus Drell-Yan cross section that will be tested by the COMPASS++/AMBER experiment at CERN. The results differ from broader and flatter distribution amplitudes obtained via Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter equations (Roberts 2021) and agree with lattice QCD predictions (Alexandrou 2021) for the first moments of kaon PDFs.
