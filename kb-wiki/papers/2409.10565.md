# Heavy flavor angular correlations as a direct probe of the glasma

> We use classical equations of motion for heavy quarks to show that the pre-equilibrium glasma phase of a heavy ion collision has an extremely strong effect on heavy quark angular correlations. At the same time, the effect on the single inclusive spectrum is much more moderate. This suggests that $D\overline{D}$ meson angular correlations in future LHC measurements could provide direct experimental access to the physics of the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium glasma phase of a heavy ion collision has an extremely strong effect on heavy quark angular correlations, while the effect on the single inclusive spectrum is much more moderate. This suggests that D overline D meson angular correlations in future LHC measurements could provide direct experimental access to the physics of the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Method

The authors use classical Yang-Mills equations solved numerically on a real-time lattice to simulate the evolving glasma background fields, with the saturation scale set to Q_s = 2 GeV for central heavy ion collisions at LHC energies. Heavy quarks are treated as classical test particles whose time evolution on this background is described by Wong's equations, solved using the leapfrog method implemented in the publicly available curraun solver. Charm quark pairs are initialized back-to-back at formation time tau_form = 1/(2 m_T) with initial transverse momentum spectrum from FONLL, and their evolution is followed up to proper time tau = 0.3 fm/c, which is a typical switching time to hydrodynamical or kinetic theory descriptions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale Q_s = 2 GeV used as a realistic value for central heavy ion collisions at LHC energies
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA evaluated at proper time tau = 0.3 fm, which is a typical switching time to hydrodynamical or kinetic theory descriptions
- Charm quark pair correlations extracted at proper times Delta tau in {0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5} fm/c, showing rapid decorrelation during the first Delta tau ~ 0.05 to 0.1 fm/c
- Initial charm quark transverse momentum set to p_T(tau_form) = 2 GeV for the main correlation results, with additional results shown for p_T(tau_form) = 1 GeV
- Gaussian p_T-broadening model with width sigma = 0.3 Q_s tuned to glasma simulations describes the effect on R_AA

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark transport in the glasma using classical transport equations (Avramescu 2023) and analytical approximations (Carrington 2020, 2021), as well as studies of heavy quark angular correlations in the equilibrium plasma phase (Nahrgang 2013, Zhao 2024). The results demonstrate that the glasma stage produces azimuthal decorrelation widths comparable to those reported for c overline c or b overline b pairs in the QGP, meaning current QGP models that ignore pre-equilibrium dynamics rely on tuned parameters that would need re-tuning if the glasma stage were included. The work enables future comparisons with D overline D correlation measurements at the HL-LHC (ALICE 2022) and suggests that combining glasma effects with subsequent kinetic theory and hydrodynamical evolution is necessary for proper interpretation of these measurements.
