# Precision mass measurements in the zirconium region pin down the mass surface across the neutron midshell at $N=66$

> Precision mass measurements of $^{104}$Y, $^{106}$Zr, $^{104,104m,109}$Nb, and $^{111,112}$Mo have been performed with the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the Ion Guide Isotope Separator On-Line facility. The order of the long-lived states in $^{104}$Nb was unambiguously established. The trend in two-neutron separation energies around the $N=66$ neutron midshell appeared to be steeper with respect to the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 extrapolations for the $_{39}$Y and $_{40}$Zr isotopic chains and less steep for the $_{41}$Nb chain, indicating a possible gap opening around $Z=40$. The experimental results were compared to the BSkG2 model calculations performed with and without vibrational and rotational corrections. All of them predict two low-lying minima for $^{106}$Zr. While the unaltered BSkG2 model fails to predict the trend in two-neutron separation energies, selecting the more deformed minima in calculations and removing the vibrational correction, the calculations are more in line with experimental data. The same is also true for the $2^+_1$ excitation energies and differences in charge radii in the Zr isotopes. The results stress the importance of improved treatment of collective corrections in large-scale models and further development of beyond-mean-field techniques.

# Precision mass measurements in the zirconium region pin down the mass surface across the neutron midshell at N=66

## Key claim

The two-neutron separation energy trends around the N=66 neutron midshell are steeper than Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 extrapolations for Y (Z=39) and Zr (Z=40) isotopic chains but less steep for the Nb (Z=41) chain, indicating a possible gap opening around Z=40, but only when the more deformed minima are selected in BSkG2 calculations and the vibrational correction is removed does the model become more consistent with experimental data.

## Method

Precision mass measurements were performed using the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the IGISOL facility, with isotopes produced via 25-MeV proton-induced fission on a natural uranium target. The time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance technique with 50 ms quadrupolar excitation was used for 109Nb and 112Mo, while the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance technique was employed for the other nuclides, with cyclotron frequencies measured relative to 133Cs reference ions.

## Key numbers

- 104Y mass excess: -53995(16) keV, differing by 85(200)# keV from the AME20 extrapolation of -54080(200)# keV
- 106Zr mass excess: -58582.7(43) keV, differing by 167(200)# keV from the AME20 extrapolation of -58750(200)# keV and by 33(173) keV from the earlier measurement of -58550(173) keV by Knobel (2016)
- 104Nb ground state mass excess: -71812.4(18) keV, agreeing with the Canadian Penning Trap value of -71811.0(18) keV by Orford (2018)
- 104Nb isomer mass excess: -71802(7) keV, consistent with the Canadian Penning Trap value of -71801.2(19) keV by Orford (2018)
- 109Nb mass excess: -56810(180) keV, compared to the AME20 extrapolation of -56690(430) keV
- 111Mo mass excess: -59939(9) keV, agreeing with the AME20 value of -59940(13) keV
- 112Mo mass excess: -57449(118) keV, agreeing with the recent MR-TOF-MS measurement of -57470(8) keV by Hou (2023)
- BSkG2 model local average deviation for 75 known masses in the region: -0.447 MeV (model overbinds on average)
- BSkG2 model local root-mean-square deviation: 0.866 MeV, compared to the global accuracy of 0.687 MeV
- 110Zr first 2+ excitation energy: 185(11) keV, with excitation energy ratio R = E(4_1+)/E(2_1+) = 3.1(2)

## Context

This work extends precision mass measurements into the poorly known N=66 neutron midshell region for refractory elements around Z=40, where previous mass measurements of 105Y and 106Zr by Knobel (2016) were deemed anomalous and removed from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020. The results complement recent mass measurements in the Sr chain by Mukul (2021) at TITAN and in the Ru and Rh chains by Hukkanen (2023, 2023b), and they enable benchmarking of the BSkG2 model by Ryssens (2022, 2023) against experimental data in a region of shape coexistence. The findings highlight the need for improved treatment of collective corrections in large-scale models and motivate further development of beyond-mean-field techniques to handle regions where multiple configurations compete.
