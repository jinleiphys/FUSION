# Strange Mesonic Transition Form Factor in the Chiral Constituent Quark Model

> The form factor $g_{ρπ}^{(S)}(Q^{2})$ of the strange vector current transition matrix element $<ρ| \bar{s}γ_\mus|π>$ is calculated within the chiral quark model. A strange vector current of the constituent $U$- and D-quarks is induced by kaon radiative corrections and this mechanism yields the nonvanishing values of $g_{ρπ}^{(S)}(0)$. The numerical result at the photon point is consistent with the one given by the $ϕ$-meson dominance model, but the fall-off in the $Q^{2}$-dependence is faster than the monopole form factor. Mesonic radiative corrections are also examined for the electromagnetic $ρ$-to-$π$ and $K^{*}$-to-$K$ transition amplitudes.

## Key claim

The strange vector current transition form factor \(g_{\rho\pi}^{(S)}(Q^{2})\) is calculated within the chiral constituent quark model, where the nonvanishing value at the photon point arises from kaon radiative corrections inducing a strangeness polarization of the nonstrange constituent quarks. The result at \(Q^{2}=0\) is consistent with the \(\phi\)-meson dominance model, but the fall-off with \(Q^{2}\) is faster than the monopole form factor, and at the kinematics of the planned \(^{4}\)He parity-violating experiment (\(Q^{2}=0.6\) GeV\(^{2}/c^{2}\)) the chiral quark model prediction is about 30% smaller than the vector meson dominance model.

## Method

The authors compute the strange vector current transition matrix element \(\langle\rho|\bar{s}\gamma_\mu s|\pi\rangle\) using the chiral constituent quark model, where Goldstone bosons couple to constituent quarks. The strange vector current of the constituent \(U\)- and \(D\)-quarks is induced by kaon loop diagrams (Figs. 1b-1g), which are the lowest order in \(1/N_C\). The loop momenta are cut off at the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda \sim 1\) GeV using a gauge-invariant monopole form factor at the quark-meson vertex. The hadronic matrix element is evaluated using light-cone quark model wave functions with a Gaussian momentum distribution, and the strange anomalous magnetic moment \(f_2^{(S)}(0)\) of the constituent quarks is related to the transition form factor.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_{\rho\pi}^{(S)}(0) = -0.21\) for cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV, and \(-0.27\) for \(\Lambda = 1.2\) GeV.
*   The strange anomalous magnetic moment of the constituent quark is \(f_2^{(S)}(0) = -0.030\) for \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV, and \(-0.041\) for \(\Lambda = 1.2\) GeV.
*   At \(Q^{2}=0.6\) GeV\(^{2}/c^{2}\) (the kinematics of the TJNAF experiment E-91-004), the chiral quark model prediction for \(g_{\rho\pi}^{(S)}(Q^{2})\) is about 30% smaller than the vector meson dominance model prediction.
*   The electromagnetic decay widths computed with mesonic radiative corrections are: \(\Gamma_{th}(\rho^{+}\rightarrow\pi^{+}\gamma)=0.069\) MeV (experiment: \(0.068 \pm 0.007\) MeV), \(\Gamma_{th}(K^{+*}\rightarrow K^{+}\gamma)=0.057\) MeV (experiment: \(0.050 \pm 0.005\) MeV), and \(\Gamma_{th}(K^{0*}\rightarrow K^{0}\gamma)=0.122\) MeV (experiment: \(0.117 \pm 0.01\) MeV).
*   The mesonic radiative corrections to the electromagnetic transition amplitudes are about 6-10% of the leading term.

## Context

This work extends the study of strangeness content in hadrons beyond the nucleon to meson transition form factors, complementing earlier hadronic calculations using the \(\phi\)-meson dominance model (Musolf and Donnelly, 1993) and the \(K^{*}K\) loop model (Goity and Musolf, 1996). The result is directly relevant to the interpretation of parity-violating electron scattering from \(^{4}\)He (TJNAF experiment E-91-004), where the \(\rho\)-to-\(\pi\) transition meson exchange current may have comparable magnitude to the one-body nucleon strangeness form factor (Musolf, Schiavilla, and Donnelly, 1994). The calculation enables an estimate of the model dependence in the corresponding meson exchange current correction, which is needed for a reliable extraction of the nucleon's strange electric form factor \(G_E^S\).
