# Screening of nuclear pairing in nuclear and neutron matter

> The screening potential in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ pairing channels in neutron and nuclear matter in different approximations is discussed. It is found that the vertex corrections to the potential are much stronger in nuclear matter than in neutron matter.

## Key claim

The authors find that medium polarization (screening) effects on nuclear pairing are dramatically stronger in symmetric nuclear matter than in neutron matter, with the one-bubble approximation showing anti-screening (additional attraction) in the \(^1S_0\) channel of nuclear matter, but the full RPA resummation with induced interaction yields such a large enhancement that the resulting pairing force may be unphysical and the many-body scheme may not be converged.

## Method

The screening potential for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) pairing channels was calculated in both neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter using the Gogny D1 force for the external vertices and for the Landau parameters describing the particle-hole residual interaction. The particle-hole bubble series was summed to all orders via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the RPA approximation, with the residual interaction expressed in terms of Landau parameters; the mechanical instability of nuclear matter was cured by applying the Babu-Brown induced interaction approximation (IIA) to renormalize the Landau parameters. The screening potential was decomposed into one-bubble exchange (Fig. 1b) and full RPA with induced interaction (Fig. 1c), and the diagonal matrix elements at zero energy and equal momenta were compared with the bare Gogny Born term.

## Key numbers

* In neutron matter, the bare \(^1S_0\) pairing interaction is screened by 20 to 30% (from Table 1: at \(k_F=1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(N_0 V_{\text{bare}} = -0.41\) vs. \(N_0^* Z^2 (V_{\text{bare}}+V_{\text{scr.}}) = -0.26\)).
* In nuclear matter \(^1S_0\) channel at \(k_F=0.7\) fm\(^{-1}\), the dimensionless pairing interaction including self-energy and screening is \(-2.46\), compared to the bare value of \(-0.43\) (Table 1), indicating a strong anti-screening effect.
* In the \(^3S_1\) channel of nuclear matter at \(k_F=1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), the bare interaction is \(-0.95\) and the screened value (with self-energy) is \(-0.70\) (Table 1), showing a net repulsive screening relative to the bare term.
* At \(k_F=0.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) in neutron matter, the one-bubble induced interaction contributes 7.93 MeV to the pairing potential, while the bare interaction is \(-36.7\) MeV.
* The bare \(np\) gap in symmetric nuclear matter at its maximum is about 8 MeV, while the \(nn\) gap is 2.5 MeV (from the introduction, citing prior work).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of screening in pure neutron matter (Shen et al., 2004) to symmetric nuclear matter, and also addresses the \(^3S_1\) neutron-proton pairing channel. It confirms the well-known result that polarization quenches \(^1S_0\) pairing in neutron matter (Clark et al., 1976; Ainsworth et al., 1989; Schulze et al., 1996), but finds that in nuclear matter the proton polarization produces a large attractive contribution, reversing the trend. The results are compared with the low-density studies of Gori et al. (2003) and Schwenk et al. (2003), and the authors note that the strong anti-screening in nuclear matter may challenge the validity of the local density approximation (LDA) for finite nuclei, as previously tested for RPA by Schuck and Jaenicke (1990).
