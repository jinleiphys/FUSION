# Ab initio estimation of $E2$ strengths in $^8$Li and its neighbors by normalization to the measured quadrupole moment

> For electric quadrupole ($E2$) observables, which depend on the large-distance tails of the nuclear wave function, ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations converge slowly, making meaningful predictions challenging to obtain. Nonetheless, the calculated values for different $E2$ matrix elements, particularly those involving levels with closely-related structure (e.g., within the same rotational band) are found to be robustly proportional. This observation suggests that a known value for one observable may be used to determine the overall scale of $E2$ strengths, and thereby provide predictions for others. In particular, we demonstrate that meaningful predictions for $E2$ transitions may be obtained by calibration to the ground-state quadrupole moment. We test this approach for well-measured low-lying $E2$ transitions in $^7$Li and $^9$Be, then provide predictions for transitions in $^8$Li and $^9$Li. In particular, we address the $2^+\rightarrow1^+$ transition in $^8$Li, for which the reported measured strength exceeds ab initio Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) predictions by over an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that _ab initio_ no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations of electric quadrupole ($E2$) observables, which converge slowly due to sensitivity to wave function tails, can yield meaningful predictions by normalizing to the experimentally measured ground-state quadrupole moment, provided the dimensionless ratio $B(E2)/(eQ)^2$ is used to cancel systematic truncation errors.

## Method

The authors performed NCCI calculations for lithium and beryllium isotopes using three internucleon interactions: Daejeon16, JISP16, and LENPIC. They computed the dimensionless ratio $B(E2)/(eQ)^2$ for transitions between low-lying states, demonstrating that this ratio converges rapidly with respect to basis truncation parameters $\Nmax$ and oscillator parameter $\hbar\omega$, even when the individual $B(E2)$ and quadrupole moment values are poorly converged. The converged ratio was then calibrated to the experimentally known ground-state quadrupole moment to obtain absolute $B(E2)$ predictions.

## Key numbers

- For $^7$Li: experimental $B(E2; 3/2^-\rightarrow 1/2^-)=8.3(5)\,e^2\fm^4$ and $Q(3/2^-)=-4.00(3)\,\fm^2$; the NCCI ratio $B(E2)/(eQ)^2\approx0.50$ yields $B(E2)\approx8\,e^2\fm^4$, consistent with the experimental ratio of $0.52(3)$.
- For $^9$Be: NCCI ratio $B(E2;3/2^-\rightarrow5/2^-)/[eQ(3/2^-)]^2\approx1.3\text{--}1.4$ yields $B(E2)\approx36\text{--}39\,e^2\fm^4$.
- For $^8$Li: the NCCI predictions give $B(E2; 2^+\rightarrow 1^+)/[eQ(2^+)]^2\approx0.1\text{--}0.2$; calibrated to $Q(2^+)=+3.14(2)\,\fm^2$, this yields $B(E2)\approx1\text{--}2\,e^2\fm^4$, more than an order of magnitude smaller than the reported experimental value of $55(15)\,e^2\fm^4$.
- For $^9$Li: NCCI ratio $B(E2;3/2^-\rightarrow 1/2^-)/[eQ(3/2^-)]^2\approx0.5\text{--}0.6$ yields $B(E2)\approx4.6\text{--}5.5\,e^2\fm^4$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Calci and Roth (2016), who used a well-measured $E2$ strength to predict an excited-state quadrupole moment, by instead using the ground-state quadrupole moment as the calibration reference. The paper addresses the anomalously large $E2$ strength reported for the $2^+\rightarrow1^+$ transition in $^8$Li by Brown (1991), which exceeds Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) predictions by Pastore (2013) by over an order of magnitude. The present NCCI results support the GFMC predictions and suggest the reported experimental strength may require reexamination. The method enables _ab initio_ predictions for $E2$ transitions in nuclei where only the ground-state quadrupole moment is known experimentally.
