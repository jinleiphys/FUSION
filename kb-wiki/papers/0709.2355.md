# A systematic expansion for relativistic causal hydrodynamics

> A systematic expansion of the Boltzmann equation for the diffusion of dilute tracers, in powers of the Knudsen number, carried out to next-to-leading order (NLO), gives the relativistic causal diffusion equation (Kelly's equation). Using dimensionless combinations of dynamical quantities, we show when the small NLO term plays a crucially important role. We proceed to show that a derivation of Kelly's equation from a microscopic theory of the correlation function of the number density of diffusers is possible. The correlator fulfils the Green-Kubo relation for the diffusion constant, as well as an f-sum which goes beyond a purely phenomenological causal theory. We argue that the construction generalizes to the full hydrodynamic equations.

# A systematic expansion for relativistic causal hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The next-to-leading order (NLO) expansion in the Knudsen number K necessarily restores relativistic causality in the diffusion equation by shielding the problematic early-time region where K is of order unity, but the resulting Kelly's equation is only valid when the expansion parameter K is small; at times when the front and wake have not clearly separated, higher-order effects or the full Boltzmann equation may be needed.

## Method
The author expands the Boltzmann equation for dilute tracer particles in powers of the Knudsen number K = τ_R/τ, where τ_R is the relaxation time, carrying the expansion to next-to-leading order (O(K)). This yields Kelly's equation, a hyperbolic diffusion equation with a second-order time derivative term. The author then derives the same equation from a microscopic correlation function approach using a memory kernel (relaxation time ansatz) and shows that the resulting spectral function satisfies both the Green-Kubo relation for the diffusion constant and an f-sum rule that relates the relaxation time to the mean-square velocity of the tracer particles.

## Key numbers
- The diffusion constant D is given by D = τ_R c_s^2, where c_s^2 is the RMS velocity of the tracer particles.
- The relative importance of the causal term to the frictional term is given by K = τ_R/τ, so the causal term arises at NLO in K.
- The Mach-like number M^2 = (L/(c_s τ))^2 characterizes the causal front, with M = 1 corresponding to the diffusive analogue of the sonic front.
- Causality is restored for τ > τ_* where K_* = τ_R/τ_* ≤ M_c^2/W, with M_c the value of M when V = c and W the Wiener number.
- The first moment of the spectral function gives ⟨ω⟩ = D/τ_R, and via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, 2mT = D/τ_R where T is temperature and m the diffuser mass.

## Context
This work extends the causal hydrodynamics program initiated by Eckart (1940) and Landau (1959), who cast Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics into covariant form but found acausality, and by Israel and Stewart (1979), who provided a phenomenological causal theory. The paper specifically builds on Kelly's derivation of the relativistic causal diffusion equation and addresses two open questions: whether the restoration of causality at NLO is necessary rather than accidental, and how to derive Kelly's equation from microscopic correlation functions. The analysis enables systematic construction of effective long-time theories from microscopic computations (e.g., from QCD using weak-coupling methods, lattice computations, or AdS/CFT correspondence) by relating new material constants to transport coefficients through f-sum rules, improving upon the Israel-Stewart formulation.
