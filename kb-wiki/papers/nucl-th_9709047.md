# Relativistic transport theory of N, Δand N^{*}(1440) interacting through $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ mesons

> A self-consistent relativistic integral-differential equation of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck-type for the $N^{*}$(1440) resonance is developed based on an effective Lagrangian of baryons interacting through mesons. The closed time-path Green's function technique and semi-classical, quasi-particle and Born approximations are employed in the derivation. The non-equilibrium RBUU-type equation for the $N^{*}$(1440) is consistent with that of nucleon's and delta's which we derived before. Thus, we obtain a set of coupled equations for the $N$, $Δ$ and $N^{*}$(1440) distribution functions. All the $N^{*}$(1440)-relevant in-medium two-body scattering cross sections within the $N$, $Δ$ and $N^{*}$(1440) system are derived from the same effective Lagrangian in addition to the mean field and presented analytically, which can be directly used in the study of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The theoretical prediction of the free $pp \to pp^{*}(1440)$ cross section is in good agreement with the experimental data. We calculate the in-medium $N + N \to N + N^{*}$, $N^{*} + N \to N + N$ and $N^{*} + N \to N^{*} + N$ cross sections in cold nuclear matter up to twice the nuclear matter density. The influence of different choices of the $N^{*}N^{*}$ coupling strengths, which can not be obtained through fitting certain experimental data, are discussed. The results show that the density dependence of predicted in-medium cross sections are sensitive to the $N^{*}N^{*}$ coupling strengths used. An evident density dependence will appear when a large scalar coupling strength of $g_{N^{*}N^{*}}^σ$ is assumed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation for the \(N^{*}(1440)\) resonance, coupled to existing equations for nucleons and \(\Delta\) baryons, using an effective Lagrangian with \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\pi\) mesons. The authors claim that the in-medium cross sections involving the \(N^{*}(1440)\) are sensitive to the unknown \(N^{*}N^{*}\) coupling strengths, and that a large scalar coupling \(g_{N^{*}N^{*}}^{\sigma}\) produces an evident density dependence, but the quantitative predictions for heavy-ion collisions depend on assumptions about these couplings which cannot be fixed by experiment.

## Method

The authors employed the closed time-path Green's function technique with semi-classical, quasi-particle, and Born approximations to derive an RBUU-type transport equation for the \(N^{*}(1440)\) distribution function. They constructed the collision term from Born diagrams of the \(N^{*}\) self-energy, obtaining analytical expressions for all in-medium two-body scattering cross sections within the \(N\), \(\Delta\), and \(N^{*}(1440)\) system. The centroid mass of the \(N^{*}(1440)\) was introduced via a Breit-Wigner distribution to account for its decay width, and the in-medium cross sections for \(N+N \rightarrow N+N^{*}\), \(N^{*}+N \rightarrow N+N\), and \(N^{*}+N \rightarrow N^{*}+N\) were calculated in cold nuclear matter up to twice nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- The free \(pp \rightarrow pp^{*}(1440)\) cross section predicted by the model agrees with experimental data; the ratio of coupling strengths used is \(g_{NN^{*}}^{\pi} / g_{NN}^{\pi} = 0.351\).
- The centroid mass of the \(N^{*}(1440)\) is defined using a Breit-Wigner function with a central mass \(M_0 = 1440\) MeV and a decay width \(\Gamma_0 = 200\) MeV.
- In-medium cross sections were calculated in cold nuclear matter up to twice nuclear matter density (\(2\rho_0\)).
- Three cases for the \(N^{*}N^{*}\) coupling strengths were considered: (1) \(\alpha(N^{*}) = \beta(N^{*}) = 1\); (2) \(\alpha(N^{*}) = \beta(N^{*}) = M_{N^{*}} / M_N\); (3) \(\alpha(N^{*}) = 1\), \(\beta(N^{*}) = M_{N^{*}} / M_N\), where \(\alpha = g_{N^{*}N^{*}}^{\omega} / g_{NN}^{\omega}\) and \(\beta = g_{N^{*}N^{*}}^{\sigma} / g_{NN}^{\sigma}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent RBUU derivations for nucleons (Mao et al., 1994-1997) and \(\Delta\) baryons (Mao et al., 1996) to include the \(N^{*}(1440)\) resonance, providing a unified transport framework for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with earlier studies by Huber and Aichelin (1994) who calculated the free \(N+N \rightarrow N+N^{*}(1440)\) cross section using a one-boson-exchange model, and with Cugnon's parameterization (1981) of free \(NN\) cross sections commonly used in transport models, which the authors argue are inadequate for in-medium resonance-relevant channels. The derived cross sections enable more realistic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at SIS to AGS energies, particularly for subthreshold antiproton production where the \(N^{*}(1440)\) is reported to be as important as the \(\Delta(1232)\).
