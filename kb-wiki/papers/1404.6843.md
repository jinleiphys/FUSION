# Second order fluid dynamics for the unitary Fermi gas from kinetic theory

> We compute second order transport coefficients of the dilute Fermi gas at unitarity. The calculation is based on kinetic theory and the Boltzmann equation at second order in the Knudsen expansion. The second order transport coefficients describe the shear stress relaxation time, non-linear terms in the strain-stress relation, and non-linear couplings between vorticity and strain. An exact calculation in the dilute limit gives $τ_R=η/P$, where $τ_R$ is the shear stress relaxation time, $η$ is the shear viscosity, and $P$ is pressure. This relation is identical to the result obtained using the Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) approximation to the collision term, but other transport coefficients are sensitive to the exact collision integral.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact calculation of second-order transport coefficients for the unitary Fermi gas in the dilute limit, showing that the shear stress relaxation time satisfies τ_R = η/P identically to the BGK approximation, but other second-order coefficients (λ_1, λ_2, λ_3) are sensitive to the exact collision integral and differ from the BGK result.

## Method

The calculation uses kinetic theory based on the Boltzmann equation expanded to second order in the Knudsen number, with the Chapman-Enskog method applied to solve for the distribution function. The collision integral is treated exactly at leading order in the fugacity (dilute limit) using s-wave scattering at unitarity (a → ∞), and the second-order stress tensor is matched to the general form of conformal fluid dynamics to extract the transport coefficients τ_R, λ_1, λ_2, and λ_3.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in the dilute limit: η = (15/(32√π)) (mT)^(3/2)
- Shear stress relaxation time: τ_R = η/P, where P is pressure
- λ_1 = (15 η^2)/(14 P)
- λ_2 = -η^2/P
- λ_3 = 0
- The first-order Sonine polynomial coefficient a₀ = -(15π/(32√2)) (m/(zT)), where z is the fugacity
- The scalar second-order collision integral C²_L = (4 T^(11/2))/(9 π^(5/2) m^(3/2))
- Corrections to λ_i from higher-order Sonine polynomials are less than 2%

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic theory calculations of shear viscosity for the unitary Fermi gas by Bruun (2005) and second-order fluid dynamics by Chao (2011), providing the first exact calculation of second-order transport coefficients beyond the BGK approximation. The result τ_R = η/P confirms the BGK estimate derived by Bruun (2007), Braby (2010), and Chao (2011), while the exact values for λ_1, λ_2, and λ_3 enable improved modeling of the transition between hydrodynamic and ballistic regimes in trapped atomic gas experiments, particularly for describing the dilute corona where the Knudsen number becomes large.
