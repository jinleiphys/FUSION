# Bayesian analysis of heavy ion collisions with the heavy ion computational framework Trajectum

> We introduce a model for heavy ion collisions named Trajectum, which includes an expanded initial stage with a variable free streaming velocity $v_{\rm fs}$ and a hydrodynamic stage with three varying second order transport coefficients. We describe how to obtain a Gaussian Emulator for this 20-parameter model and show results for key observables. This emulator can be used to obtain Bayesian posterior estimates on the parameters, which we test by an elaborate closure test as well as a convergence study. Lastly, we employ the optimal values of the parameters found in [1] to perform a detailed comparison to experimental data from PbPb and $p$Pb collisions. This includes both observables that have been used to obtain these values as well as wider transverse momentum ranges and new observables such as correlations of event-plane angles.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the Trajectum framework for heavy-ion collisions, which includes a variable free-streaming velocity \(v_{\rm fs}\) in the prehydrodynamic stage and three varying second-order transport coefficients, but the Bayesian posterior estimates and parameter constraints are presented in a companion paper (Nijs et al., 2020) rather than derived here; the present work focuses on model description, emulator construction, closure tests, and comparison to experimental data using previously obtained optimal parameter values.

## Method

The authors built a 20-parameter model combining T\(_{\rm R}\)ENTo initial conditions with nucleon substructure, a free-streaming prehydrodynamic phase with variable velocity \(v_{\rm fs}\), and 2+1D second-order viscous hydrodynamics using the 14-moment approximation with a lattice QCD/HRG hybrid equation of state and temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities. They constructed a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 1000 design points for PbPb and 2000 for \(p\)Pb collisions, validated it with closure tests and convergence studies, and then used the maximum a posteriori parameter values from their companion paper to compute observables for PbPb at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV and \(p\)Pb at 5.02 TeV, including identified particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section set to 63 mb for 2.76 TeV and 70 mb for 5.02 TeV collisions
- Free-streaming velocity \(v_{\rm fs} \approx 0.93\) minimizes deviations from hydrodynamics for the bulk viscous pressure; \(v_{\rm fs} \approx 0.6\) minimizes deviations for the shear tensor
- Switching temperature \(T_{\rm switch}\) varied in the Bayesian analysis; the lattice-to-HRG transition lies in the region 165–200 MeV, with consistent HRG EoS below 165 MeV
- Shear viscosity parametrized with \((\eta/s)_{\rm min}\), \((\eta/s)_{\rm slope}\), \((\eta/s)_{\rm crv}\); bulk viscosity parametrized with \((\zeta/s)_{\rm max}\), \((\zeta/s)_{\rm width}\), \((\zeta/s)_{T_0}\) with \(T_c = 154\) MeV
- Fixed second-order coefficients set to kinetic theory values: \(\delta_{\Pi\Pi}/\tau_\Pi = 2/3\), \(\lambda_{\Pi\pi}/[\tau_\Pi(1/3-c_s^2)] = 8/5\), \(\delta_{\pi\pi}/\tau_\pi = 4/3\), \(\phi_7 P = 9/70\), \(\lambda_{\pi\Pi}/\tau_\pi = 6/5\)
- Emulator error for charged pion multiplicity in the 1.8–2.2 GeV \(p_T\) bin for 0–20% centrality improves with logarithmic scaling; for \(v_2\{4\}\) at 20–30% centrality statistical error dominates above about 250 design points
- 10,000 minimum bias events yield centrality determination errors up to 10% for multiplicity; the authors use \(10^5\) T\(_{\rm R}\)ENTo simulations to improve centrality binning

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis approach pioneered by Bernhard (2019) and collaborators, who used the T\(_{\rm R}\)ENTo model with fixed free-streaming velocity \(v_{\rm fs}=1\) and provided the most precise temperature-dependent QGP shear viscosity estimate to date. The Trajectum framework enlarges the parameter space by allowing variable \(v_{\rm fs}\) and three second-order transport coefficients, and includes additional experimental observables such as transverse-momentum-dependent identified particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients for both PbPb and \(p\)Pb collisions. This enables testing whether hydrodynamic modeling can describe low-momentum data in \(p\)Pb collisions and assessing the sensitivity of extracted QGP properties to the prehydrodynamic parametrization, with the companion paper (Nijs et al., 2020) providing the actual Bayesian posterior estimates on the 20 parameters.
