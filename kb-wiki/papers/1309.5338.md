# Non-perturbative quantum time evolution on the light-front

> This paper introduces "time-dependent basis light-front quantization", which is a covariant, nonperturbative, and first principles numerical approach to time-dependent problems in quantum field theory. We demonstrate this approach by evaluating photon emission from an electron in a strong, time and space dependent external field. We study the acceleration of the electron in combination with the evolution of the invariant mass of the electron-photon system, in real time and at the amplitude level. Successive excitations of the electron-photon system are evident.

## Key claim

This paper introduces "time-dependent basis light-front quantization" (tBLFQ) as a covariant, nonperturbative numerical method for time-dependent quantum field theory problems, demonstrated through photon emission from an electron in a strong external field; the method relies on two approximations (basis truncation and time-step discretization) and is shown for a background field that does not obey Maxwell's equations in vacuum, serving as a proof of concept rather than a phenomenological study.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Schrodinger equation for an initial physical electron state using an interaction picture where the "free" Hamiltonian is the full QED Hamiltonian and the interaction is the external background field. They construct QED eigenstates via Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) using a discrete basis of Fock states with two-dimensional harmonic oscillator transverse modes, truncated to single-electron and electron-photon sectors. Time evolution is implemented numerically by decomposing the time-evolution operator into small steps in light-front time using a second-order difference scheme (MSD2), with repeated computation of matrix elements between eigenstates transformed from the BLFQ basis.

## Key numbers

- Background field intensity parameter: \(a_0 = 0.1\) (dimensionless)
- Longitudinal momentum of initial electron: \(K_i = 1.5\)
- Transverse truncation parameter: \(N_\text{max} = 8\)
- Longitudinal box length: \(L = 2\pi\) MeV\(^{-1}\)
- Background longitudinal frequency: \(l_- = 1\) MeV (period \(2\pi\) MeV\(^{-1}\) in \(x^-\) direction)
- Background time frequency: \(l_+ = 2\pi/50\) MeV (period 50 MeV\(^{-1}\) in \(x^+\) direction)
- Time step size: \(\delta x^+ = 0.02\) MeV\(^{-1}\)
- Background on-time: from \(x^+ = 0\) to \(x^+ = 400\) MeV\(^{-1}\)
- Invariant mass of initial peak in electron-photon states: approximately 0.85 MeV
- Invariant mass of second peak in \(K=3.5\) segment: approximately 1.1 MeV
- Invariant mass of peak in \(K=5.5\) segment: approximately 0.95 MeV
- Period of approximate oscillations between \(K=1.5\) and \(K=3.5\) single-electron states: 100 MeV\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) by Vary et al. (2009) from stationary state problems to time-dependent dynamics, addressing strong-field QED where analytic results are limited to constant backgrounds or one-dimensional field inhomogeneities as reviewed by Di Piazza et al. (2011). The method enables nonperturbative treatment of backgrounds with two-dimensional spacetime inhomogeneities, and the authors suggest future applications to jet propagation in heavy-ion collisions modeled as dissipative background media, building on the success of AdS/QCD approaches to hadron spectroscopy by de Teramond and Brodsky (2008).
