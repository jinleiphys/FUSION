# Light-front gauge propagator

> Gauge fields are special in the sense that they are invariant under gauge transformations and they lead to problems when we try quantizing them straightforwardly. To circumvent this problem we need to specify a gauge condition to fix gauge.

# Light-front gauge propagator

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new Lagrange multiplier term of the form $(n\cdot A)(\partial \cdot A)$ in the Lagrangian density for light-front gauge that completely fixes the gauge, eliminating residual gauge freedom, and yields a propagator that includes an additional contact term proportional to $n^\mu n^\nu / (k\cdot n)^2$ compared to the standard two-term light-front propagator.

## Method
The author constructs the gauge field propagator by inverting the differential operator in momentum space that results from adding the proposed gauge-fixing term $-\frac{1}{\alpha}(n\cdot A)(\partial \cdot A)$ to the Abelian gauge field Lagrangian density. The propagator is obtained by solving $O_{\mu\nu}G^{\nu\lambda}(k)=\delta_\mu^\lambda$ using the most general tensor structure built from $g^{\mu\nu}$, $k^\mu$, $n^\mu$, and $m^\mu$, then imposing the light-front condition $n^2=0$ and taking the limit $\alpha\rightarrow 0$.

## Key numbers
- The standard two-term light-front propagator (from axial gauge fixing $n\cdot A=0$ with $\alpha\rightarrow 0$ and $n^2=0$): $G^{\mu\nu}(k)=-\frac{1}{k^2}\left[ g^{\mu\nu}-\frac{k^\mu n^\nu+n^\mu k^\nu}{k\cdot n}\right]$
- The proposed complete propagator (from $(n\cdot A)(\partial \cdot A)$ gauge fixing with $n^2=0$ and $\alpha\rightarrow 0$): $G^{\mu\nu}(k)=-\frac{1}{k^2}\left\{ g^{\mu\nu}-\frac{k^\mu n^\nu+n^\mu k^\nu}{k\cdot n}+\frac{n^\mu n^\nu}{(k\cdot n)^2}k^2\right\}$
- The covariant Lorentz gauge propagator: $G^{\mu\nu}(k)=-\frac{1}{k^2}\left\{ g^{\mu\nu}-(1-\alpha)\frac{k^\mu k^\nu}{k^2}\right\}$, with $\alpha=1$ for Feynman gauge and $\alpha=0$ for Landau gauge

## Context
This work extends the light-front gauge formalism originally introduced by Dirac (1949) and addresses the problem of residual gauge freedom that remains when using only the light-front condition $n\cdot A=0$. The resulting propagator coincides with that of Srivastava and Brodsky (2002), where the additional contact term was shown not to affect the beta function for Yang-Mills theory while preserving the Ward-Takahashi identity $Z_1=Z_3$. The complete gauge fixing enables proper treatment of the so-called "unphysical" pole that appears in light-front Feynman integrals, which Pimentel and Suzuki (1990) showed is actually physical and necessary for causality.
