# Hadronic Decays and Baryon Structure

> Relativistic constituent quark models generally describe three-quark systems with particular interactions. The corresponding invariant mass eigenvalue spectra and pertinent eigenstates should exhibit the multiplet structure anticipated for baryon resonances. Taking into account the flavour content, spin structure, and spatial distribution of the baryon wave functions together with mass relations of the eigenvalues and decay properties of the eigenstates, we can link the theoretical mass eigenstates with the experimentally measured resonances. The resulting classification of baryon resonances differs in some respects from the one suggested by the Particle Data Group. With regard to the hadronic decay widths of light and strange baryon resonances a consistent picture emerges only, if the classification includes two-star resonances.

## Key claim

Relativistic constituent quark models (CQMs) can produce a consistent classification of light and strange baryon resonances into flavour multiplets, but only if two-star (less well established) resonances are included; the resulting classification differs in several respects from the Particle Data Group (PDG) scheme, and the ordering of the three \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) \(\Sigma\) states depends on which CQM (one-gluon-exchange or Goldstone-boson-exchange) is used.

## Method

The authors calculated invariant mass eigenvalue spectra and eigenstates for light and strange baryons using relativistic one-gluon-exchange (OGE) and Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) constituent quark models. They then classified the theoretical mass eigenstates into flavour multiplets by comparing the flavour content, spin structure, spatial distribution, mass relations, and hadronic decay widths (computed via the point-form spectator model) with experimentally measured resonances, including two-star resonances.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Sigma(1750)\) is identified as the third \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) excitation in the GBE CQM and is assigned to a flavour decuplet with 94% decuplet content.
- The \(\Lambda(1810)\) is classified as a flavour singlet with 92% singlet content, contrary to the PDG octet assignment.
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) has 72% octet flavour content; the \(\Lambda(1690)\) has 72% octet content; both couple strongly to singlet states.
- The \(\Xi(1950)\) is classified as \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) with 100% octet content, differing from a recent assignment as \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\) by Zhou and Ma (2006).
- The ordering of the three \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\) \(\Sigma\) states is octet-decuplet-octet for the OGE CQM and octet-octet-decuplet for the GBE CQM.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations of \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) decay widths of \(N\) and \(\Delta\) resonances by Melde et al. (2005) and similar Bethe-Salpeter studies by the Bonn group (Metsch 2003, 2004). It contradicts the PDG classification (2006) which omits one- and two-star resonances and largely follows the 1974 scheme of Samios et al. The classification is nearly identical to that of Guzey and Polyakov (2005), except for the \(\Lambda(1810)\). The results enable future relativistic studies including explicit mesonic degrees of freedom, as already attempted in the meson sector by Krassnigg (2003, 2004).
