# Evidence for Some New Hyperon Resonances -- to be Checked by $K_L$ Beam Experiments

> Quenched and unquenched quark models predict very different patterns for the spectrum of the low excited hyperon states. Evidence is accumulating for the existence of some new hyperon resonances, such as a $Σ^*$ of spin-parity $J^P=1/2^-$ around 1400 MeV instead of 1620 MeV as listed in PDG, a new $Σ(1540)3/2^-$ resonance, a new narrow $Λ(1670)3/2^-$ resonance and a new $Λ(1680)3/2^+$ resonance. All these new hyperon resonances fit in the predicted pattern of the unquenched quark models very well. It is extremely important to check and establish the spectrum of these low excited hyperon states by the proposed $K_L$ beam experiments at JLAB.

## Key claim

Evidence is accumulating for the existence of several new hyperon resonances that fit the pattern predicted by unquenched quark models, but these results are not yet conclusive and require confirmation by proposed $K_L$ beam experiments at JLAB.

## Method

The paper reviews recent analyses of new data from Crystal Ball (CB), LEPS, and CLAS experiments, combined with older $\overline{K}N$ reaction data, using effective Lagrangian approaches and coupled-channel partial-wave analyses to extract hyperon resonance parameters. The author specifically highlights analyses of $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ and $K^-p\to\pi^0\Sigma^0$ reactions with polarization data to determine the spin-parity and masses of $\Sigma^*$ and $\Lambda^*$ resonances.

## Key numbers

- A $\Sigma^*$ resonance with $J^P=1/2^-$ is suggested to be in the range of 1380 to 1500 MeV, rather than the PDG-listed 1620 MeV
- A $\Sigma(3/2^-)$ resonance is found with mass around 1542 MeV and width about 25.6 MeV from analysis of Crystal Ball data
- A new $\Lambda(1680)3/2^+$ resonance is claimed around 1680 MeV, replacing the contribution from the 4-star $\Lambda(1690)3/2^-$ in fits to $K^-p\to\pi^0\Sigma^0$ data
- A narrow $\Lambda(3/2^-)$ resonance around 1670 MeV with width about 1.5 MeV is required by analysis of $K^-p\to\eta\Lambda$ data, compared to the PDG $\Lambda(1690)3/2^-$ with width around 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the debate between quenched quark models (Capstick, 1990s) which predict the lowest $J^P=1/2^-$ $\Sigma$ resonance around 1650 MeV, and unquenched quark models (Zou, 2000s) which predict it around 1400 MeV. The new results also contradict the PDG listing of $\Sigma(1620)1/2^-$ and $\Lambda(1690)3/2^-$, instead supporting penta-quark dynamics predictions. The proposed $K_L$ beam experiments at JLAB could settle the spectrum of low excited hyperon states and provide complementary information to studies of penta-quark states with hidden charm.
