# Chiral two-pion exchange and proton-proton partial-wave analysis

> The chiral two-pion exchange component of the long-range pp interaction is studied in an energy-dependent partial-wave analysis. We demonstrate its presence and importance, and determine the chiral parameters c_i (i=1,3,4). The values agree well with those obtained from pion-nucleon amplitudes.

# Chiral two-pion exchange and proton-proton partial-wave analysis

## Key claim
The chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) component of the long-range proton-proton interaction is present and important, and its chiral parameters \(c_i\) (\(i=1,3,4\)) can be determined from pp scattering data alone, with values that agree with those obtained from pion-nucleon amplitudes, but only when the parameter \(c_1\) is fixed to a theoretical estimate derived from the pion-nucleon sigma term, because \(c_1\) and \(c_3\) are strongly correlated in the fits.

## Method
The authors performed energy-dependent partial-wave analyses (PWAs) of the Nijmegen 1998 pp database (1951 scattering data below 350 MeV) using the relativistic Schrodinger equation with a boundary condition at \(r=b\). The long-range strong potential outside the boundary was taken as either one-pion exchange (OPE) alone, OPE plus leading-order TPE, or OPE plus the full chiral TPE (leading-order plus subleading-order). The chiral parameters \(c_1, c_3, c_4\) from the subleading TPE potential were fitted to the data, with \(c_1\) fixed to \(-0.76\)/GeV from the pion-nucleon sigma term to break the correlation between \(c_1\) and \(c_3\).

## Key numbers
- With OPE plus chiral TPE at \(b=1.8\) fm: \(\chi^2_{\rm min}=1937.8\) with 22 boundary-condition parameters, compared to \(\chi^2_{\rm min}=1956.6\) with 29 parameters for OPE alone.
- With OPE plus chiral TPE at \(b=1.4\) fm: \(\chi^2_{\rm min}=1934.5\) with 23 boundary-condition parameters, compared to \(\chi^2_{\rm min}=2026.2\) with 31 parameters for OPE alone.
- Determined chiral parameters (with \(c_1\) fixed to \(-0.76\)/GeV): \(c_3 = -5.08(28)\)/GeV and \(c_4 = +4.70(70)\)/GeV, with statistical errors only.
- Fitted pion mass in the chiral TPE potential: \(m_\pi = 128(9)\) MeV, consistent with the average pion mass of 138.04 MeV.
- The standard Nijmegen 1998 pp PWA (using Nijm78 heavy-boson exchanges) gave \(\chi^2_{\rm min}=1968.7\) with 19 boundary-condition parameters and \(f^2_{pp\pi^0}=0.0756(4)\).

## Context
This work extends the Nijmegen energy-dependent PWA methodology, previously used to study OPE and electromagnetic interactions (Stoks 1993, Timmermans 1993, Bergervoet 1987), to the chiral two-pion exchange force derived from the effective chiral Lagrangian of Ordonez and van Kolck (1992). It demonstrates that the long-range pp interaction is governed by the same chiral Lagrangian as low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, with the chiral parameters agreeing with those from Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1997). This enables a new class of PWAs with a theoretically well-founded, model-independent chiral TPE potential, replacing the phenomenological heavy-boson exchanges of the Nijm78 potential (Nagels 1978) for the long-range part.
