# Charmonium production in $\bar p$-induced reactions on nuclei

> The production of charmonia in the antiproton-nucleus reactions at $p_{\rm lab}=3-10$ GeV/c is studied within the Glauber model and the generalized eikonal approximation. The main reaction channel is charmonium formation in an antiproton-proton collision. The target mass dependence of the charmonium transparency ratio allows to determine the charmonium-nucleon cross section. The polarization effects in the production of $χ_{c2}$ states are evaluated.

## Key claim

The target mass dependence of the charmonium transparency ratio in antiproton-nucleus reactions can determine the charmonium-nucleon cross section, but quantitative conclusions require careful accounting for empirical details of nuclear density profiles at the surface region.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber model calculations of charmonium production cross sections in antiproton-nucleus interactions, using the charmonium formation cross section from a Breit-Wigner resonance formula and accounting for antiproton and charmonium survival probabilities via exponential factors. For the \(\chi_{c2}\) polarization effects, they applied the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) with nondiagonal transition amplitudes between \(\chi_{cJ}\) states derived from the Clebsch-Gordan decomposition of physical states with fixed orbital and spin magnetic quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- \(J/\psi\) production on lead target at \(\bar p\)-beam momentum 4.07 GeV/c (on-shell peak) shows sensitivity to \(\sigma_{J/\psi N}\) values of 0, 3.5, and 6 mb
- \(\psi^\prime\) production on lead target at 6.23 GeV/c (on-shell peak) uses limiting cases \(\sigma_{\psi^\prime N}=0\) and 20 mb
- \(J/\psi\) formation length at \(p_{J/\psi} \simeq 4\) GeV/c is 0.4 fm, smaller than internucleon spacing \(d_{NN} \simeq 2\) fm
- \(\chi_{c2}\) helicity ratio shows \(\sim 30\%\) deviations from unity due to interference between direct \(\bar p p \to \chi_{c2}(0)\) and two-step \(\bar p p \to \chi_{c0}(0)\), \(\chi_{c0}(0) N \to \chi_{c2}(0) N\) amplitudes
- Cross sections for \(c \bar c\) states with fixed \(L_z\): \(\sigma_{\pm1}=15.9\) mb and \(\sigma_0=6.8\) mb, differing by a factor of two
- On-shell beam momenta for charmonium production on proton target: 4.07 GeV/c for \(J/\psi\), 6.23 GeV/c for \(\psi^\prime\), 5.194 GeV/c for \(\chi_{c0}\), 5.553 GeV/c for \(\chi_{c1}\), and 5.727 GeV/c for \(\chi_{c2}\)

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Brodsky (1988) and Farrar (1989) to use charmonium formation in \(\bar p p\) collisions on nuclear targets to determine genuine charmonium-nucleon cross sections, where the short formation length avoids complications from color transparency effects. The calculations use charmonium-nucleon cross section ranges from prior studies by Gerschel (1993), Kharzeev (1996), Gerland (1998), and Molina (2012), and the results are intended to inform planning for the forthcoming PANDA experiment at FAIR.
