# Single-phonon and multi-phonon excitations of the $γ$ vibration in rotating odd-$A$ nuclei

> Multi-phonon excitations in atomic nuclei were observed very rarely although collective motions in quantum many-body systems are described as bosonic excitations. In particular, the first two-phonon $γ$ vibrational ($2γ$) excitation in odd-$A$ nuclei was reported in 2006 and only a few have been known. Quite recently, conspicuously enhanced $B(E2)$s feeding $2γ$ states were observed in $^{105}$Nb and conjectured that their parent states are candidates of $3γ$ states. In the present work, the model space is enlarged from the present author's previous calculation for $^{103}$Nb. The purpose is twofold: One is to see how the description of $2γ$ states is improved, and the other is to examine the existence of collective $3γ$ states, and when they exist, study their collectivity through calculating interband $B(E2)$s. The particle-vibration coupling model based on the cranking model and the random-phase approximation is used to calculate the vibrational states in rotating odd-$A$ nuclei. Interband $B(E2)$s are calculated by adopting the method of the generalized intensity relation. The present calculation reproduces the observed spectra of $0γ$ - $2γ$ states well and gives collective $3γ$ states with enhanced $B(E2)$s to $2γ$ states in $^{103}$Nb and $^{105}$Nb. The most collective $3γ$ state with the highest $K$ at zero rotation is thought to be the main component of the observed band.

## Key claim

The particle-vibration coupling model with an enlarged model space (up to 4γ basis states) reproduces the observed 0γ–2γ states in ¹⁰³Nb and ¹⁰⁵Nb and predicts collective 3γ states with enhanced B(E2) values to 2γ states, though the calculated B(E2) ratios for 3γ→2γ transitions are smaller than the experimental values by factors of 2–3.

## Method

The author uses the particle-vibration coupling model based on the cranking model and the random-phase approximation (RPA) to calculate γ-vibrational states in rotating odd-A nuclei. The model space is enlarged from the previous calculation (which included up to 2γ basis states) to include up to 4γ basis states. Interband B(E2) transition probabilities are calculated using the generalized intensity relation method, which combines rotationally perturbed intrinsic matrix elements from the cranking model with the rotational effects described by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁰³Nb, including 3γ basis states pushes the upper 2γ band down by 0.27 MeV at ω_rot=0 and 0.23 MeV at ω_rot=0.3 MeV; including 4γ basis states pushes it down further by 0.06–0.03 MeV
- In ¹⁰³Nb, the calculated 2γ band remains 0.09–0.18 MeV higher than the experimental data
- In ¹⁰³Nb, the calculated B(E2) ratio B(E2:15/2⁺_2γ→11/2⁺_1γ)/B(E2:11/2⁺_1γ→7/2⁺_0γ) = 2.46, compared to the experimental value 1.53(16)
- In ¹⁰³Nb, the calculated B(E2) ratio B(E2:11/2⁺_3γ→15/2⁺_2γ)/B(E2:11/2⁺_1γ→7/2⁺_0γ) = 7.32, compared to the experimental value 13.5(11)
- In ¹⁰⁵Nb, the calculated B(E2) ratio B(E2:11/2⁺_3γ→15/2⁺_2γ)/B(E2:11/2⁺_1γ→7/2⁺_0γ) = 10.8, compared to the experimental value 27.5(33)
- In ¹⁰⁵Nb, the calculated B(E2) ratio B(E2:13/2⁺_3γ→17/2⁺_2γ)/B(E2:13/2⁺_1γ→9/2⁺_0γ) = 19.5, compared to the experimental value 40.5(55)
- The intrinsic quadrupole transition matrix element Q_tr for 3γ→2γ in ¹⁰³Nb is 0.3122 eb, giving Q_tr(3γ)/Q_tr(1γ) = 1.61 ≈ √3
- The zero-point amplitude γ_0 = 14° for ¹⁰³Nb and 13° for ¹⁰⁵Nb, both larger than the adopted static triaxial deformations (γ = -7° and -10°, respectively)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculation for ¹⁰³Nb (Matsuzaki, 2011) by enlarging the model space from 2γ to 4γ basis states and adding interband B(E2) calculations. It addresses the recent experimental observation of enhanced B(E2) values feeding 2γ states in ¹⁰⁵Nb, where band (4) was conjectured as a candidate 3γ state. The calculation is the first attempt to study realistic 3γ states in deformed nuclei. Prior theoretical work on ¹⁰³Nb using the triaxial projected shell model was reported by Sensharma, Bhattacharjee, and Pal (2010). The results enable future experimental confirmation of three-phonon γ-vibrational states in deformed odd-A nuclei and suggest that the most collective 3γ state with the highest K at zero rotation is observed with lowered K due to Coriolis mixing.
