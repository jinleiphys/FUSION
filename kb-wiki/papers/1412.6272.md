# Nucleon resonances in the $γp \to ϕK^+ Λ$ reaction near threshold

> We investigate the $γp \to ϕK^+ Λ$ reaction near threshold within an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model. Various nucleon resonances caused by the $π$ and $η$ meson exchanges and background contributions are considered. It is shown that the contribution from the $N^*(1535)$ resonance caused by the $η$ meson exchange plays the predominant role. Hence, this reaction provides a good new platform to study the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. The predicted total cross section and specific features of angular distributions can be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The $\gamma p \to \phi K^+ \Lambda$ reaction near threshold is dominated by the $N^*(1535)$ resonance via $\eta$ meson exchange, provided the ratio $R \equiv |g_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)}/g_{\eta N N^*(1535)}|$ is taken as 1.0 for simplicity; the dominance holds for $R$ in the range 0.5–2.6 reported in prior literature.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with the isobar model, computing tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $\gamma p \to \phi K^+ \Lambda$ reaction. They included $\pi$, $\eta$, and $K$ meson exchanges exciting intermediate nucleon resonances $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, and $N^*(1720)$, plus background contributions from the $s$-channel nucleon pole, $t$-channel $K$ exchange, and a contact term to maintain gauge invariance. Coupling constants were fixed from known partial decay widths, and dipole form factors with cutoff parameters $\Lambda_\pi = \Lambda_\eta = 1.3$ GeV, $\Lambda_K = 0.8$ GeV, and $\Lambda_{\text{baryon}} = 2.0$ GeV were applied.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section magnitude: approximately 0.2 nb at photon energy $E_\gamma = 3.3$–3.4 GeV.
- Coupling constant $g^2_{K\Lambda N^*(1535)} = 3.52$ (adopted from $R=1$ and $g^2_{\eta N N^*(1535)} = 0.28$ from Table 1).
- $g^2_{\phi\gamma\eta}$ is 26 times larger than $g^2_{\phi\gamma\pi}$ (values: $g^2_{\phi\gamma\eta}/4\pi = 4.01 \times 10^{-2}$, $g^2_{\phi\gamma\pi}/4\pi = 1.56 \times 10^{-3}$).
- Cutoff parameters: $\Lambda_\pi = \Lambda_\eta = 1.3$ GeV, $\Lambda_K = 0.8$ GeV, $\Lambda_N = \Lambda_{N^*(1535)} = \Lambda_{N^*(1650)} = \Lambda_{N^*(1710)} = \Lambda_{N^*(1720)} = 2.0$ GeV.
- $N^*(1535)$ resonance parameters: mass 1535 MeV, width 150 MeV, branching ratios $N\pi$ 0.45, $N\eta$ 0.42.

## Context

This work extends studies of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance, which has a long-standing mass inversion problem relative to the $N^*(1440)$ in the constituent quark model (Capstick 1986, PDG 2014). It builds on prior determinations of the coupling ratio $R$ from $J/\psi$ decays (Liu 2005, Geng 2008), photoproduction databases (Mart 2013), and $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda$ analysis (Wu 2014). The predicted cross sections and angular distributions (forward $\phi$, backward $K^+$) can be tested at JLab 12 GeV, enabling improved extraction of the $K\Lambda N^*(1535)$ coupling constant.
