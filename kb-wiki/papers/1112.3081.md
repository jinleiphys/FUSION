# Phenomenology of high gluon density QCD and heavy-ion physics at ISMD 2011: x smaller than ever !

> I provide a brief summary of the theory presentations at ISMD 2011 related to the phenomenology of small-x QCD evolution and its application to particle production and fluctuations in high-energy hadron and heavy-ion collisions. I also mention some challenges for quantitative phenomenology which emerged from the LHC, such as understanding the long-range "ridge" in high-multiplicity p+p collisions, the transverse momentum distributions in p+p at semi-hard pT, and the origin and scale of density fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper summarizes theory presentations at ISMD 2011, arguing that the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution with the AAMQS-modified initial condition (MV\(^\gamma\) with \(\gamma > 1\)) provides a good description of HERA DIS structure functions, RHIC forward hadron spectra, and LHC charged hadron multiplicities, but the MV\(^\gamma\) initial condition lacks a first-principles derivation and its dependence on nuclear thickness \(A^{1/3}\) is not understood.

## Method
The author reviews rcBK evolution solutions for the dipole scattering amplitude \(\mathcal{N}(r,x)\), which are Fourier-transformed to obtain the unintegrated gluon distribution \(\Phi(k_\perp,x)\). Single-inclusive hadron production is computed via \(k_\perp\)-factorization, convoluted with DSS gluon-to-hadron fragmentation functions. The initial conditions tested include the MV model (eq. 4) and the AAMQS MV\(^\gamma\) model (eq. 5), with comparisons to data from HERA, RHIC, and LHC.

## Key numbers
- The rcBK fit to HERA structure functions (AAMQS) uses an initial condition with \(\gamma > 1\) in \(\mathcal{N}_{MV^\gamma}(r,x_0) = 1 - \exp\left(-\frac{1}{4}(r^2 Q_s^2(x_0))^\gamma \log\frac{1}{r\Lambda}\right)\).
- Charged hadron transverse momentum distributions in \(p+p\) collisions at 7 TeV are computed with a \(K\) factor adjusted to fit data at \(p_\perp = 1\) GeV.
- The cross section for two jets with \(p_\perp > 10\) GeV separated by rapidity interval \(\Delta\eta\) and azimuthal angle \(\Delta\phi\) in \(p+p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV is shown (fig. 4).
- DIPSY Monte Carlo predicts \(v_2^4\{4\}\) (four-particle cumulant) is negative for high-multiplicity \(p+p\) collisions at 7 TeV, corresponding to non-flow correlations.

## Context
This work extends the CGC framework (GLR, 1983; CGC review, 2010) by testing rcBK evolution (Albacete, 2010; Kovchegov, 1999) against LHC data, specifically the CMS ridge in high-multiplicity \(p+p\) collisions and charged hadron \(p_\perp\) spectra. It highlights challenges for quantitative phenomenology: the need to understand the AAMQS parameter \(\gamma\) (Albacete et al., 2010), the role of density fluctuations from stochastic dipole splitting (Flensburg, 2011; Hatta, 2011), and the definition of \(N_{\rm coll}\) in \(R_{pA}\) (Potashnikova and Kopeliovich, 2011). The work enables future tests with \(p+A\) collisions at LHC and measurements at a future electron-ion collider (eIC).
