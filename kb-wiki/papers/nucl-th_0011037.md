# Chiral approach to the rho meson in nuclear matter

> In this work, the properties of the $ρ$ meson at rest in cold symmetric nuclear matter are studied. We make use of a chiral unitary approach to pion-pion scattering in the vector-isovector channel, calculated from the lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory ($χPT$) lagrangian including explicit resonance fields. Low energy chiral constraints are considered by matching our expressions to those of one loop $χPT$. To account for the medium corrections, the $ρ$ couples to $ππ$ pairs which are properly renormalized in the nuclear medium, accounting for both $p-h$ and $Δ-h$ excitations. The terms where the $ρ$ couples directly to the hadrons in the $p-h$ or $Δ-h$ excitations are also accounted for. In addition, the $ρ$ is also allowed to couple to $N^{*}(1520)-h$ components.

## Key claim

The properties of the rho meson at rest in cold symmetric nuclear matter are studied using a chiral unitary approach, finding that the rho meson mass is barely changed by medium effects while its width undergoes a large broadening, with the precise magnitude depending on the inclusion of vertex corrections required by gauge invariance and on the coupling to N*(1520)-hole components.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral unitary approach to pion-pion scattering in the vector-isovector channel, calculated from the lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory Lagrangian including explicit resonance fields, with low-energy chiral constraints satisfied by matching to one-loop ChPT. Medium corrections are incorporated by coupling the rho meson to pion-pion pairs that are renormalized in the nuclear medium via particle-hole and delta-hole excitations, including vertex corrections required by gauge invariance. The rho meson is also allowed to couple directly to hadrons in particle-hole and delta-hole excitations, as well as to N*(1520)-hole components.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant ratio a = 2 G_V^2 / f^2 takes the value approximately 0.7, indicating a 30% deviation from exact vector meson dominance.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' is set to 0.7 for short-range correlations in the pion selfenergy.
- The cut-off parameter for the monopole form factor at pi N N and pi N Delta vertices is set to Lambda = 1 GeV.
- The matching scale mu for the dimensional regularization constants is set to 770 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the scalar isoscalar sigma channel in nuclear matter (Chiang 1998, Oset 2000) to the vector-isovector rho meson channel. It builds upon earlier studies of rho meson medium modifications that found large broadening from coupling to delta-hole excitations (Asakawa 1992, Chanfray 1993, Herrmann 1993, Urban 1998) and from excitation of the N*(1520) resonance (Rapp 1997, Peters 1998). The results enable more reliable predictions for dilepton spectra measured at CERN and GSI, where the rho meson's large leptonic width makes it a clean probe of high-density nuclear matter.
