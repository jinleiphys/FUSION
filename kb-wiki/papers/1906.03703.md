# Ab initio calculations of p-shell nuclei up to N2LO in chiral Effective Field Theory

> Nuclear structure and reaction theory are undergoing a major renaissance with advances in many-body methods, realistic interactions with greatly improved links to Quantum Chromodynamics, the advent of high performance computing, and improved computational algorithms. State-of-the-art two- and three-nucleon interactions obtained from chiral Effective Field Theory provide a theoretical foundation for nuclear theory with controlled approximations. With highly efficient numerical codes, tuned to the current generation of supercomputers, we can perform ab-initio nuclear structure calculations for a range of nuclei to a remarkable level of numerical accuracy, with quantifiable numerical uncertainties. Here we present an overview of recent results for No-Core Configuration Interaction calculations of p-shell nuclei using these chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order, including three-body forces. We show the dependence of the ground state energies on the chiral order; we also present excitation spectra for selected nuclei and compare the results with experimental data.

# Ab initio calculations of p-shell nuclei up to N2LO in chiral Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Ground state energies and excitation spectra of p-shell nuclei up to A=12 calculated at N2LO with three-nucleon forces agree within a few percent of experimental values, but nuclei beyond A=12 become increasingly overbound, 16O is overbound by about 13%, and the origin of this overbinding remains unclear.

## Method
No-Core Configuration Interaction (NCCI) calculations were performed using a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by total oscillator quanta (Nmax), with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions from the LENPIC collaboration at LO, NLO, and N2LO using a semilocal coordinate-space regulator of R=1.0 fm. For calculations including three-nucleon forces, the Hamiltonian was first evolved via Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation to improve convergence, and ground state energies were extrapolated to the infinite-basis limit using an exponential fit to the three highest Nmax values.

## Key numbers
- 4He ground state energy at N2LO with 3NFs (SRG α=0.08 fm4): −28.298(2) MeV, compared to experimental −28.296 MeV
- 6Li ground state energy at N2LO with 3NFs (SRG α=0.08 fm4): −31.72(6) MeV, compared to experimental −31.99 MeV
- 12C ground state energy at N2LO with 3NFs (SRG α=0.08 fm4): −95.5(5) MeV, compared to experimental −92.16 MeV (overbound by about 3%)
- 16O ground state energy at N2LO with 3NFs (SRG α=0.08 fm4): −145.2(8) MeV, compared to experimental −127.62 MeV (overbound by about 13%)
- 3NF LECs fixed from 3H binding energy and nucleon-deuteron scattering: cD=7.2 and cE=−0.671
- SRG flow parameters used: α=0.04 fm4 and α=0.08 fm4

## Context
This work extends the systematic chiral EFT program of the LENPIC collaboration (Binder 2015, Binder 2018, Epelbaum 2018) from few-body systems to the full p-shell, using the semilocal coordinate-space regulated potentials of Epelbaum (2014). The calculations confirm earlier findings by Navratil (2007) and Jurgenson (2013) that 3NFs are necessary to reproduce the correct 3+ ground state of 10B. The results enable parameter-free predictions for A≥4 nuclei and identify specific states in 12B and 12C that are sensitive to the 3NF LECs cD and cE, providing targets for future interaction refinement. The overbinding of 16O and heavier nuclei indicates that either higher-order chiral contributions or induced many-body forces beyond 3NFs may be needed.
