# Fluid Dynamics and Viscosity in Strongly Correlated Fluids

> We review the modern view of fluid dynamics as an effective low energy, long wavelength theory of many body systems at finite temperature. We introduce the concept of a nearly perfect fluid, defined by a ratio $η/s$ of shear viscosity to entropy density of order $\hbar/k_B$ or less. Nearly perfect fluids exhibit hydrodynamic behavior at all distances down to the microscopic length scale of the fluid. We summarize arguments that suggest that there is fundamental limit to fluidity, and we review the current experimental situation of measurements of $η/s$ in strongly coupled quantum fluids.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the modern understanding of fluid dynamics as an effective low-energy, long-wavelength theory, and argues that there may be a fundamental lower bound on the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density ($\eta/s$), though the original conjecture that $\eta/s \geq 1/(4\pi)$ is now known to have counterexamples in certain holographic theories with higher-derivative corrections.

## Method

The paper surveys the theoretical foundations of fluid dynamics as an effective field theory, then examines three microscopic frameworks for computing transport coefficients: kinetic theory (Chapman-Enskog expansion), holographic duality (AdS/CFT correspondence applied to black hole spacetimes), and Kubo relations connecting correlation functions to transport coefficients. The review compares predictions for $\eta/s$ across these frameworks and discusses experimental measurements in strongly coupled quantum fluids.

## Key numbers

- The AdS/CFT correspondence for ${\cal N}=4$ super Yang-Mills theory gives $\eta = s/(4\pi)$, corresponding to $\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)$ in units where $\hbar = k_B = 1$
- The relaxation time from holography is $\tau_R = (2-\log(2))/(2\pi T) \approx 0.65/(\pi T)$
- In kinetic theory, the shear viscosity is given by $\eta = \frac{1}{3} n l_{\text{mfp}} \bar{p}$, where $l_{\text{mfp}}$ is the mean free path
- The Reynolds number expansion parameter is ${\it Re}^{-1} = \eta/(\rho u L)$ for non-relativistic flows
- For nearly perfect fluids, the condition for hydrodynamic behavior is $\eta \lsim \hbar n$ (non-relativistic) or $\eta \lsim \hbar s/k_B$ (relativistic)
- The uncertainty principle argument gives $\eta/s \gsim 0.09$ for a weakly interacting relativistic Bose gas

## Context

This review extends the framework of fluid dynamics as an effective theory originally developed by Martin (1963) and Forster, and builds on the KSS conjecture by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004) that $\eta/s \geq 1/(4\pi)$. The paper discusses counterexamples to this bound found in Gauss-Bonnet holographic theories by Brigante et al. (2007) and Kats et al. (2007). The work enables systematic comparison of nearly perfect fluids across different physical systems, including the quark-gluon plasma studied in heavy ion collisions and ultracold atomic gases, and establishes the fluid-gravity correspondence as a tool for studying strongly coupled fluids.
