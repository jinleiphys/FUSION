# To the nature of nuclear force

> It has been shown for the first time that $NN$ interaction, at least in some partial waves, can be quantitatively described by the superposition of a long-range one-pion exchange and a short-range mechanism based on the complex pole in the $NN$ potential corresponding to the dibaryon resonance in this partial wave. For the partial waves $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$, $^3F_3$ and $^1S_0$ the parameters of the complex poles that give the best description of the elastic and inelastic phase shifts of $NN$ scattering are very close to the empirical parameters of the corresponding isovector dibaryon resonances detected experimentally. Based on the results obtained, a general conclusion is made about the nature of nuclear force at medium and small internucleon distances.

## Key claim

The authors claim that nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in several partial waves can be quantitatively described by a superposition of long-range one-pion exchange and a short-range mechanism based on a single complex pole in the NN potential corresponding to a dibaryon resonance, but this description is explicitly limited to isovector (T=1) channels and the authors state that isoscalar channels will be considered in a separate work.

## Method

The authors construct a two-channel Hamiltonian where the external channel contains only one-pion exchange (OPE) with a soft cutoff parameter, and the internal channel contains a single six-quark eigenstate (dibaryon) with complex energy, where the imaginary part accounts for inelastic decay modes such as NNpi and NNpipi. They then eliminate the internal channel to obtain an effective energy-dependent separable potential in the NN channel, and fit the parameters of the complex pole to reproduce both elastic and inelastic phase shifts for NN scattering in the partial waves 3P2, 1D2, 3F3, and 1S0.

## Key numbers

- One-pion exchange cutoff parameter: Lambda_{pi NN} = 0.65 GeV/c (described as "soft" cutoff)
- Averaged pion-nucleon coupling constant: f_pi^2/(4pi) = 0.075
- The internal node in the radial scattering wavefunction occurs at approximately the same position as the traditional repulsive core and is almost independent of energy (see Fig. 1, which shows radial wavefunctions at 10 MeV, 100 MeV, 500 MeV, and 1 GeV)
- The excitation energy of the mixed-symmetry six-quark bag state |s^4 p^2 [42]_x LST> is approximately 2 hbar omega ~ 500-600 MeV
- The renormalized sigma meson mass inside the six-quark bag is estimated as m_sigma^ren ~ 300 MeV (near-threshold values)
- The ABC effect (near-threshold enhancement in two-pion production) is associated with the isoscalar dibaryon d*(2380)

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon model of nuclear forces previously proposed by Kukulin (1998) and developed in a series of papers (YaF2001, JPhys2001, KuInt, AnnPhys2010), and contrasts with traditional meson-exchange models (such as those reviewed by Machleidt) and Effective Field Theory/Chiral Perturbation Theory approaches (Weinberg, Machl, Eppelbaum, Ordones). The authors argue that previous hybrid approaches combining quark and meson sectors, such as the Quark Compound Bag model of Simonov (early 1980s) and the Feshbach-Lomon boundary conditions model, lacked connection to experimentally observed dibaryon resonances and could not explain inelastic processes. The present work enables a unified description of both elastic and inelastic NN scattering through the same dibaryon mechanism, and the authors note that recent experimental discoveries of dibaryon resonances (Bashkanov 2009, Adlarson 2011, 2014, 2018, Komarov) and studies of short-range nucleon correlations at Jefferson Laboratory provide strong motivation for this approach.
