# Covariant chiral kinetic equation in Wigner function approach

> The covariant chiral kinetic equation (CCKE) is derived from the 4-dimensional Wigner function by an improved perturbative method under the static equilibrium conditions. The chiral kinetic equation in 3-dimensions can be obtained by intergation over the time component of the 4-momentum. There is freedom to add more terms to the CCKE allowed by conservation laws. In the derivation of the 3-dimensional equation, there is also freedom to choose coefficients of some terms in $dx_{0}/dτ$ and $d\mathbf{x}/dτ$ ($τ$ is a parameter along the worldline, and $(x_{0},\mathbf{x})$ denotes the time-space position of a particle) whose 3-mometum integrals are vanishing. So the 3-dimensional chiral kinetic equation derived from the CCKE is not uniquely determined in the current approach. To go beyond the current approach, one needs a new way of building up the 3-dimensional chiral kinetic equation from the CCKE or directly from covariant Wigner equations.

## Key claim

The covariant chiral kinetic equation (CCKE) is derived from the 4-dimensional Wigner function using an improved perturbative method under static equilibrium conditions, but the resulting 3-dimensional chiral kinetic equation is not uniquely determined because there is freedom to add terms whose 3-momentum integrals vanish, and additional constraints from conservation laws and matching the energy-momentum tensor are needed to fix the coefficients.

## Method

The authors start from the gauge-invariant Wigner function for massless fermions in a background electromagnetic field, decompose it into Clifford algebra components, and solve the equations for the vector and axial-vector components perturbatively to first order in space-time derivatives and field strength under static equilibrium conditions (constant temperature, vanishing shear, and specific constraints on chemical potentials and fluid velocity). They then insert this first-order solution into the conservation equation for the vector current to derive the covariant chiral kinetic equation, and subsequently integrate over the time component of the 4-momentum to obtain the 3-dimensional chiral kinetic equation, introducing free coefficients in the vorticity terms that are constrained by requiring the correct currents and energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- The chiral magnetic effect coefficients are given by \(\xi_B = Q\mu_5/(2\pi^2)\) for the vector current and \(\xi_{B5} = Q\mu/(2\pi^2)\) for the axial vector current.
- The chiral vortical effect coefficients are \(\xi = \mu\mu_5/\pi^2\) for the vector current and \(\xi_5 = T^2/6 + (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2)/(2\pi^2)\) for the axial vector current.
- The constraint from currents yields \(C_{10} - \frac{1}{2}C_{11} + \frac{1}{2}C_{30} = 0\).
- The constraint from the stress tensor yields \(C_{20} - \frac{2}{3}C_{21} = 1\).
- The constraints from matching energy rate to force give \(C_{40} = \frac{1}{2}(C_{30}+1)\) and \(C_{41} = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{3}{4}(C_{30}+1)\).
- For the choice \(C_{30}=0\), the Berry curvature in 3-momentum space is \(\boldsymbol{\Omega} = \mathbf{p}/(2|\mathbf{p}|^3)\).

## Context

This work extends the derivation of the chiral kinetic equation from the Wigner function approach previously developed by Chen et al. (2012) and Gao et al. (2012), correcting and generalizing the covariant chiral kinetic equation by identifying additional freedom in the vorticity terms. The results are relevant for describing the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect in heavy-ion collisions and Weyl semimetals, and the non-uniqueness of the 3-dimensional equation highlights the need for a new method to derive it directly from the covariant Wigner equations, enabling future work to resolve the ambiguity in the 3-dimensional chiral kinetic equation.
