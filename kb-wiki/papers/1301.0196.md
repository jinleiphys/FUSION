# Spin-dipole strength functions of $^4$He with realistic nuclear forces

> Both isoscalar and isovector spin-dipole excitations of $^4$He are studied using realistic nuclear forces in the complex scaling method. The ground state of $^4$He and discretized continuum states with $J^π=0^-, 1^-, 2^-$ for A=4 nuclei are described in explicitly correlated Gaussians reinforced with global vectors for angular motion. Two- and three-body decay channels are specifically treated to take into account final state interactions. The observed resonance energies and widths of the negative-parity levels are all in fair agreement with those calculated from both the spin-dipole and electric-dipole strength functions as well as the energy eigenvalues of the complex scaled Hamiltonian. Spin-dipole sum rules, both non energy-weighted and energy-weighted, are discussed in relation to tensor correlations in the ground state of $^4$He.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that spin-dipole (SD) strength functions of $^4$He, calculated with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (AV8$^\prime$ and G3RS potentials plus a three-nucleon force) using the complex scaling method, reproduce observed resonance energies and widths of negative-parity states in fair agreement, but the comparison with experimental charge-exchange data is only qualitative due to assumptions in the experimental analysis.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body Schrödinger equation using a variational method with explicitly correlated Gaussian basis functions reinforced with global vectors for angular motion, employing the complex scaling method to treat continuum states without explicitly constructing them. They constructed basis functions for final states with $J^\pi = 0^-, 1^-, 2^-$ and isospin $T=0,1$, including single-particle excitations, $3N+N$ two-body decay channels, and $d+p+n$ three-body decay channels, using the stochastic variational method to determine basis parameters. The strength functions were computed from the imaginary part of the complex-scaled resolvent, with the scaling angle $\theta=17^\circ$ for the final results.

## Key numbers
- The ground state of $^4$He contains about 14% $D$-state (total spin $S=2$) probability due to the tensor force.
- The $0^-0$ resonance of $^4$He: calculated energy 20.42 MeV (from complex eigenvalues) and 20.54 MeV (from strength functions), compared to experimental 21.01 MeV; width 0.96 MeV and 1.06 MeV, compared to experimental 0.84 MeV.
- The $2^-0$ resonance of $^4$He: calculated energy 21.67 MeV (eigenvalues) and 22.03 MeV (strength functions), compared to experimental 21.84 MeV; width 2.12 MeV and 3.10 MeV, compared to experimental 2.01 MeV.
- The $2^-1$ resonance of $^4$He: calculated energy 23.63 MeV (eigenvalues) and 23.11 MeV (strength functions), compared to experimental 23.33 MeV; width 4.99 MeV and 5.58 MeV, compared to experimental 5.01 MeV.
- The $1_1^-1$ resonance of $^4$He: calculated energy 24.68 MeV (eigenvalues) and 24.04 MeV (strength functions), compared to experimental 25.28 MeV.
- The $2^-1$ resonance of $^4$H: calculated energy 24.45 MeV (eigenvalues) and 23.82 MeV (strength functions), compared to experimental 24.30 MeV; width 5.00 MeV and 5.29 MeV, compared to experimental 5.42 MeV.
- The $2^-1$ resonance of $^4$Li: calculated energy 23.08 MeV (eigenvalues) and 22.99 MeV (strength functions), compared to experimental 23.36 MeV; width 5.02 MeV and 6.53 MeV, compared to experimental 6.03 MeV.
- The first excited $0^+0$ state of $^4$He has calculated excitation energy 20.33 MeV from the ground state.
- SD transition probabilities from the $0_2^+0$ state are approximately 20-30 times larger than those from the ground state.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous _ab initio_ calculation of photoabsorption in $^4$He (Horiuchi, Suzuki, and Arai, 2012) to spin-dipole excitations, using the same complex scaling method with realistic nuclear forces. The study provides fundamental data for neutrino-$^4$He reaction cross sections in core-collapse supernova nucleosynthesis, where first-forbidden transitions can be leading order for $N=Z$ nuclei. The results enable comparison with charge-exchange reaction measurements such as $^4$He($^7$Li,$^7$Be$\gamma$) (Nakayama et al., 2007, 2008) and polarization transfer experiments (Wakasa et al., 2011), and complement shell-model calculations (Tsuzuki et al., 1998) and random phase approximation studies (Bai et al., 2010, 2011; Liang et al., 2012) that used effective interactions with tensor terms.
