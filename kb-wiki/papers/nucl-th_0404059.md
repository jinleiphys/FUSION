# Analysis of three-neutrino oscillations in the full mixing angle space

> We use a model, with no CP violation, of the world's neutrino oscillation data, excluding the LSND experiments, and search the full parameter space ($0\leqθ_{12} \le π/2$; $-π/2 \le θ_{13} \le π/2$; and 0\leqθ_{23} \le π/2$) for the best fit values of the mixing angles and mass-squared differences. We find that the mixing angle $θ_{13}$ is bounded by $-0.15<θ_{13}<0.20$ with an absolute minimum at $θ_{13}=0.12$ and a local minimum at $-0.04$. The importance of the negative $θ_{13}$ region and this structure in the chi-square space has heretofore been overlooked because the factorization approximation commonly employed yields oscillation probabilities that are a function of $\sin^2θ_{13}$.

## Key claim

The authors find that the mixing angle θ₁₃ is bounded by -0.15 < θ₁₃ < 0.20 with an absolute minimum at θ₁₃ = 0.12 and a local minimum at θ₁₃ = -0.04, a structure that has been overlooked because the commonly used factorization approximation yields oscillation probabilities that are a function of sin²θ₁₃ rather than allowing negative values.

## Method

The authors constructed a model of the world's neutrino oscillation data (excluding LSND and Karmen experiments) with no CP violation, and searched the full parameter space (0 ≤ θ₁₂ ≤ π/2; -π/2 ≤ θ₁₃ ≤ π/2; 0 ≤ θ₂₃ ≤ π/2) for best-fit mixing angles and mass-squared differences. They included solar neutrino data (Chlorine, Gallium, SNO, SNO-salt) with the MSW effect in the adiabatic approximation, reactor data (CHOOZ, KamLAND), atmospheric data from Super-Kamiokande, and beam-stop data from K2K, fitting by minimizing chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- θ₁₃ is bounded by -0.15 < θ₁₃ < 0.20, with absolute minimum at θ₁₃ = 0.12 and local minimum at θ₁₃ = -0.04
- Best-fit values for the absolute minimum (Fit #1): θ₁₂ = 0.48, θ₁₃ = 0.12, θ₂₃ = 0.81, Δm²₂₁ = 7.6 × 10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₃₂ = 2.6 × 10⁻³ eV², with χ²_dof = 1.19
- Best-fit values for the local minimum (Fit #2): θ₁₂ = 0.49, θ₁₃ = -0.04, θ₂₃ = 0.71, Δm²₂₁ = 7.7 × 10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₃₂ = 2.6 × 10⁻³ eV², with χ²_dof = 1.21
- The standard solution (SS) from prior analyses gives θ₁₂ = 0.54 ± 0.05, θ₁₃ ≤ 0.13, θ₂₃ = 0.79 ± 0.06, Δm²₂₁ = (8.3 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁵ eV², Δm²₃₂ = (2.4 ± 0.3) × 10⁻³ eV²

## Context

This work extends the standard three-neutrino oscillation analysis by searching the full parameter space including negative θ₁₃ values, which had been overlooked because the factorization approximation commonly employed yields oscillation probabilities as functions of sin²θ₁₃. The authors compare their results to the "standard solution" (SS) derived from Bahcall (2003) and Gonzalez-Garcia (2003) analyses, and find that their absolute minimum is reasonably consistent with those prior results. The work motivates a more thorough examination of the θ₁₃ < 0 region of parameter space, which could affect future experimental searches for CP violation and the determination of the neutrino mass hierarchy.
