# The Regulated NiCu Cycles with the new $^{57}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{58}$Zn reaction rate and the Influence on Type-I X-Ray Bursts: GS 1826$-$24 Clocked Burster

> During the X-ray bursts of GS 1826$-$24, "clocked burster", the nuclear reaction flow that surges through the rapid-proton capture process path has to pass through the NiCu cycles before reaching the ZnGa cycles that moderate the further extent of hydrogen burning in the region above germanium and selenium isotopes. The $^{57}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{58}$Zn reaction located in the NiCu cycles plays an important role in influencing the burst light curves as found by Cyburt et al. (2016). We deduce the $^{57}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{58}$Zn reaction rate based on the experimentally determined important nuclear structure information, isobaric-multiplet-mass equation, and large-scale shell model calculations. Based on the isobaric-multiplet-mass equation, we propose a possible order of $1^+_1$ and $2^+_3$ dominant resonance states and constrain the resonance energy of the $1^+_2$ state. The latter reduces the contribution of the $1^+_2$ dominant resonance state. The new reaction rate is up to a factor of four lower than the Forstner et al. (2001) rate recommended by JINA REACLIB v2.2 at the temperature regime sensitive to clocked bursts of GS 1826$-$24. Using the simulation from the one-dimensional implicit hydrodynamic code, KEPLER, to model the thermonuclear X-ray bursts of GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster, we find that the new $^{57}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{58}$Zn coupled with the latest $^{56}$Ni(p,$־³$)$^{57}$Cu and $^{55}$Ni(p,$־³$)$^{56}$Cu reaction rates redistributes the reaction flow in the NiCu cycles and strongly influences the burst ash composition, whereas the $^{59}$Cu(p,$־±$)$^{56}$Ni and $^{59}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{60}$Zn reactions suppress the influence of the $^{57}$Cu(p,$־³$)$^{58}$Zn reaction and diminish the impact of nuclear reaction flow that by-passes the important $^{56}$Ni waiting point induced by the $^{55}$Ni(p,$־³$)$^{56}$Cu reaction on burst light curve.

## Key claim

The new $^{57}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{58}$Zn reaction rate is up to a factor of four lower than the Forstner et al. (2001) rate recommended by JINA REACLIB v2.2 at the temperature regime sensitive to clocked bursts of GS 1826$-$24, but the $^{59}$Cu(p,$\alpha$)$^{56}$Ni and $^{59}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{60}$Zn reactions suppress the influence of this new rate and diminish the impact of the reaction flow that bypasses the $^{56}$Ni waiting point on the burst light curve.

## Method

The authors deduced the $^{57}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{58}$Zn reaction rate using experimentally determined nuclear structure information, the isobaric-multiplet-mass equation (IMME), and large-scale shell model calculations. They used the IMME to propose the order of the $1^+_1$ and $2^+_3$ dominant resonance states and to constrain the resonance energy of the $1^+_2$ state. The new rate was then implemented in the one-dimensional implicit hydrodynamic code KEPLER to model thermonuclear X-ray bursts of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster, coupled with the latest $^{56}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{57}$Cu and $^{55}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{56}$Cu reaction rates.

## Key numbers

* The new $^{57}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{58}$Zn reaction rate is up to a factor of four lower than the Forstner et al. (2001) rate at the temperature regime sensitive to clocked bursts of GS 1826$-$24.
* The $^{55}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{56}$Cu reaction rate was found by Ma (2019) to be up to one order of magnitude underestimated by Valverde (2018) due to an incorrect penetrability scaling factor.
* The updated $^{55}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{56}$Cu reaction rate causes merely up to 5% difference in the productions of nuclei with mass numbers A=55ג€“65 in a one-zone XRB model (Valverde 2019).
* The maximum difference between the single-particle widths calculated by the Woods-Saxon potential method and the barrier penetrability method is below 40% for the present work.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Cyburt et al. (2016), who identified $^{57}$Cu(p,$\gamma$)$^{58}$Zn as the fifth most influential (p,$\gamma$) reaction affecting the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster light curve. It builds on the experimental energy level measurements of $^{58}$Zn by Langer et al. (2014), which reduced the rate uncertainty by up to three orders of magnitude compared to the earlier Forstner et al. (2001) rate, but left the order of the $1^+_1$ and $2^+_3$ resonance states unconfirmed. The new rate, combined with the corrected $^{55}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{56}$Cu rate from Valverde (2019) and Ma (2019) and the $^{56}$Ni(p,$\gamma$)$^{57}$Cu rate from Kahl (2019), enables more accurate modeling of the NiCu cycles and burst ash composition in the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster.
