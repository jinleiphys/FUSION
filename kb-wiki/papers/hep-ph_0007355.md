# The Infrared Behavior of QCD Green's Functions - Confinement, Dynamical Symmetry Breaking, and Hadrons as Relativistic Bound States

> Recent studies of QCD Green's functions and their applications in hadronic physics are reviewed. We briefly discuss the issues of gauge fixing, BRS invariance and positivity. Evidence for the violation of positivity by quarks and transverse gluons in the covariant gauge is collected, and it is argued that this is one manifestation of confinement. We summarise the derivation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) of QED and QCD. The influence of instantons on DSEs in a 2-dimensional model is mentioned. Solutions for the Green's functions in QED in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions provide tests of various schemes to truncate DSEs. We discuss possible extensions to QCD and their limitations. Truncation schemes for DSEs of QCD are discussed in the axial gauge and in the Landau gauge. We review the available results from a systematic non-perturbative expansion scheme established for Landau gauge QCD. Comparisons to related lattice results, where available, are presented. The applications of QCD Green's functions to hadron physics are summarized. Properties of ground state mesons are discussed on the basis of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quarks and antiquarks. The Goldstone nature of pseudoscalar mesons and mechanisms of diquark confinement are reviewed. We discuss some properties of ground state baryons based on their description as Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev bound states of quark-diquark correlations in the quantum field theory of confined quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that confinement of quarks and gluons can be described within local quantum field theory by abandoning positivity of the representation space (using indefinite metric spaces) rather than relaxing locality, and that violations of positivity for transverse gluons and quarks in covariant gauge provide a manifestation of confinement, though the authors emphasize this is a status report on an ongoing effort where considerable segments remain based on model assumptions.

## Method

The authors review the derivation and truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for QCD Green's functions, focusing on Landau gauge and axial gauge formulations. They discuss how Slavnov-Taylor identities from BRS invariance are used to close the infinite tower of DSEs by expressing vertex functions in terms of two-point functions, and compare results from these continuum methods to lattice simulations where available. The review then applies the resulting Green's functions to hadron phenomenology via Bethe-Salpeter equations for mesons and Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev equations for baryons described as quark-diquark bound states.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization group invariant confinement scale is given by Λ = μ exp(-∫^g dg'/β(g')) which behaves as μ exp(-1/(2β₀g²)) as g→0, with β₀>0
- Positivity violation for transverse gluons can be inferred from perturbation theory for less than 10 quark flavours, based on asymptotic freedom and unbroken global gauge invariance
- The Landau gauge is defined by the limit ξ→0 of the gauge condition f^a(A) = iξB^a, where the Lorentz condition ∂_μA_μ^a = 0 is strictly implemented

## Context

This review extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to QCD Green's functions previously applied in QED studies (e.g., in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions) and provides a systematic non-perturbative expansion scheme for Landau gauge QCD. It builds on earlier work on gluon propagator solutions that neglected ghost contributions (e.g., Mandelstam 1979, Atkinson 1981, Brown 1989, Haeussling 1996) and on axial gauge studies (Baker 1981, Alekseev 1982, Schaden 1982, Cudell 1991), while incorporating the previously disregarded complete tensor structure of the gluon propagator. The results enable a unified description of mesons via Bethe-Salpeter equations and baryons as Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev bound states of quark-diquark correlations, connecting to lattice results from Leinweber (1998) and Bonnet (2000).
