# Axial Anomaly and Chiral Asymmetry in Magnetized Relativistic Matter

> The generation of a chiral shift parameter in the normal ground state of magnetized relativistic matter is discussed. The chiral shift contributes to the axial current density, but does not modify the conventional axial anomaly relation. The analysis based on gauge invariant regularization schemes suggests that this finding is also valid in gauge theories. We argue that the chiral shift parameter can affect observable properties of compact stars. In the regime of heavy ion collisions, the chiral shift parameter can modify the key features of the chiral magnetic effect.

# Axial Anomaly and Chiral Asymmetry in Magnetized Relativistic Matter

## Key claim
The normal ground state of magnetized relativistic matter at nonzero fermion density dynamically generates a chiral shift parameter Δ that modifies the axial current density, but this parameter does not alter the conventional axial anomaly relation. The claim is conditional on the use of gauge-invariant regularization schemes and applies to both the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and, by generalization, to gauge theories.

## Method
The analysis uses a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an external magnetic field in the Landau gauge, incorporating a mean-field ansatz for the fermion propagator that includes a chiral shift parameter Δ and an anomalous magnetic moment term. Gap equations are derived for the chemical potential μ, dynamical mass m, and chiral shift Δ, and are solved in perturbation theory and with proper-time regularization. The axial current density and axial anomaly relation are evaluated using gauge-invariant regularization schemes including proper-time and point-splitting methods.

## Key numbers
- The topological axial current density in the free theory is ⟨j³₅⟩₀ = -eBμ₀/(2π²), where e > 0 is the elementary charge, B is the magnetic field strength, and μ₀ is the bare chemical potential.
- The correction to the axial current density from the chiral shift parameter scales as ⟨j³₅⟩ − ⟨j³₅⟩₀ ∝ aΛ²Δ, where Λ is a cutoff parameter and a is a dimensionless constant of order 1.
- The chiral shift parameter is generated at leading order in the coupling constant as Δ ∝ G_int⟨j³₅⟩₀, where G_int is the four-fermion coupling constant.
- The regime where the chiral shift is relevant requires μ ≫ m (chemical potential much larger than fermion mass) for massive particles, and the crossover between LLL-dominated and higher-Landau-level-dominated regimes occurs when μ ∼ √|eB|.

## Context
This work extends the finding by Metlitski (2005) that a topological contribution to the axial current exists in free magnetized matter, and the proposal by Gorbar (2009) that the ground state develops a chiral shift parameter. It addresses the question of whether interactions modify the axial current and anomaly, confirming the earlier results of Son and Metlitski that the axial anomaly relation remains unchanged. The results enable new mechanisms for pulsar kicks via asymmetric neutrino scattering and a modified chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions that does not rely on initial topological charge fluctuations as proposed by Kharzeev (2007).
