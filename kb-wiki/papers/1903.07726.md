# Forbidden transitions in neutral and charged current interactions between low-energy neutrinos and Argon

> Background: The study of low-energy neutrinos and their interactions with atomic nuclei is crucial to several open problems in physics, including the neutrino mass hierarchy, CP-violation, candidates of Beyond Standard Model physics and supernova dynamics. Examples of experiments include CAPTAIN at SNS as well as DUNE's planned detection program of supernova neutrinos. Purpose: We present cross section calculations for quasielastic charged current and neutral current neutrinos at low energies, with a focus on $^{40}$Ar. We also take a close look at pion decay-at-rest neutrino spectra, which are used in e.g. the SNS experiment at Oakridge. Method and results: We employ a Hartree Fock + Continuum Random Phase Approximations (HF+CRPA) framework, which allows us to model the responses and include the effects of long-range correlations. It is expected to provide a good framework to calculate forbidden transitions, whose contribution which we show to be non-negligible. Conclusions: Forbidden transitions can be expected to contribute sizeably to the reaction strength at typical low-energy kinematics, such as DAR neutrinos. Modeling and Monte Carlo simulations need to take all due care to account for the influence of their contributions.

## Key claim

Forbidden transitions (multipoles beyond the allowed Fermi $0^+$ and Gamow-Teller $1^+$ operators) contribute sizeably to neutrino-argon reaction strength at typical low-energy kinematics such as decay-at-rest (DAR) neutrinos, and Monte Carlo simulations must account for these contributions; the abstract's claim of "non-negligible" contribution is specified here as strong contributions from the $1^-$ and $2^-$ multipoles with smaller contributions from higher multipolarities.

## Method

The authors employ a Hartree Fock plus Continuum Random Phase Approximation (HF+CRPA) framework to calculate inclusive charged-current and neutral-current cross sections for neutrinos and antineutrinos on $^{40}$Ar at low energies (tens of MeV). The nuclear responses are obtained through a multipole expansion of the nuclear current, with the residual Skyrme interaction used self-consistently. Coulomb interactions between the outgoing lepton and residual nucleus are treated by interpolating between the Fermi function (valid at low outgoing lepton momenta) and the Modified Effective Momentum Approximation (MEMA) at higher momenta.

## Key numbers

- Total charged-current ($\nu_e$, $^{56}$Fe) cross section folded with a DAR electron neutrino spectrum: 212.9 $\times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ (HF+CRPA), compared to experimental KARMEN value of 256 $\pm 108 \pm 43 \times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$
- Total $^{56}$Fe B(GT$^-$) strength: 8.8 (HF+CRPA), compared to experimental value of 9.9 $\pm$ 2.5
- Monoenergetic muon neutrinos from DAR pions at $E_{\nu_\mu} = 29.8$ MeV
- DAR neutrino spectra extend up to $m_\mu/2$ where $m_\mu \approx 105.7$ MeV
- For $^{40}$Ar at 25 MeV incoming energy, the differential cross section is well described by allowed approximation; at 50 MeV, significant strength comes from $1^-$ and $2^-$ transitions; at 75 MeV, even higher multipolarities become non-negligible

## Context

This work extends previous CRPA results (Kolbe 1999, Jachowicz 2002, Jachowicz 2003) and compares with shell model calculations (Kostensalo 2018), QRPA approaches (Cheoun 2011, Paar 2007), and Fermi Gas-based RPA models (Sajjad Athar 2005). The calculations are relevant to the CAPTAIN experiment at the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) and DUNE's supernova neutrino detection program. The results indicate that the Monte Carlo generator MARLEY, which currently employs only Fermi and Gamow-Teller transitions for charged-current neutrino-$^{40}$Ar scattering, should implement forbidden transitions for accurate modeling.
