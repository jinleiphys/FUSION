# Hypernuclear Weak Decay Puzzle

> A general shell model formalism for the nonmesonic weak decay of the hypernuclei has been developed. It involves a partial wave expansion of the emitted nucleon waves, preserves naturally the antisymmetrization between the escaping particles and the residual core, and contains as a particular case the weak $Λ$-core coupling formalism. The Extreme Particle-Hole Model and the Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation are explicitly worked out. It is shown that the nuclear structure manifests itself basically through Pauli Principle, and a very simple expression is derived for the neutron and proton induced decays rates, $Γ_n$ and $Γ_p$, which does not involve the spectroscopic factors. We use the standard strangeness-changing weak $ΛN\to NN$ transition potential which comprises the exchange of the complete pseudoscalar and vector meson octets ($π,η,K,ρ,ω,K^*$), taking into account some important parity violating transition operators that are systematically omitted in the literature. The interplay between different mesons in the decay of ${^{12}_ΛC}$ is carefully analyzed. With the commonly used parametrization in the One-Meson-Exchange Model (OMEM), the calculated rate $Γ_{NM}= Γ_n+Γ_p$ is of the order of the free $Λ$ decay rate $Γ^0(Γ_{NM}^{\rm th}\cong Γ^0)$ and is consistent with experiments. Yet, the measurements of $Γ_{n/p}=Γ_n/Γ_p$ and of $Γ_p$ are not well accounted for by the theory ($Γ_{n/p}^{\rm th}\lsim 0.42; Γ_p^{\rm th}\gsim 0.60 Γ^0$). It is suggested that, unless additional degrees of freedom are incorporated, the OMEM parameters should be radically modified.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general shell model formalism for hypernuclear nonmesonic weak decay and shows that, while the total decay rate \(\Gamma_{NM}\) for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}C\) calculated with the standard One-Meson-Exchange Model (OMEM) agrees with experiment (\(\Gamma_{NM}^{\rm th}\cong \Gamma^0\)), the neutron-to-proton ratio \(\Gamma_{n/p}\) and the proton-induced rate \(\Gamma_p\) are not reproduced (\(\Gamma_{n/p}^{\rm th}\lesssim 0.42\); \(\Gamma_p^{\rm th}\gtrsim 0.60\Gamma^0\)), suggesting that either additional degrees of freedom must be incorporated or the OMEM parameters must be radically modified.

## Method

A general shell model formalism for the nonmesonic weak decay \(\Lambda N \to NN\) was developed, employing a partial wave expansion of the emitted nucleon waves and preserving antisymmetrization between escaping particles and the residual core. The Extreme Particle-Hole Model (EPHM) and the Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) were explicitly worked out, and a simple expression for the neutron and proton induced decay rates \(\Gamma_n\) and \(\Gamma_p\) was derived that does not involve spectroscopic factors. The standard strangeness-changing weak \(\Lambda N \to NN\) transition potential was used, comprising exchange of the complete pseudoscalar and vector meson octets (\(\pi, \eta, K, \rho, \omega, K^*\)), including parity violating transition operators systematically omitted in earlier literature.

## Key numbers

- The free \(\Lambda\) decay rate is \(\Gamma^0 = 2.50 \times 10^{-6}\) eV.
- For \(^{12}_{\Lambda}C\), the calculated total nonmesonic decay rate \(\Gamma_{NM}^{\rm th} \cong \Gamma^0\) is consistent with experiments.
- The theoretical neutron-to-proton ratio satisfies \(\Gamma_{n/p}^{\rm th} \lesssim 0.42\).
- The theoretical proton-induced decay rate satisfies \(\Gamma_p^{\rm th} \gtrsim 0.60 \Gamma^0\).
- In the one-pion-exchange (OPE) model alone, calculations for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}C\) yield \(\Gamma_{n/p}^{\rm (OPE)} \cong 0.1-0.2\).
- Including correlated two-pion exchange potentials boosted \(\Gamma_{n/p}\) up to 0.36 for \(^{12}_{\Lambda}C\) (Itonaga et al., 1998).

## Context

This work extends the weak-coupling model (WCM) formalism used by Ramos et al. (1992), Parreno et al. (1997), and Itonaga et al. (1998) by developing a fully general shell model framework that avoids explicit spectroscopic factors. The paper addresses the long-standing "hypernuclear weak decay puzzle" where the total nonmesonic decay rate is well reproduced but the \(\Gamma_{n/p}\) ratio remains unexplained, following earlier attempts including two-nucleon stimulated processes (Alberico et al., 1991; Ramos et al., 1995, 1997), inclusion of \(\Delta T=3/2\) contributions (Parreno et al., 1998; Alberico et al., 2000), quark degrees of freedom (Inoue et al., 1998; Sasaki et al., 2000), and correlated two-pion exchange (Itonaga et al., 1998). The formalism enables systematic multipole analysis of meson contributions and can be extended to include RPA correlations, pairing, and higher seniority excitations.
