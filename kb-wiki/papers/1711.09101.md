# Parton distribution amplitudes: revealing diquarks in the proton and Roper resonance

> We present the first quantum field theory calculation of the pointwise behaviour of the leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of the proton and its lightest radial excitation. The proton's PDA is a broad, concave function, whose maximum is shifted relative to the peak in QCD's conformal limit expression for this PDA; an effect which signals the presence of both scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations in the nucleon, with the scalar generating around 60% of the proton's normalisation. The radial-excitation is constituted similarly, and the pointwise form of its PDA, which is negative on a material domain, is the result of marked interference between the contributions from both types of diquark; particularly, the locus of zeros that highlights its character as a radial excitation. These features originate with the emergent phenomenon of dynamical chiral-symmetry breaking in the Standard Model.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first quantum field theory calculation of the pointwise behavior of the leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of the proton and its lightest radial excitation (the Roper resonance), using a constrained spectral function model for the Faddeev wave function that emulates realistic solutions of the continuum three-body bound-state problem.

## Method

The authors compute the proton's leading-twist PDA from its Poincare-covariant Faddeev wave function, which includes both scalar (isoscalar) and pseudovector (isotriplet) diquark correlations. They employ perturbation theory integral representations (PTIRs) for all elements of the Faddeev wave function, dressed-quark propagators, diquark correlation amplitudes, diquark propagators, and quark-diquark amplitudes, with parameters chosen to emulate realistic Faddeev wave functions from prior studies. The PDA is obtained by evaluating Mellin moments through multi-dimensional Feynman parameter integrals, then evolving the result from the hadronic scale (0.51 GeV) to 2 GeV using leading-order evolution equations. The same framework is extended to the Roper resonance by modifying the spectral function in the quark-diquark amplitude to incorporate a zero at relative momentum approximately 0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The scalar diquark generates approximately 60% of the proton's canonical normalisation (with the scalar diquark contribution to baryon number set to 65 ± 5% via parameter r_A = 0.30 ± 0.03).
- The computed proton PDA at ζ = 2 GeV peaks at barycentric coordinates (x1, x2, x3) = (0.55, 0.23, 0.22), with a maximum value of 3.65.
- The proton's first Mellin moments at ζ = 2 GeV: ⟨x1⟩_u = 0.379(4), ⟨x2⟩_u = 0.302(1), ⟨x3⟩_d = 0.319(3), with f_N = (3.78 ± 0.14) × 10^(-3) GeV^2.
- The Roper resonance's first Mellin moments at ζ = 2 GeV: ⟨x1⟩_u = 0.245(13), ⟨x2⟩_u = 0.363(6), ⟨x3⟩_d = 0.392(6), with f_R = (5.17 ± 0.32) × 10^(-3) GeV^2.
- The Roper resonance mass is taken as m_R = (3/2) m_p, and the zero in its Faddeev amplitude occurs at quark-diquark relative momentum √ℓ² ≈ 0.4 GeV ≈ 1/(0.5 fm).

## Context

This work extends the continuum QCD approach that previously resolved the long-standing conflict over meson PDAs (Chang 2013, Shi 2015, Braun 2015) to the baryon sector, where only low-order Mellin moments from sum rules (Chernyak 1983, Stefanis 1992) and lattice QCD (Braun 2014, Bali 2015) were available. The computed proton PDA agrees with lattice QCD results for the first moments, validating a picture of the proton as a bound state with both strong scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations. The predictions enable realistic assessments of the scale at which exclusive baryon experiments can be compared with perturbative QCD hard-scattering formulae, as previously demonstrated for mesons (Chang 2013, Horn 2016, Gao 2017).
