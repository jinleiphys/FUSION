# Generalized Polarizabilities and the Chiral Structure of the Nucleon

> We are studying the electron scattering process e p to e' p' gamma in order to obtain information on the genuine virtual Compton scattering (VCS) process gamma^* N to gamma N. In addition to the two kinematical variables of real Compton scattering, e.g. the scattering angle theta and the energy omega' of the outgoing photon, the invariant amplitude for VCS depends on a third kinematical variable, which we choose as the absolute value of the three-momentum transfer to the nucleon. The structure-dependent coefficients in the VCS amplitude therefore acquire a momentum dependence and are termed ``generalized polarizabilities'' of the nucleon in analogy to real Compton scattering. Utilizing the heavy baryon formalism of chiral perturbation theory we present predictions for the momentum dependence of the ``generalized polarizabilities'' and discuss the VCS response functions to be measured in the scheduled electron scattering experiments.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) at third order in the momentum expansion provides the first complete set of predictions for the generalized polarizabilities (GPs) of the nucleon in virtual Compton scattering (VCS), but the calculation is limited to the kinematic approximation where multipoles are expanded around zero outgoing photon energy and only terms linear in that energy are retained.

## Method

The authors performed a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculation to third order in external momenta (O(p^3)) for the ten generalized polarizabilities of the proton, using only the pion mass, axial coupling constant, and pion decay constant as input parameters. The calculation included nine one-loop diagrams and a pi0-exchange t-channel pole graph from the Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian, with the GPs extracted from the VCS amplitude following the formalism of Guichon et al. The four constraints from charge-conjugation symmetry were used to check consistency among the ten GPs, and the results were used to construct three independent response functions (P_LL, P_TT, P_LT) measurable in unpolarized electron scattering experiments.

## Key numbers

- HBChPT O(p^3) prediction for the electric polarizability at zero momentum transfer: alpha_E(0) = 12.8 x 10^(-4) fm^3; experimental value: (12.1 +/- 0.8 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- HBChPT O(p^3) prediction for the magnetic polarizability at zero momentum transfer: beta_M(0) = 1.3 x 10^(-4) fm^3; experimental value: (2.1 +/- 0.8 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- The generalized magnetic polarizability beta_M(qbar^2) shows a turnover point around qbar^2 = 0.1 GeV^2
- The Mainz experiment will supply data at qbar = 600 MeV (qbar^2 = 0.36 GeV^2), where ChPT predicts alpha_E should have decreased by approximately 50% from its real-photon value
- The MIT-Bates experiment plans to collect data at qbar approximately 240 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of generalized polarizabilities that were available only within a non-relativistic constituent quark model (Guichon et al.) and a one-loop calculation in the linear sigma model (Metz), as well as earlier HBChPT work limited to the first derivative of spin-independent polarizabilities at very low momentum transfer (Hemmert et al. 1997). The predictions enable upcoming VCS experiments at Mainz (G. Audit et al.) and MIT-Bates (J. Shaw et al.) to extract the momentum dependence of generalized polarizabilities, particularly the electric polarizability, and future work will include O(p^4) calculations and explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom.
