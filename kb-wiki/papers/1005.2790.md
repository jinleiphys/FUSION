# Particle multiplicities at LHC and deviations from limiting fragmentation

> The pseudorapidity density of charged particles produced at LHC collisions are predicted by using two complementary production mechanisms with a set of consistent integrated and unintegrated parton distributions. We discuss the limiting fragmentation hypothesis and its possible violation, and we compare our model with other partonic models.

# Particle multiplicities at LHC and deviations from limiting fragmentation

## Key claim
The limiting fragmentation hypothesis, which generally appears in present data of hadron collisions, is partly violated if observations are across a wide range between RHIC and LHC energies; the deviation originates from the gluon-gluon fusion mechanism rather than the quark recombination mechanism, and becomes visible when comparing distributions at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (5.5 TeV) energies.

## Method
The authors use a two-component model combining hard gluon-gluon fusion (gg→g) for the central rapidity region and soft quark recombination for the fragmentation regions, with a set of consistent integrated and unintegrated parton distributions evolved via a modified DGLAP equation that incorporates shadowing and antishadowing corrections. The unintegrated gluon distributions are obtained from integrated distributions using the Kimber-Martin-Ryskin scheme, and the energy dependence of the normalization factor is fixed by fitting the empirical parametrization of midrapidity density from UA5 and CDF data. Predictions are made for p-p and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, and compared with the KLN saturation model and the BK equation approach.

## Key numbers
- Midrapidity density parametrization for p-p(bar p) collisions: dN/dη|_(η=0) = 2.5 − 0.25 ln s + 0.023 ln² s, fitted from √s = 15 GeV to 1.8 TeV and extended to LHC energies.
- Effective mass m_eff = 770 MeV used for p-p collisions (assuming pions produced via ρ-resonance); best fit for Au-Au collisions at RHIC requires m_eff = 400 MeV.
- Mean number of participants N_part = 339 in Au-Au collisions at √s = 130 and 200 GeV; N_part = 369 in Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 5.5 TeV.
- Coefficient λ in <N_coll> ∝ <N_part^(4/3)> fitted as λ = 29 ln √s using RHIC data at √s = 130–200 GeV.
- At y' = 0 (target rest frame), x_(1,large) = x_(2,large) = p_t/m_N is independent of √s, while x_(2,small) = p_t m_N / s; at √s = 5.5 TeV, x_(2,small) << x_(2,small) at √s = 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the two-component picture of hadron collisions (central plateau from gluon fusion, fragmentation regions from quark recombination) beyond earlier saturation models such as the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) model (2001) and the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation approach by Albacete (2007), which used a single gluon-gluon fusion mechanism with a fixed (1−x)^4 gluon distribution at pre-saturation x. The authors use their own parton distributions from a modified DGLAP equation (Ruan and Zhu, 2008) that works in a broad pre-saturation range, in contrast to the JIMWLK/BK equations valid only at very small x. The predictions enable tests of QCD evolution dynamics by comparing multiplicity distributions at LHC with those from the KLN model, PYTHIA, PHOJET, and UrQMD models, and the predicted deviation from limiting fragmentation can discriminate between different parton evolution equations.
