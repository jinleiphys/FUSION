# Isotropization from Color Field Condensate in heavy ion collisions

> The expanding fireball shortly after a heavy ion collision may be qualitatively described by a condensate of color fields or gluons which is analogous to Bose-Einstein-condensation for massive bosonic particles. This condensate is a transient non-equilibrium phenomenon and breaks Lorentz-boost symmetry. The dynamics of color field condensates involves collective excitations and is rather different from the perturbative scattering of gluons. In particular, it provides for an efficient mechanism to render the local pressure approximately isotropic after a short time of 0.2 fm/c. We suggest that an isotropic color field condensate may play a central role for a simple description of prethermalization and isotropization in the early stages of the collision.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a transient color field condensate (a non-equilibrium, Lorentz-boost-symmetry-breaking gluon field configuration) can provide an efficient mechanism for rendering the local pressure approximately isotropic within a short time of 0.2 fm/c, potentially offering a simple description of prethermalization and isotropization in the early stages of heavy ion collisions, though they note this is a suggestion rather than a proven result.

## Method

The authors analyze the dynamics of a spatially constant color field condensate in SU(3) gauge theory using a decomposition of the gauge field into irreducible representations under modified rotation symmetry. They derive the quadratic action for perturbations around the condensate, including contributions from tensor, vector, and scalar excitations with different charge parity, and solve the constraint equations from the Gauss law in Weyl gauge. The time evolution of the condensate components and the resulting longitudinal and transverse pressures are computed numerically for a homogeneous system with initial conditions where the longitudinal pressure vanishes.

## Key numbers

* Isotropization of pressure occurs after a short time of about 0.2 fm/c (the paper states "0.2 fm/c" in the abstract and "0.2 fm" in the text).
* For an order-of-magnitude estimate at LHC conditions, the authors consider a temperature scale T = 0.61 GeV and a coupling g = sqrt(4 pi alpha_S(p_Temp)) approximately 2.5 for momentum scale p_Temp approximately 1.6 T approximately 1 GeV.
* Equating the condensate pressure p = 2 g^2 sigma^4 with a realistic QCD equation of state p(T) approximately 0.68 GeV^4 yields sigma approximately 0.5 GeV.
* For these values, g^2 sigma approximately 3.1 GeV is of the order of g p_Temp approximately 2.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on prethermalization and isotropization in heavy ion collisions, building on earlier work by Berges, Borsanyi, and Wetterich (2004, 2005) on prethermalization and isotropization far from equilibrium. It contrasts with perturbative approaches such as the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which predicts much longer thermalization times, and with mechanisms based on plasma instabilities (e.g., Romatschke and Strickland, 2003; Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore, 2003; Rebhan, Romatschke, and Strickland, 2005) or chaotic behavior (e.g., Muller and Trayanov, 1992). The isotropic color field configuration used is that of Reuter and Wetterich (1994). The work enables further study of whether an isotropic color field condensate can survive in an approximate thermal equilibrium state of a quark-gluon plasma and how it decays into hadrons or quasiparticles.
