# Pion and kaon valence-quark parton distribution functions

> A rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, constrained by existing applications to hadron physics, is employed to compute the valence-quark parton distribution functions of the pion and kaon. Comparison is made to pi-N Drell-Yan data for the pion's u-quark distribution and to Drell-Yan data for the ratio u_K(x)/u_π(x): the environmental influence of this quantity is a parameter-free prediction, which agrees well with existing data. Our analysis unifies the computation of distribution functions with that of numerous other properties of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper computes valence-quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the pion and kaon using a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, and finds that the parameter-free prediction for the ratio \(u_K(x)/u_\pi(x)\) agrees well with existing Drell-Yan data, though the absolute pion PDF at high-\(x\) shows a discrepancy with earlier experimental analyses that is resolved by a recent NLO reanalysis including soft-gluon resummation.

## Method

The authors employ a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE), using a model running coupling that reproduces QCD's one-loop renormalization-group behavior for \(k^2 \gtrsim 2\) GeV\(^2\). The valence-quark PDFs are computed from the forward \(\bar{q}\)-target scattering amplitude in Euclidean momentum space, using the full pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude expanded in Chebyshev polynomials up to order \(n=0-3\). The model scale is fixed to \(Q_0 = 0.57\) GeV by matching the \(x^n\) moments for \(n=1,2,3\) to experimental analysis at (2 GeV)\(^2\), and the distributions are evolved using leading-order DGLAP evolution.

## Key numbers

* Pion valence \(u\)-quark distribution at model scale \(Q_0 = 0.57\) GeV has exponent \(\alpha = 2.1\) for the large-\(x\) behavior \(u_\pi(x) \propto (1-x)^\alpha\).
* Momentum sum rule at \(Q_0\): \(2 \langle x \rangle = 0.74\) for the pion and \(0.76\) for the kaon.
* Pion mass: calculated 0.138 GeV (experiment 0.138 GeV); pion decay constant: calculated 0.092 GeV (experiment 0.092 GeV).
* Kaon mass: calculated 0.497 GeV (experiment 0.496 GeV); kaon decay constant: calculated 0.110 GeV (experiment 0.113 GeV).
* Pion charge radius squared: calculated 0.45 fm\(^2\) (experiment 0.44 fm\(^2\)); kaon charge radius squared: calculated 0.38 fm\(^2\) (experiment 0.34 fm\(^2\)).
* Ratio \(u_K(1)/u_\pi(1)\) estimated analytically as \(\sim 0.2\) from the approximate formula \(\frac{f_\pi}{f_K} (M_u/M_s)^4\).

## Context

This work extends the first DSE study of pion PDFs by Hecht et al. (2000), which used phenomenological parametrizations, by employing numerical DSE solutions for both the pion and kaon. It unifies the computation of PDFs with successful predictions of electromagnetic elastic and transition form factors from the same RL-DSE model developed by Maris et al. (1999, 2000, 2002) and Holl et al. (2005). The results confirm the large-\(x\) behavior predicted by pQCD (Farrar 1975, Brodsky 1994) and support the recent NLO reanalysis with soft-gluon resummation by Aicher et al. (2010) as resolving the discrepancy between earlier Drell-Yan data and theory. The parameter-free prediction for \(u_K(x)/u_\pi(x)\) enables future tests of environmental effects on quark distributions in mesons.
