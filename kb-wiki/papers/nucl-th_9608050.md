# Nuclear gluon shadowing via dileptons from open charm decay in $p+A$ at $¥sqrt s=200$ AGeV

> Opposite-sign lepton pairs ($ee$,$eﾎｼ$ and $ﾎｼﾎｼ$) from open charm decay are proposed as a measure of nuclear shadowing effects. Via an approximate scaling the ratio of the dilepton spectra from $p+A$ to those from $pp$ reflects the shadowing function well. We show that the required measurements are feasible at the Relativistic Heavy ion Collider (RHIC) by considering the backgrounds according to the proposed PHENIX detector geometry.

## Key claim

The ratio of dilepton spectra from open charm decay in $p+A$ collisions to those in $pp$ collisions, when scaled appropriately, can map out the nuclear gluon shadowing function $R_{g/A}(x,Q^2)$, but this depends on the assumption that charm quarks fragment via a hard (delta-function) mechanism, which must be verified experimentally via single inclusive $D$ meson measurements.

## Method

The authors computed the $D¥bar D$ pair distribution from $p+Au$ collisions at $¥sqrt s=200$ AGeV using leading-order perturbative QCD with a constant $K=3$ factor, the MRSA parton distribution functions, and two different gluon shadowing scenarios: one where gluon shadowing equals measured quark shadowing (as in HIJING), and one from Eskola's modified GLAP evolution. The dilepton spectra from $D¥bar D$ decay were generated via Monte Carlo using JETSET7.3, and signal-to-background ratios were estimated for the PHENIX detector geometry with specific kinematic cuts ($E_e > 1$ GeV, $E_¥mu > 2$ GeV, relative azimuthal angle $¥phi_{¥ell^+¥ell^-} > 90^¥circ$).

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals to use open charm as a probe of gluon density (Author, Year) and addresses the need for systematic measurement of gluon shadowing, which is well established for quarks from deep inelastic scattering experiments (EMC effect, Author Year). The authors note that previous attempts to extract nuclear gluon shadowing from $F_2^A$ data on Sn and C (Pirner, Year) showed similar shadowing and anti-shadowing patterns. The approach avoids complications from $J/¥psi$ production, where QCD scaling was found violated at $¥sqrt s=20-40$ AGeV (Moss, Year), and enables fixing initial gluon flux conditions for $A+A$ collisions at RHIC.
