# Neutral weak currents in nucleon superfluid Fermi liquids: Larkin-Migdal and Leggett approaches

> Neutrino emission in processes of breaking and formation of nucleon Cooper pairs is calculated in the framework of the Larkin-Migdal and the Leggett approaches to the description of superfluid Fermi liquids at finite temperatures. We explain peculiarities of both approaches and explicitly demonstrate that they lead to the same expression for the emissivity in pair breaking and formation processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Larkin-Migdal and Leggett approaches to superfluid Fermi liquids, despite their technical differences, yield identical expressions for the neutrino emissivity from nucleon Cooper pair breaking and formation processes, contradicting a recent claim by Leinson (2009) that the two formalisms give different results.

## Method

The authors derived the neutrino emissivity from the PBF process using both the Larkin-Migdal equations for full in-medium vertices and the Leggett equations for response functions, working within the Nambu-Gorkov formalism extended to finite temperatures via Matsubara techniques. They solved the coupled equations for the vector and axial-vector current vertices including correlation effects in both particle-particle and particle-hole channels, parameterized by Landau-Migdal interaction amplitudes, and computed the current-current correlation functions and polarization tensors for a neutron system with s-wave pairing.

## Key numbers

- Weak interaction coupling constant: G ≈ 1.2 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- Axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.26
- Vector coupling: g_V = -1
- The PBF emissivity from the vector current is suppressed by a factor ∝ v_F² (where v_F is the Fermi velocity of non-relativistic nucleons), not ∝ v_F⁴ as claimed by Leinson (2009)
- The PBF reaction kinematics requires ω > 2Δ and |q| < ω, where Δ is the nucleon pairing gap

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky, 2008) of PBF emissivity from T=0 to arbitrary temperatures, and resolves a discrepancy with Leinson (2009) who claimed different results using the Leggett formalism. The paper reconciles the Larkin-Migdal approach (originally developed for nuclear transitions) with the Leggett approach (developed for collective modes in superfluid Fermi liquids), confirming Leggett's original assertion of their equivalence. This enables reliable calculations of neutrino cooling rates in superfluid neutron star interiors, where the PBF process is a key cooling mechanism.
