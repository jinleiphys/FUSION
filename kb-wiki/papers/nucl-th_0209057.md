# Electron capture rates for core collapse supernovae

> Electron capture rates on neutron-rich nuclei (A>65) were calculated within the Random Phase Approximation with partial number formalism, including allowed and forbidden transitions. The partial occupation numbers were provided as a function of temperature by Shell-Model Monte Carlo calculations, including an pairing+quadrupole interaction. Capture rates on relevent nuclei were calculated for density and temperature conditions during the core collapse of a massive star. It was found that electron captures on nuclei can compete with electron captures on free protons. Furthermore, they produce neutrinos with average energies lower than neutrinos emitted from captures on free protons, with possible consequences on the cooling of the core.

# Electron capture rates for core collapse supernovae

## Key claim
Electron captures on neutron-rich nuclei (A>65) can compete with electron captures on free protons during core collapse, and produce neutrinos with lower average energies, but the net effect on core cooling and the self-regulation mechanism can only be determined by future collapse simulations that include these processes.

## Method
Electron capture rates on nuclei with A>65 were calculated using the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) with partial number formalism, including both allowed and forbidden transitions. The partial occupation numbers as a function of temperature were provided by Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations using a pairing+quadrupole interaction, with a model space including the full pf+gds orbitals above an inert 40Ca core. Rates were evaluated for density and temperature conditions along a M=0.6 solar mass stellar trajectory during core collapse.

## Key numbers
- Neutrino trapping density: approximately 6 x 10^11 g cm^-3
- At electron chemical potential approximately 10 MeV (time to bounce approximately 10^-2 s), the ratio of heavy nuclei abundance to proton abundance Y_h/Y_p is approximately 100
- For the representative nucleus 72Zn at this stellar point, the ratio of capture rate on this nucleus to capture rate on free protons approximately balances the abundance ratio
- Electron capture rates on 76Ga (N=45) were compared between the Independent Particle Model and the SMMC/RPA model at three different temperatures

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of electron capture rates on nuclei in the mass range 45 ≤ A ≤ 65, which were based on large-scale shell-model diagonalization within the full pf shell (Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo, 2000). For heavier nuclei, direct diagonalization of the full pf+gds orbitals is not feasible, so the authors follow a hybrid model previously proposed for germanium isotopes (Langanke et al., 2001). Current collapse simulations assume electron captures on nuclei stop for nuclei with neutron numbers above the pf-shell closure N=40, but this work shows that thermal excitation and residual interactions can unblock Gamow-Teller transitions for heavier nuclei, potentially affecting the self-regulation mechanism that establishes similar electron fractions across progenitor models (Mathias and Messer, 1998). The results enable future collapse simulations to include electron captures on neutron-rich nuclei and assess their impact on the homologous core size and shock-wave onset.
