# Companion problems in quasispin and isospin

> We note that the same mathematical results apply to problems involving quasispin and isospin, but the problems per se are different. In the quasispin case, one deals with a system of identical fermions (e.g. neutrons) and address the problem of how many seniority conserving interactions there are. In the isospin case, one deals with a system of both neutrons and protons and the problem in question is the number of neutron-proton pairs with a given total angular momentum. Other companion problems are also discussed.

# Companion problems in quasispin and isospin

## Key claim
The same mathematical results, specifically the eigenvalue problem for a unitary Racah coefficient (a 6j-symbol), apply to two physically distinct problems: counting seniority-conserving interactions for identical fermions using quasispin, and counting neutron-proton pairs with a given total angular momentum in mixed systems using isospin. The paper demonstrates this correspondence for specific cases but notes that the connection holds only for certain j-values (j ≤ 7/2) where the relevant coefficients of fractional parentage are uniquely defined.

## Method
The authors compare the quasispin formalism for identical fermions in a single j-shell (following Rosensteel and Rowe) with the isospin formalism for mixed neutron-proton systems in the same shell. For the quasispin case, they analyze the eigenvalues of the matrix M^Ω (constructed from 6j-symbols) to obtain projection operators for quasispin S=0 and S=2, which determine the number of seniority-conserving and seniority-mixing interactions. For the isospin case, they write wave functions for ⁴⁴Ti (two protons, two neutrons) as sums over even-J configurations and derive an eigenvalue equation for the same 6j-symbol from the orthogonality condition between T=0 and T=2 states. They also derive the number of T=3/2 states in a three-particle system (one proton, two neutrons) using isospin arguments, recovering the same expression as the quasispin result.

## Key numbers
- For the quasispin case, the number of seniority-mixing interactions (quasispin rank-2 tensors minus the pairing interaction) is p₂ - 1, where p₂ = [(2j+3)/6] (integer part), derived from p₂ = (1/3)[(2j+1)/2 + 2 Σ_{even J} (2J+1){j j J; j j J}].
- For j = 3/2, 5/2, and 7/2, the sum SUM6j = Σ_{even J} (2J+1){j j J; j j J} equals 0.5, 0, and -0.5 respectively.
- For ⁴⁴Ti with j = 7/2, the number of neutron-neutron pairs equals the number of proton-neutron pairs equals the number of proton-proton pairs, given by |D(J₁₂ J₁₂)|² for J₁₂ = 0, 2, 4, and 6.
- For ⁴³Sc (T=1/2 states) with j = 7/2, the coefficients of fractional parentage for the I=15/2 state are a = √(5/22) and b = √(17/22).

## Context
This work extends the quasispin formalism of Kerman (1961), Kerman, Lawson and MacFarlane (1961-1965), and the seniority-conserving interaction analysis of Rosensteel and Rowe (2001, 2003), by showing that the same 6j-symbol eigenvalue problem appears in the isospin context of mixed neutron-proton systems. The authors connect their results to the earlier work of de Shalit and Talmi (1963), Talmi (1993), and Ginocchio and Haxton (1993) on seniority and coefficients of fractional parentage. The paper enables further exploration of partial dynamical symmetries in nuclei like ⁴³Sc, where the vanishing of certain 6j-symbols (because corresponding states do not exist in the identical-particle system) leads to degeneracies and quantum number classification for specific angular momenta. The authors also note that Zhao, Arima, Ginocchio and Yoshinaga (2003) have generalized the pattern of SUM6j results to higher j-values.
