# Many-body theory interpretation of deep inelastic scattering

> We analyze data on deep inelastic scattering of electrons from the proton using ideas from standard many-body theory involving {\em bound} constituents subject to {\em interactions}. This leads us to expect, at large three-momentum transfer ${\bf{q}}$, scaling in terms of the variable $\tilde{y}=ν-{\bf |q|}$. The response at constant ${\bf |q|}$ scales well in this variable. Interaction effects are manifestly displayed in this approach. They are illustrated in two examples.

## Key claim

The paper argues that deep inelastic scattering data can be reinterpreted using standard many-body theory, where the struck constituent is treated as bound and subject to interactions; this leads to scaling in the variable \(\tilde{y} = \nu - |\mathbf{q}|\) rather than Bjorken \(x\), and the observation of scaling does not automatically imply scattering from free constituents.

## Method

The authors analyze proton structure function \(W_1\) data from the MRS(A) fit at fixed three-momentum transfer \(|\mathbf{q}|\) as a function of \(\tilde{y} = \nu - |\mathbf{q}|\). They derive the response in the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) using a spectral function \(P_i(\mathbf{k}, e)\) for bound constituents, and discuss modifications due to final state interactions (FSI) via a folding function. Two illustrative examples are examined: the shift between valence u and d quark responses in the proton, and the modification of the deep inelastic response in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The width of the proton response in \(\tilde{y}\) is only a few hundred MeV, independent of \(|\mathbf{q}|\), implying an intrinsic energy scale of few hundred MeV.
- The shift between the valence quark responses \(\chi_1(\tilde{y})\) and \(\chi_0(\tilde{y})\) is approximately 0.1 GeV at \(\tilde{y} < -0.2\), compared to a perturbative expectation of \(\sim 0.2\) GeV from \((2/3)(m_\Delta - m)\).
- The nuclear matter response is shifted relative to the Fermi-motion-broadened response of noninteracting nucleons by approximately 40–60 MeV, comparable to the average nucleon removal energy of \(\sim 62\) MeV in nuclear matter.
- The analysis uses \(|\mathbf{q}| = 10\) GeV for the nuclear matter and valence quark response calculations.

## Context

This work extends the many-body \(y\)-scaling formalism previously applied to quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering (Day 1990) to the deep inelastic regime of the proton. It relates the scaling variable \(\tilde{y}\) to the Nachtmann variable \(\xi\) (Nachtmann 1973, Jaffe 1985) and provides a framework to interpret the EMC effect (Aubert 1983) and the Close-Thomas (1988) spin-dependent shift in terms of bound constituent dynamics. The approach enables a connection between parton distribution functions and nucleon spectral functions, and suggests that sum rules involving the spacelike response may be violated by timelike contributions from binding and final state interactions.
