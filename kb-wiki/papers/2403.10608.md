# Simulations of stochastic fluid dynamics near a critical point in the phase diagram

> We present simulations of stochastic fluid dynamics in the vicinity of a critical endpoint belonging to the universality class of the Ising model. This study is motivated by the challenge of modeling the dynamics of critical fluctuations near a conjectured critical endpoint in the phase diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We focus on the interaction of shear modes with a conserved scalar density, which is known as model H. We show that the observed dynamical scaling behavior depends on the correlation length and the shear viscosity of the fluid. As the correlation length is increased or the viscosity is decreased we observe a cross-over from the dynamical exponent of critical diffusion, $z\simeq 4$, to the expected scaling exponent of model H, $z\simeq 3$. We use our method to investigate time-dependent correlation function of non-Gaussian moments $M^n(t)$ of the order parameter. We find that the relaxation time depends in non-trivial manner on the power $n$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dynamical scaling exponent \(z\) in stochastic fluid dynamics near a critical point crosses over from \(z \simeq 4\) (critical diffusion) to \(z \simeq 3\) (model H) as the correlation length is increased or the shear viscosity is decreased, but the observation of genuine model H scaling requires both a very large correlation length and a small viscosity, which is numerically limited by the self-coupling of the momentum density.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the stochastic equations of model H (Hohenberg and Halperin classification) on a three-dimensional periodic lattice using a hybrid algorithm: a Metropolis step for the dissipative and stochastic updates of the order parameter \(\phi\) and transverse momentum density \(\pi^T\), followed by a deterministic third-order Runge-Kutta step for the advective mode-coupling terms. The advection step used skew-symmetric derivatives (Morinishi et al., 1998) to conserve the kinetic energy integrals exactly, and the transverse projection was applied in Fourier space after each momentum update.

## Key numbers

- The critical mass parameter for \(\lambda=4\) was determined to be \(m_c^2 = -2.2858\) in models A and B; in model H, a small shift of \(\delta m_c^2 = -0.030\) was observed due to the advection step.
- The renormalized shear viscosity \(\eta_R\) from the self-coupling of \(\pi^T\) follows \(\eta_R = \eta + (7/(60\pi^2)) (\rho T \Lambda / \eta)\) with UV cutoff \(\Lambda \simeq \pi/a\); the additive renormalization from critical fluctuations in model H0 is \(\eta_R = (T\xi_0)/(160\pi\Gamma)\).
- For bare viscosity \(\eta = 10^{-2}\) in model H0, the extracted dynamical exponent was \(z = 3.013 \pm 0.058\), consistent with the two-loop \(\epsilon\)-expansion prediction \(z \simeq 3.0712\).
- For QCD-relevant parameters (lattice spacing \(a = 0.75\) fm, time step \(\Delta t = 0.3\) fm, temperature \(T = 130\) MeV, enthalpy density \(\rho = 11.1\) in lattice units, \(\eta = 0.50\) corresponding to \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\)), the \(1/e\) decay time of the correlation functions \(G_n(t)\) exceeded \(10^3\) fm.

## Context

This work extends prior numerical studies of stochastic hydrodynamics for models A, B, and G (Florio et al., 2021; Schaefer et al., 2022; Chattopadhyay et al., 2023) to model H, which includes advective mode couplings between the order parameter and shear modes. It provides the first direct numerical simulation of model H, which is expected to govern the critical dynamics near a conjectured QCD critical endpoint (Son, 2004). The results enable future coupling of this stochastic framework to a realistic expanding background flow from conventional fluid dynamics simulations, or extension to fully relativistic stochastic fluid dynamics (Basar et al., 2024).
