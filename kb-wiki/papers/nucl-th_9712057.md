# Proton Polarization in the $p(\vec e,e'\vec p)π^0$ Reaction and the Measurement of Quadrupole Components in the N to $Δ$ Transition

> The recoil proton polarization in the $π^0$ production off the proton with longitudinally polarized electron beam has been studied as a means to measure quadrupole components in the N to $Δ$ transition. On top of the $Δ$ resonance a high sensitivity to a possible Coulomb quadrupole excitation is found in parallel kinematics. The ratio of $S_{1+}/M_{1+}$ multipole amplitudes can be determined from the ratio of the two in-scattering-plane recoil proton polarization components. Avoiding the absolute measurement of the polarizations, such a ratio allows small experimental uncertainties. Furthermore, the electron helicity independent proton polarization component enables the characterization of resonant and non-resonant pieces.

## Key claim

The ratio of the two in-scattering-plane recoil proton polarization components, \(P_x/P_z\), measured in parallel kinematics on top of the \(\Delta\) resonance, is directly related to \(\Re\{S_{1+}M_{1+}\}/|M_{1+}|^2\) and can determine the quadrupole-to-dipole multipole ratio \(S_{1+}/M_{1+}\) without requiring absolute calibration of either the electron beam polarization or the proton polarimeter analyzing power, thereby enabling small experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The paper derives the full 18-fold differential cross section for the \(p(\vec e,e'\vec p)\pi^0\) reaction in terms of structure functions and recoil proton polarization components \(P_n, P_l, P_t\) in the center-of-momentum frame. It then transforms these into the laboratory-frame \(\{x,y,z\}\) system fixed to the electron scattering plane, and specializes to strictly parallel kinematics (proton detected along the momentum transfer direction, \(\theta_p^{cm}=0\)). Using a multipole expansion restricted to s- and p-waves, the expressions for \(P_x\), \(P_y\), and \(P_z\) are reduced to combinations of the dominant magnetic dipole amplitude \(M_{1+}\) and the small longitudinal quadrupole amplitude \(S_{1+}\), along with other resonant and non-resonant multipoles. The separation of resonant and non-resonant contributions is performed via isospin decomposition into \(I=1/2\) and \(I=3/2\) channels, exploiting Watson's Final State Theorem.

## Key numbers

* At the MAMI kinematics (\(E=0.855\) GeV, \(W=1.232\) GeV, \(Q^2=0.12\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), \(\vartheta_e=32^\circ\), \(|\vec q|=0.53\) GeV), the longitudinal proton polarization is \(P_z = 0.7 \cdot h\), yielding \(P_z = 49\%\) for a beam polarization of 70\%.
* For a quadrupole contribution of \(-5\%\) in \(\Re\{S_{1+}M_{1+}\}/|M_{1+}|^2\), the transverse polarization is \(P_x = -2.2 \cdot h \cdot (\Re\{S_{1+}M_{1+}\}/|M_{1+}|^2)\), giving \(P_x \simeq 7.6\%\) with 70\% beam polarization.
* Early coincidence experiments at NINA and DESY extracted ratios of Coulomb quadrupole to magnetic dipole strength, \(S_{1+}/M_{1+}\), around \(-6\%\) over a range of four-momentum transfers from 0.3 to 1.56 (GeV/c)\(^2\).
* A fixed-t dispersion-relation reanalysis by Crawford (1971) of older data yielded ratios of about \(-15\%\) at momentum transfers down to 0.047 (GeV/c)\(^2\).
* A recent experiment at ELSA obtained a ratio of \((-11.0 \pm 3.7)\%\).

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to measure quadrupole components in the N to \(\Delta\) transition, which were limited by large experimental uncertainties in coincidence experiments at NINA (Siddle, 1971) and DESY (Alder, 1972; Albrecht, 1971) and by the fixed-t dispersion-relation reanalysis of Crawford (1971). It builds on the formalism of Raskin and Donnelly (1988) for polarized electroproduction and on the isospin decomposition approach of Komar (1989). The proposed ratio method avoids the systematic uncertainties that plagued absolute polarization measurements, and the use of the electron-helicity-independent component \(P_y\) enables separation of resonant and non-resonant contributions, which was achieved for the first time at the photon point in a MAMI photoproduction experiment by Beck and collaborators. This enables a precise determination of the \(S_{1+}/M_{1+}\) ratio as a function of four-momentum transfer, constraining the spatial distribution of electric charge in the N to \(\Delta\) transition.
