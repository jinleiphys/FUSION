# Balitsky-Kovchegov equation at next-to-leading order accuracy with a resummation of large logarithms

> We include resummation of large transverse logarithms into the next-to-leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. The resummed NLO evolution equation is shown to be stable, the evolution speed being significantly reduced by higher order corrections. The contributions from $α_s^2$ terms that are not enhanced by large logarithms are found to be numerically important close to phenomenologically relevant initial conditions.

# Balitsky-Kovchegov equation at next-to-leading order accuracy with a resummation of large logarithms

## Key claim
The next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation, when supplemented with resummation of large single and double transverse logarithms, yields stable evolution with significantly reduced evolution speed compared to leading order; however, the fixed-order alpha_s^2 terms that are not enhanced by large logarithms remain numerically important near phenomenologically relevant initial conditions, meaning they cannot be neglected in that regime.

## Method
The authors numerically solve the NLO BK equation in the large-N_c and mean field limit, incorporating resummation of double logarithmic corrections via a factor K_DLA and single transverse logarithms via K_STL, with a subtraction term K_sub to remove double counting between the resummed logarithms and the explicit O(alpha_s^2) kernels K_2 and K_f. The constant C_sub in the resummation factor is fixed to 0.65 by requiring that K_sub reproduces the small-r limit of other NLO terms. The initial condition is the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with resummation corrections as introduced by Iancu et al. (2015).

## Key numbers
- The resummed NLO BK equation evolves about 30% slower than the leading order equation with running coupling corrections.
- The constant C_sub in the resummation factor is fixed to 0.65.
- The saturation scale Q_s^2 is defined by N(r^2 = 2/Q_s^2) = 1 - e^{-1/2}.
- The saturation scale at the initial condition is approximately 1 GeV.
- The coefficient A_1 in the resummation exponent is 11/12.

## Context
This work extends the numerical solution of the NLO BK equation first attempted by Lappi and Mäntysaari (2015), which found unphysical negative evolution speeds for phenomenologically relevant initial conditions. The resummation scheme follows the theoretical developments by Iancu et al. (2015) for resumming large logarithmic contributions to all orders. The results enable future phenomenological NLO calculations of deep inelastic scattering and particle production, addressing the known discrepancy where leading order fits to HERA data prefer slower evolution speeds than naturally obtained in the Color Glass Condensate picture (Lappi and Mäntysaari, 2013).
