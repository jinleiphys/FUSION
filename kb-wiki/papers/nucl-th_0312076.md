# A$(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$B responses: from bare nucleons to complex nuclei

> We study the occurrence of factorization in polarized and unpolarized observables in coincidence quasi-elastic electron scattering. Starting with the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation, we reformulate the effective momentum approximation and show that the latter leads to observables which factorize under some specific conditions. Within this framework, the role played by final state interactions and, in particular, by the spin-orbit term is explored. Connection with the nonrelativistic formalism is studied in depth. Numerical results are presented to illustrate the analytical derivations and to quantify the differences between factorized and unfactorized approaches.

## Key claim

Factorization of polarized and unpolarized observables in quasielastic A(\(\vec{e},e'\vec{p}\))B reactions can be recovered from the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) only under specific conditions: the effective momentum approximation (EMA) must be applied to remove dynamical enhancement of lower components of nucleon wave functions, and the spin-orbit dependence of the problem must be suppressed, particularly for the initial bound nucleon state.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) and reformulate the effective momentum approximation (EMA) originally introduced by Kelly (1997). They express the one-body nucleon current in EMA as a sum over spin projections of the bare nucleon current times an amplitude depending on bound and ejected nucleon wave functions. They then analyze the hadronic tensor and response functions to identify conditions under which factorization holds, focusing on the role of spin-orbit coupling in the initial and final states, and compare with nonrelativistic formalism.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized cross section in nonrelativistic PWIA factorizes exactly as \(\left(\frac{d\sigma}{d\varepsilon_f d\Omega_f d\Omega_F}\right)^{PWIA} = E_F p_F f_{rec}\, \sigma_{ep} \, N_{NR}(\mathbf{p}_m)\), where \(\sigma_{ep}\) is the bare electron-proton cross section and \(N_{NR}(\mathbf{p}_m)\) is the nonrelativistic momentum distribution.
- Factorization is broken in relativistic PWIA due to negative-energy components of the bound nucleon wave function, even without final state interactions.
- For knockout from s-shells (\(\ell_b=0\)), the spin-dependent momentum distribution simplifies to \(X_{ss',hh'}^{s_F}(\mathbf{p}_F,\mathbf{q}) = N_{ss'}^{s_F}(\mathbf{p}_F,\mathbf{q}) \, \delta_{hh'}\), eliminating dependence on initial bound nucleon spin variables.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of factorization in \((e,e'p)\) reactions by Frullani and Mougey (1985) and Caballero et al. (1998), which established that factorization holds in nonrelativistic PWIA but is broken in relativistic PWIA. It also builds on the EMA approach of Kelly (1997) and prior RDWIA calculations by Udias et al. (1993, 1999, 2000, 2001). The analysis enables quantitative assessment of factorization breakdown in both polarized and unpolarized observables, which is essential for interpreting experimental reduced cross sections and ratios of transferred polarizations used to extract nucleon form factors.
