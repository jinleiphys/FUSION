# n-Boson Energies at Finite Volume and Three-Boson Interactions

> We calculate the volume dependence of the ground-state energy of n identical bosons with short-range repulsive interactions in a periodic spatial volume of side L, up to and including terms of order 1/L^6. With this result, Lattice QCD calculations of the ground-state energies of three or more pions will allow for a systematic extraction of the three-pion interaction at this order in the volume expansion.

# n-Boson Energies at Finite Volume and Three-Boson Interactions

## Key claim

The ground-state energy of n identical bosons in a periodic cubic volume of side L can be computed up to and including order 1/L^6, where the three-body interaction first enters; this enables extraction of the three-pion interaction from Lattice QCD calculations, but only if the two-body effective range and a renormalization-scale-dependent three-body coupling are simultaneously determined.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic quantum mechanics with short-range repulsive interactions modeled by pseudo-potentials, extending the classic Huang-Yang (1957) hard-sphere result. They perform perturbative calculations up to fourth order in the two-body potential (order V^4) using dimensional regularization to handle divergent integer sums that arise from the finite-volume periodic boundary conditions. The resulting expressions for the ground-state energy include contributions from two-body scattering length a, two-body effective range r, and a momentum-independent three-body contact interaction η₃(μ), with the renormalization scale μ introduced to absorb poles in d-3 dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the classic results of Huang and Yang (1957) for hard-sphere bosons from order 1/L⁵ to order 1/L⁶, and agrees with Wu (1959) for the three-body case modulo renormalization issues. The two-body finite-volume result connects to Lüscher's (1986, 1990) general field-theoretic derivation. The calculation provides the necessary formalism for Lattice QCD studies of multi-pion systems, enabling extraction of the three-pion interaction from correlation functions; the authors also note that for fermionic systems like three neutrons, the Pauli principle forces nonzero momentum in the ground state, making the volume dependence more complicated than for bosons.
