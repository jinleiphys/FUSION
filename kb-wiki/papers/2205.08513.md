# An updated nuclear-physics and multi-messenger astrophysics framework for binary neutron star mergers

> The multi-messenger detection of the gravitational-wave signal GW170817, the corresponding kilonova AT2017gfo and the short gamma-ray burst GRB170817A, as well as the observed afterglow has delivered a scientific breakthrough. For an accurate interpretation of all these different messengers, one requires robust theoretical models that describe the emitted gravitational-wave, the electromagnetic emission, and dense matter reliably. In addition, one needs efficient and accurate computational tools to ensure a correct cross-correlation between the models and the observational data. For this purpose, we have developed the Nuclear-physics and Multi-Messenger Astrophysics framework NMMA. The code allows incorporation of nuclear-physics constraints at low densities as well as X-ray and radio observations of isolated neutron stars. In previous works, the NMMA code has allowed us to constrain the equation of state of supranuclear dense matter, to measure the Hubble constant, and to compare dense-matter physics probed in neutron-star mergers and in heavy-ion collisions, and to classify electromagnetic observations and perform model selection. Here, we show an extension of the NMMA code as a first attempt of analyzing the gravitational-wave signal, the kilonova, and the gamma-ray burst afterglow simultaneously. Incorporating all available information, we estimate the radius of a $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star to be $R=11.98^{+0.35}_{-0.40}$km.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extension of the NMMA framework that performs a simultaneous analysis of gravitational-wave, kilonova, and gamma-ray burst afterglow data for the first time, finding that the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 11.98 km with asymmetric uncertainties of +0.35 and -0.40 km at 90% confidence, though the authors note this is a "first attempt" and that additional uncertainties remain from limited physics input in kilonova and semi-analytic GRB models.

## Method

The authors developed the Nuclear-physics and Multi-Messenger Astrophysics (NMMA) framework, which combines Bayesian inference on gravitational-wave data (using the IMRPhenomPv2_NRTidalv2 waveform model from 20 Hz to 2048 Hz), kilonova light curves modeled with Gaussian Process Regression, and GRB afterglow data analyzed with a synthetic Gaussian jet-model. The equation of state is constructed using chiral effective field theory calculations with Quantum Monte Carlo methods up to about twice nuclear saturation density, extended to higher densities using a speed-of-sound extension scheme with linear segments up to 12 times saturation density, and then solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R = 11.98 (+0.35, -0.40) km at 90% confidence from the combined GW170817-AT2017gfo-GRB170817A analysis
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R = 11.86 (+0.41, -0.53) km at 90% confidence from the GW170817-AT2017gfo analysis only
- The half-width of the 90% credible interval for R_1.4 decreased from about 800 m in previous work (Dietrich et al. 2020) to about 400 m in the current analysis
- Dynamical ejecta mass of about 0.006 solar masses and disk-wind ejecta mass of about 0.07 solar masses from the kilonova fit
- The analysis used gravitational-wave data from 20 Hz to 2048 Hz and electromagnetic data from 0.5 to 10 days after merger

## Context

This work extends the NMMA framework previously applied by Dietrich et al. (2020) and Pang et al. (2021) to constrain the equation of state and Hubble constant, now enabling simultaneous analysis of all three messengers (GW, kilonova, GRB afterglow) rather than combining constraints a posteriori. The results improve upon prior multi-messenger studies including those by Breschi et al. (2021), Nicholl et al. (2021), and Raaijmakers et al. (2021), and the framework enables future joint analyses of binary neutron star and black-hole-neutron-star mergers as more multi-messenger detections become available.
