# Low densities in nuclear and neutron matters and in the nuclear surface

> Nuclear and neutron matters are investigated in the low density region, well below the nuclear saturation density. Microscopic calculations, based on the Bethe-Brueckner approach with a few realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, are compared with the predictions of a set of phenomenological effective interactions, mostly employed in nuclear structure studies. An energy functional is constructed on the basis of the microscopic bulk EoS and applied to a selection of nuclei throughout the mass table. The results provide a microscopic basis for a link between nuclear surface behaviour and neutron EoS previously observed with phenomenological effective forces. Possible effects of pairing on asymmetric nuclear matter are also analyzed in detail. The results are expected to illuminate the physical mechanisms which determine the behaviour of the surface density tail in exotic nuclei.

# Low densities in nuclear and neutron matters and in the nuclear surface

## Key claim

The low-density equation of state (EoS) for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, calculated microscopically via the Bethe-Brueckner approach, is practically identical across different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne v14, Argonne v18, Paris) and can therefore be considered established in the density range from about one-tenth to one-half of saturation density. However, this agreement holds only for densities above approximately 0.02 fm⁻³, below which clustering phenomena (deuteron, triton, helium, alpha formation) are expected to occur and the microscopic approach is no longer reliable.

## Method

The authors performed Bethe-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations with the continuous choice for the single-particle spectrum, using three realistic two-body forces (Argonne v14, Argonne v18, Paris) supplemented by a three-body force whose contribution is negligible below 0.1 fm⁻³. They compared these microscopic results with predictions from phenomenological effective interactions (Skyrme forces Sly4 and SkM*, and the Gogny force) for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and asymmetric matter via the quadratic interpolation formula e = e_n·β² + (1 − β²)·e_s. They then constructed a microscopically based energy density functional by combining the BHF bulk correlation energy with a phenomenological gradient term η(∇ρ)² and a spin-orbit term, and performed Kohn-Sham calculations for the magic nuclei ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb. Pairing correlations were included in BCS approximation using the bare force for asymmetric matter near β ≈ 0.75.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density obtained from the microscopic functional is ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³, with corresponding energy E/A = −15.4 MeV.
- The incompressibility at saturation is K = 247 MeV; the symmetry energy at saturation is a_y = 31.34 MeV.
- The optimal gradient coefficient for the microscopically based functional is η ≈ −53 MeV fm⁵, compared to the SkM* value of η = −68 MeV fm⁵.
- The spin-orbit strength is fixed to 130 MeV fm⁵.
- For the Gogny force at ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, a_y = 31.62 MeV; for SkM*, a_y = 31.35 MeV; for Sly4, a_y = 32.72 MeV.
- The three-body force contribution at ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³ is less than 0.2 MeV.
- For asymmetry β ≈ 0.75, the microscopic EoS is practically flat with E/A ∼ −400 keV over the considered density range.
- With pairing included, a depression about 200 keV deep forms around ρ = ρ₀/5 to ρ₀/8 for β ≈ 0.75.
- The surface energy coefficient for ²⁰⁸Pb reduces by about a factor of 1.7 when η is changed from −53 to 0.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Brown (2000) and others of a linear correlation between the neutron-proton root-mean-square radius difference in ²⁰⁸Pb and the slope of the neutron matter EoS at ρ = 0.1 fm⁻³, which had been found using phenomenological Skyrme forces and relativistic mean-field functionals. The authors provide a microscopic justification for this correlation by showing that the bulk part of the energy functional, rather than gradient terms, dominates the value of r_n − r_p. The results enable future fine-tuning of phenomenological effective forces for low-density applications, particularly for describing the surface region of exotic neutron-rich nuclei, and suggest that an accurate measurement of the neutron radius in ²⁰⁸Pb via parity-violating electron scattering could discriminate between non-relativistic microscopic functionals and relativistic mean-field predictions.
