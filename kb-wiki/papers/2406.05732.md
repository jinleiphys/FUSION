# Relativistic Corrections to the CBF Effective Nuclear Hamiltonian

> We discuss the inclusion of relativistic boost corrections into the CBF effective nuclear Hamiltonian, derived from a realistic model of two- and three-nucleon interactions using the formalism of correlated basis functions and the cluster expansion technique. Different procedures to take into account the effects of boost interactions are compared on the basis of the ability to reproduce the nuclear matter equation of state obtained from accurate many-body calculations. The results of our study show that the repulsive contribution of the boost interaction significantly depends on the underlying model of the non relativistic potential. On the other hand, the dominant relativistic correction turns out to be the corresponding reduction of the strength of repulsive three-nucleon interactions, leading to a significant softening of the equation of state at supranuclear densities.

## Key claim

The dominant relativistic correction to the nuclear matter equation of state arises not from the direct repulsive boost interaction itself, but from the associated reduction of the repulsive three-nucleon interaction strength, leading to a significant softening at supranuclear densities; however, the magnitude of the boost interaction's repulsive contribution depends strongly on which underlying non-relativistic potential model is used.

## Method

The authors incorporated relativistic boost corrections into the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) effective nuclear Hamiltonian by treating the boost interaction as a perturbation added to the effective interaction derived from the AV6P+UIX Hamiltonian. The correlation functions were fitted to reproduce the non-relativistic equation of state, and the boost correction was computed using the approximation of Forest et al. (1995), neglecting non-static NN components and the Thomas precession term. The effective interaction was then used to compute the ground-state energy of pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, comparing results obtained using the AV6P potential versus the truncated AV18 potential for the boost interaction.

## Key numbers

- The strength of the repulsive three-nucleon interaction (UIX) is reduced by a factor γ = 0.63 when boost corrections are included, leading to the modified UIX* potential.
- The maximum neutron star mass decreases from 2.3 to 2.2 solar masses (M⊙) when boost corrections are included, based on the APR equation of state.
- The radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star is reduced from 12.7 km to 11.6 km with boost corrections.
- At saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the discrepancy between the energies per particle of pure neutron matter computed using the AV6P potential and the full AV18 potential is approximately 10-15%.

## Context

This work extends the CBF effective Hamiltonian formalism of Benhar and Lovato (2017) to include relativistic boost corrections, following the approach originally developed by Krajcik and Foldy (1974) and Friar (1975), and applied to nuclear matter by Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star properties by providing a density-dependent effective interaction that incorporates relativistic effects, and they highlight that the choice of the non-relativistic NN potential model significantly affects the predicted boost correction magnitude.
