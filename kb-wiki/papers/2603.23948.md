# Thermalization of SU(2) Lattice Gauge Fields on Quantum Computers

> We simulate the thermalization dynamics for minimally truncated SU(2) pure gauge theory on linear plaquette chains with up to 151 plaquettes using IBM quantum computers. We study the time dependence of the entanglement spectrum, Rényi-2 entropy and anti-flatness on small subsystems. The quantum hardware results obtained after error mitigation agree with extrapolated classical simulator results for chains consisting of up to 101 plaquettes. Our results demonstrate the feasibility of local thermalization studies for chaotic quantum systems, such as nonabelian lattice gauge theories, on current noisy quantum computing platforms.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that local thermalization dynamics of a minimally truncated SU(2) pure gauge theory can be simulated on current noisy IBM quantum computers for linear plaquette chains with up to 151 qubits, but only after error mitigation and only for chains up to 101 plaquettes do the hardware results agree with extrapolated classical simulations; for chains exceeding 129 plaquettes, connectivity overhead on the hardware sharply increases gate depth and limits the reliable simulation time.

## Method

The authors map the (2+1)-dimensional Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian for SU(2) pure gauge theory on a linear plaquette chain, truncated to the two lowest electric field representations (j=0,1/2), onto a one-dimensional spin model with Pauli operators. They implement first-order Trotterized time evolution on IBM quantum computers (ibm_aachen, ibm_boston, ibm_torino) for chains of N=5 to 151 plaquettes, starting from the strong-coupling vacuum state. Subsystem state tomography is performed on small subsystems (N_A=1,2,3) to reconstruct reduced density matrices, from which the entanglement spectrum, Rényi-2 entropy, and anti-flatness are computed; error mitigation uses dynamical decoupling (XY4 sequence), Pauli twirling (100 random instances), and operator decoherence renormalization.

## Key numbers

* System sizes simulated: N = 5, 7, 9, 15, 51, 101, 131, and 151 plaquettes (qubits).
* Coupling fixed at ag^2 = 1.2, where the plaquette chain exhibits quantum chaos for N ~ 20 (Yao 2023).
* Time evolution performed to final time t = 1 with first-order Trotter step size delta t = 1/3.
* For N=101, the two-qubit gate depth per Trotter step is 63 and the number of two-qubit gates is 964; for N=151, the two-qubit gate depth increases to 502 and the number of two-qubit gates to 2703.
* For N=101 and N=133, 8,000 shots per Pauli string measurement were used; for other N values, 4,000 shots were used.
* The linear fit for two-qubit gate count versus system size gives slope c_1 = 15.388 for N <= 129, with deviation for N > 129 due to connectivity limits of the ibm_aachen hardware (which supports up to 156 qubits but only 129 in a linear snake arrangement).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermalization in nonabelian lattice gauge theories, specifically the demonstration that the minimally truncated SU(2) theory on a plaquette chain satisfies the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (Yao 2023, Ebner 2023) and that the growth of entanglement entropy is connected to anti-flatness as a witness of nonlocal magic (Ebner 2025). It builds on prior quantum simulations of gauge theories that were mostly constrained to one-dimensional models without dynamical gauge fields (Mueller 2022, Mueller 2024, Halimeh 2025). The results enable future studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics of nonabelian gauge fields on near-term quantum computers, particularly during the period of rapid entanglement growth where classical simulation becomes inefficient due to elevated quantum magic.
