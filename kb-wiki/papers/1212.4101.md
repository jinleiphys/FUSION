# Azimuthal Asymmetries From Jets Quenched In Fluctuating Backgrounds

> High momentum jets and hadrons are important probes for the quark gluon plasma (QGP) formed in nuclear collisions at high energies. We investigate how fluctuations in the background density of the QGP and fluctuations in the spatial distribution of the hard process create azimuthal asymmetries of the high momentum hadron spectrum, described by the Fourier coefficients $v_n$, $n > 0$ . We estimate the coefficients up to $v_6$ in a simple energy loss model tuned to single inclusive hadron suppression.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that fluctuations in the QGP background density and in the spatial distribution of hard processes create non-zero azimuthal asymmetry coefficients \(v_n\) for high-momentum hadrons up to \(v_6\), but these coefficients are not particularly useful for discriminating between different energy loss models.

## Method

The authors used the PPM simulation package to sample initial quark and gluon jet distributions from perturbative QCD and propagate leading partons through a fluctuating fireball background generated by the Glauber Monte Carlo generator GLISSANDO. They employed a simple LPM-inspired (sLPM) deterministic energy loss model \(dE/dx \sim \hat q x\) with \(\hat q\) scaling as the 3/4th power of local energy density, and cross-checked results with the non-deterministic Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) model. Engineered events with fixed eccentricities were used to study correlations between \(v_n\) and \(\epsilon_n\), while realistic Au+Au events at top RHIC energy were used for final predictions.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy were simulated at three impact parameters: \(b = 3.2\), \(7.4\), and \(11\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- For engineered events, \(v_n\) rises monotonically with \(\epsilon_n\) for \(n = 3, 4, 5, 6\); deviations from monotony occur only for unrealistically large eccentricities.
- For realistic GLISSANDO events, the correlation between \(v_2\) and \(\epsilon_2\) persists but is washed out; correlations for \(n > 2\) are much more weakened.
- The phases \(\delta_3\) and \(\delta_5\) are randomly distributed relative to the reaction plane; \(\delta_4\) and \(\delta_6\) show correlation consistent with contributions from \(\epsilon_2\).
- For pions, the hierarchy of coefficients is \(v_2 > v_4 > v_6\) and \(v_1 > v_3 > v_5\); \(v_2\) is always the largest coefficient even in the most central events; \(v_1\) is the second largest, beating \(v_3\) and \(v_4\) by more than a factor of 2.
- Results become unreliable below 4 to 6 \(\mathrm{GeV}\) because hydrodynamic expansion was not included.

## Context

This work extends studies of initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to bulk quantities by Alver (2008, 2010) and Qin (2010), to hard probes at high transverse momentum. It shows that fluctuations produce non-zero odd \(v_n\) at high \(P_T\) analogous to triangular flow \(v_3\) in the bulk, but finds that the azimuthal asymmetry coefficients are not effective for distinguishing between different energy loss models (sLPM vs. ASW). The results enable future measurements of \(v_n\) at large momentum to provide complementary geometrical information to bulk observables and to constrain the size and granularity of initial-state fluctuations.
