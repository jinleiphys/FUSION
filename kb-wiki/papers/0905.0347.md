# Closed orbits and spatial density oscillations in the circular billiard

> We present a case study for the semiclassical calculation of the oscillations in the particle and kinetic-energy densities for the two-dimensional circular billiard. For this system, we can give a complete classification of all closed periodic and non-periodic orbits. We discuss their bifurcations under variation of the starting point r and derive analytical expressions for their properties such as actions, stability determinants, momentum mismatches and Morse indices. We present semiclassical calculations of the spatial density oscillations using a recently developed closed-orbit theory [Roccia J and Brack M 2008 Phys. Rev. Lett. 100 200408], employing standard uniform approximations from perturbation and bifurcation theory, and test the convergence of the closed-orbit sum.

## Key claim

The semiclassical closed-orbit theory developed by Roccia and Brack (2008) can successfully reproduce spatial density oscillations in the two-dimensional circular billiard, but only when standard uniform approximations are employed to regularize divergences at critical points including the center (r=0), bifurcation points, and the boundary (r=R), and when the convergence of the closed-orbit sum is carefully tested against the exact quantum-mechanical results.

## Method

The authors classify all closed periodic and non-periodic orbits in the circular billiard by pairs of integers (v,w) where v is the number of reflections at the boundary and w is the winding number around the center, deriving analytical expressions for their actions, stability determinants, momentum mismatches, and Morse indices. They then compute the oscillating parts of the particle density and kinetic-energy densities using semiclassical closed-orbit theory, summing contributions from both radial and non-radial non-periodic orbits, and regularize divergences at symmetry-breaking points, bifurcation points, and the boundary using uniform approximations from perturbation and bifurcation theory. The semiclassical results are compared to exact quantum-mechanical densities obtained by summing the eigenfunctions of the circular billiard (given by Bessel functions with Dirichlet boundary conditions) up to the Fermi energy.

## Key numbers

* The smooth (Thomas-Fermi) particle density is given by ρ_TF = mλ̃/(πℏ²) = (1/(2πR²))(λ̃/E₀), where E₀ = ℏ²/(2mR²) is the energy unit and λ̃ is the smooth Fermi energy.
* The asymptotic expansion of the Fermi energy as a function of particle number N is λ̃(N) = E₀[2N + 2(2N)^(1/2) + 4/3 + O(N^(-1/2))].
* The length of a radial orbit L_±^(k)(r) = 2[(2k+1)R ∓ r], where k is the repetition number and r is the distance from the center.
* The Jacobian for radial orbits is J_±^(k)(r) = (2/(Rp_λ)) r[(2k+1)r ∓ R], which becomes zero at r_k = R/(2k+1) for the L_+^(k) orbits, creating non-radial NPOs in pitchfork bifurcations.
* The caustic points where periodic orbits are created from non-radial NPOs are at r_(v,w)^(PO) = R cos(ϕ_(v,w)), where ϕ_(v,w) = wπ/v is the half polar angle between successive reflections.

## Context

This work extends the closed-orbit theory for spatial density oscillations developed by Roccia and Brack (2008) from one-dimensional systems to a two-dimensional system with circular symmetry, providing the first complete analytical classification of all closed orbits in the circular billiard. The paper builds on Gutzwiller's semiclassical trace formula for the level density (1971) and the Balian-Bloch classification of periodic orbits in the circular billiard (1970), and enables systematic testing of the convergence of semiclassical orbit sums for spatial densities, which is essential for applying these methods to more realistic nuclear mean-field potentials and for understanding shell effects in finite fermion systems.
