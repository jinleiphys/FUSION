# Estimating the Charm Quark Diffusion Coefficient and thermalization time from D meson spectra at RHIC and LHC

> We describe the propagation of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by means of a Boltzmann transport approach. Non-perturbative interaction between heavy quarks and light quarks have been taken into account through a quasi-particle approach in which light partons are dressed with thermal masses tuned to lQCD thermodynamics. Such a model is able to describe the main feature of the non-perturbative dynamics: the enhancement of the interaction strength near $T_c$. We show that the resulting charm in-medium evolution is able to correctly predict simultaneously the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{AA}$, and the elliptic flow, $v_2$, at both RHIC and LHC energies and at different centralities. The hadronization of charm quarks is described by mean of an hybrid model of fragmentation plus coalescence and plays a key role toward the agreeement with experimental data. We also performed calculations within the Langevin approach which can lead to very similar $R_{AA}(p_T)$ as Boltzmann, but the charm drag coefficient as to be reduced by about a $30\%$ and also generates an elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ is about a $15\%$ smaller. We finally compare the space diffusion coefficient $2πTD_s$ extracted by our phenomenological approach to lattice QCD results, finding a satisfying agreement within the present systematic uncertainties. Our analysis implies a charm thermalization time, in the $p\rightarrow 0$ limit, of about $4-6 \, fm/c$ which is smaller than the QGP lifetime at LHC energy.

# Estimating the Charm Quark Diffusion Coefficient and thermalization time from D meson spectra at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The quasi-particle model (QPM) for charm quark propagation in the quark-gluon plasma, solved via a Boltzmann transport approach, can simultaneously describe the nuclear suppression factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 of D mesons at both RHIC and LHC energies, but only when hadronization includes both coalescence and fragmentation; the Langevin approach requires a drag coefficient reduced by about 30% to match the same R_AA and still produces a v_2 about 15% smaller than the Boltzmann result.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for charm quarks scattering in a bulk medium of quarks and gluons, where the bulk evolution is constrained to a specific shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s = 1/4 pi) and reproduces the lattice QCD equation of state via thermal masses for light partons. The scattering matrix for heavy quark interactions is evaluated using leading-order diagrams with an effective temperature-dependent coupling g(T) extracted from a fit to lattice QCD thermodynamics, which produces an enhancement of the interaction strength near the critical temperature T_c. Hadronization of charm quarks into D mesons is treated with a hybrid model of coalescence (using a Gaussian Wigner function with width Delta_p = 0.283 GeV determined from the D meson charge radius) plus Peterson fragmentation, with the coalescence probability computed for each charm quark in phase space.

## Key numbers

* Charm thermalization time in the p to 0 limit: 4-6 fm/c, which is smaller than the QGP lifetime at LHC energy.
* Drag coefficient in Langevin dynamics must be reduced by about 30% relative to the Boltzmann transport approach to produce a similar R_AA(p_T).
* Elliptic flow v_2 from Langevin dynamics is about 15% smaller than from Boltzmann dynamics at the charm quark level.
* Coalescence probability for charm quarks hadronizing to D mesons (D^0, D^+, D^{*0}, D^{+*}) decreases with p_T and is marginally smaller at LHC than at RHIC due to the harder charm quark distribution at LHC.
* Initial maximum temperature at RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV): T_0 = 345 MeV with initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
* Initial maximum temperature at LHC (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 ATeV): T_0 = 490 MeV with initial time tau_0 = 0.3 fm/c.
* Peterson fragmentation parameter epsilon_c = 0.006 for RHIC and epsilon_c = 0.02 for LHC, fixed by comparison to p+p D meson data.
* Wigner function width parameter Delta_p = 0.283 GeV, determined from the D^+ meson mean square charge radius of 0.43 fm.
* Critical temperature for hadronization hypersurface: T = 155 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model approach previously applied within a Langevin framework by Das (2015) to a Boltzmann transport solution, and includes for the first time a hybrid coalescence plus fragmentation hadronization model for heavy quarks at both RHIC and LHC energies. The results are compared to experimental data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration at LHC, and the extracted spatial diffusion coefficient 2 pi T D_s is compared to lattice QCD results. The study enables a more reliable extraction of heavy quark transport coefficients from experimental data by quantifying the systematic differences between Boltzmann and Langevin dynamics, and by demonstrating that non-perturbative interactions near T_c (as in the quasi-particle model) are essential to simultaneously describe R_AA and v_2, in contrast to leading-order pQCD or AdS/CFT approaches which predict a T^2 dependence of the drag coefficient.
