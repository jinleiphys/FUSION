# The strangeness form factors of the proton

> The present empirical information on the strangeness form factors indicates that the corresponding $uuds\bar s$ component in the proton is such that the $uuds$ subsystem has the flavor spin symmetry $[4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S$ and mixed orbital symmetry $[31]_X$. This $uuds\bar s$ configuration leads to the empirical signs of all the form factors $G_E^s$, $G_M^s$ and $G_A^s$. An analysis with simple quark model wave functions for the preferred configuration shows that the qualitative features of the empirical strangeness form factors may be described with a $\sim$ 15% admixture of $uuds\bar s$ with a compact wave function in the proton. Transition matrix elements between the $uud$ and the $uuds\bar s$ components give significant contributions.

## Key claim

The empirical signs of the strangeness form factors \(G_E^s\), \(G_M^s\), and \(G_A^s\) can be explained by a specific \(uuds\bar s\) configuration in the proton where the \(uuds\) subsystem has flavor-spin symmetry \([4]_{FS}[22]_F[22]_S\) and mixed orbital symmetry \([31]_X\), provided the \(uuds\bar s\) component is compact (wave function radius \(\sim 0.2\)–\(0.3\) fm) and constitutes about 10–15% of the proton wave function.

## Method

The authors used a harmonic oscillator constituent quark model to compute the strangeness form factors from both diagonal matrix elements (within the \(uuds\bar s\) component) and non-diagonal transition matrix elements between the \(uud\) and \(uuds\bar s\) components. They treated the oscillator parameter \(\omega\), the probability \(P_{s\bar s}\) of the \(uuds\bar s\) component, and the relative phase \(\delta\) between the \(uud\) and \(uuds\bar s\) components as phenomenological parameters, fixing the strange quark constituent mass at 400 MeV/c\(^2\). The calculated form factors were compared to experimental data from the G0 experiment and phenomenological extractions from combined neutrino scattering and parity-violating electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The probability of the \(uuds\bar s\) component in the proton is taken as \(P_{s\bar s} = 0.10\)–\(0.15\) (10–15%).
- The oscillator parameter \(\omega\) is taken as 1 GeV, corresponding to a matter radius of \(1/\omega \simeq 0.2\) fm for the strangeness component.
- The overlap integral \(C_{35}\) between the \(uud\) and \(uuds\bar s\) wave functions is \(C_{35} \sim 0.4\) for a compact \(uuds\bar s\) wave function (\(\omega \sim 2\omega_3\)).
- The strangeness contribution to the proton spin at \(q^2=0\) is \(G_A^s(0) = -0.03\) to \(-0.07\), falling within the empirical range of 0 to \(-0.10\).
- The strange quark constituent mass is taken as 400 MeV/c\(^2\).
- The oscillator parameter must be larger than \(\sim 0.7\) GeV (radius \(\sim 0.3\) fm) to reproduce the data.

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive analysis of all \(uuds\bar s\) configurations with at most one orbitally excited quark by Zou (2001, 2002), which identified the lowest-energy configuration with positive strangeness magnetic moment and negative strangeness spin contribution. The present results provide a qualitative description of the momentum dependence of the strangeness form factors measured by the G0 experiment (Beck, 2004) and phenomenologically extracted by Pate (2004) from combined neutrino and parity-violating electron scattering data. The model uniquely predicts both a positive strange magnetic moment \(\mu_s\) and a positive strangeness radius \(r_s\), in agreement with current empirical values, and enables further tests with higher-precision measurements of the individual form factors.
