# The interaction region of high energy protons

> The spatial view of the interaction region of colliding high energy protons (in terms of impact parameter) is considered. It is shown that the region of inelastic collisions has a very peculiar shape. It saturates for central collisions at an energy of 7 TeV. We speculate on the further evolution with energy, which is contrasted to the "black disk" picture.

# The interaction region of high energy protons

## Key claim
The inelastic interaction region of colliding protons saturates to full absorption (a "black" center) at 7 TeV, but further energy increases could lead to a toroid-like shape with a dip at the center, contrary to the conventional "black disk" picture where the entire disk becomes uniformly black.

## Method
The authors used the unitarity condition of the S-matrix to relate elastic scattering data to the inelastic overlap function in impact-parameter space. They applied a Fourier–Bessel transform to the elastic scattering amplitude, assuming an exponential diffraction cone, and derived an analytic expression for the inelastic profile G(s,b) as a function of the measured quantities σ_tot and the diffraction cone slope B(s). The key parameter ζ = σ_tot/(4πB) ≈ 4σ_el/σ_tot determines the shape and absorption at each energy.

## Key numbers
- At √s = 7 TeV, ζ = 1.00–1.02 and G(s,0) = 1.00, indicating full absorption at the center of the collision.
- At √s = 62.5 GeV (ISR), ζ = 0.67 and G(s,0) = 0.89, showing semi-transparent central collisions.
- At √s = 546 GeV (Spp̄S), ζ = 0.83 and G(s,0) = 0.97.
- At √s = 1800 GeV (Tevatron), ζ = 0.93 and G(s,0) = 0.995.
- The ratio σ_el/σ_tot = 0.257 ± 0.005 at 7 TeV (TOTEM) would imply ζ = 1.01 with ~2% uncertainty.
- The diffraction cone slope B ≈ 20 GeV⁻² at 7 TeV, giving a plateau in G(b) extending to about 0.5 fm.
- The limiting case ζ = 2 corresponds to the "black disk" regime where σ_el = σ_inel = 0.5σ_tot and G(0) = 0.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the inelastic overlap function originally formulated by Van Hove (1964) and applies it to LHC energies using data from TOTEM. It contrasts with the standard "black disk" limit (Block and Halzen, 2005) by showing that the inelastic profile can become toroidal rather than uniformly black. The results enable tests of the Pumplin bound (1973) on inelastic diffraction, which states that the sum of elastic and diffractive cross sections is ≤ 0.5σ_tot; the authors note that the ALICE (2015) measurement at 7 TeV agrees with saturation of this bound, while CMS (2015) data leave larger uncertainty.
