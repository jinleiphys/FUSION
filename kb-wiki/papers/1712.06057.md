# The Impact of Nuclear Reaction Rate Uncertainties On The Evolution of Core-Collapse Supernova Progenitors

> We explore properties of core-collapse supernova progenitors with respect to the composite uncertainties in the thermonuclear reaction rates by coupling the reaction rate probability density functions provided by the STARLIB reaction rate library with $\texttt{MESA}$ stellar models. We evolve 1000 15 $M_{\odot}$ models from the pre main-sequence to core O-depletion at solar and subsolar metallicities for a total of 2000 Monte Carlo stellar models. For each stellar model, we independently and simultaneously sample 665 thermonuclear reaction rates and use them in a $\texttt{MESA}$ in situ reaction network that follows 127 isotopes from $^{1}$H to $^{64}$Zn. With this framework we survey the core mass, burning lifetime, composition, and structural properties at five different evolutionary epochs. At each epoch we measure the probability distribution function of the variations of each property and calculate Spearman Rank-Order Correlation coefficients for each sampled reaction rate to identify which reaction rate has the largest impact on the variations on each property. We find that uncertainties in $^{14}$N$(p,γ)^{15}$O, triple-$α$, $^{12}$C$(α,γ)^{16}$O, $^{12}$C($^{12}$C,$p$)$^{23}$Na, $^{12}$C($^{16}$O,$p$)$^{27}$Al, $^{16}$O($^{16}$O,$n$)$^{31}$S, $^{16}$O($^{16}$O,$p$)$^{31}$P, and $^{16}$O($^{16}$O,$α$)$^{28}$Si reaction rates dominate the variations of the properties surveyed. We find that variations induced by uncertainties in nuclear reaction rates grow with each passing phase of evolution, and at core H-, He-depletion are of comparable magnitude to the variations induced by choices of mass resolution and network resolution. However, at core C-, Ne-, and O-depletion, the reaction rate uncertainties can dominate the variation causing uncertainty in various properties of the stellar model in the evolution towards iron core-collapse.

## Key claim

The composite uncertainties in 665 thermonuclear reaction rates, when sampled simultaneously and independently via Monte Carlo methods in full stellar evolution models, produce variations in progenitor properties that grow with each evolutionary phase; at core H- and He-depletion these variations are comparable to those from mass and temporal resolution choices, but at core C-, Ne-, and O-depletion the reaction rate uncertainties can dominate the variation in properties relevant to iron core-collapse.

## Method

The authors coupled the temperature-dependent reaction rate probability density functions from the STARLIB library with the MESA stellar evolution code (version 7624). They evolved 1000 15 solar mass models at solar metallicity (Z=0.0153) and 1000 at subsolar metallicity (Z=0.0003) from the pre-main sequence to core oxygen depletion, for a total of 2000 Monte Carlo stellar models. For each model, they independently and simultaneously sampled 665 forward thermonuclear reaction rates according to their log-normal probability distributions and used them in an in situ reaction network following 127 isotopes from hydrogen-1 to zinc-64, with reverse rates calculated by detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- 2000 total Monte Carlo stellar models: 1000 at solar metallicity (Z=0.0153) and 1000 at subsolar metallicity (Z=0.0003)
- 665 forward thermonuclear reaction rates sampled independently and simultaneously per model
- Reaction network follows 127 isotopes from hydrogen-1 to zinc-64, coupled by 1201 reactions
- Each stellar model took approximately 60 hours on 4 CPUs, totaling about 0.48 million CPU hours and generating about 1 TB of data
- For the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction, the largest factor uncertainty is f.u.=1.403 at T=0.4 GK; a rate variation factor of p=+3.5 gives a sampled rate approximately 3.27 times the median rate, while p=-3.5 gives approximately 0.31 times the median rate
- Baseline solar model: He-core mass of 4.24 solar masses, 12C/16O ratio of 0.34 at He-depletion; main sequence lifetime of about 11.2 Myr; core He-burning duration of about 1.54 Myr
- Baseline subsolar model: He-core mass of 4.80 solar masses, 12C/16O ratio of 0.36 at He-depletion; main sequence lifetime of about 11.7 Myr; core He-burning duration of about 1.27 Myr
- At core O-depletion in the solar model: central mass fractions of sulfur-32 at 0.524, sulfur-34 at 0.189, and silicon-28 at 0.244; central electron fraction of 0.4936
- At core O-depletion in the subsolar model: central mass fractions of sulfur-32 at 0.522, sulfur-34 at 0.175, and silicon-28 at 0.236; central electron fraction of 0.4942
- Mass resolution variations at core O-depletion: largest variation of about 13 percent in central density for subsolar models; all other quantities vary by less than 7 percent
- Temporal resolution variations at core O-depletion: largest variation of less than 5 percent in central density; all other quantities vary by less than 3 percent

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo stellar model framework of Fields (2016), which studied 3 solar mass models through the first thermal pulse, by applying it to massive 15 solar mass progenitors and sampling all 665 forward reactions rather than a preselected subset. It differs from earlier sensitivity studies such as West (2013) and Nishimura (2017) by using temperature-dependent uncertainties from STARLIB and evolving full stellar models rather than post-processing thermodynamic trajectories. The results enable identification of which nuclear reactions most strongly influence progenitor structure at each burning stage, informing priorities for future nuclear experiments and providing uncertainty estimates for predictions of supernova properties, nucleosynthetic yields, and black hole formation rates.
