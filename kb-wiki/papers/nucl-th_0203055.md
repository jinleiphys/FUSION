# Effective Field Theory for Few-Nucleon Systems

> We review the effective field theories (EFTs) developed for few-nucleon systems. These EFTs are controlled expansions in momenta, where certain (leading-order) interactions are summed to all orders. At low energies, an EFT with only contact interactions allows a detailed analysis of renormalization in a non-perturbative context and uncovers novel asymptotic behavior. Manifestly model-independent calculations can be carried out to high orders, leading to high precision. At higher energies, an EFT that includes pion fields justifies and extends the traditional framework of phenomenological potentials. The correct treatment of QCD symmetries ensures a connection with lattice QCD. Several tests and prospects of these EFTs are discussed.

## Key claim

Effective field theories (EFTs) for few-nucleon systems provide a controlled expansion in momenta, but the expansion is only systematic because certain leading-order interactions must be summed to all orders rather than treated perturbatively, and the power counting relies on the existence of unnaturally large scattering lengths in the S-wave channels.

## Method

The authors construct two complementary EFTs: a "pionless" theory with only contact interactions valid for momenta below the pion mass, and a "pionful" theory that includes explicit pion fields for higher energies. For the pionless theory, they use dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS) to achieve manifest power counting, where the renormalization group flow reveals a non-trivial fixed point that explains the need to resum bubble diagrams to all orders at leading order. The low-energy constants are determined by matching to effective range parameters (scattering lengths, effective ranges, shape parameters) extracted from nucleon-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Singlet neutron-proton scattering length: \(a_s = -23.714\) fm, corresponding to momentum scale \(1/a_s = 8.3\) MeV
- Triplet (deuteron) scattering length: \(a_t = 5.42\) fm, corresponding to momentum scale \(1/a_t = 36\) MeV
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV, defining the range of the nuclear potential
- Deuteron charge mean square radius predicted at N\(^2\)LO: \(\langle r^2\rangle_d = 4.565\) fm\(^4\), compared to experimental value 4.538 fm\(^4\)
- The pionless EFT shows convergence for momenta \(k \lesssim 100\) MeV, as demonstrated by the \(^3S_1\) phase shift comparison to the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis

## Context

This review extends the framework of Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) from the mesonic and one-baryon sectors to few-nucleon systems, building on earlier work by Weinberg (1990-1992), Ordonez, van Kolck, and others. The pionless EFT formalizes and extends the old effective-range theory of Bethe (1949) and the model-independent three-body results of Efimov (1970). The approach enables manifestly model-independent calculations with rigorous error estimates, provides a systematic connection to QCD symmetries for lattice QCD comparisons, and has already been applied to cold atomic gases with large scattering lengths via Feshbach resonances.
