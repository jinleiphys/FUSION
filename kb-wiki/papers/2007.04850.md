# Exploring the centrality dependence of elliptic and triangular flows

> A detailed study of elliptical and triangular eccentricities in the initial state of relativistic heavy ion collisions is presented. A model of randomly distributed sources of energy density in the transverse plane based on the effective theory of Color Glass Condensate is used. This model describes well the ALICE and ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair in a wide range of centralities, if the second $v_2$ and third $v_3$ harmonics of the anisotropic flow are simply implied to be proportional to the eccentricities $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ as a reasonable approximation. The eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ is closely related with the collision geometry and its centrality dependence is mainly determined by the edge diffuseness of the region of the uniform distribution of the saturation pulse of the oncoming nucleus. The eccentricity $\varepsilon_3$ is completely determined by the chaotic fluctuations of the source position in the region of overlapping nuclei and is substantially dependent on the overlap area only.

## Key claim

The magma model, which uses randomly distributed sources of energy density based on the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, reproduces the centrality dependence of elliptic and triangular flow harmonics in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only if the linear response approximation (proportionality between flow harmonics and initial eccentricities) is assumed to hold with constant proportionality coefficients for centralities up to 30%.

## Method

The authors simulate the initial energy density profile in the transverse plane as a sum of contributions from elementary collisions between localized color charges and a dense nucleus, with source positions as independent random variables and the saturation momentum proportional to the nuclear thickness function (Woods-Saxon distribution). They compute the eccentricities ε₂ and ε₃ from the energy density distribution using standard formulas, then obtain the flow harmonics v₂ and v₃ by multiplying the eccentricities by constant proportionality coefficients k₂ and k₃ adjusted to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum at nucleus center: Qₛ₀ = 1.24 GeV
- Proportionality coefficients: k₂ = 0.321 and k₃ = 0.314
- Maximum number of sources in Pb nucleus (R = 6.62 fm): N_PB = 96
- Nuclear diffuseness edge parameter giving best agreement with data: d = 0.7 fm
- Infrared cutoff parameter: 1/m = 1.4 fm
- Fit parameter for fluctuation formula of v₃: K₃ = 0.0183
- Scaling prediction for most central collisions: vₙ(C=0) ~ A⁻¹/³

## Context

This work extends the magma model of Gelis (2013) to study the full centrality dependence of elliptic and triangular flow, comparing with ALICE and ATLAS data at 5.02 TeV. The authors show that the third harmonic v₃ is purely fluctuation-driven and follows a simple 1/√(1-√C) scaling, while v₂ is dominated by collision geometry and sensitive to the nuclear edge diffuseness. The calculated eccentricities can serve as input to hydrodynamic models like HYDJET++ (Lokhtin 2008, Bravina 2013) to improve flow descriptions, and the model predicts that all harmonics in the most central collisions scale as A⁻¹/³ for lighter nuclei.
