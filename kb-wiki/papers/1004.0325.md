# Masses and radii of neutron and quark stars

> We discuss new limits on masses and radii of compact stars and we conclude that they can be interpreted as an indication of the existence of two classes of stars: "normal" compact stars and "ultra-compact" stars. We estimate the critical mass at which the first configuration collapses into the second.

# Masses and radii of neutron and quark stars

## Key claim
The apparent contradiction between two re-analyses of observational data, one ruling out all soft equations of state (Özel 2006) and the other requiring a very soft equation of state (Özel et al. 2009), can be resolved if two distinct classes of compact stars coexist: "normal" hadronic stars and "ultra-compact" stars made of exotic matter such as quarks. The critical baryonic mass at which a normal configuration collapses into an ultra-compact one is estimated to be about (1.9 ± 0.1) solar masses at 1-sigma confidence.

## Method
The authors compare mass-radius limits from Özel (2006) for EXO 0748-676 with those from Özel et al. (2009) for 4U 1608-52, 4U 1820-30, and EXO 1745-248, interpreting the first as a hadronic star and the latter three as quark or hybrid stars. The hadronic configuration uses a relativistic model incorporating hyperons, while hybrid stars are constructed via Gibbs equations between that hadronic model and a MIT-bag model with a Colour-Flavour-Locked (CFL) phase; the quark star configuration uses a CFL phase with a larger superconducting gap. The critical baryonic mass is then constrained by requiring it to lie between the baryonic masses of the normal and compact configurations consistent with both data sets.

## Key numbers
- Critical baryonic mass: (1.9 ± 0.1) solar masses, corresponding to a gravitational mass of (1.8 ± 0.1) solar masses for the "normal" hadronic configuration and (1.6 ± 0.1) solar masses for the ultra-compact configurations, based on the 1-sigma limits from Özel (2006) and Özel et al. (2009).
- Energy release associated with the transition from normal to ultra-compact configuration: of order a few times 10^53 erg.

## Context
This work reconciles the contradictory conclusions of Özel (2006) and Özel et al. (2009) by proposing a two-population scenario for compact stars, extending earlier discussions of "third family" solutions by Schertler et al. and Blaschke et al. and of quark star stability by Berezhiani et al. The results enable future searches for observational signatures of the collapse transition, which could power explosive astrophysical phenomena as suggested by Cheng et al. and Berezhiani et al. The authors note that the large critical mass poses challenges for hybrid star models with first-order deconfinement transitions and for rapidly rotating quark stars made entirely of CFL matter due to negligible viscosity.
