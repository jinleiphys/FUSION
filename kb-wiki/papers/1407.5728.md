# Tests of CPT and Lorentz symmetry from muon anomalous magnetic dipole moment

> We derive the relativistic factor for splitting of the $g$-factors of a fermion and its anti-fermion partner, which is important for placing constraints on dimension-5, $CPT$-odd and Lorentz-invariance-violating interactions from experiments performed in a cyclotron. From existing data, we extract limits (1$σ$) on the coupling strengths of the temporal component, $f^0$, of a background field (including the field amplitude), which is responsible for such $g$-factor splitting, with an electron, proton, and muon: $|f^0_e|< 2.3 \times 10^{-12} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, $|f^0_p|< 4 \times 10^{-9} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, and $|f^0_μ|< 8 \times 10^{-11} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, respectively, in the laboratory frame. From existing data, we also extract limits on the coupling strengths of the spatial components, $d^{\perp}$, of related dimension-5 interactions of a background field with an electron, proton, neutron, and muon: $| {d}_e^{\perp} | \lesssim 10^{-9} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, $| {d}_p^{\perp} | \lesssim 10^{-9} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, $| {d}_n^{\perp} | \lesssim 10^{-10} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, and $| {d}_μ^{\perp} | \lesssim 10^{-9} ~μ_{\textrm{B}}$, respectively, in the laboratory frame.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic correction factor for the splitting of the g-factors of fermions and their anti-fermion partners due to dimension-5, CPT-odd and Lorentz-invariance-violating interactions, which is necessary for correctly interpreting cyclotron experiments; the limits extracted from existing data assume that the observed difference between experimental and Standard Model values for the muon anomalous magnetic dipole moment arises from this CPT-violating effect rather than from other new physics such as supersymmetry.

## Method

The authors consider dimension-5, CPT-odd and Lorentz-invariance-violating couplings that are linear in the gauge field strength, specifically the interaction Hamiltonian term f^0 B·Σ, which splits the g-factors of fermions and anti-fermions. They derive the relativistic form of this interaction for muons in a cyclotron (where the Lorentz factor γ = 29.3) and use existing experimental measurements of anomalous magnetic dipole moments for electrons, protons, and muons, along with their anti-particles, to extract limits on the coupling strengths f^0. For the spatial components d^⊥ of related dimension-5 interactions, they extract limits from existing experimental bounds on the background field b and the magnetic field strengths used in those experiments.

## Key numbers

- |f^0_e| < 2.3 × 10^{-12} μ_B (laboratory frame, 1σ limit from electron anomalous MDM data)
- |f^0_p| < 4 × 10^{-9} μ_B (laboratory frame, 1σ limit from proton and anti-proton MDM measurements)
- |f^0_μ| < 8 × 10^{-11} μ_B (laboratory frame, 1σ limit from muon and anti-muon anomalous MDM data, with γ = 29.3)
- |d_e^⊥| ≲ 10^{-9} μ_B (laboratory frame, from existing b-field limits and B = 10^{-7} T)
- |d_p^⊥| ≲ 10^{-9} μ_B (laboratory frame, from existing b-field limits and B = 5.5 × 10^{-7} T)
- |d_n^⊥| ≲ 10^{-10} μ_B (laboratory frame, from existing b-field limits and B = 5.5 × 10^{-7} T)
- |d_μ^⊥| ≲ 10^{-9} μ_B (laboratory frame, from existing b-field limits and B = 1.45 T)

## Context

This work extends the dimension-5 CPT-odd framework of Bolokhov (2005) by deriving the correct relativistic factor for cyclotron experiments, which was previously neglected. The results provide new constraints on CPT-violating couplings that complement the Standard Model Extension (SME) parametrization of Kostelecky (1997, 1998, 2000), where no leading-order corrections to g-factors were found. The limits on f^0 and d^⊥ from existing anomalous MDM data enable future experiments, particularly the planned muon g-2 measurement at Fermilab, to place more stringent constraints on CPT and Lorentz symmetry violation.
