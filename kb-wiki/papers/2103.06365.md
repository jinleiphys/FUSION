# Ab initio calculations of electric dipole moments of light nuclei

> In any finite system, the presence of a non-zero permanent electric dipole moment (EDM) would indicate CP violation beyond the small violation predicted in the Standard Model. Here, we use the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) framework to theoretically investigate the magnitude of the nuclear EDM. We calculate EDMs of several light nuclei using chiral two- and three-body interactions and a PT-violating Hamiltonian based on a one-meson-exchange model. We present a benchmark calculation for $^3$He, as well as results for the more complex nuclei $^{6,7}$Li, $^9$Be, $^{10,11}$B, $^{13}$C, $^{14,15}$N, and $^{19}$F. Our results suggest that different nuclei can be used to probe different terms of the PT violating interaction. These calculations allow us to suggest which nuclei may be good candidates in the search for a measurable permanent electric dipole moment.

## Key claim

The authors use the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) to calculate electric dipole moments of light nuclei, finding that different nuclei can probe different terms of the parity- and time-reversal-violating interaction; however, the results carry estimated uncertainties of 10-30% due to basis-size convergence and model dependencies, and the polarization contributions for ^3He are smaller by a factor of 1/2 compared to an earlier NCSM calculation by Stetcu (2008).

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for the nuclear ground state using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (NN up to N^3LO, 3N up to N^2LO) softened by the similarity renormalization group (SRG) at a resolution scale of 2.0 fm^-1. They then compute the polarization contribution to the nuclear EDM by inverting an inhomogeneous Schrodinger equation with a one-meson-exchange parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction (including pi-, rho-, and omega-meson exchanges) using the Lanczos continued fraction method, and evaluate the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution as a matrix element of spin and isospin operators.

## Key numbers

* ^3He polarization contribution from pi-exchange PTV interaction: 0.0073 e fm (coefficient of bar-G^0_pi), benchmarked against earlier NCSM results
* ^10B shows enhanced isovector pi-exchange contribution (bar-G^1_pi coefficient 0.0281 e fm), approximately a factor of 2 larger than the deuteron value from Liu (2004)
* ^13C isotensor pi-exchange contribution (bar-G^2_pi coefficient -0.0316 e fm) is significant and could not be calculated within the cluster model of Yamanaka (2017)
* Calculated magnetic moments agree with experimental values to within about 30% for the worst cases (^11B: 2.09 vs 2.689 mu_N; ^13C: 0.44 vs 0.702 mu_N)
* Largest NCSM basis spaces used: N_max=11 for ^6,7Li, N_max=9 for ^9Be, N_max=7 for ^10,11B, ^13C, ^14,15N, and N_max=5 for ^19F (M-scheme dimension 189 million)

## Context

This work extends ab initio EDM calculations beyond the A=2,3 systems studied by Stetcu (2008) and deVries (2011) using the NCSM, and provides a systematic comparison with cluster-model calculations by Yamanaka (2015, 2017, 2018, 2019) for ^6Li, ^7Li, ^9Be, ^11B, and ^13C. The results enable identification of candidate nuclei for storage-ring EDM experiments, such as ^10B for probing isovector pi-exchange and ^13C for isotensor terms, and motivate extension to the exotic nucleus ^11Be using the NCSM with continuum (NCSMC) framework.
