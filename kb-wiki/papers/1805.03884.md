# Modified Skellam, Poisson and Gaussian distributions in semi-open systems at charge-like conservation law

> A modification of the Skellam and Poisson distributions is proposed for subsystems when the constraints imposed by the charge conservation law in the complete system are taken into account. Such distributions can be applied, for example, for an analysis of the fluctuations of baryon and net baryon numbers in certain pseudo-rapidity interval in $A+A$ and $p+p$ collisions with high multiplicities. The presented modified Skellam, Poisson and Gaussian distributions can be utilized also in various branches of science, when one studies the fluctuations of the two variables related to a subsystem, as well as the distribution of the difference of these variables, while the mentioned difference in the total system is fixed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple analytic generalization of the Skellam, Poisson, and Gaussian distributions for subsystems when the total system has a fixed net charge (e.g., baryon number), but the derivation relies on six physically motivated conditions rather than a unique microscopic model; the resulting modified distributions agree with a binomial-Poissonian model to within 0.5% in the symmetric case and within 15% in the full parameter range.

## Method

The author starts from the standard Skellam distribution for the difference of two independent Poisson variables and imposes a Kronecker-delta constraint fixing the net baryon number in the total system. Six conditions (normalization, correct mean, symmetry under subsystem permutation, reduction to the standard Skellam for a small subsystem, reduction to a delta function when the subsystem becomes the total system, and correct variance suppression for a single-component system) are used to determine the parameters \(M_i, \overline{M}_i, k_i^0\) in the ansatz \(\widetilde{p}(k_i; N_i,\overline{N}_i) = e^{-(M_i+\overline{M}_i)} (M_i/\overline{M}_i)^{(k_i-k_i^0)/2} I_{k_i-k_i^0}(2\sqrt{M_i\overline{M}_i})\). The resulting modified Skellam distribution is compared numerically to a binomial-Poissonian model (Eqs. 8-10) that treats baryon and antibaryon numbers in the total system as Poissonian and then assigns particles to subsystems with fixed probabilities \(q\) and \(\overline{q}\).

## Key numbers

* The variance of the modified Skellam distribution for subsystem \(i\) is \(\sigma_i^2 = M_i + \overline{M}_i\) (Eq. 15).
* The skewness is \(S_i = (M_i - \overline{M}_i) / (M_i + \overline{M}_i)^{3/2}\) (Eq. 16).
* The excess kurtosis is \(K_i = 1 / (M_i + \overline{M}_i)\) (Eq. 17).
* For the symmetric case \(q = \overline{q}\), the ratio \(\sigma_i^2 / \sigma_{i,\text{Skellam}}^2\) coincides with the binomial-based result \(1-q\) to within 0.5%.
* The maximal deviation between the modified Skellam and the binomial-Poissonian model reaches 7% at small total net baryon number \(B=1\) when \(q = \overline{q} + 1/2\).
* For the modified Poisson distribution (Eq. 20), the variance ratio between the binomial-Poissonian model and the modified Poisson approximation agrees within 1% for a wide interval of net baryon charge \(B\).
* The deviation for the Poisson-like distribution \(\widetilde{P}_{1+2}\) can reach 14% at relatively small \(B\).

## Context

This work extends prior discussions of baryon number conservation in semi-open systems by Gorenstein (2000s), Braun-Munzinger et al. (2000s), Koch (2000s), and Asakawa (2000s), who noted the problem but did not provide a complete analytic solution. The proposed modified distributions are intended for analyzing baryon and net-baryon fluctuations in pseudo-rapidity intervals in \(A+A\) and \(p+p\) collisions at LHC and RHIC, particularly at lower collision energies (BES at RHIC, FAIR, NICA) where the subsystem is not small compared to the total system. The work enables analytic calculation of variance, skewness, and kurtosis for net-baryon fluctuations under charge conservation, and the author plans to apply the results to baryon and antibaryon observables in \(pp\) and \(AA\) collisions.
