# A Kinematically Complete Measurement of the Proton Structure Function F2 in the Resonance Region and Evaluation of Its Moments

> We measured the inclusive electron-proton cross section in the nucleon resonance region (W < 2.5 GeV) at momentum transfers Q**2 below 4.5 (GeV/c)**2 with the CLAS detector. The large acceptance of CLAS allowed for the first time the measurement of the cross section in a large, contiguous two-dimensional range of Q**2 and x, making it possible to perform an integration of the data at fixed Q**2 over the whole significant x-interval. From these data we extracted the structure function F2 and, by including other world data, we studied the Q**2 evolution of its moments, Mn(Q**2), in order to estimate higher twist contributions. The small statistical and systematic uncertainties of the CLAS data allow a precise extraction of the higher twists and demand significant improvements in theoretical predictions for a meaningful comparison with new experimental results.

## Key claim
The CLAS detector's large acceptance enabled the first kinematically complete measurement of the inclusive electron-proton cross section over a contiguous two-dimensional range of Q² and x in the nucleon resonance region (W < 2.5 GeV), allowing model-independent evaluation of the F₂ structure function moments and extraction of higher twist contributions with significantly reduced interpolation uncertainties compared to previous measurements.

## Method
The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab collected inclusive electron-proton scattering data at five beam energies (1.5, 2.5, 4.0, 4.2, and 4.4 GeV) using a liquid hydrogen target. The raw electron yields were corrected for pion contamination, detection efficiency, acceptance, bin centering, and radiative effects through detailed GEANT-based simulations and systematic error analysis. The F₂ structure function was extracted from the corrected cross sections, and Nachtmann moments M_n(Q²) were computed up to n=8 by combining CLAS data with existing world data and updated elastic form factor contributions.

## Key numbers
- Kinematic coverage: W < 2.5 GeV and Q² < 4.5 (GeV/c)²
- Beam energies used: 1.5, 2.5, 4.0, 4.2, and 4.4 GeV
- Target thickness: 0.35 g/cm² liquid hydrogen
- Average beam current: 4.5 nA, corresponding to luminosity of 6 × 10³³ cm⁻²s⁻¹
- Total statistics: > 6 × 10⁸ triggers
- Statistical error per bin: ≤ 5% in bins of Δx = 0.009 and ΔQ² = 0.05 GeV²
- Electron momentum resolution: 0.5% for θ ≤ 30°, 1-2% for θ > 30°
- Angular resolution: approximately 1 mrad polar, 4 mrad azimuthal
- Missing mass resolution: 2.5 MeV for beam energy < 3 GeV, about 7 MeV for larger energies
- Detection efficiency from simulations: approximately 97%
- Radiative correction factor range: 0.85 to 1.6
- Average radiative correction systematic error: 3%

## Context
This work extends the study of Bloom-Gilman duality and higher twist contributions to nucleon structure functions, following the theoretical framework of De Rujula, Georgi and Politzer (1975) using Operator Product Expansion. It improves upon previous analyses by Ricco et al. (1998) and the Hall C Collaboration at Jefferson Lab (2001) by providing data that span a continuous x interval at fixed Q², eliminating the need for substantial interpolation required by earlier spectrometer measurements with small angular acceptance. The precise extraction of higher twists from these moments enables quantitative comparison with lattice QCD predictions and non-perturbative models of multi-parton correlations in the nucleon.
