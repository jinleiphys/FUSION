# On the Excitation of Double Giant Resonances in Heavy Ion Reactions

> The interplay of nuclear and Coulomb processes in the inelastic excitation of single- and double-phonon giant resonances in heavy ion collisions is studied within a simple reaction model. Predominance of the Coulomb excitation mechanism on the population of the single-phonon and, on the contrary, predominance of the nuclear excitation for the double-phonon is evidenced. The effect of the spreading of the strength distribution of the giant resonances on the excitation process is analyzed, showing sizeable modifications in the case of Coulomb dominated processes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in heavy-ion collisions, the excitation of the single-phonon giant resonance is dominated by the Coulomb interaction, while the double-phonon giant resonance is predominantly excited by the nuclear interaction, a reversal of roles that the abstract does not explicitly qualify as arising from the different effective collision times and Q-value matching conditions for the two mechanisms.

## Method

The authors used a semiclassical model of Alder and Winther, where the projectile and target follow classical trajectories determined by Coulomb plus nuclear potentials, while the excitation of one- and two-phonon giant quadrupole resonance states in the target is treated quantum-mechanically within first- and second-order perturbation theory. The coupling form factors included both Coulomb and nuclear components, with the nuclear part derived from a Saxon-Woods parametrization of the proximity potential. The strength distribution of the giant resonance was modeled as a Gaussian with variable width, and the cross sections were obtained by integrating excitation probabilities over impact parameters, using an imaginary optical potential to account for absorption from non-elastic channels.

## Key numbers

- The reaction studied was ⁴⁰Ar + ²⁰⁸Pb at a bombarding energy of 40 MeV per nucleon, with deformation parameters β^C = β^N = 0.07 for the giant quadrupole resonance in lead.
- The grazing impact parameter range for this reaction covered approximately 11–13 fm, yielding scattering angles between 3.4 and 5.5 degrees.
- Maximum destructive interference between Coulomb and nuclear excitation occurred at an impact parameter of about 12.5 fm.
- For the giant quadrupole resonance energy of 11 MeV (close to the actual value in lead), the excitation probabilities for both one- and two-phonon states increased rapidly up to about 50 MeV/nucleon, then declined gradually up to about 400 MeV/nucleon.
- For a Gaussian width of 4 MeV, the distortion of the line profile at the one-phonon level was shown, and for a width of 6 MeV the apparent shift placed most of the cross section outside the initial Gaussian range.
- The cross sections for the single giant resonance calculated with only the Coulomb field increased with increasing width Γ, while the two-phonon cross section showed no appreciable variation with finite width when the nuclear interaction was included.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Catara et al. (1987), Lanza et al., and Andrés et al., who considered both nuclear and Coulomb interactions for medium-heavy nuclei at bombarding energies around 50 MeV/A but did not clearly resolve the relative contributions of each mechanism. It also builds on analyses by Canto, Bertulani, and Weidenmüller that examined the role of the resonance width on reaction cross sections, but only for Coulomb excitation and with somewhat ambiguous results. The paper enables quantitative predictions for experiments at non-relativistic energies (up to a few hundred MeV/nucleon), suggesting that relativistic bombarding energies may not be necessary to probe double-phonon giant resonances, and provides a framework for experimentalists to calculate cross sections for a wide variety of systems and conditions.
