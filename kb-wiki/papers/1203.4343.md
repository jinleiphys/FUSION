# Relativistic Thermodynamics: Transverse Momentum Distributions in High-Energy Physics

> Transverse momentum distributions measured by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and by the ALICE, ATLAS and CMS collaborations at the Large Hadron Collider can be considered in the framework of relativistic thermodynamics using the Tsallis distribution. Theoretical issues are clarified concerning the thermodynamic consistency in the case of relativistic high energy quantum distributions. An improved form is proposed for describing the transverse momentum distribution and fits are presented together with estimates of the parameter q and the temperature T.

# Relativistic Thermodynamics: Transverse Momentum Distributions in High-Energy Physics

## Key claim
The Tsallis distribution provides a thermodynamically consistent framework for describing transverse momentum distributions measured at RHIC and LHC, but only when the power \(q\) appears on the occupation number in the entropy constraints; the resulting fits yield a temperature around 70 MeV, which is substantially lower than standard thermal freeze-out temperatures because the Tsallis distribution is always larger than the Boltzmann one for \(q>1\).

## Method
The authors derive the Tsallis form of Fermi-Dirac, Bose-Einstein, and Boltzmann distributions from a variational principle that maximizes a generalized entropy (using the \(q\)-logarithm) subject to constraints where the particle number and energy are weighted by \(f_i^q\) rather than \(f_i\). They then fit the Tsallis-B distribution (classical limit) to published transverse momentum spectra for identified hadrons (\(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(K^0_S\), \(p\), \(\bar{p}\), \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^-\)) from ALICE and CMS in \(p-p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=900\) GeV, using the mid-rapidity, zero-chemical-potential form \(d^2N/(dp_T dy) = gV p_T m_T/(2\pi)^2 [1+(q-1)m_T/T]^{-q/(q-1)}\).

## Key numbers
- The Tsallis parameter \(q\) from fits to all hadron species at \(\sqrt{s}=900\) GeV lies in the range 1.1 to 1.2, with individual values such as \(q=1.154\pm0.036\) for \(\pi^+\), \(q=1.146\pm0.036\) for \(\pi^-\), and \(q=1.110\pm0.218\) for \(\Xi^-\).
- The fitted temperature \(T\) is consistently around 70 MeV, e.g., \(T=0.0682\pm0.0026\) GeV for \(\pi^+\), \(T=0.0704\pm0.0027\) GeV for \(\pi^-\), and \(T=0.0698\pm0.0148\) GeV for \(\Lambda\).
- The highest available heavy-ion collision energy cited is \(\sqrt{s}=2760\) AGeV, yet the observed temperature is only of order \(T\approx 0.16\) GeV at both RHIC and LHC.
- The \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the fits ranges from 7.06/24 (for \(K^-\)) to 16.25/24 (for \(K^+\)).

## Context
This work extends the Tsallis non-extensive statistical framework (Tsallis, 1988; Tsallis, 1998) to relativistic quantum distributions in high-energy physics, clarifying thermodynamic consistency issues that were not correctly implemented in earlier studies (Pereira, 2007; Pereira, 2009; Conroy, 2010). The proposed form differs from the parameterization used by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations (Abelev, 2006; Adare; Aamodt, 2011; Aad; Khachatryan, 2011) by including an additional factor of \(m_T\), which the authors argue leads to a more consistent interpretation of \(q\) and \(T\). The analysis enables systematic comparison of Tsallis parameters across particle species and collision energies, though the authors note that resonance decay contributions must still be included for a complete treatment.
