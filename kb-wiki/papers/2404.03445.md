# Radiative corrections to the spin asymmetry in elastic polarized electron-nucleus collisions at high energy

> Modifying the numerical codes, dispersion corrections to the beam-normal spin asymmetry which arise from low-lying transient nuclear excitations up to 30 MeV, are estimated for collision energies between 50 MeV and 1 GeV. A nonperturbative calculation of vacuum polarization and the vertex plus self-energy correction, using optimized potentials, indicates that for small scattering angles both these quantum electrodynamical (QED) effects on the spin asymmetry decrease with energy above 200 MeV and can be neglected at high energies. Examples are given for the 12C and 208Pb nuclei. The available measurements of the spin asymmetry at collision energies beyond 500 MeV cannot be explained by the present theory.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that for small scattering angles, both vacuum polarization and vertex-plus-self-energy QED corrections to the beam-normal spin asymmetry decrease with collision energy above 200 MeV and can be neglected at high energies, but the available measurements of the spin asymmetry at collision energies beyond 500 MeV cannot be explained by the present theory.

## Method

The author extended existing numerical codes to calculate dispersion corrections from low-lying transient nuclear excitations (up to 30 MeV) using the second-order Born approximation, and computed QED corrections (vacuum polarization via the Uehling potential, vertex plus self-energy via an inverse Fourier transform of the first-order Born amplitude) by including their respective potentials in the Dirac equation and solving via phase-shift analysis. A Gaussian parametrization of the nuclear ground-state charge distribution was introduced for energies up to 1 GeV, replacing the Fourier-Bessel representation which fails at high energies and large angles.

## Key numbers

- Dispersion corrections from nuclear excitations up to 30 MeV were estimated for collision energies between 50 MeV and 1 GeV
- For 56 MeV e + 208Pb collisions, the QED modification of the spin asymmetry exceeds the dispersion effects, with Δσ_QED varying from 4% to 15% depending on scattering angle
- For 208Pb at 25°, the 14.2 MeV dipole state, 10.9 MeV quadrupole state, and 2.615 MeV octupole state were among the dominant dispersive contributions
- For 12C, the dispersive spin asymmetry shows a linear increase with sin(θ_f/2) for small angles, with deviations between 2-5% for θ_f less than 20° at 56 MeV
- Up to 25000 partial waves were required for sufficient accuracy in the partial-wave analysis

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dispersive spin asymmetry from nuclear excitations by Jakubassa-Amundsen (2022, 2023) from collision energies up to 150 MeV into the GeV region, and provides nonperturbative QED corrections using optimized potentials. The hadronic model of dispersion (using forward Compton scattering cross sections) by Gorchtein and Horowitz (2008) and Koshchii et al (2021) can explain experimental spin-asymmetry results for light targets up to 90Zr but fails for 208Pb targets; the present calculations cannot explain measurements beyond 500 MeV either. The work enables improved comparisons with experimental data from the HAPPEX and PREX collaborations at Jefferson Lab.
