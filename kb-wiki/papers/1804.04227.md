# Improved description of the $2νββ$-decay and a possibility to determine the effective axial-vector coupling constant

> An improved formalism of the two-neutrino double-beta decay ($2νββ$-decay) rate is presented, which takes into account the dependence of energy denominators on lepton energies via the Taylor expansion. Till now, only the leading term in this expansion has been considered. The revised $2νββ$-decay rate and differential characteristics depend on additional phase-space factors weighted by the ratios of $2νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements with different powers of the energy denominator. For nuclei of experimental interest all phase-space factors are calculated by using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening. For isotopes with measured $2νββ$-decay half-life the involved nuclear matrix elements are determined within the quasiparticle random phase approximation with partial isospin restoration. The importance of correction terms to the $2νββ$-decay rate due to Taylor expansion is established and the modification of shape of single and summed electron energy distributions is discussed. It is found that the improved calculation of the $2νββ$-decay predicts slightly suppressed $2νββ$-decay background to the neutrinoless double-beta decay signal. Further, a novel approach to determine the value of effective weak-coupling constant in nuclear medium $g^{\rm eff}_{\rm A}$ is proposed.

# Improved description of the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay and a possibility to determine the effective axial-vector coupling constant

## Key claim

The standard treatment of two-neutrino double-beta decay neglects the dependence of energy denominators on lepton energies; by including this dependence via Taylor expansion, the decay rate acquires correction terms that depend on additional phase-space factors and ratios of nuclear matrix elements, and the shape of electron energy distributions is modified. The authors propose that these corrections, combined with precise measurements, could allow determination of the effective axial-vector coupling constant $g^{\rm eff}_A$ in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors perform a Taylor expansion of the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay nuclear matrix elements in powers of $\varepsilon_{K,L}/(E_n - (E_i+E_f)/2)$, retaining terms up to fourth order, which introduces new phase-space factors $G^{2\nu}_2$, $G^{2\nu}_4$, and $G^{2\nu}_{22}$ weighted by ratios of nuclear matrix elements $\xi^{2\nu}_{31}$ and $\xi^{2\nu}_{51}$. Phase-space factors for 13 isotopes are computed using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening (scheme B), and nuclear matrix elements are calculated within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with partial isospin restoration using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- For $^{100}$Mo, the leading-order contribution $P^{2\nu}_0$ to the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay rate is 0.8163, the second-order contribution $P^{2\nu}_2$ is 0.1578, and the third-order contribution $P^{2\nu}_4$ is 0.0259 (for $g^{\rm eff}_A = 0.800$), giving the largest correction among studied isotopes at about 25%
- The ratio $\xi^{2\nu}_{31}$ for $^{100}$Mo is 0.4230 (for $g^{\rm eff}_A = 0.800$), the largest among all isotopes studied
- For $^{76}$Ge, the phase-space factor $G^{2\nu}_0$ is $4.816 \times 10^{-20}$ yr$^{-1}$ (scheme B), compared to $5.278 \times 10^{-20}$ yr$^{-1}$ (scheme A)
- For $^{136}$Xe, the $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay phase-space factor $G^{0\nu}_0$ is $1.464 \times 10^{-14}$ yr$^{-1}$ (scheme B) and $G^{0\nu}_2$ is $5.107 \times 10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$ (scheme B)
- The $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay nuclear matrix element $M^{2\nu}_{GT-1}$ for $^{48}$Ca is 0.0214 (for $g^{\rm eff}_A = 1.269$), while $M^{2\nu}_{GT-3}$ is 0.00539 and $M^{2\nu}_{GT-5}$ is 0.00075
- The ratio $\xi^{0\nu}_{31}$ for $^{48}$Ca is $6.814 \times 10^{-4}$ (for $g^{\rm eff}_A = 1.269$), significantly smaller than the corresponding $\xi^{2\nu}_{31}$ value of 0.2514

## Context

This work extends the standard formalism of Doi et al. (1985) for $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay, which previously kept only the leading term in the Taylor expansion of energy denominators. The improved description enables more accurate background estimates for neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments (GERDA, NEMO3, CUORE, EXO, KamLAND-ZEN) and provides a novel method to determine $g^{\rm eff}_A$ by combining measured half-lives with the calculated ratios of nuclear matrix elements. The nuclear matrix elements are computed within the pn-QRPA framework with isospin restoration following the approach of Vadim et al., and the results enable future extraction of $g^{\rm eff}_A$ from high-statistics $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay data.
