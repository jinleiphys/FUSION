# Effects of a spin-flavour dependent interaction on the baryon mass spectrum

> The effective quark interaction in a relativistically covariant constituent quark model based on the Salpeter equation is supplemented by an extra phenomenological flavour dependent force in order to account for some discrepancies mainly in the description of excited negative parity Delta resonances. Simultaneously an improved description of some other features of the light-flavoured baryon mass spectrum and of some electromagnetic form factors is obtained.

## Key claim
The authors supplement their relativistically covariant constituent quark model (based on the Salpeter equation) with an extra phenomenological flavour-dependent force, motivated by pseudoscalar meson exchange, to address discrepancies in excited negative parity Delta resonances and the low mass of the Delta(1600), but they concede that the treatment is entirely phenomenological and the relation to more fundamental QCD parameters such as instanton couplings and string tension is lost.

## Method
The authors solve the Salpeter equation (an instantaneous formulation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) for three-quark bound states by numerical diagonalisation in a large oscillator basis up to quantum number N_max=18. The model includes a linear confinement potential, an instanton-induced spin-flavour dependent interaction regularised by a Gaussian, and a new phenomenological flavour-dependent interaction parametrised as a local Gaussian potential with Dirac structure gamma5⊗gamma5, with separate coupling strengths for flavour octet and singlet exchange. Parameters were fitted to a selection of baryon resonances from the Particle Data Group using a least-squares procedure with weights reciprocal to experimental uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: m_n = 325.0 MeV, m_s = 600.0 MeV (model C), compared to m_n = 330.0 MeV, m_s = 670.0 MeV in model A
- Confinement slope: b = 212.81 MeV/fm (model C), compared to 453.6 MeV/fm in model A
- Instanton-induced coupling strengths: g_nn = 341.49 MeV fm^3, g_ns = 273.55 MeV fm^3 (model C), roughly tripled compared to model A values of 130.3 and 81.8 MeV fm^3 respectively
- Flavour octet exchange coupling: g_8^2/(4π) = 100.86 MeV fm^3; flavour singlet exchange: g_0^2/(4π) = 1897.43 MeV fm^3
- Range of flavour-dependent interaction: λ_8 = λ_0 = 0.25 fm; instanton-induced interaction range: λ = 0.4 fm
- Calculated Delta(1232) mass: 1231 MeV (experimental: 1232 MeV); Delta(1600) mass: 1559 MeV (experimental: 1600 MeV)
- Calculated negative parity Delta resonances: D_33(1700) at 1591 MeV (experimental: 1700 MeV), D_33(1940) at 1871/1944 MeV (experimental: 1940 MeV)
- Nucleon ground state P_11(939): calculated 945 MeV; Roper resonance P_11(1440): calculated 1440 MeV
- Lambda(1405) not explicitly listed in results tables, but the abstract notes this resonance as a remaining discrepancy

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier Bethe-Salpeter quark model (LoeMePe2, LoeMePe3) which successfully described linear Regge trajectories, the Roper resonance, and many electroweak properties but failed to reproduce excited negative parity Delta resonances below 2 GeV and the low mass of the Delta(1600). The new flavour-dependent interaction is inspired by pseudoscalar meson exchange potentials used successfully by the Graz group (Glozman 1996, 1997, 1998; Plessas and collaborators). The model enables improved description of the Delta and Omega spectra while maintaining good agreement for nucleon resonances, though the authors note the phenomenological nature of the approach and the loss of connection to fundamental QCD parameters.
