# Spin alignment from turbulent color fields

> We study the important, yet widely overlooked, role of gluons for spin transport with a connection to local parity violation in quark gluon plasmas. We extend the newly developed quantum kinetic theory for relativistic fermions to the case coupled with non-Abelian chromo-electromagnetic fields and employ this formalism to investigate the spin polarization of quarks under dynamically generated color fields in near-equilibrium quark gluon plasmas. It is found that the spin polarization could be induced by parity-odd correlators of color fields, which may dominate over collisional effects at weak coupling. Our result provides with a possible explanation for the spin alignment of vector mesons measured in high-energy nuclear collisions and alludes to the connection with local parity violation.

## Key claim

Spin polarization of quarks can be induced by parity-odd correlators of color fields in a quark-gluon plasma, which may dominate over collisional effects at weak coupling and provide a possible explanation for the spin alignment of vector mesons measured in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the result depends on several assumptions including local parity violation and specific approximations for the color-field correlators.

## Method

The authors extended the quantum kinetic theory for relativistic fermions to include coupling with non-Abelian chromo-electromagnetic fields, deriving modified Boltzmann equations for the color-singlet distribution function and an axial-vector kinetic equation for the spin four-vector of massive quarks. They then solved these equations analytically under assumptions of local spacetime translational invariance, thermal equilibrium for the quark distribution, a hierarchy where chromo-magnetic field correlators dominate over chromo-electric and mixed correlators, and a Gaussian form for the color-field correlation function with correlation time τ_c.

## Key numbers

- The spin polarization of quarks from the dynamical source term scales as ℏ(t - t_0)/(12 p_0^2) times the derivative of the equilibrium distribution function and parity-odd color-field correlators
- The non-dynamical source term in the axial current contains a factor √π τ_c/(4 ε_p^3) multiplying combinations of color-field correlators and the derivative of the equilibrium distribution
- The spin density matrix element ρ_00 for vector mesons is related to quark and anti-quark polarization via ρ_00 = (1 - P^i_q P^i_ȳ)/(3 + P^i_q P^i_ȳ), where ρ_00 = 1/3 corresponds to no polarization
- The authors predict ρ_00(K*0) < ρ_00(φ) < 1/3 for light quarks, consistent with ALICE measurements

## Context

This work extends the quantum kinetic theory framework developed by Hattori (2019) and Yang (2020) for spin transport to include background color fields, building on earlier work by Asakawa (2006) showing that turbulent color fields from plasma instabilities can dominate transport over collisional effects. The results address the puzzle of vector meson spin alignment measurements at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2022) and LHC (ALICE Collaboration, 2019), which cannot be explained by vorticity-induced polarization alone. The finding connects spin alignment to local parity violation in QCD plasmas, a long-sought phenomenon, and opens the possibility of studying dynamical spin polarization from color fields in the glasma phase of heavy-ion collisions.
