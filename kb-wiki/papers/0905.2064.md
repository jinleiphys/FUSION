# Quantum phase transitions in rotating nuclei

> We extend the classical Landau theory for rotating nuclei and show that the backbending in 162Yb, that comes about as a result of the two-quasiparticle alignment, is identified with the second order phase transition. We found that the backbending in 156Dy, caused by the instability of $γ$-vibrations in the rotating frame, corresponds to the first order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper extends classical Landau theory to rotating nuclei and claims that backbending in \(^{162}\)Yb, resulting from two-quasiparticle alignment, corresponds to a second-order phase transition, while backbending in \(^{156}\)Dy, caused by instability of \(\gamma\)-vibrations in the rotating frame, corresponds to a first-order phase transition. The hedge is that the classification depends on the specific mechanism driving the backbending in each nucleus, not on the backbending phenomenon itself.

## Method

The authors used a cranked Nilsson model plus random phase approximation (CRPA) to calculate the mean-field energy surfaces as a function of the deformation parameter \(\gamma\) (order parameter) and rotational frequency \(\Omega\) (control parameter), with fixed \(\beta\) deformation. They analyzed the potential landscape sections near the shape transition from axially symmetric (\(\gamma=0\)) to triaxial (\(\gamma\neq0\)) shapes, fitting the energy functional to polynomial forms characteristic of Landau theory for first- and second-order phase transitions. The consistency between mean field and residual interactions was achieved by varying the strength constants of pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions in the CRPA.

## Key numbers

- The experimental rotational frequency at the transition point is \(\hbar\Omega_c \approx 0.27\) MeV for \(^{162}\)Yb and \(\hbar\Omega_c \approx 0.32\) MeV for \(^{156}\)Dy.
- The calculated triaxiality sets in at \(\hbar\Omega_c = 0.250\) MeV for \(^{162}\)Yb and \(\hbar\Omega_c = 0.301\) MeV for \(^{156}\)Dy.
- The deformation parameter \(\beta\) remains nearly constant across the transition: \(\beta_t \approx 0.2\) for \(^{162}\)Yb and \(\beta_t \approx 0.31\) for \(^{156}\)Dy.
- For \(^{156}\)Dy at the critical point, the polynomial fit coefficients are \(F_0(\Omega)=0.3169\) MeV, \(F_2(\Omega)=0.12239\), \(F_3(\Omega)=0.009199\), \(F_4(\Omega)=1.7\times10^{-4}\) (with \(\gamma\) in degrees).
- For \(^{156}\)Dy, the generic model yields two minima at \(\eta=0\) (\(\gamma\approx0^\circ\)) and \(\eta=2/3\) (\(\gamma\approx27^\circ\)) at the universal value \(\alpha=2/9\).
- For \(^{162}\)Yb, the critical exponent \(\nu=1/2\) is obtained, with \(\gamma\sim (\Omega-\Omega_c)^\nu\).

## Context

This work extends the classical Landau theory of phase transitions to rotating nuclei, building on earlier studies of quantum phase transitions in nuclear structure within the interacting boson model (Jo02). It provides a microscopic interpretation of backbending in rare-earth nuclei, contrasting the mechanisms in \(^{162}\)Yb (alignment-driven, second-order) and \(^{156}\)Dy (vibration-driven, first-order). The results enable predictions of wobbling excitations near the yrast line, with specific selection rules for quadrupole electric transitions from wobbling to yrast states, as detailed in a related work (wob).
