# Integrability and chemical potential in the (3+1)-dimensional Skyrme model

> Using a remarkable mapping from the original (3+1)dimensional Skyrme model to the Sine-Gordon model, we construct the first analytic examples of Skyrmions as well as of Skyrmions--anti-Skyrmions bound states within a finite box in 3+1 dimensional flat space-time. An analytic upper bound on the number of these Skyrmions--anti-Skyrmions bound states is derived. We compute the critical isospin chemical potential beyond which these Skyrmions cease to exist. With these tools, we also construct topologically protected time-crystals: time-periodic configurations whose time-dependence is protected by their non-trivial winding number. These are striking realizations of the ideas of Shapere and Wilczek. The critical isospin chemical potential for these time-crystals is determined.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the first analytic examples of Skyrmions and Skyrmion-anti-Skyrmion bound states in (3+1)-dimensional flat space-time within a finite box, using a mapping from the Skyrme model to the Sine-Gordon model that is exact only in a specific non-spherical topological sector; the mapping holds under the condition that the generalized hedgehog ansatz is used and the box size is finite.

## Method

The authors employ a generalized hedgehog ansatz that reduces the full (3+1)-dimensional Skyrme field equations to a single (1+1)-dimensional Sine-Gordon equation for a profile function H(r,z). They introduce an isospin chemical potential via a covariant derivative with a time-component term, and analyze static and time-periodic configurations by solving the resulting ordinary or partial differential equations with appropriate boundary conditions for baryon charge B = ±1 or B = 0.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the Skyrmion with B = ±1 is E ≃ 181.312 for a box size ℓ ≃ 0.32 (with K = 2, λ = 1, lengths in fm, energy in MeV), which is approximately 8.25% above the Bogomol'ny bound of 12√2 π².
- The critical isospin chemical potential for Skyrmions in a box is given by (μ̄_c)² = (λ + 2ℓ²) / (8λ ℓ²).
- The number n_B of Skyrmion-anti-Skyrmion bound states satisfies n_B ≤ (8π / β²) - 1, where β = 4ℓ / √(λ + 2ℓ²).
- The critical frequency for time-crystal configurations is ω_c² = (λ + 4ℓ²) / (ℓ² λ).
- The critical isospin chemical potential for time-crystals satisfies λ + ℓ² [4 - λ ω² + 8 λ μ̄*(ω - 2μ̄*)] ≤ 0.

## Context

This work extends the generalized hedgehog ansatz approach of Canfora et al. (2015-2017) to include finite volume effects and isospin chemical potential, providing the first analytic construction of Skyrmion-anti-Skyrmion bound states in flat space. It also realizes the time-crystal concept proposed by Shapere and Wilczek (2012) in a nuclear-physics context, showing that topological protection can preserve time-periodicity. The results enable analytic studies of Skyrme phase diagrams at finite density and volume, and suggest experimental signatures for topologically protected time crystals in nuclear matter.
