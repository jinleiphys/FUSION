# Relativistic model for the nonmesonic weak decay of single-lambda hypernuclei

> Having in mind its future extension for theoretical investigations related to charmed nuclei, we develop a relativistic formalism for the nonmesonic weak decay of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei in the framework of the independent-particle shell model and with the dynamics represented by the $(π,K)$ one-meson-exchange model. Numerical results for the one-nucleon-induced transition rates of ${}^{12}_Λ\textrm{C}$ are presented and compared with those obtained in the analogous nonrelativistic calculation. There is satisfactory agreement between the two approaches, and the most noteworthy difference is that the ratio $Γ_{n}/Γ_{p}$ is appreciably higher and closer to the experimental value in the relativistic calculation. Large discrepancies between ours and previous relativistic calculations are found, for which we do not encounter any fully satisfactory explanation. The most recent experimental data is well reproduced by our results. In summary, we have achieved our purpose to develop a reliable model for the relativistic calculation of the nonmesonic weak decay of $Λ$-hypernuclei, which can now be extended to evaluate similar processes in charmed nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors develop a relativistic formalism for the nonmesonic weak decay of single-Λ hypernuclei, finding that the ratio Γn/Γp is appreciably higher and closer to experimental values in the relativistic calculation compared to analogous nonrelativistic approaches, though large discrepancies with previous relativistic calculations remain without a fully satisfactory explanation.

## Method
The formalism uses the independent-particle shell model with Dirac equation solutions for bound-state wave functions and plane-wave expansions for outgoing nucleons, employing a (π,K) one-meson-exchange model for the weak decay dynamics. The decay rates are derived from Fermi's Golden Rule with angular momentum projections summed in closed form using Racah algebra, and spectroscopic factors are evaluated in second quantized formalism rather than using coefficients of fractional parentage. Recoil effects are included by performing angular integrations over the relative angle between the two outgoing nucleons.

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic OME model studies by Krmpotić et al. (2014) and others to a relativistic framework, motivated by future applications to charmed hypernuclei where large energy releases make nonrelativistic approaches inapplicable. It contrasts with earlier relativistic calculations by Ramos et al. (1991, 1992) and Conti et al. (2009) which reported decay rates differing considerably from nonrelativistic results and contradicting experimental data. The formalism enables future evaluation of similar weak decay processes in charmed hypernuclei, which may become experimentally accessible at FAIR and JPARC facilities.
