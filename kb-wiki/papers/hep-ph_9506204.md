# Chiral Prediction for the $πN$ Scattering Length $a^-$ to Order ${\cal O}(M_π^4)$

> We evaluate the S-wave pion--nucleon scattering length $a^-$ in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up--to--and--including terms of order $M_π^4$. We show that the order $M_π^4$ piece of the isovector amplitude at threshold, $T^-_{\rm thr}$, vanishes exactly. We predict for the isovector scattering length, $0.088 \, M_{π^+}^{-1} \le a^- \le 0.096 \, M_{π^+}^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The isovector pion-nucleon scattering length \(a^-\) receives no contribution at order \(M_\pi^4\) because the entire \(M_\pi^4\) piece of the threshold amplitude \(T^-_{\mathrm{thr}}\) vanishes exactly; the next corrections are suppressed by four powers of \(M_\pi/(4\pi F_\pi)\) and are expected to be small, but the quoted prediction band of \(0.088\,M_{\pi^+}^{-1} \le a^- \le 0.096\,M_{\pi^+}^{-1}\) depends on varying resonance parameters and the renormalization scale \(\lambda\) within their bounds.

## Method

The authors evaluate the S-wave isovector pion-nucleon scattering length \(a^-\) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to and including terms of order \(M_\pi^4\). They compute all one-loop graphs with one insertion from the second-order Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\) and show that the thirteen contributing diagrams cancel exactly at threshold due to crossing symmetry and properties of the loop functions. The remaining uncertainty in the one-loop result arises from the counterterm \(B^r(\lambda)\), which is estimated via resonance saturation from the \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N^*(1440)\) contributions, with the scale \(\lambda\) varied between \(M_\eta = 550\) MeV and \(m_{N^*} = 1.44\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The chiral perturbation theory prediction for the isovector scattering length is \(0.088 \, M_{\pi^+}^{-1} \le a^- \le 0.096 \, M_{\pi^+}^{-1}\).
- The current-algebra prediction of Weinberg is \(a^-_{\mathrm{CA}} = 0.079 \, M_\pi^{-1}\) (using \(M_\pi = 139.57\) MeV, \(m_p = 938.27\) MeV, \(F_\pi = 92.5\) MeV).
- The Karlsruhe-Helsinki phase shift analysis gives \(a^- = 0.092 \pm 0.002 \, M_\pi^{-1}\).
- The pionic hydrogen measurement yields \(a^- = 0.096 \pm 0.007 \, M_\pi^{-1}\); combining pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium gives \(a^- = 0.086 \pm 0.002 \, M_\pi^{-1}\).
- The Born term contribution to \(a^-\) is only 1% of the current-algebra value.

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) by showing that the \(M_\pi^4\) correction to the isovector threshold amplitude vanishes exactly, sharpening the earlier one-loop result of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1994). The prediction is consistent with the Karlsruhe-Helsinki dispersion-relation analysis and with recent pionic atom measurements from the PSI-ETH group (Badertscher et al., Sigg et al.). The result enables more precise extraction of the isoscalar scattering length \(a^+\) from pionic atom data, though no firm prediction for \(a^+\) is given due to large cancellations and poorly known counterterms.
