# Model for $J/ψ$ absorption in hadronic matter

> The cross sections for $J/ψ$ absorption by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons are studied in a meson-exchange model that includes not only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson couplings but also three-vector-meson and four-point couplings. We find that they are much larger than in a previous study where only pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson couplings were considered. Including form factors at interaction vertices, the $J/ψ$ absorption cross sections $σ_{πψ}$ and $σ_{ρψ}$ are found to have values on the order of 7 mb and 3 mb, respectively. Their thermal averages in hadronic matter at temperature T=150 MeV are, respectively, about 1 mb and 2 mb.

## Key claim

The cross sections for J/ψ absorption by π and ρ mesons, calculated in a meson-exchange model that includes three-vector-meson and four-point couplings in addition to pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson couplings, are much larger than in a previous study that considered only the latter couplings; however, including form factors at interaction vertices introduces significant uncertainties, and the quoted values of about 7 mb and 3 mb for σπψ and σρψ, respectively, are order-of-magnitude estimates that depend on the choice of cutoff parameters.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective hadronic Lagrangian based on SU(4) symmetry with minimal substitution, generating pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar-vector-meson (PPV), three-vector-meson (VVV), and four-point (PPVV and VVVV) interaction terms. They evaluated the cross sections for the processes πψ → D*D̅ (and its charge conjugate), ρψ → DD̅, and ρψ → D*D̅* using the resulting amplitudes, with coupling constants determined from D* decay width and vector meson dominance (VMD) model. Form factors of monopole form with cutoff parameters Λ = 1 or 2 GeV were introduced at interaction vertices to account for the composite nature of hadrons.

## Key numbers

- σπψ (with form factors): on the order of 7 mb
- σρψ (with form factors): on the order of 3 mb
- Thermal average ⟨σπψv⟩ at T = 150 MeV: about 1 mb
- Thermal average ⟨σρψv⟩ at T = 150 MeV: about 2 mb
- Coupling constant gπDD* = 4.4, determined from D* decay width
- Coupling constants from VMD: gρDD = gρD*D* = 2.52, gψDD = gψD*D* = 7.64
- Kinematic thresholds (m3 + m4 − m1 − m2): about 0.64 GeV for πψ → D*D̅, −0.14 GeV for ρψ → DD̅, and 0.15 GeV for ρψ → D*D̅*

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model approach of Haglin (1999) by using a different treatment of VVV and four-point couplings and by including form factors at interaction vertices. It addresses the large discrepancies among predictions from the quark-exchange model (Martins, Blaschke, and Quack, 1995; Wong, Swanson, and Barnes, 1999), the perturbative QCD approach (Kharzeev and Satz, 1995), and earlier meson-exchange models (Matinyan and Müller, 1998). The results provide cross sections comparable to those used in phenomenological studies of J/ψ absorption by comoving hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, enabling more quantitative assessments of whether such absorption can explain the anomalous J/ψ suppression observed by the NA50 experiment at CERN.
