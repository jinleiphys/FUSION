# Anomalous plasma: chiral magnetic effect and all that

> Chiral anomalous effects in relativistic plasmas are reviewed. The essence of chiral separation and chiral magnetic effects is explained in simple terms. Qualitative differences between the two phenomena, both of which are triggered by background electromagnetic fields, are highlighted. It is shown how an interplay of the chiral separation and chiral magnetic effects could lead to a new collective plasma mode, called the chiral magnetic wave. It is argued that the chiral magnetic wave is overdamped in weakly interacting plasmas. Its fate in a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma is less clear, especially in the presence of a superstrong magnetic field. The observational signatures of the chiral anomalous effects are discussed briefly.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic wave (CMW), previously predicted as a gapless propagating collective mode in chiral plasmas, is actually overdamped (non-propagating) in weakly interacting plasmas with high conductivity; its existence as a propagating mode in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma remains unclear, especially under superstrong magnetic fields.

## Method

The author derives the dispersion relations for collective modes in a chiral plasma by coupling the continuity equations for electric and chiral charge densities (including the chiral anomaly term) with Maxwell's equations, Ohm's current, and diffusion currents. The system is solved for modes propagating along the background magnetic field, and the resulting characteristic equation is analyzed in the limits of high and low electrical conductivity.

## Key numbers

- The chiral separation effect current density is given by $\mathbf{j}_5 = e\mathbf{B}\mu/(2\pi^2)$, where $\mu$ is the electric charge chemical potential and $\mathbf{B}$ is the background magnetic field.
- The chiral magnetic effect current density is given by $\mathbf{j} = e\mathbf{B}\mu_5/(2\pi^2)$, where $\mu_5$ is the chiral chemical potential.
- The chirality flip rate $\Gamma_m$ for nonzero fermion mass is estimated as $\Gamma_m \simeq c_1 \alpha^2 m_e^2/T$ for $T \lesssim m_e/\sqrt{\alpha}$ and $\Gamma_m \simeq c_2 \alpha m_e^2/T$ for $T \gtrsim m_e/\sqrt{\alpha}$, where $c_1, c_2$ are constants of order 1.
- The charge density susceptibility is $\chi = T^2/3 + (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2)/\pi^2$; the chiral charge density susceptibility $\chi_5$ has the same expression.
- In the high-conductivity limit, the two collective mode frequencies are purely imaginary: $\omega^{(+)} \simeq -i\sigma$ and $\omega^{(-)} \simeq -i e^2 B^2 (k^2 + e^2 \chi)/(4\pi^4 \chi^2 \sigma)$, indicating overdamped (diffusive) modes.
- In the vanishing-conductivity limit, the frequencies become $\omega^{(\pm)} = \mp (eB/(2\pi^2 \chi)) \sqrt{k^2 + e^2 \chi} - i(\tau/3)k^2$, describing gapped propagating modes with gap $M = e^2 B/(2\pi^2 \sqrt{\chi})$.
- For a hot relativistic QED plasma with $\mu = \mu_5 = 0$, the condition for diffusive (non-propagating) modes in the long-wavelength limit is $\sqrt{3} e^4 B/(\pi^2 C_\sigma T^2) \ln e^{-1} < 1$, where $C_\sigma$ is a constant of order 1.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of chiral anomalous transport phenomena in relativistic plasmas, building on the foundational works on the chiral magnetic effect by Fukushima (2008), Kharzeev (2009), and others, and on the chiral separation effect by Vilenkin (1980) and Metlitski (2005). The analysis critically reexamines the prediction of the chiral magnetic wave by Kharzeev (2010), showing that the original background-field approximation neglected the role of dynamical electric fields and Gauss's law, leading to an inconsistent result. The work enables future studies of whether the CMW could become propagating in regimes of reduced conductivity and superstrong magnetic fields, such as those possibly realized in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma.
