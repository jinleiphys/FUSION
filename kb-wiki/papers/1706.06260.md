# Parton model description of multiparticle azimuthal correlations in $pA$ collisions

> In arXiv:1705.00745, an initial state "parton model" of quarks scattering off a dense nuclear target was shown to qualitatively reproduce the systematics of multiparticle azimuthal anisotropy cumulants measured in proton/deuteron-nucleus ($pA$) collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The systematics included i) the behavior of the four-particle cumulant $c_2\{4\}$, which generates a real four-particle second Fourier harmonic $v_2\{4\}$, ii) the ordering $v_2\{2\}>v_2\{4\}\approx v_2\{6\}\approx v_2\{8\}$ for two-, four-, six-, and eight-particle Fourier harmonics, iii) the behavior of so-called symmetric cumulants $\text{SC}(2,3)$ and $\text{SC}(2,4)$. These features of azimuthal multiparticle cumulants were previously interpreted as a signature of hydrodynamic flow; our results challenge this interpretation. We expand here upon our previous study and present further details and novel results on the saturation scale and transverse momentum ($p_\perp$) dependence of multiparticle azimuthal correlations. We find that the dependence of $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$ on the number of color domains in the target varies with the $p_\perp$ window explored. We extend our prior discussion of symmetric cumulants and compute as yet unmeasured symmetric cumulants. We investigate the $N_c$ dependence of $v_2\{2\}$ and $v_2\{4\}$. We contrast our results, which include multiple scatterings of each quark off the target, to the Glasma graph approximation, where each quark suffers at most two gluon exchanges with the target. We find that coherent multiple scattering is essential to obtain a positive definite $v_2\{4\}$. We provide an algorithm to compute expectation values of arbitrary products of the "dipole" lightlike Wilson line correlators.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an initial-state parton model of quarks scattering coherently off a dense nuclear target can qualitatively reproduce the systematics of multiparticle azimuthal anisotropy cumulants (including negative four-particle cumulants and the hierarchy of Fourier harmonics) that were previously interpreted as unique signatures of hydrodynamic flow; the authors explicitly state that their results "challenge this interpretation" but hedge that the hydrodynamic description "is likely valid in the larger, more central collisions of heavy-ions."

## Method

The authors model proton-nucleus collisions as independent collinear quarks from the projectile scattering eikonaly off a dense nuclear target described by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, with the scattering encoded in lightlike Wilson line correlators. They compute multiparticle inclusive spectra as expectation values of products of dipole operators (traces of Wilson lines) averaged over the target color fields, using a general algorithm to evaluate multi-dipole correlators via a 24-by-24 transformation matrix that captures all topologies (dipoles, quadrupoles, sextupoles, octupoles) arising from multiple gluon exchanges. The results are contrasted with the Glasma graph approximation, which includes at most two gluon exchanges per quark.

## Key numbers

- The nonperturbative scale for the projectile Wigner function is fixed at B_p = 4 GeV^{-2}, obtained from dipole model fits to HERA deep inelastic scattering data.
- The saturation scale Q_s of the target is the inverse size of color domains; the paper studies the dependence of v_2{2} and v_2{4} on the number of color domains for varying p_perp windows.
- The four-particle cumulant c_2{4} is found to be negative (giving a real positive v_2{4}) only when coherent multiple scattering is included; the Glasma graph approximation produces positive c_2{4} and therefore no real v_2{4}.
- The ordering v_2{2} > v_2{4} ≈ v_2{6} ≈ v_2{8} is obtained in the Abelian version of the model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Dusling, Mace, Venugopalan, 2017) and earlier two-particle azimuthal correlation work by Lappi (2015) and Lappi and Schenke (2015). It directly challenges the hydrodynamic interpretation of multiparticle cumulants in small systems, which was previously applied to proton-nucleus and deuteron-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The algorithm for computing multi-dipole correlators builds on the framework of Blaizot, Iancu, and Weigert (2004) and can be generalized to higher-point functions, potentially enabling future studies of many-body final states in high-energy QCD.
