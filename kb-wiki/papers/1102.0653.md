# SGRs and AXPs as rotation powered massive white dwarfs

> SGR 0418+5729 is a "Rosetta Stone" for deciphering the energy source of Soft Gamma Ray Repeaters (SGRs) and Anomalous X-ray Pulsars (AXPs). We show a model based on canonical physics and astrophysics for SGRs and AXPs powered by massive highly magnetized rotating white dwarfs (WDs), in total analogy with pulsars powered by rotating neutron stars (NSs). We predict for SGR 0418+5729 a lower limit for its spin-down rate, $\dot{P} \geq L_X P^3/(4π^2 I)=1.18\times 10^{-16}$ where $I$ is the moment of inertia of the WD. We show for SGRs and AXPs that, the occurrence of the glitch and the gain of rotational energy, is due to the release of gravitational energy associated to the contraction and decrease of the moment of inertia of the WDs. The steady emission and the outburst following the glitch are explained by the loss of rotational energy of the Wds, in view of the much larger moment of inertia of the WDs, as compared to the one of NSs and/or quark stars. There is no need here to invoke the unorthodox concept of magnetic energy release due to decay of overcritical magnetic fields, as assumed in the magnetar model. A new astrophysical scenario for the SGRs and AXPs associated to Supernova remnants is presented. The observational campaigns of the X-ray Japanese satellite Suzaku on AE Aquarii and the corresponding theoretical works by Japanese groups and recent results of the Hubble Space Telescope, give crucial information for our theoretical model. Follow-on missions of Hubble Telescope and VLT are highly recommended to give further observational evidence of this most fundamental issue of relativistic astrophysics: the identification of the true SGRs/AXPs energy source.

## Key claim

The authors claim that Soft Gamma Ray Repeaters (SGRs) and Anomalous X-ray Pulsars (AXPs) can be explained as rotation-powered massive white dwarfs rather than magnetars, with the specific condition that the observed X-ray luminosity of SGR 0418+5729 can be accounted for by rotational energy loss if its spin-down rate is at least 1.18 x 10^-16, a prediction left to be verified by future observations.

## Method

The authors applied the standard dipole magnetic field formula to model SGRs and AXPs as massive white dwarfs with fiducial parameters (mass 1.4 solar masses, radius 10^3 km, moment of inertia ~10^49 g cm^2), comparing the rotational energy loss and magnetic field estimates to those from the neutron star magnetar model (mass 1.4 solar masses, radius 10 km, moment of inertia ~10^45 g cm^2). They analyzed the glitch-outburst connection using conservation of angular momentum during sudden decreases in the white dwarf's moment of inertia, and compared the predicted rotational energy changes to observed burst energies for specific sources including 1E 2259+586 and 1E 1048.1-5937.

## Key numbers

- For SGR 0418+5729: predicted lower limit for spin-down rate is 1.18 x 10^-16, with an upper limit of 6.0 x 10^-15 from observations; rotational period P = 9.08 s; X-ray luminosity L_X = 6.2 x 10^31 erg/s
- For the white dwarf model: rotational energy loss formula gives dot_E_rot = -3.95 x 10^50 (dot_P / P^3) erg/s; magnetic field formula gives B = 3.2 x 10^15 (P dot_P)^(1/2) G
- For the neutron star model: rotational energy loss formula gives dot_E_rot = -3.95 x 10^46 (dot_P / P^3) erg/s; magnetic field formula gives B = 3.2 x 10^19 (P dot_P)^(1/2) G
- For 1E 2259+586 glitch (June 2002): observed fractional period change Delta_P/P ~ -4 x 10^-6; predicted rotational energy change |Delta_E_rot| ~ 1.7 x 10^43 erg; observed energy released ~ 3 x 10^41 erg
- For 1E 1048.1-5937 glitch (March 2007): observed Delta_P/P ~ -1.63 x 10^-5; predicted |Delta_E_rot| ~ 7.73 x 10^43 erg; observed energy released 4.3 x 10^41 erg
- Critical magnetic field for vacuum polarization: B_c = 4.4 x 10^13 G
- For SGR 0418+5729 as a white dwarf: inferred magnetic field B < 7.5 x 10^8 G
- For SGR 0418+5729 as a neutron star: inferred magnetic field B < 7.5 x 10^12 G
- White dwarf critical rotational period for secular instability: P_crit ~ 0.94 s
- White dwarf minimum rotational period from general relativistic analysis: P_min ~ 0.3 s

## Context

This work extends the earlier white dwarf model for SGRs and AXPs proposed by Paczynski (1990) and Usov (1994), specifically applying it to the newly discovered SGR 0418+5729 which challenges the magnetar model due to its low magnetic field upper limit. The authors contrast their model with the magnetar scenario developed by Duncan and Thompson (1992) and Thompson and Duncan (1995), arguing that the white dwarf model avoids invoking overcritical magnetic fields as a primary energy source. The paper enables future observational tests by predicting a specific spin-down rate for SGR 0418+5729 that can be verified by dedicated missions, and suggests that follow-on observations with the Hubble Space Telescope and Very Large Telescope could provide further evidence for the white dwarf interpretation.
