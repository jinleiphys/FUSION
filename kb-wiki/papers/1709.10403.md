# Semiclassical catastrophe theory of simple bifurcations

> The Fedoriuk-Maslov catastrophe theory of caustics and turning points is extended to solve the bifurcation problems by the improved stationary phase method (ISPM). The trace formulas for the radial power-law (RPL) potentials are presented by the ISPM based on the second- and third-order expansion of the classical action near the stationary point. A considerable enhancement of contributions of the two orbits (pair of consisting of the parent and newborn orbits) at their bifurcation is shown. The ISPM trace formula is proposed for a simple bifurcation scenario of Hamiltonian systems with continuous symmetries, where the contributions of the bifurcating parent orbits vanish upon approaching the bifurcation point due to the reduction of the end-point manifold. This occurs since the contribution of the parent orbits is included in the term corresponding to the family of the newborn daughter orbits. Taking this feature into account, the ISPM level densities calculated for the RPL potential model are shown to be in good agreement with the quantum results at the bifurcations and asymptotically far from the bifurcation points.

# Semiclassical catastrophe theory of simple bifurcations

## Key claim
The Fedoriuk-Maslov catastrophe theory, originally developed for caustics and turning points, is extended to handle bifurcation problems in periodic-orbit theory by using the improved stationary phase method (ISPM). The parent orbit's contribution vanishes at the bifurcation point because it is included in the term for the newborn daughter orbit family, and the ISPM level densities agree with quantum results both at bifurcations and asymptotically far from them.

## Method
The authors apply the Fedoriuk-Maslov catastrophe theory to the trace integral for the level density, expanding the classical action to third order near stationary points where the second derivative vanishes (caustic points) or diverges (turning points). They use linear coordinate transformations to reduce turning-point singularities to caustic-point form, and evaluate the resulting catastrophe integrals using incomplete Airy and Gairy functions with finite integration limits. For the spherical radial power-law (RPL) potential model with parameter α controlling surface diffuseness, they compute semiclassical level densities via the ISPM trace formula and compare them to quantum calculations.

## Key numbers
- The Maslov index increases by 1 at each simple caustic or turning point, corresponding to a phase shift of −π/2 in the semiclassical propagator.
- The argument w of the Airy and Gairy functions is given by w = κ^(2/3) ε^2 / (3a)^(4/3), where ε is the dimensionless distance from the catastrophe point and κ ∝ 1/ħ is the large semiclassical parameter.
- The stationary points in the transformed variable z are z±*(ε) = ±√w(ε), which degenerate to zero at the caustic point (ε → 0).
- For the RPL potential, the spherical symmetry gives classical degeneracy K = 3 for generic orbit families, while equatorial orbits have K = 1 and circular orbits have K = 2.

## Context
This work extends the Fedoriuk-Maslov catastrophe theory (Fedoriuk, 1970s; Maslov, 1970s) to bifurcation problems in periodic-orbit theory, building on the improved stationary phase method developed by Magner and collaborators (Magner et al., 2001-2016). It contrasts with uniform approximation approaches based on normal-form theories (Ozorio de Almeida, 1988; Ullmo, 1990s; Schomerus, 1997; Sieber, 1997) that require artificial interpolation procedures through bifurcations. The ISPM provides simpler analytic expressions for Gaussian-averaged level densities and energy shell corrections, applicable to realistic nuclear mean-field potentials with deformation. The spherical RPL potential model was previously analyzed within ISPM by Magner, Khavroshkin, and Arita (2016), showing good semiclassical-quantum agreement for several surface diffuseness parameters including symmetry-breaking and bifurcation values.
