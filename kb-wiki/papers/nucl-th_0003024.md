# Kaon-baryon coupling constants in the QCD sum rule approach

> We improve our previous QCD sum rule calculation on G(KNΛ) and G(KNΣ) coupling constants by including the contributions from higher dimensional condensates, <\bar{q} g_s σ\cdot G q> and <\bar{q}q> <(α_s /π) G^2>, in the OPE. It is found that the contribution of these condensates is non-negligible compared to that of the quark condensates. Using a best-fit analysis we find |G(KNΛ)| = 2.49 $\pm$ 1.25 and |G(KNΣ)| = 0.395 $\pm$ 0.377.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculation of the kaon-baryon coupling constants G(KNΛ) and G(KNΣ) is improved by including higher-dimensional condensates (quark-gluon and mixed quark-gluon condensates), but the results show no Borel plateau and require a best-fit method; the extracted values are much smaller than SU(3) symmetry predictions and previous QCD sum rule estimates.

## Method

The author constructs three-point correlation functions using baryon currents for the nucleon, Λ, and Σ, and a pseudoscalar kaon current, then performs an operator product expansion (OPE) including dimension-5 quark-gluon condensates and dimension-7 condensates. The sum rules are derived for the q-slash i γ5 structure, Borel transformed, and solved using a best-fit minimization over a Borel interval determined by OPE convergence and pole dominance criteria, with continuum thresholds chosen to account for excited baryon states.

## Key numbers

- |G(KNΛ)| = 2.49 ± 1.25 from best-fit analysis with Borel interval (0.478, 1.068) GeV² and continuum threshold s₀ = 2.074 GeV²
- |G(KNΣ)| = 0.395 ± 0.377 from best-fit analysis with Borel interval (0.488, 1.584) GeV² and continuum threshold s₀ = 2.356 GeV²
- The quark-gluon condensate contributes about 25% of the leading term for G(KNΛ) and about 40% for G(KNΣ) at M² = 1 GeV²
- For comparison, the author also obtains |g(πNN)| = 3.65 ± 2.31 using the same method with Borel interval (0.460, 1.110) GeV²
- The ratio |G(KNΛ)/G(KNΣ)| = 6.30 using basic inputs, compared to the SU(3) symmetry prediction of 3.55

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCD sum rule calculation (Choe 1998) by including higher-dimensional condensates that were previously neglected, motivated by similar improvements in the nucleon mass sum rule (Leinweber 1997). The results differ significantly from other QCD sum rule estimates: Krippa (1998) found G(KNΛ) = 10 ± 6 and G(KNΣ) = 3.6 ± 2, while Bracco, Navarra, and Nielsen (1999) found G(KNΛ) = 2.37 ± 0.09 and G(KNΣ) = 0.025 ± 0.015. The present results are much smaller than SU(3) symmetry predictions (which give G(KNΛ) ≈ -16 to -11 and G(KNΣ) ≈ 3 to 4.5 with 20% breaking) and experimental fitting values (G(KNΛ) = -13.7, G(KNΣ) = 3.9 from Timmermans, Rijken, and de Swart 1995), indicating large SU(3) symmetry breaking. The work enables further investigation of kaon-nuclear physics and highlights the need for more precise determinations of the quark-gluon condensate and inclusion of even higher-dimensional operators.
