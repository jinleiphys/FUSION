# Anomalous Dynamical Screening of Relativistic Plasma in a Magnetic Field

> We study collective excitations in a relativistic collisionless plasma composed of massless fermions subject to an external magnetic field. We include dynamical electromagnetism and fluctuations of the chiral charge, while maintaining a vanishing net chiral charge. Within the framework of chiral kinetic theory, we find that the chiral anomaly gives a correction to the transverse photon self-energy and that a novel type of dynamical screening can emerge. In the strong magnetic field limit, we also show that the collective mode has a gap using the lowest Landau level approximation. We further discuss implications of the anomalous dynamical screening for neutron star phenomenology.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly modifies the transverse photon self-energy in a relativistic collisionless plasma under an external magnetic field, producing a novel dynamical screening of the O-mode that is distinct from conventional Landau damping. This screening is "dynamical" in the weak-field limit because the static limit cannot be taken (the gap appears only at nonzero frequency), whereas in the strong-field limit the static limit can be taken and the screening becomes genuine.

## Method

The author uses chiral kinetic theory with Berry curvature corrections to solve the collisionless Boltzmann equation for massless fermions in a uniform external magnetic field, expanding the distribution function order by order in the gauge field, derivative, and coupling constant. The permittivity tensor is derived from the induced electric current, and the dispersion relation for the ordinary mode (O-mode) is obtained by coupling the Maxwell equations with the chiral anomaly continuity equation for the fluctuating chiral charge density. In the strong magnetic field limit, the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation is employed, treating the fermions as effectively (1+1)-dimensional.

## Key numbers

- Debye screening mass for chirality λ: \( m_{{\rm D}\lambda}^2 = e^2\left( \frac{\mu_\lambda^2}{2\pi^2} + \frac{T^2}{6} \right) \)
- Anomalous screening mass: \( m_{\rm a}^2 = \frac{e^4 B_{\rm ex}^2}{4\pi^4 \chi} \), where \( \chi \) is the chiral charge susceptibility
- In the quasi-static limit (\( \xi \ll 1 \)), the photon self-energy is \( \Pi_{\rm O}(\xi) \simeq -\frac{i\pi m_{\rm D}^2}{4}\xi - \frac{i e^4 B_{\rm ex}^2}{16\pi |k_x|^2}\xi + m_{\rm a}^2 + \frac{i\pi m_{\rm a}^2}{2}\xi \)
- In the quasi-long-wavelength limit (\( \xi \gg 1 \)), the plasma oscillation dispersion is \( \omega^2 \simeq \omega_{\rm p}^2(1+\zeta^2) + \frac{6}{5}|k_x|^2\left(1-\frac{4}{3}\zeta^2\right) \), where \( \omega_{\rm p} = m_{\rm D}/\sqrt{3} \), \( \omega_{\rm a} = m_{\rm a}/\sqrt{3} \), and \( \zeta = m_{\rm a}/m_{\rm D} \)
- In the strong-field LLL limit, the dispersion relation is \( \omega^2 = |k_x|^2 + m_{\rm a}^2 \), with \( m_{\rm a} \sim e^{3/2}\sqrt{B_{\rm ex}} \)
- Relaxation time scaling from Landau damping without magnetic field: \( 1/\tau_{\rm c} \sim \alpha^2 T^{5/3} / m_{\rm D}^{2/3} \)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective excitations in magnetized relativistic plasmas, which investigated zero sound of chiral fermions at finite chiral chemical potential in a non-dynamical background magnetic field (Gorsky 2012, Stephanov 2014) without including chiral charge fluctuations. It also builds on hydrodynamic treatments of collective modes (Rybalka 2018, Shovkovy 2018) by working in the collisionless regime. The results enable future studies of transport properties in neutron star matter, particularly electron-electron scattering mediated by photons, where the anomalous dynamical screening modifies the infrared cutoff and thus the relaxation time.
