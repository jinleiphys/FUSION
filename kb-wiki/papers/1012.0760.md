# Relic Antineutrino Capture on 163-Ho decaying Nuclei

> The electron capture decay of the isotope 163-Ho has been proposed since a long time as a candidate for measuring the electron neutrino mass and recently the interest on this idea has been renewed. In this paper we note that a direct observation of the cosmic antineutrino background could be made using a target made of this isotope. We further discuss the requests for an experiment aiming to obtain this result.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the electron-capture decaying isotope 163-Ho could be used as a target for direct observation of the cosmic antineutrino background (CνB), and argues that this approach is a valid alternative to tritium beta-decay targets, contrary to earlier assessments that had discarded it as less promising.

## Method

The authors calculate the rate for relic antineutrino capture on 163-Ho using the formalism for electron capture decays, incorporating Breit-Wigner resonance forms for the atomic levels and the density of final states. They compare the signal-to-background ratio for antineutrino capture relative to the background from ordinary electron capture decays near the endpoint, using published values for binding energies, level widths, and electron wave functions at the origin. The analysis includes the effect of detector energy resolution to determine the discovery potential for a future calorimetric experiment using bolometers.

## Key numbers

- The average number density of CνB neutrinos (or antineutrinos) of each flavour is n_ν ∼ 55 cm⁻³
- The ratio of CνB antineutrino captures to total EC events is λ_ν̄/λ_EC = (7.7·10⁻²², 5.8·10⁻²³, 1.4·10⁻²³) for Q = (2.3, 2.5, 2.8) keV respectively
- The factor of merit F(0.2 eV, 0.5 eV, Q) = (3.6·10⁻¹², 2.7·10⁻¹³, 6.5·10⁻¹⁴) for Q = (2.3, 2.5, 2.8) keV
- The half-life of 163-Ho is 4570 years (compared to 12.32 years for tritium)
- To obtain 10 signal events, the minimum required quantity of 163-Ho is (23.2, 307, 1274) kg·y for Q = (2.3, 2.5, 2.8) keV
- For tritium, 137 g·y of ³H is needed for 10 signal events
- For a neutrino mass of 0.5 eV, a detector resolution FWHM better than 0.33 eV is required to attain a statistical significance of 5

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Cocco et al. (2007) for detecting CνB neutrinos via beta-decaying nuclei, and re-examines the earlier assessment by Cocco et al. (2009) that had discarded electron-capture targets as less promising. The paper shows that 163-Ho, with its record low Q-value for electron capture decays (between 2.3 and 2.8 keV), can achieve a factor of merit comparable to or better than tritium, and that the ratio of signal to background near the endpoint is mathematically identical for both types of decaying nuclei. The results enable future experimental comparisons between bolometric 163-Ho detectors and tritium-based spectrometers for CνB detection, with the note that gravitational clustering effects (not included in the estimates) would enhance the signal.
