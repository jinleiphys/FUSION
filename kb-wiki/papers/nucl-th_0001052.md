# Ordinary and radiative muon capture on the proton and the pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon

> We calculate ordinary and radiative muon capture on the proton in an effective field theory of pions, nucleons and delta isobars, working to third and second order in the small scale expansion respectively. Preceding calculations in chiral effective field theories only employed pion and nucleon degrees of freedom and were not able to reproduce the photon spectrum in the pioneering experiment of radiative muon capture on the proton from TRIUMF. For the past few years it has been speculated that the discrepancy between theory and experiment might be due to Delta(1232) related effects, which are only included via higher order contact interactions in the standard chiral approach. In this report we demonstrate that this speculation does not hold true. We show that contrary to expectations from naive dimensional analysis isobar effects on the photon spectrum and the total rate in radiative muon capture are of the order of a few percent, consistent with earlier findings in a more phenomenological approach. We further demonstrate that both ordinary and radiative muon capture constitute systems with a very well behaved chiral expansion, both in standard chiral perturbation theory and in the small scale expansion, and present some new ideas that might be at the bottom of the still unresolved discrepancy between theory and experiment in radiative muon capture. Finally we comment upon the procedure employed by the TRIUMF group to extract new information from their radiative muon capture experiment on the pseudoscalar form factor of the nucleon. We show that it is inconsistent with the ordinary muon capture data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that explicit inclusion of the Delta(1232) isobar in the small scale expansion yields only a few percent correction to the photon spectrum and total rate in radiative muon capture on the proton, contrary to earlier speculation that Delta-related effects might explain the discrepancy between theory and the TRIUMF experiment. The discrepancy between theory and experiment in radiative muon capture remains unresolved, and the authors suggest the problem may lie in non-strong physics related to the distribution of spin states of muonic atoms.

## Method

The authors calculate ordinary muon capture (OMC) and radiative muon capture (RMC) on the proton using the small scale expansion (SSE), an effective field theory of pions, nucleons, and Delta(1232) isobars, working to third order in epsilon for OMC and second order for RMC. They compute the vector-vector and vector-axial correlators needed for RMC, including the leading Delta contributions at order epsilon^2, and evaluate the spin-averaged capture rates using the heavy baryon formulation with the muon and proton at rest. The hadronic matrix elements are expressed in terms of the isovector vector form factors F1^v, F2^v, the axial form factor GA, and the induced pseudoscalar form factor GP, with the momentum dependence taken from earlier chiral calculations.

## Key numbers

- The PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant is g_P^PCAC = 8.89.
- The chiral perturbation theory prediction is g_P^CHPT = 8.44 ± 0.23.
- The TRIUMF RMC experiment extracted g_P^RMC = 12.35 ± 0.88 ± 0.38, which is 1.46 times g_P^CHPT.
- The partial branching ratio for RMC in hydrogen is approximately 10^{-8} for photons with energy k > 60 MeV.
- The maximum photon energy in RMC is about k ~ 100 MeV.
- The momentum transfer in OMC is fixed at t = -0.88 m_mu^2.
- The isovector anomalous magnetic moment is kappa_v = 3.71.
- The LEC b_1 is fixed from neutral pion photoproduction at threshold as b_1 = 12.0.
- The nucleon-delta mass splitting is Delta = M_Delta - M_N, with m_mu/(M_Delta - M_N) ~ 3 m_mu/M_N.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral effective field theory calculations of RMC by Meissner (1998) and Ando and Min (1999), which only included pion and nucleon degrees of freedom and could not reproduce the TRIUMF photon spectrum. The authors show that explicit Delta(1232) effects are small (a few percent), consistent with earlier phenomenological findings by Beder and Fearing (1980s), and that the discrepancy with the TRIUMF result remains unexplained. The analysis enables future work to focus on non-strong physics, such as the distribution of spin states in muonic atoms, and provides a framework for extracting the pseudoscalar form factor GP from upcoming experiments at MAMI-B.
