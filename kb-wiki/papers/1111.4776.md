# Charmonium Production and Corona Effect

> We study the centrality dependence to be expected if only charmonium production in the corona survives in high energy nuclear collisions, with full suppression in the hot, deconfined core. To eliminate cold nuclear matter effects as far as possible, we consider the ratio of charmonium to open charm production. The centrality dependence of this ratio is found to follow a universal geometric form, applicable to both RHIC and LHC in collisions at central and forward rapidities.

# Charmonium Production and Corona Effect

## Key claim
If the fraction of charmonia surviving in nuclear collisions is essentially independent of collision energy, then the survival probability at high energy density appears to be a geometric effect due to charmonium production only in the corona (rim) region of the collision, where the density is too low for dissociation of primary charmonium production. The authors argue that the centrality dependence of the ratio of charmonium to open charm production follows a universal geometric form applicable to both RHIC and LHC, but note that this prediction depends on the assumption that the central core is hot enough to dissolve all charmonium bound states.

## Method
The authors define the corona as the region in the transverse plane where the density of wounded nucleons or collision density has decreased by a factor of two or more relative to the central value at zero impact parameter. They calculate the ratio of wounded nucleons in the corona to the total number of wounded nucleons (R_w) and the ratio of collisions in the corona to the total number of collisions (R_c) as functions of centrality (N_part/N_max) for Au+Au collisions, using the Glauber model with nucleon density profiles projected onto the transverse plane.

## Key numbers
- For central Au+Au collisions with σ_inel^NN = 42 mb (RHIC at √s_NN = 200 GeV): n_w = n_w(R=0)/2 = 2.16 fm^{-2} at R = 5.4 fm; n_c = n_c(R=0)/2 = 9.6 fm^{-2} at R = 4.4 fm.
- For central Au+Au collisions with σ_inel^NN = 81 mb (LHC): n_c = n_c(0)/2 = 18.9 fm^{-2}.
- Typical energy densities quoted: 5-6 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC, 2-3 GeV/fm^3 at SPS, at least 8-10 GeV/fm^3 at LHC.
- The corona fraction increases to unity only for very peripheral collisions roughly corresponding to b > 1.2R.

## Context
This work extends the corona effect concept previously discussed for various observables by Stock, Becattini, Bozek, Hohne, Werner, Andronic, and Aichelin (various years), and its role in heavy quark production considered by Andronic (year not specified). The paper contrasts the corona scenario with two competing approaches: sequential suppression (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Karsch, Mehr, and Satz, 1988; Karsch and Satz, 1991; Kharzeev, Karsch, and Satz, 1991) and statistical recombination (Braun-Munzinger, Thews, Rapp, various years). The authors propose that measuring the ratio of charmonium to open charm production, as suggested by Sridhar (year not specified), can cancel cold nuclear matter effects and test whether the corona effect is the dominant mechanism. This prediction enables experimental discrimination between the corona scenario and recombination models, particularly through transverse momentum distributions.
