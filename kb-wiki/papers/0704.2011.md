# Self consistency in hadron physics

> In this talk we discuss at hand of two examples the crucial role played by self consistency in hadron physics. The first example concerns the quark-mass dependence of the baryon octet and decuplet masses. It is shown that within a self consistent one-loop approach based on the chiral Lagrangian the 'mysterious' quark-mass dependence of the $Ξ$ mass predicted by the MILC collaboration may be recovered in terms of a discontinuous chiral extrapolation. This is a consequence of self consistency imposed on the partial summation, i.e. the masses used in the loop functions are identical to those obtained from the baryon self energies. In the second example we discuss recent studies on the properties of D mesons in cold nuclear matter as they are predicted by coupled-channel dynamics. Here a self consistent many-body approach reveals the close interlink of the properties of D meson and open-charm baryon resonances in nuclear matter. The relevance of exotic baryon resonances for the spectral distortion of the $D_s^\pm$ in nuclear matter is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that imposing self consistency in a partial-summation approach to chiral perturbation theory can produce a discontinuous dependence of baryon masses on the pion mass, which may explain the "mysterious" quark-mass dependence of the Xi mass observed by the MILC collaboration, rather than requiring new physics beyond the chiral Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors evaluate baryon octet and decuplet self energies to chiral order Q^3 using a chiral Lagrangian with meson-baryon vertices, then impose self consistency by requiring that the masses used in the loop functions are identical to those obtained from the baryon self energies. For the D meson study, they model D-meson nucleon scattering amplitudes via t-channel vector meson exchange with couplings from chiral and large-N_c properties of QCD, then apply a self consistent many-body formalism to compute in-medium spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma_piN = 54 ± 2 MeV (from residual scale dependence across infrared scales 350-550 MeV)
- D^-N scattering lengths: a^{(I=0)}_{D^-N} ≈ -0.16 fm, a^{(I=1)}_{D^-N} ≈ -0.26 fm
- D_- meson mass shift at saturation density (0.17 fm^{-3}): 18 MeV from self consistent result, compared to 17 MeV from low-density theorem
- D_- meson mass shift at twice saturation density (0.34 fm^{-3}): 38 MeV
- D_+ meson main mode pushed up by about 32 MeV at saturation density relative to free-space meson
- D^-_s main mode pushed up by less than 10 MeV at nuclear saturation density
- SU(3) limit values used: M_{[8]} ≈ 1575 MeV, M_{[10]} ≈ 1710 MeV at m_pi ≈ 690 MeV

## Context

This work extends the self consistent approach previously applied to antikaon-nucleon dynamics (Lutz and Korpa, 2002) to two new domains: chiral extrapolation of baryon masses and D mesons in nuclear matter. For baryon masses, it provides an alternative interpretation of MILC collaboration lattice data (2001, 2004) that avoids the need for higher-order counter terms. For D mesons, it contradicts the earlier mean-field study by Tolos, Schaffner, and Mishra (2004), which overestimated charm-exchange channels and did not predict the isospin one resonance Sigma_c(2620). The work enables future experimental programs to search for predicted exotic baryon resonances with charm content, particularly the narrow isospin one resonance at 2.62 GeV and the exotic s-wave state in the (D^-_s N, anti-D Lambda, anti-D Sigma) system with binding of about 190 MeV.
