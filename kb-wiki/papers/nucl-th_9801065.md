# Double $Λ$ Hypernuclei and the Nuclear Medium Effective $ΛΛ$ Interaction

> We fit the $^1 S_0 ΛΛ$ interaction in the nuclear medium to the masses of the experimentally known double-$Λ$ hypernuclei: $^{\phantom{6}6}_{ΛΛ}$He, $^{10}_{ΛΛ}$Be and $^{13}_{ΛΛ}$B. We derive this effective interaction from OBE Jülich $ΛΛ$-type potentials and using both Hartree-Fock and variational approaches. We find that the inclusion of $ΛΛ$ correlations in the variational scheme leads to significant differences and a better understanding of the dynamical features of the system. We investigate the sensitivity of the binding energies and the mesonic decay widths of the above double-$Λ$ hypernuclei to the $ωΛΛ$ coupling and the form factor at the $σΛΛ$ vertex. We also use this effective interaction to predict binding energies and pionic decay widths of heavier double-$Λ$ hypernuclei, not discovered yet. Finally, we discard the existence of $^1 S_0$ $ΛΛ$ bound states provided the $ΛΛ-ΞN$ coupling can be neglected.

# Double $\Lambda$ Hypernuclei and the Nuclear Medium Effective $\Lambda\Lambda$ Interaction

## Key claim
The $^1S_0$ $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction in the nuclear medium is fitted to the masses of three experimentally known double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei ($^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, $^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be, and $^{13}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$B) using Jülich-type one-boson-exchange potentials, but the resulting effective interaction parameters may effectively include some contributions from the $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ coupled channel, which is neglected in the model; the existence of $^1S_0$ $\Lambda\Lambda$ bound states is discarded provided the $\Lambda\Lambda-\Xi N$ coupling can be neglected.

## Method
The authors model double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei as two interacting $\Lambda$ hyperons moving in the mean-field potential created by the nuclear core, solving the Schrödinger equation with a $\sigma$-$\omega$ meson exchange potential for the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction derived from the Jülich OBE model. They employ both Hartree-Fock (HF) and variational (VAR) approaches, where the variational method uses Hylleraas-type wave functions expanded up to $N=10$ (161 terms) to include $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlations. The $\Lambda$-core potentials are adjusted to reproduce the binding energies of the corresponding single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei, and the free parameter $g_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda}$ is fitted to the three experimental $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ values for various choices of $g_{\omega\Lambda\Lambda}/g_{\omega NN}$ ratio and cutoff $\Lambda_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda}$.

## Key numbers
- Experimental binding energies used for fitting: $^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 10.9 \pm 0.8$ MeV, $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 4.7 \pm 1.0$ MeV; $^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 17.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV, $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 4.3 \pm 0.4$ MeV; $^{13}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$B $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 27.5 \pm 0.7$ MeV, $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 4.8 \pm 0.7$ MeV (Table I).
- $\sigma$ meson mass taken as 550 MeV; $\omega$ meson mass taken as 782.6 MeV; $\Lambda$ mass taken as 1115.6 MeV; nucleon mass taken as 938.926 MeV.
- Fixed $\omega\Lambda\Lambda$ coupling parameters from Jülich model $\hat{A}$: $g_{\omega\Lambda\Lambda}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 2.981$, $f_{\omega\Lambda\Lambda}/\sqrt{4\pi} = -2.796$, $\Lambda_{\omega\Lambda\Lambda} = 2$ GeV; $\sigma\Lambda\Lambda$ coupling $g_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 2.138$ with $\Lambda_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda} = 1$ GeV (Table II).
- The product $g_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda} \times (1 - m_\sigma^2/\Lambda_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda}^2)$ remains constant within approximately 10% across different cutoff choices.
- Nuclear core dynamical re-ordering effects estimated to be of order $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}/A$, approximately 0.5 MeV for beryllium and boron and around 1 MeV for helium.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei by Bodmer (1984, 1987) using variational approaches with $\alpha$-cluster decomposition, and by Yamamoto (1991, 1992) and Hiyama (1993) using Nijmegen model D $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions in $G$-matrix calculations. Unlike prior work, this is the first study using Jülich-type OBE $\Lambda\Lambda$ potentials for double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei, which had previously been successful for single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei (Muther 1989, Zhou 1996). The effective interaction obtained here is intended to be used in a companion paper (Caro 1998) to extract the free-space $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction, including its spin dependence, by studying nuclear medium modifications. The results also constrain the possible existence of the $H$ dibaryon, as double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei restrict the feasibility of a long-living $H$ dibaryon (Dover 1986).
