# Estimating $\hat{q}$ in Quenched Lattice SU(2) Gauge Theory

> The propagation of a virtual quark in a thermal medium is considered. The non-perturbative jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ is estimated in quark less SU(2) lattice gauge theory. The light like correlator which defines $\hat{q}$, defined in the regime where the jet has small virtuality compared to its energy, is analytically related to a series of local operators in the deep Euclidean region, where the jet's virtuality is of the same order as its energy. It is demonstrated that in this region, for temperatures in the range of T=400-600 MeV, and for jet energies above 20 GeV, the leading term in the series is dominant over the next-to-leading term and thus yields an estimate of the value of $\hat{q}$. In these proceedings we discuss the details of the numerical calculation.

# Estimating $\hat{q}$ in Quenched Lattice SU(2) Gauge Theory

## Key claim

The jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ can be estimated from lattice gauge theory by analytically continuing a light-like correlator (defined where jet virtuality is small compared to energy) to a series of local operators in the deep Euclidean region (where virtuality is comparable to energy), and the leading term dominates for jet energies above 20 GeV and temperatures of 400-600 MeV. The estimate relies on the condition that the next-to-leading term is less than 20% of the leading term in this temperature range.

## Method

The author relates the non-perturbative definition of $\hat{q}$ (the transverse momentum squared gained per unit length by a quark traversing a thermal medium) to a contour integral $I_1$ that can be evaluated either as a series of local operator products (involving rising powers of derivatives divided by the hard scale $q^-$) or as an integral over the branch cut containing $\hat{q}$. By subtracting the vacuum (temperature-independent) Bremsstrahlung contribution, the leading local operator product $\langle F^{+i} F^{+i} \rangle$ is evaluated on a $(4 \times n_t)^3 \times n_t$ lattice using the Wilson gauge action for SU(2) with 5000 heat bath sweeps per data point, with the lattice spacing set by both perturbative and non-perturbative renormalization group formulas.

## Key numbers

- The leading operator product $\langle F^{+i} F^{+i} \rangle$ is evaluated for temperatures in the range T = 400-600 MeV, scaled by $T^4$ to make it dimensionless.
- The next-to-leading operator product (with an extra temporal derivative and suppressed by $q^-$) is less than 20% of the leading term for temperatures below T = 600 MeV, with $q^-$ chosen as 20 GeV.
- The resulting virtuality-averaged transport coefficient for an SU(2) quark in an SU(2) plasma is $\bar{\hat{q}} = 0.186$ GeV$^2$/fm at T = 400 MeV.
- Naively scaling to SU(3) with two light quark flavors yields $\bar{\hat{q}} = 1.3 - 3.3$ GeV$^2$/fm at T = 400 MeV.
- The lattice spacing is set using the two-loop perturbative RG formula with $\Lambda_L = 5.3$ MeV, and $n_t$ is varied from 3 to 6.

## Context

This work extends the Higher-Twist (HT) approach of Wang (2001) and Baier et al. (1996) by reformulating the scheme to allow evaluation of transport coefficients in lattice gauge theory at finite temperature, as described in Majumder (2012). The calculation is performed in quenched SU(2) (no dynamical quarks) using the Wilson gauge action following Engels (1980) and Creutz (1984), with a non-perturbative scale-setting method from Engels (1994). The results enable phenomenological estimates of $\hat{q}$ for heavy-ion collisions and provide a framework for future calculations in full QCD with dynamical quarks and SU(3) gauge groups.
