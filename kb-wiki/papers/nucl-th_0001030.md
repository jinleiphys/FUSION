# Effects of the secondary decays on the isotopic thermometers

> The sharp microcanonical multifragmentation model from [Al. H. Raduta and Ad. R. Raduta, Phys. Rev. C 55, 1344 (1997); Phys. Rev. C, in press] is employed for evaluating the nuclear caloric curve predictions of nine isotopic thermometers for three representative nuclei. Evaluations are performed for both primary decay and asymptotic stages. Effects of the secondary decays on the primary decay caloric curves are evidenced and discussed. In both cases a dispersive character of the isotopic caloric curves with increasing the source excitation energy is observed. A procedure of calibrating the isotopic thermometers on the microcanonical predictions for both primary decay and asymptotic stages is proposed. The resulting set of calibrating parameters for each thermometer is independent on the source size, on its excitation energy and, in the case of the primary decay, on the freeze-out radius assumption.

## Key claim

The sharp microcanonical multifragmentation model predicts that the dispersive character of isotopic caloric curves is strongly diminished after secondary decays, which can give the exaggerate impression that the uncorrected Albergo isotopic thermometers give quite close results and that finite-size effects are small. The calibrating parameters derived for each thermometer are independent of source size, excitation energy, and (for the primary decay stage) freeze-out radius assumption.

## Method

The sharp microcanonical multifragmentation model (Raduta and Raduta, 1997, 1999) was used to evaluate the nuclear caloric curve predictions of nine isotopic thermometers for three representative source nuclei: (70,32), (130,54), and (190,79). Calculations were performed for both the primary decay stage and the asymptotic (after secondary decay) stage, using a freeze-out radius parameter of 2.5 A^(1/3) fm. The calibrating factors f_T = T_micro/T_iso were fitted with linear expressions f_T(E_ex) = a E_ex + b, and the resulting parameters were tabulated for each thermometer.

## Key numbers

* Nine isotopic thermometers were evaluated: 6,7Li/3,4He, 7,8Li/3,4He, 8,9Li/3,4He, 12,13C/3,4He, 13,14C/3,4He, 11,12B/3,4He, 12,13B/3,4He, 1,2H/3,4He, and 2,3H/3,4He.
* For the primary decay stage, the calibrating parameter "a" (in nucleon x MeV^(-1)) ranges from -0.013010 (11,12B/3,4He) to 0.016886 (12,13C/3,4He), and "b" ranges from 0.880336 (11,12B/3,4He) to 1.328440 (6,7Li/3,4He).
* For the asymptotic stage, the calibrating parameter "a" ranges from -0.005079 (8,9Li/3,4He) to 0.043046 (7,8Li/3,4He), and "b" ranges from 1.081550 (8,9Li/3,4He) to 1.492390 (6,7Li/3,4He).
* The freeze-out radius was varied between 2.25 A^(1/3) fm and 2.75 A^(1/3) fm for the (70,32) source to test parameter independence.

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical calibration of isotopic thermometers described in Raduta and Raduta (1999), which only corrected for microcanonical effects, by now including a second correction for secondary decays using an improved version of the sharp microcanonical model (Raduta and Raduta, in press). The study addresses discrepancies between experimental caloric curves from the ALADIN collaboration (Pochodzalla et al., 1995; Xi et al., 1997; Trautmann et al., 1998), INDRA (Ma et al., 1997), and EOS (Hauger et al., 1996), which arise from different reactions and measurement precision. The resulting calibration parameters, being independent of source size and excitation energy, enable direct application to experimental isotopic yields for extracting the nuclear caloric curve.
