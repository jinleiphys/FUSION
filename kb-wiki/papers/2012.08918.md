# Nucleon Structure and Strong Interactions in Dark Matter Capture in Neutron Stars

> We outline two important effects that are missing from most evaluations of the dark matter capture rate in neutron stars. As dark matter scattering with nucleons in the star involves large momentum transfer, nucleon structure must be taken into account via a momentum dependence of the hadronic form factors. In addition, due to the high density of neutron star matter, we should account for nucleon interactions rather than modeling the nucleons as an ideal Fermi gas. Properly incorporating these effects is found to suppress the dark matter capture rate by up to four orders of magnitude for the heaviest stars.

## Key claim

The dark matter capture rate in neutron stars is suppressed by up to three orders of magnitude for the heaviest stars when two previously neglected effects are included: momentum-dependent hadronic form factors (because momentum transfer is large) and nucleon effective masses (because the nucleons are strongly interacting, not an ideal Fermi gas).

## Method

The authors computed the dark matter capture rate in neutron stars using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) equation of state, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain radial profiles. They replaced the free Fermi gas approximation with an interacting nucleon framework where the neutron mass becomes a radially dependent effective mass, and they introduced momentum-dependent nucleon form factors parameterized as \(c_n^I(t) = c_n^I / (1 - t/Q_0^2)^2\) with \(Q_0 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\). The capture rate was evaluated for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector effective field theory operators for neutron stars of masses \(1.0 M_\odot\), \(1.5 M_\odot\), and \(1.9 M_\odot\).

## Key numbers

* For a \(1.5 M_\odot\) neutron star with the pseudoscalar operator, including momentum-dependent form factors in the free Fermi gas approximation lowers the capture rate by more than one order of magnitude.
* For the heaviest neutron star considered (\(1.9 M_\odot\)), the form factor correction alone reduces the capture rate by more than two orders of magnitude for the pseudoscalar operator.
* Nucleon interactions (effective masses) reduce the capture rate by up to approximately 1 order of magnitude for a \(1.5 M_\odot\) neutron star when \(m_\chi \gtrsim m_n\).
* The combined suppression from both effects reaches up to 3 orders of magnitude for the pseudoscalar operator and large neutron star mass.
* Varying the form factor scale from \(Q_0 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\) to \(Q_0 = 0.9\ \mathrm{GeV}\) changes the capture rate by a factor of 1.2–1.4 depending on the operator.
* Differences between the QMC equation of state and the BSk24 Skyrme-type equation of state give capture rate differences within 2–10% for a \(1.5 M_\odot\) neutron star and within 10–30% for a \(1.0 M_\odot\) neutron star.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of dark matter capture in neutron stars that treated nucleons as an ideal Fermi gas and evaluated form factors at zero momentum transfer, as done in direct detection experiments. Earlier work by Garani (2018) introduced a correction factor \(\zeta(r)\) to retain the free Fermi gas approximation while using an interacting equation of state, but the present paper replaces that with a more consistent effective-mass treatment. The results enable more accurate predictions for neutron star heating from dark matter annihilation (Baryakhtar, 2017) and for black hole formation from non-annihilating dark matter (Kouvaris, 2010; McDermott, 2011; Bell, 2013), and they imply that multi-scattering effects will be relevant for lower dark matter masses than previously thought.
