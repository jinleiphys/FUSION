# Associated Particle Distributions in Jets Produced in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the pion distributions in jets produced in heavy-ion collisions in association with trigger particles restricted to definite momentum ranges. We determine the centrality dependence of the associated particle distributions for both d+Au and Au+Au collisions in the recombination model. The central-to-peripheral ratios show significant dependence on centrality for Au+Au collisions, but negligible dependence for d+Au collisions. The results are in agreement with the qualitative features of the data.

# Associated Particle Distributions in Jets Produced in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of associated particle distributions (APDs) in jets, calculated as the ratio of integrals over a finite trigger momentum window rather than the integral of a ratio, shows significant dependence on centrality for Au+Au collisions but negligible dependence for d+Au collisions, in agreement with qualitative features of STAR and PHENIX data.

## Method

The authors calculate the associated particle distribution for pion pairs (π⁺π⁺) using the recombination model, computing the ratio of integrals over trigger momentum windows (4-6 GeV/c and 6-8 GeV/c) as defined in Eq. (2). The calculation uses hard parton distributions proportional to the number of binary collisions, thermal parton distributions parametrized for each centrality, and shower parton distributions, with thermal-thermal recombination omitted as the study focuses on jet particles.

## Key numbers

- Trigger momentum windows used: 4-6 GeV/c and 6-8 GeV/c, corresponding to STAR trigger windows
- For Au+Au collisions at p_T ≈ 1 GeV/c, the central-to-peripheral ratio is about 3, indicating strong enhancement from thermal partons in central collisions
- The calculated APDs decrease by three orders of magnitude over the intermediate p_T region
- Results for Au+Au central-to-peripheral ratio agree with STAR data for p_T < 3 GeV/c
- Calculations are performed only for associated particle p_2 > 1 GeV/c, as the formalism is not reliable below that threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier recombination model study of dihadron correlations (Hwa and Yang, 2004) by calculating the experimentally relevant ratio-of-integrals form of the associated particle distribution rather than the integral-of-ratio form. The results for Au+Au collisions agree with STAR data (Adams et al., 2004) and support the recombination model over fragmentation-based approaches for hadronization in the intermediate p_T region. For d+Au collisions, the results agree with preliminary PHENIX data (Naglis, 2004) showing approximate centrality independence, which the model attributes to the minor role of thermal-shower recombination in that system.
