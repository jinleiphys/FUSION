# Predictions on three-particle azimuthal correlations in proton-proton collisions

> The ridge signal, which is long-ranged in rapidity, in the di-hadron correlations in high-multiplicity p-p and p-A collisions opened up a whole new research area in high-energy QCD. Although the ridge had been observed in A-A collisions and interpreted as a result of the radial flow of quark-gluon plasma, it had not appeared until recently in the data of small collision systems such as p-p and p-A, nor had it been predicted theoretically or seen in the event generators. There are two competing approaches that attempt to explain the systematics of the di-hadron ridge signal; hydrodynamics and gluon saturation physics (color glass condensate/glasma). In this work, we present predictions for the transverse momentum and rapidity dependence of the three-particle correlation function within the gluon saturation physics. Tri-hadron correlations can be measured, and the data can possibly rule out one of the two alternative approaches.

## Key claim

The three-particle correlation function within the gluon saturation/glasma framework shows a power-law decrease with increasing rapidity gap between the gluons, in contrast to the approximately constant rapidity dependence observed in two-particle correlations; this difference could allow experimental data to rule out either the gluon saturation or the hydrodynamic explanation for the ridge signal.

## Method

The author calculated the three-gluon correlation function \(C_{3}\) using the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) unintegrated gluon distribution functions, which incorporate gluon saturation. The correlation function was evaluated at the peak azimuthal configuration \(\{\Delta\phi_{qp},\Delta\phi_{lp}\}=\{0,0\}\) for proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\,\text{TeV}\), and its dependence on transverse momentum and rapidity was studied. The gluonic correlation function was computed without convolution with fragmentation functions, which is left for future work.

## Key numbers

- The three-gluon correlation function \(C_{3}\) was calculated for p-p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\,\text{TeV}\).
- The density plot of \(C_{3}\) was shown at transverse momenta \(p_{\perp}=q_{\perp}=l_{\perp}=2\,\text{GeV}\) and rapidities \(y_{p}=y_{q}=y_{l}=0\).
- The correlation function peaks at azimuthal configurations \(\{\Delta\phi_{qp},\Delta\phi_{lp}\}\approx\{\{0,0\},\{0,\pi\},\{\pi,0\},\{\pi,\pi\}\}\).
- The three-gluon correlations are large at low transverse momentum, consistent with the semi-hard gluon saturation scale \(Q_{s}\sim1\,\text{GeV}\).
- The correlation strength decreases approximately as a power-law with increasing rapidity gap between the gluons.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on three-hadron correlations (Ozonder 2014, 2016, 2017) as a way to distinguish between the gluon saturation/glasma framework and the hydrodynamic explanation for the ridge signal observed in high-multiplicity p-p and p-Pb collisions. The two-particle ridge correlations were successfully explained by the glasma model (Dusling 2009, 2012, 2013; Dumitru 2010; Venugopalan 2013), but hydrodynamic approaches (Bozek 2011, 2012, 2013; Werner 2013; Kozlov 2014; Bzdak 2014) also reproduce the ridge without gluon saturation. The predictions for three-particle correlations can be compared with future experimental data to potentially rule out one of the two competing mechanisms.
