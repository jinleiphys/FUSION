# Influence of in-medium mass width on Hanbury Brown-Twiss correlation strength

> The interactions of particles with medium may lead to a nonzero in-medium mass width of the particles and cause them to have different masses in the medium. In this letter, we investigate the influence of the in-medium mass width on the strength of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. It is found that the strength decreases with in-medium mass width of identical bosons. This influence are more significant for the boson with heavier mass and decreases with increasing particle momentum.

## Key claim

The strength of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlation for identical bosons decreases with increasing in-medium mass width of the bosons, with the effect being more significant for heavier bosons and at smaller particle momenta; the abstract does not specify that the strength reduction is about 10% on average for phi mesons at RHIC and LHC energies, while for D0 mesons the suppression is "very significant."

## Method

The authors used a quantum path-integral formalism for the probability amplitude of bosons produced in a medium, where the bosons acquire an in-medium mass distribution described by a Breit-Wigner form with a width \(\Gamma\) and a mass shift \(\Delta m'\). They derived the two-particle correlation function for a chaotic Gaussian source with lifetime \(\tau = 10\) fm/\(c\) and radius \(R\), assuming coordinate-independent production amplitude and simultaneous freeze-out. The HBT correlation strength \(I(\mathbf{k},\mathbf{k})\) was then evaluated as a function of particle momentum for \(\phi\) and \(D^0\) mesons.

## Key numbers

- For \(\phi\) mesons at RHIC and LHC, the in-medium mass width is about several MeV, leading to an average reduction of the HBT correlation strength by about 10%.
- For \(D^0\) mesons at RHIC and LHC, the in-medium mass width may reach several tens of MeV, causing a "very significant" suppression of the HBT correlation strength.
- The source lifetime used in the calculation is \(\tau = 10\) fm/\(c\).
- At zero momentum (\(p=0\)), the HBT correlation strength depends only on the width \(\Gamma\), not on the mass shift \(\Delta m'\).

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium mass modifications of bosons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, previously investigated for squeezed back-to-back correlations of boson-antiboson pairs (Asakawa and Csorgo, 1996; Padula, 2006; Zhang, 2015; and others). It addresses the effect of a nonzero in-medium mass width on the standard HBT correlation strength, which is typically parameterized by a \(\lambda\)-parameter in data analyses (Gyulassy, 1979; Wong, 1999; Wiedemann and Heinz, 1999; Csorgo, 2002; Lisa, 2005). The results enable more precise interpretation of HBT measurements for \(\phi\) and \(D^0\) mesons at RHIC and LHC, where in-medium mass widths are non-negligible.
