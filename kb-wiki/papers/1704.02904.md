# Beam energy scan theory: Status and open questions

> The goal of heavy ion reactions at low beam energies is to explore the QCD phase diagram at high net baryon chemical potential. To relate experimental observations with a first order phase transition or a critical endpoint, dynamical approaches for the theoretical description have to be developed. In this summary of the corresponding plenary talk, the status of the dynamical modeling including the most recent advances is presented. The remaining challenges are highlighted and promising experimental measurements are pointed out.

## Key claim

Dynamical modeling of heavy ion collisions at intermediate beam energies (between approximately 3 and 39 GeV center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair) is necessary to connect experimental observables to the QCD phase diagram, but no single approach currently provides a quantitative description of all signals for a first order phase transition or critical endpoint.

## Method

The paper summarizes the plenary talk on beam energy scan theory, reviewing recent advances in hybrid approaches that combine viscous hydrodynamics with hadronic transport, as well as purely hadronic transport models. It discusses initial condition modeling, equation of state inputs from lattice QCD and functional methods, extraction of transport coefficients like shear and bulk viscosity, and non-equilibrium propagation of fluctuations around phase transitions and critical points using chiral fluid dynamics and stochastic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- At high beam energies (above sqrt(s_NN) = 39 GeV), hybrid approaches based on viscous hydrodynamics and hadronic transport are accepted as the standard model.
- At very low beam energies (below sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV), hadronic transport approaches including resonance dynamics and nuclear potentials are successful.
- The isotropization time has been extracted from coarse-grained UrQMD transport calculations for beam energies ranging from E_lab = 2 to 160 A GeV.
- Lattice QCD calculations predict a cross-over transition up to mu_B/T less than 2.2.
- The hadronic rescattering diminishes the amount of correlations observable in the final state by another 50 percent (Steinheimer 2016).

## Context

This review extends the discussion of dynamical modeling for the RHIC beam energy scan program, building on prior work by Hirano (2012) and Petersen (2014) on hybrid approaches, and by Karpenko (2015) on extraction of shear viscosity. It highlights the need to interpolate between high-energy hybrid models and low-energy hadronic transport, and points to specific open questions: initial baryon stopping mechanisms, the interplay of transport and hydrodynamics, and propagation of fluctuations through the Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure. The paper enables future work by identifying promising experimental measurements and theoretical developments needed to establish definitive links between observables and structures in the QCD phase diagram.
