# Three-body Unitarity with Isobars Revisited

> The particle exchange model of hadron interactions can be used to describe three-body scattering under the isobar assumption. In this study we start from the 3->3 scattering amplitude for spinless particles, which contains an isobar-spectator scattering amplitude. Using a Bethe-Salpeter Ansatz for the latter, we derive a relativistic three-dimensional scattering equation that manifestly fulfills three-body unitarity and two-body unitarity for the sub-amplitudes. This property holds for energies above breakup and also in the presence of resonances in the sub-amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors derive a relativistic three-dimensional scattering equation for three-body scattering under the isobar assumption that manifestly fulfills three-body unitarity for energies above breakup and in the presence of resonances in the sub-amplitudes, correcting a proof in the original Aaron-Amado-Young (AAY) formalism that was only valid for bound-state particle scattering below the three-particle threshold.

## Method

Starting from the 3-to-3 scattering amplitude for spinless identical particles of mass m, the authors formulate the amplitude using an isobar-spectator decomposition where the isobar propagator S and decay vertex v parameterize the two-body subchannel dynamics. They then make a Bethe-Salpeter Ansatz for the isobar-spectator scattering amplitude T and expand both the unitarity relation and the Bethe-Salpeter expansion term-by-term, matching the resulting topologies to determine the driving term B and the isobar propagator S from their discontinuity relations.

## Key numbers

- The isobar propagator discontinuity relation yields Disc D(sigma(k)) = (i/8pi) * (K_cm / sqrt(sigma(k))) * lambda^2 for an S-wave isobar with constant vertex v = lambda, where K_cm = sqrt(sigma(k)/4 - m^2)
- The driving term B is obtained via an unsubtracted dispersion relation as B = -v(Q,q)v(Q,p) / [2 sqrt(m^2 + Q^2) (E_Q - sqrt(m^2 + Q^2) + i epsilon)], where Q = P - p - q is the momentum transfer
- The isobar propagator is reconstructed using a twice-subtracted dispersion relation: D(sigma(k)) = A + B*sigma(k) + (sigma(k)^2/pi) * integral from 4m^2 to infinity of d sigma' * Im D(sigma') / [sigma'^2 (sigma' - sigma(k) - i epsilon)]

## Context

This work extends the original formalism of Aaron, Amado, and Young (AAY) (1969) which claimed three-body unitarity but whose proof only held for bound-state particle scattering below the breakup energy sqrt(s) < 3m. The authors identify that AAY considered only the amputated isobar-spectator scattering amplitude T instead of the full 3-to-3 amplitude, and that AAY had to discard contributions from terms (2), (3a), and (3b) in the Bethe-Salpeter expansion that only vanish below breakup. The derived formalism enables consistent treatment of three-body scattering in analyses of hadron resonances studied at COMPASS, GlueX, CLAS12, LHCb, BESIII, Belle, and BaBar, and provides the analytical amplitudes needed for extracting three-particle scattering from lattice QCD calculations.
