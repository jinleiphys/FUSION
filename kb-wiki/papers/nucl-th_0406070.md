# Relativistic description of the np to eta d reaction near threshold

> Relativistic effects in a three-body calculation of the $np\toηd$ process are considered. Relativistic effects on the range and strength of the pion exchange contribution to the reaction mechanism may be large, but boost effects of the two-body interactions are negligible. The relativistic calculation confirms previous non-relativistic results, showing that the shape of the cross section near threshold is essentially determined by the $ηd$ final-state interaction alone. As for the region away from threshold, the relativistic pion exchange contribution is seen to dominate the other mechanisms of the reaction. It turns out that, within the relativistic reaction model, the $np\toηd$ experimental data excludes large values for the real part of the $ηN$ scattering length.

# Relativistic description of the np to eta d reaction near threshold

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in the three-body calculation of the np→ηd process confirm that the shape of the cross section near threshold is essentially determined by the ηd final-state interaction alone, while away from threshold the relativistic pion exchange contribution dominates the other reaction mechanisms. Within the relativistic reaction model, the experimental data exclude large values for the real part of the ηN scattering length, narrowing the uncertainty region to between 0.42 fm and 0.72 fm.

## Method

The authors constructed covariant meson-nucleon amplitudes based on data analyses of the coupled πN→ηN, ηN→ηN, and γN→ηN reactions, using a variable-mass isobar model with a single S11 resonance. These amplitudes were boosted (including their Dirac spin structure) and embedded in a meson-exchange box diagram production mechanism, while the ηd final-state distortion was calculated using a relativistic version of three-body Faddeev equations that incorporates relativistic kinematics and boosts of the two-body interactions. The two-body ηN-πN-σN coupled subsystem was described by rank-one separable potentials, and the NN interaction was adjusted from the PEST separable model by multiplying the original potential by a factor of 0.78805 to place the deuteron pole correctly.

## Key numbers

- The ηN scattering length is constrained to lie between 0.42 fm and 0.72 fm by the relativistic calculation.
- The σNN coupling constant was taken as gσ²/4π = 8.
- The ηNN coupling constant was given the value gη²/4π = 1.
- The monopole form factor cutoff for meson-nucleon vertices was Λ = 1800 MeV/c.
- The PEST NN potential was multiplied by a factor of 0.78805 to adjust the deuteron pole position in the relativistic framework.
- The σ meson mass was taken as mσ = 2mπ to simulate the ππN inelasticity.

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic calculations by Garcilazo and Pena (2003) and others (GAR1, GAR2, GATE) that established the importance of the three-body final-state interaction for the threshold cross-section shape. The relativistic treatment confirms those findings while showing that relativistic effects on the pion exchange contribution are significant away from threshold, unlike the boost effects of two-body interactions which are negligible at the energies involved (∼100 MeV). The study narrows the large uncertainty in the ηN scattering length that existed from different data analyses (Batinic 1995, Green 1996, Julich group), and enables more precise phenomenological constraints on the ηN interaction. The results also agree with the conclusion of Julich (2002) and Nakayama (2002) that pion exchange is the dominant production mechanism.
