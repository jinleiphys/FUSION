# Polarization-dependent mass modifications of $Ď•$ meson with finite momentum in nuclear matter

> We investigate the in-medium properties of the $Ď•$ meson with finite momentum, going beyond the commonly studied case at rest. In a nuclear medium, Lorentz invariance is broken, leading to distinct longitudinal and transverse polarization modes that evolve differently with density and momentum. Within an effective Lagrangian approach, we calculate the polarization-dependent mass shifts and width modifications of the $Ď•$ meson arising from kaon loops and mean-field interactions. The divergent loop integrals are regulated using two different schemes: a covariant form factor and dimensional regularization. Our results show that the mass shift of the transverse polarization is independent of the $Ď•$-meson momentum, whereas that of the longitudinal polarization decreases quadratically with momentum. This difference originates from the coupling of the longitudinal mode to the vector mean field and derivative-type interactions in the self-energy. These effects have direct implications for experimental observables, especially for upcoming measurements at J-PARC, and provide a new prediction for experiments studying hadron dynamics in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a $\phi$ meson moving with finite momentum in nuclear matter, the mass shift of the longitudinal polarization mode decreases quadratically with momentum, while the transverse polarization mode mass shift remains momentum-independent; this polarization-dependent splitting arises from the coupling of the longitudinal mode to the vector mean field and derivative-type interactions in the self-energy, and the effect is predicted to be observable in upcoming J-PARC experiments.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium $\phi$ meson self-energy from kaon loops and a contact four-point interaction within an effective Lagrangian approach, using in-medium kaon masses and energies obtained from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The divergent loop integrals are regulated using two independent Lorentz-invariant schemes: a covariant multipole form factor and dimensional regularization, to avoid artificial Lorentz-breaking effects when extending the calculation to finite momentum. The in-medium mass and width are then solved self-consistently from the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy for each polarization mode separately.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum $\phi$ meson mass: $m_\phi^{\mathrm{expt.}} = 1019.461\ \mathrm{MeV}$; vacuum kaon mass: $m_K^{\mathrm{expt.}} = 493.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$; $\phi KK$ coupling constant: $g_\phi = 4.517$, fixed by reproducing the vacuum decay width $\Gamma_\phi^{\mathrm{expt.}} = 4.249\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$; binding energy at saturation: $15.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$; quark-meson coupling constants: $(g_\sigma^q, g_\omega^q) = (5.69, 2.72)$.
- Imaginary part of the transverse self-energy: $\mathrm{Im}~\Pi^{T}_\mathrm{total}(p) = - (g_\phi^2 / 24\pi) m_\phi^{*2} \beta^3$, with $\beta = \sqrt{1 - 4m_K^{*2} / m_\phi^{*2}}$; the longitudinal imaginary part contains an additional term proportional to $V_\omega^2 |\bm{p}|^2 / m_\phi^{*4}$.
- In-medium decay width for transverse mode: $\Gamma^{*T}_\phi = (g_\phi^2 / 24\pi\gamma) m_\phi^{*} \beta^3$; longitudinal mode width: $\Gamma^{*L}_\phi = (g_\phi^2 / 24\pi\gamma) m_\phi^{*} \beta^3 \left(1 + 12 V_\omega^2 |\bm{p}|^2 / \beta^2 m_\phi^{*4}\right)$, where $\gamma = E_\phi^* / m_\phi^*$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $\phi$ meson at rest in nuclear matter (Ko 1992, Cobos-Martinez 2017, Mondal 2025) by systematically incorporating finite-momentum and polarization-dependent effects, which are relevant for realistic experiments where $\phi$ mesons are produced with nonzero momentum. The results are consistent with QCD sum rule predictions for the longitudinal mode (Kim 2019) but show a qualitatively different trend for the transverse mode, and the predicted polarization-dependent mass splitting can be tested via angular correlations in the $\phi \to K\bar{K}$ decay channel at the J-PARC E88/Sa$\phi$re experiment (Sako 2024).
