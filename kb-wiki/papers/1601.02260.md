# Hydrodynamic fluctuations and dissipation in an integrated dynamical model

> We develop a new integrated dynamical model to investigate the effects of the hydrodynamic fluctuations on observables in high-energy nuclear collisions. We implement hydrodynamic fluctuations in a fully 3-D dynamical model consisting of the hydrodynamic initialization models of the Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model, causal dissipative hydrodynamics and the subsequent hadronic cascades. By analyzing the hadron distributions obtained by massive event-by-event simulations with both of hydrodynamic fluctuations and initial-state fluctuations, we discuss the effects of hydrodynamic fluctuations on the flow harmonics, $v_n$ and their fluctuations.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations have a non-negligible effect on flow harmonics and their fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the centrality class and the specific observable; in central collisions they increase flow coefficients, while in non-central collisions they primarily broaden the event-by-event distribution of elliptic flow without changing the average.

## Method

The authors implemented hydrodynamic fluctuations as a Gaussian white noise term in the constitutive equation for shear stress in second-order relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics, with autocorrelations determined by the fluctuation-dissipation relation. They performed event-by-event simulations using an integrated dynamical model combining Monte-Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi initial conditions, causal dissipative hydrodynamics with shear viscosity fixed at the KSS bound (eta/s = 1/4pi), and hadronic cascades via JAM. For each of the two hydrodynamic types (fluctuating and conventional viscous), they ran 10^5 hydrodynamic events with 100 hadronic cascade events per hydrodynamic event, totaling 10^7 cascades per type for minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity fixed at the KSS bound: eta/s = 1/4pi
- Relaxation time chosen as tau_pi = 3/(4pi T)
- Coarse-graining scales for hydrodynamic fluctuations: sigma_perp = 1 fm, sigma_eta = 1
- Switching temperature for hadronization: 155 MeV
- Total of 10^5 hydrodynamic events and 10^7 hadronic cascade events performed for each type of hydrodynamics
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the integrated dynamical model of Hirano (2012) by incorporating hydrodynamic fluctuations, which were previously identified as an additional source of event-by-event fluctuations beyond initial-state fluctuations (Kapusta, 2011). The results show that hydrodynamic fluctuations must be accounted for when extracting transport properties from flow harmonics, and the authors note that the current parameter set produces effects that are too large, requiring future scanning of the shear viscosity and cutoff scales, as well as renormalization of transport coefficients as discussed by Kovtun (2012).
