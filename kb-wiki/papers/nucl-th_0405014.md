# New Constraint of Clustering for AMD and Its Application to the Study of $2α$-$^{12}$C Structure of $^{20}$Ne

> A new constraint of clustering for the AMD calculation is proposed. This new constraint gives us large improvement in studying the cluster structure by AMD which sometimes meets difficulty in giving rise to some specific cluster configurations. The usefulness of this new constraint is verified by applying it to the the study of the third $K^π= 0^+$ band of $^{20}$Ne which has been discussed to have $2α$-$^{12}$C structure. This band has not been easy even to construct by AMD. We see that the AMD+GCM calculation by the use of the new constraint gives rise to the third $K^π= 0^+$ band which contains the $2α$-$^{12}$C structure as an important component.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new "d-constraint" for AMD calculations that constrains the distance between the centers of mass of pre-defined quasi-clusters, which allows the construction of cluster wave functions that are difficult or impossible to obtain using the standard quadrupole deformation (β) constraint alone. The claim is hedged by noting that the d-constraint does not guarantee the formation of the intended cluster structure, nucleons may rearrange into different configurations during energy variation.

## Method

The authors perform deformed-basis AMD calculations with the Gogny D1S effective force, applying either the standard β-constraint on quadrupole deformation or the new d-constraint on the distance between quasi-cluster centers of mass. For ²⁰Ne, they generate basis wave functions including mean-field-type, α-¹⁶O, and 2α-¹²C configurations, then perform GCM (generator coordinate method) calculations by superposing parity- and angular-momentum-projected states with different β and d values. The final wave functions are obtained by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation for the coefficients of the superposition.

## Key numbers

* The d-constraint was applied with constrained distances of d = 4.5 fm for α-¹⁶O configurations and d₁ = d₂ = 3.2, 3.3, and 3.4 fm for 2α-¹²C configurations in ²⁰Ne.
* The calculated 0₁⁺ state has total energy E = -160.6 MeV, r.m.s. radius R = 3.00 fm, quadrupole deformation β = 0.50, and harmonic oscillator quantum number N = 21.2.
* The calculated 0₃⁺ state (assigned to the observed Kπ = 0₃⁺ band) has E = -152.9 MeV, R = 3.22 fm, β = 0.69, and N = 24.8.
* The calculated 0₄⁺ state (assigned to the observed Kπ = 0₄⁺ band) has E = -150.6 MeV, R = 3.61 fm, β = 0.87, and N = 31.6.
* The 0₃⁺ state contains 51% α-¹⁶O component and 29% 2α-¹²C component (90% combined); the 0₄⁺ state contains 69% α-¹⁶O component and 14% 2α-¹²C component (92% combined).

## Context

This work extends AMD methodology by addressing a known limitation: the standard β-constraint surface fails to produce certain cluster configurations, such as the α-⁴⁰Ca structure in ⁴⁴Ti (reported by Taniguchi et al. 2004) and the third Kπ = 0⁺ band of ²⁰Ne. Previous attempts by Hartree-Fock calculations and AMD with β-constraint (Kimura 2004) could not show the existence of this band. The new d-constraint enables the study of cluster structures in medium-weight nuclei, particularly the coexistence of cluster and mean-field-type structures in sd and pf-shell nuclei, and the relation between superdeformation and clustering.
