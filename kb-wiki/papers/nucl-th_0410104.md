# Charmonium at LHC: Production, Propagation and Decay

> The productions of muon pairs from the decay of heavy quarkonia have been evaluated for different centrality of the nuclear collisions at LHC energies. The effects of the various comover scenarios on the survival probability of the heavy quarkonia have been considered. The effects of shadowing and comover suppressions on the dilepton spectra originating from the decays of J/ψis found to be substantial. The dilepton yield from the thermal J/ψhas also been estimated and found to be small.

# Charmonium at LHC: Production, Propagation and Decay

## Key claim

The dilepton yield from hard (prompt) J/ψ decays in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies is substantially suppressed by both nuclear shadowing and comover interactions, while the thermal J/ψ contribution is small; however, the quantitative suppression depends sensitively on the assumed fraction of hadrons from hard collisions (x) and the fraction of comoving hadrons (f_c), which are treated as free parameters rather than fixed predictions.

## Method

The authors compute J/ψ production in the colour evaporation model (CEM) using leading-order perturbative QCD for c-cbar pair production, with non-perturbative factors for resonance formation. They embed this in a Glauber model for nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies (√s = 6000 GeV for Pb-Pb), incorporating nuclear shadowing via EKS parametrization and CTEQ parton distribution functions. Three distinct comover suppression scenarios are implemented: Scenario I assumes Bjorken scaling with hadron density from row-on-row collisions; Scenario II uses a two-component model (soft from participants, hard from binary collisions) with centrality-dependent multiplicity; Scenario III assumes comover density proportional to participant number, with a modified version including a hard-collision component.

## Key numbers

- LHC Pb-Pb collision energy: √s = 6000 GeV
- Initial temperature for thermal J/ψ estimate: T_i = 300 MeV at proper time τ_i = 1 fm
- Invariant mass window for dilepton yield comparison: 3 GeV ≤ M ≤ 3.2 GeV
- Hadron rapidity density in nucleon-nucleon collisions parameterized as: dN_NN/dy = 2.5 − 0.25 ln(s) + 0.023 ln²(s)
- K factor of approximately 2.5 included to account for higher-order QCD processes
- ρ_R = 0.5 for J/ψ (fraction of c-cbar pairs forming J/ψ in CEM)
- Centrality cut of 0-10% used for hard production comparisons

## Context

This work extends the Matsui-Satz (1986) proposal of J/ψ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signal to LHC energies, building on earlier comover absorption models by Wong (1998) and Vogt (2000). It provides predictions for the ALICE dimuon spectrometer at LHC. The three comover scenarios are compared to show that the yield may lie between the most and least suppressive cases, enabling experimental discrimination of the dominant suppression mechanism at LHC energies. The paper notes that at RHIC energies shadowing effects are small, but at LHC they become substantial.
