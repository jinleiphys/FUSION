# Unraveling the nature of the novel $\mathbf{T_{cs}}$ and $\mathbf{T_{c\bar s}}$ tetraquark candidates

> Using proton-proton collisions at centre-of-mass energies $7$, $8$, and $13$ TeV, with a total integrated luminosity of $9\,\text{fb}^{-1}$, the LHCb collaboration has performed amplitude analyses of the $B^+\to D^+D^-K^+$, $B^+\to D^- D_s^+ π^+$ and $B^0\to \bar{D}^0 D_s^+ π^-$ decays, observing that new $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ resonances are required in order to explain the experimental data. These signals could be the first observation of tetraquark candidates that do not contain a heavy quark-antiquark pair; in fact, they consist of four different flavours of quarks, one of which is a doubly charged open-charm state. We present herein an analysis of the $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ states, which is an extension of our recently published study of similar $T_{cc}^+$ exotic candidates. Our theoretical framework is a constituent-quark-model-based coupled-channels calculation of $qq^\prime \bar s \bar c$ and $cq\bar s\bar q^{\prime}$ tetraquark sectors for $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ structures, respectively. We explore the nature, and pole position, of the singularities that appear in the scattering matrix with spin-parity quantum numbers: $J^P=0^\pm$, $1^\mp$, and $2^\pm$. The constituent quark model has been widely used in the heavy quark sector, and thus all model parameters are already constrained from previous works. This makes our predictions robust and parameter-free. We find many singularities in the solution of various scattering-matrix problems which are either virtual states or resonances, but not bound states. Some of them fit well with the experimental observations of the spin-parity, mass and width of $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ candidates, and thus tentative assignments are made; however, with caution, because the experimental Breit-Wigner parameters are related to the pole characteristics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free constituent quark model analysis of the newly observed $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ tetraquark candidates, finding that the singularities in the scattering matrix are either virtual states or resonances but not bound states; the authors make tentative assignments to experimental signals but caution that experimental Breit-Wigner parameters are related to pole characteristics and should not be directly compared.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model (CQM) with Goldstone-boson exchanges, one-gluon exchange, and a screened confinement potential, where all model parameters are already constrained from previous works. They perform coupled-channels calculations using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) to derive meson-meson interactions from the underlying quark dynamics, solving Lippmann-Schwinger equations to find poles of the $T$-matrix in the complex energy plane. The analysis covers spin-parity quantum numbers $J^P=0^\pm$, $1^\mp$, and $2^\pm$ for both the $qq^\prime \bar s \bar c$ sector ($T_{cs}$ states) and the $cq\bar s\bar q^{\prime}$ sector ($T_{c\bar s}$ states), with theoretical uncertainties estimated by varying the potential strength by $\pm10\%$.

- $T_{cs1}(2900)^0$ experimental Breit-Wigner mass: $M = (2904 \pm 5 \pm 1) \,\text{MeV/c}^2$, width: $\Gamma = (110 \pm 11 \pm 4) \,\text{MeV}$ (LHCb, $B^+\to D^+D^-K^+$ decays)
- $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^0$ experimental Breit-Wigner mass: $M = (2892 \pm 14 \pm 15) \,\text{MeV/c}^2$, width: $\Gamma = (119 \pm 26 \pm 13) \,\text{MeV}$ (LHCb, $B^0\to \bar{D}^0 D_s^+ \pi^-$ decays)
- $T_{c\bar s0}^a(2900)^{++}$ experimental Breit-Wigner mass: $M = (2921 \pm 17 \pm 20) \,\text{MeV/c}^2$, width: $\Gamma = (137 \pm 32 \pm 17) \,\text{MeV}$ (LHCb, $B^+\to D^- D_s^+ \pi^+$ decays)
- Tentative assignment for $T_{cs0}(2900)^0$: $J^P=0^+$ pole at $2901.9_{-0.8}^{+0.5}\,\text{MeV/c}^2$ with width $51_{-1}^{+0}\,\text{MeV}$ (second state in $0^+$ sector)
- Tentative assignment for $T_{cs1}(2900)^0$: $J^P=1^-$ pole at $2887.7_{-0.4}^{+0.3}\,\text{MeV/c}^2$ with width $189.5_{-0.6}^{+0.4}\,\text{MeV}$
- $T_{c\bar s}$ candidates: $J^P=0^+$ pole at $2902_{-2}^{+1}\,\text{MeV/c}^2$ with width $49.4_{-0.3}^{+0.2}\,\text{MeV}$ (tentative assignment for neutral state); $J^P=0^+$ pole at $2922 \pm 2\,\text{MeV/c}^2$ with width $287_{-7}^{+8}\,\text{MeV}$ (tentative assignment for doubly charged state)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously published analysis of $T_{cc}^+$ exotic candidates (Ortega et al., 2022) to the newly observed $T_{cs}$ and $T_{c\bar s}$ states reported by the LHCb collaboration in 2020 and 2022. The theoretical framework uses a constituent quark model (Vijande et al., 2004; Segovia et al., 2013) that has been widely applied to heavy quark systems, with all model parameters already constrained from previous studies of spectra, decays, and reactions. The results provide tentative assignments for the observed resonances and predict additional poles in the $J^P=1^+$ sector that could be detected in $\bar D^*K$ channels, while also noting that alternative interpretations exist in the literature using QCD sum rules (Chen et al., 2020; Agaev et al., 2020), effective field theories (Molina et al., 2010, 2020), and triangle singularity explanations (Liu et al., 2020; Burns et al., 2020).
