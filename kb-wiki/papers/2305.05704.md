# Planar degeneracy of the three-gluon vertex

> We present a detailed exploration of certain outstanding features of the transversely-projected three-gluon vertex, using the corresponding Schwinger-Dyson equation in conjunction with key results obtained from quenched lattice simulations. The main goal of this study is the scrutiny of the approximate property denominated ``planar degeneracy'', unveiled when the Bose symmetry of the vertex is properly exploited. The planar degeneracy leads to a particularly simple parametrization of the vertex, reducing its kinematic dependence to essentially a single variable. Our analysis, carried out in the absence of dynamical quarks, reveals that the planar degeneracy is particularly accurate for the description of the form factor associated with the classical tensor, for a wide array of arbitrary kinematic configurations. Instead, the remaining three form factors display considerable violations of this property. In addition, and in close connection with the previous point, we demonstrate the numerical dominance of the classical form factor over all others, except in the vicinity of the soft-gluon kinematics. The final upshot of these considerations is the emergence of a very compact description for the three-gluon vertex in general kinematics, which may simplify significantly nonperturbative applications involving this vertex.

## Key claim

The planar degeneracy property, where the transversely-projected three-gluon vertex's form factors depend almost exclusively on a single kinematic variable \(s^2 = \frac{1}{2}(q^2+r^2+p^2)\), is highly accurate for the classical form factor across general kinematics, but the remaining three form factors show considerable violations of this property; the classical form factor numerically dominates over all others except near the soft-gluon limit.

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) for the transversely-projected three-gluon vertex in quenched (no dynamical quarks) Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory, using lattice inputs for gluon and ghost propagators. They constructed a new tensorial basis where only the classical tensor survives in the soft-gluon limit, then substituted the compact approximation \(\overline{\Gamma}^{\alpha\mu\nu} \approx \overline{\Gamma}_0^{\alpha\mu\nu} L_{sg}(s^2)\) into the SDE to determine all four form factors for general kinematics without iterative procedures.

## Key numbers

* The largest deviation from planar degeneracy for the classical form factor is \(17.5\%\) at \(q^2 = r^2 = p^2 = 2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), dropping below \(10\%\) away from this point.
* The classical form factor and soft-gluon form factor \(L_{sg}(r^2)\) acquire their tree-level value (unity) at \(4.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (the renormalization point) and reduce by half at around \(1\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The ghost loop contribution to the infrared suppression becomes discernible only below \(0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\), with the swordfish diagrams being the main source of suppression above that scale.

## Context

This work extends the lattice discovery of planar degeneracy for bisectoral kinematics by Pinto-Gomez (2022) to general kinematic configurations using SDE methods, and refines the basis introduced in that lattice study to make the degeneracy exact in the soft-gluon limit. The compact vertex approximation \(\overline{\Gamma}^{\alpha\mu\nu} \approx \overline{\Gamma}_0^{\alpha\mu\nu} L_{sg}(s^2)\), first used by Williams (2015), is validated as an excellent approximation for nonperturbative applications, potentially simplifying calculations in QCD phenomenology and functional approaches. The analysis also reassesses the origin of the three-gluon vertex's infrared suppression, finding that swordfish diagrams (not ghost loops) dominate the suppression above 0.5 GeV, contrary to earlier attributions by Aguilar (2013) and Papavassiliou (2022).
