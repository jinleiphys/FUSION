# Nuclear matter from chiral low-momentum interactions

> Nuclear matter calculations based on low-momentum interactions derived from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon effective field theory interactions and fit only to few-body data predict realistic saturation properties with controlled uncertainties. This is promising for a unified description of nuclei and to develop a universal density functional based on low-momentum interactions.

## Key claim
Nuclear matter calculations using low-momentum interactions derived from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon effective field theory interactions, fit only to few-body data (A ≤ 4), predict realistic saturation properties with controlled uncertainties, but the authors do not claim a chiral expansion for nuclear matter saturation, only that the hierarchy of potential energy contributions is maintained by RG/SRG evolution.

## Method
The authors start from the N³LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential (EM 500 MeV) and RG-evolve it to low-momentum interactions (Vlowk) with a smooth regulator. Two couplings (c_D and c_E) of the N²LO three-nucleon force (3NF) are fit to the ³H binding energy and the ⁴He matter radius using exact Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods. Nuclear matter is calculated in three approximations: Hartree-Fock, including approximate second-order contributions, and summing particle-particle-ladder contributions, with full momentum-dependent Hartree-Fock propagators and approximate 3N double-exchange diagrams beyond Hartree-Fock.

## Key numbers
- ³H binding energy used for 3NF fit: -8.482 MeV
- ⁴He matter radius used for 3NF fit: 1.95-1.96 fm
- ⁴He binding energies from 3NF fits: -28.22 to -28.45 MeV (Vlowk) and -28.53 to -28.71 MeV (SRG)
- Compressibility K = 190-240 MeV (mainly from Λ_3NF = 2.0-2.5 fm⁻¹)
- Cutoff independence achieved at the level of 1-2 MeV per particle
- 3N cutoff Λ_3NF varied independently from 2.0 fm⁻¹ to 2.5 fm⁻¹ at fixed NN cutoff Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹
- Difference of 2 MeV per particle at saturation between Vlowk and SRG-evolved interactions

## Context
This work extends prior nuclear matter calculations that required adjusting short-range three-body forces by hand (Akmal 1998, Lejeune) to achieve realistic saturation, and instead uses chiral EFT interactions fit only to few-body data (A ≤ 4). The results enable development of ab-initio density functional theory (DFT) for nuclei based on expanding about nuclear matter, analogous to DFT in quantum chemistry starting from the uniform electron gas. The approach builds on renormalization group methods (Vlowk, smooth regulator) to soften short-range repulsion and tensor components of chiral interactions (N³LO, N³LOEGM), and on improved 3NF fitting to the ⁴He radius (NCSMchiral).
