# Hadronic decays from the lattice

> I review the lattice QCD approach to determining hadronic decay transitions. Examples considered include rho to pi pi; b_1 to pi omega; hybrid meson decays and scalar meson decays. I discuss what lattices can provide to help understand the composition of hadrons.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can determine hadronic decay transitions from first principles, but the method is restricted to cases where initial and final states have similar energies on the lattice, and the extraction of resonance parameters requires finite-volume techniques (Lüscher's method) to circumvent the problem that the two-body continuum masks any resonance state at large spatial volume.

## Method

The approach uses off-diagonal correlators between a resonance state (e.g., ρ meson) and a two-body state (e.g., ππ) with definite relative momentum, arranged to be approximately degenerate in energy on the lattice. The transition amplitude x is extracted from the slope of the normalized transition versus Euclidean time T, and this amplitude is related to the continuum coupling constant ḡ. For elastic scattering, Lüscher's method uses the small energy shifts of two-body levels with lattice volume L to extract phase shifts and hence resonance mass and width.

## Key numbers

- ρ → ππ coupling from lattice: ḡ = 1.40^{+47}_{-23} (xt method) and 1.56^{+21}_{-13} (ρ shift method), compared to experimental ρ → ππ value of 1.39
- Spin-exotic hybrid meson (J^PC = 1^{-+}) mass: 2.2(2) GeV from N_f = 2 dynamical lattices
- Partial width of hybrid meson to π b₁: 400(120) MeV
- Partial width of hybrid meson to π f₁: 90(60) MeV
- B(0⁺) → B(0⁻) π decay width predicted: 162(30) MeV
- Heavy quark hybrid meson H_b decay width to χ_b f₀: around 80 MeV

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD approach to hadronic decays pioneered by Lüscher (1986, 1990) and tested for ρ → ππ by McNeile (2002). It enables first-principles predictions for hybrid meson decays and scalar meson composition, complementing experimental searches for spin-exotic hybrids and clarifying whether states like a₀(980) and B_s(0⁺) are predominantly q̄q or molecular. The work also connects to chiral perturbation theory for extrapolating lattice results to physical quark masses.
