# Symmetry energy expansion and the peak value of the bulk viscosity

> The symmetry energy expansion is a useful way to parametrize the properties of dense matter near nuclear saturation density, and much work has been done to connect physical quantities like the neutron star radius and the core-crust transition density to the symmetry energy parameters. In this work, I connect the weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity in neutron-proton-electron ($npe$) matter to the symmetry parameters by calculating the susceptibilities of dense matter in terms of the symmetry energy. I use this result to calculate the resonant-peak value of the bulk viscosity as a function of density, finding that it strongly depends on $L$, as does the minimum bulk-viscous dissipation timescale. Also resulting from this calculation is a formula for finding the conformal points of the zero-temperature equation of state. Finally, I determine for which values of the symmetry parameters the maximum r-mode-stable rotation frequency of an $npe$-matter neutron star is smaller than the Kepler frequency, in the high-temperature conditions where bulk viscosity is the dominant dissipation mechanism.

## Key claim

The resonant-peak value of the bulk viscosity in neutron-proton-electron (npe) matter at nuclear saturation density depends only on the symmetry energy parameters J and L, and vanishes if J equals L (a conformal point), but the paper's analysis relies on the parabolic approximation and the subthermal regime where the oscillation amplitude is small enough that δμ ≪ T.

## Method

The author calculates the susceptibilities A and B of dense matter (defined as derivatives of the chemical potential imbalance δμ with respect to baryon density and proton fraction) using the symmetry energy expansion of the equation of state around nuclear saturation density. These susceptibilities are then used to compute the prefactor |A²/B| that sets the resonant-peak value of the bulk viscosity ζ^max = (1/2ω)|A²/B| for a given oscillation frequency ω, without needing the beta-equilibration rate γ. The symmetry energy coefficients J, L, K_sym, and Q_sym are related through empirical correlations from Tews et al. (2016) to explore how uncertainties in these parameters affect the bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers

- The resonant-peak bulk viscosity at saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ for a 1 kHz oscillation is ζ^max(n₀) = (2/3)(n₀/ω) x_p^eq [1 - 8x_p^eq + 36(x_p^eq)²] (L-J)²/J, where x_p^eq is the beta-equilibrium proton fraction at n₀.
- The symmetry energy at saturation J is constrained to 29 MeV < J < 34 MeV (MUSES, 2023).
- The slope parameter L is constrained to 43 MeV < L < 75 MeV from nuclear experiments (MUSES, 2023), with chiral effective field theory giving L = 59.8 ± 4.1 MeV (Drischler et al., 2020), PREX-II giving L = 106 ± 37 MeV (Reed et al., 2021), and a combined analysis giving 38 MeV < L < 67 MeV (Essick et al., 2021, 90% credible interval).
- The incompressibility K is approximately 230 ± 20 MeV (Lattimer, 2023).
- The skewness Q is essentially unconstrained, with estimates ranging from -800 MeV < Q < 400 MeV (Li et al., 2019).
- K_sym is constrained to -221 MeV < K_sym < 27 MeV (Essick et al., 2021), with a possible range -400 MeV < K_sym < 100 MeV (Li et al., 2019).
- Q_sym is virtually unknown, with a suggested range -200 MeV < Q_sym < 800 MeV (Li et al., 2019).

## Context

This work extends recent efforts by Yang et al. (2023, 2025) who correlated the zero-frequency bulk viscosity with symmetry energy parameters in relativistic mean field theory, and by Vidana et al. (2012) and Tu et al. (2025) who fit viscosity coefficients to the symmetry energy slope parameter for r-mode instability studies. The results enable calculation of the minimum bulk-viscous dissipation timescale and determination of which symmetry parameter values allow the maximum r-mode-stable rotation frequency of an npe-matter neutron star to be smaller than the Kepler frequency, without requiring detailed knowledge of the Urca rate γ. The paper also provides a formula (Eq. 12) for finding conformal points of the zero-temperature equation of state, generalizing the condition that at n₀ a conformal point occurs when J = L.
