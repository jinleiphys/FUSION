# Exact spinor-scalar bound states in a QFT with scalar interactions

> We study two-particle systems in a model quantum field theory, in which scalar particles and spinor particles interact via a mediating scalar field. The Lagrangian of the model is reformulated by using covariant Green's functions to solve for the mediating field in terms of the particle fields. This results in a Hamiltonian in which the mediating-field propagator appears directly in the interaction term. It is shown that exact two-particle eigenstates of the Hamiltonian can be determined. The resulting relativistic fermion-boson equation is shown to have Dirac and Klein-Gordon one-particle limits. Analytic solutions for the bound state energy spectrum are obtained for the case of massless mediating fields.

## Key claim

The paper claims that exact two-particle bound-state eigenstates of the Hamiltonian can be determined for a model quantum field theory in which a spinor particle and a scalar particle interact via a mediating scalar field, but only for the truncated model where the term coupling particle densities to the free mediating field is suppressed (i.e., no annihilation or decay into physical chions).

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian with fermion, boson, and mediating scalar fields, then use covariant Green functions to eliminate the mediating field in favor of a nonlocal interaction containing the chion propagator. They rewrite the scalar field in the Feshbach-Villars two-component formulation, quantize canonically using an "empty" vacuum state, and construct two-particle states as products of creation operators acting on that vacuum. The resulting relativistic two-body equation is reduced to a system of coupled radial Dirac-like equations for states of definite total angular momentum and parity.

## Key numbers

- The effective dimensionless coupling constant is defined as \(\alpha = g_1 g_2 / (8\pi m_2)\).
- For massless mediating fields, the potential is Coulombic: \(V(r) = -\alpha / r\).
- For equal-mass states with \(n'=0\) (principal quantum number \(n = j+1/2\)), the bound-state energy is \(E = 2m \sqrt{1 - (\alpha/(2n))^2}\), with critical coupling \(\alpha_c = 2n\).
- The bound-state energy domain for scalar Coulombic coupling is \(|m_1 - m_2| < E < m_1 + m_2\).
- For the equal-mass \(n'=0\) case, \(E=0\) at the critical value \(\alpha_c = 2n\).

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to the scalar Yukawa (Wick-Cutkosky) model by Darewych (1998) and by Berekdar and Darewych (1998), which yielded the exact two-body spectrum \(E_\pm = \sqrt{m_1^2 + m_2^2 \pm 2 m_1 m_2 \sqrt{1 - \alpha^2/n^2}}\) for two scalar particles. The present paper generalizes that approach to include one spinor particle, obtaining a formally similar spectrum but with an energy-dependent effective quantum number. The results enable future numerical studies for massive mediating fields and provide a benchmark for comparing with other relativistic bound-state approaches such as the Bethe-Salpeter equation and Fokker action formulations.
