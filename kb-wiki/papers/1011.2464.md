# Efimov Trimers: Analytic Solution Via Separable Potentials

## Key claim
The paper derives an exact dynamical equation for Efimov trimers using Yamaguchi separable potentials in the short-range limit of dimer resonances, which overcomes the non-uniqueness problem of zero-range potentials. The derivation closely reproduces the equation of Gogolin et al (GME) without additional assumptions, but the result is conditional on taking the short-range limit where the Yamaguchi range parameter β approaches infinity.

## Method
The author starts with a 3-body formalism for identical bosons using Yamaguchi separable potentials in momentum space, where the two-body form factor is taken as g(q) = 1/(β² + q²). The 3-body Schrödinger equation is reduced to a one-dimensional integral equation for the spectator function φ(p), which corresponds to the β_K function in the GME formalism. The short-range limit (β → ∞) is then taken carefully, combining the large β/2 term from the effective range expansion with a corresponding term in the function h(k₁) to produce a finite result that yields the final one-dimensional equation.

## Key numbers
- The effective range formula from the Yamaguchi potential gives: k cot δ = 4πλ⁻¹(β² + k²)² − β/2 + k²/(2β) ≡ −1/a + (1/2)r₀k²
- In the short-range limit, the combination β/2 − 4πβ⁴ h(k₁) reduces to √(3p²/4 + α²), where α² = −E for the bound trimer state
- The final equation (3.3) is: [−1/a + R₀(3p²/4 + α²) + √(3p²/4 + α²)] φ(p) = π⁻² ∫ d³p' φ(p')/(p² + p'² + p·p')
- The parameter R₀ is identified with R* from GME, where R₀ = r₀/2 − 3/(2β) + 2/(aβ²)

## Context
This work extends the earlier treatment of Efimov states by Gogolin et al (GME, 2008) using a field-theoretic method with creation and annihilation operators, and by Danilov (1961) who identified the non-uniqueness problem of the zero-range approximation from Skoroniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1957). The Yamaguchi separable potential approach provides an elementary derivation in the language of nuclear physics quantum mechanics, automatically incorporating an effective range parameter (the R* term of GME) without needing to insert it by hand. The result enables further analytic study of the Efimov spectrum using the established one-dimensional equation, and the author notes that all benefits of the GME results subsequent to their equation (7) also apply to this formalism.
