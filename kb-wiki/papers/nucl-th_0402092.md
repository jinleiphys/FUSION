# Handling the Handbag Diagram in Compton Scattering on the Proton

> Poincare invariance, gauge invariance, conservation of parity and time reversal invariance are respected in an impulse approximation evaluation of the handbag diagram. Proton wave functions, previously constrained by comparison with measured form factors, that incorporate the influence of quark transverse and orbital angular momentum (and the corresponding violation of proton helicity conservation) are used. Computed cross sections are found to be in reasonably good agreement with early measurements. The helicity correlation between the incident photon and outgoing proton, $K_{LL}$, is both large and positive at back angles. For photon laboratory energies of $\le$ 6 GeV, we find that $K_{LL}\ne A_{LL}$, $D_{LL}\ne1$, and that the polarization $P$ can be large.

# Handling the Handbag Diagram in Compton Scattering on the Proton

## Key claim
Poincare invariance, gauge invariance, parity, and time reversal invariance can all be respected in an impulse-approximation evaluation of the handbag diagram for real Compton scattering on the proton, but only if one includes the effects of quark transverse and orbital angular momentum that violate proton helicity conservation. For photon laboratory energies of 6 GeV or less, the simplifying assumptions that lead to proton helicity conservation are not valid, causing the helicity correlation \(K_{LL}\) to differ from \(A_{LL}\) and \(D_{LL}\) to differ from unity at large scattering angles.

## Method
The Compton scattering amplitude was evaluated using the handbag diagram in an impulse approximation, employing light-front dynamics with relativistic constituent quark wave functions previously constrained by nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Frank, Miller, and Jennings, 1995; Miller, 2002). The wave functions incorporate quark transverse and orbital angular momentum through Dirac spinor structure, and the calculation respects parity and time reversal invariance by applying a shift of integration variable \(\mathbf{K}_\perp \to \mathbf{K}_\perp + \eta\mathbf{\Delta}/2\) and neglecting the component of \(\mathbf{K}\) parallel to \(\mathbf{\Delta}\) when the momentum transfer is large compared to typical wave function momenta. The 16 spin amplitudes were computed and reduced to six independent ones, from which helicity amplitudes and observables were obtained.

## Key numbers
- Computed cross sections were multiplied by a factor of 0.8 to account for a 20% overall normalization uncertainty when comparing to data from Cornell (CornellCS).
- For photon laboratory energies of 3.2 and 4.3 GeV, \(K_{LL}\) is large and positive at large scattering angles, while \(K_{LL} \approx -A_{LL}\) for backward scattering angles.
- The polarization \(P\) is found to be 0, in contrast to the approximately 3% estimate of Kroll, Diehl, and collaborators (hkm).
- \(K_{LS} = 0\) and \(A_{LS} = 0\) for sideways proton spin correlations.
- The incoming photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\) vanishes.

## Context
This work extends the handbag diagram approach of Radyushkin (1996) and Diehl (2003) by using a model that avoids the neglect of hadronic helicity non-conservation, which was a limitation of prior treatments. The results contradict the predictions of Kroll, Diehl, and collaborators (hkm) that \(K_{LL} = A_{LL}\) and \(D_{LL} \approx 1\), showing instead that these equalities fail at large scattering angles for energies up to 6 GeV. The calculations enable future experiments measuring \(A_{LL}\) or \(D_{LL}\) to determine whether proton helicity conservation holds in Compton scattering at a given energy.
