# Neutron Stars and Neutron Skins: Connecting Finite Nuclei to Dense Matter

## Key claim
The paper argues that a consistent picture of the symmetry energy and its slope parameter L emerges from Bayesian analyses combining multiple experimental probes (dipole polarizability, parity-violating electron scattering, heavy-ion fragmentation, quasi-free scattering, and ultraperipheral collisions) with energy density functionals, but the quoted constraints (J ~ 32 MeV and L ~ 45-50 MeV) are presented as typical results from a specific analysis (Azizi, Bertulani, and Davila, 2026) rather than as definitive, universally accepted values.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical connections between neutron skin thickness in finite nuclei and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure and energy density functionals (Skyrme and relativistic mean-field models) for nuclear properties. It describes how multiple experimental probes, dipole polarizability, parity-violating electron scattering asymmetry, fragmentation cross sections, quasi-free scattering analyzing powers, and ultraperipheral collision cross sections, each provide sensitivity to the neutron skin thickness. A Bayesian inference framework is outlined that combines these heterogeneous datasets with energy density functional predictions to extract posterior distributions for symmetry energy parameters J and L.

## Key numbers
- Neutron star masses: 1-2 solar masses, radii approximately 10-15 km
- PREX-II measurement: neutron skin thickness of lead-208 = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX measurement: neutron skin thickness of calcium-48 = 0.121 ± 0.026 fm
- Typical linear correlation for lead-208: neutron skin thickness ≈ (0.10-0.15) fm + (0.001-0.002) × L, with L expressed in MeV
- Bayesian analysis results (Azizi, Bertulani, and Davila, 2026): J ~ 32 MeV and L ~ 45-50 MeV
- Neutron-removal cross sections can change by approximately 20% along isotopic chains where neutron skin thickness varies by about 0.2 fm

## Context
This review extends the long-standing program connecting neutron skin measurements to the symmetry energy, building on prior work by Reinhard (2010), Roca-Maza (2011), Horowitz (2014), and others who established correlations between neutron skin thickness and the slope parameter L. The paper discusses how parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX and CREX at Jefferson Lab) provide direct neutron skin determinations with minimal theoretical uncertainties, while dipole polarizability measurements (Tamii 2011, Endres 2010, Rossi 2013, Bracco 2019) and fragmentation studies (Aumann 2017, Teixeira 2022) offer complementary constraints. The Bayesian framework from Azizi, Bertulani, and Davila (2026) enables combining these diverse datasets to constrain the equation of state, which can then be applied to predict neutron star radii and tidal deformabilities via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.
