# Exotic clusters in the excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15

> The excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15 were studied by an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics method. The theoretical results reproduced the energy levels of recently measured excited states of Be-12, and also predicted rotational bands with innovative clustering structures in Be-12, Be-14 and B-15. Clustering states with new exotic clusters (He-6, He-8 and Li-9) were theoretically suggested. One new aspect in very neutron-rich nuclei is a 6-nucleon correlation among 4 neutrons and 2 protons, which plays an important role in the formation of He-6 clusters during clustering: 8He + 6He of Be-14 and 9Li+6He of B-15.

# Exotic clusters in the excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15

## Key claim
The excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15 contain rotational bands with well-developed clustering structures involving exotic clusters (He-6, He-8, Li-9), but the theoretical binding energies systematically underestimate experimental values by 6-15 MeV, and the calculations were performed within a bound state approximation without considering particle decay widths.

## Method
The author applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) to calculate the ground and excited states of Be-12, Be-14 and B-15. The interactions used were the modified Volkov No.1 central force (case 3), the G3RS spin-orbit force, and the Coulomb force, with a Majorana parameter m=0.65 and G3RS strength u1=-u2=3700 MeV. Higher excited states were constructed by varying the energy of orthogonal components to lower states and superposing wave functions, with final results obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies: Be-12 = 61.9 MeV, Be-14 = 59.7 MeV, B-15 = 73.1 MeV (experimental values: 68.65, 69.77, 88.19 MeV respectively)
- With Majorana parameter m=0.61, Be-14 binding energy becomes 67.7 MeV
- Excitation energy E(0+_2) of Be-14: 4.2 MeV (m=0.61) and 4.4 MeV (m=0.65)
- B(GT) for beta decay from Be-12(0+_1) to B-12(1+): theoretical 0.8, experimental 0.59
- B(E1) from 1-_1 to 0+_1 in Be-12: 0.02 e^2 fm^2 (experimental 0.05 e^2 fm^2)
- B(E2;2+_1 -> 0+_1) in Be-12: 14 e^2 fm^4
- B(E2;2+_2 -> 0+_2) in Be-12: 8 e^2 fm^4
- Deformation parameters for Be-14: beta=0.49 (0+_1), beta=0.64 (0+_2)
- Partial decay width of 6+_2 and 8+_2 in Be-14 into He-8(0+)+He-6(0+): 6 keV and 50 keV respectively
- Threshold energy for He-8+He-6 separation in Be-14: 9.1 MeV excitation
- Threshold energy for He-8+He-6 decay channel in Be-14: 12.2 MeV
- B-15 K=3/2-_2 band starts at about 7 MeV above ground state

## Context
This work extends prior AMD studies of Be-10 by Kanada-En'yo (2000) and others (Itagaki, 2000; von Oertzen, 1997) that suggested molecule-like structures in Be-10, and addresses the recently measured excited states of Be-12 from inelastic scattering experiments by Tanihata (2000) and break-up reactions by Freer (2001). The calculations predict new rotational bands with exotic clustering (He-8+He-6 in Be-14, Li-9+He-6 in B-15) that could be tested in future break-up experiments, and identify a 6-nucleon correlation mechanism (4 neutrons + 2 protons) for He-6 cluster formation that may appear in other neutron-rich nuclei. The author notes that particle decay widths were not calculated within the AMD framework and require methods such as reduced width amplitude or complex scaling for further study.
