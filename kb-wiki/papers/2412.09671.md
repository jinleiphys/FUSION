# Effective temperatures of the QGP from thermal photon and dilepton production

> Thermal electromagnetic radiation is emitted by the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) throughout its space-time evolution, with production rates that depend characteristically on the temperature. We study this temperature using thermal photons and dileptons using the Trajectum heavy ion code, which is constrained by Bayesian analysis. In addition we present the elliptic flow of both the thermal photons and thermal dileptons including systematic uncertainties corresponding to the model parameter uncertainty. We give a comprehensive overview of the resulting effective temperatures $T_{\rm eff}$, obtained from thermal photon transverse momentum and thermal dilepton invariant mass distributions, as well as the dependence of $T_{\rm eff}$ on various selection criteria of these probes. We conclude that the $T_{\rm eff}$ obtained from thermal photons is mostly insensitive to the temperature of the QGP with a value of $T_{\rm eff} \sim$ 250-300 MeV depending on their transverse momentum, almost independent of collision centrality. Thermal dileptons are much better probes of the QGP temperature as they do not suffer from a blue shift as their invariant mass is used, allowing for a more precise constraint of the QGP temperature during different stages of the evolution of the system. By applying selection criteria on the dilepton transverse momentum and the invariant mass we are able to extract fluid temperatures on average times ranging from late emission ($\langle τ\rangle = 5.6\,$fm$/c$) to very early emissions ($\langle τ\rangle < 1.0\,$fm$/c$). Furthermore, we show how these selection criteria can be used to map the elliptic flow of the system all throughout its evolution.

## Key claim

The effective temperature extracted from thermal photon spectra is mostly insensitive to the actual QGP temperature, remaining around 250-300 MeV regardless of collision centrality, because the photon transverse momentum is blue-shifted by radial flow; thermal dileptons, using the Lorentz-invariant invariant mass, are much better probes and can be used to extract fluid temperatures at average emission times ranging from late emission (⟨τ⟩ = 5.6 fm/c) to very early emission (⟨τ⟩ < 1.0 fm/c).

## Method

The authors extended the Trajectum heavy-ion hybrid model, which uses a generalized T RENTo initial state, pre-equilibrium evolution, 2+1D boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamics, and the Cooper-Frye hadronization, to include next-to-leading order thermal photon and dilepton emission rates from a weakly coupled QGP. Thermal photons and dileptons were emitted from fluid cells with temperature above the switching temperature T_switch during the hydrodynamic phase, with momenta sampled from the differential production rates and boosted to the lab frame. The model parameters were constrained by Bayesian analysis of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, and systematic uncertainties were obtained from 20 parameter configurations sampled from the posterior distribution.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature from thermal photons: T_eff ~ 250-300 MeV, almost independent of collision centrality, for transverse momentum ranges 1.0 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c and 2.0 < p_T < 3.0 GeV/c.
- Average emission time for thermal dileptons with 3 < m_ll < 4 GeV/c^2 in 0-10% centrality: ⟨τ⟩ = 0.91 fm/c (for p_T > 0.2 GeV/c).
- Average emission time for thermal dileptons with 0.2 < m_ll < 1 GeV/c^2 in 0-10% centrality: ⟨τ⟩ = 4.97 fm/c (for p_T > 0.2 GeV/c).
- Average emission time for thermal photons with p_T > 2.0 GeV/c in 0-10% centrality: ⟨τ⟩ = 3.28 fm/c.
- Average emission time for thermal photons with p_T > 0.2 GeV/c in 0-10% centrality: ⟨τ⟩ = 5.37 fm/c.
- Systematic uncertainty on the temperature as a function of proper time is typically less than 5%.
- The hydrodynamic starting time parameter is tau_hydro ≈ 0.38 ± 0.17 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of thermal photon and dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions (Deng 2010, Vujanovic 2013, Gale 2014, Paquet 2017, Churchill 2023) by using the Trajectum model constrained by Bayesian analysis to provide systematic uncertainties. The results confirm earlier findings by Churchill (2023) that dileptons are better suited than photons for relating effective temperature to the actual QGP temperature. The ability to map the elliptic flow of the system throughout its evolution using selection criteria on dilepton invariant mass and transverse momentum enables future experimental comparisons at LHC energies, particularly for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
