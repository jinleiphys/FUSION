# Collective structural evolution in neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os and Pt isotopes

> An interacting boson model Hamiltonian determined from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the new microscopic Gogny energy density functional D1M, is applied to the spectroscopic analysis of neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os and Pt isotopes with mass $A\sim 180-200$. Excitation energies and transition rates for the relevant low-lying quadrupole collective states are calculated by this method. Transitions from prolate to oblate ground-state shapes are analyzed as a function of neutron number $N$ in a given isotopic chain by calculating excitation energies, $B$(E2) ratios, and correlation energies in the ground state. It is shown that such transitions tend to occur more rapidly for the isotopes with lower proton number $Z$, when departing from the proton shell closure Z=82. The triaxial degrees of freedom turn out to play an important role in describing the considered mass region. Predicted low-lying spectra for the neutron-rich exotic Hf and Yb isotopes are presented. The approximations used in the model and the possibilities to refine its predictive power are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the prolate-to-oblate ground-state shape transition in neutron-rich Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes occurs more rapidly for isotopes with lower proton number Z when departing from the proton shell closure at Z=82, but the authors note that the approximations used in the mapping procedure and the omission of intruder configurations limit the predictive power, particularly for Pt isotopes where shape coexistence is known to be important.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to generate energy surfaces as functions of quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ. They then mapped these fermionic energy surfaces onto the energy surface of a proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian containing monopole and quadrupole bosons, fitting the parameters ε, κ, χπ, and χν. The α parameter of the L·L term was determined separately by matching the cranking moment of inertia of the boson coherent state to the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia from the fermionic calculation, after which the IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The mass range studied is A ∼ 180-200, covering Yb (Z=70), Hf (Z=72), W (Z=74), Os (Z=76), and Pt (Z=78) isotopes with neutron numbers N=110-122
- The correlation energy E_Corr reaches its maximum magnitude at N ∼ 116 for each isotopic chain, corresponding to the prolate-to-oblate shape transition point
- For the W isotopes, the experimental moment of inertia decreases with N and the slope changes at N=116, indicating a gradual shape transition
- The ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) for lighter W isotopes with N=110 and 112 is 3.27 and 3.23 respectively, close to the rotor formula value
- The IBM parameters include ε values ranging from 36.8 keV (186W) to 806 keV (192Yb), and κ values ranging from 265 keV to 366 keV across all isotopes

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the Gogny-D1S functional on Pt isotopes (GognyIBMPt, 2009) and some Os and W isotopes (IBMwos, 2010) to the neighboring exotic Hf and Yb nuclei, while also testing the newer Gogny-D1M functional. The results are consistent with constrained mean-field studies (Sarriguren 2008, gradient-2) that showed triaxiality is important for describing prolate-to-oblate shape evolution in this mass region. The paper enables future work on incorporating intruder configurations from particle-hole excitations across the Z=82 shell gap, which would be needed for a more complete description of shape coexistence in Pt isotopes, and provides predicted low-lying spectra for neutron-rich Hf and Yb isotopes that could guide future experimental studies.
