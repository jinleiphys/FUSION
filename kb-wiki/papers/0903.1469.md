# Universal Flow in the Early Stages at RHIC

> Pre-thermal flow plays an important role in the final state evolution of heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We show that flow has universal features for a wide range of models. This significantly reduces the uncertainty in initializing hydrodynamic models at RHIC.

## Key claim

Pre-thermal transverse flow, defined as \(T_{0i}/T_{00}\), grows at a rate \(\alpha_x = -\partial_x T_{00}/(2T_{00})\) that is independent of the transverse stiffness \(\kappa\) (the ratio of transverse pressure to energy density), provided the stress-energy tensor is traceless, longitudinal motion is boost-invariant, and \(\kappa\) is spatially uniform. This universality holds for times up to \(\tau = 1\) fm/\(c\) across very different models, but is contingent on the three listed criteria and is only approximate at the 10% level.

## Method

The author derives the universal flow growth rate analytically from energy-momentum conservation under the assumptions of tracelessness, boost invariance, and spatially uniform transverse stiffness \(\kappa\). Three models are then compared numerically: (1) coherent classical electromagnetic fields from a single Gaussian charge density (yielding \(\kappa \approx 1\) initially), (2) incoherent point-like charges (yielding \(\kappa = 1/2\)), and (3) ideal hydrodynamics of a massless gas (yielding \(\kappa = 1/3\)). All models start with the same Gaussian transverse energy density profile of width \(R = 3\) fm, and the flow \(T_{0x}/T_{00}\) is evaluated along the \(x\)-axis at proper times 0.3, 0.6, and 1.0 fm/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- Energy densities in the first fm/\(c\) are mostly above 10 GeV/fm\(^3\), making the traceless assumption valid to the order of 10%.
- The relaxation time \(\tau_{IS}\) for viscous hydrodynamics is estimated as \(\tau_{IS} \approx 0.5\) fm/\(c\) assuming the viscosity is within a factor of 3–4 of the KSS limit.
- The initial Gaussian energy density profile has width \(R = 3\) fm.
- The flow \(T_{0x}/T_{00}\) is evaluated at proper times \(\tau = 0.3\), 0.6, and 1.0 fm/\(c\) for all three models.
- The universality allows one to state the flow at \(\tau = 1\) fm/\(c\) with approximately 10% confidence, translating to approximately 1% uncertainty in the final flow.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of pre-thermal flow in heavy ion collisions at RHIC, where early flow has been shown to be pivotal in reproducing femtoscopic two-particle correlation data from hydrodynamic models (Gyulassy 2007, Li 2007, Pratt 2008, Broniowski 2008). The universal flow result significantly reduces the uncertainty in initializing hydrodynamic models at RHIC, as the initial collective flow at \(\tau = 1\) fm/\(c\) can be set independently of the pre-thermal model. The paper also connects to Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics approaches (Israel and Stewart, Muronga, Baier and Romatschke, Heinz and Chaudhuri, Romatschke, Song and Heinz) and to earlier work on the evolution of color fields (Krasnitz 2002). The findings enable more confident initialization of both ideal and viscous hydrodynamic codes, though the initial energy density remains uncertain and varies drastically between models.
