# Cosmological Constant Effects on the Properties of Mass Twin Compact Stars

> We present a systematic investigation of the cosmological constant effects in compact stars interiors in the framework of Einstein's gravity. Consideration of a cosmological constant $Λ$ in compact stars is motivated by the mechanism of acceleration of the observable universe, where $Λ$ is usually related to the dark energy. In particular, we consider compact star mass twins, hybrid neutron stars that populate both the second and third branch of the mass-radius diagram. For those models, the need of consideration of excluded volume effects in the equation of state, resulting from the finite size volume of nucleons, leads to a stiffening of matter causing compact stars to acquire higher mass and radius values. We demonstrate that certain values of the cosmological constant can also modify the compact star properties but in an opposite way. In addition, we find that the inclusion of $Λ$ can have a similar effect to the existence of pasta phases at the hadron-quark interface.

## Key claim

The cosmological constant can modify compact star properties in the opposite direction to the stiffening caused by excluded volume effects in the equation of state, and for certain values it can produce a similar effect to the existence of pasta phases at the hadron-quark interface, though a sharp kink remains in the mass-radius relation that evidences the strong first order phase transition.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Volkov-Oppenheimer (TOV) equations of general relativity modified by the inclusion of a cosmological constant term, using two realistic equations of state: the DD2 relativistic density functional model for pure hadronic matter, and a multi-polytrope (MP) equation of state featuring a strong first order phase transition that produces high mass twins. They varied the cosmological constant parameter and computed the resulting mass-radius diagrams for both equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The typical values of the cosmological constant required to produce an observable effect in compact stars are of the order of Lambda = 10^-8 to 10^-10 m^-2.
- The Lambda values obtained in this work differ by up to five orders of magnitude from those of Bordbar (2015), apparently due to the stiffness of the equations of state used here compared to the soft equation of state in that earlier work.
- The high mass twins phenomenon involves stars with very similar masses located in different branches of the mass-radius diagram, having considerable radius differences of approximately 1-2 km.

## Context

This work extends the study of cosmological constant effects in compact stars initiated by Bordbar (2015) and Hendi (2015), using stiffer equations of state that can support massive compact stars like the 2 solar mass object measured by Antoniadis (2013). The effect of the cosmological constant on mass-radius curves is opposite to the stiffening from excluded volume effects described by Benic (2014) and Alvarez-Castillo (2016), and produces a similar mass-radius behavior to that observed when pasta phases are considered at the hadron-quark phase transition by Alvarez-Castillo (2017) and Ayriyan (2017). The results enable future comparisons with observations from the NICER X-ray detector and gravitational wave observatories that can estimate tidal deformabilities.
