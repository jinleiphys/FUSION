# Hartree-Fock+BCS approach to unstable nuclei with the Skyrme force

> We reanalyze the results of our extensive Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation from new points of view paying attention to the properties of unstable nuclei. The calculation has been done with the Skyrme SIII force for the ground and shape isomeric states of 1029 even-even nuclei ranging 2 <= Z <= 114. We also discuss the advantages of the employed three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation, especially on its remarkably high precision with apparently coarse meshes when applied to atomic nuclei. In Appendices we give the coefficients of finite-point numerical differentiation and integration formulae suitable for Cartesian mesh representation and elucidate the features of each formula and the differences from a method based on the Fourier transformation.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock+BCS calculation with the Skyrme SIII force achieves an r.m.s. mass error of 2.2 MeV for 1029 even-even nuclei, which is not competitive with the latest mass formulae (typically 0.5 MeV), but the paper reanalyzes these results from new perspectives rather than claiming high mass precision.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock equations with the Skyrme SIII effective interaction using a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation with 1 fm mesh spacing, placing an octant of each nucleus in a box of 13 × 13 × 14 fm³ (for Z ≤ 82) or 14 × 14 × 15 fm³ (for Z > 82) with D₂h symmetry. Pairing correlations were treated with a seniority force in the BCS approximation, with the pairing strength G_τ determined for each nucleus such that the continuous spectrum approximation using the Thomas-Fermi single-particle level density reproduces the empirical formula Δ = 12 MeV/√A. The calculation covered ground and shape isomeric states of 1029 even-even nuclei with 2 ≤ Z ≤ 114, obtaining second minima for 758 nuclei.

## Key numbers

- 1029 even-even nuclei calculated, ranging 2 ≤ Z ≤ 114, with second minima obtained for 758 nuclei
- r.m.s. mass error of 2.2 MeV for the HF+BCS calculation with Skyrme SIII force
- With mesh spacing a = 1 fm, the relative error of total energy is 0.5% for heavy nuclei (approximately 7 MeV for ¹⁷⁰Er)
- Energy difference between prolate and oblate solutions is determined with error of approximately 0.4 MeV at a = 1 fm mesh spacing
- The correction function of only Z and N decreases the total energy error down to approximately 0.2 MeV
- For the liquid-drop-model shape parameters derived from 5361 data points (mass, proton, and neutron distributions of ground and first-excited states), the maximum deviation of a₂₀ from the fitted polynomial is 0.05 and the r.m.s. deviation is 0.007
- For the liquid-drop radius R₀ versus r.m.s. radius, the maximum deviation is 0.3 fm and the r.m.s. deviation is 0.06 fm
- 289 even-even nuclei (4 ≤ Z ≤ 98) were compared for quadrupole moments against experimental B(E2) values
- ⁸Be has the largest deformation among calculated nuclei with a₂₀ = 0.62

## Context

This work extends the earlier HF+BCS calculation by Tajima et al. (1996) and provides the first published estimation of the r.m.s. mass error for any Skyrme force. The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) by Moller et al. (1995) for quadrupole deformation parameters, showing systematic differences around Z ≈ N ≈ 40 due to shape coexistence. The paper discusses the inadequacy of the HF+BCS scheme for neutron-rich nuclei where the Fermi level is higher than approximately -4 MeV, motivating the development of a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework in coordinate space with three-dimensional Cartesian mesh to describe large surface diffuseness and arbitrary deformations.
