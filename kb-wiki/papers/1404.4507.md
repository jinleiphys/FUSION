# Formation of light exotic nuclei in low-energy multinucleon transfer reactions

> Low-energy multinucleon transfer reactions are shown to be very effective tool for the production and spectroscopic study of light exotic nuclei. The corresponding cross sections are found to be significantly larger as compared with high energy fragmentation reactions. Several optimal reactions for the production of extremely neutron rich isotopes of elements with Z=6-14 are proposed.

# Formation of light exotic nuclei in low-energy multinucleon transfer reactions

## Key claim

Low-energy multinucleon transfer reactions on heavy targets can produce light neutron-rich nuclei with cross sections about two orders of magnitude larger than those from high-energy fragmentation reactions, provided the model assumptions about nucleon transfer rates and excitation energy sharing are correct. The claim is conditional on the nucleon transfer rate being fixed at 0.05 x 10^22 s^-1 and on the assumption that excitation energy is shared proportionally to fragment masses.

## Method

The authors used a Langevin-type dynamical model with coupled equations of motion for collective variables (distance between nuclear centers, surface deformations, neutron and proton asymmetries) to simulate multinucleon transfer in damped collisions of light heavy ions (A ~ 20) with a 238U target. The model incorporates adiabatic multi-dimensional potential energy surfaces from the two-center shell model, with nucleon exchange described by inertialess Langevin equations using transport coefficients derived from macroscopic transition probabilities. Cross sections for primary projectile-like fragments were calculated by integrating over impact parameters and then compared with high-energy fragmentation data from 48Ca beams on 181Ta and 9Be targets.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon transfer rate fixed at 0.05 x 10^22 s^-1 for all reactions analyzed
- Cross sections for production of light neutron-rich nuclei (e.g., 19C, 24O, 30Ne) in low-energy multinucleon transfer reactions are higher by about 2 orders of magnitude compared with high-energy fragmentation reactions
- For 18O + 238U at E_c.m. = 125 MeV, charge and isotopic distributions of projectile-like fragments were calculated
- For 26Mg + 238U at E_c.m. = 150 MeV, charge and isotopic distributions were calculated; the GRAZING code gives much narrower distributions
- For 36S + 238U at E_c.m. = 180 MeV, charge, mass, isotopic and excitation energy distributions were calculated
- For 14N + 159Tb at E_lab = 115 MeV and theta_lab = 30 degrees, differential cross sections for projectile-like fragments with Z = 3-7 were compared with experimental data from Balster (1987)
- For 20Ne + 100Mo at E_lab = 146 MeV, charge distribution of reaction fragments was compared with experimental data from Nagame (1984)
- For 19F + 89Y at E_lab = 140 MeV, angular, charge and isotopic distributions were compared with experimental data from Mermaz (1986)

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-type dynamical model previously developed by Zagrebaev and Greiner (2005, 2008) for heavy-ion damped collisions to the regime of light projectiles (A ~ 20) on heavy targets. It directly contradicts the narrower mass distributions predicted by the GRAZING code (Corradi, 2009), which underestimates proton transfer channels. The paper enables experimental searches for light exotic nuclei at existing low-energy facilities, proposing specific reactions (18O, 26Mg, 36S on 238U) as alternatives to high-energy fragmentation at NSCL MSU, RIKEN, and GSI. The results are compared with fragmentation data from Kwan (2006) for 48Ca on 181Ta and from RIKEN for 48Ca on 9Be.
