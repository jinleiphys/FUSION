# Evolution of Parton Fragmentation Functions at Finite Temperature

> The first order correction to the parton fragmentation functions in a thermal medium is derived in the leading logarithmic approximation in the framework of thermal field theory. The medium-modified evolution equations of the parton fragmentation functions are also derived. It is shown that all infrared divergences, both linear and logarithmic, in the real processes are canceled among themselves and by corresponding virtual corrections. The evolution of the quark number and the energy loss (or gain) induced by the thermal medium are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper derives the first-order correction to parton fragmentation functions in a thermal medium within the leading logarithmic approximation of thermal field theory, showing that the medium-modified evolution equations can be cast into a form similar to vacuum DGLAP equations but with splitting functions that depend explicitly on temperature and the partons' initial energy. The authors emphasize that their calculation includes only first-order corrections and cannot incorporate interference effects like the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) effect, which requires going beyond the leading log approximation.

## Method

The authors extend the definition of fragmentation functions to finite temperature by replacing vacuum expectation values with thermal expectation values and introducing new functions that track the absolute momentum of both the initial parton and the observed hadron separately. They calculate the first-order radiative corrections using finite-temperature field theory propagators that include thermal distribution functions (Fermi-Dirac for quarks, Bose-Einstein for gluons), then apply the collinear approximation to extract the leading-twist behavior and derive the medium-modified evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The quark splitting function at finite temperature is given by \(P_{qq}(z_f) = C_F \frac{1+z_f^2}{1-z_f}\), where \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/2N_c\) with \(N_c=3\)
- The gluon splitting functions include \(\gamma_{gg}(z) = 2C_A[\frac{z}{(1-z)_+} + \frac{1-z}{z} + z(1-z)] + \delta(1-z)[\frac{11}{6}C_A - \frac{2}{3}n_f T_F]\) with \(C_A = N_c = 3\), \(T_F = 1/2\), and \(n_f\) the number of active quark flavors
- The vacuum splitting functions are: \(\gamma_{qq}(z) = C_F[\frac{1+z^2}{(1-z)_+} + \frac{3}{2}\delta(1-z)]\), \(\gamma_{qg}(z) = C_F\frac{1+(1-z)^2}{z}\), and \(\gamma_{gq}(z) = T_F[z^2+(1-z)^2]\)

## Context

This work extends the vacuum DGLAP evolution equations (Dokshitzer, Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, Parisi, 1970s) to finite temperature by incorporating thermal field theory methods. It builds on earlier studies of parton energy loss using Debye-screened potential models (Gyulassy and Wang, 1994; Baier et al., 1997; Zakharov, 1996) and hard-thermal-loop resummed gluon propagators (Wang, 2000). The paper enables future investigation of medium-modified fragmentation functions beyond the leading log approximation, including the LPM interference effect and collision-induced radiation using HTL resummed propagators.
