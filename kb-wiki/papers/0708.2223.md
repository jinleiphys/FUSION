# Tau decay and the structure of the a1

> We analyse the decay $τ\toπππν$ based on the recently developed techniques to generate axial-vector resonances dynamically. Under the assumption that the a1 is a coupled-channel meson-molecule, the spectral function is described surprisingly well by adjusting only one free parameter. Including, in addition, an elementary a1 corrupts the results.

# Tau decay and the structure of the a1

## Key claim
The a1(1260) axial-vector meson is likely a coupled-channel meson-molecule (a dynamically generated resonance from π-ρ and K*-K interactions) rather than a quark-antiquark state, based on the observation that the τ → 3πν spectral function is described well with only one free parameter in the molecule scenario, while including an explicit elementary a1 field corrupts the agreement with data.

## Method
The authors describe the final-state interaction of the π-ρ and K*-K channels produced in τ decay using a Bethe-Salpeter equation with the kernel fixed by the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction from chiral perturbation theory. They perform two calculations: one with only the dynamically generated resonance from the coupled-channel rescattering, and a second where an explicit elementary a1 field is added alongside the Weinberg-Tomozawa term. The spectral function for τ → πππν is computed and compared to ALEPH data.

## Key numbers
- The coupling constants are fixed using f_V = 0.154 GeV/M_ρ for the ρ-to-photon coupling and g_V = 0.069 GeV/M_ρ for the ρ-to-two-pion coupling, based on experimental values.
- The subtraction point μ_2 = 8.5 M_ρ^2 (approximately corresponding to a cutoff of 1 GeV) reproduces the data well in the molecule scenario.
- In the molecule scenario, the subtraction point for the scattering amplitude loops is fixed at μ_1 = M_ρ^2.
- For the explicit a1 calculation, two parameter sets are given: set 1 uses M_a1 = 1.23 GeV with μ_1 = 2 M_ρ^2 and μ_2 = 1.05 M_ρ^2; set 2 uses M_a1 = 1.21 GeV with μ_1 = M_ρ^2 and μ_2 = 2.5 M_ρ^2.
- The K*-K threshold effect appears near s ≈ 1.4 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the unitary extensions of chiral perturbation theory previously applied to scalar mesons (σ, f0(980), a0(980), κ(900) by Oset and collaborators) and to baryon resonances (Λ(1405), N*(1535) by Kaiser and others) into the axial-vector sector for τ decay data. It builds on the coupled-channel approach of Lutz and collaborators and Oset and collaborators for vector-pseudoscalar interactions, which had previously identified poles attributed to axial-vector mesons but had not performed direct comparison to decay data. The work enables a direct test of the dynamical nature of the a1 using high-quality ALEPH data, and suggests that the a1 may serve as the chiral partner of the ρ meson in the form of a multi-particle state rather than a one-particle quark-antiquark state.
