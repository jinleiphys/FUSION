# NA60 and BR Scaling In Terms of The Vector Manifestation: Formal Consideration

> The arguments developed in the preceding article on how BR scaling would predict for dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, e.g., NA60, are augmented with more precise and rigorous arguments.

# NA60 and BR Scaling In Terms of The Vector Manifestation: Formal Consideration

## Key claim
BR scaling as interpreted through hidden local symmetry theory with vector manifestation (HLS$^{VM}$) does not predict a simple dropping vector meson mass that would be directly visible in NA60 dilepton data; rather, the vector meson pole mass remains nearly unchanged up to a "flash temperature" of approximately 125 MeV, and the green curve erroneously attributed to BR scaling in NA60 results has nothing to do with the authors' proposed scaling.

## Method
The authors provide formal arguments from Harada-Yamawaki's HLS$^{VM}$ theory, analyzing three key elements: (1) vector dominance (VD) violation in medium, where the parameter *a* flows to 1 rather than the VD value of 2; (2) the distinction between the "parametric" (bare) mass *m_bare* and the physical pole mass *m_V*, where only the former directly tracks the quark condensate; and (3) the "fusing" of sobar excitations (e.g., Δ-hole, N*(1520)-hole) with elementary mesonic modes, which produces level repulsion that shifts spectral peaks away from naive tree-level pole positions.

## Key numbers
- The parameter *a* flows to 1 in hot/dense matter, implying the vector dominance part of the Lagrangian has coefficient 1/2 compared to standard VD, so dilepton production should be 1/4 of that calculated using vector dominance.
- At the flash temperature T ≈ 125 MeV, the vector meson recovers 95% of its free-space mass, based on Miller's lattice calculation of the gluon condensate.
- The thermal loop correction ΔM up to T ∼ 125 MeV is positive and small, ∼ +10 MeV.
- At nuclear matter density n ≈ n₀, the ratio Φ ≡ m*_bare/m_bare ≈ f*_π/f_π is ∼ 0.8, implying a mass drop less than 20% at normal nuclear matter density.
- The linear density drop is parameterized as Φ(n) ≈ 1 − 0.2(n/n₀).

## Context
This paper extends the authors' preceding simplified argument (Brown and Rho, 2005) on how BR scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) applies to NA60 dilepton data, using the modern HLS$^{VM}$ framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2001). It contradicts the common practice of assuming a simple dropping vector meson mass proportional to the quark condensate, as done by many workers in the field. The work enables a more rigorous treatment of dilepton production that incorporates vector dominance violation, intrinsic background dependence, and sobar fusing, though the authors note that a full calculation from HLS$^{VM}$ theory including baryons has not yet been performed.
