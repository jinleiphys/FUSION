# Dynamical Phase Trajectories in Baryon and Isospin Density Spaces

> We review recent results obtained for charge asymmetric systems at Fermi and intermediate energies, ranging from 30 MeV/u to 1 GeV/u. Observables sensitive to the isospin dependent part of nuclear interaction are discussed, providing information on the symmetry energy behavior from sub- to supra-saturation densities.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how observables from heavy-ion collisions at Fermi and intermediate energies (30 MeV/u to 1 GeV/u) can constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but the results are conditional on the specific parametrizations (Asysoft vs. Asystiff) and the still-controversial momentum dependence in the isovector channel (Iso-MD), which can mask or mimic stiffness effects.

## Method

The authors employ the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport model to simulate charge-asymmetric nuclear reactions, testing two parametrizations of the symmetry energy: an Asysoft form (flat below saturation density, decreasing at supra-saturation) and an Asystiff form (faster decrease at low density, much stiffer above saturation). For Fermi-energy collisions (Sn+Sn at 35 and 50 MeV/u), they compute imbalance ratios from the N/Z of projectile-like and target-like fragments as a function of kinetic energy loss to study isospin equilibration through the low-density neck region. For central collisions at 30-50 MeV/u, they analyze the correlation between fragment N/Z and kinetic energy to probe isospin distillation. For relativistic collisions (132Sn+124Sn at 400 MeV/u), they examine (n/p) and (3H/3He) yield ratios versus transverse momentum and kinetic energy to test neutron/proton effective mass splitting at high baryon density (up to 1.7-1.8 ρ₀).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 30 MeV/u to 1 GeV/u (Fermi and intermediate energies)
- Sn+Sn collisions simulated at 35 and 50 MeV/u for isospin equilibration studies
- Central 132Sn+124Sn collisions at 400 MeV/u reach baryon densities of about 1.7-1.8 ρ₀ in the interacting zone for a transient time of 15-20 fm/c
- A systematic effect of the symmetry energy on the imbalance ratio of about 20 percent is observed between Asysoft and Asystiff parametrizations
- Larger (negative) slopes in fragment N/Z versus kinetic energy correlations are obtained in the Asystiff case, corresponding to the lower value of the symmetry energy at low density

## Context

This review extends prior work on isospin transport in binary reactions at Fermi energies (Tsang 1992, Isotr 2007) and on isospin distillation in multifragmentation (Baran 2005, Bao 2008, Chomaz 2004). The results for isospin equilibration are compared with experimental data from the INDRA collaboration, which points to a symmetry energy behavior between the Asysoft and Asystiff parametrizations, in agreement with other recent estimates (Betty 2008). The high-density studies enable future constraints on the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, which has direct implications for neutron star properties, and suggest that nucleon/cluster yield ratios and collective flows can disentangle effective mass splitting from symmetry energy stiffness.
