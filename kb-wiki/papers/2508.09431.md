# Extracting temperature of plasma through fusion reactions within a transport approach

> We have investigated the method of extracting the temperature from weighted proton-to-neutron yield ratio from fusion reactions as in the previous experiment~[W. Bang, {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{111}, 055002 (2013).] using the Texas Petawatt laser beam. The Coulomb explosion of deuterium clusters is simulated based on the particle-in-cell model in a box system with periodic boundary conditions, and fusion reactions are incorporated through the stochastic method. As long as the deuteron numbers in deuterium clusters follow a log-normal distribution, the low-energy part of the final deuteron spectrum can be fitted by a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, while there are more deuterons in the intermediate- and high-energy region compared to a thermal distribution, and dominate the weighted yield ratio. Therefore, the effective temperature extracted from the weighted yield ratio is generally higher than that from fitting the final deuteron spectrum. The local density fluctuation, which intrinsically exists due to the log-normal distribution of deuteron numbers, further enhances hot deuteron-deuteron collisions, and significantly affects the weighted yield ratio.

# Extracting temperature of plasma through fusion reactions within a transport approach

## Key claim
The effective temperature extracted from the density-weighted proton-to-neutron yield ratio in laser-induced fusion experiments is generally higher than the temperature obtained by fitting the final deuteron kinetic energy spectrum to a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, because fusion reactions are dominated by intermediate- and high-energy deuterons rather than the low-energy deuterons that constitute the majority of the spectrum. The local density fluctuation arising from the log-normal distribution of deuteron numbers in clusters further enhances hot deuteron-deuteron collisions and significantly affects the weighted yield ratio.

## Method
The authors performed particle-in-cell simulations using the EPOCH code in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, modeling the Coulomb explosion of deuterium clusters whose deuteron numbers follow a log-normal distribution. Fusion reactions for the D(d,n)³He and ³He(d,p)⁴He channels were incorporated via a stochastic method, with hot deuterons from cluster explosion colliding with background cold deuterons and cold ³He. The simulation used 100 events with varying cluster sizes, and the weighted proton-to-neutron yield ratio was compared to thermal model predictions at different temperatures.

## Key numbers
- The low-energy part of the final deuteron spectrum from EPOCH simulations was fitted by a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution with temperature T = 10.95 (+0.06, -0.07) keV.
- The weighted yield ratio of about 0.103 from EPOCH simulations corresponds to an effective temperature of T = 14.03 keV, which is considerably higher than the spectral fit temperature.
- The free Coulomb explosion case with the exact log-normal distribution gave a spectral fit temperature of T = 12.00 keV.
- The thermal model at T = 10.95 keV gives a density weighted yield ratio of about 0.055, while the modified thermal model using the actual EPOCH spectrum gives a ratio of about 0.187 corresponding to T = 17.98 keV.
- The deuteron number density inside clusters was set to ρ₀ = 4.9 × 10¹⁰ μm⁻³, with average deuteron density over the whole box ranging from 10⁷ to 10⁸ μm⁻³.
- Background densities were ρ_D = 2.26 × 10⁷ μm⁻³ for cold deuterons and ρ_³He = 1.50 × 10⁷ μm⁻³ for ³He.
- The grid length for EM field calculation was Δr = 8.81 × 10⁻⁴ μm, with box sizes from (351 Δr)³ to (450 Δr)³.

## Context
This work extends the experimental method of Bang (2013), who used the Texas Petawatt laser to measure plasma temperature from the weighted proton-to-neutron yield ratio in ³He(d,p)⁴He and D(d,n)³He reactions. The authors previously incorporated the D(d,n)³He channel into EPOCH with free boundary conditions (Zhu, 2022). The present study uses periodic boundary conditions and multiple reaction channels to show that the extracted temperature is systematically higher than the spectral fit temperature due to the dominance of non-thermal high-energy deuterons in fusion reactions. This work enables more accurate interpretation of temperature measurements in laser-induced plasma experiments and suggests that larger-scale simulations are needed to quantify the effect of local density fluctuations on the whole plasma system.
