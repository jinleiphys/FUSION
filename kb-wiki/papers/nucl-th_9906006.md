# Historical Survey of the Quasi-Nuclear Baryonium

> We review ideas and speculations concerning possible bound states or resonances of the nucleon--antinucleon system.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the historical development of the quasi-nuclear baryonium concept, where nucleon-antinucleon (NNbar) bound states or resonances are predicted, but notes that the existence and properties of such states remain uncertain due to the strong and poorly understood effects of annihilation, which may eliminate all bound states.

## Method

The author surveys the theoretical evolution of NNbar bound-state ideas, starting from Fermi and Yang (1959) through the bootstrap era of the 1960s, then focusing on the 1970s approach by Shapiro, Dover, and others who treated NNbar states as new meson types near threshold. The review examines the G-parity transformation applied to NN potentials (such as the Paris potential) to derive NNbar potentials, discusses uncertainties in the meson-exchange model, and compares the quasi-nuclear approach with multiquark models by Jaffe (1977) and Chan et al. (1978).

## Key numbers

- The NNbar potential is, on average, deeper than the NN potential because epsilon and omega exchanges, which partially cancel in the NN case, add coherently in the NNbar case.
- For isospin I=0 and natural parity, the predicted rich spectrum includes partial waves such as 3P0, 3S1-3D1, and 3P2-3F2, corresponding to J^PC I^G = 0^{++} 0^{+}, 1^{--} 0^{-}, and 2^{++} 0^{+}, respectively.
- An explicit calculation by Myhrer and Thomas (1976) using a complex optical potential fitting observed cross-sections showed that no NNbar bound state or resonance survives annihilation.
- For "mock-baryonium" with diquark in colour 6 state and antidiquark in colour 6bar state, the predicted width is a few MeV, perhaps.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work by Fermi and Yang (1959) on NNbar attraction and the bootstrap attempts of Ball et al. (1966), while contrasting with the quark-model approach of Jaffe (1977) on q^2 qbar^2 states and the cluster model of Chan et al. (1978) on colour excitations. The paper enables future work on hadronic molecules such as K Kbar, D Dbar, or B B*, where pion-exchange plays a crucial role in binding, and highlights open questions about the role of annihilation in NNbar spectroscopy.
