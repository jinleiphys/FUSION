# Pion Loop Contribution to Rho-Omega Mixing and Mass Splitting

> We study the self-energy amplitudes for the rho-omega meson system produced by an effective field theory model at the quark level characterized by a finite range quark-quark interaction and an isospin breaking bare quark mass difference $m_u - m_d$. Dynamical effects associated with confinement and dressing of quarks and the finite size of the produced $\bar{q}q$ meson modes are treated. Parameters of this approach, previously constrained by soft pion physics, are here supplemented in a minimal way to reproduce $m_ω$ and $g_{ρππ}$. Calculations are carried out up to the one pion loop level and the predictions for rho-omega mixing, mass splitting, the rho width and the symmetry breaking omega-pi-pi coupling constant and form factor are presented. The contribution of the direct $ω\rightarrow ππ$ process relative to the $ω\rightarrow ρ\rightarrow ππ$ process is discussed within this model.

## Key claim

The one-pion-loop contribution to the rho-omega mixing amplitude is found to be significant enough to require an increase of typically 20% in the required mixing amplitude, contradicting the conventional assumption that the direct omega-to-two-pion process cancels out in analyses of electron-positron annihilation data.

## Method

The authors use a Global Color Model (GCM) effective field theory at the quark level with a finite-range quark-quark interaction and isospin breaking from the bare quark mass difference m_u - m_d. They first integrate out quarks to produce an effective meson action for pi, rho, and omega fields with dressed quark propagators that incorporate confinement through the absence of mass-shell poles, then integrate out pions to one-loop order to compute the self-energy amplitudes for the rho-omega system.

## Key numbers

- The range parameter a = 0.194 GeV^2 for the vector meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude Gamma_V is chosen to reproduce g_{rho pi pi} = 6.05, as inferred from the experimental rho -> pi pi decay width of 151 MeV
- The calculated omega_I pi pi coupling constant g_{omega_I pi pi} = 0.105 at the triple on-mass-shell point, which is almost five times larger than the value 0.0236 obtained in a recent extended NJL model study
- The experimental 2-pi width of the physical omega is 0.185 MeV, implying g_{omega pi pi}^{expt} = 0.21
- The quark propagator parameters yield f_pi = 90.1 MeV, m_pi = 143 MeV, and the vacuum condensate <q-bar q> = (173 MeV)^3, with lambda = 0.889 GeV, m_av = 16 MeV, and c_0 = 0.581
- The isospin-breaking parameter c_1 = 0.0048 for delta m = -4 MeV, representing a 1% effect

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of rho-omega mixing limited to the quark-loop level (Mitchell and Tandy, 1995) by adding one-pion-loop corrections, and contrasts with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model approach of Refs. [FR95] and [SS96] which used a contact interaction without confinement. The results enable improved extraction of the physical rho-omega mixing amplitude from e+e- -> pi pi data, addressing the concern raised by Maltman, O'Connell, and Williams (1996) that the direct omega -> pi pi contribution may introduce larger errors than previously assumed.
