# Effect of kinematics on final state interactions in (e,e'p) reactions

> Recent data from experiment E97-006 at TJNAF using the 12C(e,e'p) reaction at very large missing energies and momenta are compared to a calculation of two-step rescattering. A comparison between parallel and perpendicular kinematics suggests that the effects of final state interactions can be strongly reduced in the former case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final state interactions (FSI) in (e,e'p) reactions at large missing energies and momenta can be strongly reduced by choosing parallel kinematics (where the momentum transfer q and the initial nucleon momentum p_i are nearly collinear), but this reduction is conditional on the nucleus being light and the missing energy remaining below the pion production threshold near 150 MeV.

## Method

The authors compare experimental data from the E97-006 experiment at Jefferson Lab on 12C(e,e'p) with a semiclassical calculation of two-step rescattering processes, where an electron knocks out a nucleon which then scatters from another nucleon in the medium before being detected. The calculation uses a model spectral function that combines a mean-field part from local density approximation with a Wood-Saxon potential and a correlated part parameterized as a Lorentzian energy distribution fitted to the parallel kinematics data, and evaluates the semi-exclusive cross section for three rescattering channels (proton-proton, proton-neutron, and neutron-proton).

## Key numbers

- The total number of protons predicted by the fitted spectral function for 12C is 5.97
- The mean-field region extends up to a momentum of approximately 250 MeV/c
- The transition between mean-field and correlated parts of the spectral function occurs at k > 230 MeV/c and E > 19 MeV
- At p_m = 600 MeV/c in perpendicular kinematics, the calculated cross section is 50% larger than the direct process, while the experimental data is about one order of magnitude above
- The deviation between experiment and direct signal starts sharply at the pion production threshold, E_m approximately 150 MeV
- For perpendicular kinematics at p_m less than 350 MeV/c and E_m approximately 50 MeV, the calculation can account for the differences between parallel and perpendicular kinematics

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of two-step rescattering developed by Barbieri and Lapikas (2004) and compares it directly with experimental data from the E97-006 collaboration (Daniela, 2004; Frick, 2004). The results confirm earlier suggestions by Vivian (1995) and Ryckebusch (2003) that multiple rescattering contributions are relatively small in light nuclei and parallel kinematics, and support the expectation from the E97-006 proposal that parallel kinematics offer the best chance for identifying short-range correlations. The paper enables future work to include additional transverse contributions such as meson exchange currents and Delta resonance excitations to improve agreement with perpendicular kinematics data.
