# Electromagnetic transitions between giant resonances within a continuum-RPA approach

> A general continuum-RPA approach is developed to describe electromagnetic transitions between giant resonances. Using a diagrammatic representation for the three-point Green's function, an expression for the transition amplitude is derived which allows one to incorporate effects of mixing of single and double giant resonances as well as to take the entire basis of particle-hole states into consideration. The radiative widths for E1 transition between the charge-exchange spin-dipole giant resonance and Gamow-Teller states are calculated for ^{90}Nb and ^{208}Bi nuclei. The importance of the mixing is stressed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a continuum-RPA approach for electromagnetic transitions between giant resonances that incorporates mixing of single and double giant resonances, and finds that including virtual excitation of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) can drastically change the calculated radiative widths, either suppressing or enhancing them depending on the transition energy relative to the GDR energy.

## Method

The authors use a diagrammatic representation of the three-point Green's function to derive an expression for the transition amplitude between RPA states. They replace the bare electromagnetic operator with an effective field that accounts for virtual excitation of the GDR, and evaluate the resulting amplitude using a spectral decomposition of the single-particle Green's function to include the continuum. The approach is applied to calculate E1 radiative widths for transitions between charge-exchange spin-dipole resonance (SDR) states and Gamow-Teller (GT) states in ⁹⁰Nb and ²⁰⁸Bi, using a zero-range Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction and a phenomenological mean field.

## Key numbers

- In ⁹⁰Nb, the radiative width for SDR (J^π=0^-) → GTR (E_GT=8.9 MeV) is Γ_γ⁰=3.40 keV without GDR mixing and Γ_γ=3.68 keV with GDR mixing; the branching ratio changes from 8.5×10⁻⁴ to 9.2×10⁻⁴.
- In ⁹⁰Nb, for SDR (J^π=0^-) → low-lying GT state (E_GT=0.0 MeV), the width increases from Γ_γ⁰=0.21 keV to Γ_γ=1.62 keV when GDR mixing is included, with branching ratio rising from 0.5×10⁻⁴ to 4.0×10⁻⁴.
- In ²⁰⁸Bi, for SDR (J^π=0^-) → GTR (E_GT=15.5 MeV), the width increases from Γ_γ⁰=2.28 keV to Γ_γ=6.49 keV, with branching ratio rising from 4.9×10⁻⁴ to 13.8×10⁻⁴.
- In ²⁰⁸Bi, for the E1 transition from GTR to a 2.9 MeV 2^- SD state, the branching ratio is b_γ=4.3×10⁻³, the largest found, because the transition energy almost exactly coincides with the GDR energy.
- The SDR states in ²⁰⁸Bi lie at (26.6, 24.1) MeV for J^π=(0^-,1^-) and at (20.0, 21.3, 23.1) MeV for J^π=2^-; in ⁹⁰Nb at (22.4, 20.1) MeV for (0^-,1^-) and (13.5, 14.4, 17.4) MeV for 2^-.
- The mean doorway-state spreading width used is Γ^↓=4.7 MeV for ²⁰⁸Bi and Γ^↓=4.0 MeV for ⁹⁰Nb.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDA and RPA calculations of radiative transitions between giant resonances (Van Giai 1995, Sagawa 2000, Rodin 2000) that only considered particle-hole structure and strongly underestimated the corresponding sum rules. The key advance is the inclusion of double-phonon admixtures (mixing of single and double giant resonances) via the virtual excitation of the GDR, following the approach used by Ponomarev (1998) for two-phonon states. The results enable interpretation of upcoming (³He,tγ) experiments on ²⁰⁸Pb (Krasznahorkay 2002), where gamma transitions to low-lying GT states have been observed with branching ratios of order 10⁻³.
