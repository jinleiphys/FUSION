# Perturbative treatment of three-nucleon force contact terms in three-nucleon Faddeev equations

> We present a perturbative approach to solving the three-nucleon continuum Faddeev equation. This approach is particularly well suited to dealing with variable strengths of contact terms in a chiral three-nucleon force. We use examples of observables in the elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering as well as in the deuteron breakup reaction to demonstrate high precision of the proposed procedure and its capability to reproduce exact results. A significant reduction of computer time achieved by the perturbative approach in comparison to exact treatment makes this approach valuable for fine-tuning of the three-nucleon Hamiltonian parameters.

# Perturbative treatment of three-nucleon force contact terms in three-nucleon Faddeev equations

## Key claim

The authors propose a perturbative approach to solving the three-nucleon continuum Faddeev equation that treats the contact terms of a chiral three-nucleon force as small perturbations, achieving approximately 1% accuracy for elastic scattering observables and 2-4% accuracy for breakup cross sections in certain kinematic regions, while reducing computation time by a factor of about four. The method is specifically validated for the case where the contact terms are short-ranged and act only in partial waves with low angular momenta, with the caveat that precision degrades at higher energies (190 MeV) in the final-state-interaction peak region.

## Method

The authors split the three-nucleon force into a parameter-free term (with all contact strengths set to zero) and a sum of contact terms with variable strengths. They solve the Faddeev equation exactly for the parameter-free term once, then solve a reduced set of equations only for the partial wave states (the "beta" set) where the contact terms have nonvanishing matrix elements, using two choices for the beta set: one comprising only the 2N subsystem states 1S0 + 3S1-3D1 (20 partial waves) and another including all 2N states with total angular momentum j ≤ 2 (34 partial waves), compared to the full set of 142 partial waves. The calculations were performed using the SMS N4LO+ chiral potential of the Bochum-Bonn group with cutoff Λ = 450 MeV, combined with a chiral N2LO 3NF augmented by one contact term (E7) from N4LO.

## Key numbers

- Computation time reduction factor: approximately 4 (from 142 partial waves to 34 or 20 partial waves in the perturbative treatment)
- Exact solution requires approximately 30 minutes on a personal computer per run
- Elastic scattering observables reproduced with approximately 1% accuracy
- Breakup cross sections at 190 MeV in the FSI peak region reproduced with 2-4% accuracy
- Calculations performed at two incoming neutron energies: E = 70 MeV and E = 190 MeV
- Contact strength parameters used: cD = -8.2053, cE = -1.0019, cE7 = 2.0
- 3NF partial wave states required for convergence: up to 2N angular momentum jmax = 5 and 3N total angular momentum J = 7/2, resulting in 142 partial waves in the full calculation

## Context

This work extends the program of the Low Energy Nuclear Physics International Collaboration (LENPIC) to apply consistent chiral two- and three-body forces in three-nucleon continuum calculations. It addresses the computational bottleneck identified in earlier work by Witała et al. (2001) and Kuros-Zołnierczuk et al. (2002), where fine-tuning of the 3N Hamiltonian parameters cD and cE requires repeated solutions of the Faddeev equations for many parameter values. The perturbative approach provides an emulator that enables systematic fitting of contact term strengths to elastic Nd scattering data (particularly the cross section minimum near Elab ≈ 70 MeV) and the triton binding energy, following the correlation line method established in earlier work by Witała et al. (1998). The method is designed to handle the increasing number of contact terms at higher chiral orders (13 short-range operators at N4LO, as worked out by Girlanda et al. (2011)) and enables future systematic optimization of 3N Hamiltonian parameters against available scattering data.
