# Deeply-bound $K^- pp$ state in the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$, $n$) spectrum and its moving pole near the $πΣN$ threshold

> The formation of a deeply-bound $K^- pp$ state with $I=1/2$, $J^π=0^-$ by the $^3$He(in-flight $K^-$, $n$) reaction is theoretically investigated in a distorted-wave impulse approximation using the Green's function method. The expected inclusive and semi-exclusive spectra at $p_{K^-} = 1.0$ GeV/c and $θ_{\rm lab} = 0^{\circ}$ are calculated for the forthcoming J-PARC E15 experiment. We demonstrate these spectra with several types of phenomenological $K^-$-``$pp$'' optical potentials $U^{\rm opt}(E)$ which have an energy-dependent imaginary part multiplied by a phase space suppression factor, fitting to recent theoretical predictions or experimental candidates of the $K^-pp$ bound state. The results show that a cusp-like peak at the $πΣN$ threshold is an unique signal for the $K^-pp$ bound state in the spectrum including the [$K^-pp$] $\to$ $Y + N$ decay process from the two-nucleon $K^-$ absorption, as well as a distinct peak of the $K^-pp$ bound state. The shape of the spectrum is explained by a trajectory of a moving pole of the $K^-pp$ bound state in the complex energy plane. The importance of the spectrum with [$K^-pp$] $\to$ $Y + N$ from the two-nucleon $K^-$ absorption is emphasized in order to extract clear evidence of the $K^-pp$ bound state.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a cusp-like peak at the πΣN threshold, rather than a distinct bound-state peak, is the unique signal for a deeply-bound K⁻pp state in the ³He(in-flight K⁻, n) spectrum when the binding energy is large enough that the πΣN decay channel is kinematically near-closure, and this spectral shape is governed by the trajectory of a moving pole in the complex energy plane.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using the Green's function method for the ³He(in-flight K⁻, n) reaction at p_K⁻ = 1.0 GeV/c and θ_lab = 0°. They constructed several phenomenological K⁻-"pp" optical potentials U^opt(E) with an energy-dependent imaginary part multiplied by phase-space suppression factors for one-nucleon (πYN) and two-nucleon (YN) K⁻ absorption channels, fitting the potentials to reproduce binding energies and widths from prior theoretical predictions or experimental candidates. The Klein-Gordon equation was solved self-consistently in the complex energy plane to obtain the strength function S(E), which was decomposed into conversion and escape components to generate inclusive and semi-exclusive spectra.

## Key numbers

- Incident K⁻ momentum: p_K⁻ = 1.0 GeV/c, forward angle θ_lab = 0°
- Fermi-averaged cross section for the isoscalar ΔI=0 transition: 16.4 mb/sr
- For potential C (fitted to Shevchenko et al. predictions): binding energy B.E. = 59 MeV, width Γ = 164 MeV
- For potential B (fitted to Yamazaki and Akaishi predictions): B.E. = 45 MeV, Γ = 82 MeV
- For potential A (fitted to Dote, Hyodo and Weise predictions): B.E. = 15 MeV, Γ = 92 MeV
- πΣN threshold energy: E_th(πΣN) = -101 MeV
- πΛN threshold energy: E_th(πΛN) = -181 MeV
- Range parameter for the optical potential: b = 1.09 fm
- Size parameter for ³He wave function: b_N = 1.30 fm, reproducing r.m.s. charge radius of 1.94 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DWIA study (Koike and Harada, 2007) by introducing energy-dependent optical potentials with phase-space suppression factors, following the approach of Mares et al. (2005) for threshold effects. It directly supports the J-PARC E15 experiment proposed by Iwasaki et al. (2006) and contrasts with calculations for heavier p-shell targets (Kishimoto, 2005; Yamagata et al., 2006) where signals are obscured by broad widths. The results enable experimental distinction between competing theoretical predictions, such as those from Yamazaki and Akaishi (2002), Shevchenko, Gal and Mares (2007), and Dote, Hyodo and Weise (2008), as well as experimental candidates from FINUDA (2005), OBELIX (2007), and DISTO (2008).
