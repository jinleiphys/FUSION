# Transverse polarization of Lambda hyperons in hadronic collisions

> The transverse polarization of $Λ$ hyperon within reconstructed jets in hadronic collisions offers a complementary platform to probe the polarized fragmentation function $D_{1T}^\perp$. We illustrate that by performing a global analysis of the transverse polarization of $Λ$ hyperons produced in different kinematic regions and in different hadronic collisions, such as $pp$, $p\bar p$, $pA$, and $γA$ collisions, we can pin down the flavor dependence of $D_{1T}^\perp$ which has been poorly constrained. Besides the single inclusive jet production, the $γ/Z^0$-boson associated jet production supplements with more capability in removing ambiguities in the flavor dependence of $D_{1T}^\perp$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a global analysis of Lambda hyperon transverse polarization across different hadronic collision systems (pp, pbar-p, pA, gamma-A) and kinematic regions can determine the flavor dependence of the polarized fragmentation function D1T^perp, which remains poorly constrained; however, the analysis is performed at leading order in perturbative QCD, and the authors acknowledge that a more accurate next-to-leading order global analysis should be left for future studies when more experimental data become available.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse polarization of Lambda hyperons within reconstructed jets using the hadron-within-jet framework at leading order in perturbative QCD, identifying TMD jet fragmentation functions with TMD parton fragmentation functions. They employ the DSV parametrization for unpolarized Lambda fragmentation functions and primarily the isospin-symmetric CLPSW parametrization for the polarized D1T^perp, with a Gaussian ansatz for the transverse momentum distribution. The production ratios of different flavor jets are computed using CT18 parton distribution functions (and EPPS21 for nuclear-modified PDFs) for single inclusive jet production and gamma/Z0-boson associated jet production in pp, pbar-p, pA, and gamma-A collisions.

## Key numbers

- Lambda transverse polarization in gamma/Z0-boson associated jet production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV can reach as large as 10-20%
- Production ratios in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV show valence quark contribution increases with increasing jet kT and rapidity, while gluon contribution decreases
- In pbar-p collisions at sqrt(s)=1.96 TeV, forward-backward asymmetry enables separation of u and anti-u quark fragmentation functions
- In pA collisions, production ratios show more u quark jets in proton-going direction and more d quark jets in nucleus-going direction at energies including sqrt(s)=200 GeV (pAu), 2.76 TeV (pPb), and 5.02 TeV (pPb)
- For photon-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV with kT=200 GeV, gluon contribution is very important in backward rapidity but becomes negligible in forward rapidity

## Context

This work extends the study of Lambda transverse polarization from e+e- annihilation measurements by the Belle collaboration (2018) and subsequent phenomenological studies (Anselmino 2019, D'Alesio 2020, Callos 2020, Chen 2021) into hadronic collisions using the hadron-within-jet framework developed by Yuan (2007), Bain (2016), and Kang (2016-2023). The paper addresses the unresolved issue of whether isospin symmetry of the polarized fragmentation function is violated, which previous studies (Chen 2021, D'Alesio 2022, D'Alesio 2023) could not settle with Belle data alone. The results enable future global analyses using data from currently operating hadron colliders (RHIC, LHC, Tevatron) to extract the gluon polarized fragmentation function and test isospin symmetry, complementing expected measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
