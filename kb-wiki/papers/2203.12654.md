# Impact of event activity variable on the ratio of observables in isobar collisions

> The STAR isobar data of $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}= 200$ GeV show that ratios of observables ($R_{\mathcal{O}}$) such as the multiplicity distribution, $p(N_{\mathrm{ch}})$, and the harmonic flow, $v_n$, deviate from unity, when presented as a function of centrality, $c$. These deviations have been attributed to the differences in the shape and radial profiles between $^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr nuclei. In addition, the ratios $R_{\mathcal{O}}(x)$ depend on the choice of the event activity variable $x$, which could be either $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ or centrality. We estimate the difference $ΔR$ between these two choices, based on the published $p(N_{\mathrm{ch}})$, as well as those from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with varied nuclear structure parameters: nuclear radius ($R_0$), surface diffuseness ($a_0$), quadrupole deformation ($β_2$), and octupole deformation ($β_3$). In contrary to $R_{v_n}(c)$, $R_{v_n}(N_{\mathrm{ch}})$ is nearly independent of the analysis approaches, suggesting that nonflow effects are better controlled by $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ than $c$. The ratios of observables sensitive to the chiral magnetic effect (CME) are also much closer to unity for $x=N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ than $x=c$, indicating that the ratios calculated at the same $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ provide a better baseline for the non-CME background. According to the AMPT results, the dominant parameter for $ΔR$ is $a_0$, while $R_0$ and $β_n$ are only important in central collisions. The published $p(N_{\mathrm{ch}})$ is also used to estimate $ΔR_{\left\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\right\rangle}$ for mean transverse momentum, which is non-negligible compared with $R_{\left\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\right\rangle}-1$.

## Key claim

The ratio of observables between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions depends on whether the event activity variable is charged-particle multiplicity ($N_{\mathrm{ch}}$) or centrality ($c$), and this dependence is dominated by the difference in surface diffuseness ($a_0$) between the two nuclei, not by the deformations or radius differences that the abstract emphasizes.

## Method

The authors used published STAR data at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV to compute the difference $\Delta R_{\mathcal{O}}$ between observable ratios at matching $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ and at matching centrality, using a first-order approximation based on the derivative of the observable with respect to the event activity variable. They also performed AMPT model calculations with varied Woods-Saxon parameters (radius $R_0$, surface diffuseness $a_0$, quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$, and octupole deformation $\beta_3$) to isolate the contribution of each nuclear structure parameter to $\Delta R_{\mathcal{O}}$. For mean transverse momentum and its fluctuations, the multiplicity dependence was taken from Au+Au data at the same energy.

- The surface diffuseness values are $a_0 = 0.46$ fm for $^{96}$Ru and $a_0 = 0.52$ fm for $^{96}$Zr (Table 1).
- The half-density radii are $R_0 = 5.09$ fm for $^{96}$Ru and $R_0 = 5.02$ fm for $^{96}$Zr (Table 1).
- The quadrupole deformation is $\beta_2 = 0.162$ for $^{96}$Ru and $\beta_2 = 0.06$ for $^{96}$Zr; the octupole deformation is $\beta_3 = 0$ for $^{96}$Ru and $\beta_3 = 0.20$ for $^{96}$Zr (Table 1).
- For mean transverse momentum, $\Delta R_{\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\rangle}$ reaches $\sim 0.0005$ at $c = 0.2$, $\sim 0.002$ at $c = 0.5$, and larger values in more peripheral collisions; this is compared to the predicted signal $R_{\langle p_{\mathrm{T}}\rangle} - 1$ of $\sim 0.003$ at $c = 0.2$ and $\sim 0.005$ at $c = 0.5$.
- For scaled-variance of $p_{\mathrm{T}}$, $\Delta R$ is $\sim -0.01$ at $c = 0.2$ and $\sim -0.03$ at $c = 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the STAR isobar collision analysis (STAR Collaboration, 2021) which found significant deviations from unity in observable ratios between $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions, attributed to nuclear structure differences. It builds on prior AMPT model studies (Jia, 2021) that showed how Woods-Saxon parameters affect the multiplicity distribution. The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear structure information from isobar collisions and provide a better baseline for chiral magnetic effect searches by showing that ratios at matching $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ control nonflow and non-CME backgrounds more effectively than ratios at matching centrality.
