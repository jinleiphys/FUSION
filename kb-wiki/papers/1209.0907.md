# Charm and Strangeness with Heavy-Quark Spin Symmetry

> We study charmed and strange baryon resonances that are generated dynamically within a unitary meson-baryon coupled-channel model which incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. This is accomplished by extending the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry and implementing a strong flavor symmetry breaking. The model generates dynamically resonances with negative parity in all the isospin, spin, and strange and charm sectors that one can form from an s-wave interaction between pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ baryons. Our results are compared with experimental data from several facilities as well as with other theoretical models. Moreover, we obtain the properties of charmed pseudoscalar and vector mesons in dense matter within this coupled-channel unitary effective model by taking into account Pauli-blocking effects and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. We obtain the open-charm meson spectral functions in this dense nuclear environment, and discuss their implications on the formation of $D$-mesic nuclei at FAIR energies.

## Key claim

The authors extend the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry to incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) into a coupled-channel model for charmed and strange baryon resonances, but the model's predictions are only semiqualitative because exact SU(8) symmetry is severely broken in nature and must be broken via a deformation of mass and decay constant parameters.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary meson-baryon coupled-channel model by extending the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry, which includes pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets together with 1/2+ and 3/2+ baryons. They implement strong flavor symmetry breaking through a deformation of mass and decay constant parameters following the chain SU(8) to SU(6) to SU(3) to SU(2). The on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in coupled channels using the interaction matrix as kernel, with the loop function regularized by subtraction point regularization. For the in-medium part, they compute D and D* meson self-energies self-consistently by summing transition amplitudes over the nucleonic Fermi sea, incorporating Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies in the intermediate propagators.

## Key numbers

- The three lowest-lying Lambda_c states from the most attractive SU(8) representations appear with masses around 2618.8 MeV (assigned to Lambda_c(2595)), 2617 MeV (broad, coupling to Sigma_c pi), and 2828 MeV (no known experimental counterpart)
- One spin-3/2 Lambda_c resonance is found at (2666.6 - i 26.7) MeV, assigned to the experimental Lambda_c(2625)
- Three Sigma_c resonances for C=1, S=0, I=1, J=1/2 have masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV with widths 0.8, 188.0, and 87.0 MeV respectively
- One spin-3/2 Sigma_c resonance appears as a bound state at 2568.4 MeV, and a second at (2692.9 - i 33.5) MeV
- The D meson quasiparticle peak mixes strongly with Sigma_c(2823)N^{-1} and Sigma_c(2868)N^{-1} states, while the Lambda_c(2595)N^{-1} is visible in the low-energy tail
- For anti-D mesons, a bound state exists at 2805 MeV, almost at anti-D N threshold

## Context

This work extends the SU(6) approach developed in the light sector by Garcia-Recio, Nieves, and Salcedo (2006) and Toki et al. (2007) to include charm quarks while respecting heavy-quark spin symmetry, which was absent in earlier coupled-channel models such as those by Tolos, Schaffner-Bielich, and Mishra (2004), Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2004), Hofmann and Lutz (2005, 2006), and Mizutani and Ramos (2006). The results enable predictions for D-mesic nuclei that could be tested at the PANDA and CBM experiments at the FAIR facility, though the authors note that experimental detection of D and anti-D meson bound states is a challenging task due to low production rates and large widths compared to level separation.
