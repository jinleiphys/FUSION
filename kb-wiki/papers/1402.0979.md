# Isovector potential of $Σ$ in nuclei and neutron star matter

> We determine the coupling constants of $Σ$ hyperon with mesons in relativistic mean field (RMF) models using $Σ^-$ atomic shift data and examine the effects of $Σ$ on the neutron star maximum mass. We find that we need to reduce the vector-isovector meson coupling with $Σ$ ($g_{ρΣ}$) from the value constrained by the SU(3)v symmetry in order to explain the $Σ^-$ atomic shifts for light symmetric and heavy asymmetric nuclei simultaneously. With the atomic shift fit value of $g_{ρΣ}$, $Σ^-$ can emerge in neutron star matter overcoming the repulsive isoscalar potential for $Σ$ hyperons. Admixture of $Σ^-$ in neutron stars is found to reduce the neutron star maximum mass slightly.

## Key claim

The authors find that the vector-isovector meson coupling with Σ (gρΣ) must be reduced from the value predicted by flavor SU(3) symmetry to simultaneously explain Σ- atomic shift data for both light symmetric and heavy asymmetric nuclei; with this reduced coupling, Σ- can appear in neutron star matter despite the repulsive isoscalar potential, but the admixture only slightly reduces the neutron star maximum mass.

## Method

The authors embed Σ and Λ hyperons into four relativistic mean field (RMF) models for normal nuclei (NL1, NL-SH, TM1, and SCL2) by fitting Λ separation energies in single-Λ hypernuclei, the ΛΛ bond energy in 6ΛΛHe, and Σ- atomic shift data for light symmetric and heavy asymmetric nuclei. They fix the isoscalar Σ couplings by fitting atomic shifts on light symmetric nuclei, then determine gρΣ by fitting shifts on heavier isospin-asymmetric nuclei (W and Pb), and finally compute the neutron star equation of state under charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- The ΛΛ bond energy used as a constraint is ΔBΛΛ = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV from the NAGARA event in 6ΛΛHe.
- The extrapolated Λ potential depth in nuclear matter is UΛ ≈ -28 MeV from the fit to separation energies.
- The fitted gρΣ values are: 2.48 (NL1), 1.85 (NL-SH), 1.87 (TM1), and 1.67 (SCL2), compared to the SU(3) symmetric value gρΣ = 2 gρN.
- The Σ- Schrödinger-equivalent potential height in nuclear medium is in the range of 20 to 30 MeV across all employed RMF models.
- The symmetry energy of Σ is reduced from around 15 MeV with SU(3) values to around 3 MeV with the atomic shift fit values in all employed RMF models.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Mares, Friedman, Gal, and Jennings (1995), who first obtained gρΣ/gρN ≈ 2/3 from Si and Pb Σ- atomic shifts, and the authors' previous SCL3 model which gave a ratio of 0.434. The study contradicts the naive quark counting and SU(3) symmetric coupling predictions that would give gρΣ ≈ 2 gρN, and it addresses the "two-solar-mass neutron star puzzle" by testing whether hyperonic equations of state can support such masses when Σ couplings are constrained by atomic shift data. The results enable more realistic neutron star matter calculations that incorporate hypernuclear and exotic atom constraints, and they suggest that three-baryon repulsion or other mechanisms beyond the present RMF framework may be needed to explain the observed two-solar-mass neutron stars.
