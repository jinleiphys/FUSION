# Quark Recombination and Heavy Quark Diffusion in Hot Nuclear Matter

> We discuss resonance recombination for quarks and show that it is compatible with quark and hadron distributions in local thermal equilibrium. We then calculate realistic heavy quark phase space distributions in heavy ion collisions using Langevin simulations with non-perturbative T-matrix interactions in hydrodynamic backgrounds. We hadronize the heavy quarks on the critical hypersurface given by hydrodynamics after constructing a criterion for the relative recombination and fragmentation contributions. We discuss the influence of recombination and flow on the resulting heavy meson and single electron R_AA and elliptic flow. We will also comment on the effect of diffusion of open heavy flavor mesons in the hadronic phase.

# Quark Recombination and Heavy Quark Diffusion in Hot Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
Resonance recombination is compatible with quark and hadron distributions in local thermal equilibrium, meaning that any quark-number or kinetic energy scaling at low hadron-pT is *not* due to quark recombination but can be mimicked by realistic flow fields and freeze-out hierarchy. The formalism extends resonance recombination to quark distributions away from local thermal equilibrium by combining Langevin simulations with non-perturbative T-matrix interactions.

## Method
The authors used relativistic Langevin simulations with non-perturbative T-matrix results for heavy-light quark interactions to model heavy quark diffusion through a hydrodynamic QGP background (using the 2+1-D boost-invariant hydro code AZHYDRO). Heavy quarks were hadronized on the critical hypersurface by constructing a coalescence probability from the resonant scattering rate in the color-singlet channel at Tc, with a time interval of 2 fm/c, and applying either resonance recombination or fragmentation depending on that probability.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark quenching of about 60% at large momenta in semi-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, with an excess at small pT due to heavy quark conservation
- Suppression for bottom quarks is significantly smaller than for charm quarks
- Resonant interactions in the T-matrix approach persist up to approximately 1.5 Tc
- The coalescence probability uses a time interval of Δτ_res = 2 fm/c, motivated by being well below the duration of the mixed phase in AZHYDRO
- Expected increase in elliptic flow of about 20-30% in the hadronic phase

## Context
This work extends the resonance recombination model (RRM) developed by Ravagli et al. (2007, 2008) based on Boltzmann equations for quarks and antiquarks scattering through resonances, which the authors had previously confirmed numerically for blastwaves (He et al., 2010) and hydrodynamic hypersurfaces (He et al., 2011). It also builds on non-perturbative T-matrix results for heavy-light quark interactions by Riek and Rapp (2010). The approach enables a consistent treatment of heavy quark diffusion and hadronization in strongly coupled QGP, with the same non-perturbative dynamics governing both in-medium interactions and the coalescence process.
