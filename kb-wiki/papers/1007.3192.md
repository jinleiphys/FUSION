# Empirical predictions of yrast energies in even-even nuclei

> The lowest excitation energies of the given multipole J^pi state (the J^pi yrast energies) are given for even-even nuclei throughout the entire periodic table. The yrast energies were calculated using the recently proposed empirical formula that depends only on the mass number A, and the valence nucleon numbers Np and Nn. We provide a complete tabulation and plots of the yrast energies calculated using the empirical formula together with the ones measured for the natural parity states up to 10+ and for the unnatural parity states up to 10^+ with the hope of encouraging active study on the possible origin of the relationship between the yrast energies, as revealed by the empirical formula.

# Empirical predictions of yrast energies in even-even nuclei

## Key claim
The paper presents a complete tabulation of yrast energies for even-even nuclei across the entire periodic table, calculated using an empirical formula that depends only on mass number A and valence nucleon numbers Np and Nn, but the authors note that the tabulation includes both measured values and calculated values (shown as measured/calculated pairs), with the hope of encouraging study into the possible origin of the relationship rather than claiming definitive predictive accuracy.

## Method
The authors applied a recently proposed empirical formula depending on mass number A, valence proton number Np, and valence neutron number Nn to calculate yrast excitation energies for natural parity states up to 10+ and unnatural parity states up to 10+ in even-even nuclei. They compiled the results into a long table showing both the measured experimental energies and the empirically calculated energies (separated by a slash) for each nuclide, organized by element and isotope.

## Key numbers
- 4He: measured 2+ yrast energy = 27.42 MeV, empirical calculation = 12.67 MeV (largest discrepancy in the table)
- 100Sr: measured 2+ yrast energy = 0.129 MeV, empirical calculation = 0.196 MeV (one of the lowest energies)
- 152Ce: measured 2+ yrast energy = 0.081 MeV, empirical calculation = 0.151 MeV
- 156Nd: measured 2+ yrast energy = 0.066 MeV, empirical calculation = 0.114 MeV
- 160Gd: measured 2+ yrast energy = 0.070 MeV, empirical calculation = 0.095 MeV
- 66Zn: measured 10+ yrast energy = 6.292 MeV, empirical calculation = 6.332 MeV (close agreement at high spin)
- 68Ge: measured 10+ yrast energy = 5.962 MeV, empirical calculation = 5.815 MeV

## Context
This work extends the empirical approach to yrast energies proposed in recent work by the same or related authors, providing a comprehensive tabulation that covers the entire periodic table from helium to gadolinium. The systematic comparison of measured and calculated values across many isotopes enables future studies to identify regions where the simple empirical formula works well (such as near mid-shell deformed nuclei) and where it fails (such as light nuclei like 4He), potentially guiding theoretical work on the microscopic origin of the observed regularities in nuclear yrast spectra.
