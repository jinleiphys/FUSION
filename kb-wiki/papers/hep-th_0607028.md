# The Skyrmion strikes back: baryons and a new large $N_c$ limit

> In the large $N_c$ limit of QCD, baryons can be modeled as solitons, for instance, as Skyrmions. This modeling has been justified by Witten's demonstration that all properties of baryons and mesons scale with $N_c^{-1/2}$ in the same way as the analogous meson-based soliton model scales with a generic meson-meson coupling constant $g$. An alternative large $N_c$ limit (the orientifold large $N_c$ limit) has recently been proposed in which quarks transform in the two-index antisymmetric representation of $SU(N_c)$. By carrying out the analog of Witten's analysis for the new orientifold large $N_c$ limit, we show that baryons and solitons can also be identified in the orientifold large $N_c$ limit. However, in the orientifold large $N_c$ limit, the interaction amplitudes and matrix elements scale with $N_c^{-1}$ in the same way as soliton models scale with the generic meson coupling constant $g$ rather than as $N_c^{-1/2}$ as in the traditional large $N_c$ limit.

## Key claim

The authors show that baryons can be identified as Skyrmions in the orientifold large $N_c$ limit (OLNC) of QCD, but with the crucial difference that interaction amplitudes and matrix elements scale with $N_c^{-1}$ rather than $N_c^{-1/2}$ as in the traditional large $N_c$ (TLNC) limit, provided one accounts for the fact that baryons contain $N_c(N_c-1)/2 \sim N_c^2$ quarks and that gluon exchange between quarks sharing a color index is combinatorially restricted.

## Method

The authors generalize Witten's diagrammatic analysis for the traditional large $N_c$ limit to the orientifold large $N_c$ limit, where quarks transform in the two-index antisymmetric representation of $SU(N_c)$. They examine representative Feynman diagrams and color-flow diagrams for baryon mass, meson-baryon couplings, baryon-meson scattering amplitudes, and baryon-baryon couplings, counting powers of $N_c$ from vertices, color loops, and combinatoric factors, while accounting for the restriction that one-gluon exchange in a baryon is only allowed between quarks sharing at least one color index.

## Key numbers

* Baryon mass scales as $N_c^2$ in the OLNC limit, consistent with the Skyrmion mass scaling as $N_c^2$ from $f_\pi \sim N_c^1$ and $\epsilon \sim N_c^1$ in the Skyrme Lagrangian.
* Meson creation amplitude (including $f_\pi$) scales as $N_c^{1/2}$ in the TLNC limit and as $N_c^1$ in the OLNC limit.
* Three-meson vertex scales as $N_c^{-1/2}$ in the TLNC limit and as $N_c^{-1}$ in the OLNC limit.
* Meson decay width scales as $N_c^{-1}$ in the TLNC limit and as $N_c^{-2}$ in the OLNC limit.
* One-gluon exchange contribution to baryon mass scales as $N_c^1$ in the TLNC limit and as $N_c^2$ in the OLNC limit (not $N_c^3$ as a naive counting would suggest).
* Meson-baryon coupling scales as $N_c^1$ in the OLNC limit.
* Baryon-meson scattering amplitude scales as $N_c^0$ in the OLNC limit.
* Baryon-meson scattering to a two-meson final state scales as $N_c^{-1}$ in the OLNC limit.
* Baryon-baryon coupling scales as $N_c^2$ in the OLNC limit.

## Context

This work extends Bolognesi's observation (2005) that baryons in the orientifold large $N_c$ limit contain $N_c(N_c-1)/2$ quarks and thus have mass scaling $N_c^2$, resolving an apparent contradiction with Skyrmion mass scaling. The authors complete the program of establishing a Skyrmionic description of baryons in the OLNC limit by providing a diagrammatic analysis analogous to Witten's original demonstration (1979) for the traditional large $N_c$ limit. The OLNC limit, proposed by Armoni, Shifman, and Veneziano (2003-2005), has the advantage that quark loops are not suppressed, so it does not impose the OZI rule and can accommodate violations such as the $\eta'-\pi$ mass difference without requiring large $1/N_c$ corrections. The results enable a consistent soliton description of baryons in this alternative large $N_c$ framework.
