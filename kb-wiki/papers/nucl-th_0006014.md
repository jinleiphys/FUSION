# The high-precision, charge-dependent Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential (CD-Bonn)

> We present a charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential that fits the world proton-proton data below 350 MeV available in the year of 2000 with a chi^2 per datum of 1.01 for 2932 data and the corresponding neutron-proton data with chi^2/datum = 1.02 for 3058 data. This reproduction of the NN data is more accurate than by any phase-shift analysis and any other NN potential. The charge-dependence of the present potential (that has been dubbed `CD-Bonn') is based upon the predictions by the Bonn Full Model for charge-symmetry and charge-independence breaking in all partial waves with J <= 4. The potential is represented in terms of the covariant Feynman amplitudes for one-boson exchange which are nonlocal. Therefore, the off-shell behavior of the CD-Bonn potential differs in a characteristic and well-founded way from commonly used local potentials and leads to larger binding energies in nuclear few- and many-body systems, where underbinding is a persistent problem.

## Key claim

The CD-Bonn potential reproduces the world proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data below 350 MeV with a chi-squared per datum of 1.01 and 1.02 respectively, which is more accurate than any phase-shift analysis or other NN potential available at the time; however, this fit required including charge-symmetry-breaking and charge-independence-breaking contributions from two-boson exchange and pi-gamma exchange beyond the standard one-pion-exchange treatment, and still left about 25% of the charge-independence-breaking scattering length difference unexplained, requiring a small phenomenological adjustment.

## Method

The potential is constructed as a one-boson-exchange model including pi, rho, omega, and two scalar-isoscalar sigma bosons, using the full relativistic Feynman amplitudes without the static/local approximation that is commonly applied. Charge dependence is incorporated by starting from a fitted proton-proton potential, then constructing the neutron-neutron and neutron-proton potentials by applying charge-symmetry-breaking effects from nucleon mass splitting (including two-boson exchange contributions from the Bonn Full Model) and charge-independence-breaking effects from pion mass splitting, two-boson exchange, and pi-gamma exchange. The potential parameters are fine-tuned by minimizing the exact chi-squared against all experimental pp and np data below 350 MeV published before the year 2000.

## Key numbers

- Chi-squared per datum of 1.01 for 2932 proton-proton data below 350 MeV
- Chi-squared per datum of 1.02 for 3058 neutron-proton data below 350 MeV
- Empirical singlet scattering lengths: a_pp^N = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm, a_nn^N = -18.9 ± 0.4 fm, a_np = -23.740 ± 0.020 fm
- Charge-symmetry-breaking scattering length difference: Delta a_CSB = 1.6 ± 0.6 fm
- Charge-independence-breaking scattering length difference: Delta a_CIB = 6.44 ± 0.40 fm
- Predicted total Delta a_CSB from revised Bonn Full Model: 1.508 fm, with two-boson exchange contributing 1.275 fm
- Pion mass splitting of 3.4% creates the dominant charge-independence-breaking effect, with two-boson exchange contributing about 1.3 fm to Delta a_CIB
- The 1992 database contains 1787 pp data and 2514 np data; after-1992 data add 1145 pp data and 544 np data

## Context

This work extends the Bonn Full Model (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster, 1987) by constructing a practical, energy-independent potential that reproduces the charge-dependence predictions of that comprehensive field-theoretic model while avoiding its complications in nuclear structure applications. The CD-Bonn potential differs from conventional local potentials like the Argonne V18 (Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla, 1995) by using nonlocal Feynman amplitudes rather than the static/local approximation, which produces a weaker off-shell tensor force and leads to larger binding energies in few- and many-body systems where underbinding is a persistent problem. The potential enables more accurate nuclear structure calculations by providing a charge-dependent interaction that fits the world NN data better than any previous potential, while also incorporating charge-symmetry-breaking effects from nucleon mass splitting that explain the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969) as shown in prior work by Machleidt and Muther (1999).
