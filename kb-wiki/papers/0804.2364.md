# Limiting fragmentation from scale-invariant merging of fast partons

> Exploiting the idea that the fast partons of an energetic projectile can be treated as sources of color radiation interpreted as wee partons, it is shown that the recently observed property of extended limiting fragmentation implies a scaling law for the rapidity distribution of fast partons. This leads to a picture of a self-similar process where, for fixed total rapidity Y, the sources merge with probability varying as 1/y.

# Limiting fragmentation from scale-invariant merging of fast partons

## Key claim

The property of extended limiting fragmentation in high-energy hadronic collisions implies a scaling law for the rapidity distribution of fast partons, where the distribution depends only on the ratio y/Y rather than on y and Y separately; this scaling law suggests a self-similar merging process in which sources merge with probability varying as 1/y, but the authors note that extended limiting fragmentation is an asymptotic property valid only at large Y, and that the rate at which it is realized depends on the shape of the source distribution.

## Method

The authors model particle production by treating fast partons of a wounded constituent as sources of bremsstrahlung radiation (wee partons), with the infrared cut-off of the bremsstrahlung treated as a random variable uniformly distributed over the available rapidity interval. They derive a relation between the produced parton distribution W(y;Y) and the source distribution R(y+;Y), then impose the constraints of extended limiting fragmentation (the rapidity distribution approaches a common straight-line curve at increasing energies) to obtain a scaling law for R. They further propose a self-similar cascade equation for the source distribution with a constant merging probability in log y, which yields an explicit scaling solution.

## Key numbers

- The bremsstrahlung distribution is taken as flat in rapidity, extending from y+ down to an infrared cut-off y-, with the cut-off probability distribution P(y-;y+) dy- = (1/y+) Theta(y-) Theta(y+ - y-) dy-.
- The scaling law for the source distribution is R(y+;Y) = R(y+/Y) ≡ R(s+), where s+ = y+/Y.
- The self-similar merging equation dR/dt+ = b [R(t+) - a R^2(t+)] has the scaling solution R(y+,Y) = R0 (y+/Y)^b / [1 + a R0 (y+/Y)^b], with b the merging probability and a the recombination factor.
- The merging probability in rapidity varies as alpha(y) = b/y.

## Context

This work extends the wounded-constituent model of Bialas and Bzdak (2008), which described single-particle rapidity distributions at 200 GeV c.m. energy, by incorporating the recently observed property of extended limiting fragmentation (ELF) from PHOBOS and UA5 collaborations. The authors build on the idea of fast partons as sources of color radiation from McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Bjorken (1973), and show that ELF implies a scaling law that differs from the original McLerran-Venugopalan picture where the separation between sources and field was fixed in rapidity. The self-similar merging cascade differs from the splitting cascade considered in Mueller (1994) in being a merging process self-similar in log y rather than in y, and not requiring large transverse momentum. The results provide guidelines for understanding non-perturbative soft QCD at high energies and suggest a theoretical basis for the superposition property of target and projectile components seen in Pomeron field theory (Braun and collaborators).
