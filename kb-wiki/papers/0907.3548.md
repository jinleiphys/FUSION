# Formation Time of QGP from Thermal Photon Elliptic Flow

> We show that the transverse momentum dependent elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ of thermal photons is quite sensitive to the initial formation time ($τ_0$) of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) for semi-central collision of gold nuclei at RHIC \cite{tau}. A smaller value of the formation time or a larger initial temperature leads to a significant increase in the thermal photon radiation from QGP phase, which has a smaller $v_2$. The elliptic flow of thermal photon is dominated by the contribution from the quark matter at intermediate and high $p_T$ range and as a result sum $v_2$ decreases with smaller $τ_0$ for $p_T \ge 1.5$ GeV. On the other hand we find that the elliptic flow parameter for hadrons depends only marginally on the value of $τ_0$.

## Key claim
The transverse momentum dependent elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) of thermal photons is quite sensitive to the initial formation time (\(\tau_0\)) of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) for semi-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, whereas the elliptic flow parameter for hadrons depends only marginally on \(\tau_0\). The sensitivity arises because a smaller \(\tau_0\) (or larger initial temperature) increases thermal photon radiation from the QGP phase, which has a smaller \(v_2\), and the sum \(v_2\) decreases with smaller \(\tau_0\) for \(p_T \ge 1.5\) GeV.

## Method
The authors used boost-invariant, impact-parameter-dependent, azimuthally anisotropic ideal hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition at 164 MeV and a freeze-out energy density of 0.075 GeV/fm\(^3\). The initial entropy density was obtained from a combination of binary collision and wounded nucleon contributions via a Glauber model, with the initial entropy density at the center set to 117 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\). Thermal photon emission from quark matter was calculated using the complete leading-order rate from Arnold et al. (AMY), and from hadronic matter using the results of Turbide et al. (TRG); the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) was extracted from the azimuthal distribution of the photon spectra.

## Key numbers
- Initial entropy density at the center (x=y=b=0) is about 117 fm\(^{-3}\) when \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).
- The phase transition temperature is about 164 MeV.
- The freeze-out energy density is 0.075 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The sum \(v_2\) of thermal photons decreases with smaller \(\tau_0\) for \(p_T \ge 1.5\) GeV.
- The hadron freeze-out temperature is about 120 MeV.
- The parameter \(\alpha\) in the entropy density formula is 0.75.
- The formation time \(\tau_0\) is varied from 0.2 to 1.0 fm/\(c\) in steps of 0.2 fm/\(c\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Chatterjee and Srivastava, 2009) on the elliptic flow of thermal photons at RHIC, which showed that thermal photon \(v_2(p_T)\) from ideal hydrodynamics differs from hadron elliptic flow at intermediate and large \(p_T\). It also builds on the standard approach where formation time is estimated as \(\tau_0 \sim 1/\langle E \rangle\). The results enable a direct experimental determination of the QGP formation time by measuring thermal photon elliptic flow, complementing hadronic flow measurements which are insensitive to \(\tau_0\).
