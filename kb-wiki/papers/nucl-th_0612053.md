# Covariant calculation of nonstrange decays of strange baryon resonances

> We report on a study of pi and eta decays of strange baryon resonances within relativistic constituent-quark models based on one-gluon-exchange and Goldstone-boson-exchange dynamics. The investigations are performed in the point form of Poincare-invariant relativistic quantum mechanics with a spectator-model decay operator. The covariant predictions of the constituent-quark models underestimate the experimental data in most cases. These findings are congruent with an earlier study of nonstrange baryon decays in the light-flavor sector. We also consider a nonrelativistic reduction of the point-form spectator model, which leads to the decay operator of the elementary emission model. For some decays the nonrelativistic results differ substantially from the relativistic ones and they exhibit no uniform behavior as they scatter above and below the experimental decay widths.

## Key claim

The covariant predictions of the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) and Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) constituent-quark models for pi and eta decays of strange baryon resonances generally underestimate the experimental data, with only a few exceptions such as the Lambda(1670) resonance where the decay widths are predicted far too high even when experimental masses are used.

## Method

The authors calculated pi and eta decay widths of Lambda, Sigma, and Xi resonances using relativistic constituent-quark models with OGE and GBE dynamics, employing the point form of Poincare-invariant relativistic quantum mechanics with a spectator-model decay operator. The transition amplitudes were computed in a manifestly covariant manner using velocity-state representations of baryon eigenstates, with the decay operator assuming only one quark directly couples to the emitted meson while the other two act as spectators. They also performed a nonrelativistic reduction of the point-form spectator model, leading to the decay operator of the elementary emission model (EEM), and calculated decay widths using both theoretical resonance masses and experimental masses from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous covariant calculation of pi and eta decays of N and Delta resonances (Melde, 2005) to the strange baryon sector, complementing that earlier study. The results parallel the earlier findings that relativistic constituent-quark models underestimate experimental decay widths, and contradict the earlier nonrelativistic calculations by Koniuk and Isgur (1980), which showed better agreement with experiment but introduced additional parameters beyond the quark models. The study provides a consistent relativistic framework for mesonic decay calculations that can serve as a starting point for future refinements, including possible resonance mixing effects and improvements to the decay mechanism.
