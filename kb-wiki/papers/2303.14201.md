# Centrality-dependent modification of hadron and jet production in electron-nucleus collisions

> Centrality-dependent measurements of hadron and jet cross section attenuation in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei can shed new light on the physics of final-state interactions in the nuclear matter, including the path-length dependence of the in-medium parton shower formation and evolution. Recent simulation studies have demonstrated the feasibility of experimental centrality determination in $e$A reactions at the electron-ion collider via neutron detection in the zero-degree calorimeter. Motivated by these results, we present the first theoretical calculation of the production rate modification for hadrons and jets in central and peripheral $e$Pb collisions. We find that the variation in the suppression of inclusive jet cross section as a function of centrality is less than a factor of two. In more differential measurements, such as the distribution of hadrons versus the hadronization fraction $z_h$, the difference can be enhanced up to an order of magnitude.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical calculation of hadron and jet production rate modification in central and peripheral electron-lead collisions, finding that the variation in inclusive jet cross section suppression as a function of centrality is less than a factor of two, while for differential hadron measurements versus the hadronization fraction \(z_h\) the difference can reach up to an order of magnitude.

## Method
The authors compute medium-modified DGLAP evolution equations for fragmentation functions and semi-inclusive jet functions using in-medium splitting kernels derived from soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET\(_{\rm G}\)). They use average interaction lengths \(\langle d \rangle\) for different centrality classes obtained from BeAGLE Monte Carlo simulations, and construct double ratios such as \(R_{e\rm A}(R)/R_{e\rm A}(R=1)\) and peripheral-to-central ratios to minimize initial-state nuclear parton distribution function effects and isolate final-state interactions.

## Key numbers
- For 0–1% central \(e\)Pb collisions, the average interaction length \(\langle d \rangle = 9.09\) fm; for 90–100% peripheral collisions, \(\langle d \rangle = 2.71\) fm; the minimum bias (0–100%) value is 4.40 fm.
- The ratio \(\langle d \rangle / \langle d \rangle_{\rm min. bias}\) is 2.07 for 0–1% central and 0.62 for 90–100% peripheral collisions.
- For small radius jets (\(R=0.3\)) in central \(e\)Pb at 10 GeV \(\times\) 100 GeV, the double ratio \(R_{e\rm A}(R)/R_{e\rm A}(R=1)\) shows more than a factor of two suppression.
- For 10 GeV \(\times\) 100 GeV \(e\)Pb collisions, the peripheral-to-central jet cross section ratio is around 1.1 for \(R=0.5\) and \(R=0.8\) at small jet \(p_T\).
- For light pions at large \(z_h\) in central collisions, the differential cross section suppression can reach more than an order of magnitude reduction.
- The gluon transport coefficient in cold nuclear matter is fixed at \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle/\lambda_g = 0.12\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(\langle k_\perp^2\rangle/\lambda_q = 0.053\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.

## Context
This work extends previous minimum bias calculations of hadron and jet suppression in electron-nucleus collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider (Li 2020, Ke 2023) by incorporating centrality dependence, motivated by feasibility studies of centrality determination via zero-degree calorimeter neutron detection (Chang 2022). The results enable future experimental measurements at the EIC to probe the path-length dependence of parton shower formation and hadronization in cold nuclear matter, and the authors suggest future exploration of centrality dependence for jet substructure observables.
