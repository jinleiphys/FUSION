# Towards a discovery of BSM physics from the Cabibbo angle anomaly

> New developments in both the theories and experiments related to the extraction of the top-row Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements $V_{ud}$ and $V_{us}$ led to a series of new anomalies, for instance the apparent violation of the top-row unitarity relation. It is important to further reduce all the associated Standard Model theory uncertainties in order to better understand whether such observations point towards the possibility of physics beyond the Standard Model, or rather some unexpectedly large Standard Model effects. This requires improved studies of tree-level and higher-order Standard Model corrections that enter the beta decays of pions, neutron, nuclei and kaons. We will briefly review the recent progress along this direction and discuss possible improvements in the future.

## Key claim

The apparent violation of top-row CKM unitarity, currently at the 2-3 sigma level depending on which specific determinations of \(|V_{ud}|\) and \(|V_{us}|\) are combined, may indicate physics beyond the Standard Model but could also arise from unexpectedly large Standard Model effects that require further reduction of theory uncertainties.

## Method

The paper reviews the extraction of \(|V_{ud}|\) from pion semileptonic decay, free neutron decay, and superallowed nuclear beta decays, and the extraction of \(|V_{us}|\) from leptonic and semileptonic kaon decays. It examines the higher-order Standard Model corrections entering these processes, focusing on the axial \(\gamma W\)-box diagrams in radiative corrections, nuclear structure corrections in superallowed decays, and the electromagnetic and isospin-breaking corrections in kaon decays.

## Key numbers

- CKM unitarity deficit: \(\Delta_\mathrm{CKM}^u = -0.0015(6)_{V_{ud}}(4)_{V_{us}}\) from PDG 2020, a \(2\sigma\) deviation
- \(|V_{ud}|\) from superallowed decays: \(0.97373(31)\) (Hardy 2020)
- \(|V_{us}|\) from \(K_{\ell 3}\): \(0.22309(56)\) (Seng 2021)
- \(|V_{us}|\) from \(K_{\mu 2}\): \(0.2252(5)\), giving a \(2.8\sigma\) discrepancy between the two kaon determinations
- \(|V_{ud}|^2_{0^+} + |V_{us}|^2_{K_{\ell 3}} - 1 = -0.0021(7)\), a \(3\sigma\) deviation from unitarity
- Single-nucleon inner radiative correction \(\Delta_R^V = 0.02467(22)\) from dispersive analysis (Seng 2018)
- Pion semileptonic branching ratio: \(\mathrm{BR}(\pi_{e3}) = 1.038(6) \times 10^{-8}\) from PIBETA (2004)
- Neutron lifetime from UCN\(\tau\): \(\tau_n = 877.75 \pm 0.28_\mathrm{stat} +0.22/-0.16_\mathrm{syst}\) s
- Axial-vector coupling from PERKEO III: \(\lambda = -1.27641(45)_\mathrm{stat}(33)_\mathrm{syst}\)
- Pion \(\gamma W\)-box correction: \(\Box_\pi = 2.830(11)_\mathrm{stat}(26)_\mathrm{syst} \times 10^{-3}\), giving \(\delta_\pi = 0.0332(3)\)
- Ratio of kaon to pion decay constants from FLAG 2021: \(f_{K^+}/f_{\pi^+} = 1.1932(21)\)

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Marciano and Sirlin (2006) on radiative corrections to beta decays, and by Hardy and Towner (2015, 2020) on superallowed nuclear decays. The dispersive treatment of the axial \(\gamma W\)-box diagram introduced by Seng (2018) shifted the central value of \(\Delta_R^V\) and produced the first recent hint of a CKM unitarity deficit, later confirmed by Czarnecki (2019), Hayen (2020), and others. The lattice QCD calculation of the pion \(\gamma W\)-box by Feng (2020) achieved a threefold precision improvement over previous Chiral Perturbation Theory results. The paper enables future work on ab-initio calculations of nuclear structure corrections and improved experimental measurements such as the PIONEER experiment for rare pion decays.
