# Fission Fragment Mass and Kinetic Energy Yields of Fermium Isotopes

> A rapidly converging 4-dimensional Fourier shape parametrization is used to model the fission process of heavy nuclei. Potential energy landscapes are computed within the macroscopic-microscopic approach, on top of which the multi-dimensional Langevin equation is solved to describe the fission dynamics. Charge equilibration at scission and de-excitation by neutron evaporation of the primary fragments after scission is investigated. The model describes various observables, including fission-fragment mass, charge, and kinetic energy yields, as well as post-scission neutron multiplicities and, most importantly, their correlations, which are crucial to unravel the complexity of the fission process. The parameters of the dynamical model were tuned to reproduce experimental data obtained from thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U, which allows us to discuss the transition from asymmetric to symmetric fission along the Fm isotopic chain.

## Key claim

The transition from asymmetric to symmetric fission along the Fermium isotopic chain, first observed by Hulet (1986), is reproduced by a dynamical model that weights two competing fission paths (asymmetric and symmetric) using WKB penetration probabilities, with the relative barrier heights determining which path dominates.

## Method

The authors used a 4-dimensional Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) shape parametrization in the deformation space {η, c, a₃, a₄} to compute potential energy surfaces within the macroscopic-microscopic approach, using the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) formula for the macroscopic part and a Yukawa-folded mean-field Hamiltonian with Strutinsky shell and BCS pairing corrections for the microscopic part. Multi-dimensional Langevin equations were solved to describe fission dynamics, with trajectories starting from either the symmetric or asymmetric second-saddle exit point. The final mass and total kinetic energy yields were obtained by weighting the yields from each path using WKB penetration probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of ²⁵⁸Fm is located at elongation c = 1.13 and neck parameter a₄ = 0.03, with a₃ = η = 0.
- Two second saddles are found for ²⁵⁸Fm: one at c = 1.41 and a₄ = 0.27 leading to compact symmetric fission, and a second one approximately 1 MeV higher at c = 1.55 and a₄ = 0.12 leading to asymmetric mass fragments.
- The FoS parametrization scission line is found around a₄ ≈ 0.72.
- Mass yields were estimated for Fermium isotopes from ²⁴⁶Fm to ²⁶²Fm.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the rapid change from asymmetric to symmetric fission in Fermium isotopes, a phenomenon first reported by Hulet (1986) and reviewed by Albertsson et al. (2021). The model uses a Fourier-type shape parametrization introduced by Schmidt (2017) and a Langevin approach developed by Pomorski et al. (2023), with the LSD mass formula from Pomorski and Dudek (2009). The results enable predictions for a wider mass and charge region, with further calculations in progress.
