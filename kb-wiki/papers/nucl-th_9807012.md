# Scaling Properties of the Giant Dipole Resonance Width in Hot Rotating nuclei

> We study the systematics of the giant dipole resonance width $Γ$ in hot rotating nuclei as a function of temperature $T$, spin $J$ and mass $A$. We compare available experimental results with theoretical calculations that include thermal shape fluctuations in nuclei ranging from A=45 to A=208. Using the appropriate scaled variables, we find a simple phenomenological function $Γ(A,T,J)$ which approximates the global behavior of the giant dipole resonance width in the liquid drop model. We reanalyze recent experimental and theoretical results for the resonance width in Sn isotopes and $^{208}$Pb.

# Scaling Properties of the Giant Dipole Resonance Width in Hot Rotating Nuclei

## Key claim
The giant dipole resonance width in hot rotating nuclei can be approximated by a simple phenomenological function of temperature, spin, and mass in the liquid drop model regime, but the scaling is approximate rather than exact and works best at higher temperatures where shell effects have melted.

## Method
The authors performed theoretical calculations of the GDR width using thermal shape fluctuations in the adiabatic limit, averaging over an ensemble of nuclear shapes described by quadrupole deformation parameters. Free energy surfaces were computed using both Nilsson-Strutinsky (including shell corrections) and liquid drop models for nuclei ranging from A=45 to A=208, and the resulting GDR strength functions were compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The GDR width is insensitive to spin for J < 20 ℏ in ⁵⁹,⁶³Cu and for J < 30 ℏ in ¹⁰⁶Sn
- At high spins, the reduced width as a function of scaled spin ξ = J/A^(5/6) falls approximately on a single curve for different masses
- The reference temperature T₀ = 1 MeV is used for the scaling function L(ξ)
- The temperature-dependent width at zero spin follows Γ(T,J=0,A) − Γ₀(A) ≈ c(A) log(1 + T/T₀) with c(A) ≈ 6.45 − A/100
- The width is approximately spin-independent up to J₁ ∼ 0.6 A^(5/6)
- For A > 200, J_max ≤ J₁, so no significant spin dependence of the GDR width is expected
- The scaling function L(ξ) can be fitted by L(ξ) − 1 ≈ 1.8[1 + e^((1.3−ξ)/0.2)]⁻¹
- For ¹²⁰Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb, Γ₀ = 3.8 MeV was used

## Context
This work extends the thermal shape fluctuation theory of the GDR developed by Alhassid (1992) and Alhassid and Bush (1990) to a systematic global analysis across many nuclei. It provides a phenomenological formula that enables prediction of GDR widths in the liquid drop regime, and reanalyzes recent experimental and theoretical results for Sn isotopes and ²⁰⁸Pb from Ref. R96 and Ref. OBB96. The revised temperature determinations for ¹²⁰Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb inelastic scattering data bring experiment and theory into better agreement, confirming the conclusion of Ref. OBB96 that shell effects on the GDR width are negligible in Sn but cause suppression of the width at low temperatures in Pb.
