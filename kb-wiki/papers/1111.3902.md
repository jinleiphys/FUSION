# Improved convergence of scattering calculations in the oscillator representation

> The Schrödinger equation for two and tree-body problems is solved for scattering states in a hybrid representation where solutions are expanded in the eigenstates of the harmonic oscillator in the interaction region and on a finite difference grid in the near-- and far--field. The two representations are coupled through a high--order asymptotic formula that takes into account the function values and the third derivative in the classical turning points. For various examples the convergence is analyzed for various physics problems that use an expansion in a large number of oscillator states. The results show significant improvement over the JM-ECS method [Bidasyuk et al, Phys. Rev. C 82, 064603 (2010)].

## Key claim

The paper presents a higher-order asymptotic matching formula that improves the accuracy of hybrid oscillator-grid scattering calculations by reducing the matching error from O(N^{-1/2}) to the level of the grid representation accuracy, though the improvement is conditional on using function values and third derivatives at the classical turning points.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation for two-body scattering states by expanding the wave function in harmonic oscillator eigenstates in the interaction region and using a finite difference grid in the near- and far-field regions. The two representations are coupled through a new asymptotic formula that includes the wave function value and its third derivative at the classical turning point R_n, derived from an Airy function approximation of the oscillator states. The resulting hybrid representation maintains a tridiagonal kinetic energy operator and can be extended with exterior complex scaling (ECS) absorbing boundary conditions on the grid portion.

## Key numbers

- The original JM-ECS matching error scales as O(N^{-1/2}), where N is the size of the oscillator basis describing the inner region.
- The grid representation on the exterior has an accuracy of O(N^{-1}), while the original matching was only accurate to O(N^{-1/2}).
- The new asymptotic formula (Eq. 4.1) gives c_n = b sqrt(2/R_n) [psi(R_n) + (b^4/(6 R_n)) psi'''(R_n)] + O(R_n^{-5/2}).
- The grid distance between classical turning points is h_n = R_n - R_{n-1} ≈ b^2 (2/R_n), making the outer grid accurate to O(h^2) = O(R_n^{-2}).
- The classical turning point for oscillator state n is R_{n,l} = b sqrt(4n + 2l + 3), with oscillator length b = 1/sqrt(omega).

## Context

This work extends the JM-ECS method introduced by Bidasyuk et al (2010) in Phys. Rev. C 82, 064603, which combined the J-matrix method with grid-based exterior complex scaling but suffered from low-order matching accuracy. The higher-order asymptotic formula builds on earlier work by S. Igashov on asymptotic approximations in the J-matrix method, though that work did not use the formula to improve scattering calculations. The method enables more accurate scattering calculations for breakup reactions of three or more particles where the asymptotic wave form is not explicitly known, and can be combined with absorbing boundary conditions such as ECS.
