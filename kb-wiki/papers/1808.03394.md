# Nucleon-$α$ Scattering and Resonances in $^5$He and $^5$Li with JISP16 and Daejeon16 $NN$ interactions

> The SS-HORSE approach to analysis of resonant states is generalized to the case of charged particle scattering utilizing analytical properties of partial scattering amplitudes and applied to the study of resonant states in the $^{5}$Li nucleus and non-resonant $s$-wave proton-$α$ scattering within the no-core shell model using the JISP16 and Daejeon16 $NN$ interactions. We present also the results of calculations of neutron-$α$ scattering and resonances in the $^{5}$He nucleus with Daejeon16 and compare with results published previously using JISP16.

## Key claim

The SS-HORSE (Single-State Harmonic Oscillator Representation of Scattering Equations) method is generalized to charged-particle scattering by using the renormalized Coulomb-nuclear amplitude, which has analytic properties similar to the neutral-particle scattering amplitude, enabling extraction of resonant-state parameters in \(^{5}\)Li and non-resonant \(s\)-wave proton-\(\alpha\) phase shifts from no-core shell model calculations with JISP16 and Daejeon16 \(NN\) interactions.

## Method

The authors perform no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of \(^{5}\)He and \(^{5}\)Li using the MFDn code with basis spaces up to \(N_{\max}=18\) and \(\hbar\Omega\) values from 10 to 40 MeV. They apply the SS-HORSE approach, which uses the eigenenergies of the Hamiltonian matrix truncated to an internal oscillator basis region to compute scattering phase shifts via Eq. (1) for neutral particles or Eq. (2) for charged particles, where the Coulomb interaction is cut at the natural channel radius. The phase shifts are parametrized by expanding the Coulomb-modified effective-range function \(\widetilde{K}_l(E)\) in even powers of energy, with coefficients \(w_0\), \(w_1\), and \(w_2\) fitted to minimize the rms deviation between NCSM eigenenergies and those predicted by the parametrization; resonance energies and widths are then obtained by numerically locating the \(S\)-matrix poles in the complex energy plane.

## Context

This work extends the SS-HORSE method, previously applied to neutron-\(\alpha\) scattering with JISP16 (Shirokov et al., 2009), to charged-particle scattering by incorporating the renormalized Coulomb-nuclear amplitude formalism of Haeringen and van Haeringen. The results are compared with prior ab initio studies of nucleon-\(\alpha\) resonances using GFMC (Nollett), NCGSM, Coupled-Cluster Theory with Berggren basis, and NCSM/RGM (various papers by Navratil, Quaglioni, and collaborators). The approach enables extraction of resonance parameters directly from standard NCSM calculations without introducing Berggren basis states or performing additional RGM calculations, and can be applied to other charged-particle scattering systems.
