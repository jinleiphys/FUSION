# The dispersion relation of the fast neutrino oscillation wave

> A dense neutrino medium can support flavor oscillation waves which are coherent among different momentum modes of the neutrinos. The dispersion relation (DR) branches of such a wave with complex frequencies and/or wave numbers can lead to the exponential growth of the wave amplitude which in turn will engender a collective flavor transformation in the neutrino medium. In this work we propose that the complex DR branches of the neutrino oscillation wave should be bound by the critical points of the DR. We demonstrate how this theory can be applied to the neutrino medium with an (approximate) axial symmetry about the propagation direction of the neutrino oscillation wave. We also show how the flavor instabilities in this medium can be identified by tracing the critical points of the DR as the electron lepton number distribution of the neutrino medium is changed continuously.

# The dispersion relation of the fast neutrino oscillation wave

## Key claim

The complex branches of the dispersion relation for fast neutrino flavor oscillation waves are bounded by critical points of the dispersion relation, not necessarily confined to the "gaps" between real branches as previously suggested. The authors demonstrate that flavor instabilities can exist even when the zero mode is stable, contradicting earlier proposals that the zero mode alone indicates instability.

## Method

The authors analyze the dispersion relation for fast neutrino flavor conversions in a dense neutrino medium with two-flavor mixing, derived from the linearized equation of motion for the flavor coherence field. They classify critical points of the dispersion function D(ω, K) into three types: type-I where ∂D/∂K = 0 (turning points of real branches), type-II where the phase velocity V = ω/K approaches a real value within the forbidden region where the ELN distribution crosses zero, and type-III at ω = 0. They apply this theory to neutrino media with axial symmetry about the wave propagation direction, using six parametrized electron lepton number (ELN) distributions with varying crossing depths, and compute the real, complex-K, and complex-ω dispersion relation branches for the symmetry-breaking (SB) modes.

## Key numbers

- The ELN distributions G_i(v_z) are normalized to the neutrino potential strength μ = √2 G_F n_{ν_e}, where n_{ν_e} is the initial number density of electron neutrinos
- For the SB modes, the real branches always end at finite ω and K because ω(N=±1) = (1/2)∫ G(v_z)(1 ± v_z) dv_z are finite
- For category-I distributions (G_1, no crossing), complex-K branches run from the turning point (ω_b, K_b) to (0, K_0) and (0, K_0^*)
- For category-II distributions (G_2, G_3, shallow crossing), an additional pair of complex-K branches runs from (ω_c, K_c) to (ω_b', K_b'), and complex-ω branches run from (ω_c, K_c) to (ω_t, K_t)
- For category-III distributions (G_4 through G_6, deep crossing), complex-K branches run from (ω_c, K_c) to (0, K_0) and (0, K_0^*), and complex-ω branches run from (ω_c, K_c) to (ω_t, K_t)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach introduced by Izaguirre et al. (2016) for fast neutrino flavor conversions, correcting the earlier suggestion that complex branches are always confined between gaps of real branches (which was limited to the two-beam toy model). It builds upon the instability classification framework of Sturrock (1958) and Briggs (1964), which was applied to neutrino oscillations by Capozzi et al. (2017) but only for the two-beam toy model. The authors address the proposal by Dasgupta et al. (2018) that the "zero mode" could identify fast flavor conversions, showing that instabilities can exist even when the zero mode is stable. This work enables identification of flavor instabilities in realistic supernova neutrino media by tracing critical points as the ELN distribution changes continuously, without requiring full knowledge of the analytic properties of the dispersion function.
