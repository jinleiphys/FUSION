# The radiation pattern of a QCD antenna in a dense medium

> We calculate the radiation spectrum off a qq-bar pair of a fixed opening angle theta_qq-bar traversing a medium of length L. Multiple interactions with the medium are handled in the harmonic oscillator approximation, valid for soft gluon emissions. We discuss the time-scales relevant to the decoherence of correlated partons traversing the medium and demonstrate how this relates to the hard scale that govern medium-induced radiation. For large angle radiation, the hard scale is given by Qhard = max(r_perp^{-1}, Qs), where r_perp = theta_qq-bar L is the probed transverse size and Qs is the maximal transverse momentum accumulated by the emitted gluon in the medium. These situations define in turn two distinct regimes, which we call "dipole" and "decoherence" regimes, respectively, and which are discussed in detail. A feature common to both cases is that coherence of the radiation is restored at large transverse momenta, k_\perp > Qhard.

## Key claim

The radiation pattern of a quark-antiquark antenna in a dense medium is governed by the hard scale Q_hard = max(r_perp^{-1}, Q_s), where r_perp = theta_qq-bar L is the probed transverse size and Q_s is the maximal transverse momentum accumulated by the emitted gluon in the medium; this scale determines two distinct regimes ("dipole" and "decoherence") in which coherence of the radiation is restored at large transverse momenta k_perp > Q_hard, but the abstract's claim that these regimes are "discussed in detail" glosses over the fact that the harmonic oscillator approximation is strictly valid only for soft gluon emissions and in the limit of vanishing mean free path.

## Method

The authors calculated the radiation spectrum off a qq-bar pair with fixed opening angle theta_qq-bar traversing a medium of length L, using the harmonic oscillator approximation to handle multiple interactions with the medium. The spectrum was decomposed into independent radiation off each constituent and interference contributions, with the medium interactions described by a dipole-medium cross section that becomes proportional to r^2 in the harmonic oscillator approximation. The analysis considered three integration regions for emission times (in-in, in-out, out-out) and identified characteristic time-scales including the decoherence time t_d = (hat q theta^2_qq-bar)^{-1/3}.

## Key numbers

* The decoherence time scale is given by t_d = (hat q theta^2_qq-bar)^{-1/3}, where hat q is the medium transport coefficient and theta_qq-bar is the opening angle of the pair.
* The saturation scale of the medium is Q_med^2 = sqrt(hat q L), where L is the size of the medium.
* The critical opening angle for the decoherence regime is theta_c = 1/sqrt(hat q L^3).
* The decoherence parameter in the dipole regime expands to Delta_med = (1/12) Q_med^2 r_perp^2, where r_perp = theta_qq-bar L.
* The hard scale is Q_hard = max(r_perp^{-1}, Q_s), where Q_s is the maximal transverse momentum accumulated by the emitted gluon in the medium.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Mehtar-Tani 2010, 2011) that considered only a single scattering center inside the medium, which was strictly valid only for a relatively dilute medium; the present paper accounts for an arbitrary number of re-scatterings with the medium in the harmonic oscillator approximation. The study builds on the BDMPS-Z framework (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff 1996-1998; Zakharov 1996-1997) for medium-induced radiation off single partons and generalizes it to correlated qq-bar pairs, enabling future treatment of multi-gluon emissions and parton shower development in dense media.
