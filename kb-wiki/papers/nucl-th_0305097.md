# Dense quark matter in compact stars

> The densest predicted state of matter is colour-superconducting quark matter, in which quarks near the Fermi surface form a condensate of Cooper pairs. This form of matter may well exist in the core of compact stars, and the search for signatures of its presence is an ongoing enterprise. Using a bag model of quark matter, I discuss the effects of colour superconductivity on the mass-radius relationship of compact stars, showing that colour superconducting quark matter can occur in compact stars at values of the bag constant where ordinary quark matter would not be allowed. The resultant ``hybrid'' stars with colour superconducting quark matter interior and nuclear matter surface have masses in the range 1.3-1.6 Msolar and radii 8-11 km. Once perturbative corrections are included, quark matter can show a mass-radius relationship very similar to that of nuclear matter, and the mass of a hybrid star can reach $1.8 \Msolar$.

## Key claim

Colour-superconducting quark matter can occur in compact stars at values of the bag constant where ordinary (unpaired) quark matter would not be allowed, and the resulting "hybrid" stars have masses in the range 1.3-1.6 solar masses and radii 8-11 km; once perturbative corrections are included, the mass of a hybrid star can reach 1.8 solar masses.

## Method

The author uses a bag model of quark matter to calculate the equation of state, including a colour-superconducting gap parameter Delta. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain mass-radius relationships for hybrid stars with a colour-superconducting quark matter core and a nuclear matter surface, using the Walecka and APR98 equations of state for nuclear matter. The bag constant is either fixed at a physically reasonable value or tuned to maintain a constant nuclear density at the transition to quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Hybrid stars with colour-superconducting quark matter interior and nuclear matter surface have masses in the range 1.3-1.6 solar masses and radii 8-11 km.
- With perturbative corrections included, the mass of a hybrid star can reach 1.8 solar masses.
- For a fixed bag constant B^{1/4}=180 MeV and strange quark mass m_s=200 MeV, mass-radius curves are shown for unpaired (Delta=0) and colour-superconducting (Delta=100 MeV) quark matter.
- The nuclear to quark matter phase transition is set to occur at nuclear matter baryon density 1.5 times nuclear saturation density (1.5 n_sat).
- A mixed phase only occurs if the surface tension of the nuclear-quark interface is less than about 40 MeV/fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the study of colour-superconducting quark matter in compact stars, building on earlier calculations of the CFL (colour-flavour-locked) phase by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (2001) and the effects of charge neutrality by Alford and Rajagopal (2002). The author and collaborator Reddy (2003) previously showed that at fixed bag constant, colour superconductivity has a strong effect on the mass-radius relationship. This paper further shows that when the bag constant is tuned to maintain a constant transition density, the effect of colour superconductivity is difficult to distinguish from a change in the bag constant, and that quark matter with perturbative corrections can closely mimic nuclear matter's mass-radius relationship. The results enable future work on signatures such as cooling by neutrino emission, r-mode spindown, and gravitational waves from mergers.
