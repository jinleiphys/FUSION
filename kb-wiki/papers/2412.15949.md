# The QCD moat regime and its real-time properties

> Dense QCD matter may exhibit crystalline phases. Their existence is reflected in a moat regime, where mesonic correlations feature spatial modulations. We study the realtime properties of pions at finite temperature and density in QCD in order to elucidate the nature of this regime. We show that the moat regime arises from particle-hole-like fluctuations near the Fermi surface. This gives rise to a characteristic peak in the spectral function of the pion at nonzero \emph{spacelike} momentum. This peak can be interpreted as a new quasi particle, the moaton. In addition, our framework also allows us to directly test the stability of the homogeneous chiral phase against the formation of an inhomogeneous condensate in QCD. We find that the formation of such a phase is highly unlikely for baryon chemical potentials $μ_B \leq 630$\,MeV.

## Key claim
The moat regime in QCD, where mesonic correlations exhibit spatial modulations, arises from particle-hole-like fluctuations near the Fermi surface and produces a characteristic peak in the pion spectral function at nonzero spacelike momentum; however, the formation of an inhomogeneous chiral condensate is highly unlikely for baryon chemical potentials up to 630 MeV, contrary to expectations from simpler models.

## Method
The authors used the functional renormalization group (fRG) to compute the fully momentum-dependent two-point functions of pions in QCD at finite temperature and density, analytically continuing the flow equations to real time to obtain the retarded propagator and spectral function without needing ill-conditioned reconstruction methods. They separated the quark contributions into creation-annihilation (CA) and particle-hole (PH) processes to identify the origin of the moat regime, and evaluated the static meson dispersion to test stability against inhomogeneous condensation.

## Key numbers
- The moat regime is found for baryon chemical potentials up to approximately 630 MeV and temperatures down to approximately 100 MeV, based on the analysis of the scalar-pseudoscalar quark scattering channel.
- At T = 145 MeV, the static pion dispersion shows a minimum at nonzero spatial momentum for large mu_B, with the moat momentum p_M quickly reaching values on the order of a few hundred MeV.
- The spatial pion wave function Z_pi^perp becomes negative in the moat regime; at mu_B = 550 MeV, it is negative for T greater than approximately 120 MeV and approaches zero at large T.
- At T = 120 MeV and mu_B = 600 MeV, particle-hole fluctuations dominate over creation-annihilation processes in driving the spatial wave function negative, as shown by the RG-scale dependence of Z_pi^perp.

## Context
This work extends the earlier finding of a moat regime in QCD using the fRG by Fu et al. (2019), where negative spatial wave functions of mesonic correlations were first identified, and provides the first real-time spectral properties of pions in this regime. The results contradict the expectation from simpler quasiparticle models that the moat regime would lead to an instability toward inhomogeneous condensation, and instead show that such a phase is unlikely for mu_B up to 630 MeV. The framework enables future phenomenological studies of experimental signatures in heavy-ion collisions, such as enhanced dilepton production around the moaton production threshold as proposed by Nussinov et al. (2024), and provides a direct stability analysis complementary to methods based on the 2PI effective action by Motta et al. (2024) and chiral susceptibility by Motta et al. (2024).
