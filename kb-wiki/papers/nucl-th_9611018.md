# One-body dissipation and chaotic dynamics in a classical simulation of a nuclear gas

> In order to understand the origin of one-body dissipation in nuclei, we analyze the behavior of a gas of classical particles moving in a two-dimensional cavity with nuclear dimensions. This "nuclear" billiard has multipole-deformed walls which undergo periodic shape oscillations. We demonstrate that a single particle Hamiltonian containing coupling terms between the particles' motion and the collective coordinate induces a chaotic dynamics for any multipolarity, independently on the geometry of the billiard. If the coupling terms are switched off the "wall formula" predictions are recovered. We discuss the dissipative behavior of the wall motion and its relation with the order-to-chaos transition in the dynamics of the microscopic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a classical Hamiltonian coupling between single-particle motion and a collective wall coordinate induces chaotic single-particle dynamics for *any* multipolarity (L=0, 2, 3), contradicting the earlier claim by Blocki et al. (1991) that chaos appears only for L > 2. The dissipation of collective motion depends only weakly on geometry because the timescale for the onset of chaoticity varies by at most a factor of two across multipolarities.

## Method

The authors simulate a gas of N=30 classical non-interacting nucleons (mass 938 MeV) confined in a two-dimensional multipole-deformed billiard with oscillating walls, using a Hamiltonian that includes coupling terms between particle motion and the collective coordinate α. They solve Hamilton's equations with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm (time step 1 fm/c) and a symplectic integrator for long runs, conserving total energy to within ΔE/E ≤ 10⁻⁴. Initial particle positions are random inside the billiard (radius R₀ = 6 fm) and momenta follow a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution at T = 36 MeV; the collective coordinate is initially displaced by δα = 0.3 from equilibrium with zero initial momentum.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass m = 938 MeV; billiard radius R₀ = 6 fm; oscillation frequency Ω = 0.05 s⁻¹
- Initial gas temperature T = 36 MeV (chosen to mimic Fermi motion, giving average velocity near nuclear Fermi velocity)
- Number of particles N = 30; for monopole (L=0) the Inglis mass factor η = 1, for quadrupole (L=2) and octupole (L=3) η = 10
- Adiabaticity parameter κ = 1.53 α₀; for the coupled case with large wall inertia (η = 100) and α₀ = 0.09, κ = 0.14; for the Swiatecki-like calculation κ = 0.04
- Lyapunov exponents calculated over trajectories of length t = 3500 fm/c with rescaling interval τ = 200 fm/c and initial separation d₀ ≈ 2 × 10⁻⁶
- Fractal correlation dimension D₂ extracted over r interval [1.83 × 10⁻², 1] from 1000-point scatter plots
- Average over 1000 events used for ensemble analysis; final gas temperature from energy conservation T = 43 MeV

## Context

This work extends the classical billiard model of Blocki et al. (1991), who found dissipation only for octupole and higher multipolarities in an uncoupled system, by introducing a Hamiltonian coupling between wall and particles that conserves total energy. It also contrasts with the Vlasov-equation study of Bauer et al. (1992), which found regular undamped collective motion coexisting with weakly chaotic single-particle dynamics. The results enable a generic understanding of one-body dissipation in nuclei as arising from chaos induced by particle-wall coupling, independent of billiard geometry, and show that the wall formula of Swiatecki and collaborators holds only for extremely small adiabaticity parameters (κ ≪ 1) and only during the first oscillation period otherwise.
