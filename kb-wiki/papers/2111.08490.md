# $B_c^+$ Formation from Random Charm and Anti-bottom Quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the $B_c^+$ production in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. In the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions, heavy quarks make random motions with the energy loss. We employ the Langevin equations to study the non-equilibrium distributions of heavy quarks and the Instantaneous Coalescence Model (ICM) to study the hadronization process. Due to abundant charm and bottom quarks in the QGP, their coalescence probability is significantly enhanced compared with the situations in proton-proton collisions. We find that the final production of $B_c^+$ is increased by the coalescence process, which makes the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of $B_c^+$ larger than the unit. Our model explains the experimental data well at semi-central and central collisions. The observation of $R_{AA}(B_c^+)>1$ is regarded as an evident and strong signal of the existence of the deconfined medium generated in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of $B_c^+$ mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV can exceed unity, but this result depends on the coalescence of random charm and anti-bottom quarks in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and is most pronounced in central and semi-central collisions; in peripheral collisions with fewer than 100 participants, the coalescence-only model underpredicts the experimental data, indicating that primordial production also contributes.

## Method

The authors simulate the non-equilibrium distributions of charm and bottom quarks in the QGP using Langevin equations that include drag, random noise, and medium-induced gluon radiation. They then apply the Instantaneous Coalescence Model (ICM) with a Wigner function derived from a Gaussian approximation of the $B_c^+$ wave function to compute the probability that a random charm and anti-bottom quark form a $B_c^+$ meson at a coalescence temperature of $1.2 T_c$. The QGP evolution is modeled with the MUSIC hydrodynamic package, and the initial heavy quark distributions are obtained from FONLL calculations with EPS09 shadowing corrections.

## Key numbers

- The production cross section of charm quarks in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV in central rapidity ($|y|<2.3$) is ${d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/dy}=1.165$ mb, measured by the ALICE Collaboration.
- The bottom production cross section at 5.02 TeV in central rapidity ($|y|<2.3$) is extracted as ${d\sigma_{pp}^{b\bar b}/dy}=47.5\ \mu$b.
- The $B_c^+$ production cross section at 5.02 TeV pp collisions for $p_T>6$ GeV/c and $|y|<2$ is ${d\sigma_{pp}^{\Bc}\over dy}=30.38$ nb (taking branching ratio $\mathcal{B}=2.37\%$) or $15.86$ nb (taking $\mathcal{B}=4.54\%$).
- The initial temperature of the QGP in most central collisions (impact parameter $b=0$) is $T(\tau_0, {\bf x_T}=0)=510$ MeV, with $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c.
- The critical temperature of the phase transition is $T_c=170$ MeV.
- The mean radius of $B_c^+$ is around 0.4 fm at $T_c$ and increases to $\sim0.8$ fm at $1.2 T_c$ when using the free energy potential.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient is taken as $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=5.0$, with variations of $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T)=4.0$ and $7.0$ studied.
- The mean transverse momentum square of $B_c^+$ in pp collisions is $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp}=25.1\ \mathrm{(GeV/c)^2}$ with $n=4.16$ in the normalized distribution.
- The ratio of $B_c^+$ production cross sections at 5.02 TeV and 1.96 TeV pp collisions is 2.40.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-plus-coalescence approach previously applied to $J/\psi$ production by Chen (2021) to the $B_c^+$ meson, which is a heavier bound state of charm and anti-bottom quarks. The observation of $R_{AA}(B_c^+)>1$ is presented as a stronger signal of deconfined QGP than the traditional $J/\psi$ suppression signal proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), because charmonium and bottomonium $R_{AA}$ values are usually smaller than unity at LHC energies (ALICE 2016, Du 2017, Brambilla 2020). The results enable future studies of the competition between primordial production and coalescence regeneration at different transverse momenta and centralities, and the model can be refined by including energy conservation in the coalescence reaction as done by Yao (2017) and Blaizot (2021).
