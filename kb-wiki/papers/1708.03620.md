# Radiative decays of the doubly charmed baryons in chiral perturbation theory

> We have systematically investigated the spin-$\frac{3}{2}$ to spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ doubly charmed baryon transition magnetic moments to the next-to-next-to-leading order in the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). Numerical results of transition magnetic moments and decay widths are presented to the next-to-leading order: $μ_{Ξ_{cc}^{*++}\rightarrowΞ_{cc}^{++}}=-2.35μ_{N}$, $μ_{Ξ_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrowΞ_{cc}^{+}}=1.55μ_{N}$, $μ_{Ω_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrowΩ_{cc}^{+}}=1.54μ_{N}$, $Γ_{Ξ_{cc}^{*++}\rightarrowΞ_{cc}^{++}}=22.0$ keV, $Γ_{Ξ_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrowΞ_{cc}^{+}}=9.57$ keV, $Γ_{Ω_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrowΩ_{cc}^{+}}=9.45$ keV.

## Key claim

The authors compute the spin-3/2 to spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryon transition magnetic moments and radiative decay widths up to next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), but the numerical results rely on quark-model estimates for the leading-order tree-level transition magnetic moments and axial couplings because no experimental data exist to fix the low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors employ heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to calculate one-loop chiral corrections to the transition magnetic moments order by order, up to next-to-next-to-leading order analytically. They use the quark model to estimate the leading-order axial coupling constants (\(\tilde{g}_A\), \(\tilde{g}_B\), \(\tilde{g}_C\)) and the tree-level transition magnetic moments, and explicitly include the mass splitting \(\delta = M_T - M_H\) between the spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons. Sixteen one-loop Feynman diagrams are evaluated, and the numerical results are presented to next-to-leading order (NLO), while analytical expressions are given to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).

## Key numbers

- \(\mu_{\Xi_{cc}^{*++}\rightarrow\Xi_{cc}^{++}} = -2.35\,\mu_N\) (transition magnetic moment, NLO result)
- \(\mu_{\Xi_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrow\Xi_{cc}^{+}} = 1.55\,\mu_N\) (transition magnetic moment, NLO result)
- \(\mu_{\Omega_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrow\Omega_{cc}^{+}} = 1.54\,\mu_N\) (transition magnetic moment, NLO result)
- \(\Gamma_{\Xi_{cc}^{*++}\rightarrow\Xi_{cc}^{++}} = 22.0\) keV (radiative decay width)
- \(\Gamma_{\Xi_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrow\Xi_{cc}^{+}} = 9.57\) keV (radiative decay width)
- \(\Gamma_{\Omega_{cc}^{*+}\rightarrow\Omega_{cc}^{+}} = 9.45\) keV (radiative decay width)
- Mass splitting \(\delta = 0.1\) GeV used between spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons
- Spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryon mass \(M_H = 3.62\) GeV used in calculations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of doubly charmed baryon magnetic moments (Lichtenberg (1976), Li3) by systematically including chiral loop corrections for the spin-3/2 to spin-1/2 transition, which had been rarely studied. The results are compared with predictions from the nonrelativistic quark model with effective quark mass (Dhir (2013)), SU(4) chiral constituent quark model (Sharma (2010)), MIT bag model (Bernotas (2013)), and nonrelativistic quark model. The analytical expressions enable future chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations of doubly charmed baryon electromagnetic properties.
