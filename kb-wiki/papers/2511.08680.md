# Isospin-breaking in the $ππ$ scattering amplitude I: Effects due to the pion mass difference

> This is the first of a series of papers devoted to a detailed analysis of isospin-breaking effects in the $ππ$ scattering amplitude and the vector form factor of the pion. Isospin breaking originates from the mass difference between up and down quarks and from electromagnetic effects. The latter can be further split into effects due to the pion-mass difference and the remaining virtual and real photonic effects. In this paper, we derive the modifications to the Roy equations for $ππ$ scattering due to the mass difference between the charged and the neutral pion. We solve the equations numerically after matching them to the chiral representation of the $ππ$ scattering amplitude evaluated in the same approximation, which is also provided. Numerical results are presented and discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives and solves modified Roy equations for ππ scattering that incorporate the mass difference between charged and neutral pions (Mπ+ − Mπ0), treating this as a separate isospin-breaking effect while neglecting the up-down quark mass difference (which is argued to be of order (mu−md)² and thus beyond the target accuracy). The authors claim that only the SP (S- and P-wave) part of the amplitude is sensitive to the pion mass difference, while the background polynomial part is little affected.

## Method

The authors start from the isospin-limit dispersive representation of the ππ scattering amplitude in terms of single-variable functions W⁰, W¹, W², then decompose the physical amplitudes (π⁺π⁻ → π⁺π⁻, π⁺π⁻ → π⁰π⁰, π⁰π⁰ → π⁰π⁰, π⁺π⁺ → π⁺π⁺, π⁺π⁰ → π⁺π⁰) into new single-variable functions labeled by the charge of the intermediate pions (e.g., W_S^{n,00}, W_S^{n,+-}). They write unitarity relations in matrix form for the coupled S-wave channels and as standard optical theorems for the remaining partial waves, using distinct phase-space factors σ(s) = √(1−4Mπ²/s) for charged pions and σ₀(s) = √(1−4Mπ0²/s) for neutral pions. The subtraction constants (scattering lengths) are fixed by matching to the chiral perturbation theory (χPT) representation of the amplitudes at order p⁴, including the pion-mass difference effects.

## Key numbers

- Charged pion mass: Mπ = 139.57039 MeV (the value used in the isospin-limit Roy equations)
- Upper limit for the dispersive integrals: √s₂ = 2 GeV
- Upper limit of validity of the Roy equations: s₁ = 68 Mπ²
- The parameter ξ = Mπ²/(16π²Fπ²) appears in the chiral expansion of threshold parameters
- The parameter δπ = (Mπ² − Mπ0²)/Mπ² is used to quantify the pion mass difference
- The average pion mass M̄π = (Mπ + Mπ0)/2 appears in the definition of the π⁺π⁰ → π⁺π⁰ amplitude

## Context

This paper is the first in a series that aims to provide a model-independent dispersive treatment of isospin-breaking effects in ππ scattering and the pion vector form factor, motivated by the need for precise input to the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon g−2. It extends the Roy-equation framework of Ananthanarayan (2000) and Colangelo (2001), which solved the equations in the isospin limit with all pion masses set equal to the charged pion mass. The treatment of the pion-mass difference in ππ scattering was previously addressed for Kₑ₄ decays by Colangelo (2008) and Bernard (2013, 2015) using χPT or non-relativistic expansions, and for η → 3π by Colangelo (2018) using a kinematic mapping approach. The present work aims to go beyond these approximations with a full dispersive treatment. The χPT matching uses the amplitudes calculated by Knecht and Urech (1997) for π⁺π⁻ → π⁰π⁰ and by Knecht and Nehme (2002) for π⁺π⁻ → π⁺π⁻.
