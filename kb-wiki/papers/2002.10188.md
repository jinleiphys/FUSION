# Perturbative Thermal QCD: Formalism and Applications

> In this review article, we discuss the current status and future prospects of perturbation theory as a means of studying the equilibrium thermodynamic and near-equilibrium transport properties of deconfined QCD matter. We begin with a brief introduction to the general topic, after which we review in some detail the foundations and modern techniques of the real- and imaginary-time formalisms of thermal field theory, covering e.g. the different bases used in the real-time formalism and the resummations required to deal with soft and collinear contributions. After this, we discuss the current status of applications of these techniques, including topics such as electromagnetic rates, transport coefficients, jet quenching, heavy quarks and quarkonia, and the Equations of State of hot quark-gluon plasma as well as cold and dense quark matter. Finally, we conclude with our view of the future directions of the field, i.e. how we anticipate perturbative calculations to contribute to our collective understanding of strongly interacting matter in the coming years.

## Key claim

This review argues that perturbative thermal QCD, despite being traditionally considered a "last resort" due to large couplings, remains a valuable first-principles tool for studying deconfined QCD matter, particularly in regimes where lattice simulations are unavailable (non-equilibrium, real-time quantities, and high baryon densities). The authors emphasize that creative application of perturbative methods has led to advances beyond the realm of very small couplings and ultrahigh energies, though they acknowledge fundamental limitations from nonperturbative infrared physics such as the Linde problem and plasma instabilities.

## Method

The paper provides a pedagogical review of both the real-time (r/a basis) and imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalisms of thermal field theory, including detailed Feynman rules for fermions and gluons in various gauges. It then surveys applications of these techniques to equilibrium thermodynamics (equation of state at high temperature and dense quark matter) and near-equilibrium transport (electromagnetic rates, transport coefficients, jet quenching, heavy quarks and quarkonia), with particular emphasis on resummation methods such as dimensional reduction (EQCD/MQCD) and Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) to handle soft and collinear contributions.

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the perturbative thermal QCD formalism previously covered in textbooks by Kapusta (1989, 2006) and Le Bellac (1996), providing the first detailed self-consistent account of modern real-time methods since Le Bellac. It complements lattice QCD results (e.g., Borsanyi 2010, 2012; Bellwied 2015) by addressing regimes inaccessible to lattice methods (real-time quantities, non-equilibrium, high baryon density). The paper identifies open challenges including the Linde problem (Linde 1980), non-abelian plasma instabilities (Mrowczynski 1993, 1996; Arnold 2003, 2004, 2005; Romatschke 2003, 2004, 2006; Rebhan 2004, 2005, 2008, 2009), and the need to extend perturbative equations of state for dense quark matter (Kurkela 2016; Gorda 2018) to lower densities relevant for neutron star physics (Annala 2019).
