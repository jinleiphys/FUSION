# High-pT hadrons from nuclear collisions: Unifying pQCD with hydrodynamics

> Hadrons inclusively produced with large pT in high-energy collisions originate from the jets, whose initial virtuality and energy are of the same order, what leads to an extremely intensive gluon radiation and dissipation of energy at the early stage of hadronization. Besides, these jets have a peculiar structure: the main fraction of the jet energy is carried by a single leading hadron, so such jets are very rare. The constraints imposed by energy conservation enforce an early color neutralization and a cease of gluon radiation. The produced colorless dipole does not dissipate energy anymore and is evolving to form the hadron wave function. The small and medium pT region is dominated by the hydrodynamic mechanisms of hadron production from the created hot medium. The abrupt transition between the hydrodynamic and perturbative QCD mechanisms causes distinct minima in the pT dependence of the suppression factor R_{AA} and of the azimuthal asymmetry v2. Combination of these mechanisms allows to describe the data through the full range of pT at different collision energies and centralities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) in heavy-ion collisions can be described across the full measured range of transverse momentum by combining a hydrodynamic mechanism for low and medium \(p_T\) with a perturbative QCD mechanism for high \(p_T\), where the abrupt transition between the two mechanisms produces distinct minima in both observables. The key hedge is that the pQCD mechanism is explicitly stated to be "irrelevant to data at \(p_T\lesssim 6\GeV\)", which are instead dominated by statistical/hydrodynamic mechanisms.

## Method

The authors combine two separate calculations: a hydrodynamic model for hadron evaporation from the hot medium (taken from prior work by the same group) and a perturbative QCD model for high-\(p_T\) hadron production based on a non-energy-loss scenario. The pQCD model treats the rare jets where a single leading hadron carries most of the jet energy, calculates the short production length \(l_p\) for color neutralization, and then computes the survival probability of the resulting colorless dipole as it propagates through the medium using a path integral technique with a single adjustable parameter, the transport coefficient \(q_0\). The two mechanisms are combined to produce predictions for \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) and \(v_2(p_T)\) at different centralities and collision energies.

## Key numbers

- Transport coefficient \(q_0 = 2\GeV^2/\fm\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\TeV\) for lead-lead collisions.
- Transport coefficient \(q_0 = 1.6\GeV^2/\fm\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\GeV\) for gold-gold collisions.
- The pQCD mechanism is stated to be irrelevant for data at \(p_T\lesssim 6\GeV\).
- The authors expect a fall of \(R_{AA}\) at higher \(p_T\gsim 100\GeV\) at LHC energies.
- The production length \(l_p\) is described as "rather short" and "nearly independent of jet energy", though no explicit numerical value is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pQCD model (Nemchik et al., 2013) and hydrodynamic model (Karpenko et al., 2013) by combining them to describe data across the full \(p_T\) range, whereas earlier treatments only covered one regime. The approach contrasts with energy-loss-based jet quenching models and instead attributes suppression to the survival probability of colorless dipoles produced on a short length scale. The results enable a unified description of both \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data from ALICE, CMS, and PHENIX experiments at LHC and RHIC energies, with the same transport coefficient used for both observables at each energy.
