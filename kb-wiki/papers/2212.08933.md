# Spatial diffusion of heavy quarks in background magnetic field

> The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density shows a valley-shaped pattern well-known in the community of heavy-ion physics. Diffusion coefficients of heavy quark and meson shows the similar structure, and both sketches have become quite popular in the community. Present work has attempted a finite magnetic field extension of the diffusion coefficients of heavy quark and meson. Using Einstein's diffusion relation, we calculated heavy quark and heavy meson diffusion by the ratio of conductivity to susceptibility in the kinetic theory framework of relaxation time approximation. The relaxation time of heavy quark and meson are tuned from the knowledge of earlier works on spatial diffusion estimations, and then we have extended the framework for a finite magnetic field, where our outcomes have revealed two aspects - anisotropic and quantum aspects of diffusion with future possibilities of phenomenological signature.

## Key claim

The paper extends the known valley-shaped pattern of normalized spatial diffusion of heavy quarks and mesons to finite magnetic field, revealing anisotropic (longitudinal vs. transverse) and quantum (Landau quantization) aspects, but the extension relies on tuning relaxation times from zero-field results and the magnetic field values considered (eB = 0.4 GeV²) are at a single representative strength rather than a systematic scan.

## Method

Using Einstein's relation D = σ/χ, the authors calculated heavy quark (charm) and heavy meson (D⁺) spatial diffusion from the ratio of conductivity to susceptibility in the kinetic theory framework with relaxation time approximation (RTA). For the finite magnetic field case, they extended the RTA expressions to include anisotropic conductivity components (longitudinal and transverse) via an effective relaxation time modified by the cyclotron time scale, and also implemented a quantum mechanical version incorporating Landau quantization of energies and phase space. The relaxation times for charm quarks and D⁺ mesons were tuned by fitting zero-field results to existing model calculations from Ghosh et al. (2011), He et al. (2011), Tolos et al. (2013), Banerjee et al. (2011), Berrehrah et al. (2014), and Rapp et al. (2008).

## Key numbers

- For charm quarks in the quark temperature domain (T = 0.2–0.4 GeV), constant relaxation times τ_c = 3–6 fm were used to cover the range of non-pQCD model estimations.
- For D⁺ mesons in the hadronic temperature domain (T = 0.1–0.18 GeV), a parameterized relaxation time τ_c(T)_Rapp = 2794.15 × e^(−37 T) fm (from He et al. 2011) was used.
- Longitudinal and transverse components of conductivity and diffusion were calculated at a fixed magnetic field strength eB = 0.4 GeV² for both charm quarks and D⁺ mesons.
- The relative percentage change Δσ/σ_RTA between quantum mechanical and RTA results for longitudinal conductivity shows quantum enhancement for charm quarks (fermions) and quantum suppression for D⁺ mesons (bosons), both decreasing with temperature at eB = 0.4 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the well-known valley-shaped pattern of normalized spatial diffusion (2πTD) for heavy quarks and mesons, previously established at zero magnetic field by studies such as Abreu (2011), Ghosh et al. (2011), He et al. (2011), Tolos et al. (2013), Banerjee et al. (2011), Berrehrah et al. (2014), and Rapp et al. (2008), into a finite magnetic field regime. It connects to holographic QCD results from Dudal et al. (2018) who first showed anisotropic splitting of spatial diffusion in a magnetic field, and to leading-order QCD estimates by Fukushima (2015). The results enable future phenomenological studies of heavy quark diffusion in off-central heavy-ion collisions where transient magnetic fields of order 10¹⁸–10¹⁹ Gauss are expected at RHIC and LHC.
