# Statistical spectroscopic calculation of expectation values and spin-cutoff factors

> Recently we proposed a formalism, based in nuclear statistical spectroscopy, for efficient computation of nuclear level density, or densities of states, through a sum of partitioned binomial functions (SUPARB). In this Letter we extend the formalism to the calculation of locally averaged expectation values, with specific application to spin-cutoff factors and the angular momentum dependence of the nuclear density of states.

# Statistical spectroscopic calculation of expectation values and spin-cutoff factors

## Key claim
The SUPARB (SUm of PARtitioned Binomials) formalism, previously developed for nuclear level densities, can be extended to compute locally averaged expectation values of operators, including spin-cutoff factors and the angular momentum dependence of the nuclear density of states, provided one computes up to second-order weighted averages of the operator with the Hamiltonian within each configuration subspace.

## Method
The authors partition the nuclear model space into single-particle configurations (e.g., $(0d_{5/2})^4$) and compute configuration moments (centroid, width, third and fourth scaled moments) directly from one- and two-body matrix elements. For each configuration, the partial density is modeled as a continuous binomial distribution fitted to these moments. To compute expectation values of an operator $\hat{\cal O}$, they additionally compute the weighted averages $\langle \hat{\cal O} \rangle_\alpha$, $\langle \hat{\cal O}(\hat{H}-\bar{E}_\alpha) \rangle_\alpha$, and $\langle \hat{\cal O}(\hat{H}-\bar{E}_\alpha)^2 \rangle_\alpha$ from the same matrix elements, then assume a quadratic energy dependence within each subspace to extract the energy-dependent locally averaged expectation value. The total expectation value is obtained by summing over configurations weighted by their partial densities.

- The spin-cutoff factor $\sigma(E)$ is related to the expectation value of $\hat{J}^2$ by $\langle \hat{J}^2 \rangle = 3 \sigma^2 - 1/4$, accounting for the $2J+1$ degeneracy in the traces.

## Context
This work extends the SUPARB formalism introduced by Johnson et al. (2001) for nuclear level densities to the computation of locally averaged expectation values, enabling efficient calculation of spin-cutoff factors and $J$-dependent level densities without random sampling or Hermite polynomial approximations used by Grimes (1983). The method builds on nuclear statistical spectroscopy techniques from French (1971), Ayik and Ginocchio (1974), and Wong (1986), and uses the binomial distribution suggestion by Zuker (2001). It enables future applications such as estimating spurious state contamination in cross-shell calculations and computing total strengths and energy-weighted sum rules for transitions.
