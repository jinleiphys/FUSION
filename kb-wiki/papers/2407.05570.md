# Volume effect on the extraction of sound velocity in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The determination of the speed of sound in quark-gluon plasma is a crucial aspect of understanding the properties of strongly interacting matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this study, we investigate the impact of initial-state fluctuations on the extraction of the speed of sound in a quark-gluon plasma in the ultra-central collisions. By employing the TRENTo model for simulating initial conditions, we demonstrate that these fluctuations lead to sizable volume effect, which in turn corrects the measured values of the sound velocity. With respect to realistic conditions of high-energy heavy-ion experiments, we provide quantitative estimate of these corrections.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that initial-state fluctuations in ultra-central heavy-ion collisions produce a finite volume effect that systematically biases the extraction of the speed of sound from the ratio of relative changes in mean transverse momentum and charged particle multiplicity, requiring a correction factor that depends on the relative change of effective volume with entropy.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTo model to simulate initial-state entropy density profiles for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, generating approximately five million events across the 0% to 5% centrality class. They solved for effective temperature and effective volume from the initial-state total energy and entropy using a parameterized lattice QCD equation of state, then extracted the speed of sound both directly from the linear relation between effective temperature and entropy and with a volume-effect correction term.

## Key numbers

- The effective nucleon width parameter w was varied around 1.0 fm (values 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 fm), with the fluctuation parameter k fixed at 1.0 and the generalized mean p fixed at 0
- The fluctuation parameter k was scanned around its most probable value of 1.0 (including k=0.5), with w=1.0 fm and p=0 fixed
- The generalized mean p was varied from -1 to 1, with k=1.0 and w=1.0 fm fixed
- At effective temperature T_eff=225 MeV, corresponding to ultra-central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, the uncorrected speed of sound squared (c_s^0)^2 showed systematic deviations depending on p and w
- The volume effect correction ratio alpha = Delta ln V_eff / Delta ln S was found to be 0.065 +/- 0.025 for the measured effective temperature of 220 MeV and charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/dy = 1943 +/- 54 in ultra-central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Gardim et al. (2019) for measuring the speed of sound in quark-gluon plasma from ultra-central collisions, which was subsequently applied by the CMS collaboration (2024) and ALICE collaboration. The authors show that the simple extraction formula from Gardim et al. (2019) must be corrected for volume effects arising from initial-state fluctuations, consistent with earlier theoretical analyses by Gardim et al. (2019, 2024), Soares Rocha et al. (2024), and Nijs et al. (2023). The corrected extraction yields results independent of initial-state model parameters, enabling more reliable experimental determination of the speed of sound.
