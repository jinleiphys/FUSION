# Di-hadron and Tri-hadron correlation and Mach-like cone structure

> In a framework of a multi-phase transport model with both partonic and hadronic interactions, azimuthal correlations between trigger particles and associated scattering particles in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV/$c$ have been studied by the mixing-event technique. The Mach-like structure has been observed in correlation function for central collisions. It is shown that the Mach-like structure is basically born in the partonic process and further developed in hadronic rescattering process. However, hadronic rescattering alone cannot reproduce the amplitude of Mach-like cone on away side, therefore partonic cascade process is necessary to describe the amplitude of Mach-like cone on away side in experiment. In addition, three-particle correlations have been investigated in central Au + Au collisions with the AMPT model, and the results support the conclusion that partonic cascade processes enhance the opening angle of Mach-like cone structures.

## Key claim

The Mach-like cone structure observed in two-particle azimuthal correlations in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is primarily formed during the partonic cascade process and further developed by hadronic rescattering; however, hadronic rescattering alone cannot reproduce the amplitude of the Mach-like cone on the away side, making the partonic cascade process necessary to match experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with string melting, which includes initial conditions from HIJING, parton scatterings via Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) with a 10 mb cross section, quark coalescence for hadronization, and hadronic rescattering via the ART model. Two-particle azimuthal correlations were computed using the mixing-event technique with ZYAM (zero yield at minimum) background subtraction for trigger particles with 3 < pTtrig < 6 GeV/c and associated particles with 0.15 < pTassoc < 3 GeV/c (soft) or 2.5 < pTtrig < 4 GeV/c and 1.0 < pTassoc < 2.5 GeV/c (hard). Three-particle correlations were also analyzed with the same hard pT cuts, subtracting three types of backgrounds (hard-soft, soft-soft, and random) to isolate signal from trigger particle and two associated particles.

## Key numbers

- Parton interaction cross section in the AMPT model with string melting: 10 mb
- Trigger hadron pT range for soft correlations: 3 < pTtrig < 6 GeV/c; associated hadron pT range: 0.15 < pTassoc < 3 GeV/c
- Trigger hadron pT range for hard correlations: 2.5 < pTtrig < 4 GeV/c; associated hadron pT range: 1.0 < pTassoc < 2.5 GeV/c
- Experimental data for soft correlations were multiplied by a factor of 1.58 to account for neutral hadron contributions
- Three-particle correlations were analyzed for the 10% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet quenching and Mach-like structures observed by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC, where two-particle correlation functions showed a double-peaked away-side structure in central Au+Au collisions. The authors use the AMPT model to test competing explanations for this structure, including Mach shock waves (Stocker, 2005; Casalderrey-Solana, 2005; Ruppert, 2005), Cherenkov radiation (Koch, 2005), and medium dragging effects (Armesto, 2005). The results support the interpretation that partonic interactions are essential for producing the observed Mach-like cone amplitude, and the three-particle correlation analysis further shows that partonic cascade processes enhance the opening angle of the Mach-like cone structures.
