# Solving Dirac equations on a 3D lattice with inverse Hamiltonian and spectral methods

> A new method to solve the Dirac equation on a 3D lattice is proposed, in which the variational collapse problem is avoided by the inverse Hamiltonian method and the fermion doubling problem is avoided by performing spatial derivatives in momentum space with the help of the discrete Fourier transform, i.e., the spectral method. This method is demonstrated in solving the Dirac equation for a given spherical potential in 3D lattice space. In comparison with the results obtained by the shooting method, the differences in single particle energy are smaller than $10^{-4}$~MeV, and the densities are almost identical, which demonstrates the high accuracy of the present method. The results obtained by applying this method without any modification to solve the Dirac equations for an axial deformed, non-axial deformed, and octupole deformed potential are provided and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method to solve the Dirac equation on a 3D lattice that simultaneously avoids the variational collapse problem (via the inverse Hamiltonian method) and the fermion doubling problem (via spectral derivatives in momentum space), achieving single-particle energy differences smaller than $10^{-4}$ MeV compared to the shooting method for spherical potentials, but only when appropriate step and box sizes are chosen.

## Method

The inverse Hamiltonian method is used to avoid variational collapse by evolving the wave function with the operator $\exp[\tau/(\hat{h} - W)]$ instead of $\exp(-\hat{h}\tau)$, where $W$ is an auxiliary parameter that selects the desired eigenstate. Spatial derivatives in the Dirac equation are performed in momentum space using discrete Fourier transforms (the spectral method) to avoid the fermion doubling problem that arises from finite-difference approximations. The method is demonstrated for Woods-Saxon type potentials on a 3D lattice with box sizes up to 31.2 fm and step sizes down to 0.8 fm, with convergence accelerated by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian within the space of evolution wave functions every 10 iterations.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energy differences between the 3D lattice calculation and the shooting method are smaller than $10^{-4}$ MeV for all bound states when using step size $d = 0.8$ fm and box size $L = 31.2$ fm
- With step size $d = 1.0$ fm and box size $L = 23.0$ fm, absolute deviations are smaller than $10^{-3}$ MeV except for weakly bound states 1f$_{5/2}$, 2p$_{3/2}$, and 2p$_{1/2}$
- With step size $d = 0.8$ fm and box size $L = 23.2$ fm, absolute deviations are less than $10^{-4}$ MeV except for 2p$_{3/2}$ and 2p$_{1/2}$
- Convergence to $10^{-4}$ MeV accuracy for all 40 bound single-particle states is achieved after 39 iterations
- The imaginary time step size $\Delta T$ is taken as 100 MeV
- The energy shift parameter $\Delta W_1$ is set to 6 MeV for the first level

## Context

This work extends the inverse Hamiltonian method of Hagino and Tanimura (2010) for avoiding variational collapse in Dirac equation solutions, and addresses the fermion doubling problem that Tanimura, Hagino, and Liang (2015) previously attempted to solve using high-order Wilson terms, which required perturbative corrections and introduced artificial symmetry breaking. The new method enables self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations on a 3D lattice without geometric restrictions, allowing future applications to nuclei with arbitrary shapes, rotating nuclei with arbitrary rotation axes, and time-dependent CDFT for heavy-ion collisions.
