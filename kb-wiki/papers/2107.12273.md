# Trapped two-nucleon system in energy-dependent effective field theory

> We discuss how to connect the energy levels of two-particle systems trapped by a harmonic-oscillator force to scattering amplitudes, with nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts in uncoupled channels as the application. At the center of the proposed framework is the energy-dependent effective field theory that aims to expand observables in a neighborhood around each reference energy, often taken to be one of the energy levels. We also investigate how to disentangle the trapping force at short distances and the intrinsic interaction between the particles.

# Trapped two-nucleon system in energy-dependent effective field theory

## Key claim

The paper proposes an energy-dependent effective field theory (EDT) that expands observables in a kinematic window around a reference center-of-mass energy, enabling connection of harmonic-oscillator trapped energy levels to scattering amplitudes; the framework recovers the BERW formula at leading order but, unlike previous approaches, allows systematic calculation of subleading corrections and extrapolation to the continuum limit by removing the artificial dependence on the trapping frequency.

## Method

The authors construct an EDT where low-energy constants (LECs) depend on a reference energy E_r, and expand scattering amplitudes and trapped-state energies in powers of (E - E_r). For the 1S0 channel, the LO contact term is iterated nonperturbatively via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while for P waves (3P0), a perturbative power counting is used due to renormalization issues with nonperturbative iteration. Energy eigenvalues from an underlying chiral NN potential (leading-order one-pion exchange plus contact term, regularized with a Gaussian cutoff Lambda = 400 MeV) serve as "data" to determine the EDT LECs, and the resulting phase shifts are compared to the underlying potential's predictions. The infrared artifact from finite trapping frequency omega is removed by extrapolating scattering observables as polynomials in omega^2 to the omega^2 -> 0 limit.

## Key numbers

- For omega = 10.0 MeV, the ground-state CM energy is E_0 = 7.47 MeV, used as the reference energy E_r.
- The 1S0 scattering length a extracted at NNLO for omega = 6.0 MeV is a^(0) = -9.66 fm, a^(1) = -5.95 fm, a^(2) = -3.24 fm, with truncation uncertainty Delta a = 1.8 fm.
- The 1S0 scattering length a extracted at NNLO for omega = 10.0 MeV is a^(0) = -6.59 fm, a^(1) = -5.34 fm, a^(2) = -3.45 fm, with truncation uncertainty Delta a = 2.2 fm.
- The infinite-volume limit of the 1S0 scattering length from a third-degree polynomial fit in omega^2 gives a_infinity = -23.4 fm, compared to the "true" value of -23.74 fm.
- For omega = 56 MeV, the ground-state phase shifts near E_r are only a few degrees off from the underlying potential, despite mu omega r_I^2 ~ 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the BERW formula (Busch et al., 1998) for connecting trapped energy levels to scattering amplitudes by providing a systematic EFT expansion beyond leading order. It builds on prior efforts using EFT for HO-trapped systems (Stetcu et al., 2010; Rotureau et al., 2010, 2011; Zhang et al., 2019, 2020; Guo et al., 2021), but differs by making LECs depend on a reference energy rather than expanding only near threshold. The framework enables future applications to more complicated nuclear reactions, including cluster/halo EFT descriptions and coupled-channel effects, though challenges remain for three-body channels and estimating required omega values for reliable continuum extrapolation.
