# Approximate degeneracy of heavy-light mesons with the same $L$

> Careful observation of the experimental spectra of heavy-light mesons tells us that heavy-light mesons with the same angular momentum $L$ are almost degenerate. The estimate is given how much this degeneracy is broken in our relativistic potential model, and it is analytically shown that expectation values of a commutator between the lowest order Hamiltonian and ${\vec L}^{~2}$ are of the order of $1/m_Q$ with a heavy quark mass $m_Q$. It turns out that nonrelativistic approximation of heavy quark system has a rotational symmetry and hence degeneracy among states with the same $L$. This feature can be tested by measuring higher orbitally and radially excited heavy-light meson spectra for $D/D_s/B/B_s$ in LHCb and forthcoming BelleII.

## Key claim

Heavy-light mesons with the same orbital angular momentum \(L\) are approximately degenerate in mass, and the breaking of this degeneracy is analytically shown to be of order \(1/m_Q\) (with heavy quark mass \(m_Q\)), meaning the degeneracy becomes exact in the combined limit of infinite heavy quark mass and nonrelativistic approximation.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic potential model where the light quark is treated as a four-component Dirac spinor, and apply the Foldy-Wouthouysen-Tani transformation to obtain the equation of motion for a \(Q\bar q\) bound system. They analytically compute the commutator \([H_0, \vec L^2]\) between the lowest-order Hamiltonian and the squared angular momentum operator, and evaluate its expectation value for heavy-light meson states using wave functions that include both positive- and negative-energy components of the heavy quark, expanded to first order in \(1/m_Q\).

## Key numbers

- Mass gap between spin multiplets with different \(L\) is approximately \(\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 300\) MeV (for \(n_f=4\)).
- For \(D\) mesons: average mass of the \((0^-,1^-)\) spin doublet with \(L=0\) is 1938 MeV; average mass of the \((0^+,1^+)\) doublet with \(L=1\) is 2394 MeV; gap between these two multiplets is 456 MeV.
- For \(D\) mesons: average mass of the \((1^+,2^+)\) doublet with \(L=1\) is 2443 MeV; gap between the two \(L=1\) doublets \((0^+,1^+)\) and \((1^+,2^+)\) is 49 MeV.
- For \(D\) mesons: average mass of the \((1^-,2^-)\) doublet with \(L=2\) is 2763 MeV; gap between the \(L=1\) \((1^+,2^+)\) doublet and the \(L=2\) \((1^-,2^-)\) doublet is 320 MeV.
- Expectation values of \([H_0, \vec L^2]\) are at most of order \(1/m_Q\) for \(0^-\) and \(1^-\) states, with the leading nonvanishing contribution starting at order \((1/m_Q)^2\) for the \(0^-\) state.

## Context

This work extends the observation that the Godfrey-Isgur (GI) model (Godfrey, 1985; Godfrey, 1986) produces near-degenerate masses for states with the same \(L\) because its lowest-order Hamiltonian commutes with \(\vec L\). The authors show that even models respecting heavy quark symmetry, such as those of ZVR, DE, EFG, and their own MMS model, produce similar degeneracy, which is analytically traced to the \(1/m_Q\) suppression of the commutator. The result enables future tests by measuring higher orbitally and radially excited heavy-light meson spectra for \(D/D_s/B/B_s\) at LHCb and forthcoming BelleII, and can be extended to baryons like \(\Lambda_c\) and multiquark states containing one light quark.
