# Is there np pairing in odd-odd N=Z nuclei?

> The binding energies of even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei are compared. After correcting for the symmetry energy we find that the lowest T=1 state in odd-odd N=Z nuclei is as bound as the ground state in the neighboring even-even nucleus, thus providing evidence for isovector np pairing. However, T=0 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei are several MeV less bound than the even-even ground states. We associate this difference with a pair gap and conclude that there is no evidence for an isoscalar pairing condensate in N=Z nuclei.

# Is there np pairing in odd-odd N=Z nuclei?

## Key claim
After correcting for the symmetry energy, the lowest T=1 state in odd-odd N=Z nuclei is as bound as the ground state in the neighboring even-even nucleus, providing evidence for isovector np pairing. However, T=0 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei are several MeV less bound than the even-even ground states, and this difference is associated with a pair gap, leading to the conclusion that there is no evidence for an isoscalar pairing condensate in N=Z nuclei.

## Method
The authors compared experimental binding energies of even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei from the Audi-Wapstra mass compilation. For T=0 states, they used the difference between the even-even binding energy and the average binding energy of two neighboring odd-odd nuclei to isolate pairing effects. For T=1 states, they applied a symmetry energy correction of 75 MeV/A * T(T+1) to account for the different isospins of the compared states, and also used the binding energies of T=1 isobaric analog states in even-even nuclei as an alternative reference.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy used for the isospin correction is given by E_sym = (75 MeV / A) * T(T+1), which for T=1 states gives 150 MeV / A.
- The conventional pair gap follows a smooth dependence of Delta ~ 12 MeV / A^(1/2).
- A BCS calculation with pairing strength of 20 MeV / A was used to compare with the extracted effective np gap.
- The volume, surface, and Coulomb terms subtracted to extract the symmetry energy were: E_vol = -15A MeV, E_surface = 17A^(2/3) MeV, and E_Coulomb = 0.7 * Z^2 / A^(1/3) MeV.
- The residual np interaction correction term is of order 20 MeV / A.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of np pairing in N=Z nuclei that began with early BCS treatments by Goodman (1979) and continued with schematic and algebraic models by Engel, Langanke, and Vogel (1996) and Van Isacker and Warner (1997), as well as realistic shell model calculations by Poves and Martinez-Pinedo (1998). The analysis uses the standard binding energy difference method from Bohr and Mottelson (1969). The results contradict the possibility of significant isoscalar T=0 np pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei, while supporting full isovector pairing (nn, pp, and np). The competition between symmetry energy and isovector pairing explains the observed switch from T=0 to T=1 ground states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei above A=40, except near closed shells. A similar analysis with comparable conclusions was reported in a preprint by P. Vogel (nucl-th/980515).
