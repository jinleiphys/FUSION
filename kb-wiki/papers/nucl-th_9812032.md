# DCC dynamics with the SU(3) linear sigma model

> The SU(3) extension of the linear sigma model is employed to elucidate the effect of including strangeness on the formation of disoriented chiral condensates. By means of a Hartree factorization, approximate dispersion relations for the 18 scalar and pseudoscalar meson species are derived and their self-consistent solution makes it possible to trace out the thermal path of the two order parameters as well as delineate the region of instability within which spontaneous pair creation becomes possible. The results depend significantly on the employed sigma mass, with the highest values yielding the largest regions of instability. An approximate solution of the equations of motion for the order parameter in scenarios emulating uniform scaling expansions show that even with a rapid quench only the pionic modes grow unstable. Nevertheless, the rapid and oscillatory relaxation of the order parameters leads to enhanced production of both pions and (to a lesser degree) kaons.

## Key claim

The SU(3) extension of the linear sigma model shows that even with a rapid quench, only the pionic modes grow unstable during disoriented chiral condensate formation, though the oscillatory relaxation of the order parameters leads to enhanced production of both pions and, to a lesser degree, kaons. The results depend significantly on the employed sigma mass, with the highest values yielding the largest regions of instability.

## Method

The authors employed the SU(3) linear sigma model with a Hartree factorization to derive approximate dispersion relations for all 18 scalar and pseudoscalar meson species. They solved the coupled equations self-consistently to trace the thermal path of the two order parameters (light quark condensate sigma and strange quark condensate zeta) and delineated regions of instability where spontaneous pair creation becomes possible. They then solved the equations of motion for the order parameters in scenarios emulating uniform scaling expansions to study DCC dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum expectation values are sigma_vac = 92.4 MeV and zeta_vac = 94.5 MeV, fixed by pion and kaon decay constants
- The sigma mass is not well known, with current estimates ranging between 400 and 1200 MeV; the authors consider four cases: m_sigma = 400, 600, 800, and 1000 MeV
- The mass parameter mu^2 is only positive for m_sigma > 724 MeV
- The pseudoscalar mixing angle is theta_p = -5 degrees, compared to the Gell-Mann-Okubo formula value of -10 degrees and experimental value of -20 degrees
- For m_sigma = 400 MeV, only pions and eta mesons exhibit instabilities, with no combination of order parameters causing kaons to become unstable
- For m_sigma = 800 MeV, instabilities become possible for all four pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K, eta, eta') in a small region near the origin
- For m_sigma = 1 GeV, kaons become unstable over a wide order-parameter region
- Kaon fluctuations constitute a 20% correction at T = 100 MeV and are half the pion contribution at T = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) linear sigma model studies of disoriented chiral condensates (Rajagopal 1993, Gavin 1994) to include strangeness via the SU(3) version first introduced by Levy (1967). The SU(3) model has a richer structure with two order parameters and eighteen meson fields rather than four. The results enable future studies of DCC formation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where the high temperatures required for approximate chiral symmetry restoration make the strange sector relevant. The authors note that the coupling constants are assumed temperature-independent, which is a strong assumption especially for the anomaly term, and leave the question of how coupling constants scale with temperature for future investigations.
