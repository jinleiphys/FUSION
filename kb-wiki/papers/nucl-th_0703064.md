# Nuclear Superfluidity and Cooling Time of Neutron-Star Crust

> We analyse the effect of neutron superfluidity on the cooling time of inner crust matter in neutron stars, in the case of a rapid cooling of the core. The specific heat of the inner crust, which determines the thermal response of the crust, is calculated in the framework of HFB approach at finite temperature. The calculations are performed with two paring forces chosen to simulate the pairing properties of uniform neutron matter corresponding respectively to Gogny-BCS approximation and to many-body techniques including polarisation effects. Using a simple model for the heat transport across the inner crust, it is shown that the two pairing forces give very different values for the cooling time.

## Key claim

The cooling time of the inner crust in a rapid-cooling neutron star depends critically on the strength of neutron pairing correlations, with the choice between a strong pairing force (Gogny-BCS type) and a weak pairing force (including polarization effects) changing the cooling time by about a factor of three, and ignoring superfluidity entirely increases the cooling time to about 90 years.

## Method

The authors solved finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (FT-HFB) equations for Wigner-Seitz cells representing the inner crust, using the Skyrme force SLy4 for the mean field and two zero-range density-dependent pairing forces (strong and weak) to simulate different scenarios for neutron matter superfluidity. The quasiparticle spectra from these calculations were used to compute the neutron specific heat for each cell at T=0.1 MeV, and the thermal diffusivity was obtained by combining this with an electron-dominated thermal conductivity parametrized by Lattimer et al. The cooling time was then estimated by summing the diffusion times through 10 layers of the inner crust using a simple planar heat-diffusion model.

## Key numbers

- For the strong pairing force, the cooling time across the inner crust is about **12 years**.
- For the weak pairing force, the cooling time increases to about **24 years** (a factor of two larger than the strong force).
- When neutron superfluidity is ignored completely, the cooling time is about **90 years**.
- In the second Wigner-Seitz cell, the specific heat obtained with the two pairing forces differs by about **7 orders of magnitude** at T=0.1 MeV.
- With two alternative zero-range pairing forces (fixed to the free-neutron scattering length), the cooling times are about **9.1 years** and **33.8 years**, respectively.
- The specific heat and diffusivity were calculated at a temperature of **T=0.1 MeV** (typical for the inner crust at the cooling stage analyzed).
- The inner crust cells span baryonic densities from **4.7 × 10^11 g/cm^3** to **7.8 × 10^13 g/cm^3**.

## Context

This work extends the FT-HFB calculations of Sandulescu et al. (2005) for the specific heat of inner crust matter to all relevant densities, and applies them to estimate cooling times using the simple heat-transport model of Brown et al. (1988) and Pizzochero et al. (2002). The results show that the uncertainty in neutron matter pairing (between Gogny-BCS and many-body calculations including polarization effects, such as those by Shen et al.) leads to a factor-of-three variation in the cooling time, highlighting the need for a precise determination of the neutron pairing gap. The authors note that collective modes, which could contribute significantly to the specific heat, were not included and will be addressed in future work.
