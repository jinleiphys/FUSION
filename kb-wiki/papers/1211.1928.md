# Angular and long range rapidity correlations in particle production at high energy

> We discuss the general mechanism leading to long-range rapidity and angular correlations produced in high energy collisions (the "ridge"). This effect naturally appears in the high energy QCD and is strongly sensitive to physics of the gluon saturation. We comment on various recent practical realizations of the main idea, paying special attention to $N_c$ counting and stress the relevance of Pomeron loops.

## Key claim

Long-range rapidity and angular correlations (the "ridge") arise naturally from high-density QCD and gluon saturation in the initial state, without requiring final-state collective flow; the mechanism is most relevant for high-multiplicity events where the gluon density is large, but the authors caution that this explanation is unlikely to account for the ridge in heavy-ion collisions, where collective flow remains the standard interpretation.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of high-density QCD (Color Glass Condensate) and derive expressions for single- and double-inclusive gluon production in the dilute-projectile-on-dense-target approximation, using eikonal scattering matrices and Weizsacker-Williams fields. They analyze the structure of the double-inclusive cross section, focusing on the term where two gluons are produced independently from different showers, and show that for fixed configurations of projectile color charge density and target fields, the production probability is maximal when the two gluon momenta are parallel, leading to angular correlations after ensemble averaging.

## Key numbers

- The ridge in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV is observed for transverse momenta in the interval 1 GeV < p_t < 3 GeV, which corresponds to the saturation scale Q_s ≈ 2 GeV typical for such events.
- High-multiplicity events in pp collisions occur at a rate of approximately 10⁻⁶ of the total event sample, with total charged hadron multiplicities 7–8 times larger than typical minimal bias collisions.
- The ridge in pp collisions emerges at multiplicities of order N ∼ 50 and becomes more pronounced at 7–8 times the average multiplicity.
- In pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV per nucleon, the ridge strength shows an approximately linear relation with total multiplicity and is significantly stronger than in pp collisions.
- The saturation momentum at LHC energies is Q_s² ≈ 5 GeV².
- The maximal correlation proper time in heavy-ion collisions is τ_latest correlation ≤ τ_freezout exp(−Δη/2).
- The double-inclusive production formula is valid for rapidity differences η − ξ < 1/α_s.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical work on long-range correlations initiated in Refs. (ddgjlv, correlations, LR) and discusses the relation to gluon saturation physics described by the BK-JIMWLK evolution equations. The authors contrast their initial-state mechanism with the collective flow explanation for heavy-ion collisions proposed by Shuryak and Voloshin, and note that the ridge in pp collisions cannot be explained by existing Monte Carlo generators. The work enables further study of Pomeron loop effects and dense-dense scattering, which the authors identify as crucial for quantitative understanding of correlations.
