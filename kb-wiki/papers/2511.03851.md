# Characterizing the initial state of hydrodynamics in pp and pA collisions

> The observation of seeming hydrodynamic-like behavior in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions presents us with the conceptual problem of how the initial state of such a hydrodynamic evolution should be characterized. This is an issue because, while nuclei can reasonably be approximated as ``large'' systems w.r.t. the characteristic Fermi momentum of their constituents, this is no longer true for nucleons. Hence, one would need to match a ``quantum'' theory, whose observables are described via highly non-commuting operators, to a ``classical'' hydrodynamics. Operationally assuming a ``fast'' thermalization, we survey what kind of object is best suited to such a matching condition. We show that it cannot be any of the objects usually associated with ``the 3D structure of the nucleon'' but rather a measure associated with entanglement entropy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the initial state for hydrodynamics in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions cannot be characterized by the usual objects associated with the 3D structure of the nucleon (such as generalized parton distributions or transverse momentum distributions), but rather requires a measure associated with entanglement entropy, specifically the Husimi-smoothed Wigner functional, under the assumption of fast thermalization.

## Method

The authors survey possible objects for matching a quantum description of the nucleon to classical hydrodynamics, examining light-cone wave functions, generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse momentum distributions (TMDs), and Wigner distributions. They show that GPDs and TMDs are projections of a pure quantum state and therefore incompatible with the maximally mixed state required for a hydrodynamic initial condition. They then propose using the Husimi transform of the Wigner functional, with a localization scale Λ, to produce a mixed state whose entropy density can be related to the parton multiplicity via the formalism of Kharzeev (2001) and can be matched to the Gaussian partition function of fluctuating hydrodynamics from Torrieri (2012).

## Key numbers

- The entropy density from the Wigner functional scales as ∼ Λ³, where Λ is the localization scale with units of momentum
- The correlator of the energy-momentum tensor in the Gaussian partition function scales as C ∼ Λ⁻⁸
- The smearing parameter ℓ in the Husimi distribution satisfies ℓ ≲ R_h (hadronic radius) in the nucleon case, and ℓ = 1/Q_s(x) in the small-x dominance regime, where Q_s(x) is the saturation scale
- The Q² dependence of the gluon structure function and the proposed entropy density is ∼ ln Q²

## Context

This work extends the conceptual framework of Kharzeev (2001) on entanglement entropy in high-energy collisions to the problem of hydrodynamic initial conditions in small systems, and builds on the fluctuating hydrodynamics formalism of Torrieri (2012) which uses a Gaussian ansatz for the partition function. It contrasts with approaches that use GPDs or TMDs as initial conditions, noting that attempts to construct hydrodynamic initial states from GPDs lead to mathematical contradictions (non-physical delta-function longitudinal profiles or violation of detailed balance). The paper enables future quantitative checks of the local equilibration hypothesis using 3D nucleon structure data from the Electron-Ion Collider and existing data from RHIC, and suggests that measurements of elliptic flow in polarized versus unpolarized proton-nucleus collisions could distinguish between the proposed entanglement-based initial state and alternatives.
