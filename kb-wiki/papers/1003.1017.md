# Phase transition from nuclear matter to color superconducting quark matter: the effect of the isospin

> We compute the mixed phase of nuclear matter and 2SC matter for different temperatures and proton fractions. After showing that the symmetry energy of the 2SC phase is, to a good approximation, three times larger than the one of the normal quark phase, we discuss and compare all the properties of the mixed phase with a 2SC component or a normal quark matter component. In particular, the local isospin densities of the nuclear and the quark component and the stiffness of the mixed phase are significantly different whether the 2SC phase or the normal quark phase are considered. If a strong diquark pairing is adopted for the 2SC phase, there is a possibility to eventually enter in the nuclear matter 2SC matter mixed phase in low energy heavy ions collisions experiments. Possible observables able to discern between the formation of the 2SC phase or the normal quark phase are finally discussed.

# Phase transition from nuclear matter to color superconducting quark matter: the effect of the isospin

## Key claim

The symmetry energy of the 2SC (color superconducting) phase is approximately three times larger than that of normal quark matter, which suppresses the neutron distillation effect and makes the critical density for the onset of the mixed phase nearly independent of the proton fraction, unlike the case with normal quark matter. Under the assumption of strong diquark pairing, the nuclear matter–2SC mixed phase could potentially be reached in low-energy heavy ion collision experiments.

## Method

The authors compute the mixed phase of nuclear matter (described by the relativistic mean field model with TM1 parameterization) and 2SC quark matter (described by an MIT bag model corrected with a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio-like diquark pairing contribution) by solving the Gibbs conditions for a two-component system with globally conserved baryon number and electric charge. The thermodynamic potential of the 2SC phase is obtained by minimizing with respect to the superconducting gap Δ and the color chemical potential μ₈, using two values of the diquark coupling (G_D = 3/4 G_S and G_D = G_S) corresponding to gaps of approximately 130 MeV and 200 MeV at zero temperature and μ = 500 MeV. The symmetry energy of the 2SC phase is derived analytically and verified numerically, and the phase diagrams are constructed for different proton fractions and temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy of the 2SC phase is approximately three times larger than that of the normal quark phase: a_sym^{2SC} ≈ 3 a_sym^{NQ}
- For the intermediate diquark coupling (G_D = 3/4 G_S), the gap Δ ≈ 130 MeV at μ = 500 MeV for symmetric matter at zero temperature
- For the strong diquark coupling (G_D = G_S), the gap Δ ≈ 200 MeV at μ = 500 MeV for symmetric matter at zero temperature
- The unpairing transition (breakdown of 2SC pairing) occurs at Z/A ≈ 0.35 for intermediate pairing at T = 0, and at Z/A ≈ 0.25 for strong pairing at T = 0
- At T = 50 MeV, the unpairing transition occurs at Z/A ≈ 0.42 for intermediate pairing and Z/A ≈ 0.3 for strong pairing
- The critical temperature for the 2SC to normal quark phase transition is T_crit = 0.57 Δ₀, where Δ₀ is the zero-temperature gap
- For symmetric matter with strong coupling, the first-order phase transition line and the second-order 2SC–normal quark line intersect at T ≈ 95 MeV and μ_B ≈ 1000 MeV
- The bag constant values used are B^{1/4} = 165 MeV and B^{1/4} = 190 MeV, chosen so that the onset of the mixed phase at T = 0 for symmetric matter occurs at densities above approximately 3 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the nuclear matter–quark matter phase transition at large isospin asymmetries (Muller 1997, DiToro 2006, Bonanno 2007, DiToro 2009) by including non-perturbative quark interactions responsible for color superconductivity in the 2SC phase, which those earlier studies neglected or treated only with perturbative QCD corrections. The results show that the neutron distillation effect proposed by DiToro (2006) is strongly suppressed when the quark component is in the 2SC state rather than the normal quark state, and that the mixed phase becomes more compressible. The phase diagrams presented enable comparison with transport model calculations (DiToro 2006) for semi-central collisions of ²³⁸U nuclei at 1 A GeV, suggesting that the NM-2SC mixed phase might be accessible in experiments at FAIR and NICA.
