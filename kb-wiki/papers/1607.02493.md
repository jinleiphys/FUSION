# The QCD equation of state at finite density from analytical continuation

> We determine the equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential, to order $(μ_B/T)^6$, for a system of 2+1 quark flavors. The simulations are performed at the physical mass for the light and strange quarks on several lattice spacings; the results are continuum extrapolated using lattices of up to $N_t=16$ temporal resolution. The QCD pressure and interaction measure are calculated along the isentropic trajectories in the $(T,~μ_B)$ plane corresponding to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan collision energies. Their behavior is determined through analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials of the baryonic density. We also determine the Taylor expansion coefficients around $μ_B=0$ from the simulations at imaginary chemical potentials. Strangeness neutrality and charge conservation are imposed, to match the experimental conditions.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to order \((\mu_B/T)^6\) at physical quark masses, using analytical continuation from imaginary chemical potentials. The method yields a more precise determination of the fourth and sixth order coefficients than direct simulation at \(\mu_B=0\), but the results carry a systematic error comparable to the statistical uncertainty, and the sixth-order coefficient is consistent with zero for temperatures above 200 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of 2+1+1 flavor QCD (with the charm contribution deliberately omitted from the final results) using the 4stout staggered discretization on lattices with temporal resolutions \(N_t = 10, 12, 16\). They simulated at six imaginary baryon chemical potentials \(\mu_B^{(j)} = i T \pi j/8\) with \(j = 3,4,5,6,6.5,7\) and at 16 temperature values between 135 and 220 MeV. The baryonic density \(n/(\mu_B T^2)\) was computed at each imaginary \(\mu_B\) and fitted with three functional forms (polynomial, Pade, and a sine-based form) to analytically continue to real \(\mu_B\); the Taylor coefficients \(c_2, c_4, c_6\) were extracted from the derivatives at \(\mu_B=0\). Continuum extrapolation was performed using a linear fit in \(1/N_t^2\), and strangeness neutrality (\(\langle n_S \rangle = 0\)) and charge conservation (\(\langle n_Q \rangle = 0.4 \langle n_B \rangle\)) were imposed to match experimental conditions.

## Key numbers
- The Taylor expansion coefficients \(c_0, c_2, c_4, c_6\) for the pressure are presented in the continuum limit as functions of temperature; \(c_6\) shows a dip slightly above the QCD transition temperature and is compatible with zero for \(T \geq 200\) MeV.
- The equation of state is determined up to \(\mu_B/T \simeq 2\), which covers RHIC Beam Energy Scan collision energies down to \(\sqrt{s} = 14.5\) GeV.
- Simulations used lattices with temporal resolutions \(N_t = 10, 12, 16\); the \(j=6.5\) data set consisted of \(N_t = 10\) and \(N_t = 12\) only.
- The analysis included 96 different fit combinations; the width of their distribution (with uniform weights) was used as the systematic error, and statistical and systematic errors were added in quadrature.

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD equation of state at finite density beyond the previously known order \((\mu_B/T)^2\) (Borsanyi 2012) and the non-continuum-extrapolated order \((\mu_B/T)^4\) (Hegde 2014). It provides the first continuum-extrapolated results for \(c_4\) and \(c_6\) at physical quark masses, enabling reliable thermodynamic calculations up to \(\mu_B/T \simeq 2\) for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The isentropic trajectories and thermodynamic observables along them are determined for the first time to order \((\mu_B/T)^6\), using freeze-out parameters from Alba (2014) and preliminary STAR data (Luo 2015).
