# Radial Modes of Neutron Stars with a Quark Core

> We make a first calculation of eigenfrequencies of radial pulsations of neutron stars with quark cores in a general relativistic formalism given by Chandrasekhar. The equations of state (EOS) used to estimate such eigenfrequencies have been derived by taking proper care of the hadron-quark phase transition. The hadronic EOS's have been obtained in the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field theories, whereas the quark EOS has been derived within the MIT bag model. We find that the periods of oscillations of neutron stars with a quark core show a kink, which is associated with the presence of a mixed phase region. Also, oscillation periods show significant differences between ordinary neutron stars and neutron stars with dynamical quark phases.

# Radial Modes of Neutron Stars with a Quark Core

## Key claim
The periods of radial oscillations in neutron stars with quark cores show a distinct kink (a local increase then decrease) as a function of gravitational mass, which is associated with the presence of a mixed hadron-quark phase region. This kink is significant only when a density-dependent bag constant is used in the MIT bag model for the quark equation of state, not when a constant bag parameter is employed.

## Method
The authors solved the general relativistic radial pulsation equations of Chandrasekhar (1964) for nonrotating neutron stars, using equations of state that include hadronic matter (from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean field theories), a mixed phase constructed via the Glendenning (1992) prescription for first-order phase transitions with two conserved charges, and quark matter from the MIT bag model with both density-dependent (Wood-Saxon parametrization) and constant bag parameters. The Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain stellar structure profiles, which were then used as input to the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem for the radial oscillation eigenfrequencies.

## Key numbers
- Oscillation periods for the fundamental mode range from 0.2 to 0.6 ms across all neutron star models considered.
- The kink in the period occurs in the gravitational mass range 0.4 < M/M_sun < 0.7 for density-dependent bag models (BHF+MW and RMF+MW), corresponding to central densities 0.2 < rho_c/(10^15 g cm^-3) < 1.7.
- For constant bag parameter models (BHF+M and RMF+M), the kink occurs in the mass range 0.5 < M/M_sun < 1.2 with central densities 0.6 < rho_c/(10^15 g cm^-3) < 1.5.
- The fundamental mode period at the maximum gravitational mass limit is approximately 0.22 ms for neutron stars with density-dependent bag parameters (BHF+MW and RMF+MW), compared to larger values for normal neutron stars without quark cores.
- Higher mode periods are less than or equal to 0.3 ms for all cases.
- All neutron stars with quark cores have maximum gravitational masses around 1.5 M_sun.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of neutron star oscillations by Cutler et al. (1990), who found periods of 3-5 ms for one-solar-mass stars, and Datta et al. (1992), who computed periods of 0.06-0.3 ms for strange quark stars. The key novelty is the use of the full Glendenning (1992) construction for the mixed phase, which avoids a sharp hadron-quark interface, unlike the earlier calculations of Haensel et al. (1989, 1990). The results suggest that the kink in oscillation periods could serve as an observational signature for the presence of quark matter in neutron star cores, motivating future asteroseismological studies of compact objects.
