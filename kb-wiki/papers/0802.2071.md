# Influence of Heavy Quark Recombination on the Nucleon Strangeness Asymmetry

> The nucleon strange and anti-strange distribution asymmetry is an important issue in the study of the nucleon structure. In this work, we show that the heavy quark recombination processes from a perturbative QCD picture can give a sizable influence on the measurement of the nucleon strangeness asymmetry from charged-current charm production processes, such as the CCFR and NuTeV dimuon measurements. When the influence of heavy quark recombination is considered, a positive effective $δS^-_{\mathrm{HR}}$ should be added to the initially extracted strangeness asymmetry $S^-\equiv\int dx[s(x)-\bar s(x)]x$, supporting the strangeness asymmetry $S^-$ being positive, which is helpful to explain the NuTeV anomaly within the framework of the standard model.

# Influence of Heavy Quark Recombination on the Nucleon Strangeness Asymmetry

## Key claim
The heavy quark recombination processes, a perturbatively calculable QCD effect not included in previous analyses, produce a sizable positive contribution to the extracted strangeness asymmetry S^- from charged-current charm production measurements; when this contribution is added, the realistic strangeness asymmetry becomes larger than the experimentally extracted value, supporting a positive S^- that can help explain the NuTeV anomaly within the standard model.

## Method
The authors calculate the heavy quark recombination subprocesses for neutrino and antineutrino induced charged-current charm production, where a c quark recombines with a light antiquark (or a cbar with a light quark) to form a D or D* meson that subsequently decays into a muon. They compute the differential cross section for these 2-to-3 processes using a perturbative QCD picture with color-singlet and color-octet contributions, employing the CTEQ6L parton distributions, and integrate over the allowed phase space to obtain the effective correction δS^-_HR to the strangeness asymmetry extracted from dimuon data.

## Key numbers
- The integrated effective contribution from heavy quark recombination is δS^-_HR ≈ 0.0023 for the nonperturbative parameter ρ_sm = 0.15, at E_ν = 160 GeV and Q² = 20 GeV² (approximate averaged NuTeV conditions).
- With the correction, the central value of the realistic strangeness asymmetry becomes S^-_real ≈ 0.0043, compared to the NLO NuTeV analysis result of S^- = 0.00196 ± 0.00046(stat) ± 0.00045(syst) ± 0.00128(external) from Mason et al. (2006).
- For ρ_sm = 0.3, δS^-_HR ≈ 0.0046, which alone is sufficient to explain the NuTeV anomaly; for ρ_sm = 0.06, δS^-_HR ≈ 0.0009.
- The result varies by a factor of about 2 when the factorization scale is changed by a factor of 2 (nearly trebles at μ = μ₀/2, reduces by half at μ = 2μ₀), and varies within 5% for m_c = 1.5 ± 0.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the heavy quark recombination mechanism developed by Braaten et al. (2002) for charm photoproduction asymmetry and leading particle effects, applying it to the extraction of nucleon strangeness asymmetry from CCFR and NuTeV dimuon measurements. Previous analyses by the NuTeV collaboration gave conflicting results: a LO analysis (NuTeV, 2002) reported a negative S^- = -0.0027 ± 0.0013, while a later NLO analysis by Mason et al. (2006) found a positive value centered at 0.00196. The difference of 0.0047 between these two values is of the same order as the δS^-_HR estimated here, suggesting that higher-order effects including heavy quark recombination may resolve the discrepancy. The work enables a reanalysis of dimuon data with the recombination processes included, potentially providing a standard-model explanation of the NuTeV anomaly without requiring new physics.
