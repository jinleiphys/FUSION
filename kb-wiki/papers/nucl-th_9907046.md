# Analyses of collective flow and space-time evolution based on relativistic hydrodynamical model

> We numerically solve fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equation with the baryon number conservation law. For realistic initial conditions we adopt the results from the event generator (URASiMA). Using this model we discuss collective flow.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model with initial conditions from the URASiMA event generator can reproduce collective flow phenomena at AGS energies, but the model produces a fluid size (about 10 fm at t=15 fm/c) that is larger than the experimental result of 6 fm derived from HBT analysis, indicating the freeze-out conditions may need refinement.

## Method

The authors solved the fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equation with baryon number conservation using Lagrangian hydrodynamics, with initial conditions generated by the URASiMA event generator (based on the Multi chain model). Energy density and baryon density from URASiMA were transformed into temperature and chemical potential using an ideal hadron gas equation of state containing resonances up to 2 GeV, with smoothing applied such that the difference between original and smoothed results was less than 1%. Particle emission from the hadronic fluid was calculated using the Cooper-Frye formula, with freeze-out occurring when temperature and chemical potential crossed a boundary designed to give a freeze-out temperature of 140 MeV at vanishing chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at 10.8 AGeV with impact parameter 6 fm: maximum initial energy density 2.06 GeV/fm^3, maximum initial baryon density 0.94 fm^{-3}, maximum initial local velocity 0.28, and hydrodynamical start time t0 = 5.0 fm/c
- For Au+Au collisions at 5.0 AGeV with impact parameter 6 fm: maximum initial energy density 1.29 GeV/fm^3, maximum initial baryon density 0.79 fm^{-3}, maximum initial local velocity 0.19, and t0 = 8.0 fm/c
- For Au+Au collisions at 20.0 AGeV with impact parameter 6 fm: maximum initial energy density 2.68 GeV/fm^3, maximum initial baryon density 0.97 fm^{-3}, maximum initial local velocity 0.39, and t0 = 4.0 fm/c
- On the freeze-out hypersurface at 10.8 AGeV: maximum energy density 0.14 GeV/fm^3, maximum baryon density 0.091 fm^{-3}, maximum local velocity 0.46, and maximum lifetime 21.3 fm/c
- On the freeze-out hypersurface at 5.0 AGeV: maximum energy density 0.10 GeV/fm^3, maximum baryon density 0.083 fm^{-3}, maximum local velocity 0.40, and maximum lifetime 24.0 fm/c
- On the freeze-out hypersurface at 20.0 AGeV: maximum energy density 0.16 GeV/fm^3, maximum baryon density 0.083 fm^{-3}, maximum local velocity 0.48, and maximum lifetime 21.5 fm/c
- The freeze-out temperature was set to 140 MeV at vanishing chemical potential
- The estimated fluid size at t=15 fm/c was about 10 fm, compared to the experimental result of 6 fm from HBT analysis by E877

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow phenomena (directed flow, elliptic flow, and radial flow) observed at AGS and SPS experiments by providing a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical calculation with realistic initial conditions, going beyond simpler models. The authors note that anisotropic flow phenomena are expected to show strong dependence on the nuclear equation of state as discussed by Danielewicz, and they plan to input different equations of state in future work to evaluate anisotropic flow in detail. The results enable comparison with experimental data from E877, E895, and WA98, and the authors indicate that detailed analysis of elliptic flow will be presented in a subsequent paper by Nonaka.
