# Strange / anti-strange asymmetry in the nucleon sea

> We analyze the non-perturbative structure of the strange sea of the nucleon within a meson cloud picture. In a low $Q^2$ approach in which the nucleon is viewed as a three valon bound state, we evaluate the probability distribution of an in-nucleon Kaon-Hyperon pair in terms of splitting functions and recombination. The resulting kaon and hyperon probability densities are convoluted with suitable strange distributions inside the meson and baryon in order to obtain non-perturbative contributions to the strange sea of the nucleon. We find a structured strange/anti-strange asymmetry, displaying a clear excess of quarks (anti-quarks) for large (small) momentum fractions

## Key claim

The paper claims a structured strange/anti-strange asymmetry in the nucleon sea, with an excess of strange quarks at large momentum fractions and an excess of anti-strange quarks at small momentum fractions, but notes that the exact shapes of the distributions depend on the chosen forms for strange quark densities inside the in-nucleon kaon and hyperon, which are not experimentally determined.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon as a three-valon bound state at a low momentum scale (Q = 0.8 GeV). They compute the probability of a perturbative s-sbar pair production from a valon via gluon emission and splitting using Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions. The resulting quarks and antiquarks are then rearranged into a kaon-hyperon bound state using a recombination model, yielding meson and baryon probability densities inside the nucleon. These densities are convoluted with assumed strange valence quark distributions inside the kaon and hyperon to obtain non-perturbative contributions to the strange sea.

## Key numbers

* The valon distribution in the nucleon is given by v(x) = (105/16) * sqrt(x) * (1 - x)^2.
* The strong coupling constant is evaluated at Q = 0.8 GeV, for which alpha_st^2 is approximately 0.3.
* The overall normalization of the strange hadronic fluctuation is fixed by a phenomenological estimate of 4-10% probability.
* The strange anti-quark distribution inside the kaon is approximated as x * sbar_M(x) = 6 * x^2 * (1 - x).
* The strange quark distribution inside the hyperon is approximated as x * s_B(x) = 12 * x^2 * (1 - x)^2.
* The asymmetry becomes negligible beyond x ~ 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the Meson Cloud Model (MCM) approach to strange sea asymmetry, previously attempted by Signal (with ad-hoc form factors) and by Brodsky and Ma (using two-body wave functions). It differs from the approach of Malheiro, which predicted a different asymmetry shape. The model's predictions are compatible with the large error bars of the CCFR Collaboration's neutrino scattering data and are similar in shape and magnitude to the results of Brodsky and Ma. The authors note that the analysis can be extended to study the ubar-dbar asymmetry measured by the E866 Collaboration.
