# Trace of $Λ(1405)$ resonance in low energy $K^{-}+\, ^{3}\mathrm{He}\rightarrow (π^{0}Σ^{0})+d$ reaction

> In present work, we investigated $K^{-}+\, ^{3}\mathrm{He}$ reaction at low energies. The coupled-channel Faddeev AGS equations were solved for $\bar{K}Nd-πΣ{d}$ three-body system in momentum representation to extract the scattering amplitudes. To trace the signature of the $Λ$(1405) resonance in the $πΣ$ invariant mass, the deuteron energy spectrum for $K^{-}+\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\rightarrowπΣ{d}$ reaction was obtained. Different types of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potentials based on phenomenological and chiral SU(3) approaches were used. As a remarkable result of this investigation, it was found that the deuteron energy spectrum, reflecting the $Λ$(1405) mass distribution and width, depends quite sensitively on the $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ model of interaction. Hence accurate measurements of the $πΣ$ mass distribution have the potential to discriminate between possible mechanisms at work in the formation of the $Λ$(1405).

## Key claim

The deuteron energy spectrum for the $K^{-}+\,^{3}\mathrm{He}\rightarrow\pi\Sigma d$ reaction, reflecting the $\Lambda(1405)$ mass distribution and width, depends quite sensitively on the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ model of interaction, but the signal of the resonance is clearly visible only at higher incident antikaon momenta (around $p_{\bar{K}}^{cm}=250$ MeV/c) while at lower momenta (50-150 MeV/c) the spectrum is strongly affected by threshold effects.

## Method

The coupled-channel Faddeev AGS equations were solved for the $\bar{K}Nd-\pi\Sigma d$ three-body system in momentum representation using separable two-body interactions. The $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ target was treated as a $p-d$ cluster structure, reducing the four-body system to a three-body problem. Five different $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ potentials were used: phenomenological SIDD$^{1}$ (one-pole) and SIDD$^{2}$ (two-pole), the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) potential, the Rev-A chiral-based energy-independent potential, and the Chiral-IKS energy-dependent chiral potential.

## Key numbers

* The SIDD$^{1}$ potential has a single pole at $1428.1-i46.6$ MeV for the $\bar{K}N$ system.
* The SIDD$^{2}$ potential has two poles: $1418.1-i56.9$ MeV and $1382.0-i104.2$ MeV.
* The Chiral-IKS potential has two poles: $1420.6-i20.3$ MeV and $1343.0-i72.5$ MeV.
* The AY potential has a single pole at $1407.6-i20.0$ MeV.
* The Rev-A potential has a single pole at $1422.0-i20.0$ MeV.
* The $\Sigma d$ scattering length extracted from Faddeev equations is $a_{\Sigma d}=-1.59+i0.71$ fm$^{-1}$.
* The $\pi\Sigma$ mass spectra were calculated for incident antikaon momenta $p_{\bar{K}}^{cm}=50$, 100, 150, and 250 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of $K^{-}d$ reactions using three-body Faddeev methods (Esmaili 2013) and the E15 experiment at J-PARC (Sakuma 2015) which measured $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass from $K^{-}+\,^{3}\mathrm{He}$ reactions. The results enable future experiments to discriminate between phenomenological and chiral SU(3) models of the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction by measuring the $\pi\Sigma$ mass distribution at optimized kaon momenta, where the $\Lambda(1405)$ signal overcomes kinetic effects while maintaining a reasonable deuteron survival probability.
