# Non-parametric Bayesian approach to extrapolation problems in configuration interaction methods

> We propose a non-parametric extrapolation method based on constrained Gaussian processes for configuration interaction methods. Our method has many advantages: (i) applicability to small data sets such as results of {\it ab initio} methods, (ii) flexibility to incorporate constraints, which are guided by physics, into the extrapolation model, (iii) providing predictions with quantified extrapolation uncertainty, etc. In the present study, we show an application to the extrapolation needed in full configuration interaction method as an example.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a non-parametric extrapolation method based on constrained Gaussian processes (cGP) for configuration interaction calculations, claiming it can provide extrapolated results with quantified uncertainty even from sparse data sets, but the method's reliability depends on the specific interaction and convergence pattern, as demonstrated by the failure of all models (including cGP) to cover the tentative exact value for the N3LO interaction case.

## Method

The author applies Gaussian process regression with a logMat52 kernel to extrapolate ground-state energies of ⁶Li from full configuration interaction (FCI) calculations at finite Nmax values to Nmax = ∞. Two physics-informed constraints are imposed: monotonicity (variational property that energy decreases with increasing Nmax) and a convergence-pattern constraint limiting the ratio of successive energy gains. The hyperparameters are sampled via particle filtering (sequential Monte Carlo) with Metropolis-Hastings updates, and two mean function choices are tested: zero mean (cGP-a) and an exponential fit to the largest three Nmax data points (cGP-b).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy data points for ⁶Li: (Nmax=6, -28.602 MeV) through (Nmax=14, -31.977 MeV) for N3LO interaction with ℏΩ=16.0 MeV
- Nmax values giving converged predictions (mean converged within 0.2 keV): 44 for NNLO_opt, 42 for JISP16, and 34 for N3LO
- 20,000 particles with 2,000 Metropolis-Hastings updates per particle give converged results within a few keV
- The strictness parameter κ for the probit function is set to approximately 10⁶, enforcing constraints with accuracy less than 0.01 keV
- The convergence-pattern constraint uses σ_r = 0.1 throughout the work

## Context

This work extends prior parametric extrapolation methods for FCI calculations, such as the exponential fit (Maris 2009) and polynomial fits, which the author argues risk overfitting due to limited model expressiveness. The method contrasts with artificial neural network approaches (Iowa, Oak Ridge groups) that require large data sets, instead offering applicability to sparse ab initio data. The constrained GP approach enables systematic uncertainty quantification that could complement eigenvector continuation methods (introduced in Ref. EC) for propagating input uncertainties from chiral effective field theory potentials to observables.
