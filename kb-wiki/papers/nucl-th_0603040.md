# The reaction pi N to pi pi N in a meson-exchange approach

> A resonance model for two-pion production in the pion-nucleon reaction is developed that includes information obtained in the analysis of pion-nucleon scattering in a meson-exchange model. The baryonic resonances Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), N*(1535), and N*(1650) are included. The model reproduces the total cross sections up to kinetic energies of the incident pion of 350 MeV and obtains the shapes of the differential cross sections in reasonable agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a resonance model for the reaction πN → ππN that incorporates information from a meson-exchange analysis of pion-nucleon scattering, and reproduces total cross sections up to pion kinetic energies of 350 MeV, but the model starts to break down in the second resonance region due to an approximate treatment of the Roper resonance.

## Method

The authors construct a resonance model for two-pion production that includes the baryonic resonances Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1520), N*(1535), and N*(1650), along with σ and ρ meson exchanges. They use a K-matrix approximation to solve the coupled-channel problem for meson-nucleon scattering, and evaluate tree-level diagrams with dressed vertices and dressed propagators taken from a pion-nucleon scattering model. The model is restricted to diagrams with two baryon propagators (diagrams a, b, c) and one baryon propagator (diagrams d1-d5), without iterating the non-pole subdiagrams.

## Key numbers

- The model reproduces total cross sections up to kinetic energies of the incident pion of 350 MeV
- The Δ-isobar corresponds to Tπ = 0.19 GeV, and the maximal pion kinetic energy Tπ = 0.4 GeV translates into √s = 1.38 GeV
- The nucleon mass is mN = 0.93893 GeV, the Δ mass is mΔ = 1.232 GeV, and the Roper mass is mP11(1440) = 1.491 GeV
- The πNN coupling constant squared is f²πNN/(4π) = 0.0778, and the πNΔ coupling constant squared is f²πNΔ/(4π) = 0.36
- The ρππ coupling constant squared is g²ρππ/(4π) = 2.905, and the σππ coupling constants are g²1/(4π) = 98.94 GeV⁻² and g²2/(4π) = 7.32 GeV⁻²
- The σ meson mass is Mσ = 0.8346 GeV, and the ρ meson mass is Mρ = 0.772 GeV

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange approach to pion-nucleon scattering developed by Krehl et al. (1999) by applying it to two-pion production, and builds on the observation from Fettes et al. (1999) that loop diagram contributions are negligible in chiral perturbation theory for this reaction. The model enables a consistent treatment of resonances and background in two-pion production, which is needed for analyses of baryon resonances from experimental data, and suggests that going beyond the Born approximation of the non-pole pion-nucleon T-matrices is required for analyses in the second resonance region.
