# 3PF2 pairing in high-density neutron matter

> The onset of the 3PF2 superfluid phase in high-density neutron matter is studied within the BCS framework with two and three body forces. Owing to the strong correlations the energy gap is so sizeably quenched as to demand to reconsider the role of superfluidity in neutron-star phenomena.

# 3PF2 pairing in high-density neutron matter

## Key claim
The inclusion of the Z-factor (quasiparticle strength) effect, which measures the deviation of the correlated Fermi system from an ideal degenerate Fermi gas, quenches the 3PF2 pairing gap in high-density neutron matter to less than 0.05 MeV, one order of magnitude smaller than the value obtained when the Z-factor is neglected. This result makes doubtful the role of 3PF2 superfluidity in neutron star cores, even when three-body forces are included.

## Method
The authors calculated the Z-factor and occupation numbers within the Brueckner theory using the Bonn B two-body force and a consistent microscopic meson-exchange three-body force with no free parameters. The energy gap was then computed from the generalized BCS gap equation for the coupled 3PF2 channel, incorporating both the Z-factor and the single-particle energy from the Brueckner calculation, with the pairing interaction including both two-body and three-body forces.

## Key numbers
- The Z-factor decreases with density; with three-body forces included, the decrease becomes much more rapid than with pure two-body forces alone.
- In the pure degenerate limit (neglecting Z-factor), the 3PF2 gap peak value is about 0.2 MeV without three-body forces and 0.5 MeV with three-body forces.
- Including the Z-factor drops the peak gap value to less than 0.05 MeV, and the superfluidity domain shrinks to a density range of 0.1 to 0.4 fm^{-3}.
- The calculation was performed at a density of 0.3 fm^{-3} for the occupation probability display.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of 3PF2 pairing in neutron matter by Zuo et al. (previous work) and the investigation of Z-factor effects on 1S0 pairing by Lombardo (2001). The authors show that the Z-factor quenching is much stronger for 3PF2 pairing than for 1S0 pairing, because the deviation from the degenerate Fermi gas limit becomes much larger at the higher densities where 3PF2 pairing occurs. The result calls into question the interpretation of recent observations of cooling in the Cassiopeia A neutron star as direct evidence of 3PF2 pairing in the neutron star core.
