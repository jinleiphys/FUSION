# Spiral structure and logarithmic evolution of deuteron form factors: evidence for a transitional regime in QCD

> We present a consistent analysis of elastic electron--deuteron scattering combining perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) scaling, helicity amplitudes, and phenomenological parameterizations of deuteron form factors. Particular attention is paid to logarithmic corrections governed by anomalous dimensions of six-quark operators and to two-photon exchange (TPE). Three classes of parameterizations corresponding to different dynamical regimes are considered: pre-asymptotic valence-quark dominance, effective higher-twist contributions, and modified logarithmic evolution. A global fit to the world data for the structure functions $A(Q^2)$ and $B(Q^2)$, differential cross sections, and tensor polarization observables is performed using a combined strategy of global and local minimization. The best description of the complete data set is achieved within the $f_1$ parameterization, which incorporates pre-asymptotic logarithmic behavior and correlated valence-quark dynamics. Among the considered models, $f_1$ gives the smallest value of $χ^2/\mathrm{dof}$. The obtained results indicate that the presently accessible momentum-transfer region corresponds to a transitional regime between hadronic and quark--gluon descriptions. Helicity-conserving amplitudes dominate, whereas the asymptotic pQCD regime has not yet been fully realized. This may indicate nontrivial multiquark correlations related to hidden-color configurations in the short-distance deuteron structure. The tensor polarization observable $t_{21}$ is especially sensitive to the asymptotic behavior of the helicity amplitudes. Future measurements at larger $Q^2$ may provide a decisive test for distinguishing between the considered dynamical scenarios.

# Spiral structure and logarithmic evolution of deuteron form factors: evidence for a transitional regime in QCD

## Key claim

The presently accessible momentum-transfer region (1.5 to 5 GeV²) corresponds to a transitional regime between hadronic and quark-gluon descriptions, where helicity-conserving amplitudes dominate but the asymptotic pQCD regime has not yet been fully realized; the best description of the complete data set is achieved by the f₁ parameterization incorporating pre-asymptotic logarithmic behavior and correlated valence-quark dynamics, which gives the smallest χ²/dof among the considered models.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit to world experimental data for the structure functions A(Q²) and B(Q²), differential cross sections, and tensor polarization observables (t₂₀, t₂₁, t₂₂) using a combined strategy of global optimization via differential evolution and local optimization via the Powell method. Three classes of phenomenological parameterizations for the deuteron helicity amplitudes were compared: f₁ (pre-asymptotic valence-quark dominance with effective logarithmic powers), f₂ (propagator-like structure incorporating higher-twist and sea-quark contributions), and f₃ (modified logarithmic evolution closer to asymptotic pQCD). The helicity amplitudes were expressed in a reduced representation factorizing nucleon structure via the dipole form factor G_D²(Q²), with logarithmic evolution governed by effective exponents Γ_{λ'λ} derived from anomalous dimensions of six-quark operators.

## Key numbers

- The global fit yields χ²/dof ≈ 1.15 for model f₁, 1.53 for f₂, and 1.39 for f₃.
- The analysis covers the momentum-transfer region 1.5 GeV² ≲ Q² ≲ 5 GeV².
- The estimated anomalous-dimension shift between helicity channels is Δγ ≈ 0.18, giving γ₊₀ − γ₀₀ ≈ 0.18 and γ₊₋ − γ₀₀ ≈ 0.36.
- The leading logarithmic exponent for the helicity-conserving amplitude is Γ₀₀ ≈ 0.9 (from Γ₀₀ ≈ 1 − ε with ε small).
- The effective logarithmic exponent for the reduced deuteron amplitude is ϕ(Q²) ∼ L^{−0.896}, where L = ln(Q²/Λ²_QCD).

## Context

This work extends the systematic development of the transitional regime interpretation by Braun, Derkachov, Korchemsky, and Manashov (2000s), who showed that the spectrum of anomalous dimensions for six-quark operators is related to integrable spin chains, and by earlier phenomenological studies (Refs. 7, 8, 14) demonstrating that modern experimental data cannot be described by pure asymptotic QCD. The analysis incorporates two-photon exchange corrections following the approach of Refs. 11, 12, which showed that TPE modifies the helicity structure beyond simple normalization corrections. The results enable future high-precision measurements of the tensor polarization observable t₂₁ at larger Q² to provide a decisive test distinguishing between the competing dynamical scenarios, particularly the pre-asymptotic valence-quark correlations versus modified logarithmic evolution toward asymptotic QCD.
