# Isospin and F-spin structure in low-lying levels of 48,50Cr isotopes

> The low-energy level structure and electromagnetic transitions of $^{48,50}$Cr nuclei have been studied by using interacting boson model with isospin (IBM-3). A sequence of isospin excitation bands with isospin $(T= T_{z}$, $T_{z}+1$ and $ T_{z}+2) $ has been assigned, and compared with available data. According to this study, the $2^{+}_{3} $ and $2^{+}_{2} $ states are the lowest mixed symmetry states in $^{48,50}$Cr respectively. In particular, the present calculations suggest that a combination of isospin and F-spin excitation can explain the structure in these nuclei. The transition probabilities between the levels are analyzed in terms of isoscalar and isovector decomposition which reveal the detailed nature of the energy levels. The results obtained are found in good agreement with recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a combination of isospin and F-spin excitation within the interacting boson model with isospin (IBM-3) can explain the low-lying level structure of 48,50Cr, but the authors hedge that the identification of mixed symmetry states depends on the specific U(6) partition available for each isotope: in 48Cr (Tz=0) the lowest mixed symmetry state comes from the [N-2,2] partition because [N-1,1] does not contain T=0, while in 50Cr it comes from [N-1,1].

## Method

The authors performed IBM-3 calculations for 48,50Cr assuming 56Ni as a closed core with bosons representing pairs of hole fermions. They fitted the Hamiltonian parameters, expressed as linear combinations of Casimir operators, to reproduce experimental energy levels and then analyzed the isospin and F-spin content of the wave functions. Electromagnetic transition probabilities (E2 and M1) were calculated using isoscalar and isovector decomposition of the transition operators and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- In 48Cr, the calculated 0+ state with T=2 (isospin analogue) lies at 8.760 MeV, compared to an estimated 8.350 MeV from binding energy and Coulomb energy considerations.
- In 50Cr, the first 1+ state is calculated at 3.613 MeV, close to the experimental 1+ state at 3.629 MeV.
- In 48Cr, the lowest mixed symmetry 2+ state (2+3) is at 5.069 MeV; in 50Cr, the lowest mixed symmetry 2+ state (2+2) is at approximately 3 MeV.
- In 50Cr, the calculated B(M1; 2+2 → 2+1) = 0.6319 μN2 compares with the experimental value of 0.3938 μN2.
- In 50Cr, the calculated B(M1; 1+ state at 8.901 MeV → 0+1) = 0.100 μN2 agrees with the experimental value of 0.0823(89) μN2.
- In 48Cr, the scissor mode state at 8.599 MeV (predominantly [2,1,1] partition with T=1) is predicted to decay to 2+3 with B(M1)=0.24 μN2 and B(E2)=0.0013 e2b2.

## Context

This work extends the IBM-3 framework, previously developed by Elliott (1985) and others, to the low-lying levels of 48,50Cr, which had been studied experimentally with gamma-ray detector arrays and theoretically with full fp-shell shell model calculations by Caurier (1994) and Martinez-Pinedo (1996). The paper identifies mixed symmetry states analogous to those found in 54,56Cr and 56,58Fe by Hitt (2000) and in 64-68Ge and 60-66Zn by Elliott (1998). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted row-rowed partition states (typical of IBM-3 and absent in IBM-1 and IBM-2) at around 9 MeV, and for the predicted scissor mode state in 48Cr.
