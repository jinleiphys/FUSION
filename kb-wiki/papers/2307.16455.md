# Hybrid star structure from perturbative QCD

> The stellar configurations of quark stars are studied using perturbative QCD (pQCD) for the equation of state (EoS). The neutron star structures with equation of state obtained from Brussels-Montreal extended Skyrme interaction are also explored. The deconfinement phase transition from quark to hadron phase in stellar interior for matter under extreme pressure is accomplished by employing the Maxwell construction. The influence of hybrid EoS on the jump in energy density has been investigated. To study hybrid stars the BSk24 hadronic model and pQCD EoS for the quark phase have been used. The properties of hybrid stars in the view of the very recent astrophysical observations have been examined. We find that the gravitational mass might exceed 2.3 $M_\odot$ in some cases, comparable with the observed mass of the pulsar PSR J0952-0607 recently detected.

# Hybrid star structure from perturbative QCD

## Key claim

The gravitational mass of hybrid stars (containing both hadronic and quark matter cores) might exceed 2.3 solar masses in some cases, comparable with the observed mass of the pulsar PSR J0952-0607, but only for specific choices of the renormalization scale parameter X in the perturbative QCD equation of state; the maximum hybrid star masses are generally smaller than the pure neutron star maximum of 2.28 solar masses.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherically symmetric stars using three types of equations of state: perturbative QCD (pQCD) for pure quark stars, the Brussels-Montreal extended Skyrme interaction BSk24 for hadronic matter, and a hybrid constructed by matching the pQCD and BSk24 equations of state via the Maxwell construction, which imposes equal pressure and equal baryonic chemical potential at the quark-hadron phase boundary. The pQCD equation of state is parameterized by a dimensionless renormalization scale parameter X (ranging from 1 to 4), and the hadronic crust is described by the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller, Baym-Pethick-Sutherland, and Baym-Bethe-Pethick equations of state at sub-nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass for BSk24 hadronic equation of state: 2.28 solar masses, with a corresponding radius of 11.05 km.
- Maximum hybrid star masses for BSk24 with quark cores: 2.24 solar masses (X = 1.8) with radius 11.66 km; 1.84 solar masses (X = 2.75) with radius 11.48 km.
- Pure quark star maximum masses for different X values: 0.41 solar masses (X = 1.0, radius 2.21 km); 0.98 solar masses (X = 1.8, radius 5.53 km); 1.81 solar masses (X = 2.75, radius 10.40 km); 3.05 solar masses (X = 4.0, radius 17.78 km).
- Maximum neutron star masses for other BSk interactions: 2.27 solar masses (BSk22) and 2.18 solar masses (BSk26).
- The matching of pQCD equation of state at X = 2.75 with BSk24 produces a phase transition with vanishing latent heat (no jump in energy density).

## Context

This work extends the study of compact star structure using the BSk24 hadronic model from Goriely et al. (2013) combined with the pQCD equation of state from Kurkela et al. (2010) and Fraga et al. (2014). It applies the Maxwell construction for the deconfinement phase transition, following the approach of Maruyama et al. (2007) and subsequent studies. The results are compared with recent mass measurements of PSR J0952-0607 (Romani et al., 2022), PSR J0740+6620 (Fonseca et al., 2021; Riley et al., 2021), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013), and the gravitational wave event GW170817 (Rezzolla et al., 2018). The work enables future studies of hybrid star structure with different choices of the renormalization scale parameter and can be extended to include effects of rotation or additional observational constraints.
