# How Wide is the Transition to Deconfinement?

> Pure SU(3) glue theories exhibit a deconfining phase transition at a nonzero temperature, Tc. Using lattice measurements of the pressure, we develop a simple matrix model to describe the transition region, when T > Tc. This model, which involves three parameters, is used to compute the behavior of the 't Hooft loop. There is a Higgs phase in this region, where off diagonal color modes are heavy, and diagonal modes are light. Lattice measurements of the latter suggests that the transition region is narrow, extending only to about 1.2 Tc. This is in stark contrast to lattice measurements of the renormalized Polyakov loop, which indicates a much wider width. The possible implications for the differences in heavy ion collisions between RHIC and the LHC are discussed.

## Key claim

The transition region above the deconfinement temperature in pure SU(3) glue theory is narrow, extending only to about 1.2 Tc, based on lattice measurements of the pressure and the behavior of diagonal gluon modes; this contradicts lattice measurements of the renormalized Polyakov loop, which suggest a much wider transition region extending to about 4.0 Tc.

## Method

The authors develop a three-parameter matrix model for the effective potential of the eigenvalues qa of the thermal Wilson line, combining a perturbative one-loop potential with a non-perturbative potential containing terms proportional to T^2 Tc^2. They fit the three parameters (c1, c2, c3) to lattice measurements of the pressure for SU(2) and SU(3) pure gauge theories, then use the model to compute the 't Hooft loop, gluon masses, and the Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

- For SU(3), the fitted parameters are: c1 = -0.41488, c2 = -5.45957, c3 = 0.21954
- For SU(2), the fitted parameters are: c1 = -0.30267, c2 = -5.97440, c3 = 0.18341
- The transition region extends only to about 1.2 Tc, in contrast to the Polyakov loop which becomes constant only above about 4.0 Tc
- For three colors, the order-disorder interface tension at Tc is sigma_dis = 0.0258012 * Tc^2 / sqrt(g^2)
- For two colors, the 't Hooft loop formula includes a factor (1 - (Tc/T)^2)^(3/2) / (1 - 0.908 (Tc/T)^2)
- For three colors, the Polyakov loop jumps to about 0.4 at Tc+ based on lattice simulations

## Context

This work extends the matrix model approach first proposed by Meisinger, Miller, and Ogilvie (2002) for describing the deconfining phase transition, and builds on lattice measurements of the pressure by Panero (2009) and others. The model predicts a Higgs phase in the semi-QGP region where off-diagonal color modes become heavy while diagonal modes remain light, which may help resolve contradictory lattice results for gluon masses. The narrow transition width has implications for understanding differences between heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, particularly regarding whether nearly ideal hydrodynamic behavior persists at higher energies.
