# Two-flavor hybrid stars with the Dyson-Schwinger quark model

> We study the properties of two-flavor quark matter in the Dyson-Schwinger model and investigate the possible consequences for hybrid neutron stars, with particular regard to the two-solar-mass limit. We find that with some extreme values of the model parameters, the mass fraction of two-flavor quark matter in heavy neutron stars can be as high as 30 percent and the possible energy release during the conversion from nucleonic neutron stars to hybrid stars can reach $10^{52}\;$erg.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two-flavor (up and down) quark matter can exist in the cores of massive hybrid neutron stars, with mass fractions up to 30% and energy release up to 10^52 erg during conversion from nucleonic stars, but only for extreme values of the Dyson-Schwinger model parameters (the damping parameter α and the bag constant B_DS); the existence of such stars is conditional on the parameter choice satisfying the 2 solar mass maximum mass constraint.

## Method

The authors combine a hadronic equation of state (EOS) from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body approach using the Argonne V_18 potential with compatible three-body forces, with a quark matter EOS derived from the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) for two-flavor quark matter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations under the Gibbs construction for phase equilibrium between hadronic and quark phases, scanning the model parameters α (screening of the effective interaction) and B_DS (bag constant) to determine which combinations yield hybrid stars with maximum mass above 2 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of nucleonic neutron stars with the BHF V18 EOS: about 2.3 solar masses
- For 2QM hybrid stars with B_DS = 90 MeV fm^-3, α must be smaller than 7.2 to satisfy M_max ≥ 2 solar masses; for B_DS = 60 MeV fm^-3, α must be smaller than 4.3
- For 3QM hybrid stars with B_DS = 90 MeV fm^-3, α must be smaller than 1.65 to satisfy M_max ≥ 2 solar masses; for B_DS = 60 MeV fm^-3, α must be smaller than 1.45
- The mass fraction of two-flavor quark matter in heavy neutron stars can be as high as 30% (with B_DS = 60 MeV fm^-3)
- In a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the mass fraction of 2QM can be as large as 18% (with B_DS = 60 MeV fm^-3)
- The maximum mass difference between nucleonic and hybrid stars with the same baryonic mass is about 7 promille of the solar mass
- The possible energy release during conversion from nucleonic neutron stars to hybrid stars can reach 10^52 erg
- The Maxwell phase transition to 2QM in the MIT limit (α = ∞) is located at ρ_B = 0.62 fm^-3 in hadronic matter, compared to ρ_B = 0.10 fm^-3 for 3QM

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of three-flavor hybrid stars (Chen 2011, Chen 2015a, Chen 2016) by investigating the less-bound two-flavor quark matter as a possible transitory phase in neutron star evolution. It contrasts with the MIT bag model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which are recognized as limiting cases of the Dyson-Schwinger model (Klahn 2013, Klahn 2015). The results enable future work on establishing Dyson-Schwinger model parameters from QCD more fundamentally and clarifying differences between current theoretical quark models.
