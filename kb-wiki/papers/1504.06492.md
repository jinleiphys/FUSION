# Skyrme Random-Phase-Approximation description of lowest $K^π=2^+_γ$ states in axially deformed nuclei

> The lowest quadrupole $γ$-vibrational $K^π=2^+$ states in axially deformed rare-earth (Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Yb, Hf, W) and actinide (U) nuclei are systematically investigated within the separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) based on the Skyrme functional. The energies $E_γ$ and reduced transition probabilities $B(E2)$ of $2^+_γ$-states are calculated with the Skyrme forces SV-bas and SkM$^*$. The energies of two-quasiparticle configurations forming the SRPA basis are corrected by using the pairing blocking effect. This results in a systematic downshift of $E_γ$ by 0.3-0.5 MeV and thus in a better agreement with the experiment, especially in Sm, Gd, Dy, Hf, and W regions. For other isotopic chains, a noticeable overestimation of $E_γ$ and too weak collectivity of $2^+_γ$-states still persist. It is shown that domains of nuclei with a low and high $2^+_γ$ -collectivity are related with the structure of the lowest 2-quasiparticle states and conservation of the Nilsson selection rules. The description of $2^+_γ$ states with SV-bas and SkM$^*$ is similar in light rare-earth nuclei but deviates in heavier nuclei. However SV-bas much better reproduces the quadrupole deformation and energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance. The accuracy of SRPA is justified by comparison with exact RPA. The calculations suggest that a further development of the self-consistent calculation schemes is needed for a systematic satisfactory description of the $2^+_γ$ states.

## Key claim

The separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) with Skyrme forces, when augmented by a pairing-blocking-effect correction to two-quasiparticle energies, systematically lowers the calculated energies of $K^\pi=2^+_\gamma$ states by 0.3-0.5 MeV and improves agreement with experiment in several isotopic chains, but a noticeable overestimation of energies and too weak collectivity persist in other chains, indicating that further development of self-consistent schemes is needed.

## Method

The authors performed systematic SRPA calculations for 41 axially deformed rare-earth (Nd, Sm, Gd, Dy, Er, Yb, Hf, W) and actinide (U) nuclei using the Skyrme forces SV-bas and SkM$^*$. The residual interaction was derived self-consistently from the Skyrme functional, and the pairing blocking effect was implemented ad hoc by correcting the energies of the five lowest $K^\pi=2^+$ two-quasiparticle states via BCS-PBE equations while keeping ground-state occupation amplitudes and wave functions unchanged. The SRPA accuracy was validated by comparison with exact 2D QRPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- The pairing blocking effect downshifts $E_\gamma$ by 0.3-0.5 MeV systematically across the studied nuclei.
- For SV-bas, the calculated ISGQR energy centroids are 12.2 MeV in $^{152}$Nd, 12.0 MeV in $^{164}$Dy, 11.8 MeV in $^{172}$Yb, and 10.7 MeV in $^{238}$U; for SkM$^*$ the corresponding values are 13.0, 12.5, 12.3, and 11.1 MeV.
- The configuration space for $\lambda\mu=22$ involves 6600-9600 proton and 9400-14200 neutron 2qp-states with excitation energies up to 55-80 MeV, exhausting the EWSR(E2,T=0) by approximately 95-98%.
- For $^{164}$Dy with SkM$^*$ and PBE, the B(E2) value reaches 0.7 $e^2b^2$, far exceeding the experimental value and indicating extreme collectivity.
- The PBE downshift of SRPA energies ranges from 0.1 to 0.6 MeV depending on the isotope.

## Context

This work extends the systematic Skyrme QRPA study of $\gamma$-vibrational states by Terasaki (2011), which covered 27 rare-earth nuclei with SkM$^*$ and SLy4, to 41 nuclei including actinides and introduces the pairing blocking effect following the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model approach of Soloviev (1976). The use of SV-bas, with its larger isoscalar effective mass ($m^*_0/m=0.9$), provides a consistent description of both ground-state deformations and the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance, which SkM$^*$ overestimates. The results enable future development of fully self-consistent PBE-QRPA schemes and highlight the need to incorporate coupling to complex configurations for a satisfactory description of low-lying vibrational states.
