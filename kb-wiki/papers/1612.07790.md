# Exploring correlations in the CGC wave function: odd azimuthal anisotropy

> We extend the CGC approach to calculation of the double inclusive gluon production by including high-density effect in the CGC wave function of the projectile (proton). Our main result is that these effects lead to the appearance of odd harmonics in the two-particle correlation C(k,p). We find that in the high momentum limit, |k|,|p|>>Q_s, this results in a positive c_1{2}. Additionally when the magnitudes of the two momenta are approximately equal, |k|/|p| ~ 1, the density effects also generate a positive third harmonic c_3{2}, which translates into a non-vanishing v_3 when the momenta of the trigger and associated particle are in the same momentum bin. The sign of c_3{2} becomes negative when |k|/|p|>1.1 suggesting an interesting experimental signature.

## Key claim

The authors show that including high-density corrections to the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) wave function of the projectile proton, specifically a squeezed (Bogoliubov) state rather than the usual coherent state, breaks the accidental symmetry that forbids odd azimuthal harmonics in two-gluon correlations, generating nonzero \(c_1\{2\}\) and \(c_3\{2\}\) without any final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors construct an improved CGC wave function that includes a Gaussian squeezing operator \(\mathcal{B}\) in addition to the standard coherent displacement operator \(\mathcal{C}\), based on earlier work by Kovner, Lublinsky, and Altinoluk (2007, 2009). They compute the gluon pair density in this wave function and then calculate the double inclusive gluon production in eikonal scattering off a target, expanding the result to leading order in \(\mu^2/k^2\) (high transverse momentum limit) and averaging over projectile color sources using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model.

## Key numbers

* The odd harmonics \(c_1\{2\}\) and \(c_3\{2\}\) are suppressed relative to even harmonics (e.g., \(c_2\{2\}\)) by a parametric factor of \(\alpha_s\).
* The sign of \(c_3\{2\}\) becomes negative when \(|k|/|p| > 1.1\) or \(|p|/|k| > 1.1\), and positive when \(1.1 > |k|/|p| > 1/1.1\).
* In the high momentum limit \(|k|,|p| \gg Q_s\), the first harmonic \(c_1\{2\}\) is positive.
* The third harmonic \(c_3\{2\}\) is positive when the two momenta are approximately equal (\(|k|/|p| \approx 1\)), but negative for \(|k|/|p| > 1.1\).

## Context

This work extends the CGC approach to correlations beyond the dilute projectile limit used in earlier calculations by Dumitru et al. (2010), Levin and Rezaeian (2010), Kovner and Lublinsky (2010, 2012), Kovchegov and Wertepny (2012), and Dusling and Venugopalan (2012, 2013), which all predicted the symmetry \(\sigma(k,p)=\sigma(k,-p)\) and thus no odd harmonics. The authors were inspired by an earlier idea of Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2004) that a dense wave function can naturally incorporate nontrivial correlations. The result contrasts with the recent work of McLerran and Skokov (2016), which required final-state Yang-Mills evolution to generate odd harmonics; here they arise from the projectile wave function alone. The calculation enables experimental tests of whether the observed \(v_3\) in p-p and p-Pb collisions can originate from initial-state correlations rather than hydrodynamic final-state interactions.
