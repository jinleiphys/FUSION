# QCD description of backward vector meson hard electroproduction

> We consider backward vector meson exclusive electroproduction off nucleons in the framework of collinear QCD factorization. Nucleon to vector meson transition distribution amplitudes arise as building blocks for the corresponding factorized amplitudes. In the near-backward kinematics, the suggested factorization mechanism results in the dominance of the transverse cross section of vector meson production ($σ_T \gg σ_L$) and in the characteristic $1/Q^8$-scaling behavior of the cross section. We evaluate nucleon to vector meson TDAs in the cross-channel nucleon exchange model and present estimates of the differential cross section for backward $ρ^0$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson production off protons. The resulting cross sections are shown to be measurable in the forthcoming JLab@12 GeV experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that backward vector meson hard electroproduction can be described by collinear QCD factorization using nucleon-to-vector-meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), but the authors explicitly note that the corresponding factorization theorems are not yet rigorously proven, unlike the forward case with generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Method

The authors parametrize the leading twist-3 nucleon-to-vector-meson TDAs for rho, omega, and phi mesons, involving 24 independent Dirac structures. They compute the hard scattering amplitude at leading order in alpha_s and 1/Q from 21 Feynman diagrams, using the cross-channel nucleon exchange model to evaluate the TDAs. The differential cross sections are then estimated for backward production of rho^0, omega, and phi mesons off protons in the near-backward kinematics regime where the u-channel momentum transfer squared is small.

## Key numbers

* The predicted scaling behavior of the cross section is 1/Q^8 for the backward kinematics regime.
* The transverse cross section dominates over the longitudinal cross section: sigma_T >> sigma_L.
* The nucleon light-cone wave function normalization constant used is f_N = 5.2 x 10^{-3} GeV^2, taken from Chernyak (1984).
* The electromagnetic fine structure constant is taken as alpha_em = 1/137.
* The resulting cross sections are estimated to be measurable in the forthcoming JLab@12 GeV experiments.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on pion-nucleon TDAs (Lansberg 2007, Lansberg 2011, Pire 2013) to the vector meson sector, specifically rho^0, omega, and phi mesons. The phi meson case addresses the strangeness content of the nucleon, which has been studied by Shanahan (2014) and others, while combined analysis of rho and omega production allows disentangling the isotopic structure of VN TDAs in the non-strange sector. This enables future experimental access to backward meson electroproduction at JLab@12 GeV, complementing the well-established forward kinematics regime described by GPDs.
