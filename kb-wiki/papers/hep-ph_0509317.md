# Paramagnetic Meissner Effect and Finite Spin Susceptibility in an Asymmetric Superconductor

> A general analysis of Meissner effect and spin susceptibility of a uniform superconductor in an asymmetric two-component fermion system is presented in nonrelativistic field theory approach. We found that, the pairing mechanism dominates the magnetization property of superconductivity, and the asymmetry enhances the paramagnetism of the system. At the turning point from BCS to breached pairing superconductivity, the Meissner mass squared and spin susceptibility are divergent at zero temperature. In the breached pairing state induced by chemical potential difference and mass difference between the two kinds of fermions, the system goes from paramagnetism to diamagnetism, when the mass ratio of the two species increases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in an asymmetric two-component fermion superconductor with mismatched Fermi surfaces, the pairing mechanism dominates the magnetization properties, and asymmetry enhances paramagnetism; at the turning point from BCS to breached pairing superconductivity, the Meissner mass squared and spin susceptibility diverge at zero temperature, and the system transitions from paramagnetism to diamagnetism as the mass ratio of the two species increases.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic field theory approach with a four-fermion point interaction to model an asymmetric two-component fermion system with different masses and chemical potentials. They derive general formulas for the Meissner mass squared and spin susceptibility in the mean-field approximation, then analyze the paramagnetic and diamagnetic contributions separately for cases of chemical potential difference alone and mass difference alone.

## Key numbers

- At the turning point from gapped to gapless excitation where the two roots of the gapless dispersion coincide (p1 = p2), the Meissner mass squared becomes negative infinity at zero temperature.
- For the case of only chemical potential difference, the total Meissner mass squared is given by M² = (ne²/m)(1 - η δμ θ(δμ - Δ)/√(δμ² - Δ²)), where η = (p1³ + p2³)/(6π²n).
- The spin susceptibility χ is zero at T = 0 for symmetric BCS superconductors and equals χ_P = 3μ₀²n/(2ε_F) at T = T_c.
- At low temperature, the effective superfluid density n_s/n approaches 1 - √(2πΔ₀/T) e^{-Δ₀/T} as T → 0, and n_s/n = 2(1 - T/T_c) as T → T_c.

## Context

This work extends the study of breached pairing (BP) states first proposed by Liu and Wilczek (2004) and addresses the Sarma instability problem discussed by Forbes (2005) and Shovkovy (2005). The paper generalizes earlier findings of paramagnetic Meissner effect in neutral color superconductors by Huang (2004) to nonrelativistic asymmetric fermion systems, and provides a unified framework applicable to electronic superconductors, cold fermionic atom gases, nuclear matter, and dense quark matter. The results enable future investigation of magnetization properties in systems with mismatched Fermi surfaces, including the LOFF state and two-flavor color superconductors.
