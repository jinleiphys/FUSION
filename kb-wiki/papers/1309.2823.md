# Anomalous-hydrodynamic analysis of charge-dependent elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> Anomalous hydrodynamics is a low-energy effective theory that captures effects of quantum anomalies. We develop a numerical code of anomalous hydrodynamics and apply it to dynamics of heavy-ion collisions, where anomalous transports are expected to occur. This is the first attempt to perform fully non-linear numerical simulations of anomalous hydrodynamics. We discuss implications of the simulations for possible experimental observations of anomalous transport effects. From analyses of the charge-dependent elliptic flow parameters ($v_2^\pm$) as a function of the net charge asymmetry $A_\pm$, we find that the linear dependence of $Δv_2^\pm \equiv v_2^- - v_2^+$ on the net charge asymmetry $A_\pm$ cannot be regarded as a robust signal of anomalous transports, contrary to previous studies. We, however, find that the intercept $Δv_2^\pm(A_\pm=0)$ is sensitive to anomalous transport effects.

# Anomalous-hydrodynamic analysis of charge-dependent elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The linear dependence of the difference in elliptic flow between negatively and positively charged particles on the net charge asymmetry cannot be regarded as a robust signal of anomalous transport effects, contrary to previous studies; however, the intercept of that linear relation at zero net charge asymmetry is sensitive to anomalous transport effects in a robust way.

## Method
The authors developed a fully non-linear numerical code for anomalous hydrodynamics in (3+1) dimensions, incorporating the chiral magnetic effect and chiral separation effect through constitutive equations with magnetic-field-induced currents. They applied this code to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma created in off-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energy, using an ideal gas equation of state with two-flavor quarks and gluons, initial conditions from a modified BGK model, and parametrized external electromagnetic fields. The charge-dependent elliptic flow parameters were computed from the final particle distributions using the Cooper-Frye freezeout formula on an isothermal hypersurface at 160 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strength at initial time: \( eB_y(\mathbf{0}, 0, \tau_{\mathrm{in}}) \sim (3m_\pi)^2 \), with \( eB_0 = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \)
- Electric field strength at initial time: \( eE_y(\mathbf{0}, 0, \tau_{\mathrm{in}}) \sim m_\pi^2 \), with \( eE_0 = 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \)
- Initial hydrodynamic time: \( \tau_{\mathrm{in}} = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm} \)
- Impact parameter for 20-30% centrality: \( b = 7.2\ \mathrm{fm} \)
- Gold nucleus radius: \( R = 6.38\ \mathrm{fm} \)
- Freezeout temperature: \( T_{\mathrm{fo}} = 160\ \mathrm{MeV} \)
- Duration time parameters for magnetic and electric fields: \( (\tau_B, \tau_E) = (1.0\ \mathrm{fm}, 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}) \) and \( (3.0\ \mathrm{fm}, 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}) \)
- Initial vector chemical potential parameter \( c \) varied between 0 and 0.03

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of anomalous hydrodynamics developed by Son and Surowka (2009) and the chiral magnetic wave concept introduced by Kharzeev, Yee, and collaborators (2010). It provides the first fully non-linear numerical simulation of anomalous hydrodynamics applied to heavy-ion collisions, going beyond the linearized calculations in uniform static temperature used by Burnier, Liao, and Shuryak (2011, 2012) and the event-by-event hydrodynamic model of Bzdak, Bozek, and Skokov (2013). The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and open the way for systematic studies of anomalous transport effects including conductive currents, back reaction of matter to electromagnetic fields, and QCD sphaleron effects.
