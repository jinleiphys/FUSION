# Neutrino Trapping in a Supernova and Ion Screening

> Neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering is reduced in dense matter because of correlations between ions. The static structure factor for a plasma of electrons and ions is calculated from Monte Carlo simulations and parameterized with a least squares fit. Our results imply a large increase in the neutrino mean free path. This strongly limits the trapping of neutrinos in a supernova by coherent neutral current interactions.

## Key claim

Ion screening via correlations between nuclei in dense supernova matter can dramatically increase the neutrino mean free path, potentially preventing neutrino trapping for energies below about 7.5 MeV, but the effect is much weaker for higher-energy neutrinos and depends on the specific composition and density regime.

## Method

The static structure factor S_q for a plasma of electrons and ions was calculated from Monte Carlo simulations of approximately 1000 ions in a box with periodic boundary conditions, using screened Coulomb potentials. The angle-averaged structure factor <S> was parameterized via a least squares fit as a function of reduced neutrino energy and the Coulomb coupling parameter Gamma, valid for 1 < Gamma < 150. The resulting <S> was then used to compute the neutrino transport mean free path in a supernova core, including both ion and electron screening corrections.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path at 10 MeV and density 5 x 10^12 g/cm^3: greater than 10 km with screening, compared to approximately 0.4 km without screening
- At 5 MeV neutrino energy and density 10^13 g/cm^3: mean free path greater than 45 km, exceeding the size of the dense system (approximately 30 km)
- For neutrino energies less than or equal to about 7.5 MeV: mean free path larger than the system size, potentially preventing neutrino-sphere formation
- Electron screening causes mean free path for electron neutrinos to be about 15 percent larger than for muon neutrinos
- Monte Carlo statistical accuracy for S_q: 2-3 x 10^-3
- Simulations performed for twelve values of Gamma between 0.87 and 151.8, corresponding to 56Fe densities from 2 x 10^7 to 9 x 10^13 g/cm^3 at 1 MeV temperature
- Fit error typically less than 0.01, with maximum error up to 0.05 at very large Gamma

## Context

This work extends earlier ion screening results from Horowitz and Wehrberger (1991) by calculating screening for a broad range of densities and providing a parameterization suitable for incorporation into neutrino transport codes. The results contradict the assumption used in almost all supernova simulations at the time (which set <S>=R_e=1), showing that screening dramatically increases the neutrino mean free path and changes its density dependence. The work enables improved supernova models that include screening effects, and suggests that the neutrino opacity is small before the shock wave (due to screening) and large afterwards, potentially affecting shock dynamics and neutrino luminosity in a manner analogous to photon opacity changes during cosmological recombination.
