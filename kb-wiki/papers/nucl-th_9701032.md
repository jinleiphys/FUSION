# Transport coefficients for shape degrees in terms of Cassini ovaloids

> Previous computations of the potential landscape with the shapes parameterized in terms of Cassini ovaloids are extended to collective dynamics at finite excitations. Taking fission as the most demanding example of large scale collective motion, transport coefficients are evaluated along a fission path. We concentrate on those for average motion, namely stiffness C, friction γand inertia M. Their expressions are formulated within a locally harmonic approximation and the help of linear response theory. Different approximations are examined and comparisons are made both with previous studies, which involved different descriptions of single particle dynamics, as well as with macroscopic models. Special attention is paid to an appropriate definition of the deformation of the nuclear density and its relation to that of the single particle potential. For temperatures above 3 MeV the inertia agrees with that of irrotational flow to less than a factor of two, but shows larger deviations below, in particular in its dependence on the shape. Also friction exhibits large fluctuations along the fission path for small excitations. They get smoothed out above 3 - 4 MeV where γattains values in the range of the wall formula. For T > (or=) 2 MeV the inverse relaxation time β= γ/M turns out to be rather insensitive to the shape and increases with T.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous computations of the potential landscape using Cassini ovaloid shape parameterization to collective dynamics at finite excitations, but the transport coefficients are only valid within a locally harmonic approximation and linear response theory, with the condition that collective motion is sufficiently slow and the nucleonic degrees of freedom follow thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors computed transport coefficients (stiffness C, friction γ, and inertia M) along a one-dimensional fission path for ²²⁴Th, using a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential parameterized by Cassini ovaloids. They applied linear response theory within a locally harmonic approximation, fitting the collective response function to an oscillator model via a variational procedure to extract the transport coefficients, and compared results from the zero-frequency limit of the collective response with those from a full fit.

## Key numbers

- For temperatures above 3 MeV, the inertia agrees with that of irrotational flow to less than a factor of two
- Friction fluctuations get smoothed out above 3–4 MeV, where γ attains values in the range of the wall formula
- For T ≥ 2 MeV, the inverse relaxation time β = γ/M is rather insensitive to shape and increases with T
- The cut-off in single-particle energy was set equal to 20 MeV; variation within 5–20 MeV did not change transport coefficients much
- The upper integration limit ω_max for the variational fit was fixed at ħω_max = 5 MeV
- The liquid drop energy temperature dependence used α = 0.000763 MeV⁻² and β = 0.00553 MeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends previous computations of potential landscapes using Cassini ovaloids (Pashkevich, 1971) to include collective dynamics at finite temperature, and is similar in aim to earlier work by Yamaji, Ivanyuk, and Hofmann using a two-center shell model. The Cassini ovaloid parameterization allows treatment of more realistic shapes than expansions in spherical harmonics (Bohr and Mottelson), and the results enable future studies of fission dynamics at finite excitations where comparisons can be made with macroscopic models like the liquid drop model and the wall formula for dissipation.
