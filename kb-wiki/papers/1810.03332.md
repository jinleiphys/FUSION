# Hadronic Molecular States Composed of Spin-$3\over 2$ Singly Charmed Baryons

> We investigate the possible deuteron-like molecules composed of a pair of charmed spin-$\frac{3}{2}$ baryons, or one charmed baryon and one charmed antibaryon within the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model. For the spin singlet and triplet systems, we consider the couple channel effect between systems with different orbital angular momentum. Most of the systems have binding solutions. The couple channel effect plays a significant role in the formation of some loosely bound states. The possible molecular states of $Ω_c^*Ω_c^*$ and $Ω_c^*\barΩ_c^*$ might be stable once produced.

## Key claim

The paper claims that most systems of two spin-3/2 singly charmed baryons (or one baryon and one antibaryon) have binding solutions within the one-boson-exchange model, but the actual binding depends strongly on the cutoff parameter (0.8–1.6 GeV) and the specific quantum numbers; the authors hedge that the numerical results are "simply numerical results under this framework" with "actual uncertainty stemming from the theoretical framework may be quite large."

## Method

The authors constructed effective interaction potentials for two spin-3/2 singly charmed baryons using the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, including scalar (sigma), pseudoscalar (pi, eta), and vector (rho, omega, phi) meson exchanges with coupling constants derived from nucleon-nucleon-meson vertices via the quark model. They solved the Schrodinger equation numerically for S-wave single-channel systems and then performed coupled-channel calculations including S-D wave mixing for J=0 systems and S-D-G wave mixing for J=1 systems, using a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Lambda between 0.8 and 1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of Sigma_c* Sigma_c* [I=1, J^P=1^+] system: 18.8–55.6 MeV (single channel, cutoff 0.8–0.9 GeV)
- Binding energy of Sigma_c* Sigma_c* [I=1, J^P=1^+] system with coupled channels: 23.3–36.8 MeV (cutoff 0.8–0.84 GeV)
- Binding energy of Xi_c* Xi_c* [I=0, J^P=1^+] system: 10.3–85.9 MeV (single channel, cutoff 0.85–0.95 GeV)
- Binding energy of Omega_c* Omega_c* [I=0, J^P=0^+] system: 2.4–6.0 MeV (single channel, cutoff 1.1–1.3 GeV)
- Binding energy of Omega_c* Omega_c* [I=0, J^P=2^+] system: 5.2–11.8 MeV (single channel, cutoff 1.0–1.4 GeV)
- Root-mean-square radius of Sigma_c* Sigma_c* [I=1, J^P=1^+] system: 0.9–1.1 fm (coupled channel, cutoff 0.8–0.84 GeV)
- Root-mean-square radius of Omega_c* Omega_c* [I=0, J^P=0^+] system: 1.5–2.5 fm (coupled channel, cutoff 1.0–1.4 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the OBE model analysis of hadronic molecules from spin-1/2 heavy baryon systems (Li et al., 2012) to spin-3/2 singly charmed baryons, and follows the same formalism used for deuteron-like states composed of two heavy mesons or two heavy baryons. The results suggest that Omega_c* Omega_c* and Omega_c* anti-Omega_c* molecules might be stable once produced, which could guide experimental searches at facilities like LHCb, Belle, and BESIII. The authors note that the baryon-antibaryon molecular states may decay into three mesons through quark rearrangement, making them candidates for molecule-type resonances rather than stable bound states.
