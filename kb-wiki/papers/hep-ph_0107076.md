# Electroweak Radiative Corrections to Parity-Violating Electroexcitation of the $Δ$

> We analyze the degree to which parity-violating (PV) electroexcitation of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance may be used to extract the weak neutral axial vector transition form factors. We find that the axial vector electroweak radiative corrections are large and theoretically uncertain, thereby modifying the nominal interpretation of the PV asymmetry in terms of the weak neutral form factors. We also show that, in contrast to the situation for elastic electron scattering, the axial $N\toΔ$ PV asymmetry does not vanish at the photon point as a consequence of a new term entering the radiative corrections. We argue that an experimental determination of these radiative corrections would be of interest for hadron structure theory, possibly shedding light on the violation of Hara's theorem in weak, radiative hyperon decays.

## Key claim

Parity-violating electroexcitation of the Δ(1232) resonance can in principle be used to extract weak neutral axial vector transition form factors, but the axial vector electroweak radiative corrections are large and theoretically uncertain, thereby modifying the nominal interpretation of the PV asymmetry. In contrast to elastic electron scattering, the axial N→Δ PV asymmetry does not vanish at the photon point due to a new term entering the radiative corrections, and an experimental determination of these corrections could shed light on the violation of Hara's theorem in weak radiative hyperon decays.

## Method

The authors computed the electroweak radiative corrections to the parity-violating (PV) axial N→Δ transition amplitude, distinguishing between one-quark corrections (calculable in the Standard Model) and many-quark corrections (organized using chiral perturbation theory through O(p³)). They analyzed the contributions from the transition anapole moment, a new PV γNΔ electric dipole coupling d_Δ (the Siegert term), and a PV NΔπ d-wave vertex, evaluating their impact on the PV asymmetry A_LR for electroexcitation of the Δ resonance.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level weak mixing angle is sin²θ_W⁰ = 0.21215 ± 0.00002
- The planned Jefferson Lab measurement aims for a ≤25% determination of the axial vector transition amplitude for |q²| in the range 0.1–0.6 (GeV/c)²
- The PV asymmetry at the photon point is approximately A_LR(q²=0) ≈ -2d_Δ/(C₃^V) × (M_N/Λ_χ), where Λ_χ = 4πF_π ≈ 1 GeV and C₃^V ≈ 2
- The N→Δ asymmetry at the photon point could be ~10⁻⁶ or larger if the d_Δ effect is significantly enhanced over its natural scale
- The ratio of Siegert to anapole contributions scales as A_LR^(Siegert)/A_LR^(anapole) ~ (d_Δ/a_Δ)(Λ_χδ/q²) ~ (d_Δ/a_Δ)(1/N_c)(Λ_χ²/q²), with δ = M_Δ - M_N
- For |q²| ≲ Λ_χ²/3 ≈ 0.3 (GeV/c)², the Siegert contribution is expected to be comparable to or larger than the anapole contribution

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of non-resonant backgrounds to PV electroexcitation of the Δ (Mukhopadhyay et al.) and parallels their earlier studies of axial vector radiative corrections to PV elastic electron-proton scattering (Zhu et al., Ramsey-Musolf et al.), where corrections were found to be unexpectedly large and theoretically uncertain. The SAMPLE collaboration's results suggested that the elastic correction R_A^p may be substantially larger than the best theoretical estimate. The paper also connects to the puzzle of Hara's theorem violation in weak radiative hyperon decays (Σ⁺ → pγ and Ξ⁻ → Σ⁻γ), where the measured asymmetry parameter is five times larger than the expected SU(3)-breaking scale, and to the proposal by resonance models that 1/2⁻ resonances could enhance the electric dipole amplitude. The analysis enables future separation of the d_Δ contribution from other axial vector terms through kinematic dependence, and comparison with prospective neutrino excitation measurements would be particularly interesting since inelastic neutrino scattering is insensitive to the large γ-exchange effects at O(αG_F).
