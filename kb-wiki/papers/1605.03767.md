# The chiral S=-1 meson-baryon interaction with new constrains on the NLO contributions

> We present a study of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon interaction, employing a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and implementing unitarization in coupled channels. The parameters of the model have been fitted to a large set of experimental scattering data in different two-body channels, to threshold branching ratios, and to the precise SIDDHARTA value of the energy shift and width of kaonic hidrogen. In contrast to other groups, we have taken into consideration the $K^- p\to K^+Ξ^-, K^0Ξ^0$ reaction data, since we found in a previous work to be especially sensitive to the NLO parameters of the chiral Lagrangian. In the present work we also include the Born terms, which usually have very little effect, and find them to be non-negligible in the $K^- p\to KΞ$ channels, correspondingly causing significant modifications to the NLO parameters. We furthermore show that the importance of the Born terms becomes more visible in the isospin projected amplitudes of the $K^-p \to KΞ$ reactions. The measurement of processes that filter single isospin components, like the $K^0_L p \to K^+ Ξ^0$ reaction that could be measured at the proposed secondary $K^0_L$ beam at Jlab, would put valuable constraints on the chiral models describing the meson-baryon interaction in the $S=-1$ sector.

## Key claim

The authors show that Born terms, usually considered negligible in chiral unitary calculations of the S=-1 meson-baryon interaction, produce non-negligible effects specifically in the K⁻p → KΞ channels and cause significant modifications to the next-to-leading order (NLO) low-energy constants, provided that the KΞ production data are included in the fit.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral SU(3) Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and implement unitarization in coupled channels via the Bethe-Salpeter equation solved on-shell. They fit the model parameters to 166 experimental data points including K⁻p elastic and inelastic cross sections, threshold branching ratios, and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width, and crucially include K⁻p → K⁺Ξ⁻, K⁰Ξ⁰ reaction data. Three fits are performed: one with only Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) and Born terms excluding KΞ data, one adding NLO terms but still excluding KΞ data, and one full fit including both NLO terms and KΞ data with Born terms.

## Key numbers

* The SIDDHARTA experimental values for kaonic hydrogen are ΔE₁s = 283 ± 36 eV and Γ₁s = 541 ± 92 eV; the full WT+NLO+Born fit yields ΔE₁s = 291 eV and Γ₁s = 558 eV.
* The K⁻p scattering length from the full WT+NLO+Born fit is a = (−0.67 + i 0.84) fm, compared to the experimental value (−0.66 + i 0.81) fm.
* The threshold branching ratios from the full fit are γ = 2.36, R_n = 0.191, and R_c = 0.664, matching the experimental values (2.36, 0.189, 0.664) within uncertainty.
* The χ² per degree of freedom for the full WT+NLO+Born fit is 0.73, compared to 0.41 for the fit excluding KΞ data and 0.56 for the LO fit.
* The pion decay constant ratio f/f_π is 1.21 ± 0.03 in the full fit, with f_π = 92.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to the S=-1 meson-baryon sector by including Born terms, which previous studies such as those by Borasoy et al. (2005) and Mizutani et al. (2012) found to have little effect on most channels. The authors build on their own prior work (Feijoo et al., 2015) which demonstrated that K⁻p → KΞ reactions are especially sensitive to NLO parameters. The inclusion of Born terms significantly modifies the NLO low-energy constants when KΞ data are fitted, and the authors argue that isospin-filtered reactions such as K⁰L p → K⁺Ξ⁰, measurable at a proposed secondary K⁰L beam at JLab, would provide valuable additional constraints on chiral models in this sector.
