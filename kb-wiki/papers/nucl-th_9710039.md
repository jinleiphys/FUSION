# Gauging hadronic systems

> We present a general method for incorporating the electromagnetic interaction into descriptions of hadronic processes based on four-dimensional scattering integral equations. The method involves the idea of gauging the scattering equations themselves, and results in electromagnetic amplitudes where an external photon is effectively coupled to every part of every strong interaction diagram in the model. Current conservation is therefore implemented in the theoretically correct fashion. To illustrate our gauging procedure we apply it to the three-nucleon problem whose strong interactions are described by standard three-body integral equations. In this way we obtain the expressions needed to calculate all possible electromagnetic processes of the three-nucleon system: the electromagnetic form factors of the three-body bound state, pd->pd gamma, gamma 3He-> pd, gamma 3He-> ppn, etc. As the photon is coupled everywhere in the strong interaction model, a unified description of the NNN-gamma NNN system is obtained. An interesting aspect of our results is the natural appearance of a subtraction term needed to avoid the overcounting of diagrams.

# Gauging hadronic systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a general method for incorporating electromagnetic interactions into hadronic processes described by four-dimensional scattering integral equations, claiming that current conservation is guaranteed when the photon is coupled to every part of every strong interaction diagram. The method is illustrated on the three-nucleon system, but the authors note that the gauging procedure itself is independent of the type of external field and can also be applied to weak probes where current is not conserved.

## Method
The authors gauge the scattering equations themselves by applying a product rule for attaching photons to Green functions, potentials, and bound-state wave functions. For the two-body case, they derive the electromagnetic vertex function from the gauged Green function equation; for the three-body case, they gauge the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations and the three-body Green function, obtaining expressions that include a subtraction term to avoid overcounting of diagrams. The method is then applied to the three-nucleon system with antisymmetry taken into account, using two-body t-matrices as input.

## Key numbers
- The three-particle electromagnetic vertex function is given as $\Gamma^\mu = \sum_{i=1}^3 (\ggama_i^\mu D_{0i}^{-1} + v_i^\mu d_i^{-1} - v_i \ggama_i^\mu)$, where the negative sign on $v_i \ggama_i^\mu$ prevents overcounting.
- The gauged AGS amplitude for identical particles is $\tZ^\mu = \tZ d_3^{-1}d_3^\mu + \tZ( D_{03}^{-1}D_{03}^\mu D_{03}^{-1}d_3^{-1} + d_3^{-1}t_3^\mu{\cal P}) \tZ$, which serves as the central result for the gauged three-nucleon problem.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Gross and Riska (who used Feynman diagram analysis for the two-body case) to the three-body problem, and corrects an overcounting error present in the pioneering four-dimensional calculations of the three-nucleon system by Rupp and Tjon. The method enables unified calculations of all electromagnetic processes of the three-nucleon system, including form factors, radiative capture, and photodisintegration, and has been applied by the authors to gauge the $NN-\pi NN$ system and to generate three-dimensional descriptions via spectator equations.
