# Polarization Rotation of Chiral Fermions in Vortical Fluid

> The rotation of polarization occurs for light interacting with chiral materials. It requires the light states with opposite chiralities interact differently with the materials. We demonstrate analogous rotation of polarization also exists for chiral fermions interacting with quantum electrodynamics plasma with vorticity using chiral kinetic theory. We find that the rotation of polarization is perpendicular both to vorticity and fermion momentum. The effect also exists for chiral fermions in quantum chromodynamics plasma with vorticity. It could lead to generation of a vector current when the probe fermions contain momentum anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral fermions in a vortical QED or QCD plasma experience a polarization rotation effect analogous to optical activity, where the new polarization component is perpendicular to both vorticity and fermion momentum. This effect arises from the first-order vortical correction to the collision term in the Kadanoff-Baym equation, specifically from the vortical correction to the medium fermions (not the probe fermion), and requires the probe fermions to have momentum anisotropy to generate a net vector current.

## Method

The authors used chiral kinetic theory based on the Kadanoff-Baym equation for fermion propagators in a weakly coupled QED plasma at high temperature, treating vorticity as a first-order gradient. They solved the kinetic equation for probe fermions in a vortical fluid by expanding to first order in vorticity, computing the collisional self-energy from soft photon exchange (Coulomb scattering) with the leading infrared divergence, and extracting the spin-dependent contribution from the vortical correction to the photon self-energy. The calculation was then generalized to QCD plasma by including color factors and the gluon loop contribution to the gluon self-energy.

## Key numbers

- The damping rate from the zeroth-order collisional term is \(\Gamma_0 = \frac{e^2 T}{2\pi} \ln\frac{m_D}{q_{\text{IR}}}\) for QED, where \(m_D\) is the Debye mass and \(q_{\text{IR}}\) is an infrared cutoff.
- The spin-dependent damping coefficient from the vortical correction is \(\Gamma_1 = -\frac{e^2}{8p} \varepsilon(p_0)(2f(p_0)-1) \frac{1}{2\pi} \ln\frac{m_D}{q_{\text{IR}}}\) for QED at zero chemical potential.
- For QCD, the corresponding coefficient is \(\Gamma_1 = -g^2 C_F \frac{\varepsilon(p_0)(2f(p_0)-1)}{8p} \frac{1}{4\pi} \frac{N_c \mathcal{C}_0 + N_f \mathcal{C}}{N_c \mathcal{C}_0 + N_f \mathcal{C}/2} \ln\frac{m_D}{q_{\text{IR}}}\), with \(\mathcal{C}_0 = \pi^2 T^2/3\) and \(\mathcal{C} = \pi^2 T^2/3 + \mu^2\).
- The Debye mass for QCD is \(m_D^2 = (N_c \mathcal{C}_0 + N_f \mathcal{C}/2)/\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-dependent transport in vortical fluids, building on prior developments in chiral kinetic theory by Son and others (2012-2013) and spin kinetic theory by Hattori, Wang, Gao, and others (2019-2020). It introduces a new term in the spin polarization decomposition (the \(A_3\) term) beyond the known spin-vorticity coupling (chiral vortical effect) studied by Vilenkin (1980), Erdmenger (2008), Banerjee (2008), Neiman (2010), and Landsteiner (2011). The effect could enable the generation of a vector current in anisotropic systems such as the early stage of heavy ion collisions, where significant vorticity is produced as measured by the STAR collaboration (2017).
