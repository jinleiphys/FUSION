# Neutrinoless double beta decay: searching for new physics with comparison of different nuclei

> The neutrinoless double beta decay is analyzed using a general Lorentz invariant effective Lagrangian for various decaying nuclei of current experimental interest: $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe. We work out the half-lives and angular correlation coefficients of the outgoing electrons in several scenarios for new physics: the left-right symmetric models, the R-parity-violating SUSY and models with leptoquarks. The theoretical uncertainty in the nuclear matrix elements is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that comparing half-lives and electron angular correlations across different nuclei can help identify the underlying new physics mechanism in neutrinoless double beta decay, but the half-life ratios are dominated by nuclear model uncertainties, while the angular correlation ratios are not discriminating among theories as they are all consistent with unity within anticipated experimental uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used a general Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian for the long-range mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by light Majorana neutrinos, parametrizing new physics contributions through coefficients epsilon. They computed half-life ratios and angular correlation coefficients for five nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo, 130Te, 136Xe) in two scenarios: standard model plus Majorana neutrinos with nonzero effective Majorana mass, and vanishing effective Majorana mass with nonzero new physics parameters, using two different QRPA nuclear models (without and with p-n pairing) from Pantis (1996).

## Key numbers

- For 100Mo, the half-life ratio R = R_{V-A}^{V-A} is 1.08 (QRPA without p-n pairing) and 195.18 (QRPA with p-n pairing), showing extreme nuclear model dependence
- For 82Se, the half-life ratio R_{V-A}^{V+A} is 2.10 (without p-n pairing) and 3.07 (with p-n pairing), making it the most sensitive nucleus to this new physics contribution
- The angular coefficient ratios K for the V±A to V-A case are all near unity: 1.08 for 82Se, 1.08 for 100Mo, 1.04 for 130Te, and 1.03 for 136Xe
- For 100Mo, the angular coefficient ratio K_{V+A}^{V+A} is 1.13 (without p-n pairing) and 0.84 (with p-n pairing)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ali, Borisov, and Zhuridov (2007) on 76Ge to a set of five experimentally relevant nuclei, following earlier studies by Deppisch (2006), Gehman (2007), and Fogli (2009) on using multiple nuclei to determine the underlying physics mechanism. The results enable experimental facilities like NEMO3, SuperNEMO, MOON, and EXO to potentially identify the dominant new physics mechanism once neutrinoless double beta decay is unambiguously observed, though no current experiment is designed to measure the angular correlations.
