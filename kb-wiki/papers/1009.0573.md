# Enhancement of sub-barrier fusion of two-neutron halo nuclei

> The tunneling of composite systems, where breakup may occur during the barrier penetration process is considered in connection with the fusion of halo-like radioactive, neutron- and proton-rich nuclei on heavy targets. The large amount of recent and new data clearly indicates that breakup hinders the fusion at near and below the Coulomb barrier energies. However, clear evidence for the halo enhancements, seems to over ride the breakup hindrance at lower energies, owing, to a large extent, to the extended matter density distribution. In particular we report here that at sub-barrier energies the fusion cross section of the Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus $^{6}$He with the actinide nucleus $^{238}$U is significantly enhanced compared to the fusion of a no-halo $^{6}$He. This conclusion differs from that of the original work, where it was claimed that no such enhancement ensues. This sub-barrier fusion enhancement was also observed in the $^{6}$He + $^{209}$% Bi system. The role of the corresponding easily excitable low lying dipole pygmy resonance in these systems is therefore significant. The consequence of this overall enhanced fusion of halo nuclei at sub-barrier energies, on stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis is evident.

## Key claim

At sub-barrier energies, the fusion cross section of the Borromean two-neutron halo nucleus ⁶He with the actinide nucleus ²³⁸U is significantly enhanced compared to the fusion of a no-halo ⁶He, contradicting the original work of Raabe et al. (2004) which claimed no such enhancement. The enhancement arises from the static effect of the extended matter density distribution of the halo, which lowers the fusion barrier and increases penetrability, and this effect overrides the breakup hindrance that dominates at near-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing fusion data for ⁶He + ²³⁸U (Raabe et al., 2004) and ⁶He + ²⁰⁹Bi (Kolata et al., 1998) using a universal fusion function (UFF) method, which reduces experimental fusion cross sections to a system-independent benchmark. They performed coupled-channels calculations with the double-folding São Paulo potential, comparing results using a realistic ⁶He halo density (including the extended matter distribution) against a scaled ⁴He density that omits the halo static effect, while noting that dynamic breakup coupling effects are insignificant at sub-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶He + ²³⁸U, the fusion cross section at sub-barrier energies is enhanced when the realistic ⁶He halo density is used in the double-folding potential, compared to using a scaled ⁴He density (no-halo case); the barrier is lowered by the halo, as shown in Figure 3 of the paper.
- For ⁶He + ²⁰⁹Bi, the same static halo enhancement is observed at sub-barrier energies (Figure 5), confirming the effect is independent of the target.
- For tightly bound systems, the renormalized experimental fusion function closely follows the universal fusion function \(F_0(x)\), which has the limiting value \(\ln 2\) at \(x=0\) (center-of-mass energy equal to the Coulomb barrier height).
- For the weakly bound systems ⁹Be + ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹⁷F + ²⁰⁸Pb, total fusion shows good agreement above the barrier (\(x > 0\)) and slight enhancement at sub-barrier energies (\(x < 0\)).
- For ⁶,⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi and ⁹Be + ²⁰⁸Pb, complete fusion is suppressed by about 30% at energies above the barrier.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fusion for weakly bound and halo nuclei, resolving a contradiction between the original experimental claim of no sub-barrier enhancement for ⁶He + ²³⁸U (Raabe et al., 2004) and theoretical expectations. It builds on the universal fusion function method developed by Canto et al. (2009) to isolate static halo effects from dynamic breakup couplings, and it supports the theoretical prediction by Goriely (1998) that enhanced fusion of halo nuclei at sub-barrier energies could affect the r-process in nucleosynthesis. The results enable future studies of the role of pygmy dipole resonances in these systems and their astrophysical consequences.
