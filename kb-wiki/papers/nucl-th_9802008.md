# Nuclear breakup of $^8$B in a direct fragmentation model

> We calculate the cross sections of the elastic and inelastic breakup modes for the inclusive breakup reaction $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be) at beam energies between 10 - 40 MeV/nucleon within a direct fragmentation model formulated in the framework of the post form distorted-wave Born-approximation. In contrast to the case of the stable isotopes, the inelastic breakup mode is found to contribute only upto 30% to the total breakup cross section, which is in agreement with the recently measured experimental data. However, the high energy tail of the energy spectra of $^7$Be fragment is dominated by the inelastic breakup mode. The breakup amplitude is found to be dominated by the contributions from distrances well beyond the nuclear surface.

# Nuclear breakup of $^8$B in a direct fragmentation model

## Key claim

The inelastic breakup mode contributes only up to 30% of the total breakup cross section for the $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be) reaction at beam energies between 10-40 MeV/nucleon, in contrast to stable isotopes where this mode makes up 75-80% of the total; however, the high energy tail of the $^7$Be fragment energy spectrum is dominated by the inelastic breakup mode.

## Method

The authors employed a direct fragmentation model formulated in the post form distorted-wave Born-approximation (PFDWBA), using a constant range approximation (CRA) to reduce the six-dimensional transition amplitude to three dimensions while allowing non-zero orbital angular momentum values. The elastic breakup (diffraction dissociation) and inelastic breakup (diffraction stripping) cross sections were calculated separately, with the inelastic breakup amplitude obtained via a surface approximation that expresses the form factor in terms of the elastic scattering wave function and Coulomb functions. The $^8$B ground state was treated as a pure $0p_{3/2}$ proton single-particle state with separation energy 0.137 MeV, using a Woods-Saxon potential with geometry parameters $r_0$ = 1.54 fm and $a_0$ = 0.52 fm.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic breakup mode contributes only up to 30% to the total breakup cross section for $^{28}$Si($^8$B,$^7$Be), compared to 75-80% for stable isotopes.
- The breakup probability $T_\ell$ peaks at an impact parameter of about 8 fm, which is larger than the sum of the radii of $^8$B and $^{28}$Si (approximately 6 fm).
- The $^8$B ground state wave function was calculated with a proton separation energy of 0.137 MeV, yielding $D_a$ = -39.0 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm for the reference potential.
- Alternative potential parameters from Barker ($r_0$ = 1.25 fm, $a_0$ = 0.65 fm) gave $D_a$ = -40.4 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm; from Esbensen and Bertsch ($r_0$ = 1.25 fm, $a_0$ = 0.52 fm) gave $D_a$ = -41.7 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.7 fm; RPA models gave $D_a$ = -38.0 MeV fm$^{3/2}$ and $d$ = 1.8 fm.
- The optical potential used had parameters: $V_R$ = 123.0 MeV, $r_V$ = 0.75 fm, $a_V$ = 0.80 fm, $W$ = 65.0 MeV, $r_W$ = 0.78 fm, $a_W$ = 0.80 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical approaches to nuclear breakup of $^8$B, such as the Serber-type model used by Negoita et al. (1996) and the diffraction dissociation picture of Henchen et al. (1996), by providing a fully quantum mechanical calculation within the PFDWBA framework. The results are consistent with experimental data from Negoita et al. (1996) and show that the breakup of $^8$B occurs at impact parameters well beyond the nuclear surface, similar to observations for neutron halo nuclei $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$Be reported by Shyam et al. (1992). The study is relevant to extracting the astrophysical $S$-factor for the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B reaction from Coulomb dissociation measurements, as discussed by Baur (1997) and Kikuchi et al. (1997). The authors note that the $0p_{3/2}$ component carries more than 90% of the spectroscopic weight in the $^8$B ground state, and that the lack of precise optical potential parameters for the $^8$B,$^7$Be + $^{28}$Si system is a source of uncertainty.
