# Direct microscopic calculations of nuclear level densities in the shell model Monte Carlo approach

> Nuclear level densities are crucial for estimating statistical nuclear reaction rates. The shell model Monte Carlo method is a powerful approach for microscopic calculation of state densities in very large model spaces. However, these state densities include the spin degeneracy of each energy level, whereas experiments often measure level densities in which each level is counted just once. To enable the direct comparison of theory with experiments, we introduce a method to calculate directly the level density in the shell model Monte Carlo approach. The method employs a projection on the minimal absolute value of the magnetic quantum number. We apply the method to nuclei in the iron region as well as the strongly deformed rare-earth nucleus $^{162}$Dy. We find very good agreement with experimental data including level counting at low energies, charged particle spectra and Oslo method at intermediate energies, neutron and proton resonance data, and Ericson's fluctuation analysis at higher excitation energies.

## Key claim
The authors introduce a method to directly calculate nuclear level densities (counting each level once) within the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach, rather than state densities (which include spin degeneracy), by projecting onto the minimal absolute value of the magnetic quantum number (M=0 for even-even and odd-odd nuclei, M=1/2 for odd-even nuclei). The method avoids the large statistical errors that would arise from summing individually projected spin-J level densities.

## Method
The SMMC method uses a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to express the imaginary-time propagator as a path integral over auxiliary fields, with Monte Carlo sampling of the one-body propagators. The authors project onto a fixed magnetic quantum number M using a discrete Fourier transform over the rotation angle about the z-axis, then compute the M-projected thermal energy and partition function. The M-projected level density is obtained via the saddle-point approximation from the M-projected canonical entropy and heat capacity, with the heat capacity computed using a correlated-error method inside the path integral to reduce statistical fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- For nuclei in the iron region (⁵⁶Fe, ⁶⁰Ni, ⁶²Ni, ⁶⁰Co), SMMC level densities are compared with experimental data including level counting at low excitation energies, charged particle spectra at intermediate energies, and Ericson's fluctuation analysis at higher excitation energies; for ⁶⁰Co, proton resonance data at around 8 MeV are also shown.
- For the rare-earth nucleus ¹⁶²Dy, the SMMC level density is compared with level counting at low excitation energies, renormalized Oslo data at intermediate energies, and neutron resonance data (triangle).
- The thermodynamic moment of inertia is extracted from the ratio of state density to level density; for the even-even nucleus ⁵⁶Fe, a suppression of the moment of inertia is observed at low excitation energies, reflecting pairing correlations.

## Context
This work extends the SMMC approach for state densities (Nakada 1997, Ormand 1997, Alhassid 1999) to directly compute level densities, enabling direct comparison with experimental data without the need to sum over spin-projected densities (which had large statistical errors as noted in Alhassid 2007). The method is applied to medium-mass nuclei in the iron region and the deformed rare-earth nucleus ¹⁶²Dy, and the results agree with multiple experimental data sets including level counting, charged particle spectra, Oslo method data, neutron and proton resonance data, and Ericson's fluctuation analysis. This enables future microscopic calculations of level densities for a wide range of nuclei, including those with strong correlations and deformation.
