# Double Scattering in Deuteron Electrodisintegration

> We demonstrate that at sufficiently high energies where the eikonal regime is established for hadronic interactions, the double scattering subprocess can be clearly identified and isolated in quasi-elastic deuteron electro-disintegration processes. Comparing theoretical calculations with the recent high precision experimental data we present a ``proof of principle" that these processes can be used to study advanced issues related to hadron formation in QCD. In this case, the double scattering represents as a fermi-scale ``detector" which probes products of high $Q^2$ scattering from the bound nucleon through their rescattering from the spectator nucleon in the deuteron.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that at sufficiently high energies where the eikonal regime is established for hadronic interactions, the double scattering subprocess can be clearly identified and isolated in quasi-elastic deuteron electro-disintegration processes, but only under the condition that the four-momentum transfer squared Q^2 is large enough to isolate the double scattering while still moderate enough that the hadronic picture of rescattering remains valid and no color transparency effects are expected.

## Method

The authors use the Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) to calculate the exclusive deuteron electro-disintegration reaction e + d → e' + N_f + N_r at large Q^2 kinematics, where the struck nucleon (proton) and recoil nucleon (neutron) can be clearly distinguished. They sum the contributions from four Feynman diagrams: plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), final state pn → pn rescattering (FSI), charge-exchange pn → np rescattering (ChEx-FSI), and resonance production RN → pn rescattering. The theoretical calculations are compared with recent high-precision experimental data from Jefferson Lab to test whether the energy dependence of the double scattering subprocess reflects the known energy dependence of free pn elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The analysis targets Q^2 values of "few (GeV/c)^2" where the double scattering subprocess can be isolated
- A dedicated experiment has been approved at Jefferson Lab to probe color transparency for double scattering processes up to Q^2 = 14 (GeV/c)^2
- The kinematics require p_f ~ q >> p_r ~ m_N, with Q^2 >> m_N^2 to establish the high-energy condition

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework proposed in the 1990s by Frankfurt (1994) and Laget (1998), who suggested using the energy dependence of double scattering strength in deuteron electro-disintegration as a test for the onset of color transparency phenomena. The authors provide a "proof of principle" that the double scattering subprocess can be isolated and its energy dependence matches free pn scattering, establishing a baseline for future studies of color transparency at higher Q^2 and hadron formation processes in QCD. This enables the use of the deuteron as a fermi-scale "detector" to probe products of high Q^2 scattering from bound nucleons through their rescattering from the spectator nucleon.
