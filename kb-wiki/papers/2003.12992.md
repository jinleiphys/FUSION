# Statistical hadronization model for heavy-ion collisions in a few GeV energy regime

> We show that the transverse-mass and rapidity spectra of protons and pions produced in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.4 GeV can be well reproduced in a thermodynamic model assuming single freeze-out of particles from a spherically symmetric hypersurface. This scenario corresponds to a physical picture used by Siemens and Rasmussen in the original formulation of the blast-wave model. Our framework modifies and extends this approach by incorporation of a Hubble-like expansion of QCD matter and inclusion of resonance decays. In particular, the Delta(1232) resonance is taken into account, with a width obtained from the virial expansion. Altogether, our results bring evidence for substantial thermalization of the matter produced in heavy-ion collisions in a few GeV energy regime and its nearly spherical expansion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse-mass and rapidity spectra of protons and pions from Au-Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.4 GeV can be well reproduced by a thermodynamic model with a spherically symmetric freeze-out hypersurface and a Hubble-like radial flow, but the model's rapidity distributions are too narrow, indicating the system is more elongated along the beam axis than the spherical assumption allows.

## Method

The authors implement the Siemens-Rasmussen blast-wave model with spherical symmetry into the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo framework, replacing the original constant radial flow with a Hubble-like parametrization v(r) = tanh(H r). They first determine thermodynamic parameters (temperature, chemical potentials, strangeness suppression factor) by fitting to experimental hadron multiplicities (protons, pions, kaons, Lambdas) measured by HADES for the 10% most central Au-Au collisions. The Hubble parameter H is then fixed by fitting the proton transverse-mass spectrum, and the resulting model is used to predict the rapidity and transverse-mass spectra of protons and pions without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

* Temperature T = 49.6 +/- 1 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B = 776 +/- 3 MeV, isospin chemical potential mu_{I3} = -14.1 +/- 0.2 MeV, strangeness chemical potential mu_S = 123.4 +/- 2 MeV, and strangeness suppression factor gamma_s = 0.16 +/- 0.02, obtained from fitting hadron multiplicities in central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.4 GeV.
* Hubble parameter H = 0.04 fm^{-1}, with corresponding freeze-out radius R = 16.02 fm and mean radial flow <v> ~ 0.4, obtained from fitting the proton transverse-mass spectrum.
* Goodness-of-fit Q = 0.20 for the proton transverse-mass spectrum, Q = 0.28 for the proton rapidity distribution, Q = 0.46 for negatively charged pion transverse-mass spectrum, and Q = 0.12 for negatively charged pion rapidity distribution.
* Proton multiplicity 77.6 +/- 2.4, bound proton multiplicity 46.5 +/- 1.5, pi+ multiplicity 9.3 +/- 0.6, pi- multiplicity 17.1 +/- 1.1, K+ multiplicity 5.98e-2 +/- 6.79e-3, K- multiplicity 5.6e-4 +/- 5.96e-5, Lambda multiplicity 8.22e-2 (+5.2e-3, -9.2e-3) used as input for thermodynamic parameter extraction.

## Context

This work extends the original blast-wave model of Siemens and Rasmussen (1978) by incorporating a Hubble-like expansion and resonance decays, particularly the Delta(1232) resonance with a width from the virial expansion of Lo (2017). It provides an alternative to transport model approaches for describing HADES data at sqrt(sNN) = 2.4 GeV, where thermalization is still debated (Lang 1990, Endres 2015, Galatyuk 2015, Staudenmaier 2017). The results enable future studies of strangeness production and more detailed comparisons with hydrodynamic models at low beam energies, and suggest that boost-invariant blast-wave models (Schnedermann 1993) cannot reproduce the Gaussian rapidity distributions observed at these energies.
