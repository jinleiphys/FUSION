# Chemical Freeze-out and the QCD Phase Transition Temperature

> We argue that hadron multiplicities in central high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions are established very close to the phase boundary between hadronic and quark matter. In the hadronic picture this can be described by multi-particle collisions whose importance is strongly enhanced due to the high particle density in the phase transition region. As a consequence of the rapid fall-off of the multi-particle scattering rates the experimentally determined chemical freeze-out temperature is a good measure of the phase transition temperature.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature T$_{ch}$ extracted from hadron multiplicities in central high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions is very close to the critical temperature T$_c$ of the QCD phase transition, to within a few MeV, because multi-particle scattering processes that can establish chemical equilibrium are only fast enough in the immediate vicinity of T$_c$.

## Method

The authors argue that two-particle scattering and decays are far too slow to achieve or maintain chemical equilibrium for multi-strange hadrons (e.g., $\Omega$ baryons) at temperatures near T$_{ch}$. They instead evaluate rates for multi-particle scattering processes (e.g., $2\pi+3K \to \bar{N}\Omega$) using measured cross sections and thermal densities from the statistical model, and show that these rates become sufficient only within a narrow temperature window near T$_c$. The temperature dependence of the particle density is taken from lattice QCD results, and the rapid drop of multi-particle rates with decreasing density forces T$_{ch}$ to coincide with T$_c$ within about 5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- T$_{ch}$ = 176 MeV at RHIC energies (chemical freeze-out temperature from thermal model fits)
- For the reaction $2\pi+3K \to \bar{N}\Omega$ at T = 176 MeV: $r_{\Omega} = 1.39 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-4}$, yielding $\tau_{\Omega} = n_{\Omega}/r_{\Omega} = 2.2$ fm
- Pion density at T = 176 MeV: $n_{\pi} = 0.174$ fm$^{-3}$; kaon-to-pion ratio $n_K/n_{\pi} = 0.172$
- Two-particle scattering rate for $\Omega+\bar{K}\to\Xi+\pi$: $\bar{r}_{\Omega}/n_{\Omega} = 0.018$ fm$^{-1}$ (using $\sigma = 10$ mb, $v=0.6$)
- Two-particle kaon production rate $\pi+\pi\to K+\bar{K}$: $\bar{r}_K/n_K = 0.18$ fm$^{-1}$ (using $\sigma = 3$ mb)
- Equilibration times for other strange baryons: $\tau_{\Xi} = 0.71$ fm/c, $\tau_{\Lambda} = 0.66$ fm/c
- Estimated critical temperature: T$_c = 176^{+5}_{-18}$ MeV (including uncertainty from possible temperature-dependent hadron masses)
- Temperature decrease of $\Delta$T = 5 MeV below T$_c$ reduces the five-meson contribution to $r_{\Omega}$ by more than a factor of 10

## Context

This work extends the long-standing observation that hadron yields in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC are well described by a hadro-chemical equilibrium model (reviewed in Braun-Munzinger et al. (2003)), and addresses the puzzle raised by Rafelski (1982) that two-body equilibration times for $\Omega$ baryons exceed fireball lifetimes. The authors argue that the chemical freeze-out temperature T$_{ch}$ traces the QCD phase boundary, enabling experimental mapping of the critical line in the QCD phase diagram. This contrasts with alternative hadronization-based explanations (Stock, Heinz) and provides a quantitative connection between measured freeze-out parameters and lattice QCD results (Karsch).
