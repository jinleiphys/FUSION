# The Continuum Spectrum of Hypernuclear Trios

> The spectrum of hypernuclear trios composed of a $Λ$ baryon and two nucleons is the subject of an ongoing experimental campaign, aiming to study the interaction of the $Λ$ particle with a neutron, and the 3-body $Λ$-nucleon-nucleon force. In this manuscript we utilize baryonic effective field theory at leading order, constrained to reproduce the available low energy light hypernuclear data, to study the continuum spectrum of such hypernuclear trios. Using the complex scaling method and the inverse analytic continuation in the coupling constant method we find the existence of a virtual state in the $Λn p$ $J^π=\frac{3}{2}^{+}$ channel, leading to cross-section enhancement near threshold. For the $Λn n$ $J^π=\frac{1}{2}^{+}$ channel we predict a resonance state. Depending, however, on the value of the $ΛN$ scattering length, the resonance pole moves from the physical to the unphysical complex energy sheet within the experimental bounds.

## Key claim

Using baryonic effective field theory at leading order, the authors find that the excited state of hypertriton, \(^3_\Lambda\text{H}^*(J^\pi=3/2^+)\), is a virtual state, and the \(\Lambda n n\) system exhibits a resonance pole whose location depends on the \(\Lambda N\) scattering length; within current experimental bounds on that scattering length, the pole can move from the physical to the unphysical complex energy sheet.

## Method

The authors employ baryonic (pionless) effective field theory at leading order, with two-body and three-body contact interactions regularized by a gaussian regulator with momentum cutoff \(\lambda\). The low-energy constants are fitted to reproduce \(\Lambda N\) and \(NN\) scattering lengths, the triton binding energy, and the \(\Lambda\) separation energies of \(^3_\Lambda\text{H}\), \(^4_\Lambda\text{H}\), and \(^4_\Lambda\text{H}^*\). Three-body bound and continuum states are calculated using the Stochastic Variational Method with correlated gaussians; continuum states are located via the Complex Scaling Method and the Inverse Analytic Continuation in the Coupling Constant method.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda\) separation energy of the hypertriton is \(B_{\Lambda} = 0.13 \pm 0.05\) MeV (from prior experiment, cited as Davis (2005)).
* For the \(\Lambda nn\) system, the imaginary part of the physical pole energy lies in the interval \(-1.32 \leq \text{Im}(E^\lambda_{\Lambda nn}) \leq -0.58\) MeV for all sets of \(\Lambda N\) scattering lengths considered, regardless of cutoff.
* The minimal spin-averaged \(\Lambda N\) scattering length required for the \(\Lambda nn\) pole to become a physical resonance (fourth quadrant of the complex energy plane) is estimated as \(\bar{a}^{\Lambda N} \approx 1.7\) fm.
* The \(\Lambda d\) scattering length \(a^{\Lambda d}_{3/2}\) and effective range \(r^{\Lambda d}_{3/2}\) extracted from CSM calculations for the \(J=3/2\) channel are negative and positive respectively, confirming a virtual state; specific values are given in Table~\ref{lambdad} for several \(\Lambda N\) interaction models at cutoff \(\lambda = 6\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The IACCC method yields virtual state energies \(E_v\) for \(^3_\Lambda\text{H}^*\) that agree qualitatively with CSM results; for example, with the Alexander B \(\Lambda N\) scattering lengths at \(\lambda = 6\) fm\(^{-1}\), the IACCC result for \(E_v\) is reported.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies that ruled out bound \(\Lambda nn\) and \(^3_\Lambda\text{H}^*\) states, such as those by Dalitz and Downs (1959), Garcilazo et al. (2007), and Afnan and Gibson (2015), by providing the first comprehensive calculation of the continuum spectrum using a baryonic effective field theory constrained to reproduce \(A=3,4,5\) hypernuclear data. The results directly address the ongoing experimental campaign, including the JLab E12-17-003 experiment searching for the \(\Lambda nn\) system and the JLab proposal P12-19-002 studying \(^3_\Lambda\text{H}^*\). The findings enable future work to refine the \(\Lambda N\) interaction parameters and to predict cross-section enhancements near threshold in hypernuclear trio reactions.
