# Astronomy meets QCD: cooling constraints for the theories of internal structure of compact objects

> We discuss a set of tests which confront observations of cooling compact objects and theories of their thermal evolution. As an example we apply the recently developed $\mathrm{Log N}$-$\mathrm{Log S}$ test of compact star cooling theories to hybrid stars with a color superconducting quark matter core, we also apply and discuss other existing tests. While there is not yet a microscopically founded superconducting quark matter phase which would fulfill constraints from cooling phenomenology, we explore the hypothetical 2SC+X phase and show that the magnitude and density-dependence of the X-gap can be chosen to satisfy a set of tests: the temperature -- age ($\mathrm{T}$-$\mathrm{t}$) test, the $\mathrm{Log N}$-$\mathrm{Log S}$ test, the brightness constraint, and the mass spectrum constraint. Some recent modifications of the population synthesis model used to obtain the $\mathrm{Log N}$-$\mathrm{Log S}$ distribution are briefly discussed. In addition, we propose to use the age-distance diagram as a new tool to study the local population of young isolated neutron stars.

# Astronomy meets QCD: cooling constraints for the theories of internal structure of compact objects

## Key claim
While no microscopically founded superconducting quark matter phase yet satisfies constraints from cooling phenomenology, the hypothetical 2SC+X phase with a density-dependent X-gap can be tuned to simultaneously pass four observational tests: the temperature-age test, the Log N-Log S test, the brightness constraint, and the mass spectrum constraint.

## Method
The authors applied a population synthesis code to generate artificial populations of neutron stars using cooling curves for hybrid stars with a color superconducting quark matter core (2SC+X phase). They compared the resulting Log N-Log S distributions, temperature-age tracks, brightness limits, and mass spectrum predictions against observations of isolated neutron stars from ROSAT and other surveys, using a model that includes initial spatial distributions from the Galactic disc and Gould Belt, velocity distributions, interstellar absorption, and detector response.

## Key numbers
- Birth rate in the disc: 250 neutron stars per Myr; in the Gould Belt: 20 per Myr; total 270 newborn NS per Myr in the modeled region
- The X-gap parameterization uses a critical chemical potential μ_c = 330 MeV with an exponential density dependence Δ_X = Δ_0 exp[-α ((μ - μ_c)/μ_c)]
- The population synthesis code uses a time step of 10^4 years and calculates 5,000 tracks per model
- The Gould Belt is modeled with radii of 300 pc and 500 pc, with an inclination of 18° relative to the galactic plane
- The brightness constraint excludes neutron stars with log T > 6.3-6.4 K at ages ~10^3-10^4 years
- The mass spectrum shows that the fraction of neutron stars with masses larger than ~1.4 M_⊙ is very small
- The age-distance diagram visibility line uses a limiting unabsorbed flux of 10^{-12} erg cm^{-2} s^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the population synthesis approach of Popov et al. (2006) and the Log N-Log S test developed by Popov, Grigorian, Blaschke, and Turolla (2006) to hybrid stars with color superconducting quark cores. It builds on cooling curve calculations by Grigorian, Blaschke, and Voskresensky (2005) and the temperature-age test framework described by Page et al. (2004) and Blaschke et al. (2004). The paper enables future testing of dense matter theories by providing a multi-test methodology that can discriminate between different internal compositions of neutron stars, and proposes the age-distance diagram as a new diagnostic tool for the local population of young isolated neutron stars once more precise age and distance measurements become available.
