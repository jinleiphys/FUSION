# Yrast line for weakly interacting trapped bosons

> We compute numerically the yrast line for harmonically trapped boson systems with a weak repulsive contact interaction, studying the transition to a vortex state as the angular momentum L increases and approaches N, the number of bosons. The L=N eigenstate is indeed dominated by particles with unit angular momentum, but the state has other significant components beyond the pure vortex configuration. There is a smooth crossover between low and high L with no indication of a quantum phase transition. Most strikingly, the energy and wave function appear to be analytical functions of L over the entire range 2 < L < N. We confirm the structure of low-L states proposed by Mottelson, as mainly single-particle excitations with two or three units of angular momentum.

# Yrast line for weakly interacting trapped bosons

## Key claim

The authors find that for harmonically trapped bosons with a weak repulsive contact interaction, the yrast line and wave functions appear to be analytical functions of angular momentum L over the entire range 2 ≤ L ≤ N, with no indication of a quantum phase transition, contradicting Mottelson's prediction of a phase transition between low-L and high-L regimes.

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalize the two-body contact interaction Hamiltonian within the Fock space basis for N bosons in a two-dimensional harmonic trap, using a Lanczos algorithm to compute yrast states (ground states at fixed angular momentum L). They restrict calculations to L ≤ 50, corresponding to a maximal Fock space dimension of about 2 × 10^5 basis states, and analyze the inverse participation ratio and occupation numbers to characterize the wave function structure.

## Key numbers

- For N = 25 and N = 50 bosons, the interaction energy ε_L follows the algebraic expression ε_L = N(2N − L − 2)/2 to machine precision.
- The inverse participation ratio 1/I_L for N = 50 bosons shows that at midvalues of L, only about 30 basis states are active participants in the yrast state.
- At L = N = 25, the pure vortex state |0 N 0 ... 0⟩ has less than half the probability of the complete wave function.
- The numerical computation is limited to angular momenta below about L ≈ 50.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Mottelson (1999), who proposed a quantum phase transition in Fock space between low-L yrast states (dominated by quadrupole and octupole modes) and high-L vortex states for weakly interacting trapped bosons. The numerical results contradict that prediction, showing instead a smooth crossover with no phase transition. The authors also confirm that the L = N yrast state agrees with the conjecture of Wilkin, Gunn, and Smith (1998), and they propose a generalized analytic formula for all yrast states with 2 ≤ L ≤ N. This work enables further study of localization in Fock space, which is relevant to molecular and condensed matter physics.
