# Statistical features of the thermal neutron capture cross sections

> We discuss the existence of huge thermal neutron capture cross sections in several nuclei. The values of the cross sections are several orders of magnitude bigger than expected at these very low energies. We lend support to the idea that this phenomenon is random in nature and is similar to what we have learned from the study of parity violation in the actinide region. The idea of statistical doorways is advanced as a unified concept in the delineation of large numbers in the nuclear world. The average number of maxima per unit mass, $<n_A>$ in the capture cross section is calculated and related to the underlying cross section correlation function and found to be $<n_A> = 3/(π\sqrt{2}γ_{A})$, where $γ_{A}$ is a characteristic mass correlation width which designates the degree of remnant coherence in the system. We trace this coherence to nucleosynthesis which produced the nuclei whose neutron capture cross sections are considered here.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalously large thermal neutron capture cross sections observed in a few nuclei (e.g., \(^{157}\)Gd, \(^{113}\)Cd, \(^{135}\)Xe) are random, rare events caused by isolated 2p-1h doorway states that happen to lie near the neutron separation energy, rather than being systematic properties of those nuclei. The authors hedge that this explanation is statistical in nature and that the probability of such a doorway enhancement is very small, consistent with the data.

## Method

The authors analyze thermal neutron capture cross sections from the compilation of Mughabghab (2003) across the periodic table. They model the enhancement using a statistical doorway resonance formalism based on Feshbach's reaction theory, where a 2p-1h doorway state couples to the neutron channel and then damps into compound nucleus resonances, which subsequently emit gamma rays. They also compute the average number of maxima per unit mass number \(\langle n_A \rangle\) in the cross section versus A data and relate it to an Efetov-type correlation function to extract a mass correlation width \(\gamma_A\).

## Key numbers

* Thermal neutron (0.025 eV) capture cross section for \(^{157}\)Gd: \(2.54 \times 10^{5}\) barns (with uncertainty \(\pm 815\) barns).
* Thermal neutron capture cross section for \(^{113}\)Cd: \(2.06 \times 10^{4}\) barns (\(\pm 400\) barns).
* Thermal neutron capture cross section for \(^{135}\)Xe: \(3 \times 10^{6}\) barns.
* For ultra-cold neutrons (\(E_n < 0.001\) eV), the capture cross section for \(^{157}\)Gd can reach \(1.2 \times 10^{8}\) barns.
* The average number of maxima per unit mass number extracted from the data: \(\langle n_A \rangle = 0.39\).
* The corresponding mass correlation width: \(\gamma_A = 1.94\) (dimensionless, in units of mass number).
* The estimated doorway escape width for \(^{158}\)Gd: \(\Gamma^{\uparrow}_D = 0.18\) keV.
* The estimated damping width of the doorway: \(\Gamma^{\downarrow}_D \approx 1\) keV.
* The ratio of doorway total width to doorway spacing: \(\Gamma_D / \overline{D}_D = 0.024\), indicating isolated resonances.

## Context

This work extends the concept of statistical doorways, previously applied to parity violation in epithermal neutron scattering on actinides (Bowman et al., 1990s) and to pre-equilibrium reactions (Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin, 1980; Bonetti, Chiesa, and Herman, 1991), to explain anomalously large thermal neutron capture cross sections. The authors also adapt the Brink and Stephen (1963) formalism for the average density of maxima in fluctuating cross sections, combined with Efetov's (1995) correlation function for variation of an external parameter, to the mass-number dependence of capture cross sections. The work enables future searches for other cases of very large capture cross sections and may inform studies of radiative capture on exotic nuclei relevant to the s-process in astrophysics.
