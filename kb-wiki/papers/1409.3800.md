# Particle-hole symmetry parameters for nuclei

> Two new numbers, $ν$ and $ζ$, inspired by particle-hole symmetry are introduced. These numbers have extreme values at a closed shell and vanish mid-shell. A combination of even powers of these numbers has been used to model experimentally measured quantities such as $R4/2=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ and the "microscopic" contribution to binding energies. A binding energy fit consisting of a total of six fit coefficients, including one new shell term, reproduces the experimental binding energies of 2353 nuclei with an r.m.s. standard deviation of 1.55 MeV. The difference between the experimental and fit values of observables, specifically the $R4/2$, provides an indication of where shell closure features are less pronounced and where sub-shells closures occur.

# Particle-hole symmetry parameters for nuclei

## Key claim
Two new valence numbers, ν and ζ, derived from particle-hole symmetry, can model global nuclear shell effects in binding energies and excitation energy ratios using only even-power combinations, with the caveat that the symmetries are approximate (10–30% level) and the fits require empirical tuning of cross-term coefficients.

## Method
The author defines ν and ζ as normalized differences between valence particle and hole numbers for neutrons and protons respectively, ranging from -1 at shell boundaries to 0 at mid-shell. Even powers of these numbers are combined into polynomial forms (ν⁴ − ν²ζ² + ζ⁴ for R₄/₂ ratios, and (ν² + ζ²)² for binding energy shell corrections) and fitted to experimental data across the chart of nuclides. For binding energies, a six-parameter semi-empirical formula is used that includes a macroscopic liquid-drop model with modified Coulomb, pairing, and symmetry terms plus the new shell term, with magic numbers adjusted from conventional values.

## Key numbers
- The R₄/₂ fit using ν⁴ − ν²ζ² + ζ⁴ with two parameters (R_shell = -1.46, R_max = 2.88) reduces the standard deviation from σ = 0.58 (mean of 557 even-even nuclei) to σ = 0.37.
- The binding energy fit for 2353 nuclei (N>8, Z>8) using six coefficients achieves a root-mean-square standard deviation of σ = 1.55 MeV after adjusting shell closures at N,Z=14 and Z=114.
- The coefficient of variation for B(E2) values among particle-hole symmetric nuclei in the 50 ≤ N,Z ≤ 82 region averages c_B(E2) = 13% for 11 groups; for valence mirror-symmetric octets, the average is c_B(E2) = 29% for 19 groups.
- With the shell term set to zero, the binding energy fit degrades to σ = 2.65 MeV; with the pairing term set to zero, it degrades to σ = 1.88 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the promiscuity factor P factor introduced by Casten (1985) for correlating nuclear collectivity, which cannot distinguish singly from doubly magic nuclei. It also builds on IBM-based binding energy fits by Dieperink and Van Isacker (2007) that used eight parameters to achieve σ = 1.4 MeV, and on Van Isacker's (2009) global fit with σ = 1.2 MeV after modifying three shell closures. The new formalism reduces the number of adjustable coefficients while maintaining comparable accuracy, and the residuals ΔR₄/₂ provide a diagnostic for identifying sub-shell closures such as N=32 in titanium and chromium, as studied by Hagen (2012).
