# Final State Interactions in Hadronic D decays

> We show that the large corrections due to final state interactions (FSI) in the D^+\to ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+, D^+_s\to ŽĆ^-ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+, and D^+\to K^-ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+ decays can be accounted for by invoking scattering amplitudes in agreement with those derived from phase shifts studies. In this way, broad/overlapping resonances in S-waves are properly treated and the phase motions of the transition amplitudes are driven by the corresponding scattering matrix elements determined in many other experiments. This is an important step forward in resolving the puzzle of the FSI in these decays. We also discuss why the Žāand ╬║resonances, hardly visible in scattering experiments, are much more prominent and clearly visible in these decays without destroying the agreement with the experimental ŽĆŽĆand KŽĆlow energy S-wave phase shifts.

# Final State Interactions in Hadronic D Decays

## Key claim

The large corrections due to final state interactions (FSI) in hadronic D decays can be accounted for by invoking scattering amplitudes that agree with those derived from phase shift studies, provided that broad/overlapping S-wave resonances are treated using chiral unitary amplitudes rather than simple Breit-Wigner parameterizations. The Žā and ╬║ resonances appear much more prominently in D decays than in scattering experiments because the Adler zeros that produce large destructive backgrounds in ŽĆŽĆ and KŽĆ scattering are absent in production processes like D decays.

## Method

The author replaces the Breit-Wigner parameterizations for the Žā and f0(980) resonances used by the E791 Collaboration with the I=0 S-wave ŽĆŽĆ and KŽĆ partial waves derived from chiral perturbation theory at leading order combined with a unitarization scheme (the chiral unitary approach). For the D+ ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+, D+s ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+, and D+ ŌåÆ K-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+ decays, the author fits the production amplitudes using the inverse of the matrix [I+N┬Ęg] to resum unitarity bubbles, keeping the same non-resonant and higher-spin resonance contributions as in the original E791 parameterizations. The fits are performed on 20├Ś20 bin Dalitz plots generated from the E791 signal functions, normalized to the same number of events (1124 for D+ ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+, 625 for D+s ŌåÆ ŽĆ-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+, and 14185 for D+ ŌåÆ K-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Oller and collaborators (NPA, 1998; IAM, 1999) to hadronic D decays, resolving the puzzle of why the Žā and ╬║ resonances appear so prominently in D decays despite being hardly visible in ŽĆŽĆ and KŽĆ scattering. The author directly addresses the E791 Collaboration analyses (PRL D+ ŌåÆ 3ŽĆ, 2001; PRL D+s ŌåÆ 3ŽĆ, 2002; PRL D+ ŌåÆ K-ŽĆ+ŽĆ+, 2002), showing that their Breit-Wigner parameterizations for S-wave resonances violate Watson's final state theorem. The work also critiques the K-matrix fit by the FOCUS Collaboration (2003) for not satisfying chiral power counting constraints. This enables future analyses of D and B decays to use theoretically consistent parameterizations that respect both unitarity and chiral symmetry, making the extraction of scalar resonance properties more reliable.
