# Final State Interactions in Hadronic D decays

> We show that the large corrections due to final state interactions (FSI) in the D^+\to π^-π^+π^+, D^+_s\to π^-π^+π^+, and D^+\to K^-π^+π^+ decays can be accounted for by invoking scattering amplitudes in agreement with those derived from phase shifts studies. In this way, broad/overlapping resonances in S-waves are properly treated and the phase motions of the transition amplitudes are driven by the corresponding scattering matrix elements determined in many other experiments. This is an important step forward in resolving the puzzle of the FSI in these decays. We also discuss why the σand κresonances, hardly visible in scattering experiments, are much more prominent and clearly visible in these decays without destroying the agreement with the experimental ππand Kπlow energy S-wave phase shifts.

# Final State Interactions in Hadronic D Decays

## Key claim

The large corrections due to final state interactions (FSI) in hadronic D decays can be accounted for by invoking scattering amplitudes that agree with those derived from phase shift studies, provided that broad/overlapping S-wave resonances are treated using chiral unitary amplitudes rather than simple Breit-Wigner parameterizations. The σ and κ resonances appear much more prominently in D decays than in scattering experiments because the Adler zeros that produce large destructive backgrounds in ππ and Kπ scattering are absent in production processes like D decays.

## Method

The author replaces the Breit-Wigner parameterizations for the σ and f0(980) resonances used by the E791 Collaboration with the I=0 S-wave ππ and Kπ partial waves derived from chiral perturbation theory at leading order combined with a unitarization scheme (the chiral unitary approach). For the D+ → π-π+π+, D+s → π-π+π+, and D+ → K-π+π+ decays, the author fits the production amplitudes using the inverse of the matrix [I+N·g] to resum unitarity bubbles, keeping the same non-resonant and higher-spin resonance contributions as in the original E791 parameterizations. The fits are performed on 20×20 bin Dalitz plots generated from the E791 signal functions, normalized to the same number of events (1124 for D+ → π-π+π+, 625 for D+s → π-π+π+, and 14185 for D+ → K-π+π+).

## Key numbers

- D+ → π-π+π+: χ²/ν = 3/152 when replacing the σ Breit-Wigner with the pure σ pole contribution at sσ = (0.47 − i 0.22)² GeV², with residue γ0² = 5.3 + i 7.7 GeV²
- D+ → π-π+π+: χ²/ν = 2/152 when using the full chiral unitary T-matrix from Oller (NPA) for the S-wave FSI, with the σ pole fraction reaching 102% and the (K̄K)π+ fraction at 6%
- D+s → π-π+π+: χ²/ν = 11/142 with f0(1370) mass and width fixed to E791 values; χ²/ν = 8.5/140 when f0(1370) mass and width float to m = 1.46 GeV and Γ = 0.16 GeV
- σ pole position from the chiral unitary approach: approximately 448 − i 224 MeV
- f0(980) pole position from the chiral unitary approach: approximately 990 − i 13 MeV
- The σ Breit-Wigner employed by E791 has a phase that starts at approximately +90 degrees at the σ mass (478 MeV), while the chiral unitary σ pole contribution starts at −58 degrees at threshold and reaches only +122 degrees at infinity, consistent with experimental ππ phase shifts

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Oller and collaborators (NPA, 1998; IAM, 1999) to hadronic D decays, resolving the puzzle of why the σ and κ resonances appear so prominently in D decays despite being hardly visible in ππ and Kπ scattering. The author directly addresses the E791 Collaboration analyses (PRL D+ → 3π, 2001; PRL D+s → 3π, 2002; PRL D+ → K-π+π+, 2002), showing that their Breit-Wigner parameterizations for S-wave resonances violate Watson's final state theorem. The work also critiques the K-matrix fit by the FOCUS Collaboration (2003) for not satisfying chiral power counting constraints. This enables future analyses of D and B decays to use theoretically consistent parameterizations that respect both unitarity and chiral symmetry, making the extraction of scalar resonance properties more reliable.
