# Stochastic Estimation of Nuclear Level Density in the Nuclear Shell Model: An Application to Parity-Dependent Level Density in $^{58}$Ni

> We introduce a novel method to obtain level densities in large-scale shell-model calculations. Our method is a stochastic estimation of eigenvalue count based on a shifted Krylov-subspace method, which enables us to obtain level densities of huge Hamiltonian matrices. This framework leads to a successful description of both low-lying spectroscopy and the experimentally observed equilibration of $J^π=2^+$ and $2^-$ states in $^{58}$Ni in a unified manner.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a stochastic method for estimating nuclear level densities in large-scale shell-model calculations that can handle matrices with dimensions up to at least 1.5 × 10^10, and demonstrates that this method can reproduce the experimentally observed early equilibration of 2^+ and 2^- level densities in ^58Ni at around 8 MeV, in contrast to previous microscopic calculations that showed strong parity dependence up to about 20 MeV.

## Method

The method uses a shifted Krylov-subspace approach with the complex orthogonal conjugate gradient (COCG) method to stochastically estimate the trace of the resolvent of the Hamiltonian matrix via contour integration, avoiding the need for full diagonalization. The trace is estimated by sampling random vectors whose elements take values of 1 or -1 with equal probability, and the shifted Krylov-subspace technique allows simultaneous solution for multiple energy points without repeated matrix-vector products. For spin-dependent level densities, the random vectors are projected onto the desired total angular momentum using the Sakurai-Sugiura method, and the calculations are performed by combining the shell-model code KSHELL with the eigenvalue-solver library z-Pares.

## Key numbers

* The M-scheme dimension for the 2^- states in ^58Ni reaches 1.5 × 10^10, which is beyond the capability of direct Lanczos diagonalization but within the scope of the present method.
* In the benchmark test for ^28Si with the USD interaction, the stochastic estimation reproduces exact values with 8% error at around E_x = 15 MeV using N_s = 32 random vectors and a 200 keV energy bin.
* Only 28 iterations of the BCOCG method are required for convergence of the level density at E_x = 10 MeV in ^28Si.
* The 3^-_1 level in ^58Ni is reproduced at 4.55 MeV (calculated) versus 4.47 MeV (experimental).
* The spurious center-of-mass states are lifted to E_x ~ 600 MeV using the Lawson method with βℏω/A = 10 MeV.
* The 2^- level densities were calculated with N_s = 16 random vectors and 1000 BCOCG iterations.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic eigenvalue counting method of Futamura et al. (2015) to nuclear shell-model calculations of level density, and addresses a puzzle identified by Kalmykov et al. (2007) where previous microscopic calculations using the shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method and HFB-based approaches failed to reproduce the early equilibration of 2^+ and 2^- level densities in ^58Ni. The method enables a unified description of low-lying spectroscopy and level densities using realistic effective interactions, and the authors note that the SMMC method has difficulty using realistic residual interactions due to the sign problem, while the present approach is applicable to any effective interaction. The work enables future applications to ab initio shell-model calculations in light nuclei and further studies bridging low-lying spectroscopy with the statistical region.
