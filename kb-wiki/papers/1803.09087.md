# On the use of running $α_s$ in calculations of radiative energy loss of fast partons in a quark-gluon plasma

> The incorporation of running $α_s$ for the gluon emission vertex in calculations of radiative parton energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma is discussed. It is argued that the virtuality scale for running $α_s$ for induced gluon emission is determined by the square of the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon rather than by the square of the invariant mass of the final two-parton state often used in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the correct virtuality scale for running $\alpha_s$ in the gluon emission vertex for induced radiative energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma is the square of the transverse momentum of the emitted gluon ($k_\perp^2$), not the square of the invariant mass of the final two-parton state ($k_\perp^2/x(1-x)$) often used in the literature, based on the requirement of preserving the factorized form of the effective Bethe-Heitler cross section and the property of color transparency.

## Method

The author analyzes the $q\to gq$ transition in a medium modeled as a slab of color centers, using the light cone path integral (LCPI) approach. The effective Bethe-Heitler cross section is expressed in terms of the three-body cross section $\sigma_3$ for a color-singlet $q\bar{q}g$ system, which is derived from the dipole cross section for a $q\bar{q}$ pair. The running coupling is introduced by replacing the fixed $\alpha_s$ in the light cone wavefunctions for the $q\to gq$ vertex, and the consequences of using either $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2)$ or $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2/x(1-x))$ are compared analytically and through numerical calculations for LHC conditions.

## Key numbers

- For a QGP with initial temperature $T = 400$ MeV at proper time $\tau_0 = 0.5$ fm (central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV), in the gluon energy range $\omega\sim 1-3$ GeV, increasing the initial parton energy from $20$ to $150$ GeV changes the induced gluon spectrum by $\sim 3-10\%$ increase for $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2)$ and $\sim 23-30\%$ decrease for $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2/x(1-x))$.
- The gluon quasiparticle mass in the QGP is $m_g\sim 400$ MeV for RHIC and LHC conditions.
- The freezing value of $\alpha_s$ for vacuum processes is $\alpha_s^{fr}\approx 0.7-0.8$, while analyses of RHIC and LHC data on $R_{AA}$ show the QGP can reduce this to $\sim 0.4-0.6$.

## Context

This work extends the debate on running coupling prescriptions in jet quenching calculations, directly challenging the approach used in the CUJET model (based on the GLV formalism) and other works that employ $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2/x(1-x))$. The argument builds on the DDT analysis (1979) for vacuum jets, where $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2)$ is established, and on the dipole BFKL equation analyses by Nikolaev and Zakharov (1994) and Nikolaev and Zoller (1991) that successfully described HERA data using $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2)$. The paper enables a theoretically consistent treatment of running coupling in LCPI-based calculations of radiative energy loss and jet quenching, while noting that the variant using $\alpha_s(k_\perp^2/x(1-x))$ gives better agreement with LHC data on $R_{AA}$ despite being theoretically unsatisfactory.
