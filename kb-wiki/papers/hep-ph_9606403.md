# Nonperturbative study of generalized ladder graphs in a ϕ^2χtheory

> The Feynman-Schwinger representation is used to construct scalar-scalar bound states for the set of all ladder and crossed-ladder graphs in a ϕ^2χtheory in (3+1) dimensions. The results are compared to those of the usual Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and of several quasi-potential equations. Particularly for large couplings, the ladder predictions are seen to underestimate the binding energy significantly as compared to the generalized ladder case, whereas the solutions of the quasi-potential equations provide a better correspondence. Results for the calculated bound state wave functions are also presented.

# Nonperturbative study of generalized ladder graphs in a ϕ²χ theory

## Key claim

The Feynman-Schwinger representation (FSR) provides a nonperturbative method to compute scalar-scalar bound states including the complete set of ladder and crossed-ladder graphs in a ϕ²χ theory, and for large couplings the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation significantly underestimates binding energy compared to the full generalized ladder result, while several quasi-potential equations (QPEs) yield better agreement. The authors note that self-energy and vertex corrections are neglected, and these are expected not to lead to qualitatively different predictions.

## Method

The authors used the Feynman-Schwinger representation to construct the two-body Green function for two scalar φ particles (mass m) interacting via exchange of a third scalar χ particle (mass μ) in (3+1) dimensions, summing all ladder and crossed-ladder graphs without ordering in the interaction kernel. The functional integrals were discretized with N=35-40 time steps and evaluated using the Metropolis Monte-Carlo algorithm, with the ground state mass extracted from the logarithmic derivative of the Green function at large timelike separations (mT=40). Results were compared to solutions of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation and three quasi-potential equations: the BSLT equation, the equal-time (ET) equation, and the Gross equation.

## Key numbers

- Ground state mass m₀ was computed as a function of the dimensionless coupling g²/4πm² for the case μ/m = 0.15
- For the Gross equation, the physical and unphysical solutions "annihilate" at g²/4πm² ≈ 5.1, where √s ≈ 1.4m
- Convergence in the discretization parameter N was achieved at typical values of N=35-40
- The asymptotic estimate for the ground state mass required mT=40 for L(T) to become independent of T
- Wave function comparisons were performed for a bound state at m₀ = 1.882m with μ/m = 0.15

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic two-body bound states beyond the ladder approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which had been the standard approach but is known to fail in the one-body limit and cannot satisfy gauge invariance. The authors compare their FSR results to the ladder BSE and to three quasi-potential equations (BSLT, equal-time, and Gross) that were previously proposed as effective theories for systems including mesons, small nuclei, few-electron atoms, and positronium. The FSR approach enables the first nonperturbative calculations of bound state properties including crossed-ladder contributions, providing a benchmark for testing which QPE provides the best effective description, and the authors note that extending this method to include fermionic degrees of freedom is an important next step.
