# Reexamination of The Standard Model Nucleon Electric Dipole Moment

> The Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix in the Standard Model is currently the only experimentally-confirmed source of CP-violation. The intrinsic electric dipole moment of the nucleon induced by this CP-phase via hadronic loop and pole diagrams has been studied more than two decades ago, but the existing calculation is subject to various theoretical issues such as the breakdown of chiral power counting and uncertainties in the determination of low energy constants. We carry out an up-to-date re-analysis on both one-loop and pole diagram contributions to the nucleon electric dipole moment based on Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory in a way that preserves power counting, and redo the determination of the low energy constants following the results of more recent articles. Combined with an estimation of higher-order contributions, we expect the long-distance contribution to the Standard Model nucleon electric dipole moment to be approximately $(1\times10^{-32}-6\times10^{-32})e\,\mathrm{cm}$.

## Key claim

The long-distance Standard Model contribution to the nucleon electric dipole moment is expected to be approximately (1×10⁻³²–6×10⁻³²) e·cm, but this range is subject to uncertainties from higher-order effects in the heavy baryon expansion and from unknown short-distance counterterms that could be as large as 4×10⁻³² e·cm.

## Method

The author recalculates both one-loop and pole diagram contributions to the nucleon EDM using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBchPT) with SU(3) flavor symmetry, which preserves chiral power counting unlike earlier relativistic calculations. Low energy constants (LECs) are redetermined from updated experimental data on kaon decays, nonleptonic hyperon decays, and baryon electromagnetic transitions, with CP-violating phases extracted from recent theoretical analyses including operator mixing and renormalization group running. The one-loop diagrams involve pseudoscalar meson octet and ground-state (1/2)⁺ baryon octet intermediate states, while pole diagrams include (1/2)⁻ baryon resonances.

## Key numbers

- Long-distance SM nucleon EDM estimate: (1×10⁻³²–6×10⁻³²) e·cm
- One-loop neutron EDM magnitude: |dₙ¹⁻ˡᵒᵒᵖ| = 1.5×10⁻³² e·cm
- One-loop proton EDM from g₈hᵢ terms: |dₚ¹⁻ˡᵒᵒᵖ,¹| = 6.1×10⁻³³ e·cm
- One-loop proton EDM from hᵢhⱼ terms: |dₚ¹⁻ˡᵒᵒᵖ,²| = 1.1×10⁻³² e·cm
- Estimated short-distance counterterm size: dₚ⁰, dₙ⁰ ∼ 4×10⁻³² e·cm from naive dimensional analysis
- CP-violating phase φ from kaon decay: φ ≈ 1.89×10⁻⁴ ≈ 6.4J, where J = (2.96⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀.₁₆)×10⁻⁵ is the Jarlskog invariant
- Weak LECs: h_D ≈ 0.44×10⁻⁷ GeV, h_F ≈ −0.50×10⁻⁷ GeV, w_D ≈ −1.8×10⁻⁷ GeV, w_F ≈ 2.3×10⁻⁷ GeV
- Electromagnetic B–R coupling: e r_D = 0.033 GeV⁻¹, e r_F = −0.046 GeV⁻¹
- CP-phases from operator analysis: φ_D ≈ −1.5J, φ_F ≈ −3.6J; resonance phases φ̃_D ≈ 0.04J, φ̃_F ≈ −0.01J (negligible)

## Context

This work reexamines the long-distance SM nucleon EDM calculation originally performed by He et al. (1989), which used relativistic chiral perturbation theory with pseudoscalar coupling and suffered from broken chiral power counting and large uncertainties in the CKM phase and LECs. The author improves upon that calculation by using HBchPT to restore power counting, updating the Jarlskog invariant to its current precision, and incorporating modern determinations of weak LECs from Tandean (2002) and Borasoy (1999). The result provides a sharper SM benchmark for interpreting future neutron EDM experiments at PSI, ILL, Oak Ridge, TRIUMF, and Munich (targeting 10⁻²⁸ e·cm precision) and proton EDM storage ring experiments at COSY and BNL (targeting 10⁻²⁹ e·cm), enabling discrimination between SM and beyond-Standard-Model CP-violating signals.
