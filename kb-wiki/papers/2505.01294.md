# Deblurring fission fragment mass distributions

> Measurements of fission fragment mass distributions provide valuable insights into the properties of fissioning systems and the dynamics of the fission process. Pre-neutron emission distributions, essential for fission fragment evaporation codes like \cgmf{}, are extracted from distributions that are always measured after neutron emission, as the time scale of the emission of prompt fission neutrons is too short for direct measurement before the emission. However, obtaining accurate pre-neutron emission distributions requires methods that eliminate the effects of mass resolution and detector efficiency. We propose a deblurring technique based on the Richardson-Lucy (RL) algorithm, commonly used in optics for image restoration, to correct for these experimental effects. The RL algorithm uses the measured mass distributions and a transfer matrix to perform iterative deconvolution. The advantage of this method over others is that it does not assume any predefined shape such as a sum of Gaussians, as in \cgmf{}, for the distributions. In this paper, we apply the algorithm to the fission fragment mass distributions measured in the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf to extract pre-neutron emission fission fragment mass distributions. The results from deblurring are then used as inputs to \cgmf{}, and we compare the \cgmf{} results obtained using deblurring inputs with the default \cgmf{} results.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Richardson-Lucy (RL) deblurring algorithm to extract pre-neutron emission fission fragment mass distributions from post-neutron emission measurements, claiming the method does not assume any predefined shape (such as a sum of Gaussians) for the distributions, unlike the default parameterization used in the CGMF code.

## Method

The RL algorithm, originally developed for image restoration in optics, is applied iteratively to measured fission fragment mass distributions using a Gaussian-shaped transfer matrix that models the detector response and mass resolution effects. For the spontaneous fission of 252Cf, the algorithm is used both to remove mass resolution effects from measured pre-neutron distributions and to extract pre-neutron distributions directly from post-neutron emission measurements. The deblurred pre-neutron mass distributions are then used as inputs to the CGMF Monte Carlo Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay code, and the resulting prompt neutron and gamma-ray observables are compared with those obtained using the default three-Gaussian parameterization in CGMF.

## Context

This work extends prior applications of the Richardson-Lucy algorithm to nuclear physics problems, including restoration of decay energy spectra from invariant mass spectroscopy (PhysRevC.107.064315) and determination of source functions from two-particle correlations in heavy-ion collisions (Nzabahimana 2023). The method is applied to fission fragment mass distributions measured for 252Cf(sf) by Hambsch (1997), Romano, Meierbachtol (2015), and Knitter, and the results are compared with the default three-Gaussian parameterization used in the CGMF code developed at Los Alamos National Laboratory (Talou). The approach enables extraction of pre-neutron emission distributions without assuming a Gaussian shape, potentially improving inputs for fission fragment evaporation codes like CGMF, FREYA, BeoH (Lovell 2021), and GEF.
