# The Problem of Renormalization of Chiral Nuclear Forces

> Ever since quantum field theory was first applied to the derivation of nuclear forces in the mid-20th century, the renormalization of pion exchange with realistic couplings has presented a challenge. The implementation of effective field theories (EFTs) in the 1990s promised a solution to this problem but unexpected obstacles were encountered. The response of the nuclear community has been to focus on "chiral potentials" with regulators chosen to produce a good description of data. Meanwhile, a successful EFT without explicit pion exchange --- Pionless EFT --- has been formulated where renormalization is achieved order by order in a systematic expansion of low-energy nuclear observables. I describe how lessons from Pionless EFT are being applied to the construction of a properly renormalized Chiral EFT.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Weinberg's power counting for Chiral EFT nuclear potentials fails to produce properly renormalized amplitudes because attractive singular potentials (like one-pion exchange in certain partial waves) require additional contact interactions beyond those predicted by naive dimensional analysis, and that a consistent renormalization scheme can be constructed by applying lessons from Pionless EFT.

## Method

The author analyzes the renormalization of singular potentials in coordinate space by examining the radial Schrödinger equation with a sharp cutoff at short distances, matching the logarithmic derivative of the external wavefunction to a spherical well representing the contact interaction. The analysis is extended to the two-nucleon system by considering how reducible loops in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation introduce additional cutoff dependence that must be absorbed by low-energy constants, leading to a modified power counting where pion exchange is treated nonperturbatively only in low partial waves where it is sufficiently attractive.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic QCD scale is M_QCD ~ 1 GeV, which sets the breakdown scale of the EFT expansion
- The pion decay constant f_pi ~ 92 MeV appears in the estimate of the scale M_NN = 16 pi f_pi^2 / (g_A^2 m_N) = O(f_pi), where g_A ~ 1.27 is the axial-vector coupling
- The binding energy per nucleon from the LO potential scales as B_A/A ~ M_NN^2 / M_QCD ~ f_pi / (4 pi) ~ 10 MeV
- For a singular central potential V_L(r) = -alpha / (2 mu r^n) with n >= 3, the wavefunction at small distances behaves as psi_l(r) proportional to r^(n/4 - 1) cos( sqrt(alpha) r^(1-n/2) / (n/2 - 1) + phi_l ), where the phase phi_l is not fixed by the long-range potential
- For the n=2 case, the critical value separating regular from singular behavior is alpha_l = (l + 1/2)^2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of renormalization in nuclear effective field theories initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and addresses the failure of Weinberg's power counting identified by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) and Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005). The paper builds on the successful formulation of Pionless EFT (Bedaque, van Kolck 1997; van Kolck 1997, 1998) and applies its lessons to construct a properly renormalized Chiral EFT where subleading pion exchanges are treated perturbatively, following the approach of Long and van Kolck (2007). This enables a systematic expansion of low-energy nuclear observables with controlled regulator dependence.
