# Initial spin fluctuations as a probe of cluster spin structure in $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ nuclei

> We investigate the imprint of $α$ clustering on initial spin fluctuations in relativistic $^{16}\mathrm{O}+{}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+{}^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.36$~TeV. Utilizing \textit{ab initio} configurations from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and phenomenological $α$-cluster models within a Monte-Carlo Glauber framework, we compute the event-by-event variance of the initial net spin polarization. We find that the strong short-range spin--isospin correlations characteristic of $α$ clusters lead to a significant suppression of spin fluctuations compared to a spherical Woods--Saxon baseline with uncorrelated spins. By constructing a scaled fluctuation observable that accounts for trivial finite-size effects, we demonstrate that this suppression exhibits a non-monotonic centrality dependence sensitive to the detailed cluster geometry. Furthermore, we propose the ratio of scaled spin fluctuations between $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ systems as a robust probe. Our results predict distinct percent-level deviations from the baseline for clustered nuclei, suggesting that measurements of final-state $Λ$-hyperon spin correlations can provide novel constraints on the ground-state spin structure of light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the strong short-range spin-isospin correlations characteristic of alpha clusters in $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ produce a measurable suppression of initial spin fluctuations compared to a spherical Woods-Saxon baseline, and that this suppression exhibits a non-monotonic centrality dependence sensitive to the detailed cluster geometry, provided that the final-state $\Lambda$-hyperon spin correlations approximately preserve the initial polarization variance during hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method

The authors compute event-by-event initial spin fluctuations using a Monte-Carlo Glauber framework (the TRENTo model) with three types of nuclear-structure input for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$: (i) ab initio configurations from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) that preserve full position-spin correlations; (ii) phenomenological alpha-cluster models with tetrahedral ($^{16}\mathrm{O}$) and bowling-pin ($^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$) geometries enforcing strict spin-isospin singlet cancellation within each cluster; and (iii) a spherical three-parameter Fermi (3pf) Woods-Saxon baseline with only the global $J=0$ constraint and no additional spin-position correlations. The variance of the event-by-event net polarization $\langle \mathcal{P}^2 \rangle$ is evaluated for $^{16}\mathrm{O}+{}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+{}^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.36$ TeV, and a scaled fluctuation observable is constructed to remove trivial finite-size effects associated with the global $J=0$ constraint.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.36$ TeV for both $^{16}\mathrm{O}+{}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}+{}^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ systems
- Alpha-cluster edge length $\ell_c$ for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ tetrahedron: 3.17 fm (NLEFT reference), 3.06 fm (PGCM reference), 3.26 fm (VMC reference)
- Alpha-cluster RMS radius $r_L$ for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$: 1.84 fm (NLEFT), 1.88 fm (PGCM), 1.52 fm (VMC)
- Alpha-cluster parameters for $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ bowling-pin: $r_L=2.20$ fm, $\ell_c=3.00$ fm, $\ell_h=3.50$ fm (NLEFT reference)
- 3pf Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$: $R=2.608$ fm, $a=0.513$ fm, $w=-0.051$
- 3pf Woods-Saxon parameters for $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$: $R=2.791$ fm, $a=0.698$ fm, $w=-0.168$
- $^{16}\mathrm{O}+\alpha$ configuration for $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$: $d_1=3.0$ fm, $r_L=2.164$ fm
- The paper predicts percent-level deviations from the baseline for clustered nuclei in the ratio of scaled spin fluctuations between $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ systems

## Context

This work extends the framework of Giacalone and Speranza (2025), who proposed relating initial-state spin fluctuations to final-state $\Lambda$-pair angular correlations via relativistic spin hydrodynamics, by applying it for the first time to investigate alpha-cluster spin structures in light nuclei. The study builds on established alpha-cluster models for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{20}\mathrm{Ne}$ from low-energy nuclear physics (Hafstad 1938, Ikeda 1968, Freer 2018) and recent ab initio NLEFT calculations (Giacalone 2024, Summerfield 2021), as well as phenomenological cluster parameter sets from Mehrabpour (2025). The results enable future experimental measurements of $\Lambda$-hyperon spin correlations at the LHC to provide novel constraints on the ground-state spin structure of light nuclei, complementing existing probes of alpha clustering through anisotropic flow and eccentricity observables.
