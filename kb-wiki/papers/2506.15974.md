# Bound Deuteron-Antideuteron System (Deuteronium): Leading Radiative and Internal-Structure Corrections to Bound-State Energies

> We evaluate the energy levels of the deuteronium bound system, which consists of a deuteron and an antideuteron, with a special emphasis on states with nonvanishing orbital angular momenta. The excited atomic bound states of deuteronium constitute probes for the understanding of higher-order quantum electrodynamic corrections for spin-1 particles in a bound system where the typical field strength of the binding Coulomb field (at a distance of the generalized Bohr radius) exceeds Schwinger's critical field strength. For states with nonvanishing angular momenta, effects due to the internal structure of the deuteron and virtual annihilation contributions are highly suppressed. Relevant transitions are found to be in a frequency range accessible by standard laser spectroscopic techniques. We evaluate the leading and next-to-leading energy corrections of orders alpha^3 m_d and alpha^4 m_d , where alpha is the fine-structure constant and m_d is the deuteron mass, and also investigate internal-structure corrections: hadronic vacuum polarization, finite-size effects, and strong-interaction corrections.

## Key claim

The deuteronium bound system (deuteron-antideuteron) provides a testbed for quantum electrodynamic corrections for spin-1 particles in Coulomb fields exceeding the Schwinger critical field strength, with the leading and next-to-leading energy corrections evaluated up to order α⁴ m_d, though the paper emphasizes states with nonvanishing orbital angular momenta where internal-structure and annihilation effects are highly suppressed.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Breit Hamiltonian for two interacting spin-1 particles, derived from the single-photon exchange kernel, and compute its matrix elements in the basis of total spin quantum numbers S = 0, 1, 2 for states with principal quantum numbers n = 2, 3, 4 and orbital angular momenta L ≥ 1. They combine these with radiative corrections from one-loop and two-loop electron vacuum polarization (Uehling potential and its iterations), hadronic vacuum polarization, finite-size effects from the deuteron charge radius and quadrupole moment, and strong-interaction corrections, using perturbation theory up to third order where needed.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously developed for protonium (Ba1989, KlBaMaRi2002) to the deuteron-antideuteron system, which adds a neutron and its antiparticle to the constituent particles. The spin-1 Breit Hamiltonian used here builds on the general formalism for arbitrary-spin particles developed by Pa2007, Pa2008, and ZaPa2010, and the authors note that the strong-interaction shifts for S states are large and challenging to analyze, referencing their companion work AdJe2025trueman. The results enable experimental searches for deuteronium transitions using standard laser spectroscopic techniques, with potential production pathways at the AD/ELENA facility (Ca2024) and GBAR beamlines (BlOhCr2025).
