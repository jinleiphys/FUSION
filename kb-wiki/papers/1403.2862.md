# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for multi-nucleon transfer processes: Effects of particle evaporation on production cross sections

> We present a microscopic calculation of multi-nucleon transfer reactions employing the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. In our previous publication [Phys. Rev. C 88, 014614 (2013)], we reported our analysis for the multi-nucleon transfer processes for several systems. Here we discuss effects of particle evaporation processes on the production cross sections. Since particle evaporation processes may not be described adequately by the TDHF calculations, we evaluate them using a statistical model. As an input of the statistical model, excitation energies of the final fragments are necessary. We evaluate them from the TDHF wave function after collisions, extending the particle number projection technique. From the calculation, the particle evaporation effects are found to improve descriptions of the production cross sections. However, the production cross sections are still underestimated for processes where a number of protons are transferred. Possible origins of the discrepancy are discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of particle evaporation effects, evaluated via a statistical model using excitation energies extracted from the TDHF wave function, improves the description of production cross sections for multi-nucleon transfer reactions, but the calculations still underestimate measured cross sections for processes where a number of protons are transferred, indicating that correlation effects not included in TDHF may be significant.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations for the $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reaction at $E_\mathrm{lab}=$ 328.4 MeV, using the Skyrme SLy5 effective interaction. They extended the particle number projection technique to evaluate the excitation energy of each projectile-like fragment after collision, then used the statistical model of Dostrovsky et al. to simulate evaporation cascades of neutrons, protons, deuterons, tritons, $^3$He, and $\alpha$ particles from those excited fragments. The production cross sections including evaporation effects were computed by summing over all parent channels weighted by the evaporation probabilities.

## Key numbers

* For the $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reaction at $E_\mathrm{lab}=$ 328.4 MeV, the TDHF theory describes measured cross sections "surprisingly well" when the number of transferred nucleons is small, with no empirical parameter in the calculations.
* The TDHF wave function after collision is evaluated at a time scale of order $10^{-21}$ s after reseparation, while evaporation processes occur on a much longer time scale.
* The minimum impact parameter for the cross section integration was taken as the border dividing fusion and binary reactions, determined by the maximum impact parameter $b_\mathrm{f}$ for which fusion occurs.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TDHF analysis of multi-nucleon transfer for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reactions (Sekizawa and Yabana, 2013), where cross sections were found to shift towards larger neutron and proton number sides compared with measurements as the number of transferred nucleons increased. The particle number projection technique used to extract excitation energies was originally proposed by Simenel (2010) for calculating transfer probabilities. The statistical evaporation model follows the approach of Dostrovsky et al. (1959). The results enable future work to identify what correlation effects beyond TDHF (such as pair transfer or cluster transfer) are needed to fully describe multi-nucleon transfer cross sections.
