# Quantifying the breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory

> We use Bayesian statistics to infer the breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory in its standard power counting and with renormalization of observables carried out using the power-divergence subtraction scheme and cutoff regularization. We condition our inference on predictions of the total neutron-proton scattering cross section up next-to-next-to leading order. We quantify a median breakdown scale of approximately 1.4$m_π$. The 68% degree of belief interval is $[0.96,1.69]m_π$. This result confirms the canonical expectation that the pion mass is a relevant scale in low-energy nuclear physics.

# Quantifying the breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory

## Key claim
The median breakdown scale of pionless effective field theory (EFT) is approximately 1.4 times the pion mass (1.4 m_pi), with a 68% degree-of-belief interval of [0.96, 1.69] m_pi, confirming the canonical expectation that the pion mass sets the relevant scale for this EFT. The inference is conditioned on order-by-order predictions of the total neutron-proton scattering cross section up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) and is robust under variations of prior assumptions.

## Method
The authors used Bayesian statistics to infer the breakdown scale M_hi from order-by-order predictions of the total neutron-proton scattering cross section at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in pionless EFT. They employed the power-divergence subtraction (pds) scheme for regularization and renormalization, calibrated low-energy constants to reproduce effective range parameters from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis, and computed cross sections at three laboratory scattering energies (10, 40, and 70 MeV) corresponding to relative momenta of 68.5, 137.0, and 181.2 MeV. The likelihood was constructed from the EFT expansion coefficients under an assumption of independent and identically distributed natural coefficients with an inverse-chi-squared hyperprior.

## Key numbers
- Median breakdown scale M_hi = 1.4 m_pi (approximately 193 MeV) at N2LO, with 68% degree-of-belief interval [0.96, 1.69] m_pi (approximately [133, 233] MeV) and 95% interval [96, 303] MeV.
- At NLO, median M_hi = 1.1 m_pi (approximately 154 MeV), with 68% interval [61, 216] MeV and 95% interval [23, 363] MeV.
- Effective range parameters used for calibration: singlet scattering length a_s = -23.714 fm, singlet effective range r_s = 2.678 fm, triplet inverse binding momentum gamma^{-1} = 4.318946 fm, triplet effective range rho_t = 1.765 fm.
- Triplet scattering length a_t = 5.42 fm used in the soft-scale function.
- The N2LO posterior is robust for regularization cutoff values greater than or approximately equal to 1.5 fm^{-1} when using cutoff regularization.

## Context
This work extends Bayesian methods for quantifying EFT breakdown scales, previously applied by Melendez (2019) to other EFTs, to the specific case of pionless EFT. The result confirms the canonical expectation that the pion mass (approximately 138 MeV) is the relevant breakdown scale for an EFT that integrates out pion exchange, as originally described by Yukawa (1935). The analysis enables improved uncertainty quantification for pionless EFT calculations of low-energy nuclear observables and opens the door to Bayesian model mixing between pionless EFT and pionful EFT (Epelbaum 2008, Machleidt 2011, Hammer 2022), which could improve predictions for reactions such as proton-proton fusion. The authors note that a concurrent study by Bub (2024) investigated the breakdown scale of contact-only theories for nucleon-nucleon scattering using a non-standard ordering scheme, making results not directly comparable.
