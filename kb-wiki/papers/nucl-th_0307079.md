# Field gauge divergences in the light-front

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for ground state of two fermions exchanging a gauge boson presents divergences. We used a prescription that allowed an apropriate prescription of the singularity in the boson propagator.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the ground state of two fermions exchanging a gauge boson in the light-front formulation contains divergences in the transverse momentum, even in the ladder approximation; the authors propose a prescription to handle the singularity in the gauge boson propagator, but the paper only verifies the existence of the singularity and does not demonstrate that the prescription fully removes it.

## Method

The authors derive the light-front Green's function from the covariant propagator for scalar fields, fermion fields, and gauge bosons in the light-front gauge \(A^{+}=0\). They then compute the first-order perturbative correction to the two-body propagator for a fermion-antifermion system exchanging one virtual gauge boson, performing the Fourier transform from light-front time \(x^{+}\) to total momentum \(P^{-}\) and integrating over the internal \(k^{-}\) variables analytically.

## Key numbers

- The gauge boson propagator components in light-front gauge are given as \(S^{--}=4\frac{ik^{-}}{k^{+}(k^{2}+i\varepsilon)}\), \(S^{-\perp}=S^{\perp -}=2\frac{ik^{\perp}}{k^{+}(k^{2}+i\varepsilon)}\), and \(S^{\perp\perp}=-1\frac{i}{k^{2}+i\varepsilon}\) (Equation 7a).
- The interaction Lagrangian density is \(\mathcal{L}_{I}=g\overline{\Psi}_{1}\gamma_{\mu}A^{\mu}\Psi_{1}+g\overline{\Psi}_{2}\gamma_{\nu}A^{\nu}\Psi_{2}\) with coupling constant \(g\) and gauge boson mass \(\mu=0\).
- The singularity in the components \((--)\) and \((-\perp)\) occurs at the coordinate \(q^{+}=k^{+}-k^{\prime +}\) in the perturbative correction (Equation 10a).

## Context

This work extends the light-front dynamics approach originally proposed by Dirac (1949) and developed by Kogut and collaborators, addressing the specific problem of divergences in the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-fermion systems with gauge boson exchange. The authors reference their own prior work (Sales, Frederico, and Pimentel, 2002) on light-front variables and a technique using a displacement \(\delta^{+}\) of the pole in \(q^{+}\) (Sales, 1997) as a potential method to remove the singularities, though the present paper only verifies their existence. This enables future work on properly regularizing light-front gauge theories for bound-state calculations.
