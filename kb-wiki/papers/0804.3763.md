# Physics Revealed at Intermediate p_T

> A review is given on the subject of hadron production at intermediate $p_T$ in heavy-ion collisions. The underlying dynamical processes are inferred from interpreting the data in the framework of recombination. Ridge formation with or without triggers is found to play an important role in nearly all observables in that $p_T$ region. Correlation data would be hard to interpret without taking ridges into account. The semi-hard partons that create the ridges may even be able to drive elliptic flow without fast thermalization.

# Physics Revealed at Intermediate p_T

## Key claim
The recombination model provides a unified framework for understanding hadron production at intermediate p_T (2–6 GeV/c) in heavy-ion collisions, with ridge formation from semi-hard partons playing a crucial role in nearly all observables; however, the model leaves open the question about the nature of the partons that hadronize, and the ridge interpretation depends on the specific centrality, trigger momentum, and associated momentum ranges studied.

## Method
The paper reviews single-particle distributions (p_T spectra, baryon-to-meson ratios, elliptic flow v_2, forward production) and two-particle correlations (near-side ridge and away-side double-hump structures) in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV at RHIC, interpreting data within the recombination model (RM) where hadrons form from thermal (T) and shower (S) partons via TT, TS, SS, TTT, TTS, and SSS recombination. Ridge properties are extracted from triggered and untriggered correlation data, and a geometric model relates ridge yields to elliptic flow without requiring fast thermalization.

## Key numbers
- The intermediate p_T region is defined as 2 < p_T < 6 GeV/c for RHIC heavy-ion collisions at √s = 200 GeV.
- The baryon-to-meson ratio peaks at p_T ≈ 3 GeV/c with p/π ∼ 1 and Λ/K ∼ 2.
- For forward production at η ≈ 3.2 in Au+Au collisions at 62.4 GeV, the p/π⁺ ratio reaches as high as 10 for p_T < 1.2 GeV/c (preliminary BRAHMS result).
- The ridge yield for p_T^{assoc} > 2 GeV/c decreases with N_part and vanishes as N_part approaches the minimum corresponding to pp collisions.
- The ridge yield decreases only slightly for 0–10% centrality as p_T^{trig} increases from 3 to 9 GeV/c.
- The Λ/K⁰_s ratio in the ridge is around 1 at p_T^{assoc} ∼ 2 GeV/c.
- The p/π ratio in the ridge relative to the jet peak is very large (>2) for 3 < p_T^{trig} < 5 GeV/c.
- Relative ridge yields R/J for p and π are roughly 4.8 ± 0.8 and 2.2 ± 0.3, respectively, at p_T^{trig} ∼ 3.5 GeV/c and p_T^{assoc} > 2 GeV/c.
- The Ω p_T spectrum shows exponential behavior up to 5.5 GeV/c.
- The B/M ratio of associated particles increases with p_T^{assoc} to about 0.2 at p_T^{assoc} ≈ 1.8 GeV/c for 2.5 < p_T^{trig} < 4.0 GeV/c.

## Context
This review extends the recombination/coalescence model (ReCo) approach developed by several groups (Greco et al., Fries et al., Hwa and Yang) to interpret RHIC data, contrasting with hydrodynamical studies (Kolb and Heinz, PHOBOS) that work at lower p_T and pQCD approaches (Wang and Gyulassy, Muller and Gupta) that apply at higher p_T. The ridge phenomenology builds on the work of Putschke (QM06) and STAR collaboration data, and the geometric model for elliptic flow from ridges (Hwa) offers an alternative to the fast-thermalization hydro picture. The paper enables predictions for LHC where semi-hard parton densities may produce qualitatively new recombination phenomena not seen at RHIC.
