# Femtoscopy of stopped protons

> The longitudinal proton-proton femtoscopy (HBT) correlation function, based on the idea that in a heavy ion collision at $\sqrt{s} \lesssim 20$ GeV stopped protons are likely to be separated in configuration space, is evaluated. It shows a characteristic oscillation which appears sufficiently pronounced to be accessible in experiment. The proposed measurement is essential for estimating the baryon density in the central rapidity region, and can be also viewed as an (almost) direct verification of the Lorentz contraction of the fast-moving nucleus.

## Key claim

The longitudinal proton-proton femtoscopy correlation function for stopped protons in heavy ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s} \lesssim 20\) GeV shows characteristic oscillations that are sufficiently pronounced to be accessible in experiment, provided that the stopped protons are indeed separated in configuration space as predicted by a simple string model with linear energy loss.

## Method

The authors model the source function for stopped protons using a Gaussian distribution of nucleon collision points in the longitudinal direction, combined with a linear energy-loss model (string tension \(\sigma = 3\) GeV/fm) to compute the spatial and temporal shifts \(\Delta z\) and \(\Delta t\) between the collision point and the point where a nucleon reaches its final rapidity. Fermi motion of nucleons inside the nuclei is included by smearing the initial momentum distribution with a Gaussian width \(\Gamma_F = \gamma \cdot 165\) MeV. The femtoscopy correlation function \(C(\delta q_z; \delta q_0)\) is then computed from the Fourier transform of the source distribution, and final-state strong and Coulomb interactions are added using an existing code.

## Key numbers

- The string tension used for fully stopped nucleons (three wounded quarks) is \(\sigma = 3\) GeV/fm.
- The Fermi-motion smearing width is \(\Gamma_F = \gamma \sqrt{2/5} \, k_F \simeq \gamma \cdot 165\) MeV, where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum.
- The transverse mass of the final proton is taken as \(M_\perp = 1.2\) GeV; results are not sensitive to this value.
- The nuclear radius used is \(R = 7\) fm.
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 20\) GeV, the oscillations in the correlation function are clearly visible; at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) GeV the signal is much weaker.
- The anti-proton to proton ratio at \(\sqrt{s} \simeq 20\) GeV is \(\bar{p}/p \simeq 0.1\), justifying the neglect of produced protons.

## Context

This work extends the observation of Bialas (2016) that stopped nucleons in heavy ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s} \gtrsim 10\) GeV may not overlap significantly in configuration space, based on a simple string model. The proposed longitudinal femtoscopy measurement would provide an experimental test of that idea and, if successful, allow estimation of the baryon density in the central rapidity region. It also offers an almost direct verification of Lorentz contraction of fast-moving nuclei, as discussed in the context of recent work by Rafelski (2017). The measurement would constrain the mechanism of baryon number transport to mid-rapidity, which is essential for exploring the QCD phase diagram at large baryon density.
