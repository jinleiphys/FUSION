# Friedel Oscillations in Relativistic Nuclear Matter

> We calculate the low-momentum N-N effective potential obtained in the OBE approximation, inside a nuclear plasma at finite temperature, as described by the relativistic $ σ$-$ ω$ model. We analyze the screening effects on the attractive part of the potential in the intermediate range as density or temperature increase. In the long range the potential shows Friedel-like oscillations instead of the usual exponential damping. These oscillations arise from the sharp edge of the Fermi surface and should be encountered in any realistic model of nuclear matter.

# Friedel Oscillations in Relativistic Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The long-range behavior of the N-N effective potential in relativistic nuclear matter shows Friedel-like oscillations instead of the usual exponential damping, provided the matter polarization contributions are fully included in the meson propagators; these oscillations arise from the sharp edge of the Fermi surface and should be present in any realistic model of nuclear matter, but they were not found in earlier work by Durso, Kim, and Wambach (1993) because that study omitted the complete effects of matter polarizations.

## Method

The authors calculated the low-momentum N-N effective potential in the one-boson exchange (OBE) approximation using a relativistic sigma-omega model solved in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) for symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. They derived the dressed meson propagators from the linear response of the system around the Hartree equilibrium, including scalar, vector, and mixing polarization tensors with renormalized vacuum contributions, and introduced monopolar form factors to remove spurious tachyonic poles. The non-relativistic potential in coordinate space was obtained by expanding the OBE amplitude in nucleon momenta to second order, applying the Blankenbecler-Sugar prescription, and Fourier transforming in the static (k^0 = 0) limit.

## Key numbers

- Saturation occurs at Fermi momentum P_f0 = 1.42 fm^{-1} with binding energy E_b = -15.46 MeV, using coupling constants g_sigma^2 = 183.3 (mu_sigma/m)^2 and g_omega^2 = 114.7 (mu_omega/m)^2.
- The meson masses used are mu_sigma = 550 MeV, mu_omega = 783 MeV, and nucleon mass m = 939 MeV.
- For the potential calculation, the coupling constants are g_sigma^2 = 8.7171 and g_omega^2 = 25, with cut-off parameters Lambda_sigma = 2.0 GeV and Lambda_omega = 1.4 GeV.
- At saturation density and T = 0, the first oscillation in the central component reaches an amplitude of 2 MeV at a distance of around 2 fm.
- At 2.4 times saturation density (P_f/m = 0.4), the first oscillation in the central component has amplitude ~8 MeV at r_max ~ 1.5 fm, while the spin-spin component reaches 10 MeV at the same distance.
- The position of the first oscillation maximum satisfies P_f * r_max ~ pi, and the ratio r_max/d ~ 1.28, where d is the mean interparticle distance.
- The potential well in the central component disappears for temperatures beyond 40 MeV.
- Friedel oscillations disappear for temperatures between 40-80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of screening effects on the N-N interaction in relativistic nuclear matter, building on the relativistic sigma-omega model framework of Serot and Walecka (1986) and the relativistic Hartree approximation of Chin (1977). It contradicts the findings of Durso, Kim, and Wambach (1993), who did not observe Friedel oscillations in a 2-pi-exchange calculation because they did not include the complete matter polarization effects in the meson propagators. The results show that the long-range oscillatory behavior is a universal feature of degenerate Fermi systems, similar to that found in non-relativistic electromagnetic plasmas by Rensik (1968), relativistic electromagnetic plasmas by Sivak (1985), and QCD plasmas by Kapusta and Toimela (1988), and it enables future studies of collective phenomena in dense nuclear matter at finite temperature.
