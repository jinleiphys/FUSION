# Quark Hadron Phase Transition and Hybrid Stars

> We investigate the properties of hybrid stars consisting of quark matter in the core and hadron matter in outer region. The hadronic and quark matter equations of state are calculated by using nonlinear Walecka model and chiral colour dielectric (CCD) model respectively. We find that the phase transition from hadron to quark matter is possible in a narrow range of the parameters of nonlinear Walecka and CCD models. The transition is strong or weak first order depending on the parameters used. The EOS thus obtained, is used to study the properties of hybrid stars. We find that the calculated hybrid star properties are similar to those of pure neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a hadron-to-quark matter phase transition in hybrid stars is possible only for a narrow range of parameters in the nonlinear Walecka model and the chiral colour dielectric model (CCDM), and that the resulting hybrid star properties are similar to those of pure neutron stars, making observational distinction difficult.

## Method

The hadronic equation of state (EOS) was computed using the nonlinear Walecka model in mean-field theory, including nucleons, Λ and Σ⁻ hyperons, electrons, and muons, with meson-hyperon couplings varied across three choices from the literature. The quark matter EOS was computed using the chiral colour dielectric model (CCDM) with parameters fitted to baryon spectroscopy, and the phase transition was determined by applying Gibbs' criterion (equal pressure at equal chemical potential) at zero temperature. The resulting composite EOS was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for non-rotating hybrid star structure.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear compressibility was varied between 250 MeV and 350 MeV.
- Saturation properties used to fix hadronic parameters: binding energy per nucleon = −16 MeV, baryon density = 0.15 fm⁻³, symmetry energy coefficient = 32.5 MeV, Landau mass = 0.83 m_N.
- Latent heat of the phase transition reduced from 154 MeV/fm³ to 96 MeV/fm³ when compressibility was increased from 250 MeV to 300 MeV.
- For CCDM, a representative parameter set used: B^{1/4} = 152.1 MeV, m_q(u,d) = 91.6 MeV, m_q(s) = 294.9 MeV, α = 36, α_s = 0.08.
- For B^{1/4} ≤ 140 MeV, the transition density fell below nuclear matter density (unphysical).
- Maximum gravitational masses for pure neutron stars: 1.86 M_⊙ to 1.60 M_⊙; radii: 10.69 km to 11.00 km.
- Maximum gravitational masses for hybrid stars: 1.47 M_⊙ to 1.49 M_⊙; radii: 12.36 km to 12.0 km.
- Minimal Keplarian rotation periods: 0.66 ms to 0.75 ms for neutron stars; 0.93 ms to 0.89 ms for hybrid stars.
- Observed mass of PSR1913+16: M/M_⊙ = 1.44 ± 0.003.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutron star structure (Tolman, Oppenheimer, and Volkoff, 1939) and later improved models using different nuclear EOS (references 2–3a). It specifically builds on the nonlinear Walecka model for hadronic matter (references 5, 5a, 5b) and the chiral colour dielectric model for quark matter (reference 7), contrasting with the more common bag model approach. The results enable future studies of the mechanism and rate of strangeness production during the hadron-to-quark phase transition, particularly following the suggestion by Alcock, Farhi, and Olinto (reference 13) that pre-existing strangeness in the hadronic phase could accelerate conversion to quark matter.
