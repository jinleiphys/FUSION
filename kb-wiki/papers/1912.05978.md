# $^{78}$Ni revealed as a doubly magic stronghold against nuclear deformation

> Nuclear magic numbers, which emerge from the strong nuclear force based on quantum chromodynamics, correspond to fully occupied energy shells of protons, or neutrons inside atomic nuclei. Doubly magic nuclei, with magic numbers for both protons and neutrons, are spherical and extremely rare across the nuclear landscape. While the sequence of magic numbers is well established for stable nuclei, evidence reveals modifications for nuclei with a large proton-to-neutron asymmetry. Here, we provide the first spectroscopic study of the doubly magic nucleus $^{78}$Ni, fourteen neutrons beyond the last stable nickel isotope. We provide direct evidence for its doubly magic nature, which is also predicted by ab initio calculations based on chiral effective field theory interactions and the quasi-particle random-phase approximation. However, our results also provide the first indication of the breakdown of the neutron magic number 50 and proton magic number 28 beyond this stronghold, caused by a competing deformed structure. State-of-the-art phenomenological shell-model calculations reproduce this shape coexistence, predicting further a rapid transition from spherical to deformed ground states with $^{78}$Ni as turning point.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first direct spectroscopic evidence that ⁷⁸Ni is a doubly magic nucleus, but also finds the first indication that the neutron magic number 50 and proton magic number 28 break down beyond this nucleus, with a competing deformed structure emerging that may lead to a rapid transition from spherical to deformed ground states in heavier isotopes.

## Method

The experiment used in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy following proton removal reactions at the RIBF facility. A 345 MeV/u ²³⁸U primary beam was fissioned on a beryllium target to produce ⁷⁹Cu and ⁸⁰Zn beams, which then underwent (p,2p) and (p,3p) reactions in a liquid hydrogen target within the MINOS system. Prompt de-excitation gamma rays were detected with the DALI2 spectrometer, and reaction products were identified with the ZeroDegree spectrometer.

## Key numbers

- The first 2⁺ state in ⁷⁸Ni was observed at 2,600(33) keV with a significance level of 7.5
- The 4⁺ state was tentatively assigned at 583(10) keV above the 2⁺ state, giving an E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) ratio of 1.22
- A second 2⁺ state was observed at 2,910(43) keV with a significance level of 3.5
- Inclusive cross sections for producing ⁷⁸Ni were 1.70(42) mbarn for ⁷⁹Cu(p,2p) and 0.016(6) mbarn for ⁸⁰Zn(p,3p)
- The neutron separation energy Sₙ of ⁷⁸Ni is 5.16(78) MeV
- The 2.6 MeV excitation energy of ⁷⁸Ni is comparable to the 2.7 MeV for doubly magic ⁵⁶Ni

## Context

This work extends previous indirect measurements that suggested persistent N=50 and Z=28 shell closures at ⁷⁸Ni (Walle 2007, Hakala 2008, Mazzocchi 2000, Hosmer 2005, Xu 2014, Olivier 2017, Sahin 2017, Welker 2017), and ab initio predictions by Hagen (2016). It contradicts studies of ⁶⁶Cr and ⁷⁰,⁷²Fe that questioned the N=50 shell closure for proton numbers below Z=28 (Santamaria 2015, Nowacki 2016). The results enable future measurements in ⁷⁶Fe and ⁷⁴Cr to verify the predicted shape coexistence, and motivate mass measurements to validate the predicted collapse of traditional shell closures relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis.
