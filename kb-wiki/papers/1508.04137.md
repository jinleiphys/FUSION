# Hard Matching for Boosted Tops at Two Loops

> Cross sections for top quarks provide very interesting physics opportunities, being both sensitive to new physics and also perturbatively tractable due to the large top quark mass. Rigorous factorization theorems for top cross sections can be derived in several kinematic scenarios, including the boosted regime in the peak region that we consider here. In the context of the corresponding factorization theorem for $e^+e^-$ collisions we extract the last missing ingredient that is needed to evaluate the cross section differential in the jet-mass at two-loop order, namely the matching coefficient at the scale $μ\simeq m_t$. Our extraction also yields the final ingredients needed to carry out logarithmic resummation at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic order (or N$^3$LL if we ignore the missing 4-loop cusp anomalous dimension). This coefficient exhibits an amplitude level rapidity logarithm starting at $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$ due to virtual top quark loops, which we treat using rapidity renormalization group (RG) evolution. Interestingly, this rapidity RG evolution appears in the matching coefficient between two effective theories around the heavy quark mass scale $μ\simeq m_t$.

## Key claim
The authors extract the two-loop matching coefficient \(H_m\) at the scale \(\mu \simeq m_t\) for boosted top quark production in \(e^+e^-\) collisions, which is the last missing ingredient needed to evaluate the jet-mass-differential cross section at two-loop order and to perform logarithmic resummation at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) order (or N\(^3\)LL if the four-loop cusp anomalous dimension is ignored). The coefficient contains an amplitude-level rapidity logarithm starting at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) from virtual top quark loops, which is treated using rapidity renormalization group (RG) evolution.

## Method
The matching coefficient \(C_m\) (and hence \(H_m = |C_m|^2\)) was extracted using two complementary methods. The indirect method used the known two-loop QCD heavy quark form factor from Bernreuther (2004) and Gluza (2009), the known SCET hard coefficient \(C_Q\), and the bHQET renormalization factor \(Z_{\rm bHQET}\) determined by RG consistency, via the relation \(C_m = \langle \mathcal{J}_{\rm QCD} \rangle / (Z_{\rm bHQET} C_Q)\). The direct method computed the SCET and bHQET form factors for the \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2 C_F T_F)\) color structure using a gluon-mass IR regulator and dispersion integrals, factorizing the amplitudes into collinear and soft components each involving a single rapidity scale.

## Key numbers
- The top quark mass from Tevatron and LHC combined analysis is \(m_t = 173.34 \pm 0.76\) GeV (ATLAS 2014).
- The hard matching coefficient \(H_m\) is computed at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\), with the rapidity logarithm appearing as \(\alpha_s^2 C_F T_F \ln(Q^2/m^2) \ln^{0,1,2}(\mu^2/m^2)\).
- The SCET current anomalous dimension \(\gamma_{\rm SCET}^{(n_l+1)}\) is given to two loops, with coefficients involving \(L_Q = \ln[(-Q^2-i0)/\mu^2]\) and numerical constants such as \(-\frac{268}{9} + \frac{4\pi^2}{3}\) for the \(C_F C_A\) term.
- The bHQET anomalous dimension \(\gamma_{\rm bHQET}\) is given to two loops, with the \(n_l C_F T_F\) term proportional to \(\frac{80}{9}L - \frac{80}{9}\) where \(L = \ln[(-Q^2-i0)/m^2]\).

## Context
This work extends the boosted top jet analysis of Fleming (2007) and Fleming (2008), which was carried out at NLL accuracy with \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) ingredients, to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) fixed-order matching. It provides the final missing ingredient for NNLL (or N\(^3\)LL) resummation of the double-differential cross section in \(e^+e^-\) collisions, complementing known \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) results for the heavy-quark jet function from Jain (2008), the massless soft function from Kelley (2011), Hornig (2011), and Monni (2011), and form factor calculations from Matsuura (1987, 1988). The result enables precision top mass extractions using low-scale short-distance mass schemes such as the jet mass scheme or MSR mass scheme.
