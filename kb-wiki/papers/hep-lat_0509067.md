# Baryon magnetic moments in the background field method

> We present a calculation of the magnetic moments for the baryon octet and decuplet using the background-field method and standard Wilson gauge and fermion actions in the quenched approximation of lattice QCD. Progressively smaller static magnetic fields are introduced on a $24^4$ lattice at beta=6.0 and the pion mass is probed down to about 500 MeV. Magnetic moments are extracted from the linear response of the masses to the background field.

# Baryon magnetic moments in the background field method

## Key claim
The background field method on the lattice can extract baryon magnetic moments from two-point functions alone, avoiding the momentum extrapolation required by the form factor method, but the results are obtained in the quenched approximation with pion masses down to about 500 MeV, which is too large for reliable chiral extrapolation to the physical point.

## Method
The authors introduce a static uniform magnetic field on a 24^4 lattice by modifying the gauge links with a phase factor, abandoning the usual quantization condition to work with smaller fields that produce mass shifts of only 1-5% of the proton mass. They compute baryon masses from two-point correlation functions at five nonzero field values (plus their reversals) and one zero-field baseline, then extract magnetic moments from the linear term in the mass shift as a function of field. The calculation uses standard Wilson gauge and fermion actions in the quenched approximation at beta=6.0, with six kappa values corresponding to pion masses from 1015 MeV down to 522 MeV, and analyzes 87 configurations.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 24^4 at beta=6.0, with lattice spacing a=0.1 fm (implied from 1/a=2 GeV)
- Pion masses probed: 1015, 908, 794, 732, 667, and 522 MeV
- Five dimensionless field values eta = qBa^2 = +0.00036, -0.00072, +0.00144, -0.00288, +0.00576
- Proton magnetic moment extrapolated to physical point: 3.04(6) mu_N from linear chiral fit, 3.78(5) from quadratic fit, 4.21(5) from Pade form
- Neutron magnetic moment extrapolated to physical point: -1.84(3) mu_N from linear chiral fit, -2.03(2) from quadratic fit, -1.84(2) from Pade form
- Critical kappa value: kappa_c = 0.1571
- Strange quark mass set at kappa = 0.1535

## Context
This work extends the background field method previously applied by the same group to electric and magnetic polarizabilities (Lee et al., 2004; Zhou et al., 2002, 2004) and contrasts with the form factor approach used by many groups (Leinweber, 1993; Wilcox, 1992; Ji, 2002; Horsley, 2003; Zanotti, 2004; Cloet, 2004). The results show opposite curvatures for proton and Delta+ magnetic moments as a function of pion mass, consistent with quenched chiral physics observed by Zanotti (2003) and Young (2003) using the form factor method. The calculation enables future dynamical (full-QCD) background field calculations and highlights the need for smaller pion masses and larger lattices to control systematic errors from finite-volume effects and chiral extrapolation.
