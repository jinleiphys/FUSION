# Revealing Dark Matter's Role in Neutron Stars Anisotropy: A Bayesian Approach Using Multi-messenger Observations

> Dark matter (DM) continues to evade direct detection, but neutron stars (NSs) serve as natural laboratories where even a modest DM component can alter their structure. While many studies have examined DM effects on NSs, they often rely on specific choices of equations of state (EOS) models, assume isotropy, and lack a Bayesian statistical framework, limiting their predictive power. In this work, we present a Bayesian framework that couples pressure-anisotropic nuclear EOS to a self-interacting fermionic DM component, constrained by NICER and GW170817 data. Our results show that DM mass fractions up to $\sim10\%$ remain consistent with current data, which softens the high-density EOS, leading to reduced stellar radii and tidal deformabilities while requiring negligible pressure anisotropy. Bayesian model comparison reveals no statistically significant preference between pure baryonic and DM-admixed NSs, indicating that DM inclusion enhances physical realism without complexity penalties. However, existing data cannot tightly constrain the DM parameters, and our empirical radius definition introduces a systematic bias toward the DM core configurations. To address this, we therefore introduce the DM radius span $ΔR_χ\equiv R_{χ,\mathrm{max}} - R_{χ,\mathrm{min}}$ as a unified diagnostic for DM distributions. This parameter simultaneously characterizes core-halo transition features while exhibiting strong linear correlations ($ΔR_χ< 4\,\mathrm{km}$) with both DM and BM parameters, providing a clear avenue for future constraints. Our approach bridges current limitations and future potential in probing DM through compact star observations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dark matter mass fractions up to ~10% remain consistent with current NICER and GW170817 data, but existing observations cannot tightly constrain the dark matter parameters, and Bayesian model comparison reveals no statistically significant preference between pure baryonic neutron stars and those containing dark matter.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bayesian framework coupling pressure-anisotropic nuclear equations of state (using three representative models: Skyrme BSk22, relativistic DBHF MPA1, and microscopic variational AP3) to a self-interacting fermionic asymmetric dark matter component described by a Yukawa interaction. They solved the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with Bowers-Liang pressure anisotropy in the baryonic sector only, then performed nested-sampling Bayesian inference using mass-radius posteriors from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715, together with tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter mass fractions up to ~10% remain consistent with current multimessenger data
- The three baryonic equations of state support maximum masses of 2.48 solar masses (MPA1), 2.37 solar masses (AP3), and 2.26 solar masses (BSk22)
- The dark matter radius span Delta R_chi is defined as R_chi,max minus R_chi,min and exhibits strong linear correlations with Delta R_chi less than 4 km for both dark matter and baryonic parameters
- Dark matter fermion mass prior range: 10^2 MeV to 10^4 MeV
- Yukawa coupling prior range: 10^{-6} to 10^{-3.5}
- Anisotropy parameter alpha prior range: -2 to 2
- Mediator mass fixed at 10^{-3} MeV
- Central density prior range: 10^{9.1} MeV^4 to max(rho_c,max, 10^{10.8} MeV^4)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars by simultaneously incorporating anisotropic baryonic matter effects and consistent dark matter-baryonic matter interactions within a unified Bayesian statistical framework, addressing limitations of earlier fixed-equation-of-state or isotropic approaches such as those by Collier et al. (2022) and Mahapatra et al. (2024). The framework enables future constraints from next-generation x-ray missions and gravitational-wave detectors by introducing the dark matter radius span as a diagnostic parameter that characterizes core-halo transition features.
