# The reaction $πN{\to}ωN$ revisited: the $ω{-}N$ scattering length

> We reinvestigate the experimental data on $ω$--meson production in pion-nucleon collisions based on both an analytical approach and a Monte Carlo simulation. Our analysis allows us to study the kinematical peculiarities of the $π^-p{\to}ω{n}$ reaction at energies close to threshold. Based on the hypothesis, that the formalism derived in ref. [1] was applied improperly, the unusually strong energy dependence claimed for the near--threshold cross section is identified as a purely kinematical effect. Based on this hypothesis, we deduce an effective ${π^-}p{\to}ωn$ cross section that is larger by an order of magnitude compared to the one commonly used. In addition we extract a lower limit for the imaginary part $\Im a_{ωN}$ of the $ωN$ scattering length. We find a value of $\Im a_{ωN}{=}0.24{\pm}0.05$ fm, which is significantly larger than that presently found in the literature.

## Key claim

The unusually strong energy dependence of the near-threshold cross section for the reaction π⁻p → ωn, previously interpreted as a physical suppression of the matrix element, is identified as a purely kinematical effect arising from an improper application of the formalism derived by Binnie et al. The authors argue that the experimental data were likely analyzed using an incorrect Jacobian, and under this hypothesis the corrected cross section is larger by an order of magnitude than the commonly used value.

## Method

The authors reinvestigate the experimental data on ω-meson production in pion-nucleon collisions from Binnie, Keyne, and Karami using both an analytical derivation of the differential cross section formula and a Monte Carlo event-by-event simulation. They derive the relation between the measured cross section σ_exp and the squared matrix element |M|², accounting for the finite width of the ω-meson and the detector resolution ΔP in the neutron momentum. The analysis compares two possible interpretations of the Jacobian used in the original data analysis to determine whether the strong energy dependence is physical or kinematic.

## Key numbers

- The imaginary part of the ωN scattering length is extracted as Im a_ωN = 0.24 ± 0.05 fm, which is significantly larger than values previously found in the literature.
- The measured cross sections from Karami et al. range from 197 ± 18 μb at P* = 50 MeV/c to 1560 ± 83 μb at P* = 190 MeV/c, with bin widths ΔP = 20 MeV/c.
- The reduced cross section σ_exp/P* ranges from 3.94 ± 0.36 μb/(MeV/c) at P* = 50 MeV/c to 8.21 ± 0.44 μb/(MeV/c) at P* = 190 MeV/c.
- The squared matrix element |M|² extracted from the Landolt-Börnstein compilation ranges from 182 ± 17 at q_n = 62 MeV/c to 504 ± 27 at q_n = 193 MeV/c (units not specified in text).
- The critical condition for two-body behavior is given by (2 P* ΔP)/(μ Γ) >> 1; using ΔP = 20 MeV, deviations from two-body behavior are expected for P* << 90 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the critique by Hanhart et al. (2001) that the experiments by Binnie (1970), Keyne (1973), and Karami (1979) on π⁻p → ωn were misinterpreted. The corrected cross section is relevant for identifying baryonic resonances predicted by Capstick and Isgur that couple to the ωN channel, for evaluating in-medium ω-meson properties via the tρ approximation used by Friman, Lutz, and Klingl, and for estimating the feasibility of ω-mesonic nuclei as proposed by Tsushima and Yamazaki. The authors strongly call for a remeasurement of the reaction πN → ωN close to threshold to resolve the ambiguity.
