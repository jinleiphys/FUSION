# Nuclear structure studies with the 7Li(e,e'p) reaction

> Experimental momentum distributions for the transitions to the ground state and first excited state of 6He have been measured via the reaction 7Li(e,e'p)6He, in the missing momentum range from -70 to 260 MeV/c. They are compared to theoretical distributions calculated with mean-field wave functions and with variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions which include strong state-dependent correlations in both 7Li and 6He. These VMC calculations provide a parameter-free prediction of the momentum distribution that reproduces the measured data, including its normalization. The deduced summed spectroscopic factor for the two transitions is 0.58 +/- 0.05, in perfect agreement with the VMC value of 0.60. This is the first successful comparison of experiment and ab initio theory for spectroscopic factors in p-shell nuclei.

## Key claim
The summed spectroscopic factor for proton knockout from 7Li to the ground and first excited states of 6He is 0.58 ± 0.05, which agrees with the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) prediction of 0.60; this is the first successful comparison of experiment and ab initio theory for spectroscopic factors in p-shell nuclei, but the agreement depends on including strong state-dependent correlations in the nuclear wave functions.

## Method
The reaction 7Li(e,e'p)6He was measured at NIKHEF using a 1% duty factor electron beam with incident energies of 329.7 and 454.7 MeV, covering a missing momentum range from -70 to 260 MeV/c. Experimental momentum distributions were extracted for the ground state (0+, missing energy 9.97 MeV) and first excited state (2+, missing energy 11.77 MeV) of 6He, and compared to Coulomb Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (CDWIA) calculations using mean-field (Woods-Saxon) wave functions and VMC wave functions from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon plus Urbana IX three-nucleon potential (AV18/UIX model).

## Key numbers
- Summed spectroscopic factor for the two transitions: 0.58 ± 0.05 (experimental), compared to VMC value of 0.60
- Ground state spectroscopic factor: 0.42 ± 0.04 (experimental), 0.41 (VMC)
- First excited state spectroscopic factor: 0.16 ± 0.02 (experimental), 0.19 (VMC with 1p+1f components)
- Ground state rms radius of overlap wave function: 3.17 ± 0.06 fm (experimental), 3.16 fm (VMC)
- First excited state rms radius: 3.47 ± 0.09 fm (experimental), 3.16 fm (VMC with 1p+1f components)
- Missing energy resolution: 180 keV (FWHM)
- Missing momentum range: -70 to 260 MeV/c
- Outgoing proton energy: 90 MeV
- Target thickness: roughly 25 mg/cm^2

## Context
This work extends earlier electron scattering studies of shell occupancies in 30Si, 31P, and 32S by Lapikas and Wesseling (1992, 1997), and provides the first experimental test of VMC calculations for spectroscopic factors in p-shell nuclei. The observed reduction of single-particle strength to about 58% of the mean-field value is consistent with the quenching found in many other complex nuclei (Lapikas 1993), and the VMC calculations using bare nucleon-nucleon interactions now reproduce this reduction without free parameters, unlike earlier Green's function calculations for 16O (Geurts 1996) which overpredicted the strength. The results enable future tests of the VMC wave functions at higher missing momenta, where 1f components would become visible.
