# Explaining the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly

> The determinations of the Cabibbo angle from kaon, pion, $D$ and tau decays disagree with the one from super-allowed beta decays. The resulting $\approx 3\,σ$ deficit in first row (but also first column) CKM unitarity is known as the Cabibbo angle anomaly (CAA). Determining $V_{ud}$ from beta decays requires knowledge of the Fermi constant $G_F$, usually extracted from muon decay. However, because new physics might also affect muon decay, and thus the determination of the Fermi constant, an interesting interplay with the global electroweak fit arises where $G_F$ is a crucial input. In these proceedings we review the CAA and its different interpretations in terms of physics beyond the SM, first discussing the corresponding effect in the effective field theory and then using (simplified) models.

## Key claim

The Cabibbo angle anomaly (CAA) is an approximately 3σ deficit in first-row CKM unitarity, but its significance depends on the radiative corrections applied to beta decays and on the treatment of tensions between different kaon decay modes; the anomaly could originate from new physics in beta decays, muon decay, or both, and explaining it solely via muon decay increases tension with the global electroweak fit.

## Method

The author reviews the CAA by comparing determinations of the Cabibbo angle from super-allowed beta decays, kaon decays, pion decays, tau decays, and D decays. The analysis uses the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) at dimension-six to classify operators that affect either beta decays (d→u eν) or muon decay (μ→eνν), and then examines simplified new physics models (W' boson, vector-like leptons, vector-like quarks, singly charged scalar, Z' boson, leptoquarks) that can generate those operators.

## Key numbers

- First-row CKM unitarity: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 0.9985(5), a ≈3σ deficit from unity
- First-column CKM unitarity: |V_ud|^2 + |V_cd|^2 + |V_td|^2 = 0.9970(18)
- Fermi constant from muon decay: G_F^μ = 1.1663787(6) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} (0.5 ppm precision)
- Fermi constant from global electroweak fit: G_F^EW|_full = 1.16716(39) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} (≈2σ above G_F^μ)
- Fermi constant required to restore CKM unitarity: G_F^CKM = 0.99925(25) × G_F^μ = 1.16550(29) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} (≈3σ below G_F^μ)
- Tension between G_F^EW and G_F^CKM: 3.4σ
- Preferred Wilson coefficient for operator Q_ℓq^(3)1111: C_ℓq^(3)1111 ≈ 0.7 × 10^{-3} G_F
- Preferred Wilson coefficient for operator Q_ϕq^(3)11: C_ϕq^(3)11 ≈ 0.7 × 10^{-3} G_F
- Preferred Wilson coefficient for operator Q_ℓℓ^(2112): C_ℓℓ^(2112) ≈ -1.4 × 10^{-3} G_F
- Constraint on Q_ℓℓ^(1221) from LEP: -4π/(9.8 TeV)^2 < C_ℓℓ^(1221) < 4π/(12.2 TeV)^2
- Constraint on Q_ℓℓ^(3312) from μ→e conversion: |C_ℓℓ^(3312)| < 6.4 × 10^{-5} G_F
- Constraint on Q_ℓℓ^(2212) from μ→e conversion: |C_ℓℓ^(2212)| < 2.8 × 10^{-5} G_F

## Context

This proceedings paper reviews the Cabibbo angle anomaly, extending earlier work by Hardy (2020) on radiative corrections to beta decays and by Moulson (2017) on kaon decay tensions. The analysis builds on the global electroweak fit framework of Baak (2014) and de Blas (2016), and uses the SMEFT classification of Buchmuller (1985) and Grzadkowski (2010). The paper enables future searches for new physics at the LHC (non-resonant di-electron searches) and at the proposed PIONEER experiment (measuring R(π)), and connects the CAA to anomalies in CMS and ATLAS di-lepton data.
