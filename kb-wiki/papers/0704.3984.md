# Exact renormalisation group flow for ultracold Fermi gases in unitary limit

> We study the exact renormalisation group flow for ultracold Fermi-gases in unitary regime. We introduce a pairing field to describe the formation of the Cooper pairs, and take a simple ansatz for the effective action. Set of approximate flow equations for the effective couplings including boson and fermionic fluctuations is derived. At some value of the running scale, the system undergoes a phase transition to a gapped phase. The values of the energy density, chemical potential, pairing gap and the corresponding proportionality constants relating the interacting and non-interacting Fermi gases are calculated. Standard mean field results are recovered if we omit the boson loops.

# Exact Renormalisation Group Flow for Ultracold Fermi Gases in Unitary Limit

## Key claim

The exact renormalisation group (ERG) flow for ultracold Fermi gases in the unitary regime yields universal proportionality constants (ξ, η, ε) relating the ground-state energy density, chemical potential, and pairing gap to the non-interacting Fermi gas, but the results depend on the truncation of the effective action and the choice of regulator, with boson loop contributions being small but sensitive to the cutoff function.

## Method

The author introduces a pairing field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to describe Cooper pair formation and adopts a simple ansatz for the average effective action expanded to quartic order in the fields. A set of approximate flow equations for the effective couplings (including boson and fermionic fluctuations) is derived from the ERG flow equation, using a regulator that satisfies the conditions R_{k→0}→0 and R_{k→∞}→k². The system is evolved from a starting scale Λ down to k=0, with fixed particle number density and running chemical potential, and the flow equations are solved numerically to extract the universal coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Universal coefficient ξ (energy density ratio E_GS/E_FG) = 0.62, with mean-field value ξ_MF = 0.65
- Universal coefficient η (chemical potential ratio μ/E_FG) = 0.96, with mean-field value η_MF = 0.98
- Universal coefficient ε (pairing gap ratio Δ/E_FG) = 1.11, with mean-field value ε_MF = 1.14
- Phase transition to condensed phase occurs at critical scale k_crit ≈ p_F (Fermi momentum)
- Results become practically independent of starting scale when Λ ≥ 10 p_F
- Uncertainties related to regulator form are at the 5% level
- Boson scattering length ratio a_B/a_F = 1.13 when boson loops are included, compared to the mean-field result a_B = 2 a_F and the full 4-body result a_B = 0.6 a_F from Petrov (2004)
- The obtained ξ = 0.62 is compared to experimental values: ξ = 0.74(7) from Gehm (2003); ξ = 0.34(15) from Bourdel (2004); ξ = 0.46(5) from Partridge (2006); ξ = 0.51(4) from Kinast (2005)

## Context

This work extends the ERG approach previously applied to nonrelativistic many-fermion systems with pairing by Krippa (2004) and subsequent studies by Krippa (2005, 2006), Salmhofer (2004), and Diehl (2005). It provides a fully nonperturbative alternative to lattice field theory simulations (Carlson 2003, Astrakharchik 2004) and epsilon expansion methods (Son 2005). The results enable future studies including running of all coupling constants, calculations with the entire effective potential incorporating screening effects (Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov 1961), extension to finite temperature, and analysis of deviations from the unitary limit.
