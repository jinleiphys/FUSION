# Ratio of viscosity to entropy density in a strongly coupled one-component plasma

> String theoretical arguments led to the hypothesis that the ratio of viscosity to entropy of any physical system has a lower bound. Strongly coupled systems usually have a small viscosity compared to weakly coupled plasmas in which the viscosity is proportional to the mean free path. In the case of a one-component plasma the viscosity as a function of the coupling strength shows a minimum. Here we show that the ratio of viscosity to entropy of a strongly coupled one-component plasma is always above the lower bound predicted by string theory.

# Ratio of viscosity to entropy density in a strongly coupled one-component plasma

## Key claim
The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density in a strongly coupled one-component plasma (OCP) always exceeds the string-theory lower bound of ℏ/(4πk) = 6.08 × 10⁻¹³ K·s, but the OCP comes closer to this bound than most other systems considered, with the minimum of the relevant dimensionless function R(Γ) reaching 4.89 at Γ = 12 under classical conditions.

## Method
The authors combined existing molecular dynamics simulation results for the normalized shear viscosity η* of a one-component plasma (fitted as η* = α/Γ + βΓ + γ) with Monte Carlo data for the excess energy and entropy of the OCP. They derived an expression for the ratio η/s in terms of the Coulomb coupling parameter Γ and the ratio d/Λ (interparticle distance to de Broglie wavelength), then evaluated this ratio at the classical limit Λ = d to test the string theory bound.

## Key numbers
- String theory lower bound: η/s ≥ ℏ/(4πk) = 6.08 × 10⁻¹³ K·s
- Minimum of normalized viscosity η* = 0.62 at Γ = 20.9 (from fit with α = 0.96, β = 0.0022, γ = -0.030)
- Minimum of the dimensionless function (32π³)¹/² R(Γ) = 4.89 at Γ = 12, exceeding the string theory bound of 1
- For a white dwarf with T = 10⁷ K, Γ = 45, and density ρ ≈ 10⁹ kg/m³: η/s = 2.6 × 10⁻¹⁰ K·s, approximately a factor of 400 above the string theory limit
- White dwarf parameters: n = 4.3 × 10³⁴ m⁻³, d = 1.8 × 10⁻¹² m, v_T = 7.7 × 10⁴ m/s, Λ = 1.5 × 10⁻¹³ m

## Context
This work tests the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (2005) conjecture that η/s ≥ ℏ/(4πk) for all physical systems, extending it from relativistic quantum field theories to non-relativistic classical systems. The OCP result (factor 400 above the bound) is compared to liquid helium, which Kovtun et al. (2005) found to be a factor of 9 above the limit. The authors suggest that multi-component and complex plasmas, where additional entropy contributions from charge fluctuations (Pandey, 2007) or distinguishability of particles may reduce η/s further, could potentially challenge the string theory hypothesis.
