# Extraction of Heavy-Flavor Transport Coefficients in QCD Matter

> We report on broadly based systematic investigations of the modeling components for open heavy-flavor diffusion and energy loss in strongly interacting matter in their application to heavy-flavor observables in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, conducted within an EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force framework. Initial spectra including cold-nuclear-matter effects, a wide variety of space-time evolution models, heavy-flavor transport coefficients, and hadronization mechanisms are scrutinized in an effort to quantify pertinent uncertainties in the calculations of nuclear modification factors and elliptic flow of open heavy-flavor particles in nuclear collisions. We develop procedures for error assessments and criteria for common model components to improve quantitative estimates for the (low-momentum) heavy-flavor diffusion coefficient as a long-wavelength characteristic of QCD matter as a function of temperature, and for energy loss coefficients of high-momentum heavy-flavor particles.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares multiple modeling components for open heavy-flavor transport in QCD matter, but the actual extraction of transport coefficients is conditional on reducing uncertainties from bulk evolution models, hadronization mechanisms, and the choice between Boltzmann and Langevin implementations, with deviations between these two approaches reaching up to 35% for charm quarks depending on the scattering matrix and medium properties.

## Method

The study employed a coordinated comparison of initial heavy-flavor spectra, bulk medium evolution models (including 2+1D and 3+1D ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, transport models, and event generators), heavy-flavor transport coefficients from leading-order pQCD and quasiparticle models, and hadronization mechanisms, all applied to open heavy-flavor observables in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. The groups calculated nuclear modification factors (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v_2) for charm and bottom quarks using a common pQCD-based transport coefficient with alpha_s=0.4 and Debye mass m_D=gT, then compared results across different bulk evolution models and between Boltzmann and Langevin implementations.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass set to M_Q=1.3 GeV and bottom quark mass to M_Q=5 GeV for the baseline transport coefficient comparisons
* For charm quarks (M_Q=1.5 GeV) in Pb+Pb collisions at b=9.5 fm, the Boltzmann approach yields R_AA about 25-30% larger than the Langevin approach in the intermediate p_T region
* For the quasiparticle model (QPM) with alpha_s=0.62, m_D=0.85 GeV, m_g=0.69 GeV, and m_q=0.46 GeV, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann ratio of R_AA reaches up to 35% deviation for M_Q/T=4
* For pQCD*3 scenario with alpha_s=0.4 and m_D=0.67 GeV, the Langevin-to-Boltzmann deviation for charm quarks is in the 5-20% range
* The QGP temperature range explored at LHC is T=160-500 MeV, giving M_Q/T values of approximately 3-11 for charm quarks
* The box calculation at fixed T=300 MeV used an evolution time of t=4 fm, chosen so that R_AA approximately equals 0.4 for the Boltzmann case

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of heavy-flavor transport models initiated by the EMMI Rapid Reaction Task Force, building on prior approaches including the Catania group's Boltzmann simulations (Ferini 2008, Ruggieri 2013), the TAMU Langevin model (He 2011), and the Nantes MC@sHQ+EPOS2 framework (Gossiaux 2009). The study enables future quantitative extraction of the heavy-flavor diffusion coefficient as a function of temperature by establishing procedures for error assessment and identifying which model components (particularly the choice between Boltzmann and Langevin dynamics) introduce the largest uncertainties for charm quarks, while showing that bottom quarks are largely insensitive to this choice.
