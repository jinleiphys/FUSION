# Perturbative chiral nucleon-nucleon potential for the $^3P_0$ partial wave

> We study perturbativeness of chiral nuclear forces in the $^3P_0$ channel. In previous works, the focus has been on one-pion exchange, and the applicable window of perturbative pion exchanges has been shown to span from threshold to the center-of-mass momentum $k \simeq$ 180 MeV. We will examine, instead, whether cancellation of short and long-range parts can sufficiently soften the $^3P_0$ chiral force to make it more amenable to perturbation theory. The result is encouraging, as the combined $^3P_0$ force is shown to be perturbative up to $k \simeq$ 280 MeV, covering many nuclear-structure calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the combined effect of the attractive one-pion exchange (OPE) and the repulsive short-range counterterm in the \(^3P_0\) partial wave can cancel sufficiently to make the total chiral force perturbative up to center-of-mass momentum \(k \simeq 280\) MeV, extending the perturbative window beyond the previous limit of \(k \simeq 180\) MeV where OPE alone was considered perturbative.

## Method

The authors construct a perturbative power-counting scheme for the \(^3P_0\) partial wave where the leading-order (LO) potential is set to zero, and the next-to-leading-order (NLO) potential is the sum of OPE and a contact term \(C_0 pp'\). Higher-order corrections include two-pion exchanges (TPEs) and additional contact terms, with the full amplitude computed via Born expansion. The counterterms are fitted to empirical phase shifts from the SAID program for various cutoff values \(\Lambda\) between 400 and 2400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative scheme describes empirical phase shifts up to \(k \simeq 280\) MeV, compared to the previous OPE-only perturbative limit of \(k \simeq 180\) MeV (Wu 2018, Kaplan 2019).
- The \(^3P_0\) phase shifts reach a maximum of 10.8\(^\circ\) around \(k \simeq 160\) MeV and vanish around \(k \simeq 295\) MeV.
- The pion mass \(m_\pi = 139.57\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.2\) MeV, and axial coupling \(g_A = 1.289\) are used in the OPE potential.
- The subleading TPE couplings are \(c_1 = -0.74\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.61\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 2.44\) GeV\(^{-1}\), taken from Siemens (2017).
- For \(\Lambda = 400\) MeV, the dimensionless counterterm \(C_0 \Lambda^3 = 1.79\) in the perturbative scheme; for \(\Lambda = 2400\) MeV, \(C_0 \Lambda^3 = 408\).

## Context

This work extends the study of perturbative pion exchanges in chiral effective field theory, which previously focused on OPE alone in the \(^3P_0\) channel (Wu 2018, Kaplan 2019) and found a perturbative window only up to \(k \simeq 180\) MeV. The proposed cancellation mechanism between OPE and the short-range counterterm provides a more convergent EFT expansion that covers momenta relevant for many nuclear-structure calculations. The approach contrasts with the nonperturbative OPE scheme (Nogga 2005) which requires fewer parameters but is conceptually the underlying theory; the perturbative scheme enables smaller computational model spaces for few-body and many-body methods.
