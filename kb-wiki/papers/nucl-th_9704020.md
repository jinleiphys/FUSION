# The new limits of the neutrinoless $(μ^-,e^-)$ conversion branching ratio

> The nuclear physics dependence of the exotic $(μ^-,e^-)$ conversion branching ratio $R_{μe^-}$ for the experimentally most interesting nuclei $^{208}Pb$ and $^{48}Ti$, is investigated in various nuclear models. The results thus obtained are combined with the new experimental limits extracted at PSI for these nuclei to put bounds on the elementary particle parameters entering $R_{μe^-}$ such as intermediate neutrino masses and mixing angles as well as relevant parameters of intermediate supersymmetric particles (masses and mixing of s-fermions and neutralinos).

## Key claim

By combining quasi-particle RPA nuclear structure calculations with the new experimental limits from PSI for $^{208}Pb$ and $^{48}Ti$, the authors set upper bounds on elementary particle parameters such as intermediate neutrino masses and mixing angles, as well as masses and mixing of supersymmetric s-fermions and neutralinos, under the condition that the coherent channel dominates the branching ratio with a contribution of at least 90%.

## Method

The authors employed the proton-proton and neutron-neutron quasi-particle RPA to evaluate the nuclear matrix elements entering the neutrinoless $(\mu^-,e^-)$ conversion branching ratio $R_{\mu e^-}$ for $^{208}Pb$ and $^{48}Ti$. They computed both coherent and incoherent contributions to the nuclear form factors at the appropriate momentum transfer, including the muon binding energy in the phase space and form factors, and incorporated Z-exchange diagrams not included in prior studies. The nuclear results were combined with experimental upper limits from PSI to extract bounds on the lepton-flavor-violating parameter $\rho$ for photonic, W-boson exchange, and supersymmetric mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- Experimental upper limit for $^{48}Ti$: $R^{Ti}_{\mu e^-} < 4.3 \times 10^{-12}$ (PSI, Dohmen et al.)
- Experimental upper limit for $^{208}Pb$: $R^{Pb}_{\mu e^-} < 4.6 \times 10^{-11}$ (PSI, Honecker et al.)
- Muon binding energy for $^{48}Ti$: $\epsilon_b = 1.450$ MeV; for $^{208}Pb$: $\epsilon_b = 10.450$ MeV
- Phase space factor $E_e p_e / m_\mu^2$: 0.97 for $^{48}Ti$, 0.81 for $^{208}Pb$
- Coherent fraction $\eta = M^2_{coh} / M^2_{tot} \ge 90\%$ for both isotopes and all mechanisms studied
- Example supersymmetric model prediction: $\rho = 8.2 \times 10^{-18}$ (Kosmas et al., 1990)
- Z-exchange parameter in SUSY model #2: $\xi = 2.8 \times 10^{-2}$
- For photonic diagrams: $Q(\beta) = 0$; for W-boson exchange: $Q(\beta) = 13/23$; for SUSY photonic+box: $Q(\beta) = 2/3$; for SUSY Z-exchange: $Q(\beta) = -14.04$

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear structure calculations for $(\mu^-,e^-)$ conversion by Kosmas et al. (1990) and Chia (1993) using quasi-particle RPA, and incorporates the improved experimental limit for $^{208}Pb$ from Honecker et al. (1996) at PSI, which is an order of magnitude better than the previous limit by Ahmad et al. (1988). The results enable tighter constraints on lepton-flavor-violating parameters in gauge models and supersymmetric extensions, and the authors note that the experimental sensitivity is expected to improve by two to three orders of magnitude in the next few years.
