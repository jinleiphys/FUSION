# Spectrum generating algebra of the symmetric top

> We consider an algebraic treatment of a three-body system. We develop the formalism for a system of three identical objects and show that it provides a simultaneous description of the vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate symmetric top.

## Key claim

The paper develops a U(7) spectrum-generating algebra for three identical objects that simultaneously describes both vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate symmetric top, but the algebraic description is valid only in the limit of a large model space (N large) and for one- and two-body interactions that conserve an additional quantum number M_F.

## Method

The authors introduce a bosonic quantization method using dipole bosons (p†_ρ, p†_λ) for each independent Jacobi coordinate and an auxiliary scalar boson (s†), with total boson number N conserved, to construct a U(7) spectrum-generating algebra. They impose S_3 (permutation) symmetry for three identical objects and construct a Hamiltonian with one- and two-body S_3-invariant interactions, then analyze the intrinsic Hamiltonian for vibrational excitations and a rotational Hamiltonian with L·L and F_2^2 terms. The eigenstates are labeled by quantum numbers including M_F, which is shown to relate to the geometric labels K (angular momentum projection) and l (vibrational angular momentum) of the oblate top via M_F = |K ∓ 2l|.

## Key numbers

- Vibrational energies: E_int = ε_1 v_1 + ε_2 v_2, with ε_1 = 4Nξ_1 R^2 and ε_2 = 4Nξ_2 R^2/(1+R^2), where v_1 is the number of quanta in the symmetric stretching mode (A_1 symmetry) and v_2 the total number in the antisymmetric stretching and bending modes (E symmetry doublet)
- Rotational energies: E_rot = κ_3 L(L+1) + κ_4 M_F^2 = κ_3 L(L+1) + κ_4 (K^2 ∓ 4Kl + 4l^2), with the Coriolis term giving an 8κ_4 Kl splitting between +l and -l levels that increases linearly with K
- The vibrational angular momentum l can take values l = v_2, v_2-2, ..., 1 or 0 for v_2 odd or even, respectively
- Rotational states have K = 0,1,2,... and L = K, K+1, K+2, ... with parity P = (-)^K

## Context

This work extends algebraic methods previously applied to collective excitations in nuclei (the interacting boson model) and molecules (the vibron model) to the three-body problem, specifically for three identical objects forming an oblate symmetric top. The formalism provides a unified algebraic treatment that has found applications in hadronic physics for nonstrange qqq baryons (Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan, 1994) and in molecular physics for X_3 molecules (Bijker, Dieperink, and Leviatan, 1994), and the authors note the procedure can be generalized to three nonidentical objects and more complicated systems such as the four-body system with tetrahedral symmetry.
